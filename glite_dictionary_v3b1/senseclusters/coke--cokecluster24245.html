coke

verb
to become converted into coke (fuel)
6.6
Pronunciation: / koʊk / (AmE), / kəʊk / (BrE)
to convert (something, especially coal) into coke by heating it without air

Examples

To coke coal
During the refining process, heavier oils can coke and form hard carbon deposits.
Engineers monitor the temperature closely to prevent the material from coking in the reactor.
If the residue is heated for too long, it will eventually coke and clog the equipment.
The factory coked the coal to produce fuel for the steelmaking process.
After the coal was coked, it was ready to be used in the blast furnace.
Engineers monitored the temperature carefully as they coked large quantities of coal.
