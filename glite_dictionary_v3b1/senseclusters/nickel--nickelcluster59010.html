nickel

noun
a silvery corrosion-resistant metal element
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈnɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈnɪkəl / (BrE)
a hard, silvery-white, malleable metallic element, resistant to corrosion and used mainly in alloys and as a coating for other metals

Examples

Pure nickel metal
Cans sometimes have a thin coating of nickel to prevent corrosion.
Pure nickel is rarely used; it's commonly combined with other metals to make stainless steel.
The scientist demonstrated how nickel can resist rust in various environments.
