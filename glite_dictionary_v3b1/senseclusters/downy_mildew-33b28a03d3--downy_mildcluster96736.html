downy mildew

noun
fungal plant disease; downy mold
5.8 5.6
Pronunciation: /ˈdaʊ.niː ˈmɪl.duː/ /; / /ˈdaʊ.niː ˈmɪl.djuː/ (AmE), /ˈdaʊ.niː ˈmɪl.djuː/ /; / /ˈdaʊ.niː ˈmɪl.duː/ (BrE)
a plant disease caused by certain fungi of the family Peronosporaceae, characterized by the growth of downy, mold-like patches on leaves and stems of plants such as grapes, potatoes, and melons

Examples

Downy mildew disease
Farmers use fungicides to protect crops from downy mildew infestations.
Leaves infected with downy mildew often develop yellowish spots and a fuzzy growth on the underside.
The grape vines showed clear signs of downy mildew after a week of wet weather.

Related Terms

  • synonym: powdery mildew - a similar plant disease caused by fungi, producing a white powdery coating
