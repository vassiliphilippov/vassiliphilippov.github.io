undo

verb
cause ruin or downfall
5.4 1.78/m
Pronunciation: /  ånÀàdu / (AmE), /  ånÀàduÀê / (BrE)
to cause the ruin or downfall of someone or something

Examples

To undo a career
A single scandal was enough to undo the politician's promising career.
Her reckless spending threatened to undo years of careful saving.
It would be tragic to let one mistake undo all our hard work.

Synonyms

  • ruin
    2.0 38/m
    ruin means to destroy or severely damage things or reputations, while undo means to cause a person's downfall or total collapse.
  • unmake
    7.5 0.33/m
    unmake means to reverse or destroy something that was made, while undo means to cause ruin or bring about someone's downfall.

Related Terms

  • synonym: destroy - to cause the destruction of; to ruin completely
  • synonym: ruin - to destroy or severely damage something or someone
  • antonym: save - to rescue from danger, ruin, or loss
