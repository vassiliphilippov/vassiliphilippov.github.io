chancery court

noun
court handling equity cases
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃæn.sɚ.i kɔrt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːn.sər.i kɔːt / (BrE)
A court with jurisdiction over cases of equity, such as trusts, estates, and injunctions, rather than criminal or common law matters.

Examples

Chancery court cases
Disputes involving trusts and estates are often settled in chancery court.
The company sued its former director in the chancery court to recover embezzled funds.
The judge in chancery court issued an injunction to prevent further demolition of the historic building.

Related Terms

  • synonym: court of equity - a court that resolves disputes based on fairness rather than strict application of law
