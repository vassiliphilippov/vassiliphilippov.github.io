marching orders

noun
military order to move or depart
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑrtʃɪŋ ˌɔrdɚz / (AmE), / ˈmɑːtʃɪŋ ˌɔːdəz / (BrE)
official instructions or orders telling someone to begin a task or to leave a place (originally military, now also general)

Examples

Receive marching orders
After the briefing, the soldiers waited for their marching orders.
The captain gave the company their marching orders at dawn.
When the alarm sounded, we knew our marching orders would follow soon.
After the presentation, the team received their marching orders for the new project.
She knew she was in trouble when the boss gave her marching orders.
We're just waiting for our marching orders before getting started on the assignment.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: discharge - official release from military service
  • antonym: dismissal - an act of ordering someone to leave
  • synonym: instructions - detailed information about how to do something
