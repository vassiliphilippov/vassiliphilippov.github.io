food chain

noun
sequence of organisms as food sources
Pronunciation: / ˈfuːd tʃeɪn /; / fuːd tʃeɪn / (AmE), / ˈfuːd tʃeɪn /; / fuːd tʃeɪn / (BrE)
a series of living things in which each one is eaten by the next, showing how energy and nutrients move through nature

Examples

Animals in the food chain
In science class, we learned how energy flows through the food chain from plants to predators.
Pesticides in the water have impacted the food chain, harming fish and birds alike.
The disappearance of a single species can affect the entire food chain.
Humans are often considered to be at the top of the food chain in many ecosystems.
In the ocean food chain, plankton are consumed by small fish, which are then eaten by larger predators.
The food chain can be disrupted by environmental changes such as pollution or deforestation.
