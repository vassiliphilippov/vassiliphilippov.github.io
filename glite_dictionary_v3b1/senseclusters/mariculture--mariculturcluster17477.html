mariculture

noun
marine farming
Pronunciation: / ˈmærɪˌkʌltʃər / (AmE), / ˈmærɪˌkʌltʃə / (BrE)
the cultivation of marine organisms such as fish, shellfish, and seaweed in the ocean or saltwater environments for commercial purposes

Examples

Mariculture of oysters
The coastal community relies on mariculture to produce oysters and seaweed for export.
Advances in mariculture have made it possible to farm fish sustainably in open ocean cages.
She is researching how mariculture can help meet the world's growing demand for seafood.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aquaculture - the farming of aquatic plants and animals in water environments
  • antonym: fishing - the activity of catching fish for food or sport
