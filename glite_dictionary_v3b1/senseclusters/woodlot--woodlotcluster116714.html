woodlot

noun
small managed forest
Pronunciation: / ˈwʊdˌlɑt / (AmE), / ˈwʊdˌlɒt / (BrE)
a small area of land with trees, often managed for wood production or wildlife habitat

Examples

A woodlot area
The family owns a woodlot where they gather firewood every autumn.
Many birds and small animals make their homes in the woodlot near the river.
He planted new saplings to help restore the old woodlot on his property.
The family enjoys hiking through their private woodlot every autumn.
Many farmers maintain a woodlot to supply firewood and timber for their homes.
Wildlife such as deer and birds often find shelter in a well-managed woodlot.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: forest - a large area covered chiefly with trees and undergrowth
  • synonym: grove - a small group of trees, especially one with no undergrowth
