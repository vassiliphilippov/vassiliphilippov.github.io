faulting

noun
cracking and displacement in Earth's crust
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔltɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːltɪŋ / (BrE)
(Geology) The process of forming a crack or fracture in the Earth's crust, where displacement of one side relative to the other has occurred.

Examples

Faulting in rocks
Ancient rocks exhibit evidence of repeated faulting over millions of years.
Scientists studied the patterns of faulting after the major tectonic event.
The region is known for extensive faulting that leads to frequent earthquakes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fault - a crack in the earth's crust causing displacement
