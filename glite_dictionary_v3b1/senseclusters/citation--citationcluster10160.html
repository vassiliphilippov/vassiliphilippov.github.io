a quotation, reference, or the act of quoting or referring to a source, especially in academic or legal writing

Examples

Academic citation
Proper use of citation is essential in academic writing to give credit to original authors.
The article included a citation from a renowned scientist to support its claims.
The judge referenced a legal citation during the trial.
Proper citation is required in all academic papers.
She forgot to include a citation for the statistics in her report.
The essay contained a citation for every source the student used.
In academic writing, every citation must correspond to a source in the bibliography.
The judge dismissed the claim due to the lack of proper legal citations.
The lawyer included a citation from a recent Supreme Court case to support her argument.

Synonyms

  • reference
    3.2 37/m
    a reference names or points to a source in text, while a citation is the formal academic or legal note that gives source details.
  • quotation
    2.6 7.07/m
    a quotation repeats exact words from a text, while a citation refers to a source or records where those words appear.
  • quote
    1.6 25/m
    a quote is a short passage people repeat in speech or writing, while a citation is the formal reference used in academic or legal texts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: paraphrase - to restate a text or passage in other words
  • synonym: quotation - a passage or phrase taken from a book, speech, or another text and repeated by someone other than the original author
  • synonym: quote - to repeat or copy out (a passage or statement)
  • synonym: reference - mention of a source of information
