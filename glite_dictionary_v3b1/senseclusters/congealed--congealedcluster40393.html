congealed

adjective
hardened or solidified (by cooling)
5.8 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàd íild / (AmE), / k…ônÀàd íiÀêld / (BrE)
(of a liquid or soft substance) having become solid or thickened, usually by cooling.

Examples

The congealed soup
After being left out overnight, the soup had congealed into a thick mass.
Fat congealed on the surface of the stew as it cooled.
He grimaced at the sight of congealed gravy on his plate.

Synonyms

  • solidified
    3.8 0.00/m
    solidified is fully turned into a hard or firm mass, while congealed often means thickened or set by cooling.
  • coagulated
    7.0 0.00/m
    coagulated forms clots or a thick mass, often in blood or proteins, while congealed is a general thickening by cooling.
  • jellied
    3.4 0.04/m
    jellied becomes gelatinous like jelly, often in food by cooling, while congealed simply means thickened or solidified.
