overinsured

adjective
excessively insured
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərɪnˈʃʊrd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərɪnˈʃʊəd / (BrE)
having more insurance coverage than is needed or practical

Examples

To be overinsured
After buying several policies, she discovered she was overinsured and paying too much in premiums.
Many people become overinsured when they do not review their coverage regularly.
The financial advisor warned him that being overinsured could waste money without providing extra benefits.
The company was overinsured, so employees felt less concerned about potential losses.
Being overinsured can sometimes lead to unnecessary expenses for individuals or businesses.
After reviewing their policies, they realized they were overinsured and decided to reduce coverage.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: insured - having insurance coverage
  • antonym: underinsured - having less insurance coverage than is needed
