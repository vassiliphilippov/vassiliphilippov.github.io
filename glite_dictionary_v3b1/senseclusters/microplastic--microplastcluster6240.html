microplastic

noun
tiny plastic particle
5.7
Pronunciation: /maɪ.kroʊ.ˈplæs.tɪk/ (AmE), /maɪ.krəʊ.ˈplæs.tɪk/ (BrE)
a very small piece of plastic (less than 5 millimeters), found in the environment as pollution

Examples

Microplastic pollution
Researchers discovered microplastics in fish taken from the river near the city.
Many cosmetic products once contained microplastics that could end up in the ocean.
The study showed that microplastic pollution is a growing concern for marine life.
Scientists are studying the effects of microplastic on marine life and human health.
Many cosmetic products once contained microplastic beads that ended up in rivers and oceans.
The presence of microplastic in drinking water has raised concerns among environmentalists.

Related Terms

  • antonym: macroplastic - large pieces of plastic debris in the environment
  • degree lower: nanoplastic - an even smaller piece of plastic, usually less than one micrometer in size
  • synonym: plastic debris - pieces of plastic waste found in the environment
  • synonym: plastic pollution - pollution caused by plastic waste in the environment
