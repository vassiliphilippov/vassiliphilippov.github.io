Enron

proper noun
American energy company
Pronunciation: / ˈɛnˌrɑn / (AmE), / ˈɛnˌrɒn / (BrE)
a major American energy company that became infamous for a large corporate fraud scandal in the early 2000s

Examples

Enron corporation
The collapse of Enron led to stricter regulations for public companies in the United States.
Many employees lost their savings when Enron filed for bankruptcy.
The Enron scandal is often cited as one of the biggest corporate frauds in history.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Arthur Andersen - an accounting firm implicated in the Enron scandal
  • synonym: WorldCom - an American telecommunications company involved in a major accounting scandal
