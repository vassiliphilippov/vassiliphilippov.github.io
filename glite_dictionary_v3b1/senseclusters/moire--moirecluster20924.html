An interference pattern (often wavy or rippled) that appears when two regular patterns or grids overlap, commonly seen in digital images, scans, or printed materials.

Examples

Moire pattern effect
Photographers often try to avoid a moire appearing when shooting patterned fabrics under studio lights.
When you scale the image, the printer produced a noticeable moire across the tiled background.
The software's anti-aliasing filter reduced the visible moire in the scanned photograph.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aliasing - visual artefacts caused by insufficient sampling or resolution in digital images
  • synonym: interference pattern - a pattern produced by the overlap of two or more repetitive structures
