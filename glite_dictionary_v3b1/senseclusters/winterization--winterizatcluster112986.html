winterization

noun
oil purification process
Pronunciation: / ˌwɪntərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌwɪntəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a process in which an oil or fat is chilled and filtered to remove waxes and impurities

Examples

Winterization of oil
The winterization of vegetable oil helps improve its clarity and stability.
Before bottling, the company uses winterization to remove waxes from the product.
Proper winterization ensures that the oil remains liquid at lower temperatures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contamination - the presence of unwanted substances
  • synonym: refining - the process of purifying a substance
