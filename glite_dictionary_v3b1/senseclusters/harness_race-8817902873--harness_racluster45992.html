harness race

noun
race of horses pulling sulkies
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑrnɪs reɪs / (AmE), / ˈhɑːnɪs reɪs / (BrE)
a horse race in which drivers ride in small two-wheeled carts (sulkies) pulled by horses trotting or pacing

Examples

A harness race event
He trained for months before entering his first harness race.
The annual harness race drew crowds from all over the state.
Unlike standard horse racing, a harness race requires the driver to ride in a sulky.

Related Terms

  • antonym: thoroughbred racing - horse racing where the rider sits directly on the horse
