countercultural

adjective
opposing mainstream values
Pronunciation: / ˌkaʊntərˈkʌltʃərəl / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntəˈkʌltʃərəl / (BrE)
opposing or rejecting the dominant values, norms, or practices of mainstream society

Examples

Countercultural movements rise
The 1960s saw a rise in countercultural movements challenging traditional social norms.
Her countercultural views often sparked debates among her more conventional friends.
Many artists in the city are known for their countercultural approach to music and fashion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mainstream - conforming to the dominant trends or values of society
  • synonym: rebellious - resisting authority or control
