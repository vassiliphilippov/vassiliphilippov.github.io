contractionary

adjective
reducing economic activity
Pronunciation: / kənˈtrækʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / kənˈtrækʃənəri / (BrE)
causing a reduction in economic activity, growth, or the money supply

Examples

Contractionary policy
The government implemented contractionary measures to slow down rising inflation.
A contractionary monetary policy can lead to higher interest rates and less borrowing.
Economists debated whether the contractionary approach would help stabilize the economy.
