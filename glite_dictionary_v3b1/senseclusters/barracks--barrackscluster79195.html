barracks

noun
military housing
2.3
Pronunciation: /ˈbær.əks/ /; / /ˈber.əks/ (AmE), /ˈbær.əks/ (BrE)
a building or group of buildings where groups of people, especially soldiers, live and sleep together

Examples

Military barracks
After a long day of drills, the soldiers marched back to the barracks.

Each barracks was equipped with beds, lockers, and a common area for the troops.

After the hurricane, many families stayed in emergency barracks provided by the government.
The university converted old barracks into student housing for the new semester.
