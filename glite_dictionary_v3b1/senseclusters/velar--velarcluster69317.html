velar

adjective
relating to the soft palate (in speech)
9.9 7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈviːlər / (AmE), / ˈviːlə / (BrE)
(of a speech sound) produced with the back of the tongue touching or approaching the soft palate, as in the sounds 'k' in 'cat' or 'ng' in 'sing'.

Examples

Velar consonants
Children often substitute alveolar sounds for velar ones when learning to speak.
Many languages feature both voiced and voiceless velar sounds.
The English 'k' is a classic example of a velar consonant.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: palatal - produced with the body of the tongue near the hard palate
  • degree higher: uvular - produced with the back of the tongue near the uvula
