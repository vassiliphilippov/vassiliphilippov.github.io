bring oneself

verb
make oneself do something difficult
1.0 4.9
Pronunciation: /brɪŋ wʌn.ˈself/ (AmE), /brɪŋ wʌn.ˈself/ (BrE)
to force or persuade oneself to do something, especially when it is difficult or unpleasant (usually used in the negative)

Examples

To bring oneself to act
He finally brought himself to confront his fears after years of avoidance.
I never thought I would bring myself to forgive him, but I did.
She couldn't bring herself to say goodbye at the airport.
