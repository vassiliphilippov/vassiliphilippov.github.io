class-action suit

noun
lawsuit filed on behalf of a group
9.0 3.8
Pronunciation: /ˈklæs.æk.ʃən suːt/ /; / /ˈklæs.æk.ʃən sjuːt/ (AmE), /ˈklɑːs.æk.ʃən sjuːt/ /; / /ˈklɑːs.æk.ʃən suːt/ (BrE)
A lawsuit filed by one or more people on behalf of a larger group with similar legal claims.

Examples

A class-action suit filed
A major class-action suit against the company was filed after the faulty product caused widespread harm.
The court approved the class-action suit, allowing thousands of consumers to seek compensation together.
The settlement from the class-action suit provided relief for many affected employees.
