participant role

noun
linguistics: relationship of a noun phrase to a verb
8.0
Pronunciation: / pərˈtɪsəpənt ˌroʊl / (AmE), / pɑːˈtɪsɪpənt ˌrəʊl / (BrE)
(Linguistics) the underlying semantic relationship that a noun phrase or constituent has with the main verb in a clause, such as agent, patient, or beneficiary.

Examples

Participant role in a sentence
A linguist may identify several participant roles in a single clause, such as agent and patient.
Different participant roles help to clarify who is doing what to whom in a sentence.
In the sentence 'The boy kicked the ball,' the boy fills the agent participant role.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: agent - the doer of an action in a sentence
  • degree lower: patient - the entity that is affected by an action in a sentence
  • synonym: semantic role - the role played by a noun phrase in the meaning of a sentence
