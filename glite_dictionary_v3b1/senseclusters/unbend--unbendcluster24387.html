unbend

verb
make less taut; relax tension
6.0 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / ÊŒnËˆbÉ›nd / (AmE), / ÊŒnËˆbÉ›nd / (BrE)
to make something less taut or less tightly stretched; to relax the tension of something

Examples

To unbend a line
Carefully, he began to unbend the fishing line so it wouldn't snap.
She watched the gymnast unbend her body after holding the pose.
The crew had to unbend the sail ropes to lower the mast.

Synonyms

    5.5 0.17/m
    slacken means to make a rope or material less tight gradually, while unbend means to relax tension in something stretched.
  • slack
    4.1 0.10/m
    slack is to let a rope or line become loose quickly, while unbend means to reduce tension in any stretched material.
  • loosen
    1.0 6.23/m
    loosen means to make knots, screws, or grips less tight, while unbend means to ease the tightness of stretched things.
  • relax
    1.0 5.54/m

  • unloose
    7.0 0.12/m
    unloose means to set free or let something go loose, while unbend focuses on lowering tension in a stretched object.
