FSH

noun
hormone stimulating eggs/sperm production
Pronunciation: / ˌɛfˌɛsˈeɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˌɛfˌɛsˈeɪtʃ / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the anterior pituitary gland that stimulates the growth of ovarian follicles in females and the production of sperm in males

Examples

FSH hormone levels
A blood test can measure your FSH levels to assess fertility.
Doctors often check both FSH and LH for a complete hormonal profile.
High FSH may indicate problems with ovarian function in women.

Related Terms

  • synonym: LH - another gonadotropic hormone, luteinizing hormone
