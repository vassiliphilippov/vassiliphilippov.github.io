pretender

noun
one who claims a throne or position
5.9 7.0
Pronunciation: /prɪ.ˈten.dər/ /; / /priː.ˈten.dər/ (AmE), /prɪ.ˈten.də/ (BrE)
a person who claims the right to a throne or high office, especially without a legitimate title

Examples

Royal pretender
After the king's death, a pretender emerged to stake his claim to the throne.
History books often discuss the exploits of the Jacobite pretender to the British crown.
Many saw him as nothing more than a pretender with no real right to rule.

Related Terms

  • synonym: claimant - a person making a claim, especially to a throne or title
  • antonym: ruler - a person who rules or governs
  • synonym: usurper - a person who seizes a position without legal right
