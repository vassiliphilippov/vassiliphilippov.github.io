defeasance

noun
contract voiding condition
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfiːzəns / (AmE), / dɪˈfiːzəns / (BrE)
a legal provision that causes a contract or deed to become void or ineffective if a specific condition is met

Examples

Defeasance clause
The contract contained a defeasance clause that would void the agreement if certain payments were made.
Lawyers reviewed the defeasance to ensure the property deed could be released upon repayment.
A defeasance provision protected the borrower by allowing early termination of the mortgage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: enforcement - the act of making sure a law or contract is followed
  • synonym: nullification - the act of making something legally void
