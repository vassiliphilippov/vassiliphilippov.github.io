probabilism

noun
doctrine relying on probability for belief/action
4.6
Pronunciation: / proʊˈbæb.əˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / prəˈbæb.ɪ.lɪz.əm / (BrE)
The philosophical doctrine that, since certainty is unattainable, probability is a sufficient basis for belief and decision-making.

Examples

Probabilism in philosophy
He rejected absolute certainty, instead embracing probabilism as a guide for belief.
Some philosophers argue that probabilism provides a practical basis for making decisions in the absence of certainty.
The concept of probabilism has influenced debates in both ethics and the philosophy of science.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dogmatism - tendency to lay down principles as incontrovertibly true
  • antonym: skepticism - philosophical doubting of knowledge or certainty
