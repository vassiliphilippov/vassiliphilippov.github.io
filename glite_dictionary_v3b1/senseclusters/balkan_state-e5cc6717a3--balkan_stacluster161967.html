Balkan state

noun
country on the Balkan Peninsula
Pronunciation: / ˈbæl.kən steɪt / (AmE), / ˈbæl.kən steɪt / (BrE)
any of the countries located on the Balkan Peninsula in southeastern Europe, often referring to nations such as Albania, Bulgaria, Greece, and others in the region.

Examples

A Balkan state country
Croatia is considered a Balkan state due to its location on the Balkan Peninsula.
Many Balkan states joined the European Union in the early 21st century.
The political situation in each Balkan state has been shaped by a complex history.
