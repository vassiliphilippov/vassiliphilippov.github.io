fiber

noun
compressed paper/cloth material
4.0 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈfaɪ.bər/ (AmE), /ˈfaɪ.bə/ (BrE)
A leatherlike material made by compressing layers of paper or cloth, used as an inexpensive substitute for leather or wood.

Examples

Fiber board

Some early phonograph records were produced from compressed fiber.
The suitcase was made of sturdy fiber instead of real leather.
