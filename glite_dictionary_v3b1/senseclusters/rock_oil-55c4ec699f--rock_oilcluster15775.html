rock oil

noun
old term for petroleum or crude oil
2.3
Pronunciation: / ˈrɑk ˌɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈrɒk ˌɔɪl / (BrE)
a dark, thick oil composed chiefly of hydrocarbons, occurring naturally in the earth; another term for petroleum or crude oil, now rarely used

Examples

Dark rock oil
Early industrialists used rock oil as a source of fuel and lubrication.
In the 19th century, people often referred to crude petroleum as rock oil.
The discovery of rock oil transformed economies and led to the modern petroleum industry.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crude oil - unrefined petroleum as extracted from the ground
  • synonym: petroleum - liquid mixture of hydrocarbons found in the earth, refined for fuel
