rescind

verb
revoke or cancel officially
9.9 6.3
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈsɪnd/ /; / /riː.ˈsɪnd/ /; / /rɪ.ˈzɪnd/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈsɪnd/ /; / /rɪ.ˈzɪnd/ (BrE)
to officially cancel or revoke a law, order, agreement, or decision

Examples

To rescind a law
Parliament voted to rescind the outdated regulation last week.
The board may rescind its decision if new evidence comes to light.
The company decided to rescind the job offer after discovering inconsistencies in the candidate’s application.

Related Terms

  • synonym: annul - to declare invalid, especially legally or officially
  • antonym: enact - to make into law, to put into practice (opposite process)
  • synonym: revoke - to officially cancel something such as a law or agreement
