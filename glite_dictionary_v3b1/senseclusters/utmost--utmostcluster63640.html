utmost

adjective
most remote or furthest point
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈʌtˌmoʊst / (AmE), / ˈʌtməʊst / (BrE)
furthest or most remote in space, time, or order; at the farthest limit

Examples

Utmost edge
At the utmost edge of the forest stood a single, ancient oak tree.
He pushed on to the utmost reaches of the mountain range, far beyond the nearest village.
The explorers journeyed to the utmost limits of the Arctic Circle.

Related Terms

  • antonym: innermost - situated at the very center or most inward part
  • synonym: uttermost - furthest or most extreme; alternative form of utmost
