polyvalent

adjective
effective against several antigens or toxins
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːliˈveɪlənt / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈveɪlənt / (BrE)
(of a vaccine, serum, or antibody) containing several antibodies, each effective against a specific antigen.

Examples

Polyvalent vaccine
A polyvalent serum was administered to treat the mixed bacterial infection.
Doctors recommend a polyvalent antivenom for bites from snakes with varied toxins.
The new polyvalent vaccine helps protect against several different strains of the virus.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monovalent - containing a single type of antibody or effective against a single antigen
