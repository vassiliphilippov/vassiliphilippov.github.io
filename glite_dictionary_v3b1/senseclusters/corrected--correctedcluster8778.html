corrected

adjective
made right or neutralized (esp. vision, values)
Pronunciation: / kəˈrɛktɪd / (AmE), / kəˈrɛktɪd / (BrE)
(of a problem or undesirable condition) made right, improved, or neutralized; especially when referring to vision or other measurable conditions after adjustment.

Examples

Corrected vision
After surgery, her corrected vision allowed her to drive without glasses.
His accountant provided the corrected figures after catching a calculation error.
The laboratory posted both the raw and corrected data for transparency.

Related Terms

  • synonym: correct - to make right or remove errors from
  • antonym: uncorrected - not made right, still containing errors or faults
