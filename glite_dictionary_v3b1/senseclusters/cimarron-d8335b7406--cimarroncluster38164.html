Cimarron

proper noun
river in New Mexico and Oklahoma, US
Pronunciation: / sɪˈmɑːrɒn / (AmE), / sɪˈmarən / (BrE)
a river in the southwestern United States that rises in northeastern New Mexico and flows eastward into Oklahoma as a tributary of the Arkansas River.

Examples

Cimarron River
After heavy rainfall, the Cimarron can swell rapidly, causing local flooding.
Many settlers followed the Cimarron during westward expansion in the nineteenth century.
The Cimarron River is known for its winding course through northern Oklahoma.
