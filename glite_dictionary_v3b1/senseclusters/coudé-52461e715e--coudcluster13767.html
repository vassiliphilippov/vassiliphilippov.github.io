coudé

noun
curved-tip catheter
9.9
Pronunciation: / kuːˈdeɪ / (AmE), / kuːˈdeɪ / (BrE)
a catheter with a slightly curved tip designed to pass more easily through a narrowed or obstructed urethra

Examples

A coudé catheter
The urologist selected a coudé to help navigate the patient's enlarged prostate.
A coudé is often used when a standard catheter cannot pass through a blockage.
Nurses are trained to insert a coudé for patients with certain urinary difficulties.

Related Terms

  • synonym: catheter - a flexible tube inserted into the body to remove or deliver fluids
  • antonym: straight catheter - a catheter with a straight tip
