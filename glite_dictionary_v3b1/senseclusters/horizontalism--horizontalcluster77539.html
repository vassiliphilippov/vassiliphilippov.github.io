horizontalism

noun
bank-driven money supply
Pronunciation: / ˌhɔːrɪˈzɑːntəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌhɒrɪˈzɒntəlɪzəm / (BrE)
an economic theory that banks supply money according to demand at a given interest rate, rather than controlling the money supply directly

Examples

Horizontalism in banking
The economist explained horizontalism as a key idea in post-Keynesian monetary theory.
According to horizontalism, banks create money in response to borrowers' needs at prevailing interest rates.
Debates between horizontalism and verticalism shape modern discussions about central banking.

Related Terms

  • antonym: verticalism - the theory that central banks control the money supply directly
