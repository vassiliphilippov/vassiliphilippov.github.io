overemployment

noun
excessive employment
7.9
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ɪm.ˈplɔɪ.mənt/ /; / /ə.vər.ɪm.ˈplɔɪ.mənt/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ɪm.ˈplɔɪ.mənt/ (BrE)
a situation in which more people are employed than necessary or employees work more hours than they want

Examples

High overemployment rates
Some economists warn that overemployment can lead to worker burnout.
The company faced overemployment after hiring too many staff for a short-term project.
Policies to reduce overemployment may help improve employees' work-life balance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: underemployment - a situation where people work fewer hours than they want or are overqualified for their jobs
  • antonym: unemployment - the state of not having a job despite being able and willing to work
