ecclesiologist

noun
church society member
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˌkliːziˈɑːlədʒɪst / (AmE), / ɪˌkliːziˈɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a member or supporter of the 19th-century Ecclesiological Society, especially one involved in the study or restoration of church buildings

Examples

An active ecclesiologist
The ecclesiologist gave a lecture on the restoration of medieval church interiors.
As a dedicated ecclesiologist, he traveled across England to study historic parish churches.
Many ecclesiologists contributed to the preservation of important religious architecture in the 19th century.

Related Terms

  • synonym: architectural restorer - a person who restores old buildings, especially churches
  • synonym: church historian - a person who studies the history of the church
