jewelweed

noun
North American plant with yellow/orange flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒuːəlˌwid /; / ˈdʒuːəlˌwiːd / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːəlˌwiːd / (BrE)
a North American plant with yellow or orange flowers, often found in wet places, whose sap can be used to soothe skin irritation

Examples

Jewelweed plant
Jewelweed often grows along streams and in moist woodlands throughout the summer.
Gardeners appreciate jewelweed for its bright orange blossoms and ability to attract hummingbirds.
The sap from jewelweed is sometimes used as a remedy for poison ivy rash.
Many hikers use jewelweed to relieve itching from poison ivy.
The bright orange flowers of jewelweed attract hummingbirds in the summer.
Applying the sap of jewelweed can help soothe minor skin irritations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Impatiens - a genus of flowering plants including jewelweed
  • antonym: poison ivy - a plant that causes skin irritation, often treated with jewelweed
