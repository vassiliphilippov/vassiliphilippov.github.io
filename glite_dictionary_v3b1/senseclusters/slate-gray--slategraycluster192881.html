slate-gray

adjective
dark bluish-gray color
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˈsleɪtˌɡreɪ /; / sleɪt ɡreɪ / (AmE), / ˈsleɪtˌɡreɪ /; / sleɪt ɡreɪ / (BrE)
Of a color resembling the gray of slate rock; a dark bluish-gray.

Examples

A slate-gray suit
He wore a slate-gray suit to the interview, giving him a professional and modern look.
Her kitchen walls are painted a soft slate-gray that complements the marble countertops.
The sky turned a deep slate-gray as the storm approached.
Heavy clouds gave the morning sky a slate-grey hue.
She chose a slate-grey suit for the formal event.
The artist used slate-grey to paint the shadows on the stone wall.
