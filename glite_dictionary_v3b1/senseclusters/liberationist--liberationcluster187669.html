liberationist

noun
advocate of liberation theology
8.0
Pronunciation: /lɪ.bə.ˈreɪ.ʃən.ɪst/ (AmE), /lɪ.bə.ˈreɪ.ʃən.ɪst/ (BrE)
a person who supports or advocates for liberation theology, focusing on social justice and political freedom for the poor

Examples

Liberationist theology
The liberationist argued that the church should fight for the rights of the oppressed.
Many liberationists believe that faith must be connected to social action.
As a liberationist, she worked tirelessly to improve conditions for the poor in her community.
