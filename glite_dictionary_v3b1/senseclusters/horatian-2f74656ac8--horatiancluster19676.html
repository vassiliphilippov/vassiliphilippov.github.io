Horatian

adjective
of Horace's style
9.9
Pronunciation: / hɔːˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / hɒˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the Roman poet Horace or his literary style, especially gentle satire

Examples

Horatian satire style
The essayist adopted a Horatian tone, using gentle humor to criticize society.
Her satire was more Horatian than harsh, preferring wit over anger.
Many English poets admired the Horatian approach to irony and moderation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Juvenalian - relating to the harsher satirical style of the Roman poet Juvenal
