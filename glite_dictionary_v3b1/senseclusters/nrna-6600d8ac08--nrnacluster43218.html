nRNA

noun
nucleolar ribonucleic acid in cells
Pronunciation: / ɛn ɑr ɛn eɪ / (AmE), / ɛn ɑː ɛn eɪ / (BrE)
a type of ribonucleic acid found in the nucleolus of a cell, involved in producing ribosomes

Examples

nRNA in nucleolus
Abnormal levels of nRNA can indicate disruptions in cellular protein production.
Researchers extracted nRNA from the nucleolus to study its role in ribosome assembly.
The presence of nRNA is essential for the synthesis of ribosomal components.
