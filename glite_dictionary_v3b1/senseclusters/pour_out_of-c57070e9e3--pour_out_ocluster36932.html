pour out of

verb
exit in large numbers
Pronunciation: / pɔr aʊt ʌv / (AmE), / pɔː aʊt ɒv / (BrE)
(of people or things) to exit or emerge from a place quickly and in large numbers, often all at once.

Examples

People pour out of doors
Children poured out of the school gates the moment the bell rang.
Smoke poured out of the building during the fire.
Thousands of fans poured out of the stadium after the final whistle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stream out of - move out of a place in a continuous flow
  • degree lower: trickle out of - exit slowly or in small numbers from a place
