redshirting

noun
delaying athletic eligibility
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛdˌʃɜːrtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɛdˌʃɜːtɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of delaying a student's or athlete's participation in school or sports to allow more time for development or to gain an advantage

Examples

Redshirting a player
The coach considered redshirting the freshman to give him more time to develop his skills.
Due to an early injury, she ended up redshirting her first year on the college team.
Some athletes benefit from redshirting because it allows them to adjust to academic and athletic demands.
Some parents consider redshirting their child to give them a maturity advantage in kindergarten.
In college athletics, redshirting allows players to develop skills before competing officially.
The debate over redshirting in early education continues among teachers and parents.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acceleration - the process of advancing a student to a higher grade or level earlier than usual
  • synonym: benching - not allowing a player to participate in a game
  • synonym: gap year - a year taken off between stages of education, often for personal development
  • antonym: starting - allowing a player to participate from the beginning of a game
