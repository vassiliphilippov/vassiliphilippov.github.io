stiff

adverb
to a great or extreme degree (informal intensifier)
Pronunciation: / stɪf / (AmE), / stɪf / (BrE)
(informal, usually postpositive in set phrases) To an extreme or excessive degree; very much.

Examples

Scared stiff
After the accident, he was scared stiff to drive again.
I was bored stiff during the three-hour lecture.
She was frightened stiff when she heard the strange noise at night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rigid - not willing to change; stiff or unyielding
  • degree lower: slightly - to a small degree
