glibbery

adjective
smooth but insincere
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlɪbəri / (AmE), / ˈɡlɪbəri / (BrE)
expressed in a smooth, fluent manner that suggests insincerity or superficiality

Examples

Glibbery speech
The politician's glibbery promises failed to convince the skeptical audience.
She gave a glibbery response that sounded rehearsed rather than heartfelt.
His glibbery remarks made it clear he was not taking the issue seriously.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glib - speaking in a smooth, easy way that is often insincere
  • antonym: sincere - genuine and honest in expression
