resurge

verb
rise again; become active again
7.4 0.06/m
Pronunciation: / r…™Ààs…úrd í / (AmE), / r…™Ààs…úÀêd í / (BrE)
to rise or become active again after a period of inactivity or decline

Examples

To resurge quickly
After a brief period of calm, hostilities began to resurge in the region.
Interest in vintage clothing has started to resurge among young people.
The company's profits resurged after the successful product launch.

Synonyms

  • revive
    4.2 8.27/m
    revive brings something back to life or wide use through action, while resurge describes something rising on its own after decline.
  • rekindle
    5.1 2.18/m
    rekindle restarts a feeling, interest, or hope often by a person, while resurge means becoming active again without outside help.
