B lymphocyte

noun
antibody-producing immune cell
1.4
Pronunciation: / bi ˈlɪm.foʊ.saɪt / (AmE), / bi ˈlɪm.fə.saɪt / (BrE)
A type of white blood cell originating from bone marrow, responsible for producing antibodies as part of the immune system's humoral response.

Examples

B lymphocytes produce antibodies
B lymphocytes play a key role in defending against bacterial infections.
The patient's immune response improved after her body produced more B lymphocytes.
When activated, B lymphocytes can differentiate into plasma cells that secrete antibodies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: T lymphocyte - type of lymphocyte involved in cell-mediated immunity
