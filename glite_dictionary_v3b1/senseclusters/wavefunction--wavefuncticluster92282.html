wavefunction

noun
quantum state function
Pronunciation: / ˈweɪvˌfʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˈweɪvˌfʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
a mathematical function that describes the quantum state and behavior of a particle or system in quantum mechanics

Examples

The wavefunction of particle
The wavefunction of an electron in a hydrogen atom can be calculated using Schrödinger's equation.
Physicists use the wavefunction to predict the probability of finding a particle in a certain region.
If the wavefunction collapses, the particle's position becomes definite.

Related Terms

  • synonym: eigenstate - a specific quantum state with a well-defined value for a physical property
  • synonym: probability density - a function that describes the likelihood of finding a particle in a particular location
