black huckleberry

noun
a shrub with edible black berries
7.8
Pronunciation: / blæk ˈhʌkəlˌbɛri / (AmE), / blak ˈhʌkl̩ˌbɛri / (BrE)
a low shrub native to the eastern United States, bearing shiny black edible berries, considered the most familiar type of huckleberry

Examples

A black huckleberry bush
A patch of black huckleberry thrives in the sandy soil by the stream.
She added a handful of fresh black huckleberry to the pie for extra flavor.
The woods behind our house are full of black huckleberry bushes in late summer.
