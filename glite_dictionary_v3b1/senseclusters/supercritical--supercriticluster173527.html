supercritical

adjective
above critical point
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərˈkrɪtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˈkrɪtɪkəl / (BrE)
describing a substance that is above its critical temperature and pressure, where it exhibits properties of both a liquid and a gas

Examples

Supercritical fluid state
At high temperatures and pressures, water becomes supercritical and can dissolve materials like a solvent.
The extraction process uses supercritical carbon dioxide to separate compounds from plant material.
Scientists study supercritical fluids to develop new industrial and environmental technologies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: subcritical - below the critical temperature or pressure
