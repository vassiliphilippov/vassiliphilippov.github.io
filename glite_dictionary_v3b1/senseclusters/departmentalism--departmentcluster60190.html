departmentalism

noun
group loyalty at work

the tendency for departments or groups within an organization to act separately and not cooperate, often because of strong loyalty to one's own department

Examples

Departmentalism in companies
The company's progress was slowed by departmentalism and a lack of communication between teams.
Efforts to reduce departmentalism included organizing more cross-department projects.
Her strong departmentalism sometimes made it difficult to cooperate with other groups.
The university's departmentalism made it difficult for students to take courses outside their major.
Critics argue that departmentalism can prevent effective communication between different parts of an organization.
Efforts to reduce departmentalism led to more collaboration between the hospital's various units.
Strong departmentalism can prevent a company from achieving its overall goals.
The new manager tried to break down departmentalism by encouraging more teamwork.
Many organizations struggle with departmentalism when departments compete instead of cooperating.

Synonyms

  • siloing
    7.5 0.01/m
    siloing keeps teams or information isolated and blocks communication, while departmentalism describes loyalty to one's own department that causes separation.
  • factionalism
    8.8 0.7/m
    factionalism divides an organization into competing smaller groups that fight for power, while departmentalism makes departments loyal and unwilling to cooperate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bureaucracy - a system of government or management with many departments and rules
  • antonym: centralization - the concentration of control and decision-making in a central authority
  • antonym: collaboration - working together with others for a common goal
  • synonym: sectarianism - strong support for a particular group or section, often causing division
  • synonym: silo mentality - a mindset where departments do not share information with others
