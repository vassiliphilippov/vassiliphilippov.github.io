Vatican Council

noun
major Catholic Church councils held at the Vatican
Pronunciation: / ˈvætɪkən ˈkaʊnsəl / (AmE), / ˈvætɪkən ˈkaʊnsəl / (BrE)
Either of two major ecumenical councils of the Roman Catholic Church held at the Vatican; the First Vatican Council (1869–1870) and the Second Vatican Council (1962–1965).

Examples

The Vatican Council decisions
He wrote his thesis on the decisions made during the Second Vatican Council.
Many modern practices of the Church emerged from the Vatican Council's decrees.
The Vatican Council brought about significant reforms to the Catholic Church in the 20th century.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ecumenical council - A general assembly of bishops of the Catholic Church to decide on matters of doctrine and practice.
