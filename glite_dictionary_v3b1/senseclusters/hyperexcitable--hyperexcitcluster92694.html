hyperexcitable

adjective
overly responsive
8.9
Pronunciation: /haɪ.pə.rɪk.ˈsaɪ.tə.bəl/ (AmE), /haɪ.pə.rɪk.ˈsaɪ.tə.bəl/ (BrE)
having an abnormally low threshold for activation and responding excessively to stimuli, especially in cells, nerves, or muscles

Examples

Hyperexcitable nerve cells
Certain genetic mutations can make nerve cells hyperexcitable, leading to neurological disorders.
The patient's muscles were found to be hyperexcitable during the clinical examination.
Researchers observed that the damaged tissue became hyperexcitable after the injury.
