rifampicin

noun
antibiotic for tuberculosis
Pronunciation: / rɪˈfæmpɪsɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈfæmpɪsɪn / (BrE)
an antibiotic drug used to treat bacterial infections, especially tuberculosis and leprosy

Examples

Rifampicin antibiotic drug
The doctor prescribed rifampicin to help treat the patient's tuberculosis infection.
Some bacteria can develop resistance to rifampicin if the medication is not taken properly.
Patients taking rifampicin should be monitored for possible side effects during treatment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antibiotic - a drug that kills or stops the growth of bacteria
  • synonym: isoniazid - an antibiotic commonly used to treat tuberculosis
