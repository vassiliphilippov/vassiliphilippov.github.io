Synonyms

  • choose
    1.0 276/m
    choose is the regular verb for making a decision from options, while pick feels more casual and everyday.
  • pick out
    2.0 5.92/m
    pick out means to single someone or something from a group, while pick is more general choosing among items.
  • select
    2.5 99/m
    select is used for careful, often formal choosing of the best option, while pick is more casual and wide.
  • handpick
    3.7 0.19/m
    handpick describes choosing the best items one by one, while pick is a general act of choosing from options.
  • opt
    3.2 21/m
    opt means to make a deliberate choice, often with options named, while pick is the everyday verb for choosing.
  • take
    1.0 73/m
    take is often used to choose or buy something quickly, while pick stresses selecting carefully from among options.
  • go with
    1.1 6.23/m
    go with is a casual phrase for choosing one option, while pick implies a more active, possibly careful, selection.
