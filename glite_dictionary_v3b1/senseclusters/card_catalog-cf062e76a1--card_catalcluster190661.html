card catalog

noun
library index with cards for items
3.4 3.1
Pronunciation: /kɑːrd ˈkæt.ə.lɔːg/ (AmE), /kɑːd ˈkæt.ə.lɒg/ (BrE)
a set of cards, usually in drawers, used in libraries to list and organize books or other items, with one card for each item

Examples

Library card catalog
Researchers often consulted the card catalog to locate obscure manuscripts.
The library still maintains a card catalog for older collections not in the digital database.
With the advent of computers, the card catalog has become largely obsolete.
Before computers, libraries used a card catalog to organize and locate their books.
Many students today have never seen a card catalog in a modern library.
The librarian taught us how to use the card catalog to find research materials.
She searched the card catalogue to locate books on ancient history.
The library still maintains a card catalogue for older collections not found online.
With the advent of computers, many libraries replaced the card catalogue with a digital system.

Related Terms

  • synonym: card index - a system of recording information on index cards, often used as a synonym for card catalogue
  • synonym: catalogue - a complete list of items, typically in alphabetical or systematic order
  • synonym: library catalog - a list of all the books and materials in a library
  • antonym: online catalog - a digital system for listing items, especially in a library
  • antonym: online catalogue - a computerized listing of library holdings, often replacing the card catalogue
