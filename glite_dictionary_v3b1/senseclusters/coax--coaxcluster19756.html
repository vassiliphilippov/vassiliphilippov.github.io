coax

verb
gently persuade or influence
7.0 3.3
Pronunciation: /koʊks/ /; / /koʊ.ˈæks/ (AmE), /kəʊks/ /; / /kəʊ.ˈæks/ (BrE)
to persuade someone to do something gently and patiently, often by being kind or offering encouragement

Examples

To coax a child
He finally coaxed his friend into joining the dance.
She tried to coax the kitten out from under the sofa with a treat.
The teacher patiently coaxed an answer from the shy student.

Related Terms

  • antonym: force - compel someone to do something against their will
