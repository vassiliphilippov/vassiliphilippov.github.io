preplan

noun
advance arrangement
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːˌplæn / (AmE), / ˈpriːˌplæn / (BrE)
a plan or arrangement that is prepared in advance for a specific situation or event

Examples

An emergency preplan
The emergency team followed the preplan to evacuate the building quickly and safely.
Each school is required to have a preplan for fire drills and other emergencies.
Before the festival, organizers reviewed the preplan to ensure everything would run smoothly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contingency plan - a plan designed for a possible future event or circumstance
  • antonym: improvisation - the act of creating or performing something spontaneously
