sclera

noun
white outer layer of the eyeball
1.6
Pronunciation: / ˈsklɛrə / (AmE), / ˈsklɪərə / (BrE)
the tough, white outer layer of the eyeball that, together with the cornea, forms its outer covering and provides protection

Examples

The sclera layer
A healthy sclera is usually white and free from visible blood vessels.
The surgeon carefully examined the sclera for any signs of damage before proceeding.
Yellowing of the sclera can indicate underlying liver problems.
