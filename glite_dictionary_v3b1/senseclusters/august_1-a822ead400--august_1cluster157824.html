August 1

noun
date of St Peter's release and harvest festival
Pronunciation: / ˈɔɡəst ˈfɜrst / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡəst ˈfɜːst / (BrE)
A date marking St Peter's miraculous deliverance from prison, celebrated as a quarter day in Scotland and as a traditional harvest festival (Lammas) in England.

Examples

August 1 harvest festival
In parts of Scotland, August 1 is still observed as a quarter day for settling rents and contracts.
Many churches hold special services on August 1 to commemorate St Peter's release from prison.
The old English tradition of baking bread from the first wheat harvest was celebrated on August 1.
