cannon fodder

noun
soldiers treated as expendable in war
Pronunciation: / ˈkænən ˌfɑdɚ / (AmE), / ˈkænən ˌfɒdə / (BrE)
soldiers who are regarded as expendable and likely to be killed in large numbers in war, especially when exposed to great danger with little concern for their survival.

Examples

cannon fodder soldiers
In the brutal offensive, the recruits were treated as little more than cannon fodder by their commanders.
Many believe that conscripts are often seen as cannon fodder in times of war.
The film critic described the background warriors as mere cannon fodder to drive the action scenes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: expendable - easily replaced and considered not essential
  • synonym: sacrificial - offered or used for sacrifice; given up for a cause
