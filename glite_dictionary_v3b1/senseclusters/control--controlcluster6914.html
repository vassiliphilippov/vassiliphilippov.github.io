control

verb
place under legal or official restrictions
1.0 2.1
Pronunciation: / kənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / kənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
to have power over, direct, manage, or restrain people, things, or situations

Examples

To control a situation
New laws were introduced to control the use of pesticides in agriculture.
Some countries strictly control access to the internet for political reasons.
The government decided to control the sale of certain pharmaceuticals due to safety concerns.
He struggled to control his anger during the heated discussion.
She tried hard to control her laughter during the serious meeting.
You must control your emotions if you want to be a good leader.
As a manager, he likes to control every aspect of the project.
She found it difficult to control her emotions during the meeting.
The dictator tried to control the media to shape public opinion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: command - to give an order; to have authority over
  • synonym: dominate - to have control or power over someone or something
  • synonym: manage - to be in charge of; to handle or direct
  • synonym: manipulate - to handle or control a person or situation, often to one's advantage
  • synonym: moderate - make less intense or extreme; restrain
  • antonym: obey - to follow the orders or rules of someone
  • antonym: prohibit - to officially forbid something by law, rule, or authority
  • synonym: regulate - to control (something) by means of rules or regulations
  • antonym: release - allow to act freely; set loose; opposite of restrain or control
  • synonym: restrain - hold back or keep under control
  • antonym: submit - to yield or surrender to someone else's authority
