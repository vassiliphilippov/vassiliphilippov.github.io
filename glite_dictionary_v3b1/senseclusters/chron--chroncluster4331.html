chron

noun
geological time interval
9.9 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / krÉ‘n / (AmE), / krÉ’n / (BrE)
a unit or interval of geological time defined by a single magnetic polarity, used in paleomagnetism and magnetostratigraphy

Examples

A chron of magnetic polarity
The Brunhes chron is characterized by a normal magnetic polarity in Earth's history.
Scientists use the duration of each chron to help date volcanic rocks.
A reversal in Earth's magnetic field marks the boundary between one chron and the next.
