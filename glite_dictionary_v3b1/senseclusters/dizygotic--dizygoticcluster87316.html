dizygotic

adjective
developed from two separately fertilized eggs
7.9
Pronunciation: / daɪzaɪˈɡɑːtɪk / (AmE), / daɪzaɪˈɡɒtɪk / (BrE)
(of twins) developed from two different eggs fertilized by two different sperm; fraternal, not identical.

Examples

Dizygotic twins
The likelihood of having dizygotic twins increases with certain fertility treatments.
The researchers studied dizygotic twins to understand the effects of genetics on behavior.
Unlike identical twins, dizygotic twins can be of different sexes and may look quite different from each other.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monozygotic - (of twins) developed from a single fertilized egg; identical
