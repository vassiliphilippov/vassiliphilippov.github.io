bond

noun
money pledged as guarantee for court appearance
6.7 5.4
Pronunciation: /bɑnd/ /; / /bɔːnd/ (AmE), /bɒnd/ (BrE)
An amount of money pledged as a guarantee that an accused person will appear in court, to be forfeited if they fail to do so.

Examples

Court bond payment
If the bond is forfeited, the court keeps the money.
She could not afford the bond, so she remained in custody until her trial.
The defendant was released after posting a bond of $50,000.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bail - money paid to temporarily release someone awaiting trial, ensuring appearance in court
