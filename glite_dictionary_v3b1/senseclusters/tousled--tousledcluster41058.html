tousled

adjective
untidy or disordered (esp. hair, clothing)
5.3 0.59/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆtaÊŠzÉ™ld / (AmE), / ËˆtaÊŠzÉ™ld / (BrE)
(of hair, clothing, etc.) untidy or disordered, often from being thrown into a state of confusion or carelessness.

Examples

Tousled hair
His tousled hair made it look like he had just rolled out of bed.
She wore a tousled sweater that gave her a carefree appearance.
The wind left my tousled locks impossible to comb.

Synonyms

  • disheveled
    6.1 2.09/m
    disheveled looks generally messy and untidy, while tousled often describes hair or clothes mussed by movement or wind.
  • unkempt
    5.1 1.39/m
    unkempt appears neglected over time and shows poor care, while tousled suggests recent messiness from handling or wind.
  • rumpled
    4.2 1.52/m
    rumpled has creases and wrinkles in cloth or clothing, while tousled often means hair or fabric roughly ruffled.
  • untidy
    1.0 0.81/m
    untidy describes general disorder or mess in places or things, while tousled focuses on hair or clothes messed by movement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: neat - arranged in an orderly, tidy way; the opposite of 'tousled'
