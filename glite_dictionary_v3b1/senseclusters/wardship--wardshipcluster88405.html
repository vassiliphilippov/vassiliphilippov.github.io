wardship

noun
legal guardianship
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔrdʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈwɔːdʃɪp / (BrE)
the legal situation where a person (especially a minor) is under the care and authority of a guardian

Examples

The wardship of a child
The court granted wardship of the child to her grandparents after the accident.
During the Middle Ages, wardship often involved managing the property of young heirs.
He applied for wardship to ensure his niece's well-being and education.
In medieval England, wardship allowed nobles to control the estates of orphaned heirs.
The king granted wardship of the young lord to a trusted advisor.
Disputes over wardship often led to conflicts among powerful families.
After her parents passed away, she was placed in the wardship of her aunt.
The court decided that wardship was necessary to protect the child's interests.
He applied for wardship to become the legal guardian of his younger brother.

  • synonym: guardianship - the position of being legally responsible for someone
