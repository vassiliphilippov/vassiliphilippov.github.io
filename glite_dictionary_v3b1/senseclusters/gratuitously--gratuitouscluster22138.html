gratuitously

adverb
without good reason; needlessly
7.9 0.72/m
Pronunciation: / …°r…ôÀàtuÀê…™t…ôsli / (AmE), / …°r…ôÀàtjuÀê…™t…ôsli / (BrE)
In a way that is unwarranted or done without good reason or justification.

Examples

To gratuitously insult
He gratuitously insulted his colleague during the meeting, surprising everyone.
She was offended by his gratuitously harsh remarks.
The critic argued that the film contained scenes of violence that were gratuitously included.

Synonyms

  • unnecessarily
    3.7 3.66/m
    unnecessarily describes something not needed in everyday talk, while gratuitously stresses that it has no good reason or justification.
  • needlessly
    3.9 2.22/m
    needlessly is used in casual speech for actions that were not necessary, while gratuitously sounds more formal and critical about lacking justification.
  • unwarrantedly
    8.2 0.02/m
    unwarrantedly appears mainly in formal or legal contexts to say something lacks support, while gratuitously is a more general lack of reason.
  • unwarrantably
    9.9 0.00/m
    unwarrantably sounds rare and formal, often in careful writing, while gratuitously is common to say actions lack any good reason.

Related Terms

  • antonym: justifiably - in a manner that is justified or warranted
  • synonym: needlessly - without necessity or reason
