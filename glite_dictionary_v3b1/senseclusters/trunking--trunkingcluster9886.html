trunking

noun
shared communication lines
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈtrʌŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtrʌŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
the system of using shared communication lines to carry multiple telephone calls or data streams between networks or switches

Examples

Telephone trunking network
Modern telephone systems use trunking to efficiently manage large volumes of calls.
The network upgrade included advanced trunking to support more simultaneous data streams.
By implementing trunking, the company reduced the number of physical lines needed between offices.
