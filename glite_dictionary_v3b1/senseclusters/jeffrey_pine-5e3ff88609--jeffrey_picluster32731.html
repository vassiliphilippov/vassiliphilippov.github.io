Jeffrey pine

noun
a tall pine tree native to western North America
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛfri paɪn / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛfri paɪn / (BrE)
a tall pine tree of western North America, distinguished by its long blue-green needles in groups of three and elongated cones, known scientifically as Pinus jeffreyi

Examples

A Jeffrey pine tree
Botanists can distinguish a Jeffrey pine from a ponderosa pine by its cones and needle color.
Several Jeffrey pine trees towered above the forest trail, their long needles shimmering in the sunlight.
The Jeffrey pine is common in the mountainous regions of California and Oregon.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ponderosa pine - a closely related pine species of western North America
