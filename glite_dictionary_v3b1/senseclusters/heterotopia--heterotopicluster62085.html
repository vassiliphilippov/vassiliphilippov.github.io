heterotopia

noun
tissue in wrong place
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛtərəˈtoʊpiə / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəˈtəʊpiə / (BrE)
a condition in which tissue is found in an abnormal location within the body

Examples

Brain heterotopia
The MRI revealed a small heterotopia in the patient's temporal lobe.
Doctors often monitor heterotopia to determine if it affects brain function.
A rare form of heterotopia can cause seizures in some individuals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ectopia - the abnormal position of an organ or tissue
