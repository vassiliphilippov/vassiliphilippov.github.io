wreck

noun
serious vehicle accident
2.7 4.07/m
Pronunciation: / rÉ›k / (AmE), / rÉ›k / (BrE)
a serious accident, especially one involving vehicles such as cars, trains, or planes

Examples

A car wreck

Investigators arrived at the scene of the plane wreck early this morning.
The highway was closed for hours after the massive wreck involving five cars.

Synonyms

  • crash
    2.3 40/m
    crash is a sudden violent collision of vehicles that may cause injuries, while wreck names a serious vehicle accident or damaged vehicle.
  • smashup
    4.4 0.02/m
    smashup is an informal word for a violent vehicle accident with heavy damage, while wreck describes any serious vehicle accident.
  • collision
    3.8 9.21/m
    collision is a neutral term for two moving things hitting each other, while wreck means a more serious vehicle accident.
  • smash
    3.7 0.23/m
    smash is a strong impact or break often used as a verb or for objects, while wreck is a serious vehicle accident.
