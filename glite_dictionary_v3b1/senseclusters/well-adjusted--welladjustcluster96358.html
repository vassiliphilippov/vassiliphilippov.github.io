well-adjusted

adjective
emotionally and psychologically stable
Pronunciation: / ˌwɛl əˈdʒʌstɪd / (AmE), / ˌwɛl əˈdʒʌstɪd / (BrE)
emotionally and psychologically stable and able to deal effectively with the demands of everyday life

Examples

A well-adjusted person
A well-adjusted personality can handle stress and setbacks in a healthy way.
Children who feel loved and secure are more likely to be well-adjusted.
Despite his difficult childhood, Tom became a well-adjusted adult.

Related Terms

  • antonym: maladjusted - having difficulty in coping with the demands of everyday life
