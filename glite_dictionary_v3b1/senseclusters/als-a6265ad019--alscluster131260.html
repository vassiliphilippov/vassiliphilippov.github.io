ALS

noun
progressive neurological disease causing muscle weakness
Pronunciation: / eɪ ɛl ɛs / (AmE), / eɪ ɛl ɛs / (BrE)
A serious progressive neurological disease in which nerve cells in the spinal cord and brain degenerate, leading to muscle weakness and atrophy; also known as amyotrophic lateral sclerosis.

Examples

ALS disease
After being diagnosed with ALS, she began to experience muscle weakness in her hands.
Researchers are working to find new treatments to slow the progression of ALS.
Stephen Hawking lived for decades with ALS, defying typical medical expectations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amyotrophic lateral sclerosis - progressive neurological disease causing the death of nerve cells controlling voluntary muscles
