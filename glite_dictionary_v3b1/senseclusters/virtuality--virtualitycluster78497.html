virtuality

noun
state of being virtual
Pronunciation: / ˌvɜrtʃuˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌvɜːtʃuˈælɪti / (BrE)
the state or quality of existing in effect or as a potential, rather than as something physically present

Examples

Philosophical virtuality
In philosophy, virtuality refers to something that exists as a possibility but not in physical form.
The virtuality of digital worlds allows people to interact in ways not possible in reality.
Some argue that imagination is a form of virtuality, shaping our perception of what could be.

Related Terms

  • antonym: actuality - the state of being real or actual
  • synonym: potentiality - the quality of having potential or possibility
