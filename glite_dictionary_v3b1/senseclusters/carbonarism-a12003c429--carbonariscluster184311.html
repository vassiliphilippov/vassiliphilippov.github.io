Carbonarism

noun
the ideology or practices of the Carbonari society
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːr.bəˈnɑːr.ɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˌkɑː.bəˈnɑː.rɪ.zəm / (BrE)
The principles, practices, or organization of the Carbonari, a secret society that was active in Italy, Spain, and France in the early 19th century, particularly associated with revolutionary and liberal movements.

Examples

Carbonarism secret society
Carbonarism played a significant role in shaping the revolutionary movements across nineteenth-century southern Europe.
Historians debate to what extent Carbonarism influenced later secret societies and nationalist groups.
The authorities viewed Carbonarism as a threat to the established political order.
