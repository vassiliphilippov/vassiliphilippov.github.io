blow out

verb
(of bulbs, fuses) to fail or break
4.4
Pronunciation: / bloʊ aʊt / (AmE), / bləʊ aʊt / (BrE)
(of electrical devices such as bulbs or fuses) to fail, break, or become unusable due to excessive current or overheating.

Examples

The bulb blows out
Don’t worry if the fuse blows out; it’s easy to replace.
The old lamp suddenly blew out while I was reading.
We had to stop the party because the lights blew out during the storm.
