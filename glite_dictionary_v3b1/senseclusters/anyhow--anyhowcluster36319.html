anyhow

adverb
in any case; as an explanation or support
2.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɛniˌhaʊ / (AmE), / ˈenihaʊ / (BrE)
Used to indicate that a statement supports or explains a previous statement; in any case.

Examples

Anyhow, I left
Anyhow, she decided to go ahead with the plan despite their objections.
He missed the train, but anyhow, he still arrived on time.
I'm not sure what went wrong; anyhow, the project didn't succeed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anyway - used to confirm or support a previous statement; in any case
  • synonym: in any case - no matter what has happened or will happen
