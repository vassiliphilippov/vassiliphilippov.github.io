rousseauistic

adjective
of Rousseau's philosophy
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌruːsoʊˈɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌruːsəʊˈɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to the ideas, philosophy, or style of Jean-Jacques Rousseau, especially his emphasis on nature and individual freedom

Examples

Rousseauistic ideas
The novel's rousseauistic themes highlight the beauty and purity of life in nature.
His rousseauistic approach to education focused on nurturing a child's natural curiosity.
Critics described the artist's work as rousseauistic because of its celebration of innocence and freedom.

Related Terms

  • antonym: enlightenment - relating to the intellectual movement emphasizing reason and individualism
  • synonym: romantic - emphasizing emotion, nature, and individualism
