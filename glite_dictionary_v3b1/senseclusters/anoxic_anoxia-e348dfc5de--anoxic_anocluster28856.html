anoxic anoxia

noun
anoxia from poor lung oxygenation
9.9 7.0
Pronunciation: /æn.ˈɑːk.sɪk æn.ˈɑːk.sɪ.ə/ (AmE), /æn.ˈɒk.sɪk æn.ˈɒk.sɪ.ə/ (BrE)
A type of anoxia caused by a failure of the lungs to oxygenate the blood properly.

Examples

Anoxic anoxia in lungs
Chronic respiratory diseases can lead to anoxic anoxia if the lungs are unable to adequately oxygenate blood.
Doctors must quickly address anoxic anoxia to prevent irreversible tissue damage in affected patients.
The patient developed anoxic anoxia after severe pneumonia compromised his lung function.
