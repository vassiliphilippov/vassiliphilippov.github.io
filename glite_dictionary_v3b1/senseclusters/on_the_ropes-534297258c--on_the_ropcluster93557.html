on the ropes

adjective
near defeat or failure
6.6
Pronunciation: / ɑn ðə roʊps / (AmE), / ɒn ðə rəʊps / (BrE)
in a situation where one is very close to failure, defeat, or collapse, often with little chance of recovery

Examples

To be on the ropes
After the third consecutive loss, the team looked on the ropes heading into the playoffs.
During the heated debate, the politician appeared on the ropes as opponents pressed for answers.
With mounting debts and declining sales, the business is clearly on the ropes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: in trouble - experiencing difficulties or in danger of failing
  • antonym: safe - not in danger or difficulty
