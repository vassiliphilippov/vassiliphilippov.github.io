puncheon

noun
log walkway
Pronunciation: / ˈpʌntʃən / (AmE), / ˈpʌntʃən / (BrE)
a rough road, track, or walkway made by placing heavy planks or split logs over wet or soft ground

Examples

Puncheon walkway
The hikers carefully crossed the swamp on a narrow puncheon to avoid getting their boots wet.
Early settlers built a puncheon to make travel possible across the marshy land.
After heavy rain, the only way to reach the cabin was by walking on the old puncheon.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boardwalk - a walkway made of wooden boards, often along a beach or wetland
  • synonym: corduroy road - a road made of logs laid side by side over a muddy area
