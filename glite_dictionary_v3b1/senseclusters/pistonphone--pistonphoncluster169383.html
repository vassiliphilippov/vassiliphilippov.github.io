pistonphone

noun
device for calibrating microphones via sound pressure
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪstənˌfoʊn / (AmE), / ˈpɪstənfəʊn / (BrE)
an apparatus that generates a precise sound pressure using a moving piston in a closed chamber, used to calibrate measurement microphones.

Examples

A pistonphone device
Because of its accuracy, the pistonphone is preferred for verifying sound level meters.
Before taking measurements, ensure the pistonphone is operating at the correct frequency.
The laboratory technician used a pistonphone to calibrate the new set of microphones.
