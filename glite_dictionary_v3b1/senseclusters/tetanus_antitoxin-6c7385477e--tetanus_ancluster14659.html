tetanus antitoxin

noun
antibody preparation for tetanus protection
2.0 4.6
Pronunciation: /ˈte.tə.nəs æn.tə.ˈtɑːk.sɪn/ /; / /ˈte.tə.nəs æn.tə.ˈtɑːk.sən/ (AmE), /ˈte.tə.nəs æn.tɪ.ˈtɒk.sɪn/ /; / /ˈte.tə.nəs æn.tɪ.ˈtɒk.sən/ (BrE)
an antibody preparation used for short-term protection against tetanus, given to people who may have been exposed to the tetanus bacterium

Examples

A dose of tetanus antitoxin
A single dose of tetanus antitoxin can provide immediate protection until a vaccine takes effect.
Doctors administered a tetanus antitoxin after he stepped on a rusty nail.
The hospital kept tetanus antitoxin on hand for emergency situations involving contaminated wounds.
