amberjack

noun
large fork-tailed warm-water fish
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈæm.bɚˌdʒæk / (AmE), / ˈæm.bəˌdʒæk / (BrE)
A large, predatory, fork-tailed fish found in warm seas, especially of the genus Seriola, with a yellowish to coppery coloration.

Examples

Large amberjack fish
Divers spotted schools of amberjack swimming near the coral reef.
Many seafood restaurants in Florida serve grilled amberjack as a specialty dish.
The fishermen caught a massive amberjack off the coast early this morning.
