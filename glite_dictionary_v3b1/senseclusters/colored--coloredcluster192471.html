colored

adjective
dated term for people with dark skin
9.9 4.86/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆkÊŒlÉšd / (AmE), / ËˆkÊŒlÉ™d / (BrE)
(of people) having skin rich in melanin pigments; formerly used to describe people of African or other nonwhite descent, or, in some regions (e.g., South Africa), to classify people of mixed race; now considered offensive or dated in many contexts.

Examples

Colored people
Her grandmother still recalls the era when buses had separate sections for 'colored' passengers.
In much of the early twentieth century, schools were designated as either 'white' or 'colored'.
The old sign outside the building read 'Colored Entrance Only,' a painful reminder of segregation.
He grew up in a community where most of the residents were identified as coloured.
Historical documents often referred to people of mixed ancestry as coloured in South Africa.
The term coloured is now considered offensive and is largely avoided in modern usage.
