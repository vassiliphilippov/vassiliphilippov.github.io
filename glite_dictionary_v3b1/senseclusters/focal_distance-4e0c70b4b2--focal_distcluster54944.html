focal distance

noun
distance from lens to focus
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊkəl ˈdɪstəns / (AmE), / ˈfəʊkəl ˈdɪstəns / (BrE)
the distance from a lens or mirror to its point of focus, where parallel rays converge or appear to diverge

Examples

The focal distance of lens
Astronomers carefully calculate the focal distance needed for their telescopes to focus starlight.
Changing the lens alters the focal distance, affecting the magnification and clarity of the image.
The focal distance of the camera lens can be adjusted to obtain a sharp image of distant objects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: focal length - the distance between the center of a lens and its focus
