death instinct

noun
(psychoanalysis) unconscious drive toward death
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛθ ˌɪn.stɪŋkt / (AmE), / ˈdɛθ ˌɪn.stɪŋkt / (BrE)
(in psychoanalysis) an unconscious drive or urge toward death, self-destruction, or a return to an inorganic state.

Examples

The death instinct drive
His self-destructive behavior seemed to reflect an unconscious death instinct.
In Freud's theory, the death instinct opposes the drive for self-preservation.
Some psychoanalysts debate the existence of a universal death instinct in human beings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: life instinct - (psychoanalysis) the urge toward survival, propagation, and life-affirming behaviors
