baby-boom generation

noun
generation born in the US after WWII
6.4 3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪ.bi buːm ˌd͡ʒɛn.əˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈbeɪ.bi buːm ˌdʒɛn.əˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the generation of people in the United States born during the period of increased birth rates following World War II, typically from 1946 to 1964

Examples

The baby-boom generation
Healthcare services are adapting to meet the needs of the aging baby-boom generation.
Many members of the baby-boom generation are now reaching retirement age.
The baby-boom generation greatly influenced American culture and the economy.
