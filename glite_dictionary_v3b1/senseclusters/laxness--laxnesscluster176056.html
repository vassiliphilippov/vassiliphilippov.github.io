laxness

noun
state of physiological looseness or lack of tone
8.5 6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈlæk.snəs/ (AmE), /ˈlæk.snəs/ (BrE)
The condition of being physiologically loose, lacking firmness or normal tone, especially in muscles or bodily tissues.

Examples

Muscle laxness
Dietary changes may reduce the laxness of the intestines.
Muscle laxness can result in poor posture and back pain.
Physiotherapists often work to correct joint laxness after injury.

Related Terms

  • antonym: firmness - the quality of being solid or taut, opposite of laxness in this context
  • synonym: flaccidity - the state of being soft or lacking firmness, especially in muscles
