tabernacle

verb
to dwell temporarily
Pronunciation: / ˈtæbərˌnækəl / (AmE), / ˈtæbənækəl / (BrE)
to dwell or take up temporary residence, especially in a tent or shelter

Examples

To tabernacle in tents
During the festival, the community tabernacled in small tents by the river.
The explorers tabernacled under the stars while crossing the vast plain.
For several weeks, the family tabernacled in a makeshift shelter after the storm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: camp - to stay in a tent or temporary shelter
  • antonym: settle - to establish a permanent home
