European hare

noun
a large brown hare native to Europe
Pronunciation: / jʊˈroʊpiən hɛr / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən heə / (BrE)
a large species of hare native to Europe and parts of Asia, introduced elsewhere, with brown fur that does not turn white in winter

Examples

The European hare animal
Scientists track the spread of the European hare in many parts of North America.
The European hare is known for its long, powerful hind legs and fast running speed.
Unlike the snowshoe hare, the European hare keeps its brown coat throughout the winter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brown hare - another common name for Lepus europaeus
