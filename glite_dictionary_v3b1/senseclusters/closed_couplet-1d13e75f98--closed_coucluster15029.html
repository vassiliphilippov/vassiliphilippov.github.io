closed couplet

noun
a pair of rhymed lines forming a complete unit
Pronunciation: / kloʊzd ˈkʌp.lɪt / (AmE), / kləʊzd ˈkʌp.lɪt / (BrE)
A pair of rhymed lines in poetry that form a complete syntactic unit or thought.

Examples

A closed couplet poem
In eighteenth-century English poetry, the closed couplet became a dominant form.
Many poets used the closed couplet to end their sonnets with a strong, final statement.
The closed couplet delivers a complete idea in just two lines, making it memorable.
