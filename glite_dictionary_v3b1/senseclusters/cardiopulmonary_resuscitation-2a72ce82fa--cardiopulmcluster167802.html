cardiopulmonary resuscitation

noun
procedure for restoring heart and lung function
6.4 3.7
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːrdioʊˈpʌlməˌnɛri rɪˌsʌsɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɑːdiəʊˌpʌlməˈneəri rɪˌsʌsɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
an emergency medical procedure involving chest compressions and artificial respiration to restore circulation and breathing in a person whose heart has stopped

Examples

Cardiopulmonary resuscitation procedure
After the accident, bystanders attempted cardiopulmonary resuscitation until the ambulance arrived.
Learning how to perform cardiopulmonary resuscitation can help save lives in emergencies.
The doctor began cardiopulmonary resuscitation as soon as the patient collapsed.
