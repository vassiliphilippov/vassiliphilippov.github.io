trail

verb
follow in order to catch
2.5 3.51/m
Pronunciation: / treÉªl / (AmE), / treÉªl / (BrE)
to follow someone or something, especially with the intent to catch or capture them

Examples

To trail a suspect
The detective decided to trail the suspect through the crowded marketplace.
The reporter trailed the celebrity in hopes of getting an exclusive interview.
Wolves may trail a herd of deer for hours before making their move.
