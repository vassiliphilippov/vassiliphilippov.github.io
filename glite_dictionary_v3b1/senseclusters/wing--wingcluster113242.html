wing

noun
military aviation unit
Pronunciation: / wɪŋ / (AmE), / wɪŋ / (BrE)
a unit of military aircraft and personnel forming part of an air force, typically consisting of two or more squadrons

Examples

An Air Force wing
She served as commander of an Air Force wing overseas.
The airfield housed several wings of strategic bombers during the war.
The fighter wing was deployed to the base for joint exercises.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: group - a larger organizational unit than a wing in some air forces
  • degree lower: squadron - a smaller unit of military aircraft, part of a wing
