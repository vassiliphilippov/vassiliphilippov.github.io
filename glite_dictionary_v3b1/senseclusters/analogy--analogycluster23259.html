analogy

noun
theological principle: similarity to God is always outmatched by dissimilarity
3.8
Pronunciation: / əˈnælədʒi / (AmE), / əˈnælədʒi / (BrE)
(in theology) the view that any similarity between God and creatures is always surpassed by a greater dissimilarity; any analogy between God and humans is inherently inadequate.

Examples

Analogy in theology
In classical theology, the doctrine of analogy asserts that all human descriptions of God are at best partial and inadequate.
Scholars debate how far analogy can bridge the gap between human language and divine reality.
The concept of analogy explains why, despite some resemblances, our understanding of God remains limited.
