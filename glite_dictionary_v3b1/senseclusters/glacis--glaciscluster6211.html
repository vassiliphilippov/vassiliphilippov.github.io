glacis

noun
thin paint layer
Pronunciation: / ˈɡleɪsɪs / (AmE), / ˈɡlæsiː / (BrE)
a thin, transparent layer of paint or glaze applied over another layer to change its color, tone, or finish in painting

Examples

A thin glacis layer
The artist finished the portrait by applying a thin glacis to deepen the shadows and unify tones.
A subtle glacis can give a painting a luminous, glowing effect that is hard to achieve otherwise.
She learned to use a glacis to adjust the warmth of the background without repainting the entire area.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glaze - a thin, transparent layer of paint used to alter color or finish
  • antonym: impasto - a painting technique using thick, textured paint
