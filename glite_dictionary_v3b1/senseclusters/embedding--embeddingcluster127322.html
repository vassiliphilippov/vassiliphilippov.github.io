embedding

noun
process of insertion
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈbɛdɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɛdɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of placing something firmly within a surrounding material, structure, or context

Examples

Image embedding in text
The embedding of images in the document made it more engaging.
Modern websites often use embedding to display videos from other platforms.
The scientist explained the embedding of sensors within the device for better data collection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: insertion - the act of putting something into something else
  • antonym: removal - the act of taking something out
