Mohammedan calendar

noun
the Muslim lunar calendar dating from 622 AD
6.8
Pronunciation: / moʊˈhæmɪdən ˈkæl.ən.dɚ / (AmE), / məˈhæmɪdən ˈkæl.ən.də / (BrE)
the lunar calendar used by Muslims, beginning in 622 AD (the year of the Hegira), in which the new year moves through the solar year over a 32-year cycle

Examples

The Mohammedan calendar year
Dates in the Mohammedan calendar do not correspond exactly to those in the Gregorian calendar.
Historical records are sometimes dated according to the Mohammedan calendar.
The Mohammedan calendar is still in use for determining Islamic religious observances.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Islamic calendar - the lunar calendar used by Muslims; more common term today
