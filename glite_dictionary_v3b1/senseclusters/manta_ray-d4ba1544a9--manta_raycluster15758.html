manta ray

noun
large tropical sea ray
Pronunciation: / ˈmæntə ˌreɪ / (AmE), / ˈmantə ˌreɪ / (BrE)
a very large tropical sea ray with triangular pectoral fins, feeding mainly on plankton, generally harmless to humans

Examples

A large manta ray
A manta ray can reach a width of over seven meters, making it the largest species of ray.
Divers were amazed by the graceful movements of the manta ray as it glided through the water.
We spotted a manta ray feeding on plankton near the coral reef during our snorkeling trip.
