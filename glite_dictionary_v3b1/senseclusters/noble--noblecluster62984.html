noble

adjective
impressive and grand in appearance
Pronunciation: / ˈnoʊbəl / (AmE), / ˈnəʊbəl / (BrE)
impressive in size, manner, or appearance; having grandeur or dignity

Examples

Noble building
A noble oak tree provided shade for generations of villagers in the square.
She entered the room with a noble bearing that commanded everyone's attention.
The ancient castle stood on the hill, its noble towers visible for miles around.

Related Terms

  • antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance or appearance
  • synonym: majestic - having or showing impressive beauty or dignity
  • synonym: stately - impressive in appearance; dignified and graceful
