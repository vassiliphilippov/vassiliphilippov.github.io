time-fuse

noun
fuse burning for set time before detonation
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪm ˌfjuz / (AmE), / ˈtaɪm ˌfjuːz / (BrE)
a fuse designed to burn for a specific length of time before detonating an explosive device, such as a bomb or shell

Examples

A time-fuse delay
He adjusted the time-fuse for precisely ten seconds before lighting it.
The artillery shell was equipped with a time-fuse to detonate after reaching its target.
The demolition team set a time-fuse to ensure everyone had evacuated before the explosion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fuse - a device used to ignite an explosive
