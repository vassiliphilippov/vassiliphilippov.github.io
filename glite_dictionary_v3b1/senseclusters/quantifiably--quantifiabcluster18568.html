quantifiably

adverb
measurably
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɑːntɪfaɪəbli / (AmE), / ˈkwɒntɪfaɪəbli / (BrE)
in a way that can be measured or expressed as a quantity

Examples

To be quantifiably better
The new process is quantifiably more efficient than the old one.
Her performance improved quantifiably after she started practicing daily.
The benefits of the program are quantifiably significant according to the latest data.
The new policy has quantifiably improved employee satisfaction across all departments.
Her efforts were quantifiably successful, as shown by the increased test scores.
The results are quantifiably better than last year's performance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: immeasurably - in a way that cannot be measured
  • synonym: measurably - in a way that can be measured
