maudlinness

noun
excessive sentimentality
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔdlɪnnəs / (AmE), / ˈmɔːdlɪnnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being overly sentimental or tearfully emotional, sometimes especially when affected by alcohol

Examples

The maudlinness of movies
The maudlinness of the movie made some viewers roll their eyes.
He tried to hide his maudlinness during the farewell party, but tears welled up anyway.
Critics accused the novel of unnecessary maudlinness in its final chapters.
The maudlinness of his speech made everyone in the room uncomfortable.
After several drinks, his maudlinness became apparent as he started reminiscing about old friends.
Writers of the era often criticized the maudlinness that accompanied public celebrations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sentimentality - the quality of being overly emotional or tender
  • antonym: sobriety - the state of being sober; not affected by alcohol
  • antonym: stoicism - the quality of showing little or no emotion
