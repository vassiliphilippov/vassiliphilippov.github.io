technical knockout

noun
referee-stopped fight in boxing
6.3 3.3
Pronunciation: /ˈtek.nɪ.kəl ˈnɑːk.aʊt/ /; / /ˈtek.nə.kəl ˈnɑːk.aʊt/ (AmE), /ˈtek.nɪ.kəl ˈnɒk.aʊt/ /; / /ˈtek.nə.kəl ˈnɒk.aʊt/ (BrE)
an occasion in boxing or other combat sports when a fight is stopped by the referee because one competitor is unable to continue safely, even though they are not literally knocked out

Examples

A technical knockout win
She won the title fight by technical knockout, dominating every round until the stoppage.
The referee called a technical knockout in the eighth round after the boxer took too many blows.
Three of his last five victories have come by technical knockout rather than by decision.

Related Terms

  • synonym: knockout - when a boxer is rendered unconscious or unable to rise within the count
