stooped

adjective
having a rounded, bent posture
4.7 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / stupt / (AmE), / stuÀêpt / (BrE)
having the back and shoulders rounded forward; not standing erect

Examples

A stooped figure
A stooped figure emerged from the shadows, moving slowly and carefully.
Even as a child, she was noticeably stooped from poor posture.
The old man walked with a stooped posture, leaning heavily on his cane.

Synonyms

  • hunched
    4.1 0.17/m
    hunched describes someone with a strongly curved upper back and folded shoulders, while stooped describes a more general bent posture.
  • round-shouldered
    4.8 0.09/m
    round-shouldered refers to shoulders and upper back pushed forward, while stooped describes an overall bent stance often from age or tiredness.
  • round-backed
    5.9 0.01/m
    round-backed means having a curved back like a small hump, while stooped means a forward-bent posture of back and shoulders.

Related Terms

  • antonym: erect - upright in posture or position
  • synonym: hunched - having the back arched or bent forward
