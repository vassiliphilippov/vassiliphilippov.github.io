put to sleep

verb
make unconscious, euthanize, or cause to forget
6.3 0.59/m
Pronunciation: / p ät t…ô sliÀêp / (AmE), / p ät t…ô sliÀêp / (BrE)
to cause to become unconscious, especially by anesthesia; to euthanize an animal humanely; or to cause someone to forget or stop thinking about something.

Examples

To put to sleep a patient
After the accident, the doctor decided to put her to sleep before operating.
He tries to put to sleep the painful memories of his childhood.
It was heartbreaking when we had to put our cat to sleep last year.

Synonyms

  • anesthetize
    7.1 0.85/m
    anesthetize means giving a medical drug to make someone unconscious for surgery, while put to sleep is more informal and also means euthanize.
  • put under
    6.0 0.45/m
    put under means giving an anesthetic before an operation, while put to sleep also means humanely killing animals or calming someone.
  • put out
    7.3 0.23/m
    put out means administering an anesthetic so a person becomes unconscious for a procedure, while put to sleep also means euthanize.
  • put down
    5.5 1.94/m
    put down means killing an animal humanely to end suffering, while put to sleep can also mean giving anesthesia or soothing someone.
  • put away
    7.7 0.05/m
    put away means ending an animal's life humanely to stop suffering, while put to sleep also means giving anesthesia or calming.
  • put out of someone's misery
    7.3 0.00/m
    put out of someone's misery means ending suffering, sometimes by euthanasia, while put to sleep also includes simple soothing or anesthesia.

Related Terms
