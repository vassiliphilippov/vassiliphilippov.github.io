weary

verb
become tired or exhausted
6.0 3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈwɪr.iː/ (AmE), /ˈwɪə.riː/ (BrE)
to make or become exhausted or tired, especially through prolonged effort or strain

Examples

To weary the body
If you weary yourself now, you'll have no energy left for the evening.
Over time, the constant noise began to weary her.
The soldiers were wearied by the long march through the mountains.

Related Terms

  • antonym: refresh - to restore energy or vigor; to revive
  • synonym: tire - to reduce strength or energy; to exhaust
