corrie

noun
steep mountain basin formed by glacier
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔːri / (AmE), / ˈkɒri / (BrE)
A steep-walled, semicircular hollow on a mountainside, often containing a small lake, formed by glacial erosion.

Examples

A corrie lake
Loch Coire an Lochain is one of the highest lakes found in a Scottish corrie.
Snow sometimes lingers in the shadowy depths of the highest corries well into summer.
The hikers stopped to admire the lake nestled in a dramatic corrie.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cirque - a steep-walled, amphitheatre-like valley formed by glacial erosion
