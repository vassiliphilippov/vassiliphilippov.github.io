control account

noun
summary account in general ledger
2.7 5.2
Pronunciation: / kənˈtroʊl əˈkaʊnt / (AmE), / kənˈtrəʊl əˈkaʊnt / (BrE)
an account in the general ledger that summarizes the total amounts recorded in a subsidiary ledger, used to ensure consistency and accuracy in accounting records

Examples

The control account balance
Auditors often check the control account to verify that the summarized balances agree with detailed records.
If the totals do not agree, the discrepancy may be found by reconciling the control account with the subsidiary ledger.
The control account in the general ledger matches the total of all the individual balances in the subsidiary ledger.
