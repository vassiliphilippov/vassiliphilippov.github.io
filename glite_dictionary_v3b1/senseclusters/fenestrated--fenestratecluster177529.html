fenestrated

adjective
having openings
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛnəˌstreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈfɛnɪstreɪtɪd / (BrE)
having windows or openings in the surface, especially in a building or structure, often for light or decoration

Examples

Fenestrated wall
The architect designed a fenestrated wall to allow more natural light into the lobby.
Many modern buildings feature fenestrated facades for both aesthetic and practical reasons.
The museum's fenestrated roof creates interesting patterns of sunlight on the exhibits below.

Related Terms

  • synonym: perforated - having a series of small holes or openings
  • antonym: solid - having no openings or gaps
