acinus

noun
small drupe of aggregate fruit
Pronunciation: / ˈæsɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈasɪnəs / (BrE)
one of the small drupes or drupelets that collectively form an aggregate or multiple fruit, such as a blackberry or raspberry

Examples

Raspberry acinus
Each acinus in a raspberry contains its own tiny seed.
The fruit is made up of many acini clustered together.
Under a microscope, the acinus of a blackberry can be seen as a separate unit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drupe - a fleshy fruit with a single stone or pit, like a cherry or peach
