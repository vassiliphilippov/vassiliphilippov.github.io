westerly

adjective
(of wind) from the west
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛstərli /; / ˈwɛstɚli / (AmE), / ˈwɛstəli / (BrE)
(of wind, movement, or direction) coming from or going toward the west

Examples

Westerly wind
A strong westerly wind brought clouds and rain to the coast.
Farmers often worry about crop damage when a cold westerly arrives in spring.
The sailors adjusted their course to take advantage of the westerly breeze.
Migrating birds often follow a westerly route to reach their winter habitats.
The ship set a westerly course across the Atlantic Ocean.
We continued our westerly journey until we reached the coastline.

Related Terms

  • antonym: easterly - (of wind) blowing from the east
  • antonym: eastward - moving toward the east
  • antonym: northerly - (of wind) blowing from the north
  • antonym: southerly - (of wind) blowing from the south
  • degree higher: westerly - blowing from the west or situated in the west
