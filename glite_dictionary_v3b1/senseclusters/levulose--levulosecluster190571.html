levulose

noun
simple sugar found in fruits and honey
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛv.jʊˌloʊs /; / ˈlɛvjuˌloʊs / (AmE), / ˈliːvjʊˌləʊs / (BrE)
a simple sugar occurring naturally in honey and many ripe fruits; another name for fructose

Examples

Natural levulose sugar
Honey contains a high amount of levulose, making it sweeter than many other natural sugars.
Some food labels list levulose as an ingredient, especially in fruit-based products.
The scientist analyzed the fruit and confirmed the presence of levulose among its natural sugars.
Laevulose is abundant in ripe apples and pears, contributing to their sweetness.
Some people find that laevulose is easier to digest than other types of sugar.
The laboratory analysis confirmed the presence of laevulose in the honey sample.

Related Terms
