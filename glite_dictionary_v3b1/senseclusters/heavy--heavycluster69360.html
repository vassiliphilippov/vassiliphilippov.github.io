heavy

adjective
requiring or showing effort
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛvi / (AmE), / ˈhɛvi / (BrE)
requiring a lot of effort, being hard, intense, or demanding

Examples

Heavy work
His heavy breathing after the run worried the trainer.
She climbed the stairs with heavy steps, clearly tired from work.
The text is rather heavy reading, full of technical jargon.
Rescue teams struggled against the heavy rain to reach the flooded village.
The winds were so heavy that the trees began to sway dangerously.
There was heavy traffic on the highway after the accident.
After hours of heavy lifting at the warehouse, she was completely exhausted.
Completing the mountain trail involved heavy exertion and tested everyone's limits.
The construction job required heavy manual labor every day.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arduous - involving or requiring strenuous effort; difficult and tiring
  • antonym: effortless - not requiring effort, easy or smooth
  • synonym: intense - having strong force, degree, or strength
  • synonym: labored - showing signs of strain or requiring effort
  • antonym: light - not demanding much effort; easy
  • antonym: mild - not intense, moderate in force or strength
  • synonym: steep - having a sharp inclination or slope
