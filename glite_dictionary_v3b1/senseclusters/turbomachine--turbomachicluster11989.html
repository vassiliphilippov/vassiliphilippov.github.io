turbomachine

noun
energy-transferring machine
Pronunciation: / ˈtɜrboʊməˌʃiːn / (AmE), / ˈtɜːbəməˌʃiːn / (BrE)
a machine that transfers energy between a moving rotor and a fluid, such as air or water

Examples

A turbomachine rotor
A jet engine is a type of turbomachine used in aviation.
Engineers study the efficiency of each turbomachine to improve energy conversion.
The power plant relies on a large turbomachine to generate electricity from steam.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compressor - a machine that increases the pressure of a gas
  • synonym: turbine - a machine that produces power from a fast-moving flow of water, steam, or air
