conflux

noun
a coming together or merging, esp. of streams
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑnˌflʌks / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.flʌks / (BrE)
A coming or flowing together of two or more things, especially streams, rivers, or ideas.

Examples

The conflux of rivers
The conflux of ideas at the conference sparked innovative solutions.
The conflux of the two rivers created a wider, more powerful stream.
There was a remarkable conflux of cultures at the international festival.

Related Terms

  • synonym: confluence - a meeting or joining of rivers
