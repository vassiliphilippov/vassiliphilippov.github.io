tamponade

noun
blockage to stop bleeding, usually medical
5.7 8.4
Pronunciation: /tæm.pə.ˈneɪd/ (AmE), /tæm.pə.ˈneɪd/ (BrE)
the blockage or closure of a wound or body cavity, especially to stop bleeding, by means of a tampon or similar material

Examples

Cardiac tamponade
In cases of cardiac tamponade, immediate intervention is required to relieve pressure on the heart.
Nasal tamponade is commonly used to treat severe nosebleeds in emergency rooms.
The surgeon applied a tamponade to control the patient's internal bleeding.
