property-owning

adjective
owning property as main income source
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑːpərti ˌoʊnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈprɒpəti ˌəʊnɪŋ / (BrE)
Owning land or other property as a principal source of income.

Examples

A property-owning class
A property-owning class emerged during the nineteenth century, shaping political and economic power.
The country was once dominated by a property-owning elite who controlled most of the farmland.
The reforms aimed to reduce the influence of the property-owning aristocracy over rural communities.
