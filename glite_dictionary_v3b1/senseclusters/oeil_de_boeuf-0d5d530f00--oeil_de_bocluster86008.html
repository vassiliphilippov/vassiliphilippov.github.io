oeil de boeuf

noun
small circular/oval window in French architecture
Pronunciation: / wɛl də bʊf / (AmE), / wɜːl də bɜːf / (BrE)
A small, usually round or oval window, especially in 17th or 18th century French architecture.

Examples

A small oeil de boeuf window
Many Parisian buildings from the Baroque period feature an oeil de boeuf above the main doors.
The grand hallway was illuminated by a single oeil de boeuf high above the entryway.
The restoration brought back the original stained-glass oeil de boeuf that had been bricked over for years.
