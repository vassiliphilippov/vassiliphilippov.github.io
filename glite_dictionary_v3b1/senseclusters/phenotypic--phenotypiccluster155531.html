phenotypic

adjective
relating to a phenotype
Pronunciation: / ˌfiːnəˈtɪpɪk / (AmE), / ˌfiːnəˈtɪpɪk / (BrE)
relating to or constituting a phenotype; describing characteristics or traits resulting from the interaction of an organism's genes and environment.

Examples

Phenotypic variation
Drug response may vary due to phenotypic variation among individuals.
The phenotypic traits observed in the study were influenced by both genetics and environment.
The researchers analyzed the phenotypic differences between wild and domesticated plants.

Related Terms

  • antonym: genotypic - relating to or constituting a genotype
