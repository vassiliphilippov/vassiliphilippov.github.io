jurisdiction

noun
the area under legal authority
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒɝːɪsˈdɪkʃən /; / ˌdʒɝː.ɪsˈdɪk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌdʒʊərɪsˈdɪkʃən /; / ˌdʒʊə.rɪsˈdɪk.ʃən / (BrE)
the official power or authority to make legal decisions and judgments, especially within a certain area or over certain types of cases

Examples

Local jurisdiction
Each city operates independently within its own jurisdiction.
Environmental regulations may vary from one jurisdiction to another.
The court has no jurisdiction outside the state borders.
Different agencies may have overlapping jurisdictions in environmental matters.
The dispute is outside the jurisdiction of local authorities.
The federal court does not have jurisdiction over this case.
The dispute falls outside the jurisdiction of this particular court.
The federal court has jurisdiction over cases involving federal law.
The new law limits the jurisdiction of local authorities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authority - the power or right to give orders and make decisions
  • synonym: power - the ability or capacity to do something or act in a particular way
