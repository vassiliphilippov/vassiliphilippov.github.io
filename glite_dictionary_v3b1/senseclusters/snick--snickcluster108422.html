snick

noun
slight contact with cricket ball by bat edge
Pronunciation: / snɪk / (AmE), / snɪk / (BrE)
a slight, glancing contact with the ball off the edge of the cricket bat, especially as detected in cricket

Examples

A snick off the bat
The umpire gave him out after hearing a faint snick as the ball passed the bat.
The wicketkeeper heard the snick and immediately appealed for a catch.
There was a clear snick, but the batsman stood his ground.
