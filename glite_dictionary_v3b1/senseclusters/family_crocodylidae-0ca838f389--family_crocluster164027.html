family Crocodylidae

noun
biological family of true crocodiles
Pronunciation: / ˈfæməli ˌkrɑːkəˈdaɪlɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ˌkrɒkəˈdaɪlɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of large, predatory, semiaquatic reptiles known as true crocodiles, native to tropical regions worldwide

Examples

The family Crocodylidae reptiles
Most members of the family Crocodylidae are formidable predators adapted for life in rivers and swamps.
Scientists study evolutionary differences between the family Crocodylidae and its close relatives such as alligators and gharials.
The family Crocodylidae contains several species of large, powerful reptiles found in Africa, Asia, the Americas, and Australia.
