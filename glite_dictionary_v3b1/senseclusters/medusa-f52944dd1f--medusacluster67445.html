Medusa

proper noun
Gorgon in Greek mythology slain by Perseus
Pronunciation: / məˈdjuːzə / (AmE), / mɪˈdjuːzə / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a woman who was transformed into a Gorgon by Athena and whose gaze could turn people to stone; she was slain by Perseus.

Examples

Medusa in mythology
According to legend, Perseus used a mirrored shield to avoid Medusa's deadly gaze.
Many people mistakenly believe that Medusa was always a monster, but she was once a beautiful woman.
The myth of Medusa has inspired countless works of art and literature.
