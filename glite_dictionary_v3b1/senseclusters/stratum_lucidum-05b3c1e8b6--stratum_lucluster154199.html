stratum lucidum

noun
clear layer in thick skin under outermost skin layer
Pronunciation: / ˈstrætəm luˈsɪdəm / (AmE), / ˈstrɑːtəm luːˈsiːdəm / (BrE)
a thin, clear layer of the epidermis found between the stratum corneum and the stratum granulosum, present only in the thick skin of the palms and soles

Examples

The stratum lucidum layer
Damage to the stratum lucidum can impair the barrier function of thick skin on the soles.
In thin skin, the stratum lucidum is usually absent or very difficult to detect.
The stratum lucidum is clearly visible in the skin of the palms under a microscope.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: stratum corneum - the outermost layer of the epidermis
  • degree lower: stratum granulosum - the layer beneath the stratum lucidum in the epidermis
