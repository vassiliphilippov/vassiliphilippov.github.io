clear

verb
remove obstructions; make empty
1.9 1.4
Pronunciation: / klɪr / (AmE), / klɪə / (BrE)
to remove obstructions, people, or unwanted things from a place; to make empty or unobstructed

Examples

To clear the table
It took all morning to clear the garage of old boxes.
Please clear the table before dinner.
The workers cleared the path so hikers could pass safely.
After the storm, they worked hard to clear the road of fallen branches and debris.
She used a shovel to clear a path through the thick snow to the front door.
The volunteers quickly cleared the entrance to allow emergency vehicles to pass.
Security personnel quickly cleared everyone from the stadium following the threat.
The authorities had to clear the building after the fire alarm went off.
They began clearing the area as soon as the emergency was reported.
