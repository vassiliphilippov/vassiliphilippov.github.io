sea robber

noun
pirate, nautical robber
5.4 8.6
Pronunciation: / si ˈrɑbər / (AmE), / si ˈrɒbə / (BrE)
someone who robs ships at sea or raids land from the sea without official authority; a pirate

Examples

A sea robber attacks ships
A sea robber lurked off the coast, waiting for merchant ships to pass by.
In the 17th century, many coastal towns lived in fear of the notorious sea robbers.
The difference between a privateer and a sea robber was often just a matter of paperwork.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pirate - a person who attacks and robs ships at sea
  • antonym: privateer - a person authorized by a government to attack enemy ships, similar to a pirate but with legal protection
