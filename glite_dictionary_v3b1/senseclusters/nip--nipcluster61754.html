nip

verb
squeeze tightly with fingers or similar
Pronunciation: / nɪp / (AmE), / nɪp / (BrE)
to pinch, bite, or snip something quickly and sharply, often with fingers, teeth, or a tool

Examples

To nip a finger
If you nip the dough too hard, it may lose its shape.
She warned the puppy not to nip her fingers during play.
The little boy tried to nip his sister's arm in retaliation.
A cold wind seemed to nip at her cheeks as she stepped outside.
If you nip the apple with your teeth, you'll see how crisp it is.
The playful puppy likes to nip at people's fingers during playtime.
He carefully nipped the end of the thread with small scissors.
The gardener used her fingers to nip off the wilted buds.
You should nip the damaged leaves from the plant to encourage growth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bite - to use the teeth to cut into something
  • synonym: clip - to cut off a small part with shears or scissors
  • synonym: pinch - to squeeze (someone or something) between finger and thumb or between two surfaces
  • synonym: snip - to cut or remove with small quick strokes, especially with scissors
