hurt

verb
cause damage; affect negatively
1.5 1.0
Pronunciation: /hɜːrt/ (AmE), /hɜːt/ (BrE)
to cause or feel pain, injury, or harm (physical or emotional)

Examples

To hurt someone
Her harsh words really hurt his feelings.
Rising oil prices hurt the global economy year after year.
The decision to cut funding will hurt local schools significantly.
He was hurting after the tough workout at the gym.
It hurts to think about how things ended between us.
My leg really hurts after I fell on the stairs.
He didn't mean to hurt you with his honest opinion.
It really hurt her when her friend forgot her birthday.
Sometimes, even small comments can hurt deeply.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ache - to suffer a continuous dull pain
  • antonym: comfort - to ease emotional pain or distress
  • synonym: damage - to cause physical harm or injury to something
  • synonym: harm - to physically or mentally injure or damage
  • antonym: heal - to become healthy or cause to become healthy again
  • antonym: help - to make something better or easier
  • synonym: injure - to cause physical harm to
  • synonym: pain - to cause physical or emotional pain
  • synonym: wound - to inflict emotional or psychological pain
