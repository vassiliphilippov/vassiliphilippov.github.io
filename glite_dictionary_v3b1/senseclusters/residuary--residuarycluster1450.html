residuary

adjective
entitled to remainder of an estate
9.3 7.4
Pronunciation: /rez.ɪ.ˈdʒʊr.iː/ /; / /rɪz.ɪ.ˈdʒʊr.iː/ /; / /rez.ɪ.ˈdjuːr.iː/ (AmE), /rez.ɪ.ˈdʒʊə.riː/ /; / /rɪz.ɪ.ˈdʒʊə.riː/ /; / /rez.ɪ.ˈdjuə.riː/ (BrE)
relating to what is left after the main part has been taken or used, especially the remainder of an estate after debts and gifts

Examples

The residuary estate
The residuary clause ensures nothing in the estate is left unallocated.
The trustee distributed the property to the residuary beneficiary after debts were cleared.
Under the will, her niece was named the residuary legatee.
After all specific debts were paid, the residuary funds were divided among the children.
Her will named her nephew as the residuary beneficiary.
The lawyer explained the distribution of the residuary estate to the heirs.
