Apsu

proper noun
Babylonian god, father of the gods
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɑp.suː / (AmE), / ˈap.suː / (BrE)
In Babylonian and Sumerian mythology, the father of the gods and consort of Tiamat.

Examples

Apsu in mythology
In Mesopotamian mythology, Apsu is considered the primordial deity of fresh water.
Scholars often analyze the role of Apsu in early creation narratives.
The myths describe Apsu as the consort of Tiamat and the father of the younger gods.
