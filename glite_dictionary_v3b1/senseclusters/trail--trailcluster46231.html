trail

verb
drag or let hang loosely behind
3.5
Pronunciation: / treɪl / (AmE), / treɪl / (BrE)
to drag or let something hang loosely so that it touches or sweeps the ground behind

Examples

To trail a leash
He watched his dog trail its leash through the mud.
Leaves trailed from the bottom of her basket as she carried it home.
The bride let her wedding dress trail behind her as she walked down the aisle.
The bride's veil trailed along the ground as she walked down the aisle.
The little girl's scarf trailed behind her in the snow.
Vines trailed over the edge of the old stone wall.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drag - to pull something along the ground
