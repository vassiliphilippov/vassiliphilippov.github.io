Boehm flute

noun
modern concert flute type
Pronunciation: / boʊm fluːt / (AmE), / bəʊm fluːt / (BrE)
A modern Western concert flute with a key system developed by Theobald Boehm, typically made of metal and played by blowing across a hole on the side.

Examples

The Boehm flute design
He compared the Boehm flute with earlier wooden flutes to discuss advances in instrument design.
Modern orchestras primarily use the Boehm flute because of its range and tonal quality.
The soloist performed beautifully on her Boehm flute during the evening recital.

Related Terms

  • synonym: concert flute - a Western orchestral flute
  • synonym: transverse flute - a flute held horizontally and played by blowing across the mouthpiece
