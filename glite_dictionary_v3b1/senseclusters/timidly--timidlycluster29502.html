timidly

adverb
in a shy or fearful manner
Pronunciation: / ˈtɪmɪdli / (AmE), / ˈtɪmɪdli / (BrE)
In a shy or lacking-in-confidence manner; showing nervousness or fear.

Examples

To timidly knock
He timidly knocked on the office door, nervous about his job interview.
She spoke timidly at the meeting, barely raising her voice above a whisper.
The kitten approached the new toy timidly, unsure if it was safe to play with.

Related Terms

  • antonym: boldly - in a confident and courageous manner
  • synonym: shyly - in a shy or reserved manner
