abridgeable

adjective
can be shortened
7.1
Pronunciation: / əˈbrɪdʒəbəl / (AmE), / əˈbrɪdʒəbəl / (BrE)
able to be shortened or condensed without losing essential meaning

Examples

Abridgeable novel
The novel is abridgeable, so a shorter version can be published for younger readers.
Legal documents are rarely abridgeable because every detail is important.
The professor explained that the lecture notes were abridgeable into a concise summary.

Related Terms

  • synonym: condensable - able to be made shorter or more concise
  • antonym: unabridgeable - not able to be shortened without losing meaning
