family Liparididae

noun
a family of marine snailfishes
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfæməli lɪˌpærɪˈdaɪdi / (AmE), / ˈfamɪli lɪˌparɪˈdʌɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of marine fishes known as snailfishes, typically found in cold waters and characterized by their soft, scaleless bodies.

Examples

Family Liparididae fishes
Members of the family Liparididae are often called snailfishes due to their rounded bodies.
Scientists discovered a new member of the family Liparididae in the Pacific trenches.
The family Liparididae includes many species adapted to deep and cold ocean waters.
