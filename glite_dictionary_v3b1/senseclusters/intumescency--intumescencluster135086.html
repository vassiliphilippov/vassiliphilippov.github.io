intumescency

noun
expansion of substances when heated
6.1 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.tjuˈmɛs.ən.si / (AmE), / ˌɪn.tjʊˈmɛs.ən.si / (BrE)
The increase in volume of certain substances when they are heated, often accompanied by the release of water.

Examples

Intumescency of materials
New materials with improved intumescency are being tested in construction for enhanced safety.
The effect of intumescency in certain clays can cause cracking in ceramic tiles during firing.
The fireproof coating relies on intumescency to swell and protect the steel structure beneath.

Related Terms
