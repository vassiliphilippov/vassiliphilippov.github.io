pollock

noun
lean white flesh of North Atlantic fish
9.9 4.2
Pronunciation: /ˈpɑː.lək/ (AmE), /ˈpɒ.lək/ (BrE)
The lean white flesh of a North Atlantic fish, eaten as food and similar to cod.

Examples

Pollock fillet
Frozen pollock is often used to make imitation crab meat.
Many fish and chip shops use pollock instead of cod for their fried dishes.
The fillets of pollock are flaky and mild in flavor, making them popular in sandwiches.
