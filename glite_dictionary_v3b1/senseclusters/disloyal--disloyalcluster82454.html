disloyal

adjective
not loyal; unfaithful or untrue
1.0
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈlɔɪəl /; / dɪsˈlɔɪ.əl / (AmE), / dɪsˈlɔɪəl /; / dɪsˈlɔɪ.əl / (BrE)
not loyal; not supporting or being faithful to a person, group, principle, or country that you should support

Examples

Disloyal behavior
Her disloyal behavior ended many of her long-standing friendships.
Some considered him disloyal for criticizing his team in public.
The company dismissed several disloyal employees who leaked information.
His disloyal actions during the war shocked everyone in the village.
She was accused of being disloyal because she criticized her country's policies abroad.
The government punished those who were seen as disloyal to the nation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: faithful - remaining true to someone or something
  • antonym: loyal - faithful to someone or something
  • antonym: patriotic - having or expressing devotion to one's country
  • synonym: unpatriotic - not having or showing love for one's country
