fan-out

noun
number of driven outputs
Pronunciation: / ˈfæn aʊt / (AmE), / ˈfæn aʊt / (BrE)
the number of outputs that a single input or device can drive in an electronic circuit

Examples

Circuit fan-out
The fan-out of this logic gate determines how many other gates it can connect to.
Engineers must consider fan-out when designing complex digital circuits.
A low fan-out can limit the performance of an integrated circuit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: load - the amount of electrical demand placed on a circuit
