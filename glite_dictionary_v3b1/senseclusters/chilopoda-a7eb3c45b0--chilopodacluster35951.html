Chilopoda

proper noun
a class of arthropods: centipedes
Pronunciation: / kaɪˈlɑpədə / (AmE), / kɪˈlɒpədə / (BrE)
a class of arthropods comprising the centipedes, characterized by bodies with many segments, each bearing a single pair of legs

Examples

Chilopoda includes centipedes
Chilopoda includes all true centipedes commonly found in soil and leaf litter.
Biology students studied the anatomy of the Chilopoda during their arthropod classification unit.
Unlike millipedes, members of Chilopoda have one pair of legs per body segment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: centipede - a predatory arthropod with many body segments and one pair of legs per segment
