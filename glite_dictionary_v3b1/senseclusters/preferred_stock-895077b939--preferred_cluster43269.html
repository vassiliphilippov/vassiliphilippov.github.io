preferred stock

noun
stock with priority dividends, no voting rights
3.6 4.5
Pronunciation: /prə.ˈfɜːrd stɑːk/ /; / /prɪ.ˈfɜːrd stɑːk/ (AmE), /prə.ˈfɜːd stɒk/ /; / /prɪ.ˈfɜːd stɒk/ (BrE)
A type of stock that gives its holders priority in the payment of dividends but usually does not provide voting rights.

Examples

Holders of preferred stock
Holders of preferred stock are paid dividends before common shareholders.
Many investors choose preferred stock for its reliable dividend payments.
The company issued new preferred stock to raise capital.

Related Terms

  • antonym: common stock - ordinary shares that usually include voting rights and lower dividend priority
