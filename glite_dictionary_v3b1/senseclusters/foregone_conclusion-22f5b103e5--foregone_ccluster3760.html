foregone conclusion

noun
an inevitable or certain outcome
Pronunciation: / fɔrˌɡɔn kənˈkluʒən /; / fɔrˈɡɔn kənˈkluʒən / (AmE), / fɔːˌɡɒn kənˈkluːʒən /; / fɔːˈɡɒn kənˈkluːʒən / (BrE)
a result or outcome that is certain to happen and cannot be avoided

Examples

A foregone conclusion
It is not a foregone conclusion that the economy will improve this year.
The result of the match was a foregone conclusion after the star player joined the team.
With all the evidence against him, his conviction seemed like a foregone conclusion.
Many considered the election result to be a foregone conclusion due to the candidate's popularity.
The outcome of the match was a foregone conclusion after the first half.
With such a strong lead, their victory became a foregone conclusion long before the final results.

Related Terms

  • synonym: certainty - something that is definitely true or bound to happen
  • antonym: possibility - something that may happen but is not certain
