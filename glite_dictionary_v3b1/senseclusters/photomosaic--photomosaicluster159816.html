photomosaic

noun
composite picture from multiple photographs
5.1 6.4
Pronunciation: /foʊ.tə.moʊ.ˈzeɪ.ɪk/ /; / /foʊ.toʊ.moʊ.ˈzeɪ.ɪk/ (AmE), /fəʊ.tə.məʊ.ˈzeɪ.ɪk/ /; / /fəʊ.toʊ.məʊ.ˈzeɪ.ɪk/ (BrE)
a large image made by arranging many smaller photographs so that they form a single picture

Examples

A photomosaic image
A photomosaic assembled from satellite pictures helps researchers monitor changes in the coastline.
Scientists created a photomosaic of the rainforest using hundreds of drone images taken over several days.
The photomosaic revealed the entire landscape in remarkable detail, making it easier to plan the route.
The graduation gift was a framed photomosaic of the class, formed from thousands of tiny student photos.
For the gallery show, she printed a large photomosaic that resolved into a city skyline when viewed from afar.
They used a software tool to assemble the team portrait as a colourful photomosaic for the club's website.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mosaic - an image or pattern produced by arranging many small pieces together
  • synonym: photomontage - a composite picture created by combining and layering multiple photographs
