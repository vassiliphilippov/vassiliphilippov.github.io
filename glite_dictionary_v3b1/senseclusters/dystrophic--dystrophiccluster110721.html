dystrophic

adjective
acidic, nutrient-poor
9.6
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈtrɑːfɪk / (AmE), / dɪsˈtrɒfɪk / (BrE)
describing an environment, especially a lake or soil, that is acidic, rich in organic matter, and low in nutrients and productivity

Examples

Dystrophic lake water
The researchers found that the dystrophic lake supported only a few hardy species of plants and animals.
Because the soil was dystrophic, farmers struggled to grow crops in the region.
Scientists measured the water's acidity to confirm it was a dystrophic environment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: eutrophic - rich in nutrients and supporting abundant plant life
  • synonym: oligotrophic - having low nutrient content and low productivity
