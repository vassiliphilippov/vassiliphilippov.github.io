vernal equinox

noun
time in spring when day and night are equal
7.3 0.11/m
Pronunciation: / Ààv…úrn…ôl ÀàiÀêkw…ôn…ëks /; / Ààv…úrn…ôl Ààikw…ôÀån…ëks / (AmE), / Ààv…úÀên…ôl ÀàiÀêkw…™n…íks / (BrE)
the time in spring (around March 21 in the Northern Hemisphere) when day and night are of equal length, marking the start of spring

Examples

The vernal equinox date
Many cultures hold festivals to celebrate the vernal equinox each year.

The vernal equinox marks the beginning of spring in the northern hemisphere.

On the vernal equinox, the Sun rises exactly in the east and sets exactly in the west.
The vernal equinox marks the point when day and night are nearly equal in length.

Synonyms

  • spring equinox
    4.3 0.08/m
    spring equinox is the everyday name for the equinox that marks spring in the north, while vernal equinox sounds more formal.
  • March equinox
    5.3 0.01/m
    March equinox falls around March 20 or 21 and applies to both hemispheres, while vernal equinox refers specifically to spring in the Northern Hemisphere.
