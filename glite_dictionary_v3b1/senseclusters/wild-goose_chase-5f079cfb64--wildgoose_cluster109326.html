wild-goose chase

noun
hopeless or pointless pursuit
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪld ˌɡus ˌtʃeɪs / (AmE), / ˈwaɪld ˌɡuːs ˌtʃeɪs / (BrE)
a search or pursuit that is hopeless, pointless, or impossible to achieve

Examples

A wild-goose chase
After hours tracking bad directions, we realized the entire trip was a wild-goose chase.
Chasing the old urban legend turned out to be nothing but a wild-goose chase.
He sent her on a wild-goose chase looking for documents that didn't exist.
After hours of searching, we realized we had been sent on a wild-goose chase.
Looking for his keys at the crowded beach turned out to be a wild-goose chase.
The detective refused to waste time on what he believed was just a wild-goose chase.
