run

verb
unravel or develop a split in fabric
Pronunciation: / rʌn / (AmE), / rʌn / (BrE)
to develop a long, narrow split or unravel in fabric, especially where threads come undone

Examples

Fabric runs
Be careful; if you snag your sleeve, the fabric could run easily.
Her favorite tights started to run after she caught them on a chair.
If your pantyhose run, you might need to replace them before the party.
Her patience wore thin when her new stockings ran after just one use.
If you catch the snag early, the sweater won't run all the way.
My tights ran just before the interview, leaving a ladder down the side.
