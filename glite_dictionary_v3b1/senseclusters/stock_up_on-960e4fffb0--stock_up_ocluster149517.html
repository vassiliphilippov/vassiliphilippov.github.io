stock up on

verb
buy or collect a large quantity in advance
2.5 1.61/m
Pronunciation: / stÉ‘k ÊŒp É‘n / (AmE), / stÉ’k ÊŒp É’n / (BrE)
to buy or collect a large quantity of something for future use

Examples

To stock up on food
Many people stocked up on masks during the pandemic.
She always stocks up on snacks for the holidays.
We need to stock up on groceries before the storm hits.

Synonyms

  • stockpile
    4.6 1.78/m
    stockpile means keeping a large supply for emergencies or future needs, while stock up on means buying many items for future use.
  • load up on
    4.5 0.69/m
    load up on is informal and can mean buying or eating a lot, while stock up on focuses on buying for future use.
