direct

verb
cause to go somewhere
1.0 1.5
Pronunciation: /də.ˈrekt/ /; / /dɪ.ˈrekt/ /; / /daɪ.ˈrekt/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈrekt/ /; / /də.ˈrekt/ /; / /daɪ.ˈrekt/ (BrE)
to give instructions, orders, or guidance to someone; to show or tell someone the way

Examples

To direct someone
During the tour, our guide directed us toward the historic castle.
The officer directed traffic away from the accident site to ensure everyone's safety.
The teacher directed the group to the science lab for the activity.
Can you direct me to the conference room, please?
He directed us to the nearest subway station when we asked for help.
The security guard directed the visitors towards the main entrance.
During the emergency, the officer directed everyone to evacuate the building immediately.
He directs his employees to start work at eight o'clock each day.
The teacher directed the students to form a single line.

Related Terms

  • synonym: escort - to go with someone as a companion or guide
  • synonym: guide - to show the way to someone; lead or direct
  • synonym: instruct - to tell someone what to do, usually formally or officially
  • synonym: lead - to go with or guide a person to a place
  • synonym: order - to command or instruct with authority
  • antonym: request - to ask politely or formally
  • synonym: send - to cause to go or be taken to a particular destination
