Examples

To direct someone

Synonyms

  • instruct
    2.8 14/m
    instruct teaches someone step by step how to do a task, while direct can mean giving orders or showing the way.
  • guide
    1.0 22/m
    guide walks with or shows someone a route or idea in a friendly way, while direct can also mean to give orders.
  • order
    1.3 30/m
    order gives a clear, often strict command from a person in charge, while direct includes both orders and gentler guidance.
  • tell
    1.0 602/m
    tell says something to someone, often spoken and less formal, while direct can be formal or used to show the way.
  • lead
    1.0 86/m
    lead takes or directs people by going with them or being in front, while direct often focuses on giving instructions.
  • conduct
    4.3 0.67/m
    conduct leads people carefully in formal situations like a tour or ceremony, while direct also means to give orders or directions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: escort - to go with someone as a companion or guide
  • synonym: guide - to show the way to someone; lead or direct
  • synonym: instruct - to tell someone what to do, usually formally or officially
  • synonym: lead - to go with or guide a person to a place
  • synonym: order - to command or instruct with authority
  • antonym: request - to ask politely or formally
  • synonym: send - to cause to go or be taken to a particular destination
