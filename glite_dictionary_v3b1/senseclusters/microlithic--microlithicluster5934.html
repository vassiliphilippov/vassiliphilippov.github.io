microlithic

adjective
small stone tools
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪkrəˈlɪθɪk / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəˈlɪθɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving very small stone tools or implements, especially from prehistoric times

Examples

Microlithic tools
Archaeologists discovered microlithic artifacts at the ancient settlement site.
The microlithic industry marked a significant technological advance in prehistoric toolmaking.
Researchers studied the microlithic blades to learn about early human hunting techniques.

Related Terms

  • antonym: megalithic - relating to large stone monuments or structures
  • synonym: microlith - a very small stone tool, often used in prehistoric times
