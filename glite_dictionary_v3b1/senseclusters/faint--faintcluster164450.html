faint

verb
lose consciousness briefly
3.2 4/m
Pronunciation: / feÉªnt / (AmE), / feÉªnt / (BrE)
to lose consciousness for a short time, typically because of a temporary insufficient supply of blood to the brain

Examples

To faint suddenly
He fainted unexpectedly and was helped by bystanders.
Several people began to faint from the heat during the crowded ceremony.
She thought she might faint when she saw the sight of blood.

Video Examples

Synonyms

  • pass out
    3.7 4/m
    pass out is becoming unconscious suddenly, often said in casual speech, while faint describes losing consciousness from low blood flow.
  • swoon
    7.7 2/m
    swoon is fainting from strong feeling or weakness and appears poetic, while faint usually names a medical loss of consciousness.
  • black out
    5.4 1/m
    black out is losing consciousness suddenly, often after injury or shock and sometimes meaning memory loss, while faint is usually from low blood flow.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collapse - to fall down or give way suddenly, often due to illness or weakness
  • antonym: recover - to regain consciousness or strength after fainting
