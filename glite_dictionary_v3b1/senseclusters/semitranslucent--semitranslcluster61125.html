semitranslucent

adjective
partly see-through
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmitrænzˈluːsənt / (AmE), / ˌsɛmitrænzˈluːsənt / (BrE)
allowing some light to pass through but not completely clear or transparent

Examples

A semitranslucent paper
The artist used semitranslucent paper to create a soft, glowing effect in her collage.
We installed semitranslucent panels to let in natural light while maintaining privacy.
The jellyfish appeared semitranslucent as it floated gently in the aquarium.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: opaque - not letting any light pass through
  • degree higher: translucent - allowing light to pass through but not detailed shapes
