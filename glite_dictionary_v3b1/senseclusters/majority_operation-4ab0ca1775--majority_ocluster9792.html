majority operation

noun
operation outputting 1 if most inputs are 1
6.7 6.9
Pronunciation: /mə.ˈdʒɔːr.ɪ.tiː ɑːp.ər.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /mə.ˈdʒɑːr.ɪ.tiː ɑːp.ər.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /mə.ˈdʒɒ.rɪ.tiː ɒp.ə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
A threshold operation in which the output is 1 if and only if more than half the inputs are 1; otherwise, the output is 0.

Examples

A majority operation output
In digital circuits, a majority operation helps to reduce the effect of faulty inputs.
The algorithm uses a majority operation to decide the next state based on three input bits.
Voting systems can be modeled by a majority operation where the final result reflects the majority of votes.
