flimsy

adjective
lacking strength or solidity
3.8 2.23/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆflÉªmzi / (AmE), / ËˆflÉªmzi / (BrE)
lacking solidity, strength, or substance; easily broken or damaged

Examples

A flimsy dress
She wore a flimsy dress on a chilly evening and regretted it.
The box was made of flimsy cardboard that could easily tear.
The evidence against him was flimsy and unconvincing.

Synonyms

  • fragile
    3.4 5.54/m
    fragile is easily broken and thin in build, while flimsy often suggests poor material or weak construction that falls apart quickly.
  • breakable
    1.3 0.34/m
    breakable means plainly able to be broken, while flimsy implies light weight or poor construction that fails under small force.
  • insubstantial
    6.8 1.03/m
    insubstantial feels thin or weak and can mean unconvincing, while flimsy usually describes physical items that break or tear easily.
  • weak
    1.0 24/m
    weak lacks strength or power and can be general, while flimsy describes things that are fragile or fall apart with little force.
