the calendar system introduced by Julius Caesar in 46 b.c., establishing a 12-month year of 365 days with a leap year of 366 days every four years; also known as the Julian calendar

Examples

The Old Style calendar system
Many historical documents use dates from the Old Style calendar, which can differ from modern dates.
Russia officially switched from the Old Style calendar to the Gregorian calendar in 1918.
When comparing events across countries, scholars often clarify whether they are referring to the Old Style calendar.
