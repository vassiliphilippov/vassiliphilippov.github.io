stratification

noun
arrangement in layers or strata
6.6 5.8
Pronunciation: /stræt.ɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /stræt.ə.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /stræt.ɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /stræt.ə.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the arrangement or formation of something in layers or strata (such as in rocks or other materials)

Examples

Rock stratification
Geologists study the stratification of rock formations to understand Earth's history.

Water in the lake shows clear stratification during the summer months.
Social stratification within the city has led to sharply defined class divisions.
Soil stratification occurs over thousands of years, creating distinct layers with different properties.
The geologist studied the stratification of the rock formation to determine its age.
