sidereal month

noun
period of the Moon’s orbit relative to the stars
Pronunciation: / saɪˈdɪri.əl mʌnθ / (AmE), / saɪˈdɪəri.əl mʌnθ / (BrE)
The period of about 27.322 days it takes the Moon to return to the same position relative to the fixed stars.

Examples

A sidereal month period
A sidereal month lasts about 27.3 days, marking the Moon’s position relative to distant stars.
Astronomers use the sidereal month to measure the Moon’s true orbit around Earth.
The sidereal month is slightly shorter than the synodic month.

Related Terms

  • antonym: synodic month - the period of the Moon's phases; time from new moon to new moon
