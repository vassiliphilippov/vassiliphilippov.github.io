bear market

noun
period of falling asset prices in finance
8.0 5.3
Pronunciation: / bɛr ˈmɑrkɪt / (AmE), / beə ˈmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
A period in which the prices of securities or other assets fall over a sustained period, usually by 20% or more from recent highs, and widespread pessimism causes the negative sentiment to be self-sustaining.

Examples

A bear market period
During a prolonged bear market, many investors become cautious about buying stocks.
Some people see opportunity in a bear market, while others choose to stay out until conditions improve.
The 2008 financial crisis triggered a significant bear market in global equities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bull market - a market characterized by rising prices for securities
