satellite

noun
celestial body orbiting a planet or star
6.0 3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsæt̬.əl.aɪt / (AmE), / ˈsæt.əl.aɪt / (BrE)
A celestial body that orbits a planet or star, especially a natural one such as a moon.

Examples

A moon satellite
Astronomers discovered a new satellite orbiting Saturn last year.
Jupiter has many satellites that can be observed through a telescope.
The Earth's only natural satellite is the Moon.

Related Terms

  • synonym: moon - The natural satellite of the Earth, or by extension, any natural satellite of a planet
