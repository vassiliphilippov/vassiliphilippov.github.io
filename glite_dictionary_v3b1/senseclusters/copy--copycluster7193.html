copy

verb
imitate someone's actions or looks
1.0 7.67/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆkÉ‘pi / (AmE), / ËˆkÉ’pi / (BrE)
to do something in the same way as someone else; to imitate someone's actions, behavior, or appearance

Examples

To copy her style
He tried to copy her style of dress for the party.
Students sometimes copy each other's answers during an exam, which is not allowed.
Young children often copy the way their parents speak and act.
