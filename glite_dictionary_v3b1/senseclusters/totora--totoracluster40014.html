totora

noun
South American reed
9.9 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / to äÀàt…îÀêr…ô / (AmE), / t…ôÀàt…îÀêr…ô / (BrE)
a tall reed native to South America, especially around Lake Titicaca, used for building, crafts, and making boats

Examples

Dried totora reeds
The Uros people build floating islands from dried totora on Lake Titicaca.
Artisans weave baskets and mats using strong totora stems collected from the lakeshore.
Traditional boats made of totora have been used for centuries in the Andes region.
