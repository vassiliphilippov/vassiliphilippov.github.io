oxgall

noun
liquid from ox gallbladder
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɑksˌɡɔl / (AmE), / ˈɒksˌɡɔːl / (BrE)
a liquid obtained from the gallbladder of an ox, used in painting and cleaning to improve the flow of watercolors or remove grease

Examples

A drop of oxgall
The artist added a drop of oxgall to her watercolors to help them spread smoothly.
Traditional cleaning methods sometimes use oxgall to remove stubborn grease stains from fabrics.
He purchased a bottle of oxgall for his painting supplies at the art store.

Related Terms
