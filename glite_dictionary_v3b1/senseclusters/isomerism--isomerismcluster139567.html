isomerism

noun
the property of a substance to exist as isomers
8.0 6.8
Pronunciation: /ˈaɪ.sə.mə.rɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈaɪ.zə.mə.rɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈaɪ.sə.mə.rɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈaɪ.zə.mə.rɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
The existence of two or more compounds with the same molecular formula but different arrangements of atoms in the molecule, resulting in different properties.

Examples

Isomerism in chemistry
Modern chemistry often discusses the importance of isomerism in pharmaceuticals.
Structural isomerism leads to distinct physical and chemical properties in organic molecules.
The study of isomerism helps scientists understand why substances with the same formula can behave differently.
