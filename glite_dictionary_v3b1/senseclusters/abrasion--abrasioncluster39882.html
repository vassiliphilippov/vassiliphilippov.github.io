the process by which rock or other surfaces are gradually worn down by friction, especially from water, wind, or ice

Examples

Glacial abrasion
Glacial abrasion occurs when rocks embedded in ice scrape against the valley floor.
The sharp edges of the rocks were smoothed by abrasion from centuries of river flow.
Wind-driven sand can cause significant abrasion to exposed rock surfaces in the desert.
