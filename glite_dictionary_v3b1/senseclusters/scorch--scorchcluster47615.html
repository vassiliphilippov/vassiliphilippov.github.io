scorch

verb
criticize harshly; denounce severely
5.3 5.8
Pronunciation: /skɔːrtʃ/ (AmE), /skɔːtʃ/ (BrE)
to criticize someone or something very harshly; to subject to severe denunciation or reproach

Examples

To scorch a review
Her review scorched the new novel, calling it uninspired and dull.
Social media users often scorch public figures for their controversial statements.
The editor did not hesitate to scorch the government for its mishandling of the crisis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blister - to criticize harshly
  • synonym: lambaste - to attack or criticize severely
  • antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration
