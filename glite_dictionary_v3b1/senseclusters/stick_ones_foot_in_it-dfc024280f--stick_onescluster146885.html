stick one's foot in it

verb
make an embarrassing mistake
5.3 6.8
Pronunciation: /stɪk wʌnz fʊt ɪn ɪt/ /; / /stɪk wʌns fʊt ɪn ɪt/ (AmE), /stɪk wʌnz fʊt ɪn ɪt/ /; / /stɪk wʌns fʊt ɪn ɪt/ (BrE)
to say or do something embarrassing, tactless, or inappropriate, often by accident

Examples

To stick one's foot in it badly
Every time I try to help, I somehow stick my foot in it and make it worse.
He really stuck his foot in it when he mentioned her ex-boyfriend at the party.
She stuck her foot in it by asking about his job, not knowing he'd just been laid off.
