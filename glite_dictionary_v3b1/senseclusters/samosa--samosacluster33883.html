samosa

noun
fried Indian pastry with filling
Pronunciation: / səˈmoʊsə / (AmE), / səˈməʊsə / (BrE)
A small, triangular pastry of Indian origin filled with vegetables or meat, deep-fried and served hot as a snack or appetizer.

Examples

A samosa snack
At the party, guests enjoyed both vegetarian and meat samosas with chutney.
He ordered a plate of samosa as a starter at the Indian restaurant.
The samosa was crispy on the outside and filled with spicy potatoes inside.
