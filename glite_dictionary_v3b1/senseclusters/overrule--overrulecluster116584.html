overrule

verb
reject by superior authority
5.7 4/m
Pronunciation: / Àåo äv…ôrÀàruÀêl / (AmE), / Àå…ô äv…ôÀàruÀêl / (BrE)
(of a person or body in authority) to reject or set aside a decision, opinion, or objection by exercising superior authority.

Examples

To overrule a decision
Her suggestion was overruled by the committee despite strong support from several members.
The board may overrule the manager if they disagree with his decision.
The judge decided to overrule the objection raised by the defense lawyer.

Synonyms

  • override
    6.7 7/m
    override is to cancel or take priority over a rule or decision in practice, while overrule emphasizes formal authority rejecting a decision.
  • overturn
    5.6 8/m
    overturn means a higher body officially reverses a law or judgment, while overrule focuses on an authority rejecting a decision.
  • reverse
    4.9 8/m
    reverse means to cancel or change a previous legal decision or outcome, while overrule highlights an authority setting aside a decision.
  • quash
    8.1 0.7/m
    quash means to declare a ruling legally void and stop it, while overrule refers to an authority rejecting or setting aside a decision.

Related Terms

  • synonym: override - to use one's authority to reject or cancel a decision
