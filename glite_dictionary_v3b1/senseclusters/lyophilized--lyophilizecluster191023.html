lyophilized

adjective
dried by freeze-drying process
Pronunciation: / laɪˈɑːfəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / laɪˈɒfɪˌlaɪzd /; / laɪˈɒfɪlaɪzd / (BrE)
(of tissue, blood, serum, or other biological substances) dried by freezing in a high vacuum, a process known as freeze-drying.

Examples

Lyophilized blood samples
Scientists analyzed the lyophilized blood samples for trace elements.
The lyophilized vaccine can be stored for several months without refrigeration.
The medication is supplied as a lyophilized powder to be reconstituted before injection.
Scientists use lyophilised blood samples for analysis in many experiments.
The lab technician carefully rehydrated the lyophilised tissue for further testing.
The vaccine must be kept lyophilised until it is ready for use.

Related Terms

  • synonym: freeze-dried - dried by freezing and then reducing surrounding pressure to remove moisture
  • synonym: lyophilized - American spelling: dried by freezing in a high vacuum
