splenic artery

noun
artery supplying blood to the spleen
Pronunciation: / ˈsplɛnɪk ˈɑːr.ti / (AmE), / ˈspliː.nɪk ˈɑː.təri / (BrE)
an artery that branches from the celiac trunk and supplies blood to the spleen

Examples

The splenic artery branch
An aneurysm in the splenic artery can be life-threatening if not detected early.
During the surgery, the surgeon carefully identified the splenic artery to avoid accidental injury.
The splenic artery is a major blood vessel that delivers oxygenated blood to the spleen.
