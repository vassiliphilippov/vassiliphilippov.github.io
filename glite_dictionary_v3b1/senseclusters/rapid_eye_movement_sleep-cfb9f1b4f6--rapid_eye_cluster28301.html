rapid eye movement sleep

noun

A stage of sleep characterized by rapid movement of the eyes, during which most dreaming occurs and brain activity is high.

Examples

Synonyms

  • REM sleep
    4.1 0.60/m
    REM sleep is the common clinical name for the dream-rich sleep stage, while rapid eye movement sleep describes that same stage.
  • REM
    5.2 0.93/m
    REM is the abbreviation used in speech and papers for the stage with vivid dreaming, while rapid eye movement sleep is the full phrase.
  • rapid eye movement
    3.9 0.15/m
    rapid eye movement often names the quick eye motions seen during sleep, while rapid eye movement sleep names the whole dreaming stage.
  • paradoxical sleep
    6.3 0.01/m
    paradoxical sleep is a technical name that highlights brain activity like wakefulness during dreams, while rapid eye movement sleep focuses on eye movements.

  • antonym: non-REM sleep - the other main type of sleep, without rapid eye movement, usually dreamless
