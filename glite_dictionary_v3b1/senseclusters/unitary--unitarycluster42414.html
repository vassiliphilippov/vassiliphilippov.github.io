unitary

adjective
centralized government; not federal
Pronunciation: / ˈjuː.nə.ˌtɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈjuː.nɪ.təri / (BrE)
(of a country or system) characterized by a single central government in which most or all powers are held by the central authority, rather than divided among regional governments.

Examples

Unitary state
A unitary system allows policies to be applied uniformly across the entire country.
France is governed as a unitary state, with power concentrated in the national government.
Unlike the United States, which is federal, the United Kingdom has traditionally been described as unitary.

Related Terms

  • antonym: federal - relating to a system in which power is shared between a central authority and constituent regions or states
