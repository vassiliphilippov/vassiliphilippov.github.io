body forth

verb
to give physical or concrete form
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑdi ˌfɔrθ / (AmE), / ˈbɒdi ˌfɔːθ / (BrE)
to give physical or concrete form to something abstract; to represent or express in bodily or tangible form

Examples

To body forth ideas
In his poetry, abstract emotions are bodied forth in powerful imagery.
The characters in her novel body forth the hopes and fears of a whole generation.
The myth helps to body forth the values of ancient society.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embody - to give visible or tangible form to an idea, quality, or feeling
  • synonym: incarnate - to make something manifest or physical, often used in a religious or metaphorical sense
