overexcitement

noun
excessive excitement
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərɪkˈsaɪtmənt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərɪkˈsaɪtmənt / (BrE)
a state of being too excited or overly stimulated, sometimes causing restlessness or problems

Examples

Classroom overexcitement
The overexcitement in the classroom made it difficult for the teacher to begin the lesson.
His overexcitement about the trip kept him awake all night.
Parents tried to calm the children's overexcitement before the birthday party started.
The neurologist warned that overexcitement of the brain's neurons could result in seizures.
Researchers studied how overexcitement in electrical circuits might cause malfunctions in medical devices.
Certain medications can sometimes lead to overexcitement in the nervous system, causing unwanted side effects.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calmness - a state of being relaxed and not excited
  • degree lower: excitement - a feeling of lively and cheerful joy
  • synonym: hyperactivity - a state of being unusually or excessively active
