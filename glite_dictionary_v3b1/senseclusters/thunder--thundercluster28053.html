thunder

noun
booming noise caused by lightning
Pronunciation: / ˈθʌn.dɚ /; / ˈθʌndɚ / (AmE), / ˈθʌn.də /; / ˈθʌndə / (BrE)
a loud, deep noise in the sky during a storm, caused by lightning

Examples

Loud thunder in storms
After the flash of lightning, we counted the seconds until we heard thunder.
The thunder was so loud that it frightened the dog.
The sudden thunder shook the house and made the windows rattle.
A low thunder rolled across the hills as the storm approached.
The thunder shook the windows during last night's storm.
We heard the distant thunder long before the rain started.
