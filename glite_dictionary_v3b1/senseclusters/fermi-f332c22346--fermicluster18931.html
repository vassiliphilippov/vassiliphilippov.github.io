Fermi

proper noun
Italian-American nuclear physicist (1901–1954)
Pronunciation: / ˈfɜr.mi / (AmE), / ˈfɜː.mi / (BrE)
Enrico Fermi (1901–1954), Italian-American nuclear physicist known for his work on artificial radioactivity and for leading the creation of the first controlled nuclear reaction.

Examples

Fermi the physicist
Fermi played a leading role in the development of the first nuclear reactor in Chicago.
Fermi's contributions to physics earned him a Nobel Prize in 1938.
Many scientific concepts, such as the 'Fermi paradox,' are named after Fermi.
