antitypical

adjective
relating to an antitype (symbolic counterpart)
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.tiˈtɪp.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈtɪp.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of an antitype, especially as a symbol that fulfills or corresponds to an earlier type or symbol

Examples

Antitypical fulfillment
In literature, certain characters may serve an antitypical function, completing symbolic patterns established earlier.
The antitypical fulfillment of the prophecy was believed to occur centuries after the original events.
The Old Testament rituals are seen as types, while Christ's sacrifice is considered antitypical in Christian theology.

Related Terms

  • antonym: typical - relating to or serving as a type; representative
