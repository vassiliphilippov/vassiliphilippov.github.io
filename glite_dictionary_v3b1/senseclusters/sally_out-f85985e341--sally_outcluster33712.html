sally out

verb
rush out suddenly, often to attack
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsæli aʊt / (AmE), / ˈsæli aʊt / (BrE)
to leave a place suddenly and energetically, often to attack, surprise, or go on a short excursion

Examples

To sally out suddenly
Just as the thief passed by, the police sallied out from their hiding spot and arrested him.
She sallied out of her room, determined to confront whoever was making the noise.
The soldiers sallied out from behind the gates to launch a surprise attack on the enemy.
She sallied out into the rain, determined not to let the weather spoil her day.
The soldiers decided to sally out at dawn to surprise the enemy.
When the alarm rang, they sallied out of the office, eager for their lunch break.
