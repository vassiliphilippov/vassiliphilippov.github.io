latewood

noun
dense wood in growth ring
8.0
Pronunciation: /ˈleɪt.wʊd/ (AmE), /ˈleɪt.wʊd/ (BrE)
the denser, darker wood produced later in a tree's annual growth ring, usually in late summer or autumn

Examples

The latewood layer
The latewood in the tree ring appears much darker than the earlywood.
Scientists study latewood to understand how climate affects tree growth each year.
The density of latewood makes it important for determining the strength of timber.

Related Terms

  • antonym: earlywood - the lighter, less dense wood formed early in a tree's annual growth ring
