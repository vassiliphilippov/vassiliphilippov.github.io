cinnabar moth

noun
a red-and-black European moth species
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪn.ə.bɑr mɔθ / (AmE), / ˈsɪn.ə.bɑː mɒθ / (BrE)
a large European moth with striking red and black wings, whose larvae feed on ragwort; introduced to other regions to control ragwort growth

Examples

The cinnabar moth larvae
Farmers welcomed the introduction of the cinnabar moth to help control invasive ragwort.
The distinctive markings of the cinnabar moth make it easy to spot in meadows and grasslands.
We noticed dozens of cinnabar moths fluttering above the wildflowers on our hike.
