osteonecrosis

noun
bone tissue death
9.7
Pronunciation: /ɑːs.tiː.oʊ.nə.ˈkroʊ.sɪs/ /; / /ɑːs.tiː.oʊ.nə.ˈkroʊ.səs/ (AmE), /ɒs.tiː.əʊ.nə.ˈkrəʊ.sɪs/ /; / /ɒs.tiː.əʊ.nə.ˈkrəʊ.səs/ (BrE)
a medical condition in which bone tissue dies because it does not receive enough blood supply

Examples

Osteonecrosis of bone
Long-term use of certain medications can increase the risk of osteonecrosis in patients.
The doctor explained that osteonecrosis may require surgery if the bone damage is severe.
Early diagnosis of osteonecrosis can help prevent further joint problems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: avascular necrosis - death of bone tissue due to lack of blood supply
  • antonym: osteomyelitis - infection and inflammation of bone or bone marrow
