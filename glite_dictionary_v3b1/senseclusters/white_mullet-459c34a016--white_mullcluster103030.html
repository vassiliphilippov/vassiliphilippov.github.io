white mullet

noun
a silvery coastal fish species
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪt ˈmʌlɪt / (AmE), / ˈwaɪt ˈmʌlɪt / (BrE)
a silvery fish (Mugil curema) found along the Atlantic and Pacific coasts, known for its slender body and commonly used as food or bait

Examples

Fresh white mullet
Fishermen often catch white mullet in shallow waters along the coastline.
Juvenile white mullet are a common sight in estuaries during the summer months.
The chef prepared grilled white mullet with lemon and herbs for dinner.
