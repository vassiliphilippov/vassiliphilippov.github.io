steelhead

noun
migratory rainbow trout
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈstiːlˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈstiːlˌhɛd / (BrE)
a large, silvery, migratory type of rainbow trout found in North American rivers that travels between freshwater and the ocean

Examples

A steelhead trout
Many anglers travel to the Pacific Northwest to fish for steelhead in the spring.
The steelhead is prized for its strength and fighting ability when caught on a line.
During migration, steelhead swim upstream to their spawning grounds in freshwater rivers.
Many anglers hope to catch a large steelhead during the spring migration.
The river is famous for its annual run of steelhead returning to spawn.
Scientists study the life cycle of the steelhead to understand migratory fish behavior.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rainbow trout - a species of trout with a colorful, iridescent stripe along its side
  • synonym: salmon - a large fish that migrates from the sea to rivers to spawn
