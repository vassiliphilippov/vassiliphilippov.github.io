tanbark

noun
tree bark used in tanning or as ground cover
9.2 8.1
Pronunciation: /ˈtæn.bɑːrk/ (AmE), /ˈtæn.bɑːk/ (BrE)
The bark of certain trees, rich in tannin, which is cut and used in the process of tanning leather; also, bark that has been used for tanning and is sometimes spread as a ground covering.

Examples

Tanbark for tanning
After processing, the spent tanbark was spread over garden paths.
During the 19th century, tanbark was commonly used in the tanning of animal hides.
The playground was covered in tanbark to provide a soft surface for the children.
