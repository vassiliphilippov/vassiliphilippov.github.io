Elymus canadensis

noun
species of North American wild rye grass
Pronunciation: / ˈɛlɪməs kəˈneɪdənəs / (AmE), / ˈɛlɪməs kəˈneɪdɛnɪs / (BrE)
A species of wild rye native to North America, commonly known as Canada wild rye, with tall bristly spikes often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Examples

Elymus canadensis plant
Elymus canadensis is often planted in restoration projects to stabilize soil and support native wildlife.
Farmers sometimes use Elymus canadensis as a cover crop to prevent soil erosion in the off-season.
The tall, graceful spikes of Elymus canadensis can be seen swaying in many Midwestern prairies.
