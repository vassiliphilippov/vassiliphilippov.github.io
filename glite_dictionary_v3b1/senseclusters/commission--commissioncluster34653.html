commission

verb
order or authorize something
5.5 3.6
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈmɪʃ.ən/ (AmE), /kə.ˈmɪʃ.ən/ (BrE)
to formally order, authorize, or assign someone to do a task or produce something; to put something (such as a ship) into active service

Examples

To commission a painting
The artist was commissioned to paint a portrait of the queen.
The city council commissioned a sculpture for the new park.
We decided to commission a new study to better understand the results.
After years of construction, the vessel was finally commissioned into active duty.
The navy will commission its newest submarine next spring.
They are commissioning two new ships to strengthen the fleet.
The artist was commissioned to create a sculpture for the new public park.
The government commissions reports from independent experts before making major decisions.
They commissioned a team of engineers to investigate the structural problem.

Related Terms

  • synonym: appoint - to assign a job or role to someone
  • antonym: cancel - decide or announce that an order will not be fulfilled
  • antonym: decommission - to withdraw from active service, especially a ship
  • synonym: order - request something to be made, supplied, or delivered
