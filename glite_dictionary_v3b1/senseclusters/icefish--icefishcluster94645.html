icefish

noun
Antarctic fish species
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪsˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈaɪsˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a fish found in cold southern waters, especially Antarctica, often noted for its transparent blood and adaptation to freezing temperatures

Examples

Antarctic icefish
The icefish has unique adaptations that allow it to survive in icy Antarctic waters.
Researchers discovered that the blood of the icefish lacks hemoglobin, making it almost transparent.
Many scientists travel to study the icefish and its remarkable ability to live in freezing temperatures.
