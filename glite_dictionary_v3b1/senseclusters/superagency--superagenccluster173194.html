superagency

noun
powerful regulatory body
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːpərˌeɪdʒənsi / (AmE), / ˈsuːpərəɪdʒənsi /; / ˈsuːpərˌeɪdʒənsi / (BrE)
a very large or powerful organization, especially in government, that oversees or coordinates the work of several smaller agencies

Examples

Government superagency
The government established a superagency to oversee all environmental regulations nationwide.
Critics argue that a superagency could become too powerful and lack proper oversight.
A new superagency was proposed to coordinate responses to national emergencies across different sectors.
The government created a superagency to manage disaster response across multiple departments.
A new superagency was established to oversee environmental protection efforts nationwide.
The superagency coordinates the activities of several independent regulatory bodies.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: agency - a government department or organization responsible for a specific function
  • synonym: regulator - an organization that supervises a particular industry or activity
  • synonym: umbrella organization - an organization that supervises or represents several smaller groups
