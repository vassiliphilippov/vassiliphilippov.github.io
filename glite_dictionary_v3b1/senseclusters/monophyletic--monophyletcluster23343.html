monophyletic

adjective
from one ancestor
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑnəfaɪˈlɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəfaɪˈlɛtɪk / (BrE)
describing a group of organisms that all descend from a single common ancestor

Examples

A monophyletic group
Scientists agree that mammals form a monophyletic group because they share a single common ancestor.
The classification was revised to ensure that each taxon is truly monophyletic.
A monophyletic group includes all descendants of a particular evolutionary ancestor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: paraphyletic - descended from a common ancestor but not including all descendants
  • antonym: polyphyletic - derived from more than one ancestral group
