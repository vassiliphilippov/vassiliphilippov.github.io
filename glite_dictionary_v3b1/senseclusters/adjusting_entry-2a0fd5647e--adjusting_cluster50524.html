adjusting entry

noun
an end-of-period accounting entry
5.5 6.0
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈdʒʌs.tɪŋ ˈen.triː/ /; / /ə.ˈdʒʌs.tɪŋ ˈen.trɪ/ (AmE), /ə.ˈdʒʌs.tɪŋ ˈen.trɪ/ /; / /ə.ˈdʒʌs.tɪŋ ˈen.triː/ (BrE)
an accounting entry made at the end of an accounting period to allocate income or expenses to the correct period

Examples

An adjusting entry in accounting
An adjusting entry is necessary to align the reported income with actual business activity.
At the end of the fiscal year, the accountant prepared an adjusting entry to record accrued expenses.
Each adjusting entry ensures the financial statements reflect accurate values for the period.
