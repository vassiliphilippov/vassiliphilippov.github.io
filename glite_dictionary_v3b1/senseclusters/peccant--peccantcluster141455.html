peccant

adjective
liable to sin; morally faulty
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛkənt / (AmE), / ˈpɛkənt / (BrE)
Morally faulty or liable to commit sin; having a tendency to do wrong.

Examples

Peccant thoughts
He confessed his peccant thoughts with sincere remorse.
In medieval literature, the peccant nature of humankind is a frequent theme.
The sermon warned against following the peccant desires of the flesh.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sinful - full of sin; wicked
  • antonym: virtuous - having or showing high moral standards
