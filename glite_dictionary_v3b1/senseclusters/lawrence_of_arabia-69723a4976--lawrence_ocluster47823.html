Lawrence of Arabia

proper noun
British soldier and writer, leader in Arab revolt (1888–1935)
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈlɔːrəns əv əˈreɪbiə / (AmE), / ˈlɒrəns əv əˈreɪbɪə / (BrE)
the nickname of T. E. Lawrence (1888–1935), a British soldier, archaeologist, and writer known for organizing the Arab revolt against the Ottoman Turks during World War I, and for his influential memoir 'Seven Pillars of Wisdom'.

Examples

Lawrence of Arabia memoir
Lawrence of Arabia played a crucial role in the Arab revolt against the Ottoman Empire.
His book, 'Seven Pillars of Wisdom', chronicles the adventures of Lawrence of Arabia during World War I.
Many people know Lawrence of Arabia from the famous 1962 film about his life.
