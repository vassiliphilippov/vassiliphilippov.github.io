slick

adjective
smooth and slippery (often due to ice or grease)
2.6 3.2
Pronunciation: / slɪk / (AmE), / slɪk / (BrE)
smooth, shiny, or slippery in appearance or texture

Examples

A slick surface
After the spill, the kitchen floor was slick with oil, making it hard to walk safely.
Be careful—the steps are slick with ice and very dangerous.
The car skidded because the road was slick from the rain.
After washing the car, its finish was so slick that you could see your reflection.
She wore a slick black dress that shimmered under the party lights.
The dolphin's slick skin glistened in the sunlight as it leapt through the waves.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rough - having an uneven or coarse surface
  • synonym: satiny - having a smooth, glossy surface like satin
  • synonym: silky - smooth and soft like silk
  • synonym: slippery - difficult to hold or walk on because it is smooth, wet, or oily
