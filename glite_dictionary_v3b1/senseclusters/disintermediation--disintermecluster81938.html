disintermediation

noun
removal of intermediaries
9.8
Pronunciation: /dɪ.sɪn.tər.miː.di.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /dɪ.sɪn.tər.mɪd.i.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /dɪ.sɪn.tə.miː.di.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /dɪ.sɪn.tə.mɪd.i.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of removing intermediaries or middlemen from a process, transaction, or supply chain, so that two parties can interact directly

Examples

Disintermediation in business
Online marketplaces have encouraged disintermediation by connecting buyers and sellers directly.
The rise of digital banking has led to disintermediation in the financial sector.
Many artists benefit from disintermediation by distributing their music without record labels.
The rise of online platforms has accelerated disintermediation in the travel industry.
Banks fear disintermediation as customers increasingly use financial technology services.
By adopting disintermediation, companies can reduce costs and reach customers more efficiently.
The rise of online trading platforms has accelerated disintermediation in the financial industry.
Many investors prefer disintermediation to avoid paying high fees to banks or brokers.
Regulators are studying the effects of disintermediation on the stability of traditional banks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: direct investment - investing money directly into assets or markets without intermediaries
  • synonym: direct selling - selling products directly from producer to consumer without intermediaries
  • antonym: intermediation - the involvement of intermediaries in a process or transaction
