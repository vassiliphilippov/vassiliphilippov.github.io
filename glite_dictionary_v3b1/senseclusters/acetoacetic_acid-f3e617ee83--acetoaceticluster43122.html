acetoacetic acid

noun
organic acid found in abnormal metabolism
7.4
Pronunciation: / əˌsiːtoʊəˈsɛtɪk ˌæsɪd / (AmE), / əˌsiːtəʊəˈsiːtɪk ˌæsɪd / (BrE)
an unstable organic acid found in blood and urine in cases of abnormal metabolism, such as diabetes mellitus or starvation

Examples

High acetoacetic acid levels
Abnormally high levels of acetoacetic acid can be a sign of uncontrolled diabetes.
Ketosis is characterized by elevated concentrations of acetoacetic acid in the bloodstream.
The laboratory test detected the presence of acetoacetic acid in the patient’s urine sample.
