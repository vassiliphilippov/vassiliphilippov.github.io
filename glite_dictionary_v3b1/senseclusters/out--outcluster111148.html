Unconscious, especially as a result of a blow to the head or body.

Examples

Knocked out cold
After falling and hitting her head, she was out for several minutes.
He was knocked out in the first round of the match.
The boxer was completely out before the referee even started counting.

Synonyms

  • knocked out
    4.6 0.80/m
    knocked out means unconscious after a heavy blow to the head, while out means being unconscious more generally.
  • out cold
    6.9 0.79/m
    out cold means completely unconscious or in a deep sleep often from injury or tiredness, while out can mean unconscious.
  • out like a light
    7.3 0.18/m
    out like a light means falling asleep or becoming unconscious very suddenly, while out refers to being unconscious, often after a blow.
