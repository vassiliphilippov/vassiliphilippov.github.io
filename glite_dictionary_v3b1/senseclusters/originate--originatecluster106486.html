originate

verb
to bring into existence
Pronunciation: / əˈrɪdʒəˌneɪt /; / əˈrɪdʒ.ə.neɪt / (AmE), / əˈrɪdʒɪneɪt /; / əˈrɪdʒ.ɪ.neɪt / (BrE)
to begin or to cause something to begin; to come into existence or bring into existence

Examples

To originate an idea
He helped originate the idea of community-supported agriculture.
Many popular dances originate in urban communities before spreading nationwide.
The new company plans to originate several innovative projects next year.
Many modern diseases originate in animals before spreading to humans.
The rumor originated on social media and quickly spread throughout the town.
The tradition is said to originate from ancient harvest festivals.

  • antonym: destroy - to put an end to the existence of something
  • antonym: end - to stop or cease to exist; to finish
  • synonym: initiate - to start or set in motion
