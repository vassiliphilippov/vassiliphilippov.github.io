preeclampsia

noun
pregnancy condition with high blood pressure and fluid retention
8.3 7.4
Pronunciation: / ˌpriː.ɪˈklæmp.si.ə / (AmE), / ˌpriː.ɪˈklamp.sɪ.ə / (BrE)
A serious condition of pregnancy characterized by high blood pressure, fluid retention, and protein in the urine, which may progress to eclampsia if untreated.

Examples

Severe preeclampsia symptoms
Symptoms of preeclampsia include swelling in the hands and face, and high blood pressure.
The doctor closely monitored her for signs of preeclampsia during her third trimester.
Untreated preeclampsia can pose serious risks to both the mother and the baby.
