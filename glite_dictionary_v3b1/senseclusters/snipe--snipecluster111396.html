snipe

noun
wading bird with a long, straight bill
Pronunciation: / snaɪp / (AmE), / snaɪp / (BrE)
a wading bird with a long, straight bill, found in wetlands, belonging to the sandpiper family; similar to the woodcock

Examples

A snipe bird
Several species of snipe migrate across continents each year.
The snipe uses its long bill to search for food in muddy ground.
We spotted a snipe in the marsh early this morning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: woodcock - a woodland bird similar in appearance to the snipe
