ghost

verb
suddenly stop communicating (informal)
6.8
Pronunciation: /goʊst/ (AmE), /gəʊst/ (BrE)
to end a personal relationship or contact by suddenly and deliberately stopping all communication without explanation.

Examples

To ghost a friend
After three awkward dates, he simply ghosted her without any explanation or goodbye.
When coworkers started asking, she admitted ghosting the recruiter because the job felt wrong.
It's rude to ghost people after making plans; send a message if you need to cancel.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ignore - to deliberately not pay attention to or respond to someone.
  • antonym: respond - to reply to or engage in communication with someone.
