torpedo

noun
self-propelled underwater explosive weapon
6.2 6.4
Pronunciation: / tɔrˈpiːdoʊ / (AmE), / tɔːˈpiːdəʊ / (BrE)
A long, cylindrical, self-propelled underwater weapon that explodes on contact with or near its target.

Examples

A torpedo attack
Early torpedoes were relatively slow, but modern versions travel at high speeds underwater.
The destroyer managed to evade the incoming torpedo just in time.
The submarine launched a torpedo at the enemy ship during the naval exercise.
