sirocco

noun
hot, dusty wind from North Africa
Pronunciation: / səˈrɑːkoʊ / (AmE), / sɪˈrɒkəʊ / (BrE)
a hot, dry wind blowing from North Africa across the Mediterranean, often carrying dust or sand

Examples

The sirocco wind
After days of calm, the arrival of the sirocco brought both heat and a yellowish haze to the coast.
Farmers watched nervously as a sudden sirocco threatened this year’s olive crop.
The sirocco blew relentlessly, filling the streets with dust and sand for hours.
