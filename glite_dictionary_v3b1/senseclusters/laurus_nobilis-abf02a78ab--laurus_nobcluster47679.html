Laurus nobilis

noun
aromatic Mediterranean evergreen tree (bay laurel)
7.0
Pronunciation: /ˈlɔːr.əs ˈnoʊ.bɪ.lɪs/ (AmE), /ˈlɔːr.əs ˈnɒ.bɪ.lɪs/ (BrE)
A small evergreen tree of the Mediterranean region, with aromatic glossy leaves used as a culinary herb (bay leaf) and blackish berries; also used by ancient Greeks to crown victors.

Examples

Laurus nobilis leaves
In ancient times, victors were crowned with wreaths made from Laurus nobilis.
Many traditional Mediterranean dishes call for a Laurus nobilis leaf to enhance flavor.
We planted a Laurus nobilis in our herb garden for its fragrant leaves.
