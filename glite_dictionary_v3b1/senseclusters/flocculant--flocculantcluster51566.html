flocculant

noun
particle-clumping agent
Pronunciation: / ˈflɑːkjələnt / (AmE), / ˈflɒkjʊlənt / (BrE)
a substance added to a liquid to cause suspended particles to clump together and form larger masses

Examples

Add a flocculant
The engineer added a flocculant to the wastewater to help remove contaminants.
Using a flocculant can speed up the process of clarifying muddy water.
The laboratory tested several types of flocculant to find the most effective one for the treatment plant.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coagulant - a substance that causes particles in a liquid to come together and form a mass
