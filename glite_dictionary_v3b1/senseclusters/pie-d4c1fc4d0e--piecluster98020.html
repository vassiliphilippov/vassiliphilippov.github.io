PIE

noun
Proto-Indo-European ancestral language
9.9
Pronunciation: / pi aɪ i / (AmE), / piː aɪ iː / (BrE)
a prehistoric unrecorded language considered the common ancestor of all Indo-European languages; Proto-Indo-European.

Examples

The PIE language
Linguists reconstruct the vocabulary and grammar of PIE using evidence from its descendant languages.
Many modern languages, including English and Hindi, are descended from PIE.
The word 'mother' in several Indo-European languages can be traced back to PIE.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Proto-Indo-European - the hypothesized ancestor language of all Indo-European languages
