fear

verb
to be uneasy or apprehensive about
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /fɪr/ /; / /fɪər/ (AmE), /fɪə/ (BrE)
to be afraid, anxious, or worried that something bad may happen

Examples

People fear danger
Many investors fear a downturn in the economy.
She fears she may have made a mistake in the report.
Some parents fear for their children's safety in big cities.
I fear that we will not finish the project on time.
Many investors fear a sudden drop in the stock market.
She feared she might offend someone with her comment.
Despite his warnings, we did not fear the approaching storm.
Many people fear public speaking more than death.
She has long feared flying and avoids air travel whenever possible.

Related Terms

  • antonym: despise - to feel contempt or a strong dislike (opposite of reverence/fear in emotional valence)
  • synonym: dread - to fear greatly or feel extreme reluctance about something
  • antonym: hope - to want something to happen and feel confident that it will
  • degree lower: revere - to regard with deep respect and awe (sometimes mixed with fear)
  • synonym: worry - to feel anxious or concerned about something unpleasant
