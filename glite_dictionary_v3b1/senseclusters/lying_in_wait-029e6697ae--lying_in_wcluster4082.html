lying in wait

noun
ambush or act of waiting in concealment
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪɪŋ ɪn ˈweɪt / (AmE), / ˈlaɪɪŋ ɪn ˈweɪt / (BrE)
the act of hiding and waiting in order to attack someone by surprise

Examples

Lying in wait for victim

The leopard spent hours lying in wait for unsuspecting prey.
The soldiers were lying in wait for the enemy near the abandoned barn.
