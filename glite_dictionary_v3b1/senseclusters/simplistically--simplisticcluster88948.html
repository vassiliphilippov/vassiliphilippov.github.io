simplistically

adverb
overly simple manner
6.4
Pronunciation: / sɪmˈplɪstɪkli / (AmE), / sɪmˈplɪstɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that is overly simple and fails to consider important details or complexities

Examples

Explained simplistically
The article explained the economic crisis simplistically, leaving out many contributing factors.
If you look at the issue simplistically, you might miss the underlying causes.
She answered the complex question simplistically, which did not satisfy the audience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: superficially - in a way that only considers the surface or obvious aspects
  • antonym: thoroughly - in a way that is complete and detailed
