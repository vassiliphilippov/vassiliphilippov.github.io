compositeness

noun
the state of being a composite number
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpɑzət.nəs / (AmE), / kəmˈpɒzɪt.nəs / (BrE)
the property of being a composite number; the state of not being prime in mathematics

Examples

Compositeness of numbers
A number's compositeness determines whether it can be factored into smaller integers.
Mathematicians study the compositeness of numbers to understand their factors.
The test quickly determines the compositeness or primality of any given integer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: composite number - a positive integer that has at least one positive divisor other than one or itself
  • antonym: primality - the quality of being a prime number
