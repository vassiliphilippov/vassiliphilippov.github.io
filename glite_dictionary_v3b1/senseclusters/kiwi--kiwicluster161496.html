kiwi

noun
flightless bird of New Zealand
Pronunciation: / ˈkiːwi / (AmE), / ˈkiːwi / (BrE)
a nocturnal flightless bird native to New Zealand, with a long slender bill and stout legs, and the only surviving member of the order Apterygiformes.

Examples

A kiwi bird
Because of its unique appearance, the kiwi is easily distinguished from other birds.
Conservationists are working hard to protect the remaining wild kiwi populations.
The kiwi is recognized as a national symbol of New Zealand.
