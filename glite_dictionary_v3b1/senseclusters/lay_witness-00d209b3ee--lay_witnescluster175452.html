lay witness

noun
non-expert witness in court
5.6
Pronunciation: / leɪ ˈwɪtnəs / (AmE), / leɪ ˈwɪtnɪs / (BrE)
a person who gives testimony in court based on their personal knowledge or perception, and not as an expert in a particular field

Examples

A lay witness testifies
A lay witness is only permitted to testify about things they personally observed or experienced.
The court relied heavily on the testimony of the lay witness who saw the accident occur.
Unlike an expert, a lay witness may not offer opinions based on specialized knowledge.

Related Terms

  • antonym: expert witness - a witness with specialized knowledge who gives expert testimony in court
