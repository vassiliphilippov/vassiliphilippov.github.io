Polonius

noun
pedantic busybody
9.9
Pronunciation: / pəˈloʊniəs / (AmE), / pəˈləʊniəs / (BrE)
a person who is overly talkative, pedantic, or meddlesome, especially in a pompous or self-important way

Examples

A Polonius at meetings
During the discussion, he played the Polonius, giving long-winded advice that no one asked for.
She was called a Polonius because she constantly interfered in her colleagues' work.
Nobody wanted to sit next to the office Polonius at lunch, fearing endless lectures on minor topics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: busybody - a person who meddles in others' affairs
  • synonym: meddler - someone who interferes in other people's business
  • antonym: taciturn - someone who is reserved or says little
