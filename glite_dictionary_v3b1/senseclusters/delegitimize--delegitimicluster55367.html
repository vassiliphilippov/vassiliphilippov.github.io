delegitimize

verb
to remove legitimacy
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːlɪˈdʒɪtəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˌdiːlɪˈdʒɪtəmaɪz / (BrE)
to make something or someone seem less legitimate, valid, or acceptable

Examples

To delegitimize a group
The new law was designed to delegitimize the opposition party and reduce its influence.
Critics argue that such actions only serve to delegitimize the entire electoral process.
Attempts to delegitimize scientific research can undermine public trust in important findings.
The opposition tried to delegitimize the election results by spreading false information.
Critics argued that the new law would delegitimize peaceful protests.
Negative media coverage can quickly delegitimize a public figure in the eyes of the public.
Some politicians attempted to delegitimize the new law by spreading misinformation about its origins.
The article accused the media of trying to delegitimize the protest movement.
False rumors can quickly delegitimize a leader in the eyes of the public.

Related Terms

  • synonym: discredit - to harm the reputation or credibility of someone or something
  • synonym: invalidate - to make something not valid or legally acceptable
  • antonym: legitimize - to make something legitimate or officially acceptable
  • antonym: validate - to confirm or make something officially acceptable or legitimate
