biconcave

adjective
concave on both sides
9.9 7.5
Pronunciation: /baɪ.ˈkɑːn.keɪv/ (AmE), /baɪ.ˈkɒn.keɪv/ (BrE)
having a surface that is concave on both sides, typically used to describe a lens or a red blood cell.

Examples

A biconcave lens
A biconcave lens diverges light rays as they pass through it.
Red blood cells have a distinctive biconcave shape that increases their surface area.
The disc was designed with a biconcave profile to mimic the shape of certain cells.
