lungless salamander

noun
salamander that lacks lungs
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈlʌŋləs ˌsæləˈmændɚ / (AmE), / ˈlʌŋləs ˌsæləˈmændə / (BrE)
A salamander belonging to a family that lacks lungs and breathes through its skin and the lining of its mouth.

Examples

A lungless salamander species
Biologists discovered a rare species of lungless salamander in the Appalachian Mountains.
The lungless salamander absorbs oxygen directly through its skin, which must remain moist for survival.
Unlike most amphibians, the lungless salamander spends its whole life on land and avoids ponds.
