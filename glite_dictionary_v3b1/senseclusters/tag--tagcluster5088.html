tag

verb
chase in order to catch
4.0 1.8
Pronunciation: /tæg/ (AmE), /tæg/ (BrE)
to touch or catch someone in a game, especially to change their status or put them out

Examples

To tag a player
He tried to tag his friend before she reached the safe zone.
In the game, you must tag as many players as you can.
The children decided to tag each other around the playground.
If you tag the player holding the ball, they're out.
She tried to tag him quickly but he dodged out of the way.
The catcher managed to tag the runner before he reached home plate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chase - to pursue in order to catch; pursue rapidly
  • antonym: miss - fail to touch (in game or sport), opposite of tag
  • synonym: touch - make physical contact with someone or something
