defense

noun
the defendant and their legal team in court
1.0
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfɛns / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛns / (BrE)
the legal team or the plea made by the accused in a court case

Examples

The defense lawyer
Her lawyer assured her that the defense had a strong case prepared.
The defense argued that there was insufficient evidence to convict the accused.
The defense called several expert witnesses to testify during the trial.
She was called to testify for the defense during the trial.
The judge listened carefully to the defense before making a decision.
The lawyer presented a strong defense against the allegations.
During the trial, the judge called both the prosecution and the defence to her chambers.
His lawyer led the defence in presenting new witnesses.
The defence argued that the evidence was inadmissible.

Related Terms

  • antonym: prosecution - the legal party conducting the case against the defendant in a criminal trial
