Chrysophyta

noun
group of eukaryotic algae
9.9
Pronunciation: / kraɪˈsɑːfɪtə / (AmE), / kraɪˈsɒfɪtə / (BrE)
A group of mostly freshwater eukaryotic algae characterized by brown or yellow pigments that mask the chlorophyll, including yellow-green and golden-brown algae and diatoms.

Examples

Chrysophyta algae group
Members of the Chrysophyta are often found in freshwater lakes and ponds.
Microscopists have long studied the unique silica shells of Chrysophyta species.
The classification of Chrysophyta has changed as scientists learn more about algae diversity.
