tenuto

adverb
held notes in music
8.7
Pronunciation: / təˈnuːtoʊ / (AmE), / tɛˈnuːtəʊ / (BrE)
in music, played or sung with each note held for its full length or slightly longer

Examples

Play tenuto notes
The pianist played the melody tenuto to emphasize each note's full value.
When marked tenuto, the violinist held each note slightly longer than usual.
The choir sang the phrase tenuto, giving the music a smooth and sustained quality.

Related Terms

  • antonym: legato - in music, played or sung smoothly and connected
  • antonym: staccato - in music, played with each note sharply separated from the others
