roughcast

verb
shape or cut roughly
7.3 6.4
Pronunciation: /ˈrʌf.kæst/ /; / /ˈrʌf.kɑːst/ (AmE), /ˈrʌf.kɑːst/ /; / /ˈrʌf.kæst/ (BrE)
to give a rough or basic shape to something, without finishing or smoothing it

Examples

To roughcast a stone
He roughcasted the stone to get the basic shape before the detailed carving began.
The workers roughcast the beams before smoothing them for the final structure.

Before adding detail, it's helpful to roughcast your design so you can see the proportions.
The sculptor began to roughcast the block of marble before carving the finer details.
They roughcasted the model from clay to establish its general form.
