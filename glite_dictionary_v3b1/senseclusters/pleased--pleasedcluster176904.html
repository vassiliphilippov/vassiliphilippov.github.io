pleased

adjective
feeling or showing pleasure or satisfaction
1.0 41/m
Pronunciation: / plizd / (AmE), / pliÀêzd / (BrE)
feeling happy or satisfied about something; showing pleasure or contentment

Examples

To be pleased
I'm pleased to meet you for the first time in person.
She was pleased with the results of her hard work.
The manager looked pleased when the team completed the project early.
He seemed genuinely pleased by the recognition from his peers.
I am pleased to announce that I have been promoted to manager.
She felt pleased with herself after finishing the marathon.

Synonyms

  • happy
    1.0 212/m
    happy is a general feeling of joy or pleasure in many situations, while pleased often implies being satisfied by something specific.
  • glad
    1.0 106/m
    glad expresses quick, often spoken pleasure or relief, while pleased is more neutral and can be used in formal contexts.
  • satisfied
    1.3 17/m
    satisfied describes having needs or goals met, while pleased can be a milder feeling without the idea of a completed result.
  • gratified
    4.0 0.16/m
    gratified feels like deep pleasure after getting praise or reward, while pleased is a lighter, more general sense of satisfaction.
  • contented
    2.0 0.71/m
    contented is a calm, long-lasting feeling of having enough, while pleased often refers to a shorter, event-based satisfaction.

Related Terms

  • antonym: displeased - feeling dissatisfaction or annoyance
  • synonym: proud - feeling deep pleasure or satisfaction as a result of achievements, qualities, or possessions that do one credit
  • synonym: satisfied - contented; fulfilled by having needs or wishes met
