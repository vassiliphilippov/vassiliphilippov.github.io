occupiable

adjective
fit for occupation
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːkjupaɪəbəl / (AmE), / ˈɒkjʊpaɪəbəl / (BrE)
able to be lived in or used by people

Examples

An occupiable apartment
The apartment will be occupiable after the repairs are finished next week.
Fire safety inspections must be completed before a building is declared occupiable.
The new office space is not yet occupiable due to ongoing construction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: habitable - suitable or good enough to live in
  • antonym: uninhabitable - not suitable for living in
