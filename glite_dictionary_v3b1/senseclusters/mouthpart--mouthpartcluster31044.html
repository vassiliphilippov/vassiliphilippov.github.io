mouthpart

noun
specialized structure forming the mouth of an insect or arthropod
Pronunciation: / ˈmaʊθpɑrt / (AmE), / ˈmaʊθpɑːt / (BrE)
any of the specialized appendages or structures forming the mouth of an insect or other arthropod, often adapted for a specific method of feeding

Examples

Insect mouthparts
Different insects have evolved unique mouthparts suited to their diets.
Scientists examined the mouthpart of the beetle to determine how it feeds on plants.
The mosquito's mouthpart is shaped like a needle for piercing skin and sucking blood.
