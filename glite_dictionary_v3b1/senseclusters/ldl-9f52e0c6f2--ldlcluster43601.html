LDL

noun
lipoprotein carrying cholesterol in blood
Pronunciation: / ˌɛl ˌdiː ˈɛl / (AmE), / ˌɛl ˌdiː ˈɛl / (BrE)
a type of lipoprotein in the blood that carries cholesterol, with high levels increasing the risk of heart disease

Examples

LDL cholesterol
A blood test can measure both LDL and HDL cholesterol levels.
Doctors often recommend lowering your LDL to reduce the risk of heart disease.
Eating a diet high in saturated fats may increase your LDL cholesterol.

Related Terms

  • antonym: HDL - a type of lipoprotein that carries cholesterol from tissues to the liver
