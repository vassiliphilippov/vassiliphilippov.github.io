Qabbala

noun
Jewish mystical and esoteric tradition
7.7 9.9
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈbɑː.lə/ /; / /kə.ˈbæ.lə/ (AmE), /kə.ˈbɑː.lə/ /; / /kə.ˈbæ.lə/ (BrE)
A form of Jewish mysticism and esoteric theosophy based on interpretations of the Hebrew scriptures, particularly developed between the 7th and 18th centuries.

Examples

Qabbala teachings
Interest in Qabbala spread to non-Jewish thinkers during the Renaissance.
Many scholars dedicate their lives to studying the texts and mysteries of Qabbala.
The teachings of Qabbala often require knowledge of Hebrew and a deep understanding of symbolism.
