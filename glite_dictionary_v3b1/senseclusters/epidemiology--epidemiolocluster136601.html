epidemiology

noun
study of disease spread and control
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˌdiːmiˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˌdiːmiˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The branch of medical science that studies the transmission, distribution, and control of diseases in populations.

Examples

Modern epidemiology study
Modern epidemiology relies heavily on data analysis to track patterns of disease.
She decided to specialize in epidemiology after witnessing the effects of an outbreak firsthand.
The basics of epidemiology are taught in many medical and public health programs.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: infectious disease - a disorder caused by organisms such as bacteria, viruses, fungi, or parasites
  • synonym: public health - the science and art of preventing disease and promoting health in communities
