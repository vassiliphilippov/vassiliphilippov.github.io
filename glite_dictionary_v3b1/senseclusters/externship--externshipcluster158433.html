externship

noun
clinical training period
6.4
Pronunciation: /ɪk.ˈstɜːr.ʃɪp/ (AmE), /ɪk.ˈstɜːn.ʃɪp/ (BrE)
a short period of practical work experience or training, usually for students, in a professional setting such as a hospital, clinic, or company

Examples

Complete an externship program
She completed an externship at a local hospital to fulfill her graduation requirements.
During his externship, he observed several complex surgeries and assisted with patient care.
Many students apply for an externship to gain hands-on experience before starting their careers.
During her university studies, she completed an externship at a local hospital.
The externship gave him valuable insight into the daily work of an engineer.
Many students apply for an externship to explore different career options before graduating.
