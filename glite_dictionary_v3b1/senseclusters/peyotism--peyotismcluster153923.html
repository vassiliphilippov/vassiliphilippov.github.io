peyotism

noun
peyote-based religion
Pronunciation: / piˈjoʊtɪzəm / (AmE), / pɪˈjəʊtɪzəm / (BrE)
a religious practice or belief system involving the ceremonial use of the peyote cactus, especially among some Native American groups

Examples

Peyotism religious practice
Anthropologists have studied peyotism among Native American communities.
The ceremony was an important part of peyotism and brought the community together.
Many followers of peyotism believe the peyote cactus has spiritual significance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: animism - the belief that objects, places, and creatures all possess a spiritual essence
  • synonym: shamanism - a religious practice involving a practitioner reaching altered states of consciousness
