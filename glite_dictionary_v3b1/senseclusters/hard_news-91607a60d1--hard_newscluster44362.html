hard news

noun
serious news coverage of important events
Pronunciation: / hɑrd nuz / (AmE), / hɑːd njuːz / (BrE)
News reports that deal with serious subjects or major events, such as politics, crime, disasters, or significant developments, rather than entertainment or human interest stories.

Examples

Hard news stories
In addition to entertainment segments, the program includes in-depth coverage of hard news topics such as politics and economics.
Many viewers turn to the evening broadcast for the latest hard news about national and international events.
The newspaper is respected for its commitment to reporting hard news rather than gossip or celebrity stories.
