blurter

noun
impulsive speaker
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈblɜrtər / (AmE), / ˈblɜːtə / (BrE)
a person who speaks out suddenly or without thinking, often revealing information unintentionally

Examples

A blurter of secrets
As a child, he was a notorious blurter who could never keep a secret.
The meeting was disrupted when a blurter shouted out the confidential plan.
She tried not to be a blurter, but sometimes her excitement got the better of her.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blabbermouth - a person who talks too much and reveals secrets
  • antonym: tactful person - someone who speaks carefully and considers others' feelings
