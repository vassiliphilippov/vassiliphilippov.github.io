cut in line

verb
move ahead of others in a queue
Pronunciation: / kʌt ɪn laɪn / (AmE), / kʌt ɪn laɪn / (BrE)
to move ahead of others in a queue without waiting for one's turn

Examples

To cut in line suddenly
If you cut in line at the theater, you might upset people who have been waiting.
It's considered rude to cut in line, especially when the line is long.
She got angry when someone tried to cut in line at the ticket counter.
