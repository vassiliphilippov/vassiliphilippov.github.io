widder

noun
dialectal 'widow'
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪdər / (AmE), / ˈwɪdə / (BrE)
a nonstandard or dialectal form of 'widow', meaning a woman whose spouse has died

Examples

The old widder
The old widder lived alone at the end of the lane.
Everyone in the village knew the widder and often brought her food.
Stories about the mysterious widder were told to children on stormy nights.
