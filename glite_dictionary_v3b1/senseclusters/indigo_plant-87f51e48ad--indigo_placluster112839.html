indigo plant

noun
plant source of indigo dye
Pronunciation: / ˈɪn.dɪ.ɡoʊ plænt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.dɪ.ɡəʊ plɑːnt / (BrE)
A deciduous subshrub of southeastern Asia with pinnate leaves and clusters of red or purple flowers, used as a source of indigo dye.

Examples

The indigo plant flowers
Many traditional textiles are colored using dyes extracted from the indigo plant.
Researchers are studying the growth habits of the indigo plant to improve crop yields.
The indigo plant was once widely cultivated in India for its valuable dye.
