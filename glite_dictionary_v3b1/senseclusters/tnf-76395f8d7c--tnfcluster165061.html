TNF

noun
proinflammatory cytokine in immune response
7.9
Pronunciation: /ˈtiː.en.ef/ (AmE), /ˈtiː.en.ef/ (BrE)
a proinflammatory cytokine produced mainly by white blood cells, which plays a key role in inflammation and immune responses, and is involved in some diseases such as rheumatoid arthritis.

Examples

High TNF levels
High levels of TNF were detected in the inflamed tissues of the patient.
New therapies target TNF to manage chronic inflammatory diseases.
Recent studies show that blocking TNF can reduce symptoms in patients with rheumatoid arthritis.
