confession

noun
admission of fault or wrongdoing
1.8 2.9
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈfe.ʃən/ (AmE), /kən.ˈfe.ʃən/ (BrE)
a statement, spoken or written, in which someone admits to having done something wrong or committed a crime

Examples

Police confession
After hours of questioning, he finally made a confession to the police.
Her honest confession surprised everyone in the room.
The letter contained a heartfelt confession of his past mistakes.
Police presented the court with a written confession detailing the events of the crime.
The suspect signed a confession in the presence of his lawyer.
Without a valid confession, the prosecution had little evidence to rely on.

Related Terms

  • synonym: admission - the act of admitting something, especially a fact or fault
  • antonym: denial - a statement that something is not true or that one has not done something
