ninhydrin

noun
chemical fingerprint reagent
Pronunciation: / nɪnˈhaɪdrɪn / (AmE), / nɪnˈhaɪdrɪn / (BrE)
a chemical reagent used to detect amino acids and reveal fingerprints, especially in forensic science

Examples

ninhydrin test
The laboratory technician applied ninhydrin to the paper to test for the presence of amino acids.
Forensic investigators often use ninhydrin to reveal fingerprints on documents and other surfaces.
After spraying ninhydrin on the envelope, purple marks appeared where fingerprints had been left.

Related Terms

  • synonym: indicator - a chemical that shows the presence of another substance by a visible change
  • synonym: reagent - a substance used in a chemical reaction to detect or measure other substances
