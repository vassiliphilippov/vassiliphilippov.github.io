digenic

adjective
involving two genes
Pronunciation: / daɪˈdʒɛnɪk / (AmE), / daɪˈdʒɛnɪk / (BrE)
relating to or caused by the interaction of two different genes

Examples

Digenic inheritance pattern
Some rare diseases have a digenic inheritance pattern that requires mutations in two genes.
Researchers discovered that the condition was digenic, involving both the A and B genes.
A digenic disorder may not appear unless both genetic variants are present in an individual.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monogenic - relating to or caused by a single gene
  • antonym: polygenic - relating to or caused by many genes
