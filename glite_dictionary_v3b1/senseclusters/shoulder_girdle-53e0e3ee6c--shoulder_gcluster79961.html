shoulder girdle

noun
the bony structure connecting arms to trunk
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈʃoʊldər ˌɡɜrdl̩ / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊldə ˌɡɜːdl̩ / (BrE)
the bony structure in humans formed by the collarbones (clavicles) and the shoulder blades (scapulae), connecting the arms to the trunk.

Examples

The shoulder girdle bones
Injuries to the shoulder girdle can impair your ability to lift or carry objects.
The shoulder girdle supports all arm movements and provides attachment for several important muscles.
The anatomy class focused on the bones that make up the shoulder girdle.
