Granville wilt

noun
bacterial disease of tobacco plants
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrænˌvɪl wɪlt / (AmE), / ˈɡran.vɪl wɪlt / (BrE)
a bacterial disease affecting tobacco plants, causing wilting and often death of the plant

Examples

Granville wilt disease
Farmers in the region suffered major losses when Granville wilt spread rapidly through their tobacco fields.
Researchers are studying resistant tobacco varieties to combat Granville wilt.
Symptoms of Granville wilt include yellowing leaves and wilting stems in affected plants.
