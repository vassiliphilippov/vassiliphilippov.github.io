shroud

noun
burial cloth or garment
7.2 2.31/m
Pronunciation: / ÊƒraÊŠd / (AmE), / ÊƒraÊŠd / (BrE)
a cloth or covering used to wrap, hide, or protect something, especially a dead body for burial

Examples

A burial shroud
According to tradition, the shroud was made of white linen.
The ancient tomb contained a shroud remarkably well preserved after centuries.
The deceased was wrapped in a simple shroud before being placed in the coffin.
The ancient mummy was wrapped in a linen shroud before being placed in the tomb.
A thick shroud of mist made it difficult to see the road ahead.
The mountain was hidden beneath a shroud of clouds all morning.

Synonyms

  • winding-sheet
    9.9 0.08/m

  • winding-clothes
    9.9 0.00/m
    winding-clothes is a garment used to wrap a corpse for burial, while shroud is a broader covering used to hide or protect objects.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exposure - the state of being uncovered or unprotected
  • synonym: veil - a thin piece of material used to cover or hide something
  • synonym: winding sheet - a sheet in which a corpse is wrapped for burial
