restock

verb
to fill with goods again
3.2 1.0/m
Pronunciation: / riËˆstÉ‘k / (AmE), / riËˆstÉ’k / (BrE)
to fill something, such as a store or supply, with goods or materials again after they have been used or sold

Examples

To restock the shelves
After selling out of toys, they quickly restocked for the holiday shoppers.
The shopkeeper had to restock the shelves after the weekend rush.
We need to restock our pantry before the storm hits.

Synonyms

    4.3 3/m
    replenish means to fill supplies again in many settings, while restock focuses on refilling a shop's shelves after sales.
  • resupply
    3.9 0.6/m
    resupply is the act of sending needed goods to a group or base, while restock often means refilling store shelves.
  • reprovision
    7.4 0.01/m
    reprovision means supplying equipment or services again for long trips or projects, while restock usually refers to store goods.
