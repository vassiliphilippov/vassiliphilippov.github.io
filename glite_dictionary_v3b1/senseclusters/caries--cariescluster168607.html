caries

noun
tooth decay; dental cavity
8.1 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…õriz / (AmE), / Ààke…ôriÀêz / (BrE)
Decay and softening of a bone or tooth, especially tooth decay caused by bacterial action.

Examples

Tooth caries
Regular brushing and flossing can help prevent caries in children and adults.
The dentist identified several caries during the routine check-up.
Untreated caries may lead to tooth pain and infection.

Synonyms

  • dental caries
    4.1 0.10/m
    dental caries is the clinical term dentists use for decay in teeth, while caries can mean decay in bone or tooth.
  • tooth decay
    2.2 0.36/m
    tooth decay is the common everyday phrase for teeth being destroyed by bacteria, while caries is the medical name.
  • cavity
    3.4 1.12/m
    a cavity is the visible hole or soft pit in a tooth after decay, while caries names the disease process causing it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cavity - a hole in a tooth caused by decay
  • synonym: tooth decay - process resulting in the destruction of tooth structure
