black pine

noun
a tall pine tree of western North America
1.3
Pronunciation: / blæk paɪn / (AmE), / blak paɪn / (BrE)
a tall pine tree of western North America with long blue-green needles in bunches of three and elongated cones, sometimes considered a variety of ponderosa pine

Examples

Western black pine
Botanists sometimes classify the black pine as a variety of ponderosa pine.
Many parks in the western United States feature stands of black pine.
The black pine is noted for its long, slender needles and tall, straight trunk.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ponderosa pine - a large pine of western North America with long needles and large cones
