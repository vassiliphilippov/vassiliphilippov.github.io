circulation

noun
movement of air, fluid, or sap through a system
Pronunciation: / ˌsɝː.kjəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɜː.kjʊˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the movement or passage of a substance such as air, fluid, or sap through or within a system or organism

Examples

Air circulation
Opening the windows improves the circulation of fresh air in the room.
Plants depend on the circulation of water and nutrients to survive.
Scientists study ocean circulation to understand climate changes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flow - continuous movement in a current, especially of liquids, gases, or electricity
