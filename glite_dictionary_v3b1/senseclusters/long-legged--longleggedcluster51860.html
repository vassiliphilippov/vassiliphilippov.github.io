long-legged

adjective
having long legs
Pronunciation: / lɔŋ ˈlɛɡd / (AmE), / lɒŋ ˈlɛɡɪd / (BrE)
having legs that are long in proportion to the rest of the body

Examples

A long-legged spider
A long-legged spider scurried across the garden path.
Many models are admired for their long-legged appearance on the runway.
The long-legged heron waded gracefully through the shallow water.

Related Terms

  • antonym: short-legged - having legs that are short in proportion to the body
