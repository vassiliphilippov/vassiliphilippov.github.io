trade book

noun
book for general readers
Pronunciation: / treɪd bʊk / (AmE), / treɪd bʊk / (BrE)
a book published for sale to the general public, rather than to a specialized or academic audience

Examples

A trade book sale
Many publishers rely on the success of a single trade book to boost their yearly profits.
The author’s first trade book quickly became a bestseller.
Unlike a textbook, a trade book is written for enjoyment by the general public.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reference book - book intended to be consulted for information, not read from cover to cover
  • antonym: textbook - book used for study or instruction in schools or colleges
