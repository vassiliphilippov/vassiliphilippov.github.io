triceps

noun
three-headed arm muscle
Pronunciation: / ˈtraɪˌsɛps / (AmE), / ˈtraɪ.sɛps / (BrE)
A large muscle on the back of the upper arm or hind limb, having three parts (heads) and responsible for extending the arm or leg.

Examples

The triceps muscle
He injured his triceps while lifting heavy weights at the gym.
Proper stretching can help prevent triceps strains and injuries.
The triceps is crucial for pushing movements like the bench press.

Related Terms

  • antonym: biceps - a muscle with two heads, especially the front arm muscle
