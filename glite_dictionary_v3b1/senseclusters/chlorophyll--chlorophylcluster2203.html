chlorophyll

noun
green pigment found in plants for photosynthesis
2.4 4.7
Pronunciation: /ˈklɔː.rə.fɪl/ (AmE), /ˈklɒ.rə.fɪl/ /; / /ˈklɔː.rə.fɪl/ (BrE)
a green pigment found in plants and other photosynthetic organisms that is essential for absorbing light energy during photosynthesis

Examples

Green chlorophyll pigment
Photosynthesis relies on chlorophyll to capture sunlight and convert it into energy.
The leaves appeared bright green due to the presence of chlorophyll.
Without chlorophyll, plants would not be able to produce their own food.
During autumn, chlorophyll breaks down, revealing other pigments in the leaves.
Scientists studied how chlorophyll absorbs sunlight for photosynthesis.
The leaves appear green because they contain chlorophyll.
