neck-deep

adjective
deeply involved or immersed
Pronunciation: / nɛkˈdip / (AmE), / nɛkˈdiːp / (BrE)
very deeply involved in a difficult, unpleasant, or overwhelming situation or activity.

Examples

Be neck-deep in trouble
She's neck-deep in financial troubles after losing her job.
They were already neck-deep in paperwork when the new regulations arrived.
We became neck-deep in preparations for the annual festival within a week.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: over one's head - more involved than one can handle
  • synonym: up to one's ears - deeply involved or immersed in something
