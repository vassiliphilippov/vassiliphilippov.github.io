grip

verb
hold the attention or emotions strongly
Pronunciation: / ɡɹɪp / (AmE), / ɡɹɪp / (BrE)
(of a feeling or emotion) to capture and hold the attention or emotions of someone, often so strongly that movement or response is inhibited.

Examples

To grip the audience
A sense of wonder gripped her as she gazed at the ancient ruins.
His chilling story gripped everyone in the room, leaving them speechless.
The audience was gripped by fear as the suspenseful scene unfolded.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fascinate - to attract and hold the attention of someone deeply
  • synonym: mesmerize - to capture the complete attention of someone
