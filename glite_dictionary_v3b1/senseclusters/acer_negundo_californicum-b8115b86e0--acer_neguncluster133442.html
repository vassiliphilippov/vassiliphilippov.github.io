Acer negundo Californicum

noun
a Pacific coast variety of box elder maple
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪsər nɪˈɡʌndoʊ ˌkælɪˈfɔrnɪkəm / (AmE), / ˈeɪsə nɪˈɡʌndəʊ ˌkælɪˈfɔːnɪkəm / (BrE)
A variety of box elder maple native to the Pacific coast of the United States, distinguished by its white mature fruits.

Examples

The Acer negundo Californicum tree
Botanists studied the Acer negundo Californicum to learn more about regional plant diversity.
In California, the Acer negundo Californicum provides important habitat for local wildlife.
The Acer negundo Californicum is known for its pale fruits that turn white as they ripen.
