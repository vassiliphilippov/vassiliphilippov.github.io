reboard

verb
to accommodate again
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈbɔrd / (AmE), / ˌriːˈbɔːd / (BrE)
to admit or provide accommodation and meals to someone again, especially as a boarder or resident.

Examples

To reboard old residents
After the renovations, the hostel decided to reboard its previous residents.
The school agreed to reboard students who had left during the summer break.
When her situation improved, the family was able to reboard their son at the academy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: evict - to force someone to leave a place
  • synonym: readmit - to allow someone to enter or join again
