sit-down

noun
form of strike where workers occupy the workplace
2.5
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪtˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈsɪt.daʊn / (BrE)
a form of strike in which workers occupy their workplace and refuse to leave until their demands are met or a settlement is reached

Examples

Factory sit-down
After two days of sit-down, management finally agreed to negotiate with the union.
The 1930s saw a rise in sit-down strikes across the automobile industry.
The factory workers staged a sit-down to demand better wages and safer working conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: strike - a work stoppage caused by the mass refusal of employees to work
