GP

noun
general medical doctor (not a specialist)
3.2 11/m
Pronunciation: / d íiÀê piÀê / (AmE), / d íiÀê piÀê / (BrE)
A doctor who provides general medical care to people, treating a wide variety of illnesses and conditions rather than specializing in one area.

Examples

Visit the GP
If you feel unwell, you should make an appointment with your GP.
Many people rely on their GP for regular health checkups.
My GP referred me to a specialist for further tests.

Synonyms

  • general practitioner
    2.7 0.79/m
    a general practitioner treats a wide range of illnesses in many patients, while a GP is the common short name for that doctor.
  • family doctor
    2.0 1.31/m
    a family doctor cares for people of all ages in one household over many years, while a GP treats a wide range of illnesses.
  • primary care physician
    3.8 1.59/m
    a primary care physician looks after general health and refers patients to specialists, while a GP mainly gives general treatment for illnesses.
