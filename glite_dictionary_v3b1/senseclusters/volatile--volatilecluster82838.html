volatile

adjective
liable to sudden or frequent change
Pronunciation: / ˈvɑːlətl /; / ˈvɑː.lə.t̬əl / (AmE), / ˈvɒl.ə.taɪl / (BrE)
likely to change suddenly and unpredictably, sometimes becoming worse or more dangerous

Examples

Volatile situation
Because the weather is so volatile, it's best to check the forecast before planning anything.
Her volatile mood shifts made it hard to predict how she would react.
The stock market has been particularly volatile in recent months due to global events.
Due to the volatile situation, many residents decided to leave the city for their own safety.
Her temper was so volatile that even a small disagreement could set off a major argument.
The political climate in the country was extremely volatile after the controversial election results.

Related Terms

  • synonym: changeable - liable to change; variable
  • antonym: stable - not likely to change or fail; firmly established
  • synonym: unstable - likely to change or fail; not stable
