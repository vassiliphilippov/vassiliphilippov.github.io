take its toll

verb
have a harmful or damaging effect
Pronunciation: / teɪk ɪts toʊl / (AmE), / teɪk ɪts təʊl / (BrE)
to cause harm or have a gradually worsening effect on someone or something over time

Examples

Late nights take their toll
Late nights eventually took their toll on her performance.
The heavy workload is beginning to take its toll on his well-being.
Years of pollution have taken their toll on the river's health.
