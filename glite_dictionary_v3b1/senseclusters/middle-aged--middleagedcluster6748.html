middle-aged

adjective
between 45 and 65 years old
2.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪdəl eɪdʒd / (AmE), / ˌmɪdlˈeɪdʒd / (BrE)
(of a person) being roughly between 45 and 65 years old; characterized by or relating to this stage of life.

Examples

Middle-aged person
As a middle-aged man, he started to focus more on his health and fitness.
Both of his parents are middle-aged and still working full-time.
The class was mostly attended by middle-aged women interested in learning new skills.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: elderly - being old, usually over 65 years of age
  • degree lower: young - being in an early period of life
