fire-raising

noun
malicious act of arson (British)
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪərˌreɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌreɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
The deliberate act of setting fire to property with malicious intent; arson, chiefly in British usage.

Examples

Deliberate fire-raising
Fire-raising is considered a serious offence under Scottish law.
He was charged with fire-raising after the warehouse burned down.
The authorities launched an investigation into the suspicious fire-raising at the historic building.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arson - the criminal act of deliberately setting fire to property
