homophone

noun
word pronounced the same as another but different in meaning or spelling
2.4 6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈhoʊ.mə.foʊn/ /; / /ˈhɑː.mə.foʊn/ (AmE), /ˈhɒ.mə.fəʊn/ /; / /ˈhəʊ.mə.fəʊn/ (BrE)
each of two or more words having the same pronunciation but different meanings, origins, or spellings, such as bare and bear

Examples

English homophones examples
'Knight' and 'night' serve as classic examples of English homophones.
The words 'flour' and 'flower' are homophones commonly confused in writing.
When teaching spelling, it's important to explain what homophones are to young students.

  • synonym: homonym - word that shares the same spelling or pronunciation as another but has a different meaning
