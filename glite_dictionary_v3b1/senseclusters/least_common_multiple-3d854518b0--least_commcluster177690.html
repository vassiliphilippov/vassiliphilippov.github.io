least common multiple

noun
smallest number exactly divisible by given numbers
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlist ˈkɑːmən ˈmʌltəpəl / (AmE), / ˈliːst ˈkɒmən ˈmʌltɪpl̩ / (BrE)
the smallest number that is a multiple of each number in a given set, especially two or more integers.

Examples

The least common multiple of numbers
Calculating the least common multiple helps when working with recurring events.
The least common multiple of 4 and 6 is 12.
To add fractions, you often need to find the least common multiple of the denominators.

Related Terms

  • antonym: greatest common divisor - the largest number that divides two or more numbers exactly
