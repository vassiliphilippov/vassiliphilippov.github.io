damp-proof course

noun
layer preventing rising damp in buildings

A layer of impermeable material built into the walls of a building near the ground to prevent moisture from rising through the structure.

Examples

A damp-proof course layer
A properly laid damp-proof course can prevent long-term structural damage from rising damp.
The builder installed a damp-proof course to keep the basement free of moisture.
When renovating older homes, it's important to check whether a damp-proof course was used in the walls.
