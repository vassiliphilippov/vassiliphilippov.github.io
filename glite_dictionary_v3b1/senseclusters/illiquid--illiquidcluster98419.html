illiquid

adjective
unable to pay debts
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɪkwɪd / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪkwɪd / (BrE)
not having enough cash or easily sold assets to pay debts or meet short-term financial obligations

Examples

Illiquid company
The company became illiquid after a sudden drop in sales and could not pay its suppliers on time.
Investors avoid illiquid assets because they are difficult to sell quickly for cash.
During the crisis, several banks were declared illiquid and required government assistance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: insolvent - unable to pay debts owed
  • antonym: liquid - having enough cash or assets that can be quickly converted to cash
