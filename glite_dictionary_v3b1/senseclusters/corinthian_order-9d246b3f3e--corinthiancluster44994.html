Corinthian order

noun
classical Greek column style with ornate leaf capitals
9.6 8.5
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈrɪn.θiː.ən ˈɔːr.dər/ (AmE), /kə.ˈrɪn.θiː.ən ˈɔː.də/ (BrE)
The classical Greek architectural order characterized by slender columns with ornate capitals decorated with carved acanthus leaves.

Examples

The Corinthian order columns
Architects often choose the Corinthian order to add a sense of grandeur to buildings.
The ancient temple featured columns in the Corinthian order, crowned with intricately carved acanthus leaves.
Unlike the simpler Doric style, the Corinthian order is admired for its elaborate detail and elegance.
