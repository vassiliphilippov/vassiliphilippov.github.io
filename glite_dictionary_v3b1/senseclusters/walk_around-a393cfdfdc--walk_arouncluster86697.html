walk around

verb
move about on foot without a fixed aim
1.0
Pronunciation: / wɔk əˈraʊnd /; / wɑk əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / wɔːk əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to walk in a place without a specific purpose or direction, often for leisure or to pass the time

Examples

She likes to walk around the park in the mornings to clear her mind.
Tourists often walk around the area to discover interesting shops and cafes.
We decided to walk around the city and explore its hidden streets.
After dinner, she would often walk around the garden to relax.
On weekends, I like to walk around the city and explore new neighborhoods.
They decided to walk around the park while waiting for their friends.
