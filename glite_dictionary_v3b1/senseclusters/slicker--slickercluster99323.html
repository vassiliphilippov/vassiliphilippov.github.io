slicker

verb
to polish smooth
Pronunciation: / ˈslɪkər / (AmE), / ˈslɪkə / (BrE)
to make something very smooth or shiny, often by polishing

Examples

To slicker shoes
He slickered his shoes until they gleamed for the big interview.
The car was slickered to a mirror-like shine before the show.
She slickered the wooden table to make it look brand new.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dull - to make something less shiny or bright
  • synonym: polish - to make something smooth and shiny by rubbing
