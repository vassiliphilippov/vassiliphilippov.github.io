ingrained

adjective
deeply rooted or firmly fixed
4.5 5.2
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈgreɪnd/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈgreɪnd/ (BrE)
(of a habit, belief, or attitude) deeply rooted; firmly established and difficult to change.

Examples

An ingrained habit
His ingrained distrust of outsiders made it difficult for him to make new friends.
It can take years to break an ingrained habit such as smoking.
Many older traditions remain ingrained in rural communities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deep-rooted - firmly established and difficult to remove
  • synonym: deep-seated - firmly established at a deep or fundamental level
  • antonym: superficial - existing or occurring at or on the surface; not deep or thorough
