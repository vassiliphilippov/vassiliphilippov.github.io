urchin

noun
mischievous street child
Pronunciation: / ˈɝːtʃɪn / (AmE), / ˈɜːtʃɪn / (BrE)
A poor and often mischievous child, especially one who lives or spends a lot of time in the streets of a city.

Examples

Street urchin
Dressed in tattered clothes, the urchin begged for coins at the busy intersection.
In Victorian novels, it was common to encounter a clever urchin surviving on the city streets.
The little urchin darted through the alley, laughing as he escaped the fruit vendor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ragamuffin - a ragged, dirty child
  • synonym: waif - a homeless, neglected child
