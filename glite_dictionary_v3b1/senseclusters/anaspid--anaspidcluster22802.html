anaspid

noun
extinct jawless fish of Silurian and Devonian
Pronunciation: / əˈnæspɪd / (AmE), / əˈnaspɪd / (BrE)
an extinct group of small freshwater jawless fish, usually with a heterocercal tail and an armored head, known from the Silurian and Devonian periods

Examples

Small anaspid fish
Paleontologists study anaspids to understand the evolution of jawless vertebrates.
The fossilized remains of an anaspid were discovered in ancient Devonian rocks.
The unique tail structure of an anaspid distinguishes it from other early fish.
