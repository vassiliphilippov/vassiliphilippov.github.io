erratic

noun
glacial boulder
8.2
Pronunciation: / ɪˈrætɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈrætɪk / (BrE)
a large rock or boulder that has been transported and deposited by a glacier, often far from its original location

Examples

A glacial erratic
The farmer discovered a huge erratic in the middle of his field after the snow melted.
Many erratics can be found scattered across the landscape, left behind by ancient glaciers.
Geologists studied the erratic to learn more about the glacier's path thousands of years ago.
