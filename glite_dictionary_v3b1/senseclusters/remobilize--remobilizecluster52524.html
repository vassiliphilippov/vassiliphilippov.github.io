remobilize

verb
to make usable again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈmoʊbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌriːˈməʊbɪlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something able to move or be used again, or to organize or prepare again for movement or action, especially after a pause or interruption

Examples

To remobilize damaged vehicles
After the flood, engineers worked quickly to remobilize the damaged vehicles.
The government decided to remobilize resources to support the recovery effort.
Physical therapy helped her remobilize her injured arm after months in a cast.
After the storm, the city had to remobilize emergency crews to clear the roads.
The organization decided to remobilize its volunteers for the new relief effort.
Once the negotiations failed, the army was quickly remobilized for action.
After the storm passed, the city quickly remobilized its emergency response teams.
The organization had to remobilize volunteers when the crisis escalated again.
Once the negotiations failed, the leaders decided to remobilize their supporters for another campaign.

Related Terms

  • antonym: demobilize - to disband or release from organized movement or action
  • antonym: immobilize - to prevent something from moving or being used
  • synonym: mobilize - to prepare or organize for movement or use
  • synonym: reactivate - to make active again
