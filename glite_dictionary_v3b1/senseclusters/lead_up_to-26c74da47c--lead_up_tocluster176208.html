lead up to

verb
develop in preparation for something
1.0
Pronunciation: / lid ʌp tu / (AmE), / liːd ʌp tuː / (BrE)
to gradually develop or progress towards a particular event or conclusion

Examples

To lead up to an event
Disagreements between the teams led up to the project's failure.
Months of training led up to her marathon debut.
The negotiations led up to the signing of the historic treaty.

Related Terms

  • antonym: follow - to come after in time or order
  • synonym: precede - to come before in time, order, or position
