scented penstemon

noun
fragrant North American plant with tubular flowers
8.5 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛntɪd ˈpɛnstəmɒn / (AmE), / ˈsɛntɪd ˈpɛnstɪmən / (BrE)
A North American plant with fragrant, tubular white to reddish-pink flowers borne in narrow clusters on upright stems, native to Arizona, New Mexico, and Utah.

Examples

Scented penstemon flowers
Gardeners often plant scented penstemon for its showy and fragrant flowers.
In spring, the scented penstemon attracts bees and hummingbirds to the garden.
The scented penstemon thrives in dry, rocky soils under full sunlight.
