theme

noun
base form of a word (linguistics)
Pronunciation: / θim / (AmE), / θiːm / (BrE)
(Linguistics) The base form of a word after all affixes have been removed, often used as the foundation for inflection or derivation.

Examples

Word theme
In historical linguistics, scholars often analyze the theme to understand patterns of word formation.
The theme is essential for accurately adding inflectional endings.
The distinction between the theme and the root can clarify how verbs are conjugated.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stem - the main part of a word to which affixes are added
