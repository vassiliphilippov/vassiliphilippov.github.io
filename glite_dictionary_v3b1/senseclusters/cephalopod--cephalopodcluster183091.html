cephalopod

noun
marine mollusk with tentacles (e.g., octopus, squid)
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛfələˌpɑd / (AmE), / ˈsɛfələˌpɒd / (BrE)
A marine mollusk of the class Cephalopoda, characterized by a well-developed head, large eyes, and tentacles with suckers, such as an octopus or squid.

Examples

A cephalopod like an octopus
An octopus is one of the most intelligent members of the cephalopod class.
Scientists studied the behavior of a cephalopod to better understand its problem-solving abilities.
The giant cephalopod glided silently through the deep sea, searching for prey.

Related Terms

  • synonym: octopus - a soft-bodied, eight-limbed mollusk
  • synonym: squid - a cephalopod with an elongated body and ten limbs
