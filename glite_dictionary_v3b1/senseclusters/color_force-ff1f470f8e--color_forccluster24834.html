color force

noun
force binding protons and neutrons in nuclei
8.8 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk ål…ôr f…îrs / (AmE), / Ààk ål…ô f…îÀês / (BrE)
(Physics) The fundamental force that binds protons and neutrons together in atomic nuclei, mediated by gluons.

Examples

The color force in physics
Physicists study the color force to understand the interactions between quarks.
The color force plays a crucial role in holding atomic nuclei together despite the repulsion between protons.
Without the color force, atoms could not exist as stable entities in the universe.
