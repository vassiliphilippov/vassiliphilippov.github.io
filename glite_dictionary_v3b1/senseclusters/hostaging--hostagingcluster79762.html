hostaging

noun
holding as hostage
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑstɪdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɒstɪdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or situation of holding someone as a hostage to force others to meet demands

Examples

The hostaging of diplomats
The hostaging of diplomats led to a tense international standoff.
Authorities worked tirelessly to end the hostaging without violence.
The news reported on the hostaging that lasted for several days.
The hostaging lasted for several days before the authorities intervened.
After the traumatic hostaging, the victims received counseling and support.
News of the hostaging spread quickly throughout the community.

Related Terms

  • synonym: captivity - the state of being kept imprisoned or confined
  • antonym: freedom - the state of being free and not held captive
  • synonym: kidnapping - the act of taking someone away by force, often for ransom
  • antonym: release - to set someone free from captivity
