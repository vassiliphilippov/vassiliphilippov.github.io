turn out

verb
gather for a public event
3.5 3.74/m
Pronunciation: / tÉn aÊŠt / (AmE), / tÉœËn aÊŠt / (BrE)
to come or gather for an event, especially in large numbers

Examples

Crowds turn out for events
Despite the rain, a large crowd turned out for the outdoor concert.
Only a few students turned out for the early morning lecture.
Thousands of people turned out to see the royal parade.
A large crowd turned out for the opening ceremony.
Only a few people turned out despite the invitations sent to everyone.
We were surprised by how many guests turned out to support the charity event.

Synonyms

  • flock to
    3.4 3.12/m
    flock to is used when people go in large numbers because they are attracted, while turn out describes people coming to an event.
  • crowd
    1.6 15/m
    crowd means to fill a place in large numbers and may cause cramped space, while turn out focuses on people coming for an event.
  • gather
    1.0 65/m
    gather often implies people or things come together or are brought together, while turn out usually highlights many people arriving for an event.
  • attend
    1.7 99/m
    attend means to be present at an event, often formally or regularly, while turn out stresses many people coming to it.
