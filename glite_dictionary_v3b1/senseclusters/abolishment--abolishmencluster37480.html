abolishment

noun
formal ending of a system or practice
Pronunciation: / əˈbɑːlɪʃmənt / (AmE), / əˈbɒlɪʃmənt / (BrE)
the act of formally ending a system, practice, or institution, especially through legal or official means

Examples

The abolishment of slavery
Historians discussed the significance of the abolishment of slavery in the 19th century.
Many support the abolishment of outdated regulations to promote innovation.
The abolishment of unfair laws took many years of activism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abolition - the act of abolishing a law, system, or practice, often used in reference to slavery
