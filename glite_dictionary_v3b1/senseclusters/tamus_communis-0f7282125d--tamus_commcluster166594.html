Tamus communis

noun
species of European climbing vine
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈteɪ.məs kəˈmjuː.nɪs / (AmE), / ˈteɪ.məs kəˈmjuː.nɪs / (BrE)
A common European climbing vine with tuberous roots, heart-shaped leaves, and red berries, also known as black bryony.

Examples

Tamus communis climbing vine
Botanists studied the tuberous roots of Tamus communis for their medicinal properties.
In the hedgerows, you can spot Tamus communis twining among other plants.
The red berries of Tamus communis are attractive but poisonous.
