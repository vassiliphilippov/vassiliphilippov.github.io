consociationalism

noun
power-sharing system
9.9
Pronunciation: / kənˌsoʊsiˈeɪʃənəlɪzəm / (AmE), / kənˌsəʊsiˈeɪʃənəlɪzəm / (BrE)
a political system or theory in which power is shared among different social, ethnic, or religious groups to maintain stability and fair representation

Examples

Consociationalism power sharing
The government adopted consociationalism to ensure fair representation of all ethnic groups.
Many political scientists study consociationalism as a way to manage conflict in divided societies.
Critics argue that consociationalism can entrench divisions rather than promote unity.
The country adopted consociationalism to ensure fair representation for all ethnic groups.
Political scientists often study consociationalism as a way to manage deeply divided societies.
Critics argue that consociationalism can sometimes reinforce divisions rather than overcome them.

Related Terms

  • antonym: majoritarianism - a political system where the majority holds power
  • synonym: power-sharing - a system where different groups share political power
