Spenserian stanza

noun
nine-line poetic stanza, specific rhyme scheme
9.6
Pronunciation: / spɛnˈsɛri.ən ˈstænzə / (AmE), / spɛnˈsɪəri.ən ˈstænzə / (BrE)
A stanza consisting of eight lines of iambic pentameter followed by a single line of iambic hexameter (Alexandrine), with the rhyme scheme abab bcbc c.

Examples

A Spenserian stanza poem
Many Romantic poets experimented with the Spenserian stanza in their own works.
Students analyzed the rhyme scheme of the Spenserian stanza in their English literature class.
The poet chose the Spenserian stanza to give his epic poem a traditional and elegant structure.
