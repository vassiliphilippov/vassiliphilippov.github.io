high priest

noun
leading authority or proponent in a field
4.0
Pronunciation: / haɪ prist / (AmE), / haɪ priːst / (BrE)
a person who is the leading authority or most prominent advocate of a particular movement, trend, or doctrine, especially in a non-religious context

Examples

Art high priest
As the high priest of minimalism, his influence extended throughout the art world.
Miles Davis was often called the high priest of modern jazz.
She is considered the high priest of avant-garde fashion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: follower - a person who supports and admires a particular person or set of ideas
  • synonym: leader - a person who leads or commands a group, organization, or country
