unresponsiveness

noun
software or device failing to respond
5.9
Pronunciation: /ʌn.rɪ.ˈspɑːn.sɪv.nəs/ /; / /ən.rɪ.ˈspɑːn.sɪv.nəs/ (AmE), /ʌn.rɪ.ˈspɒn.sɪv.nəs/ /; / /ən.rɪ.ˈspɒn.sɪv.nəs/ (BrE)
(in computing) the state in which software, an app, or a device fails to respond to user input or commands, causing delays or making the interface temporarily unusable.

Examples

App unresponsiveness
During peak hours, the shopping app's unresponsiveness led customers to abandon their carts in frustration.
Gamers complained that the game's sudden unresponsiveness caused them to lose matches and miss objectives.
Engineers traced the tablet's repeated unresponsiveness to a background process consuming all available memory.

Related Terms

  • synonym: freeze - a state when a program or device stops responding to input
  • synonym: lag - a delay or slow response in a system or application
  • antonym: responsiveness - the quality of reacting quickly and correctly to input; opposite of unresponsiveness in computing
