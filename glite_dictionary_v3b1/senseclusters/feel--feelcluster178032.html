feel

verb
search for by touch
3.1 2.0
Pronunciation: /fiːl/ (AmE), /fiːl/ (BrE)
to search for, examine, or explore something by touching with your hands, especially when you cannot see it

Examples

To feel for keys
He felt around in his bag for his keys.
She felt for the light switch in the dark hallway.
With the power out, they felt along the wall to find the door.
As part of the exam, the nurse will feel your pulse and check your breathing.
He carefully felt the patient's arm to locate the swelling.
The doctor began to feel her abdomen for any signs of tenderness.
He felt inside the bag for his keys without looking.
She had to feel her way along the wall to find the door in the blackout.
The child slowly felt around the unfamiliar room, trying not to bump into anything.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grope - to search for something by feeling with hands
  • synonym: handle - to touch, hold, or manipulate with the hands
  • synonym: palpate - to examine by touch, especially medically
  • synonym: touch - to come into contact with something, typically with the hands
