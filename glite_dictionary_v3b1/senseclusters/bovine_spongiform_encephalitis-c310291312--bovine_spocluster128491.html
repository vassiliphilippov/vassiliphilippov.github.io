bovine spongiform encephalitis

noun
fatal brain disease in cattle
8.7 6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈboʊ.vaɪn ˈspʌn.dʒɪ.fɔːrm en.sə.fə.ˈlaɪ.tɪs/ /; / /ˈboʊ.vɪn ˈspʌn.dʒɪ.fɔːrm en.sə.fə.ˈlaɪ.tɪs/ (AmE), /ˈbəʊ.vaɪn ˈspʌn.dʒɪ.fɔːm en.sə.fə.ˈlaɪ.tɪs/ /; / /ˈbəʊ.vɪn ˈspʌn.dʒɪ.fɔːm en.sə.fə.ˈlaɪ.tɪs/ (BrE)
A fatal neurological disease of cattle, characterized by degeneration of the central nervous system, causing symptoms such as unsteadiness and agitation.

Examples

Bovine spongiform encephalitis disease
Scientists studied the progression of bovine spongiform encephalitis to understand how the disease affects the brain.
The government implemented strict regulations to control the outbreak of bovine spongiform encephalitis in the region.
There was widespread public concern following several cases of bovine spongiform encephalitis in local cattle herds.
