past progressive

noun
past tense for ongoing actions
7.1
Pronunciation: / pæst prəˈɡrɛsɪv / (AmE), / pɑːst prəˈɡrɛsɪv / (BrE)
a verb tense used to describe actions that were ongoing at a particular time in the past, formed with a past tense of 'to be' and the present participle of a verb

Examples

The past progressive tense
After dinner, we were watching a movie when the lights went out.
They were playing football when it started to rain heavily.
When she called, I was reading a book, so I didn't hear the phone.
