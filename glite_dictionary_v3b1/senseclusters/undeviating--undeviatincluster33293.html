undeviating

adjective
steady and constant; unchanging
8.4 5.7
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnˈdiːvieɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈdiːvieɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
Not changing or varying; steady and consistent, especially in values or principles.

Examples

Undeviating commitment
His undeviating commitment to honesty won him great respect.
She followed the plan with undeviating precision, never straying from the guidelines.
The soldier swore undeviating loyalty to his country.

  • antonym: fickle - changing frequently, especially in loyalty or affections
  • synonym: steady - firmly fixed, supported, or balanced; not shaking or moving
  • synonym: unwavering - steady or resolute, not changing or faltering
