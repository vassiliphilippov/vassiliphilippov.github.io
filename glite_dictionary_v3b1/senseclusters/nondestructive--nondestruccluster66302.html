nondestructive

adjective
not causing damage
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑndɪˈstrʌktɪv / (AmE), / ˌnɒndɪˈstrʌktɪv / (BrE)
not causing damage or harm, especially to the object being examined or tested

Examples

Nondestructive testing methods
The laboratory uses nondestructive methods to analyze ancient artifacts without harming them.
A nondestructive inspection was carried out to check for cracks in the airplane wing.
They prefer nondestructive testing so the equipment can be used again after examination.

Related Terms

  • antonym: destructive - causing great and irreparable damage
  • synonym: harmless - not causing any damage or injury
