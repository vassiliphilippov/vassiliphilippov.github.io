fair

adjective
in baseball, within the foul lines
3.4 3.4
Pronunciation: /fer/ (AmE), /feə/ (BrE)
(in baseball) hit between the foul lines, so that it is considered in play.

Examples

Fair ball
If the ball lands fair, the play will continue and the batter will run to first base.
The batter hit a fair ball deep into left field, allowing the runner to score.
Umpires had to check if the line drive was fair or foul as it flew down the right-field line.

Related Terms

  • antonym: foul - (in baseball) outside the foul lines; not in play
