tentativeness

noun
uncertainty; hesitation
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛntətɪvnəs / (AmE), / ˈtɛntətɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being uncertain, hesitant, or not fully confident or decided

Examples

Clear tentativeness in voice
There was a clear tentativeness in her voice as she announced the new plan.
The tentativeness of the agreement made investors nervous about the project's future.
His tentativeness during the interview suggested he was not fully prepared.
There was a clear tentativeness in his answer when asked about the project.
Her tentativeness made it difficult for the team to make a final decision.
Despite his tentativeness, he agreed to try the new approach.

Related Terms

  • antonym: confidence - a feeling of certainty or assurance
  • synonym: hesitation - the act of pausing or being slow to act because of uncertainty
  • synonym: uncertainty - the state of being unsure or not definite
