structural gene

noun
gene coding for a specific protein
Pronunciation: / ˈstrʌktʃərəl dʒiːn / (AmE), / ˈstrʌktʃərəl dʒiːn / (BrE)
A gene that determines the structure of a specific protein or peptide by coding for its amino acid sequence, as opposed to a gene that regulates other genes.

Examples

A structural gene codes protein
Mutation in a structural gene can alter the amino acid sequence of a protein.
Scientists isolated the structural gene and inserted it into a bacterial plasmid.
The lac operon contains several structural genes responsible for lactose metabolism.

Related Terms

  • antonym: regulatory gene - a gene that controls the expression of one or more other genes
