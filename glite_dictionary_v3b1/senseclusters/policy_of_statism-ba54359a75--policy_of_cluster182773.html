policy of statism

noun
government control of the economy
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑləsi əv ˈsteɪtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈpɒləsi əv ˈsteɪtɪzəm / (BrE)
a political or economic policy in which the government exercises significant control over economic activities and resources, often limiting private enterprise

Examples

A policy of statism
After years of a policy of statism, the government began to privatize major industries.
Critics argued that the policy of statism stifled private business and slowed economic growth.
Many developing nations experimented with a policy of statism during the twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • antonym: laissez-faire - an economic philosophy of minimal government intervention in the free market
  • synonym: statism - a system in which the state has substantial centralized control over social and economic affairs
