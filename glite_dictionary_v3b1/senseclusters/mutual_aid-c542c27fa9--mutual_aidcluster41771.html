mutual aid

noun
reciprocal assistance agreement
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmjuːtʃuəl eɪd / (AmE), / ˈmjuːtʃu.əl eɪd / (BrE)
arrangements or actions in which two or more parties, especially nations, agree to assist each other, typically in times of need or crisis

Examples

Mutual aid agreement
After the earthquake, international mutual aid made recovery efforts far more effective.
The principle of mutual aid is central to many global alliances and partnerships.
The two countries signed a mutual aid treaty to support each other in case of an emergency.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cooperation - working together towards a common goal
  • antonym: self-reliance - independence and relying on oneself
