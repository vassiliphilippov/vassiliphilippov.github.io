buckwheat

noun
an annual plant with edible seeds used as food
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌkˌwiːt / (AmE), / ˈbʌkwiːt / (BrE)
An annual plant of the genus Fagopyrum, native to Asia, with small pinkish-white flowers and edible triangular seeds used for food and flour.

Examples

Buckwheat plant
He enjoyed the nutty taste of buckwheat in his porridge each morning.
She made pancakes using buckwheat flour for a wholesome breakfast.
The fields were blooming with buckwheat during the late summer months.
