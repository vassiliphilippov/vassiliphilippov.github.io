buckwheat

noun
an annual plant with edible seeds used as food
6.3 0.35/m
Pronunciation: / Ààb åkÀåwiÀêt / (AmE), / Ààb åkwiÀêt / (BrE)
An annual plant of the genus Fagopyrum, native to Asia, with small pinkish-white flowers and edible triangular seeds used for food and flour.

Examples

Buckwheat plant
He enjoyed the nutty taste of buckwheat in his porridge each morning.
She made pancakes using buckwheat flour for a wholesome breakfast.
The fields were blooming with buckwheat during the late summer months.

Synonyms

  • Fagopyrum esculentum
    9.9 0.00/m
    Fagopyrum esculentum is the scientific species name used by botanists, while buckwheat is the common food plant name.
  • Polygonum fagopyrum
    9.9 0.00/m
    Polygonum fagopyrum is an older botanical name seen in some texts, while buckwheat is the common modern name for the plant.
