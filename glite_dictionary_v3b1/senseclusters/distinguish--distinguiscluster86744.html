Synonyms

  • differentiate
    4.4 9.55/m
    differentiate means to show or make differences between things clearly, while distinguish means to recognize or point out those differences.
  • tell apart
    3.0 1.56/m
    tell apart is a casual phrase for noticing which items differ, while distinguish is the general word for recognizing differences between things.
  • discriminate
    4.2 4.28/m
    discriminate often means notice small or important differences, sometimes in formal contexts, while distinguish means simply to tell things apart.
  • discern
    5.8 9.48/m
    discern means to detect or notice something hard to see, while distinguish means to recognize or show differences more generally.
  • pick out
    3.0 3.60/m
    pick out means to find or notice someone or something among others, while distinguish means to show how things differ from each other.
  • identify
    1.8 167/m
    identify means to name or say who someone or something is, while distinguish means to point out how items are different.
  • recognize
    1.4 65/m
    recognize means to know someone or something again by sight or memory, while distinguish means to notice or show differences.
  • make out
    4.6 7.03/m
    make out means to manage to see, hear, or understand something hard to sense, while distinguish means to tell things apart clearly.
