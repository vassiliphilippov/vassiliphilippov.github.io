distinguish

verb
recognize or point out differences
Pronunciation: / dɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃ / (AmE), / dɪˈstɪŋɡwɪʃ / (BrE)
to recognize or show the difference between things or people; to identify something as different from others

Examples

To distinguish two objects
It can be hard to distinguish one species of butterfly from another without close observation.
Trained botanists can distinguish edible mushrooms from poisonous ones.
With experience, you learn to distinguish subtle differences between similar plants.
From this distance, she could barely distinguish the sound of a flute from the hum of traffic.
In the darkness, they struggled to distinguish which road led back to the campsite.
It was hard to distinguish the shapes of the boats in the thick fog.
It can be difficult to distinguish the twins from each other when they dress alike.
She could distinguish the sound of his footsteps in the hallway.
The study aims to distinguish different types of anxiety disorders.

Related Terms

  • antonym: confuse - fail to distinguish; mix up
  • synonym: detect - to discover or identify the presence of something
  • synonym: differentiate - to recognize or show the difference between things
  • synonym: discern - to see, recognize, or understand something, especially with difficulty
  • synonym: identify - to recognize and name someone or something
