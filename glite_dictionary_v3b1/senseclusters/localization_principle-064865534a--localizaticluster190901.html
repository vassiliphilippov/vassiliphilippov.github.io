localization principle

noun
principle of specific functions having distinct locations
Pronunciation: / ˌloʊ.kə.ləˈzeɪ.ʃən ˈprɪn.sə.pəl /; / loʊˌkæləˈzeɪʃən ˈprɪnsəpəl / (AmE), / ˌləʊ.kə.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən ˈprɪn.sɪ.pəl /; / ləʊˌkælɪˈzeɪʃən ˈprɪnsɪpəl / (BrE)
The principle that specific functions are associated with particular, localized areas in an organ or part of the body, especially in the brain.

The localisation principle suggests that language ability is largely situated in specific regions of the brain.
