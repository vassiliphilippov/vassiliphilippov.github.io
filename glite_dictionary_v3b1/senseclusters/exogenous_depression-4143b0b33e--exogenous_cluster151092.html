exogenous depression

noun
depression caused by outside events
1.9
Pronunciation: / ɛɡˈzoʊdʒənəs dɪˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / ɛɡˈzɒdʒənəs dɪˈprɛʃən / (BrE)
a type of depression triggered by external events or life circumstances, as opposed to arising from internal or biological factors; distinct from normal grief.

Examples

Exogenous depression after loss
Following the loss of his job, Mark developed exogenous depression that required professional support.
Major life changes such as divorce or bereavement can lead to exogenous depression in vulnerable individuals.
Therapists distinguish between exogenous depression and normal grief to determine appropriate treatment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: endogenous depression - depression originating from internal or biological factors
