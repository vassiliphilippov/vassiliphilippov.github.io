hitcher

noun
person seeking rides
Pronunciation: / ˈhɪtʃər / (AmE), / ˈhɪtʃə / (BrE)
a person who tries to get a free ride from passing vehicles by standing at the roadside and signaling drivers

Examples

A hitcher on road
The hitcher stood on the highway with his thumb out, hoping someone would stop.
After college, she became a hitcher and traveled across the country for free.
It can be risky for a hitcher to accept rides from strangers late at night.
The hitcher stood by the highway, hoping someone would stop and offer a ride.
We picked up a hitcher on our way to the next town.
Traveling as a hitcher can be unpredictable but also full of adventure.

Related Terms

  • antonym: driver - a person who drives a vehicle
  • synonym: hitchhiker - a person who travels by getting free rides from passing vehicles
  • degree lower: passenger - a person who is traveling in a vehicle but is not driving it
