St. Jude

proper noun
Christian apostle and patron saint of desperate causes
6.7 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / se…™nt d íuÀêd / (AmE), / se…™nt d íuÀêd / (BrE)
A Christian saint, one of the Twelve Apostles, traditionally invoked as the patron saint of lost or desperate causes.

Examples

Prayer to St. Jude
Many people pray to St. Jude when facing seemingly impossible problems.
Several hospitals are named after St. Jude in recognition of his legacy of hope.
The church held a special service honoring St. Jude as the patron of lost causes.

Synonyms

  • Saint Jude
    6.8 0.00/m
    Saint Jude is the full written name used in formal church texts and prayer, while St. Jude is the short, common form.
  • Jude the Apostle
    7.6 0.00/m
    Jude the Apostle appears in biblical and historical texts as an apostle, while St. Jude is mainly the saint people pray to for desperate causes.
  • Thaddaeus
    9.9 0.03/m
    Thaddaeus is an alternate name found in some Bible lists, while St. Jude is the common name used in churches and prayers.
