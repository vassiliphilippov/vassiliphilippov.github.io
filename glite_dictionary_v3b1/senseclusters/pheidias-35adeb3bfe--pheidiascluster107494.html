Pheidias

proper noun
ancient Greek sculptor
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfiːdiəs / (AmE), / ˈfiːdɪəs / (BrE)
an ancient Greek sculptor (c. 500–432 BC) renowned for his statues of gods and his contribution to Classical Greek art, including works on the Parthenon.

Examples

Pheidias the sculptor
Pheidias is famed for sculpting the statue of Zeus at Olympia, one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World.
Scholars believe that Pheidias supervised the decoration of the Parthenon in Athens.
The artistic legacy of Pheidias has inspired generations of sculptors across Europe.
