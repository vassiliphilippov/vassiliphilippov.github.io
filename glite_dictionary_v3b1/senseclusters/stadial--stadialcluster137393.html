stadial

noun
cold glacial phase
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsteɪdiəl / (AmE), / ˈsteɪdiəl / (BrE)
a short cold period or phase within a glacial epoch, usually separated from warmer intervals called interstadials

Examples

A stadial phase
The last ice age included several stadials that dramatically affected global temperatures.
Researchers identified a stadial in the ice core data, indicating a brief return to colder conditions.
A stadial can interrupt longer periods of warming during a glacial epoch.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glaciation - a period when large areas of the Earth are covered with ice
  • antonym: interstadial - a warmer period between two cold stadials during a glacial epoch
