hypersensitivity

noun
abnormal or excessive sensitivity
3.2 7.0
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪ.pər.sen.səˈtɪv.ə.ti /; / ˌhaɪpərsɛnˈsɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪ.pə.sɛns.ɪˈtɪv.ɪ.ti /; / ˌhaɪpəsɛnsɪˈvɪlɪti / (BrE)
an extreme or abnormal degree of sensitivity to something, such as a substance, stimulus, or even criticism (often used in medical or emotional contexts)

Examples

Hypersensitivity reaction
Certain foods can trigger hypersensitivity reactions in some individuals, such as swelling or difficulty breathing.
Research is ongoing to understand the causes of immune system hypersensitivity in children.
The patient's hypersensitivity made her skin break out in rashes after minimal contact with wool.
His emotional hypersensitivity made criticism especially painful for him.
Some people develop hypersensitivity to certain foods, resulting in allergic reactions.
The patient's hypersensitivity to sunlight required her to wear protective clothing outdoors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allergy - a condition in which the immune system reacts abnormally to a foreign substance
  • antonym: insensitivity - lack of sensitivity or responsiveness
  • degree lower: sensitivity - the capacity to respond to physical or emotional stimuli
