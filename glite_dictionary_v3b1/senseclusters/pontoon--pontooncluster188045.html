pontoon

noun
float supporting a seaplane
Pronunciation: / pɑnˈtun / (AmE), / pɒnˈtuːn / (BrE)
a hollow, floating structure used to support things on water, such as seaplanes, docks, or bridges

Examples

A pontoon bridge
After the crash, one pontoon was found floating separately from the rest of the wreckage.
Engineers inspected each pontoon before the aircraft was cleared for takeoff.
The seaplane's pontoon allowed it to land smoothly on the lake.
Engineers quickly assembled a pontoon to allow vehicles to cross the flooded area.
The temporary bridge was supported by a series of pontoons across the river.
They moored the boat alongside the pontoon at the marina.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barge - a flat-bottomed boat for carrying freight, especially on canals and rivers
  • synonym: float - a structure that rests on the surface of water and provides buoyancy
