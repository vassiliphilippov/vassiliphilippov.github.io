subshell

noun
secondary shell
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌʃɛl / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌʃɛl / (BrE)
a secondary shell or environment within a main shell, especially in computing or physics

Examples

Open a subshell
The script executed in a separate subshell to avoid affecting the main process.
You can open a new subshell to test commands without leaving your current session.
In physics, an electron may occupy a different subshell within the same energy level.

Related Terms

  • synonym: environment - a setting or context in which processes run
  • synonym: shell - a command-line interface or environment in computing
