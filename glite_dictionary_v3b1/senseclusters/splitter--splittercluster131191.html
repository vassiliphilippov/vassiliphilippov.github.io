splitter

noun
person who splits logs for fences
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈsplɪtər /; / ˈsplɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈsplɪtə / (BrE)
a person whose job is to split things, such as logs or fish

Examples

A wood splitter
Before machines, a splitter was needed to craft each rail by hand.
Every spring, the splitter would prepare the rails for the new fencing around the fields.
The old farmhouse was built by a skilled splitter using local timber.
As a splitter at the fish processing plant, she became highly skilled at preparing cod for drying.
Many experienced splitters can process hundreds of fish in a single day.
The job of a splitter is essential in producing high-quality salted fish.
