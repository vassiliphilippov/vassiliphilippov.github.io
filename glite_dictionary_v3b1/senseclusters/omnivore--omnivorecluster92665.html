omnivore

noun
animal that eats plants and animals
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˈɑm.nɪˌvɔr /; / ˈɑːm.nɪˌvɔɹ / (AmE), / ˈɒm.nɪ.vɔː / (BrE)
an animal or person that eats both plants and animals

Examples

A omnivore animal
Bears are considered omnivores because they eat both berries and fish.
Many humans act as omnivores, consuming both meat and vegetables.
Unlike herbivores, omnivores can thrive on a varied diet.
As an omnivore, he enjoys both steak and fresh vegetables at every meal.
Humans are generally considered omnivores because they can digest a wide variety of foods.
The diet of an omnivore is much more flexible than that of a strict vegetarian.

Related Terms

  • antonym: carnivore - an animal that eats only other animals
  • antonym: herbivore - an animal that feeds mainly on plants
