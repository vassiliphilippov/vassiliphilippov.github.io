Unitarian Church

proper noun
Protestant denomination rejecting the Trinity
5.7
Pronunciation: /juː.nə.ˈtɪr.iː.ən tʃɜːrtʃ/ (AmE), /juː.nə.ˈtɪə.riː.ən tʃɜːtʃ/ (BrE)
A Protestant Christian denomination that rejects the doctrine of the Trinity, emphasizing the oneness of God.

Examples

Unitarian Church members
He became a member of the Unitarian Church after studying different branches of Christianity.
The Unitarian Church has a long tradition of social justice and community involvement.
The Unitarian Church promotes freedom of belief and a rational approach to religion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Unitarianism - The religious belief system associated with the Unitarian Church
