long run

noun
distant future period; eventual outcome
2.4
Pronunciation: / lɔŋ rʌn / (AmE), / lɒŋ rʌn / (BrE)
a long period of time, especially when considering the final result or outcome of actions

Examples

In the long run
Changes to the policy may cause disruption at first, but they are necessary for success in the long run.
It may seem costly now, but in the long run it will save money.
They believed that honesty would benefit everyone in the long run.
Her decision was not just for now but for the long run of her career.
In the long run, persistence and dedication can make a big difference.
Training for a marathon requires commitment to the long run.

Related Terms

  • antonym: short run - a short period of time, especially as opposed to the long run
