go ahead

verb
proceed; move forward with something
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ əˈhɛd / (AmE), / ɡəʊ əˈhɛd / (BrE)
to proceed with something, especially after a pause or hesitation

Examples

To go ahead with plans
After a brief discussion, the team decided to go ahead with the new marketing strategy.
Despite the risks, she chose to go ahead and invest in the project.
If there are no objections, let's go ahead and start the meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hold back - to delay or hesitate to do something
  • synonym: proceed - to begin or continue a course of action
