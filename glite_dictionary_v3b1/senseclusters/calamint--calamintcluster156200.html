calamint

noun
aromatic herb of the mint family
Pronunciation: / ˈkæləˌmɪnt / (AmE), / ˈkaləmɪnt / (BrE)
A perennial aromatic herb of the mint family, found in hedgerows, scrub, or open woodlands in Europe, Asia, and North America.

Examples

Calamint leaves
Cooks sometimes use calamint leaves to flavor dishes or herbal teas.
Native bees are often attracted to the blossoms of calamint.
The calamint in her garden gave off a pleasant, minty fragrance on warm days.
