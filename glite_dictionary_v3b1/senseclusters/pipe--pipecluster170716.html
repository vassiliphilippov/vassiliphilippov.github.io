pipe

noun
tubular wind musical instrument
3.9 2.28/m
Pronunciation: / paÉªp / (AmE), / paÉªp / (BrE)
a musical instrument or part of an instrument that is a tube played by blowing air through it (such as a flute or an organ pipe)

Examples

Flute pipe
Ancient ceremonies often included music from a simple reed pipe.

Organ builders meticulously tune every pipe during installation to ensure perfect harmony.
The organist pulled out a stop, allowing air to flow through the largest pipe for a deep tone.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flute - a woodwind instrument with a slender tube played by blowing across an opening
  • synonym: horn - a wind instrument typically made of metal and played by blowing
  • synonym: stop - a set of organ pipes of similar tone and pitch
