hemochromatosis

noun
iron overload disorder
9.9 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåhiÀêmo äÀåkro äm…ôÀàto äs…™s / (AmE), / ÀåhiÀêm…ô äÀåkr…ô äm…ôÀàt…ô äs…™s / (BrE)
A medical condition in which excessive iron accumulates in the body, especially in the tissues, potentially causing bronzed skin, liver enlargement, diabetes, and joint abnormalities.

Examples

Hemochromatosis disease
Early diagnosis of hemochromatosis is crucial for effective management and prevention of organ damage.
The doctor suspected hemochromatosis after noticing the patient's unusually high iron levels.
Untreated hemochromatosis can lead to serious complications such as liver disease and diabetes.

Synonyms

  • iron-storage disease
    6.0 0.00/m
    iron-storage disease is a medical name for excess iron in body tissues, while hemochromatosis is the specific disorder causing tissue damage.
  • bronzed diabetes
    9.9 0.00/m
    bronzed diabetes describes skin darkening plus diabetes from iron build-up, while hemochromatosis is the medical name for that condition.
  • iron overload
    5.8 0.05/m
    iron overload means excess iron in the body from any cause, while hemochromatosis is the inherited disease causing it.
