flashover

noun
unintended electrical discharge
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈflæʃˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈflæʃˌəʊvə / (BrE)
an unintended electrical discharge that occurs over or around the surface of an insulator or between adjacent conductors

Examples

Electrical flashover
A flashover can cause serious equipment damage in high-voltage power lines.
Engineers regularly inspect transformers to prevent flashover incidents.
Insulation failures often lead to sudden flashovers during thunderstorms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arcing - an electric discharge between two electrodes
