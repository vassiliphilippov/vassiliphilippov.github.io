macroprudential

adjective
financial system regulation
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˌmækroʊpruːˈdɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ˌmakrəʊpruːˈdɛnʃəl / (BrE)
relating to policies or regulations that aim to protect or monitor the stability of the whole financial system

Examples

Macroprudential policies
The government implemented macroprudential policies to reduce risks in the banking sector.
Experts debated the effectiveness of macroprudential tools in preventing future financial crises.
International organizations recommend macroprudential oversight to maintain global financial stability.
The central bank introduced new macroprudential policies to prevent a future financial crisis.
Effective macroprudential regulation can help reduce the risk of widespread banking failures.
International organizations often recommend macroprudential measures to strengthen global financial stability.

Related Terms

  • antonym: microprudential - relating to regulation of individual financial institutions
  • synonym: systemic - affecting or relating to a whole system
