hearing officer

noun
official who conducts administrative hearings
Pronunciation: / ˈhɪrɪŋ ˌɑːfɪsər / (AmE), / ˈhɪərɪŋ ˌɒfɪsə / (BrE)
an official appointed by a government agency to conduct investigations or administrative hearings, enabling the agency to exercise its authority

Examples

The hearing officer decides
After reviewing all the evidence, the hearing officer issued a formal decision.
Applicants can appeal a denial by requesting a review with a hearing officer.
The hearing officer presided over the case to determine whether the company had violated regulations.
