commutation

noun
reduction of legal punishment
9.2 6.2
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːmjʊˈteɪʃən /; / ˌkɑːmjəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒmjʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
an official decision to reduce a criminal's punishment to a less severe one

Examples

Commutation of a sentence
Legal counsel requested a commutation of the death penalty in light of new evidence.
She was granted a commutation after serving ten years of her original sentence.
The governor signed a commutation that reduced the prisoner's sentence from life to twenty years.
She appealed to the president for commutation after spending a decade in prison.
The governor granted a commutation of his sentence, reducing it from life imprisonment to twenty years.
The legal team argued that the harsh penalty warranted commutation due to new evidence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pardon - the action of forgiving someone for an error or offense, especially in law
  • synonym: remission - the cancellation or reduction of a punishment or debt
  • degree higher: sentence - the punishment assigned to a defendant found guilty by a court
