Examples

Tragopogon porrifolius is often cultivated for its edible root, which has a mild flavor reminiscent of oysters.
Gardeners in the United States sometimes grow Tragopogon porrifolius as a biennial vegetable for its nutritional value.
The long, slender root of Tragopogon porrifolius can be roasted or boiled and added to soups.
