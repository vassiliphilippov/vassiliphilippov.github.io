motor nerve fiber

noun
nerve fiber controlling muscles or glands
4.3 4.0
Pronunciation: /ˈmoʊ.tər nɜːrv ˈfaɪ.bər/ (AmE), /ˈməʊ.tə nɜːv ˈfaɪ.bə/ (BrE)
a nerve fiber that carries impulses from the brain or spinal cord to a muscle or gland, causing movement or action

Examples

Motor nerve fiber damage
Damage to a motor nerve fiber may result in muscle weakness or paralysis.
Each motor nerve fiber innervates a specific group of muscle fibers.
The doctor explained how signals travel along a motor nerve fiber to produce movement.
