underwork

noun
insufficient work
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndərˌwɜrk / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌwɜːk / (BrE)
an amount of work that is less than what is required or expected

Examples

Employee underwork
The manager noticed a pattern of underwork among several employees during the project.
Due to underwork, the department failed to meet its quarterly goals.
Persistent underwork can lead to poor performance reviews and missed opportunities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: insufficiency - the state of not having enough of something
  • antonym: overwork - an excessive amount of work
