leptosporangiate

adjective
having sporangia from a single epidermal cell (ferns)
7.3 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌlɛp.toʊ.spəˈræn.dʒi.eɪt / (AmE), / ˌlɛp.təʊ.spəˈræn.dʒi.eɪt / (BrE)
(of ferns) having each sporangium developed from a single epidermal cell, characteristic of the majority of fern species.

Examples

Leptosporangiate ferns
Most modern ferns are classified as leptosporangiate because of their sporangium structure.
The leptosporangiate development is an important evolutionary step in fern reproduction.
Unlike eusporangiate ferns, leptosporangiate ferns have delicate sporangia arising from a single cell.

Related Terms

  • antonym: eusporangiate - having sporangia developed from a group of cells (contrasts with leptosporangiate)
