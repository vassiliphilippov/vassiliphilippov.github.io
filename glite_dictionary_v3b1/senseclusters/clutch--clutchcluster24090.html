clutch

verb
to hold tightly or firmly
Pronunciation: / klʌtʃ / (AmE), / klʌtʃ / (BrE)
to grasp or hold something tightly, especially with the hands or due to a strong emotion

Examples

To clutch a bag
During the scary part of the movie, she found herself clutching her pillow tightly.
He clutched the trophy in disbelief, unable to hide his excitement.
The little girl clutched her mother's hand as they crossed the busy street.
He clutched the letter to his chest, afraid it might be taken away.
She clutched her coat around her as the icy wind blew.
The little boy clutched his mother’s hand tightly in the crowded street.
A sudden panic clutched at her as she realized the door was locked.
Cold dread clutched his heart before the speech began.
The terrible news clutched him with a wave of despair.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grip - to hold something firmly
  • antonym: release - to let go of something
  • synonym: seize - to take hold of something suddenly or forcefully
