relict

noun
organism surviving as remnant of extinct group
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛlɪkt / (AmE), / ˈrɛlɪkt / (BrE)
an organism or species that survives as a remnant of an otherwise extinct group in a changed environment

Examples

Relict species survive
Some ancient pine species survive as relicts in isolated mountain regions.
The discovery of a living relict species excited biologists around the world.
The tuatara is considered a relict of a once-diverse group of reptiles.
