Chumash

proper noun
California indigenous people
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃuːmæʃ / (AmE), / ˈtʃuːmæʃ / (BrE)
a Native American people indigenous to the coastal regions of California

Examples

The Chumash tribe
The Chumash lived along the central and southern coasts of California for thousands of years.
Many museums in California display artifacts made by the Chumash people.
Researchers study the language and traditions of the Chumash to preserve their cultural heritage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Miwok - a Native American people indigenous to Northern California
  • synonym: Tongva - a Native American people indigenous to the Los Angeles Basin in California
