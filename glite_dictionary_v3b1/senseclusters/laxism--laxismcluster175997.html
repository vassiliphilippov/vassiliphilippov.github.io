laxism

noun
lenient moral doctrine
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlæksɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈlæksɪzəm / (BrE)
the practice or belief of being too lenient or not strict enough, especially in moral or rule-based situations

Examples

The laxism belief
Some theologians argue that laxism undermines the seriousness of moral obligations.
The debate between laxism and rigorism shaped many ethical discussions in the church.
He was accused of promoting laxism by always choosing the easiest moral path.
The school administration was criticized for its laxism in dealing with repeated rule violations.
Some argue that laxism in law enforcement can undermine public trust in the system.
His approach to deadlines bordered on laxism, frustrating his more punctual colleagues.

Related Terms

  • synonym: leniency - the quality of being more permissive or tolerant
  • antonym: rigorism - the doctrine of following the strictest moral opinion
  • antonym: strictness - the quality of being very careful to follow rules or standards
