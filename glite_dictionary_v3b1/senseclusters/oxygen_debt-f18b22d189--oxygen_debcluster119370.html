oxygen debt

noun
temporary shortage of oxygen after exercise
5.8 4.6
Pronunciation: /ˈɑːk.sə.dʒən det/ /; / /ˈɑːk.sɪ.dʒən det/ (AmE), /ˈɒk.sɪ.dʒən det/ /; / /ˈɒk.sə.dʒən det/ (BrE)
a temporary shortage of oxygen in the body tissues after strenuous exercise, which is repaid by increased breathing and heart rate at rest

Examples

Oxygen debt after exercise
After sprinting up the hill, her body accumulated an oxygen debt that left her gasping for air.
As the oxygen debt is repaid, the heart rate gradually returns to normal.
The concept of oxygen debt explains why athletes breathe heavily after intense workouts.
