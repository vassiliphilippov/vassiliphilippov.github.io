harmonious

adjective
musically pleasing; well blended sound
4.2 0.32/m
Pronunciation: / h…ërÀàmo äni…ôs / (AmE), / h…ëÀêÀàm…ô äni…ôs / (BrE)
(of music or sound) having a pleasant and agreeable combination of tones; musically pleasing or well blended.

Examples

Harmonious music
The choir produced a harmonious blend of voices during the performance.
Their instruments created a harmonious sound that was soothing and pleasant to the ear.
We listened to the harmonious notes of the orchestra as they filled the hall.

Synonyms

  • euphonious
    8.3 0.11/m
    euphonious describes sounds that please the ear and sound well balanced, while harmonious means tones that blend smoothly together.
  • symphonious
    8.1 0.01/m
    symphonious is a formal, rare word for music that sounds united, while harmonious means tones that fit together nicely.
  • mellifluous
    8.5 0.35/m
    mellifluous describes a smooth, sweet sound often in voices or instruments, while harmonious focuses on a pleasing blend of tones.
  • melodious
    4.0 0.56/m
    melodious describes a clear, tuneful melody people can easily hum, while harmonious stresses how different tones mix well together.
