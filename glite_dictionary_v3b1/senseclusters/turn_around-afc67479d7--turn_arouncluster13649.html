turn around

verb
to face the opposite direction
2.0 13/m
Pronunciation: / t…ùn …ôÀàra änd / (AmE), / t…úÀên …ôÀàra änd / (BrE)
to turn and face the opposite direction, either physically or in attitude or action

Examples

To turn around quickly
After realizing his mistake, he decided to turn around and apologize.
Please turn around so I can zip up the back of your dress.
She suddenly turned around when she heard her name called from behind.

Synonyms

  • turn round
    1.4 0.08/m
    turn round means moving your body to face the opposite way, while turn around also includes changing attitude or action.
  • swing around
    3.2 1.15/m
    swing around suggests turning suddenly to face the other way, while turn around can be slower or more general.
  • swing about
    4.8 0.08/m
    swing about means moving abruptly to face a different direction, while turn around can be gradual or figurative too.
  • about-face
    6.1 0.05/m
    about-face is a sudden 180-degree turn or a sharp change of opinion, while turn around is any turn or shift.

Related Terms

  • synonym: turn back - to reverse direction and go back
