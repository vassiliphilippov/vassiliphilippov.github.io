borrow

verb
take something temporarily with intent to return
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑːroʊ / (AmE), / ˈbɒrəʊ / (BrE)
to take and use something that belongs to someone else, intending to return it

Examples

To borrow a book
Can I borrow your notes for the lecture this afternoon?
He borrowed my umbrella and promised to return it tomorrow.
She often borrows books from the local library.
Can I borrow your phone for a moment to make a quick call?
He is always borrowing tools from his neighbors.
She forgot to return the book she had borrowed from the library.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lend - to give something to someone for a period of time, expecting it to be returned
