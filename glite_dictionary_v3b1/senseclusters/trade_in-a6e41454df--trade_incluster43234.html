trade in

verb
exchange old item as part payment for new
1.0
Pronunciation: / treɪd ɪn / (AmE), / treɪd ɪn / (BrE)
to give something you own, such as a car or appliance, to a dealer in part payment for something new

Examples

To trade in a car
He traded in his smartphone when his contract expired.
Many people trade in their cars every few years to get the latest features.
She decided to trade in her old laptop for a discount on a newer model.
