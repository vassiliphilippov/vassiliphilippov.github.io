mid vowel

noun
vowel at tongue's mid height
Pronunciation: / mɪd ˈvoʊ.əl / (AmE), / mɪd ˈvəʊ.əl / (BrE)
A vowel sound pronounced with the tongue positioned midway between a high (close) and a low (open) vowel.

Examples

A mid vowel sound
A mid vowel is articulated with the tongue halfway between its highest and lowest positions.
In many languages, the distinction between high, mid vowel, and low vowels is important.
The word contains a mid vowel between two consonants.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: high vowel - a vowel sound made with the tongue positioned high in the mouth
  • degree lower: low vowel - a vowel sound made with the tongue positioned low in the mouth
