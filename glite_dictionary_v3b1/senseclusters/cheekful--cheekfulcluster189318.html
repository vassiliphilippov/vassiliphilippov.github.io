cheekful

noun
mouthful in cheek
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃiːkfʊl / (AmE), / ˈtʃiːkfʊl / (BrE)
an amount that fills one cheek, especially of food or liquid held in the mouth

Examples

A cheekful of water
He took a cheekful of water before answering the question.
The squirrel stored a cheekful of nuts before running up the tree.
She tried to hide a cheekful of candy during the class.
