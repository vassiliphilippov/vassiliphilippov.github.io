gnomonic

adjective
concise and wise
Pronunciation: / noʊˈmɑːnɪk / (AmE), / nəʊˈmɒnɪk / (BrE)
expressed in short, wise, or aphoristic statements; concise and full of meaning

Examples

Gnomonic wisdom
Her speech was gnomonic, filled with brief but powerful insights.
The philosopher's gnomonic remarks stayed with the audience long after the lecture ended.
He admired the gnomonic style of ancient proverbs for their clarity and wisdom.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aphoristic - expressed in short, memorable statements
  • synonym: sententious - using wise or moral sayings, often in a concise way
  • antonym: verbose - using more words than needed
