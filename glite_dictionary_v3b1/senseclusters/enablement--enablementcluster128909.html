enablement

noun
supporting harmful behavior
7.5
Pronunciation: / ɪˈneɪbəlmənt / (AmE), / ɪˈneɪblmənt / (BrE)
the act of supporting or allowing someone's harmful or irresponsible behavior to continue, especially in situations involving addiction or abuse

Examples

Addiction enablement
Her constant excuses for his actions were a form of enablement that kept him from changing.
Family members sometimes struggle to recognize their own enablement of a loved one's addiction.
Therapists often discuss the dangers of enablement in support groups for families.

Related Terms

  • synonym: facilitation - the act of making something easier or helping it happen
  • antonym: intervention - the act of becoming involved to stop or change harmful behavior
