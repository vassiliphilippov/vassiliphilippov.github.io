superlative degree

noun
highest degree of adjective or adverb
3.3 3.3
Pronunciation: /sʊp.ˈɜːr.lə.tɪv dɪ.ˈgriː/ /; / /suː.ˈpɜːr.lə.tɪv dɪ.ˈgriː/ (AmE), /sjuː.ˈpɜː.lə.tɪv dɪ.ˈgriː/ /; / /suː.ˈpɜː.lə.tɪv dɪ.ˈgriː/ (BrE)
the form of an adjective or adverb that expresses the highest or most extreme degree of a quality, typically formed by adding '-est', 'most', or 'least'

Examples

The superlative degree form
In the sentence 'She is the smartest student in the class,' 'smartest' is in the superlative degree.
The superlative degree of 'good' is 'best.'
When describing three or more items, we often use the superlative degree to highlight the most extreme option.
