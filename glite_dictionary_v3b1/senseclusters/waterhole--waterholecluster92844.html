waterhole

noun
radio spectrum region
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtɚˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌhəʊl / (BrE)
a region of the radio spectrum between hydrogen and hydroxyl emissions, considered suitable for interstellar communication.

Examples

Signals in the waterhole
Scientists often search the waterhole for signals that might indicate intelligent life beyond Earth.
The waterhole is considered a promising frequency range for interstellar communication.
Radio telescopes are tuned to the waterhole when listening for possible extraterrestrial messages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: radio window - a range of radio frequencies that can pass through Earth's atmosphere
