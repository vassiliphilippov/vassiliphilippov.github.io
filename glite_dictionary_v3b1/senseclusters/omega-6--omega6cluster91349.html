omega-6

noun
type of polyunsaturated fatty acid
5.7 3.8
Pronunciation: /oʊ.ˈmeɪ.gə sɪks/ /; / /oʊ.ˈme.gə sɪks/ (AmE), /əʊ.ˈmiː.gə sɪks/ /; / /əʊ.ˈmɪ.gə sɪks/ (BrE)
a polyunsaturated fatty acid with its first double bond six carbons from the methyl end of the carbon chain, commonly found in plant oils and important in the human diet

Examples

Omega-6 fatty acid

Many vegetable oils are rich sources of omega-6 fatty acids, which are essential to human health.
Research suggests that omega-6 plays a role in both promoting and reducing inflammation.
