deepwater

adjective
very deep water
Pronunciation: / ˈdiːpˌwɑtər / (AmE), / ˈdiːpˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
describing water that is very deep, especially in oceans or large lakes

Examples

Deepwater fish
The submarine was designed for deepwater exploration far below the ocean's surface.
Many oil rigs are built to operate in deepwater locations off the coast.
Some species of fish can only survive in deepwater habitats where sunlight does not reach.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abyssal - relating to the deepest parts of the ocean
  • antonym: shallow - not deep; having little depth
