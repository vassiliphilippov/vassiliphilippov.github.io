unilaterally

adverb
by one side only; without others' agreement
Pronunciation: / ˌjuː.nəˈlæt̬.rə.li / (AmE), / ˌjuː.nɪˈlæt.rə.li / (BrE)
In a way that involves or is decided by only one person, group, or country, without the agreement or participation of others.

Examples

To unilaterally change terms
She unilaterally changed the terms of the contract without informing her partner.
The company announced unilaterally that the office would close early for the holiday.
The government decided to impose new tariffs unilaterally despite calls for negotiation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bilaterally - In a bilateral manner; by both sides or parties
