folkway

noun
traditional custom
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊkˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈfəʊkˌweɪ / (BrE)
a traditional custom or social rule that guides everyday behavior in a community or group, but is not strictly enforced

Examples

A folkway custom
Respecting elders is an important folkway in many Asian societies.
The village has its own folkways that have been followed for generations.
Learning about local folkways can help visitors understand the community better.
Greeting people with a handshake is a common folkway in many cultures.
Wearing casual clothes to a formal event may break a folkway but is not a crime.
In some countries, removing your shoes before entering a home is an important folkway.

Related Terms

  • synonym: custom - a traditional practice or usual way of doing something
  • antonym: law - a rule established by authority
  • synonym: norm - an accepted standard or way of behaving in a group
  • synonym: tradition - a belief or behavior passed down within a group or society
