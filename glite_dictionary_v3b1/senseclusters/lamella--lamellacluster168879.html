lamella

noun
a thin, leaflike structure in fungi
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / ləˈmɛlə / (AmE), / ləˈmiːlə / (BrE)
any of the thin, flat, radiating spore-producing structures found on the underside of the cap of a mushroom or similar fungus

Examples

Mushroom lamella
The lamella of the mushroom are responsible for the production of spores.
The cap's underside revealed closely packed lamella covered in budding spores.
When identifying wild mushrooms, carefully examine the color and spacing of the lamellae.
