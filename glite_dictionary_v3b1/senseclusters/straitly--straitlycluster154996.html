straitly

adverb
with hardship
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈstreɪtli / (AmE), / ˈstreɪtli / (BrE)
in a way that involves severe difficulty, hardship, or strictness; with great distress or urgency (archaic).

Examples

Straitly living in poverty
After the harvest failed, the family lived straitly, unable to buy even basic supplies.
The law was straitly enforced, leaving no room for exceptions.
During the famine, many villagers were straitly pressed for food and shelter.

Related Terms

  • antonym: easily - without difficulty or hardship
  • synonym: severely - in a very difficult or harsh way
