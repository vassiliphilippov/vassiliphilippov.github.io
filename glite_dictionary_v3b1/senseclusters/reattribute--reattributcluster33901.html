reattribute

verb
assign to another source
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˌriː.əˈtrɪ.bjuːt / (AmE), / ˌriː.əˈtrɪ.bjuːt / (BrE)
to assign or credit something to a different person, source, or cause than before

Examples

To reattribute success
After further research, the success of the project was reattributed to the engineering team rather than the marketing department.
Scholars decided to reattribute the manuscript's authorship based on recent findings.
The painting was reattributed to a different artist after new evidence came to light.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attribute - to regard something as belonging to or caused by a person or thing
