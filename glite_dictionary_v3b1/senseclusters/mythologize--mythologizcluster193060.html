mythologize

verb
to make or treat as a myth
2.6
Pronunciation: / mɪˈθɑːlədʒaɪz /; / mɪˈθɑl.əˌdʒaɪz /; / mɪˈθɑlədʒaɪz / (AmE), / mɪˈθɒlədʒaɪz /; / mɪˈθɒl.ə.dʒaɪz / (BrE)
to make something into a myth or treat it as legendary, often by exaggerating or idealizing it

Examples

To mythologize a story
Historians warn against attempts to mythologize national leaders of the past.
Over time, people began to mythologize his life story, turning ordinary events into legends.
The new biography tends to mythologize the achievements of the artist, ignoring her flaws.
Over time, people began to mythologize his achievements, turning him into a cultural icon.
The film helped to mythologize the life of the famous musician.
Writers often mythologize historical events to make them more compelling.
It is easy to mythologise the past when you ignore its problems.
Some authors mythologise history to make their stories more appealing.
The film tends to mythologise the life of its main character.

Related Terms

  • antonym: debunk - to expose the falseness or hollowness of a myth, idea, or belief
  • antonym: demythologize - to remove mythical elements from a narrative or belief
  • synonym: mythologize - create or popularise a myth (American spelling)
  • synonym: romanticize - to make something more appealing or interesting than it really is
