white paper

noun
official government report
Pronunciation: / waɪt ˈpeɪpɚ /; / waɪt ˈpeɪpə / (AmE), / waɪt ˈpeɪpə / (BrE)
an official, detailed report published by a government, company, or organization to explain information, proposals, or technical details about a specific issue

Examples

The white paper report
Before passing the new law, officials consulted the recommendations in the white paper.
The white paper details the environmental impact of expanding public transportation.
The government released a white paper outlining proposed changes to healthcare policy yesterday.
The startup released a technical white paper explaining its blockchain consensus algorithm and token economics.
Investors read the project's white paper carefully before deciding to fund the new decentralized app.
The consulting firm published a detailed white paper describing best practices for secure cloud deployments.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: green paper - a preliminary government report presenting proposed policy; issued for discussion rather than final proposal
  • synonym: position paper - a statement of an organisation's policies or arguments on a particular issue
  • synonym: technical report - a document that describes technical research, methods, or results
