tch

noun
spelling for /tʃ/ sound
9.9
Pronunciation: / tiː tiːʃ / (AmE), / tiː tiːʃ / (BrE)
the sequence of three letters 'tch' used in English spelling to represent the /tʃ/ sound, especially after a short vowel

Examples

The tch in 'match'
In the word 'match', the tch at the end represents the /tʃ/ sound.
English words like 'catch' and 'witch' use tch after a short vowel.
Teachers often explain when to use tch instead of 'ch' in spelling lessons.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ch - the two-letter sequence representing the /tʃ/ sound in English
