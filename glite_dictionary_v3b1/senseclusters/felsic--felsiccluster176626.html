felsic

noun
silica-rich rock
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛlsɪk / (AmE), / ˈfɛlsɪk / (BrE)
a light-colored igneous rock or magma that is high in silica and rich in minerals such as quartz and feldspar

Examples

A felsic sample
The geologist identified the sample as a felsic based on its light color and mineral content.
Many continental crust rocks are classified as felsic due to their high silica levels.
During the eruption, the volcano released both felsic and mafic materials.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mafic - a dark-colored igneous rock or magma low in silica and rich in magnesium and iron
