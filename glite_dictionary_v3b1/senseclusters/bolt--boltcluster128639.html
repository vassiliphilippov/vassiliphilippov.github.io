bolt

noun
sliding bar in a firearm breech
7.0 7.2
Pronunciation: /boʊlt/ (AmE), /bəʊlt/ /; / /bɒlt/ (BrE)
a sliding bar in a breech-loading firearm that ejects an empty cartridge, replaces it with a new one, and closes the breech

Examples

A rifle bolt
A malfunctioning bolt can render a firearm inoperable until it is repaired.
After firing, the shooter pulled back the bolt to eject the spent casing.
He examined the bolt of his rifle to make sure it was working smoothly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: breechblock - the part of a firearm that closes the rear of the barrel
