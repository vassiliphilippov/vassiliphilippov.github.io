temporization

noun
delaying decision
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛmpərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtɛmpəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of delaying or avoiding making a decision or taking action, often to gain time or avoid commitment

Examples

Political temporization
The manager's temporization led to missed opportunities for the company.
Her constant temporization annoyed her friends, who wanted a clear plan.
Instead of making a decision, he resorted to temporization to avoid conflict.
The manager's temporization frustrated employees who wanted a clear decision on the project.
Instead of making a final choice, the board resorted to temporization to avoid immediate conflict.
Critics accused the government of temporization rather than addressing the issue directly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decisiveness - the ability to make decisions quickly and confidently
  • synonym: expediency - the use of quick or temporary solutions for immediate advantage
  • synonym: procrastination - the act of delaying something that must be done
