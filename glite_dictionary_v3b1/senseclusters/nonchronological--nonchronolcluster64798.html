nonchronological

adjective
not time-ordered
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˌkrɑnəˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌkrɒnəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
not arranged in the order in which events happened in time

Examples

Nonchronological order
The documentary follows a nonchronological sequence, jumping between different years in the subject's life.
Her essay was confusing because it was written in a nonchronological order.
Many modern films use nonchronological storytelling to create suspense and surprise.

Related Terms

  • antonym: chronological - arranged in the order in which events happened in time
