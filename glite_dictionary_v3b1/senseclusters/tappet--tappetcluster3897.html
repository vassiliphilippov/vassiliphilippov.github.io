tappet

noun
mechanical lever moving another part
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtæpɪt / (AmE), / ˈtæpɪt / (BrE)
A lever or rod in a machine, especially an engine, that moves up and down to open or close a valve or perform a similar function by tapping or striking another part.

Examples

Engine tappet
A worn tappet can cause unusual noises in your car's engine.
Modern engines use hydraulic tappets to maintain proper valve clearance automatically.
The mechanic adjusted the tappet to ensure optimal engine performance.
