California fern

noun
poisonous plant with fernlike leaves
6.2 8.3
Pronunciation: /kæl.ə.ˈfɔːr.njə fɜːrn/ /; / /kæl.ɪ.ˈfɔːr.njə fɜːrn/ (AmE), /kæl.ɪ.ˈfɔː.niː.ə fɜːn/ /; / /kæl.ə.ˈfɔː.niː.ə fɜːn/ (BrE)
A large, branching biennial plant native to Eurasia and Africa, also found in North America, with fernlike leaves, white flowers, and all parts extremely poisonous.

Examples

The California fern plant
Despite its name, the California fern is not a true fern but resembles one in appearance.
The leaves of the California fern are beautiful but potentially deadly if ingested.
You can sometimes find California fern growing in moist, shaded areas along the coast.
