lysolecithin

noun
emulsifying agent
9.9
Pronunciation: /laɪ.soʊ.ˈles.ɪ.θɪn/ (AmE), /laɪ.əʊ.ˈles.ɪ.θɪn/ (BrE)
a surfactant or emulsifier used in foods, cosmetics, and industry to help mix oil and water

Examples

Lysolecithin as emulsifier
The salad dressing uses lysolecithin to keep the oil and vinegar from separating.
Cosmetic creams often contain lysolecithin to create a smooth, stable texture.
Industrial cleaners may include lysolecithin to help dissolve oily residues.
