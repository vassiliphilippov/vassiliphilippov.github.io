unmethylated

adjective
without methyl group
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnˈmɛθəˌleɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈmiːθəleɪtɪd / (BrE)
not having a methyl group attached, especially referring to DNA or other molecules in chemistry or biology

Examples

Unmethylated DNA regions
The scientist analyzed the unmethylated regions of the DNA to study gene expression.
Certain genes remain unmethylated to allow for active transcription in the cell.
The presence of unmethylated molecules can indicate a specific biological process is occurring.
