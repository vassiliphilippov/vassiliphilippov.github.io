Louis de Broglie

proper noun
French physicist; wave-particle duality pioneer
Pronunciation: / luˈi də ˈbroɪ / (AmE), / luːˈiː də ˈbrɔɪ / (BrE)
A French physicist (1892–1987) who pioneered the theory of wave-particle duality in quantum mechanics, proposing that matter exhibits both wave and particle properties.

Examples

Louis de Broglie theory
Einstein praised the innovative ideas of Louis de Broglie regarding wave-particle duality.
Modern quantum mechanics owes much to the early work of Louis de Broglie.
The groundbreaking theory developed by Louis de Broglie changed our understanding of particles at the atomic scale.
