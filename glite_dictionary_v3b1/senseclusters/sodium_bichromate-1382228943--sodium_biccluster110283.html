sodium bichromate

noun
red-orange salt used as a mordant
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊdiəm baɪˈkroʊˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsəʊdiəm baɪˈkrəʊmeɪt / (BrE)
a red-orange crystalline salt (Na2Cr2O7) used as a mordant and in various industrial processes

Examples

Sodium bichromate salt
Exposure to sodium bichromate requires strict safety precautions due to its toxicity.
Historically, sodium bichromate played a key role in leather tanning processes.
The textile industry commonly uses sodium bichromate as a mordant in dyeing fabrics.
