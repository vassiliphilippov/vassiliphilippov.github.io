neuromuscular blocking agent

noun
drug that blocks nerve-muscle transmission
3.1 4.8
Pronunciation: / ˌnuːroʊˌmʌskjələr ˈblɑːkɪŋ ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəʊˌmʌskjʊlə ˈblɒkɪŋ ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a drug or substance that interferes with signal transmission between nerves and skeletal muscles, causing muscle relaxation or paralysis

Examples

A neuromuscular blocking agent drug
Before surgery, the physician administered a neuromuscular blocking agent to relax the patient's muscles.
Some neuromuscular blocking agents must be carefully dosed to avoid prolonged muscle paralysis.
Use of a neuromuscular blocking agent can facilitate endotracheal intubation during anesthesia.

Related Terms

  • synonym: muscle relaxant - a drug that reduces muscle tension or spasms
