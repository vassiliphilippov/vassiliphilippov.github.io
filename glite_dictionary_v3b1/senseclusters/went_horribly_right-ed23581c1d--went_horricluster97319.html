went horribly right

verb
unexpectedly successful outcome
Pronunciation: / wɛnt ˈhɔrəbli raɪt / (AmE), / wɛnt ˈhɒrəbli raɪt / (BrE)
(of an event or situation) to have an unexpectedly positive or successful outcome, often when failure was expected.

Examples

Her speech went horribly right
Her improvised speech at the event went horribly right and led to several new job offers.
The software update went horribly right, fixing more issues than anyone expected.
What was supposed to be a small prank went horribly right and made everyone laugh for hours.
