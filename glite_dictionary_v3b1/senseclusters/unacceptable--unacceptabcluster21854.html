not good enough, not allowed, or not meeting standards; not acceptable

Examples

Unacceptable behaviour
It is unacceptable for students to submit their assignments late without a valid reason.
Such rude behavior is simply unacceptable in this restaurant.
The company's proposal was deemed unacceptable by the board of directors.
Her rude comments at the dinner table were considered unacceptable by everyone present.
It is unacceptable to ignore the rules of the workplace.
The manager found his repeated lateness to be unacceptable.
Her proposal contained terminology that was unacceptable to the editing committee.
The use of double negatives was once considered unacceptable in formal English.
Their behavior at the ceremony was deemed unacceptable by traditional standards.

Synonyms

  • not acceptable
    2.2 2.21/m
    not acceptable is a direct phrase often used in speech, while unacceptable is the single-word adjective seen in writing and speech.
  • unsatisfactory
    3.9 2.20/m
    unsatisfactory describes work or quality that fails a standard, while unacceptable covers both poor quality and actions that are not allowed.
  • inadequate
    4.4 14/m
    inadequate means not enough or lacking what is needed, while unacceptable can mean either not allowed or failing a standard.
  • impermissible
    7.7 0.60/m
    impermissible is a formal term for actions forbidden by rules, while unacceptable also covers poor quality as well as rule breaches.
  • unallowable
    6.4 0.01/m
    unallowable means clearly not permitted under rules or law, while unacceptable can also mean bad quality or socially wrong.
  • unfit
    3.7 2.31/m
    unfit describes someone or something not suitable or safe for a task, while unacceptable is broader and covers many faults.
  • unsuitable
    2.6 1.71/m
    unsuitable means not right for a purpose or person, while unacceptable can also mean forbidden or cause strong disapproval.
  • inappropriate
    3.7 21/m
    inappropriate means not proper for a situation or time, while unacceptable can mean either improper behavior or poor quality.
  • improper
    4.1 5.88/m
    improper means not following accepted rules or morals, while unacceptable can be used for both rule-breaking and low quality.
  • objectionable
    6.7 3.10/m
    objectionable causes offense or protest and seems morally wrong, while unacceptable is a general word for what people reject.
  • unseemly
    7.8 1.87/m
    unseemly describes behavior that looks rude or indecent in public, while unacceptable covers more general faults or bans.
  • intolerable
    4.9 3.30/m
    intolerable describes something too bad to accept or endure, while unacceptable can mean poor or simply not allowed.
