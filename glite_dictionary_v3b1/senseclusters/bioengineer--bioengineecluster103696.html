bioengineer

noun
biological systems engineer
5.5
Pronunciation: /baɪ.oʊ.en.dʒɪ.ˈnɪr/ /; / /baɪ.oʊ.ən.dʒɪ.ˈnɪr/ (AmE), /baɪ.əʊ.en.dʒɪ.ˈnɪə/ /; / /baɪ.əʊ.ən.dʒɪ.ˈnɪə/ (BrE)
a person who applies engineering principles to biological systems to solve medical or biological problems

Examples

A skilled bioengineer
A team of bioengineers created an artificial organ to help patients awaiting transplants.
Many bioengineers work in research labs developing new medical technologies.
The university offers a degree program for aspiring bioengineers interested in healthcare innovation.
The bioengineer developed a new method for growing artificial organs in the lab.
As a bioengineer, she works on improving medical implants for patients.
A team led by a bioengineer created bacteria that can clean up oil spills.
