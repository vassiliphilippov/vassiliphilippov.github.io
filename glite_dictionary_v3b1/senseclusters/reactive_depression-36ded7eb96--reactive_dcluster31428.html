reactive depression

noun
depression caused by life events
4.0
Pronunciation: / riˈæktɪv dɪˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / riˈæktɪv dɪˈprɛʃən / (BrE)
depression that is triggered by stressful life events, distinguished from depression with no clear external cause

Examples

Reactive depression after loss
After losing his job, he developed reactive depression and struggled to find motivation.
Therapists may distinguish reactive depression from normal grief in their diagnosis.
Unlike major depressive disorder, reactive depression often arises after a specific traumatic event.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clinical depression - depression not necessarily caused by external events
