fly

verb
move or travel through the air
Pronunciation: / flaɪ / (AmE), / flaɪ / (BrE)
to move through the air using wings, or to travel through the air in an aircraft or as an object

Examples

Birds fly south
Birds fly south for the winter every year.
She dreams that she can fly above the city at night.
The new drone can fly for over thirty minutes on a single charge.
He flied the ball deep to left, but the outfielder made the catch.
She flies to right field almost every time she's up to bat.
The batter managed to fly out to center field in the seventh inning.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fall - to drop or come down freely
  • antonym: ground - to hit a ball along the ground in baseball
  • synonym: soar - to fly or rise high in the air
