restrictor

noun
domain-specifying phrase
9.9
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈstrɪk.tər/ /; / /rə.ˈstrɪk.tər/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈstrɪk.tə/ /; / /rə.ˈstrɪk.tə/ (BrE)
in linguistics and logic, the part of a quantifier or determiner phrase that specifies the set or domain to which the quantifier applies

Examples

Quantifier restrictor
In the phrase 'some dogs bark', 'dogs' is the restrictor for the quantifier 'some'.
Linguists analyze the restrictor to understand how quantifiers limit meaning in sentences.
The restrictor helps define the group to which a statement applies in formal logic.

  • synonym: scope - the part of a sentence affected by a quantifier or operator
