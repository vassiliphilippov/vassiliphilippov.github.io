transvaluation

noun
official value change
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌtrænzvæl.juˈeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌtrænzvæl.juˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
an official change in the recorded monetary value of a currency, asset, or price level, usually made by a government or financial authority

Examples

Currency transvaluation
The central bank announced a transvaluation of the national currency to stabilize the economy.
After the transvaluation of assets, the company's balance sheet showed a significant increase in value.
International investors closely monitored the government's decision on transvaluation policies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: revaluation - an increase in the value of a currency or asset
