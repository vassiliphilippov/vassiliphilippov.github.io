second joint

noun
upper joint of a fowl's leg
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛk.ənd dʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈsɛk.ənd dʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
the upper joint of the leg of a fowl, located between the drumstick and the body, often referred to in culinary contexts

Examples

The second joint of fowl
Many people prefer the tender meat found in the second joint of the fowl.
The recipe calls for two second joints and two drumsticks from a chicken.
When cutting up the chicken, I separated the second joint from the rest of the leg.
