threeness

noun
state of being three
Pronunciation: / ˈθriːnəs / (AmE), / ˈθriːnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being three or composed of three parts

Examples

The threeness of design
The threeness of the design is seen in its three distinct sections.
Many stories use threeness as a structural element, such as three wishes or three trials.
In mathematics, threeness can refer to the property of a set containing exactly three elements.

Related Terms

  • antonym: oneness - the quality of being one or single
  • synonym: trinity - a group of three or the state of being threefold
  • degree lower: twoness - the quality or state of being two
