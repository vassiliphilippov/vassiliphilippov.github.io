slow

verb
make something move or progress more slowly
Pronunciation: / sloʊ / (AmE), / sləʊ / (BrE)
to make something move or happen at a lower speed, or to become slower

Examples

To slow traffic
Heavy traffic may slow your journey to work.
Medication can slow the progression of the disease.
The manager asked if we could slow production until demand increased.
After a burst of activity, her progress noticeably slowed.
Sales began to slow as the holiday season ended.
The train started to slow as it approached the station.
Heavy rain can slow traffic considerably during rush hour.
The car began to slow as it approached the intersection.
You should slow down when driving in bad weather.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decelerate - to reduce speed; to slow down
  • antonym: hasten - to make something go faster
  • antonym: speed up - to move or happen faster
