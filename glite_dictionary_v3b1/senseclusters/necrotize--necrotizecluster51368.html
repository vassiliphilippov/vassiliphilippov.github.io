necrotize

verb
to cause tissue death
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛkrəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnɛkrəˌtaɪz /; / ˈnɛkrətaɪz / (BrE)
to cause tissue or cells to die or to undergo death (necrosis), usually because of disease or injury

Examples

To necrotize tissue
If left untreated, the infection can quickly necrotize surrounding tissues.
Doctors acted fast to prevent the wound from beginning to necrotize after the accident.
Some toxins released by bacteria may necrotize skin and muscle cells.
If not treated quickly, the bacteria can necrotize large areas of skin.
Doctors worked to remove the necrotized tissue to prevent the infection from spreading.
Certain toxins may necrotize muscle cells after a snake bite.
If left untreated, the infected tissue may necrotize and require surgical removal.
Doctors monitored the wound closely to ensure it did not necrotize after the operation.
Frostbite can cause skin and muscle to necrotize in extreme cold conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decay - to rot or decompose through natural processes
  • synonym: gangrene - the death of body tissue due to lack of blood flow or infection
  • antonym: heal - to make or become healthy again
