combined

adjective
made or joined into one
3.7
Pronunciation: / kəmˈbaɪnd / (AmE), / kəmˈbaɪnd / (BrE)
made or formed by joining two or more things or people together into a single unit; united.

Examples

Combined experience
Her combined experience in law and finance made her a valuable advisor.
The combined efforts of the team led to their success.
The two companies launched a combined marketing campaign that reached millions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: separate - existing or kept apart; not joined or united
  • synonym: united - joined together for a common purpose or by common feelings
