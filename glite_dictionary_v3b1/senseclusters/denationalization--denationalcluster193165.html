denationalization

noun
transfer from state to private control
7.4 6.9
Pronunciation: /diː.næ.ʃən.əl.ɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /diː.næ.ʃən.əl.ɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
The process of transferring ownership or control of an industry, business, service, or asset from the government to private individuals, companies, or other private hands.

Examples

Denationalization of industry
Critics argued that denationalization would not benefit ordinary citizens.
Several countries have undergone denationalization since the 1980s to encourage private investment.
The denationalization of public utilities led to major changes in service delivery.
Debates about denationalisation often center on the efficiency and accountability of services.
Following the denationalisation policy, several state-owned companies were sold to private enterprises.
The denationalisation of the railway system led to increased private investment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nationalisation - the transfer of private assets to state ownership
  • antonym: nationalization - the transfer of a major branch of industry or commerce from private to state ownership or control
  • synonym: privatization - the transfer of a business or industry from public to private ownership or control
