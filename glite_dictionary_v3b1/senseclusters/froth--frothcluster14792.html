froth

verb
to form bubbles or foam
7.9 2.6
Pronunciation: /frɔːθ/ /; / /frɑːθ/ (AmE), /frɒθ/ (BrE)
to form or produce a mass of small bubbles (to foam or become bubbly)

Examples

To froth at the top
As the sea grew rougher, the waves began to froth along the shore.
If you shake the bottle vigorously, the liquid will froth up quickly.
The bartender made the milk froth before pouring it into the coffee.
She accidentally made the milk froth over the sides of the cup.
The angry man was frothing at the mouth as he yelled at the referee.
The chemical mixture began to froth violently when heated.
Sugar makes the mixture froth when you add the yeast.
The milk began to froth as it was heated for the cappuccino.
The waves frothed angrily against the rocky shore during the storm.
