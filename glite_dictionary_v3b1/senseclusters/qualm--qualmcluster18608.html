qualm

noun
feeling of doubt or uneasiness
8.4 2.30/m
Pronunciation: / kw…ëm / (AmE), / kw…ëÀêm / (BrE)
a feeling of uneasiness or doubt, especially about whether one is doing the right thing

Examples

Qualms about lying
Despite his qualms, he went along with the risky plan.
He had no qualm about speaking his mind openly in the meeting.
She felt a qualm about lying to her friend, but did it anyway.

Synonyms

  • scruple
    8.4 1.02/m
    scruple is a moral hesitation about right and wrong, while qualm is a general uneasy doubt about doing something.
  • misgiving
    5.9 2.48/m

  • doubt
    1.6 87/m
    doubt is a lack of certainty about facts or beliefs, while qualm is an uneasy, moral worry about actions.
