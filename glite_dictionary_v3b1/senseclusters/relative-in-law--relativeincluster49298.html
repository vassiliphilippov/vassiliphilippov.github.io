relative-in-law

noun
a person related by marriage
Pronunciation: / ɹəˈlɛtɪv ɪn lɔ / (AmE), / rɪˈlɛtɪv ɪn lɔː / (BrE)
A person who is related to someone by marriage rather than by blood, such as a mother-in-law or brother-in-law.

Examples

Relatives-in-law at holidays
After the wedding, she had to get used to spending holidays with her new relatives-in-law.
He invited his relatives-in-law to the family reunion along with his parents and siblings.
Some people stay close to their relatives-in-law even after a divorce.
