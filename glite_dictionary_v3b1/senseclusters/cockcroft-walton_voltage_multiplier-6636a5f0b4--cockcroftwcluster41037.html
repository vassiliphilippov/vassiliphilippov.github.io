Cockcroft-Walton voltage multiplier

noun
device that multiplies voltage using capacitors and rectifiers
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑk.krɑft ˈwɔl.tən ˈvoʊl.tɪdʒ məlˌtɪ.plaɪ.ər / (AmE), / ˈkɒk.krɒft ˈwɔl.tən ˈvəʊl.tɪdʒ ˌmʌl.tɪˈplaɪ.ə / (BrE)
a device that uses a network of capacitors and rectifiers to generate high voltages, commonly used to accelerate charged particles in physics experiments
