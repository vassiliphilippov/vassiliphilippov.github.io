Malachi

proper noun
Hebrew prophet; author of biblical book
Pronunciation: / ˈmæləˌkaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmaləkʌɪ / (BrE)
a Hebrew prophet of the 5th century BC, regarded as the author of the last book of the Old Testament in the Hebrew Bible.

Examples

Malachi the prophet
Malachi is considered the last of the minor prophets in the Hebrew Bible.
Many scholars debate the exact historical period in which Malachi lived and preached.
The Book of Malachi addresses the spiritual and moral state of Israel after the exile.
