underhand

adjective, adverb
deceptive or secretive
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndərˌhænd /; / ˌʌndɚˈhænd / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌhænd /; / ˌʌndəˈhænd / (BrE)
in a secret, dishonest, or sly way; intended to deceive or cheat

Examples

Underhand trick
Her underhand tactics at work earned her few friends among colleagues.
The politician denied using any underhand methods during the election campaign.
They won the contract through underhand dealings that eventually came to light.
Politicians sometimes make underhand deals to secure their positions.
She acted underhand to get the promotion, going behind her colleague's back.
The company won the contract underhand, using insider information.

Related Terms

  • synonym: covertly - in a way that is not openly acknowledged or displayed
  • synonym: deceptive - intended to mislead or trick
  • antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere
  • antonym: openly - in a way that is not secret or hidden
