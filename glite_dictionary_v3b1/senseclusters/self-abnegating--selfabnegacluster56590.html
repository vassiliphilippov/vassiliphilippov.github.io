self-abnegating

adjective
denying or sacrificing one's own interests
6.9 7.9
Pronunciation: /self ˈæb.nɪ.geɪ.tɪŋ/ (AmE), /self ˈæb.nɪ.geɪ.tɪŋ/ (BrE)
Tending to deny or sacrifice one's own interests, needs, or desires, especially for the sake of others.

Examples

Self-abnegating behavior
He was admired for his self-abnegating devotion to the welfare of the community.
Her self-abnegating attitude led her to always put her family's needs ahead of her own.
In a self-abnegating gesture, he declined all credit for the success of the project.

Related Terms

  • antonym: self-assertive - confidently asserting one's own rights or needs
  • synonym: self-denying - restraining oneself from indulgence
  • synonym: self-sacrificing - willing to give up personal interests for others
