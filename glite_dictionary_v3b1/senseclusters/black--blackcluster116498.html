black

adjective
(face) darkened with blood or emotion
Pronunciation: / blæk / (AmE), / blak / (BrE)
covered in dirt, soot, or something that makes it appear dark or dirty

Examples

Black hands
He stormed into the room, his face black after the insult.
Her face became black with fury when she heard the news.
The child's face turned black with anger during the argument.
After a long day working in the garden, his hands were black with soil.
She came home from the campsite with her clothes black from the fire pit.
The chimney sweep's face was black after cleaning the old fireplace.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clean - free from dirt, marks, or stains
  • synonym: dirty - covered or marked with dirt; not clean
  • synonym: livid - (of a face) dark bluish-grey color, as from anger or bruising
