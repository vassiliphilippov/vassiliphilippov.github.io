meet

verb
become adjacent or join together
1.0 18/m
Pronunciation: / mit / (AmE), / miÀêt / (BrE)
to come together or join at a point (of lines, objects, or people collecting in one place)

Examples

Two roads meet
At the corner, the walls meet to form a right angle.
The rails meet at the station creating a junction.
The two rivers meet just north of the city.
Let's meet at the library to work on our project.
The committee will meet in the conference room at noon.
We all agreed to meet in the park for the picnic.

Synonyms

  • come together
    2.1 2.38/m
    come together means moving toward each other to meet, often physically or in an embrace, while meet is a general coming together.
  • converge
    5.5 4.92/m
    converge means coming together from different directions to a single point, while meet is a more general word for coming together.
  • gather
    1.0 65/m
    gather means collecting people or things into one place, often by choice, while meet is simply coming together.
  • assemble
    3.3 11/m
    assemble means bringing people or parts together often for a planned purpose, while meet can be sudden or unplanned.
  • congregate
    4.1 2.55/m
    congregate means people gather in a group, usually for a set purpose, while meet covers any coming together.
  • join
    1.0 59/m
    join means connect or become united, often forming a single thing, while meet focuses on coming together at a point.

Related Terms

  • synonym: converge - to come together at a point
  • antonym: disperse - to scatter or move apart in different directions
  • antonym: diverge - to move apart from a common point
  • synonym: gather - to come or bring together in one group or place
