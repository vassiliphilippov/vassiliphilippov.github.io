chemosynthesis

noun
production of organic compounds using chemical energy
Pronunciation: / ˌkiːmoʊˈsɪnθəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌkiːməʊˈsɪnθəsɪs / (BrE)
the production of organic compounds, especially carbohydrates, from carbon dioxide and water using energy derived from chemical reactions rather than sunlight, occurring in certain bacteria and fungi

Examples

Chemosynthesis in bacteria
Certain deep-sea bacteria rely on chemosynthesis instead of sunlight to produce food.
The process of chemosynthesis is crucial in ecosystems near hydrothermal vents.
Unlike photosynthesis, chemosynthesis uses chemical reactions to obtain energy for carbohydrate production.

Related Terms

  • antonym: photosynthesis - production of organic compounds using sunlight
