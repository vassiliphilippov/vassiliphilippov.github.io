prohibit

verb
formally forbid; make illegal
3.7
Pronunciation: / pɹəˈhɪbɪt / (AmE), / prəˈhɪbɪt / (BrE)
to officially forbid something by law, rule, or authority

Examples

To prohibit dangerous actions
Her doctor prohibited strenuous exercise until her shoulder healed.
The law prohibits the use of certain chemicals in food production.
The school prohibits smoking on its premises.

Related Terms

  • antonym: allow - to let someone do something
  • synonym: forbid - to order someone not to do something; refuse to allow
  • antonym: permit - to allow something to happen
