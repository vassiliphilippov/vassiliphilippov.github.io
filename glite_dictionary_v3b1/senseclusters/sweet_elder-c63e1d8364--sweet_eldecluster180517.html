sweet elder

noun
North American elder shrub with edible berries
Pronunciation: / swit ˈɛldɚ / (AmE), / swiːt ˈɛldə / (BrE)
a North American elder shrub (Sambucus canadensis) that bears purple-black berries used in wines and jellies

Examples

The sweet elder shrub
Farmers planted sweet elder along the fence row to harvest its fruit every summer.
Jellies made from sweet elder have a rich, distinctive flavor.
The sweet elder is prized for its berries, often used to make homemade wine.
