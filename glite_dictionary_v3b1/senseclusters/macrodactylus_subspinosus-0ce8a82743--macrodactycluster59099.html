Macrodactylus subspinosus

noun
species of North American beetle
Pronunciation: / ˌmækroʊˌdækˈtaɪləs sʌbˌspaɪˈsoʊnəs / (AmE), / ˌmakrəʊˌdækˈtɪləs sʌbˌspaɪˈsəʊnəs / (BrE)
A species of North American beetle whose larvae feed on roots and whose adults feed on the leaves and flowers of plants such as rose bushes, apple trees, and grape vines.

Examples

Macrodactylus subspinosus beetle
Gardeners often recognize Macrodactylus subspinosus by its yellowish body and affinity for rose bushes.

The larvae of Macrodactylus subspinosus can cause significant damage to plant roots in gardens.
