shoptalk

noun
work-related conversation
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɑpˌtɔk / (AmE), / ˈʃɒpˌtɔːk / (BrE)
conversation or language about work that uses technical terms or jargon understood mainly by people in the same field

Examples

Shoptalk at work
The doctors tried to avoid shoptalk at the dinner table so their families could join the conversation.
After the meeting, the managers engaged in some shoptalk about the latest industry trends.
She found the constant shoptalk at parties boring because she was not in the same profession.
The engineers quickly slipped into shoptalk that was hard for outsiders to follow.
During lunch, the doctors avoided shoptalk and discussed their hobbies instead.
Her book explains legal concepts without using confusing shoptalk.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jargon - special words or expressions used by a particular profession
  • antonym: plain language - language that is clear and easy to understand
  • degree lower: slang - very informal language used by a particular group
  • antonym: small talk - casual conversation about unimportant topics
