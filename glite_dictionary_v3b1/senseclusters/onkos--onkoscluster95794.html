onkos

noun
high headdress in Greek tragedy
Pronunciation: / ˈɑn.kəs / (AmE), / ˈɒŋ.kɒs / (BrE)
A high, ornate headdress worn by actors in ancient Greek tragedy to add height and prominence.

Examples

The onkos headdress
Historians debate how the onkos influenced the visual impact of Greek theater performances.
The actor wore a tall onkos as part of his elaborate costume.
To portray the gods on stage, performers often donned an onkos to appear more imposing.
