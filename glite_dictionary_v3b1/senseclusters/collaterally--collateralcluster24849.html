collaterally

adverb
by collateral relation
Pronunciation: / kəˈlæt̬ɚrəli / (AmE), / kəˈlæt.ər.əli / (BrE)
by means of collateral lines of relation, especially in inheritance, rather than by direct descent

Examples

Inherited collaterally
The property was inherited collaterally by his nephew after he died without children.
In some legal systems, assets may pass collaterally to relatives outside the direct family line.
The title was transferred collaterally to a cousin when no direct heirs could be found.

  • antonym: directly - in a direct line of descent or relation
