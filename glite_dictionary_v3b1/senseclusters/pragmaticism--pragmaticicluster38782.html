pragmaticism

noun
Peirce's philosophy
Pronunciation: / præɡˈmætɪsɪzəm / (AmE), / præɡˈmætɪsɪzəm / (BrE)
a philosophical theory developed by Charles Peirce as a more precise form of pragmatism, emphasizing logical analysis of concepts

Examples

Pragmaticism theory
Charles Peirce introduced pragmaticism to distinguish his ideas from other forms of pragmatism.
The seminar explored the main differences between pragmaticism and classical pragmatism.
Many scholars study pragmaticism to better understand Peirce's contributions to philosophy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pragmatism - a philosophical approach focusing on practical consequences and real effects
