antireform

noun
opponent of reform
Pronunciation: / ˌæntaɪrɪˈfɔrm / (AmE), / ˌæntɪrɪˈfɔːm / (BrE)
a person who opposes reforms or changes in policies, institutions, or social practices

Examples

An antireform leader
The antireform argued that the new policies would harm traditional values.
Many antireform activists attended the rally to voice their opposition to the changes.
As an outspoken antireform, she published articles against the proposed education reforms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conservative - a person who resists change and prefers traditional views
  • antonym: reformer - a person who supports or works for change and improvement
