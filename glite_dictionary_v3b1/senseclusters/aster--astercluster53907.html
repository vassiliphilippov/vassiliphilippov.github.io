aster

noun
star-shaped cell structure in mitosis
Pronunciation: / ˈæstər / (AmE), / ˈɑːstə / (BrE)
a star-shaped structure in the cytoplasm of a cell, consisting of radiating fibers that form around the centrosome during cell division (mitosis)

Examples

Cell aster formation
During mitosis, an aster helps to organize the spindle fibers.
Scientists observed irregular asters in the mutant cells under the microscope.
The formation of the aster is essential for proper chromosome separation.
