Entomostraca

noun
obsolete group of small crustaceans
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɛntəˈmɑstrəkə / (AmE), / ˌɛntəməˈstrɑːkə /; / ˌɛntəˈmɒstrəkə / (BrE)
(in older zoology) a former group of small crustaceans (including branchiopods, copepods, ostracods, and barnacles), no longer used in modern classification

Examples

The Entomostraca group
In nineteenth-century zoology, Entomostraca was regarded as a major subclass of crustaceans.

The group Entomostraca is now considered obsolete, but it once encompassed a wide variety of small aquatic animals.
