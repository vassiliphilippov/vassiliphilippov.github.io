overcorrect

verb
to adjust too much
6.8
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.kə.ˈrekt/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.kə.ˈrekt/ (BrE)
to make a correction or adjustment that is too extreme, causing a new problem or going past the intended balance

Examples

To overcorrect a turn
When drivers start to skid, they sometimes overcorrect and lose control of the car.
In trying to fix the error, the editor overcorrected and introduced new mistakes.
Teachers should avoid overcorrecting students, as it can discourage participation.
When the car started to skid, he overcorrected and lost control of the vehicle.
If you overcorrect your posture, you might end up with back pain in a different area.
She realized she had overcorrected her mistake and needed to find a better balance.
If you overcorrect while steering, you might lose control of the car.
The teacher warned not to overcorrect students' mistakes, as it could discourage them.
Automatic systems can sometimes overcorrect and create new problems instead of solving the original one.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overcompensate - to react to a problem by doing too much in the opposite way
  • antonym: under-correct - to correct too little, not fully fixing the problem
  • antonym: underreact - to respond with too little action or change
