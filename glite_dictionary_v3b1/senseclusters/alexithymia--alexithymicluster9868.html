alexithymia

noun
difficulty expressing emotions
Pronunciation: / əˌlɛksəˈθaɪmiə / (AmE), / əˌlɛksɪˈθɪmɪə / (BrE)
a condition in which a person has difficulty recognizing and describing their own emotions

Examples

Alexithymia difficulty describing feelings
People with alexithymia may find it hard to describe how they feel to others.
Therapists often help clients with alexithymia learn to identify their emotions.
Researchers are studying the causes and effects of alexithymia in different populations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: emotional awareness - the ability to recognize and understand one's own emotions
