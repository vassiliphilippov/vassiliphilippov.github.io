noun

a colorless, pungent gas (HI) that forms hydroiodic acid when dissolved in water

Examples

Hydrogen iodide gas
Chemists must handle hydrogen iodide with care due to its corrosive and reactive properties.
Industrial production of iodine often involves the use of hydrogen iodide as an intermediate compound.
When hydrogen iodide gas comes in contact with water, it forms a strong acid known as hydroiodic acid.
