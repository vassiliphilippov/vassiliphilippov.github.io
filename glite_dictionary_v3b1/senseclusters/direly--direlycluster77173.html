direly

adverb
extremely seriously
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪərli / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəli / (BrE)
in a very serious, urgent, or extreme way

Examples

To be direly affected
The villagers were direly affected by the sudden food shortage.
Medical supplies are direly needed in the aftermath of the earthquake.
The team was direly unprepared for the unexpected turn of events.
The community was direly in need of clean drinking water after the storm.
He realized too late that he had direly underestimated the difficulty of the exam.
The hospital is direly short of medical supplies this winter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: extremely - to a very great degree
  • antonym: mildly - in a gentle or not serious way
  • synonym: seriously - in a grave or important manner
  • synonym: urgently - in a way that requires immediate action or attention
