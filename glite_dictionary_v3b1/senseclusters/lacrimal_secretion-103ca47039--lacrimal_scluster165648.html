lacrimal secretion

noun
fluid (tears) from the lacrimal glands
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈlækrəməl sɪˈkriʃən / (AmE), / ˈlækrɪməl sɪˈkriːʃən / (BrE)
A clear, salty fluid produced by the lacrimal glands that lubricates and protects the surface of the eyeball; commonly known as tears.

Examples

Lacrimal secretion production
An insufficient production of lacrimal secretion can cause dry eyes and discomfort.
Certain medications may alter the composition of lacrimal secretion, leading to eye irritation.
The ophthalmologist examined her lacrimal secretion for signs of infection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tears - watery secretion from the eyes
