factor out

verb
exclude from consideration
5.4 4.9
Pronunciation: /ˈfæk.tər aʊt/ (AmE), /ˈfæk.tə aʊt/ (BrE)
to consider an element as irrelevant or remove it from consideration when making a decision or calculation

Examples

To factor out special cases
If we factor out one-time events, the company's profits have been stable.
She factored out personal bias to ensure her judgment was fair and objective.
When analyzing sales trends, economists often factor out seasonal variations to see the real changes.
