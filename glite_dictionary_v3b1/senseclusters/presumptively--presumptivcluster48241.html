By reasonable assumption; based on what is presumed to be true.

Examples

Is presumptively innocent
The defendant is presumptively innocent until proven guilty in a court of law.
The new policy will presumptively apply to all employees unless otherwise noted.
These findings are presumptively valid, but further research is needed to confirm them.

Synonyms

  • presumably
    4.3 23/m
    presumably expresses a likely belief in everyday speech, while presumptively sounds more formal and appears in legal or academic statements.
  • putatively
    8.1 0.39/m
    putatively is used in formal claims or reports when something is assumed but unproven, while presumptively suggests a general reasonable assumption.
  • supposedly
    3.4 24/m
    supposedly is what people say or claim without proof, while presumptively means something is taken as true on reasonable grounds.
  • plausibly
    6.3 1.41/m
    plausibly means something seems believable from the evidence, while presumptively means it is accepted as true by reasonable assumption.
