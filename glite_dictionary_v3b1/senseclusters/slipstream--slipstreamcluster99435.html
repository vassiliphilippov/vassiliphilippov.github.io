slipstream

noun
airflow behind a moving aircraft
9.9 6.4
Pronunciation: /ˈslɪp.striːm/ (AmE), /ˈslɪp.striːm/ (BrE)
The flow of air driven backward by a moving aircraft, especially one created by its propeller or wings.

Examples

The slipstream of aircraft
A smaller plane flew through the slipstream of the jet ahead to save fuel.
Pilots must be cautious of turbulence in the slipstream left by large aircraft.
The racing car gained speed by staying in the slipstream of its competitor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: wake - the region of disturbed flow behind a moving object
