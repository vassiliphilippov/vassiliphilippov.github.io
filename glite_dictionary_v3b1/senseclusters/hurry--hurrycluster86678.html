hurry

verb
to urge someone to act faster
1.3 1.0
Pronunciation: /ˈhʌ.rɪ/ /; / /ˈhɜː.rɪ/ (AmE), /ˈhʌ.rɪ/ (BrE)
to push or urge someone to go faster than they would naturally; to make someone act more quickly than is comfortable

Examples

To hurry someone
He hates being hurried when making important decisions.
Please don't hurry me, I need more time to finish this report.
She tried not to hurry the children as they got ready for school.
