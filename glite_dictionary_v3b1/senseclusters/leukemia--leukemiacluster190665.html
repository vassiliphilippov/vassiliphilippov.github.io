leukemia

noun
cancer of blood-forming tissues
4.6 4.48/m
Pronunciation: / luÀàkimi…ô /; / luÀàkiÀêmi…ô / (AmE), / luÀêÀàkiÀêmi…ô / (BrE)
a serious cancer in which the bone marrow produces too many abnormal white blood cells, affecting the blood and immune system

Examples

Blood leukemia
After months of treatment, her leukemia finally went into remission.
Scientists are researching new drugs to treat leukemia more effectively.
The doctor explained that leukemia is a cancer affecting the body's blood-forming tissues.
Fundraising events were organised to help support leukaemia research.
Her child was diagnosed with acute leukaemia and began treatment immediately.
The doctor explained that leukaemia affects the production of white blood cells.

Synonyms

  • cancer of the blood
    3.8 0.08/m
    cancer of the blood is a plain, descriptive phrase used in everyday speech, while leukemia is the medical term doctors and reports use.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anemia - a condition with a deficiency of red blood cells
  • synonym: leukemia - American spelling of 'leukaemia', same meaning
  • synonym: lymphoma - a cancer of the lymphatic system
