co-sleeping

noun
shared sleeping arrangement
3.7
Pronunciation: /koʊ ˈsliː.pɪŋ/ (AmE), /kəʊ ˈsliː.pɪŋ/ (BrE)
the practice of parents and children sleeping in close proximity, often sharing the same bed or room

Examples

Practice of co-sleeping
Many parents choose co-sleeping to make nighttime feedings easier for their infants.
The debate over the safety of co-sleeping continues among pediatricians and parents.
Cultural traditions often influence whether families practice co-sleeping or have children sleep separately.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bed-sharing - parents and children sleeping in the same bed
  • synonym: room-sharing - parents and children sleeping in the same room but not the same bed
  • antonym: solitary sleeping - children sleeping alone in a separate room
