self-service

adjective
where customers serve themselves
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlf ˈsɝːvɪs / (AmE), / ˌself ˈsɜːvɪs / (BrE)
(of a shop, restaurant, etc.) in which customers serve themselves rather than being served by staff.

Examples

Self-service shop
Many laundromats operate on a self-service basis, letting customers use machines independently.
The hotel offers a self-service breakfast bar with a variety of options.
This supermarket was one of the first to introduce self-service checkouts in the city.

Related Terms

  • antonym: counter-service - service in which staff serve customers from behind a counter
  • antonym: full service - a service in which staff serve customers at their tables or personally; not self-service
