iliocostalis

noun
back muscle
Pronunciation: / ˌɪlioʊkɒˈsteɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˌɪliəʊkɒˈstɑːlɪs / (BrE)
a muscle in the back (the outermost of the erector spinae group) that helps extend and bend the spine

Examples

The iliocostalis muscle
The iliocostalis plays a key role in keeping the spine upright during movement.
Injury to the iliocostalis can cause back pain and limit flexibility.
Physical therapists often target the iliocostalis when treating posture problems.
