predecision

adjective
before a decision
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːdɪˈsɪʒən / (AmE), / ˌpriːdɪˈsɪʒən / (BrE)
occurring or existing before a decision is made, especially relating to preparatory actions or information.

Examples

Predecision meeting
The committee reviewed all predecision documents before making their final choice.
A predecision analysis can help identify potential risks early in the process.
They held several predecision meetings to discuss the available options.

Related Terms

  • antonym: postdecision - occurring after a decision has been made
  • synonym: preliminary - happening before the main event or action
