tephra

noun
volcanic ash deposit
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈtef.rə/ (AmE), /ˈtef.rə/ (BrE)
fragments of rock, ash, and other material thrown out by a volcanic eruption and deposited on the ground

Examples

Volcanic tephra layer
A thick layer of tephra covered the fields after the volcanic eruption.
Geologists use tephra deposits to help date ancient environmental changes.
The village was buried under tephra following the sudden explosion of the volcano.
The eruption blanketed the town in a thick layer of tephra.
Scientists collected samples of tephra to study the volcano's history.
After the explosion, fields were covered with tephra and ash.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ash - fine particles left after burning, especially from volcanoes
  • antonym: lava - molten rock expelled by a volcano during an eruption
