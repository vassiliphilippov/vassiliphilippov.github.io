free pass

noun
exemption from consequences

A figurative exemption from rules, criticism, or consequences that someone receives because of their status, influence, or past actions.

Examples

He gets a free pass for mistakes
Because of his celebrity, he seemed to have a free pass when scandals emerged last year.
The manager gave the intern a free pass after the mistake, citing her strong performance.
Critics argued the policy gave companies a free pass to ignore environmental regulations.

Synonyms

  • free ride

    a free ride gives benefits without effort or cost, while a free pass lets someone avoid blame or rules.
