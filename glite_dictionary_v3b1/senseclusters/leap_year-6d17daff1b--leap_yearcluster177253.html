leap year

noun
a year with an extra day in February
1.8 0.23/m
Pronunciation: / lip jÉªr / (AmE), / liËp jÉªÉ™ / (BrE)
A year, occurring every four years, that has 366 days instead of 365, with the extra day added to February.

Examples

A leap year
February has 29 days during a leap year instead of the usual 28.
People born on February 29 celebrate their birthday only in a leap year.
The next leap year will be in 2028, adding an extra day to the calendar.

Synonyms

  • bissextile year
    9.3 0.00/m
    bissextile year is an older, rare term for the same extra-day year, while leap year is the common modern name.
  • intercalary year
    8.3 0.00/m
    intercalary year is a technical calendar term used to keep dates aligned by adding a day, while leap year is the everyday name.
