explosive

adjective
sudden and very loud (of sound/action)
4.1
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈsploʊsɪv / (AmE), / ɪkˈspləʊsɪv / (BrE)
(of a sound or action) sudden and very loud, often startling or forceful.

Examples

Explosive sound
His explosive laugh echoed through the quiet room.
She made an explosive entrance that startled everyone at the party.
The comedian's joke was met with an explosive burst of applause.

Related Terms

  • antonym: quiet - making little or no noise
  • synonym: thunderous - very loud and powerful (of a sound)
