dynamicity

noun
quality of action
8.0
Pronunciation: /daɪ.nə.ˈmɪs.ɪ.tɪ/ /; / /daɪ.næ.ˈmɪs.ɪ.tɪ/ (AmE), /daɪ.nə.ˈmɪs.ɪ.tɪ/ /; / /daɪ.næ.ˈmɪs.ɪ.tɪ/ (BrE)
the quality of expressing action, process, or change, especially in grammar when describing verbs or predicates

Examples

Dynamicity of verbs
The dynamicity of certain verbs allows them to be used in continuous tenses.
Researchers analyzed the dynamicity of language to understand how actions are described.
In grammar, dynamicity distinguishes verbs that show action from those that show a state.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dynamism - the quality of being characterized by vigorous activity and progress
  • antonym: stasis - a state of inactivity or lack of change
