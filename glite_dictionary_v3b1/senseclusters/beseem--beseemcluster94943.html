beseem

verb
to be suitable or appropriate for
8.4
Pronunciation: / bɪˈsiːm / (AmE), / bɪˈsiːm / (BrE)
(formal or archaic) To be appropriate or suitable for; to suit or befit.

Examples

It beseems the occasion
Her modesty beseemed the solemnity of the occasion.
It ill beseems a gentleman to speak in such a coarse manner.
Such bravery would beseem a knight of old.

Related Terms

  • synonym: befit - to be suitable or proper for
  • antonym: misbecome - to be unsuitable for
  • synonym: suit - to be appropriate for
