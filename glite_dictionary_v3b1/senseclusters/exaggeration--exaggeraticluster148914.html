Synonyms

  • overstatement
    4.4 0.6/m
    an overstatement presents facts as more extreme or serious, while exaggeration usually stretches truth to seem bigger or more important than it is.
  • magnification
    5.9 0.03/m
    magnification makes something seem more important or impressive than it really is, while exaggeration is the common word used for that kind of over saying.
  • hyperbole
    6.4 2/m
    hyperbole uses deliberate, colorful overstatements for effect in speech or writing, while exaggeration covers any statement made larger than the truth.
  • overclaim
    8.4 0.00/m
    an overclaim asserts more than the evidence supports, while exaggeration is a broader overstatement that may be untrue or overstated.
  • overestimation
    5.7 0.07/m
    an overestimation judges size, value, or effect as greater than reality, while exaggeration can be any overstatement not limited to numbers.
  • overrating
    4.9 0.00/m
    overrating gives something higher praise or value than it deserves, while exaggeration can change size, importance, or truth in many ways.
