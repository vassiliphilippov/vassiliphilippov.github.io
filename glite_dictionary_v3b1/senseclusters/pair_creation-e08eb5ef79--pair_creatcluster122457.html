pair creation

noun
creation of electron-positron pair by a photon
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛr kriˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpeə kriˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process in which a gamma-ray photon transforms into an electron and a positron when passing near an atomic nucleus

Examples

Pair creation process
Pair creation occurs when a high-energy gamma photon passes near the nucleus of an atom.
In astrophysics, pair creation is important in the study of black holes and neutron stars.
The experiment confirmed the theoretical predictions about pair creation during high-energy photon interactions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: annihilation - the process by which a particle and its antiparticle destroy each other, typically resulting in photons
