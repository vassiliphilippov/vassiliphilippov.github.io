own world

noun
state of being emotionally or mentally isolated
Pronunciation: / oʊn wɜːrld / (AmE), / əʊn wɜːld / (BrE)
a state in which someone or a group feels emotionally isolated or absorbed in their own private experience, often oblivious to the surroundings; especially used to describe couples deeply engaged with each other

Examples

In own world
During the long walk, they talked quietly together, as if existing in their own world.
Teenagers sometimes retreat into their own world, ignoring everyone around them.
When the music started, the couple began to dance, lost in their own world.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bubble - a state of being separated and protected from outside influences
