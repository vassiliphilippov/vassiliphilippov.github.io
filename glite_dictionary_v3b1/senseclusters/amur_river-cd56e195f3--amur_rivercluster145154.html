Amur River

proper noun
river on the China–Russia border in Asia
Pronunciation: / əˈmʊr ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / əˈmʊə ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a major river in eastern Asia forming part of the border between northeastern China and Russia, flowing into the Sea of Okhotsk

Examples

The Amur River flood
During spring, the Amur River is known for dramatic floods that threaten nearby settlements.
Many rare fish species can be found in the waters of the Amur River.
The Amur River serves as a natural boundary between northeastern China and Russia.
