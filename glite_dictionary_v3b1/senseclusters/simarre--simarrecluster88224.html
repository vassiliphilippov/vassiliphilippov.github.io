simarre

noun
sixteenth-century fur-lined coat
Pronunciation: / sɪˈmɑr / (AmE), / sɪˈmɑː / (BrE)
A fur-lined coat decorated with braid, worn in the sixteenth century.

Examples

A simarre coat
During the masquerade, the count arrived draped in a splendid simarre.
Portraits of the era often depict nobles wearing a richly decorated simarre.
The simarre was both a fashion statement and a symbol of status among the Renaissance elite.
