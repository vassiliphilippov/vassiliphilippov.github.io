actualism

noun
ethical theory on actions
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈæktʃuəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈæktʃuəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the ethical or philosophical view that what a person ought to do depends on what they will actually do, not on all possible actions.

Examples

Ethical actualism
Some ethicists argue that actualism better reflects real human decision-making than other theories.
Debates between actualism and possibilism are common in moral philosophy classes.
The professor explained how actualism can affect our understanding of moral responsibility.

Related Terms

  • antonym: possibilism - the view that what a person ought to do is based on all possible actions they could perform
