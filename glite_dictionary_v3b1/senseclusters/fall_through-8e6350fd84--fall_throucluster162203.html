fall through

verb
fail to be completed or happen as planned
Pronunciation: / fɔl ˈθru /; / fɔl θru / (AmE), / fɔːl ˈθruː /; / fɔːl θruː / (BrE)
(of a plan or arrangement) to fail to happen or be completed as intended

Examples

Plans fall through
Despite months of negotiations, the merger fell through at the last minute.
Our vacation plans fell through when the airline went on strike.
The deal will fall through if the funding is not approved by tomorrow.
If the proposal falls through, we'll have to come up with another solution.
Our plans to visit Paris might fall through if we can't get tickets.
The deal fell through at the last minute due to financial disagreements.

Related Terms

  • antonym: succeed - to achieve the desired aim or result
