remobilization

noun
making active again
7.7
Pronunciation: /riː.moʊ.bəl.ɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /rɪ.moʊ.bɪl.ɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /riː.məʊ.bəl.ɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /rɪ.məʊ.bɪl.ɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act of making something active or mobile again, especially by reallocating or redeploying resources, people, or services after a period of inactivity

Examples

Remobilization of workers
After the natural disaster, the remobilization of emergency services was crucial for recovery efforts.
The government announced the remobilization of resources to address the new outbreak.
Following the peace talks, the remobilization of troops was seen as a sign of renewed conflict.
After the crisis, the government ordered the remobilization of emergency teams to the affected region.
The remobilization of funds allowed the company to invest in new technology.
Successful remobilization of staff helped the project meet its tight deadline.

Related Terms

  • antonym: demobilization - the act of disbanding or standing down troops or resources
  • synonym: mobilization - the act of making something ready for movement or action
  • synonym: redeployment - the act of moving resources or personnel to a new position or use
