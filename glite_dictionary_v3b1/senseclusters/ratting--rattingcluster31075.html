ratting

noun
use of RAT malware
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈræ.tɪŋ/ /; / /ˈræ.dɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈræ.tɪŋ/ (BrE)
(computing, cybersecurity) the act of using a remote access Trojan (RAT) to gain unauthorized remote access to a computer or network, typically to steal data or maintain persistent control.

Examples

Ratting a computer
Security researchers warned that attackers conducted ratting to steal data from the enterprise.
During the breach, forensic analysts found evidence of persistent ratting on multiple servers.
Companies must patch vulnerabilities quickly to reduce the risk of ratting by remote attackers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: malware - software designed to harm, exploit, or otherwise compromise computer systems
  • synonym: remote access Trojan (RAT) - a type of malware that gives an attacker covert, remote control of an infected computer
