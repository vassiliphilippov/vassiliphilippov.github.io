multipath

noun
signal interference phenomenon
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌltipaθ / (AmE), / ˈmʌltipaːθ / (BrE)
the phenomenon in radio or wireless systems where signals reach a receiver by multiple routes, often causing interference or errors

Examples

Multipath interference problem
Engineers designed the antenna to reduce multipath effects in urban environments.
The accuracy of the navigation system was affected by severe multipath near tall buildings.
Wireless communication can suffer from multipath when signals reflect off various surfaces.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fading - weakening of a radio signal over distance or due to obstacles
  • synonym: interference - disturbance in a signal caused by overlapping waves
