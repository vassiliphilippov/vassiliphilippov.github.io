drear

adjective
bleak, depressing; causing dejection
9.3
Pronunciation: / drɪr / (AmE), / drɪə / (BrE)
(literary) dull, bleak, and depressing; causing a feeling of sadness or dejection.

Examples

A drear silence
A drear silence filled the old, abandoned house.
He gazed out at the drear November landscape, missing the warmth of spring.
The drear weather seemed to sap everyone's spirits throughout the long afternoon.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic
  • synonym: dreary - dull, bleak, and depressing; causing a feeling of sadness
