trichina

noun
a parasitic roundworm (Trichinella)
8.3 9.2
Pronunciation: /trɪ.ˈkiː.nə/ /; / /traɪ.ˈkiː.nə/ (AmE), /trɪ.ˈkiː.nə/ /; / /traɪ.ˈkiː.nə/ (BrE)
A small parasitic roundworm (genus Trichinella), especially Trichinella spiralis, which infects the intestines of various mammals (including pigs, rats, and humans) and produces larvae that encyst in skeletal muscles.

Examples

A trichina worm
Proper cooking of meat is essential to kill any trichina that might be present.
Scientists used a microscope to study the life cycle of the trichina in infected muscle tissue.
The undercooked pork contained trichina larvae, which led to several cases of trichinosis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: roundworm - a type of parasitic or free-living worm of the phylum Nematoda
  • synonym: trichinosis - a disease caused by Trichinella larvae infecting muscle tissue
