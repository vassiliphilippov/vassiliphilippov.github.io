lightcraft

noun
vehicle of light or energy
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪtkræft / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtkrɑːft / (BrE)
a vehicle (real or imagined) that uses light or energy instead of traditional materials or fuel

Examples

A lightcraft vehicle
The science fiction novel described a lightcraft that could travel faster than any known spaceship.
In the movie, the heroes escaped danger by boarding a shimmering lightcraft powered by pure energy.
Children imagined flying through the galaxy in a lightcraft made entirely of glowing beams.
The engineers designed a lightcraft that could travel to Mars using only laser beams for propulsion.
A lightcraft does not need heavy fuel tanks, making it much lighter than traditional rockets.
Scientists hope that lightcraft technology will make deep space exploration more affordable in the future.
