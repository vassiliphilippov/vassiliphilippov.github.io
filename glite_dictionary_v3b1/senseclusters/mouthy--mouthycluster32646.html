mouthy

adjective
rude and talkative
Pronunciation: / ˈmaʊði / (AmE), / ˈmaʊði / (BrE)
tending to talk a lot in a rude, disrespectful, or impudent way

Examples

Mouthy student
The teacher grew tired of the mouthy remarks from the back of the classroom.
His mouthy attitude often got him into trouble with his parents.
She can be quite mouthy when she disagrees with someone.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cheeky - rudely bold or disrespectful
  • antonym: respectful - showing politeness and respect
