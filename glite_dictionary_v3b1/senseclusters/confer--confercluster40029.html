confer

verb
to grant (a degree, honor, etc.) officially
6.2 3.72/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàf…ùÀê / (AmE), / k…ônÀàf…ôÀê / (BrE)
to officially give or grant (a degree, honor, right, or title) to someone

Examples

To confer a degree
After much deliberation, the board conferred full membership on the new partners.
Each year, the college confers honorary degrees to distinguished guests.
The committee decided to confer the award upon Dr. Lee for her outstanding research.

Synonyms

  • grant
    3.3 35/m
    grant is used for officially giving rights, money, or permission, while confer is mainly used for giving degrees or honors.
  • bestow
    5.6 4.44/m
    bestow is used in formal or poetic speech to present honors or gifts, while confer is a formal act of giving titles or degrees.

Related Terms

  • synonym: award - to give (something such as a prize or a degree) officially to someone
  • synonym: bestow - to give (an honor or right) formally
  • antonym: withhold - to refuse to give something that is due or desired
