hold

verb
reserve in advance for someone
Pronunciation: / hoʊld / (AmE), / həʊld / (BrE)
to keep, retain, or reserve something for future use or for someone else; to pause or delay action

Examples

To hold a seat
Could you please hold a table for us at eight o'clock?
The receptionist will hold a spot for your interview tomorrow afternoon.
They asked the theater to hold two seats in the front row.
She decided to hold the tickets for her friend in case he could make it.
The bank will hold your funds until the transaction clears.
The committee may hold the proposal for review at a later date.
Please hold all emails until the boss returns from vacation.
The operator will hold your call if you need more time.
They decided to hold the shipment due to a payment issue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: book - to arrange for (tickets, seats, etc.) in advance
  • antonym: continue - keep on with an activity or process
  • antonym: release - to let go of or set free
  • synonym: reserve - to arrange for something to be kept for use by someone
  • synonym: retain - to keep possession of
  • synonym: suspend - temporarily stop something from continuing
