nationalist

noun
person advocating national independence or strong government
1.0 4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈnæʃ(ə)nəlɪst /; / ˈnæʃənəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈnaʃ(ə)nəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who strongly supports their own country and its interests, often believing in national independence or putting their country above others

Examples

A proud nationalist
During the debate, she was labeled a nationalist for her strong stance on border control.
He described himself as a proud nationalist who always put his country's interests first.
The uprising was led by a group of young nationalists demanding independence from colonial rule.
He was known as a fierce nationalist who fought for his country's independence.
Some critics argue that nationalist movements can lead to exclusionary policies.
The party appealed to the voters' nationalist sentiments during the campaign.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cosmopolitan - a person who considers themselves a member of all countries and rejects narrow national loyalties
  • antonym: internationalist - a person who supports cooperation among nations
  • synonym: patriot - a person who loves and supports their country
