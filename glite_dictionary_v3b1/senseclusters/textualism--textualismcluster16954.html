textualism

noun
legal interpretation theory
8.8
Pronunciation: /ˈtek.stʃʊ.ə.lɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈtek.stjuː.ə.lɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈtek.stʃʊ.ə.lɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈtek.stjuː.ə.lɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the practice or theory of interpreting texts, especially laws or constitutions, strictly according to their actual words

Related Terms

  • synonym: formalism - an approach that emphasizes form and structure over content or context
  • antonym: intentionalism - an approach that interprets texts based on the author's intended meaning
  • synonym: originalism - a legal theory that interprets the constitution based on original intent or meaning
  • antonym: purposivism - a legal theory that interprets laws based on their intended purpose
