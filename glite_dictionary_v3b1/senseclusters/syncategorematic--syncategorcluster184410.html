syncategorematic

adjective
not able to stand alone in propositions
Pronunciation: / sɪnˌkætɪɡɔːrɪˈmætɪk / (AmE), / sɪnˌkætɪɡəˌrəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
(of a word or expression) not able to stand alone as a term in a proposition, requiring combination with other terms to have meaning.

Examples

Syncategorematic word meaning
In logic, the word or is considered syncategorematic because it cannot be used alone as a term.
Many function words in language are syncategorematic and must combine with other words for meaning.
The distinction between syncategorematic and categorematic terms is fundamental in traditional logic.
