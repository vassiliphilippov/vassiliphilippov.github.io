ghoulie

noun
morbid, unpleasant person
Pronunciation: / ˈɡuːli / (AmE), / ˈɡuːli / (BrE)
an unpleasant or morbid person who takes pleasure in the suffering, misfortune, or death of others, often in a disapproving or informal sense

Examples

A ghoulie reporter
The tabloid reporters acted like ghoulies, chasing every tragic detail for their next headline.
Nobody wanted to sit near the ghoulie who laughed at the accident on the news.
She called him a ghoulie for making jokes about the disaster.

Related Terms

  • antonym: empath - a person who feels and understands others' emotions
  • synonym: ghoul - a person who is fascinated by death or disaster
  • synonym: sadist - a person who enjoys seeing others suffer
