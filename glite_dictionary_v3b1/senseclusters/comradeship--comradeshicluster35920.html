comradeship

noun
friendship and close sociability
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːmrædˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈkɒmrædʃɪp / (BrE)
a feeling of friendship and trust between people who spend a lot of time together; the quality of being easy to get along with in a group

Examples

Strong comradeship feeling
After months of teamwork, a strong sense of comradeship developed within the group.
She valued the comradeship she found in the hiking club.
The comradeship among the soldiers helped them endure the hardships of war.

Related Terms

  • synonym: friendship - a close and supportive relationship between friends
  • antonym: hostility - unfriendly or antagonistic behavior or attitude
