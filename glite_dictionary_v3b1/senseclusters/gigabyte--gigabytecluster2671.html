gigabyte

noun
unit of digital information (1,073,741,824 bytes)
1.3 2.3
Pronunciation: /ˈɡɪ.ɡə.baɪt/ /; / /ˈdʒɪ.ɡə.baɪt/ (AmE), /ˈɡɪ.ɡə.baɪt/ /; / /ˈdʒɪ.ɡə.baɪt/ (BrE)
a unit for measuring digital information or computer storage, equal to about one billion bytes

Examples

One gigabyte of data
Downloading a high-definition movie can require several gigabytes of data.
External hard drives often come with capacities of one or two gigabytes or more.
My new laptop has a 512 gigabyte solid-state drive for storage.
My smartphone has 128 gigabytes of storage for apps and photos.
The movie file was over two gigabytes in size, so it took a while to download.
You need at least one gigabyte of RAM to run this program efficiently.
