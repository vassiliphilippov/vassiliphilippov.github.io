intaglio

noun
printmaking process using engraved plates
8.2 8.7
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈtæl.joʊ/ /; / /ɪn.ˈtæ.ɡlɪ.oʊ/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈtæl.jəʊ/ /; / /ɪn.ˈtæ.ɡlɪ.əʊ/ (BrE)
A printmaking technique where an image is incised into a surface, and the incised area holds the ink to create a print.

Examples

Intaglio printing technique
He admired how intaglio printing produced such rich, textured images.
Original currency designs often use the intaglio method for security and detail.
The artist demonstrated the intaglio process in her printmaking workshop.

Related Terms

  • antonym: relief printing - printing process where the image is raised above the surface
