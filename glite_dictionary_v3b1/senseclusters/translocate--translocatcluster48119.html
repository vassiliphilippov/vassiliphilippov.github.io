translocate

verb
to move within a system
7.5
Pronunciation: / trænsˈloʊkeɪt / (AmE), / trænsˈləʊkeɪt / (BrE)
to move something from one place to another, especially within a body, cell, or biological system

Examples

To translocate proteins
The scientist observed how the protein translocates from the cytoplasm to the nucleus during cell division.
Certain diseases can cause nutrients to translocate abnormally within the body.
Researchers are studying how bacteria translocate across cell membranes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: relocate - to move to a new place
  • synonym: transfer - to move something from one place to another
