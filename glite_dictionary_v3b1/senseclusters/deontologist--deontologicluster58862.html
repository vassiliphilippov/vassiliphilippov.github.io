deontologist

noun
ethics specialist
Pronunciation: / diˌɑːnˈtɑːlədʒɪst / (AmE), / diˌɒnˈtɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a person who studies or practices deontological ethics, focusing on duties and moral rules

Examples

A deontologist studies ethics
The deontologist insisted that telling the truth is always the right thing to do.
As a deontologist, she believes that some actions are morally required regardless of the outcome.
In the debate, the deontologist disagreed with the utilitarian about the best way to make ethical decisions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ethicist - a person who studies or is an expert in ethics
  • antonym: utilitarian - a person who believes in or practices utilitarianism
