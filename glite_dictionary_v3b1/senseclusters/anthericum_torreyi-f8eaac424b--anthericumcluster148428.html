Anthericum torreyi

noun
species of flowering plant native to southwestern US
Pronunciation: / ˌænθəˈraɪkəm tɔːˈreɪ.aɪ / (AmE), / ˌænθəˈrɪkəm tɒˈriː.aɪ / (BrE)
A plant species with basal grasslike leaves and a narrow, open cluster of starlike yellowish-orange flowers on a leafless stalk, found in the southwestern United States; the only Anthericum species native to North America.

Examples

Anthericum torreyi plant
Anthericum torreyi grows in dry, rocky habitats across the southwestern United States.
Botanists confirmed the presence of Anthericum torreyi in several desert regions this spring.
The starlike yellow flowers of Anthericum torreyi make it easy to identify in the wild.
