Juvenal

proper noun
Roman satirical poet (c. 60–c. 140 AD)
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒuːvəˌnæl / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːvənl / (BrE)
(c. 60–c. 140 AD) A Roman satirist noted for his sharp criticism of vice and folly in Roman society during the reign of Emperor Domitian.

Examples

Juvenal satirist works
Many of Juvenal's satires critique the corruption of Roman officials.
Scholars have debated the tone of irony in Juvenal's poetry for centuries.
The phrase 'bread and circuses' originates from a work by Juvenal.
