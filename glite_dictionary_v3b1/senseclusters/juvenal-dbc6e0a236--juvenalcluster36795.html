Juvenal

proper noun
Roman satirical poet (c. 60–c. 140 AD)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒuːvəˌnæl / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːvənl / (BrE)
(c. 60–c. 140 AD) A Roman satirist noted for his sharp criticism of vice and folly in Roman society during the reign of Emperor Domitian.
