English horn

noun
double-reed woodwind instrument, lower than oboe
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ hɔrn / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ hɔːn / (BrE)
a double-reed woodwind instrument of the oboe family with a lower pitch, known for its rich, mellow tone

Examples

An English horn instrument
An English horn has a distinctive, haunting sound cherished by many composers.
She learned to play both the oboe and the English horn during her music studies.
The solo in the symphony was played beautifully on the English horn.
