misguide

verb
to give bad advice; to lead astray
1.3 3.5
Pronunciation: /mɪs.ˈgaɪd/ (AmE), /mɪs.ˈgaɪd/ (BrE)
to lead or advise someone wrongly; to cause someone to make a mistake or go in the wrong direction

Examples

To misguide a student
As a teacher, she would never intentionally misguide her students.
He did not intend to misguide you, but his advice was based on incorrect information.
Politicians who misguide the public can cause serious harm to society.
False information on the internet can easily misguide people who are seeking answers.
His friends tried to warn him, but he was misguided by his own assumptions.
The teacher did not intend to misguide her students, but her explanation was unclear.

Related Terms

  • antonym: guide - to show someone the way; to lead or direct
  • synonym: mislead - to cause someone to have a wrong idea or impression
