Pelecanidae

noun
family of water birds; pelicans
Pronunciation: / pɛlɪˈkænɨˌdi / (AmE), / pɛlɪˈkænɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large water birds known as pelicans, characterized by a long bill and a large throat pouch.

Examples

The Pelecanidae family
Members of Pelecanidae are easily recognized by their long bills and throat pouches.
Scientists have conducted extensive research on the feeding habits of Pelecanidae.
The family Pelecanidae includes some of the world's largest flying birds.

Related Terms
