serve

verb
to function for a purpose or provide a service
1.8 126/m
Pronunciation: / s…ùv / (AmE), / s…úÀêv / (BrE)
to be used for a particular purpose, to fulfill a function, to be sufficient, or to help bring about a result

Examples

Serve a purpose
Solar panels serve both to reduce energy costs and to protect the environment.
The central heating system serves all apartments in the building.
This drawer serves as a safe place for important documents.
Her previous experience will serve her well in her new position.
The study room can serve as an office if needed.
These old boxes serve a useful purpose during the move.
A short note will serve to explain the situation.
If you don't have any flour, cornmeal will serve in this recipe.
This chair will serve until we can buy a new one.

Synonyms

  • suffice
    4.7 8.61/m
    suffice is used to say something is enough in formal statements, while serve has a broader role and purpose.
  • answer
    3.5 1.68/m
    answer often means to meet a need or match a requirement, while serve also covers general use and helping functions.
  • do
    1.0 1498/m
    do is a casual word meaning to be good enough for a purpose, while serve is more formal and broader in use.
  • function
    2.6 35/m
    function means to work or operate in a role, while serve also means to help or be used for a purpose.
  • service
    3.4 3.21/m
    service refers to providing use or help to others or places, while serve describes something used for a purpose.
