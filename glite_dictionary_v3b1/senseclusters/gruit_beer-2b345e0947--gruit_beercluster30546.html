gruit beer

noun
beer made with herbs, not hops
Pronunciation: / ˈɡruɪt bɪr / (AmE), / ˈɡruːɪt bɪə / (BrE)
A type of beer brewed with a mixture of herbs and spices instead of hops, as was common in medieval Europe.

Examples

Gruit beer with herbs
He prefers the earthy flavors of gruit beer over more bitter varieties.
The brewery introduced a new gruit beer featuring a blend of rosemary and yarrow.
Unlike most modern ales, gruit beer does not contain any hops.
