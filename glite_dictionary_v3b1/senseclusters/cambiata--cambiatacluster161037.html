cambiata

noun
nonharmonic musical note
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkæmbiˈɑːtə /; / kæmˈbjɑːtə / (AmE), / ˌkæmbiˈɑːtə /; / kamˈbɪətə / (BrE)
a nonharmonic note or short melodic figure in music (especially in counterpoint) that is approached by step and resolved by leap

Examples

A well-placed cambiata can create a smooth connection between two chord tones.

Related Terms

  • synonym: neighbor note - a nonharmonic note that moves by step away from and back to a harmonic note
  • synonym: passing note - a nonharmonic note that connects two harmonic notes by step
