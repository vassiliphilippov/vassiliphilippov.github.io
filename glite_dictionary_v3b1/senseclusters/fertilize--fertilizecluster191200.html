fertilize

verb
make fertile or productive
1.8
Pronunciation: / ˈfɜrtəˌlaɪz /; / ˈfɜːrtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfɜːtɪlaɪz / (BrE)
to make land, soil, or something else fertile or productive; to enrich or stimulate growth or development

Examples

To fertilize the mind
A good book can fertilize your mind with new ideas.
Creative workshops are meant to fertilize innovation within the company.
The teacher's guidance helped to fertilize his curiosity about science.
Regular reading can fertilise a child's imagination.
The new policies helped fertilise economic growth in the region.
They used natural compost to fertilise the garden soil.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deplete - to make less fertile or productive
  • synonym: enrich - to improve the quality or value of something
  • synonym: fertilize - to make (soil or land) more fertile
  • antonym: impoverish - to make something poor or less productive
