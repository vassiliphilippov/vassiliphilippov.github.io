tripeptide

noun
three-amino-acid molecule
Pronunciation: / traɪˈpɛptaɪd / (AmE), / traɪˈpɛptaɪd / (BrE)
a molecule consisting of three amino acids joined together by peptide bonds

Examples

A tripeptide molecule
The tripeptide was synthesized in the laboratory for use in the experiment.
Researchers discovered that the tripeptide could help regulate certain cellular processes.
A tripeptide is formed when three amino acids are linked together by peptide bonds.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: polypeptide - a molecule made of many amino acids linked by peptide bonds
