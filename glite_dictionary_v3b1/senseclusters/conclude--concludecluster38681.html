conclude

verb
bring to an end
3.1 16/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàkluÀêd /; / k…ônÀàklud / (AmE), / k…ônÀàkluÀêd / (BrE)
to bring something to an end or to come to an end

Examples

To conclude a speech
She will conclude her speech with a call to action.
The board decided to conclude the discussion after everyone had given their opinions.
The ceremony concluded with a moment of silence.
Once negotiations conclude, the agreement will be signed.
The ceremony concluded with a standing ovation from the audience.
The meeting will conclude at 5 p.m. sharp.

Synonyms

  • end
    1.0 300/m
    end means to stop or reach a final point, while conclude is often more formal and used to finish something.
  • finish
    1.0 180/m
    finish means to complete something or come to an end, while conclude sounds slightly more formal and bookish.
  • close
    1.0 70/m
    close means to bring something to an end officially or practically, while conclude is used in formal speech or writing.
  • come to a close
    3.5 1/m
    come to a close describes reaching an end naturally, while conclude often suggests deciding or stating an end more formally.
  • wrap up
    3.0 7/m
    wrap up is informal and means finishing a task or meeting, while conclude is more formal and fits reports or speeches.
  • terminate
    4.6 9/m
    terminate implies ending something completely or by force, while conclude is more neutral and can mean finish or close.
  • put an end to
    3.0 5/m
    put an end to means actively causing something to stop, often forcibly, while conclude can mean simply finish or close.
  • finalize
    3.8 5/m
    finalize means to complete last details and make something definite, while conclude means to bring something to an end more generally.
