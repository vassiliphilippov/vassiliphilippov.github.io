knee-high

adjective, adverb
reaching up to the knees
4.9 3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈniˌhaɪ /; / ˈniː haɪ / (AmE), / ˈniːˌhaɪ /; / ˈniː hʌɪ / (BrE)
reaching as high as the knees; extending up to the knee in height

Examples

Knee-high boots
She wore knee-high boots with her dress to the party.
The snow was knee-high by the time we finished shoveling the driveway.
The wheat in the field is already knee-high this early in the season.
Children love to run in knee-high snow during the winter.
She splashed through the knee-high water on the flooded street.
The wild grass in the meadow grew knee-high by summer.
