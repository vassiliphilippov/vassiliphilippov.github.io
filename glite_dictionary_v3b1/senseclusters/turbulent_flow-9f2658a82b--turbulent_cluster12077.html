turbulent flow

noun
irregular, chaotic flow of fluid
3.7 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈtɜːr.bjə.lənt floʊ/ (AmE), /ˈtɜː.bjə.lənt fləʊ/ (BrE)
a type of fluid motion in which the speed and direction at each point change unpredictably and irregularly, often resulting in eddies and swirls

Examples

Turbulent flow pattern
Engineers must account for turbulent flow when designing efficient water distribution systems.
In rivers, turbulent flow often results in rapid mixing of sediments and nutrients.
The transition from turbulent flow to laminar flow can be observed under carefully controlled conditions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: laminar flow - smooth, orderly fluid motion in parallel layers
