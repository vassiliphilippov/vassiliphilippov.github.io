dysregulation

noun
breakdown of control
8.9
Pronunciation: /dɪs.reɡ.jʊ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /dɪs.reɡ.jə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /dɪs.reɡ.jʊ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /dɪs.reɡ.jə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a failure or breakdown in the normal control or regulation of a system, process, or function.

Examples

Dysregulation of markets
The dysregulation of financial markets can lead to economic crises.
Scientists are studying the dysregulation of hormones in patients with chronic stress.
Government oversight aims to prevent dysregulation in important public sectors.

Related Terms

  • antonym: regulation - the act of controlling or managing something
