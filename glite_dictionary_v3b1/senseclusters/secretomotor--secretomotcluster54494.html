secretomotor

noun
nerve stimulating secretion
Pronunciation: / ˌsiːkrɪtoʊˈmoʊtər / (AmE), / ˌsiːkrɪtəʊˈməʊtə / (BrE)
a nerve or nerve fiber that stimulates or increases secretion from a gland

Examples

Secretomotor fiber in mouth
The secretomotor fibers play a crucial role in stimulating saliva production in the mouth.
Damage to the secretomotor nerves can result in reduced glandular secretions.
During the procedure, the surgeon carefully preserved the secretomotor supply to the lacrimal gland.

Related Terms

  • synonym: motor nerve - a nerve that causes movement or activity in muscles or glands
  • antonym: sensory nerve - a nerve that transmits sensory information to the brain
