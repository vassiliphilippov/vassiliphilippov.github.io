ferricyanide

noun
a salt of ferricyanic acid
Pronunciation: / fɛr.iˈsaɪ.əˌnaɪd / (AmE), / ˌfɛr.ɪˈsaɪ.ə.naɪd / (BrE)
a salt of ferricyanic acid, formed by the oxidation of a ferrocyanide, containing the anion Fe(CN)6^3−

Examples

Potassium ferricyanide
Potassium ferricyanide is commonly used as an oxidizing agent in analytical chemistry.
The laboratory ordered a new supply of ferricyanide for the experiments.
The presence of ferricyanide ions can be detected through a characteristic color change.
