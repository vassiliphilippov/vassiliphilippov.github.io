soulish

adjective
focused on worldly desires
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊlɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈsəʊlɪʃ / (BrE)
dominated by human desires, emotions, or worldly concerns rather than spiritual or divine qualities, especially in religious contexts

Examples

Soulish desires
The novel explores the conflict between soulish desires and spiritual aspirations.
He warned against living a soulish life focused only on material success.
Some religious teachings caution believers not to be overly soulish in their pursuits.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carnal - relating to physical or worldly desires rather than spiritual matters
  • antonym: spiritual - relating to the soul or spirit rather than material or physical things
