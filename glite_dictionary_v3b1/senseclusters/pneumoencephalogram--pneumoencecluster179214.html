pneumoencephalogram

noun
brain X-ray using injected gas
9.9
Pronunciation: /njuː.moʊ.ən.ˈsef.ə.loʊ.græm/ /; / /nuː.moʊ.ən.ˈsef.ə.loʊ.græm/ (AmE), /njuː.məʊ.ən.ˈsef.ə.ləʊ.ɡræm/ /; / /nuː.məʊ.ən.ˈsef.ə.ləʊ.ɡræm/ (BrE)
an X-ray image of the brain taken after replacing some of the cerebrospinal fluid with a gas, usually oxygen, to improve contrast

Examples

A pneumoencephalogram image
Before the advent of MRI, a pneumoencephalogram was a common diagnostic tool for neurological disorders.
Technological advances have made the pneumoencephalogram largely obsolete in modern medicine.
The doctor reviewed the pneumoencephalogram to identify any abnormalities in the patient's brain structure.
