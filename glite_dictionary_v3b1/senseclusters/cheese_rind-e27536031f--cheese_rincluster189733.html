cheese rind

noun
outer layer of a cheese
9.4
Pronunciation: / tʃiz raɪnd / (AmE), / tʃiːz raɪnd / (BrE)
the firm outer layer or skin that forms on cheese during its production and aging

Examples

Natural cheese rind
Artisan cheeses often have a natural cheese rind that develops as they age.
Some people remove the cheese rind before eating, while others enjoy its flavor.
The cheese rind became golden and crispy after baking in the oven.
