saphenous nerve

noun
nerve supplying inner leg and foot
6.7
Pronunciation: / səˈfiːnəs nɜːrv / (AmE), / səˈfiːnəs nɜːv / (BrE)
a branch of the femoral nerve that supplies sensory innervation to the inner side of the leg and foot

Examples

The saphenous nerve branch
Injury to the saphenous nerve can cause numbness along the inner edge of the leg and foot.
The saphenous nerve is often at risk during surgeries involving the medial side of the knee.
The surgeon carefully avoided damaging the saphenous nerve while repairing the patient's varicose veins.
