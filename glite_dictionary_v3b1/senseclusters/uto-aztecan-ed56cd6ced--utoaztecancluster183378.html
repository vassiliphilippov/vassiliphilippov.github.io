Uto-Aztecan

noun
family of American Indian languages
Pronunciation: / ˌjuːtoʊ æzˈtɛkən / (AmE), / ˌjuːtəʊ æzˈtɛkən / (BrE)
a family of American Indian languages spoken in the western United States and Mexico, including Ute, Hopi, and Nahuatl

Examples

Uto-Aztecan language family
Linguists often study Uto-Aztecan to better understand the history of indigenous peoples of the Southwest.
Nahuatl, the language of the Aztecs, belongs to the Uto-Aztecan family.
The Uto-Aztecan languages are spoken from the western United States to Central America.
