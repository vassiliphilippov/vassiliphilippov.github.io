roll bar

noun
vehicle stability bar
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊl bɑr / (AmE), / ˈrəʊl bɑː / (BrE)
a strong metal bar fitted in a vehicle to improve safety, especially to protect people if the vehicle rolls over or to improve stability during turns

Examples

A roll bar in car
The mechanic installed a new roll bar to make the car safer on sharp turns.
Racing vehicles often have a reinforced roll bar for added protection and stability.
After the accident, the damaged roll bar had to be replaced before the truck could be driven again.
The off-road truck was equipped with a sturdy roll bar to protect the driver.
Installing a roll bar is essential for anyone planning to race their car.
After the accident, the roll bar prevented the roof from collapsing on the passengers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anti-roll bar - a bar that reduces a vehicle's body roll during cornering
  • synonym: roll cage - a framework of metal bars in a vehicle for rollover protection
  • synonym: seat belt - a safety strap in vehicles to secure occupants
  • synonym: sway bar - a bar that helps stabilize a vehicle during turns
