geostationary orbit

noun
Earth orbit where satellite stays above same spot
6.1 0.09/m
Pronunciation: / Àåd íio äÀàste…™ É…ôÀån…õri Àà…îrb…™t / (AmE), / Àåd íiÀê…ô( ä)Ààste…™ É…ôn(…ô)ri Àà…îÀêb…™t / (BrE)
an orbit around the Earth in which a satellite moves at the same rotational speed as the Earth and remains fixed above the same point on the equator

Examples

A geostationary orbit
A geostationary orbit is ideal for communication satellites because their position relative to a ground antenna does not change.
Satellite television relies on transmitters located in geostationary orbit for consistent signal coverage.
The weather satellite was placed in a geostationary orbit to continuously monitor the same region of Earth.
