reacquired stock

noun
stock bought back by a corporation
4.1 6.5
Pronunciation: /riː.ə.ˈkwaɪ.ərd stɑːk/ (AmE), /riː.ə.ˈkwaɪ.əd stɒk/ (BrE)
Stock that has been bought back by the issuing corporation and is no longer outstanding, with no voting rights or dividends.

Examples

Reacquired stock by company
Reacquired stock cannot be voted or receive dividends until it is reissued.
After issuing new shares, the board decided to purchase reacquired stock from the open market.
The company increased its reacquired stock in order to boost earnings per share.
