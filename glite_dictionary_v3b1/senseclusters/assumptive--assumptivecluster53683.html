assumptive

adjective
accepted as true without proof
Pronunciation: / əˈsʌmptɪv / (AmE), / əˈsʌmptɪv / (BrE)
Accepted as true or real without proof; based on assumption rather than evidence.

Examples

Assumptive reasoning
His assumptive explanation for being late was questioned by his boss.
She made several assumptive statements without any supporting data.
The team's assumptive attitude led them to overlook crucial evidence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: evident - Clearly seen or understood; obvious
  • synonym: presumptive - Based on probability or presumption rather than certain evidence
  • synonym: speculative - Based on conjecture rather than knowledge
