stereoscopy

noun
three-dimensional vision technique
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˌstɛriˈɑːskəpi / (AmE), / ˌstɛriˈɒskəpi / (BrE)
the technique or process of creating or experiencing three-dimensional visual images by combining two slightly different views of a scene, one for each eye

Examples

Stereoscopy technique
Early experiments in stereoscopy paved the way for modern virtual reality devices.
Medical imaging increasingly employs stereoscopy for more precise visualization of internal structures.
The film uses stereoscopy to immerse viewers in a lifelike three-dimensional world.

Related Terms

  • synonym: 3D imaging - the process of creating or displaying three-dimensional images
  • synonym: binocular vision - the ability to perceive a single three-dimensional image from two eyes
