dovish

adjective

(in economics and finance) favoring expansionary monetary policy, such as lower interest rates or other measures to stimulate the economy.

Examples

Dovish central bank
Following weak data, the central bank took a dovish tone and hinted at lower rates next quarter.
Investors pushed bond yields down after the committee's dovish statement suggested more monetary stimulus might follow.
Her voting record shows she is consistently dovish, favoring cuts and stimulus during economic slowdowns.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accommodative - favoring easy or expansionary monetary policy (e.g., low interest rates, asset purchases).
  • antonym: hawkish - favoring higher interest rates or tighter monetary policy to control inflation.
