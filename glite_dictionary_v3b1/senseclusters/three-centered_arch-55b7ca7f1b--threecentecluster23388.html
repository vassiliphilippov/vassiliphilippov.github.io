three-centered arch

noun
arch with inner curve drawn from three centers
6.9
Pronunciation: / θri ˈsɛnɾɚd ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / θri ˈsɛntəd ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
an arch whose inner curve is constructed using three arcs, each drawn from a different center point, creating a flattened or elliptic appearance

Examples

A wide three-centered arch
A three-centered arch allows for wider openings without excessive height.
Architects used a three-centered arch to achieve both stability and a distinctive aesthetic.
The cathedral was designed with a prominent three-centered arch over the main entrance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: elliptical arch - an arch having a shape similar to an ellipse
