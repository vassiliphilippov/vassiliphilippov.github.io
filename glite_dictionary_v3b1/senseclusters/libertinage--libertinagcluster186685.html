libertinage

noun
rejection of authority
Pronunciation: / ˌlɪbərtɪˈnɑːʒ / (AmE), / ˌlɪbətɪˈnɑːʒ / (BrE)
the practice or attitude of rejecting traditional religious or moral authority, often involving free thinking or unorthodox beliefs

Examples

Philosophical libertinage
The philosopher's libertinage challenged the accepted views of his time.
Many artists in the 18th century embraced libertinage as a way to explore new ideas.
Her outspoken libertinage often put her at odds with conservative members of society.

Related Terms

  • synonym: freethinking - the practice of forming opinions independently of authority
  • antonym: orthodoxy - conventional or traditional beliefs, especially in religion
