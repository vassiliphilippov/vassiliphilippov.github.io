incapacitation

noun
inability to function
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnkəˌpæsɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəˌpæsɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the state of being unable to act or function normally, often due to illness or injury

Examples

Long-term incapacitation
The accident resulted in his complete incapacitation for several months.
Temporary incapacitation can occur after a serious surgery or illness.
Her incapacitation made it difficult for her to return to work immediately.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ability - the power or skill to do something
  • synonym: disability - a physical or mental condition that limits activities
