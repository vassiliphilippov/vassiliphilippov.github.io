dichromat

noun
person with a type of color blindness
9.9 8.2
Pronunciation: /ˈdaɪ.krə.mæt/ /; / /ˈdɪ.krə.mæt/ (AmE), /ˈdaɪ.krə.mæt/ /; / /ˈdɪ.krə.mæt/ (BrE)
a person who has a form of color blindness resulting in the perception of only two primary colors instead of the usual three

Examples

A dichromat person
A dichromat may have difficulty distinguishing between reds and greens.
The genetic basis of being a dichromat is often inherited from one's parents.
Unlike a dichromat, most people see the full spectrum of colors.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: monochromat - person perceiving only one color or shades of gray
  • antonym: trichromat - person with normal color vision (three primary colors)
