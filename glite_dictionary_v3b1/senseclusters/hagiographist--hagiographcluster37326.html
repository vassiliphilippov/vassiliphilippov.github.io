hagiographist

noun
author of an idealizing biography
Pronunciation: / ˌheɪdʒiˈɑːɡrəfɪst / (AmE), / ˌheɪɡiˈɒɡrəfɪst / (BrE)
a person who writes a worshipful or idealizing biography, often presenting the subject in an exaggeratedly positive light

Examples

A hagiographist writes
As a renowned hagiographist, she often glossed over her subjects' failings.
His biography of the leader was so uncritical that many readers saw him as a hagiographist.
The historian accused the author of being a hagiographist rather than a serious researcher.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biographer - someone who writes a biography
  • antonym: critic - someone who evaluates or points out faults, often an antonym in style to hagiographist
