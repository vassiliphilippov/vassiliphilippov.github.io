glossopharyngeal nerve

noun
ninth cranial nerve in the head and neck
8.1 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Àå…°l…ëÀêso äf…ôÀàr…™nd íi…ôl n…úrv / (AmE), / Àå…°l…ís…ô äf…ôÀàr…™nd íi…ôl n…úÀêv / (BrE)
the ninth cranial nerve, which supplies sensory fibers to the pharynx and back of the tongue, and motor fibers to muscles that raise the pharynx and larynx, as well as parasympathetic fibers to the otic ganglion.

Examples

The glossopharyngeal nerve function
After the injury, tests showed reduced function of the patient's glossopharyngeal nerve.
Damage to the glossopharyngeal nerve can cause difficulty in swallowing and loss of taste in the back of the tongue.
The doctor explained that the glossopharyngeal nerve is critical for both taste and the gag reflex.
