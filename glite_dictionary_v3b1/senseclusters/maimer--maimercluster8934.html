maimer

noun
person who causes severe injury
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪmər / (AmE), / ˈmeɪmə / (BrE)
A person who injures someone, especially by causing permanent damage to their body.

Examples

A maimer of people
He was known as a ruthless maimer in the criminal underworld.
Stories of the maimer spread fear throughout the city.
The court sentenced the maimer to ten years in prison for his brutal attack.

Related Terms

  • synonym: maim - to wound or injure seriously, often causing permanent damage
