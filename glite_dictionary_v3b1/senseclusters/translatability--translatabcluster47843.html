translatability

noun
ability to convert
5.3
Pronunciation: / trænsˌleɪtəˈbɪləti /; / trænzˌleɪtəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / trænsˌleɪtəˈbɪləti /; / trænzˌleɪtəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality of being able to be translated or converted into another language, form, medium, or system

Examples

The translatability of poetry
The translatability of poetry often depends on the skill of the translator and the languages involved.
Researchers discussed the translatability of scientific concepts into practical classroom activities.
Some cultural references have limited translatability when adapting books for international audiences.
The translatability of technical terms often depends on the availability of equivalent words in the target language.
Some jokes lose their humor because their translatability is limited by cultural context.
Literary critics debate the translatability of ancient poetry into modern languages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: convertibility - the quality of being able to be changed into a different form or use
  • antonym: intranslatability - the quality of not being able to be translated or converted
  • synonym: translation - the process of changing words from one language to another
  • antonym: untranslatability - the quality of not being able to be translated
