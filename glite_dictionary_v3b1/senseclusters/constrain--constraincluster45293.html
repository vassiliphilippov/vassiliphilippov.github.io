constrain

verb
to restrict or limit movement or action
1.0 4.6
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈstreɪn/ (AmE), /kən.ˈstreɪn/ (BrE)
to limit or restrict someone or something, or to force someone to act in a certain way by limiting their options

Examples

To constrain choices
Laws are designed to constrain certain behaviors in society.
She felt constrained by the rigid rules of her office environment.
The prison walls constrain the movement of the inmates.
Efforts to improve efficiency may constrain staff from taking necessary breaks.
Her options were severely constrained by financial limitations.
Strict regulations constrain the ability of companies to expand into new markets.
Legal obligations can sometimes constrain companies from acting quickly.
She felt constrained to follow her parents' wishes despite her own dreams.
The strict rules constrain his ability to express himself freely.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compel - force someone to do something
  • synonym: confine - to keep or restrict someone or something within certain limits
  • antonym: free - to release from restriction or confinement
  • synonym: limit - to place boundaries or limits on something
  • synonym: restrain - hold back from action or restrict movement
  • synonym: restrict - to limit the size, amount, or range of something
