Fahrenheit

noun
temperature scale
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfærənˌhaɪt / (AmE), / ˈfærənhaɪt / (BrE)
a temperature scale in which water freezes at 32 degrees and boils at 212 degrees under standard conditions

Examples

85 Fahrenheit degrees
The weather report predicted a high of 85 Fahrenheit for tomorrow.
Water boils at 212 degrees Fahrenheit under normal atmospheric pressure.
He prefers to use Fahrenheit instead of Celsius when discussing temperatures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Celsius - a temperature scale where water freezes at 0 degrees and boils at 100 degrees
  • antonym: Kelvin - an absolute temperature scale used in science
