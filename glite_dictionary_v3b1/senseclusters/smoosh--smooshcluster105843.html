smoosh

verb
to press together
7.9
Pronunciation: /smuːʃ/ /; / /smʊʃ/ (AmE), /smuːʃ/ /; / /smʊʃ/ (BrE)
to press, crush, squash, or flatten something so it becomes soft, flat, or misshapen

Examples

To smoosh berries
She used her hands to smoosh the berries into a thick jam.

If you smoosh the bread too hard, it will lose its fluffiness.
She likes to smoosh the berries before adding them to the yogurt.
The child tried to smoosh the clay into a flat pancake shape.
If you smoosh the bread too much, it will lose its fluffiness.
She accidentally smooshed the cake while trying to fit it into the box.
The clay was smooshed into a flat shape by the heavy book.
If you smoosh the berries, they will make a mess on the table.
