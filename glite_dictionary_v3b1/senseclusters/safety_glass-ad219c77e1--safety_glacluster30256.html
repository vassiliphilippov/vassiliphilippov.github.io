safety glass

noun
glass reinforced to prevent shattering
2.1 3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪfti ɡlæs / (AmE), / ˈseɪfti ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
A type of glass made with a layer of plastic, resin, or other material between two sheets of glass to prevent it from shattering.

Examples

Safety glass window
After the vase fell, the safety glass only cracked instead of shattering everywhere.
Modern office buildings often use safety glass in their windows for improved security.
The windshield was made from safety glass to protect passengers during an accident.
