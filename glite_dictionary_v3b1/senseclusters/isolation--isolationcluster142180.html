isolation

noun
act of separating or being alone
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪ.səˈleɪ.ʃən /; / ˌaɪsəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.səˈleɪ.ʃən /; / ˌaɪsəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the state of being alone or separated from others, either physically or emotionally

Examples

Social isolation
His isolation from the group made it difficult for him to adjust.
Many patients experienced a sense of isolation during the long hospital stay.
The research lab ensures the isolation of sensitive samples to prevent contamination.
After moving to the remote village, he experienced a deep sense of isolation.
The conference aimed to reduce the political isolation of the minority community.
The strict quarantine was intended to keep the infectious patients in isolation from the rest of the population.
After moving to a new city, she struggled with isolation and missed her family deeply.
Social isolation can have a significant impact on a person's mental health.
The feeling of isolation grew stronger as he realized how different he was from those around him.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alienation - the state or experience of being isolated from a group or an activity to which one should belong
  • antonym: integration - the process of combining or incorporating into a whole
  • synonym: loneliness - the state of being lonely; sadness because one has no friends or company
  • synonym: seclusion - the state of being private and away from other people
