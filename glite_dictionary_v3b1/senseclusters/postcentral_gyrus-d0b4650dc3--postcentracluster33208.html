postcentral gyrus

noun
brain ridge behind central sulcus
9.5
Pronunciation: /poʊst.ˈsen.trəl ˈdʒaɪ.rəs/ (AmE), /pəʊst.ˈsen.trəl ˈdʒaɪ.rəs/ (BrE)
the convolution of the parietal lobe of the brain, located just behind the central sulcus, that is primarily responsible for processing sensory information from the body

Examples

The postcentral gyrus of brain
Damage to the postcentral gyrus can result in impaired touch sensation.
Neuroscientists often map brain activity in the postcentral gyrus during tactile experiments.
The postcentral gyrus plays a crucial role in receiving sensory input from the skin.
