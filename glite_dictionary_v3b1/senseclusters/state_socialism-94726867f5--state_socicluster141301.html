state socialism

noun
government-controlled economic system allowing limited private enterprise
6.2 5.7
Pronunciation: / steɪt ˈsoʊʃəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / steɪt ˈsəʊʃəlɪzəm / (BrE)
An economic system in which the government owns and controls most means of production, while permitting some degree of private enterprise.

Examples

State socialism system
Many European countries experimented with state socialism in the twentieth century.
The government adopted state socialism to control key industries while allowing small businesses to operate privately.
Under state socialism, the state manages major resources but citizens can still own small enterprises.

Related Terms

  • antonym: capitalism - an economic system based on private ownership and free markets
  • synonym: socialism - a political and economic theory of social organization advocating collective or governmental ownership of production
