interrogative

noun
a word or sentence that asks a question
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.təˈrɑː.ɡə.tɪv / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈrɒ.ɡə.tɪv / (BrE)
a word, sentence, or grammatical form that asks a question (especially in grammar)

Examples

An interrogative word
Each interrogative sentence in the worksheet requires an answer.
The teacher wrote an interrogative on the board to start the discussion.
Words like 'who' and 'what' are examples of interrogatives in English.
In English, an interrogative often begins with a wh-word such as 'what' or 'where'.
Please turn this statement into an interrogative to practice forming questions.
The teacher asked the students to write an interrogative about their favorite book.
