Gamay

noun
red wine grape
Pronunciation: / ɡæˈmeɪ / (AmE), / ɡaˈmeɪ / (BrE)
a variety of grape used to produce light-bodied red wines, especially in the Beaujolais region of France

Examples

Gamay grapes
The vineyard specializes in producing Gamay wines that are fresh and fruity.
Many sommeliers recommend Gamay for those who enjoy lighter red wines.
The Beaujolais Nouveau is made exclusively from Gamay grapes harvested in the region.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Pinot Noir - a type of grape used for red wines, known for its delicate flavor
