litharge

noun
lead oxide compound
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪθɑrdʒ / (AmE), / ˈlɪθɑːdʒ / (BrE)
a yellow or red form of lead oxide used in making glass, ceramics, and other industrial products

Examples

Litharge in glass
The glass manufacturer added litharge to improve the quality of their products.
Workers handled litharge carefully due to its toxic properties.
In the past, litharge was commonly used in the production of certain types of paint.
