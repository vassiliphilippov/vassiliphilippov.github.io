nominative

adjective
registered in a specific person's name (finance)
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑmənətɪv / (AmE), / ˈnɒmɪnətɪv / (BrE)
(of a share, bond, etc.) registered in the name of a specific person, rather than being bearer or anonymous.

Examples

Nominative shares
Banks often require clients to hold securities in nominative form for greater transparency.
Only nominative bonds can be reissued if lost or stolen, as they are tied to the owner's identity.
The investor preferred nominative shares because they were registered in his own name.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bearer - a security or document that is not registered in any person's name, owned by whoever holds it
