antiglare

noun
glare-reducing device
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˌæntiˈɡlɛr / (AmE), / ˌæntiˈɡlɛə /; / ˌæntiˈɡleə / (BrE)
a device, film, or coating used to reduce or prevent glare from lights, screens, or reflective surfaces

Examples

A camera antiglare
The photographer attached an antiglare to the camera lens to avoid unwanted reflections.
Many computer monitors now come with an antiglare coating to make screens easier to read.
Drivers often use an antiglare visor to reduce the brightness of headlights at night.
The antiglare coating on her glasses made it easier to drive at night.
He applied an antiglare film to his laptop screen to reduce eye strain.
Many modern smartphones come with built-in antiglare protection for better visibility outdoors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antireflective - designed to reduce reflection from surfaces
  • antonym: glare - strong, unpleasant light reflected from a surface
  • synonym: shade - a cover that blocks or reduces light
  • synonym: visor - a shield or cover to protect the eyes from light
