tourist court

noun
roadside hotel with direct room access
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈtʊrɪst kɔrt / (AmE), / ˈtʊərɪst kɔːt / (BrE)
a roadside hotel or group of cabins where guests can park their car near their room, especially common in the early and mid-20th century

Examples

Old tourist courts
Many tourist courts from the 1950s have been converted into modern motels.
The tourist court offered parking right outside each cabin for the travelers' convenience.
We stayed at a charming tourist court during our cross-country road trip.

Related Terms

  • synonym: motel - a hotel designed for motorists, often with outdoor parking next to rooms
