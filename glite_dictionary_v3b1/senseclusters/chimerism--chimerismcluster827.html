chimerism

noun
mixed genetic cells
9.9
Pronunciation: / kaɪˈmɪrɪzəm / (AmE), / kaɪˈmɪərɪzəm / (BrE)
the condition of having cells or tissues from two different genetic sources within one organism

Examples

Genetic chimerism
The doctor explained that chimerism can occur naturally or after a bone marrow transplant.
Researchers detected chimerism in the patient following the organ transplant procedure.
Genetic testing revealed unexpected chimerism in the animal's blood cells.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mosaicism - the presence of two or more populations of cells with different genotypes in one individual
