Marlovian

noun
Marlowe authorship supporter
Pronunciation: / mɑrˈloʊviən / (AmE), / mɑːˈləʊviən / (BrE)
a person who supports the theory that Christopher Marlowe, not William Shakespeare, wrote certain plays attributed to Shakespeare

Examples

Marlovian authorship supporter
As a dedicated Marlovian, he spent years researching Marlowe's possible authorship of Shakespeare's works.
The debate between Marlovians and traditional scholars continues in literary circles.
She joined a group of Marlovians who believe Marlowe wrote several famous plays.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Shakespearean - a person who supports the traditional view that Shakespeare wrote his plays
