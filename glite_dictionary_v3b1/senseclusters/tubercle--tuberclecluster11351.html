tubercle

noun
swelling or nodule from tuberculosis

Pronunciation: /ˈtuː.bɜːr.kəl/ /; / /ˈtjuː.bɜːr.kəl/ (AmE), /ˈtjuː.bə.kəl/ /; / /ˈtuː.bə.kəl/ (BrE)
A small, rounded swelling or nodule, especially one that is a characteristic lesion caused by tuberculosis.

Examples

Lung tubercle
A tubercle is often found in tissues infected by the tuberculosis bacterium.
During autopsy, several tubercles were observed in the affected organs.
The doctor identified a tubercle in the patient's lung during the X-ray scan.
