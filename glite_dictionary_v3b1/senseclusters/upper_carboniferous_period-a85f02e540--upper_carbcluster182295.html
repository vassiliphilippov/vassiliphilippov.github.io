Upper Carboniferous period

noun
later part of the Carboniferous period (geology)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpɚ ˌkɑːr.bəˈnɪf.ər.əs ˈpɪr.i.əd / (AmE), / ˈʌp.ə ˌkɑː.bəˈnɪf.ər.əs ˈpɪə.ri.əd / (BrE)
the later subdivision of the Carboniferous period, lasting from about 310 to 280 million years ago, noted for warm climates and extensive swampy land

Examples

The Upper Carboniferous period
Fossils from the Upper Carboniferous period provide important clues about Earth's prehistoric climate.
Many coal beds formed during the Upper Carboniferous period are still mined today.
The Upper Carboniferous period was marked by extensive tropical swamps and lush vegetation.
