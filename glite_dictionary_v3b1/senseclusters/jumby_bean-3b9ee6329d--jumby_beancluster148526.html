jumby bean

noun
tropical tree with spotted seeds
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʌm.bi ˌbin / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌm.bi ˌbiːn / (BrE)
A small tree native to the West Indies and northeastern Venezuela, with large oblong pointed leaflets, panicles of purple flowers, and seeds that are black or scarlet with black spots.

Examples

The jumby bean tree
Botanists documented several specimens of jumby bean during their expedition to Venezuela.
The jumby bean tree produces striking panicles of purple flowers each spring.
The brightly colored seeds of the jumby bean are sometimes used for jewelry in the Caribbean.
