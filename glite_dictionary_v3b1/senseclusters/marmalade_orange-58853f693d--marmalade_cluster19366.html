marmalade orange

noun
type of orange tree with sour fruit
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑrməˌleɪd ˈɔrɪndʒ / (AmE), / ˈmɑːməleɪd ˈɒrɪndʒ / (BrE)
a type of orange tree producing sour or bitter fruit, often used for making marmalade or as grafting stock

Examples

A marmalade orange tree
Gardeners often use the marmalade orange as grafting stock for sweet orange varieties.
The marmalade orange is excellent for making traditional preserves because of its tart flavor.
Unlike sweet oranges, the marmalade orange produces fruit that is best suited for marmalades and other preserves.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bitter orange - orange tree with sour, bitter fruit, often used similarly
