downhill

adjective
sloping downwards steeply
1.0 1.67/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆdaÊŠnËŒhÉªl / (AmE), / ËˆdaÊŠnËŒhÉªl / (BrE)
(of a slope or surface) sloping downwards, especially at a steep angle.

Examples

Downhill path
After climbing the hill, we enjoyed a quick downhill walk back to the village.
The downhill trail was much faster but required extra caution.
They built their house on a downhill street that flooded easily during rainstorms.

Synonyms

  • downward-sloping
    4.1 0.01/m
    downward-sloping describes a surface that leans noticeably downward, while downhill names a slope, often steep and used in walking or skiing.
  • descending
    3.2 0.00/m
    descending describes something moving or falling toward a lower level, while downhill describes a sloping surface rather than motion.
  • downward
    1.0 7.58/m
    downward means toward a lower place or level in general, whereas downhill specifically refers to a sloping surface or direction.

Related Terms

  • antonym: uphill - sloping upward, opposite of downhill
