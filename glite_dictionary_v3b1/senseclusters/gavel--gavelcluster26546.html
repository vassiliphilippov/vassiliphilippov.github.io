gavel

verb
to use a gavel
Pronunciation: / ˈɡævəl / (AmE), / ˈɡævəl / (BrE)
to bring a meeting to order or end it by striking a gavel

Examples

To gavel a meeting
The judge gaveled the courtroom to silence before announcing the verdict.
After a heated discussion, the chairperson gaveled the meeting to a close.
She quickly gaveled the session to order so the board could begin.
The judge gaveled the courtroom to silence before announcing the verdict.
After a long discussion, the chair finally gaveled the meeting to an end.
She gaveled the session open promptly at nine o'clock.
