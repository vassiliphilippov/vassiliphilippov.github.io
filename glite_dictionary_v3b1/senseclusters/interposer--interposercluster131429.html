interposer

noun
mediator or intervener
8.4
Pronunciation: /ɪn.tər.ˈpoʊ.zər/ (AmE), /ɪn.tə.ˈpəʊ.zə/ (BrE)
a person who intervenes in a dispute or situation, often to help settle it or act as a mediator

Examples

A helpful interposer
The company brought in an interposer to help resolve the contract dispute.
As an experienced interposer, she was able to calm both sides and find a solution.
Without an interposer, the argument might have escalated further.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arbitrator - a person officially appointed to settle a dispute
  • antonym: bystander - a person who is present but does not take part
  • synonym: mediator - a person who helps others resolve a conflict
