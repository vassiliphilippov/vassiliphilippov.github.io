stroma

noun
supporting tissue of an organ
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈstroʊmə / (AmE), / ˈstrəʊmə / (BrE)
The connective and supporting tissue of an organ, as distinguished from the functional tissue (parenchyma).

Examples

Organ stroma
In some tumors, an abundance of stroma can affect the progression of the disease.
The stroma provides the structural framework for the kidney.
The function of the stroma is distinct from that of the parenchyma.

Related Terms

  • antonym: parenchyma - the functional tissue of an organ as opposed to the supporting or connective tissue
