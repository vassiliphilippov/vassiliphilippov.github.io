overexpand

verb
to add too much detail
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərɪkˈspænd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərɪkˈspænd / (BrE)
to make something longer or more detailed than necessary by adding excessive information or explanation

Examples

To overexpand an essay
Try not to overexpand your essay, as it should be clear and concise.
The teacher warned students not to overexpand their answers on the test.
If you overexpand your explanation, the main idea might get lost.

Related Terms

  • antonym: condense - to make something shorter by removing details
  • synonym: elaborate - to add more detail or information
