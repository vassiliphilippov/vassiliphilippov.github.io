metaleptically

adverb
in a narratively transgressive way
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˈlɛptɪkli / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈlɛptɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that blurs or crosses the boundaries between different narrative levels, such as when a narrator enters the story world.

Examples

Narrator acts metaleptically
The novel shifts metaleptically when the author suddenly becomes a character in the story.
In the play, the characters speak metaleptically to the audience, breaking the narrative boundary.
The director uses metaleptically layered scenes to blur the line between reality and fiction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: metafictionally - in a way that draws attention to the fictionality of a narrative
