straight fairlead

noun
device for guiding anchor lines straight
8.4
Pronunciation: / streɪt ˈfɛrˌliːd / (AmE), / streɪt ˈfeəˌliːd / (BrE)
A type of fairlead designed to guide anchor lines in a straight path on a ship or boat.

Examples

A straight fairlead
A straight fairlead is preferable when the anchor line must be kept from chafing against the railing.

The crew installed a straight fairlead to ensure the anchor line ran smoothly off the deck.
