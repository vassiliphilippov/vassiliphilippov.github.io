variance

noun
official exception to a rule or regulation
3.4 6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈver.ɪ.əns/ /; / /ˈvær.ɪ.əns/ (AmE), /ˈveə.ri.əns/ /; / /ˈvæ.ri.əns/ (BrE)
an official permission to do something that is normally not allowed by rules or regulations, especially regarding building or zoning laws

Examples

Building variance
She applied for a variance because her renovation plans did not comply with current regulations.
The city council granted a variance allowing the homeowner to build closer to the property line.
To add an extra floor, the developer needed a variance from the local zoning board.
