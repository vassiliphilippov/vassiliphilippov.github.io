chelicera

noun
fang-like mouth appendage of an arachnid
9.4 9.9
Pronunciation: / kəˈlɪsərə / (AmE), / kɪˈlɪsərə / (BrE)
either of the first pair of fang-like appendages near the mouth of an arachnid, often adapted for grasping or piercing

Examples

A chelicera fang
A scorpion's chelicerae look like small pincers near its mouth.
Spiders inject venom through their chelicerae when subduing prey.
The scientist used a microscope to study the structure of the tick's chelicera.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mandible - jaw or jawbone, especially the lower jawbone in vertebrates
