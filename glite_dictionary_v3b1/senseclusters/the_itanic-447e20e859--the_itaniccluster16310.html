the Itanic

noun
humorous metaphor for a failed project
9.9
Pronunciation: / ði aɪˈtænɪk / (AmE), / ði aɪˈtænɪk / (BrE)
A humorous or sarcastic reference comparing a failed or doomed project, especially Intel's Itanium processor, to the Titanic disaster.

Examples

The Itanic failure
Investors realized too late that the Itanic was destined for disaster.
Some engineers started calling the ambitious project the Itanic after it ran into major problems.
Tech writers often refer to the failed chip as the Itanic in critical retrospectives.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Titanic - a famous passenger ship that sank in 1912
