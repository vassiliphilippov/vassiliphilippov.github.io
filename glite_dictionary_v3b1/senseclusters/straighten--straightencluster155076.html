straighten

verb
make or become straight
Pronunciation: / ˈstreɪtn̩ /; / ˈstreɪtən / (AmE), / ˈstreɪtn /; / ˈstreɪt(ə)n /; / ˈstreɪtən / (BrE)
to make or become straight or put into the correct or upright position (including posture, objects, or clothing)

Examples

To straighten your back
After the storm, we had to straighten the fence that was leaning.
He paused to straighten the pile of papers on his desk.
The yoga instructor asked everyone to straighten their backs and relax their shoulders.
After the storm, workers helped straighten the damaged street signs.
Please straighten the pictures on the wall; they're all crooked.
She used a flat iron to straighten her hair before the party.
After sitting at her desk all day, she straightened and stretched to relieve the tension in her back.
He straightened suddenly when he realized someone was watching him.
The students straightened as soon as the principal entered the room.
