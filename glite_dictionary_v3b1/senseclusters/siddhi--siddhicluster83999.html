siddhi

noun
spiritual power
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪdi / (AmE), / ˈsɪdi / (BrE)
a special spiritual power or state of perfection believed to be attained through meditation or yoga, especially in Indian traditions

Examples

A siddhi power
The yogi claimed to have gained a siddhi after years of dedicated meditation.
Ancient texts describe various siddhi that can be achieved through spiritual discipline.
Some believe that a true siddhi is a sign of deep spiritual progress.
After years of meditation, she believed she had reached a siddhi that brought her inner peace.
In some traditions, a siddhi is considered a sign of spiritual progress.
He viewed completing the pilgrimage as a personal siddhi and a milestone in his life.
