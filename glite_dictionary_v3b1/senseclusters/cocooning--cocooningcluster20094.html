cocooning

noun
staying at home for comfort or privacy
3.8
Pronunciation: / kəˈkunɪŋ / (AmE), / kəˈkuːnɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of staying inside one's home, often for comfort, privacy, or to avoid socializing or outside stress.

Examples

Cocooning at home
After a hectic month at work, she spent the weekend cocooning with books and movies.
Many people turned to cocooning during the winter, preferring home comforts to social outings.
Since the pandemic began, cocooning has become a common lifestyle choice.

Related Terms

  • synonym: self-isolation - the act of remaining apart from others, especially to prevent disease transmission
