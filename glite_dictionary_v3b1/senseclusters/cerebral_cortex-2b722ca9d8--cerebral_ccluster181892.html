cerebral cortex

noun
outer layer of the brain's cerebrum
2.9
Pronunciation: / səˈribɹəl ˈkɔɹˌtɛks / (AmE), / səˈriː.brəl ˈkɔː.teks / (BrE)
the outer layer of the cerebrum in the brain, made up of grey matter and involved in various high-level brain functions such as thought, perception, and decision-making

Examples

The cerebral cortex layer
Damage to the cerebral cortex can affect memory and reasoning abilities.
Scientists are studying how the cerebral cortex processes complex visual information.
The cerebral cortex is divided into different areas responsible for sensory input and motor control.
