gastric artery

noun
artery supplying the stomach
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæstrɪk ˈɑrtəri / (AmE), / ˈɡæstrɪk ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
an artery that supplies blood to the walls of the stomach

Examples

The gastric artery supplies blood
The main function of the gastric artery is to supply oxygenated blood to the stomach tissues.
The surgeon carefully ligated the gastric artery during the procedure to prevent any bleeding.
Ulcers near the gastric artery may result in severe complications if the vessel is damaged.
