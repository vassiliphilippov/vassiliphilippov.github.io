stampede

noun
synchronized request surge (computing)
Pronunciation: / stæmˈpid / (AmE), / stæmˈpiːd / (BrE)
(in computing) a sudden, synchronized burst of requests or actions toward a service or resource that overwhelms backend systems or creates a bottleneck.

Examples

A server stampede
A sudden traffic surge caused a stampede of requests that brought the API to its knees.
Developers implemented locking to prevent a cache stampede from overwhelming the database under high load.
When the sale went live, the sudden stampede of connections created a backend bottleneck and many timeouts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: surge - a sudden large increase in load or traffic
  • synonym: thundering herd - a problem where many processes or requests wake and act at the same time, overwhelming resources
