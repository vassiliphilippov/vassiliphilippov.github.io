running headline

noun
heading at top of pages in a book
6.2 5.3
Pronunciation: /ˈrʌn.ɪŋ ˈhed.laɪn/ (AmE), /ˈrʌn.ɪŋ ˈhed.laɪn/ (BrE)
A heading printed at the top of every page, or every other page, of a book or document, typically indicating the title or section.

Examples

A running headline on pages
Many academic books include a running headline that repeats the author’s name.
The running headline on each page helps readers keep track of the chapter title.
Without a running headline, it can be hard to know where you are in a long document.

Related Terms

  • synonym: running head - shortened title or heading appearing at the top of pages
