ergosterol

noun
plant sterol; vitamin D2 precursor
7.9 7.6
Pronunciation: / ərˈɡɑːstərɒl / (AmE), / ɜːˈɡɒstərɒl / (BrE)
A sterol found in fungi and some plants that can be converted into vitamin D2 when exposed to ultraviolet light.

Examples

Ergosterol in fungi
Exposure to sunlight converts ergosterol in yeast into vitamin D2, an important supplement for humans.
Many fungi contain high levels of ergosterol, which can be measured to estimate fungal biomass.
Researchers study ergosterol as a target for antifungal drugs because it is absent in animal cells.
