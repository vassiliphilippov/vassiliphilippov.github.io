William Butler Yeats

proper noun
Irish poet and dramatist (1865–1939)
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪljəm ˈbʌtlɚ ˈjeɪts / (AmE), / ˈwɪljəm ˈbʌtlə ˈjeɪts / (BrE)
(1865–1939) An Irish poet and dramatist, a leading figure of 20th-century literature and a founder of the Irish Literary Revival.

Examples

William Butler Yeats Nobel Prize
William Butler Yeats was awarded the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1923.
