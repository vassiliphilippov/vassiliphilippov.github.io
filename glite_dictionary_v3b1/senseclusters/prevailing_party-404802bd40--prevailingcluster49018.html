prevailing party

noun
party winning a lawsuit
5.6 5.8
Pronunciation: /prɪ.ˈveɪ.lɪŋ ˈpɑːr.ti/ /; / /priː.ˈveɪ.lɪŋ ˈpɑːr.ti/ (AmE), /prɪ.ˈveɪ.lɪŋ ˈpɑː.ti/ (BrE)
the party in a lawsuit who obtains a judgment in their favor, typically entitled to certain legal costs or remedies

Examples

The prevailing party wins
After a lengthy trial, the court declared her the prevailing party.
Legal costs are generally awarded to the prevailing party in civil litigations.
The prevailing party may be entitled to recover attorney fees from the opposing side.
