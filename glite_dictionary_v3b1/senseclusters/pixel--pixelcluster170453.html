pixel

noun
smallest unit of a digital image
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪk.səl / (AmE), / ˈpɪk.səl / (BrE)
The smallest discrete unit of a digital image or display, usually a tiny square or dot that forms part of a larger picture.

Examples

A pixel on screen
Each pixel on the screen can display a different color, creating a full image.
Modern smartphone cameras have millions of pixels to capture sharp, detailed pictures.
When you zoom in too much, you can see the individual pixels that make up the photo.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dot - a tiny round mark, often used to describe the appearance of a pixel
  • degree higher: resolution - the fineness of detail that can be distinguished in an image, directly related to the number of pixels
