negator

noun
word expressing denial
Pronunciation: / nɪˈɡeɪtər / (AmE), / nɪˈɡeɪtə / (BrE)
a word or element that expresses denial, refusal, or negation in a sentence

Examples

Negator word 'not'
The word 'not' is the most common negator in English sentences.
In the sentence 'She never goes there,' 'never' acts as a negator.
Some languages use double negators for emphasis in negative statements.

Related Terms

  • antonym: affirmative - a word or form expressing agreement or affirmation
  • synonym: negative - a word or form expressing negation
