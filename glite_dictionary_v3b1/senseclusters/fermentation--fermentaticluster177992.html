fermentation

noun
state of agitation or change
Pronunciation: / ˌfɜr.mənˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɜː.mənˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
A state of intense agitation, unrest, or rapid change, often referring to social or political situations.

Examples

Political fermentation
The city is in a period of political fermentation as groups demand reform.
The decade was marked by cultural fermentation and new artistic movements.
There was a sense of fermentation in the air before the revolution started.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - a period or state of freedom from storms, wind, or agitation
  • synonym: turmoil - a state of great disturbance, confusion, or uncertainty
  • synonym: unrest - a state of dissatisfaction, disturbance, and agitation in a group of people
