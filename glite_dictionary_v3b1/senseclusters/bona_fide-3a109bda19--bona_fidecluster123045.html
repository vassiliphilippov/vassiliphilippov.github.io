bona fide

adjective
genuine; in good faith
5.9 1.29/m
Pronunciation: / Àåbo än…ô Ààfa…™d / (AmE), / Àåb…ô än…ô ÀàfiÀêde…™ /; / Àåb…ín…ô Ààfa…™di / (BrE)
genuine, real, or done in good faith; not fake or copied

Examples

A bona fide offer
Regulations require that any bona fide offer must be considered carefully.
She produced a bona fide document to prove her identity.
The company is looking for bona fide investors who are serious about the project.
Only bona fide documents will be accepted as proof of identity.
The offer appeared to be a bona fide opportunity for investment.
The painting was confirmed to be a bona fide work by the artist.

Synonyms

  • authentic
    3.8 20/m
    authentic is clearly real and original, while bona fide emphasizes legitimacy and that something is made or offered in good faith.
  • veritable
    7.4 2.65/m
    veritable is a strong, often formal intensifier meaning truly, while bona fide focuses on being genuine or acting in good faith.
