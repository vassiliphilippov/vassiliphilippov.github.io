your other left

x
humorous correction for confusing left and right
7.7
Pronunciation: / jɚ ˈʌðɚ lɛft / (AmE), / jɔː ˈʌðə lɛft / (BrE)
used humorously to indicate that someone has mistaken left for right or vice versa, typically after they turn in the wrong direction

Examples

It's your other left
He looked confused when told to go left, so his friend quipped, "It's your other left!"
She started to turn right, so I laughed and said, "No, your other left."
Whenever someone mixes up directions, I jokingly tell them, "Try your other left."
