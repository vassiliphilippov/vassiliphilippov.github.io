case law

noun
law based on judicial decisions
1.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪs lɔ /; / keɪs lɔ / (AmE), / ˈkeɪs lɔː /; / keɪs lɔː / (BrE)
the body of law created by judges' decisions and previous court cases, rather than by written laws made by governments

Examples

Case law changes
Changes in case law can have a major impact on future court rulings.
Students often study case law when preparing for careers in the legal profession.
The judge cited several examples of case law to support her decision.
In many legal systems, case law evolves over time as new judicial decisions are made.
Law students must study case law to understand how courts interpret statutes.
The judge referred to case law when making her ruling on the dispute.

Related Terms

  • synonym: common law - law developed through court decisions, often used interchangeably with case law
  • antonym: statute law - law made by legislative bodies, in contrast to case law
  • antonym: statutory law - law created by legislation, in contrast to case law
