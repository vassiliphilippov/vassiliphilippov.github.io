disdar

noun
castle warden
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈdɑːr / (AmE), / dɪsˈdɑː / (BrE)
an old term for the warden or keeper of a castle or fortress, especially in the Ottoman Empire

Examples

The disdar of castle
The disdar ensured that the castle gates were locked every night.
During the siege, the disdar organized the defense of the fortress.
Visitors had to report to the disdar before entering the stronghold.

Related Terms

  • synonym: castellan - the governor or warden of a castle
  • synonym: warden - a person responsible for the supervision of a place
