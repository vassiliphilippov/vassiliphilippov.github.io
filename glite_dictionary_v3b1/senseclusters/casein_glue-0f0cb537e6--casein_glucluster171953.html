casein glue

noun
glue made from milk protein
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪ.siː.ɪn ɡluː / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.siː.ɪn ɡluː / (BrE)
a strong adhesive made from casein (a milk protein), often used in woodworking, plywood, and cabinetmaking

Examples

Casein glue for wood
Before the popularity of synthetic adhesives, casein glue was widely used in making furniture.
Some vintage guitars were assembled using casein glue for its strong, natural bond.
The craftsman used casein glue to bond the pieces of plywood together.

Related Terms

  • synonym: animal glue - adhesive made from animal parts
  • synonym: casein - principal milk protein used to make certain adhesives
