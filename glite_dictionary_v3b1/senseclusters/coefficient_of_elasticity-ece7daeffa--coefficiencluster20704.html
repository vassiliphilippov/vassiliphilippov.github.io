(physics) The ratio of an applied stress to the resulting change in shape (strain) of an elastic body, measuring how stiff or flexible a material is.

Examples

The coefficient of elasticity value
Engineers must consider the coefficient of elasticity when selecting materials for building bridges.
Rubber has a much lower coefficient of elasticity compared to steel, making it more flexible.
The coefficient of elasticity of the metal was determined through a series of laboratory tests.
