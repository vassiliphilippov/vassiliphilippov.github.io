histiocytic leukemia

noun
type of leukemia involving monocytes/monoblasts
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˌhɪstioʊˈsɪtɪk lɪˈkiːmiə /; / ˌhɪstioʊˌsɪtɪk luˈkiːmiə / (AmE), / ˌhɪstɪəʊˈsɪtɪk lɪˈkiːmiə /; / ˌhɪstɪəˌsɪtɪk ljuːˈkiːmiə / (BrE)
a form of leukemia marked by the abnormal increase of monocytes and monoblasts in the blood

Examples

Histiocytic leukemia cases
Pediatric cases of histiocytic leukemia are rare but require immediate medical attention.
The patient was diagnosed with histiocytic leukemia following a series of blood tests and biopsies.
Treatments for histiocytic leukemia often include chemotherapy and stem cell transplants.
Doctors monitor blood counts closely in cases of histiocytic leukaemia to track disease progression.
The patient was diagnosed with histiocytic leukaemia after tests showed increased monocyte levels.
Treatment options for histiocytic leukaemia are still under clinical research.
