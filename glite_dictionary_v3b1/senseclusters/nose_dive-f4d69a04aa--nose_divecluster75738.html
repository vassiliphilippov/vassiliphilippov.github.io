nose dive

A steep downward plunge made by an aircraft or other object, usually with the nose pointing down.

Examples

A nose dive in stocks
Stock prices took a nose dive following the disappointing earnings report.
The airplane went into a nose dive after losing engine power.
The pilot quickly corrected the nose dive and regained control of the aircraft.

Synonyms

  • nosedive
    5.6 0.12/m
    nosedive is the common single-word term for a sudden steep drop, while nose dive is the two-word phrase that names the same nose-first plunge.
  • dive
    6.5 1.62/m
    dive often means a steep, controlled nose-down descent used as a maneuver, while nose dive suggests a sudden uncontrolled plunge with the nose down.

Related Terms
