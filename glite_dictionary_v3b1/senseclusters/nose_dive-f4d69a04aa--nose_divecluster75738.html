nose dive

noun
steep downward plunge of an aircraft
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈnoʊz ˌdaɪv / (AmE), / ˈnəʊz ˌdaɪv / (BrE)
A steep downward plunge made by an aircraft or other object, usually with the nose pointing down.

Examples

A nose dive in stocks
Stock prices took a nose dive following the disappointing earnings report.
The airplane went into a nose dive after losing engine power.
The pilot quickly corrected the nose dive and regained control of the aircraft.

Related Terms

  • antonym: climb - an upward ascent by an aircraft
  • synonym: plunge - a sudden and rapid fall
