formalist

noun
supporter of formalism
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrməlɪst / (AmE), / ˈfɔːməlɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports the view that mathematics or logic is based on the manipulation of symbols within formal systems rather than on abstract objects

Examples

A math formalist
The formalist insisted that mathematical statements are only meaningful within a formal system.
As a formalist, she rejected the idea that numbers exist independently of human thought.
Many debates in philosophy of mathematics involve the views of a formalist versus those of an intuitionist.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intuitionist - a person who believes mathematics is based on mental constructions and intuition
  • antonym: logicist - a person who believes mathematics is reducible to logic
