generalship

noun
leadership and skill of a military general
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛnɚ(ə)lˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛn(ə)rəlʃɪp / (BrE)
the skill or ability of a military general in planning and directing military operations; leadership and strategy in warfare

Examples

Brilliant generalship
Her brilliant generalship turned the tide of the war in their favor.
Napoleon’s generalship is still studied in military academies around the world.
The troops admired his generalship and trusted his decisions unconditionally.

Related Terms

  • synonym: command - the act of leading or controlling a military unit
  • synonym: strategy - the skill of planning military operations or maneuvers
