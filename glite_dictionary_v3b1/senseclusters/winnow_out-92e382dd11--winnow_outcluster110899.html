winnow out

verb
eliminate from consideration
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪnoʊ aʊt / (AmE), / ˈwɪnəʊ aʊt / (BrE)
to remove or eliminate someone or something from consideration, often as part of a selection process

Examples

To winnow out candidates
After reviewing the applications, she quickly winnowed out those that didn’t meet the requirements.
The first round of interviews will winnow out the least qualified candidates.
The judges have to winnow out the weaker entries before the final competition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: eliminate - to completely remove or get rid of something
  • synonym: exclude - to prevent someone or something from entering or being considered
  • antonym: include - to make someone or something part of a group or category
