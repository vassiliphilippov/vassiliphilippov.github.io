parliamentary-cabinet system

noun
political system where Cabinet directs government
1.0 6.2
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːrləˈmɛn.t̬ə.ri ˈkæb.ɪ.nət ˈsɪs.təm / (AmE), / ˌpɑː.lɪˈmɛn.təri ˈkæb.ɪ.nət ˈsɪs.təm / (BrE)
A political system in which the Cabinet collectively determines the government's policies and direction, especially concerning legislation passed by parliament.

Examples

The parliamentary-cabinet system
In a parliamentary-cabinet system, the Cabinet's unity is crucial to the government's ability to pass legislation.
Many democracies adopted the parliamentary-cabinet system to ensure collective decision-making by executive leaders.
The United Kingdom operates under a parliamentary-cabinet system in which the Prime Minister and Cabinet shape governmental policy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: parliamentary system - system of government where the executive derives its legitimacy from the legislature
