meiosis

noun
cell division forming gametes/spores
9.2
Pronunciation: / maɪˈoʊsɪs / (AmE), / mɪˈəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A type of cell division in sexually reproducing organisms that results in four daughter cells, each with half the number of chromosomes of the parent cell, producing gametes in animals or spores in plants.

Examples

Cell meiosis
During meiosis, the chromosome number is reduced by half to form haploid gametes.
Errors in meiosis can lead to genetic disorders in the offspring.
The process of meiosis ensures genetic diversity in sexually reproducing organisms.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mitosis - ordinary cell division resulting in two identical cells
