difficulty

noun
requiring effort or inconvenience
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪfɪˌkʌlti /; / ˈdɪfɪkəlti / (AmE), / ˈdɪfɪkəlti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being hard to do, deal with, or understand; requiring extra effort

Examples

A difficulty problem
Crossing the river posed less difficulty than we expected.
He had difficulty understanding the instructions at first.
She managed to finish the task, but only with great difficulty.
Due to the difficulty of the task, the project took several weeks to complete.
She underestimated the difficulty of learning a new language as an adult.
The difficulty of the mountain trail made the hike much more challenging than we expected.

Related Terms

  • synonym: challenge - something that tests one's abilities or requires great effort
  • antonym: ease - the absence of difficulty or effort
  • degree higher: hardship - severe suffering or privation; more serious than ordinary difficulty
