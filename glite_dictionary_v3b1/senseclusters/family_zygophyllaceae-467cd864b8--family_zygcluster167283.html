A family of small trees, shrubs, and herbs native to warm, arid, and saline regions, often resinous and sometimes poisonous, including the genera Zygophyllum, Tribulus, Guaiacum, and Larrea.

Examples

Family Zygophyllaceae plants
Botanists classify the guaiac tree within the family Zygophyllaceae due to its distinctive resinous properties.
Many desert plants, such as some in the family Zygophyllaceae, have adapted to saline soils.
The creosote bush, a member of the family Zygophyllaceae, dominates large areas of the southwestern United States.
