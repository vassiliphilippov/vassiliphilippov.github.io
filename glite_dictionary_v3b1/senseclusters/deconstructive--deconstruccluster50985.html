deconstructive

adjective
analyzing structures
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːkənˈstrʌktɪv / (AmE), / ˌdiːkənˈstrʌktɪv / (BrE)
relating to the process of analyzing and breaking down ideas, texts, or structures to reveal hidden meanings or assumptions

Examples

Deconstructive reading method
The professor's deconstructive approach encouraged students to question traditional literary interpretations.
A deconstructive analysis can reveal hidden biases in historical documents.
Her deconstructive critique of the film exposed underlying social themes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: analytical - relating to careful examination or analysis
  • antonym: constructive - helpful in developing or building up something
