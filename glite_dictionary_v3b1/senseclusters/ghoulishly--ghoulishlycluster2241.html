ghoulishly

adverb
morbidly; gruesomely
9.3
Pronunciation: /ˈguː.lɪʃ.liː/ (AmE), /ˈguː.lɪʃ.liː/ (BrE)
in a way that is disturbingly morbid, gruesome, or shows cruel pleasure in horror or others' pain

Examples

To ghoulishly enjoy pain
He ghoulishly recounted the details of the haunted house to his friends.
The journalist ghoulishly described the crime scene in vivid detail.
She laughed ghoulishly at the horror movie's most frightening moment.
He ghoulishly recounted the details of the accident to anyone who would listen.
The tabloid reported ghoulishly on the celebrity's personal troubles.
She smiled ghoulishly as her rival stumbled during the performance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cheerfully - in a happy and positive way
  • synonym: gleefully - in a way that shows great delight or joy, sometimes at others' expense
  • synonym: macabrely - in a way that is disturbing and horrifying
  • synonym: maliciously - in a way that shows a desire to harm or upset others
  • antonym: sympathetically - in a way that shows understanding or compassion for others' suffering
