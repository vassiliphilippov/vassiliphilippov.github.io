spit out

verb
say something angrily or forcefully
Pronunciation: / spɪt aʊt / (AmE), / spɪt aʊt / (BrE)
to say something quickly or forcefully, especially with anger or contempt

Examples

To spit out words
He spat out the accusation before anyone could stop him.
He spits out insults at anyone who disagrees with him.
She spit out her response without even looking up from her desk.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: mutter - to speak in a low, angry or complaining voice
  • synonym: snap - to say something suddenly and angrily
