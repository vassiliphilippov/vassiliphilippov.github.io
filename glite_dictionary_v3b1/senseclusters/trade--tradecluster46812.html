trade

noun
an equal exchange of goods or services
2.5 1.0
Pronunciation: /treɪd/ (AmE), /treɪd/ (BrE)
the act or instance of buying, selling, or exchanging goods or services

Examples

A trade of goods
The two collectors arranged a trade, exchanging rare stamps with each other.
They agreed on a trade of wheat for fresh produce from the neighboring farm.
Without money, early villagers often relied on trade to obtain the items they needed.
After a quick trade with his colleague, he got the tool he needed.
It was a fair trade, and both parties were satisfied with the outcome.
The arms trade between the two countries has increased significantly in recent years.
Restrictions on trade can affect the global economy significantly.
The company's success is largely due to its strong focus on international trade.
Venice was once a major hub of trade between Europe and Asia.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bargain - an agreement between parties on the terms of a transaction
  • synonym: barter - the direct exchange of goods or services without using money
  • synonym: business - the activity of buying and selling as a means of earning money
  • synonym: commerce - the activity of buying and selling, especially on a large scale
  • synonym: deal - an agreement or arrangement in business
  • synonym: transaction - an instance of buying or selling something
