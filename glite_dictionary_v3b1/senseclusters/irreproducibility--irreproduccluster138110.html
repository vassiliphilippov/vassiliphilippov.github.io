irreproducibility

noun
impossibility of being reproduced
Pronunciation: / ˌɪri.prəˌduː.səˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɪəri.prəˌdjuː.səˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being impossible to reproduce or duplicate, especially in scientific experiments or studies.

Examples

The irreproducibility problem
Scientists are concerned about the increasing reports of irreproducibility in published experiments.
She addressed the problem of irreproducibility by updating the laboratory protocols.
The irreproducibility of the study's findings raised questions about its research methods.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reproducibility - the quality of being able to be reproduced
