nonduality

noun
oneness belief
9.9
Pronunciation: / nɑnˌduːˈæləti / (AmE), / nɒnˌdjuːˈælɪti / (BrE)
the philosophical or spiritual belief that all things are fundamentally one and not separate

Examples

Nonduality belief
Many meditation practices focus on realizing nonduality and the unity of all existence.
The concept of nonduality is central to several Eastern philosophies and spiritual traditions.
Some people find peace through the understanding of nonduality and the idea that everything is interconnected.

Related Terms

  • antonym: duality - the state of having two parts or aspects, often seen as separate
  • synonym: oneness - the state of being unified or whole
