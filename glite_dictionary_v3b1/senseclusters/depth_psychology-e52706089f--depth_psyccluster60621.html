depth psychology

noun
psychological approach focused on the unconscious
1.0
Pronunciation: / dɛpθ saɪˈkɑlədʒi / (AmE), / dɛpθ saɪˈkɒlədʒi / (BrE)
A branch of psychology focused on exploring unconscious mental processes and underlying motives, often for therapeutic purposes.

Examples

Depth psychology theory
Carl Jung was a major figure in the development of depth psychology.
Many therapists incorporate depth psychology techniques to understand their clients' behavior.
The study of dreams is a key component in depth psychology.

Related Terms

  • synonym: psychoanalysis - a therapeutic method developed by Freud to explore unconscious motives
