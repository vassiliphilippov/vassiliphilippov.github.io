readdress

verb
to change address or reconsider
5.3
Pronunciation: /riː.ə.ˈdres/ /; / /rɪ.ə.ˈdres/ (AmE), /riː.ə.ˈdres/ /; / /rɪ.ə.ˈdres/ (BrE)
to change the address or focus of something, or to deal with an issue, plan, or question again

Examples

The manager decided to readdress the complaint after new evidence was found.
Please readdress the package to the new office location before sending it.

He readdressed the letter to his new office after moving last month.

You should readdress the envelope if the recipient has moved to a new address.
The company is readdressing its marketing strategy to reach a younger audience.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ignore - to pay no attention to something
  • synonym: reconsider - to think about something again, especially to change a decision
  • synonym: redirect - to send something to a different place or address
  • synonym: revise - to change or modify something, especially a plan or document
