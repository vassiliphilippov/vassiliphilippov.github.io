river birch

noun
a birch tree native to swampy areas of eastern US
3.3
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪvɚ bɝːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈrɪvə bɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a North American birch tree (Betula nigra) found in swamps and river bottoms, noted for its peeling reddish-brown bark

Examples

The river birch tree
In the eastern United States, river birch is a popular tree for planting along rivers and ponds.
Many landscapers choose river birch for its attractive peeling bark and resistance to disease.
The river birch in our backyard thrives because it loves the moist soil near the stream.

Related Terms

  • synonym: birch - a tree of the genus Betula, known for its thin, often peeling bark
