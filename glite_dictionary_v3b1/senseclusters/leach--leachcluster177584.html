leach

noun
the process of leaching out substances
Pronunciation: / liːtʃ / (AmE), / liːtʃ / (BrE)
the process of washing or draining out soluble substances from a material by the action of percolating liquid, especially water

Examples

Soil leach after rain
During heavy rainfall, nutrients can leach from the soil into nearby waterways.
Scientists study the leach of minerals to understand soil composition changes.
The leach of harmful chemicals from old batteries poses environmental risks.
