chromosomal abnormality

noun

a change in the normal structure or number of chromosomes, often resulting in physical or mental disorders

Examples

A chromosomal abnormality detected
Doctors detected a chromosomal abnormality during prenatal testing.
Down syndrome is a common chromosomal abnormality resulting from an extra copy of chromosome 21.
Some forms of infertility are linked to a subtle chromosomal abnormality present in one of the parents.

Synonyms

  • chromosomal aberration
    8.9 0.02/m
    chromosomal aberration describes a change in chromosome number or structure, while chromosomal abnormality names that change often causing disorders.
  • chromosomal anomaly
    6.7 0.01/m
    chromosomal anomaly is an unexpected abnormal change in chromosome structure or number, while chromosomal abnormality is the general term used.
  • chromosomal disorder
    5.3 0.02/m
    chromosomal disorder is a diagnosed medical condition caused by chromosome change, while chromosomal abnormality names the underlying structural or number change.
  • chromosomal mutation
    6.3 0.00/m
    chromosomal mutation is a genetic change altering chromosome material, while chromosomal abnormality refers to structure or number differences that often cause disorders.
