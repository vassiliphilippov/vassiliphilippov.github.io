pitch-out

verb
to throw outward
7.0
Pronunciation: /pɪtʃ aʊt/ /; / /ˈpɪtʃ.aʊt/ (AmE), /pɪtʃ aʊt/ /; / /ˈpɪtʃ.aʊt/ (BrE)
to throw or toss a ball outward, especially as a deliberate move in baseball to prevent a steal or as a lateral pass in football

Examples

To pitch-out the ball
The coach signaled the pitcher to pitch-out when he saw the runner taking a big lead.
In football, the quarterback may pitch-out to a running back to avoid a tackle.
The team practiced how to pitch-out quickly to stop opponents from stealing bases.
