dosemeter

noun
instrument that measures radiation dose
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈdoʊsɪˌmiːtər / (AmE), / ˈdəʊsɪˌmiːtə / (BrE)
an instrument used to measure an absorbed dose of ionizing radiation, such as X-rays or radioactivity

Examples

Personal dosemeter
After the accident, each worker was provided with a personal dosemeter for safety.
Modern hospitals equip their radiology staff with electronic dosemeters to ensure compliance with safety regulations.
The technician used a dosemeter to monitor exposure to X-rays during the experiment.
