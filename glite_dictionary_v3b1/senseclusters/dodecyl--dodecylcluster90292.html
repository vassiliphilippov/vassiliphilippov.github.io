dodecyl

adjective
twelve-carbon group
Pronunciation: / ˈdoʊdəˌsɪl / (AmE), / ˈdəʊdɪsɪl / (BrE)
having, containing, or derived from a dodecyl group, which is a twelve-carbon alkyl chain, especially in chemical compounds

Examples

Dodecyl compound
The scientist added a dodecyl group to the molecule to increase its solubility.
Many detergents contain dodecyl compounds to help break down oils and fats.
A dodecyl chain is often used in surfactants for its effective cleaning properties.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alkyl - a group of carbon and hydrogen atoms arranged in a chain, derived from an alkane
