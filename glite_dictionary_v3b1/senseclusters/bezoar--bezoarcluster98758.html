bezoar

noun
hard stomach mass
Pronunciation: / ˈbiːzɔr / (AmE), / ˈbiːzɔː / (BrE)
a hard mass found in the stomach or intestines of animals or humans, sometimes believed in the past to protect against poison

Examples

A bezoar mass
The surgeon discovered a large bezoar blocking the patient's intestine.
In ancient times, a bezoar was believed to have magical healing properties.
Veterinarians sometimes find a bezoar in the stomachs of grazing animals.
In medieval times, a bezoar was believed to cure any poison.
The museum displayed a rare bezoar taken from a goat's stomach.
Doctors once carried a bezoar as a charm against poisoning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antidote - a substance that counteracts poison
  • synonym: calculus - a hard mass formed in the body, such as a kidney stone
  • antonym: poison - a substance that can cause harm or death
