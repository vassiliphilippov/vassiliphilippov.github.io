xerodermia

noun
abnormally dry, rough skin condition
Pronunciation: / ˌzɪroʊˈdɜrmiə / (AmE), / ˌzɪərəʊˈdɜːmɪə / (BrE)
A mild medical condition in which the skin becomes abnormally dry and rough.

Examples

xerodermia skin condition
People with xerodermia may experience discomfort and itchiness, especially in dry climates.
The dermatologist diagnosed the patient with xerodermia after observing the rough patches on her arms.
Treating xerodermia often involves using moisturizers to restore the skin's natural barrier.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: ichthyosis - A family of disorders causing dry, scaly skin
  • synonym: xerosis - Abnormal dryness, especially of the skin or eyes
