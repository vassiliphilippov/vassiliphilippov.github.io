desperate

adjective
showing extreme urgency or need
1.4 2.8
Pronunciation: /ˈdes.pər.ət/ /; / /ˈdes.prɪt/ (AmE), /ˈdes.pə.rət/ /; / /ˈdes.prɪt/ (BrE)
feeling or showing a great need, urgency, or hopelessness

Examples

Desperate for help
After losing his job, he became desperate to find another source of income.
She felt a desperate need to speak with her friend after receiving the news.
They made a desperate attempt to save the child from the burning house.
He took a desperate risk, knowing it was his only chance of success.
In a desperate attempt to save the project, she worked through the night.
The team made a desperate effort to score before the final whistle blew.
After hours with no response, she let out a desperate cry for help.
His desperate attempt to escape showed how hopeless he felt in the situation.
The refugees crossed the border in a desperate search for safety.

Related Terms

  • antonym: apathetic - showing or feeling no interest, enthusiasm, or concern
  • synonym: despairing - showing loss of hope or dejection
  • synonym: determined - having made a firm decision and being resolved not to change it
  • antonym: hopeful - feeling or inspiring optimism
  • degree higher: reckless - without regard for danger or consequences
  • synonym: urgent - requiring immediate action or attention
