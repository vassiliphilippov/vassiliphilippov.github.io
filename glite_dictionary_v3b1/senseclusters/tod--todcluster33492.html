tod

noun
young deer or fox
Pronunciation: / tɑd / (AmE), / tɒd / (BrE)
an old or regional term for a young male deer or a fox cub

Examples

A young tod
The hunter spotted a tod moving quietly through the underbrush.
In old stories, a clever tod often outsmarts the hounds.
The villagers believed seeing a tod at dawn was a sign of good luck.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cub - a young fox, bear, lion, or other carnivorous mammal
  • synonym: fawn - a young deer, especially in its first year
