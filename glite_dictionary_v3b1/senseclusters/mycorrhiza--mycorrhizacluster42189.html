mycorrhiza

noun
fungus-root partnership
9.2
Pronunciation: /maɪ.kə.ˈraɪ.zə/ (AmE), /maɪ.kə.ˈraɪ.zə/ (BrE)
a beneficial relationship between a fungus and the roots of a plant, where both help each other

Examples

Plant mycorrhiza relationship
Many trees rely on mycorrhiza to obtain nutrients from the soil.
The presence of mycorrhiza can greatly improve a plant's growth and health.
Scientists study mycorrhiza to understand how plants and fungi cooperate in nature.
Many forest plants rely on mycorrhiza to help them absorb water and nutrients from the soil.
Scientists study mycorrhiza to understand how fungi and plants cooperate in natural ecosystems.
The presence of mycorrhiza can improve crop growth and soil health in agriculture.

Related Terms

  • antonym: parasitism - a relationship where one organism benefits and the other is harmed
  • synonym: symbiosis - a close, beneficial relationship between two different organisms
