cut costs

verb
reduce expenses
3.0 2.86/m
Pronunciation: / kÊŒt kÉ”sts / (AmE), / kÊŒt kÉ’sts / (BrE)
to reduce the amount of money that is spent, especially in order to improve efficiency or profitability

Examples

To cut costs quickly
By switching to more efficient equipment, we can significantly cut costs each year.
Many families are looking for ways to cut costs during tough economic times.
The company had to cut costs in order to stay competitive in the market.

Synonyms

  • drive down costs
    3.3 0.24/m
    drive down costs aims to push business expenses lower through action and negotiation, while cut costs means reducing spending overall.
  • cut back
    2.9 7.86/m
    cut back reduces the amount or use of something to save money, while cut costs focuses on lowering spending specifically.
  • curtail spending
    4.3 0.04/m
    curtail spending is a formal way to reduce how much money is being spent, while cut costs is a general, common phrase.
  • slash spending
    4.2 0.23/m
    slash spending means cutting expenses drastically and quickly, while cut costs can mean smaller or steady reductions over time.
  • prune the budget
    4.9 0.00/m
    prune the budget removes small or wasteful items from a budget, while cut costs may include broader or deeper savings.
  • retrench
    8.1 0.39/m
    retrench means reduce spending in response to financial trouble and is formal, while cut costs is more general and neutral.
  • economize
    5.0 0.60/m
    economize means use money or resources carefully to avoid waste, while cut costs implies actively lowering spending amounts.
