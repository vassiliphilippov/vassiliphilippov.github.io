collaborate

verb
cooperate with an enemy (as a traitor)
Pronunciation: / kəˈlæbəˌreɪt / (AmE), / kəˈlæbəreɪt / (BrE)
to cooperate with an enemy force, especially by betraying one's own country

Examples

To collaborate with the enemy
During the occupation, some citizens collaborated with the enemy authorities.
He was accused of having collaborated with the invaders during the war.
Many refused to collaborate and instead joined the resistance movement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: betray - to be disloyal or hand over to an enemy
  • antonym: resist - to oppose or fight against an occupying force
