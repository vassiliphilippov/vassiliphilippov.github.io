bream

verb
remove marine growth from ship's bottom using heat
Pronunciation: / bɹiːm / (AmE), / bɹiːm / (BrE)
to clean the bottom of a ship by applying heat to remove barnacles, seaweed, or other marine growths.

Examples

To bream a ship
Before setting sail, the crew had to bream the old vessel to improve her speed.
It was common practice in earlier centuries to bream wooden ships before long voyages.
Shipwrights would bream the hull regularly to prevent damage from encrusting organisms.
