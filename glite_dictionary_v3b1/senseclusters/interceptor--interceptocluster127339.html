interceptor

noun
fast fighter plane for intercepting enemy aircraft
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈsɛptər / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈsɛptə / (BrE)
a fast and agile fighter aircraft designed to intercept and destroy enemy planes, especially bombers

Examples

Fighter interceptor jet
During the Cold War, many countries developed advanced interceptors to protect their airspace.
The air force deployed an interceptor to challenge the approaching enemy bombers.
The new interceptor is capable of reaching supersonic speeds within minutes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fighter - a military aircraft designed for air-to-air combat
