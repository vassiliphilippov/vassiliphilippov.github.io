pabulum

noun
bland or dull intellectual material
Pronunciation: / ˈpæbjələm / (AmE), / ˈpabjʊləm / (BrE)
Bland or insipid intellectual material that offers little stimulation or challenge.

Examples

Intellectual pabulum
Some television shows offer little more than intellectual pabulum to their viewers.
The novel was dismissed as mere pabulum for mass consumption, lacking real depth.
The speech was criticized for being pabulum that failed to inspire or provoke thought.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fodder - material that is considered readily available and of little value, often used for dull or mediocre intellectual material
  • degree higher: grist - something that can be used for a particular purpose, especially as material for thinking or discussion
