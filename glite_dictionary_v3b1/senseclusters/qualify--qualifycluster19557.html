qualify

verb
become fit or eligible; meet requirements
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɑlɪˌfaɪ /; / ˈkwɑːləˌfaɪ /; / ˈkwɑl.ɪ.faɪ / (AmE), / ˈkwɒlɪfaɪ /; / ˈkwɒl.ɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to have or make someone have the necessary skills, qualities, or conditions to be suitable or eligible for something

Examples

To qualify for a job
Only those who qualify will be invited to the next stage of the competition.
She hopes to qualify for the national swimming team next year.
You must qualify by passing three exams before you can join the program.
Her work experience qualifies her for a management position.
Only those who pass the exam will qualify for a scholarship.
The training program qualifies participants to become certified instructors.
After months of training, the coach finally qualified her to join the national team.
Only representatives who complete the course will be qualified to vote in the assembly.
The government recently qualified additional hospitals to provide this specialized treatment.
