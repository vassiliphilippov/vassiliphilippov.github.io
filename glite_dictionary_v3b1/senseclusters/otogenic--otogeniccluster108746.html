otogenic

adjective
from ear disease
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊtəˈdʒɛnɪk / (AmE), / ˌəʊtəˈdʒɛnɪk / (BrE)
originating in or caused by a disease or condition of the ear

Examples

Otogenic infection
The doctor diagnosed an otogenic infection that had spread from the middle ear.
Complications from otogenic sources can sometimes affect the brain if not treated promptly.
The surgeon explained that the abscess was otogenic in origin, resulting from chronic ear disease.
