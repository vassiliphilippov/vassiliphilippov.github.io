Lukasiewicz notation

noun
mathematical prefix notation system
9.9
Pronunciation: / luːˈkɑːʃəvɪtʃ noʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / luːˈkaʃəvɪtʃ nəʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
A parenthesis-free method of writing mathematical expressions in which each operator comes before its operands; also known as prefix notation.

Examples

Learning Lukasiewicz notation
Learning Lukasiewicz notation can help students understand the structure of complex formulas.
Many calculators use Lukasiewicz notation to allow for easier computation without parentheses.
The programming language was designed around Lukasiewicz notation to simplify parsing mathematical expressions.
