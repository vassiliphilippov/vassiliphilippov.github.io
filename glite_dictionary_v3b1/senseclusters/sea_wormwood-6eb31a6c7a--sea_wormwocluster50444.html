sea wormwood

noun
a coastal aromatic plant (Artemisia maritima)
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / si Ààw…úrm.w äd / (AmE), / siÀê Ààw…úÀêm.w äd / (BrE)
a perennial aromatic plant (Artemisia maritima) native to western and northern European coasts, with finely divided silvery leaves and small yellowish flower heads

Examples

A patch of sea wormwood
After the storm, a patch of sea wormwood thrived in the sandy dunes near the harbour.
Gardeners sometimes plant sea wormwood for its silvery foliage and resilience to saline soil.
The salt marshes along the coast are dotted with sea wormwood in the summer months.
