carbamic acid

noun
a hypothetical acid known via its salts/esters
9.9
Pronunciation: / kɑrˈbæmɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / kɑːˈbamɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
A hypothetical acid, H2NCOOH, known only through its salts and esters such as ammonium carbamate and urethane.

Examples

Carbamic acid salts
Carbamic acid itself cannot be isolated in pure form but is important in organic synthesis.
The reaction produces ammonium carbamate, a salt of carbamic acid.
Urethane is an ester derived from carbamic acid, often used in pharmaceuticals.
