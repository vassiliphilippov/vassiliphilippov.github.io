limpy

adjective
weak or unconvincing
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪmpi / (AmE), / ˈlɪmpi / (BrE)
lacking strength or firmness; weak, floppy, or unconvincing (literal or figurative)

Examples

Limpy excuse
His limpy excuse did not convince anyone in the meeting.
The team gave a limpy performance and lost the match easily.
She offered a limpy apology that failed to resolve the issue.
After the rain, the banner looked limpy as it drooped from the fence.
He wore a limpy hat that had lost its shape over time.
The dog's limpy ear flopped down while the other stood upright.

Related Terms

  • synonym: feeble - lacking physical or mental strength
  • synonym: floppy - soft and hanging down loosely
  • synonym: limp - not stiff or firm; lacking strength
  • antonym: rigid - stiff and not flexible
  • antonym: strong - having great power or effectiveness
