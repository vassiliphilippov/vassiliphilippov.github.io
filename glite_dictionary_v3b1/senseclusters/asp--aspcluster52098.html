asp

noun
venomous snake, symbol of ancient Egyptian power
Pronunciation: / æsp / (AmE), / asp /; / æsp / (BrE)
a venomous snake, especially one associated with ancient Egypt or the story of Cleopatra

Examples

The asp snake
In Egyptian art, the asp often appears as an emblem of royal power.
Legend says Cleopatra used an asp to end her life.
The ancient Pharaoh wore an asp on his crown as a sign of royalty and divine authority.
Hikers in the Mediterranean region are sometimes warned about the presence of asps.
The asp is smaller than the common adder but is still highly venomous.
The legendary queen Cleopatra is said to have died from the bite of an asp.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adder - a European venomous snake similar in appearance
  • synonym: cobra - a large, venomous snake known for its hood
  • synonym: viper - a type of venomous snake, related to the asp
