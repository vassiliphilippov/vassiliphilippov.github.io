decree

verb
order or decide officially
5.3 3.14/m
Pronunciation: / d…™Ààkri / (AmE), / d…™ÀàkriÀê / (BrE)
to officially order, decide, or declare something by authority

Examples

To decree a holiday
The King decreed a national day of celebration.
The court may decree the dissolution of the partnership if necessary.
The government decreed that all businesses must close by 8 p.m.
After a lengthy debate, the committee decreed that the new policy would take effect immediately.
The government decreed new regulations to protect the environment.
The king decreed that a festival would be held every spring.

Synonyms

  • ordain
    6.9 1.79/m
    ordain is used for formal orders often by church or law, while decree is a general official order by authority.
  • rule
    3.7 26/m
    rule means to make an official legal decision in a case, while decree is a formal authoritative order or declaration.
  • prescribe
    4.2 8.98/m
    prescribe states officially what should be done in a situation, while decree is an authoritative order that creates or enforces it.
  • enact
    4.7 14/m
    enact means to make a rule into law through formal process, while decree means to order or declare something by authority.
  • hold
    6.7 22/m
    hold refers to a court's formal legal decision in a case, while decree is an authoritative order issued by an authority.
  • declare
    3.7 63/m
    declare means to state or announce something publicly, while decree means to officially order or decide by authority.
