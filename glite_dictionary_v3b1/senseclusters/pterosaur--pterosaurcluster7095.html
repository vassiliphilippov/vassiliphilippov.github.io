pterosaur

noun
extinct flying reptile
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛr.əˌsɔr / (AmE), / ˈtɛr.ə.sɔː / (BrE)
an extinct flying reptile from the Jurassic and Cretaceous periods, characterized by a bird-like beak and membranous wings supported by an elongated fourth finger

Examples

A pterosaur flying
Scientists believe that the pterosaur could glide long distances using its membranous wings.
The fossilized bones of a pterosaur were discovered in the limestone quarry.
The museum's new exhibit features a life-sized model of a pterosaur in flight.
