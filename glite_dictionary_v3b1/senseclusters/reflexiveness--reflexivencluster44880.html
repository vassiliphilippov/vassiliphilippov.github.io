reflexiveness

noun
property of a relation; holds between element and itself
6.5 7.4
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈflek.sɪv.nəs/ /; / /rɪ.ˈflek.səv.nəs/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈflek.sɪv.nəs/ /; / /rɪ.ˈflek.səv.nəs/ (BrE)
(logic, mathematics) the property of a relation whereby it holds between any element and itself.

Examples

Reflexiveness of equality
In mathematics, the reflexiveness of the equality relation is taken for granted.
The reflexiveness of a relation means every element is related to itself.
To prove reflexiveness, you must show that each object stands in the relation to itself.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reflexivity - the quality or state of being reflexive; especially in mathematics, the property that a relation holds between an element and itself
