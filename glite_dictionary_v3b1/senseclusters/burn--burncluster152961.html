burn

verb
cause a sharp or stinging pain
1.0
Pronunciation: / bɝn / (AmE), / bɜːn / (BrE)
to feel or cause a sensation of heat, pain, or stinging, often from heat, sun, chemicals, or strong emotion

Examples

To burn your skin
After a day at the beach, his shoulders began to burn from the sun.
She didn't realize the lotion would burn on her sensitive skin.
The spicy sauce made my tongue burn for several minutes after eating it.
Despite the cloudy weather, she still managed to burn during her afternoon walk.
Don't forget your sunscreen or you'll burn after only an hour at the beach.
I tend to burn very quickly if I don't wear a hat on sunny days.
He burns to prove himself in the new position.
She was burning with curiosity as she waited for the results.
They burned with shame after realizing their mistake.

Related Terms

  • synonym: seethe - to be filled with intense but unexpressed emotion
  • synonym: smolder - to show suppressed strong emotion, especially anger; to burn slowly without flame
  • antonym: soothe - to ease or relieve pain or discomfort
  • synonym: sting - to cause a sharp or burning pain
  • synonym: sunburn - injury to the skin caused by excessive exposure to the sun
  • degree lower: tingle - to feel a slight stinging or prickling sensation
