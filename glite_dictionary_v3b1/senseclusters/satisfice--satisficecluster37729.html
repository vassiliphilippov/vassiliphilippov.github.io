satisfice

verb
choose a course meeting minimum requirements
Pronunciation: / ˈsætɪˌfaɪs / (AmE), / ˈsætɪˌfaɪs / (BrE)
to decide on and pursue a course of action that meets the minimum requirements for achieving a goal, rather than striving for the best possible outcome

Examples

To satisfice a goal
Faced with limited time, she decided to satisfice rather than seek the perfect answer.
Instead of conducting an exhaustive search, managers often satisfice by choosing the first acceptable solution they find.
Our team tends to satisfice when project deadlines are tight, meeting basic goals instead of aiming for excellence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: optimize - seek the best or most effective outcome
