criminologically

adverb
relating to criminology
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌkrɪmɪnəˈlɑdʒɪkli / (AmE), / ˌkrɪmɪnəˈlɒdʒɪkli / (BrE)
in a manner that relates to the study of crime and criminals

Examples

To criminologically analyze evidence
The evidence was criminologically analyzed to determine the suspect's motive.
This theory is criminologically important for understanding patterns of organized crime.
The report was written criminologically to appeal to experts in the field.
