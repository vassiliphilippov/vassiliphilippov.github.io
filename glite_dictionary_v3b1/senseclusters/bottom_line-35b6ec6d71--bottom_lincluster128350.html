bottom line

noun
final net profit or loss; key result
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑːɾəm laɪn / (AmE), / ˈbɒtəm laɪn / (BrE)
the final total of an account, balance sheet, or other financial document, showing net profit or loss; the most important outcome or result

Examples

Company bottom line
The company's bottom line improved significantly after the new strategy was implemented.
Ultimately, your expenses will affect your bottom line more than you think.
When the audit was complete, the bottom line showed a healthy profit for the year.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gross income - total earnings before deductions
  • synonym: net income - total profit after all expenses
