take over

verb
seize control without authority
Pronunciation: / teɪk ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / teɪk ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to gain control of or become the owner of a business or organization, especially by acquisition or force

Examples

To take over a company
A rival company tried to take over the family business through aggressive tactics.
After the king died suddenly, his brother sought to take over the throne.
The general attempted to take over the government after the president resigned.
A foreign investor tried to take over several local businesses in the region.
After the founder retired, her son decided to take over the company.
The larger corporation plans to take over its main competitor next year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acquire - to buy or obtain for oneself, especially a company (business context)
  • synonym: merge - to combine or cause to combine to form a single entity, especially in business
  • antonym: relinquish - to give up power or control voluntarily
  • synonym: seize - to take hold of suddenly and forcibly
  • antonym: sell off - to sell all or part of a company, often to another company
  • synonym: usurp - to take a position of power or importance illegally or by force
