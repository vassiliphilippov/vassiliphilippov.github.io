philo

x

Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈfɪləʊ / (BrE)
a combining form meaning 'loving' or 'fond of', used to create words that describe a love or fondness for something

Examples

Philo- prefix in words
The word philo is used in 'bibliophile' to mean someone who loves books.
In 'Anglophile', the combining form philo shows a fondness for English culture.
Many scientific terms use philo to indicate an attraction or affinity for something.

Related Terms

  • synonym: -phile - a suffix meaning 'a person who loves or is fond of something'
  • synonym: phil- - a prefix meaning 'loving' or 'fond of'
  • antonym: phobia - an extreme or irrational fear of something
