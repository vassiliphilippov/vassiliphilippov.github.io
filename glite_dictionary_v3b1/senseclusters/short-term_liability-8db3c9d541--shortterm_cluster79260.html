short-term liability

noun
liability due within one year
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɔrt tɜrm laɪəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːt tɜːm laɪəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
a financial obligation that is expected to be settled within one year

Examples

High short-term liabilities
High short-term liabilities can affect a firm's liquidity and credit rating.
The company must pay all short-term liabilities within the next twelve months.
When reviewing the balance sheet, analysts look at short-term liabilities to assess financial health.

Related Terms

  • antonym: long-term liability - a financial obligation due in more than one year
