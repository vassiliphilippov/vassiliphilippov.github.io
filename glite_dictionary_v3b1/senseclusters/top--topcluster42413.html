top

verb
reach the highest point of something
1.5 5.34/m
Pronunciation: / tÉ‘p / (AmE), / tÉ’p / (BrE)
to reach, be at, or form the highest point or part of something; to provide with a top

Examples

To top the cake
She topped the staircase and gazed out over the city.
The climbers topped the ridge just as the sun began to set.
We cheered as we finally topped the last hill of the marathon.
The athlete managed to top the bar in the high jump without grazing it.
The drone easily topped the fence as it moved across the field.
The river narrowly topped the rocks without causing any splashes.
A bright star tops the Christmas tree during the holidays.
A delicate sculpture topped the ornate building, drawing everyone's attention upwards.
The sundae was topped with a cherry and some whipped cream.

Synonyms

  • cap
    3.8 2.48/m
    cap means to cover or sit on the highest part of something, while top means to reach or form that highest point.
  • top out
    5.6 0.03/m
    top out is used to finish or add the top of a building, while top can also mean to reach the highest point.
  • culminate
    6.7 0.24/m
    culminate describes rising to a highest point in a formal way, while top is a more general verb for highest part.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cap - to cover or close the top or end of something
  • synonym: clear - to go over or past without touching
  • synonym: crown - to form the highest point or to complete the top of something
  • synonym: summit - to reach the summit (of a mountain)
