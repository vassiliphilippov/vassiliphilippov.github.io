Huntington's disease

noun
hereditary neurodegenerative disorder
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌntɪŋtənz dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈhʌntɪŋtənz dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A hereditary disorder of the central nervous system that typically develops in adulthood, causing progressive movement, cognitive, and psychiatric problems, often ending in dementia.

Examples

Huntington's disease symptoms
Genetic counseling is recommended for families with a history of Huntington's disease.
Her father was diagnosed with Huntington's disease when he was in his forties.
Scientists are researching new treatments for Huntington's disease to slow its progression.
