exceptionalist

adjective
belief in uniqueness
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈsɛpʃənəlɪst / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɛpʃənəlɪst / (BrE)
believing that something, especially a country, is unique or superior compared to others

Examples

Exceptionalist thinking
The leader's exceptionalist attitude influenced the country's foreign policy decisions.
Some historians criticize the exceptionalist perspective as ignoring global similarities.
Her exceptionalist beliefs made her proud of her nation's achievements.

Related Terms

  • antonym: egalitarian - believing in the equality of all people
  • synonym: nationalistic - having strong patriotic feelings or belief in national superiority
