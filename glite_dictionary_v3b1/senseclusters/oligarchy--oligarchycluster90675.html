oligarchy

noun
government by a small group
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːlɪˌɡɑːrki / (AmE), / ˈɒlɪˌɡɑːki / (BrE)
A form of government or control in which a small group of people holds power.

Examples

The oligarchy rules
Critics argue that without reforms, the system will always favor the interests of the oligarchy.
Many believe the country is shifting towards an oligarchy controlled by wealthy elites.
The ancient city-state was ruled by a powerful oligarchy of noble families.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aristocracy - government by a privileged minority or upper class
  • antonym: autocracy - government by one individual with absolute power
  • antonym: democracy - government by the people, typically through elected representatives
