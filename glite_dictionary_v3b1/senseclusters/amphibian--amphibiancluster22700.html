amphibian

adjective
of or relating to amphibians (frogs, etc.)
Pronunciation: / æmˈfɪbiən / (AmE), / amˈfɪbiən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of animals belonging to the class Amphibia, which includes frogs, toads, salamanders, and similar creatures that typically live both on land and in water.

Examples

Amphibian species study
Museum exhibits often feature amphibian skeletons alongside those of reptiles and mammals.
She studied the amphibian skin's ability to absorb water from its environment.
The scientists discovered an amphibian species that can tolerate extreme temperatures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reptilian - relating to or characteristic of reptiles
