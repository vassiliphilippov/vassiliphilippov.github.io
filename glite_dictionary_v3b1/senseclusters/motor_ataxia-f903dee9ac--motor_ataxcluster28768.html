motor ataxia

noun
loss of muscle coordination
9.9 6.8
Pronunciation: /ˈmoʊ.tər ə.ˈtæk.sɪ.ə/ /; / /ˈmoʊ.tər eɪ.ˈtæk.sɪ.ə/ (AmE), /ˈməʊ.tə ə.ˈtæk.sɪ.ə/ /; / /ˈməʊ.tə eɪ.ˈtæk.sɪ.ə/ (BrE)
A loss of control over voluntary muscle movements, resulting in unsteady movements and a staggering gait.

Examples

Motor ataxia symptoms
Damage to the cerebellum can often result in motor ataxia and coordination problems.
Physical therapy is recommended for individuals suffering from motor ataxia to improve balance and movement.
The patient showed signs of motor ataxia after the accident, struggling to walk steadily.
