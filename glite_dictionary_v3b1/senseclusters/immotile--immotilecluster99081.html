immotile

adjective
not capable of movement (biology)
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmoʊ.taɪl / (AmE), / ɪˈməʊ.taɪl / (BrE)
(of cells, spores, or microorganisms) not able to move; lacking the ability for self-propelled motion.

Examples

Immotile sperm cells
Because they are immotile, these sperm cells cannot fertilize an egg.
Researchers observed that the mutant spores were completely immotile in liquid culture.
The bacteria in this sample are immotile and remain fixed under the microscope.
