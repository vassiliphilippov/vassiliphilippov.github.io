turgor

noun
rigidity in a cell or tissue due to internal pressure
9.0 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / Ààt…úr…°…ôr / (AmE), / Ààt…úÀê…°…ô / (BrE)
(biology) The normal state of tension and rigidity in a cell or tissue, caused by internal fluid pressure pressing against the cell wall or membrane.

Examples

Skin turgor test
A decrease in skin turgor can be a sign of dehydration in patients.
The turgor of plant cells is essential for keeping leaves upright and firm.
When cells lose water, they lose turgor and become flaccid.
