lambaste

verb
criticize harshly
9.3
Pronunciation: / læmˈbeɪst / (AmE), / læmˈbɑːst / (BrE)
to criticize someone or something very severely, especially in public

Examples

To lambaste a speech
Several politicians lambasted the proposed policy during the debate.
She was lambasted by the critics for her controversial remarks.
The coach did not hesitate to lambaste the team after their disappointing performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: berate - to scold or criticize angrily
  • antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration
