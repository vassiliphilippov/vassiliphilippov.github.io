cable length

noun
nautical unit of length
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪ.bəl lɛŋkθ / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.bəl leŋkθ / (BrE)
A nautical unit of length equal to one tenth of a nautical mile, typically about 185 meters or 608 feet.

Examples

One cable length
Fishermen use a cable length to estimate safe distances when laying their nets.
Modern charts still indicate positions in cable lengths for reference.
The ship anchored half a cable length from the shore to avoid shallow water.
