stern

adjective
strict or forbidding in appearance
4.3 3.36/m
Pronunciation: / st…ùn / (AmE), / st…úÀên / (BrE)
serious, strict, and showing no signs of weakness or giving in; unrelenting in attitude or manner

Examples

Stern warning
He gave me a stern look when I broke the rule.
Her stern expression made it clear she would tolerate no nonsense.
The teacher's stern voice silenced the class immediately.
His stern warning convinced the children to behave properly during the trip.
The teacher was known for her stern approach to discipline in the classroom.
Under her stern supervision, the team's performance improved significantly.
He gave a stern warning about the dangers of cheating.
Her father's stern expression left no room for argument.
The teacher took a stern approach to discipline in the classroom.

Synonyms

  • strict
    3.2 22/m
    strict demands that rules are followed exactly and often punishes mistakes, while stern is more generally serious and unyielding.
  • unrelenting
    7.0 2.16/m
    unrelenting keeps pressure or action going without stopping, while stern focuses on a strict, serious manner in behavior.
  • severe
    4.2 5.65/m
    severe is harsh and unsparing in judgment or punishment, while stern is more about a serious, strict personal manner.
  • austere
    7.8 0.36/m
    austere looks simple, plain, or unfriendly in style, while stern shows strict seriousness in attitude or voice.
  • unforgiving
    5.8 1.99/m
    unforgiving will not forgive mistakes or show mercy, while stern can be strict yet not always refuse to pardon.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lenient - not strict; antonym in terms of demeanor
  • synonym: severe - very great; intense or strict in discipline
  • synonym: strict - enforcing rules or standards rigidly
