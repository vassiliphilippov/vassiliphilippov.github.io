retrograde

verb
move backward in orbit (astronomy)
5.9 7.0
Pronunciation: /ˈre.trə.greɪd/ /; / /ˈre.troʊ.greɪd/ (AmE), /ˈre.trə.greɪd/ /; / /ˈre.trəʊ.greɪd/ (BrE)
(of a celestial body) to move backward in its orbit, apparently reversing its usual direction as observed from a particular vantage point.

Examples

Ancient astronomers struggled to explain why some planets seemed to retrograde temporarily.
During certain times of the year, Mercury appears to retrograde as seen from Earth.
Jupiter is now starting to retrograde in the night sky, a phenomenon that fascinates astronomers.
