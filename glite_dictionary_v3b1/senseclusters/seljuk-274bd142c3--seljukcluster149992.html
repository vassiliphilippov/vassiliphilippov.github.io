Seljuk

noun
member of medieval Turkish dynasty
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛlˌdʒuk / (AmE), / ˈsɛl.dʒʊk / (BrE)
a member of any of several Turkish dynasties that ruled parts of Central Asia and the Middle East from the 11th to the 13th centuries, notable for their conquests and defense against the Crusaders

Examples

A Seljuk ruler
Historians often study the art and architecture of the Seljuk period.
The Seljuks established a powerful empire across Persia and Anatolia.
The rise of the Seljuks marked a turning point in medieval Middle Eastern history.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Ottoman - Member of the Turkish dynasty that succeeded the Seljuks in ruling much of the Middle East
