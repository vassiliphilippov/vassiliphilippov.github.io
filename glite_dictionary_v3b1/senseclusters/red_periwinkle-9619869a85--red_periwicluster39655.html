red periwinkle

noun
cultivated plant with pink-red flowers
Pronunciation: / rɛd ˈpɛriˌwɪŋkəl / (AmE), / rɛd ˈpɛriˌwɪŋkl̩ / (BrE)
A woody herb of the Old World, widely cultivated for its large pinkish to red flowers.

Examples

The red periwinkle flower
Botanists are interested in certain medicinal compounds found in the red periwinkle.
She planted several red periwinkles along the edge of the walkway for a continuous bloom.
The red periwinkle adds a splash of vibrant color to gardens in tropical regions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Madagascar periwinkle - a closely related or synonymous plant with pink or white flowers
  • degree lower: periwinkle - any plant of the genus Vinca or Catharanthus, especially with blue or purple flowers
