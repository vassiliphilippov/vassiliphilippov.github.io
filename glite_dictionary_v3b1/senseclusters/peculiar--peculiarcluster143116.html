peculiar

adjective
distinctive; characteristic of one only
7.6
Pronunciation: / pɪˈkjuːljɚ /; / pɪˈkjuljɚ / (AmE), / pɪˈkjuːljə /; / pɪˈkjuːliə / (BrE)
belonging or relating exclusively to one person, group, or place; characteristic or distinctive

Examples

Peculiar to this place
Each family has traditions peculiar to their own heritage.
That legal system has features peculiar to the country’s unique history.
The festival is peculiar to this region, with customs found nowhere else.
That phrase is peculiar to Scottish English and not used elsewhere.
There are privileges peculiar to members of the organization.
This species is peculiar to the islands of Hawaii.

Related Terms

  • synonym: distinctive - having a quality or characteristic that makes something different and easily noticed
  • antonym: general - common to a whole group, not specific or exclusive
  • synonym: particular - belonging specifically to one person or thing; not general
