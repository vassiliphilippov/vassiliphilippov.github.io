petit

adjective
small; minor
7.1
Pronunciation: / pəˈtiː / (AmE), / ˈpɛti / (BrE)
small in size; little, often used in legal or culinary contexts to describe something minor or of small amount

Examples

Petit theft
The lawyer explained the difference between a petit theft and a grand theft.
She ordered a petit salad as a light starter before the main course.
In some courts, a petit jury decides the outcome of a trial.

Related Terms

  • antonym: large - of considerable or relatively great size
  • synonym: minor - lesser in importance, seriousness, or size
  • synonym: small - of limited size; not large
