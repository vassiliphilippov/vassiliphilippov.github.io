lophophore

noun
tentacle feeding organ
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑfəˌfɔr / (AmE), / ˈlɒfəˌfɔː / (BrE)
a ring or crown of tentacles around the mouth of certain aquatic animals, used for feeding

Examples

The lophophore ring
The lophophore of a bryozoan is used to capture tiny food particles from the water.
Scientists observed the lophophore extending and retracting as the animal fed.
Brachiopods rely on their lophophore to filter nutrients from their aquatic environment.
