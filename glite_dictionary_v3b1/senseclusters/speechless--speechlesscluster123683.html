speechless

adjective
temporarily unable to speak
3.1 3.93/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàspiÀêt É.l…ôs / (AmE), / ÀàspiÀêt É.l…ôs / (BrE)
unable to speak, especially temporarily, as a result of shock, surprise, or strong emotion

Examples

Be speechless with anger
He stood speechless with anger, unable to find the words to respond.
She was so surprised by the news that she was speechless for several minutes.
The breathtaking scenery left everyone in the group completely speechless.

Synonyms

  • dumbstruck
    6.5 0.48/m
    dumbstruck is a plain word for being so shocked you cannot speak, while speechless describes temporary silence from strong emotion.
  • dumbfounded
    4.6 0.44/m
    dumbfounded leaves a person wide-eyed and frozen with surprise, while speechless means being unable to speak from shock or strong feeling.
  • flabbergasted
    6.7 0.53/m
    flabbergasted is a very informal, strong word showing extreme surprise, while speechless covers milder or simply temporary loss of words.
  • thunderstruck
    7.0 0.27/m
    thunderstruck feels dramatic and literary, showing deep shock that stops reaction, while speechless focuses on being briefly unable to speak.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dumb - unable to speak, often temporarily due to shock or emotion
  • antonym: vocal - expressing oneself readily and clearly
