roll call

noun
calling out a list of names to check attendance
Pronunciation: / roʊl kɔl / (AmE), / rəʊl kɔːl / (BrE)
the act of calling out a list of names to establish who is present, especially in a meeting, classroom, or group

Examples

Roll call at start
Please be on time for roll call at the start of your shift.
The teacher took roll call as soon as the students entered the classroom.
There was a daily roll call at the army camp to ensure everyone was present.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attendance - the action or state of being present
