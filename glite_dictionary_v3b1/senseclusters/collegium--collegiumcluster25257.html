collegium

noun
official group
Pronunciation: / kəˈliːdʒiəm / (AmE), / kəˈliːdʒiəm / (BrE)
a group of people with shared authority or function, especially in official, academic, or administrative contexts

Examples

University collegium meeting
The university's collegium met to discuss curriculum changes.
A collegium of experts was formed to oversee the research project.
Decisions were made collectively by the collegium rather than by a single leader.

Related Terms

  • synonym: council - an assembly for discussion or advice
