metasedimentary

adjective
changed sedimentary rock
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˌsɛdəˈmɛntəri / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˌsɛdɪˈmɛntəri / (BrE)
describing a sedimentary rock that has been changed by heat, pressure, or chemical processes into a metamorphic rock

Examples

Metasedimentary rock layer
The mountain range contains layers of metasedimentary rocks formed over millions of years.
Scientists study metasedimentary rocks to understand the geological history of the region.
A metasedimentary formation can reveal past conditions of heat and pressure deep within the Earth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: metamorphic - relating to rocks altered by heat and pressure
  • antonym: sedimentary - relating to rocks formed by the accumulation of sediment
