recut

adjective
edited again
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈkʌt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈkʌt / (BrE)
edited or altered again after the original version, especially of a film or recording

Examples

Recut movie scenes
The director released a recut version of the movie with additional scenes.
Fans preferred the recut album because it included remastered tracks.
After negative reviews, the studio quickly produced a recut trailer for the film.

Related Terms

  • antonym: original - existing from the beginning; not changed or edited
  • synonym: revised - changed or improved after the original version
