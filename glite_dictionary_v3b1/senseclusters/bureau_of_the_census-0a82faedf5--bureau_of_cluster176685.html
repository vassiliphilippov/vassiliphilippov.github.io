Bureau of the Census

proper noun
U.S. agency for demographic census data
4.6
Pronunciation: / bjʊˈroʊ əv ðə ˈsɛnsəs / (AmE), / bjʊəˈrəʊ əv ðə ˈsɛnsəs / (BrE)
the government agency of the U.S. Department of Commerce responsible for conducting the decennial census and providing demographic information about the population of the United States

Examples

The Bureau of the Census report
According to a recent report from the Bureau of the Census, the U.S. population grew by 7% in the last decade.
The Bureau of the Census releases detailed data on household income every year.
The government relies on information from the Bureau of the Census to allocate federal funding.
