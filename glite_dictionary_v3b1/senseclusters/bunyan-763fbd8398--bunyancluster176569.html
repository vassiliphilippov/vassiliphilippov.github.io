Bunyan

proper noun
legendary giant lumberjack in North American folklore
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌnjən / (AmE), / ˈbʌnjən / (BrE)
(in North American folklore) a legendary giant lumberjack of the north woods of the United States and Canada, known for his superhuman feats.

Examples

Bunyan the giant
According to legend, Bunyan created the Great Lakes with his enormous footprints.
Children gathered around to hear tales of Bunyan and his incredible adventures.
The story of Bunyan and his blue ox, Babe, is popular throughout the Midwest.
