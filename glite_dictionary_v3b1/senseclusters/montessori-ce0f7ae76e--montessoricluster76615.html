Montessori

adjective
relating to Montessori method
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːntəˈsɔːri / (AmE), / ˌmɒntəˈsɔːri / (BrE)
relating to the Montessori method of education, its materials, or its classroom practices, which emphasize self-directed activity and hands-on learning

Examples

Montessori classroom
The preschool uses a Montessori approach to encourage independence in young children.
Parents often choose Montessori schools for their focus on hands-on learning and creativity.
A Montessori classroom typically features child-sized furniture and a variety of learning materials.

Related Terms

  • synonym: progressive - favoring or implementing new, innovative educational methods
  • antonym: traditional - relating to conventional or established educational methods
