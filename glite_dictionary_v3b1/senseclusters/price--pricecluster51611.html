price

noun
cost of persuading to do wrong (bribe)
Pronunciation: / praɪs / (AmE), / praɪs / (BrE)
the sum of money or other consideration required to persuade someone to act dishonestly or betray their principles, especially as a bribe

Examples

His price for silence
It turned out his price was much lower than I expected.
She swore she had no price and could not be bribed.
They say every politician has a price.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bribe - a sum of money or gift given to persuade someone to act dishonestly
