paralipsis

noun
rhetorical device: emphasizing by seeming omission
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəˈlɪpsɪs / (AmE), / ˌparəˈlɪpsɪs / (BrE)
A rhetorical device in which an idea is emphasized by pretending to pass over it or suggesting that much more could be said.

Examples

Paralipsis in speech
In literature, paralipsis is often employed to draw attention to something while pretending to exclude it.
She cleverly resorted to paralipsis to hint at information she chose not to reveal.
The speaker used paralipsis when he said, 'I won't even mention his past mistakes.'

  • synonym: preterition - another term for this rhetorical device
