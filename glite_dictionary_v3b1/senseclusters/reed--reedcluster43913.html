reed

noun
tall marsh plant with hollow stems
Pronunciation: / rid / (AmE), / riːd / (BrE)
a tall, slender-leaved plant of the grass family, typically growing in marshy places, with hollow stems; especially species such as Arundo and Phragmites.

Examples

A reed plant
Herons often build their nests among the reeds near the lake.
The riverbank was lined with thick reeds swaying gently in the wind.
They used dried reed to make simple musical instruments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rush - a tall, grasslike plant of wet places, similar to reed
