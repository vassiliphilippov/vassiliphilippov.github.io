auditory sense

noun
the faculty of hearing
Pronunciation: / ˈɔːdəˌtɔri sɛns / (AmE), / ˈɔːdɪt(ə)ri sɛns / (BrE)
the sense that enables a person or animal to perceive sound; the faculty of hearing.

Examples

The auditory sense of hearing
Damage to the ear can impair a person's auditory sense.
Some animals have a more sensitive auditory sense than humans do.
The auditory sense allows us to detect a wide range of sounds in our environment.
