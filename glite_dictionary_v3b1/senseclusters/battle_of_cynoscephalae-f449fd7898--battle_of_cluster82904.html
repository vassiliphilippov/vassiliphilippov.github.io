battle of Cynoscephalae

proper noun
historical battle between Rome and Macedon, 197 BC
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbæt̬l əv ˌsaɪnəˈsɛfəˌli / (AmE), / ˈbætl əv ˌsaɪnəˈsɛfəˌliː / (BrE)
a decisive battle fought in 197 BC during the Second Macedonian War, in which Roman forces defeated Philip V of Macedon and ended Macedonian dominance in Greece

Examples

The battle of Cynoscephalae
During the battle of Cynoscephalae, Roman tactics overcame the traditional phalanx formation of the Macedonians.
Historians often discuss the battle of Cynoscephalae as a turning point in Roman expansion.
The aftermath of the battle of Cynoscephalae significantly weakened Macedonian influence in Greece.
