docility

noun
willingness to be taught or controlled
5.8
Pronunciation: / dɑːˈsɪləti / (AmE), / dɒˈsɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being willing to accept control or instruction; submissiveness

Examples

Excessive docility
Critics argue that excessive docility can hinder creativity and independent thinking.
Her docility made her a favorite among the instructors at the academy.
The farmer valued the docility of his new horse, as it responded calmly to every command.

Related Terms

  • synonym: obedience - the act or practice of following orders or instructions
  • antonym: rebelliousness - the quality of resisting control or authority
