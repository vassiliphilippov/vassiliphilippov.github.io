private corporation

noun
corporation with privately held shares
2.0
Pronunciation: / ˈpraɪvət ˌkɔɹpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈpraɪvət ˌkɔːpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
A corporation owned by a small number of individuals, with shares that are not offered for sale to the general public on stock exchanges.

Examples

A private corporation owner
Many family businesses choose to remain a private corporation to retain control over their operations.
She decided to invest in a private corporation rather than buy stock on the open market.
Unlike a public company, a private corporation is not required to disclose as much financial information.

Related Terms

  • antonym: public corporation - a corporation whose shares are traded freely on the stock exchange
