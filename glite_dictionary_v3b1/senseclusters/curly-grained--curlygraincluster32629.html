curly-grained

adjective
of wood, with irregular fiber pattern
Pronunciation: / ˈkɜrli ɡreɪnd / (AmE), / ˈkɜːli ɡreɪnd / (BrE)
(of timber) having fibers that run irregularly rather than in parallel, often creating a distinctive wavy or rippled appearance.

Examples

Curly-grained wood
Collectors often seek instruments crafted from curly-grained wood for their unique appearance.
Furniture made from curly-grained maple is especially valued for its beautiful, rippled effect.
The carpenter admired the curly-grained pattern in the slab of walnut he was about to use.
