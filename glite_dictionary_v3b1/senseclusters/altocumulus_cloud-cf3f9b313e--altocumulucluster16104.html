altocumulus cloud

noun
cloud of rounded masses at mid-level
6.5 5.7
Pronunciation: /æl.tə.ˈkjuː.mə.ləs klaʊd/ /; / /æl.tə.ˈkjuː.mjʊ.ləs klaʊd/ (AmE), /æl.tə.ˈkjuː.mə.ləs klaʊd/ /; / /æl.tə.ˈkjuː.mjʊ.ləs klaʊd/ (BrE)
A type of white or gray cloud forming rounded masses or rolls at intermediate altitudes, typically between 2,000 and 7,000 meters (6,500 to 23,000 feet).

Examples

Altocumulus clouds in sky
Meteorologists observed altocumulus clouds as a sign of changing weather conditions.
Pilots often report altocumulus clouds when flying at mid-level altitudes.
The sky was filled with altocumulus clouds that created a textured pattern above the city.
