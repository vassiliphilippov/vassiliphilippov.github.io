glyoxaline

noun
organic compound; histamine inhibitor
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɡlaɪˈɑksəˌleɪn / (AmE), / ɡlaɪˈɒksəleɪn / (BrE)
a colorless organic compound (C3H4N2) that is an isomer of imidazole and acts as a histamine inhibitor

Examples

Glyoxaline compound
Glyoxaline is commonly used in laboratories to study the inhibition of histamine in biological tissues.
Because of its role as a histamine inhibitor, glyoxaline is of interest in allergy treatments.
The researchers synthesized glyoxaline derivatives for use in pharmaceutical applications.
