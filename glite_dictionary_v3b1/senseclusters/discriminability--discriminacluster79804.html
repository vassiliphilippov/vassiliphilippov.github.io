discriminability

noun
ability to distinguish
Pronunciation: / dɪˌskrɪmɪnəˈbɪləti /; / dɪˌskrɪmɪnəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / dɪˌskrɪmɪnəˈbɪləti /; / dɪˌskrɪmɪnəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the ability to notice or tell the difference between things or categories

Examples

The discriminability of tests
The discriminability of the new medical test allows doctors to better identify different types of infections.
Researchers evaluated the discriminability of their machine learning model using several statistical measures.
High discriminability is important for exams that aim to separate advanced students from beginners.
The discriminability of the new security features makes it easier to spot fake documents.
Researchers tested the discriminability of colors in low light conditions.
High discriminability is important for students learning to recognize similar-sounding words.

Related Terms

  • antonym: confusion - a lack of clear distinction between categories or classes
  • synonym: discernment - the ability to judge well or recognize differences
  • synonym: discrimination - the ability to recognize or make distinctions between things
  • antonym: indistinguishability - the quality of not being able to be distinguished or told apart
  • synonym: sensitivity - the ability of a test to correctly identify true positives
