cocci

noun
spherical bacteria
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑksaɪ / (AmE), / ˈkɒksaɪ / (BrE)
spherical or nearly spherical bacteria, especially as a morphological category in microbiology.

Examples

Spherical cocci bacteria
Staphylococcus and Streptococcus are both types of cocci commonly found in the human body.
The lab technician identified the bacterial sample as cocci based on its round shape under the microscope.
These cocci are known to cause respiratory infections in children.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bacilli - rod-shaped bacteria
