low-cost

adjective
inexpensive; not costing much
2.0 5.27/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆloÊŠ ËŒkÉ‘st / (AmE), / ËˆlÉ™ÊŠ ËŒkÉ’st / (BrE)
available at a price that is not high; inexpensive to buy or maintain.

Examples

Low-cost flights
Many airlines now offer low-cost flights to popular destinations across Europe.
She prefers low-cost options when shopping for everyday items.
The government is investing in low-cost housing for families with modest incomes.

Synonyms

  • inexpensive
    1.0 12/m
    inexpensive describes something that costs relatively little and sounds neutral, while low-cost highlights low purchase or maintenance cost.
  • low-priced
    1.0 1.03/m
    low-priced refers to a product sold at a lower price, while low-cost stresses both buying and upkeep being inexpensive.
  • affordable
    2.1 21/m
    affordable means something many people can pay for, while low-cost simply says the price or upkeep is not high.
  • cheap
    1.0 59/m
    cheap describes a very low price and often poor quality, while low-cost focuses on low price without a quality judgment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cheap - costing little money; low in price
  • antonym: expensive - costing a lot of money
