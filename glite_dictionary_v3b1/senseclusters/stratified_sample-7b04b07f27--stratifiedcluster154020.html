stratified sample

noun
sample from subdivided population
5.2 4.4
Pronunciation: /ˈstræt.ə.faɪd ˈsæm.pəl/ /; / /ˈstræt.ɪ.faɪd ˈsæm.pəl/ (AmE), /ˈstræt.ɪ.faɪd ˈsɑːm.pəl/ /; / /ˈstræt.ə.faɪd ˈsɑːm.pəl/ (BrE)
A sample drawn from a population that has been divided into distinct subgroups, with random samples taken from each subgroup.

Examples

A stratified sample method
A stratified sample can reduce sampling bias by accounting for important subgroups in the population.
A stratified sample was chosen to ensure all income levels were included in the study.
To improve accuracy, the researcher used a stratified sample representing different age groups.
