tauri

noun
young star
Pronunciation: / ˈtɔːraɪ / (AmE), / ˈtɔːraɪ / (BrE)
a young or newly formed star, especially one in the early stages of stellar evolution

Examples

A young tauri star
Astronomers study tauri to learn about the earliest stages of star formation.
The Orion Nebula contains many tauri that are still developing.
Observing a tauri can reveal how planets might form around young stars.

Related Terms

  • antonym: main sequence star - a mature star in the stable phase of its life cycle
  • synonym: protostar - a very young star still gathering mass from its parent molecular cloud
