hydraulic engineering

noun
branch of civil engineering for moving water
Pronunciation: / haɪˈdrɑːlɪk ˌɛn.dʒɪˈnɪr.ɪŋ / (AmE), / haɪˈdrɔːlɪk ˌɛn.dʒɪˈnɪə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
the branch of civil engineering concerned with the use and control of moving water, especially for the design of dams, canals, and water supply systems

Examples

Hydraulic engineering design dams
Advances in hydraulic engineering have greatly improved urban flood control.
After graduating, she specialized in hydraulic engineering and worked on major dam projects.
Many public works depend on principles developed in hydraulic engineering.
