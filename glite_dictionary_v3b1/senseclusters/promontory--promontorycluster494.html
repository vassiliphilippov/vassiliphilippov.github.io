promontory

noun
high land projecting into water
8.1 0.78/m
Pronunciation: / Ààpr…ëÀê.m…ônÀåt…îÀêr.i / (AmE), / Ààpr…í.m…ôn.t…ôri / (BrE)
a high point of land or rock that juts out into a body of water, especially the sea

Examples

A promontory by the sea
From the promontory, you can see for miles along the rugged coastline.
The old lighthouse stands on a rocky promontory above the crashing waves.
We hiked to the end of the promontory to watch the sunset over the water.

Synonyms

  • headland
    6.2 0.64/m
    headland is a high, steep coastal area that juts into the sea, while promontory is a high point of land or rock.
  • foreland
    9.2 0.03/m
    foreland is a stretch of coast that sticks out into the sea and may be low or broad, while promontory is a high jutting point.
  • pointe
    7.9 0.61/m
    pointe is a place-name word for a small point of land into the water, while promontory is a general high jutting point.

Related Terms

  • synonym: headland - a narrow piece of land that sticks out from the coast into the sea
