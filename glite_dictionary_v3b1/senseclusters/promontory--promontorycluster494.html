promontory

noun
high land projecting into water
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑː.mənˌtɔːr.i / (AmE), / ˈprɒ.mən.təri / (BrE)
a high point of land or rock that juts out into a body of water, especially the sea

Examples

A promontory by the sea
From the promontory, you can see for miles along the rugged coastline.
The old lighthouse stands on a rocky promontory above the crashing waves.
We hiked to the end of the promontory to watch the sunset over the water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: headland - a narrow piece of land that sticks out from the coast into the sea
