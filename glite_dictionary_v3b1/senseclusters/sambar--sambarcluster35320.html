sambar

noun
large Asian deer with three-tined antlers
Pronunciation: / ˈsæmbɑr / (AmE), / ˈsæmbɑː / (BrE)
A large deer native to southern Asia, characterized by antlers with three tines.

Examples

Wild sambar deer
A sambar stood motionless by the riverbank, alert to any sign of danger.
Male sambars are known for their impressive antlers that feature three distinct tines.
The sambar is commonly found in forests across India and Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deer - hoofed grazing animal of the family Cervidae
