Brittanic

noun
southern group of Celtic languages
8.1
Pronunciation: / brɪˈtænɪk / (AmE), / brɪˈtanɪk / (BrE)
A group of southern Celtic languages, including Welsh, Cornish, and Breton.

Examples

Brittanic languages
Cornish is considered a revived member of the Brittanic group.
Scholars often compare Goidelic and Brittanic branches of the Celtic family.
The Brittanic languages include Welsh, Cornish, and Breton.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Brythonic - another term for the Brittanic group of Celtic languages
  • antonym: Goidelic - a northern group of Celtic languages, including Irish, Scottish Gaelic, and Manx
