recurring decimal

noun
decimal with repeating digits
4.9 5.3
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈkɜːr.ɪŋ ˈde.sɪ.məl/ /; / /riː.ˈkɜːr.ɪŋ ˈde.sə.məl/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈkɜː.rɪŋ ˈde.sɪ.məl/ /; / /riː.ˈkɜː.rɪŋ ˈde.sə.məl/ (BrE)
A decimal in which a sequence of one or more digits repeats itself indefinitely after the decimal point.

Examples

A recurring decimal number
0.333... is an example of a recurring decimal because the digit 3 repeats forever.
Every rational number can be expressed as either a terminating or a recurring decimal.
When you divide 1 by 7, the result is a long recurring decimal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: repeating decimal - a decimal with repeating digits after the decimal point
  • antonym: terminating decimal - a decimal that has a finite number of digits after the decimal point
