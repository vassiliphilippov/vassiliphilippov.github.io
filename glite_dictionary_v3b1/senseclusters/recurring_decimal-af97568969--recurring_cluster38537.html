A decimal in which a sequence of one or more digits repeats itself indefinitely after the decimal point.

Examples

A recurring decimal number
0.333... is an example of a recurring decimal because the digit 3 repeats forever.
Every rational number can be expressed as either a terminating or a recurring decimal.
When you divide 1 by 7, the result is a long recurring decimal.

Synonyms

  • repeating decimal
    4.2 0.01/m
    repeating decimal names a decimal whose digits repeat indefinitely, while recurring decimal is a closely matching term often used in maths texts.
  • circulating decimal
    7.9 0.00/m
    circulating decimal is an older or regional name for a decimal with a repeating digit block, while recurring decimal is the common modern term.

Related Terms
