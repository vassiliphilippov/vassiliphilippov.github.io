polyrhythm

noun
simultaneous rhythms
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑliˌrɪðəm / (AmE), / ˈpɒliˌrɪðəm / (BrE)
a musical effect created by playing two or more different rhythms at the same time

Examples

A polyrhythm pattern
The jazz band used a polyrhythm to make the music sound more complex and interesting.
Learning to play a polyrhythm can be challenging for beginner drummers.
African music often features polyrhythm as a key part of its traditional sound.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monorhythm - music with only one rhythm at a time
  • synonym: syncopation - a shifting of the normal accent in music to create unexpected rhythms
