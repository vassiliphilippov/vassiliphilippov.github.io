expectorant

noun
medicine promoting removal of mucus
7.1 5.4
Pronunciation: /ɪk.ˈspek.tə.rənt/ /; / /ɛk.ˈspek.tə.rənt/ (AmE), /ɪk.ˈspek.tə.rənt/ /; / /ɛk.ˈspek.tə.rənt/ (BrE)
A medicine that helps to clear mucus or phlegm from the airways, especially the lungs, bronchi, and trachea.

Examples

An expectorant medicine
Many over-the-counter medicines act as expectorants to relieve chest congestion.
The doctor prescribed an expectorant to help loosen the mucus in his chest.
Using an expectorant can make it easier to cough up phlegm during a cold.
