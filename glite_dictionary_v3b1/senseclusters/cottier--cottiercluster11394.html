cottier

noun
medieval rural tenant or laborer
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑtiər / (AmE), / ˈkɒtɪə / (BrE)
a rural laborer or tenant who occupied a cottage and small plot of land, especially under the system prevalent in England and Ireland in the medieval period

Examples

A cottier in medieval England
As a cottier, he had few rights and owed labor to the local lord.
In medieval England, a cottier typically farmed a small plot of land attached to a cottage.
The landlord collected rent from each cottier at the end of the harvest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: serf - an agricultural laborer bound under the feudal system to work on his lord's estate
  • synonym: villein - a feudal tenant entirely subject to a lord or manor
