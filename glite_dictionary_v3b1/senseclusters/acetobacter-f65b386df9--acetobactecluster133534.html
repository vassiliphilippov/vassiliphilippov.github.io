Acetobacter

proper noun
bacteria for vinegar
7.9
Pronunciation: / əˌsiːtəˈbæktər / (AmE), / əˌsiːtəˈbæktə / (BrE)
a genus of bacteria that convert alcohol into acetic acid, commonly used in vinegar production

Examples

Acetobacter bacteria
Acetobacter is essential in the process of turning wine into vinegar.
Scientists studied Acetobacter to improve the efficiency of vinegar fermentation.
The presence of Acetobacter can be detected in many natural fermentation environments.
