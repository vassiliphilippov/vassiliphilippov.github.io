gastrulation

noun
process of gastrula formation in embryology
Pronunciation: / ɡæstrəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ɡæstrʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process during embryonic development in which a gastrula forms from a blastula by the inward migration of cells

Examples

Gastrulation process
Gastrulation is a crucial step in the early development of all multicellular animals.
Failure in gastrulation can lead to serious developmental abnormalities.
Researchers studied how cell migration occurs during gastrulation in zebrafish embryos.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: blastula - an early stage of embryonic development prior to gastrulation
  • degree higher: neurulation - developmental stage following gastrulation involving formation of the neural tube
