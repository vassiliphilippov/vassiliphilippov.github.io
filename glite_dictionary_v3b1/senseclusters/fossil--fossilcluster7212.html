fossil

noun
preserved remains of ancient life
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑsəl / (AmE), / ˈfɒsəl / (BrE)
the remains or impression of a prehistoric plant or animal that have been preserved in rock and excavated from the earth

Examples

Dinosaur fossil
Paleontologists study fossils to learn about ancient life on Earth.
She found a fossil embedded in the limestone while hiking.
The museum displays a fossil of a dinosaur found in the desert.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fossilize - to become or make into a fossil
  • synonym: petrifaction - the process of turning organic material into stone
