reconstructionism

noun
belief in reforming traditions
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkənˈstrʌkʃənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˈstrʌkʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
a movement or belief in reconstructing, reinterpreting, or reforming traditional systems, beliefs, or practices

Examples

Reconstructionism in religion
The rise of reconstructionism led many communities to rethink their religious customs.
Some educators promote reconstructionism to update outdated teaching methods.
Modern reconstructionism often challenges long-standing cultural traditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reformism - the belief in gradual or moderate change to improve a system
  • antonym: traditionalism - the belief in maintaining traditional values and practices
