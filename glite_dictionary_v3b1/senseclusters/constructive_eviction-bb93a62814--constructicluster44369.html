constructive eviction

noun
landlord's act forcing tenant to leave premises
Pronunciation: / kənˈstrʌktɪv ɪˈvɪkʃən / (AmE), / kənˈstrʌktɪv ɪˈvɪkʃən / (BrE)
an act by a landlord that forces a tenant to leave leased premises by making them unfit or impossible to occupy, without physically expelling the tenant or using legal process

Examples

Constructive eviction by landlord
A landlord who shuts off the water and heat may be guilty of constructive eviction.
Breaking the lease due to constructive eviction is sometimes allowed under the law.
The tenant claimed constructive eviction after repeated flooding made the apartment uninhabitable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: eviction - the action of expelling someone from a property
