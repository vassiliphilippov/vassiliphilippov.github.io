update

verb
bring something up to date
Pronunciation: / ʌpˈdeɪt / (AmE), / ʌpˈdeɪt / (BrE)
to make something more modern or current by adding new information, improvements, or technology

Examples

To update software
I need to update my resume before applying for the new job.
She updated the committee on the latest changes in the regulations.
The software automatically updates itself whenever a new version is available.
She is updating her résumé to include her latest job experience.
The website was updated with new photos and information last week.
You should update your software regularly to protect against security threats.
She hired a contractor to update her bathroom with modern fixtures.
The software will automatically update your files to the latest version.
We're planning to update the company website next month.

  • synonym: refresh - to update or make current, especially digital content or data
  • synonym: renew - to make new or fresh again
  • synonym: upgrade - to raise something to a higher standard, often technologically
