spot price

noun
current price for immediate commodity delivery
Pronunciation: / spɑt praɪs / (AmE), / spɒt praɪs / (BrE)
the current price at which a commodity can be bought or sold for immediate delivery in the spot market.

Examples

The spot price today
At the end of the trading day, the spot price for oil was at its highest level this month.
The spot price of gold rose sharply due to increased demand.
Traders closely monitor the spot price when deciding whether to buy or sell commodities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: futures price - price at which a commodity is agreed to be bought or sold for future delivery
