into the wind

adverb
against the direction of the wind
Pronunciation: / ˈɪn tu ðə wɪnd / (AmE), / ˈɪn tə ðə wɪnd / (BrE)
in the direction directly opposing the direction from which the wind is blowing

Examples

Fly into the wind
Planes often take off into the wind to gain extra lift from air resistance.
The crew struggled to row into the wind as waves crashed over the sides of the boat.
The golfer aimed carefully before driving the ball into the wind.

Related Terms

  • antonym: downwind - in the direction in which the wind is blowing
