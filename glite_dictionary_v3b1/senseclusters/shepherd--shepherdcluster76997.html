shepherd

noun
clergyman guiding a congregation
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɛpərd / (AmE), / ˈʃɛpəd / (BrE)
a clergyman who cares for and guides a congregation or group of people, especially in a spiritual context

Examples

Church shepherd
As a shepherd of his flock, Reverend Martin visited each family in times of need.
Many considered her the shepherd of the small rural parish.
The shepherd delivered a heartfelt sermon that inspired the entire community.
