polemicize

verb
engage in controversy or argue forcefully
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / pəˈlɛmɪˌsaɪz / (AmE), / pəˈlɛmɪsaɪz / (BrE)
to engage in a heated or argumentative discussion or controversy, especially on controversial, political, or intellectual topics.

Examples

To polemicize strongly
Historians frequently polemicize about the causes of major world events.
She tends to polemicize whenever political topics arise in conversation.
The scholars continued to polemicize over the interpretation of the ancient texts.
He tended to polemicise in his writing, challenging prevailing views with fierce arguments.
Instead of seeking common ground, she chose to polemicise on divisive topics in her column.
The professors would often polemicise over the finer points of philosophy during their meetings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agree - to have the same opinion
  • synonym: argue - to give reasons for or against something; to debate
  • synonym: debate - to discuss or argue about something formally
