observability

noun
degree of detectability
7.7
Pronunciation: / əbˌzɜːrvəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / əbˌzɜːvəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the degree to which something can be seen, detected, or noticed, especially by surveillance or sensors

Examples

Observability of the target
The observability of the submarine was minimized to avoid detection by enemy sonar.
Engineers improved the observability of the system to help operators monitor its performance.
Reducing observability is a key goal in designing modern stealth aircraft.

Related Terms

  • synonym: detectability - the quality of being able to be discovered or identified
  • antonym: stealth - the quality of being difficult to detect or observe
  • synonym: visibility - the state of being able to be seen
