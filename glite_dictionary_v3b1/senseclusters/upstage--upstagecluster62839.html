upstage

adverb
toward the back of the stage
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpˌsteɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈʌpsteɪdʒ / (BrE)
at or toward the rear part of a stage (from the audience's point of view).

Examples

Move upstage
During the performance, she subtly retreated upstage to let others take focus.
Please enter from upstage right as the curtain rises.
The actors moved upstage to reveal the new set piece.
