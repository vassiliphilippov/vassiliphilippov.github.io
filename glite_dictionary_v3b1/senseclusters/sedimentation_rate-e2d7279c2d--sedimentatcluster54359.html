sedimentation rate

noun
rate at which red blood cells settle in blood
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛdəˌmɛnˈteɪʃən reɪt / (AmE), / ˌsɛdɪmɛnˈteɪʃən reɪt / (BrE)
the rate at which red blood cells settle in a tube of blood under standard conditions, used as an indicator of inflammation or disease

Examples

High sedimentation rate
A high sedimentation rate can indicate infection or autoimmune disease.
After treatment, her sedimentation rate returned to normal levels.
The doctor ordered a sedimentation rate test to check for signs of inflammation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: erythrocyte sedimentation rate - medical term for sedimentation rate, abbreviated as ESR
