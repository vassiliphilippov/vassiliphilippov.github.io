dead-man's float

noun
face-down floating swimming position
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛdˌmænz floʊt / (AmE), / ˈdɛdˌmænz fləʊt / (BrE)
A floating position in swimming where the body is facedown in the water with arms extended forward, used typically to conserve energy or rest.

Examples

The dead-man's float position
If you get tired while swimming, you can use the dead-man's float to catch your breath.
Learning the dead-man's float helps swimmers stay safe in deep water.
The instructor taught the beginner to relax in the dead-man's float before attempting other strokes.
