lustration

noun
political cleansing process
9.9
Pronunciation: /lʌs.ˈtreɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /lʌs.ˈtreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of purifying or cleansing, especially by removing people associated with a previous regime from positions of power

Examples

Political lustration process
After the revolution, the country began a process of lustration to remove officials linked to the old regime.
The committee debated whether lustration was necessary to restore public trust in government.
Many historians study the effects of lustration on post-communist societies in Eastern Europe.

Related Terms

  • synonym: purge - the removal of people considered undesirable from an organization or government
  • antonym: rehabilitation - restoring someone to a former position or status
