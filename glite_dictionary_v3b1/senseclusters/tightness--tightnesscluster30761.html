tightness

noun
limited availability of money or credit
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈtaɪtnəs / (BrE)
a situation in which money or credit is scarce or difficult to obtain, especially in a financial or economic context

Examples

Financial tightness
Periods of financial tightness often lead to higher interest rates and reduced investment.
The central bank intervened to reduce the tightness of the money supply.
The recent tightness in the credit market has made it difficult for small businesses to secure loans.

Related Terms

  • antonym: liquidity - the availability of liquid assets to a market or company
