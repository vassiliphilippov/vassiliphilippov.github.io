sow

verb
introduce or spread (an idea or feeling)
5.9
Pronunciation: / soʊ / (AmE), / səʊ / (BrE)
to introduce or spread (an idea, feeling, or condition) in a particular place or among people, often with the implication of causing it to develop over time

Examples

To sow fear
Political leaders sometimes sow distrust among communities for their own gain.
Rumors were deliberately sown to create confusion before the election.
The teacher hoped to sow a love of learning in her students.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plant - to put (an idea, feeling, etc.) firmly in someone's mind
