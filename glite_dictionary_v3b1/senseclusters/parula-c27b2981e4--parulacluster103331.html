Parula

noun
data-visualization colormap
8.5
Pronunciation: / pəˈruːlə / (AmE), / pəˈruːlə / (BrE)
(in data visualization) a colormap — a specific sequence of colours used to represent numerical data values on plots and charts; originally created for MATLAB and later adopted in other plotting libraries.

Examples

Parula colormap
The plot used the Parula colormap to display temperature variations across the map.
She changed the heatmap's colours to Parula because its gradient shows smooth value transitions.
Many data scientists prefer Parula over rainbow colormaps for readability and perceptual uniformity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: colormap - a mapping from numerical data values to colours used in visualisation
  • synonym: viridis - another perceptually uniform colormap commonly used in plotting libraries
