freezing mixture

noun
mixture for producing very low temperatures
Pronunciation: / ˈfriːzɪŋ ˈmɪkstʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈfriːzɪŋ ˈmɪkstʃə / (BrE)
a mixture, typically of salt and ice, used to lower the temperature below the freezing point of water

Examples

Salt and freezing mixture
By adding salt to the ice, the freezing mixture can cool the flask far below zero.
Chemists often use a freezing mixture to conduct experiments at low temperatures.
He prepared a freezing mixture in the laboratory to make ice cream without a freezer.
