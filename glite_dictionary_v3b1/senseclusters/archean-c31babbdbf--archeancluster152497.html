Archean

noun
geologic era; early Earth; first life forms
6.4 9.9
Pronunciation: / ɑrˈkiː.ən / (AmE), / ɑːˈkiː.ən / (BrE)
The geologic era spanning from about 3,800 million to 2,500 million years ago, during which Earth's crust formed and the earliest unicellular life appeared.

Examples

The Archean era
During the Archean, Earth's atmosphere was very different from what it is today.
Scientists study Archean fossils to learn about the planet's earliest life forms.
The oldest rocks on Earth are believed to have formed during the Archean.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: Hadean - the geologic eon before the Archean
  • degree higher: Proterozoic - the geologic era following the Archean
