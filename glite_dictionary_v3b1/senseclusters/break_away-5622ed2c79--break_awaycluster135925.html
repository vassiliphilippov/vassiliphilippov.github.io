break away

verb
interrupt a continued activity or pattern
1.0 3.2
Pronunciation: /breɪk ə.ˈweɪ/ (AmE), /breɪk ə.ˈweɪ/ (BrE)
to stop doing something that has been continuing, especially in order to do something different or new; to interrupt a pattern or activity

Examples

To break away from routine
After ten years in the company, she decided to break away from the routine and start her own business.
He finally managed to break away from his family's expectations and pursue a career in music.
It's not easy to break away from habits you've had since childhood.
