hydroelectric turbine

noun
turbine for generating electricity from water power
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪdroʊɪˈlɛktrɪk tɝːˈbaɪn / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrəʊɪˈlɛktrɪk ˈtɜːbaɪn / (BrE)
a turbine used in hydroelectric power plants to convert the energy of flowing water into mechanical energy for generating electricity

Examples

A hydroelectric turbine unit
Engineers installed a new hydroelectric turbine to improve the dam's energy output.
Modern hydroelectric turbines can generate enough electricity to power entire cities.
The efficiency of a hydroelectric turbine depends on the design and speed of the water flow.

Related Terms

  • synonym: turbine - a machine for producing continuous power in which a wheel or rotor is made to revolve by a fast-moving flow of water, steam, gas, or air
