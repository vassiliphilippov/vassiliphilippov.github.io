kick-out

verb
to eject someone
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪk aʊt / (AmE), / ˈkɪk aʊt / (BrE)
to force someone to leave a place, group, or job, often suddenly or against their will

Examples

To kick-out a member
The manager decided to kick-out the disruptive customer from the restaurant.
If you break the rules again, they might kick-out you from the team.
After several warnings, the landlord finally kicked-out the tenants for not paying rent.

Related Terms

  • antonym: admit - to allow someone to enter a place or group
  • synonym: eject - to force someone to leave a place or position
  • synonym: expel - to officially force someone to leave a place or organization
