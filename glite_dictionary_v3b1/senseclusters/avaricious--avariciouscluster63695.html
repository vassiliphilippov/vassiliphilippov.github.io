avaricious

adjective
excessively greedy for wealth or gain
Pronunciation: / ˌævəˈrɪʃəs / (AmE), / ˌævəˈrɪʃəs / (BrE)
Having an extreme or insatiable desire for wealth or gain; greedy.

Examples

An avaricious person
Driven by his avaricious nature, he would stop at nothing to accumulate more wealth.
Many critics denounced the corporation's avaricious pursuit of profit over public welfare.
The avaricious landlord continued to raise rents despite complaints from his tenants.

Related Terms

  • antonym: generous - showing kindness and willingness to give more than necessary
  • synonym: greedy - having or showing an intense and selfish desire for something
