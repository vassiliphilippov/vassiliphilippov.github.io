redness

noun
red coloration of tissues due to irritation or injury
3.7 1.36/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆrÉ›dnÉ™s / (AmE), / ËˆrÉ›dnÉ™s / (BrE)
A physical condition in which the skin or other tissues become red, often as a result of injury, irritation, or inflammation, typically accompanied by pain, swelling, and heat.

Examples

Skin redness
Prolonged sun exposure can cause redness and discomfort to the skin.
She noticed a slight redness and swelling after bumping her arm.
The redness around the wound indicated possible infection.

Synonyms

  • erythema
    9.5 0.61/m
    erythema is medical redness caused by blood vessel widening, while redness is a general skin change often with pain, swelling, and heat.
  • rubor
    9.9 0.00/m
    rubor is the classic medical sign of inflammation seen as local redness, whereas redness can describe any skin redness, often with heat and swelling.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inflammation - a localized physical condition in which part of the body becomes reddened, swollen, hot, and often painful
