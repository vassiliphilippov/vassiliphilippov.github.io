seral

noun
succession stage
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪrəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪərəl / (BrE)
a stage or community in ecological succession, representing a temporary phase before reaching a stable climax community

Examples

A seral community
After the wildfire, the area developed a seral community of grasses before trees returned.
Ecologists studied the seral stages to understand how the forest would recover over time.
A seral phase often includes fast-growing plants that prepare the environment for later species.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: climax community - the final, stable community in ecological succession
  • synonym: succession - the process of change in the species structure of an ecological community over time
