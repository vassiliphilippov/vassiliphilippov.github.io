stroke

noun
sudden brain attack; loss of blood supply
2.4 3.1
Pronunciation: /stroʊk/ /; / /strəʊk/ (AmE), /strəʊk/ (BrE)
A sudden, serious medical condition in which the blood supply to part of the brain is cut off, causing loss of consciousness, movement, or speech.

Examples

Suffer a stroke
Early signs of a stroke may include sudden numbness or confusion.
He suffered a stroke and was rushed to the hospital for treatment.
Quick medical attention can greatly increase the chances of recovery after a stroke.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cerebrovascular accident - medical term for a stroke
  • degree lower: transient ischemic attack - brief, temporary blockage of blood to the brain, 'mini-stroke'
