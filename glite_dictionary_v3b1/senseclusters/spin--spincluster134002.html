spin

verb
revolve rapidly around own axis
Pronunciation: / spɪn / (AmE), / spɪn / (BrE)
to turn around quickly or cause something to turn rapidly (used for objects, people, or aircraft)

Examples

To spin around
Dancers would spin across the stage in perfect synchronization.
The little girl loves to spin in circles until she gets dizzy.
The top continued to spin for more than a minute before falling over.
Could you spin the wheel to see who wins the prize?
He decided to spin the globe to pick his next travel destination.
She likes to spin her ring absent-mindedly while thinking.
After the announcement, rumors started to spin out of control on social media.
The dancer began to spin gracefully across the stage, her dress swirling around her.
The strong gusts of wind made the empty bottle spin across the street.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rotate - move in a circle around an axis
  • antonym: steady - to remain constant and controlled, not chaotic
  • antonym: stop - cease to move or rotate
  • synonym: twirl - turn around and around quickly
  • synonym: whirl - move or be moved rapidly around and around
