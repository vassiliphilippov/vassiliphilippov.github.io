encounter

verb
to experience or face (something)
3.6
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkaʊntər / (AmE), / ɪnˈkaʊntə / (BrE)
to meet or come across someone or something, or to experience something, especially unexpectedly or by chance

Examples

To encounter a problem
During her travels, she often encountered unexpected challenges along the way.
The company encountered significant resistance when proposing new policies.
You may encounter some difficulties during the installation process, but support is available.
During the trip, we encountered some unexpected difficulties with the weather.
She encountered many obstacles while trying to start her own business.
The scientists often encounter technical challenges during their research.
During our trip, we encountered several unexpected delays due to road construction.
I never expected to encounter an old friend at such a remote location.
On her walk through the forest, she encountered a family of deer grazing by the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • antonym: avoid - to stay away from someone or something
  • synonym: come across - to find or meet by chance
  • synonym: confront - to face up to and deal with a problem or difficult situation
  • synonym: face - to confront or deal with something difficult
  • synonym: meet - to experience (something, usually negative or challenging)
  • antonym: miss - to fail to meet or find someone or something
