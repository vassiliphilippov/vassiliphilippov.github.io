bobbin lace

noun
handmade lace made with bobbins and pins
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑbɪn leɪs / (AmE), / ˈbɒbɪn leɪs / (BrE)
a handmade lace created by twisting and braiding threads wound on bobbins, with the pattern fixed by pins on a pillow

Examples

Making bobbin lace
Creating bobbin lace requires patience and skill, as each thread is carefully manipulated over the pillow.
She learned to make bobbin lace from her grandmother, who had practiced the craft for decades.
The museum displayed intricate bobbin lace pieces dating back to the 17th century.
