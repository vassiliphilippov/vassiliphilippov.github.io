unfaithful

adjective
not loyal or faithful
1.0
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈfeɪθfəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈfeɪθfəl / (BrE)
not remaining loyal to a commitment, promise, or duty; not faithful

Examples

Unfaithful to a promise
Employers expect their workers not to be unfaithful to their duties.
Rumors spread quickly about the celebrity's alleged unfaithful behavior.
She felt betrayed when she discovered her partner had been unfaithful to her.

Related Terms

  • antonym: faithful - remaining loyal and steadfast to a person, cause, or belief
