Animalia

noun
the animal kingdom (taxonomy)
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌænəˈmeɪliə / (AmE), / ˌanɪˈmeɪlɪə / (BrE)
The taxonomic kingdom comprising all living or extinct animals.

Examples

The kingdom Animalia
Biologists classify both insects and mammals under the kingdom Animalia.
Dinosaurs, though extinct, were part of Animalia along with modern birds and reptiles.
Unlike plants, all members of Animalia are multicellular organisms that consume organic material.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Plantae - the taxonomic kingdom including all plants
