organized

adjective
belonging to a labor union
Pronunciation: / ˈɔːrɡəˌnaɪzd /; / ˈɔr.gə.naɪzd / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡənaɪzd /; / ˈɔː.ɡə.naɪzd / (BrE)
(of a worker, group, or workplace) belonging to or formed into a labor union.

Examples

Organized labor group
An organized shop must follow the rules agreed upon by the labor union.
Many organized workers participated in the strike to demand better wages.
The company has both organized and non-organized employees on its staff.
Many organised workers joined the protest for better wages.
Only organised shops receive representation from the union.
The company faced challenges from both organised and non-unionized staff.

Related Terms

  • antonym: non-unionized - not belonging to a labor union
  • synonym: unionized - belonging to or formed into a labor union
