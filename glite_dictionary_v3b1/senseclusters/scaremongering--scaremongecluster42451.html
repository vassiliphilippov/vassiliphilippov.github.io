scaremongering

adjective
causing fear deliberately
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈskɛrˌmʌŋɡərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskeəˌmʌŋɡərɪŋ / (BrE)
intended to cause fear or alarm by spreading exaggerated or misleading information

Examples

Scaremongering headlines
The politician's scaremongering comments made the public more anxious about the situation.
Some media outlets have been criticized for their scaremongering coverage of health risks.
Experts warned that scaremongering reports could lead to unnecessary panic in the community.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alarmist - causing unnecessary fear or worry
  • antonym: reassuring - making someone feel less worried or afraid
