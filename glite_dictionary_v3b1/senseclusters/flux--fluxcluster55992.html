flux

verb
to become liquid when heated
5.0 7.5
Pronunciation: /flʌks/ (AmE), /flʌks/ (BrE)
to become liquid or fluid when heated, especially in a chemical or metallurgical process

Examples

Minerals flux when heated
Certain minerals flux at relatively low temperatures during the refining process.
If the alloy is heated sufficiently, it will flux and flow into the mold.
When exposed to intense heat, the solder began to flux rapidly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: melt - to become liquid by heat
  • antonym: solidify - to become solid; to harden from a liquid state
