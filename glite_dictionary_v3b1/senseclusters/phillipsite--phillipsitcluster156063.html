phillipsite

noun
a mineral of the zeolite family
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪlɪpˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈfɪlɪpˌsaɪt / (BrE)
a white or reddish crystalline mineral of the zeolite family, consisting of a hydrous silicate of calcium, potassium, and aluminum

Examples

White phillipsite mineral
Researchers discovered a deposit of phillipsite near the coastal cliffs.
The volcanic rocks in the region are rich in phillipsite and other zeolites.
Thin sections of the specimen revealed the presence of phillipsite crystals.
