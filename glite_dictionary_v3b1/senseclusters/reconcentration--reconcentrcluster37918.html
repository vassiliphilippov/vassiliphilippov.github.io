reconcentration

noun
forced civilian relocation
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkɑːnsənˈtreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːkɒnsənˈtreɪʃən / (BrE)
the policy or act of forcibly relocating and confining civilian populations into camps or restricted areas, especially during war or colonial rule

Examples

Reconcentration camps
The policy of reconcentration led to the suffering of thousands of displaced families.
International organizations condemned the government's use of reconcentration camps during the conflict.
Historical records detail the effects of reconcentration on rural communities under colonial rule.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deportation - the act of forcing someone to leave a country
  • synonym: internment - the act of confining people, especially during wartime
  • synonym: resettlement - the act of moving people to a new place to live
