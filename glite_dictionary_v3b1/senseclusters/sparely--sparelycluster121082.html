sparely

adverb

Pronunciation: /ˈsper.liː/ /; / /ˈsper.lɪ/ (AmE), /ˈspeə.liː/ /; / /ˈspeə.lɪ/ (BrE)
in a way that uses only a small amount of something; with restraint or not much

Examples

He decorated the cake sparely, using just a few berries on top.
The director used music sparely in the film to create a quiet atmosphere.
The artist decorated the gallery sparely to draw attention to each individual piece.
Meals were served sparely during the expedition to conserve supplies.
Her writing style is sparely elegant, using only the words necessary to convey meaning.
