plug in

verb
connect to power or become involved
1.0
Pronunciation: / plʌɡ ɪn / (AmE), / plʌɡ ɪn / (BrE)
To connect an electrical device to a power source by inserting its plug; also, to become involved with or connected to an activity, organization, or system.

Examples

To plug in to a group
At the conference, she encouraged participants to plug in to the latest developments in the field.
Before you use the blender, make sure to plug in the cord.
If your speakers aren't working, did you remember to plug in both wires?

Related Terms

  • antonym: unplug - to disconnect from a power source, or to remove oneself from involvement
