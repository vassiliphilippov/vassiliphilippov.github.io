shift register

noun
computer/electronics register for shifting bits
Pronunciation: / ʃɪft ˈrɛdʒɪstər / (AmE), / ʃɪft ˈrɛdʒɪstə / (BrE)
A register in a computer or electronic circuit in which all bits can be shifted one or more positions to the left or right.

Examples

A shift register circuit
Designing an efficient shift register is important for optimizing microprocessor performance.
Most digital circuits use a shift register to temporarily store and shift information.
The data was moved through the shift register one bit at a time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: register - a device for storing data temporarily in a computer
