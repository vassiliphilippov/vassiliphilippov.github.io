return

verb
go back to a previous state
Pronunciation: / rɪˈtɜrn /; / rɪˈtɝn /; / ɹəˈtɝn /; / rɪˈtɝːn / (AmE), / rɪˈtɜːn /; / ɹɪˈtɜːn / (BrE)
to go or come back to a place, state, or activity

Examples

To return home
After a brief improvement, the patient's health returned to its previous condition.
Sales figures returned to last year's levels by the end of the quarter.
The city has returned to normal after the storm passed.
After spending several years abroad, she decided to return home.
He promised to return the book as soon as he finished reading it.
Once the rain stopped, the children returned to playing in the park.
After months of illness, her strength finally returned.
Hope returns when you least expect it.
With winter, the bitter cold returned to the city.
