overwork

verb
work too hard; work excessively
4.1 0.97/m
Pronunciation: / Àåo ä.v…öÀàw…ùÀêk /; / Àåo äv…ôrÀàw…úrk / (AmE), / Àå…ô ä.v…ôÀàw…úÀêk /; / Àå…ô äv…ôÀàw…úÀêk / (BrE)
to make someone or yourself work too much or too hard, often causing exhaustion or harm

Examples

To overwork yourself
Employees who overwork are at greater risk of burnout and health problems.
If you overwork the dough, the bread will turn out tough.
She tends to overwork herself before important deadlines.
If you overwork your employees, they may become less productive over time.
The doctor warned him not to overwork after his recent illness.
They overworked the engine until it began to smoke and lose power.
