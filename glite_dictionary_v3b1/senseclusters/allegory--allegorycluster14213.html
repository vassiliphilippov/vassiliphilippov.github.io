allegory

noun
a story with a hidden moral or political meaning
Pronunciation: / ˈæl.əˌɡɔːr.i /; / ˈæləˌɡɔri / (AmE), / ˈæl.ɪɡəri /; / ˈalɪɡəri / (BrE)
a story, poem, or picture in which the characters and events represent deeper meanings, such as moral or political ideas

Examples

Allegory of good and evil
George Orwell's 'Animal Farm' is often read as an allegory for totalitarianism.
Teachers often use fables as an allegory to explain complex moral lessons.
The painting uses visual elements as an allegory for the passage of time.
In English class, we studied an allegory where each animal represented a human virtue or flaw.
Many ancient allegories were designed to teach children important life lessons.
The story of the tortoise and the hare is an allegory about perseverance and overconfidence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fable - a short story, typically with animals as characters, conveying a moral
  • synonym: metaphor - a figure of speech in which a word or phrase is applied to something it does not literally denote
