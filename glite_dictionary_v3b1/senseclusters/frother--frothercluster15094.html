frother

noun
chemical for froth
9.3
Pronunciation: /ˈfrɑː.ðər/ /; / /ˈfrɔː.ðər/ (AmE), /ˈfrɒ.ðə/ (BrE)
a chemical substance added to a liquid to produce or stabilize froth, especially in mineral flotation processes

Examples

Mineral frother
The addition of a frother helped create stable bubbles during the mineral separation process.
Technicians measured the amount of frother needed to optimize the flotation tank's performance.
Without enough frother, the froth collapsed too quickly for effective mineral recovery.
