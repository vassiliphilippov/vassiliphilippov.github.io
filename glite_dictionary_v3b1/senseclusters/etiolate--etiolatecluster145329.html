etiolate

verb
to grow pale and weak from lack of light
7.4 8.7
Pronunciation: /ˈiː.tɪ.ə.leɪt/ (AmE), /ˈiː.tɪ.ə.leɪt/ (BrE)
(of a plant) to make or become pale and weak by growing in insufficient light

Examples

To etiolate a plant
If you etiolate your houseplants by placing them in a closet, they will become weak and yellowish.
The gardener noticed that the seedlings had etiolated due to being kept in a shaded greenhouse.
When celery is grown in the dark, it can quickly become etiolated and lose its green color.
Gardeners sometimes etiolate shoots deliberately to produce tender, pale vegetables.
If you etiolate the seedlings by keeping them in the dark, they will turn yellow and spindly.
Plants etiolated by lack of sunlight exhibit weak stems and pale leaves.
