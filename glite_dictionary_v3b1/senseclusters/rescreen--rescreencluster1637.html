rescreen

verb
to screen again
4.6
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈskriːn/ /; / /riː.ˈskriːn/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈskriːn/ /; / /riː.ˈskriːn/ (BrE)
to test, examine, or check again, especially by repeating a screening process such as a medical or security check

Examples

To rescreen a patient
After the initial test, the doctor decided to rescreen the patient for any hidden infections.
Airport staff may rescreen passengers if the security equipment detects something unusual.
The school will rescreen all students for vision problems at the start of the year.
