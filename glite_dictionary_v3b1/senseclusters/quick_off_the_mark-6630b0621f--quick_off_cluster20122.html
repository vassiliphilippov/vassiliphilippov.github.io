quick off the mark

adjective
prompt or fast to respond or act
Pronunciation: / kwɪk ɑf ðə mɑrk / (AmE), / kwɪk ɒf ðə mɑːk / (BrE)
prompt or fast to respond or take action, especially at the very start of an event or situation

Examples

Quick off the mark in response
Politicians have to be quick off the mark in responding to breaking news.
She was quick off the mark when it came to solving the problem.
You need to be quick off the mark if you want to get the best deals.

Related Terms
