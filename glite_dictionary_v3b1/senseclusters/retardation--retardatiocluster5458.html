retardation

noun
intellectual developmental delay (outdated)
9.6
Pronunciation: /riː.tɑːr.ˈdeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /rɪ.tɑːr.ˈdeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /rɪ.tɑːr.ˈdeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /riː.tɑːr.ˈdeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the lack of normal development of intellectual abilities, especially as a clinical or medical diagnosis (now considered outdated and potentially offensive)

Examples

Intellectual retardation
A team of specialists assessed her for potential retardation due to genetic factors.
In the past, schools used the term retardation, but now they prefer 'intellectual disability'.
The doctor explained that the child was showing signs of retardation compared to developmental milestones.
