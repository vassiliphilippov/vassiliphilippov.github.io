berserk

adjective
wildly frenzied or out of control
7.2 0.06/m
Pronunciation: / b…ôrÀàz…úrk / (AmE), / b…ôÀàz…úÀêk / (BrE)
out of control with anger or excitement; frenzied or wild

Examples

Go berserk with anger
He drove berserk through the streets, ignoring every traffic light.
She went berserk when she found out her phone was missing.
The fans were berserk with excitement after their team won the championship.

Synonyms

  • frenzied
    6.5 2.24/m
    frenzied is wild and driven by strong emotion or excitement, while berserk is more violently out of control and angry.
  • maniacal
    8.6 1.05/m
    maniacal shows an extreme, almost crazed energy or laughter, while berserk describes losing control from rage or excitement.
  • crazed
    5.0 0.10/m
    crazed looks mentally wild and out of touch with reality, while berserk focuses on furious, uncontrolled action or feeling.
  • raving mad
    7.2 0.27/m
    raving mad is loudly insane or speaking wildly, while berserk is violent, out of control anger or frenzied action.
  • mad
    1.3 5.17/m
    mad can mean angry or slightly crazy in everyday speech, while berserk means fully out of control and wildly violent.
  • amok
    7.8 1.48/m
    amok describes running or acting in a violent, uncontrolled way, while berserk stresses furious loss of control from rage or excitement.
  • ballistic
    6.5 1.06/m
    ballistic is informal for becoming extremely angry very quickly, while berserk is more about wild, often violent, loss of control.
