impassable

adjective
impossible to cross or travel through

(of a road, path, or area) impossible to travel across or through.

Examples

An impassable road
Because of flooding, the trail was completely impassable for hikers.
The mountain road became impassable after the heavy snowfall.
They had to turn back when they reached an impassable barrier of fallen trees.

Synonyms

  • untraversable
    9.1 0.01/m
    untraversable means impossible to travel across or through, while impassable describes a road you cannot go along or through.
  • unpassable
    6.9 0.09/m
    unpassable describes a road or path not able to be crossed or traveled through, while impassable is the standard term.
  • uncrossable
    5.6 0.18/m
    uncrossable means you cannot cross or pass over something, while impassable is used for roads or areas you cannot travel through.
  • impenetrable
    7.3 0.91/m
    impenetrable describes something thick or dense that blocks entry, while impassable refers to roads you cannot travel across.
  • inaccessible
    7.9 3.38/m
    inaccessible means hard or impossible to reach or use for people, while impassable specifically means you cannot travel through.

Related Terms

  • synonym: impassible - incapable of being passed through (occasionally confused with 'impassable')
  • antonym: passable - able to be passed, crossed, or traveled through
