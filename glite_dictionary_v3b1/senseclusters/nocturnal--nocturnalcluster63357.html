nocturnal

adjective
active or happening at night
4.0
Pronunciation: / nɑkˈtɝnəl /; / nɑkˈtɜrnəl / (AmE), / nɒkˈtɜːnəl / (BrE)
active, happening, or existing at night rather than during the day

Examples

Nocturnal animals
Owls are nocturnal creatures that hunt when it is dark.
Some flowers only bloom during their nocturnal cycle.
The museum organized a nocturnal tour to showcase its exhibits at night.
Because bats are nocturnal, they rest in caves during the day and emerge after dusk.
Many flowers that bloom at night are adapted to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
Owls are nocturnal birds that hunt for food after sunset.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diurnal - active or occurring during the daytime
