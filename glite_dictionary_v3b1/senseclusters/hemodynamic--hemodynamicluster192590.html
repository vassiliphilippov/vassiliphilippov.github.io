hemodynamic

adjective
relating to blood flow or circulation
Pronunciation: / ˌhiːmoʊdaɪˈnæmɪk / (AmE), / ˌhiːməʊdaɪˈnæmɪk / (BrE)
relating to the flow of blood or the forces involved in blood circulation within the body

Examples

Hemodynamic response
Certain drugs are used to manage hemodynamic responses during anesthesia.
Improving hemodynamic function is essential in treating patients with heart failure.
The patient required careful monitoring of hemodynamic stability during the surgery.
The surgeon carefully monitored the patient's haemodynamic status throughout the operation.
Certain medications can affect haemodynamic stability in critically ill patients.
Researchers studied the haemodynamic responses to exercise in older adults.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cardiovascular - relating to the heart and blood vessels
  • synonym: circulatory - relating to the movement of blood through the body
  • synonym: hemodynamics - the study of blood flow and forces in the circulatory system
