autosuggestion

noun
self-improvement through self-suggestion
5.2 7.3
Pronunciation: /ɑː.toʊ.sə.ˈdʒes.tʃən/ /; / /ɔː.toʊ.sə.ˈdʒes.tʃən/ (AmE), /ɔː.təʊ.sə.ˈdʒes.tʃən/ (BrE)
a technique in which a person uses self-induced suggestions or affirmations to influence their own thoughts, feelings, or behavior, as popularized by Emile Coué in the early 20th century

Examples

Positive autosuggestion practice
Emile Coué's theory of autosuggestion encouraged people to repeat positive phrases to themselves.
Many self-help books draw on principles of autosuggestion to promote personal growth.
She practiced autosuggestion every morning to boost her confidence before work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: affirmation - a positive statement used to encourage oneself
