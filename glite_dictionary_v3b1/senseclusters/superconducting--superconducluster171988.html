superconducting

adjective
zero-resistance electrical
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərkənˈdʌktɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəkənˈdʌktɪŋ / (BrE)
able to conduct electricity with zero resistance, typically at very low temperatures

Examples

Superconducting materials
Scientists are developing superconducting materials to improve the efficiency of power grids.
A superconducting magnet is used in MRI machines for medical imaging.
The laboratory cooled the metal to a superconducting state to study its properties.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: conductive - able to conduct electricity
  • antonym: insulating - not allowing electricity to pass through
