rye grass

noun
species of Eurasian grass used for pasture or lawns
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈraɪ ˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈraɪ ˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
any of several species of annual or perennial Eurasian grasses of the genus Lolium, cultivated for pasture, hay, or lawns.

Examples

Rye grass lawn
Many lawns use rye grass because it grows quickly and stays green in cooler seasons.
The field was covered with lush rye grass that provided excellent feed for the cattle.
We planted rye grass last fall to prevent soil erosion during the winter.
