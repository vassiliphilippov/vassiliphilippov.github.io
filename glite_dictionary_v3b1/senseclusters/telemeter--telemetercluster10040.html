telemeter

noun
instrument for remote measurement and data transmission
5.9 6.8
Pronunciation: /ˈtel.ə.miː.tər/ (AmE), /ˈtel.ə.miː.tə/ (BrE)
a device that measures something from a distance and sends the data to another place

Examples

A telemeter device
A telemeter allowed scientists to study volcanic eruptions without being near the danger zone.
Each rocket is equipped with a telemeter to monitor its performance during flight.
The weather balloons use a telemeter to send atmospheric data back to researchers on the ground.
The engineer installed a telemeter to monitor the bridge's vibrations from a control room.
Wildlife researchers use a telemeter to track animal movements over large distances.
The spacecraft sent data back to Earth using an onboard telemeter.
