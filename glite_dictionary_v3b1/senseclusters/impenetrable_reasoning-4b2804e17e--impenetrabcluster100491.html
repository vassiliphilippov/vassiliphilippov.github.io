impenetrable reasoning

noun
reasoning that is hard to understand
4.1
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɛnɪtrəbəl ˈriːzənɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛnɪtrəbl ˈriːzənɪŋ / (BrE)
reasoning or argument that is extremely difficult or impossible to understand

Examples

Impenetrable reasoning style
After reading the dense manuscript, she criticized its impenetrable reasoning in her review.
He tends to use impenetrable reasoning when arguing, making it hard for others to follow his point.
The professor's impenetrable reasoning left many students confused during the lecture.
