Hamilton

proper noun
U.S. statesman; first Secretary of the Treasury
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈhæməltən / (AmE), / ˈhæməltən / (BrE)
(Alexander Hamilton) (1755–1804), United States statesman, one of the Founding Fathers, the first Secretary of the Treasury, who established a federal bank and was killed in a duel with Aaron Burr.

Examples

Alexander Hamilton duel
Hamilton was instrumental in shaping the financial system of the early United States.
Many policies created by Hamilton continue to influence the U.S. government today.
The famous duel between Hamilton and Aaron Burr resulted in Hamilton's death in 1804.
