carryover

noun
residual contamination
Pronunciation: / ˈkæriˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈkæriˌəʊvə / (BrE)
residual material or contamination from one test or sample that affects the results of the next measurement or analysis

Examples

Sample carryover
The laboratory implemented new procedures to reduce carryover between patient samples.
High levels of carryover can compromise the accuracy of test results in analytical chemistry.
Technicians must thoroughly clean equipment to prevent carryover from previous experiments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contamination - the presence of unwanted substances that make something impure
  • antonym: purity - the state of being free from contamination
