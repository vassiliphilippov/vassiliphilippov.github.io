make head against

verb
progress despite difficulties
Pronunciation: / meɪk hɛd əˈɡɛnst / (AmE), / meɪk hɛd əˈɡɛnst / (BrE)
to succeed in making progress or resisting difficulties or opposition

Examples

To make head against problems
Despite numerous setbacks, the company managed to make head against fierce competition.
It's difficult to make head against the waves in such stormy weather.
She was determined to make head against all the challenges she faced at work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overcome - to succeed in dealing with a problem or difficulty
  • antonym: succumb - to fail to resist pressure, temptation, or some negative force
