vapor lock

noun
engine fuel blockage caused by gas bubbles
9.9 5.9
Pronunciation: /ˈveɪ.pər lɑːk/ (AmE), /ˈveɪ.pə lɒk/ (BrE)
A stoppage in the flow of fuel in an internal combustion engine, caused by gas bubbles forming in the fuel line, often due to excessive heat.

Examples

Engine vapor lock
After the engine cooled, the vapor lock cleared, and the vehicle started again.
Mechanics often encounter vapor lock during hot summers, especially in older vehicles.
The car stalled on the highway because of vapor lock in the fuel line.
Drivers often experience vapour lock when operating vehicles in high temperatures.
Mechanics recommend insulating fuel lines to prevent vapour lock during hot weather.
The car engine stalled on the highway because of vapour lock in the fuel line.

Related Terms

  • synonym: airlock - a stoppage of liquid flow caused by trapped air in a pipe or tube
