muck out

verb
clean waste from a place, esp. a stable
9.9
Pronunciation: / mʌk aʊt / (AmE), / mʌk aʊt / (BrE)
to clean waste, especially manure, from a place such as a stable or animal pen

Examples

To muck out stalls
She spent the afternoon mucking out the horse stalls at the riding school.
The farmer wakes up early each day to muck out the cowshed.
They had to muck out the barn after the flood left it full of debris.
