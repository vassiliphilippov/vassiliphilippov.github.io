immanentist

adjective
belief in divine presence
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɪmənəntɪst / (AmE), / ˈɪmənəntɪst / (BrE)
relating to the belief that the divine or ultimate reality exists within the world rather than outside it.

Examples

Immanentist philosophy
The philosopher's immanentist views challenged traditional ideas about the separation of the divine and the world.
Her immanentist approach to theology emphasizes the sacredness found in everyday life.
Many modern thinkers adopt an immanentist perspective, seeing ultimate reality as present within nature.

Related Terms

  • antonym: transcendent - existing beyond or independent from the material world
