interposition

noun
the act of placing between
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.tɚ.pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.tə.pəˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
the act of placing something or someone between others, or the state of being so placed

Examples

Interposition of a barrier
He resented the constant interposition of outside parties in their private affairs.
She objected to the interposition of unnecessary steps between the proposal and its approval.
The interposition of a translator helped them communicate more effectively.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interpose - to place or insert between one thing and another
