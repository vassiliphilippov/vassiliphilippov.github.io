Eumycota

noun
a group of true fungi
Pronunciation: / juːmaɪˈkoʊtə / (AmE), / juːmaɪˈkəʊtə / (BrE)
A phylum or division comprising the true fungi, which are eukaryotic, heterotrophic organisms with cell walls, distinguished from slime molds.

Examples

The Eumycota group
Many of the organisms studied in mycology belong to the Eumycota.
The classification of Eumycota includes several major subdivisions of fungi.
Unlike slime molds, the Eumycota have well-defined cell walls.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Myxomycota - Fungus-like slime molds, a group distinct from true fungi.
  • synonym: fungus - A member of the kingdom Fungi; a spore-producing organism feeding on organic matter.
