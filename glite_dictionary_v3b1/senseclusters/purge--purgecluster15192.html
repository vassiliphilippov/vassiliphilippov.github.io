purge

verb
clear of a charge or accusation
1.9 5.6
Pronunciation: /pɜːrdʒ/ (AmE), /pɜːdʒ/ (BrE)
to clear someone or oneself of blame, guilt, accusation, or sin, especially in a legal or moral context

Examples

To purge guilt
After the review, the board decided to purge her of all allegations of misconduct.
He must purge the fine by fulfilling the community service order.
The defendant was able to purge himself of the contempt charge by apologizing to the court.
Many religions offer ways to purge believers of their sins.
She hoped that confession would purge her soul of wrongdoing.
The ritual was meant to purge the community of its collective guilt.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absolve - declare someone free of blame, guilt, or responsibility
  • synonym: cleanse - to make thoroughly clean or morally pure
  • antonym: contaminate - to make impure by exposure to or addition of something harmful
  • antonym: convict - find or declare someone guilty of a crime or offense
