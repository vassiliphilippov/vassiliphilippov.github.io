nonobjectivity

noun
lack of objectivity
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˌɑbˌdʒɛkˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌɒbˌdʒɛkˈtɪvəti / (BrE)
the quality or state of not being objective or impartial

Examples

Judicial nonobjectivity
The judge was criticized for his nonobjectivity during the trial.
Some readers noticed a clear nonobjectivity in the report's conclusions.
Academic research should avoid nonobjectivity to maintain credibility.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bias - a tendency to favor one side or perspective
  • antonym: objectivity - the quality of being fair and impartial
