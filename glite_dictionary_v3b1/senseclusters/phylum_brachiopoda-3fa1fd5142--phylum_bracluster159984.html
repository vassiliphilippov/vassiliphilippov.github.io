phylum Brachiopoda

noun
group of shelled marine invertebrates
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪləm ˌbrækiˈoʊpədə / (AmE), / ˈfʌɪləm ˌbrækɪˈəʊpədə / (BrE)
A major group of marine invertebrates that have hard shells on the upper and lower surfaces and superficially resemble mollusks.

Examples

The phylum Brachiopoda fossils
Fossils of the phylum Brachiopoda are abundant in Paleozoic rocks.
Researchers studied the shell structure of several species in the phylum Brachiopoda.
Unlike true mollusks, members of the phylum Brachiopoda have a distinct type of shell symmetry.
