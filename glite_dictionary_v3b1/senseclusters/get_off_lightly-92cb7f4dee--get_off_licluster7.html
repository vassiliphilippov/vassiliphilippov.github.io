get off lightly

verb
escape with little punishment or consequence
3.2
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt ɔf ˈlaɪtli / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɒf ˈlaɪtli / (BrE)
to receive little or no punishment or have minimal consequences for wrongdoing or a mistake

Examples

To get off lightly with a warning
Despite being late three times, he got off lightly with only a warning from his boss.
If you forget your homework again, don't expect to get off lightly next time.
Some thought the company would be fined, but it actually got off lightly after the investigation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: get off easy - to receive less punishment than expected
