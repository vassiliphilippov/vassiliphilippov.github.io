pizzicato

noun
plucking of stringed instruments
5.3 7.1
Pronunciation: /pɪt.sɪ.ˈkɑː.toʊ/ /; / /pɪt.sə.ˈkɑː.toʊ/ (AmE), /pɪt.sɪ.ˈkɑː.təʊ/ /; / /pɪt.sə.ˈkɑː.təʊ/ (BrE)
A note or passage played by plucking the strings of a violin or other stringed instrument with the fingers, rather than using the bow.

Examples

A pizzicato note
In the orchestra, the cellos performed a rapid pizzicato passage during the second movement.
The composer indicated that the entire line should be played pizzicato for a unique effect.
The violinist played the delicate section pizzicato, creating a light and playful sound.
