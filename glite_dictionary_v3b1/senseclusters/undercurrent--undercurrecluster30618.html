undercurrent

noun
current below the surface of water or air
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndɚˌkɝrənt / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌkʌrənt / (BrE)
a current of water or air that flows beneath the surface, often differing in direction from the surface current

Examples

Strong undercurrent
Divers must always be aware of hidden undercurrents when exploring coastal caves.
Powerful undercurrents made swimming in the river especially dangerous after heavy rain.
The lake appeared calm, but a strong undercurrent flowed beneath the surface.
