crystallin

adjective
crystal-like appearance
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪstəlɪn / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstəlɪn / (BrE)
having the appearance or qualities of crystal; clear, glassy, or resembling a crystalline structure, especially in an archaic or literary context

Examples

Crystallin water
The poet described the lake as having a crystallin surface that sparkled in the morning sun.
Through the crystallin air, the distant mountains appeared almost within reach.
The old manuscript used the word crystallin to evoke a sense of purity and clarity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crystalline - having the structure or appearance of crystal
  • antonym: opaque - not able to be seen through; not transparent
