esthesia

noun
mental responsiveness and awareness
7.6
Pronunciation: / ɛsˈθiːʒə / (AmE), / ɛsˈθiːzɪə / (BrE)
the capacity for mental responsiveness and awareness to sensory stimuli

Examples

Heightened esthesia response
Heightened esthesia can occur with some neurological conditions.
Reduced esthesia may suggest nerve damage in certain areas of the body.
The patient’s esthesia was tested to assess his reaction to light touch and pain.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anesthesia - loss of sensation
  • synonym: sensation - a physical feeling or perception
