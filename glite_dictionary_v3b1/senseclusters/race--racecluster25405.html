race

noun
canal to direct water
Pronunciation: / reɪs / (AmE), / reɪs / (BrE)
A channel or canal constructed to direct the flow of water, especially to or from a waterwheel or mill.

Examples

A water race
The engineer redirected the race to improve the efficiency of the mill's machinery.
The water rushed swiftly through the race to power the old mill.
Workers cleared debris from the race to ensure a steady flow to the waterwheel.

Related Terms

  • synonym: millrace - the current of water that turns a mill wheel
