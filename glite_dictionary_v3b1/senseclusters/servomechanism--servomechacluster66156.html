servomechanism

noun
automatic control system with feedback
9.9 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / Àås…úr.vo äÀàm…õk.…ôÀån…™z.…ôm / (AmE), / Àås…úÀê.v…ô äÀàm…õk.…ô.n…™z.…ôm / (BrE)
A mechanical or electronic control system that uses a small input signal or motion to control a much larger mechanical movement, often employing negative feedback to achieve precise operation.

Examples

A servomechanism control system
A faulty servomechanism caused the telescope to lose its alignment.
Aircraft often rely on a servomechanism to control the movement of their flaps.
The robotic arm used a servomechanism to ensure precise positioning.

Synonyms

  • servosystem
    8.8 0.00/m
    a servosystem is the whole control setup used to steer larger motion with feedback, while a servomechanism is the formal technical name.
  • servo
    8.5 1.86/m
    a servo is the single motor or device that moves and controls power, while a servomechanism refers to the full control system using feedback.

Related Terms

  • synonym: servo - short form for servomechanism, especially in electronics
