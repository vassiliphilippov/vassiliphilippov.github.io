easy

adjective
not strict or severe
1.0 7.94/m
Pronunciation: / Ààizi / (AmE), / ÀàiÀêzi / (BrE)
not strict or severe; allowing freedom and not enforcing rigid rules or standards

Examples

Easy rules
He thought the team's coach was too easy on the players during practice.
Her professor is known for being an easy grader who rarely marks students down.
The school adopted more easy rules to reduce student stress.

Synonyms

  • permissive
    4.9 1.36/m
    permissive allows a lot of freedom and relaxed rules, while easy is a more general, everyday word for not strict.
  • lenient
    4.4 1.58/m
    lenient shows mercy or mild punishment toward people, while easy describes rules or standards that are not strict.
  • lax
    4.6 2.00/m
    lax is not careful and often too weak in enforcing rules, while easy simply means not strict.

Related Terms

  • antonym: strict - enforcing rules and standards rigidly; not easy or lenient
