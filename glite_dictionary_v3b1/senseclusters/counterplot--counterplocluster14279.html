counterplot

verb
devise a plot in response to another
8.6 7.6
Pronunciation: /ˈkaʊn.tər.plɑːt/ (AmE), /ˈkaʊn.tə.plɒt/ (BrE)
to devise or enact a plot in response to another plot, especially as a form of opposition or retaliation

Examples

To counterplot against enemies
In response to the scheme, she quickly counterplotted to outwit her rivals.
The villain counterplotted against the hero’s plan to save the city.
They spent the night counterplotting after discovering their enemy’s intentions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plot - to make a secret plan, typically to achieve something illicit
