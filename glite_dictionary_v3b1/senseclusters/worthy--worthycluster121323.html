Synonyms

  • deserving
    3.5 1.77/m
    deserving often says someone should get help or reward, while worthy stresses value, honor, or being chosen.
  • honorable
    3.7 11/m
    honorable shows high moral standards and public respect, while worthy names someone fit to be chosen for value or merit.
  • admirable
    3.3 5.32/m
    admirable inspires approval or praise for actions or character, while worthy highlights general merit or being worth attention.
  • meritorious
    7.5 0.71/m
    meritorious is a formal word for deeds that earn reward or praise, while worthy applies more broadly in daily use.
  • praiseworthy
    4.5 0.75/m
    praiseworthy means clearly deserving praise for a specific act, while worthy can mean general merit or reason to be respected.
  • commendable
    4.6 1.52/m
    commendable deserves approval or praise for good qualities or actions, while worthy covers wider ideas of value and honor.
  • laudable
    7.2 1.18/m
    laudable is a formal word praising an action or goal, while worthy is more general and used for people or things.
