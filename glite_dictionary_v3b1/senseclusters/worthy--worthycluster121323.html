worthy

adjective
deserving of recognition or attention
3.3
Pronunciation: / ˈwɝːði / (AmE), / ˈwɜːði / (BrE)
deserving respect, attention, or to be chosen because of having value, merit, or honorable qualities

Examples

A worthy cause
She received an award for her worthy contributions to the community.
That was a worthy effort, even if it did not succeed.
We only support worthy causes that truly help those in need.
He searched for years to meet someone truly worthy of sharing his life.
She was considered a worthy match for the prince by both families.
The tradition is to find a worthy partner who will uphold family values.
He proved himself to be a worthy opponent in the championship match.
Only worthy candidates will be considered for the award.
She dedicated her time to a worthy cause, helping people in need.

  • synonym: suitable - appropriate; right for a particular person or purpose
  • antonym: unworthy - not deserving; lacking merit or value
