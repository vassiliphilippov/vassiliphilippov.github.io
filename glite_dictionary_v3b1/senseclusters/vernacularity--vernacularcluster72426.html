vernacularity

noun
use of everyday language
Pronunciation: / vərˌnækjəˈlærəti / (AmE), / vəˌnækjʊˈlærɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being expressed in the everyday language spoken by ordinary people

Examples

Vernacularity of language
The vernacularity of the novel helped readers connect with the characters on a personal level.
Teachers praised the vernacularity in his speech for making complex ideas easy to understand.
Some critics argue that too much vernacularity can make literature seem less serious.

Related Terms

  • synonym: colloquialism - a word or phrase used in informal, everyday speech
  • antonym: formality - the quality of being formal or official in language
