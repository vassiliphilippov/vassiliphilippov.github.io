rule

verb
decide with authority
3.7 26/m
Pronunciation: / ruÀêl / (AmE), / ruÀêl / (BrE)
to make an official or authoritative decision, especially in a legal or formal context

Examples

To rule on a case
After much debate, the committee ruled in favor of the new policy.
The Supreme Court is expected to rule that the law is unconstitutional.
The judge will rule on the case tomorrow afternoon.
After hearing both sides, the referee ruled in favor of the home team.
The committee ruled that the evidence was inadmissible in court.
The judge will rule on the objection before the end of the day.

Synonyms

  • adjudicate
    7.8 1.67/m

  • adjudge
    8.1 0.25/m

  • find
    6.5 72/m
    find means a court decides facts or gives a verdict after hearing evidence, while rule covers formal legal decisions.
  • pronounce
    3.5 9.20/m
    pronounce is to state an official judgment aloud or formally, while rule means to make an official legal decision.
  • judge
    2.5 48/m
    judge means to form an opinion or decision after considering facts, while rule refers to making an official, authoritative decision.
  • decree
    5.3 3.14/m

Related Terms

  • antonym: overrule - to reject or set aside a previous decision or ruling
