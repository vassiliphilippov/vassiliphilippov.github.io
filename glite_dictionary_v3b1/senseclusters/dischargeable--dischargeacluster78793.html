dischargeable

adjective
able to be released
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈtʃɑrdʒəbl̩ / (AmE), / dɪsˈtʃɑːdʒəbl̩ / (BrE)
able to be released, dismissed, or removed from a duty, obligation, or liability

Examples

Dischargeable debt
Certain debts are dischargeable in bankruptcy court, meaning they can be eliminated.
The contract included several dischargeable obligations that could be removed under specific conditions.
Not all offenses are dischargeable from a person's criminal record.

Related Terms

  • antonym: irremovable - not able to be removed or dismissed
  • synonym: removable - able to be taken away or eliminated
