bed

verb
provide with a bed or sleeping place
9.9 1.0
Pronunciation: /bɛd/ (AmE), /bɛd/ (BrE)
to provide with a bed or sleeping accommodation, or to go to bed or put someone to bed

Examples

To bed a guest
The hotel staff will bed the guests in a nearby annex if rooms are full.
The shelter can bed up to fifty people during cold nights.
They quickly bed all new arrivals to make them comfortable after their journey.
During the storm, the caretaker bedded all the animals securely inside.
Every night at nine, she beds the youngest child herself.
The children were always bedded by their nanny before the parents returned.
After a long day, she was ready to bed early.
He likes to bed right after reading a book.
The children always bed at nine o'clock during the school year.

Related Terms

  • antonym: arise - to get out of bed; to get up
  • synonym: put to bed - to help someone get into bed in order to sleep
  • synonym: retire - to go to bed; to withdraw for sleep
  • synonym: turn in - (informal) to go to bed
