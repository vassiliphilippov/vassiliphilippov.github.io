greenwash

verb
to mislead about eco-friendliness
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈɡriːnwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈɡriːnwɒʃ / (BrE)
to make misleading claims that something is more environmentally friendly than it really is, especially for marketing purposes

Examples

To greenwash a company
The company tried to greenwash its image by advertising eco-friendly packaging that was not actually recyclable.
Critics accused the brand of greenwashing when it launched a campaign about sustainability without changing its practices.
Some businesses greenwash their products to attract environmentally conscious customers, even if their claims are exaggerated.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deceive - to make someone believe something that is not true
  • antonym: expose - to reveal the truth about something hidden or dishonest
  • synonym: mislead - to give a false impression or lead someone in the wrong direction
