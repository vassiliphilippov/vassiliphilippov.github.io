capsulotomy

noun
brain surgery for mental illness
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkæpsjəˈlɑtəmi / (AmE), / ˌkæpsjʊˈlɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which part of the brain's internal capsule is cut, often to treat severe psychiatric disorders

Examples

Brain capsulotomy procedure
A capsulotomy may be considered for patients with severe, treatment-resistant obsessive-compulsive disorder.
The neurosurgeon explained the risks and benefits of capsulotomy to the patient's family.
After undergoing capsulotomy, some patients report a significant reduction in their symptoms.
