hypermotility

noun
excessive movement, especially of the gut
9.7
Pronunciation: / haɪpɚmoʊˈtɪlɪti / (AmE), / haɪpəməʊˈtɪlɪti / (BrE)
excessive movement, especially abnormal or increased movement of the gastrointestinal tract

Examples

Chronic hypermotility symptoms
Chronic hypermotility can lead to nutrient malabsorption in some cases.
Symptoms like diarrhea may be caused by hypermotility of the intestines.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with hypermotility after observing rapid bowel movements.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hypomotility - abnormally decreased movement, especially of the gastrointestinal tract
