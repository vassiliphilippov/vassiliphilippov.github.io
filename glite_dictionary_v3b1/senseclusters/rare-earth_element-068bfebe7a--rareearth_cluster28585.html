rare-earth element

noun
metallic element of the lanthanide series
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛr ˈɜrθ ˈɛləmənt / (AmE), / ˌreə ˈɜːθ ˈelɪmənt / (BrE)
any of a series of metallic elements with atomic numbers 57 through 71, known as the lanthanides, used in various industrial and technological applications.

Examples

Rare-earth element neodymium
Neodymium is a rare-earth element widely used in high-strength magnets.
Scientists are searching for new ways to recycle rare-earth elements from electronic waste.
The demand for rare-earth elements has grown due to their use in electronic devices.
