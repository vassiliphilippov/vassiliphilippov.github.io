pollard

noun
tree with upper branches cut back
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːlərd / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɑːd / (BrE)
a tree with its upper branches cut back regularly to promote a denser, bushy growth of foliage

Examples

A pollard tree
After years of regular cutting, the oak became a dense pollard.
Gardeners decided to make the willow a pollard to encourage more foliage.
The ancient pollard by the river provides a home for many birds.
