Hamiltonian

noun
supporter of Hamilton
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhæməlˈtoʊniən / (AmE), / ˌhæmɪlˈtəʊniən / (BrE)
a person who supports or follows the political ideas or policies of Alexander Hamilton, especially favoring a strong central government

Examples

Hamiltonian supporter
As a proud Hamiltonian, he advocated for a strong national bank and federal authority.
The debate between the Hamiltonians and Jeffersonians shaped early American politics.
Her Hamiltonian views influenced her support for centralized economic policies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Federalist - a supporter of a strong federal government in early U.S. history
  • antonym: Jeffersonian - a supporter of Thomas Jefferson's ideas, favoring states' rights and limited central government
