teeter

verb
move unsteadily, rock or sway
5.7 2.96/m
Pronunciation: / Ààtit…ôr / (AmE), / ÀàtiÀêt…ô / (BrE)
to move or stand unsteadily, often rocking or swaying back and forth

Examples

To teeter on the edge
She watched the small boat teeter in the rough waves.
The old ladder began to teeter as the man climbed higher.
The toddler would often teeter when taking his first steps.

Synonyms

  • totter
    4.8 1.35/m
    totter moves or walks in a very unsteady way, while teeter often describes standing or rocking and nearly losing balance.
  • waver
    4.8 1.84/m
    waver shakes or trembles in a weak, unsteady way, while teeter means to stand or move as if about to fall.
  • sway
    2.4 8.40/m
    sway moves slowly and smoothly from side to side, while teeter shows an unsteady balance and risk of falling.
  • seesaw
    3.6 0.13/m
    seesaw moves up and down or back and forth like a playground board, while teeter means unsteady standing or rocking.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stagger - to walk or move unsteadily, as if about to fall
  • antonym: steady - firm, secure, not likely to move or change
  • synonym: totter - to move in an unsteady way, as if about to fall
