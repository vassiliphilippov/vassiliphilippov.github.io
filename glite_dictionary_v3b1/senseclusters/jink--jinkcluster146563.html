jink

verb
to dodge suddenly
8.1
Pronunciation: / dʒɪŋk / (AmE), / dʒɪŋk / (BrE)
to move quickly and suddenly to avoid something, often by changing direction unexpectedly

Examples

To jink past defenders
The football player jinked past two defenders to score the winning goal.
She jinked to the side to avoid bumping into the cyclist on the path.
The bird jinks sharply in flight to escape predators.
The football player managed to jink past two defenders before scoring a goal.
Birds often jink in flight to escape predators.
She tried to jink out of the way as the ball came flying toward her.
