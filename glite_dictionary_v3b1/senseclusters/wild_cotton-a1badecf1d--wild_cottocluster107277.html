wild cotton

noun
shrub producing wild cotton
Pronunciation: / waɪld ˈkɑtn̩ / (AmE), / waɪld ˈkɒtn̩ / (BrE)
a shrub native to southern Arizona and Mexico, known for producing a type of cotton; often refers to plants in the genus Gossypium that grow wild

Examples

Wild cotton plant
Wild cotton can be found growing along riverbanks in southern Arizona.
Botanists studied the fibers produced by wild cotton to compare them with cultivated varieties.
The seeds of wild cotton are an important food source for some desert animals.
