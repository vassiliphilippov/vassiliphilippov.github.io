duty

noun
tax on imports or exports
3.0 7.14/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàduÀêti / (AmE), / ÀàdjuÀêti / (BrE)
a tax paid to the government, especially on goods imported into or exported from a country

Examples

Pay duty on goods
Importers must pay a duty on certain electronics brought into the country.
The government lowered the duty on automobiles to encourage trade.
When the duty was increased, the price of luxury goods rose significantly.

Synonyms

  • customs duty
    3.5 0.16/m
    customs duty is the charge collected at the border on imported or exported goods, while duty is the general tax term.
  • tariff
    5.6 14/m
    tariff is the official rate or schedule a government sets on imported goods, while duty is the actual tax paid on each shipment.
  • impost
    9.6 0.04/m
    impost is a formal or old-fashioned word for a tax on imports, while duty is the common term used today.

Related Terms

  • synonym: excise - a tax levied on certain goods produced or sold within a country
  • synonym: tariff - a tax imposed on imported or exported goods
