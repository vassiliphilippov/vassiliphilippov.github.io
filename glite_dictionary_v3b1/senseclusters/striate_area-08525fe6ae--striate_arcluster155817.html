striate area

noun
region of the brain for visual reception
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈstraɪ.eɪt ˈɛəri.ə / (AmE), / ˈstraɪ.eɪt ˈeəri.ə / (BrE)
the part of the occipital cortex in the brain that receives visual information from the optic radiation, serving as the primary receptive area for vision

Examples

The striate area cortex
Damage to the striate area can result in loss of visual perception.
Research on the striate area helps scientists understand how the brain processes images.
The striate area is also known as the primary visual cortex.
