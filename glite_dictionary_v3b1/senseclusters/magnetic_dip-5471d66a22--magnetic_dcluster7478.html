magnetic dip

noun
angle between magnetic field and horizon
8.7 6.3
Pronunciation: /mæɡ.ˈnet.ɪk dɪp/ (AmE), /mæɡ.ˈnet.ɪk dɪp/ (BrE)
(physics) The angle between the direction of the Earth's magnetic field and the horizontal plane at a specific location, as indicated by a magnetic needle.

Examples

The magnetic dip angle
A special instrument called a dip circle can be used to measure the magnetic dip precisely.
At higher latitudes, the magnetic dip increases significantly, affecting compass accuracy.
Geologists record the magnetic dip at various field sites to study Earth's magnetic field.

Related Terms
