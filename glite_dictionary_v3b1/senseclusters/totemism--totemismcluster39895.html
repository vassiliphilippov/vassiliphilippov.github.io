totemism

noun
belief system involving spiritual kinship with a totem
Pronunciation: / ˈtoʊtəˌmɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈtəʊtəmɪzəm / (BrE)
a system of belief in which a group of people has a spiritual connection or kinship with a particular animal, plant, or object, known as a totem

Examples

Totemism belief system
Totemism often involves rituals and stories connected with the group's chosen animal or plant.
Many indigenous cultures practice totemism as a central part of their spiritual beliefs.
Modern anthropologists have studied totemism to understand the social structures of various societies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: animism - belief that natural objects and phenomena possess a spiritual essence
