dobson fly

noun
large aquatic insect; larva used as bait
8.8 9.4
Pronunciation: /ˈdɑːb.sən flaɪ/ (AmE), /ˈdɒb.sən flaɪ/ (BrE)
A large soft-bodied insect of the family Corydalidae, with long slender jaws in the male; its aquatic larvae are often used as fishing bait.

Examples

A dobson fly insect
A dobson fly is usually active at night and is attracted to porch lights in summer.
Male dobson flies are known for their intimidating long, curved mandibles.
The fisherman used a dobson fly larva as bait to catch trout in the river.
