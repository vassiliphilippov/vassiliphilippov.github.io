strike down

verb
declare a law or rule invalid
Pronunciation: / straɪk daʊn / (AmE), / straɪk daʊn / (BrE)
(of a court or authority) to declare that a law, rule, or order is invalid and no longer in effect.

Examples

Court strikes down a law
Activists cheered when the court moved to strike down the discriminatory law.
Several provisions of the new policy were struck down after a lengthy legal battle.
The Supreme Court decided to strike down the controversial legislation as unconstitutional.

Related Terms

  • synonym: invalidate - to make or declare something no longer legally acceptable or correct
  • antonym: uphold - to support or confirm a law, decision, or principle, often by a court
