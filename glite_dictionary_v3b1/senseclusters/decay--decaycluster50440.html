decay

noun
gradual decline in quality or condition
4.4
Pronunciation: / dɪˈkeɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈkeɪ / (BrE)
the gradual process of becoming worse, weaker, or ruined, especially over time

Examples

Urban decay
The abandoned building fell into decay after years of neglect.
The report warns about the decay of social values in modern society.
Tooth decay can be prevented by regular brushing and dental check-ups.
The abandoned building showed clear signs of decay after years of neglect.
Tooth decay can occur if you don't brush regularly.
Urban decay has left entire neighborhoods empty and crumbling.
