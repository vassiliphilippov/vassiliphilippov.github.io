bacteremia

noun
presence of bacteria in the blood
4.8 5.4
Pronunciation: / ˌbæk.təˈriː.mi.ə /; / ˌbæktəˈriːmiə / (AmE), / ˌbæk.təˈriː.mi.ə /; / ˌbæktɪəˈriːmɪə / (BrE)
the presence of bacteria in the bloodstream, usually for a short time and often without symptoms

Examples

Bacteremia in blood
Early detection of bacteremia is crucial to prevent more severe infections like sepsis.
Routine dental procedures can sometimes cause transient bacteremia in healthy individuals.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with bacteremia after finding bacteria in her blood sample.
Doctors routinely check blood cultures when they suspect bacteremia in critically ill patients.
Early detection of bacteremia is crucial in preventing the spread of infection throughout the body.
The patient developed bacteremia after his surgical procedure, requiring immediate antibiotic treatment.
