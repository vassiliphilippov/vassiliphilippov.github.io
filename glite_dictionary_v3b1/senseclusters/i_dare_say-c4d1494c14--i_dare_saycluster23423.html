I dare say

interjection
expressing doubt or irony
7.7
Pronunciation: /aɪ der seɪ/ (AmE), /aɪ deə seɪ/ (BrE)
used, often ironically, to express doubt, skepticism, or mild surprise about what someone has said

Examples

I dare say that's unlikely
He claims he can run a marathon without training—I dare say that's unlikely.
You think the meeting will end early? I dare say it will go on for hours.
She believes the weather will improve soon, but I dare say it might rain all day.

Related Terms

  • synonym: I doubt it - used to express disbelief or skepticism
  • synonym: I suppose - used to express uncertainty or reluctant agreement
