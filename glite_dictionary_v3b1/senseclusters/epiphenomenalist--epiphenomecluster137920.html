epiphenomenalist

adjective
mind as byproduct
9.9
Pronunciation: /e.pɪ.fə.ˈnɑː.mə.nəl.ɪst/ /; / /e.pɪ.fɪ.ˈnɑː.mə.nəl.ɪst/ (AmE), /e.pɪ.fə.ˈnɒm.ə.nəl.ɪst/ /; / /e.pɪ.fɪ.ˈnɒm.ə.nəl.ɪst/ (BrE)
relating to the belief that mental events are caused by physical processes but do not affect physical events themselves

Examples

Epiphenomenalist view
The philosopher defended an epiphenomenalist position in the debate about the mind and body.
Her epiphenomenalist perspective led her to doubt that thoughts could influence physical actions.
Some scientists adopt an epiphenomenalist view when discussing consciousness and brain activity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dualist - believing mind and body are separate and can affect each other
  • synonym: materialist - believing only physical matter exists and mental states are physical
