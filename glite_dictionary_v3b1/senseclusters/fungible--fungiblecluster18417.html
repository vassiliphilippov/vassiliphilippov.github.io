(of goods or commodities) able to be replaced by another identical item; mutually interchangeable, especially to satisfy an obligation.

Examples

Fungible goods
Cryptocurrencies are typically fungible, meaning one token is exchangeable for another of equal value.
In commercial transactions, gold is considered a fungible commodity because each unit is essentially identical.
The court ruled that the loan was to be repaid with fungible items of the same quality and quantity.
