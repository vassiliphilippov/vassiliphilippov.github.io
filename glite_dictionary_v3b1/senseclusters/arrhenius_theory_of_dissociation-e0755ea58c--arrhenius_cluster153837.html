Arrhenius theory of dissociation

noun
theory explaining acid-base dissociation in water
8.5
Pronunciation: / əˈreɪniəs ˌθiəri əv dɪˌsoʊsiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / əˈreɪniəs ˌθɪəri əv dɪˌsəʊsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
a theory in chemistry that states acids dissociate in water to produce hydrogen ions, while bases dissociate to produce hydroxyl ions; their reaction forms salt and water

Examples

Arrhenius theory of dissociation in chemistry
According to the Arrhenius theory of dissociation, mixing an acid and a base yields salt and water.
In high school chemistry, we studied the Arrhenius theory of dissociation to understand reactions in aqueous solutions.
The Arrhenius theory of dissociation explains how acids release hydrogen ions in solution.
