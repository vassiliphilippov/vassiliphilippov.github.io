misjudgment

noun
wrong decision
3.6
Pronunciation: /mɪs.ˈdʒʌdʒ.mənt/ (AmE), /mɪs.ˈdʒʌdʒ.mənt/ (BrE)
an incorrect or mistaken decision, opinion, or assessment about someone or something

Examples

A serious misjudgment
His costly misjudgment led to the loss of a major client for the company.
The coach admitted that her misjudgment affected the outcome of the game.
A single misjudgment can sometimes have serious consequences in business.
His misjudgment of the weather caused the team to cancel the trip unnecessarily.
The company suffered losses due to a serious misjudgment in their investment strategy.
She admitted her misjudgment and apologized for the confusion it caused.

Related Terms

  • synonym: error - a wrong decision or incorrect action
  • antonym: judgment - the ability to make considered decisions or come to sensible conclusions
  • synonym: mistake - an action or opinion that is wrong or incorrect
