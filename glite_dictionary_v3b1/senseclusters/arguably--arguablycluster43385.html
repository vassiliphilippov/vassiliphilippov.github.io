arguably

adverb
possibly, as can be supported by argument
4.7 3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈɑːr.gjuː.ə.bliː/ /; / /ˈɑːr.gjuː.bliː/ (AmE), /ˈɑː.gjuː.ə.bliː/ /; / /ˈɑː.gjuː.bliː/ (BrE)
Used to indicate that a statement can be supported by reasoning or evidence, though it may not be universally accepted.

Examples

He is arguably the best
He is arguably the best player in the league this season.
The movie is arguably the director's finest work to date.
This decision was arguably the turning point of her career.

  • synonym: possibly - perhaps, maybe
