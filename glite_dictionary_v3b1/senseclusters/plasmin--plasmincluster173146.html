plasmin

noun
enzyme that dissolves blood clots
Pronunciation: / ˈplæzmɪn / (AmE), / ˈplazmɪn / (BrE)
an enzyme in the blood that dissolves fibrin and breaks down blood clots

Examples

plasmin enzyme
Activation of plasmin can prevent dangerous blood clots from forming in the veins.
Doctors may measure plasmin levels to assess blood clotting disorders.
The body's natural production of plasmin helps to ensure wounds heal without excessive clotting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fibrinolysin - an enzyme that dissolves fibrin in blood clots
