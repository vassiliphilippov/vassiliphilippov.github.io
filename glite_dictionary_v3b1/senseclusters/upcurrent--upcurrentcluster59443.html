upcurrent

noun
upward flow
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpˌkɝːrənt / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌkʌrənt / (BrE)
a flow or area of water or air that is moving upward or is upstream from a particular point

Examples

A strong upcurrent
The fish struggled to swim against the strong upcurrent in the river.
Gliders use the upcurrent to gain altitude without using their engines.
Debris was carried upward by a sudden upcurrent in the atmosphere.
Researchers collected samples from the upcurrent to analyze water quality before it reached the city.
The pollution was traced back to a factory located in the upcurrent region.
Fish populations were healthier in the upcurrent compared to areas further downstream.

Related Terms

  • antonym: downcurrent - a flow of water or air moving downward
  • synonym: updraft - an upward movement of air
  • synonym: upstream - the direction toward the source of a river or stream
