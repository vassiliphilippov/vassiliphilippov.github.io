Alfred Dreyfus

proper noun
French army officer falsely accused of treason
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌælfrəd ˈdreɪfəs / (AmE), / ˌælfrɪd ˈdreɪfəs / (BrE)
(1859–1935) French army officer of Jewish descent who was falsely accused and convicted of treason, leading to a major political scandal and debates over anti-Semitism in France.

Examples

Alfred Dreyfus case
Many writers and intellectuals came to the defense of Alfred Dreyfus during his imprisonment.
The case of Alfred Dreyfus became a symbol of injustice and antisemitism in France.
The wrongful conviction of Alfred Dreyfus deeply divided French society at the time.
