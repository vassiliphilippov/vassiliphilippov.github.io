recurrently

adverb
in a repeated or recurring manner
4.3 0.11/m
Pronunciation: / r…™Ààk…úrn.tli / (AmE), / r…™Ààk åÀên.tli / (BrE)
In a way that occurs repeatedly or at regular intervals.

Examples

To recurrently happen
Data backups are performed recurrently to prevent information loss.
Electrical failures occurred recurrently throughout the week, disrupting normal operations.
The symptoms may appear recurrently over several months, causing discomfort for the patient.

Synonyms

  • repeatedly
    1.0 29/m
    repeatedly means doing something many times in general, while recurrently refers to events that return regularly or at intervals.
  • regularly
    1.0 40/m
    regularly describes actions at fixed, usual times, while recurrently emphasizes that events come back over time, not necessarily on schedule.
  • cyclically
    4.3 0.21/m
    cyclically means following a repeating cycle or pattern, while recurrently means something happens again without implying a fixed cycle.
  • again and again
    1.0 14/m
    again and again describes repeated acts in plain speech, while recurrently sounds more formal and highlights repeated occurrences.

Related Terms

  • synonym: periodically - at regular intervals of time
  • antonym: sporadically - at irregular or infrequent intervals
