axial muscle

noun
skeletal muscle of trunk or head
Pronunciation: / ˈæk.si.əl ˈmʌs.əl / (AmE), / ˈæk.si.əl ˈmʌs.l̩ / (BrE)
A skeletal muscle located in the trunk or head, responsible for movements of the axial skeleton.

Examples

Strong axial muscles
In fish, powerful axial muscles enable swift movement through the water.
Injuries to the axial muscles can affect both breathing and trunk stability.
The axial muscles support the vertebral column and maintain posture.

Related Terms

  • antonym: appendicular muscle - a muscle of the limbs, as opposed to the axial muscles of the trunk or head
