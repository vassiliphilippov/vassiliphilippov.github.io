feather

noun
hair-like structure on bird's body
5.3 1.0
Pronunciation: /ˈfe.ðər/ (AmE), /ˈfe.ðə/ (BrE)
A light, flat structure with a central shaft and many fine hair-like parts, growing from the skin of birds and forming their plumage.

Examples

A bird feather
She picked up a bright blue feather from the path in the park.
The eagle lost a feather as it swooped down to catch its prey.
The peacock's tail was covered in shimmering feathers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fur - the thick hair covering the skin of some animals
  • synonym: plume - a long, soft feather or arrangement of feathers
