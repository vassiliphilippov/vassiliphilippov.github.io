Hamiltonianism

noun
principles of Hamilton
Pronunciation: / ˌhæməlˈtoʊniənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌhæmɪlˈtəʊniənɪzəm / (BrE)
the political and economic principles associated with Alexander Hamilton, especially favoring a strong central government and industrial development

Examples

Hamiltonianism in politics
The founding fathers often disagreed about the merits of Hamiltonianism versus other political philosophies.
Many historians credit Hamiltonianism with shaping the early economic policies of the United States.
Debates about federal power in America can be traced back to the influence of Hamiltonianism.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Jeffersonianism - the political principles associated with Thomas Jefferson, favoring states' rights and agrarianism
  • synonym: federalism - support for a strong central government
