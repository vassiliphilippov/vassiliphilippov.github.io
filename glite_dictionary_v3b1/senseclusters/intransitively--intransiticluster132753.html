intransitively

adverb
in an intransitive manner
9.7
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtrænsətɪvli / (AmE), / ɪnˈtrænsətɪvli / (BrE)
In an intransitive manner; used to describe a verb being used without a direct object.

Examples

To use intransitively
In this exercise, you are asked to use each verb intransitively.
Some verbs work both transitively and intransitively, depending on the context.
The verb 'run' can be used intransitively in the sentence 'She runs every morning.'

Related Terms

  • antonym: transitively - in a transitive manner (with a direct object)
