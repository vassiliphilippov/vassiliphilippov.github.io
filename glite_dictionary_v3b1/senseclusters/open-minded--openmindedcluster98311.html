open-minded

adjective
willing to consider new ideas
3.3
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊ.pənˈmaɪn.dɪd / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.pənˈmaɪn.dɪd / (BrE)
willing to consider new ideas or opinions; receptive to different views

Examples

An open-minded person
A good leader should be open-minded when listening to new suggestions.
She is very open-minded and loves learning about different cultures.
The professor encouraged an open-minded discussion in class.
Despite their differences, the committee remained open-minded during the discussion.
Her open-minded attitude allowed her to make friends from all walks of life.
It's important to be open-minded when traveling to new countries and experiencing different cultures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: broad-minded - having or showing an ability to understand and accept other people's ideas or views
  • antonym: narrow-minded - unwilling to accept or consider others' ideas
  • antonym: prejudiced - having or showing a dislike based on preconceived opinions
  • synonym: tolerant - showing willingness to accept others' opinions or practices
