Saint Lawrence River

proper noun
major river in eastern North America
2.8
Pronunciation: / seɪnt ˈlɔːr(ə)ns ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈlɒr(ə)ns ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
A large river in eastern North America, flowing northeast from Lake Ontario into the Gulf of Saint Lawrence and the North Atlantic Ocean.

Examples

The Saint Lawrence River flow
Many ships travel along the Saint Lawrence River to reach the Great Lakes.
Salmon are commonly found in the waters of the Saint Lawrence River.
The Saint Lawrence River forms part of the border between Canada and the United States.
