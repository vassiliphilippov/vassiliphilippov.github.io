taboo

adjective
forbidden by custom or tradition
Pronunciation: / təˈbu / (AmE), / təˈbuː / (BrE)
forbidden or not allowed by social or cultural custom

Examples

Taboo food
In traditional Polynesian societies, certain foods were considered taboo for women.
It was taboo to speak the chief's name aloud.
Some rituals remain taboo and are avoided by the local villagers.
Discussing her divorce was considered taboo in the family.
In some cultures, eating certain foods is completely taboo.
The topic of money remains taboo in many professional settings.
In some cultures, eating pork is considered taboo.
The topic of salary was taboo in the office, so no one discussed it openly.
Wearing hats indoors used to be taboo in many households.

  • antonym: permissible - allowed, not forbidden
  • antonym: permitted - allowed by rules or custom
