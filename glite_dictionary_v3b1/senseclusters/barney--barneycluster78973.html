barney

noun
noisy argument
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑːrni / (AmE), / ˈbɑːni / (BrE)
a noisy argument, disagreement, or fight, especially in an informal or British context

Examples

A loud barney
After the match, the fans got into a barney outside the stadium.
They had a bit of a barney over who should pay for dinner.
A barney broke out in the pub when someone spilled a drink.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agreement - a situation in which people have the same opinion
  • synonym: fight - a violent confrontation or struggle
  • synonym: row - a loud argument or quarrel
