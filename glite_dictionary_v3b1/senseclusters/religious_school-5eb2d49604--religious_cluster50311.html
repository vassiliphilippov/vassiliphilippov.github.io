religious school

noun
school operated by a religious body
Pronunciation: / rɪˈlɪdʒəs skuːl / (AmE), / rɪˈlɪdʒəs skuːl / (BrE)
A school that is operated by a religious organization and often incorporates religious instruction into its curriculum.

Examples

A religious school class
Admission to the religious school requires participation in weekly services.
Many families choose to send their children to a religious school for a faith-based education.
The local religious school offers both secular and religious subjects in its curriculum.
