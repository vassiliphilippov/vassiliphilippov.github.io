rationalization

noun
systematic reorganization for efficiency
Pronunciation: / ˌræʃənələˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌræʃ.ə.nəl.əˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌræʃənəlʌɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌræʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌræʃ.ə.nə.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən /; / ˌræʃ.ə.nəlaɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of organizing or improving a process, business, or system to make it more logical or efficient

Examples

Rationalization of the system
After the rationalization of the process, there was less confusion among employees.
Governments often implement rationalization to improve the functioning of public services.

The rationalization of the supply chain led to faster delivery times and higher profits.
The company underwent a complete rationalization to reduce costs and boost productivity.
Government rationalisation of public spending has affected many social programs.
The rationalisation of the library's cataloging system simplified the search process for users.
The company's rationalisation of its workflow led to greater efficiency and productivity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bureaucratization - the process of introducing bureaucracy or rigid administrative procedures
  • antonym: inefficiency - lack of efficiency, especially in business processes
  • synonym: rationalization - systematic organization; American spelling of rationalisation
  • synonym: streamlining - the process of making an organization or system more efficient
  • synonym: systematization - the act of arranging according to a system or plan
