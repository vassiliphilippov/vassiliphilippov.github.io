tenseless

adjective
without tense marking
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛnsləs / (AmE), / ˈtɛnsləs / (BrE)
not marked for grammatical tense; lacking any indication of past, present, or future time

Examples

Tenseless verb form
Some languages use tenseless verbs to express actions without specifying time.
The study focused on the use of tenseless forms in indigenous languages.
A tenseless sentence can still communicate meaning through context or other grammatical markers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aspectless - not marked for grammatical aspect
  • antonym: tense - a grammatical category expressing time reference
