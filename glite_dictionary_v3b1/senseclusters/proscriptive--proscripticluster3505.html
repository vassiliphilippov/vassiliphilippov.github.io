proscriptive

adjective
forbidding actions
Pronunciation: / proʊˈskrɪptɪv / (AmE), / prəʊˈskrɪptɪv / (BrE)
forbidding or restricting certain actions or behaviors by rule or law

Examples

Proscriptive rules
The new law is highly proscriptive, banning several previously accepted practices.
Some schools have proscriptive dress codes that limit students' clothing choices.
A proscriptive approach can sometimes discourage creativity in the workplace.

Related Terms

  • antonym: permissive - allowing or tolerant of many behaviors
  • synonym: restrictive - limiting what is allowed or possible
