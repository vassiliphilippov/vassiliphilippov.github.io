diastase

noun
starch-digesting enzyme
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪəˌsteɪs / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəsteɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme (or preparation of enzymes) that breaks down starch into sugar, used in digestion or food processing

Examples

Diastase enzyme
The presence of diastase in saliva helps start the digestion of starchy foods.
Scientists measured the activity of diastase in different types of honey.
Without diastase, the body would have difficulty converting starches into usable sugars.
The laboratory tested the diastase activity in the new digestive supplement.
Adding diastase to the mixture helped convert the starches into simple sugars.
Pharmacists sometimes recommend diastase to aid patients with digestive problems.
