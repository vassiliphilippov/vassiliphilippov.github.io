latency

noun
delay before data becomes accessible (computing)
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪ.tən.si /; / ˈleɪtənsi / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.tən.si /; / ˈleɪtənsi / (BrE)
the delay or waiting time before something starts or responds, especially in technical or scientific contexts

Examples

Network latency
Engineers work to minimize network latency to improve user experience.
High latency can cause noticeable delays in video streaming and online gaming.
The latency of hard drives affects how quickly files can be accessed.
Reducing signal latency is important for real-time computer applications.
Scientists measured the latency of the subject's response to the visual cue.
The latency between pressing the button and hearing the sound was only a few milliseconds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lag - a delay in response or progress
  • synonym: reaction time - the length of time between a stimulus and a response
