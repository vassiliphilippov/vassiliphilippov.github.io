computer simulation

noun
imitation of real-world systems by computer program
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpjuːt̬ɚ ˌsɪmjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / kəmˈpjuːtə ˌsɪmjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
A process in which a computer program is used to imitate or represent real-world systems or processes.

Examples

A computer simulation of traffic
A computer simulation of traffic flow can help city planners improve road designs.
The researchers used a computer simulation to predict climate change over the next century.
Through computer simulation, students can safely experiment with chemical reactions without any real danger.
