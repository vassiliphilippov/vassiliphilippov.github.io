worrisome

adjective
causing worry or concern
Pronunciation: / ˈwɝː.i.səm /; / ˈwɜːriˌsəm / (AmE), / ˈwʌr.i.səm /; / ˈwʌrɪsəm / (BrE)
causing worry, concern, or anxiety

Examples

Worrisome test results
She received some worrisome test results from her doctor yesterday.
The lack of rain is becoming increasingly worrisome for local farmers.
The recent spike in unemployment figures is particularly worrisome to economists.
She found his sudden silence worrisome and wondered if something had happened.
The disappearance of the documents was quite worrisome to the investigators.
There has been a worrisome increase in the number of car thefts this year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: distressing - causing anxiety, sorrow, or pain
  • synonym: disturbing - making someone feel anxious or unsettled
  • antonym: reassuring - making you feel less worried or more confident
