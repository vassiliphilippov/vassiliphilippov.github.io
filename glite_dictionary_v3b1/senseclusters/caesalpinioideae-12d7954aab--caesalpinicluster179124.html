Caesalpinioideae

noun
subfamily of leguminous plants (Caesalpinioideae)
Pronunciation: / siːˌsælpɪniˈoʊɾiː.i / (AmE), / siːˌsæl.pɪ.niˈəʊ.diː.iː / (BrE)
a subfamily of flowering plants in the legume family, sometimes treated as a separate family (Caesalpiniaceae) in certain classification systems, including trees, shrubs, and lianas mostly with pinnate leaves

Examples

Caesalpinioideae flowering plants
Bauhinia is a well-known genus within the Caesalpinioideae.
Many tropical forests are dominated by legumes, particularly those of the Caesalpinioideae.
The Caesalpinioideae subfamily includes many ornamental and economically important trees.
