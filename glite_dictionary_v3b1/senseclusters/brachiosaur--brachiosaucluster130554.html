brachiosaur

noun
long-necked dinosaur
Pronunciation: / ˈbrækiəˌsɔr / (AmE), / ˈbrækiəˌsɔː / (BrE)
a large, long-necked herbivorous dinosaur from the Jurassic period, known for its front legs being longer than its back legs

Examples

A brachiosaur dinosaur
The paleontologist carefully reconstructed the skull of a brachiosaur for the new exhibit.
Children were amazed by the towering skeleton of a brachiosaur at the natural history museum.
Unlike many other dinosaurs, the brachiosaur had longer front legs than back legs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diplodocus - a long-necked herbivorous dinosaur from the Jurassic period
  • antonym: tyrannosaurus - a large carnivorous dinosaur from the Cretaceous period
