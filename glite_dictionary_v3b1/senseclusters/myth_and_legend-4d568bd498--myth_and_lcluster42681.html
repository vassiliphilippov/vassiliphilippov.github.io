myth and legend

noun
traditional explanatory stories or beliefs
1.0
Pronunciation: / mɪθ ænd ˈlɛdʒənd / (AmE), / mɪθ ænd ˈlɛdʒənd / (BrE)
traditional stories, especially those explaining historical events or cultural beliefs, often regarded as unverified or exaggerated

Examples

Myths and legends of old
In Scottish history, myths and legends are often intertwined with real events.
Many ancient civilizations have their own myths and legends to explain natural disasters.
The museum exhibit explores the myths and legends behind famous archaeological sites.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fact - a thing that is known or proved to be true
  • synonym: folklore - the traditional beliefs, customs, and stories of a community
  • synonym: myth - a traditional story, especially one about the early history of a people or explaining a natural or social phenomenon
