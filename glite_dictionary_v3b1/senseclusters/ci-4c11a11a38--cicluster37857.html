Ci

noun
unit of radioactivity (curie)
9.9 1.49/m
Pronunciation: / siÀê a…™ / (AmE), / siÀê a…™ / (BrE)
a unit of radioactivity equal to the amount of a radioactive substance in which 37,000,000,000 atomic disintegrations occur per second; the curie

Examples

The Ci value
All radioactive materials are labeled according to their Ci value.
In medical imaging, doses are often measured in millicuries (mCi) or microcuries (ŒºCi), subdivisions of the Ci.
The sample had an activity of 5 Ci and required careful handling.
