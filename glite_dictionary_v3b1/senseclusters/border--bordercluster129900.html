border

verb
add a border or edge
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈbɔrdər /; / ˈbɔrdɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɔːdə / (BrE)
to provide something with a border or edge, especially as decoration or a frame

Examples

To border a picture
The artist bordered the painting with a thin gold frame.
We decided to border the curtain with lace for a decorative touch.
You can border your garden path with small stones or bricks.
Tall hedges border the garden, giving it the look of a private sanctuary.
The invitation cards were bordered with an intricate floral pattern.
The photo was bordered with a thin strip of gold.
