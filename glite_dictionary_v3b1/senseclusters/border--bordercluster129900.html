border

verb
add a border or edge
3.1 1.86/m
Pronunciation: / Ààb…îrd…ôr /; / Ààb…îrd…ö / (AmE), / Ààb…îÀêd…ô / (BrE)
to provide something with a border or edge, especially as decoration or a frame

Examples

To border a picture
The artist bordered the painting with a thin gold frame.
We decided to border the curtain with lace for a decorative touch.
You can border your garden path with small stones or bricks.
Tall hedges border the garden, giving it the look of a private sanctuary.
The invitation cards were bordered with an intricate floral pattern.
The photo was bordered with a thin strip of gold.

Synonyms

  • frame
    1.9 8.74/m
    frame means to put an item in a physical frame and surround it, while border means to add a decorative edge or trim.
  • frame in
    4.5 0.34/m
    frame in means to enclose something tightly as if in a frame, while border means to add a decorative edge around it.
  • edge
    4.2 0.16/m
    edge means to give an object a thin rim or to sharpen it, while border means to add a decorative outer trim.
