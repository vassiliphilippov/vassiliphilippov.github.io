rebaptize

verb
to baptize again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːbæpˈtaɪz /; / ˌriːˈbæptaɪz / (AmE), / ˌriːbæpˈtaɪz /; / ˌriːˈbæptaɪz / (BrE)
to baptize someone again, or to give someone or something a new name or identity (often in a religious context)

Examples

To rebaptize a person
After joining the new church, he was rebaptized to mark his renewed faith.
The city was rebaptized after independence to reflect its cultural heritage.
Some traditions require that converts be rebaptized even if they were baptized as infants.
The church decided to rebaptize new members who had doubts about their first baptism.
Some religious groups will rebaptize individuals who join from other denominations.
After learning more about the faith, she chose to be rebaptized as an adult.

Related Terms
