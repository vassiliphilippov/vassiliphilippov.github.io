tule

noun
marsh plant
Pronunciation: / ˈtuːli / (AmE), / ˈtuːli / (BrE)
a tall, grasslike marsh plant found in wetlands of western North America, especially in California

Examples

A tule plant
The ducks built their nests among the tule along the lake shore.
Many wetlands in California are covered with dense stands of tule.
Native peoples used tule to make mats, boats, and shelters.
The shallow lake was surrounded by dense stands of tule swaying in the breeze.
Many birds nest among the tule in the wetland preserve each spring.
Early settlers used tule to build shelters and mats along the riverbanks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bulrush - a tall marsh plant with cylindrical stems
  • synonym: cattail - a marsh plant with long leaves and brown flower spikes
