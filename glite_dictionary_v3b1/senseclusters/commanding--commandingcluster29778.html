commanding

adjective
authoritative presence
Pronunciation: / kəˈmændɪŋ / (AmE), / kəˈmɑːndɪŋ / (BrE)
having a strong presence or showing authority that makes others pay attention or obey

Examples

Commanding voice
The general gave a commanding speech that inspired the entire army.
Her commanding voice made everyone in the room fall silent.
He has a commanding presence that makes him a natural leader.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authoritative - having or showing the confidence of someone in power
  • degree higher: domineering - asserting one's will over others in an arrogant way
  • antonym: submissive - willing to obey or give in to others
