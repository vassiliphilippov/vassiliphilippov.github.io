chalaza

noun
spiral band connecting egg yolk to membrane
9.7 8.8
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈleɪ.zə/ (AmE), /kə.ˈleɪ.zə/ (BrE)
either of two spiral bands of tissue that connect the yolk of an egg to its surrounding membrane at either end of the shell

Examples

Egg chalaza
Some recipes advise removing the chalaza for a smoother custard texture.
The chalaza keeps the yolk suspended in the center of the egg.
When separating an egg, you might notice the chalaza attached to the yolk.
