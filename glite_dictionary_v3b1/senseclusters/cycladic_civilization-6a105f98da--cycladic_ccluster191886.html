Cycladic civilization

noun
Bronze Age civilisation of the Cyclades islands
Pronunciation: / səɪˈklædɪk ˌsɪvələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / sɪˈkladɪk ˌsɪvɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the Bronze Age civilisation that developed on the Cyclades islands in the southern Aegean Sea, flourishing from about 3000 to 1100 BC

Examples

Ancient Cycladic civilization sites
Archaeologists have uncovered impressive settlements from the Cycladic civilisation on several Aegean islands.
The art of the Cycladic civilisation is renowned for its abstract marble figurines.
The study of the Cycladic civilisation helps us understand early Aegean cultures.
