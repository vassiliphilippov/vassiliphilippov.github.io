prefabricated

adjective
made in advance
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈfæbrɪˌkeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈfæbrɪkeɪtɪd / (BrE)
made in advance, usually in sections or parts, so it can be quickly assembled or used

Examples

Prefabricated building parts
The new school was constructed from prefabricated units to save time and money.
Many modern homes use prefabricated walls that are assembled on site.
His answer sounded prefabricated, as if he had memorized it beforehand.
The workers assembled the prefabricated walls in just a few hours.
Many schools use prefabricated classrooms to quickly add space for students.
After the storm, families moved into prefabricated shelters provided by the government.

Related Terms

  • antonym: custom-built - made to individual specifications
  • synonym: modular - made with standardized units for easy assembly
  • synonym: ready-made - already made and available for use
