spoonleaf yucca

noun
a yucca plant with long, stiff leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈspuːnˌlif ˈjʌkə / (AmE), / ˈspuːnˌliːf ˈjʌkə / (BrE)
A species of yucca with long, stiff leaves that have filamentlike appendages.

Examples

A spoonleaf yucca plant
A cluster of spoonleaf yuccas provides architectural interest to the front yard.
Gardeners appreciate the spoonleaf yucca for its striking foliage and low maintenance needs.
The spoonleaf yucca thrives in dry, sandy soils and is common in desert landscaping.
