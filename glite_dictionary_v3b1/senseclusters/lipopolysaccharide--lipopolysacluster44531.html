lipopolysaccharide

noun
bacterial membrane molecule
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌlɪpoʊˌpɑliˈsækəˌraɪd / (AmE), / ˌlɪpəʊˌpɒliˈsækəraɪd / (BrE)
a large molecule found in the outer membrane of Gram-negative bacteria that can trigger strong immune responses

Examples

Lipopolysaccharide molecule
Researchers measured the immune response after injecting lipopolysaccharide into the test subjects.
The presence of lipopolysaccharide in the bloodstream can cause fever and inflammation.
Many antibiotics target the lipopolysaccharide layer of harmful bacteria.
Scientists measured the immune response after exposing cells to lipopolysaccharide in the laboratory.
The presence of lipopolysaccharide in the bloodstream can lead to severe inflammation.
Researchers often use lipopolysaccharide to study how the body reacts to bacterial infections.

Related Terms

  • synonym: endotoxin - a toxin present inside bacterial cells, often a lipopolysaccharide
