englobe

verb
to include within
Pronunciation: / ɛnˈɡloʊb / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡləʊb / (BrE)
to include or incorporate something within a larger whole, group, or system

Examples

To englobe all rules
The new policy englobes all previous regulations under a single framework.
Several small villages were englobed into the expanding city limits last year.
Her research englobes various theories from different scientific fields.

Related Terms

  • synonym: encompass - to include or surround completely
  • antonym: exclude - to leave out or not include
