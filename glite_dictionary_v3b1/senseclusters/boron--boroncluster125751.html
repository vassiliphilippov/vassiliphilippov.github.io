boron

noun
a chemical element (B), trivalent metalloid
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈbɔːrɑn / (AmE), / ˈbɔːrɒn / (BrE)
a chemical element (symbol B), a trivalent metalloid found in nature both as a hard black crystal and as a yellow or brown powder, used in various industrial and chemical applications

Examples

Boron element
Boron is used in the production of borosilicate glass, which is known for its durability.
Researchers discovered that plants require tiny amounts of boron for healthy growth.
The laboratory handled the boron powder with care to prevent any contamination.
