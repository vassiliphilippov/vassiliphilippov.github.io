white ash

noun
Australian tree with white or grey shedding bark
7.8
Pronunciation: / waɪt æʃ / (AmE), / waɪt æʃ / (BrE)
a small to medium-sized tree native to Australia and Tasmania, with smooth white to light-grey bark that sheds in patches or strips

Examples

Australian white ash
Botanists studied the white ash for its unique adaptation to the local soil.
Several white ash trees grow along the nature reserve in Tasmania.
The white ash is easily recognized by its distinctive light-grey peeling bark.
