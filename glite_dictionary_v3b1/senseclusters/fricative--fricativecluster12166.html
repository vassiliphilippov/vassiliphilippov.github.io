fricative

adjective
sound made by forcing air through a narrow passage
9.9 6.4
Pronunciation: /ˈfrɪ.kə.tɪv/ (AmE), /ˈfrɪ.kə.tɪv/ (BrE)
(of a speech sound) produced by forcing air through a narrow passage, causing friction; characteristic of sounds such as 'f', 's', 'z', or 'th' in 'thin' and 'then'.

Examples

Fricative sound
In English, the ‘s’ in ‘see’ is pronounced as a voiceless fricative.
She studied the different ways languages use fricative sounds in their phonetic systems.
The sound at the beginning of ‘fish’ is a fricative consonant.

Related Terms

  • antonym: plosive - a speech sound produced by stopping airflow and then releasing it (e.g. 'p', 't', 'k')
