grapple

verb
to seize or struggle with someone
8.1 4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈɡræpəl / (AmE), / ˈɡræpəl / (BrE)
to seize hold of someone or something, especially in a firm struggle or wrestling match

Examples

To grapple with an opponent
As the sailors grappled with each other on deck, the captain tried to break up the fight.
During the scuffle, he grappled with the intruder to prevent him from escaping.
The two boxers grappled in the center of the ring, each trying to gain the upper hand.

Related Terms

  • antonym: release - to let go or free from physical hold or grip
  • synonym: wrestle - to struggle or fight physically with someone, especially in sport
