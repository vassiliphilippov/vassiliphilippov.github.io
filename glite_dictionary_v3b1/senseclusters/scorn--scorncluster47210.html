scorn

noun
strong contempt or disrespect
Pronunciation: / skɔrn / (AmE), / skɔːn / (BrE)
a strong feeling or open display of contempt or lack of respect for someone or something

Examples

Public scorn
His words were met with scorn from the audience.
She looked at the broken vase with obvious scorn.
The proposal was dismissed with scorn by the committee.
He spoke about the proposal with obvious scorn in his voice.
Her ideas were met with scorn by the other scientists at the conference.
The critic's review was full of scorn for the new film.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contempt - the feeling that something is worthless or beneath consideration
  • antonym: respect - a feeling of deep admiration for someone or something
