delegate

verb
assign a task to someone
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛl.əˌɡeɪt /; / ˈdɛlɪˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛl.ɪ.ɡeɪt /; / ˈdɛlɪɡeɪt / (BrE)
to give a job, responsibility, or authority to someone else so they can do it for you

Examples

To delegate tasks
He prefers to delegate responsibility rather than handle every detail himself.
Learning to delegate effectively is an important skill for any leader.
The manager decided to delegate several key tasks to her assistant.
Effective leaders know when to delegate authority to others.
He struggled at first to delegate important decisions, preferring to do everything himself.
The manager decided to delegate some of her tasks to junior staff members.

  • antonym: relinquish - to give up or hand over a responsibility or possession
