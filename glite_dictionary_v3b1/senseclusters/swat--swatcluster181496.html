swat

verb
hit or slap swiftly and forcefully
2.3 1.53/m
Pronunciation: / swÉ‘t / (AmE), / swÉ’t / (BrE)
to hit or slap something quickly and forcefully, especially to kill or drive away an insect

Examples

To swat a fly
He tried to swat the mosquito on his arm before it bit him.
She missed when she tried to swat the fly buzzing around the kitchen.
The cat jumped up to swat at the toy dangling from the string.
She kept swatting mosquitoes at dusk to avoid being bitten.
He swatted the fly with a rolled-up newspaper.
The child tried swatting at the bee buzzing near his head.

Synonyms

  • slap
    1.0 27/m
    slap is a quick flat hand blow often aimed at a person, while swat is a short forceful hit usually aimed at insects.
  • hit
    1.0 142/m
    hit means to make forceful contact with something in many situations, while swat is a quick strike used mainly against insects.
  • strike
    2.4 29/m
    strike is a formal or planned blow often used in work or military contexts, while swat is a quick informal hit at insects.

Related Terms
