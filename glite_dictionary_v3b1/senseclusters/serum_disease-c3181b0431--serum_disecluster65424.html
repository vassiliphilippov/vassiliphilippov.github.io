serum disease

noun
allergic reaction to injected antiserum
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪrəm dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈsɪərəm dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
a reaction, often delayed, that occurs after the injection of an antiserum, caused by the body's immune response to proteins in the donor serum

Examples

Serum disease reaction
Doctors must monitor for signs of serum disease when administering animal-derived antivenoms.
Symptoms of serum disease can include fever, rash, and joint pain.
The patient developed serum disease several days after receiving the antiserum injection.
