packed cells

noun
blood cells separated from plasma for transfusion
4.9
Pronunciation: /pækt selz/ (AmE), /pækt selz/ (BrE)
blood cells that have been separated from plasma, used for transfusions to raise red blood cell count without increasing blood volume significantly

Examples

Packed cells transfusion
After donating blood, the laboratory technician separated the plasma from the packed cells.
The doctor ordered packed cells for the patient who was suffering from severe anemia.
Transfusions with packed cells help increase hemoglobin levels without adding unnecessary fluid.
