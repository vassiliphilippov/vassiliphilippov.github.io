malediction

noun
a spoken curse; invocation of harm
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˌmæl.ɪˈdɪk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌmal.ɪˈdɪk.ʃən / (BrE)
A magical or formal spoken curse intended to bring about harm or evil; an utterance of a curse.

Examples

An angry malediction
He feared that the old man's angry malediction would bring bad luck to the village.
In tales of ancient kings, a single malediction could doom an entire lineage.
The witch uttered a malediction upon those who had wronged her.

Related Terms

  • antonym: benediction - a blessing, especially at the end of a religious service; expression of good wishes
  • synonym: curse - an appeal to a supernatural power to inflict harm
