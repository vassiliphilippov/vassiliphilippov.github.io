spot

verb
become stained or marked with spots
2.2 3.93/m
Pronunciation: / spÉ‘t / (AmE), / spÉ’t / (BrE)
to make or become marked, stained, or blemished with spots; to mark something for identification

Examples

To spot the shirt
Be careful with red wine; this fabric spots very easily.
If it rains on the polished car, the surface spots quickly.
The tablecloth spotted after I spilled coffee on it.
Rainwater spotted the windows, making them look dirty.
She was upset when ink spotted her favorite notebook.
The red paint spotted his shirt during the job.
She was upset when ink spotted her favorite dress during the project.
The rain spotted the surface of the newly cleaned car.
Years of neglect had spotted the walls with stains and marks.

Synonyms

  • dot
    1.0 2.11/m
    dot is a very small round mark used deliberately to label or highlight, while spot is a general mark or stain.
  • speck
    5.6 0.08/m
    speck is a tiny dot or particle that lightly covers a surface, while spot is any mark or stain of various sizes.
  • fleck
    6.0 1.40/m
    fleck is a small irregular spot of color or light on a surface, while spot is a broader, general mark or stain.
  • blot
    3.7 0.27/m
    blot is a dark stain often made by liquid absorption on paper or cloth, while spot is any mark or stain.
  • blotch
    5.3 0.25/m
    blotch is an irregular patch of color or stain that stands out visibly, while spot is a more general small mark.
  • splotch
    6.7 0.28/m
    splotch is a messy, irregular mark of liquid or paint that spreads, while spot is a simpler or smaller mark or stain.
  • mottle
    8.0 0.62/m
    mottle is a pattern of many small spots or streaks of different shades, while spot is a single mark or stain.
  • blemish
    5.5 0.28/m
    blemish is a flaw that spoils appearance and quality, while spot is simply a mark or stain that may or may not spoil.
