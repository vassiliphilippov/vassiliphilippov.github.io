revisit

verb
to return to consider
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈvɪzɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈvɪzɪt / (BrE)
to return to an idea, topic, or issue again, often to consider or examine it further

Examples

To revisit an idea
The committee decided to revisit the proposal after receiving new information.
He plans to revisit his hometown for the first time in years.
Let's revisit this topic at the end of the meeting to see if opinions have changed.
Every few years, the author likes to revisit the themes of her earlier novels.
We should revisit this issue at the next meeting to see if anything has changed.
He plans to revisit his childhood home during the holidays.

Related Terms

  • synonym: return - to go back to a place or situation
  • synonym: review - to examine or assess something again
