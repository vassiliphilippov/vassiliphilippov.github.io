eyehole

noun
hole in fabric for a cord or hook
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈaɪˌhəʊl / (BrE)
a small hole, usually round and edged, made in cloth or leather to allow the passage of a cord, hook, or bar

Examples

Curtain eyehole
Make sure the curtain rod fits easily into the eyehole.
She threaded the ribbon through each eyehole on her corset.
The leather belt's eyehole tore from repeated use.

Related Terms

  • synonym: eyelet - a small hole with a reinforced edge, especially in fabric, for a lace or cord
