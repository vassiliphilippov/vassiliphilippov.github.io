Auriga

proper noun
a constellation in the northern sky
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈɔː.rɪ.gə/ /; / /ɔː.ˈraɪ.gə/ (AmE), /ˈɔː.rɪ.gə/ /; / /ɔː.ˈraɪ.gə/ (BrE)
A prominent constellation in the northern sky, located between the constellations of Ursa Major and Orion at the edge of the Milky Way.

Examples

Auriga constellation
According to mythology, Auriga represents a charioteer holding the reins of a chariot.
Astronomers often point out Capella, the brightest star in Auriga.
The constellation Auriga is easily visible in the winter night sky.
