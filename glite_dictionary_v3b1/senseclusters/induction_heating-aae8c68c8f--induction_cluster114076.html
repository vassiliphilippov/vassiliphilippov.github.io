induction heating

noun
heating by induced electric current
4.5
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdʌkʃən ˈhitiŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌkʃən ˈhiːtɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of heating a conducting material by generating an electric current within it using electromagnetic induction

Examples

Induction heating process
Induction heating is commonly used in metalworking for tasks such as forging and hardening.
The laboratory installed new equipment that relies on induction heating for faster and cleaner processing.
Using induction heating helps improve efficiency and reduces contamination during the manufacturing process.

Related Terms

  • synonym: resistance heating - heating produced by passing an electric current through a conductor's resistance
