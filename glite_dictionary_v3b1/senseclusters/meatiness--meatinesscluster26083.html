meatiness

noun
rich, meaty quality
4.5
Pronunciation: / ˈmiːtinəs / (AmE), / ˈmiːtinəs / (BrE)
the quality of resembling meat in taste, texture, or substance, or having richness and depth

Examples

Meatiness of the stew
The meatiness of the mushroom stew surprised everyone at the vegetarian dinner.
She appreciated the meatiness of the novel, which was full of complex ideas.
Adding lentils increased the meatiness of the soup without using any actual meat.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blandness - the quality of lacking strong flavor or substance
  • synonym: richness - the quality of having a strong, full flavor or substance
