looseness

noun
lack of strict accuracy or rigor
1.7 5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈluːs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈluːs.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of not being strict, precise, or controlled; lack of accuracy or rigor

Examples

Looseness in writing
Journalists must avoid looseness in reporting facts to maintain credibility.
The looseness of his research methods raised questions about the validity of the results.

Some scholars argue that the looseness of legal definitions can lead to inconsistent enforcement.
The looseness of the committee's rules allowed for lively debate and creative ideas.
