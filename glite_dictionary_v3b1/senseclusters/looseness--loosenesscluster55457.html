looseness

noun
lack of strict accuracy or rigor
5.3 0.21/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàluÀês.n…ôs / (AmE), / ÀàluÀês.n…ôs / (BrE)
the quality of not being strict, precise, or controlled; lack of accuracy or rigor

Examples

Looseness in writing
Journalists must avoid looseness in reporting facts to maintain credibility.
The looseness of his research methods raised questions about the validity of the results.
There is a certain looseness in his definition that could lead to confusion.
She admired the looseness with which he interpreted the deadlines, but it sometimes caused problems.
Some scholars argue that the looseness of legal definitions can lead to inconsistent enforcement.
The looseness of the committee's rules allowed for lively debate and creative ideas.

Synonyms

  • bagginess
    6.0 0.00/m
    bagginess suggests wide, unclear limits in rules or texts, while looseness covers both lack of control and lack of precision.
  • laxity
    8.3 0.29/m
    laxity means careless lack of strictness in rules or practice, while looseness also implies weak control and less accuracy.
  • imprecision
    6.5 0.43/m
    imprecision means not being exact or detailed in facts or measurements, while looseness also suggests lack of strict control or rigor.
  • inexactitude
    8.6 0.03/m
    inexactitude is a formal word for small inaccuracies in facts or numbers, while looseness adds vagueness or weak control.

Related Terms
