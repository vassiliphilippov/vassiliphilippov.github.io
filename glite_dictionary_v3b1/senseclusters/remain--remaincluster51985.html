remain

verb
stay in a place; not leave
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: / rɪˈmeɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈmeɪn / (BrE)
to stay in the same place, state, or condition; to continue to exist or not change

Examples

To remain in place
Despite the alarm, she chose to remain in the building.
Only a few students remained in the classroom after the bell rang.
You must remain in your seats until the end of the performance.
Only a few trees remain after the fire.
She decided to remain at the company despite the merger.
You can remain in the waiting room until you are called.
After the fire, only the old stone chimney remained standing.
Despite the changes in the company, several important issues still remain unresolved.
Only two pieces of cake remain on the plate.

  • synonym: persist - to continue to exist, especially for longer than expected
