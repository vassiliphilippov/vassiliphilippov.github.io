remain

verb

to stay in the same place, state, or condition; to continue to exist or not change

Examples

To remain in place
Despite the alarm, she chose to remain in the building.
Only a few students remained in the classroom after the bell rang.
You must remain in your seats until the end of the performance.
Only a few trees remain after the fire.
She decided to remain at the company despite the merger.
You can remain in the waiting room until you are called.
After the fire, only the old stone chimney remained standing.
Despite the changes in the company, several important issues still remain unresolved.
Only two pieces of cake remain on the plate.

Synonyms

  • stay
    1.0 422/m
    stay is used often in everyday speech to mean staying in a place or condition, while remain is more neutral or slightly formal.
  • rest
    1.0 1.20/m
    rest describes something that stays unchanged or still, while remain is a general verb for staying in a place or condition.
  • continue
    1.0 42/m
    continue means going on without stopping, often after a pause, while remain focuses on staying in the same state or place.
  • persist
    3.5 9.78/m
    persist means to continue existing, often longer than expected or despite problems, while remain is neutral about duration or difficulty.
  • last
    1.0 86/m
    last means to continue for a period of time and survive changes, while remain focuses on staying in the same place or condition.
  • endure
    3.6 9.53/m
    endure means to continue for a long time despite hardship or pain, while remain describes staying without change or struggle.
