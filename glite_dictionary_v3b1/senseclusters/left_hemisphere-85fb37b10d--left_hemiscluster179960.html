left hemisphere

noun
left half of the brain
Pronunciation: / lɛft ˈhɛmɪˌsfɪr /; / ˌlɛft ˈhɛmɪˌsfɪr / (AmE), / lɛft ˈhɛmɪsˌfɪə /; / ˌlɛft ˈhɛmɪsfɪə / (BrE)
the left half of the brain, which controls the right side of the body and is involved in language, analytical, and logical functions

Examples

The left hemisphere of brain
Damage to the left hemisphere can affect a person's ability to speak clearly.
Doctors studied the activity in the left hemisphere during the reading task.
The left hemisphere is often associated with logical thinking and language skills.
Damage to the left hemisphere can affect a person's ability to speak or understand language.
Researchers studied how the left hemisphere processes written and spoken words.
The left hemisphere is often dominant in right-handed individuals.
