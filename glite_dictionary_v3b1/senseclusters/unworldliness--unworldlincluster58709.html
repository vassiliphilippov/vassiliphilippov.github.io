unworldliness

noun
lack of worldly experience
8.6
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈwɜrldlinəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈwɜːldlinəs / (BrE)
the quality of being inexperienced or unconcerned with practical or material matters, often focusing on spiritual or moral values instead

Examples

Her unworldliness showed
Her unworldliness made city life challenging for her.
His unworldliness was obvious when he tried to negotiate the contract.
The novel's main character is admired for her unworldliness and pure intentions.
His unworldliness made him uninterested in wealth or social status.
Many admired her unworldliness and dedication to helping others rather than seeking personal gain.
The monk's unworldliness was clear in his simple lifestyle and focus on meditation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: innocence - lack of worldly experience or sophistication
  • antonym: materialism - the belief that material possessions and physical comfort are more important than spiritual values
  • synonym: naivety - lack of experience, wisdom, or judgment
  • synonym: spirituality - the quality of being concerned with the human spirit or soul
  • antonym: worldliness - experience and knowledge of practical or material matters
