ototoxicity

noun
drug-induced hearing damage
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊtoʊtɑkˈsɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌəʊtəʊtɒkˈsɪsəti / (BrE)
damage to hearing or balance caused by certain drugs or chemicals

Examples

Ototoxicity from drugs
The patient developed ototoxicity after receiving high doses of the antibiotic.
Doctors monitor for signs of ototoxicity when prescribing certain chemotherapy drugs.
Early detection of ototoxicity can help prevent permanent hearing loss.
