diluter

noun
one who weakens
6.8
Pronunciation: / daɪˈluːtər / (AmE), / daɪˈluːtə / (BrE)
a person or thing that makes something weaker, less effective, or less pure by reducing its strength or quality

Examples

Diluter of tradition
Some critics called the new manager a diluter of the company's original values.
Adding too much water can act as a diluter and ruin the taste of the juice.
The committee feared that outside influence would be a diluter of their mission.

Related Terms

  • antonym: strengthener - a person or thing that makes something stronger or more effective
  • synonym: weaken - to make something less strong or effective
