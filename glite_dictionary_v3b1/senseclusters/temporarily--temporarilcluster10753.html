For a short or limited period of time; not permanently.

Examples

To be temporarily closed
Due to renovations, the library will be temporarily closed to the public.
She moved temporarily to New York for an internship.
The website was temporarily unavailable because of maintenance work.

Synonyms

  • for a while
    1.0 60/m
    for a while means lasting for a short period in everyday speech, while temporarily is a general adverb usable in formal and informal contexts.
  • awhile
    2.9 3.06/m
    awhile means for a short time used after verbs, while temporarily is a general adverb used in many formal and informal contexts.
  • for the moment
    3.4 10/m
    for the moment means just for now and often feels immediate, while temporarily covers short periods without that urgent sense.
  • for the time being
    3.4 4.78/m
    for the time being means until something changes or a plan is different for now, while temporarily simply means not permanent.
  • transiently
    6.8 0.12/m
    transiently means in a short or passing way and sounds formal or technical, while temporarily is common in everyday and formal use.
