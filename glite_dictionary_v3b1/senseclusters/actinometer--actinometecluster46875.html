actinometer

noun
instrument for measuring electromagnetic radiation
7.3 9.3
Pronunciation: /æk.ˈtɪn.ə.mɪ.tər/ (AmE), /æk.ˈtɪn.ə.mɪ.tə/ (BrE)
an instrument used to measure the intensity of electromagnetic radiation, usually by means of a photochemical effect

Examples

An actinometer instrument
Before modern sensors, an actinometer was commonly employed in solar research.
Scientists used an actinometer to determine the amount of ultraviolet radiation reaching the surface.
The actinometer provided precise measurements crucial for the photochemical experiment.
