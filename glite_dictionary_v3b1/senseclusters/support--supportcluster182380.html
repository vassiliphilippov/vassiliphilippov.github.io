support

verb
provide financial or material help
1.4 64/m
Pronunciation: / s…ôÀàp…îrt / (AmE), / s…ôÀàp…îÀêt / (BrE)
to provide someone with money or other material help needed for their basic needs or to achieve something

Examples

To support a family
Many parents continue to support their children through university.
She worked two jobs to support her family after her husband lost his job.
The organization aims to support students with scholarships and grants.

Synonyms

  • provide
    1.9 98/m
    provide means to supply money or necessities for living, while support means giving material help for someone's needs.
  • provide aid
    2.9 0.41/m
    provide aid is formal help, often with money or supplies for people in crisis, while support is broader personal assistance.
  • assist
    1.8 1.49/m
    assist means to help make a task easier, while support focuses on giving the material or long-term help someone needs.
  • help out
    1.0 11/m
    help out is informal, short-term assistance in a specific situation, while support covers ongoing material or financial help.
