wadcutter

noun
flat-nosed bullet
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑdˌkʌtər / (AmE), / ˈwɒdˌkʌtə / (BrE)
a flat-nosed bullet designed to cut clean, round holes in paper targets for shooting practice or competitions

Examples

A wadcutter bullet
For the competition, she chose a wadcutter to ensure clean holes in the target.
Many target shooters prefer using wadcutters because they make scoring easier.
The wadcutter design helps judges see exactly where each shot landed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hollow point - a bullet with a hollowed tip designed to expand on impact
  • antonym: round nose - a bullet with a rounded tip used for general shooting
