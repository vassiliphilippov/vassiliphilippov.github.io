mouch

verb
to wander aimlessly
Pronunciation: / maʊtʃ / (AmE), / maʊtʃ / (BrE)
to move about or wander in a slow, aimless, or lazy way, often without a clear purpose.

Examples

To mouch around the house
On rainy afternoons, he would mouch around the house with nothing to do.
They mouched through the park, chatting and watching the world go by.
Instead of working, she spent the morning mouching about the city center.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hurry - to move or act with speed
  • synonym: loiter - to stand or move about without purpose
  • synonym: wander - to walk or move in a leisurely or aimless way
