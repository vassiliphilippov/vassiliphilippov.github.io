summation

noun
final summary, especially in law
Pronunciation: / sʌˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / sʌˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
A final summary or review, especially one that concludes an argument or case, such as at the end of a trial.

Examples

Lawyer's summation speech
Each side presented a concise summation before the judge delivered the verdict.
In her closing summation, the prosecutor reminded the jury of the key evidence.
The defense attorney began his summation to reinforce the main points of the case.

Related Terms

  • synonym: summary - a brief statement of the main points
