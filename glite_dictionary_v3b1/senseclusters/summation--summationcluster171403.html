summation

noun
final summary, especially in law

A final summary or review, especially one that concludes an argument or case, such as at the end of a trial.

Examples

Lawyer's summation speech
Each side presented a concise summation before the judge delivered the verdict.
In her closing summation, the prosecutor reminded the jury of the key evidence.
The defense attorney began his summation to reinforce the main points of the case.

Synonyms

  • summing up
    4.4 0.35/m
    summing up gives a final spoken summary at the close of a case or argument, while summation names that formal ending.
  • recapitulation
    7.8 0.12/m
    recapitulation restates the main points in a formal way at the end of a talk, while summation is the concluding review.
  • sum-up
    3.9 0.01/m
    sum-up gives a short, casual statement of the main points, while summation sounds formal and is used in legal endings.
  • review
    1.9 0.42/m
    review repeats the main points at the end of a lecture or document, while summation often ends an argument or trial.
  • summary
    2.7 33/m
    summary gives the main ideas without details for any text or talk, while summation specifically concludes an argument or case.
  • recap
    3.2 2.31/m
    recap quickly reviews what was said, often in informal settings like meetings or shows, while summation is more formal and legal.
  • rundown
    3.9 3.02/m
    rundown gives a quick list or report of events or facts, while summation is a final, formal review of an argument.
