turnoff

noun
something that causes loss of interest or attraction
Pronunciation: / ˈtɜrnˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈtɜːn.ɒf / (BrE)
something that causes someone to lose interest, feel repelled, or become disinterested

Examples

A big turnoff
Bad manners at the dinner table can be a real turnoff for guests.
For me, arrogance is a major turnoff in any relationship.
His constant complaining was such a turnoff that she stopped returning his calls.

Related Terms

  • antonym: attraction - something that draws interest or attention
  • synonym: put-off - something that discourages or repels someone
