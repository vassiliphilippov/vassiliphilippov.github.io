quail bush

noun
spiny shrub of southwestern US and Mexico
Pronunciation: / ˈkweɪl bʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈkweɪl bʊʃ / (BrE)
A spiny shrub with silvery-scurfy leaves, native to the alkaline plains of the southwestern United States and Mexico.

Examples

The quail bush plant
Botanists studied the drought resistance of the quail bush in its natural habitat.
The desert was dotted with quail bush, providing shelter for birds and small animals.
We planted a quail bush in our garden to attract local wildlife.
