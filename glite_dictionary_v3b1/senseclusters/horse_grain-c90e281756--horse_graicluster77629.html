horse grain

noun
Indian twining herb for food and fodder
Pronunciation: / hɔrs ɡreɪn / (AmE), / hɔːs ɡreɪn / (BrE)
a twining herb of Old World tropics, cultivated in India for food and fodder, sometimes placed in the genus Dolichos.

Examples

Planting horse grain
Farmers in the region often plant horse grain as a reliable source of animal feed.
Many rural communities rely on horse grain during the dry season for their livestock.
The leaves of horse grain are collected and used for both fodder and cooking.
