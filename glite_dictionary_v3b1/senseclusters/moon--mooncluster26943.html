moon

noun
period between new moons; lunar month
3.7 3/m
Pronunciation: / mun / (AmE), / muÀên / (BrE)
A period of about 29.5 days between successive new moons, used in lunar calendars.

Examples

A harvest moon
Many festivals occur once every moon in some cultures.
The ancient calendar was based on the moon rather than the solar year.
The tides were measured from one moon to the next.

Synonyms

  • lunar month
    3.6 0.1/m
    a lunar month is the everyday calendar name for the roughly 29.5-day lunar cycle, while moon is used here to mean that period.
  • lunation
    9.9 0.02/m
    lunation is a formal word for the time between successive lunar phases, while moon is the calendar name for that period.
  • synodic month
    9.3 0.02/m
    a synodic month is the precise astronomical term for the average time between new lunar phases, while moon is the calendar period people use.

Related Terms

  • synonym: month - period of about four weeks, especially as based on the lunar cycle
