aggravating

adjective
making worse or more serious
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈæɡrəˌveɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈaɡrəveɪtɪŋ /; / ˈæɡrəveɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
making a situation worse, more serious, or more annoying

Examples

Aggravating situation
His constant criticism was only aggravating the tense atmosphere in the office.
Loud noises can be aggravating to people with migraines.
Missing the deadline will have an aggravating effect on the project's outcome.
The delay at the airport was especially aggravating after a long day of travel.
His aggravating habit of interrupting made meetings difficult for everyone.
It is aggravating when people talk loudly on their phones in public places.

Related Terms

  • antonym: alleviating - making less severe or more bearable
  • synonym: annoying - causing slight anger or irritation
  • synonym: irritating - causing annoyance or discomfort
  • antonym: soothing - making someone feel calmer or less annoyed
