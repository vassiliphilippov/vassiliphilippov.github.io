parapineal

noun
organ near pineal gland
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəˈpaɪniəl / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈpaɪniəl / (BrE)
a small organ or neural structure located beside the pineal gland in some vertebrates, often involved in light detection or circadian rhythm regulation

Examples

The parapineal organ
Scientists studied the parapineal to understand its role in regulating the fish's daily activity cycles.
The parapineal organ is found next to the pineal gland in some species of amphibians.
Research suggests the parapineal may help certain animals detect changes in light.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pineal gland - a small endocrine gland in the brain that produces melatonin
