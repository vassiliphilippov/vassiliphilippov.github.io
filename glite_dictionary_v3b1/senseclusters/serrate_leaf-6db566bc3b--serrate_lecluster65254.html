serrate leaf

noun
leaf with saw-toothed edge
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛr.eɪt lif / (AmE), / ˈsɛr.eɪt liːf / (BrE)
A leaf with a margin shaped like a saw, having teeth pointing toward the tip of the leaf.

Examples

A serrate leaf edge
Many roses have a characteristic serrate leaf that feels rough along the edges.
The serrate leaf of the elm tree helps distinguish it from other species.
When identifying plants, look for a serrate leaf with teeth pointing toward the tip.

Related Terms

  • antonym: entire leaf - a leaf with a smooth, unnotched margin
