monopole

noun
single-conductor antenna
8.9
Pronunciation: /ˈmɑː.nə.poʊl/ (AmE), /ˈmɒ.nə.pəʊl/ (BrE)
a radio antenna made of a single conductor mounted over a ground plane, typically a quarter-wavelength long

Examples

Monopole antenna
The technician installed a monopole antenna to improve the radio signal in the laboratory.
A monopole is often used in car radios because of its simple design and effectiveness.
Engineers measured the performance of the monopole on different ground planes during the experiment.
