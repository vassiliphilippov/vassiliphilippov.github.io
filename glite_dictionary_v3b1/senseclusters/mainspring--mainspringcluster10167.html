mainspring

noun
principal spring driving a clock or watch
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪnˌsprɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪnˌsprɪŋ / (BrE)
the main spring in a clock or watch that stores energy and makes the mechanism work

Examples

The mainspring in a watch
A skilled watchmaker replaced the mainspring to restore the clock's function.
The broken mainspring caused the old pocket watch to stop working.
Without a tightened mainspring, the music box would not play its tune.
A skilled horologist can repair a damaged mainspring without replacing the entire mechanism.
After years of use, he needed to replace the worn mainspring in his antique watch.
The mainspring of the clock broke, leaving it unable to keep time.
