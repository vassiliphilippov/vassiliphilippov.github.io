shylock

noun
moneylender charging high interest
Pronunciation: / ˈʃaɪˌlɑk / (AmE), / ˈʃaɪlɒk / (BrE)
a person who lends money at extremely high rates of interest, typically in an unethical or illegal manner

Examples

A shylock lender
Desperate for cash, he borrowed money from a local shylock and soon found himself in crippling debt.
The character in the novel reminded readers of a classic shylock, preying on the vulnerable.
The police arrested the notorious shylock for charging exorbitant interest rates.

Related Terms

  • synonym: usurer - a person who lends money at unreasonably high rates of interest
