scatback

noun
jazz scat singer
Pronunciation: / ˈskætˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈskætˌbæk / (BrE)
a backing singer who performs scat singing—improvised, wordless vocal sounds—to support a lead vocalist, especially in jazz music

Examples

Jazz scatback singer
The band's scatback added lively improvisations that energized the jazz performance.
During the concert, the scatback supported the lead singer with creative vocal sounds.
Many jazz groups feature a talented scatback to enhance their live shows.

Related Terms

  • synonym: backing vocalist - a singer who provides vocal support to the lead singer
  • antonym: lead vocalist - the main singer in a band or musical group
  • synonym: scat singer - a singer who improvises melodies and rhythms using wordless syllables
