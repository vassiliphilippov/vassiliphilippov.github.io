Erica cinerea

noun
a low European shrub with purple-red flowers
Pronunciation: / ˌɛrɪkə sɪˈnɪəriə / (AmE), / ˌɛrɪkə sɪˈnɪəriə / (BrE)
A species of low-growing European shrub bearing purple-red bell-shaped flowers, commonly known as bell heather.

Examples

Erica cinerea shrub
Erica cinerea is found widely on heathlands throughout western Europe.
Gardeners often plant Erica cinerea to attract bees and other pollinators.
The purple-red blossoms of Erica cinerea add vibrant color to moorland areas.
