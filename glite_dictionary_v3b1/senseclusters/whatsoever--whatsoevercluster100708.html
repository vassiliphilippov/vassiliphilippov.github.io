whatsoever

adverb
emphasizing 'at all'
Pronunciation: / ˌwʌtsoʊˈɛvər / (AmE), / ˌwɒtsəʊˈɛvə / (BrE)
used after a noun or clause to emphasize that there is no amount or degree of something at all (especially in negative statements)

Examples

No proof whatsoever
There was no evidence whatsoever to support the claim.
She showed no interest whatsoever in the proposal.
If you have any questions whatsoever, feel free to ask.
There was no evidence whatsoever to support the claim.
She had no interest whatsoever in joining the club.
The new policy made no difference whatsoever to the outcome.

Related Terms

  • synonym: at all - in any way or to any extent
