incarnate

adjective
embodied in bodily or physical form
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkɑrnət /; / ɪnˈkɑrˌneɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɑːnət /; / ɪnˈkɑː.nət / (BrE)
(of a spirit, quality, or deity) embodied or manifested in bodily or physical form

Examples

Kindness incarnate
She was kindness incarnate, always ready to help anyone in need.
Some cultures believe the gods can become incarnate as animals or humans.
The villain was described as evil incarnate in the novel.
For the artist, beauty incarnate appeared in the form of a graceful dancer.
Many cultures considered their ruler to be a god incarnate, worthy of worship.
She saw evil incarnate in the dictator who oppressed the nation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embodied - given a tangible or visible form; made concrete
