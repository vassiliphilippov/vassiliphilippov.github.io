Azilian

noun
late Paleolithic culture
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈzɪliən / (AmE), / əˈzɪliən / (BrE)
an archaeological culture of late Paleolithic western Europe, known for small stone tools and painted pebbles

Examples

The Azilian period
Archaeologists discovered painted pebbles typical of the Azilian in the cave.
The Azilian is recognized for its distinctive microlithic stone tools.
Artifacts from the Azilian period show a transition from earlier Paleolithic cultures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Epipaleolithic - the period following the Upper Paleolithic, marked by microlithic tools
  • antonym: Magdalenian - an earlier Upper Paleolithic culture in western Europe
