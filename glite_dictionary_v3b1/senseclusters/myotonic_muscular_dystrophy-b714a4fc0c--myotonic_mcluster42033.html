myotonic muscular dystrophy

noun
inherited muscle disorder causing progressive weakness
2.4
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪ.əˈtɑn.ɪk ˈmʌs.kjə.lɚ dɪsˈtrɒ.fi / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.əˈtɒn.ɪk ˈmʌs.kjʊ.lə dɪsˈtrɒ.fi / (BrE)
A severe inherited form of muscular dystrophy characterized by progressive muscle weakness and wasting, particularly affecting the face, hands, feet, and neck, with difficulties in speech and movement; typically progresses slowly and can begin at any age from birth to middle age.

Examples

Myotonic muscular dystrophy symptoms
Difficulty in releasing a grip is a classic symptom of myotonic muscular dystrophy.
People diagnosed with myotonic muscular dystrophy often experience muscle weakness in their hands and facial muscles.
She learned about her condition after genetic testing confirmed myotonic muscular dystrophy.

  • synonym: muscular dystrophy - a group of genetic diseases causing progressive weakness and loss of muscle mass
