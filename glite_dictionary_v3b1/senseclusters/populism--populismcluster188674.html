populism

noun
political movement for ordinary people's rights
7.2 7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑː.pjəˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈpɒp.jʊˌlɪz.əm / (BrE)
a political doctrine or movement that aims to represent the interests and rights of ordinary people, often in opposition to the established elite

Examples

Populism movement
Many scholars debate the long-term effects of populism on modern democracies.
Recent elections in Europe have shown a rise in populism across several countries.
The candidate's campaign was built on populism and promises to empower everyday workers.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: demagoguery - political activity that seeks support by appealing to popular desires and false claims
  • antonym: elitism - the belief that society should be led by an elite
