tryptophane

noun
essential amino acid in proteins
Pronunciation: / trɪpˈtoʊfeɪn / (AmE), / trɪpˈtəʊfeɪn / (BrE)
an essential amino acid found in proteins, important for growth and metabolism, and a precursor of niacin

Examples

The tryptophane amino acid
Tryptophane is required for the production of serotonin in the body.
A deficiency of tryptophane can lead to problems with growth and metabolism.
Many nutritional supplements contain tryptophane to support healthy sleep and mood.
