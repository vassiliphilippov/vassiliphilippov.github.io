hydroxyl

noun
the -OH chemical group
2.8 6.3
Pronunciation: /haɪ.ˈdrɑːk.səl/ /; / /haɪ.ˈdrɑːk.sɪl/ (AmE), /haɪ.ˈdrɒk.səl/ /; / /haɪ.ˈdrɒk.sɪl/ (BrE)
a chemical functional group consisting of one oxygen atom bonded to one hydrogen atom (-OH), found in some acids, bases, and alcohols

Examples

The hydroxyl group
Many alcohols contain the hydroxyl group, which makes them soluble in water.
Replacing a hydrogen atom with a hydroxyl group can change the chemical properties of a molecule.
The hydroxyl radical is highly reactive and plays a significant role in atmospheric chemistry.
