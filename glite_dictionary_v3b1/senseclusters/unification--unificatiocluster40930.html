Synonyms

  • uniting
    3.5 0.30/m
    uniting is the act of joining separate people or things together, while unification refers to the whole formal process.
  • unifying
    3.7 0.48/m
    unifying brings people or groups together in a single group, while unification names the official process that creates that unity.
  • combining
    2.4 0.00/m
    combining puts two or more things together physically, while unification focuses on making them one unified whole over time.
  • amalgamation
    7.5 0.75/m
    amalgamation is a formal joining of entities like companies, while unification is a broader process that can include many kinds of joining.
  • coalescence
    7.8 0.28/m
    coalescence is a gradual coming together of parts into one, while unification often implies an intended, planned joining process.
  • fusion
    4.1 4.05/m
    fusion mixes elements into a single new form, while unification stresses the act of bringing separate things into one unit.
  • consolidation
    5.5 5.25/m
    consolidation joins parts to make a stronger, more coherent whole, while unification simply describes bringing things together into one.
  • union
    2.7 11/m
    union is the state or result of being joined, often social or legal, while unification is the active process that makes that state.
  • merger
    3.9 9.19/m
    merger is the joining of companies or organizations into one, while unification covers many kinds of joining beyond business.
  • aggregation
    6.1 2.35/m
    aggregation collects items or data into a group without blending them, while unification implies forming a single, inseparable whole.
