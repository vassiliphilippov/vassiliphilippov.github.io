sleepwalking

noun
walking or acting while asleep
Pronunciation: / ˈsliːpˌwɑːkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsliːpˌwɔːkɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of walking or performing other complex behaviors while asleep, typically with no memory of the event upon waking.

Examples

Sleepwalking at night
Cases of sleepwalking are more common in children than in adults.
He was embarrassed to learn that his sleepwalking had led him to raid the kitchen every night.
Her parents were worried about her sleepwalking after she wandered out of the house one night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: somnambulism - the medical term for sleepwalking
