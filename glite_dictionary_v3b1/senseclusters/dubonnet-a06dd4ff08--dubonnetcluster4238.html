Dubonnet

noun
sweet French aperitif wine (red or white)
Pronunciation: / duːˈbɒn.eɪ / (AmE), / djuːˈbɒn.eɪ / (BrE)
A sweet, aromatic French fortified wine, usually red or white, served chiefly as an aperitif.

Examples

A glass of Dubonnet
Before dinner, she served a chilled glass of Dubonnet as an aperitif.
He enjoys sipping Dubonnet over ice with a slice of lemon on warm evenings.
The cocktail recipe called for equal parts gin and Dubonnet.
