clam

verb
to close tightly
8.6
Pronunciation: /klæm/ (AmE), /klæm/ (BrE)
to close tightly or suddenly, especially in a way that prevents opening or speaking

Examples

To clam up
When the teacher asked about the missing homework, he clammed up and refused to answer.
She clammed her mouth shut as soon as the secret was mentioned.
The box clammed shut so tightly that I could not open it.

Related Terms

  • antonym: open - to move something so that it is no longer closed
  • synonym: shut - to close something so that it is no longer open
