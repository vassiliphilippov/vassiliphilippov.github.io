blind gut

noun
beginning pouch of large intestine
8.9 6.2
Pronunciation: / blaɪnd ɡʌt / (AmE), / blaɪnd ɡʌt / (BrE)
the pouch-like cavity at the beginning of the large intestine, into which the ileum opens; also called the cecum

Examples

The blind gut pouch
Food from the small intestine passes into the blind gut before entering the colon.
In some animals, the blind gut is larger and plays an important role in digestion.
The surgeon examined the blind gut for signs of inflammation near the appendix.
