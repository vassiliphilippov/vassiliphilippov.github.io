Plagianthus regius

noun
New Zealand cotton tree; deciduous, fibrous bark
Pronunciation: / pleɪˈdʒiːænθəs ˈriːdʒiəs / (AmE), / plaˈɡianθəs ˈriːdʒiəs / (BrE)
A deciduous tree native to New Zealand whose inner bark yields a strong fibre known as New Zealand cotton.

Examples

The Plagianthus regius tree
Many New Zealand gardens feature the striking Plagianthus regius for its showy white flowers.
Māori traditionally used Plagianthus regius to weave strong fabrics from its bark.
The inner bark of the Plagianthus regius is harvested to produce a fibre similar to flax.
