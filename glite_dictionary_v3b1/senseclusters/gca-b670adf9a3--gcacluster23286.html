GCA

noun
aircraft landing guided by ground control radar
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒiː siː ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌdʒiː siː ˈeɪ / (BrE)
a method of aircraft landing in bad weather in which the pilot is guided by ground control using precision approach radar

Examples

GCA landing method
Pilots are trained to rely on GCA in bad weather conditions.
Thanks to the GCA procedure, the aircraft landed safely despite poor visibility.
The tower instructed the pilot to request a GCA due to the heavy fog.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ground-controlled approach - an aircraft landing method using ground guidance and radar
