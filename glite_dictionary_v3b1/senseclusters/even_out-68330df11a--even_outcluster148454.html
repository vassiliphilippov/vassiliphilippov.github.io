even out

verb
make smooth, level, or equal
3.4 0.92/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàiÀêv…ôn a ät /; / Ààiv…ôn a ät / (AmE), / ÀàiÀêv…ôn a ät / (BrE)
to make something smooth, level, or equal by removing differences or irregularities (can refer to physical surfaces or more abstract things like relationships or amounts)

Examples

To even out differences
After the argument, their relationship began to even out again.
Over time, your income and expenses usually even out.
The painter had to even out the surface before applying a new coat of paint.
After spreading the plaster, the workers evened out the wall's surface.
Please even out the dirt before laying the foundation.
The gardener uses a rake to even out the soil in the flowerbeds.
His income tends to even out over the year despite seasonal fluctuations.
The teacher tried to even out the teams to make the game fair.
We added extra material to even out the surface before painting the wall.
