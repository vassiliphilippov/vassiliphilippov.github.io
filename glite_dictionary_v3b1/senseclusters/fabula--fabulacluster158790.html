fabula

noun
story events sequence
Pronunciation: / ˈfæbjələ / (AmE), / ˈfæbjʊlə / (BrE)
the chronological sequence of events in a story, as distinct from the order in which they are presented to the reader or audience

Examples

Fabula timeline
In literary theory, the fabula refers to the actual timeline of events in the narrative.
The professor asked students to reconstruct the fabula from the novel's non-linear structure.
Understanding the fabula helps readers distinguish between what happened and how it is told.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plot - the sequence and structure of events as presented in a story
  • antonym: syuzhet - the way a story's events are arranged and presented
