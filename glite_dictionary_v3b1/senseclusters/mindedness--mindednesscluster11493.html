mindedness

noun
state of having a mind
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪndɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈmaɪndɪdnəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of having a mind, consciousness, or mental awareness

Examples

Human mindedness
Philosophers debate whether artificial intelligence can possess true mindedness like humans.
The novel explores the mindedness of animals and their ability to feel emotions.
Some scientists question if self-driving cars could ever achieve genuine mindedness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unconsciousness - the state of not being aware or awake
