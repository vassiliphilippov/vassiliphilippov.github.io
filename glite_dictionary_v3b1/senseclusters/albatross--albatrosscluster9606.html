albatross

noun
large seabird with long wings
Pronunciation: / ˈæl.bəˌtrɔs / (AmE), / ˈæl.bə.trɒs / (BrE)
a large seabird of the southern oceans, with long narrow wings and known for its powerful gliding flight

Examples

A soaring albatross
An albatross can glide great distances without flapping its wings.
Several albatrosses were spotted by the research vessel near Antarctica.
The albatross soared effortlessly above the rolling waves for hours.
