braccae

noun
ancient trousers
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbrækiː / (AmE), / ˈbrækiː / (BrE)
loose-fitting trousers worn in ancient times by people in parts of Europe, especially by Celts and some Roman soldiers

Examples

Ancient braccae trousers
Archaeologists discovered a pair of braccae in an ancient burial site in Germany.
Roman soldiers sometimes wore braccae in colder regions to protect themselves from the weather.
The museum displayed replicas of braccae alongside other ancient clothing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: trousers - an outer garment covering the body from the waist to the ankles, with a separate part for each leg
