lower respiratory infection

noun
infection of the lower respiratory tract
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊ.ɚ rɪˌspɪr.ə.tɔri ɪnˈfɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.ə rɪˌspɪr.ə.təri ɪnˈfɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
an infection affecting the lower part of the respiratory tract, such as the bronchi or lungs

Examples

A lower respiratory infection
Antibiotics are sometimes prescribed for a lower respiratory infection caused by bacteria.
Infants and elderly people are more vulnerable to severe forms of lower respiratory infection.
The doctor diagnosed her with a lower respiratory infection after she developed a persistent cough and fever.
