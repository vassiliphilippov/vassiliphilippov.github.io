stable

adjective
not readily undergoing chemical change
Pronunciation: / ˈsteɪ.bəl / (AmE), / ˈsteɪ.bəl / (BrE)
(of a substance or compound) not likely to undergo or participate in chemical change.

Examples

Stable compound
A stable molecule is less likely to participate in chemical reactions.
Helium is a stable gas that does not react easily with other elements.
The compound remains stable at high temperatures and pressures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inert - chemically inactive or unreactive
  • antonym: reactive - readily undergoing chemical change
