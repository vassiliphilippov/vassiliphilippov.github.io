strangler

noun
software migration pattern
8.3
Pronunciation: /ˈstræŋ.glər/ (AmE), /ˈstræŋ.glə/ (BrE)
(in software architecture) an approach to migrating or replacing a legacy system gradually by adding new components and routing new functionality to them until the old system can be retired.

Examples

Strangler pattern migration
The team used the strangler pattern to replace the old billing system without downtime.
By routing new requests to microservices, they turned the monolith into a strangler over months.
Our architecture document recommends a strangler approach for gradual migration and risk reduction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: strangler fig pattern - a software-architecture pattern for incrementally replacing a legacy system, named after the strangler fig plant
