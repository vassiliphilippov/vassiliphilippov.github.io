preflight

noun
document check before printing
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːˌflaɪt / (AmE), / ˈpriːˌflaɪt / (BrE)
a check of digital files or documents for errors, fonts, colors, and other issues before printing or publication

Examples

Preflight of files
The graphic designer performed a preflight to ensure all images were high resolution before sending the file to print.
Running a preflight can help catch missing fonts or color issues in your document.
Before publication, the editor always insists on a thorough preflight of every file.

Related Terms

  • antonym: postflight - a check performed after a process or operation is completed
  • synonym: proofreading - checking written material for errors before final publication
