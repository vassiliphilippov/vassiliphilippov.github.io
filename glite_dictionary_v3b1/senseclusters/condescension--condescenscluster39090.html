condescension

noun
patronizing attitude or behavior

an attitude or behavior that shows you think you are more important or intelligent than other people, often by being patronizing or talking down to them

Examples

Condescension towards others
Many people found the politician's condescension towards the audience insulting.
She responded to his condescension with a calm but firm correction.
The manager's condescension was evident in the way he explained the simplest details to his team.
Carol resented the condescension in his voice whenever he offered to help.
His condescension was evident in the way he explained even simple ideas as if to children.
She addressed the staff with barely concealed condescension, making everyone feel uncomfortable.
Despite his best efforts, his condescension was obvious to everyone in the meeting.
Her condescension made the staff feel uncomfortable rather than grateful.
There was a hint of condescension in his tone when he addressed the interns.

Synonyms

  • condescendingness
    8.9 0.00/m
    condescendingness is the quality of acting politely superior, while condescension names the same patronizing attitude more commonly.
  • patronization
    8.2 0.07/m
    patronization is the act of treating someone as less intelligent or important, while condescension labels that patronizing behavior.
  • superciliousness
    9.8 0.03/m
    superciliousness is a proud, haughty attitude showing superiority, while condescension often adds a direct tone of talking down.

Related Terms

  • antonym: humility - the quality of not thinking you are better than others
  • synonym: patronize - to treat with apparent kindness that betrays a feeling of superiority
  • synonym: patronizing - showing an apparent kindness that betrays a feeling of superiority
  • antonym: respect - a feeling of deep admiration for someone or something
