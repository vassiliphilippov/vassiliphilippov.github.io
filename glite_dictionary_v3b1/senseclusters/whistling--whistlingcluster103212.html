whistling

noun
high-pitched sound of air or movement
4.8 1.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪslɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪslɪŋ / (BrE)
a high-pitched sound produced by something moving rapidly or by air or steam escaping through a narrow opening

Examples

Kettle whistling
Suddenly, a sharp whistling came from the kettle as the water began to boil.
The whistling of the wind kept her awake all night.
We could hear the whistling of the train as it sped past the station.
