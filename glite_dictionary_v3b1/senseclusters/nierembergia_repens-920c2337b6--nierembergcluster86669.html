Nierembergia repens

noun
South American ground-cover herb with white-blue flowers
9.9
Pronunciation: / niˌɛrəmˈbɜrɡiə rɪˈpɛnz / (AmE), / niˌɛrəmˈbɜːɡiə rɪˈpɛnz / (BrE)
A prostrate woody herb native to South America, with white tubular flowers often tinged with blue or rose; grown as a ground cover.

Examples

Nierembergia repens ground cover
Nierembergia repens is popular in gardens as an ornamental ground cover due to its attractive flowers.
Botanists recommend Nierembergia repens for areas needing erosion control.
The white blooms of Nierembergia repens often have a delicate blue or rose tint.
