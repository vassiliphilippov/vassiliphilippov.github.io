Bonapartism

noun
authoritarian political system
Pronunciation: / ˌboʊnəˈpɑːrtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌbəʊnəˈpɑːtɪzəm / (BrE)
a political movement or system that supports strong authoritarian leadership, especially modeled after Napoleon Bonaparte's rule

Examples

Bonapartism political movement
The rise of Bonapartism influenced European politics in the nineteenth century.
Some leaders have been accused of promoting Bonapartism by concentrating power in their own hands.
Historians debate whether Bonapartism can exist outside the context of French history.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authoritarianism - a political system favoring strict obedience to authority
  • antonym: democracy - a system of government by the whole population, typically through elected representatives
  • synonym: dictatorship - a form of government with absolute power held by one person
