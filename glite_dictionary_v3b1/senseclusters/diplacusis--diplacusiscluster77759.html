diplacusis

noun
hearing two pitches
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪpləˈkjuːsɪs / (AmE), / ˌdɪpləˈkjuːsɪs / (BrE)
a hearing disorder in which a single sound is heard as two different pitches in the same ear

Examples

Diplacusis in one ear
After the infection, she noticed diplacusis and described hearing two notes instead of one.
Musicians may struggle with diplacusis because it affects their ability to recognize correct pitch.
The doctor explained that diplacusis can make everyday sounds seem distorted or doubled.

Related Terms

  • synonym: binaural diplacusis - hearing a single sound as different pitches in each ear
  • antonym: normal hearing - the ability to hear sounds as a single pitch
