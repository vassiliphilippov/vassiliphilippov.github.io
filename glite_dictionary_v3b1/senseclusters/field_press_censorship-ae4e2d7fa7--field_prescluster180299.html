field press censorship

noun
military review of news for security reasons
4.0 8.6
Pronunciation: /fiːld pres ˈsen.sər.ʃɪp/ /; / /fɪld pres ˈsen.sər.ʃɪp/ (AmE), /fiːld pres ˈsen.sə.ʃɪp/ /; / /fɪld pres ˈsen.sə.ʃɪp/ (BrE)
the review and control of news reports and other information intended for public release, conducted by military authorities in an active operations area to maintain security

Examples

Field press censorship during war
During wartime, field press censorship ensured sensitive military information was not published by journalists.
Reporters working in combat zones must submit their articles to field press censorship before publication.
The army imposed strict field press censorship to prevent leaks of operational details.
