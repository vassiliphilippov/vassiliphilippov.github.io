overbanked

adjective
too many banks
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈbæŋkt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈbæŋkt / (BrE)
having more banks or banking services in an area than are necessary or economically justified

Examples

Overbanked city
The downtown area is overbanked, with several branches on every street corner.
Analysts warn that an overbanked market can lead to increased competition and lower profits.
Some small towns are underbanked, while major cities are often overbanked.

Related Terms

  • synonym: saturated - filled to capacity, especially with businesses or services
  • antonym: underbanked - having too few banks or banking services available
