irreality

noun
unreal or imaginary condition
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˌɪriˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌɪriːˈælɪti / (BrE)
The state or quality of being unreal, insubstantial, or imaginary; lack of objective existence or reality.

Examples

A feeling of irreality
He described his experience of déjà vu as a brief feeling of irreality.
Many surrealist paintings evoke a sense of irreality, blurring the line between dream and wakefulness.
The novel's plot moves gradually from reality to irreality, challenging the reader's perceptions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist
  • synonym: unreality - the state of being unreal; lack of reality
