republish

verb
make a cancelled legal document effective again
3.3 8.0
Pronunciation: / riˈpʌblɪʃ / (AmE), / riˈpʌblɪʃ / (BrE)
(law) To revive or make effective again a document, such as a will or libel, that was previously cancelled or invalidated.

Examples

To republish a will
His attorney filed a motion to republish the libel after the original was set aside.
If a document is mistakenly revoked, a judge can republish it under certain conditions.
The court may republish a cancelled will if new evidence is presented.
