z-fold

noun
zigzag folding pattern; accordion fold
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈziː ˌfoʊld / (AmE), / ˈziː ˌfəʊld / (BrE)
a continuous series of parallel folds with alternating directions, forming a zigzag or accordion pattern, typically used in paper, packaging, or geological formations

Examples

A z-fold brochure
The brochure was printed using a z-fold so it would open up in sections.
To save space, the map features a z-fold that makes it easy to carry.
We used a z-fold to create the invitation, giving it an elegant, layered look.

Related Terms
