rationalist

noun
person valuing reason over metaphysics
Pronunciation: / ˈræʃənəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈræʃənəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who emphasizes reason and observable facts, and tends to reject metaphysical speculation or supernatural explanations

Examples

A rationalist thinker
As a proud rationalist, she refuses to accept claims without objective evidence.
Many scientists consider themselves rationalists who seek explanations based solely on observable data.
The debate between the rationalist and his religious counterpart was spirited and thought-provoking.

Related Terms

  • antonym: empiricist - one who relies on experience or observation alone; often contrasted with rationalists
