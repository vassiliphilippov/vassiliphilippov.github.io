crack

noun
narrow opening or gap
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: / kræk / (AmE), / kræk / (BrE)
a long, narrow opening or split in a surface, usually caused by damage or pressure

Examples

A crack in the wall
A faint light shone through the crack in the door.
Over time, water seeped into the crack in the wall.
She peered out from behind a crack in the curtains.
A crack appeared in the wall after the earthquake.
He filled the crack in the pavement with cement.
There was a narrow crack running across the windshield.
A thin crack ran across the windshield, spreading with every bump.
Light shone through a crack in the old wooden door.
The plate had a crack in it, but it was still usable.
