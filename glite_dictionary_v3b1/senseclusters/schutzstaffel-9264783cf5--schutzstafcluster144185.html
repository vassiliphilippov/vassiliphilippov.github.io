Schutzstaffel

noun
Nazi paramilitary organization (SS)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈʃʊtsˌʃtɑːfəl / (AmE), / ˈʃʊtsˌʃtaːfəl / (BrE)
the paramilitary organization under the Nazi regime, originally founded as Hitler's personal bodyguard, which later ran concentration camps and enforced Nazi policies

Examples

The Schutzstaffel organization
Many war criminals from the Schutzstaffel were tried at the Nuremberg Trials after World War II.
The Schutzstaffel played a central role in the enforcement of Nazi policies across Europe.
The Schutzstaffel was responsible for operating many concentration camps during the Holocaust.
