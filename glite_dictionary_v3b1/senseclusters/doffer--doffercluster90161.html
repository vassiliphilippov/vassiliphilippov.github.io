doffer

noun
textile mill worker
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈdɑː.fər/ (AmE), /ˈdɒ.fə/ (BrE)
a worker in a textile mill who removes full bobbins or spindles from spinning machines and replaces them with empty ones

Examples

A doffer in a mill
The young doffer worked quickly to keep the spinning machines running smoothly.
In the early 1900s, many children were employed as doffers in textile factories.
After the shift, the doffer cleaned up the area around the spinning frames.
