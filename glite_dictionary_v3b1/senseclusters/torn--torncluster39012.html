torn

adjective
emotionally conflicted; pulled by opposing feelings
4.2 1.47/m
Pronunciation: / t…îrn / (AmE), / t…îÀên / (BrE)
Experiencing strong emotional conflict due to opposing feelings, desires, or loyalties.

Examples

Torn between choices
He was torn over whether to accept the new job offer or stay with his current employer.
Many voters were torn by the conflicting promises made during the campaign.
She felt torn between loyalty to her family and her dreams of independence.

Synonyms

  • conflicted
    4.4 1.95/m
    conflicted means feeling strong opposing emotions inside, while torn describes being painfully pulled between loyalties or desires.
  • ambivalent
    7.1 4.01/m
    ambivalent has mixed or unclear feelings about something, while torn implies a stronger, more painful choice between loyalties.
  • of two minds
    6.7 0.24/m
    of two minds describes unsure or undecided thinking about two options, while torn suggests a deep emotional pull between them.
  • divided over
    5.4 0.31/m
    divided over shows disagreement or different opinions among people, while torn focuses on one person's strong inner conflict.
