drifty

adjective
prone to drifting
8.8
Pronunciation: /ˈdrɪf.tiː/ /; / /ˈdrɪf.tɪ/ (AmE), /ˈdrɪf.tiː/ /; / /ˈdrɪf.tɪ/ (BrE)
liable to become covered with wind-blown drifts of snow or sand; subject to accumulation by drifting material

Examples

Drifty mountain road
The mountain road became dangerously drifty after hours of heavy snowfall.
Farmers avoided the drifty fields where sand piled up after every strong wind.
After the blizzard, the parking lot was so drifty that cars were buried under snow.
