alkanol

noun
alcohol from alkane
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈælkəˌnɒl / (AmE), / ˈælkənɒl / (BrE)
an alcohol formed by replacing one hydrogen atom in an alkane with a hydroxyl group

Examples

A simple alkanol
Methanol is the simplest alkanol and is widely used as a solvent.
Ethanol, a common alkanol, is found in alcoholic beverages.
The laboratory synthesized a new alkanol for use in chemical research.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alcohol - an organic compound with a hydroxyl group attached to a carbon atom
  • antonym: alkane - a saturated hydrocarbon with only single bonds
