back to square one

x
returning to the starting point after a setback
Pronunciation: / bæk tə skwɛr wʌn / (AmE), / bæk tə skwɛː wʌn / (BrE)
used to indicate that one has returned to the starting point after a failure or setback, and must begin again from the beginning

Examples

Be back to square one
After months of negotiations failed, the team was back to square one with the contract.
The new evidence proved useless, so detectives found themselves back to square one.
When the experiment didn’t work out, the scientists were back to square one.

Related Terms

  • synonym: make a fresh start - to start something anew, especially after a failure
  • synonym: start over - to begin again from the beginning
