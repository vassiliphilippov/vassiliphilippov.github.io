deglycerolize

verb
remove glycerol from something
9.9 9.5
Pronunciation: /diː.ˈglɪ.sə.rə.laɪz/ /; / /dɪ.ˈglɪ.sə.rə.laɪz/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈglɪ.sə.rə.laɪz/ /; / /də.ˈglɪ.sə.rə.laɪz/ (BrE)
to remove glycerol from a substance, especially as part of a laboratory or medical process

Examples

To deglycerolize blood
Before transfusion, blood is often deglycerolized to remove the preservative agent.
Laboratory equipment is used to deglycerolize stored red blood cells for safe clinical use.
The team deglycerolized the samples as part of the preparation procedure.
A special filter is used to deglycerolise stored blood units in the laboratory.
After thawing, it is necessary to deglycerolise the red blood cells before transfusion.
The samples were carefully deglycerolised to prepare them for further analysis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glycerol - a simple polyol compound often used as a preserving agent
