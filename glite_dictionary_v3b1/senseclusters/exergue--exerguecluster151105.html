exergue

noun
inscription area on coin
Pronunciation: / ˈɛɡˌzɜrɡ / (AmE), / ˈɛɡzɜːɡ / (BrE)
a small space on a coin, medal, or monument, usually at the bottom, where an inscription, motto, or date is placed

Examples

Coin exergue
The date was engraved in the exergue at the bottom of the commemorative coin.
Collectors often examine the exergue for special marks or inscriptions.
The artist included a brief motto in the exergue of the medal to honor the event.
The year of minting was clearly visible in the exergue of the silver coin.
Collectors often examine the exergue for important details about a medal's origin.
The artist's initials were engraved in the exergue beneath the main image.
