turbulence

noun
atmospheric instability or disturbance
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈtɜrbjələns / (AmE), / ˈtɜːbjʊləns / (BrE)
a state of instability or irregular motion in the atmosphere, often causing sudden changes in air movement and discomfort during flight

Examples

Airplane turbulence
Clear air turbulence can occur even when the sky looks calm and cloudless.
Passengers were advised to fasten their seatbelts because of unexpected turbulence.
The plane encountered severe turbulence as it flew through the storm clouds.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - absence of disturbance or movement in the atmosphere
