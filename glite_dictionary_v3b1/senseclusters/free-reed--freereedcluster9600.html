free-reed

noun
type of reed in musical instruments
9.1 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàfriÀê Àårid / (AmE), / ÀàfriÀê ÀåriÀêd / (BrE)
A reed in a musical instrument that vibrates freely through a slot without sealing it, as in harmonicas or accordions.

Examples

A free-reed instrument
An accordion creates sound when air flows over its free-reed mechanisms.
Most Asian mouth organs, such as the sheng, also use free-reed technology to produce musical tones.
The harmonica is a free-reed instrument widely used in blues, folk, and classical music.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reed - a thin strip of material that vibrates to produce a sound in musical instruments
