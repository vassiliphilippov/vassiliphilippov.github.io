eviscerate

verb
deprive of essential part
Pronunciation: / ɪˈvɪsəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈvɪsəreɪt / (BrE)
to deprive something of its essential content or effectiveness; to remove a vital or crucial part of

Examples

To eviscerate a company
Mass layoffs can eviscerate a company's ability to compete in the market.
The amendments effectively eviscerated the proposed law, stripping it of real impact.
Their drastic budget cuts would eviscerate essential community services.

  • antonym: strengthen - make or become stronger or more effective
  • synonym: weaken - make or become weaker or less effective
