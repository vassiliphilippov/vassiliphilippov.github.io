pick up on

verb
notice or understand (often subtle)
Pronunciation: / pɪk ʌp ɑn / (AmE), / pɪk ʌp ɒn / (BrE)
to notice, detect, or understand something that is not immediately obvious

Examples

To pick up on a hint
It took me a while to pick up on the new rules at work.
Reporters often pick up on inconsistencies in politicians' statements.
She didn't pick up on the sarcasm in his voice and thought he was being sincere.

Related Terms

  • antonym: miss - to fail to notice or understand
  • synonym: notice - to become aware of; to observe
