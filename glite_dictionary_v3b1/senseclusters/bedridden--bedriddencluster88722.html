Confined to bed because of illness or infirmity.

Examples

Bedridden with illness
He became bedridden as his illness progressed.
She was bedridden for months after her accident.
The bedridden patient required constant care from the nurses.

Synonyms

  • bedfast
    9.9 0.01/m
    bedfast describes someone kept in bed by illness in formal contexts, while bedridden is the common medical or everyday term.
  • bedrid
    9.9 0.00/m
    bedrid often suggests a long or permanent stay in bed due to illness, while bedridden simply means confined to bed.
  • sick-abed
    9.9 0.00/m
    sick-abed is an old-fashioned phrase for being in bed with illness, while bedridden is the neutral modern medical term.
  • laid up
    5.6 0.36/m
    laid up means temporarily unable to work or active because of illness or injury, while bedridden means confined to bed.
