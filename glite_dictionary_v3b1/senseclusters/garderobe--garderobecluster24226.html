garderobe

noun
medieval toilet or closet
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑrdəˌroʊb / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːdəˌrəʊb / (BrE)
a small room in a medieval building used as a toilet or for storing clothes

Examples

Castle garderobe
The castle's garderobe was built into the outer wall for ventilation.
Visitors were surprised to see a restored garderobe in the ancient tower.
In medieval times, a garderobe often served both as a toilet and a place to hang clothes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: latrine - a simple toilet, especially in a camp or military context
  • synonym: wardrobe - a large piece of furniture for storing clothes
