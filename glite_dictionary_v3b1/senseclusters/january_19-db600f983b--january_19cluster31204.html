January 19

noun
Robert E. Lee's birthday holiday (U.S. South)
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒæn.juˈɛri ˈnaɪnˌtiːn / (AmE), / ˌdʒan.juˈɛː.ri ˈnaɪn.tiːn / (BrE)
In some southern U.S. states, a holiday marking the birth date of Confederate General Robert E. Lee, observed annually on January 19.

Examples

January 19 holiday
Many southern states used to recognize January 19 as a holiday in honor of Robert E. Lee.
Teachers in some regions used January 19 to discuss the American Civil War with their students.
The local courthouse was closed on January 19 for the Lee birthday observance.
