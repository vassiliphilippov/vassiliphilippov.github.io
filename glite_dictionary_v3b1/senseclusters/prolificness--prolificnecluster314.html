prolificness

noun
ability to produce abundantly
Pronunciation: / prəˈlɪfɪknəs / (AmE), / prəˈlɪfɪknəs / (BrE)
the quality of producing many works, results, or offspring

Examples

The prolificness of artist
The prolificness of the artist's career amazed critics and fans alike.
Her garden's prolificness resulted in baskets of fresh vegetables every week.
Scientists admired the prolificness of the research team in publishing new findings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: barrenness - the state of being unable to produce
  • synonym: fertility - the ability to produce offspring or new growth
  • synonym: productivity - the state of being able to produce a lot
