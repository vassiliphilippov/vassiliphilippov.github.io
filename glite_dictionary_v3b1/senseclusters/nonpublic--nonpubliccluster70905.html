nonpublic

adjective
not prominent or publicly recognized
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈpʌblɪk / (AmE), / nɒnˈpʌblɪk / (BrE)
not having a prominent or widely recognized position or status; not publicly known or notable.

Examples

A nonpublic role
He preferred to keep a nonpublic role in the company, working behind the scenes.
Her contributions remained nonpublic, but they were highly valued within the organization.
Most of the research was conducted by nonpublic figures who rarely appeared in the media.

Related Terms

  • synonym: obscure - not clearly known, not important or famous
  • antonym: prominent - important; well-known; standing out so as to be easily seen
