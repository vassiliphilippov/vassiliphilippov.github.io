line squall

noun
squall forming a line along a weather front
9.9
Pronunciation: / laɪn skwɑl / (AmE), / laɪn skwɔːl / (BrE)
A squall that moves along a front and forms a distinct line, often associated with sudden, severe weather changes.

Examples

A line squall front
A powerful line squall moved through the region, causing heavy rain and strong winds.
Pilots are trained to avoid flying through a line squall due to turbulent conditions.
The weather service issued a warning about an approaching line squall along the cold front.
