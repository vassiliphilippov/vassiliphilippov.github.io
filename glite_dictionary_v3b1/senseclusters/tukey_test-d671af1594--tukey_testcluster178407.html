Tukey test

noun
statistical comparison method
7.4 0.06/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàtuÀêki t…õst / (AmE), / ÀàtjuÀêki t…õst / (BrE)
a statistical method used after an analysis of variance (ANOVA) to compare group means or check for interactions between groups

Examples

The Tukey test method
After running ANOVA, the scientist applied the Tukey test to determine which groups differed significantly.
The Tukey test is commonly used in research to compare the means of several treatments.
Using the Tukey test, the analyst found that only two groups had statistically significant differences.
The researcher used the Tukey test to check for interaction effects in her experiment.
Before interpreting the results, they performed a Tukey test to ensure the model was appropriate.
A Tukey test can reveal whether the effects in a two-way ANOVA are additive or not.
