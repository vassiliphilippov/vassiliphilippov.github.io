ferredoxin

noun
electron transfer protein
Pronunciation: / ˌfɛrəˈdɑksɪn / (AmE), / ˌfɛrəˈdɒksɪn / (BrE)
a protein that transfers electrons in biological processes, especially in photosynthesis in plants

Examples

Ferredoxin protein transfer
In plant cells, ferredoxin plays a key role in the process of photosynthesis.
Scientists study ferredoxin to understand how plants convert sunlight into energy.
The transfer of electrons by ferredoxin is essential for many metabolic reactions.
