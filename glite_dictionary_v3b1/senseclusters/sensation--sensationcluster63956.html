sensation

noun
remarkably skilled or impactful person
Pronunciation: / sɛnˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / sɛnˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
a person or thing that causes great excitement or attracts a lot of public attention

Examples

A sports sensation
He is a basketball sensation known for his extraordinary skills on the court.
She was hailed as a fashion sensation at her first runway show.
The young pianist became a sensation overnight after her stunning performance.
Her performance created a sensation in the art world.
The band became an overnight sensation after their song went viral.
The scandal was a sensation that filled the front pages for weeks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: commotion - a state of confused and noisy disturbance
  • synonym: prodigy - a young person with exceptional abilities
