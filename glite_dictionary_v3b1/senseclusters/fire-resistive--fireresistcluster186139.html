fire-resistive

adjective
difficult to burn; resists fire
3.1 4.6
Pronunciation: / ˌfaɪər rɪˈzɪstɪv / (AmE), / ˌfaɪə rɪˈzɪstɪv / (BrE)
(of a material or construction) difficult to burn; able to withstand exposure to fire for a specified period without sustaining significant damage.

Examples

Fire-resistive material
Insulation with fire-resistive properties is required in high-risk areas.
The hotel advertised its fire-resistive design to reassure guests about safety.
The new building was constructed with fire-resistive materials to meet safety codes.
