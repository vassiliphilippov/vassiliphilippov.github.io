Synonyms

  • make up
    2.7 11/m
    make up means joining parts together to form a whole, while compose names what those parts form.
  • constitute
    4.4 7/m
    constitute refers to parts that make or build something, while compose focuses on forming the whole from those parts.
  • comprise
    4.4 6/m
    comprise commonly lists the parts that make a whole, while compose names how smaller parts form that whole.
  • form
    2.1 5/m
    form usually means give shape or be part of a thing, while compose means to make up that whole.
  • consist
    2.5 15/m
    consist describes what parts make up something, while compose describes building or creating that whole from the parts.
