sandwich

verb
insert or squeeze tightly between two things

Pronunciation: /ˈsæn.dwɪtʃ/ /; / /ˈsæn.wɪtʃ/ /; / /ˈsæm.wɪtʃ/ (AmE), /ˈsæn.dwɪtʃ/ /; / /ˈsæn.wɪtʃ/ /; / /ˈsæm.wɪtʃ/ (BrE)

Examples

To sandwich people
Commuters often find themselves sandwiched into packed subway cars during rush hour.
He sandwiched the confidential file between two old magazines for secrecy.
Sarah was sandwiched between her two brothers on the crowded bench.
