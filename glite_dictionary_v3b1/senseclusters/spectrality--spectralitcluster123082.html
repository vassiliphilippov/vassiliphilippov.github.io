spectrality

noun
quality of being ghostly
Pronunciation: / spɛkˈtræləti / (AmE), / spɛkˈtrælɪti / (BrE)
The quality or condition of being spectral or ghostly.

Examples

The spectrality of ghosts
Critics discuss the spectrality in Shakespeare's tragedies, focusing on characters that haunt the narrative.
Her novel is filled with a sense of spectrality, creating an atmosphere both mysterious and unsettling.
The film explored the spectrality of memory, blurring the lines between the living and the dead.
