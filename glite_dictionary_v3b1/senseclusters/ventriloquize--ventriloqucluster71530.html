ventriloquize

verb
to speak for another
9.9
Pronunciation: /ven.trə.ˈlɑː.kwaɪz/ /; / /ven.trɪ.ˈlɑː.kwaɪz/ (AmE), /ven.trɪ.ˈlɒ.kwaɪz/ /; / /ven.trə.ˈlɒ.kwaɪz/ (BrE)
to express or present someone else's words or opinions as if they were your own, often to promote their views

Examples

To ventriloquize opinions
The journalist was accused of ventriloquizing the politician's message instead of offering independent reporting.
In the debate, she seemed to ventriloquize her mentor's arguments rather than present her own ideas.
Critics argued that the film merely ventriloquized popular opinions without adding new perspectives.

Related Terms

  • synonym: echo - to repeat or imitate another's words or ideas
  • antonym: originate - to create or express something original
  • synonym: parrot - to repeat someone else's words without understanding
