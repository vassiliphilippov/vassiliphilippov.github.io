on-site

adjective
at the actual place of activity
1.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɑnˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈɒnˌsaɪt / (BrE)
Located or taking place at the location where a particular activity or operation is carried out.

Examples

On-site repairs
All repairs must be completed on-site to ensure safety and efficiency.
Our conference includes an on-site tour of the new facility.
The company requires an on-site manager to supervise daily operations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: off-site - taking place or located away from the site
