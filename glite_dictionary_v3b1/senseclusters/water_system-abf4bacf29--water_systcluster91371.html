water system

noun
network of a river and its tributaries
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtɚ ˌsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌsɪstəm / (BrE)
a river together with all of its tributaries; a network of interconnected streams and rivers forming a drainage basin.

Examples

River water system
Scientists study the local water system to understand how pollutants travel downstream.
The Amazon water system supports countless species through its extensive network of tributaries.
The Mississippi River water system covers a vast region of North America.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drainage basin - the area of land where precipitation collects and drains off into a common outlet, such as a river
