locative

noun
linguistic role indicating location
9.9 8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊkətɪv /; / ˈlɑːk.ə.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈlɒkətɪv /; / ˈlɒk.ə.tɪv / (BrE)
the grammatical case or role in language that shows the place where something happens or exists

Examples

The locative case
In the sentence 'She lives in London', the prepositional phrase acts as a locative.

The locative often answers the questions 'where?' or 'in what place?' in a sentence.
