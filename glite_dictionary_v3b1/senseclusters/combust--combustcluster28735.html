combust

verb
suddenly become extremely angry
7.6
Pronunciation: / kəmˈbʌst / (AmE), / kəmˈbʌst / (BrE)
to suddenly become extremely angry or erupt emotionally (figurative)

Examples

To combust with anger
If you keep provoking him, he might just combust in front of everyone.
She tried to stay calm, but eventually combusted when the mistakes kept happening.
Some parents combust when they see their child's poor report card.
His anger finally combusted after months of frustration.
Social tensions may combust if grievances are not heard.
The controversy threatened to combust the entire community into protest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: erupt - to suddenly and violently express anger
  • synonym: explode - to burst or cause to burst violently and noisily; also to lose self-control suddenly (get angry)
