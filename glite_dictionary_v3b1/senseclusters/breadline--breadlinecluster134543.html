breadline

noun
poverty threshold
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɛdˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈbrɛdˌlaɪn / (BrE)
the level of income or resources below which a person is considered very poor or unable to afford basic necessities

Examples

Below the breadline
After losing his job, he found himself living just above the breadline for several months.
Government assistance programs aim to help families who are below the breadline.
Many people are struggling to stay off the breadline as living expenses increase.

Related Terms

  • antonym: affluence - the state of having a lot of money and resources
  • degree higher: destitution - the state of having no money or resources
  • synonym: poverty line - the official minimum income needed to meet basic needs
