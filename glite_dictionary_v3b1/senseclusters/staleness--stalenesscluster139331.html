staleness

noun
unoriginal or hackneyed quality
Pronunciation: / ˈsteɪlnəs / (AmE), / ˈsteɪlnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being unoriginal, dull, or hackneyed, especially as a result of overuse or repetition

Examples

Staleness of ideas
Many critics commented on the staleness of the new sitcom, describing its jokes as tired and predictable.
The advertisement campaign failed due to the staleness of its recycled ideas.
There was a certain staleness in his writing that made the novel feel uninspired.

Related Terms

  • antonym: freshness - the quality of being new or interesting
  • antonym: originality - the quality of being novel or inventive
