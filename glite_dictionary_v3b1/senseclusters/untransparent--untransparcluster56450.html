untransparent

adjective
not open or honest
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˌʌntrænˈspɛrənt / (AmE), / ˌʌntrænˈspærənt / (BrE)
not open, honest, or clear; deliberately withholding information or acting in a secretive way.

Examples

Untransparent decisions
The company's untransparent policies made it difficult for employees to understand how decisions were made.
Critics accused the government of being untransparent about the true costs of the project.
Her untransparent answers during the interview raised suspicions among the panel.

Related Terms

  • synonym: opaque - not able to be seen through; not transparent or clear
  • synonym: secretive - deliberately keeping information hidden
  • antonym: transparent - open, honest, and easy to understand
