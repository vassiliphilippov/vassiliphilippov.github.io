walk about

verb
walk with no particular goal
Pronunciation: / wɔk əˈbaʊt / (AmE), / wɔːk əˈbaʊt / (BrE)
to walk in various directions or places without a specific purpose or destination

Examples

To walk about freely
After dinner, they liked to walk about the village, enjoying the evening air.
She would often walk about the forest, lost in her thoughts.
Tourists walk about the city center, taking in the sights at their own pace.

Related Terms

  • antonym: march - walk quickly and with determination, often with a specific goal
  • synonym: roam - move about or travel aimlessly or unsystematically
  • synonym: wander - to walk or move in a leisurely, casual, or aimless way
