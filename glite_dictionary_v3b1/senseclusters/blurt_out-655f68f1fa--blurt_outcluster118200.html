blurt out

verb
say something suddenly and impulsively
Pronunciation: / blɝt aʊt / (AmE), / blɜːt aʊt / (BrE)
to say something suddenly and impulsively, especially without thinking carefully first

Examples

To blurt out a secret
She didn't mean to blurt out the surprise, but she was too excited.
When questioned by the police, he blurted out everything he knew.
Without thinking, he suddenly blurted out the answer during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: spill the beans - to reveal secret information unintentionally or suddenly
