zealot

noun
fanatic or fervent supporter

A person who is extremely passionate and uncompromising in pursuit of their religious, political, or other ideals, often to the point of fanaticism.

Examples

A zealot for beliefs
He was labeled a zealot by his colleagues because of his uncompromising opinions.
She became a zealot for environmental protection, dedicating her life to the cause.
Social media can amplify the voices of zealots from all sides of the debate.

Synonyms

  • extremist
    6.9 10/m
    an extremist holds very strong political or religious views and may push for radical action, while a zealot follows belief without compromise.
  • votary
    9.9 0.08/m
    a votary is deeply devoted to a cause in a religious or formal way, while a zealot is more fanatical and uncompromising.
  • diehard
    6.8 1.18/m
    a diehard stays loyal and resists change in beliefs or teams, while a zealot acts from extreme passion and intolerance.
  • maniac
    6.8 1.71/m
    a maniac shows wild or excessive enthusiasm for something, while a zealot focuses that passion on strict beliefs or causes.
