catch a glimpse

verb
see briefly; spot momentarily
1.7 2.9
Pronunciation: /kætʃ ə ɡlɪmps/ (AmE), /kætʃ ə ɡlɪmps/ (BrE)
to see something or someone for a very short time, often only partially

Examples

To catch a glimpse quickly
As the train sped by, he caught a glimpse of the countryside.

As the train sped by, I caught a glimpse of her waving from the platform.
He caught a glimpse of the secret document before it was whisked away.
Through the crowd, she caught a glimpse of the famous actor signing autographs.
