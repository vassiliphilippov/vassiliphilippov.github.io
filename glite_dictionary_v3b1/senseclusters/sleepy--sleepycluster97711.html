sleepy

adjective
drowsy; ready to sleep
1.0 8.04/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàsliÀêpi / (AmE), / ÀàsliÀêpi / (BrE)
feeling ready to fall asleep; drowsy or inclined to sleep.

Examples

A sleepy person
After a long day at work, I always feel sleepy around 9 p.m.
She was too sleepy to finish her book and decided to go to bed early.
The sleepy child yawned and rubbed his eyes during the car ride home.

Synonyms

  • drowsy
    2.7 1.74/m
    drowsy is a slow, heavy feeling from tiredness or medicine, while sleepy is a general readiness to fall asleep.
  • dozy
    4.3 0.03/m
    dozy feels half awake and likely to doze off in a chair, while sleepy simply means ready to sleep.
  • snoozy
    4.6 0.03/m
    snoozy sounds playful and can mean dull or causing sleepiness in listeners, while sleepy describes needing actual sleep.
  • somnolent
    8.4 0.25/m
    somnolent is a formal or medical word for causing or feeling drowsy, while sleepy is everyday and informal.

Related Terms

  • antonym: alert - fully awake and attentive
  • synonym: drowsy - feeling sleepy or lethargic
