ruff

verb
to make ruffles
Pronunciation: / rʌf / (AmE), / rʌf / (BrE)
to gather or pleat fabric into ruffles or a frill along an edge for decoration

Examples

To ruff the hem
The seamstress ruffed the hem to create a decorative frill around the skirt.
She carefully ruffs the sleeves to add a touch of elegance to the dress.
After ruffing the fabric, the costume looked much more elaborate and festive.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gather - to draw fabric together in folds
  • synonym: pleat - to fold fabric into even, narrow folds
  • antonym: smooth - to make fabric flat and even
