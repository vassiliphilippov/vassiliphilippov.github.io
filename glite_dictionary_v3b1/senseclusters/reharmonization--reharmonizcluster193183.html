reharmonization

noun
alteration of a piece's original harmony
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌhɑːrmənaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / riˌhɑrməˌnaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌhɑːmənʌɪˈzeɪʃən /; / riːˌhɑːmənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or result of revising or altering the harmony of an existing piece of music, often to create a new harmonic effect

Examples

Creative reharmonization
Her creative reharmonization made the old folk tune sound completely new to the audience.
Many arrangers use reharmonization as a tool to reinterpret familiar songs in unique ways.
The jazz pianist performed a reharmonization of the standard, giving it a fresh harmonic structure.
Many contemporary covers of older songs use reharmonisation to give familiar melodies a fresh sound.
The reharmonisation of that piece maintained its original melody but completely changed its emotional impact.
The jazz pianist performed a brilliant reharmonisation of the classic tune, surprising the audience with unexpected chords.
