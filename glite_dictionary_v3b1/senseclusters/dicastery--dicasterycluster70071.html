dicastery

noun
ancient court
Pronunciation: / daɪˈkæstəri / (AmE), / dɪˈkæstəri / (BrE)
a court or tribunal, especially one in ancient Greece or in classical contexts, responsible for administering justice

Examples

Ancient dicastery trial
The ancient city held trials in a dicastery attended by hundreds of citizens.
Jurors in the dicastery listened carefully before delivering their verdict.
Historical records describe the dicastery as central to the Greek legal system.

Related Terms

  • synonym: court - a place where legal cases are heard and decided
  • synonym: tribunal - a court of justice or a body established to settle disputes
