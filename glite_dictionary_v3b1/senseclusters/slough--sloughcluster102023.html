slough

noun
a stagnant swamp or marsh
6.5 7.1
Pronunciation: /slʌf/ (AmE), /slʌf/ (BrE)
a swampy, muddy, or slow-moving area of water, often found in low-lying regions

Examples

A slough of water
Birds flocked to the slough each spring, drawn by the abundance of water and shelter.
Heavy rains turned the field into a muddy slough that was difficult to cross.
The alligators watched from the slough as fishermen passed in their boats.
In spring, the slough floods, creating a temporary wetland for migrating birds.
The hikers struggled to cross the slough after heavy rains turned the ground to muck.
Wildlife is abundant near the slough, where the water pools in the muddy depression.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bog - wet, spongy ground with decaying vegetation
  • synonym: fen - a low and marshy or frequently flooded area of land
  • synonym: quagmire - a soft boggy area of land that gives way underfoot
  • synonym: swamp - an area of low-lying, uncultivated ground where water collects
