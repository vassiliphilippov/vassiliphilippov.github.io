ulceration

noun
formation of an open sore (medical)
8.8 5.6
Pronunciation: /ʌl.sə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ʌl.sə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a localized area of tissue loss with inflammation, often forming an open sore on the skin or a mucous membrane

Examples

Mouth ulceration
Chronic ulceration of the mouth can make eating difficult.
Early treatment can reduce the risk of skin ulceration in diabetic patients.
The persistent ulceration on his leg required frequent medical attention.
