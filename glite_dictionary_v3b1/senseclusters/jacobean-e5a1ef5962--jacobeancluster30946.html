Jacobean

adjective
relating to the reign of James I of England
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒækəˈbiː.ən / (AmE), / ˌdʒækəˈbiː.ən / (BrE)
Relating to, or typical of the period of the reign of James I of England (1603–1625), or to the literature, architecture, or styles of that time.

Examples

Jacobean architecture style
Her thesis explored the symbolism found in Jacobean literature.
Many Jacobean plays reflect the complexities of political life during James I's rule.
The Jacobean architecture in this church is famous for its elaborate woodwork.
