frequency-response characteristic

noun
graph showing amplitude vs frequency (electronics)
3.0 6.2
Pronunciation: /ˈfriː.kwən.siː rɪ.ˈspɑːns kær.ək.tə.ˈrɪs.tɪk/ /; / /ˈfriː.kwən.siː riː.ˈspɑːns kær.ək.tə.ˈrɪs.tɪk/ (AmE), /ˈfriː.kwən.siː rɪ.ˈspɒns kær.ək.tə.ˈrɪs.tɪk/ (BrE)
In electronics, a graph or measure showing how the amplitude or gain of a system varies with frequency.

Examples

Flat frequency-response characteristic
A flat frequency-response characteristic is desirable in high-fidelity audio systems.
Audio engineers examine the frequency-response characteristic to ensure accurate sound reproduction.
The frequency-response characteristic of the amplifier shows a steep drop-off at higher frequencies.
