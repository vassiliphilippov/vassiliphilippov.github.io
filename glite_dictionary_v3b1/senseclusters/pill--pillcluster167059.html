pill

noun
something unpleasant that must be endured
1.9
Pronunciation: / pɪl / (AmE), / pɪl / (BrE)
an unpleasant or offensive fact, situation, or experience that must be accepted or endured

Examples

A bitter pill
It was a real pill when his project was rejected after months of work.
Losing the championship was a hard pill for the team to swallow.
The criticism from her boss was a tough pill to accept.
