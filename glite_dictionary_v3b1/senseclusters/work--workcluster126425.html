work

verb
exert influence; have an effect
Pronunciation: / wɝk /; / wɝːk / (AmE), / wɜːk / (BrE)
to influence, impress, or affect people or situations, often by using charm or effort

Examples

To work your charm
He tried to work his charm to persuade the committee.
Political activists work behind the scenes to influence policy decisions.
She knew how to work the crowd to get the reaction she wanted.
He tried to work his contacts to secure the best deal.
She knows how to work the system to get extra benefits.
Some people work the rules to gain an unfair advantage.
She managed to work the team into high spirits before the match.
The comedian worked the crowd with his rapid-fire jokes and lively energy.
The speaker skillfully worked the audience into a state of excitement.
