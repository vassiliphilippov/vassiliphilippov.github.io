melanin

noun
natural pigment in skin and hair
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛlənɪn / (AmE), / ˈmɛlənɪn / (BrE)
A natural pigment found in most living organisms, responsible for the coloration of skin, hair, feathers, and eyes.

Examples

Melanin pigment
A lack of melanin can lead to conditions such as albinism.
Birds often have different colors in their feathers due to varying amounts of melanin.
The amount of melanin in your skin determines how dark or light your complexion appears.

Related Terms

  • antonym: albinism - condition marked by absence of melanin
  • synonym: pigment - a substance that gives color to tissue
