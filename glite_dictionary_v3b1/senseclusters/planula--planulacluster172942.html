planula

noun
ciliated larval stage of cnidarians
2.6
Pronunciation: / ˈplæn.jʊ.lə / (AmE), / ˈplan.jʊ.lə / (BrE)
the free-swimming, flattened, ciliated larval stage of certain cnidarians, especially hydrozoans

Examples

The planula larva
After a few days, the planula will attach itself and transform into a polyp.
Researchers observed the movement of the planula under a microscope.
The fertilized egg develops into a planula before settling on the ocean floor.
