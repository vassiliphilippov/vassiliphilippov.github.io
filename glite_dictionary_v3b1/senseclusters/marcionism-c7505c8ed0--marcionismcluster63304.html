Marcionism

noun
early Christian doctrine rejecting the Old Testament
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈmɑːr.ʃə.nɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈmɑːr.sɪ.ə.nɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈmɑː.ʃə.nɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈmɑː.sɪ.ə.nɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
A Christian doctrine of the 2nd and 3rd centuries that rejected the Old Testament and denied that God became incarnate in Jesus Christ.

Examples

Marcionism rejects Old Testament
Marcionism was condemned by early church authorities for rejecting the Old Testament.

The spread of Marcionism prompted debates about the biblical canon in the second century.
