down

verb
bring down; cause to fall
5.5 3.18/m
Pronunciation: / daÊŠn / (AmE), / daÊŠn / (BrE)
to cause someone or something to fall to the ground, defeat, or bring down (including shooting down an aircraft or knocking down an opponent)

Examples

To down an opponent
Firefighters downed the burning tree before it could cause more damage.
The boxer downed his opponent with a swift uppercut in the second round.
The pilot skillfully downed the enemy aircraft during the battle.
Rebels attempted to down the government helicopter as it flew overhead.
The fighter jet was downed by an antiaircraft missile.
The pilot managed to down two enemy planes in a single mission.
After a fierce struggle, the team downed their long-time rivals.
She finally downed the reigning champion with a powerful serve.
The champion managed to down his opponent in the final round.
