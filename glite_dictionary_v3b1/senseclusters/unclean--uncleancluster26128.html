unclean

adjective
impure by religious or ceremonial standards
5.5 1.59/m
Pronunciation: /  ånÀàklin / (AmE), /  ånÀàkliÀên / (BrE)
Not pure or holy, especially considered impure according to religious, dietary, or ceremonial laws.

Examples

Unclean animals
Certain animals were regarded as unclean in ancient religious practices.
The laws dictated that unclean foods should not be consumed by followers.
Touching a unclean object required ritual purification.

Synonyms

  • impure
    6.0 0.73/m
    impure often means morally or physically not pure and can imply corruption, while unclean means ritually or legally impure.
  • unholy
    6.1 0.26/m
    unholy feels strongly opposed to holiness and often sounds negative or evil, while unclean focuses on ritual impurity.
  • unsanctified
    8.9 0.02/m
    unsanctified means not made sacred or consecrated, while unclean refers to being ritually or legally impure under religious rules.

Related Terms
