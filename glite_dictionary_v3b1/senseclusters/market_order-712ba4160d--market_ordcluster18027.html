market order

noun
order to buy or sell at current market price
1.0 3.0
Pronunciation: /ˈmɑːr.kɪt ˈɔːr.dər/ /; / /ˈmɑːr.kət ˈɔːr.dər/ (AmE), /ˈmɑː.kɪt ˈɔː.də/ /; / /ˈmɑː.kət ˈɔː.də/ (BrE)
an order given to a broker to buy or sell stocks or commodities immediately at the current market price

Examples

A market order now
Because the price can change quickly, a market order carries some risk in volatile markets.
He placed a market order to buy 100 shares of the company.
The broker explained the difference between a market order and a limit order to his client.
