neurapophysis

noun
vertebral projection
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌnʊrəˈpɑfəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəˈpɒfɪsɪs / (BrE)
a dorsal projection from a vertebra that forms part of the neural arch in vertebrates

Examples

The neurapophysis projection
The neurapophysis is an important structure in the vertebral column of many animals.
Damage to the neurapophysis can affect the protection of the spinal cord.
Researchers studied the development of the neurapophysis in different species of fish.

Related Terms

  • antonym: centrum - the main body of a vertebra
  • synonym: neural arch - the curved part of a vertebra enclosing the spinal cord
