fiddle

verb
touch or adjust something experimentally
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪd.əl / (AmE), / ˈfɪd.əl / (BrE)
to touch, move, or adjust something in a casual, restless, or experimental way, often without a clear purpose

Examples

To fiddle with buttons
He tried to fiddle with the remote control, but it still wouldn’t work.
If you fiddle with the settings too much, you might make things worse.
She likes to fiddle with the engine on weekends, hoping to make it run better.
He kept fiddling with the radio, trying to find a better station.
Please don't fiddle with the thermostat; I've set it to the right temperature.
She nervously fiddled with her pen while she waited for the interview to begin.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fidget - to make small movements, especially with the hands, through nervousness
  • synonym: tinker - to attempt to repair or improve something in a casual or experimental way
  • synonym: toy - to handle or play with something without sincere intent
