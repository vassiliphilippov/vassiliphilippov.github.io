catch

verb
to suffer or receive (something unpleasant)
2.1
Pronunciation: / kætʃ / (AmE), / katʃ / (BrE)
to experience or suffer as a consequence, especially something unpleasant or unwelcome

Examples

Catch trouble
He knew he would catch hell for breaking the window.
If you skip class again, you’ll catch a lot of trouble from your parents.
They caught some criticism for the way they handled the situation.
