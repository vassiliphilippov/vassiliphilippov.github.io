maintain

verb
keep property or vehicle for use
1.1 2.6
Pronunciation: /meɪn.ˈteɪn/ /; / /mə.ˈteɪn/ (AmE), /meɪn.ˈteɪn/ /; / /mə.ˈteɪn/ (BrE)
to keep something in good condition by making repairs, protecting it, or ensuring it does not deteriorate

Examples

To maintain a car
He maintains an old sports car in the garage for special occasions.
She maintains a small cottage by the lake for weekend retreats.
They maintain a second apartment in the city for business trips.
It takes time and effort to maintain an old house in good condition.
Regular cleaning helps maintain the value and appearance of your car.
The organization works hard to maintain national parks for future generations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conserve - to protect something from harm or loss, especially from being wasted, damaged, or destroyed
  • synonym: keep - to have or retain possession of something for use
  • antonym: neglect - to fail to care for or maintain; the opposite of maintain
  • synonym: preserve - to keep something in its original state or in good condition
