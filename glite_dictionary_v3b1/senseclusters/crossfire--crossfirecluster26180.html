crossfire

noun
heated exchange of opinions
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɔsˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌfaɪə / (BrE)
a lively or heated interchange of ideas and opinions, often involving rapid back-and-forth debate

Examples

Crossfire of opinions
Discussions at the meeting quickly broke into a crossfire of opposing viewpoints.
She found herself in the crossfire of a heated argument between her colleagues.
The candidate was caught in the crossfire during the televised debate, struggling to respond to both sides.
