mesopic

adjective
intermediate light levels
9.9
Pronunciation: / mɛˈsɑpɪk / (AmE), / mɛˈsɒpɪk / (BrE)
relating to light levels that are between bright daylight and darkness, such as those at dawn or dusk

Examples

Mesopic light levels
Drivers often rely on mesopic vision when traveling at dawn or dusk.
Street lighting is designed to support mesopic conditions for better visibility at night.
Researchers study how the human eye adapts to mesopic environments during twilight hours.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: photopic - relating to vision under well-lit conditions
  • degree lower: scotopic - relating to vision under very low light conditions
