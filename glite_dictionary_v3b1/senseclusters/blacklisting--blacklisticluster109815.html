blacklisting

noun
exclusion by list
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈblækˌlɪstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈblækˌlɪstɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of putting a person, group, or thing on a list to be excluded, avoided, or denied privileges or services

Examples

The blacklisting of workers
The company faced criticism for its blacklisting of certain job applicants.
Some countries use blacklisting to block access to specific websites.
After the scandal, there was widespread blacklisting of the artist's work.

Related Terms

  • antonym: approval - the act of accepting or allowing something
  • synonym: ban - an official or legal prohibition
  • synonym: boycott - the act of refusing to deal with or buy from a person or organization
