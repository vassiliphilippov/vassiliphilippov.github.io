A territory or area under the rule or control of a particular ruler, government, or authority.

Examples

A king's domain
Each feudal lord governed his own domain independently from the throne.
The company expanded its domain to other countries in the region.
The king's domain included vast forests and several towns.

Synonyms

  • territory
    3.0 46/m
    territory names a specific area of land under control, while domain can mean a broader area or field of authority.
  • territorial dominion
    7.7 0.00/m
    territorial dominion refers to land held by a ruler or state, while domain is a more general word for ruled area.
  • dominium
    9.9 0.00/m
    dominium is an older or legal term for control over land, while domain is the common word for that ruled area.
  • realm
    5.5 5.03/m
    realm means a country ruled by a king or queen, while domain can refer to any area under a ruler's control.
  • possession
    6.7 4.59/m
    possession often means land held as an overseas or distant holding, while domain is a general term for any ruled territory.
