impress

verb
produce a vivid impression in the mind
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈprɛs / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɛs / (BrE)
to make someone admire you or to have a strong effect on someone's feelings or thoughts

Examples

To impress your teacher
He wanted to impress the value of hard work in their minds.
Parents often impress good manners on their children from an early age.
The teacher tried to impress the importance of honesty on her students.
He wanted to impress his new boss with a well-prepared presentation.
Her talent and dedication always impress her teachers.
The beautiful architecture truly impressed the visitors.
Her speech really impressed the judges with its clarity and passion.
I was impressed by how quickly he solved the problem.
The grand scale of the building never fails to impress visitors.
