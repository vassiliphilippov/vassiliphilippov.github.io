Synonyms

  • step by step
    1.8 1/m
    step by step is a common spoken phrase for doing tasks slowly in order, while step-by-step is the staged description.
  • one step at a time
    2.8 2/m
    one step at a time tells someone to handle tasks slowly and calmly, while step-by-step describes a process made of clear stages.
  • stepwise
    6.0 0.10/m
    stepwise describes movement or change in clear steps, while step-by-step focuses on actions done in separate stages.
  • in stages
    3.5 0.3/m
    in stages means something happens gradually over several parts, while step-by-step stresses completing each part before moving on.
  • sequential
    4.9 0.8/m
    sequential describes items arranged in a set order, while step-by-step highlights a staged process where each stage is finished before the next.
  • successive
    4.3 2/m
    successive means one thing follows another without breaks, while step-by-step suggests a planned series of stages with tasks completed each time.
