Synonyms

  • disastrous
    3.5 10/m
    disastrous causes large damage and failure to plans or communities, while devastating causes deep ruin and strong emotional harm.
  • catastrophic
    7.0 10/m
    catastrophic causes sudden, wide damage and danger to many people, while devastating causes extreme harm that overwhelms people.
  • cataclysmic
    9.1 1/m
    cataclysmic describes a violent, earth-shaking event that changes places, while devastating focuses on heavy damage and deep sorrow.
  • annihilating
    7.8 0.00/m
    annihilating means destroying something completely with no chance of return, while devastating means causing severe damage and long-lasting loss.
  • ruinous
    7.0 1/m
    ruinous brings loss, often to money or buildings, leading to ruin, while devastating covers both severe physical harm and deep emotional damage.
  • calamitous
    7.9 0.6/m
    calamitous sounds formal and talks about great misfortune for communities, while devastating is more common and stresses heavy damage.
  • destructive
    4.2 16/m
    destructive causes damage and tends to break or ruin things, while devastating causes more total loss and strong emotional effect.
  • crushing
    6.0 0.00/m
    crushing often means a heavy physical or emotional blow that overwhelms someone, while devastating covers wide damage and lasting harm.
  • overwhelming
    4.4 30/m
    overwhelming describes a very strong feeling or force that is hard to handle, while devastating means it destroys or causes deep harm.
  • withering
    7.4 0.00/m
    withering often means harsh criticism or slow decline that hurts reputation, while devastating means rapid, wide damage or great loss.
