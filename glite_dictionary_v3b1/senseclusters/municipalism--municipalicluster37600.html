municipalism

noun
city government control
7.9
Pronunciation: /mjuː.ˈnɪs.ə.pə.lɪ.zəm/ /; / /mə.ˈnɪs.ə.pə.lɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /mjuː.ˈnɪs.ə.pə.lɪ.zəm/ /; / /mə.ˈnɪs.ə.pə.lɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
a political belief or movement that supports local (city or town) government control over public services and community affairs

Examples

Municipalism movement
The rise of municipalism led the city to manage its own public transportation system.
Advocates of municipalism believe local governments should control essential utilities like water and electricity.
Debates about municipalism often focus on whether cities should own their own services or rely on private companies.
The city council adopted policies inspired by municipalism to give residents more decision-making power.
Advocates of municipalism believe that local governments can better address community needs.
Recent elections have seen a surge in support for municipalism across several major cities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: centralism - concentration of power in a central authority
  • synonym: localism - support for local control and local government
  • synonym: municipal socialism - public ownership of services by city governments
  • antonym: privatization - transferring public services to private ownership
