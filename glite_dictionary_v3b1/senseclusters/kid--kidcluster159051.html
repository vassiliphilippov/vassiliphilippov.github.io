kid

verb
tease or joke playfully
2.8
Pronunciation: / kɪd / (AmE), / kɪd / (BrE)
to joke or tease someone in a playful way (not serious)

Examples

To kid a friend
Are you kidding? Of course I remembered your birthday.
He likes to kid his friends, but it's always good-natured.
We spent the afternoon kidding around in the park.
Are you kidding me, or is that really true?
He likes to kid around with his friends, making up silly stories.
Please don't kid her about her new haircut; she's sensitive.

Related Terms

  • synonym: joke - to say things to make people laugh or amuse them
  • antonym: lie - to say something false with the intention to deceive
  • antonym: mock - to tease or make fun of, sometimes in a less playful or more critical way
  • synonym: tease - to make fun of or attempt to provoke in a playful way
