chrominance

noun
color information
Pronunciation: / ˈkroʊmɪnəns / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊmɪnəns / (BrE)
the part of an image or video signal that represents color (hue and saturation), separate from brightness

Examples

The chrominance signal
Television engineers measure chrominance to ensure accurate color reproduction on the screen.
The camera's sensor separates chrominance from luminance to process images more efficiently.
Adjusting the chrominance can make the colors in a video appear more vivid or natural.
The technician adjusted the chrominance to make the colors on the screen appear more natural.
Poor chrominance can cause the colors in a video to look faded or incorrect.
Modern televisions process chrominance and luminance separately to improve picture quality.

Related Terms

  • synonym: color - the property of an object that depends on the wavelengths of light it reflects or emits
  • antonym: luminance - the brightness component of an image or light
