recall

verb
order to return; call back
4.1 2.15/m
Pronunciation: / r…™Ààk…îl / (AmE), / r…™Ààk…îÀêl / (BrE)
to order someone to return to a place, especially someone who has been sent somewhere for a particular purpose; to ask employees to return to work after being laid off

Examples

To recall an ambassador
After diplomatic tensions increased, the government decided to recall its ambassador from the neighboring country.
Due to safety issues, the airline was forced to recall several pilots from their overseas assignments.
During the recovery, the company started to recall workers it had laid off.

Synonyms

  • call back
    3.3 1.15/m
    call back is a phrasal, informal way to ask someone to return, while recall is a formal order used in work or official settings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dismiss - to send someone away from a place or position
  • synonym: summon - to order someone to come to a place
