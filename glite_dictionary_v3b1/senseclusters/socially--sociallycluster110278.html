socially

adverb
in regard to society or social affairs
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊʃəli / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to society, social activities, or how people interact with each other

Examples

To be socially active
He is socially active and enjoys spending time with friends.
Some behaviors are not socially acceptable in public settings.
The new policy was socially beneficial, improving life for the entire community.
He interacts very socially at work, often joining his colleagues for lunch.
Living socially can help improve mental health and build supportive networks.
She was raised to behave socially in both formal and informal settings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: antisocially - in a manner that is contrary to social norms or unfriendly
  • synonym: culturally - in regard to culture or cultural aspects
  • antonym: individually - with regard to each person separately
