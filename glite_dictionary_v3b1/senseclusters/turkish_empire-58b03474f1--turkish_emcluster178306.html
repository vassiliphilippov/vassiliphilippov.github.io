Turkish Empire

noun
historic empire ruled by Ottoman Turks
Pronunciation: / ˈtɝːkɪʃ ˈɛm.paɪr / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.kɪʃ ˈɛm.paɪə / (BrE)
the empire established by the Ottoman Turks at the end of the 13th century, spanning southwestern Asia, northeastern Africa, and southeastern Europe, and lasting until the end of World War I

Examples

The Turkish Empire expansion
After centuries of expansion, the Turkish Empire eventually dissolved at the end of World War I.
Istanbul was the capital of the Turkish Empire for over 400 years.
The Turkish Empire was one of the most powerful states in the world during the 16th and 17th centuries.
