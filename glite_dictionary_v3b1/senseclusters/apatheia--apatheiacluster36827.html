apatheia

noun
absence of emotion
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌæpəˈθiːə / (AmE), / ˌæpəˈθiːə / (BrE)
a state of complete lack of emotional response or motivation, often seen in clinical or pathological contexts

Examples

Clinical apatheia
The neurologist noted a profound apatheia in the patient, who no longer reacted to stimuli.
After the trauma, she exhibited apatheia, showing no interest in activities she once loved.
Doctors observed apatheia as a symptom in several cases of advanced neurological disease.

Related Terms

  • synonym: apathy - lack of interest, enthusiasm, or concern
  • antonym: emotion - a strong feeling such as love, anger, or joy
  • synonym: indifference - lack of interest or concern
