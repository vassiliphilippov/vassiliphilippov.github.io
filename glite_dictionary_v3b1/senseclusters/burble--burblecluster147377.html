burble

verb
flow with a bubbling sound
7.5 0.52/m
Pronunciation: / Ààb…ùÀê.b…ôl / (AmE), / Ààb…úÀê.b…ôl / (BrE)
(of a liquid) to flow with a low, continuous, bubbling sound; to make such a noise while moving.

Examples

A brook burbled softly
A brook burbled cheerfully near the edge of the meadow.
She listened to the fountain burbling softly in the courtyard.
The little stream began to burble as it flowed over the smooth stones.

Synonyms

  • babble
    4.2 0.08/m
    babble is a soft, irregular stream sound often used for small rivers, while burble is a low, continuous bubbling flow.
  • purl
    9.3 0.03/m
    purl flows with a soft, murmuring, swirling motion in a stream, while burble refers to a low continuous bubbling sound.
  • gurgle
    3.7 1.85/m
    gurgle makes a bubbling, irregular liquid sound often heard when pouring or drinking, while burble is a low continuous stream sound.
  • bubble
    2.0 1.80/m
    bubble forms visible bubbles and may produce a gurgling noise, while burble emphasizes a low, steady bubbling flow in motion.
  • guggle
    9.9 0.00/m
    guggle makes a gurgling, bubbling noise especially when liquid is poured or drunk, while burble describes flowing with a low continuous sound.

Related Terms

  • synonym: babble - to make a continuous, low, murmuring sound, like water flowing gently
  • synonym: gurgle - to make a hollow bubbling sound like that made by water running out of a bottle
