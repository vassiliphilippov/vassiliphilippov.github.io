explicandum

noun
something to be explained (logic)
Pronunciation: / ˌɛksplɪˈkændəm / (AmE), / ˌɛksplɪˈkændəm / (BrE)
(logic) a statement, fact, or expression that is to be explained, especially in contrast to an explanation (explicans).

Examples

The explicandum statement
Before proposing a theory, one must clearly state the explicandum to be addressed.
Careful identification of the explicandum helps avoid ambiguity in scientific explanation.
The explicandum in this case is why the phenomenon occurs under such conditions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: explicans - the explanation or that which explains (logic)
