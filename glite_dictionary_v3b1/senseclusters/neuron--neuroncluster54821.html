neuron

noun
nerve cell; transmits impulses
Pronunciation: / ˈnʊrˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈnjʊərəʊn / (BrE)
A specialized cell that transmits nerve impulses in the nervous system.

Examples

A neuron cell
Researchers study how neurons communicate to better understand neurological diseases.
The human brain contains billions of neurons that help process information.
When a neuron is damaged, it can affect how messages are sent in the body.
