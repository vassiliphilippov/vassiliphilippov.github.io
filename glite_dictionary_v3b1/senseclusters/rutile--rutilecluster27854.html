rutile

noun
mineral form of titanium dioxide
Pronunciation: / ˈruːtaɪl / (AmE), / ˈruːtaɪl / (BrE)
a reddish-brown or black mineral consisting of titanium dioxide, found as needlelike crystals in metamorphic and igneous rocks, and used as a major source of titanium.

Examples

Needles of rutile
Fine needles of rutile can be found embedded in some gemstones, creating a star-like effect.
Industrial extraction of titanium often starts with the mineral rutile.
The geologist identified the reddish crystals as rutile under the microscope.
