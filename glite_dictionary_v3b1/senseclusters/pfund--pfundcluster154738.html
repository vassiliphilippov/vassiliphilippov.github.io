pfund

noun
historic pound currency
Pronunciation: / pfʊnt / (AmE), / pfʊnt / (BrE)
a unit of currency equivalent to the pound, used historically or in German-speaking regions to refer to pounds sterling or local pound currencies

Examples

A pfund note
The merchant recorded the price as five pfund in the old account book.
In medieval Germany, taxes were often paid in pfund rather than coins.
The contract specified payment in British pfund for imported goods.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pound - a unit of currency used in the United Kingdom and other countries
