QRS complex

noun
ECG ventricular pattern
6.5
Pronunciation: / kjuː ɑr ɛs ˈkɑmplɛks / (AmE), / kjuː ɑː ɛs ˈkɒmplɛks / (BrE)
the part of an electrocardiogram (ECG) that shows the electrical activity during the contraction of the heart's ventricles

Examples

The QRS complex wave
The cardiologist carefully analyzed the QRS complex to check for signs of heart disease.
A widened QRS complex on the ECG may indicate a problem with the heart's electrical conduction.
Medical students learn to identify the QRS complex as a key part of reading an electrocardiogram.

Related Terms

  • synonym: P wave - the part of an ECG showing atrial depolarization
  • synonym: T wave - the part of an ECG showing ventricular repolarization
