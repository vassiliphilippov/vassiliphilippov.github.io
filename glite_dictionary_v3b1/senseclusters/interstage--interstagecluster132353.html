interstage

noun
section between stages
Pronunciation: / ˈɪntərˌsteɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɪntəˌsteɪdʒ / (BrE)
a section or component that connects or separates two main stages in a process or device

Examples

Rocket interstage
The interstage of the rocket is designed to separate smoothly during flight.
Engineers inspected the interstage to ensure all connections between the stages were secure.
A malfunction in the interstage can affect the performance of the entire launch vehicle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interface - a point where two systems or stages meet and interact
  • degree lower: stage - a particular step or phase in a process or device
