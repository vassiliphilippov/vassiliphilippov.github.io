nonrelease

noun
failure to open
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑn.rɪˈlis / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.rɪˈliːs / (BrE)
the failure of a mechanism, valve, lock, or safety device to open, disengage, or allow passage as intended

Examples

Nonrelease of a lock
The engineer traced the problem to a nonrelease of the emergency brake system.
A nonrelease of the locking mechanism caused the door to remain shut during the test.
Safety checks are important to prevent any nonrelease of critical valves in the system.

Related Terms

  • synonym: malfunction - a failure to function properly
  • antonym: release - the act of allowing something to move, open, or be set free
