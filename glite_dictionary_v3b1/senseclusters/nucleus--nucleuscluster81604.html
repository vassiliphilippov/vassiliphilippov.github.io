nucleus

noun
group of neural cell bodies in the brain or spinal cord
Pronunciation: / ˈnuː.kli.əs / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.kli.əs / (BrE)
A distinct group of nerve cell bodies in the brain or spinal cord, identifiable by histological methods.

Examples

Brainstem nucleus
Each nucleus in the brainstem has a specific function related to sensory or motor control.
Researchers identified a previously unrecognized nucleus involved in emotional processing.
The hypothalamic nucleus plays a crucial role in regulating hunger and thirst.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ganglion - group of nerve cell bodies outside the central nervous system
