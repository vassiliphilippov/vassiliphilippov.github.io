tinker

verb
attempt casual repair or adjustment
9.5 3.7
Pronunciation: /ˈtɪŋ.kər/ (AmE), /ˈtɪŋ.kə/ (BrE)
to repair, adjust, or experiment with something in a casual or unskilled way

Examples

To tinker with machines
He likes to tinker with old radios in his spare time.
If you tinker with the settings, you might get better results.
She spent the afternoon quietly tinkering with her broken bike.
He loves to tinker with electronics in his garage, even if he doesn't always fix them.
She spent the afternoon tinkering with the old clock to see if she could make it work again.
Sometimes it's best not to tinker with a system that already works.
He likes to tinker in the garage with old radios and gadgets.
Instead of working seriously, he spent the afternoon tinkering with his model trains.
On weekends, she often tinkers with new recipes just for fun.
