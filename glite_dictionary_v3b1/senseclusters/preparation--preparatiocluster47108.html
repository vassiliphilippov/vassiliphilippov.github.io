preparation

noun
musical note moving from consonance to dissonance
Pronunciation: / ˌprɛpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌprɛpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
(Music) A note that is first heard as part of a consonant chord, which then becomes dissonant in the next chord.

Examples

Chord preparation
A careful preparation of the chord added a sense of anticipation before the musical tension.
In counterpoint exercises, the teacher emphasized the importance of preparation before introducing a suspension.
The composer used preparation ingeniously to smooth the transition into dissonant harmonies.
