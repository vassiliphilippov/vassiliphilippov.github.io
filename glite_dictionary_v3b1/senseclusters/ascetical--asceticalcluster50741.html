ascetical

adjective
relating to rigorous self-discipline
5.3 7.8
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈse.tɪ.kəl/ (AmE), /ə.ˈse.tɪ.kəl/ (BrE)
relating to or showing strict self-discipline and avoidance of pleasure, especially for religious or spiritual reasons

Examples

An ascetical lifestyle
Her ascetical approach to diet impressed her friends with its strictness.
The ascetical traditions of the order demanded long periods of silence and fasting.
The monks led an ascetical life, devoting themselves to prayer and meditation.
Her ascetical habits included fasting and meditating for hours each day.
Many admire his ascetical dedication to spiritual growth over worldly pleasure.
The monk lived an ascetical life, rejecting all material comforts and luxuries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ascetic - practicing strict self-denial as a measure of personal and spiritual discipline
  • synonym: austere - severely simple; without luxury or comfort
  • antonym: indulgent - allowing oneself or others excess or luxury; yielding to desires
