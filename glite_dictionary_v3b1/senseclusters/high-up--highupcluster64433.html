high-up

noun
important or influential person
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪ ʌp / (AmE), / ˈhaɪ ʌp / (BrE)
a person who has an important or influential position, especially in an organization

Examples

The high-ups in charge
He always tries to impress the high-ups to get a promotion.
She was introduced to several high-ups in the company during the conference.
The decision has to be approved by one of the high-ups.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bigwig - an important, influential person
  • antonym: underling - a person lower in rank or status
