angiofibroma

noun
vascular nasal tumor
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌændʒioʊfaɪˈbroʊmə / (AmE), / ˌændʒiəʊfaɪˈbrəʊmə / (BrE)
a benign tumor made of blood vessels and fibrous tissue, usually found in the nasal or nasopharyngeal area

Examples

Nasal angiofibroma
The doctor diagnosed the teenager with a angiofibroma after he experienced frequent nosebleeds.
Surgery is often required to remove a angiofibroma that causes nasal obstruction.
A angiofibroma can grow rapidly and may lead to facial swelling if not treated.
The surgeon removed the angiofibroma from the patient's nasal cavity.
A teenage boy was diagnosed with a rare angiofibroma after experiencing frequent nosebleeds.
Treatment for angiofibroma often involves surgery to prevent further complications.
