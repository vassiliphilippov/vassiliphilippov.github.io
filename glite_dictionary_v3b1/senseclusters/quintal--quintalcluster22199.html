quintal

noun
U.S. unit of weight (100 pounds)
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɪn.tl̩ / (AmE), / ˈkwɪn.təl / (BrE)
A unit of weight used in the United States, equal to 100 pounds.

Examples

A quintal of grain
Each bag weighed exactly one quintal, making it easy to calculate the total shipment.
Farmers stored several quintals of grain in their barns for the winter.
The price of wheat is often quoted per quintal in the United States.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hundredweight - a unit of weight equal to 100 pounds (U.S.) or 112 pounds (British)
