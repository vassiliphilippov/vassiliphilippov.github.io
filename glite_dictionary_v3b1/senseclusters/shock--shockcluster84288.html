shock

noun
sudden jarring impact or collision
Pronunciation: / ʃɑk / (AmE), / ʃɒk / (BrE)
a sudden, violent impact, collision, or disturbance

Examples

Shock from impact
A good pair of boots reduces the shock to your feet when hiking on rough terrain.
The bridge was designed to withstand the shock of an earthquake.
The car came to a halt with a violent shock when it hit the curb.
History books describe the shock between the armies as decisive in the war.
The shock of combat left both sides with heavy losses.
The two cavalry units met in a fierce shock at dawn.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collision - a violent coming together of two bodies
  • synonym: engagement - a battle or fight between armed forces
  • synonym: impact - the action of one object coming forcibly into contact with another
  • synonym: jolt - a sudden jerk or movement
