palas

noun
East Indian tree with vivid red flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːləs / (AmE), / ˈpælɑːs / (BrE)
An East Indian tree (Butea monosperma) known for its intense vermilion, velvet-textured blooms and for yielding a yellow dye.

Examples

Palas tree flowers
Artisans extract a yellow dye from the flowers of the palas for use in traditional textiles.
In some villages, the palas is considered sacred and is planted near temples.
The palas tree is renowned for its striking scarlet blossoms that cover the branches in spring.
