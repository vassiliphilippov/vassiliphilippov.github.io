option

verb
to acquire rights
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈɑpʃən /; / ˈɑːpʃən / (AmE), / ˈɒpʃən / (BrE)
to acquire the right to buy or use something, especially for future purchase or adaptation

Examples

To option a book
The producer optioned the book, hoping to turn it into a successful movie.
Several companies have optioned the technology for future development.
She optioned the rights to the story before anyone else could.
The film studio is optioning the rights to the bestselling novel for a possible adaptation.
She optioned the property last year, hoping to develop it when the market improved.
Several companies are optioning new technologies to stay ahead in the industry.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: purchase - to buy something
  • antonym: relinquish - to give up or let go of a right or claim
  • synonym: secure - to obtain or guarantee something, such as a right or opportunity
