lay reader

noun
non-ordained person leading church service parts
8.6 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / le…™ ÀàriÀêd…ôr / (AmE), / le…™ ÀàriÀêd…ô / (BrE)
A layperson authorized by a bishop to lead certain parts of a church service, especially in Anglican or Episcopal churches.

Examples

Lay reader in church
After his training, John became a lay reader and regularly assisted during Sunday services.
As a lay reader, she is permitted to preach and conduct certain ceremonies, though not sacraments.
The church relies on several lay readers to help with scripture readings and prayers.

Synonyms

  • reader
    7.4 0.94/m
    reader often only reads aloud during services and may be a minor cleric, while lay reader is a layperson licensed to lead parts of worship.
