ironclad

adjective
rigid and unchangeable (esp. rule or agreement)
3.2 5.9
Pronunciation: /ˈaɪ.ərn.klæd/ /; / /ˈaɪrn.klæd/ (AmE), /ˈaɪ.ən.klæd/ (BrE)
(of a rule, agreement, or principle) very rigid, unchangeable, or impossible to break or get around

Examples

Ironclad rule
Despite their pleas, the governor remained committed to his ironclad decision.
She sets ironclad rules for her children that must be obeyed without exception.
The company had an ironclad contract that protected it from any legal challenges.
Her ironclad alibi convinced the police there was no way she could be guilty.
The lawyers drafted an ironclad agreement that left no room for interpretation.
We need an ironclad plan to make sure nothing goes wrong this time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bulletproof - impossible to get through or weaken (argument, plan)
  • antonym: flawed - having mistakes or weaknesses
  • antonym: flexible - able to change or be modified easily; adaptable
  • synonym: unyielding - not willing to change or bend; inflexible
  • synonym: watertight - having no weaknesses or gaps (argument, case, plan)
