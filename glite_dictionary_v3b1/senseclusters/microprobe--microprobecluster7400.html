microprobe

noun
tiny testing instrument
7.9
Pronunciation: /ˈmaɪ.kroʊ.proʊb/ (AmE), /ˈmaɪ.krəʊ.prəʊb/ (BrE)
a very small probe or instrument used to test, measure, or make contact with microscopic circuits or devices

Examples

Circuit microprobe
The engineer used a microprobe to examine the delicate wiring on the microchip.
A microprobe allows scientists to analyze materials at a microscopic scale with great precision.
Careful handling of the microprobe is essential to avoid damaging sensitive electronic components.
