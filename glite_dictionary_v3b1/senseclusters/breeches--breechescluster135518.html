breeches

noun
knee-length trousers (historical)
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɪtʃɪz / (AmE), / ˈbriː.tʃɪz / (BrE)
Trousers that end above the knee, typically fastened just below the knee; especially worn in the past.

Examples

Leather breeches
During the festival, many participants wore traditional breeches and sashes.
He put on his finest breeches for the evening banquet.
In the painting, the officer is dressed in a red coat and white breeches.
