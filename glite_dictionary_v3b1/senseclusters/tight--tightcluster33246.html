tight

adjective
evenly matched; close contest
2.1 3.10/m
Pronunciation: / taÉªt / (AmE), / taÉªt / (BrE)
(of a contest or competitors) having little difference in ability or result; evenly matched or very close.

Examples

Tight contest
Experts predicted a tight contest between the two top teams in the final.
It was a tight election, with both candidates receiving nearly equal votes.
The basketball match was a tight game that went into overtime.

Synonyms

  • close
    1.0 274/m
    close describes a contest with very little difference between sides, while tight suggests a narrow, tense margin.
  • neck and neck
    3.5 0.88/m
    neck and neck describes two competitors running almost even in a race, while tight means a close contest in general.
  • nip and tuck
    8.1 0.04/m
    nip and tuck describes a match whose outcome stays uncertain until the end, while tight means simply closely matched.
