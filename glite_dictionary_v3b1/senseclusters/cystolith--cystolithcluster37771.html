cystolith

noun
stone formed in the bladder
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪstəˌlɪθ / (AmE), / ˈsɪstəˌlɪθ / (BrE)
A hard mineral deposit (stone) formed in the urinary bladder.

Examples

A cystolith stone
A cystolith can cause severe pain if it obstructs the flow of urine.
Doctors detected a cystolith during the patient's ultrasound.
Surgical removal may be required when a cystolith becomes too large.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bladder stone - a calculus formed in the bladder
