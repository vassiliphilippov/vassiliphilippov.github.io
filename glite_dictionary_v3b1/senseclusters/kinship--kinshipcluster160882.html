kinship

noun
close connection or similarity
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪnʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈkɪnʃɪp / (BrE)
a strong connection or similarity between people, groups, or things, especially because of shared interests or character

Examples

Kinship with friends
Many people experience a sense of kinship when they share similar values.
She felt a strong kinship with the other artists in the group.
There is a clear kinship between jazz and blues music.

Related Terms

  • synonym: affinity - a natural liking or connection with someone or something
  • synonym: relationship - the way in which two or more concepts, objects, or people are connected
