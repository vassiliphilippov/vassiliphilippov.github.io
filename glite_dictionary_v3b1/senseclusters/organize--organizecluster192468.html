organize

verb
plan and direct a complex undertaking
1.2 1.4
Pronunciation: /ˈɔːr.gə.naɪz/ (AmE), /ˈɔː.gə.naɪz/ (BrE)
to arrange, plan, or coordinate things, activities, or people in an orderly or systematic way

Examples

To organize a meeting
It took weeks to organize the international conference successfully.
She was asked to organize the company's annual charity gala.
They worked together to organize a large fundraising campaign for local schools.
Could you please help me organize all these documents before the meeting?
He spent the afternoon organizing his desk to make it more efficient.
She likes to organize her books by genre and author.
She worked hard to organize the community fundraiser, ensuring every detail was planned.
The teachers organized a field trip for the students to visit the museum.
We are organizing a meeting to discuss the new project timeline.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arrange - to put things in a particular order or position; to organize an event
  • synonym: coordinate - to organize people or groups so that they work together properly and well
  • antonym: disorganise - to make things disordered or chaotic
  • antonym: disorganize - to disturb the organization of; to make something less ordered
  • antonym: dissolve - to officially end or disband an entity such as a group or organization
  • synonym: establish - to set up or create something that is meant to last
  • synonym: form - to bring together parts to create something new
  • synonym: organize - to arrange or structure systematically (American spelling)
  • synonym: plan - to decide the details of how something will be done
