narrowness

noun
small margin or difference
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛroʊnəs / (AmE), / ˈnærəʊnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being small in amount, distance, or difference, especially when referring to an amount by which someone wins or achieves something

Examples

Narrowness of the lead
Despite the narrowness of their lead, the team managed to hold on to victory.
He was well aware of the narrowness of his escape from the accident.
The narrowness of the vote surprised even seasoned political analysts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: margin - an amount by which something is won or falls short
  • antonym: width - the quality of being broad or wide, as opposed to narrowness
