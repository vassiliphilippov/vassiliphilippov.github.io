rearguard

noun
military group protecting the rear
5.5 7.1
Pronunciation: /ˈrɪər.ɡɑːrd/ /; / /ˈrɪr.ɡɑːrd/ (AmE), /ˈrɪə.ɡɑːd/ (BrE)
A group of soldiers assigned to protect the rear of a main military force, especially during a retreat.

Examples

A rearguard unit
A small rearguard unit remained behind to cover the army's withdrawal.
During the retreat, the commander placed his best soldiers in the rearguard.
The rearguard fought bravely to delay the enemy's advance.

  • antonym: vanguard - the troops at the front of a military formation or movement
