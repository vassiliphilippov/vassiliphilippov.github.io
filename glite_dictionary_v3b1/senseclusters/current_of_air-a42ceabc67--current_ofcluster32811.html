current of air

noun
a flow of moving air
3.5 0.10/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ùÀê…ônt …ôv Àà…õr / (AmE), / Ààk år…ônt …ôv Ààe…ô / (BrE)
a flow of air moving from an area of higher pressure to one of lower pressure, sometimes with considerable force

Examples

A cool current of air
A cool current of air entered the room through the open window.
Gliders rely on rising currents of air to stay aloft for long periods.
The mountain pass funneled a strong current of air that made walking difficult.

Synonyms

  • airstream

  • wind
    1.0 135/m
    wind is the large natural movement of air outdoors, while current of air refers to any pressure-driven flow, sometimes strong.
  • airflow
    3.5 2.46/m

  • current

    current is a steady flow used for water, air, or ideas in many contexts, while current of air means air moving by pressure.
