current of air

noun
a flow of moving air
Pronunciation: / ˈkɝːənt əv ˈɛr / (AmE), / ˈkʌrənt əv ˈeə / (BrE)
a flow of air moving from an area of higher pressure to one of lower pressure, sometimes with considerable force

Examples

A cool current of air
A cool current of air entered the room through the open window.
Gliders rely on rising currents of air to stay aloft for long periods.
The mountain pass funneled a strong current of air that made walking difficult.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: breeze - a gentle, light current of air
  • degree higher: gale - a very strong wind or current of air
  • synonym: wind - air in motion relative to the surface of the earth
