Basil the Great

proper noun
4th-century bishop and Christian theologian
Pronunciation: / ˈbæz.əl ðə ɡreɪt / (AmE), / ˈbæz.ɪl ðə ɡreɪt / (BrE)
(c. 329–379) A bishop of Caesarea, early Christian theologian, and Doctor of the Church, known for defending the faith against heresies in the 4th century.

Examples

Basil the Great bishop
Basil the Great is respected as a Doctor of the Church in Roman Catholic tradition.
Many churches celebrate the feast day of Basil the Great on January 2nd.
The writings of Basil the Great had a lasting impact on Christian theology.
