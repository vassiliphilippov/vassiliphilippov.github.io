mesoglea

noun
jelly-like animal layer
9.9
Pronunciation: /mez.ə.ˈgliː.ə/ /; / /miːz.ə.ˈgliː.ə/ (AmE), /mez.ə.ˈgliː.ə/ /; / /mez.ə.ˈglɪə/ (BrE)
the jelly-like layer found between the outer and inner cell layers in certain simple animals such as jellyfish and corals

Examples

The mesoglea layer
The mesoglea gives jellyfish their soft, gelatinous texture.
Scientists studied the thickness of the mesoglea in different species of sea anemones.
The transparent mesoglea helps support the body structure of many simple aquatic animals.
