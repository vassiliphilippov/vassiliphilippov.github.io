flutteringly

adverb
with trembling motion
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈflʌtərɪŋli /; / ˈflʌt̬ərɪŋli / (AmE), / ˈflʌtərɪŋli / (BrE)
in a way that moves lightly, quickly, or with a trembling or fluttering motion (like fluttering wings, or with playful or nervous gestures)

Examples

Leaves fall flutteringly
The leaves fell flutteringly to the ground as the wind picked up.
She spoke flutteringly, her voice trembling with excitement.
A small bird landed flutteringly on the branch, barely making a sound.
She smiled flutteringly at her friend, her hands nervously playing with her scarf.
The butterfly hovered flutteringly above the flowers before landing gently.
He glanced flutteringly at the audience, unsure if he should begin his speech.

Related Terms

  • antonym: awkwardly - in a clumsy or uncomfortable manner
  • synonym: coquettishly - in a flirtatious or teasing manner
  • synonym: nervously - in a way that shows anxiety or unease
  • antonym: steadily - in a firm, regular, or even manner
