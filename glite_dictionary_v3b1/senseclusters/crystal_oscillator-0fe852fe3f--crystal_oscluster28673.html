crystal oscillator

noun
electronic component generating precise frequency
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪstl ɑːˈsɪleɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstl ɒˈsɪleɪtə / (BrE)
an electronic oscillator that generates a precise frequency using the mechanical resonance of a piezoelectric quartz crystal

Examples

A crystal oscillator circuit
A typical wristwatch uses a tiny crystal oscillator to keep precise time.
Engineers replaced the faulty crystal oscillator to restore stable frequency output.
The clock in most computers is driven by a crystal oscillator for high accuracy.
