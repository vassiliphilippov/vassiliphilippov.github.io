polysynthetic

adjective
forming complex words from many morphemes (linguistics)
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːliˈsɪnθətɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈsɪnθətɪk / (BrE)
(of a language) characterized by the formation of complex words from many morphemes, each expressing a definite meaning, often resulting in words that convey the meaning of a whole sentence.

Examples

Polysynthetic language structure
Her research focused on the polysynthetic nature of certain Australian Aboriginal languages.
Many indigenous languages of the Americas are described as polysynthetic by linguists.
The polysynthetic structure of Inuktitut allows for the expression of complex ideas in single words.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: agglutinative - (linguistics) forming words by stringing together morphemes
