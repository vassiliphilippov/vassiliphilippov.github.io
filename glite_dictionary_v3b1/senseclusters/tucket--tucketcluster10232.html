tucket

noun
short lively brass music signal
Pronunciation: / ˈtʌkɪt / (AmE), / ˈtʌkɪt / (BrE)
a short, lively piece of music played on brass instruments, especially to announce or signal something

Examples

A tucket fanfare
A tucket rang out, drawing everyone's attention to the stage.
In the play, the king is greeted with a tucket as he enters the hall.
The musicians prepared a tucket to signal the beginning of the ceremony.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fanfare - a short ceremonial tune or flourish played on brass instruments
  • synonym: flourish - a musical passage performed to attract attention
