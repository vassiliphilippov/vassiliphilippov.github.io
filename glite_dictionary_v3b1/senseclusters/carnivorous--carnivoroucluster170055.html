carnivorous

adjective
feeding on other animals
1.8 4.5
Pronunciation: / kɑrˈnɪvərəs /; / kɑrˈnɪv.ɚ.əs / (AmE), / kɑːˈnɪvərəs /; / kɑːˈnɪv.ər.əs / (BrE)
(of animals or plants) feeding on other animals; meat-eating

Examples

Carnivorous animals
Bears are not strictly carnivorous because they also eat berries and plants.
Some plants, like the Venus flytrap, are carnivorous and digest insects to supplement their nutrition.
The carnivorous diet of the tiger helps maintain the balance of the ecosystem.
Bears, although mostly omnivorous, can exhibit carnivorous behavior during certain seasons.
Lions are carnivorous predators that thrive at the top of the food chain.
Some plants, like the venus flytrap, are carnivorous and capture insects for nutrients.
