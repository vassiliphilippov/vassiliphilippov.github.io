desensitization procedure

noun
behavior therapy technique to reduce anxiety
9.8 5.4
Pronunciation: / dɪˌsɛnsɪˌtaɪˈzeɪʃən prəˈsiːdʒər /; / diːˌsɛnsəˌtaɪˈzeɪʃən prəˈsiːdʒər / (AmE), / dɪˌsɛnsɪtaɪˈzeɪʃən prəˈsiːdʒə /; / diːˌsɛnsɪtaɪˈzeɪʃən prəˈsiːdʒə / (BrE)
a technique in behavior therapy for treating phobias and anxiety problems, involving exposure to feared situations in a controlled, relaxed way until the anxiety lessens

Examples

The desensitization procedure in therapy
During the desensitization procedure, patients are gradually exposed to their source of anxiety.
Many psychologists use a desensitization procedure as part of their treatment for specific phobias.
The therapist suggested a desensitization procedure to help Sarah overcome her fear of dogs.
A successful desensitisation procedure can enable individuals to face situations that once triggered intense fear.
Many patients benefit from a desensitisation procedure when dealing with severe anxiety disorders.
The therapist recommended a desensitisation procedure to help him overcome his fear of spiders.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exposure therapy - a method of treating anxiety by exposing patients to fears
  • synonym: systematic desensitisation - a structured form of desensitisation procedure used in behavior therapy
  • synonym: systematic desensitization - a specific form of desensitization therapy for phobias
