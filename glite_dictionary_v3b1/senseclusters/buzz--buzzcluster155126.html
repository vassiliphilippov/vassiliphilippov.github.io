buzz

verb
be filled with busy or lively noise
3.1 3.44/m
Pronunciation: / bÊŒz / (AmE), / bÊŒz / (BrE)
(of a place) to be filled with a continuous busy or lively noise, usually from people talking or moving about energetically.

Examples

The kitchen buzzes with noise
By 8 a.m., the kitchen was already buzzing with activity as the staff prepared breakfast.
During the festival, the whole town was buzzing with excitement and laughter.
The city center buzzes on Saturday nights, with crowds filling the streets and restaurants.

Synonyms

  • hum
    3.5 1.10/m
    hum is a steady, low background noise from people working or moving quietly, while buzz is louder, lively chatter and busy movement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hum - make a low continuous sound, often of activity
  • degree higher: roar - make a loud, deep, continuous noise
