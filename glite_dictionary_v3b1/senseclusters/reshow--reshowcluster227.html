reshow

verb
to show again
3.4 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀêÀà Éo ä / (AmE), / ÀåriÀêÀà É…ô ä / (BrE)
to show something again, such as a film, program, or object

Examples

To reshow a film
The museum will reshow the famous painting during its anniversary exhibition.
After popular demand, the channel decided to reshow the documentary next month.
They plan to reshow the play for audiences who missed the first run.

Synonyms

  • rescreen
    4.2 0.00/m
    rescreen is used for showing a film or program again in cinemas or on TV, while reshow means to show something again more generally.
  • rerun
    2.3 0.10/m
    rerun is a TV or theatre showing that appears again after its first time, while reshow can mean any showing again.
  • replay
    2.1 1.76/m
    replay is playing a recorded event or clip again to watch or hear, while reshow often means showing something again live or on screen.

Related Terms

  • antonym: premiere - to show or perform something for the first time
  • synonym: repeat - to do or show something again
  • synonym: replay - to play something again, especially a recording or broadcast
