reshow

verb
to show again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈʃoʊ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈʃəʊ / (BrE)
to show something again, such as a film, program, or object

Examples

To reshow a film
The museum will reshow the famous painting during its anniversary exhibition.
After popular demand, the channel decided to reshow the documentary next month.
They plan to reshow the play for audiences who missed the first run.

Related Terms

  • antonym: premiere - to show or perform something for the first time
  • synonym: repeat - to do or show something again
  • synonym: replay - to play something again, especially a recording or broadcast
