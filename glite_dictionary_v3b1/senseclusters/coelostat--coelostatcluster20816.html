coelostat

noun
device for directing light into a telescope
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːloʊˌstæt / (AmE), / ˈsiːlɒstæt / (BrE)
an optical device with a movable and a fixed mirror, used to direct the light of a celestial object into a telescope as the object moves across the sky

Examples

A coelostat mirror
A coelostat uses a motor to continually adjust its mirror, compensating for Earth's rotation.
Modern solar telescopes often employ a coelostat to provide a steady image for detailed analysis.
The observatory's coelostat allowed astronomers to study the sun for hours without moving the telescope.
