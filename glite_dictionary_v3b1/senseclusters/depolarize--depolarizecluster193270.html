depolarize

verb
eliminate polarization (especially electrically)
Pronunciation: / diˈpɔləˌraɪz /; / diːˈpoʊləˌraɪz / (AmE), / diˈpɔːləraɪz /; / diːˈpəʊləraɪz / (BrE)
To eliminate or reduce the polarization of something, especially in the context of electrical charge, such as a cell membrane or an electrical device.

Examples

To depolarize a cell
Exposure to certain toxins can depolarize muscle fibers, affecting their function.
The technician used a special device to depolarize the capacitor.
When a nerve cell is stimulated, it will depolarize and generate an electrical impulse.
Certain drugs can depolarise neurons, affecting how signals are transmitted in the brain.
Researchers used electrical stimulation to depolarise the cardiac muscle.
The nerve cell membranes depolarise rapidly during the transmission of a signal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: depolarize - alternative spelling (American variant) of 'depolarise', meaning to eliminate polarization
  • antonym: polarise - cause to acquire polarity or polarization
  • antonym: polarize - to cause to become polar or more polar, especially electrically
