home guard

noun
volunteer defense unit for homeland
3.5 4.8
Pronunciation: /hoʊm gɑːrd/ (AmE), /həʊm gɑːd/ (BrE)
a volunteer military unit formed to protect the homeland while the regular army is engaged elsewhere, especially during wartime

Examples

The home guard unit
After the regular troops were deployed overseas, the home guard was responsible for local defense.
During World War II, the home guard played a crucial role in protecting rural communities.
My grandfather volunteered for the home guard when he was too old for regular service.
