crucial

adjective
extremely important; decisive
3.6 41/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàkruÀê É…ôl / (AmE), / ÀàkruÀê É…ôl / (BrE)
extremely important or decisive, especially for the outcome or success of something

Examples

A crucial moment
Accurate information is crucial when making important decisions.
Her support was crucial to the team's victory in the tournament.
It is crucial that we meet this deadline to secure the contract.
Her experience was crucial to the success of the project.
It's crucial that we arrive on time for the interview.
The evidence presented was crucial in reaching a verdict.
Choosing the right supplier is crucial for the success of our business.
Her testimony was crucial to the final verdict of the trial.
It is crucial that we finish this project before the deadline.
