existentialist philosophy

noun
philosophical movement on individual freedom and responsibility
4.2 8.2
Pronunciation: /eg.zɪ.ˈsten.ʃəl.ɪst fə.ˈlɑː.sə.fiː/ (AmE), /eg.zɪ.ˈsten.ʃəl.ɪst fə.ˈlɒ.sə.fiː/ (BrE)
A philosophical movement of the 20th century, primarily in Europe, asserting that individuals are entirely free and responsible for creating their own meaning and values.

Examples

Existentialist philosophy ideas
Courses on existentialist philosophy often discuss themes of freedom and personal responsibility.
Many artists were influenced by existentialist philosophy and explored ideas of meaning and self-creation.
Sartre played a key role in the development of existentialist philosophy during the 20th century.
