daylighting

noun
uncovering buried streams
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪˌlaɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdeɪˌlaɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of uncovering a buried stream, culvert, or tunnel so it is exposed to open air and can flow naturally

Examples

Daylighting a stream
The city invested in daylighting the old creek to improve the local environment.
Community groups supported the daylighting of the stream to create more green space.
After daylighting, the once-hidden river became a popular spot for walking and wildlife.

Related Terms

  • antonym: culverting - the process of enclosing a stream in a culvert
  • synonym: restoration - the act of returning something to its original condition
