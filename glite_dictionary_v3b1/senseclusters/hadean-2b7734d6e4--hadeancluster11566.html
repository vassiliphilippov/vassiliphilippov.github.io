Hadean

noun
the earliest eon in Earth's history
Pronunciation: / ˈheɪdiən / (AmE), / ˈheɪdiən / (BrE)
the earliest eon in Earth's history, from the formation of the planet about 4.6 billion years ago to about 4 billion years ago, before the appearance of the oldest known rocks or evidence of life

Examples

The Hadean eon
No rocks older than the Hadean eon have yet been discovered on Earth.
Scientists study zircon crystals to learn about the conditions on Earth during the Hadean.
The Hadean eon was characterized by intense meteorite bombardment and a molten surface.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Archean - the eon following the Hadean, marked by the formation of the Earth's crust
