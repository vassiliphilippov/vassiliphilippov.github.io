mute

adjective
unable to speak due to deafness
9.8 0.46/m
Pronunciation: / mjuËt / (AmE), / mjuËt / (BrE)
(dated, sometimes offensive) Unable to speak, especially as a result of hereditary deafness.

Examples

Mute child
He was born mute and learned to communicate using sign language.
In the past, children who were mute due to deafness often lacked educational support.
The school provided special resources for students who were mute from birth.

Synonyms

  • dumb
    9.3 1.90/m
    dumb is an older, sometimes offensive word for someone who cannot speak, while mute is a dated term often used for similar cases.
  • voiceless
    5.8 0.55/m
    voiceless describes people or groups who lack a voice, often in a figurative way, while mute refers specifically to inability to speak.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deaf - lacking the sense of hearing
  • synonym: speechless - unable to speak; without speech
