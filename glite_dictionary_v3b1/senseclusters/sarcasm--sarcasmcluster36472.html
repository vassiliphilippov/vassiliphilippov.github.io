The use of irony to mock or convey contempt; witty language used to insult or scorn.

Examples

Use of sarcasm
Her voice dripped with sarcasm as she congratulated him on his 'brilliant' idea.
The teacher's sarcasm made the students laugh, but also made her point clear.
Using sarcasm in emails can often be misunderstood and cause confusion.

Synonyms

  • sardonicism
    9.3 0.01/m
    sardonicism is a grim, mocking attitude that sounds cold and bitter, while sarcasm is often witty and sharply mocking.
  • mordacity
    9.9 0.00/m
    mordacity is a biting, sharply sarcastic manner often used in harsh speech, while sarcasm can be witty or playful.
  • snideness
    8.1 0.05/m
    snideness is a small, mean mocking tone used to insult quietly, while sarcasm often uses irony in a sharper, witty way.
  • causticity
    9.7 0.00/m
    causticity is a harsh, critical tone that burns words and feelings, while sarcasm specifically uses irony to mock.
