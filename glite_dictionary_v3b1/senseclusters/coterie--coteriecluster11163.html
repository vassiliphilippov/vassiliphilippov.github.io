coterie

noun
small exclusive group
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊtəˌri / (AmE), / ˈkəʊtəri / (BrE)
A small, exclusive group of people who share a common interest or purpose, often seen as secretive or selective.

Examples

A coterie of writers
A coterie of writers gathered in the café every Friday evening.
She found it difficult to break into the coterie that dominated local politics.
The decision was made by a coterie of influential business leaders.

Related Terms

  • synonym: circle - a group of people with shared interests
  • synonym: clique - a small, exclusive group of people
  • antonym: outsider - someone not belonging to a particular group or coterie
