free-flying

adjective
able to fly independently
Pronunciation: / ˈfriˌflaɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfriːˌflaɪɪŋ / (BrE)
able to fly freely or independently through the air, especially after being raised in captivity or as applied to birds and other flying animals

Examples

Free-flying birds
After rehabilitation, the free-flying parrots returned to their natural habitat.
The sanctuary specializes in raising free-flying falcons for release into the wild.
We watched a group of free-flying bats emerge from the cave at dusk.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flightless - unable to fly (applied to birds, insects, etc.)
