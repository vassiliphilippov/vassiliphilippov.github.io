demoralization

noun
undermining moral foundation of a belief or policy
1.4 6.5
Pronunciation: /dɪ.mɔːr.əl.ɪz.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /dɪ.mɒr.ə.laɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
The act of undermining or destroying the moral foundation or justification for a doctrine, policy, or belief.

Examples

Demoralization of values
Revelations of hypocrisy resulted in the demoralization of the religious group's core beliefs.
The constant criticism led to the demoralization of the party's ideology.
The policy changes aimed at the demoralization of outdated traditions.
The investigative report contributed to the demoralisation of the party's core beliefs.
The movement's main strategy was the demoralisation of old political dogmas.
Widespread demoralisation of the doctrine occurred after the ethics scandal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corruption - the process of becoming morally degraded or dishonest
  • synonym: demoralization - the act of undermining moral standards (US spelling)
  • antonym: reinforcement - the process of strengthening or supporting something
  • synonym: undermining - the act of weakening or subverting something, especially authority or morale
