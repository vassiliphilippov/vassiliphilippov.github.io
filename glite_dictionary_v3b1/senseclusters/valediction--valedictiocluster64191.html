valediction

noun
the act of saying farewell
Pronunciation: / ˌvælɪˈdɪkʃən / (AmE), / ˌvalɪˈdɪkʃən / (BrE)
a formal or ceremonial farewell, especially a speech given at a graduation or similar event

Examples

A valediction speech
He ended his letter with a heartfelt valediction to his colleagues.
Her brief valediction was both gracious and memorable.
The principal's valediction at the ceremony left many in tears.
He prepared his valediction for weeks to ensure it was both heartfelt and memorable.
Her valediction expressed gratitude and hope for her classmates' futures.
The ceremony ended with a moving valediction delivered by the top student.

Related Terms

  • synonym: farewell - an expression of good wishes when parting
  • synonym: valedictorian - the student who delivers the valediction at graduation
