raise

verb
articulate a vowel with higher tongue position
Pronunciation: / reɪz / (AmE), / reɪz / (BrE)
(phonetics) To pronounce a vowel with the tongue positioned closer to the roof of the mouth.

Examples

To raise a vowel
It is common for English learners to unintentionally raise their 'a' sounds when imitating American accents.
Linguists observed that younger speakers tend to raise certain vowel sounds in informal speech.
Some dialects raise the vowel in 'cat' so it sounds closer to 'ket'.
