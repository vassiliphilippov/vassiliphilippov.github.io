biological

noun
living-derived substance
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪəˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌbaɪəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
a substance derived from living organisms, used especially in medicine or agriculture to prevent or treat disease

Examples

A new biological
The new biological was approved to treat several types of cancer.
Farmers are turning to biologicals instead of traditional pesticides to protect their crops.
Researchers are developing innovative biologicals to fight antibiotic-resistant bacteria.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biologic - a drug or substance made from living organisms
  • antonym: chemical - a substance produced by or used in a chemical process
