saurischian dinosaur

noun
dinosaur with a crocodile-like pelvis
Pronunciation: / sɔːˈrɪskiən ˈdaɪnəˌsɔr / (AmE), / sɔːˈrɪskɪən ˈdaɪnəˌsɔː / (BrE)
any dinosaur belonging to the group characterized by a three-pronged pelvis similar to that of a crocodile, including both herbivorous and carnivorous species

Examples

Saurischian dinosaur group
Modern birds are actually descended from a branch of saurischian dinosaurs known as theropods.
Researchers recently discovered new fossils belonging to a previously unknown saurischian dinosaur.
The saurischian dinosaur group includes both the massive Brachiosaurus and the fierce Tyrannosaurus rex.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ornithischian dinosaur - dinosaur belonging to the group with a bird-like pelvis
