pity

verb
feel sympathy for someone’s suffering
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪti / (AmE), / ˈpɪti / (BrE)
to feel or show sympathy or compassion for someone who is suffering or in trouble

Examples

To pity a friend
I pitied him when I heard about his loss, but there was little I could do to help.
She couldn't help but pity the injured bird as it struggled to fly.
They pitied the homeless man, offering him food and warm clothing.
She stood by the window, pitying the struggling kitten outside.
He could not help pitying his friend after hearing about the accident.
Instead of pitying her, they offered support and encouragement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: empathize - to understand and share another person's feelings
  • antonym: ignore - to pay no attention to someone or something
  • synonym: sympathize - to feel or express compassion; to share in someone's feelings
