analemma

noun
sun's yearly path diagram
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌænəˈlɛmə / (AmE), / ˌænəˈlɛmə / (BrE)
a diagram showing the position of the sun in the sky at the same time each day over a year, often forming a figure-eight shape

Examples

The analemma diagram
The analemma is often printed on globes to show the sun's changing position throughout the year.
By tracking the sun's location at the same time each day, you can create your own analemma.
The unique figure-eight shape of the analemma helps explain why the length of days changes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: solar noon - the time of day when the sun is at its highest point in the sky
  • synonym: sundial - a device that tells the time of day by the position of the sun's shadow
