go around

verb
to be widely known or spread (of information)
2.2 2.3
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / ɡəʊ əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to circulate among people, such as information, gossip, or stories becoming widely known

Examples

Rumors go around
False information tends to go around on social media very fast.
The story quickly went around the whole school by lunch time.
There's a rumor going around that the company will be sold.
Gossip tends to go around quickly in a small town.
The cake was cut so there would be enough to go around for everyone at the party.
There weren’t enough invitations to go around, so some people had to be left out.

Related Terms

  • synonym: circulate - (of stories, information) to move around and be passed from person to person
  • synonym: go round - alternative (chiefly British) form of the phrasal verb meaning to circulate or be enough for everyone
  • synonym: spread - (of information) to become known by more people
