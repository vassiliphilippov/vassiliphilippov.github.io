Apollonian

adjective
calm and rational
Pronunciation: / əˌpɑːˈloʊniən / (AmE), / əˌpɒˈləʊniən / (BrE)
calm, rational, and balanced; showing harmony, order, and reason (like the Greek god Apollo)

Examples

Apollonian style
Her Apollonian temperament helped her remain calm during the crisis.
The painting's Apollonian qualities are seen in its symmetry and balance.
He admired the Apollonian order of classical architecture.
The sculpture's Apollonian beauty reflected perfect balance and calm.
Her approach to life was distinctly Apollonian, valuing logic and self-control above all.
In art history, the Apollonian ideal is often contrasted with the passionate Dionysian spirit.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Dionysian - emotional, passionate, and chaotic, especially as opposed to rational
  • synonym: orderly - organized and neat; not chaotic
  • synonym: rational - based on reason or logic
