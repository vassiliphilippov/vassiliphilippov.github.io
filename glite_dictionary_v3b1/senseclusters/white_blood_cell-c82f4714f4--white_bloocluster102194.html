white blood cell

noun
type of blood cell that fights infection
4.5
Pronunciation: / waɪt blʌd sɛl / (AmE), / waɪt blʌd sɛl / (BrE)
A type of blood cell that helps protect the body by fighting infections and foreign substances.

Examples

A white blood cell count
A blood test revealed that his white blood cell count was within the normal range.
Infections often cause the body's white blood cells to increase in number.
The doctor explained that a low white blood cell count could indicate a weakened immune system.

Related Terms

  • synonym: leukocyte - another term for white blood cell
  • antonym: red blood cell - a different type of blood cell that carries oxygen
