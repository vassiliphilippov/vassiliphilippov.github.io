metamorphous

adjective
relating to metamorphosis, especially of rocks
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˈmɔrfoʊs / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈmɔːfəʊs / (BrE)
Relating to or resulting from metamorphosis, especially of rocks changed in form or structure by heat and pressure.

Examples

Metamorphous rock
Marble is a classic example of a metamorphous rock formed from limestone.
The geologist explained the metamorphous changes that granite undergoes deep within the Earth.
The region is known for its abundance of metamorphous rock formations created by ancient tectonic activity.
