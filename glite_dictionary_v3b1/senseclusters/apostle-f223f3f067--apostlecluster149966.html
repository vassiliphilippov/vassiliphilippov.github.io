Apostle

noun
original disciple of Christ
6.3
Pronunciation: / əˈpɑːsəl / (AmE), / əˈpɒsl̩ / (BrE)
(in the New Testament) one of the original twelve disciples chosen by Jesus Christ to spread his teachings.

Examples

The Apostles of Jesus
Many churches are named in honor of the Apostles.
Peter was one of the most prominent Apostles in the early Christian church.
The Apostles spread the teachings of Jesus throughout the Roman Empire.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disciple - a follower or student of a teacher, leader, or philosopher
