plenitude

noun
state of fullness or abundance
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈplɛnɪˌtud / (AmE), / ˈplɛnɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
a condition or quality of being full or complete; an abundance

Examples

The plenitude of opportunities
He spoke of the plenitude of opportunities in the modern world.
She was grateful for the plenitude of resources available to help her research.
The garden yielded a plenitude of fruits and vegetables last summer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something
  • antonym: scarcity - a lack or shortage of something
