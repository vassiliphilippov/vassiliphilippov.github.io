beat down

verb
persuade to lower a price
Pronunciation: / bit daʊn /; / biːt daʊn / (AmE), / biːt daʊn / (BrE)
to persuade someone to lower the price of something through bargaining or negotiation

Examples

To beat down the price
After a long discussion, they beat down the landlord to a more reasonable rent.
He tried to beat down the car dealer on the final price but was only partly successful.
She managed to beat down the price by pointing out several flaws in the item.
After some tough negotiating, she managed to beat down the price to half its original value.
He always attempts to beat down the seller whenever he goes to the market.
I tried to beat down the cost of the used car, but the dealer wouldn't budge.

Related Terms

  • synonym: haggle - to argue about the price of something
  • antonym: raise - to increase the price or amount
