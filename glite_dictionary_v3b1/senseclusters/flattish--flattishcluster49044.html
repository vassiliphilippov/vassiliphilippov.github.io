flattish

adjective
slightly out of tune
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈflætɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈflætɪʃ / (BrE)
slightly flat in musical pitch or somewhat out of tune

Examples

Flattish singing
The singer's performance was a bit flattish in the final verse of the song.
If your notes sound flattish, try adjusting your breath support.
The choir director noticed a flattish tone during the rehearsal and asked them to listen more carefully.

  • antonym: sharp - above the correct musical pitch
