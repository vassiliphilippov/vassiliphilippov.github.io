putdownable

adjective
easy to stop reading; not compelling
7.4 7.3
Pronunciation: /ˈpʊt.daʊ.nə.bəl/ (AmE), /ˈpʊt.daʊ.nə.bəl/ (BrE)
(of a book or other reading material) easy to stop reading because it is not interesting or poorly written.

Examples

A putdownable book
After a string of bestsellers, her latest book proved disappointingly putdownable.
He described the biography as entirely putdownable and not worth the hype.
The novel was so putdownable that I struggled to finish even a few chapters.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unputdownable - (of a book) so interesting or exciting that you cannot stop reading it
