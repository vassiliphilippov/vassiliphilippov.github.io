wangle

verb
obtain by clever or devious means
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈwaŋɡl /; / ˈwaŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
to achieve or obtain something by clever, indirect, or dishonest methods

Examples

To wangle a job
He managed to wangle an invitation to the exclusive party.
She tried to wangle a day off work without losing pay.
They wangled their way into the concert, even though it was sold out.
He managed to wangle an invitation to the exclusive party through his connections.
She tried to wangle a better grade by persuading the teacher to review her test.
The accountant was caught attempting to wangle the company’s financial reports.

  • antonym: honestly - in a truthful or sincere way
  • synonym: manipulate - to control or influence in a clever or unscrupulous way
  • antonym: straighten - to make or become honest or truthful, stop being crooked
