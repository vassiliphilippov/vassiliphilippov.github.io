adulation

noun
excessive or insincere praise
Pronunciation: / ˌædʒəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌadjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
excessive or insincere praise, especially when given to flatter or win favor

Examples

Excessive adulation
Her speech was met with adulation that seemed more flattery than genuine appreciation.
Politicians often crave adulation, but true leaders remain humble.
The actor was uncomfortable with the constant adulation he received from his fans.

Related Terms

  • antonym: criticism - the expression of disapproval or finding fault
  • synonym: praise - the expression of approval or admiration
