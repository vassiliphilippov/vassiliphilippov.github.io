heroics

noun
extravagant or melodramatic actions
2.2
Pronunciation: / hɪˈroʊɪks / (AmE), / hɪˈrəʊɪks / (BrE)
ostentatious, exaggerated, or melodramatic actions or speech, often intended to impress others or show bravery

Examples

Empty heroics
She ignored all the heroics and simply focused on getting the job done.
The actor's off-screen heroics were almost as dramatic as his movie roles.
We don't need any last-minute heroics to solve this problem.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bravado - a show of boldness intended to impress or intimidate
  • antonym: modesty - the quality of not being boastful or showing off
