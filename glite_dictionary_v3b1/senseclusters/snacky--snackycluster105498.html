snacky

adjective
wanting snacks
Pronunciation: / ˈsnæki / (AmE), / ˈsnæki / (BrE)
feeling like eating snacks or often wanting small amounts of food between meals

Examples

Feeling snacky
I always feel snacky in the afternoon and look for something to nibble on.
After dinner, she still felt a bit snacky and grabbed some fruit.
When watching movies, he tends to get snacky and eats popcorn.
She always feels snacky in the afternoon and grabs a bag of chips.
If you are feeling snacky, try eating some fruit instead of candy.
His snacky habits make it hard for him to sit down for a full meal.

Related Terms

  • antonym: full - having eaten enough food; not wanting more
  • degree higher: hungry - feeling the need or desire to eat food
  • synonym: peckish - slightly hungry or wanting to eat
