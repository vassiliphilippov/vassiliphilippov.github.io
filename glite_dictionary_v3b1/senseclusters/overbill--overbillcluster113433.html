overbill

verb
to charge too much
6.1
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ˈbɪl/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ˈbɪl/ (BrE)
to charge someone more than the correct or agreed amount for goods or services

Examples

To overbill a customer
The hotel accidentally overbilled us for two extra nights we did not stay.
If you think your account was overbilled, contact customer service for a correction.
The company promised to refund customers who were overbilled due to a system error.
The contractor tried to overbill the client for materials that were never used.
Auditors discovered that the company had overbilled several customers over the past year.
If you think your doctor has overbilled you, contact your insurance provider immediately.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overcharge - to ask for too much money for something
  • antonym: underbill - to charge less than the correct or agreed amount
  • antonym: undercharge - to ask for too little money for something
