come

verb
to originate; arise from a source
Pronunciation: / kʌm / (AmE), / kʌm / (BrE)
to originate from or be produced by a particular source or place

Examples

Come from Italy
Great inventions often come from unexpected ideas.
Success comes from hard work and determination.
The best honey comes from local bees.
He came from a family of musicians.
Many international students come from Asia to study here.
She comes from a small town in France.

Related Terms

  • antonym: depart - to leave or move away from a place
  • synonym: hail - to be or have been born in a particular place
  • synonym: originate - to have a specified beginning or source
  • synonym: result - to happen as an outcome of something
