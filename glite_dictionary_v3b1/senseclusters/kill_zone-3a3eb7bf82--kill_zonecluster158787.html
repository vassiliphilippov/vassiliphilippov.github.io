kill zone

noun
area with many battlefield fatalities
1.0 6.7
Pronunciation: /kɪl zoʊn/ (AmE), /kɪl zəʊn/ (BrE)
an area where people are at high risk of being killed, especially due to concentrated attacks or threats (in real battles or video games)

Examples

A kill zone area
Commanders try to avoid sending troops directly into a known kill zone.
Heavy casualties were reported in the kill zone after the ambush.
The soldiers cautiously advanced through the kill zone, aware of hidden dangers.
The final kill zone forced the remaining players into close combat near the bridge.
Streamers warned viewers about a new kill zone that always forms around the tall radio tower.
Game designers often avoid having an obvious kill zone to keep matches fair and strategic.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hot zone - an area of heightened danger or combat; similar term used in both military and gaming contexts
  • synonym: killing field - an area where many people have died, especially in battle or mass executions
  • antonym: safe zone - an area where players are protected from harm or excluded from combat; often the opposite of a kill zone in games
