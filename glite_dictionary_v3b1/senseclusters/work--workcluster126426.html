work

verb
make progress through or along a path
Pronunciation: / wɝk / (AmE), / wɜːk / (BrE)
to make progress, move through, or achieve something gradually, often with effort

Examples

To work your way up
He worked his way up the crowded aisle toward the exit.
She worked her way through the dense undergrowth, searching for the trail.
They slowly worked through the traffic jam, inching forward one car at a time.
It took him years to work through his feelings after the breakup.
She slowly worked her way up from assistant to manager.
You need to work your way through the entire textbook before the exam.

Related Terms

  • synonym: advance - to move forward or make progress, especially gradually
  • antonym: backtrack - to go back over the path by which one has come
  • synonym: progress - to move forward or develop to a higher stage
  • antonym: struggle - to make forceful efforts to move or attain something despite difficulties
