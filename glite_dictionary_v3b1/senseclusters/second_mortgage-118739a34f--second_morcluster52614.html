second mortgage

noun
a subordinate mortgage on a property
6.3 4.0
Pronunciation: /ˈsek.ənd ˈmɔːr.gɪdʒ/ /; / /ˈsek.ənd ˈmɔːr.gədʒ/ (AmE), /ˈsek.ənd ˈmɔː.gɪdʒ/ /; / /ˈsek.ənd ˈmɔː.gədʒ/ (BrE)
A mortgage taken out on a property that already has a first mortgage, subordinate to the original loan.

Examples

A second mortgage loan
After renovating the house, they took out a second mortgage to pay for the kitchen remodel.
Many homeowners use a second mortgage to consolidate debt or fund major expenses.
The bank approved his application for a second mortgage despite the outstanding loan on the property.
