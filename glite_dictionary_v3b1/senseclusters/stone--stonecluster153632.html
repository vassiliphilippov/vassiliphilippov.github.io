stone

noun
hard, solid mineral material
Pronunciation: / stoʊn / (AmE), / stəʊn / (BrE)
a hard, solid mineral material, often used for building, or a small piece of such material

Examples

A stone wall
Ancient buildings were often constructed using large blocks of stone.
She collected a small stone from the riverbed as a souvenir.
The new path was paved with stone, making it very durable.
A commemorative stone was placed at the entrance of the park.
Each stone in the wall had been precisely measured and cut.
The mason selected a large stone for the foundation of the house.
A small stone rolled down the hill and landed near my feet.
He picked up a stone and skipped it across the lake.
The path was covered in loose stones that made walking difficult.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rock - the solid mineral material forming part of the surface of the earth
