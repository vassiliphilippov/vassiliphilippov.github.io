sterility

noun
state of being free from pathogens
Pronunciation: / stəˈrɪləti / (AmE), / stəˈrɪləti / (BrE)
the state or condition of being free from living pathogenic microorganisms; complete absence of infectious agents

Examples

Surgical sterility
A breach in sterility could lead to a serious infection in the patient.
Laboratories use autoclaves to ensure the sterility of glassware and instruments.
The surgical equipment must maintain sterility throughout the procedure.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contamination - the presence of unwanted microorganisms or substances
  • degree lower: disinfection - the process of eliminating most pathogenic microorganisms
