microhardness

noun
hardness on small scale
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪkroʊˈhɑrdnəs / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəʊˈhɑːdnəs / (BrE)
the hardness of a material measured on a microscopic or very small scale, often using specialized instruments

Examples

Microhardness test
Scientists measured the microhardness of the crystal to determine its resistance to scratching.
The microhardness test revealed differences in the surface properties of the treated metals.
Engineers use microhardness values to compare the durability of various thin coatings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hardness - the quality of being hard or resistant to scratching
  • antonym: macrohardness - the hardness of a material measured on a larger scale
