ketonemia

noun
high level of ketones in blood
9.9 8.2
Pronunciation: /kiː.toʊ.niː.ˈmiː.ə/ /; / /kiː.toʊ.ə.ˈmiː.ə/ (AmE), /kiː.təʊ.niː.ˈmiː.ə/ /; / /kiː.təʊ.ə.ˈmiː.ə/ (BrE)
A medical condition in which there is an abnormally high concentration of ketone bodies in the blood, typically seen in diabetes mellitus or prolonged fasting.

Examples

High ketonemia levels
Ketonemia can be a serious complication in people with poorly controlled diabetes.
A blood test confirmed that the patient was suffering from ketonemia.
During fasting, mild ketonemia may occur as the body breaks down fat for energy.

  • synonym: ketosis - a condition with elevated ketone bodies in tissues
