dystonic

adjective
in conflict with self-image
9.9
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈtɑnɪk / (AmE), / dɪsˈtɒnɪk / (BrE)
describing thoughts, impulses, or behaviors that are in conflict with a person's self-image or values and cause distress

Examples

Dystonic thoughts
The patient described his obsessive thoughts as dystonic and very distressing.
Many people with anxiety experience dystonic feelings that do not match their true character.
Her therapist explained that dystonic impulses often cause significant emotional discomfort.

Related Terms

  • antonym: egosyntonic - consistent with a person's self-image or values
