moony

adjective
dreamy or absent-minded
7.3 0.05/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàmuÀêni / (AmE), / ÀàmuÀêni / (BrE)
having a dreamy, absent-minded, or distracted expression or mood

Examples

Moony look
His moony manner made it clear he wasn't paying attention to the meeting.
On warm summer nights, he would often become moony and drift into his own world.
She had a moony look on her face as she stared out the window, lost in thought.

Synonyms

  • dreamy
    3.3 1.26/m
    dreamy feels vague and lost in thought, while moony more often describes a distracted, quietly absent-minded mood.
  • moonish
    9.3 0.00/m
    moonish suggests romantic, fanciful daydreams often seen in someone who seems lost in their thoughts, while moony is a more general distracted, absent-minded expression.
  • moonstruck
    7.9 0.04/m
    moonstruck feels wildly romantic or slightly crazy, while moony means simply dreamy or quietly distracted by one's thoughts.
  • daydreamy
    5.2 0.01/m
    daydreamy describes a soft, hazy, dreamlike scene or look, while moony describes a person who is absent-minded and distracted.
