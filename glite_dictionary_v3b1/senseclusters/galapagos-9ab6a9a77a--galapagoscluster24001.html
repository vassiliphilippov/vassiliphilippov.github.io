Galapagos

proper noun
Pacific islands known for unique wildlife
Pronunciation: / ɡəˈlæpəɡoʊs / (AmE), / ɡəˈlæpəɡɒs / (BrE)
a group of volcanic islands in the Pacific Ocean, off the coast of Ecuador, famous for their unique animal life and significance in the study of evolution

Examples

Galapagos Islands
Charles Darwin developed his theory of evolution after visiting the Galapagos Islands.
Many scientists visit the Galapagos each year to study its unique species.
The Galapagos are famous for their giant tortoises and marine iguanas.
