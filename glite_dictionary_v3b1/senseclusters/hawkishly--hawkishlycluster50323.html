hawkishly

adverb
in a strict policy manner
Pronunciation: / ˈhɔːkɪʃli / (AmE), / ˈhɔːkɪʃli / (BrE)
in a way that strongly supports strict or aggressive policies, especially favoring higher interest rates to control inflation

Examples

To act hawkishly on rates
The finance minister spoke hawkishly about the need to raise interest rates to fight inflation.
Investors reacted nervously after the central bank chair commented hawkishly on future monetary policy.
Analysts noted that the committee acted hawkishly by prioritizing inflation control over economic growth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aggressively - in a forceful or assertive manner
  • antonym: dovishly - in a way that favors more relaxed or lenient policies
