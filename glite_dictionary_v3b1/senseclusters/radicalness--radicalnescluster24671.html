radicalness

noun
quality of being extreme
Pronunciation: / ˈrædɪkəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈrædɪkəlnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being extreme, very different from tradition, or favoring major or fundamental change

Examples

Some critics questioned the radicalness of the proposed reforms, fearing they went too far.
His radicalness made him a controversial figure in political debates.
The radicalness of the new policy shocked many members of the organization.
Some admired the radicalness of her approach to solving old problems.
The radicalness in his artwork set him apart from other painters of his time.
The radicalness of his proposals surprised even his closest supporters.
Some admired her radicalness, while others thought her ideas were too extreme.
The party's radicalness attracted young voters eager for change.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conservatism - the tendency to resist change and keep things as they are
  • synonym: extremeness - the state of being very severe or intense
  • synonym: extremism - the holding of extreme views or actions
  • antonym: moderation - the quality of being moderate or avoiding extremes
  • synonym: radicalism - the beliefs or actions of people who advocate for major reforms
