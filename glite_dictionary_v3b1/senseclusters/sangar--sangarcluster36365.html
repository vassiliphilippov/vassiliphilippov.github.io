sangar

noun
stone shelter or wall
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsæŋɡɑr / (AmE), / ˈsæŋɡɑː / (BrE)
a small, temporary shelter or low wall made of stones or similar materials, built for protection (such as from wind or danger)

Examples

A sangar shelter
The shepherds took cover behind a sangar when the wind grew stronger on the hillside.
Climbers rested in a small sangar they found near the summit.
We built a rough sangar from loose stones to shield ourselves from the cold breeze.
The soldiers quickly built a sangar to protect themselves from enemy fire.
During the night, they took turns keeping watch from the sangar on the hillside.
A makeshift sangar provided shelter for the patrol in the exposed area.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bunker - a reinforced underground shelter used for protection
  • synonym: shelter - a place giving protection from bad weather or danger
  • synonym: trench - a long, narrow ditch used for military defense
  • synonym: windbreak - a structure that protects from the wind
