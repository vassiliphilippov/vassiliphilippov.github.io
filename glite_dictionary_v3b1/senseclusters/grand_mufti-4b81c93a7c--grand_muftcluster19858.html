grand mufti

noun
chief Muslim legal authority
Pronunciation: / ɡrænd ˈmʌfti / (AmE), / ɡrænd ˈmʌfti / (BrE)
the chief Islamic legal authority in a particular country or district, often serving as the highest interpreter of religious law.

Examples

The grand mufti speaks
After consulting with the grand mufti, the government decided to revise its laws.
He sought the guidance of the grand mufti on matters of religious importance.
The grand mufti issued a new religious ruling regarding the observance of the holiday.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: mufti - an Islamic jurist qualified to issue legal opinions or fatwas
