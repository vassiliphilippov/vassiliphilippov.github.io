pre-exposure

noun
prior exposure
Pronunciation: / ˌpriː ɪkˈspoʊʒər / (AmE), / ˌpriː ɪkˈspəʊʒə / (BrE)
the state or condition of being exposed to something before a particular event, risk, or potential harm

Examples

Pre-exposure vaccination
The doctor recommended pre-exposure vaccination for people traveling to high-risk areas.
Researchers studied the effects of pre-exposure to allergens in young children.
Some medications are given as pre-exposure prophylaxis to prevent infection.

Related Terms

  • antonym: post-exposure - exposure occurring after a specific event or risk
