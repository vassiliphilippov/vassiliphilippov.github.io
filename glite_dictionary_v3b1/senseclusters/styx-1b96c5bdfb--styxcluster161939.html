Styx

proper noun
mythical river in Greek underworld
9.9
Pronunciation: /stɪks/ (AmE), /stɪks/ (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the principal river in the underworld, across which souls of the dead were ferried by Charon.

Examples

The Styx river
According to legend, souls must cross the Styx to enter the realm of the dead.
Charon is the boatman who ferries the dead across the Styx.
Many poets have referenced the Styx as a symbol of the boundary between life and death.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Lethe - another river in the Greek underworld whose water causes forgetfulness
