disguise

noun
clothing/appearance to hide identity
2.9
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈɡaɪz / (AmE), / dɪsˈɡaɪz / (BrE)
something (such as clothing or a way of behaving) or the act of changing appearance to hide who you are

Examples

A clever disguise
His clever disguise fooled everyone at the masquerade party.
She wore a disguise to avoid being recognized by the press.
The spy donned a disguise before meeting his contact in the city.
For Halloween, she wore a vampire disguise that fooled everyone.
He escaped the city in disguise, avoiding recognition at every checkpoint.
The spy relied on a clever disguise to attend the meeting unnoticed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: camouflage - the act of hiding by blending in with surroundings
  • synonym: costume - clothing worn to create a particular appearance, especially in theatre or celebrations
  • antonym: reveal - to make something known openly or publicly
