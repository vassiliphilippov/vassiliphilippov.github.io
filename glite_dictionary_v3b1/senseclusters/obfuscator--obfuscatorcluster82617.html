obfuscator

noun
one who makes unclear
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːbfəˌskeɪtər /; / ˈɑbfəˌskeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɒbfʌskeɪtə / (BrE)
a person or tool that makes something unclear or difficult to understand, especially intentionally (often used for making computer code hard to read)

Examples

Code obfuscator
The obfuscator in the software made the original code almost impossible to read.
As an obfuscator of facts, he often left his audience more confused than informed.
Some writers act as an obfuscator by using overly complex language in their articles.
The programmer used an obfuscator to make the source code harder to reverse engineer.
An obfuscator can help protect intellectual property in software development.
Some people act as an obfuscator by making simple topics seem overly complex.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clarifier - someone or something that makes things clear
  • synonym: confuser - someone or something that causes confusion
  • synonym: scrambler - a device or program that makes information hard to read or understand
