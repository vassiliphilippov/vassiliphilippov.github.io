express trust

noun
deliberately created trust (legal)
6.6 5.6
Pronunciation: /ɪk.ˈspres trʌst/ /; / /ɛk.ˈspres trʌst/ (AmE), /ɪk.ˈspres trʌst/ /; / /ɛk.ˈspres trʌst/ (BrE)
a trust that is intentionally created by the parties involved, usually set out in written documentation

Examples

An express trust document
An express trust must be clearly documented to ensure the settlor’s intentions are carried out.
The court upheld the terms of the express trust as they were explicitly stated in the deed.
The will established an express trust for the benefit of the grandchildren.

Related Terms

  • antonym: implied trust - a trust created by operation of law from the conduct or circumstances of the parties, not intent
