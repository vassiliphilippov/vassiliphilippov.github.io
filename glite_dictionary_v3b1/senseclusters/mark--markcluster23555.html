mark

verb
insert punctuation marks into text
6.2
Pronunciation: / mɑrk / (AmE), / mɑːk / (BrE)
to use a symbol, sign, or notation to identify, distinguish, or indicate something (including punctuation, labels, or company marks)

Examples

To mark the text
Before publishing, the editor will mark the text with the appropriate punctuation.
She carefully marked the essay, adding commas and full stops where needed.
You should always mark your sentences clearly so that readers can understand your meaning.
A ceremony marked the opening of the new bridge.
A tall fence marks the end of the property.
These lines mark the official boundary between the two countries.
All the moving boxes were marked with colored stickers for easy sorting.
Please mark each test tube with the correct chemical name.
The bottles are marked with expiration dates to ensure freshness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: designate - to mark or point out; to specify
  • antonym: erase - to remove or eliminate a mark or indication
  • synonym: indicate - to point out or show
  • synonym: label - to attach a label to; to mark for identification
  • synonym: tag - attach a tag to something as identification
