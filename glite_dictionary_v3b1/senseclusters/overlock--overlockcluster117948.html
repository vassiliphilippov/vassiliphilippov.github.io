overlock

noun
edge-securing stitch
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvərlɑk / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəlɒk / (BrE)
a stitch or seam sewn over the edge of fabric to prevent it from fraying or unraveling

Examples

An overlock stitch
The tailor used an overlock to finish the raw edges of the dress.
An overlock is essential for making sure the fabric does not unravel after washing.
She examined the overlock on the inside seams to check the garment's quality.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hem - a folded and sewn edge of fabric to prevent fraying
  • synonym: serge - to sew with an overlock or similar stitch to prevent fraying
