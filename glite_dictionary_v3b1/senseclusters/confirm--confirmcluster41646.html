confirm

verb
establish with new evidence
1.8 62/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàf…úrm /; / k…ônÀàf…ùÀêm / (AmE), / k…ônÀàf…úÀêm / (BrE)
to establish the truth or validity of something; to make something certain by providing evidence or facts

Examples

To confirm the facts
I need one more source to confirm this information.
New research confirms the effectiveness of the treatment.
The witness's testimony confirmed the suspect's alibi.
Her success confirmed her belief that hard work pays off.
The new evidence confirmed the police's suspicions about the suspect.
The results of the experiment confirmed the scientist's initial hypothesis.

Synonyms

  • verify
    3.5 21/m
    verify checks facts or data carefully, while confirm makes something certain by giving clear evidence or proof.
  • substantiate
    7.2 3.59/m
    substantiate provides concrete evidence to support a claim, while confirm is a general act of making something certain.
  • corroborate
    9.0 4.84/m
    corroborate adds extra evidence or testimony to support an idea, while confirm simply makes that idea appear true.
  • prove
    3.1 156/m
    prove shows something is true by strong argument or demonstration, while confirm can be a simpler check or agreement.
  • establish
    4.1 29/m
    establish demonstrates truth through evidence or reasoning in a formal way, while confirm often means to make something certain.
  • validate
    3.9 10/m
    validate makes an idea or process officially acceptable or correct, while confirm means to show or make something certain.
  • bear out
    4.7 0.55/m
    bear out supports or confirms facts with evidence or authority, while confirm is the general act of making something certain.
