memorialization

noun
commemoration ceremony or act
5.8
Pronunciation: / məˌmɔriələˈzeɪʃən /; / məˌmɔɹiəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / mɪˌmɔːriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / mɪˌmɔːrɪəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of commemorating someone or something, especially through a ceremony, a formal event, or the creation of a monument

Examples

The memorialization ceremony
His painting served as a lasting memorialization of his grandfather's achievements.
The annual memorialization included speeches, music, and the laying of wreaths.
The community gathered for a memorialization of the fallen soldiers.
His speech was a moving part of the memorialisation for those who lost their lives in the disaster.
Public art has often played a role in the memorialisation of historical events.
The annual memorialisation of the fallen soldiers brings the community together each November.
