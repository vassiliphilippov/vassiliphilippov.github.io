beaconing

noun
sending regular signals
Pronunciation: / ˈbiːkənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbiːkənɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of sending regular signals to indicate presence, status, or availability, especially in computer networks

Examples

Bluetooth beaconing
Bluetooth devices use beaconing to announce their availability to nearby smartphones.
The network administrator monitored beaconing to ensure all devices were still connected.
Frequent beaconing can help track the location of equipment within a large building.

Related Terms

  • synonym: broadcasting - sending information to multiple recipients at once
  • synonym: signaling - sending a signal to communicate information
