fanal

noun
archaic lantern
9.9
Pronunciation: / fəˈnæl / (AmE), / fəˈnɑːl / (BrE)
an archaic or literary term for a lantern or portable lamp, especially one used in processions or to mark a place

Examples

A fanal lantern
The mourners carried a fanal to light their way through the night.
In the old tale, a single fanal marked the entrance to the hidden cave.
During the festival, each child held a small fanal as they walked in the procession.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lantern - a portable light with a protective case
  • synonym: torch - a portable light source, typically a stick with a flame
