shake

verb
move or vibrate rapidly
1.0 50/m

to move or vibrate rapidly, or to cause something to move back and forth or up and down (including trembling, shaking objects, or causing something to come out by shaking)

Examples

To shake the table
My hands would shake whenever I had to give a speech.
The ground shook violently during the earthquake.
The table began to shake as the train passed by.
She tried to shake the dust off the rug by hitting it against the wall.
The earthquake caused the entire house to shake violently.
The wind made the branches shake back and forth.
He was so nervous that his hands began to shake during the interview.
The ground will shake if there is an earthquake nearby.
When she heard the news, she couldn't help but shake with emotion.

Synonyms

    vibrate moves quickly back and forth in small repeated waves, while shake means moving or causing movement in many ways.
  • agitate
    4.1 0.72/m
    agitate means to stir or move a liquid by shaking, while shake covers moving or causing motion in many objects.
  • quiver
    4.5 5.34/m
    quiver trembles with small, quick movements often in living things, while shake is a general move or cause to move.
  • shimmy
    7.4 0.18/m
    shimmy is a quick, slight shaking often used in dance or style, while shake is any moving or vibrating action.
  • wobble
    3.8 3.50/m
    wobble moves or shakes unsteadily from side to side, while shake can be quicker or stronger and used in many situations.
  • judder
    8.4 0.05/m
    judder shakes or vibrates intensely and often loudly, usually in machines or vehicles, while shake covers many speeds and objects.
  • rock
    1.0 16/m
    rock moves gently back and forth to calm or support, while shake often means quicker or more forceful movement.
