nodding acquaintance

noun
a person known only slightly
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑdɪŋ əˈkweɪntəns / (AmE), / ˈnɒdɪŋ əˈkweɪntəns / (BrE)
a person whom one knows only slightly and with whom one exchanges minimal interaction, such as a nod of recognition

Examples

Have a nodding acquaintance
Although we worked in the same company, we were merely nodding acquaintances.
At the party, I recognized a nodding acquaintance from my evening class.
She greeted her neighbor, who was only a nodding acquaintance after all these years.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: acquaintance - a person one knows slightly but who is not a close friend
  • degree lower: stranger - a person whom one does not know at all
