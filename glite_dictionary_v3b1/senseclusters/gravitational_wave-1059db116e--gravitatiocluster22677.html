gravitational wave

noun
wave in space-time; propagates gravity
Pronunciation: / ɡrəˌveɪʃənəl weɪv / (AmE), / ɡravɪˈteɪʃənəl weɪv / (BrE)
A ripple in the fabric of space-time caused by some of the most violent and energetic processes in the universe, theorized by Einstein's general relativity and observed traveling at the speed of light.

Examples

A gravitational wave ripple
A gravitational wave can pass through matter without being significantly absorbed or scattered.
Scientists detected a gravitational wave from the collision of two black holes billions of light-years away.
The discovery of the gravitational wave confirmed a major prediction of Einstein's general theory of relativity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gravity - the force that attracts objects toward the center of the earth, or toward any other physical body having mass
