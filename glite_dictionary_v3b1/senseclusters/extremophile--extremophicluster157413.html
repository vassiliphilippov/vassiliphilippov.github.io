extremophile

noun
organism in harsh environments
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstriːməˌfaɪl / (AmE), / ɪkˈstriːməˌfaɪl / (BrE)
an organism that lives and thrives in extremely harsh or hostile environments, such as very hot, cold, acidic, or salty places

Examples

A heat-loving extremophile
Some extremophiles can survive in boiling hot springs where most life cannot exist.
Researchers discovered new extremophiles living deep under the Antarctic ice.
The study of extremophiles helps scientists understand the possibilities of life on other planets.

Related Terms

  • synonym: microbe - a microscopic organism, such as a bacterium
  • synonym: organism - a living thing, such as an animal, plant, or microbe
