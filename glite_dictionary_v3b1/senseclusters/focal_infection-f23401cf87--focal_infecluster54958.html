focal infection

noun
localized infection causing symptoms elsewhere
9.0 6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊkəl ɪnˈfɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˈfəʊkəl ɪnˈfɛkʃən / (BrE)
A bacterial infection localized to a specific organ or region of the body, especially one that also produces symptoms in other parts of the body.

Examples

A focal infection site
Research has shown that a focal infection may contribute to chronic health problems.
The doctor suspected the patient’s arthritis was triggered by a focal infection in the tonsils.
Untreated dental abscesses can sometimes lead to a focal infection that affects other organs.
