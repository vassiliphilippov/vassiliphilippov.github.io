ascon

noun
basic sponge structure
Pronunciation: / ˈæskən / (AmE), / ˈæskɒn / (BrE)
a simple sponge body structure with a tubular shape and a single central cavity

Examples

The ascon sponge
The ascon type is considered the simplest form of sponge anatomy.
Scientists observed that the ascon structure allows water to flow directly through the sponge.
Among sponges, the ascon body plan is found only in a few primitive species.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: leucon - the most complex sponge body structure with many chambers
  • degree higher: sycon - a more complex sponge body structure with folded walls
