symploce

noun
rhetorical repetition at start and end of clauses
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪmpləsi / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpləsi / (BrE)
A rhetorical device in which a word or phrase is repeated at the beginning and another at the end of successive clauses or sentences, combining anaphora and epistrophe.

Examples

Symploce in speech
The politician's speech demonstrated symploce by beginning and ending each clause with the same powerful words.
Understanding symploce helps students analyze persuasive techniques in texts.
Writers often use symploce to create emphasis and rhythm in their arguments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anaphora - repetition of a word or phrase at the beginning of successive clauses
  • synonym: epistrophe - repetition of a word or phrase at the end of successive clauses
