shroud-waving

noun
excessively dramatic display for effect or gain
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈʃraʊdˌweɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃraʊdˌweɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
Displaying excessive grief, outrage, or dramatic emotion, often to gain political advantage or manipulate public sentiment.

Examples

Shroud-waving in politics
Accusations of shroud-waving often arise in times of national tragedy.
Her constant shroud-waving eventually undermined her credibility with the public.
The senator's shroud-waving during the debate was criticized by both opponents and the media.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emotional blackmail - using strong emotions to manipulate or pressure others
  • synonym: grandstanding - behaving in a way that seeks to attract applause or favorable attention
