wing

noun
a limb or organ for flying
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /wɪŋ/ (AmE), /wɪŋ/ (BrE)
the part of a bird, insect, or animal used for flying, or the corresponding part when cooked and eaten as food

Examples

A bird's wing
A butterfly's delicate wings shimmered with color in the sunlight.
Bats have fleshy wings that allow them to fly at night.
The eagle spread its wing and soared into the sky.
He prefers drumsticks, but I always take the wing when we have roast chicken.
She ordered a plate of spicy chicken wings at the restaurant.
The bird injured its wing and couldn't fly anymore.

  • antonym: fin - an appendage used for movement in water in fish and aquatic animals
