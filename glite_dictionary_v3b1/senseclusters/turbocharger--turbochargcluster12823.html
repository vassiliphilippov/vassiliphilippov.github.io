turbocharger

noun
powerful accelerator
Pronunciation: / ˈtɜrboʊˌtʃɑrdʒər / (AmE), / ˈtɜːbəʊˌtʃɑːdʒə / (BrE)
something that greatly increases the power, speed, or effectiveness of an activity or process, especially in a figurative sense

Examples

Turbocharger for growth
The new software acted as a turbocharger for the company's productivity.
Her enthusiasm was the turbocharger that pushed the team to finish the project early.
The policy reforms served as a turbocharger for economic growth in the region.

Related Terms

  • synonym: catalyst - something that causes a significant change or action
  • antonym: impediment - something that slows down or prevents progress
