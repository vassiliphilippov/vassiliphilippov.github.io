hark back

verb
refer back to or recall something earlier
Pronunciation: / hɑrk bæk / (AmE), / hɑːk bæk / (BrE)
to refer to or recall something from an earlier time; to be reminiscent of or originate from a previous period or event

Examples

To hark back to history
Her style of painting harks back to the Impressionist movement.
The architect’s design harked back to classical Roman buildings.
Whenever he tells that story, he harks back to his childhood summers in the countryside.

Related Terms

  • synonym: refer back - to mention or allude to something that happened before
