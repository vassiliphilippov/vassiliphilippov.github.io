capacitor microphone

noun
microphone using a capacitor to detect sound
2.0 5.5
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈpæs.ə.tər ˈmaɪ.krə.foʊn/ /; / /kə.ˈpæs.ɪ.tər ˈmaɪ.krə.foʊn/ (AmE), /kə.ˈpæs.ɪ.tə ˈmaɪ.krə.fəʊn/ /; / /kə.ˈpæs.ə.tə ˈmaɪ.krə.fəʊn/ (BrE)
A microphone that uses a capacitor, with one fixed plate and a movable diaphragm, to convert sound waves into electrical signals.

Examples

A capacitor microphone
A capacitor microphone requires a power source to maintain the electrical charge between its plates.
Many broadcasters rely on a capacitor microphone for clear and accurate sound reproduction.
The studio engineer preferred to use a capacitor microphone for recording vocals because of its sensitivity to detail.
