Hinayana

noun
offensive term for early conservative Buddhism
9.9
Pronunciation: / hiːnəˈjɑːnə / (AmE), / hɪnəˈjɑːnə / (BrE)
an offensive term for the early, conservative branch of Buddhism, often identified with Theravada; no longer used by practitioners and considered disrespectful

Examples

The term Hinayana
Many scholars prefer to use 'early Buddhism' instead of Hinayana to avoid offense.
The term Hinayana is now considered pejorative and is avoided in academic writing.
Theravada Buddhism, sometimes labeled Hinayana by its critics, is practiced mainly in Sri Lanka and Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Theravada - the earliest surviving Buddhist school, emphasizing personal enlightenment
