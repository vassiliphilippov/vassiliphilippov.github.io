hold

noun
state of temporary confinement
1.3 1.7
Pronunciation: /hoʊld/ (AmE), /həʊld/ (BrE)
a period of time when something is paused, suspended, or delayed

Examples

On hold status
Due to a security threat, all passengers were kept on hold for several hours.
Journalists learned that the activist was in police hold pending investigation.
The suspect was taken into hold until the hearing could be arranged.
Due to the technical issue, the project was put on a hold until further notice.
The judge called for a hold in proceedings to review the new evidence.
There was a brief hold during the meeting while they fixed the microphone.

Related Terms

  • synonym: custody - the protective care or guardianship of someone, especially by police or authorities
  • synonym: delay - a period of waiting before an event or action begins or resumes
  • synonym: detention - the act of detaining someone in official custody
  • synonym: pause - a temporary stop in an activity or process
  • synonym: suspension - the act of stopping something temporarily
