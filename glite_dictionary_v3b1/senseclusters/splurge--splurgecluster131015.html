splurge

verb
be ostentatious or show off
7.0 1.09/m
Pronunciation: / spl…ùd í / (AmE), / spl…úÀêd í / (BrE)
to spend a lot of money or behave in a showy, extravagant way, especially to indulge yourself

Examples

To splurge on gifts
He loves to splurge at parties, always wearing designer clothes and flashy jewelry.
The celebrity splurged on a lavish wedding just to impress her fans.
They were splurging in the VIP section, making sure everyone noticed their expensive champagne.
After getting her bonus, she decided to splurge on a fancy dinner.
He rarely splurges, but he couldn't resist those designer shoes.
Sometimes it's nice to splurge a little on things you love.

Synonyms

  • splash out
    7.2 0.07/m
    splash out means spending a lot on something special or unnecessary, while splurge means behaving extravagantly to indulge yourself.
  • push the boat out
    8.7 0.00/m
    push the boat out is spending generously for a special occasion or celebration, while splurge is a broader, more general showy self-indulgence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boast - to talk with excessive pride and self-satisfaction
  • antonym: save - to keep money rather than spend it
  • synonym: show off - to behave in a way meant to attract attention or admiration
  • synonym: spend - to pay out money for something
  • antonym: understate - to present or describe something as being smaller or less important than it really is
