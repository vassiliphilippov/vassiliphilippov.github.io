Siwan

proper noun
Jewish calendar month (May–June)
Pronunciation: / siˈwɑːn / (AmE), / siˈwæn / (BrE)
The ninth month of the civil year and the third month of the ecclesiastical year in the Jewish calendar, usually falling in May and June.

Examples

Siwan month
Many important historical events for the Jewish people are remembered in Siwan.
The festival of Shavuot is celebrated during Siwan in the Jewish calendar.
The month of Siwan usually corresponds to late May and early June in the Gregorian calendar.
