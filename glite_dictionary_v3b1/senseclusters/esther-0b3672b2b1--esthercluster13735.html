Esther

proper noun
Old Testament book about a Jewish queen
Pronunciation: / ˈɛstər / (AmE), / ˈɛstə / (BrE)
a book of the Old Testament that tells the story of a Jewish woman who became queen of Persia and saved her people from massacre

Examples

The Book of Esther
Esther played a crucial role in saving the Jewish people from danger in Persia.
Many biblical scholars study the Book of Esther for its themes of courage and identity.
The story of Esther is read each year during the Jewish festival of Purim.
