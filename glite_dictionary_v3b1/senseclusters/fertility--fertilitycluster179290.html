fertility

noun
ability of soil to support abundant growth
Pronunciation: / fərˈtɪləti / (AmE), / fəˈtɪləti / (BrE)
the ability of soil or land to produce abundant and vigorous growth of plants

Examples

Soil fertility
Careful crop rotation helps maintain the fertility of agricultural land.
The fertility of the valley made it an ideal place for farmers to settle.
Years of overfarming reduced the fertility of the region's once-rich soil.

Related Terms

  • antonym: infertility - the inability of soil or land to support growth
  • synonym: richness - the quality of being rich in nutrients for plant growth
