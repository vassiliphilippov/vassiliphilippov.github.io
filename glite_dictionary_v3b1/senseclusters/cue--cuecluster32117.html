cue

verb
to prompt someone with the next words (theatre, speech)
Pronunciation: / kjuː / (AmE), / kjuː / (BrE)
to give a signal, prompt, or reminder to someone so they know when to do or say something

Examples

To cue an actor
If you forget your lines during the play, the stage manager will cue you.
She stood just offstage, ready to cue the actors if they stumbled.
When he hesitated in the middle of his speech, his assistant quietly cued him with the next sentence.
The director will cue the dancers when it is time to enter the stage.
She cued her friend to start speaking by nodding her head.
When the lights dim, that will cue the audience to be quiet.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ignore - to pay no attention to or disregard
  • synonym: prompt - to supply someone with a forgotten word or line during a performance
  • synonym: signal - to make a gesture or action to communicate information
