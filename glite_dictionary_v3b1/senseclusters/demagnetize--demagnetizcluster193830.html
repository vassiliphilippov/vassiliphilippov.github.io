demagnetize

verb
remove magnetic properties
Pronunciation: / diˈmæɡnəˌtaɪz /; / diˈmæɡnɪˌtaɪz / (AmE), / diˈmæɡnɪtaɪz /; / diˈmaɡnɪtʌɪz / (BrE)
to remove or reduce the magnetic properties of something (including erasing data from magnetic storage devices)

Examples

To demagnetize a hard drive
Modern devices may include a feature to automatically demagnetize their hard drives.
The technician demagnetized the old television screen to fix the color distortion.
You need to demagnetize the screwdriver before using it on sensitive electronics.
Before selling the old hard drive, the technician decided to demagnetize it to ensure no data remained.
The IT department regularly demagnetizes outdated storage disks for security reasons.
You should demagnetize magnetic tapes before disposal to prevent sensitive information leaks.
He used a special tool to demagnetise the floppy disks for reuse.
Old videotapes are often demagnetised to permanently erase their contents.
The technician had to demagnetise the hard drive before disposing of it.
