Bombacaceae

noun
family of tropical trees with woolly seeds
9.9
Pronunciation: /bɑːm.bə.ˈkeɪ.siː/ (AmE), /bɒm.bə.ˈkeɪ.siː/ (BrE)
a family of tropical trees with large dry or fleshy fruit, usually containing woolly seeds (including the kapok and baobab trees)

Examples

Bombacaceae family trees
Botanists have reclassified Bombacaceae as a subfamily within Malvaceae in recent years.
Many Bombacaceae produce fruits containing silky fibers, such as the kapok tree.
The Bombacaceae family includes the majestic baobab tree found in Africa.
Botanists have reclassified many members of the Bombacaceae into other families.
Some fruits of the Bombacaceae are harvested for their fiber or edible seeds.
The Bombacaceae includes such notable trees as the baobab and kapok.
