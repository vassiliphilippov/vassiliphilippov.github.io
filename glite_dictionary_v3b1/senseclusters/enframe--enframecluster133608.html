enframe

verb
to shape perspective
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfreɪm / (AmE), / ɪnˈfreɪm / (BrE)
to present or express an idea, issue, or argument within a particular conceptual framework to influence understanding

Examples

To enframe an argument
The documentary attempts to enframe the debate in terms of social justice.
Politicians often enframe economic issues to appeal to their supporters.
She tried to enframe the discussion as a matter of public safety.

Related Terms

  • synonym: frame - to express something in a particular way to influence perception
  • synonym: reframe - to change the way something is presented or considered
