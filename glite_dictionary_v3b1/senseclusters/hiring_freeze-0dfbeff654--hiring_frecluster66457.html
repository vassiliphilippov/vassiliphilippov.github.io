hiring freeze

noun
suspension of hiring new employees
3.4 3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈhaɪ.ər.ɪŋ friːz/ /; / /ˈhaɪ.rɪŋ friːz/ (AmE), /ˈhaɪ.ər.ɪŋ friːz/ /; / /ˈhaɪ.rɪŋ friːz/ (BrE)
An official stoppage or suspension of hiring new employees, usually imposed by an organization to reduce costs or control workforce size.

Examples

A hiring freeze policy
Due to budget cuts, the company announced a hiring freeze for the rest of the year.
Many government agencies implemented a hiring freeze during the economic downturn.
The hiring freeze has made it difficult for new graduates to find employment at the firm.
