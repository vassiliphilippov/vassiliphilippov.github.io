public-service corporation

noun
company providing regulated public services

Pronunciation: /ˈpʌb.lɪk ˈsɜːr.vɪs kɔːr.pə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ˈpʌb.lɪk ˈsɜːr.vəs kɔːr.pə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈpʌb.lɪk ˈsɜː.vɪs kɔː.pə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ˈpʌb.lɪk ˈsɜː.vəs kɔː.pə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
A company that provides essential public services, such as utilities or transportation, and is subject to government regulation.

Examples

A public-service corporation
As a public-service corporation, the electric company must comply with government regulations on pricing and access.
Many public-service corporations are required by law to provide service to all residents within their jurisdictions.
The city awarded the contract to a public-service corporation to operate its water supply system.
