suppressed

adjective
kept from public knowledge
3.8
Pronunciation: / səˈprɛst / (AmE), / səˈprɛst / (BrE)
kept from being known, revealed, or expressed; deliberately held back or restrained

Examples

Information suppressed
Details of the investigation were suppressed to protect the victim's identity.
Evidence that contradicted their claims was suppressed during the trial.
The government suppressed reports of the incident to avoid public panic.
A suppressed yawn escaped as he tried to stay attentive in class.
She covered her mouth to hide her suppressed laughter during the meeting.
The comedian's joke was so funny that it led to barely suppressed giggles in the audience.
His suppressed laughter threatened to escape during the serious meeting.
Many artists worked under a suppressed culture where creative freedom was not permitted.
The suppressed report contained valuable information that the public never saw.

Related Terms

  • synonym: concealed - kept secret or hidden
  • antonym: expressed - shown openly or made known
  • antonym: released - allowed to be expressed or made public
  • synonym: repressed - restrained or held back, especially thoughts or feelings
  • synonym: restrained - kept under control or within limits
  • antonym: revealed - made known to the public
