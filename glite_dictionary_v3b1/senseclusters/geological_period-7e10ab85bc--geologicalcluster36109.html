geological period

A division of geological time, during which a particular system of rocks was formed; a major subdivision of an era.

Examples

A geological period division
Each geological period is marked by unique fossil records and rock formations.
The Carboniferous is a well-known geological period that saw the development of vast swampy forests.
The dinosaurs dominated during the Jurassic geological period.
