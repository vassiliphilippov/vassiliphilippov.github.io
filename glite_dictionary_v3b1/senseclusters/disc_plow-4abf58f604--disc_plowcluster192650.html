disc plow

noun
agricultural plow with steel discs
7.2 4.6
Pronunciation: / dɪsk plaʊ / (AmE), / dɪsk plaʊ / (BrE)
An agricultural plow with a row of concave steel discs, used to cut, break up, and turn over hard or dry soil.

Examples

A disc plow blade
A disc plow can cut through heavy clay and rocky soil where other plows might fail.
Older models of the disc plow are still valued for their durability on rough terrain.
The farmer used a disc plow to prepare the compacted field for planting.
A disc plough makes it easier to work land that is too rough for traditional ploughs.
Modern disc ploughs are ideal for preparing fields with tough, dry soil.
The farmer used a disc plough to break up the hard ground before planting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: moldboard plow - a plow with a curved blade that turns over the earth
  • synonym: plough - a tool used to turn over earth in farming
