hyperpolarize

verb
to make extremely divided
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˈpoʊləˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəˈpəʊləraɪz / (BrE)
to cause opinions, groups, or institutions to become extremely divided or polarized, especially in a figurative sense

Examples

To hyperpolarize a group
The heated debate on social media served to hyperpolarize the community even further.
Some leaders worry that harsh rhetoric will hyperpolarize the political landscape.
The new policy changes could hyperpolarize opinions among employees, making compromise difficult.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: polarize - to cause people or opinions to separate into opposing groups
  • antonym: unify - to bring people or groups together
