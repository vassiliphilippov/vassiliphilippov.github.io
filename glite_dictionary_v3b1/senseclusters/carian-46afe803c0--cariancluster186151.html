Carian

noun
ancient Caria inhabitant
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛəriən / (AmE), / ˈkeəriən / (BrE)
a person from ancient Caria, a historical region in southwestern Anatolia (modern-day Turkey)

Examples

A Carian artisan
The museum displayed pottery made by a skilled Carian artisan from the 6th century BC.
Historians have studied the language spoken by the Carian people to learn more about their culture.
A Carian merchant traveled across the Aegean Sea to trade with the Greeks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Greek - a person from ancient Greece, which influenced Caria
  • synonym: Lydian - a person from ancient Lydia, a neighboring region of Caria
