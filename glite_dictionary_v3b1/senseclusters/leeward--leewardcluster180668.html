leeward

adjective, adverb
away from the wind
6.4 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàliÀêw…öd / (AmE), / ÀàliÀêw…ôd / (BrE)
on or toward the side or direction away from the wind; sheltered from the wind

Examples

Leeward side of the boat
As the weather shifted, we adjusted our course leeward.
He tied the boat leeward to keep it steady against the strong breeze.
The sailors moved leeward to find shelter from the storm.
The campsite was set up in a leeward position for protection from the weather.
They anchored the boat on the leeward side of the bay to escape the strong winds.
Vegetation grows more densely on the leeward side of the mountain.

Synonyms

  • lee
    7.5 0.00/m
    lee names the sheltered side in both everyday and sailing talk, while leeward sounds more formal and directional.
  • to leeward
    8.0 0.03/m
    to leeward indicates direction away from the wind in navigation, while leeward is a single-word adjective used more generally.
  • alee
    9.9 0.00/m
    alee is the sailor's word for the sheltered side on a ship, while leeward is the wider, non-technical term.
  • downwind
    2.8 0.63/m
    downwind describes the path in the wind's direction and often marks where smell or smoke travel, while leeward focuses on shelter.

Related Terms

  • antonym: windward - in the direction from which the wind is blowing
