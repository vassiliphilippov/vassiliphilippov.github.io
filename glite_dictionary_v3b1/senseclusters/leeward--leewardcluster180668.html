leeward

adjective, adverb
away from the wind
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈliːwɚd / (AmE), / ˈliːwəd / (BrE)
on or toward the side or direction away from the wind; sheltered from the wind

Examples

Leeward side of the boat
As the weather shifted, we adjusted our course leeward.
He tied the boat leeward to keep it steady against the strong breeze.
The sailors moved leeward to find shelter from the storm.
The campsite was set up in a leeward position for protection from the weather.
They anchored the boat on the leeward side of the bay to escape the strong winds.
Vegetation grows more densely on the leeward side of the mountain.

Related Terms

  • antonym: windward - in the direction from which the wind is blowing
