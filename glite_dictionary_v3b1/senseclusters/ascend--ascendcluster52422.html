ascend

verb
move or slope upwards
Pronunciation: / əˈsɛnd / (AmE), / əˈsɛnd / (BrE)
to move or go upward to a higher place or level (physically or in space)

Examples

To ascend a mountain
The road ascends steeply toward the mountain pass.
The trail began to ascend sharply after the wooden bridge.
We watched the hot air balloon ascend slowly into the morning sky.
A team of scientists plans to ascend the Nile next month for research purposes.
Early traders would often ascend the river to find new settlements.
The explorers decided to ascend the Amazon to reach its source.
She felt a rush of excitement as she ascended the stairs to the stage.
The hikers began to ascend the rocky trail as the sun rose.
We watched the hot air balloon ascend gracefully into the sky.

Related Terms

  • synonym: climb - to go upward, often by using the hands and feet
  • antonym: descend - to move or slope downwards
  • synonym: rise - to move upward or appear above the horizon (as the sun, moon, etc.)
  • antonym: set - to go below the horizon (of a celestial body)
