gaspy

noun
sudden breath intake
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæspi / (AmE), / ˈɡaspi / (BrE)
a short, involuntary intake of breath, often caused by surprise or shock

Examples

A sudden gaspy
A quiet gaspy escaped from the crowd when the lights suddenly went out.
He let out a gaspy as the cold water splashed over him unexpectedly.
The audience's collective gaspy showed their surprise at the magician's trick.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gasp - a sudden, short intake of breath
  • antonym: sigh - a long, deep breath expressing emotion
