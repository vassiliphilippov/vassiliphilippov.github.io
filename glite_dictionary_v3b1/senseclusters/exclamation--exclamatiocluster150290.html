exclamation

noun
sudden or emphatic cry or expression
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˌɛkskləˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛkskləˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
a sudden, loud, or emphatic cry or expression of strong feeling, such as surprise, anger, or pain

Examples

A loud exclamation
His loud exclamation echoed through the hall.
She gave an exclamation of surprise when she saw the gift.
The news was met with an exclamation of joy.
He made an angry exclamation about the new policy changes.
Her exclamation at the unfair decision was heard across the room.
The meeting was interrupted by a sudden exclamation of protest from the back.

Related Terms

  • synonym: complaint - an expression of dissatisfaction or annoyance
  • synonym: interjection - a word or phrase that expresses emotion or exclamation
  • antonym: praise - an expression of approval or admiration
  • synonym: protest - an expression or declaration of objection
