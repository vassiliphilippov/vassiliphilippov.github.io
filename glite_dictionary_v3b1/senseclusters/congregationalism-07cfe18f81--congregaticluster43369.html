Congregationalism

noun
Protestant church government emphasizing congregational autonomy
Pronunciation: / kənˌɡrɛɡəˈneɪʃənəlɪzəm / (AmE), / kɒnˌɡrɛɡəˈneɪʃənəlɪzəm / (BrE)
a system of Protestant church government in which each congregation governs itself independently

Examples

Congregationalism church government
Congregationalism played a key role in shaping religious communities in New England.
She wrote a thesis examining the growth of Congregationalism during the nineteenth century.
Unlike other denominations, Congregationalism emphasizes the independence of each local church.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Episcopalianism - church government by bishops
  • antonym: Presbyterianism - system of church government by assemblies of elders
