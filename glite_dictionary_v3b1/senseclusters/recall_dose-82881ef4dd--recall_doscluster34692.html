recall dose

noun
additional vaccine or medication dose for effectiveness
7.0
Pronunciation: / rɪˈkɔl doʊs / (AmE), / rɪˈkɔːl dəʊs / (BrE)
an additional dose of a vaccine or medication given to ensure the effectiveness of an initial dose

Examples

A recall dose vaccine
A recall dose may be necessary if the body's immune response to the first dose is not sufficient.
Many children receive a recall dose to ensure long-term immunity against certain diseases.
The doctor scheduled a recall dose six months after the initial vaccination.

Related Terms

  • synonym: booster - an extra dose of a vaccine to maintain immunity
