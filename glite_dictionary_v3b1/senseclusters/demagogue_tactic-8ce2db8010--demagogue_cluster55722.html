demagogue tactic

noun
manipulative method used by a demagogue
9.1 7.6
Pronunciation: /ˈdem.ə.gɑːg ˈtæk.tɪk/ /; / /ˈdem.ə.gɔːg ˈtæk.tɪk/ (AmE), /ˈdem.ə.gɒɡ ˈtæk.tɪk/ (BrE)
a manipulative method used by a demagogue to gain support or followers, often by appealing to emotions, prejudices, or false promises

Examples

Demagogue tactics appeal emotions
Critics accused the leader of relying on demagogue tactics rather than honest debate.
Social media platforms can amplify the effects of a demagogue tactic during political campaigns.
The candidate used every demagogue tactic in the book to sway the undecided voters.
