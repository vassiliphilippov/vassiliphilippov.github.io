dry socket

noun
painful condition after tooth extraction
6.4 8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdraɪ ˌsɑk.ɪt / (AmE), / ˈdraɪ ˌsɒk.ɪt / (BrE)
A painful inflammation in the socket of a tooth that can occur after extraction when a blood clot fails to form or is lost, exposing bone and nerves.

Examples

A dry socket pain
Pain from a dry socket is often severe and may require a dentist's intervention.
The patient developed a dry socket a few days after having her wisdom tooth removed.
Using a straw too soon after extraction can increase the risk of dry socket.
