postulate

verb
assume or suggest as a basis
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːstʃəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɒstjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to suggest or assume that something exists or is true as a basis for reasoning or argument

Examples

To postulate a theory
The scientist chose to postulate the existence of a new particle to explain the data.
Their hypothesis postulates a link between stress and heart disease.
We often postulate certain rules when developing mathematical theories.
Several philosophers postulate the existence of a higher reality.
She postulated that the results were due to an unknown factor.
The scientist postulated that life could exist under extreme conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assert - to state a fact or belief confidently and forcefully
  • synonym: assume - to accept something as true without proof
  • antonym: deny - to state that something is not true or does not exist
  • synonym: suppose - to think or believe something is true or possible
