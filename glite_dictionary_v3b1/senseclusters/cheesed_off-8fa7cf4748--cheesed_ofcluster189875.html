cheesed off

adjective
annoyed or irritated
8.2 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / t Éizd …ëf / (AmE), / t ÉiÀêzd …íf / (BrE)
Annoyed or irritated; out of patience.

Examples

Cheesed off with problems
He seemed really cheesed off after hearing the bad news.
I'm getting cheesed off with all these delays at the station.
She was cheesed off by the manager's constant criticism.

Synonyms

  • browned off
    8.9 0.01/m
    browned off is British and shows repeated irritation after delays, while cheesed off means suddenly annoyed and out of patience.
  • ticked off
    7.9 0.19/m
    ticked off is stronger and shows clear anger, while cheesed off is more about mild irritation and losing patience.
  • fed up
    3.6 5.42/m
    fed up shows long-term boredom or frustration, while cheesed off is a shorter, sharper feeling of annoyance and impatience.
