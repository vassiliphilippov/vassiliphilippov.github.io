copper-bottom

noun
ship hull covering
8.5
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑː.pər ˈbɑː.təm/ (AmE), /ˈkɒp.ə ˈbɒt.əm/ (BrE)
a sheet or covering of copper attached to the bottom of a ship's hull to protect it from marine growth and damage

Examples

Ship's copper-bottom
The old sailing ship still had its original copper-bottom after more than a century at sea.
Workers inspected the copper-bottom for signs of corrosion before the vessel was launched.
Installing a copper-bottom helped protect ships from barnacles and other marine organisms.
