cambium

noun
growth tissue layer in plants
7.1 8.2
Pronunciation: /ˈkæm.bɪ.əm/ (AmE), /ˈkæm.bɪ.əm/ (BrE)
a thin layer of dividing cells in plants or bones that helps with growth and repair

Examples

The cambium layer
Damage to the cambium can prevent a plant from growing properly.
In trees, the cambium produces new layers of wood and bark each year.
The cambium is essential for the thickening of stems and roots in most plants.
Damage to the cambium can hinder both bone and plant regeneration.
Scientists studied the cambium to better understand plant and animal tissue growth.
The cambium of the periosteum plays a crucial role in bone healing after fractures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: phloem - vascular tissue in plants that transports sugars and nutrients
  • synonym: xylem - vascular tissue in plants that transports water and nutrients
