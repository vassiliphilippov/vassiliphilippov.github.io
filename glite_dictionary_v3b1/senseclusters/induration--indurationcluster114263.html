induration

noun
pathological tissue hardening
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndjʊˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪndjʊˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a pathological hardening or thickening of tissue, often as a result of inflammation or disease

Examples

Tissue induration
Chronic inflammation can lead to tissue induration over time.
Palpation revealed a firm induration beneath the patient’s skin.
The doctor observed an area of induration near the site of the injection.
