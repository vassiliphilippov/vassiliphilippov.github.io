closet

verb
shut in a small space for work or discussion
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈklɑzət /; / ˈklɑːzət / (AmE), / ˈklɒzɪt / (BrE)
to shut oneself or someone else in a private room or small space, especially for work, discussion, or secrecy

Examples

To closet yourself away
He closeted his team in the study to brainstorm new strategies.
She closeted herself with her research notes in preparation for the big presentation.
The board members closeted themselves in the meeting room for hours to finalize the contract.
The manager closeted herself with the team to discuss the confidential project.
After the incident, the witness was closeted in a private office for questioning.
They closeted the committee members to negotiate the final terms in secret.

  • synonym: seclude - to keep someone away from others
