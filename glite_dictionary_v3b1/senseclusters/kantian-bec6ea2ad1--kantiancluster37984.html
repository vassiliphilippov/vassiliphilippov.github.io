Kantian

adjective
of or relating to Kant or his philosophy
Pronunciation: / ˈkæntiən / (AmE), / ˈkantiən / (BrE)
Relating to the German philosopher Immanuel Kant or his philosophy, especially his theories of knowledge, ethics, or aesthetics.

Examples

Kantian ethics
Her argument was based on Kantian ethics, emphasizing duty and moral law.
Many modern philosophers have responded to Kantian theories in their own work.
The professor delivered a lecture on Kantian concepts of knowledge and reality.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Kantianism - the philosophical doctrines or system of Immanuel Kant
