yer

noun
Cyrillic letter for ultra-short vowels
Pronunciation: / jɜr / (AmE), / jɜː / (BrE)
Either of two letters in Cyrillic alphabets, representing ultra-short vowels in early Slavic languages.

Examples

Cyrillic yer letter
Linguists debate the pronunciation of the original yer characters in the proto-Slavic language.
Medieval Slavic manuscripts frequently use a yer to mark reduced vowel sounds.
The presence or absence of a yer can change the meaning of a word in Old Church Slavonic.
