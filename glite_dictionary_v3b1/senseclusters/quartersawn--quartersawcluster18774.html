quartersawn

adjective
cut with straight grain
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɔrtərˌsɔn / (AmE), / ˈkwɔːtəsɔːn / (BrE)
cut from a log so that the growth rings are at a right angle to the board's surface, producing straight grain

Examples

Quartersawn wood grain
The craftsman chose quartersawn wood for its stability and attractive grain pattern.
Furniture made from quartersawn oak is often more durable and visually appealing.
Many antique pieces feature quartersawn boards because of their resistance to warping.

Related Terms

  • antonym: plain-sawn - cut from a log so the growth rings are parallel to the board's surface
