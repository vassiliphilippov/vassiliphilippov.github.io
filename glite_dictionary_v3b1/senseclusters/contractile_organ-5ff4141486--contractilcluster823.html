contractile organ

noun
organ that contracts (e.g. muscle, heart)
Pronunciation: / kənˈtræktəl ˈɔrɡən / (AmE), / kənˈtræktʌɪl ˈɔːɡən / (BrE)
a bodily organ that is capable of contracting, such as a muscle or the heart

Examples

Contractile organ muscle
Involuntary movements are often controlled by contractile organs such as smooth muscles.
Scientists study how contractile organs respond to electrical stimulation in biological research.
The heart is a contractile organ responsible for pumping blood throughout the body.
