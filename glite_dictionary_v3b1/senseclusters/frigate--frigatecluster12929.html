frigate

noun
type of warship
6.8 6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈfrɪɡət / (AmE), / ˈfrɪɡɪt / (BrE)
A warship that is typically larger than a destroyer and smaller than a cruiser, especially in the United States Navy.

Examples

Modern frigate
Compared to a destroyer, a frigate offers more advanced anti-submarine capabilities.
During the ceremony, the admiral praised the crew of the frigate for their bravery.
The navy deployed a new frigate to patrol the disputed waters.
