soft-cast steel

noun
steel with very low carbon content
5.2
Pronunciation: / sɑft kæst stiːl / (AmE), / sɒft kɑːst stiːl / (BrE)
Steel containing less than 0.15% carbon, making it relatively soft and easy to shape.

Examples

Soft-cast steel material
Because of its low carbon content, soft-cast steel is often used in applications requiring easy welding.
Engineers chose soft-cast steel for the frame to ensure it could be shaped without cracking.
The factory specializes in the production of soft-cast steel for delicate machinery parts.
