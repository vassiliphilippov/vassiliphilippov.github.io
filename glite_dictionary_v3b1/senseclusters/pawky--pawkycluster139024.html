pawky

adjective
slyly clever; dryly humorous
Pronunciation: / ˈpɔki / (AmE), / ˈpɔːki / (BrE)
clever in a sly, cunning, or subtly humorous way, often with a dry wit.

Examples

pawky sense of humor
He was known for his pawky sense of humor that often left his friends guessing his true intentions.
Her pawky remarks always made everyone laugh at the dinner table.
The detective gave the suspect a pawky smile as he revealed the evidence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: artless - without deception; innocent and simple
  • synonym: sly - clever in deceiving; cunning
  • synonym: wily - skilled at gaining advantage, especially deceitfully
