capitulation

noun
document detailing terms of surrender
Pronunciation: / kəˌpɪtʃəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / kəˌpɪtjʊˈleɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a formal document that sets out the terms under which one party surrenders to another

Examples

Capitulation document
A copy of the capitulation was kept in the national archives for historical record.
The general signed the capitulation after his forces were surrounded.
The lengthy negotiations resulted in a capitulation that both sides agreed to honor.
