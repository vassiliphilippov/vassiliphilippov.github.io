referee

noun
person assessing manuscripts for publication
8.7 4.2
Pronunciation: /re.fə.ˈriː/ (AmE), /re.fə.ˈriː/ (BrE)
a person who reviews and judges the quality or suitability of work, such as a manuscript for publication or a case for a court

Examples

Manuscript referee
As a referee, she provided detailed feedback on the manuscript's methodology and clarity.
The editor thanked the anonymous referee for his constructive comments.
The journal requires each paper to be evaluated by at least one referee.
After weeks of investigation, the referee submitted her findings to the court.
The judge appointed a referee to review the complex financial records in the lawsuit.
The legal team requested a referee be assigned to help clarify the disputes between the parties.
