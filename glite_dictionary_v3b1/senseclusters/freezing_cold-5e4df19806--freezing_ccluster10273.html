Synonyms

  • freezing
    1.0 0.00/m
    freezing describes temperatures at or below water's freezing point, while freezing cold is a stronger, more emphatic everyday phrase.
  • ice-cold
    1.0 2/m
    ice-cold feels as cold as ice to touch, while freezing cold often describes very low outdoor temperatures or strong feeling.
  • icy
    1.0 4/m
    icy can mean covered with ice or very cold on surfaces, while freezing cold focuses on low air temperature and feeling.
  • subfreezing
    3.9 0.02/m
    subfreezing names temperatures below zero, while freezing cold is a common everyday phrase that describes extreme, unpleasant cold.
  • frigid
    4.1 1/m
    frigid reads as very formal and clinical in tone, while freezing cold sounds more everyday and vividly physical.
  • bone-chilling
    7.0 0.2/m
    bone-chilling suggests deep, shivering cold that reaches your bones, while freezing cold simply states a very low outdoor temperature.
  • arctic
    4.5 0.7/m
    arctic evokes the extreme, icy conditions of the polar north, while freezing cold refers to very low temperature in general.
  • frosty
    1.0 0.5/m
    frosty often means a thin layer of frost or mildly cold air, while freezing cold means much colder and more harsh.
  • stone-cold
    4.1 0.03/m
    stone-cold means completely without warmth or emotion, often figurative, while freezing cold usually describes very strong physical cold.
