defalcation

noun
fraudulent misappropriation of entrusted funds
3.5
Pronunciation: / dɪˌfælˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːfælˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of fraudulently taking or misappropriating money or property entrusted to one's care, especially in a professional or fiduciary capacity.

Examples

Defalcation by an employee
Strict auditing procedures are designed to prevent defalcation by employees handling sensitive assets.
The accountant was arrested for defalcation after misusing company funds for personal gain.
Years of unnoticed defalcation eventually led to the nonprofit's financial collapse.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embezzlement - theft or misappropriation of funds placed in one's trust
