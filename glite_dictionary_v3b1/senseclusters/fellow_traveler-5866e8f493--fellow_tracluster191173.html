fellow traveler

noun
person sympathetic to a political cause (esp. Communism)
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛloʊ ˈtrævəlɚ /; / ˈfɛloʊ ˈtrævələr / (AmE), / ˈfɛləʊ ˈtrævələ / (BrE)
a person who sympathizes with the aims of a political group, especially Communists, without formally joining or being a member

Examples

Fellow traveler in politics
During the Cold War, many artists were labeled as fellow travelers because of their leftist sympathies.
He was accused of being a fellow traveler although he never joined the party.
The senator warned against fellow travelers spreading subversive ideas in universities.
During the 1950s, many artists and writers were accused of being fellow travellers in the United States.
She insisted she was just a fellow traveller, not a card-carrying party member.
Though never a member of the party, he was often labeled a fellow traveller due to his outspoken support for leftist causes.
