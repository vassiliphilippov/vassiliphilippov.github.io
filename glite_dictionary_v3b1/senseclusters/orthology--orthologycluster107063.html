orthology

noun
gene ancestry relationship
Pronunciation: / ɔrˈθɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ɔːˈθɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the relationship or study of genes in different species that come from a common ancestral gene

Examples

Orthology of genes
Scientists studied the orthology of certain genes to understand evolutionary relationships between species.
Accurate identification of orthology is important for transferring gene function knowledge across organisms.
The database provides information about orthology among genes in humans, mice, and other animals.
Researchers use orthology to compare genes between humans and mice.
Advances in orthology have improved our understanding of evolutionary biology.
The scientist published a paper on the orthology of plant genes across species.

Related Terms

  • synonym: homology - the existence of shared ancestry between genes or structures
  • antonym: paralogy - the relationship between genes related by duplication within a genome
