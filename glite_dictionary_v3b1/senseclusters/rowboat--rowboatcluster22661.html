rowboat

noun
small boat powered by oars
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊˌboʊt / (AmE), / ˈrəʊˌbəʊt / (BrE)
a small, open boat propelled by oars, typically with seats for rowers and used on lakes or rivers

Examples

A rowboat on lake
Four people can comfortably fit in the rowboat for a relaxed ride down the river.
He learned to row in an old wooden rowboat his grandfather had built.
We rented a rowboat to explore the calm lake on a sunny afternoon.

Related Terms

  • antonym: canoe - a light narrow boat, pointed at both ends and paddled from within
  • synonym: dinghy - a small open boat, often used as a tender
