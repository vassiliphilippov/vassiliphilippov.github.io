denatured

adjective
having natural properties altered
Pronunciation: / diˈneɪtʃɚd / (AmE), / diˈneɪtʃəd / (BrE)
(of a substance) having had its natural qualities or properties altered, especially so that it is no longer suitable for certain uses.

Examples

Denatured alcohol
Some solvents are made safer by using denatured alcohol.
The denatured form of the protein can no longer function properly.
Wine used for cleaning is often denatured to prevent consumption.

Related Terms

  • antonym: pure - not mixed or altered with any other substance or ingredient
