taiga

noun
northern evergreen forest
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪɡə / (AmE), / ˈtaɪɡə / (BrE)
a vast forest of evergreen trees found in cold northern regions, especially across Russia, Canada, and Scandinavia

Examples

The taiga forest
The Siberian taiga stretches for thousands of miles across northern Russia.
Many animals, such as wolves and moose, make their home in the taiga.
Scientists study the taiga to understand how climate change affects northern forests.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boreal forest - a forest biome of northern regions with mostly coniferous trees
  • antonym: tundra - a cold, treeless region found north of the taiga
