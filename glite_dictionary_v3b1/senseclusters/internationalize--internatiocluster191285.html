internationalize

verb
make international in character or scope
1.8
Pronunciation: / ɪn.tɚˈnæʃ.ə.nəˌlaɪz /; / ɪn.tərˈnæʃ.ə.nə.laɪz / (AmE), / ɪn.təˈnæʃ.ə.nə.laɪz / (BrE)
to make something international in character, scope, or involvement

Examples

To internationalize a company
Many companies seek to internationalize their operations in order to reach a global market.
The organization decided to internationalize its annual conference by including participants from over 40 countries.
The university plans to internationalize its curriculum to attract more overseas students.
Efforts to internationalise the event have attracted participants from over twenty countries.
Several universities are working hard to internationalise their student bodies.
The company decided to internationalise its operations to access new markets.
