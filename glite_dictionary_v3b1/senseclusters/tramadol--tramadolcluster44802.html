tramadol

noun
pain relief medicine
Pronunciation: / ˈtræməˌdɔl / (AmE), / ˈtræmədɒl / (BrE)
a prescription medicine used to treat moderate to moderately severe pain, often after surgery or injury

Examples

Prescribed tramadol medicine
After her knee operation, the doctor prescribed tramadol to help manage her pain.
Some patients experience dizziness when taking tramadol for several days.
You should not drive or operate machinery after taking tramadol.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acetaminophen - a common over-the-counter pain reliever
  • degree lower: ibuprofen - a non-prescription medicine for mild to moderate pain
  • synonym: morphine - a strong prescription pain medication
