priority

noun
the condition of being more important or urgent

The fact or condition of being regarded or treated as more important than others; the right to take precedence in order, importance, or urgency.

Examples

Top priority
Our top priority is to ensure everyone's safety during the event.
Rapid response was given priority because the issue was urgent.
She struggled to balance work commitments with family priority in her busy life.

Synonyms

  • precedence
    6.4 3.31/m
    precedence is the right to go first in order or rank, while priority means being more important or needing attention first.
  • precedency
    8.5 0.01/m
    precedency is a less common form meaning coming before others in time or order, while priority focuses on importance or urgent need.
  • primacy
    8.0 3.98/m
    primacy is being first in importance or rank, while priority often means the right to be treated or acted on first when urgent.
