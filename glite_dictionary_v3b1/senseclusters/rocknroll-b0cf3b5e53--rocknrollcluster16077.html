rock'n'roll

noun
1950s popular music genre
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌrɑk ən ˈroʊl / (AmE), / ˌrɒk ən ˈrəʊl / (BrE)
A genre of popular music originating in the United States in the 1950s, blending elements of rhythm and blues with country and western.

Examples

Classic rock'n'roll music
Elvis Presley was one of the pioneers of rock'n'roll in the 1950s.
Many teenagers in the 1960s were passionate fans of rock'n'roll.
The energetic rhythms of rock'n'roll changed the face of popular music.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rhythm and blues - A genre of popular African-American music that influenced rock'n'roll.
  • degree higher: rock - A broad genre of popular music evolved from rock'n'roll.
