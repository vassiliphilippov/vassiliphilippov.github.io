Booker T. Washington

proper noun
African American educator and founder of Tuskegee Institute
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊkər ti ˌwɑːʃɪŋtən /; / ˈbʊkər ti ˈwɑʃɪŋtən / (AmE), / ˈbʊkə ti ˌwɒʃɪŋtən /; / ˈbʊkə ti ˈwɒʃɪŋtən / (BrE)
(1856–1915) An American educator, author, and leader who was born into slavery, founded the Tuskegee Institute in Alabama, and became a prominent advocate for African American advancement.
