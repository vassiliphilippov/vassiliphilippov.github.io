unspeaking

adjective
silent; not talking
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈspiːkɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈspiːkɪŋ / (BrE)
not speaking or remaining silent, either by choice, circumstance, or inability

Examples

An unspeaking person
He remained unspeaking during the entire dinner, lost in his thoughts.
The children sat unspeaking as the teacher entered the classroom.
She gave him an unspeaking look that said more than words could.
After the accident, he remained unspeaking for several weeks, communicating only through gestures.
The child sat unspeaking in the corner, overwhelmed by the events of the day.
She was unspeaking during the meeting, letting her colleague answer all the questions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: quiet - making little or no noise; not talking much
  • synonym: silent - not making any sound or not speaking
  • antonym: talkative - liking to talk a lot
