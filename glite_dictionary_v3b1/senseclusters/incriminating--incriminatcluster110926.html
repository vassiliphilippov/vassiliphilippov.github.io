incriminating

adjective
suggesting guilt or blame
4.2
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkrɪməˌneɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈkrɪmɪneɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
(of information, evidence, or conduct) suggesting that someone is guilty of a crime or wrongdoing.

Examples

Incriminating evidence
His fingerprints on the weapon were highly incriminating.
She deleted the incriminating emails before the investigation began.
The detectives found incriminating evidence at the scene of the crime.

Related Terms

  • synonym: damning - strongly suggesting guilt or blame
  • antonym: exculpatory - (of evidence) tending to clear someone of blame or guilt
