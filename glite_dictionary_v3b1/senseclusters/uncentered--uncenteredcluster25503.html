uncentered

adjective
not mean-adjusted
7.3
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈsɛntərd / (AmE), / ʌnˈsɛntəd / (BrE)
not adjusted by subtracting the mean; calculated using raw values rather than centered values, especially in statistics or mathematics

Examples

Uncentered data values
The researcher reported the uncentered variance to show the total spread of the data.
In some analyses, using uncentered values can lead to different interpretations of the results.
The formula for uncentered moments does not involve subtracting the mean from each observation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: centered - adjusted by subtracting the mean from each value
