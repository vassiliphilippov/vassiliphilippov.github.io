malleolus

noun
arachnid sensory organ
9.9
Pronunciation: / məˈliːələs / (AmE), / məˈliːələs / (BrE)
a small fan- or racquet-shaped sensory organ on the underside of the hind legs of certain arachnids, used to detect vibrations and chemical cues

Examples

Spider malleolus
The scientist observed the malleolus to understand how the camel spider senses its environment.
Each malleolus helps the arachnid detect tiny vibrations in the ground.
Researchers discovered that the malleolus is sensitive to chemical signals as well as movement.
