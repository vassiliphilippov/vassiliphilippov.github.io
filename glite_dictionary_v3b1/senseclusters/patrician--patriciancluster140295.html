patrician

noun
member of the aristocracy
Pronunciation: / pəˈtrɪʃən / (AmE), / pəˈtrɪʃ(ə)n /; / pəˈtrɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
a person who belongs to the noble or upper class, often with refined manners or high social rank

Examples

A wealthy patrician
As a patrician, she was accustomed to the privileges and responsibilities of noble birth.
Despite his patrician background, he dedicated his life to helping the poor.
The council was once dominated by patrician families who controlled city policies.
As a patrician, she was accustomed to attending elegant social gatherings.
He carried himself with the quiet confidence of a true patrician.
The old city was ruled by a wealthy patrician class.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aristocrat - a member of the highest social class
  • antonym: plebeian - a commoner; member of the lower social class, especially in ancient Rome
