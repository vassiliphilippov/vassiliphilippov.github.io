inferiorly

adverb
in a lower manner
7.4
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfɪriɚli / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɪəriəli / (BrE)
in a way that is lower in quality, rank, importance, or effectiveness; less well or poorly.

Examples

Performed inferiorly
The team performed inferiorly compared to their rivals in the championship match.
This software runs inferiorly on older computers, causing frequent delays.
He felt that his work was judged inferiorly because of a lack of experience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: poorly - in a way that is not good or effective
  • antonym: superiorly - in a way that is higher in quality, rank, or effectiveness
