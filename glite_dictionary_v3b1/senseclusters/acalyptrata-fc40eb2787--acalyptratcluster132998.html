Acalyptrata

proper noun
group of flies
Pronunciation: / ˌækəˌlɪpˈtreɪtə / (AmE), / ˌækəˌlɪpˈtrɑːtə / (BrE)
a group of flies in entomology characterized by the absence of a calypter, a small membranous lobe at the base of the wing

Examples

Acalyptrata flies group
Many families of small flies are classified within the Acalyptrata group by entomologists.
The absence of a calypter is a key feature distinguishing Acalyptrata from other fly groups.
Researchers studied the diversity of Acalyptrata in tropical rainforests.

Related Terms
