cubic kilometer

noun
unit of volume, one billion cubic meters
Pronunciation: / ˌkjuːbɪk kɪˈlɑːmɪtɚ /; / ˌkjuːbɪk kəˈlɪmɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˌkjuːbɪk kɪˈlɒmɪtə / (BrE)
a unit of volume equal to that of a cube with edges one kilometer long, equivalent to one billion cubic meters

Examples

One cubic kilometer
If all that ice melted, it would release several cubic kilometers of water into the ocean.
Scientists estimate the lake contains over 1,000 cubic kilometers of water.
The reservoir holds about two cubic kilometers of fresh water.
Lake Baikal contains more than 23,000 cubic kilometres of water, making it the largest freshwater lake by volume.
The annual flow of the Amazon River averages 6,600 cubic kilometres of water.
This glacier holds an estimated 2,000 cubic kilometres of ice.
