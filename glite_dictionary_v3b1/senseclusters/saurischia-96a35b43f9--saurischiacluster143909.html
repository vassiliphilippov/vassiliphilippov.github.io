Saurischia

noun
order of dinosaurs with lizard-like hips
Pronunciation: / sɔːˈrɪskiə / (AmE), / sɔːˈrɪskɪə /; / sɔːˈrɪskiə / (BrE)
a major order of extinct dinosaurs with a lizard-like pelvic structure, including theropods (meat-eating dinosaurs) and sauropods (large, long-necked dinosaurs)

Examples

Saurischia dinosaurs
Saurischia were dominant during the Jurassic and Cretaceous periods.
Paleontologists differentiate Saurischia from Ornithischia based on their hip structure.
The Saurischia include some of the most famous dinosaurs, such as Tyrannosaurus and Brachiosaurus.
Differences in pelvic structure distinguish Saurischia from their dinosaurian counterparts, the Ornithischia.
Paleontologists discovered a new dinosaur fossil belonging to the Saurischia order.
The Saurischia included both the towering sauropods and the swift, predatory theropods.
