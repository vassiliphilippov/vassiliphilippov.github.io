overbought

adjective
excessively purchased asset
7.0 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / Àåo äv…ôrÀàb…ît / (AmE), / Àå…ô äv…ôÀàb…îÀêt / (BrE)
describing a stock or asset that has been purchased so much that its price is likely higher than its true value

Examples

Overbought stocks
Many investors believed the market was overbought after months of rapid gains.
The analyst warned that the currency appeared overbought and could soon decline in value.
Some traders use technical indicators to spot when a stock is overbought.

Synonyms

  • overpriced
    3.7 3.21/m
    overpriced describes something that costs more than its real value, while overbought means an asset pushed up by heavy buying.
  • overinflated
    4.8 0.13/m
    overinflated suggests value is blown up or exaggerated, whereas overbought refers to an asset driven higher by too much buying.

Related Terms

  • antonym: oversold - describing an asset that has been sold so much that its price is likely lower than its true value
  • synonym: overvalued - describing something considered to be worth more than its actual value
  • antonym: undervalued - describing an asset priced below its true value
