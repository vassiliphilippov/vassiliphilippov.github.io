syllabic

adjective
forming the nucleus of a syllable (linguistics)
6.1 5.8
Pronunciation: /sə.ˈlæb.ɪk/ /; / /sɪ.ˈlæb.ɪk/ (AmE), /sɪ.ˈlæb.ɪk/ (BrE)
(of a speech sound) forming the nucleus of a syllable, usually as a vowel or a consonant acting as a vowel.

Examples

Syllabic consonant sound
In 'table', the 'l' is not syllabic, unlike in 'bottle'.
Some English dialects use a syllabic 'm' sound at the end of certain words.
The syllabic 'n' in 'button' acts as the vowel of the final syllable.
