thyrotomy

noun
surgical thyroid incision
Pronunciation: / θaɪˈrɑːtəmi / (AmE), / θaɪˈrɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical incision into the thyroid cartilage or thyroid gland

Examples

A thyrotomy surgery
The doctor recommended a thyrotomy to relieve the patient's airway obstruction.
During the emergency, a quick thyrotomy was necessary to save the patient's life.
A thyrotomy may be performed to access tumors in the thyroid region.
