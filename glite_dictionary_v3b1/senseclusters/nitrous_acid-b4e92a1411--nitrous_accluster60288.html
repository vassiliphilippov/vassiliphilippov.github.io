nitrous acid

noun
a weak, unstable inorganic acid (HNO₂)
7.6
Pronunciation: /ˈnaɪ.trəs ˈæs.ɪd/ /; / /ˈnɪ.trəs ˈæs.ɪd/ /; / /ˈnaɪ.trəs ˈæs.əd/ (AmE), /ˈnaɪ.trəs ˈæs.ɪd/ /; / /ˈnɪ.trəs ˈæs.ɪd/ /; / /ˈnaɪ.trəs ˈæs.əd/ (BrE)
an unstable, weak inorganic acid (HNO₂) known only in solution or as nitrite salts, used mainly in chemical reactions

Examples

Dilute nitrous acid
Dilute nitrous acid quickly decomposes, making it difficult to isolate as a pure substance.
Exposure to sunlight accelerates the breakdown of nitrous acid in aqueous solutions.
In organic synthesis, nitrous acid is often prepared in situ for diazotization reactions.
