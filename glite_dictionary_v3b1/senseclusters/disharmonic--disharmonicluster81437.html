disharmonic

adjective
lacking harmony
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪshɑːrˈmɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˌdɪshɑːˈmɒnɪk / (BrE)
lacking harmony or producing a jarring, unpleasant sound

Examples

Disharmonic music
The orchestra played a disharmonic passage that made the audience uneasy.
Some modern composers use disharmonic sounds to create tension in their music.
The disharmonic notes clashed, making the song difficult to listen to.

Related Terms

  • synonym: discordant - harsh or jarring in sound; not in harmony
  • antonym: harmonious - pleasantly combined or in agreement in sound
