rebase

verb
to change base
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈbeɪs / (AmE), / ˌriːˈbeɪs / (BrE)
to change the base or reference point of something, especially in computing or finance

Examples

To rebase a branch
Before submitting your changes, make sure to rebase your branch onto the latest version.
The company decided to rebase its financial reports using the new currency values.
In some workflows, developers prefer to rebase rather than merge to keep the history clean.

Related Terms

  • antonym: merge - to combine two or more things into one
  • synonym: reset - to set something back to its original or a new state
