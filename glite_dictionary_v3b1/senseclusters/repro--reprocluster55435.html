repro

noun
copy of artwork
8.2 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàriÀêpro ä / (AmE), / ÀàriÀêpr…ô ä / (BrE)
an informal term for a copy or reproduction of an original artwork or document, especially in art or print

Examples

A repro painting
He bought a repro of a famous painting for his living room.
The museum gift shop sells high-quality repros of their most popular exhibits.
She framed a repro of the historic document for her office wall.
He decorated his office with a repro of a classic movie poster.
The gallery sold a repro of the sculpture for a fraction of the original's price.
Many people display a repro of famous paintings in their homes.

Synonyms

  • reproduction
    4.4 1.24/m
    reproduction is the full formal term for a copy often used in museums and catalogs, while repro is informal art slang.
  • copy
    1.0 64/m
    copy is a general duplicate seen in everyday documents or prints, while repro is informal art talk for a printed reproduction.
  • facsimile
    7.1 0.80/m
    facsimile is a precise, formal copy kept for study in libraries or archives, while repro is casual art or print slang.
  • replica
    3.6 6.22/m
    replica is a model copy often made as a three-dimensional object for display, while repro names printed reproductions in art.

Related Terms

  • antonym: original - the first or authentic version of something
  • synonym: reproduction - a copy of an original work of art or document
