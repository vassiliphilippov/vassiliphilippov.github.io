powder photography

noun
method for mineral/crystal identification using X-ray diffraction
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈpaʊdɚ fəˈtɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˈpaʊdə fəˈtɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
a process for identifying minerals or crystals by coating a small rod with powdered material and exposing it to X-rays to produce a diffraction pattern for identification

Examples

Powder photography process
Advancements in powder photography have improved the accuracy of crystal identification in geology.
By applying powder photography, the scientist confirmed the substance was a rare mineral.
The laboratory used powder photography to determine the mineral composition of the rock sample.
