process cheese

noun
cheese made by blending several cheeses
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑː.ses tʃiːz / (AmE), / ˈprəʊ.ses tʃiːz / (BrE)
a type of cheese made by blending several varieties of natural cheese, often with emulsifying agents, to produce a uniform and meltable product

Examples

Process cheese product
He prefers natural cheddar to process cheese for its stronger flavor.
Many American sandwiches use process cheese because it melts smoothly.
The recipe calls for grated process cheese to create a creamy sauce.

Related Terms

  • synonym: processed cheese - cheese that has been blended and emulsified for uniformity; alternative name
