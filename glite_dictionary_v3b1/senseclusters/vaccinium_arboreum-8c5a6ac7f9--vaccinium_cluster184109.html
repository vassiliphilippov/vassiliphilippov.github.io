Vaccinium arboreum

noun
North American shrub with black berries
Pronunciation: / vækˈsɪniəm ɑrˈbɔriəm / (AmE), / vækˈsɪniəm ɑːˈbɔːriəm / (BrE)
A shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States, noted for its black inedible berries; also called sparkleberry or farkleberry.

Examples

Vaccinium arboreum shrub
Despite its appealing appearance, the berries of Vaccinium arboreum are not considered edible.
Gardeners sometimes plant Vaccinium arboreum as an ornamental shrub for its attractive branches.
The Vaccinium arboreum is often found growing in dry, sandy soils in the southern United States.
