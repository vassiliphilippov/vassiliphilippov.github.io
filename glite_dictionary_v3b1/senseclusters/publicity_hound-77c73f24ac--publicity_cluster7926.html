publicity hound

noun
someone who craves media attention
Pronunciation: / pəˈblɪsəti haʊnd / (AmE), / pʌˈblɪsəti haʊnd / (BrE)
a person who aggressively seeks public attention or fame, especially through media coverage or sensational behavior

Examples

Publicity hound seeks fame
Some politicians become publicity hounds during election season just to keep their names in the news.
The singer has been called a publicity hound for constantly sharing details of her personal life online.
Tired of being labeled a publicity hound, he decided to keep a lower profile for a while.
