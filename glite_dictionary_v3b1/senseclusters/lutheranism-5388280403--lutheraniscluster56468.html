Lutheranism

noun
branch of Christianity founded on Luther's teachings
Pronunciation: / ˈluːθərənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈluːθərənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the branch of Christianity based on the teachings of Martin Luther, especially the doctrine of justification by faith alone

Examples

Lutheranism faith alone
Lutheranism places strong emphasis on salvation through faith rather than by works.
Many Germans converted to Lutheranism during the Reformation period in the 16th century.
The pastor explained the core beliefs of Lutheranism to the new members of the congregation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Calvinism - a major Protestant theological system emphasizing predestination and the sovereignty of God
  • antonym: Catholicism - the faith and practices of the Roman Catholic Church
  • degree higher: Protestantism - the faith and churches resulting from the Reformation
