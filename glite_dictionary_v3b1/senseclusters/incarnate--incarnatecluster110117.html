incarnate

verb
make something real or concrete; embody
8.0 5.6
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈkɑːr.neɪt/ /; / /ɪn.ˈkɑːr.nət/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈkɑː.neɪt/ /; / /ɪn.ˈkɑː.nət/ (BrE)
to embody or represent something abstract in a physical or tangible form

Examples

To incarnate an idea
She hoped her new project would incarnate her ideals in practical ways.
The artist's work brilliantly incarnates the spirit of freedom through bold colors and dynamic shapes.
The company aims to incarnate its values in every product they create.
His character incarnates the struggle between good and evil within us all.
Some people believed she incarnated compassion and kindness.
The monument incarnates the nation's hopes for peace and unity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disembody - to divest of a body, to make immaterial
  • synonym: embody - to give a tangible or visible form to an idea or quality
  • synonym: materialize - to make something become actual or real
  • synonym: personify - to attribute human form or nature to something nonhuman
