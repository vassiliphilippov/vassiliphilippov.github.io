lay in

verb
store something for future use
Pronunciation: / leɪ ɪn / (AmE), / leɪ ɪn / (BrE)
to obtain and store a supply of something for future use

Examples

To lay in supplies
Farmers often lay in supplies early to prepare for harsh weather.
She decided to lay in enough food to last through the storm.
We always lay in extra firewood before the winter begins.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hoard - to accumulate and store for future use, often secretly or excessively
  • synonym: stock up - to obtain a large quantity of something for future use
