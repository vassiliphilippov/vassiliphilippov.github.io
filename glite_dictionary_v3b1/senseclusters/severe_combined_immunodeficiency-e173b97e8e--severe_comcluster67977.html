severe combined immunodeficiency

noun
rare inherited disorder with severely impaired immune system
8.4
Pronunciation: / səˈvɪr kəmˈbaɪnd ɪˌmjunəʊ dɪˈfɪʃənsi / (AmE), / sɪˈvɪə kəmˈbaɪnd ɪˌmjuːnəʊ dɪˈfɪʃənsi / (BrE)
a rare, inherited disorder in which the immune system is severely compromised due to dysfunctional or absent T cells, making affected individuals extremely vulnerable to infections

Examples

Children with severe combined immunodeficiency
Children with severe combined immunodeficiency require protective isolation to prevent life-threatening infections.
Gene therapy offers hope for treating some forms of severe combined immunodeficiency.
The newborn was diagnosed with severe combined immunodeficiency after a series of blood tests.
