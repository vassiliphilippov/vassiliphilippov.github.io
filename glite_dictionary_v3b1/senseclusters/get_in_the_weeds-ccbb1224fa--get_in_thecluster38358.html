get in the weeds

verb
to become bogged down in details
7.2 6.7
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt ɪn ðə widz / (AmE), / ɡɛt ɪn ðiː wiːdz / (BrE)
To become overly involved or lost in the small or intricate details of something, often to the detriment of the main point.

Examples

To get in the weeds about details
During the meeting, she started to get in the weeds about minor budget issues.
If we get in the weeds now, we'll never finish the project on time.
Try not to get in the weeds during your presentation; focus on the main ideas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bogged down - to become stuck or overwhelmed, especially in details
  • synonym: lose the thread - to lose track of the main point or argument
