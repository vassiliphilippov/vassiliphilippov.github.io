hidebound view

noun
rigid or conservative opinion
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪdˌbaʊnd vjuː / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdˌbaʊnd vjuː / (BrE)
a conservative or rigidly inflexible opinion that resists change or new ideas

Examples

A hidebound view resists change
His hidebound view prevented the team from adopting new technology.
It's difficult to innovate in an environment where hidebound views dominate discussions.
Many critics accused the committee of taking a hidebound view on reform proposals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dogmatic - asserting opinions in an arrogant and inflexible manner
  • antonym: open-minded - willing to consider new ideas; unprejudiced
