clegg

noun
large biting fly (horsefly)
9.9
Pronunciation: / klɛg / (AmE), / klɛg / (BrE)
A large, fast-flying fly, especially a horsefly, whose females suck the blood of various animals.

Examples

A clegg fly
A clegg landed on the horse's neck, causing it to shake its head nervously.
Farmers often use insect repellent to protect cattle from cleggs during the summer months.
She was bitten by a clegg while hiking near the river.

Related Terms

  • synonym: horsefly - a large flying insect that bites animals and humans
