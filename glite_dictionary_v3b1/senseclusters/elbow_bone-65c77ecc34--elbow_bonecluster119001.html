elbow bone

noun
inner and longer forearm bone (ulna)
Pronunciation: / ˈɛl.boʊ ˌboʊn / (AmE), / ˈɛl.bəʊ ˌbəʊn / (BrE)
the inner and longer of the two bones of the human forearm, running from the elbow to the wrist; also called the ulna

Examples

The elbow bone fracture
A fracture in the elbow bone can limit arm movement significantly.
Doctors often refer to the elbow bone as the ulna in medical contexts.
The elbow bone connects the upper arm to the wrist, allowing for bending and rotation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ulna - the inner and longer of the two bones in the human forearm
