output device

noun
device transferring computer data out (e.g. printer, monitor)
4.0 2.9
Pronunciation: /ˈaʊt.pʊt dɪ.ˈvaɪs/ /; / /ˈaʊt.pʊt də.ˈvaɪs/ (AmE), /ˈaʊt.pʊt də.ˈvaɪs/ /; / /ˈaʊt.pʊt dɪ.ˈvaɪs/ (BrE)
A piece of electronic or electromechanical equipment connected to a computer, used to transfer data from the computer to the outside world in the form of text, images, sounds, or other media.

Examples

An output device monitor
A monitor serves as an output device by displaying information visually.
Sound cards are used as an output device to play audio from the computer.
The printer is an output device that produces documents and images on paper.

Related Terms

  • antonym: input device - device used to enter data into a computer
