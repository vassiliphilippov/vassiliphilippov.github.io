seep into

verb
to slowly pass or leak through
3.0 2.20/m
Pronunciation: / sip Àà…™ntu / (AmE), / siÀêp Àà…™ntuÀê / (BrE)
(of a liquid or substance) to slowly move or leak through a surface or into something.

Examples

To seep into walls
Darkness seemed to seep into every corner of the abandoned house.
Over time, the smell of smoke would seep into the old furniture.
The rainwater began to seep into the basement after the storm.

Synonyms

  • seep
    2.7 7.15/m
    seep moves slowly through small openings or pores, while seep into emphasizes entering something directly through a surface.
  • infiltrate
    7.3 1.08/m
    infiltrate moves gradually into or through material and often describes people or groups, while seep into means a liquid slowly enters.
  • percolate
    6.4 0.41/m
    percolate makes a liquid move slowly through porous material, often by filtering, while seep into focuses on leaking through a surface.
  • leak
    1.0 9.44/m
    leak lets liquid, gas, or light escape through a hole or crack and can be sudden or large, while seep into means slow entry.

Related Terms

  • synonym: leak - to allow liquid or gas to escape slowly
