regressively

adverb
in a backward way
8.1
Pronunciation: / rɪˈɡrɛsɪvli / (AmE), / rɪˈɡrɛsɪvli / (BrE)
in a way that returns to an earlier, less advanced, or less mature state

Examples

Change regressively
The policy changed regressively, undoing many of the previous improvements.
If the system is adjusted regressively, it may disadvantage those who need help the most.
Some laws have been applied regressively, affecting vulnerable groups more than others.
When faced with criticism, he responded regressively by sulking and refusing to talk.
The patient behaved regressively during therapy, showing signs of earlier childhood habits.
Under pressure, she sometimes acts regressively, seeking comfort in old routines.

Related Terms

  • synonym: childishly - in a way that is typical of a child or immature
  • antonym: maturely - in a way that shows emotional or mental maturity
  • antonym: progressively - in a way that becomes more advanced or develops gradually
