digit

noun
unit of measure based on a finger's breadth
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪdʒɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɪdʒɪt / (BrE)
an old unit of linear measurement based on the width or length of a human finger, typically about 3/4 of an inch (2 centimeters)

Examples

A digit of length
A digit was once used to measure small objects before standard units became common.
In ancient Egypt, builders measured lengths in digits, each representing the breadth of a finger.
The statue's width was four digits, according to the old inscription.
