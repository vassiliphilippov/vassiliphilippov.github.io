gastropod

noun
class of mollusks with coiled shells and a muscular foot
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæstrəˌpɑd / (AmE), / ˈɡæstrəˌpɒd / (BrE)
a mollusk of the class Gastropoda, typically having a single, coiled shell and a muscular foot used for locomotion, often with a distinct head and tentacles bearing eyes.

Examples

A gastropod mollusk
Biologists study gastropods to understand molluscan evolution.
Many gastropods can withdraw into their shells for protection.
The garden snail is a common example of a gastropod.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mollusk - an invertebrate of the phylum Mollusca, such as a snail or clam
