photogrammetry

noun
measurement from photos
8.5
Pronunciation: /foʊ.tə.ˈgræm.ə.triː/ /; / /foʊ.tə.ˈgræm.triː/ (AmE), /fəʊ.tə.ˈgræm.ə.triː/ /; / /fəʊ.tə.ˈgræm.triː/ (BrE)
the science or technique of making accurate measurements and maps or 3D models from photographs, especially aerial or satellite images

Examples

Modern photogrammetry techniques
Modern photogrammetry allows scientists to create detailed maps from satellite images.
Engineers used photogrammetry to measure the exact dimensions of the construction site.
Advances in photogrammetry have improved the accuracy of 3D modeling in archaeology.
The team used photogrammetry to create a detailed 3D model of the archaeological site.
Advances in drone technology have made photogrammetry more accessible for construction projects.
Accurate maps of the forest were generated through photogrammetry using aerial photographs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cartography - the science or practice of drawing maps
  • synonym: surveying - the science of measuring land and mapping its features
