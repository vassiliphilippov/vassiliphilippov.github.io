permeance

noun
measure of passage
8.6 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…úrmi…ôns / (AmE), / Ààp…úÀêmi…ôns / (BrE)
the ability of a material to allow gases or vapors, especially water vapor, to pass through it.

Examples

Water vapor permeance
The permeance of the new building material was tested to ensure it met safety standards.
Low permeance is important in insulation to prevent moisture from entering the walls.
Engineers compared the permeance of several fabrics to choose the best one for outdoor gear.

Synonyms

    7.4 0.67/m
