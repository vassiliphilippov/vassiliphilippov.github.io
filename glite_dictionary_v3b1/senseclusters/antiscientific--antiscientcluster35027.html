antiscientific

adjective
opposed to science
Pronunciation: / ˌæntaɪˌsaɪənˈtɪfɪk / (AmE), / ˌæntiˌsaɪənˈtɪfɪk / (BrE)
opposed to or not based on scientific principles, methods, or evidence

Examples

Antiscientific ideas
Many experts warned that the policy was antiscientific and could harm public health.
The debate became heated when antiscientific claims were presented as facts.
Some groups promote antiscientific ideas despite overwhelming evidence to the contrary.
The new policy was criticized as antiscientific by many researchers in the field.
Spreading antiscientific ideas can lead to public misunderstanding of important health issues.
His antiscientific attitude made it difficult to discuss the evidence objectively.

Related Terms

  • antonym: scientific - relating to or based on science
  • synonym: unscientific - not based on or not following scientific methods
