trust deed

noun
legal document transferring property to a trustee
5.2 7.1
Pronunciation: / trʌst did / (AmE), / trʌst diːd / (BrE)
a legal document in which property is transferred to a trustee, often as security for a loan such as a mortgage

Examples

A trust deed document
Lenders often require a trust deed instead of a traditional mortgage in some states.
The property was transferred under a trust deed as security for the loan.
They signed a trust deed to ensure repayment of the mortgage.
