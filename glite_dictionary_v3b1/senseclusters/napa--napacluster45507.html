napa

noun
Chinese cabbage used in East Asian cooking
7.5 2.04/m
Pronunciation: / Ààn…ëÀê.p…ô / (AmE), / Ààn…ëÀê.p…ô / (BrE)
A type of Chinese cabbage with an elongated head and broad, pale green stalked leaves, commonly used as a vegetable in East Asian cooking.

Examples

Fresh napa cabbage
She picked up a fresh head of napa from the farmer's market for tonight's dumplings.
Stir-fried napa with garlic is a popular dish in many Chinese restaurants.
The kimchi was made with napa and a spicy red pepper paste.

Synonyms

  • pe-tsai
    8.8 0.00/m
    pe-tsai is a common kitchen name for the elongated Chinese cabbage, while napa is the everyday cooking name used by cooks.
  • Brassica rapa pekinensis
    7.3 0.00/m
    Brassica rapa pekinensis is the formal botanical name used in science, while napa is the common cooking name used daily.
  • nappa
    6.6 0.01/m
    nappa often refers to a pale, tightly packed Chinese cabbage in recipes, while napa refers to the elongated, broad-stalked cooking vegetable.
