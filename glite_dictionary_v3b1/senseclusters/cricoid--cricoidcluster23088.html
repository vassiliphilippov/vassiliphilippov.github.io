cricoid

noun
laryngeal cartilage
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˈkraɪkɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈkraɪkɔɪd / (BrE)
a ring-shaped cartilage in the larynx that supports and maintains the airway

Examples

Cricoid cartilage
The surgeon carefully avoided damaging the cricoid during the procedure.
Swelling of the cricoid can cause breathing difficulties in young children.
The anatomy class included a detailed model of the cricoid and other laryngeal cartilages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: epiglottis - a flap of cartilage at the root of the tongue, which covers the windpipe while swallowing
  • synonym: thyroid cartilage - the largest cartilage of the larynx, commonly known as the Adam's apple
