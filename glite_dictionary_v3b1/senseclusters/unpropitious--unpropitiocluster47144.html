unpropitious

adjective
unfavorable; unlikely to succeed
1.2
Pronunciation: / ˌʌn.prəˈpɪʃ.əs / (AmE), / ˌʌn.prəˈpɪʃ.əs / (BrE)
Unlikely to be successful or favorable; not propitious.

Examples

Unpropitious weather
Despite their efforts, the team faced unpropitious weather conditions during the tournament.
It was an unpropitious time to launch a new business, given the economic downturn.
The signs were unpropitious, yet she remained hopeful for a positive outcome.
