mangle

verb
alter or damage beyond recognition
Pronunciation: / ˈmæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈmaŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
to alter or damage something so badly that it is unrecognizable, often by crushing, twisting, or mishandling it

Examples

To mangle the facts
If you mangle the facts, the story will lose its credibility.
She completely mangled his name during the introduction, making it unrecognizable.
The translation mangled the original meaning of the poem.

Related Terms

  • synonym: destroy - to put an end to the existence of something by damaging or attacking it
  • antonym: preserve - to keep something in its original state or in good condition
