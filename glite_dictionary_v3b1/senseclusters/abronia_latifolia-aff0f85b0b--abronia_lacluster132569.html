Abronia latifolia

noun
a coastal plant with yellow flowers
Pronunciation: / əˈbroʊniə ˌlætɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / əˈbrəʊniə ˌlatɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A species of flowering plant in the four o'clock family, native to coastal dunes from California to British Columbia, with hemispherical heads of yellow trumpet-shaped flowers.

Examples

Abronia latifolia yellow flowers
Abronia latifolia is commonly known as yellow sand-verbena and thrives along the Pacific coastline.
Botanists studied the dune ecosystem where Abronia latifolia played an important role.
The bright yellow flowers of Abronia latifolia attract many pollinators in the spring.
