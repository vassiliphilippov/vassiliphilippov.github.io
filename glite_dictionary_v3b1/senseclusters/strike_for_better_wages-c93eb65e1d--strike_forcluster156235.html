strike for better wages

verb
stop work to demand higher pay
6.2
Pronunciation: / straɪk fɔr ˈbɛtər ˈweɪdʒɪz / (AmE), / straɪk fə ˈbɛtə ˈweɪdʒɪz / (BrE)
(of workers) to stop working as a collective action in order to demand higher pay from their employer.

Examples

To strike for better wages
She joined her colleagues to strike for better wages in front of the company headquarters.
The teachers decided to strike for better wages when salary negotiations broke down.
Union members said they would strike for better wages if their demands were not met.
