Gothic language

noun
extinct East Germanic language of the Goths
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑːθɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɡɒθɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an extinct East Germanic language spoken by the ancient Goths, known chiefly from a 4th-century translation of the Bible by Bishop Ulfilas

Examples

Gothic language Bible translation
Linguists study the Gothic language to understand the development of Germanic tongues.
The Gothic language is primarily known from a surviving translation of the Bible.
Very few people can read the Gothic language in its original script today.
