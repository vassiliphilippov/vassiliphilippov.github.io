chalkiness

noun
dry powdery texture
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɔːkinəs / (AmE), / ˈtʃɔːkinɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being like chalk, especially in taste, texture, appearance, or leaving a powdery residue

Examples

The chalkiness of wine
The wine's chalkiness was noticeable on the finish, giving it a unique mineral quality.
Some tablets leave a slight chalkiness in your mouth after swallowing.
The artist commented on the chalkiness of the paint, which affected the final texture of the canvas.
The chalkiness of the tablet made it difficult to swallow without water.
Some wines are criticized for their chalkiness, which affects the mouthfeel.
After years of sun exposure, the paint developed a noticeable chalkiness on the surface.
The chalkiness of the old paint made the wall look faded and dusty.
She noticed a slight chalkiness on her hands after handling the limestone rock.
The tablet dissolved in water, leaving a mild chalkiness at the bottom of the glass.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grittiness - a texture that feels rough or contains small hard particles
  • synonym: powderiness - the quality of being powdery or easily crumbled
  • antonym: smoothness - a texture that is even and free from roughness
