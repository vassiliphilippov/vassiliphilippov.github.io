maladjusted

adjective
unable to adjust to life's demands
6.9
Pronunciation: / mæləˈdʒʌstɪd /; / ˌmæl.əˈdʒʌs.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌmaləˈdʒʌstɪd /; / ˌmal.əˈdʒʌs.tɪd / (BrE)
unable to cope well emotionally or socially; having difficulty adapting to normal life

Examples

Maladjusted teenager
A maladjusted teenager may have trouble building lasting friendships.
Growing up in a chaotic household left him feeling maladjusted as an adult.
The school provides counseling for maladjusted students who struggle to fit in.
As a teenager, he was considered maladjusted and struggled to make friends at school.
Social workers often help children who are maladjusted to adapt better to their environment.
The maladjusted student frequently acted out in class and had difficulty following rules.
