commute

verb
reduce a punishment to something less severe
Pronunciation: / kəˈmjut / (AmE), / kəˈmjuːt / (BrE)
(of a punishment) to reduce a penalty to a less severe one, especially officially by authority.

Examples

To commute a sentence
After a thorough review, the governor chose to commute the prisoner's punishment to time served.
The judge has the power to commute a death sentence if new evidence emerges.
The president decided to commute her life sentence to twenty years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mitigate - to make a punishment or penalty less severe
