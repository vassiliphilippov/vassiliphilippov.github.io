nullifier

noun
cryptographic spent-note identifier
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈnʌl.ə.faɪ.ər/ /; / /ˈnʌl.ɪ.faɪ.ər/ (AmE), /ˈnʌl.ə.faɪ.ə/ /; / /ˈnʌl.ɪ.faɪ.ə/ (BrE)
(in cryptography, especially privacy-focused cryptocurrencies) a cryptographic value derived from a note or coin that proves the note has been spent and prevents it from being spent again.

Examples

Cryptographic nullifier value
In Zcash, the nullifier proves a spent note cannot be reused.
Miners check transaction histories for matching nullifiers before accepting shielded payments into blocks.
The wallet software computed a new nullifier to prevent accidental double-spending of the note.

Related Terms

  • synonym: serial number - a unique identifier for a coin or token used to detect reuse; plays a similar role to a nullifier in preventing double-spending
