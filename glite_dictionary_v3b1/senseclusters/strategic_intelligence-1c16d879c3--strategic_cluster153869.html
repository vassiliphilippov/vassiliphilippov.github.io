strategic intelligence

noun
intelligence for policy and military planning
Pronunciation: / strəˈtiːdʒɪk ɪnˈtɛlədʒəns / (AmE), / strəˈtiːdʒɪk ɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəns / (BrE)
Intelligence gathered and analyzed to inform long-term policy and military planning at national or international levels.

Examples

Strategic intelligence analysis
Advancements in technology have transformed the collection and analysis of strategic intelligence worldwide.
Military leaders emphasized the significance of strategic intelligence when preparing for long-term security challenges.
The government relies on strategic intelligence to anticipate threats and guide its foreign policy decisions.
