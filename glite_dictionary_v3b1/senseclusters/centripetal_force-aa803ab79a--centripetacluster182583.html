centripetal force

noun
unifying social or political influence
Pronunciation: / s5bnc8tr6ap6atl f54rs / (AmE), / s5bnc8tr6ap6atl f54d0s / (BrE)
(figurative) an influence, idea, or factor that tends to unite people or groups and pull them toward a common center or identity.

Examples

Centripetal force in society
In times of crisis, patriotism can act as a centripetal force uniting diverse communities.
Economic stability often serves as a centripetal force, keeping competing factions focused on common goals.
Effective leadership became the centripetal force that drew the scattered groups together after the election.

Related Terms

  • antonym: centrifugal force - an influence that pushes people or groups away from a center; often treated as the opposite of a unifying force
  • synonym: cohesion - the action or property of forming a united whole
  • synonym: unity - the state of being united or joined as a whole
