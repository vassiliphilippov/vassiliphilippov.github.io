inviolability

noun
state of being secure
9.3
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌvaɪələˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ɪnˌvʌɪələˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being secure from violation, harm, or infringement

Examples

The inviolability of rights
The inviolability of human rights is a fundamental principle in many countries.
They argued for the inviolability of the nation's borders in international law.
The treaty affirms the inviolability of diplomatic correspondence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sanctity - the state of being holy or extremely important and protected
  • synonym: security - the state of being free from danger or threat
  • antonym: vulnerability - the quality of being open to harm or attack
