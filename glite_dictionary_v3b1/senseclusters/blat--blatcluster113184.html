blat

verb
cry out plaintively; bleat-like sound
5.4
Pronunciation: / blæt / (AmE), / blæt / (BrE)
to make a loud, harsh, or plaintive noise, especially like a sheep, calf, or horn

Examples

To blat loudly
All day, the young goats could be heard blatting for food from their shed.
Calves blatted plaintively as the farmer walked by their enclosure.
The frightened lamb began to blat loudly in the field.
The sheep blatted loudly as the farmer approached the pen.
A car horn blatted in the distance, startling the birds nearby.
The calf blats every morning when it wants to be fed.
The old car would blat every time the driver pressed the horn.
Cows sometimes blat loudly when they are hungry or upset.
As we walked past the farm, a goat began to blat at us.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bellow - to shout or make a loud deep sound
  • synonym: blare - to make a loud and unpleasant sound
  • synonym: bleat - to make the wavering cry of a sheep, goat, or calf
  • synonym: screech - to make a loud, high, and unpleasant sound
  • antonym: whisper - to speak or make a sound very quietly
