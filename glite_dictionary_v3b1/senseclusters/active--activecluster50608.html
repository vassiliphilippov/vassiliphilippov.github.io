active

noun
grammatical voice where subject acts
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈæktɪv / (AmE), / ˈæktɪv / (BrE)
The grammatical voice in which the subject of a verb performs the action or causes the event described by the verb.

Examples

The active in grammar
In the active, the subject typically comes before the verb, as in 'She wrote the letter.'
Teachers often encourage students to use the active in their writing to make sentences clearer.
The active is generally preferred in scientific writing for its directness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: passive - the grammatical voice where the subject receives the action
