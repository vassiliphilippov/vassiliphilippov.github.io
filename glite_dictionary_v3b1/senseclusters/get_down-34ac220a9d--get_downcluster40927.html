get down

verb
start doing something seriously
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt daʊn / (AmE), / ɡɛt daʊn / (BrE)
to begin doing something, especially seriously or with effort, often used with reference to starting work or a task

Examples

To get down to work
After breakfast, we should get down to planning the rest of the project.
It's time to get down to business if we want to finish today.
They finally stopped chatting and got down to work on the assignment.

  • synonym: set about - to begin doing or dealing with something
