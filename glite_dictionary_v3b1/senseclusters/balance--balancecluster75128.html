balance

verb
be in a steady position; be in equilibrium
Pronunciation: / ˈbæl.əns /; / ˈbæləns / (AmE), / ˈbæl.əns /; / ˈbæləns / (BrE)
to remain steady or keep something steady so that it does not fall; to keep in equilibrium

Examples

To balance a tray
He tried to balance the tray on his fingertips, but it was difficult.
She could balance perfectly on one leg during yoga class.
The gymnast managed to balance on the narrow beam for several seconds.
He tried to balance the box on his head as he walked across the room.
She balances her laptop on her knees while working on the train.
The waitress skillfully balanced three plates on one arm.
It's important to balance study with relaxation to maintain good health.
She managed to balance her career and family life admirably.
The artist tried to balance the composition by adding color to the left side.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disturb - to interfere with the balance or arrangement of something
  • antonym: fall - to lose one's balance and drop downward
  • synonym: offset - to counterbalance or compensate for
  • synonym: stabilize - to make or become stable
  • synonym: steady - to make or become steady or stable
  • antonym: tip - to cause to tilt or overturn
