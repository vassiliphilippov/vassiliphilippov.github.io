intensify

verb
become or make more intense
4.4 3.3
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɛn.səˌfaɪ /; / ɪnˈtɛnsəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛn.sɪ.faɪ /; / ɪnˈtɛnsɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to become or make something stronger, more intense, or more extreme

Examples

To intensify the effort
As the storm approached, the winds began to intensify and the sky turned dark.
Her symptoms seemed to intensify overnight, prompting a visit to the doctor.
Their rivalry continued to intensify throughout the season, leading to a dramatic final match.
Efforts to reduce pollution must intensify if we are to meet our climate goals.
Strong winds will intensify throughout the night, causing hazardous conditions.
The debate began to intensify as more people joined the discussion.
She tried to intensify her efforts to meet the deadline.
Tensions in the region may intensify if the conflict escalates.
The storm continued to intensify as it approached the coast.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lessen - to become smaller in amount, degree, or intensity
  • synonym: strengthen - to make stronger; synonym for intensify in some contexts
  • antonym: weaken - to become less intense or strong
