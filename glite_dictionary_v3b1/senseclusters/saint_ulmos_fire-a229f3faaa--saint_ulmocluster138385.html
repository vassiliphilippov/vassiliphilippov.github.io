Saint Ulmo's fire

noun
bluish glow from electrical discharge in storms
Pronunciation: / seɪnt ˈʌlmoʊz ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈʌlməʊz ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
A weather phenomenon in which a bluish or violet electrical glow appears near pointed objects, caused by electrical discharge and ionization of the air, especially on ship masts or aircraft during storms.

Examples

The Saint Ulmo's fire glow
During the storm, a mysterious Saint Ulmo's fire gleamed on the tips of the ship's masts.
Pilots occasionally report seeing Saint Ulmo's fire dancing on cockpit windows in turbulent weather.
Sailors took the appearance of Saint Ulmo's fire as a sign of protection during dangerous voyages.
