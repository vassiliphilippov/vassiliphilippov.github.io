positive chemotaxis

noun
movement toward a chemical stimulus
8.8 6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑzɪtɪv ˌkiːmoʊˈtæksɪs / (AmE), / ˈpɒzɪtɪv ˌkiːməʊˈtaksɪs / (BrE)
the movement of a cell or organism toward a higher concentration of a chemical substance

Examples

Positive chemotaxis to chemicals
Many bacteria display positive chemotaxis when attracted to nutrients in their environment.

The immune response relies on positive chemotaxis to guide white blood cells to sites of infection.
