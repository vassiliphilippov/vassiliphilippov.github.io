resolving power

noun
ability to distinguish close images (optics)
2.9 5.4
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈzɑːl.vɪŋ ˈpaʊ.ər/ /; / /rɪ.ˈzɑːl.vɪŋ paʊr/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈzɒl.vɪŋ ˈpaʊ.ə/ /; / /rɪ.ˈzɒl.vɪŋ pɑː/ (BrE)
the ability of an optical instrument, such as a microscope or telescope, to distinguish two objects that are close together as separate images

Examples

The resolving power of a microscope
Improving the resolving power of an instrument helps reveal details that were previously invisible.
The astronomer chose a telescope with greater resolving power to study distant stars.
This microscope has a high resolving power, allowing scientists to see very small structures clearly.
