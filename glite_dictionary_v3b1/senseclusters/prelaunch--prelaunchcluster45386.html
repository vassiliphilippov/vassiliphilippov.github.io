prelaunch

verb
to release before launch
5.1
Pronunciation: /priː.ˈlɔːntʃ/ /; / /priː.ˈɑːntʃ/ (AmE), /priː.ˈlɔːntʃ/ (BrE)
to release, promote, or make available a product or service to a limited audience before its official launch

Examples

To prelaunch a product
The company decided to prelaunch its new software to select users for early feedback.
They will prelaunch the product next week before the official release in July.
By prelaunching the service, the team hopes to identify and fix any major issues.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: launch - to officially release or introduce a product or service
  • synonym: preview - to show or introduce something before it is officially available
