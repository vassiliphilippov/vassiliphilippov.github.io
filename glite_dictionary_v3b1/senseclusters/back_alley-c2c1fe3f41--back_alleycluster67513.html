back alley

noun
narrow street behind buildings
Pronunciation: / bæk ˈæli / (AmE), / bak ˈæli / (BrE)
A narrow passage or street situated behind or between buildings, often used for deliveries or as shortcuts.

Examples

A back alley street
After the concert, the band slipped out through a back alley to avoid the crowd.
She took a shortcut through the back alley on her way to work.
The old bookstore was tucked away in a back alley downtown.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alley - a narrow passageway or street, especially between or behind buildings
