musty

adjective
having a stale, moldy smell
3.9 2.41/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆmÊŒsti / (AmE), / ËˆmÊŒsti / (BrE)
having a stale, damp, or moldy smell, often because of age or lack of fresh air

Examples

A musty smell
After months without ventilation, the cabin became unpleasantly musty.
She threw away the musty loaf of bread after smelling it in the pantry.
The old basement had a musty odor that was impossible to ignore.
Her clothes became musty from being left damp in the suitcase.
The old books in the attic smelled musty after years of neglect.
We opened the cellar door and a musty odor greeted us immediately.

Synonyms

  • mildewy
    4.5 0.06/m
    mildewy smells strongly of damp mildew on surfaces, while musty describes a more general stale, old smell from age or poor air.
  • moldy
    2.1 1.50/m
    moldy has visible or strong fungal growth and rot on things, while musty is a milder stale smell from damp or age.
  • fusty
    9.5 0.03/m
    fusty smells stale, stuffy, and old, often from closed, aged rooms, while musty focuses more on damp, moldy odors.
  • dank
    6.2 1.54/m
    dank is very damp and cold, often in caves or basements, while musty mainly describes a stale, moldy smell.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fresh - recent, not stale or spoiled
  • synonym: moldy - covered with or smelling of mold
