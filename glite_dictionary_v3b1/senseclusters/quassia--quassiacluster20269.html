quassia

noun
tropical shrub/tree used medicinally
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɑːsiə / (AmE), / ˈkwɒsiə / (BrE)
A tropical shrub or small tree native to South America, known for its scarlet flowers and yellowish wood, from which a bitter medicinal extract is obtained.

Examples

Quassia tree flowers
Botanists are studying the unique flowers of the quassia tree found in South America.
Extracts from quassia wood are sometimes used as natural insecticides.
The bark of quassia is often used in herbal medicine for its bitter properties.
