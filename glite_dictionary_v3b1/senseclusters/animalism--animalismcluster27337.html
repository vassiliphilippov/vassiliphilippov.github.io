animalism

noun
doctrine that humans are purely animal
Pronunciation: / ˈænɪməlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈænɪməlɪzəm / (BrE)
the doctrine or belief that human beings are purely animal in nature and do not possess a spiritual nature

Examples

Philosophical animalism
Debates in philosophy often contrast animalism with views that emphasize the soul or spirit.
Some critics believe animalism reduces human beings to their biological instincts.
The philosopher argued that animalism neglects the complexities of human consciousness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: materialism - the doctrine that nothing exists except matter and its movements and modifications
  • antonym: spiritualism - the doctrine that spirits exist and humans have a spiritual nature
