poverty trap

noun
situation where escaping poverty is difficult due to lost benefits
1.0 5.0
Pronunciation: /ˈpɑːv.ər.tɪ træp/ (AmE), /ˈpɒv.ə.tɪ træp/ (BrE)
A situation in which an increase in income causes a loss of benefits or support, leaving individuals or groups no better off financially, thus making it difficult to escape poverty.

Examples

Escape the poverty trap
Escaping the poverty trap often requires a combination of education, employment opportunities, and policy support.
Many families find themselves stuck in a poverty trap where earning more money actually reduces their overall welfare.
Welfare reforms aim to break the poverty trap that keeps people dependent on government assistance.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: safety net - government-provided services or benefits to protect people from poverty
  • synonym: vicious circle - a sequence of events that reinforce themselves, making a situation worse
