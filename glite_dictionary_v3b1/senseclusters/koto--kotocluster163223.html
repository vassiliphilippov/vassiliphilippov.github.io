koto

noun
Japanese stringed musical instrument
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈkəʊtəʊ / (BrE)
A traditional Japanese stringed instrument with a long, rectangular body and usually 13 silk strings, which are plucked to produce music.

Examples

A koto instrument
He learned to pluck the strings of the koto from a skilled musician in Kyoto.
She played a beautiful melody on the koto during the concert.
The museum displayed an ornate koto as part of its exhibit on traditional Japanese music.

Related Terms

  • synonym: zither - a musical instrument with strings stretched over a flat body
