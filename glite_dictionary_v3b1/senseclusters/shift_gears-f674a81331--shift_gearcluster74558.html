shift gears

verb
change approach or strategy
2.1
Pronunciation: / ʃɪft ɡɪrz / (AmE), / ʃɪft ɡɪəz / (BrE)
to change one's approach, activity, or strategy, especially to adapt to new circumstances.

Examples

To shift gears quickly
After months of slow progress, the project team needed to shift gears and try a new approach.
In his career, he often had to shift gears to stay ahead of industry changes.
When the discussion stalled, the moderator decided to shift gears to a lighter topic.

Related Terms

  • synonym: change tack - to adopt a different course of action or approach
