spire

verb
to rise to a point
Pronunciation: / spaɪr / (AmE), / spaɪə / (BrE)
to rise upward in a tall, narrow, and pointed shape like a spire; to tower upward.

Examples

Mountains spire upward
The cathedral's towers spired above the surrounding buildings, visible from miles away.
New apartment towers spired above the old district, changing the city's silhouette.
Tall pine trees spire into the sky along the mountain ridge.

Related Terms

  • antonym: descend - to move or slope downward
  • synonym: tower - to rise or stand tall above something
