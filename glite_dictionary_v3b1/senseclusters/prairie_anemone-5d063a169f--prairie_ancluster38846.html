prairie anemone

noun
a spring-flowering perennial plant
4.5
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛri əˈnɛməni / (AmE), / ˈprɛəri əˈnɛməni / (BrE)
a short, hairy perennial plant with blue-violet or lilac flowers that blooms in early spring, native to North America and Siberia

Examples

The prairie anemone flower
Gardeners value the prairie anemone for its hardiness and early spring blossoms.
In the wild meadow, the prairie anemone added a splash of violet among the green grasses.
The prairie anemone thrives in well-drained soil across the plains of North America.
