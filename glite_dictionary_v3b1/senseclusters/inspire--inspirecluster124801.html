inspire

verb
breathe in (draw air into lungs)
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈspaɪɚ / (AmE), / ɪnˈspaɪə / (BrE)
(of a person or animal, in physiology) to draw air into the lungs; to inhale.

Examples

To inspire air
During exercise, athletes must inspire large amounts of oxygen-rich air.
Some medications can make it difficult for patients to inspire fully.
The doctor asked the patient to inspire deeply before listening to his chest.

Related Terms

  • antonym: expire - to breathe out (air); to exhale
  • synonym: inhale - to breathe in (air, smoke, etc.)
