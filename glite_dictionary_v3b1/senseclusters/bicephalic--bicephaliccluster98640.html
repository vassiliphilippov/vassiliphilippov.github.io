bicephalic

adjective
having two leaders
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪsəˈfælɪk / (AmE), / ˌbaɪsɪˈfælɪk / (BrE)
having two leaders, chiefs, or persons in authority who share control

Examples

Bicephalic leadership
The company adopted a bicephalic structure, with two CEOs sharing equal authority.
A bicephalic approach to management can sometimes lead to conflicts if the leaders disagree.
The new constitution established a bicephalic executive, dividing power between two officials.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dual leadership - a system where two people share leadership
  • antonym: unicameral - having a single legislative or governing body
