warrant

noun
right to buy shares at set price
8.1 0.57/m
Pronunciation: / Ààw…îÀêr…ônt / (AmE), / Ààw…ír…ônt / (BrE)
a security issued by a company that gives the holder the right to purchase shares of stock at a specified price, usually within a certain time period

Examples

A stock warrant
Many investors view warrants as a way to speculate on a company's future growth potential.
She exercised her warrants to buy shares at a lower price than the current market value.
The company issued warrants with their new bonds to attract more investors.

Synonyms
