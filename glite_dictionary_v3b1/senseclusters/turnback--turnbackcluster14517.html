turnback

noun
retreat or reversal
Pronunciation: / ˈtɜrnˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnˌbæk / (BrE)
an act of reversing direction or retreating from a planned course, journey, or action

Examples

Hiker turnback
Due to heavy snow, the hikers had to call a turnback before reaching the summit.
The pilot announced a turnback to the airport because of engine trouble.
After seeing the flooded road ahead, the group decided on a turnback for safety.

Related Terms

  • antonym: advance - the act of moving forward or proceeding
  • synonym: retreat - the act of withdrawing or moving back
