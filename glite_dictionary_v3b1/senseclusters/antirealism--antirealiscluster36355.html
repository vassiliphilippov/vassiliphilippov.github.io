antirealism

noun
belief reality is perception-dependent
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌæntiˈriːəlɪzəm /; / ˌæntaɪˈriːəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌæntiˈrɪəˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
the philosophical belief that reality, truth, or certain entities do not exist independently of our minds or perception

Related Terms

  • synonym: constructivism - the view that knowledge and entities are constructed by the mind
  • synonym: idealism - the theory that reality is fundamentally mental or immaterial
  • synonym: instrumentalism - the view that theories are useful instruments for predicting observations
  • antonym: realism - the belief that reality exists independently of perception
  • synonym: subjectivism - the view that knowledge or values are dependent on individual minds or perspectives
