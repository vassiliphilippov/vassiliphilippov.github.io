fragile

adjective
lacking substance or significance
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈfrædʒ.əl / (AmE), / ˈfrædʒ.aɪl / (BrE)
Lacking substance, strength, or significance; easily destroyed or dismissed.

Examples

Fragile hope
His fragile claim to leadership quickly fell apart when challenged.
The report was based on fragile evidence and did not convince the committee.
Their alliance was always fragile, held together by only a thin thread of mutual interest.

Related Terms

  • antonym: strong - having great substance or significance
  • synonym: tenuous - lacking a sound basis; weak or insubstantial
