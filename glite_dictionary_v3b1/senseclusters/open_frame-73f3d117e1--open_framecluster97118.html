open frame

noun
bowling frame without a strike or spare
4.4 5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊpən freɪm / (AmE), / ˈəʊpən freɪm / (BrE)
(in bowling) a frame in which a player fails to knock down all the pins with two deliveries, resulting in neither a strike nor a spare.

Examples

An open frame in bowling
A single open frame in the final round cost him the championship.
After scoring several open frames, she struggled to recover her lead in the game.
Experienced bowlers know how to avoid an open frame by aiming carefully for the spare.

Related Terms

  • antonym: spare - all ten pins are knocked down in two tries in a frame (in bowling)
  • antonym: strike - all ten pins are knocked down with the first ball in a frame (in bowling)
