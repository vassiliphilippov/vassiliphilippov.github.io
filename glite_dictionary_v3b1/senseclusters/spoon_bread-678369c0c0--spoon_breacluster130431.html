spoon bread

noun
soft cornmeal-based Southern bread
9.9 5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈspun ˌbrɛd / (AmE), / ˈspuːn ˌbrɛd / (BrE)
A soft, moist bread made from cornmeal, and sometimes rice or hominy, typically served with a spoon; a traditional dish of the Southern United States.

Examples

Traditional spoon bread
At the family reunion, Grandma served her famous spoon bread alongside fried chicken and collard greens.
The restaurant's spoon bread was warm, creamy, and full of corn flavor.
You need a spoon to eat spoon bread because of its soft, pudding-like texture.
