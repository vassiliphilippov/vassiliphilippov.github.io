etatist

noun
supporter of state control
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪtəˌtɪst / (AmE), / ˈeɪtəˌtɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports strong government control over economic and social policy

Examples

An etatist leader
The etatist believed that only the government could solve the country's economic problems.
Critics accused the new administration of appointing too many etatists to key positions.
As an outspoken etatist, she often debated for increased state regulation in public forums.

Related Terms

  • antonym: libertarian - a person who advocates minimal government intervention
  • synonym: statist - a person who believes in extensive government control
