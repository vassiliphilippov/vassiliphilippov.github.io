choose up sides

verb
divide into teams for a game
Pronunciation: / tʃuz ʌp saɪdz / (AmE), / tʃuːz ʌp saɪdz / (BrE)
to divide into two groups or teams, especially before a game, by selecting players alternately.

Examples

To choose up sides for game
Every recess, the students choose up sides before starting a soccer match.
The coach asked the kids to choose up sides quickly so the game could begin.
We spent more time choosing up sides than actually playing dodgeball.
