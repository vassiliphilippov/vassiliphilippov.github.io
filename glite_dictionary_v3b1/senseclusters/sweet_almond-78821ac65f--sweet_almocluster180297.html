sweet almond

noun
tree with edible nuts and pink blossoms
Pronunciation: / swit ˈɑlmənd / (AmE), / swiːt ˈɑːlmənd / (BrE)
a small deciduous tree native to Asia and North Africa, cultivated for its edible nuts with a hard shell and pink blossoms

Examples

The sweet almond tree
In early spring, the sweet almond produces beautiful pink blossoms.
Some desserts use ground sweet almond as a key ingredient.
The sweet almond is widely cultivated in California for its nuts.
