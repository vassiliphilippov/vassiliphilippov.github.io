paucity

noun
small or insufficient quantity
Pronunciation: / ˈpɔːsəti / (AmE), / ˈpɔːsəti / (BrE)
The presence of only a small or insufficient quantity or amount of something; scarcity.

Examples

The paucity of evidence
Because of the paucity of evidence, the case could not go to trial.
The company struggled to grow due to a paucity of qualified candidates.
There is a noticeable paucity of medical supplies in the rural area.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abundance - a large quantity of something
  • synonym: scarcity - a lack or shortage of something
