leapfrog strategy

noun
tactic of bypassing steps or obstacles
8.2 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈliːp.frɔːg ˈstræt.ə.dʒiː/ /; / /ˈliːp.frɑːg ˈstræt.ə.dʒiː/ (AmE), /ˈliːp.frɒg ˈstræt.ə.dʒiː/ (BrE)
a tactic that involves bypassing intermediate steps or obstacles to make rapid progress, especially in competitive or strategic situations

Examples

A leapfrog strategy plan
By implementing a new technology, the team executed a leapfrog strategy that bypassed traditional development stages.
Governments in developing countries sometimes use a leapfrog strategy to modernize infrastructure rapidly.
The company adopted a leapfrog strategy to overtake its competitors in emerging markets.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bypass - to go around an obstacle or avoid something
  • antonym: direct approach - strategy of proceeding without detours or unnecessary steps
