sawfly

noun
insect with saw-like ovipositor
8.2 7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈsɑː.flaɪ/ /; / /ˈsɔː.flaɪ/ (AmE), /ˈsɔː.flaɪ/ (BrE)
an insect whose female has a saw-like ovipositor for inserting eggs into the tissue of leaves or stems of host plants

Examples

A sawfly insect
Gardeners often watch for sawfly infestations on their rose bushes.
The female sawfly uses her saw-like ovipositor to lay eggs inside plant stems.
The larva of the sawfly can sometimes be mistaken for a caterpillar.
