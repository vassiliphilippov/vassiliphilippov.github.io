microorganism

noun
organism visible only under a microscope
2.3 4.1
Pronunciation: /maɪ.kroʊ.ˈɔːr.gə.nɪ.zəm/ /; / /maɪ.kroʊ.ˈɑːr.gə.nɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /maɪ.krəʊ.ˈɔː.ɡə.nɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
An organism that is too small to be seen with the naked eye, such as bacteria, protozoa, and some fungi and algae.

Examples

A single microorganism can multiply rapidly under the right conditions.
Proper sanitation helps prevent the spread of harmful microorganisms.
Scientists are studying a new microorganism found in deep-sea vents.
