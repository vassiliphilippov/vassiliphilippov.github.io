floating dry dock

noun
portable structure for raising ships out of water
Pronunciation: / ˈfloʊtɪŋ ˈdraɪ dɑk / (AmE), / ˈfləʊtɪŋ ˈdraɪ dɒk / (BrE)
A portable structure that can be submerged to allow a vessel to enter and then raised to lift the vessel out of the water for repair or maintenance.

Examples

A floating dry dock
A floating dry dock can be towed to different ports as needed.
The damaged cargo ship was moved to a floating dry dock for urgent repairs.
The navy invested in a new floating dry dock to maintain its fleet more efficiently.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dry dock - enclosed dock from which water can be pumped out for ship repair
