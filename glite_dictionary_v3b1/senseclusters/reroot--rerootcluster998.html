reroot

verb
to change tree root
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈruːt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈruːt / (BrE)
to change the root of a tree or hierarchical data structure to a different node, altering parent–child relationships

Examples

To reroot a tree structure
The algorithm must reroot the tree to optimize search times.
After several changes, the developer decided to reroot the hierarchy at a different node.
You can reroot the data structure to make certain operations more efficient.

Related Terms

  • synonym: restructure - to organize something in a new way
  • degree lower: root - to establish the root of a tree or data structure
