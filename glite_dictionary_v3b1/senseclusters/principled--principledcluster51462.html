principled

adjective
having strong moral principles
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪn.sə.pəld / (AmE), / ˈprɪn.sɪ.pəld / (BrE)
acting in accordance with moral or ethical principles; based on or showing clear standards of right and wrong

Examples

A principled decision
His decision was both difficult and principled despite the pressure to compromise.
She is a principled leader who always does what she believes is right.
The company is known for its principled approach to ethical business practices.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unprincipled - lacking moral principles or scruples
