tumor suppressor gene

noun
gene that prevents tumor formation
2.5
Pronunciation: / ˈtuːmər səˈprɛsər dʒiːn / (AmE), / ˈtjuːmə səˈprɛsə dʒiːn / (BrE)
a gene that regulates cell division by preventing the formation of tumors or excessive cell growth

Examples

A tumor suppressor gene controls growth
BRCA1 is a well-known tumor suppressor gene associated with breast cancer risk.
Researchers identified a new tumor suppressor gene that blocks abnormal cell division.
The mutation of a tumor suppressor gene can lead to the uncontrolled growth of cells.

Related Terms

  • antonym: oncogene - gene that has the potential to cause cancer
