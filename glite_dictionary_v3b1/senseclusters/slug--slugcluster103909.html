slug

noun
shell-less land mollusc
Pronunciation: / slʌɡ / (AmE), / slʌɡ / (BrE)
a small, soft-bodied terrestrial gastropod mollusc, usually with a slimy elongated body and no external shell

Examples

Garden slug
A slug was slowly making its way across the garden path, leaving a trail of slime.
Many gardeners find slugs harmful because they feed on young plants and vegetables.
The child was fascinated by the slug and watched it stretch and contract as it moved.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: snail - a gastropod mollusc with a coiled shell
