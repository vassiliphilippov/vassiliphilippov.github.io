clue-by-four

noun
blunt or forceful hint
Pronunciation: / kluː baɪ fɔr / (AmE), / kluː baɪ fɔː / (BrE)
A blunt or obvious hint or explanation delivered in such a way as to force understanding, often experienced as a sudden or painful realization.

Examples

A clue-by-four hint
After missing the point several times, the teacher delivered a clue-by-four that finally made the concept clear.
His sarcastic comment was such a clue-by-four that everyone finally understood the situation.
Sometimes it takes a clue-by-four to get someone to realize their mistake.
