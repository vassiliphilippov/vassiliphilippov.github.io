bondsman

noun
man bound to serve without pay
7.1 9.0
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑndzmən / (AmE), / ˈbɒndzmən / (BrE)
a man who is legally or forcibly bound to serve another person without pay; a slave or serf (especially in a historical context)

Examples

Medieval bondsman
In medieval times, a bondsman was not free to leave the land he worked on.
The bondsman hoped one day to purchase his freedom and escape servitude.
The plantation owner treated the bondsman harshly, denying him any wages for his labor.
Literature often depicts the harsh realities of life as a bondsman.
The plantation's bondsman was promised freedom after many years of loyal service.
Through the centuries, the bondsman toiled on the land for his master.

Related Terms

  • antonym: freeman - a person who is not a slave or serf
  • synonym: slave - a person who is legal property of another and forced to obey them
