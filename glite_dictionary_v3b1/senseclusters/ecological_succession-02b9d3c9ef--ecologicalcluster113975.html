ecological succession

noun
gradual replacement of ecosystem communities
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌiːkəˈlɑdʒɪkl səkˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / ˌiːkəˈlɒdʒɪkl səkˈsɛʃən / (BrE)
the gradual and orderly process of change in an ecosystem in which one community is progressively replaced by another until a stable climax community is established

Examples

Ecological succession after fire
Ecological succession can often be observed after a forest fire, as new plant species gradually colonize the area.
Primary ecological succession occurs on bare rock where no soil initially exists, such as after a volcanic eruption.
The concept of ecological succession helps scientists understand how ecosystems develop and recover from disturbances.
