ghoulish

adjective
morbid or macabre; like a ghoul
7.3 6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈguː.lɪʃ/ (AmE), /ˈguː.lɪʃ/ (BrE)
suggesting or displaying a morbid interest in death or the macabre; resembling or characteristic of a ghoul

Examples

A ghoulish joke
His ghoulish jokes about accidents made everyone uncomfortable.
The reporter's ghoulish fascination with the crime scene upset the victims' families.
They decorated the house with ghoulish props for Halloween, including skulls and fake graves.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic
  • synonym: macabre - disturbing because of involvement with or depiction of death and injury
  • synonym: morbid - having an unhealthy interest in disturbing or unpleasant subjects, particularly death
