cauterize

verb
make someone unfeeling or insensitive
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔːtəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈkɔːtəraɪz / (BrE)
to make someone insensitive or unfeeling; to deaden someone's emotions or moral sensibilities

Examples

To cauterize feelings
Repeated exposure to violence can cauterize one's sense of compassion.
She tried to cauterize her conscience to avoid feeling any guilt.
Years of disappointment had cauterized his hopes and feelings.
Repeated disappointments can cauterise a person's ability to trust others.
She feared that constant criticism might cauterise her enthusiasm.
Years of conflict had cauterised his sense of compassion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: harden - make or become emotionally insensitive or less affected by feelings
  • antonym: soften - make or become more sensitive, gentle, or compassionate
