nonprotein

noun
nonprotein component
6.8
Pronunciation: /nɑːn.ˈproʊ.tiːn/ /; / /nɑːn.ˈproʊ.tən/ (AmE), /nɒn.ˈprəʊ.tiːn/ /; / /nɒn.ˈprəʊ.tɪn/ (BrE)
a component of a conjugated protein, such as a prosthetic group or cofactor, that is not itself a protein but is necessary for the protein's function

Examples

Nonprotein part of enzyme
The enzyme cannot function without its nonprotein component attached.
Many vitamins act as nonprotein cofactors in important biochemical reactions.
A nonprotein group may be required for the stability of some proteins.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cofactor - a nonprotein chemical compound required for a protein's activity
  • synonym: prosthetic group - a nonprotein molecule tightly bound to a protein and essential for its function
  • antonym: protein - a large molecule made of amino acids, essential for living organisms
