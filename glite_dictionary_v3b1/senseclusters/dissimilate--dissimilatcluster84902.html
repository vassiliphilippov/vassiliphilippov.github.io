dissimilate

verb
to become less similar in sound (linguistics)
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsɪməˌleɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɪmɪleɪt / (BrE)
(of sounds, especially in a word) to become less similar to each other in pronunciation, usually through a phonetic change.

Examples

Sounds dissimilate in words
In some dialects, certain consonants dissimilate over time to reduce similarity.
Phonologists have observed that the two 'r' sounds in 'February' often dissimilate.
When placed together, linguistic elements may dissimilate to ease pronunciation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: assimilate - (linguistics) to make sounds more similar in pronunciation
