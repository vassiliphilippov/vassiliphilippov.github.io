execute

verb
carry out legal formalities (will, deed, etc.)
Pronunciation: / ˈɛksɪˌkjut / (AmE), / ˈɛksɪˌkjuːt / (BrE)
to carry out the formal legal requirements of a document, such as signing it to make it valid

Examples

To execute a contract
Before the estate can be settled, the executor needs to execute all necessary legal documents.
The lawyer will execute her client's will according to the testator's instructions.
To legally transfer the property, both parties must execute the deed in front of a notary.
All parties must execute the document before it becomes effective.
She executed her will in accordance with state law.
The company president executed the contract in the boardroom with two witnesses present.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sign - to write one's signature on (a document)
  • synonym: validate - to make a legal document officially valid
