padrone

noun
Italian inn or establishment owner
Pronunciation: / pɑˈdroʊneɪ /; / pəˈdroʊni / (AmE), / paˈdrəʊneɪ /; / pəˈdrəʊni / (BrE)
an owner or employer, especially in Italian or historical immigrant contexts (sometimes refers to someone who exploited workers)

Examples

The padrone system
During the nineteenth century, many Italian immigrants worked under a padrone who arranged their employment.
She asked the padrone of the inn for a room with a view of the piazza.
The padrone greeted us warmly as we entered his family-run trattoria.
Historians have written extensively about the abuses committed by padrones against vulnerable newcomers.
Many Italian immigrants suffered under the control of the padrone system in the early twentieth century.
The padrones often took a large share of the workers' wages as payment for finding them jobs.
