prestimulus

noun
time before stimulus
Pronunciation: / priːˈstɪmjələs / (AmE), / priːˈstɪmjʊləs / (BrE)
the period of time immediately before a stimulus is presented, often used as a baseline in experiments

Examples

The prestimulus period
Researchers measured brain activity during the prestimulus to compare it with responses after the sound.
The prestimulus interval is important for understanding baseline neural activity in cognitive studies.
Changes in the prestimulus period can affect how participants respond to the main experimental task.

Related Terms

  • synonym: baseline - a standard or reference point for comparison in experiments
  • antonym: poststimulus - the period of time immediately after a stimulus is presented
