layman

noun
non-expert; non-clerical person
4.9 1.83/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆleÉªmÉ™n / (AmE), / ËˆleÉªmÉ™n / (BrE)
A person who is not a member of the clergy or a professional in a particular field; a non-expert.

Examples

A layman in medicine
As a layman, he found the medical jargon confusing but trusted his doctor's advice.
Even to the layman, the technical explanation was easy to understand.
She asked a layman to give his perspective on the legal case.

Synonyms

  • layperson
    4.9 1.22/m
    layperson is a neutral modern word for a non-expert or non-clergy person, while layman sounds older and male-centered.
  • nonspecialist
    3.9 0.23/m
    nonspecialist is someone without expert training in a subject, while layman also suggests someone outside a profession or clergy.
  • layfolk
    6.8 0.01/m
    layfolk are ordinary people often contrasted with clergy, while layman refers to one non-expert individual.

Related Terms

  • antonym: expert - a person with extensive knowledge or ability based on research, experience, or occupation
