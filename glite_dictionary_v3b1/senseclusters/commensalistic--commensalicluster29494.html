commensalistic

adjective
benefiting without harm
9.8
Pronunciation: / kəˌmɛnsəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / kəˌmɛnsəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to a relationship between two organisms where one benefits and the other is neither helped nor harmed

Examples

Commensalistic relationship
The relationship between cattle and cattle egrets is often described as commensalistic.
Some fish live in a commensalistic way with sea anemones, gaining protection without affecting the host.
Scientists observed a commensalistic interaction between the birds and the grazing animals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mutualistic - relating to a relationship where both organisms benefit
  • antonym: parasitic - relating to a relationship where one organism benefits and the other is harmed
