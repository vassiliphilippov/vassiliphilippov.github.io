deactivation

noun
disbanding of a military unit
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːˌæktɪˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːˌæktɪˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of disbanding or breaking up a military unit, typically by transferring or discharging its members.

Examples

Military deactivation
After decades of service, the deactivation of the regiment was marked by a formal ceremony.
Personnel were reassigned during the deactivation of the military unit.
The deactivation of several bases followed the end of the conflict.

Related Terms

  • antonym: activation - the process of making something active, especially a military unit being brought into service
