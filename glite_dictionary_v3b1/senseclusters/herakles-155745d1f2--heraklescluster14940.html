Herakles

proper noun
mythical Greek hero famed for his strength
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛrəˌkliːz / (AmE), / ˈhɛrəˌkliːz / (BrE)
A hero in Greek mythology known for his extraordinary strength and for completing twelve arduous labors to gain immortality.

Examples

Herakles the hero
In ancient tales, Herakles was celebrated as the greatest of all Greek heroes.
Legend says Herakles gained immortality after completing his heroic feats.
The twelve labors of Herakles have inspired countless works of art and literature.

Related Terms
