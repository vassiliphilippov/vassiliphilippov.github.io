pleiotropism

noun
one gene, many traits
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌplaɪiˈɑːtrəˌpɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌpliːɪˈɒtrəˌpɪzəm / (BrE)
the phenomenon in genetics where a single gene affects two or more seemingly unrelated traits

Examples

Pleiotropism in genetics
Researchers discovered that pleiotropism explains why one mutation can cause several symptoms.
Understanding pleiotropism is important for studying genetic disorders that affect multiple body systems.
The concept of pleiotropism helps scientists link different traits to a single gene.

Related Terms

  • antonym: polygenism - the influence of multiple genes on a single trait
