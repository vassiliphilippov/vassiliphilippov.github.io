vomitorium

noun
Roman exit passage
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌvɑːməˈtɔːriəm / (AmE), / ˌvɒmɪˈtɔːriəm / (BrE)
a passage or opening in an ancient Roman theater or stadium through which crowds could exit quickly

Examples

Stadium vomitorium exit
The ancient amphitheater had a vomitorium on each side to allow the audience to leave quickly.
Historians explained that a vomitorium was not a place for eating, but for exiting the stadium.
After the performance, the crowd streamed out through the main vomitorium into the city streets.

  • synonym: exit - a way out of a place
