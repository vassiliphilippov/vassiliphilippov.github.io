modern

noun
person of the present age
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑː.dɚn / (AmE), / ˈmɒd.ən / (BrE)
a person who lives in the present age, especially one who has ideas and attitudes typical of contemporary times rather than of past eras

Examples

A modern thinker
As a modern, she embraced new technologies more eagerly than her parents did.
Many moderns reject conventional customs in favor of progressive ideas.
The conflict between the moderns and the classics defined much of the literary debate of the era.

Related Terms

  • antonym: traditionalist - a person who supports traditions and is opposed to change or innovation
