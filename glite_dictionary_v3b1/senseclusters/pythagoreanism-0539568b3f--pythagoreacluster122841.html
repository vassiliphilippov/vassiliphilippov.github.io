Pythagoreanism

noun

Pronunciation: /pɪ.θə.ɡə.ˈriː.ə.nɪz.əm/ /; / /pɪ.θə.ˈɡɔːr.i.ə.nɪz.əm/ (AmE), /pɪ.θə.ɡə.ˈriː.ə.nɪz.əm/ /; / /pɪ.θə.ˈɡɒr.i.ə.nɪz.əm/ (BrE)
the philosophical and religious system based on the teachings of Pythagoras and his followers, emphasizing mathematics, harmony, and the transmigration of souls

Examples

Pythagoreanism teachings
Many ancient philosophers were inspired by Pythagoreanism and its emphasis on the importance of numbers.
The belief in the transmigration of souls is a key aspect of Pythagoreanism.
Music and mathematics were closely linked in Pythagoreanism as expressions of universal harmony.

Related Terms

  • antonym: materialism - the belief that only physical matter exists
