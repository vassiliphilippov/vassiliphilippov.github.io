housemaid's knee

noun
swelling of knee bursa from kneeling
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈhaʊsˌmeɪdz ˈni / (AmE), / ˈhaʊsmeɪdz ˈniː / (BrE)
Swelling of the bursa at the front of the knee, typically caused by trauma or prolonged kneeling; prepatellar bursitis.

Examples

Housemaid's knee swelling
Repeated kneeling on hard surfaces led to a painful case of housemaid's knee.
The doctor diagnosed her with housemaid's knee after weeks of scrubbing floors.
Wearing protective pads can help prevent housemaid's knee among those who kneel for work.
