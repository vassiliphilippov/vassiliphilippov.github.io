multimodality

noun
multiple transport modes
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌltiˌmoʊˈdæləti / (AmE), / ˌmʌltiˌməʊˈdælɪti / (BrE)
the use of two or more different modes of transport for moving goods or passengers in a single journey or system

Examples

Multimodality transport system
The shipping company adopted multimodality to deliver goods faster and more efficiently.
By using multimodality, the logistics team combined trucks and trains for long-distance transport.
Modern supply chains often rely on multimodality to reduce costs and improve flexibility.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intermodal - involving two or more different modes of transport
  • antonym: unimodal - involving only one mode of transport
