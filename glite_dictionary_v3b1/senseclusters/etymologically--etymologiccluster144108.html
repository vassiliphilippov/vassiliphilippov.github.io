etymologically

adverb
relating to word origins
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɛtɪməˈlɑdʒɪkli / (AmE), / ˌɛtɪməˈlɒdʒɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to the origin or historical development of a word or words

Examples

Etymologically speaking
Etymologically, the word 'biology' comes from Greek roots.
The term 'salary' is etymologically linked to the Latin word for salt.
He explained that, etymologically, 'nice' once meant foolish or ignorant.
