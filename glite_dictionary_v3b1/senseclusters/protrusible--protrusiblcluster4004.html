protrusible

adjective
able to be thrust forward (biological term)
8.4 7.9
Pronunciation: /prə.ˈtruː.zə.bəl/ /; / /prə.ˈtruː.sə.bəl/ (AmE), /prə.ˈtruː.zə.bəl/ /; / /prə.ˈtruː.sə.bəl/ (BrE)
(of a part of the body) capable of being extended or thrust forward, especially beyond the normal limit.

Examples

Protrusible tongue
Many reptiles possess a protrusible tongue for reaching food hidden in crevices.
Some species of fish have a protrusible jaw that helps them capture prey efficiently.
The frog’s protrusible tongue snaps out quickly to catch insects.
