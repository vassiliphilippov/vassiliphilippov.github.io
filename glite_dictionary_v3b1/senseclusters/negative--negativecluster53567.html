Synonyms

  • unfavorable
    4.5 1/m
    unfavorable describes a situation or opinion that makes success less likely, while negative means harmful or critical in effect or attitude.
  • adverse
    6.0 4/m
    adverse often appears in formal contexts to mean harmful or opposing, while negative is broader and can mean critical or unfavorable.
  • disadvantageous
    6.8 0.1/m
    disadvantageous focuses on causing loss or difficulty in a situation, while negative covers harm, criticism, or an unfavorable attitude.
  • damaging
    4.6 2/m
    damaging describes words or actions that hurt someone's reputation, while negative refers more generally to harmful or critical effects or attitudes.
