paleographer

noun
specialist in ancient handwriting
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpeɪliˈɑːɡrəfər / (AmE), / ˌpeɪliˈɒɡrəfə / (BrE)
A person who specializes in the study and interpretation of ancient or historical handwriting and manuscripts.

Examples

A paleographer studies manuscripts
As a paleographer, she specializes in reading ancient Greek papyri.
The paleographer's expertise was essential for dating the historical documents accurately.
The museum hired a renowned paleographer to help decipher the medieval manuscripts.
