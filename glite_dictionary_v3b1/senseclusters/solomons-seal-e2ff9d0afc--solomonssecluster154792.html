Solomon's-seal

noun
plant with arching stems and drooping flowers
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑləmənzˌsil / (AmE), / ˈsɒləmənzˌsiːl / (BrE)
A plant of the genus Polygonatum, with arching stems, paired drooping greenish or yellowish-green flowers, and a thick rootstock marked with circular scars resembling a seal.

Examples

The Solomon's-seal plant
After the rain, drops clung to the bell-shaped flowers of the Solomon's-seal.
She recognized the plant as Solomon's-seal by the rows of scars on its thick root.
The woodland garden was filled with Solomon's-seal, its curved stems gracing the shade.
