patter

verb
make light, repeated sounds
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈpætɚ /; / ˈpætər / (AmE), / ˈpætə / (BrE)
to make light, rapid, repeated sounds, like rain falling or small feet moving

Examples

To patter on the floor
I could hear tiny feet pattering across the kitchen floor early in the morning.
Leaves pattered down onto the roof during the storm.
The rain began to patter gently on the windowpane as evening fell.
As we drank our tea, the rain continued to patter outside.
I love listening to the rain patter softly on the roof as I fall asleep.
The gentle spring shower began to patter on the windows.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drizzle - to rain lightly in very fine drops
  • synonym: drum - to strike a surface repeatedly to make a rhythmic sound
  • degree higher: pour - to rain heavily
