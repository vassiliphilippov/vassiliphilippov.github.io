transmission shaft

noun
shaft transferring motion from engine to wheels
4.4 4.2
Pronunciation: /trænz.ˈmɪʃ.ən ʃæft/ /; / /trænsm.ˈɪʃ.ən ʃæft/ (AmE), /trænz.ˈmɪʃ.ən ʃɑːft/ /; / /trænsm.ˈɪʃ.ən ʃɑːft/ (BrE)
a rotating shaft that transfers rotary motion from a vehicle's engine to its differential or wheels

Examples

Bent transmission shaft
After the accident, the mechanic discovered that the transmission shaft was bent and needed replacing.
The transmission shaft must be well-lubricated to prevent excessive wear and tear during operation.
The smooth transfer of power from the engine relies heavily on the condition of the transmission shaft.
