freak out

verb
lose control due to shock or fear
6.8 11/m
Pronunciation: / Ààfrik a ät / (AmE), / ÀàfriÀêk a ät / (BrE)
to suddenly become very anxious, upset, or afraid; to lose control due to shock or fear

Examples

To freak out suddenly
Don't freak out; everything is going to be fine.
He freaked out after hearing the sudden loud noise in the middle of the night.
She started to freak out when she realized her passport was missing.

Synonyms

    7.1 19/m
    freak is an informal verb meaning to react strongly from shock or fear, while freak out is a sudden loss of control.
  • wig out
    9.2 0.18/m
    wig out is an informal phrase for an extreme emotional reaction, often loud or panicky, while freak out is sudden fearful loss of control.
  • lose it
    7.5 6.31/m
    lose it means suddenly losing control of your emotions, often by anger or upset, while freak out centers on fear or shock.
  • flip out
    7.2 1.07/m
    flip out means losing control through anger or excitement, often louder and personal, while freak out leans more toward fear and shock.
  • panic
    2.3 7.82/m
    panic means feeling sudden, uncontrollable fear that stops clear thought, while freak out can include upset not always extreme panic.
  • come unhinged
    8.2 0.07/m
    come unhinged means to lose emotional or mental stability, often more serious and lasting, while freak out is sudden fear-driven loss of control.
  • go into a tizzy
    8.3 0.01/m
    go into a tizzy means to become flustered, agitated, or confused in a lighter way, while freak out implies strong fear and loss of control.
