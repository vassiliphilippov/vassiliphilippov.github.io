masculinize

verb
cause to develop male characteristics
2.3
Pronunciation: / ˈmæskjələˌnaɪz /; / ˈmæskjəlɪˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈmæskjʊlɪnaɪz / (BrE)
to cause a person, animal, or part of the body to develop male characteristics, often as a result of hormones

Examples

Hormones masculinize the body
Certain medical conditions may masculinize women by increasing levels of androgens.
Hormone therapy can masculinize the voice and body shape during gender transition.
Scientists studied how prenatal exposure to testosterone could masculinize an animal’s behaviour.
Hormone therapy may masculinise the body over time.
Some medical conditions can unexpectedly masculinise female patients.
The treatment was designed to masculinise certain physical features.

Related Terms

  • antonym: feminise - to cause to develop female or feminine characteristics
  • antonym: feminize - to cause to develop female characteristics, often via hormones
