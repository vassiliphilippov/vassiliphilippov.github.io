organicism

noun
theory emphasizing organism as a whole
Pronunciation: / ɔrˈɡænɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ɔːˈɡænɪˌsɪzəm / (BrE)
the theory that the overall organization of an organism, rather than the individual functions of its parts, determines its life processes

Examples

Organicism in biology
In biology, organicism contrasts with reductionism by emphasizing the whole organism.
Philosophers have debated organicism as it applies to both living creatures and societies.
The lecture explored the origins and implications of organicism in scientific thought.
