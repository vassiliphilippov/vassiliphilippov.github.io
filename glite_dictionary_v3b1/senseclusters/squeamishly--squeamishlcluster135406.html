squeamishly

adverb
in a squeamish or easily disgusted manner
Pronunciation: / ˈskwiːmɪʃli / (AmE), / ˈskwiːmɪʃli / (BrE)
In a way that shows discomfort, disgust, or an easily upset or nauseated feeling, especially when dealing with unpleasant things.

Examples

Look squeamishly at food
He looked squeamishly at the plate of raw oysters before declining to eat any.
She squeamishly avoided discussing topics that involved blood or medical procedures.
The boy squeamishly stepped back when asked to clean up the spilled garbage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: squeamish - easily made to feel sick, faint, or disgusted
