flak

noun
press agent who defends their client
Pronunciation: / flæk / (AmE), / flæk / (BrE)
a person, often a press agent or publicist, who promotes and defends their employer in the media, especially by deflecting criticism.

Examples

Company flak defends boss
A skilled flak can transform a scandal into a publicity opportunity for their company.
Every celebrity hires a flak to handle the media during controversies.
The senator's flak managed to dodge every tough question from the press.
As a seasoned flack, she knew exactly how to deflect tough questions from reporters.
The company hired a flack to handle its public relations during the crisis.
Whenever there was bad press, their chief flack spun a positive story for the media.

Related Terms

  • synonym: publicist - a person responsible for public relations and media coverage
  • synonym: spokesperson - a person who speaks on behalf of an organization
