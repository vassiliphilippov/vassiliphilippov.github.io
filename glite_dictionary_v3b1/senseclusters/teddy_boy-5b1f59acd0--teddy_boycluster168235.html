Teddy boy

noun
young British man in 1950s/60s Edwardian fashion
9.9 0.03/m

A young British man, especially in the 1950s and 1960s, known for wearing Edwardian-style clothes, including drape jackets and narrow trousers, often associated with a rebellious youth culture.

Examples

Teddy boy style
He styled his hair into a quiff, hoping to look like a Teddy boy from the old photographs.
My grandfather used to tell stories about his days as a Teddy boy dancing at the local hall.
The Teddy boys were well known for their flashy suits and rebellious attitudes in post-war London.

Synonyms

  • Ted
    9.9 34/m
    Ted is a chiefly British short form used in speech, while Teddy boy is the fuller historical label for Edwardian-dressed 1950s-60s youth.
