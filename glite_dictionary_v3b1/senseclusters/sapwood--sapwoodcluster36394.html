sapwood

noun
young outer wood in a tree conducting water
6.8 6.4
Pronunciation: /ˈsæp.wʊd/ (AmE), /ˈsæp.wʊd/ (BrE)
the soft, younger outer wood in a tree, found just beneath the bark, which conducts water and nutrients; typically paler than heartwood

Examples

The sapwood layer
Carpenters sometimes prefer sapwood for its lighter color and ease of use.
Moisture moves quickly through the sapwood before reaching the leaves.
The sapwood of the maple tree was infected by a fungus.

Related Terms

  • antonym: heartwood - the dense inner part of a tree trunk, no longer conducting water
