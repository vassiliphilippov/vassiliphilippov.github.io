right enough

adjective
sufficiently correct or justified
Pronunciation: / raɪt ɪˈnʌf / (AmE), / raɪt ɪˈnʌf / (BrE)
sufficiently correct or justified; correct or acceptable to a satisfactory degree

Examples

Right enough to say
He might not have all the facts, but he is right enough to raise the issue.
The prediction was right enough to convince most experts to proceed.
Your conclusion is right enough, though the details could use more work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: right - morally or factually correct
  • antonym: wrong - incorrect or not justifiable
