grayout

verb
to disable visually
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɡreɪˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪˌaʊt / (BrE)
to make a control, option, or part of a computer screen appear dim or inactive, usually by displaying it in grey to indicate it cannot be used

Examples

To grayout a menu
The software will grayout the print option if no printer is connected.
When you enter the wrong password, the system may grayout the login button temporarily.
Some menu items are grayout until you select a file to open.

Related Terms

  • antonym: activate - to make something active or usable
  • synonym: disable - to make something unable to function or be used
