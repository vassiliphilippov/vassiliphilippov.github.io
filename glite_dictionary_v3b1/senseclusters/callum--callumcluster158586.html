callum

noun
hardened area on organism
Pronunciation: / ˈkæləm / (AmE), / ˈkæləm / (BrE)
a thickened or hardened area, especially on a plant or animal structure, often serving as protection

Examples

The callum on stem
The callum on the plant's stem helps it resist damage from insects.
Scientists observed a thick callum forming on the injured part of the animal's shell.
A callum can develop as a protective response to repeated stress or injury.

Related Terms

  • synonym: callus - a thickened and hardened part of the skin or tissue
