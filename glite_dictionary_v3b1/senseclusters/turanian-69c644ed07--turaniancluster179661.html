Turanian

adjective
Central Asian peoples
Pronunciation: / tuˈreɪniən / (AmE), / tjʊəˈreɪniən / (BrE)
relating to a historical group of Central Asian peoples or languages once classified together by 19th-century scholars

Examples

Turanian languages group
The Turanian hypothesis is no longer widely accepted in modern linguistics.
Some 19th-century maps labeled large areas of Central Asia as inhabited by Turanian peoples.
He studied the Turanian languages as part of his research into the history of Eurasia.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Ural-Altaic - relating to a proposed language family including Uralic and Altaic languages
