lockdown

noun
prisoners confined to cells as a security measure
2.4 4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑkˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈlɒkˌdaʊn / (BrE)
a situation or period when people are required to stay in a specific place and movement is restricted, usually for safety or security reasons (such as in prisons or during emergencies)

Examples

Citywide lockdown
After the riot, the warden initiated a full lockdown to restore order.
During the lockdown, all prisoners were kept in their cells for 24 hours a day.
The prison was placed under lockdown after the fight broke out between inmates.
During the lockdown, all non-essential businesses were closed and people stayed at home.
The school went into lockdown after a security threat was reported nearby.
Many families found creative ways to stay connected while under lockdown.
