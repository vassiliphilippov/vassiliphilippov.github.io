demagnetization

noun
removal of magnetization
7.1 6.5
Pronunciation: /diː.mæɡ.nə.taɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /də.mæɡ.nə.taɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /də.mæɡ.nə.taɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /diː.mæɡ.nə.taɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of removing magnetization from an object, especially a magnetic material

Examples

The demagnetization process
Exposure to heat can result in the demagnetization of a magnet.
Repeated shocks may cause partial demagnetization of certain ferromagnetic materials.
The technician performed demagnetization of the hard drive to erase all data.
After repeated demagnetisation, the metal no longer responded to the magnetic field.
Proper demagnetisation is crucial before calibrating sensitive electronic instruments.
The engineer performed a demagnetisation procedure on the tools before use.
