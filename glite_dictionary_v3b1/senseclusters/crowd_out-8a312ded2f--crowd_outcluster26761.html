crowd out

verb
force out by taking up space
4.9 1.02/m
Pronunciation: / kraÊŠd aÊŠt / (AmE), / kraÊŠd aÊŠt / (BrE)
to force someone or something out of a place or situation by being more numerous, strong, or prominent

Examples

To crowd out others
Her loud voice seemed to crowd out all other conversation in the room.
Invasive species can crowd out native plants in an ecosystem.
Rising rents may crowd out small businesses in the city center.
Invasive plant species often crowd out native vegetation in the ecosystem.
Rising property prices can crowd out young families from the neighborhood.
The new shopping mall may crowd out local businesses in the area.

Synonyms
