gizzard

noun
muscular digestive pouch in birds
5.5 5.6
Pronunciation: /ˈgɪz.ərd/ (AmE), /ˈgɪz.əd/ (BrE)
a thick-walled muscular pouch in the digestive tract of birds and some reptiles, used for grinding food, typically with the aid of ingested stones or grit

Examples

Fried gizzard snack
At the market, you can buy fried gizzard as a popular street food snack.
Some birds swallow small stones to aid their gizzard in grinding food.
The chicken's gizzard helps break down hard seeds that it eats.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stomach - the organ in animals where digestion of food begins
