hands-down

adjective
easily achieved; without effort
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈhændzˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈhændzˌdaʊn / (BrE)
(of a victory or achievement) achieved easily or without serious effort or competition.

Examples

A hands-down win
She won the competition hands-down, impressing all the judges with her performance.
The proposal was accepted hands-down at the meeting.
The team secured a hands-down victory in the championship match.

Related Terms

  • synonym: easy - not difficult; requiring little effort
  • antonym: hard-fought - achieved with a lot of effort and struggle
