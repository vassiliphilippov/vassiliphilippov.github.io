hard-boiled egg

noun
egg cooked until both yolk and white are solid
Pronunciation: / ˌhɑrd ˈbɔɪld ɛg / (AmE), / ˌhɑːd ˈbɔɪld ɛg / (BrE)
An egg that has been boiled until both the white and the yolk are solid.

Examples

A hard-boiled egg snack
After peeling the hard-boiled egg, he sprinkled it with a little salt.
I packed a hard-boiled egg in my lunch for extra protein.
She sliced a hard-boiled egg to put on her salad.

Related Terms

  • antonym: soft-boiled egg - an egg boiled so the yolk remains runny
