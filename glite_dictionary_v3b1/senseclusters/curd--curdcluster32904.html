curd

noun
coagulated milk used for cheese making
Pronunciation: / kɝd / (AmE), / kɜːd / (BrE)
A soft, white substance formed when milk coagulates, used to make cheese and other dairy products.

Examples

Fresh curd cheese
Miss Muffet was eating curd and whey when the spider appeared.
She added lemon juice to milk to form curd for the recipe.
The farmer collected fresh curd every morning to make cheese.

Related Terms

  • antonym: whey - the watery part of milk that remains after the formation of curds
