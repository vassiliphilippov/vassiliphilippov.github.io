sparkle

noun
quality of shining or glittering
1.6 1.28/m
Pronunciation: / Ààsp…ërk…ôl /; / Ààsp…ërk(…ô)l / (AmE), / Ààsp…ëÀêk…ôl /; / Ààsp…ëÀêk(…ô)l / (BrE)
a quality of shining with bright flashes of light; brilliance or glitter

Examples

A diamond sparkle
His eyes had a mischievous sparkle when he told the story.
The sparkle of the diamond caught her eye under the bright lights.
There was a sparkle on the lake's surface at sunrise.
A sudden sparkle from the candle lit up the dark room.
The sparkle of sunlight on the water caught my eye.
Tiny diamonds added a delicate sparkle to her necklace.

Synonyms

  • glitter
    1.0 1.99/m
    glitter is small bright bits or a steady sparkling surface, while sparkle names the quick flashes and bright brilliance you see.
  • scintillation
    8.4 0.02/m
    scintillation is a brief, often technical word for tiny light flashes, while sparkle is a common word for lively, bright shining.
