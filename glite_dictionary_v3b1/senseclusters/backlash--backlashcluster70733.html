backlash

verb
to react strongly
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌlæʃ / (AmE), / ˈbækˌlæʃ / (BrE)
to react strongly or negatively against an idea, action, or change

Examples

People backlash against
Many citizens backlashed against the government's decision to raise taxes.
The company faced criticism when customers backlashed over the sudden price increase.
After the announcement, several groups backlashed by organizing public demonstrations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accept - to receive or approve willingly
  • synonym: protest - to express strong objection or disapproval
