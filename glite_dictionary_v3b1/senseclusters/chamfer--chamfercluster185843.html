chamfer

verb
cut a sloping edge on a material
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃæmfər /; / ˈtʃæm.fər / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːmfə /; / ˈtʃæm.fə / (BrE)
to cut or shape an edge or corner so that it is sloping or beveled instead of sharp

Examples

To chamfer a corner
Before assembly, each metal piece must be chamfered according to the blueprints.
Stone columns are often chamfered to enhance their appearance and prevent sharp corners.
The carpenter carefully chamfered the edges of the table for a smoother finish.
Make sure to chamfer the edges to prevent splintering.
The craftsman carefully chamfered the corners for a smoother finish.
You should chamfer the ends of the pipe before assembling them.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bevel - to cut or shape at an angle other than 90 degrees
