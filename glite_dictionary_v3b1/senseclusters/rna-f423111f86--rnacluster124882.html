RNA

noun
nucleic acid that transmits genetic information
Pronunciation: / ˌɑr ˌɛn ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌɑː ˌɛn ˈeɪ / (BrE)
a nucleic acid present in all living cells, acting mainly as a messenger carrying instructions from DNA for controlling the synthesis of proteins, and in some viruses serving as genetic material

Examples

Messenger RNA
Messenger RNA plays a crucial role in the process of protein synthesis.
Scientists use RNA sequencing to study gene expression in different types of cells.
The COVID-19 virus stores its genetic code as RNA instead of DNA.

Related Terms

  • antonym: DNA - deoxyribonucleic acid, the molecule that carries genetic instructions in living things
