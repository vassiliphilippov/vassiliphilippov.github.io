Continent

noun
the mainland of Europe (especially from a British perspective)
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːn.tɪ.nənt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.tɪ.nənt / (BrE)
the mainland of Europe as distinct from the British Isles, especially in reference to Britain or Ireland.

Examples

The Continent of Europe
Many British families spend their holidays on the Continent each summer.
Some English traditions are quite different from those found on the Continent.
Trade between the UK and the Continent increased after the construction of the Channel Tunnel.
