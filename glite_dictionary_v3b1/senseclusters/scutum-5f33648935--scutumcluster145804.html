Scutum

proper noun
constellation in southern sky
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈskuːtəm / (AmE), / ˈskjuːtəm / (BrE)
a small constellation in the southern sky, named after the Latin word for shield

Examples

Scutum constellation
The constellation Scutum is best seen in the late summer months from the Northern Hemisphere.
Astronomers often use Scutum as a reference point when mapping the Milky Way.
With a small telescope, you can spot several star clusters within Scutum.
