psych

verb
to mentally prepare
7.4
Pronunciation: /saɪk/ (AmE), /saɪk/ (BrE)
to mentally prepare or influence someone (including yourself), often to boost confidence or unsettle an opponent before a challenge

Examples

To psych yourself up
She tried to psych herself up before giving the important presentation.
The coach used music to psych the team before the final game.
He attempted to psych his opponent by staring him down before the match.
She needed to psych herself before stepping onto the stage for her speech.
The coach helped the team psych up before the championship game.
He tried to psych his friend into feeling confident about the interview.

Related Terms

  • antonym: discourage - to make someone less confident or enthusiastic
  • synonym: motivate - to encourage or inspire someone to take action
