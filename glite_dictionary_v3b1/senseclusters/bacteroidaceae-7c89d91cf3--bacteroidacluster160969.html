Bacteroidaceae

noun
family of bacteria in animal intestines or mucous surfaces
Pronunciation: / bækˌtɪəriˈoʊdəˌsiː / (AmE), / bækˌtɪəriəʊˈdeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of bacteria that typically inhabit the alimentary canal or mucous surfaces of warm-blooded animals, and are sometimes linked with acute infections

Examples

Bacteroidaceae family bacteria
Many species of Bacteroidaceae are normal inhabitants of the human digestive tract.
Researchers have found that Bacteroidaceae can sometimes cause infections in immunocompromised patients.
The composition of the Bacteroidaceae family can influence gut health and disease susceptibility.
