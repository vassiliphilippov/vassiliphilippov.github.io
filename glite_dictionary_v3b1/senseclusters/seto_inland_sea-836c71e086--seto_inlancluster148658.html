Seto Inland Sea

proper noun
sea in southern Japan bordered by three main islands
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪtoʊ ˈɪnlənd si / (AmE), / ˈseɪtəʊ ˈɪnlənd siː / (BrE)
an arm of the Pacific Ocean in southern Japan, bordered by the islands of Honshu, Shikoku, and Kyushu, and linked to the Sea of Japan; the largest city on its coast is Hiroshima.

Examples

The Seto Inland Sea coast
A ferry crosses the Seto Inland Sea from Honshu to Shikoku several times a day.
Fishing communities have thrived along the shores of the Seto Inland Sea for centuries.
The Seto Inland Sea is famous for its scenic islands and historic towns.
