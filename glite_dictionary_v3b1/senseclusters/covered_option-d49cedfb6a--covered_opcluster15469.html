covered option

noun
option contract backed by underlying assets
2.0 6.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkʌv.ərd ˈɑːp.ʃən/ /; / /ˈkʌv.ərd ˈɔːp.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈkʌv.əd ˈɒp.ʃən/ (BrE)
a put or call option for which the seller holds the underlying shares or sufficient assets to cover the obligation

Examples

A covered option trade
Many investors use covered options as a conservative trading strategy.
She sold a covered option to reduce her investment risk.
Writing a covered option requires you to own the underlying shares.
