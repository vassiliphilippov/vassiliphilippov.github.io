homospory

noun
production of a single spore type
9.5
Pronunciation: / hoʊˈmɑːspəri / (AmE), / həʊˈmɒspəri / (BrE)
the production of only one type of spore, as seen in some plants and fungi

Examples

Homospory in plants
Botanists study homospory to understand the reproductive strategies of ancient plants.
Many ferns exhibit homospory, producing spores that all look alike.
The difference between homospory and heterospory is fundamental in plant evolution.

Related Terms

  • antonym: heterospory - the production of two different types of spores
