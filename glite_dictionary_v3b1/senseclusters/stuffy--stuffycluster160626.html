stuffy

adjective
lacking fresh air
2.7 0.73/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆstÊŒfi / (AmE), / ËˆstÊŒfi / (BrE)
(of a room or building) lacking fresh air and unpleasant to breathe in.

Examples

Stuffy room
It felt stuffy in the small classroom with so many people inside.
The office became stuffy after the windows were closed all afternoon.
We had to open a door because it was too stuffy during the meeting.

Synonyms

  • close
    3.4 1.98/m
    close describes a warm, uncomfortable room with poor air, while stuffy focuses on an airless feeling that makes breathing unpleasant.
  • airless
    4.9 0.52/m
    airless means there is little or no fresh air in a space, while stuffy stresses the unpleasant, close feeling that makes breathing hard.
  • unventilated
    6.3 0.07/m
    unventilated describes a space lacking proper air circulation, while stuffy is the everyday word people use for unpleasant, stale air.

Related Terms

  • synonym: airless - lacking air or ventilation
  • antonym: ventilated - allowing air to flow through; having good airflow
