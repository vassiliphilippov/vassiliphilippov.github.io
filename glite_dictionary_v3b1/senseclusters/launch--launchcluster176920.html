launch

verb
send a ship into water for the first time
Pronunciation: / lɔnʧ /; / lɔntʃ / (AmE), / lɔːntʃ / (BrE)
to send or propel something, such as a ship, boat, or vehicle, into motion or into the air

Examples

To launch a rocket
Hundreds of people gathered to watch them launch the new cruise liner.
The ship will be officially launched at the dockyard next week.
They launched the vessel after years of construction and preparation.
After months of preparation, they finally launched the new satellite into orbit.
Spectators gathered to watch as the navy launched the newly built vessel into the water.
The company plans to launch its latest electric car this summer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: commission - to officially put a ship into active service
  • antonym: land - to bring a vehicle, aircraft, or vessel to the ground or shore
  • synonym: send - to cause something to go or be taken to a particular destination, often with force or direction
