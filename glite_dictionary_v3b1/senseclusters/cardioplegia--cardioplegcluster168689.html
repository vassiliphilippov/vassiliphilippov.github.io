cardioplegia

noun
stopping the heart
9.5
Pronunciation: /kɑːr.di.oʊ.ˈpliː.dʒə/ /; / /kɑːr.di.ə.ˈpliː.dʒə/ (AmE), /kɑː.di.ə.ˈpliː.dʒə/ /; / /kɑː.di.əʊ.ˈpliː.dʒə/ (BrE)
a medical technique or condition where the heart is intentionally stopped during surgery to protect the heart muscle

Examples

Cardioplegia during surgery
The surgeon administered cardioplegia to safely stop the patient's heart during the bypass procedure.
Proper delivery of cardioplegia is essential to protect the heart muscle during open-heart surgery.
After the operation, the medical team carefully reversed the effects of cardioplegia to restart the heart.
During open-heart surgery, the doctor induced cardioplegia to protect the patient's heart.
The use of cardioplegia allows surgeons to operate on a still and bloodless heart.
Proper administration of cardioplegia is essential for successful cardiac procedures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: arrhythmia - an abnormal heart rhythm
  • synonym: asystole - a state of no cardiac electrical activity
  • antonym: cardiac arrest - sudden stopping of the heart's function
  • synonym: myocardial protection - methods to protect the heart muscle during surgery
