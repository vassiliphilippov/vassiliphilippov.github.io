staphylococci

noun
cluster-forming spherical bacteria; some cause infections
7.0 7.5
Pronunciation: /stæ.fə.lə.ˈkɑːk.aɪ/ /; / /stæ.fɪ.lə.ˈkɑːk.aɪ/ (AmE), /stæ.fə.lə.ˈkɒk.aɪ/ /; / /stæ.fɪ.lə.ˈkɒk.aɪ/ (BrE)
Spherical Gram-positive bacteria that tend to form irregular clusters; some species cause infections such as boils and septicemia.

Examples

Staphylococci bacteria clusters
Doctors often treat skin infections caused by staphylococci with antibiotics.
Some staphylococci are harmless, while others can cause serious disease in humans.
Under the microscope, staphylococci appear as grape-like clusters of cells.
