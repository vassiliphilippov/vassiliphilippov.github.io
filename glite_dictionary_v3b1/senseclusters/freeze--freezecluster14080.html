freeze

verb
suddenly stop moving; become motionless
1.6 8.53/m
Pronunciation: / friÀêz / (AmE), / friÀêz / (BrE)
to stop moving or become unable to move, often suddenly and usually because of fear or surprise

Examples

To freeze in fear
If you see a bear in the woods, it's best to freeze and stay still.
She froze with her hand on the doorknob when she heard a strange noise.
When the teacher yelled, the students froze in their seats.

Synonyms

  • stop cold
    5.2 0.29/m
    stop cold is a sudden complete stop from shock or fear, while freeze is to become unable to move from fear.
  • stop dead
    3.9 1.31/m
    stop dead means to stop moving or acting suddenly and completely because of shock, while freeze is to become unable to move from fear.
  • freeze like a deer in the headlights
    7.7 0.00/m
    freeze like a deer in the headlights describes a person who becomes unable to move from fear, while freeze means to stop moving in general.
  • stop (someone) in (their) tracks
    3.9 0.35/m
    stop (someone) in (their) tracks means causing someone to stop suddenly in surprise, while freeze means to become unable to move from fear.
