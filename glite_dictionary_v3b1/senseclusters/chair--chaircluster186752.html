chair

noun
person presiding over a meeting or group
3.1 19/m
Pronunciation: / t É…õr / (AmE), / t É…õÀê / (BrE)
the person who leads or presides at a meeting, committee, or organization

Examples

Chair of the meeting
After three years as chair, she decided to step down from the position.
Please submit your questions to the chair before the discussion begins.
The chair called the meeting to order promptly at nine o'clock.

Synonyms

  • chairperson
    3.5 2.81/m
    chairperson is the neutral leader of a meeting or group, while chair is the common title used in many organizations.
  • presiding officer
    6.1 0.26/m
    presiding officer is a formal person who enforces rules in an assembly, while chair leads the meeting more generally.
  • chairman
    5.7 0.00/m
    chairman is the traditional male title often used for leaders, while chair is gender-neutral and commonly used today.
  • chairwoman
    3.2 1.50/m
    chairwoman is the female title used for a leader, while chair is the neutral word people use to avoid gender.
  • presider
    7.3 0.10/m
    presider leads a ceremony or event in a formal way, while chair leads meetings and often manages committees.
  • moderator
    3.9 5.36/m
    moderator manages debate and keeps speakers fair and brief, while chair runs the whole meeting and makes final decisions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chairwoman - the female leader of a meeting or organization
