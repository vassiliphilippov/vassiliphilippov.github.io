predicate

noun
term asserted about a sentence's subject (logic)
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛdɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈprɛdɪkət / (BrE)
the part of a sentence or proposition that says something about the subject, usually containing the verb

Examples

Sentence predicate
In the sentence 'All dogs are mammals,' 'are mammals' is the predicate.
Logic students learn to identify the predicate in different types of propositions.
The predicate often tells us something about the subject's state or action.
Every complete sentence must contain both a subject and a predicate.
In the sentence 'The dog barked loudly,' the predicate is 'barked loudly.'
Teachers often underline the predicate to help students identify the sentence's main action.
