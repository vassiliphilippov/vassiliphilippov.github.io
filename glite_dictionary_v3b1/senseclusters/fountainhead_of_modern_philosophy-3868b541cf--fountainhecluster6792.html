fountainhead of modern philosophy

noun
original source or leader in modern philosophy
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈfaʊntnˌhɛd əv ˈmɑdərn fəˈlɑsəfi / (AmE), / ˈfaʊntənˌhɛd əv ˈmɒdən fəˈlɒsəfi / (BrE)
the original source or leading figure in the development of modern philosophy

Examples

The fountainhead of modern philosophy
Many scholars view Immanuel Kant as the fountainhead of modern philosophy due to his groundbreaking work.
René Descartes is often called the fountainhead of modern philosophy for his influential ideas on reason and skepticism.
Some argue that Francis Bacon was actually the true fountainhead of modern philosophy because of his emphasis on scientific method.
