nonrival

adjective
shared without depletion
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˈraɪvəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈraɪvəl / (BrE)
describing something that can be used or consumed by multiple people at the same time without reducing its availability to others

Examples

Nonrival resource
A national park is often considered a nonrival resource because many people can enjoy it at once.
Digital music files are nonrival since one person's listening does not prevent others from listening too.
The internet is an example of a nonrival service when it is not congested.

Related Terms

  • synonym: public good - a good that is both nonrival and nonexcludable
  • antonym: rival - describing something that cannot be used by more than one person at the same time without reducing its availability
