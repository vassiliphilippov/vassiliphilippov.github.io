on high

adverb
in a high or elevated state or position
7.4 1.98/m
Pronunciation: / É‘n haÉª / (AmE), / É’n haÉª / (BrE)
in a state of excitement, optimism, or in an elevated or high position; sometimes used to refer to a source of authority or heaven

Examples

Looked on high
He looked on high from his office on the top floor.
Many believe that our fate is determined by powers on high.
The crowd was on high after the team's unexpected victory.

Synonyms

  • in high spirits
    3.0 0.31/m

  • on top of the world
    2.8 0.73/m
    on top of the world is extreme happiness and success, while on high often means being excited or up above, sometimes heavenly.
  • high up
    2.3 1.79/m
    high up describes a physical place far above the ground, while on high also suggests excitement or a heavenly source.
