on high

adverb
in a high or elevated state or position
Pronunciation: / ɑn haɪ / (AmE), / ɒn haɪ / (BrE)
in a state of excitement, optimism, or in an elevated or high position; sometimes used to refer to a source of authority or heaven

Examples

Looked on high
He looked on high from his office on the top floor.
Many believe that our fate is determined by powers on high.
The crowd was on high after the team's unexpected victory.

Related Terms

  • synonym: above - at a higher level or place
  • antonym: down below - in a lower position; opposite of above
