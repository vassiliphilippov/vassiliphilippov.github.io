fascicle

noun
bundle of fibers (nerve or muscle)
Pronunciation: / ˈfæsɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈfæsɪkəl / (BrE)
A bundle of fibers, especially nerve or muscle fibers, bound together within the body.

Examples

Muscle fascicle
Damage to a nerve fascicle can impair sensation in the affected area.
During dissection, the scientist separated a fascicle from the main nerve trunk.
Each fascicle in the muscle contains many individual fibers.
