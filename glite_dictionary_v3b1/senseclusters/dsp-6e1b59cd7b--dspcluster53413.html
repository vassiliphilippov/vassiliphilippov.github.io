DSP

noun
police rank
Pronunciation: / diː ɛs piː / (AmE), / diː ɛs piː / (BrE)
a police rank meaning Deputy Superintendent of Police, a senior officer in some countries' police forces

Examples

DSP officer
After the promotion, she served as a DSP overseeing law enforcement operations in the district.
The DSP addressed the media about the recent changes in police procedures.
He aspired to become a DSP after years of dedicated service as an inspector.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: inspector - a police officer of lower rank than a superintendent
  • degree higher: superintendent - a senior police officer in charge of a district or department
