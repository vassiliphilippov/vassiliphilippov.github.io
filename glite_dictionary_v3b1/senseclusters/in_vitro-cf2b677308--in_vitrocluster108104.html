in vitro

adjective, adverb
performed outside a living organism
6.2 4.4
Pronunciation: /ɪn ˈviː.troʊ/ /; / /ɪn ˈvɪ.troʊ/ (AmE), /ɪn ˈviː.trəʊ/ /; / /ɪn ˈvɪ.trəʊ/ (BrE)
happening outside a living organism, in an artificial environment such as a test tube or laboratory (used in science and medicine)

Examples

In vitro experiment
Many genetic experiments are first performed in vitro to assess safety.
The development of in vitro fertilization has helped many people conceive children.
The scientists studied the effects of the drug in vitro before moving on to animal trials.
Many drugs are first tested in vitro before being given to patients.
The fertilization of the egg was achieved in vitro using specialized laboratory techniques.
The scientists conducted the experiment in vitro to observe the effects without interference from the organism's immune system.
