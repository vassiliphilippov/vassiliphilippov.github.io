tortilla

noun
thin flatbread made from corn or wheat
Pronunciation: / tɔrˈtiːə / (AmE), / tɔːˈtiːə / (BrE)
A thin, round, unleavened flatbread typically made from cornmeal or wheat flour, commonly used in Mexican and Central American cuisine.

Examples

Fresh tortillas
I made chicken tacos using fresh tortillas from the market.
She heated a tortilla on the stove before filling it with beans and cheese.
The restaurant offers both corn and wheat tortillas for their burritos.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flatbread - a type of bread made with flour, water, and salt, and then thoroughly rolled into flattened dough
