moderate

verb
preside over a discussion or debate
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑdəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɒdəˌreɪt / (BrE)
to act as the person in charge of a discussion, debate, or meeting, ensuring it proceeds in an orderly way

Examples

To moderate a debate
He moderated a lively Q&A session after the lecture.
She was invited to moderate the panel discussion at the conference.
The teacher will moderate the debate to ensure everyone gets a turn to speak.
