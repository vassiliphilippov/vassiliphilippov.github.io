Apostle Paul

proper noun
Christian missionary and New Testament author
7.2
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈpɔː.səl pɔːl/ /; / /ə.ˈpɑː.səl pɑːl/ (AmE), /ə.ˈpɒs.əl pɔːl/ (BrE)
(in the New Testament) a Christian missionary to the Gentiles and author of several Epistles, regarded as an Apostle despite not being present at the Last Supper.

Examples

Apostle Paul letters
According to tradition, Apostle Paul wrote several letters to early churches that are now part of the New Testament.
Before his conversion, Apostle Paul was known as Saul and persecuted Christians.
Many Christians regard Apostle Paul as a major figure in the spread of Christianity across the Roman Empire.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Saint Paul - another name for Apostle Paul, Christian missionary and writer of Epistles
  • synonym: Saul of Tarsus - name of Apostle Paul before his conversion
