an area of land or a natural depression where water collects and drains into a river, lake, or sea

Examples

River basin
Dams built in the river basin help control periodic flooding.
Intensive farming can affect the ecology of an entire basin.
The Mississippi basin covers an enormous part of North America.
Rainwater collected in the natural basin, eventually forming a small lake at the bottom.
The Colorado River flows through a vast basin surrounded by mountains and plateaus.
The Nile basin is home to millions of people and covers several countries.

Synonyms

  • drainage basin
    4.0 0.11/m
    a drainage basin is the whole land area that drains into one river, while a basin is any low-lying place where water collects.
  • catchment basin
    6.2 0.00/m
    a catchment basin collects rainfall and runoff for a single outlet, while a basin often means a natural hollow where water sits.
  • catchment area
    5.6 0.21/m
    a catchment area is the wider land that funnels rain to one outlet, while a basin can mean a smaller hollow or depression.
  • drainage area
    3.7 0.05/m
    a drainage area describes the full land draining into a water body, while a basin often suggests a natural dip where water pools.
  • catchment
    6.4 0.97/m
    catchment can mean land or a man-made structure for collecting water, while a basin usually means a natural hollow.

Related Terms

  • antonym: plain - a large area of flat, often treeless land
  • synonym: valley - a low area between hills or mountains, often with a river running through it
  • synonym: watershed - an area of land that separates waters flowing to different rivers or basins
