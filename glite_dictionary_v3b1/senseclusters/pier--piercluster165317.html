pier

noun
structure projecting into water for boats
1.4 8.07/m
Pronunciation: / pÉªr / (AmE), / pÉªÉ™ / (BrE)
A structure built out from the shore into the water, supported by piles, used for docking boats and providing access to ships.

Examples

Fishing pier
Crowds gathered on the pier to celebrate the arrival of the new cruise ship.
The fishermen tied their boat to the old wooden pier after a long day at sea.
We walked along the pier to watch the boats come in at sunset.

Synonyms

  • wharf
    6.7 2.59/m

  • landing
    2.6 6.33/m
    a landing is a small platform where boats load or unload people or goods, while a pier is a larger structure reaching into the water.
