busy

adjective
unavailable for use (of facility)
1.0 3.00/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆbÉªzi / (AmE), / ËˆbÉªzi / (BrE)
(of a telephone line, lavatory, or similar facility) unavailable for use because it is already occupied or engaged.

Examples

Busy phone line
Every time I call her, the line is busy.
Sorry, the conference room is busy with another meeting right now.
The bathroom is busy, so you'll have to wait your turn.

Synonyms

    3.2 0.00/m
    engaged means reserved or already in use for a phone, seat, or room, while busy means not free to use.
  • in use
    1.0 6.33/m
    in use means being used so something is not available, while busy highlights that a facility is occupied or engaged.
  • occupied
    1.8 0.00/m
    occupied means held or filled by someone or activity, while busy focuses on a device or place not free for use.
