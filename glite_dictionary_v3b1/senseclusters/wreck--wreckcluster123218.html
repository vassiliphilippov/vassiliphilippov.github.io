wreck

noun
damaged person or thing
2.4 3.4
Pronunciation: /rek/ (AmE), /rek/ (BrE)
a person, vehicle, building, or thing that is badly damaged, ruined, or in a state of disrepair

Examples

An emotional wreck
After the stressful week, he felt like an emotional wreck.
The abandoned car was a total wreck after years in the junkyard.
The hurricane left the coastal town a complete wreck.
The old wreck of a car sat abandoned at the edge of the field for years.
After the storm, the building was nothing but a wreck of twisted metal and broken glass.
Tourists visited the wreck of the ancient ship lying on the sea floor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disaster - a sudden event causing great damage or loss
  • antonym: restoration - the act of repairing or bringing something back to its original condition
  • synonym: ruin - the physical destruction or disintegration of something
  • antonym: treasure - something of great value, often found in good condition
