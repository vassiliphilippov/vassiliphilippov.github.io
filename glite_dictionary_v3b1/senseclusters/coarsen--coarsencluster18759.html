coarsen

verb
make or become less refined
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrsən / (AmE), / ˈkɔːsən / (BrE)
to make or become less refined, delicate, or more rough in quality, character, or texture

Examples

To coarsen manners
As years passed, the harsh environment began to coarsen his manners.
Political debates often coarsen during election season.
Repeated exposure to crude language can coarsen a person's sense of humor.
Repeated washing can coarsen the fabric over time.
The comedian's jokes seemed to coarsen as the night went on.
Years of exposure to the sun have coarsened her skin.
