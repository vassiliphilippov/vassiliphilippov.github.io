American germander

noun
North American flowering subshrub
Pronunciation: / əˈmɛrɪkən dʒərˈmændər / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən dʒɜːˈmændə / (BrE)
A perennial subshrub native to North America, with serrated leaves and cream to purple flowers arranged in spikelike racemes.

Examples

American germander flowers
Bees are often attracted to the pale purple flowers of the American germander.
