fourteener

noun
fourteen-syllable verse
9.1
Pronunciation: /fɑːr.ˈtiː.nər/ /; / /fɔːr.ˈtiː.nər/ (AmE), /fɔː.ˈtiː.nə/ (BrE)
a line of verse with fourteen syllables, often in iambic heptameter, or a poem made of such lines

Examples

A poetic fourteener
The poet experimented with a fourteener to give his ballad a unique rhythm.
Many traditional English ballads are written in the fourteener form, making them easy to sing.
She counted each syllable carefully to ensure her line was a perfect fourteener.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sonnet - a poem of fourteen lines with a specific rhyme scheme
