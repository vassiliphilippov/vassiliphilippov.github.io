tete-a-tete

adverb
in private between two people
Pronunciation: / teɪtə teɪt / (AmE), / teɪt ə teɪt / (BrE)
In private, with only two people and no one else present; privately and intimately between two individuals.

Examples

Speak tete-a-tete
After the party, they enjoyed a long tete-a-tete on the balcony.
The two leaders met tete-a-tete to discuss sensitive issues.
We spent the afternoon talking tete-a-tete in the quiet café.

Related Terms

  • antonym: in public - in a place or situation where others are present and can see
  • synonym: privately - in a private manner; without others present
