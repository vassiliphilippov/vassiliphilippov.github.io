verticalism

noun
strict hierarchy
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈvɝːtɪkəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈvɜːtɪkəˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
a system or style characterized by strict hierarchy or vertical organization, where authority flows from the top down

Examples

Corporate verticalism
The company's verticalism limited communication between different departments.
Critics argue that verticalism can slow down decision-making in large organizations.
Some modern businesses are moving away from verticalism toward more flexible structures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: egalitarianism - a belief in or system of equal rights and opportunities for all
  • synonym: hierarchy - a system in which people or things are ranked one above another
