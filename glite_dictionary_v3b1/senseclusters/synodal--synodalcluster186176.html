synodal

adjective
astronomical period
9.9
Pronunciation: / sɪˈnoʊdəl / (AmE), / sɪˈnəʊdəl / (BrE)
relating to the period between successive alignments of celestial bodies, such as the moon and the sun, in astronomy.

Examples

Synodal month
The synodal month is the time it takes for the moon to return to the same phase.
Scientists use synodal periods to calculate the timing of eclipses and planetary alignments.
The synodal cycle of Venus helps astronomers predict its appearances in the night sky.

Related Terms

  • synonym: synodic - relating to the period between similar phases of a celestial body as seen from Earth
