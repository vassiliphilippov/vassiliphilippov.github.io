stiff-necked

adjective
proudly stubborn
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪfˌnɛkt / (AmE), / ˈstɪfˌnɛkt / (BrE)
stubborn and unwilling to change your opinions or behavior

Examples

A stiff-necked person
He remained stiff-necked despite everyone's advice to reconsider his position.
Her stiff-necked attitude made negotiations extremely difficult.
The stiff-necked leader refused all proposals for compromise.
He remained stiff-necked even when presented with clear evidence.
Her stiff-necked refusal to apologize only made things worse.
The company’s stiff-necked policies frustrated many of its employees.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flexible - willing to change or adapt
  • synonym: obstinate - stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion
  • synonym: stubborn - unwilling to change one's attitude or position
