vindicatory

adjective
serving as punishment or retribution
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪndəˌkeɪtɔri /; / ˈvɪndɪkəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈvɪndɪkətəri / (BrE)
relating to or intended as retribution or punishment; serving to vindicate by requital

Examples

Vindicatory punishment
Her actions, though harsh, had a distinctly vindicatory character meant to restore justice.
Some philosophers debate whether punishment should be vindicatory or rehabilitative in nature.
The court imposed a vindicatory sentence to reflect the seriousness of the crime.
Some critics argue that a vindicatory approach to punishment fails to promote rehabilitation.
The government's actions were widely perceived as vindicatory rather than just.
The judge imposed a harsh sentence in keeping with the vindicatory nature of the law.

Related Terms

  • synonym: retributive - relating to or involving retribution; deserved punishment
