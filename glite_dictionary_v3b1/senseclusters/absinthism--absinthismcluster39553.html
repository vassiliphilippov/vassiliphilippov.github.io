absinthism

noun
illness from absinthe
Pronunciation: / æbˈsɪnθɪzəm / (AmE), / æbˈsɪnθɪzəm / (BrE)
a harmful condition caused by drinking too much absinthe, often involving physical and mental health problems such as poisoning, hallucinations, or tremors

Examples

Absinthism symptoms
In the 19th century, many artists suffered from absinthism due to their frequent drinking habits.
Doctors warned that absinthism could lead to serious mental and physical health issues.
The decline of absinthe's popularity was partly due to fears about absinthism and its effects.
Chronic absinthism was a serious problem among artists in 19th-century France.
Doctors warned that repeated episodes of absinthism could lead to permanent neurological damage.
The symptoms of absinthism include hallucinations, muscle tremors, and sometimes violent convulsions.
