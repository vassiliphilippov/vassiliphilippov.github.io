patentable

adjective
eligible for patent
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpætəntəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˈpeɪtəntəbl̩ / (BrE)
able to be protected by a legal patent because it is new, useful, and meets the requirements of patent law

Examples

Patentable invention
The new drug formula was considered patentable by the company's legal team.
Not every invention is patentable under the law.
To be patentable, an idea must be both novel and non-obvious.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unpatentable - not eligible to be protected by a patent
