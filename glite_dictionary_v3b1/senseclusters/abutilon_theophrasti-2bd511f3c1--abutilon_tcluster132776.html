Abutilon theophrasti

noun
tall Asian herb yielding strong fiber
Pronunciation: / əˌbjuːtɪˌlɒn θiˈɑːfræstaɪ / (AmE), / əˌbjuːtɪˌlɒn θiˈɒfrəsti / (BrE)
A tall annual herb or subshrub native to tropical Asia, with velvety leaves and yellow flowers, noted for its strong fiber and now naturalized in southeastern Europe and the United States.
