random sampling

noun
selection of a sample by chance
1.0 3.3
Pronunciation: /ˈrænd.əm ˈsæm.plɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈrænd.əm ˈsæm.plɪŋ/ (BrE)
the process of selecting a sample from a population so that every member has an equal chance of being chosen

Examples

Random sampling method
Larger sample sizes with random sampling tend to give more reliable results.
The study relied on random sampling to select participants from the entire population.
We used random sampling to ensure that our survey results were unbiased.
