early mark

noun
permission to leave early
Pronunciation: / ˈɜrli mɑrk / (AmE), / ˈɜːli mɑːk / (BrE)
permission or an opportunity to leave work or an event earlier than usual.

Examples

An early mark from work
After finishing all her tasks, she was given an early mark from the office.
He was happy to have an early mark before the holiday weekend started.
The coach announced that everyone would get an early mark if practice ended on time.
