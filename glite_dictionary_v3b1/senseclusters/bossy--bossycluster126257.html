bossy

adjective
domineering; fond of ordering others
2.6 1.53/m
Pronunciation: / Ààb…ëÀêsi / (AmE), / Ààb…ísi / (BrE)
Fond of giving people orders and tending to tell others what to do, often in a domineering or unpleasant way.

Examples

A bossy person
He was becoming increasingly bossy at work, always telling others what to do.
Her bossy attitude made it difficult for her colleagues to work with her.
The older sibling can sometimes be bossy and insist on getting their own way.

Synonyms

  • domineering
    7.4 0.81/m
    domineering asserts control over others in an arrogant, long-term way, while bossy is more about telling people what to do.
  • overbearing
    5.8 1.37/m
    overbearing pressures others with unwanted control and arrogance, while bossy means telling people what to do in a blunt way.
  • dominating
    5.6 0.01/m
    dominating seeks strong power and control over a situation or people, while bossy focuses on ordering others in daily life.
  • dictatorial
    7.7 1.47/m
    dictatorial enforces obedience as if by rule, often in public or political settings, while bossy is more personal and everyday.
  • autocratic
    8.0 2.34/m
    autocratic holds complete power in organizations or states, while bossy usually means telling people what to do in casual settings.
  • peremptory
    9.3 0.58/m
    peremptory gives quick, final orders that allow no refusal, while bossy often nags or tells others without formal authority.
  • magisterial
    7.9 0.19/m
    magisterial sounds formal and dignified, like an authority figure in a courtroom, while bossy sounds informal and pushy.
  • high-and-mighty
    8.2 0.40/m
    high-and-mighty acts superior and looks down on others, while bossy focuses on giving orders and telling people what to do.

Related Terms

  • antonym: authoritative - able to be trusted as accurate or true; considered to be in control
  • synonym: domineering - asserting one's will over another in an arrogant way
  • antonym: submissive - ready to conform to the authority or will of others
