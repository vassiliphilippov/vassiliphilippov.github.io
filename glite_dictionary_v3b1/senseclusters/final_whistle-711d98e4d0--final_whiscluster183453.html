final whistle

noun
signal ending a sports match
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪ.nəl ˈwɪs.əl / (AmE), / ˈfaɪ.nəl ˈwɪs.əl / (BrE)
the signal, usually a blow from a referee's whistle, indicating the end of a sporting match

Examples

The final whistle blows
Despite their efforts, the score remained tied when the final whistle blew.
Everyone cheered as the final whistle signaled our victory.
The players collapsed to the ground in relief at the sound of the final whistle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: full-time - the end of a normal period of play in a football match
