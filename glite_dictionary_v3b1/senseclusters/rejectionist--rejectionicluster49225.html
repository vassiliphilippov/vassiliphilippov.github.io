rejectionist

adjective
opposed to proposals
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdʒɛkʃənɪst / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒɛkʃənɪst / (BrE)
refusing to accept, approve, or agree with proposals, ideas, or changes, especially in a political context

Examples

Rejectionist attitude
The rejectionist members of the committee blocked every attempt at compromise.
His rejectionist attitude made it difficult to reach any agreement during the negotiations.
Some political groups are known for their rejectionist stance on international treaties.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accepting - willing to receive or approve something
  • synonym: oppositional - actively resisting or opposing something
