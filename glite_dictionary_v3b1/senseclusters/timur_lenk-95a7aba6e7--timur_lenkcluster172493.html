Timur Lenk

proper noun
Mongol conqueror, founder of Timurid Empire
9.8
Pronunciation: / tiːˌmʊr ˈlɛŋk / (AmE), / ˈtɪmjʊər ˈlɛŋk / (BrE)
(1336–1405) Mongol conqueror who founded the Timurid Empire in Central Asia and led military campaigns from Turkey to Mongolia.

Examples

Timur Lenk empire
Timur Lenk built a vast empire stretching from the Middle East to India during the 14th century.
Timur Lenk is often remembered for both his conquests and his patronage of the arts in Samarkand.
Many historians study the military strategies of Timur Lenk and their influence on later rulers.
