Cheyne-Stokes respiration

noun
abnormal alternating breathing pattern
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃeɪn ˌstoʊks ˌrɛspəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪn ˌstəʊks ˌrɛspɪˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by abnormal breathing in which periods of shallow breathing alternate with periods of deep breathing, often seen in serious illnesses.

Examples

Cheyne-Stokes respiration pattern
Cheyne-Stokes respiration is often associated with advanced heart failure or neurological disorders.
Nurses monitored the patient's breathing patterns and identified Cheyne-Stokes respiration as a sign of deteriorating health.
The patient displayed Cheyne-Stokes respiration during his hospital stay, prompting further investigation by doctors.
