irradiate

verb
expose to radiation
Pronunciation: / ɪˈreɪdiˌeɪt /; / ɪˈreɪdieɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈreɪdiˌeɪt /; / ɪˈreɪdieɪt / (BrE)
to expose something to radiation, especially for medical, scientific, or preservation purposes

Examples

To irradiate food
Doctors may irradiate cancerous tumors as part of radiation therapy.
Modern food processing plants often irradiate products to eliminate bacteria and extend shelf life.
The laboratory irradiated the samples to test their resistance to radiation exposure.
Before analyzing the minerals, the technician will irradiate the sample in a controlled environment.
Some laboratories irradiate samples to eliminate any biological contaminants before testing.
You should always wear protective gear when you irradiate the sample in the lab.
