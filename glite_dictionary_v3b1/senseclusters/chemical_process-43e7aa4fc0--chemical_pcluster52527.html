chemical process

noun
process involving chemical changes
3.5 0.40/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…õm…™k…ôl Ààpr…ëÀê.s…õs / (AmE), / Ààk…õm…™k…ôl Ààpr…ô ä.ses / (BrE)
a process involving changes in the atomic or molecular composition and structure of substances

Examples

A chemical process change
Fermentation is a chemical process used in the production of alcohol and bread.
Rusting is a slow chemical process that affects iron when exposed to moisture.
The breakdown of food in the stomach involves a complex chemical process.

Synonyms

  • chemical reaction
    3.1 2.16/m
    a chemical reaction changes substances into new ones in a single event, while a chemical process covers a broader series of changes.
  • chemical change
    3.7 0.17/m
    a chemical change turns substances into different ones at the molecular level, while a chemical process can mean a sequence of such steps.
  • reaction
    3.0 10/m
    a reaction is a process where substances change into others, while a chemical process often describes the whole set of steps and conditions.
  • chemical action
    3.5 0.01/m
    chemical action refers to forces or effects between substances, while a chemical process names the chain of molecular changes that happen.
