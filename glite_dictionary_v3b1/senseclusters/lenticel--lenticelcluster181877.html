lenticel

noun
raised pore on woody plant stems
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛn.tɪˌsɛl / (AmE), / ˈlɛn.tɪ.sɛl / (BrE)
A raised pore on the stem of a woody plant that allows gas exchange between the internal tissues and the atmosphere.

Examples

Tree lenticels
During heavy rainfall, the lenticels may become blocked, affecting the plant's respiration.
Some species of trees have prominent lenticels that can be seen even without magnification.
Under the microscope, the lenticel appeared as a small, corky bump on the twig's surface.
