prelapsarian

adjective
before the Fall of Adam and Eve; innocent state
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːlæpˈsɛriən / (AmE), / ˌpriːlæpˈsɛəriən / (BrE)
relating to the time or state before the Fall of Adam and Eve in the Bible; innocent and unspoiled, as in a state of original innocence

Examples

A prelapsarian state
Many poets have been fascinated by the idea of a prelapsarian paradise lost to humanity.
Some philosophers long for a return to a prelapsarian state, free from guilt and corruption.
The garden was described in almost prelapsarian terms, evoking innocence and harmony with nature.

Related Terms

  • antonym: postlapsarian - relating to the period after the Fall of Adam and Eve
