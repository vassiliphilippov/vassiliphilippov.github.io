PCI

noun
heart artery procedure
5.1
Pronunciation: /piː siː aɪ/ (AmE), /piː siː aɪ/ (BrE)
a medical procedure to open narrowed or blocked coronary arteries using a catheter, balloon, or stent

Examples

PCI procedure
The cardiologist recommended a PCI to treat the patient's blocked artery.
After his heart attack, he underwent an emergency PCI to restore blood flow.
Many hospitals now perform PCI as a standard treatment for certain heart conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: angioplasty - a procedure to restore blood flow through the artery
  • antonym: bypass surgery - surgery to create a new route for blood flow around blocked arteries
