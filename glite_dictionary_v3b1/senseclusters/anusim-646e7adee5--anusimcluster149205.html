Anusim

noun
Jews forced to convert
Pronunciation: / ɑːˈnuːsiːm / (AmE), / ɑːˈnuːsiːm / (BrE)
Jews who were forced to convert to another religion but secretly continued to practice Judaism.

Examples

Anusim secretly practice
During the Spanish Inquisition, many Anusim risked their lives to keep Jewish traditions alive.
The descendants of Anusim are rediscovering their Jewish heritage today.
Historians study the lives of Anusim to understand religious persecution in medieval Europe.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conversos - Jews in Spain and Portugal who converted to Christianity, often under duress
  • synonym: crypto-Jews - Jews who secretly maintained Jewish practices after forced conversion
