tally

noun
the act of counting items or numbers
Pronunciation: / ˈtæli / (AmE), / ˈtæli / (BrE)
a count, record, or mark used to keep track of a total or score (including a point in a game)

Examples

A tally of votes
At the end of the night, the election officials completed the tally of all the votes.
During roll call, each student's name was added to the tally as they answered.
She kept a careful tally of all the donations received during the fundraiser.
He crossed home plate to add another tally to the scoreboard.
The team celebrated after scoring their first tally of the game.
Their only tally came late in the ninth inning.
She kept a tally of all the books she read this year.
The referee updated the tally after each goal was scored.
At the end of the day, the tally showed they had sold fifty tickets.

Related Terms

  • synonym: count - the action of finding the total number of something
  • synonym: record - an official written account of something
  • synonym: run - a score in baseball made by a runner touching all four bases
  • synonym: score - a record of points or items achieved or counted
