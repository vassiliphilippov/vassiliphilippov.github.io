flanker

noun
wide-positioned pass receiver (sports)
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈflæŋkər / (AmE), / ˈflæŋkə / (BrE)
a player in sports (such as football or rugby) who is positioned on the side of the main formation, often to support attack or defense

Examples

Rugby flanker
As a flanker, she caught several passes in yesterday's game.
Each flanker waited for the perfect moment to run down the field.
The coach decided to line up an extra flanker to stretch the defense.
The flanker quickly moved to intercept the opposing team's pass.
As a skilled flanker, she supported both the defense and the attack throughout the match.
Coaches often rely on the flanker to create opportunities on the edge of the field.

Related Terms

  • antonym: center - a player positioned in the middle of the formation
  • synonym: wide receiver - an offensive player who specializes in catching passes
  • synonym: winger - a player positioned near the side of the field, often in attack
