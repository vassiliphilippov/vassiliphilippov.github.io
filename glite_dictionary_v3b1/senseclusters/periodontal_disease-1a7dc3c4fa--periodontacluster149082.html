periodontal disease

noun
disease of gums and tooth-supporting bone
3.7 3.3
Pronunciation: /pɪr.iː.ə.ˈdɑːn.təl dɪ.ˈziːz/ /; / /pɪr.iː.ə.ˈdɑː.nəl dɪ.ˈziːz/ (AmE), /pɪə.rɪ.ə.ˈdɒn.təl dɪ.ˈziːz/ /; / /pɪə.rɪ.ə.ˈdɒn.əl dɪ.ˈziːz/ (BrE)
A disease affecting the gums and bone supporting the teeth, often leading to tooth loss if untreated.

Examples

Periodontal disease symptoms
Many adults develop periodontal disease as they age due to poor oral hygiene.
Regular flossing can help prevent periodontal disease by removing plaque from between the teeth.
The dentist explained that untreated periodontal disease can result in tooth loss over time.
