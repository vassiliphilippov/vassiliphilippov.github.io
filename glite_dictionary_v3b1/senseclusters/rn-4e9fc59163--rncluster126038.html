RN

noun
registered nurse; licensed professional nurse
3.4
Pronunciation: /ɑːr en/ /; / /ˈre.dʒɪ.stərd nɜːrs/ (AmE), /ɑː en/ /; / /ˈre.dʒɪ.stəd nɜːs/ (BrE)
a nurse who has completed the required training and passed examinations to be officially registered and licensed to practice

Examples

Hospital RN
After passing her exams, she became an RN and started working at the hospital.
Many RNs specialize in fields such as pediatrics or emergency care during their careers.
The RN administered medication and checked the patient's vital signs.
