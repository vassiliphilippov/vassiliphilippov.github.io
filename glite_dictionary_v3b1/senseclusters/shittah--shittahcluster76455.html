shittah

noun
biblical acacia tree
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɪtə / (AmE), / ˈʃɪtə / (BrE)
A species of acacia tree, especially as mentioned in the Bible as a source of durable wood.

Examples

The shittah tree
In biblical times, shittah trees were prized for building sacred objects.
Some botanists believe the shittah is actually a species of acacia native to the Sinai Peninsula.
The Ark of the Covenant was constructed from shittah wood, valued for its durability.
