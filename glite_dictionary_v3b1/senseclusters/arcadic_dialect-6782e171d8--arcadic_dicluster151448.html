Arcadic dialect

noun
Ancient Greek dialect of Arcadia
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɑːrˈkeɪdɪk ˈdaɪ.əˌlɛkt / (AmE), / ɑːˈkeɪdɪk ˈdaɪ.əlekt / (BrE)
the dialect of Ancient Greek that was spoken by the Arcadians in the central Peloponnese region.

Examples

Arcadic dialect of Greek
Scholars have studied inscriptions in the Arcadic dialect to better understand Greek linguistic history.
The Arcadic dialect preserves several archaic features not found in other forms of Ancient Greek.
The use of the Arcadic dialect was mostly confined to Arcadia and its immediate surroundings.
