red fire

noun
bright-red burning material for flares or fireworks
Pronunciation: / rɛd ˈfaɪr / (AmE), / rɛd ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
a type of combustible material, usually containing salts of lithium or strontium, that burns with a bright red flame; commonly used in flares and fireworks

Examples

Red fire flare
At the festival, performers lit red fire to create a dramatic effect on stage.
Flares containing red fire are often used for emergency signaling at sea.
The chemist demonstrated how red fire could be used to produce vivid colors in a fireworks display.

  • synonym: flare - a device producing a bright flame for signaling or illumination
