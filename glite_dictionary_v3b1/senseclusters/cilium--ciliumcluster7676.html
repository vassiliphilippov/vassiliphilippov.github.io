cilium

noun
microscopic hairlike cell structure
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪliəm / (AmE), / ˈsɪlɪəm / (BrE)
A microscopic hairlike structure extending from the surface of certain cells, especially in protozoa and tissue cells, that moves fluid or provides locomotion.

Examples

A cilium moves fluid
Each epithelial cell is equipped with at least one cilium that sweeps debris away.
The movement of the cilium is essential for the respiratory tract to clear mucus effectively.
Under the microscope, the cilium can be seen moving rhythmically to propel the cell forward.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flagellum - a long, whip-like appendage used for movement by cells and microorganisms
