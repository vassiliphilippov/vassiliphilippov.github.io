presumption

noun
assumption taken as true without proof
5.8 3.10/m
Pronunciation: / prÉªËˆzÊŒmpÊƒÉ™n / (AmE), / prÉªËˆzÊŒmpÊƒÉ™n / (BrE)
An idea or assumption that is accepted as true without proof, often as the basis for further reasoning or action.

Examples

A presumption of truth
Her theory relies on the presumption that people act rationally.
The judge operated under the presumption that the defendant was innocent until proven guilty.
There is a widespread presumption that taxes will rise next year.

Synonyms

    3.5 54/m
    assumption is a common general idea accepted without proof and used in everyday thought, while presumption is more formal or legal.
  • presupposition
    7.7 1.69/m
    presupposition is an idea taken for granted before a discussion or argument, while presumption is accepted as true without proof.
  • supposition
    7.3 2.32/m
    supposition is a tentative idea used for testing or thought, while presumption is taken as true and used as a basis for action.
  • preconception
    6.8 1.84/m

  • supposal
    9.5 0.01/m
