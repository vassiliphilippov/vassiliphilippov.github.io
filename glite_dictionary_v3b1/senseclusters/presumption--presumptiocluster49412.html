presumption

noun
assumption taken as true without proof
2.7 4.7
Pronunciation: /prɪ.ˈzʌmp.ʃən/ /; / /priː.ˈzʌmp.ʃən/ (AmE), /prɪ.ˈzʌmp.ʃən/ (BrE)
An idea or assumption that is accepted as true without proof, often as the basis for further reasoning or action.

Examples

A presumption of truth
Her theory relies on the presumption that people act rationally.
The judge operated under the presumption that the defendant was innocent until proven guilty.
There is a widespread presumption that taxes will rise next year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assumption - something that is accepted as true or as certain to happen, without proof
  • synonym: supposition - an uncertain belief or guess
