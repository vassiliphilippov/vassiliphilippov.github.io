black body

noun
ideal object absorbing all radiation
Pronunciation: / blæk ˈbɑːdi / (AmE), / blæk ˈbɒdi / (BrE)
A theoretical object that absorbs all incident electromagnetic radiation, regardless of frequency or angle of incidence.

Examples

A black body object
A perfect black body emits radiation at all wavelengths when it is heated.
Physicists often use the black body model to understand thermal radiation.
The concept of a black body is crucial in explaining the spectrum of stars.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: gray body - an object that absorbs a fixed fraction of incident radiation at all wavelengths
  • antonym: white body - an object that reflects all incident electromagnetic radiation
