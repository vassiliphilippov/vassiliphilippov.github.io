Oxalis

noun
a genus of plants called wood sorrels
8.4
Pronunciation: / ɑːkˈsælɪs / (AmE), / ɒkˈsælɪs / (BrE)
a genus of flowering plants, commonly called wood sorrels, with clover-like leaves and colorful clustered flowers, chiefly found in temperate and tropical regions

Examples

Oxalis plant
Many gardeners appreciate Oxalis for its delicate, brightly colored flowers in rock gardens.
Some species of Oxalis can become invasive when introduced outside their native range.
The leaves of Oxalis are often mistaken for those of clover due to their similar appearance.
