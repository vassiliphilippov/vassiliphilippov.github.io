sodium cyanide

noun
poisonous chemical compound (NaCN)
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊdiəm saɪˈænaɪd / (AmE), / ˈsəʊdiəm saɪˈənaɪd / (BrE)
a highly poisonous white crystalline compound (NaCN) used in electroplating, mining, and as a chemical reagent

Examples

Pure sodium cyanide powder
Exposure to even small amounts of sodium cyanide can be rapidly fatal.
Proper storage of sodium cyanide is crucial due to its extreme toxicity.
The mining industry often uses sodium cyanide to extract gold from ore.

Related Terms

  • synonym: potassium cyanide - another highly toxic cyanide compound (KCN)
