restaffing

noun
hiring new staff
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈstæfɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈstɑːfɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of hiring or appointing new employees to replace or add to the current staff of an organization

Examples

Company restaffing process
After the merger, the company underwent restaffing to fill key positions.
The hospital's restaffing efforts improved patient care and reduced wait times.
Budget cuts delayed the planned restaffing of the customer service department.
After the merger, the company underwent a complete restaffing to improve efficiency.
The school district's restaffing brought in teachers with new skills and fresh ideas.
Due to budget cuts, a restaffing was necessary to meet the hospital's changing needs.
