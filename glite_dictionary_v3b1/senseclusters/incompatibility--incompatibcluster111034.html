incompatibility

noun
state of being unable to coexist or work together
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnkəmˌpætəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəmˌpætəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The state of being unable to exist or work together without conflict or difficulty.

Examples

Incompatibility between teams
A high degree of incompatibility between the software programs caused frequent system crashes.
Some materials have an incompatibility that makes it unsafe to store them together.
The couple separated due to fundamental incompatibility in their life goals and values.

Related Terms

  • antonym: compatibility - the quality of being able to exist or work together without conflict
