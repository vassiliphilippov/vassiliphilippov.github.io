herbal medicine

noun
medicine using plants
3.3 0.37/m
Pronunciation: / Ààh…úrb…ôl Ààm…õd…ôs…™n / (AmE), / Ààh…úÀêb…ôl Ààm…õd…ôs…™n / (BrE)
A system of medicine that uses plants or plant extracts to prevent or treat diseases or to promote health.

Examples

Herbal medicine practice
Many people turn to herbal medicine for relief from chronic conditions.
She studied the properties of plants in order to practice herbal medicine.
Some modern doctors integrate herbal medicine with conventional treatments.

Synonyms

  • botanical medicine
    4.7 0.02/m
    botanical medicine is plant-based remedies used in clinics and shops, while herbal medicine is the wider medical system using plants for health.
  • herbal therapy
    4.2 0.01/m
    herbal therapy is the use of plant remedies to treat patients, while herbal medicine names the whole system of plant care and prevention.
  • herbalism
    5.1 0.09/m
    herbalism is the study and practice of using plants for healing by traditional healers, while herbal medicine is the formal system using plants for health.
  • phytotherapy
    9.6 0.02/m
    phytotherapy is the clinical, scientific use of plant extracts in trials and hospitals, while herbal medicine covers traditional and general plant-based care.

Related Terms

  • synonym: phytotherapy - the use of plant-derived medicines in the treatment and prevention of disease
