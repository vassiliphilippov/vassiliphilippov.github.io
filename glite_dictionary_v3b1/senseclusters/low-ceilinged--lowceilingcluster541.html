low-ceilinged

adjective
having a lower than normal ceiling
Pronunciation: / loʊ ˈsiːlɪŋd / (AmE), / ləʊ ˈsiːlɪŋd / (BrE)
(of a room or building) having a ceiling that is lower than usual.

Examples

A low-ceilinged room
The low-ceilinged basement was used for storage and rarely visited.
The cottage was charming but rather low-ceilinged, making it feel a bit cramped.
We ducked our heads as we walked through the low-ceilinged hallway.

Related Terms

  • antonym: high-ceilinged - having a ceiling that is higher than usual
