juxtaposition

noun
placing things side by side for comparison
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒʌkstəpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdʒʌkstəpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of placing things side by side, especially to compare or contrast them

Examples

The juxtaposition of colors
Critics admired the juxtaposition of bright colors in the artist's latest painting.
Her photography often explores the juxtaposition of city life and nature.
The juxtaposition of modern furniture with vintage decor created a striking effect.
Her artwork relies on the juxtaposition of bright colors and dark shadows.
The juxtaposition of old and new buildings creates a unique cityscape.
The documentary uses the juxtaposition of different cultures to highlight their similarities and differences.
