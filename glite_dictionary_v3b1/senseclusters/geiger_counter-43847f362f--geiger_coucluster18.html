Geiger counter

noun
instrument for detecting ionizing radiation
Pronunciation: / ˈɡaɪɡər ˌkaʊntər / (AmE), / ˈɡaɪɡə ˌkaʊntə / (BrE)
an instrument for detecting and measuring ionizing radiation, such as alpha particles, beta particles, and gamma rays

Examples

Geiger counter device
Before entering the old building, he checked the area with his Geiger counter.
The Geiger counter clicked rapidly, indicating the presence of strong radioactive material.
The scientist used a Geiger counter to measure the radiation levels in the laboratory.
