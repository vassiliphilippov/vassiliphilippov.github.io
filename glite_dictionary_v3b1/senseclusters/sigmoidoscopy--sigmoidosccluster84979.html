sigmoidoscopy

noun
visual examination of the lower colon
2.8 7.0
Pronunciation: /sɪg.mɔɪ.də.ˈskoʊ.piː/ (AmE), /sɪg.mɔɪ.də.ˈskɒp.iː/ (BrE)
a medical examination in which a thin, flexible tube with a light and camera (a sigmoidoscope) is inserted into the lower third of the colon to check for polyps or other abnormalities

Examples

A sigmoidoscopy exam
A sigmoidoscopy is less invasive than a full colonoscopy but still provides valuable information.
Some patients find sigmoidoscopy more comfortable because it only examines part of the colon.
The doctor recommended a sigmoidoscopy to check for signs of colon cancer.
