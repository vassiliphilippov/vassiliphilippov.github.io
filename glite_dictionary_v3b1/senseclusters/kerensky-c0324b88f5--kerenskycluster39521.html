Kerensky

proper noun
Russian revolutionary and provisional leader, 1881–1970
9.1
Pronunciation: / kəˈrɛnski / (AmE), / kəˈrɛnski / (BrE)
(Alexander) Kerensky (1881–1970), Russian revolutionary and head of the provisional government after the abdication of Nicholas II, later overthrown by the Bolsheviks.

Examples

Kerensky government
Kerensky led the Russian Provisional Government in 1917 during a critical moment of the revolution.
After being exiled, Kerensky continued to speak out against the Soviet regime.
Many historians discuss how Kerensky's policies failed to prevent the Bolshevik takeover.
