latissimus dorsi

noun
large back muscle aiding arm movement
9.9 5.6
Pronunciation: /lə.ˈtɪs.ɪ.məs ˈdɔːr.siː/ (AmE), /lə.ˈtɪs.ɪ.məs ˈdɔː.siː/ (BrE)
a large, flat muscle on each side of the back that helps move the shoulder and arm

Examples

The latissimus dorsi muscle
He injured his latissimus dorsi while lifting weights at the gym.
Stretching the latissimus dorsi can improve flexibility in the shoulders.
The latissimus dorsi plays a key role in swimming and rowing movements.

Many swimmers have well-developed latissimus dorsi muscles due to repetitive arm movements.
The latissimus dorsi is often targeted in strength training exercises like pull-ups and rows.
