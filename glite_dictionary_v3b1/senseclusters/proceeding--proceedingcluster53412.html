proceeding

noun
legal action or steps in court
Pronunciation: / prəˈsiːdɪŋ / (AmE), / prəˈsiːdɪŋ / (BrE)
(law) An action or series of steps taken in a court of law to settle a legal dispute or invoke a legal judgment.

Examples

A proceeding in court
After a lengthy proceeding, the judge finally delivered the verdict.
All documents related to the legal proceeding must be submitted by next week.
The court scheduled a proceeding to determine the validity of the contract.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hearing - a proceeding where evidence and arguments are presented in court
  • synonym: lawsuit - a case brought before a court of law
  • antonym: settlement - a resolution between disputing parties outside of court
