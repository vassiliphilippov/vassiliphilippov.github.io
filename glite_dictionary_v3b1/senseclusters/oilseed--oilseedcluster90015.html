oilseed

noun
seed that yields oil
Pronunciation: / ˈɔɪlˌsid /; / ˈɔɪlˌsiːd / (AmE), / ˈɔɪlsiːd / (BrE)
a plant grown mainly for the oil that can be extracted from its seeds, or the seed itself used for oil production

Examples

Oilseed plant
After harvesting, the oilseed is processed to extract edible oil for cooking.
Farmers in the region grow oilseed crops such as canola and sunflower for export.
The production of oilseed has increased due to the demand for vegetable oils.
Farmers often rotate oilseed crops with grains to maintain soil health.
Sunflower is a common oilseed grown for its valuable oil.
The demand for oilseed has increased due to the popularity of plant-based oils.

Related Terms

  • synonym: canola - a plant grown for its oil-rich seeds
  • synonym: oilcake - the solid residue after oil has been extracted from oilseed
  • synonym: rapeseed - a type of oilseed crop known for canola oil
  • synonym: sunflower - a tall plant whose seeds are used to produce oil
  • synonym: sunflower seed - the seed of the sunflower, often used for oil extraction
