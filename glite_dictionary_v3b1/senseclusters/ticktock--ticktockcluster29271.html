ticktock

verb
to progress steadily
Pronunciation: / ˈtɪkˌtɑk / (AmE), / ˈtɪkˌtɒk / (BrE)
to move forward steadily in small, regular steps, especially used figuratively to describe the passage of time or the approach of an event.

Examples

Minutes ticktock by
The minutes ticktocked by as she waited anxiously for the results.
With each passing day, the deadline ticktocked closer and the team worked harder.
Time ticktocks on, whether we are ready or not.

Related Terms

  • synonym: advance - to move forward or make progress
  • antonym: halt - to stop moving or progressing
  • synonym: proceed - to continue or move forward
