fogging

noun
conflict-reducing technique
8.1
Pronunciation: /ˈfɑːɡ.ɪŋ/ /; / /ˈfɔːɡ.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈfɒɡ.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
a communication technique of calmly accepting part of a criticism to reduce conflict or tension

Examples

Fogging during arguments
During the heated discussion, she used fogging to acknowledge the valid points without escalating the argument.
By practicing fogging, he managed to stay calm and avoid a confrontation with his colleague.
Many therapists teach fogging as a way to handle criticism constructively in difficult conversations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: active listening - listening attentively and responding to show understanding
  • synonym: assertiveness - the quality of expressing oneself confidently without aggression
