unit cost

noun
cost per single unit of product or service
Pronunciation: / ˈjuːnɪt kɑst / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪt kɒst / (BrE)
the cost incurred to produce, store, and sell one unit of a particular product or service

Examples

The unit cost of production
A lower unit cost can be achieved through economies of scale.
Before setting prices, the company calculated its unit cost for each product line.
Reducing the unit cost allowed the manufacturer to offer more competitive prices.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cost per unit - the expense assigned to each individual item or service unit
