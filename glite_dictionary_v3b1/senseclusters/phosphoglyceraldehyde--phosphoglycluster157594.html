phosphoglyceraldehyde

noun
glycolysis intermediate
Pronunciation: / ˌfɑsfoʊˌɡlɪsərˈældəˌhaɪd / (AmE), / ˌfɒsfəʊˌɡlɪsərˈældɪhaɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound formed as an intermediate during the breakdown of sugars in cells, especially in glycolysis

Examples

Phosphoglyceraldehyde intermediate
During glycolysis, phosphoglyceraldehyde is produced as a key intermediate in the breakdown of glucose.
Researchers measured the concentration of phosphoglyceraldehyde in muscle tissue after exercise.
The conversion of phosphoglyceraldehyde to other compounds releases energy for cellular processes.
