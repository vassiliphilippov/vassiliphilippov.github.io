reductive approach

noun
overly simplified analysis or method
7.4
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdʌktɪv əˈproʊtʃ / (AmE), / rɪˈdʌktɪv əˈprəʊtʃ / (BrE)
analyzing or presenting something in an overly simplified way that ignores important complexities

Examples

A reductive approach to ideas
Applying a reductive approach to literature fails to capture its deeper meanings.
Critics argued that the report used a reductive approach in assessing social problems.
His reductive approach overlooks the nuanced realities of the situation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: simplistic - treating complex issues as if they were much simpler than they are
