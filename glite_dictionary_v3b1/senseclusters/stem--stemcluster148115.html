stem

noun
main part of a word after affixes are removed
7.0 1.19/m
Pronunciation: / stÉ›m / (AmE), / stÉ›m / (BrE)
(Linguistics) The main part of a word to which affixes can be added, remaining after all inflectional and derivational affixes are removed.

Examples

Word stem
In the word 'unhappiness', the stem is 'happy'.
Language learners often study the stem to understand word formation.
The addition of prefixes or suffixes does not usually change the stem itself.
