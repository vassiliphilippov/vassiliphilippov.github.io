obdurately

adverb
in a stubborn and unyielding way
9.1 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…ëÀêb.dj…ô.r…ôt.li / (AmE), / Àà…íb.dj ä.r…ôt.li / (BrE)
in a stubborn and unyielding manner; refusing to change one's mind or actions

Examples

To obdurately refuse
He obdurately refused to admit any wrongdoing despite the overwhelming evidence.
She obdurately held to her opinion, even when everyone else disagreed.
The committee obdurately rejected every proposal for compromise.

Synonyms

    7.2 0.34/m
    obstinately means refusing to change in ordinary situations, while obdurately suggests a harder, longer-lasting refusal.
  • stubbornly
    3.7 3.19/m
    stubbornly shows a plain refusal to change, while obdurately feels more severe and unyielding in tone.
  • intransigently
    8.2 0.01/m
    intransigently describes refusing to agree or compromise, while obdurately highlights stubborn refusal to change actions or mind.
  • uncompromisingly
    7.5 0.20/m
    uncompromisingly means refusing to make concessions or soften demands, while obdurately focuses on stubbornly not changing one's mind.
  • mulishly
    9.3 0.02/m
    mulishly is stubborn in a rough, animal-like way, while obdurately is a more formal word for firm refusal.
  • adamantly
    6.4 1.78/m
    adamantly states something with firm certainty, while obdurately implies stubborn refusal to change despite reason or pressure.
