predestiny

noun
divine preordination
Pronunciation: / priˈdɛstəni /; / priːˈdɛstəni / (AmE), / priˈdɛstɪni /; / priːˈdɛstəni / (BrE)
the belief or state that all events are determined in advance, especially by a higher power or fate

Examples

The predestiny belief
Many religious debates center on the concept of predestiny and its implications for human choice.
He struggled to reconcile his belief in predestiny with the idea of personal responsibility.
The novel explores how predestiny shapes the lives of its main characters.
Many philosophers have debated whether predestiny controls the course of our lives.
She wondered if her success was due to hard work or simply predestiny.
Stories about heroes often explore the idea of predestiny shaping their journeys.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fate - the development of events beyond a person's control, regarded as determined by a supernatural power
  • antonym: free will - the ability to choose actions independently
  • synonym: predestination - the doctrine that all events are willed by God
