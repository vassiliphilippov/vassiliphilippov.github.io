A small, neutral country situated between two larger, often rival powers, serving to reduce the risk of conflict between them.

Examples

A buffer state between countries
Afghanistan served as a buffer state between the British and Russian empires in the 19th century.
Switzerland has often been considered a buffer state in European geopolitics.
The existence of a buffer state can sometimes help maintain peace between great powers.
