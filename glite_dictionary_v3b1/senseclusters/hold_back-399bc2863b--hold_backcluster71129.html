Synonyms

  • hold in
    4.5 3.50/m
    hold in means quietly keeping emotions inside, while hold back means stopping yourself from showing or revealing those feelings.
  • suppress
    5.8 8.73/m
    suppress means consciously stop an emotion or thought, while hold back is more general and can be less formal in speech.
  • bottle up
    5.6 0.65/m
    bottle up means to keep feelings inside over time until they build up, while hold back can be a single, immediate action.
  • stifle
    5.3 4.75/m
    stifle means to stop an emotion or sound tightly, often smothering its expression, while hold back is a more neutral restraint.
  • smother
    6.0 2.12/m
    smother means to cover or suffocate feelings so they cannot show, while hold back can be milder and less forceful.
  • repress
    6.6 4.37/m
    repress means to push feelings or memories out of conscious thought, while hold back is a conscious, surface-level restraint.
  • choke back
    7.0 0.36/m
    choke back means to forcefully hold an emotion with visible struggle, while hold back can be calmer or less obvious.
  • choke down
    7.7 0.09/m
    choke down means to suppress an emotion or reaction with effort, while hold back may not show that same effort.
  • swallow
    7.0 0.30/m
    swallow means to stop yourself saying something or showing feeling quietly, while hold back covers many ways of restraining expression.
  • inhibit
    6.3 1.15/m
    inhibit means to make someone unable to act naturally, often by shyness or rules, while hold back is direct self-restraint.
  • restrain
    4.6 6.50/m
    restrain means to prevent action or control someone or yourself physically or emotionally, while hold back is mainly about not showing feelings.
  • keep back
    5.1 0.17/m
    keep back means to stop yourself or others from showing an emotion, often to hide it, while hold back is more common speech.
