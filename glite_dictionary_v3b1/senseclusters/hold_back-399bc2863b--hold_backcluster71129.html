hold back

verb
to conceal or restrain something
1.0
Pronunciation: / hoʊld ˈbæk /; / hoʊld bæk / (AmE), / həʊld ˈbæk /; / həʊld bæk / (BrE)
to restrain or suppress emotions, reactions, or information; to stop yourself from expressing or revealing something

Examples

To hold back tears
He decided not to hold back any information from his colleagues.
It's hard to hold back your true feelings when you're upset.
She tried to hold back her tears during the sad movie.
Despite the pain, he managed to hold back his tears.
She tried to hold back her laughter during the serious meeting.
Sometimes it's better not to hold back your true feelings.
He couldn't hold back his laughter at the comedian's joke.
She tried to hold back her tears during the farewell ceremony.
You should not hold back your true feelings from your friends.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conceal - prevent something from being seen or known; keep secret
  • synonym: forbear - to refrain from an action or expression
  • antonym: let go - to stop holding something back; to release control
  • synonym: restrain - to hold something or someone back physically or emotionally
  • antonym: reveal - make known to others; allow to be seen
  • synonym: suppress - to put an end to something, especially an emotion or reaction
