Kirkia wilmsii

noun
African tree with creamy lilac-like flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈkɜːrkiə ˈvɪlmsiː / (AmE), / ˈkɜːkɪə ˈwɪlmsiː / (BrE)
A small African deciduous tree with a spreading crown, leaves clustered toward the ends of branches, and clusters of creamy lilac-like flowers.

Examples

Kirkia wilmsii tree
Botanists studied the leaf patterns of Kirkia wilmsii to better understand its adaptation to dry climates.
Clusters of creamy flowers make the Kirkia wilmsii particularly attractive in the spring.
The Kirkia wilmsii can often be found in woodlands of southern Africa.
