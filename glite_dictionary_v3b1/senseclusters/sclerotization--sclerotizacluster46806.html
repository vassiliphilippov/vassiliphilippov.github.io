sclerotization

noun
insect cuticle hardening
9.9
Pronunciation: / skləˌroʊtəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / skləˌrɒtəˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which an insect's cuticle hardens and darkens after molting

Examples

Sclerotization of the cuticle
The sclerotization of the beetle's exoskeleton protects it from predators and environmental damage.
After molting, sclerotization strengthens the insect's new outer shell.
Scientists study sclerotization to understand how insects develop their tough, protective layers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tanning - the process of making animal skin into leather by hardening it
