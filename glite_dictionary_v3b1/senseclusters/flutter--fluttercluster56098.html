flutter

noun
disorderly outburst or commotion
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈflʌt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈflʌtə / (BrE)
A sudden, disorderly outburst or commotion; a brief state of agitation or confusion.

Examples

A flutter in crowd
A sudden flutter in the crowd indicated that something unexpected had occurred.
The announcement caused a brief flutter of excitement in the hall.
There was quite a flutter in the office when the news broke.

Related Terms

  • synonym: commotion - a state of confused and noisy disturbance
  • synonym: tumult - a loud, confused noise, especially one caused by a large mass of people
