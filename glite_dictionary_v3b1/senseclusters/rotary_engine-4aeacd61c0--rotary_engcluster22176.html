rotary engine

noun
internal-combustion engine with cylinders arranged radially
6.7 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈroʊ.tə.riː ˈen.dʒən/ /; / /ˈroʊ.tə.riː ˈɪn.dʒən/ (AmE), /ˈrəʊ.tə.riː ˈen.dʒɪn/ /; / /ˈrəʊ.tə.riː ˈɪn.dʒɪn/ (BrE)
an internal-combustion engine in which parts rotate around a central point, often used in vehicles and aircraft (such as the Wankel engine)
