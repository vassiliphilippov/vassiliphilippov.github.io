protrusion

noun
the act of sticking out
6.8 0.57/m
Pronunciation: / pro äÀàtruÀê í…ôn / (AmE), / pr…ôÀàtruÀê í…ôn / (BrE)
something that sticks out from a surface; the state of sticking out

Examples

A protrusion on skin
The broken bone caused a visible protrusion beneath the skin.
The lizard's tongue shot out in a quick protrusion to catch the insect.
There was a small protrusion on the wall where a nail once was.
A small protrusion at the base of the statue gave it a unique look.
The protrusion on the wall made it difficult to move the furniture.
The doctor noticed a protrusion along the patient's spine during the examination.

Synonyms

  • protuberance
    8.6 0.23/m
    protuberance is a rounded bulge or bump on a surface, while protrusion is a general term for anything that sticks out.
  • projection
    4.6 0.93/m
    projection is a part that extends outward from a surface, while protrusion focuses simply on sticking out without implying length.
  • extrusion
    8.1 0.14/m
    extrusion is often a body pushed out or formed by force, while protrusion is any part that sticks out.
  • bulge
    3.2 3.80/m
    bulge is a rounded swelling that pushes outward from a surface, while protrusion covers any form that sticks out.
  • prominence
    5.5 0.75/m
    prominence is a part that is especially high or noticeable in its surroundings, while protrusion just means sticking out.
  • jut
    6.5 0.25/m
    jut is a sharp point or short extension from an edge, while protrusion is any part that projects from a surface.
