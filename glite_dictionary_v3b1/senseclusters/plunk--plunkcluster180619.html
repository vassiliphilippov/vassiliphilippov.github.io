plunk

noun
baseball hit that drops suddenly
Pronunciation: / plʌŋk / (AmE), / plʌŋk / (BrE)
(Baseball) A hit in which the ball drops suddenly after being struck, often softly.

Examples

A soft plunk hit
Sometimes a well-placed plunk can be just as effective as a powerful drive.
The batter managed a plunk that landed just beyond the pitcher's mound.
With a gentle plunk, the ball fell between the infielders and allowed a runner to advance.
