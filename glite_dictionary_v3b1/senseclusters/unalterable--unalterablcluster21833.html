unalterable

adjective
impossible to change
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈɔltərəbəl /; / ʌnˈɔltɚəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈɔːltərəbəl / (BrE)
impossible to change or modify; fixed and unchangeable

Examples

An unalterable rule
Her decision was firm and unalterable, no matter what arguments were made.
Some traditions have become so deeply rooted that they seem unalterable.
The judge handed down an unalterable sentence that could not be appealed.
His views on tradition were unalterable, no matter how much anyone argued.
She made an unalterable decision to pursue her dream of becoming a doctor.
The constitution contains certain unalterable provisions to protect individual rights.
His unalterable decision surprised his friends and family.
She faced her fate with unalterable calm and dignity.
The document's terms are unalterable without the agreement of both parties.

  • synonym: invariable - never changing; always the same
