sodium carbonate

noun
white, water-soluble sodium salt (Na2CO3)
Pronunciation: / ˌsoʊdiəm ˈkɑːrboʊneɪt / (AmE), / ˌsəʊdiəm ˈkɑːbənət / (BrE)
a white, water-soluble sodium salt (Na2CO3) used in making soap, glass, and paper, and as a cleaning agent

Examples

Sodium carbonate cleaning agent
Many household cleaning products contain sodium carbonate as an active ingredient.
The factory uses sodium carbonate in the production of glass bottles.
To adjust the pH level in aquariums, fish keepers often add sodium carbonate.
