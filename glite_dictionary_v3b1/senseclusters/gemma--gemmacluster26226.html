gemma

noun
asexual reproductive body in some plants
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛmə / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛmə / (BrE)
a small asexual reproductive structure found in certain non-vascular plants, such as liverworts and mosses, which detaches from the parent and develops into a new individual

Examples

A gemma plant
A single gemma can grow into a genetically identical new plant.
In liverworts, the gemma is produced in cup-like structures on the surface of the thallus.
Scientists studied how the release of each gemma leads to rapid colonization by the moss.
