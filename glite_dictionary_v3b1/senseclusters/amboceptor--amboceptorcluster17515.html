amboceptor

noun
antibody linking antigen and complement
Pronunciation: / ˈæm.boʊˌsɛp.tər / (AmE), / ˈam.bəʊˌsɛp.tə / (BrE)
an antibody that, during an immune response, attaches both to an antigen and to complement, thereby facilitating the destruction of the antigenic cell

Examples

An amboceptor antibody
After immunization, levels of amboceptor can rise significantly in the bloodstream.
In early immunological research, scientists measured the effect of the amboceptor in complement-fixation tests.
The amboceptor binds to both the target cell and complement, resulting in cell lysis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antibody - a protein produced by the immune system that binds to foreign substances
  • antonym: antigen - a substance that triggers an immune response
