monofocal lens implant

noun
artificial lens for distance vision after cataract surgery
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːnoʊˈfoʊkəl lɛnz ɪmˈplænt / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈfəʊkəl lɛnz ɪmˈplɑːnt / (BrE)
an artificial lens with a single focal point, used after cataract surgery to provide clear distance vision

Examples

A monofocal lens implant after surgery
A monofocal lens implant usually requires patients to wear reading glasses for near tasks after surgery.
After her cataract operation, she received a monofocal lens implant to improve her distance vision.
The ophthalmologist explained the benefits of a monofocal lens implant compared to multifocal alternatives.

Related Terms

  • antonym: multifocal lens implant - lens implant allowing focus at multiple distances
