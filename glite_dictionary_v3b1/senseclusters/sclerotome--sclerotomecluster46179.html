sclerotome

noun
nerve-related bone area
Pronunciation: / ˈsklɛrəˌtoʊm / (AmE), / ˈsklɛrəˌtəʊm / (BrE)
an area of bone or deep connective tissue supplied by sensory fibers from a single spinal nerve root

Examples

Sclerotome pain area
The doctor traced the patient's pain along the sclerotome to identify the affected nerve root.
Understanding the sclerotome can help diagnose the source of deep bone pain.
The injury caused discomfort in the entire sclerotome associated with the lumbar region.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dermatome - an area of skin supplied by sensory fibers from a single spinal nerve root
  • synonym: myotome - a group of muscles supplied by motor fibers from a single spinal nerve root
