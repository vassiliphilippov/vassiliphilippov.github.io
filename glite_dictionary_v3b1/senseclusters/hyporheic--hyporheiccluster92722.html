hyporheic

noun
streambed mixing zone
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpoʊˈriːɪk / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəˈriːɪk / (BrE)
the zone beneath and alongside a stream bed where groundwater and surface water mix

Examples

The hyporheic area
Scientists studied the hyporheic to understand how nutrients move beneath the river.
The hyporheic plays a crucial role in filtering water as it flows under the stream bed.
Researchers collected samples from the hyporheic to analyze the interaction between groundwater and surface water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aquifer - an underground layer of water-bearing rock
  • antonym: surface water - water that collects on the surface of the ground
