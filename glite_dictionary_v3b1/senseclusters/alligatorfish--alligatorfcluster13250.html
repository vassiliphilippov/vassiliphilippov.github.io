alligatorfish

noun
small elongated marine fish (Agonidae)
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈælɪˌɡeɪtərˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈælɪˌɡeɪtəˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a small, very elongated marine fish of the family Agonidae, found in northern Pacific waters, known for its bony armor and slender body

Examples

The alligatorfish species
Marine biologists discovered several species of alligatorfish during their expedition in the North Pacific.
The alligatorfish relies on its camouflage to hide from predators along the ocean floor.
Unlike most fish, the alligatorfish has a tough, bony armor covering its body.
