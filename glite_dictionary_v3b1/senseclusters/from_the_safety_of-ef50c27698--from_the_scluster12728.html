from the safety of

preposition
in a secure position, away from danger
Pronunciation: / frəm ðə ˈseɪfti ʌv / (AmE), / frəm ði ˈseɪfti ɒv / (BrE)
used to indicate that someone is in a secure position or place while observing or experiencing something potentially dangerous.

Examples

From the safety of exile
He spoke out against the regime from the safety of exile.
Reporters filmed the protest from the safety of the police lines.
She observed the storm from the safety of her house.
