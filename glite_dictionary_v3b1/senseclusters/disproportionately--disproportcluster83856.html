disproportionately

adverb
to an unequal or inappropriate degree
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsprəˈpɔrʃənətli /; / ˌdɪs.prəˈpɔr.ʃən.ət.li / (AmE), / ˌdɪsprəˈpɔːʃənətli /; / ˌdɪs.prəˈpɔː.ʃən.ət.li / (BrE)
to a degree that is out of proportion to something else; too much or too little compared to what is normal or expected

Examples

To disproportionately allocate resources
Resources were disproportionately allocated to certain departments, causing frustration among staff.
The new law will disproportionately affect low-income families.
Women are disproportionately represented in leadership positions across the industry.
The funding was disproportionately allocated to urban rather than rural schools.
The species has been disproportionately impacted by climate change compared to others in the region.
The tax increase would disproportionately affect people on lower incomes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: proportionately - in a way that is in correct relation to something else
