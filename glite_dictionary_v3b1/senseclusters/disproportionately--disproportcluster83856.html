disproportionately

adverb
to an unequal or inappropriate degree
7.1 5.60/m
Pronunciation: / Àåd…™spr…ôÀàp…îr É…ôn…ôtli /; / Àåd…™s.pr…ôÀàp…îr. É…ôn.…ôt.li / (AmE), / Àåd…™spr…ôÀàp…îÀê É…ôn…ôtli /; / Àåd…™s.pr…ôÀàp…îÀê. É…ôn.…ôt.li / (BrE)
to a degree that is out of proportion to something else; too much or too little compared to what is normal or expected

Examples

To disproportionately allocate resources
Resources were disproportionately allocated to certain departments, causing frustration among staff.
The new law will disproportionately affect low-income families.
Women are disproportionately represented in leadership positions across the industry.
The funding was disproportionately allocated to urban rather than rural schools.
The species has been disproportionately impacted by climate change compared to others in the region.
The tax increase would disproportionately affect people on lower incomes.

Synonyms

  • unduly
    6.9 2.35/m
    unduly means more than is fair or justified in a situation, while disproportionately compares size or amount to something else.
  • excessively
    3.0 3.81/m
    excessively shows a large, too-great degree without comparison, while disproportionately highlights a mismatch between things.
  • overly
    3.5 14/m
    overly means more than is needed in everyday, casual talk, while disproportionately stresses difference compared with another factor.
  • inordinately
    7.5 0.75/m
    inordinately describes an unusually large or extreme degree, often in formal contexts, while disproportionately compares two things' sizes.
  • exorbitantly
    7.3 0.16/m
    exorbitantly usually refers to very high cost or price, while disproportionately refers to amounts out of proportion to something else.
