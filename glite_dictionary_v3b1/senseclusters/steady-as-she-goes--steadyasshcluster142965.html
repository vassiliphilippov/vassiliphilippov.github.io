steady-as-she-goes

adjective
cautious, consistent approach
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˌstɛdi əz ʃi ˈɡoʊz / (AmE), / ˌstɛdi əz ʃi ˈɡəʊz / (BrE)
used to describe a situation in which things are being managed cautiously and consistently, without sudden changes or risks

Examples

A steady-as-she-goes approach
After the turbulent quarter, the CEO recommended a steady-as-she-goes approach for the next few months.
He kept his investments steady-as-she-goes, avoiding both risks and hasty profits.
They adopted a steady-as-she-goes policy to maintain stability during uncertain times.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cautious - careful to avoid potential problems or dangers
  • synonym: conservative - averse to sudden change or innovation
  • antonym: reckless - not showing proper concern for danger or consequences
