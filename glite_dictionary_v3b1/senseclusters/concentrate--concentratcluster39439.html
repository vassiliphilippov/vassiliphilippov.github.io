concentrate

verb
to cook until liquid is reduced
1.4 3.6
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈsen.treɪt/ /; / /kɑːn.ˈsen.treɪt/ (AmE), /kən.ˈsen.treɪt/ /; / /kɒn.ˈsen.treɪt/ (BrE)
to make a liquid or substance denser, stronger, or purer by removing water or other elements (especially in cooking or chemistry)

Examples

To concentrate a sauce
As the stock concentrates, its flavor becomes much more intense.
Simmer the mixture gently until the sauce concentrates and thickens.
The chef allowed the soup to concentrate before serving it.
If you concentrate the syrup, its flavor will become much stronger.
The factory uses evaporation to concentrate orange juice for easier shipping.
They concentrated the solution by removing half of the water.
The chef concentrated the sauce by simmering it for several hours.
The company decided to concentrate its efforts on the most profitable markets.
To save space, she concentrated the notes into a single, concise document.

Related Terms

  • synonym: condense - to make something denser or more concentrated
  • antonym: dilute - to make a liquid thinner or weaker by adding water or another solvent
  • synonym: reduce - to cook a liquid so that it becomes thicker and more concentrated
  • synonym: strengthen - to make something stronger or more concentrated
