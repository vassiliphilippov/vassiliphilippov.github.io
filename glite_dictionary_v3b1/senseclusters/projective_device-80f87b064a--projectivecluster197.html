projective device

noun
psychology: personality test using ambiguous stimuli
Pronunciation: / prəˈdʒɛktɪv dɪˈvaɪs / (AmE), / prəˈdʒɛktɪv dɪˈvaɪs / (BrE)
a type of personality test in which individuals respond to ambiguous stimuli or situations, revealing hidden aspects of their personality

Examples

A projective device test
Clinicians sometimes use a projective device to explore subconscious aspects of a patient's personality.
The Rorschach inkblot test is a classic example of a projective device used in psychology.
Unlike structured questionnaires, a projective device relies on open-ended responses to ambiguous materials.
