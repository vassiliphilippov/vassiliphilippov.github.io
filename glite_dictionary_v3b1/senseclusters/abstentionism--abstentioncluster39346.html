abstentionism

noun
refusal to vote
9.9
Pronunciation: /əb.ˈsten.ʃə.nɪ.zəm/ /; / /æb.ˈsten.ʃə.nɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /əb.ˈsten.ʃə.nɪ.zəm/ /; / /æb.ˈsten.ʃə.nɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the practice of deliberately refusing to participate in an official vote or assembly, especially for political reasons

Examples

Abstentionism in voting
The party's policy of abstentionism meant they did not take their seats in parliament.
Some activists used abstentionism to protest against what they saw as an unfair election.
Debates about abstentionism often arise during times of political crisis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boycott - the act of refusing to take part in something as a protest
  • antonym: participation - the act of taking part in something
