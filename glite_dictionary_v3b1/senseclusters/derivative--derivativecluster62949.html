derivative

noun
compound derived from another
Pronunciation: / dəˈrɪv.ə.tɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈrɪv.ə.tɪv / (BrE)
a compound that is obtained from another compound and has a related structure, often differing by the replacement of one atom or group by another

Examples

A derivative compound
Many dyes are derivatives of coal tar compounds.
Scientists studied the new derivative to understand its chemical properties.
This medication is a derivative of a naturally occurring hormone.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compound - a substance composed of two or more elements chemically combined
