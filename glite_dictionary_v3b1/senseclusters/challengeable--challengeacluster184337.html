challengeable

adjective
capable of being challenged
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃælɪndʒəbəl / (AmE), / ˈtʃælɪndʒəbl̩ / (BrE)
Able to be questioned, disputed, or formally opposed, especially in a court of law or official setting.

Examples

Challengeable decision
All administrative decisions are challengeable within a specified time frame.
The judge ruled that the verdict was challengeable on procedural grounds.
Their authority is not absolute and remains challengeable in certain cases.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unchallengeable - impossible to challenge or dispute
