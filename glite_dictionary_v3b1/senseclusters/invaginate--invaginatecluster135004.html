invaginate

verb
to fold inwards; form a sheath
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈvædʒɪˌneɪt /; / ɪnˈvædʒɪneɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈvadʒɪneɪt / (BrE)
to fold or turn inward to form a pouch, sheath, or cavity (especially in tissues or organs)

Examples

To invaginate tissue
During embryonic development, certain tissues invaginate to create complex body structures.
The membrane may invaginate under pressure, forming a small pouch.
When the caterpillar transforms, its outer layer invaginates to form the chrysalis.
During development, the embryonic tissue will invaginate to create new structures.
In some animals, the gut begins to form when certain cells invaginate.
The membrane can invaginate in response to specific cellular signals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: evaginate - to turn outward or inside out (opposite action in biology)
  • antonym: evert - turn outward or inside out
