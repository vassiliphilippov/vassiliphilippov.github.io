windward

noun
direction from which the wind blows

the direction or side from which the wind is blowing

Examples

Windward side of the island
Clouds often gather on the windward slopes of the island.
The sailors adjusted their course toward the windward to make better speed.
You should always position the tent on the windward side of the hill for stability.
Plants grow shorter on the windward slopes of the mountain due to harsh winds.
The windward side of the island receives most of the rainfall.
The tents were set up on the windward side to catch the cool breeze.

Synonyms

  • windward side
    5.8 0.16/m
    windward side faces into the wind and names a surface, while windward names the general direction the wind is coming from.
  • windward position
    6.7 0.00/m
    windward position describes the specific place facing the wind on a vessel or shore, while windward names the wind's direction.
  • to the windward
    8.1 0.01/m
    to the windward means moving toward where the wind blows on a ship, while windward is the wind's direction.
  • upwind
    3.5 0.12/m
    upwind means moving toward the direction the wind comes from, while windward names that direction or side.
  • weather side
    7.9 0.00/m
    weather side is the side exposed to wind and storms, while windward refers specifically to the side the wind is blowing from.
