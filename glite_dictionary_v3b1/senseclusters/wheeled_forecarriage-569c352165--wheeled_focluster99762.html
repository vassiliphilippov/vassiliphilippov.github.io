wheeled forecarriage

noun
small wheeled front attachment for a plow
Pronunciation: / wiːld ˈfɔrˌkærɪdʒ / (AmE), / wiːld ˈfɔːkærɪdʒ / (BrE)
a small wheeled carriage attached under the front end of a plow beam, supporting and guiding it.

Examples

A wheeled forecarriage under plow
Mud and debris often get stuck in the wheeled forecarriage during early spring planting.
The farmer repaired the wheeled forecarriage to ensure the plow moved smoothly over the soil.
This model of plow includes a sturdy wheeled forecarriage for better maneuverability on uneven ground.
