Carassius vulgaris

noun
a species of European carp
Pronunciation: / kəˈræsiəs vʌlˈɡɛərɪs / (AmE), / kəˈrasɪəs vʌlˈɡɑːrɪs / (BrE)
A species of European carp closely resembling the wild goldfish, often found in freshwater ponds and lakes.

Examples

Carassius vulgaris fish
Researchers studied the differences in fin shape between Carassius vulgaris and domesticated goldfish.
