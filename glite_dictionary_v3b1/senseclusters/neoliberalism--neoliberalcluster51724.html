neoliberalism

noun
political ideology focused on free markets and limited government
7.9 8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌniː.oʊˈlɪb.ər.ɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌniː.əˈlɪb.ər.ɪz.əm / (BrE)
a political and economic philosophy favoring free-market capitalism, deregulation, and reduction in government spending, developed in the late 20th century

Examples

Neoliberalism economic policy
Many critics argue that neoliberalism leads to greater inequality and less government protection for the poor.
Protesters gathered to oppose the government's new neoliberalism-inspired reforms.
The rise of neoliberalism in the 1980s shaped many countries' economic policies.
