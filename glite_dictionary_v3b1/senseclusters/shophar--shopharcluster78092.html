shophar

noun
ancient ram's-horn musical instrument
Pronunciation: / ˈʃoʊfɑr / (AmE), / ˈʃɒfə / (BrE)
an ancient musical instrument made from a ram's horn, traditionally used by the Israelites for signaling, and still used during Jewish religious ceremonies.

Examples

Blow the shophar
During Rosh Hashanah, the rabbi blew the shophar to mark the new year.
He studied the history and significance of the shophar for his thesis on Jewish traditions.
The sound of the shophar echoed through the ancient city walls as a call to assembly.

Related Terms
