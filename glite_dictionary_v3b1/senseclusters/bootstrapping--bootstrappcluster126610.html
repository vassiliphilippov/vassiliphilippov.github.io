bootstrapping

noun
self-funded startup process
5.6
Pronunciation: /ˈbuːt.stræp.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈbuːt.stræp.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
the process of starting a business or project with minimal resources and little or no outside help

Examples

Startup bootstrapping
The founders relied on bootstrapping to launch their company before seeking outside investment.
Many small businesses use bootstrapping to grow slowly without taking on debt.
Through careful bootstrapping, she managed to build her tech startup from the ground up.

Related Terms

  • synonym: self-funding - providing money for a business or project from one's own resources
  • antonym: venture capital - funding provided by investors to startups and small businesses
