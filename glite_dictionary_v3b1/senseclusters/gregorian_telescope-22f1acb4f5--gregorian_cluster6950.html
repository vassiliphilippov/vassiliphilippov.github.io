Gregorian telescope

noun
type of reflecting telescope
Pronunciation: / ɡrəˈɡɔriən ˈtɛləskoʊp / (AmE), / ɡrəˈɡɔːriən ˈtɛlɪskəʊp / (BrE)
a type of reflecting telescope with a paraboloidal primary mirror and a hyperboloidal secondary mirror, focusing light through an aperture in the primary mirror

Examples

The Gregorian telescope design
James Gregory invented the Gregorian telescope in the 17th century, revolutionizing astronomical observation.
The observatory uses a Gregorian telescope to study distant galaxies.
Unlike the Cassegrain design, the Gregorian telescope employs a concave secondary mirror.
