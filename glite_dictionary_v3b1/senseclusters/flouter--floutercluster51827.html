flouter

noun
one who mocks or disregards rules
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈflaʊtər / (AmE), / ˈflaʊtə / (BrE)
a person who mocks or shows contempt for rules, laws, conventions, or authority

Examples

A flouter of rules
As a known flouter of tradition, he refused to attend the formal ceremony.
Many believed the group’s leader was a habitual flouter of authority and law.
The most notorious flouter of the new regulations was finally penalized for his repeated offenses.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conformer - one who complies with rules or standards
  • synonym: mockery - ridicule, derision
