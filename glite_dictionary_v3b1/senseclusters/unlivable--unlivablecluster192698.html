unlivable

adjective
not fit to live in
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈlɪvəbl /; / ʌnˈlɪvəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈlɪvəbl /; / ʌnˈlɪvəbəl / (BrE)
not suitable or safe to live in; unfit for habitation.

Examples

An unlivable apartment
Extreme heat made the apartment unlivable during the summer months.
The abandoned building is completely unlivable due to mold and structural damage.
They were forced to leave their home after flooding rendered it unlivable.
After the flood, the house became unliveable due to severe water damage.
The apartment was so cold and damp that it was considered unliveable by health inspectors.
Toxic fumes from the factory made the village unliveable for its residents.

  • antonym: inhabitable - suitable to live in
  • synonym: inhospitable - (of a place) harsh and difficult to live in
  • synonym: uninhabitable - not fit to live in
