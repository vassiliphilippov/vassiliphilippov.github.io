hyperbole

noun
exaggerated statement for effect
4.2
Pronunciation: / haɪˈpɝːbəli / (AmE), / haɪˈpɜːbəli / (BrE)
exaggerated statements or claims not meant to be taken literally; deliberate and extravagant exaggeration for emphasis or effect.

Examples

A hyperbole statement
Her claim that her bag weighed a ton was clearly hyperbole, not a literal statement.
In literature, authors often use hyperbole to create strong impressions or evoke humor.
The phrase 'I'm so hungry I could eat a horse' is an example of hyperbole.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exaggeration - statement that makes something seem greater, better, or worse than it really is
  • antonym: understatement - presentation of something as being smaller or less important than it actually is
