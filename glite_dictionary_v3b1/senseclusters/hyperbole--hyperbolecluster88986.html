exaggerated statements or claims not meant to be taken literally; deliberate and extravagant exaggeration for emphasis or effect.

Examples

A hyperbole statement
Her claim that her bag weighed a ton was clearly hyperbole, not a literal statement.
In literature, authors often use hyperbole to create strong impressions or evoke humor.
The phrase 'I'm so hungry I could eat a horse' is an example of hyperbole.

Synonyms

  • hyperbolism
    8.9 0.00/m
    hyperbolism is a rare or formal term for using extreme exaggeration in speech, while hyperbole is the common literary device for emphasis.
  • exaggeration
    3.0 3.99/m
    exaggeration makes something seem larger or more important in plain speech, while hyperbole is a deliberate, often playful literary exaggeration.
  • overstatement
    4.4 0.85/m
    overstatement describes saying something is more serious or important than it is in formal claims, while hyperbole is deliberate, colorful exaggeration.
