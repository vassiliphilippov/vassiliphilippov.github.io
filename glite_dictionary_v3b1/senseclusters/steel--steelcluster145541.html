steel

verb
prepare oneself mentally for difficulty
7.0 1.10/m
Pronunciation: / stil / (AmE), / stiÀêl / (BrE)
to prepare yourself mentally to face something difficult or unpleasant with determination or courage

Examples

To steel yourself
He steeled himself for the criticism he knew was coming.
She tried to steel herself before walking into the interview.
You have to steel your nerves when delivering bad news.

Synonyms

  • steel oneself
    7.6 0.57/m
    steel oneself is the common reflexive phrase for gathering courage in speech, while steel is the base verb describing that action.
  • nerve
    6.6 0.19/m
    nerve means to force yourself to be brave in a tense moment, while steel means preparing mentally with firm determination.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brace - prepare for something difficult or unpleasant
  • synonym: fortify - strengthen mentally or emotionally
