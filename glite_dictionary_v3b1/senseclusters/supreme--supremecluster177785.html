supreme

adjective
greatest in status or authority
3.4
Pronunciation: / səˈprim /; / suˈprim / (AmE), / suːˈpriːm / (BrE)
highest in rank, quality, importance, or degree; greatest or most extreme

Examples

Supreme leader
In times of crisis, she displayed supreme confidence and leadership.
The court is the supreme authority on constitutional matters.
Winning the championship was his supreme achievement.
He showed supreme confidence during the final round of the competition.
Her supreme performance earned her a standing ovation from the audience.
The constitution is considered the supreme law of the land.
In an act of supreme generosity, he donated his entire fortune to charity.
She showed supreme confidence in her abilities during the interview.
Winning the award was a moment of supreme joy for the entire team.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inferior - lower in rank, status, or quality
  • synonym: paramount - more important than anything else; supreme
  • synonym: superior - higher in rank, quality, or importance
  • synonym: ultimate - being or happening at the end of a process; final
