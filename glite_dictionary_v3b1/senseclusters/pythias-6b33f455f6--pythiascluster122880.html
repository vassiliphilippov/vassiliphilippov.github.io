Pythias

proper noun
legendary friend of Damon in Greek legend
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪθiəs / (AmE), / ˈpɪθiəs / (BrE)
(in Greek legend) the friend of Damon, who, according to tradition, pledged his life when Pythias was condemned to death and allowed to arrange his affairs; his loyalty and the outcome are famous for exemplifying friendship and trust.

Examples

Pythias and Damon
He admired the trust between Pythias and Damon, finding it inspiring.
Many plays and poems have celebrated the loyalty of Pythias.
The story of Pythias and Damon is often cited as a symbol of true friendship.
