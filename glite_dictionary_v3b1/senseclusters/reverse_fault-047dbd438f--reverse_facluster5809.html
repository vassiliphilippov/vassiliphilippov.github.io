reverse fault

noun
geological fault with upward movement of upper block
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvɝs fɔlt / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːs fɔːlt / (BrE)
a geological fault in which the hanging wall has moved upward relative to the footwall due to compressional forces

Examples

A reverse fault moves up
A reverse fault forms when the Earth's crust is compressed, causing one side to be pushed up over the other.
Scientists identified a major reverse fault responsible for the recent earthquake in the region.
The mountain range was created by a system of reverse faults over millions of years.

Related Terms

  • antonym: normal fault - a geological fault where the hanging wall moves downward relative to the footwall
