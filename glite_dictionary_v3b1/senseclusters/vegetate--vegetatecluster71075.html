vegetate

verb
to grow or develop like a plant
3.1 7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛdʒəˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈvɛdʒɪteɪt / (BrE)
(of a plant, fungus, or organism) to grow or develop, including by asexual or abnormal means

Examples

Fungi vegetate quickly
The mold vegetated vigorously on the decaying wood.
The mushrooms vegetate rapidly in this damp cellar.
This species of algae vegetates best in shallow water.
If the lesion continues to vegetate, surgical intervention may be necessary.
Polyps can vegetate rapidly if they are not treated promptly.
Some abnormal tissues may vegetate and cause further complications.
Certain plants can easily vegetate from cuttings placed in soil.
The bacterial colony vegetated across the nutrient medium, forming a visible layer.
Under favorable conditions, the fungus will vegetate by producing new hyphae asexually.

  • synonym: reproduce - to produce offspring; to multiply biologically
