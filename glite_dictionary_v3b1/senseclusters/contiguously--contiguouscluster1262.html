contiguously

adverb
without gaps
6.4
Pronunciation: / kənˈtɪɡjuəsli / (AmE), / kənˈtɪɡjuːəsli / (BrE)
in a way that is next to or touching something else, or arranged without any gaps or interruption

Examples

Built contiguously along street
The houses were built contiguously along the narrow street, forming a single row.
She read all the chapters contiguously without taking a break between them.
The museum displays the paintings contiguously to show the artist's development over time.
The apartments were built contiguously, sharing walls with each other.
The two countries are located contiguously and share a long border.
The files are stored contiguously on the disk to improve access speed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adjacently - in a way that is next to something else
  • synonym: continuously - without stopping or interruption
  • antonym: separately - not together or not in a continuous sequence
