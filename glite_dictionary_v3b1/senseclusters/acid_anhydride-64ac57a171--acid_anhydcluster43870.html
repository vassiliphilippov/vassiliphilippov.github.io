acid anhydride

noun
compound forming acid with water
Pronunciation: / ˈæsɪd ænˈhaɪdraɪd / (AmE), / ˈæsɪd anˈhʌɪdraɪd / (BrE)
an organic compound that reacts with water to form an acid, usually by splitting into two acid molecules

Examples

Acid anhydrides react with water
Laboratory synthesis often involves the use of acid anhydrides as reagents to introduce acyl groups.
Maleic anhydride and acetic anhydride are well-known examples of acid anhydrides used in industry.
When acid anhydrides react with water, they produce two equivalents of the corresponding acid.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anhydride - a compound derived from another by removing water
