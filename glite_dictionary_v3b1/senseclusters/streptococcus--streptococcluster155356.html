streptococcus

noun
a genus of spherical bacteria; some cause disease
Pronunciation: / ˌstrɛptəˈkɑkəs / (AmE), / ˌstrɛptəˈkɒkəs / (BrE)
a genus of spherical, Gram-positive bacteria that typically occur in pairs or chains and can cause diseases such as scarlet fever and tonsillitis

Examples

Streptococcus infection
Doctors treated the infection caused by streptococcus with antibiotics.
Some strains of streptococcus are responsible for causing strep throat in children.
The laboratory test identified the presence of streptococcus in the sample.
