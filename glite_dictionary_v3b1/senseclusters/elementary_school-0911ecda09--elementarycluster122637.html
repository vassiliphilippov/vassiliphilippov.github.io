elementary school

noun
school for young children (first grades)
1.0 15/m
Pronunciation: / Àå…õl…ôÀàm…õn.t…ô.ri skul / (AmE), / Àå…õl.…™Ààm…õn.t…ôri skuÀêl / (BrE)
A school for children between the ages of about five and eleven, typically covering the first six or eight grades.

Examples

Elementary school children
Children usually learn basic math and reading skills in elementary school.
My daughter will start elementary school next fall when she turns six.
The city is building a new elementary school to accommodate the growing population.

Synonyms

  • primary school
    1.0 2.15/m
    primary school teaches children from about five to eleven in the first stages of schooling, while elementary school labels those early grades.
  • grade school
    1.4 3.08/m
    grade school covers the first six to eight years of schooling for young children, while elementary school names the same early school levels.
  • grammar school
    1.1 0.05/m
    grammar school once names the elementary grades in some places, while elementary school is the common term for those early grades.
