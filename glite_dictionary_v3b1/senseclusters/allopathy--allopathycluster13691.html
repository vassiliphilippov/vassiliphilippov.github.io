allopathy

noun
treatment of disease by conventional medicine
3.2
Pronunciation: / əˈlɑːpəθi / (AmE), / əˈlæpəθi / (BrE)
the treatment of disease by conventional methods, especially with drugs or surgery, as opposed to alternative forms of therapy

Examples

Critics of allopathy often advocate for the use of homeopathic remedies instead.
Many modern hospitals practice allopathy as the primary approach to treating illnesses.
She studied allopathy in medical school before learning about alternative therapies.
