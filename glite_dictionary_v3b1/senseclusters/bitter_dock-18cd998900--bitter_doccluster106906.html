bitter dock

noun
a weed with broad leaves and bitter root
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪtər dɑk / (AmE), / ˈbɪtə dɒk / (BrE)
a European plant (Rumex obtusifolius) with broad, rounded leaves and a bitter-tasting root, commonly found as a weed in North America

Examples

bitter dock plant
Gardeners often remove bitter dock because its roots can be tough and invasive.
The field was overgrown with bitter dock, making it difficult to plant new crops.
You can identify bitter dock by its broad, wavy-edged leaves and tall seed stalks.
