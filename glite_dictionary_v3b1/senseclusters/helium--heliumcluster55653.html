helium

noun
a very light, inert gaseous element
3.3 6.61/m
Pronunciation: / Ààhi.li.…ôm / (AmE), / ÀàhiÀê.li.…ôm / (BrE)
a very light, colorless, and inert gaseous chemical element, the second simplest element, used especially in balloons and scientific applications

Examples

helium gas
Most party balloons that float use helium because it is lighter than air.
Weather balloons are usually filled with helium instead of hydrogen because it is non-flammable.
When you inhale helium, your voice sounds much higher due to the change in sound velocity.
