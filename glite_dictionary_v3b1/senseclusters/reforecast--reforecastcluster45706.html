reforecast

noun
retrospective forecast
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈfɔːrkæst / (AmE), / ˌriːˈfɔːkɑːst / (BrE)
a forecast for a past period generated using a current model, typically to test or calibrate forecasting systems

Examples

Climate reforecast
The climate scientists analyzed the reforecast to assess the accuracy of their new model.
Each reforecast helps researchers understand how well the system would have predicted past events.
By comparing the reforecast with actual historical data, the team improved their predictions.

  • synonym: hindcast - a calculation of past conditions using present models
