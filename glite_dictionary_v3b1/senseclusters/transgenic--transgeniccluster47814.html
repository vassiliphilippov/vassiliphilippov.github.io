transgenic

adjective
with foreign genes
Pronunciation: / trænzˈdʒɛnɪk / (AmE), / trænzˈdʒɛnɪk / (BrE)
describing an organism that has had genes from another species added to its DNA

Examples

Transgenic plants
Researchers created transgenic plants that are resistant to certain pests.
The laboratory bred transgenic animals to study genetic diseases.
Many medicines are now produced using transgenic bacteria.
Scientists created transgenic mice to study the effects of a specific human gene.
The use of transgenic crops has increased in many countries over the past decade.
Some people are concerned about the safety of eating transgenic foods.

Related Terms

  • synonym: genetically modified - having genes altered by genetic engineering
  • antonym: natural - not altered or changed by genetic engineering
