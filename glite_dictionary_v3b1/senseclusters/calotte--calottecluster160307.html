calotte

noun
space-filling molecular model
9.9
Pronunciation: / kəˈlɒt / (AmE), / kəˈlɒt / (BrE)
a type of molecular model in which atoms are represented as solid spheres proportional to their actual sizes, showing how they fill space

Examples

Molecular calotte
The professor used a calotte to demonstrate the three-dimensional structure of the molecule.
In the laboratory, students compared the calotte with other types of molecular models.
A calotte helps visualize how atoms occupy space within a compound.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ball-and-stick model - a molecular model showing atoms as balls and bonds as sticks
  • synonym: space-filling model - a molecular model where atoms are shown as spheres filling the space
