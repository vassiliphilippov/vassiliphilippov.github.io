antireform

adjective
opposed to change
Pronunciation: / ˌæntaɪrɪˈfɔrm / (AmE), / ˌæntɪrɪˈfɔːm / (BrE)
opposed to changes or improvements, especially in political or social systems

Examples

Antireform politicians
The antireform politicians blocked the new education bill in parliament.
Many antireform groups voiced their concerns about the proposed changes to the healthcare system.
Public protests grew as antireform attitudes slowed progress on social issues.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conservative - favoring traditional views and resisting change
  • antonym: progressive - favoring or promoting change and reform
