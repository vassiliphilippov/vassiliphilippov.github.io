hypocalcemic

noun
person with low blood calcium
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpoʊkælˈsiːmɪk / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəʊkælˈsiːmɪk / (BrE)
a person who has hypocalcemia, meaning an abnormally low level of calcium in the blood

Examples

A hypocalcemic patient
The doctor carefully monitored the hypocalcemic for signs of muscle weakness and cramps.
A hypocalcemic may require supplements to restore normal calcium levels in the blood.
Emergency treatment was given to the hypocalcemic after he experienced severe tingling and spasms.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hypercalcemic - a person with an abnormally high level of calcium in the blood
  • synonym: patient - a person receiving medical care
