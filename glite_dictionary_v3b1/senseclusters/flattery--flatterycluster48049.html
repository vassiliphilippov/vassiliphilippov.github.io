flattery

noun
excessive or insincere praise
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈflæt̬.ɚ.i / (AmE), / ˈflæt.ər.i / (BrE)
excessive or insincere praise, especially given to further one's own interests

Examples

False flattery
He saw through their flattery and knew they wanted something from him.
Her compliments were mere flattery, designed to win his favor.
Too much flattery can make people suspicious of your intentions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: criticism - expression of disapproval or finding fault
  • synonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration
