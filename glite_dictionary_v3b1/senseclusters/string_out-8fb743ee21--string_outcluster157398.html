string out

verb
arrange in a line or series
Pronunciation: / strɪŋ aʊt / (AmE), / strɪŋ aʊt / (BrE)
to arrange or stretch something, or a series of things, in a line or over a distance; to space things out along a line or path

Examples

String out the beads
A line of hikers was strung out along the narrow mountain path.
The lights were strung out across the fence, creating a festive look for the party.
The runner's belongings were strung out from the starting point all the way to the finish line.

Related Terms

  • synonym: spread out - to distribute things so that they cover a larger area or distance
