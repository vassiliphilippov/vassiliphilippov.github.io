quiver

noun
a slight trembling or shaking motion
4.4 3.2
Pronunciation: /ˈkwɪ.vər/ (AmE), /ˈkwɪ.və/ (BrE)
a slight trembling or shaking, often caused by emotion such as fear or excitement

Examples

A nervous quiver
A slight quiver in his voice betrayed his nervousness.
She felt a quiver of excitement as the curtain rose.
The leaves had a barely noticeable quiver in the gentle breeze.
A quiver of anticipation ran through the crowd as the curtain rose.
His voice had a quiver when he spoke about the accident.
She felt a quiver of fear at the thought of going on stage.
A quiver in her voice revealed her nervousness.

The leaves had a slight quiver as the breeze passed through them.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shiver - a trembling movement, often from cold or fear
  • degree higher: shudder - a strong or violent trembling movement
  • antonym: stillness - the absence of movement or vibration
  • synonym: tremor - an involuntary shaking movement
  • synonym: vibration - rapid back-and-forth motion; can mean shaking
