sequencing

noun
arranging music electronically
4.4
Pronunciation: /ˈsiː.kwən.sɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈsiː.kwən.sɪŋ/ (BrE)
the process of using electronic hardware or software to record, arrange, and play back musical notes or events in a specific order

Examples

Music sequencing
Modern producers often use sequencing to build complex electronic tracks quickly and efficiently.
The band's new album features creative sequencing of drum patterns and synthesizer lines.
He learned the basics of sequencing to experiment with different arrangements for his songs.
