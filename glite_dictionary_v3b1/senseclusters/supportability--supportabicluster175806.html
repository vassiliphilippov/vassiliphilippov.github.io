supportability

noun
capacity to bear weight
Pronunciation: / səˌpɔrtəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / səˌpɔːtəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the ability of a structure, object, or design to bear weight or remain stable without collapsing

Examples

Supportability of a bridge
The supportability of the bridge was evaluated before allowing vehicles to cross.
Designers must consider the supportability of new furniture to ensure safety.
After several modifications, the supportability of the structure improved significantly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fragility - the quality of being easily broken or damaged
  • synonym: stability - the quality of being steady and not likely to fall or change
