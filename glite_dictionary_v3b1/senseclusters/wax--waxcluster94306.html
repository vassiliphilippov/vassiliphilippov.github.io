wax

verb
(of the moon) to increase in visible size
7.5 6.3
Pronunciation: /wæks/ (AmE), /wæks/ (BrE)
to grow or increase in size, strength, or intensity (often used for the moon or in literary contexts)

Examples

The moon waxes
As the days passed, we could see the moon wax from a thin crescent to nearly full.
Each night this week, the waxing moon appeared larger in the sky.
In folklore, certain rituals were performed only when the moon was waxing toward fullness.
Her confidence seemed to wax with each new achievement.
In the tale, the moon would wax until it became full and bright.
Their influence continued to wax throughout the region during the early centuries.
