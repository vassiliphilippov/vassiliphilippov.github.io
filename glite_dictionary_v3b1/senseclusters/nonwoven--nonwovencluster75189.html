nonwoven

noun
bonded fiber fabric
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˈwoʊvən / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈwəʊvən / (BrE)
a fabric made from fibers bonded together by chemical, mechanical, heat, or solvent treatment, rather than being woven or knitted

Examples

A nonwoven material
The hospital uses nonwoven materials for disposable gowns and masks.
A new type of nonwoven is being developed for use in air filters.
Unlike traditional cloth, a nonwoven does not require weaving or knitting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: felt - a nonwoven fabric made by matting fibers together
  • antonym: knitted - fabric made by interlocking loops of yarn
  • antonym: woven - fabric made by interlacing threads in a regular pattern
