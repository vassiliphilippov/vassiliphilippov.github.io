Ezechiel

proper noun
alternative spelling of Ezekiel (Old Testament book)
Pronunciation: / ɪˈziːkiɛl / (AmE), / ɪˈziːkiəl / (BrE)
an alternative spelling of Ezekiel, referring to the Old Testament book that contains the prophet Ezekiel's visions and prophecies regarding the downfall and restoration of Jerusalem and Judah

Examples

Ezechiel book
In medieval manuscripts, the prophet Ezechiel is often depicted alongside other major figures of the Old Testament.
Some biblical translations use the spelling Ezechiel instead of the more common 'Ezekiel'.
The Book of Ezechiel describes powerful visions seen by the prophet during the Babylonian exile.
