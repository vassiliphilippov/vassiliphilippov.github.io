rear

verb
rise up on the hind legs (of a quadruped)
9.9
Pronunciation: / rɪr / (AmE), / rɪə / (BrE)
to rise up or stand upright, especially of animals, things, or in a sudden or tall manner

Examples

The horse rears up
The circus trainer taught the elephant to rear on command.
The frightened horse suddenly reared and threw its rider.
When startled by a loud noise, the zebra reared up on its hind legs.
Flames reared into the night sky, threatening the nearby trees.
The horse suddenly reared as the thunder crashed overhead.
With effort, he reared the heavy banner upright.
A massive cliff reared before them as they approached the coastline.
The monument reared into view as we turned the corner.
The mountain seemed to rear above the surrounding hills, dominating the landscape.

  • synonym: raise - to lift or move to a higher position
  • synonym: rise - to move upwards or to increase in position or level
