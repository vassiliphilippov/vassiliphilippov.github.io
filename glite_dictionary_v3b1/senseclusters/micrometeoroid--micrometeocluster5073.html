micrometeoroid

noun
tiny meteoroid or meteorite
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪ.kroʊˈmiː.ti.ɚˌrɔɪd / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.krəʊˈmiː.ti.ɔɪd / (BrE)
A very small meteoroid, typically too small to burn up from friction upon entering the Earth's atmosphere and often reaching the surface.

Examples

A micrometeoroid strike
A single micrometeoroid can puncture the wall of a satellite if it strikes at high speed.
Scientists study micrometeoroids to learn more about the composition of the solar system.
The spacecraft is equipped with shielding to protect against micrometeoroid impacts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: meteoroid - a small body moving in the solar system, smaller than an asteroid
