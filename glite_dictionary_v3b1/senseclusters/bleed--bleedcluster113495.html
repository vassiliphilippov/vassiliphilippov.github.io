bleed

verb
color or dye spreading or running
4.4
Pronunciation: / bliːd / (AmE), / bliːd / (BrE)
(of a color or dye) to spread or run into surrounding areas, causing blurring or diffusion.

Examples

Colors bleed onto clothes
Be careful—if the fabric gets wet, the red dye might bleed onto your other clothes.
Some cheap markers tend to bleed through the paper and stain the next page.
The blue paint started to bleed into the white areas when it rained.
