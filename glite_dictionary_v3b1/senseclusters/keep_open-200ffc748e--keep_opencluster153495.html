keep open

verb
retain possibility or rights to something
3.1 3.37/m
Pronunciation: / kip Àào äp…ôn / (AmE), / kiÀêp Àà…ô äp…ôn / (BrE)
to retain the possibility, rights, or availability of something for future use or consideration

Examples

To keep open communication
Please keep open the line of communication between departments.
She wants to keep open her seat on the committee until she returns.
The company decided to keep open the option of hiring more staff next year.

Synonyms

  • hold open
    3.7 1.03/m
    hold open is a formal phrase used to keep something available or rights intact, while keep open means retaining availability for future use.
  • leave the door open
    3.6 0.58/m
    leave the door open is an informal idiom that keeps possibilities for later, while keep open is a neutral phrase about retaining availability.
  • keep one's options open
    4.2 0.46/m
    keep one's options open is a personal strategy to avoid deciding now, while keep open is about preserving rights or future availability.

Related Terms

  • synonym: keep available - refrain from making something unavailable; to maintain for possible future use
