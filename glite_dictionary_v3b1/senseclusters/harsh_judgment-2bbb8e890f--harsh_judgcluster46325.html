harsh judgment

noun
severe criticism or condemnation
4.4
Pronunciation: / hɑrʃ ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (AmE), / hɑːʃ ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (BrE)
severe criticism or condemnation, often considered to be unreasonably strict or unkind.

Examples

Harsh judgment from public
She received harsh judgment from the public for her controversial speech.
The committee delivered a harsh judgment on the company's unethical practices.
Writers sometimes fear harsh judgment when they share their personal stories.

Related Terms

  • synonym: criticism - the act of expressing disapproval and noting faults or problems
  • antonym: praise - the expression of approval or admiration
