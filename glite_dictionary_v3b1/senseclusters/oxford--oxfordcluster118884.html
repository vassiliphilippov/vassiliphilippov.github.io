oxford

noun
a low laced shoe
Pronunciation: / ˈɑks.fɚd / (AmE), / ˈɒks.fəd / (BrE)
a type of low shoe fastened with laces over the instep, typically made of leather and worn as formal footwear

Examples

A pair of oxfords
A pair of classic leather oxfords completed his formal outfit.
He polished his black oxfords before the job interview.
She prefers wearing oxfords to high heels at work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brogue - a sturdy shoe with decorative perforations
  • antonym: loafer - a slip-on shoe with no laces
