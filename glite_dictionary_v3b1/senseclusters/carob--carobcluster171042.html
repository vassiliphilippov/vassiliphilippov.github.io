carob

noun
Mediterranean tree with edible pods
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkærəb / (AmE), / ˈkærəb / (BrE)
an evergreen Mediterranean tree (Ceratonia siliqua) whose long, edible pods are often used as a chocolate substitute

Examples

Carob tree
Health-conscious bakers sometimes use carob instead of cocoa in their recipes.
Seeds from the carob are sometimes used as a natural thickener in food products.
The carob tree produces large, sweet pods that are often ground into powder.
