prograde

adjective
advancing outward
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈproʊˌɡreɪd / (AmE), / ˈprəʊˌɡreɪd / (BrE)
advancing or building outward, especially seaward, as when sediments extend a shoreline or delta

Examples

Prograde shoreline growth
The coastline experienced prograde development as new sediments built up over time.
A prograde delta forms when a river deposits more material, extending the land into the sea.
Geologists studied the prograde movement of the shoreline after the construction of the dam.

Related Terms

  • antonym: regressive - moving backward or landward, especially of shorelines or deltas
