exclusivity

noun
state of being exclusive
Pronunciation: / ˌɛkskluˈsɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌɛkskluːˈsɪvɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being exclusive or limited to a particular person, group, or area and not shared with others

Examples

Exclusivity of the club
The exclusivity of the club means only members can attend its events.
There is a sense of exclusivity when a product is available to a limited group of people.
The agreement was based on the exclusivity of the two options, so only one could be chosen.
The luxury brand is known for its exclusivity and high prices.
Many people are attracted to the exclusivity of private clubs.
The event's exclusivity made it difficult for most people to attend.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exclusiveness - the quality of being exclusive or not shared
  • antonym: inclusivity - the quality of including all or many people or things
  • synonym: mutual exclusivity - the condition where two things cannot both be true or occur at the same time
