contrastiveness

noun
distinctive quality
8.1
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈtræs.tɪv.nəs/ (AmE), /kən.ˈtræs.tɪv.nəs/ /; / /kɒn.ˈtræs.tɪv.nəs/ (BrE)
the quality of being able to distinguish meaning, especially in language, by making differences clear

Examples

Contrastiveness of sounds
The contrastiveness of vowel sounds in English allows speakers to distinguish between words like 'ship' and 'sheep'.
Linguists study the contrastiveness of certain features to understand how languages encode meaning.
In some languages, tone has a high degree of contrastiveness and can change the meaning of a word entirely.

Related Terms

  • synonym: distinctiveness - the quality of being clearly different or unique
  • antonym: similarity - the state of being alike or having features in common
