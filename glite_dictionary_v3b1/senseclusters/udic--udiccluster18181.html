udic

adjective
soil with ample moisture
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈjuːdɪk / (AmE), / ˈjuːdɪk / (BrE)
having a soil moisture regime with enough water available for plant growth during most of the year

Examples

Udic soil moisture
Farmers prefer udic soils because they provide reliable moisture for crops throughout the growing season.
The region's udic conditions support a wide variety of plant species year-round.
In areas with udic soil, irrigation is rarely necessary for successful agriculture.

  • antonym: arid - having very little moisture; dry
  • degree lower: xeric - having a soil moisture regime that is very dry
