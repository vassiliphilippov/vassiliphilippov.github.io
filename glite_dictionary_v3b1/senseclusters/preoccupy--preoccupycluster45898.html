preoccupy

verb
to absorb the mind; to engross attention
6.3 5.44/m
Pronunciation: / priÀà…ëÀêkj…ôÀåpa…™ / (AmE), / priÀà…íkj äÀåpa…™ / (BrE)
to absorb or dominate the mind or attention of (a person) to the exclusion of other thoughts; to engage urgently or obsessively.

Examples

Thoughts preoccupy her mind
His research project continues to preoccupy him, even during his vacation.
Lately, worries about the future preoccupy her mind so much that she loses sleep.
Thoughts of the upcoming event preoccupied everyone in the office for days.

Synonyms

  • obsess
    5.5 12/m
    obsess fills a person's mind so they cannot stop thinking, while preoccupy means to hold attention and distract from other thoughts.
  • fixate
    7.0 2.05/m
    fixate means to focus attention or emotion strongly on someone or something, while preoccupy means to absorb the mind and exclude other thoughts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absorb - to take up the attention or interest completely
  • antonym: distract - to prevent someone from giving full attention to something
