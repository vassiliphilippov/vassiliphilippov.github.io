draw parallel

verb
point out similarities; make a comparison
1.1
Pronunciation: / drɔː ˈpærəˌlɛl / (AmE), / drɔː ˈparəlɛl / (BrE)
to point out or identify similarities between two or more things, often to make a comparison or analogy

Examples

To draw parallels between events
Historians often draw parallels between past political events and current affairs.
It's easy to draw parallels between her experience and the challenges many new immigrants face.
She tried to draw parallel between the two novels to show their thematic similarities.
