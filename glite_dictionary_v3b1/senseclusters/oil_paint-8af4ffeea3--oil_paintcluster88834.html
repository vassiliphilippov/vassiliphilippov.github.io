oil paint

noun
paint with drying oil as a base
1.5
Pronunciation: / ɔɪl peɪnt / (AmE), / ɔɪl peɪnt / (BrE)
A type of paint in which pigment is suspended in a drying oil, commonly used in art and painting.

Examples

Oil paint colors
Many classic portraits were created using oil paint due to its rich colors and texture.
The artist worked for hours to blend oil paint on the canvas.
You should let the oil paint dry thoroughly before adding another layer.

Related Terms

  • antonym: watercolor - paint where pigments are suspended in water-based solution
