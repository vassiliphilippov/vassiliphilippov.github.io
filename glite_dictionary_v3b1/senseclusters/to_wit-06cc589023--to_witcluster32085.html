to wit

adverb
namely; that is to say
9.9
Pronunciation: / tu ˈwɪt / (AmE), / tə ˈwɪt / (BrE)
namely; that is to say; as follows (used to introduce an explanation, itemization, or clarification)

Examples

To wit namely
Several countries, to wit, France and Germany, objected to the proposal.
The committee will address three key issues, to wit: funding, staffing, and scheduling.
There are two ways to solve the problem, to wit, by negotiation or by direct action.
