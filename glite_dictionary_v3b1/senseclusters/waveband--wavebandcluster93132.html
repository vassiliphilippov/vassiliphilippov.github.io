waveband

noun
range of frequencies for radio/TV signals
Pronunciation: / ˈweɪvˌbænd /; / ˈweɪvbænd / (AmE), / ˈweɪvbænd / (BrE)
a range of radio frequencies or wavelengths used for broadcasting or transmitting signals (such as radio or television)

Examples

A waveband range
Modern televisions can detect multiple wavebands for better signal clarity.
The emergency services use a dedicated waveband for their communications.
The radio station switched to a different waveband to reach a wider audience.
Each radio station broadcasts on a different waveband to avoid interference.
Scientists measured the intensity of light in each waveband to study the star.
Television signals are transmitted over a specific waveband assigned by authorities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: channel - a specific frequency or band used for transmission
  • synonym: frequency band - a range of frequencies within a given band, especially that used for transmitting a signal
