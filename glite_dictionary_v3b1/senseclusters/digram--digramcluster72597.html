digram

noun
pair of letters representing one sound
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈdɪɡræm / (BrE)
a pair of successive letters, especially two that together represent a single sound, such as 'sh' in 'shoe'

Examples

A digram in words
Linguists use the term digram when discussing combinations of letters like 'ch' in 'chess'.
Teachers explained that 'th' in 'this' is an example of a digram.
The English word 'phone' contains the digram 'ph', representing the /f/ sound.
