disunify

verb
cause to be separated or divided
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈjuːnɪfaɪ / (AmE), / dɪsˈjuːnɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to cause something that was united or together to become divided or separated

Examples

To disunify a group
Conflicting interests can disunify a team that once worked well together.
Leaders worry that proposed reforms might disunify the organization and weaken its influence.
The policy changes threatened to disunify the previously united federation.
The controversial policy disunified the community, leading to heated debates among its members.
A lack of clear communication can quickly disunify even the strongest teams.
The leader's actions disunified the organization, resulting in several groups breaking away.

Related Terms

  • synonym: divide - to separate into parts or groups
  • antonym: unify - bring together or combine into a single entity
