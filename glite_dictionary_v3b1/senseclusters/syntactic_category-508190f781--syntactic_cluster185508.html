syntactic category

noun
class of words with shared grammatical function
9.6
Pronunciation: / sɪnˈtæktɪk ˈkætəˌɡɔri / (AmE), / sɪnˈtaktɪk ˈkatəɡəri / (BrE)
(linguistics) a class of words that share the same grammatical properties, such as noun, verb, or adjective.

Examples

A syntactic category class
A noun is a typical syntactic category in English and many other languages.
Learning the syntactic categories helps students understand how sentences are structured.
Linguists debate whether prepositions form a separate syntactic category from adverbs.
