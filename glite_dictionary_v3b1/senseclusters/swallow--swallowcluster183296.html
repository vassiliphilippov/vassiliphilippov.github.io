swallow

verb
accept or tolerate something unpleasant
7.2 7/m
Pronunciation: / Ààsw…ëÀêlo ä / (AmE), / Ààsw…íl…ô ä / (BrE)
to accept, tolerate, or believe something unpleasant or hard to believe, often without protest

Examples

To swallow the truth
He found it difficult to swallow the criticism from his colleagues.
She had to swallow her disappointment and congratulate the winner.
Sometimes you just have to swallow your pride and ask for help.
He just smiled and swallowed the lie, though he knew it wasn't true.
I can't believe you actually swallowed his excuse for being late.
She tends to swallow everything the tabloids print without a second thought.
