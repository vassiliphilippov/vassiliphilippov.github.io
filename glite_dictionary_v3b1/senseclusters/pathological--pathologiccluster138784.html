(of a person or their behavior) exhibiting or caused by a strong and often irrational compulsion or obsession, especially relating to a mental disturbance.

Examples

Pathological liar
His obsession with cleanliness reached a pathological level, making daily life difficult.
She was known as a pathological liar, unable to tell the truth.
The company's pathological need for control eventually drove away its most creative employees.

Synonyms

  • obsessional
    7.7 0.21/m
    obsessional describes repeated intrusive thoughts or urges that focus on one idea, while pathological covers broader irrational compulsive behaviors.
  • psychopathological
    9.9 0.38/m
    psychopathological refers to mental disorders in a clinical, technical way, while pathological is used more broadly for disturbed behavior.
