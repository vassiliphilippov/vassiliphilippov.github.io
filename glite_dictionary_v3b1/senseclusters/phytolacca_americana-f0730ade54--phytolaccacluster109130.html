Phytolacca americana

A tall, coarse perennial plant native to North America, with small white flowers and blackish-red berries on long drooping clusters; its young stems are edible, but its berries and roots are poisonous.

Examples

Phytolacca americana plant
Gardeners are advised to remove Phytolacca americana promptly because its berries can pose a danger to children and pets.
Some people cook the young shoots of Phytolacca americana as a traditional spring green, known as poke salad.
The berries of Phytolacca americana are a striking reddish-black and are highly toxic to humans.
