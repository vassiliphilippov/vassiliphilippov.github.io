kickback

noun
illegal payment to influence a business decision
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪkˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈkɪkˌbak / (BrE)
an illicit payment made to someone in return for facilitating a transaction or appointment, especially as a commercial bribe

Examples

Illegal kickbacks
Government officials were accused of taking kickbacks in exchange for awarding contracts.
He lost his job when it was discovered he'd accepted a kickback from a supplier.
The company was investigated after allegations of a kickback scheme involving major contractors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bribe - money or favor given to influence judgment or conduct
  • degree lower: commission - a sum of money paid to an agent in a commercial transaction, usually legitimate
  • synonym: graft - dishonest or corrupt practices, especially in politics or business
