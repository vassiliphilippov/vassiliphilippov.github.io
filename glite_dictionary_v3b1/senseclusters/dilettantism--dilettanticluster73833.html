dilettantism

noun
superficial interest
9.9
Pronunciation: /dɪl.ə.ˈtæn.tɪ.zəm/ /; / /dɪl.ə.ˈtɑːn.tɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /dɪl.ə.ˈtæn.tɪ.zəm/ /; / /dɪl.ɪ.ˈtæn.tɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
an amateur or superficial interest in a subject, especially without serious commitment or deep knowledge

Examples

Her dilettantism in painting
Her dilettantism in painting was clear when she quickly lost interest after a few lessons.
The professor criticized the student's dilettantism and encouraged deeper study of the subject.
Many people mistake his dilettantism for genuine passion, but he rarely finishes what he starts.
His dilettantism in photography led him to experiment with many different styles.
Critics accused the author of dilettantism because she lacked formal training in literature.
While some saw her dilettantism as unfocused, others admired her curiosity and passion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amateurism - the practice of engaging in an activity without professional skill or commitment
  • antonym: expertise - advanced knowledge or skill in a particular area
  • antonym: professionalism - the competence or skill expected of a professional
