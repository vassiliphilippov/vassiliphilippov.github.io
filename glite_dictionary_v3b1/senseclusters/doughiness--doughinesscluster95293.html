doughiness

noun
the quality of being like dough
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˈdoʊ.i.nəs / (AmE), / ˈdəʊ.i.nəs / (BrE)
The quality of being like dough, especially in appearance or texture; softness and slight stickiness often resulting from being underbaked or uncooked.

Examples

The doughiness of crust
A certain doughiness in pizza crust indicates it needs more time in the oven.
She found the doughiness of the cookies unappealing and preferred them crispier.
The bread's doughiness made it seem as if it had not fully baked.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chewiness - the quality of being chewy in texture
  • antonym: crispness - the quality of being crisp, especially in texture
