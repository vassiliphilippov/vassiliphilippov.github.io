European elder

noun
shrub/tree with black berries, used for wines/jellies
Pronunciation: / jʊˈroʊpiən ˈɛldər / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən ˈɛldə / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub or small tree (Sambucus nigra) native to Europe and Asia, with black berry-like fruit used for making wines and jellies.

Examples

The European elder bush
Last summer, we planted a European elder in our backyard for its beautiful flowers.
Scientists are studying extracts from the European elder for potential health benefits.
The berries of the European elder are often used to make wine and jam.

Related Terms

  • synonym: elderberry - the dark purple berry of the elder tree, used in foods and drinks
