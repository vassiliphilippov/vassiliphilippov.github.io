au pair

noun
young foreign child-care assistant
9.9
Pronunciation: / oʊ pɛr / (AmE), / əʊ pɛə / (BrE)
A young person, usually from a foreign country, who lives with a family and helps with child care and light housework in exchange for room, board, and a small allowance.

Examples

An au pair family
An au pair typically receives room, board, and a modest weekly allowance.
Living as an au pair gave her a chance to experience British culture firsthand.
They hired an au pair from France to help with their children during the summer.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: domestic worker - a person who works within the employer's household
  • synonym: nanny - someone employed to look after children in a household
