saddle roof

noun
roof with two sloping sides and gables
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈsæd.əl ˌruːf / (AmE), / ˈsæd.əl ˌruːf / (BrE)
A roof consisting of two sloping sides that meet at a ridge, with gables at each end; also known as a gable roof.

Examples

A saddle roof design
Many barns in the region feature a classic saddle roof design.
The architect recommended a saddle roof to ensure proper rainwater drainage.
The old church has a traditional saddle roof with red clay tiles.
