decollation

noun
removal of shell tip
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːkəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːkɒˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
in zoology, the loss or removal of the apex or upper whorls of a shell, resulting in a truncated structure

Examples

Snail decollation
The scientist observed decollation in several snail species as they matured.
During decollation, the upper part of the shell is lost, leaving a blunt end.
Researchers documented the process of decollation in their study of mollusk shell development.

Related Terms

  • synonym: truncation - the act of shortening by cutting off a part
