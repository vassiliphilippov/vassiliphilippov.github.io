subbituminous

adjective
type of coal
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌbˌbɪtəˈmɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˌbɪtjʊˈmɪnəs / (BrE)
describing a type of coal that has properties between lignite and bituminous coal

Examples

Subbituminous coal type
The region is known for its large deposits of subbituminous coal.
Many power plants prefer subbituminous coal because it produces less sulfur dioxide.
Geologists studied the characteristics of subbituminous samples from the new mine.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: bituminous - a type of coal with higher carbon content than subbituminous coal
  • degree lower: lignite - a soft brownish coal showing traces of plant structure
