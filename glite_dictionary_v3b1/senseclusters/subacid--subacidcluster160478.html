subacid

adjective
slightly sour in taste
Pronunciation: / sʌbˈæsɪd / (AmE), / sʌbˈæsɪd / (BrE)
Slightly sour or tart to the taste; less acidic than true acid.

Examples

A subacid fruit
Gardeners often choose subacid fruits for pies because they provide a subtle tartness.
Some varieties of grapes are known for their subacid taste, making them excellent for eating fresh.
The apple had a subacid flavor that balanced its sweetness perfectly.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: acid - having a sharp, sour taste
  • antonym: sweet - having a taste characteristic of sugar, not sour or bitter
