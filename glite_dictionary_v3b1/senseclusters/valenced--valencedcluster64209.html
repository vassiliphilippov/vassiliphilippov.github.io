valenced

adjective
having specified valency
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈveɪlənst / (AmE), / ˈveɪlənst / (BrE)
having a specified valency or number of required arguments, especially in reference to verbs in linguistics

Examples

Valenced verbs
Some verbs in English are highly valenced and require both a direct and an indirect object.
The linguist explained how valenced verbs differ from those that do not take objects.
In this language, most action words are valenced and need at least one argument.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intransitive - not requiring a direct object
  • synonym: transitive - requiring a direct object to complete its meaning
