dower

noun
widow's right to husband's property
7.0 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊ.ər / (AmE), / ˈdaʊ.ə / (BrE)
A widow's legal right to a portion of her deceased husband's property, typically for her lifetime.

Examples

Dower rights for widows
After her husband died, she claimed her dower rights in the family estate.
The couple arranged the property to include a dower for the wife.
The law ensured that a widow could not be deprived of her dower.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dowry - property or money brought by a bride to her husband upon marriage
