bacteria bed

noun
sewage treatment filter of sand or gravel
Pronunciation: / bækˈtɪriə bɛd / (AmE), / bækˈtɪəriə bɛd / (BrE)
a layer of sand or gravel used in sewage treatment to expose effluent to air and the action of microorganisms

Examples

A bacteria bed layer
A bacteria bed relies on microorganisms to break down organic waste in the sewage.
Routine maintenance is required to prevent clogging of the bacteria bed.
The new wastewater plant features a modern bacteria bed to improve purification.
