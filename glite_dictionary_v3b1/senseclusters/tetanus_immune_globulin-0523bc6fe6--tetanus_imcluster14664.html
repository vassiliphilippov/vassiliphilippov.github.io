tetanus immune globulin

noun
antibody solution for tetanus protection
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛt̬.ən.əs ɪˈmjun ɡloʊˈbju.lɪn / (AmE), / ˈtɛt.ə.nəs ɪˈmjuːn ˌɡlɒb.jʊ.lɪn / (BrE)
a sterile solution of antibodies obtained from the blood plasma of individuals immunized against tetanus, used to provide temporary protection against tetanus infection

Examples

tetanus immune globulin injection
Emergency guidelines recommend tetanus immune globulin for people with deep puncture wounds.
If your vaccination is out of date, you may need a dose of tetanus immune globulin.
The doctor administered tetanus immune globulin to the patient after the dog bite.
