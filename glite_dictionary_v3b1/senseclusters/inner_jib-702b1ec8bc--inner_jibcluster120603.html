inner jib

noun
sail between outer jib and fore staysail
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnɚ dʒɪb / (AmE), / ˈɪnə dʒɪb / (BrE)
A sail set between the outer jib and the fore staysail on a large square-rigged ship.

Examples

The inner jib sail
During the storm, the captain ordered the inner jib to be taken down for safety.
Sailors trimmed the inner jib to improve the ship's speed and maneuverability.
The crew worked quickly to hoist the inner jib before the wind changed direction.
