boastful

adjective
proudly self-important; inclined to boast
3.7 0.58/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆboÊŠstfÉ™l / (AmE), / ËˆbÉ™ÊŠstfÉ™l / (BrE)
Showing excessive pride and self-satisfaction in one's achievements, possessions, or abilities; given to boasting.

Examples

A boastful person
Although he is talented, his boastful attitude makes it hard to work with him.
Her boastful remarks about her accomplishments annoyed everyone at the meeting.
The boastful tone of the brochure exaggerated the company's success.

Synonyms

  • braggy
    7.8 0.10/m
    braggy means talking with loud pride about achievements in an informal way, while boastful shows excessive self-satisfaction and pride.
  • crowing
    6.3 0.00/m
    crowing is a noisy way of showing pride and gloating about success, while boastful means openly showing excessive self-satisfaction.
  • self-aggrandizing
    8.6 0.51/m
    self-aggrandizing describes actions that make you seem more important or powerful, while boastful focuses on showing pride in achievements.
  • vainglorious
    9.9 0.18/m
    vainglorious sounds formal and shows proud display of ability or looks, while boastful is general public bragging about success.
  • conceited
    5.4 0.84/m
    conceited thinks they are better than others in a quiet way, while boastful loudly shows pride and self-satisfaction.
  • overproud
    7.2 0.02/m
    overproud is simply too proud in feeling or attitude, while boastful usually shows that pride by talking about success.
  • prideful
    6.1 0.50/m
    prideful feels an arrogant sense of superiority toward others, while boastful expresses that pride openly by praising oneself.
  • overweening
    9.8 0.18/m
    overweening shows excessive confidence or ambition that may seem strong or reckless, while boastful focuses on showing pride aloud.
  • egotistical
    6.6 1.53/m
    egotistical is being self-centered and focused on oneself, while boastful is specifically about showing pride in achievements to others.
  • cock-a-hoop
    9.9 0.01/m
    cock-a-hoop sounds idiomatic and means loudly triumphant and proud after success, while boastful is a general trait of bragging.
