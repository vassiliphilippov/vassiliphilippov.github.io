consummatory

adjective
completing an act
Pronunciation: / kənˈsʌməˌtɔri / (AmE), / kənˈsʌmjʊˌtəri /; / kənˈsʌmjʊtəri / (BrE)
relating to the completion or fulfillment of something, especially the final stage of an action or the act that finalizes a marriage or union

Examples

The consummatory ceremony
The consummatory ceremony marked the official completion of their union.
In some cultures, a consummatory act is required to validate the marriage.
The contract was not considered binding until the consummatory step was completed.
The scientist observed the animal's consummatory behavior after it found the hidden food.
A consummatory act, such as eating, follows a period of searching or preparation.
The ritual ended with a consummatory gesture that signified completion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: concluding - bringing something to an end or close
  • synonym: final - coming at the end; concluding
  • antonym: preliminary - coming before the main event; introductory
  • antonym: preparatory - serving as preparation for something
