Mâcon

noun
dry white Burgundy wine from France
Pronunciation: / mɑːˈkoʊn / (AmE), / mæˈkɒ̃ / (BrE)
a dry white wine produced in the Burgundy region of France, especially from the Mâconnais area

Examples

A Mâcon wine
She prefers the crisp acidity of a Mâcon to the fuller-bodied Chardonnays.
The wine list included several fine Mâcons from reputable vineyards.
We enjoyed a bottle of chilled Mâcon with our seafood dinner.
