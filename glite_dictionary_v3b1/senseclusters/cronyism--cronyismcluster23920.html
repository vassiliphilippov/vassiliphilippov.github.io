cronyism

noun
unfair favoritism toward friends, especially in appointments
9.9 6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈkroʊ.ni.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈkroʊ.ni.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈkrəʊ.nɪ.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈkrəʊ.nɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the practice of giving unfair preference to friends or associates, especially in political appointments or employment, regardless of their qualifications

Examples

Cronyism in hiring
Efforts to curb cronyism include greater transparency in public sector hiring.
Many voters are frustrated by the persistent cronyism in local politics.
The government was accused of cronyism after appointing several unqualified friends to key positions.
