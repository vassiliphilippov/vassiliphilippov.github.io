pretext

noun
false reason to conceal real motive
5.1 5.4
Pronunciation: /ˈpriː.tekst/ (AmE), /ˈpriː.tekst/ (BrE)
a false reason or excuse given to hide the real motive for an action

Examples

A pretext for leaving
He left the party early on the pretext of an urgent phone call.
She called in sick under the pretext of having a cold, but she actually wanted a day off.
The meeting was just a pretext for discussing their secret project.
He used a pretext of illness to avoid going to the meeting.
She called her friend on the pretext of needing help with homework, but really wanted to chat.
The company cited budget cuts as a pretext for laying off several employees.

Related Terms

  • synonym: excuse - a reason or explanation given to justify a fault or offense
  • synonym: guise - an external form or appearance concealing the true nature of something
  • synonym: pretense - an attempt to make something that is not the case appear true
  • synonym: ruse - an action intended to deceive someone; a trick
  • antonym: truth - the real reason or actual state of affairs
