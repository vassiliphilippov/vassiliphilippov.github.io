moderating

adjective
reducing intensity or strength
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːdɚˌeɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɒdəˌreɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
making something less intense or less strong; serving to reduce or temper extreme conditions.

Examples

Moderating effect
Certain chemicals have a moderating effect on the reaction, making it safer to handle.
She played a moderating role in the heated discussion, preventing it from escalating.
The moderating influence of the ocean keeps temperatures mild throughout the year.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intensifying - making something stronger or more intense
  • synonym: tempering - serving to moderate or soften something
