line up

verb
form a line; queue
1.0 2.2
Pronunciation: /laɪn ʌp/ (AmE), /laɪn ʌp/ (BrE)
to form or arrange people or things in a straight line or in an orderly row

Examples

People line up outside
During the sale, shoppers would line up around the block.
People started to line up outside the theater hours before the doors opened.
Please line up in an orderly fashion before entering the classroom.
All the cars lined up perfectly along the street.
The children lined up in pairs for their field trip.
We were asked to line up in front of the theater before entering.
He carefully lined up the photos on the shelf so they looked neat.
Please line up the chairs against the wall before the event starts.
The teacher asked the students to line up in front of the classroom.

Related Terms

  • synonym: align - to arrange in a straight line or into correct relative positions
  • synonym: arrange - to put things in a particular order or position
  • antonym: disperse - to spread people or things over a wide area; opposite of lining up
  • synonym: queue - a line of people or vehicles waiting their turn
  • antonym: scatter - to separate and spread out people or things over an area
  • degree lower: wait - remain in a place until an expected event happens
