geosynchronous orbit

noun
an orbit with a 24-hour period around Earth
7.3 5.5
Pronunciation: /dʒiː.oʊ.ˈsɪŋ.krə.nəs ˈɔːr.bət/ /; / /dʒiː.oʊ.ˈsɪŋ.krə.nəs ˈɔːr.bɪt/ (AmE), /dʒiː.əʊ.ˈsɪŋ.krə.nəs ˈɔː.bɪt/ /; / /dʒiː.əʊ.ˈsɪŋ.krə.nəs ˈɔː.bət/ (BrE)
an orbit around the Earth in which a satellite takes exactly 24 hours to complete one revolution, thus remaining in sync with the Earth's rotation

Examples

A geosynchronous orbit satellite
A satellite in a geosynchronous orbit appears to return to the same spot in the sky each day.
Many communication satellites are placed in a geosynchronous orbit to match the Earth's rotation.
Weather satellites benefit from being in a geosynchronous orbit to provide continuous coverage of a particular region.
