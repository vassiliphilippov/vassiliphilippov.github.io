dB

noun
logarithmic unit of sound intensity or signal power
4.9 0.2/m
US / diÀê ÀàbiÀê /
UK / diÀê ÀàbiÀê /
a logarithmic unit used to measure the intensity of sound or the power level of an electrical signal, equal to ten times the logarithm to base 10 of the ratio of the measured value to a reference value
