dB

noun
logarithmic unit of sound intensity or signal power
4.9 5/m
Pronunciation: / diÀê ÀàbiÀê / (AmE), / diÀê ÀàbiÀê / (BrE)
a logarithmic unit used to measure the intensity of sound or the power level of an electrical signal, equal to ten times the logarithm to base 10 of the ratio of the measured value to a reference value

Examples

Sound level in dB
Normal conversation typically has a sound level of around 60 dB.
She adjusted the volume, increasing it by just a few dB for better clarity.
The noise level in the factory reached 90 dB, which requires ear protection.
