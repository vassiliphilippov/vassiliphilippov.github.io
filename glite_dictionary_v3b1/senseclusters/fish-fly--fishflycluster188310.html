fish-fly

noun
a bait insect, smaller than the dobsonfly
6.9 7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪʃˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃˌflʌɪ / (BrE)
An insect similar to, but smaller than, the dobsonfly; its larvae are often used as fishing bait.

Examples

The fish-fly larvae
Anglers often use fish-flies as bait because their larvae attract many types of fish.
I spotted several fish-flies near the riverbank during our summer camping trip.
The larvae of the fish-fly are popular in freshwater fishing for catching trout and bass.
