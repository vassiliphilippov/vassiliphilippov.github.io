displume

verb
strip of honors, possessions, or attributes
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈplum / (AmE), / dɪsˈpluːm / (BrE)
to strip someone or something of honors, possessions, or distinguishing attributes, often in a figurative sense

Examples

To displume a leader
After the defeat, the general was displumed of his command and titles.
The scandal threatened to displume him of his reputation and dignity.
They sought to displume their rivals by exposing their secrets.

Related Terms
