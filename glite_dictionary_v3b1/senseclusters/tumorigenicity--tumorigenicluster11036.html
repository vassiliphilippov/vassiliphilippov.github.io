tumorigenicity

noun
ability to cause tumors
Pronunciation: / tuːˌmɔːrədʒəˈnɪsəti / (AmE), / tjuːˌmɒrɪdʒəˈnɪsəti / (BrE)
the ability of cells, substances, or agents to cause or promote the formation of tumors

Examples

Tumorigenicity of cells
Scientists are studying the tumorigenicity of stem cells before using them in clinical treatments.
The new drug was found to have low tumorigenicity in laboratory tests.
Researchers compared the tumorigenicity of different compounds in animal models.

Related Terms

  • antonym: benignity - the quality of not being harmful or malignant
  • synonym: carcinogenicity - the ability to cause cancer
