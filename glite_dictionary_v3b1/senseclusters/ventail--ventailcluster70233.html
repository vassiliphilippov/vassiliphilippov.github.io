ventail

noun
medieval mail hood protecting face and neck
Pronunciation: / vɛnˈteɪl / (AmE), / vɛnˈteɪl / (BrE)
a piece of medieval armor, usually of mail, attached to or suspended from a helmet to protect the lower face, neck, and throat

Examples

A ventail of mail
Historians studied the intricately woven mail of the ventail to understand medieval armor construction.
The ventail was essential for protecting a knight’s face during combat.
The knight lowered his ventail before entering the battlefield.

Related Terms

  • synonym: visor - a movable part of a helmet that protects the face
