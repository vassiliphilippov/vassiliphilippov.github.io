continued

adjective
lasting without interruption
Pronunciation: / kənˈtɪn.juːd / (AmE), / kənˈtɪn.juːd / (BrE)
existing or happening without interruption; lasting over a period of time.

Examples

The continued growth
Despite the difficulties, the company's continued growth astonished its competitors.
Her continued efforts eventually led to a major breakthrough in the project.
The continued rain caused severe flooding in several areas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: continuous - going on without stopping or being interrupted
  • antonym: interrupted - stopped and then started again
