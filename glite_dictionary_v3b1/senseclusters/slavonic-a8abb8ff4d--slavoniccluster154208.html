Slavonic

noun
branch of Indo-European languages
1.0 7.1
Pronunciation: / sləˈvɑnɪk / (AmE), / sləˈvɒnɪk / (BrE)
a branch of the Indo-European family of languages, including Russian, Polish, Czech, and others spoken in Eastern Europe and northern Asia

Examples

Slavonic languages
Linguists study the origins and development of Slavonic languages.
Russian and Polish are considered Slavonic languages spoken in Eastern Europe.
The Slavonic language family includes a variety of tongues such as Czech and Bulgarian.
