pre-emptor

noun
bridge player making a preemptive bid
7.8
Pronunciation: / priˈɛmptər / (AmE), / priˈɛmptə / (BrE)
A player in the card game bridge who makes a preemptive bid to disrupt the opponents' bidding.

Examples

Pre-emptor in bridge
As the pre-emptor, she opened with a weak three-bid in hearts to block her opponents.
Experienced players can often identify a pre-emptor by their aggressive early bids.
The pre-emptor hoped his bold bid would disrupt the opposing team's communication.
