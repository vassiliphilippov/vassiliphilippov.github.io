enthalpy

noun
thermodynamic heat content
Pronunciation: / ˈɛnˌθælpi / (AmE), / ˈɛnθəlpi / (BrE)
(in thermodynamics) a measure of the total heat content of a system, equal to its internal energy plus the product of its pressure and volume.

Examples

Change in enthalpy
During the chemical reaction, the change in enthalpy indicated whether the process absorbed or released heat.
Engineers often use enthalpy calculations to design more efficient heating systems.
The concept of enthalpy is fundamental when discussing energy transfer in thermodynamics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: internal energy - the total energy contained within a system
