exclude

verb
to leave out or not include
3.0 34/m
Pronunciation: / …™kÀàsklud /; / …™kÀàskluÀêd / (AmE), / …™kÀàskluÀêd / (BrE)
to deliberately not include or to keep someone or something out

Examples

To exclude someone
He felt excluded from the conversation by his colleagues.
The membership fee excludes taxes and other charges.
The policy excludes any pre-existing medical conditions.
Certain foods are excluded from his diet for health reasons.
She felt hurt when she was excluded from the invitation list.
The committee decided to exclude late submissions from the final review.
Because of his behavior, he was excluded from the meeting.
The policy does not exclude anyone based on their background.
The school decided to exclude students who repeatedly broke the rules.

Synonyms

  • except
    4.1 0.15/m
    except is used to leave someone out in a list or rule, while exclude means to deliberately keep someone or something out.
  • leave out
    2.3 10/m
    leave out means to not include someone or something in a group or text, while exclude implies a deliberate decision to keep them out.
  • omit
    3.4 11/m
    omit means to leave something out, sometimes by accident, while exclude implies an intentional choice to keep it out.
  • debar
    7.9 0.05/m
    debar is to officially prevent someone from joining or holding a post, while exclude is a general act of keeping someone out.
  • bar
    3.3 2.20/m
    bar is to officially forbid or block someone from an activity or place, while exclude is broader and can be less formal.
  • shut out
    3.8 3.89/m
    shut out is to stop someone from entering or being considered, while exclude is a general choice not to include them.
  • keep out
    1.3 6.90/m
    keep out is used to stop people physically entering a place, while exclude covers broader decisions not to include someone or something.

Related Terms

  • antonym: admit - to allow entrance or inclusion
  • synonym: bar - to prohibit or prevent entry or participation
  • antonym: include - to make someone or something part of a group or whole
  • synonym: omit - to leave out; fail to include
