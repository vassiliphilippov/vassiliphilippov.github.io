fermium

noun
a synthetic radioactive element, Fm
Pronunciation: / ˈfɜrmiəm / (AmE), / ˈfɜːmiəm / (BrE)
a synthetic, radioactive metallic element (symbol: Fm), produced by bombarding plutonium with neutrons; atomic number 100.

Examples

Fermium element
Because fermium is highly radioactive and scarce, it has no practical uses outside of basic scientific research.
Scientists first discovered fermium in the debris of a hydrogen bomb test in 1952.
The team used neutron bombardment on plutonium to synthesize fermium in the laboratory.
