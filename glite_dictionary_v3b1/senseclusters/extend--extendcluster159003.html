extend

verb
to reach outward in space
1.0 2.3
Pronunciation: /ɪk.ˈstend/ /; / /ek.ˈstend/ (AmE), /ek.ˈstend/ /; / /ɪk.ˈstend/ (BrE)
to stretch out or reach with your body or an object; to make something physically longer or straighter

Examples

To extend an arm
A long arm seemed to extend from the statue, reaching toward the sky.
The highway is planned to extend another ten miles to the east.
The roof will extend over the porch to provide extra shade.
He politely extended his hand for a handshake.
She extended her arm to help the child reach the shelf.
The cat extended its paw through the gap in the door.
He extended his arm to reach the remote control on the table.
She asked him to extend the ladder so she could reach the higher shelf.
The cat likes to extend its body and stretch each morning.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bend - to make something not straight; opposite of straightening
  • synonym: hold out - to offer or reach out, especially with the hand
  • synonym: project - to stick out from a surface
  • antonym: retract - to draw back or withdraw
  • antonym: shorten - to make something shorter in length
  • synonym: straighten - to make or become straight
  • synonym: stretch - to make something longer or wider without tearing or breaking
  • antonym: withdraw - to pull back or remove (something extended)
