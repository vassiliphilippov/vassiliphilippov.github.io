prestrain

noun
initial applied strain
8.9
Pronunciation: /priː.ˈstreɪn/ /; / /prɪ.ˈstreɪn/ (AmE), /priː.ˈstreɪn/ /; / /prɪ.ˈstreɪn/ (BrE)
a strain or deformation applied to a material before it is put into use or subjected to load

Examples

A prestrain in steel
The prestrain in the steel cables helps the bridge support heavy loads.
Engineers must calculate the correct prestrain to ensure the structure's safety.
Applying a controlled prestrain can improve the performance of certain building materials.

Related Terms
