scuffle

verb
to move noisily
Pronunciation: / ˈskʌfəl / (AmE), / ˈskʌfəl / (BrE)
to move with a shuffling or dragging motion, often making a noise

Examples

To scuffle down the hall
The children scuffled down the hallway, their shoes making soft noises on the floor.
He could hear someone scuffle across the hallway late at night.
As the cat tried to escape, it scuffled under the couch, leaving a trail of dust.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shuffle - to walk by dragging one's feet along the ground
  • antonym: tiptoe - to walk quietly and carefully on the toes
