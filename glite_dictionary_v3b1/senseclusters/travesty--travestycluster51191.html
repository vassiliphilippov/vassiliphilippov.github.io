travesty

noun
humorous imitation; distorted version
8.6 6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈtræv.ə.stiː/ /; / /ˈtræv.ɪ.stiː/ (AmE), /ˈtræv.ə.stiː/ /; / /ˈtræv.ɪ.stiː/ (BrE)
a distorted, absurd, or exaggerated imitation or parody of something, often for comic or satirical effect

Examples

A travesty of justice
His speech was considered a travesty of the scientific method.
That movie adaptation was a complete travesty of the original novel's spirit.
The comedian's travesty of the politician had the audience in stitches.
Many early nineteenth-century novels were adapted into stage travesties that exaggerated their melodramatic moments.
The film is a bold travesty of the detective genre, filled with outrageous situations and biting satire.
The playwright's new production is a travesty that lampoons the original tragedy with slapstick and absurd plot twists.

Related Terms

  • synonym: imitation - something that copies the appearance or character of something else
  • synonym: parody - a humorous or satirical imitation of a serious piece of literature or writing
