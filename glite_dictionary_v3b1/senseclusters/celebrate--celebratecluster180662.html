celebrate

verb
observe or honor a special occasion
1.0 1.3
Pronunciation: /ˈse.lə.breɪt/ (AmE), /ˈse.lə.breɪt/ /; / /ˈse.lɪ.breɪt/ (BrE)
to observe or mark a special day, event, or achievement with ceremonies, festivities, or enjoyable activities

Examples

To celebrate a birthday
Every year, our family gathers to celebrate Thanksgiving with a big dinner.
Many people celebrate Diwali by lighting lamps and sharing sweets.
She loves to celebrate her birthday with friends and music.
After winning the championship, the whole team celebrated together at a local restaurant.
People around the world celebrate New Year's Eve in different ways.
We celebrate our anniversary every year with a special dinner.
After finishing the project successfully, they decided to celebrate by going out for ice cream.
The entire city came together to celebrate the team's championship win.
We plan to celebrate our grandparents' anniversary with a family dinner.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to a day or occasion
  • synonym: observe - to mark or celebrate a special event, especially a holiday or anniversary
