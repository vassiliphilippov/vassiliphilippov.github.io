sulfa drug

noun
synthetic antibacterial drug (sulfonamide)
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌlfə drʌɡ / (AmE), / ˈsʌlfə drʌɡ / (BrE)
any of a group of synthetic antibacterial drugs containing sulfonamides, used to inhibit the growth of bacteria.

Examples

Sulfa drug treatment
Before the introduction of antibiotics like penicillin, sulfa drugs were widely used to fight bacterial diseases.
Some people may experience allergic reactions to sulfa drugs.
The doctor prescribed a sulfa drug to treat the infection.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: antibiotic - a substance used to kill or inhibit bacteria; broader than sulfa drug
  • synonym: sulfonamide - a class of compounds containing the sulfonamide group; the chemical group underlying sulfa drugs
