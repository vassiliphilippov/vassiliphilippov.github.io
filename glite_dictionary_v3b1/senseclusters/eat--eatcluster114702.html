eat

verb
to have a meal
1.0 309/m
Pronunciation: / it / (AmE), / iÀêt / (BrE)
to put food in your mouth, chew it, and swallow it; to consume food (by people or animals)

Examples

To eat breakfast
She didn't eat before the meeting, so she was hungry.
They invited us to eat with them after the ceremony.
We usually eat at six o'clock in the evening.
Most birds eat seeds, insects, or small creatures depending on their species.
Pandas primarily eat bamboo in the wild.
Sharks will eat smaller fish and marine animals to survive.
He forgot to eat breakfast this morning and felt hungry by noon.
The children eat lunch together every day at school.
Vegetarians do not eat meat, but they may eat dairy or eggs.

Synonyms

  • consume
    2.4 14/m
    consume is a formal word for eating food, while eat is the common everyday verb for taking food into your body.
  • have
    1.0 167/m
    have often refers to taking a meal or drink in casual speech, while eat names the general act of putting food into your mouth.
  • devour
    2.9 1.29/m
    devour means to eat quickly and eagerly with big appetite, while eat is neutral and does not show speed or hunger.
  • feed
    1.0 22/m
    feed often describes animals taking food, while eat is the general verb for people or animals consuming food.

Related Terms

  • synonym: devour - to eat something quickly and eagerly
  • synonym: dine - to eat dinner or the main meal of the day
  • antonym: fast - to abstain from eating food
  • synonym: feed - to give food to, especially animals
  • antonym: starve - to suffer or die from lack of food
