predestinate

adjective
determined or established in advance
7.7
Pronunciation: / priˈdɛstəˌneɪt / (AmE), / priˈdɛstɪnət / (BrE)
(of a thing or event) established or determined in advance and unable to be changed, often by fate or divine will.

Examples

Predestinate future
Her predestinate role as a leader was spoken of since her childhood.
The play suggests that our destinies may be predestinate and not shaped by free will.
There was a predestinate air to their encounter, as if fate itself arranged the meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contingent - subject to chance or not fixed; occurring only if certain circumstances are the case
  • synonym: foreordained - decided or established in advance, usually by destiny or divine decree
  • synonym: predestined - determined in advance, especially by fate or divine will
