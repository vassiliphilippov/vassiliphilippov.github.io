predestinate

adjective
determined or established in advance

(of a thing or event) established or determined in advance and unable to be changed, often by fate or divine will.

Examples

Predestinate future
Her predestinate role as a leader was spoken of since her childhood.
The play suggests that our destinies may be predestinate and not shaped by free will.
There was a predestinate air to their encounter, as if fate itself arranged the meeting.

Synonyms

  • foreordained
    9.9 0.06/m
    foreordained means fixed by fate or divine will and cannot be changed, while predestinate refers to something established in advance.
  • fated
    5.3 2.12/m
    fated describes an outcome seen as caused by fate, often said 'fated to', while predestinate stresses being fixed and unchangeable.
  • destined
    4.1 0.15/m
    destined often suggests a future plan or purpose and can sound positive, while predestinate implies prior determination and no chance to change.
  • predetermined
    5.3 0.00/m
    predetermined means decided or arranged beforehand, often by people or plans, while predestinate stresses fate or divine unchangeability.
  • foregone
    4.6 1.09/m
    foregone describes an outcome that is already certain or expected, while predestinate suggests a fate or divine decision set before events.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contingent - subject to chance or not fixed; occurring only if certain circumstances are the case
  • synonym: foreordained - decided or established in advance, usually by destiny or divine decree
  • synonym: predestined - determined in advance, especially by fate or divine will
