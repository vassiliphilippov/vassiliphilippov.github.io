Kurdish Hezbollah

proper noun
Kurdish Sunni extremist group in Turkey
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɜrdɪʃ ˌhɛzbəˈlɑ / (AmE), / ˈkɜːdɪʃ ˌhɛzbəˈlɑː / (BrE)
A militant Kurdish Sunni organization founded in southeastern Turkey in the late 1980s, seeking to establish an Islamic state governed by sharia law and known for violent activities, including bombings and assassinations.

Examples

Kurdish Hezbollah group
Kurdish Hezbollah was known to have received support from Iran during its campaign against secular authorities.
Journalists reporting on Kurdish Hezbollah faced significant risks due to the group's violent activities.
The Turkish government launched several operations against Kurdish Hezbollah in the early 2000s.
