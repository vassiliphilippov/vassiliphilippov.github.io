noddy

noun
small historic vehicle
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑdi / (AmE), / ˈnɒdi / (BrE)
a small, simple motor vehicle used in the past for short local journeys, especially in rural areas

Examples

Old noddy car
The farmer drove his old noddy down the lane to deliver fresh milk.
In the early 20th century, a noddy was a common sight in English villages.
They restored a vintage noddy for the local history museum's exhibition.
