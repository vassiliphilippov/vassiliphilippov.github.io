reseed

verb
to plant seed again
3.0 4.2
Pronunciation: / riˈsid /; / ˌriːˈsiːd / (AmE), / riːˈsiːd /; / ˌriːˈsiːd / (BrE)
to plant seeds in an area again, especially to restore or improve grass, crops, or other plants

Examples

To reseed a lawn
After the harsh winter, the field needed to be reseeded to restore the grass.
The company plans to reseed the park with native wildflowers next spring.
You should reseed the lawn every few years for a healthier appearance.
After the drought, the farmer decided to reseed the entire pasture to encourage new growth.
The city is reseeding the park lawns to repair damage from last summer's festival.
Gardeners often reseed bare patches in their lawns each spring for a fuller look.

Related Terms

  • antonym: harvest - to gather mature crops from the fields
  • synonym: plant - to put seeds or plants in the ground to grow
  • degree lower: seed - to plant seeds in (an area) for the first time
  • synonym: sow - to plant seeds in soil
