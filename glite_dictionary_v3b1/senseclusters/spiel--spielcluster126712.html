spiel

verb
talk at length, often persuasively or extravagantly
7.9 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / spiÀêl / (AmE), / spiÀêl / (BrE)
to talk at length or flamboyantly, typically to persuade or advertise; to deliver a lengthy or extravagant speech.

Examples

To spiel about products
He spieled for nearly an hour about the virtues of his new product.
She tends to spiel whenever she gets excited about her favorite topic.
The salesman spieled his pitch so quickly that I barely understood a word.
