redrive

noun
repeated attempt
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈriːˌdraɪv / (AmE), / ˈriːˌdraɪv / (BrE)
an act of driving, sending, or processing something again, especially after a previous attempt has failed

Examples

System redrive event
The system automatically initiates a redrive if the first data transfer fails.
After the error, a redrive of the process was scheduled for the next morning.
Engineers monitored the redrive to ensure all messages were delivered successfully.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attempt - an effort to achieve or complete something
  • synonym: retry - an attempt to do something again
  • antonym: success - the achievement of a desired result
