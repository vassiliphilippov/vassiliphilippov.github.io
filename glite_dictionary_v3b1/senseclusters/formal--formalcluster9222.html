formal

adjective
adhering to traditional language standards
1.0 2.6
Pronunciation: /ˈfɔːr.məl/ (AmE), /ˈfɔː.məl/ (BrE)
Used to describe language that follows established rules of grammar and usage, avoiding casual, contracted, or colloquial forms.

Examples

Formal written language
Her speech at the conference was remarkably formal, avoiding all contractions and slang.
The teacher insisted that all essays be written in formal English.
When writing a business letter, it is important to use formal language.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: colloquial - language that is characteristic of informal speech
  • antonym: informal - language that is casual or conversational
