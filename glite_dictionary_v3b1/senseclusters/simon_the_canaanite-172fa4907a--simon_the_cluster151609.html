Simon the Canaanite

proper noun
one of the twelve Apostles of Jesus
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪmən ðə kəˈneɪ.əˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈsaɪmən ðə kəˈneɪ.əˌnaɪt / (BrE)
one of the twelve Apostles of Jesus Christ, also known as Simon the Zealot, active in the first century.

Examples

Simon the Canaanite apostle
According to the Gospels, Simon the Canaanite was among Jesus' chosen twelve apostles.
In Christian art, Simon the Canaanite is often depicted with a saw, his traditional symbol.
Many scholars believe Simon the Canaanite is the same person as Simon the Zealot.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Apostle - one of the twelve chief disciples of Jesus Christ
  • antonym: Simon Peter - an Apostle of Jesus, also called Peter, not to be confused with Simon the Canaanite
