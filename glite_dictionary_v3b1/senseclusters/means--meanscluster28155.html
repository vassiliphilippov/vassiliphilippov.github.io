means

noun
method or resource for achieving something
2.7 79/m
Pronunciation: / minz / (AmE), / miÀênz / (BrE)
a method, instrument, or way of achieving something

Examples

A means to success
Education is often considered a means to personal and professional development.
The Internet has become a powerful means of communication worldwide.
They found an effective means to reduce energy consumption in the building.
Education is often considered a means to a better life.
For many, public transport is the only means of commuting to work.
The company found a new means of increasing productivity without hiring more staff.

Synonyms

  • instrumentality
    8.0 0.46/m
    instrumentality is a formal word for a tool or method people use to reach a goal, while means is the normal everyday word.
  • way
    1.0 1306/m
    way is a common word for the manner or method people use to do something, while means is slightly more formal.
  • instrument
    5.5 6.24/m
    instrument is a person or thing someone uses to reach a goal, while means is a broader word for any method or tool.
  • measure
    3.8 69/m
    measure is an action or rule people take to solve a problem, while means is any method or tool people use to achieve something.
