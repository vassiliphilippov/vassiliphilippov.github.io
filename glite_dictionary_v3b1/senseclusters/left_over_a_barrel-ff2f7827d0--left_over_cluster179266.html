left over a barrel

x
put in a difficult or powerless position
Pronunciation: / lɛft ˈoʊvər ə ˈbærəl / (AmE), / lɛft ˈəʊvə ə ˈbærəl / (BrE)
in a position where one is at a disadvantage or unable to act freely, typically dependent on someone else's decision or mercy

Examples

Left over a barrel by creditors
After missing the deadline, the company was left over a barrel by its creditors.
She was left over a barrel when her only supplier suddenly raised prices.
The negotiations stalled, and they felt completely left over a barrel without any leverage.
