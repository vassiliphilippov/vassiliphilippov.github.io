Reconstruction Period

noun
era after the American Civil War (1865–1877)
6.3
Pronunciation: /riː.kən.ˈstrʌk.ʃən ˈpɪr.ɪ.əd/ /; / /rɪ.kən.ˈstrʌk.ʃən ˈpɪr.ɪ.əd/ (AmE), /riː.kən.ˈstrʌk.ʃən ˈpɪə.rɪ.əd/ /; / /rɪ.kən.ˈstrʌk.ʃən ˈpɪə.rɪ.əd/ (BrE)
the period in United States history immediately following the Civil War (1865–1877), when the southern states were reorganized and reintegrated into the Union.

Examples

The Reconstruction Period in America
During the Reconstruction Period, new laws aimed to secure civil rights for formerly enslaved people.
Historians often debate the successes and failures of the Reconstruction Period.
The Reconstruction Period radically changed political and social structures in the southern United States.
