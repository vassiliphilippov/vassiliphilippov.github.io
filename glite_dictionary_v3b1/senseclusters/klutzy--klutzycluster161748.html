klutzy

adjective
awkward or clumsy
7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈklʌt.siː/ /; / /ˈklʌt.sɪ/ (AmE), /ˈklʌt.siː/ /; / /ˈklʌt.sɪ/ (BrE)
awkward, clumsy, or likely to have minor accidents or cause embarrassment

Examples

A klutzy person
He is so klutzy that he trips over his own feet almost every day.
The klutzy design of the chair made it uncomfortable to sit in for long periods.
She felt klutzy trying to dance at the wedding, but everyone encouraged her.
She felt klutzy after tripping over her own feet in front of everyone.
His klutzy attempts to carry the cake ended with it on the floor.
Despite being klutzy, he always made people laugh with his good-natured mistakes.
She felt klutzy after dropping her phone twice in one day.
His klutzy attempts at dancing made everyone laugh at the party.
I can be pretty klutzy when I'm tired and not paying attention.
