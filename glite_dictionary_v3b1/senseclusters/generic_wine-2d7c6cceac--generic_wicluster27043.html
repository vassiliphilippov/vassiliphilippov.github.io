generic wine

noun
wine not meeting designation standards
5.5 5.1
Pronunciation: /dʒə.ˈne.rɪk waɪn/ /; / /dʒɪ.ˈne.rɪk waɪn/ (AmE), /dʒə.ˈne.rɪk waɪn/ /; / /dʒɪ.ˈne.rɪk waɪn/ (BrE)
Wine that does not meet the legal standards for designation by appellation of origin or grape variety, and may only be labeled by a general name, color, or class.

Examples

Generic wine label
Many European countries have strict regulations for labeling, and a generic wine may only carry a basic description.
Some producers sell their surplus as generic wine under a simple color or class label.
The sommelier explained that generic wine is often less expensive than those with a protected designation of origin.

Related Terms

  • synonym: table wine - wine of ordinary quality, not sparkling or fortified
