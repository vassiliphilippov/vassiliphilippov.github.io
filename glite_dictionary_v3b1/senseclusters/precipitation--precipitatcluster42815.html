precipitation

noun
unexpected hastening of an event
8.7
Pronunciation: / prəˌsɪpɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / prɪˌsɪpɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of doing something too quickly or without enough thought; rashness or suddenness

Examples

Precipitation of action
Historians argue over the precipitation of revolution by the unpopular new policy.
The CEO's sudden resignation led to the precipitation of sweeping changes within the company.
Their disagreement resulted in the precipitation of a hasty decision.
She criticized the precipitation with which the announcement was made.
Sometimes, precipitation causes more harm than waiting for the right moment.
Their precipitation in signing the contract led to unexpected problems.
