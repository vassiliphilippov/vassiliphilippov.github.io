Wernicke's area

noun
region of the brain for language comprehension
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛrnɪkəz ˈɛriə / (AmE), / ˈvɜːnɪkəz ˈeəriə / (BrE)
A region of the brain in the posterior part of the superior temporal gyrus, responsible for language comprehension.

Examples

Wernicke's area in brain
Wernicke's area is typically found in the left hemisphere of the brain.
Damage to Wernicke's area can lead to difficulties in understanding spoken language.
Researchers studied brain activity in Wernicke's area during a language comprehension task.
