formant

noun
grammatical affix
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrmənt / (AmE), / ˈfɔːmənt / (BrE)
a morphological element, such as an affix, that indicates grammatical inflection or is used in word formation

Examples

Past tense formant
The -ed ending in 'walked' is a formant that shows the past tense.
In some languages, a vowel change acts as a formant to indicate plural forms.
Adding the prefix 'un-' as a formant can change the meaning of a word to its opposite.

Related Terms

  • synonym: affix - a prefix, suffix, or infix added to a word to change its meaning or function
  • antonym: root - the basic part of a word to which affixes are added
