nuclearization

noun
movement into nucleus
9.1
Pronunciation: /nuː.kli.ə.rə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /nuː.klɪ.ə.rə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /njuː.klɪ.ə.rə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /njuː.kli.ə.rə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process by which a molecule or protein moves into and accumulates within a cell's nucleus

Examples

Nuclearization of proteins
The nuclearization of the signaling protein was detected within minutes after stimulation.
Researchers studied the nuclearization of various transcription factors in response to stress.
Abnormal nuclearization can indicate changes in cellular function or disease states.

Related Terms

  • antonym: export - the process of moving a molecule out of the nucleus
  • synonym: translocation - the movement of a molecule from one part of a cell to another
