lockup

noun
temporary jail at a police station
5.5 0.87/m
Pronunciation: / Ààl…ëÀêkÀå åp / (AmE), / Ààl…ík. åp / (BrE)
a room or building in a police station where people are kept under temporary detention; a small jail

Examples

Police lockup
After the arrest, the suspect was held in the lockup overnight.
He spent a few hours in the lockup before being released on bail.
The local lockup can only hold a few prisoners at a time.
