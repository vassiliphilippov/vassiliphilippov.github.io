spectatorial

adjective
of a spectator
8.1
Pronunciation: /spek.tə.ˈtɑːr.i.əl/ /; / /spek.tə.ˈtɔːr.i.əl/ (AmE), /spek.tə.ˈtɔː.rɪ.əl/ (BrE)
relating to spectators or the act of observing rather than participating; intended for people who are watching

Examples

Spectatorial interest
She took a spectatorial interest in the debate, choosing not to join the discussion.
The event offered both participatory and spectatorial activities for attendees.
His involvement was purely spectatorial, as he watched the match from the stands.
The stadium was built with spectatorial comfort in mind, offering excellent views from every seat.
The director focused on the spectatorial aspects of the play to engage the audience visually.
Many sports have evolved to include more spectatorial elements to attract larger crowds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: audience - a group of people watching or listening to a performance or event
  • synonym: observational - relating to watching or observing
  • antonym: participatory - involving active participation
  • synonym: spectator - a person who watches an event or show
