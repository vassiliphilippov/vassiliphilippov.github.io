petit juror

noun
member of a trial jury
8.5 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / ËŒpÉ›ti ËˆdÊ’ÊŠrÉ™r / (AmE), / ËŒpeti ËˆdÊ’ÊŠÉ™rÉ™ / (BrE)
A member of a petit jury who serves on a trial and helps determine the verdict in a court case.

Examples

Petit juror in court
As a petit juror, she took her responsibility to the court very seriously.
Each petit juror listened carefully to the evidence before reaching a decision.
The judge instructed every petit juror on their duty to remain impartial during the trial.

Synonyms

  • petty juror
    7.9 0.00/m
    petty juror is an alternate form seen in some texts, while petit juror is the standard legal term for trial jurors.
  • juror
    3.7 21/m
    juror means any member of a jury in general, while petit juror refers specifically to a trial juror who helps decide a verdict.

Related Terms

  • antonym: grand juror - a member of a grand jury, which determines whether there is enough evidence for a trial
  • synonym: juror - a member of a jury
