rehash

noun
reused material with minor changes
Pronunciation: / riˈhæʃ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈhæʃ / (BrE)
something that consists of old material that has been reworked and presented as if new, often with only minor changes.

Examples

A tired rehash
Critics dismissed the movie as a rehash of last year's blockbuster.
The author's latest book is just a rehash of old ideas with little innovation.
Their presentation felt like a rehash of material we've already seen.

Related Terms

  • antonym: original - something created or produced for the first time rather than copied or reworked
  • synonym: remake - a new version of something, such as a film, based on an earlier work
