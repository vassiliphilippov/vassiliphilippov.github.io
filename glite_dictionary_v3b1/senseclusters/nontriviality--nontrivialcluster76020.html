nontriviality

noun
significance or complexity
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˌtrɪviˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌtrɪviˈælɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being significant, complex, or not simple; not trivial (especially in mathematics or logic)

Understanding the nontriviality of the problem is essential before attempting a solution.

  • antonym: simplicity - the quality of being easy to understand or not complicated
  • antonym: triviality - the quality of being simple, obvious, or unimportant
