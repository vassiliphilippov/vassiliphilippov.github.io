magnetic dipole

noun
object with two opposite magnetic poles
5.3
Pronunciation: / mæɡˈnɛtɪk ˈdaɪpoʊl / (AmE), / maɡˈnɛtɪk ˈdʌɪpəʊl / (BrE)
a system or object with two equal and opposite magnetic poles, creating a magnetic field similar to that of a bar magnet

Examples

A magnetic dipole system
A bar magnet is a simple example of a magnetic dipole in everyday life.
The Earth's magnetic field can be approximated by a magnetic dipole at its center.
The strength of a magnetic dipole depends on the distance between its poles and their magnitude.

Related Terms

  • synonym: electric dipole - pair of equal and opposite electric charges or magnetic poles
