drowsily

adverb
in a sleepy or tired manner
2.4 0.27/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆdraÊŠzÉªli / (AmE), / ËˆdraÊŠzÉªli / (BrE)
In a manner that shows a feeling of being sleepy or tired.

Examples

Answer drowsily
He answered the phone drowsily, barely awake after a late night.
She smiled drowsily as she lay back on the pillow.
The cat blinked drowsily in the sun, uninterested in moving.

Synonyms

  • sleepily
    1.0 1.03/m
    sleepily shows being tired and ready to fall asleep in everyday talk, while drowsily describes a general sleepy manner.
  • dozily
    6.2 0.01/m
    dozily suggests nodding off and being inattentive or slow, while drowsily simply means feeling sleepy or tired.
  • somnolently
    8.1 0.01/m
    somnolently sounds formal and literary and suggests being nearly asleep, while drowsily is a neutral word used in speech.
