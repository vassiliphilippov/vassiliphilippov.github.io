hypostasis

noun
underlying reality or essence (philosophy)
Pronunciation: / haɪˈpɑstəsɪs / (AmE), / hɪˈpɒstəsɪs / (BrE)
(in philosophy) the underlying reality or substance, especially as opposed to attributes or that which is apparent.

Examples

Philosophical hypostasis
Classical metaphysics seeks to define the hypostasis underlying all physical phenomena.
In certain doctrines, the soul is considered the hypostasis of the self.
The philosopher spoke of the hypostasis that exists beyond mere appearances.
