sidetrack

verb
to distract or divert from the main course
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪdˌtræk / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdˌtræk / (BrE)
to cause someone to turn away from the main subject or course; to distract or divert from the intended path or focus

Examples

To sidetrack a meeting
Please do not sidetrack the discussion with unrelated topics.
She tends to sidetrack easily when working on group projects.
The speaker was sidetracked by questions from the audience, losing her train of thought.

Related Terms

  • synonym: distract - to turn one's attention away from something
  • antonym: focus - to pay particular attention to something
