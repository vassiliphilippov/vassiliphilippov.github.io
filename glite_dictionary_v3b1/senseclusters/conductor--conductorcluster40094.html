conductor

noun
material allowing electric or heat flow
1.0
Pronunciation: / kənˈdʌktər /; / kənˈdʌktɚ / (AmE), / kənˈdʌktə / (BrE)
a substance or material that allows electricity, heat, or sound to pass through it easily

Examples

Metal conductor
Copper is often used as a conductor of electricity in wiring.
Rubber is not a good conductor of heat.
Silver is the best conductor of electricity among all metals.
Copper is a very good conductor of electricity, making it ideal for use in wires.
Glass is not a conductor of heat, which is why it is often used as insulation.
The experiment required a conductor to demonstrate the flow of electric current.

Related Terms

  • antonym: insulator - a material through which electricity or heat does not easily flow
