whisper

noun
very quiet way of speaking
3.5 1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪspɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɪspə / (BrE)
a very quiet way of speaking in which only the breath is used, without vibration of the vocal cords

Examples

A quiet whisper
I heard a faint whisper behind the curtain, but I couldn't make out the words.
She delivered the message in a hurried whisper, afraid someone might overhear.
The children spoke in a whisper so they wouldn't wake the baby.

Related Terms

  • synonym: murmur - a low, continuous, but indistinct sound
  • antonym: shout - a loud call or cry
