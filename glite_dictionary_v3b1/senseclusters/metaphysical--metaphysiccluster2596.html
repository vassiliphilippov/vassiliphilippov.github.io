metaphysical

adjective
relating to metaphysics or philosophical theory
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˈfɪzɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈfɪzɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the branch of philosophy that studies the fundamental nature of reality and existence, or to things that are beyond what can be seen or experienced physically

Examples

Metaphysical questions
She has a keen interest in metaphysical questions about time and existence.
Some critics argue that poetry can address metaphysical ideas more effectively than prose.
The novel explores metaphysical themes through its dreamlike narrative.
She was fascinated by metaphysical questions about the nature of existence and consciousness.
Some philosophers debate the existence of metaphysical forces that lie beyond scientific explanation.
The story explores metaphysical realms where the boundaries of reality and imagination blur.

Related Terms

  • synonym: philosophical - relating to the study or knowledge of fundamental nature of reality
  • antonym: physical - relating to material nature or the body
  • synonym: supernatural - existing or occurring outside the laws of nature
