hitch

verb
to hook or entangle
4.4 2.9
Pronunciation: /hɪtʃ/ (AmE), /hɪtʃ/ (BrE)
to hook, catch, or entangle something, often so that it becomes temporarily secured or held

Examples

To hitch on a branch
One of the tent ropes hitched around a tree root, making it hard to move.
She managed to hitch her bag on a protruding nail and tore the strap.
The loose branch hitched itself onto my coat as I passed by.

Related Terms

  • synonym: snag - to catch or tear something accidentally on a sharp projection
