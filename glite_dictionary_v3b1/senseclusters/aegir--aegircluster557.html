aegir

noun
dangerous tidal wave caused by tides
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàiÀêd í…ôr / (AmE), / ÀàiÀêd í…ô / (BrE)
a high, often dangerous tidal wave caused by the flow of tides, especially in narrow estuaries or where tidal currents collide

Examples

A aegir wave
Fishermen watched as the massive aegir surged up the estuary, flooding the banks.
The river was unusually dangerous that day because of an incoming aegir.
Warnings were issued to avoid boating due to the risk of a sudden aegir.

Synonyms

    8.9 0.03/m

  • eagre
