telepathically

adverb
by mental communication
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛləˈpæθɪkli / (AmE), / ˌtɛlɪˈpæθɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that involves communicating or understanding directly from one mind to another, without using speech or physical signals (as if by telepathy)

Examples

To telepathically communicate
The two friends seemed to understand each other telepathically without saying a word.
Some people believe animals can communicate telepathically with their owners.
In the science fiction novel, the characters exchanged messages telepathically across great distances.
The twins seemed to communicate telepathically, always knowing what the other was thinking.
Without exchanging a glance, the team acted telepathically to win the match.
She answered his question telepathically, as if she had read his mind.
