pretended

adjective
not genuine; intended to deceive
2.1
Pronunciation: / prɪˈtɛndɪd / (AmE), / prɪˈtɛndɪd / (BrE)
Not genuine; made to appear real in order to deceive.

Examples

Pretended enthusiasm
He greeted us with a pretended enthusiasm that did not seem sincere.
Her pretended innocence fooled no one in the room.
The children listened with pretended interest while the teacher explained the assignment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: genuine - real; truly what something is said to be
  • synonym: sham - not genuine; fake or false
