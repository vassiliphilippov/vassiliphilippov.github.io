penicillin V

noun
acid-resistant form of penicillin antibiotic
3.2
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛnɪˈsɪlən ˈvi / (AmE), / ˌpɛnɪˈsɪlɪn ˈviː / (BrE)
a crystalline antibiotic similar to penicillin G, but more resistant to gastric acids and commonly used to treat bacterial infections

Examples

Penicillin V antibiotic
Patients are often given penicillin V to treat mild skin and respiratory infections.
The doctor prescribed penicillin V for her throat infection because it can be taken orally.
Unlike some other penicillins, penicillin V is stable in stomach acid.
