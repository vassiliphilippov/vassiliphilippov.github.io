leapfrog

noun
significant advance by surpassing obstacles
Pronunciation: / ˈliːpˌfrɑg / (AmE), / ˈliːpˌfrɒg / (BrE)
an advance or improvement that surpasses obstacles or competitors, especially by innovative or unexpected means

Examples

A business leapfrog
Small startups can sometimes make a leapfrog by adopting disruptive business models.
The company's investment resulted in a leapfrog in market share and brand recognition.
The new technology was seen as a leapfrog over all previous designs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: breakthrough - a sudden important advance or discovery
  • antonym: setback - an obstacle or reversal in progress
