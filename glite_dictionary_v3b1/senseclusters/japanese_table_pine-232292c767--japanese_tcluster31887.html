Japanese table pine

noun
pine tree species from Japan and Korea
5.6
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈpæniz ˈteɪbəl paɪn / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæniːz ˈteɪbəl paɪn / (BrE)
A species of pine tree native to Japan and Korea, known for its wide-spreading, irregular crown and commonly grown as an ornamental plant.

Examples

A Japanese table pine tree
A mature Japanese table pine can develop a broad, irregular crown unlike most other pines.
Botanists admire the Japanese table pine for its unique ornamental qualities.
The Japanese table pine is often planted in gardens for its striking shape.
