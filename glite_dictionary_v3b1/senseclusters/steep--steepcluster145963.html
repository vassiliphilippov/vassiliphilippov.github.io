steep

verb
devote oneself fully to something
5.8 2.57/m
Pronunciation: / stiÀêp / (AmE), / stiÀêp / (BrE)
to devote oneself completely or be deeply involved in or absorbed by something

Examples

To steep in music
After retiring, she steeped herself in the world of classical music.
During the summer, I steeped myself in learning new languages.
He steeped himself in historical research for years before publishing his book.

Synonyms

  • immerse
    4.8 6.14/m
    immerse means to put yourself fully into an activity to learn or feel it deeply, while steep means to devote oneself completely.
  • engross
    5.8 1.80/m
    engross means to take all your attention in a task or story, while steep means to be deeply involved or absorbed.
  • absorb
    3.1 10/m
    absorb means to feel fully occupied by an idea or task over time, while steep means to devote oneself completely.
