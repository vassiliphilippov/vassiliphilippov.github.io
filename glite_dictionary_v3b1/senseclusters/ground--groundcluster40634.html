ground

noun
reason or justification for something
Pronunciation: / ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
A reason or justification for something, especially used as the basis for an action or belief.

Examples

Grounds for appeal
She appealed on the grounds of unfair dismissal.
The lawyer argued that there was no valid ground for the lawsuit.
They called for a retrial on the ground that new evidence had emerged.

Related Terms

  • synonym: justification - a good reason for something that exists or has been done
  • synonym: reason - a cause or explanation for an action or event
