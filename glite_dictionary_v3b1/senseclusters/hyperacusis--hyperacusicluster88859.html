hyperacusis

noun
abnormal sensitivity to sound
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərəˈkusɪs / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpərəˈkjuːsɪs / (BrE)
a medical condition in which normal levels of sound are perceived as excessively loud or intolerable

Examples

Hyperacusis sound sensitivity
Hyperacusis can significantly affect a person's quality of life, making social situations difficult.
People with hyperacusis may find everyday noises uncomfortably loud or even painful.
The patient was diagnosed with hyperacusis after complaining about intolerance to normal sounds.
