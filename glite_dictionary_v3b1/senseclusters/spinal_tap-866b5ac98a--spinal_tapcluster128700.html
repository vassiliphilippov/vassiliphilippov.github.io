spinal tap

noun
procedure to remove spinal fluid
Pronunciation: / ˈspaɪ.nəl tæp /; / ˈspaɪnəl tæp / (AmE), / ˈspaɪ.nəl tæp /; / ˈspaɪnəl tap / (BrE)
a medical procedure in which a needle is inserted into the lower back to remove fluid from the spinal canal, usually for diagnosis or treatment

Examples

A spinal tap procedure
A spinal tap can help diagnose infections, bleeding, or diseases affecting the brain and spinal cord.
After the spinal tap, she was advised to rest and drink plenty of fluids to prevent headaches.
The doctor performed a spinal tap to collect a sample of cerebrospinal fluid for testing.
A spinal tap is often used to diagnose conditions like meningitis or multiple sclerosis.
After the spinal tap, the patient was advised to rest for several hours to avoid headaches.
The doctor ordered a spinal tap to check for signs of infection in her nervous system.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lumbar puncture - medical procedure to withdraw cerebrospinal fluid from the lower back
