compound eye

noun
eye with many visual units in insects/crustaceans
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmˌpaʊnd aɪ / (AmE), / ˈkɒmpaʊnd aɪ / (BrE)
an eye, found in insects and some crustaceans, made up of many small visual units, each contributing to the total image

Examples

Insect compound eye
Dragonflies rely on their compound eyes to spot prey moving in different directions.
The design of the robot’s camera was inspired by the structure of a bee’s compound eye.
Under the microscope, the fly’s compound eye revealed thousands of tiny lenses packed closely together.

Related Terms

  • antonym: simple eye - an eye with a single lens, as in humans or spiders
