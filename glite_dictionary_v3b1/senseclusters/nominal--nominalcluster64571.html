nominal

adjective
unadjusted for inflation (economics)
2.7
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑːmɪnəl / (AmE), / ˈnɒmɪnəl / (BrE)
(of a value or amount) stated without adjustment for changes in price or inflation; not accounting for the effect of inflation.

Examples

Nominal value
Banks often advertise nominal interest rates, which do not reflect the effects of inflation.
The country's nominal GDP grew by 5% last year, but real growth was lower due to inflation.
When comparing salaries over time, it's important to distinguish between nominal and real values.

Related Terms

  • antonym: real - adjusted for inflation; expressing value in constant prices
