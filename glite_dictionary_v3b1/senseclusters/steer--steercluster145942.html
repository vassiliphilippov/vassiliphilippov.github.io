steer

verb
guide or direct one's course
1.6 15/m
Pronunciation: / stÉªr / (AmE), / stÉªÉ™ / (BrE)
to guide or direct the course of something or someone, either literally (like a vehicle) or figuratively (like a conversation or career)

Examples

To steer a car
After the accident, he learned to steer himself away from risky behaviors.
She tried to steer clear of any controversial topics during the meeting.
Tourists are advised to steer clear of the dangerous area at night.
Her mentor helped steer her toward a successful scientific career.
Parents often try to steer their children away from bad influences.
The organization aims to steer public opinion in favor of environmental protection.
She tried to steer the conversation away from politics.
The captain managed to steer the ship safely through the storm.
You need to steer the car carefully around the sharp bend.

Synonyms

  • guide
    2.7 27/m
    guide is to influence or show a person or thing toward a goal, while steer means actively set its direction or course.
  • direct
    3.7 38/m
    direct is to control or manage people's actions or projects, while steer is to guide a course more gently or gradually.
  • navigate
    3.8 2.26/m
    navigate is to plan and control a vehicle's route over distance, while steer is to change or keep its immediate direction.
  • maneuver
    5.8 4.97/m
    maneuver is to move or act skillfully to gain an advantage, while steer is to guide the general course or direction.

Related Terms

  • antonym: follow - to go in the same direction as someone else
  • synonym: guide - to show or lead the way
  • synonym: lead - to cause a person or group to go with one by holding them by the hand, a rope, etc.; to guide
  • synonym: navigate - to plan and direct the course
