tachy response

noun
rapid heartbeat response (medical)
Pronunciation: / ˈtæki rɪˈspɑns / (AmE), / ˈtæki rɪˈspɒns / (BrE)
an abnormally rapid heartbeat response, typically used in medical contexts

Examples

A tachy response
During the stress test, the patient exhibited a tachy response.
Researchers studied how caffeine can trigger a tachy response in some individuals.
The nurse checked for a tachy response after administering the medication.
