crashing

adjective
intensifying adjective, esp. in negative phrases
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkræʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkræʃɪŋ / (BrE)
(informal) Used to emphasize or intensify the bad or notable quality of something or someone, especially in negative contexts.

Examples

A crashing nuisance
Everyone agreed it was a crashing nuisance to have to start over.
He's a crashing bore who talks about nothing but himself.
What a crashing disaster that meeting turned out to be!

  • synonym: flaming - used informally to intensify the negative quality of something in British English
