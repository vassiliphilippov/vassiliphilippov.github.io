VLSI

noun
integrated circuit design
9.9
Pronunciation: / viː ɛl ɛs aɪ / (AmE), / viː ɛl ɛs aɪ / (BrE)
the field or process of designing and creating very-large-scale integrated circuits with thousands or millions of components on a single chip

Examples

VLSI engineering
Many modern smartphones rely on VLSI technology to fit powerful processors into small devices.
He took a course in VLSI to understand how complex chips are designed and manufactured.
Advances in VLSI have made it possible to create faster and more efficient computers.
Advances in VLSI have made modern computers faster and more efficient.
Engineers use VLSI techniques to design complex microprocessors for smartphones.
The development of VLSI was a major milestone in the history of electronics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: integrated circuit - an electronic circuit formed on a small piece of semiconductor material
  • synonym: microchip - a small semiconductor used to relay information in electronic devices
  • synonym: microelectronics - the study and manufacture of very small electronic designs and components
  • synonym: semiconductor - a material or device used in electronic circuits
