Kirchhoff's laws

noun
laws of currents and voltages in electric circuits
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪrk.hɑfs lɔz / (AmE), / ˈkɪək.hɒfs lɔːz / (BrE)
two fundamental laws in electricity governing electric circuits: one stating that the sum of currents entering a junction equals the sum leaving it, and the other that the sum of voltage changes around a closed loop is zero

Examples

Kirchhoff's laws in circuits
Electrical engineers often rely on Kirchhoff's laws to analyze complex networks.
The physics exam required us to apply Kirchhoff's laws to solve circuit problems.
Using Kirchhoff's laws, we can determine the current through each resistor in the circuit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Ohm's law - the relationship between voltage, current, and resistance in an electric circuit
