midseason

noun
middle of a season
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪdˌsiːzən / (AmE), / ˈmɪdˌsiːzən / (BrE)
the middle part of a season, especially in sports or television series

Examples

Midseason break
The coach decided to change the lineup at midseason to improve the team's performance.
Many television shows introduce new characters during midseason to keep the story interesting.
Injuries often force teams to make adjustments around midseason.

Related Terms

  • antonym: offseason - the period when a sport or activity is not in its main season
  • antonym: preseason - the period before the official start of a season
