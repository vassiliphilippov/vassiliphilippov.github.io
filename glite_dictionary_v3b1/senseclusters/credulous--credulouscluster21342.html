credulous

adjective
easily fooled; gullible
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɛdʒʊləs /; / ˈkrɛdʒələs / (AmE), / ˈkrɛd.jʊ.ləs / (BrE)
too ready to believe things, especially without enough evidence, and therefore easily tricked or deceived

Examples

A credulous person
Children are often credulous and believe whatever adults tell them.
Only the most credulous tourists would fall for such an obvious scam.
She gave a credulous look as she listened to the wild story.
Children can be especially credulous, often believing stories without question.
Despite repeated warnings, he remained credulous and fell for every scam.
The journalist was not credulous enough to accept the politician's claims without verification.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gullible - easily persuaded to believe something; easily deceived
  • antonym: skeptical - not easily convinced; having doubts or reservations
