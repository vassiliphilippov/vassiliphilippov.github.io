bicameralism

noun
two-chamber legislature
Pronunciation: / baɪˈkæmərəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / baɪˈkæmərəlɪzəm / (BrE)
a system of government in which the legislature is divided into two separate chambers or houses

Examples

Bicameralism in parliament
Many countries, such as the United States, use bicameralism to balance representation between regions.
Debates about bicameralism often focus on the efficiency and fairness of having two legislative houses.
The adoption of bicameralism was intended to prevent any one group from gaining too much power.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unicameralism - a system of government with a single legislative chamber
