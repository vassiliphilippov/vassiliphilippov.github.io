neutrophile

noun
a type of white blood cell
Pronunciation: / ˈnuː.trəˌfaɪl / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.trəˌfaɪl / (BrE)
a type of white blood cell that destroys bacteria by phagocytosis and stains with both acidic and basic dyes

Examples

Neutrophiles destroy bacteria
Neutrophiles play a vital role in the body's defense against bacterial infections.
A blood test showed an increased number of neutrophiles after the patient developed a fever.
Doctors study the activity of neutrophile cells when investigating immune system disorders.

Related Terms

  • synonym: neutrophil - another spelling of neutrophile, commonly used in modern medical texts
