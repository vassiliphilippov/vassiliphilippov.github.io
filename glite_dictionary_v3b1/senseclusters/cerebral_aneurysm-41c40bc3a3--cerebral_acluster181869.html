cerebral aneurysm

noun
bulging of a brain artery wall
5.9 5.4
Pronunciation: /ˈse.rə.brəl ˈæn.jʊ.rɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈse.rə.brəl ˈæn.jə.rɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈse.rə.brəl ˈæn.jʊ.rɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈse.rə.brəl ˈæn.jʊə.rɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
A bulging, weakened area in the wall of an artery in the brain, which can rupture and cause a hemorrhagic stroke.

Examples

A cerebral aneurysm rupture
Advances in surgery have improved the prognosis for people with a cerebral aneurysm.
If left untreated, a cerebral aneurysm can rupture and cause a life-threatening brain hemorrhage.
The doctor diagnosed a cerebral aneurysm after the patient experienced a sudden severe headache.
