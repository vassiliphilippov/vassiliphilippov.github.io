involucrum

noun
new bone around dead bone
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnvəˈluːkrəm / (AmE), / ˌɪnvəˈluːkrəm / (BrE)
a layer of new bone that forms around a piece of dead bone, typically seen in bone infections

Examples

Bone infection involucrum
The X-ray showed an involucrum developing around the area of bone infection.
Doctors identified the presence of an involucrum as a sign of chronic osteomyelitis.
Treatment focused on removing the sequestrum and allowing the involucrum to heal properly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sequestrum - a piece of dead bone separated from living bone in an infection
