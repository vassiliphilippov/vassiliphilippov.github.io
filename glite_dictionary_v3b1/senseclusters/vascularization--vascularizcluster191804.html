vascularization

noun
development of blood vessels in tissue
Pronunciation: / ˌvæskjələrɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌvæskjʊləraɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌvæskjʊləraɪˈzeɪʃn / (BrE)
the process by which body tissue develops blood vessels and capillaries

Examples

Rapid vascularization
Rapid vascularization is crucial for proper wound healing in skin graft procedures.
Researchers are studying how to promote vascularization in engineered tissues for transplantation.
The surgeon encouraged vascularization of the transplant by using specialized techniques.
Delayed vascularisation can lead to poor wound healing.
Research focuses on improving vascularisation in engineered tissues for transplantation.
The tumor showed increased vascularisation after several weeks of treatment.
