Xanthophyceae

noun
a class of yellow-green algae
9.9
Pronunciation: / zænˌθɑːfəˈsiː.iː /; / zænˌθɑfəˈsiːiː / (AmE), / zanˌθɒfɪˈsiː.iː /; / zanˌθɒfɪˈsiːiː / (BrE)
a class of yellow-green algae mainly found in freshwater, distinguished by their pigment composition and cell structure

Examples

Xanthophyceae yellow-green algae
Members of the Xanthophyceae are characterized by the yellow-green color of their chloroplasts.
Scientists often study Xanthophyceae to understand pigment diversity in aquatic environments.
Unlike other algae, Xanthophyceae have distinctive cell wall compositions.
Members of the Xanthophyceae are often significant in the plankton communities of freshwater habitats.
Some researchers study the unique pigments produced by Xanthophyceae to understand their adaptation to light conditions.
The cell walls of most Xanthophyceae are composed primarily of cellulose and occasionally silica.
