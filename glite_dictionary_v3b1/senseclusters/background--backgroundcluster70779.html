background

noun
extraneous signals or noise interfering with observation
1.0 4.6
Pronunciation: /ˈbæk.graʊnd/ /; / /ˈbæk.raʊnd/ (AmE), /ˈbæk.graʊnd/ /; / /ˈbæk.raʊnd/ (BrE)
(in science and technology) Extraneous signals, noise, or data that may interfere with or obscure the phenomenon being observed or measured.

Examples

Background noise
Astronomers filter out background signals to focus on distant celestial objects.
Researchers must account for background noise when analyzing the data from the experiment.
The detector picked up too much background for the readings to be reliable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interference - signals that disrupt the reception of a desired signal
  • synonym: noise - random or unwanted signals that obscure a desired signal
