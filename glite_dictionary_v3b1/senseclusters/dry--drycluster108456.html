dry

adjective
abstaining from alcohol
Pronunciation: / draɪ / (AmE), / draɪ / (BrE)
not drinking or involving alcohol; supporting prohibition of alcohol

Examples

Dry for a year
He has been dry for over five years after struggling with addiction.
Many members of the club choose to stay dry at social gatherings.
She decided to go dry for a month as part of a health challenge.
After the election, the town stayed dry despite some opposition from younger residents.
Several southern counties remain dry and do not permit the sale of beer or wine.
The law establishing a dry zone was enacted in 1920 and is still in effect today.

Related Terms

  • synonym: teetotal - practicing complete abstinence from alcohol
  • antonym: wet - allowing the sale of alcoholic beverages
