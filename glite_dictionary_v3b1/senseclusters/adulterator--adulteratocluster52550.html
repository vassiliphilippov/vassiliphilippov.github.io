adulterator

noun
person or thing that adulterates
Pronunciation: / əˈdʌltəreɪtər / (AmE), / əˈdʌltəreɪtə / (BrE)
a person or thing that reduces the purity or effectiveness of a substance by adding inferior or prohibited ingredients

Examples

A food adulterator
As an adulterator of medicines, he harmed many unsuspecting people.
Strict laws punish any adulterator who contaminates food products.
The health inspector caught the adulterator mixing chemicals into the olive oil.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adulterate - to make something poorer in quality by adding another substance
  • antonym: purifier - person or thing that removes impurities
