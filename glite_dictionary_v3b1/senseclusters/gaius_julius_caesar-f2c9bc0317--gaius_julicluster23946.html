Gaius Julius Caesar

proper noun
Roman general and dictator (100–44 BC)
Pronunciation: / ˈɡaɪ.əs ˈdʒuː.li.əs ˈsiː.zɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡaɪ.əs ˈdʒuː.li.əs ˈsiː.zə / (BrE)
(100–44 BC) Roman general, statesman, and dictator who played a critical role in the transformation of the Roman Republic into the Roman Empire.
