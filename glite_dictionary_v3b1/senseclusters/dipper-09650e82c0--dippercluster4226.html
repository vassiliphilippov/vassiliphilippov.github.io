Dipper

noun
member of a specific Baptist denomination
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪpər / (AmE), / ˈdɪpə / (BrE)
a member of a Baptist denomination founded in 1708 by Americans of German descent, known for opposing military service and legal oaths and practicing trine immersion

Examples

Dipper baptism
Many historical records describe how the Dippers practiced trine immersion during baptism.
Members of the Dippers refused military service on religious grounds.
The Dippers were known for their strict interpretation of nonviolence and refusal to take legal oaths.
