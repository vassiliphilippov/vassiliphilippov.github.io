dip

verb
decrease briefly or slightly
4.6 2.5
Pronunciation: / dɪp / (AmE), / dɪp / (BrE)
(of a value or level) to decrease slightly and usually for a short time.

Examples

Sales dipped slightly
After the strong performance last quarter, sales dipped slightly in April.
Oil prices dipped to their lowest point of the week yesterday.
The temperature dips just before sunrise every morning.
