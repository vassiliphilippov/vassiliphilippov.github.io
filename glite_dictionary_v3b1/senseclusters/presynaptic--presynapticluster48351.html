presynaptic

adjective
before synapse
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːsɪˈnæptɪk / (AmE), / ˌpriːsɪˈnæptɪk / (BrE)
relating to the transmitting side of a nerve cell at a synapse, before the synaptic gap

Examples

The presynaptic neuron
The presynaptic neuron releases chemicals that carry signals across the synapse.
Damage to the presynaptic membrane can affect how nerves communicate.
Certain drugs can alter presynaptic activity and influence brain function.

Related Terms

  • antonym: postsynaptic - relating to the receiving side of a synapse
