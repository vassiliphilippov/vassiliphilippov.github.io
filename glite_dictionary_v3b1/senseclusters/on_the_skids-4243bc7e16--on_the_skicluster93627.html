on the skids

adjective
in decline or serious difficulty
Pronunciation: / ɑn ðə skɪdz / (AmE), / ɒn ðə skɪdz / (BrE)
(of a person or organization) in a state of decline or experiencing serious difficulties, especially financially or professionally.

Examples

On the skids financially
After the scandal, his career was clearly on the skids.
Many feared the once-great restaurant was on the skids after losing its chef.
The company has been on the skids for several years due to poor management.

Related Terms

  • synonym: in trouble - experiencing difficulties or problems
  • antonym: prosperous - successful and flourishing
