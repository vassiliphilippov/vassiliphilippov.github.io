sloughing

noun
the shedding of a layer, e.g. skin or tissue
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈslʌfɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈslʌfɪŋ / (BrE)
the process by which a layer or covering, such as dead tissue or skin, is naturally shed or cast off.

Examples

Sloughing of skin
After a severe sunburn, sloughing of the damaged skin can occur over several days.
Proper wound care can help speed up the sloughing of necrotic tissue.
The wound showed signs of sloughing, which indicated dead tissue was being removed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shedding - the action of letting something fall away or be lost
