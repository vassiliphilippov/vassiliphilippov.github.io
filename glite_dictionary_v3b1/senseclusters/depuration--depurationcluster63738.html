depuration

noun
purification process
Pronunciation: / ˌdɛpjʊˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɛpjʊˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of cleansing or purifying something to remove impurities or contaminants (for example, making food like shellfish safe to eat)

Examples

Depuration of shellfish
The depuration of shellfish is necessary to ensure they are safe for consumption.
Modern water treatment plants use advanced depuration techniques to provide clean drinking water.
After the oil spill, a long period of depuration was required to restore the river's ecosystem.
Before being sold, the clams undergo depuration to ensure they are safe for consumption.
Strict regulations require depuration of oysters harvested from polluted waters.
The seafood company invested in new tanks to improve the depuration process for their shellfish.
