Fabaceae

noun
the legume or pea plant family
8.9
Pronunciation: /fə.ˈbeɪ.siː/ /; / /fæ.ˈbeɪ.siː/ /; / /fə.ˈbeɪ.sɪ/ (AmE), /fə.ˈbeɪ.siː/ /; / /fæ.ˈbeɪ.siː/ /; / /fə.ˈbeɪ.sɪ/ (BrE)
a large family of flowering plants, including trees, shrubs, vines, and herbs, that produce bean pods; commonly known as the legume, pea, or bean family

Examples

Fabaceae plant family
Botanists have classified thousands of species within the Fabaceae.
Many important food crops, such as soybeans and lentils, belong to the Fabaceae family.
The Fabaceae are easily recognized by their characteristic seed pods.
Acacia trees, which belong to the Fabaceae, thrive in warm climates around the world.
Botanists have classified many nitrogen-fixing plants under the Fabaceae family.
The Fabaceae family includes important crops such as peas, beans, and lentils.
