countervailing

adjective
balancing or offsetting
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˌkaʊntərˈveɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntəˈveɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
acting with equal force to balance or offset the effect of something else

Examples

Countervailing measures
The government introduced countervailing measures to reduce the negative effects of inflation.
There are countervailing arguments that challenge the main points of the proposal.
Strong countervailing forces prevented the company from gaining a monopoly in the market.

Related Terms

  • synonym: balancing - keeping things equal or steady
  • antonym: reinforcing - making something stronger or more intense
