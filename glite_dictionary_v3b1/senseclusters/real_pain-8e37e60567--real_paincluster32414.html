real pain

noun
something or someone causing annoyance or discomfort
Pronunciation: / ɹiəl peɪn / (AmE), / ɹɪəl peɪn / (BrE)
A person or thing that causes significant discomfort or annoyance; an inconvenience.

Examples

A real pain to carry
Carrying all these bags is a real pain when you have to take the stairs.
My little brother can be a real pain when I'm trying to concentrate.
The traffic in the city center can be a real pain during rush hour.
