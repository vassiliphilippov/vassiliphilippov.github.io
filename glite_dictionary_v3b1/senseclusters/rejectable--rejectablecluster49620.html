rejectable

adjective
able to be rejected
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdʒɛktəbl̩ / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒɛktəbl̩ / (BrE)
capable of being attacked and expelled by a recipient's immune system, especially referring to a transplant or graft

Examples

Rejectable organ
The doctor explained that the tissue was highly rejectable without proper immunosuppressive drugs.
A rejectable organ transplant may fail if the immune response is not controlled.
Scientists are working to make grafts less rejectable by modifying donor cells.

Related Terms

  • antonym: compatible - able to exist or work together without conflict, especially in medical contexts
  • degree higher: rejected - not accepted or expelled, especially by the immune system
