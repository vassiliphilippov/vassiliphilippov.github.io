decentering

noun
cognitive shift to recognize others' viewpoints
Pronunciation: / diˈsɛn.tɚ.ɪŋ / (AmE), / diˈsɛntərɪŋ / (BrE)
In psychology, the process by which a child moves from viewing the world solely from their own perspective to recognizing and considering the viewpoints of others; particularly associated with cognitive development theories.

Examples

Decentering in psychology
Piaget argued that decentering is essential for children to understand that others may hold different beliefs.
The therapist encouraged decentering to help the child see their sibling's feelings.
Through decentering, children become better at sharing and cooperating with others.

Related Terms

  • antonym: egocentrism - the inability to differentiate between self and others' perspectives
