bloodletting

noun
historical medical procedure of removing blood
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈblʌdˌlɛt̬ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈblʌdˌlɛtɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or practice of withdrawing blood from a patient for therapeutic purposes, especially as an ancient medical treatment to cure or prevent illness

Examples

Medieval bloodletting
In medieval times, bloodletting was often performed to balance the body's humors.
Many historic illustrations depict doctors using leeches for bloodletting.
The physician recommended bloodletting as a remedy, reflecting the beliefs of his era.
