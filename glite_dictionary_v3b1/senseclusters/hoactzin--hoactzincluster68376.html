hoactzin

noun
crested, ill-smelling South American bird
Pronunciation: / hoʊˈætsɪn / (AmE), / həʊˈætsɪn / (BrE)
a crested, ill-smelling bird from South America (Opisthocomus hoazin), notable for its chicks having claws on the first and second digits of their wings

Examples

The hoactzin bird
Despite its awkward appearance, the hoactzin plays an important role in the ecosystem.
The Amazon rainforest is home to the remarkable hoactzin, a bird known for its distinctive odor.
Young hoactzins use the claws on their wings to climb among the branches.
