nucleon number

noun
the sum of protons and neutrons in a nucleus
Pronunciation: / ˈnuː.kli.ɑn ˈnʌm.bɚ / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.kli.ɒn ˈnʌm.bə / (BrE)
the total number of protons and neutrons in the nucleus of an atom; also called mass number

Examples

The nucleon number of atom
Knowing the nucleon number helps scientists determine the isotope of an element.
The nucleon number of carbon-12 is 12, as it has 6 protons and 6 neutrons.
The difference between the nucleon number and the atomic number gives the number of neutrons.
