thinness

noun
quality of being sparse or not dense
Pronunciation: / ˈθɪnnəs / (AmE), / ˈθɪnnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being sparse or not dense; the condition of being scanty or scattered

Examples

Thinness of her hair
She was concerned about the thinness of her hair after years of coloring.
The thinness of the crowd made the concert feel almost empty.
The thinness of the tree cover allowed more sunlight to reach the forest floor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: density - the quality of being dense or closely packed
  • synonym: sparseness - the quality of being thinly distributed or scattered
