EMDA

noun
electrically assisted drug delivery therapy
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌiː ˌɛm ˌdiː ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌiː ˌɛm ˌdiː ˈeɪ / (BrE)
a medical therapy that uses a local electric current to introduce the ions of a medicine into body tissues

Examples

EMDA therapy
EMDA is considered an effective technique for delivering chemotherapy drugs locally.
Patients undergoing EMDA typically experience few side effects compared to traditional systemic treatments.
The doctor recommended EMDA for treating the patient's bladder condition, as it enhances drug absorption.
