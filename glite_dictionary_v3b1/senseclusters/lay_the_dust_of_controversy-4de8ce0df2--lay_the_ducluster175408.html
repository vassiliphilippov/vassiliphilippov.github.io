lay the dust of controversy

verb
to resolve an argument or dispute
Pronunciation: / leɪ ðə dʌst əv ˌkɑːntrəˈvɝːsi / (AmE), / leɪ ðə dʌst əv kənˈtrɒvəsi / (BrE)
to settle or resolve arguments or disputes; to bring an end to controversy

Examples

To lay the dust of controversy
New evidence helped lay the dust of controversy that followed the committee's decision.
The company's statement was intended to lay the dust of controversy surrounding its practices.
The prime minister introduced reforms to lay the dust of controversy over the policy changes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: stir up controversy - to cause or provoke disagreement or argument
