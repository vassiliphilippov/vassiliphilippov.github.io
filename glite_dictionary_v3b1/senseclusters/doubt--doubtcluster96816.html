doubt

noun
a feeling of uncertainty
1.6 87/m
Pronunciation: / daÊŠt / (AmE), / daÊŠt / (BrE)
a feeling of uncertainty or lack of conviction about something

Examples

A doubt remains
Despite their reassurance, a doubt still lingered in his mind.
She had a nagging doubt about the accuracy of the information.
There is little doubt that the company will succeed.
Despite her doubt, she decided to trust his explanation.
He expressed serious doubt about the accuracy of the report.
There was a lingering doubt in his mind after hearing the news.

Synonyms

    4.0 27/m
    uncertainty is a general state of not knowing what will happen, while doubt is a personal lack of conviction.
  • dubiety
    9.9 0.05/m
    dubiety is a formal word for being doubtful and unsure, while doubt is the common everyday feeling of lack of conviction.
  • incertitude
    8.9 0.00/m
    incertitude is a rare, formal term for a state of uncertainty, while doubt is the usual word people use for that feeling.
  • dubiousness
    7.8 0.03/m
    dubiousness suggests you find something suspicious or doubtful, while doubt is a more general feeling of uncertainty.
  • reservation
    4.8 5.37/m
    reservation is a hesitation or unwillingness to fully agree, while doubt is a broad lack of conviction about an idea.
  • misgiving
    5.9 2.48/m
    misgiving is a worried feeling that something may go wrong, while doubt is a simple lack of certainty or conviction.
  • qualm
    8.4 2.30/m
    qualm is a sudden uneasy feeling about whether something is right, while doubt is a general lack of confidence or conviction.
