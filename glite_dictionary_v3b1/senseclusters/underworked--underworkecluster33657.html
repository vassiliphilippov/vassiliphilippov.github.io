underworked

adjective
insufficiently processed
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈwɜrkt / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈwɜːkt / (BrE)
not sufficiently worked, processed, or refined, especially referring to materials like dough, metal, or clay.

Examples

Underworked dough
The sculpture looked rough because the clay was underworked and not properly shaped.
If the dough is underworked, the bread may turn out dense and heavy.
The metal piece was underworked, resulting in a weak and uneven surface.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overworked - worked or processed too much
  • synonym: unfinished - not fully completed or processed
