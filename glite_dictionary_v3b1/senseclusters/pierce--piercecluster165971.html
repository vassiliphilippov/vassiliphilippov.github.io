pierce

verb
affect deeply or sharply (feelings/sensations)
7.9
Pronunciation: / pɪrs / (AmE), / pɪəs / (BrE)
to affect someone's emotions or sensations deeply or sharply, often in a sudden or powerful way

Examples

Fear pierces the heart
A sudden sense of loneliness pierced him as he entered the empty house.
His harsh criticism pierced her confidence, leaving her feeling vulnerable.
The news of her friend's illness pierced her heart.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stab - to wound or pierce with a pointed object; can be used figuratively to mean deeply hurt emotionally
  • synonym: touch - to affect emotionally, to move someone
