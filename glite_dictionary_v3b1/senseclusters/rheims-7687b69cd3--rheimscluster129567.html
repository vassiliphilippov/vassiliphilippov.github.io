Rheims

proper noun
city in northeastern France, royal coronation site
Pronunciation: / riːmz / (AmE), / riːmz / (BrE)
A city in northeastern France, east of Paris, notable as the traditional site of the coronation of most French kings and the location where Germany's unconditional surrender was signed in 1945, ending World War II in Europe.

Examples

City of Rheims
In 1945, Germany's unconditional surrender was signed at Rheims, marking the end of World War II in Europe.
Many French kings were crowned in Rheims Cathedral, a symbol of national heritage.
Tourists often visit Rheims to admire its beautiful Gothic architecture and famous champagne houses.
