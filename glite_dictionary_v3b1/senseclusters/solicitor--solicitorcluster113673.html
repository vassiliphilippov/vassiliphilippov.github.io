solicitor

noun
British lawyer giving legal advice
4.8 7.0
Pronunciation: / səˈlɪsɪtɚ / (AmE), / səˈlɪsɪtə / (BrE)
(in the UK and some other countries) a lawyer who gives legal advice, prepares legal documents, and may represent clients in lower courts.

Examples

Solicitor gives advice
After consulting with her solicitor, she decided to proceed with the purchase of the house.
He works as a solicitor for a well-known law firm in London.
The solicitor prepared all the necessary documents for the business contract.

Related Terms

  • antonym: barrister - a lawyer entitled to represent clients in higher courts (UK)
  • synonym: lawyer - a person who practices or studies law; an attorney or a counselor
