syncopator

noun
musician who plays syncopated jazz music
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪŋ.kəˌpeɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋ.kəˌpeɪ.tə / (BrE)
a musician, especially in a jazz or dance band, who plays or emphasizes syncopated rhythms

Examples

Jazz syncopator
As a skilled syncopator, she kept dancers on their feet all night.
Several famous syncopators emerged during the Jazz Age in New Orleans.
The talented syncopator gave the band a distinctive swinging rhythm.
