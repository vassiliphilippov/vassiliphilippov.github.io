cheer

verb
make someone feel happier
Pronunciation: / tʃɪr / (AmE), / tʃɪə / (BrE)
to make someone (or yourself) feel happier or more cheerful

Examples

To cheer someone up
A funny movie often helps to cheer people on a gloomy day.
Her friends tried to cheer her after she lost the match.
We brought flowers to cheer him up while he was in the hospital.
After hearing the good news, she began to cheer considerably.
It may take a while for him to cheer after such disappointment.
Sometimes a simple joke can make people cheer up even on a gloomy day.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brighten up - to become happier or more cheerful
  • synonym: comfort - make someone feel less unhappy or worried
  • synonym: encourage - give support or confidence to someone
  • antonym: sadden - to become sadder
