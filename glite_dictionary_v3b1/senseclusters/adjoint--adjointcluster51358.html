adjoint

noun
related mathematical operator
Pronunciation: / ˈædʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈædʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
a mathematical operator or function that is related to another by a specific relationship, often involving transposition or conjugation

Examples

Adjoint matrix
The adjoint of a matrix is used frequently in linear algebra.
In quantum mechanics, the adjoint operator plays a crucial role in calculations.
She learned how to compute the adjoint for different types of transformations.
