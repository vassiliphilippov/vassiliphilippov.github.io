seceder

noun
person who withdraws
Pronunciation: / sɪˈsiːdər / (AmE), / sɪˈsiːdə / (BrE)
a person who formally withdraws from an organization, group, or country, especially for political or religious reasons

Examples

The seceder left party
The seceder left the political party to start a new movement.
During the meeting, one seceder explained his reasons for leaving the group.
History books often discuss the actions of seceders during times of national crisis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dissenter - a person who disagrees with or opposes official opinion
  • antonym: member - a person who belongs to a group or organization
