positive

adjective
having a positive electric charge
5.9 2.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…ëÀêz…ôt…™v / (AmE), / Ààp…íz…ôt…™v / (BrE)
Having an electric charge of the type carried by protons, as opposed to electrons.

Examples

Positive charge
A proton has a positive charge, while an electron is negative.
In chemical reactions, sodium tends to acquire a positive charge by losing one electron.
The ends of the battery are marked with positive and negative signs.

Synonyms

  • positively charged
    5.1 0.25/m
    positively charged describes having an electric positive charge in general situations, while positive is the shorter technical adjective used in physics.
  • cationic
    7.8 0.03/m
    cationic refers to molecules or substances that contain positive ions and sounds more chemical and formal, while positive names the basic electric charge.
