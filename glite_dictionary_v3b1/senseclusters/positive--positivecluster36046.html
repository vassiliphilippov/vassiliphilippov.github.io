positive

adjective
having a positive electric charge
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːzətɪv / (AmE), / ˈpɒzətɪv / (BrE)
Having an electric charge of the type carried by protons, as opposed to electrons.

Examples

Positive charge
A proton has a positive charge, while an electron is negative.
In chemical reactions, sodium tends to acquire a positive charge by losing one electron.
The ends of the battery are marked with positive and negative signs.
