slowworm

noun
a small legless European lizard
Pronunciation: / ˈsloʊˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈsləʊˌwɜːm / (BrE)
A small, legless European lizard (Anguis fragilis) with smooth skin and tiny eyes, often mistaken for a snake.

Examples

A slowworm lizard
Although a slowworm resembles a snake, it is actually a species of legless lizard.
The slowworm moves quietly through the grass, looking for insects to eat.
We discovered a slowworm hiding beneath the garden compost bin.
