preach

verb
speak in favor of something
3.9
Pronunciation: / priːtʃ / (AmE), / priːtʃ / (BrE)
to speak, plead, or argue in favor of a particular cause or idea, often with conviction or moral fervor

Examples

To preach healthy living
He is always quick to preach the benefits of a healthy lifestyle to his friends.
Instead of taking action, she just continued to preach about the importance of recycling.
The activist preached against environmental destruction at the community meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: advocate - to publicly recommend or support a particular cause or policy
  • antonym: oppose - to act against or resist a cause or idea
