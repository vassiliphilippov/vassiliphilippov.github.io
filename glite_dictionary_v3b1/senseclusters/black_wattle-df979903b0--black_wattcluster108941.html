black wattle

noun
Australian acacia tree used for tanning
9.9 7.0
Pronunciation: / blæk ˈwɑt̬l̩ / (AmE), / blæk ˈwɒtəl / (BrE)
An Australian acacia tree, especially Acacia mearnsii, whose bark yields tannins used in tanning leather.

Examples

Australian black wattle
Farmers in Australia often plant black wattle to help control soil erosion.
Several species of black wattle thrive in the warm climate of New South Wales.
The bark of the black wattle is processed to produce tannins for the leather industry.
