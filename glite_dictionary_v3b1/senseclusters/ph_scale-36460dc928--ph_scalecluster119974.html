pH scale

noun
scale for acidity or alkalinity of solutions
3.6 3.0
Pronunciation: /ˈpiː.eɪtʃ skeɪl/ /; / /piː.ˈeɪtʃ skeɪl/ (AmE), /ˈpiː.eɪtʃ skeɪl/ /; / /piː.ˈeɪtʃ skeɪl/ (BrE)
a scale from 0 to 14 used to measure how acidic or basic a solution is, with 7 being neutral, values below 7 indicating acidity, and values above 7 indicating alkalinity

Examples

The pH scale value
Household cleaning products often have a high rating on the pH scale, making them more alkaline.
Many plants grow best in soil that is neutral on the pH scale.
The scientist tested the liquid and recorded its pH scale value as 3, indicating strong acidity.
