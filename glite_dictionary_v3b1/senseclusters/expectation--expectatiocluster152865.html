expectation

noun
belief about what will happen
1.0 2.4
Pronunciation: / ˌɛkˌspɛkˈteɪʃən /; / ˌɛk.spɛkˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛk.spɛkˈteɪ.ʃən /; / ˌɛk.spekˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a belief or feeling that something will happen or is likely to happen in the future

Examples

High expectation
Her high expectation of success motivated her to work harder.
The company's earnings report failed to meet investors' expectations.
When reality did not match his expectations, he felt disappointed.
As the concert began, a sense of expectation filled the air.
Her heart was pounding with expectation as she waited for the results.
There is always an expectation of rain during the monsoon season.
He waited with great expectation for the results of the competition.
Her expectation of getting the job was based on her impressive qualifications.
There was a general expectation that interest rates would rise soon.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anticipation - expectancy or prediction, often with excitement
  • degree higher: certainty - something sure or inevitable
  • antonym: doubt - a feeling of uncertainty or lack of conviction
  • degree lower: hope - a feeling of desire for a particular outcome
  • antonym: surprise - a feeling resulting from something unexpected happening
