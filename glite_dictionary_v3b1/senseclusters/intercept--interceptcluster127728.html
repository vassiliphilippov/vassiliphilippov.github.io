intercept

verb
tap a phone or wire for information
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈsɛpt / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈsɛpt / (BrE)
to secretly listen to phone conversations or telegraph messages in order to gather information, especially by tapping a line

Examples

To intercept a call
Authorities managed to intercept a message by tapping into the secure communication line.
The agents tried to intercept the suspect's phone calls to gather evidence.
The police intercepted several conversations related to the investigation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: eavesdrop - to secretly listen to someone else's conversation
  • synonym: tap - to listen secretly to telephone calls by connecting to the line
