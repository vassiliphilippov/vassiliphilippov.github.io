to secretly listen to phone conversations or telegraph messages in order to gather information, especially by tapping a line

Examples

To intercept a call
Authorities managed to intercept a message by tapping into the secure communication line.
The agents tried to intercept the suspect's phone calls to gather evidence.
The police intercepted several conversations related to the investigation.

Synonyms

  • tap
    8.2 1.65/m
    tap is done by connecting a device to a telephone or telegraph wire to hear messages, while intercept means secretly gathering such communications.
  • wiretap
    7.5 0.23/m
    wiretap is listening to telephone lines with a device, often in a police inquiry, while intercept covers secretly collecting messages more broadly.
  • listen in
    5.0 2.55/m
    listen in means quietly hearing a conversation or broadcast without being noticed, while intercept implies secretly capturing communications for information.
  • eavesdrop
    5.0 1.09/m
    eavesdrop is overhearing people's talk nearby without permission, while intercept refers to secretly capturing phone or telegraph messages often by tapping.

Related Terms

  • synonym: eavesdrop - to secretly listen to someone else's conversation
  • synonym: tap - to listen secretly to telephone calls by connecting to the line
