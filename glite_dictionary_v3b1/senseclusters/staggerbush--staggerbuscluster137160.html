staggerbush

noun
poisonous shrub of eastern US
Pronunciation: / ˈstæɡərˌbʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈstæɡəbʊʃ / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to the coastal plains of the eastern United States, with drooping pinkish-white flowers and poisonous to livestock.

Examples

The staggerbush plant
Botanists studied the spread of staggerbush in the sandy soils of the coastal plains.
Cows should be kept away from the staggerbush because it is toxic to livestock.
In spring, the staggerbush produces clusters of delicate pinkish-white bell-shaped flowers.
