yellow-dog contract

noun
contract forbidding union membership (now illegal)
9.9 8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈje.loʊ dɔːg ˈkɑːn.trækt/ /; / /ˈje.loʊ dɑːg ˈkɑːn.trækt/ (AmE), /ˈje.ləʊ dɒg ˈkɒn.trækt/ (BrE)
An agreement between an employer and an employee in which the employee promises not to join a trade union, now illegal in the United States.

Examples

Yellow-dog contracts forbid unions
Yellow-dog contracts were declared illegal in the United States under the Norris-LaGuardia Act.
During the early 1900s, yellow-dog contracts were common in efforts to curb the growing strength of labor unions.
The company pressured new hires to sign a yellow-dog contract in order to prevent unionization.
