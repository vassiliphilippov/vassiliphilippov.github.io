Arthur Neville Chamberlain

proper noun

(1869–1940) British statesman who served as Prime Minister from 1937 to 1940, known for his policy of appeasement toward Nazi Germany before World War II.

Examples

Arthur Neville Chamberlain Prime Minister
Arthur Neville Chamberlain is often remembered for signing the Munich Agreement in 1938.
As Prime Minister, Arthur Neville Chamberlain aimed to maintain peace in Europe but faced criticism after World War II began.
Many historians debate whether Arthur Neville Chamberlain's appeasement policy was justified at the time.
