Impressionism

noun
19th-century art movement emphasizing light and color
4.6 7.9
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈprɛʃənˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɛʃənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
a style or movement in art, especially painting, originating in 19th-century France, that seeks to capture the impression of the moment, especially in terms of light and color, using quick and visible brush strokes.

Examples

Impressionism art style
Many consider Claude Monet the most famous painter associated with Impressionism.
She studied the principles of Impressionism during her art history coursework.
The Impressionism movement revolutionized the way artists depicted light and atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Post-Impressionism - an art movement that developed from Impressionism, emphasizing more structure and form
