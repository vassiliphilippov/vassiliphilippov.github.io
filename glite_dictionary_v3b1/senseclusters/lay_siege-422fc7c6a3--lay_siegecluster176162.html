lay siege

verb
attack or surround persistently
8.2 0.40/m
Pronunciation: / le…™ siÀêd í / (AmE), / le…™ siÀêd í / (BrE)
to surround a place with armed forces in order to capture it or force it to surrender, usually by cutting off supplies or attacking over time

Examples

To lay siege a city
During the war, several cities were laid siege as opposing forces struggled for control.
Reporters laid siege to the celebrity's hotel after news broke of the scandal.
The invading army laid siege to the fortress, cutting off all supplies for months.
During the war, the army laid siege to the castle for several months.
The general threatened to lay siege if the town refused to negotiate.
The rebels tried to lay siege to the city, hoping to force a surrender.

Synonyms

  • besiege
    8.3 1.32/m
    besiege is a single formal verb often used in writing, while lay siege is a phrasal verb for a long military blockade.
  • beleaguer
    9.5 0.17/m
    beleaguer often means to surround or harass and appears in literary or figurative speech, while lay siege means to surround militarily to force surrender.

Related Terms

  • synonym: besiege - to surround a place with armed forces in order to capture it or force its surrender
  • antonym: retreat - to withdraw from enemy forces as a result of their superior power or after a defeat
