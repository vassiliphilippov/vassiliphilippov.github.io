eutherian

noun
placental mammal
9.9 9.1
Pronunciation: /juː.ˈθɪə.ri.ən/ /; / /juː.ˈθiː.ri.ən/ (AmE), /juː.ˈθɪə.ri.ən/ /; / /juː.ˈθiː.ri.ən/ (BrE)
a mammal belonging to the group Eutheria, characterized by having a placenta and including all mammals except monotremes and marsupials

Examples

A eutherian animal
Most familiar animals, such as dogs and whales, are classified as eutherians.
The elephant is a large eutherian that gestates for nearly two years.
Unlike marsupials, eutherian mammals nourish their young through a well-developed placenta.

Related Terms

  • antonym: marsupial - a mammal of the group that carries young in a pouch
  • antonym: monotreme - an egg-laying mammal, such as a platypus or echidna
  • synonym: placental - a mammal of the group that has a placenta
