grid

noun
network of intersecting lines
1.8 2.9
Pronunciation: / ɡrɪd / (AmE), / ɡrɪd / (BrE)
A network of regularly spaced horizontal and vertical lines that forms squares or rectangles, often used as a guide for locating or arranging things.

Examples

A grid of lines
Engineers use a grid to plan the placement of buildings.
The city streets are laid out in a grid pattern.
The puzzle had a 10-by-10 grid of empty squares.
