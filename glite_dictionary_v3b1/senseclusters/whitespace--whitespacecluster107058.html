whitespace

noun
empty space in layout
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪtˌspeɪs / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtˌspeɪs / (BrE)
the empty space between elements in writing, design, or computer code, used to improve readability or organization

Examples

Page whitespace
Good use of whitespace makes a page easier to read.
Designers often add whitespace to create a clean and balanced look.
In programming, whitespace can help separate sections of code for clarity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clutter - a crowded or disorganized arrangement with little empty space
  • synonym: margin - the blank space around the edges of a page
