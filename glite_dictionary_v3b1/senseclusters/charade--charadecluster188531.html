charade

noun
word acted out in charades
Pronunciation: / ʃəˈreɪd / (AmE), / ʃəˈrɑːd / (BrE)
a game in which players act out words or phrases without speaking, and others try to guess; also, a word or phrase acted out in this game

Examples

Play charades at parties
During the party, my favorite charade was pretending to be a famous actor.
Each team took turns acting out a charade while the others tried to solve it.
He chose a difficult charade that no one could guess.
At the family gathering, we played charade and laughed at everyone's creative acting.
She guessed the movie title quickly during the charade game last night.
Acting out difficult words in charade can be both challenging and entertaining.

Related Terms

  • synonym: game - an activity with rules for entertainment or competition
  • synonym: pantomime - the act of expressing meaning through gestures without speech
