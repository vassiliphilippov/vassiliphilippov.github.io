operculum

noun
hard flap covering gills or shell opening
9.9 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / o äÀàp…úÀêrkj…ôl…ôm / (AmE), / …ôÀàp…úÀêkj…ôl…ôm /; / …ô äÀàp…úÀêkj äl…ôm / (BrE)
a structure (such as a hard flap or lid) that covers or closes an opening in an animal or plant, for example, the gill cover in fish or the lid in snails

Examples

Fish operculum flap
In many bony fishes, the operculum visibly moves as the fish breathes.
The fish uses its operculum to protect its delicate gills from debris.
When the snail feels threatened, it pulls into its shell and seals the entrance with its operculum.
The snail withdraws into its shell and closes the opening with its operculum.
In some fish, the gills are protected by a bony operculum.
The plant's seed capsule is sealed by an operculum that falls off when the seeds are ready to disperse.
