gauntness

noun
extreme thinness (from illness or hunger)
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɔnt.nəs / (AmE), / ˈɡɔːnt.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being extremely thin, especially because of illness or lack of food

Examples

Extreme gauntness
Her gauntness was a clear sign that she had been suffering for a long time.
Over a period of months, his gauntness became more apparent to everyone around him.
The gauntness of the refugees shocked the aid workers who arrived at the camp.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emaciation - the state of being abnormally thin or weak, especially because of illness
  • antonym: plumpness - the quality of being pleasantly fat or rounded
