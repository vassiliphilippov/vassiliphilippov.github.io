crosslinking

noun
polymer bonding process
5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈkrɑːs.lɪŋ.kɪŋ/ /; / /ˈkrɔːs.lɪŋ.kɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈkrɒs.lɪŋ.kɪŋ/ (BrE)
a process in which chemical bonds connect one polymer chain to another, increasing strength and stability

Examples

Polymer crosslinking
The crosslinking of rubber molecules makes tires more durable and heat-resistant.
Scientists studied how crosslinking affects the flexibility of synthetic materials.
Medical treatments sometimes use crosslinking to strengthen corneal tissue in the eye.
