grume

noun
thick viscous mass or clot
Pronunciation: / ɡruːm / (AmE), / ɡruːm / (BrE)
a thick or semisolid mass, especially a clot of blood

Examples

Microscopic examination revealed a grume consisting of both red and white blood cells.
The grume blocked the narrow blood vessel, impeding normal circulation.
The surgeon noted a dark grume at the site of the injury.
