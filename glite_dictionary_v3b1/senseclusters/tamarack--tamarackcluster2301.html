tamarack

noun
a North American larch tree
9.9 7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈtæməˌræk / (AmE), / ˈtæmərak / (BrE)
a medium-sized larch tree native to Canada and northern United States, having a broad conical crown and rust-brown scaly bark

Examples

The tamarack tree

Moose often feed on young shoots of the tamarack in northern forests.
The tamarack can thrive in swampy or wetland areas where other trees struggle.
