copy editing

noun
reviewing written material before publication
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑpi ˌɛdɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɒpi ˌɛdɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of reviewing and correcting written material to improve accuracy, clarity, and style before publication, especially for print

Examples

Professional copy editing
Effective copy editing ensures that the final document is clear and error-free.
She spent her afternoon copy editing the manuscript to fix grammar and style issues.
The magazine hires professionals for copy editing before sending issues to print.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: content editing - editing that involves substantive changes to structure and content of a text
  • degree lower: proofreading - checking written material for errors after copy editing and before final printing
