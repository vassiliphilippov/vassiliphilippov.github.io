faceting

noun
formation of flat surfaces
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈfæsɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfæsɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the formation or creation of flat surfaces (facets) on a crystal, mineral, or gemstone, either naturally or by cutting

Examples

Gemstone faceting
The faceting of the quartz crystal made it sparkle under the light.
Scientists studied the faceting patterns to understand the mineral's growth history.
Natural faceting can reveal much about the conditions in which a crystal formed.
The faceting of the sapphire brought out its deep blue color.
Modern faceting techniques allow jewelers to create intricate patterns on gemstones.
Careful faceting can greatly increase the value of a diamond.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crystallization - the process by which a solid forms with a crystal structure
  • synonym: engraving - the process of cutting designs into a hard surface
  • antonym: erosion - the gradual wearing away of a surface by natural forces
  • synonym: polishing - the process of making a surface smooth and shiny
