CAT scanner

noun
machine for 3D medical imaging
Pronunciation: / ˈkæt ˌskænər / (AmE), / ˈkat ˌskanə / (BrE)
A machine that creates detailed three-dimensional images of the inside of the body by combining X-ray pictures taken from different angles.

Examples

A CAT scanner machine
Modern hospitals are equipped with at least one CAT scanner for advanced diagnostics.
Technicians carefully calibrated the CAT scanner before the first patient of the day arrived.
The doctor ordered a CAT scanner test to examine the patient's internal injuries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: CT scanner - machine for computerised tomography imaging
  • antonym: MRI scanner - machine for imaging the body using magnetic resonance
