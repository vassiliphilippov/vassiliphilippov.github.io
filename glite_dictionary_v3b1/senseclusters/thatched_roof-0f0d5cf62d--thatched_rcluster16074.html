thatched roof

noun
roof made of straw or reeds
5.3
Pronunciation: / θætʃt ruːf / (AmE), / θætʃt ruːf / (BrE)
a roof made of dried plant material such as straw, reeds, or rushes, used especially in traditional buildings

Examples

A thatched roof house
Many traditional English villages still feature houses with thatched roofs.
The cottage had a beautiful thatched roof that blended into the landscape.
The workers spent weeks repairing the old thatched roof before winter arrived.

Related Terms

  • antonym: shingle roof - a roof covered with overlapping shingles
  • antonym: tile roof - a roof covered with tiles, commonly made of clay or concrete
