contra danse

noun
folk dance with lines of couples
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑn.trə dæns / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.trə dɑːns / (BrE)
A type of folk dance in which couples are arranged in sets or face one another in lines, typically moving through a sequence of figures.

Examples

Traditional contra danse
At the festival, dancers formed two long lines for a traditional contra danse.
Many communities host regular contra danse events open to all ages.
The group spent the evening learning a lively contra danse from the instructor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: country dance - a similar folk dance with couples arranged in lines or circles
