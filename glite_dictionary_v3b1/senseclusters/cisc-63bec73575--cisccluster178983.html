CISC

noun
computer architecture with many CPU instructions
Pronunciation: / siː aɪ ɛs siː / (AmE), / siː aɪ ɛs siː / (BrE)
(in computer science) a type of computer architecture with a large number of specialized instructions built into the CPU.

Examples

CISC computer architecture
Many early personal computers used the CISC architecture to handle complex operations directly in hardware.
Our professor explained how CISC processors are designed to execute multi-step operations with a single instruction.
The debate over CISC versus RISC architectures shaped the development of modern microprocessors.
