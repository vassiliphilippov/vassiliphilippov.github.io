snobbism

noun
condescending attitude toward lower social status
9.7 6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈsnɑːb.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈsnɑːb.ə.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈsnɒb.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈsnɒb.ə.zəm/ (BrE)
The attitude or behavior of believing oneself to be superior to those of lower social or economic status and behaving condescendingly toward them.

Examples

Snobbism attitude
He tried to hide his snobbism, but his contempt for working-class people was obvious.
Her snobbism made it hard for her to make genuine friends outside of her social circle.
The film satirizes the snobbism of the wealthy elite in the city.

Related Terms

  • antonym: egalitarianism - the belief in human equality, especially with respect to social, political, and economic rights and privileges
  • synonym: snobbery - the behavior or attitude of people who think they are superior to others
