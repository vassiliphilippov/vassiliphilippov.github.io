snobbism

noun
condescending attitude toward lower social status
8.4 0.05/m
Pronunciation: / Ààsn…ëÀêbÀå…™z…ôm / (AmE), / Ààsn…íb…™z(…ô)m / (BrE)
The attitude or behavior of believing oneself to be superior to those of lower social or economic status and behaving condescendingly toward them.

Examples

Snobbism attitude
He tried to hide his snobbism, but his contempt for working-class people was obvious.
Her snobbism made it hard for her to make genuine friends outside of her social circle.
The film satirizes the snobbism of the wealthy elite in the city.

    snobbishness is the quality of acting superior about social rank or money, while snobbism is the same class-based attitude.
  • snootiness
    8.1 0.01/m
    snootiness is a casual, slightly mocking way to act superior, while snobbism is a broader social belief in being better.
  • superciliousness
    9.8 0.03/m
    superciliousness is a formal word for a proud, looking-down manner, while snobbism focuses on class-based superiority and condescension.
  • condescension
    7.9 1.39/m

  • patronization
    8.2 0.07/m

  • uppishness
