amid

preposition
in the middle of
1.0
Pronunciation: / əˈmɪd / (AmE), / əˈmɪd / (BrE)
surrounded by or in the middle of something, especially a situation or group

Examples

In amid trouble
The president spoke amid growing concerns about the economy.
She remained calm amid the chaos of the busy airport.
The city was rebuilt amid the ruins left by the earthquake.

Related Terms

  • synonym: among - in the middle of or surrounded by others
  • antonym: outside - not in or surrounded by something
