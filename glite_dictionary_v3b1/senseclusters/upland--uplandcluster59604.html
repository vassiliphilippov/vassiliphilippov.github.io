upland

noun
high or elevated land area
5.4 4.0
Pronunciation: /ˈʌ.plənd/ (AmE), /ˈʌ.plənd/ (BrE)
an area of high or elevated land, especially one that is not mountainous but stands higher than the surrounding country; often contrasted with lowland

Examples

A grassy upland
Much of the region consists of upland dotted with small villages and farms.
The sheep graze on the upland during the summer months.
Water from the upland flows down to feed the fertile valleys below.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lowland - area of land that is at a low elevation, usually near sea level
