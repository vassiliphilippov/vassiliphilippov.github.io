estivate

verb
become dormant during summer
Pronunciation: / ˈɛstəˌveɪt /; / ˈɛs.tɪ.veɪt / (AmE), / ˈiːstɪveɪt /; / ˈiː.stɪ.veɪt / (BrE)
(of an animal) to spend a hot or dry period in a dormant or inactive state, typically during the summer.

Examples

To estivate in summer
During extreme heat, certain amphibians estivate instead of remaining active.
Some desert snails estivate to survive the harsh, dry summer months.
The lungfish will estivate in the mud until the rains return.
In order to survive drought, the African lungfish will aestivate for several months.
Many desert snails aestivate in shallow burrows during the hottest months.
Some amphibians aestivate when water becomes scarce in the summer.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hibernate - to spend the winter in a dormant state
