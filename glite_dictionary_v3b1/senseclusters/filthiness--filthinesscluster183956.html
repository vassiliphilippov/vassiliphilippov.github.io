filthiness

noun
extreme dirtiness or foulness
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪlθinəs / (AmE), / ˈfɪlθinɪs / (BrE)
the state of being extremely dirty or foul, often due to the presence of dirt, refuse, or other unsanitary conditions

Examples

The filthiness of the room
Despite the filthiness of the river, some children still played near its banks.
She was shocked by the filthiness of the public restroom.
The filthiness of the abandoned house made it unfit for living.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cleanliness - the quality of being clean and free from dirt
