orlop deck

noun
lowest deck in a ship
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɔr.lɑp dɛk / (AmE), / ˈɔː.lɒp dɛk / (BrE)
the lowest deck in a ship, especially the fourth deck from the top in old sailing ships; often used for storage or housing cables.

Examples

The orlop deck storage
Heavy supplies were stored on the orlop deck during the long voyage.
The sailors went below to the orlop deck to secure the ship's cables.
Water sometimes leaked onto the orlop deck during storms, making conditions difficult for the crew.
