visiting professor

noun
temporary academic at another institution
5.6 3.4
Pronunciation: /ˈvɪz.ɪ.tɪŋ prə.ˈfes.ər/ (AmE), /ˈvɪz.ɪ.tɪŋ prə.ˈfes.ə/ (BrE)
A professor who teaches or conducts research at a college or university other than their home institution for a limited period.

Examples

A visiting professor abroad
After his fellowship, he spent a year as a visiting professor in Germany.
Dr. Smith accepted a position as a visiting professor at Oxford University for the spring semester.
The university's new visiting professor will teach advanced seminars in molecular biology.
