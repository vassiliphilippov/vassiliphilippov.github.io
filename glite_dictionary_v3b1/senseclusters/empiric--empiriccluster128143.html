empiric

adjective
relating to medical quackery or unscientific practice
8.4
Pronunciation: / ɛmˈpɪrɪk / (AmE), / ɛmˈpɪrɪk / (BrE)
(historical) Involving or characteristic of medical quackery or unscientific practice; relying on trial-and-error rather than scientific methods.

Examples

Empiric medicine
In the eighteenth century, empiric medicine often relied on anecdotal remedies.
Many empiric treatments were later discredited as ineffective or dangerous.
The doctor was accused of using empiric methods with no scientific basis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: quack - a person who dishonestly claims to have medical knowledge
