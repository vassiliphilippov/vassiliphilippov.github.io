quiet quit

verb
reduce effort at work without resigning
9.5 8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkwaɪət kwɪt / (AmE), / ˈkwaɪət kwɪt / (BrE)
To disengage from work while remaining employed by intentionally doing the minimum required and not going beyond basic job duties.

Examples

To quiet quit work
After years of high pressure, she decided to quiet quit and only do what was necessary at work.
He realized he was beginning to quiet quit when he stopped volunteering for extra projects.
Many employees are choosing to quiet quit instead of outright resigning their jobs.
