pear blight

noun
disease of pear and apple trees
9.9 6.5
Pronunciation: /peər blaɪt/ (AmE), /peə blaɪt/ (BrE)
a disease that causes blackening and wilting of the leaves on pear and apple trees, often leading to significant damage to the affected plants

Examples

Pear blight disease
Farmers are advised to inspect their fruit trees regularly for signs of pear blight.
The orchard suffered significant losses after pear blight spread among the trees.
Treatment for pear blight must be applied quickly to prevent further damage.

Related Terms
