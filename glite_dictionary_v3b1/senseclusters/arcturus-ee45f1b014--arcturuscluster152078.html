Arcturus

proper noun
brightest star in Boötes constellation
8.8
Pronunciation: / ɑːrkˈtʊr.əs / (AmE), / ɑːkˈtjʊə.rəs / (BrE)
the fourth brightest star in the night sky and the brightest in the constellation Boötes, located about 36 light-years from Earth

Examples

Star Arcturus
Astronomers study Arcturus to learn more about the evolution of stars.
On a clear night, Arcturus is easily visible in the northern hemisphere.
The ancient Greeks used Arcturus to help predict the onset of summer.
