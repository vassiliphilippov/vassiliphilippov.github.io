Synonyms

    6.1 2/m
    combative is eager to start fights or arguments, while aggressive can describe many forceful or hostile behaviors.
  • belligerent
    7.5 1/m
    belligerent is openly hostile and ready to fight, while aggressive also covers forceful behavior that may not be violent.
  • assaultive
    8.2 0.1/m
    assaultive means likely to attack physically, while aggressive covers forceful or hostile actions not only physical.
  • confrontational
    7.0 3/m
    confrontational describes dealing with others in an argumentative way, while aggressive focuses on readiness to attack or fight.
  • antagonistic
    6.8 2/m
    antagonistic shows active opposition or dislike toward someone, while aggressive shows a readiness to attack or argue.
  • hostile
    4.6 13/m
    hostile feels unfriendly or strongly against someone, while aggressive suggests forceful action or eagerness to fight or argue.
  • bellicose
    8.8 0.6/m
    bellicose sounds formal and warlike in tone, while aggressive is common and can mean forceful or ready to argue.
