combining form

noun
word element used in compounds
8.2
Pronunciation: / kəmˈbaɪnɪŋ fɔrm / (AmE), / kəmˈbaɪnɪŋ fɔːm / (BrE)
a word element, typically of Greek or Latin origin, that occurs only in combination with other forms to form compound words

Examples

A combining form element
'Hydro-' serves as a combining form in words such as 'hydroelectric' and 'hydrology'.
Medical terms often include more than one combining form derived from Greek or Latin.
The prefix 'bio-' is a combining form used in words like 'biology' and 'biography'.
