layoff

noun
dismissal of employees, often for economic reasons
3.8 2.2
Pronunciation: /ˈleɪ.ɔːf/ /; / /ˈleɪ.ɑːf/ (AmE), /ˈleɪ.ɒf/ (BrE)
an act of ending a worker's employment, usually temporarily or for economic reasons rather than personal performance

Examples

A layoff notice
After the sudden layoff, many workers struggled to find new jobs in the area.
The company announced a major layoff affecting over 2,000 employees nationwide.
These frequent layoffs have left staff morale at an all-time low.
