morphophonemic

adjective
relating to morphophonemics in linguistics
Pronunciation: / mɔr.foʊ.fəˈniː.mɪk / (AmE), / mɔː.fə.fəˈniː.mɪk / (BrE)
Relating to the branch of linguistics that studies the interaction between morphological and phonological structures, especially the way that morphemes are affected by phonological rules.

Examples

Morphophonemic change
In linguistics, a morphophonemic change refers to sound alterations conditioned by a word's morphological structure.
The analysis included a detailed study of morphophonemic alternations in English plural formation.
Understanding morphophonemic rules is essential for deciphering complex inflectional patterns.

Related Terms

  • synonym: morphological - relating to the form and structure of words
  • synonym: phonemic - relating to phonemes or phonemics
