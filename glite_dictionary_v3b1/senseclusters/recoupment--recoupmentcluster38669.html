recoupment

noun
deduction by offset
7.7
Pronunciation: / rɪˈkuːpmənt / (AmE), / rɪˈkuːpmənt / (BrE)
the act or process of getting back money or value that was lost, spent, or paid out

Examples

The recoupment of losses
The company sought recoupment of its losses by deducting them from future payments.
In court, the defendant argued for recoupment to offset the damages claimed by the plaintiff.
Accounting rules allow for recoupment when related debts can be balanced against each other.
The audit revealed a recoupment of funds that had been mistakenly paid out last year.
After the lawsuit, the company sought recoupment of its legal expenses from the defendant.
Government agencies often pursue recoupment when overpayments are discovered.
The company focused on the recoupment of its initial investment.
After the lawsuit, he sought recoupment of the damages he had suffered.
Successful recoupment of expenses is important for any business to remain profitable.

Related Terms

  • antonym: loss - the state of no longer having something or having less than before
  • synonym: recovery - the act of getting something back that was lost
  • synonym: reimbursement - the act of paying back money owed or spent
  • synonym: setoff - a legal claim to reduce a debt by an opposing claim
