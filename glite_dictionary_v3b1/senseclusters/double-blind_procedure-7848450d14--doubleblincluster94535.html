double-blind procedure

noun
experimental method hiding treatment allocation
2.5
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌb.əl ˈblaɪnd prəˈsi.dʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʌb.əl ˈblaɪnd prəˈsiː.dʒə / (BrE)
an experimental method in which neither the participants nor the experimenters know which subjects are receiving the treatment or the placebo, used to eliminate bias

Examples

A double-blind procedure in research
A double-blind procedure makes it less likely that expectations will influence the outcome of the experiment.
The researchers implemented a double-blind procedure to ensure their results were unbiased.
To minimize placebo effects, the clinical trial used a double-blind procedure.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: single-blind procedure - experimental method where only the subjects are unaware of key details
