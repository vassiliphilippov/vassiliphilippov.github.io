surrealism

noun
20th-century art/literature movement of dreamlike imagery
Pronunciation: / səˈriəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / səˈrɪəˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
A 20th-century artistic and literary movement that sought to express the unconscious mind through fantastic or dreamlike imagery and unexpected combinations.

Examples

Surrealism art movement
Surrealism challenged traditional art by using unexpected juxtapositions and fantastic imagery.
Many poets embraced surrealism to explore the workings of the subconscious mind.
The painting’s bizarre, dreamlike quality is characteristic of surrealism.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: dadaism - an earlier artistic movement emphasizing absurdity and anti-art
  • antonym: realism - artistic movement focused on representing reality accurately
