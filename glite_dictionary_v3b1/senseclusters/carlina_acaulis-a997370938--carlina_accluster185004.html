Carlina acaulis

noun
stemless alpine perennial plant with large flowers
9.9
Pronunciation: /kɑːr.ˈliː.nə ə.ˈkaʊ.lɪs/ (AmE), /kɑː.ˈliː.nə ə.ˈkaʊ.lɪs/ (BrE)
A stemless perennial plant of the daisy family, native to alpine regions of southern and eastern Europe, with large silvery-white or purplish-brown flowers and spiny, hairy leaves.

Examples

Carlina acaulis plant
Botanists admire the striking appearance of the Carlina acaulis when it blooms in late summer.
Some traditional herbal remedies in Europe have used parts of the Carlina acaulis for medicinal purposes.
The Carlina acaulis is often seen growing close to the ground in sunny alpine meadows.
