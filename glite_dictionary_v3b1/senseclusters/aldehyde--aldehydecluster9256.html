aldehyde

noun
type of organic chemical compound
Pronunciation: / ˈældəˌhaɪd / (AmE), / ˈaldɪhʌɪd / (BrE)
a class of organic chemical compounds containing a carbonyl group bonded to a hydrogen atom, used in making resins, dyes, and organic acids

Examples

A aldehyde compound
Formaldehyde is the simplest aldehyde and is widely used as a disinfectant.
Many fragrances contain aldehyde compounds to enhance their scent.
The reaction produces an aldehyde group as one of its main products.
