umbilical vein

noun
vein in umbilical cord carrying blood to fetus
8.0 4.6
Pronunciation: /əm.ˈbɪl.ɪ.kəl veɪn/ /; / /ʌm.ˈbɪl.ɪ.kəl veɪn/ (AmE), /ʌm.ˈbɪl.ɪ.kəl veɪn/ /; / /ʌm.ˈbɪl.aɪ.kəl veɪn/ (BrE)
a vein in the umbilical cord that carries oxygenated, nutrient-rich blood from the placenta to the fetus

Examples

The umbilical vein carries blood
After birth, the umbilical vein rapidly closes as the newborn no longer relies on placental blood flow.
Doctors examine the umbilical vein for signs of proper fetal circulation in prenatal assessments.
The umbilical vein supplies the developing fetus with oxygen and nutrients during pregnancy.
