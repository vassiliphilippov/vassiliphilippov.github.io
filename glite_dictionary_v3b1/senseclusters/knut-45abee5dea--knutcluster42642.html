Knut

proper noun
King of Denmark, Norway, and England (c. 995–1035)
Pronunciation: / nut / (AmE), / kˈnuːt / (BrE)
A king of Denmark, Norway, and England in the early 11th century, known for consolidating his rule over all England after 1016.

Examples

Knut the Great
Coins from Knut's reign have been discovered across England and Scandinavia.
In British folklore, Knut is often remembered for the story of commanding the waves.
Many historians regard Knut as a powerful ruler who helped stabilize northern Europe.
