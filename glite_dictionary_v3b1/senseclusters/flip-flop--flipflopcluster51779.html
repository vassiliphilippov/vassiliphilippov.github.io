flip-flop

noun
a reversal of a previous decision
Pronunciation: / ˈflɪp ˌflɑp / (AmE), / ˈflɪp ˌflɒp / (BrE)
a sudden or unexpected reversal in policy, opinion, or decision

Examples

A political flip-flop
After public outcry, the company made a quick flip-flop and reinstated the old policy.
The senator's flip-flop on the issue angered many of his supporters.
Voters are often frustrated by politicians' frequent flip-flops during election season.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reversal - the act of changing to the opposite direction, position, or course of action
