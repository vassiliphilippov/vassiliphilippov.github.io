meltwater

noun
water from melted snow or ice
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛltˌwɔtɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɛltˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
Water formed by the melting of snow or ice, especially on glaciers or in cold climates.

Examples

Meltwater from glaciers
Each spring, meltwater flows into the rivers, raising the water levels significantly.
Glacial meltwater carved deep crevasses through the rocky landscape.
Scientists study meltwater to understand climate change in polar regions.
