Nigella hispanica

noun
a flowering plant species native to Spain and France
Pronunciation: / naɪˈdʒɛlə hɪˈspænɪkə / (AmE), / nɪˈdʒɛlə hɪˈspænɪkə / (BrE)
A species of flowering plant in the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae), native to Spain and southern France, known for its ornamental blue flowers.

Examples

Nigella hispanica blue flowers
Gardeners often choose Nigella hispanica for its striking blue flowers and decorative seed pods.
Seeds of Nigella hispanica are sometimes collected for use in ornamental gardens.
The climate of southern France is ideal for growing Nigella hispanica in well-drained soils.
