harbor

noun
place of safety or comfort
4.0 1.69/m
Pronunciation: / Ààh…ërb…ôr /; / Ààh…ërb…ö / (AmE), / Ààh…ëÀêb…ô / (BrE)
a place or situation where someone feels safe, comfortable, or protected, especially from danger or trouble

Examples

A harbor from danger
After a long and difficult journey, his home became a harbor from the troubles of the world.
In her grandmother's arms, the frightened child found a harbor where worries faded away.
Many people seek friendship as a harbor when life becomes overwhelming.
For many, the church became a harbour from the storms of life.
He sought a harbour where he could rest and recover.
In times of trouble, her family home was her harbour.

Synonyms

  • safe harbor
    3.9 0.08/m
    safe harbor is a formal phrase for clear protection from legal or other trouble, while harbor means a place someone feels safe.
  • haven
    3.9 5.45/m
    haven is a small, quiet place that protects people from danger or stress, while harbor stresses feeling safe and comfortable.
  • refuge
    3.3 18/m
    refuge is any person or place that gives protection or relief in danger, while harbor focuses on feeling safe and comfortable.
  • sanctuary
    4.1 4.03/m
    sanctuary is a safe shelter often with legal or religious protection for people in danger, while harbor is a general safe, comfortable place.
