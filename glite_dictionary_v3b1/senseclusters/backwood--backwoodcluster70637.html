backwood

noun
rural, unsophisticated person
Pronunciation: / ˈbækwʊd / (AmE), / ˈbækwʊd / (BrE)
a person from a remote rural area, often regarded as unsophisticated or lacking modern education

Examples

A backwood person
He was called a backwood because he had never used a computer before moving to the city.
Some people unfairly judge a backwood by their accent or clothing.
Despite being a backwood, she quickly adapted to life in the big city.

Related Terms

  • antonym: city dweller - a person who lives in a city
  • synonym: countryman - a person who lives in the countryside
  • synonym: hillbilly - a person from a rural, mountainous area, often considered unsophisticated
