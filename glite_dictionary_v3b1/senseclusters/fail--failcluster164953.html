fail

verb
be unable to do something
Pronunciation: / feɪl / (AmE), / feɪl / (BrE)
to not succeed in doing something or to not achieve what you intended

Examples

To fail a task
Despite his efforts, he would often fail to meet the deadline.
If you fail to respond by Friday, your application will not be considered.
Many people fail to realize how difficult the journey can be.
After weeks without rain, the village's water supply finally failed and residents had to seek alternatives.
If the heating system fails in winter, the building could become dangerously cold.
Sometimes technology fails us just when we need it most.
He fails every time he tries to fix the old car.
If we fail to act now, the situation will only get worse.
The company failed to meet its sales targets for the year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: break down - to stop functioning properly
  • synonym: flunk - to fail especially in an examination or course
  • antonym: succeed - to accomplish what is intended or desired
