nervous breakdown

noun
period of severe emotional distress
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈnɜrvəs ˈbreɪkˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈnɜːvəs ˈbreɪkdaʊn / (BrE)
a period of severe mental distress that prevents normal functioning, often requiring medical intervention

Examples

A nervous breakdown episode
His nervous breakdown was a turning point that made him seek help for his anxiety.
She suffered a nervous breakdown after months of unrelenting stress at work.
The pressure of exams led to her having a nervous breakdown during her final year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mental breakdown - severe psychological crisis causing inability to function
  • antonym: recovery - return to normal health after illness or breakdown
