adjust

verb
adapt to new conditions
3.0 23/m
Pronunciation: / É™ËˆdÊ’ÊŒst / (AmE), / É™ËˆdÊ’ÊŒst / (BrE)
to change or adapt oneself to new or different conditions; to get used to something new

Examples

To adjust to change
After moving to a new city, it took her months to adjust to the different climate.
Employees often find it difficult to adjust when a company changes its management style.
It is important that children adjust quickly to being back in school after the holidays.
After moving to a new country, it took her a while to adjust to the culture.
His eyes needed time to adjust to the dimly lit room.
You may need to adjust your expectations when starting a new job.

Synonyms

  • adapt
    3.0 14/m
    adapt is used for changing behavior or tools to fit new situations, while adjust is about changing yourself to new conditions.
  • get used to
    2.4 15/m
    get used to describes becoming familiar and comfortable over time with something, while adjust emphasizes actively changing to new conditions.
  • acclimate
    5.2 1.29/m
    acclimate often refers to getting used to climate or a new place slowly, while adjust covers changing yourself to any new situation.
  • settle in
    3.3 6.96/m
    settle in is about beginning to feel comfortable in a new home or job, while adjust is broader and means changing to new conditions.
  • habituate
    6.7 0.31/m
    habituate means making someone slowly get used to something by repeated exposure, while adjust focuses on changing oneself to fit new conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acclimate - to become accustomed to a new environment
  • synonym: adapt - to change in order to suit new conditions
  • antonym: resist - to refuse to accept or adjust to change
