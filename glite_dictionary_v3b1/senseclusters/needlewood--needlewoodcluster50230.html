needlewood

noun
Australian shrub with pointed leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈniːdəlˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈniːdəlˌwʊd / (BrE)
A large bushy shrub with sharp, pointed leaves and creamy white flowers, native to central and eastern Australia.

Examples

The needlewood shrub
During spring, the needlewood blooms with clusters of creamy white flowers.
Many farmers plant needlewoods along fence lines as windbreaks and barriers.
The dense branches of the needlewood provide excellent shelter for small birds.
