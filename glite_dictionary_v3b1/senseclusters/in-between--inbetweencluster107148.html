in-between

adjective
being in the middle; neither one extreme nor the other
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.bɪˈtwin / (AmE), / ˌɪn.bɪˈtwiːn / (BrE)
Situated somewhere in the middle of two extremes, stages, or positions; not at the beginning or at the end.

Examples

An in-between stage
Adolescence is an in-between stage that can be confusing for many teens.
He chose an in-between option rather than committing to either side.
The weather today is that awkward in-between of not warm but not cold.

Related Terms

  • antonym: extreme - being at either end of a range or scale; the furthest point
  • synonym: intermediate - coming between two things in time, place, order, character, etc.
  • synonym: middle - at an equal distance from the extremes or outer limits; central
