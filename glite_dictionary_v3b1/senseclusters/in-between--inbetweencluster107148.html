in-between

adjective
being in the middle; neither one extreme nor the other
4.0 7.89/m
Pronunciation: / Àå…™n.b…™Ààtwin / (AmE), / Àå…™n.b…™ÀàtwiÀên / (BrE)
Situated somewhere in the middle of two extremes, stages, or positions; not at the beginning or at the end.

Examples

An in-between stage
Adolescence is an in-between stage that can be confusing for many teens.
He chose an in-between option rather than committing to either side.
The weather today is that awkward in-between of not warm but not cold.

Synonyms

  • intermediate
    3.6 13/m
    intermediate describes a stage between two levels or steps, while in-between means situated somewhere between two extremes or positions.
  • middle
    1.0 122/m
    middle refers to the center point of space or time, while in-between means simply located between two extremes or stages.
  • midway
    3.2 3.61/m
    midway means at the equal or central point between two places or times, while in-between covers any position between extremes.
  • halfway
    1.3 24/m
    halfway says something is at an equal distance between two points, while in-between can mean any place between two extremes.
