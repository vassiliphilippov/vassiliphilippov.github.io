tenth cranial nerve

noun
the vagus nerve; major nerve of the body
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛnθ ˈkreɪniəl nɜrv / (AmE), / ˈtɛnθ ˈkreɪnɪəl nɜːv / (BrE)
the nerve, also called the vagus nerve, that supplies the pharynx, larynx, lungs, heart, esophagus, stomach, and most abdominal organs

Examples

The tenth cranial nerve function
Damage to the tenth cranial nerve can lead to difficulties with swallowing and speech.
Doctors examined the patient's tenth cranial nerve function as part of the neurological assessment.
The tenth cranial nerve plays a crucial role in regulating heart rate and digestion.
