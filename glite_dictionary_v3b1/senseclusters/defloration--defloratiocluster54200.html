defloration

noun
spoiling innocence or beauty
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːfləˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːfləˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of spoiling or destroying the innocence or beauty of something

Examples

Defloration of nature
Many critics argued that the construction of highways through the park was an act of defloration of a natural treasure.
Some saw the graffiti as a defloration of the building’s historic façade.
The artist mourned the defloration of the old city center by modern development.

Related Terms

  • synonym: despoil - to strip of value or beauty
  • antonym: preserve - to maintain the purity or beauty of something
