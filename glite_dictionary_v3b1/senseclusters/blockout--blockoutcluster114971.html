blockout

noun
temporary construction void
Pronunciation: / ˈblɑːkaʊt / (AmE), / ˈblɒkaʊt / (BrE)
a temporary space or form left in concrete or other construction to allow for future openings or installations

Examples

Floor blockout
The engineer specified a blockout in the floor slab for future plumbing access.
Before pouring the concrete, workers placed a foam blockout to create space for electrical conduits.
A blockout was used to ensure there would be an opening for the elevator shaft.

Related Terms

  • synonym: formwork - temporary molds used to shape concrete
  • synonym: void - an empty space left in construction for a purpose
