freshwater eel

noun
an eel inhabiting rivers or lakes as an adult
3.0 3.0
Pronunciation: /ˈfreʃ.wɔː.tər iːl/ /; / /ˈfreʃ.wɑː.tər iːl/ (AmE), /ˈfreʃ.wɔː.tə iːl/ (BrE)
A type of eel that lives in fresh water during adulthood but migrates to the sea to spawn, found in Europe and America, and sold both fresh and smoked as food.

Examples

Smoked freshwater eel
Many restaurants in Europe serve freshwater eel either fresh or smoked as a delicacy.
Scientists track the migration of freshwater eels to understand their unique spawning behavior.
The freshwater eel spends much of its life in rivers but returns to the sea to reproduce.
