rhotic

adjective
pronouncing 'r' sounds
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊtɪk / (AmE), / ˈrəʊtɪk / (BrE)
describing a dialect, accent, or speech sound where the 'r' sound is pronounced clearly or has an r-like quality

Examples

Rhotic accent
In a rhotic accent, the 'r' in 'car' is pronounced clearly.
Many regions in the United States have a rhotic way of speaking.
Linguists study whether a dialect is rhotic or non-rhotic to understand language variation.
Some English accents are described as rhotic because they pronounce the 'r' in all positions.
The linguist explained that the vowel in 'fur' is a rhotic vowel in American English.
Children learning to speak may struggle with producing rhotic sounds correctly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: non-rhotic - describing a dialect or accent in which the 'r' sound is not pronounced at the end of syllables
  • synonym: r-colored - having an 'r' quality in a vowel sound
