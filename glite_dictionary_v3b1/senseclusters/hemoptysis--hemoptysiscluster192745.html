hemoptysis

noun
coughing up blood (medical)
Pronunciation: / hɪˈmɑːptɪsɪs /; / hiːˈɑːmp.tɪ.sɪs / (AmE), / hɪˈmɒptɪsɪs /; / hɪˈmɒp.tɪ.sɪs / (BrE)
The coughing up of blood or blood-stained mucus from the lungs, airways, or bronchial tubes, often due to infection or disease.

Examples

Hemoptysis from lungs
Chronic bronchitis can sometimes lead to episodes of hemoptysis.
Doctors became concerned when she presented with hemoptysis and a persistent cough.
The patient was admitted to the emergency room after experiencing sudden hemoptysis.
He went to the hospital after noticing episodes of haemoptysis over several days.
Patients with chronic bronchitis are at increased risk of developing haemoptysis.
The presence of haemoptysis can indicate a serious infection that requires urgent evaluation.
