forastero

noun
common cacao bean
Pronunciation: / ˌfɔrəˈstɛroʊ / (AmE), / ˌfɒrəˈstɛərəʊ / (BrE)
a variety of cacao bean widely grown and commonly used in mass-produced chocolate

Examples

Forastero cocoa beans
Most mass-produced chocolate is made from forastero beans.
The robust flavor of forastero makes it popular among chocolate manufacturers worldwide.
Farmers in West Africa primarily cultivate forastero cacao for export.

Related Terms

  • antonym: criollo - a rare and high-quality variety of cacao bean
  • synonym: trinitario - a hybrid variety of cacao bean combining forastero and criollo traits
