choroid plexus

noun
network of vessels in brain ventricles producing cerebrospinal fluid
9.1
Pronunciation: /ˈkɔː.rɔɪd ˈplek.səs/ /; / /ˈkɑː.rɔɪd ˈplek.səs/ (AmE), /ˈkɒ.rɔɪd ˈplek.səs/ /; / /ˈkɔː.rɔɪd ˈplek.səs/ (BrE)
a network of blood vessels in the ventricles of the brain that produces cerebrospinal fluid and helps regulate its pressure

Examples

The choroid plexus network
A tumor affecting the choroid plexus can disrupt normal fluid balance in the ventricles.
During the procedure, the surgeon carefully avoided damaging the choroid plexus.
The choroid plexus is responsible for producing much of the cerebrospinal fluid in the human brain.
