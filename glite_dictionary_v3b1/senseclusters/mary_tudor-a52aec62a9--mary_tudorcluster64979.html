Mary Tudor

proper noun
Queen of England, 1553–1558; restored Catholicism
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛri ˈtuːdər / (AmE), / ˈmɛːri ˈtjuːdə / (BrE)
(1516–1558) Queen of England from 1553 to 1558, daughter of Henry VIII and Catherine of Aragon, noted for restoring Roman Catholicism and persecuting Protestants under her reign.

Examples

Mary Tudor queen
Mary Tudor was the first queen to rule England in her own right.
Historians often debate whether Mary Tudor's reign was a failure or a necessary counter to the Reformation.
The religious policies of Mary Tudor led to the execution of many Protestants.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Bloody Mary - nickname given to Mary Tudor due to her persecution of Protestants
