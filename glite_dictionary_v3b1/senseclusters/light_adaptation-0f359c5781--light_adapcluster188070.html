light adaptation

noun
eye adjustment to bright light
4.9 4.9
Pronunciation: /laɪt æ.dəp.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /laɪt ə.dəp.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /laɪt ə.dæp.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /laɪt æ.dəp.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process by which the eyes adjust to high levels of light, involving constriction of the pupil and activation of the cone cells

Examples

Light adaptation process
After being outside on a sunny day, it takes a moment for light adaptation to help you see clearly indoors.
Scientists study light adaptation to better understand how the visual system manages sudden changes in illumination.
The pupils respond quickly during light adaptation, reducing the amount of light entering the eyes.
