weather-beaten

adjective
worn from exposure to the weather
9.9 5.3
Pronunciation: /ˈweð.ər.biː.tən/ (AmE), /ˈweð.ə.biː.tən/ (BrE)
changed, worn, or roughened in appearance by long exposure to the weather, especially sun and wind (often used for skin or faces)

Examples

Weather-beaten face
After decades at sea, the captain's face was weather-beaten and deeply lined.
The weather-beaten fence had gray, splintered wood from years of sun and rain.
We admired the old, weather-beaten barn standing alone in the windswept field.
After months at sea, the sailor returned with a weather-beaten look.
Her weather-beaten face told the story of years spent working outdoors.
The weather-beaten walls of the cottage gave it a rustic charm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sunburned - having skin reddened or darkened from exposure to the sun
  • antonym: unblemished - not damaged or marked; perfect (especially of skin or surfaces)
  • synonym: weathered - worn, changed in appearance due to wind and rain
