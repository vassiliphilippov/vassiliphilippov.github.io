rejoinder

noun

(Law) A pleading submitted by a defendant in response to a plaintiff's replication.

Examples

Legal rejoinder
The defendant filed a detailed rejoinder challenging each point made in the replication.
The judge reviewed both the replication and the rejoinder before making a ruling.
Under common law, a rejoinder must address the specific allegations raised by the plaintiff.
