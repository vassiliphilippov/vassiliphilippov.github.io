rebellion

noun
refusal to accept authority or rules
Pronunciation: / rɪˈbɛljən / (AmE), / rɪˈbɛljən / (BrE)
an act of refusing to obey authority, control, or tradition; open resistance against established rules, customs, or power

Examples

Teen rebellion
Her decision to dye her hair bright green was seen as an act of rebellion by her conservative parents.
His constant questioning of authority was interpreted as a quiet rebellion at work.
The spirit of rebellion was evident among the students protesting for change.

Related Terms

  • synonym: revolt - open resistance and refusal to obey authority
  • antonym: submission - the act of accepting authority or yielding to control
