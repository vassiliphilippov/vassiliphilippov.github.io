milk

noun
nutritive, milky liquid from animals or plants
Pronunciation: / mɪlk / (AmE), / mɪlk / (BrE)
a white liquid produced by female mammals to feed their young, or similar liquids from plants (such as coconut milk), used as food or drink

Examples

A glass of milk
Coconut milk is a common ingredient in Southeast Asian cuisine.
Many people use almond milk as a dairy alternative in their coffee.
Some infant formulas are based on goat's milk instead of cow's milk.
Goats, cows, and humans all produce milk for their offspring.
Many mammals depend on milk to nourish their young during the early stages of life.
She poured a glass of cold milk to have with her cookies.
Cows are raised on the farm primarily for their milk.
Would you like some milk in your tea or coffee?
Young mammals rely on their mother's milk for nourishment.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: cow's milk - milk obtained from cows, most commonly consumed
  • synonym: cream - thick, fatty part of milk that rises to the top
  • degree lower: skimmed milk - milk with most of the cream removed
  • degree higher: soy milk - milky liquid made from soybeans
  • antonym: water - clear, colorless, odorless, and tasteless liquid, opposite of the white milk
