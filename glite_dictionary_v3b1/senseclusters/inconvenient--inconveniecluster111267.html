inconvenient

adjective
causing trouble or difficulty
1.0 4.1
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.kənˈviː.ni.ənt /; / ˌɪnkənˈviːniənt / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kənˈviː.ni.ənt /; / ˌɪnkənˈviːniənt / (BrE)
causing trouble, difficulty, or discomfort; not suited to someone's needs or comfort

Examples

An inconvenient time
He apologized for making such an inconvenient request at the last minute.
The road closure was very inconvenient for local residents trying to get to work.
Their flight was scheduled at an inconvenient time early in the morning.
An inconvenient location made it hard for guests to attend the party.
It would be inconvenient for me to meet at that time, as I have another appointment.
The store's limited hours are really inconvenient for people who work late.
