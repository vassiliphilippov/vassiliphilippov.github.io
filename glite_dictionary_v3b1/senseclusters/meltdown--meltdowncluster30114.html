meltdown

noun
catastrophic collapse or failure
5.8 4.61/m

a sudden and disastrous failure, especially one that is catastrophic or total in nature

Examples

Financial meltdown
After several technical errors, the company experienced a complete meltdown.
Analysts fear that a political meltdown could destabilize the country.
The financial meltdown of 2008 had global consequences.

  • collapse
    4.1 28/m
    a collapse is a sudden falling or breakdown of a structure or system, while a meltdown is usually total and disastrous.
  • catastrophe
    7.0 11/m
    a catastrophe is a sudden terrible event causing wide damage, while a meltdown focuses on total failure often of a system.
  • disaster
    1.0 68/m
    a disaster is an event causing great damage or loss, while a meltdown refers to a catastrophic, often complete, failure.
  • breakdown
    4.4 6.30/m
    a breakdown is when a system stops working, often slowly or in parts, while a meltdown is sudden and total.
  • calamity
    6.7 3.78/m
    a calamity is a severe sudden event that brings pain or loss to people, while a meltdown highlights total system failure.
  • cataclysm
    9.2 0.98/m
    a cataclysm is a violent event that reshapes nature or society, while a meltdown is a sudden catastrophic failure of something.
