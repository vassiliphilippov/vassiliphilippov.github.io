tributary

noun
a stream flowing into a larger one
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɪbjəˌtɛri / (AmE), / ˈtrɪbjʊtəri / (BrE)
a river or stream that flows into a larger river or lake

Examples

A tributary river
Flooding occurred when the tributary overflowed its banks after heavy rain.
The Amazon River has many tributaries feeding into it from the rainforest.
The town is situated at the junction of the main river and a small tributary.

Related Terms

  • antonym: distributary - a branch of a river that flows away from the main stream
