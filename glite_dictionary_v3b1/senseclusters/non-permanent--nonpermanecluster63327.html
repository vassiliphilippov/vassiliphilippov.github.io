non-permanent

adjective
temporary; not lasting
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈpɜrmənənt / (AmE), / nɒnˈpɜːmənənt / (BrE)
not lasting or intended to last for a long time; temporary

Examples

The organization offered non-permanent contracts to seasonal employees.
She used a non-permanent marker to write on the whiteboard.
Many students found non-permanent housing while searching for a long-term place to stay.
