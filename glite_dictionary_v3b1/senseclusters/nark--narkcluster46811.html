nark

verb
to inform or spy for the police
9.9
Pronunciation: / nɑrk / (AmE), / nɑːk / (BrE)
to inform or act as a spy, especially for the police; to betray someone's secrets to the authorities

Examples

To nark on friends
He threatened to hurt anyone who tried to nark on him to the police.
If you nark, you'll lose everyone's trust.
The gang suspected that someone had narked about their plans.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grass - to inform or act as a police informer, especially in British slang
  • synonym: snitch - to secretly inform on someone, especially to the police
  • synonym: tip off - to give secret information to the authorities
