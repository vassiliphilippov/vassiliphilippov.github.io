Nicene Creed

noun
formal Christian statement of faith
8.8
Pronunciation: / naɪˈsiːn ˈkriːd / (AmE), / naɪˈsiːn ˈkriːd / (BrE)
a formal statement of Christian faith, adopted by the first Council of Nicaea in 325 and expanded in later councils, defining key doctrines of Christianity

Examples

Many Christian denominations include the Nicene Creed in their regular worship.
The Nicene Creed affirms the fundamental doctrines of Christianity.
