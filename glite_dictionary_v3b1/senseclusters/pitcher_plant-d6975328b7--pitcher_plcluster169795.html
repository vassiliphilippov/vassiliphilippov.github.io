pitcher plant

noun
carnivorous plant that traps insects
3.9 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈpɪtʃ.ər plænt/ (AmE), /ˈpɪtʃ.ə plɑːnt/ (BrE)
A carnivorous plant with a deep cavity filled with liquid, used to trap and digest insects, found especially in the family Sarraceniaceae and related families.

Examples

A pitcher plant trap
A pitcher plant can survive in nutrient-poor soils by absorbing nutrients from captured insects.
Scientists study the digestive fluids of the pitcher plant to understand how it breaks down its prey.
The pitcher plant lures insects with its nectar before trapping them in its tubular leaf.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carnivorous plant - plant that derives some or most nutrients by trapping and consuming animals
