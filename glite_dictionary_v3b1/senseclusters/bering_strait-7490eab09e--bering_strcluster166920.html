Bering Strait

noun
strait between Russia and Alaska
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛrɪŋ streɪt / (AmE), / ˈbɛərɪŋ streɪt / (BrE)
the narrow strait connecting the Bering Sea with the Arctic Ocean, separating eastern Russia and Alaska

Examples

The Bering Strait crossing
Many scientists believe humans migrated to North America via the Bering Strait during the last Ice Age.
Ships crossing the Bering Strait must navigate challenging weather conditions throughout the year.
The Bering Strait is only about 85 kilometers wide at its narrowest point.
