calligram

noun
text forming an image
Pronunciation: / ˈkælɪˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈkælɪɡræm / (BrE)
a piece of text arranged so that its layout forms a visual image related to its meaning

Examples

A calligram picture
The artist designed a calligram where the words formed the outline of a bird.
In her project, she used a calligram to visually represent the theme of the poem.
Children enjoyed creating a calligram shaped like a sun for their art class.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acrostic - a poem in which certain letters form a word or message
  • synonym: concrete poetry - poetry in which the arrangement of words forms a visual image
