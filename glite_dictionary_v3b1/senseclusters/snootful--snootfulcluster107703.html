snootful

noun
severe reprimand
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsnuːtfʊl / (AmE), / ˈsnuːtfʊl / (BrE)
a sharp scolding or severe reprimand, often delivered in a direct or harsh manner

Examples

A snootful from the boss
After missing the deadline, she received a snootful from her manager in front of the team.
He got a real snootful from his coach after arriving late to practice.
If you keep ignoring the rules, expect a snootful from the supervisor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dressing-down - a strong verbal reprimand
  • antonym: praise - an expression of approval or admiration
  • synonym: rebuke - an expression of sharp disapproval or criticism
