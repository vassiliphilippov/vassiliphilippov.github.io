Synonyms

  • persuade
    2.7 27/m
    persuade uses reasons to change someone's view or action, while convince focuses on making someone believe or accept an idea.
  • bring round
    4.4 0.01/m
    bring round is a friendly way to persuade someone in person, while convince is a general term for making someone accept an idea.
  • prevail upon
    7.7 0.33/m
    prevail upon persuades someone after effort when they resist, while convince can work by clear argument or evidence.
  • win over
    3.3 3.17/m
    win over slowly gains someone's support or loyalty, while convince aims to make someone believe or agree.
  • sway
    3.6 4.33/m
    sway influences feelings or opinion without full reasons, while convince tries to make someone believe with clear reasons.
  • induce
    4.9 6.33/m
    induce sounds formal and can mean cause or lead to action, while convince focuses on persuading belief or agreement.
  • talk into
    3.0 5.33/m
    talk into is an informal phrasal verb used in speech, while convince is more general and fits both speech and writing.
  • get
    1.8 1171/m
    get is a casual causative used to make someone do something, while convince is about making someone believe or accept.
  • sell
    2.0 44/m
    sell often means persuading someone to buy or accept an idea, while convince is a general word for making someone believe or comply.
  • prevail
    6.7 2.56/m
    prevail often means to win or succeed and appears in 'prevail upon', while convince means making someone believe or accept.
  • change someone's mind
    3.0 0.95/m
