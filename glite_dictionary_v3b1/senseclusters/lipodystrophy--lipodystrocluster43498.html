lipodystrophy

noun
abnormal fat distribution
Pronunciation: / ˌlɪpoʊˈdɪstrəfi / (AmE), / ˌlɪpəʊˈdɪstrəfi / (BrE)
a medical condition where the body has an abnormal or uneven distribution of fat tissue

Examples

Body lipodystrophy
Some patients with HIV develop lipodystrophy as a side effect of their medication.
Doctors monitor changes in fat distribution to diagnose lipodystrophy early.
Children with congenital lipodystrophy may require special medical care.
Repeated insulin injections can sometimes cause lipodystrophy at the injection site.
Doctors monitor patients for signs of lipodystrophy when they are on long-term medication.
The nurse explained how to rotate injection sites to help prevent lipodystrophy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lipoatrophy - loss of fat tissue in certain areas of the body
  • synonym: lipohypertrophy - localized accumulation of fat tissue
  • antonym: obesity - excessive accumulation of body fat
