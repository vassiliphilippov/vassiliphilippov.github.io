mineral wax

noun
hydrocarbon wax from minerals, used in products
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪnərəl wæks / (AmE), / ˈmɪnərəl wæks / (BrE)
a naturally occurring waxy substance, consisting mainly of hydrocarbons, found in association with petroleum and used in products like ceresin and candles

Examples

Purified mineral wax
Ceresin is produced from purified mineral wax.
Some candles are made from mineral wax rather than beeswax or soy.
The lab analyzed a sample of mineral wax taken from an oil field.

Related Terms

  • synonym: paraffin wax - a common type of mineral wax derived from petroleum, used in candles and coatings
