recalculation

noun
the act of calculating again
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌkælkjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌkælkjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of calculating something again, especially to correct errors or include new information

Examples

A recalculation of budget
A recalculation of the budget revealed unexpected discrepancies.
After spotting an error, the engineer performed a recalculation of the figures.
Due to new evidence, the recalculation produced a more accurate result.
