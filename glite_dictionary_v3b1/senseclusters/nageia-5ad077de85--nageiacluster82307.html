Nageia

noun
genus of Asian evergreen trees
9.9
Pronunciation: / nəˈdʒiːə / (AmE), / nəˈdʒiːə / (BrE)
A small genus of Asian evergreen trees with columnar crowns and leaves lacking a midrib, found in eastern Asia including India, the Philippines, and New Guinea.

Examples

Nageia species found
Botanists discovered a rare species belonging to the genus Nageia in New Guinea.
Several forest reserves in Southeast Asia are home to Nageia evergreens.
The Nageia trees are notable for their unique leaves without a central midrib.
