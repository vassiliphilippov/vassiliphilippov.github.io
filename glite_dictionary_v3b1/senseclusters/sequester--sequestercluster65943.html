sequester

verb
take possession by legal authority
7.5 6.8
Pronunciation: /sɪ.ˈkwes.tər/ /; / /sə.ˈkwes.tər/ (AmE), /sɪ.ˈkwes.tə/ /; / /sə.ˈkwes.tə/ (BrE)
to take temporary legal possession of property by authority or court order

Examples

To sequester property
Authorities decided to sequester the disputed land until the ownership is resolved.
The court may sequester the defendant's assets during the trial.
The government can sequester property that is involved in criminal investigations.
During the war, the government decided to sequester the land owned by enemy nationals.
If a business fails to pay its debts, authorities may sequester its assets until the dispute is resolved.
The judge ordered the court to sequester the disputed funds while the case was under review.

Related Terms

  • synonym: confiscate - to take property with authority, especially as a penalty
  • synonym: impound - to take legal custody of something, especially by law enforcement
