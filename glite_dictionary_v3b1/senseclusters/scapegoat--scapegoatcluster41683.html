scapegoat

verb
to unfairly blame
Pronunciation: / ˈskeɪpˌɡoʊt / (AmE), / ˈskeɪpɡəʊt / (BrE)
to unfairly blame someone for problems or mistakes, especially to avoid personal responsibility

Examples

To scapegoat an employee
The manager scapegoated his assistant to avoid taking responsibility for the failed project.
After the error was discovered, she was unfairly scapegoated by her colleagues.
It is wrong to scapegoat one person for a problem caused by many.
The manager tried to scapegoat his assistant when the project failed.
