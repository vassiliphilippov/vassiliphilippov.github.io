deviate

verb
cause to turn from an expected course
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈdiːviˌeɪt /; / ˈdiːvieɪt /; / ˈdiː.viˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈdiːvɪeɪt /; / ˈdiː.vɪ.eɪt / (BrE)
to turn aside or depart from a course, standard, or norm

Examples

To deviate from rules
Authorities plan to deviate traffic onto a new bypass during the festival.
Engineers deviated the stream to protect the village from flooding.
The construction work deviated the path of the hiking trail away from its original route.
Drivers who deviate from the route risk getting lost.
Her opinions often deviate from the group consensus.
If you deviate from the instructions, the result may not be as expected.
If you deviate too much from standard procedures, mistakes are likely to happen.
She tends to deviate from the original recipe when she cooks.
The train will deviate from its usual route due to construction work.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conform - to act in accordance with rules, standards, or laws
  • synonym: depart - to leave a usual path, route, or direction
  • synonym: diverge - to move or extend in different directions from a common point
  • synonym: divert - to cause someone or something to change direction
  • synonym: veer - to change direction suddenly
