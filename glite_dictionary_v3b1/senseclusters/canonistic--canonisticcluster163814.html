canonistic

adjective
of church law
Pronunciation: / ˌkænəˈnɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌkænəˈnɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of canon law or the rules of the church

Examples

Canonistic law
The professor gave a canonistic interpretation of the church's historical decisions.
Her research focused on the canonistic traditions that shaped medieval Europe.
A canonistic approach was necessary to understand the legal structure of the early church.

Related Terms

  • antonym: secular - not connected with religious or church matters
