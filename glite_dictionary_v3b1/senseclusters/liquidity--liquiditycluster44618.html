liquidity

noun
ability to convert assets to cash
Pronunciation: / lɪˈkwɪdəti / (AmE), / lɪˈkwɪdɪti / (BrE)
The quality of being able to be quickly and easily converted into cash; the ability to meet short-term financial obligations.

Examples

Bank liquidity crisis
Banks monitor their liquidity to ensure they can handle withdrawals.
During the crisis, many firms struggled with liquidity issues.
The company improved its liquidity by selling some of its assets.

Related Terms

  • antonym: illiquidity - the state of not being easily converted into cash
  • synonym: solvency - ability to meet all long-term financial obligations
