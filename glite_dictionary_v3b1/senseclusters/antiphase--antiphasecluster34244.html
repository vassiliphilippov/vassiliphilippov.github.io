antiphase

adjective
180-degree phase difference
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈæntiˌfeɪz / (AmE), / ˈæntiˌfeɪz / (BrE)
having a phase difference of 180 degrees with another wave or signal, resulting in being exactly out of phase

Examples

Antiphase sound waves
When the two sound waves are in antiphase, they cancel each other out, creating silence.
The engineer adjusted the signals so that they were perfectly antiphase for the experiment.
Destructive interference occurs when light waves are in antiphase with each other.

Related Terms

  • antonym: in phase - having the same phase or phase difference of 0 degrees
  • synonym: out of phase - not in phase; having a phase difference, often less than 180 degrees
