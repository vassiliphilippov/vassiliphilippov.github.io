absolver

noun
person who grants absolution
7.3
Pronunciation: / əbˈzɑlvər / (AmE), / əbˈzɒlvə / (BrE)
a person who grants absolution, especially in a religious context

Examples

The absolver grants forgiveness
As the absolver, she bore the responsibility of declaring sins forgiven during confession.
Only a qualified absolver could grant sacramental absolution in that community.
The priest acted as an absolver for the penitent sinners who sought forgiveness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: confessor - a priest who hears confessions and grants absolution
