excavate

verb
to form by hollowing out
5.4 4.8
Pronunciation: /ˈek.skə.veɪt/ (AmE), /ˈek.skə.veɪt/ (BrE)
to form a space or hole by hollowing out; to remove earth or material to create a cavity or depression

Examples

To excavate a hole
Over centuries, the river gradually excavated a deep gorge through the landscape.
The workers used heavy machinery to excavate a large pit for the new building foundation.
They plan to excavate a tunnel through the mountains to improve transportation.
