draw

verb
to execute by pulling apart with horses
4.2
Pronunciation: / drɔ / (AmE), / drɔː / (BrE)
(historical) To pull (a person) apart by tying their limbs to horses and forcing them in opposite directions, as a method of execution.

Examples

Drawn and quartered
In medieval England, traitors were sometimes drawn and quartered as punishment for their crimes.
The king ordered the rebel to be drawn for his attempt to seize the throne.
They drew the condemned man as part of a brutal public execution.
