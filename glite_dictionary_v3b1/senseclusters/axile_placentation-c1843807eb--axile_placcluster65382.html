axile placentation

noun
central ovule arrangement in compound ovary
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈæk.saɪl plə.sen.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ˈæk.sɪl plə.sen.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ˈæk.saɪl plæs.ən.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈæk.saɪl plə.sen.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ˈæk.sɪl plə.sen.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ˈæk.saɪl plæs.ən.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a type of placentation in which ovules are attached at or around the center of a compound ovary, on an axis formed by the joined septa

Examples

Axile placentation in ovary
Botany students often compare axile placentation with parietal placentation to understand floral anatomy.
In lilies, axile placentation is found, where ovules develop along a central axis.
The fruit of the tomato plant also displays axile placentation in its ovary structure.

Related Terms

  • antonym: free central placentation - ovules attached to a central column not connected by septa
  • antonym: parietal placentation - ovules attached to the ovary wall rather than a central axis
