pull out of the deal

verb
withdraw from an agreement
Pronunciation: / pʊl aʊt əv ðə diːl / (AmE), / pʊl aʊt əv ðə diːl / (BrE)
to withdraw from an agreement or arrangement, especially after previously agreeing to participate

Examples

To pull out of the deal
Negotiations broke down, and he was forced to pull out of the deal.
The company decided to pull out of the deal after reviewing the financial risks.
We were ready to sign, but our partners suddenly pulled out of the deal.
