snap

verb
pass ball backward to start play (US football)
6.7 5.8
Pronunciation: /snæp/ (AmE), /snæp/ (BrE)
(in American football) to put the ball into play by quickly passing it backward between the legs to a teammate, typically the quarterback.

Examples

To snap the ball
He snapped the ball quickly, catching the defense off guard.
The center will snap the ball as soon as the quarterback calls for it.
You must learn how to snap the football accurately in practice.
