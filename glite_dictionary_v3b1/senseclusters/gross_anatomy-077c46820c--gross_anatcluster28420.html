gross anatomy

noun
study of body structures without a microscope
7.1 3.4
Pronunciation: /groʊs ə.ˈnæt.ə.miː/ (AmE), /grəʊs ə.ˈnæt.ə.miː/ (BrE)
the study of the structure of the body and its parts as seen with the naked eye, without the use of a microscope

Examples

Gross anatomy study
First-year medical students often take a course in gross anatomy to learn about the human body.
The surgeon relied on knowledge of gross anatomy to navigate the procedure without damaging vital organs.
Unlike microscopic anatomy, gross anatomy does not require a microscope to examine tissues.
