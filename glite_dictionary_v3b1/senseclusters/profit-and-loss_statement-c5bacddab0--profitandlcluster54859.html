profit-and-loss statement

noun
company's financial performance summary

A financial statement summarizing a company's revenues, expenses, and profits or losses over a particular period.

Examples

The profit-and-loss statement
Auditors reviewed the profit-and-loss statement to assess the firm's financial health.
Before approving the loan, the bank requested the latest profit-and-loss statement from the business owner.
The company's annual profit-and-loss statement showed a significant increase in revenue compared to the previous year.

Synonyms

  • income statement
    3.6 0.45/m
    income statement names the formal report of a company's revenues and expenses, while profit-and-loss statement refers to the same period summary document.
  • profit and loss
    3.4 0.17/m
    profit and loss is the financial statement showing revenues and expenses at period end, while profit-and-loss statement is the formal name for that report.
  • profit and loss account
    4.7 0.00/m
    profit and loss account is an end-of-period account showing gross and net profit, while profit-and-loss statement is the full period summary document.
