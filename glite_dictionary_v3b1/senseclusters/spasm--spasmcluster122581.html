spasm

noun
sudden muscular constriction (medical)
Pronunciation: / ˈspæzəm / (AmE), / ˈspazəm / (BrE)
a sudden, involuntary contraction or tightening of a muscle or organ, often causing pain

Examples

A muscle spasm
A coronary artery spasm can restrict blood flow to the heart.
During the procedure, the patient experienced a brief intestinal spasm.
Migraine headaches are sometimes triggered by a spasm of blood vessels in the brain.
A sudden spasm in her leg woke her up in the middle of the night.
He suffered a painful spasm in his lower back after lifting the heavy box.
If you experience a muscle spasm, gently stretch the affected area to relieve the pain.
