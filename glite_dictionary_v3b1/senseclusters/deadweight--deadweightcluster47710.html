deadweight

noun
burdensome load
5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈded.weɪt/ (AmE), /ˈded.weɪt/ (BrE)
a heavy load or burden that slows down progress or movement, either physically or figuratively

Examples

A deadweight on progress
After his injury, he felt like a deadweight on the team.
Carrying unnecessary equipment became a deadweight during the long hike.
The outdated software acted as a deadweight on the company's productivity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: asset - something valuable or useful
  • synonym: burden - something that is difficult to carry or deal with
  • synonym: encumbrance - something that makes movement or progress difficult
