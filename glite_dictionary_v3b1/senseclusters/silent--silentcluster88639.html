silent

adjective
not pronounced (of a letter)
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪlənt / (AmE), / ˈsaɪlənt / (BrE)
(of a letter) written but not pronounced in speech.

Examples

Silent letter
In the word 'psychology', the 'p' is silent.
Many English words borrowed from French contain a silent letter at the end.
The silent 'k' in 'knight' often confuses young readers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: voiced - (of a letter) pronounced with vocal cord vibration
