tularemia

noun
infectious disease affecting rodents; transmissible to humans
6.3 9.2
Pronunciation: /tjuː.lə.ˈriː.mɪ.ə/ /; / /tuː.lə.ˈriː.mɪ.ə/ (AmE), /tjuː.lə.ˈriː.mɪ.ə/ /; / /tuː.lə.ˈriː.mɪ.ə/ (BrE)
A highly infectious bacterial disease mainly affecting wild rodents, especially rabbits and squirrels, that can be transmitted to humans by ticks, flies, or by handling or contact with infected animals.
