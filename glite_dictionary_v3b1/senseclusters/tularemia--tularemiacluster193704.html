tularemia

noun
infectious disease affecting rodents; transmissible to humans
Pronunciation: / ˌtuːləˈriːmiə / (AmE), / ˌtjuːləˈriːmiə / (BrE)
A highly infectious bacterial disease mainly affecting wild rodents, especially rabbits and squirrels, that can be transmitted to humans by ticks, flies, or by handling or contact with infected animals.

Examples

Tularemia infection
After being bitten by a tick, she developed symptoms of tularemia and required hospitalization.
Laboratory workers must use special precautions when studying tularemia due to its high infectivity.
The outbreak of tularemia prompted local authorities to warn hunters about handling wild rabbits.
Cases of tularaemia have been reported after people handled wild rabbits.
He contracted tularaemia after being bitten by an infected tick while hiking.
Public health officials warn of increased tularaemia risk during the summer months.
