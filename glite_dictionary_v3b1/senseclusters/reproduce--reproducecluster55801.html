reproduce

verb
to recreate or copy something such as a sound or image
4.0 3.59/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀê.pr…ôÀàdus / (AmE), / ÀåriÀê.pr…ôÀàdjuÀês / (BrE)
to create again or make a copy of a sound, image, idea, mood, or atmosphere

Examples

To reproduce a sound
It is difficult to reproduce the warm atmosphere of her old studio in a new space.
The artist managed to reproduce the intricate patterns of the tapestry in his drawing.
This state-of-the-art speaker can reproduce the subtle notes of a live concert.

Synonyms

  • duplicate
    3.5 6.29/m
    duplicate makes an exact copy or repeats something in the same way, while reproduce can mean creating copies or replaying sounds.
  • replicate
    4.9 6.03/m
    replicate makes a precise, often scientific copy under controlled conditions, while reproduce is broader and fits many sounds, images, or moods.
  • copy
    1.0 17/m
    copy makes a close or exact version of a document, image, or text, while reproduce has a wider sense for sounds, ideas, and moods.
  • recreate
    4.5 5.86/m
    recreate makes something again or imitates it, often with change or skill, while reproduce usually means making a true copy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: copy - to make a thing that is similar or identical to another
  • synonym: replicate - to make an exact copy of; to duplicate
