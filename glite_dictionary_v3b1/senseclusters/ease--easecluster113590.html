ease

verb
make pain or discomfort less severe
6.1
Pronunciation: / iːz / (AmE), / iːz / (BrE)
to make something less severe, difficult, or troubling; to relieve, calm, or make easier

Examples

To ease the pain
A hot bath may help to ease sore muscles after exercise.
Meditation can ease feelings of anxiety and stress.
The doctor gave her medicine to ease her back pain.
He tried to ease her concerns by explaining the situation clearly.
Meditation can ease stress and anxiety after a long day.
The new software is designed to ease the workload for teachers.
His apology did little to ease her disappointment after the error.
The gentle music helped to ease her anxiety before the interview.
They introduced new policies to ease the burden on families.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alleviate - to make pain or a problem less severe
  • synonym: facilitate - to make an action or process easier
  • antonym: impede - to hinder or obstruct progress
  • antonym: intensify - to make something more intense or severe
  • synonym: relieve - to reduce or remove pain, worry, or difficulty
