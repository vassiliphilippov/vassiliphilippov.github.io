match

verb
bring suitable people/things together
1.0 1.8
Pronunciation: /mætʃ/ (AmE), /mætʃ/ (BrE)
to bring together or arrange people or things that are suitable for each other (including jobs, marriage, or competition)

Examples

To match people
Online platforms can match job seekers to suitable employers quickly.
The program aims to match volunteers with families in need of help.
The teacher tried to match each student with an appropriate mentor.
In olden times, it was common for parents to match their children for political alliances.
The queen hoped to match her daughter with a prince from a noble family.
They were matched in a quiet ceremony attended only by close friends and relatives.
The coach decided to match their top sprinter against the reigning champion in the finals.
The game show often matches contestants with similar skills to make the competition fair.
We should match our best debater against their team leader to increase our chances of winning.

  • antonym: separate - to move or keep people or things apart
