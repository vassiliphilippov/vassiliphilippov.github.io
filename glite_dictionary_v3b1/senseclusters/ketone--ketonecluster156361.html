ketone

noun
organic compound with carbonyl group
6.4 5.2
Pronunciation: /ˈkiː.təʊn/ /; / /ˈkiː.toʊn/ (AmE), /ˈkiː.təʊn/ (BrE)
a type of chemical compound with a carbonyl group bonded to two carbon atoms; in the body, ketones are produced when fat is broken down for energy

Examples

A ketone compound
Acetone is the simplest ketone and is commonly used as a solvent.
Many fragrances contain ketone compounds to enhance their scent.
The presence of ketones in urine may indicate diabetes or fasting.
When the body lacks carbohydrates, it starts producing ketones for energy.
Doctors may test for ketone levels in patients with diabetes.
A high-fat diet can increase the amount of ketones in the bloodstream.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acetone - a simple ketone used as a solvent
  • antonym: aldehyde - organic compound with a carbonyl group bonded to at least one hydrogen atom
  • antonym: glucose - a simple sugar used by the body for energy
