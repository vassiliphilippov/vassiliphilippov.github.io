hematite

noun
a mineral form of iron ore
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛməˌtaɪt /; / ˈhiːməˌtaɪt / (AmE), / ˈhiːməˌtaɪt /; / ˈhiːmətaɪt / (BrE)
a reddish-black mineral, the principal ore of iron, consisting of ferric oxide in crystalline or earthy form

Examples

Miners extracted hematite from the site to supply the steel industry.

Jewellery made of polished haematite has a metallic sheen and deep color.
The miners extracted haematite from the ancient quarry, using it to produce iron.
You can easily identify haematite by its reddish streak when scraped on a tile.
