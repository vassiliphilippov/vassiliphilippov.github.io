sociobiology

noun
biology of social behavior and evolution
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˌsoʊsiˌoʊbaɪˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌsəʊsiəʊbaɪˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the scientific study of the biological basis of social behavior in animals and humans, especially in terms of evolutionary theory

Examples

Sociobiology studies behavior
Sociobiology explores how evolutionary forces shape social behaviors in both animals and humans.
Many scientists debate the implications of sociobiology for understanding human culture and society.
The field of sociobiology was popularized by E.O. Wilson in the 1970s.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ethology - the scientific study of animal behavior, typically under natural conditions
