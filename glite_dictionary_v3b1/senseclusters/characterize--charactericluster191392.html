characterize

verb
be a typical feature of
1.0 3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈkærɪktəˌraɪz /; / kəˈræk.tə.raɪz /; / ˈkɛrəktəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈkærɪktəraɪz /; / ˈkærəktəraɪz /; / kəˈræk.tə.raɪz / (BrE)
to be or describe the typical or distinctive qualities or features of someone or something

Examples

To characterize a person
A strong sense of community characterizes life in the small town.
Openness and honesty characterize her approach to teaching.
The 19th century characterized itself by major technological innovations.
Historians often characterize the period as one of rapid social change.
It is unfair to characterize her as lazy based on one incident.
The city's architecture can be characterized by its ornate facades and narrow streets.
A love of freedom characterises the nation's culture.
The region is characterised by its rocky landscape and dry climate.
What really characterises her paintings is their vibrant use of color.
