immunochemical assay

noun
method for detecting substances using antigen-antibody reactions
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˌɪmjunoʊˈkɛmɪkəl əˈseɪ / (AmE), / ˌɪmjʊənəʊˈkɛmɪkəl əˈseɪ / (BrE)
a laboratory test for identifying or measuring a substance, especially a protein, by using its reaction as an antigen with specific antibodies

Examples

An immunochemical assay test
An immunochemical assay can help diagnose certain diseases by identifying specific proteins.
Researchers rely on the sensitivity of an immunochemical assay to track changes in biomarker levels.
The laboratory used an immunochemical assay to detect the presence of hormones in the patient's blood.

Related Terms

  • synonym: immunoassay - laboratory technique that uses antibodies to detect or quantify substances
