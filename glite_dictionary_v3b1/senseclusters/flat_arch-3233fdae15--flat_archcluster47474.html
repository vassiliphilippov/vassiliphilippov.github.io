flat arch

noun
straight arched architectural element
5.2 6.3
Pronunciation: /flæt ɑːrtʃ/ (AmE), /flæt ɑːtʃ/ (BrE)
an architectural arch with a straight horizontal upper and lower surface, consisting of mutually supporting wedge-shaped stones (voussoirs)

Examples

A flat arch doorway
The doorway was topped by a flat arch made of carefully cut bricks.
The restoration revealed the original flat arch above the old shop window.
Unlike traditional Roman arches, a flat arch does not curve but relies on the precise placement of its stones.
