heterotroph

noun
organism that consumes organic nutrition
1.4
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛtəroʊˌtroʊf / (AmE), / ˈhɛtərətrəʊf / (BrE)
An organism that obtains its nutrition by consuming complex organic substances, rather than producing its own food.

Examples

A heterotroph organism
Many bacteria are heterotrophs, deriving nutrients from organic matter in their environment.
The ecosystem includes both heterotrophs and autotrophs, each fulfilling a distinct role.
Unlike plants, all animals are heterotrophs and rely on eating other organisms for energy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: autotroph - organism that produces its own food from inorganic substances
