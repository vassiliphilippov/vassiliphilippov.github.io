interdiffusion

noun
mutual diffusion process
8.9
Pronunciation: /ɪn.tər.dɪ.ˈfjuː.ʒən/ (AmE), /ɪn.tə.dɪ.ˈfjuː.ʒən/ (BrE)
the process by which two substances diffuse into each other when they are in contact

Examples

The interdiffusion of gases
The interdiffusion of gases in the laboratory was measured over several hours.
Engineers study interdiffusion to improve the strength of metal alloys.
Temperature can greatly influence the rate of interdiffusion between two materials.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diffusion - the movement of particles from an area of higher concentration to lower concentration
