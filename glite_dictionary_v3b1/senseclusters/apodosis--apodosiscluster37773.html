apodosis

noun
result in conditionals
Pronunciation: / əˈpɑːdəsɪs / (AmE), / əˈpɒdəsɪs / (BrE)
the part of a conditional sentence that states the result or consequence if the condition is met

Examples

The apodosis part
In the sentence 'If you study, you will pass,' the apodosis is 'you will pass.'
Teachers often ask students to identify the apodosis in conditional sentences.
The apodosis usually follows the protasis in English grammar.
In the sentence 'If it rains, we will cancel the picnic,' the apodosis is 'we will cancel the picnic.'
Teachers often ask students to identify the apodosis in conditional sentences during grammar lessons.
The apodosis usually follows the word 'then' in formal logic statements.
