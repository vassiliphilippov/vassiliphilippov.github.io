electrum

noun
alloy of gold and silver
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɛktrəm / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrəm / (BrE)
A natural or artificial alloy of gold and silver, often with trace amounts of other metals, used especially in ancient times for coins and jewelry.

Examples

Ancient electrum coins
Ancient coins were often made of electrum, prized for its pale yellow color.
Scientists analyzed the electrum composition to better understand ancient mining techniques.
The museum displayed a cup crafted from electrum dating back to the Lydian era.
