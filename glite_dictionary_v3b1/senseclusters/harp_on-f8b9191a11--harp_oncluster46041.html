harp on

verb
to repeat complaints
6.7
Pronunciation: / hɑrp ɑn / (AmE), / hɑːp ɒn / (BrE)
to talk or complain about something repeatedly in a way that becomes annoying

Examples

To harp on mistakes
She tends to harp on her past mistakes instead of looking forward.
I wish you would stop harping on the same issue every day.
They kept harping on about the unfair decision long after the meeting ended.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dwell on - to keep thinking or talking about something for a long time
  • antonym: move on - to stop talking or thinking about something and focus on something else
