equivalent weight

noun
chemistry: combining capacity of an element or compound
8.1 0.01/m

the quantity of an element or compound that will combine with or displace a fixed amount of another element or compound, usually measured in relation to hydrogen or oxygen; in chemistry, often defined as the atomic or molecular weight divided by the valence.

Examples

The equivalent weight in chemistry
In titration calculations, accurate knowledge of the equivalent weight is essential.
The equivalent weight of an acid determines how much of it will react with a base.
To find the equivalent weight of a metal, you divide its atomic weight by its valence.

Synonyms

  • combining weight
    8.8 0.01/m
    combining weight is the historical term for the atomic amount that combines with a standard like oxygen, while equivalent weight is the modern equivalent based on valence.
  • equivalent
    8.1 0.94/m
    equivalent is the amount of substance used in reactions, while equivalent weight names the quantity calculated from atomic or molecular mass and valence.
  • gram equivalent
    7.4 0.00/m
    gram equivalent is the mass in grams that combines with a set amount of another element, while equivalent weight names the same idea without specifying mass units.
