equivalent weight

noun
chemistry: combining capacity of an element or compound
Pronunciation: / ɪˈkwɪvələnt weɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈkwɪvələnt weɪt / (BrE)
the quantity of an element or compound that will combine with or displace a fixed amount of another element or compound, usually measured in relation to hydrogen or oxygen; in chemistry, often defined as the atomic or molecular weight divided by the valence.

Examples

The equivalent weight in chemistry
In titration calculations, accurate knowledge of the equivalent weight is essential.
The equivalent weight of an acid determines how much of it will react with a base.
To find the equivalent weight of a metal, you divide its atomic weight by its valence.
