polariton

noun
photon-matter quasiparticle
Pronunciation: / poʊˈlɛrɪˌtɑn / (AmE), / pəˈlærɪtɒn / (BrE)
a quasiparticle formed when photons strongly interact with excitations in a material, such as phonons or excitons

Examples

A polariton particle
Scientists observed a new type of polariton in the laboratory experiment.
The unique properties of a polariton make it important for advanced optical devices.
Researchers are exploring how polaritons can be used in quantum computing technologies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exciton - a bound state of an electron and an electron hole in a material
  • synonym: phonon - a quasiparticle representing a quantum of vibrational energy in a crystal lattice
