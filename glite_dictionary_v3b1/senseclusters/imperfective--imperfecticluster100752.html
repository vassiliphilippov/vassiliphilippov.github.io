imperfective

noun
verb aspect not specifying completion
Pronunciation: / ɪm.pɚˈfɛk.tɪv / (AmE), / ɪm.pəˈfɛk.tɪv / (BrE)
In grammar, relating to a verb aspect that expresses an action or state without specifying its beginning or completion.

Examples

The imperfective aspect
In Russian, you must choose between imperfective and perfective verb forms depending on meaning.
Many Slavic languages distinguish between the imperfective and perfective aspects in their verbs.
The imperfective aspect is used to describe ongoing or habitual actions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: perfective - aspect expressing completed action
