loyalist

noun
person loyal to a government or cause
Pronunciation: / ˈlɔɪ.ə.lɪst / (AmE), / ˈlɔɪ.ə.lɪst / (BrE)
a person who remains loyal to a government, leader, or cause, especially during a revolt or time of opposition

Examples

A loyalist supporter
During the civil war, each loyalist risked their life by supporting the old regime.
He was known as a steadfast loyalist even when many others switched sides.
The new government offered amnesty to former loyalists if they pledged their allegiance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: patriot - a person who vigorously supports their country and is prepared to defend it
  • antonym: rebel - a person who rises in opposition or armed resistance against an established government or leader
