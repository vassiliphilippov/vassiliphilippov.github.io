hypoxemia

noun
low blood oxygen
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpɑkˈsiːmiə / (AmE), / ˌhɪpɒkˈsiːmiə / (BrE)
a medical condition in which there is an abnormally low level of oxygen in the blood

Examples

Low hypoxemia level
The doctor diagnosed the patient with hypoxemia after reviewing the blood test results.
Supplemental oxygen was given to treat her hypoxemia during the emergency.
Chronic lung diseases can increase the risk of developing hypoxemia.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hyperoxemia - a condition of abnormally high oxygen in the blood
  • synonym: hypoxia - a condition of low oxygen in the body tissues
