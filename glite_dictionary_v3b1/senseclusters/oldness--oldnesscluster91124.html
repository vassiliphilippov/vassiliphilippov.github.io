oldness

noun
the quality of being old
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊld.nəs / (AmE), / ˈəʊld.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being old; having existed for a long time

Examples

The oldness of things
He admired the oldness of the manuscript, feeling connected to the past through its yellowed pages.
Sometimes, the oldness of a tradition can make it feel more meaningful and respected.
The oldness of the castle was visible in its crumbling walls and faded tapestries.
She could sense the oldness in his eyes, filled with memories of a long life.
The oldness of the manuscript made it particularly valuable to historians.
The building's oldness is evident in its weathered façade and antique details.
