ridgeline

noun
line along a ridge or hilltop
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪdʒˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈrɪdʒˌlaɪn / (BrE)
the line formed along the highest points of a range of hills or mountains; a long narrow crest or area of elevated land

Examples

A ridgeline trail
A hiking trail follows the ridgeline to provide spectacular views of the valley below.
From the summit, you could see the entire ridgeline stretching for miles.
The ancient fortress was built along the ridgeline for defensive advantage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ridge - a long narrow hilltop, mountain range, or watershed
