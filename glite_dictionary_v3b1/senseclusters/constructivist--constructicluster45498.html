constructivist

noun
active learning believer
7.5
Pronunciation: / kənˈstrʌktɪvɪst / (AmE), / kənˈstrʌktɪvɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that knowledge is constructed through experience and active exploration, rather than just absorbing information

Examples

Constructivist teaching approach
As a constructivist, she encourages students to learn by exploring real-world problems.
The constructivist approach values hands-on activities and student collaboration in the classroom.
Many modern teachers identify as constructivists and design lessons that promote active learning.
As a constructivist, she encourages students to build their own understanding through hands-on activities.
Many modern educators identify as constructivists and design lessons that promote active learning.
The constructivist argued that knowledge is not simply absorbed but created by each learner.

Related Terms

  • antonym: behaviorist - a person who believes behavior is learned through conditioning
  • synonym: empiricist - a person who believes knowledge comes primarily from sensory experience
  • synonym: progressivist - a person who supports progressive education methods
  • antonym: traditionalist - a person who prefers conventional or established methods
