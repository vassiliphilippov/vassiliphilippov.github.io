midsole

noun
shoe cushioning layer
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪdˌsoʊl / (AmE), / ˈmɪdˌsəʊl / (BrE)
the layer of material between the insole and outsole of a shoe that provides cushioning and support

Examples

Shoe midsole layer
The new sneakers feature a foam midsole that absorbs shock during running.
After months of use, the midsole started to lose its cushioning effect.
A well-designed midsole can make a big difference in comfort for athletes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: insole - the inner part of a shoe that touches the bottom of the foot
  • synonym: outsole - the outermost sole of a shoe that contacts the ground
