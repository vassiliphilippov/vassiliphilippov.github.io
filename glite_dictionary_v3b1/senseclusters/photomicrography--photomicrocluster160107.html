photomicrography

noun
microscope photography
8.4
Pronunciation: /fə.tə.maɪ.krə.ˈgræf.iː/ /; / /foʊ.tə.maɪ.krə.ˈgræf.iː/ (AmE), /fəʊ.tə.maɪ.krə.ˈgræf.iː/ (BrE)
the process of taking photographs through a microscope to capture magnified images of small objects

Examples

Photomicrography of cells
The scientist used photomicrography to document the structure of the insect's wing.
Advances in photomicrography have allowed researchers to study cells in much greater detail.
Her award-winning photomicrography revealed the hidden beauty of mineral crystals.
Advances in photomicrography have allowed scientists to study the fine details of cell structures.
The biology textbook included several stunning images created using photomicrography.
She learned the basics of photomicrography during her laboratory training at university.

Related Terms

  • antonym: macrophotography - the process of taking close-up photographs of small subjects at life-size or larger
  • synonym: microphotography - the process of making photographs of very small objects, often using a microscope
