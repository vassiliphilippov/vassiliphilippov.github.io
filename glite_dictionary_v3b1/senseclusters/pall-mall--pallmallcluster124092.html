pall-mall

noun
17th-century lawn game with mallet and ball
Pronunciation: / pælˈmæl / (AmE), / pælˈmæl / (BrE)
A lawn game popular in the 17th century, in which a wooden ball was struck with a mallet along an alley.

Examples

The pall-mall game
Archaeologists found remnants of pall-mall equipment in the old estate gardens.
During the 1600s, nobles often played pall-mall in specially prepared alleys.
The rules of pall-mall are believed to have influenced the development of croquet.
