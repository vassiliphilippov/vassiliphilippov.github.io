imagination

noun
the ability to form mental images or ideas
Pronunciation: / ɪˌmædʒəˈneɪʃən /; / ɪˌmædʒɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌmadʒɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the ability to form new ideas, images, or concepts in your mind that are not present to your senses

Examples

Vivid imagination
Children often demonstrate a vivid imagination when they play pretend games.
Great works of literature are often born from the author's imagination.
He used his imagination to solve the puzzle that no one else could.
A child's imagination can transform an empty box into a castle.
Her imagination turned the simple story into a magical adventure.
The artist's imagination soared as he created his latest masterpiece.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist
