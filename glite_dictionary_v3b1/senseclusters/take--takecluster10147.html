take

verb
cause loss or damage as a consequence
1.0
Pronunciation: / teɪk / (AmE), / teɪk / (BrE)
to claim, capture, or gain control of something (such as a place, position, or credit)

Examples

To take control
The disease eventually took her life despite all efforts to save her.
The earthquake took hundreds of lives in the region.
Years of stress took a heavy toll on his health.
After the project succeeded, they tried to take all the praise.
He always takes credit for my suggestions at work.
It is unfair to take someone else's idea without acknowledgement.
After a long siege, they finally managed to take the fortress.
During the war, several towns were quickly taken by the invading army.
The rebels planned to take the city before dawn.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adopt - to accept or take up an idea or method as one's own
  • synonym: appropriate - to take something for one's own use, often without permission
  • synonym: capture - to seize or take control of something, especially by force
  • synonym: claim - to cause the death of; to take (a life)
  • antonym: lose - to fail to keep control of something once held
