Badlands

noun
heavily eroded, barren region (U.S.)
Pronunciation: / ˈbædˌlændz / (AmE), / ˈbædˌlændz / (BrE)
A region characterized by heavily eroded, barren land with little vegetation, especially in southwestern South Dakota and northwestern Nebraska.

Examples

The Badlands region
The Badlands are famous for their unique rock formations and fossils.
The harsh climate and rugged terrain make the Badlands a challenging place for wildlife.
We drove through the Badlands National Park during our road trip.
