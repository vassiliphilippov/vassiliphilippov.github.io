slack

noun
loose part of a rope or cable
6.2 2.8
Pronunciation: /slæk/ (AmE), /slæk/ (BrE)
the part of a rope, cable, or similar object that is loose or not taut; the condition of being loose

Examples

Take up the slack
He took up the slack in the rope before starting to climb.
Pull the cable tight to remove any slack before securing it in place.
There was too much slack on the fishing line, making it harder to feel a bite.
Make sure you take up any slack in the cable before operating the winch.
The climber pulled on the slack to tighten his harness before moving up.
There was too much slack in the rope for it to support the load properly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: looseness - the property of being not tight
  • antonym: taut - stretched or pulled tight; opposite of slack (in ropes or cables)
  • antonym: tautness - the quality of being stretched tight
