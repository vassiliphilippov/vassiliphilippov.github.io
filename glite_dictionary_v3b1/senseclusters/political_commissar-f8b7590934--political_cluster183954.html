political commissar

noun
Communist Party official supervising military unit
6.8
Pronunciation: / pəˈlɪɾɪkəl ˈkɑmɪˌsɑr / (AmE), / pəˈlɪtɪkəl ˈkɒmɪˌsɑː / (BrE)
an official appointed by a Communist Party to oversee a military unit and ensure compliance with party principles and loyalty

Examples

Army political commissar
During the revolution, a political commissar was assigned to each regiment.
The political commissar ensured that all officers followed Communist ideology.
The army's political commissar attended every meeting to monitor loyalty to the party.
