clamp down on

verb
take strict action to stop something
6.7 5.3
Pronunciation: / klæmp daʊn ɑn / (AmE), / klæmp daʊn ɒn / (BrE)
to take strong or strict action to reduce or put an end to something, especially something considered undesirable or illegal

Examples

To clamp down on crime
Police are starting to clamp down on underage drinking near schools.
The city council promised to clamp down on illegal parking in busy areas.
The manager had to clamp down on employees arriving late to work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crack down on - to take severe measures against something or someone
  • antonym: go easy on - to treat with less severity or strictness
