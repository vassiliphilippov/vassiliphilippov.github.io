corticospinal tract

noun
motor nerve pathway from cortex to spinal cord
7.6
Pronunciation: /kɔːr.tɪ.koʊ.ˈspaɪ.nəl trækt/ (AmE), /kɔː.tɪ.kəʊ.ˈspaɪ.nəl trækt/ (BrE)
a bundle of nerve fibers that carries motor signals from the cerebral cortex through the brainstem to the spinal cord

Examples

The corticospinal tract bundle
Damage to the corticospinal tract can cause weakness or paralysis on one side of the body.
Doctors observed reduced function in the patient's corticospinal tract after the stroke.
The corticospinal tract plays a crucial role in voluntary motor control.

Related Terms

  • antonym: extrapyramidal system - a neural pathway involved in motor control, but outside the pyramidal (corticospinal) tract
  • synonym: pyramidal tract - another name for the corticospinal tract
