sine

noun
mathematical ratio in triangles; trigonometric function
6.2 5.4
Pronunciation: /saɪn/ (AmE), /saɪn/ (BrE)
(Mathematics) The ratio of the length of the side opposite a given angle to the length of the hypotenuse in a right-angled triangle; also, the trigonometric function of an angle.

Examples

The sine function
In the equation, the sine of 30 degrees equals one-half.
The graph of the sine function is a smooth, periodic curve.
To solve the triangle, you first need to find the sine of the angle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cosine - trigonometric function: ratio of adjacent side to hypotenuse
  • synonym: tangent - trigonometric function: ratio of opposite side to adjacent side
