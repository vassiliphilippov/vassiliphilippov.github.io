nondiscrimination

noun
fair, equal treatment
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑndɪskrɪməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnɒndɪskrɪməˈneɪʃən /; / ˌnɒndɪskrɪmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the practice or principle of treating all people fairly and equally, without bias or prejudice

Examples

Policy of nondiscrimination
The school promotes nondiscrimination by ensuring all students have equal opportunities.
Laws require nondiscrimination in hiring regardless of race, gender, or religion.
A culture of nondiscrimination helps everyone feel respected and included.
The new law promotes nondiscrimination in the workplace, ensuring everyone has the same opportunities.
International agreements often include clauses on nondiscrimination to protect minority groups.
Schools must follow strict nondiscrimination policies when admitting students.

Related Terms

  • antonym: discrimination - unfair treatment of people based on group, class, or category
  • synonym: equality - the state of being equal, especially in status or rights
  • synonym: impartiality - the quality of treating all people and groups equally
