make contact

verb
establish communication with someone
Pronunciation: / meɪk ˈkɑnˌtækt / (AmE), / meɪk ˈkɒntækt / (BrE)
to establish communication with somebody, especially for the first time or after a period without communication

Examples

To make contact with people
After moving to the new city, she tried to make contact with local community groups.
If you lose your passport abroad, you should make contact with your country's embassy immediately.
It took several attempts before the researchers could make contact with the rare tribe.
