watertight alibi

noun
impossible-to-disprove excuse or alibi
9.9 3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈwɔː.tər.taɪt ˈæ.lə.baɪ/ /; / /ˈwɑː.tər.taɪt ˈæ.lə.baɪ/ (AmE), /ˈwɔː.tə.taɪt ˈæl.ɪ.baɪ/ /; / /ˈwɑː.tə.taɪt ˈæl.ɪ.baɪ/ (BrE)
An excuse or statement of innocence that is impossible to disprove or refute.

Examples

A watertight alibi
His lawyer argued that he had a watertight alibi for the night of the robbery.
The suspect presented a watertight alibi that convinced the police he was innocent.
Without a watertight alibi, she became the prime suspect in the investigation.
