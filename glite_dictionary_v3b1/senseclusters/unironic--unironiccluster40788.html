unironic

adjective
sincere, not ironic
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnaɪˈrɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˌʌnaɪˈrɒnɪk / (BrE)
sincere or genuine, not intended as a joke or with irony

Examples

An unironic apology
She gave an unironic apology, showing she truly meant it.
His unironic enthusiasm for classic rock surprised his friends.
The review was unironic praise for the artist's latest work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: genuine - real and honest, not fake or pretended
  • antonym: ironic - using words to express the opposite of what is meant, often as a joke
  • synonym: sincere - genuine and honest, not pretending
