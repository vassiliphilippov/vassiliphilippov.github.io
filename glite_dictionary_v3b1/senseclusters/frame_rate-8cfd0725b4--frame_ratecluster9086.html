frame rate

noun
frames per second
3.4
Pronunciation: /freɪm reɪt/ (AmE), /freɪm reɪt/ (BrE)
the number of individual frames or images shown or recorded per second in video, animation, or games

Examples

High frame rate
Gamers often prefer a higher frame rate for smoother and more responsive gameplay.
The movie was filmed at a frame rate of 24 frames per second, which is standard for cinema.
If the frame rate drops too low, the animation may appear choppy or laggy.
A higher frame rate makes video look smoother, especially during fast action scenes.
Gamers often prefer monitors with a high frame rate for a better experience.
The animation studio increased the frame rate to improve the quality of their latest film.

Related Terms

  • synonym: refresh rate - the number of times a display updates per second
