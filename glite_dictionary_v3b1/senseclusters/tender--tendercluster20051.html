tender

verb
make a formal offer or payment
3.5 5.7
Pronunciation: /ˈten.dər/ (AmE), /ˈten.də/ (BrE)
to formally offer or present something, such as money, a bid, or a resignation, for acceptance

Examples

To tender a resignation

The company tendered payment for the outstanding invoice to settle the dispute.
The defendant tendered full compensation to the claimant during mediation.
She tendered her resignation along with a formal letter.
The company tendered payment in full, but the offer was declined.

Bidders are required to tender their proposals by the end of the month.

The company will tender its resignation at the shareholders' meeting.
