in and out

adverb
moving quickly into and out of a place
Pronunciation: / ɪn ənd aʊt / (AmE), / ɪn ənd aʊt / (BrE)
quickly entering and exiting a place; frequently coming and going from a place

Examples

Be in and out quickly
He was in and out of the meeting room all afternoon.
Kids were going in and out of the house during the party.
She rushed in and out of the pharmacy in five minutes.
