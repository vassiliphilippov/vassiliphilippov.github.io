maladroit

adjective
clumsy or lacking skill
9.9 7.4
Pronunciation: /mæ.lə.ˈdrɔɪt/ (AmE), /mæ.lə.ˈdrɔɪt/ (BrE)
not skillful or tactful; clumsy, awkward, or inept in movement or handling situations.

Examples

A maladroit remark
He made a maladroit remark that offended the entire audience.
Her maladroit attempt to comfort him only made things worse.
The company's response to the scandal was widely regarded as maladroit.

Related Terms

  • antonym: adroit - skillful and clever, especially in the use of hands or mind
  • synonym: clumsy - awkward in movement or handling things
