co-sponsor

noun
joint bill supporter
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊˌspɑnsər / (AmE), / ˈkəʊˌspɒnsə / (BrE)
a member of a legislative body who joins with others to officially support a proposed bill or measure

Examples

Bill co-sponsor
The senator served as a co-sponsor for the new healthcare legislation.
Several lawmakers became co-sponsors to show their support for the environmental bill.
Without enough co-sponsors, the proposed law struggled to gain momentum in parliament.

Related Terms

  • antonym: opponent - a person who opposes a bill or proposal
  • synonym: sponsor - a person who introduces and supports a bill in a legislative body
