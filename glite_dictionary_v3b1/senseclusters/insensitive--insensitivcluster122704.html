Examples

Synonyms

  • inconsiderate
    4.1 1/m
    inconsiderate is acting without thinking about others' feelings or rights, while insensitive is more about not noticing or caring.
  • uncaring
    4.2 1/m
    uncaring shows a lack of concern or kindness, while insensitive often means not aware of others' feelings or needs.
  • unsympathetic
    4.9 0.5/m
    unsympathetic does not show pity or support, while insensitive can mean failing to notice or respond to feelings.
  • indifferent
    4.4 5/m
    indifferent feels no strong interest or concern, while insensitive often involves not noticing or upsetting other people.
  • tactless
    4.9 0.2/m
    tactless speaks or acts without care for delicate feelings, while insensitive is a broader unawareness or lack of concern.
  • untactful
    7.2 0.01/m
    untactful is similar to tactless and can upset people, while insensitive is more general and may be unintentional.
  • tone-deaf
    7.7 0.2/m
    tone-deaf misses emotional or social cues and gives wrong replies, while insensitive is broader and may ignore feelings.
  • dismissive
    5.6 3/m
    dismissive treats people's views as unimportant and shows contempt, while insensitive mainly lacks awareness or concern for feelings.
  • insensate
    9.9 0.02/m
    insensate lacks feeling or awareness entirely and sounds formal or extreme, while insensitive is common and often means unaware or unkind.
