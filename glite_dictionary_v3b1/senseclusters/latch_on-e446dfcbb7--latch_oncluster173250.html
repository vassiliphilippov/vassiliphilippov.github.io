latch on

verb
attach or take hold of firmly
Pronunciation: / lætʃ ɑn / (AmE), / lætʃ ɒn / (BrE)
to take hold of or become firmly attached to something

Examples

Latch on to the handle
After several attempts, the hook finally latched on to the metal loop.
Certain viruses latch on to host cells to start an infection.
The infant instinctively latched on to its mother's finger.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grab - to seize suddenly and roughly
  • antonym: let go - to release hold or attachment
