mobilization

noun
release of stored nutrients
Pronunciation: / ccmo8a.b59.la6ac8ze6a.8359n / (AmE), / ccm598a.b6a.la6ac8ze6a.8359n / (BrE)
(in biology and medicine) the process of releasing stored substances (for example glycogen, fats, or minerals) so they become available for metabolic or physiological use.

Examples

Fatty acid mobilization
During fasting, the mobilisation of fatty acids provides energy to muscles.
Hormone-driven mobilisation of glycogen supplies glucose to the bloodstream during intense exercise.
Clinicians monitor mineral mobilisation from bone in patients with metabolic bone disease.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lipolysis - the biochemical breakdown of fats into fatty acids and glycerol; often the mechanism by which fatty acids are mobilised
  • synonym: mobilization - American English spelling of 'mobilisation'; used for the same biological and organizational senses
