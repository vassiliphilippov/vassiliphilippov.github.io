extensity

noun
property of extent
9.4
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstɛnsəti / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɛnsəti / (BrE)
the quality or state of having extension or occupying space, especially as a measurable property in science

Examples

Physical extensity
In physics, mass is a property that shows extensity because it depends on the amount of matter present.
The extensity of the solution increased as more liquid was added to the container.
Unlike temperature, which is intensive, volume demonstrates extensity in scientific measurements.
The extensity of a physical object refers to its ability to occupy space.
Philosophers often debate the concept of extensity in relation to matter and perception.
In geometry, extensity distinguishes objects that have measurable dimensions from those that do not.

Related Terms

  • synonym: extension - the act of stretching out or the state of being extended
  • synonym: extent - the area, length, or scope of something
  • antonym: intensity - the quality of being an intensive quantity, independent of size or amount
