affordability

noun
being inexpensive
Pronunciation: / əˌfɔrdəˈbɪləti /; / əˌfɔrdəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / əˌfɔːdəˈbɪləti /; / əˌfɔːdəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being inexpensive enough for people to buy or pay for

Examples

The affordability of food
The affordability of healthy food is a concern for many families.
Government programs aim to improve the affordability of higher education.
Many people move to the suburbs for the affordability of housing.
The affordability of healthy food is a concern for many low-income families.
Government programs aim to improve the affordability of housing in urban areas.
Rising prices have affected the affordability of basic necessities for students.

  • antonym: expensiveness - the quality of costing a lot of money
