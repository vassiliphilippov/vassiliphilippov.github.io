unwhite

verb
to remove from whitelist
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnˈwaɪt / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈwaɪt / (BrE)
to remove someone or something from a whitelist, revoking their previously allowed or trusted status, especially in computing

Examples

To unwhite a user
The administrator decided to unwhite the user's account after detecting unusual activity.
If you unwhite an email address, it may be blocked by the spam filter.
You should only unwhite devices that you no longer trust on the network.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blacklist - to put someone or something on a list of things that are not allowed
  • antonym: whitelist - to add someone or something to a list of things that are allowed or trusted
