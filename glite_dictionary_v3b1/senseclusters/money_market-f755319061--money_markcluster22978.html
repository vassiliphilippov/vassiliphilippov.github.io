money market

noun
financial market for short-term debt
2.6
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌni ˌmɑrkɪt / (AmE), / ˈmʌni ˌmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
a financial market in which short-term debt instruments such as treasury bills, commercial paper, and certificates of deposit are traded

Examples

Money market fund
After learning about financial instruments, she decided to invest in the money market.
Large corporations often use the money market to manage their short-term funding needs.
The central bank's actions influenced the rates in the money market last week.

Related Terms

  • antonym: capital market - a market for long-term debt and equity securities
