Lancastrian

adjective
relating to the house of Lancaster
Pronunciation: / lænˈkæstriən / (AmE), / laŋˈkæstriən / (BrE)
of or relating to the English royal house of Lancaster or its supporters, especially during the Wars of the Roses.

Examples

Lancastrian family
During the War of the Roses, many noble families were staunchly Lancastrian in their allegiance.
Some historians analyze the Lancastrian influence on English culture during the 15th century.
The Lancastrian army suffered a decisive defeat at the Battle of Towton.
