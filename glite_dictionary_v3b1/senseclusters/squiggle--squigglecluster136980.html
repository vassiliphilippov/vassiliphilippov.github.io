squiggle

verb
to make wavy lines
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈskwɪɡəl / (AmE), / ˈskwɪɡl / (BrE)
to make a wavy, twisting, or irregular line or mark, especially in a playful or casual way

Examples

To squiggle with a pen
The child loved to squiggle on the margins of her homework with colorful pens.
He absentmindedly squiggled patterns on the napkin while waiting for his food.
During the meeting, she kept squiggling lines in her notebook to stay focused.
She likes to squiggle patterns on the corners of her notebooks during class.
The child squiggled a line across the page with a bright red crayon.
He absentmindedly squiggles on scraps of paper while talking on the phone.
