mythologization

noun
the making of something into a myth
Pronunciation: / mɪˌθɑlədʒəˈzeɪʃən /; / mɪˌθɑːlədʒəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / mɪˌθɒlədʒaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of turning a story, event, person, or idea into a myth or representing it in mythical terms, often by exaggerating or idealizing it

Examples

The mythologization of history
Many critics have analyzed the mythologization of the American West in literature and film.
Some stories undergo a process of mythologization, gaining new symbolic meanings over time.
The mythologization of historical figures can obscure the facts of their real lives.
Recent films have contributed to the mythologisation of the space race era.
The mythologisation of historical figures can often obscure the reality of their lives.
There is ongoing debate about the mythologisation of national events in textbooks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mythologization - the American English spelling of 'mythologisation'
  • synonym: mythologize - to turn something into a myth
