pretend

verb
act as if something is true to deceive
1.0 2.0
Pronunciation: /prɪ.ˈtend/ /; / /prə.ˈtend/ (AmE), /prɪ.ˈtend/ /; / /prə.ˈtend/ (BrE)
to act as if something is true when it is not, often to deceive, entertain, or simulate a feeling

Examples

To pretend to sleep
He would often pretend to be asleep when his parents came in.
She tried to pretend she wasn't disappointed, but her face gave her away.
The children love to pretend they are pirates searching for treasure.
He often pretends indifference, though he cares deeply about the outcome.
She tried to pretend she was happy, but her smile was obviously forced.
The child likes to pretend to be a superhero, acting much braver than he feels.
He can pretend to be anyone he wants on stage.
She tried to pretend she wasn't nervous before the interview.
The children like to pretend they are pirates when playing in the garden.

Related Terms

  • antonym: be genuine - to behave truly and sincerely, opposite of pretending
  • synonym: fake - to pretend in order to deceive; to forge
  • synonym: feign - to pretend to be affected by a feeling, state, or injury
  • antonym: genuine - truly what something is said to be; authentic, not pretending
  • synonym: profess - to claim, often insincerely, that something is the case
  • synonym: sham - to pretend or fake something
  • synonym: simulate - to imitate or reproduce the appearance or character of something
