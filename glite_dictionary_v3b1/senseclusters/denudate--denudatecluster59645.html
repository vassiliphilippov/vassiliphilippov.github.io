denudate

verb
to strip or lay bare
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛnjuˌdeɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈnjuːdeɪt / (BrE)
to strip the covering from something; to lay bare, especially by removing vegetation, soil, or other surface layers

Examples

To denudate a hillside
Clear-cutting can denudate a hillside, leading to soil erosion.
If you denudate the land, natural habitats may be destroyed.
Overgrazing has denudated much of the region's grassland.

Related Terms

  • synonym: denude - to strip something of its covering or possessions; to lay bare
