mode

noun
fixed musical scale sequence
Pronunciation: / moʊd / (AmE), / məʊd / (BrE)
(in music) any of several fixed sequences of diatonic notes within an octave, such as the major, minor, or modal scales.

Examples

Major mode scale
Early church music frequently used the Phrygian mode.
Jazz musicians often experiment by switching modes within a single composition.
The piece is written in the Dorian mode, giving it a unique sound.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scale - a sequence of musical notes in ascending or descending order
