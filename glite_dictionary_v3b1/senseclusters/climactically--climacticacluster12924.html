climactically

adverb
in a dramatic way
Pronunciation: / klaɪˈmæktɪkli / (AmE), / klaɪˈmæktɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to or forms a climax, especially reaching the most exciting or important point

Examples

Ends climactically
The movie ended climactically with a breathtaking final scene.
The debate climactically concluded when both sides reached an unexpected agreement.
The concert built up slowly and finished climactically with the band's biggest hit.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anticlimactically - in a way that is disappointing after a build-up
  • synonym: dramatically - in a way that is exciting or impressive
