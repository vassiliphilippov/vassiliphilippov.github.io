French

verb
to recess for style
Pronunciation: / frɛntʃ / (AmE), / frɛntʃ / (BrE)
to set or recess an object, such as a headlight or handle, into a surface so it sits flush or slightly recessed, often for styling purposes

Examples

To French headlights
The custom car shop specializes in Frenching headlights to give vehicles a sleek appearance.
He spent hours Frenching the door handles so they would sit perfectly flush with the body.
Classic car enthusiasts often admire the look achieved by Frenching various exterior parts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embed - to fix an object firmly and deeply in a surrounding mass
  • antonym: protrude - to stick out from a surface
