church school

noun
private religious school run by a church
2.8 0.13/m
Pronunciation: / t É…ùt É skuÀêl / (AmE), / t É…úÀêt É skuÀêl / (BrE)
A private school run by a church or parish, providing religious as well as general education.

Examples

A church school
Many parents choose to send their children to a church school for its religious values and strong community.
She attended a church school where daily prayers were part of the curriculum.
The city has one public school and one church school serving local families.

Synonyms

    3.9 1.47/m
