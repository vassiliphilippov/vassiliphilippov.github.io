embargo

verb
officially ban the release of information
8.5 5.9
Pronunciation: /ɪm.ˈbɑːr.ɡoʊ/ /; / /əm.ˈbɑːr.ɡoʊ/ (AmE), /ɪm.ˈbɑː.ɡəʊ/ /; / /əm.ˈbɑː.ɡəʊ/ (BrE)
to officially prohibit the publication or release of information or documents, especially for reasons of security or copyright

Examples

To embargo news
Journalists were informed that the data would be embargoed until Monday morning.
Sensitive documents are often embargoed to prevent leaks before an official announcement.
The government decided to embargo the report until the investigation was complete.
