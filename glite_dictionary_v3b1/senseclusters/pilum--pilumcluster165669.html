pilum

noun
Roman throwing spear
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪləm / (AmE), / ˈpiːləm / (BrE)
a heavy javelin used by Roman soldiers as a throwing weapon in battle

Examples

A pilum weapon
The Roman soldier hurled his pilum at the advancing enemy.
Archaeologists discovered a well-preserved pilum at the ancient battlefield site.
Each legionary carried at least one pilum as part of his standard equipment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: javelin - a light spear designed to be thrown
  • synonym: spear - a weapon with a pointed tip, used for thrusting or throwing
