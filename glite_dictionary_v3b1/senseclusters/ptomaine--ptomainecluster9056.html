ptomaine

noun
obsolete term for food poisoning toxin
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtoʊˌmeɪn /; / toʊˈmeɪn / (AmE), / ˈtəʊmeɪn /; / təˈmeɪn / (BrE)
a toxic compound produced during the decomposition of animal or plant matter (an old scientific term, now rarely used)

Examples

Food ptomaine poisoning
In the nineteenth century, cases of food poisoning were often blamed on ptomaine contamination.
She feared that eating the spoiled meat would result in ptomaine poisoning.
The old textbooks referred to ptomaine poisoning before bacteria were widely understood.
Certain species of bacteria produce ptomaine compounds when decomposing animal tissue.
Researchers studied the role of ptomaine in early theories of foodborne poisoning.
The presence of ptomaine in spoiled food can cause severe illness if ingested.

  • synonym: toxin - a poisonous substance produced by living organisms
