irreplaceability

noun
impossible to replace
Pronunciation: / ˌɪrɪˌpleɪsəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌɪrɪˌpleɪsəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being impossible to replace, especially because of unique value or importance

Examples

The irreplaceability of heirlooms
The irreplaceability of family heirlooms makes them especially valuable to each generation.
Conservationists stress the irreplaceability of endangered species in maintaining biodiversity.
She realized the irreplaceability of true friendship after moving to a new city.
The irreplaceability of ancient forests makes their conservation a top priority for environmentalists.
Many people underestimate the irreplaceability of cultural heritage sites until they are lost.
Scientists highlighted the irreplaceability of the coral reef in maintaining marine biodiversity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: replaceability - the quality of being able to be replaced
  • synonym: uniqueness - the quality of being the only one of its kind
