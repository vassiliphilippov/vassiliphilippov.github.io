antievolutionary

adjective
opposed to change
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌæntiˌɛvəˈluːʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˌæntiˌiːvəˈluːʃənəri / (BrE)
opposed to gradual change or development, especially in social, cultural, or institutional contexts

Examples

Antievolutionary stance
The committee adopted an antievolutionary stance, resisting any reforms to the existing system.
Some critics labeled the new law as antievolutionary because it discouraged innovation.
Her antievolutionary views made her reluctant to accept changes in workplace policies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conservative - favoring traditional views and values; resistant to change
  • antonym: progressive - favoring or promoting gradual social, political, or institutional change
