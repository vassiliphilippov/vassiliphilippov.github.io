coach-and-four

noun
carriage drawn by four horses
Pronunciation: / koʊtʃ ənd fɔr / (AmE), / kəʊtʃ ənd fɔː / (BrE)
A large horse-drawn carriage with four horses and a coachman.

Examples

A coach-and-four carriage
In the novel, the wealthy gentleman arrived at the estate in his coach-and-four.
She dreamed of riding through London in a gleaming coach-and-four on her wedding day.
Tourists marveled as the ornate coach-and-four rolled through the village square.
