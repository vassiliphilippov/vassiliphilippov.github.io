Westernism

noun
Western genre style
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛstərnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈwɛstənɪzəm / (BrE)
the themes, motifs, or stylistic features typical of the Western (cowboy) genre in literature, film, or music

Examples

Westernism in movies
The director used classic Westernism in the film, including dusty towns and dramatic showdowns.
Her music is influenced by Westernism, with guitar riffs and cowboy imagery throughout the album.
The novel's strong frontier setting and lone-horse hero displayed clear Westernism throughout.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Western - a genre of stories set in the American West, often featuring cowboys
  • antonym: noir - a genre characterized by dark, cynical themes and style
