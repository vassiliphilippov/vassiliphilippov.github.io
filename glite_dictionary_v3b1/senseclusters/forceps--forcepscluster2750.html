forceps

noun
medical pincers for surgery or birth
4.5 5.1
Pronunciation: /ˈfɔːr.səps/ /; / /ˈfɔːr.seps/ (AmE), /ˈfɔː.səps/ /; / /ˈfɔː.seps/ (BrE)
A medical instrument resembling a pair of pincers, used for grasping, holding, or extracting, especially to assist in the delivery of babies or during surgery.

Examples

Surgical forceps
Obstetricians sometimes use forceps to assist during difficult childbirths.
Sterilized forceps are necessary for many surgical procedures.
The surgeon used forceps to carefully extract the splinter from the patient's hand.
