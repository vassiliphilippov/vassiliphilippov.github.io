superstitiousness

noun
belief in superstitions
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərˈstɪʃəsnəs / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˈstɪʃəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of believing in or practicing superstitions, such as rituals, omens, or supernatural causes, instead of logical reasoning

Examples

Superstitiousness about luck
His superstitiousness led him to carry a lucky charm everywhere he went.
The team's superstitiousness was obvious when they wore the same socks for every game.

Related Terms

  • synonym: superstition - a belief or practice resulting from fear of the unknown or faith in magic
