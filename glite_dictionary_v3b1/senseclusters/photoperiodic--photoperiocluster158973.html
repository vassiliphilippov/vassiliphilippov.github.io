photoperiodic

adjective
affected by day length
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊtoʊˌpɪriˈɑːdɪk / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊˌpɪəriˈɒdɪk / (BrE)
relating to or affected by the length of day or night, especially in biological processes

Examples

Photoperiodic plants flower
Certain crops have photoperiodic requirements and will only flower when days reach a specific length.
The photoperiodic response in animals can trigger migration or breeding behaviors.
Researchers study photoperiodic effects to understand how plants adapt to different climates.

Related Terms

  • synonym: photoperiodism - the response of organisms to the length of day or night
