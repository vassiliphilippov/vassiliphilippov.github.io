filmstrip

noun
strip of images
3.3
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪlmˌstrɪp / (AmE), / ˈfɪlmˌstrɪp / (BrE)
a long strip of photographic film with a sequence of images, used for projection or viewing, especially in education

Examples

A filmstrip in class
The teacher showed a filmstrip about the solar system in class.
We watched an old filmstrip that explained how volcanoes erupt.
Each filmstrip contained dozens of images to illustrate the story.
The class watched a filmstrip about the history of ancient Egypt during the lesson.
Before digital projectors, teachers often used a filmstrip to show pictures to students.
He found an old filmstrip in the attic, showing family photos from the 1970s.

Related Terms

  • synonym: movie reel - a reel of film containing a motion picture
  • synonym: slide - a single photographic image on a small piece of film for projection
  • antonym: video - a recording of moving visual images
