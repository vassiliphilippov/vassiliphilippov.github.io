seed

noun
mature plant ovule for reproduction
Pronunciation: / sid /; / siːd / (AmE), / siːd / (BrE)
the small part of a plant from which a new plant can grow

Examples

A seed grows
After flowering, the plant produces seeds that can be collected and planted.
Each apple contains several seeds at its core.
The gardener planted a seed in the soil to grow a sunflower.
Farmers plant seeds in the spring to grow their crops.
Some birds eat seeds as a main part of their diet.
The apple was full of seeds that needed to be removed before eating.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grain - a small, hard, dry seed from a cereal plant
  • synonym: spore - a reproductive cell capable of developing into a new individual without fusion with another cell
