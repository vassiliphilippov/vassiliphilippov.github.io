two-hand touch

noun
version of football without tackling
Pronunciation: / ˈtuː hænd tʌtʃ / (AmE), / ˈtuː hænd tʌtʃ / (BrE)
a variation of football in which a player is considered tackled when touched simultaneously by both hands of an opponent, instead of being physically brought to the ground

Examples

Two-hand touch football
Every Saturday, the local kids meet at the park to play two-hand touch.
Some families prefer two-hand touch to tackle football because it's safer for children.
We organized a quick game of two-hand touch during our lunch break.

Related Terms

  • synonym: touch football - a form of football where tackling is replaced by touching an opponent
