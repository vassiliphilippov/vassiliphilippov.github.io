hooded seal

noun
Arctic and northern Atlantic seal with inflatable sac on head
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhʊdɪd sil / (AmE), / ˈhʊdɪd siːl / (BrE)
a medium-sized blackish-grey seal found in Arctic and northern Atlantic waters, notable for the large inflatable sac on its head

Examples

A hooded seal in Arctic
A researcher observed a hooded seal swimming among ice floes in the frigid Arctic Ocean.
The hooded seal uses its inflated nasal sac to attract mates and ward off rivals.
Unlike other seals, the male hooded seal can dramatically inflate an elastic hood over its nose.
