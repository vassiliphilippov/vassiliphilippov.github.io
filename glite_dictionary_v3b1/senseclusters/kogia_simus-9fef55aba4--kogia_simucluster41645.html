Kogia simus

noun
species of small sperm whale
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊ.dʒə ˈsaɪ.məs / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.dʒɪə ˈsɪ.məs / (BrE)
A species of very small sperm whale, reaching up to 8 feet in length, found along the central coasts of the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans.

Examples

A Kogia simus whale
A stranded Kogia simus was found on the beach, prompting concern from local wildlife experts.
Marine biologists study the Kogia simus to learn about the adaptation of deep-diving mammals.
The Kogia simus is often mistaken for other small cetaceans due to its size and elusive behavior.
