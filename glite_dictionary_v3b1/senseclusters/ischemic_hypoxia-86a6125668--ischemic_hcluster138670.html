ischemic hypoxia

noun
hypoxia from poor blood circulation
Pronunciation: / ɪˈskimɪk haɪˈpɑksiə / (AmE), / ɪˈskiːmɪk hɪˈpɒksɪə / (BrE)
A form of hypoxia caused by reduced blood flow to tissues, typically due to slow peripheral circulation such as occurs after congestive heart failure.

Examples

Ischemic hypoxia from slow circulation
Ischemic hypoxia can lead to tissue damage if blood flow is not restored quickly.
Doctors monitor for ischemic hypoxia after major cardiovascular surgery.
Patients with severe heart failure may develop ischemic hypoxia in their extremities.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: anemic hypoxia - hypoxia caused by low oxygen-carrying capacity of the blood
  • degree lower: hypoxic hypoxia - hypoxia caused by insufficient oxygen in the blood itself
