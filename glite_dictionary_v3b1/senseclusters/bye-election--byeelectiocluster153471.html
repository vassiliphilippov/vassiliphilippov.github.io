bye-election

noun
special election to fill a vacant office
9.3 5.9
Pronunciation: /baɪ ɪ.ˈlek.ʃən/ (AmE), /baɪ ɪ.ˈlek.ʃən/ (BrE)
an election held to fill a political office that becomes vacant between regularly scheduled elections

Examples

A bye-election contest
She won the seat in a closely contested bye-election after her predecessor stepped down.
The local MP resigned, prompting a bye-election in the district next month.
Voter turnout at the bye-election was lower than during a general election.

Related Terms

  • synonym: by-election - synonym; alternative spelling for the same type of special election
  • antonym: general election - an election to choose all members of a political body
