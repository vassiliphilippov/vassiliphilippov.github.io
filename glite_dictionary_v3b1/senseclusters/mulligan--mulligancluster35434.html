mulligan

noun
extra chance in games
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌlɪɡən / (AmE), / ˈmʌlɪɡən / (BrE)
an extra chance to do something again, especially after a poor attempt, often in games or sports (especially golf)

Examples

Take a mulligan shot
He missed the putt but was allowed a mulligan by his friends.
In casual games, players sometimes agree to give each other a mulligan for a bad start.
She asked for a mulligan after her first attempt went wrong.
After missing the putt, she asked if she could take a mulligan and try again.
In friendly games, players sometimes allow a mulligan on the first tee shot.
He joked that life would be easier if everyone got a mulligan once in a while.

Related Terms

  • synonym: do-over - an opportunity to try something again after a failed attempt
