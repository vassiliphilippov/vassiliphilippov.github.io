leopard skin

noun
ceremonial leopard cloak
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛpərd skɪn / (AmE), / ˈlɛpəd skɪn / (BrE)
a ceremonial garment or cloak made from the pelt of a leopard, often worn as a symbol of rank or status in certain cultures

Examples

Ceremonial leopard skin
The chief wore a leopard skin during the important tribal ceremony.
A genuine leopard skin is considered a symbol of high status in some African cultures.
The museum displayed a traditional leopard skin used by local leaders centuries ago.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mantle - a cloak or covering, often worn as a symbol of authority
  • synonym: robe - a long, loose outer garment worn on formal occasions
