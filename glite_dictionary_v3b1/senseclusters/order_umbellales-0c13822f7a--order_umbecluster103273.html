order Umbellales

noun
plant order including Umbelliferae and Cornaceae
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrdər ˌʌmbəˈleɪliːz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌʌmbəˈleɪliːz / (BrE)
a former order of flowering plants characterized by umbels or corymbs of uniovulate flowers, including the families Umbelliferae (mainly herbs) and Cornaceae (mainly trees or shrubs)

Examples

The order Umbellales plants
Botanists once classified carrots and celery in the order Umbellales based on their floral structures.
Many edible herbs belong to the order Umbellales, though newer systems may place them differently.
The order Umbellales includes plants with characteristic umbrella-shaped clusters of flowers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Cornaceae - a family of chiefly woody plants within the order Umbellales
  • synonym: Umbelliferae - a family of mainly herbaceous flowering plants with characteristic umbels
