calcine

verb
heat to bring about chemical change
9.7
Pronunciation: / kælˈsaɪn / (AmE), / kælˈsaɪn / (BrE)
to heat a substance to a high temperature so that it undergoes oxidation, reduction, or thermal decomposition, usually to remove volatile substances or change its composition

Examples

To calcine limestone
Industrial processes often calcine limestone to produce lime for cement and other uses.
The laboratory technician was instructed to calcine the sample at 900°C before further analysis.
When you calcine the ore, unwanted substances are driven off, leaving behind the metal oxide.

Related Terms

  • synonym: roast - to heat ore in air to drive off impurities or bring about oxidation
