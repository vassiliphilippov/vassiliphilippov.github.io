sedge warbler

noun
small European migratory songbird
7.9
Pronunciation: / sɛdʒ ˈwɔrb.lɚ / (AmE), / sɛdʒ ˈwɔː.blə / (BrE)
a small European songbird (Acrocephalus schoenobaenus) that breeds in reed beds and wetlands and migrates to Africa for winter

Examples

The sedge warbler sings
Birdwatchers often visit the marshes in spring to observe the arrival of sedge warblers.
The sedge warbler migrates thousands of miles to Africa each autumn.
We spotted a sedge warbler singing among the rushes by the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reed warbler - a similar European warbler species found in reeds
