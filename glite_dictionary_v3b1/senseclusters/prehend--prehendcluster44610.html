prehend

verb
to grasp or seize
Pronunciation: / prɪˈhɛnd / (AmE), / prɪˈhɛnd / (BrE)
to seize or grasp something, especially with the hand or a similar appendage

Examples

To prehend an object
Some animals use their tails to prehend objects in their environment.
The monkey can prehend food with its dexterous fingers.
Certain robots are designed to prehend small parts during assembly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grasp - to take hold of something firmly
  • antonym: release - to let go of something held
  • synonym: seize - to take hold of suddenly and forcibly
