flange

verb
to add a projecting edge
Pronunciation: / flændʒ / (AmE), / flændʒ / (BrE)
to provide or fit with a projecting edge or rim for strength, attachment, or alignment

Examples

To flange a pipe
The engineer flanged the metal plate to make it easier to bolt onto the frame.
Pipes are often flanged at the ends to allow for secure connections.
The mechanic flanges the exhaust pipe to ensure it fits tightly with the rest of the system.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attach - to fasten or join one thing to another
  • antonym: remove - to take something away or off
