leap

verb
cause someone or something to jump
1.0 21/m
Pronunciation: / lip / (AmE), / liÀêp / (BrE)
to jump or spring quickly or forcefully from one place to another, or to cause someone or something to do this

Examples

To leap over a fence
At the circus, performers leap lions through flaming hoops for the audience.
The coach skillfully leaped the horse over the fence during the show.
The dog was leaped through the ring by its trainer.
He leapt onto the stage, full of excitement and energy.
She leaps across the puddles on her way to school every morning.
The deer leaped over the fence and disappeared into the forest.
Firefighters leap from burning buildings to escape danger when there's no other option.
He leapt from the tree branch, landing safely on the grass.
She took a deep breath and leapt from the cliff into the water below.

Synonyms

  • jump
    1.0 52/m
    jump is a general action of pushing off the ground with your legs, while leap is often quicker and more forceful.
  • spring
    1.0 9.35/m
    spring is a sudden move often with a bounce or quick upward action, while leap stresses a strong, single jump.
  • bound
    4.2 2.63/m
    bound is to move forward in large, springing steps over ground, while leap is a quick, single jump often upward.
