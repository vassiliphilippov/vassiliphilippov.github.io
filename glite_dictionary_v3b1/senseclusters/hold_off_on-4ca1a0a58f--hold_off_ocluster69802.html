hold off on

verb
delay or postpone an action
4.1
Pronunciation: / hoʊld ɔf ɑn / (AmE), / həʊld ɒf ɒn / (BrE)
to delay or postpone doing something; to wait before proceeding with an action

Examples

To hold off on action
Let's hold off on discussing the budget until others arrive.
They decided to hold off on the announcement until they had more information.
We should hold off on making changes until we review all the data.

Related Terms

  • synonym: postpone - to delay something to a later time
  • antonym: proceed - to begin or continue with an action
