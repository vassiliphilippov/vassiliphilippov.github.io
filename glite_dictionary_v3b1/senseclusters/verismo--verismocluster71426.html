verismo

noun
realism in opera
Pronunciation: / vɛˈrizmoʊ / (AmE), / vɛˈrɪzməʊ / (BrE)
a style of realism in opera or literature, especially in late 19th-century Italian opera, focusing on everyday life and ordinary people

Examples

Verismo opera style
The composer was influenced by verismo and sought to depict real-life struggles in his music.
Many critics praised the novel's verismo for its honest portrayal of working-class life.
Puccini's operas are famous examples of verismo.

Related Terms

  • synonym: realism - the artistic movement aiming to represent subjects truthfully
  • antonym: romanticism - an artistic movement emphasizing emotion and imagination over realism
