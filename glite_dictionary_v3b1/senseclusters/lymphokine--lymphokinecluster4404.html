lymphokine

noun
cytokine produced by lymphocytes
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪmfoʊˌkaɪn / (AmE), / ˈlɪmfəʊˌkaɪn / (BrE)
a type of cytokine produced by lymphocytes, especially helper T cells, that influences the activity of other cells in the immune system

Examples

Helper T cell lymphokines
Helper T cells release lymphokines to help activate other immune cells during infection.
Research into various lymphokine therapies is ongoing for autoimmune diseases.
The doctor explained that lymphokines play a crucial role in regulating the immune response.
