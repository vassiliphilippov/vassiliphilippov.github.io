Nestor

proper noun
wise old counselor in Greek mythology
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛstər / (AmE), / ˈnɛstə / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, a wise old king of Pylos who served as counselor to the Greeks during the Trojan War; by extension, any wise old man or senior advisor.

Examples

Wise Nestor of Greece
Homer described Nestor as a respected leader who shared his experiences from past battles.
In times of crisis, the community looked to their own Nestor for thoughtful advice.
The advisor was regarded as the Nestor of the company, offering wisdom and guidance to younger executives.
