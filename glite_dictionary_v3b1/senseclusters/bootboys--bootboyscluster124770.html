bootboys

noun
British working-class youth subculture (1960s-70s)
Pronunciation: / ˈbuːt.bɔɪz / (AmE), / ˈbuːt.bɔɪz / (BrE)
A member of a British youth subculture from the late 1960s, known for cropped hair, workwear, suspenders, and heavy boots, often associated with violence and football hooliganism.

Examples

Bootboys in groups
In the 1970s, groups of bootboys were often seen gathering outside football stadiums.
Newspaper headlines frequently blamed bootboys for violence at football matches.
The distinctive style of bootboys involved suspenders, short jeans, and heavy boots.
