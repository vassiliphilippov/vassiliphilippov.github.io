cyclic

adjective
having atoms arranged in a ring (chemistry)
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪklɪk, ˈsɪklɪk / (AmE), / ˈsɪklɪk, ˈsaɪklɪk / (BrE)
(of a chemical compound) having atoms arranged in a ring structure.

Examples

Cyclic compound ring
Benzene is a classic example of a cyclic compound.
Many sugars can form cyclic structures in solution.
Some vitamins, like vitamin C, have cyclic molecular structures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acyclic - (of a compound) not arranged in a ring structure
