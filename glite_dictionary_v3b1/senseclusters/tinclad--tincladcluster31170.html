tinclad

noun
lightly armored warship
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈtɪn.klæd/ (AmE), /ˈtɪn.klæd/ (BrE)
a lightly armored river gunboat or warship protected by thin sheets of metal, especially used during the American Civil War

Examples

A tinclad gunboat
During the Civil War, the Union navy deployed several tinclads to patrol the Mississippi River.
A tinclad offered some protection against small arms fire but was vulnerable to heavy artillery.
Historians studied the remains of a tinclad found near the old riverbank.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gunboat - a small armed ship used for river or coastal operations
  • degree higher: ironclad - a warship protected by heavy iron or steel armor plates
