sweet false chamomile

noun
a Eurasian herb resembling chamomile
Pronunciation: / swit fɔls ˈkæməˌmaɪl / (AmE), / swiːt fɔːls ˈkæməmaɪl / (BrE)
an annual Eurasian herb similar in scent and medicinal use to chamomile, but with a more bitter taste and considered less effective

Examples

Sweet false chamomile plant
Although sweet false chamomile resembles true chamomile, its taste is noticeably more bitter.
Farmers can find sweet false chamomile growing wild along the edges of their fields.
Many herbalists use sweet false chamomile as a mild remedy for digestive issues.
