raucously

adverb
in a loud, harsh way
Pronunciation: / ˈrɔːk.əs.li /; / ˈrɔːkəsli / (AmE), / ˈrɔː.kəs.li /; / ˈrɔːkəsli / (BrE)
in a loud, harsh, or rough (and possibly rowdy) manner

Examples

To laugh raucously
He laughed raucously at every joke, disturbing the quiet café.
The crowd cheered raucously as the team took the field.
The parrots squawked raucously in the early morning light.
She laughed raucously, drawing attention from everyone nearby.
The party grew raucously loud as midnight approached.
The students cheered raucously as their team scored the winning goal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: loudly - in a way that produces a lot of noise
  • synonym: noisily - in a loud or boisterous manner
  • antonym: quietly - in a quiet or calm manner
  • antonym: softly - in a quiet or gentle manner
