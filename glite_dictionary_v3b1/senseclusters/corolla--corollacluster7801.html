corolla

noun
whorl of petals in a flower
Pronunciation: / kəˈɹɑːlə / (AmE), / kəˈrɒlə / (BrE)
(botany) the collective term for all the petals of a flower, forming the inner floral envelope within the perianth.

Examples

The corolla petals
Botanists examine the corolla to help identify different flower species.
In some plants, the corolla is fused into a tubular shape.
The bright red corolla of the hibiscus attracts many pollinators.
