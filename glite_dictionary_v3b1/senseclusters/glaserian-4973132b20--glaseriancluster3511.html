Glaserian

adjective
of Glaser's methodology
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡleɪzəriən / (AmE), / ˈɡleɪzəriən / (BrE)
relating to the grounded theory methodology developed by Barney Glaser, especially the approach where theory emerges from data.

Examples

Glaserian theory
The researcher used a Glaserian approach to let patterns emerge naturally from the interview data.
Her thesis followed Glaserian principles, focusing on generating theory directly from observations.
Many qualitative studies adopt a Glaserian methodology to ensure findings are grounded in real-world data.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Straussian - relating to the grounded theory approach developed by Anselm Strauss
  • synonym: grounded theory - a research method where theory is developed from data
