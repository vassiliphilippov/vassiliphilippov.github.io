kingdom Prokaryotae

noun
biological kingdom of prokaryotic organisms
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪŋdəm proʊˌkærioʊˈteɪ / (AmE), / ˈkɪŋdəm prəʊˌkærɪəʊˈtiː / (BrE)
a former biological kingdom including prokaryotic organisms such as bacteria and blue-green algae, characterized by cells lacking a nucleus

Examples

Kingdom Prokaryotae organisms
Recent advances in genetics have led to the division of the kingdom Prokaryotae into new domains.
Textbooks from the 20th century frequently refer to the kingdom Prokaryotae in their classification diagrams.
The kingdom Prokaryotae was once used to classify all prokaryotic organisms, including bacteria.
