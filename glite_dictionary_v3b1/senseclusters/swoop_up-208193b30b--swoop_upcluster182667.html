swoop up

verb
pick up or seize quickly in a sweeping motion
4.2 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / swup  åp / (AmE), / swuÀêp  åp / (BrE)
to seize, collect, or pick up something quickly with a sweeping or downward motion

Examples

To swoop up quickly
Paramedics swooped up the injured cyclist and rushed him to the hospital.
She quickly swooped up the scattered papers before the wind carried them away.
The eagle swooped up its prey in a matter of seconds.

Synonyms

  • scoop up
    2.8 3.46/m
    scoop up lifts things quickly with a rounded hand or bowl, while swoop up emphasizes a fast downward grab.
  • snatch up
    4.6 1.93/m
    snatch up takes something quickly and eagerly with a sudden grab, while swoop up implies a swift downward sweeping motion.
  • swoop
    3.1 3.44/m
    swoop moves rapidly down through the air to catch things, whereas swoop up adds the action of picking those things up.
  • pick up
    1.0 49/m
    pick up means lifting something from a surface by hand, while swoop up implies a fast sweeping or downward snatch from above.
