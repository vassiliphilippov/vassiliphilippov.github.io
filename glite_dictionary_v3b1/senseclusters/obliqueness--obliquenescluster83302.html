obliqueness

noun
the state of being at an angle
9.0 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàbliÀêkn…ôs / (AmE), / …ôbÀàliÀêkn…™s / (BrE)
the quality or state of being neither parallel nor perpendicular, but at an angle; slanting or inclined position

Examples

Obliqueness of a line
Artists often use obliqueness in their work to create a sense of depth or movement.
The obliqueness of the road made it difficult for drivers to see oncoming traffic.
The obliqueness of the sun's rays at dawn casts long shadows across the landscape.

Synonyms

  • obliquity
    8.9 0.10/m
    obliquity is a formal technical term for being slanted at an angle, while obliqueness is the general quality of that slant.
  • slant
    3.4 1.18/m
    slant is a visible slope or lean on a surface, while obliqueness names the overall state of being angled.
  • tilt
    2.8 2.23/m
    tilt is a sudden or physical lean of an object, while obliqueness refers to the general angled position.
  • inclination
    6.3 0.93/m
    inclination is the exact angle or degree of tilt often measured, while obliqueness is the general state of being slanted.
