Salmonella typhosa

noun
bacteria causing typhoid fever
Pronunciation: / sæl.məˈnɛl.ə taɪˈfoʊ.sə / (AmE), / sæl.məˈnɛl.ə tɪˈfəʊ.sə / (BrE)
a species of Salmonella bacteria that causes typhoid fever in humans

Examples

Salmonella typhosa bacteria
Outbreaks of typhoid fever are often traced to water contaminated with Salmonella typhosa.
Researchers developed a vaccine to protect people against Salmonella typhosa infections.
The presence of Salmonella typhosa in the blood confirms a diagnosis of typhoid fever.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Salmonella enterica - a broader species of bacteria including many serotypes, some causing human disease
