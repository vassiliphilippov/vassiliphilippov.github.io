shedu

noun
Mesopotamian guardian figure
Pronunciation: / ˈʃeɪduː / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪduː / (BrE)
a protective spirit or guardian figure in ancient Mesopotamian mythology, often shown as a winged bull or lion with a human head, and commonly carved as statues at entrances

Examples

A shedu statue
The museum displayed a massive shedu that once guarded the entrance to an ancient temple.
Scholars studied the inscriptions carved into the wings of the shedu for clues about Mesopotamian beliefs.
Tourists marveled at the detailed features of the shedu statues standing outside the reconstructed palace.
A massive stone shedu stood at the entrance of the ancient palace.
The shedu was believed to guard the city from evil spirits.
Archaeologists discovered a carved shedu among the ruins of the temple.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gargoyle - a carved stone figure used as a decorative water spout and protector on buildings
  • synonym: lamassu - a similar Assyrian protective deity with a human head, body of a bull or lion, and wings
