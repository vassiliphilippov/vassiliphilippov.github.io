widespread

adjective
widely prevalent or distributed
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪdˌsprɛd / (AmE), / ˈwaɪdˌsprɛd / (BrE)
existing or happening over a large area or among many people or things; widely distributed or prevalent

Examples

A widespread problem
Concerns about climate change are now widespread throughout society.
The rumor became so widespread that the city officials had to issue a statement.
There is widespread support for the new policy among educators.
Climate change has widespread effects on ecosystems around the world.
The disease caused widespread panic in several countries.
There was widespread confusion following the sudden announcement.
