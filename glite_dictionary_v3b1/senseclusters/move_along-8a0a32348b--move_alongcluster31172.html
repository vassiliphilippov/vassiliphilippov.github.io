move along

verb
to proceed or leave, especially when told to
3.0 3.36/m
Pronunciation: / muÀêv …ôÀàl…î≈ã / (AmE), / muÀêv …ôÀàl…í≈ã / (BrE)
to go away from a place or to continue moving forward, especially when told to do so by someone in authority; to proceed without delaying

Examples

To move along quickly
After a brief pause to take photos, we decided to move along the trail.
If you have no further questions, let's move along to the next topic.
The police officer told the crowd to move along and not block the entrance.

Synonyms

  • go
    1.0 2519/m
    go is the common verb for leaving or traveling, while move along often tells people to leave or keep moving quickly.
  • proceed
    3.0 43/m
    proceed is a more formal word for continuing an action or journey, while move along is a casual command to keep moving.
  • travel along
    1.6 0.94/m
    travel along means following a route over distance, while move along often means leaving or continuing forward on the spot.

Related Terms

  • synonym: go away - to leave a place
  • antonym: linger - to stay somewhere longer than necessary
