whippersnapper

noun
cheeky, presumptuous young person
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪpərˌsnæpər / (AmE), / ˈwɪpəˌsnæpə / (BrE)
a young person who is regarded as presumptuous, cheeky, or lacking in respect

Examples

A young whippersnapper
Don't let that whippersnapper tell you how to run your business!
He was tired of being called a whippersnapper just because of his youth and enthusiasm.
The old professor dismissed his young critic as a mere whippersnapper with no real expertise.
