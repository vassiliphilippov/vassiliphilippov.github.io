alpha decay

noun
radioactive decay with emission of an alpha particle
Pronunciation: / ˈælfə dɪˌkeɪ / (AmE), / ˈælfə dɪˌkeɪ / (BrE)
a type of radioactive decay in which an unstable atomic nucleus emits an alpha particle and transforms into a new element

Examples

Alpha decay process
Certain heavy elements are known to exhibit alpha decay as a primary means of losing instability.
Scientists measured the rate of alpha decay in the radioactive material.
The uranium nucleus undergoes alpha decay, releasing an alpha particle and forming thorium.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gamma decay - radioactive decay involving emission of gamma radiation
