carbonic acid

noun
weak acid formed from CO₂ and water
2.5
Pronunciation: / kɑrˈbɑnɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / kɑːˈbɒnɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a weak acid formed when carbon dioxide dissolves in water, known mainly in solution and important in biological and chemical processes

Examples

Carbonic acid solution
Rainwater is naturally acidic because it absorbs CO₂ and forms small amounts of carbonic acid.
The buffering effect of carbonic acid is essential in human blood to maintain pH balance.
When carbon dioxide dissolves in water, carbonic acid is formed, which makes the solution slightly acidic.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: bicarbonate - a salt containing the anion HCO₃⁻, related to carbonic acid
  • synonym: carbon dioxide - a colorless gas formed by respiration and combustion
