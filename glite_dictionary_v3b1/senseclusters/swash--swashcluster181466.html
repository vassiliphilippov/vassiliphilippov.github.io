swash

verb
to swagger or act arrogantly
Pronunciation: / swɑʃ / (AmE), / swɒʃ / (BrE)
to behave in an arrogant, showy, or swaggering manner

Examples

To swash across stage
Actors in old plays sometimes swashed across the stage, full of bluster and pride.
He likes to swash about the office as if he owns the place, but no one is impressed.
The arrogant knight would often swash through the halls, boasting of his deeds.
