denigration

noun
unfair criticism or belittling remark
9.1 6.7
Pronunciation: /dɪ.nɪ.ˈɡreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /də.nɪ.ˈɡreɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /dɪ.nɪ.ˈɡreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /də.nɪ.ˈɡreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act of unfairly criticizing, belittling, or disparaging someone or something

Examples

Unfair denigration of others
Online forums can sometimes become places for unwarranted denigration of others.
She was hurt by the repeated denigration of her creative work.
The article was filled with denigration of the politician's achievements.
Online denigration can seriously harm a person's reputation.
She was hurt by the constant denigration of her efforts by her colleagues.
The politician's speech was full of denigration towards his opponents.
His constant denigration of other people's work made him unpopular among his colleagues.
Sometimes, self-denigration can be a sign of low self-esteem.
The article was full of denigration against the new policy, offering little constructive criticism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defamation - the act of damaging the good reputation of someone; slander or libel
  • synonym: disparagement - the act of speaking about someone in a derogatory manner
  • antonym: praise - the act of expressing approval or admiration
