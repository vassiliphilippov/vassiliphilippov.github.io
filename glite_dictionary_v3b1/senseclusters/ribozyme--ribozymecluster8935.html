ribozyme

noun
RNA enzyme
Pronunciation: / ˈraɪboʊˌzaɪm / (AmE), / ˈraɪbəʊˌzaɪm / (BrE)
an RNA molecule that acts as an enzyme to catalyze specific chemical reactions

Examples

A ribozyme molecule
Scientists discovered that a ribozyme can cut and join RNA strands inside cells.
The function of a ribozyme is crucial in some genetic processes.
Researchers are studying how a ribozyme might be used in new medical treatments.
