H

noun
the SI unit of inductance (henry)
7.1
Pronunciation: /eɪtʃ/ (AmE), /eɪtʃ/ (BrE)
the symbol for henry, the SI unit of inductance, equal to the inductance for which an electromotive force of one volt is produced when the current changes at the rate of one ampere per second

Examples

2 H coil
A value of 2 H means the coil will generate 2 volts if the current changes at one ampere per second.
Capacitors are rated in farads, while inductors are rated in H.
The inductance of the coil is measured in H (henries).
