cleistogamic

adjective
describing flowers that self-pollinate without opening
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˌklaɪstəˈɡæmɪk / (AmE), / ˌklaɪstəˈɡæmɪk / (BrE)
(of flowers) exhibiting or relating to cleistogamy; producing flowers that self-pollinate without opening.

Examples

cleistogamic flowers
Many violets produce cleistogamic flowers that do not open but still set seed.
Researchers observed both showy and cleistogamic blooms on the same plant.
The cleistogamic nature of certain plants ensures self-pollination in harsh conditions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: chasmogamic - (of flowers) pollinated after opening
