nones

noun
fifth canonical hour (about 3 p.m.)
Pronunciation: / noʊnz / (AmE), / nəʊnz / (BrE)
The fifth of the seven canonical hours in Christian liturgy, traditionally observed at about 3 p.m.

Examples

The nones prayer
Medieval churches often rang bells at nones to signal the mid-afternoon prayer.
The monks gathered in the chapel to recite nones before returning to their daily duties.
The practice of saying nones at 3 p.m. has largely faded, but it remains part of some monastic traditions.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: sext - the canonical hour at noon
  • degree higher: vespers - the canonical hour at sunset, following nones
