tonsil

noun
one of two lymphatic tissues in the throat
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑːn.səl / (AmE), / ˈtɒn.sɪl / (BrE)
Either of two small masses of lymphatic tissue located on each side of the throat at the back of the mouth, which help protect the body from infection.

Examples

Inflamed tonsil
A sore throat can sometimes be caused by an inflamed tonsil.
Her tonsil became swollen and red during her illness.
The doctor examined my tonsil to check for signs of infection.
