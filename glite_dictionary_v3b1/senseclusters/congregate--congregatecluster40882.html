Synonyms

  • assemble
    3.3 11/m
    assemble brings people together in one place for a planned activity, while congregate means simply to come together as a group.
  • come together
    2.7 13/m
    come together describes people uniting to work or agree, while congregate focuses on physically gathering in a place.
  • gather
    1.0 65/m
    gather can mean collecting people, objects, or information broadly, while congregate usually means people meet physically for a purpose.
  • muster
    5.4 6.14/m
    muster often means calling or gathering people formally, for action or duty, while congregate is a general coming together.
  • flock
    2.0 5.18/m
    flock suggests many people move quickly toward one place, while congregate simply means people come together in a group.
