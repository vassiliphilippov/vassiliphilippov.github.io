tack

verb
change direction or approach suddenly
Pronunciation: / tæk / (AmE), / tæk / (BrE)
to abruptly change the direction, policy, or course of action, especially to do the opposite of what was previously pursued

Examples

To tack the plan
If the project runs into trouble, we may need to tack and reconsider our strategy.
Midway through the negotiations, she tacked and took a much harder stance.
When criticism grew, the government decided to tack and adopt a new approach.

Related Terms

  • antonym: persist - to continue steadily in an action or course
  • synonym: veer - to change direction suddenly
