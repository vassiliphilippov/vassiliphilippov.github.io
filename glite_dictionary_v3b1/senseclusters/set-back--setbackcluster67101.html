set-back

noun
architectural step where building narrows
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛtˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈsɛtˌbæk / (BrE)
A part of a wall or building where it suddenly becomes narrower, usually in steps moving upward, often for structural or aesthetic reasons.

Examples

Building set-backs
Historic buildings often feature decorative set-backs that create distinctive silhouettes.
The architect added a set-back on the upper floors to create more terrace space.
The new skyscraper was designed with multiple set-backs to comply with zoning regulations.
