crescent-cell anemia

noun
anemia with crescent-shaped red blood cells
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɛsənt sɛl əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˈkrɛsnt sɛl əˈniːmiə /; / ˈkrɛsənt sɛl əˈniːmiə / (BrE)
A hereditary or congenital form of anemia, primarily affecting people of African descent, in which red blood cells become crescent-shaped and break down prematurely.

Examples

Children with crescent-cell anemia may experience episodes of pain and fatigue due to reduced oxygen flow.
Research on treatments for crescent-cell anemia has significantly advanced in recent years.
The doctor explained that crescent-cell anemia is a genetic disorder affecting the shape of red blood cells.
Crescent-cell anaemia is inherited and often detected in early childhood.
Doctors monitor patients with crescent-cell anaemia for complications such as infections and organ damage.
She was diagnosed with crescent-cell anaemia after experiencing frequent episodes of pain and fatigue.
