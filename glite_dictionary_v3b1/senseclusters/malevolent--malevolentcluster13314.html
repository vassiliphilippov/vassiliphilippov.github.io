malevolent

adjective
having a harmful or evil influence
9.9
Pronunciation: / məˈlɛvələnt / (AmE), / məˈlɛvələnt / (BrE)
having or showing a desire to cause harm or evil to others

Examples

Malevolent spirit
People believed that the ruins were haunted by a malevolent spirit.
She gave him a malevolent glare that sent shivers down his spine.
The malevolent influence of the dictator was felt throughout the country.
He always wore a malevolent grin when speaking about his rivals.
Some believed the haunted house was inhabited by a malevolent spirit.
The villain in the story was truly malevolent, plotting revenge at every turn.
