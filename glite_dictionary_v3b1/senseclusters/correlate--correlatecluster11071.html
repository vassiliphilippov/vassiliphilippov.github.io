correlate

verb
show a relationship between things
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔːrəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒrɪləˌteɪt /; / ˈkɒrɪˌleɪt / (BrE)
to show or have a mutual or reciprocal relationship or connection between two or more things

Examples

To correlate results
He tried to correlate his past experiences with his current behavior.
It's difficult to correlate these findings with previous studies.
The researchers attempted to correlate environmental factors with disease rates.
Researchers found that the two variables correlate strongly in most cases.
The increase in temperature does not necessarily correlate with higher levels of pollution.
You should check whether the symptoms correlate with those described in previous studies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: associate - to connect or bring into relation, as in thought
  • antonym: disconnect - to separate or break the relationship between things
  • synonym: relate - to show or make a connection between things
