complicity

noun
involvement as an accomplice in wrongdoing
5.1 6.7
Pronunciation: /kəm.ˈplɪs.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /kəm.ˈplɪs.ə.tiː/ (AmE), /kəm.ˈplɪs.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /kəm.ˈplɪs.ə.tiː/ (BrE)

Examples

Complicity in crime

The investigation revealed his complicity in the fraudulent scheme.
The judge considered the defendant’s complicity when delivering the sentence.
