malice

noun
desire to harm others
5.0 5.1
Pronunciation: /ˈmæ.lɪs/ /; / /ˈmæ.ləs/ (AmE), /ˈmæ.lɪs/ (BrE)
the intention or desire to cause harm, pain, or suffering to others

Examples

He denied that there was any malice behind his comment.
She acted with such malice that nobody trusted her motives.
The anonymous letter was written out of pure malice and jealousy.
