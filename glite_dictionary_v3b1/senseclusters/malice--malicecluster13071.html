Synonyms

  • maliciousness

    maliciousness is the quality of wanting to hurt others, while malice names the specific intention to cause harm.
  • malintent
    9.3 0.04/m
    malintent is a direct intention to harm someone, while malice can mean a general wish to cause pain.
  • malevolence
    8.8 0.48/m
    malevolence is a formal word for wanting great harm or evil, while malice is the everyday word for harm intent.
  • malignity
    9.9 0.09/m
    malignity is long-lasting, intense ill will toward others, while malice is a more general wish or intent to harm.
  • venom
    7.3 1.33/m
    venom is a bitter, poisonous feeling of hate, while malice is the plain intention or desire to cause harm.
  • spitefulness
    7.4 0.04/m
    spitefulness is mean behavior that tries to upset someone, while malice is the deliberate wish to cause real harm.
  • spite
    4.4 27/m
    spite is a small, often petty wish to annoy or hurt someone, while malice is a stronger desire to cause harm.
