cautionary

adjective
serving as a warning
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔʃəˌnɛri /; / ˈkɔʃəˌneri / (AmE), / ˈkɔːʃənəri / (BrE)
serving as a warning to prevent danger or undesirable outcomes

Examples

A cautionary tale
Her failure served as a cautionary tale for her classmates.
The film ended with a cautionary message about the dangers of texting while driving.
The teacher gave a cautionary lecture about the consequences of plagiarism.
A cautionary symbol was placed on the fence to deter trespassers from entering the property.
Some ancient charms were believed to have cautionary powers, protecting families from misfortune.
The old tales acted as cautionary legends to keep children from wandering into the woods.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exhortatory - acting as encouragement or urging; providing warning or guidance
  • synonym: prophylactic - serving to prevent or ward off disease or danger
  • synonym: warning - giving notice of danger
