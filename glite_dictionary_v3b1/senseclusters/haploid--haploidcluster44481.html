haploid

adjective
having a single set of chromosomes
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhæplɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈhæplɔɪd / (BrE)
(of a cell or organism) having a single set of unpaired chromosomes; characteristic of gametes such as eggs and sperm.

Examples

Haploid cell
Most gametes in animals and plants are haploid and contain only one set of chromosomes.
Researchers studied the genetic traits of haploid yeast cells in the laboratory.
The haploid number is restored to diploid after fertilization occurs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diploid - (of a cell or organism) having two complete sets of chromosomes
