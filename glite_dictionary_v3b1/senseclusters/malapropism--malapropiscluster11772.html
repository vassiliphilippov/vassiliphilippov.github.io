malapropism

noun
mistaken use of similar-sounding word
Pronunciation: / ˈmæləˌprɑːˌpɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈmaləprɒˌpɪzəm / (BrE)
the mistaken use of a word in place of a similar-sounding one, often with unintentionally amusing effect

Examples

A malapropism mistake
Calling a 'fire distinguisher' instead of 'fire extinguisher' is a classic malapropism.
His speech was full of malapropisms that made the audience chuckle.
The comedian deliberately used malapropisms to add humor to her performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: solecism - a grammatical mistake in speech or writing
