military advisor

noun
officer advising allied troops
1.0 3.7
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪl.əˌtɛr.i ædˈvaɪ.zər /; / ˈmɪl.ɪˌtɛri ædˈvaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈmɪl.ɪtə.ri ədˈvaɪ.zə /; / ˈmɪl.ɪ.tər.i ədˈvaɪ.zə / (BrE)
a military officer who provides guidance and expertise to the armed forces of an allied nation, often without engaging directly in combat

Examples

Military advisor to commanders
As a military advisor, she worked closely with local commanders to improve their operational effectiveness.
During the conflict, several military advisors were embedded with the foreign army to provide strategic counsel.
The country sent a military advisor to assist its allies in training their infantry forces.
After years of service as a military adviser overseas, he returned home with a wealth of experience.
As a military adviser, she was instrumental in developing cooperation between the two allied forces.
The country requested several military advisers to help train their army in modern tactics.
