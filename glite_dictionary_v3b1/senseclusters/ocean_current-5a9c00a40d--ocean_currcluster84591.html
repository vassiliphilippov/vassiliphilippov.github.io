ocean current

noun
continuous movement of seawater
7.2 3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊʃən ˈkɜrənt / (AmE), / ˈəʊʃən ˈkʌrənt / (BrE)
A continuous, directed movement of seawater generated by forces acting upon the ocean, such as wind, temperature, and salinity differences.

Examples

Strong ocean current

Scientists study ocean currents to better understand global weather patterns.
The Gulf Stream is a well-known ocean current that influences the climate of Western Europe.
