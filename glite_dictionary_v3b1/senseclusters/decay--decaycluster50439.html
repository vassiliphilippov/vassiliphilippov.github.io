decay

verb
to rot or deteriorate over time
Pronunciation: / dɪˈkeɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈkeɪ / (BrE)
to gradually deteriorate, decompose, or fall into ruin, especially as a result of neglect, age, or natural processes

Examples

To decay over time
As the wood was exposed to moisture, it started to decay from within.
If you leave food out in the open, it will quickly decay and become unsafe to eat.
The old mansion began to decay after years of abandonment and neglect.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crumble - to break apart or fall to pieces, especially due to age or neglect
  • synonym: deteriorate - to become progressively worse
  • antonym: flourish - to grow or develop in a healthy or vigorous way
  • synonym: rot - to decompose and break down due to bacteria or fungi
