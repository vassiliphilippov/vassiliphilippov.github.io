contrast

noun
a clear distinction or difference
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑnˌtræst /; / ˈkɑːntræst / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.trɑːst /; / ˈkɒntrɑːst / (BrE)
a clear difference or distinction between two or more things, especially when compared

Examples

Sharp contrast
Her optimistic attitude stood in clear contrast to his pessimism.
The contrast between light and dark was striking in the painting.
There is a sharp contrast between the rural and urban lifestyles represented in the novel.
By contrast, rural life tends to be quieter and slower-paced than city life.
The contrast in styles between the two artists is immediately noticeable.
There is a clear contrast between her cheerful personality and his reserved demeanor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: distinction - a difference or contrast between similar things or people
  • antonym: similarity - the state of being similar; likeness; resemblance
