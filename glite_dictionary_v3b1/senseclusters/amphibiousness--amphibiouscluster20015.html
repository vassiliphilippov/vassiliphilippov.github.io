amphibiousness

noun
ability on land and water
8.5
Pronunciation: / æmˈfɪbiəsnəs / (AmE), / æmˈfɪbiəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being able to live or operate both on land and in water

Examples

The amphibiousness of frogs
The amphibiousness of frogs allows them to thrive in both ponds and forests.
Engineers admired the amphibiousness of the new rescue vehicle during the flood.
Some military operations rely on the amphibiousness of specialized equipment.
