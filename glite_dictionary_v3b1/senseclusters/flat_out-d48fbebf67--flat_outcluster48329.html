flat out

adverb
at top speed; with maximum effort
5.7
Pronunciation: / flæt aʊt / (AmE), / flæt aʊt / (BrE)
as fast or as hard as possible; at top speed or with maximum effort

Examples

Run flat out
He ran flat out and barely made it to the train before it left.
She drove flat out for two hours to reach the hospital in time.
The team worked flat out to finish the project before the deadline.

Related Terms

  • antonym: at a crawl - moving very slowly
  • synonym: full tilt - at full speed or with maximum energy
