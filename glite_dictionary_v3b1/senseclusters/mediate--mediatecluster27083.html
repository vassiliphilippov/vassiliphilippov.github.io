mediate

verb
act as intermediary to resolve disputes
2.0 4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈmiːdiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈmiːdieɪt / (BrE)
to act as an intermediary in order to resolve a disagreement or bring about an agreement between conflicting parties

Examples

To mediate a dispute
He is often called upon to mediate disagreements in the community.
She skillfully mediated a compromise that satisfied both parties.
The manager was asked to mediate in the dispute between employees.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arbitrate - to officially settle a dispute between two parties
  • antonym: exacerbate - to make a situation or a conflict worse
  • synonym: intervene - to become involved in a situation, especially to improve or prevent it from getting worse
