excused

adjective
given permission to be absent or exempt
3.5 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / …™kÀàskjuzd / (AmE), / …™ksÀàkjuÀêzd / (BrE)
allowed to leave or not take part in something, typically because permission has been granted to be absent or not to do something required.

Examples

Excused from duty
She was excused from jury duty due to a prior commitment.
Students with a doctor's note will be excused from the exam.
The teacher excused him from participating in gym class because of his injury.
