cadency

noun
rhythmic flow or pattern
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪdənsi / (AmE), / ˈkeɪdənsi / (BrE)
the quality or state of having a rhythmic flow, especially a recurrent rhythmic pattern in language, music, or motion

Examples

Musical cadency pattern
In music, the cadency of the notes set a calming rhythm throughout the piece.
Poets often rely on the cadency of language to create memorable verses.
The cadency of her speech captivated the audience during the recital.
