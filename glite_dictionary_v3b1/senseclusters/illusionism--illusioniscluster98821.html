illusionism

noun
art of creating illusions
Pronunciation: / ɪˈluːʒənɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪˈluːʒənɪzəm / (BrE)
the practice or technique of creating the appearance of reality, especially in art or performance

Examples

Artistic illusionism
The artist used illusionism to make the painted window look almost real.
Stage magicians rely on illusionism to amaze their audiences with seemingly impossible tricks.
Renaissance painters developed new techniques of illusionism to create depth and perspective in their works.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abstraction - artistic style that does not attempt to represent reality
  • synonym: realism - artistic style aiming to represent subjects truthfully
