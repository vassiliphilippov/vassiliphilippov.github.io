cleave

verb
adhere or stick together
Pronunciation: / kliːv / (AmE), / kliːv / (BrE)
to stick closely to something or someone; to remain attached or faithful

Examples

Cleave to tradition
Despite the strong wind, the paper managed to cleave to the wet pavement.
The child would cleave to his mother whenever he felt afraid.
These beliefs have cleaved to the community through many generations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cling - to hold on tightly or adhere closely
  • antonym: detach - to separate or remove from something to which it is attached
  • synonym: stick - to attach or become fixed to a surface or object
