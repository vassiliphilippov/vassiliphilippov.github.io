interrogative

adjective
relating to questions or inquiry
Pronunciation: / ɪn.təˈrɑː.ɡə.tɪv /; / ˌɪn.təˈrɑː.ɡə.tɪv / (AmE), / ɪn.təˈrɒ.ɡə.tɪv /; / ˌɪn.təˈrɒ.ɡə.tɪv / (BrE)
relating to or having the form of a question; used in asking questions

Examples

Interrogative sentence
She raised her eyebrow in an interrogative manner, waiting for an explanation.
Some sentences are interrogative, ending with a question mark.
The teacher's interrogative tone made the students feel nervous during the test.
An interrogative sentence often begins with a question word such as 'who', 'what', or 'where'.
Some languages use special verb endings for the interrogative mood.
The teacher asked the class to rewrite the statement as an interrogative form.

Related Terms

  • antonym: declarative - relating to statements rather than questions
  • antonym: imperative - relating to commands or requests
