radicle

noun
embryonic root
9.0
Pronunciation: /ˈræ.dɪ.kəl/ (AmE), /ˈræ.dɪ.kəl/ (BrE)
the part of a plant embryo that develops into the primary root during germination

Examples

Seed radicle
The radicle is the first part of the seedling to emerge during germination.
If the radicle is damaged, the plant may not develop a healthy root system.
Scientists observed the growth of the radicle under different soil conditions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: plumule - the part of a plant embryo that develops into the shoot
  • synonym: root - the part of a plant that typically lies below the surface and absorbs water and nutrients
