advocation

noun
church patronage right
9.9
Pronunciation: /æd.və.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /æd.və.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the right or privilege of presenting a clergyman to a church benefice, especially as a form of church patronage (historical)

Examples

Church advocation
In medieval England, the lord of the manor held the advocation of the local parish church.
The advocation was often passed down through noble families as a valuable privilege.
Disputes over advocation sometimes led to conflicts between landowners and church authorities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: patronage - the support or right to appoint someone to a position, especially in the church
