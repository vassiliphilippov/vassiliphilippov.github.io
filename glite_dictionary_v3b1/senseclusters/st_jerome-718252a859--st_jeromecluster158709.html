St. Jerome

proper noun
Christian saint and translator of the Bible
7.8
Pronunciation: /seɪnt dʒə.ˈroʊm/ (AmE), /seɪnt dʒə.ˈrəʊm/ (BrE)
A Christian saint and scholar (c. 347–420), best known for translating the Bible into Latin (the Vulgate) and recognized as one of the early Fathers and Doctors of the Church.

Examples

St. Jerome the scholar
St. Jerome is credited with producing the first complete Latin translation of the Bible.
Many Renaissance artists depicted St. Jerome in their paintings, often shown working in his study.
The feast day of St. Jerome is celebrated on September 30th in the Christian liturgical calendar.
