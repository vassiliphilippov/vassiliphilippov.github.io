Associate in Nursing

noun
two-year nursing degree (associate level)
4.3
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈsoʊ.siː.ət ɪn ˈnɜːr.sɪŋ/ (AmE), /ə.ˈsəʊ.siː.ət ɪn ˈnɜː.sɪŋ/ (BrE)
an academic degree awarded after completion of a course of study in nursing, typically taking two years, qualifying graduates to become registered nurses.

Examples

An Associate in Nursing degree
Many community colleges offer an Associate in Nursing for aspiring healthcare professionals.
She earned her Associate in Nursing before continuing her studies for a bachelor's degree.
With an Associate in Nursing, you may be eligible to take the state licensure exam.
