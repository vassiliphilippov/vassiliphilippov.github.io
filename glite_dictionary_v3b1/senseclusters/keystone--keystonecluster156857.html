keystone

noun
central stone at top of an arch
7.2 0.09/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàkiÀêsto än / (AmE), / ÀàkiÀêst…ô än / (BrE)
the central, wedge-shaped stone at the apex of an arch or vault, locking the whole together

Examples

Stone keystone in an arch
Architects admired the intricately carved keystone above the entrance.
The mason carefully placed the keystone to complete the archway.
Without the keystone, the entire structure would collapse.

Synonyms

  • key
    8.4 0.06/m
    key is a shorter, common architectural name for the top locking stone, while keystone is the full traditional technical term.
  • quoin
    9.9 0.00/m
    quoin is an architectural term often used in building descriptions, while keystone names the same central locking stone.
  • coign
    9.9 0.00/m
    coign is an older spelling seen in historic texts for the arch's top locking stone, while keystone is the modern standard word.
  • headstone
    6.4 0.07/m
    headstone sometimes names the top stone in architecture but often confuses learners with gravestones, while keystone clearly means the arch's locking stone.
