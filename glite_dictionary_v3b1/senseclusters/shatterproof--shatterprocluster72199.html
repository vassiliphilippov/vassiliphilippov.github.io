shatterproof

adjective
designed not to shatter into pieces
Pronunciation: / ˈʃæt̬ɚˌpruf / (AmE), / ˈʃætəˌpruːf / (BrE)
(of a material or object) made so that it does not break into sharp pieces when it is hit or dropped.

Examples

Shatterproof windows
She installed shatterproof windows in her car for extra safety.
The phone case is advertised as shatterproof and highly durable.
These glasses are made from shatterproof plastic, making them suitable for children.

Related Terms

  • antonym: breakable - capable of being broken easily
  • synonym: unbreakable - not able to be broken
