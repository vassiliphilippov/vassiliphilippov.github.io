suppositum

noun
fraudulent impostor
9.9
Pronunciation: / səˈpoʊzɪtəm / (AmE), / səˈpɒzɪtəm / (BrE)
an impostor or person fraudulently presented as someone else, especially to gain rights or property

Examples

Fraudulent suppositum
The investigation revealed that the supposed prince was actually a suppositum seeking to claim the inheritance.
In old legal cases, a suppositum might be used to fraudulently obtain property or titles.
The family was shocked to discover the heir was a suppositum and not truly related to them.
