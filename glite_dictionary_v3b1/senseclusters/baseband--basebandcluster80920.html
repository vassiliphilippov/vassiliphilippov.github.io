baseband

noun
original signal frequency
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪsbænd / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsbænd / (BrE)
the original frequency range of a signal before it is modified for transmission or processing

Examples

Analyze the baseband
The technician analyzed the baseband to check for any distortions before transmission.
Audio signals are often processed in baseband before being converted for radio broadcast.
In digital communication, the baseband signal is typically modulated to a higher frequency for transmission.

Related Terms

  • antonym: broadband - a wide range of frequencies used to transmit multiple signals simultaneously
