spur

noun
a sharply pointed projection
5.4 0.69/m
Pronunciation: / sp…ùÀê / (AmE), / sp…úÀê / (BrE)
a sharply pointed projection from a surface or structure, often resembling a spike or prong

Examples

A plant spur
A sharp spur on the plant's stem protects it from animals.
Mountain ranges sometimes extend a long, narrow spur into the surrounding plains.
The rooster has a distinctive spur on each leg.

Synonyms

  • prong
    4.3 1.39/m
    prong is a thin pointed projection often one of several on a tool or antler, while spur is a single projection from a surface.
  • tine
    8.1 1.17/m
    tine is a sharp projecting point usually on a fork, pitchfork, or antler, while spur is a projection from a surface.
  • spike
    3.6 4.08/m
    spike is a long, thin sharp point or object used for defense or support, while spur is a surface projection.

Related Terms
