begin

verb
start doing or experiencing something
1.0
Pronunciation: / bɪˈɡɪn /; / bəˈɡɪn / (AmE), / bɪˈɡɪn / (BrE)
to start or initiate something, such as an activity, event, process, or speech

Examples

To begin a project
He began a new book during his vacation.
She began her speech with a humorous story to relax the audience.
We began dinner as soon as everyone arrived.
He began learning Japanese after traveling to Tokyo.
Many students begin French in middle school.
She began Spanish lessons when she was only six years old.
'Let me explain,' he began in a calm voice.
She paused a moment, then began, 'Thank you all for coming.'
The speaker began by greeting the audience warmly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: end - to bring or come to a final point; to finish
  • antonym: finish - to bring something to an end; the opposite of starting
  • synonym: start - to take the first step or action in an activity
  • antonym: stop - to cease doing something
