AM

noun
radio signal modulation technique
6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈeɪ.em/ /; / /æm/ (AmE), /ˈeɪ.em/ /; / /æm/ (BrE)
a method of modulating a radio signal in which the amplitude of the carrier wave is varied in accordance with the signal being sent

Examples

AM radio signal
Early car radios often only received AM frequencies.
Many classic radio stations still broadcast in AM.
The quality of AM radio is susceptible to interference from electrical equipment.
