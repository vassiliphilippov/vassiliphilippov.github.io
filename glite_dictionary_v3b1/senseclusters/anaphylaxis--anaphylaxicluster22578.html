anaphylaxis

noun
severe allergic reaction
4.6 8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌænəfəˈlæksɪs / (AmE), / ˌanəfəˈlaksɪs / (BrE)
a severe and often life-threatening allergic reaction to a substance, typically occurring after previous exposure

Examples

Severe anaphylaxis reaction
Exposure to peanuts triggered anaphylaxis in the young patient.
If untreated, anaphylaxis can rapidly progress to shock or death.
People with severe allergies should carry medication in case of anaphylaxis.
