good

adjective
valid, in force (law/agreement)
Pronunciation: / ɡʊd / (AmE), / ɡʊd / (BrE)
in valid, genuine, or undamaged condition; legally or physically acceptable

Examples

Good condition
The contract remains good until both parties agree to terminate it.
The membership card is only good for one year from the date of issue.
This permit is good for unlimited entries within the stated period.
Even though the sell-by date has passed, the cheese is perfectly good to eat.
She checked the eggs to make sure they were good before making breakfast.
The milk is still good, so you don't need to throw it away.
Only good tickets purchased from authorized sellers will be honored at the concert.
She examined the signature carefully to confirm it was good and not a forgery.
The cashier checked to make sure the bill was good before accepting it.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bad - not good in condition; damaged or impaired
  • antonym: counterfeit - made in exact imitation of something valuable with the intention to deceive
  • synonym: fresh - food that is recently obtained or produced and not stale or spoiled
  • synonym: genuine - truly what it is said to be; authentic
  • synonym: healthy - in a good physical or mental condition
  • synonym: sound - in good condition; free from damage or injury
  • antonym: spoiled - food that is no longer fit to eat because it has deteriorated
  • synonym: valid - having legal force or effectiveness
  • antonym: void - having no legal force or effect
