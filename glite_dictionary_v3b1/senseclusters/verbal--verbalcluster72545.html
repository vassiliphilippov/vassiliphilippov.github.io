verbal

adjective
expressed in words; relating to words
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈvɜrbəl /; / ˈvɝːbəl / (AmE), / ˈvɜː.bəl /; / ˈvɜːbəl / (BrE)
relating to or involving words or language, especially as opposed to action, images, or symbols; having skill in the use of words

Examples

Verbal skills
He preferred verbal instructions to diagrams when learning new tasks.
Her verbal explanation clarified the issue for everyone in the room.
The contract was a verbal agreement, not written down.
His verbal fluency impressed the interviewers.
She has a strong verbal aptitude that helps her excel in debates.
The test measures verbal reasoning skills rather than mathematical ability.
Children with strong verbal reasoning tend to excel in language arts.
Her verbal skills made her an excellent communicator.
The test measures both mathematical and verbal ability.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nonverbal - not involving words or speech
  • synonym: oral - spoken rather than written; transmitted by speech
