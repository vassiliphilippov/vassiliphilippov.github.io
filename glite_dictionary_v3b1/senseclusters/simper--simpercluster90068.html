simper

verb
smile in a silly or affected way
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪmpɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpə / (BrE)
to smile in a self-conscious, affected, or silly way, often suggesting insincerity or derision

Examples

To simper at jokes
He tends to simper at his boss's jokes, even when they aren't funny.
Martha simpered awkwardly, trying to hide her embarrassment during the interview.
She couldn't help but simper when the teacher praised her drawing in front of the class.

Related Terms

  • synonym: smile - to make a facial expression showing pleasure or amusement
  • synonym: smirk - to smile in an irritatingly smug, conceited, or silly way
