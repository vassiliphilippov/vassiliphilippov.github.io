reprehend

verb
rebuke; express strong disapproval
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛprɪˈhɛnd / (AmE), / ˌrɛprɪˈhɛnd / (BrE)
to express strong disapproval or criticism of someone or something; to rebuke or reprimand

Examples

To reprehend bad behavior
He was quick to reprehend any sign of dishonesty among his peers.
The professor did not hesitate to reprehend students for their lack of preparation.
They openly reprehended the committee's decision, calling it unjust and poorly considered.

Related Terms

  • antonym: approve - to accept or express a favorable opinion
  • synonym: rebuke - to express sharp disapproval or criticism
  • synonym: reprimand - to formally express disapproval or criticism
