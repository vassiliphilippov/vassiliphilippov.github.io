maraca

noun
Latin American percussion instrument
4.2 4.1
Pronunciation: /mə.ˈræk.ə/ /; / /mə.ˈrɑː.kə/ (AmE), /mə.ˈræk.ə/ /; / /mə.ˈrɑː.kə/ (BrE)
A percussion instrument consisting of a hollow gourd or container filled with small objects such as pebbles or beans, shaken to produce sound and typically played in pairs.

Examples

A pair of maracas
He handed me a maraca so I could join in with the song.
She played the maraca rhythmically during the band's performance.
The children made their own maracas with plastic bottles and beans as part of a music lesson.
