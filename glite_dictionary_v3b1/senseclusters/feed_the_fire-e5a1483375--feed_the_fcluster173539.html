feed the fire

verb
add fuel literally or intensify a situation
Pronunciation: / fid ðə ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / fiːd ðə ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
to add fuel to a fire to keep it burning, or to make a situation or conflict more intense

Examples

To feed the fire with wood
Before the logs were gone, we had to feed the fire again to keep warm.
Bringing up old grievances will only feed the fire in their argument.
Don't feed the fire by spreading more rumors about the issue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fan the flames - to make a bad situation worse
