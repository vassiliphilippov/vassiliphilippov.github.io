one by one

adverb
individually, in succession
1.5 1.3
Pronunciation: /wʌn baɪ wʌn/ /; / /hwʌn baɪ hwʌn/ (AmE), /wʌn baɪ wʌn/ /; / /wɒn baɪ wɒn/ (BrE)
used to say that things happen or are done individually, one after another, not all at the same time

Examples

One by one in order
She handed out the gifts one by one to each guest.

The students entered the classroom one by one as the bell rang.
As the candles were lit one by one, the room grew brighter.
She checked the documents one by one to make sure nothing was missing.
The students entered the classroom one by one as the bell rang.

The guests approached the host one by one to express their thanks.
The teacher called on the students one by one to answer the question.
