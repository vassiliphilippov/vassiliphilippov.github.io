one by one

adverb
individually, in succession
1.4 9.94/m
Pronunciation: / wÊŒn baÉª wÊŒn / (AmE), / wÊŒn baÉª wÊŒn / (BrE)
used to say that things happen or are done individually, one after another, not all at the same time

Examples

One by one in order
She handed out the gifts one by one to each guest.
The lights went out one by one until the room was completely dark.
The students entered the classroom one by one as the bell rang.
As the candles were lit one by one, the room grew brighter.
She checked the documents one by one to make sure nothing was missing.
The students entered the classroom one by one as the bell rang.
As the clock struck midnight, the candles went out one by one.
The guests approached the host one by one to express their thanks.
The teacher called on the students one by one to answer the question.

Synonyms

  • one at a time
    1.9 4.61/m
    one at a time treats each person or thing in turn, while one by one stresses doing items individually, not all at once.
  • one after another
    2.3 1.71/m
    one after another happens in order, with each event following the last in a chain, while one by one highlights individual actions.
  • one after the other
    2.0 1.26/m
    one after the other follows immediately, with very little gap between events, while one by one focuses on items done separately.
  • individually
    2.5 13/m
    individually treats each person or thing on its own, whereas one by one implies a series where each happens one at a time.
  • singly
    4.6 0.85/m
    singly means one at a time or alone, while one by one more often suggests a clear sequence of actions.
  • separately
    2.1 14/m
    separately means apart or not together in space or time, while one by one stresses doing things in order, one after another.
  • consecutively
    4.1 0.61/m
    consecutively means happening one right after another without breaks, whereas one by one can be used more casually for each item.
  • sequentially
    4.5 1.21/m
    sequentially means following a set order or plan step by step, while one by one is more general for doing items individually.
