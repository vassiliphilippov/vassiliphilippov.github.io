bergamot mint

noun
aromatic herb with showy flowers, native to eastern North America
Pronunciation: / ˈbɜːrɡəˌmɑt mɪnt /; / ˈbɝːɡəˌmɑt mɪnt / (AmE), / ˈbɜːɡəmət mɪnt /; / ˈbɜːɡəmɒt mɪnt / (BrE)
a type of aromatic mint plant with a citrus-like (bergamot) scent, often used in teas and potpourri

Examples

Bergamot mint plant
Many herbal teas contain bergamot mint for its refreshing, citrus-floral aroma.
Pollinators like bees and butterflies are attracted to bergamot mint during the summer months.
The garden was filled with bergamot mint blooming in shades of red and lavender.
Dried bergamot mint is sometimes used in potpourri because of its pleasant aroma.
She added a few leaves of bergamot mint to her tea for a citrusy flavor.
The gardener recommended planting bergamot mint to attract bees and butterflies.
