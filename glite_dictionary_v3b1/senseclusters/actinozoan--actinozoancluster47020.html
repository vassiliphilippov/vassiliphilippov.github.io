actinozoan

noun
sessile marine animal (sea anemones and corals)
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌæk.tə.nəˈzoʊ.ən / (AmE), / ˌæk.tɪ.nəˈzəʊ.ən / (BrE)
A member of a group of sessile marine animals, including sea anemones and corals, characterized by a polyp form with the medusoid phase suppressed.

Examples

Marine actinozoans group
Many actinozoans form complex coral reefs in warm, shallow seas.
Scientists study actinozoans to better understand marine biodiversity.
The sea anemone is a solitary actinozoan with colorful tentacles.
