carboy

noun
large bottle for corrosive liquids
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑr.bɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.bɔɪ / (BrE)
a large, rigid bottle typically used for storing or transporting corrosive liquids, often enclosed in a protective outer container

Examples

A glass carboy
A glass carboy was delivered to the facility, packed in a wooden crate.
He carefully poured the solution from the carboy into smaller containers.
The laboratory stored the sulfuric acid in a carboy for safety.
