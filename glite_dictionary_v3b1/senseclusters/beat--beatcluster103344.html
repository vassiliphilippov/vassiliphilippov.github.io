beat

noun
member of the Beat Generation; nonconformist
9.4
Pronunciation: / bit / (AmE), / biːt / (BrE)
A member of the Beat Generation, characterized by nonconformity in dress and behavior, often associated with the literary movement of the 1950s and 1960s.

Examples

A beat poet
He considered himself a beat, embracing the values of the counterculture movement.
Her poetry was influenced by the beat writers she admired.
In the 1960s, many young people aspired to be a beat and reject mainstream society.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bohemian - person leading an unconventional lifestyle, often in the arts
