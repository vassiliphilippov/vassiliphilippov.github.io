thrown-away

adjective
designed to be discarded; disposable
3.7
Pronunciation: /θroʊn.ə.ˈweɪ/ /; / /θroʊn ə.ˈweɪ/ (AmE), /θrəʊn.ə.ˈweɪ/ /; / /θrəʊn ə.ˈweɪ/ (BrE)
(of a product or item) designed to be used once or for a short time and then discarded; disposable.

Examples

Thrown-away cup

Many companies sell cheap, thrown-away electronics that fail after only a few months of use.
Critics blame a thrown-away culture for rising waste and environmental damage in the city.
