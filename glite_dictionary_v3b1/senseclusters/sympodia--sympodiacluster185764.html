sympodia

noun
fused legs condition
Pronunciation: / sɪmˈpoʊdiə / (AmE), / sɪmˈpəʊdiə / (BrE)
a rare congenital condition in which a person's legs are fused together

Examples

Congenital sympodia
Doctors diagnosed the newborn with sympodia after observing the fused lower limbs.
Cases of sympodia are extremely rare and often require specialized medical care.
The medical team discussed treatment options for the infant born with sympodia.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sirenomelia - a congenital disorder where the legs are fused, also called mermaid syndrome
