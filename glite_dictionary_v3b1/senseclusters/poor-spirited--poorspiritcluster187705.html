Lacking courage or determination; timid and easily discouraged.

Examples

A poor-spirited apology
Instead of standing up for his beliefs, he gave a poor-spirited apology and walked away.
She thought his poor-spirited response to the criticism showed a lack of leadership.
The team became poor-spirited after several crushing defeats.

Synonyms

  • fainthearted
    7.7 0.12/m
    fainthearted is easily afraid to try new things, while poor-spirited is timid and often quickly loses courage.
  • pusillanimous
    9.9 0.16/m
    pusillanimous is formal and describes weak courage in serious settings, while poor-spirited is simple and often easily discouraged.
  • weak-kneed
    7.1 0.03/m
    weak-kneed describes someone who lacks courage under pressure, while poor-spirited is simply timid and often easily discouraged.
  • wimpy
    5.7 0.98/m
    wimpy sounds informal and rude, meaning weak or lacking guts, while poor-spirited is more neutral and timid.
  • weak
    1.5 13/m
    weak often means lacking strength or moral backbone in many areas, while poor-spirited focuses on fear and low courage.
  • spiritless
    6.0 0.11/m
    spiritless shows no energy or enthusiasm, often from tiredness or illness, while poor-spirited means deeply lacking courage.
