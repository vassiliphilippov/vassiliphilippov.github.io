shakiness

noun
unsteady or trembling motion
Pronunciation: / ˈʃeɪkinəs / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪkɪnɪs /; / ˈʃeɪkinɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being unsteady, trembling, or lacking firmness (physically or emotionally)

Examples

The shakiness of voice
He tried to hide the shakiness in his voice during the speech.
The shakiness of her hands made it difficult to pour the tea.
There was a noticeable shakiness in the old bridge as we walked across.
Her voice had a slight shakiness when she spoke in public.
Investors worried about the shakiness of the financial markets.
The shakiness of the ladder made me nervous as I climbed it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: instability - lack of stability; tendency to change or fail
  • antonym: steadiness - firm, steady motion or state
  • synonym: tremor - a slight shaking movement
