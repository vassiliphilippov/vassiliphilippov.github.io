converse

noun
proposition obtained by reversing terms
8.2 1.16/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëÀênÀåv…ùÀês / (AmE), / Ààk…ín.v…úÀês / (BrE)
(Logic, mathematics) A statement or proposition derived from another by reversing its terms, especially in conditional statements.

Examples

The converse of a statement
In logic, the converse of 'If it rains, then the ground is wet' is 'If the ground is wet, then it rains.'
Students often confuse the converse of a statement with its inverse.
The converse is not always true, even if the original proposition is valid.
