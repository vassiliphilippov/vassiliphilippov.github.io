mischaracterize

verb
to describe falsely
8.0
Pronunciation: /mɪs.ˈkær.ək.tə.raɪz/ (AmE), /mɪs.ˈkær.ək.tə.raɪz/ (BrE)
to describe someone or something in a false or misleading way

Examples

To mischaracterize a person
It is unfair to mischaracterize her comments as rude when she was simply being honest.
The report mischaracterized the company's financial situation, causing confusion among investors.
He warned not to mischaracterize the results of the study in the media.
The politician claimed that the media had mischaracterized his comments during the interview.
It is unfair to mischaracterize her actions without knowing the full story.
The report mischaracterizes the company's financial situation, making it seem worse than it is.
The politician accused the media of mischaracterizing his statements during the interview.
It is unfair to mischaracterize her intentions based on a single mistake.
The report mischaracterized the company's financial situation, causing unnecessary concern.

Related Terms

  • antonym: characterize - to describe the qualities or features of someone or something
  • synonym: distort - to change the facts or meaning of something so it is no longer accurate
  • synonym: misrepresent - to give a false or misleading account of something
