concentrate

verb
gather or come together
1.0 3.0
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈsen.treɪt/ /; / /kɑːn.ˈsen.treɪt/ (AmE), /kən.ˈsen.treɪt/ /; / /kɒn.ˈsen.treɪt/ (BrE)
to bring people or things together in one place or under one authority

Examples

To concentrate forces
After the earthquake, emergency services quickly concentrated in the affected region to help those in need.
Many financial institutions concentrate in the city center, making it a business hub.
Wildlife tends to concentrate near water sources during the dry season.
Recent reforms aim to concentrate economic power in the capital city.
The government decided to concentrate all emergency services under one department.
They concentrated decision-making authority among a small group of managers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: centralize - to bring under a single, central authority
  • antonym: disperse - to scatter or spread over a wide area
