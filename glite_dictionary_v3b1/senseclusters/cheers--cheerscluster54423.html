cheers

interjection
friendly toast or thanks
6.7
Pronunciation: / tʃɪrz / (AmE), / tʃɪəz / (BrE)
a friendly or informal expression used to wish someone well, say thanks, or say goodbye (especially in British English, or as a toast before drinking)

Examples

Say cheers before drinking
Everyone raised their glasses and said 'cheers' before the meal.
He handed me the book and I replied, 'cheers!' with a smile.
As she left the office, she waved and called out 'cheers' to her colleagues.
I have to go now, so cheers and have a great evening.
He waved and said cheers as he left the café.
Thanks for your help today! Cheers!
She handed him the book, and he replied with a quick cheers before leaving.
"Cheers for helping me carry these bags," he said gratefully.
When the taxi driver dropped her off, she smiled and said, "cheers!"
