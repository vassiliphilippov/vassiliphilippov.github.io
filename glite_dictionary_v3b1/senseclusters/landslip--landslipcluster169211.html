landslip

noun
a sudden slide of earth or rock down a slope
8.5 6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈlænd.slɪp/ (AmE), /ˈlænd.slɪp/ (BrE)
a mass of earth or rock that moves down a slope, often suddenly, especially from a mountain or cliff

Examples

A landslip after rain
After heavy rain, a landslip blocked the mountain road for several days.
Engineers worked to stabilize the hillside after the recent landslip.
The village was evacuated due to the risk of a landslip during the storm.
