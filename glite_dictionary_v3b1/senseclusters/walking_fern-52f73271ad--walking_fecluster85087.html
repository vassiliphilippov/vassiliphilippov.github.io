walking fern

noun
fern with fronds rooting at tip
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔkɪŋ fɜrn / (AmE), / ˈwɔːkɪŋ fɜːn / (BrE)
A type of fern with lance-shaped fronds that root at their tips, allowing the plant to spread in a 'walking' manner.

Examples

The walking fern plant
Because the fronds of the walking fern root at the tip, the plant slowly spreads across the mossy rocks.
Gardeners admire the resilience of the walking fern in challenging environments.
The walking fern can often be found growing in shaded limestone outcrops.
