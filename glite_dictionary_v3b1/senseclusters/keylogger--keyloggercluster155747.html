keylogger

noun
keystroke recording tool
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈkiːˌlɔɡər / (AmE), / ˈkiːˌlɒɡə / (BrE)
a software program or device that records every keystroke made on a computer or device, often without the user's knowledge

Examples

A keylogger program
A keylogger can capture sensitive information like passwords without the user's knowledge.
The security team discovered a keylogger installed on several company computers.
Using antivirus software can help protect your system from a keylogger attack.

Related Terms

  • synonym: malware - software designed to harm or gain unauthorized access to a computer
  • synonym: spyware - software that secretly collects information from a computer
