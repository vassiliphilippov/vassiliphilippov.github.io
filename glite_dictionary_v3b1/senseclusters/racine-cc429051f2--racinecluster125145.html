Racine

proper noun
French tragedian and advocate of Jansenism
9.9
Pronunciation: /rə.ˈsiːn/ (AmE), /rə.ˈsiːn/ (BrE)
(1639–1699) A French dramatist and advocate of Jansenism, known for his influential tragedies based on Greek and Roman themes.

Examples

Racine French playwright
Many scholars compare Racine's tragedies to those of Shakespeare and Sophocles.
Students of French literature study Racine for his masterful use of the alexandrine verse.
The themes of fate and passion are central to Racine's plays.
