tempera

noun
a painting medium using pigments with egg yolk or similar binders
8.4 6.8
Pronunciation: /ˈtem.pə.rə/ (AmE), /ˈtem.pə.rə/ (BrE)
a painting medium in which pigments are mixed with water and a water-soluble binder such as egg yolk, commonly used before the development of oil painting

Examples

Tempera paint
Mediaeval icons are often painted using tempera on wooden panels.
She mixed her own pigments for the tempera artwork in her studio.
The artist preferred working with tempera because of its bright, matte colors and fast drying time.

Related Terms

  • antonym: oil paint - a painting medium where pigments are mixed with oil, typically linseed oil
