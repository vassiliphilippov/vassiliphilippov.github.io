partial

adjective
showing favoritism or bias
4.7 1.98/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…ër É…ôl / (AmE), / Ààp…ëÀê É…ôl / (BrE)
favoring one side or person over others; showing bias or preference

Examples

Partial judge
Parents should strive not to be partial when settling disputes between their children.
The committee was criticized for being partial towards certain candidates.
The judge cannot be partial to either side in the case.

Synonyms

  • biased
    4.0 6.25/m
    biased unfairly favors one side or group, while partial means showing a preference for someone rather than being neutral.
  • preferential
    6.0 4.82/m
    preferential describes giving special advantage to a person or group, while partial is simply showing favor or bias.
  • partisan
    6.7 21/m
    partisan strongly supports a cause or party, often without fairness, while partial means favoring someone but not necessarily politically.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biased - showing unfair preference for or against something or someone
  • antonym: neutral - taking no sides or showing no bias
