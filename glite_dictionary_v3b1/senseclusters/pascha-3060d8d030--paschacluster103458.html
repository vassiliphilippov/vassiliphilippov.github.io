Pascha

noun
the Jewish feast of Passover
9.8 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…ëÀêsk…ô / (AmE), / Ààpask…ô / (BrE)
A name for the Jewish festival of Passover, commemorating the Israelites' liberation from slavery in Egypt; sometimes also used for Easter in Christian tradition.

Examples

Jewish Pascha festival
Early Christians often referred to Easter using the term Pascha, linking it to the Passover festival.
In some ancient texts, the celebration of Pascha is described in detail.
The date of Pascha was carefully calculated by Jewish scholars each year.
