Fresnel

noun
compact ringed lens
9.4
Pronunciation: /freɪ.ˈnel/ /; / /fre.ˈnel/ (AmE), /freɪ.ˈnel/ /; / /fre.ˈnel/ (BrE)
a type of compact lens with concentric rings that focuses light efficiently, commonly used in lighthouses and stage lighting

Examples

A Fresnel lens
The lighthouse used a large Fresnel to send its beam far across the water.
Modern stage lights often use a Fresnel to create a soft, even light.
Engineers admired the efficiency of the Fresnel in focusing light with less material.
