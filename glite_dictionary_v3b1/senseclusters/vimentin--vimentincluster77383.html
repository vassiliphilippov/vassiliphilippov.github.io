vimentin

noun
cell marker protein
Pronunciation: / vaɪˈmɛntɪn / (AmE), / vɪˈmɛntɪn / (BrE)
a protein used as a laboratory marker to identify mesenchymal cells or certain types of tumors

Examples

Vimentin marker test
The laboratory used vimentin staining to help diagnose the type of tumor present.
High levels of vimentin can indicate that a cell is of mesenchymal origin.
Researchers tested the tissue sample for vimentin to confirm its cellular identity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cytokeratin - a protein used as a marker for epithelial cells
