gyrus cinguli

noun
curved brain fold in limbic system
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒaɪrəs sɪŋˈɡjuːlaɪ / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪrəs sɪŋˈɡjuːlɪ / (BrE)
A long curved fold located on the medial surface of each cerebral hemisphere; the cortical component of the limbic system.

Examples

The gyrus cinguli fold
Damage to the gyrus cinguli can affect emotional regulation and decision-making processes.
Researchers observed heightened activity in the gyrus cinguli during tests of memory recall.
The gyrus cinguli is often studied in relation to psychiatric disorders such as depression.
