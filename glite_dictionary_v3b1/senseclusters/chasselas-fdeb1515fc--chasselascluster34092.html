Chasselas

noun
white grape variety
9.5
Pronunciation: / ʃæˈsɛləs / (AmE), / ʃaˈsɛla / (BrE)
a variety of white grape grown for eating and for making wine, especially in Switzerland and France

Examples

Chasselas grapes
The vineyard is famous for its crisp Chasselas wines enjoyed throughout the region.
She bought a bunch of fresh Chasselas grapes at the market for dessert.
Many Swiss winemakers use Chasselas as their primary grape variety.
