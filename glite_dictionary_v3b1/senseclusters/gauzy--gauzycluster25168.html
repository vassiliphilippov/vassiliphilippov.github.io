gauzy

adjective
thin and semi-transparent
6.7 0.99/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…°…îzi / (AmE), / Àà…°…îÀêzi / (BrE)
very thin and light, often semi-transparent, allowing light to pass through

Examples

A gauzy curtain
A gauzy curtain drifted in the breeze, letting sunlight softly filter through.
She wore a gauzy scarf that shimmered in the afternoon light.
The painter captured the gauzy glow of early morning mist over the fields.

Synonyms

  • gauze-like
    7.1 0.00/m
    gauze-like describes fabric that looks and feels like gauze, while gauzy is the common adjective for that thin, semi-transparent quality.
  • sheer
    4.0 1.41/m
    sheer refers to very thin, often transparent fabric seen on curtains or clothes, while gauzy implies a softer, gauze-like thinness.
  • diaphanous
    9.6 0.48/m
    diaphanous sounds formal and means very light and delicate fabric in poetry or shops, while gauzy is common everyday usage.
  • gossamer
    8.6 0.02/m
    gossamer describes an extremely light, almost magical fabric or cobweb, while gauzy means thin, soft, and visibly see-through.
  • filmy
    5.8 0.51/m
    filmy means covered by a thin, often moist layer like a film, while gauzy refers to thin, airy cloth that lets light through.
  • semi-sheer
    5.6 0.01/m
    semi-sheer means partly see-through fabric used in clothes, while gauzy emphasizes a loose, gauze-like thinness and soft texture.
  • semitransparent
    4.3 0.14/m
    semitransparent lets some light pass but hides details, while gauzy suggests thin fabric texture that also allows light through.
  • semitranslucent
    6.6 0.01/m
    semitranslucent lets light through but blurs shapes, while gauzy usually describes thin fabric that gives a soft, see-through look.
  • translucent
    4.8 4.08/m
    translucent means a solid material that spreads light and hides detail, while gauzy refers to thin, cloth-like material letting light through.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diaphanous - light, delicate, and translucent
  • antonym: opaque - not allowing light to pass through
