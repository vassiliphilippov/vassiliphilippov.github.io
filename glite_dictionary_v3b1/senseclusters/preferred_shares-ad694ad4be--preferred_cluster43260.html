Shares in a company that give the holder priority in the payment of dividends but usually carry no voting rights.

Examples

Fixed dividend preferred shares
Although preferred shares have a fixed dividend, they typically do not grant voting rights at shareholder meetings.
Investors in preferred shares receive dividends before those holding common stock.
Many companies issue preferred shares to attract more conservative investors seeking stable returns.
