precedent

noun
an earlier legal decision serving as authority
4.6 5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛsɪdənt / (AmE), / ˈprɛsɪdənt / (BrE)
an earlier event, action, or legal decision that is used as an example or guide for future similar situations

Examples

A legal precedent
Lawyers searched for a precedent that would support their client's case.
Setting a new precedent can shape future interpretations of the law.
The court relied on the precedent set by an earlier ruling when making its decision.
Lawyers often cite precedent to support their arguments in court.
The court's decision set a significant precedent for future legal cases.
There is no precedent for such a rapid technological change in history.
