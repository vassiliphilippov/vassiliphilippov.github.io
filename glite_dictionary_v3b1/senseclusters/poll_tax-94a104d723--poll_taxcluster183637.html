poll tax

noun
fixed tax per person, often for voting
4.0 6.2
Pronunciation: /poʊl tæks/ (AmE), /pəʊl tæks/ /; / /pɒl tæks/ (BrE)
A fixed tax levied on each person, often as a requirement for the right to vote.

Examples

A poll tax payment
Before the law changed, citizens had to pay a poll tax before they could cast their ballots.
Many activists campaigned to abolish the poll tax as it unfairly restricted access to voting.
The introduction of a poll tax in some states was used to disenfranchise certain groups of voters.

Related Terms

  • synonym: capitation - A tax levied as a fixed sum on every person
  • antonym: income tax - A tax based on individual earnings or income
