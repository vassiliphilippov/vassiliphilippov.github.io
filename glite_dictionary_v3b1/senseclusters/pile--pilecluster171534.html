pile

noun
raised surface of fabric (textiles)
Pronunciation: / paɪl / (AmE), / paɪl / (BrE)
the soft, raised surface of a fabric or material, such as velvet, carpet, or animal fur

Examples

Carpet pile
Always brush velvet in the direction of the pile to keep it looking new.
Corduroy trousers are recognized by their distinctively ribbed pile.
The pile of the carpet felt soft under her feet.
Certain types of carpets have a longer pile, making them plusher underfoot.
She brushed the dog's pile to keep it looking smooth and healthy.
The velvet sofa's pile felt incredibly soft to the touch.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nap - the raised, fuzzy surface of a fabric, similar in texture to pile
