Salsola

proper noun
a genus of saltwort plants
9.9
Pronunciation: / sælˈsoʊlə / (AmE), / salˈsəʊlə / (BrE)
A genus of mostly Old World herbs or shrubs, commonly known as saltworts, some species of which are adapted to saline soils.

Examples

Salsola genus
Biologists identified the shrub as part of the Salsola genus, known for its resilience in arid regions.
Several species within the Salsola genus are used in traditional medicine for their purported benefits.
The salt marsh was dominated by Salsola plants thriving in the high salinity.
