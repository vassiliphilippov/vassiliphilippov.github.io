work to rule

verb
protest by rigidly following rules at work
Pronunciation: / wɝːk tə ruːl / (AmE), / wɜːk tə ruːl / (BrE)
to follow all official rules and regulations very strictly as a form of protest, especially by employees in order to reduce efficiency without striking

Examples

To work to rule strictly
Faced with unfair conditions, staff began to work to rule rather than strike.
The union advised its members to work to rule as a protest against the management’s new policy.
When negotiations failed, the workers decided to work to rule to pressure the company.

Related Terms

  • synonym: strike - a refusal to work as a form of protest
