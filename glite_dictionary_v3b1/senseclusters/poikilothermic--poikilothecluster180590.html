poikilothermic

adjective
having body temperature that varies with environment

(of animals) having a body temperature that varies with the temperature of the environment; not maintaining a constant internal temperature.

Examples

Poikilothermic animals
Because amphibians are poikilothermic, their activity level changes with the surrounding temperature.
Reptiles are poikilothermic and rely on external heat sources to regulate their body temperature.
Unlike mammals, fish are poikilothermic and cannot maintain a steady internal heat.

Synonyms

  • ectothermic
    9.1 0.03/m
    ectothermic refers to animals that use external heat to control body temperature, while poikilothermic emphasizes temperature varying with surroundings.
  • heterothermic
    9.9 0.00/m
    heterothermic describes animals whose body temperature can change or be partly controlled, while poikilothermic means it simply varies with the environment.
  • cold-blooded
    3.7 0.22/m
    cold-blooded is a common informal label suggesting low or changing body heat, while poikilothermic is a technical term about temperature varying.

Related Terms
