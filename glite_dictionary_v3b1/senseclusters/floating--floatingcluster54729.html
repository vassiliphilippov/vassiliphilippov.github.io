floating

adjective
not firmly attached (body part)
Pronunciation: / ˈfloʊ.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfləʊ.tɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a part of the body) not firmly attached; movable or out of its normal position.

Examples

Floating rib
In humans, the last two pairs of ribs are known as floating ribs because they are not attached to the sternum.
Some people have a condition called a floating kidney, where the organ is unusually mobile.
The doctor explained that a floating bone fragment may cause discomfort until it is removed.
