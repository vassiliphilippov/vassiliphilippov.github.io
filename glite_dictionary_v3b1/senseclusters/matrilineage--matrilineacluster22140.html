matrilineage

noun
descent traced through the maternal line
Pronunciation: / ˌmætrɪˈlɪniɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌmætrɪˈlɪniɪdʒ / (BrE)
a line of descent traced through the mother's side of a family

Examples

The matrilineage line
Anthropologists studied the clan's matrilineage to understand their social structure.
In some cultures, inheritance follows the matrilineage rather than the father's line.
Their matrilineage can be traced back several generations to a renowned ancestor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: matrilineal - relating to descent traced through the mother
  • antonym: patrilineage - line of descent traced through the paternal side
