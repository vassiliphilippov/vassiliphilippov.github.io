tangle

verb
make twisted or disordered
Pronunciation: / ˈtæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈtæŋɡl̩ /; / ˈtæŋɡ(ə)l / (BrE)
to twist or mix things together so that they are difficult to separate or straighten out

Examples

To tangle hair
If you don't brush your hair, the wind will tangle it badly.
She tried not to tangle her necklace while packing for the trip.
The kitten managed to tangle the ball of yarn in just a few minutes.
Don't let the headphones tangle in your bag, or you'll spend minutes trying to undo them.
Her long hair would often tangle after swimming in the pool.
If you tangle the ropes, it will take ages to separate them again.

Related Terms

  • synonym: entangle - to twist together or ensnare
  • antonym: untangle - to straighten out or resolve something that is tangled
