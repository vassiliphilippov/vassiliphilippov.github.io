burn up

verb
use up energy or fuel through activity
3.4 0.7/m
Pronunciation: / b…ùn  åp / (AmE), / b…úÀên  åp / (BrE)
to use energy, especially as a result of physical activity; to expend (calories, fuel, etc.) through activity or use

Examples

To burn up calories
After climbing the hill, I could feel my body burning up energy quickly.
Intense cardio workouts can help you burn up more calories.
You burn up a lot of fuel walking several miles each day.

Synonyms

  • burn off
    3.4 0.5/m
    burn off uses physical activity to remove calories and fat, while burn up means to use or expend energy generally.
  • burn
    3.2 9/m
    burn often means using or wasting energy, money, or fuel quickly, while burn up focuses on expending energy through activity.
  • work off
    3.5 0.8/m
    work off means getting rid of weight or stress by exercise, while burn up is about using energy or calories.
  • use up
    2.7 3/m
    use up means to consume all of a supply until none remains, while burn up focuses on expending energy through activity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conserve - to use as little as possible; to avoid waste
  • synonym: expend - to use up (a resource, such as energy, time, or money)
