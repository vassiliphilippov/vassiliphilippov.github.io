chlamydia

noun
sexually transmitted bacterial infection
Pronunciation: / kləˈmɪdiə / (AmE), / kləˈmɪdiə / (BrE)
A common sexually transmitted infection caused by bacteria of the genus Chlamydia, often producing no symptoms but potentially leading to serious health complications if untreated.

Examples

Chlamydia infection
If left untreated, chlamydia can cause serious health problems, especially for women.
It is important to get tested regularly for sexually transmitted infections such as chlamydia.
Many young adults are unaware they have chlamydia because it often has no symptoms.

  • synonym: syphilis - a sexually transmitted infection caused by the bacterium Treponema pallidum
