post-conquest

noun
period after conquest
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊstˈkɑːŋkwɛst / (AmE), / ˌpəʊstˈkɒŋkwɛst / (BrE)
the period of time following a conquest, especially after a military takeover of a region or country

Examples

The post-conquest period
During the post-conquest, many local traditions were replaced by the customs of the new rulers.
Historians study the post-conquest to understand how societies adapt after being conquered.
The post-conquest era brought significant changes to the region's language and culture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aftermath - the period following an important event, especially one with significant consequences
  • antonym: pre-conquest - the period before a conquest or military takeover
