bokeh

noun
blurred photo background
Pronunciation: / ˈboʊkeɪ / (AmE), / ˈbəʊkeɪ / (BrE)
the soft, blurred visual effect of the out-of-focus areas in a photograph, often used to highlight the subject

Examples

Soft bokeh effect
The photographer used a wide aperture to create beautiful bokeh behind the model.
Many portrait photographers love the soft bokeh produced by certain lenses.
The city lights turned into colorful circles of bokeh in the background of the image.
The portrait's beautiful bokeh made the background lights look like soft glowing circles.
Many photographers choose lenses with smooth bokeh for artistic effect in their images.
The bokeh in this picture draws attention to the subject by gently blurring the background.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blur - an area in a photo that is not in sharp focus
  • antonym: sharpness - the clarity and detail in a photograph
