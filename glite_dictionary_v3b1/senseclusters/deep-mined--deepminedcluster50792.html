deep-mined

adjective
extracted from underground mines
Pronunciation: / diːp maɪnd / (AmE), / diːp maɪnd / (BrE)
(of coal) extracted from underground mines, as opposed to obtained from strip mining.

Examples

Deep-mined coal
Much of Britain's deep-mined coal industry closed during the 1980s and 1990s.
New regulations require more safety checks in deep-mined operations than in surface mines.
The company produces both deep-mined and strip-mined coal for power plants.

Related Terms

  • antonym: strip-mined - (of coal, etc.) obtained by surface-mining (strip mining)
