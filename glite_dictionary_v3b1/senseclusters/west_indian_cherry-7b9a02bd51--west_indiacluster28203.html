West Indian cherry

noun
tropical American shrub with cherry-like fruit
3.2
Pronunciation: / ˌwɛst ˈɪndiən ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / ˌwest ˈɪndiən ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
a tropical American shrub or small tree (Malpighia emarginata) bearing small, edible, acid red fruits that resemble cherries

Examples

West Indian cherry tree
Farmers in Brazil cultivate the West Indian cherry for its nutritional benefits and tart flavor.
She added fresh West Indian cherries to the salad for a burst of color and taste.
The juice of the West Indian cherry is high in vitamin C and is often used in health drinks.
