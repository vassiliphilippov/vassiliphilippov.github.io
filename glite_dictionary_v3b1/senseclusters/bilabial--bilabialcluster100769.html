bilabial

adjective
speech sound made with both lips
8.8
Pronunciation: / baɪˈleɪbiəl / (AmE), / baɪˈleɪbɪəl / (BrE)
(of a speech sound) produced by using both lips together.

Examples

Bilabial sound
In the word 'map', both 'm' and 'p' are bilabial consonants.
Phonetics students often practice bilabial sounds by closing their lips together.
The English sound bilabial /p/ appears in words like 'pat' and 'spin'.

Related Terms

  • synonym: labial - a speech sound produced with the lips
