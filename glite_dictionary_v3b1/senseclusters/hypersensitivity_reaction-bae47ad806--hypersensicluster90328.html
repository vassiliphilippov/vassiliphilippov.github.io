hypersensitivity reaction

noun
excessive immune response to an allergen
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərsɛnˈsɪtɪvəti riˈækʃən / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəsɛnsɪˈtɪvɪti rɪˈakʃən / (BrE)
an excessive or inappropriate immune response to an allergen, which can range in severity from mild allergy to life-threatening anaphylactic shock

Examples

A hypersensitivity reaction to shellfish
After eating shellfish, he suffered a hypersensitivity reaction that required immediate medical treatment.
Some medications can trigger a hypersensitivity reaction in sensitive patients, ranging from mild hives to severe swelling.
The doctor explained that a hypersensitivity reaction to pollen caused her persistent sneezing and watery eyes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allergic reaction - an immune response to a normally harmless substance
  • degree higher: anaphylactic shock - a severe, life-threatening allergic reaction
