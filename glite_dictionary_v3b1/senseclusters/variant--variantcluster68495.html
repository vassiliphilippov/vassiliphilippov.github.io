variant

noun
a different form or version
2.1 4.0
Pronunciation: /ˈve.rɪ.ənt/ /; / /ˈvær.ɪ.ənt/ (AmE), /ˈveə.rɪ.ənt/ (BrE)
a form, version, or instance of something that is different from others of the same kind (including in biology)

Examples

A variant of the virus
Scientists discovered a previously unknown variant of the gene.
The new COVID-19 variant spread rapidly across several countries.
This recipe is a spicy variant of the traditional dish.
Scientists were puzzled by a variant that did not fit any known pattern.
The sudden drop in temperature was a variant from the usual spring weather.
This year's flu includes a new variant that is more contagious than previous strains.
Researchers are studying how this variant differs genetically from other known strains.
Several variants of the plant species have adapted to different climates.
The laboratory detected a new variant of the virus spreading rapidly in several countries.
