absolute space

noun
space existing independently of objects
Pronunciation: / ˈæbsəˌluːt speɪs / (AmE), / ˈæbsəluːt speɪs / (BrE)
physical space considered as existing independently of any objects or events within it, often discussed in classical physics and philosophy

Examples

Absolute space concept
Debates about absolute space have played a crucial role in the history of physics.
Some scientists once believed that absolute space provided a fixed stage for all physical events.
The theory of relativity challenged the notion of absolute space by emphasizing the role of the observer.

Related Terms

  • antonym: relative space - space defined by its relationship to objects and motion, not existing independently
