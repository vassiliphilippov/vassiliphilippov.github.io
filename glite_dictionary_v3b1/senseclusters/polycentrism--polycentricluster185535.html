polycentrism

noun
multiple language standards
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑliˈsɛntrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˈsɛntrɪzəm / (BrE)
the condition in which a single language has two or more independent standard varieties used in different countries or regions

Examples

Linguistic polycentrism
German is often cited as an example of polycentrism because it has several standard forms.
Linguists study polycentrism to understand how English differs between the UK, the US, and other countries.
The concept of polycentrism helps explain why spelling and grammar rules can vary across regions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monocentrism - the condition where a language has only one standard variety
