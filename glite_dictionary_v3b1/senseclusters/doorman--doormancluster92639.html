doorman

noun
man guarding or attending a building entrance
1.2 2.93/m
Pronunciation: / Ààd…îrÀåm√¶n / (AmE), / Ààd…îÀêm√¶n / (BrE)
A man whose job is to guard, oversee, or attend the entrance to a building, such as a hotel, apartment, or club.

Examples

A doorman at hotel
A uniformed doorman stood outside the exclusive nightclub, checking invitations.
The doorman greeted guests with a smile as they entered the hotel.
The building hired a new doorman to improve security at the main entrance.

Synonyms

  • doorkeeper
    2.8 0.09/m
    doorkeeper is a neutral title for someone who controls entry and checks people, while doorman names the male entrance attendant.
  • doorwoman
    4.2 0.01/m
    doorwoman is a female entrance attendant who greets guests at hotels or clubs, while doorman refers to a male in the same job.
  • door guard
    2.5 0.02/m
    door guard is a plain phrase for someone who watches an entrance, while doorman commonly names a hotel or club attendant.
  • porter
    4.3 0.69/m
    porter often helps carry luggage and serve guests in hotels, while doorman focuses on guarding and attending a building entrance.
