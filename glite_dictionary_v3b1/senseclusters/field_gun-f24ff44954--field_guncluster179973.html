field gun

noun
movable artillery for direct troop support
Pronunciation: / ˈfiːld ˌɡʌn / (AmE), / ˈfiːld ˌɡʌn / (BrE)
a type of movable artillery piece, excluding antiaircraft guns, used by armies in the field to support front-line troops with direct fire

Examples

A field gun firing
During the battle, a single field gun helped repel several attacks with its accurate fire.
Modern armies have replaced many traditional field guns with self-propelled artillery systems.
The soldiers positioned the field gun behind the ridge to provide cover for the advancing infantry.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: howitzer - a type of artillery piece with a shorter barrel than a field gun, used for firing shells on higher trajectories
