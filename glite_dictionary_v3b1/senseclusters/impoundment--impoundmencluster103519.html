impoundment

noun
legal seizure of property by authorities
5.2
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpaʊndmənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈpaʊndmənt / (BrE)
the act of placing private property, especially vehicles, animals, or funds, in the custody of a law enforcement or government officer, typically for legal reasons

Examples

Vehicle impoundment
After failing to pay property taxes, the homeowner faced the impoundment of his land by the authorities.
The court ruled in favor of the city's impoundment of illegally parked vehicles.
The police ordered the impoundment of several cars involved in the recent incident.

Related Terms

  • antonym: release - the act of setting property free from custody
  • synonym: seizure - the action of confiscating property by legal authority
