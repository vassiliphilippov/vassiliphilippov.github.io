generalized

adjective
not specialized; broadly adapted
6.5 5.3
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒen.ər.ə.laɪzd/ (AmE), /ˈdʒen.ər.ə.laɪzd/ (BrE)
(of an organism or part) not specialized for a particular function or environment; able to adapt to a wide range of conditions

Examples

A generalized limb
A generalized limb structure means the animal can perform a wide range of movements.
Primitive mammals, like the hedgehog, are often described as generalized rather than specialized.
The generalized structure of the animal allows it to adapt to various habitats.
Early amphibians were more generalized than modern species, allowing them to adapt to varied environments.
In evolutionary terms, a generalized limb can perform multiple functions but is less efficient at any single task.
The platypus is considered a generalized mammal due to its mixture of primitive traits.
