underbill

verb
to charge too little
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈbɪl / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈbɪl / (BrE)
to charge someone less than the correct or expected amount for goods or services

Examples

To underbill a client
The accountant discovered that they had underbilled several clients last quarter.
If you underbill for your services, your business may lose money over time.
The hospital realized it had underbilled insurance companies for certain procedures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overbill - to charge someone more than the correct or expected amount
  • synonym: undercharge - to ask for less money than is correct or usual
