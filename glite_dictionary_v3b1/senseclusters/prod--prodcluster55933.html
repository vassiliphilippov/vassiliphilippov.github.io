prod

noun
encouraging prompt or stimulus
4.4 0.13/m
Pronunciation: / prÉ‘d / (AmE), / prÉ’d / (BrE)
an act of encouragement or prompting to do something, especially when it is needed to overcome reluctance

Examples

A gentle prod
Her teacher's prod gave her the confidence to submit her story for publication.
Sometimes all you need is a gentle prod to get started on a difficult task.
With a firm prod from his friends, he finally called the company to apply for the job.

Synonyms

  • prompting
    3.8 0.03/m
    prompting gives a gentle suggestion or cue to act, while prod means a small push to overcome reluctance.
  • spur
    4.1 0.94/m
    spur is a quick remark or event that motivates action, while prod is a gentle push to start someone moving.
  • goad
    7.4 0.10/m
    goad is a sharper prompt that may annoy or provoke someone, while prod is a milder, simple encouragement.
  • urging
    3.9 2.01/m
    urging is strong pressure or repeated requests to make someone act, while prod is a light nudge to overcome reluctance.

Related Terms
