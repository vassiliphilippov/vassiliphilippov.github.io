antirealism

noun
rejection of realism
Pronunciation: / ˌæntiˈriːəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌæntiˈrɪəˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
an approach in art or literature that rejects realistic representation in favor of imagination, abstraction, or altered perception

Examples

Antirealism in art
The artist's antirealism challenged viewers to see the world in new and imaginative ways.
Literary critics praised the novel's antirealism for its creative use of symbolism and fantasy.
Many modern art movements embrace antirealism as a way to break free from traditional forms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abstraction - art that does not attempt to represent external reality accurately
  • antonym: realism - the practice of representing subjects truthfully and lifelike
