corn chamomile

noun
white-flowered weed of the daisy family
6.9
Pronunciation: / kɔrn ˈkæməˌmaɪl / (AmE), / kɔːn ˈkaməmʌɪl / (BrE)
A European white-flowered weed of the daisy family that is commonly found in cornfields and has become naturalized in North America.

Examples

corn chamomile weed
Gardeners often remove corn chamomile because it spreads rapidly and competes with crops.
The field was dotted with patches of corn chamomile among the ripening wheat.
The white petals and yellow centers of corn chamomile are easily mistaken for other daisies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mayweed - a similar weed of the daisy family
