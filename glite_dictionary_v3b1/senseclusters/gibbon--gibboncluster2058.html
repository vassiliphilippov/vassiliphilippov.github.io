gibbon

noun
small tailless ape of Asia
4.5 5.2
Pronunciation: /ˈgɪ.bən/ (AmE), /ˈgɪ.bən/ (BrE)
a small, tailless ape with long arms, found in the forests of southern Asia and East Indies, known for its agile movement in trees

Examples

A gibbon ape
Researchers observed a family of gibbons communicating with loud calls in the rainforest.
The gibbon swung effortlessly from branch to branch using its long arms.
Unlike most primates, the gibbon does not have a tail.

Related Terms
