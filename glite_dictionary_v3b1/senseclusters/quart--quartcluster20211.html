quart

noun
unit of liquid capacity (US, 32 fl oz)
3.9 1.4
Pronunciation: /kwɔːrt/ /; / /kwɑːrt/ (AmE), /kwɔːt/ /; / /kwɑːt/ (BrE)
a unit of capacity equal to a quarter of a gallon, or two pints (used for both liquid and dry substances, in US and UK systems)

Examples

A quart of milk
A quart is equal to two pints in the United States measurement system.
She bought a quart of ice cream to share with her friends.
The recipe calls for a quart of milk, so make sure you have enough.
A US dry quart is slightly larger than a liquid quart.

In the UK, a quart is slightly larger than in the US.
