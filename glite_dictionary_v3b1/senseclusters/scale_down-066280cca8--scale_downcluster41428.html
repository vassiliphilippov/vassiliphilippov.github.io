scale down

verb
reduce in proportion to original size
Pronunciation: / skeɪl daʊn / (AmE), / skeɪl daʊn / (BrE)
to reduce the size, amount, or extent of something; to make something smaller in proportion to its original size

Examples

To scale down operations
Due to budget cuts, the company had to scale down its operations.
The architect scaled down the model to fit it on the table.
They decided to scale down the event and invite fewer people.
The company decided to scale down its operations in response to declining demand.
The image can be scaled down before uploading to save storage space.
We had to scale down our plans for the house renovation due to budget constraints.

Related Terms

  • antonym: enlarge - to make something bigger in size or extent
  • synonym: reduce - make smaller in amount, size, or number
