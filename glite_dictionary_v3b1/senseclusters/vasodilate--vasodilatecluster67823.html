vasodilate

verb
to widen blood vessels
7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈveɪ.zə.daɪ.leɪt/ (AmE), /ˈveɪ.zə.daɪ.leɪt/ (BrE)
to become or make blood vessels wider, increasing blood flow

Examples

To vasodilate blood vessels
When the body overheats, blood vessels vasodilate to help release excess heat.
Certain medications can cause arteries to vasodilate, lowering blood pressure.
During exercise, your muscles need more oxygen, so the blood vessels vasodilate to increase blood flow.
Doctors may prescribe drugs that vasodilate to help reduce high blood pressure.
During exercise, your blood vessels naturally vasodilate to deliver more oxygen to your muscles.
Some foods can help vasodilate blood vessels, improving overall circulation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: constrict - to make blood vessels narrower
  • synonym: dilate - to make or become wider or larger
  • antonym: vasoconstrict - to become narrower, especially of blood vessels
