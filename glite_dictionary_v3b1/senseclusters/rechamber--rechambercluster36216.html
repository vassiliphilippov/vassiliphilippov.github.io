rechamber

verb
to reload firearm
9.2
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈtʃeɪm.bər/ /; / /riː.ˈtʃeɪm.bər/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈtʃeɪm.bə/ /; / /riː.ˈtʃeɪm.bə/ (BrE)
to place a cartridge into the chamber of a firearm again, especially after it has been removed or ejected

Examples

To rechamber a round
After fixing the jam, he quickly rechambered a round and prepared to fire again.
The soldier had to rechamber his rifle before continuing the exercise.
If the cartridge is ejected, you must rechamber it to make the weapon ready.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unload - to remove ammunition from a firearm
