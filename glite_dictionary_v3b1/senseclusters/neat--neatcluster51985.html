neat

adjective
served plain (of a drink)
6.3 0.35/m
Pronunciation: / nit / (AmE), / niÀêt / (BrE)
(of an alcoholic drink) served plain, without water, ice, or any other mixers.

Examples

Whiskey neat
At the bar, she ordered a vodka neat instead of a cocktail.
He always drinks his bourbon neat, with no ice or water.
Some people prefer their scotch neat to savor the full flavor.

Synonyms

  • straight
    4.7 0.55/m
    straight is common in bar speech to mean a drink served without ice or mixers, while neat is the usual bartender term.
  • full-strength
    4.5 0.04/m
    full-strength means the drink keeps its full alcohol level and is undiluted, while neat focuses on serving it without ice or mixers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: straight - (of an alcoholic drink) served without a mixer, though sometimes with ice
