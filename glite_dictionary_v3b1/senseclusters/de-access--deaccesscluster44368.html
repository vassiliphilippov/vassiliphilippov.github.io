de-access

verb
formally remove from a collection
Pronunciation: / ˌdiː.əˈsɛs / (AmE), / ˌdiː.əˈsɛs / (BrE)
to remove an item from a library, museum, or similar collection by formally selling or disposing of it

Examples

To de-access museum items
After careful review, the librarian chose to de-access outdated reference materials from the archives.
The art gallery decided to de-access several lesser-known paintings to make room for new acquisitions.
They needed additional funding, so the museum board voted to de-access select pieces from the collection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deaccession - to officially remove an item from a museum or collection, especially by sale or transfer
