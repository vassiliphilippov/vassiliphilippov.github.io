geneticism

noun
belief in genetic determination of traits
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈnɛtɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / dʒɪˈnɛtɪsɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The belief or theory that all human characteristics are determined by genetic factors.

Examples

The theory of geneticism
Her research challenges the concept of geneticism by highlighting cultural influences on behavior.
Some critics argue that geneticism downplays the role of environment in shaping personality.
The theory of geneticism sparked intense debate among social scientists and biologists.
