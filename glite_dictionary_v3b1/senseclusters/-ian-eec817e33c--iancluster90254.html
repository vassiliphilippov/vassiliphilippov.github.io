a suffix used to form nouns or adjectives meaning a person or thing related to, belonging to, or associated with a place, group, profession, or idea

Examples

The suffix -ian
The word 'Brazilian' uses the suffix -ian to refer to someone from Brazil.
Adding -ian to 'Shakespeare' creates 'Shakespearean', meaning related to Shakespeare.
Many country names form their demonyms by adding -ian at the end.
A person who studies history is called a historian.
The word 'musician' refers to someone skilled in music.
A politician is someone involved in politics, often as a career.
A person who studies Shakespeare is often called a Shakespearean.
The term Newtonian refers to someone who follows the ideas of Isaac Newton.
Many Freudians believe in the theories developed by Sigmund Freud.
