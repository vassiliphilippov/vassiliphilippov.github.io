compulsoriness

noun
state of being compulsive
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpʌlsərinəs / (AmE), / kəmˈpʌlsərinəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being driven by irresistible impulses or uncontrollable urges

Examples

Compulsoriness of habits
The compulsoriness of his actions made it difficult for him to resist temptation.
Many people underestimate the compulsoriness that can accompany certain habits or addictions.
She spoke about the compulsoriness of her need to check her phone constantly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compulsiveness - the tendency to act on irresistible urges
  • antonym: voluntariness - the quality of acting by free will or choice
