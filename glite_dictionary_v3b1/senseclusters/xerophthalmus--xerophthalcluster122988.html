xerophthalmus

noun
medical: abnormal eye dryness
5.4 9.9
Pronunciation: /zɪr.əf.ˈθæl.məs/ /; / /zɪə.rəf.ˈθæl.məs/ (AmE), /zɪə.rəf.ˈθæl.məs/ /; / /zɪ.rəf.ˈθæl.məs/ (BrE)
Abnormal dryness of the conjunctiva and cornea of the eye, often caused by a systemic deficiency of vitamin A.

Examples

Severe xerophthalmus symptoms
Patients suffering from severe xerophthalmus may experience blurred vision and eye discomfort.
The doctor diagnosed him with xerophthalmus after examining his extremely dry eyes.
Vitamin A deficiency can lead to xerophthalmus in children living in developing countries.
