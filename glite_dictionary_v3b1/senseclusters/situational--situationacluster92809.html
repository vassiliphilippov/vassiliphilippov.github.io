situational

adjective
due to circumstances
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪtʃuˈeɪʃənəl / (AmE), / ˌsɪtjʊˈeɪʃənəl / (BrE)
depending on or caused by a particular situation or set of circumstances, rather than by someone's character

Examples

Situational anxiety
Her anxiety was mostly situational, triggered by stressful events at work.
The manager recognized that the mistake was situational and not a reflection of the employee's abilities.
Some people show situational leadership, adapting their style to fit the needs of the moment.
Her leadership style is highly situational, adapting to the needs of her team.
The effectiveness of the policy is largely situational and may not work everywhere.
He made a situational decision based on the information available at the time.
The manager used a situational approach to adapt his leadership style to each team member.
Her response was not typical; it was a situational reaction to unexpected stress.
In psychology, situational factors can greatly influence a person's behavior.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contextual - depending on the context or situation
  • antonym: dispositional - caused by a person's character or inherent qualities
  • antonym: universal - applying everywhere or in all situations
