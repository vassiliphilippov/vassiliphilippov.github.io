discount

noun
reduction from usual price
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪsˌkaʊnt / (AmE), / ˈdɪsˌkaʊnt / (BrE)
an amount or percentage by which the usual price of something is reduced

Examples

A 20% discount
All store items are available at a 20% discount this week.
He bought the shoes at a discount during the holiday sales.
She was offered a special discount for being a regular customer.
If you apply before the deadline, you will receive a special discount.
Susan received a discount on her next purchase as a reward for her loyalty.
The store offered a 10% discount on all electronics this weekend.
If you buy in bulk, you can get a substantial discount.
The store attracted customers by announcing a massive discount weekend.
They offered a 20% discount on all kitchen appliances during the holiday sale.

Related Terms

  • antonym: markup - an increase added to the cost price to determine the selling price
  • synonym: rebate - a partial refund to someone who has paid too much money for tax, rent, or a utility
  • antonym: surcharge - an additional charge or payment
