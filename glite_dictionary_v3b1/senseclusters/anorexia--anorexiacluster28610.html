anorexia

noun
prolonged loss of appetite disorder
4.0 6.3
Pronunciation: /æ.nə.ˈrek.sɪ.ə/ /; / /ə.nə.ˈrek.sɪ.ə/ (AmE), /æ.nə.ˈrek.sɪ.ə/ /; / /ə.nə.ˈrek.sɪ.ə/ (BrE)
A prolonged disorder marked by loss of appetite and inability to eat, often resulting in significant weight loss and health complications.

Examples

Severe anorexia symptoms
After months of battling anorexia, she finally began eating regular meals again.
Doctors are concerned about the rise of anorexia among teenagers in recent years.
Loss of appetite can signal anorexia or other underlying health conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anorexia nervosa - an eating disorder characterized by refusal to eat and an extreme fear of gaining weight
  • antonym: bulimia - an eating disorder involving episodes of binge eating followed by purging
