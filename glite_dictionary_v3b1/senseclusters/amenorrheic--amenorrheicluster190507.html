amenorrheic

adjective
lacking menstrual periods (non-pregnancy cause)
7.3 9.5
Pronunciation: /ə.mə.nə.ˈriː.ɪk/ /; / /æ.mə.nə.ˈriː.ɪk/ (AmE), /ə.mə.nə.ˈriː.ɪk/ /; / /æ.mə.nə.ˈriː.ɪk/ (BrE)
relating to or affected by the absence of normal menstrual periods not due to pregnancy.

Examples

Amenorrheic athletes
Athletes with extremely low body fat may become amenorrheic due to hormonal imbalances.
Some medications can cause women to become temporarily amenorrheic.
The patient was diagnosed as amenorrheic after missing her menstrual cycle for several months.
Certain eating disorders can result in women becoming amenorrhoeic.
The amenorrhoeic state may be temporary or persistent depending on the underlying cause.
The doctor noted that the patient was amenorrhoeic for over six months.
