aggrievement

noun
legal harm or injury
9.9
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈgriːv.mənt/ (AmE), /ə.ˈgriːv.mənt/ (BrE)
the state of having suffered harm or injury that gives someone the legal right to bring a claim in court

Examples

Legal aggrievement
The court determined that the plaintiff lacked aggrievement and could not proceed with the case.
Without clear aggrievement, the lawsuit was dismissed at an early stage.
Demonstrating aggrievement is essential for anyone wishing to challenge a government decision in court.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grievance - a real or imagined cause for complaint or protest
  • synonym: standing - the legal right to bring a lawsuit
