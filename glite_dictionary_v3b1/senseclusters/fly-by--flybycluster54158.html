fly-by

noun
low-altitude flight over spectators
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈflaɪˌbaɪ / (AmE), / ˈflaɪˌbaɪ / (BrE)
a flight at low altitude, typically by a military aircraft, over spectators on the ground

Examples

A fly-by show
After the fly-by, the pilots waved to the audience from the cockpit.
The air show concluded with a dramatic fly-by from the Air Force.
The crowd cheered as the jets performed a spectacular fly-by during the parade.
