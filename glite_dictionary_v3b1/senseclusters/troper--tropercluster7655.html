troper

noun
medieval liturgical book
Pronunciation: / ˈtroʊpər / (AmE), / ˈtrəʊpə / (BrE)
a medieval book containing tropes, which are musical or textual additions to liturgical chants

Examples

A medieval troper manuscript
The monastery preserved a rare troper dating back to the twelfth century.
Scholars examined the troper to understand the evolution of medieval church music.
A beautifully illuminated troper was displayed in the museum's medieval collection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antiphonary - a book containing antiphons for liturgical use
  • synonym: gradual - a book containing chants for the Mass
