outsail

verb
sail faster or better than another
Pronunciation: / aʊtˈseɪl / (AmE), / aʊtˈseɪl / (BrE)
to sail faster or more skillfully than another person, boat, or group

Examples

To outsail other boats
The British ship outsailed all others during the regatta.
Their new yacht can easily outsail the competition in light winds.
We hoped to outsail our rivals by choosing a different route.

Related Terms

  • synonym: outpace - to go faster than; to surpass in speed
