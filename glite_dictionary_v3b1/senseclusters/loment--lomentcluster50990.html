loment

noun
seedpod type that splits into single-seeded parts
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊmənt / (AmE), / ˈləʊmɛnt / (BrE)
A type of seedpod that is constricted between the seeds and breaks apart at maturity into single-seeded segments.

Examples

A loment seedpod
Plants in the pea family sometimes develop a loment rather than a typical pod.
The botanist carefully examined the loment to study how it splits apart when mature.
The wild indigo plant produces a loment as its fruit, which breaks into small segments containing single seeds.
