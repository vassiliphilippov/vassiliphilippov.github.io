kickstart

verb
to start energetically
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪkˌstɑrt / (AmE), / ˈkɪkˌstɑːt / (BrE)
to start or launch something quickly or with energy, often to stimulate progress or activity

Examples

To kickstart a project
The government hopes the new policy will kickstart the economy after months of stagnation.
A strong cup of coffee helped her kickstart her busy morning.
They used a creative video to kickstart their fundraising campaign.

Related Terms

  • antonym: halt - to stop or bring to an end
  • synonym: initiate - to begin or start something
  • synonym: launch - to start or introduce something new
