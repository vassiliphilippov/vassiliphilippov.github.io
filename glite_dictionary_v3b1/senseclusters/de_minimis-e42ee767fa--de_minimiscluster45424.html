de minimis

adjective, adverb
insignificant amount
Pronunciation: / deɪ ˈmɪnɪmɪs / (AmE), / deɪ ˈmɪnɪmɪs / (BrE)
too small or unimportant to be considered; so minor as to be negligible (especially in legal contexts)

Examples

De minimis amount
The tax authority ignored the de minimis error in the paperwork.
Any differences in the results were considered de minimis by the researchers.
The company argued that the violation was de minimis and did not warrant a penalty.
The tax increase was considered de minimis and did not affect most residents.
Any errors in the report are de minimis and do not change the overall findings.
The court ruled that the violation was de minimis and required no penalty.

  • synonym: negligibly - to such a small degree as to be unimportant

  • antonym: significantly - to a large or important degree
