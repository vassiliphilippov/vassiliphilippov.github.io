nazir

noun
ancient religious devotee
9.9
Pronunciation: / nɑːˈzɪr / (AmE), / ˈneɪzɪə / (BrE)
a person in ancient Israel who took a vow of special religious devotion, abstaining from wine and not cutting their hair

Examples

A nazir with long hair
The nazir followed strict rules as part of his vow, including not drinking wine.
In ancient Israel, a nazir was recognized by his uncut hair and dedication to God.
The story describes how a nazir lived apart from others to fulfill his religious promise.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ascetic - a person who practices severe self-discipline and abstention
  • synonym: priest - a person authorized to perform religious ceremonies
