rough edge

noun
sharp or uneven part; character flaw
4.9 3.8
Pronunciation: /rʌf edʒ/ (AmE), /rʌf edʒ/ (BrE)
A part of an object or surface that is sharp or uneven, which may cause harm or injury; also used to refer to imperfections or lack of refinement in a person's character or behavior.

Examples

Smooth rough edges
After years of experience, her rough edges had been smoothed and she became a great leader.
Be careful not to cut yourself on the rough edge of the metal sheet.
The coach said the young player still had some rough edges to work out in his game.

  • antonym: smooth edge - an even or non-abrasive boundary or surface
