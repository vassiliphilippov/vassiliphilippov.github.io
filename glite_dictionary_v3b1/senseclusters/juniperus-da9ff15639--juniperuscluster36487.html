Juniperus

noun
genus of juniper trees and shrubs
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒuːnɪˈpɛrəs / (AmE), / ˌdʒuːnɪˈpɛːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of evergreen coniferous trees and shrubs commonly known as junipers, characterized by needle-like or scale-like leaves and berry-like cones.

Examples

Juniperus berry flavor
Gin gets much of its distinctive flavor from the berries of Juniperus plants.
Many species within the genus Juniperus are cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and parks.
The botanist identified the shrub in the rocky landscape as Juniperus communis.
