diffusiveness

noun
rambling quality
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfjuːsɪvnəs / (AmE), / dɪˈfjuːsɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being unfocused, rambling, or lacking clarity, especially in speech or writing

Examples

Diffusiveness in writing
The diffusiveness of his explanation made it hard for the audience to follow his main argument.
Editors often reduce diffusiveness in articles to make them more concise and effective.
Her writing style was criticized for its diffusiveness, which distracted from the main message.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear and easy to understand
  • synonym: verbosity - the use of too many words
