Pelagian

noun
follower of Pelagius
Pronunciation: / pəˈleɪdʒiən / (AmE), / pəˈleɪdʒiən / (BrE)
a person who follows the teachings of Pelagius, especially one who denies that original sin corrupts human nature

Examples

A Pelagian thinker
The council condemned the monk as a Pelagian for rejecting the doctrine of original sin.
As a Pelagian, he believed that humans are born without the stain of original sin.
Debates between Pelagians and their opponents shaped early Christian theology.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Augustinian - a follower of the teachings of St. Augustine, especially regarding original sin and grace
