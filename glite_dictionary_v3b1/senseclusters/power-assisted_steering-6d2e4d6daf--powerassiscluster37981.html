power-assisted steering

noun
automotive steering system using engine power
1.8 3.0
Pronunciation: /ˈpaʊ.ər ə.ˈsɪs.tɪd ˈstɪr.ɪŋ/ /; / /paʊr ə.ˈsɪs.tɪd ˈstɪr.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈpaʊ.ə ə.ˈsɪs.tɪd ˈstɪə.rɪŋ/ /; / /ˈpɑː ə.ˈsɪs.tɪd ˈstɪə.rɪŋ/ (BrE)
a system in a motor vehicle where power from an engine or electric motor is used to make steering easier, usually by amplifying the force applied to the steering wheel

Examples

Power-assisted steering system
Modern vehicles typically come equipped with power-assisted steering as a standard feature.
Older cars without power-assisted steering can be difficult to maneuver at low speeds.
The mechanic recommended repairing the power-assisted steering system to improve handling.

Related Terms

  • antonym: manual steering - a steering system operated without power assistance
  • synonym: power steering - a steering system in which power is supplied to assist with turning
