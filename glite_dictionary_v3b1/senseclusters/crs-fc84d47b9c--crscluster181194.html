CRS

proper noun
US legislative research agency
Pronunciation: / si ɑr ɛs / (AmE), / si ɑː ɛs / (BrE)
the Congressional Research Service, a nonpartisan agency that provides research and analysis for the United States Congress

Examples

CRS research report
The senator requested a CRS report to better understand the proposed legislation.
Members of Congress often rely on CRS for objective policy analysis.
The CRS helps lawmakers make informed decisions by providing nonpartisan research.

Related Terms

  • synonym: CBO - the Congressional Budget Office, an agency that provides budget and economic information to Congress
  • synonym: GAO - the Government Accountability Office, an agency that audits and evaluates government activities for Congress
