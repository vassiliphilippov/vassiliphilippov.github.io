grain moth

noun
moth with larvae that feed on grain
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡreɪn mɑθ / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪn mɒθ / (BrE)
a type of moth whose larvae feed on stored grain, often causing damage to crops and food supplies

Examples

Grain moth larvae
Proper sealing of grain bins can help prevent grain moth problems.
The farmer discovered a grain moth infestation in his wheat storage silo.
They inspected the shipment for signs of grain moth larvae.
