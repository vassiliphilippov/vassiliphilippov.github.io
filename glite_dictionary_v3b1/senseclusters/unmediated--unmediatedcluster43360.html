unmediated

adjective
not involving intermediaries; direct
8.2 6.6
Pronunciation: /ʌn.ˈmiː.di.eɪ.tɪd/ /; / /ən.ˈmiː.di.eɪ.tɪd/ (AmE), /ʌn.ˈmiː.di.eɪ.tɪd/ /; / /ən.ˈmiː.di.eɪ.tɪd/ (BrE)
(of an experience, action, or effect) not involving any intervening agents or influences; direct.

Examples

An unmediated experience
She valued the unmediated experience of seeing the artwork in person rather than viewing photos.
Social media offers a relatively unmediated connection between celebrities and their fans.
The testimony gave the jury an unmediated account of what happened that night.
