suckering

noun
growth of new shoots
6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈsʌk.ər.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈsʌk.ə.rɪŋ/ (BrE)
the process by which new shoots grow from the base or roots of a plant.

Examples

Plant suckering growth
Regular suckering helps maintain the health and shape of many garden plants.
The gardener noticed suckering at the base of the old tree after the spring rains.
Controlling suckering is important to prevent unwanted growth in fruit orchards.

Related Terms

  • antonym: pruning - the act of cutting away unwanted parts of a plant
  • synonym: sprouting - the process of new shoots or leaves beginning to grow
