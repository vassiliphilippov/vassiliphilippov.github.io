macroalga

noun
large aquatic algae
Pronunciation: / ˌmækroʊˈælɡə / (AmE), / ˌmakrəʊˈalɡə / (BrE)
a large, visible type of algae, such as seaweed, found in aquatic environments

Examples

A large macroalga
Kelp is a well-known macroalga that forms underwater forests along many coastlines.
Scientists study macroalgae to understand their role in marine ecosystems.
Some macroalgae are harvested for use in food and cosmetics.

Related Terms

  • antonym: microalga - a microscopic, single-celled type of algae
  • synonym: seaweed - large marine algae found in the sea
