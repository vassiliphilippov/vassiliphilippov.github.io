cassoulet

noun
French cooking pot
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˌkæs.uˈleɪ / (AmE), / ˌkæs.uˈleɪ / (BrE)
a deep, round earthenware pot used for cooking and serving slow-cooked stews, especially in southern France

Examples

Earthenware cassoulet pot
The traditional French stew was served in a large cassoulet at the center of the table.
She bought a beautiful cassoulet to prepare authentic recipes from southern France.
After hours of slow cooking, the chef presented the meal in a rustic cassoulet.
