band-tail pigeon

noun
wild pigeon of western North America
Pronunciation: / ˈbænd teɪl ˈpɪdʒən / (AmE), / ˈbænd teɪl ˈpɪdʒən / (BrE)
A wild pigeon native to western North America, larger than the typical city pigeon, often mistaken for the extinct passenger pigeon.

Examples

The band-tail pigeon
Because of its size and shape, the band-tail pigeon is sometimes confused with the extinct passenger pigeon.
I spotted a flock of band-tail pigeons feeding on berries in the mountains.
The band-tail pigeon is most commonly found in the forests along the Pacific coast.

Related Terms

  • synonym: passenger pigeon - an extinct species of wild pigeon once native to North America
