seroconversion

noun
development of antibodies
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪroʊkənˈvɜrʒən / (AmE), / ˌsɪərəʊkənˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
the process by which detectable antibodies develop in the blood following infection or immunization

Examples

Seroconversion after vaccination
After vaccination, the patient showed seroconversion within two weeks.
Doctors monitor seroconversion to determine if an infection has triggered an immune response.
The timing of seroconversion can vary depending on the disease and the individual's immune system.

Related Terms

  • antonym: seronegative - not having detectable antibodies in the blood
  • synonym: seropositive - having detectable antibodies in the blood
