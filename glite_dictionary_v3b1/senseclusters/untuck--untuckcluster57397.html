untuck

verb
to remove a sewn fold
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈtʌk / (AmE), / ʌnˈtʌk / (BrE)
to open or remove a sewn tuck in clothing or fabric, usually by unpicking stitches to alter its shape or size

Examples

To untuck a seam
The tailor needed to untuck the skirt to give it a looser fit.
She carefully untucked the pleats to update the dress style.
If the shirt feels tight, you can untuck the seams for extra comfort.

Related Terms

  • synonym: let out - to make a garment larger by loosening seams or tucks
  • antonym: tuck - to fold and sew fabric to make it smaller or decorative
