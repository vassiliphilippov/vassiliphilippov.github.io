territorialism

noun
claiming territory
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛrɪˈtɔːriəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌtɛrɪˈtɔːriəˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
the practice or tendency of claiming and defending a specific area or territory as one's own

Examples

Animal territorialism
The cat's territorialism was clear when it chased away other animals from the yard.
Some countries' foreign policies are influenced by a strong sense of territorialism.
In the workplace, territorialism can lead to conflicts over office space and resources.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cooperation - working together without claiming exclusive territory
  • degree higher: expansionism - policy of expanding a nation's territory
  • synonym: territoriality - behavior of defending a territory
