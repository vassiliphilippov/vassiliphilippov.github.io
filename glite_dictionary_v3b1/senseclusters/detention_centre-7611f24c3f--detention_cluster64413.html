detention centre

noun
facility for holding people in custody temporarily
4.6 1.66/m
Pronunciation: / dÉªËˆtÉ›nÊƒÉ™n ËŒsÉ›n.tÉš / (AmE), / dÉªËˆtÉ›nÊƒÉ™n ËŒsÉ›n.tÉ™ / (BrE)
a place where people, such as prisoners, refugees, or illegal immigrants, are held in custody temporarily while awaiting trial, sentence, or resolution of their status

Examples

Local detention centre
After his arrest, he was held overnight in the local detention centre before being released.
Hundreds of refugees have been living in overcrowded conditions in the temporary detention centre.
The government announced plans to close the controversial detention centre by the end of the year.

Synonyms

  • detention camp
    3.2 0.09/m
    a detention camp holds migrants or asylum seekers in large guarded facilities, while a detention centre also holds prisoners awaiting trial or sentence.
  • house of detention
    7.7 0.01/m
    a house of detention holds people sent by a court, often juveniles, while a detention centre holds migrants, suspects, or other detainees.
