Ladoga

proper noun
large lake in northwestern Russia
8.9
Pronunciation: /lə.ˈdoʊ.gə/ (AmE), /lə.ˈdəʊ.gə/ (BrE)
a large lake in northwestern Russia, north of St. Petersburg, and the largest lake in Europe, which drains through the Neva River into the Gulf of Finland

Examples

Lake Ladoga
During winter, the surface of Ladoga often freezes over due to the harsh Russian climate.
Many species of fish inhabit the waters of Ladoga, supporting local fisheries.
The Neva River flows from Ladoga into the Gulf of Finland.
