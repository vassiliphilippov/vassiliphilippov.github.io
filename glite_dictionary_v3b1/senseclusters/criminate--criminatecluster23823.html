criminate

verb
formally accuse of a crime
4.3 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪməˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈkrɪmɪneɪt / (BrE)
to accuse or charge someone with a crime or wrongdoing, especially in a formal or legal context

Examples

To criminate a suspect
Evidence emerged that could potentially criminate the officials involved in the case.
He was careful not to criminate anyone when discussing the incident.
The lawyer did not wish to criminate her client without substantive evidence.
He refused to criminate himself when questioned by the authorities.
Her testimony could potentially criminate several senior officials implicated in the case.
The witness was careful not to criminate anyone without concrete evidence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accuse - to say that someone has done something wrong or illegal
  • antonym: exonerate - to officially state that someone is not to blame or is free of guilt
