ashfall

noun
volcanic ash deposit
6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈæʃ.fɑːl/ /; / /ˈæʃ.fɔːl/ (AmE), /ˈæʃ.fɔːl/ (BrE)
a layer of ash that falls from the air and settles on the ground, especially after a volcanic eruption or a large fire

Examples

Volcanic ashfall
After the eruption, a thick ashfall blanketed the nearby villages and farmland.
Residents wore masks to protect themselves from the ashfall in the days following the volcanic activity.
The airport was closed due to heavy ashfall making visibility poor and runways unsafe.
After the wildfire, a thick layer of ashfall covered the entire neighborhood.
Residents wore masks to protect themselves from the ashfall drifting through the air.
The ashfall from the burning forest made driving conditions dangerous for several days.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fallout - particles that fall to the ground after an explosion or fire
  • antonym: lava flow - molten rock that moves over the ground during a volcanic eruption
  • synonym: soot - black powdery substance produced by burning
  • synonym: tephra - fragmented material ejected by a volcanic eruption
