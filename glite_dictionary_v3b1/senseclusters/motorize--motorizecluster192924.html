motorize

verb
equip with a motor
2.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmoʊtəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈməʊtəraɪz / (BrE)
to equip something (such as a vehicle, device, or organization) with a motor or motor vehicles, making it powered by a motor instead of manual or animal power; also used specifically to mean equipping military units with armed and armored motor vehicles

Examples

To motorize delivery carts
The company plans to motorize all its delivery carts next year.
The old farm equipment was eventually motorized to increase efficiency.
With advances in technology, many bicycles are now easy to motorize.
Many local police forces were motorized in the 1930s to improve response times.
The army began to motorize its infantry units after the invention of reliable trucks.
They plan to motorize their entire fleet of delivery carts by next year.
During World War II, many armies sought to motorize their forces to enhance battlefield effectiveness.
The government decided to motorize its infantry divisions for greater mobility.
To improve logistics, the unit was quickly motorized and supplied with new armored vehicles.

Related Terms

  • antonym: demotorise - to remove motor vehicles or revert to non-motorized equipment, especially in a military context
  • synonym: mechanize - equip with machinery, usually to replace manual labor
