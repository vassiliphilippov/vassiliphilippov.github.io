coexecutor

noun
joint executor
8.1
Pronunciation: /koʊ.ɪg.ˈzek.jə.tər/ /; / /kə.ɪg.ˈzek.jə.tər/ (AmE), /kəʊ.ɪg.ˈzek.jə.tə/ /; / /kə.ɪg.ˈzek.jə.tə/ (BrE)
a person who shares the legal responsibility with others for carrying out the duties of an executor, especially for a will

Examples

The coexecutors of estate
The will named her and her brother as coexecutors of the estate.
As coexecutor, he worked closely with his cousin to settle all legal matters.
The two coexecutors divided the responsibilities to ensure everything was handled efficiently.
The lawyer explained that each coexecutor must agree before any assets are distributed.
As a coexecutor, she was responsible for managing the estate with her brother.
The will named two coexecutors to ensure the process was fair and transparent.
