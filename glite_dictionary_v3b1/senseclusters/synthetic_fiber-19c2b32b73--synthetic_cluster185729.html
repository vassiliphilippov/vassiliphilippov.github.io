synthetic fiber

noun
man-made fiber produced by chemical synthesis
3.4 4.0
Pronunciation: / sɪnˈθɛtɪk ˈfaɪbɚ / (AmE), / sɪnˈθɛtɪk ˈfaɪbə / (BrE)
a fiber produced by chemical synthesis, usually from polymers derived from petroleum or other sources, rather than directly from natural substances

Examples

Synthetic fiber carpet
Carpets made from synthetic fiber are durable and easy to clean.
Many clothes are now made from synthetic fiber rather than cotton or wool.
Nylon is a popular synthetic fiber used in the textile industry.
