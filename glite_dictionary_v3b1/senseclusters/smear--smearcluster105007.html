smear

noun
untrue accusation; defamation
9.2
Pronunciation: / smɪr / (AmE), / smɪə / (BrE)
an untrue or unfair accusation or remark intended to damage someone's reputation

Examples

A political smear
Despite repeated smear attempts, his reputation remained intact.
She became the victim of a vicious smear orchestrated by her rivals.
The politician dismissed the story as a smear intended to ruin his career.
He was determined to clear his name after the smear had been published.
Politicians are often the target of a smear intended to undermine their public image.
The malicious smear against her reputation was later proven to be false.

Related Terms

  • antonym: commendation - the act of praising or recommending someone
  • synonym: libel - a published false statement that is damaging to a person's reputation
  • antonym: praise - express warm approval or admiration of
  • synonym: slander - the action or crime of making a false spoken statement damaging to a person's reputation
