solubilize

verb
to dissolve in liquid
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑːljəbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɒljʊbɪˌlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something dissolve or become soluble in a liquid, especially by using chemicals

Examples

To solubilize powder
The scientist added a chemical to solubilize the powder in water.
Some vitamins only solubilize in fat, not in water.
Heating the mixture helped the compound solubilize more quickly.
The researcher added a chemical to solubilize the fat in the sample.
Enzymes can help solubilize nutrients, making them easier to absorb.
To analyze the compound, the technician needed to solubilize it in water first.
Scientists use special agents to solubilize proteins for laboratory analysis.
Adding salt can help solubilize certain minerals in water.
The new formula is designed to solubilize vitamins for better absorption.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dissolve - to mix with a liquid and become part of it
  • antonym: precipitate - to cause a substance to separate from a solution as a solid
