Shebat

noun
month in the Jewish calendar (Jan–Feb)
Pronunciation: / ʃəˈbat / (AmE), / ʃɛˈbat / (BrE)
the fifth month of the civil year and the eleventh month of the ecclesiastical year in the Jewish calendar, usually falling in January and February

Examples

The month Shebat
Shebat usually begins in January or February according to the Gregorian calendar.
Many important events in Jewish tradition are commemorated during Shebat.
The festival of Tu Bishvat is celebrated in the month of Shebat.
