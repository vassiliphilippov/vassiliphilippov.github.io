scintillator

noun
radiation-detecting material
9.4
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪn.tə.leɪ.tər/ /; / /ˈsɪn.tɪ.leɪ.tər/ (AmE), /ˈsɪn.tɪ.leɪ.tə/ /; / /ˈsɪn.tə.leɪ.tə/ (BrE)
a material or device that emits light when it absorbs ionizing radiation, used to detect radiation

Examples

Scintillator crystal detector
The scintillator produced a flash of light when exposed to the radioactive sample.
Scientists use a scintillator to measure the intensity of ionizing radiation in the lab.
A plastic scintillator is often used in medical imaging equipment for detecting X-rays.
The laboratory uses a scintillator to measure radiation levels in the samples.
A plastic scintillator is often chosen for its fast response time in particle detectors.
Technicians checked the scintillator for any signs of malfunction before starting the experiment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: detector - a device that identifies the presence of something
  • synonym: photomultiplier - a device that detects and amplifies light signals
