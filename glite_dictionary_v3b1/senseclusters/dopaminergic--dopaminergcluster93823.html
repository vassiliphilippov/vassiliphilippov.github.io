dopaminergic

noun
dopamine-acting drug
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˌdoʊpəmaɪˈnɜrdʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌdəʊpəmaɪˈnɜːdʒɪk / (BrE)
a drug or agent that increases or mimics the effects of dopamine in the brain

Examples

A dopaminergic drug
The neurologist prescribed a dopaminergic to help manage the patient's symptoms of Parkinson's disease.
Some dopaminergics are used to treat restless legs syndrome and other movement disorders.
Researchers are studying new dopaminergic agents for their potential in treating depression.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agonist - a substance that activates a receptor to produce a biological response
  • antonym: antidopaminergic - a drug or agent that blocks or reduces the effects of dopamine
