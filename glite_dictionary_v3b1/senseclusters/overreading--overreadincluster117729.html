overreading

noun
excessive interpretation
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈriːdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈriːdɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of interpreting something in an excessively detailed or imaginative way, often beyond the intended meaning

Examples

Overreading a poem
The professor warned students against overreading minor details in the poem.
Some viewers are guilty of overreading every scene in the film for hidden messages.
Her overreading of the contract led to unnecessary confusion about its terms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overinterpretation - interpreting something with more meaning than intended
  • antonym: underreading - failing to notice or interpret important details
