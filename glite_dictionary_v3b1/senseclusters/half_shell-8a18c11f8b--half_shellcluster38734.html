half shell

noun
mollusk shell used to serve food
Pronunciation: / hæf ʃɛl / (AmE), / hɑːf ʃɛl / (BrE)
a shell of a mollusk, typically used as a serving dish after being cut or split in half, especially for serving seafood such as oysters or clams

Examples

Scallops on half shell
Seafood lovers often prefer their scallops served on a half shell for maximum flavor.
She ordered clams on the half shell as an appetizer.
The chef presented the oysters on a half shell with a wedge of lemon.
