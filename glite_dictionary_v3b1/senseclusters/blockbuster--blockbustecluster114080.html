blockbuster

noun
hugely popular and successful work
Pronunciation: / ˈblɑkˌbʌstər / (AmE), / ˈblɒkˌbʌstə / (BrE)
a film, book, play, or other work that is extremely popular and commercially successful

Examples

A blockbuster movie
Every summer, studios hope to release a blockbuster that draws large audiences.
Her novel became an instant blockbuster and topped bestseller lists for months.
The latest superhero movie was a blockbuster, earning millions worldwide.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flop - an unsuccessful entertainment work
  • synonym: hit - a successful and popular entertainment work
