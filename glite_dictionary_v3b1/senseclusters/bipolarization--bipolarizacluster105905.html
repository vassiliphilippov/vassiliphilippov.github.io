bipolarization

noun
formation of two poles
Pronunciation: / baɪˌpoʊlərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / baɪˌpəʊləraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which something develops or acquires two opposite electrical or magnetic poles

Examples

Bipolarization of atoms
The scientist observed bipolarization in the material when it was exposed to a strong electric field.
Rapid bipolarization can occur in certain crystals under specific laboratory conditions.
The experiment demonstrated how bipolarization leads to the creation of distinct positive and negative regions.
