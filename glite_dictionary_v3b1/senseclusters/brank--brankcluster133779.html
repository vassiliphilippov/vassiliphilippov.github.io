brank

verb
to gag as punishment
9.9
Pronunciation: /bræŋk/ (AmE), /bræŋk/ (BrE)
to punish or silence someone by putting a brank (scold's bridle) on them, especially as a form of public humiliation.

Examples

To brank a woman
In the seventeenth century, authorities would brank women accused of gossiping in the village square.
The threat to brank offenders was meant to discourage public arguments and slander.
She feared they might brank her if she continued to speak out against the council.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gag - to prevent someone from speaking
  • synonym: silence - to make someone stop speaking
