profuse

adjective
abundant; in great quantity
6.5 0.38/m
Pronunciation: / pr…ôÀàfjus / (AmE), / pr…ôÀàfjuÀês / (BrE)
(especially of something offered or produced) existing or occurring in large amounts; abundant or excessive.

Examples

Profuse thanks
He apologized with profuse gratitude after making the mistake.
The accident caused a profuse bleeding that needed immediate attention.
The garden was filled with profuse blooms in every color imaginable.

Synonyms

  • abundant
    3.0 12/m
    abundant appears in general descriptions of large supply, while profuse often suggests excess or overflowing amounts, especially in offers or gifts.
  • plentiful
    2.5 4.90/m
    plentiful refers to resources or food in large supply, while profuse suggests more than needed and can seem excessive.
  • copious
    6.8 1.70/m
    copious describes large, often written or recorded amounts like notes, while profuse is used for excess, often in gestures or speech.
  • bountiful
    4.9 1.25/m
    bountiful describes generous, often natural produce or gifts, while profuse implies an overflowing, sometimes excessive amount or praise.
