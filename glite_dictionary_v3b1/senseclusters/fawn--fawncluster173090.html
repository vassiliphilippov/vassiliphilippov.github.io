fawn

verb
to flatter or behave submissively
7.7 1.37/m
Pronunciation: / f…în / (AmE), / f…îÀên / (BrE)
to behave in an overly flattering or submissive way to gain someone's favor or approval

Examples

To fawn over a boss
He fawned on his superiors in hopes of getting a promotion.
Politicians sometimes fawn on influential donors to secure their support.
The employee would fawn over the manager whenever she entered the room.
Instead of honest feedback, he would fawn on anyone in authority.
She tends to fawn over her supervisors, hoping for a promotion.
You don't have to fawn to succeed; genuine effort is more valuable.
