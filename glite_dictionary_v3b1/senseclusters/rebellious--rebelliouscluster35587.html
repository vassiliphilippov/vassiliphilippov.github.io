rebellious

adjective
showing resistance to authority
1.4
Pronunciation: / rɪˈbɛliəs / (AmE), / rɪˈbɛlɪəs /; / rɪˈbɛliəs / (BrE)
showing a desire to resist authority, control, or rules; disobedient or defiant

Examples

Rebellious teenager
Her rebellious attitude often got her into trouble at school.
Many artists have a rebellious spirit that challenges conventional ideas.
The teenager became increasingly rebellious after his parents set strict curfews.
After a rebellious outburst, the student was sent to the principal's office.
The rebellious teenager often questioned her parents' rules and refused to follow them.
The government struggled to control the rebellious province during the unrest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defiant - showing resistance or opposition
  • antonym: obedient - willing to do what one is told by authority
