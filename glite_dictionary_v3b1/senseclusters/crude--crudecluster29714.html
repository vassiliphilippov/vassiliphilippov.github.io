crude

adjective
in a raw, unrefined state
9.2
Pronunciation: / kruːd / (AmE), / kruːd / (BrE)
in a natural, raw, or unprocessed state; not yet refined or developed

Examples

Crude oil
The miners transported crude ores to the factory for further processing.
The refinery turns crude oil into gasoline and other useful products.
This region has vast reserves of crude petroleum underground.
At the archaeological site, they found crude tools made from stone and bone.
Early filmmakers had to work with crude equipment that could barely capture moving images.
The first computers were crude by modern standards but marked a major technological leap.
The crude figures do not account for seasonal variations.
The report showed only the crude statistics without any interpretation.
We started our analysis with crude data collected directly from the sensors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: primitive - relating to an early stage of development; simple or basic
  • synonym: raw - not processed, in a natural state (especially of materials or data)
  • antonym: refined - (of a substance) processed to remove impurities or unwanted elements
