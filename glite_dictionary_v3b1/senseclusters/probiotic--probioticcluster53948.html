probiotic

noun
supplement with beneficial live bacteria
2.2 3.3
Pronunciation: /proʊ.baɪ.ˈɑː.tɪk/ /; / /prəʊ.baɪ.ˈɑː.tɪk/ (AmE), /prəʊ.baɪ.ˈɒ.tɪk/ (BrE)
A dietary supplement or food containing beneficial live bacteria, intended to restore or improve the natural balance of microorganisms in the gut.

Examples

A probiotic supplement
After taking antibiotics, her doctor recommended a daily probiotic to help restore her gut flora.
Many yogurts are marketed as containing added probiotic cultures to promote digestive health.
Researchers are studying the effects of probiotic supplements on immune system function.

Related Terms

  • antonym: antibiotic - a medicine that destroys or inhibits the growth of microorganisms
