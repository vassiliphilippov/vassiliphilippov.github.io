have a say

verb
have input or influence in a matter
Pronunciation: / hæv ə seɪ / (AmE), / hæv ə seɪ / (BrE)
to have the right or opportunity to express an opinion or influence a decision

Examples

To have a say in decisions
All team members should have a say in choosing the new logo.
He felt frustrated when he didn't have a say in the budget discussion.
Parents want their children to have a say about family vacations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: have a voice - to be able to express opinions or influence decisions
