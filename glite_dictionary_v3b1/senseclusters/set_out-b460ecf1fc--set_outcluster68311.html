set out

verb
begin a journey or leave for somewhere
2.4 6.66/m
Pronunciation: / sÉ›t aÊŠt / (AmE), / sÉ›t aÊŠt / (BrE)
to begin a journey; to leave one place in order to go somewhere else

Examples

Set out on a trip
She packed her bags and set out for Paris last week.
The explorers set out on their expedition with high hopes.
We decided to set out early in the morning to avoid traffic.

  • hit the road

  • take off
    3.2 7.01/m

  • get going

  • start
