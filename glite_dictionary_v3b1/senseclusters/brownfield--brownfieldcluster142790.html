brownfield

adjective
previously developed land
Pronunciation: / ˈbraʊnˌfiːld / (AmE), / ˈbraʊnˌfiːld / (BrE)
relating to the redevelopment or reuse of land or buildings that have been previously developed, especially in urban areas.

Examples

Brownfield redevelopment project
The city encourages brownfield projects to revitalize old industrial neighborhoods.
Developers face unique challenges when working on brownfield sites due to possible contamination.
A brownfield approach can help reduce urban sprawl by reusing existing land.

Related Terms

  • antonym: greenfield - relating to undeveloped land or new projects on previously unused sites
