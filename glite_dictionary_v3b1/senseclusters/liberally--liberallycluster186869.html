liberally

adverb
freely or nonliterally; with exaggeration
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪb.ɚ.ə.li / (AmE), / ˈlɪb.ər.ə.li / (BrE)
In a way that is free and not literal, often involving exaggeration or embellishment.

Examples

Liberally interpret rules
He used metaphors liberally when describing his childhood adventures.
She quoted Shakespeare liberally, sometimes twisting the lines for dramatic effect.
The novelist liberally embellished the facts to make the story more exciting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: figuratively - in a metaphorical or nonliteral sense
  • antonym: literally - in a strict, literal sense
