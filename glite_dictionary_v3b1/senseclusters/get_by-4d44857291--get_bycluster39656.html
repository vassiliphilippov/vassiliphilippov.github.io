get by

verb
manage or survive with limited means
1.0 2.6
Pronunciation: /get baɪ/ /; / /gɪt baɪ/ (AmE), /get baɪ/ /; / /gɪt baɪ/ (BrE)
to manage to survive or continue, especially with limited resources or in difficult situations

Examples

To get by on little money
During the lean years, she had to get by with simple meals and no luxuries.
Even with little training, he managed to get by in his new job.
We barely get by on my salary, but we manage to pay the bills each month.
During the recession, many families had to get by on reduced incomes.
She doesn't earn much, but she can get by with what she has.
When studying abroad, I managed to get by with only basic knowledge of the language.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cope - to deal effectively with something difficult
  • synonym: make do - to manage with what is available, often when resources are lacking
  • synonym: manage - to succeed in doing something, especially something difficult
  • antonym: thrive - to grow, develop, or be successful (often in contrast to merely surviving)
