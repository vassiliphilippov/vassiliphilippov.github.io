tarnishment

noun
loss of metal shine
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑrnɪʃmənt / (AmE), / ˈtɑːnɪʃmənt / (BrE)
a dull or discolored layer that forms on the surface of a metal, reducing its shine, usually due to a chemical reaction

Examples

Silver tarnishment
The tarnishment on the old silver tray made it look much older than it really was.
Regular cleaning can help prevent tarnishment of your jewelry and keep it looking bright.
After years of neglect, the statue suffered significant tarnishment and lost its original shine.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corrosion - the gradual destruction of materials, especially metals, by chemical reaction
  • synonym: oxidation - a chemical reaction in which a substance combines with oxygen, often causing rust or tarnish
  • antonym: polish - to make a surface smooth and shiny by rubbing
