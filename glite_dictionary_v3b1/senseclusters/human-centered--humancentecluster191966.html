human-centered

adjective
focused on human needs and welfare
Pronunciation: / ˌhjuːmənˈsɛntɚd /; / ˈhjuːmən ˌsɛntərd / (AmE), / ˌhjuːmənˈsɛntəd /; / ˈhjuːmən ˌsɛntəd / (BrE)
marked by concern for human needs, values, and welfare; emphasizing the importance of humans in decision-making or design.

Examples

A human-centered design
Many modern offices are shifting towards more human-centered work environments to boost well-being.
Our company takes a human-centered approach to product design, always considering the user's experience first.
She advocates for human-centered policies that prioritize people over profits.
Modern urban planning increasingly favors human-centred solutions to improve quality of life.
The new product was designed with a human-centred approach to ensure it met users' needs.
Their company culture is distinctly human-centred, valuing employee well-being above profits.

Related Terms

  • synonym: humanitarian - concerned with promoting human welfare
