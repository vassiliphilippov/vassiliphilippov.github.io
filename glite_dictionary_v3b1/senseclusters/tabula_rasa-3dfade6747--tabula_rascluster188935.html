tabula rasa

noun
the mind as a blank slate; unmarked by experience
Pronunciation: / ˌtæbjələ ˈrɑsə / (AmE), / ˌtabjʊlə ˈrɑːsə /; / ˌtabjʊlə ˈrɑːzə / (BrE)
the idea of a mind or situation as a blank slate, unmarked by experience or existing content; a fresh start

Examples

A tabula rasa mind
John Locke believed that each child is born as a tabula rasa, ready to be shaped by experience.
She approached her new life as a tabula rasa, free from her past.
The concept of tabula rasa is often discussed in theories of human development.
After the company merger, the organization treated its culture as a tabula rasa to avoid old conflicts.
The abandoned building was seen by artists as a tabula rasa for creative expression.
With each new year, she viewed her goals as a tabula rasa, ready to write a new story.
