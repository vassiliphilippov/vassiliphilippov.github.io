Federal Housing Administration

proper noun
US agency insuring home mortgages
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˌfɛdɚəl ˈhaʊzɪŋ ədˌmɪnəˈstreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɛdərəl ˈhaʊzɪŋ ədˌmɪnɪˈstreɪʃən / (BrE)
a US government agency within the Department of Housing and Urban Development that insures residential mortgages to promote home ownership

Examples

A Federal Housing Administration loan
An Federal Housing Administration loan often allows a lower down payment than conventional mortgages.
Many first-time homebuyers rely on loans insured by the Federal Housing Administration.
The Federal Housing Administration was established to help stabilize the housing market during the Great Depression.
