Leto

proper noun
Greek goddess, mother of Apollo and Artemis
9.4 0.19/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàliÀêto ä / (AmE), / ÀàliÀêt…ô ä / (BrE)
A goddess in Greek mythology, wife or mistress of Zeus and mother of Apollo and Artemis; known as Latona in Roman mythology.

Examples

Leto goddess mother
According to myth, Leto gave birth to Apollo and Artemis on the island of Delos.
In Roman mythology, Leto is known by the name Latona.
The ancient Greeks honored Leto as a gentle and protective deity.

Synonyms

  • Latona
    9.9 0.07/m
    Latona is the Roman mother of Apollo and Artemis in Roman myths, while Leto is her Greek name in Greek stories.
