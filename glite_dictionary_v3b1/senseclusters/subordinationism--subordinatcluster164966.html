subordinationism

noun
theological doctrine of hierarchy
9.9
Pronunciation: /səb.ɔːr.də.ˈneɪ.ʃən.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /sʌb.ɔːr.də.ˈneɪ.ʃən.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /səb.ɔː.də.ˈneɪ.ʃən.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /sʌb.ɔː.də.ˈneɪ.ʃən.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the doctrine in Christian theology that the Son and Holy Spirit are subordinate to the Father in nature or status

Examples

Subordinationism in theology
The early church debated subordinationism during several important councils.
Many theologians wrote treatises opposing subordinationism in defense of the Trinity.
Some historical Christian groups were accused of teaching subordinationism rather than orthodox doctrine.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arianism - the belief that the Son is distinct from and subordinate to the Father
  • antonym: trinitarianism - the doctrine that the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit are coequal and coeternal
