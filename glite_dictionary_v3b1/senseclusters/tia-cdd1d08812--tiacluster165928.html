TIA

noun
transient ischemic attack; brief brain ischemia
7.7 0.3/m
Pronunciation: / tiÀê a…™ Ààe…™ / (AmE), / tiÀê a…™ Ààe…™ / (BrE)
A brief episode of neurological dysfunction resulting from a temporary interruption of blood flow to the brain, often serving as a warning sign for stroke.

Examples

A TIA episode
After experiencing a TIA, he was prescribed medication to reduce his risk factors.
Recent research focuses on early diagnosis and treatment after a TIA to prevent long-term complications.
The doctor informed her that a TIA could be a warning sign for a future stroke.

Synonyms

  • mini stroke

    mini stroke is an informal, everyday phrase people use to describe a brief event, while TIA is the clinical term.

Related Terms
