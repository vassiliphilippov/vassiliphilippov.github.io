nonsignificant

adjective
not statistically significant
7.6 6.0
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑːn.sɪɡˈnɪf.ɪ.kənt /; / ˌnɑn.sɪɡˈnɪf.ɪ.kənt / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.sɪɡˈnɪf.ɪ.kənt / (BrE)
not statistically or practically important; likely due to chance or too small to matter

Examples

A nonsignificant result
A nonsignificant result suggests that the observed effect could be due to random variation.
After analysis, the differences between the two groups proved to be nonsignificant.
The change in blood pressure was nonsignificant according to the study results.
The study found a nonsignificant increase in test scores after the new teaching method was introduced.
There was a nonsignificant difference in recovery times between the two treatments.
The changes in temperature were nonsignificant and did not affect the experiment's outcome.
