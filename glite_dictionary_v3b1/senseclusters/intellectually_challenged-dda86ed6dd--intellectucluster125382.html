intellectually challenged

adjective
having cognitive impairments; with learning difficulties
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.t̬əˈlɛk.tʃu.ə.li ˈtʃæl.ɪndʒd / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈlɛk.tʃu.ə.li ˈtʃæl.ɪndʒd / (BrE)
having cognitive impairments or learning difficulties; used as a polite or less direct way to refer to someone with an intellectual disability

Examples

Children intellectually challenged
New teaching methods can greatly benefit children who are intellectually challenged.
She has dedicated her career to supporting the intellectually challenged in her community.
The school offers special programs for students who are intellectually challenged.
