dilator

noun
muscle, nerve, or device that widens a body part
1.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪ.leɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.leɪ.tə / (BrE)
a muscle, nerve, or device that causes a body part to become wider or more open

Examples

Dilator muscle action
After the surgery, the doctor used a dilator to gradually open the passageway.
Certain drugs affect the dilator nerves, making blood vessels wider.
The ophthalmologist explained how the dilator muscles in the eye control pupil size.

Related Terms

  • antonym: constrictor - a muscle that narrows a body part
