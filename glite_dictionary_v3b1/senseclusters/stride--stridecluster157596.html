stride

noun
distance of a single step
Pronunciation: / straɪd / (AmE), / straɪd / (BrE)
a long step or the distance covered by a single step when walking or running

Examples

A long stride
His long stride allowed him to cross the room in just three steps.
She measured the field by counting each stride as she walked.
The runner increased his stride to gain speed during the race.
He covered the distance in just a few strides.
She took a confident stride towards the stage.
With each stride, he moved closer to the finish line.
