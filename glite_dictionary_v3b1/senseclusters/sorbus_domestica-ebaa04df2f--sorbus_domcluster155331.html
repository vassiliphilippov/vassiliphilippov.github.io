Sorbus domestica

noun
European tree with edible fruit
Pronunciation: / ˈsɔrbəs doʊˈmɛstɪkə / (AmE), / ˈsɔːbəs dəʊˈmɛstɪkə / (BrE)
A medium-sized European tree of the rose family, resembling the rowan, with edible, pear-shaped fruit.

Examples

Sorbus domestica tree
Botanists are working to preserve the endangered Sorbus domestica in its natural habitat.
Several Sorbus domestica trees grow in the ancient forests of central Europe.
The fruit of the Sorbus domestica is edible and was once popular for making cider.
