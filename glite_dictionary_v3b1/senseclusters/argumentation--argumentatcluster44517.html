argumentation

noun
systematic reasoning or discussion
5.1 4.6
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːrgjəmenˈteɪʃən /; / ˌɑːrɡjəmenˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɑːgjʊmenˈteɪʃən /; / ˌɑːɡjʊmenˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of reasoning, discussing, or presenting arguments to support or oppose a point, especially in academic or persuasive contexts

Examples

Academic argumentation
Effective argumentation requires both logical thinking and persuasive skills.

Her argumentation was thorough and convinced many of those present.
The lawyer's argumentation led the jury to question the credibility of the witness.
