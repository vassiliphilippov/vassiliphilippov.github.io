thunderously

adverb
very loudly
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈθʌndɚəsli /; / ˈθʌndəɹəsli / (AmE), / ˈθʌndərəsli / (BrE)
in a very loud, forceful, or intense way, like thunder

Examples

Cheer thunderously
The crowd cheered thunderously as the team scored the winning goal.
Rain fell thunderously on the metal roof during the storm.
He slammed the door thunderously, startling everyone in the house.
The audience thunderously applauded the final scene of the performance.
Her ideas were thunderously rejected by the committee, leaving no room for debate.
The team was thunderously defeated in the championship match, losing by a wide margin.

Related Terms

  • synonym: extremely - to a very high degree
  • synonym: loudly - in a manner that produces a lot of noise
  • antonym: mildly - to a small or moderate degree
  • antonym: softly - in a quiet or gentle manner
