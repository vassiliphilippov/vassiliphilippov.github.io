skyglow

noun
natural night brightness
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈskaɪˌɡloʊ / (AmE), / ˈskaɪˌɡləʊ / (BrE)
the natural brightness of the night sky caused by atmospheric or astronomical sources, not by artificial lighting

Examples

Natural skyglow
The faint skyglow made it possible to see the outlines of the mountains at midnight.
Astronomers prefer remote locations to avoid any interference with the natural skyglow.
During the aurora, the skyglow was especially bright and colorful above the horizon.

Related Terms

  • synonym: airglow - faint natural light in the night sky from atmospheric processes
  • antonym: light pollution - artificial brightening of the night sky from human-made lights
