backstep

verb
withdraw from a commitment
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌstɛp / (AmE), / ˈbækˌstɛp / (BrE)
to withdraw or retreat from a commitment, promise, or stated position

Examples

To backstep from promise
After announcing his support, the senator chose to backstep when the proposal became unpopular.
She rarely backsteps on her promises, but this situation was exceptional.
The company decided to backstep from the merger due to unforeseen financial risks.
