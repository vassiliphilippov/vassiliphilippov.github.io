complexion

noun
archaic: mix of qualities determining health/temperament
Pronunciation: / kəmˈplɛkʃən / (AmE), / kəmˈplɛkʃən / (BrE)
(archaic) The combination of qualities, such as dryness and warmth or the four humors, once believed to determine a person's health and temperament.

Examples

Melancholic complexion
He was believed to have a melancholic complexion, affecting both his mood and appearance.
In ancient medicine, a person's complexion was thought to influence their character.
Medieval physicians diagnosed illnesses based on an imbalance in one's complexion.
