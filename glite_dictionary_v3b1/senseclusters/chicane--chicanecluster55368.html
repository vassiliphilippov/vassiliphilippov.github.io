chicane

noun
a sharp bend or barrier on a racetrack
Pronunciation: / ʃɪˈkeɪn / (AmE), / ʃɪˈkeɪn / (BrE)
a sharp double bend or series of bends in a road or on a racetrack, often formed by movable barriers to slow vehicles down before a dangerous section

Examples

Racetrack chicane
A temporary chicane was installed on the track to improve safety before the sharp curve.
Drivers must slow down to navigate the chicane without losing control of their cars.
The addition of a chicane helped to prevent accidents at the high-speed section of the circuit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barrier - something that blocks or obstructs passage
