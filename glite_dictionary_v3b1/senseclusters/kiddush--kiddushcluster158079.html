kiddush

noun
Jewish blessing ritual
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪdɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkɪdɪʃ / (BrE)
a Jewish blessing recited over wine or bread to sanctify the Sabbath or a holiday

Examples

Friday night kiddush
Every Friday evening, the family gathers to recite kiddush before the Sabbath meal.
The rabbi led the congregation in kiddush to mark the beginning of the holiday.
Children listened quietly as their grandfather performed kiddush over a cup of wine.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blessing - a prayer asking for divine favor and protection
  • synonym: prayer - words spoken to God or a deity
