carryout

adjective
for taking away
2.8
Pronunciation: / ˈkæriˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈkæriˌaʊt / (BrE)
intended to be taken away from the place of purchase rather than eaten or used there

Examples

Carryout menu
The restaurant offers a special carryout menu for customers who want to eat at home.
We decided to order carryout because we were in a hurry and couldn't stay.
Many places provide discounts on carryout orders during the week.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dine-in - intended to be eaten at the place of purchase
  • synonym: takeout - prepared food intended to be eaten elsewhere
