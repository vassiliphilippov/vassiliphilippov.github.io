thru

adjective
direct, uninterrupted
6.6
Pronunciation: / θru / (AmE), / θru / (BrE)
continuing directly without change, stop, or transfer, especially used before a noun to describe uninterrupted service or movement

Examples

Thru train service
We took a thru train from New York to Washington, so we did not have to change.
The airline offers several thru flights each day between the two cities.
A thru bus service makes the journey much faster and more convenient.

Related Terms

  • antonym: connecting - requiring a change or transfer, especially in travel
  • synonym: direct - without stops or changes; straight from start to finish
  • synonym: nonstop - without any stops between the beginning and end
