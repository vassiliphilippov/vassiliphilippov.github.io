Synonyms

  • completely
    1.0 160/m
    completely describes something done in every way or to the full extent, while wholly sounds more formal and careful in writing.
  • entirely
    2.2 79/m
    entirely means with nothing else included or involved, while wholly feels slightly more formal and is common in careful writing.
  • fully
    1.0 102/m
    fully means to the greatest possible extent and often fits with verbs like recover or agree, while wholly sounds more formal.
  • totally
    1.0 109/m
    totally is informal and strong, often used in speech for emphasis, while wholly is more formal and neutral in tone.
  • altogether
    3.3 12/m
    altogether can mean completely or in total and also 'on the whole', while wholly stresses full inclusion and sounds more formal.
  • utterly
    3.6 18/m
    utterly stresses an extreme degree and often pairs with strong negatives, while wholly is neutral and used in formal contexts.
  • absolutely
    1.0 139/m
    absolutely adds strong emphasis or is used to agree in speech, while wholly is a formal adverb meaning completely.
