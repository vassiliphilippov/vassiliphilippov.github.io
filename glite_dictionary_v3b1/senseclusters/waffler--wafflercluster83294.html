waffler

noun
person who is vague or evasive in speech
6.0 6.8
Pronunciation: /ˈwɑːf.lər/ /; / /ˈwɔːf.lər/ (AmE), /ˈwɒf.lə/ (BrE)
a person who speaks or writes in a vague or evasive manner, especially to avoid making a clear decision or statement

Examples

A waffler avoids answers
He earned a reputation as a waffler after repeatedly dodging difficult questions.
No one wanted a waffler on the debate team because decisive responses were essential.
The politician was criticized as a waffler for never giving clear answers during interviews.

  • antonym: straight-talker - someone who speaks plainly and directly
