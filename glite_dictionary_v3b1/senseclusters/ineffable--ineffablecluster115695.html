ineffable

adjective
too great to be described in words
8.7 7.6
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈef.ə.bəl/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈnef.ə.bəl/ (BrE)
too great, extreme, or sacred to be described or expressed in words

Examples

An ineffable feeling
She was overwhelmed by an ineffable feeling of joy.
Some experiences are so profound, they are simply ineffable.
The ineffable beauty of the sunset left everyone speechless.
Ancient texts refer to an ineffable word that only high priests could know.
In certain traditions, the divine is considered ineffable and thus unpronounceable by mortals.
To some believers, the name of God is so ineffable that it must never be spoken aloud.

Related Terms

  • antonym: expressible - able to be expressed or described in words
  • synonym: indescribable - impossible to describe; beyond words
  • synonym: unspeakable - too bad or extreme to be expressed in words
