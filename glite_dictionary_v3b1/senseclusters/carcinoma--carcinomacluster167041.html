carcinoma

noun
malignant tumor of epithelial tissue
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːrsɪˈnoʊmə / (AmE), / ˌkɑːsɪˈnəʊmə / (BrE)
A malignant tumor that develops from epithelial cells and is one of the main types of cancer.

Examples

Basal cell carcinoma
After a biopsy, the growth was identified as a basal cell carcinoma.
Early detection of carcinoma greatly improves the chances of successful treatment.
The doctor explained that the carcinoma had developed in her lung tissue.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sarcoma - malignant tumor of connective tissue
