snug

adjective
warm and cozy (esp. in a small space)
5.5 1.3
Pronunciation: / snʌɡ / (AmE), / snʌɡ / (BrE)
warm, comfortable, and fitting closely or securely; cozy and protected

Examples

Snug blanket
She felt snug under the thick blanket as the rain fell outside.
The cat curled up in a snug corner of the sofa, purring contentedly.
They found a snug cabin to stay in during the winter trip.
His new jacket has a snug fit that keeps him warm on cold days.
She wrapped the package in a snug layer of bubble wrap.
The family lived in a snug cottage by the lake.
Make sure the lid is snug so that the container doesn't leak.
She wrapped herself in a snug sweater on the chilly morning.
The new shoes were a snug fit, hugging his feet without being too tight.

Related Terms

  • antonym: chilly - uncomfortably cool or cold
  • synonym: comfortable - providing physical ease and relaxation
  • synonym: compact - closely and neatly packed together
  • synonym: cozy - giving a feeling of comfort, warmth, and relaxation
  • antonym: exposed - unprotected or vulnerable
  • antonym: loose - not fitting closely; opposite of snug or tight
  • antonym: roomy - spacious; having plenty of space
  • synonym: secure - free from danger or threat
  • degree higher: tight - fitting closely, often uncomfortably so
