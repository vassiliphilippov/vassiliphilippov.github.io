sulfur bacteria

noun
bacteria that oxidize sulfur for energy
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌlfər bækˈtɪəriə / (AmE), / ˈsʌlfə bækˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
Any bacterium (including members of the genus Thiobacillus and other bacteria) that obtains energy by oxidizing or reducing sulfur or sulfur compounds as part of its metabolism.

Examples

Sulfur bacteria play an important role in the natural cycling of sulfur in the environment.

Some types of sulfur bacteria are used in bioremediation to treat polluted water.
Sulphur bacteria are important for their role in the global sulfur cycle.
Certain sulphur bacteria can convert hydrogen sulfide into sulfur in natural environments.
Scientists discovered a new species of sulphur bacteria thriving in the hot springs.
