cayman

noun
a Central/South American reptile like an alligator
7.3 6.8
Pronunciation: /ˈkeɪ.mən/ (AmE), /ˈkeɪ.mən/ (BrE)
A semiaquatic reptile of Central and South America, closely related to the alligator but distinguished by a more heavily armored belly.

Examples

A cayman reptile
Researchers observed several caymans basking on the muddy banks in the midday sun.
The cayman slipped quietly into the murky water as we approached the riverbank.
Unlike the alligator, the cayman has a distinctly more armored belly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alligator - a large semiaquatic reptile similar to a cayman, but native to the Americas and China
  • synonym: crocodile - a large aquatic reptile with a long snout and powerful jaws, broader category than cayman
