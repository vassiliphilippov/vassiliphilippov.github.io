narrowband

verb
to limit frequency range
8.1
Pronunciation: /ˈnær.oʊ.bænd/ (AmE), /ˈnæ.rəʊ.bænd/ (BrE)
to make or convert a signal, channel, or system so that it operates within a small frequency range.

Examples

To narrowband a system
The company plans to narrowband its communication systems to meet new regulations.
Engineers will narrowband the signal to reduce interference from other frequencies.
To improve efficiency, the team decided to narrowband the radio channels used for emergency services.

Related Terms

  • synonym: restrict - to limit or confine within boundaries
  • antonym: widen - to make broader or increase the range
