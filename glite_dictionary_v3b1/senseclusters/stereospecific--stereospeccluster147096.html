stereospecific

adjective
specific to one isomer
9.5
Pronunciation: /ste.riː.oʊ.spə.ˈsɪf.ɪk/ (AmE), /ste.riː.əʊ.spə.ˈsɪf.ɪk/ (BrE)
describing a molecule, enzyme, or process that interacts only with a particular stereoisomer or spatial arrangement of atoms

Examples

Stereospecific enzyme action
The enzyme is stereospecific and will only react with the left-handed form of the molecule.
A stereospecific reaction produces only one possible stereoisomer as a product.
Because the receptor is stereospecific, only the (R)-enantiomer produces a biological response.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: stereoselective - favoring one stereoisomer over another but not exclusively
