clairvoyance

noun
supposed ability to perceive things beyond normal senses
Pronunciation: / klɛrˈvɔɪəns / (AmE), / klɛˈvɔɪəns / (BrE)
the supposed power to gain information about an object, person, or event through extrasensory perception

Examples

Clairvoyance power
His stories about clairvoyance fascinated those who believed in supernatural powers.
Skeptics dismissed the idea of clairvoyance as mere trickery.
The psychic claimed her clairvoyance enabled her to locate missing objects.

Related Terms

  • antonym: normal perception - ordinary ability to sense things through sight, hearing, taste, touch, or smell
  • synonym: precognition - the supposed ability to perceive future events before they happen
  • synonym: telepathy - the supposed communication of thoughts or ideas by means other than the known senses
