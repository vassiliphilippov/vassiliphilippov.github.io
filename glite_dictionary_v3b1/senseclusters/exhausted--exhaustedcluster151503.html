exhausted

adjective
completely used up or worn out
Pronunciation: / ɪɡˈzɔstɪd / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɔːstɪd / (BrE)
extremely tired or completely used up (of energy, strength, or resources)

Examples

Feeling exhausted after work
After hiking for eight hours, everyone felt completely exhausted and ready for bed.
Decades of farming left the land exhausted and in need of restoration.
The exhausted supplies forced the researchers to abandon their campsite early.
After running the marathon, she was completely exhausted and could barely stand.
By midnight, everyone at the party looked utterly exhausted from dancing all night.
He felt exhausted after working a twelve-hour shift at the hospital.
After running the marathon, she felt exhausted and could barely walk.
He looked exhausted after working overtime all week.
The children collapsed on the couch, completely exhausted from a day at the amusement park.

Related Terms

  • synonym: depleted - reduced in quantity or fullness
  • antonym: energetic - full of energy; vigorous
  • synonym: fatigued - physically or mentally exhausted by hard work or effort
  • antonym: refreshed - restored in strength or energy
  • degree lower: tired - in need of sleep or rest; weary
