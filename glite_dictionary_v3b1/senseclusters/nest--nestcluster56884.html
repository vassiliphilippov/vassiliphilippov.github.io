nest

verb
to build and occupy a nest
4.4
Pronunciation: / nɛst / (AmE), / nɛst / (BrE)
to build or occupy a nest, especially for laying eggs or raising young (of animals)

Examples

Turtles nest on beaches
Sea turtles return to the same beach to nest every season.
Several pairs of sparrows nest under the eaves each year.
The swallows have started to nest in our garage again.
Every spring, the robins nest in the old oak tree near our house.
The dolls are designed to nest inside one another for easy storage.
Some animals nest underground to protect their young from predators.
