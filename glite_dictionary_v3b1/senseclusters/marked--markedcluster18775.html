marked

adjective
having a distinguishing mark; noticeably characterized
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑrkt / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkt / (BrE)
clearly noticeable or easily recognized; having a distinguishing mark or feature

Examples

Marked difference
She entered the room with a marked look of concern on her face.
The animal had a marked stripe along its back that made it easy to identify.
The path to the summit was marked with red flags.
His resignation led to a marked drop in company morale.
The patient spoke with a marked improvement in confidence after the therapy sessions.
There was a marked difference between the two proposals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: obvious - easily perceived or understood; clear and apparent
  • synonym: pronounced - very noticeable or strongly marked
  • antonym: subtle - not obvious or easily noticeable
  • antonym: unmarked - not having any distinguishing marks
