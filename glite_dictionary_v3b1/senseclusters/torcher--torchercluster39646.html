torcher

noun
torch bearer
Pronunciation: / ˈtɔrtʃər / (AmE), / ˈtɔːtʃə / (BrE)
a person who carries a torch or other portable light, especially during ceremonies or processions

Examples

Ceremony torcher
During the parade, the torcher led the group with a brightly burning flame.
Each torcher wore a special robe as they walked through the ancient streets at night.
The ceremony began with a torcher lighting the main bonfire in the town square.
