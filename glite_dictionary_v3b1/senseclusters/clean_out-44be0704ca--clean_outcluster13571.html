clean out

verb
remove or eject entirely from a place
Pronunciation: / klin aʊt / (AmE), / kliːn aʊt / (BrE)
to remove or eject someone or something completely from a place, especially as a result of being unwanted

Examples

To clean out staff
A new manager was hired to clean out the department of underperforming staff.
After the scandal, the company cleaned out several corrupt executives.
The landlord threatened to clean out tenants who didn't pay their rent on time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: evict - to force someone to leave a place, especially legally
  • synonym: expel - to officially force someone to leave a place or organization
