clean out

verb
remove or eject entirely from a place
3.6 0.43/m
Pronunciation: / klin a ät / (AmE), / kliÀên a ät / (BrE)
to remove or eject someone or something completely from a place, especially as a result of being unwanted

Examples

To clean out staff
A new manager was hired to clean out the department of underperforming staff.
After the scandal, the company cleaned out several corrupt executives.
The landlord threatened to clean out tenants who didn't pay their rent on time.

Synonyms

  • clear out
    3.1 2.11/m
    clear out means emptying a place completely, often taking or removing all things, while clean out can also mean ejecting unwanted people.
  • throw out
    3.0 5.66/m
    throw out is to remove someone or something from a place or job by decision, while clean out stresses total emptying or removal.
  • eject
    4.2 1.47/m
    eject forces someone or something out, often by authority or force, while clean out can mean removing everything unwanted.
  • force out
    4.2 0.41/m
    force out means make someone leave by pressure or force, while clean out can mean emptying a place completely.
  • kick out
    3.0 11/m
    kick out is an informal way to force someone to leave a group or place, while clean out sounds more neutral.
  • flush out
    4.5 1.29/m
    flush out is to drive something hidden out, often by water or tactics, while clean out means remove completely from a place.
  • chuck out
    5.0 0.01/m
    chuck out is informal British English for throwing someone or something away, while clean out is more general and can be neutral.
  • toss out
    2.7 1.52/m
    toss out means throw away or discard items casually, while clean out often implies removing everything or ejecting unwanted people.

Related Terms

  • synonym: evict - to force someone to leave a place, especially legally
  • synonym: expel - to officially force someone to leave a place or organization
