obstruction

noun

something that physically blocks a passage, path, or flow, making movement or progress difficult or impossible

Examples

An obstruction in traffic
A serious obstruction in the artery requires immediate medical attention.
After the accident, the doctors treated the patient for a bowel obstruction.
The drainpipe suffered from an obstruction caused by accumulated leaves and debris.
Doctors removed an obstruction from the patient's airway to help him breathe normally again.
The fallen tree acted as an obstruction on the road, causing traffic to back up for miles.
They complained about the constant obstruction of the view caused by the new construction.

Synonyms

  • blockage
    4.1 2.24/m
    a blockage fills a pipe or tube and stops flow, while an obstruction means any physical thing that blocks movement.
  • barrier
    2.8 12/m
    a barrier is a built structure that stops access or keeps things apart, while an obstruction is any physical blockage in a passage.
  • roadblock
    3.9 2.25/m
    a roadblock is an obstacle placed to stop or slow progress on a road or goal, while an obstruction is any physical blocker.
  • bottleneck
    4.6 2.28/m
    a bottleneck is a narrow point that slows or limits movement in a process or road, while an obstruction fully blocks passage.
  • obstacle
    3.4 26/m
    an obstacle can be a thing, situation, or problem that makes progress hard, while an obstruction always means a physical blockage.
