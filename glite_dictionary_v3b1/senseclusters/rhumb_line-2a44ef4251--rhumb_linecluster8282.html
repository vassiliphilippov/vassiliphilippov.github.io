rhumb line

noun
line crossing all meridians at the same angle
9.9 8.8
Pronunciation: /rʌm laɪn/ /; / /rʌmb laɪn/ (AmE), /rʌm laɪn/ /; / /rʌmb laɪn/ (BrE)
a line on the surface of a sphere, such as the earth, crossing all meridians at the same angle; the path taken by a vessel or aircraft maintaining a constant compass direction.

Examples

A rhumb line path
Pilots sometimes choose a rhumb line for simplicity, even though it is not the shortest path.
The ship plotted its course along a rhumb line to maintain a steady compass bearing.
Unlike great circle routes, a rhumb line appears as a straight line on a Mercator projection map.

Related Terms

  • synonym: loxodrome - a curve crossing all meridians at a constant angle; another term for rhumb line
