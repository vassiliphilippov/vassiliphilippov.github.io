ling ko

noun
water chestnut with two-pronged fruit
9.9
Pronunciation: / lɪŋ koʊ / (AmE), / lɪŋ kəʊ / (BrE)
A type of water chestnut whose spiny fruit has two prongs rather than four.

Examples

The ling ko fruit
Botanists are studying the unique two-pronged fruit of the ling ko compared to other water chestnuts.
In traditional dishes, ling ko adds a crisp texture and a mild, sweet flavor.
The vendor sold fresh ling ko at the market, neatly arranged in baskets.
