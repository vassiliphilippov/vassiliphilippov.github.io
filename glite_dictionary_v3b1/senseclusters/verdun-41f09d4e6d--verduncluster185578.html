Verdun

proper noun
World War I battle in 1916
Pronunciation: / vɛrˈdʌn / (AmE), / vɛəˈdʌn / (BrE)
a major battle fought in 1916 during World War I between French and German forces, known for its length and heavy casualties

Examples

Verdun battle 1916
Historians often study Verdun to understand the horrors of trench warfare.
Many soldiers lost their lives in the trenches around Verdun.
The Battle of Verdun was one of the longest and bloodiest battles of World War I.

  • synonym: Stalingrad - site of another major battle, but in World War II
