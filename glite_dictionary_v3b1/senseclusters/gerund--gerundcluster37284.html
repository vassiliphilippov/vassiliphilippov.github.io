gerund

noun
noun form derived from a verb
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛrənd / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛrənd / (BrE)
A noun made from a verb, typically by adding '-ing' in English, and used as the subject or object of a verb.

Examples

A gerund form
Running is good exercise for your health.
She enjoys swimming in the lake every summer.
The teacher explained how to use a gerund as a subject in a sentence.

  • synonym: participle - a verb form used as an adjective or to form verb tenses
