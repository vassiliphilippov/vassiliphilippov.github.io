dull

adjective
not sharp or intense (sensation)
8.0 1.5
Pronunciation: /dʌl/ (AmE), /dʌl/ (BrE)
not intense, sharp, or clear (used for pain, sensations, or sounds)

Examples

Dull pain
After running, he felt a dull throbbing in his knee.
She described the sensation as a dull ache rather than a sharp pain.
The patient reported a dull pain in his lower back for several days.
The dull thud from the other room made it hard to tell what had fallen.
The sound of their voices was dull and hard to understand through the thick wall.
We listened to the dull rumble of thunder in the distance.
A dull thud came from behind the closed door.
He struck the ground, and a dull noise echoed through the tunnel.
The rain beat with a dull sound on the window panes.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: mild - not severe or strong in degree
  • synonym: muffled - (of a sound) not loud because of being obstructed or softened
  • synonym: muted - (of a sound) not as loud or clear; softened
  • antonym: resonant - (of a sound) deep, clear, and continuing to sound or ring
  • antonym: sharp - (of pain) intense, acute, strongly felt
