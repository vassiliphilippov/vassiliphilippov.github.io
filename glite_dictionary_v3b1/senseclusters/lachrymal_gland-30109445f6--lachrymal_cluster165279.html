lachrymal gland

noun
gland that produces tears in the eye
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈlækrəməl ˌɡlænd / (AmE), / ˈlakrɪməl ˌɡland / (BrE)
a gland located in the upper outer region of each eye that produces tears to lubricate and protect the eye

Examples

lachrymal gland produces tears
Doctors examined her lachrymal gland when she complained of persistent dry eyes.
Inflammation of the lachrymal gland can lead to swelling and discomfort.
The lachrymal gland is essential for maintaining healthy eyes by producing tears.

  • synonym: tear gland - gland that secretes tears for the eye
