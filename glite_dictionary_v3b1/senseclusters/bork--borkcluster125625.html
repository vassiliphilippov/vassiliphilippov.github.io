bork

verb
damage or break (informal)
Pronunciation: / bɔrk / (AmE), / bɔːk / (BrE)
(informal, slang) To damage or break something, especially a piece of equipment or a computer system, often accidentally or through ineptitude.

Examples

To bork a device
Be careful not to bork your phone by rooting it without proper instructions.
If you install the wrong driver, you might bork your operating system.
The update completely borked the app, making it unusable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: break - to separate into pieces as a result of a blow, shock, or strain
  • antonym: fix - to repair or restore to a working condition
