Prunus dulcis

noun
almond tree species
Pronunciation: / ˈpruː.nəs ˈdʌl.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈpruː.nəs ˈdʌl.sɪs / (BrE)
A small deciduous tree native to Asia and North Africa, cultivated for its edible nuts (almonds) and pink blossoms.

Examples

Prunus dulcis tree
Gardeners admire the delicate pink blossoms of Prunus dulcis in early April.
Many commercial almonds are harvested from Prunus dulcis grown in Mediterranean climates.
The orchards in California are filled with blooming Prunus dulcis each spring.
