indiscriminate

adjective
without careful selection or distinction
6.4 5.7
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndɪˈskrɪmɪnət / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈskrɪmɪnət / (BrE)
done without careful choice, selection, or consideration; not showing careful judgment or distinction

Examples

Indiscriminate criticism
His criticism was indiscriminate, targeting everyone present without exception.
The indiscriminate use of pesticides has endangered many species of birds.
The bomb caused indiscriminate destruction throughout the neighborhood.
Her criticism was harsh and indiscriminate, directed at everyone present.
The indiscriminate use of pesticides can harm the environment.
They carried out an indiscriminate attack that affected both soldiers and civilians.
