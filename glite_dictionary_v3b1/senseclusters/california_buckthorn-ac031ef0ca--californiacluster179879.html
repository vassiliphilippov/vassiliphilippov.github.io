California buckthorn

noun
evergreen shrub of the western U.S.
Pronunciation: / kælɪˈfɔrnjə ˈbʌkˌθɔrn / (AmE), / kælɪˈfɔːniə ˈbʌkˌθɔːn / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub native to the western United States, bearing small red or black fruits, also known as coffeeberry

Examples

The California buckthorn shrub
Many gardeners use California buckthorn as a native landscaping plant because it is drought-tolerant.
The California buckthorn produces clusters of small, dark berries in the summer.
Wildlife such as birds are attracted to the fruit of the California buckthorn.
