evasion

noun
indirect way of avoiding the truth
Pronunciation: / ɪˈveɪʒən / (AmE), / ɪˈveɪʒ(ə)n / (BrE)
a statement that avoids directly addressing an unpleasant truth, often by being indirect or misleading without being literally false

Examples

A clever evasion
He was caught using an evasion to avoid admitting his mistake.
Her answer was an evasion rather than a clear response to the difficult question.
When pressed for details, the politician's evasion frustrated the reporters.

Related Terms

  • antonym: directness - the quality of being clear and straightforward
  • synonym: prevarication - the act of speaking or acting in an evasive way
