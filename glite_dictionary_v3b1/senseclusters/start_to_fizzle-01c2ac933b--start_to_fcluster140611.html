start to fizzle

verb
begin to lose energy or excitement
Pronunciation: / stɑrt tə ˈfɪzəl / (AmE), / stɑːt tə ˈfɪzl̩ / (BrE)
to begin to lose strength, enthusiasm, or excitement gradually until it fades away

Examples

To start to fizzle out
After a few days, their motivation to exercise started to fizzle as routines became repetitive.
Her interest in learning Spanish started to fizzle after she missed several classes.
The party was lively at first, but the fun started to fizzle as people left early.
