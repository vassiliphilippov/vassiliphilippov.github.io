unsubstantialize

verb
make immaterial or incorporeal
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌʌn.səbˈstæn.ʃi.əˌlaɪz /; / ˌʌnsəbˈstænʃəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌʌn.səbˈstæn.ʃɪə.laɪz /; / ˌʌnsəbˈstænʃəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make (something) immaterial or incorporeal; to deprive of substance or physical reality

Examples

To unsubstantialize a story
Legends often unsubstantialize historical figures, turning them into myths.
She worried that digital art might unsubstantialize the experience of painting on canvas.
The philosopher argued that abstract thought can unsubstantialize the physical world.
Over time, her memories seemed to unsubstantialise, fading into vague impressions.
The philosopher attempted to unsubstantialise the concept of the soul in his writings.
The story seeks to unsubstantialise the boundaries between reality and dreams.

Related Terms

  • synonym: immaterialize - cause to become immaterial; render incorporeal
  • antonym: materialize - cause to become material or corporeal
  • antonym: substantialize - to make substantial or give substance to
