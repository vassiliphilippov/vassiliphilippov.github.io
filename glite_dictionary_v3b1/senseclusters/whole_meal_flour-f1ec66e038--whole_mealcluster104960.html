whole meal flour

noun
flour from the entire wheat grain
Pronunciation: / ˈhoʊl miːl ˌflɔr / (AmE), / ˈhəʊl miːl ˌflɔː / (BrE)
Flour made by grinding the entire wheat grain, including the bran; used chiefly in British English.

Examples

Whole meal flour baking
Health-conscious shoppers often choose whole meal flour as a source of extra fiber.
She bakes her bread with whole meal flour for a richer texture and greater nutrition.
The recipe calls for both plain flour and whole meal flour to balance flavor and density.

Related Terms

  • synonym: whole wheat flour - flour made from the whole wheat grain, primarily American usage
