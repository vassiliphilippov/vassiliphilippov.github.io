inveigle

verb
persuade by flattery or trickery
7.9 7.6
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈveɪ.gəl/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈveɪ.gəl/ (BrE)
to persuade or influence someone to do something by using clever, gentle, or sneaky means, especially by flattering or coaxing them

Examples

To inveigle a friend
He is skilled at inveigling people with his charming and persuasive manner.
She tried to inveigle her friend into attending the party despite her hesitation.
The salesman managed to inveigle the couple into buying the extended warranty.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cajole - to persuade by flattery or promises
  • synonym: coax - to persuade gently and persistently
