erythroblastosis fetalis

noun
anemia in newborns due to Rh incompatibility
Pronunciation: / əˌrɪθroʊblæˈstoʊsɪs fiˈteɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛrɪθrəʊblæˈstəʊsɪs fɪˈtælɪs / (BrE)
A severe form of anemia in newborn babies caused by Rh incompatibility between the mother and fetus, in which the mother's immune system attacks the baby's red blood cells.

Examples

erythroblastosis fetalis disease
Advances in prenatal care have greatly reduced the incidence of erythroblastosis fetalis over recent decades.
Babies affected by erythroblastosis fetalis often need immediate medical treatment after birth.
The doctor explained that erythroblastosis fetalis can be prevented with early intervention during pregnancy.
