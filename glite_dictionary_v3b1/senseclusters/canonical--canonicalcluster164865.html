canonical

adjective
recognized as part of biblical canon
Pronunciation: / kəˈnɑːnɪkəl / (AmE), / kəˈnɒnɪkəl / (BrE)
officially accepted or recognized as genuine and authoritative, especially in religion or by church law

Examples

Canonical gospels
Only canonical gospels are read in most Christian churches during services.
Scholars debate whether certain ancient writings should be deemed canonical or apocryphal.
The Book of Daniel is considered a canonical text in the Old Testament.
Certain marriage rites are not considered canonical within the Roman Catholic Church.
He studied the canonical texts to better understand the church's legal structure.
The bishop ensured that all decisions were strictly canonical and adhered to church law.

Related Terms

  • antonym: apocryphal - not included in the accepted biblical canon
  • synonym: ecclesiastical - relating to the Christian Church or its clergy
