jitter

noun
small rapid variations in a signal or waveform
9.7 6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒɪt.ər/ (AmE), /ˈdʒɪt.ə/ (BrE)
small, rapid, irregular changes or movements, especially in signals or systems

Examples

Signal jitter
Audio engineers often try to minimize jitter to preserve sound quality in digital recordings.
Network jitter can cause delays and interruptions during video conferences.
Signal jitter in electronic devices may result from unstable power supplies.
Engineers monitor jitter to ensure stable data transmission in networks.
Seismic sensors detected a slight jitter before the main tremor occurred.
The video feed suffered from jitter, causing the image to shake slightly.
