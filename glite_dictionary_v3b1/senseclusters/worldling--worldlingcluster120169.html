worldling

noun
person focused on worldly matters
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈwɝːld.lɪŋ /; / ˈwɜrldlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɜːld.lɪŋ /; / ˈwɜːldlɪŋ / (BrE)
a person who is mainly interested in material or worldly things, not spiritual values

Examples

A worldling person
He was regarded as a mere worldling by the ascetic monks.
The worldling valued wealth above all spiritual matters.
Unlike the devoted priest, she was a practical worldling at heart.
He saw himself as different from the worldlings who chased after wealth and status.
The philosopher warned against becoming a mere worldling lost in pursuit of pleasure.
Unlike the worldling, she valued mindfulness and detachment from material desires.

Related Terms

  • synonym: materialist - someone concerned mainly with possessions or material gain
  • antonym: spiritualist - someone concerned primarily with spiritual matters
