admonitor

noun
church adviser
9.9
Pronunciation: /əd.ˈmɑːn.ɪ.tər/ /; / /ˈæd.mɑːn.ɪ.tər/ (AmE), /əd.ˈmɒn.ɪ.tə/ /; / /ˈæd.mɒn.ɪ.tə/ (BrE)
an official adviser in a church or religious organization whose role is to warn, counsel, or reprimand clergy on moral or disciplinary issues

Examples

Church admonitor
The bishop appointed an admonitor to oversee the conduct of the clergy.
Before making his decision, the priest sought advice from the admonitor of the diocese.
An admonitor may issue warnings to clergy who violate church rules.
