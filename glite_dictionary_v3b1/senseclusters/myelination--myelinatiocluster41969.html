myelination

noun
nerve fiber insulation
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪəˌlɪˈneɪʃən /; / ˌmaɪə.lɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌmaɪəˌlɪˈneɪʃən /; / ˌmaɪ.ə.lɪˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process or state of nerve fibers being covered with a protective myelin sheath, which increases the speed of nerve signal transmission

Examples

Proper myelination
Proper myelination is essential for healthy brain development in children.
Diseases like multiple sclerosis can disrupt myelination and affect nerve function.
The process of myelination continues into early adulthood in some parts of the brain.
Proper myelination is essential for the rapid transmission of electrical signals in the nervous system.
Delayed myelination can affect a child's motor and cognitive development.
Researchers study myelination to better understand diseases like multiple sclerosis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: demyelination - the loss or destruction of the myelin sheath around nerve fibers
