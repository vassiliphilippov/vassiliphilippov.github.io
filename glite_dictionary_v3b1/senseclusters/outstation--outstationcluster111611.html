outstation

noun
remote or isolated station/settlement
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtˌsteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌsteɪʃən / (BrE)
A small station or settlement located in a remote or sparsely populated area, often serving administrative or operational purposes.

Examples

A remote outstation
She volunteered to teach at an outstation in the far north, many hours from the nearest town.
The government built an outstation to support research in the remote desert region.
Workers at the outstation only receive supplies once a month due to its isolation.
