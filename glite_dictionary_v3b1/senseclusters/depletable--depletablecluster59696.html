depletable

adjective
able to be used up or exhausted
4.8
Pronunciation: / dɪˈplitəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈpliːtəbəl / (BrE)
Able to be used up or exhausted; capable of being depleted.

Examples

Depletable natural resources
Care must be taken with depletable supplies to ensure they last as long as possible.
Oil is considered a depletable resource because it cannot be replaced once used.
The advantage of solar power is that it is not depletable, unlike fossil fuels.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inexhaustible - impossible to use up completely
  • synonym: nonrenewable - not able to be replenished or restored
