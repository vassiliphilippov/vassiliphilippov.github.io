ornamentality

noun
display traits
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌɔrnəˌmɛnˈtæləti / (AmE), / ˌɔːnəˌmɛnˈtælɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of having features or traits used mainly for display, especially to attract mates.

Examples

Peacock ornamentality
The peacock's tail is a classic example of ornamentality in the animal kingdom.
Scientists study ornamentality to understand how certain traits evolve through sexual selection.
The bright colors of the fish are a sign of ornamentality used to attract mates.

  • antonym: plainness - the quality of lacking decoration or adornment
