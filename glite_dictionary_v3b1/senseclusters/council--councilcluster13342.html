council

noun
assembly of church leaders to decide doctrine
7.3 2.58/m

an assembly of theologians, bishops, and other representatives of various Christian churches or dioceses, convened to regulate matters of doctrine or discipline

Examples

Church council assembly
Roman Catholic doctrine was clarified at the council of Trent.
The Nicene council was convened to address theological disputes in early Christianity.
The church historian wrote extensively about the outcomes of each major council.

Synonyms

  • synod
    9.8 1.91/m
    A synod is a meeting of church leaders to discuss church matters, while a council is any formal assembly that sets doctrine or discipline.
  • ecumenical council
    9.1 0.15/m
    An ecumenical council brings bishops from across the Christian world to settle major doctrine, while a council can be any church assembly.
  • consistory
    9.9 0.06/m
    A consistory is an official church court or governing body handling discipline and administration, while a council is a wider assembly to decide doctrine.
