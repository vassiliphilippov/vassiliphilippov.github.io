revelator

noun
person who reveals
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛvələˌtɔr / (AmE), / ˈrɛvələˌtɔː / (BrE)
a person who reveals or makes something known, especially something previously hidden or secret

Examples

A revelator of secrets
The whistleblower acted as a revelator by bringing the hidden facts to light.
In the story, the mysterious revelator uncovers secrets that change everything.
Historians often rely on a single revelator to learn the truth about past events.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concealer - a person who hides or keeps something secret
  • synonym: informer - a person who gives information, especially secretly
  • synonym: revealer - a person who makes something known
