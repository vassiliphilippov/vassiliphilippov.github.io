echoic

adjective
imitating a natural sound (of words)
2.9
Pronunciation: / ɛˈkoʊɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈkəʊɪk / (BrE)
(of a word or speech sound) formed by or resembling the imitation of a natural sound; onomatopoeic.

Examples

Echoic animal names
Many animal names in children’s books are deliberately echoic to help with recognition.
The linguist gave several echoic examples, such as 'hiss' and 'clang'.
The word 'buzz' is an echoic term that mimics the sound it represents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: onomatopoeic - (of a word) formed by imitating a sound associated with what is named
