chancellor

noun
honorary head of a university
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃæn.sə.lɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːn.səl.ə / (BrE)
the honorary or titular head of a university, often serving in a ceremonial capacity

Examples

University chancellor
Although the chancellor holds an important title, the vice-chancellor manages the university's day-to-day operations.
She was appointed chancellor of the university in recognition of her contributions to education.
The university's chancellor presided over the graduation ceremony with great dignity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: vice-chancellor - the chief executive officer of a university, especially in British English
