denaturalization

noun
loss of citizenship
9.4
Pronunciation: / diˌnætʃərələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌnatʃərələˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of taking away a person's citizenship or nationality, usually by legal process

Examples

Court denaturalization
The court ordered the denaturalization of the individual after evidence of fraud was discovered.
Laws regarding denaturalization vary from country to country and are often controversial.
He faced denaturalization because he had lied on his citizenship application.

Related Terms

  • synonym: expatriation - the act of leaving one's native country to live elsewhere, often losing citizenship
  • antonym: naturalization - the process of becoming a citizen of a country
