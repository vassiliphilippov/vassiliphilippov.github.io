demijohn

noun
large glass bottle for liquids, often in wicker
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛm.iˌdʒɑn / (AmE), / ˈdɛ.mɪ.dʒɒn / (BrE)
a large narrow-necked bottle, often with handles and enclosed in wickerwork, used for storing liquids such as wine or chemicals

Examples

A demijohn bottle
In the corner of the cellar stood an old wicker-covered demijohn.
She bought a demijohn to store extra virgin olive oil from the market.
The winemaker poured the young red wine into a demijohn for fermentation.
