hollow

adjective
echoing, empty-sounding (of a sound)
2.9 3.5
Pronunciation: /ˈhɑː.loʊ/ (AmE), /ˈhɒ.ləʊ/ (BrE)
(of a sound) echoing as if made in an empty space; resonant and empty-sounding.

Examples

Hollow sound
His footsteps echoed with a distinctly hollow quality in the empty gymnasium.
The cave produced a deep, hollow noise each time the wind blew in.
The deserted hallway had a hollow sound when she called out.

Related Terms

  • synonym: echoing - (of a sound) continuing to reverberate after the source has stopped
