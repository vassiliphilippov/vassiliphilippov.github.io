siderography

noun
steel plate engraving
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪdəˈrɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌsɪdəˈrɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
a method of engraving and printing using steel plates, especially for making secure documents like banknotes, or a print made by this method

Examples

Siderography printing method
The invention of siderography greatly improved the security of printed banknotes in the 19th century.
Many historical documents were produced using siderography to prevent counterfeiting.
Modern currency design still draws on techniques developed through siderography.
The rare book featured beautiful siderography on its title page.
Advances in siderography made it harder to counterfeit banknotes in the 19th century.
He studied siderography to learn how steel plates were used in historical printing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: engraving - the art or process of cutting designs into a hard surface
  • synonym: intaglio - a printing technique where the image is incised into a surface
  • antonym: lithography - a printing process using a flat stone or metal plate
