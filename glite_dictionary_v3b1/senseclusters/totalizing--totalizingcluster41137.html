totalizing

adjective
all-encompassing
9.3
Pronunciation: /ˈtoʊ.təl.aɪ.zɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈtəʊ.təl.aɪ.zɪŋ/ (BrE)
attempting to include, explain, or control everything as a whole, often overlooking differences or details

Examples

Totalizing theory
The philosopher criticized the totalizing approach for ignoring individual experiences.
Some political ideologies are seen as totalizing because they try to explain every aspect of society.
A totalizing narrative can make it difficult to recognize important differences between groups.
The regime's totalizing policies left no room for personal privacy or dissent.
Critics warned that the new law had a totalizing effect on society, stifling free expression.
A totalizing ideology can shape every aspect of daily life, from education to personal beliefs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authoritarian - favoring strict obedience to authority at the expense of personal freedom
  • synonym: comprehensive - including all or nearly all elements or aspects
  • antonym: liberal - favoring individual freedom and limited government control
  • antonym: partial - not complete; only a part of the whole
  • synonym: totalitarian - exercising absolute control over all aspects of life
