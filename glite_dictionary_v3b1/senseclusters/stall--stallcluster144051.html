stall

verb
cause an aircraft to lose lift
7.4 6.1
Pronunciation: /stɔːl/ /; / /stɑːl/ (AmE), /stɔːl/ (BrE)
(of an aircraft) to lose lift and begin to fall due to insufficient speed or a disruption in airflow

Examples

To stall a plane
If you stall the plane during takeoff, you risk losing control and crashing.
She learned how to recover quickly if she ever stalled the glider in flight.
The inexperienced pilot inadvertently stalled the aircraft while attempting a steep climb.
If you pull the nose up too sharply, the plane may stall and lose altitude quickly.
Sudden wind changes can cause a small plane to stall unexpectedly in midair.
The pilot regained control of the aircraft after it began to stall during takeoff.
