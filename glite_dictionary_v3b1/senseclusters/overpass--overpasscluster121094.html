overpass

verb
to cross over
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈpæs / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈpɑːs / (BrE)
to go over or cross something by passing above it, especially using a bridge or elevated structure

Examples

To overpass the tracks
The new bridge allows cars to overpass the busy railway tracks safely.
During the hike, we overpassed several streams using wooden planks.
A walkway was built so pedestrians could overpass the highway without danger.
The new highway will overpass the river to connect the two towns.
Engineers designed the road to overpass the existing railway line safely.
The train tracks overpass the busy intersection to prevent traffic delays.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bypass - to go around or avoid something
  • synonym: cross - to go from one side to the other
  • antonym: underpass - to go beneath something, especially a road or bridge
