fall in

verb
collapse inward or break down
1.4
Pronunciation: / fɔl ɪn / (AmE), / fɔːl ɪn / (BrE)
(of a structure or organization) to collapse inward or break down, either physically or metaphorically.

Examples

The roof fell in
Several companies in the industry may fall in if the economic crisis continues.
The ceiling started to fall in after the heavy rainstorm.
We had to evacuate because the roof threatened to fall in at any moment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collapse - to fall down or give way suddenly
  • synonym: give way - to yield or break under pressure
  • antonym: hold up - to remain standing or intact
