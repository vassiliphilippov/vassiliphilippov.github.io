euhemerism

noun
myths from history
Pronunciation: / juˈhiːməˌrɪzəm / (AmE), / juːˈhiːmərɪzəm / (BrE)
the theory that myths originate from real historical people or events that were later exaggerated or deified

Examples

Euhemerism theory
The historian used euhemerism to explain the origins of ancient Greek gods.
Some researchers argue that euhemerism helps us understand how real people became legendary figures.
Through euhemerism, myths are seen as distorted accounts of actual historical events.

Related Terms

  • synonym: legend - a traditional story sometimes regarded as historical but unauthenticated
  • synonym: mythology - the study or collection of myths
