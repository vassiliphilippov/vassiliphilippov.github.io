wild leek

noun
a coarse perennial plant; large bulb, purple-tinged flowers
6.2
Pronunciation: / waɪld lik / (AmE), / waɪld liːk / (BrE)
a coarse perennial plant with a large bulb and tall stalk bearing greenish purple-tinged flowers, native to the Old World but widely naturalized elsewhere

Examples

A wild leek patch
A patch of wild leeks was discovered growing near the edge of the forest.
Researchers studied how the wild leek has adapted to different environments across Europe.
The wild leek blooms in late spring and attracts many pollinators in the meadow.
