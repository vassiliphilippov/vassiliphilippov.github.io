gust

verb
to blow in bursts
3.2
Pronunciation: /gʌst/ (AmE), /gʌst/ (BrE)
to blow or move in strong, sudden bursts, especially of wind

Examples

Winds gust suddenly
The wind began to gust suddenly, rattling the windows of the old house.
Leaves and dust gusted down the empty street as the storm approached.
As we reached the hilltop, the cold air started to gust around us.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blow - to move air, especially wind, in a current
  • antonym: calm - to be still or without wind
  • synonym: howl - to blow with a loud, strong sound, as wind
