earth

verb
connect electrically to the ground
Pronunciation: / ɜrθ / (AmE), / ɜːθ / (BrE)
to connect an electrical device or circuit to the ground, especially as a safety measure

Examples

To earth a device
An electrician must always earth any exposed metal parts for safety.
Failure to earth the circuit can result in a dangerous electric shock.
The technician earthed the equipment to prevent static buildup during installation.
