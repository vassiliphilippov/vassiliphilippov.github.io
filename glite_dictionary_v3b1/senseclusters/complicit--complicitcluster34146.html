complicit

adjective
involved in wrongdoing with others
6.4
Pronunciation: / kəmˈplɪs.ɪt / (AmE), / kəmˈplɪs.ɪt / (BrE)
involved with others in an activity that is unlawful or morally wrong

Examples

Be complicit in crime
By remaining silent, she became complicit in the cover-up.
He was accused of being complicit in the company's illegal actions.
The executive was found complicit in the fraud scandal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: culpable - deserving blame or responsible for wrongdoing
  • antonym: innocent - not guilty of a crime or wrongdoing
