bound up

adjective
deeply devoted; emotionally attached
6.4
Pronunciation: / baʊnd ʌp / (AmE), / baʊnd ʌp / (BrE)
closely connected or deeply involved with someone or something

Examples

Bound up in work
He became bound up in the success of his students.
She is so bound up in her work that she rarely takes breaks.
Their lives are bound up with their family's traditions.
His personal happiness was bound up with the success of the project.
My identity is bound up with where I come from and how I was raised.
The history of the town is bound up with the development of the railway.

Related Terms

  • antonym: detached - not connected or associated
  • synonym: wrapped up - deeply absorbed or devoted to
