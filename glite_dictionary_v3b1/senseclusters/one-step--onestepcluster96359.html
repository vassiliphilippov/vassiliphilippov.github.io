one-step

noun
early ballroom dance, precursor to fox-trot
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈwʌn.stɛp / (AmE), / ˈwʌn.stɛp / (BrE)
an early ballroom dance that was a precursor to the fox-trot, typically characterized by a single step per beat in 2/4 time

Examples

A lively one-step
In the early 1900s, couples would dance the one-step at elegant social gatherings.
She learned both the one-step and the waltz at her grandmother's dance class.
The one-step was popular before the fox-trot became fashionable.
