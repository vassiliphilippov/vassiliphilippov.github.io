trust

noun
group of companies limiting competition
7.0 3.49/m
Pronunciation: / trÊŒst / (AmE), / trÊŒst / (BrE)
a group of companies or organizations joined together to control the production and distribution of goods or services, often to limit competition

Examples

A business trust
In the late 1800s, the oil industry was dominated by a trust that controlled prices and supply.
Many argued that the formation of the trust was harmful to smaller businesses.
The government broke up the trust to restore competition in the market.

Synonyms

  • corporate trust
    6.9 0.00/m
    a corporate trust brings several companies under shared control to limit competition and manage markets, while trust refers to such business consolidation.
  • combine
    7.1 0.15/m
    a combine is a group of independent firms that act together to control production and sales, while trust is a group formed to limit competition.
  • cartel
    7.9 9.43/m
    a cartel sets prices and marketing rules among members, while trust is a business grouping mainly aimed at controlling production and distribution.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cartel - an association of manufacturers or suppliers formed to maintain prices and restrict competition
  • antonym: competition - the act or process of competing; rivalry
  • degree higher: monopoly - exclusive control by one group over the means of producing or selling a product
