tyke

noun
informal term for a young child
8.2
Pronunciation: / taɪk / (AmE), / taɪk / (BrE)
an informal term for a young child, especially one who is lively or mischievous

Examples

Cheeky tyke
She always calls her grandson a cheeky tyke.
The little tyke ran through the garden, laughing at his own joke.
Those tykes made a mess in the living room again.
She scolded the tyke for tracking mud into the house.
The tyke couldn't wait to ride his new bicycle.
Their neighbor's little tyke always makes everyone laugh with his jokes.
