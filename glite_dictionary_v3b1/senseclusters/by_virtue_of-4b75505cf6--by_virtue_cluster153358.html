Synonyms

  • in virtue of
    7.5 0.18/m
    in virtue of is a formal phrase meaning because of a quality or fact, while by virtue of serves the same formal use.
  • because of
    1.0 261/m
    because of is everyday and direct when giving reasons, while by virtue of sounds more formal and often marks status or authority.
  • on account of
    1.7 3.66/m
    on account of is slightly more formal or old-fashioned and fits many contexts, while by virtue of leans toward official or legal tone.
  • due to
    1.0 106/m
    due to is often used after the verb be to state cause, while by virtue of is more formal and usually precedes nouns.
