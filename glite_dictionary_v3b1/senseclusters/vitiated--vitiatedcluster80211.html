vitiated

adjective
impaired or spoiled
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪʃieɪtɪd /; / ˈvɪʃiˌeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈvɪtɪeɪtɪd /; / ˈvɪʃɪeɪtɪd / (BrE)
spoiled, weakened, or made less effective in quality, character, or effectiveness

Examples

Ability vitiated by fatigue
His ability to make sound decisions was vitiated by fatigue.
The contract was declared invalid because it was vitiated by fraud.
The evidence was considered vitiated due to improper handling.
Centuries of neglect had vitiated the once-grand structure.
Her testimony was vitiated by contradictions and inconsistencies.
The report was dismissed because its findings were vitiated by bias.

  • synonym: spoiled - damaged or impaired in quality or value
  • synonym: tainted - affected with a trace of something bad or harmful
  • antonym: unimpaired - not damaged or weakened; whole and intact
  • antonym: unspoiled - not damaged or made impure
