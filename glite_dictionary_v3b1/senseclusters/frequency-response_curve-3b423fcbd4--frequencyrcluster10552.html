frequency-response curve

noun
graph of system response across frequencies
7.3 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààfrikw…ônsi r…™Ààsp…ëns k…úrv / (AmE), / ÀàfriÀêkw…ônsi r…™Ààsp…íns k…úÀêv / (BrE)
a graph showing how the amplitude or gain of a system varies with frequency, used especially in electronics to illustrate frequency response

Examples

A frequency-response curve graph
Before installing new speakers, we checked their frequency-response curve to ensure a flat response.
The frequency-response curve of the amplifier shows a sharp drop-off at high frequencies.
The engineer analyzed the frequency-response curve to adjust the equalizer settings for the best sound quality.

Synonyms

  • frequency-response characteristic
    7.8 0.00/m
    frequency-response characteristic is used in electronics to describe gain across frequencies, while frequency-response curve refers to the plotted graph showing that variation.
  • frequency response
    6.9 0.65/m
    frequency response measures a system's output across frequencies as a ratio, while frequency-response curve is the actual graph showing that variation.
