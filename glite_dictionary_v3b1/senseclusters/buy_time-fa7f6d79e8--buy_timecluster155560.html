buy time

verb
delay to gain advantage
Pronunciation: / baɪ taɪm / (AmE), / baɪ taɪm / (BrE)
to deliberately delay something in order to gain extra time to prepare, decide, or gain an advantage

Examples

To buy time in meeting
She asked unnecessary questions during the meeting to buy time for her team to finish the report.
The lawyer tried to buy time by requesting a last-minute extension.
The negotiators bought time by promising to consider the offer overnight.
He made small talk with the client to buy time until his manager arrived.
She asked irrelevant questions to buy time before giving her presentation.
The company tried to buy time by requesting a review of the contract.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hasten - to move or act quickly; opposite of delaying
  • synonym: stall - to delay taking action in order to gain time
