Lycopus virginicus

noun
an aromatic herb native to eastern U.S.
Pronunciation: / laɪˈkoʊpəs vɜːrˈdʒɪnɪkəs / (AmE), / lɪˈkəʊpəs vɜːˈdʒɪnɪkəs / (BrE)
A mildly narcotic and astringent aromatic herb native to the eastern United States, bearing small whitish flowers; also known as Virginia water horehound or bugleweed.

Examples

Lycopus virginicus herb
Lycopus virginicus grows in damp environments along streams and marshes in the eastern United States.
Herbalists sometimes use Lycopus virginicus to help manage mild thyroid conditions.
The leaves of Lycopus virginicus have a slightly minty aroma and are occasionally used in traditional remedies.
