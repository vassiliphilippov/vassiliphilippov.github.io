pour fuel on the fire

verb
make a bad situation worse
Pronunciation: / pɔr fjuːəl ɑn ðə ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / pɔː fjʊəl ɒn ðə ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
to say or do something that makes a bad situation even worse

Examples

To pour fuel on the fire
Her harsh criticism only poured fuel on the fire during their heated debate.
Instead of calming things down, his remarks poured fuel on the fire.
Spreading rumors will pour fuel on the fire and escalate the conflict.
