palmy

adjective
prosperous, flourishing (period)
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːlmi / (AmE), / ˈpɑːmi / (BrE)
(of a time or period) prosperous; flourishing or successful.

Examples

The palmy years
During the palmy years of the company, profits soared and the staff doubled in size.
Historians refer to the 1920s as a palmy period for the arts and culture.
She often reminisced about the palmy days of her youth, when everything seemed possible.

Related Terms

  • antonym: declining - growing weaker or less successful
  • synonym: flourishing - developing rapidly and successfully
  • synonym: prosperous - successful, wealthy, or flourishing
