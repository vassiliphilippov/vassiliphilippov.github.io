diaeresis

noun
two-dot diacritical mark over a vowel
8.6
Pronunciation: / daɪˈɛrəsɪs / (AmE), / daɪˈɪərəsɪs /; / daɪˈɪərɪsɪs / (BrE)
A diacritical mark (¨) placed over a vowel to show it is pronounced separately from an adjacent vowel, not as part of a diphthong.

Examples

A diaeresis mark
In some English words, a diaeresis is used, as in 'naïve' or 'coöperate'.
The diaeresis is not commonly used in modern English writing.
The name 'Chloë' uses a diaeresis to show both vowels are pronounced separately.
A dieresis distinguishes the pronunciation of vowels in words like 'coöperate'.
In older editions, the publisher used a dieresis to clarify the pronunciation of certain words.
The word 'naïve' is often written with a dieresis over the 'i'.

Related Terms

  • synonym: umlaut - two dots over a vowel indicating a vowel change, especially in German
