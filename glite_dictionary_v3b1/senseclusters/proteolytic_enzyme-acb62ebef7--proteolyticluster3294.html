proteolytic enzyme

noun
enzyme that digests proteins
Pronunciation: / ˌproʊtioʊˈlɪtɪk ˈɛnˌzaɪm / (AmE), / ˌprəʊtɪəˈlɪtɪk ˈɛn.zaɪm / (BrE)
an enzyme that breaks down proteins into smaller peptides and amino acids by catalyzing proteolysis

Examples

Proteolytic enzyme breaks proteins
Papain is a plant-derived proteolytic enzyme used in tenderizing meat.
Some bacteria secrete a proteolytic enzyme to invade host tissues.
The digestive system relies on proteolytic enzyme activity to break down food proteins.

Related Terms

  • synonym: protease - another term for an enzyme that breaks down proteins
