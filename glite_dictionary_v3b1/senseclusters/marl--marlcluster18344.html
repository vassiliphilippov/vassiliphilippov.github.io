marl

noun
earthy mineral deposit used as fertilizer
Pronunciation: / mɑrl / (AmE), / mɑːl / (BrE)
A loose, crumbly deposit of earth composed mainly of calcium carbonate or dolomite, often used to improve soils lacking lime.

Examples

A layer of marl
Analysis revealed a thick layer of marl beneath the riverbed.
In some regions, marl was historically quarried as a natural fertilizer.
The farmer added marl to the field to enrich the soil with calcium.
