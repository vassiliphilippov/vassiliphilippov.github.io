alienation

noun
legal transfer of property ownership
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪliəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌeɪliəˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
(Law) the voluntary and complete transfer of ownership and possession of real property from one party to another.

Examples

Alienation of property
Alienation of land often involves complex legal procedures.
A clause restricting alienation was included in the property agreement.
The alienation of the estate required the owner's written consent.
