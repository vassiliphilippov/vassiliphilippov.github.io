sink

noun
a natural depression in the ground (geology)
Pronunciation: / sɪŋk / (AmE), / sɪŋk / (BrE)
A depression in the ground, especially in limestone areas, that communicates with a subterranean passage and is formed by the solution or collapse of a cavern roof.

Examples

A deep sink
After heavy rainfall, a new sink appeared near the edge of the forest.
Geologists investigated the limestone sink to study underground water movement.
The sudden formation of a sink swallowed part of the farmer's field.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sinkhole - a natural depression or hole in the ground caused by collapse, especially in limestone regions
