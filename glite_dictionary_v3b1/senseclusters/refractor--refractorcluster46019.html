refractor

noun
lens-based telescope
7.0
Pronunciation: / rɪˈfræktər / (AmE), / rɪˈfræktə / (BrE)
a telescope that uses lenses to bend and focus light to form an image

Examples

Astronomical refractor
The astronomer adjusted the refractor to get a clearer view of the planet.
Early scientists relied on a refractor to make important discoveries about the solar system.
A large refractor can reveal details of the moon's surface.
The observatory houses a historic refractor that is over a century old.
She looked at the moon through a powerful refractor in her backyard.
Many amateur astronomers prefer a refractor for its clear and sharp images.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reflector - a telescope that uses mirrors to form an image
  • synonym: telescope - an instrument for viewing distant objects
