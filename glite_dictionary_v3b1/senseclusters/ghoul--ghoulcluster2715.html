ghoul

noun
person who steals corpses from graves
9.9 0.09/m
Pronunciation: / …°uÀêl / (AmE), / …°uÀêl / (BrE)
a person who exhumes bodies from graves, especially to sell them for anatomical dissection

Examples

A ghoul steals bodies
In the 19th century, a ghoul could earn a living by supplying medical schools with cadavers.
Rumors spread that a ghoul was stealing bodies from the churchyard at night.
The police caught the notorious ghoul as he was leaving a freshly disturbed grave.

Synonyms

  • body snatcher
    9.9 0.07/m
    body snatcher steals dead bodies to sell to medical schools or doctors, while ghoul sounds older and more shocking for the same act.
  • resurrectionist
    9.9 0.01/m
    resurrectionist is a historical term for someone who digs up dead bodies to sell, while ghoul sounds darker and more literary.
  • resurrection man
    9.9 0.01/m
    resurrection man steals corpses from graves to sell for dissection, while ghoul is a more negative, often literary name for that person.
  • graverobber
    9.4 0.04/m
    graverobber takes bodies or valuables from graves to steal goods, while ghoul refers to someone who digs up bodies to sell for dissection.
