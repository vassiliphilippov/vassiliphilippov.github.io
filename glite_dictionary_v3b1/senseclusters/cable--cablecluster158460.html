cable

noun
insulated wire for power or signals
2.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪ.bəl / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.bəl / (BrE)
a thick, strong wire or rope used for transmitting electricity, signals, or mechanical force

Examples

Electric cable
Fiber optic cable allows for high-speed internet connections.
Please check whether the cable connecting the monitor is plugged in properly.
The company installed a new cable to supply power to the building.
A steel cable was used to lift the heavy machinery into place.
The climbers secured the ropes to a metal cable anchored in the rock.
The old bridge was held together with thick cables running from tower to tower.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rope - a length of strong cord made by twisting strands together
  • degree lower: wire - thin, flexible thread of metal used to conduct electricity
