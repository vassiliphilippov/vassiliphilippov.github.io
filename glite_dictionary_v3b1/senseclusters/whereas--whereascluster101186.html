whereas

conjunction
introducing contrast
Pronunciation: / werˈæz /; / wɛrˈæz / (AmE), / weəˈræz /; / wɛəˈræz /; / wɛərˈæz / (BrE)
used to introduce a statement that contrasts with or explains the previous statement

Examples

Whereas he rests
He enjoys hiking in the mountains, whereas his brother prefers relaxing at the beach.
Some people like to work early in the morning, whereas others are more productive at night.
The old model is very affordable, whereas the new one is quite expensive.
Whereas the old model was slow, the new version is much faster and more efficient.
He prefers to work in the morning, whereas his partner is more productive at night.
Whereas many people enjoy spicy food, she cannot tolerate it at all.
Whereas the company has met all requirements, the license will be granted.
The contract states, whereas both parties agree to the terms, the agreement is valid.
Whereas she prefers tea, her brother always chooses coffee.

Related Terms

  • synonym: although - used to introduce a statement that makes the main statement seem surprising
  • synonym: while - used to introduce a contrast between two facts
