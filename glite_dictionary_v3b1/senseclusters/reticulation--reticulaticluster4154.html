reticulation

noun
network of cracks in photo emulsion
9.9 8.0
Pronunciation: /re.tɪ.kjʊ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /rə.tɪ.kjʊ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /re.tɪ.kjʊ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /rə.tɪ.kjʊ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
(Photography) The process or result of forming a network of cracks or wrinkles in the emulsion layer of photographic film, usually caused by improper temperature control during development.

Examples

Film reticulation damage
Improper temperature changes during film development can cause reticulation in your negatives.
Many vintage photographs show visible reticulation due to early chemical processing errors.
The photographer was disappointed to discover reticulation in his prints after accidentally using hot water in the rinse.
