monocarpic

adjective
flowering and fruiting once, then dying (botanical term)
6.5 8.5
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːnəˈkɑːrpɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈkɑːpɪk / (BrE)
(of a plant) flowering and bearing fruit only once before dying.

Examples

Monocarpic plant
Bamboo is a monocarpic plant, flowering only once before it withers and dies.
Many agave species are monocarpic, producing seeds a single time in their life cycle.
The rare monocarpic flowered in its greenhouse, marking the end of its life.

Related Terms

  • antonym: polycarpic - (of a plant) able to flower and fruit multiple times
