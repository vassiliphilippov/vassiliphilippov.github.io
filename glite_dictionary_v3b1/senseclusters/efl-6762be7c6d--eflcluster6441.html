EFL

noun
lens focal length
8.2
Pronunciation: / i ɛf ɛl / (AmE), / iː ɛf ɛl / (BrE)
the effective focal length of a lens, which determines the angle of view in photography

Examples

EFL of a camera lens
The EFL of this lens makes it perfect for landscape photography.
By changing the EFL, you can capture a wider or narrower scene.
Photographers often consider EFL when choosing the right lens for their camera.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aperture - the opening in a lens that controls the amount of light entering
  • synonym: focal length - the distance between the lens and the image sensor when focused
