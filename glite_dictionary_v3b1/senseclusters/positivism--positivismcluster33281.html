positivism

noun
attitude of certainty or affirmation
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑs.ə.tɪˌvɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈpɒz.ɪ.tɪˌvɪz.əm / (BrE)
A quality or attitude characterized by certainty, acceptance, or confident affirmation, often involving dogmatic assertiveness.

Examples

Strong positivism
Her positivism helped the team face challenges with unwavering certainty.
Some critics argue that extreme positivism can become close-minded and dismissive of valid doubts.
The manager's positivism led to confident, affirmative decisions even in uncertain situations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assertiveness - quality of being self-assured and confident without being aggressive
  • synonym: dogmatism - tendency to lay down principles as undeniably true
