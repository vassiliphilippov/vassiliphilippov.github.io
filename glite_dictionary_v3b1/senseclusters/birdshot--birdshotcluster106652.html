birdshot

noun
small shotgun pellets
Pronunciation: / ˈbɜrdˌʃɑt / (AmE), / ˈbɜːdˌʃɒt / (BrE)
small round pellets, usually made of lead or steel, used in shotgun shells for hunting birds

Examples

Shotgun birdshot
The hunter loaded his shotgun with birdshot before heading into the field.
Using birdshot is common when hunting small game like quail or pheasant.
He found several pieces of birdshot embedded in the old wooden fence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: buckshot - larger pellets used in shotgun shells for hunting bigger game
  • synonym: pellet - a small, round piece of material, often used as ammunition
