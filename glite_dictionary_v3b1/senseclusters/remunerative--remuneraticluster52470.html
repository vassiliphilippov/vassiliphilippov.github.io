remunerative

adjective
producing profit or reward
Pronunciation: / rɪˈmjunərətɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈmjuːnərətɪv / (BrE)
(of work or activity) providing good payment or financial reward; well-paid

Examples

A remunerative career
Despite being demanding, law can be a remunerative career.
She was looking for a more remunerative position in her field.
The investment turned out to be highly remunerative for everyone involved.
Freelance consulting can be highly remunerative if you have in-demand skills.
Many people seek remunerative employment to support their families.
She left her old job for a more remunerative position at another company.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lucrative - producing a great deal of profit; highly profitable
  • synonym: profitable - producing a financial gain or advantage
  • antonym: unpaid - not receiving payment for work or services
  • antonym: unprofitable - not producing a profit or useful result
