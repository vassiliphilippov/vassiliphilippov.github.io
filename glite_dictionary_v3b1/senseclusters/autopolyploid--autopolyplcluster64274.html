autopolyploid

noun
organism with extra chromosomes
Pronunciation: / ˌɔːtoʊˈpɑːlɪplɔɪd / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəʊˈpɒlɪplɔɪd / (BrE)
an organism that has more than two complete sets of chromosomes, all derived from a single species

Examples

A plant autopolyploid
The scientist discovered that the new species was an autopolyploid with six sets of chromosomes.
Many cultivated plants, such as potatoes, can be autopolyploids that arise naturally or through breeding.
Researchers study autopolyploid organisms to understand how extra chromosome sets affect growth and reproduction.

Related Terms

  • antonym: allopolyploid - an organism with multiple chromosome sets from different species
  • synonym: polyploid - an organism with more than two sets of chromosomes
