modal

adjective
relating to the mood of a verb
1.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmoʊ.dl̩ / (AmE), / ˈməʊ.dəl / (BrE)
relating to or expressing the mood of a verb (such as indicative, subjunctive, or imperative); used to refer to modal verbs or auxiliary verbs expressing necessity, possibility, permission, or ability.

Examples

Modal meaning in verbs
She gave a lecture on modal auxiliaries and their uses in English.
The sentence contains a modal verb expressing possibility.
Understanding modal meanings is crucial for advanced grammar learners.

Related Terms

  • synonym: auxiliary - a verb used to form the tenses, moods, voices, etc., of other verbs
  • degree higher: imperative - the grammatical mood expressing commands or requests
  • degree lower: indicative - the grammatical mood that expresses facts or statements
