kurchee

noun
tropical Asian tree used in traditional medicine
9.9
Pronunciation: / kərˈtʃi / (AmE), / kəˈtʃiː / (BrE)
A tropical Asian tree (Holarrhena antidysenterica) with hard white wood and bark formerly used as a remedy for dysentery and diarrhea.

Examples

The kurchee tree
Botanists study the distribution of the kurchee throughout South Asia.
Local healers once relied on kurchee for treating stomach ailments.
The bark of the kurchee is valued in traditional medicine.
