blockout

noun
restricted access period
7.0 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààbl…ëÀêkÀåa ät / (AmE), / Ààbl…íkÀåa ät / (BrE)
a period during which access to a service, event, or broadcast is intentionally prevented or restricted

Examples

TV blockout
Due to licensing agreements, there was a blockout of the live concert in some countries.
Fans were disappointed by the unexpected blockout of the championship game on local television.
The streaming service announced a blockout for certain movies during the holiday season.

Synonyms

  • blackout
    6.0 0.31/m
    blackout is a temporary stop of radio and television broadcasts for legal reasons, while blockout is a planned restriction of access to a service or event.
  • lockout
    4.9 0.22/m
    lockout is an automatic blocking of a user account after failed logins, while blockout is a period when access to a service or event is kept closed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blackout - a period when a broadcast or service is intentionally suspended
