Benghal bean

noun
Asian vine plant with hairy pods, used for green manure
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛŋɡəl ˌbin / (AmE), / ˈbɛŋɡəl ˌbiːn / (BrE)
an annual woody vine of Asia with long clusters of purplish flowers and hairy pods, cultivated in some regions as green manure and for grazing

Examples

Benghal bean plant
Because of its dense foliage, Benghal bean provides excellent grazing for livestock.
The farmer planted Benghal bean in the fallow fields to improve soil fertility.
You can recognize the Benghal bean by its purplish flower clusters and hairy seed pods.
