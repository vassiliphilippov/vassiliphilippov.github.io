shipman

noun
archaic sailor
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɪpmən / (AmE), / ˈʃɪpmən / (BrE)
an old-fashioned term for a sailor or seaman, especially one working on a ship

Examples

A shipman at sea
The shipman stood at the helm, guiding the vessel through the storm.
In old tales, a shipman would often share stories of distant lands and adventures.
The captain trusted the experienced shipman to manage the crew during the long voyage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: landlubber - a person unfamiliar with the sea or sailing
  • synonym: sailor - a person who works on a ship
  • synonym: seaman - a member of a ship's crew
