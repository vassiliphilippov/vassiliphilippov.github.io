surface fire

noun
forest fire burning only the surface layer
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsɝː.fɪs ˌfaɪr / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.fɪs ˌfaɪə / (BrE)
a forest fire that burns only the leaf litter and undergrowth, not the larger trees

Examples

A surface fire in forest
A surface fire spread quickly through the dry leaves, but left the tree trunks undamaged.
Firefighters managed to contain the surface fire before it reached the denser forest.
Periodic surface fires can help maintain healthy forest ecosystems by removing undergrowth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: crown fire - a forest fire that burns through the canopy of trees
