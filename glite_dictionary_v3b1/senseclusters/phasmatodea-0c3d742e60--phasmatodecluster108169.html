Phasmatodea

noun
order of stick and leaf insects
9.9
Pronunciation: / fæz.məˈtoʊ.di.ə /; / ˌfæzməˈtoʊdiə / (AmE), / faz.məˈtəʊ.di.ə /; / ˌfazmətəˈdiːə / (BrE)
an order of insects including stick insects and leaf insects, known for their ability to camouflage as twigs or leaves

Examples

Phasmatodea stick insects
Members of the Phasmatodea are renowned for their camouflage that makes them resemble twigs or leaves.
Researchers discovered several new species of Phasmatodea in the rainforest during their expedition.
The Phasmatodea order includes some of the world’s longest insects, such as the giant walking stick.
Scientists study Phasmatodea to understand the evolution of camouflage and mimicry.
Several members of the Phasmatodea can regrow lost limbs during their development.
The order Phasmatodea contains over 3,000 species of stick and leaf insects worldwide.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stick insect - an insect with a long, resembling stick body; member of Phasmatodea
