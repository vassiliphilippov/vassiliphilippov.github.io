arborize

verb
to branch out like a tree
7.6 7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈɑːr.bə.raɪz/ /; / /ˈɑːr.bər.aɪz/ (AmE), /ˈɑː.bə.raɪz/ (BrE)
To branch out or spread in a tree-like manner, especially of structures in anatomy or biology (for example nerve fibers, blood vessels, or bronchioles).

Examples

Bronchioles arborize extensively as they reach the alveoli in the lungs.

The nerve fibers arborize into complex patterns within the brain.
Some blood vessels arborise extensively as they reach muscle tissues.
The neurons begin to arborise during early development, creating complex networks.
Under the microscope, the dendrites were seen to arborise beautifully throughout the sample.
