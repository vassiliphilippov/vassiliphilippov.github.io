cafeteria Christianity

noun
selective adherence to Christian beliefs
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkæfəˈtɪriə ˌkrɪstʃiˈænəti / (AmE), / ˌkæfəˈtɪəriə ˌkrɪstʃiˈænəti / (BrE)
a practice of selectively choosing which aspects of Christian doctrine or beliefs to follow, rather than adhering strictly to all official teachings

Examples

cafeteria Christianity practice
Debates over cafeteria Christianity often arise in discussions about modern religious practice.
Her approach to faith has been labeled cafeteria Christianity because she disagrees with certain doctrines.
Many people criticize cafeteria Christianity for contradicting the church's teachings.
