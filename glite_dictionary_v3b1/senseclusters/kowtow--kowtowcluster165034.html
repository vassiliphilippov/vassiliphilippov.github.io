kowtow

noun
act of deep respect or submission (Chinese custom)
9.9
Pronunciation: / kaʊˈtaʊ / (AmE), / kaʊˈtaʊ / (BrE)
an act of kneeling and touching the forehead to the ground as a sign of deep respect, worship, or submission, especially in former Chinese custom

Examples

A formal kowtow
Historians discussed the role of the kowtow in diplomatic ceremonies.
The emperor expected every visitor to perform the kowtow upon entering the throne room.
The painting depicts envoys performing the kowtow before the emperor.
