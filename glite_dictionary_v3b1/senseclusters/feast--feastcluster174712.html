feast

noun
ceremonial or festive large meal
Pronunciation: / fiːst / (AmE), / fiːst / (BrE)
a large, elaborate meal or banquet, usually for a special occasion or celebration

Examples

A holiday feast
Families gather for a traditional feast during the holiday season.
The king hosted a lavish feast to celebrate the victory.
The medieval feast featured music, dancing, and endless courses of food.
During the festival, villagers gathered in the square for a grand feast under the stars.
The wedding reception turned into an outdoor feast lasting late into the night.
We prepared a delicious feast for the whole family to celebrate the holiday.
After their long journey, they sat down to a hearty feast with friends.
The Thanksgiving feast included turkey, stuffing, and a variety of pies.
The wedding was followed by a delicious feast that everyone enjoyed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: banquet - a formal large meal or elaborate dinner
  • antonym: famine - a severe shortage of food
  • degree lower: picnic - an informal meal eaten outdoors
  • antonym: snack - a small, light meal
  • synonym: spread - an elaborate variety of food laid out for a meal
