magnetopause

noun
magnetic boundary in space
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈmæɡ.nə.tə.pɑːz/ /; / /ˈmæɡ.nɪ.tə.pɑːz/ /; / /ˈmæɡ.nə.tə.pɔːz/ (AmE), /ˈmæɡ.nə.tə.pɔːz/ /; / /ˈmæɡ.nɪ.tə.pɔːz/ /; / /ˈmæɡ.nə.təʊ.pɔːz/ (BrE)
the boundary around a planet or other body where its magnetic field meets and balances the solar wind or surrounding plasma

Examples

Scientists study the magnetopause to understand how Earth's magnetic field interacts with the solar wind.
The spacecraft crossed the magnetopause and entered the region dominated by Earth's magnetic field.
Changes in the solar wind can cause the magnetopause to move closer to or farther from the planet.
The solar wind pushes against Earth's magnetopause, shaping the planet's magnetic environment.

Scientists study the magnetopause to understand how Earth's magnetic field protects us from solar wind.
Jupiter's magnetopause is much farther from the planet than Earth's due to its stronger magnetic field.
