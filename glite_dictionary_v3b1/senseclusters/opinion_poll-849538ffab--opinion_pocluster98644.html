Synonyms

  • public opinion poll
    3.1 1/m
    a public opinion poll asks a representative group about public views, while an opinion poll is a general survey of people's opinions.
  • poll
    1.6 100/m
    a poll asks a number of people their views, while an opinion poll is often a formal survey using a random sample.
  • polling
    2.6 11/m
    polling means the ongoing act of collecting votes or opinions, while an opinion poll is a one-time set of questions.
  • survey
    3.2 13/m
    a survey collects information by asking questions about many topics, while an opinion poll focuses on public views about one issue.
  • canvass
    7.2 0.4/m
    a canvass asks people directly often door to door for political support, while an opinion poll measures public views through sampled questions.
