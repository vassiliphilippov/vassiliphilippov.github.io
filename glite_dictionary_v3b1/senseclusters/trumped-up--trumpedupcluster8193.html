trumped-up

adjective
deliberately fabricated or falsified
7.9 0.53/m
Pronunciation: / ËŒtrÊŒmptËˆÊŒp / (AmE), / ËŒtrÊŒmptËˆÊŒp / (BrE)
deliberately fabricated or invented, especially with the intention to deceive or wrongly accuse someone of wrongdoing.

Examples

A trumped-up charge
The defense attorney argued that all the evidence was completely trumped-up.
The journalist exposed the trumped-up charges brought against the innocent activist.
They were imprisoned on trumped-up accusations with no real evidence.

Synonyms

    6.0 0.00/m

  • put-up
    8.1 0.02/m
    put-up describes a secretly arranged plan often meant to trick people, while trumped-up usually refers to false charges against someone.
