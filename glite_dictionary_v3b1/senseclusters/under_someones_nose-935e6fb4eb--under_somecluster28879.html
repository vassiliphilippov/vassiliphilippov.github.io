under someone's nose

adverb
in someone's presence without their knowledge
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndɚ ˈsʌmwʌnz noʊz / (AmE), / ˈʌndə ˈsʌmwənz nəʊz / (BrE)
in someone's presence or nearby, without their knowledge or while they are unaware

Examples

Under someone's nose quietly
Important documents went missing under the manager's nose during the busy conference.
She hid the surprise gift under his nose the whole time.
The thieves stole the jewels under the guard's nose without him noticing.
