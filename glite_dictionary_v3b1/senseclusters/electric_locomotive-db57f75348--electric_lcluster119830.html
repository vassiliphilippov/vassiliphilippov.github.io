electric locomotive

noun
train engine powered by electricity
2.5 3.2
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɛktrɪk ˌloʊkəˈmoʊtɪv / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪk ˌləʊkəˈməʊtɪv / (BrE)
A railway locomotive powered by an electric motor, usually supplied with electricity through overhead lines or a third rail.

Examples

An electric locomotive engine
The engineer inspected the electric locomotive before the train's departure.
The new high-speed railway line uses only electric locomotives to reduce environmental pollution.
Unlike older models, an electric locomotive requires less maintenance and produces no exhaust fumes.
