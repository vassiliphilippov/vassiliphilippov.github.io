monophthong

noun
single vowel sound
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑnəfˌθɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɒnəfˌθɒŋ / (BrE)
a vowel sound that is pronounced with a single, unchanging quality throughout its duration

Examples

A monophthong vowel
The vowel in the word 'cat' is a monophthong because its sound does not change.
Linguists study how monophthongs differ from diphthongs in various languages.
In some accents, the vowel in 'go' is pronounced as a monophthong rather than a diphthong.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diphthong - a vowel sound that glides from one quality to another
  • synonym: vowel - a speech sound made without significant constriction of airflow
