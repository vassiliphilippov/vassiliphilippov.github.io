luminous flux unit

noun
unit measuring luminous flux
Pronunciation: / ˈluːmɪnəs flʌks ˈjuːnɪt / (AmE), / ˈluːmɪnəs flʌks ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a unit used to measure luminous flux, which is the perceived power of light.

Examples

The luminous flux unit
In lighting design, the luminous flux unit is essential for determining illumination levels in a room.
LED bulbs are rated according to their luminous flux unit output, making comparisons easier.
The luminous flux unit helps scientists quantify the amount of visible light emitted by a source.
