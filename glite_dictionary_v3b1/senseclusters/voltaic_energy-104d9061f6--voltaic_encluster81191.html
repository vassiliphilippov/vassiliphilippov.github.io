voltaic energy

noun
electricity from chemical reactions
7.3
Pronunciation: / voʊlˈteɪɪk ˈɛnɚdʒi / (AmE), / vɒlˈteɪɪk ˈɛnədʒi / (BrE)
electricity generated by chemical reactions, especially as produced in a voltaic cell or battery

Examples

Voltaic energy from batteries
Early experiments with voltaic energy led to the development of the first batteries.
Scientists use voltaic energy to power small electronic devices in the laboratory.
The chemistry class built a basic battery to observe voltaic energy in action.

Related Terms

  • synonym: electrical energy - energy made available by the flow of electric charge
  • antonym: static electricity - electricity accumulated in one place, not flowing as a current
