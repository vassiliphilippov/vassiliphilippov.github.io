vesicoureteral reflux

noun
backflow of urine from bladder to ureter
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˌvɛsɪkoʊjʊˈritərəl ˈriːflʌks / (AmE), / ˌvɛsɪkjʊˈrɪtərəl ˈriːflʌks / (BrE)
a condition in which urine flows backward from the bladder into the ureter

Examples

Vesicoureteral reflux condition
Children with vesicoureteral reflux may require regular monitoring by a urologist.
Early detection of vesicoureteral reflux is important to prevent renal damage.
The doctor explained that vesicoureteral reflux could lead to kidney infections if left untreated.
