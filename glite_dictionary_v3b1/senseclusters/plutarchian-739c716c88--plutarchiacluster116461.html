Plutarchian

adjective
of Plutarch's style
Pronunciation: / pluːˈtɑːrkiən / (AmE), / pluːˈtɑːkiən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of Plutarch or his writings, especially in style or moral comparison

Examples

Plutarchian style
The historian's Plutarchian approach compared the virtues of famous leaders.
Her essay adopted a Plutarchian style, focusing on moral lessons from ancient lives.
Critics praised the Plutarchian tone of the biography for its balanced character analysis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: classical - relating to ancient Greek or Roman literature or culture
