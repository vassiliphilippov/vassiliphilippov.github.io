pterodactyl

noun
extinct flying reptile
5.4
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛrəˈdæktl̩ / (AmE), / ˌtɛrəˈdæktɪl / (BrE)
an extinct flying reptile of the Jurassic and Cretaceous periods, with a long slender head and a short tail, belonging to the order Pterosauria

Examples

A pterodactyl flying
Children are often fascinated by the idea that a pterodactyl could glide through prehistoric skies.
The museum displays a life-size model of a pterodactyl soaring above the dinosaur hall.
When he saw the skeleton, the paleontologist identified it as a pterodactyl from the late Jurassic period.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pterosaur - general group of prehistoric flying reptiles
