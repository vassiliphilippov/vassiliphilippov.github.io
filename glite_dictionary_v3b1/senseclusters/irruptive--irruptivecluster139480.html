irruptive

adjective
sudden increase
9.5
Pronunciation: / ɪˈrʌptɪv / (AmE), / ɪˈrʌptɪv / (BrE)
characterized by a sudden increase or rapid appearance, especially in numbers or population

Examples

Irruptive insect numbers
The forest saw an irruptive growth of insects after the warm, wet spring.
Scientists studied the irruptive migration of birds into the northern regions this year.
An irruptive outbreak of algae affected the lake's ecosystem within a few weeks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: explosive - happening suddenly and with great force
  • antonym: gradual - happening slowly over time
