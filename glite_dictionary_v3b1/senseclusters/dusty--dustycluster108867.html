dusty

adjective
old-fashioned; lacking freshness or originality
6.5 0.63/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆdÊŒsti / (AmE), / ËˆdÊŒsti / (BrE)
(of ideas, practices, etc.) lacking originality, freshness, or spontaneity; considered old-fashioned or stale.

Examples

Dusty ideas
His dusty approach to teaching bored the students every year.
Many critics found the director's latest play dusty and uninspired.
The conference was filled with dusty debates that nobody wanted to revisit.

Synonyms

    3.1 1.86/m

  • moth-eaten
    7.3 0.00/m

  • old-hat
    6.8 0.03/m

  • stodgy
    7.8 0.61/m
    stodgy is heavy, dull, and resistant to change, while dusty refers to ideas that are simply old-fashioned or uninspired.
