revoke

verb
officially cancel; withdraw
4.6 5.0
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈvoʊk/ /; / /riː.ˈvoʊk/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈvəʊk/ (BrE)
to officially cancel the validity or effect of something, such as a law, agreement, or privilege

Examples

To revoke a license
Her license was revoked due to repeated violations of the law.
If you violate the agreement, the organization may revoke your membership.
The company decided to revoke his access to sensitive files after the incident.

Related Terms

  • synonym: annul - to declare invalid, especially legally
  • synonym: cancel - to decide that something will not take place or is no longer valid
  • antonym: confirm - to establish the truth or validity of something
