juried

adjective
evaluated by judges
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʊriːd / (AmE), / ˈdʒʊəriːd / (BrE)
selected or evaluated by a panel of judges, especially for quality or skill in a competition or exhibition

Examples

A juried exhibition
Her painting was accepted into a juried exhibition at the local art gallery.
The festival only features juried films that have passed a strict selection process.
He was proud to win first place in a juried photography contest.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unjudged - not evaluated by a judge or panel
