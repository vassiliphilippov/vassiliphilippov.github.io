anticoagulation

noun
prevention of blood clotting by drugs
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.tiˌkoʊ.æɡ.jəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌæn.tiˌkəʊ.æɡ.jʊˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of preventing blood from clotting, especially by the administration of drugs known as anticoagulants

Examples

Anticoagulation therapy
Before surgery, anticoagulation may need to be temporarily stopped to prevent excessive bleeding.
Long-term anticoagulation therapy requires careful monitoring for side effects.
The patient was started on anticoagulation to reduce the risk of stroke.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anticoagulant - a drug that prevents blood clotting
  • antonym: coagulation - the process of blood clotting
