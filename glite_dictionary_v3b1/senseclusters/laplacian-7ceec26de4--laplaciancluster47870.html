Laplacian

noun
mathematical operator
Pronunciation: / ləˈpleɪsiən / (AmE), / ləˈplæsɪən / (BrE)
a mathematical operator that measures the rate at which a quantity changes around a point, often used in physics and engineering

Examples

The Laplacian operator
The Laplacian is essential in solving equations related to heat flow and diffusion.
In image processing, the Laplacian helps detect edges by highlighting regions of rapid intensity change.
Students learned how to apply the Laplacian to analyze physical systems in their engineering class.

Related Terms

  • synonym: divergence - a mathematical operator measuring the magnitude of a source or sink at a point
  • synonym: gradient - a vector indicating the direction and rate of fastest increase of a function
