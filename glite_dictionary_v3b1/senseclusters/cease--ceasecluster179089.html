cease

verb
bring or come to an end
3.6 23/m
Pronunciation: / sis / (AmE), / siÀês / (BrE)
to stop or bring something to an end; to come to an end

Examples

To cease the noise
If the noise does not cease soon, I will file a complaint.
Once the hostilities ceased, peace was finally restored to the region.
We were asked to cease all activity immediately during the inspection.
All communication between the two companies ceased abruptly last year.
At midnight, the music will cease so the neighbors are not disturbed.
The rain finally ceased after two hours of thunder and lightning.

Synonyms

  • end
    1.0 299/m
    end is to bring something to a close and finish an action, while cease means to stop or come to an end.
  • stop
    1.0 539/m
    stop brings a sudden or physical end to movement or activity, while cease is more neutral or formal about stopping.
  • terminate
    4.6 8.77/m
    terminate sounds formal and appears in contracts or technical texts, while cease is a general verb for stopping.
  • halt
    2.4 14/m
    halt often describes a short, sudden stop in movement or action, while cease can describe stopping more broadly.
  • discontinue
    4.2 4.49/m
    discontinue usually means to stop providing or making something, while cease can mean to stop any action or process.
  • desist
    5.6 1.22/m
    desist is used to tell someone to stop an unwanted act, while cease is a general verb for stopping or ending.
  • finish
    1.0 172/m
    finish means complete something until its natural end, while cease just means to stop, not necessarily fully complete.

Related Terms

  • antonym: continue - to persist in an activity or process; to keep going
  • synonym: stop - to bring to an end or halt; to discontinue
