cease

verb
bring or come to an end
Pronunciation: / sis / (AmE), / siːs / (BrE)
to stop or bring something to an end; to come to an end

Examples

To cease the noise
If the noise does not cease soon, I will file a complaint.
Once the hostilities ceased, peace was finally restored to the region.
We were asked to cease all activity immediately during the inspection.
All communication between the two companies ceased abruptly last year.
At midnight, the music will cease so the neighbors are not disturbed.
The rain finally ceased after two hours of thunder and lightning.

Related Terms

  • antonym: continue - to persist in an activity or process; to keep going
  • synonym: stop - to bring to an end or halt; to discontinue
