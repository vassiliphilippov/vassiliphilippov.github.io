chamaeleon

noun
color-changing lizard of Africa and Madagascar
Pronunciation: / kəˈmiːliən / (AmE), / kəˈmiːliən / (BrE)
A lizard found in Africa and Madagascar able to change the color of its skin and possessing a long, projectile tongue.

Examples

The chamaeleon changes color
Biologists study the chamaeleon to understand how its skin pigments react to different stimuli.
During our safari, we spotted a chamaeleon on a tree branch, its tongue darting out to catch insects.
The chamaeleon changes color to blend with its surroundings and avoid predators.
