monocyclic

adjective
having one cycle
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːnəˈsaɪklɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈsaɪklɪk / (BrE)
having exactly one cycle, such as a single closed path in a graph or a single life or disease cycle in a season or year

Examples

Monocyclic graph
In graph theory, a monocyclic graph contains exactly one simple cycle.
The chemist explained that the compound was monocyclic, consisting of a single ring structure.
The network was found to be monocyclic, making its analysis much simpler.
The monocyclic nature of the insect means it produces only one generation each summer.
Farmers prefer crops with monocyclic disease cycles because they are easier to manage.
A monocyclic fungus infects plants only once during the growing season.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acyclic - having no cycles or closed paths
  • antonym: polycyclic - having more than one cycle or closed path
