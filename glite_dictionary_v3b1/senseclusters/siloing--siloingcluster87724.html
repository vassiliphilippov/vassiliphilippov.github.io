siloing

noun
organizational isolation
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪloʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪləʊɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of keeping groups, departments, or information isolated within an organization, limiting communication or collaboration

Examples

Siloing of teams
The company's siloing of departments led to repeated mistakes and missed opportunities.
Effective leaders try to break down siloing to encourage better teamwork and communication.
Siloing can make it difficult for employees to access the information they need from other teams.

Related Terms

  • antonym: collaboration - working together with others to achieve a common goal
  • synonym: isolation - the state of being separated from others
