under fire

adjective
being attacked or criticized
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndɚ ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈʌndə ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
being attacked or strongly criticized, especially in public

Examples

Under fire from media
After the scandal broke, the minister was under fire from the media.
Rescue workers came under fire while trying to reach the injured soldier.
The company's policies have come under fire from environmental groups.
Journalists are under fire for spreading misinformation online.
The coach has been under fire after several disappointing losses this season.
The company came under fire for its handling of customer complaints.
