overtraining

noun
excessive model training
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈtreɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈtreɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
the condition in which a machine learning model is trained so much on its training data that it performs poorly on new, unseen data

Examples

Model overtraining
If you do not use proper validation, your model may suffer from overtraining and fail on new data.
The researchers adjusted their algorithm to prevent overtraining during the learning process.
Regular testing on separate data sets helps avoid overtraining in machine learning projects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overfitting - a modeling error where a model fits the training data too closely and fails to generalize
  • antonym: underfitting - a modeling error where a model is too simple to capture the underlying pattern in the data
