mastabah

noun
ancient Egyptian tomb with sloping sides
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈmæs.tə.bə/ (AmE), /ˈmæs.tə.bə/ (BrE)
an ancient Egyptian tomb with a rectangular base, sloping sides, and a flat roof, typically made of mud-brick

Examples

An ancient mastabah tomb
Archaeologists discovered a well-preserved mastabah near the site of the Great Pyramid.
Each mastabah contained a chapel and burial chamber for the deceased.
The earliest Egyptian pharaohs were buried in rectangular mastabas before the development of pyramids.
