voluntary

noun
musical piece for solo organ, often improvised
Pronunciation: / ˈvɑːlənˌtɛri / (AmE), / ˈvɒl.ən.tər.i / (BrE)
A musical composition, often improvised, for a solo instrument (especially the organ) played before, during, or after a religious service and not forming a regular part of the service.

Examples

Organ voluntary
She composed a voluntary to showcase her skills on the church organ.
The concert opened with a Baroque voluntary that set a majestic tone.
The organist performed an exquisite voluntary at the end of the service.
