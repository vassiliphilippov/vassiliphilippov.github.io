nearshore

verb
to relocate nearby
6.1
Pronunciation: /nɪər.ˈʃɔːr/ /; / /nɪr.ˈʃɔːr/ (AmE), /nɪə.ˈʃɔː/ (BrE)
to move business operations or services to a nearby country, usually to reduce costs or improve collaboration.

Examples

To nearshore services
The company decided to nearshore its customer support to a neighboring country for better communication.
Many tech firms nearshore their software development to take advantage of similar time zones.
To reduce travel costs, the business chose to nearshore its manufacturing operations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: offshore - to move business operations to a distant country
  • synonym: outsource - to contract work out to another company or country
