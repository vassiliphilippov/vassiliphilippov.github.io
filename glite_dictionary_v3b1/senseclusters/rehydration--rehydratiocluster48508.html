rehydration

noun
adding water again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːhaɪˈdreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːhaɪˈdreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of adding water back to something that has become dry or dehydrated (such as food, chemicals, etc.)

Examples

Rehydration of dried food
The rehydration of the dried fruit made it soft and ready to eat.
Proper rehydration is essential after intense exercise to restore lost fluids.
In chemistry, rehydration can reverse the effects of a dehydration reaction.
The rehydration of dried fruit is necessary before using it in baking recipes.
Proper rehydration of athletes is important after intense physical activity.
Scientists studied the rehydration process of soil after a long drought.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dehydration - the process of removing water from something
  • synonym: hydration - the process of combining with water
