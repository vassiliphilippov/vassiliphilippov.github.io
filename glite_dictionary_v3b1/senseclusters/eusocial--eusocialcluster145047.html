eusocial

adjective
cooperatively social
9.9
Pronunciation: / juːˈsoʊʃəl / (AmE), / juːˈsəʊʃəl / (BrE)
living in highly organized groups with cooperative care, division of labor, and overlapping generations, especially in insects

Examples

Eusocial insects
Bees are eusocial insects that work together to build and protect their hive.
Termites display eusocial behavior, with different castes performing specific roles in the colony.
Scientists study eusocial animals to understand how cooperation evolved in nature.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: social - living together in groups or communities
  • antonym: solitary - living alone, not in groups
