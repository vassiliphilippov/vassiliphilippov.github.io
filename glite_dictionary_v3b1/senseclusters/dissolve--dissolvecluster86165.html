dissolve

verb
to cause to go into solution
1.3
Pronunciation: / dɪˈzɑlv / (AmE), / dɪˈzɒlv / (BrE)
to mix or become mixed with a liquid so that it forms a solution or becomes liquid

Examples

Salt dissolves in water
Add salt to the water and let it dissolve before cooking the pasta.
Some chemicals dissolve more easily in hot water than in cold.
Stir the mixture until the sugar has completely dissolved.
Add the powder to the water and stir until it dissolves.
Salt dissolved in the soup, making it taste better.
Sugar dissolves more quickly in hot tea than in cold.
As the ice cubes dissolve in the drink, it becomes less cold.
If you add sugar to hot water, it will dissolve quickly.
The medicine is designed to dissolve under your tongue for faster relief.

Related Terms

  • synonym: melt - to change from solid to liquid by heating
  • antonym: precipitate - cause a solid to be separated from a solution
  • antonym: solidify - to make or become hard or solid
