onboard

adjective, adverb
present on a vehicle
3.3
Pronunciation: / ˈɑnˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈɒnˌbɔːd / (BrE)
on or onto a ship, aircraft, train, or other vehicle

Examples

All onboard the plane
All passengers must be onboard before the train can leave the station.
The flight attendant checked that everyone was onboard and seated for takeoff.
There are emergency supplies onboard the ship for all crew members.
The passengers were asked to step onboard as the train prepared to depart.
All equipment must be loaded onboard before the ship leaves the harbor.
She climbed onboard the plane and found her seat by the window.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aboard - on or into a ship, aircraft, train, or other vehicle
  • antonym: offboard - not on or inside a vehicle
