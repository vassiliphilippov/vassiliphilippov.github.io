airy-fairy

adjective
impractical or overly fanciful
Pronunciation: / ˌɛri ˈfɛri / (AmE), / ˌeəri ˈfeəri / (BrE)
Impractical, excessively fanciful, or lacking in seriousness; whimsical or unrealistic.

Examples

An airy-fairy idea
Her airy-fairy ideas about changing the world were charming but lacked substance.
People sometimes accuse artists of being a bit airy-fairy when they talk about inspiration.
The committee dismissed his airy-fairy plan as completely unrealistic.

Related Terms

  • antonym: practical - concerned with actual use or practice rather than theory or ideas
  • synonym: whimsical - playfully quaint or fanciful, especially in an appealing and amusing way
