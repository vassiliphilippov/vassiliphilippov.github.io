shawm

noun
medieval double-reed woodwind instrument
Pronunciation: / ʃɑm / (AmE), / ʃɔːm / (BrE)
a medieval woodwind instrument with a double reed, considered an ancestor of the modern oboe.

Examples

A shawm instrument
A shawm was often used in Renaissance and medieval dance music.
She identified the unusual instrument in the painting as a shawm.
The musicians played a lively tune on the shawm during the town festival.
