open-end credit

noun
revolving consumer credit system

a type of consumer credit agreement that allows the borrower to access funds up to a set limit and repay at their discretion, reusing the credit as it's repaid.

Examples

An open-end credit account
Home equity lines of credit are a popular form of open-end credit for homeowners.
Many credit cards operate as open-end credit, letting consumers borrow up to a predetermined limit.
With open-end credit, you can repay part or all of your balance and borrow again as needed.

Synonyms

  • revolving credit
    5.6 0.08/m
    revolving credit lets a consumer borrow repeatedly up to a set limit and reuse funds, while open-end credit names the consumer loan agreement.
  • charge account credit
    3.9 0.00/m
    charge account credit often serves store purchases within a set limit and ongoing balance, while open-end credit describes a broader consumer borrowing system.
  • line of credit
    3.1 1.43/m
    line of credit is a bank arrangement to borrow up to a maximum amount, while open-end credit focuses on consumer reusable borrowing agreements.
