kindle

verb
arouse or inspire feelings
3.8 5.3
Pronunciation: /ˈkɪn.dəl/ (AmE), /ˈkɪn.dəl/ (BrE)
to arouse or inspire an emotion, feeling, or interest in someone

Examples

To kindle hope
A simple act of kindness can kindle feelings of gratitude in others.
Her speech managed to kindle hope in the hearts of the audience.
The film kindled strong emotions among viewers, leaving many in tears.
A sudden idea can kindle creativity in a team.
Her teacher's encouragement helped kindle a lifelong love for science.
The campers tried to kindle a flame using only dry twigs and a flint.
