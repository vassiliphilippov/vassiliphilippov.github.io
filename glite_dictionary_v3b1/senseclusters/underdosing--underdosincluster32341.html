underdosing

noun
insufficient medication
7.3
Pronunciation: /ˈʌn.dər.doʊ.zɪŋ/ /; / /ˈʌn.dər.doʊ.sɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈʌn.də.dəʊ.zɪŋ/ /; / /ˈʌn.də.dəʊ.sɪŋ/ (BrE)
the act or result of taking or giving less than the recommended amount of a drug or medicine

Examples

To underdose medicine
Patients who practice underdosing may not achieve the desired results from their medication.
Doctors warn that underdosing can lead to the development of drug-resistant bacteria.
Accidental underdosing sometimes happens when patients misunderstand their prescription instructions.
The patient suffered complications due to underdosing of the prescribed medication.
Accidental underdosing can make treatments less effective and delay recovery.
Strict guidelines help prevent both underdosing and overdosing in clinical settings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overdosing - taking more than the recommended amount of a drug or medicine
  • synonym: underdose - to give or take less than the recommended dose
  • synonym: undermedication - not receiving enough medication for effective treatment
