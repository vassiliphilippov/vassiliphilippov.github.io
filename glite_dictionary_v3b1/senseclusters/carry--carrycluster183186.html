carry

verb
have with you or on your person
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /ˈkæ.riː/ /; / /ˈkæ.rɪ/ (AmE), /ˈkæ.rɪ/ (BrE)
to hold, support, or move someone or something from one place to another, often by hand, on your person, or in a vehicle

Examples

To carry a bag
During the hike, she carried a first aid kit in her backpack at all times.
He always carries his wallet in his back pocket.
I usually carry a notebook to jot down ideas whenever they come to me.
Buses carry thousands of commuters into the city each morning.
Pipes carry fresh water to all the apartments in the building.
She asked me to carry her bags upstairs.
Each student must carry a minimum of twelve credits to be considered full-time.
She is able to carry the team's hopes on her shoulders.
The company cannot carry any more debt without risking bankruptcy.
