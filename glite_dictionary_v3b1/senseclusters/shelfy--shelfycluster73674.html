shelfy

adjective
having many submerged reefs or shoals
4.5
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɛlfi / (AmE), / ˈʃɛlfi / (BrE)
(of water or coastline) characterized by the presence of many submerged reefs, sandbanks, or shoals.

Examples

A shelfy coastline
Marine life thrives in shelfy environments, where reefs provide shelter and food sources.
Near the coast, the waters became dangerously shelfy and required careful navigation.
The shipwreck occurred in a particularly shelfy part of the bay, notorious among sailors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reef - a ridge of jagged rock, coral, or sand just above or below the surface of the sea
  • synonym: shoaly - (of water) full of shoals; shallow and having many sandbanks or submerged features
