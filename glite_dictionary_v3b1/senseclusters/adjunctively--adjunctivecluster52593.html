adjunctively

adverb
in an assistant role
Pronunciation: / əˈdʒʌŋktɪvli / (AmE), / əˈdʒʌŋktɪvli / (BrE)
in an additional, supporting, or secondary way; not as the main thing

Examples

Teach adjunctively at colleges
She taught adjunctively at two colleges while working full-time in a research lab.
Many professionals work adjunctively to gain experience while holding another main job.
The professor contributed adjunctively to the department, assisting with special projects and lectures.
The medication was used adjunctively to support the main therapy.
In linguistics, adverbs can function adjunctively to modify verbs.
The new software was introduced adjunctively to improve the existing system.

Related Terms

  • synonym: additionally - in an extra or supplementary way
  • antonym: full-time - working or serving for the whole of the usual time
  • synonym: part-time - working or serving for only part of the usual time
  • antonym: primarily - mainly or chiefly
