circa

preposition
about a date
7.0
Pronunciation: /ˈsɜːr.kə/ (AmE), /ˈsɜː.kə/ (BrE)
used to indicate that a date or number is approximate, especially in historical contexts

Examples

Circa 500 BCE
The ancient temple was constructed circa 500 BCE, according to archaeologists.
The artist was born circa 1880, though the exact year is uncertain.
This document dates from circa 1750 and provides insight into life at that time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: approximately - close to a particular number or date but not exact
  • antonym: exactly - precisely; without any approximation
