harvest

noun
large-scale data collection
4.4
Pronunciation: /ˈhɑːr.vəst/ /; / /ˈhɑːr.vɪst/ (AmE), /ˈhɑː.vəst/ /; / /ˈhɑː.vɪst/ (BrE)
large-scale collection of data or information from multiple sources, often by automated systems, used for analysis, profiling, or other processing.

Examples

Data harvest
Tech firms rely on a data harvest to build user profiles for targeted advertising.
The researchers scheduled a nightly harvest of public tweets to analyze sentiment trends across cities.
After the campaign there was a huge harvest of customer emails and purchase histories for study.
