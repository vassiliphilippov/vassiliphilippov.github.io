semitrance

noun
state of partial trance
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmiˈtræns / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈtrɑːns / (BrE)
a trancelike state in which a person can follow instructions but has weak or absent voluntary action

Examples

In semitrance state
In a semitrance, he responded to simple commands but showed little initiative of his own.
The ceremony left her in a semitrance, her eyes open but her actions subdued.
The patient appeared to be in a semitrance during the hypnosis session.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: trance - a state of focused attention or semi-consciousness, often with reduced voluntary movement
