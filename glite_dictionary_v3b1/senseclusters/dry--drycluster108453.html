dry

adjective
not shedding tears
Pronunciation: / draɪ / (AmE), / draɪ / (BrE)
free from moisture or liquid; not wet or damp (including substances, objects, body parts, or symptoms)

Examples

Dry clothes
Even though he was clearly upset, only dry sobs escaped his lips.
He tried to comfort her, but she sat there with dry eyes, refusing to cry.
She received the bad news with dry eyes, her emotions hidden deep inside.
After hours of waiting, the paint was completely dry and ready to touch.
Please store the books in a dry place to prevent them from getting damaged.
The clothes are finally dry after hanging in the sun all afternoon.
During allergy season, she was relieved to only have a dry nose.
If you develop a dry throat, try drinking more water to stay hydrated.
The doctor noted that his dry cough was not producing any phlegm.

Related Terms

  • antonym: productive - (of a cough) accompanied by phlegm or mucus
  • antonym: tearful - (of eyes) full of or shedding tears
  • antonym: wet - covered or saturated with liquid; not dry
