squelcher

noun
electronic circuit suppressing weak signals
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈskwɛltʃər / (AmE), / ˈskwɛltʃə / (BrE)
an electronic circuit that automatically suppresses the audio output of a receiver when the received signal is weaker than a preset threshold, usually to eliminate noise

Examples

Radio squelcher
A squelcher is essential in walkie-talkies to prevent constant static noise.
The technician adjusted the squelcher to improve radio reception quality.
Without a properly set squelcher, the receiver picked up a lot of background noise.
