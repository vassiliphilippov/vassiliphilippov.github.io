reroot

verb
to grow new roots
6.4
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈruːt/ /; / /rə.ˈruːt/ (AmE), /riː.ˈruːt/ /; / /rə.ˈruːt/ (BrE)
to cause something, especially a plant or system, to grow or establish new roots again

Examples

To reroot a plant
Gardeners often reroot cuttings to propagate new plants.
After the storm damaged the tree, experts tried to reroot it in better soil.
You can sometimes reroot a houseplant by placing its stem in water until new roots form.

Related Terms
