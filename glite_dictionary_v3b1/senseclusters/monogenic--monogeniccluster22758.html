monogenic

adjective
controlled by a single gene
6.3 7.0
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑnəˈdʒɛnɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈdʒɛnɪk / (BrE)
(of a trait or disease) controlled by or relating to a single gene or a single pair of genes.

Examples

Monogenic disease
Researchers are studying monogenic forms of diabetes to better understand the genetic basis of the illness.
Some diseases, like sickle cell anemia, are monogenic and result from a mutation in a single gene.
The inheritance pattern of eye color in this species is monogenic, making it easy to predict offspring outcomes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: polygenic - (of a trait) controlled by many genes
