orphan

noun
first line of a paragraph at bottom of a page (typography)
Pronunciation: / ˈɔr.fən / (AmE), / ˈɔː.fən / (BrE)
In typesetting, the first line of a paragraph that appears alone at the bottom of a page or column, separated from the rest of the paragraph.

Examples

A orphan line
Editors often adjust spacing to prevent an orphan from appearing at the bottom of a column.
In print design, a single orphan at the end of a page can disrupt the visual flow of the text.
The book's layout program automatically detects and fixes any orphan lines to improve readability.

Related Terms

  • synonym: widow - the last line of a paragraph appearing alone at the top of a page or column (in typography)
