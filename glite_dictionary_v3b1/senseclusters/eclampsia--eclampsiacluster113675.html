eclampsia

noun
pregnancy condition involving seizures
Pronunciation: / ɪˈklæmpsiə / (AmE), / ɪˈklæmpsiə / (BrE)
A serious medical condition in pregnancy, marked by the onset of seizures or coma, usually following high blood pressure.

Examples

Severe eclampsia symptoms
Early diagnosis is crucial to reduce the risks associated with eclampsia.
Severe headaches and blurred vision can sometimes precede an eclampsia episode.
The doctor monitored her closely for signs of eclampsia during the final weeks of her pregnancy.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: preeclampsia - a related condition in pregnancy with high blood pressure, but without convulsions
