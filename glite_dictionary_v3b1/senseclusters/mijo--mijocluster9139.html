mijo

noun
term of endearment
Pronunciation: / ˈmiːhoʊ / (AmE), / ˈmiːhəʊ / (BrE)
an affectionate term, originally from Spanish, used to address a boy or young man as 'my son' or 'dear boy'

Examples

Sweet mijo
She smiled and said, 'Come here, mijo, let me fix your hair.'
Whenever he visits his grandmother, she greets him warmly as mijo.
In some families, the word mijo is used for any young boy they care about.
