triangulate

verb
divide or shape into triangles
7.3
Pronunciation: / traɪˈæŋɡjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / traɪˈaŋɡjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to divide (something) into triangles or to give it a triangular form, especially for purposes of measurement, construction, or analysis

Examples

To triangulate a canvas
Artists sometimes triangulate a canvas by drawing lines from corner to corner for better composition.
The computer program can triangulate complex shapes to make them easier to analyze.
To create a stable structure, the engineers decided to triangulate the framework.
