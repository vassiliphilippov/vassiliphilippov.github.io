restudy

verb
to study again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈstʌdi / (AmE), / ˌriːˈstʌdi / (BrE)
to study something again, often to improve understanding or remember information

Examples

To restudy the notes
Before the final test, he decided to restudy all his notes from the semester.
The teacher encouraged students to restudy the chapter to prepare for the quiz.
After failing the first attempt, she had to restudy the material more thoroughly.
Before the final exam, he decided to restudy all his notes from the semester.
The teacher encouraged students to restudy the material to ensure they understood the concepts.
She had to restudy the chapter because she missed some important details the first time.

Related Terms

  • antonym: forget - to fail to remember something
  • synonym: review - to go over material again to refresh memory
  • synonym: revise - to reread or study material again, especially for an exam
