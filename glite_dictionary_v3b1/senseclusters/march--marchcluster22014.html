march

noun
border district or frontier region
8.2 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / m…ërt É / (AmE), / m…ëÀêt É / (BrE)
a district located along a border, especially one between countries or regions, often with historical or administrative significance

Examples

A border march
He owned lands in the eastern march, near the hostile border.
Historical records often mention skirmishes along the northern march of the realm.
The nobles controlled the march to defend the kingdom from invasion.

Synonyms

  • marchland
    9.9 0.00/m
    marchland is a historic name for a border region often seen in older texts or maps, while march is an administrative border district.
  • border district
    4.2 0.01/m
    border district refers to an official area along a border used for administration, while march often has historic or regional status.
  • borderland
    5.6 0.69/m
    borderland is a general area near a border, often rural or cultural, while march denotes a defined border district with legal history.

Related Terms

  • synonym: frontier - a border between two countries, especially one that is open or less settled
