precertification

noun
insurance approval process
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˌsɜːrtɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːˌsɜːtɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of obtaining approval from an insurance company before receiving specific medical services or procedures

Examples

Insurance precertification needed
Many insurance plans require precertification before you can be admitted to the hospital.
Without precertification, the insurer may not cover the cost of the procedure.
The doctor's office handled the precertification paperwork for the patient's upcoming surgery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authorization - official permission or approval for something
  • antonym: denial - refusal to approve or allow something
