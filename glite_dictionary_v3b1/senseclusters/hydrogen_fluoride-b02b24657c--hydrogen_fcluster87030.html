hydrogen fluoride

noun
corrosive liquid chemical compound HF
1.0 6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪdrədʒən ˈflʊɚˌaɪd / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdrɪdʒən ˈflʊə.raɪd / (BrE)
a colorless, poisonous, and corrosive liquid compound (HF) used in industry, especially in the production of fluorine compounds; forms hydrofluoric acid when dissolved in water

Examples

Hydrogen fluoride liquid
Exposure to hydrogen fluoride can cause serious burns and systemic health effects.
Industrial facilities must take special precautions when handling hydrogen fluoride due to its extreme toxicity.
When hydrogen fluoride is dissolved in water, it forms hydrofluoric acid, a powerful etching agent.
