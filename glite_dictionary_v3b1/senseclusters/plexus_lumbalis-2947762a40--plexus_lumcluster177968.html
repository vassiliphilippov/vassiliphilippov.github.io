plexus lumbalis

noun
lymphatic network near lower aorta and iliac vessels
Pronunciation: / ˈplɛksəs lʊmˈbeɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˈplɛksəs lʌmˈbeɪlɪs / (BrE)
a network of lymphatic vessels located along the lower portion of the aorta and iliac vessels

Examples

Plexus lumbalis lymph vessels
Damage to the plexus lumbalis can disrupt lymphatic drainage in the lower abdomen.
Surgeons must take care to avoid injury to the plexus lumbalis during abdominal operations.
Swelling in the legs can sometimes be linked to obstruction of the plexus lumbalis.
