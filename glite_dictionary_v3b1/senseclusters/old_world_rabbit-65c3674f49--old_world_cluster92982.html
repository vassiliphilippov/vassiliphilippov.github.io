Old World rabbit

noun
species of burrowing rabbit, ancestor of domestic rabbit
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊld ˈwɝːld ˈræbɪt / (AmE), / ˌəʊld ˈwɜːld ˈræbɪt / (BrE)
a common burrowing mammal (Oryctolagus cuniculus) native to southern Europe and northern Africa, widely introduced and domesticated elsewhere

Examples

The Old World rabbit species
Most pet rabbits are descended from the Old World rabbit rather than other rabbit species.
The Old World rabbit has adapted to a wide range of climates since its introduction to Australia.
Unlike hares, young Old World rabbits are born blind and hairless.

Related Terms

  • synonym: European rabbit - another name for the species Oryctolagus cuniculus
