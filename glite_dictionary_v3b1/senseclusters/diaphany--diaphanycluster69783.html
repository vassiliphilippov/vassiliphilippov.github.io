diaphany

noun
thin translucent material
Pronunciation: / daɪˈæfəni / (AmE), / daɪˈæfəni / (BrE)
a thin, translucent material or object that allows light to pass through, such as gauze or fine fabric

Examples

A silk diaphany
A delicate diaphany hung between the two rooms, shimmering in the afternoon sun.
The artist used a layer of diaphany to create a soft, glowing effect in her painting.
He admired the diaphany of the curtain as it gently filtered the morning light.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gauze - a thin, transparent fabric with a loose open weave
  • antonym: opaque - not able to be seen through; not transparent
  • synonym: veil - a piece of fine material worn to cover the face or as a screen
