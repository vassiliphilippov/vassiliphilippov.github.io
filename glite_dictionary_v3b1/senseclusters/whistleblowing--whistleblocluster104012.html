whistleblowing

noun
reporting wrongdoing
5.6
Pronunciation: /ˈwɪs.əl.bloʊ.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈwɪs.əl.bləʊ.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
the act of reporting illegal, unethical, or improper activities within an organization to authorities or the public

Examples

Whistleblowing on fraud
The company changed its policies after whistleblowing exposed financial misconduct.
Laws protect employees from retaliation for whistleblowing in many countries.
Her act of whistleblowing led to a major investigation into the organization.
The company changed its policies after whistleblowing revealed serious safety violations.
Laws protect employees from retaliation for whistleblowing about illegal activities.
Her whistleblowing led to a major investigation into government corruption.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concealment - the act of hiding or keeping something secret
  • antonym: cover-up - an attempt to prevent the public from discovering information about wrongdoing
  • synonym: reporting - the act of informing authorities about something
  • synonym: whistleblower - a person who reports wrongdoing within an organization
