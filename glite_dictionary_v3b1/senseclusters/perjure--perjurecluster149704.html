perjure

verb
to lie under oath in court
Pronunciation: / ˈpɝːdʒər / (AmE), / ˈpɜːdʒə / (BrE)
to knowingly make a false statement under oath, especially in a court of law

Examples

To perjure oneself
He was warned not to perjure himself during the testimony.
If you perjure yourself in court, you may face prison time for lying under oath.
Witnesses who perjure themselves damage the integrity of the justice system.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lie - to say something untrue with intent to deceive
  • antonym: testify - to give evidence under oath
