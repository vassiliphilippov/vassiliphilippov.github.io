perjure

verb
to lie under oath in court
9.9 0.81/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…ùÀêd í…ôr / (AmE), / Ààp…úÀêd í…ô / (BrE)
to knowingly make a false statement under oath, especially in a court of law

Examples

To perjure oneself
He was warned not to perjure himself during the testimony.
If you perjure yourself in court, you may face prison time for lying under oath.
Witnesses who perjure themselves damage the integrity of the justice system.

Synonyms

  • lying under oath
    8.9 0.43/m
    lying under oath is the criminal act of deliberately telling false facts in court, while perjure is the formal verb used for that crime.
  • bearing false witness
    8.8 0.19/m
    bearing false witness sounds formal and old-fashioned, while perjure is the modern legal verb for lying under oath.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lie - to say something untrue with intent to deceive
  • antonym: testify - to give evidence under oath
