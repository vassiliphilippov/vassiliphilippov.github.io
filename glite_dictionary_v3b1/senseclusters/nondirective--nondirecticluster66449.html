nondirective

adjective
not giving instructions
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑn.dəˈrɛktɪv / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.dɪˈrɛktɪv / (BrE)
not giving explicit instructions or guidance, especially allowing others to make their own decisions or lead the process

Examples

Nondirective style
The therapist adopted a nondirective style, encouraging the client to explore their own feelings.
In a nondirective classroom, students choose their own projects and work at their own pace.
Many counselors believe a nondirective approach helps clients find their own solutions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: directive - giving clear instructions or guidance
  • synonym: permissive - allowing freedom of choice or action
