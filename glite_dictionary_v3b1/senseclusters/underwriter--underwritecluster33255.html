underwriter

noun
person who assesses and sells insurance
5.6 6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈʌn.dər.raɪ.tər/ (AmE), /ˈʌn.də.raɪ.tə/ (BrE)
a person or company that assesses and accepts risk in insurance, usually by approving or selling insurance policies

Examples

Insurance underwriter
As an underwriter, she must assess risk carefully before selling insurance to clients.
Many underwriters work closely with agents to customize coverage for each customer.
The underwriter reviewed my application before approving my health insurance policy.
After reviewing the applicant's information, the underwriter decided to approve the life insurance policy.
Our company works closely with the underwriter to evaluate new insurance products.
The bank acted as the underwriter for several major corporate bonds this year.
