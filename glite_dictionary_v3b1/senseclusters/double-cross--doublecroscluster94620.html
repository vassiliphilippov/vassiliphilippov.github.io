double-cross

verb
betray by pretending loyalty
1.0 5.3
Pronunciation: /dʌb.əl.ˈkrɔːs/ /; / /dʌb.əl.ˈkrɑːs/ (AmE), /dʌb.əl.ˈkrɒs/ (BrE)
to betray someone, especially after pretending to support or work with them

Examples

To double-cross a friend
After promising to help, he double-crossed his allies and gave the secret to their rivals.
He realized too late that his partner would double-cross him for the money.
She warned me not to double-cross the gang or there would be consequences.
