teleologically

adverb
with reference to purpose
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛliəˈlɑdʒɪkli / (AmE), / ˌtɛlɪəˈlɒdʒɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that explains or interprets things by referring to their purpose, goal, or final cause (especially in philosophy or science)

Examples

To think teleologically
The philosopher argued teleologically, suggesting that everything in nature serves a specific purpose.
Some scientists reject explaining evolution teleologically, preferring natural selection as the main cause.
The teacher warned against interpreting historical events teleologically as if they were destined to happen.
The scientist argued teleologically that the structure of the eye suggests it was designed for seeing.
Some philosophers interpret evolution teleologically, as if it is directed toward a specific goal.
The teacher explained the process teleologically, focusing on its intended outcome rather than its mechanism.
The scientist explained the animal's behavior teleologically, suggesting it was aimed at survival.
Some philosophers argue that history unfolds teleologically, moving toward a specific end.
The project was managed teleologically, with every step designed to achieve the final objective.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mechanistically - in a way that explains events by physical or mechanical causes, not purpose
  • synonym: purposively - in a way that shows intention or purpose
  • antonym: randomly - in a way that lacks purpose or plan
