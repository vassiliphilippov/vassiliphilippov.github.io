suppress

verb
restrain from showing emotion or impulse
Pronunciation: / səˈprɛs / (AmE), / səˈprɛs / (BrE)
to consciously hold back or restrain an emotion, desire, thought, or reaction

Examples

To suppress feelings
He struggled to suppress his excitement when he heard the good news.
It can be unhealthy to continually suppress your true feelings.
She tried to suppress her laughter during the serious meeting.
He tried to suppress his laughter during the serious meeting.
Scientists are developing drugs to suppress the growth of harmful bacteria.
The medication can suppress a cough for several hours.
Despite her efforts to suppress her fear, her hands were shaking.
He managed to suppress his feelings of guilt long enough to finish his speech.
She tried hard to suppress the unpleasant memory, but it kept resurfacing in her mind.

Related Terms

  • antonym: express - to show or make known an emotion or feeling
  • synonym: inhibit - to hinder or restrain the process or action of something
  • synonym: repress - to restrain or hold back thoughts or feelings
  • synonym: restrain - to hold back or control an emotion or impulse
