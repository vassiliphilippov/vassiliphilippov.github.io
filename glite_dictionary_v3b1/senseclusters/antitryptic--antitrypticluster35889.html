antitryptic

noun
enzyme inhibitor
Pronunciation: / ˌæntaɪˈtrɪptɪk / (AmE), / ˌæntɪˈtrɪptɪk / (BrE)
a substance that inhibits the activity of trypsin or similar protein-digesting enzymes

Examples

An antitryptic substance
The laboratory used an antitryptic to prevent the breakdown of proteins during the experiment.
Adding an antitryptic can help preserve tissue samples for further analysis.
Researchers tested the effectiveness of a new antitryptic in blocking enzyme activity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: protease inhibitor - a substance that blocks the activity of protease enzymes
  • antonym: trypsin - a digestive enzyme that breaks down proteins
