ripening

noun
process of becoming mature (esp. fruit/crops)
Pronunciation: / ˈraɪ.pən.ɪŋ /; / ˈraɪpənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈraɪ.pən.ɪŋ /; / ˈraɪpənɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of becoming fully mature or developing desirable qualities, especially in fruit or crops

Examples

The ripening of fruit
Farmers monitor the ripening of wheat closely to determine the best time for harvest.
Proper ripening ensures that the cheese develops its characteristic flavor and texture.
The ripening of the grapes was delayed by the unusually cold spring.
Experts monitor the ripening of the fruit to ensure the best flavor.
Proper ripening is essential for developing the rich taste of certain wines.
The ripening of the cheese occurs over several months in a controlled environment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decay - the process of rotting or deteriorating after ripening
  • synonym: maturation - the process of maturing or developing fully
