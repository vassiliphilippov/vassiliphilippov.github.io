perfumy

adjective
superficially elegant
Pronunciation: / pərˈfjuːmi / (AmE), / pəˈfjuːmi / (BrE)
seeming elegant or attractive in a superficial or artificial way, lacking real depth or substance

Examples

Perfumy movie style
The film was visually stunning but felt a bit perfumy and lacked emotional depth.
Her speech sounded perfumy, full of elegant words but little real meaning.
Some critics found the restaurant's decor perfumy, prioritizing style over comfort.

Related Terms

  • synonym: showy - attractive in a way that is superficial or meant to impress
  • antonym: substantial - having real worth or importance
