bored

adjective
feeling uninterested or weary
1.0
Pronunciation: / bɔrd / (AmE), / bɔːd / (BrE)
feeling uninterested, tired, or impatient because something is not interesting or because of doing the same thing too much

Examples

To feel bored easily
After so many visits, he was bored by the museum's displays.
She looked bored during the long and repetitive meeting.
The student's bored expression made it clear he had heard this lecture before.
As the hours dragged on, he grew increasingly bored with the story.
Many children become bored if there is nothing interesting to do on rainy days.
She looked bored during the long meeting and kept glancing at her watch.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blase - unimpressed or indifferent because of frequent exposure
  • antonym: interested - showing curiosity or concern about something
  • synonym: uninterested - not interested; indifferent or having no curiosity
