transition

noun
connecting passage or segment
2.1
Pronunciation: / trænˈzɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / trænˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
a passage or phrase that connects one topic, part, or section to another, especially in speech, writing, or music

Examples

Transition sentence
A musical transition linked the first movement to the second seamlessly.
The writer used a smooth transition between paragraphs to maintain the flow of the argument.
Without a clear transition, the audience struggled to follow the speaker's change of topic.
After a brief transition, the orchestra entered a new, brighter key.
The transition section connects the main theme to the development in the sonata.
The composer used a transition to elegantly move from C major to G minor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: modulation - change from one key to another in music
  • synonym: segue - a smooth transition from one topic or section to another
