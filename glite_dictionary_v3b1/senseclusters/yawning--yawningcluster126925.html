yawning

adjective
mouth open, showing boredom or tiredness
Pronunciation: / ˈjɔnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈjɔːnɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person or group) having the mouth wide open, or showing boredom or lack of attention (often by yawning)

Examples

Yawning audience
A yawning crowd waited impatiently for the event to begin.
She glanced at her yawning coworkers and suggested they take a break.
The students sat in a yawning lecture hall, struggling to stay awake.
A yawning silence filled the room during the dull presentation.
The speaker tried to energize the yawning audience with a joke.
The teacher noticed a yawning class and decided to change her strategy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: attentive - paying close attention
  • synonym: bored - feeling weary and uninterested
  • synonym: sleepy - feeling drowsy or ready for sleep
