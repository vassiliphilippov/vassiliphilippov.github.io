cannel coal

noun
bituminous coal that burns with a bright flame
9.9 8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈkæn.əl koʊl/ (AmE), /ˈkæn.əl kəʊl/ (BrE)
A type of bituminous coal that burns with a bright, luminous flame and was once valued for gas production and illumination.

Examples

Bright cannel coal
Early gasworks often used cannel coal to produce illuminating gas for city lighting.
The high oil content in cannel coal made it especially valuable before the widespread use of petroleum.
The lamps were fueled with cannel coal to provide a steady, bright light.
