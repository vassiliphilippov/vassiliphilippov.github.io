cauliflower ear

noun
deformed ear caused by repeated injury
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔlɪˌflaʊɚ ɪr / (AmE), / ˈkɒlɪˌflaʊə ɪə / (BrE)
A permanent deformity of the outer ear caused by repeated trauma, resulting in a swollen, lumpy appearance resembling a cauliflower; common among boxers and wrestlers.

Examples

A cauliflower ear injury
Doctors recommend early treatment to prevent permanent cauliflower ear after injuries.
He wore protective headgear to avoid developing cauliflower ear during training.
The wrestler's cauliflower ear was a sign of years spent on the mat.
