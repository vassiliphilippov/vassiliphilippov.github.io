condemnation

noun
final guilty judgment and punishment (law)
4.1 5.9
Pronunciation: /kɑːn.dəm.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ /; / /kən.dəm.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /kɒn.dəm.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ /; / /kɒn.dɛm.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
(in criminal law) a final judgment declaring someone guilty in a criminal case and the punishment imposed.

Examples

Court condemnation
After the lengthy trial, the condemnation of the defendant was read out in court.
His lawyer plans to appeal the condemnation on the grounds of insufficient evidence.
The judge’s condemnation included a lengthy prison sentence and a heavy fine.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acquittal - a judgment that a person is not guilty of the crime with which they have been charged
  • synonym: conviction - the act of officially finding someone guilty of a crime in a court of law
