factory farming

noun
industrialized large-scale animal farming
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈfæk.tə.ri ˌfɑr.mɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfæk.təri ˌfɑː.mɪŋ / (BrE)
a system of large-scale, industrialized agriculture in which animals are raised intensively for food production, often resulting in concerns about animal welfare and environmental impact

Examples

Factory farming animals
Documentaries on factory farming have influenced public attitudes toward animal welfare.
Many critics argue that factory farming contributes to environmental pollution and antibiotic resistance.
She prefers to buy eggs from farms that reject factory farming practices.

Related Terms

  • antonym: free-range farming - farming in which animals are allowed to roam freely outdoors
  • synonym: intensive farming - agriculture using intensive methods to maximize yield
