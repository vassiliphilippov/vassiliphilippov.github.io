lobar pneumonia

noun
pneumonia affecting one or more lung lobes
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊˌbɑr nuˈmoʊniə / (AmE), / ˈləʊˌbɑː njuːˈməʊniə / (BrE)
A type of pneumonia that affects one or more lobes of the lung, usually caused by bacterial infection, especially by streptococci.

Examples

Lobar pneumonia infection
Children are particularly susceptible to lobar pneumonia during the winter months.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with lobar pneumonia after reviewing the chest X-ray.
Treatment of lobar pneumonia typically involves antibiotics to target the most common bacteria involved.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pneumonia - lung infection causing inflammation and fluid in the lungs
