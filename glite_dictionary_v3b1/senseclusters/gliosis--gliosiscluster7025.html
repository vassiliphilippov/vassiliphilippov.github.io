gliosis

noun
glial cell increase
9.8
Pronunciation: /glaɪ.ˈoʊ.sɪs/ (AmE), /glaɪ.ˈəʊ.sɪs/ (BrE)
an abnormal increase in glial cells in the brain or spinal cord, usually as a response to injury

Examples

Brain gliosis after injury
The neurologist explained that gliosis can occur after a traumatic brain injury.
Chronic inflammation in the spinal cord may lead to gliosis over time.
Researchers are studying how gliosis affects recovery after a stroke.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scarring - the formation of fibrous tissue after injury
