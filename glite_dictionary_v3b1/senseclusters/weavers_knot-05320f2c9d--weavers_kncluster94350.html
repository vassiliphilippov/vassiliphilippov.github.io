weaver's knot

noun
a knot for joining ropes temporarily
8.4 7.1
Pronunciation: /ˈwiː.vərz nɑːt/ (AmE), /ˈwiː.vəz nɒt/ (BrE)
a knot used for temporarily tying a rope to the middle of another rope or to an eye, often used in weaving and fishing

Examples

The weaver's knot is commonly used to join two threads when weaving cloth.
To repair the broken fishing line, he tied a weaver's knot to reconnect the ends.
