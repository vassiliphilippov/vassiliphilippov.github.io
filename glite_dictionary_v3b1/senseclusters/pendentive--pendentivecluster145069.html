pendentive

noun
dome-supporting section
Pronunciation: / pɛnˈdɛntɪv / (AmE), / pɛnˈdɛntɪv / (BrE)
a curved triangular section of masonry that supports a dome over a square space or room

Examples

Dome pendentive
The architect designed the dome to rest on four large pendentives at each corner of the room.
Restoration work revealed intricate mosaics decorating the surface of each pendentive in the ancient church.
Without a pendentive, it would be difficult to place a round dome over a square hall.

Related Terms

  • synonym: squinch - an architectural device used as a transition from a square to a dome
