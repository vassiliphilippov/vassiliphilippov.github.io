Santeria

noun
Afro-Caribbean religion
Pronunciation: / ˌsæn.təˈriː.ə / (AmE), / ˌsæn.təˈriː.ə / (BrE)
a religion that blends West African Yoruba traditions with Roman Catholicism and indigenous Caribbean beliefs, especially practiced in Cuba and the Caribbean

Examples

Santeria religion
Many families in Cuba celebrate important events with rituals from Santeria.
She became interested in the history and practices of Santeria after visiting the Caribbean.
Some music and dance traditions in the region are influenced by Santeria ceremonies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Candomblé - an Afro-Brazilian religion blending African beliefs with Catholicism
  • synonym: Vodou - a syncretic religion practiced chiefly in Haiti, combining West African and Catholic elements
