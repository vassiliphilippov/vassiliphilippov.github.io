forecaddie

noun
golf ball spotter
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔːrkædi / (AmE), / ˈfɔːkædi / (BrE)
a person who stands ahead on a golf course to watch and indicate where golf balls land

Examples

Fairway forecaddie
The forecaddie raised his flag to show where the ball had landed on the fairway.
During the tournament, a forecaddie helped the players keep track of their shots.
Without a forecaddie, it would have been difficult to find the ball in the tall grass.

Related Terms

  • synonym: caddie - a person who carries a golfer's clubs and gives advice
