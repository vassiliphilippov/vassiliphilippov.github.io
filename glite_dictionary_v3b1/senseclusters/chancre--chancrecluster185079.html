chancre

noun
painless sore (esp. in syphilis)
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈʃæŋkɚ / (AmE), / ˈʃæŋkə / (BrE)
A painless, firm sore or ulcer that typically appears at the site of infection in the early stage of syphilis or certain other diseases.

Examples

A chancre sore
A painless chancre may go unnoticed but is an important sign of early syphilis.
He was diagnosed after a chancre appeared at the site of infection.
The doctor identified a chancre on the patient's lip, suggesting a primary syphilitic infection.
