inbounds

noun
returning ball to play
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌbaʊndz / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌbaʊndz / (BrE)
a pass, throw, or play that returns the ball to play from out of bounds in sports such as basketball or football

Examples

Inbounds pass
The team practiced their inbounds plays to improve their scoring opportunities.
After the timeout, she made a quick inbounds to her teammate under the basket.
A well-executed inbounds can catch the defense off guard in a close game.

Related Terms

  • antonym: out of bounds - the area outside the playing field
  • synonym: throw-in - a method of returning the ball to play from out of bounds
