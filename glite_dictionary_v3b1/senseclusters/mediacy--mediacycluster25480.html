mediacy

noun
the state of being mediate or indirect
Pronunciation: / ˈmiːdiəsi / (AmE), / ˈmiːdiəsi / (BrE)
the quality or condition of being mediate; the state of acting as an intermediate or means, rather than directly.

Examples

The mediacy of camera
Photography engages with reality but always through the mediacy of the camera lens.
Some philosophers argue that knowledge always involves some form of mediacy.
The mediacy of language allows us to express complex ideas indirectly.
