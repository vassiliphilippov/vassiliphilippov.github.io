break into

verb
suddenly begin an activity or new pace
2.4
Pronunciation: / breɪk ˈɪntu / (AmE), / breɪk ˈɪntu / (BrE)
to suddenly begin doing something, especially to start moving or behaving in a different way (such as starting a new pace or activity)

Examples

To break into a run
As the music changed, the dancers broke into a fast waltz.
When the director arrived, the actors broke into rehearsal immediately.
Without warning, the dog broke into a run across the park.
