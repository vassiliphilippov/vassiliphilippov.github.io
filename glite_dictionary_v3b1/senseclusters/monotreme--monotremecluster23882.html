monotreme

noun
egg-laying primitive mammal (e.g. platypus, echidna)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːnəˌtrim / (AmE), / ˈmɒnətriːm / (BrE)
a primitive mammal of the order Monotremata, such as the platypus or echidna, that lays eggs instead of giving birth to live young

Examples

A monotreme animal
Scientists study monotremes to learn more about the evolution of mammals.
The platypus is perhaps the most famous living monotreme in Australia.
Unlike other mammals, monotremes lay eggs instead of giving birth to live young.

Related Terms

  • antonym: marsupial - a mammal with a pouch, like a kangaroo or koala
  • antonym: placental - a mammal whose young develop inside the uterus connected by a placenta
