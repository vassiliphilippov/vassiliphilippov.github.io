now-nowism

noun
focus on immediate concerns; urgency
Pronunciation: / ˈnaʊ ˌnaʊˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈnaʊ ˌnaʊˌɪzəm / (BrE)
an attitude characterized by focusing on immediate concerns or demanding urgent action, often neglecting long-term planning

Examples

Now-nowism attitude
Critics say now-nowism is harming our ability to address long-term challenges.
Her impatience reflected a strong sense of now-nowism throughout the meeting.
The team's now-nowism led to hasty decisions that caused problems later.

Related Terms

  • synonym: immediacy - the quality of bringing one into direct and instant involvement with something
  • synonym: short-termism - concentration on short-term projects or objectives for immediate profit at the expense of long-term security
