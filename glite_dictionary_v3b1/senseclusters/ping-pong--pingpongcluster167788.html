ping-pong

verb
send or alternate back and forth (informal)
Pronunciation: / \u02c8p\u026a\u014b p\u0251\u014b /; / \u02c8p\u026a\u014b p\u0254\u014b / (AmE), / \u02c8p\u026a\u014b p\u0252\u014b / (BrE)
to move, send, or alternate something rapidly back and forth between people, options, or positions (like the ball in the game of ping-pong)

Examples

To ping-pong ideas
The committee began to ping-pong between two proposals, delaying a final vote for weeks.
She ping-ponged the contract back and forth until both lawyers agreed on the wording.
Caught between tasks, he started ping-ponging assignments all afternoon and finished none.
During the meeting, they ping-ponged between budget proposals until no decision was reached.
Emails ping-ponged all afternoon as the client and designer traded quick suggestions.
She kept ping-ponging her ideas between the two committees to find common ground.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alternate - to switch repeatedly between two or more people, things, or options
  • synonym: bounce - to move up, down, or back and forth after hitting a surface; used metaphorically for back-and-forth exchange
  • synonym: bounce (back and forth) - to be passed repeatedly between people or places
  • synonym: oscillate - to move or swing repeatedly between two points or states; to alternate
