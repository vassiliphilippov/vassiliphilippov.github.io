gumption

noun
practical judgment and initiative
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɡʌmpʃən / (AmE), / ˈɡʌmpʃən / (BrE)
the ability to make sensible decisions and act with courage, determination, and resourcefulness

Examples

Show gumption
He admired her gumption for standing up to the unfair policy.
If you have the gumption to ask for help, you'll learn much faster.
She showed remarkable gumption in starting her own business at such a young age.
Despite his age, he had enough gumption to climb the mountain.
It takes gumption to stand up to your boss when something is wrong.
She showed real gumption in starting her own business from scratch.

Related Terms

  • synonym: courage - The ability to do something that frightens one; bravery
  • synonym: fortitude - Strength of mind that enables a person to face adversity bravely
  • synonym: initiative - the ability to assess and initiate things independently
  • synonym: sense - practical intelligence or sound judgment
  • antonym: timidity - Lack of courage or confidence
