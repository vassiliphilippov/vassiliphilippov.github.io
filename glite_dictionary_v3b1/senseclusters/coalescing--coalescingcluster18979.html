coalescing

adjective
merging adjacent memory blocks (computing)
7.0
Pronunciation: /koʊ.ə.ˈles.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /kəʊ.ə.ˈles.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
(in computing) merging adjacent free memory blocks or data fragments into a single contiguous block to reduce fragmentation and improve allocation efficiency.

Examples

Coalescing memory blocks
A coalescing memory allocator reduces fragmentation by merging adjacent free blocks.
During garbage collection, the system performs coalescing to combine contiguous free spaces into larger segments.
Developers noticed poor performance until the coalescing feature was enabled in the allocator settings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defragment - to reorganize storage to reduce fragmentation and make data contiguous
  • synonym: merge - to combine two or more things into a single unit
