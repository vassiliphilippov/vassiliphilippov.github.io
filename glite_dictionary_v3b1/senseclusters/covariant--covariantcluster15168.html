covariant

adjective
changing to preserve a relationship
Pronunciation: / ˌkoʊˈvɛriənt / (AmE), / kəʊˈveəriənt / (BrE)
(of a quantity or function) changing in such a way that the form of its relationship with another quantity or set of quantities remains unchanged.

Examples

Covariant mathematical form
A mathematical law is sometimes formulated in a covariant form so it holds in all reference frames.
In general relativity, tensors are required to be covariant under coordinate transformations.
The physicist explained how a covariant derivative differs from an ordinary derivative.
