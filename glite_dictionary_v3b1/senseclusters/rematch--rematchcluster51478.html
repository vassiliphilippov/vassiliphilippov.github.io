rematch

verb
play a second contest
Pronunciation: / ˈriˌmætʃ / (AmE), / ˈriːˌmatʃ / (BrE)
To play or arrange a second or subsequent contest or game between the same opponents; to meet again in competition.

Examples

To rematch the rivals
After the controversial call, the teams agreed to rematch the game next month to settle the rivalry.
They rematched last season, and the defending champions won by a narrow two-point margin.
Fans hoped the singers would rematch their earlier duet with a new arrangement at the awards.

Related Terms

  • synonym: replay - to play or show something again; used of games or events that occur again
