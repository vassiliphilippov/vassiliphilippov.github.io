plucked

adjective
bird or animal with feathers removed
Pronunciation: / plʌkt / (AmE), / plʌkt / (BrE)
(of a bird or animal) having had its feathers removed, especially in preparation for cooking.

Examples

Plucked chicken
At the market, you can buy either plucked or unplucked geese, depending on your needs.
She learned how to properly process a plucked duck before making her grandmother’s soup recipe.
The chef placed the plucked chicken on the cutting board to prepare it for roasting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unplucked - (of a bird) not having had its feathers removed
