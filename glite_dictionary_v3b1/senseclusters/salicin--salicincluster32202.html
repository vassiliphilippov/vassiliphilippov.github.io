salicin

noun
compound from willow bark; anti-inflammatory
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsælɪsɪn / (AmE), / ˈsælɪsɪn / (BrE)
A bitter, crystalline compound obtained from the bark of willows, used as an anti-inflammatory agent and precursor to aspirin.

Examples

Salicin from bark
After isolating salicin from willow bark, scientists developed modern aspirin.
Extracts containing salicin are sometimes used in herbal medicine as natural remedies.
The pain relief properties of salicin have been known since ancient times.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aspirin - a common pain-relieving and anti-inflammatory drug derived from salicin
