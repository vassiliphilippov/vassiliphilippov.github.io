Sinicization

noun
adoption of Chinese culture
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪnɪsəˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌsaɪnɪsɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪnɪsaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which people, societies, institutions, or customs become more Chinese in culture, language, or political norms

Examples

Sinicization of culture
The Sinicization of the region led to changes in local architecture and traditions.
Government policies encouraged the Sinicization of minority groups in the area.
Historians debate the long-term effects of Sinicization on language and education.
The Sinicization of local customs was encouraged by the authorities to promote national unity.
Many schools underwent Sinicization by adopting Mandarin as the primary language of instruction.
Religious organizations faced pressure to comply with Sinicization policies in recent years.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Westernization - the process of adopting Western culture or ideas
  • synonym: acculturation - the process of adopting the cultural traits of another group
  • synonym: assimilation - the process of becoming similar to something else, especially culturally
