Sinicization

noun
adoption of Chinese culture
9.9
Pronunciation: /sɪ.nɪ.sɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /sɪ.nɪ.saɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /sɪ.nɪ.sɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /sɪ.nɪ.saɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process by which people, societies, institutions, or customs become more Chinese in culture, language, or political norms

Examples

Sinicization of culture
The Sinicization of the region led to changes in local architecture and traditions.
Government policies encouraged the Sinicization of minority groups in the area.

Religious organizations faced pressure to comply with Sinicization policies in recent years.
