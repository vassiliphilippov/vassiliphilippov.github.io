cytopathological

adjective
cell changes from disease
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪtoʊˌpæθəˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌsaɪtəʊˌpæθəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to changes in cells caused by disease, or the study of such changes

Examples

Cytopathological changes
The laboratory report described several cytopathological features in the tissue sample.
Doctors rely on cytopathological analysis to diagnose certain types of cancer.
A cytopathological examination revealed abnormal changes in the patient's liver cells.
A cytopathological examination can help detect early signs of cancer in tissue samples.
The laboratory specializes in cytopathological testing for infectious diseases.
Doctors reviewed the cytopathological findings before making a diagnosis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cytological - relating to the study of cells
  • synonym: histopathological - relating to the study of diseased tissue
  • antonym: normal - not showing signs of disease or abnormality
  • synonym: pathological - relating to or caused by disease
