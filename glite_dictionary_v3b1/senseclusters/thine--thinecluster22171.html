thine

determiner
archaic 'your'
Pronunciation: / ðaɪn / (AmE), / ðaɪn / (BrE)
archaic form of 'your', used before a noun, especially one starting with a vowel sound

Examples

Thine own eyes
Take thine umbrella with thee, for it may rain today.
Let not thine heart be troubled by the words of others.
I have read thine letter and understand thy concerns.

Related Terms

  • synonym: thy - archaic form of 'your', used before consonant sounds
  • synonym: your - modern possessive determiner for the second person singular or plural
