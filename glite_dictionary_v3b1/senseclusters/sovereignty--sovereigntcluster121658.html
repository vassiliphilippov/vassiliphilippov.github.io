sovereignty

noun
supreme royal or governing authority
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑːv(ə)rnti, ˈsɑːvrənti /; / ˈsɑvrənti /; / ˈsɑːvrənti /; / ˈsɑvərənti / (AmE), / ˈsɒv(ə)rɪnti, ˈsɒvrənti /; / ˈsɒvərənti /; / ˈsɒvrənti / (BrE)
supreme power or authority, especially the power of a state or government to govern itself or its affairs without outside interference

Examples

National sovereignty
After the revolution, the people's sovereignty replaced that of the monarchy.
The king's sovereignty was unquestioned throughout the land for decades.
The treaty recognized the full sovereignty of the nation over its territories.
International law often debates issues of sovereignty when one state interferes in another's affairs.
The colonial power exercised sovereignty over its overseas territories for centuries.
The treaty transferred sovereignty of the region from one country to another.
Debates over national sovereignty often arise in the context of international organizations.
Many countries fought hard to gain their sovereignty from colonial powers.
The treaty affirmed the sovereignty of each nation over its territory.

Related Terms

  • synonym: autonomy - the right or condition of self-government
  • antonym: dependency - a territory that is subject to the authority of another state
  • synonym: jurisdiction - the official power to make legal decisions and judgments in a particular area or over particular people or things
  • antonym: subjugation - the act of bringing under control; conquest
