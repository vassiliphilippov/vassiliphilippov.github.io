fan

verb
blow away with air
3.7
Pronunciation: / fæn / (AmE), / fæn / (BrE)
to move air or blow something away by waving a fan or similar object

Examples

To fan the air
He used a piece of paper to fan the smoke away from his face.
She fanned the dust off the old book with her hand.
The farmer fanned the grains to separate the chaff.
He fanned his face with his hat, hoping to cool down.
She used a magazine to fan herself in the heat.
The servant began to fan the embers to get the fire going.

Related Terms

  • synonym: waft - to cause something, such as air or a smell, to go gently through the air
  • synonym: winnow - to blow a current of air through grain to remove the chaff
