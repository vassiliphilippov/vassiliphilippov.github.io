rin

noun
old Japanese coin
Pronunciation: / rɪn / (AmE), / rɪn / (BrE)
a small Japanese coin that was formerly used as currency, equal to one-thousandth of a yen

Examples

A rin coin
In the early 20th century, a single rin could buy a small piece of candy.
Collectors sometimes search for rare rin coins at antique markets in Japan.
The price was so low that it only cost one rin to send a postcard.
