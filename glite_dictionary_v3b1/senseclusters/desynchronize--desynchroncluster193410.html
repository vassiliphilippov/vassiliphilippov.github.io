desynchronize

verb
cause to lose synchronization
Pronunciation: / diˈsɪŋkrəˌnaɪz /; / diːˈsɪŋkrəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / diːˈsɪŋ.krə.naɪz /; / diːˈsɪŋkrənaɪz / (BrE)
to cause to lose synchronization or to occur at different or unrelated times

Examples

To desynchronize audio and video
If the audio and video are out of sync, a technician will need to desynchronize and then re-sync them.
Jet lag can desynchronize your internal clock from the local time.
Network errors may desynchronize the time across computers, causing serious issues.
Jet lag can desynchronise a person's internal body clock from local time.
Software errors may desynchronise the audio and video tracks in a recording.
The two clocks began to desynchronise after a power outage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: synchronise - to cause to occur at the same time; to make simultaneous or coordinated
  • antonym: synchronize - cause to occur at the same time or rate
