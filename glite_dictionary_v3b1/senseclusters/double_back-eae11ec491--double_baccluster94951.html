double back

verb
to retrace one's steps
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌb.əl ˈbæk /; / ˈdʌbəl bæk / (AmE), / ˈdʌb.əl ˈbæk /; / ˈdʌbəl bæk / (BrE)
to turn around and go back the way you came, retracing your steps

Examples

To double back quickly
If you see a dead end, just double back and try another route.
She realized she had lost her keys, so she quickly doubled back to the office.
We missed the trail marker and had to double back to find it.
He forgot his wallet at home and needed to double back to get it.
The hikers doubled back when they saw the fork in the path.
We realized we'd gone too far and had to double back to find the right trail.
