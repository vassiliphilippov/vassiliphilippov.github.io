Shiah Islam

noun
the second major branch of Islam
5.8
Pronunciation: / ʃiːˈɑː ɪsˈlɑːm / (AmE), / ʃiːˈɑː ɪsˈlɑːm / (BrE)
One of the two main branches of Islam, characterized by the belief in the leadership of Ali and his descendants; particularly prominent in Iran and parts of Iraq, Lebanon, and elsewhere.

Examples

Shiah Islam beliefs
Iran is widely known as a center for Shiah Islam in the modern world.
Many followers of Shiah Islam observe the holiday of Ashura with unique religious rituals.
The history of Shiah Islam is closely tied to the events following the death of Prophet Muhammad.

Related Terms
