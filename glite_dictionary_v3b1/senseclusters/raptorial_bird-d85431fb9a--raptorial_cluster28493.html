raptorial bird

noun
bird of prey
5.9
Pronunciation: / ræpˈtɔriəl bɝd / (AmE), / ræpˈtɔːriəl bɜːd / (BrE)
a bird of prey; any bird that captures and kills live animals for food

Examples

A raptorial bird hunting
A raptorial bird uses its sharp talons to catch small mammals and reptiles.
Researchers observed several species of raptorial birds nesting along the cliffs.
The eagle is a well-known raptorial bird that soars high while searching for prey.
