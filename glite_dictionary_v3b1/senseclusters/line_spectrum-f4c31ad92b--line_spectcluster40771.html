line spectrum

noun
spectrum with discrete lines at specific wavelengths
2.6 5.5
Pronunciation: /laɪn ˈspek.trəm/ (AmE), /laɪn ˈspek.trəm/ (BrE)
A spectrum consisting of discrete lines at specific wavelengths, produced when atoms or ions emit or absorb light at particular energies.

Examples

The line spectrum of stars
Astronomers use the line spectrum of stars to determine their chemical composition.
The emission of sodium vapor produces a distinctive line spectrum that scientists can use to identify the element.
Unlike a continuous spectrum, a line spectrum consists of bright lines separated by dark spaces.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absorption spectrum - a spectrum showing dark lines due to absorption of specific wavelengths
  • antonym: continuous spectrum - a spectrum that shows all wavelengths without interruption
