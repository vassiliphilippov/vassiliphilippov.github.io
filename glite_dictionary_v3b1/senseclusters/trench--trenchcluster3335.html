trench

noun
deep depression in the ocean floor
6.6 5.5
Pronunciation: /trentʃ/ (AmE), /trentʃ/ (BrE)
a long, deep, steep-sided depression in the ocean floor, typically formed by the subduction of one tectonic plate beneath another

Examples

An ocean trench
Scientists are studying the unique ecosystems found in the oceanic trench.
The Mariana trench is the deepest part of the world's oceans.
Using sonar mapping, they discovered an uncharted trench along the Pacific floor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ridge - an elongated raised section on the ocean floor
  • synonym: rift - a long, narrow fissure in Earth's surface
