makara

noun
mythical sea creature
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːkərə / (AmE), / ˈmʌkərə / (BrE)
a mythical sea creature in Hindu and Buddhist tradition, often depicted as a water monster with features of various animals

Examples

Mythical makara statue
The ancient sculpture features a makara with the body of a fish and the trunk of an elephant.
In many temples, the entrance is guarded by a carved makara symbolizing protection.
Artists often depict the makara as a combination of aquatic and terrestrial animals in traditional art.
