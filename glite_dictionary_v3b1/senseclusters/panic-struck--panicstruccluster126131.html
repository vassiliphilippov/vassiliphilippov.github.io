panic-struck

adjective
suddenly seized by panic
Pronunciation: / ˈpænɪkˌstrʌk / (AmE), / ˈpænɪkˌstrʌk / (BrE)
Suddenly overcome by a strong feeling of fear or anxiety; seized by panic.

Examples

A panic-struck crowd
As the fire alarm sounded, the panic-struck crowd rushed toward the exit.
His panic-struck expression told me he had just remembered the deadline.
She appeared panic-struck when she realized her child was missing in the busy store.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - not affected by panic or fear; peaceful and composed
  • synonym: panic-stricken - overwhelmed by sudden and intense fear or anxiety
