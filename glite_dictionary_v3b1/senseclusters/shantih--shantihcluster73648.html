Sanskrit for peace
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɑːnti / (AmE), / ˈʃɑːnti / (BrE)
a Sanskrit word meaning peace, often used at the end of prayers or poems to invoke calm and tranquility

Examples

Shantih at prayer's end
The monk softly chanted shantih at the end of the meditation session.
T. S. Eliot's poem concludes with the word shantih repeated three times.
Many people say shantih as a blessing for peace and harmony.

Related Terms

  • synonym: peace - a state of calm and absence of conflict
  • antonym: war - a state of armed conflict or hostility
