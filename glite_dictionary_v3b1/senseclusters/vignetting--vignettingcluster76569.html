vignetting

noun
darkened image edges
7.8
Pronunciation: / vɪˈnjɛtɪŋ /; / vɪnˈjɛtɪŋ / (AmE), / vɪˈnjɛtɪŋ /; / vɪnˈjɛtɪŋ / (BrE)
a visual effect in photography or graphics where the edges of an image are darker or blurred, often to highlight the center

Examples

Vignetting effect in photos
The photographer noticed vignetting in the corners of his pictures after using a new lens.
Some editing software can correct vignetting to make the image appear more evenly lit.
Intentional vignetting can draw attention to the center of a photograph.
The photographer used vignetting to draw the viewer's eye toward the center of the picture.
You can add vignetting in most photo editing software to create a dramatic effect.
Subtle vignetting around the edges made the portrait look more artistic.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blurring - making the edges or details of an image less clear
  • antonym: highlighting - making parts of an image brighter
  • synonym: shading - a gradual darkening in an image or drawing
