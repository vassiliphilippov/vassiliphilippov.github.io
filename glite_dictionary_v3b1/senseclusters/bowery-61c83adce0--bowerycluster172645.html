Bowery

proper noun
Manhattan street known for cheap hotels, poverty
Pronunciation: / ˈbaʊəri / (AmE), / ˈbaʊəri / (BrE)
A street in Manhattan, New York City, historically known for its cheap hotels and association with poverty and homelessness.

Examples

The Bowery street
In the past, the Bowery was infamous for its flophouses and transient population.
Many social reformers worked to improve conditions on the Bowery during the early twentieth century.
Tourists visiting New York are often unaware of the Bowery's complicated social history.
