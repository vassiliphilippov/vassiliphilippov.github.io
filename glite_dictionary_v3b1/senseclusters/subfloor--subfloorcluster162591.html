subfloor

noun
layer under flooring
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌflɔr / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌflɔː / (BrE)
a layer of material or structure beneath the finished floor in a building, providing support and stability

Examples

The subfloor layer
The carpenter checked the subfloor for any signs of moisture before laying the hardwood.
A damaged subfloor can cause problems with the new tiles if not repaired properly.
They installed a new subfloor to ensure the room would be level and stable.
The workers repaired the damaged subfloor before installing new hardwood planks.
Moisture can cause the subfloor to rot if it is not properly sealed.
After removing the carpet, they discovered the subfloor was uneven and needed leveling.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flooring - the finished surface layer of a floor
  • synonym: underlayment - a material placed under flooring for support or insulation
