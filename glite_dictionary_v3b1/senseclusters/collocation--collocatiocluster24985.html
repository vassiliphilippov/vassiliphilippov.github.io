collocation

noun
habitual word combination
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːloʊˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒləʊˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a habitual combination of words that commonly occur together in a language

Examples

Common collocation in English
In linguistics, a collocation refers to words that frequently appear together.
Strong collocations are important to learn for more fluent speech and writing.
The collocation 'make a decision' is more natural than 'do a decision' in English.

Related Terms

  • synonym: phrase - a small group of words standing together as a conceptual unit
