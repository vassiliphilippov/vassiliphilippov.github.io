appressorium

noun
fungal attachment cell
Pronunciation: / ˌæprəˈsɔːriəm / (AmE), / ˌæprəˈsɔːriəm / (BrE)
a specialized cell in certain fungi that helps the fungus attach to and penetrate the surface of a host

Examples

Fungal appressorium cell
The fungus develops an appressorium to break through the plant's outer layer.
Researchers observed the formation of appressoria under the microscope during the infection process.
Without a functional appressorium, the fungus cannot invade its host successfully.
