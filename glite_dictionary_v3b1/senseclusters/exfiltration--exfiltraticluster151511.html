exfiltration

noun
liquid leakage outward

Pronunciation: /eks.fɪl.ˈtreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ɪks.fɪl.ˈtreɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /eks.fɪl.ˈtreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ɪks.fɪl.ˈtreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the movement or leakage of liquid, especially water, from a pipe, drainage system, or channel into the surrounding soil or environment

Examples

Exfiltration of water

Regular inspections help reduce exfiltration and protect the environment from polluted runoff.
