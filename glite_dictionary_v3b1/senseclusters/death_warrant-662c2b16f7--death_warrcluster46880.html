death warrant

noun
official order for execution
Pronunciation: / dɛθ ˈwɔrənt /; / dɛθ ˈwɔɹənt / (AmE), / dɛθ ˈwɒrənt / (BrE)
an official document or legal order that authorizes the execution of a person who has been sentenced to death

Examples

A death warrant document
For centuries, a death warrant meant inevitable execution for those condemned.
The governor refused to issue the death warrant, delaying the prisoner's execution.
The judge signed the death warrant after the final appeal was denied.
After a lengthy trial, the judge signed the death warrant for the convicted prisoner.
Protesters gathered outside the prison, condemning the recent issuance of a death warrant.
The governor has the authority to delay the death warrant if new evidence emerges.
