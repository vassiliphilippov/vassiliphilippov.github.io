vena brachiocephalica

noun
large neck veins draining blood to the heart
Pronunciation: / ˈviːnə ˌbrækiˌoʊsɪˈfælɪkə / (AmE), / ˈviːnə ˌbrækiə(ʊ)sɪˈfælɪkə / (BrE)
Either of the two large veins on each side of the neck formed by the union of the internal jugular and subclavian veins, which drain blood from the head, neck, and upper limbs to the heart.

Examples

The vena brachiocephalica vein
During the operation, the surgeon carefully identified the vena brachiocephalica to avoid accidental injury.
Obstruction of the vena brachiocephalica can lead to swelling in the neck and arm.
The vena brachiocephalica quickly transports blood from the upper limbs to the superior vena cava.
