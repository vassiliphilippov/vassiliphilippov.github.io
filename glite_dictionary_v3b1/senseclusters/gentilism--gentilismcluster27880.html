gentilism

noun
ancient pagan beliefs
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛntɪˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛntɪlɪzəm / (BrE)
an archaic term for paganism or the religious beliefs and practices of non-Jewish peoples

Examples

Ancient gentilism beliefs
In ancient times, gentilism was widespread among many European tribes.
The historian compared the rise of Christianity to the decline of gentilism in the Roman Empire.
Old texts often refer to non-Jewish religious practices as gentilism.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monotheism - belief in a single god
  • synonym: paganism - beliefs and practices outside the main world religions, especially before Christianity
  • synonym: polytheism - belief in or worship of more than one god
