angiogenesis

noun
formation of new blood vessels
Pronunciation: / ˌændʒi.oʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌandʒi.əʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (BrE)
the formation of new blood vessels, especially from pre-existing vessels, as in healing or tumor growth

Examples

Abnormal angiogenesis
Abnormal angiogenesis is associated with diseases such as diabetic retinopathy and arthritis.
Cancer researchers hope to develop drugs that inhibit angiogenesis to prevent tumor growth.
Recent studies have shown that angiogenesis plays a critical role in wound healing.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: vasculogenesis - the formation of entirely new blood vessels, especially during embryonic development
