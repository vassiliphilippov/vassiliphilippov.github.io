occupy

verb
take control of a place by force
5.9 18/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…ëÀêkj…ôÀåpa…™ / (AmE), / Àà…íkj äpa…™ / (BrE)
(of an army or force) to enter and take control of a place, especially by military means.

Examples

Troops occupy a city
During the war, foreign troops occupied several territories for years.
The army plans to occupy the capital city by the end of the week.
The decision to occupy the region sparked widespread protests.

Synonyms

  • invade
    4.4 14/m
    invade means to enter a country with armed force to begin control, while occupy means to take and hold the place.
  • conquer
    3.7 16/m
    conquer means to defeat people or a country and gain full control, while occupy means to take and hold a place.
  • overrun
    7.0 0.90/m
    overrun means to seize a place quickly and completely, while occupy means to enter and hold it for control.

Related Terms

  • synonym: invade - to enter a place by force, especially for conquest
  • antonym: retreat - to withdraw troops from an area
