irreligious

adjective
not religious; indifferent or hostile to religion
Pronunciation: / ˌɪrəˈlɪdʒəs / (AmE), / ˌɪrɪˈlɪdʒəs / (BrE)
hostile or indifferent to religion; not religious

Examples

An irreligious person
Despite growing up in a religious household, she became increasingly irreligious as an adult.
His irreligious views often sparked debates at family gatherings.
The book explores irreligious attitudes in a society dominated by faith.

Related Terms

  • antonym: religious - having or showing belief in and reverence for a deity
