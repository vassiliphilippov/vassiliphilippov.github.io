jack-in-the-box

noun
a toy clown that pops out of a box
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒæk ɪn ðə bɑks / (AmE), / ˈdʒæk ɪn ðə bɒks / (BrE)
a toy consisting of a clown or other figure that pops out of a box when the lid is opened, often accompanied by music

Examples

A jack-in-the-box toy
He gave his niece a jack-in-the-box for her birthday, and she played with it for hours.
The children laughed in delight when the jack-in-the-box popped up unexpectedly.
The old jack-in-the-box sat on the shelf as a reminder of childhood memories.
