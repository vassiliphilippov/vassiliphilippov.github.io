oliguria

noun
abnormally low urine output
7.1 8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɑlɪˈgjʊriə /; / ˌɑːlɪˈɡjʊriə / (AmE), / ˌɒlɪˈgjʊəriə /; / ˌɒlɪˈɡjʊəriə / (BrE)
the medical condition of producing an abnormally small amount of urine

Examples

Severe oliguria
Oliguria can be a sign of dehydration or acute kidney injury.
Doctors monitor urine output closely in ICU patients for signs of oliguria.
The patient was diagnosed with oliguria after producing less than 400 ml of urine in 24 hours.
Severe dehydration can result in oliguria and other complications.
The doctor monitored her fluid intake and oliguria during treatment for kidney failure.
The patient was diagnosed with oliguria after his daily urine output dropped sharply.
