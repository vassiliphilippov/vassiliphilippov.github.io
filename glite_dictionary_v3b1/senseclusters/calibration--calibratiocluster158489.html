calibration

noun
checking and adjusting instrument accuracy
Pronunciation: / ˌkælɪˈbreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkælɪˈbreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of checking and adjusting the accuracy of a measuring instrument by comparing it with a known standard

Examples

Device calibration
After a year of use, the equipment showed errors that only a complete calibration could fix.
Regular calibration ensures that our scales provide accurate weight measurements.
The laboratory required routine calibration of all its pressure gauges.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adjustment - the act of making something more accurate or suited to requirements
