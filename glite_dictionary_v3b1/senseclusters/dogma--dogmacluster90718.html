dogma

noun
authoritative principle or doctrine
Pronunciation: / ˈdɔɡmə / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡmə / (BrE)
a principle or set of principles laid down by an authority as unquestionably true, especially in religion, politics, or ideology

Examples

Religious dogma
Political parties often create their own dogma that members are expected to follow.
Some people reject religious dogma in favor of independent thought.
The scientist challenged the established dogma that had gone unquestioned for decades.
He was criticized for adhering to political dogma instead of thinking independently.
Scientific progress often requires challenging established dogma.
The priest emphasized that the church's dogma must be accepted without question.

Related Terms

  • synonym: doctrine - a belief or set of beliefs held and taught by a church or other group
  • antonym: heresy - belief or opinion contrary to orthodox doctrine
