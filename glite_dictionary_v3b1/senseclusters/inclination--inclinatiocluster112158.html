inclination

noun
angle between x-axis and a line (geometry)
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnkləˈneɪʃən /; / ˌɪnklɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪŋklɪˈneɪʃən /; / ˌɪnklɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the angle or degree to which something is slanted or tilted from a reference line or plane (such as in geometry, astronomy, or physics)

Examples

Angle of inclination
In the graph, the steepest inclination indicates the fastest rate of change.
The inclination of the line was determined to be 30 degrees above the x-axis.
To find the inclination, measure the angle from the positive x-axis to the given line.
Astronomers calculated the asteroid's inclination to predict its future path.
Saturn has an orbital inclination of about 2.5 degrees.
The planet's inclination determines how its orbit is tilted relative to Earth's.
After the earthquake, the inclination of the tower increased noticeably.
The inclination of the hillside made it difficult to build on.
The architect measured the inclination of the ramp to ensure it met safety standards.

Related Terms

  • synonym: angle - the space, measured in degrees, between two intersecting lines or surfaces
  • synonym: declination - the angle between magnetic north and true north
  • synonym: obliquity - the angle between the equatorial plane and the orbital plane
  • synonym: tilt - a sloping position or movement; a slant
  • antonym: vertical - being in a position or direction perpendicular to the horizon
