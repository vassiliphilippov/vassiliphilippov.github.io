trace

noun
a visible mark left by passage
Pronunciation: / treɪs / (AmE), / treɪs / (BrE)
a mark, sign, or very small amount showing that something has existed or was present

Examples

A trace of blood
After the rain, all trace of the car had disappeared from the muddy road.
The detective found a trace of footprints near the window.
The hunters followed the trace left by the deer through the forest.
She spoke English fluently, but with a trace of her native accent.
The investigation revealed no trace of toxic chemicals in the water.
There was only a trace of sugar left in the bowl after breakfast.
She spoke without a trace of anger in her voice.
The suspect showed not even a trace of remorse during the trial.
There was barely a trace of sugar left in the bowl.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something
  • synonym: hint - a very slight or hardly noticeable amount
  • synonym: modicum - a small quantity of a particular thing
  • synonym: touch - a small quantity; a slight feeling or suggestion of something
  • synonym: track - a mark or line left by something that has passed; a path or trail
  • synonym: vestige - a trace or remnant of something that is disappearing or no longer exists
