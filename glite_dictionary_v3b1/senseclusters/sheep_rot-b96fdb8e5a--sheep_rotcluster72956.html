sheep rot

noun
parasitic liver disease in livestock
8.8 7.7
Pronunciation: /ʃiːp rɑːt/ /; / /ʃiːp rɔːt/ (AmE), /ʃiːp rɒt/ (BrE)
A parasitic disease affecting the liver of sheep and cattle, caused by infestation with liver flukes and their toxins.

Examples

Sheep rot disease
Sheep rot can lead to significant economic losses in areas where liver flukes are common.
Farmers are advised to monitor their flocks regularly for signs of sheep rot.
The veterinarian diagnosed the dying sheep with sheep rot after observing damage to the animal's liver.
