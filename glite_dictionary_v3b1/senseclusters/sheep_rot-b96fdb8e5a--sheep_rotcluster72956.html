sheep rot

noun
parasitic liver disease in livestock
9.8 0.00/m
Pronunciation: /  ÉiÀêp r…ët / (AmE), /  ÉiÀêp r…ít / (BrE)
A parasitic disease affecting the liver of sheep and cattle, caused by infestation with liver flukes and their toxins.

Examples

Sheep rot disease
Sheep rot can lead to significant economic losses in areas where liver flukes are common.
Farmers are advised to monitor their flocks regularly for signs of sheep rot.
The veterinarian diagnosed the dying sheep with sheep rot after observing damage to the animal's liver.

Synonyms

  • liver rot
    7.5 0.00/m
    liver rot is a common name for liver disease from fluke infestation in sheep and cattle, while sheep rot is the regional term.
  • distomatosis
    9.9 0.00/m
    distomatosis is a technical term for liver infection with flukes used by vets and scientists, while sheep rot is the everyday name.
  • black disease
    9.8 0.00/m
    black disease causes sudden death and dark skin in sheep due to liver fluke toxins, while sheep rot means liver damage from flukes more broadly.
