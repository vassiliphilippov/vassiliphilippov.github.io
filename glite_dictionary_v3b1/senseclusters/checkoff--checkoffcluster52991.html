checkoff

verb
to deduct from pay
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɛkˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛkˌɒf / (BrE)
to arrange for money to be automatically deducted from an employee's pay for a specific purpose, such as union dues

Examples

To checkoff union dues
The company agreed to checkoff union dues directly from staff pay each month.
Employees can authorize the payroll department to checkoff charitable donations automatically.
Many organizations use a system to checkoff insurance premiums from workers' salaries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deduct - to take an amount away from a total, especially from money
  • synonym: withhold - to hold back or keep something, especially money, from someone
