roll

noun
round or spiral shape; concentric circles
Pronunciation: / roʊl / (AmE), / rəʊl / (BrE)
a cylindrical or spiral object or quantity, or something that has been rolled up (such as bread, film, tape, money, or other material)

Examples

A roll of tape
The fern's young leaves unfurled from a tight roll as they grew.
The rosebud opened to reveal a beautiful roll of petals at its center.
They admired the intricate roll formed by the onion's concentric layers.
She loaded a new roll of film into her camera before heading out to take pictures.
The photographer bought a roll of black-and-white film for the project.
We found an old roll of undeveloped film in the back of the drawer.
Please hand me that roll of duct tape from the toolbox.
She bought a roll of paper towels from the supermarket.
The architect unrolled the blueprints from a large roll.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bankroll - a supply of money, especially as available resources
  • synonym: bun - a small, often sweet bread roll
  • synonym: coil - a length of something wound in a continuous series of loops
  • synonym: film - thin flexible strip of material used in photography
  • synonym: spiral - a curve that winds around a center point, gradually getting further away
  • synonym: wad - a bundle of paper money
