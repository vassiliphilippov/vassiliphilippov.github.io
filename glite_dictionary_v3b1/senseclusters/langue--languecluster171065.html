Examples

Langue and parole
In structural linguistics, langue is contrasted with the actual speech of individuals.
Saussure introduced the concept of langue to explain the underlying structure of language.
The rules of grammar are part of the langue shared by speakers of a language.
In structural linguistics, langue refers to the underlying system that makes communication possible.
Saussure's distinction between langue and parole is fundamental to modern linguistic theory.
The rules of langue are shared by all speakers of a language, even if they speak differently.
