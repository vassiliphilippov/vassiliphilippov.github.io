lower oneself

verb
behave in a less dignified or honorable way
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊər wʌnˌsɛlf / (AmE), / ˈləʊə wʌnˌsɛlf / (BrE)
to do something that makes you lose respect or dignity; to act in a way that is beneath your usual standards or principles

Examples

To lower oneself in pride
He refused to lower himself by arguing with them online.
I would never lower myself to cheating, no matter how tempting it is.
She didn't want to lower herself by spreading rumors about her coworkers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stoop - to lower your moral standards by doing something you consider beneath you
