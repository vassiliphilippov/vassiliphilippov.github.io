out of step

adjective
not in agreement or harmony with others
4.4 6.1
Pronunciation: / aʊt əv stɛp / (AmE), / aʊt əv stɛp / (BrE)
not in agreement with or not matching the ideas, beliefs, or actions of others; not conforming to a prevailing standard or practice.

Examples

Out of step with others
His views on technology are out of step with those of his colleagues.
Many politicians found themselves out of step with public opinion on the issue.
The company's rigid dress code is out of step with current workplace culture.
