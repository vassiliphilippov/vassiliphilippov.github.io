Examples

Synonyms

  • accompanying
    3.4 4.27/m
    accompanying describes things that happen together or as a side effect, while attendant links them to an event or situation.
  • concomitant
    8.4 2.75/m
    concomitant often names things that occur at the same time in formal or scientific contexts, while attendant stresses a result or connection.
  • consequent
    6.1 2.61/m
    consequent means happening as a direct result of something, while attendant can also mean merely occurring in connection.
  • resultant
    4.9 3.28/m
    resultant describes outcomes that follow from causes in a clear chain, while attendant is more general about being connected.

Related Terms

  • synonym: resultant - resulting from a particular cause
