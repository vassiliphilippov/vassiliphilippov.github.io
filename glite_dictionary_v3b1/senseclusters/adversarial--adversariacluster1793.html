adversarial

adjective
involving conflict
Pronunciation: / ˌædvərˈsɛriəl / (AmE), / ˌædvəˈsɛːriəl /; / ˌædvəˈsɛəriəl / (BrE)
involving opposition, conflict, or competition between people or groups (including legal or social situations)

Examples

Adversarial debate
The debate took on an adversarial tone as both sides refused to compromise.
In an adversarial legal system, lawyers argue against each other in court.
Their adversarial relationship made teamwork difficult during the project.
The adversarial nature of the trial meant both lawyers argued strongly for their clients.
Some countries use an adversarial legal system, while others use an inquisitorial one.
Debates in parliament can often become quite adversarial when opinions differ sharply.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antagonistic - showing opposition or hostility
  • antonym: cooperative - working together toward a common goal
  • synonym: hostile - unfriendly or antagonistic
