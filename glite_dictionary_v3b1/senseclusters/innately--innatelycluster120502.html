innately

In a natural, inborn, or inherent manner; as an intrinsic quality.

Examples

Be innately curious
She was innately curious about the world around her.
Some people are innately sensitive to the feelings of others.
The dog seemed to innately understand what was expected of it.

Synonyms

  • intrinsically
    6.4 3.33/m
    intrinsically is used to say something is essential to its nature, while innately stresses being present from birth or as an inborn trait.
  • inherently
    4.7 13/m
    inherently means something is a basic or permanent part of it, while innately points to being present at birth.
  • naturally
    1.8 12/m
    naturally describes a usual tendency or ease that comes from nature, while innately refers to traits fixed from birth.
