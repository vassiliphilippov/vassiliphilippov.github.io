slit

noun
long, narrow opening
Pronunciation: / slɪt / (AmE), / slɪt / (BrE)
a long, narrow opening or cut, especially one made deliberately

Examples

A narrow slit
A narrow slit in the curtains allowed a beam of sunlight to enter the room.
She peered through the slit in the fence to see what was happening outside.
The envelope had a small slit where it had accidentally been torn open.
A narrow slit was scratched into the metal to mark the measurement.
He peered through the slit in the curtain to see who was outside.
The envelope had a small slit along one edge, through which the letter slipped out.
A thin ray of light shone through the slit in the curtain.

There was a narrow slit in the rock face, just wide enough to squeeze through.
