cyclopean masonry

noun
ancient masonry using huge, irregular stones
8.6
Pronunciation: / saɪˌkloʊˈpiːən ˈmeɪsən.i / (AmE), / sɪˌkləʊˈpiːən ˈmeɪs(ə)n.i / (BrE)
a style of building using massive, irregularly shaped stones fitted together without mortar, especially in ancient structures

Examples

Cyclopean masonry walls
Archaeologists studied the cyclopean masonry techniques used by early civilizations in the Mediterranean region.
The ancient walls of Mycenae are famous for their cyclopean masonry, constructed from enormous limestone boulders.
The fortress featured cyclopean masonry, with rough-hewn stones seamlessly interlocked without the use of mortar.
