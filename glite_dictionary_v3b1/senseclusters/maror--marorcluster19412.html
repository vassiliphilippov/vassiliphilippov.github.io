maror

noun
Passover bitter herb
9.9
Pronunciation: / mɑˈrɔr / (AmE), / mɑˈrɔː / (BrE)
a bitter herb eaten during the Jewish holiday of Passover to symbolize the bitterness of slavery in Egypt

Examples

Passover maror
During the Seder meal, each person eats a portion of maror to fulfill the tradition.
The rabbi explained the significance of maror as a reminder of the hardships faced by the Israelites.
Some families use romaine lettuce as maror instead of horseradish at their Passover table.

Related Terms

  • synonym: horseradish - a pungent root often used as maror during Passover
  • synonym: lettuce - a leafy vegetable sometimes used as maror at Passover
