BCD

noun
scuba buoyancy device
Pronunciation: / bi si ˈdi / (AmE), / biː siː ˈdiː / (BrE)
a piece of scuba-diving equipment, usually an inflatable jacket or wing, that allows a diver to control buoyancy underwater

Examples

Scuba BCD jacket
Before entering the water, she checked her BCD to ensure it was properly inflated.
A well-fitted BCD helps divers maintain neutral buoyancy throughout the dive.
He learned how to release air from his BCD to descend smoothly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: buoyancy compensator - a device used by divers to control their buoyancy
  • antonym: scuba tank - a cylinder containing compressed air for breathing underwater
