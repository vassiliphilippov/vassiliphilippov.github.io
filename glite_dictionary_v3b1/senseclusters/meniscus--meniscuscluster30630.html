meniscus

noun
curved surface of liquid in a tube
9.9
Pronunciation: / məˈnɪskəs / (AmE), / mɪˈnɪskəs / (BrE)
The curved upper surface of a liquid in a tube, caused by surface tension.

Examples

Liquid meniscus in a tube
Always read the measurement at the bottom of the meniscus for accurate results.
The meniscus forms because of the interaction between the liquid and the container's surface.
The meniscus of the water in the graduated cylinder was concave.

Related Terms

  • synonym: surface tension - the elastic tendency of liquids which makes them acquire the least surface area possible
