fumble

verb
mishandle or drop a ball in play (sports)
6.2 3.8
Pronunciation: /ˈfʌm.bəl/ (AmE), /ˈfʌm.bəl/ (BrE)
to mishandle or fail to play cleanly a ball that is in play, especially by dropping or juggling it, typically in sports such as baseball or football

Examples

To fumble the ball
During the ninth inning, he unexpectedly fumbled a routine ground ball.
If you fumble the ball on a crucial play, it can cost your team the game.
The shortstop managed to fumble the grounder, allowing the runner to reach first base safely.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bobble - to handle a ball clumsily, causing it to be dropped or nearly dropped
