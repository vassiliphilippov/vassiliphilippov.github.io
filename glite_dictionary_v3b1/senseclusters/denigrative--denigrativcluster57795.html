denigrative

adjective
tending to discredit or malign
8.1 9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛnɪˌɡreɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈdɛnɪɡrətɪv / (BrE)
(of statements) harmful and often untrue; intended to discredit or malign someone or something.

Examples

Denigrative comments
Her denigrative comments about his work were both unfair and unfounded.
Politicians often use denigrative language to discredit their opponents during campaigns.
The article contained many denigrative remarks aimed at the company's leadership.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defamatory - (of statements) damaging the good reputation of someone; slanderous or libelous
  • antonym: laudatory - expressing praise or commendation
