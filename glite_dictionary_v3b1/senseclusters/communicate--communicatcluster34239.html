communicate

verb
receive Holy Communion (Catholic context)
3.0
Pronunciation: / kəˈmjunɪˌkeɪt /; / kəˈmjuː.nɪ.keɪt / (AmE), / kəˈmjuːnɪkeɪt /; / kəˈmjuː.nɪ.keɪt / (BrE)
to receive or give Holy Communion in a Christian church

Examples

To communicate at church
On Easter Sunday, many parishioners choose to communicate during the special Mass.
She communicated for the first time after her confirmation.
Those who had properly confessed were able to communicate at the service.
In some churches, deacons may also communicate parishioners when the priest is unavailable.
It is traditional for the bishop to communicate the congregation on special holy days.
The priest will communicate the faithful during the Easter service.
