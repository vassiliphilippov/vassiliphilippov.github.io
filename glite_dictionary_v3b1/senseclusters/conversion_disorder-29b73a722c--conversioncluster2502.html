conversion disorder

noun
mental disorder causing physical symptoms without medical explanation
5.8 6.2
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈvɜːr.ʒən dɪs.ˈɔːr.dər/ /; / /kən.ˈvɜːr.ʃən dɪs.ˈɔːr.dər/ (AmE), /kən.ˈvɜː.ʃən dɪs.ˈɔː.də/ /; / /kən.ˈvɜː.ʒən dɪs.ˈɔː.də/ (BrE)
A mental disorder in which psychological stress or conflict is expressed through physical symptoms such as paralysis or loss of sensation without a medical cause.

Examples

Conversion disorder symptoms
After the accident, doctors diagnosed her with conversion disorder when she suddenly lost the ability to walk.
The patient's unexplained paralysis was traced to conversion disorder after thorough neurological testing.
Therapists treated him for conversion disorder, which had caused his numbness during stressful situations.
