reverberation

noun
prolonged reflected sound; echoing
Pronunciation: / rəˌvɝː.bəˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / rɪˌvɜː.bərˈeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the persistence or repetition of a sound after its source has stopped, caused by reflection of sound waves from surfaces such as walls or ceilings

Examples

Loud reverberation
After the thunderclap, the reverberation echoed through the valley for several seconds.
Engineers measured the reverberation time of the concert hall to improve its acoustics.
The reverberation of footsteps in the empty hall made the space seem even larger.

Related Terms

  • synonym: echo - a sound or sounds caused by the reflection of sound waves from a surface
