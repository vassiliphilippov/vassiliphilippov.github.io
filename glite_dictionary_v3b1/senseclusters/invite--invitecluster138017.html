invite

verb
cause or increase likelihood (usually of something bad)
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈvaɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈvaɪt / (BrE)
to cause or increase the likelihood of something, especially something undesirable

Examples

To invite trouble
His lack of preparation only served to invite trouble during the important meeting.
Ignoring safety protocols can invite disaster in hazardous workplaces.
Publishing such harsh comments may invite criticism from your colleagues.

Related Terms

  • antonym: avoid - to keep away from or stop oneself from doing something
  • synonym: provoke - to cause a particular reaction or have a particular effect
