Cerenkov

proper noun
Soviet physicist
Pronunciation: / tʃəˈrɛŋkɒf / (AmE), / tʃəˈrɛŋkɒf / (BrE)
Pavel Alekseyevich Cerenkov (1904–1990), a Soviet physicist known for discovering Cerenkov radiation and sharing the 1958 Nobel Prize in Physics.

Examples

Cerenkov discovered radiation
Pavel Cerenkov was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1958 for his groundbreaking work.
The phenomenon known as Cerenkov radiation is named after the physicist Cerenkov.
Many students learn about Cerenkov and his contributions to nuclear physics in university courses.
