menace

noun
threat or act of threatening
1.0 3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈmen.əs/ /; / /ˈmen.ɪs/ (AmE), /ˈmen.ɪs/ /; / /ˈmen.əs/ (BrE)
a person or thing that is likely to cause harm or danger; a threat

Examples

A menace to safety
He saw her as a menace to his authority at work.
The dark clouds overhead were a real menace to their picnic plans.
There was a hint of menace in his calm, quiet voice.
After several attacks, the wild dog became a menace to the villagers.
Plastic pollution is a growing menace to marine life around the world.
The leaking gas pipe was considered a serious menace to public safety.

Related Terms

  • synonym: danger - the possibility of suffering harm or injury
  • synonym: hazard - a potential source of danger
  • antonym: safeguard - a measure taken to protect from harm or risk
  • antonym: safety - the condition of being protected from danger
  • synonym: threat - a statement or indication of likely harm or danger
