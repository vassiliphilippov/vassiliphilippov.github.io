benefice

noun
endowed church office
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛn.ɪ.fɪs / (AmE), / ˈbɛn.ɪ.fɪs / (BrE)
a permanent church appointment for which property and income are provided to its holder

Examples

A church benefice
Holding a benefice was once a common way for clergymen to earn a living.
Many historical disputes arose over the right to appoint someone to a vacant benefice.
The parish priest received a small benefice that helped support his ministry.
