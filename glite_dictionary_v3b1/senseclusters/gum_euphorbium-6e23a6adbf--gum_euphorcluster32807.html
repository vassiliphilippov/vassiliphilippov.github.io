gum euphorbium

noun
acrid gum resin from Euphorbia used in medicine
Pronunciation: / ɡʌm juːˈfɔːrbiəm / (AmE), / ɡʌm juːˈfɔːbɪəm / (BrE)
an acrid brown gum resin obtained from some species of Euphorbia, formerly used in medicine and now mainly in veterinary applications

Examples

Gum euphorbium resin
Because of its acrid nature, gum euphorbium must be handled with care in medical settings.
In the nineteenth century, gum euphorbium was sometimes prescribed as a purgative despite its harsh effects.
The veterinarian explained that gum euphorbium is now rarely used except for certain livestock treatments.
