repairability

noun
ability to heal
Pronunciation: / rɪˌpɛrəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / rɪˌpeərəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the ability of something, especially living tissue or an organism, to heal or recover after being damaged or injured

Examples

Repairability of tissue
Scientists are studying the repairability of nerve cells to develop new treatments for injuries.
The repairability of the material makes it ideal for use in medical implants.
After the accident, doctors were surprised by the repairability of the patient's liver tissue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: regeneration - the process of renewal or regrowth, especially in living tissue
  • synonym: resilience - the ability to recover quickly from difficulties or damage
