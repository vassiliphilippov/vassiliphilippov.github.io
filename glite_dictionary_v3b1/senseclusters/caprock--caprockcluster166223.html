caprock

noun
protective rock layer
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈkæpˌrɑk / (AmE), / ˈkæpˌrɒk / (BrE)
a hard layer of rock that lies above softer or more porous material and protects or seals it

Examples

A caprock layer
The mesa was topped with a thick layer of caprock.
Erosion was slowed because the caprock shielded the softer stone beneath.
Geologists studied the caprock to understand how the landscape was formed.
The geologist explained how the caprock prevents oil from escaping the underground reservoir.
Drilling teams must identify the caprock before extracting natural gas from deep layers.
A thick caprock is essential for trapping valuable resources beneath the earth's surface.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bedrock - the solid rock layer beneath soil or loose material
  • antonym: reservoir - a porous rock layer that stores oil, gas, or water underground
  • synonym: seal - a layer that prevents the movement of fluids underground
