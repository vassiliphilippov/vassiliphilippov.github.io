nucleic acid

noun
macromolecule in cells such as DNA or RNA
Pronunciation: / nuˈkliɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / njuːˈkliːɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
a complex organic substance present in living cells, especially DNA or RNA, consisting of long chains of nucleotides

Examples

Nucleic acids like DNA
A major breakthrough in biochemistry was understanding the structure of nucleic acids.
The genetic information of all living organisms is stored in nucleic acids such as DNA and RNA.
Viruses use nucleic acids to hijack the genetic machinery of their host cells.

Related Terms

  • synonym: DNA - deoxyribonucleic acid, a type of nucleic acid
  • synonym: RNA - ribonucleic acid, a type of nucleic acid
