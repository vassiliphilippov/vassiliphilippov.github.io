Adherence to conventional standards, especially in belief, conduct, or artistic expression; orthodoxy resulting from following established conventions.

Examples

Strict conventionalism rules
His conventionalism made it difficult for him to accept new ideas.
Some writers reject conventionalism in favor of experimental styles.

Synonyms

  • conventionality
    6.9 0.11/m
    conventionality means the quality of following usual customs and rules, while conventionalism is an active doctrine of following those conventions.
  • conformity
    4.8 4.26/m
    conformity is behaving or thinking to match accepted rules and people, while conventionalism is a belief system that follows such conventions.
  • conformism
    7.7 0.16/m
    conformism means habitually following rules often without question, while conventionalism is broader, naming a deliberate adherence to established conventions.
  • orthodoxy
    7.4 5.20/m
    orthodoxy refers to accepted beliefs especially in religion, while conventionalism covers broader social or artistic adherence to conventions.
  • traditionalism
    6.5 0.65/m
    traditionalism values long-held customs and past ways, while conventionalism stresses following present conventions and expected standards today.
