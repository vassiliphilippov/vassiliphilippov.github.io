Synonyms

  • everywhere
    1.0 74/m
    everywhere means in all places and is a short common adverb, while all over describes things across every part of a place.
  • everyplace
    2.0 0.03/m
    everyplace appears in speech as a less common variant for everywhere, while all over is the common phrase for throughout an area.
  • throughout
    1.0 134/m
    throughout is used across every part or during a whole period, while all over focuses on places within an area.
  • here, there and everywhere
    4.3 0.10/m
    here, there and everywhere sounds informal and playful, while all over is plain and focuses on every part of a place.
  • far and wide
    2.6 1.64/m
    far and wide suggests reaching many distant places and spread out, while all over means in every part of a single area.
