hyperinflation

noun
extreme price increase
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərɪnˈfleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpərɪnˈfleɪʃən / (BrE)
an extremely rapid and out-of-control increase in prices, causing money to lose its value quickly

Examples

Severe hyperinflation crisis
The country experienced hyperinflation that made everyday items unaffordable for most people.
During periods of hyperinflation, savings can lose their value almost overnight.
Many citizens lost confidence in the currency because of ongoing hyperinflation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deflation - a decrease in the general price level of goods and services
  • degree lower: inflation - a general increase in prices and fall in the value of money
