RTW

adjective
right-to-work laws
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɑr ti ˈdʌbəl.ju / (AmE), / ɑː tiː ˈdʌbəl.juː / (BrE)
relating to laws or policies that prohibit requiring union membership as a condition of employment

Examples

RTW laws
The company operates in several RTW states where union membership is not mandatory.
New RTW legislation was introduced to give workers more freedom of choice.
Many businesses support RTW policies to attract a wider range of employees.

Related Terms

  • synonym: right-to-work - relating to laws that ban mandatory union membership for employment
  • antonym: unionized - requiring or involving union membership
