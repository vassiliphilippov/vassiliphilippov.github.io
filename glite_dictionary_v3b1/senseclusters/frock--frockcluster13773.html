frock

verb
to dress in a robe
Pronunciation: / frɑk / (AmE), / frɒk / (BrE)
to dress someone in a frock or ceremonial robe, especially as part of a ceremony

Examples

To frock a priest
The bishop frocked the new priest during the ceremony.
After years of study, she was finally frocked and welcomed into the order.
The tradition requires that each candidate be frocked before taking on official duties.
The bishop will frock the new priest during the ceremony.
Traditionally, they frock the officials before the start of the festival.
She watched as the children were frocked in white robes for the performance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: defrock - to remove someone from a position, especially in the church
  • synonym: robe - to dress in a robe or ceremonial garment
  • antonym: undress - to remove clothing from someone
