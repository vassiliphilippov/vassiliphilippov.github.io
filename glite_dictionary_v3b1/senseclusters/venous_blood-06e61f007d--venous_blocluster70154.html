venous blood

noun
blood in the veins, low in oxygen
1.9
Pronunciation: / ˈviːnəs blʌd / (AmE), / ˈviːnəs blʌd / (BrE)
blood that flows through the veins, usually carrying less oxygen and more carbon dioxide than arterial blood, except in the pulmonary vein.

Examples

Venous blood sample
A sample of venous blood was drawn to check for infection.
The laboratory measured the patient's venous blood to assess carbon dioxide levels.
Unlike arterial blood, venous blood contains more waste products and less oxygen.

Related Terms

  • antonym: arterial blood - blood found in arteries, rich in oxygen
