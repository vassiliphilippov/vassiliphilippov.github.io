combination

noun
mathematical selection, order not important
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːmbəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒmbɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
in mathematics, a selection of items from a group, where the order of selection does not matter

Examples

Math combination
In how many ways can you choose three books from a shelf of five? This is a combination problem.
Order does not matter in a combination, unlike in a permutation.
The number of possible combinations increases with the number of items.

Related Terms

  • antonym: permutation - mathematical arrangement where order of selection matters
