Pollyannish approach

noun
excessively optimistic attitude
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːliˈænɪʃ əˈproʊtʃ / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˈænɪʃ əˈprəʊtʃ / (BrE)
an excessively optimistic attitude or way of dealing with problems, often ignoring negative aspects

Examples

A Pollyannish approach ignores problems
Critics argue that a Pollyannish approach can prevent genuine problem-solving.
Even in the face of setbacks, she maintained a Pollyannish approach that inspired her colleagues.
His Pollyannish approach to business led him to overlook obvious risks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: optimistic - hopeful and confident about the future
  • antonym: realistic - accepting things as they are; practical rather than idealistic
