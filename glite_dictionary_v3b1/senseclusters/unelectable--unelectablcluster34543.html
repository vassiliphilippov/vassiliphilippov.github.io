unelectable

adjective
not able to win election
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnɪˈlɛktəbəl / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪˈlɛktəbəl / (BrE)
not able to win an election or be chosen for public office

Examples

Unelectable candidate
Many voters saw the candidate as unelectable after the debate.
Her extreme policies made her unelectable in most districts.
Despite his experience, the party feared he was unelectable in a national race.

Related Terms

  • antonym: electable - able to win an election or be chosen for public office
  • synonym: unpopular - not liked or approved by many people
