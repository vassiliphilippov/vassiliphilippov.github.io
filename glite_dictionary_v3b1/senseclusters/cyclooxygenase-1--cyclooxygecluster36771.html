cyclooxygenase-1

noun
enzyme regulating prostaglandins in body tissues
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪkloʊˌɑksɪˈdʒeɪs wʌn / (AmE), / ˌsɪkləʊˌɒksɪˈdʒeɪs wʌn / (BrE)
an enzyme that helps regulate the production of prostaglandins, important for maintaining the health of the stomach lining and kidneys

Examples

cyclooxygenase-1 enzyme
Researchers are studying how cyclooxygenase-1 affects kidney function.
Some medications relieve pain by inhibiting cyclooxygenase-1, but this can lead to stomach problems.
The difference between COX-1 and COX-2 is important in the design of anti-inflammatory drugs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cyclooxygenase-2 - an enzyme similar to cyclooxygenase-1, but with different roles and regulation
