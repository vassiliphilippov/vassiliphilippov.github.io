durian

noun
large spiny Southeast Asian fruit with strong smell
Pronunciation: / ˈdʊriən / (AmE), / ˈdjʊəriən / (BrE)
A large, spiny fruit native to Southeast Asia, noted for its strong odor and creamy, flavorful flesh; its seeds can be roasted and eaten.

Examples

A durian fruit
After removing the spiky shell, the creamy flesh of the durian was surprisingly sweet.
The durian is famous for its pungent smell, which some people find overwhelming.
We tasted fresh durian at the market during our trip to Malaysia.
