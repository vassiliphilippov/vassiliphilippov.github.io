burnout

noun
fabric pattern technique
7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈbɜːr.naʊt/ (AmE), /ˈbɜːn.aʊt/ (BrE)
a fabric treatment in which parts of a material are chemically removed to create a pattern of sheer and solid areas

Examples

Burnout velvet fabric
The designer chose a burnout velvet for the evening gown, giving it a unique textured look.
You can see the floral pattern created by the burnout process on this scarf.
Many high-end dresses use burnout fabric to add sheer details and visual interest.
