untame

verb
to make wild again
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈteɪm / (AmE), / ʌnˈteɪm / (BrE)
to make wild again or to remove from a tamed or domesticated state

Examples

To untame the land
The conservation project aimed to untame the land by removing fences and letting nature take over.
Years of neglect had begun to untame the once carefully manicured gardens.
Some believe it is possible to untame animals that have lived in captivity for generations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: domesticate - to train or adapt an animal to live with humans
  • synonym: re-wild - to restore to a natural, wild state
  • antonym: tame - to domesticate or make less wild
