to face

adverb
directly and openly, in someone's presence
Pronunciation: / tə feɪs / (AmE), / tə feɪs / (BrE)
in a direct and open manner; directly to the person involved rather than behind their back

Examples

To face someone directly
He always prefers to give feedback to face rather than through email.
If you have criticism, say it to face rather than gossip behind their back.
She praised his performance to face, surprising everyone with her honesty.

Related Terms

  • antonym: behind someone's back - in someone's absence, not directly or openly
