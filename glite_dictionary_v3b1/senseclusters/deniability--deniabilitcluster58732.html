deniability

noun
ability to deny
Pronunciation: / dɪˌnaɪəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / dɪˌnaɪəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the ability to deny involvement, knowledge, or responsibility for something, especially to avoid blame or consequences

Examples

Deniability of involvement
The encrypted app's design emphasizes deniability, making it impossible to prove who sent a message.
He maintained deniability by ensuring there was no written record of the conversation.
In some security protocols, deniability protects users from being linked to specific actions.
The politician insisted on maintaining deniability about the leaked documents.
Corporate leaders often seek deniability when controversial decisions are made.
He structured the plan to give himself deniability if anything went wrong.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accountability - the state of being responsible for one's actions
  • antonym: admission - the act of acknowledging or accepting responsibility for something
  • synonym: plausible deniability - the ability to deny knowledge of or responsibility for actions due to lack of evidence
