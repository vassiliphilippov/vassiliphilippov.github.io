play down

verb
make something seem less important
5.1 3.5
Pronunciation: /pleɪ daʊn/ (AmE), /pleɪ daʊn/ (BrE)
to make something seem less important or significant than it really is

Examples

To play down problems
Officials attempted to play down concerns about the new policy at the press conference.
She tends to play down her achievements, even though she works very hard.
The company tried to play down the impact of the data breach in its statement.
Politicians often play down their opponents’ achievements during campaigns.
She tends to play down her role in the project, even though she was crucial.
The company spokesperson tried to play down the negative impact of the news.
