spiteful

adjective
showing a desire to hurt or upset
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈspaɪtfəl / (AmE), / ˈspaɪtf(ə)l / (BrE)
having or showing a desire to harm, anger, or upset someone on purpose; full of spite

Examples

A spiteful email
He sent a spiteful email just to make her angry.
Her spiteful remarks left everyone in the room feeling uncomfortable.
The review was so spiteful that it seemed more personal than professional.

Related Terms

  • antonym: kind - having or showing a friendly, generous, and considerate nature
  • synonym: malicious - intending or intended to do harm
  • synonym: vindictive - having or showing a strong desire for revenge
