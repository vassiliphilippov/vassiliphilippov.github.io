flourish

noun
bold or sweeping gesture
6.2 1.76/m
Pronunciation: / Ààfl…ùr.…™ É /; / Ààfl…ùÀê…™ É /; / Ààfl år…™ É / (AmE), / Ààfl år.…™ É /; / Ààfl år…™ É / (BrE)
a bold or dramatic gesture or display, either physical (like a sweeping movement) or verbal (like an impressive use of language), intended to attract attention or impress

Examples

A dramatic flourish
He signed the letter with a final flourish of his pen.
She waved her hand with a dramatic flourish as she exited the room.
The magician tipped his hat with a flourish, delighting the audience.
She entered the room with a dramatic flourish and everyone's attention turned to her.
The magician finished his trick with a final flourish that drew applause from the crowd.
With a flourish of his hat, he bowed to the audience.
His essay relies more on flourish than on factual evidence.
The politician's speech was filled with flourish but lacked real substance.
With a flourish of eloquence, she welcomed the guests to the gala.

Synonyms

  • gesticulation
    7.4 0.13/m
    gesticulation is a deliberate, expressive hand or body movement used when speaking, while flourish is a bolder gesture or verbal display to impress.
  • fanfare
    5.1 0.99/m
    fanfare is a loud, public or ceremonial display that attracts attention, while flourish is a single bold gesture or verbal phrase meant to impress.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gesture - a movement of part of the body to express an idea or meaning
  • antonym: plainness - lack of ornamentation or embellishment in speech
  • synonym: rhetoric - the art of effective or persuasive speaking or writing
