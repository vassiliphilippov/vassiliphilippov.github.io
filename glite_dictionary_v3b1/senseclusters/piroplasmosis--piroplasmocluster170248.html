piroplasmosis

noun
parasitic animal disease
9.9
Pronunciation: /paɪ.rə.plæz.ˈmoʊ.sɪs/ /; / /paɪ.roʊ.plæz.ˈmoʊ.sɪs/ (AmE), /paɪ.rə.plæz.ˈməʊ.sɪs/ (BrE)
an infectious disease of animals (especially cattle and horses), and sometimes humans, caused by blood parasites such as Babesia, usually transmitted by ticks and leading to fever and anemia

Examples

Veterinarians diagnosed several horses with piroplasmosis after they showed signs of fever and anemia.
The spread of piroplasmosis in cattle can cause significant economic losses for farmers.
Ticks are the main carriers of the parasites that cause piroplasmosis in livestock.

Farmers are concerned about the spread of piroplasmosis among their cattle during tick season.
Treatment for piroplasmosis often involves medication to target the Babesia parasites.

Travelers in tick-infested areas should be aware of the risk of piroplasmosis.
Treatment for piroplasmosis often involves specific antibiotics to target the Babesia parasites.
