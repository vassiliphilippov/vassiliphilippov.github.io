catechist

noun
religious instructor, esp. in Christianity
2.7
Pronunciation: / ˈkætəkɪst / (AmE), / ˈkætəkɪst / (BrE)
a person who instructs others, especially catechumens, in the principles of the Christian religion using a catechism, often in preparation for baptism

Examples

A catechist teaches faith
She became a catechist to help new members understand the church's teachings.
The catechist patiently explained the meaning of baptism to the children.
The role of a catechist is vital in preparing catechumens for their first sacraments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: teacher - a person who teaches, especially in a school
