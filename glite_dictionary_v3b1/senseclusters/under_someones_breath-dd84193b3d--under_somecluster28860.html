under someone's breath

adverb
in a quiet voice (not to be overheard)
3.0
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndɚ ˈsʌmwʌnz brɛθ / (AmE), / ˈʌndə ˈsʌmwənz brɛθ / (BrE)
in a very quiet voice, so that other people cannot hear clearly or at all

Examples

Under someone's breath quietly
He apologized under his breath, hoping no one else would notice.
She muttered a complaint under her breath as she walked away.
The students laughed under their breath at the teacher's joke.
