secondhand

adverb
indirectly, not from original source
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛkəndˈhænd / (AmE), / ˌsɛkəndˈhænd / (BrE)
(of information) by indirect means, not from the original source; through an intermediary.

Examples

Hear secondhand
I only know about the incident secondhand, through friends who were at the event.
Most of my information came secondhand from colleagues who read the report.
She heard the news secondhand rather than from the person involved.

Related Terms

  • antonym: directly - in a direct way, from the original source
