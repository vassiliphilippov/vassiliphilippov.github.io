kiddush

noun
Jewish post-service gathering
Pronunciation: / kɪˈdʊʃ / (AmE), / kɪˈdʊʃ / (BrE)
a light social gathering with refreshments held after Jewish prayer services, especially on the Sabbath or holidays

Examples

After-service kiddush
After the morning service, everyone gathered for kiddush in the community hall.
The rabbi invited visitors to join the kiddush and enjoy some traditional pastries.
At the end of the holiday prayers, a generous kiddush was served to all attendees.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: banquet - a formal meal for many people, often to celebrate something
  • synonym: reception - a social gathering, often with food and drink, after an event
