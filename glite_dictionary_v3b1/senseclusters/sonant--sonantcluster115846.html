sonant

noun
a voiced speech sound
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊ.nənt / (AmE), / ˈsɒn.ənt / (BrE)
a speech sound produced with vibration of the vocal cords; a voiced sound

Examples

A nasal sonant
Linguists classify sounds such as 'l', 'm', and 'n' as sonants.
The distinction between a sonant and a voiceless sound can be heard in 'z' versus 's'.
The letter 'm' is a sonant because it is pronounced with vocal cord vibration.

Related Terms

  • synonym: voiced sound - a speech sound produced with vocal cord vibration
  • antonym: voiceless sound - a speech sound produced without vocal cord vibration
