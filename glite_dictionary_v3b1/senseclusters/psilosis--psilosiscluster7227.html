psilosis

noun
malabsorption disorder causing diarrhea and emaciation
Pronunciation: / saɪˈloʊsɪs / (AmE), / saɪˈləʊsɪs / (BrE)
A chronic disorder affecting nutrient absorption, characterized by foul-smelling diarrhea and emaciation, occurring in both children and adults in tropical and non-tropical regions.

Examples

Tropical psilosis cases
Doctors in both tropical and non-tropical regions report cases of psilosis among adults and children.
In children, early recognition of psilosis can prevent serious complications related to malnutrition.
The patient was diagnosed with psilosis after months of unexplained weight loss and severe diarrhea.
