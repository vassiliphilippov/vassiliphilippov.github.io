movement

noun
act of changing location or position
1.5 77/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàmuÀêvm…ônt / (AmE), / ÀàmuÀêvm…ônt / (BrE)
the act or process of changing position or place; the action of something moving or being moved

Examples

Movement of people
The police closely monitored the movement of vehicles through the city center.
The sudden movement of the ship made several passengers lose their balance.
There was a noticeable movement of people into urban areas during the 20th century.
He noticed a movement in the curtains and turned around quickly.
Rapid eye movement occurs during sleep in the REM phase.
The slightest movement of her fingers showed she was waking up.
Any sudden movement could trigger the alarm system.
Strict regulations govern the movement of animals across the border.
The movement of passengers between terminals is carefully managed.

Synonyms

  • motion
    1.4 53/m
    motion is the continuous change of position often used in science, while movement is the general act of changing place.
  • move
    1.0 9.39/m
    move is a single change of position or action you can see, while movement is a broader or ongoing change of place.
  • shift
    3.5 43/m
    shift is a change from one position, state, or focus often seen as a small or planned move, while movement is general motion.
  • passage
    3.2 7.90/m
    passage is the process of moving through or over something, often emphasizing travel or time, while movement is simply change of place.
  • locomotion
    4.6 1.08/m
    locomotion is the ability or act of moving from place to place, often used for animals, while movement is a general change of position.
