gaon

noun
genius or prodigy
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɡɑːˈoʊn / (AmE), / ɡɑːˈəʊn / (BrE)
a person who is exceptionally intelligent or talented; a genius, especially in modern Hebrew usage

Examples

A math gaon
After winning the international math competition, she was called a gaon by her teachers.
Many consider Albert Einstein to be a true gaon in the world of science.
His innovative ideas in technology earned him the reputation of a gaon among his colleagues.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mediocrity - a person of only average ability
  • synonym: prodigy - a young person with exceptional abilities
