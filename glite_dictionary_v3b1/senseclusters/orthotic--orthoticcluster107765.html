orthotic

noun
supportive device
Pronunciation: / ɔrˈθɑtɪk / (AmE), / ɔːˈθɒtɪk / (BrE)
a device worn to support, align, or improve the function of a body part, especially the foot or leg

Examples

Wear an orthotic insert
The doctor recommended an orthotic to help relieve her arch pain.
Many athletes use a custom orthotic in their shoes for better support.
After his injury, he wore an orthotic to stabilize his ankle.
The doctor recommended an orthotic to help relieve her foot pain.
Wearing a custom orthotic can improve walking comfort for people with flat feet.
He inserted the orthotic into his shoe before going for a run.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brace - a device that supports or holds a body part in position
  • antonym: prosthesis - an artificial device that replaces a missing body part
