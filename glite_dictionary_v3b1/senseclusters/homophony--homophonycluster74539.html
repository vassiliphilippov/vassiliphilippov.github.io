homophony

noun
same pronunciation for different words
4.3
Pronunciation: / hoʊˈmɑːfəni / (AmE), / həˈmɒfəni / (BrE)
The state of two or more words having the same pronunciation but different meanings and origins.

Examples

Word homophony
French contains much homophony, so words like 'ver' and 'vers' sound the same.
Poets may use homophony to create puns and play with the meaning of words.
The homophony of 'knight' and 'night' often amuses students learning English.

Related Terms

  • synonym: homograph - a word spelled the same as another but with different meaning and sometimes pronunciation
  • synonym: homonym - a word that is spelled or pronounced like another word but has a different meaning
  • degree lower: polysemy - the coexistence of many possible meanings for a word or phrase
