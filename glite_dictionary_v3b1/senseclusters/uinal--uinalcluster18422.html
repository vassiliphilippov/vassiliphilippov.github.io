uinal

noun
Mayan 20-day period
9.9
Pronunciation: / wiˈnɑl / (AmE), / wiˈnɑːl / (BrE)
a period of 20 days used as a unit of time in the ancient Mayan calendar system

Examples

A uinal period
In the ancient Mayan calendar, a uinal was made up of 20 days.
The priest recorded the ceremony date as the third uinal of the year.
Each uinal played an important role in tracking agricultural cycles.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: kin - a single day in the Mayan calendar
  • degree higher: tun - a period of 360 days in the Mayan calendar
