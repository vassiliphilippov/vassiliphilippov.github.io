padawan

noun
Jedi apprentice
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈpædəˌwɑn / (AmE), / ˈpædəˌwæn / (BrE)
a young apprentice or student, especially one learning from a mentor; originally from Star Wars, where it means a Jedi apprentice

Examples

A padawan learner
The Jedi Knight took a new padawan to teach the ways of the Force.
Every padawan must complete a series of trials before becoming a Jedi Knight.
She was proud to be chosen as a padawan by such a respected Jedi Master.
As a new padawan, she was eager to learn everything her mentor could teach her.
The experienced chef took a young padawan under his wing in the busy kitchen.
He joked that he was just a padawan compared to his expert colleagues.

Related Terms

  • synonym: apprentice - a person learning from a skilled employer
  • antonym: mentor - an experienced and trusted advisor
