woolsorter's disease

noun
infection from inhaling anthrax spores, severe respiratory illness
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈwʊlˌsɔrtərz dɪˈziz / (AmE), / ˈwʊlˌsɔːtəz dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
a form of anthrax infection caused by inhaling spores of Bacillus anthracis, typically found in contaminated wool or animal products, characterized by severe respiratory symptoms and high fatality

Examples

Woolsorter's disease infection
Cases of woolsorter's disease were historically common among workers handling raw wool.
The patient developed respiratory symptoms typical of woolsorter's disease after exposure to contaminated fibers.
Vaccination programs dramatically reduced the incidence of woolsorter's disease in industrial areas.
