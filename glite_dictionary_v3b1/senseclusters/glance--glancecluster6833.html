glance

verb
look quickly or briefly
4.9
Pronunciation: / ɡlæns / (AmE), / ɡlɑːns / (BrE)
to look quickly or briefly at someone or something

Examples

To glance at someone
During the meeting, Susan glanced around the room, hoping to catch someone’s eye.
He glanced quickly at the menu, unsure what to order.
She glanced at her watch before heading out the door.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gaze - look steadily and intently, especially in admiration or thought
  • synonym: peek - look quickly, typically in a secretive or cautious way
  • antonym: stare - look at someone or something with fixed eyes for a long time
