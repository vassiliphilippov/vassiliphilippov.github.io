sit

verb
hold a formal meeting or session
Pronunciation: / sɪt / (AmE), / sɪt / (BrE)
to serve as a member of a committee, jury, or similar group, or to hold a formal meeting or session

Examples

To sit on a committee
Parliament sat for several hours debating the new law.
The committee will sit next Thursday to discuss the proposal.
The court usually sits every weekday during the trial season.
He was proud to sit as a member of the review committee.
Several judges will sit on the appeals panel next week.
She has been asked to sit on the board of directors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: serve - to carry out duties as a member of a committee, jury, etc.
