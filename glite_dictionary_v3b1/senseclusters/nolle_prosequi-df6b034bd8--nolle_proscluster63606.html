nolle prosequi

verb
to formally drop legal charges
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈnɑːl.ɪ prə.ˈseɪ.kwiː/ /; / /ˈnɑːl.ə prə.ˈseɪ.kwiː/ (AmE), /ˈnɒl.ɪ prə.ˈseɪ.kwiː/ /; / /ˈnɒl.ə prə.ˈseɪ.kwiː/ (BrE)
(of a prosecutor) to formally discontinue or abandon prosecution of a charge by entering a record in court.

Examples

To nolle prosequi a case
Counsel argued that the state should nolle prosequi given the lack of credible witnesses.
The district attorney chose to nolle prosequi the case after new evidence came to light.
The judge asked whether the prosecution planned to nolle prosequi any of the charges.
