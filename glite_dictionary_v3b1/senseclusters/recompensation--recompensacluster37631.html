recompensation

noun
reward or payment
8.2
Pronunciation: /re.kəm.pən.ˈseɪ.ʃən/ /; / /re.kəm.pen.ˈseɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /re.kəm.pen.ˈseɪ.ʃən/ /; / /re.kəm.pən.ˈseɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
money or something given to reward someone for their effort or to make up for a loss, damage, or injury

Examples

Financial recompensation
The company offered recompensation to employees who worked overtime during the busy season.
He did not seek recompensation for his help, believing kindness was its own reward.
After the accident, the victim received recompensation for the damages caused.
The company offered recompensation to customers whose orders were lost during shipping.
Victims of the disaster sought recompensation for their damaged homes and belongings.
He received recompensation after proving the injury was caused by negligence.
After the accident, the company provided recompensation to the injured workers.
Victims of the error received recompensation for their inconvenience and losses.
The court ordered recompensation to be paid to those affected by the breach.
