parabasis

noun
direct address to audience
9.9
Pronunciation: / pəˈræbəsɪs / (AmE), / pəˈræbəsɪs / (BrE)
a moment in a play or literary work, especially in ancient Greek comedy, when the speaker or chorus directly addresses the audience, breaking the narrative flow

Examples

A parabasis moment
The playwright used a parabasis to comment on the political issues of the time.
In the middle of the story, a sudden parabasis draws the reader's attention to the author's personal views.
Ancient Greek comedies often featured a parabasis where the chorus spoke directly to the audience.
During the parabasis, the chorus stepped forward to share the playwright's opinions with the audience.
Scholars often analyze the parabasis to understand the social commentary in Greek comedies.
The parabasis interrupts the story, allowing the chorus to address contemporary issues directly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: soliloquy - a speech in a play where a character speaks their thoughts aloud
