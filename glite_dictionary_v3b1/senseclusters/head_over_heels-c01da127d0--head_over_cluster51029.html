head over heels

adverb
in disorderly haste
4.2 0.06/m
Pronunciation: / h…õd o äv…ö hilz / (AmE), / h…õd …ô äv…ô hiÀêlz / (BrE)
In a state of disorderly haste; moving quickly and often clumsily or without control.

Examples

Head over heels in a rush
In his hurry to board the train, he rushed head over heels through the crowd.
Papers flew everywhere as the wind sent her notes head over heels across the room.
The children ran head over heels down the hill to catch the ice cream truck.

Synonyms

  • headlong
    6.8 0.59/m
    headlong is very hasty and rash, while head over heels describes moving quickly in a disorderly, often clumsy way.
  • headfirst
    3.3 1.27/m
    headfirst means leading with the head when plunging or moving forward, while head over heels means rushing in disorder and often clumsily.
