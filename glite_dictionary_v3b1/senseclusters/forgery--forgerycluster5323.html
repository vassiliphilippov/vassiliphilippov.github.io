forgery

noun
criminal falsification of documents or objects
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrdʒɚi / (AmE), / ˈfɔːdʒəri / (BrE)
The crime of making a false document, signature, or imitation of an object with the intent to deceive or defraud.

Examples

Forgery of documents
forgery is a serious offense that can result in imprisonment.
Forgery
The art dealer was arrested for selling a forgery of a famous painting.
The bank detected a forgery on one of the checks submitted for payment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: authenticity - the quality of being genuine or real
  • synonym: counterfeiting - the crime of making an exact imitation of something, usually money, with the intent to deceive
