discount rate

noun
interest rate deducted in advance on a loan
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪskaʊnt ˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɪskaʊnt ˌreɪt / (BrE)
the rate of interest charged on a loan, with the interest amount deducted in advance for a set period, usually expressed as an annual percentage

Examples

Loan discount rate
At a lower discount rate, businesses can save more money on advance loans.
The bank charges a discount rate of 5% on short-term commercial loans.
Understanding the discount rate is crucial when calculating the true cost of borrowing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interest rate - the percentage charged on a loan or paid on deposits
