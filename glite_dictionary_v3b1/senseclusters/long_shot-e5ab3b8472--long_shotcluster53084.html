long shot

noun
unlikely contender or winner
7.3
Pronunciation: / lɔŋ ʃɑt / (AmE), / lɒŋ ʃɒt / (BrE)
A person or thing regarded as unlikely to succeed or win, especially in a competition or contest.

Examples

A long shot wins
Despite being a long shot, the horse pulled ahead in the final stretch and won the race.
He entered the tournament as a long shot, but surprised everyone by reaching the finals.
Many investors considered the tech startup a long shot in the crowded market.

Related Terms

  • synonym: underdog - a competitor thought to have little chance of winning
