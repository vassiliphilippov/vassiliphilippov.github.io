pontification

noun
term of a pontiff
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːntɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɒntɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the period of office, authority, or leadership of a pontiff, such as a pope or bishop

Examples

Pontification of a pope
During the pontification of Pope John Paul II, many significant changes occurred in the Catholic Church.
Historians often compare the pontification of different popes to understand shifts in church policy.
The pontification of the new bishop brought renewed energy to the local diocese.

Related Terms

  • synonym: papacy - the office or term of a pope
  • synonym: reign - the period during which a sovereign rules
