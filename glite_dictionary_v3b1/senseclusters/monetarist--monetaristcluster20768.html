monetarist

noun
advocate of the monetary supply theory in economics
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːnɪtərɪst / (AmE), / ˈmɒnɪtərɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports the theory that variations in the money supply are the main cause of economic fluctuations

Examples

A monetarist economist
Critics argue that monetarist policies ignore other crucial economic factors.
Milton Friedman was the most influential monetarist of the twentieth century.
The central bank governor described herself as a committed monetarist.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Keynesian - advocate of the economic theories of John Maynard Keynes, often contrasted with monetarism
  • synonym: monetary - relating to money or the money supply
