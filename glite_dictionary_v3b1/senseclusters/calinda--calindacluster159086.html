calinda

noun
Afro-Caribbean dance
Pronunciation: / kəˈlɪndə / (AmE), / kəˈlɪndə / (BrE)
a traditional Afro-Caribbean dance, often performed at festivals, that combines music, singing, and sometimes martial arts or stick-fighting

Examples

Energetic calinda dance
The crowd gathered to watch the energetic calinda at the carnival.
Drummers set the rhythm for the dancers performing the calinda in the village square.
Children learned the steps of the calinda as part of their cultural heritage.
During the festival, performers demonstrated the energetic calinda for an enthusiastic crowd.
The tradition of calinda has been passed down through generations in some Caribbean villages.
Spectators cheered as two skilled fighters engaged in a lively calinda match.

Related Terms

  • synonym: capoeira - a Brazilian martial art that combines dance, acrobatics, and music
  • synonym: limbo - a traditional Caribbean dance involving bending backward under a bar
  • synonym: stick-fighting - a form of combat using sticks as weapons
