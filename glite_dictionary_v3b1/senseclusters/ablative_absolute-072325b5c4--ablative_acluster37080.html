ablative absolute

noun
Latin grammatical construction; noun+modifier phrase
Pronunciation: / ˈæblətɪv ˈæbsəˌluːt / (AmE), / ˈablətɪv ˈabsəluːt / (BrE)
(in Latin grammar) a construction consisting of a noun and participle both in the ablative case, forming a phrase that modifies the sentence as a whole and is grammatically independent of the rest of the sentence.
