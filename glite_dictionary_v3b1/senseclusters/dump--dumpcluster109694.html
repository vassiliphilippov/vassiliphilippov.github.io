dump

verb
sell goods at artificially low prices
6.7 2.87/m
Pronunciation: / dÊŒmp / (AmE), / dÊŒmp / (BrE)
to sell goods at artificially low prices, especially in order to undermine competitors or gain market share

Examples

To dump cheap goods
After losing market share, the manufacturer decided to dump excess stock overseas.
Several countries imposed tariffs to prevent foreign firms from dumping steel at below-market prices.
The company was accused of dumping cheap products in the European market.

Synonyms

  • undercut
    4.4 2.31/m
    undercut is selling at a lower price than rivals to win customers, while dump means selling at artificially low prices to harm competitors.
  • undersell
    4.7 0.12/m
    undersell is selling for a lower price than a competitor to attract buyers, while dump involves selling so cheaply to undermine competitors.
  • underprice
    5.2 0.44/m
    underprice means selling below market or real value, often by mistake or strategy, while dump stresses very low pricing to harm rivals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: undersell - to sell goods at a lower price than a competitor
