weak

adjective
lacking moral strength or will
3.6
Pronunciation: / wik / (AmE), / wiːk / (BrE)
lacking moral strength, courage, or determination; easily swayed or overcome by temptation or adversity.

Examples

Weak will
Although he appears confident, he has a weak will when it comes to standing up for himself.
His weak resolve crumbled under pressure from his peers.
She admitted she was weak and unable to resist temptation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cowardly - lacking courage
  • antonym: strong - having great moral strength or courage
