plainspoken

adjective
using simple and direct language
3.4 3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈpleɪnˌspoʊkən / (AmE), / ˈpleɪnˌspəʊkən / (BrE)
expressing oneself or speaking in a simple, direct, and honest way, without using complicated language or hiding meaning

Examples

Plainspoken leader
As a plainspoken leader, she earned respect for her honesty and clarity.
Her plainspoken advice made it easy for everyone to understand what needed to be done.
The senator was known for his plainspoken responses, which appealed to many voters.
His plainspoken manner sometimes surprises people who are used to more tactful conversation.
She is known for her plainspoken approach to difficult topics, never avoiding the hard questions.
The senator gained respect for his plainspoken honesty during debates.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blunt - straightforward in speech, even if not polite
  • antonym: evasive - tending to avoid commitment or self-revelation, especially by responding only indirectly
  • synonym: frank - open, honest, and direct in speech or writing
  • synonym: straightforward - honest, clear, and direct in speech or manner
