Pygmalion

proper noun
mythical king who loved a statue he created
Pronunciation: / pɪɡˈmeɪliən / (AmE), / pɪɡˈmeɪlɪən / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a king who carved a statue of a woman, fell in love with it, and had it brought to life by Aphrodite.

Examples

Pygmalion statue story
In the story, Pygmalion falls deeply in love with the statue he carved from ivory.
Many people reference Pygmalion when discussing the power of art to shape reality.
The myth of Pygmalion has inspired countless artists and writers throughout the centuries.
