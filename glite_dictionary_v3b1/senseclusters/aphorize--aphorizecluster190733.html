aphorize

verb
to speak or write in aphorisms
9.9 8.3
Pronunciation: /ˈæf.ə.raɪz/ (AmE), /ˈæf.ə.raɪz/ (BrE)
to express oneself by means of aphorisms; to write or speak in concise, pithy statements of general truth.

Examples

To aphorize about success
During the interview, he aphorized about success and happiness with memorable, concise phrases.
She tends to aphorize when discussing life's great questions, always offering a witty maxim or two.
The philosopher preferred to aphorize rather than write long essays.
She tends to aphorise rather than elaborate, giving brief but insightful comments.
The philosopher would often aphorise during his lectures, leaving the audience with memorable maxims.
Writers who aphorise can make profound observations in just a few words.
