katamorphism

noun
breakdown of minerals near surface
9.9
Pronunciation: /kæt.ə.ˈmɔːr.fɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /kæt.ə.ˈmɔː.fɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
Metamorphism occurring at or near the earth's surface that breaks down complex minerals into simpler ones.

Examples

Katamorphism near surface
Geologists study katamorphism to understand how rocks are broken down at the surface.
The soil's mineral content was altered through extensive katamorphism over thousands of years.
Weathering processes often lead to katamorphism in the upper layers of the earth's crust.
