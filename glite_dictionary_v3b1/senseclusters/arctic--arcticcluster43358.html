arctic

adjective
extremely cold
4.5 1.06/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…ëÀêrk.t…™k / (AmE), / Àà…ëÀêk.t…™k / (BrE)
Extremely cold; resembling the conditions of the Arctic region.

Examples

Arctic weather
After stepping outside on that arctic morning, her breath instantly turned to mist in the frigid air.
The wind blowing off the lake made the city feel arctic in the middle of January.
They huddled by the fire, escaping the arctic chill that crept through the old cabin.

Synonyms

  • glacial
    6.1 0.17/m
    glacial suggests ice like, glacier cold and is more formal, while arctic means the severe polar cold of the Arctic region.
  • frigid
    4.1 2.97/m
    frigid describes very low temperature in general places, while arctic names the extreme, polar cold of the Arctic region.
  • freezing cold
    1.3 0.48/m
    freezing cold is a common phrase for temperatures at or below freezing, while arctic refers to much more extreme polar conditions.
  • icy
    1.0 7.35/m
    icy describes being covered with ice or a chilly feeling and can be literal, while arctic means extreme polar cold.
