multiplane

noun
aircraft with many wings
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌltɪˌpleɪn / (AmE), / ˈmʌltɪˌpleɪn / (BrE)
an aircraft with more than two main supporting wings arranged one above the other

Examples

Old multiplane aircraft
The museum displays a rare multiplane from the early days of aviation.
Engineers studied the advantages and disadvantages of the multiplane design for increased lift.
Few modern aircraft use the multiplane configuration due to its complexity and drag.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: biplane - an aircraft with two main wings stacked one above the other
  • degree lower: monoplane - an aircraft with a single main wing
  • degree lower: triplane - an aircraft with three main wings stacked above each other
