hackling

noun
fiber combing process
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhæk.lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhæk.lɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of combing plant fibers, such as flax, to separate, clean, and align them for spinning

Examples

The hackling of flax
The hackling of flax is an important step in making high-quality linen.
After retting, the fibers undergo hackling to remove any remaining impurities.
Skilled workers performed hackling by hand before the invention of modern machinery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carding - the process of disentangling and cleaning fibers before spinning
  • synonym: combing - the act of separating and aligning fibers with a comb
