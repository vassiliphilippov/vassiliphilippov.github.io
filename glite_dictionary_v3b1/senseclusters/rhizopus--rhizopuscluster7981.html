rhizopus

noun
a genus of rot-causing fungi
3.8 8.5
Pronunciation: /raɪ.ˈzoʊ.pəs/ (AmE), /raɪ.ˈzəʊ.pəs/ /; / /raɪ.ˈzɒ.pəs/ (BrE)
A genus of fungi commonly found on decaying organic material, some species of which cause rot in fruits and vegetables.

Examples

Rhizopus mold growth
Rhizopus is often responsible for the black bread mold seen on stale loaves.
Certain species of rhizopus can spoil fruits and cause soft rot in vegetables.
In laboratories, rhizopus is used to study fungal growth and decomposition.
