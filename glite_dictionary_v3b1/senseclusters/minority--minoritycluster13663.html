minority

noun
the smaller number or part in a group
1.8 2.8
Pronunciation: / məˈnɔːrəti / (AmE), / maɪˈnɒrəti / (BrE)
the smaller number or part; the group or party with fewer members or votes than the majority in a situation involving division or votes

Examples

Minority of voters
Although the minority lost the vote, their views were still respected.
She found herself in a minority when the final decision was made.
The bill was opposed by a minority of the committee members.
