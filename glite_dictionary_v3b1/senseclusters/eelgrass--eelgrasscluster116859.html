eelgrass

noun
submerged marine plant with long leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈilˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈiːlɡrɑːs /; / ˈiːlˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a submerged aquatic plant with long, narrow leaves that grows underwater and forms meadows, providing habitat for marine animals

Examples

Underwater eelgrass meadows
Large underwater meadows of eelgrass provide vital habitats for many marine creatures.
Scientists are studying how pollution affects the growth of eelgrass along the coast.
Young fish often hide among the leaves of eelgrass to escape predators.
Many fish and invertebrates use eelgrass meadows as important nursery habitats.
Researchers carefully mapped the distribution of eelgrass along the coastline.
The restoration of eelgrass beds has been vital for improving water quality in the bay.
