quasi-stellar radio source

noun
a type of star-like astronomical object emitting radio waves (quasar)
Pronunciation: / ˌkwɑːzaɪˈstɛlɚ ˈreɪdioʊ sɔːrs / (AmE), / ˌkwɑːzaɪˈstɛlə ˈreɪdiəʊ sɔːs / (BrE)
An astronomical object resembling a star in appearance that emits strong radio waves and other energy; now commonly known as a quasar.

Examples

A bright quasi-stellar radio source
A quasi-stellar radio source can be billions of light-years away, yet visible with powerful telescopes.
Researchers identified several new quasi-stellar radio sources using the Very Large Array.
The discovery of a quasi-stellar radio source challenged astronomers' understanding of the universe.

Related Terms

  • synonym: quasar - an extremely luminous active galactic nucleus, short for quasi-stellar object
