transformability

noun
ability to be genetically changed
Pronunciation: / trænsˌfɔrməˈbɪləti / (AmE), / trænsˌfɔːməˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the ability of a cell, organism, or strain to take up external DNA and become genetically altered

Examples

Bacterial transformability
The transformability of the bacteria was crucial for the success of the genetic experiment.
Scientists measured the transformability of different strains to identify the most suitable one for DNA uptake.
High transformability allows researchers to introduce new genes into cells more efficiently.

Related Terms

  • synonym: competence - the ability of a cell to take up DNA from its environment
