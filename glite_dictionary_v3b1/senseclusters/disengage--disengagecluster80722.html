disengage

verb
release or separate from something
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪs.ɪnˈɡeɪdʒ /; / ˌdɪsɪnˈɡeɪdʒ /; / ˌdɪsənˈɡeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.ɪnˈɡeɪdʒ /; / ˌdɪsɪnˈɡeɪdʒ / (BrE)
to release or separate from something that holds, connects, or involves

Examples

To disengage the grip
She tried to disengage her arm from the man's grip.
The commander ordered the troops to disengage from the front line.
You must disengage the safety catch before starting the engine.
She learned to disengage her emotions from the stressful situation.
The gears disengage when the car is shifted into neutral.
When the lever is pulled, the safety mechanism will disengage automatically.
Before moving the furniture, we must disengage all the cables from the wall.
She disengaged the gears to stop the motor smoothly.
The engineer had to disengage the safety lock before opening the machine.

Related Terms

  • synonym: detach - to separate from something to which it is attached
  • antonym: engage - to cause to become involved or attached
  • synonym: free - to make or become free; to release
  • synonym: release - to let go or set free from confinement or attachment
