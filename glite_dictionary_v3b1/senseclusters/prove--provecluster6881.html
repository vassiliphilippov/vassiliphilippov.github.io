prove

verb
establish the validity of a will legally
1.0 2.6
Pronunciation: / pruv / (AmE), / pruːv / (BrE)
to show that something is true or correct by providing evidence, argument, or demonstration (including legal or mathematical proof)

Examples

To prove a fact
If no one contests the document, it is usually straightforward to prove a will.
She hired a solicitor to help prove her father's will after his death.
The executor must prove the will in court before assets can be distributed.
It took weeks to prove that the new method actually worked.
Scientists must prove their theories with careful experimentation and data analysis.
The lawyer tried to prove his client's innocence in court.
Despite the critics' doubts, her theory proved correct.
He proved to be a reliable friend in difficult times.
The new system proved very effective in reducing errors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: demonstrate - to show clearly by giving proof or evidence
  • antonym: disprove - to show that something is not true or correct
  • synonym: probate - the legal process of proving a will's validity
  • synonym: show - to make something clear or visible, often with evidence
  • synonym: turn out - to result or end up in a particular way
