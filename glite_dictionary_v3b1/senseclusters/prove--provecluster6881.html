prove

verb
establish the validity of a will legally
3.1 150/m
Pronunciation: / pruv / (AmE), / pruÀêv / (BrE)
to show that something is true or correct by providing evidence, argument, or demonstration (including legal or mathematical proof)

Examples

To prove a fact
If no one contests the document, it is usually straightforward to prove a will.
She hired a solicitor to help prove her father's will after his death.
The executor must prove the will in court before assets can be distributed.
It took weeks to prove that the new method actually worked.
Scientists must prove their theories with careful experimentation and data analysis.
The lawyer tried to prove his client's innocence in court.
Despite the critics' doubts, her theory proved correct.
He proved to be a reliable friend in difficult times.
The new system proved very effective in reducing errors.

Synonyms

    4.1 30/m
    establish is used to show that something is true and accepted by others, while prove focuses on giving clear evidence.
  • demonstrate
    3.0 80/m
    demonstrate often uses examples or experiments to show a point, while prove demands firm evidence or formal proof.
  • substantiate
    7.2 4/m
    substantiate gives evidence to support a claim, while prove means to show that the claim is fully true.
  • verify
    3.5 19/m
    verify checks facts or details to see if they are correct, while prove shows something is true beyond doubt.
  • confirm
    1.8 60/m
    confirm makes an earlier report or idea certain or official, while prove requires full evidence or demonstration.
  • validate
    3.9 10/m
    validate makes a method or choice officially acceptable or correct, while prove demonstrates truth with evidence.
  • show
    1.0 300/m
    show means make something visible or obvious, often informally, while prove means give strong evidence or formal proof.
