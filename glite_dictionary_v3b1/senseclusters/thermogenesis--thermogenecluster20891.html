thermogenesis

noun
heat production in organisms
7.4
Pronunciation: /θɜːr.mə.ˈdʒen.ɪs/ /; / /θɜːr.mə.ˈdʒen.əs/ (AmE), /θɜː.mə.ˈdʒen.ɪs/ /; / /θɜː.mə.ˈdʒen.əs/ (BrE)
the process by which living organisms produce heat, especially to maintain body temperature

Examples

Body thermogenesis
Shivering is one way the body increases thermogenesis to stay warm.
Certain foods can boost thermogenesis and help regulate body temperature.
Brown fat tissue is important for thermogenesis in newborn babies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hypothermia - a dangerous drop in body temperature
  • synonym: metabolism - the chemical processes that occur within a living organism to maintain life
