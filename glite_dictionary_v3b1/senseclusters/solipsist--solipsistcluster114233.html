solipsist

noun
self-only believer
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑːlɪpsɪst / (AmE), / ˈsɒlɪpsɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that only their own mind or self is sure to exist

Examples

Philosophical solipsist
The solipsist argued that nothing outside his own mind could be proven to exist.
Some philosophers debate whether a true solipsist can ever trust their senses.
Her friends found her views extreme, calling her a modern solipsist.
The philosopher was often called a solipsist because of his radical ideas about reality.
Some argue that a true solipsist cannot meaningfully communicate with others.
In literature, a solipsist character may struggle to trust the world around them.

Related Terms

  • synonym: egocentrist - a person who is excessively focused on themselves
  • antonym: realist - a person who accepts the existence of an external reality
