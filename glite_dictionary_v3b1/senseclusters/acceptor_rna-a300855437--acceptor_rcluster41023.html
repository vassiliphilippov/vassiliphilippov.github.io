acceptor RNA

noun
type of RNA involved in protein synthesis
6.5
Pronunciation: / əkˈsɛptɚ ɑrˌɛnˈeɪ / (AmE), / əkˈsɛptə ɑːˌɛnˈeɪ / (BrE)
a type of RNA molecule (tRNA) that attaches the appropriate amino acid to a growing protein chain during synthesis, according to genetic code instructions from mRNA

Examples

The acceptor RNA molecule
A mutation in acceptor RNA can disrupt the formation of functional proteins.
Scientists studied how acceptor RNA interacts with enzymes in the ribosome.
The acceptor RNA helps ensure that each amino acid is added in the correct order during protein synthesis.

Related Terms
