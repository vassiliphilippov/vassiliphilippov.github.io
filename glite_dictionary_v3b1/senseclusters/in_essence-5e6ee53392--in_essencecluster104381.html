in essence

adverb
basically; fundamentally
4.2
Pronunciation: / ɪn ˈɛsəns / (AmE), / ɪn ˈesəns / (BrE)
used to emphasize the most important or fundamental aspect of something, ignoring details or exceptions

Examples

In essence means
In essence, she believes that honesty is the best policy.
In essence, the two proposals offer the same benefits.
While their methods differ, their goals are in essence identical.

Related Terms

  • synonym: in principle - in theory or in fundamental terms, though not necessarily in all details
