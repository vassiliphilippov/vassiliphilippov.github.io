voicelessness

noun
absence of vocal cord vibration in speech sounds
7.2 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈvɔɪs.ləs.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈvɔɪs.ləs.nəs/ (BrE)
(Phonetics) the quality of a speech sound being produced without vibration of the vocal cords

Examples

Voicelessness of consonants
In English, the voicelessness of certain consonants can change the meaning of a word.
Students learned to produce voicelessness by not vibrating their vocal cords during pronunciation exercises.
The voicelessness of the sound /s/ distinguishes it from its voiced counterpart /z/.
Many consonants, such as 'p' and 't', are characterized by their voicelessness.
The distinction between voicelessness and voicing is important when learning English pronunciation.
Whispered speech is notable for its complete voicelessness throughout.
