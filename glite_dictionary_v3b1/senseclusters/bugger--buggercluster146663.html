bugger

verb
to ruin something
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌɡər / (AmE), / ˈbʌɡə / (BrE)
to damage, spoil, or ruin something, often accidentally or carelessly.

Examples

To bugger a phone
He accidentally buggered his phone by dropping it in water.
The mechanic said the engine was completely buggered after the accident.
If you don't follow the instructions, you might bugger the whole project.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fix - to repair or restore something that is broken
  • synonym: spoil - to make something less good or enjoyable
  • synonym: wreck - to completely destroy or severely damage something
