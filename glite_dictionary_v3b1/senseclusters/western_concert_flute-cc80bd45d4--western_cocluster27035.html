Western concert flute

noun
standard side-blown woodwind instrument
6.2 3.8
Pronunciation: /ˈwe.stərn ˈkɑːn.sərt fluːt/ /; / /ˈhwe.stərn ˈkɑːn.sərt fluːt/ (AmE), /ˈwe.stən ˈkɒn.sət fluːt/ (BrE)
A side-blown woodwind musical instrument, usually made of metal or wood, commonly used in orchestras and bands, also known as the transverse flute or Boehm flute.

Examples

The Western concert flute
Many students begin with the recorder before moving on to the Western concert flute for advanced music studies.
She has been playing the Western concert flute in the orchestra since she was a teenager.
The Western concert flute is prized for its clear, bright, and expressive tone.

  • antonym: recorder - a woodwind instrument with a whistle mouthpiece
