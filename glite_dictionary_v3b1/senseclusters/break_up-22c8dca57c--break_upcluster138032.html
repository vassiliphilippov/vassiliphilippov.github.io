break up

verb
cause a group to disperse
1.0
Pronunciation: / breɪk ˈʌp /; / breɪk ʌp / (AmE), / breɪk ˈʌp /; / breɪk ʌp / (BrE)
to cause a group, meeting, or organization to end or disperse

Examples

To break up a meeting
A loud whistle was used to break up the crowd at the end of the match.
Police arrived to break up the protest before it turned violent.
The teacher had to break up the group of students arguing in the hallway.
After three hours, the committee decided to break up for the day.
Schools usually break up for the holidays in mid-December.
The meeting will break up at five o'clock sharp, so please wrap up your points.
After years of disagreement, the partners decided to break up the business.
The government voted to break up the monopoly to encourage competition.
The teacher had to break up the group when it grew too large to manage.
