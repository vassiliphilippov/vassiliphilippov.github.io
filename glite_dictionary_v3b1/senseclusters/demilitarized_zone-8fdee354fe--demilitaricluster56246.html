demilitarized zone

noun
an area where military forces are forbidden
4.5 3.9
Pronunciation: /diː.ˈmɪl.ɪ.tə.raɪzd zoʊn/ /; / /dɪ.ˈmɪl.ɪ.tə.raɪzd zoʊn/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈmɪl.ɪ.tə.raɪzd zəʊn/ /; / /diː.ˈmɪl.ɪ.tə.raɪzd zəʊn/ (BrE)
an area in which military forces, operations, or installations are not permitted, usually established by treaty or agreement between parties in conflict

Examples

A demilitarized zone
After years of negotiation, the nations agreed to establish a demilitarized zone as a buffer area.
The peace treaty specified that both armies must withdraw from the demilitarized zone by the end of the month.
Tourists are not allowed to enter the demilitarized zone along the border between the two countries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: no man's land - land between two enemy lines, often unoccupied or unclaimed
