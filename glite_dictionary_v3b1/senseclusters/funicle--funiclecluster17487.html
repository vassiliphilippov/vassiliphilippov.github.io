funicle

noun
stalk attaching ovule or seed to ovary
Pronunciation: / ˈfjʊnɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈfjʊnɪkəl / (BrE)
the stalk that attaches an ovule or seed to the ovary wall in a plant

Examples

The funicle stalk
Botanists carefully examined the funicle under a microscope to understand its structure.
Damage to the funicle can affect seed development and dispersal.
The funicle is a slender stalk that connects the seed to the placenta in many flowering plants.
