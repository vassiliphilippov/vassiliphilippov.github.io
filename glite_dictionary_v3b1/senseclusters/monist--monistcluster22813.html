monist

adjective
supporting monism
Pronunciation: / ˈmoʊnɪst / (AmE), / ˈməʊnɪst / (BrE)
relating to or supporting the philosophical view that all things are fundamentally one substance or principle.

Examples

Monist theory
The philosopher presented a monist argument that everything in the universe is interconnected.
Her monist perspective led her to see mind and matter as two aspects of the same reality.
A monist approach to ethics suggests that all moral values stem from a single principle.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dualistic - relating to the belief that reality consists of two fundamental substances or principles
  • synonym: monistic - relating to or characterized by monism
