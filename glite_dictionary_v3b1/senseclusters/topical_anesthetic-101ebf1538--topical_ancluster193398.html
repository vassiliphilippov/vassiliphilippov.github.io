topical anesthetic

noun
anesthetic applied to the surface of the body
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑːpɪkəl ˌænəsˈθɛtɪk /; / ˈtɑpɪkəl əˈnɛsθətɪk / (AmE), / ˈtɒpɪkəl ˌanɪsˈθɛtɪk /; / ˈtɒpɪkəl əˈniːsθətɪk / (BrE)
A drug or preparation that produces numbness in a specific area of the body when applied to the skin or mucous membranes.

Examples

A topical anesthetic cream
A topical anesthetic cream can help reduce pain during minor skin procedures.
Before removing the wart, the doctor used a topical anesthetic to make the process more comfortable.
The dentist applied a topical anesthetic to numb the area before giving the injection.

Related Terms

  • antonym: general anaesthetic - an anaesthetic that affects the entire body and induces unconsciousness
  • antonym: general anesthetic - anesthetic that induces unconsciousness and loss of sensation throughout the body
  • synonym: local anaesthetic - an anaesthetic that produces loss of sensation in a limited area
  • synonym: local anesthetic - an anesthetic that numbs a specific area of the body
