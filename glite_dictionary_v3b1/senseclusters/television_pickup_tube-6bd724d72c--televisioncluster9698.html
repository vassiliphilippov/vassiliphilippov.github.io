television pickup tube

noun
device converting images to electronic signals in TV cameras
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛləˌvɪʒən ˈpɪkˌʌp tuːb / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪˌvɪʒən ˈpɪkʌp tjuːb / (BrE)
an electronic tube that rapidly scans an optical image and converts it into electronic signals, used in early television cameras

Examples

A television pickup tube device
A television pickup tube converts light from a scene into an electronic signal for broadcast.
Inventors improved the television pickup tube to enhance image quality in early television systems.
The early studio cameras used a television pickup tube to capture black-and-white images.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: cathode ray tube - a vacuum tube used to display images in older television sets and monitors
  • synonym: video camera tube - an electronic device that converts images into electrical signals for television
