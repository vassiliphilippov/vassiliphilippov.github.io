embryoid

noun
lab-grown embryo-like mass
Pronunciation: / ˈɛm.briˌɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈɛm.brɪˌɔɪd / (BrE)
a mass of cells grown in laboratory conditions that resembles an embryo in structure or development

Examples

A lab-grown embryoid
Scientists created an embryoid to better understand the earliest stages of human development.
The laboratory used an embryoid model to test the effects of new drugs on cell growth.
By studying the embryoid, researchers gained insights into how organs begin to form.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embryo - an early stage of development in living organisms
  • synonym: organoid - a miniaturized and simplified version of an organ produced in vitro
