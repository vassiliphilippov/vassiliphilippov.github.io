sclerotic thinking

noun
rigid, inflexible thought processes
9.9
Pronunciation: / skləˈrɑːtɪk ˈθɪŋkɪŋ / (AmE), / sklɪəˈrɒtɪk ˈθɪŋkɪŋ / (BrE)
rigid and inflexible ways of thinking, resistant to change or new ideas

Examples

Sclerotic thinking resists change
Academic institutions can sometimes be hampered by sclerotic thinking that resists new teaching methods.
Her sclerotic thinking prevented the team from adopting any innovative approaches.
The company suffered from sclerotic thinking among its leadership, stifling growth and change.

Related Terms

  • antonym: open-mindedness - willingness to consider new ideas
  • synonym: rigid thinking - thinking that is strict, unbending, and not open to change
