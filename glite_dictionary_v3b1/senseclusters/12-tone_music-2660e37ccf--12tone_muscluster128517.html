12-tone music

noun
serial music using all twelve chromatic tones
9.1 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààtw…õlv to än ÀàmjuÀêz…™k / (AmE), / Ààtw…õlv t…ô än ÀàmjuÀêz…™k / (BrE)
A style of serial music using a fixed order of all twelve tones in the chromatic scale, introduced by Arnold Schoenberg.

Examples

12-tone music style
Arnold Schoenberg is credited with developing 12-tone music as a compositional method.
Many composers experimented with 12-tone music during the twentieth century.
Understanding 12-tone music requires familiarity with the chromatic scale and serial techniques.

Synonyms

  • twelve-tone music
    8.6 0.01/m
    twelve-tone music names the full composition style used in scores and books, while 12-tone music is the shorter label often used in headings.
  • twelve-tone system
    9.9 0.01/m
    twelve-tone system describes the compositional method that orders all twelve notes, while 12-tone music is the musical style that uses that method.
  • 12-tone system
    9.3 0.00/m
    12-tone system is the numeric label for Schoenberg's compositional method, while 12-tone music is the musical style created from that method.
