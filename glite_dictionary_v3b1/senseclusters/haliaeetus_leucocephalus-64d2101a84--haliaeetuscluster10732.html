Haliaeetus leucocephalus

noun
bald eagle of North America
Pronunciation: / ˌheɪliˈiːtəs ˌluːkoʊˈsɛfələs / (AmE), / ˌhæliˈiːtəs ˌluːkəˈsɛfələs / (BrE)
A large eagle native to North America, having a white head and tail with dark wings and body; commonly known as the bald eagle.

Examples

The Haliaeetus leucocephalus eagle
A Haliaeetus leucocephalus soared over the river, searching for fish.
Strict protections have helped the population of Haliaeetus leucocephalus recover across the United States.
The Haliaeetus leucocephalus is easily recognized by its striking white head and yellow beak.
