an animal that maintains a constant body temperature by producing heat internally

Examples

An endotherm animal
A mammal is an endotherm because it can keep its body warm even in cold environments.
Unlike reptiles, endotherms do not depend on the sun to regulate their body temperature.
Birds are classic examples of endotherms that remain active in winter.
