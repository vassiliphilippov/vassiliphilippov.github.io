formidable

adjective
inspiring fear or respect
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrmɪdəbəl, fərˈmɪdəbəl /; / ˈfɔrmɪdəbəl, fɔrˈmɪdəbəl / (AmE), / ˈfɔːmɪdəbl, fəˈmɪdəbl /; / ˈfɔːmɪdəbəl, fəˈmɪdəbəl / (BrE)
inspiring fear or respect because of being very large, powerful, intense, or capable

Examples

A formidable mountain
Climbing the formidable mountain left us in awe of nature’s power.
She faced a formidable task at work, but she tackled it with determination.
The champion boxer was a formidable opponent in the ring, known for his strength and speed.
She overcame a formidable series of obstacles to achieve her dream.
The castle was protected by a formidable stone wall and deep moat.
The team faced a formidable opponent in the championship final.

Related Terms

  • synonym: daunting - seeming difficult to deal with; intimidating
  • synonym: impressive - evoking admiration through size, quality, or skill
  • synonym: intimidating - making someone feel afraid or nervous
  • antonym: reassuring - making someone feel less worried; calming
  • antonym: weak - lacking strength or power
