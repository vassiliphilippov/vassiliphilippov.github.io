reweigh

verb
to reassess value
5.5
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈweɪ/ /; / /rə.ˈweɪ/ (AmE), /riː.ˈweɪ/ /; / /rə.ˈweɪ/ (BrE)
to consider or evaluate something again, especially to reassess its importance, merits, or consequences

Examples

To reweigh the evidence
After hearing the new arguments, the judge decided to reweigh the evidence before making a final ruling.
It is important to reweigh your priorities when major life changes occur.
The team had to reweigh their options after the unexpected delay in the project.
