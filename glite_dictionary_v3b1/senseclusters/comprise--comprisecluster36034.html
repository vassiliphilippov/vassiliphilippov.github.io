Synonyms

  • consist
    2.5 60/m
    consist is used with 'of' to say what something is made of, while comprise names the whole that includes those parts.
  • make up
    2.7 20/m
    make up describes parts joining to create a whole in everyday speech, while comprise sounds more formal and names the whole.
  • constitute
    4.4 20/m
    constitute is a formal verb meaning parts form or make something, while comprise often appears to name the whole that includes those parts.
  • compose
    3.8 18/m
    compose often has parts as the subject that build the whole, while comprise usually puts the whole first and lists its parts.
  • form
    2.1 15/m
    form can mean take shape or make a part of something, while comprise usually names a whole that includes parts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: constitute - to make up or form (something)
  • antonym: exclude - to leave out or not include
  • synonym: include - to contain, as part of a whole
