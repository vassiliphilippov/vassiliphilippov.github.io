African elephant

noun
large African species of elephant
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈæfrɪkən ˈɛlɪfənt / (AmE), / ˈæfrɪkən ˈelɪfənt / (BrE)
A large, gray elephant native to Africa, distinguished by its large flapping ears and ivory tusks.

Examples

The African elephant tusks
Poachers have endangered the African elephant by illegally hunting them for their ivory.
Researchers are studying the migration patterns of the African elephant across the savannah.
The African elephant is recognized for its enormous ears and powerful trunk.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Asian elephant - a species of elephant native to Asia with smaller ears and a convex or level back
