inquisitorially

adverb
in an investigative way
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌkwɪzɪˈtɔːriəli / (AmE), / ɪnˌkwɪzɪˈtɔːriəli / (BrE)
in a way that involves intense or official questioning or investigation

Examples

Question inquisitorially
The lawyer questioned the witness inquisitorially, seeking every detail about the incident.
During the hearing, the official acted inquisitorially to uncover the truth.
She listened inquisitorially, probing for inconsistencies in the story.
The detective stared inquisitorially at the suspect, waiting for him to speak.
She asked inquisitorially about every detail of his trip, not missing a thing.
He responded calmly, even though the reporter questioned him inquisitorially about the incident.
