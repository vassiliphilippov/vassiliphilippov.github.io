drop

verb
fall in value or amount
Pronunciation: / drɑp / (AmE), / drɒp / (BrE)
(of a value, price, or level) to decrease or fall to a lower amount.

Examples

Prices drop quickly
After the announcement, the value of the currency dropped to a record low.
Investors were alarmed when oil prices suddenly dropped last week.
The company's profits are expected to drop this quarter due to increased competition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fall - to decrease in amount, number, or value
  • antonym: rise - to increase in amount, number, or value
