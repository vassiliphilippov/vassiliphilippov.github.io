redivision

noun
change in word division
Pronunciation: / ˌriːdɪˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / ˌriːdɪˈvɪʒən / (BrE)
a change in the division of words, syllables, or phonological units in speech or writing, often resulting in a new interpretation or form

Examples

Word redivision
The word 'nickname' is the result of a historical redivision of 'an ekename'.
Linguists study redivision to understand how language evolves over time.
A redivision of syllables in old texts sometimes leads to new word forms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: misdivision - an incorrect division of words or syllables in speech or writing
  • synonym: rebracketing - the process of changing the perceived boundaries between words or syllables
