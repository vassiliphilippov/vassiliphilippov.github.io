shift someone's investments

verb
move assets from one investment to another
Pronunciation: / ʃɪft ˈsʌmwʌnz ɪnˈvɛstmənts / (AmE), / ʃɪft ˈsʌmwʌnz ɪnˈvɛstmənts / (BrE)
to move financial assets from one type of investment to another, often to adjust risk or seek better returns

Examples

Many retirees choose to shift their investments into lower-risk funds.
