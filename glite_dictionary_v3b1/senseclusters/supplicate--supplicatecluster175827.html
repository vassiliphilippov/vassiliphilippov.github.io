Synonyms

    8.2 2/m

  • entreat
    8.1 0.6/m
    entreat asks earnestly and sometimes urgently in serious requests, while supplicate stresses humble, respectful pleading often tied to prayer.
  • pray
    1.6 12/m
    pray is used to ask a deity or to hope with religious feeling, while supplicate is a humble, earnest plea often toward authority.
  • implore
    5.3 2/m
    implore asks desperately and urgently when someone needs help, while supplicate is more humble and often linked to prayer or authority.
  • beg
    1.0 7/m
    beg asks for help or money in a direct, lowly way, while supplicate sounds more formal, humble, and often prayerful.
