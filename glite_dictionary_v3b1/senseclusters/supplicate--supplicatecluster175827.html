supplicate

verb
ask humbly or in prayer
9.6 0.14/m

to ask or beg for something in a humble and earnest way, especially in prayer or from someone in authority

Examples

To supplicate for mercy
Desperate for help, she supplicated the authorities for mercy.
In times of great need, many choose to supplicate with sincere prayers.
They knelt to supplicate the favor of the gods before the ceremony began.
He knelt to supplicate the king for mercy.
She would supplicate the gods for a good harvest every spring.
The refugees supplicated for safe passage across the border.
He fell to his knees and supplicated the judge for mercy.
She supplicates daily for the safety of her family.
The villagers supplicated the gods for rain during the long drought.

Synonyms

  • beseech
    8.2 1.62/m
    beseech asks someone earnestly and often with strong feeling or formality, while supplicate is more humble and prayerlike.
  • entreat
    8.1 0.73/m
    entreat asks earnestly and sometimes urgently in serious requests, while supplicate stresses humble, respectful pleading often tied to prayer.
  • pray
    1.6 8.35/m
    pray is used to ask a deity or to hope with religious feeling, while supplicate is a humble, earnest plea often toward authority.
  • implore
    5.3 2.17/m
    implore asks desperately and urgently when someone needs help, while supplicate is more humble and often linked to prayer or authority.
  • beg
    1.0 5.10/m
    beg asks for help or money in a direct, lowly way, while supplicate sounds more formal, humble, and often prayerful.
