urinary calculus

noun
stone formed in the urinary tract
Pronunciation: / ˈjʊrəˌnɛri ˈkælkjələs / (AmE), / ˈjʊərɪnəri ˈkalkjʊləs / (BrE)
a stone-like concretion formed in the urinary tract, especially in the kidney

Examples

A large urinary calculus
A large urinary calculus can sometimes block the flow of urine, requiring surgical intervention.
Doctors often use ultrasound to detect a urinary calculus in the kidney.
The patient was diagnosed with a urinary calculus after experiencing severe pain.

  • synonym: kidney stone - a stone formed in the kidney or urinary tract
