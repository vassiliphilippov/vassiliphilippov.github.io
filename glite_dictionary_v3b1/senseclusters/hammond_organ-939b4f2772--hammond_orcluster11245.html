Hammond organ

noun
electronic musical instrument resembling a pipe organ
Pronunciation: / ˈhæmənd ˌɔrɡən / (AmE), / ˈhæmənd ˌɔːɡən / (BrE)
an electronic keyboard instrument invented in the 1930s, designed to simulate the sound of a pipe organ, widely used in jazz, blues, and rock music

Examples

A vintage Hammond organ
He bought a vintage Hammond organ for his recording studio.
The band featured a guitarist, a drummer, and a talented Hammond organ player.
The soulful sound of the Hammond organ defines much of classic jazz music.

Related Terms

  • synonym: electric organ - an electronic keyboard instrument that produces organ-like sounds
  • antonym: pipe organ - a large musical instrument that produces sound by forcing air through pipes
