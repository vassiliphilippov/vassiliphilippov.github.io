polyphonically

adverb
in a polyphonic manner
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːliˈfɑːnɪkli / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈfɒnɪkli / (BrE)
In a polyphonic manner; using or relating to two or more independent melodic lines simultaneously.

Examples

Arranged polyphonically
Baroque music is often arranged polyphonically to showcase complex counterpoint.
Her compositions are remarkable for being structured polyphonically rather than harmonically.
The choir sang the piece polyphonically, with each voice following its own melody.

Related Terms

  • antonym: homophonically - in a homophonic manner (one main melody with accompaniment)
  • synonym: polyphonic - using two or more independent melodies simultaneously
