trireme

noun
ancient warship with three rows of oars
Pronunciation: / ˈtraɪˌrim / (AmE), / ˈtraɪˌriːm / (BrE)
an ancient Greek or Roman warship with three rows of oars on each side

Examples

Ancient trireme warship
A trireme could move quickly and outmaneuver larger, less agile ships.
Historians often study the construction techniques used in building a trireme.
The ancient Athenians deployed their powerful triremes during the battle of Salamis.
