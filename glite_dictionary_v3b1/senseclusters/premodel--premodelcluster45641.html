premodel

verb
to design in advance
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈmɑːdəl / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈmɒdəl / (BrE)
to create or design a model in advance, usually as preparation for later work or construction

Examples

To premodel a design
The architects will premodel the building to identify any design issues early.
Before starting the project, the team decided to premodel the entire process digitally.
She premodeled the sculpture using clay before working with stone.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plan - to design or arrange something in advance
  • synonym: prototype - to make an early sample or model of something
