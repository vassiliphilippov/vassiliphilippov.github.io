Arum maculatum

noun

A common European woodland plant with arrow-shaped leaves, a hood-like greenish spathe, and a short purple spadix, also known as lords-and-ladies.

Examples

Arum maculatum plant

Botanists often find Arum maculatum growing in the shaded understory of European woodlands.
The berries of Arum maculatum are poisonous and should not be eaten.
