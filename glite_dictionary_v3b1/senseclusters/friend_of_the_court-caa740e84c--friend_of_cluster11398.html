friend of the court

noun
legal adviser not party to a case
9.4 6.3
Pronunciation: /frend əv ðə ˈkɔːrt/ /; / /frend əv ðiː ˈkɔːrt/ (AmE), /frend əv ðə ˈkɔːt/ /; / /frend əv ðiː ˈkɔːt/ (BrE)
a person or organization who is not a party to a legal case but advises the court on a matter of law, usually to influence the outcome in the public interest

Examples

Friend of the court brief
Several advocacy groups joined the case as friends of the court to support the petitioners' arguments.
The judge welcomed the input from a friend of the court, noting its value in clarifying complex legal questions.
The organization acted as a friend of the court during the hearings, submitting a brief on environmental impacts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amicus curiae - a person or organization offering advice to a court in a legal case to which they are not a party
