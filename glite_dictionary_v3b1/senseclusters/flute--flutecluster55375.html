flute

noun
high-pitched woodwind instrument
Pronunciation: / fluːt / (AmE), / fluːt / (BrE)
a high-pitched woodwind instrument consisting of a slender tube closed at one end, with finger holes and an opening near the closed end across which the breath is blown

Examples

Play the flute
Children in the orchestra were excited to try the silver flute for the first time.
She played a beautiful melody on her flute during the concert.
The haunting sound of the flute echoed through the ancient hall.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clarinet - a single-reed woodwind musical instrument with a cylindrical tube
  • synonym: oboe - a woodwind instrument with a double-reed mouthpiece and a slender tubular body
  • synonym: recorder - a woodwind musical instrument similar to the flute but with a mouthpiece
