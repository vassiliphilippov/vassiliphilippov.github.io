parodistic

adjective
like a parody
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəˈdɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈdɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of parody, especially in imitating something for comic effect

Examples

Parodistic imitation
The comedian's parodistic impressions of politicians made the audience laugh out loud.
Her writing style is often parodistic, cleverly mocking popular novels.
The play took a parodistic approach to classic fairy tales, adding humor and exaggeration.

Related Terms

  • synonym: satirical - using humor or exaggeration to criticize or mock
  • antonym: serious - not intended to be funny or mocking
