Populus nigra italica

noun
columnar black poplar cultivar; Lombardy poplar
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːpjʊləs ˈnaɪɡrə ɪˈtælɪkə / (AmE), / ˌpɒpjʊləs ˈnaɪɡrə ɪˈtælɪkə / (BrE)
A fastigiate (narrow, columnar) cultivar of black poplar, known for its tall, upright form and erect branches; commonly called the Lombardy poplar.

Examples

The Populus nigra italica tree
Populus nigra italica is valued for its narrow, upright shape in formal gardens.
Many gardeners choose Populus nigra italica for windbreaks due to its dense, columnar growth.
The driveway was lined with Populus nigra italica, creating a dramatic vertical accent in the landscape.
