act out

verb
perform or represent something as if in a play
1.0
Pronunciation: / æktaʊt /; / ækt aʊt / (AmE), / ækt aʊt / (BrE)
to perform or represent a situation, event, or feeling as if in a play or role-play

Examples

To act out a story
During therapy, children are encouraged to act out their feelings through role-play.
He likes to act out funny situations to entertain his friends.
The teacher asked the students to act out a scene from the story.
In drama class, the students were asked to act out scenes from their favorite movies.
She was able to act out the character’s sorrow with remarkable realism.
Therapists sometimes have children act out their feelings using puppets or toys.

Related Terms

  • synonym: enact - to perform or represent something, often in a theatrical setting
  • synonym: reenact - to perform again as in a reenactment or role-play
