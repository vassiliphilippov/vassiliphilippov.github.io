dolichocephalic

adjective
having a relatively long head (cephalic index under 75)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌdoʊlɪkoʊsəˈfælɪk / (AmE), / ˌdɒlɪkəʊsɪˈfælɪk / (BrE)
Having a relatively long head, specifically with a cephalic index (the ratio of head width to length) under 75.

Examples

Dolichocephalic skull shape
In anthropology, dolichocephalic features are often used to describe ancient populations.
Some breeds of dogs, like the Greyhound, are known for their dolichocephalic skulls.
The radiologist noted that the infant's head was dolichocephalic in shape on the scan.

Related Terms

  • antonym: brachycephalic - having a relatively broad, short head (cephalic index over 80)
