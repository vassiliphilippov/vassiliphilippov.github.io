trabeated

adjective
built with horizontal beams, not arches
6.7
Pronunciation: / treɪˈbiː.ə.tɪd / (AmE), / trəˈbiː.ə.tɪd / (BrE)
(of a building or architectural structure) constructed with straight horizontal beams or lintels, rather than arches.

Examples

A trabeated structure
The Parthenon is a classic example of a trabeated structure in ancient Greek architecture.
The museum's entrance is marked by a trabeated colonnade, emphasizing straight lines and right angles.
Unlike Roman buildings that often use arches, Greek temples are strictly trabeated.

Related Terms

  • antonym: arcuate - characterized by arches or curved lines
