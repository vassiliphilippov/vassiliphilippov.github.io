commendatory

adjective
expressing praise
Pronunciation: / kəˈmɛndəˌtɔri / (AmE), / kəˈmɛndətəri / (BrE)
expressing praise, approval, or admiration for someone or something

Examples

Commendatory remarks
The teacher gave a commendatory speech about the student's achievements at the ceremony.
Her commendatory remarks boosted his confidence before the interview.
A commendatory review in the newspaper helped the new restaurant attract more customers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: critical - expressing disapproval or finding fault
  • synonym: laudatory - expressing praise or admiration
  • synonym: praiseful - full of praise or approval
