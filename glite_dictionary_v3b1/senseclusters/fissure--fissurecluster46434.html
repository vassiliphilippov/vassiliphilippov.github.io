fissure

noun
a long, narrow crack or opening
9.9 5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪʃɚ /; / ˈfɪʃər / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃə / (BrE)
a long, narrow opening or crack in a surface, rock, earth, or a body part

Examples

A deep fissure
A deep fissure had formed in the glacier after the recent thaw.
The earthquake caused a narrow fissure to appear across the road.

The earthquake left a deep fissure in the road, making it unsafe to drive on.
