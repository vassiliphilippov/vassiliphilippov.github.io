dustily

adverb
in a dull way
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌstɪli / (AmE), / ˈdʌstɪli / (BrE)
in a manner that is old-fashioned, dull, or lacking interest or excitement

Examples

She speaks dustily
The professor spoke dustily, making it hard for students to stay awake.
The museum was arranged dustily, with little to capture visitors' attention.
He recited the poem dustily, missing the passion in the words.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dully - in a boring or uninteresting way
  • antonym: lively - in an energetic or exciting way
