vascular ray

noun
a layer of plant tissue separating vascular bundles
Pronunciation: / ˈvæskjələr reɪ / (AmE), / ˈvæskjʊlə reɪ / (BrE)
a plate-like layer of vascular tissue in plants that separates and connects the vascular bundles, often involved in the radial transport of water and nutrients

Examples

Vascular rays in plants
In woody plants, vascular rays play an essential role in transporting nutrients across stem tissues.
The study highlighted how the width of the vascular ray can affect a plant's ability to store water.
When viewed in cross-section, the vascular ray appears as a thin, radial stripe between the circular bundles.
