Upper Paleolithic

noun
later part of the Old Stone Age
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpɚ ˌpeɪliəˈlɪθɪk / (AmE), / ˈʌpə ˌpæliəˈlɪθɪk / (BrE)
the later part of the Paleolithic era, marked by the presence of only modern Homo sapiens, lasting until about 10,000 BC

Examples

The Upper Paleolithic era
Archaeologists study stone tools from the Upper Paleolithic to learn about early human life.
Many cave paintings were created during the Upper Paleolithic period.
The Upper Paleolithic saw the development of complex language and art.
