patulin

noun
toxic mold compound
Pronunciation: / ˈpætjʊlɪn / (AmE), / ˈpætjʊlɪn / (BrE)
a toxic substance produced by certain molds, commonly found in spoiled fruit such as apples

Examples

Toxic patulin in apples
Scientists discovered high levels of patulin in the spoiled apple juice samples.
Food safety regulations require testing for patulin in fruit products to protect consumers.
The presence of patulin indicates that the fruit has been contaminated by mold.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aflatoxin - a type of toxic substance produced by certain molds
  • synonym: mycotoxin - a toxic compound produced by fungi
