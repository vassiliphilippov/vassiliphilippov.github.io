radiation field

noun
region where electromagnetic energy radiates
1.4 4.5
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən fiːld / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən fiːld / (BrE)
the region or space in which electromagnetic energy is radiated from a source and through which the energy propagates

Examples

The radiation field around stars
Astronomers measure the radiation field around distant stars to study their atmospheres.
Shielding is used to reduce the effects of the radiation field in sensitive electronic equipment.
The strength of the radiation field decreases with distance from the source.
