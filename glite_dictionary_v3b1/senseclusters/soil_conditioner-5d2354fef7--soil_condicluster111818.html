soil conditioner

noun
substance that improves soil structure
Pronunciation: / sɔɪl kənˈdɪʃənɚ / (AmE), / sɔɪl kənˈdɪʃənə / (BrE)
a substance added to soil to improve its physical properties, such as structure, aeration, and water retention

Examples

Add soil conditioner
Adding a soil conditioner can help sandy soil retain more moisture.
Compost is often used as an organic soil conditioner in vegetable gardens.
The gardener recommended using a soil conditioner to improve the quality of my flower beds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fertilizer - a substance added to soil to supply nutrients for plant growth
