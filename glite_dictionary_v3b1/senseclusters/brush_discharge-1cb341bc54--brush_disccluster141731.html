brush discharge

noun
electrical discharge with visible streamers
Pronunciation: / brʌʃ dɪsˌtʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / brʌʃ dɪsˌtʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
an electrical discharge between electrodes in which visible, brush-like streamers of ionized particles are produced, usually at points of high voltage gradients

Examples

Brush discharge glow
A faint blue glow, typical of brush discharge, appeared at the tip of the metal rod.
Scientists studied the brush discharge patterns formed under high-voltage conditions.
The presence of a brush discharge near the power line indicated insulation failure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corona discharge - electrical discharge caused by ionization of a fluid surrounding a conductor
