ball cartridge

noun
bullet-containing cartridge for firearms
8.6 6.3
Pronunciation: /bɔːl ˈkɑːr.trɪdʒ/ /; / /bɑːl ˈkɑːr.trɪdʒ/ /; / /bɔːl ˈkɑːr.trədʒ/ (AmE), /bɔːl ˈkɑː.trɪdʒ/ /; / /bɔːl ˈkɑː.trədʒ/ (BrE)
A cartridge for firearms containing a bullet (as opposed to blank or shot), with a primer and full charge of powder.

Examples

A ball cartridge
The soldier loaded a ball cartridge into his rifle before advancing.
This box contains only ball cartridges, suitable for live ammunition training.
Unlike blanks, a ball cartridge is designed to fire an actual projectile.
