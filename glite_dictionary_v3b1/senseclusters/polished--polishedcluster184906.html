polished

adjective
shiny, smooth, or refined
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑlɪʃt /; / ˈpɑːlɪʃt / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪʃt / (BrE)
shiny and smooth from being rubbed, cleaned, or treated; (of materials or grains) having a smooth, finished surface

Examples

Polished shoes
He wore a suit with polished shoes to the interview.
Her polished performance impressed both the audience and the judges.
The table looked brand new after it was polished.
Each polished plank of wood was carefully placed to form the new table.
The polished marble floor gleamed under the hallway lights.
They admired the polished granite countertop after the renovation.
After harvesting, the grain is often polished to remove the bran and germ layers.
Health experts sometimes recommend choosing unpolished over polished grains for more fiber.
Many people prefer polished rice for its smooth texture and mild flavor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: refined - elegantly improved, free from roughness
  • antonym: rough - having an uneven or unrefined surface, not smooth
  • synonym: smooth - having an even and flat surface; not rough
  • antonym: unpolished - (of grains) with the outer layers left intact
