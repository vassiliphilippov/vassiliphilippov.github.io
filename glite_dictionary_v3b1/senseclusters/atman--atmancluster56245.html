atman

noun
true self in Hinduism
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːtmən / (AmE), / ˈɑːtmən / (BrE)
in Hindu philosophy, the true self or soul of an individual, considered eternal and beyond the physical body

Examples

The atman in Hinduism
In Hindu teachings, the atman is believed to be eternal and unchanging.
Meditation practices often focus on realizing the nature of the atman within oneself.
Philosophers have debated the relationship between the atman and the universe for centuries.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ego - the self, especially as distinct from the world and other selves
  • synonym: soul - the spiritual or immaterial part of a person
