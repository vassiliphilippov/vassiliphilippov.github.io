Chondrus crispus

noun
edible Atlantic seaweed (Irish moss)
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːndrəs ˈkrɪspəs / (AmE), / ˈkɒndrəs ˈkrɪspəs / (BrE)
a dark purple edible seaweed found along Atlantic coasts of Europe and North America, used as a thickener in foods and as a source of carrageenan

Examples

Chondrus crispus seaweed
Botanists studied the growth patterns of Chondrus crispus along the Atlantic coastline.
Carrageenan is extracted from Chondrus crispus for use in food products such as ice cream and yogurt.
On the rocky shores of Ireland, Chondrus crispus is harvested for its culinary and medicinal properties.
