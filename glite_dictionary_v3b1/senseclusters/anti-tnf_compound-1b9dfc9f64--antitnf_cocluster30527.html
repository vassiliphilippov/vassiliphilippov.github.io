anti-TNF compound

noun
drug that blocks tumor necrosis factor
Pronunciation: / ˌænti ˌti ɛn ˈɛf ˈkɑmpaʊnd / (AmE), / ˌænti ˌtiː ɛn ˈɛf ˈkɒmpaʊnd / (BrE)
a drug that inhibits the action of tumor necrosis factor (TNF), used to reduce inflammation, particularly in autoimmune diseases like rheumatoid arthritis

Examples

Anti-TNF compound drug
Many patients respond well to anti-TNF compounds when other therapies have failed.
Research has shown that treatment with an anti-TNF compound can significantly reduce joint inflammation.
The doctor recommended an anti-TNF compound to help control the patient's rheumatoid arthritis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biologic - a drug derived from living organisms used to treat disease
  • synonym: immunosuppressant - a drug that suppresses the immune response
