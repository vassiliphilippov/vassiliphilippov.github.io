twin-prop

noun
aircraft with two propellers
Pronunciation: / ˈtwɪn ˌprɑp / (AmE), / ˈtwɪn ˌprɒp / (BrE)
a propeller-driven aircraft equipped with two engines, each driving a separate propeller, usually for added stability and power

Examples

Twin-prop aircraft
Many regional flights are operated using twin-prop aircraft for efficiency on short routes.
The pilot expertly landed the twin-prop on the short runway despite strong crosswinds.
The vintage twin-prop plane attracted attention at the airshow due to its distinctive sound.

  • synonym: twin-engine - aircraft powered by two engines
