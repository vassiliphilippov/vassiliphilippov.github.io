khamsin

noun
hot, dry wind from the Sahara
9.8
Pronunciation: / kɑmˈsiːn / (AmE), / kæmˈsiːn / (BrE)
a hot, dry, dusty wind from the Sahara that blows across Egypt and surrounding regions, especially in spring

Examples

The khamsin wind
Farmers prepare for the khamsin every spring by protecting their crops from the harsh wind.
The khamsin swept through the desert, coating the town in a layer of fine sand.
Tourists were warned of possible travel disruptions due to an approaching khamsin.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sirocco - a hot wind, often dusty or rainy, blowing from North Africa across the Mediterranean to southern Europe
