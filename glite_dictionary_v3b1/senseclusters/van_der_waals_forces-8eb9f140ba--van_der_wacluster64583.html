van der Waals forces

noun
weak attraction between atoms or molecules
Pronunciation: / ˌvɑn dɚ ˈvɑlz ˌfɔrsɪz / (AmE), / ˌvan də ˈvɑːlz ˌfɔːsɪz / (BrE)
Relatively weak attractive forces between neutral atoms or molecules resulting from temporary polarization induced by their proximity.

Examples

van der Waals forces between molecules
Although van der Waals forces are weak compared to covalent bonds, they play a crucial role in molecular interactions.
The gecko's ability to cling to walls is partly due to van der Waals forces between its feet and the surface.
Water molecules are held together by van der Waals forces, as well as hydrogen bonds.
