pale

adjective
lacking normal color, often from illness or distress
1.0 30/m
Pronunciation: / peÉªl / (AmE), / peÉªl / (BrE)
very light in color or lacking color, brightness, or intensity (such as a face, light, or color)

Examples

Pale skin color
His pale cheeks made everyone worry about his health.
She looked pale after hearing the shocking news.
Standing under the harsh lights, her face appeared unusually pale and drawn.
A pale moon hung over the quiet, deserted street.
She noticed the pale glow of stars barely visible through the mist.
The pale light of dawn filtered through the thin curtains.

The evening sky was lit by a pale glow from the moon.

Synonyms

  • pale-hued
    5.1 0.00/m
    pale-hued means having a light or faint tint on an object, while pale describes something very light or lacking brightness.
  • palish
    7.1 0.00/m
    palish describes something only slightly pale in color, while pale indicates a stronger, clearly light or washed-out appearance.
  • pallid
    7.0 0.15/m
    pallid suggests a weak, often sick-looking color on skin, while pale covers any very light or dull color.
  • wan
    7.3 0.00/m
    wan describes a sickly, weak look often on faces, while pale simply means very light in color or lacking brightness.
  • colorless
    2.9 1.23/m
    colorless means having little or no color and little brightness, while pale still allows some light tint or faint shade.
  • whitish
    1.6 0.68/m
    whitish means leaning toward white or pale white tones, while pale can be any very light color or low intensity.
  • light
    1.0 6.93/m
    light can mean pale in color but often means brightness or low weight, while pale focuses on faint color or low intensity.
