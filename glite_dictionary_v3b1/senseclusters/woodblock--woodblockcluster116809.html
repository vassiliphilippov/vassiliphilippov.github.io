woodblock

noun
percussion instrument
Pronunciation: / ˈwʊdˌblɑk / (AmE), / ˈwʊdblɒk / (BrE)
a small, hollow wooden percussion instrument that produces a sharp sound when struck, used in musical groups

Examples

Orchestra woodblock
The percussionist added a sharp rhythm with the woodblock during the concert.
In the school band, she learned to play the woodblock for the first time.
A single strike on the woodblock signaled the start of the piece.

Related Terms

  • synonym: claves - a pair of wooden sticks used as a percussion instrument
  • synonym: triangle - a metal percussion instrument shaped like a triangle
