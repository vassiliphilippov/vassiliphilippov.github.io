average

noun
usual or typical amount or level
Pronunciation: / ˈævɚɪdʒ /; / ˈæv.ɚ.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈævərɪdʒ /; / ˈæv.ər.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
a number that shows the typical or central value in a set of data, often found by adding all the values and dividing by the number of values; also, the usual or typical level or standard (including in sports)

Examples

The average score
Her grades last semester were just above average.
The temperature this spring was close to the average for the region.
This player is taller than the team average.
The city's temperature average in July is 28°C.
The class average on the exam was higher than expected.
To find the average, add up all the scores and divide by the number of students.
A high scoring average is crucial for a basketball player's reputation.
His batting average reached .320 this season, making him the team's top hitter.
The pitcher's earned run average dropped after three outstanding games.

Related Terms

  • synonym: batting average - the average number of hits per at-bat by a baseball player
  • antonym: extreme - very unusual or exceptional compared to what is normal or typical
  • synonym: mean - the sum of values divided by their number; mathematical average
  • synonym: median - the middle value in a set of numbers
  • antonym: mode - the value that appears most frequently in a data set
  • antonym: score - the number of points, goals or runs achieved in a game
