SLE

noun
autoimmune disease (systemic lupus erythematosus)
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɛs ˌɛl ˈi / (AmE), / ˌɛs ˌɛl ˈiː / (BrE)
an autoimmune disease, known as systemic lupus erythematosus, characterized by inflammation of connective tissue, often causing fatigue, joint pain, and skin lesions

Examples

SLE disease
Many patients with SLE experience skin rashes that can worsen with sun exposure.
Recent advances in treatment have improved the quality of life for those living with SLE.
The doctor diagnosed her with SLE after she presented with persistent fatigue and joint pain.
