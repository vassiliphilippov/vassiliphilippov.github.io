SLE

noun
autoimmune disease (systemic lupus erythematosus)
7.0 0.49/m

an autoimmune disease, known as systemic lupus erythematosus, characterized by inflammation of connective tissue, often causing fatigue, joint pain, and skin lesions

Examples

SLE disease
Many patients with SLE experience skin rashes that can worsen with sun exposure.
Recent advances in treatment have improved the quality of life for those living with SLE.
The doctor diagnosed her with SLE after she presented with persistent fatigue and joint pain.

Synonyms

  • systemic lupus erythematosus
    5.1 0.03/m
    systemic lupus erythematosus is the full medical name used in tests and reports, while SLE is the common abbreviation used in notes.
  • disseminated lupus erythematosus
    7.7 0.00/m
    disseminated lupus erythematosus describes widespread skin and joint disease and older phrasing, while SLE is the modern standard abbreviation used today.
  • lupus erythematosus
    5.9 0.11/m
    lupus erythematosus often refers to skin forms or general lupus in older texts, while SLE names the systemic disease affecting many organs.
