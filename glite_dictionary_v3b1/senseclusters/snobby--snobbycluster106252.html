snobby

adjective
showing a sense of superiority; elitist
6.3 0.61/m
Pronunciation: / Ààsn…ëÀêbi / (AmE), / Ààsn…íbi / (BrE)
having or showing an attitude of superiority and dislike for people regarded as socially inferior

Examples

Snobby attitude
Despite his wealth, James avoids snobby behavior and treats everyone with respect.
He sounded snobby when he refused to eat at any restaurant that wasn't five-star.
Her snobby attitude made her unpopular among her classmates.

Synonyms

    5.3 0.49/m

  • snooty
    7.4 0.68/m
    snooty sounds polite and quiet in manner, while snobby more often links to class and open dislike of lower-status people.
  • stuck-up
    7.5 0.60/m
    stuck-up acts proud and unfriendly in casual speech, while snobby focuses on social rank and showing dislike for others.
  • haughty
    8.1 1.33/m
    haughty behaves with formal pride and scorn, while snobby often carries a social-class tone and personal dislike.
  • supercilious
    8.9 0.39/m
    supercilious looks down with cold contempt in formal settings, while snobby usually shows social snobbery and dislike for lower people.
  • condescending
    7.4 3.19/m
    condescending talks to others as if they are lesser, while snobby focuses more on social class and exclusive attitudes.
  • arrogant
    4.5 12/m
    arrogant feels overly important about skills or status, while snobby centers on social class and dislike of those seen as inferior.
  • snotty
    7.8 0.51/m
    snotty is rude and contemptuous in a blunt, informal way, while snobby usually implies social snobbery and class-based dislike.

Related Terms
