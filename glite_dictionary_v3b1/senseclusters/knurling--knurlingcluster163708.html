knurling

noun
patterned surface process
Pronunciation: / ˈnɜrlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnɜːlɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of creating a pattern of small ridges or bumps on a surface, usually metal or plastic, to improve grip or appearance

Examples

Metal knurling process
The technician used knurling to add grip to the metal handle.
Proper knurling on the tool ensures it does not slip during use.
They inspected the knurling on each knob to maintain quality standards.

Related Terms

  • synonym: engraving - the process of cutting or carving a design onto a hard surface
  • antonym: smoothing - the process of making a surface even and free of bumps
