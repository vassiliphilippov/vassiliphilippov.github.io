Amati

noun
violin by the Amati family
Pronunciation: / əˈmɑːti / (AmE), / əˈmɑːti / (BrE)
a violin made by Nicolo Amati or a member of the Amati family, renowned Italian violin makers of the 16th to 18th centuries

Examples

An Amati violin
Many musicians consider an Amati to be among the finest violins ever made.
She played the Amati with remarkable delicacy during the concert.
The museum displayed a rare Amati in its collection of historic instruments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Guarnerius - violin made by a member of the Guarneri family
  • synonym: Stradivarius - violin made by Antonio Stradivari
