prescriptivism

noun
ethical doctrine prescribing actions/attitudes
6.5 7.8
Pronunciation: /prɪ.skrɪp.ˈtɪ.vɪ.zəm/ /; / /prə.skrɪp.ˈtɪ.vɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /prɪ.skrɪp.ˈtɪ.vɪ.zəm/ /; / /prə.skrɪp.ˈtɪ.vɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
The doctrine, especially in ethics, that holds moral statements express prescriptions for action or attitudes rather than factual claims.

Examples

Ethical prescriptivism
According to prescriptivism, saying 'You ought to be honest' is prescribing a standard of behavior.
He wrote his doctoral thesis on the impact of prescriptivism in twentieth-century ethical theory.
Many ethicists debate the merits of prescriptivism versus descriptivism in moral philosophy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: descriptivism - the view that moral statements describe facts rather than prescribe actions
