declarant

noun
person making declaration
8.0
Pronunciation: /də.ˈkleɪr.ənt/ /; / /dɪ.ˈkleɪr.ənt/ (AmE), /də.ˈkleə.rənt/ /; / /dɪ.ˈkleə.rənt/ (BrE)
a person who makes a formal or official statement or declaration to authorities (such as for legal, civil, or customs purposes)

Examples

The declarant signs document
The declarant signed the official document to register the marriage with the local authorities.
Only the declarant is authorized to submit the birth certificate application at the city office.
In some countries, a declarant must provide identification before registering a death.
The declarant provided a written statement to support the case.
Before the hearing, the declarant reviewed the details of her declaration with her lawyer.
A notary public must verify the identity of the declarant before accepting the document.
The declarant signed the form to confirm the value of the imported goods.
Each declarant must provide accurate information to avoid penalties at customs.
As the declarant, she was responsible for declaring all items in the shipment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: affiant - a person who makes a sworn statement in writing
  • synonym: deponent - a person who gives sworn evidence or testimony
  • synonym: registrant - a person who registers something officially
  • antonym: witness - a person who gives testimony or evidence
