piano action

noun
mechanism that connects piano keys to hammers
Pronunciation: / piˈænoʊ ˈækʃən / (AmE), / piˈænəʊ ˈakʃən / (BrE)
the mechanical system in a piano that uses levers to move hammers and strike the strings when a key is pressed

Examples

The piano action system
A well-maintained piano action is essential for smooth and responsive playing.
Grand pianos often have a more sophisticated piano action than uprights.
The technician repaired the piano action to eliminate sticking keys.
