interminably

adverb
endlessly; without stopping
9.9 6.1
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈtɜːr.mɪ.nə.bliː/ /; / /ˈɪn.tər.mɪ.nə.bliː/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈtɜː.mɪ.nə.bliː/ /; / /ˈɪn.tə.mɪ.nə.bliː/ (BrE)
In a way that seems to have no end; endlessly or ceaselessly.

Examples

To speak interminably
He spoke interminably about subjects that bored everyone else.
She waited interminably for the results to be announced.
The meeting dragged on interminably, with no conclusion in sight.
