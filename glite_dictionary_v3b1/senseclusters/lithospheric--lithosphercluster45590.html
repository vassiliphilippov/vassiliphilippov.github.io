lithospheric

adjective
of the lithosphere
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˌlɪθəˈsfɪrɪk / (AmE), / ˌlɪθəˈsfɪərɪk / (BrE)
relating to the rigid outer layer (the lithosphere) of a planet, especially the Earth

Examples

The lithospheric plates
The lithospheric plates move slowly, causing earthquakes and volcanic eruptions.
Scientists study lithospheric changes to understand the history of Earth's surface.
Mars has a thinner lithospheric layer compared to Earth, affecting its geological activity.
Scientists study lithospheric plates to understand the causes of earthquakes.
The lithospheric boundary marks the division between the Earth's crust and mantle.
Volcanic eruptions often occur along lithospheric plate edges.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crustal - relating to the outermost layer (crust) of a planet
  • synonym: mantle - the layer of a planet between the crust and the core
  • synonym: tectonic - relating to the structure and movement of the Earth's crust
