blamelessness

noun
no-blame incident-review practice
7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈbleɪm.ləs.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈbleɪm.ləs.nəs/ (BrE)
the practice or culture of investigating incidents or failures without assigning personal blame, focusing instead on systemic causes and organizational learning.

Examples

Blamelessness culture
During the postmortem, the team emphasized blamelessness to focus on systems, not individual mistakes.
Healthcare leaders promote blamelessness to encourage staff to report errors without fear of punishment.
Adopting blamelessness in reviews helped the company identify processes to prevent future outages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: just culture - a safety-focused approach that balances learning from errors with appropriate accountability, emphasizing system-level causes.
  • synonym: no-blame culture - an organizational approach that avoids blaming individuals for errors and encourages open reporting.
