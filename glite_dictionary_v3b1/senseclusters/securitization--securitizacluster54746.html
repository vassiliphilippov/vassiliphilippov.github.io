securitization

noun
making an issue a threat
8.6
Pronunciation: /sɪ.kjʊr.ə.tə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /sɪ.kjʊr.ɪ.tɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /sɪ.kjʊə.rɪ.tɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /sɪ.kjʊə.rə.tə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of presenting an issue as a security threat that requires urgent or exceptional action, especially in political or social contexts

Examples

Securitization of a threat
The securitization of climate change has led to new government policies and emergency funding.
Critics argue that the securitization of public health issues can undermine civil liberties.
Media coverage contributed to the securitization of cyber attacks as a national security concern.
