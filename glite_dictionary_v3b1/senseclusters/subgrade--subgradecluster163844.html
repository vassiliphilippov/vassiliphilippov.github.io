subgrade

noun
roadbed soil layer
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌɡreɪd / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌɡreɪd / (BrE)
the layer of soil or material prepared to support a road, railway, or pavement

Examples

Road subgrade
The engineers inspected the subgrade to ensure it was stable before construction began.
A weak subgrade can cause cracks to appear in the road surface over time.
Contractors added gravel to the subgrade to improve drainage beneath the new highway.

Related Terms

  • synonym: foundation - the base or supporting layer for a structure
  • antonym: surface - the top layer of a road or pavement
