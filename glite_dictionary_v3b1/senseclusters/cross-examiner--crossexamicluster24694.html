cross-examiner

noun
person questioning witness in court
Pronunciation: / ˌkrɔs ɪɡˈzæmənər / (AmE), / ˌkrɒs ɪɡˈzæmɪnə / (BrE)
a person who questions a witness closely, especially in a court of law, typically challenging or testing previous testimony

Examples

A cross-examiner in court
As a skilled cross-examiner, she often exposed the weaknesses in the opposition's case.
During the trial, the cross-examiner asked probing questions that unsettled the defendant.
The cross-examiner pressed the witness to explain inconsistencies in her story.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interrogator - a person who questions someone closely, especially in an official context
  • antonym: witness - a person giving testimony in court, often questioned by a cross-examiner
