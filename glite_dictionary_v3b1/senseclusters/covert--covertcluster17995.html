covert

noun
bird feather type
9.1
Pronunciation: /ˈkoʊ.vərt/ (AmE), /ˈkəʊ.vət/ (BrE)
a feather that covers the base of a bird's main flight or tail feathers, helping to smooth airflow over the wings or tail

Examples

Bird's covert feathers
The bird's coverts help smooth airflow over its wings during flight.
Scientists examined the covert to study how birds control their movement in the air.
Some species have brightly colored coverts that are visible when the wings are spread.
