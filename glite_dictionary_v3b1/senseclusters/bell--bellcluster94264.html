bell

noun
nautical time division on ships
7.0
Pronunciation: / bɛl / (AmE), / bɛl / (BrE)
(nautical) One of the eight half-hour periods marking divisions of a four-hour watch, indicated by the strokes of a ship's bell; eight bells indicates the end of a watch at 4:00, 8:00, or 12:00, a.m. or p.m.

Examples

Four bells sounded
After four bells it was time for the crew's midday meal.
He woke up suddenly when six bells rang through the ship.
The change of the watch was signaled by eight bells.

Related Terms

  • synonym: watch - a period of duty on a ship
