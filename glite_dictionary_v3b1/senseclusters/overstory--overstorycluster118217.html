overstory

noun
connecting narrative
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvərˌstɔːri / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌstɔːri / (BrE)
an overarching narrative or theme that connects and gives context to smaller stories or events

Examples

Book overstory
The novel's overstory weaves together the lives of several unrelated characters.
Without a strong overstory, the film's individual scenes felt disconnected.
The teacher explained how the overstory helps students understand the main message of the book.

Related Terms

  • synonym: framework - a structure that supports or connects different parts
  • antonym: subplot - a secondary plot that supports the main story
