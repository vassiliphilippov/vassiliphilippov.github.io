spoiling

noun
the process of becoming spoiled
Pronunciation: / ˈspɔɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈspɔɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of becoming spoiled, damaged, or ruined, especially by decay or improper care

Examples

Spoiling of food
Prolonged exposure to warm temperatures accelerates the spoiling of milk.
Proper packaging helps prevent the spoiling of perishable goods during shipping.
The spoiling of fresh fruit can occur quickly if it is not kept cool.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decay - the process of rotting or decomposing
  • antonym: preservation - the act of keeping something from being spoiled
