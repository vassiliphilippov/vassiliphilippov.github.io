Henry's law

noun
gas solubility principle in liquids
7.1 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààh…õnriz l…î / (AmE), / Ààh…õnriÀêz l…îÀê / (BrE)
In chemistry, the principle that the amount of a gas dissolved in a liquid is directly proportional to the pressure of the gas above the liquid, at constant temperature.

Examples

Henry's law in chemistry
According to Henry's law, more carbon dioxide dissolves in soda at higher pressures.
Divers must understand Henry's law to avoid decompression sickness when ascending too quickly.
The behavior of oxygen in blood plasma can be explained by Henry's law.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Boyle's law - law describing the relationship between pressure and volume of a gas
  • synonym: Charles's law - law relating volume and temperature of a gas
