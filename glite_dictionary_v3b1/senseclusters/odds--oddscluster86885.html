odds

noun
ratio expressing likelihood or wager in betting
Pronunciation: / ɑdz / (AmE), / ɒdz / (BrE)
the likelihood or probability of something happening, especially as shown by numbers in betting or statistics

Examples

The odds of winning
Bookmakers set the odds based on their assessment of each team's chances.
He checked the odds before placing his bet on the football match.
The odds for the horse race were five to one, attracting many hopeful gamblers.
Bookmakers gave high odds on the underdog team winning the match.
The odds of winning the lottery are extremely low.
What are the odds that it will rain tomorrow?

Related Terms

  • antonym: certainty - something sure or certain to happen
  • synonym: chance - likelihood that something will happen
  • antonym: even money - a bet where the amount won is equal to the amount staked
  • synonym: probability - the likelihood or chance of an event occurring
