fuguing

verb
to compose in fugue style
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfjuːɡɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfjuːɡɪŋ / (BrE)
to write, arrange, or perform music in the style of a fugue, especially with staggered voice entries in a contrapuntal manner

Examples

To fuguing a hymn
The choir began fuguing the hymn's last verse, each voice entering a bar later.
In the final movement, the composer skillfully employed fuguing to create a lively and complex texture.
Students practiced fuguing short melodies to understand the structure of a fugue.
