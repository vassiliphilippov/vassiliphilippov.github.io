aspirated

adjective
with extra breath
Pronunciation: / ˈæspəˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈæspɪreɪtɪd / (BrE)
pronounced with a strong burst of breath, especially referring to certain consonant sounds in speech

Examples

An aspirated consonant
In English, the 't' in aspirated words like 'top' is pronounced with a noticeable burst of air.
The teacher explained that the 'k' in 'kite' is an aspirated consonant.
Some languages do not have aspirated sounds, making them difficult for learners to pronounce.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unaspirated - pronounced without a strong burst of breath
