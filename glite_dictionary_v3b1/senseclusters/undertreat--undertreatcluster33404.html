undertreat

verb
to give insufficient treatment
7.7
Pronunciation: /ʌn.dər.ˈtriːt/ /; / /ən.dər.ˈtriːt/ (AmE), /ʌn.də.ˈtriːt/ /; / /ən.də.ˈtriːt/ (BrE)
to give less medical treatment than is necessary or appropriate for a condition or illness

Examples

To undertreat pain
Doctors should not undertreat patients with severe pain.
Some hospitals may unintentionally undertreat certain conditions due to lack of resources.
It is dangerous to undertreat infections, as they can become more serious.
Some patients with chronic illnesses are often undertreated due to lack of resources.
It is important not to undertreat infections, as this can lead to complications.
The study found that elderly patients were frequently undertreated for their pain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: neglect - to fail to care for or give proper attention to
  • antonym: overtreat - to provide more medical treatment than is necessary
