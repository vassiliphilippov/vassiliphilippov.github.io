generation gap

noun
difference in views between generations
1.0 2.8
Pronunciation: /dʒen.ə.ˈreɪ.ʃən gæp/ (AmE), /dʒen.ə.ˈreɪ.ʃən gæp/ (BrE)
a difference of opinions, values, or attitudes between one generation and another, especially between young people and their parents

Examples

The generation gap
Many parents struggle with the generation gap when trying to understand their teenage children.
Music and fashion choices often reveal a generation gap between old and young.
The generation gap can lead to disagreements about traditions and values.
