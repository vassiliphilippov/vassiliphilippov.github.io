oil meal

noun
ground residue after oil extraction
Pronunciation: / ɔɪl miːl / (AmE), / ɔɪl miːl / (BrE)
the ground residue left after extracting oil from seeds, often used as animal feed or fertilizer

Examples

The oil meal residue
After oil extraction, the oil meal was packaged and shipped to animal feed producers.
Sunflower oil meal is a popular supplement in livestock diets.
The farmer added oil meal to the cattle feed to boost protein content.
