tick

noun
discrete time unit in computing/games
7.5
Pronunciation: /tɪk/ (AmE), /tɪk/ (BrE)
(computing, gaming) a discrete unit of time used by a computer, game engine, or simulation during which the system updates its state.

Examples

Game tick
The physics engine updates the object's position on every tick to keep the simulation stable.
Servers with lower tick rates can cause players to experience lag or inconsistent movements in matches.
You should measure AI decisions in ticks rather than seconds for deterministic replay across machines.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clock cycle - the basic time interval of a processor or clock, often used as a synonym in computing contexts
