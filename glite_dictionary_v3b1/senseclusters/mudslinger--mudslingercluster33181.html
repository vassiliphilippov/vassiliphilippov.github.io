mudslinger

noun
one who spreads scandal about others
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌdˌslɪŋər / (AmE), / ˈmʌdˌslɪŋə / (BrE)
a person who spreads malicious or scandalous information about others, especially for political advantage

Examples

A mudslinger in politics
By refusing to become a mudslinger, she gained respect even from her rivals.
During the heated election, one candidate was labeled a mudslinger for constantly making accusations against his opponent.
The debate quickly descended as both sides accused each other of being a mudslinger.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defamer - a person who attacks the reputation of another
  • antonym: praiser - a person who expresses approval or admiration
  • synonym: smearer - one who vilifies others with false or damaging reports
