red-baiting

verb
to accuse of communism
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛd ˌbeɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɛd ˌbeɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
to attack or try to discredit someone by accusing them of being a communist or sympathizer, often unfairly or falsely

Examples

To red-bait a rival
The senator was accused of red-baiting his opponent during the heated campaign.
Journalists criticized the government for red-baiting activists who demanded reforms.
Instead of discussing the proposal, they resorted to red-baiting to silence dissent.

Related Terms

  • antonym: defend - to protect someone from criticism or attack
  • synonym: smear - to damage someone's reputation with false accusations
