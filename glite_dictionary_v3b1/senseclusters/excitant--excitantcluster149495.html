excitant

adjective
producing excitement or stimulation
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈsaɪtənt / (AmE), / ɪkˈsaɪtənt / (BrE)
(of drugs or substances) having the ability to excite or stimulate physiological or psychological activity.

Examples

Excitant effects
Caffeine is commonly regarded as an excitant due to its stimulating effects.
Some medications act as an excitant on the central nervous system, increasing alertness.
The doctor advised avoiding excitant substances before bedtime to prevent insomnia.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sedative - a substance that induces sedation by reducing irritability or excitement
  • synonym: stimulant - a substance that raises levels of physiological or nervous activity
