arraign

verb
call to court to answer a charge
9.3
Pronunciation: / əˈreɪn / (AmE), / əˈreɪn / (BrE)
to officially call someone before a court to answer a criminal charge or indictment

Examples

To arraign a suspect
After being indicted, the suspect was formally arraigned before the judge.
She was arraigned on charges of fraud and theft.
The defendant will be arraigned in court on Monday morning.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acquit - to free someone from a criminal charge by a verdict of not guilty
  • synonym: indict - to formally accuse of or charge with a crime
