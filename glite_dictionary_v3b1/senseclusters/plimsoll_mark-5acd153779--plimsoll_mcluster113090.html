Plimsoll mark

noun
ship's load line marking
9.9 8.3
Pronunciation: /ˈplɪm.soʊl mɑːrk/ (AmE), /ˈplɪm.səʊl mɑːk/ (BrE)
a marking on the hull of a ship indicating the maximum safe draft, and therefore the legal limit to which the ship may be loaded

Examples

The Plimsoll mark on hull
After heavy rainfall, water approached the Plimsoll mark on the freighter's hull.
Every vessel must have a clearly visible Plimsoll mark to comply with international regulations.
The ship's Plimsoll mark was carefully checked before loading began.
