proper noun

noun
a noun denoting a specific name, capitalized
2.1
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑːpər naʊn / (AmE), / ˈprɒpə naʊn / (BrE)
A noun that names a specific individual, place, organization, or entity, and is usually capitalized.

Examples

A proper noun name
A proper noun always starts with a capital letter, unlike a common noun.
Teachers often ask students to identify proper nouns in a sentence.
The names 'London' and 'Shakespeare' are both proper nouns in English.
