Rabbi Moses Ben Maimon

proper noun

Pronunciation: /ˈræ.baɪ ˈmoʊ.zɪz bɛn ˈmaɪ.mən/ /; / /ˈræ.baɪ ˈmoʊ.zəs bɛn ˈmaɪ.mən/ (AmE), /ˈræ.baɪ ˈməʊ.zɪz bɛn ˈmaɪ.mən/ /; / /ˈræ.baɪ ˈməʊ.zəs bɛn ˈmaɪ.mən/ (BrE)
A Spanish-Jewish philosopher and scholar (1135–1204), known as Maimonides, considered the foremost Jewish thinker of the Middle Ages and renowned for codifying Jewish law and writing influential philosophical works.
