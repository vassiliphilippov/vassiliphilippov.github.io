North Star

noun
the star Polaris, used for navigation
3.5 0.51/m
Pronunciation: / n…îrŒ∏ st…ër / (AmE), / n…îÀêŒ∏ st…ëÀê / (BrE)
the brightest star in the constellation Ursa Minor, also known as Polaris, which lies nearly in a direct line with Earth's northern axis and has traditionally been used for navigation

Examples

The North Star
If you can find the North Star, you can always figure out which direction is north.
Sailors once used the North Star to guide them across the open seas at night.
The North Star appears almost stationary in the night sky because it aligns with Earth's axis.

Synonyms

  • Polaris
    5.6 0.78/m
    Polaris is the star name used by astronomers and sailors for navigation, while North Star is the traditional common name.
  • pole star
    5.6 0.03/m
    pole star refers to any star near the Earth's axis used to find direction, while North Star names Polaris specifically.
  • polestar
    6.8 0.02/m
    polestar is a single-word form seen in maps or old texts, while North Star is the common two-word name.
  • polar star
    4.6 0.02/m
    polar star sounds more descriptive and rare in speech, while North Star is the fixed traditional name used for navigation.
