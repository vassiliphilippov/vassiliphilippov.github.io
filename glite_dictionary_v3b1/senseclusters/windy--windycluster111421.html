windy

adjective
using too many words; verbose
4.8 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈwɪn.diː/ (AmE), /ˈwɪn.diː/ (BrE)
using or containing more words than necessary; long-winded or verbose in expression.

Examples

Windy speech
Her windy explanation of the process made it hard to follow her main point.
The politician's windy speech left the audience impatient and bored.
Tired of windy lecturers, the students preferred concise presentations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concise - giving a lot of information clearly and in a few words
  • synonym: long-winded - talking or writing at length, especially in a tedious way
  • synonym: verbose - using more words than are needed; wordy
  • synonym: wordy - using more words than necessary
