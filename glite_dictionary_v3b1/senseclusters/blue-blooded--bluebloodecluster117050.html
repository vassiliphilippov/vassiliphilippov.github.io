blue-blooded

adjective
of noble or aristocratic descent
8.2 0.10/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåbluÀàbl åd…™d / (AmE), / ÀåbluÀêÀàbl åd…™d / (BrE)
Belonging to or characteristic of the nobility or aristocracy.

Examples

A blue-blooded noble
As a blue-blooded member of the royal family, she was accustomed to a life of privilege.
Many believed his blue-blooded background made him a natural leader in society.
Though she was not blue-blooded, her manners impressed even the aristocrats.

Synonyms

  • aristocratic
    7.2 3.38/m
    aristocratic describes manners, style, or family linked to the upper class, while blue-blooded stresses noble birth and lineage.
  • noble
    4.4 4.75/m
    noble refers to someone of high social rank or title by birth, while blue-blooded focuses on family lineage and aristocratic origins.
  • highborn
    7.5 0.11/m
    highborn means born into the upper classes by family, while blue-blooded is a more informal phrase for the same idea.
  • wellborn
    9.1 0.01/m
    wellborn indicates noble or upper-class birth and polite manners, while blue-blooded is a more casual, playful way to say it.
  • patrician
    8.6 0.68/m
    patrician describes refined, upper-class manners and style, while blue-blooded mainly points to noble birth and family background.
  • highbred
    7.4 0.01/m
    highbred suggests good breeding, upbringing, and refined manners, while blue-blooded highlights noble ancestry and class by birth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: common - not belonging to the nobility; of ordinary people
  • synonym: patrician - of or belonging to the upper class or nobility
