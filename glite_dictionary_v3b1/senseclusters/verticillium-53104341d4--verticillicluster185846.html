Verticillium

noun
genus of fungi causing plant wilts
Pronunciation: / vɚˌtɪsɪˈliːəm / (AmE), / vɜːˌtɪsɪˈliːəm / (BrE)
A genus of imperfect fungi with spores borne singly at the tips of whorled branchlets, some species of which cause plant wilt diseases.

Examples

Verticillium plant disease
Proper soil management can help prevent Verticillium from infecting plants.
Scientists identified Verticillium as the cause of the sudden dying of several trees.
The outbreak of Verticillium wilt devastated tomato crops across the region.
