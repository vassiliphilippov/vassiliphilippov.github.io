overfull

adjective
exceeding demand; more than full
5.5 0.09/m
Pronunciation: / ËŒoÊŠvÉ™rËˆfÊŠl / (AmE), / ËŒÉ™ÊŠvÉ™ËˆfÊŠl / (BrE)
(of a market or situation) having a supply that exceeds demand; excessively filled beyond capacity.

Examples

An overfull inventory
An overfull inventory often leads to price reductions and storage issues.
The housing market became overfull after too many developments were built in a short period.
The supermarket had to discount products because shelves were overfull with unsold goods.

Synonyms

  • glutted
    6.3 0.02/m
    glutted describes markets flooded with more goods than buyers, while overfull refers to situations filled beyond their capacity.
  • overstocked
    3.9 0.06/m
    overstocked means shops or warehouses hold more products than they can sell, while overfull covers any excess beyond capacity.
  • overabundant
    5.8 0.05/m
    overabundant describes things produced in very large amounts, often in nature, while overfull focuses on being filled beyond capacity.
