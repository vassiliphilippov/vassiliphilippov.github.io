overfull

adjective
exceeding demand; more than full
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈfʊl / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈfʊl / (BrE)
(of a market or situation) having a supply that exceeds demand; excessively filled beyond capacity.

Examples

An overfull inventory
An overfull inventory often leads to price reductions and storage issues.
The housing market became overfull after too many developments were built in a short period.
The supermarket had to discount products because shelves were overfull with unsold goods.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glutted - (of a market) excessively supplied; oversupplied
  • antonym: understocked - (of a market) lacking sufficient supply
