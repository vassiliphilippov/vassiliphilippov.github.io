anthophilous

adjective
feeding on or attracted to flowers
Pronunciation: / ænˈθɑːfɪləs / (AmE), / ænˈθɒfɪləs / (BrE)
(of an animal, especially an insect) feeding on or attracted to flowers.

Examples

Anthophilous insects
Bees are classic examples of anthophilous insects as they collect nectar from flowers.
Many butterflies display anthophilous behavior, spending much of their lives on blossoms.
The lush garden attracts a variety of anthophilous species, including hummingbirds and moths.

  • synonym: anthophagous - feeding on flowers
