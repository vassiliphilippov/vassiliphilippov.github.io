manyatta

noun
temporary hut settlement
9.9
Pronunciation: /mæn.ˈjæt.ə/ /; / /mə.ˈnjæt.ə/ (AmE), /mæn.ˈjæt.ə/ /; / /mə.ˈnjæt.ə/ (BrE)
a traditional or temporary settlement of huts, especially used by the Maasai or other East African pastoralists

Examples

A Maasai manyatta
The Maasai built a manyatta to stay in while moving their cattle across the plains.
During the dry season, families lived together in a manyatta near the river.
Workers constructed a temporary manyatta close to the fields for the harvest period.
The Maasai family welcomed us into their manyatta for tea.
Each manyatta is built using local materials like mud, sticks, and cow dung.
Tourists often visit a manyatta to learn about Maasai culture and traditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: encampment - a place where a group of people set up temporary shelters
  • synonym: kraal - a traditional African village of huts, typically enclosed by a fence
  • degree higher: village - a small group of houses in a rural area
