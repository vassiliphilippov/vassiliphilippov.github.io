Carissa bispinosa

noun
South African spiny shrub with edible fruit
9.9
Pronunciation: / kəˈrɪsə bɪsˈpaɪnəsə / (AmE), / kəˈrɪsə bɪsˈpɪnəsə / (BrE)
A South African shrub with forked spines and plumlike fruit, often used for hedging.

Examples

Carissa bispinosa shrub
Many gardeners in South Africa plant Carissa bispinosa to create protective borders.
The fruit of Carissa bispinosa is edible and resembles a small plum.
The thick branches of Carissa bispinosa make it an excellent choice for natural hedges.
