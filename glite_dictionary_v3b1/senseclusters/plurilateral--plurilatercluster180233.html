plurilateral

noun
partial group agreement
Pronunciation: / ˌplʊrɪˈlætərəl / (AmE), / ˌplʊərɪˈlætərəl / (BrE)
an agreement, arrangement, or group involving several but not all members of a larger organization, especially in international relations or trade.

Examples

A plurilateral deal
The new plurilateral was negotiated among several countries to address digital trade issues.
A plurilateral can help a subset of nations move forward when consensus is difficult.
Only five members signed the plurilateral, while others chose not to participate.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: bilateral - an agreement involving two parties
  • antonym: multilateral - an agreement involving all or most parties in a group
