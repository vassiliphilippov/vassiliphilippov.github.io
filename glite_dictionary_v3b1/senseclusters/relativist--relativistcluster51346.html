relativist

adjective
supporting relativism
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛlətɪvɪst / (AmE), / ˈrɛlətɪvɪst / (BrE)
supporting or relating to the belief that truth, morality, or values depend on perspective or context rather than being absolute

Examples

Relativist thinking in ethics
Her relativist approach to ethics means she avoids making universal judgments.
The professor presented a relativist argument about cultural differences in moral values.
Some critics argue that a relativist view can make it difficult to condemn harmful practices.

Related Terms

  • antonym: absolute - not dependent on anything else; fixed and universal
  • synonym: subjective - based on personal feelings or opinions
