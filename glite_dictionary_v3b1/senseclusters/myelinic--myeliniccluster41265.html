myelinic

adjective
relating to myelin (nerve fiber sheath)
Pronunciation: / maɪˈɛlɪnɪk / (AmE), / maɪˈɛlɪnɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or containing myelin, the substance that forms a sheath around the axon of certain nerve fibers.

Examples

Myelinic nerve tissue
Damage to myelinic tissue can result in neurological disorders.
Researchers studied the myelinic sheaths to better understand nerve signal conduction.
The loss of myelinic fibers in the brain is a hallmark of multiple sclerosis.
