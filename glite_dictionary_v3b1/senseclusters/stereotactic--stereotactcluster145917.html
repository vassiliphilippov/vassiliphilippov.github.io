stereotactic

adjective
precise 3D positioning
Pronunciation: / ˌstɛri.oʊˈtæktɪk / (AmE), / ˌstɪəri.əˈtæktɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving precise three-dimensional positioning, especially in medical procedures

Examples

A stereotactic biopsy
A stereotactic biopsy allows doctors to target tissue accurately.
The surgeon used a stereotactic frame to guide the instrument to the exact location in the brain.
Advances in stereotactic technology have improved the safety of certain neurological procedures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stereotaxic - relating to precise positioning in three dimensions, especially in surgery
