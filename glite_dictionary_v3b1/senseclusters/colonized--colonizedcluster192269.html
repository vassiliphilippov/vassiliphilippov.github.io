colonized

adjective
inhabited or controlled by colonists
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑː.ləˌnaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈkɒl.əˌnaɪzd /; / ˈkɒl.ə.naɪzd / (BrE)
(of a place) inhabited and controlled by settlers from another country.

Examples

Colonized territory
A colonized territory often experiences profound cultural changes.
Many African nations were colonized by European powers in the nineteenth century.
The once colonized regions gained independence after years of struggle.
Many indigenous cultures were suppressed in colonised regions of the world.
The colonised territories struggled for independence throughout the twentieth century.
The documentary explores the effects of being colonised on local populations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: colonial - relating to or characteristic of a colony or colonies
  • synonym: settled - established by people or settlers, often referring to habitation
  • antonym: uncolonised - not having been colonised
  • antonym: uncolonized - not inhabited or controlled by colonists
