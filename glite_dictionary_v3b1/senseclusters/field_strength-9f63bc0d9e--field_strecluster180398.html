field strength

noun
magnitude of electric or magnetic field at a point
3.2 4.6
Pronunciation: /fiːld streŋθ/ /; / /fiːld streŋkθ/ (AmE), /fiːld streŋkθ/ /; / /fiːld streŋθ/ (BrE)
the magnitude of the effect produced by an electric or magnetic field at a given point, typically measured as force per unit charge or mass

Examples

Field strength measurement
Radio reception quality depends largely on the field strength at the receiver.
Scientists calculate field strength to determine how a charged particle will move.
The field strength of the magnet was measured in teslas.
