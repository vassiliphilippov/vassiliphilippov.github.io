diagenetic

adjective
changes after burial
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌdaɪəˈdʒɛnətɪk / (AmE), / ˌdaɪəˈdʒɛnətɪk / (BrE)
relating to the chemical and physical changes that happen to sediments or organic remains after burial, especially as they turn into rock or fossils

Examples

Diagenetic changes in rock
The diagenetic processes altered the mineral composition of the ancient shells found in the rock layer.
Scientists study diagenetic changes to understand how fossils are preserved over millions of years.
The color of the bone was affected by diagenetic reactions with the surrounding soil.
The diagenetic changes in the sediment affected the quality of the resulting rock.
Scientists study diagenetic processes to understand how fossils are preserved in stone.
The color of the sandstone was altered by diagenetic reactions over millions of years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fossilization - the process by which organic material becomes a fossil
  • antonym: metamorphic - relating to rocks changed by heat and pressure
  • synonym: sedimentary - relating to rocks formed by the accumulation of sediment
