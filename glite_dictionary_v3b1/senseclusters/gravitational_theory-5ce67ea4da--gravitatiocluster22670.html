gravitational theory

noun
theory of the force of gravity
2.0 3.9
Pronunciation: /græv.ɪ.ˈteɪ.ʃən.əl ˈθiː.ə.ri/ /; / /græv.ɪ.ˈteɪ.ʃən.əl ˈθiː.riː/ (AmE), /græv.ɪ.ˈteɪ.ʃən.əl ˈθɪə.ri/ /; / /græv.ɪ.ˈteɪ.ʃən.əl ˈθiː.ə.ri/ (BrE)
the scientific theory stating that any two particles of matter attract each other with a force directly proportional to their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them

Examples

Gravitational theory of physics
Modern physics extends gravitational theory to explain phenomena near black holes.
Newton's gravitational theory was revolutionary for its time.
Scientists have been testing gravitational theory using precise measurements in space.
