magilp

noun
oil painting medium (linseed oil with mastic or turpentine)
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈmædʒ.ɪlp/ /; / /ˈmædʒ.əlp/ (AmE), /ˈmædʒ.ɪlp/ /; / /ˈmædʒ.əlp/ (BrE)
A medium for oil paints, made by mixing linseed oil with mastic varnish or turpentine, used to alter the texture and drying time of oil colours.

Examples

A magilp medium
Some painters avoid magilp because it can yellow and crack over time.
The artist prepared a mixture of magilp to achieve a smoother glaze on his painting.
Using magilp allowed her to blend her oil colours more easily on the canvas.
