substitutional

adjective
replacing host atom
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌbstɪˈtuːʃənəl / (AmE), / ˌsʌbstɪˈtjuːʃənəl / (BrE)
describing an atom or impurity that replaces a host atom in a crystal lattice, especially in alloys or solid solutions

Examples

Substitutional atom in steel
In steel, carbon atoms are not substitutional but fit into spaces between iron atoms.
A substitutional impurity can change the electrical properties of a semiconductor.
The alloy's strength increased due to the presence of substitutional atoms in the metal lattice.

Related Terms

  • antonym: interstitial - describing an atom that occupies a space between the regular positions in a crystal lattice
