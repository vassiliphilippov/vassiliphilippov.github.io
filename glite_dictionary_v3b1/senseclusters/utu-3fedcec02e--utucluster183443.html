Utu

proper noun
Sumerian sun god
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈuː.tuː/ /; / /ˈuː.tu/ (AmE), /ˈuː.tuː/ /; / /ˈuː.tu/ (BrE)
In Sumerian mythology, the god of the sun and justice, counterpart of the Akkadian god Shamash.

Examples

Utu god of sun
Ancient Sumerians worshipped Utu as the god who brought light and justice to the world.
Depictions of Utu often show him rising between two mountains, symbolizing the dawn.
In Sumerian myths, Utu played a vital role in maintaining order among gods and humans.
