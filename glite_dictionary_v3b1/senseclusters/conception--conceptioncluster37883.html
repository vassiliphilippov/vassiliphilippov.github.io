conception

noun
abstract or general idea
Pronunciation: / kənˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / kənˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
the creation, formation, or existence of an idea, concept, or project

Examples

A new conception
Artists often challenge traditional conceptions of beauty in their work.
Her conception of freedom was influenced by her personal experiences.
The philosopher explained his conception of justice in the lecture.
At its conception, the project aimed to revolutionize the local economy.
She has been involved in the conception and planning of this new policy.
The conception of the company occurred in a small apartment in 2001.
Her remarkable invention began with the conception of a simple idea.
His conception of happiness differs greatly from mine.
The initial conception of the project was inspired by advances in technology.
