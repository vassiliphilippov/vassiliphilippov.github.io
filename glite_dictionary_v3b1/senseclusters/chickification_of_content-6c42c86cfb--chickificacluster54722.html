chickification of content

noun
making content appeal to women
Pronunciation: / ˌtʃɪkɪfɪˈkeɪʃən əv ˈkɑntɛnt / (AmE), / ˌtʃɪkɪfɪˈkeɪʃ(ə)n əv ˈkɒntɛnt / (BrE)
The process of making content specifically appealing to women, often by tailoring themes, language, or imagery to target a female audience.

Examples

Chickification of content in media
Media companies often practice chickification of content to attract a larger female audience.
Some critics argue that the chickification of content undermines the complexity of women's interests.
The chickification of content is evident in the increasing number of romantic subplots in mainstream films.
