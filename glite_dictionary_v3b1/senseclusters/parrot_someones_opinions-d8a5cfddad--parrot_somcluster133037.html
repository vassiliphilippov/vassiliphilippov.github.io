parrot someone's opinions

verb
repeat others' views uncritically
Pronunciation: / ˈpærət ˈsʌmwʌnz əˈpɪnjənz / (AmE), / ˈpærət ˈsʌmwʌnz əˈpɪnjənz / (BrE)
to repeat someone else's views or information without understanding or adding personal insight

Examples

To parrot someone's opinions
Instead of offering his own analysis, he would just parrot someone's opinions during the meetings.
Journalists should investigate thoroughly rather than parrot someone's opinions in their reports.
She was criticized for parroting someone's opinions without understanding the complexities of the issue.
