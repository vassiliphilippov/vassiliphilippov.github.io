glue

verb
fix or attach as if with glue
1.8 2.69/m
Pronunciation: / …°luÀê / (AmE), / …°luÀê / (BrE)
to fasten or fix firmly, as if with glue; to cause to be immovably focused or attached

Examples

Glued to the screen
Her attention was glued on the speaker throughout the presentation.
I was so interested in the novel that I glued myself to the couch and finished it in one sitting.
The children were glued to the television during the cartoon show.

Synonyms

  • stick
    1.0 29/m
    stick is a common verb for simple surface adhesion like tape, while glue often means using adhesive for a stronger, lasting bond.
  • bond
    3.2 1.93/m
    bond means making a very strong connection often used in formal or technical speech, while glue means fastening with adhesive in everyday use.
  • fasten
    1.6 6.55/m
    fasten means to secure something in place with any method like screws or clips, while glue means stick things using an adhesive.
  • fix
    1.0 17/m
    fix is to make something stable or repair it so it does not move, while glue means attach items by using an adhesive.
  • attach
    2.5 39/m
    attach means to join or connect two things, sometimes loosely or temporarily, while glue implies a firm, lasting adhesion.
  • secure
    3.3 16/m
    secure means to fasten so it cannot move or be taken, often for safety, while glue means join parts by a sticky substance.
  • affix
    5.0 2.32/m
    affix means to attach something like a label or stamp in formal contexts, while glue means stick objects together with paste.

Related Terms

  • antonym: detach - to remove or separate; to become unfastened
