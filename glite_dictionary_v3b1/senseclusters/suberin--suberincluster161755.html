suberin

noun
plant waxy barrier
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːbərɪn / (AmE), / ˈsjuːbərɪn / (BrE)
a waxy, waterproof substance found in plant cell walls that helps prevent water loss and protects tissues

Examples

Waxy suberin layer
The cork cells in tree bark are rich in suberin, making them resistant to water.
Scientists study suberin to understand how plants survive in dry environments.
The presence of suberin in roots helps prevent excessive water loss.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cutin - a waxy substance in plant cuticles that reduces water loss
