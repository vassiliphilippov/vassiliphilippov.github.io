parietal placentation

noun
ovule arrangement on ovary walls in plants
8.7 9.9
Pronunciation: /pə.ˈraɪ.ə.təl/ /; / /pə.ˈraɪ.ɪt.əl/ (AmE), /pə.ˈraɪ.ə.təl/ /; / /pə.ˈraɪ.ɪt.əl/ (BrE)
a type of placentation in which the ovules develop on the inner walls or slight outgrowths of the wall of a compound ovary, forming incomplete partitions

Examples

Parietal placentation in plants
In plants with parietal placentation, the seeds line the inner wall of the ovary rather than the central axis.
The poppy is a classic example of a species exhibiting parietal placentation.
Understanding parietal placentation is important in the study of floral morphology and plant classification.

Related Terms

  • antonym: axile placentation - ovules attached to a central axis in the ovary
