curve

noun
property of being curved
3.4
Pronunciation: / kɝːv /; / kɝv / (AmE), / kɜːv / (BrE)
a smooth, rounded shape or line; a part of something that bends; a line on a graph showing how something changes

Examples

A curve on paper
He admired the elegant curve of the sculpture's surface.
The curve in her handwriting gave it a distinctive style.
The curve of the road made it difficult to see around the corner.
A warning sign indicated a dangerous curve ahead.
The driver slowed down as he approached the sharp curve in the road.
The river takes a gentle curve to the left just past the bridge.
A parabola is a type of mathematical curve studied in geometry.
The curve of the road made it difficult to see oncoming traffic.
The artist drew a graceful curve on the canvas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arc - a part of the circumference of a circle or curve
  • synonym: bend - a curve in a road, river, or path
  • synonym: line graph - a type of chart that depicts data points connected by straight lines
  • antonym: straight line - a line without any curves; the shortest distance between two points
  • antonym: straightness - the quality of being straight, not curved
