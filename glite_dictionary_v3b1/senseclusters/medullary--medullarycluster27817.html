medullary

adjective
containing or resembling bone marrow
6.4
Pronunciation: / məˈdʌləri / (AmE), / mɪˈdʌləri / (BrE)
relating to bone marrow or the inner/central part of an organ, tissue, or bone

Examples

Medullary cavity
In anatomy, the medullary cavity refers to the central cavity of bone shafts.
The biopsy revealed a medullary pattern characteristic of certain types of tumors.
The doctor explained that the disease affected the medullary region of the adrenal gland.
Medullary thyroid carcinoma is a rare form of thyroid cancer.
Certain bones have a medullary cavity where marrow is stored.
The doctor explained that the tumor was located in the medullary region of the kidney.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cortical - relating to the outer layer (cortex) of an organ or structure, especially the brain
