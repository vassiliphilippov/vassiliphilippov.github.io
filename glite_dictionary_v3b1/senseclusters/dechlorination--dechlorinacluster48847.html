dechlorination

noun
removal of chlorine
6.3
Pronunciation: / diˌklɔːrəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌklɔːrɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of removing chlorine from water, chemicals, or other substances

Examples

Water dechlorination
The dechlorination of drinking water is important to reduce harmful byproducts before distribution.
Researchers developed a new method for rapid dechlorination of industrial waste chemicals.
Microorganisms can assist in the dechlorination of toxic compounds in contaminated soil.
Many water treatment plants use dechlorination to make water safe for drinking.
Before adding tap water to an aquarium, dechlorination is often necessary to protect the fish.
The dechlorination process helps reduce harmful chemicals in swimming pools.

Related Terms

  • antonym: chlorination - the process of adding chlorine to a substance
  • synonym: purification - the process of making something pure by removing contaminants
