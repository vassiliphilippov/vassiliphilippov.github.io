putrescible

adjective
liable to decay or spoil
Pronunciation: / pjuˈtrɛsɪbəl / (AmE), / pjuːˈtrɛsɪbəl / (BrE)
liable to decay, decompose, or spoil, especially by bacterial action; capable of becoming putrid.

Examples

Putrescible waste
Food scraps and other putrescible waste should be disposed of in sealed containers to avoid attracting pests.
Organic waste is highly putrescible and must be processed quickly to prevent odors.
The putrescible nature of the material made it unsuitable for long-term storage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: putrid - decaying or rotting and emitting a fetid smell
