stigmatism

noun
condition of having stigmata
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪɡməˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈstɪɡmətɪzəm / (BrE)
the condition of having or being marked by stigmata, often referring to apparent wounds resembling those of Christ’s crucifixion

Examples

Religious stigmatism
Skeptics questioned whether her stigmatism could be explained by natural causes.
Some devotees were said to exhibit stigmatism after intense periods of prayer.
The phenomenon of stigmatism is often reported in the accounts of certain saints.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stigmata - bodily marks, sores, or sensations of pain in locations corresponding to the crucifixion wounds of Jesus
