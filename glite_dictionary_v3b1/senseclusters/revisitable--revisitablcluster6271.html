revisitable

adjective
can be visited again
Pronunciation: / riˈvɪzɪtəbəl / (AmE), / riˈvɪzɪtəbəl / (BrE)
able to be visited again or reconsidered after an initial visit or review

Examples

A revisitable exhibition
The exhibition is revisitable because it changes its displays every few months.
Some decisions should be revisitable in case new information comes to light.
The charming old town is highly revisitable for tourists who enjoy history and culture.

Related Terms

  • antonym: irreversible - not able to be changed or undone
  • synonym: repeatable - able to be done again
