appendage

noun
a part joined to something larger
Pronunciation: / əˈpɛndɪdʒ / (AmE), / əˈpɛndɪdʒ / (BrE)
a part that is attached to something larger, especially a limb or projection on an animal or plant

Examples

A appendage of limb
A leaf is an appendage of a plant's stem.
Some regarded the new committee as merely an appendage to the main organization.
The crab used its large appendage to protect itself from predators.
A lobster’s claw is an appendage specialized for grasping and defense.
Insects have various appendages such as antennae, legs, and wings.
The octopus uses each appendage independently to explore its environment.
A crab’s pincers are specialized appendages used for feeding and defense.
Botanists often examine the small appendage found at the base of certain leaves.
The starfish uses each appendage to move slowly across the ocean floor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: limb - an arm, leg, or similar appendage of an animal
  • antonym: main body - the principal or central part to which an appendage is attached
