release

verb
give up a right or possession
2.6 2.90/m
Pronunciation: / r…™Ààlis / (AmE), / r…™ÀàliÀês / (BrE)
to give up or surrender a possession, right, or claim; to relinquish ownership or control over something

Examples

To release a claim
After the dispute, she decided to release all claims to the property.
He was required to release his interest in the business before moving abroad.
The company will release its rights to the patent after the merger.

Synonyms

  • relinquish
    7.2 5.71/m
    relinquish is a formal verb used when someone gives up rights or control, while release is more general.
  • give up
    2.3 11/m
    give up means to stop holding or using something by choice, while release sounds slightly more formal and neutral.
  • surrender
    4.4 23/m
    surrender often happens under pressure or to an authority, while release covers giving up control by choice or agreement.
  • renounce
    6.3 5.03/m
    renounce is used to publicly abandon a claim, title, or belief, while release is a neutral term for giving up possessions.
  • resign
    3.7 0.94/m
    resign usually refers to leaving a job or official role by choice, while release covers giving up ownership or control.
  • cede
    8.2 3.28/m
    cede is a formal transfer of land, power, or rights to another party, while release is broader and less legal.
  • sign away
    4.8 0.35/m
    sign away means to give up rights or ownership by signing a legal document, while release does not imply paperwork.
  • concede
    5.5 0.96/m
    concede often means reluctantly admitting or giving something after argument, while release focuses on giving up control or ownership.

Related Terms

  • synonym: relinquish - to voluntarily give up a claim or possession
  • antonym: retain - to keep possession or control
  • synonym: surrender - to give up possession or claim to someone else
