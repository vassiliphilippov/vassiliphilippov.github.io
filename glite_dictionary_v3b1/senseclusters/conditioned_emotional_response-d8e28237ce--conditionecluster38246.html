conditioned emotional response

noun
emotion acquired through conditioning
1.6 5.7
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈdɪʃ.ənd ɪ.ˈmoʊ.ʃən.əl rɪ.ˈspɑːns/ /; / /kən.ˈdɪʃ.ənd ɪ.ˈmoʊ.ʃən.əl rɪ.ˈspɔːns/ (AmE), /kən.ˈdɪʃ.ənd ɪ.ˈməʊ.ʃən.əl rɪ.ˈspɒns/ /; / /kən.ˈdɪʃ.ənd iː.ˈməʊ.ʃən.əl rɪ.ˈspɒns/ (BrE)
a learned emotional reaction to a stimulus, developed through the process of conditioning

Examples

A conditioned emotional response
A conditioned emotional response can develop after repeated associations between a stimulus and an unpleasant event.
Children may exhibit a conditioned emotional response such as fear when they hear thunder if it is paired with frightening experiences.
The sight of the laboratory caused a conditioned emotional response of anxiety in the test subject.
