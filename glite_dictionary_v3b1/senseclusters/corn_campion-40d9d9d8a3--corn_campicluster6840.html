corn campion

noun
a European weed with trumpet-shaped reddish-purple flowers
7.7
Pronunciation: / kɔrn ˈkæm.pi.ən / (AmE), / kɔːn ˈkæm.pɪ.ən / (BrE)
a European annual plant (Silene dichotoma) with large, trumpet-shaped reddish-purple flowers and poisonous seeds, commonly found as a weed in grainfields and roadsides, naturalized in America

Examples

The corn campion flower
Gardeners often remove corn campion to prevent its spread in wildflower meadows.
The corn campion is known for its striking reddish-purple flowers and poisonous seeds.
The field was dotted with corn campion blooming among the wheat.
