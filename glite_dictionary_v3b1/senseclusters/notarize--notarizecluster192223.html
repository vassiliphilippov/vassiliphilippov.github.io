notarize

verb
digitally certify software for distribution
7.1
Pronunciation: /ˈnoʊ.tə.raɪz/ (AmE), /ˈnəʊ.tə.raɪz/ (BrE)
(in computing) to submit or have a software package, installer, or app checked and certified by a platform or service (often automatically) so it can be distributed without security warnings.

Examples

To notarize an app
Developers must notarize their macOS installers to prevent security warnings during user installation.
The company notarized the update through the platform's automated scanner before releasing it worldwide.
She spent the afternoon notarizing several app packages to meet the store's submission requirements.

Related Terms

  • synonym: certify - to confirm officially that something meets required standards or is authentic.
  • synonym: code sign - to sign software with a digital certificate to verify its publisher and integrity.
