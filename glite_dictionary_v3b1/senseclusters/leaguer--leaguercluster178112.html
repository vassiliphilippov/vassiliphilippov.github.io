leaguer

noun
besieger or blockader
Pronunciation: / ˈliːɡər / (AmE), / ˈliːɡə / (BrE)
a person or military force involved in a siege, especially someone surrounding or camping near a place to capture it

Examples

Enemy leaguer camp
The leaguer surrounded the city and cut off all supplies until it surrendered.
During the long siege, the leaguers built camps around the fortress.
The defenders held out bravely against the leaguer for several months.
The old novel described the daily life of a leaguer during the siege.
Each leaguer had specific duties to help defend the camp from attack.
After months in the field, the leaguers grew accustomed to the hardships of camp life.

Related Terms

  • synonym: besieger - a person or group that lays siege to a place
  • antonym: civilian - a person not in the armed forces
  • antonym: defender - a person or group protecting a place under attack
  • synonym: soldier - a person who serves in an army
