Locusta migratoria

noun
a species of migratory locust
Pronunciation: / loʊˈkʌstə mɪˌɡreɪtɪˈɔriə / (AmE), / ləˈkʌstə mɪˌɡreɪtɪˈɔːriə / (BrE)
a large grasshopper species native to the Old World, known for forming vast swarms that strip large areas of vegetation; the migratory locust

Examples

Locusta migratoria swarm
Locusta migratoria is responsible for some of the world's most devastating agricultural plagues.
Scientists are studying Locusta migratoria to understand what triggers its swarming behavior.
The rapid movement of Locusta migratoria swarms can strip farmlands of all vegetation in days.
