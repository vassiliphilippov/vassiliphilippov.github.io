seed shrimp

noun
small crustacean with bivalve shell
Pronunciation: / sid ʃrɪmp / (AmE), / siːd ʃrɪmp / (BrE)
A tiny marine or freshwater crustacean with a shrimp-like body enclosed in a bivalve shell, belonging to the order Ostracoda.

Examples

Tiny seed shrimp
Scientists collected samples of seed shrimp to study freshwater ecosystems.
The pond water was teeming with seed shrimp darting between the plants.
Unlike true shrimp, seed shrimp have their bodies entirely enclosed in a shell.
