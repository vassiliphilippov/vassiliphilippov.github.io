push one's luck

verb
take unnecessary risks; risk losing success
3.0
Pronunciation: / pʊʃ wʌnz lʌk / (AmE), / pʊʃ wʌnz lʌk / (BrE)
to risk losing your success by continuing to take chances or ask for more than is sensible

Examples

To push my luck
I wouldn't push my luck by asking for another extension on the project.
She knows she succeeded once, but she doesn't want to push her luck again.
They were caught sneaking in, proving that sometimes it's better not to push your luck.
After two victories, he thought entering a third race would be pushing his luck.
If you keep asking for favors, you might be pushing your luck with your boss.
She realized she was pushing her luck by staying out so late.

Related Terms

  • antonym: play it safe - to avoid unnecessary risks
  • synonym: tempt fate - risk bad luck by taking unnecessary chances
