mimetism

noun
imitation in art
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪməˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈmɪmɪˌtɪzəm / (BrE)
the representation or imitation of reality in art, literature, or drama as an artistic principle

Examples

Artistic mimetism
The critic argued that the novel's mimetism created a vivid sense of everyday life.
Many painters in the 19th century embraced mimetism to capture the world as they saw it.
Some modern artists reject mimetism in favor of abstract forms and ideas.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abstraction - art that does not attempt to represent external reality
  • synonym: mimesis - imitation or representation of the real world in art and literature
  • synonym: realism - the artistic movement aiming to represent subject matter truthfully
