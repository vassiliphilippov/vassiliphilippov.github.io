noncooperation

noun
refusal to cooperate
5.4
Pronunciation: /nɑːn.koʊ.ə.ˈpreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /nɑːn.koʊ.ˈpreɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /nɒn.kəʊ.ə.ˈpreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /nɒn.kəʊ.ˈpreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act of deliberately refusing to work together with others, provide help, or obey as a form of protest or disagreement

Examples

Noncooperation movement
The workers' noncooperation slowed down the project significantly.
Due to noncooperation from several members, the committee could not reach a decision.
The teacher addressed the issue of noncooperation among students during group activities.
The movement used noncooperation to challenge the unfair government policies.
Acts of noncooperation included boycotting official events and refusing to pay certain taxes.
Through peaceful noncooperation, the protesters hoped to bring about social change.

Related Terms

  • synonym: civil disobedience - the refusal to obey certain laws as a peaceful form of protest
  • antonym: cooperation - the act of working together with others
  • synonym: resistance - refusal to accept or comply with something
