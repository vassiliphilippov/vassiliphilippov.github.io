reversive

adjective
tending to be turned back; describing reversal
7.2 8.1
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈvɜːr.sɪv/ /; / /rə.ˈvɜːr.sɪv/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈvɜː.sɪv/ /; / /rə.ˈvɜː.sɪv/ (BrE)
(of a process or action) tending to be turned back to a previous state, or characterized by reversal; especially used to describe verbs that indicate a return to a former condition.

Examples

Reversive action
His attitude was reversive, always reverting to what had come before rather than seeking change.
Some chemical reactions are reversive and can proceed in either direction under certain conditions.
The linguist explained that a reversive verb describes an action that returns something to its prior state.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reversible - able to be reversed or turned back
