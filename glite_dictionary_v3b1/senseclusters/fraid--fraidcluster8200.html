fraid

adjective
informal for afraid
Pronunciation: / freɪd / (AmE), / freɪd / (BrE)
informal or dialect spelling of 'afraid', meaning feeling fear or worry about something

Examples

To be fraid
He said he wasn't fraid of the dark, but I could tell he was nervous.
Don't be fraid to try something new, even if it seems difficult at first.
She looked a little fraid when the thunderstorm started outside.
