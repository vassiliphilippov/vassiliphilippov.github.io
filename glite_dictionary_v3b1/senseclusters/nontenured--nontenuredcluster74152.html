nontenured

adjective
without permanent position
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˈtɛnjɚd / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈtɛnjəd / (BrE)
not having a permanent or secure job, especially in a university or other workplace

Examples

Nontenured professor
She worked as a nontenured professor at the university for several years.
Many nontenured faculty members face uncertainty about their future employment.
The college hired several nontenured instructors to teach introductory courses.
The company offered only nontenured positions to new employees during the hiring freeze.
As a nontenured worker, she had to renew her contract every year.
Many nontenured staff members are concerned about job security and future prospects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: temporary - lasting for only a limited period of time
  • antonym: tenured - having a permanent academic position with job security
