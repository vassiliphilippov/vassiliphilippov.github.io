dissociate

verb
to separate oneself from someone or something
5.3
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsoʊsiˌeɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈsəʊsieɪt / (BrE)
to separate oneself from or break involvement with a person, group, or idea

Examples

To dissociate from a group
After the scandal, she decided to dissociate herself from the company.
He tried to dissociate his personal beliefs from his professional responsibilities.
Many politicians publicly dissociate themselves from controversial policies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: associate - to connect or join with someone or something
  • synonym: distance (oneself) - to make oneself less involved or connected
