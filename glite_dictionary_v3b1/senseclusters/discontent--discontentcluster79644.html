discontent

verb
make dissatisfied
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪskənˈtɛnt / (AmE), / ˌdɪskənˈtɛnt / (BrE)
to make someone dissatisfied or unhappy with their current situation

Examples

To discontent the team
Management worried that new policies would discontent experienced employees.
The changes in the schedule have discontented several students.
The unfair decision discontented many fans of the team.

Related Terms

  • antonym: content - to make someone satisfied or pleased
  • synonym: dissatisfy - to make someone not satisfied or pleased
