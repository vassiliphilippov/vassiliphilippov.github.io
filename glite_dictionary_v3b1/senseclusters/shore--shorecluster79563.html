shore

verb
to arrive at or reach the shore
3.3 0.17/m
Pronunciation: /  É…îr / (AmE), /  É…îÀê / (BrE)
(of a ship, boat, or passenger) to arrive at or reach the shore; to land.

Examples

To shore safely
After weeks at sea, the sailors were relieved to finally shore safely.
Several canoes shored on the sandy beach during the storm.
The boat will shore at dawn after crossing the lake.

Synonyms

  • land
    1.0 47/m
    land means coming down onto a surface or bringing something down, while shore means arriving specifically at the coast from a boat.
  • set ashore
    4.1 0.01/m
    set ashore means to bring someone or something onto the shore from a boat deliberately, while shore means to arrive or land at the coast.
