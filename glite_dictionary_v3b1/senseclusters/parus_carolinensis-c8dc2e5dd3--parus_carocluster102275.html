Parus carolinensis

noun
southern US chickadee species
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛɹ.əs ˌkæɹ.əˌlaɪˈnɛn.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌpæɹ.əs ˌkæ.rə.laɪˈnɛn.sɪs / (BrE)
A species of chickadee found in the southern United States, similar to the blackcap but smaller; also known as the Carolina chickadee.

Examples

The Parus carolinensis bird
The Parus carolinensis is a common sight at bird feeders in the southern states.
Unlike its northern cousin, the Parus carolinensis has a less distinct black cap.
We spotted a Parus carolinensis singing in the oak trees this morning.
