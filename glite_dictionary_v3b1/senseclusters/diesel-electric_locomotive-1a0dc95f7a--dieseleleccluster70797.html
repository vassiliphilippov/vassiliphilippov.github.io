diesel-electric locomotive

noun
locomotive powered by diesel-generated electricity
3.8 4.1
Pronunciation: /ˈdiː.zəl ɪ.ˈlek.trɪk loʊ.kə.ˈmoʊ.tɪv/ /; / /ˈdiː.zəl ə.ˈlek.trɪk loʊ.kə.ˈmoʊ.tɪv/ (AmE), /ˈdiː.zəl ɪ.ˈlek.trɪk ləʊ.kə.ˈməʊ.tɪv/ /; / /ˈdiː.zəl ə.ˈlek.trɪk ləʊ.kə.ˈməʊ.tɪv/ (BrE)
a locomotive powered by electric motors that receive electricity generated by an onboard diesel engine

Examples

A diesel-electric locomotive
A diesel-electric locomotive combines the power of a diesel engine with the reliability of electric traction.
The railway company purchased a new diesel-electric locomotive to improve fuel efficiency on its main line.
Unlike steam engines, diesel-electric locomotives require less maintenance and can operate for longer distances.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diesel locomotive - a locomotive powered solely by a diesel engine without electrical transmission
  • degree lower: electric locomotive - a locomotive powered by electricity, typically from an external source
  • degree lower: steam locomotive - a locomotive powered by steam from boiled water
