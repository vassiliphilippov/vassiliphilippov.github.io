King's Counsel

noun
senior barrister appointed by the king
1.9
Pronunciation: / kɪŋz ˈkaʊnsəl / (AmE), / kɪŋz ˈkaʊnsəl / (BrE)
A senior barrister appointed as counsel to the Crown when the British monarch is a king, recognized for outstanding ability and status in the legal profession.

Examples

King's Counsel appointment
She was appointed King's Counsel after many years of distinguished service at the bar.
The trial featured a renowned King's Counsel leading the prosecution.
When the monarch changed from queen to king, all existing Queen's Counsel became King's Counsel automatically.
