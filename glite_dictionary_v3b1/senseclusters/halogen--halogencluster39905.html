halogen

noun
group of five reactive nonmetallic elements in chemistry
2.7 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈhæ.lə.dʒən/ /; / /ˈheɪ.lə.dʒən/ (AmE), /ˈhæ.lə.dʒən/ /; / /ˈheɪ.lə.dʒən/ (BrE)
any of a group of five related nonmetallic chemical elements (fluorine, chlorine, bromine, iodine, and astatine) that are highly reactive and readily form negative ions

Examples

The halogen group
All members of the halogen group are highly reactive, especially with metals.
Chlorine and iodine are two common halogens used in everyday products.
The chemical reactivity of each halogen increases as you move up the group in the periodic table.
