cattily

adverb
spitefully or meanly
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkætɪli / (AmE), / ˈkætɪli / (BrE)
in a way that is spiteful, subtly cruel, or mean, especially in speech or behavior

Examples

To speak cattily
She answered cattily when asked about her colleague's mistake.
He cattily remarked on her choice of outfit at the meeting.
The two friends exchanged cattily comments during the heated discussion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: kindly - in a friendly, generous, or considerate way
  • synonym: maliciously - in a way that shows a desire to harm someone
  • synonym: spitefully - in a way that shows a desire to hurt or upset someone
