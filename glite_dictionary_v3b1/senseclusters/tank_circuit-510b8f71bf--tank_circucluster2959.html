tank circuit

noun
oscillatory electric circuit
9.9
Pronunciation: / tæŋk ˈsɝː.kɪt / (AmE), / tæŋk ˈsɜː.kɪt / (BrE)
an electric circuit consisting of an inductor and a capacitor connected together, used to produce or receive oscillating currents at a particular frequency

Examples

An LC tank circuit
An LC tank circuit is often used in signal processing applications.
The radio relies on a tank circuit to tune into specific frequencies.
When designing oscillators, an efficient tank circuit can minimize energy loss.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oscillatory circuit - a circuit that generates oscillations, usually using inductors and capacitors
