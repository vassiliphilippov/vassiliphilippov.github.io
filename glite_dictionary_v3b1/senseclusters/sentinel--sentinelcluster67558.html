sentinel

noun
system-monitoring service
6.8
Pronunciation: /ˈsen.tɪ.nəl/ /; / /ˈsen.tə.nəl/ (AmE), /ˈsen.tɪ.nəl/ /; / /ˈsen.tə.nəl/ (BrE)
(in computing) a software component or service that monitors the health or status of systems, applications, or nodes and triggers alerts or automated failover when problems are detected.

Examples

System sentinel
The database cluster uses a sentinel to monitor node health and trigger automatic failover.
Our deployment includes multiple sentinel instances that alert engineers when latency exceeds thresholds.
Cloud providers offer a sentinel service to continuously check service health and restart failing nodes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: monitor - a tool or process that observes system performance or status
  • synonym: watchdog - a program or device that detects failures and initiates recovery actions
