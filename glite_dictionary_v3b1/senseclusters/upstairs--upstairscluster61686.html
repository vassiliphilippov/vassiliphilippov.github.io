upstairs

adverb
concerning the mind; mentally
Pronunciation: / ʌpˈstɛɹz / (AmE), / ʌpˈstɛəz / (BrE)
(informal) In or with respect to the mind; mentally; relating to one's mental faculties.

Examples

Not right upstairs
People say he isn't quite right upstairs after the accident.
She seems perfectly normal, but sometimes I wonder what's going on upstairs.
You need to be sharp upstairs to solve these kinds of puzzles.

Related Terms

  • antonym: downstairs - relating to the physical or practical, as opposed to the mental (sometimes used in contrast)
