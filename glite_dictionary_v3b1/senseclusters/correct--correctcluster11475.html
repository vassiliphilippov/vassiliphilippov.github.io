correct

verb
punish to enforce obedience
Pronunciation: / kəˈrɛkt / (AmE), / kəˈrɛkt / (BrE)
to punish, reprimand, or censure someone in order to improve their behavior

Examples

To correct bad behavior
In the past, teachers would correct students who misbehaved with detention or stricter measures.
Some parents believe it is necessary to correct their children when they break important rules.
The organization was criticized for the harsh methods it used to correct juvenile offenders.
He was corrected by his manager in front of the whole team for his careless mistake.
Some parents believe in physically correcting their children, though it is a controversial practice.
The teacher did not hesitate to correct students who displayed disrespectful behavior.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chastise - to criticize or punish severely
  • synonym: discipline - to punish or train someone to obey rules or a code of behavior
  • antonym: praise - to express warm approval or admiration; the opposite of censure
  • synonym: scold - to speak angrily to someone because they have done something wrong
