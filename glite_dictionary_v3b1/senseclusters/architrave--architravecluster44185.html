architrave

noun
molding around a door or window
Pronunciation: / ˈɑrkɪˌtreɪv /; / ˈɑrtʃɪˌtreɪv / (AmE), / ˈɑːkɪˌtreɪv /; / ˈɑːtʃɪtreɪv / (BrE)
a horizontal molded frame or band above a doorway, window, or opening, or the lowest part of an entablature above columns in classical architecture

Examples

The architrave above window
As the carpenter worked, he carefully installed the architrave above the window.
Many classical buildings feature a decorative architrave as part of their interior trim.
The ornate architrave around the entrance caught everyone's attention.
Each column bore an ornately carved architrave that highlighted the entrance to the hall.
Restorers carefully cleaned the architrave to reveal its original stonework details.
The decorative architrave supports the frieze and cornice above the ancient columns.
