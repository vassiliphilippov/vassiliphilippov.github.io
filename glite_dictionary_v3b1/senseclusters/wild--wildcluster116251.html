wild

adjective
intensely vivid or loud (colors or sounds)
Pronunciation: / waɪld / (AmE), / waɪld / (BrE)
(of colors, sounds, weather, movement, or emotions) very intense, vivid, extreme, or uncontrolled

Examples

Wild colors
She wore a wild red dress that caught everyone's attention.
Suddenly, we heard wild shouts coming from the concert hall.
The painting uses wild colors to create a dramatic effect.
A wild shot ricocheted off the wall, missing its target completely.
Her first serve was wild, landing several feet outside the court.
The pitcher threw a wild ball that sailed far above the catcher's glove.
As the wild storm approached, everyone hurried to find shelter.
Dark, wild clouds gathered overhead, threatening a powerful downpour.
The wild winds battered the coastline all night long.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accurate - precisely aimed, hitting intended target
  • antonym: calm - peaceful or tranquil (of natural forces)
  • antonym: dull - (of sounds or colors) not vivid, bright, or intense
  • synonym: furious - extremely angry or violent (of winds, storms, etc.)
  • synonym: vivid - (of colors) intensely deep or bright
