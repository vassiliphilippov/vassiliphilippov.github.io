replate

verb
to replace a plate
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈpleɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈpleɪt / (BrE)
to attach a new plate or replace an existing plate, especially for support or repair in surgery or engineering

Examples

To replate a bone
The engineer had to replate the damaged section of the bridge to ensure its safety.
After the initial surgery failed, the doctor decided to replate the bone with a stronger material.
It is sometimes necessary to replate old equipment to restore its structural integrity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: remove - to take something away or off
  • synonym: replace - to put something new in the place of something old
