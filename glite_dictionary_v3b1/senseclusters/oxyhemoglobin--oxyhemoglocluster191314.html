oxyhemoglobin

noun
oxygen-carrying form of hemoglobin
3.4
Pronunciation: / ˌɑksiˌhiːməˈɡloʊbɪn / (AmE), / ˌɒksɪˌhiːməˈɡləʊbɪn / (BrE)
the bright red compound formed when hemoglobin combines with oxygen, responsible for transporting oxygen from the lungs to the tissues

Examples

Bright oxyhemoglobin compound
Red blood cells contain oxyhemoglobin, which gives arterial blood its bright color.
The ability of oxyhemoglobin to release oxygen is vital to cellular respiration.
When oxygen binds to hemoglobin in the lungs, it forms oxyhemoglobin.
Oxyhaemoglobin is responsible for the red color of oxygenated blood.
Doctors measured the patient's oxyhaemoglobin levels to assess how well his lungs functioned.
The rapid release of oxygen from oxyhaemoglobin is vital for cellular respiration.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hemoglobin - the iron-containing protein in red blood cells that carries oxygen
