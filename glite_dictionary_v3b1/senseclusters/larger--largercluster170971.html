larger

adjective
comparative of 'large'; greater in size
1.0 1.74/m
Pronunciation: / Ààl…ërd í…ôr / (AmE), / Ààl…ëÀêd í…ô / (BrE)
greater in size, amount, or extent than something else; comparative form of 'large'.

Examples

A larger suitcase
She chose a larger suitcase to fit all her clothes for the trip.
The elephant is larger than the horse in both height and weight.
This file is larger than the one I sent you yesterday.

Synonyms

  • bigger
    1.0 0.19/m
    bigger is more informal and usually talks about physical size, while larger is neutral and can mean size or amount.
  • greater
    1.0 0.05/m
    greater is often more formal and used for importance or degree, while larger mainly describes size or amount.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: large - of considerable or relatively great size
  • degree higher: largest - the greatest in size or extent
