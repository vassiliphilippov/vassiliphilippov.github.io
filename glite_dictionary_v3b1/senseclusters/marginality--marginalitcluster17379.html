marginality

noun
state of being on the fringe or margin
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑrdʒɪˈnæləti / (AmE), / ˌmɑːdʒɪˈnælɪti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being marginal, especially being on the fringes of a group, society, or field

Examples

Cultural marginality
Her work explores themes of cultural marginality and identity in the modern world.
The marginality of certain social groups can lead to feelings of exclusion and disenfranchisement.
Urban planners are addressing the marginality of neighborhoods left behind by economic development.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mainstream - the prevailing current of thought, influence, or activity
