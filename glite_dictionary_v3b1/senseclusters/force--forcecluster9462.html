force

verb
to impose something insistently
2.3 128/m
Pronunciation: / f…îrs / (AmE), / f…îÀês / (BrE)
to make someone do something or make something happen by using power, pressure, or influence; to compel

Examples

To force a decision
He disliked it when others force their beliefs on him.
She tried to force her opinions on her colleagues during every meeting.
The government cannot force people to accept new laws without proper debate.
His illness forced him to quit his job sooner than planned.
She tried not to force her opinion on her colleagues, but sometimes it happened.
The teacher had to force the students to finish the assignment before leaving.
Circumstances forced her to make a difficult decision.
I was forced to apologize, even though I hadn't done anything wrong.
They tried to force him to sign the contract, but he refused.

Synonyms

  • compel
    4.8 18/m
    compel makes someone act because of strong power or necessity, while force is a general act of making someone do something.
  • coerce
    6.4 5.28/m
    coerce uses threats or force to make someone obey, while force means using power, pressure, or influence to make things happen.
  • make
    1.0 79/m
    make forces or causes someone to act in everyday speech, while force suggests using power, pressure, or strong influence.
  • oblige
    4.5 7.37/m
    oblige makes someone act because law, duty, or circumstances require it, while force uses power or pressure to make action happen.
  • impose
    4.8 23/m
    impose forces people to accept rules, duties, or costs from authority, while force means making someone act by power or pressure.
  • pressure
    4.2 7.73/m
    pressure tries to persuade or push someone to act over time, while force uses direct power or compulsion to make them do it.
  • press
    3.9 15/m
    press urges or persistently ask someone to do something, often by argument, while force uses power or pressure to compel action.

Related Terms

  • antonym: allow - to let someone do something

  • synonym: compel - to force someone to do something
  • synonym: impose - to force something to be accepted or put in place
  • degree lower: persuade - to convince someone to do something, often by reasoning
