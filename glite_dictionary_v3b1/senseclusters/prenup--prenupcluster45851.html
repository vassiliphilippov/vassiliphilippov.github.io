prenup

noun
marriage asset agreement
5.4 0.97/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàpriÀên åp / (AmE), / ÀàpriÀên åp / (BrE)
a legal agreement made before marriage that sets out how assets will be divided if the couple divorces

Examples

Sign a prenup
They decided to sign a prenup to avoid disputes over property in the future.
Some people believe a prenup is essential for protecting personal assets before marriage.
The lawyer explained the benefits of having a prenup to both partners.
They both agreed to sign a prenup to protect their individual assets before getting married.
Some people believe a prenup can help avoid conflicts if a marriage ends in divorce.
Her lawyer recommended a prenup to clarify financial responsibilities in the marriage.

Synonyms

    6.1 0.56/m
    prenuptial agreement is the full formal name used in law and papers, while prenup is the short informal term people use.
  • ante-nuptial agreement
    8.4 0.00/m
    ante-nuptial agreement is a formal legal term often used in older documents, while prenup is the common modern word.
  • marriage contract
    3.6 0.35/m
    marriage contract is a legal agreement covering marriage terms and property, while prenup specifically focuses on dividing assets if the couple divorces.
  • marriage settlement
    6.4 0.01/m
    marriage settlement is an older formal deal that may cover wider financial arrangements, while prenup focuses on asset division at divorce.
