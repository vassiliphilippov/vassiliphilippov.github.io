Donar

proper noun
Teutonic god of thunder
Pronunciation: / ˈdoʊ.nɑr / (AmE), / ˈdəʊ.nɑː / (BrE)
In Teutonic mythology, the god of thunder, considered the counterpart of Norse Thor.

Examples

Donar god of thunder
According to Germanic legend, Donar was responsible for thunder and lightning.
In some historical texts, Donar is described as wielding a mighty hammer.
The ancient tribes worshipped Donar to protect their villages from storms.
