tough-minded

adjective
realistic and determined in facing problems
4.5
Pronunciation: / tʌfˈmaɪndɪd / (AmE), / tʌfˈmaɪndɪd / (BrE)
Dealing with facts or problems in a realistic and determined way; not easily influenced by emotions or sentiment.

Examples

Tough-minded judge
Despite criticism, the judge remained tough-minded in delivering the verdict.
Journalists must be tough-minded when investigating sensitive issues, never shying away from the truth.
The company's new CEO is known for her tough-minded approach to management and problem-solving.

Related Terms

  • synonym: realistic - accepting things as they are in fact
  • antonym: soft-hearted - easily moved by feelings of sympathy or pity
