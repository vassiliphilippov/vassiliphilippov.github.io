black rot

noun
fungal disease of plants causing decay
Pronunciation: / ˈblæk rɑt / (AmE), / ˈblæk rɒt / (BrE)
A fungal disease that causes darkening and decay of the leaves, stems, or fruits of various plants, especially fruits and vegetables.

Examples

Black rot disease
Cabbage with black rot develops v-shaped lesions at the leaf edges.
Farmers must remove infected plants quickly to prevent black rot from spreading in the field.
Grapes can be severely damaged by black rot during warm, humid weather.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blight - a plant disease, typically caused by fungi, that results in damage or death to parts of the plant
  • synonym: mildew - a fungal disease affecting plants, causing a white, powdery coating on leaves and stems
