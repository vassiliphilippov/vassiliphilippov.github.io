supertrain

verb
to train intensively
8.7
Pronunciation: /ˈsuː.pər.treɪn/ /; / /suː.pər.ˈtreɪn/ (AmE), /ˈsuː.pə.treɪn/ /; / /suː.pə.ˈtreɪn/ (BrE)
to train someone or something with very high intensity or using advanced methods to achieve superior results

Examples

To supertrain athletes
The coach decided to supertrain the athletes before the big competition to maximize their performance.
If you supertrain your dog, it can learn advanced tricks much faster than usual.
Some people believe you should not supertrain because it can lead to exhaustion or injury.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: train - to teach or exercise someone or something to improve skills or fitness
