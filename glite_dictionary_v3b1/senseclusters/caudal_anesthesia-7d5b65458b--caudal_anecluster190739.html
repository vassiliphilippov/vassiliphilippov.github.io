caudal anesthesia

noun
regional anesthesia via injection into the lower spine
1.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔdəl ænəsˈθiʒə /; / ˈkɔdəl əˌniːsθiə / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdəl ænɪsˈθiːzɪə /; / ˈkɔːdəl əˌniːsθiːə / (BrE)
a type of regional anesthesia produced by injecting an anesthetic into the caudal (lower) end of the spinal canal, typically used for surgical procedures involving the lower part of the body; now largely replaced by epidural anesthesia

Examples

Caudal anesthesia injection
Compared to epidural anesthesia, caudal anesthesia is now less commonly performed.
Some pediatric surgeries use caudal anesthesia because it is both effective and safe for children.
The patient received caudal anesthesia before the lower limb surgery.
Caudal anaesthesia is often used in pediatric surgery to numb the lower part of the body.
Advances in epidural techniques have made caudal anaesthesia less common in adults.
The surgeon opted for caudal anaesthesia instead of general anesthesia for the procedure.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: epidural anesthesia - regional anesthesia injected into the epidural space of the spinal cord
  • synonym: spinal anesthesia - regional anesthesia injected into the spinal canal but above the caudal region
