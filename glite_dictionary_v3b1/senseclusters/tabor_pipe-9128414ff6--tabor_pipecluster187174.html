tabor pipe

noun
small one-handed flute for playing with a drum
Pronunciation: / ˈteɪbər paɪp / (AmE), / ˈteɪbə paɪp / (BrE)
a small fipple flute designed to be played with one hand, typically the left, while the other hand plays a tabor (drum)

Examples

A tabor pipe player
In medieval festivals, performers often entertained crowds with a tabor pipe and tabor combination.
The musician played a lively tune on the tabor pipe while keeping rhythm on a small drum.
Traditionally, the tabor pipe allows one person to provide both melody and percussion simultaneously.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fipple flute - a flute with a duct that directs air onto the edge of a hole, producing sound
  • degree higher: recorder - a type of fipple flute with a whistle mouthpiece; related instrument
