Dnieper River

proper noun
major river in Eastern Europe
5.8
Pronunciation: / niˈpiːpər ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈdniːpə ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
A major river in Eastern Europe that rises near Smolensk in Russia and flows south through Belarus and Ukraine into the Black Sea.

Examples

The Dnieper River flows
Many historic cities, including Kyiv, are located along the Dnieper River.
The Dnieper River is one of the most important waterways in Ukraine.
Trade routes have followed the Dnieper River for centuries.
