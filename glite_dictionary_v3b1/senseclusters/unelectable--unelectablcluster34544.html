unelectable

adjective
not eligible for election
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnɪˈlɛktəbəl / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪˈlɛktəbəl / (BrE)
not legally permitted or qualified to be elected to a particular office or position due to disqualifying rules or requirements

Examples

Unelectable by law
Due to his criminal record, he was considered unelectable for public office.
The new law made anyone under thirty unelectable for the council position.
After losing her citizenship, she became unelectable according to the organization's rules.

Related Terms

  • antonym: eligible - qualified or allowed to be chosen or to do something
  • synonym: ineligible - not qualified or allowed to do or receive something
