liabilities

noun
debts or obligations owed
5.6 3.9
Pronunciation: /laɪ.ə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiz/ /; / /laɪ.ə.ˈbɪl.ə.tiz/ (AmE), /laɪ.ə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiz/ /; / /laɪ.ə.ˈbɪl.ə.tiz/ (BrE)
the amounts of money or other obligations that a person or company owes to others, especially as recorded in financial accounts

Examples

Financial liabilities
Student loans and mortgages are considered liabilities in personal finance statements.
The company's total liabilities exceeded its assets, causing concern among investors.
When preparing the balance sheet, she carefully calculated all the liabilities owed by the business.

Related Terms

  • synonym: debt - something, typically money, that is owed or due
