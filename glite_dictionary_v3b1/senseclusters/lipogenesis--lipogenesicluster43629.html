lipogenesis

noun
fat creation process
8.8
Pronunciation: /lɪp.ə.ˈdʒen.ə.sɪs/ (AmE), /lɪp.ə.ˈdʒen.ə.sɪs/ (BrE)
the process by which the body creates fat (fatty acids and triglycerides) from other substances, such as carbohydrates

Examples

Lipogenesis process
The liver is a major site of lipogenesis in the human body.
High carbohydrate intake can stimulate lipogenesis and increase fat storage.
Scientists study lipogenesis to understand how the body regulates energy and fat levels.
A high-carbohydrate diet can stimulate lipogenesis in the liver.
Researchers study lipogenesis to understand how the body stores excess energy as fat.
Certain hormones can regulate the rate of lipogenesis in animals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fat synthesis - the creation of fat from other substances
  • synonym: fatty acid synthesis - the creation of fatty acids from smaller molecules in cells
  • antonym: lipolysis - the breakdown of fats and other lipids to release fatty acids
