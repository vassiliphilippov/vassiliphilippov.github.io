mushroom

verb
grow or spread rapidly
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌʃˌruːm /; / ˈmʌʃˌrum / (AmE), / ˈmʌʃrʊm /; / ˈmʌʃ.ruːm / (BrE)
to grow, increase, or spread very quickly

Examples

Problems mushroom quickly
Concerns about data security have mushroomed in recent years.
Small businesses mushroomed throughout the neighborhood as the economy improved.
The number of new restaurants mushroomed after the city lifted restrictions.
If we aren't careful, the scope of this assignment could quickly mushroom beyond our resources.
Under her leadership, they managed to mushroom the project into a national initiative.
With new funding, the research team will mushroom the project in the next year.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contract - to become smaller in size or amount
  • synonym: expand - to become larger in size, number, or amount
  • synonym: proliferate - to increase rapidly in number or amount
