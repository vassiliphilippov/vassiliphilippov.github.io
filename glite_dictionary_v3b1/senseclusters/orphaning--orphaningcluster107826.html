orphaning

noun
isolated text line
Pronunciation: / ˈɔːrfənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɔːfənɪŋ / (BrE)
the occurrence of a single line or very short fragment of a paragraph being left alone at the top or bottom of a page or column in printed or digital text

Examples

Orphaning in text layout
Good page design avoids orphaning to make text easier to read.
The editor adjusted the layout to prevent orphaning in the final printed book.
Modern word processors include features to automatically reduce orphaning in documents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: widowing - the occurrence of a single line of a paragraph left at the bottom of a page or column
