trust fund

noun
fund held and managed by a trustee
Pronunciation: / trʌst fʌnd / (AmE), / trʌst fʌnd / (BrE)
a fund consisting of money or assets held and managed by a trustee for the benefit of another person or group

Examples

A trust fund account
Many charities use a trust fund to manage donations and ensure long-term financial support.
She inherited a substantial trust fund from her grandparents.
The child's education was paid for entirely by a trust fund set up at birth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: endowment - fund donated to an institution, often for ongoing support
