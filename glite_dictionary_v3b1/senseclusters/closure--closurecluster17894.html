closure

noun
parliamentary procedure to end debate
Pronunciation: / ˈkloʊʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈkləʊʒə / (BrE)
a procedure for ending debate and taking a vote in a legislative or deliberative body

Examples

Closure motion
A motion for closure was introduced to bring the lengthy debate to a halt.
After hours of discussion, the chairperson moved to invoke closure on the matter.
The senator called for closure to prevent further delays in passing the bill.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cloture - a procedure aimed at ending a debate and taking a vote
