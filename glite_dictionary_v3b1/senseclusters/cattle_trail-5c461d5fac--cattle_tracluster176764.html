cattle trail

noun
route for driving cattle to market
Pronunciation: / ˈkæt̬əl treɪl / (AmE), / ˈkætəl treɪl / (BrE)
A route or path along which cattle are driven, especially to market or railheads.

Examples

The cattle trail route
Many towns grew up along the famous cattle trail routes of the Old West.
Ranchers depended on the cattle trail to move their herds each spring.
The historic cattle trail stretched for hundreds of miles across the plains.
