monoxide

noun
compound with one oxygen atom
Pronunciation: / məˈnɑksaɪd / (AmE), / məˈnɒksaɪd / (BrE)
A compound consisting of one atom of oxygen combined with another element.

Examples

Carbon monoxide
Carbon monoxide is a colorless, odorless, and poisonous gas found in car exhaust.
Exposure to monoxide can be dangerous without proper ventilation.
The scientist analyzed the sample for traces of nitrogen monoxide.
