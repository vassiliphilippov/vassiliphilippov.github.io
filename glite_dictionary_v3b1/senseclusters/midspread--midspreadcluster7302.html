midspread

noun
difference between upper and lower quartiles (IQR)
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪdˌsprɛd / (AmE), / ˈmɪdˌsprɛd / (BrE)
a measure of statistical dispersion, equal to the difference between the upper and lower quartiles (the interquartile range) in a data set.

Examples

The midspread measure
If there are outliers in a data set, the midspread gives a better sense of variability than the full range.
Statisticians often prefer to use midspread as a robust measure of spread.
The midspread is useful for understanding how the middle half of the data is distributed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interquartile range - a measure of statistical dispersion, the difference between the upper and lower quartiles
  • degree higher: range - the difference between the highest and lowest values in a data set
