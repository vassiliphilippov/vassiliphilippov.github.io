globality

noun
system-wide scope
Pronunciation: / ɡloʊˈbæləti / (AmE), / ɡləʊˈbælɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of applying to an entire system or whole, rather than just a part, especially in computing or mathematics

Examples

Globality of a rule
The globality of the rule means it affects every part of the system equally.
In mathematics, globality refers to properties that hold for an entire structure, not just a section.
Programmers must consider the globality of variables to avoid unexpected errors in large projects.

Related Terms

  • antonym: locality - the property of being limited to a specific part or area
  • synonym: universality - the quality of being true or applicable everywhere or in all cases
