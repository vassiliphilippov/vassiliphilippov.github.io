technical analysis

noun
forecasting prices by analyzing past market trends
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛknɪkəl əˈnæləsɪs /; / ˈtɛknɪkəl əˈnæləsɪs / (AmE), / ˌtɛknɪkəl əˈnalɪsɪs /; / ˈtɛknɪkəl əˈnalɪsɪs / (BrE)
the study of past market prices and trading volumes to predict future trends in financial markets

Examples

Technical analysis of stocks
He used technical analysis to predict when the stock price would reach its next high point.
Many investors rely on technical analysis to identify patterns and make informed trading decisions.
The finance course introduced students to both technical analysis and fundamental analysis of stock markets.
Many investors use technical analysis to identify potential buying opportunities.
She gave a presentation on technical analysis techniques for forecasting stock prices.
Unlike technical analysis, fundamental analysis focuses on company earnings and economic data.
