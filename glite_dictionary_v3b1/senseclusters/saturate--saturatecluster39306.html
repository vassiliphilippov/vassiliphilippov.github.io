saturate

verb
cause to absorb the maximum of another substance
1.7 4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsætʃəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈsætjʊreɪt / (BrE)
to cause (a chemical compound, vapor, solution, or magnetic material) to unite with or contain the greatest possible amount of another substance

Examples

To saturate a solution
If you saturate iron with magnetism, it cannot become magnetized further.
The chemist tried to saturate the solution with salt until no more would dissolve.
The vapor in the chamber began to saturate the air, reaching its maximum possible concentration.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: supersaturate - to increase the concentration of a solution beyond its normal saturation point
