clamor

verb
make loud demands or protests
2.9
Pronunciation: / ˈklæmɚ /; / ˈklæmər / (AmE), / ˈklæmə / (BrE)
to make loud and persistent demands or protests, often as a group

Examples

To clamor for change
Fans clamored to see the celebrity as she arrived at the event.
Reporters clamored for answers as soon as the announcement was made.
The protestors clamored for better working conditions outside the government building.
Parents clamored the school board into extending the library hours.
The fans clamored the team into singing one more song after the game.
The protesters clamored the management into reconsidering their decision.
Activists clamored their demands for immediate government action.
Reporters clamored questions at the mayor as she left the building.
The crowd clamored for the band to return to the stage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coerce - to persuade or force someone to do something by pressure
  • synonym: demand - ask for something forcefully
  • antonym: murmur - speak or make sound softly or indistinctly
  • synonym: protest - express strong objection or disapproval publicly
  • degree lower: request - ask for something politely
  • synonym: shout - utter a loud call or cry
