blot

verb
to dry ink with blotting paper
Pronunciation: / blɑt / (AmE), / blɒt / (BrE)
to absorb or dry up liquid from a surface by pressing something soft, like paper or cloth, against it

Examples

To blot up water
After writing his name, he used a sheet of paper to blot the excess ink.
Make sure to blot the letter before folding it.
She gently blotted the signature to prevent the ink from smudging.
She quickly blotted the spilled coffee with a napkin to prevent a stain.
After washing his face, he blotted it dry with a soft towel.
If you drop ink on the table, blot it gently with paper towels.
She quickly blotted the ink spill with a tissue before it could stain the table.
After washing his face, he blots it dry with a clean towel.
If you spill juice on the carpet, gently blot the area instead of rubbing it.
