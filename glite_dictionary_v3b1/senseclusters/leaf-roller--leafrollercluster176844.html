leaf-roller

noun
moth whose larvae roll leaves into nests
Pronunciation: / ˈlif ˌroʊlər / (AmE), / ˈliːf ˌrəʊlə / (BrE)
a moth whose larvae roll and tie leaves with silk to form nests, especially those of the family Tortricidae

Examples

A leaf-roller moth
A biologist collected several leaf-rollers for her entomology research project.
Gardeners often look for signs of a leaf-roller infestation in the spring and summer.
The caterpillar of the leaf-roller can cause significant damage to crops by rolling up leaves.
