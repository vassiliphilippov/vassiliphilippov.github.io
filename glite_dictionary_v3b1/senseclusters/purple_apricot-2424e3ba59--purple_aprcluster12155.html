purple apricot

noun
a hybrid apricot with purple fruit and twigs
Pronunciation: / ˈpɝːpəl ˈæp.rɪ.kɑt / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.pəl ˈæp.rɪ.kɒt / (BrE)
a small hybrid apricot native to Asia and Asia Minor, notable for its purplish twigs, white flowers, and purple fruit considered inferior in quality

Examples

The purple apricot fruit
Botanists have documented the purple apricot in several regions of Central Asia.
Despite its attractive color, the purple apricot is rarely grown for commercial fruit production.
The purple apricot is prized more for its unusual appearance than for its taste.
