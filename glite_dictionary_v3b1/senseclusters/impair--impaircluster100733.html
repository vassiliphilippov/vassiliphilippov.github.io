impair

verb
to make something weaker or less good
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɛr / (AmE), / ɪmˈpeə / (BrE)
to damage or weaken something, making it less effective or less good

Examples

To impair vision
His vision was impaired by the heavy fog.
Smoking can impair your lung function over time.
Too much alcohol will impair your ability to drive safely.
Drinking alcohol may impair your ability to drive safely.
Her hearing was impaired after the accident.
Lack of sleep can impair your memory and concentration.

Related Terms

  • synonym: damage - to cause physical harm to something
  • antonym: improve - to make something better
