rude

adjective
impolite; lacking social manners
3.8
Pronunciation: / ruːd / (AmE), / ruːd / (BrE)
not polite; showing a lack of manners, consideration, or social refinement

Examples

Rude comment
His rude remarks offended several people at the dinner party.
It was very rude of him to interrupt their conversation without asking.
She found the waiter's rude behavior unacceptable and asked to speak to the manager.
Her rude remarks betrayed her lack of social grace.
His rude behavior at the formal dinner shocked everyone present.
The rude countryman struggled to adapt to the elegant city life.
It was very rude of him to interrupt the conversation.
Please do not be rude to the staff, even if you are frustrated.
She found his comments unexpectedly rude and left the party early.

Related Terms

  • antonym: courteous - polite and showing respect for others
  • synonym: discourteous - showing rudeness and a lack of consideration for other people
  • synonym: impolite - not having or showing good manners; rude
  • antonym: polite - having or showing good manners and respect
  • antonym: refined - polite, cultured, and having good manners
  • synonym: uncouth - lacking good manners, refinement, or grace
