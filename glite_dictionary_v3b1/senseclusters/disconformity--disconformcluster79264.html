disconformity

noun
gap in rock layers
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪskənˈfɔːrməti / (AmE), / ˌdɪskənˈfɔːməti / (BrE)
a break or gap in a sequence of parallel rock layers where some layers are missing, indicating a period of erosion or non-deposition

Examples

Rock layer disconformity
The geologist identified a disconformity between the two sedimentary layers.
A disconformity can indicate a significant period of erosion in the geological record.
By studying the disconformity, scientists learned about ancient changes in sea level.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conformity - continuous sequence of rock layers without interruption
  • synonym: unconformity - a surface representing a break or gap in the geological record
