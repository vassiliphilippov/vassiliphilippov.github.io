unconfronted

adjective
not faced directly
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnkənˈfrʌntɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnkənˈfrʌntɪd / (BrE)
not having been faced, addressed, or dealt with directly.

Examples

An unconfronted problem
Many problems in the organization remained unconfronted despite repeated warnings.
His fears stayed unconfronted until he finally decided to seek help.
Leaving the issue unconfronted only made the situation worse over time.
