atretic

adjective
abnormally closed
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈtrɛtɪk / (AmE), / əˈtrɛtɪk / (BrE)
describing a body part or passage that is abnormally closed or lacks an opening

Examples

Atretic passage
The surgeon noted that the infant had an atretic esophagus requiring immediate attention.
An atretic bile duct can cause serious complications in newborns.
The scan revealed an atretic passage where the normal opening should have been.

Related Terms

  • synonym: occluded - blocked or closed up
  • antonym: patent - open and unobstructed, especially of a bodily passage
