shift

noun
a displacement without rotation
1.0 2.9
Pronunciation: /ʃɪft/ (AmE), /ʃɪft/ (BrE)
an act or event of moving or changing from one place, position, state, or focus to another

Examples

A sudden shift
A sudden shift in the position of the cargo caused the truck to tip slightly.
Careful measurement detected a shift in the alignment of the machine parts.
The shift of the tectonic plates resulted in a minor earthquake.
Her mood underwent a noticeable shift during the meeting.
The shift from manual to automated systems changed the factory's operations.
There was a sudden shift in the crowd as the doors opened.
A sudden shift in the wind changed the direction of the fire.
The company's shift to remote work improved employee satisfaction.
There was a noticeable shift in public opinion after the debate.
