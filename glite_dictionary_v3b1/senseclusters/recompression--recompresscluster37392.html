recompression

noun
restoring pressure
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkəmˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːkəmˈprɛʃən / (BrE)
the process of increasing pressure again, especially as a treatment for decompression sickness in divers or patients.

Examples

Diver recompression
After surfacing too quickly, the diver was taken for immediate recompression to treat his symptoms.
Special chambers are used for recompression therapy in cases of decompression sickness.
The doctor explained that recompression can help reverse the effects of the bends.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decompression - the process of reducing pressure, especially after being under high pressure
  • synonym: pressurization - the act of increasing or maintaining pressure in a space
