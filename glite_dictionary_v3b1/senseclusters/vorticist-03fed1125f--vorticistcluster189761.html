Vorticist

noun
Vorticism movement member
Pronunciation: / ˈvɔrtɪsɪst / (AmE), / ˈvɔːtɪsɪst / (BrE)
a member or supporter of the early 20th-century British art movement Vorticism, which emphasized abstract, geometric forms

Examples

A Vorticist painter
Wyndham Lewis was a prominent Vorticist who helped define the movement's style.
The exhibition featured works by several influential Vorticists from the early 1900s.
As a Vorticist, she embraced bold lines and abstract forms in her paintings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Cubist - an artist associated with Cubism, emphasizing abstract, geometric shapes
  • synonym: Futurist - a member of the Futurism art movement focused on modernity and technology
