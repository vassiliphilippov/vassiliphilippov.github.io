stare

verb
look with fixed, wide-open eyes
5.3
Pronunciation: / stɛr / (AmE), / stɛə /; / stɛː / (BrE)
to look at someone or something with a fixed, steady gaze, often for a long time or with intensity

Examples

To stare at someone
People often stare when something unusual happens in public.
She couldn't help but stare at the breathtaking view from the mountain top.
The child stared at the magician, mesmerized by the trick.
He couldn't help but stare at the unusual sculpture in the gallery.
Please stop staring; it's making everyone uncomfortable.
She stared out the window, lost in thought for several minutes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gaze - look steadily and intently
  • antonym: glance - take a brief or hurried look
  • synonym: glare - stare in an angry or fierce way
