weak force

noun
a fundamental force causing radioactive decay
4.8 6.6
Pronunciation: /wiːk fɔːrs/ (AmE), /wiːk fɔːs/ (BrE)
a fundamental force of nature responsible for certain types of radioactive decay, involving interactions between elementary particles such as neutrinos and antineutrinos

Examples

The weak force interaction
Neutrinos interact with other particles via the weak force, making them difficult to detect.
Radioactive decay occurs through the action of the weak force in atomic nuclei.
The weak force is much weaker than the strong nuclear force but still essential to particle physics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: electromagnetic force - the fundamental force responsible for electric and magnetic interactions
  • antonym: gravitational force - the force of attraction between masses
  • antonym: strong force - the fundamental force that holds atomic nuclei together
