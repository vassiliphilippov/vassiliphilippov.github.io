fakir

noun
Muslim or Hindu ascetic holy man
Pronunciation: / fəˈkɪr / (AmE), / fəˈkɪə / (BrE)
A Muslim or Hindu religious ascetic who lives by begging and is traditionally regarded as holy.

Examples

A fakir living simply
The fakir renounced material possessions and dedicated his life to spiritual practice.
The disciples gathered around the fakir to listen to his teachings and seek blessings.
Travelers sometimes give alms to a fakir seated outside the temple.
Many villagers sought advice from the local fakeer, believing he possessed spiritual wisdom.
The elderly fakeer sat cross-legged at the temple gate, blessing those who offered alms.
Travelers often described encounters with a wandering fakeer during their journeys across India.
As a faqir, he wandered from village to village, living on alms and preaching spirituality.
Hundreds of villagers visited the faqir each year, seeking blessings and advice.
The faqir led a life of poverty, devoting himself entirely to prayer and meditation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ascetic - a person who practices severe self-discipline and abstention from indulgence
  • synonym: dervish - Muslim mendicant known for asceticism, especially Sufi orders
  • synonym: fakir - Muslim or Hindu holy man; mendicant monk (alternative spelling)
  • synonym: monk - a member of a religious community of men typically living under vows of poverty, chastity, and obedience
