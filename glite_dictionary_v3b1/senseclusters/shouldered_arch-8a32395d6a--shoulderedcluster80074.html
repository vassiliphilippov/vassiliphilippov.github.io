shouldered arch

noun
arched opening with corbel-supported lintel
7.2 8.1
Pronunciation: /ˈʃoʊl.dərd ɑːrtʃ/ (AmE), /ˈʃəʊl.dəd ɑːtʃ/ (BrE)
An arch formed by a horizontal lintel resting on corbels that project inward at either side of an opening.

Examples

A shouldered arch design
Medieval builders often used the shouldered arch design in church doorways for added strength.
Preservationists carefully restored the shouldered arch above the castle window.
The entrance featured a shouldered arch supported by intricately carved stone corbels.
