speech defect

noun
disorder affecting clear speech
1.8
Pronunciation: / spiːtʃ dɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / spiːtʃ dɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
a disorder that affects a person's ability to speak clearly or correctly

Examples

A speech defect problem
A speech defect can make it difficult for others to understand what you are saying.
After his accident, he developed a slight speech defect that required years of therapy.
The therapist worked with children who had a speech defect to improve their communication skills.
