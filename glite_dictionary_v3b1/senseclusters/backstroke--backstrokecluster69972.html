backstroke

verb
swim on one's back
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌstroʊk / (AmE), / ˈbækˌstrəʊk / (BrE)
to swim on one's back using a backward arm motion and flutter kick

Examples

To backstroke a lap
During the race, he decided to backstroke the final lap to conserve his energy.
She backstroked across the pool with smooth, even strokes.
The children practiced backstroking during their swimming lesson.

Related Terms

  • synonym: breaststroke - swim with the chest forward and arms moving in a half-circle simultaneously
  • synonym: butterfly - swim using a dolphin-like kick and overhand arm movement
  • synonym: freestyle - swim using any preferred stroke, usually the front crawl
