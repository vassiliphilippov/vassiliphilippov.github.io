grapheme

noun
written symbol representing speech sound
Pronunciation: / ˈɡræˌfim /; / ˈɡræfiːm / (AmE), / ˈɡræfiːm / (BrE)
the smallest unit in a writing system, such as a letter or character, that represents a sound or meaning

Examples

A single grapheme
Children learn to associate each grapheme with its matching sound when learning to read.
In written Korean, each grapheme corresponds to a specific syllable block.
The English alphabet uses twenty-six different graphemes to represent its sounds.
In the word 'cat', each letter is a separate grapheme representing a different sound.
Some languages use one grapheme to represent more than one sound.
The Chinese writing system contains thousands of unique graphemes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: character - a symbol used in writing or printing
  • synonym: letter - a written symbol representing a speech sound
  • antonym: phoneme - the smallest unit of sound in speech
