chebab

noun
Palestinian youth involved in conflict
Pronunciation: / ʃəˈbɑːb / (AmE), / ʃəˈbɑːb / (BrE)
A Palestinian boy or youth, typically aged 10–15, who participates in political resistance against Israeli forces.

Examples

Young chebab protestors
During the demonstration, several chebab clashed with soldiers near the checkpoint.

The reporter described how a group of chebab gathered at the edge of the street, hurling stones.
