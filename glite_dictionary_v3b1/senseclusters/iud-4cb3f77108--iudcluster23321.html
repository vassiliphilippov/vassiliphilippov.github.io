IUD

noun
contraceptive device for the uterus
Pronunciation: / aɪ juː ˈdiː / (AmE), / aɪ juː ˈdiː / (BrE)
a contraceptive device, usually a small T-shaped piece of plastic or metal, inserted into the uterus to prevent pregnancy

Examples

A IUD device
After her consultation, the doctor recommended an IUD as a suitable contraceptive option.
Many women choose an IUD because it provides long-term birth control without daily attention.
The IUD needs to be inserted by a healthcare professional in a clinic.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contraceptive - a device or drug used to prevent pregnancy
