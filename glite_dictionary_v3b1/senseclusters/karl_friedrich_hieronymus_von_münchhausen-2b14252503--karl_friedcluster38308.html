Karl Friedrich Hieronymus von Münchhausen

proper noun
German nobleman known for exaggerated stories
Pronunciation: / kɑrl ˈfriːdərɪk hiˌɛrəˈnoʊməs vɑn ˈmʊnchaʊzən / (AmE), / kɑːl ˈfriːdərɪk hiːəˈrɒnɪməs fɒn ˈmʊnchaʊzən / (BrE)
(1720–1797) A German nobleman famous for telling wildly exaggerated stories about his adventures as a soldier and hunter; his name has become associated with any extravagant tale or tall story.

Examples

Karl Friedrich Hieronymus von Münchhausen stories
Historians debate how much of Karl Friedrich Hieronymus von Münchhausen's stories were based on real events.
Many consider the tales of Karl Friedrich Hieronymus von Münchhausen to be the epitome of imaginative storytelling.
The novel's protagonist was likened to Karl Friedrich Hieronymus von Münchhausen for the absurdity of his adventures.
