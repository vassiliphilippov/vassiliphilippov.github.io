southern red cedar

noun
a juniper tree found in southeastern US
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌðɚn rɛd ˈsiːdɚ / (AmE), / ˈsʌðən rɛd ˈsiːdə / (BrE)
A type of juniper tree (Juniperus silicicola) native to swampy coastal regions of the southeastern United States, similar in appearance to the eastern red cedar.

Examples

The southern red cedar tree
Carpenters sometimes use the fragrant wood of the southern red cedar for making furniture.
Many birds nest in the dense branches of the southern red cedar.
The southern red cedar thrives in the humid conditions along the Gulf Coast.
