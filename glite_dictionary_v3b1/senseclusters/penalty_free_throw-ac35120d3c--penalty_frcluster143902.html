penalty free throw

noun
free shot in basketball after a foul
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛnəlti fri ˈθroʊ / (AmE), / ˈpɛnəlti friː ˈθrəʊ / (BrE)
an unhindered shot taken from the foul line in basketball, awarded to a player as a result of a foul committed by the opposing team

Examples

A penalty free throw
After the defender's foul, she was awarded a penalty free throw.
He calmly made both of his penalty free throws in the final seconds.
Missing a penalty free throw can be costly in a close basketball game.
