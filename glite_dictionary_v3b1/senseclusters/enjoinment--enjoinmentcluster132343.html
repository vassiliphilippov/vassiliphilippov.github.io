enjoinment

noun
legal prohibition by court order
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdʒɔɪnmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒɔɪnmənt / (BrE)
(law) a judicial order that prohibits a party from carrying out or continuing a particular activity; the act of being enjoined by an injunction.

Examples

An enjoinment order
An enjoinment can halt construction projects until further legal review is completed.
Lawyers filed for an enjoinment against the use of the disputed trademark.
The court issued an enjoinment to prevent the company from discharging pollutants into the river.

Related Terms

  • synonym: injunction - a court order requiring a person to do or cease doing a specific action
