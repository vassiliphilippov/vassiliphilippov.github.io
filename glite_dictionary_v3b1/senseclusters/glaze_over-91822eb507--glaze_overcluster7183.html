glaze over

verb
eyes become shiny and unfocused
2.3 5.3
Pronunciation: / ɡleɪz ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ɡleɪz ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
(of the eyes) to become shiny, glassy, and unfocused, usually because of tiredness, boredom, or lack of attention

Examples

Eyes glaze over
After staring at the computer for hours, his eyes began to glaze over.
I could see her eyes glaze over as the meeting dragged on.
If you don't get enough sleep, your eyes might glaze over during class.
After hours of studying, I could feel my eyes start to glaze over.
His eyes would glaze over every time someone mentioned tax law.
Whenever the meeting drags on, her eyes glaze over and she stops paying attention.
