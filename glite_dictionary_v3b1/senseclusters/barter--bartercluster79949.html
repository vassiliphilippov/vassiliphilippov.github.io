barter

verb
exchange goods or services without money
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑrtɚ / (AmE), / ˈbɑːtə / (BrE)
to exchange goods or services directly for other goods or services, without using money

Examples

To barter goods
During the economic crisis, neighbors often bartered labor and products to get what they needed.
Instead of paying cash, they decided to barter their homegrown vegetables for fresh eggs.
Many indigenous communities still barter goods and services as a form of economic exchange.

Related Terms

  • antonym: purchase - to acquire something by paying money for it
  • synonym: trade - to buy and sell goods and services
