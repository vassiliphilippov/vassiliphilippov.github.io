    6.0 0.15/m
    tatty is a British informal word for shabby, worn items, while tattered specifically suggests fabric torn and aged by long use.
  • ratty
    5.4 1.00/m
    ratty feels dirty and shabby, often with small holes, while tattered stresses being old and torn from heavy use.
  • shabby
    2.5 3.04/m
    shabby looks worn and poorly cared for, often faded, while tattered implies cloth or items are ripped and badly worn.
  • worn-out
    3.1 1.46/m
    worn-out means too old or used to work well, while tattered describes things that are torn and in poor condition.
  • battered
    3.7 0.00/m
    battered shows heavy damage from rough use, like dents or scuffs, while tattered mainly means torn and worn by time.
  • beat-up
    5.2 2.19/m
    beat-up is informal for objects left damaged by rough treatment, while tattered focuses on fabric torn and worn by long use.
  • torn
    1.0 2.44/m
    torn has simple meaning of being ripped or having jagged edges, while tattered adds age, wear, and long, hard use to that ripping.
  • tatterdemalion
