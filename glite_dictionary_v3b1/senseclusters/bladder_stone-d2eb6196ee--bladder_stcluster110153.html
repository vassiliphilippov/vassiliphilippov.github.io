bladder stone

noun
hard mass formed in the bladder
3.6 4.5
Pronunciation: / ˈblædɚ stoʊn / (AmE), / ˈblædə stəʊn / (BrE)
a hard mass or calculus formed in the urinary bladder, usually as a result of mineral buildup

Examples

A large bladder stone
Over time, mineral deposits can build up and form a bladder stone in some individuals.
The doctor explained that the patient's pain was caused by a bladder stone.
Treatment for a bladder stone may require surgery if it is too large to pass naturally.
