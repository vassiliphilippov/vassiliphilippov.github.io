mumpsimus

noun
an obstinately held mistaken belief or practice
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌmpsɪməs / (AmE), / ˈmʌmpsɪməs / (BrE)
An outdated or unreasonable custom, belief, or idea that is obstinately adhered to, especially after being shown to be wrong.

Examples

Old mumpsimus belief
Despite all the evidence, he continued to defend his old mumpsimus about grammar rules.

Some traditions persist as mere mumpsimus long after their logic has faded.
