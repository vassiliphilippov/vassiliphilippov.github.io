New Netherland

proper noun
former Dutch colony in North America
Pronunciation: / nuː ˈnɛðərlænd / (AmE), / njuː ˈnɛðələnd / (BrE)
a former Dutch colony in North America along the Hudson and lower Delaware Rivers, centered in New Amsterdam, annexed by the English in 1664

Examples

New Netherland colony
New Netherland played a significant role in the early history of North America.
Many settlers in colonial America traced their roots back to New Netherland.
The English seized New Netherland from the Dutch in 1664, renaming it New York.
