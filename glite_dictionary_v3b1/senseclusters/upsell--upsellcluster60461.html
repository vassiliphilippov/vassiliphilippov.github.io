upsell

noun
additional sale
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpˌsɛl / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌsɛl / (BrE)
the act or result of persuading a customer to buy a more expensive or additional product or service

Examples

A quick upsell
The new phone case was an easy upsell for the sales associate at the electronics store.
Offering a premium subscription is a common upsell strategy for online services.
The waiter suggested dessert as an upsell after we finished our main course.
The waiter used an upsell by suggesting a premium wine with our meal.
Training staff in the art of the upsell can boost profits for the business.
She recognized the upsell when the salesperson offered an extended warranty.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cross-sell - to sell a different product or service to an existing customer
  • antonym: downsell - to persuade a customer to buy a less expensive product or service
