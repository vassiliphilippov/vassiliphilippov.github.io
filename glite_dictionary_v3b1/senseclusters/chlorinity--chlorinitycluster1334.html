chlorinity

noun
measure of chlorine/halides in water
Pronunciation: / klɔːˈrɪnəti / (AmE), / klɔːˈrɪnɪti / (BrE)
the concentration of chlorine or other halides in water, especially in seawater, usually expressed in parts per thousand

Examples

Water chlorinity level
An increase in chlorinity can affect the types of organisms that live in marine environments.
Scientists monitor the chlorinity of seawater to study changes in ocean chemistry.
The laboratory tested the chlorinity levels of various water samples collected from the bay.

Related Terms

  • synonym: salinity - the concentration of salts in water
