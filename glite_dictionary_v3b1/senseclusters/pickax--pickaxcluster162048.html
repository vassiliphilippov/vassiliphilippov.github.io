pickax

noun
heavy tool for breaking ground or rocks
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪkˌæks / (AmE), / ˈpɪkˌaks / (BrE)
a heavy iron tool with a wooden handle and a curved head that is pointed at both ends, used for breaking up hard ground or rocks

Examples

Heavy pickax tool
He swung the pickax overhead before striking the stubborn boulder.
It took several hours with a pickax to dig the trench for the foundation.
The workers used a pickax to break through the rocky soil.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pick - short for 'pickax', another name for the same tool
