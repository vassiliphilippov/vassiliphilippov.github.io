corrigendum

noun
a printer's error needing correction
Pronunciation: / ˌkɔːrɪˈdʒɛndəm /; / ˌkɔrɪˈdʒɛndəm / (AmE), / ˌkɒrɪˈdʒɛndəm / (BrE)
an error in a published work that is formally corrected, or the official notice of such a correction

Examples

A corrigendum notice
A corrigendum was printed in the following issue to clarify the dates.
Readers should consult the corrigenda for corrections to the original text.
The publisher issued a corrigendum to address a typographical mistake on page three.
The publisher issued a corrigendum to fix the data error in the original report.
A corrigendum was added to the journal to correct the author’s name.
Readers should check the corrigendum for updates to the scientific article.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amendment - a formal change or addition to a document
  • synonym: erratum - an error in printing or writing, especially one noted in a published list
