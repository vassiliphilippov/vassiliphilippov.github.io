beat

noun
sailing close to the wind direction
5.8
Pronunciation: / bit / (AmE), / biːt / (BrE)
(Nautical) The act of sailing a vessel as close as possible toward the direction from which the wind is blowing, usually by zigzagging back and forth.

Examples

A long beat at sea
During the beat, the crew had to work quickly to adjust the sails for each tack.
Sailors often spend hours on a beat when racing upwind in challenging conditions.
The yacht struggled against strong gusts on the final beat to the finish line.
