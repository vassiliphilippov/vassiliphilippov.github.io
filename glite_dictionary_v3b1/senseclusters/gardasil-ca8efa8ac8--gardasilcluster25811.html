Gardasil

proper noun
HPV vaccine brand
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑrdəsɪl / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːdəsɪl / (BrE)
a brand-name vaccine that protects against certain types of human papillomavirus (HPV), which can cause cancers and other diseases

Examples

Gardasil vaccine
Many teenagers receive the Gardasil vaccine to protect against HPV.
Doctors recommend Gardasil for both boys and girls to prevent certain types of cancer.
After getting Gardasil, she felt reassured about her future health.
Many parents choose to have their children vaccinated with Gardasil to reduce the risk of HPV-related diseases.
The doctor explained that Gardasil is most effective when given before exposure to HPV.
After receiving Gardasil, she felt reassured about her protection against certain types of cervical cancer.
