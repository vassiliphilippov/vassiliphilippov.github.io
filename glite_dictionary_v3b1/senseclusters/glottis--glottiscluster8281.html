glottis

noun
part of the larynx with the vocal cords

the part of the larynx consisting of the vocal cords and the opening between them, where sounds are produced

Examples

The glottis opening
During speech, the glottis opens and closes to control airflow.
Swelling of the glottis can cause difficulty in breathing.
The doctor used a mirror to examine her glottis for any abnormalities.

Synonyms

  • true glottis
    8.8 0.00/m
    true glottis is the space between the two true vocal folds, while glottis also names those vocal cords and their opening used to make sounds.
  • rima glottidis
    9.9 0.00/m
    rima glottidis is the opening between the vocal folds through which air passes, while glottis can also refer to the cords themselves.
  • rima vocalis
    9.9 0.00/m
    rima vocalis is the narrow space between the vocal folds used in breathing and speech, while glottis can mean those folds and the gap.
  • glottis vera
    9.9 0.00/m
    glottis vera is the anatomical space between the true vocal folds, while glottis is a broader term that includes the cords and opening used to speak.
