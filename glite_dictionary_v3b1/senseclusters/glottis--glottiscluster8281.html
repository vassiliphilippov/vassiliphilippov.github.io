glottis

noun
part of the larynx with the vocal cords
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlɑːtɪs / (AmE), / ˈɡlɒtɪs / (BrE)
the part of the larynx consisting of the vocal cords and the opening between them, where sounds are produced

Examples

The glottis opening
During speech, the glottis opens and closes to control airflow.
Swelling of the glottis can cause difficulty in breathing.
The doctor used a mirror to examine her glottis for any abnormalities.
