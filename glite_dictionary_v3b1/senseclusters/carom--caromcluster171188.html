carom

verb
to strike and rebound (as a ball)
Pronunciation: / ˈkærəm /; / ˈkær əm / (AmE), / ˈkærəm /; / ˈkær əm / (BrE)
to rebound or bounce off something after hitting it, usually at an angle

Examples

To carom off a surface
He caromed around the corner, barely missing the pedestrians.
The cue ball caromed off the cushion and struck the red ball.
The puck caromed off the post and into the goal.
During the accident, the truck caromed into a fence before coming to a stop.
The basketball caromed off the backboard and into the net.
The hockey puck caromed wildly around the rink after hitting the goalpost.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rebound - to spring back after hitting something
  • synonym: ricochet - to rebound one or more times off a surface
