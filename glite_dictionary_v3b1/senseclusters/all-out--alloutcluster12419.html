all-out

adjective
using all resources; maximum effort
Pronunciation: / ˈɔlˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˌɔːlˈaʊt / (BrE)
Using all available resources or effort; involving the maximum possible intensity or commitment.

Examples

An all-out effort
She made an all-out attempt to solve the problem before the deadline.
The company declared an all-out push to improve customer service standards.
They launched an all-out effort to win the championship this year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: full-scale - using the full amount of available resources or having the maximum possible effect
  • antonym: limited - restricted in extent, amount, or scope
