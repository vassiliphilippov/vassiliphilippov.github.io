foul

verb
become dirty or contaminated
Pronunciation: / faʊl / (AmE), / faʊl / (BrE)
to make or become dirty, polluted, or contaminated; to block or obstruct with dirt or waste

Examples

To foul a boat
Barnacles often foul the hulls of boats, causing them to slow down.
If water sits in a vessel too long, it will foul and smell bad.
The engine began to foul after years of use without maintenance.
Debris from the storm began to foul the river's narrow channels.
If too much grease gets into the sink, it can foul the drain.
The engine broke down because dirt had fouled some of the pipes.
Industrial waste fouled the river, making it unsafe for swimming.
Please avoid fouling the carpets with muddy shoes.
Years of neglect have fouled the lake beyond recognition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: block - to obstruct movement or passage through something
  • antonym: cleanse - to make clean
  • synonym: clog - to block or become blocked with an accumulation of matter
  • synonym: contaminate - to make something impure or harmful by adding dangerous substances
  • synonym: pollute - to make air, water, or soil dirty or harmful
  • synonym: soil - to make dirty
