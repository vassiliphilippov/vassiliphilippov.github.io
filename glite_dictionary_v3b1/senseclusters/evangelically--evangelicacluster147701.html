evangelically

adverb
in an evangelical way
Pronunciation: / ɪˌvæn.dʒəˈlɪk.li / (AmE), / ɪˌvæn.dʒɪˈlɪk.əl.i / (BrE)
in a manner relating to or promoting evangelical Christianity, especially with enthusiasm or zeal

Examples

Evangelically preach faith
She spoke evangelically about her beliefs, inspiring many in the audience.
The organization promotes its values evangelically, focusing on personal faith and scripture.
He argued evangelically for the importance of biblical teachings in daily life.
He spoke evangelically about his faith at the community meeting.
The group campaigned evangelically to encourage others to join their cause.
She approached her volunteer work evangelically, hoping to inspire others.

Related Terms

  • antonym: indifferently - without interest or concern
  • synonym: religiously - in a way that relates to religion or religious practices
  • antonym: secularly - in a way that is not connected with religion
  • synonym: zealously - with great energy or enthusiasm
