cryptogam

noun
plant reproducing by spores, not seeds or flowers
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪp.toʊˌɡæm / (AmE), / ˈkrɪp.təˌɡam / (BrE)
A plant or plant-like organism that reproduces by spores and does not produce flowers or seeds, such as a fern, moss, algae, or fungus.

Examples

A cryptogam plant
Ferns and mosses are classic examples of cryptogams found in moist forests.
In older biology textbooks, plants are divided into cryptogams and phanerogams based on reproductive methods.
Seaweeds belong to a group of cryptogams that thrive in marine environments.
