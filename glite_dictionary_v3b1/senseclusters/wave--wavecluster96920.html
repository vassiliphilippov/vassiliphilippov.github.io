wave

verb
move hand/arm to signal or greet
3.1 1.0
Pronunciation: /weɪv/ (AmE), /weɪv/ (BrE)
to move your hand or arm (or something) back and forth to greet, signal, or attract attention

Examples

To wave a hand
He waved his hand, signaling for us to follow.
She smiled and waved as the train left the station.
The teacher waved to get the students' attention.
He waved the paper back and forth to cool himself.
She waved her hand to get his attention.
The referee waved the flag to stop the game.
As the train departed, she waved to her family from the window.
He waved his hand to get the waiter's attention.
Please wave if you can hear me at the back of the room.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beckon - to make a gesture with the hand to signal someone to approach
  • synonym: brandish - wave or flourish something, especially a weapon, as a threat or in anger or excitement
  • synonym: greet - to give a polite word or sign of welcome or recognition to someone
  • synonym: signal - to make a movement that gives a message or information
  • synonym: wag - move or cause to move rapidly and repeatedly from side to side or up and down
