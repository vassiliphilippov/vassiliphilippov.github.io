honey mesquite

noun
thorny shrub; sweet pods; southwestern US and Mexico
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌni mɛˌskiːt / (AmE), / ˈhʌni mɛˌskiːt / (BrE)
a thorny, deep-rooted shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, producing sweet pods used as livestock feed and often forming dense thickets

Examples

Dense honey mesquite thickets
Dense thickets of honey mesquite can be challenging to clear from pastureland.
Farmers often use honey mesquite pods as a nutritious supplement for their cattle.
The honey mesquite thrives in arid regions, providing both shade and food for grazing animals.
