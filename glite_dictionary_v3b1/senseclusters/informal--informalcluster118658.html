informal

adjective
casual, not formal
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfɔrml̩ /; / ɪnˈfɔrməl / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɔːml̩ /; / ɪnˈfɔːməl / (BrE)
relaxed, casual, or not official; not following strict rules or formal procedures

Examples

The dinner was informal, so everyone wore jeans and T-shirts.
They arranged an informal meeting to discuss the project.
He prefers informal conversations to official meetings.
Her email was written in a very informal style, using everyday language.
The meeting was informal, so there was no need for suits or ties.
She prefers to keep her meetings informal and open to discussion.
The two companies reached an informal agreement over lunch.
Their informal group meets every Saturday at the park.
