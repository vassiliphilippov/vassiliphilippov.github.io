subdued

adjective
restrained in style or quality
9.8
Pronunciation: / səbˈduːd /; / səbˈdud / (AmE), / səbˈdjuːd / (BrE)
(of color, light, sound, or style) soft, muted, or not intense; gentle and restrained

Examples

Subdued colors
Her subdued manner contrasted with his energetic presentation.
The room was decorated in subdued shades of blue and gray, creating a calm atmosphere.
They spoke in subdued voices so as not to disturb the meeting.
Her dress was a subdued shade of blue, not drawing much attention.
The restaurant had subdued lighting to create a calm atmosphere.
We spoke in subdued voices so as not to disturb anyone.
A subdued light filled the room, creating a calm atmosphere.
After the announcement, laughter became subdued and conversations quieted.
She spoke in a subdued voice so as not to wake the baby.
