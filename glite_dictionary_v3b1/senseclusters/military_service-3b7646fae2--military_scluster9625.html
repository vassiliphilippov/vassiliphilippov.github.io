military service

noun
land tenure by serving in a lord's army
1.3 7.1
Pronunciation: /ˈmɪl.ɪ.trɪ ˈsɜːr.vɪs/ /; / /ˈmɪl.ə.tɪr.iː ˈsɜːr.vɪs/ /; / /ˈmɪl.ɪ.trɪ ˈsɜːr.vəs/ (AmE), /ˈmɪl.ɪ.trɪ ˈsɜː.vɪs/ /; / /ˈmɪl.ə.trɪ ˈsɜː.vɪs/ /; / /ˈmɪl.ɪ.trɪ ˈsɜː.vəs/ (BrE)
A system of land tenure in which a person held land in exchange for serving in the lord's army, especially in feudal society.

Examples

Feudal military service
Failure to provide military service could result in the loss of one’s land during the Middle Ages.
The obligation of military service shaped both the army and the social hierarchy in medieval England.
Under the feudal system, knights were often granted land in return for military service.
