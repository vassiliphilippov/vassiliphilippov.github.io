fretboard

noun
part of stringed instrument
Pronunciation: / ˈfrɛtbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈfrɛtbɔːd / (BrE)
the long, flat part of a stringed instrument where the fingers press the strings to change the pitch

Examples

The fretboard of guitar
He carefully cleaned the fretboard of his guitar after every practice session.
The violinist placed her fingers on the fretboard to play a new melody.
Learning to move quickly along the fretboard is important for advanced guitar players.

Related Terms

  • antonym: body - the main part of a stringed instrument that produces sound
  • synonym: fingerboard - the surface on a stringed instrument where the fingers press the strings
