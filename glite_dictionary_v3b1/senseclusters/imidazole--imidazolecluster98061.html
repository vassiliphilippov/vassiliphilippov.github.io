imidazole

noun
an organic heterocyclic compound
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmɪdəˌzoʊl / (AmE), / ɪˈmɪdəzəʊl / (BrE)
a colorless, crystalline organic compound (C3H4N2) with a five-membered ring containing two nitrogen atoms, used as a base and in the synthesis of various pharmaceuticals and other chemicals.

Examples

Imidazole compound
Imidazole serves as an important building block in the synthesis of antifungal medications.
Histidine contains an imidazole side chain, which is vital for enzyme activity.
The laboratory purchased high-purity imidazole for their latest biochemical experiments.
