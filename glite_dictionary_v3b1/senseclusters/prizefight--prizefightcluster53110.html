prizefight

verb
box for a prize or money
Pronunciation: / ˈpraɪzˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˈpraɪz.faɪt / (BrE)
(of two people) to box each other for a prize or money, especially in a professional contest.

Examples

To prizefight for money
He once prizefought under the name "The Tiger" for quick cash.
Many young men dreamed of making a living by prizefighting in the city's underground clubs.
The two champions agreed to prizefight in Las Vegas next month.
