stalking-horse

noun
horse used as cover by hunters
Pronunciation: / ˈstɔkɪŋ hɔrs / (AmE), / ˈstɔːkɪŋ hɔːs / (BrE)
a horse or object used by hunters to hide behind while approaching animals

Examples

Hunter's stalking-horse
He described the technique of using a stalking-horse as both clever and resourceful.
In the past, hunters would approach wildfowl behind a stalking-horse to avoid detection.
The use of a stalking-horse allowed hunters to get close enough to their prey for a clean shot.
By hiding behind a stalking-horse, he managed to get closer to the flock without alarming them.
In the old days, a stalking-horse made from canvas was a common tool among waterfowl hunters.
The hunter crept forward slowly, using his stalking-horse to remain hidden from the deer.
