literalism

noun
realistic depiction in art or literature
7.2 6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪt̬.ə.rəˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈlɪt.ər.əˌlɪz.əm / (BrE)
The doctrine or practice of representing things in art or literature in a realistic or literal manner, avoiding figurative or symbolic interpretations.

Examples

Literalism in art
His writing style is marked by literalism, as he tends to avoid metaphor or allegory.
Some critics praised the painting's literalism, while others found it unimaginative.
The novel's extreme literalism gave the story a sense of authenticity but limited its emotional depth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: realism - the technique or principle of depicting subjects as they appear in everyday life
  • antonym: symbolism - the use of symbols to represent ideas in art or literature
