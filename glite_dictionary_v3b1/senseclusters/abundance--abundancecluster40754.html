abundance

noun
ratio of an element in earth's crust (chemistry)
1.0 6.5
Pronunciation: / əˈbʌndəns / (AmE), / əˈbʌndəns / (BrE)
(science) the proportion or ratio of an element or isotope in a mixture or environment

Examples

Element abundance
Oxygen has a high abundance in the earth's crust, making it one of its most common elements.
Scientists measure the abundance of various elements to understand the composition of minerals.
The abundance of silicon in the earth's crust is much higher than that of copper.
Oxygen-16 has the highest natural abundance among oxygen isotopes.
Scientists measured the abundance of each isotope to determine the sample's origin.
The abundance of carbon-14 in a sample is used for radiocarbon dating.

Related Terms

  • synonym: concentration - the amount of a substance in a given volume or mass
  • antonym: scarcity - lack or shortage, the opposite of abundance
