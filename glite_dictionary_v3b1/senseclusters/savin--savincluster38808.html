savin

noun
a low-spreading juniper shrub
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪvɪn / (AmE), / ˈseɪvɪn / (BrE)
a small evergreen shrub or low-spreading juniper (Juniperus sabina) with scale-like leaves, native to southern Europe and western Asia

Examples

The savin shrub
Gardeners appreciate the savin for its evergreen foliage and low-growing habit.
The savin thrives best in rocky, mountainous regions with well-drained soil.
Traditional herbalists sometimes used extracts from the savin shrub, though it is now considered unsafe.
