expansionary

adjective
causing growth
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈspænʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ɪkˈspænʃənəri / (BrE)
causing or encouraging growth or an increase in size, amount, or activity, especially in an economy or business

Examples

Expansionary economic policy
The government introduced expansionary policies to encourage economic growth.
An expansionary approach was taken to increase the company's market share.
The architect proposed an expansionary design that would double the building's size.
The government introduced expansionary measures to boost the economy during the recession.
An expansionary policy can lead to increased investment and job creation.
Many economists debated the effects of the central bank's expansionary approach.

Related Terms

  • synonym: expansive - able or tending to expand or increase
  • antonym: restrictive - limiting or preventing increase or growth
  • synonym: stimulative - encouraging activity or growth, especially in economics
