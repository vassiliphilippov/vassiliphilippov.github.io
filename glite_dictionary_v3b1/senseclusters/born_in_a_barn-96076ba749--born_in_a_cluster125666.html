born in a barn

x
criticizing careless or rude behavior
Pronunciation: / bɔrn ɪn ə bɑrn / (AmE), / bɔːn ɪn ə bɑːn / (BrE)
used, usually as a rhetorical question, to criticize someone for being careless or having poor manners, especially for leaving doors open

Examples

To be born in a barn
Are you born in a barn? Shut the fridge door properly, please.
He never wipes his feet; it's like he was born in a barn.
Whenever you leave the door open, your mother asks if you were born in a barn.
