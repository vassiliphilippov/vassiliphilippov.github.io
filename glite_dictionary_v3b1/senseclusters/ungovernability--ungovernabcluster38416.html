ungovernability

noun
lack of control
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnˌɡʌvərnəˈbɪləti /; / ˌʌnˌɡʌvərnəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌʌnˌɡʌvənəˈbɪləti /; / ˌʌnˌɡʌvənəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being difficult or impossible to govern or control

Examples

The ungovernability of crowds
The ungovernability of the crowd made it impossible for the police to restore order quickly.
Some experts warn that political ungovernability can threaten the stability of a nation.
Teachers struggled with the ungovernability of the class after the new rules were introduced.
The growing ungovernability of the region made it difficult to maintain peace.
Economic crises can contribute to the ungovernability of a nation.
Leaders struggled to address the ungovernability caused by widespread protests.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anarchy - a state of society without government or law
  • antonym: order - a state in which rules and authority are respected
  • antonym: orderliness - the quality of being well organized and easy to control
  • synonym: unruliness - the quality of being difficult to control or discipline
