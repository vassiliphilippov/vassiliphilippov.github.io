wimple

noun
cloth headdress worn by medieval women
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪmpəl / (AmE), / ˈwɪmpəl / (BrE)
A headdress made of cloth, worn over the head and around the neck and ears by women, especially in medieval Europe.

Examples

A wimple headdress
In medieval Europe, the wimple was a sign of modesty among noblewomen.
She dressed as a 12th-century lady, complete with a flowing gown and a white wimple.
The nun wore a traditional wimple as part of her habit.
