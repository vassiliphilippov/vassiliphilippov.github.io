codicology

noun
study of manuscripts
Pronunciation: / ˌkoʊdɪˈkɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌkɒdɪˈkɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the study of books as physical objects, especially ancient or handwritten manuscripts

Examples

The study of codicology
She is an expert in codicology and often examines medieval manuscripts in libraries.
The field of codicology helps scholars understand how ancient books were made and used.
A course in codicology can reveal the history behind handwritten texts and their materials.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bibliography - the study or description of books and publications
  • synonym: paleography - the study of ancient handwriting and scripts
