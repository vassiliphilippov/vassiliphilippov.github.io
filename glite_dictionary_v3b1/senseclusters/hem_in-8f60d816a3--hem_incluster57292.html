hem in

verb
surround closely to restrict movement
9.4 6.7
Pronunciation: / hɛm ɪn / (AmE), / hɛm ɪn / (BrE)
to surround someone or something so closely that movement or freedom is restricted

Examples

To hem in tightly
I feel like the high walls of the city hem in its residents.
Protesters were hemmed in by police on all sides during the march.
The enemy soldiers managed to hem in the last of the defenders.
She felt hemmed in by her family's constant demands and expectations.
The sheep were hemmed in by a circle of fencing to keep them from wandering.
The tiny house was hemmed in by tall apartment buildings on all sides.

Related Terms

  • synonym: besiege - to surround a place with armed forces to capture it or force surrender
  • synonym: encircle - to form a circle around; to surround
  • synonym: enclose - to surround on all sides; to close in
  • antonym: free - to release from confinement or restriction
  • antonym: release - to allow to go free; to let go
