to find out or make sure of something with certainty, usually by investigation or effort

Examples

To ascertain the facts
It is important to ascertain the facts before drawing any conclusions.
The investigator tried to ascertain the cause of the incident before making any statements.
We need to ascertain whether the data has been corrupted during the transfer.
Before making a decision, she wanted to ascertain all the facts.
It took several hours to ascertain that the documents were genuine.
The investigator tried to ascertain the cause of the accident.
Before publishing the article, the reporter tried to ascertain all the facts from multiple sources.
The manager asked her assistant to ascertain that all the lights were switched off before leaving.
We need to ascertain whether everyone has received the updated schedule for tomorrow's meeting.

Synonyms

  • determine
    2.3 92/m
    determine means to find an exact answer by study or calculation, while ascertain means to make sure by investigation.
  • establish
    4.1 29/m
    establish means to prove a fact with evidence and reasoning, while ascertain means to find out and be sure of it.
  • verify
    3.5 21/m
    verify means to check and show that something is correct using facts, while ascertain means to find out and make sure.
  • confirm
    1.8 62/m
    confirm means to make sure something already said is true by evidence, while ascertain means to discover and be certain.
  • find out
    2.1 131/m
    find out means to learn or discover information, often by chance or effort, while ascertain means to make sure by investigation.
  • check
    1.0 161/m
    check means to examine quickly to see if something is right, while ascertain means to investigate carefully to be certain.
