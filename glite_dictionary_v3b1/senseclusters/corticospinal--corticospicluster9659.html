corticospinal

adjective
brain-spinal connection
Pronunciation: / ˌkɔrtɪkoʊˈspaɪnəl / (AmE), / ˌkɔːtɪkəʊˈspaɪnəl / (BrE)
relating to the nerve fibers that connect the cerebral cortex to the spinal cord

Examples

Corticospinal tract
Damage to the corticospinal tract can result in muscle weakness or paralysis.
Doctors studied the corticospinal pathways to understand the patient's movement problems.
The corticospinal system plays a key role in voluntary motor control.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corticobulbar - relating to nerve fibers connecting the cerebral cortex and the brainstem
