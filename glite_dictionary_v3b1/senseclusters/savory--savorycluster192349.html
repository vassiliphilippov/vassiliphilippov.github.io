savory

adjective
pleasant-tasting; appetizing
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪvəri / (AmE), / ˈseɪvəri / (BrE)
having a pleasant, appetizing, spicy, or salty taste (not sweet)

Examples

Savory snacks
The chef is famous for her savory pies and quiches.
The soup had a savory flavor that everyone enjoyed.
They served savory snacks at the reception, much to the guests’ delight.
A savory aroma filled the kitchen as the stew simmered on the stove.
She prefers savory snacks like pretzels over sweet treats.
The soup had a savory flavor that reminded me of home-cooked meals.
She prefers savoury snacks like crackers and cheese over sweet treats.
The aroma of savoury spices filled the kitchen as the stew simmered on the stove.
The chef prepared a savoury sauce that perfectly complemented the grilled chicken.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sweet - having a taste like sugar or honey
  • synonym: tasty - having a pleasant flavor
  • synonym: umami - having a rich, meaty, or savory taste
  • antonym: unsavory - not pleasant in taste or smell
