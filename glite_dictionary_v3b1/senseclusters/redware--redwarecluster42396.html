redware

noun
reddish clay pottery
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛdˌwɛr / (AmE), / ˈrɛdˌweə / (BrE)
a type of pottery made from reddish clay and fired at a low temperature, often used for household items

Examples

Redware pottery
The antique shop had a beautiful collection of redware dishes from the eighteenth century.
Early American settlers often used redware for everyday kitchenware and storage jars.
She admired the simple design and earthy color of the redware bowl on the shelf.

Related Terms

  • synonym: earthenware - pottery made from clay and fired at a low temperature
  • antonym: stoneware - pottery made from clay and fired at a higher temperature
