midgut

noun
middle digestive tract
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪdˌɡʌt / (AmE), / ˈmɪdˌɡʌt / (BrE)
the middle section of the digestive tract in an animal, insect, or embryo, located between the foregut (or stomach) and hindgut, where most absorption occurs

Examples

The midgut section
The biologist carefully dissected the insect to study its midgut under the microscope.
Nutrients are absorbed in the midgut during the digestive process of many animals.
Damage to the midgut can seriously affect an organism's ability to process food.
The midgut of the insect is responsible for absorbing most of the nutrients from its food.
During embryonic development, the midgut forms between the foregut and the hindgut.
Scientists studied how toxins affect the midgut cells in caterpillars.
The surgeon carefully examined the patient's midgut for signs of obstruction.
Certain nutrients are mainly absorbed in the midgut during digestion.
In embryology, the midgut develops into much of the small intestine.
