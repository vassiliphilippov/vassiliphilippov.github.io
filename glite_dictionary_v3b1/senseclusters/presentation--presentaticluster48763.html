presentation

noun
visual display of information
1.0 3.0
Pronunciation: /prɛ.zən.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /prɪ.zən.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /prɛ.zən.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /prɪ.zən.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act of showing, displaying, or communicating information, ideas, or proposals to an audience, often in a formal way (such as a talk, visual display, or making information public)

Examples

Business presentation
He spent hours designing a presentation for the annual conference.
Her presentation included charts and diagrams to explain the results clearly.
The team gave a presentation using colorful slides and graphics.
Each student must prepare a presentation for the upcoming science fair.
She gave a presentation on the new policy to the board of directors.
The presentation of our proposal was met with enthusiasm by the committee.
Her presentation of the trophies was well-received by the audience.
The new product presentation impressed everyone at the conference.
They set up a visual presentation to explain their research findings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: announcement - a formal public statement about a fact, occurrence, or intention
  • synonym: broadcast - the act of transmitting a program or information by radio or television
  • synonym: demonstration - an act of showing something, often to explain or prove it
  • synonym: explanation - a statement that makes something clear
  • synonym: proposal - a suggestion or plan put forward for consideration
  • synonym: submission - the act of presenting something for consideration or judgment
