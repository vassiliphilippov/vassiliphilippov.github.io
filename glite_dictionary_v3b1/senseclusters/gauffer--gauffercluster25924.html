gauffer

noun
tool for pressing pleats into fabric
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɔːfər / (AmE), / ˈɡɔːfə / (BrE)
a heated tool for pressing pleats and ridges into fabric, especially used in fine textile work

Examples

A heated gauffer
She carefully heated the gauffer before pressing it onto the delicate ruffles.
The antique linen napkin was shaped with a gauffer to create intricate patterns.
Victorian dressmakers often relied on a gauffer to add decorative ridges to collars and cuffs.
