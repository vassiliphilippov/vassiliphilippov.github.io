fantasy

noun
imaginative fiction genre
Pronunciation: / ˈfæn.tə.si / (AmE), / ˈfæn.tə.si / (BrE)
A genre of fiction characterized by the use of magic, supernatural elements, or imaginative worlds that do not exist in reality.

Examples

Fantasy novel
His first book is a classic work of fantasy filled with dragons and wizards.
She enjoys reading fantasy novels set in magical kingdoms.
The movie blended elements of fantasy and adventure to captivate audiences.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist
  • synonym: science fiction - fiction based on imagined future scientific discoveries or technological advances
