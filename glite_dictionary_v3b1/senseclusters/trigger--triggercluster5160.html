trigger

verb
pull the trigger of a gun or device
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɪɡɚ / (AmE), / ˈtrɪɡə / (BrE)
to cause something to start or happen; to set something in motion (including by pulling a trigger or starting a process)

Examples

To trigger an alarm
She accidentally triggered the alarm by pulling the safety lever.
The officer triggered the rifle during the training exercise.
You should never trigger a weapon unless you are certain it is safe.
Exposure to the chemical can trigger an allergic reaction in some people.
Her remarks triggered a heated debate among the panel members.
The recent announcement triggered widespread protests across the city.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cause - to make something happen
  • synonym: fire - to discharge a gun or other firearm
  • synonym: initiate - to start something
  • antonym: prevent - to stop something from happening
