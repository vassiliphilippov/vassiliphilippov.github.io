New Stone Age

noun
later part of the Stone Age (Neolithic)
6.1 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / nu sto än e…™d í / (AmE), / njuÀê st…ô än e…™d í / (BrE)
the later part of the Stone Age, marked by the development of agriculture and polished stone tools, beginning around 10,000 BC in the Middle East

Examples

The New Stone Age period
Artifacts from the New Stone Age include pottery, stone tools, and early agricultural implements.
The New Stone Age was a time of great change, as humans began to settle and farm the land.
The transition into the New Stone Age marked the beginning of permanent villages and domesticated animals.
