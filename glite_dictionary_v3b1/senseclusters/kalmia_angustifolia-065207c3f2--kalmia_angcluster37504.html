Kalmia angustifolia

noun
a North American dwarf shrub, also called sheep laurel
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkælmi.ə ˌæŋɡəstəˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈkælmɪə ˌæŋɡəstəˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
A North American dwarf shrub resembling mountain laurel, with narrow leaves and small red flowers; poisonous to young livestock.

Examples

Kalmia angustifolia shrub
Kalmia angustifolia is often found in boggy areas and open woods across eastern North America.
Gardeners should be cautious when planting Kalmia angustifolia in areas frequented by livestock.
The leaves and flowers of Kalmia angustifolia can be dangerous if eaten by grazing animals.
