oolong

noun
partially fermented Chinese tea
Pronunciation: / ˈuˌlɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈuːlɒŋ / (BrE)
A type of Chinese tea made from leaves that have been partially fermented before drying, producing a flavor between green and black tea.

Examples

A cup of oolong tea
I prefer oolong over green tea because of its richer taste.
Many tea connoisseurs appreciate the complex flavors of oolong.
She ordered a pot of oolong at the Chinese restaurant.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: black tea - tea made from fully fermented tea leaves
  • degree lower: green tea - tea made from unfermented tea leaves
  • synonym: tea - a drink made by infusing dried leaves in boiling water
