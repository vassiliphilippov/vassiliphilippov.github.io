aridity

noun
lack of value or interest
5.8
Pronunciation: / əˈrɪdəti / (AmE), / əˈrɪdɪti / (BrE)
the quality of yielding nothing of value; lack of interest, excitement, or meaningful content

Examples

Aridity of ideas
He was frustrated by the aridity of her responses, which offered no new ideas or insights.
Some critics have noted the aridity of the novel's dialogue, finding it uninspiring and bland.
The intellectual aridity of the debate left many participants feeling disappointed and uninterested.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barrenness - the state of being unproductive or unfruitful
  • synonym: dryness - the quality of having no moisture; literal sense of aridity
  • antonym: richness - the quality of having great value or interest
