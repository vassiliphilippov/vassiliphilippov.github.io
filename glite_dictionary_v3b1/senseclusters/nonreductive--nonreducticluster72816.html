nonreductive

adjective
not simplifying
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnrɪˈdʌktɪv / (AmE), / ˌnɒnrɪˈdʌktɪv / (BrE)
not involving or characterized by reducing something to simpler forms or explanations

Examples

Nonreductive explanation
The philosopher argued for a nonreductive explanation of human consciousness.
Her approach to literature is nonreductive, considering cultural and historical context.
A nonreductive perspective can reveal complexities that simple models might miss.

Related Terms

  • synonym: holistic - considering something as a whole rather than as separate parts
  • antonym: reductive - characterized by simplifying something to its basic parts
