midvein

noun
central vein of a leaf
5.3 6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈmɪd.veɪn/ (AmE), /ˈmɪd.veɪn/ (BrE)
the central vein running through the middle of a leaf, from which smaller veins branch off

Examples

The midvein of a leaf
Damaged midveins can affect the transport of nutrients through the leaf.
If you follow the midvein, you can see how the other veins branch out.
The midvein of the leaf is thicker than the smaller side veins.
