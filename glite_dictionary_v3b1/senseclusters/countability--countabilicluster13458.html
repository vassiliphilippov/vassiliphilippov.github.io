countability

noun
property of being countable
Pronunciation: / ˌkaʊntəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the property of a set in mathematics that allows its elements to be counted individually, either because the set is finite or can be listed in a sequence

Examples

Mathematical countability
The countability of natural numbers means they can be listed in order, one after another.
Mathematicians study countability to understand the size of different infinite sets.
The concept of countability helps distinguish between types of infinity in mathematics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cardinality - the number of elements in a set
  • antonym: uncountability - the property of a set that cannot be counted or listed in a sequence
