averagely

adverb
in an average way
Pronunciation: / ˈævərɪdʒli / (AmE), / ˈævərɪdʒli / (BrE)
in an average way; neither very good nor very bad; typical or representing the mean

Examples

Perform averagely
The students in the class performed averagely on the final exam, with most scores near the mean.
This car is averagely priced compared to others in its category.
He is averagely tall, neither particularly short nor especially tall.
The restaurant was averagely busy for a Tuesday evening.
He performed averagely on the test, scoring neither high nor low.
The weather has been averagely warm this month, with no extreme temperatures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exceptionally - in a way that is much greater or better than usual
  • synonym: moderately - to an average or medium degree
  • synonym: typically - in a way that is usual or expected
