dipsy

adjective
silly or playful
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪpsi / (AmE), / ˈdɪpsi / (BrE)
behaving in a silly, playful, or slightly crazy way

Examples

A dipsy mood
She always gets a little dipsy when she is excited about something.
The children were acting dipsy after eating too much candy at the party.
His dipsy sense of humor made everyone laugh during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: goofy - silly or playful in a harmless way
  • antonym: sensible - showing good judgment and reason
