Mentha suaveolens

noun
a species of mint with apple-scented leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛnθə swɑːviˈoʊlɛnz / (AmE), / ˈmɛnθə swɑːviˈəʊlɛnz / (BrE)
A species of mint with apple-scented leaves and stems, native to southern and western Europe and naturalized in the United States.

Examples

Mentha suaveolens plant
Mentha suaveolens is often planted in herb gardens for its distinctive apple aroma.
Due to its mild flavor, Mentha suaveolens is a popular choice for teas and salads.
Experienced gardeners recommend trimming Mentha suaveolens regularly to maintain its shape and fragrance.
