derived

adjective
formed from something else
1.0
Pronunciation: / dɪˈraɪvd / (AmE), / dɪˈraɪvd / (BrE)
Formed or developed from something else; not original.

Examples

Derived from nature
Many modern medicines are derived from natural sources such as plants.
The English word 'biology' is derived from Greek roots.
This conclusion is derived from extensive scientific research.

Related Terms

  • antonym: original - existing from the beginning; not derived
