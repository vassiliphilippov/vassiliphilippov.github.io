sidereal time

noun
time based on stars' apparent motion
9.5 8.0
Pronunciation: / saɪˈdɪriəl taɪm / (AmE), / saɪˈdɪə.ri.əl taɪm / (BrE)
time measured by the apparent daily motion of the stars, used in astronomy to keep track of the rotation of the earth relative to the stars rather than the sun

Examples

Sidereal time measurement
Astronomers use sidereal time to precisely locate celestial objects in the night sky.
The observatory set its clocks by sidereal time for more accurate tracking of planets.
Unlike solar time, sidereal time is based on the position of distant stars.
