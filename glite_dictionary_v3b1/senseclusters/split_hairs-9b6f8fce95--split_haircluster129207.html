split hairs

verb
make overly subtle distinctions
9.5
Pronunciation: / splɪt hɛrz / (AmE), / splɪt heəz / (BrE)
to make overly fine or unnecessarily subtle distinctions in reasoning or argument; to focus on trivial details

Examples

To split hairs unnecessarily
Instead of splitting hairs, let's focus on what really matters.
It's pointless to split hairs over such a minor issue.
We shouldn't split hairs about the wording when the main idea is clear.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nitpick - to look for small or unimportant errors or faults
  • synonym: quibble - to argue over unimportant details
