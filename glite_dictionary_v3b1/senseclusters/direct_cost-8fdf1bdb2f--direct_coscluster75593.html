direct cost

noun
cost directly tied to a project or product
3.4 2.8
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈrekt kɑːst/ /; / /dɪ.ˈrekt kɔːst/ /; / /də.ˈrekt kɑːst/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈrekt kɒst/ /; / /daɪ.ˈrekt kɒst/ (BrE)
A cost that can be directly attributed to a specific project, product, or cost object, such as raw materials or labor.

Examples

A direct cost item
Raw steel is a direct cost in automobile manufacturing, while electricity for the entire plant is not.
The company tracks direct costs like materials and wages for each construction project.
When budgeting, separating direct costs from overhead allows for more accurate pricing.

Related Terms
