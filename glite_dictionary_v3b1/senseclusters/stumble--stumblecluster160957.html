stumble

verb
make a mistake or error
6.8 2.6
Pronunciation: / ˈstʌmbəl / (AmE), / ˈstʌmbl / (BrE)
to make a mistake or error, especially by accident or in the course of speaking, acting, or making a decision

Examples

To stumble over words
During the presentation, she stumbled over the facts and gave out incorrect information.
He didn't want to stumble in front of the audience, so he practiced his speech carefully.
It's normal to stumble when learning a new language and make a few mistakes.

  • antonym: succeed - to achieve the desired aim or result
