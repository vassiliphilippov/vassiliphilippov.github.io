tow

noun
act of pulling something by a rope or hitch
Pronunciation: / toʊ / (AmE), / təʊ / (BrE)
an act of pulling a vehicle or object behind another, typically with a rope, chain, or tow bar

Examples

A car tow
After her car broke down, she needed a tow to the nearest repair shop.
If you park here, your vehicle may get a tow by the city.
The broken-down truck received a tow from a helpful passerby.

Related Terms

  • synonym: haul - the act of pulling or dragging something with effort or force
