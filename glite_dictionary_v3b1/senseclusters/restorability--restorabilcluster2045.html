restorability

noun
ability to be restored
Pronunciation: / rɪˌstɔːrəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / rɪˌstɔːrəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being able to be restored to a former condition

Examples

The restorability of buildings
The restorability of the historic building was a key factor in the decision to renovate.
Experts debated the restorability of the damaged artwork after the fire.
Before starting repairs, engineers evaluated the restorability of the old bridge.

Related Terms

  • antonym: irreparability - the state of being impossible to repair or restore
  • synonym: recoverability - the ability to be recovered or brought back
