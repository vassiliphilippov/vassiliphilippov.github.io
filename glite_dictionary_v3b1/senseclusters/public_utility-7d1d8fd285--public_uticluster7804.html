public utility

noun
company providing regulated public services

An organization or company that provides essential services such as water, electricity, or transportation to the public and is subject to government regulation.

Examples

A public utility company
Many public utilities are regulated to ensure fair prices and reliable services for consumers.
She filed a complaint with the public utility over frequent power outages.
The local public utility is responsible for maintaining the city's water supply.

Synonyms

  • public utility company
    2.9 0.01/m
    public utility company runs services like water and electricity for customers, while public utility is the regulated organization providing those services.
  • public-service corporation
    4.4 0.00/m
    public-service corporation often names a formal, regulated company that runs services, while public utility is the broad term for those providers.
  • utility
    3.3 18/m
    utility usually means the service like water, gas, or electricity itself, while public utility refers to the regulated organization providing them.
