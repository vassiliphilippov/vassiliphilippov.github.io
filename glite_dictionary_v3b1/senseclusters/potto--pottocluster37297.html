potto

noun
nocturnal African primate
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpɒtəʊ / (BrE)
a small, slow-moving, nocturnal mammal that lives in trees, found in Africa and tropical America

Examples

A potto animal
Researchers observed a potto moving slowly along the branches at night.
The potto spends much of its life high in the forest canopy.
Unlike most primates, the potto is known for its extremely quiet movements.
Researchers observed a potto feeding quietly on figs during the night.
The potto uses its long tail to grasp branches as it moves through the forest canopy.
Unlike monkeys, the potto is slow and deliberate in its movements.
