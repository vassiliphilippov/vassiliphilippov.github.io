solid-fuel rocket

noun
rocket using solid chemical propellant
5.3 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / Ààs…ël…™d Àåfjul Ààr…ëk…ôt / (AmE), / Ààs…íl…™d ÀåfjuÀêl Ààr…ík…™t / (BrE)
A rocket engine that burns a chemical propellant in solid form.

Examples

A solid-fuel rocket engine
After ignition, a solid-fuel rocket cannot be easily shut down or controlled.
Because of its simple design, the solid-fuel rocket is often used in missile technology.
The space agency launched a solid-fuel rocket to deliver the satellite into orbit.

Synonyms

  • solid propellant rocket
    5.7 0.00/m

  • solid-propellant motor
    6.5 0.00/m
    a solid-propellant motor is the engine that burns a solid fuel and oxidizer, while a solid-fuel rocket names the vehicle or engine.
