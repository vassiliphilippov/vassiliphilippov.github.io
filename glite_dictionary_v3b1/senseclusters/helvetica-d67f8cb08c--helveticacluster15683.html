Helvetica

proper noun
sans-serif typeface
5.3
Pronunciation: /hel.ˈve.tɪ.kə/ (AmE), /hel.ˈve.tɪ.kə/ (BrE)
a widely used sans-serif typeface created in Switzerland in 1957, known for its clean and modern appearance

Examples

Helvetica by Swiss designers
The designer chose Helvetica for the website to give it a clean and professional look.
Many public signs in Switzerland use Helvetica because it is easy to read from a distance.
Our presentation slides looked more modern after we switched the font to Helvetica.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Arial - a popular sans-serif typeface similar in appearance to Helvetica
  • antonym: Times New Roman - a widely used serif typeface, contrasting with Helvetica's sans-serif style
