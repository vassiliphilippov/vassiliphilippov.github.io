bog

verb
to get stuck in mud
Pronunciation: / bɑɡ / (AmE), / bɒɡ / (BrE)
to become stuck or make progress very difficult, especially in soft, wet ground or mud

Examples

Cars bog in mud
The car started to bog down in the muddy field after the heavy rain.
Hikers can easily bog if they stray from the main path in the wetlands.
The tractor will bog if it tries to cross that swampy area.

Related Terms

  • antonym: free - to release from being stuck or trapped
  • synonym: sink - to go down below the surface of something, especially a liquid or soft ground
