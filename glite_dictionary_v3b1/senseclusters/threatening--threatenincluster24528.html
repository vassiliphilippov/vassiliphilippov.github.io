threatening

adjective
(of sky) dark with clouds, suggesting rain
1.5
Pronunciation: / ˈθrɛtənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθrɛtənɪŋ / (BrE)
(of the sky or weather) darkened by clouds and seeming likely to produce rain or a storm.

Examples

Threatening clouds
A threatening sky hung over the stadium, and soon heavy rain began to fall.
The threatening clouds gathered overhead, making the afternoon seem much darker than usual.
We decided to postpone our hike because the sky looked particularly threatening.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ominous - giving the impression that something bad or unpleasant is going to happen
