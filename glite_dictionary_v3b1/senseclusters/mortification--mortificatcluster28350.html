mortification

noun
strong embarrassment or shame
Pronunciation: / ˌmɔrtɪfɪˈkeɪʃən /; / ˌmɔrt̬əfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɔːtɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a feeling or instance of great embarrassment, shame, or humiliation

Examples

Public mortification
She turned bright red in mortification when she realized her mistake in front of the entire class.
The letter was read aloud, much to his mortification and dismay.
To his mortification, he slipped and fell during the important presentation.
His constant teasing eventually led to her mortification in public.
She felt a wave of mortification when she realized her mistake at the meeting.
To his utter mortification, he forgot his lines in front of the whole audience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embarrassment - a feeling of self-consciousness, shame, or awkwardness
  • synonym: humiliation - the action of humiliating someone or the state of being humiliated
