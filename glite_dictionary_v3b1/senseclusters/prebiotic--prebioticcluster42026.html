prebiotic

adjective
before life existed
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːbaɪˈɑːtɪk / (AmE), / ˌpriːbaɪˈɒtɪk / (BrE)
relating to the period or conditions on Earth before the existence of living organisms, especially in reference to early chemistry or environments.

Examples

Prebiotic Earth conditions
Researchers simulate prebiotic conditions to study how the first molecules of life could have formed.
The discovery of prebiotic chemistry on other planets raises questions about the possibility of life elsewhere.
Understanding prebiotic Earth helps scientists learn about the origins of life.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abiotic - not involving or derived from living organisms
  • antonym: biotic - relating to or resulting from living organisms
