refurbish

verb
to renovate or redecorate
4.6 2.73/m
Pronunciation: / riÀàf…ùÀê.b…™ É / (AmE), / r…™Ààf…úÀê.b…™ É / (BrE)
to make (something, especially a building or furniture) clean, bright, or like new again by repairing or decorating it

Examples

To refurbish old furniture
She plans to refurbish the antique table by sanding and repainting it.
The company spent millions to refurbish its headquarters after the fire.
They decided to refurbish the old cottage before moving in.

Synonyms

  • renovate
    3.8 5.00/m
    renovate is to repair and improve a building so it works well and looks new, while refurbish focuses on cleaning and decorating.
  • furbish up
    6.2 0.00/m
    furbish up is an informal phrasal verb for restoring furniture or rooms to a better look, while refurbish is the neutral term.
  • revamp
    4.9 3.16/m
    revamp means change and update the style or layout to look modern and fresh, while refurbish stresses repair and cleaning.
  • redo
    3.4 0.58/m
    redo means do again or remodel parts of a room, often changing its design, while refurbish focuses on repair and fresh decoration.
  • rehabilitate
    4.9 0.76/m
    rehabilitate means restore something to proper working condition, often fixing structure or systems, while refurbish aims at surface repair and decoration.
  • overhaul
    4.6 2.37/m
    overhaul means examine and repair something completely and deeply, making big changes, while refurbish often means lighter repairs and cleaning.
