The quality of expressing much in few words; terseness and brevity in speech or writing.

Examples

Good concision in writing

Synonyms

  • conciseness
    5.5 0.08/m
    conciseness is the everyday word used in speech and writing, while concision sounds slightly more formal and abstract.
  • succinctness
    6.3 0.03/m
    succinctness is a neat, compact way to say ideas in few words, while concision is the general quality of brief expression.
  • brevity
    5.3 1.48/m
    brevity refers to shortness in words or time, while concision focuses on saying much clearly using few words.
  • pithiness
    7.7 0.02/m
    pithiness gives a strong, often witty short line, while concision simply means being brief and to the point.
  • terseness
    6.7 0.07/m
    terseness sounds abrupt and sometimes rude in speech, while concision is neutral brevity used for clear, polite expression.
  • laconicism
    9.6 0.00/m
    laconicism shows a sparse, often blunt style tied to a culture or person, while concision is general short, clear wording.
