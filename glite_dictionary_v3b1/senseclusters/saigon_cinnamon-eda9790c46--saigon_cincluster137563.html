Saigon cinnamon

noun
type of cinnamon tree and spice
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪˈɡɑn ˈsɪnəmən / (AmE), / ˌsaɪˈɡɒn ˈsɪnəmən / (BrE)
A tropical Southeast Asian tree (Cinnamomum loureiroi) with aromatic bark, used as a spice and in medicine.

Examples

Ground Saigon cinnamon
Ground Saigon cinnamon can be found in the spice aisle at most supermarkets.
Many chefs prefer Saigon cinnamon for its stronger, sweeter flavor in baking recipes.
The medicinal properties of Saigon cinnamon make it valuable in traditional herbal remedies.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: cinnamon - spice made from the bark of several trees in the genus Cinnamomum
