generosity

noun
the quality of being generous
2.1 7.65/m
Pronunciation: / Àåd í…õn.…ôÀàr…ëÀê.s…ô.ti / (AmE), / Àåd í…õn.…ôÀàr…ís.…™.ti / (BrE)
the quality of being kind and willing to give money, time, or help to others

Examples

Great generosity
He showed great generosity by donating a large sum to the charity.
Her generosity towards the less fortunate is widely admired.
We were touched by the generosity of our neighbors after the storm.
Her generosity toward the community inspired many others to donate.
The fund relies entirely on the generosity of local residents.
We were touched by his generosity when he offered us his home for the night.

Synonyms

  • liberality
    7.0 0.11/m
    liberality is offering money or help openly and without strict limits, while generosity is a general kindness in giving.
  • openhandedness
    7.2 0.01/m
    openhandedness means giving freely and often, with little hesitation, while generosity is the willing kindness to share time, money, or help.
  • munificence
    9.9 0.10/m
    munificence is great, sometimes impressive giving of money or gifts, while generosity is everyday kindness and willingness to help others.
  • charitableness
    5.3 0.01/m
    charitableness is giving money or aid to people in need, while generosity is a broader willingness to give time, help, or money.
  • beneficence
    8.4 0.40/m
    beneficence is doing good acts or services, often in formal settings, while generosity focuses on freely giving time, money, or help.
  • benevolence
    6.1 1.88/m
    benevolence is a kind wish or act toward others, often gentle, while generosity means actually giving money, time, or practical help.
  • kindheartedness
    3.9 0.01/m
    kindheartedness is warm sympathy and care for others, often shown in words or small acts, while generosity is ready giving.
  • bigheartedness
    4.5 0.00/m
    bigheartedness is being very warm and giving in feeling and action, often casual, while generosity covers any willing help or gift.
  • unselfishness
    3.4 0.14/m
    unselfishness is putting others' needs first and acting without thought of self, while generosity means freely giving time, help, or money.

Related Terms

  • synonym: charity - the voluntary giving of help, typically money, to those in need
  • synonym: kindness - the quality of being friendly, generous, and considerate
  • antonym: selfishness - the quality of caring only for oneself and not for others
  • antonym: stinginess - a lack of generosity; unwillingness to give or spend
