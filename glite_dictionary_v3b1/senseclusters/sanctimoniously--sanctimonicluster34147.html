sanctimoniously

adverb
in a hypocritically pious manner
Pronunciation: / ˌsæŋk.təˈmoʊ.ni.əs.li / (AmE), / ˌsaŋk.tɪˈməʊ.ni.əs.li / (BrE)
In a way that shows falsely or hypocritically pious or morally superior behavior.

Examples

To sanctimoniously lecture
He sanctimoniously lectured his colleagues about honesty while hiding his own mistakes.
She sanctimoniously insisted that everyone follow the rules, even as she broke them herself.
The official sanctimoniously claimed to care about the poor during the campaign.

Related Terms

  • antonym: humbly - in a modest or lowly manner, without self-importance
  • synonym: self-righteously - in a self-satisfied, morally superior manner
