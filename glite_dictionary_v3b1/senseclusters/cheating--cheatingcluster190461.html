cheating

noun
dishonest deception for gain
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃiːtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtʃiːtɪŋ / (BrE)
An act of dishonestly deceiving someone in order to gain an unfair advantage or personal benefit.

Examples

Exam cheating
Academic cheating is taken very seriously at the university.
The coach was fired after his cheating was discovered by the league officials.
There was evidence of cheating during the final round of the competition.

Related Terms

  • antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest
