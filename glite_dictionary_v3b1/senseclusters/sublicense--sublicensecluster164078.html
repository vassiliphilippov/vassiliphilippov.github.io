sublicense

noun
secondary legal license
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌlaɪsəns / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌlaɪsəns / (BrE)
a legal permission given by someone who holds a license, allowing another party to use some or all of those rights

Examples

A software sublicense
The company obtained a sublicense to distribute the software in several countries.
Without a proper sublicense, the third party could not legally use the patented process.
Negotiations for the sublicense took several months before both parties reached an agreement.
The publisher received a sublicense to translate the book into several languages.
Without a sublicense, the company could not legally distribute the patented technology.
They negotiated a sublicense agreement to allow another firm to use the software.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authorization - official approval or permission
  • synonym: license - official permission to do or use something
