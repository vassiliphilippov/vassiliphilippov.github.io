anemophily

noun
wind pollination
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈnɛməˌfɪli / (AmE), / əˈniːməˌfɪli / (BrE)
the process of pollination in which pollen is carried from one plant to another by the wind

Examples

Anemophily by wind
Many types of grass depend on anemophily to reproduce successfully each year.
Pine trees use anemophily, releasing large amounts of pollen into the air during spring.
Plants that rely on anemophily often have small, inconspicuous flowers.
