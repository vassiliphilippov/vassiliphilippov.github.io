note receivable

noun
written promise to receive money; promissory note asset
5.3
Pronunciation: / noʊt rɪˈsiːvəbl̩ / (AmE), / nəʊt rɪˈsiːvəbl̩ / (BrE)
A written promise to receive a specified sum of money at a certain time from another party; an asset created by a promissory note.

Examples

A note receivable asset
Auditors carefully reviewed each note receivable to ensure the terms were being met.
She expected payment on the note receivable within sixty days, as stated in the agreement.
The company recorded a note receivable on its balance sheet after lending money to a supplier.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accounts receivable - amounts owed to a business by customers for goods or services delivered
