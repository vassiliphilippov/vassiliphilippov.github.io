come from

verb
originate or be connected by origin
Pronunciation: / kʌm frəm / (AmE), / kʌm frɒm / (BrE)
to have as an origin, background, or source; to be connected by family, social, or geographical ties

Examples

Come from a family
Her love of music comes from her mother, who was a talented singer.
Many successful scientists come from very humble beginnings.
My family comes from a small village in southern France.

Related Terms

  • synonym: descend from - to be related to someone who lived in the past; to originate from
  • synonym: originate - to have a specified beginning or origin
