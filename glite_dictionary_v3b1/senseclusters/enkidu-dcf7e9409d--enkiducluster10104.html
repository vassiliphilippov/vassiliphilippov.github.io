Enkidu

proper noun
legendary friend of Gilgamesh in Mesopotamian myth
Pronunciation: / ɛnˈkiːdu / (AmE), / ɛnˈkiːduː / (BrE)
a legendary figure in Mesopotamian mythology and the close friend and companion of Gilgamesh; featured prominently in the Epic of Gilgamesh

Examples

Enkidu in mythology
In the ancient tale, Enkidu is created by the gods to be a companion for Gilgamesh.
Scholars often analyze the friendship between Enkidu and Gilgamesh as one of the earliest literary examples of camaraderie.
The tragic fate of Enkidu deeply affects Gilgamesh and motivates his quest for immortality.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Gilgamesh - legendary king of Uruk and main character in the Epic of Gilgamesh
