plate

noun
flat body structure or anatomical part
5.0 2.9
Pronunciation: / pleɪt / (AmE), / pleɪt / (BrE)
a flat, thin sheet or layer of material, or a flat part or structure (such as in metal, glass, wood, anatomy, geology, construction, photography, or illustration)

Examples

Metal plate
Some fish have rows of plates along their bodies instead of scales.
The sternum is a flat plate of bone located in the center of the chest.
The turtle's shell is made up of several bony plates that provide protection.
Early photographers used a plate coated with chemicals to capture images.
She stored each glass plate in a protective envelope after developing the photos.
The museum has a rare plate from the nineteenth century showing the city skyline.
Earthquakes often occur where one plate meets another at a boundary.
Scientists measure how quickly each plate shifts using GPS technology.
The Pacific plate is the largest on Earth and is constantly moving.

Related Terms

  • synonym: armor - protective covering made to prevent damage from being inflicted
  • synonym: illustration - a picture or diagram that helps explain or decorate a text
  • synonym: sheet - a broad, flat piece of material, especially one made of paper, metal, or cloth
  • synonym: shield - a broad piece of metal or other material carried as protection against blows or missiles
  • synonym: tectonic plate - a large, movable segment of the Earth's lithosphere
