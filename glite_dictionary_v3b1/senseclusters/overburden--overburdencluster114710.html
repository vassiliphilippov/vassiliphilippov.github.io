overburden

verb
give too much work or responsibility
1.0 5.9
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈbɝːdən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈbɜːdn /; / ˌəʊvəˈbɜːdən / (BrE)
to give someone or something too much work, responsibility, or pressure; to overload

Examples

To overburden a worker
She felt overburdened by the number of projects assigned to her at once.
The company often overburdens its employees with tight deadlines and extra shifts.
We must ensure we do not overburden the healthcare system during flu season.
Don't overburden your employees with unnecessary paperwork and long meetings.
She felt overburdened by her many responsibilities at home and work.
The old truck was overburdened with stones and broke down on the way.

Related Terms

  • synonym: burden - to load with a heavy load or responsibility
  • synonym: overload - to load with too great a burden or cargo
  • antonym: relieve - to remove or reduce a burden or responsibility
