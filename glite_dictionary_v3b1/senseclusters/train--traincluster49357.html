train

verb
exercise or prepare for a competition
1.0 21/m
Pronunciation: / treÉªn / (AmE), / treÉªn / (BrE)
to exercise or prepare physically or mentally, especially for a sport, competition, or skill

Examples

To train for a race
He is training for his first triathlon this summer.
She trains every morning to prepare for the marathon next month.
The team trained intensively before the championship game.
If you train regularly, you'll gradually notice improvements in your performance.
Most athletes train intensively during the weeks leading up to a big competition.
She decided to train every morning before work to prepare for the race.

Synonyms

  • practice
    1.6 37/m
    practice involves repeating an activity to improve skill, while train covers practice plus planning, fitness, and formal coaching.
  • exercise
    1.4 2.82/m
    exercise is physical activity done to build fitness or skill, while train also includes skill planning and mental preparation.
  • coach
    2.0 12/m
    coach means teaching and guiding a person or team directly, while train can mean both to teach and to practice oneself.
  • prepare
    1.3 91/m
    prepare means get someone ready in many ways, while train focuses on active practice and skill development for performance.
  • work out
    2.0 6.71/m
    work out is exercising the body to improve fitness, while train covers that plus skill practice, tactics, and mental work.
