iambic

adjective
relating to iambs in poetry
Pronunciation: / aɪˈæmbɪk / (AmE), / aɪˈæmbɪk / (BrE)
Of, relating to, or consisting of iambs (metrical feet with an unstressed syllable followed by a stressed syllable), especially in poetry.

Examples

Iambic meter
Many English nursery rhymes use iambic rhythms for their sing-song quality.
Shakespeare often wrote his sonnets in iambic pentameter.
She analyzed the iambic structure of each line in the poem.

Related Terms

  • antonym: trochaic - relating to trochees (metrical feet with a stressed syllable followed by an unstressed syllable)
