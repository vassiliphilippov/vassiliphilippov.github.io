cadenza

noun
solo passage near the end of a musical piece
9.2
Pronunciation: / kəˈdɛnzə / (AmE), / kəˈdɛnzə / (BrE)
a virtuosic solo passage, typically improvised or written by the performer, occurring near the end of a piece of music, especially in concerto movements

Examples

A cadenza solo
In the concerto's first movement, the violinist played an elaborate cadenza before the orchestra rejoined.
The composer left room for a cadenza to showcase the soloist's improvisational skills.
The pianist delivered a stunning cadenza that captivated the audience.
