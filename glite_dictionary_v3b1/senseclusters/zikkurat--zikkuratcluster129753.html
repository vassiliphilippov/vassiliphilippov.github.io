zikkurat

noun
ancient Assyrian/Babylonian temple mound
Pronunciation: / ˈzɪk.ʊˌræt / (AmE), / ˈzɪk.ʊ.ræt / (BrE)
a rectangular, terraced temple mound built by the ancient Assyrians and Babylonians, typically with successively receding levels.

Examples

An ancient zikkurat temple
A zikkurat was often the center of religious life in Mesopotamian cities.
Archaeologists uncovered inscriptions on the walls of a zikkurat in southern Iraq.
The ancient city ruins included a massive zikkurat rising above the surrounding landscape.
