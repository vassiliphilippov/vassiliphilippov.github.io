taken for granted

adjective
accepted without question or proof
2.2
Pronunciation: / ˈteɪkən fər ˈɡræn.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈteɪkən fə ˈɡrɑːn.tɪd / (BrE)
considered as true or certain without questioning, proof, or further thought

Examples

Taken for granted kindness
His kindness is often taken for granted by those around him.
She always felt that her hard work was taken for granted by her colleagues.
We shouldn't take our health for granted, as it can change overnight.
