advance

verb
help progress or development
2.6
Pronunciation: / ədˈvæns / (AmE), / ədˈvɑːns / (BrE)
to move forward, make progress, or help something develop or improve

Examples

To advance a cause
She worked tirelessly to advance the cause of equal rights.
The company is investing more to advance its green technologies.
This initiative will advance scientific research in the field.
Her ideas helped advance the understanding of climate change impacts.
New medical discoveries continue to advance the treatment of rare diseases.
The company hopes to advance its research into renewable energy technologies this year.
She hopes her chess skills will help her advance past the qualifying rounds.
Thanks to their decisive win, the team was able to advance to the top of the league standings.
With every correct answer, the contestant managed to advance on the scoreboard.

Related Terms

  • antonym: demote - to reduce someone to a lower position or rank
  • antonym: fall behind - to lose position or lag in a competition
  • antonym: hinder - to make it difficult for something to develop or succeed
  • synonym: improve - to make or become better
  • synonym: move - to change position or cause to change position
  • synonym: progress - to move forward or develop towards a better state
  • synonym: promote - to further the progress of something; support or encourage
  • synonym: pull ahead - to move in front in a competition
  • antonym: retreat - to move back or withdraw to a previous position
  • antonym: stagnate - to stop developing or making progress
