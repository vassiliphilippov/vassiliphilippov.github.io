principal

noun
main party in financial transaction
4.1 3.0
Pronunciation: /ˈprɪn.sə.pəl/ /; / /ˈprɪn.sɪ.pəl/ (AmE), /ˈprɪn.sə.pəl/ /; / /ˈprɪn.sɪ.pəl/ (BrE)
a person who is the main or most important participant in an activity or organization (such as a school, a play, a crime, or a financial transaction)

Examples

School principal
As a principal, the firm is trading securities for its own portfolio rather than on behalf of clients.
The broker acted as a principal in the stock transaction, assuming full risk and reward.
When buying equities, an individual investor can be classified as either an agent or a principal.
Only the principals are required at today's dress rehearsal; extras can arrive later.
She started her career as an extra and eventually became a principal in several major films.
The casting director was thrilled when she secured a principal for the new Broadway show.
Both the principal and the accessory faced criminal charges after the theft.
The court found that she was a principal in the burglary, not merely an accomplice.
Under criminal law, a principal is considered equally responsible for the offense regardless of profit.
