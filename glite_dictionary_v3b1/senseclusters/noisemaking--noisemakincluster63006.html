noisemaking

noun
making loud sounds
Pronunciation: / ˈnɔɪzˌmeɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnɔɪzˌmeɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of producing loud or disruptive sounds, especially intentionally

Examples

Children's noisemaking
The children were scolded for their constant noisemaking during class.
At midnight, the crowd's noisemaking could be heard throughout the neighborhood.
Some animals use noisemaking as a way to attract attention or warn others.

Related Terms

  • synonym: commotion - a state of noisy confusion or disturbance
  • antonym: silence - the absence of sound or noise
