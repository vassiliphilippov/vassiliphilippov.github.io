a long time in politics

noun
time in which politics can change quickly
Pronunciation: / ə lɔŋ taɪm ɪn pəˈlɪtɪks / (AmE), / ə lɒŋ tɑɪm ɪn pəˈlɪtɪks / (BrE)
a phrase indicating that political circumstances can change rapidly in a short period, making predictions uncertain

Examples

A long time in politics
As the election proved, a week can be a long time in politics.
He cautioned that a long time in politics can change fortunes overnight.
Political analysts often remind us that even a day is a long time in politics.
