unwaivable

adjective
cannot be given up
9.3
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈweɪvəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈweɪvəbəl / (BrE)
not able to be given up, relinquished, or set aside, especially by choice or agreement

Examples

An unwaivable right
Some legal rights are unwaivable and cannot be surrendered by the individual.
The contract included an unwaivable clause to protect both parties.
Access to clean water is considered an unwaivable human right in many countries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inalienable - impossible to take away or give up
  • antonym: waivable - able to be given up or set aside by choice
