poison ivy

noun
a rash-causing North American plant
3.6 3.4
Pronunciation: /ˈpɔɪ.zən ˈaɪ.viː/ (AmE), /ˈpɔɪ.zən ˈaɪ.viː/ (BrE)
a climbing or trailing plant common in eastern and central North America, with three-part leaves and white berries, that produces an oil causing skin irritation and rash when touched

Examples

Poison ivy plant
After gardening near the woods, she discovered a poison ivy rash on her arm.
Be careful when hiking, as poison ivy can cause a painful rash if you touch it.
Many children learn to recognize poison ivy by its group of three leaflets.
