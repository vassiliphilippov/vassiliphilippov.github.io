writ large

adjective
more obvious or prominent
6.2
Pronunciation: / rɪt lɑrdʒ / (AmE), / rɪt lɑːdʒ / (BrE)
very obvious, prominent, or clearly shown; made plain or easy to see

Examples

Personality writ large
The artist's bold style is his personality writ large on canvas.
The country's problems are its past mistakes writ large in the present.
The tension in the room was writ large on everyone's face.
Her concern for her students was writ large in every lesson she taught.
The company's priorities are writ large in its new advertising campaign.
The effects of climate change are writ large in the rising sea levels.
