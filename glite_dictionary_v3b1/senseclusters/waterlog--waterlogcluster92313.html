waterlog

noun
flooded ground
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtərˌlɔɡ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəlɒɡ / (BrE)
an area of ground that is flooded or saturated with water, making it soft and difficult to cross

Examples

A muddy waterlog
After the storm, the field turned into a waterlog that was impossible to walk across.
The construction was delayed because the site became a waterlog after days of rain.
Farmers avoided the waterlog at the edge of the pasture during the wet season.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bog - wet, muddy ground too soft to support a heavy body
  • antonym: desert - a dry, barren area of land with little rainfall
  • synonym: swamp - an area of low, wet, spongy land
