rouge

verb
to corrode metal
Pronunciation: / ruːʒ / (AmE), / ruːʒ / (BrE)
to form or cause a red or brown oxide film or discoloration on metal surfaces, especially stainless steel, due to corrosion or chemical reaction

Examples

Metal rouges over time
Engineers discovered that the pipes had begun to rouge after years of exposure to high temperatures.
If stainless steel equipment starts rouging, it may indicate a problem with the water chemistry.
The maintenance team worked to remove the rouged layer from the metal surfaces before further damage occurred.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corrode - to gradually destroy or damage metal by chemical action
  • synonym: rust - to form a reddish-brown coating on iron or steel due to oxidation
