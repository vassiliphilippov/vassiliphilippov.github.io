Solferino

proper noun
site of a key 1859 battle in northern Italy
Pronunciation: / ˌsoʊlfəˈriːnoʊ / (AmE), / ˌsɒlfəˈriːnəʊ / (BrE)
the site of a significant but indecisive battle in 1859 between French and Sardinian forces under Napoleon III and the Austrians under Francis Joseph I

Examples

Battle of Solferino
Many historians study Solferino for its impact on humanitarian efforts after the war.
The Battle of Solferino was a turning point in the Italian Wars of Independence.
The aftermath of Solferino led to the founding of the International Red Cross.
