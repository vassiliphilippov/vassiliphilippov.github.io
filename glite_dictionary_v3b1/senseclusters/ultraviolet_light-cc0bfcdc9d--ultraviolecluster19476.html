electromagnetic radiation with a wavelength shorter than visible light but longer than X-rays, often responsible for causing sunburn

Examples

Ultraviolet light rays
Insects like bees can see ultraviolet light that is invisible to humans.
Special lamps emit ultraviolet light to sterilize medical equipment.
Sunscreen helps protect your skin from the harmful effects of ultraviolet light.

Synonyms

  • ultraviolet radiation
    4.1 0.68/m
    ultraviolet radiation is the formal scientific name for rays that cause sunburn, while ultraviolet light is the common everyday term.
  • ultraviolet
    5.1 1.55/m
    ultraviolet is the general name for short-wavelength rays invisible to the eye, while ultraviolet light highlights them as a type of light.
  • UV
    4.2 0.03/m
    UV is the short label seen on sunscreen bottles and weather reports, while ultraviolet light is the full descriptive term.
  • ultraviolet illumination
    6.5 0.01/m
    ultraviolet illumination describes using UV lamps to light or test objects, while ultraviolet light refers to the radiation itself.
