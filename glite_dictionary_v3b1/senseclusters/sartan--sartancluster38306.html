sartan

noun
antihypertensive drug suffix
8.2
Pronunciation: /ˈsɑːr.tən/ /; / /ˈsɑːr.tæn/ (AmE), /ˈsɑː.tən/ /; / /ˈsɑː.tæn/ (BrE)
a suffix used in the generic names of drugs that are angiotensin II receptor antagonists, commonly used to treat high blood pressure

Examples

Sartan ending
Drugs ending with sartan, such as losartan and valsartan, are prescribed to manage high blood pressure.
Your doctor may recommend a medication with the sartan suffix if you have hypertension.
Many patients tolerate sartan drugs better than other blood pressure medications.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ACE inhibitor - a drug that lowers blood pressure by inhibiting the angiotensin-converting enzyme
  • synonym: angiotensin receptor blocker - a drug that blocks angiotensin II receptors to lower blood pressure
