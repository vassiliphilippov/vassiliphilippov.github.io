swallow up

verb
enclose or envelop completely
4.6 1.53/m
Pronunciation: / Ààsw…ëÀêlo ä  åp / (AmE), / Ààsw…íl…ô ä  åp / (BrE)
To enclose or envelop completely, often so that something disappears or is no longer visible.

Examples

To swallow up money
Rising rents have swallowed up most of their monthly income.
The dark clouds swallowed up the sun, casting the town into shadow.
Within minutes, the dense fog swallowed up the entire coastline.

Synonyms

  • swallow
    4.8 8.38/m
    swallow means to take something inside so it disappears, while swallow up often stresses total enclosure and disappearance.
  • engulf
    5.1 3.01/m
    engulf covers or surrounds suddenly and forcefully, while swallow up often appears in everyday speech for total covering.
  • eat up
    2.8 0.21/m
    eat up is often literal or informal for using or destroying something, while swallow up means fully enclose or hide.
  • immerse
    4.9 0.39/m
    immerse means to put or cover deeply, often in liquid or work, while swallow up focuses on enclosing until hidden.
  • submerge
    4.6 1.91/m
    submerge means to put entirely under a surface, usually water, while swallow up can be used more broadly beyond liquids.

Related Terms

  • synonym: consume - to completely destroy or use up
  • synonym: engulf - to sweep over and cover completely
  • antonym: release - to set free or let go (opposite of enclosing or enveloping)
