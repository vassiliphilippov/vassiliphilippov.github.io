sporangiophore

noun
stalk that bears sporangia
Pronunciation: / spəˈrændʒiəˌfɔr / (AmE), / spɔːˈrændʒiəˌfɔː / (BrE)
a specialized stalk or hyphal branch that bears one or more sporangia, commonly found in fungi and some plants

Examples

The sporangiophore stalk
In some species, the length of the sporangiophore helps in effective spore distribution.
Rhizopus fungi produce a tall sporangiophore to release their spores into the air.
The sporangiophore carries sporangia at its tip for spore dispersal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sporangium - the structure where spores are produced or stored
