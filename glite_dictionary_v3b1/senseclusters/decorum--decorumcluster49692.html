decorum

noun
proper and polite behavior
5.7
Pronunciation: / dɪˈkɔrəm / (AmE), / dɪˈkɔːrəm / (BrE)
behavior in keeping with good taste and propriety; proper and polite conduct.

Examples

Proper decorum
Despite the chaos outside, she managed to keep her decorum throughout the ceremony.
He was praised for maintaining decorum during the heated debate.
The teacher reminded the students about the importance of decorum in the classroom.

Related Terms

  • antonym: impropriety - failure to observe standards of proper behavior
  • synonym: politeness - the quality of being polite and showing good manners
