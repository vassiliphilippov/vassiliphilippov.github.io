trebly

adverb
with more treble
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɛbli / (AmE), / ˈtrɛbli / (BrE)
in a way that emphasizes or increases high-pitched tones in sound

Examples

Trebly sharp sound
The old radio made the singer's voice sound unusually trebly and sharp.
After adjusting the equalizer, the music became much more trebly than before.
Some people prefer a trebly sound for listening to classical music.
