Examples

Synonyms

  • complete
    1.0 39/m
    complete means having all necessary parts or steps, while comprehensive focuses on covering many aspects or the whole subject.
  • exhaustive
    6.6 3.31/m
    exhaustive describes including every possible item or case, while comprehensive describes covering many aspects of a whole topic.
  • all-encompassing
    6.0 1.31/m
    all-encompassing covers everything or everyone in scope, while comprehensive usually refers to a complete but more focused treatment.
  • encyclopedic
    7.2 0.83/m
    encyclopedic suggests a very wide, book-like range of facts, while comprehensive means complete across most parts of a subject.
  • thorough
    4.6 14/m
    thorough means done with careful detail and attention, while comprehensive means covering all or nearly all parts of a subject.

Related Terms

  • synonym: extensive - covering a large area or scope
  • antonym: narrow - limited in scope or extent
  • antonym: partial - existing only in part; not complete
  • synonym: thorough - paying great attention to detail, careful and complete
