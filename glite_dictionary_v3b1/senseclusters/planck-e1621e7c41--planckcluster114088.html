Planck

proper noun
German physicist
Pronunciation: / plæŋk / (AmE), / plaŋk / (BrE)
Max Planck (1858–1947), a German physicist who founded quantum theory and won the 1918 Nobel Prize in Physics.

Examples

Planck won the Nobel
The Nobel Prize in Physics was awarded to Planck in 1918 for his work on quantum theory.
Many scientific constants, such as the Planck constant, are named after Planck.
Students studied Planck's contributions to understand the origins of quantum mechanics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Einstein - Albert Einstein, a German-born theoretical physicist known for the theory of relativity
