forestation

noun
planting trees
Pronunciation: / ˌfɔːrɪˈsteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɒrɪˈsteɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of planting trees or making an area into a forest

Examples

Tree forestation program
The government launched a forestation program to combat soil erosion in rural areas.
Local communities participated in forestation efforts to improve air quality and biodiversity.
Successful forestation can transform barren land into a thriving ecosystem over time.
Government policies have encouraged forestation to combat soil erosion in rural areas.
The forestation of the region has improved air quality and provided new habitats for wildlife.
Rapid forestation efforts have transformed the landscape over the past decade.

Related Terms

  • synonym: afforestation - the process of planting trees in an area where there was no previous forest
  • antonym: deforestation - the removal of trees from a forested area
  • synonym: reforestation - the process of replanting trees in an area where a forest was cut down
