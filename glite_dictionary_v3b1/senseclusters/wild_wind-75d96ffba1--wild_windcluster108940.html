wild wind

noun
sudden powerful force
4.4
Pronunciation: / waɪld wɪnd / (AmE), / waɪld wɪnd / (BrE)
a powerful or sudden force or influence, especially one that brings rapid change or excitement

Examples

Wild wind of change
A wild wind of excitement swept through the city after the announcement.
The new manager brought a wild wind of change to the company.
After the victory, a wild wind of celebration filled the streets.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - a state of peace or lack of disturbance
  • synonym: whirlwind - a situation or event marked by rapid movement or change
