preinstall

verb
to install in advance
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːɪnˈstɔl / (AmE), / ˌpriːɪnˈstɔːl / (BrE)
to install software or features on a device before it is delivered to or used by the end user

Examples

To preinstall software
Many computers come with antivirus software preinstalled for added security.
The company decided to preinstall their app on all new devices.
Some users prefer to remove programs that are preinstalled by the manufacturer.
Most new laptops preinstall antivirus programs to protect users from the start.
The phone comes with several apps preinstalled by the manufacturer.
Some computers preinstall trial versions of office software for customers.

  • antonym: uninstall - to remove software or features from a device
