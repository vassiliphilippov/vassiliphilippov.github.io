Gauri

proper noun
Hindu goddess of purity and prosperity
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡaʊri / (AmE), / ˈɡaʊəri / (BrE)
In Hinduism, a goddess associated with purity, prosperity, and benevolence; a form of Devi often linked to Parvati.

Examples

Gauri goddess
According to legend, Gauri is a gentle and benevolent aspect of the goddess Parvati.
Many devotees worship Gauri during the spring festival to seek her blessings for prosperity.
The statue in the temple represents Gauri in a standing posture adorned with jewels.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: Devi - General term for goddess in Hinduism, of whom Gauri is an aspect
  • synonym: Parvati - Hindu goddess; consort of Shiva, often identified with Gauri
