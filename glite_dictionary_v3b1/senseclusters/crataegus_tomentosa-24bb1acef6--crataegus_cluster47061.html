Crataegus tomentosa

noun
species of nearly thornless American hawthorn
9.9
Pronunciation: / krəˈtiːɡəs tɑːmənˈtoʊsə / (AmE), / krəˈtiːɡəs tɒmɛnˈtəʊsə / (BrE)
A species of American hawthorn that is erect, nearly thornless, and has somewhat pear-shaped berries.

Examples

Crataegus tomentosa berries
Botanists admire the Crataegus tomentosa for its almost thornless branches.
Many native birds feed on the berries of the Crataegus tomentosa during the winter months.
The Crataegus tomentosa in our garden produces bright red, pear-shaped berries every autumn.
