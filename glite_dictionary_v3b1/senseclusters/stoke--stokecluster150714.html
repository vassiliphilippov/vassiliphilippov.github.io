stoke

verb
to intensify feelings
4.6
Pronunciation: / stoʊk / (AmE), / stəʊk / (BrE)
to encourage, increase, or intensify strong feelings, interest, or activity

Examples

To stoke excitement
The coach's speech stoked the team's determination before the final match.
Rumors about the new policy stoked anxiety among the employees.
The festival stokes excitement in the community every summer.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dampen - to make something less strong or intense
  • synonym: fuel - to supply or stimulate with something that increases intensity
