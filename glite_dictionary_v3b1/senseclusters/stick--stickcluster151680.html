stick

verb
fasten by inserting into something
1.0 29/m
Pronunciation: / stÉªk / (AmE), / stÉªk / (BrE)
to attach, fasten, or adhere something to a surface or object, or to insert or pierce with a pointed object

Examples

To stick a label
He stuck the end of the rope into the crack to hold it in place.
Please stick this pin into the board to secure your report.
She tried to stick the note onto the fridge with a magnet.
Be sure to press down so the bandage will stick properly.
If you don't clean the surface, the tape may not stick well.
The label wouldn't stick to the wet bottle.
Be careful not to stick yourself with that pin.
He quickly stuck the needle into the fabric to start sewing.
She stuck the piece of meat with a fork before eating it.

Synonyms

  • attach
    2.5 39/m
    attach means to join or fasten one thing to another, while stick can also mean to press or make something stay on a surface.
  • adhere
    5.2 2.26/m
    adhere means to stick firmly to a surface, while stick can also mean to attach or push into something.
  • bond
    3.2 1.93/m
    bond joins two things strongly, often by glue or chemistry, while stick is more general and may be weaker or casual.
  • affix
    5.0 2.32/m
    affix is a formal verb for attaching items like labels or stamps, while stick is everyday and more physical.
  • fasten
    1.6 6.55/m
    fasten means to secure something firmly so it does not move, while stick can mean simply press or join to a surface.
  • fix
    1.0 17/m
    fix means to fasten or make stable, and it also means to repair, while stick focuses on holding or piercing.
  • bind
    3.9 10/m
    bind means to tie or hold tightly often with rope or bands, while stick usually means attach by pressure or adhesive.
  • prick
    3.9 1.01/m
    prick means to make a small hole with a sharp point, while stick can mean to pierce but also to attach or fasten.
