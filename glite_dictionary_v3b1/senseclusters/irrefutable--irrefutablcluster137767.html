Synonyms

  • irrebuttable
    9.9 0.00/m
    irrebuttable is a legal term saying evidence cannot be refuted in court, while irrefutable is used more generally.
  • incontrovertible
    8.2 0.8/m
    incontrovertible describes facts so clear they leave no doubt, while irrefutable means they cannot be denied or disproved.
  • indisputable
    5.7 2/m
    indisputable means clearly true and accepted by most people, while irrefutable stresses that nothing can disprove it.
  • inarguable
    6.7 0.2/m
    inarguable says a point does not allow argument in debate, while irrefutable means evidence cannot be disproved.
  • undeniable
    4.2 4/m
    undeniable feels obvious and clear to most listeners, while irrefutable means no evidence can prove it false.
  • indubitable
    8.7 0.1/m
    indubitable means you cannot doubt its truth in feeling or thought, while irrefutable focuses on being impossible to disprove.
  • unquestionable
    4.1 0.6/m
    unquestionable is accepted without question by people or records, while irrefutable means no one can logically disprove it.
  • incontestable
    7.5 0.2/m
    incontestable is a formal way to say a claim cannot be argued, while irrefutable stresses it cannot be disproved by evidence.
  • unchallengeable
    7.2 0.1/m
    unchallengeable means no one can contest a position easily, while irrefutable means evidence or logic cannot prove it false.
  • unassailable
    8.0 0.9/m
