conflict

noun
literary opposition driving a plot
3.0 20/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ën.fl…™kt /; / Ààk…ëÀên.fl…™kt / (AmE), / Ààk…ín.fl…™kt / (BrE)
a struggle or opposition between different feelings, desires, or attitudes within a person (internal conflict)

Examples

Inner conflict
Internal conflict within a character can be as compelling as external struggles in a novel.
Many classic tragedies are built around the central conflict of opposing values or desires.
The conflict between the protagonist and antagonist propels the story forward.
His conflict over whether to accept the job offer left him feeling anxious and restless all week.
She experienced intense conflict when choosing between family expectations and her own dreams.
Therapy helped her resolve the emotional conflict that had been troubling her for years.
