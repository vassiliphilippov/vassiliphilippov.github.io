doat

verb
to love excessively
9.9
Pronunciation: / doʊt / (AmE), / dəʊt / (BrE)
to love or show excessive fondness for someone or something, often in an old-fashioned or foolish way

Examples

To doat on children
In her old age, she would doat on her cats, treating them like royalty.
He used to doat upon his garden, spending hours tending every flower.
The old man would doat over his grandchildren, telling stories and giving them sweets.
The elderly woman would doat on her cat, treating it like her own child.
He used to doat on his grandchildren, always bringing them little gifts.
In her letters, she would doat on her husband with affectionate words.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dote - to show excessive love or fondness
  • antonym: neglect - to fail to care for or give attention to
