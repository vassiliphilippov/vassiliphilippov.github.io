ulterior

adjective
beyond what is immediate or obvious
2.5 6.1
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈtɪr.iː.ər/ /; / /ʌl.ˈtɪr.iː.ər/ (AmE), /ʌl.ˈtɪə.riː.ə/ /; / /ə.ˈtɪə.riː.ə/ (BrE)
existing beyond what is obvious or stated, especially a hidden or secret reason or motive

Examples

Ulterior motive
His reasons for investing in the project are ulterior and not immediately clear to everyone.

The debate strayed into issues ulterior to the original topic.

She assured us there was no ulterior agenda behind her proposal.
The company's generosity seemed suspicious, leading some to wonder about its ulterior intentions.
