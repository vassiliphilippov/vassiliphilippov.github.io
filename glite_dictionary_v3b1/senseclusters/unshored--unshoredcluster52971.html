unshored

adjective
not at shore
9.9
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈʃɔrd / (AmE), / ʌnˈʃɔːd / (BrE)
not brought to or lying at the shore; remaining afloat or away from land, especially at sea.

Examples

Unshored raft at sea
The small raft remained unshored after the storm, drifting far from the coastline.
Rescuers searched for the unshored vessel that had not returned to port.
After losing its anchor, the boat was left unshored and vulnerable to the open sea.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adrift - floating without being anchored or moored
  • antonym: ashore - on or to the shore
