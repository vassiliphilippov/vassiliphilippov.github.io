tonne-kilometer

noun
freight transport unit (1 tonne over 1 km)
Pronunciation: / ˈtʌn kɪˈlɑmətər /; / tʌn kɪˈlɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / ˈtʌn kɪˈlɒmɪtə /; / tʌn kɪˈlɒmɪtə / (BrE)
a unit of measurement in freight transport equal to moving one tonne of goods over a distance of one kilometer

Examples

One tonne-kilometer unit
A short journey of heavy cargo can amount to the same number of tonne-kilometers as a long haul of lighter goods.
By combining weight and distance, the tonne-kilometer offers a clear measure of transport efficiency.
The total freight transported last year was reported as 500 million tonne-kilometers.
A railway company reported an increase in tonne-kilometres due to longer haul distances.
In logistics, calculating tonne-kilometres helps track environmental impact per transport mode.
The total freight moved last year was measured in tonne-kilometres to compare efficiency.
