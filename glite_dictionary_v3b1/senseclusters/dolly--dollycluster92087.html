dolly

verb
to move camera smoothly
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑli / (AmE), / ˈdɒli / (BrE)
to move a camera smoothly on a wheeled platform during filming

Examples

To dolly the camera
The cinematographer decided to dolly the camera forward to create a dramatic effect.
During the scene, the crew dollied the camera smoothly along the track.
Directors often dolly the camera to follow the movement of actors in a shot.
The director asked the crew to dolly the camera forward for a dramatic effect.
During the scene, they dollied smoothly alongside the actors as they walked.
It takes skill to dolly the camera without any noticeable shaking.

Related Terms

  • antonym: pan - to rotate a camera horizontally from a fixed position
  • synonym: track - to move a camera along a track during filming
