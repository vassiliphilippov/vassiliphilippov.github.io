feel up

verb
touch or examine by hand
6.6
Pronunciation: / fiːl ʌp / (AmE), / fiːl ʌp / (BrE)
to touch or examine something by hand, typically to assess its quality or condition

Examples

To feel up tires
He leaned over to feel up the tires to check if they were properly inflated.
She prefers to feel up the fruits at the market to ensure they are ripe.
The customer wanted to feel up the sweater before making a purchase.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inspect - to look at something closely to assess its condition
  • synonym: palpate - to examine by touch, especially medically
