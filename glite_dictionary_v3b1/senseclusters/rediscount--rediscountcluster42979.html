rediscount

verb
to discount again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈdɪskaʊnt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈdɪskaʊnt / (BrE)
to discount a bill or financial instrument again, especially by selling it to a central bank for liquidity

Examples

To rediscount a note
The central bank agreed to rediscount commercial paper to support liquidity in the market.
Banks may rediscount bills with the central bank during times of financial stress.
To manage cash flow, the company decided to rediscount several of its promissory notes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: discount - to sell a bill or financial instrument for less than its face value
