fog

verb
make less visible or unclear
1.0
Pronunciation: / fɑɡ / (AmE), / fɒɡ / (BrE)
to make something less visible or more difficult to understand; to obscure or cloud

Examples

Fog the memory
Her memory of that day is fogged by time and emotion.
The car windows began to fog during the cold night, making it hard to see outside.
The truth was fogged by conflicting reports in the media.
