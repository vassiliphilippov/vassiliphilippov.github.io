shellacking

noun
severe defeat
Pronunciation: / ʃəˈlækɪŋ / (AmE), / ʃəˈlækɪŋ / (BrE)
a severe defeat or beating, especially in a competition, contest, or argument

Examples

A shellacking in sports
The home team took a real shellacking in the championship game last night.
After the shellacking they received, the players promised to train harder for next season.
No one expected such a one-sided shellacking in the final round of the tournament.
The visiting team gave the hosts a complete shellacking in the championship game.
After the shellacking in last week's debate, the candidate worked hard to improve his arguments.
Our basketball team suffered a surprising shellacking despite being favored to win.

Related Terms

  • synonym: thrashing - a decisive or overwhelming defeat
  • antonym: victory - an act of defeating an opponent or enemy
