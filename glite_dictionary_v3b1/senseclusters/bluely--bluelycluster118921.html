bluely

adverb
sadly or with melancholy
8.8 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàbluÀêli / (AmE), / ÀàbluÀêli / (BrE)
in a way that expresses or suggests sadness or melancholy

Examples

She sighs bluely
She gazed out the window bluely, lost in her thoughts about the past.
His voice sounded bluely as he spoke about the missed opportunity.
The poem ended bluely, leaving the reader with a sense of longing.

Synonyms

    7.7 0.02/m
    melancholically shows deep, quiet sadness and a thoughtful mood, while bluely suggests plain sadness or soft melancholy.
  • dolefully
    7.2 0.15/m
    dolefully sounds mournful and slow, often like sad music, while bluely simply expresses gentle sadness.
  • mournfully
    4.7 0.67/m
    mournfully shows strong grief, like at a funeral or loss, while bluely suggests less intense, reflective sadness.
  • sorrowfully
    4.6 0.29/m
    sorrowfully feels deep regret or sadness about something lost, while bluely can be more about quiet melancholy.
  • sadly
    1.0 6.18/m
    sadly is the common, direct way to say someone is unhappy, while bluely feels more poetic and melancholic.
  • plaintively
    6.7 0.52/m
    plaintively sounds or looks sad in a gentle, pleading way, often in voice, while bluely means general sadness.
  • dejectedly
    6.5 0.34/m
    dejectedly shows low spirits and disappointment, like someone who failed, while bluely suggests quieter, moodier sadness.
  • gloomily
    4.1 0.57/m
    gloomily shows a dark, hopeless mood and low hope, while bluely usually means sad feeling without full hopelessness.
  • dismally
    4.8 0.41/m

  • somberly
    5.5 0.42/m
