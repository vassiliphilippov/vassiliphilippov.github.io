procyonid

noun
mammal of the family Procyonidae
9.9
Pronunciation: / proʊˈsaɪ.ə.nɪd / (AmE), / prəˈsʌɪ.ənɪd / (BrE)
a mammal of the family Procyonidae, including raccoons and their relatives, characterized by plantigrade feet and typically omnivorous diets

Examples

A procyonid mammal
Scientists observed a group of procyonids foraging for food in the forest.
The procyonid family includes animals such as raccoons and coatis.
Unlike other carnivores, the feet of procyonids are plantigrade, similar to those of bears.

Related Terms

  • synonym: raccoon - a medium-sized, nocturnal mammal native to North America, known for its masked face and ringed tail
