gas-filled

adjective
containing or full of gas
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæs fɪld / (AmE), / ˈɡæs fɪld / (BrE)
containing or filled with gas, rather than being empty or filled with another substance.

Examples

Gas-filled light bulbs
Modern light bulbs are often gas-filled to improve their efficiency and lifespan.
The scientist examined a gas-filled balloon to study how it rose in the air.
They found a gas-filled chamber inside the old diving suit, which helped provide buoyancy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: air-filled - filled with air rather than another substance
