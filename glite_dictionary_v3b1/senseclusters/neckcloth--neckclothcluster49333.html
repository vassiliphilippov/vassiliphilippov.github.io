neckcloth

noun
ornamental cloth worn around the neck
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛkˌklɔθ / (AmE), / ˈnɛkˌklɒθ / (BrE)
An ornamental piece of cloth, typically white, worn around the neck as a cravat, especially in the 18th and 19th centuries.

Examples

A white neckcloth
During formal occasions, he would always wear a pristine white neckcloth.
He tied a fresh, starched neckcloth before attending the ball.
The portrait depicts the gentleman with a ruffled neckcloth typical of the Regency era.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cravat - a strip of fabric worn around the neck, especially as a formal wear accessory
