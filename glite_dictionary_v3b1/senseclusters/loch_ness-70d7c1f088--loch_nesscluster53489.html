Loch Ness

proper noun
lake in the Scottish Highlands
4.3
Pronunciation: / lɑk ˈnɛs / (AmE), / lɒx ˈnɛs / (BrE)
A large, deep lake in the Scottish Highlands, famous for its alleged monster and being the largest body of fresh water by volume in Great Britain.

Examples

Loch Ness lake
Many tourists visit Loch Ness hoping to catch a glimpse of the legendary monster.
Scientists have conducted numerous studies to determine the depth and ecosystem of Loch Ness.
The stunning scenery around Loch Ness makes it a popular destination for hikers.
