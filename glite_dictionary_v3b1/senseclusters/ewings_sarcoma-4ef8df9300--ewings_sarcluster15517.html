Ewing's sarcoma

noun
malignant bone or bone marrow tumor
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈjuːɪŋz sɑːrˈkoʊmə / (AmE), / ˈjuːɪŋz sɑːˈkəʊmə / (BrE)
a rare and malignant tumor that develops in the bone or bone marrow, most often found in the pelvis or long bones and primarily affects children and young adults

Examples

Ewing's sarcoma tumor
Early detection of Ewing's sarcoma can significantly impact the outcome of therapy.
Researchers are developing new treatments to improve survival rates for Ewing's sarcoma.
The patient was diagnosed with Ewing's sarcoma after several weeks of persistent bone pain.
