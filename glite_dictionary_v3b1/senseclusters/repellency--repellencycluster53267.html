repellency

noun
ability to resist
Pronunciation: / rɪˈpɛlənsi / (AmE), / rɪˈpɛlənsi / (BrE)
the quality or ability of keeping something away or resisting it, especially liquids or insects

Examples

Repellency of water
The raincoat's repellency keeps water from soaking through.
Scientists tested the repellency of the new mosquito spray in the laboratory.
High repellency is important for outdoor gear used in wet conditions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: absorption - the process of taking in or soaking up something
  • synonym: resistance - the ability to withstand or oppose something
