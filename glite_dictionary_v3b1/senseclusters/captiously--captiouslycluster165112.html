captiously

adverb
in an overly critical manner
8.1 8.5
Pronunciation: /ˈkæp.ʃəs.liː/ (AmE), /ˈkæp.ʃəs.lɪ/ (BrE)
In a way that is inclined to find petty faults or make trivial objections.

Examples

To speak captiously
He questioned the plan captiously, focusing on insignificant flaws.
She captiously pointed out the errors in his presentation, missing the main point.
The reviewer wrote captiously about every minor detail in the manuscript.

  • synonym: carpingly - in a carping or fault-finding manner
