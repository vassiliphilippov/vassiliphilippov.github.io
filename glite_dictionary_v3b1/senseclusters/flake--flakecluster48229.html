flake

verb
cancel or fail to attend plans
8.4
Pronunciation: /fleɪk/ (AmE), /fleɪk/ (BrE)
informal: to cancel or fail to attend a planned meeting or arrangement, often at short notice (often used with on: 'flake on someone').

Examples

To flake on plans
Don't flake on me again; we already scheduled dinner with our friends.
He flaked on the band rehearsal, leaving the other musicians frustrated and short of players.
I'm tired of her flaking — she rarely tells anyone ahead of time that she'll miss plans.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bail (on) - to abandon or withdraw from a commitment or plan, often informally
  • antonym: show up - to arrive for a meeting or appointment; opposite of failing to attend
  • synonym: stand up (someone) - to fail to meet someone for a planned meeting, leaving them waiting
