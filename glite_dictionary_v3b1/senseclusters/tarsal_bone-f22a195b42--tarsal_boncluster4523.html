tarsal bone

noun
bone in the tarsus (ankle) of the foot
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑrsəl boʊn / (AmE), / ˈtɑːsəl bəʊn / (BrE)
any of the small bones that make up the tarsus of the foot (ankle region) in humans and other vertebrates.

Examples

A tarsal bone fracture
A fracture in a tarsal bone can cause significant mobility problems.
Doctors often use X-rays to diagnose injuries to the tarsal bones of the foot.
The ankle is made up of seven tarsal bones that connect the foot to the leg.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tarsus - the group of bones forming the ankle and back part of the foot
