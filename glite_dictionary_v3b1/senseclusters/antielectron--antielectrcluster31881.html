antielectron

noun
elementary particle with positive charge (positron)
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.ti.ɪˈlɛk.trɒn / (AmE), / ˌæn.ti.ɪˈlɛk.trɒn / (BrE)
an elementary particle with the same mass as an electron and a positive charge; also called a positron.

Examples

The antielectron particle
Scientists detected the presence of a antielectron in the laboratory experiment.
The discovery of the antielectron was a major breakthrough in particle physics.
When a antielectron collides with an electron, they annihilate each other and produce gamma rays.

Related Terms

  • antonym: electron - a stable elementary particle with negative charge, found in all atoms
  • synonym: positron - the elementary particle that is the antiparticle of the electron
