locative role

noun
semantic role indicating place
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊkətɪv roʊl / (AmE), / ˈləʊkətɪv rəʊl / (BrE)
(Linguistics) the semantic role of a noun phrase that designates the place where the action or state indicated by a verb occurs.

Examples

The locative role in a sentence
In the sentence 'She sat on the bench,' the phrase 'on the bench' has a locative role.
Linguists study how different languages express the locative role in grammar.
Prepositional phrases often serve a locative role in English sentences.

Related Terms

  • synonym: location - a place or position; also the grammatical function denoting place
