noncondensable

noun
gas not liquefied
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnkənˈdɛnsəbəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkənˈdɛnsəbəl / (BrE)
a gas or vapor that cannot be turned into a liquid by cooling or compression under specific conditions, especially in engineering systems

Examples

Remove noncondensable gases
The engineers vented the noncondensable from the condenser to improve cooling efficiency.
Removing noncondensables from the system is essential for optimal refrigeration performance.
A buildup of noncondensable gases can reduce the effectiveness of heat exchangers.
