temper

noun
state of mind or mood
4.1 3.09/m

a characteristic or temporary state of mind or mood, especially one that affects how a person behaves

Examples

A calm temper
His temper seemed unusually short this morning.
She was in a cheerful temper after receiving the good news.
Try not to let your temper affect your decisions at work.

Synonyms

  • mood
    1.0 22/m
    mood is a short-lasting feeling that changes often, while temper can be a lasting trait or a sudden, brief mood.
  • temperament
    4.3 5.62/m
    temperament is a person's usual emotional style that stays stable, while temper can mean a lasting nature or a quick mood.
  • humor
    2.1 1.87/m
    humor is an older, idiomatic word for a short mood often used in set phrases, while temper covers both lasting and temporary moods.
