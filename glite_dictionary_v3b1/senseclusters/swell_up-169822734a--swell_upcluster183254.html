swell up

verb
become larger in size or intensity
3.3 2.7
Pronunciation: /swel ʌp/ /; / /swel əp/ (AmE), /swel ʌp/ /; / /swel əp/ (BrE)
(of a part of the body or other object) to become larger or rounder in size, usually as a result of internal pressure, injury, or emotion.

Examples

Feelings swell up
A sense of excitement started to swell up within the crowd as the concert began.
If you add too much yeast, the dough will swell up in the oven.
When she twisted her ankle, it began to swell up almost immediately.
