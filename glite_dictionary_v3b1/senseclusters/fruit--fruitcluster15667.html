fruit

noun
edible ripened part of a plant
1.0
Pronunciation: / frut / (AmE), / fruːt / (BrE)
The soft, edible, ripened reproductive body of a seed plant, typically sweet or sour in taste and often eaten as food.

Examples

Fresh fruit salad
Bananas, oranges, and grapes are all types of fruit.
The apple tree bore a lot of fruit this year.
You should eat a piece of fruit with your breakfast every morning.

Related Terms

  • antonym: vegetable - edible part of a plant typically not sweet, such as roots or leaves
