inertia

noun
resistance to change in motion (physics)
Pronunciation: / ɪˈnɜrʃə / (AmE), / ɪˈnɜːʃə / (BrE)
(physics) The tendency of an object to remain at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by an external force.

Examples

Physics inertia
Because of inertia, a stationary object will stay still unless something pushes or pulls it.
Once the car started moving, inertia kept it rolling even after the driver released the gas pedal.
The concept of inertia explains why passengers are thrown forward when a vehicle stops suddenly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: momentum - the quantity of motion an object has, related to its mass and velocity
