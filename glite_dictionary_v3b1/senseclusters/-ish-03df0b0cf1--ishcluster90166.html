-ish

x
nationality suffix
4.1
Pronunciation: / ɪʃ / (AmE), / ɪʃ / (BrE)
a suffix used to form adjectives or nouns indicating nationality, origin, or association, as in 'English' or 'Spanish'

Examples

-ish in 'English'
The word 'English' uses the suffix -ish to show both the language and the nationality.
Adding -ish to 'Spain' creates 'Spanish', referring to the people or language of Spain.
Many country names in English form adjectives by adding -ish, such as 'Danish' or 'Swedish'.

Related Terms

  • synonym: -ese - a suffix forming adjectives and nouns indicating nationality or language, as in 'Chinese'
  • synonym: -ian - a suffix forming adjectives and nouns indicating relation or belonging, as in 'Canadian'
