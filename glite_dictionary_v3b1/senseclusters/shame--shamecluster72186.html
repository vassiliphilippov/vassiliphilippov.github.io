shame

verb
bring shame or dishonor upon
1.0 3.2
Pronunciation: /ʃeɪm/ (AmE), /ʃeɪm/ (BrE)
to make someone feel ashamed or guilty, or to pressure them to do something by making them feel this way

Examples

To shame a friend
He was shamed by his actions and could not face his friends.
The scandal shamed the entire organization in the eyes of the public.
Their betrayal shamed the country and angered its people.
She shamed him into admitting the truth.
The group was shamed into apologizing for their behavior.
They shamed the company into improving working conditions after public criticism.
He tried to shame his younger brother into apologizing for his mistake.
Her refusal to help did not shame him as she had hoped.
The politician was shamed by the public outcry over his actions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dishonor - to bring disgrace or loss of respect to someone or something
  • synonym: embarrass - to make someone feel awkward, self-conscious, or ashamed
  • antonym: honor - to show great respect for (opposite on a moral spectrum)
