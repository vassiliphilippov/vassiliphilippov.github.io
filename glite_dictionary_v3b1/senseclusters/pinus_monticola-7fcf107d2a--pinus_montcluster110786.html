Pinus monticola

noun
a tall western North American pine tree
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪnəs mɑnˈtaɪkələ / (AmE), / ˈpɪnəs mɒnˈtɪkələ / (BrE)
a tall pine tree native to western North America, with stout blue-green needles and grey-brown bark that develops rectangular plates as it matures

Examples

Pinus monticola tree
Botanists studied the bark pattern of Pinus monticola to understand its growth stages.
Lumber from Pinus monticola is highly valued for its straight grain and workability.
The forest was dominated by towering Pinus monticola with their distinctive blue-green needles.
