cold storage

noun
archival digital storage
4.4
Pronunciation: / koʊld ˈstɔrɪdʒ / (AmE), / kəʊld ˈstɔːrɪdʒ / (BrE)
(in computing) archival storage for digital data that is rarely accessed, typically kept offline or in low-cost storage tiers to reduce costs.

Examples

Data in cold storage
The company moved legacy backups to cold storage to cut costs.
We transferred months of raw footage into cold storage since it was rarely accessed.
Automatic tiering sends older database snapshots to cold storage for long-term retention.

Related Terms

  • synonym: archive - a collection or place for long-term storage of data; also the action of storing data for preservation
  • antonym: hot storage - storage designed for frequently accessed data, typically higher-cost and faster than cold storage
  • synonym: offline storage - storage kept disconnected from a network or easily accessible systems, often used for long-term retention
