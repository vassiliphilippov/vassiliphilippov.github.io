synergize

verb
to work together effectively
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪnərˌdʒaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪnədʒaɪz / (BrE)
to combine or work together with others to achieve a greater effect than acting alone

Examples

To synergize teams
The marketing and sales teams decided to synergize their strategies for the new product launch.
If we synergize our skills, we can finish this project much faster.
The two companies hope to synergize to create innovative solutions for their customers.
The two teams decided to synergize their efforts to complete the project ahead of schedule.
Certain nutrients can synergize in the body to improve overall health.
When creative minds synergize, the results can be truly innovative.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collaborate - to work together with others to achieve a goal
  • antonym: compete - to try to win or gain something against others
  • synonym: cooperate - to act or work together for a common purpose
  • antonym: oppose - to act against or resist
