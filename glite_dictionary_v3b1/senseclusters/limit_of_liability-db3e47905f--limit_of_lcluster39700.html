limit of liability

noun
maximum payable by insurer under a policy
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪmɪt əv laɪˈəbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˈlɪmɪt əv laɪəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the maximum amount that an insurance company is required to pay under a policy for a covered loss.

Examples

The limit of liability amount
Be sure to review the limit of liability before purchasing any insurance policy.
If the damages exceed the limit of liability, the insured is responsible for the additional amount.
The limit of liability stated in the policy is $100,000 per incident.
