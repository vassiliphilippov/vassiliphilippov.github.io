pity

noun
compassion for others' suffering
5.3 1.1
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪti / (AmE), / ˈpɪti / (BrE)
the feeling of sorrow and compassion for someone who is suffering or has bad luck

Examples

Feel pity for people
He felt a deep sense of pity for the victims of the disaster.
It is not always helpful to show pity; sometimes people just want understanding.
She looked at the stray dog with such pity that she decided to take it home.
He looked at the damaged painting with a sense of pity and regret.
It is not pity that the victims need, but support and respect.
She felt deep pity for the child left alone in the rain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compassion - sympathetic pity and concern for others' sufferings
  • synonym: empathy - the ability to understand and share the feelings of another
  • antonym: indifference - lack of interest or concern
