market

verb
to make commercial or promote for sale
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑrkɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
to promote, sell, or distribute products or services, especially as part of a business activity

Examples

To market a product
She helped the farmers market their produce directly to consumers.
Some small towns have begun to market their local traditions to attract tourists.
The company plans to market its new line of organic foods in Europe.
After researching consumer trends, the team decided to market their product to young adults.
Many pharmaceutical companies market new drugs through doctors and direct advertisements.
The startup is currently marketing its eco-friendly gadgets primarily online.
Many companies market their products through social media channels.
The cooperative marketed fresh vegetables directly to local restaurants.
They are currently marketing a new range of organic snacks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: advertise - to make a product or service known to the public
  • antonym: buy - to acquire something by paying for it; opposite of selling
  • synonym: commercialize - to make commercial in character; to exploit for profit
  • synonym: promote - to help sell or publicize a product or service
  • synonym: sell - to give or hand over something in exchange for money
