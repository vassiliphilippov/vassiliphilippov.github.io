regressive

adjective
opposing progress; returning to a former state
Pronunciation: / rɪˈɡrɛsɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈɡrɛsɪv / (BrE)
Opposing progress or tending to return to a former, less advanced state or condition.

Examples

Regressive policies
Some tax systems are considered regressive because they disproportionately affect those with lower incomes.
The movement's regressive ideals sparked debate among advocates of modernization.
The new policy was criticized as regressive and detrimental to social advancement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: progressive - favoring or implementing progress, reform, or innovation
