elicitor

noun
person who draws out responses
7.4
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈlɪ.sɪ.tər/ /; / /ə.ˈlɪ.sɪ.tər/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈlɪ.sɪ.tə/ /; / /ə.ˈlɪ.sɪ.tə/ (BrE)
a person who draws out information, feelings, or reactions from others, often by asking questions or prompting discussion

Examples

A skilled elicitor
The skilled elicitor encouraged the children to share their thoughts about the story.
As an elicitor, she was able to uncover important details during the interview.
A good elicitor listens carefully and asks thoughtful questions to help others open up.
