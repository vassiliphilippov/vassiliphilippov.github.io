hyponatremia

noun
abnormally low blood sodium level
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpoʊnəˈtriːmiə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəʊnəˈtriːmɪə / (BrE)
a medical condition in which the level of sodium in the blood is abnormally low, often associated with dehydration or other underlying illnesses

Examples

Low hyponatremia level
Doctors monitor sodium levels closely to prevent hyponatremia in hospital patients.
Symptoms of hyponatremia may include headache, confusion, and nausea.
The athlete was hospitalized after developing hyponatremia during the marathon.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hypernatremia - abnormally high level of sodium in the blood
