intraverbal

noun
verbal response to words
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntrəˈvɜrbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪntrəˈvɜːbəl / (BrE)
a verbal response that is triggered by other words or verbal stimuli, not by objects or direct events

Examples

An intraverbal answer
During the session, the child answered the therapist's question with an intraverbal response.
Teachers often use intraverbal exercises to help students build conversational skills.
The ability to give an intraverbal reply is important in language development.

Related Terms

  • antonym: echoic - a verbal response that repeats what is heard
  • antonym: tact - a verbal response to an object or event
