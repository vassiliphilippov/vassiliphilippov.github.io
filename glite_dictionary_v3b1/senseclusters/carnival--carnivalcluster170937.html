carnival

noun
wild, noisy, disorganized activity
1.0 2.7
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑːr.nə.vəl/ /; / /ˈkɑːr.nɪ.vəl/ (AmE), /ˈkɑː.nɪ.vəl/ /; / /ˈkɑː.nə.vəl/ (BrE)
A situation characterized by wild, noisy, and disorganized activity, often with a playful or chaotic atmosphere, similar to that of a public festival.

Examples

Her birthday party was a carnival of noise, games, and excited children.
The debate quickly turned into a carnival of shouting and laughter.
