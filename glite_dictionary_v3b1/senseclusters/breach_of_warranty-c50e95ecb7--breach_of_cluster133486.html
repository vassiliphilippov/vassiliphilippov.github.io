breach of warranty

noun
failure to meet warranty terms
Pronunciation: / briːtʃ əv ˈwærənti / (AmE), / briːtʃ əv ˈwɒrənti / (BrE)
the failure to fulfill the terms of a warranty, occurring when a product or service does not meet the promised standard or conditions

Examples

A breach of warranty claim
A breach of warranty may entitle the buyer to compensation or repair under the law.
If there is evidence of a breach of warranty, the seller could be held liable for damages.
The customer sued the company for breach of warranty after the new appliance stopped working within a week.

Related Terms

  • synonym: breach of contract - the violation of any agreed-upon terms in a contract
