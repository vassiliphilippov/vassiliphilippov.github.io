high-key

adjective
openly or clearly expressed with enthusiasm
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪˌki / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌkiː / (BrE)
(informal, slang) Openly or clearly expressed, especially with enthusiasm or intensity.

Examples

A high-key feeling
Her high-key excitement over the new movie was obvious to everyone.
I'm high-key disappointed that the event was canceled.
They were high-key proud of their accomplishment and didn't try to hide it.

Related Terms

  • antonym: low-key - not elaborate, showy, or openly expressed
