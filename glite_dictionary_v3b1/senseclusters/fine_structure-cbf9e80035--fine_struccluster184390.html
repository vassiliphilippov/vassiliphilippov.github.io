fine structure

noun
small-scale splitting in atomic spectra
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪn ˌstrʌktʃər / (AmE), / ˈfaɪn ˌstrʌktʃə / (BrE)
the group of closely spaced lines in the atomic spectrum of an element, resulting from small differences in energy levels

Examples

The fine structure lines
Measuring the fine structure allows researchers to better understand atomic energy levels.
Scientists observed a fine structure in the spectral lines of hydrogen.
The fine structure arises due to interactions between electrons and the atomic nucleus.
