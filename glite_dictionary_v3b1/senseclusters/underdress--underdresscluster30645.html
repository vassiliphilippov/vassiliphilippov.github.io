underdress

verb
wear less formal clothing than expected
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈdrɛs / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈdrɛs / (BrE)
to dress in a way that is less formal or less elaborate than is suitable for a particular occasion

Examples

To underdress for a party
He realized he had underdressed when everyone else arrived in suits and dresses.
It is easy to underdress for a formal dinner if you are unsure of the dress code.
Some students tend to underdress during warm weather, even for special assemblies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overdress - to dress in a way that is more formal than necessary
