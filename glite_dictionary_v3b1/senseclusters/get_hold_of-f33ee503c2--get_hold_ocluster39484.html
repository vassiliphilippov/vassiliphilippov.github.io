get hold of

verb
physically take or grasp something
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt hoʊld ʌv / (AmE), / ɡɛt həʊld ɒv / (BrE)
to take or grasp something with one's hands; to physically obtain or take possession of something

Examples

To get hold of the handle
Can you get hold of the handle and pull the door open?
He tried to get hold of the rope before it slipped away.
Let me get hold of that book so I can show it to the class.

  • synonym: take - to get into one's possession; physically seize
