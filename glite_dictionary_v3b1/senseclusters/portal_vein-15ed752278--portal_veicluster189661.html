portal vein

noun
vein carrying blood to the liver
Pronunciation: / ˈpɔɹ.tl̩ veɪn / (AmE), / ˈpɔː.tl veɪn / (BrE)
a short vein that carries blood from the digestive organs to the liver

Examples

The portal vein
A blockage in the portal vein can cause serious medical complications.
Doctors examined the patient's portal vein during the ultrasound to check for signs of disease.
The portal vein transports nutrient-rich blood to the liver for processing.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hepatic vein - vein that carries blood away from the liver
