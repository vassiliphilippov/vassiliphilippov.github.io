photolysis

noun
chemical breakdown by light
Pronunciation: / foʊˈtɑləsɪs / (AmE), / fəʊˈtɒlɪsɪs / (BrE)
the chemical breakdown of a substance caused by exposure to light

Examples

Photolysis of water
Chlorophyll plays a key role in the photolysis of water during photosynthesis.
Scientists study photolysis to understand how sunlight affects environmental chemicals.
The photolysis of pollutants in the atmosphere can help reduce air contamination.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hydrolysis - the chemical breakdown of a compound due to reaction with water
  • synonym: photodecomposition - the decomposition of a substance by light
