burlesque

noun
theatrical comedy show, often with parody or striptease
7.4
Pronunciation: / bɜːrˈlɛsk / (AmE), / bɜːˈlɛsk / (BrE)
a type of theatrical entertainment featuring comic skits, parody, and occasionally striptease, often characterized by broad and earthy humor

Examples

Burlesque show
Historically, burlesque was known for its playful parodies of serious drama.
The theater downtown puts on a burlesque show every Friday night featuring comedians and dancers.
The troupe revived the art of burlesque with clever skits and dazzling costumes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: parody - an imitation of the style of a particular writer or genre with deliberate exaggeration for comic effect
  • synonym: satire - the use of humor, irony, or exaggeration to expose or criticize
