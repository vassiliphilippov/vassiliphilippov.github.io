upper-middle-class

adjective
of the upper part of the middle class
Pronunciation: / ˌʌpɚ ˈmɪdəl ˌklæs / (AmE), / ˌʌpə ˈmɪdl ˌklɑːs / (BrE)
belonging to the upper part of the middle class; having above-average income, education, and status within the middle socioeconomic range

Examples

Upper-middle-class family
Her family is considered upper-middle-class because both parents have professional jobs and a comfortable home.
Many upper-middle-class couples prioritize their children's education above almost everything else.
The upper-middle-class neighborhood is known for its excellent schools and leafy streets.

Related Terms
