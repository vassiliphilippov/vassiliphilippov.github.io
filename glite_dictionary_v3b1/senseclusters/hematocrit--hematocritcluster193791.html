hematocrit

noun
instrument for measuring blood cell ratio
8.4 9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈhiːmətoʊˌkrɪt /; / hiˈmæt̬əkrɪt / (AmE), / hɪˈmatəkrɪt /; / hiˈmætəkrɪt / (BrE)
An instrument used to measure the proportion of red blood cells to plasma in blood, usually by centrifugation.

Examples

A hematocrit device
A portable hematocrit is often used in field hospitals to quickly determine blood cell concentrations.
After spinning the tube in the hematocrit, the doctor assessed the patient's red blood cell level.
The laboratory technician placed the blood sample into the hematocrit for analysis.
A precise haematocrit reading is essential when diagnosing certain types of anaemia.
The hospital recently upgraded its old haematocrit to a more automated machine.
The laboratory technician placed the blood sample into the haematocrit to measure the packed cell volume.
