polytonality

noun
use of multiple musical keys at once
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑli.toʊˈnæləti / (AmE), / ˌpɒli.təʊˈnæl.ɪti / (BrE)
The use of two or more different keys played or sung simultaneously in a piece of music.

Examples

Polytonality in music
Modern composers often experiment with polytonality to achieve unique harmonic effects.
Stravinsky is known for his innovative use of polytonality in early 20th-century compositions.
The piece’s striking polytonality creates a sense of tension and complexity unusual in classical music.
