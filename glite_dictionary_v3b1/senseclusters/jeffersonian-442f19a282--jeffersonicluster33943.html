Jeffersonian

adjective
relating to Thomas Jefferson or his principles
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒɛfərˈsoʊniən / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛfəˈsəʊniən / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of Thomas Jefferson or his principles, especially as reflected in American political thought and democracy.

Examples

Jeffersonian democracy
His political philosophy was distinctly Jeffersonian in its emphasis on individual liberties.
She wrote an essay exploring Jeffersonian democracy in early America.
The new constitution reflected many Jeffersonian ideals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Hamiltonian - relating to Alexander Hamilton or his principles
