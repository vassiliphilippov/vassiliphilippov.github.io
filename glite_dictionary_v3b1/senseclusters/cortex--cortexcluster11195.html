cortex

noun
outer layer of an organ, especially the brain
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔr.tɛks / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.tɛks / (BrE)
the outer layer of an organ or structure, especially the layer of unmyelinated neurons (grey matter) covering the cerebrum in the brain

Examples

Brain cortex
Damage to the visual cortex can result in loss of sight even if the eyes are healthy.
Scientists studied the thickness of the cortex in different areas of the brain.
The human brain's cortex is responsible for higher cognitive functions such as thought and reasoning.

Related Terms

  • antonym: medulla - the inner part of an organ or structure
