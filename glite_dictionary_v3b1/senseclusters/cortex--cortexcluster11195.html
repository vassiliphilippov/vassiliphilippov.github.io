cortex

noun
outer layer of an organ, especially the brain
7.2 4.76/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…îr.t…õks / (AmE), / Ààk…îÀê.t…õks / (BrE)
the outer layer of an organ or structure, especially the layer of unmyelinated neurons (grey matter) covering the cerebrum in the brain

Examples

Brain cortex
Damage to the visual cortex can result in loss of sight even if the eyes are healthy.
Scientists studied the thickness of the cortex in different areas of the brain.
The human brain's cortex is responsible for higher cognitive functions such as thought and reasoning.

Synonyms

  • cerebral cortex
    5.0 0.80/m
    cerebral cortex is the outer brain layer involved in thought and perception, while cortex can mean any organ's outer layer.
  • pallium
    9.9 0.06/m
    pallium is the technical anatomical term for the cerebrum's grey layer, while cortex is a general word for an organ's outer layer.
  • cerebral mantle
    9.9 0.00/m
    cerebral mantle names the grey matter covering the cerebrum in formal or older texts, while cortex is the common modern term.

Related Terms

  • antonym: medulla - the inner part of an organ or structure
