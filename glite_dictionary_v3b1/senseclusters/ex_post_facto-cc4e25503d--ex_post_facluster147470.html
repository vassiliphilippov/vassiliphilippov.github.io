ex post facto

adjective
having retroactive effect
9.9 7.9
Pronunciation: / ɛks poʊst ˈfæktoʊ / (AmE), / ɛks pəʊst ˈfæktəʊ / (BrE)
(of a law or decision) having retroactive effect; applying to events that occurred before its enactment.

Examples

An ex post facto law
An ex post facto law is generally considered unfair because it punishes past behavior retroactively.
Congress passed a law, but its ex post facto application was challenged in court.
The new policy cannot be applied ex post facto to previous cases.

Related Terms

  • synonym: retroactive - (of a law or decision) applying to events or actions that took place in the past
