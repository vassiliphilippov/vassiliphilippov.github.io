tone down

verb
reduce the intensity of a sound
1.0 3.1
Pronunciation: /toʊn daʊn/ (AmE), /təʊn daʊn/ (BrE)
to make something less strong, intense, or forceful (such as sound, color, emotion, or language)

Examples

To tone down the noise
She used thick curtains to tone down the noise from outside.
They toned down the music by placing a blanket over the speakers.
You should tone down your voice when speaking in the library.
She tried to tone down her excitement during the meeting.
The company decided to tone down the advert after receiving complaints.
The editor asked the writer to tone down his critical remarks.
Could you tone down the colors on this slide? They are a bit too bright.
He decided to tone down his speech so as not to offend anyone in the audience.
The editor asked the writer to tone down the harsh language in the article.

Related Terms

  • antonym: amplify - increase the strength or intensity of sound
  • antonym: intensify - to make something stronger or more intense
  • synonym: soften - make something less loud or harsh
