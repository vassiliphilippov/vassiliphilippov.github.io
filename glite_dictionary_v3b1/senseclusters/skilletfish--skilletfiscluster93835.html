skilletfish

noun
a small, skillet-shaped clingfish
Pronunciation: / ˈskɪlɪtˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈskɪlɪtˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A small marine fish of the clingfish family, characterized by a flattened, skillet-shaped body that allows it to cling to surfaces.

Examples

A skilletfish clinging
During the low tide, we spotted a tiny skilletfish clinging firmly beneath a dock.
The skilletfish uses its suction disc to attach securely to rocks and oyster shells.
Unlike other fish, the skilletfish is easily identified by its round, flattened shape.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clingfish - a type of small fish with a suction disc for clinging to surfaces
