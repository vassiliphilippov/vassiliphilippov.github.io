fragility

noun
quality of being easily broken or weak
2.0 4.2
Pronunciation: /frə.ˈdʒɪ.lɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /frə.ˈdʒɪ.lɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
the quality of being easily broken, damaged, or destroyed (physically or metaphorically)

Examples

Physical fragility
Her fragility made it difficult for her to lift anything heavy.
Scientists tested the fragility of the material under pressure.
The fragility of the old bridge became apparent during the storm.
Economic fragility can be seen when markets react strongly to minor changes.
Her voice had a slight fragility that made her singing especially moving.
The fragility of the glass vase made it unsuitable for children to play near it.

Related Terms

  • antonym: durability - the ability to withstand damage or wear
  • synonym: weakness - state or condition of lacking strength
