awapuhi

noun
Hawaiian plant shampoo
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːwəˈpuːhi / (AmE), / ˌɑːwəˈpuːhi / (BrE)
a clear, fragrant liquid from the flower heads of a ginger plant, traditionally used as a natural shampoo or conditioner in Hawaii

Examples

Awapuhi shampoo
She massaged awapuhi into her hair to leave it soft and fragrant.
Traditional Hawaiian remedies often include awapuhi for its gentle cleansing properties.
After a swim, he rinsed his hair with awapuhi to refresh it naturally.
