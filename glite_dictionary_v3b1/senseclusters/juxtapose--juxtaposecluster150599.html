juxtapose

verb
place side by side for comparison
7.9 7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʌk.stəˌpoʊz / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌk.stə.pəʊz / (BrE)
to place two or more things side by side, especially for comparison or contrast

Examples

To juxtapose two images
The artist decided to juxtapose images of urban life with scenes of nature.
The documentary juxtaposes archival footage with modern interviews to highlight societal changes.
When you juxtapose these two styles, their differences become much clearer.
