paleostriatum

noun
inner part of the lenticular nucleus in the brain
Pronunciation: / ˌpeɪli.oʊˈstraɪətəm / (AmE), / ˌpalɪəʊˈstraɪətəm / (BrE)
the inner pale yellow part of the lenticular nucleus in the brain, involved in movement and coordination

Examples

The paleostriatum area
Damage to the paleostriatum can affect motor coordination and balance.
During the dissection, the pathologist identified the paleostriatum within the basal ganglia.
The paleostriatum plays an important role in regulating voluntary movement.
