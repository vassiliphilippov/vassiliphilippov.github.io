Eastern Malayo-Polynesian

noun
eastern subfamily of Malayo-Polynesian languages
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈiːstərn məˌleɪoʊ pälɪˈniːʒən / (AmE), / ˈiːstən məˌleɪəʊ pɒlɪˈniːʒən / (BrE)
A major eastern grouping of the Malayo-Polynesian branch of the Austronesian language family, including languages spoken in eastern Indonesia, the Pacific, and neighboring areas.

Examples

Eastern Malayo-Polynesian languages
Linguists classify several Pacific languages as part of the Eastern Malayo-Polynesian subfamily.
Most languages spoken in eastern Indonesia belong to the Eastern Malayo-Polynesian group.
The migration patterns of Eastern Malayo-Polynesian speakers have been studied extensively.
