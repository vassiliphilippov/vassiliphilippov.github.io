palimpsestic

adjective
layered with traces
Pronunciation: / ˌpælɪmpˈsɛstɪk / (AmE), / ˌpælɪmpˈsɛstɪk / (BrE)
having layers or traces of earlier forms, meanings, or features beneath the surface

Examples

Palimpsestic city history
The city's palimpsestic architecture shows evidence of many different historical periods.
Her memories were palimpsestic, with new experiences layered over old ones.
The artist created a palimpsestic painting by painting over earlier images but letting parts show through.

Related Terms

  • synonym: multilayered - having several layers or levels
  • antonym: simple - having a single, clear structure without layers
