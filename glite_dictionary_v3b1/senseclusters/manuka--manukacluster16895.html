manuka

noun
New Zealand shrub
8.1
Pronunciation: / məˈnuːkə / (AmE), / məˈnuːkə / (BrE)
a shrub native to New Zealand, valued for its aromatic flowers and honey produced from its nectar

Examples

Manuka bush
The manuka plant is famous for producing honey with unique antibacterial properties.
Farmers in New Zealand cultivate manuka for both its flowers and its valuable honey.
She planted a manuka bush in her garden to attract bees and butterflies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tea tree - a related shrub known for its essential oil
