Indian Mutiny

noun
1857–58 uprising against British rule in India
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈɪndiən ˈmjuːtəni / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən ˈmjuːtɪni / (BrE)
a major revolt against British rule in India in 1857–1858, characterized by widespread uprisings and significant battles such as the siege of Lucknow

Examples

The Indian Mutiny 1857
Many historians debate the causes and consequences of the Indian Mutiny.
The Indian Mutiny marked a turning point in the relationship between Britain and India.
The siege of Lucknow was one of the most famous episodes of the Indian Mutiny.
