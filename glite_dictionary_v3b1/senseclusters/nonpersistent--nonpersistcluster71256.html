nonpersistent

adjective
temporary; not saved
6.3
Pronunciation: /nɑːn.pər.ˈsɪs.tənt/ (AmE), /nɒn.pə.ˈsɪs.tənt/ (BrE)
not saved to permanent storage and cleared when a session, process, or system restarts, especially in computing.

Examples

Nonpersistent data
The application uses nonpersistent data that disappears when you close the program.
For security reasons, the system stores passwords in nonpersistent memory only.
Because the cache is nonpersistent, all information is lost after a reboot.

Related Terms

  • antonym: persistent - continuing to exist or endure over a prolonged period; not cleared after restart
  • synonym: temporary - lasting for only a limited period of time
