Chesapeake Bay

proper noun
large estuary between Virginia and Maryland
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˌtʃɛsəpiːk ˈbeɪ / (AmE), / ˌtʃɛsəpiːk ˈbeɪ / (BrE)
a large estuary of the North Atlantic Ocean between Virginia and Maryland in the eastern United States, fed mainly by the Susquehanna River

Examples

The Chesapeake Bay estuary
Environmental groups work to restore the water quality of the Chesapeake Bay each year.
Several rivers, including the Susquehanna, flow into the Chesapeake Bay.
The Chesapeake Bay is renowned for its blue crabs and diverse wildlife habitats.
