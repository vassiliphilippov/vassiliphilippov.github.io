overbuilt

adjective
excessively strong or complex
6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈoʊ.vər.bɪlt/ (AmE), /ˈəʊ.və.bɪlt/ (BrE)
constructed or designed with more strength, capacity, or complexity than is needed for its purpose

Examples

Overbuilt bridge
The bridge was overbuilt to withstand even the most severe storms.
Some critics argue that the stadium is overbuilt for the size of the local audience.
This watch feels overbuilt, with a heavy case and extra features most people never use.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overengineered - designed with unnecessary complexity or features
  • antonym: underbuilt - constructed with less strength or capacity than needed
