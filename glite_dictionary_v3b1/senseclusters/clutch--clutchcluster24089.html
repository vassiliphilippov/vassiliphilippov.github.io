clutch

noun
group of eggs or young birds hatched together
8.2 4.7
Pronunciation: /klʌtʃ/ (AmE), /klʌtʃ/ (BrE)
a group of eggs or young birds produced or hatched at the same time by a bird

Examples

A clutch of eggs
Each spring, the goose lays a new clutch in the reeds.
Scientists observed that the number of chicks in the clutch depended on food availability.
The robin's nest contained a clutch of four eggs.
