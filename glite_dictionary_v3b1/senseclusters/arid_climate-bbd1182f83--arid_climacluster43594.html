arid climate

noun
very dry, low-rainfall climate
4.4 3.4
Pronunciation: /ˈær.ɪd ˈklaɪ.mət/ /; / /ˈer.ɪd ˈklaɪ.mət/ (AmE), /ˈær.ɪd ˈklaɪ.mət/ (BrE)
A climate characterized by very low rainfall and dry conditions, typically supporting little vegetation.

Examples

An arid climate
Because of its arid climate, the area supports only hardy shrubs and cacti.
The arid climate of the region meant water was scarce and crops were difficult to grow.
Tourists found the arid climate challenging, especially during the hottest months of the year.
