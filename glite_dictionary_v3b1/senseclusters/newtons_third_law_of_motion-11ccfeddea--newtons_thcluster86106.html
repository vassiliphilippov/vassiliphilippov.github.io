Newton's third law of motion

noun
physics law: equal and opposite reactions
Pronunciation: / ˈnuːtənz θɜrd lɔː əv ˈmoʊʃən / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtənz θɜːd lɔːr əv ˈməʊʃən / (BrE)
the principle in physics that for every action there is an equal and opposite reaction

Examples

Newton's third law of motion principle
Children see Newton's third law of motion in action when they push against a wall and feel themselves move backwards.
Rockets work thanks to Newton's third law of motion, as the hot gases push backward and the rocket moves forward.
When you jump off a boat, Newton's third law of motion explains why the boat moves in the opposite direction.
