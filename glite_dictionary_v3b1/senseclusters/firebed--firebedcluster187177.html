firebed

noun
layer of burnt material
9.3
Pronunciation: /ˈfaɪ.ər.bed/ /; / /ˈfaɪr.bed/ (AmE), /ˈfaɪ.ə.bed/ (BrE)
a layer of burnt material such as ash or charcoal in soil or rock, showing evidence of a past fire

Examples

Ancient firebed
The archaeologists discovered a firebed beneath the ancient dwelling, suggesting people once cooked there.
A thick firebed in the soil indicated that a large wildfire had swept through the area centuries ago.
Scientists analyzed the firebed to learn about the types of plants burned in prehistoric times.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ash layer - a stratum of ash left by burning, often found in soil
  • synonym: charcoal - black carbon material left after burning wood
