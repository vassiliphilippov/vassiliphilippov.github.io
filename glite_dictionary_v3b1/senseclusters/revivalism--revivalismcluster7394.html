revivalism

noun
renewal movement
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvaɪvəlɪzəm / (AmE), / rɪˈvaɪvəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief in or practice of renewing interest in something, especially religion, art, or culture

Examples

Cultural revivalism
The rise of revivalism in the 19th century led to renewed interest in traditional religious practices.
Art movements often experience revivalism when artists look to the past for inspiration.
Cultural revivalism can help preserve languages and customs that are at risk of disappearing.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decline - a gradual loss of strength or importance
  • synonym: reformation - the act of making changes to improve something, often used for religious movements
  • synonym: renaissance - a revival or renewed interest in something, especially art or learning
