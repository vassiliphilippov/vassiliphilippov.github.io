probatory

adjective
serving to prove something, esp. legally
Pronunciation: / ˈproʊbəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈprəʊbəˌtəri / (BrE)
tending to prove or demonstrate the truth of a particular statement or allegation, especially in a legal context

Examples

Probatory evidence
Her testimony was considered probatory evidence in the courtroom.
Only probatory facts should be admitted during the trial.
The lawyer presented probatory documents to establish her client's innocence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: probative - having the quality or function of proving or demonstrating something
