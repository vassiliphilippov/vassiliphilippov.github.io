parenchymatous

adjective
of parenchyma tissue
9.9
Pronunciation: / pəˌrɛŋˈkaɪmətəs / (AmE), / pəˌrɛŋˈkɪmətəs / (BrE)
relating to or consisting of the main, functional tissue of an organ or plant (not the supporting tissue)

Examples

Parenchymatous tissue
The parenchymatous tissue in the liver is responsible for most of its metabolic functions.
Botanists studied the parenchymatous cells to understand how plants store nutrients.
Damage to parenchymatous organs can seriously affect overall health.
The doctor explained that the parenchymatous cells in the kidney are responsible for filtering blood.
Damage to the parenchymatous tissue can seriously affect the function of the liver.
Researchers studied the parenchymatous regions of the lung to understand the effects of the disease.

Related Terms

  • antonym: stromal - relating to the supporting tissue of an organ
