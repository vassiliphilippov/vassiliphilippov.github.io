torpedo

noun
railway warning explosive device
6.7 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / t…îrÀàpido ä /; / t…îrÀàpiÀêdo ä / (AmE), / t…îÀêÀàpiÀêd…ô ä / (BrE)
a small explosive device or firework that explodes to make a loud noise or to trigger a warning or action (such as on railroads, in oil wells, or as a toy)

Examples

Upon hearing the loud bang of the torpedo, the engineer immediately slowed down the train.
Be careful not to step on a stray torpedo after the fireworks show.
He bought a box of torpedoes for the New Year's celebration.
The children threw a torpedo on the pavement, and it made a loud bang.
After the torpedo was detonated, the flow of gas increased dramatically.
Drillers lowered the torpedo into the shaft to stimulate the underground reservoir.
The company used a torpedo to boost oil production from the old well.
