ligament

noun
strong connective tissue band in the body
2.2 3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈlɪg.ə.mənt/ (AmE), /ˈlɪg.ə.mənt/ (BrE)
a strong band or sheet of tough fibrous tissue that connects bones or cartilages, or supports muscles or organs

Examples

Knee ligament injury
She tore a ligament in her knee while playing soccer.
Strong ligaments help keep the joints stable.
The surgeon repaired the damaged ligament during the operation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: tendon - a flexible but inelastic cord of strong fibrous collagen tissue attaching a muscle to a bone
