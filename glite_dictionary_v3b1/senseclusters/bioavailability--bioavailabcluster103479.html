bioavailability

noun
uptake by organisms
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪoʊəˌveɪləˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌbaɪəʊəˌveɪləˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the amount or proportion of a substance (like a nutrient or medicine) that can be absorbed and used by the body

Examples

The bioavailability of vitamins
The bioavailability of vitamins in food can be affected by how the food is prepared.
Scientists study the bioavailability of pollutants to understand their impact on wildlife.
Improving the bioavailability of a drug can make it more effective for patients.
The bioavailability of this medicine is affected by food.
Doctors consider the bioavailability of nutrients when recommending dietary supplements.
Researchers measured the bioavailability of the new drug in clinical trials.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absorption - the process by which substances are taken up by living tissues
  • antonym: elimination - the removal of substances from the body
  • antonym: toxicity - the degree to which a substance can harm organisms
