fallibilism

noun
belief knowledge is uncertain
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfæləbɪˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈfælɪbɪˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
the philosophical belief that human knowledge is always subject to error and can be revised in light of new evidence

Examples

Fallibilism in philosophy
Many scientists embrace fallibilism because it encourages ongoing questioning and testing of ideas.
The philosopher argued that fallibilism is essential for intellectual progress and humility.
By accepting fallibilism, we recognize that our beliefs may need to change as we learn more.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dogmatism - the tendency to lay down principles as undeniably true
  • synonym: skepticism - the attitude of doubting the truth of something
