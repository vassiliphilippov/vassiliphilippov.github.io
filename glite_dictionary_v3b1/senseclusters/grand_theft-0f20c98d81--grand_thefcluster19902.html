grand theft

noun
theft of property above a set value (legal term)
1.2 5.5
Pronunciation: /grænd θeft/ (AmE), /grænd θeft/ (BrE)
The crime of stealing property of significant value, with the minimum value determined by law and varying by jurisdiction.

Examples

Grand theft crime
Grand theft is considered a felony in most states and carries severe penalties.
He was charged with grand theft after stealing a car worth thousands of dollars.
The lawyer explained the difference between grand theft and petty theft to the jury.
