A severe episode of emotional distress, often resulting in the inability to cope with everyday life or function normally.

Examples

An emotional breakdown episode
After losing his job, he suffered an emotional breakdown and needed time off to recover.
The constant stress eventually led to an emotional breakdown that affected her personal and professional life.
Witnesses described her emotional breakdown as a sudden and overwhelming response to the bad news.

Synonyms

  • nervous breakdown
    5.3 1.42/m
    nervous breakdown is a period of severe mental distress often treated by doctors, while emotional breakdown means losing the ability to cope.
  • breakdown
    3.9 4.44/m
    breakdown means a sudden failure of mental health that stops normal life, while emotional breakdown focuses on deep emotional collapse.
  • crack-up
    7.2 0.08/m
    crack-up is an informal term for a sudden mental collapse or burst of laughter, while emotional breakdown is a serious loss of coping.
