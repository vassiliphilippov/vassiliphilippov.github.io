unrealized

adjective
not having achieved full potential
3.2
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈriːəˌlaɪzd /; / ʌnˈriəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ʌnˈrɪəlaɪzd / (BrE)
(of a person or their qualities, ambitions, etc.) Not having achieved or developed their full potential.

Examples

Unrealized potential
His talents went unrealized due to a lack of opportunity and support.
Many artists remain unrealized because they never share their work with the world.
She left the company with a sense of unrealized potential and unfinished projects.
Her unrealised potential troubled her parents, who believed she could achieve great things.
Many talented athletes remain unrealised because they never get the chance to compete at a high level.
The team's unrealised ambitions were a constant source of frustration for the coach.
