toggle joint

noun
mechanical joint applying pressure via pivoted arms
6.7 7.0
Pronunciation: /ˈtɑːg.əl dʒɔɪnt/ (AmE), /ˈtɒg.əl dʒɔɪnt/ (BrE)
a joint consisting of two arms connected by a pivot, designed so that straightening the arms applies pressure at the ends

Examples

A toggle joint device
By straightening the toggle joint, the device exerts more pressure on the workpiece.
Many folding mechanisms make use of a toggle joint for efficient movement and locking.
The press operates by means of a toggle joint that multiplies the applied force.
