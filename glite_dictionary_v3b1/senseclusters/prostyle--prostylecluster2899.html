prostyle

adjective
building with columns across the front
Pronunciation: / ˈproʊˌstaɪl / (AmE), / ˈprəʊ.staɪl / (BrE)
(of a building) having a row of free-standing columns only at the front, typically as part of a portico.

Examples

A prostyle building
A prostyle façade can be seen in many neoclassical government buildings.
The architect decided on a prostyle design to emphasize the grand entryway.
The museum's entrance features a prostyle portico reminiscent of ancient Greek temples.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: amphiprostyle - building with columns at both front and back
  • degree higher: peristyle - building with columns on all sides
