obloquy

noun
strong public criticism or verbal abuse
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈɑː.blə.kwi /; / ˈɑːbləˌkwi / (AmE), / ˈɒb.lə.kwi /; / ˈɒbləkwɪ / (BrE)
strong public criticism or verbal abuse that causes disgrace or shame

Examples

Fierce obloquy from press
After the scandal, the politician faced fierce obloquy from the press and the public.
Despite enduring years of obloquy, he maintained his innocence and dignity.
She became a target of obloquy after false rumors about her spread online.
After the scandal, the politician became the target of widespread obloquy in the media.
He struggled for years to overcome the obloquy that followed his mistake.
The scientist faced obloquy when her controversial findings were released to the public.

Related Terms

  • synonym: calumny - the making of false and defamatory statements
  • synonym: disgrace - loss of reputation or respect as a result of a dishonorable action
  • antonym: honor - high respect or esteem; opposite of disgrace or shame
  • antonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration
  • synonym: slander - the action of making a false spoken statement damaging to a person's reputation
