incorruptible

adjective
incapable of being morally corrupted
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.kəˈrʌp.tə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kəˈrʌp.tɪ.bəl / (BrE)
Unable to be morally corrupted; not susceptible to bribery or dishonesty.

Examples

An incorruptible judge
Despite numerous temptations, she proved to be incorruptible in her principles.
Many believe that scientists should remain incorruptible in their research and reporting.
The leader was known for his incorruptible character and unwavering sense of justice.

Related Terms

  • antonym: corruptible - capable of being corrupted
  • synonym: honest - always telling the truth, not cheating or stealing
