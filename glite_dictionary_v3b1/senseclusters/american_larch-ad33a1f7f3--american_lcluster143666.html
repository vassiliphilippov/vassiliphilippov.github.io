American larch

noun
a North American larch tree species
Pronunciation: / əˈmɛrɪkən lɑrtʃ / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən lɑːtʃ / (BrE)
a medium-sized larch tree native to Canada and the northern United States, with a broad conic crown and rust-brown scaly bark

Examples

A American larch tree
Our cabin was surrounded by a grove of American larch trees.
Scientists observed that American larch forests support a variety of wildlife.
The American larch is known for its brilliant golden-yellow foliage in autumn.
