theory of gravitation

noun
scientific theory of gravitational attraction
Pronunciation: / ˈθɪri əv ˌɡrævəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈθɪəri əv ˌɡravɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
(physics) the scientific theory that any two bodies attract each other with a force proportional to their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them.

Examples

The theory of gravitation in physics
Modern physics has built on the theory of gravitation to explore deeper aspects of space and time.
Newton proposed the theory of gravitation to explain why planets orbit the Sun.
The theory of gravitation changed scientific understanding of the universe.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: general relativity - Einstein's theory of gravitation, superseding Newton's law
  • synonym: law of universal gravitation - Newton's law defining the force of gravity between two masses
