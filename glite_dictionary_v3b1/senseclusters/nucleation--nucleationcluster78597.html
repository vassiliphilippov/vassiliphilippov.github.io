nucleation

noun
formation of nuclei
8.2
Pronunciation: /nuː.kliː.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /njuː.kliː.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process by which small clusters or nuclei form to initiate a new phase or structure, such as in crystal growth

Examples

The nucleation of crystals
The nucleation of ice crystals began as the temperature dropped below freezing.
Dust particles in the air can promote the nucleation of water droplets in clouds.
Scientists study nucleation to better understand how materials change from liquid to solid.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crystallization - the process by which a solid forms from a solution or melt
  • antonym: dissolution - the process of dissolving or breaking apart a structure
