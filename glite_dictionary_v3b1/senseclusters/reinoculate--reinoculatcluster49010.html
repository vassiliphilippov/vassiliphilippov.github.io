reinoculate

verb
to vaccinate again
8.2
Pronunciation: /riː.ɪ.ˈnɑː.kjə.leɪt/ /; / /riː.ˈnɑː.kjə.leɪt/ (AmE), /riː.ɪ.ˈnɒ.kjʊ.leɪt/ /; / /riː.ˈnɒ.kjʊ.leɪt/ (BrE)
to give another dose of a vaccine or microorganism to someone or something, usually to restore or ensure immunity

Examples

To reinoculate patients
Doctors may need to reinoculate patients if immunity fades.
After several years, it is sometimes necessary to reinoculate livestock to prevent disease outbreaks.
The school organized a campaign to reinoculate children whose initial vaccinations were incomplete.
After the initial treatment failed, doctors decided to reinoculate the patients to ensure immunity.
Veterinarians may reinoculate animals if the first vaccine dose was ineffective.
Researchers had to reinoculate the soil with beneficial bacteria after the contamination.

Related Terms

  • synonym: revaccinate - to vaccinate again after an initial vaccination
