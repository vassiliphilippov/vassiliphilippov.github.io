xanthan

noun
food thickener
Pronunciation: / ˈzænθæn / (AmE), / ˈzænθæn / (BrE)
a substance produced by bacteria and used as a thickener or stabilizer in foods and other products

Examples

xanthan gum thickener
Many gluten-free recipes call for xanthan to improve texture.
The salad dressing contains xanthan to keep the ingredients from separating.
Manufacturers often add xanthan to toothpaste to give it a smooth consistency.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gelatin - a protein used as a gelling agent in food
  • synonym: guar gum - a plant-derived thickening agent used in food
