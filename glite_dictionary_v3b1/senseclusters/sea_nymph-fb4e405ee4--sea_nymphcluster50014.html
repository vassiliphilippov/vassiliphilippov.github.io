sea nymph

noun
female sea spirit in Greek mythology
9.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / si n…™mf / (AmE), / siÀê n…™mf / (BrE)
(In Greek mythology) a female spirit or minor goddess of the sea, especially one of the daughters of Oceanus or Nereus.

Examples

Sea nymph in myth
According to legend, a sea nymph rescued the hero after his shipwreck.
Artists often depicted sea nymphs swimming gracefully among the waves.
The ancient Greeks believed that sea nymphs protected sailors from danger.

Synonyms

  • Oceanid
    9.9 0.00/m
    An Oceanid is a sea nymph who is a daughter of Oceanus and Tethys, while a sea nymph is the general term for such spirits.
  • Nereid
    9.9 0.15/m
    A Nereid is one of Nereus and Doris's fifty sea maidens often shown near the sea, while a sea nymph is any female sea spirit.
