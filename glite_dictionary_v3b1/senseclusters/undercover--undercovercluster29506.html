undercover

adjective
done secretly to avoid detection
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndɚˈkʌvɚ / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈkʌvə / (BrE)
involving secret or concealed methods, especially to gain information or carry out investigations without being detected

Examples

Undercover police work
Several police officers worked undercover to infiltrate the criminal gang.
The journalist went undercover to expose corruption within the organization.
The operation required everyone to remain undercover for several months.

Related Terms

  • synonym: covert - not openly acknowledged or displayed
  • antonym: overt - done or shown openly; not secret
