obtuse angle

noun
angle between 90° and 180°
6.3
Pronunciation: / əbˈtuːs ˈæŋɡəl / (AmE), / əbˈtjuːs ˈæŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
An angle that measures more than 90 degrees and less than 180 degrees.

Examples

An obtuse angle
In geometry class, we learned that an obtuse angle measures more than 90 degrees but less than 180 degrees.
The architect designed the roof with an obtuse angle to create a unique and modern appearance.
The triangle has one obtuse angle, which makes it an obtuse triangle.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acute angle - an angle less than 90 degrees
  • degree higher: reflex angle - an angle greater than 180 degrees and less than 360 degrees
  • antonym: right angle - an angle of exactly 90 degrees
