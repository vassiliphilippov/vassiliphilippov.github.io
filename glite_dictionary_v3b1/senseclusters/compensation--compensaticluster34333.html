compensation

noun

In psychiatry, a defense mechanism by which a person conceals perceived shortcomings by emphasizing desirable traits or behaviors.

Examples

Psychological compensation
His constant boasting about his achievements could be seen as compensation for feelings of inadequacy.
In psychology, compensation often occurs unconsciously when individuals overemphasize positive attributes.
Therapists recognized her perfectionism as a form of compensation for her lack of confidence.
