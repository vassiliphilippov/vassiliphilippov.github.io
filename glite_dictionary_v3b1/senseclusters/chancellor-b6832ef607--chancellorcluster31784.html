Chancellor

noun
Britain's finance minister
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃænsələr / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːnsələ / (BrE)
(in the UK) the cabinet minister responsible for the government's economic and financial matters, formally known as the Chancellor of the Exchequer.

Examples

The Chancellor of the Exchequer
Each year, the Chancellor is responsible for outlining the government's financial plans.
Many people watched closely as the Chancellor delivered his speech in the House of Commons.
The Chancellor announced a new set of tax reforms in his annual budget statement.
