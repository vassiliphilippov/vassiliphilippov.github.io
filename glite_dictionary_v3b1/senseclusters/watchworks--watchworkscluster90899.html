watchworks

noun
inner mechanisms
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑtʃˌwɝks / (AmE), / ˈwɒtʃˌwɜːks / (BrE)
the complex and intricate inner parts or mechanisms of a system, often used figuratively to describe how something functions internally

Examples

Watchworks of a system
The engineer explained the watchworks of the new computer chip to the team.
Understanding the watchworks of government can help citizens make informed decisions.
She was fascinated by the watchworks of the human brain and how it processes information.

Related Terms

  • synonym: machinery - the working parts of a machine or system
  • synonym: mechanism - a system of parts working together in a machine or process
  • antonym: surface - the outside or visible part of something, not its inner workings
