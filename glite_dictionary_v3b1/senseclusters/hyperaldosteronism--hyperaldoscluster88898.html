A medical condition characterized by excessive production of aldosterone by the adrenal glands, often resulting in high blood pressure and low potassium levels.

Examples

Diagnosed hyperaldosteronism
The doctor diagnosed her with hyperaldosteronism after noticing persistent high blood pressure and low potassium levels.
Treatment options for hyperaldosteronism include medication or, in some cases, surgery to remove a tumor.
Untreated hyperaldosteronism can lead to serious cardiovascular complications.
