doctrine of analogy

noun
theological principle on divine-human analogy
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑːktrɪn əv əˈnæləˌdʒi / (AmE), / ˈdɒktrɪn əv əˈnælədʒi / (BrE)
(in theology) The principle that any similarity between God and creatures is always outweighed by a greater dissimilarity, meaning that analogies between the divine and the human are inherently inadequate.

Examples

The doctrine of analogy in theology
According to the doctrine of analogy, all similarities between humans and God are limited by even greater differences.
Many philosophers have debated the implications of the doctrine of analogy for our understanding of religious language.
The doctrine of analogy helps theologians explain how human language can refer to God, despite profound differences.
