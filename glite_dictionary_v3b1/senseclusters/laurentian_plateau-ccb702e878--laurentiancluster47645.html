Laurentian Plateau

noun
large Canadian plateau region
8.5
Pronunciation: /lɔːr.ˈen.ʃən plæ.ˈtoʊ/ /; / /lɑːr.ˈen.ʃən plæ.ˈtoʊ/ (AmE), /lɒr.ˈen.ʃən plæ.ˈtəʊ/ /; / /lɒr.ˈen.ʃɪ.ən plæ.ˈtəʊ/ (BrE)
a vast plateau region in eastern and northern Canada, covering more than 40% of the country's land area, extending from the Great Lakes to the Arctic Ocean

Examples

The Laurentian Plateau region
Glacial activity has greatly influenced the landscape of the Laurentian Plateau.
Many rivers flow from the Laurentian Plateau into the surrounding lowlands and lakes.
The Laurentian Plateau is rich in minerals and has shaped much of Canada's geography.
