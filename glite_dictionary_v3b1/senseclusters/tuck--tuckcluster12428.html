tuck

verb
to fit something snugly by folding or pushing in
6.1 1.7
Pronunciation: /tʌk/ (AmE), /tʌk/ (BrE)
to push, fold, or place something carefully into a particular place so that it is neat, secure, or hidden

Examples

To tuck a shirt
He quickly tucked the letter into his pocket when someone walked in.
Please tuck your shirt into your pants before the meeting.
She tucked the stray hair behind her ear as she spoke.
He likes to tuck his shirt into his pants before leaving for work.
She carefully tucked the blanket around the sleeping child.
The tailor will tuck the excess fabric to make your dress fit perfectly.
He tucked the napkin into his shirt collar before eating.
She carefully tucked the sheets under the mattress to keep them tight.
The dress was tucked at the waist to create a more flattering shape.
