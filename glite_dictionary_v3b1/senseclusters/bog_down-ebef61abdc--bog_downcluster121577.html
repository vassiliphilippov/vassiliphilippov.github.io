bog down

verb
to become stuck or unable to progress
8.3
Pronunciation: / bɑɡ daʊn /; / bɔɡ daʊn / (AmE), / bɒɡ daʊn / (BrE)
to become or cause to become stuck or unable to make progress, especially in a difficult or complicated situation

Examples

To bog down in problems
Negotiations bog down when participants can't agree on key points.
Progress on the project bogged down after several technical issues arose.
The truck bogged down in the thick mud, unable to move forward.
Disagreements over details can bog down a project for months.
The heavy truck began to bog down in the wet field after the rain.
We got bogged down in traffic during our drive to the airport.
Heavy rain can bog down even the best-prepared hikers on the trail.
Let’s not bog down this meeting with minor details.
The negotiations began to bog down after several weeks of debate.

Related Terms

  • antonym: advance - to move forward or make progress

  • synonym: impede - to hinder or obstruct the progress of someone or something
  • synonym: mire - to cause to become stuck in mud or a difficult situation
  • antonym: move forward - to make progress; to proceed
  • synonym: stall - to stop running or moving, especially suddenly or unexpectedly
