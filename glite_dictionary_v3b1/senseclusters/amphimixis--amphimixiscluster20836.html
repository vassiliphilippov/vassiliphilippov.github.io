amphimixis

noun
sexual reproduction via gamete fusion
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˌæmfɪˈmɪksɪs /; / ˌæm.fəˈmɪk.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌamfɪˈmɪksɪs /; / ˌam.fɪˈmɪk.sɪs / (BrE)
the process of sexual reproduction in which a male and a female gamete (sperm and egg) fuse to form a new organism

Examples

Amphimixis in reproduction
Amphimixis is the typical mode of reproduction in most animals and flowering plants.
Scientists study amphimixis to better understand genetic diversity among offspring.
Unlike parthenogenesis, amphimixis requires the contribution of both a male and a female gamete.
In sexual reproduction, amphimixis occurs when the nuclei of sperm and egg merge.
Scientists study amphimixis to understand inheritance and variation in offspring.
The process of amphimixis ensures genetic diversity within a species.

Related Terms

  • antonym: parthenogenesis - reproduction without fertilization by a male gamete
