consumption

noun
historical term for tuberculosis
2.8
Pronunciation: / kənˈsʌmpʃən / (AmE), / kənˈsʌmpʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
(historical) A wasting disease, especially pulmonary tuberculosis, characterized by progressive wasting of the body.

Examples

Died of consumption
In the 19th century, many poets succumbed to consumption before reaching old age.
She was said to have died of consumption, a common fate in Victorian England.
The sanatorium was established for those suffering from consumption.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tuberculosis - an infectious disease usually affecting the lungs
