terrane

noun
geological crust block
Pronunciation: / təˈreɪn / (AmE), / təˈreɪn / (BrE)
a block of the Earth's crust with a distinct geological history from the surrounding areas

Examples

Geological terrane
Geologists mapped the boundaries of the terrane to study its unique rock formations.
The Pacific Northwest contains several distinct terranes that collided with the continent millions of years ago.
Each terrane tells a different story about the Earth's tectonic past.
