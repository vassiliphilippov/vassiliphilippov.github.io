logical implication

noun
if-then relation between propositions
3.8 6.2
Pronunciation: /ˈlɑː.dʒɪ.kəl ɪm.plə.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ˈlɑː.dʒɪ.kəl ɪm.plɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈlɒdʒ.ɪ.kəl ɪm.plɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ˈlɒdʒ.ɪ.kəl ɪm.plə.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a logical relationship between two propositions such that if the first is true, the second must also be true; typically expressed as 'if p, then q'.

Examples

Logical implication in logic
He confused causation with logical implication in his argument.
The proof relies on the logical implication that if all mammals are warm-blooded, then whales are warm-blooded.
Understanding logical implication is essential in learning the foundations of mathematical logic.
