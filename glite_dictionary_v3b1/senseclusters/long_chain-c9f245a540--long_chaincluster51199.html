long chain

noun
long sequence of atoms in a molecule
Pronunciation: / lɔŋ tʃeɪn / (AmE), / lɒŋ tʃeɪn / (BrE)
(Chemistry) a relatively long sequence of atoms, typically carbon atoms, linked together in a molecule.

Examples

A long chain molecule
Fatty acids can be categorized by the length of their long chain hydrocarbons.
Polyethylene consists of a long chain of carbon atoms bonded together.
The properties of a polymer depend on whether it has a long chain or a short chain structure.

Related Terms

  • antonym: short chain - a relatively short sequence of atoms in a molecule
