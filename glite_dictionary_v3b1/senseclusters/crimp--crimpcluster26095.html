crimp

verb
to pinch into ridges or waves
Pronunciation: / krɪmp / (AmE), / krɪmp / (BrE)
to press or pinch together material so that it forms ridges or a wavy edge

Examples

To crimp the edge
After braiding her hair, she crimped the ends with small rubber bands.
She carefully crimped the edges of the pie crust to seal in the filling.
The metal is crimped to create a secure joint that will not come apart.
