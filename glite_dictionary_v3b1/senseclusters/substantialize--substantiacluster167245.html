substantialize

verb
to give importance
9.1
Pronunciation: / səbˈstænʃəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / səbˈstænʃəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something more important, significant, or substantial, especially in a figurative sense

Examples

To substantialize a problem
Her research helped substantialize the issue, gaining attention from policymakers and funders.
The new evidence served to substantialize their claims about the importance of the project.
He worked hard to substantialize his ideas so that others would take them seriously.

Related Terms

  • antonym: downplay - to make something seem less important or significant
  • synonym: emphasize - to give special importance or attention to something
  • synonym: highlight - to make something stand out or be more noticeable
