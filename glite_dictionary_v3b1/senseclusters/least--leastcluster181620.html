least

adverb
to the smallest extent or degree
5.6
Pronunciation: / list /; / liːst / (AmE), / liːst / (BrE)
to the smallest or lowest degree; used to show the superlative form of 'little' (modifying verbs, adjectives, or adverbs)

Examples

Least affected
He was the least affected by the news among his friends.
Of all the candidates, she was the least experienced for the job.
You should worry least about what others think and focus on your goals.
Of all the books I've read this year, I enjoyed this one the least.
She was the one who was least interested in going to the party.
This is the least expensive option on the menu.

Related Terms

  • antonym: most - to the greatest extent or degree, superlative of 'much' or 'many'
