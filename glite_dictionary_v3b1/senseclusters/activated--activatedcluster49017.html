activated

adjective
sewage treated with bacteria and aeration
1.0 3.9
Pronunciation: /ˈæk.tɪ.veɪ.tɪd/ (AmE), /ˈæk.tɪ.veɪ.tɪd/ (BrE)
(of sewage or sludge) treated by aeration and the addition of bacteria to accelerate decomposition of organic matter.

Examples

Activated sludge process
After being activated, the sewage undergoes further processing to remove harmful substances.
During the cleaning process, activated biosolids help break down organic pollutants efficiently.
The activated sludge process is widely used for wastewater treatment in large cities.
