tapetum

noun
anther cell layer
Pronunciation: / təˈpiːtəm / (AmE), / təˈpiːtəm / (BrE)
the inner cell layer of an anther in flowering plants that provides nutrients to developing pollen grains

Examples

Anther tapetum layer
The tapetum plays a crucial role in nourishing pollen grains as they develop.
Researchers studied the tapetum to understand how it affects pollen fertility in plants.
Damage to the tapetum can result in poor pollen development and reduced plant reproduction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anther - the part of a stamen that contains pollen
