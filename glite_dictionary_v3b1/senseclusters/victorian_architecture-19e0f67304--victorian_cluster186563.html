Victorian architecture

noun
architectural style from Victorian era Britain
2.8
Pronunciation: / vɪkˈtɔːriən ˌɑːrkɪˈtɛktʃɚ / (AmE), / vɪkˈtɔːriən ˌɑːkɪˈtɛktʃə / (BrE)
A style of architecture popular in Britain during the reign of Queen Victoria (1837–1901), characterized by massive construction, elaborate ornamentation, and eclectic influences.

Examples

Victorian architecture style
During the tour, the guide explained the unique features of Victorian architecture seen in the old town hall.
Many homeowners have restored their houses to reflect the splendor of original Victorian architecture.
The city is famous for its beautifully preserved Victorian architecture lining the historic streets.
