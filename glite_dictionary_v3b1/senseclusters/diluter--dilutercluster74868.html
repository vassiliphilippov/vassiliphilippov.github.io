diluter

noun
substance that weakens
Pronunciation: / daɪˈluːtər / (AmE), / daɪˈluːtə / (BrE)
a substance that is added to another substance to make it less strong or concentrated

Examples

Water as a diluter
Water is often used as a diluter in chemical solutions.
The technician added a diluter to the sample to reduce its concentration.
Alcohol can act as a diluter in some laboratory procedures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concentrate - a substance made stronger by removing water or other diluting agents
  • synonym: diluent - a liquid used to dilute another substance
