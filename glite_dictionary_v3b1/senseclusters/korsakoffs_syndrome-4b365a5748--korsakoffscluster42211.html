Korsakoff's syndrome

noun
chronic memory disorder linked to alcoholism
8.6 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…îÀêr.s…ô.k…ëfs Àås…™nÀådro äm / (AmE), / Ààk…îÀê.s…ô.k…ífs Àås…™n.dr…ô äm / (BrE)
A chronic memory disorder usually caused by severe deficiency of thiamine (vitamin B1), most often linked to alcoholism, in which the ability to form new memories is lost while long-term memory remains relatively preserved.

Examples

Korsakoff's syndrome memory loss
Because of his Korsakoff's syndrome, he struggled to remember recent events but could recall details from his childhood.
Doctors noted that people with Korsakoff's syndrome often confabulate to fill gaps in their memory.
The patient was diagnosed with Korsakoff's syndrome following years of heavy drinking and poor nutrition.
