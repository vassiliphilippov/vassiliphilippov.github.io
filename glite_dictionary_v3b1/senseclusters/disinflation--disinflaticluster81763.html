disinflation

noun
reduction in token issuance rate
8.2
Pronunciation: /dɪs.ɪn.ˈfleɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /dɪs.ɪn.ˈfleɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
(in crypto and token economies) a reduction in the rate at which new tokens are issued, lowering the rate of token-supply inflation.

Examples

Token disinflation
Many DeFi projects aim for disinflation by burning tokens to slow supply growth.
After the ICO, the team announced a schedule to create long-term disinflation through capped issuance.
Investors cheered the protocol's update because it signaled future disinflation of the token supply.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inflation - an increase in the supply of tokens or in general prices
