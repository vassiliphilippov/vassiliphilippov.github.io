Acragas

proper noun
ancient Greek city in Sicily
Pronunciation: / ˈækrəˌɡæs / (AmE), / ˈakrəɡas / (BrE)
an ancient town in southwestern Sicily, near the coast, known for its six Greek temples; modern-day Agrigento.

Examples

Ancient Acragas town
Historians have praised the beauty and wealth of ancient Acragas during the classical period.
Many tourists visit the Valley of the Temples, a major attraction in Acragas.
The ruins of Acragas are among the most impressive archaeological sites in Sicily.

Related Terms
