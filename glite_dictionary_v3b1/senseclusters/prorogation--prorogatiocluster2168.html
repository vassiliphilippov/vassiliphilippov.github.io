prorogation

noun
formal temporary suspension of a legislature
Pronunciation: / proʊ.roʊˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / prɒ.rəˈɡeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The formal discontinuation of a session of a legislative body without dissolving it, usually ending its current business until a later date.

Examples

The prorogation of parliament
After the prorogation, legislators will reconvene to address pending issues.
Debate on the bill was suspended due to the unexpected prorogation of the assembly.
The government announced the prorogation of Parliament until the new session begins next month.
