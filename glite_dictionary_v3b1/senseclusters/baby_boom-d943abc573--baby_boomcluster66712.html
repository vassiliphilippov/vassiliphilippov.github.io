baby boom

noun
a sharp rise in births after World War II
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪ.bi ˌbum / (AmE), / ˈbeɪ.bi ˌbuːm / (BrE)
a marked increase in the birth rate, especially the one following World War II in the United States and other countries

Examples

The baby boom generation
Historians often cite the baby boom as a turning point in demographic trends.
Many schools were built to accommodate the baby boom generation.
The postwar baby boom greatly affected the American economy and society.

Related Terms

  • synonym: baby boomer - a person born during the baby boom, especially post–World War II
