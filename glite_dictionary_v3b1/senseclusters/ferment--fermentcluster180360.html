ferment

verb
to incite or stir up agitation or excitement
8.0
Pronunciation: / fərˈmɛnt / (AmE), / fəˈmɛnt / (BrE)
to cause or experience agitation, excitement, or unrest; to stir up trouble or be in a restless state

Examples

To ferment unrest
Radical groups attempted to ferment dissent within the city.
Revolutionary ideas started to ferment among the young intellectuals.
The leader's speech began to ferment unrest among the population.
His anger continued to ferment beneath the surface, growing with each new insult.
Ideas and protests fermented among the students, leading to widespread demonstrations.
The entire region began to ferment after the controversial decision was announced.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agitate - to make someone or something feel worried or excited
  • synonym: foment - to instigate or stir up (trouble or rebellion)
