echo chamber

noun
environment reinforcing one's own beliefs
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɛkoʊ ˌtʃeɪmbər / (AmE), / ˈɛkəʊ ˌtʃeɪmbə / (BrE)
an environment in which people encounter only opinions or information that reflect and reinforce their own beliefs

Examples

Online echo chamber
He left the online forum because it felt like nothing but an echo chamber.
Social media can easily become an echo chamber where diverse perspectives are rarely heard.
The members of the group lived in an echo chamber, repeating the same ideas to one another.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bubble - a situation where one is isolated from opposing views
  • antonym: debate - discussion involving opposing viewpoints
