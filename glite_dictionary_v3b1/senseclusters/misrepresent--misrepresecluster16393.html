Examples

To misrepresent facts
It is unethical to deliberately misrepresent data in scientific research.
She accused the newspaper of trying to misrepresent her views on the issue.
The company was fined for misrepresenting the safety of its products.

Synonyms

  • mischaracterize
    8.0 0.6/m
    mischaracterize is to describe someone in a false or misleading way, while misrepresent usually adds an intention to deceive.
  • distort
    4.6 8/m
    distort changes facts or meaning until they seem false or unclear, while misrepresent focuses on giving a wrong account to mislead.
  • misstate
    6.2 1/m
    misstate reports facts inaccurately or incorrectly, while misrepresent often reshapes those facts to create a false impression.
  • mislead
    3.9 5/m
    mislead causes people to believe something untrue or wrong, while misrepresent involves presenting information wrongly to influence belief.
  • misdescribe
    6.3 0.03/m
    misdescribe describes something incorrectly or roughly, while misrepresent suggests a fuller, often intentional, false or misleading account.

  • degree higher: fabricate - to invent or make up something, typically with deceitful intent
  • synonym: falsify - to alter (information or evidence) so as to mislead
  • degree lower: overstate - to state something in too strong terms
