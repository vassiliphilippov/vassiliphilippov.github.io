misrepresent

verb
to describe falsely or misleadingly
1.0 5.5
Pronunciation: /mɪs.re.prə.ˈzent/ /; / /mɪs.re.prɪ.ˈzent/ (AmE), /mɪs.re.prə.ˈzent/ /; / /mɪs.re.prɪ.ˈzent/ (BrE)
to give a false or misleading account of something or someone, often to deceive

Examples

To misrepresent facts
It is unethical to deliberately misrepresent data in scientific research.
She accused the newspaper of trying to misrepresent her views on the issue.
The company was fined for misrepresenting the safety of its products.
He chose to misrepresent the facts in order to gain public support.
The company was accused of trying to misrepresent its financial results to investors.
They deliberately misrepresented the data to make their case seem stronger.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clarify - to make something clearer or more accurate
  • synonym: distort - to change the shape or meaning of something so that it is no longer accurate
  • degree higher: fabricate - to invent or make up something, typically with deceitful intent
  • synonym: falsify - to alter (information or evidence) so as to mislead
  • degree lower: overstate - to state something in too strong terms
