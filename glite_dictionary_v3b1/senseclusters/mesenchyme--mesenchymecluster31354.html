mesenchyme

noun
embryonic connective tissue
8.7 9.5
Pronunciation: /ˈme.zən.kaɪm/ (AmE), /ˈme.zən.kaɪm/ (BrE)
A type of embryonic connective tissue derived from the mesoderm, which develops into connective tissues, blood, and smooth muscles.

Examples

Mesenchyme tissue
During development, mesenchyme differentiates into a variety of tissues including bone and cartilage.
Researchers studied the role of mesenchyme in organ formation.
The abnormal development of mesenchyme can lead to congenital disorders.
