Humean

adjective
of David Hume's philosophy
Pronunciation: / ˈhjuːmiən / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmiən / (BrE)
relating to the ideas, philosophy, or views of the philosopher David Hume

Examples

Humean philosophy
The philosopher defended a Humean approach to understanding human nature.
Many scientists adopt a Humean perspective when discussing causation and observation.
Her essay compared Humean skepticism with other philosophical traditions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Kantian - relating to the philosophy of Immanuel Kant
  • synonym: empiricist - relating to the theory that knowledge comes from experience
