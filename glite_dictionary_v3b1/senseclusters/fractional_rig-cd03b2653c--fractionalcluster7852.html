fractional rig

noun
sailing rig with shorter foresail
Pronunciation: / ˈfrækʃənəl rɪɡ / (AmE), / ˈfrækʃənəl rɪɡ / (BrE)
a sailing rig in which the foresail, such as a jib or genoa, does not extend all the way to the top of the mast

Examples

A fractional rig sail
Installing a fractional rig allows sailors to better control the shape of their sails.
Many modern racing yachts use a fractional rig to improve their performance in various wind conditions.
The boat's fractional rig makes it easier to adjust the mainsail when sailing upwind.
