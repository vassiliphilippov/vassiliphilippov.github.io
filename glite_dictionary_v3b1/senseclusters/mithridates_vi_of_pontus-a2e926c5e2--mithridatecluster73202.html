Mithridates VI of Pontus

proper noun
ancient king of Pontus, opponent of Rome
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈmɪ.θrɪ.deɪts ðə sɪksθ əv ˈpɑːn.təs/ (AmE), /ˈmɪ.θrɪ.deɪts ðə sɪksθ əv ˈpɒn.təs/ (BrE)
(c. 132–63 BC) An ancient king of Pontus who expanded his kingdom in Asia Minor, led several wars against Rome, and was eventually defeated and driven from power by the Roman general Pompey.
