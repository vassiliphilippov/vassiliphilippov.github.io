patent

noun
official inventor's right document
Pronunciation: / ˈpætənt / (AmE), / ˈpeɪtnt /; / ˈpeɪtənt / (BrE)
an official document that gives someone the exclusive right to make, use, or sell an invention for a certain number of years

Examples

Patent application
Applying for a patent can be a lengthy and expensive process.
She was thrilled to receive a patent for her innovative new device.
The company holds several patents on cutting-edge medical technology.
A patent protects inventors from having their ideas copied without permission.
She holds several patents on medical devices.
The company received a patent for its newly developed technology.
