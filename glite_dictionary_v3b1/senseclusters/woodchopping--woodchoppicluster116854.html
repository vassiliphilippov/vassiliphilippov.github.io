woodchopping

verb
to cut wood
3.2
Pronunciation: /wʊd.ˈtʃɑː.pɪŋ/ (AmE), /wʊd.ˈtʃɒp.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
to cut or split wood into smaller pieces, usually using an axe or saw, for fuel or timber

Examples

To woodchop firewood
He spent the afternoon woodchopping to prepare enough firewood for winter.
After hours of woodchopping, they finally had a large pile of logs ready for the fireplace.
Many people find woodchopping to be a good form of exercise and stress relief.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chop - to cut something into pieces with repeated sharp blows
  • synonym: split - to break or divide something into parts, especially along a natural line
