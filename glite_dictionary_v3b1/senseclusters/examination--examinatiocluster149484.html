examination

noun
close inspection or study
Pronunciation: / ɪɡˌzæməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪɡˌzæmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of inspecting or studying something carefully, especially in order to find problems, errors, or details

Examples

Careful examination
A close examination of the document showed there were minor errors.
During the examination of the patient, the doctor found no immediate cause for concern.
The detective's examination of the crime scene revealed several important clues.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inspection - careful examination or look at something
  • antonym: neglect - failure to give proper care or attention
  • synonym: survey - a general view or examination of something
