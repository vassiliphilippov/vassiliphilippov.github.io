subserve

verb
help to achieve; promote a purpose
9.9 0.11/m
Pronunciation: / s…ôbÀàs…ùv / (AmE), / s…ôbÀàs…úÀêv / (BrE)
to be helpful or useful in achieving something; to promote or further a purpose or function

Examples

To subserve a purpose
Research in this field subserves the development of new therapies for patients.
The new software is designed to subserve the needs of small businesses.
These policies ultimately subserve the national interest by promoting economic stability.

Synonyms

  • serve
    1.8 126/m
    serve is a general word meaning to perform a role or help achieve something, while subserve specifically means to promote or further a purpose.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assist - to give support or help
  • antonym: hinder - to make it difficult for something to happen
