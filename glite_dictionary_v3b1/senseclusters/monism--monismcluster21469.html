monism

noun
philosophical doctrine of one substance
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈmoʊnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈməʊnɪzəm / (BrE)
The doctrine that reality is ultimately made up of only one substance or principle.

Examples

Philosophical monism theory
In contrast to dualism, monism claims there is only one fundamental kind of thing in the universe.
Spinoza is often cited as a key proponent of monism in Western philosophy.
The philosopher argued that monism best explains the nature of consciousness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dualism - the doctrine that reality consists of two fundamental substances or principles
