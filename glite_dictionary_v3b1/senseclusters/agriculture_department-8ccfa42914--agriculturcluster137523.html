Agriculture Department

noun
U.S. federal department for agriculture and food policy
Pronunciation: / əˈɡrɪk.jə.l̩ dɪˌpɑrt.mənt / (AmE), / əˈɡrɪk.jʊ.lə dɪˈpɑːt.mənt / (BrE)
the federal department of the United States government responsible for developing and executing policy on farming, agriculture, and food; commonly known as the USDA.

Examples

The Agriculture Department policy
Many food safety regulations in the United States are overseen by the Agriculture Department.
The Agriculture Department announced new support programs for small farmers this year.
The Agriculture Department conducts research aimed at improving crop yields and sustainability.
