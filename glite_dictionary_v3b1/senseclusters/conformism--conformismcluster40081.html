conformism

noun
adherence to conventional beliefs
7.7 0.16/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàf…îrÀåm…™z…ôm / (AmE), / k…ônÀàf…îÀêm…™z…ôm / (BrE)
The practice of adhering to conventional rules, standards, or beliefs, often without questioning them.

Examples

Social conformism
His refusal to accept conformism made him an outsider in his community.
Some critics see educational systems as encouraging a culture of conformism.
The rise of conformism in the media limits the exchange of new ideas.

Synonyms

  • conformity
    4.8 4.26/m
    conformity means matching accepted standards and behaviors, while conformism highlights unthinking adherence to conventions without questioning them.
  • conventionalism
    8.1 0.13/m
    conventionalism follows established customs and beliefs in formal areas, while conformism stresses plain social pressure and lack of personal questioning.
  • compliance
    5.0 22/m
    compliance is obeying rules or orders often from authority, while conformism means following social norms by habit or peer pressure.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nonconformity - refusal to conform to established customs or beliefs
  • synonym: orthodoxy - conventional or generally accepted beliefs or practices
