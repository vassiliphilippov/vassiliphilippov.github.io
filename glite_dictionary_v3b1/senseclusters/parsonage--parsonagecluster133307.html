parsonage

noun
church minister's residence
9.3 0.29/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…ërs…ôn…™d í / (AmE), / Ààp…ëÀês…ôn…™d í / (BrE)
a house provided by a church for its parson, vicar, or rector to live in

Examples

The parsonage house
After the storm, repairs were needed to the roof of the parsonage.
The minister and his family warmly welcomed us into their parsonage for tea.
The new parsonage was built beside the old stone church.

Synonyms

  • rectory
    7.7 1.01/m
    rectory is the official house of a church rector, while parsonage is a general house provided by a church for clergy.
  • vicarage
    8.8 0.09/m
    vicarage is the official home given to a vicar by the church, while parsonage is the general house for church clergy.
  • manse
    9.9 0.37/m
    manse is the often large or grand house where a minister lives, while parsonage is any church-provided home for clergy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rectory - a residence for a rector, especially in the Anglican Church
  • synonym: vicarage - the residence of a vicar
