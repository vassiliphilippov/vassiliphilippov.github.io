put one's best foot forward

verb
make a strong effort or impression
Pronunciation: / pʊt wʌnz bɛst fʊt ˈfɔrwərd / (AmE), / pʊt wʌnz bɛst fʊt ˈfɔːwəd / (BrE)
to make a great effort to make a good impression or to succeed, especially when starting something new

Examples

To put one's best foot forward at interview

The company encouraged employees to put their best foot forward during the big project.
You should always put your best foot forward when meeting new people.
