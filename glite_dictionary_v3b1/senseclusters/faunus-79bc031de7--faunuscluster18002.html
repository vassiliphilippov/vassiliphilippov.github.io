Faunus

proper noun
Roman rural deity; counterpart of Pan
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔːnəs / (AmE), / ˈfɔːnəs / (BrE)
In Roman mythology, an ancient rural deity associated with nature, forests, and fertility, later identified with the Greek god Pan.

Examples

Roman god Faunus
During the festival of Lupercalia, people would honor Faunus with rituals and celebrations.
In classical literature, Faunus is often depicted as a protector of shepherds and animals.
The ancient Romans believed that Faunus could inspire dreams and oracles in the forests.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Pan - Greek god of the wild, shepherds, and flocks
