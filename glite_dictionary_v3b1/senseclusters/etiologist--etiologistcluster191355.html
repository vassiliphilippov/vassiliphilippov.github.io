etiologist

noun
specialist in causes of diseases
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌiːtiˈɑlədʒɪst /; / ˌiːtiˈɑːlədʒɪst / (AmE), / ˌiːtiˈɒlədʒɪst /; / ˌiːtɪˈɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a medical specialist who studies the causes or origins of diseases

Examples

An etiologist studies causes
As an etiologist, Dr. Lee specializes in researching the causes of autoimmune disorders.
Hospitals often consult an etiologist when facing unexplained outbreaks of illness.
The etiologist presented his findings on the origins of the rare disease at the conference.
Her research as an aetiologist has advanced the understanding of rare infectious diseases.
Hospitals often consult an aetiologist when a new illness emerges without a clear cause.
The aetiologist examined environmental factors that could have contributed to the outbreak.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pathologist - a specialist in diagnosing diseases by examining tissues, cells, and organs
