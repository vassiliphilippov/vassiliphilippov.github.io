popish behavior

noun
behavior typical of Roman Catholic practices (archaic, often derogatory)
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊ.pɪʃ bɪˈheɪ.vjɚ / (AmE), / ˈpəʊ.pɪʃ bɪˈheɪ.vjə / (BrE)
Behavior that is characteristic of a follower or adherent of the Pope, especially with reference to Roman Catholic practices; often suggestive of disapproval or anti-Catholic sentiment in historical usage.

Examples

Popish behavior at ceremony
His popish behavior at the ceremony did not go unnoticed by the Protestant congregation.
In 17th-century England, accusations of popish behavior could damage one’s reputation.
Pamphlets of the era warned against adopting any popish behavior in public life.
