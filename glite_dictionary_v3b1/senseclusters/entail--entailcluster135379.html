entail

verb
to involve as a necessary result
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈteɪl / (AmE), / ɪnˈteɪl / (BrE)
to involve something as a necessary or inevitable part, result, or consequence

Examples

Entail extra work
Adopting the new system may entail some initial challenges.
Becoming a parent entails a great deal of responsibility.
This decision will entail significant changes to our working practices.
Building the bridge entails a great deal of planning and investment.
Switching to renewable energy entails significant infrastructure changes.
The new law will entail changes in existing policies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exclude - to keep something out; not to involve
  • synonym: imply - to suggest something as a necessary consequence
  • synonym: involve - to include something as a necessary part or result
  • synonym: necessitate - to make something necessary as a result
