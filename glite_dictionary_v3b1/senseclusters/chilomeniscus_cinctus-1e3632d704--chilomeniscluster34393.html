Chilomeniscus cinctus

noun
species of banded sand snake in southwestern US
Pronunciation: / kɪˌloʊməˈnɪskəs ˈsɪŋktəs / (AmE), / kɪˌləʊməˈnɪskəs ˈsɪŋktəs / (BrE)
A small sand snake of the southwestern United States, living in fine to coarse sand or loamy soil and moving through it by 'swimming'; its banded appearance resembles that of coral snakes.

Examples

The Chilomeniscus cinctus snake
Researchers observed a Chilomeniscus cinctus 'swimming' through the loose sand in the Arizona desert.
The Chilomeniscus cinctus is often mistaken for the highly venomous coral snake due to its similar banding.
Unlike many other snakes, the Chilomeniscus cinctus spends most of its life buried beneath the soil.
