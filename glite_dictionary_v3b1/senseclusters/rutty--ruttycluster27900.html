rutty

adjective
full of ruts; bumpy or grooved
7.0 0.06/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆrÊŒti / (AmE), / ËˆrÊŒti / (BrE)
(of a surface, especially a road or path) full of ruts; marked with deep tracks or grooves caused by wheels.

Examples

A rutty road
After weeks of use, the lane was so rutty that bicycles could barely pass.
Farmers struggled to drive their trucks along the rutty track leading to the barn.
The old dirt road became especially rutty after the heavy spring rains.

Synonyms

  • rutted
    6.9 0.04/m
    rutted describes a surface with long, deep grooves from repeated wheels, while rutty means a surface that is simply full of ruts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bumpy - having many bumps or uneven spots
  • antonym: smooth - even or without irregularities on the surface
