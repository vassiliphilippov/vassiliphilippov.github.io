mesothelium

noun
epithelial tissue lining body cavities
6.6 9.3
Pronunciation: /me.zə.ˈθiː.lɪ.əm/ /; / /miː.zə.ˈθiː.lɪ.əm/ (AmE), /me.zə.ˈθiː.lɪ.əm/ /; / /miː.zə.ˈθiː.lɪ.əm/ (BrE)
A layer of epithelial cells derived from the embryonic mesoderm that lines the body's serous cavities and internal organs.

Examples

The mesothelium layer
Damage to the mesothelium can lead to inflammation and various diseases.
Researchers study the mesothelium to better understand certain types of cancer, such as mesothelioma.
The mesothelium is essential for producing fluid that lubricates the organs within the chest cavity.
