fig leaf

noun
cover used to hide something shameful
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪɡ ˌliːf / (AmE), / ˈfɪɡ ˌliːf / (BrE)
something that is used to hide or cover up an embarrassing or shameful fact or action; a pretense or cover-up

Examples

Fig leaf excuse
He apologized, but everyone knew it was only a fig leaf for his lack of action.
The corporation's environmental report was just a fig leaf to hide its real agenda.
The reforms were seen as a fig leaf, offering little real change.
His charitable donation acted as a fig leaf, distracting attention from his controversial business practices.
Many saw the sudden resignation as a mere fig leaf to deflect criticism from the government.
The company's public apology was little more than a fig leaf to cover its ongoing misconduct.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cover - something that conceals or protects
  • synonym: pretense - a false or insincere claim or excuse
