cecal appendage

noun
vestigial pouch from the cecum
Pronunciation: / ˈsiː.kəl əˈpɛn.dɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈsiː.kəl əˈpɛn.dɪdʒ / (BrE)
a small, vestigial pouch-like structure extending from the lower end of the cecum, also known as the appendix

Examples

Inflamed cecal appendage
During the surgery, the inflamed cecal appendage was removed.
Pain in the lower right abdomen may indicate an issue with the cecal appendage.
The human cecal appendage has no significant digestive function.
