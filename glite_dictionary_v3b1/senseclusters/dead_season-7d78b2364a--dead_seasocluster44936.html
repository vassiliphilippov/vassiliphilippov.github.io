dead season

noun
period of little business activity
Pronunciation: / dɛd ˈsiːzən / (AmE), / dɛd ˈsiːzn / (BrE)
a period during the year when there is very little demand for a particular product or service

Examples

The dead season
His bookstore struggles to make a profit in the dead season every summer.
Retailers often use the dead season to restock shelves and plan for busier times.
The hotel offers discounts during the dead season to attract more guests.

Related Terms

  • synonym: off-season - period when demand for products or services is low
