baseload

noun
minimum steady demand
5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈbeɪs.loʊd/ (AmE), /ˈbeɪs.ləʊd/ (BrE)
the minimum level of demand or supply that is always needed in a system, especially for electricity

Examples

The baseload demand
Power plants are designed to supply the baseload required by the electrical grid at all times.
The company depends on a stable baseload of orders to keep its factories running efficiently.
During holidays, the baseload of customers ensures the store remains open even when business is slow.
Nuclear power plants are often used to provide the baseload for a country's electricity grid.
The city relies on hydroelectric stations to meet its baseload energy needs throughout the year.
Renewable sources are increasingly contributing to the baseload in many modern power systems.
