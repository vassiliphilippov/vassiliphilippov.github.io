DBH

noun
dopamine-converting enzyme
Pronunciation: / diː biː eɪtʃ / (AmE), / diː biː eɪtʃ / (BrE)
a copper-containing enzyme (dopamine β-hydroxylase) that converts dopamine into norepinephrine, or the gene encoding this enzyme

Examples

Blood DBH levels
Researchers measured levels of DBH in the blood to study its link with stress reactions.
A mutation in the DBH gene can affect the production of norepinephrine in the body.
Doctors sometimes test DBH activity to help diagnose certain neurological disorders.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dopamine β-hydroxylase - an enzyme that converts dopamine to norepinephrine
  • degree higher: norepinephrine - a neurotransmitter produced from dopamine by DBH
