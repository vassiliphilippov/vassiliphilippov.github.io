Arian

adjective
of Arius's doctrine
Pronunciation: / ˈɛəriən / (AmE), / ˈɛəriən / (BrE)
relating to the teachings of Arius, an early Christian priest, or to the doctrine known as Arianism

Examples

Arian doctrine debate
The Arian controversy was a major issue in early Christian history.
Some bishops supported Arian beliefs during the Council of Nicaea.
The emperor tried to resolve conflicts between Arian and orthodox Christians.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Trinitarian - relating to the Christian doctrine of the Trinity, opposed to Arianism
