affricate

verb
to pronounce as affricate
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈæfrɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈæfrɪˌkeɪt / (BrE)
to pronounce a speech sound as an affricate, combining a stop and a fricative element

Examples

To affricate a 't' sound
Some English speakers affricate the 't' in 'nature', making it sound like 'ch'.
Linguists study how children sometimes affricate certain consonants when learning to speak.
In some dialects, people affricate the 'd' in 'educate', pronouncing it like 'j'.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deaffricate - to pronounce an affricate as a simple stop or fricative
  • synonym: palatalize - to pronounce a sound with the tongue near the hard palate
