second reading

noun
second stage of a bill's consideration in a legislature
1.0 6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈsek.ənd ˈriː.dɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈsek.ənd ˈriː.dɪŋ/ (BrE)
the second occasion on which a bill is presented to a legislative body, typically involving approval of its general principles (Britain) or discussion and voting on a committee's report (US)

Examples

The second reading debate
During the second reading, members debated the main purpose of the proposed legislation.
The House of Commons will hold the second reading of the education reform bill next week.
The bill passed its second reading and was sent to committee for further review.
