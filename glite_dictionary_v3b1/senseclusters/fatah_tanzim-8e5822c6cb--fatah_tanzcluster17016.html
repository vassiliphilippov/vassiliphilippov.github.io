Fatah Tanzim

noun
Palestinian Fatah-affiliated militant group
9.9
Pronunciation: / fəˈtɑː tɑnˈziːm / (AmE), / fəˈtɑː tɑnˈziːm / (BrE)
an armed Palestinian militant organization established in 1995 as the military wing of Fatah, involved in confrontations with Israel and internal Palestinian politics.

Examples

Fatah Tanzim group
Many analysts consider the Fatah Tanzim to be distinct from other Palestinian militant groups due to its ties with al-Fatah.
Members of the Fatah Tanzim were often involved in both political activity and armed resistance.
The Fatah Tanzim played a significant role in the uprisings against Israeli forces in the early 2000s.
