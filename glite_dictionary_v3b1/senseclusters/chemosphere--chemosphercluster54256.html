chemosphere

noun
chemical environment
Pronunciation: / ˈkiːmoʊˌsfɪr / (AmE), / ˈkɛməsfɪə / (BrE)
the collection of chemical substances and chemical processes present in a particular environment or on Earth as a whole

Examples

Earth's chemosphere
Scientists study the chemosphere to understand how chemicals interact in the environment.
Changes in the chemosphere can have significant effects on air and water quality.
The chemosphere of a city is influenced by both natural and human-made substances.

Related Terms

  • synonym: atmosphere - the layer of gases surrounding a planet
  • synonym: biosphere - the regions of Earth where living organisms exist
