Colorado potato beetle

noun
a beetle that feeds on potato crops
7.9
Pronunciation: / kəˌlɑːrəd.oʊ pəˈteɪ.t̬oʊ ˈbiː.t̬əl / (AmE), / kəˌlɒr.ə.dəʊ pəˈteɪ.təʊ ˈbiː.tl̩ / (BrE)
a black-and-yellow beetle that feeds on potato leaves in both its adult and larval stages, originally native to the eastern Rocky Mountains but now found worldwide

Examples

The Colorado potato beetle larvae
Eggs laid by the Colorado potato beetle can hatch into larvae that quickly strip potato plants of their leaves.
Gardeners often struggle to control infestations of Colorado potato beetles in their vegetable patches.
The Colorado potato beetle is a major pest for farmers growing potatoes.
