epiphyte

noun
plant growing non-parasitically on another
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɛpɪˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪˌfaɪt / (BrE)
A plant that grows on another plant but is not parasitic, deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Examples

An epiphyte plant
Many orchids are epiphytes, thriving on the branches of rainforest trees.
Mosses and ferns can often be found as epiphytes on old tree trunks.
Unlike parasites, epiphytes do not harm the plants they live on.
