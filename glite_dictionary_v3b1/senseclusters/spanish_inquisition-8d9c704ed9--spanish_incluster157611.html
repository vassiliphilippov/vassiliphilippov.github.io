Spanish Inquisition

proper noun
historic Catholic tribunal in Spain (1478–1834)
Pronunciation: / ˌspænɪʃ ɪnkwəˈzɪʃən /; / ˌspænɪʃ ɪnˌkwɪˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌspænɪʃ ɪŋkwɪˈzɪʃən /; / ˌspanɪʃ ɪnˌkwɪˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
the tribunal established by the Spanish monarchy in the late 15th century to enforce Catholic beliefs, known for its religious investigations and persecution, especially from the 15th to 19th centuries
