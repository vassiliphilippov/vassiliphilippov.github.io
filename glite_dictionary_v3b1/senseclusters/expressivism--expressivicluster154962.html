expressivism

noun
art as emotional expression
9.8
Pronunciation: /ɪk.ˈspre.sɪ.vɪz.əm/ /; / /ek.ˈspre.sɪ.vɪz.əm/ (AmE), /ɪk.ˈspre.sɪ.vɪz.əm/ /; / /ek.ˈspre.sɪ.vɪz.əm/ (BrE)
the theory in aesthetics that art primarily expresses the emotions or inner life of the artist or subject

Examples

Artistic expressivism
Many modern artists are influenced by expressivism in their approach to painting and sculpture.
The debate between expressivism and formalism has shaped art criticism for decades.
Her interpretation of the poem reflected the principles of expressivism, focusing on the poet's feelings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emotivism - the theory that moral or aesthetic statements express emotional attitudes
  • antonym: formalism - the theory that art's value lies in its form and structure rather than emotional content
