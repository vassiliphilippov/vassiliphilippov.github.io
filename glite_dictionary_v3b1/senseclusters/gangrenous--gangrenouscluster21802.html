gangrenous

adjective
affected by gangrene
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæŋɡrəˌnəs / (AmE), / ˈɡaŋɡrɪnəs / (BrE)
affected by or suffering from gangrene; having dead or decaying tissue caused by a loss of blood supply or infection.

Examples

Gangrenous wound
Because the wound was left untreated, it became gangrenous within a week.
Doctors found that his toes were gangrenous due to poor circulation.
The surgeon had to remove the gangrenous tissue to prevent the infection from spreading.

Related Terms

  • antonym: healthy - in good physical condition, not diseased
  • synonym: necrotic - affected by necrosis or dead tissue
