barycenter

noun
center of mass in astronomy
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛriˌsɛntər / (AmE), / ˈbarɪˌsɛntə / (BrE)
(astronomy) The common center of mass around which two or more bodies, such as planets and stars, revolve.

Examples

Planetary barycenter
Astronomers calculate the barycenter to understand how planets and their moons move in space.
The solar system's barycenter is actually outside the surface of the Sun due to Jupiter's mass.
The two stars in a binary system orbit a barycenter located between them.

Related Terms

  • synonym: center of mass - the point representing the mean position of the mass in a body or system
