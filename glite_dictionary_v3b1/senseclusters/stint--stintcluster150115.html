stint

verb
be sparing with; restrict amount
Pronunciation: / stɪnt / (AmE), / stɪnt / (BrE)
to supply, give, or use something only in small or restricted amounts; to be sparing with

Examples

To stint on food
During the war, families were forced to stint their use of sugar and butter.
She never stints with praise when her children do well in school.
The company had to stint on office supplies to save money this quarter.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lavish - to give or use something in generous or excessive amounts
  • synonym: skimp - to use less of something than is necessary to economize
