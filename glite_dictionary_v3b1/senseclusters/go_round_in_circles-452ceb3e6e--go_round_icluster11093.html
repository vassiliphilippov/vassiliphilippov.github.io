go round in circles

verb
repeatedly make no progress
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ raʊnd ɪn ˈsɜr.kəlz / (AmE), / ɡəʊ raʊnd ɪn ˈsɜː.kəlz / (BrE)
to engage in a pointless or repetitive discussion or activity that does not lead to any progress or resolution

Examples

To go round in circles endlessly
If you keep going round in circles, you'll never finish the project.
The negotiations went round in circles and failed to produce any agreement.
We argued for hours but just went round in circles without finding a solution.
