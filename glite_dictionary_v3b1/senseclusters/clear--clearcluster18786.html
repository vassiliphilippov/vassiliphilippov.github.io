clear

adjective
free from obstructions or obstacles
1.0
Pronunciation: / klɪr / (AmE), / klɪə / (BrE)
free from obstructions, obstacles, or impurities; transparent or unobstructed

Examples

Clear sky
After the storm, the roads were finally clear and safe to drive.
The police made a clear path for the ambulance through the crowd.
We had a clear view of the mountains from our hotel room.
From the summit, we enjoyed a clear view of the valley below.
It was a clear day, perfect for a picnic in the park.
The mountains looked stunning against the clear sky.
Her skin had a clear glow, free from any spots or blemishes.
The jeweler examined the clear surface of the diamond for any imperfections.
We admired the clear water of the mountain lake, untouched by pollution.

  • synonym: free - not under the control or in the power of another; not physically obstructed or in contact
  • antonym: opaque - not letting light pass through; not transparent
  • synonym: open - not blocked, free to pass through or see
  • synonym: pure - not mixed with anything else; uncontaminated
  • synonym: safe - protected from or not exposed to danger or risk
  • synonym: transparent - allowing light to pass so that objects behind can be seen
