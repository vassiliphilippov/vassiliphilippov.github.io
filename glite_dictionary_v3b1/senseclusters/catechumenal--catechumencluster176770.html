catechumenal

adjective
relating to church instruction
Pronunciation: / ˌkætɪˈkjuːmənl /; / ˌkætɪˈkjuːmənl̩ / (AmE), / ˌkætɪˈkjuːmɪnəl / (BrE)
relating to the instruction, period, or people preparing for baptism and entry into a Christian church

Examples

Catechumenal instruction
The church offered a catechumenal program for adults preparing for baptism.
During the catechumenal period, participants learned about the core beliefs of Christianity.
A catechumenal class is often required before joining the congregation.
The church held a catechumenal class for adults interested in joining the congregation.
During the catechumenal period, candidates learn about the core beliefs of Christianity.
Special catechumenal ceremonies are performed before new members are baptized.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: baptismal - relating to baptism or the ceremony of baptism
  • synonym: catechetical - relating to religious instruction or teaching
