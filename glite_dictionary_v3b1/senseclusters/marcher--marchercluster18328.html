marcher

noun
inhabitant of a border district
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑrtʃər / (AmE), / ˈmɑːtʃə / (BrE)
a person who lives in or is native to a border district, especially historically in the Welsh or Scottish Marches of Britain

Examples

A Welsh marcher
Historians study the unique laws that applied to a marcher during the reign of Edward I.
In medieval England, a marcher often held special privileges to defend and govern the borderlands.
The castle was ruled by a powerful marcher who protected the frontier from invasion.
