guilty

adjective
responsible for an offense or wrongdoing
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɪlti / (AmE), / ˈɡɪlti / (BrE)
Responsible for committing a specified offense, crime, or wrong; deserving blame or punishment.

Examples

Guilty of a crime
Many people feel guilty for mistakes they made in the past.
She looked guilty when confronted about the missing documents.
The jury found him guilty of all charges after the lengthy trial.

Related Terms

  • antonym: innocent - not guilty of a crime or wrongdoing
