aerophyte

noun
plant living on air and rain, non-parasitic
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…õr.…ôÀåfa…™t / (AmE), / Àà…õÀê.r…ôÀåf å…™t / (BrE)
A plant that obtains moisture and nutrients from the air and rain, typically growing on another plant but not being parasitic.

Examples

An aerophyte plant
Because it is an aerophyte, this fern does not need soil to survive.
Many rainforest plants are aerophytes, relying solely on rainfall and humidity.
The orchid is a classic example of an aerophyte, obtaining nutrients from the moist air.

Synonyms

  • air plant
