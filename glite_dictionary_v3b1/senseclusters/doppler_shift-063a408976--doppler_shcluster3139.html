Doppler shift

noun
change in frequency due to relative motion
7.7 5.2
Pronunciation: /ˈdɑː.plər ʃɪft/ (AmE), /ˈdɒ.plə ʃɪft/ (BrE)
a change in the observed frequency or wavelength of a wave when the source and observer are moving relative to each other

Examples

The Doppler shift effect
A police radar gun calculates a car’s speed by analyzing the Doppler shift of the reflected signal.
Astronomers use the Doppler shift to measure the speed at which stars are moving away from Earth.
The characteristic red or blue Doppler shift in light reveals information about a galaxy's motion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Doppler effect - the general phenomenon describing the change in frequency of a wave due to motion
