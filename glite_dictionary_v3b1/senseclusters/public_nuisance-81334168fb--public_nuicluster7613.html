public nuisance

noun
act interfering with public rights (legal)
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˌpʌblɪk ˈnuːsns / (AmE), / ˌpʌblɪk ˈnjuːsns / (BrE)
an act or condition that unreasonably interferes with the rights common to the general public, especially as recognized in law

Examples

A public nuisance
Authorities declared the overflowing dumpster a public nuisance that needed immediate removal.
The abandoned building became a public nuisance due to the persistent noise and illegal activity.
The city council discussed new policies to prevent any event from becoming a public nuisance in residential areas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nuisance - an act that causes annoyance or inconvenience
