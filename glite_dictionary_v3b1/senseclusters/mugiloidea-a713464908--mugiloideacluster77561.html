Mugiloidea

noun
a superfamily of fishes with abdominal pelvic fins
Pronunciation: / ˌmjuːdʒəˈlɔɪdiə / (AmE), / ˌmjuːgɪˈlɔɪdiːə / (BrE)
a superfamily of fishes distinguished by abdominal pelvic fins, including the families Mugilidae, Atherinidae, and Sphyraenidae

Examples

The Mugiloidea fishes
Researchers studied the evolutionary relationships within Mugiloidea to understand the diversification of these fishes.
Specimens from Mugiloidea are often found in coastal and brackish waters worldwide.
The Mugiloidea superfamily includes species commonly known as mullets and silversides.
