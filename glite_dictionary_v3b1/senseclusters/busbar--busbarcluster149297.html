busbar

noun
electrical conductor connecting circuits
8.6 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààb åsÀåb…ër / (AmE), / Ààb åsb…ëÀê / (BrE)
a rigid electrical conductor, usually in the form of a bar or strip, that distributes power and forms a common connection between several circuits

Examples

Copper busbar
A copper busbar is often used in electrical panels to carry high current efficiently.
Engineers must ensure the busbar is insulated to prevent accidental contact with live circuits.
The busbar system made maintenance easier by organizing the incoming and outgoing connections.

Synonyms
