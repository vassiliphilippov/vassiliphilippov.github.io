pseudomorphosis

noun
false resemblance
9.9
Pronunciation: /suː.doʊ.ˈmɔːr.fə.sɪs/ /; / /sjuː.doʊ.ˈmɔːr.fə.sɪs/ (AmE), /suː.dəʊ.ˈmɔː.fə.sɪs/ /; / /sjuː.dəʊ.ˈmɔː.fə.sɪs/ (BrE)
the condition of having the outward form or appearance of something else, but not its true nature; a false resemblance or imitation

Examples

Visual pseudomorphosis
The sculpture was a clever pseudomorphosis, looking like marble but actually made of plastic.
Cultural pseudomorphosis can occur when traditions are copied without understanding their true meaning.
The new policy was criticized as a pseudomorphosis of real reform, changing only the surface.

Related Terms

  • antonym: authenticity - the quality of being genuine or real
  • synonym: imitation - the act of copying or mimicking something
