ostracization

noun
exclusion from a group
Pronunciation: / ˌɑstrəsəˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌɑstrəsɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒstrəsɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of excluding or banishing someone from a group or society, especially as a form of punishment or social rejection

Examples

The ostracization of members
The ostracization of the whistleblower made others afraid to speak out.
After the scandal, he faced social ostracization from his former colleagues.
Ancient Athens used ostracization as a way to remove powerful political figures.
After the scandal, he faced social ostracization from his former friends and colleagues.
The community's ostracization of newcomers made it difficult for them to feel welcome.
Fear of ostracization often prevents people from expressing unpopular opinions.

  • synonym: banishment - the act of forcing someone to leave a place or community
  • synonym: exclusion - the act of preventing someone from joining or being part of something
  • synonym: exile - the state of being barred from one's native country
  • antonym: inclusion - the act of including or being included within a group
