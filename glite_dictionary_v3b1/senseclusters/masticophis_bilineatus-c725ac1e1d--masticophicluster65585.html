Masticophis bilineatus

noun
species of snake native to the US southwest
Pronunciation: / ˌmæstɪˈkoʊfɪs baɪˌlɪniˈeɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌmæstɪˈkəʊfɪs baɪˌlɪniːˈɑːtəs / (BrE)
A species of snake, also known as the Sonoran whipsnake, found in the southwestern United States; it lives both on the ground and in trees.

Examples

Masticophis bilineatus snake
Researchers found a Masticophis bilineatus basking on a rocky ledge in Arizona.
The Masticophis bilineatus is recognized for its speed and agility in both terrestrial and arboreal habitats.
The diet of Masticophis bilineatus includes lizards, birds, and small mammals.
