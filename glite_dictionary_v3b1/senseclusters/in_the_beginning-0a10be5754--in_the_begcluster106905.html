Synonyms

  • initially
    3.2 39/m
    initially describes the first stage of a process, while in the beginning is a neutral phrase for that early time.
  • at first
    1.0 62/m
    at first describes what happens before things change later, while in the beginning simply marks the early time.
  • to begin with
    2.3 13/m
    to begin with introduces the first point in speech or writing, while in the beginning refers to the initial time.
  • originally
    2.0 42/m
    originally speaks about how something was at the start before change, while in the beginning marks the early time.
  • early on
    2.5 12/m
    early on describes events during an early stage, while in the beginning emphasizes the very start of something.
  • from the word go
    5.3 0.18/m
    from the word go means right from the very start of an activity, while in the beginning is more neutral about timing.
  • from the get-go
    6.6 2.37/m
    from the get-go is an informal phrase for right from the start, while in the beginning is neutral and fits many contexts.
