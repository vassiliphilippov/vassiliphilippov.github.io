twinflower

noun
creeping subshrub with paired pink flowers
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈtwɪnˌflaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈtwɪnˌflaʊə / (BrE)
a small creeping evergreen subshrub of northern Europe and Asia, with delicate, fragrant, tubular, usually pink flowers borne in pairs

Examples

Twinflowers with pink blooms
Twinflowers are admired by botanists for their lovely paired blooms and slender stems.
He knelt to examine the delicate fragrance of a single twinflower growing among the moss.
The forest floor was covered with charming twinflower blossoms in early summer.
