river pear

noun
West Indian fruit, like a mango
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪvər pɛr / (AmE), / ˈrɪvə pɛə / (BrE)
A West Indian fruit resembling the mango, often eaten pickled.

Examples

Pickled river pears
Many Caribbean dishes feature pickled river pears as a tangy side.
She described the flavor of a river pear as unique, somewhere between a mango and a plum.
The market stall was selling fresh river pears that had just been harvested.
