index

noun
alphabetical list of contents in a book
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌdɛks /; / ˈɪndɛks / (AmE), / ˈɪn.deks /; / ˈɪndɛks / (BrE)
an alphabetical list at the end of a book or document that shows where names or subjects are mentioned, or a number showing position or value in a list

Examples

Book index
All the key terms in the textbook are listed in the index at the back.
She turned to the index to find which pages discussed climate change.
Without an index, it would be much harder to locate specific information in this book.
The index of the book helped me quickly find the chapter I needed.
Stock market analysts watch the main index to track economic trends.
Each student was assigned an index number for the exam results.
