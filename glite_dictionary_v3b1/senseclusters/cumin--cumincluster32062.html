cumin

noun
aromatic spice seed

Pronunciation: /ˈkjuː.mən/ /; / /ˈkjuː.mɪn/ /; / /ˈkuː.mɪn/ /; / /ˈkʊ.mɪn/ /; / /ˈkʌ.mɪn/ (AmE), /ˈkʌ.mɪn/ /; / /ˈkjuː.mɪn/ (BrE)
The aromatic seeds of a plant of the parsley family, used as a spice in cooking, especially in Asian, Middle Eastern, and Latin American cuisine.

Examples

Ground cumin spice
She always keeps a jar of cumin seeds in her spice rack.
Sprinkle a pinch of cumin over the roasted vegetables for extra flavor.
The recipe called for ground cumin and coriander to create a rich, aromatic blend.
