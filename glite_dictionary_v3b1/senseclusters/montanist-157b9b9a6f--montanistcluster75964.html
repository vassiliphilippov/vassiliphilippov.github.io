Montanist

noun
early Christian sect member
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːntənɪst / (AmE), / ˈmɒntənɪst / (BrE)
a member of an early Christian movement known for emphasizing prophecy and strict moral standards

Examples

A Montanist leader
The early church condemned the Montanist movement for its radical prophetic claims.
A Montanist preacher traveled across Asia Minor spreading messages of repentance.
Historians study the beliefs and practices of the Montanist community in ancient Phrygia.

Related Terms

  • synonym: heretic - a person holding beliefs contrary to official religious doctrine
  • antonym: orthodox - conforming to established or traditional beliefs, especially in religion
