biconditional

noun
logical equivalence
9.9
Pronunciation: /baɪ.kən.ˈdɪʃ.ən.əl/ /; / /bɪ.kən.ˈdɪʃ.ən.əl/ (AmE), /baɪ.kən.ˈdɪʃ.ən.əl/ /; / /bɪ.kən.ˈdɪʃ.ən.əl/ (BrE)
a logical operator or statement that is true only when both parts are either true or both are false

Examples

A biconditional in logic
In logic, a biconditional is true only if both statements are either true or false.
The teacher explained how the biconditional connects two statements with the phrase 'if and only if'.
You can use a biconditional to show that two conditions are logically equivalent.
In logic, a biconditional is often written as 'p if and only if q'.

Mathematicians use the biconditional to express that two conditions are logically equivalent.
