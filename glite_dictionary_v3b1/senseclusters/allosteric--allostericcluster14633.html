allosteric

adjective
enzyme binding site
Pronunciation: / ˌæləˈstɛrɪk / (AmE), / ˌaləˈstɛrɪk / (BrE)
relating to the regulation of an enzyme or protein by a molecule binding at a site other than the active site, changing its activity or function

Examples

An allosteric inhibitor
An allosteric inhibitor can decrease the activity of an enzyme by binding to a specific site.
The scientist studied how allosteric regulation affects metabolic pathways in cells.
Some drugs work by targeting the allosteric sites of enzymes rather than the active sites.
The scientist studied the allosteric effects of the new compound on enzyme activity.
Some drugs work by binding to allosteric sites rather than the main active site.
The allosteric regulation of proteins is important in many biological processes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: active site - the region on an enzyme where substrate molecules bind
  • antonym: orthosteric - relating to the main active site of a protein or enzyme
