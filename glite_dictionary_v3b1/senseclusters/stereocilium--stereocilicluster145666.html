stereocilium

noun
inner ear projection
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌstɛri.oʊˈsɪliəm / (AmE), / ˌstɪəri.əʊˈsɪliəm / (BrE)
a tiny hair-like projection on the surface of certain cells, especially those in the inner ear, involved in sensing sound or movement

Examples

The stereocilium hair
Each stereocilium in the inner ear plays a crucial role in detecting sound vibrations.
Damage to a single stereocilium can lead to hearing loss or balance problems.
Scientists study the movement of stereocilia to better understand how we perceive sound.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cilia - small hair-like structures on the surface of cells
  • synonym: microvillus - a microscopic cellular membrane protrusion that increases surface area
