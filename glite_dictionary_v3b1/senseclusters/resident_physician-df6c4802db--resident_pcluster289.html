resident physician

noun
physician in training working in a hospital
4.3 3.4
Pronunciation: /ˈre.zɪ.dənt fə.ˈzɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ˈre.zɪ.dənt fɪ.ˈzɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈre.zɪ.dənt fɪ.ˈzɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ˈre.zɪ.dənt fə.ˈzɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
A physician who lives and works in a hospital, undergoing specialist training and caring for patients under the supervision of senior medical staff.

Examples

Resident physician training
After medical school, she became a resident physician in internal medicine at a major hospital.
The resident physician checked the patient's vital signs every morning during his rotation.
The hospital relies on resident physicians to provide care at all hours, supervised by senior doctors.

Related Terms

  • antonym: attending physician - a physician who has completed training and supervises residents
  • synonym: intern - a physician in the first year of training after medical school
