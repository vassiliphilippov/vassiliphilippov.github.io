out of tune

adjective
not at correct musical pitch or harmony
3.3 0.61/m
Pronunciation: / a ät …ôv tun / (AmE), / a ät …ôv tjuÀên / (BrE)
not having the correct musical pitch or harmony; not matching or in accord with something

Examples

Out of tune piano
If the piano is out of tune, even a simple melody will sound unpleasant.
She realized her singing was out of tune and quickly adjusted her pitch.
The violin sounded out of tune during the concert and distracted the audience.

Synonyms

  • out of key
    5.8 0.04/m
    out of key describes a note or voice in the wrong pitch, while out of tune covers wider pitch or harmony problems.
  • off-key
    4.5 0.59/m
    off-key sounds sharp or harsh in singing, while out of tune describes any note or instrument missing correct pitch.
  • false
    6.1 0.19/m
    false is used for a single wrong musical note, while out of tune describes general bad pitch or poor harmony.
  • untuned
    5.3 0.02/m
    untuned describes instruments not set to the correct pitch, while out of tune can describe voices or whole groups sounding wrong.
  • unattuned
    7.5 0.00/m
    unattuned means not in harmony emotionally or musically, while out of tune focuses on wrong musical pitch.

Related Terms

  • antonym: in tune - having the correct musical pitch or harmony
