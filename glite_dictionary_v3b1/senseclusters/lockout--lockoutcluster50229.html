lockout

noun
employer action to bar workers during dispute
7.2 1.80/m
Pronunciation: / Ààl…ëÀêkÀåa ät / (AmE), / Ààl…íka ät / (BrE)
an occasion when an employer prevents employees from entering the workplace until they agree to certain terms or conditions, usually during an industrial dispute

Examples

Factory lockout
A lockout can put financial pressure on workers to accept the employer's demands.
After negotiations broke down, the company declared a lockout and shut the factory gates.
The union is preparing to take legal action in response to the lockout imposed by management.

Related Terms

  • antonym: strike - when workers stop working as a protest, often over pay or conditions
