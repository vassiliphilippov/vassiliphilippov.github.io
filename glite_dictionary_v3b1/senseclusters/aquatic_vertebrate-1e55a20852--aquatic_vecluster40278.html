aquatic vertebrate

noun
water-dwelling animal with a backbone
Pronunciation: / əˈkwɑtɪk ˈvɜrtɪˌbreɪt / (AmE), / əˈkwætɪk ˈvɜːtɪbrət / (BrE)
an animal with a backbone that lives wholly or mainly in water

Examples

Many aquatic vertebrates
Many aquatic vertebrates are adapted to extract oxygen from water using gills or lungs.
Salmon, dolphins, and frogs are all examples of aquatic vertebrates.
The exhibit at the aquarium focused on the diversity of aquatic vertebrates from around the world.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fish - cold-blooded aquatic vertebrate with gills and fins
