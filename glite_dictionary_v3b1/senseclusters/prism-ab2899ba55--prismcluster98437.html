PRISM

proper noun
U.S. surveillance program
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈprɪzəm / (BrE)
A U.S. government electronic-surveillance program (revealed 2013) that collects internet communications from major technology providers for intelligence purposes.

Examples

PRISM program
The whistleblower revealed that the PRISM program collects data from major tech companies.
Journalists examined leaked documents showing how PRISM allowed agencies to access emails and chats.
Civil liberties advocates protested after learning that PRISM could gather information about ordinary citizens.
