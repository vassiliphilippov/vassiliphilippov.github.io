witness

noun
person giving evidence in court
2.6
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈwɪtnəs / (BrE)
a person who gives testimony under oath in a court of law

Examples

Courtroom witness
Each witness must swear an oath before testifying in court.
The witness described what she had seen on the night of the robbery.
The defense attorney cross-examined the key witness during the trial.

Related Terms

  • antonym: defendant - a person being accused or sued in court
  • synonym: testifier - a person who gives testimony in a legal context
