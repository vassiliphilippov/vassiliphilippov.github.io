malcontent

noun
dissatisfied or rebellious person
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈmæl.kənˌtɛnt / (AmE), / ˈmæl.kən.tɛnt / (BrE)
a person who is dissatisfied or rebellious, often against established authority or prevailing conditions

Examples

A malcontent leader
He was dismissed as a malcontent who always complained about management, regardless of the situation.
The new regulations turned several employees into outspoken malcontents unwilling to accept the changes.
Throughout history, malcontents have often sparked significant social and political change.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contented - feeling or expressing happiness or satisfaction
  • synonym: rebel - a person who resists authority or control
