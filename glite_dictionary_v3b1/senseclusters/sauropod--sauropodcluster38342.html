sauropod

noun
large long-necked herbivorous dinosaur
9.9 0.58/m
Pronunciation: / Ààs…îÀêr…ôÀåp…ëd / (AmE), / Ààs…îÀêr…ôÀåp…íd / (BrE)
a very large herbivorous dinosaur from the Jurassic and Cretaceous periods with a small head, a long neck and tail, and five-toed limbs; among the largest known land animals

Examples

A sauropod dinosaur
Paleontologists discovered new sauropod fossils in the desert, shedding light on their evolution.
Scientists believe that the sauropod used its long neck to reach leaves from tall trees.
The sauropod skeleton on display towers above museum visitors, highlighting its enormous size.

Synonyms

  • Sauropoda
    9.9 0.00/m
    Sauropoda is the scientific name for the whole group of very large long-necked dinosaurs, while sauropod means one individual animal.
