cation

noun
positively charged ion
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkætaɪ.ɑn / (AmE), / ˈkætaɪ.ɒn / (BrE)
A positively charged ion formed by the loss of one or more electrons.

Examples

A cation ion
Potassium ions, as cations, play a vital role in nerve transmission.
Sodium becomes a cation when it loses an electron.
The solution contained several metal cations and chloride anions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anion - a negatively charged ion
