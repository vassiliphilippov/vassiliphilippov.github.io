baker's eczema

noun
allergic eczema from baking ingredients
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪkɚz ɛkˈsiːmə / (AmE), / ˈbeɪkəz ɪkˈsiːmə / (BrE)
a form of allergic eczema affecting bakers, caused by exposure to flour or other ingredients handled during baking

Examples

Baker's eczema on hands
After years of working in the bakery, she developed baker's eczema on her hands.
Doctors recommend wearing gloves to prevent baker's eczema in people sensitive to baking ingredients.
Frequent exposure to flour is a leading cause of baker's eczema among professional bakers.
