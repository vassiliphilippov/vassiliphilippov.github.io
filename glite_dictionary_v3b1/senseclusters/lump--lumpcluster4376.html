lump

verb
group together indiscriminately
9.9 6.1
Pronunciation: / lʌmp / (AmE), / lʌmp / (BrE)
to group things together without considering their differences

Examples

To lump things together
It's unfair to lump all teenagers together when every individual is different.
Some critics lump modern art with abstract art, though they are not the same.
The report tends to lump minor offenses with serious crimes.
Critics argue that the data should not be lumped into a single category.
People often lump these issues together, but they are actually quite different.
The report tends to lump all minor offenses together with serious crimes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: separate - to set or keep apart; not put together
