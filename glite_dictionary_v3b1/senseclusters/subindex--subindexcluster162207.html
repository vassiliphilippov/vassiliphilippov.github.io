subindex

noun
secondary index
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌɪndɛks / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌɪndɛks / (BrE)
a secondary or subordinate index that forms part of a larger index, often used to track specific categories or sectors

Examples

A subindex of sectors
The financial report breaks down performance by subindex to show trends in different industries.
Each subindex provides detailed information about a specific sector within the overall market index.
Analysts use the technology subindex to monitor changes in the tech industry separately from other sectors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: index - an organized list or measure, often used for reference or comparison
  • antonym: main index - the primary or overall index that includes subindexes
