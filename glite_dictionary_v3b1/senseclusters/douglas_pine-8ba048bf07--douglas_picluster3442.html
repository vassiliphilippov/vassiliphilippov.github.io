Douglas pine

noun
a tall evergreen tree of NW North America
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌɡləs paɪn / (AmE), / ˈdʌɡləs paɪn / (BrE)
A tall evergreen tree of northwestern North America, also known as Douglas fir, with short needles and egg-shaped cones.

Examples

Tall Douglas pine
Many forests in Oregon are dominated by Douglas pine, valued for its strong timber.
The needles of the Douglas pine are soft and have a distinct citrus scent.
We spotted a Douglas pine towering above the other trees along the hiking trail.

  • synonym: Douglas fir - another common name for the same tree species
