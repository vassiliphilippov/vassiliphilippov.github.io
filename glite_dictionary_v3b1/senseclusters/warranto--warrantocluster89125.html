warranto

noun
legal writ of authority
9.9
Pronunciation: / wɔːˈræntoʊ / (AmE), / wɒˈræntəʊ / (BrE)
a writ or legal action requiring someone to show by what authority they hold a public office or privilege

Examples

Court warranto order
The court issued a warranto to determine if the official was lawfully appointed.
A warranto was filed to challenge the company's right to operate under its current license.
The judge reviewed the warranto before making a decision about the disputed office.

Related Terms

  • synonym: injunction - a court order requiring someone to do or stop doing something
  • synonym: mandamus - a court order requiring a public official to perform a duty
