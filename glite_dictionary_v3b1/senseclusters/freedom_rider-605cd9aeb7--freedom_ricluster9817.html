freedom rider

noun
civil rights activist protesting segregation on buses
4.8 6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈfriː.dəm ˈraɪ.dər/ (AmE), /ˈfriː.dəm ˈraɪ.də/ (BrE)
a member of an interracial group of civil rights activists in the 1960s who rode interstate buses into the segregated southern United States to challenge racial segregation laws

Examples

Freedom rider activists
James Farmer was a prominent freedom rider during the 1960s Civil Rights Movement.
My grandfather spoke about meeting a freedom rider who inspired his views on equality.
The actions of the freedom riders drew national attention to racial segregation in the South.
