bleak

adjective
bare and inhospitable (of a place)
4.9 1.62/m
Pronunciation: / blik / (AmE), / bliÀêk / (BrE)
(of a place) exposed and empty, offering little or no shelter or sustenance; bare and inhospitable.

Examples

Bleak landscape
From the summit, he looked out over a bleak and empty landscape.
The wind swept across the bleak moor with nothing to break its force.
They set up camp in a bleak stretch of desert far from civilization.

Synonyms

  • desolate
    6.8 2.73/m
    desolate describes a place that is empty and abandoned, while bleak describes a cold, exposed place with little shelter or life.
  • barren
    4.6 4.42/m
    barren refers to land that cannot support plants or life, while bleak refers to a place that feels cold and empty.
  • stark
    4.8 2.43/m
    stark means very plain or severe in look, while bleak means empty and cold and often feels depressing or harsh.
  • bare
    2.2 22/m
    bare means simply not covered or lacking decoration, while bleak describes an exposed, lifeless place that offers little shelter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barren - (of land) too poor to produce much or any vegetation
  • antonym: fertile - (of land) producing many plants or crops
