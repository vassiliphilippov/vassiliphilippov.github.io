tritium

noun
radioactive hydrogen isotope
6.5 7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈtrɪ.tɪ.əm/ /; / /ˈtrɪ.tiː.əm/ (AmE), /ˈtrɪ.tɪ.əm/ /; / /ˈtrɪ.tiː.əm/ (BrE)
a radioactive isotope of hydrogen, with atoms that have one proton and two neutrons in the nucleus, three times the mass of ordinary hydrogen

Examples

Tritium radioactive isotope
Because tritium is radioactive, it must be handled with caution in the laboratory.
Nuclear fusion reactions often use tritium as a fuel along with deuterium.
Scientists carefully measure the decay of tritium to estimate the age of certain materials.
