bite one's lip

verb
suppress laughter or emotion
1.0 4.9
Pronunciation: /baɪt wʌnz lɪp/ /; / /baɪt wʌns lɪp/ /; / /baɪt wənz lɪp/ (AmE), /baɪt wʌnz lɪp/ /; / /baɪt wʌns lɪp/ /; / /baɪt wənz lɪp/ (BrE)
to suppress laughter, emotion, or words, typically by pressing one's lips together to avoid expressing them

Examples

To bite his lip tightly
He bit his lip and said nothing as the criticism continued.
Maria was biting her lip to prevent herself from crying in front of everyone.
She had to bite her lip to keep from laughing during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: let it out - to express emotions openly
