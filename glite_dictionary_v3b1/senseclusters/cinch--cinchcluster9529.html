cinch

verb
secure a saddle on a horse
Pronunciation: / sɪntʃ / (AmE), / sɪntʃ / (BrE)
to fasten or tighten something securely, especially by pulling a strap or belt

Examples

To cinch a belt
Before the long ride, the groom cinched each of the horses securely.
He carefully cinched the saddle before mounting the horse.
Make sure to cinch the strap tightly so the saddle doesn't slip.
She cinched her belt before stepping onto the stage to perform.
Make sure to cinch the straps on your helmet for a safe fit.
He cinched the drawstring bag tightly so nothing would fall out.
Before riding the horse, she carefully cinched the saddle strap to keep it in place.
Make sure to cinch your belt so your pants do not fall down during the run.
He cinched the drawstring on his bag to prevent anything from falling out.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fasten - to attach or secure something firmly
  • synonym: girth - to secure with a girth (a broad strap around a horse)
  • antonym: loosen - to make something less tight or secure
