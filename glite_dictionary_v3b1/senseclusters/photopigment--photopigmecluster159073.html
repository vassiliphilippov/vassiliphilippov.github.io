photopigment

noun
pigment in the eye's retina for sensing light
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊtəˌpɪɡmənt / (AmE), / ˈfəʊtəʊˌpɪɡmənt / (BrE)
a pigment in the rods and cones of the retina that absorbs light and initiates the process of vision

Examples

Eye photopigment
Exposure to bright light can cause temporary changes in the sensitivity of photopigment in the eye.
Research into photopigment mutations has helped us understand color blindness.
The retina contains several types of photopigment that respond to different wavelengths of light.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rhodopsin - a photopigment found in rod cells of the retina
