common jasmine

noun
fragrant climbing shrub, Jasminum officinale
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmən ˈdʒæzmɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈdʒæzmɪn / (BrE)
A climbing deciduous shrub (Jasminum officinale) with fragrant white, yellow, or red flowers, often used in perfumery and to flavor tea.

Examples

Common jasmine flowers
Common jasmine is often grown on trellises for its beautiful flowers and pleasant scent.
She uses dried common jasmine flowers to infuse a delicate aroma into her tea.
The sweet fragrance of common jasmine filled the evening air as we walked through the garden.
