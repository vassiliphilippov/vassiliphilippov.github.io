reconditioning

noun
restoring physical fitness
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkənˈdɪʃənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˈdɪʃənɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of restoring physical fitness, strength, or ability through exercise or rehabilitation after injury or illness

Examples

Physical reconditioning
After surgery, the patient started reconditioning to regain muscle strength and flexibility.
Athletes often undergo reconditioning programs following a serious injury to return to competition.
The clinic specializes in reconditioning for people recovering from long-term illnesses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rehabilitation - the process of helping someone recover physical or mental abilities
