Crataegus laevigata

noun
Eurasian shrub, Midland hawthorn
9.9
Pronunciation: / krəˈtiːɡəs ˌliːvəˈɡeɪtə / (AmE), / krəˈtiːɡəs ˌliːvɪˈɡɑːtə / (BrE)
A thorny Eurasian shrub or small tree with dense clusters of white to scarlet flowers and deep red berries, also called Midland hawthorn.

Examples

Crataegus laevigata shrub
Botanists identified the shrub as Crataegus laevigata due to its distinctive leaf shape.
Gardeners often plant Crataegus laevigata for its attractive spring blossoms.
The Crataegus laevigata in the park was covered in vibrant red berries.
