unforgiving

adjective
not willing to forgive; harsh
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌʌn.fərˈɡɪ.vɪŋ /; / ˌʌn.fərˈɡɪv.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌn.fəˈɡɪv.ɪŋ / (BrE)
not willing to forgive or show mercy; harsh and unyielding (of people, situations, or things)

Examples

An unforgiving attitude
He faced the consequences of his mistake in an unforgiving manner.
Her unforgiving nature made it hard for her to maintain friendships over time.
The unforgiving look in his eyes made everyone uncomfortable.
She faced the unforgiving reality of her new responsibilities.
The coach is notoriously unforgiving when it comes to mistakes on the field.
The mountain terrain is unforgiving, challenging even for experienced climbers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: forgiving - willing to forgive or show mercy
  • synonym: harsh - cruel or severe in manner
  • antonym: merciful - showing compassion or forgiveness
  • synonym: merciless - showing no mercy or pity
