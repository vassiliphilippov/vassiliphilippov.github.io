recodification

noun
rewriting laws or rules
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌkoʊdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌkəʊdɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of rewriting or reorganizing laws, rules, or codes to make them clearer or more effective

Examples

Legal recodification
The committee recommended a complete recodification of the city’s building regulations.
After years of confusion, the school board approved the recodification of its disciplinary policies.
Legal experts debated the impact of the recent recodification on contract law.

Related Terms

  • synonym: codification - the process of arranging laws or rules into a systematic code
  • synonym: revision - the act of changing or improving something
