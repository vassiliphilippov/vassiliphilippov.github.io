fishing line

noun
strong cord used in angling
1.0 2.0
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪʃɪŋ laɪn / (AmE), / ˈfɪʃɪŋ laɪn / (BrE)
A strong, thin cord used for angling, to which the hook, sinker, float, and leader are attached.

Examples

A strong fishing line
A strong fishing line is essential for catching large fish.
He replaced the old fishing line before heading out to the lake.
She untangled the fishing line after it got caught in the reeds.
