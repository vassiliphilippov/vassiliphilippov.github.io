ionized

adjective
converted into ions
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪəˌnaɪzd /; / ˈaɪ.əˌnaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈaɪənaɪzd /; / ˈaɪ.əˌnaɪzd / (BrE)
(of a substance) converted wholly or partly into ions, typically by chemical or physical means such as removing or adding electrons, exposure to radiation, or electrical discharge.

Examples

Ionized water
The ionized water was used in the laboratory experiment.
The gas in the chamber became highly ionized after the electrical discharge.
Ultraviolet light can produce ionized particles in the upper atmosphere.
Drinking water can be ionised through a process involving electrolysis.
Scientists measured the ionised gas in the laboratory to study its properties.
The air near the waterfall is highly ionised, which is thought to have health benefits.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ionized - US spelling of 'ionised'
  • antonym: neutral - not ionized; having no net electrical charge
  • antonym: nonionised - not converted into ions; in molecular form
