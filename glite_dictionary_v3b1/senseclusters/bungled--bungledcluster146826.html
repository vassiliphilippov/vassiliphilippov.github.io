bungled

adjective
spoiled by incompetence or clumsiness
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌŋɡəld / (AmE), / ˈbʌŋɡəld / (BrE)
Spoiled or ruined through incompetence, clumsiness, or poor handling.

Examples

A bungled job
Due to a bungled response to the emergency, the problem grew worse.
Her bungled attempt at repairing the computer only made things worse.
The construction of the bridge was a bungled job that led to safety concerns.

Related Terms

  • synonym: botched - spoiled or badly done through incompetence
  • antonym: flawless - without any mistakes or faults
