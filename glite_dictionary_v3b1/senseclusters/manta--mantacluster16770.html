manta

noun
large tropical ray (fish)
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈmæntə / (AmE), / ˈmæntə / (BrE)
A very large species of tropical ray with triangular pectoral fins, which feeds on plankton and small fish and is generally harmless.

Examples

A manta ray
Divers were thrilled to spot a manta gliding gracefully through the clear blue water.
Harpooning a manta is dangerous because of its massive size and power.
The manta can reach a width of over six meters, making it one of the largest rays in the ocean.
