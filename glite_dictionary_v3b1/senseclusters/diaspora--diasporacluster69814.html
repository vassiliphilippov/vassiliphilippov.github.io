diaspora

noun
dispersion of a people or culture
8.7
Pronunciation: / daɪˈæspərə / (AmE), / daɪˈæspərə / (BrE)
the movement, scattering, or spread of a people or culture from their original homeland to other places (especially used for the Jewish people living outside Israel)

Examples

The diaspora community
Recent decades have seen a growing African diaspora in Europe and the Americas.
The Jewish diaspora has communities all over the world.
The language survived in the diaspora long after it disappeared from its original homeland.
Many cities developed vibrant communities as part of the diaspora following the destruction of the Second Temple.
The Jewish diaspora has maintained its identity through centuries of living in foreign lands.
The history curriculum included a detailed discussion of the diaspora and its effects on Jewish culture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exile - the state of being barred from one's native country
  • synonym: migration - the movement of people from one place to another
