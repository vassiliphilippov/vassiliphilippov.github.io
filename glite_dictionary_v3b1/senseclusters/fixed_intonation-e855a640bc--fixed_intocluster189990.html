fixed intonation

noun
unchangeable pitch system in musical instruments
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪkst ˌɪntoʊˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈfɪkst ˌɪntəˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a system in which each note of a musical instrument, such as a keyboard, has a fixed pitch that cannot be altered by the performer during playing

Examples

Fixed intonation system
Most pianos have fixed intonation, so players cannot bend notes as they can on a violin.
Some musicians prefer instruments without fixed intonation because they can express subtle changes in pitch.
The concept of fixed intonation is crucial when comparing keyboard instruments to wind or stringed instruments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: variable intonation - a system in which the pitch of notes can be altered by the performer
