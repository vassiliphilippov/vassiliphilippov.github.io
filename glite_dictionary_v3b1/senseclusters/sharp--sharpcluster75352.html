sharp

adjective
sudden and intense (change)
2.1 1.7
Pronunciation: /ʃɑːrp/ (AmE), /ʃɑːp/ (BrE)
happening very suddenly or with great intensity; abrupt or steep

Examples

Sharp increase
A sharp rise in rent forced many families to move out.
There was a sharp increase in temperature overnight.
We noticed a sharp decline in the company's profits this quarter.
She felt a sharp pain in her arm after bumping into the table.
The boxer delivered a sharp jab to his opponent's chin.
The teacher gave a sharp reprimand to the noisy students.
A sharp incline made cycling up the hill quite challenging.
Be careful—there is a sharp drop just beyond the edge of the trail.
The mountain path became so sharp that we had to use our hands to climb.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gradual - happening slowly over a period of time
  • synonym: steep - very inclined or near vertical (of a slope)
  • synonym: sudden - occurring or done quickly and unexpectedly
