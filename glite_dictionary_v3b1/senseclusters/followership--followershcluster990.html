followership

noun
supportive group role
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑloʊɚˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊəʃɪp / (BrE)
the ability or willingness to follow a leader or group, especially as a supportive member

Examples

Strong followership
Good followership is just as important as strong leadership in any organization.
She demonstrated excellent followership by supporting her manager's decisions and helping the team succeed.
Workshops on followership can help employees become more effective team members.

Related Terms

  • antonym: leadership - the action or ability of leading a group or organization
  • synonym: teamwork - the combined action of a group, especially when effective and efficient
