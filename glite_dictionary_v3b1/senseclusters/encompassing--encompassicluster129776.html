encompassing

adjective
including many things; broad in scope
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkʌmpəsɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪnˈkʌmpəsɪŋ / (BrE)
including a wide range of people, ideas, or things; covering or affecting everything or everyone within its scope.

Examples

Encompassing knowledge
Her interests are so encompassing that she can talk about nearly any subject.
The report offers an encompassing overview of the current economic situation.
We support an encompassing approach to solving environmental problems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: comprehensive - including all or nearly all elements or aspects
  • antonym: limited - restricted in size, amount, or extent
