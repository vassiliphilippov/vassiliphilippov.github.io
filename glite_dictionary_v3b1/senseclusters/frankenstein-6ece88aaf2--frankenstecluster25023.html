Frankenstein

noun
a creation that harms its creator
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈfræŋkənˌstaɪn / (AmE), / ˈfraŋkənˌstaɪn / (BrE)
a monster or creation that escapes the control of its creator and causes harm to them (originally, the creature made by Victor Frankenstein in Mary Shelley's novel)

Examples

A Frankenstein monster
His once-promising invention turned into a Frankenstein that ruined his career.
Some fear that artificial intelligence could become a modern Frankenstein if left unchecked.
The new technology became a Frankenstein, spiraling out of control and threatening its inventors.
After reading the novel, she understood why people sympathize with Frankenstein despite his terrifying appearance.
In popular culture, Frankenstein is often depicted as a green-skinned giant with bolts in his neck.
Many people mistakenly call the monster Frankenstein, though that was actually the name of its creator.

Related Terms

  • synonym: monster - a large, frightening imaginary creature; used figuratively for anything powerful and uncontrollable
