settled

adjective
inhabited by colonists or residents
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛt̬.əld / (AmE), / ˈsɛt.əld / (BrE)
(of a place) inhabited or established by colonists or permanent residents.

Examples

Settled area
Many indigenous communities were displaced when the region became settled.
The settled areas had more developed infrastructure than the surrounding wilderness.
The eastern coast was quickly settled by European colonists in the 17th century.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unsettled - not inhabited by colonists or permanent residents
