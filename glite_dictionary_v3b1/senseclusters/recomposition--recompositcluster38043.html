recomposition

noun
new work from existing
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌkɑːmpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌkɒmpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of creating a new musical or artistic work by rearranging, altering, or reusing an existing composition

Examples

Artistic recomposition
The artist's recomposition of the classic painting brought new life to the old masterpiece.
Her recomposition of the folk song introduced electronic elements and a faster tempo.
Many modern albums feature at least one recomposition of a well-known melody.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arrangement - a new version of a musical or artistic work
  • antonym: original - a work created for the first time, not based on another
  • synonym: remix - a reworked or altered version of an existing song or piece
