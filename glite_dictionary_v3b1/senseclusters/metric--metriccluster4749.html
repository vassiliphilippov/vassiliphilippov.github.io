metric

noun
unit or system of measurement based on the metric system
3.8 3.1
Pronunciation: /ˈme.trɪk/ (AmE), /ˈme.trɪk/ (BrE)
a unit of measurement or a system of measurements based on the metric system, which uses meters, kilograms, and seconds as fundamental units

Examples

Metric units
Schools in the UK now teach children to use metric units rather than imperial.
Scientists prefer to collect data using the metric system for consistency.
The recipe gives all ingredient amounts in metric measures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: imperial - a system of weights and measures formerly used officially in the UK, contrasting with metric
