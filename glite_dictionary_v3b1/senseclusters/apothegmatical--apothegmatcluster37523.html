apothegmatical

adjective
concise and characteristic of terse sayings
Pronunciation: / əˌpɑθɪɡˈmætɪkəl / (AmE), / əˌpɒθɪɡˈmatɪkəl / (BrE)
Terse, concise, and characteristic of or given to apothegms (short, witty sayings).

Examples

An apothegmatical phrase
Her apothegmatical style left a strong impression in just a few words.
His apothegmatical responses often ended debates before they began.
The professor's lectures were apothegmatical, filled with clever, pithy remarks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aphoristic - expressed in or resembling an aphorism; terse and witty
  • antonym: verbose - using more words than needed; wordy
