maximin

noun
decision rule for worst case
Pronunciation: / ˈmæksɪˌmɪn / (AmE), / ˈmæksɪˌmɪn / (BrE)
a principle or strategy in decision-making or game theory that seeks to maximize the minimum possible outcome or benefit

Examples

Maximin principle use
The policy was designed according to the maximin principle to ensure the poorest citizens benefited most.
In game theory, players may use maximin strategies to guard against the worst possible outcomes.
Rawls's theory of justice famously incorporates the maximin rule to address social inequality.
In competitive games, players often use a maximin strategy to ensure the best possible outcome in the worst case.
The economist explained how the maximin principle can guide fair resource distribution.
By applying a maximin approach, the team guaranteed they would not fall below a certain performance level.
