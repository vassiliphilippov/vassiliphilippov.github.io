Windsor spectacles

noun
round-framed eyeglasses style
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪn.zər ˈspɛk.tə.kəlz / (AmE), / ˈwɪn.zə ˈspɛk.tə.klz / (BrE)
A style of eyeglasses with round frames, often featuring thin rims and a simple bridge, popularized in the early 20th century and associated with figures such as John Lennon.

Examples

Vintage Windsor spectacles
His vintage Windsor spectacles gave him a distinctive, intellectual look.
Many fashion designers have revived Windsor spectacles in their eyewear collections.
She always wears her signature Windsor spectacles, reminiscent of John Lennon's iconic style.
