aristoi

noun
elite social class
Pronunciation: / ˈærɪstɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈærɪstɔɪ / (BrE)
the privileged or elite class of people, especially in ancient Greece

Examples

The aristoi class
In ancient Athens, the aristoi often made important decisions for the city.
The aristoi were respected for their education and leadership in society.
Many reforms aimed to reduce the power of the aristoi and give more rights to ordinary citizens.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aristocracy - the highest social class, often holding hereditary titles or offices
  • antonym: commoners - people who are not part of the elite or privileged class
