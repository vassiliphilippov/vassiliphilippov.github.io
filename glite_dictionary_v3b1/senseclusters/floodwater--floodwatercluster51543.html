floodwater

noun
water from flooding
Pronunciation: / ˈflʌdˌwɔtər / (AmE), / ˈflʌdˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
water that covers land because of a flood or heavy rain

Examples

Rising floodwater
The rising floodwater forced residents to evacuate their homes overnight.
After the storm, floodwater covered the fields and damaged the crops.
Volunteers worked together to clear debris left by the receding floodwater.
The rising floodwater forced residents to evacuate their homes overnight.
After the storm, floodwater covered the fields and made the roads impassable.
Volunteers worked together to build barriers against the advancing floodwater.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deluge - a great flood of water or a heavy downpour
  • antonym: drought - a long period with little or no rain
  • synonym: flood - an overflow of water that submerges land
