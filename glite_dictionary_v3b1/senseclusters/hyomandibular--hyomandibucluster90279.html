hyomandibular

adjective
jaw-to-skull bone
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪoʊmænˈdɪbjələr / (AmE), / ˌhaɪəʊmænˈdɪbjʊlə / (BrE)
relating to the bone or cartilage that connects the jaw to the skull in certain vertebrates, especially fishes

Examples

Hyomandibular bone structure
The hyomandibular bone plays a crucial role in the jaw movement of many fish species.
Scientists studied the hyomandibular structure to understand the evolution of vertebrate skulls.
Damage to the hyomandibular cartilage can affect how a fish opens its mouth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mandibular - relating to the jaw or jawbone
