improvisational

adjective
unplanned; makeshift
Pronunciation: / ɪmˌprɑːvɪˈzeɪʃənəl / (AmE), / ɪmˌprɒvɪˈzeɪʃənəl / (BrE)
made, performed, or done without prior planning or preparation; spontaneous

Examples

An improvisational performance
The actors gave an improvisational performance, reacting to the audience's suggestions in real time.
Her improvisational cooking style meant she rarely followed recipes exactly.
During the blackout, they used an improvisational lamp made from a jar and a candle.
The comedian's improvisational style kept the audience laughing throughout the show.
She joined an improvisational theater group to improve her acting skills.
The musicians gave an improvisational performance, creating new melodies on the spot.
