fuffle

verb
to fuss nervously
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈfʌf.əl/ (AmE), /ˈfʌf.əl/ (BrE)
to act in a nervous or fussy way, showing unnecessary agitation or making a small fuss

Examples

To fuffle nervously
She began to fuffle with her notes before giving her presentation to the class.
Whenever he is anxious, he tends to fuffle by rearranging objects on his desk.
Try not to fuffle so much before interviews; just take a deep breath and stay calm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fuss - to show unnecessary or excessive concern or excitement
  • antonym: relax - to become calm and free from agitation
