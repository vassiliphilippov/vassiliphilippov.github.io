stooge

noun
person targeted for ridicule or pranks
7.9 1.37/m
Pronunciation: / stuÀêd í / (AmE), / stuÀêd í / (BrE)
a person who is used by others, either as the target of jokes or as someone who does what they are told without question

Examples

Office stooge
He always ended up as the stooge in their elaborate office pranks.
Nobody likes being the stooge of the group, always getting laughed at.
The comedian picked a random audience member to act as the stooge in his show.
He acted like a stooge for the corrupt politician, never questioning any order.
She refused to be anyone's stooge and stood up for what she believed.
The company needs independent thinkers, not just stooges who do as they're told.
