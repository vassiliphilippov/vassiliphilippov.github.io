subduct

verb
to deduct or remove
8.2
Pronunciation: / səbˈdʌkt / (AmE), / səbˈdʌkt / (BrE)
to remove, take away, or deduct an amount from a total

Examples

To subduct costs
The accountant will subduct the expenses from the total revenue to calculate the profit.
Please subduct any discounts before submitting the final bill.
They agreed to subduct the damaged items from the shipment's value.

Related Terms

  • antonym: add - to put together or increase an amount
  • synonym: deduct - to subtract or take away an amount from a total
