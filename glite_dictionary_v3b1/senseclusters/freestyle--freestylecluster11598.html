freestyle

verb
to improvise performance
Pronunciation: / ˈfriːstaɪl / (AmE), / ˈfriːstaɪl / (BrE)
to perform or improvise music, dance, or speech without prior preparation or a set plan

Examples

To freestyle a song
During the dance battle, she started freestyling impressive moves that amazed the crowd.
He loves to freestyle on the guitar, making up melodies as he plays.
The comedian began freestyling jokes based on suggestions from the audience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: improvise - to create or perform spontaneously without preparation
  • antonym: rehearse - to practice or prepare something before performing
