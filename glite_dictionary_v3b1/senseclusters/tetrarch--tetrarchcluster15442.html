tetrarch

noun
ruler of a quarter
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛtrɑrk / (AmE), / ˈtɛtrɑːk / (BrE)
a ruler of one of four divisions of a country or province, especially in ancient times

Examples

An ancient tetrarch
In ancient Rome, each tetrarch governed a separate part of the empire.
The four tetrarchs met to discuss the future of their divided territory.
A tetrarch was responsible for maintaining order in his assigned region.
The Roman Empire was once divided, and each tetrarch controlled a separate region.
As a tetrarch, he was responsible for maintaining order in his quarter of the province.
Historians studied the reign of the tetrarch to understand ancient systems of government.

Related Terms

  • synonym: governor - an official in charge of a region or province
  • antonym: monarch - a sole ruler of a kingdom or empire
