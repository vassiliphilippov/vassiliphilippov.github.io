two-handed saw

noun
saw operated by two people
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˌtuː ˈhændɪd sɔ / (AmE), / ˌtuː ˈhændɪd sɔː / (BrE)
a long saw with handles at both ends, designed to be operated by two people, typically for cutting wood

Examples

A two-handed saw tool
A two-handed saw is ideal for felling large trees and making precise cuts.
It takes teamwork to operate a two-handed saw efficiently in the forest.
The lumberjacks used a two-handed saw to cut through the massive tree trunk.
