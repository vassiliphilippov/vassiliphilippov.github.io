enrich the soil

verb
make soil more fertile or rich
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈrɪtʃ ðə sɔɪl / (AmE), / ɪnˈrɪtʃ ðə sɔɪl / (BrE)
to improve the quality of soil by adding nutrients or organic material, making it more fertile

Examples

To enrich the soil with compost
Farmers often enrich the soil with compost to ensure healthy crop growth.
Organic gardeners prefer to enrich the soil using natural fertilizers like manure.
Regularly enriching the soil can lead to bigger harvests and healthier plants.
