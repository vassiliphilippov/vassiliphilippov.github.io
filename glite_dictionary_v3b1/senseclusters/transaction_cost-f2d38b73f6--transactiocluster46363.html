transaction cost

noun
cost incurred in making a market exchange
3.1
Pronunciation: / trænˈzækʃən kɒst / (AmE), / trænˈzækʃən kɒst / (BrE)
the expenses incurred during the process of buying or selling goods or services, including costs such as information gathering, negotiation, and enforcement of agreements.

Examples

High transaction cost
Before choosing a new supplier, companies must consider the transaction cost involved in the switch.
In some cases, the transaction cost of selling an asset can exceed the benefit of the sale.
Modern technology can significantly reduce the transaction cost associated with online purchases.

Related Terms

  • synonym: opportunity cost - the loss of potential gain from other alternatives when one choice is made
