Alliaria

noun
genus of herbs with white flowers
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌæliˈɛriə / (AmE), / ˌalɪˈɑːrɪə / (BrE)
A genus of flowering herbs in the family Brassicaceae (Cruciferae), known for broad leaves, white flowers, and long seed pods.

Examples

Botanists have studied the reproductive cycle of Alliaria in temperate forests.
The species Alliaria petiolata, or garlic mustard, is considered invasive in some regions.
