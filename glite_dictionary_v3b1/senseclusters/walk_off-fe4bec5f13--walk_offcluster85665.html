walk off

verb
leave suddenly or abruptly
3.5 2.73/m
Pronunciation: / w…ëk …îf / (AmE), / w…îÀêk …íf / (BrE)
to leave a place or situation, often suddenly or in a way that shows annoyance or disapproval

Examples

To walk off angrily
After the argument, Tom walked off without saying a word.
She got upset and walked off in the middle of the meeting.
The referee walked over to the player, who simply walked off the field.

Synonyms

  • leave with a huff
    7.8 0.00/m
    leave with a huff means leaving suddenly to show clear anger, while walk off can mean leaving in annoyance or disapproval.
  • storm off
    4.5 0.61/m
    storm off means leaving quickly in strong anger or rage, while walk off often shows annoyance or quiet disapproval.
  • walk out
    3.4 3.90/m
    walk out means leaving an event suddenly often as protest or anger, while walk off is more general leaving in annoyance.
  • walk out of
    3.1 4.63/m
    walk out of means leaving a meeting or event to protest, while walk off describes leaving a place to show annoyance.
  • shove off
    7.7 0.30/m
    shove off is an informal rude way to leave a place quickly, while walk off focuses on leaving in annoyance or disapproval.
  • blow
    8.4 3.90/m
    blow is informal and means leaving quickly without warning, while walk off implies leaving to show annoyance or disapproval.
