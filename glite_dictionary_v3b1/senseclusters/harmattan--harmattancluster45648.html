harmattan

noun
dry, dusty West African wind
Pronunciation: / hɑrˈmætən / (AmE), / hɑːˈmætən / (BrE)
a dry, dusty wind that blows from the Sahara over West Africa, typically during the winter months

Examples

The harmattan wind
Farmers in the region prepare their fields before the harmattan arrives each year.
The harmattan covered the city in a layer of fine, red dust every December.
Visibility on the roads drops sharply when the harmattan blows across northern Nigeria.
