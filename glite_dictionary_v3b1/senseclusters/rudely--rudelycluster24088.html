rudely

adverb
in an impolite manner
1.3 1.56/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàruÀêdli / (AmE), / ÀàruÀêdli / (BrE)
In an impolite or disrespectful manner.

Examples

To rudely interrupt
He interrupted the speaker rudely during the meeting, which shocked everyone in the room.
She rudely turned her back on him while he was still talking.
The customer spoke rudely to the waiter and demanded special treatment.

Synonyms

  • impolitely
    4.2 0.05/m
    impolitely describes a lack of good manners in words or actions, while rudely covers a wider disrespectful tone.
  • discourteously
    6.9 0.02/m
    discourteously sounds more formal and is used in writing or reports, while rudely fits casual speech and everyday actions.
  • disrespectfully
    4.4 0.14/m
    disrespectfully shows clear lack of respect toward someone or their role, while rudely can mean simple bad manners.
  • insolently
    7.6 0.14/m
    insolently means boldly showing contempt toward authority or people, while rudely is more general bad manners or blunt behavior.
  • impertinently
    7.6 0.04/m
    impertinently is cheeky or disrespectful in a talkative way, while rudely covers many forms of impolite action.
  • insultingly
    5.9 0.15/m
    insultingly aims to offend with hurtful words or acts, while rudely may be impolite without a desire to hurt someone.

Related Terms

  • synonym: impolitely - in an impolite or rude manner
  • antonym: politely - in a courteous or respectful manner
