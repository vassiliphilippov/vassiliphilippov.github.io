caucus

noun
closed political party meeting
7.3 6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈkɔː.kəs/ /; / /ˈkɑː.kəs/ (AmE), /ˈkɔː.kəs/ (BrE)
A meeting of members of a political party or group, typically held to select candidates, decide policy, or coordinate strategy, usually closed to outsiders.

Examples

Party caucus meeting
After a heated caucus, they finally agreed on a candidate to support.
She attended the caucus every year to have her voice heard in party decisions.
The party members held a caucus to discuss their strategy for the upcoming election.

Related Terms

  • synonym: convention - a large formal assembly of people, especially political party members or delegates
