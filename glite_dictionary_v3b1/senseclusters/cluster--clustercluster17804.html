Synonyms

  • clump
    4.4 0.3/m
    clump is to come together into a compact, thick group, while cluster is a more general way to name a group.
  • bunch
    1.2 1/m
    bunch is to gather into a loose, often informal group, while cluster suggests a tighter, more compact grouping.
  • group
    1.2 2/m
    group is to arrange people or things into sets for a reason, while cluster means they form a close natural collection.
  • congregate
    4.1 1/m
    congregate is to come together for a particular purpose or meeting, while cluster simply describes people gathering closely.
  • crowd together
    3.0 0.1/m
    crowd together is to gather closely in large numbers, while cluster can be smaller and used for things or people.
  • throng
    6.4 0.3/m

  • flock
