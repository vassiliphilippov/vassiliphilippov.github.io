cluster

verb
gather closely together
3.5 6.08/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆklÊŒstÉš / (AmE), / ËˆklÊŒstÉ™ / (BrE)
to come together or gather in a close group, or to cause things or people to do this

Examples

To cluster around friends
After the concert, fans clustered around the stage, hoping for an autograph.
Bees cluster around the hive entrance on warm afternoons.
During the emergency, people clustered near the exits, waiting for instructions.
The children clustered together to look at the strange insect.
Tourists often cluster around famous landmarks for photos.
Wildflowers cluster along the edge of the forest each spring.

Synonyms

  • gather
    1.0 65/m
    gather is to bring people or things together into one place, while cluster often means they form a tight, close group.
  • clump
    4.4 0.91/m
    clump is to come together into a compact, thick group, while cluster is a more general way to name a group.
  • bunch
    1.2 2.52/m
    bunch is to gather into a loose, often informal group, while cluster suggests a tighter, more compact grouping.
  • group
    1.2 6.92/m
    group is to arrange people or things into sets for a reason, while cluster means they form a close natural collection.
  • congregate
    4.1 2.55/m
    congregate is to come together for a particular purpose or meeting, while cluster simply describes people gathering closely.
  • crowd together
    3.0 0.28/m
    crowd together is to gather closely in large numbers, while cluster can be smaller and used for things or people.
  • throng
    6.4 0.78/m
    throng is to press closely in a busy place with many people, while cluster is a neutral term for a close group.
  • flock
    2.0 5.18/m
    flock is to move together toward something, often following an attraction, while cluster means simply to form a group.
  • mass
    4.8 2.78/m
    mass is to assemble into a large group often for a purpose, while cluster can mean a smaller, tight group.
  • come together
    2.1 2.38/m
    come together is to move toward each other and meet, often physically or emotionally, while cluster focuses on forming a close group.
