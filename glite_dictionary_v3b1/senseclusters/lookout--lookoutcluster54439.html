lookout

noun
person who keeps watch for something
3.6 3.1
Pronunciation: /ˈlʊk.aʊt/ (AmE), /ˈlʊk.aʊt/ (BrE)
a person whose job is to watch for something expected or warn others of danger or trouble

Examples

Ship lookout
A sharp-eyed lookout spotted the ship on the horizon hours before anyone else.
During the night, a lookout was stationed by the campfire to alert the group to any danger.
The gang posted a lookout at the corner to warn them of approaching police.
