fudge

verb
evade or avoid directly addressing
5.9
Pronunciation: / fʌdʒ / (AmE), / fʌdʒ / (BrE)
to avoid or try to avoid dealing with a duty, question, or issue directly; to be deliberately vague or evasive

Examples

To fudge an answer
He tends to fudge his responsibilities when things get complicated at work.
Instead of answering the question directly, the politician chose to fudge and change the subject.
She tried to fudge her way through the interview without giving a clear answer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dodge - to avoid (a question, issue, or duty) by being evasive
  • synonym: evade - to escape or avoid (something), especially by cleverness or trickery
