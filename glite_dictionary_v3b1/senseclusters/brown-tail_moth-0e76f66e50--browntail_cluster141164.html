brown-tail moth

noun
a European pest moth species
Pronunciation: / ˈbraʊn taɪl mɑθ / (AmE), / ˈbraʊn tʌɪl mɒθ / (BrE)
a small brown and white European moth, introduced to eastern North America, that is a pest of various shade and fruit trees

Examples

The brown-tail moth pest
Efforts to control the spread of the brown-tail moth include removing nests during the winter.
Infestations of the brown-tail moth have caused severe defoliation in several orchards.
The caterpillars of the brown-tail moth can irritate human skin with their tiny hairs.
