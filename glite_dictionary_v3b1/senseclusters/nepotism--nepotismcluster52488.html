nepotism

noun
favoritism toward relatives or friends
6.2 5.7
Pronunciation: /ˈne.pə.tɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈne.pə.tɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the practice of giving unfair advantages, such as jobs or promotions, to relatives or close friends, especially by those in power

Examples

Nepotism in hiring
Many organizations have policies to guard against nepotism and ensure fair hiring practices.
Public outrage grew as evidence of nepotism within the government came to light.
The company was accused of nepotism after the manager hired his son for a key position.

Related Terms

  • synonym: favoritism - the practice of giving unfair preferential treatment to one person or group
  • antonym: meritocracy - a system where people are chosen and moved into positions of success, power, and influence on the basis of their abilities and merit
