allo-

x
combining form: other
Pronunciation: / ˈæloʊ / (AmE), / ˈaləʊ / (BrE)
a prefix meaning 'other' or 'different', used especially in scientific, medical, and linguistic terms

Examples

Allo- prefix meaning other
The term allo-immune describes an immune response to antigens from another individual of the same species.
An allo-graft is a tissue transplant from a donor of the same species but with a different genetic makeup.
Researchers studied allo-antibodies that react against non-self cells in blood transfusions.
In linguistics, allo- is used to form terms like allophone and allomorph.
The prefix allo- helps describe alternative forms of a single linguistic unit.
Words beginning with allo- often refer to variation or difference within a category.
The prefix allo- is used in words like 'allopatric', meaning occurring in separate areas.
In linguistics, allo- appears in 'allophone', referring to different sounds of a phoneme.
Scientists use allo- to form terms that indicate something is different or other.
