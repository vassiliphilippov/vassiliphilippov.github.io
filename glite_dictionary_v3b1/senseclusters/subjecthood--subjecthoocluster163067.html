subjecthood

noun
status as a subject
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbdʒɛkthʊd / (AmE), / ˈsʌbdʒɛkthʊd / (BrE)
the legal or political status of being a subject who owes allegiance to a state or ruler

Examples

Subjecthood under the king
After the conquest, many residents sought subjecthood of the new king to secure protection.
Debates about subjecthood often arise when discussing colonial history and allegiance.
The law clearly defined the rights and responsibilities associated with subjecthood in the kingdom.

Related Terms

  • synonym: citizenship - the status of being a citizen with rights and duties in a state
  • antonym: sovereignty - supreme power or authority, especially of a state
