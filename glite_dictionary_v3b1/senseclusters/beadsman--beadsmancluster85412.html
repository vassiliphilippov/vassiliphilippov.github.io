beadsman

noun
man paid to pray for another's soul
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪdzmən / (AmE), / ˈbiːdzmən / (BrE)
a man who was paid or supported to pray for the soul of another, especially in former times

Examples

A beadsman prays
Historically, a beadsman often lived near a church or monastery.
In medieval England, a beadsman would pray daily for the soul of his benefactor.
The wealthy nobleman endowed a beadsman to ensure his spiritual welfare after death.
