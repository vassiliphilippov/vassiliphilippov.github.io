counterculturist

noun
member of counterculture
Pronunciation: / ˌkaʊntərˈkʌltʃərɪst / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntəkˈʌltʃərɪst / (BrE)
a person who actively supports or belongs to a counterculture, often opposing mainstream values or norms

Examples

A counterculturist person
As a proud counterculturist, she often challenged traditional ideas in her community.
Many counterculturists in the 1970s promoted alternative lifestyles and music.
The documentary featured interviews with several influential counterculturists from the art world.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mainstreamer - a person who supports or belongs to mainstream culture
  • synonym: nonconformist - a person who does not follow established customs or beliefs
