supremacy

noun
the state of being superior or dominant
7.1 5.27/m
Pronunciation: / s…ôÀàpr…õm…ôsi / (AmE), / suÀêÀàpr…õm…ôsi / (BrE)
the state or condition of being superior to all others in authority, power, or status

Examples

Maintain supremacy over rivals
During the match, the team fought hard to maintain their supremacy over their rivals.
The court case challenged the supremacy of federal law over state law.
The nation's military supremacy was unquestioned after the conflict.

Synonyms

  • paramountcy
    9.9 0.01/m
    paramountcy is a formal, rare word for supreme authority, while supremacy is the common term for highest power and status.
  • dominance
    5.1 11/m
    dominance is a common term that focuses on power and control over others, while supremacy means overall superiority in authority and status.
  • domination
    4.7 8.62/m
    domination describes active control or forcing influence over people, while supremacy refers to the top position in power and status.
  • hegemony
    9.5 5.28/m
    hegemony names political or cultural leadership by one group or nation, while supremacy covers general highest authority or status.
  • ascendancy
    8.2 1.79/m
    ascendancy shows growing influence or control over time, while supremacy means already being highest in authority and status.
  • primacy
    8.0 3.98/m
    primacy means being first in importance or rank, often by law or rule, while supremacy stresses power and overall superiority.
  • preeminence
    7.9 0.84/m
    preeminence suggests being best in quality or achievement, while supremacy points to the top position in power and authority.
