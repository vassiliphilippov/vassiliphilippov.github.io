blockader

noun
person who obstructs
8.6 0.00/m

a person who deliberately delays or prevents a decision, law, or action, often by using procedural tactics.

Examples

Senate blockader
The senator acted as a blockader, using procedural tactics to delay the vote until next week.
As a known blockader, she often prevented new policies from being implemented quickly.
The committee grew frustrated with the constant efforts of the blockader to stall their progress.

Synonyms

  • obstructionist
    8.2 1.23/m
    an obstructionist uses formal delays in meetings or parliaments to stop laws, while a blockader uses procedural tactics to prevent decisions.
  • obstructor
    8.1 0.01/m
    an obstructor blocks or hinders actions in many settings, often physically or by rule, while a blockader focuses on procedural delay.
  • staller
    7.9 0.00/m
    a staller hesitates or puts off decisions in personal or small tasks, while a blockader uses rules to stop laws or votes.
