plea

noun
formal response by a defendant in court
4.3
Pronunciation: / pliː / (AmE), / pliː / (BrE)
(Law) a formal statement or response by a defendant in court, saying if they are guilty or not guilty

Examples

A plea of guilty
Her lawyer advised her to change her plea before the trial began.
The defendant entered a plea of not guilty to all charges.
The judge asked for the defendant's plea at the arraignment.
After hearing the plea, the judge scheduled a hearing for next month.
His lawyer filed a plea arguing that the case should be dismissed on technical grounds.
The defendant entered a plea of not guilty at the arraignment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defense - an argument or plea made in defense of oneself in court
  • antonym: demurrer - an objection that an opponent's point is irrelevant or invalid, without answering the substance of the claim
  • degree higher: guilty plea - a formal admission of guilt in court
  • degree lower: not guilty plea - a formal statement of innocence in response to a charge
