tepidarium

noun
Roman warm room
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛpɪˈdɛriəm / (AmE), / ˌtɛpɪˈdeəriəm / (BrE)
a warm room in an ancient Roman bathhouse, used for relaxing between hot and cold baths

Examples

Roman tepidarium
The ancient villa featured a beautifully decorated tepidarium for guests to relax in.
Archaeologists discovered the remains of a tepidarium with intricate wall paintings.
After the hot bath, visitors would rest in the tepidarium before moving to the cold room.
