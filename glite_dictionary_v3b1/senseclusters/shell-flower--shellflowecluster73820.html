shell-flower

noun
a marshland perennial plant with waxy leaves and pinkish flowers
Pronunciation: / ʃɛl ˈflaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ʃɛl ˈflaʊ.ə / (BrE)
A showy perennial plant of marshlands in eastern and central North America with waxy lance-shaped leaves and flowers that are creamy white below and pink to purple above.

Examples

The shell-flower plant
Because of its waxy leaves, the shell-flower is well suited to marshy environments.
The shell-flower adds vibrant color to wetland gardens each spring.
We spotted a cluster of shell-flowers growing beside the stream.
