moral bankruptcy

noun
complete lack of moral principles
4.5
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔrəl ˈbæŋkrəptsi / (AmE), / ˈmɒrəl ˈbaŋkrəptsi / (BrE)
a complete lack of moral principles or ethical standards, especially as shown by someone's actions or decisions

Examples

Moral bankruptcy of leaders
Critics argue that child labor in the industry is evidence of moral bankruptcy.
Many believe that political leaders' lies are signs of moral bankruptcy.
The scandal exposed the company's moral bankruptcy to the entire public.

Related Terms

  • antonym: integrity - the quality of being honest and having strong moral principles
