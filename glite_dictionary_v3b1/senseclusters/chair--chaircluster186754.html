chair

noun
professorship or academic post
Pronunciation: / tʃɛr / (AmE), / tʃɛː / (BrE)
a professorship, especially a distinguished or endowed academic position at a university or college

Examples

A history chair
After decades of research, she earned the chair in medieval history at the university.
Dr. Lee was appointed to the endowed chair of physics last year.
He is widely respected as the current chair of linguistics at Cambridge.

Related Terms
