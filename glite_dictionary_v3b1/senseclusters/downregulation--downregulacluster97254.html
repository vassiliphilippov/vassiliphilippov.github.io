downregulation

noun
reduction in activity
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˌdaʊnˌrɛɡjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdaʊnˌrɛɡjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a process in which the activity or number of cells, receptors, or molecules is reduced, often to decrease a response

Examples

Downregulation of receptors
The downregulation of certain genes can help protect the body from disease.
Chronic exposure to a drug may cause downregulation of its target receptors.
Scientists observed downregulation of immune responses in patients after treatment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: suppression - the act of reducing or stopping something
  • antonym: upregulation - an increase in the activity or number of cells, receptors, or molecules
