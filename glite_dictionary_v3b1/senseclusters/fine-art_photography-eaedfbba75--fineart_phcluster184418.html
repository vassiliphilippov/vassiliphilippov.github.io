fine-art photography

noun
artistic photography created for expression
1.9
Pronunciation: / ˌfaɪn ɑːrt fəˈtɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌfaɪn ɑːt fəˈtɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
photography created primarily for its artistic expression, reflecting the photographer's creative vision rather than serving a documentary or commercial purpose.

Examples

Fine-art photography exhibition
Galleries often feature exhibitions dedicated entirely to fine-art photography.
His passion for fine-art photography grew after studying classical painting techniques.
She specialized in fine-art photography, focusing on abstract patterns and light.

Related Terms

  • antonym: commercial photography - photography created for commercial use, such as advertising
  • antonym: documentary photography - photography that provides a factual record or report
