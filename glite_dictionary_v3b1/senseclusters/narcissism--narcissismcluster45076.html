narcissism

noun
excessive self-admiration
8.1 2.53/m
Pronunciation: / Ààn…ëÀêrs…™Àås…™z…ôm / (AmE), / Ààn…ëÀês…™s…™z…ôm / (BrE)
excessive interest in or admiration of oneself and one's physical appearance or abilities

Examples

Signs of narcissism
His constant need for attention was seen as a sign of narcissism by his friends.
The politician's narcissism made it difficult for him to listen to criticism from others.
Therapists often encounter cases where extreme narcissism damages personal relationships.

Synonyms

  • vanity
    5.6 3.78/m
    vanity is excessive pride in appearance, while narcissism is a broader self-focus on appearance and abilities.
  • vainglory
    9.9 0.06/m
    vainglory is boastful pride about achievements, while narcissism centers on admiring oneself and one's looks or abilities.
  • self-worship
    8.6 0.01/m
    self-worship is treating oneself like an object of adoration, while narcissism is strong self-admiration of looks or skills.
  • egomania
    9.1 0.10/m
    egomania is an obsessive, often harmful focus on oneself, while narcissism is excessive self-admiration about looks or abilities.
  • self-love
    7.0 0.04/m
    self-love is care or pride in oneself that can be healthy, while narcissism is excessive, self-centered admiration.
  • egotism
    6.1 1.19/m
    egotism is talking about your own importance and often showing it, while narcissism is deep self-admiration of looks or skills.
  • self-importance
    6.2 0.66/m
    self-importance is an exaggerated sense of being important to others, while narcissism is mainly overvaluing one's looks or abilities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: egotism - the practice of talking and thinking about oneself excessively
  • antonym: selflessness - concern more with the needs and wishes of others than one's own
