associationist

noun
believer in associationism
Pronunciation: / əˌsoʊsiˈeɪʃənɪst / (AmE), / əˌsəʊsiˈeɪʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that mental processes work by the association of ideas or experiences

Examples

A leading associationist
The associationist explained memory as a process of connecting related ideas.
As an associationist, she believed learning happens through repeated experiences.
Many early psychologists were influenced by associationist theories of the mind.

Related Terms

  • synonym: empiricist - a person who believes that knowledge comes from experience
  • antonym: nativist - a person who believes that certain ideas are inborn
