wet behind the ears

adjective
inexperienced or naive (idiom)
8.3
Pronunciation: / wɛt bəˈhaɪnd ði ɪrz / (AmE), / wɛt bɪˈhaɪnd ðiː ɪəz / (BrE)
having little experience and therefore not fully competent or knowledgeable; naive or inexperienced.

Examples

Wet behind the ears young player
Although talented, the team was a bit wet behind the ears in international competitions.
Don’t be too hard on her—she’s just wet behind the ears and eager to learn.
The new employee is still wet behind the ears and needs time to adjust.

  • antonym: seasoned - having a lot of experience in a particular activity or job
