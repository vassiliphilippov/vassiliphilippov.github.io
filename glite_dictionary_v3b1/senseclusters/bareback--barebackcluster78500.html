bareback

adverb
riding without a saddle
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛrˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈbeəˌbak /; / ˈbeəˌbæk / (BrE)
without a saddle; riding a horse without using a saddle

Examples

Ride bareback
He won the race by galloping bareback across the finish line.
She prefers to ride her horse bareback during the summer months.
The children learned to sit confidently by practicing bareback in the paddock.
Many skilled riders enjoy the challenge of going bareback across open fields.
She learned to ride bareback before ever using a saddle.
The performer wowed the crowd with a bareback routine at the rodeo.
