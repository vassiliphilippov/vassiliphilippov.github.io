advantaged

adjective
having more benefits
Pronunciation: / ədˈvæntɪdʒd / (AmE), / ədˈvɑːntɪdʒd / (BrE)
having more benefits, opportunities, or a better situation than others

Examples

Advantaged students
Students from urban areas are often advantaged when applying to top universities.
The new policy aims to help those who are less advantaged in society.
Being fluent in several languages is an advantaged position in the job market.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disadvantaged - lacking benefits or opportunities compared to others
  • synonym: privileged - having special rights or advantages
