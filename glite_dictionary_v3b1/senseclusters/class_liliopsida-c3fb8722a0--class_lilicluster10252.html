class Liliopsida

noun
a class of monocot flowering plants
9.1
Pronunciation: /klæs lɪl.i.ɑːp.ˈsɪd.ə/ /; / /klæs lɪl.i.ɑːp.ˈsiː.də/ (AmE), /klɑːs lɪl.i.ɒp.ˈsɪd.ə/ /; / /klɑːs lɪl.i.ɒp.ˈsiː.də/ (BrE)
A class of flowering plants (monocots) that produce seeds with a single cotyledon and typically have parallel-veined leaves, including grasses, lilies, palms, and orchids.

Examples

Class Liliopsida plants
Botanists often refer to monocots as plants belonging to the class Liliopsida.
Palms, orchids, and most grasses are members of the class Liliopsida.
The class Liliopsida includes a wide diversity of economically important crops, such as rice and wheat.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dicotyledon - plants with two seed leaves (cotyledons)
  • synonym: monocot - plants with one seed leaf (cotyledon)
