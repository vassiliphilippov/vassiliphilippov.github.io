fearmonger

verb
to spread fear
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪrˌmʌŋɡər / (AmE), / ˈfɪəˌmʌŋɡə / (BrE)
to deliberately spread or promote fear, especially to influence people's opinions or actions

Examples

To fearmonger news
Some politicians have been accused of fearmongering to gain support for their policies.
The article was criticized for fearmongering about new technology without evidence.
Instead of fearmongering, leaders should provide clear and accurate information.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alarm - to cause fear or worry in others
  • antonym: reassure - to make someone feel less afraid or worried
