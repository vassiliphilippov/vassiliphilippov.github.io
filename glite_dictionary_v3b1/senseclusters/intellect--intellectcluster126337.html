intellect

noun
mental capacity, reasoning ability
Pronunciation: / ˈɪn.təˌlɛkt /; / ˈɪntəˌlɛkt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.tə.lekt /; / ˈɪntɪlɛkt / (BrE)
the ability or faculty to think, reason, and understand things, especially at a high or abstract level

Examples

Sharp intellect
His sharp intellect made him a formidable opponent in debates.
Reading challenging books can help develop your intellect over time.
She admired the professor's brilliant intellect and wide-ranging curiosity.
Children develop their intellect through play and learning.
He admired his professor's remarkable intellect and depth of knowledge.
Her keen intellect made her an excellent scientist.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intelligence - the ability to acquire and apply knowledge and skills
  • synonym: mind - the element of a person that enables them to think, feel, and reason
  • antonym: stupidity - lack of intelligence or common sense
  • synonym: wit - mental sharpness and inventiveness
