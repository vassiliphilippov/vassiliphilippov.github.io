microphonic

adjective
producing microphone-like noise
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪkrəˈfɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəˈfɒnɪk / (BrE)
relating to or producing effects similar to those of a microphone, especially in generating unwanted noise from mechanical vibrations

Examples

Microphonic noise
The old amplifier became microphonic and picked up every vibration from the table.
Engineers tested the cable for microphonic properties before using it in the recording studio.
If a vacuum tube is microphonic, it can cause unwanted noise in the audio system.

Related Terms

  • synonym: microphonic effect - unwanted noise caused by mechanical vibrations in audio equipment
  • antonym: silent - not producing any sound or noise
