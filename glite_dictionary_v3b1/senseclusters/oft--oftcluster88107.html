oft

adverb
frequently; many times
9.9 0.92/m
Pronunciation: / É‘ft / (AmE), / É’ft / (BrE)
On many occasions; frequently.

Examples

He oft mentioned
His name was oft mentioned in the old legends.
In times past, stories were oft recited by the fireside.
She would oft recall her childhood days with fondness.

Synonyms

  • often
    1.0 476/m
    often is the common everyday adverb for things that happen many times, while oft sounds older and more literary.
  • oftentimes
    4.9 4.80/m
    oftentimes is a slightly old-fashioned or regional adverb meaning frequently, while oft sounds more poetic and less used in speech.
  • frequently
    1.0 60/m
    frequently is a neutral, slightly formal adverb used in writing and reports, while oft is shorter and more poetic.
  • time and again
    1.8 3.56/m
    time and again is an idiom that stresses repeated occasions in speech and stories, while oft is brief and poetic.
  • repeatedly
    1.0 29/m
    repeatedly describes actions done again and again over time, while oft simply notes something happens often or many times.
