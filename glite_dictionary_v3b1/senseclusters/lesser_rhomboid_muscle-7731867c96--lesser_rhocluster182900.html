lesser rhomboid muscle

noun
muscle in upper back moving scapula
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛsər ˈrɑmbɔɪd ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈlesə ˈrɒmbɔɪd ˈmʌsl̩ / (BrE)
a muscle of the upper back, beneath the trapezius, that draws the scapula toward the vertebral column and slightly upward

Examples

The lesser rhomboid muscle
In cases of poor posture, the lesser rhomboid muscle can become weak or strained.
Physical therapists often recommend exercises to strengthen the lesser rhomboid muscle for improved back health.
The lesser rhomboid muscle plays a vital role in retracting the shoulder blade during movement.
