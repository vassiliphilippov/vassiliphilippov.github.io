bind

verb
wrap around to cover or enclose
1.7 2.7
Pronunciation: /baɪnd/ (AmE), /baɪnd/ (BrE)
to tie, fasten, or wrap something tightly with rope, string, or similar material

Examples

To bind a rope
She used a bandage to bind the injured arm tightly.
The chef binds the herbs with string before adding them to the stew.
To keep the book closed, he bound it with a length of ribbon.
Please bind these newspapers with string before recycling them.
The captors bound the prisoners' hands to prevent them from escaping.
The farmer used a length of rope to bind the hay bales together.
Please bind these sticks together with some twine before the storm arrives.
She binds the parcels securely so nothing falls out during delivery.
The prisoner was bound hand and foot to prevent escape.

Related Terms

  • antonym: loosen - to make less tight or free from being secured
  • synonym: tie - to fasten or secure with a cord or rope
  • antonym: unbind - to release from a tied or fastened state
  • antonym: untie - to release or unfasten something previously tied or bound
  • antonym: unwrap - to remove a wrapping from something
  • synonym: wrap - to cover or enclose something with paper, cloth, or other material
