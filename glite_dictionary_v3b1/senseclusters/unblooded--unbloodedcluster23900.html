unblooded

adjective
inexperienced in battle
9.3
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈblʌdɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈblʌdɪd / (BrE)
not having experienced battle or not yet tested in conflict or competition

Examples

Unblooded recruits
The unblooded soldiers listened carefully to their commander's advice before the battle.
As an unblooded competitor, she felt nervous entering her first major tournament.
Many unblooded recruits were eager to prove themselves on the front lines.

Related Terms

  • antonym: veteran - someone experienced in a particular field, especially battle
