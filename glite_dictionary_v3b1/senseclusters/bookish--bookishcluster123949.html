bookish

adjective
fond of reading or study
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊkɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbʊkɪʃ / (BrE)
(of a person) fond of books and reading; given to study or learning rather than practical matters.

Examples

A bookish person
Even as a child, he was already quite bookish and preferred reading to playing outside.
Her bookish interests made her a natural fit for the literary club.
The bookish student spent most weekends immersed in novels and textbooks.

Related Terms

  • antonym: practical - concerned with actual doing or use rather than theory or ideas
  • synonym: studious - spending a lot of time studying or reading
