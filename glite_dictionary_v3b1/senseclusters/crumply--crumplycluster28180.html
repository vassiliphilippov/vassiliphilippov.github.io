crumply

adjective
crisp and crumbly
5.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkrʌm.plɪ/ /; / /ˈkrʌm.pliː/ (AmE), /ˈkrʌm.plɪ/ /; / /ˈkrʌm.pliː/ (BrE)
having a crisp, crumbly texture, especially referring to baked goods or food that easily breaks apart

Examples

Crumply pastry edges
The crumply edges of the pastry made it especially delicious with tea.
She loved the crumply texture of the freshly baked scones.
After baking, the cookies turned out perfectly crumply and golden brown.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crumbly - easily breaking into small pieces
  • antonym: soft - not hard or firm to the touch
