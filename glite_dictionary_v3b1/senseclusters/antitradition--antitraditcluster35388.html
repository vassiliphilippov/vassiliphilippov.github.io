antitradition

noun
opposition to tradition
Pronunciation: / ˌænti.trəˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌænti.trəˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
a belief, attitude, or movement that opposes established traditions or customs

Examples

Antitradition in art
The new movement was founded on principles of antitradition and creative freedom.
Many avant-garde artists embrace antitradition in their work to challenge societal norms.
Her philosophy of antitradition led her to question long-standing family customs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: iconoclasm - the rejection or destruction of established beliefs or institutions
  • antonym: traditionalism - the upholding of traditional customs and values
