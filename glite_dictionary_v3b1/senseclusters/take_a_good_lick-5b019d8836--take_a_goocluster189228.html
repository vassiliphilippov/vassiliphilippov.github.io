take a good lick

verb
take a large lick or bite
3.6
Pronunciation: / teɪk ə ɡʊd lɪk / (AmE), / teɪk ə ɡʊd lɪk / (BrE)
to take a large taste or bite, especially by licking something such as an ice cream cone

Examples

To take a good lick of ice cream
After scoring the winning goal, she took a good lick of her celebratory ice cream.
He took a good lick at the lollipop before handing it back to his friend.
The puppy eagerly took a good lick at the peanut butter on my hand.
