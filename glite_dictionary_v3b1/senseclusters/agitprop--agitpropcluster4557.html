agitprop

noun
political propaganda via art or literature
Pronunciation: / ˈædʒ.ɪtˌprɑp / (AmE), / ˈædʒ.ɪtˌprɒp / (BrE)
political propaganda, especially that which is spread through art, literature, or other creative media, often with a leftist or communist perspective

Examples

Political agitprop art
Her play was criticized for being too much agitprop and not enough genuine drama.
Modern artists sometimes use agitprop techniques to highlight social and political issues.
The museum curated an exhibition of agitprop posters from the early Soviet era.

Related Terms

  • synonym: propaganda - information, especially biased or misleading, used to promote a political cause
