berth

verb
provide a place to sleep or dock
6.1
Pronunciation: / bɝːθ /; / bɝθ / (AmE), / bɜːθ / (BrE)
to provide or secure a place for sleeping or docking, especially for ships

Examples

To berth a ship
The cruise line berths all passengers in comfortable state rooms.
The harbormaster berthed the ship safely alongside the pier.
They berth the yacht in the marina every summer.
After a long voyage, the ferry finally berthed in the early morning.
Large cargo ships often berth at this port due to its deep waters.
The captain decided to berth the ship at the new wharf for unloading.
Once the ship is berthed, passengers can disembark safely.
The captain skillfully berthed the yacht at the marina.
They will berth the cargo vessel at pier six for unloading.
