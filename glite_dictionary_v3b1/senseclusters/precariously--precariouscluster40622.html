precariously

adverb
in a dangerous or unstable manner
Pronunciation: / prɪˈkɛriəsli / (AmE), / prɪˈkeəriəsli / (BrE)
in a way that is not securely held or in a dangerous or unstable manner

Examples

Walk precariously along ledge
She walked precariously along the narrow ledge, afraid she might slip and fall.
The ladder was balanced precariously against the wall, making it unsafe to climb.
The vase sat precariously on the edge of the table, ready to tip at any moment.
