junk DNA

noun
metaphorical nonfunctional parts of a system
4.6
Pronunciation: /dʒʌŋk ˈdiː.en.eɪ/ (AmE), /dʒʌŋk ˈdiː.en.eɪ/ (BrE)
(informal) Nonfunctional, unnecessary, or obsolete parts of a system (such as software code, data, or processes) that remain after development and are candidates for removal.

Examples

Software junk DNA
The legacy codebase is full of junk DNA that should be pruned to improve performance.
During the audit they discovered customer records that were junk DNA and increased storage costs.
The team's best refactor removed dozens of functions that behaved like junk DNA in production.
