delusory

adjective
giving a false impression; deceptive
9.1 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / d…™ÀàluÀês…ôri / (AmE), / d…™ÀàljuÀês…ôri / (BrE)
giving a false or misleading impression; likely to deceive or mislead someone into believing what is not true.

Examples

A delusory mask
Her apparent happiness was merely a delusory mask for her anxiety.
The investor was lured by a delusory sense of security in the unstable market.
The treaty created a delusory hope for lasting peace in the region.

Synonyms

  • delusive
    8.1 0.10/m
    delusive gives a false or misleading appearance in speech or writing, while delusory is more formal and suggests deception.
  • deceptive
    5.3 4.30/m
    deceptive often hides facts or tricks people in everyday situations, while delusory sounds more formal and means likely to mislead.
  • illusory
    7.0 2.57/m
    illusory describes something that seems real but is not, whereas delusory focuses on creating a false impression that misleads people.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deceptive - giving an appearance or impression different from the true one; misleading
