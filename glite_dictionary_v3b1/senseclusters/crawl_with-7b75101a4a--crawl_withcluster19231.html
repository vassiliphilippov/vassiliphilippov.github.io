crawl with

verb
be filled with (often unpleasant) things
6.5
Pronunciation: / krɔl wɪð / (AmE), / krɔːl wɪð / (BrE)
To be filled or infested with a large number of people, animals, or things, often unpleasant or undesirable.

Examples

To crawl with ants
Her kitchen became crawling with ants over the summer.
The abandoned building was crawling with rats after years of neglect.
The festival grounds were crawling with people by noon.
