mercury-vapor lamp

noun
lamp using mercury vapor to emit bluish light
9.8 5.3
Pronunciation: /ˈmɜːr.kjə.riː ˈveɪ.pər læmp/ (AmE), /ˈmɜː.kjʊ.riː ˈveɪ.pə læmp/ (BrE)
an electric lamp in which light is produced by passing an electric current through vaporized mercury, emitting bluish light rich in ultraviolet radiation

Examples

Mercury-vapor lamp light
Many streetlights were once fitted with mercury-vapor lamps for their brightness and efficiency.
Overhead, a mercury-vapor lamp cast a bluish glow across the warehouse floor.
The aquarium uses a mercury-vapor lamp to mimic natural sunlight for the plants and fish.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fluorescent lamp - an electric lamp that produces light by causing a phosphor coating to fluoresce with ultraviolet light from mercury vapor
