sea-ear

noun
a type of abalone (marine mollusk)
Pronunciation: / ˈsi ɛr / (AmE), / ˈsiː ɪə / (BrE)
A type of abalone, a marine mollusk with an ear-shaped shell, especially one found near the Channel Islands.

Examples

The sea-ear shell
Many divers visit the Channel Islands to search for sea-ear and other shellfish.
Scientists are studying the population decline in local sea-ear species.
The shell of the sea-ear is prized for its iridescent mother-of-pearl lining.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abalone - a large marine mollusk with a flattened shell lined with mother-of-pearl
