malabsorption

noun
impaired absorption of nutrients
9.9 7.1
Pronunciation: /mæl.əb.ˈsɔːr.p.ʃən/ /; / /mæl.əb.ˈsɑːr.p.ʃən/ (AmE), /mæl.ə.ˈsɔːp.ʃən/ (BrE)
Failure of the digestive tract to properly absorb nutrients from food, often resulting in nutritional deficiencies.

Examples

Malabsorption of nutrients
Conditions such as celiac disease and Crohn's disease can cause malabsorption of vital nutrients.
Long-term malabsorption may require specialized dietary supplements to prevent deficiencies.
The patient was diagnosed with malabsorption after experiencing persistent weight loss and fatigue.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: malnutrition - lack of proper nutrition, often caused by malabsorption
