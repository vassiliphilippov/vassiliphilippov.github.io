tribute

noun
payment extorted as protection money
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɪb.juːt /; / ˈtrɪbˌjut / (AmE), / ˈtrɪb.juːt / (BrE)
a payment made to someone, often under threat or as a sign of dependence or submission

Examples

Pay tribute money
Refusing to pay tribute often resulted in threats or violence from the racketeers.
The shopkeepers had to pay a weekly tribute to the local gang or risk being attacked.
Throughout the decade, many restaurants were forced to offer a secret tribute to avoid trouble.
Failure to deliver the required tribute often resulted in military action.
The smaller kingdom agreed to pay an annual tribute to its more powerful neighbor.
Throughout history, invading empires demanded tribute from conquered territories.

Related Terms

  • synonym: levy - a sum of money collected as a tax or payment
  • synonym: protection money - money paid for protection by criminals
  • synonym: tax - a compulsory contribution to state revenue
