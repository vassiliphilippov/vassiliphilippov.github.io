welting

noun
attaching a welt
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛltɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɛltɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of attaching or sewing a strip of material (a welt) to the edge of an item, such as a shoe, cushion, or garment

Examples

Shoe welting
The shoemaker spent hours welting the soles to ensure they would last for years.
Upholsterers use welting to create a neat edge on cushions and furniture.
Traditional boots often feature visible welting along the edge for added strength and style.

Related Terms

  • synonym: binding - the process of finishing the edge of fabric with a strip of material
  • synonym: sewing - the act of joining or attaching by stitches
