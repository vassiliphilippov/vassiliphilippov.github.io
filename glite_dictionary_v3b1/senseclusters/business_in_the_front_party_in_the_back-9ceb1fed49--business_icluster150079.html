business in the front, party in the back

noun
formal on one side, fun on the other (esp. hairstyle)
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪznəs ɪn ðə frʌnt ˈpɑːrti ɪn ðə bæk / (AmE), / ˈbɪznɪs ɪn ðə frʌnt ˈpɑːti ɪn ðə bæk / (BrE)
having a formal or serious appearance in one aspect, and a relaxed or playful aspect in another; originally describing a hairstyle that is short at the front and sides and long at the back (the mullet), but now used more broadly for things or situations with a dual nature.

Examples

Business in the front, party in the back hairstyle
His haircut was classic business in the front, party in the back, with neatly trimmed sides and a long back.
She wore a blazer and jeans combo that screamed business in the front, party in the back.
Their restaurant design is business in the front, party in the back: the dining area looks formal, but the outdoor patio has a bar and games.
