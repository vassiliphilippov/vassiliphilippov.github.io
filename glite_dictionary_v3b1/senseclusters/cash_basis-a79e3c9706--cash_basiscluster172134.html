cash basis

noun
accounting method using actual cash flow
4.2 4.3
Pronunciation: /kæʃ ˈbeɪ.sɪs/ /; / /kæʃ ˈbeɪ.səs/ (AmE), /kæʃ ˈbeɪ.sɪs/ /; / /kæʃ ˈbeɪ.səs/ (BrE)
an accounting method in which income and expenses are recorded only when cash is actually received or paid

Examples

The cash basis accounting
Many small businesses use the cash basis for their accounting because it is simpler to manage.
Switching from the cash basis to the accrual basis requires significant changes in record keeping.
Under the cash basis, expenses are only recognized when payment is actually made.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accrual basis - an accounting method recording revenues and expenses when incurred, not when cash is exchanged
