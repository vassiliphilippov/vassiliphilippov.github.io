massivity

noun
massive uniformity
9.5
Pronunciation: /mə.ˈsɪv.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /mæ.ˈsɪv.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /mə.ˈsɪv.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /mæ.ˈsɪv.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
the quality or state of being massive and uniform, especially in rocks or mineral deposits without distinct layers

Examples

Rock massivity
The massivity of the granite made it difficult for water to seep through the rock.
Geologists were impressed by the massivity of the mineral deposit, which showed no visible layers.
The massivity of the mountain's core suggests it formed from a single, massive intrusion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: massiveness - the quality of being very large, heavy, or solid
  • antonym: stratification - the arrangement of layers, especially in rocks or sediments
