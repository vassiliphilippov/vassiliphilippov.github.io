impend

verb
to be imminent or about to occur
6.1
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɛnd / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛnd / (BrE)
to be about to happen, especially something bad or causing worry

Examples

Trouble impends
A sense of doom seemed to impend over the entire community.
As the dark clouds gathered, a storm appeared to impend.
Economic crisis may impend if the current policies continue.
A sense of disaster seemed to impend as the stock market began to tumble.
Dark clouds suggest that a storm may impend over the town tonight.
They cancelled the trip because danger appeared to impend on the mountain roads.

Related Terms

  • synonym: approach - to draw near in time or space
  • synonym: loom - to appear as a large or threatening event about to happen
  • antonym: recede - to move back or withdraw, especially from an imminent state
  • synonym: threaten - to be likely to happen soon (of something bad)
