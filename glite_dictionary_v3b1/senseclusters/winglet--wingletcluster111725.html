winglet

noun
aircraft wing extension
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪŋlət / (AmE), / ˈwɪŋlɪt / (BrE)
a small, wing-shaped projection on an aircraft or other vehicle, designed to improve aerodynamic efficiency or stability

Examples

Aircraft winglet
The new jet features winglets that help reduce drag and save fuel.
Engineers added winglets to the aircraft to improve its performance during long flights.
Many modern airplanes are equipped with winglets to increase efficiency and stability.
The airplane's winglets help reduce drag and improve fuel efficiency during flight.
Engineers added a winglet to the race car to enhance its cornering stability.
Modern wind turbines often feature a winglet at the tip of each blade.

  • synonym: spoiler - a device on a vehicle to reduce lift or increase stability
