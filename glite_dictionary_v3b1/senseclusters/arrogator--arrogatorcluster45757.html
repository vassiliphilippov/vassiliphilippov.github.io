arrogator

noun
person making arrogant, unjustified claims
Pronunciation: / ˈærəˌɡeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈærəˌɡeɪtə / (BrE)
a person who arrogantly claims rights, advantages, or qualities to which they have no legitimate entitlement

Examples

An arrogator claims rights
History is full of examples where the arrogator overstepped and faced public backlash.
No one respected the arrogator who insisted on privileges he hadn't earned.
The new manager was seen as an arrogator, constantly taking credit for others' work.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: claimant - person making a claim, often with some justification
  • synonym: pretender - person who falsely claims a right or title
  • synonym: usurper - person who takes a position or right by force or without legal right
