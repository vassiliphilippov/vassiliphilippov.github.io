wave-particle duality

noun
matter or radiation with wave and particle properties
7.0
Pronunciation: / weɪv ˈpɑrtɪkəl duˈæləti / (AmE), / weɪv ˈpɑːtɪkl djuːˈalɪti / (BrE)
The principle in physics that matter and electromagnetic radiation exhibit both wave-like and particle-like properties, depending on how they are observed.

Examples

wave-particle duality principle
Physicists demonstrated wave-particle duality through experiments with electrons and photons.
She explained wave-particle duality using the famous double-slit experiment.
The concept of wave-particle duality is central to understanding quantum mechanics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: particle theory - the theory that light or other phenomena consist of particles
  • synonym: wave theory - the theory that light or other phenomena propagate as waves
