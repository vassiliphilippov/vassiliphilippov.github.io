queen regent

noun
a queen ruling as regent for a king
2.0 5.9
Pronunciation: /kwiːn ˈriː.dʒənt/ (AmE), /kwiːn ˈriː.dʒənt/ (BrE)
A queen who rules a country as regent when the king is unable to rule, especially due to minority, incapacity, or absence.

Examples

The queen regent rules
As queen regent, she faced many challenges in stabilising the realm.
During the king's illness, the council appointed the queen regent to govern the kingdom.
Mary de Guise served as queen regent of Scotland during her daughter's minority.

Related Terms

  • synonym: regent - a person appointed to administer a state because the monarch is a minor or is absent or incapacitated
