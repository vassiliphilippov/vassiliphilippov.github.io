Synonyms

  • sever
    5.6 4.96/m
    sever is a formal verb for cutting something completely away, while cut off is a common phrasal verb for detaching.
  • chop off
    3.4 1.38/m
    chop off is a strong action using sharp blows to remove parts, while cut off is more general removal by cutting.
  • lop off
    4.6 0.52/m
    lop off means removing a part in one quick cut, often wood, while cut off covers many cutting situations.
  • cut away
    3.4 0.47/m
    cut away means taking away surrounding or excess material, while cut off focuses on detaching or severing a part completely.
  • snip off
    3.7 0.21/m
    snip off is a neat, quick cut with scissors for small items, while cut off can mean larger, rougher removal.
  • nip off
    5.1 0.02/m
    nip off is a light pinch or tiny cut for small bits like buds, while cut off often means wider or full removal.
