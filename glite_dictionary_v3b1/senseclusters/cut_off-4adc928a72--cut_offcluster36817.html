cut off

verb
remove by cutting
Pronunciation: / kʌt ɔf / (AmE), / kʌt ɒf / (BrE)
to remove or detach something by cutting it away from what it is attached to, including amputation

Examples

To cut off a finger
She accidentally cut off a piece of her hair while trimming it.
The doctor had to cut off the infected toe to prevent the spread of the disease.
Workers cut off the damaged branch from the tree.
If the infection spreads, they may need to cut off the damaged tissue.
Severe frostbite forced surgeons to cut off two of her toes.
The doctor had to cut off his leg to save his life.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amputate - to cut off a limb, usually as a medical procedure
  • synonym: lop off - to cut off, especially branches or parts of trees or plants
