Pilosella aurantiaca

noun
orange-red European hawkweed species
Pronunciation: / paɪ.ləˈsɛl.ə ɔːˌræn.tiˈeɪ.kə / (AmE), / pɪ.ləˈsɛl.ɪ.ə ɔːˌræn.tɪˈɑː.kə / (BrE)
A European hawkweed with bright orange-red flower heads, often considered a weed, especially in northeastern North America; sometimes classified in the genus Hieracium.

Examples

Bright Pilosella aurantiaca flower
Hikers in New England often spot Pilosella aurantiaca growing wild by the roadside.
Some botanists debate whether Pilosella aurantiaca belongs in the genus Hieracium or should remain separate.
The garden was overtaken by Pilosella aurantiaca, its vivid orange-red flowers standing out among the grass.
