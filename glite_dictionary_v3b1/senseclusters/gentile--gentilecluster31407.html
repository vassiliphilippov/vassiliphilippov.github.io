gentile

noun
non-Jewish person
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛn.taɪl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛn.taɪl / (BrE)
a person who is not Jewish (used in general, biblical, or historical contexts, sometimes especially a Christian as contrasted with a Jew)

Examples

Gentile Christian
As a gentile, she was curious to learn more about Jewish traditions.
Some biblical passages discuss the relationship between Jews and gentiles.
The synagogue welcomed both Jews and gentiles to the event.
Historically, early converts were called gentiles to distinguish them from Jewish Christians.
In biblical discussions, Paul is described as the apostle to the gentiles.
Some church traditions use gentile as a term for non-Jewish believers.
In medieval Europe, many gentiles were unaware of Jewish customs and traditions.
She married a gentile, although she was raised in a Jewish home.
The early church included both Jews and gentiles among its members.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Christian - A person who believes in Christianity
  • antonym: Jew - a member of the Jewish people or religion
