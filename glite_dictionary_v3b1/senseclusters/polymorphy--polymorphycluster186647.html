polymorphy

noun
many forms or shapes
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːliˈmɔːrfi / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈmɔːfi / (BrE)
the quality or state of existing in or assuming many different forms or shapes

Examples

Artistic polymorphy
The artist's work displayed remarkable polymorphy in its variety of styles.
Biologists study polymorphy to understand how species adapt to different environments.
The architecture of the city is notable for its polymorphy, blending old and new designs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monomorphy - the quality of having only one form or shape
  • synonym: polymorphism - the condition of occurring in several different forms
