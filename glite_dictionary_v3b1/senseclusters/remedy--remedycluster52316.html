remedy

noun
action to correct a fault or problem
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛm.ə.di / (AmE), / ˈrɛm.ɪ.di / (BrE)
an action taken to correct a mistake, fault, or bad situation

Examples

A remedy for injustice
Education is often seen as a remedy for social inequality.
Immediate action must be taken as a remedy to the nation's growing unemployment problem.
The lawyer suggested a legal remedy for the error in the contract.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cure - something that eliminates a disease or problem
  • antonym: problem - an issue to be solved; opposite to remedy
  • synonym: solution - a way of solving a problem or dealing with a difficult situation
