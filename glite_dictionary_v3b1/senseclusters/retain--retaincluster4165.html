retain

verb
keep or hold within
2.0
Pronunciation: / rɪˈteɪn / (AmE), / rɪˈteɪn / (BrE)
to keep or hold something within, preventing it from leaving or escaping

Examples

To retain water
Certain medications retain their effectiveness over a long period.
Good soil can retain moisture for crops even during dry weather.
The new containers are designed to retain freshness by sealing tightly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: release - to let go or allow to escape
