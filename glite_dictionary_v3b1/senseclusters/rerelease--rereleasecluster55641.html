rerelease

verb
to issue again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːrɪˈliːs / (AmE), / ˌriːrɪˈliːs / (BrE)
to make something, such as a film, album, or product, available to the public again after its original release

Examples

To rerelease an album
The record label decided to rerelease the album with bonus tracks for its anniversary.
After gaining popularity, the publisher chose to rerelease the out-of-print novel.
Fans were excited when the studio rereleased the classic film in theaters.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reissue - to publish or release something again
  • antonym: withdraw - to remove something from availability
