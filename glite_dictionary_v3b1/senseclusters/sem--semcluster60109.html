sem

noun
statistical measure
Pronunciation: / ˌɛs iː ˈɛm / (AmE), / ˌɛs iː ˈɛm / (BrE)
the standard error of the mean, a statistical measure showing how much a sample mean is likely to differ from the true population mean

Examples

SEM value
The scientist included the sem in the results to indicate the reliability of the average.
When comparing groups, a smaller sem suggests more precise estimates of the mean.
The report listed both the mean and the sem for each measurement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mean - the average value of a set of numbers
  • synonym: standard deviation - a measure of the amount of variation in a set of values
