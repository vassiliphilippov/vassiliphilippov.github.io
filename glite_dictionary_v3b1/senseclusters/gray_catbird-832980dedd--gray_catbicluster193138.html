gray catbird

noun
North American songbird with catlike call
3.9
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪ ˈkætbɝd /; / ɡreɪ ˈkætˌbɝd / (AmE), / ɡreɪ ˈkatbɜːd /; / ɡreɪ ˈkatˌbɜːd / (BrE)
A North American songbird (Dumetella carolinensis) known for its slate-gray color and a call that resembles a cat's mewing.

Examples

The gray catbird is easily recognized by its slate-gray plumage and catlike cry.

A grey catbird built its nest near the garden last spring.
The grey catbird is named for its soft, catlike call.
We heard a grey catbird singing in the shrubs behind the house.
