huller

noun
machine for removing husks
7.3
Pronunciation: /ˈhʌl.ər/ (AmE), /ˈhʌl.ə/ (BrE)
a machine or tool used to remove the outer covering (hull or husk) from seeds, grains, or other produce

Examples

Rice huller
The farmer used a huller to process the freshly harvested rice.
Modern coffee production often relies on a mechanical huller to remove the beans' outer layers.
After running the seeds through the huller, only the edible parts remained.
The factory uses a modern huller to process large quantities of rice every day.
A skilled huller can remove the husks from seeds quickly by hand.
After passing through the huller, the grains are ready for packaging.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dehuller - a machine that removes hulls from seeds or grains
  • synonym: sheller - a device for removing shells or husks from nuts or seeds
