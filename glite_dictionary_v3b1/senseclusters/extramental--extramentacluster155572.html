extramental

adjective
outside the mind
Pronunciation: / ˌɛkstrəˈmɛntəl / (AmE), / ˌɛkstrəˈmɛntəl / (BrE)
existing or occurring outside the mind, not dependent on mental perception

Examples

Extramental reality
Philosophers argue whether moral truths are extramental or created by human minds.
The scientist claimed that physical laws are extramental realities, not just ideas.
Some theories suggest that numbers have an extramental existence, independent of thought.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mental - existing in or relating to the mind
  • synonym: objective - existing independently of thought or an observer
