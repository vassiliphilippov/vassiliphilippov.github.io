inducible

adjective
able to be triggered
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈduːsəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈdjuːsəbəl / (BrE)
able to be caused, triggered, or brought about by a specific factor or condition

Examples

Inducible protein expression
The scientist explained that the protein is inducible only under certain laboratory conditions.
Some plants have inducible defenses that activate when they are attacked by insects.
The symptoms are inducible by exposure to specific allergens in the environment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: spontaneous - happening without being caused by something else
  • synonym: triggerable - able to be set off or activated by something
