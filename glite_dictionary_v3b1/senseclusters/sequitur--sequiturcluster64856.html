sequitur

noun
logical conclusion
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛkwɪtər / (AmE), / ˈsɛkwɪtə / (BrE)
a statement or conclusion that logically follows from what was previously said

Examples

A logical sequitur
The lawyer's argument was strong because every sequitur was based on solid evidence.
In a well-structured essay, each point should be a clear sequitur from the previous one.
Her explanation made sense, as every sequitur built logically on her earlier statements.
His argument was strong because every point was a clear sequitur from the previous one.
The lawyer's statement was a logical sequitur in the context of the evidence presented.
Unlike her earlier remarks, this time her response was a perfect sequitur.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conclusion - a judgment or decision reached after consideration
  • antonym: non sequitur - a statement that does not logically follow from what preceded
