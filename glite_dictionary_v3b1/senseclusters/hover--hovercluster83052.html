hover

verb
remain suspended in the air
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌvɚ / (AmE), / ˈhʌvə / (BrE)
to stay in one place in the air, without moving in any direction

Examples

A helicopter hovers above
A helicopter began to hover over the accident scene, searching for a place to land.
As the magician waved his hands, the ball appeared to hover in midair.
The hummingbird seemed to hover effortlessly above the flower.
A helicopter can hover over the landing pad before touching down.
Suspended in midair, the drone continued to hover silently above the crowd.
The hummingbird appeared to hover in front of the brightly colored flowers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: descend - to move or fall downward
  • synonym: float - to rest or move on or near the surface of a liquid without sinking
  • synonym: levitate - to rise or float in the air, especially by means of supernatural power or as if by magic
