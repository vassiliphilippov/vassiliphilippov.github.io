ductility

noun
ability to be drawn into wire or hammered thin
3.7 6.4
Pronunciation: /dək.ˈtɪl.ə.tiː/ /; / /dʌk.ˈtɪl.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /dʌk.ˈtɪl.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /dək.ˈtɪl.ə.tiː/ (BrE)
the ability of a material to be drawn out into a thin wire or to be hammered into thin sheets without breaking

Examples

High ductility metal
Copper is valued in electrical wiring because its high ductility allows it to be drawn into thin, flexible wires.
Engineers measure ductility to determine whether a metal can be safely shaped during manufacturing processes.
The ductility of gold makes it ideal for crafting fine jewelry and intricate decorations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: brittleness - the tendency to break or shatter easily
  • synonym: malleability - the ability to be hammered or pressed permanently out of shape without breaking
