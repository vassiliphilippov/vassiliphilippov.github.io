kero

noun
Andean ceremonial cup
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛroʊ / (AmE), / ˈkɛrəʊ / (BrE)
a decorated wooden or metal drinking cup used in traditional Andean ceremonies, especially by the Inca

Examples

Ceremonial kero cup
The ancient kero was beautifully carved and painted with traditional Andean designs.
During the festival, the priest poured chicha into a kero to honor the mountain spirits.
Collectors value each kero for its unique patterns and historical significance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chalice - a large cup used in ceremonies or religious rituals
  • synonym: goblet - a drinking glass with a stem and base
