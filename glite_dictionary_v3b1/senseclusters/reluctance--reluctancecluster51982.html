Synonyms

  • unwillingness
    3.9 4/m
    unwillingness is a direct lack of will or readiness to act, while reluctance feels milder and shows little enthusiasm.
  • disinclination
    8.0 0.3/m
    disinclination implies a personal dislike or avoidance, while reluctance is a more general unwillingness without strong aversion.
  • hesitancy
    4.2 0.7/m
    hesitancy is a state of doubt or slow decision, while reluctance is a broader unwillingness to take action.
  • hesitation
    2.8 8/m
    hesitation is a short pause before acting because of doubt, while reluctance is a lasting lack of willingness.
  • indisposition
    7.7 0.01/m
    indisposition often means a slight unwillingness or mild illness, while reluctance means a clear lack of enthusiasm to act.
