g-force

noun
force of acceleration relative to gravity
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒi ˌfɔrs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː ˌfɔːs / (BrE)
a unit of force equal to the force exerted by gravity on a body; used to express the acceleration force experienced relative to standard gravity

Examples

High g-force
Astronauts must train to withstand high levels of g-force during launch.
Roller coasters subject riders to brief bursts of intense g-force.
The pilot experienced a sudden increase in g-force during the steep ascent.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gravity - the force that attracts a body toward the center of the earth
