philoprogenitive

adjective
highly fertile
Pronunciation: / ˌfɪloʊproʊˈdʒɛnətɪv / (AmE), / ˌfɪləʊprəˈdʒɛnɪtɪv / (BrE)
having a strong tendency to produce many offspring; highly fertile or prolific.

Examples

Philoprogenitive animals
The philoprogenitive rabbit population quickly overwhelmed the small island.
Some plants are so philoprogenitive that they can cover an entire field in one season.
The family was known for being philoprogenitive, with many children in each generation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: barren - unable to produce offspring or fruit
  • synonym: fertile - able to produce many young or much fruit
  • synonym: prolific - producing many offspring or much fruit
