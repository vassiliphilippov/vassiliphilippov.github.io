Smithian

adjective
of Adam Smith's ideas
Pronunciation: / ˈsmɪθiən / (AmE), / ˈsmɪθiən / (BrE)
relating to the ideas or theories of Adam Smith, especially in economics or free market principles

Examples

Smithian economics
The government adopted a Smithian approach, emphasizing minimal intervention in the economy.
Many economists debate whether modern policies are truly Smithian in their support of free markets.
Her thesis explores the influence of Smithian thought on contemporary economic theory.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Keynesian - relating to the economic theories of John Maynard Keynes
