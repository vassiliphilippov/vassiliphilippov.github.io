contrariness

noun
deliberate stubborn resistance
7.7 0.08/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàtr…õri.n…ôs /; / k…ônÀàtr…õr.i.n…ôs / (AmE), / k…ônÀàtr…ëÀêr.i.n…™s /; / k…ônÀàtr…ëÀê.ri.n…ôs / (BrE)
the quality of being deliberately difficult, uncooperative, or inclined to oppose others

Examples

Deliberate contrariness
Despite repeated instructions, his contrariness ensured that he did the opposite of what was asked.
Her contrariness made even simple tasks unnecessarily difficult for the entire team.
The child's contrariness was evident when she refused to eat anything offered to her.
He displayed a certain contrariness that set him apart from his more agreeable siblings.
Her persistent contrariness made it challenging to organize group activities.
The professor's contrariness showed in his unconventional teaching methods and critical questions.

Synonyms

  • cussedness
    9.9 0.02/m
    cussedness is a stubborn, deliberately disagreeable attitude often used in everyday speech, while contrariness names opposing others more generally.
  • oppositionality
    9.2 0.02/m
    oppositionality is a formal term for active resistance in social or political settings, while contrariness is general uncooperativeness.
  • obstinacy
    7.6 0.48/m
    obstinacy is steady refusal to change one's view or action, while contrariness often means opposing others to be difficult.
  • perversity
    8.6 0.98/m
    perversity is a willful turning away from reason or accepted standards, while contrariness is simply being difficult or oppositional.

Related Terms

  • antonym: compliance - willingness to follow rules or guidance
  • synonym: obstinacy - stubborn refusal to change opinion or action
