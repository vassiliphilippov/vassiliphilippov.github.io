drippily

adverb
in an excessively sentimental manner
Pronunciation: / ˈdrɪpɪli / (AmE), / ˈdrɪpɪli / (BrE)
In a mawkish, excessively sentimental, or emotional manner.

Examples

To speak drippily
He spoke drippily about his childhood memories, leaving the audience slightly embarrassed.
She tends to write drippily sentimental poems that some readers find overly emotional.
The movie ended drippily, with everyone hugging and crying in slow motion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dryly - in a matter-of-fact or unsentimental way
  • synonym: mawkishly - in a sentimental or overly emotional way
