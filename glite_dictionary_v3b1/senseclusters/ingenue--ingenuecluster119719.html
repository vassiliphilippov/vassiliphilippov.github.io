ingenue

noun
innocent young woman role (acting)
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌænʒəˈnu /; / ˈænʒəˌnu / (AmE), / ˈænʒeɪnjuː /; / ˈænʒəˌnjuː / (BrE)
an innocent, naive, or unsophisticated young woman, especially as a character in a play, film, or literature, or the actress who plays such a role

Throughout the novel, the ingenue learns to navigate the complexities of adult life.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sophisticate - a person with much worldly experience and knowledge
