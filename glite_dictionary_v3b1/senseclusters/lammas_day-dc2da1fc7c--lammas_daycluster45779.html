Lammas Day

noun
harvest festival on 1 August
9.8
Pronunciation: /ˈlæ.məs deɪ/ (AmE), /ˈlæ.məs deɪ/ (BrE)
1 August, formerly observed as a Christian festival to mark the beginning of the harvest season, especially in England and Scotland, sometimes associated with Saint Peter's deliverance from prison.

Examples

Lammas Day festival
Every year, the village holds a fair to celebrate Lammas Day and the start of the harvest season.
In Scotland, rents and leases often began or ended on Lammas Day.
Traditionally, farmers brought loaves of bread to church on Lammas Day as an offering.
