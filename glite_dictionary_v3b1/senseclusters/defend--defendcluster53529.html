defend

verb
argue or speak in support of
1.0 2.8
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfɛnd / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛnd / (BrE)
to argue or speak in support of someone or something, especially when criticized or challenged

Examples

To defend an idea
He defended the proposal, explaining its potential benefits in detail.
It is important to defend your point of view in a debate.
She stood up to defend her colleague against the unfair accusations.
He strongly defended his position during the debate, despite heavy criticism.
She continued to defend her decision, explaining her reasoning to the committee.
The scientist defended her theory against skeptical colleagues in the conference.
