monoester

noun
compound with one ester group
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑnoʊˌɛstər / (AmE), / ˈmɒnəʊˌɛstə / (BrE)
a chemical compound that contains only one ester group in its molecule

Examples

A monoester molecule
The scientist isolated a monoester from the reaction mixture for further analysis.
Some enzymes specifically break down monoesters but not diesters.
A monoester can be identified by its unique chemical properties in laboratory tests.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: diester - a compound containing two ester groups
  • synonym: ester - a compound formed from an acid and an alcohol
