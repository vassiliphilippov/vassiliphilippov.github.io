subclimax

noun
secondary high point
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌklaɪmæks / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌklaɪmæks / (BrE)
a secondary or lesser point of excitement or tension in a narrative, speech, or series of events, occurring before the main climax

Examples

Story subclimax
The movie included a dramatic subclimax before the final showdown between the main characters.
Her speech built up to a subclimax that kept the audience engaged until the end.
Several subclimaxes in the novel added suspense before the ultimate resolution.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: climax - the most intense or exciting point in a story or event
