short-circuit

verb
to create a short circuit
7.3 4.6
Pronunciation: /ʃɔːrt ˈsɜːr.kət/ /; / /ʃɔːrt ˈsɜːr.kɪt/ (AmE), /ʃɔːt ˈsɜː.kɪt/ /; / /ʃɔːt ˈsɜː.kət/ (BrE)
to create a short circuit in an electrical device or circuit, typically by causing a path of lower resistance to bypass the intended circuit

Examples

To short-circuit a device
A loose connection could easily short-circuit the entire system.
He accidentally short-circuited the toy when he spilled water on it.
If you short-circuit the wires, the machine could be ruined.

  • synonym: overload - damage by too much current
