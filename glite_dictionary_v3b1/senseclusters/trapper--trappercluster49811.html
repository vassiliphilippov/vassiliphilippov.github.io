trapper

noun
person who traps animals for fur
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˈtræpər / (AmE), / ˈtræpə / (BrE)
a person who catches wild animals, especially for their fur, by setting traps

Examples

Fur trapper
During the 19th century, a trapper could make a living selling beaver pelts.
Modern wildlife laws limit what a trapper can catch and sell.
The trapper checked his snares early every morning in the forest.
