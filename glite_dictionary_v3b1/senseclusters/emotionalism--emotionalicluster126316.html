emotionalism

noun
tendency to be emotional or display strong emotions
6.0 7.3
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmoʊʃənəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪˈməʊʃənəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the quality or condition of being emotional, or the tendency to express emotions strongly, sometimes excessively

Examples

Excessive emotionalism
He criticized the speech for its excessive emotionalism rather than logical arguments.
Some appreciate emotionalism in poetry, while others prefer restraint.
The artist's work is characterized by intense emotionalism and vivid colors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sentimentality - the quality of being excessively emotional or sentimental
  • antonym: stoicism - the quality of being unemotional or showing restraint in emotions
