bit

noun
a small fragment from something larger
4.1
Pronunciation: / bɪt / (AmE), / bɪt / (BrE)
a small piece, part, or amount of something (including a small amount of food, time, or quantity)

Examples

A bit of cake
A bit of glass was stuck in her shoe after the accident.
Could you give me a bit of that chocolate bar?
He pulled a tiny bit of metal from the engine.
He took a bit and then wrapped up the rest for later.
She ate only a bit of the cake because she was full.
Would you like a bit of my sandwich?
Can you hold on for a bit while I finish this email?
I'll be ready in just a bit, so please wait.
It only took a bit for the water to boil.
