vanity press

noun
publisher that authors pay to print their books
9.1 6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈvæn.ə.tiː pres/ /; / /ˈvæn.ɪ.tiː pres/ (AmE), /ˈvæn.ɪ.ti pres/ /; / /ˈvæn.ə.ti pres/ (BrE)
a publishing business that prints books at the author's expense, often without editorial selection, typically catering to self-publishing authors

Examples

A vanity press publisher
After being rejected by traditional publishers, she decided to use a vanity press for her novel.
He discovered that his book would not be marketed widely if he used a vanity press.
Many aspiring writers are warned about the risks of paying for publication with a vanity press.
