rush

verb
carry the ball by running (football)
Pronunciation: / rʌʃ / (AmE), / rʌʃ / (BrE)
(in football) to carry the ball forward by running rather than passing it.

Examples

To rush the ball
He rushed the ball past the defense for a first down.
Quarterbacks sometimes rush when no receivers are open.
The running back rushed for 120 yards and two touchdowns in yesterday's game.

Related Terms

  • antonym: pass - to throw the ball to a teammate (football)
