IJsselmeer

proper noun
shallow artificial lake in the Netherlands
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪsəlˌmɪər / (AmE), / ˈeisəlˌmeə / (BrE)
a shallow artificial lake in northwestern Netherlands, created in 1932 by damming the Zuider Zee

Examples

The IJsselmeer lake
After the construction of the Afsluitdijk, the IJsselmeer replaced the Zuider Zee as a major inland water body.
Sailing across the IJsselmeer is a popular activity for tourists visiting the Netherlands.
The IJsselmeer serves as an important source of freshwater for the surrounding regions.
