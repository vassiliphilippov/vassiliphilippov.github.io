sorority

noun
female students' social club (college)
5.5
Pronunciation: / səˈrɔrəti / (AmE), / səˈrɒrəti / (BrE)
A social organization for female students at a college or university, especially in North America, formed chiefly for social, professional, or philanthropic purposes.

Examples

A sorority house
Emily decided to join a sorority to make friends and get involved on campus.
She attended a sorority meeting to learn more about their activities and values.
The sorority organized a charity event to raise money for local shelters.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fraternity - a social club for male undergraduates
