rip

noun
an act of tearing or splitting
1.9 1.11/m
Pronunciation: / rÉªp / (AmE), / rÉªp / (BrE)
a tear or split made by pulling something apart quickly and forcefully, especially in fabric or material

Examples

A rip in jeans
He noticed a rip down the sleeve of his old jacket.
The rip in the fabric was caused by catching it on a nail.
With a loud rip, she opened the package in seconds.
She tried to sew the rip in her dress before the party.
The rip along the seam made the jeans unwearable.
There was a rip in the sleeve of his jacket after the fall.

Synonyms

  • tear
    1.0 10/m
    tear is an ordinary word for a fabric opening made by force, while rip emphasizes a quick, violent pull.
  • rent
    8.4 0.78/m
    rent is a formal or old-fashioned word for a forced opening in cloth, while rip sounds more common and quick.
  • split
    1.1 1.11/m
    split is a general break or crack that can be slow or wide, while rip is a sharp, quick tear from pulling.
