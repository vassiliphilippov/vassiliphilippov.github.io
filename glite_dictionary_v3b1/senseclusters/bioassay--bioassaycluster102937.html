bioassay

noun
test measuring a substance's biological effects
9.9 6.9
Pronunciation: /ˈbaɪ.oʊ.æs.eɪ/ (AmE), /ˈbaɪ.əʊ.æs.eɪ/ (BrE)
A scientific test to measure the biological activity of a substance by examining its effect on living organisms and comparing the results with a standard.

Examples

A bioassay test
A mosquito bioassay was conducted to evaluate the effectiveness of the insecticide.
Researchers performed a bioassay to determine the potency of the new drug.
The lab uses bioassays to detect toxic substances in samples.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assay - determination of the content or quality of a substance
