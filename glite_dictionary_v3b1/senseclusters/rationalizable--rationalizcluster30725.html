rationalizable

adjective
justifiable in game theory
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌræʃənəˈlaɪzəbəl / (AmE), / ˌræʃənəˈlaɪzəbəl / (BrE)
able to be justified as a best response to some beliefs about other players' strategies, especially after eliminating inferior options in game theory.

Examples

Rationalizable strategy
In the final round, only rationalizable strategies remained for the players to consider.
Economists often analyze which choices are rationalizable in complex strategic games.
A rationalizable move is one that can be explained as a logical response to others' possible actions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: irrational - not logical or reasonable
  • synonym: optimal - the best or most effective possible in a situation
