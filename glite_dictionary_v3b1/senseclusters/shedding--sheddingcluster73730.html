shedding

noun
release of virus particles
4.8
Pronunciation: /ˈʃed.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈʃed.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
(in medicine, especially virology and epidemiology) the release of virus particles from an infected person, animal, or environment, which can allow infection to spread to others.

Examples

Virus shedding
Public health officials monitor shedding to determine when patients are most contagious during outbreaks.
High levels of nasal shedding were detected in volunteers after they received the experimental vaccine.
Researchers measured viral shedding rates to assess how long infected individuals may spread the disease.

Related Terms

  • synonym: transmission - the passing of an infectious agent from one host or source to another.
  • synonym: viral shedding - the release of virus particles from an infected host (same medical concept).
