retread

noun
remake or re-release of an old work
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˈriːˌtrɛd / (AmE), / ˈriːˌtrɛd / (BrE)
a new version of something that has appeared or been done before, especially a remake or re-release of an old film or program

Examples

A movie retread
Many critics complained that the movie felt like a dull retread of earlier science fiction hits.
The director's latest film is a retread of a classic comedy from the 1980s.
While some fans enjoy a retread, others prefer original material over remakes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: original - the first or earliest version of something, not a remake or copy
  • synonym: remake - a new version of an earlier film or work
