responsive

adjective
alternating or involving replies; in exchange
1.7 3.6
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈspɑːn.sɪv/ /; / /rə.ˈspɑːn.sɪv/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈspɒn.sɪv/ /; / /rə.ˈspɒn.sɪv/ (BrE)
(of an activity or communication) involving an exchange of responses or replies, especially alternately between two parties.

Examples

Responsive prayers
The ceremony included responsive prayers between the leader and the audience.
The congregation participated in a responsive reading during the service.
Their laughter had an almost responsive quality, echoing back and forth across the room.
