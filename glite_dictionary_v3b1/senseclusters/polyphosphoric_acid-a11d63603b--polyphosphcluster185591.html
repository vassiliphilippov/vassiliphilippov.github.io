polyphosphoric acid

noun
a group of oxyacids of phosphorus
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑliˌfɑsˈfɔrɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˌfɒsˈfɒrɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
any of a series of oxyacids of phosphorus that contain more than one phosphoric acid unit linked together, often used in chemical synthesis and as a dehydrating agent

Examples

Polyphosphoric acid in synthesis
Polyphosphoric acid is often used as a dehydrating agent in organic synthesis.
Some industrial processes require the use of polyphosphoric acid to modify the properties of materials.
The laboratory ordered a new supply of polyphosphoric acid for chemical experiments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: phosphoric acid - an oxyacid of phosphorus commonly used in fertilizers and soft drinks
