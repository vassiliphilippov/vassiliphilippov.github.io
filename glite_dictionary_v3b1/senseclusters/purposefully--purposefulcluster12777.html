purposefully

adverb
with determination or clear aim
3.9 3.25/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…úÀêrp…ôsf…ôli / (AmE), / Ààp…úÀêp…ôsf…ôli / (BrE)
In a way that shows determination or has a clear aim; done with a particular purpose.

Examples

Walk purposefully forward
He walked purposefully toward the exit, determined to leave the meeting early.
She purposefully ignored his comments, focusing on her work instead.
The artist arranged the objects purposefully to achieve the perfect composition.

Synonyms

    3.5 4.57/m
    purposely is a common adverb used in everyday speech for deliberate acts, while purposefully often adds stronger aim or determination.
  • intentionally
    3.1 11/m
    intentionally means doing something on purpose for a clear reason, while purposefully also suggests a planned aim and firm resolve.
  • deliberately
    3.7 22/m
    deliberately describes doing something with care or thought, while purposefully focuses more on a clear aim or strong determination.
  • determinedly
    3.8 0.78/m
    determinedly shows firm effort or resolve, while purposefully adds the idea of acting with a clear reason or plan.
  • resolutely
    4.4 2.08/m
    resolutely describes steady, unwavering action, while purposefully combines that firmness with a clear aim or deliberate intention.
  • on purpose
    1.6 6.03/m
    on purpose means not by accident and is often informal, while purposefully highlights deliberate, goal-directed action with determination.
  • by design
    4.0 2.16/m
    by design means planned or made that way, often in systems, while purposefully focuses on a person's clear aim and determined action.
  • consciously
    3.7 9.90/m
    consciously means with full awareness of what one does, while purposefully adds a sense of clear aim and determined effort.
  • wittingly
    7.5 0.20/m
    wittingly means doing something with knowledge and intent, often formal or rare, while purposefully stresses clear aim and firm resolve.
