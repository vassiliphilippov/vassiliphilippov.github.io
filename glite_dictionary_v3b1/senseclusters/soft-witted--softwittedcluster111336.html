soft-witted

adjective
lacking intelligence or sense
Pronunciation: / ˌsɑftˈwɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌsɒftˈwɪtɪd / (BrE)
Lacking intelligence, sense, or good judgment; foolish or slow to understand.

Examples

A soft-witted person
Only a soft-witted person would fall for such an obvious scam.
People considered him soft-witted because he believed every story he heard.
The novel portrays a soft-witted character who is easily manipulated by others.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sensible - showing good sense or judgment
  • synonym: stupid - lacking intelligence or common sense
