alliterative

adjective
repeating initial consonants
Pronunciation: / əˈlɪt̬ɚˌeɪtɪv / (AmE), / əˈlɪtərətɪv / (BrE)
using or containing words that begin with the same sound or letter, especially at the beginning of each stressed syllable.

Examples

Alliterative verse
In Old English poetry, alliterative verse was a popular literary technique.
Many nursery rhymes are written in alliterative language to make them memorable for children.
The poet crafted an alliterative line to emphasize the sound of the sea.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alliteration - the repetition of the same consonant at the beginning of adjacent or closely connected words
