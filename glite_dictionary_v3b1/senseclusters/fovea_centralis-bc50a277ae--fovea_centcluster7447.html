fovea centralis

noun
central retinal depression for sharpest vision
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊ.vi.ə sɛnˈtreɪ.lɪs / (AmE), / ˌfəʊ.vi.ə sɛnˈtreɪ.lɪs / (BrE)
A small depression in the center of the retina, containing densely packed cones and responsible for the sharpest vision.

Examples

The fovea centralis area
Damage to the fovea centralis can cause a significant loss of visual acuity.
In humans, the highest concentration of cones is found in the fovea centralis of the retina.
The fovea centralis is responsible for sharp central vision needed for activities like reading and driving.

Related Terms

  • synonym: macula - the area of the retina surrounding the fovea centralis
