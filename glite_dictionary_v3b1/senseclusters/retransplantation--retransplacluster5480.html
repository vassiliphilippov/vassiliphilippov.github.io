retransplantation

noun
repeat organ transplant
Pronunciation: / ˌriːtrænsplænˈteɪʃən /; / ˌriːtrænˌsplænˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːtrænsplɑːnˈteɪʃən /; / ˌriːtrænˌsplɑːnˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of performing a transplant again, especially of an organ, tissue, or cells, after a previous transplant has failed, been rejected, or for research purposes
