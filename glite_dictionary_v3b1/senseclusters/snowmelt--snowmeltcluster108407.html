snowmelt

noun
melted snow water
Pronunciation: / ˈsnoʊˌmɛlt / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊˌmɛlt / (BrE)
water produced by the melting of snow, especially during the spring

Examples

Snowmelt water
The river rose quickly due to heavy snowmelt in the mountains.
Farmers rely on snowmelt each spring to irrigate their fields.
Rapid snowmelt can sometimes cause flooding in low-lying areas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: runoff - water that flows over the ground surface after rain or snowmelt
