blunder

verb
make a serious or careless mistake
2.1 3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈblʌn.dər/ (AmE), /ˈblʌn.də/ (BrE)
to make a careless or serious mistake, either by doing or saying something without thinking

Examples

To blunder badly
He blundered into the wrong meeting room by mistake.
She realized she had blundered only after seeing the disappointed faces around her.
The company blundered when it released a faulty product to the public.
During the interview, I blundered details I should have kept private.
He sometimes blunders inappropriate remarks during meetings.
Without thinking, she blundered the truth to the entire group.

  • synonym: err - make a mistake or error
  • synonym: misjudge - form a wrong opinion or conclusion
