battle of Ipsus

noun
decisive battle between Alexander's successors, 301 BC
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbæt̬əl əv ˈɪpsəs / (AmE), / ˈbætəl əv ˈɪpsəs / (BrE)
a decisive battle fought in 301 BC between the successors of Alexander the Great, in which Lysimachus and Seleucus defeated Antigonus and Demetrius

Examples

The battle of Ipsus
Historians consider the battle of Ipsus a turning point in the division of Alexander's empire.
Many ancient sources recount the strategies used in the battle of Ipsus.
The battle of Ipsus saw the combined forces of Lysimachus and Seleucus emerge victorious.
