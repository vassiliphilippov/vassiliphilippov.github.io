disconsolation

noun
utter sadness
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪskɒnsəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪskɒnsəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a state of being unable to be comforted or cheered up

Examples

Deep disconsolation
A deep sense of disconsolation overcame him after hearing the tragic news.
The team's defeat brought a feeling of disconsolation to their loyal fans.
She sat in silence, lost in her disconsolation after the breakup.

Related Terms

  • antonym: consolation - comfort received after a loss or disappointment
  • synonym: despair - a complete loss of hope
