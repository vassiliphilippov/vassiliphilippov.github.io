pion

noun
subatomic particle (meson) in atomic nuclei
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪ.ɑn / (AmE), / ˈpaɪ.ɒn / (BrE)
a subatomic particle of the meson family, involved in holding atomic nuclei together, produced in high-energy collisions

Examples

A pion particle
A pion decays into lighter particles shortly after being produced in cosmic ray interactions.
Physicists detected a pion during their high-energy experiment in the particle accelerator.
The exchange of pions between protons and neutrons helps bind the nucleus together.

Related Terms

  • synonym: meson - subatomic particle composed of one quark and one antiquark
