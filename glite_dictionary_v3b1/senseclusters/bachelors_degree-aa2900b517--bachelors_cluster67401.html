bachelor's degree

noun
undergraduate academic degree
Pronunciation: / ˈbætʃələrz dɪˌɡri / (AmE), / ˈbætʃələz dɪˌɡriː / (BrE)
An academic degree awarded to students who have completed undergraduate studies at a college or university.

Examples

A bachelor's degree in biology
After four years of study, she finally earned her bachelor's degree in biology.
He is considering returning to university for a second bachelor's degree in computer science.
Most entry-level positions require at least a bachelor's degree from an accredited institution.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: master's degree - academic degree above bachelor's, for graduate studies
