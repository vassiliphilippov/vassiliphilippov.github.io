carnivalesque

adjective
subverting order humorously
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑrnɪvəˈlɛsk / (AmE), / ˌkɑːnɪvəˈlɛsk / (BrE)
relating to a style or atmosphere that subverts social order through humor, chaos, and mockery of authority.

Examples

Carnivalesque humor
The play's carnivalesque atmosphere allowed the audience to laugh at traditional authority figures.
Her paintings are filled with carnivalesque imagery, blending chaos and humor in every scene.
The festival took on a carnivalesque spirit, with costumes and performances that turned social norms upside down.

Related Terms

  • antonym: orderly - organized and following rules or structure
  • synonym: satirical - using humor or exaggeration to criticize or mock
