periodic movement

noun
motion repeating at constant intervals
2.5 4.9
Pronunciation: /pɪr.iː.ˈɑː.dɪk ˈmuːv.mənt/ (AmE), /pɪə.ˈrɒd.ɪk ˈmuːv.mənt/ /; / /pɪr.ɪ.ˈɒd.ɪk ˈmuːv.mənt/ (BrE)
motion that repeats at regular intervals, with each cycle taking the same amount of time

Examples

Regular periodic movement
Many biological processes display periodic movement, such as the rhythmic beating of a heart.
The pendulum of a clock exhibits periodic movement, swinging back and forth at a consistent rate.
Waves on the surface of the ocean are a clear example of periodic movement in nature.
