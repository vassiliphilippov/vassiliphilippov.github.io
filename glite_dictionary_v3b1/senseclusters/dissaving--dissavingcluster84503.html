dissaving

adjective
spending savings
Pronunciation: / dɪˈseɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈseɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by spending savings instead of increasing them, often resulting in reduced personal wealth.

Examples

Dissaving behavior
Periods of dissaving are common among retirees who rely on their accumulated funds.
A dissaving trend can indicate that households are struggling to cover their expenses.
Economic downturns often lead to increased dissaving as people use their savings to get by.

Related Terms

  • antonym: saving - the act of setting aside money rather than spending it
