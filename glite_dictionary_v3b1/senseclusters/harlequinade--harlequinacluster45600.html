harlequinade

noun
comic clowning or buffoonery act
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhɑːr.lə.kwɪˈneɪd / (AmE), / ˌhɑː.lɪ.kwɪˈneɪd / (BrE)
a performance or behavior using comic clowning or buffoonery, especially in the style of the character Harlequin in traditional pantomime

Examples

A harlequinade performance
She grew tired of his constant harlequinade during serious discussions.
The comedian's performance turned into a harlequinade that had the entire audience laughing.
Their meeting descended into a harlequinade as everyone began acting foolishly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: farce - a comic dramatic work using buffoonery and horseplay
