pale

verb
become pale in color (from fear, etc.)
Pronunciation: / peɪl / (AmE), / peɪl / (BrE)
to become pale in color, especially from fear, shock, or illness

Examples

To pale with fear
He seemed to pale visibly at the mention of her name.
Her cheeks would always pale whenever she heard bad news.
The boy began to pale as the doctor approached with the needle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blanch - to become pale, especially suddenly or from shock
  • antonym: flush - to become red in the face, usually from emotion
