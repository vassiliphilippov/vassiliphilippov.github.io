viscacha

noun
South American burrowing rodent
9.9 8.6
Pronunciation: /vɪ.ˈskɑː.tʃə/ /; / /vɪ.ˈskæ.tʃə/ (AmE), /vɪ.ˈskæ.tʃə/ /; / /vɪ.ˈskɑː.tʃə/ (BrE)
A gregarious burrowing rodent of South America, larger than a chinchilla, known for its soft fur and social colonies.

Examples

A viscacha rodent
A group of viscachas was seen darting in and out of their burrows at dusk.
The soft fur of the viscacha is prized in some regions for making clothing.
Unlike chinchillas, viscachas are known for creating elaborate tunnel systems underground.
