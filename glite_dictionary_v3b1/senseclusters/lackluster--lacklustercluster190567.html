lackluster

adjective
dull; lacking brightness or vitality
6.9 4.9
Pronunciation: /ˈlæk.lʌs.tər/ /; / /ˈlæk.lə.stər/ (AmE), /ˈlæk.lʌs.tə/ /; / /ˈlæk.lə.stə/ (BrE)
dull or lacking in brightness, energy, or vitality; uninspired

Examples

A lackluster performance
He gazed at me with lackluster eyes, showing no sign of excitement.
Her performance was disappointingly lackluster compared to her usual energetic style.
The movie received criticism for its lackluster plot and uninspired dialogue.
Her lackluster response to the news made everyone wonder if she cared at all.
The movie received poor reviews due to its lackluster plot and acting.
The team's lackluster performance disappointed fans who expected more excitement.
Despite her fame, her latest movie received mostly lacklustre reviews from critics.
He looked up with lacklustre eyes, clearly exhausted from the journey.
The team gave a lacklustre performance in last night's match, disappointing their fans.
