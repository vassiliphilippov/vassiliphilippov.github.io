pit

noun
trading area on a commodity exchange floor
Pronunciation: / pɪt / (AmE), / pɪt / (BrE)
a special area or arena for a particular activity, such as trading, music, animal fighting, or car racing

Examples

A trading pit
He made his fortune working in the options pit during the 1980s.
The soybean pit was unusually busy after the government crop report was released.
Traders gathered in the pit to shout and signal orders throughout the day.
From my seat in the balcony, I could see the conductor clearly in the pit.
The musicians gathered in the pit before the performance began.
The new theater design features a larger pit to accommodate more orchestra members.
Authorities raided the warehouse and found a pit used for animal fights.
Illegal dog fights took place in the pit behind the old factory.
The cockfighting pit was hidden from public view.
