evaporate

verb
remove liquid by vaporization
4.3 3.5
Pronunciation: / ɪˈvæpəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈvapəreɪt / (BrE)
to change from a liquid (or solid) into vapor, especially by heating, or to cause this to happen

Examples

To evaporate water
If you evaporate water from the mixture, the flavor will become more intense.
The solution was heated to evaporate the excess solvent, leaving crystals behind.
To make condensed milk, you must evaporate most of the water from the milk.
Applying heat will quickly evaporate the liquid in the container.
Scientists evaporate the solvent to isolate the solid compound.
The sun can evaporate water from the surface of a lake.
After the rain, the puddles on the street began to evaporate.
Heat from the stove caused the soup to evaporate over time.
If you leave a glass of water in the sun, it will evaporate quickly.
