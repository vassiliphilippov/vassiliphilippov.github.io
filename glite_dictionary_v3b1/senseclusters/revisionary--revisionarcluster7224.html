revisionary

noun
advocate of change
8.8
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvɪʒəˌnɛri / (AmE), / rɪˈvɪʒənəri / (BrE)
a person who advocates for changing or reinterpreting established beliefs, doctrines, or historical views

Examples

A revisionary historian
The historian was known as a revisionary who questioned accepted narratives about the past.
Many considered her a revisionary for proposing new interpretations of classic literature.
As a revisionary, he often faced criticism from those who preferred traditional viewpoints.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reformer - a person who works to change and improve a system or practice
  • antonym: traditionalist - a person who supports maintaining established customs or beliefs
