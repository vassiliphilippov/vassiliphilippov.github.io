Hanukkah

proper noun
Jewish festival of lights
5.4
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑːnəkə / (AmE), / ˈhænəkə / (BrE)
an eight-day Jewish festival commemorating the rededication of the Second Temple in Jerusalem, celebrated by lighting candles each night

Examples

Hanukkah begins
Each night during Hanukkah, families gather to light the menorah and sing traditional songs.
Children often receive small gifts or chocolate coins as part of the Hanukkah celebrations.
The story of Hanukkah teaches about perseverance and the importance of faith.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Christmas - a Christian holiday celebrating the birth of Jesus Christ
  • synonym: Passover - a major Jewish festival commemorating the Exodus from Egypt
