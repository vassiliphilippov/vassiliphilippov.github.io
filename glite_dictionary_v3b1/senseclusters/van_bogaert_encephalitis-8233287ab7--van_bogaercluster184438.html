Van Bogaert encephalitis

noun
rare chronic brain inflammation from measles virus
9.9
Pronunciation: /væn ˈboʊ.gɑːrt en.sef.ə.ˈlaɪ.tɪs/ /; / /væn bə.ˈgɑːrt en.sef.ə.ˈlaɪ.tɪs/ (AmE), /væn ˈbəʊ.gɑːt en.sef.ə.ˈlaɪ.tɪs/ /; / /væn bə.ˈgɑːt en.sef.ə.ˈlaɪ.tɪs/ (BrE)
A rare, chronic, and progressive brain inflammation caused by measles virus, primarily affecting children and young adults, and often leading to death within a few years.

Examples

Van Bogaert encephalitis disease
Doctors diagnosed the child with Van Bogaert encephalitis after a series of neurological tests and his history of measles infection.
Research into Van Bogaert encephalitis has revealed the importance of early measles vaccination.
Symptoms of Van Bogaert encephalitis include cognitive decline and seizures in previously healthy children.

  • synonym: subacute sclerosing panencephalitis - Rare, chronic, progressive neurological disorder caused by persistent measles virus infection
