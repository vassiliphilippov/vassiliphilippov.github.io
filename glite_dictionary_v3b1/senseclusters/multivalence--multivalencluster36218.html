multivalence

noun
state of having a valence greater than two
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌltiˈveɪləns / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪˈveɪl(ə)ns / (BrE)
(Chemistry) The property of having a valence greater than two; the ability of an atom to form more than two chemical bonds.

Examples

The multivalence of atoms
Certain transition metals exhibit multivalence in their chemical behavior.
Researchers studied the multivalence of manganese in biological systems.
The multivalence of iron allows it to form compounds with different properties.

Related Terms

  • synonym: valence - The capacity of an atom or molecule to combine with others, measured by the number of bonds formed
