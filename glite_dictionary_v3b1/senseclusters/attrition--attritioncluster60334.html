attrition

noun
gradual weakening or wearing down
9.2 6.3
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈtrɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ə.ˈtrɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of gradually weakening or destroying something through sustained pressure or repeated attacks

Examples

Staff attrition
Constant employee turnover led to a slow attrition of the company's workforce.
The army's strategy relied on a war of attrition to exhaust the enemy.
Through years of neglect, the building suffered significant attrition and decay.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abrasion - the process of scraping or wearing away
  • antonym: building-up - the process of increasing strength or intensity
  • synonym: erosion - the gradual destruction or diminution of something
