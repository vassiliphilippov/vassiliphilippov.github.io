introduce

verb
to bring in something or someone new
2.1 56/m
Pronunciation: / Àå…™ntr…ôÀàdus / (AmE), / Àå…™ntr…ôÀàdjuÀês / (BrE)
to bring or present a new person, thing, idea, or system into a place, group, or situation for the first time

Examples

Introduce a new product
The company will introduce innovative products to attract more customers.
The school plans to introduce new technology into its classrooms next year.
They decided to introduce a new member to the committee during the meeting.
The discovery of penicillin introduced a new era in modern medicine.
The invention of the internet introduced unprecedented changes to global communication.
The leader's reforms introduced significant improvements in education throughout the country.
She introduced the composer to the audience before the performance began.
The company will introduce its new smartphone at the conference next week.
The director hopes to introduce several talented young actors in his latest film.

Synonyms

  • bring in
    2.9 25/m
    bring in is used to cause a new person or thing to join a group or situation, while introduce is general for first presentation.
  • bring out
    2.7 0.56/m
    bring out is to make something available to the public, especially products or books, while introduce covers any first presentation.
  • inaugurate
    6.3 3.46/m
    inaugurate is to officially begin something, often with a ceremony, while introduce is any first presentation or entry.
  • usher in
    4.6 2.84/m
    usher in is to mark the start of an era or event, while introduce means to present something for the first time.
