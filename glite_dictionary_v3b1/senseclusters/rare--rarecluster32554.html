rare

adjective
meat lightly cooked and still red inside
6.0
Pronunciation: / rɛr / (AmE), / reə / (BrE)
(of meat) cooked for only a short time, so that it remains red and soft inside.

Examples

Rare steak
At the restaurant, she ordered a rare hamburger despite warnings about undercooked meat.
He prefers his steak rare, with a cool, red center.
Many chefs recommend serving lamb rare to keep it tender and juicy.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: medium - (of meat) cooked longer than rare but still slightly pink inside
  • degree higher: well-done - (of meat) cooked thoroughly so that no pink remains
