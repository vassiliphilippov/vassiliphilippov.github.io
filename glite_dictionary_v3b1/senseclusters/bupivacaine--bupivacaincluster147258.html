bupivacaine

noun
local anesthetic drug
9.1
Pronunciation: / bjuˌpɪvəˈkeɪn / (AmE), / bjuˌpɪvəˈkeɪn / (BrE)
a local anesthetic drug used to numb a specific area during surgery or medical procedures

Examples

bupivacaine injection
The doctor used bupivacaine to numb the area before stitching the wound.
After receiving bupivacaine, the patient felt no pain during the minor procedure.
Dentists sometimes choose bupivacaine for longer-lasting numbness during oral surgery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anesthetic - a substance that causes loss of sensation
  • synonym: lidocaine - a commonly used local anesthetic drug
