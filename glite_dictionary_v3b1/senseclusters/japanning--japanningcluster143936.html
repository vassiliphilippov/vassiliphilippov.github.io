japanning

verb
to varnish surfaces
9.2
Pronunciation: /dʒə.ˈpæn.ɪŋ/ /; / /dʒæ.ˈpæn.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /dʒə.ˈpæn.ɪŋ/ /; / /dʒæ.ˈpæn.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
to apply a hard, glossy varnish or lacquer, especially to metal or wood, to create a shiny, decorative finish.

Examples

To japan a box
The craftsman spent hours japanning the wooden box to achieve a deep, glossy finish.
Restorers are japanning antique trays to bring back their original shine and color.
She learned the art of japanning to decorate metal ornaments with a durable, beautiful coating.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: painting - to cover a surface with paint
