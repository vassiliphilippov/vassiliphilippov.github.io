at the threshold

adverb
at the point of beginning or entering
Pronunciation: / æt ðə ˈθrɛʃhoʊld / (AmE), / ət ðə ˈθrɛʃhəʊld / (BrE)
at the point of beginning or entering; about to start something new or important

Examples

At the threshold of change
As the sun rose, the village found itself at the threshold of a new day.
She stood at the threshold of her new career, filled with excitement and uncertainty.
We are at the threshold of a major discovery that could change medicine forever.
