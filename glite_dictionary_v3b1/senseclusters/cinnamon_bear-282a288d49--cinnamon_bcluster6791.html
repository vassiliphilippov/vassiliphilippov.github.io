cinnamon bear

noun
reddish-brown phase of the black bear
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪnəmən bɛr / (AmE), / ˈsɪnəmən beə / (BrE)
A reddish-brown color phase of the American black bear (Ursus americanus), found mainly in western North America.

Examples

The cinnamon bear
The cinnamon bear is often mistaken for a different species due to its unique coloring.
Unlike the typical black bears, cinnamon bears have a distinctive reddish coat.
We spotted a cinnamon bear near the river while hiking in Montana.
