vena umbilicalis

noun
vein in umbilical cord carrying blood to fetus
Pronunciation: / ˌviːnə ʌmˌbɪlɪˈkeɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˌveːnə ʌmˌbɪlɪˈkeɪlɪs / (BrE)
the vein in the umbilical cord that carries oxygenated, nutrient-rich blood from the placenta to the fetus

Examples

The vena umbilicalis vein
Doctors examine the vena umbilicalis during prenatal ultrasounds for any abnormalities.
The vena umbilicalis closes soon after the baby is born, leaving a remnant in the liver.
The vena umbilicalis is essential for transporting oxygenated blood to the developing fetus.

Related Terms

  • synonym: umbilical vein - the vein present during fetal development that carries blood from the placenta to the fetus
