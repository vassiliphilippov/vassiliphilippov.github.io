Fourier series

noun
sum of trigonometric functions to represent periodic functions
8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈfʊr.i.eɪ ˈsɪr.iːz/ /; / /ˈfʊr.i.ər ˈsɪr.iːz/ /; / /ˈfɔːr.i.eɪ ˈsɪr.iːz/ (AmE), /ˈfʊə.ri.eɪ ˈsɪə.riːz/ /; / /ˈfʊə.ri.ə ˈsɪə.riːz/ (BrE)
A mathematical representation of a periodic function as the sum of sine and cosine terms with specific coefficients.

Examples

Fourier series analysis
Engineers use the Fourier series to analyze electrical signals that repeat periodically.
Every continuous periodic function can be written as a Fourier series under certain conditions.
In advanced mathematics, the Fourier series helps break down complex waveforms into simple components.
