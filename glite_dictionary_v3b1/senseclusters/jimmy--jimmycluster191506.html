jimmy

verb
to force open with a tool, especially illegally
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɪmi / (AmE), / ˈdʒɪmi / (BrE)
to force open (a door, window, etc.), typically with a crowbar or similar tool, especially illegally or forcibly

Examples

To jimmy a door
After several attempts, she finally jimmied the rusty lock and got inside the shed.
The thief managed to jimmy the window open using a screwdriver.
They tried to jimmy the door instead of finding the key.

Related Terms

  • synonym: force - to open something by using strength or effort
  • synonym: pry - to use force to move or open something
