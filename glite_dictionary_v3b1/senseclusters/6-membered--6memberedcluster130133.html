6-membered

adjective
having a six-atom ring structure
Pronunciation: / sɪks ˈmɛmbɚd / (AmE), / sɪks ˈmɛmbəd / (BrE)
(of a chemical compound) having a ring structure consisting of six atoms or members.

Examples

A 6-membered ring
Many organic compounds contain a 6-membered aromatic ring, such as benzene.
Pyridine is an example of a 6-membered heterocyclic compound.
The 6-membered ring in glucose creates its stable chair conformation.
