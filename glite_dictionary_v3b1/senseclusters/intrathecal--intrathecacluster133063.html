intrathecal

adjective
within spinal canal
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntrəˈθikəl / (AmE), / ˌɪntrəˈθiːkəl / (BrE)
relating to or occurring within the space surrounding the spinal cord, especially the area containing cerebrospinal fluid

Examples

Intrathecal injection
The patient received an intrathecal injection to deliver medication directly to the spinal fluid.
Doctors sometimes use intrathecal chemotherapy to treat cancers affecting the central nervous system.
An intrathecal pump can help manage severe chronic pain by releasing medicine near the spinal cord.

Related Terms

  • antonym: epidural - relating to the area outside the dura mater of the spinal cord
