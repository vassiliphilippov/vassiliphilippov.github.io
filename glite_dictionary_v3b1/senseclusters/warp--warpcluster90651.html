warp

noun
twisted or distorted shape
Pronunciation: / wɔrp / (AmE), / wɔːp / (BrE)
A distortion or twist in shape, usually caused by bending, folding, or external forces.

Examples

A plastic warp
Exposure to heat can cause a plastic sheet to form a warp.
The craftsman examined the warp in the metal before attempting to fix it.
The wooden shelf developed a noticeable warp after years of holding heavy books.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bend - a curve or angular deviation in something straight
  • synonym: twist - an act of twisting or the state of being twisted
