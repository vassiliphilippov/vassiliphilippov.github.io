come on

verb
to appear or make an entrance
1.0
Pronunciation: / kʌm ɑn / (AmE), / kʌm ɒn / (BrE)
to appear or become visible; to make a showing or an entrance

Examples

The actor comes on stage
I waited anxiously for the show to come on.
The missing document may come on when we least expect it.
The sun finally began to come on through the clouds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: surface - to become visible or apparent after being hidden
  • synonym: turn up - to arrive or appear unexpectedly or after being lost
