come on

verb
to appear or make an entrance
2.1 2.53/m
Pronunciation: / kÊŒm É‘n / (AmE), / kÊŒm É’n / (BrE)
to appear or become visible; to make a showing or an entrance

Examples

The actor comes on stage
I waited anxiously for the show to come on.
The missing document may come on when we least expect it.
The sun finally began to come on through the clouds.

Synonyms

  • come out
    2.3 20/m
    come out is used when something emerges or becomes visible, while come on also suggests making a show or entrance.
  • appear
    1.0 86/m
    appear comes from sight or existence in a calm way, while come on can mean entering to be seen or noticed.
  • show up
    1.4 63/m
    show up is often used for arriving at a place, while come on focuses more on making a visible entrance or showing.
  • make an entrance
    4.9 0.17/m
    make an entrance is arriving in a dramatic, attention-getting way, while come on can simply mean appearing or coming into view.
  • turn up
    2.0 5.52/m
    turn up often means arrive unexpectedly or after being lost, while come on usually means appear or make a visible entrance.
