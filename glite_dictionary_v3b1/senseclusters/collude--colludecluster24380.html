collude

verb
secretly cooperate for deceitful or illegal purpose
8.3 1.62/m
Pronunciation: / k…ôÀàlud / (AmE), / k…ôÀàluÀêd / (BrE)
to secretly work together with others in order to deceive or cheat someone

Examples

To collude on pricing
It is illegal for companies to collude on pricing to limit competition.
The officials were found to collude in rigging the election results.
They were accused of colluding with criminal organizations to avoid taxes.

Synonyms

  • coconspire
    9.7 0.00/m
    coconspire means to secretly join others in a plan to break the law, while collude focuses on cheating or deception.
  • conspire
    5.8 5.97/m
    conspire means secretly planning with others to do illegal acts, while collude stresses working together to deceive people.
  • scheme
    6.5 0.67/m
    scheme is to plan secret or dishonest actions for gain, while collude always implies working together to cheat someone.
  • connive
    8.8 0.84/m
    connive means secretly allow or help wrong acts by ignoring them, while collude means actively work together to deceive.
