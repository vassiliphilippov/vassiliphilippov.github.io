cathexis

noun
psychoanalytic term for invested mental energy
Pronunciation: / kəˈθɛksɪs / (AmE), / kəˈθɛksɪs / (BrE)
(psychoanalysis) the concentration of mental or emotional energy, especially libidinal energy, on a particular person, idea, or object.

Examples

Cathexis of energy
During therapy, a patient's cathexis towards a parent may become apparent.
Freud believed the cathexis of energy could be redirected from one object to another.
The sudden withdrawal of cathexis from a goal can lead to disappointment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: libido - psychic energy associated with instinctual biological drives
