work

verb
be employed; have a job
5.2
Pronunciation: / wɜrk /; / wɝːk /; / wɝk / (AmE), / wɜːk / (BrE)
to have a job, be employed, or do tasks to achieve a goal

Examples

To work in an office
After college, she plans to work in the healthcare industry.
I used to work as a librarian before I changed careers.
Many people work late into the night to meet project deadlines.
He worked the team hard to prepare them for the championship match.
She works her horse for an hour every morning to keep it fit.
The manager worked his employees overtime during the busy season.
He worked the California market for more than a decade.
She works the morning shift at the hospital every weekday.
The company works mainly in the automotive sector these days.

  • synonym: labor - to work, especially hard physical work
  • synonym: operate - to control or work a machine or process
  • degree higher: overwork - to make someone work too hard or too much
  • synonym: perform - to carry out an action or task
  • antonym: rest - to cease work or movement in order to relax
