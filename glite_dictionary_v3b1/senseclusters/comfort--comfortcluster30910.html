comfort

noun
physical or emotional ease or satisfaction
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌmfərt /; / ˈkʌmfɚt / (AmE), / ˈkʌmfət / (BrE)
a state of physical ease and relaxation, free from pain or discomfort, or something that provides this feeling

Examples

Physical comfort
A warm blanket can provide comfort on a chilly night.
Her kind words brought me great comfort during a difficult time.
They enjoyed all the comforts of modern living in their new apartment.
After the operation, the nurses did everything they could to maintain his comfort.
Proper shoes are essential for your walking comfort on long hikes.
She found great comfort in her warm, cozy bed after a long day.

Related Terms

  • antonym: discomfort - a state of slight pain or unease
  • synonym: ease - Freedom from difficulty or pain
  • synonym: soothe - to make someone feel less upset, anxious, or distressed
