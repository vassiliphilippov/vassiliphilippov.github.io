solidaristic

adjective
sharing responsibility
Pronunciation: / ˌsɑːlɪdəˈrɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɒlɪdəˈrɪstɪk / (BrE)
organized or based on the principle of solidarity, where people support each other and share responsibilities or benefits equally

Examples

A solidaristic system
The new healthcare policy is solidaristic, ensuring everyone contributes and benefits equally.
A solidaristic approach to education means wealthier districts help support poorer ones.
Trade unions often promote solidaristic wage agreements to reduce inequality among workers.
The organization took a solidaristic stance, ensuring all members were treated equally.
A solidaristic community often works together to overcome challenges.
Their solidaristic values inspired them to support each other during difficult times.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collective - done by people acting as a group
  • antonym: individualistic - focused on individual rather than group interests
  • synonym: supportive - giving help or encouragement to others
