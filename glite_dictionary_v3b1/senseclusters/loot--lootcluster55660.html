loot

verb
take or use illegally (esp. ideas)
6.1 0.53/m
Pronunciation: / luÀêt / (AmE), / luÀêt / (BrE)
to take or use something illegally or without permission, especially in relation to intellectual property or ideas

Examples

To loot ideas
He was notorious for looting jokes from classic comedians.
Many artists believe their work has been looted by others without credit.
The professor accused the student of trying to loot ideas from published research.

Synonyms

  • plunder
    6.6 0.48/m
    plunder is to take work or ideas dishonestly and often without credit, while loot focuses on taking or using things without permission.
  • steal
    1.0 96/m
    steal means taking something secretly or by force, while loot often refers to taking or using ideas or work without permission.
  • purloin
    9.6 0.29/m
    purloin means to take something in a secret, dishonest way, while loot often means taking or using ideas or work without permission.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plagiarize - to use someone else's work or ideas as your own without permission
  • synonym: steal - to take something that does not belong to you
