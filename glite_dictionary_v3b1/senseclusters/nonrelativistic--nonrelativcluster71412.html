nonrelativistic

adjective
not involving relativity
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnrɛləˈvɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌnɒnrɛləˈvɪstɪk / (BrE)
not involving or relating to the theory of relativity, typically describing speeds much less than the speed of light

Examples

The nonrelativistic speed
The nonrelativistic equations are accurate when the object's speed is much less than the speed of light.
In a nonrelativistic system, Newton's laws of motion can be applied without correction.
Physicists often use nonrelativistic models to simplify calculations for slow-moving particles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: classical - relating to physics before the theory of relativity
  • antonym: relativistic - involving or relating to the theory of relativity
