pharmacodynamic

adjective
drug effects on body
Pronunciation: / ˌfɑːrməkodəˈnæmɪk / (AmE), / ˌfɑːməkəʊdaɪˈnæmɪk / (BrE)
relating to the effects of drugs on the body and how they produce their effects

Examples

The pharmacodynamic response
The pharmacodynamic response of the drug was measured in clinical trials.
Doctors consider both pharmacodynamic and pharmacokinetic factors when prescribing medication.
Understanding pharmacodynamic interactions helps prevent harmful side effects in patients.

Related Terms

  • antonym: pharmacokinetic - relating to how drugs move through the body
