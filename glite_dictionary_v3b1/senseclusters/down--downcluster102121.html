down

noun
soft fine feathers on birds
Pronunciation: / daʊn / (AmE), / daʊn / (BrE)
the soft, fine feathers or hair found on young birds or beneath the outer feathers of adult birds, or as an undercoat in some animals; also used as filling for bedding and clothing

Examples

Goose down
He collected the fluffy down that lined the bird's nest.
She bought a comforter filled with pure goose down for winter.
The down of the duck made the jacket incredibly warm and light.
Baby chicks are covered in a yellow down that keeps them warm.
The down of the duck is used to fill luxurious comforters and jackets.
The soft down on the underside of the leaf protects it from cold weather.

Related Terms

  • synonym: feather - one of the light, flat structures covering a bird's body
  • synonym: fluff - light, soft, or downy material, especially when loose or detached
  • degree higher: plumage - the feathers covering a bird's body (especially collectively)
