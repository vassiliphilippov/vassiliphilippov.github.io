dit

noun
short Morse code signal (dot)
9.9
Pronunciation: / dɪt / (AmE), / dɪt / (BrE)
the shorter of the two signals used in Morse code, representing a dot in written form

Examples

Dit and dah
A sequence of dits and dahs forms every letter in Morse code.
He practiced tapping out dit-dah patterns on the desk.
The letter 'E' in Morse code is a single dit.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dah - the longer signal in Morse code (dash)
