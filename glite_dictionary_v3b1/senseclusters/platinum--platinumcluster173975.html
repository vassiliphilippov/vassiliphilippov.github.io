platinum

noun
a chemical element; silvery precious metal
2.4
Pronunciation: / ˈplæt.nəm / (AmE), / ˈplæt.nəm / (BrE)
a heavy, valuable, silvery-white metallic element, highly resistant to corrosion, used especially in jewelry, scientific equipment, and as a catalyst

Examples

A platinum ring
Industrial chemists often use platinum as a catalyst in chemical reactions.
She received a platinum medal for her outstanding performance in the competition.
The ring was made of platinum, giving it a sleek and modern appearance.

  • synonym: silver - a soft, white, lustrous metallic element used for coins and jewelry
