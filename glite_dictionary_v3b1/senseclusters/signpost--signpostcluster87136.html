signpost

verb
mark with a signpost
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪnˌpoʊst / (AmE), / ˈsaɪnpəʊst / (BrE)
to mark or provide directions or information by putting up signposts

Examples

To signpost a path
If the route isn't signposted, hikers might easily take a wrong turn.
The hiking trail was carefully signposted to prevent anyone from getting lost.
Volunteers spent the afternoon signposting the new cycle path through the park.
Volunteers were signposting the footpaths early in the morning before the charity walk began.
The organizers signposted the event area to help visitors find their way easily.
Clear arrows signpost the different sections of the museum for all guests.

  • synonym: label - to mark or attach a label to something
  • synonym: mark - to indicate a place or position with a sign or symbol
