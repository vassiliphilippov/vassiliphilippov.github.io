inflation rate

noun
percentage increase in prices over time
1.5 3.1
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈfleɪ.ʃən reɪt/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈfleɪ.ʃən reɪt/ (BrE)
The percentage increase in the general level of prices for goods and services in an economy over a period, usually measured annually or monthly.

Examples

The inflation rate rises
A stable inflation rate is crucial for long-term economic planning.
Central banks closely monitor the inflation rate to make decisions about interest rates.
The country's inflation rate rose to 8% last year, causing concern among economists.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deflation rate - the rate at which the general price level decreases over a period
