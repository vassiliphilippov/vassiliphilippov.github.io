martlet

noun
archaic bird name
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːrtlət / (AmE), / ˈmɑːtlɪt / (BrE)
an archaic or poetic term for a small swallow-like bird, especially a martin or swallow.

Examples

A poetic martlet
In old English poetry, the martlet is often mentioned as a symbol of freedom.
The castle's towers were decorated with carvings of the martlet in flight.
He watched a martlet dart gracefully across the evening sky.

Related Terms

  • synonym: martin - a type of swallow with a forked tail
  • synonym: swallow - a small migratory bird with long pointed wings
