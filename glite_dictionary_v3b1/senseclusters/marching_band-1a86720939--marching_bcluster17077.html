marching band

noun
group of musicians who march and play music
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑrtʃɪŋ bænd / (AmE), / ˈmɑːtʃɪŋ band / (BrE)
A group of musicians who march in formation, typically in parades or at sporting events, while playing musical instruments together.

Examples

The marching band plays
Every summer, the marching band leads the annual Fourth of July parade through town.
She played the trumpet in her university's award-winning marching band.
The high school's marching band performed at halftime during the football game.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brass band - group of musicians playing brass instruments, often performing outdoors
  • antonym: orchestra - large group of instrumentalists, especially classical
