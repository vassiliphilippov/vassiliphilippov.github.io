surge protector

noun
device protecting electronics from power surges
3.2 0.1/m

An electrical device connected to a power line that protects electronic equipment from sudden increases in voltage.

Examples

A surge protector device
Before plugging in your laptop, make sure to use a surge protector to prevent damage during storms.
Many offices equip their computers with a surge protector to avoid costly repairs from power fluctuations.
The new television was connected to a surge protector to guard against voltage spikes.

Synonyms

  • surge suppressor
    5.0 0.05/m
    surge suppressor reduces sudden rises in voltage or current on a power line, while surge protector is the everyday name people use.
  • spike suppressor
    6.3 0.01/m
    spike suppressor cuts very short, high bursts of electricity on a line, while surge protector refers to general protection devices.
  • spike arrester
    7.2 0.00/m
    spike arrester directs extreme brief surges to ground at a line point, while surge protector is a broader device for equipment safety.
  • arrestor
    7.5 0.01/m
    arrestor diverts or limits high-voltage surges, often in power systems, while surge protector is the common device used at equipment plugs.
