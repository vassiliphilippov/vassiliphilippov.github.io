corroborate

verb
support with evidence
6.3 9.0
Pronunciation: / kəˈrɑːbəˌreɪt /; / kəˈrɑbəˌreɪt / (AmE), / kəˈrɒbəˌreɪt / (BrE)
to provide evidence or information that supports or confirms a statement, theory, or finding

Examples

To corroborate evidence
Her story was corroborated by photographs taken at the scene.
Several studies corroborate the theory that regular exercise benefits mental health.
The witness was able to corroborate the defendant's alibi with new evidence.
Can you corroborate these allegations with documentation?
Several witnesses were able to corroborate the defendant's account of the incident.
The scientist's findings corroborate previous research on climate change.
Her account was corroborated by video evidence from the security cameras.
Several witnesses corroborated the suspect's alibi during the trial.
The findings were corroborated by independent researchers in a separate study.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contradict - to assert the opposite of a statement
  • antonym: refute - to prove that something is wrong or false
