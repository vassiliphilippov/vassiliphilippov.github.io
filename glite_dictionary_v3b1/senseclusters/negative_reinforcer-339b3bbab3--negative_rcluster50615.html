negative reinforcer

noun
stimulus whose removal reinforces behavior
3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛɡətɪv rɪnˈfɔrsər / (AmE), / ˈnɛɡətɪv rɪnˈfɔːsə / (BrE)
a stimulus whose removal strengthens the likelihood of a particular behavior by allowing escape or avoidance of that stimulus

Examples

A negative reinforcer stimulus
In behavioral therapy, a negative reinforcer can encourage desirable actions by removing unpleasant conditions.
The loud noise acted as a negative reinforcer, as its removal encouraged the rats to press the lever.
The teacher used the cessation of extra homework as a negative reinforcer for improved class participation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: positive reinforcer - a stimulus that, when presented, increases the likelihood of a behavior
