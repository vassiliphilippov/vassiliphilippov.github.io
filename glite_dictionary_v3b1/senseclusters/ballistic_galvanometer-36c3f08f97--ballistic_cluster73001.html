ballistic galvanometer

noun
instrument for measuring electric charge
6.6 8.5
Pronunciation: /bə.ˈlɪs.tɪk gæl.və.ˈnɑː.mə.tər/ (AmE), /bə.ˈlɪs.tɪk gæl.və.ˈnɒm.ɪ.tə/ (BrE)
a type of galvanometer designed to measure the quantity of electric charge passed through it, rather than steady current

Examples

A ballistic galvanometer device
A ballistic galvanometer responds to a brief surge of current rather than a steady flow.
Calibration of the ballistic galvanometer was necessary before starting the experiment on capacitance.
The physics lab used a ballistic galvanometer to determine the total charge passing through the circuit.
