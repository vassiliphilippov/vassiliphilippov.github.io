palladium

noun
a metallic element of the platinum group
Pronunciation: / pəˈleɪdiəm / (AmE), / pəˈleɪdiəm / (BrE)
a silver-white metallic element (symbol Pd) of the platinum group, used in jewelry, electronics, and as a catalyst

Examples

Pure palladium metal
Automobile manufacturers use palladium in catalytic converters to reduce emissions.
Russia is one of the largest producers of palladium in the world.
The ring was made of pure palladium alloyed with gold.
