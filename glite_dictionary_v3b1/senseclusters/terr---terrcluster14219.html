Pronunciation: / tɛr / (AmE), / tɛr / (BrE)
a Latin root meaning 'earth' or 'land', found in words like 'terrain' and 'territory'

Examples

Terr- in 'territory'
The word 'territory' contains the Latin root terr-, which means 'land'.
In 'terrestrial', the root terr- refers to things related to the earth.
Many English words about land or earth, like 'terrain', use the root terr-.
