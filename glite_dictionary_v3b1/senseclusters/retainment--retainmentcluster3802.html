retainment

noun
group of attendants
Pronunciation: / rɪˈteɪnmənt / (AmE), / rɪˈteɪnmənt / (BrE)
a group of attendants, followers, or retainers serving a noble or important person, especially in historical or formal contexts

Examples

A royal retainment
The king arrived at the castle with a large retainment of loyal knights and servants.
During the ceremony, her retainment stood behind her, dressed in matching uniforms.
In medieval times, a noble's retainment often included both soldiers and household staff.

Related Terms

  • synonym: entourage - a group of people who travel with and support an important person
  • synonym: retinue - a group of advisers or attendants accompanying an important person
