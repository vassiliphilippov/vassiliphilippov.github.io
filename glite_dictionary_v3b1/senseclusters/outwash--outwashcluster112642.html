outwash

verb
to deposit glacial sediment
8.5
Pronunciation: /ˈaʊt.wɑːʃ/ /; / /ˈaʊt.wɔːʃ/ (AmE), /ˈaʊt.wɒʃ/ (BrE)
to carry away or deposit sand, gravel, or other sediment by meltwater from a glacier

Examples

To outwash sediment
During the last ice age, rivers outwashed large amounts of sediment onto the valley floor.
The retreating glacier outwashes gravel and sand, creating broad plains.
Scientists study how meltwater outwashes materials to understand ancient glacial movements.
