stuiver

noun
old Dutch coin
Pronunciation: / ˈstaɪvər / (AmE), / ˈstɔɪvə / (BrE)
a small Dutch coin formerly used in the Netherlands, worth five cents before the euro

The price of a loaf of bread was just one stuiver in the 18th century.
Collectors often search for rare stuivers at coin fairs in the Netherlands.
