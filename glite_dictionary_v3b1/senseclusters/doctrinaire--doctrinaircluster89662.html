doctrinaire

adjective
rigidly applying theory without regard for practicality
Pronunciation: / ˌdɑktrəˈnɛr / (AmE), / ˌdɒktrɪˈnɛə / (BrE)
believing in or applying a set of ideas or theories very rigidly, without considering whether they are practical or suitable in a given situation

Examples

Doctrinaire approach
Her doctrinaire beliefs made it difficult for her to compromise with colleagues.
Some politicians are too doctrinaire to adapt their policies to changing circumstances.
The committee rejected his doctrinaire approach in favor of more flexible methods.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dogmatic - expressing opinions very strongly as if they are facts
