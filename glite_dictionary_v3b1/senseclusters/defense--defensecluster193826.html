Examples

Psychological defense
Denial is a common psychological defense that helps people avoid facing uncomfortable truths.
Some individuals develop a strong defense against painful memories without consciously realizing it.
Therapists often help clients recognize the defense mechanisms that may be affecting their behavior.
Repression is considered a common psychological defence according to Freud's theories.
