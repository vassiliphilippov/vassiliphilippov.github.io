provoke

verb
cause a reaction or response
3.7 3.1
Pronunciation: /prə.ˈvoʊk/ (AmE), /prə.ˈvəʊk/ (BrE)
to cause a particular reaction, feeling, or condition by triggering or stimulating it

Examples

To provoke a reaction
Exposure to bright light can provoke the growth of certain plants.
The announcement was intended to provoke discussion among community members.
The new study may provoke a change in public policy.
Her speech managed to provoke a strong emotional response from the audience.
His words provoked a wave of laughter throughout the room.
The film is likely to provoke tears among viewers.
Her harsh words provoked an angry response from the audience.
Nothing he said could provoke her into losing her composure.
The smell of smoke provoked memories of summer camp in his mind.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arouse - to stir up or awaken a feeling or response
  • antonym: calm - to make someone or something quiet or less agitated
  • synonym: evoke - to bring a feeling, memory, or image into the mind
  • synonym: incite - to encourage or stir up a particular action or feeling
  • antonym: inhibit - to hinder or prevent an occurrence or process
  • synonym: prompt - to cause someone to take a course of action
  • antonym: suppress - to prevent something, such as a feeling, from being expressed
  • synonym: trigger - to cause something to start or happen suddenly
