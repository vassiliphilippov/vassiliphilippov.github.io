overprescribe

verb
to set excessive rules
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərprɪˈskraɪb / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəprɪˈskraɪb / (BrE)
to require, recommend, or set rules, procedures, or measures more strictly or extensively than necessary, often causing inefficiency or burden

Examples

To overprescribe rules
The new policy tends to overprescribe safety checks, making simple tasks take much longer.
Some managers overprescribe procedures, which can frustrate employees and slow down progress.
If we overprescribe rules, people may ignore the most important ones.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overregulate - to control or govern with too many rules or restrictions
  • degree lower: prescribe - to set down as a rule or guide
  • antonym: underprescribe - to require or recommend less than is necessary
