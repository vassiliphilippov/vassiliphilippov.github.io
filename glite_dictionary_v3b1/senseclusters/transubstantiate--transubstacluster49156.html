transubstantiate

verb
change the form or substance of
Pronunciation: / ˌtræn.səbˈstæn.ʃi.eɪt / (AmE), / ˌtræn.sʌbˈstæn.ʃɪ.eɪt / (BrE)
to change the form or substance of something, especially to transform into a different kind or nature

Examples

To transubstantiate matter
According to the doctrine, the bread and wine transubstantiate into the body and blood of Christ during the ceremony.
Some philosophers believe that profound experiences can transubstantiate one's outlook on life.
The alchemist aimed to transubstantiate base metals into gold through mysterious processes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: metamorphose - to change completely in form or nature
  • synonym: transform - to change the form, appearance, or character of something
