stinking wattle

noun
Australian acacia with foul-smelling flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪŋkɪŋ ˈwɑtəl / (AmE), / ˈstɪŋkɪŋ ˈwɒtəl / (BrE)
a scrubby Australian acacia tree or shrub known for its extremely foul-smelling blossoms

Examples

The stinking wattle tree
Botanists studied the stinking wattle to understand why its blossoms have such a foul odor.
The scent of the stinking wattle was so strong it could be detected from several meters away.
We avoided camping near the stinking wattle because of its unpleasant smell during flowering season.

  • synonym: acacia - a genus of shrubs and trees, many with yellow or white blossoms
