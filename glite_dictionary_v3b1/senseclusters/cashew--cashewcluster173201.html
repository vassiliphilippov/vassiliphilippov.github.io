cashew

noun
tropical tree producing edible nuts
3.4
Pronunciation: / kəˈʃu / (AmE), / kəˈʃuː / (BrE)
A tropical American evergreen tree (Anacardium occidentale) that produces kidney-shaped edible nuts, which must be roasted before eating.

Examples

Cashew tree
A single cashew tree can produce hundreds of nuts each season.
Farmers roast the cashew seeds before selling them as snacks.
The cashew thrives in tropical climates with sandy soil and plenty of sunlight.
