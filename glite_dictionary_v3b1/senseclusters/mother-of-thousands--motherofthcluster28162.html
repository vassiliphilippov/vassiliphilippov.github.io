mother-of-thousands

noun
a plant with red-and-white flowers that spreads by stolons
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌðər əv ˈθaʊzəndz / (AmE), / ˌmʌðə əv ˈθaʊzəndz / (BrE)
An eastern Asian saxifrage with clusters of small red-and-white flowers; it spreads by numerous creeping stolons.

Examples

The mother-of-thousands plant
Botanists were fascinated by the way mother-of-thousands spreads through its creeping stolons.
She planted a mother-of-thousands under the window for its delicate blooms.
The mother-of-thousands in the garden quickly covered the ground with its runners.
