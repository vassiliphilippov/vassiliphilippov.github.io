Fahrenheit

proper noun
German physicist, inventor of Fahrenheit scale
Pronunciation: / ˈfærənˌhaɪt / (AmE), / ˈfærənˌhaɪt / (BrE)
Daniel Gabriel Fahrenheit (1686–1736), the German physicist who invented the mercury thermometer and developed the Fahrenheit temperature scale.

Examples

Fahrenheit invented the thermometer
Fahrenheit was responsible for significant advancements in temperature measurement.
Many historians credit Fahrenheit with inventing the modern mercury thermometer.
The temperature scale named after Fahrenheit is still widely used in the United States.
