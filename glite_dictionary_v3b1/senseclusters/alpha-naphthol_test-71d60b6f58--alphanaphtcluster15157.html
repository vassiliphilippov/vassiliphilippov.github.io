alpha-naphthol test

noun
test for carbohydrates using alpha-naphthol
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˌælfə ˈnæfθɔl tɛst / (AmE), / ˌalfə ˈnafθɒl tɛst / (BrE)
a biochemical test used to detect the presence of carbohydrates in a solution, producing a violet ring when carbohydrates react with alpha-naphthol and sulfuric acid

Examples

Positive alpha-naphthol test
After adding sulfuric acid, a violet ring appeared, confirming a positive alpha-naphthol test.
Students learned to perform the alpha-naphthol test as part of their biochemistry practical.
The alpha-naphthol test is frequently used in laboratories to identify the presence of carbohydrates.
