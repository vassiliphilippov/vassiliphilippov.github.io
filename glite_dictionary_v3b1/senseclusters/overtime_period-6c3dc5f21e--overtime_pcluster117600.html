overtime period

noun
extra play period to break a tie in sports
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvərˌtaɪm ˈpɪriəd / (AmE), / ˈəʊvətaɪm ˈpɪəriəd / (BrE)
an additional period of play in a sports game, used to resolve a tie after normal playing time has ended

Examples

An overtime period in sports
If the score remains tied, the game will go into an overtime period.
Many fans stayed until the end of the overtime period to see who would win.
The team won in the second overtime period after a dramatic basket.
