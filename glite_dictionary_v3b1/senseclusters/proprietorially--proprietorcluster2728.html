proprietorially

adverb
with ownership attitude
Pronunciation: / prəˌpraɪəˈtɔːriəli / (AmE), / prəˌprʌɪəˈtɔːrɪəli /; / prəˌpraɪəˈtɔːriəli / (BrE)
in a way that shows ownership or control over something, as if it belongs to you

Examples

To act proprietorially
He placed his hand proprietorially on the back of the chair, making it clear it was his.
The manager walked through the office proprietorially, inspecting every detail as if it were his own.
She smiled proprietorially at her new car, proud to finally own it.
She guarded her research notes proprietorially, refusing to share them with colleagues.
The landlord acted proprietorially toward the shared garden, setting strict rules for its use.
He looked at the new equipment proprietorially, as if it belonged only to him.

Related Terms

  • antonym: casually - in a relaxed or unconcerned way
  • synonym: exclusively - in a way that excludes others
  • antonym: openly - in a way that is not restricted or controlled
  • synonym: possessively - in a way that shows a desire to own or control
