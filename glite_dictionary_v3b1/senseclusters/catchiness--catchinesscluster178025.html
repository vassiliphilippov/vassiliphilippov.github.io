catchiness

noun
tendency to snag
5.3
Pronunciation: /ˈkætʃ.ɪ.nəs/ /; / /ˈkætʃ.iː.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈkætʃ.ɪ.nəs/ /; / /ˈkætʃ.iː.nəs/ (BrE)
the quality of a material or surface that makes it likely to catch, snag, or hold onto other objects

Examples

Catchiness of the fabric
The catchiness of the sweater made it easy for it to snag on rough edges.
Because of its catchiness, the fabric often got stuck on rings and bracelets.
She avoided wearing clothes with high catchiness to prevent accidental tears.

Related Terms

  • antonym: smoothness - the quality of having an even, unbroken surface that does not catch
  • synonym: snag - a sharp or rough part that catches or tears something
