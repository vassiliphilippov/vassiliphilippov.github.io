In a stiff, awkward, or ungraceful manner; without flexibility or expression.

Examples

Answer woodenly
During the interview, she answered every question woodenly, barely changing her expression.
He moved woodenly across the stage, unable to relax his posture.
She spoke so woodenly that the audience quickly lost interest.

Synonyms

    4.4 4.23/m
    stiffly is rigid and tense in movement or speech, while woodenly is rigid plus cold and lacking all expression.
  • stiltedly
    8.8 0.01/m
    stiltedly sounds formal and unnatural, while woodenly describes rigid, expressionless speech or movement without ease.
  • ungracefully
    4.8 0.05/m
    ungracefully moves or acts without grace, while woodenly adds a hard, inflexible feeling that seems cold or mechanical.
  • gracelessly
    6.5 0.13/m
    gracelessly lacks elegance in movement or style, while woodenly suggests stiff, expressionless action that feels tight and lifeless.
  • awkwardly
    2.7 4.43/m
    awkwardly is clumsy or socially uncomfortable, while woodenly focuses on stiff, rigid motion or speech without any natural flow.
  • clumsily
    2.8 1.67/m
    clumsily shows poor skill or coordination, while woodenly presents stiffness and lack of expression rather than mere lack of skill.
  • angularly
    7.0 0.00/m
    angularly moves with sharp, jerky turns, while woodenly means stiff, expressionless movement that lacks smooth, flowing shape.
  • lumpishly
