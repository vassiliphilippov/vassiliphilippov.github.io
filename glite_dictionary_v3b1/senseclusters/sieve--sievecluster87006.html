sieve

verb
to examine for suitability
Pronunciation: / sɪv /; / siv / (AmE), / sɪv / (BrE)
to examine or sort information or items carefully in order to separate what is important or wanted from what is not

Examples

To sieve information
Researchers sieved the data to identify relevant patterns for their study.
The committee is sieving applications to shortlist candidates for the scholarship.
The council will sieve all the proposals to find the most feasible ones.
Carefully sieve all the data before presenting your final report to management.
The editor will sieve out any unnecessary details from the article.
The research team had to sieve through hundreds of documents to find the relevant evidence.
He sieves information from many sources to ensure accuracy in his reports.
Researchers must sieve the relevant data from this enormous dataset before analysis.
The recruiter will sieve through hundreds of applications to find the best candidates.
