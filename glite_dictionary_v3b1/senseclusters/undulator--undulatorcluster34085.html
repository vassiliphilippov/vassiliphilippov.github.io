undulator

noun
synchrotron radiation device
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndjəˌleɪtər / (AmE), / ˈʌndjʊˌleɪtə / (BrE)
a device that creates a periodic magnetic field to generate synchrotron radiation in particle accelerators

Examples

Synchrotron undulator
The undulator is essential for producing high-intensity X-rays in synchrotron facilities.
Scientists adjusted the undulator to fine-tune the wavelength of the emitted radiation.
A malfunction in the undulator can disrupt experiments that rely on precise light sources.

Related Terms

  • synonym: wiggler - a similar device that produces stronger magnetic fields for synchrotron radiation
