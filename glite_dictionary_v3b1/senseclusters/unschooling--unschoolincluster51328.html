unschooling

verb
to educate without school
7.6
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈskuːlɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈskuːlɪŋ / (BrE)
to educate a child outside the formal school system by allowing learning to be guided by the child's interests and daily experiences rather than a fixed curriculum

Examples

To unschool a child
After much research, they began unschooling their children to encourage curiosity and self-motivation.
Many parents choose unschooling because it allows for a more flexible and personalized education.
The family found that unschooling helped their son develop a love of learning through real-life experiences.

Related Terms

  • synonym: homeschool - to educate a child at home instead of in a traditional school
  • antonym: school - to educate in a formal school setting
