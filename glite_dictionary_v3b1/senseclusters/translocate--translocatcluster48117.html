translocate

verb
move a chromosomal segment to a new position
5.0 7.9
Pronunciation: /træn.ˈsloʊ.keɪt/ (AmE), /træn.ˈsləʊ.keɪt/ (BrE)
to transfer a chromosomal segment to a new position, especially on a different chromosome

Examples

Translocate a gene
A cell may become cancerous if it translocates a gene to an inappropriate location.
Certain genetic disorders occur when chromosomes translocate segments to nonhomologous partners.
Scientists were able to translocate a portion of the chromosome during the experiment.
