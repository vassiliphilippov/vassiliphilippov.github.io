Synonyms

  • gleaming
    3.2 0.06/m
    gleaming is a bright, clear shine from reflected light, while shining is a general word for something that reflects light.
  • shiny
    1.0 18/m
    shiny is smooth and strongly reflective on the surface, while shining describes anything that gives or reflects light.
  • bright
    1.0 45/m
    bright gives off or reflects a lot of light and feels strong, while shining stresses a clean or polished reflective surface.
  • glossy
    2.6 4.57/m
    glossy has a smooth, shiny surface like paper or leather, while shining is a more general term for reflecting light.
  • polished
    2.1 3.19/m
    polished looks smooth and bright because someone cleaned or rubbed it, while shining can describe any surface that reflects light.
  • burnished
    6.5 0.07/m
    burnished is made shiny by careful polishing and sounds formal, while shining is a neutral word for something that reflects light.
  • lustrous
    5.3 0.85/m
    lustrous shines with a soft, glowing light and sounds slightly poetic, while shining is plain and widely used.
  • glistening
    3.4 0.00/m
    glistening shines with reflected light often from wetness or smoothness, while shining is more general and not necessarily wet.
  • glinting
    3.6 0.00/m
    glinting gives brief, bright flashes of light, while shining describes a steady or overall reflective appearance.
  • sparkling
    1.3 0.35/m
    sparkling sends quick flashes and feels lively or festive, while shining usually means a steady, plain reflection of light.
