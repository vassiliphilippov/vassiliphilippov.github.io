keening

noun
mournful wailing
8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈkiː.nɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈkiː.nɪŋ/ (BrE)
a loud, high-pitched, mournful wail, especially as an expression of grief for the dead

Examples

Funeral keening
The keening of the mourners could be heard throughout the village after the funeral.
A long, sorrowful keening filled the air as the family gathered to say goodbye.
In some cultures, keening is a traditional way to express grief for the dead.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lamentation - the passionate expression of grief or sorrow
  • antonym: rejoicing - the expression of great joy
  • synonym: wailing - a long, loud, high-pitched cry expressing pain or sadness
