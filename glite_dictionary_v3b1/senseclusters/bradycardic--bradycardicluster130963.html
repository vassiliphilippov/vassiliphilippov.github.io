bradycardic

adjective
slow heart rate
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˌbreɪdiˈkɑrdɪk / (AmE), / ˌbrædɪˈkɑːdɪk / (BrE)
having a slower than normal heart rate

Examples

A bradycardic heart
The nurse noticed the patient was bradycardic after reviewing the heart monitor readings.
If someone becomes bradycardic, they may feel dizzy or weak due to the slow heart rate.
Doctors monitor athletes closely to determine if being bradycardic is a sign of fitness or a health concern.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bradycardia - a condition of having a slow heart rate
  • antonym: tachycardic - having a faster than normal heart rate
