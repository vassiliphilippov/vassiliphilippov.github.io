soffritto

noun
flavor base of vegetables
7.5
Pronunciation: / soʊˈfritoʊ / (AmE), / sɒˈfritəʊ /; / sɒˈfrɪtəʊ / (BrE)
a mixture of chopped onions, carrots, and celery gently fried in oil or fat, used as a flavor base in Italian cooking

Examples

A soffritto of vegetables
The recipe begins with a soffritto of onions, carrots, and celery cooked slowly in olive oil.
A well-made soffritto adds depth and richness to soups and stews.
She prepared the soffritto before adding tomatoes and meat to the sauce.
The chef began the sauce by preparing a flavorful soffritto in a large pan.
Many traditional Italian soups start with a base of soffritto to enhance the taste.
She carefully chopped the vegetables for the soffritto before adding them to the pot.
