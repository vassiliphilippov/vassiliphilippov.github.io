inboard

adjective
toward the center of a vessel or aircraft
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌbɔːd / (BrE)
located within the hull or closer to the centerline of a vessel or aircraft

Examples

The inboard flaps
During takeoff, the inboard flaps are extended first.
The inboard engines are easier to maintain than the outboard ones.
The inboard seating provides more stability in rough seas.
