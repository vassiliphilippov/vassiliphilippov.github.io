play the victim card

verb
pretend to be a victim for sympathy or avoidance
Pronunciation: / pleɪ ðə ˈvɪktəm kɑrd / (AmE), / pleɪ ðə ˈvɪktɪm kɑːd / (BrE)
to act as if one is a victim in order to gain sympathy or avoid blame or accountability

Examples

To play the victim card to avoid blame
It's clear he's playing the victim card to avoid taking responsibility for his actions.
She accused him of playing the victim card to get out of trouble at work.
Whenever criticized, he tends to play the victim card instead of addressing the issue.
