pyramidalis

noun
abdominal muscle
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpɪrəˈmɪdəlɪs / (AmE), / ˌpɪrəˈmɪdəlɪs / (BrE)
a small triangular muscle in the lower front part of the abdomen, in front of the rectus abdominis

Examples

Pyramidalis muscle
The pyramidalis muscle is sometimes absent in humans.
During abdominal surgery, the pyramidalis may be visible just above the pubic bone.
Anatomy students learned to identify the pyramidalis in their dissection class.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rectus abdominis - a long muscle in the front of the abdomen
