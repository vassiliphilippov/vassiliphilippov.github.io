phenylephrine

noun
vasoconstrictor and decongestant drug
Pronunciation: / ˌfɛnəlˈɛfrin / (AmE), / ˌfiːnɪlˈɛfrin / (BrE)
a synthetic drug used as a vasoconstrictor, to dilate the pupils, or to relieve nasal congestion

Examples

Phenylephrine nasal spray
Before the eye examination, the nurse applied drops of phenylephrine to dilate the patient's pupils.
Many over-the-counter cold medicines contain phenylephrine to relieve nasal congestion.
The doctor administered phenylephrine to help raise the patient's blood pressure during surgery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: epinephrine - a hormone and medication, also known as adrenaline
