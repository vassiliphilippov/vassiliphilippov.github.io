recrystallization

noun
formation of new crystals
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌkrɪstəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌkrɪstəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process in which minerals or substances form new or larger crystals, often due to heat and pressure, especially during metamorphism

Examples

Rock recrystallization
The recrystallization of minerals can change the texture and appearance of a rock.
During metamorphism, recrystallization often results in the growth of larger mineral grains.
Scientists studied the recrystallization process to understand how rocks evolve under extreme conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crystallization - the process of forming crystals from a solution or melt
  • synonym: metamorphism - the process by which rocks change form due to heat and pressure
