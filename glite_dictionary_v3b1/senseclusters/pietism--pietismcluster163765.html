pietism

noun
exaggerated or affected piety
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪəˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈpaɪətɪzəm / (BrE)
Exaggerated or affected display of piety or religious zeal.

Examples

Excessive pietism
Critics dismissed the politician's speech as empty moralizing and pietism.
Her public displays of pietism seemed more for show than genuine faith.
The preacher was often accused of pietism by those who doubted his sincere devotion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hypocrisy - pretending to have moral standards or beliefs to which one's own behavior does not conform
  • antonym: sincerity - the quality of being genuine and honest
