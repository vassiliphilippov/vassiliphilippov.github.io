acceptor

noun
chemical compound that receives electrons
Pronunciation: / əkˈsɛptɚ /; / əkˈsɛptər / (AmE), / əkˈsɛptə / (BrE)
a person, thing, or substance that receives or accepts something, especially in science or technology (such as an atom that receives electrons)

Examples

Electron acceptor
A coordinate bond forms when an electron acceptor combines with a donor molecule.
In this reaction, the oxygen atom acts as the acceptor of the electron pair.
The acceptor plays a crucial role in the transfer of electrons during the process.
In chemistry, an acceptor atom gains electrons from a donor.
The new payment system acts as an acceptor for digital transactions.
In biology, an enzyme can serve as an acceptor of specific molecules during a reaction.
