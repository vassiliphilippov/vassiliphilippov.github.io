waylay

verb
wait in hiding to attack
7.9 6.8
Pronunciation: /weɪ.ˈleɪ/ /; / /ˈweɪ.leɪ/ (AmE), /weɪ.ˈleɪ/ /; / /ˈweɪ.leɪ/ (BrE)
to stop or approach someone unexpectedly, often to talk to them or attack them

Examples

To waylay a person
Journalists tried to waylay the politician as he left the conference.
She feared someone might waylay her on her walk home at night.
The robber attempted to waylay travelers on the lonely country road.
On her way to the meeting, she was waylaid by a group of reporters.
The travelers were waylaid by bandits on the remote road.
He tried to leave quickly, but a colleague waylaid him with questions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ambush - to attack by surprise from a hidden place
  • degree higher: attack - to take aggressive action against
  • antonym: ignore - to pay no attention to someone or something
  • synonym: interrupt - to stop someone from continuing what they are doing
