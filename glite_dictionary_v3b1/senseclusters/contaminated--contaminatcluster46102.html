contaminated

adjective
made impure or dirty by contact
2.2
Pronunciation: / kənˈtæmɪˌneɪtɪd /; / kənˈtæməˌneɪtɪd / (AmE), / kənˈtæmɪneɪtɪd / (BrE)
made impure, unsafe, or harmful by contact with something undesirable, unclean, or harmful

Examples

Contaminated food
Food left uncovered overnight can easily become contaminated by bacteria.
Investigators dismissed the laboratory results because the samples were contaminated.
The water supply was found to be contaminated with harmful chemicals after the industrial spill.
Before using the medical equipment, make sure it is not contaminated.
Residents were warned not to eat fish from the contaminated river due to high levels of mercury.
The contaminated water supply posed a serious health risk to the local community.

Related Terms

  • synonym: polluted - made dirty or impure, especially by waste or poisonous substances
  • antonym: pure - not mixed or adulterated with any other substance
  • synonym: tainted - having a trace of something bad, offensive, or harmful
