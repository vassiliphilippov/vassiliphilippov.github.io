synkinesis

noun
involuntary linked movement
9.9
Pronunciation: /sɪŋ.kɪ.ˈniː.sɪs/ /; / /sɪn.kɪ.ˈniː.sɪs/ /; / /sɪŋ.kə.ˈniː.sɪs/ (AmE), /sɪŋ.kɪ.ˈniː.sɪs/ /; / /sɪn.kɪ.ˈniː.sɪs/ /; / /sɪŋ.kə.ˈniː.sɪs/ (BrE)
an involuntary movement of one part of the body that occurs when another part moves voluntarily, often due to nerve injury

Examples

Synkinesis after injury
After his facial nerve injury, synkinesis made his eye twitch whenever he smiled.
Physical therapy can help reduce the effects of synkinesis after nerve damage.
Doctors observed synkinesis in the patient's hand when he tried to move his arm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: involuntary movement - a movement that happens without conscious control
  • antonym: paralysis - loss of the ability to move a part of the body
