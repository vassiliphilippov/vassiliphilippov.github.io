languor

noun
oppressive stillness in the air
Pronunciation: / ˈlæŋɡər /; / ˈlæŋɚ / (AmE), / ˈlæŋɡə /; / ˈlaŋɡə / (BrE)
a state of tiredness, stillness, or lack of energy, sometimes pleasant and relaxing, sometimes heavy or oppressive

Examples

Deep languor after storm
After the storm, the city was cloaked in a deep languor that stilled every movement.
By noon, a sense of languor filled the silent, sun-drenched streets.
The air hung heavy with languor during the humid summer afternoon.
After the long meal, a sense of languor settled over the guests, making conversation slow and quiet.
She stretched out in the hammock, overwhelmed by a delicious languor in the afternoon heat.
The honeyed air and gentle breeze filled him with a dreamy languor as he sat by the lake.
A sense of languor filled the room as the afternoon heat wore on.
After a long day in the sun, she was overcome by a feeling of languor and wanted to rest.
He tried to shake off his morning languor with a brisk walk in the fresh air.

  • antonym: vigor - physical strength and good health; energy
