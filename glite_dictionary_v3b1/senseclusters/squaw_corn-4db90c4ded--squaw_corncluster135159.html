squaw corn

noun
corn with soft-starch kernels
Pronunciation: / skwɔ kɔrn / (AmE), / skwɔː kɔːn / (BrE)
A variety of corn with kernels that consist almost entirely of soft starch.

Examples

A squaw corn variety
Researchers studied the nutritional content of squaw corn compared to other varieties.
The farmer saved seeds of squaw corn for use in traditional dishes.
Unlike hard corn, squaw corn is prized for its soft, starchy kernels.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dent corn - a type of corn with dented kernels used for animal feed and industrial products
