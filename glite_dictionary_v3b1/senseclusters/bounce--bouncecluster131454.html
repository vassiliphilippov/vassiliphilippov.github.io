bounce

verb
move up and down repeatedly
1.0
Pronunciation: / baʊns / (AmE), / baʊns / (BrE)
to move quickly away from a surface after hitting it, or to move up and down or in a lively, springy way

Examples

To bounce a ball
Children love to bounce on trampolines in the summer.
Raindrops bounce off the pavement after a heavy downpour.
The basketball began to bounce as soon as it hit the court.
After dropping from the table, the tennis ball continued to bounce across the floor.
He tried to bounce a stone on the frozen lake, but it just slid.
The ball hit the ground and began to bounce uncontrollably.
As soon as the bell rang, the children bounced out of their seats.
He bounced to his feet when his name was called.
She bounced up from the couch, eager to answer the door.

Related Terms

  • antonym: absorb - to take in or soak up (opposite of bouncing back)
  • synonym: jump - to push oneself off a surface and into the air by using the muscles in one's legs and feet
  • synonym: leap - to jump into the air quickly or energetically
  • synonym: rebound - to bounce back after hitting a surface
  • synonym: ricochet - to bounce off a surface, especially repeatedly
  • synonym: skip - to move with light, quick steps, often bouncing slightly
  • synonym: spring - to move or jump suddenly or rapidly upward or forward
