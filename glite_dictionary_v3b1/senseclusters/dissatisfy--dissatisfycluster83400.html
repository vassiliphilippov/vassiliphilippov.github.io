dissatisfy

verb
fail to satisfy
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsætɪsˌfaɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈsætɪsfaɪ / (BrE)
to fail to satisfy someone; to make someone feel unhappy or not content with something

Examples

To dissatisfy customers
If the results consistently dissatisfy customers, the brand's reputation may suffer.
The new policy dissatisfied many employees, causing morale to drop throughout the company.
The solution may work for some people but could dissatisfy others who have different needs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: satisfy - to fulfill the desires or needs of
