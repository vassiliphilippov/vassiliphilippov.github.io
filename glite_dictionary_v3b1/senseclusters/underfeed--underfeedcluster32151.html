underfeed

noun
bottom-feeding mechanism
9.1
Pronunciation: /ˈʌn.dər.fiːd/ (AmE), /ˈʌn.də.fiːd/ (BrE)
a mechanism that supplies fuel or material from below, especially in a furnace, boiler, or machine

Examples

Underfeed mechanism
The engineer inspected the underfeed to ensure it was delivering coal efficiently to the furnace.
A malfunction in the underfeed caused the boiler to lose heat rapidly.
Modern systems often use an automated underfeed to improve fuel distribution and reduce manual labor.
