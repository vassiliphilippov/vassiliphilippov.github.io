judicial doctrine

A legal principle or rule developed and applied by courts, forming a basis for judicial decisions and guiding jurisprudence.

Examples

Existing judicial doctrines
Many lower courts rely on existing judicial doctrines to resolve complex legal disputes.
The Supreme Court established a new judicial doctrine regarding voting rights in its latest decision.
This judicial doctrine has evolved over decades of case law.

Synonyms

  • judicial principle
    7.4 0.00/m
    judicial principle is a basic court rule used in single cases, while judicial doctrine is a developed set of such rules guiding future decisions.
  • legal principle
    4.6 0.60/m
    legal principle is a general rule from law or statutes used across cases, while judicial doctrine is a court-made rule developed through judicial decisions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: precedent - an earlier court decision that guides future cases
  • antonym: statute - a written law passed by a legislative body
