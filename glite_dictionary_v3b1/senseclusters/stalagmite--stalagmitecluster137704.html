stalagmite

noun
cave floor mineral column
7.4 6.6
Pronunciation: /stə.ˈlæɡ.maɪt/ (AmE), /ˈstæ.ləg.maɪt/ (BrE)
A column of mineral deposits, typically of calcium carbonate, rising from the floor of a cave, formed by the dripping of mineral-rich water.

Examples

A stalagmite in cave
A drop of water fell from the ceiling, slowly adding to the growing stalagmite below.
The stalagmite in the center of the cave had taken thousands of years to form.
Tourists admired the tall stalagmites that rose from the limestone cave floor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: stalactite - an icicle-shaped formation hanging from a cave ceiling
