commensuration

noun
shared unit measurement
9.9
Pronunciation: /kə.men.sə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /kə.men.sjʊ.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /kə.men.sjʊ.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /kə.men.sə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the property of two quantities being measurable by the same unit, so their ratio is a rational number

Examples

Commensuration of lengths
The commensuration of the two line segments allows their lengths to be compared using a common unit.
In ancient mathematics, commensuration was important for understanding ratios and proportions.
The concept of commensuration is fundamental in determining whether two quantities can be directly compared.

Related Terms

  • synonym: commensurable - able to be measured by the same unit
  • antonym: incommensurable - not able to be measured by the same unit
