reform

verb
chemically break up and rearrange molecules
Pronunciation: / rɪˈfɔrm / (AmE), / rɪˈfɔːm / (BrE)
(Chemistry) to change the structure of a substance (such as petroleum or gas) by a chemical process to improve its quality or usability

Examples

To reform crude oil
Certain catalysts are used to reform crude oil during the refining process.
Modern refineries reform low-quality gasoline to meet environmental standards.
Scientists reform natural gas to produce hydrogen for fuel cells.
Certain catalysts are used to reform methane into synthesis gas.
Modern refineries often reform naphtha to improve fuel quality.
The process can reform hydrocarbons into high-octane gasoline components.

  • synonym: crack - to break down complex molecules in petroleum refining
