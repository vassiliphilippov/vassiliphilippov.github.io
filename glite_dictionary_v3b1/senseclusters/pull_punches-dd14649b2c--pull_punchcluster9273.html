pull punches

verb
restrain criticism or effort
Pronunciation: / pʊl ˈpʌntʃɪz / (AmE), / pʊl ˈpʌntʃɪz / (BrE)
to hold back from expressing criticism or from doing something with full force, often to avoid offending or hurting others

Examples

To pull punches gently
If you want honest advice, don't expect me to pull punches.
She refused to pull punches in her report on the project's failures.
The new manager doesn't pull punches when giving feedback to his team.

Related Terms

  • antonym: be blunt - to speak in a direct and unmoderated way
  • synonym: spare someone's feelings - to avoid hurting someone by not being fully honest
