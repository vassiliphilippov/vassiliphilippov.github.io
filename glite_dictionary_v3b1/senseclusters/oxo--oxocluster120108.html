oxo

adjective
with double-bonded oxygen
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɑksoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɒksəʊ / (BrE)
containing an oxygen atom double-bonded to another atom, especially in chemical compounds

Examples

Oxo group in molecules
The oxo group is found in many important organic molecules.
Chemists often refer to compounds with a double-bonded oxygen as oxo derivatives.
Adding an oxo group can change the reactivity of a chemical compound.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carbonyl - a functional group with a carbon atom double-bonded to an oxygen atom
