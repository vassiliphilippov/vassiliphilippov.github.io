BCC

noun
body-centered cubic structure
8.8
Pronunciation: / bi si si / (AmE), / biː siː siː / (BrE)
a crystal structure in which atoms are positioned at each corner and the center of a cube, known as body-centered cubic

Examples

BCC crystal structure
At room temperature, iron has a BCC crystal structure, which changes at higher temperatures.
Many metals, such as chromium and tungsten, naturally form a BCC lattice.
The BCC arrangement affects the strength and properties of the metal.

Related Terms

  • antonym: FCC - a crystal structure with atoms at each corner and the centers of all cube faces
