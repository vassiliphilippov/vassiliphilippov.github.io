contractibility

noun
ability to form contractions
8.1
Pronunciation: /kən.træk.tə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /kən.træk.tə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
the quality of a word or phrase that allows it to be shortened by forming a contraction

Examples

Word contractibility
The contractibility of 'will not' to 'won't' is a common feature in English.
Teachers often explain the contractibility of auxiliary verbs to help students sound more natural.
Some phrases lack contractibility and cannot be shortened in spoken English.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contraction - a shortened form of a word or group of words
  • antonym: expandability - the ability of a contraction to be expanded to its full form
