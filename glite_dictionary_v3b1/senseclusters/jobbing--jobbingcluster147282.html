jobbing

adjective
doing casual work
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɑːbɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɒbɪŋ / (BrE)
working in a variety of short-term or casual jobs rather than in a permanent or specialist position

Examples

Jobbing builder
He worked as a jobbing gardener, taking on small projects for different clients each week.
Many jobbing actors take temporary roles while waiting for a big break.
The repairs were handled by a jobbing plumber who specialized in quick, short-term jobs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: freelance - working independently for different companies rather than being permanently employed by one
  • antonym: permanent - lasting or intended to last indefinitely; not temporary
