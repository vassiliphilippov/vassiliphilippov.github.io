self-pollination

noun
fertilization within the same flower
1.0 4.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlf pɑːlɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌself pɒlɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the fertilization of a flower by the transfer of pollen from its own anthers to its stigma

Examples

Self-pollination in flowers
In pea plants, self-pollination was crucial to Mendel's experiments on heredity.
Some plants have evolved mechanisms to prevent self-pollination and encourage genetic diversity.
The closed structure of certain flowers promotes self-pollination rather than cross-pollination.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cross-pollination - fertilization by transfer of pollen from one flower to the stigma of another
