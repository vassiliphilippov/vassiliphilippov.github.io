self-pollination

noun
fertilization within the same flower

the fertilization of a flower by the transfer of pollen from its own anthers to its stigma

Examples

Self-pollination in flowers
In pea plants, self-pollination was crucial to Mendel's experiments on heredity.
Some plants have evolved mechanisms to prevent self-pollination and encourage genetic diversity.
The closed structure of certain flowers promotes self-pollination rather than cross-pollination.

Synonyms

  • autogamy
    9.9 0.00/m
    autogamy is the botanical term for a flower fertilizing itself by its own pollen, while self-pollination names that pollen transfer.
  • self-fertilization
    6.5 0.03/m
    self-fertilization means an organism's own gametes unite to make offspring, while self-pollination focuses on pollen moving within the same flower.
  • selfing
    8.9 0.07/m
    selfing describes the process of an organism fertilizing itself, often for plants or hermaphrodites, while self-pollination is pollen transfer in flowers.
