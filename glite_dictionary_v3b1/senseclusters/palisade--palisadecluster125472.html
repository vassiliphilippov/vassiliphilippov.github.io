palisade

verb
to enclose with a defensive wall or fence
Pronunciation: / ˌpælɪˈseɪd /; / ˈpælɪˌseɪd / (AmE), / ˌpælɪˈseɪd /; / ˈpælɪseɪd / (BrE)
to surround, protect, or fortify an area by building a strong fence of stakes or posts around it

Examples

To palisade a fort
In anticipation of the invasion, the army quickly palisaded the fortress.
The colonists decided to palisade their village after several attacks from hostile forces.
They worked through the night, palisading the encampment to ensure everyone's safety.
The settlers began palisading their camp to protect it from wild animals.
Workers spent days palisading the construction site for added security.
They finished palisading the garden before the rainy season started.

Related Terms

  • synonym: enclose - to surround something completely
  • antonym: expose - to leave unprotected or uncovered
  • synonym: fortify - to strengthen a place with defensive works
