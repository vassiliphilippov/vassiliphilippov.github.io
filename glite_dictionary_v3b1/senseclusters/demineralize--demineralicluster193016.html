demineralize

verb
remove minerals or salts
7.5
Pronunciation: / diˈmɪnərəˌlaɪz /; / diˈmɪnəˌraɪz / (AmE), / diˈmɪnərəlaɪz / (BrE)
to remove minerals or salts from something, especially from water or biological tissue

Examples

To demineralize water
Before using the water in the steam iron, you should demineralize it to prevent limescale buildup.
Exposure to acids can demineralize tooth enamel, leading to cavities.
The laboratory uses special filters to demineralize water for experiments.
Certain medical treatments require completely demineralised water for patient safety.
The company uses advanced filters to demineralise the water before bottling it.
You should demineralise your water supply to prevent scale buildup in the equipment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mineralise - add minerals or salts to a substance
  • synonym: purify - remove contaminants from something
  • antonym: remineralize - add minerals or salts back to something
