immovableness

noun
state of being immovable
5.8
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmoʊvəblnəs / (AmE), / ɪˈməʊvəblnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being unable to be moved or rearranged.

Examples

The immovableness of objects
His immovableness during the argument frustrated everyone in the room.
The immovableness of the ancient statue made it impossible to relocate.
The legal dispute centered on the immovableness of the property boundary.

Related Terms

  • synonym: immovability - the quality of being immovable
  • antonym: mobility - the quality of being able to move
