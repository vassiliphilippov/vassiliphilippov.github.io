frequency response

noun
output-to-input ratio over frequencies
5.3 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈfriː.kwən.siː rɪ.ˈspɑːns/ /; / /ˈfrɪ.kwən.siː rɪ.ˈspɑːns/ (AmE), /ˈfriː.kwən.siː rɪ.ˈspɒns/ /; / /ˈfrɪ.kwən.siː rɪ.ˈspɒns/ (BrE)
the measure of a system’s output spectrum in response to an input signal, shown as the ratio of output to input across a range of frequencies

Examples

Flat frequency response
A flat frequency response is desirable in high-fidelity audio equipment.
Engineers measured the frequency response to ensure the amplifier performed as expected.
The frequency response of the speakers extends from 40 Hz to 20 kHz.
