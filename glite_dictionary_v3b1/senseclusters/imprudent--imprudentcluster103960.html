imprudent

adjective
not wise; lacking good judgment
7.6 0.66/m
Pronunciation: / …™mÀàprud…ônt /; / …™mÀàpruÀêd…ônt / (AmE), / …™mÀàpruÀêdnt /; / …™mÀàpruÀêd…ônt / (BrE)
not showing care or good judgment; unwise

Examples

An imprudent decision
Driving without insurance is extremely imprudent and risky.
Her imprudent remarks embarrassed everyone at the dinner table.
It would be imprudent to invest all your savings in one company.
He was criticized for making an imprudent decision without consulting his team.
Her imprudent comments caused unnecessary confusion during the meeting.
It would be imprudent to invest all your money in a single stock.

Synonyms

  • unwise
    3.5 3.07/m
    unwise is a plain, general word for poor judgment, while imprudent sounds slightly more formal and warns of risk.
  • injudicious
    8.2 0.07/m
    injudicious means showing poor judgment in choices, while imprudent is a common warning about risky or careless acts.
  • ill-judged
    6.3 0.11/m
    ill-judged describes a decision made without thought, while imprudent covers any act lacking care or good judgment.
  • ill-advised
    5.4 1.17/m
    ill-advised warns that a choice will likely cause harm, while imprudent signals a lack of care or judgment.
  • ill-considered
    6.8 0.38/m
    ill-considered labels plans made too quickly without thought, while imprudent covers general acts that show poor judgment.
  • foolish
    1.3 22/m
    foolish feels silly or childish and often insults the person, while imprudent focuses on a lack of good judgment.
  • irresponsible
    3.2 9.39/m
    irresponsible shows neglect of duties or carelessness toward others, while imprudent emphasizes poor judgment or risky choices.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reckless - without thought of danger or consequences; rash
