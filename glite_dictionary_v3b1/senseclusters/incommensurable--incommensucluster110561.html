incommensurable

adjective
not sharing a common measure or factor
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnkəˈmɛnʃ(ə)rəbəl /; / ˌɪnkəˈmɛnʃərəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəˈmɛnʃ(ə)rəbəl / (BrE)
not able to be measured or compared because there is no common standard or way to do so

Examples

An incommensurable difference
Many philosophical systems are considered incommensurable because their basic assumptions cannot be directly compared.
The concept of incommensurable magnitudes was crucial in the development of ancient Greek mathematics.
The lengths of the two line segments are incommensurable, making their ratio an irrational number.
Many argue that artistic quality is incommensurable and cannot be evaluated by objective standards.
The achievements of these two artists are incommensurable in terms of style and impact.
Their traditions are so different that their values seem incommensurable.

Related Terms

  • antonym: commensurable - having a common measure or factor
  • synonym: incomparable - unable to be compared; without an equal in quality or excellence
