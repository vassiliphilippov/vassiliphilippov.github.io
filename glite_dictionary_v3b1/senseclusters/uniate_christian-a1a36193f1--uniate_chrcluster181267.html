Uniate Christian

noun
member of Eastern church united with Rome
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈjuːni.eɪt ˈkrɪs.tʃən / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪeɪt ˈkrɪs.tʃən / (BrE)
A member of an Eastern Christian Church that is in union with the Roman Catholic Church but retains its own liturgical rites.

Examples

Uniate Christians in Europe
Many Uniate Christians in Eastern Europe maintain distinctive traditions within the Catholic Church.
Several Uniate Christians attended the ecumenical service alongside Orthodox and Roman Catholic believers.
The local cathedral serves as a center for the Uniate Christian community in the region.
