oxidized

adjective
combined with oxygen
Pronunciation: / ˈɑksɪˌdaɪzd /; / ˈɑːksɪˌdaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈɒksɪˌdaɪzd / (BrE)
having combined with oxygen or undergone a chemical reaction involving the addition of oxygen

Examples

Oxidized metal surface
The copper roof gradually became oxidized and turned green over time.
The scientist studied the oxidized metal to understand the corrosion process.
Vitamin C can help prevent fruit from becoming oxidized after it is cut.
An oxidised layer forms on aluminum, protecting it from further corrosion.
The once shiny copper roof had become oxidised and turned green over the years.
Vitamin C is less effective when it is oxidised by exposure to air.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reduced - having gained electrons or lost oxygen in a chemical reaction
