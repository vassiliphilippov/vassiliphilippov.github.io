Neolithic

noun
later part of the Stone Age
6.0 6.7
Pronunciation: /niː.ə.ˈlɪθ.ɪk/ /; / /niː.oʊ.ˈlɪθ.ɪk/ (AmE), /niː.ə.ˈlɪθ.ɪk/ /; / /niː.əʊ.ˈlɪθ.ɪk/ (BrE)
the latest part of the Stone Age, beginning around 10,000 BC in the Middle East, characterized by the development of farming, domestication of animals, and polished stone tools

Examples

The Neolithic period
Many ancient villages from the Neolithic have been discovered in the region.
Pottery was commonly produced throughout the Neolithic.
The transition to agriculture occurred during the Neolithic.
