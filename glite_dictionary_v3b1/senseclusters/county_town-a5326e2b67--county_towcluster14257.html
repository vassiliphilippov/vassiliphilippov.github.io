county town

noun
administrative center of a county
3.4 0.05/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆkaÊŠnti taÊŠn / (AmE), / ËˆkaÊŠnti taÊŠn / (BrE)
the town or city that serves as the administrative center of a county, typically where the county government is located

Examples

The county town
Each county town typically has a courthouse and administrative buildings.
Norwich is the historic county town of Norfolk.
The records were transferred to the county town after the new council offices opened.

Synonyms

  • county seat
    4.3 0.58/m
    county seat is the common US term for the town with county government offices, while a county town is the British name for it.
  • shire town
    8.4 0.01/m
    shire town is a regional name used in parts of England and New England for a county centre, while county town is the British term.
