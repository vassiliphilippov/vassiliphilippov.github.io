power train

noun
system transmitting engine power
6.6 4.1
Pronunciation: /ˈpaʊ.ər treɪn/ /; / /paʊr treɪn/ (AmE), /ˈpaʊ.ə treɪn/ /; / /pɑː treɪn/ (BrE)
The system of connected gears and shafts in a vehicle or machine that transmits mechanical power from the engine or motor to the wheels or other working parts.

Examples

Vehicle power train
Modern electric vehicles have a different type of power train compared to traditional gasoline cars.
Noise coming from the power train can indicate wear in the gears or bearings.
The mechanic diagnosed a problem in the power train that was affecting the car’s performance.
