destructibility

noun
ability to be destroyed
Pronunciation: / dɪˌstrʌktəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / dɪˌstrʌktəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being able to be destroyed; vulnerability to destruction

Examples

Material destructibility
Scientists study the destructibility of materials to find safer construction options.
The destructibility of the old bridge was a major concern during the storm.
Understanding the destructibility of certain ecosystems is crucial for conservation efforts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: indestructibility - the quality of being impossible to destroy
