matrixing

noun
perceiving false patterns
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪtrɪksɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪtrɪksɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of perceiving patterns or meaningful connections in random or unrelated data, especially visual images

Examples

Matrixing faces in clouds
Some people experience matrixing when they see faces in clouds or patterns in static.
The phenomenon of matrixing explains why ghost hunters sometimes hear voices in random noise.
Artists sometimes use matrixing to inspire creativity by finding shapes in abstract forms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pareidolia - the tendency to see familiar shapes or patterns, especially faces, in random objects
