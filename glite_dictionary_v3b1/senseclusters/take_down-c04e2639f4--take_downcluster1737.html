take down

verb
remove or dismantle something
Pronunciation: / teɪk daʊn / (AmE), / teɪk daʊn / (BrE)
to remove something from a place where it was fixed or displayed, or to dismantle something so it is no longer standing or operational

Examples

To take down decorations
After the concert, the crew began to take down the stage equipment.
Could you take down those curtains for washing?
They had to take down the tent before the rain started.

Related Terms

  • antonym: put up - to fix something in place or assemble it
  • synonym: remove - to take something away from a place
