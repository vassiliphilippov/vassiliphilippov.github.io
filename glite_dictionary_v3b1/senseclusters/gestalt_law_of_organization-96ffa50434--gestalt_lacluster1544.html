Gestalt law of organization

noun
psychological principle of perceptual grouping
9.9
Pronunciation: / gəˈstɑlt lɔ ʌv ˌɔrɡənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / gəˈʃtalt lɔː əv ˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
a principle in Gestalt psychology stating that certain factors influence how people perceive visual elements as organized patterns or wholes, rather than as separate components

Examples

Gestalt law of organization principle
According to the Gestalt law of organization, our minds naturally group similar objects together.
Designers use the Gestalt law of organization to create cohesive and visually appealing layouts.
The Gestalt law of organization helps explain why we often perceive a series of dots as a straight line.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Gestalt principle - any general rule in Gestalt psychology about perceptual organization
  • degree higher: law of closure - Gestalt law where incomplete shapes tend to be seen as complete
  • degree lower: law of proximity - Gestalt law where objects close to each other are perceived as a group
