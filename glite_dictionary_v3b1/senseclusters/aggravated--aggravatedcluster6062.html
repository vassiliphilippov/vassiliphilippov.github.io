aggravated

adjective
made more serious; legal context
Pronunciation: / ˈæɡrəˌveɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈaɡ.rə.veɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
(of an offense or circumstance) made more serious by specific factors such as intent, violence, or other circumstances, especially in law.

Examples

Aggravated assault
An aggravated offense usually results in a longer sentence than a simple one.
He was charged with aggravated assault after the incident involving a weapon.
The court found her actions to be aggravated by prior convictions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mitigated - (of an offense) made less severe or serious
