bast fiber

noun
strong woody fiber from plant phloem
Pronunciation: / bæst ˈfaɪbər / (AmE), / bɑːst ˈfaɪbə / (BrE)
a strong, woody fiber obtained from the phloem (bast) of certain plants, used in making rope, textiles, and matting

Examples

The bast fiber rope
Linen, one of the oldest textiles, is produced from the bast fiber of the flax plant.
Scientists are studying the properties of bast fiber for use in sustainable materials.
The rope was made from durable bast fiber sourced from local plants.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flax - a plant whose bast fibers are used to make linen
  • synonym: jute - a type of bast fiber used for ropes and sacks
