slip

noun
sideways flight maneuver (aviation)
9.9
Pronunciation: / slɪp / (AmE), / slɪp / (BrE)
A flight maneuver in which an aircraft moves sideways through the air, usually to lose altitude without increasing airspeed.

Examples

An airplane slip
During training, students learn how to recover safely from a slip.
Executing a slip can help align the aircraft with a short runway.
The pilot performed a slip to quickly lose altitude before landing.

Related Terms

  • antonym: skid - An uncontrolled sideways slide of an aircraft during flight
