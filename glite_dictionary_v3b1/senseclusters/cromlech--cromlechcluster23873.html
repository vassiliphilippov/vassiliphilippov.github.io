cromlech

noun
prehistoric stone tomb monument
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɑm.lɛk / (AmE), / ˈkrɒm.lɛk / (BrE)
A prehistoric stone monument consisting of large upright stones supporting a flat capstone, typically serving as a tomb.

Examples

A cromlech tomb
Archaeologists discovered a well-preserved cromlech surrounded by scattered stone fragments.
Many tourists visit the famous cromlech ruins to learn about prehistoric burial practices.
The ancient cromlech stood atop the hill, marking the burial site of tribal leaders.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dolmen - a type of megalithic tomb with upright stones and a capstone
