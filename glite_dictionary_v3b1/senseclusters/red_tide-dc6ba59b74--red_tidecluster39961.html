red tide

noun
sea discoloration by toxic algae blooms
7.6
Pronunciation: / rɛd taɪd / (AmE), / rɛd taɪd / (BrE)
a phenomenon in which seawater is discolored, usually red or brown, due to large concentrations of certain algae, especially dinoflagellates, that can produce harmful toxins

Examples

A red tide event
A severe red tide caused a massive fish kill along the gulf coast last year.
Scientists are studying the impact of red tides on marine life and human health.
The health department issued warnings when they detected a red tide near the popular beaches.

Related Terms

  • synonym: algal bloom - sudden increase in algae in water, often causing discoloration
