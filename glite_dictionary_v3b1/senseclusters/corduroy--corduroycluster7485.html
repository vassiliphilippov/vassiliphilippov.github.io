corduroy

noun
road made of logs laid crosswise
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔːrdəˌrɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈkɔːdjʊˌrɔɪ / (BrE)
A road made of logs laid crosswise, typically used in wet or marshy areas to improve passage.

Examples

Corduroy road
During the pioneer era, travelers often used makeshift corduroy to traverse muddy ground.
The construction crew laid logs side by side to create a temporary corduroy.
The old trail became impassable until a corduroy was built across the swamp.
