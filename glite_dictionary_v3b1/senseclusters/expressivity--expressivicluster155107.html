expressivity

noun
degree of gene expression
Pronunciation: / ˌɛkˌsprɛˈsɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌɛkˌsprɛˈsɪvəti / (BrE)
in genetics, the degree to which a particular gene or genotype is expressed in an individual's traits or symptoms

Examples

Gene expressivity
The expressivity of the gene can vary greatly, leading to different symptoms in each patient.
Researchers studied the expressivity of the mutation to understand why some individuals were more severely affected.
Variable expressivity means that even within the same family, the disorder can look very different.

Related Terms

  • synonym: penetrance - the proportion of individuals with a genotype who show the expected trait
