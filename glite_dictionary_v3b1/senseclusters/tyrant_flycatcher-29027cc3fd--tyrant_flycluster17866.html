tyrant flycatcher

noun
large American insect-catching bird
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪrənt ˈflaɪˌkætʃər / (AmE), / ˈtaɪərənt ˈflaɪˌkatʃə / (BrE)
a large American bird of the family Tyrannidae, known for catching insects in flight

Examples

A tyrant flycatcher bird
Scientists observed a tyrant flycatcher snatching mosquitoes midair during their field study.
Several species of tyrant flycatchers migrate between North and South America each year.
The tyrant flycatcher perched boldly at the top of the tree, scanning for insects.
