anharmonic

adjective
not harmonically related
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.hɑːrˈmɑː.nɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.hɑːˈmɒn.ɪk / (BrE)
describing a sound or waveform whose overtones are not whole-number multiples of the fundamental frequency, resulting in an inharmonic or metallic quality

Examples

Anharmonic sound
The old bell produced an anharmonic sound that was noticeably different from a pure tone.
Engineers studied the anharmonic vibrations in the metal to improve the instrument's design.
Some percussion instruments are valued for their unique anharmonic timbres.

Related Terms

  • antonym: harmonic - having overtones that are whole-number multiples of the fundamental frequency
  • synonym: inharmonic - not consisting of frequencies that are whole-number multiples of the fundamental
