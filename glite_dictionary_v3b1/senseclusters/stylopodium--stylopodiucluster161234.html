stylopodium

noun
proximal limb segment
9.9
Pronunciation: / staɪləˈpoʊdiəm / (AmE), / staɪləˈpəʊdiəm / (BrE)
the proximal segment of a vertebrate limb, such as the upper arm or thigh

Examples

Limb stylopodium bone
The humerus is the main bone of the stylopodium in the forelimb of tetrapods.
In humans, the femur forms the stylopodium of the lower limb.
Comparative anatomy studies often focus on the structure of the stylopodium across different species.

Related Terms

  • antonym: autopodium - the distal segment of a vertebrate limb, such as the hand or foot
  • antonym: zeugopodium - the middle segment of a vertebrate limb, such as the forearm or shank
