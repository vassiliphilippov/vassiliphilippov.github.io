supermajority

noun
large required majority
6.8
Pronunciation: /suː.pər.mə.ˈdʒɔːr.ɪ.ti/ (AmE), /suː.pə.mə.ˈdʒɒ.rɪ.ti/ (BrE)
a number or percentage much greater than half, often required to approve important decisions or changes

Examples

A supermajority vote
The new law required a supermajority in parliament to be passed.
Changing the club's constitution needs a supermajority of members to agree.
A supermajority is often necessary for making significant amendments to company policies.
Passing the new law required a supermajority in the parliament, not just a simple majority.
The board could not amend the bylaws because they failed to reach a supermajority vote.
Some constitutional changes demand a supermajority of two-thirds or more to be approved.

  • degree higher: unanimity - agreement by everyone involved
