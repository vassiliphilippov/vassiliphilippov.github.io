revulse

verb
to tear away forcibly
9.9
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvʌls / (AmE), / rɪˈvʌls / (BrE)
to pull, tear, or wrench something away by force; to remove suddenly and violently

Examples

To revulse a sign
The storm's fierce winds revulsed the old sign from its post, sending it crashing to the ground.
With a powerful tug, he revulsed the branch from the tree.
The rescuers revulsed the debris to free the trapped animal.

Related Terms

  • antonym: attach - to fasten or join one thing to another
  • synonym: detach - to remove or separate something from something else
  • synonym: wrench - to pull or twist something suddenly and violently
