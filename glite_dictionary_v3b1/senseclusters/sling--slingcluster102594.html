sling

noun
Y-shaped stick with elastic for shooting
4.1
Pronunciation: / slɪŋ / (AmE), / slɪŋ / (BrE)
a simple weapon or toy made of a strap or Y-shaped stick used to shoot or hurl small objects

Examples

A sling shot
He made a sling from a forked branch and a strip of rubber to play in the backyard.
The children competed to see who could hit the tin can with their sling from the farthest distance.
Using a sling, she launched a pebble across the pond.
Ancient armies often employed soldiers skilled in using a sling during battles.
David used a sling to defeat Goliath in the famous biblical story.
The shepherd fashioned a sling from leather to protect his flock from wolves.

Related Terms

  • synonym: catapult - a device used to launch objects, often with a similar Y-shape and elastic
