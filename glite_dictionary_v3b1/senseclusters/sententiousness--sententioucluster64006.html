sententiousness

noun
concise moralizing style
9.9
Pronunciation: / sɛnˈtɛnʃəsnəs / (AmE), / sɛnˈtɛnʃəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of expressing ideas or opinions in a moralizing, self-important, or pompous way, often using concise or aphoristic statements

Examples

Moral sententiousness
The professor's sententiousness made his lectures sound like a series of moral lessons.
Critics noted the sententiousness of her writing, which often delivered wisdom in just a few words.
His sententiousness sometimes annoyed listeners who preferred a more relaxed conversation style.
The professor's sententiousness made his lectures difficult to enjoy.
She was criticized for the sententiousness of her remarks during the meeting.
Despite his sententiousness, his advice was not very helpful.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aphoristic - expressed in short, witty statements
  • antonym: modesty - the quality of being humble and not boastful
  • synonym: pomposity - the quality of being self-important or arrogant
  • synonym: terse - using few words; brief and direct
  • antonym: verbosity - the quality of using more words than needed
