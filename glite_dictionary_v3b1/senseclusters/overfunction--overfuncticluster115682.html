overfunction

noun
excessive responsibility
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈfʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈfʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
the act or tendency of taking on too much responsibility or doing more work than is reasonable, especially in relationships or organizations

Examples

Chronic overfunction
Her chronic overfunction in relationships left her exhausted and resentful.
Managers should be aware of overfunction among team members to prevent burnout.
His tendency to overfunction at work often led to stress and frustration.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overwork - to work too much or too hard
  • antonym: underfunction - to do less than what is expected or required
