exogenous

adjective
originating from outside
3.2
Pronunciation: / ɛɡˈzoʊdʒənəs / (AmE), / ɛkˈsɒdʒənəs / (BrE)
Originating or produced from outside; derived from external sources, especially in scientific contexts.

Examples

An exogenous shock
An exogenous shock to the economy can have widespread consequences.
Infections can be caused by exogenous bacteria entering the body.
The researchers studied the effects of exogenous hormones on plant growth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: endogenous - originating internally
