overcapacity

noun
excess production ability
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərkəˈpæsəti / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəkəˈpæsəti / (BrE)
a situation in which an industry or organization has more production capacity than is needed to meet demand, leading to inefficiency and lower prices.

Examples

Industry overcapacity
The airline industry faced overcapacity after too many new planes were purchased.
Due to overcapacity, factories were running below their maximum output for several years.
Persistent overcapacity in the steel sector led to falling prices and layoffs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: shortage - a situation in which there is not enough of something
  • synonym: surplus - an amount that is more than what is needed
