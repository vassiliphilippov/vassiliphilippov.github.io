overcapacity

noun
excess production ability

a situation in which an industry or organization has more production capacity than is needed to meet demand, leading to inefficiency and lower prices.

Examples

Industry overcapacity
The airline industry faced overcapacity after too many new planes were purchased.
Due to overcapacity, factories were running below their maximum output for several years.
Persistent overcapacity in the steel sector led to falling prices and layoffs.

Synonyms

  • oversupply
    4.5 0.48/m
    oversupply is too many goods on sale that push prices down, while overcapacity is extra production capacity firms cannot use.
  • oversaturation
    6.5 0.01/m
    oversaturation is a market so full of products that sales fall quickly, while overcapacity is unused production capacity in firms.
  • overproduction
    4.1 0.41/m
    overproduction is making more goods than can be sold, creating waste and storage costs, while overcapacity is unused ability to produce.
