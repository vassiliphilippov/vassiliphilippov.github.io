dichromasy

noun
color vision deficiency involving two wavelengths
9.9 9.5
Pronunciation: /daɪ.ˈkroʊ.mə.siː/ /; / /dɪ.ˈkroʊ.mə.siː/ (AmE), /daɪ.ˈkrəʊ.mə.siː/ /; / /dɪ.ˈkrəʊ.mə.siː/ (BrE)
a deficiency of color vision in which a person can match any color by mixing only two different wavelengths of light, instead of the three required for normal color vision
