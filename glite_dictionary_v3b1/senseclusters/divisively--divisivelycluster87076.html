divisively

adverb
causing disagreement
Pronunciation: / dɪˈvaɪsɪvli / (AmE), / dɪˈvɪsɪvli / (BrE)
in a way that causes disagreement, separation, or hostility between people or groups

Examples

To speak divisively
The leader spoke divisively, which led to increased tension within the community.
Social media can sometimes be used divisively to spread misinformation and create conflict.
The issue was discussed divisively at the meeting, leaving the group split on the decision.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contentiously - in a way that causes or is likely to cause an argument
  • antonym: harmoniously - in a way that is peaceful and friendly
