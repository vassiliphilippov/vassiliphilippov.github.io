Rhodesian man

noun
extinct African hominid species
Pronunciation: / roʊˈdiːʒən mæn / (AmE), / rəʊˈdiːʒɪən man / (BrE)
an extinct African hominid species resembling Neanderthals, known from fossils found in Zambia, also called Homo rhodesiensis

Examples

Rhodesian man fossils
Fossil evidence suggests that Rhodesian man lived in Africa over 300,000 years ago.
Scientists debate whether Rhodesian man should be classified as a distinct species or grouped with Homo heidelbergensis.
The skull of the Rhodesian man was unearthed in what is now Zambia in 1921.
