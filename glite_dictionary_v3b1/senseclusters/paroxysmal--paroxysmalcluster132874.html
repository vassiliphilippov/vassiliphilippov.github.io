paroxysmal

adjective
sudden and intense (medical)
Pronunciation: / ˌpæɹ.əkˈsɪz.məl / (AmE), / ˌpar.əkˈsɪz.məl / (BrE)
Occurring in sudden, brief, and intense episodes, especially of symptoms or attacks; characteristic of or relating to a paroxysm.

Examples

A paroxysmal headache
Doctors noted her paroxysmal headaches, which appeared and disappeared without warning.
Paroxysmal atrial fibrillation can cause heart palpitations that begin suddenly and may last a few minutes or hours.
The patient experienced a paroxysmal episode of coughing during the night.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gradual - Happening slowly over time
  • synonym: sudden - Happening quickly or unexpectedly
