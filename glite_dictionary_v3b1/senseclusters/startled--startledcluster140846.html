startled

adjective
suddenly surprised or alarmed
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈstɑr.təld / (AmE), / ˈstɑː.təld / (BrE)
feeling or showing sudden surprise or alarm, often making a quick involuntary movement

Examples

A startled look
His startled reaction made everyone in the room laugh.
She looked startled when the phone rang unexpectedly late at night.
The startled cat leapt onto the windowsill as the door slammed shut.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - not showing any sudden surprise or alarm, remaining composed
  • synonym: shocked - feeling a sudden and intense surprise, often with distress
