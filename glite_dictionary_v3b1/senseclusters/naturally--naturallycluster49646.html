In a way that is part of a person's or thing's inherent character or tendency, rather than the result of external influences.

Examples

Naturally lazy
He was naturally lazy, preferring to relax rather than work hard.
She is naturally curious and always asks many questions about the world.
Some people are naturally talented at music, while others need to practice more.

Synonyms

  • inherently
    4.7 13/m
    inherently describes a basic, built-in quality of something, while naturally describes what is usual or part of someone's tendency.
  • intrinsically
    6.4 3.33/m
    intrinsically means in the essential nature of something, while naturally suggests how someone usually behaves or tends to act.
  • innately
    5.6 0.81/m
    innately refers to qualities present from birth, while naturally covers both inborn traits and habits that develop over time.
  • by nature
    4.1 4.57/m
    by nature says why someone has a trait as part of their character, while naturally describes how they commonly behave.

Related Terms

  • antonym: artificially - not occurring by nature; through human intervention
  • synonym: innately - in a way that exists from birth, not learned
