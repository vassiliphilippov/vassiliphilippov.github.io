granulosa cell

noun
ovarian supporting cell
Pronunciation: / ˌɡrænjuˈloʊsə sɛl / (AmE), / ˌɡranjʊˈləʊsə sɛl / (BrE)
a cell in the ovary that surrounds and supports developing eggs and helps produce hormones

Examples

The granulosa cell layer
The granulosa cell plays a crucial role in the development of ovarian follicles.
Researchers are investigating how granulosa cells contribute to hormone regulation in the ovary.
Damage to the granulosa cell can affect fertility and hormone balance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oocyte - an immature egg cell in the ovary
  • synonym: theca cell - a type of ovarian cell involved in hormone production
