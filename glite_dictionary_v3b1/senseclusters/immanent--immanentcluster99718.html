immanent

adjective
inherent; existing within something
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈɪmənənt /; / ˈɪm.ə.nənt / (AmE), / ˈɪmənənt /; / ˈɪm.ə.nənt / (BrE)
existing or operating within something; inherent and internal (often used for qualities, divine presence, or within the mind)

Examples

Immanent in nature
She argued that creativity is immanent in human beings, not learned from outside.
The belief that the sacred is immanent in all things is central to many spiritual traditions.
The philosopher described moral law as immanent in the fabric of society.
Her immanent reasoning led her to a decision without any spoken words.
Some theories of perception emphasize the immanent nature of mental acts.
The philosopher argued that thought is an immanent process, not dependent on external expression.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inherent - existing as a natural or essential part of something
  • antonym: transcendent - existing outside or beyond the normal limits; not inherent
