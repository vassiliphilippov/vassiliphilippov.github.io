Parry manzanita

noun
a treelike shrub native to California
Pronunciation: / ˈpæri ˌmænzəˈnitə / (AmE), / ˈpæri ˌmænzəˈniːtə / (BrE)
A shrub native to California, characterized by dense thickets, drooping panicles of white or pink flowers, and red berrylike drupes.

Examples

Parry manzanita shrub
During spring, the Parry manzanita displays clusters of pink blooms that attract native pollinators.
Many gardeners appreciate Parry manzanita for its striking reddish bark and elegant flowers.
The Parry manzanita is commonly found on rocky slopes throughout central California.
