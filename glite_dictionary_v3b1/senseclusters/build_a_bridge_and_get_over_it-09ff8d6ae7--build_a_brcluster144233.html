build a bridge and get over it

verb
stop dwelling on a problem, move on
6.8
Pronunciation: / bɪld ə brɪdʒ ənd ɡɛt ˈoʊvər ɪt / (AmE), / bɪld ə brɪdʒ ənd ɡɛt ˈəʊvə r ɪt / (BrE)
used to tell someone to stop dwelling on a disagreement or problem and to move on

Examples

To build a bridge and get over it quickly
After their disagreement, his friends told him to build a bridge and get over it.
If you made a mistake, just build a bridge and get over it instead of beating yourself up.
Your boss criticised your work; now it's time to build a bridge and get over it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: let it go - to stop being upset or angry about something
