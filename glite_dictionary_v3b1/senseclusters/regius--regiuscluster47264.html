regius

adjective
royal appointment
Pronunciation: / ˈriːdʒiəs / (AmE), / ˈriːdʒiəs / (BrE)
used in titles to indicate royal appointment or patronage, especially in academic or scientific contexts

Examples

Regius Professor
He was honored with the title of Regius Professor of History at the university.
The Regius Chair of Physics was established by royal charter in the 19th century.
Several universities have a Regius Professorship in recognition of royal patronage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: royal - relating to a king, queen, or royal family
