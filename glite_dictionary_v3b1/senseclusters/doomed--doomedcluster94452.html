doomed

adjective
condemned to destruction or eternal punishment
4.9 0.21/m
Pronunciation: / duÀêmd /; / Ààdumd / (AmE), / duÀêmd /; / ÀàduÀêmd / (BrE)
certain to experience a bad outcome, such as failure, destruction, or suffering, often as if decided by fate

Examples

A doomed mission
He prayed for forgiveness, fearing he was doomed beyond hope.
In the story, lost souls wander the earth, forever doomed for their sins.
Many believed the traitor was doomed to suffer in the afterlife.
Despite their courage, the expedition was doomed in the face of the harsh winter.
Everyone thought the project was doomed from the very beginning.
Their relationship seemed doomed by constant arguments and misunderstandings.
His project was doomed from the start due to lack of support.
She believed their love was doomed, no matter how hard they tried.
The explorers felt doomed when the storm grew stronger and supplies ran low.

Synonyms

  • ill-fated
    6.1 1.53/m
    ill-fated is likely to end in failure or disaster, while doomed feels certain of a bad outcome as if by fate.
  • ill-starred
    9.0 0.09/m
    ill-starred shows long bad luck or misfortune, while doomed suggests a fixed fate leading to failure or ruin.
  • no-win
    6.5 0.65/m
    no-win describes a situation with no chance of success, while doomed describes a person or plan certain to fail.
