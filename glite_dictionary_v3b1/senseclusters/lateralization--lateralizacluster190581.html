lateralization

noun
localization of a brain function to one hemisphere
6.3 7.0
Pronunciation: /læt.ər.əl.aɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /læt.ər.əl.aɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the localization of a particular function or activity on either the right or left side of the brain

Examples

Lateralization of brain function
Language ability is often associated with lateralization to the left hemisphere of the brain.
Researchers have studied lateralization in patients with brain injuries to understand how functions are distributed.
The degree of lateralization can vary greatly between individuals, especially for tasks like language and spatial processing.
Researchers examined how lateralisation might be affected in people who are ambidextrous.
Studies on brain lateralisation have shown that language is typically processed in the left hemisphere.
The lateralisation of motor functions allows for specialization between the brain’s hemispheres.
