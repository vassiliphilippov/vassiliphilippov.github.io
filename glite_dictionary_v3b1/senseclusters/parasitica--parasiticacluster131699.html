parasitica

noun
plant-parasitic fungi
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəˈsɪtɪkə / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈsɪtɪkə / (BrE)
a group of fungi that cause diseases in plants by living as parasites on them

Examples

Parasitica fungi
The spread of parasitica has devastated many chestnut tree populations.
Researchers are studying how parasitica infects and damages crops.
Farmers must monitor their fields for signs of parasitica to prevent plant disease.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pathogen - an organism that causes disease
  • antonym: saprophyte - an organism that feeds on dead organic matter
