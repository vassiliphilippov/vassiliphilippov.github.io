transfuse

verb
transfer blood or fluid medically
Pronunciation: / trænsˈfjuːz / (AmE), / trænsˈfjuːz / (BrE)
to transfer blood or another fluid into a person's vein or circulatory system, especially as a medical treatment

Examples

To transfuse blood
After losing so much blood in the accident, she had to be transfused immediately.
The doctor decided to transfuse two units of blood to stabilize the patient.
They are transfusing plasma to support his recovery during surgery.
