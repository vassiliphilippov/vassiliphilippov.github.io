galvanic skin response

noun
change in skin's electrical properties due to stress or arousal
9.8
Pronunciation: / ɡælˈvænɪk skɪn rɪˈspɑns / (AmE), / ɡælˈvɑːnɪk skɪn rɪˈspɒns / (BrE)
a change in the electrical properties of the skin in response to stress, anxiety, or arousal, measurable by changes in skin resistance or current

Examples

Galvanic skin response measurement
A spike in the galvanic skin response can indicate heightened emotional arousal.
Researchers observed a significant change in her galvanic skin response during the experiment.
The polygraph measures the galvanic skin response to detect signs of stress.
