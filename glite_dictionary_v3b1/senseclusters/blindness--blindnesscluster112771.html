blindness

noun
state of being unable to see
Pronunciation: / ˈblaɪnd.nəs / (AmE), / ˈblaɪnd.nɪs / (BrE)
The condition of being unable to see because of injury, disease, or a congenital condition.

Examples

Total blindness
Her gradual blindness made everyday tasks increasingly difficult.
Many charities work to prevent blindness in developing countries.
The accident caused permanent blindness in one eye.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sightlessness - the state of lacking sight
  • antonym: vision - the faculty or state of being able to see
