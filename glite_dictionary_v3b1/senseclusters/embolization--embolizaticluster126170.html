embolization

noun
blocking blood vessels
8.2
Pronunciation: /em.bə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /em.bə.lɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /em.bə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /em.bə.lɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a medical procedure in which a blood vessel is blocked to stop or reduce blood flow to an area

Examples

Embolization procedure
The surgeon performed embolization to control the patient's internal bleeding.
After the embolization, the tumor received less blood and began to shrink.
Doctors may recommend embolization as a less invasive alternative to surgery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: occlusion - the blockage or closing of a blood vessel
  • antonym: recanalization - the process of restoring flow in a blocked blood vessel
