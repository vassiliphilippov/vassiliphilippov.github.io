parabolic reflector

noun
concave reflector for focusing or parallel beams
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛrəˈbɑlɪk rɪˈflɛktər / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈbɒlɪk rɪˈflɛktə / (BrE)
A concave reflective surface shaped as a paraboloid, used to focus light, sound, or other waves to a point or to produce a parallel beam when the source is placed at its focus.

Examples

A parabolic reflector surface
He adjusted the position of the parabolic reflector to focus the sunlight precisely.
Radio telescopes often have a parabolic reflector to collect signals from space.
The headlight uses a parabolic reflector to direct its beam forward.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dish antenna - reflector antenna in the shape of a dish, often a type of parabolic reflector
