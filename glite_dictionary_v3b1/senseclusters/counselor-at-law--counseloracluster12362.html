counselor-at-law

noun
lawyer entitled to plead cases in court
1.7 5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈkaʊn.səl.ər.ət.lɔː/ /; / /ˈkaʊn.səl.ər.ət.lɑː/ (AmE), /ˈkaʊn.səl.ə.ət.lɔː/ (BrE)
A lawyer who is qualified to represent clients in court and plead their cases.

Examples

Counselor-at-law in court
As a counselor-at-law, she has appeared in several high-profile criminal cases.
He consulted his counselor-at-law before making any official statements to the press.
The court appointed a counselor-at-law to represent the defendant.
