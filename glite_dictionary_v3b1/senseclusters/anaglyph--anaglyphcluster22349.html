anaglyph

noun
three-dimensional image using color layers
Pronunciation: / ˈænəɡlɪf / (AmE), / ˈænəɡlɪf / (BrE)
a stereoscopic image, often in contrasting colors, that appears three-dimensional when viewed with special glasses, typically using red and cyan lenses

Examples

Anaglyph 3D image
She created an anaglyph of the landscape, bringing a vivid sense of depth to the picture.
The movie included several anaglyph scenes that required 3D glasses to view correctly.
To appreciate the effect, you need to wear red-cyan glasses when looking at any anaglyph.
