semipermanent

adjective
lasting for a long but limited time
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmiˈpɜrmənənt / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈpɜːmənənt / (BrE)
lasting or intended to last for a relatively long, but not indefinite, period of time; not permanent.

Examples

Semipermanent position
Her position was only semipermanent, with the possibility of becoming permanent after one year.
The salon offers semipermanent hair color that gradually fades after several weeks.
They built semipermanent shelters for the workers during the construction project.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: permanent - lasting or intended to last indefinitely; not expected to change
  • degree lower: temporary - lasting for only a limited period; not permanent
