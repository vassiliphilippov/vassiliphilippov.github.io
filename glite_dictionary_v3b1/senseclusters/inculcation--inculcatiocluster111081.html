inculcation

noun
persistent teaching or instilling ideas
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.kəlˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kʌlˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
The act of teaching or instilling an idea, attitude, or habit by persistent instruction or repetition.

Examples

The inculcation of habits
His inculcation of healthy habits had a lasting influence on his children.
The inculcation of good manners begins at an early age in many households.
Through constant inculcation, the organization ensured its core values were understood by all members.

Related Terms

  • synonym: indoctrination - the process of teaching a person or group to accept a set of beliefs uncritically
