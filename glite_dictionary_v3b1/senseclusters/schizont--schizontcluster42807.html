schizont

noun
protozoan reproductive cell
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈskɪ.zɑːnt/ /; / /ˈskɪ.zənt/ (AmE), /ˈskɪ.zɒnt/ /; / /ˈskɪ.zənt/ (BrE)
a cell stage in the asexual reproduction of certain protozoa, especially malaria parasites, where multiple daughter cells are produced

Examples

A schizont cell stage
The malaria parasite multiplies inside a schizont before infecting new cells.
Under the microscope, the schizont can be seen dividing into many smaller cells.
Doctors study the schizont stage to better understand how malaria spreads in the body.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: merozoite - a cell formed from a schizont that can infect new cells
  • degree lower: sporozoite - an early infectious stage of malaria parasites
