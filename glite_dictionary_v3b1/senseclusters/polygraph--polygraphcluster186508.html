polygraph

noun
lie detector machine
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑliˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a machine that records physiological responses such as heart rate and breathing to help determine if someone is telling the truth

Examples

Police polygraph exam
The police used a polygraph to check the suspect's statements during the investigation.
She was nervous about taking the polygraph test for her new job.
A polygraph measures changes in heart rate, blood pressure, and breathing.
