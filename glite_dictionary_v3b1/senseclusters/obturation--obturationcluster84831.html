obturation

noun
blocking a passage
Pronunciation: / ˌɑb.tʃəˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɒb.tjʊˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of closing or blocking a passage or opening, especially in a medical or dental context

Examples

Root canal obturation
The dentist performed obturation to seal the root canal and prevent infection.
Proper obturation of the wound is essential to stop further bleeding.
In endodontic treatment, obturation ensures that the canal is completely sealed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: obstruction - something that blocks or closes a passage
  • synonym: occlusion - the blockage or closing of a blood vessel or hollow organ
  • antonym: opening - an act of making something open or unblocked
