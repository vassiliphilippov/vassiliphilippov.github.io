brachial vein

noun
vein in the upper arm draining into axillary vein
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˈbreɪkiəl veɪn / (AmE), / ˈbrækiəl veɪn / (BrE)
either of two veins in the upper arm that run alongside the brachial artery and drain into the axillary vein

Examples

Brachial vein in arm
A blood clot in the brachial vein can cause significant swelling in the arm.
The brachial vein is often used for intravenous access in emergency situations.
The surgeon carefully avoided the brachial vein during the procedure.
