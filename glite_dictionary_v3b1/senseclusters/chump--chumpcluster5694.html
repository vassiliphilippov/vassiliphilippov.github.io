chump

verb
to trick someone
Pronunciation: / tʃʌmp / (AmE), / tʃʌmp / (BrE)
to trick or deceive someone, making them appear foolish

Examples

To chump a friend
He realized too late that he had been chumped by the clever scam.
They chumped their friend with a fake lottery ticket as a joke.
I can't believe I got chumped into believing that story.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dupe - to deceive or trick someone
  • synonym: fool - to trick or deceive someone
  • synonym: outsmart - to defeat or trick someone by being more clever
