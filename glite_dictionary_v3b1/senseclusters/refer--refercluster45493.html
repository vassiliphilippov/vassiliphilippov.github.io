to mention or speak about someone or something; to direct attention to something or someone.

Examples

To refer to achievements
In her speech, she referred to the achievements of women in science.
Please refer to page 12 for more details about the project.
The article refers to a recent study on climate change.

Synonyms

  • mention
    1.0 180/m
    mention says something briefly in speech or writing, while refer can also direct attention or point to related information.
  • make mention of
    3.6 0.32/m
    make mention of is a formal phrase used to say something briefly in passing, while refer is more general and neutral.
  • bring up
    2.6 30/m
    bring up means to introduce or start a topic for discussion, while refer usually means simply to mention or point to something.
