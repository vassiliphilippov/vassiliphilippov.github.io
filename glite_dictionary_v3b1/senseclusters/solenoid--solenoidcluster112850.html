solenoid

noun
coil producing a magnetic field when electrified
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊləˌnɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈsəʊlɪnɔɪd / (BrE)
A cylindrical coil of wire, often wound around an iron core, that produces a magnetic field when an electric current passes through it.

Examples

A solenoid coil
Car starters often use a solenoid to engage the motor with the engine.
The engineer tested the solenoid to see if it would activate the mechanical switch.
When an electric current passes through the solenoid, it creates a magnetic field inside the coil.

Related Terms

  • synonym: electromagnet - a type of magnet created by electrical current
