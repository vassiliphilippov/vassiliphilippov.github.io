draw off

verb
remove a portion from a supply source
2.2
Pronunciation: / drɔ ɔf /; / drɔ ɑf / (AmE), / drɔː ɒf / (BrE)
to remove a liquid from a container or source, usually by pouring, draining, or taking a sample

Examples

To draw off water
The technician drew off a sample of the liquid for testing.
They drew off funds from the reserve account to cover the expenses.
You should draw off any excess oil before refilling the engine.
After boiling the pasta, remember to draw off the remaining water before adding the sauce.
The brewery drew off a sample from the large fermentation tank for testing.
They carefully draw off excess oil to prevent spilling during repairs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: add - to put something with something else; opposite of remove
  • synonym: drain - to cause liquid to flow away from something
  • synonym: withdraw - remove money or supplies from a source
