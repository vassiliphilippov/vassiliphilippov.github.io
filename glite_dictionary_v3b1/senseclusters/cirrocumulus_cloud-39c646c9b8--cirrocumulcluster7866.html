cirrocumulus cloud

noun
high-altitude cloud with rippled appearance
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪroʊˈkjuːmjələs klaʊd / (AmE), / ˌsɪrəʊˈkjuːmjʊləs klaʊd / (BrE)
a high-altitude cloud consisting of small, regularly arranged cloudlets that often appear as ripples or patches in the sky

Examples

Cirrocumulus clouds in sky
Meteorologists noted the presence of cirrocumulus clouds ahead of the approaching cold front.
On our hike, we admired the pattern of cirrocumulus clouds stretching across the blue sky.
The sky was dotted with delicate cirrocumulus clouds that looked like tiny ripples in the morning light.

Related Terms

  • synonym: altocumulus cloud - a middle-altitude cloud of white or gray patches
  • synonym: stratocumulus cloud - a low, large, white, or gray cloud in groups, lines, or waves
