mooncalf

noun
foolish person
Pronunciation: / ˈmuːnˌkæf / (AmE), / ˈmuːnˌkɑːf / (BrE)
a foolish or absent-minded person, especially as described in old literature

Examples

Silly mooncalf
In Shakespeare's play, the character was mocked as a mooncalf for his silly behavior.
The villagers laughed at the mooncalf who wandered aimlessly through the fields.
He was dismissed as a mooncalf because of his absent-minded mistakes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fool - a person who acts unwisely or imprudently
  • antonym: genius - a person with exceptional intellectual ability
  • synonym: simpleton - a person lacking in common sense or intelligence
