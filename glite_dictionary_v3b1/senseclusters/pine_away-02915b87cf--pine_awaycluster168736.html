pine away

verb
gradually weaken from grief or longing
9.8
Pronunciation: / paɪn əˈweɪ / (AmE), / paɪn əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to gradually become weak or ill because of deep sadness, grief, or longing for someone or something

Examples

To pine away slowly
After her husband died, she just pined away for months, unable to regain her spirit.
Many prisoners pined away from homesickness during their long captivity.
The elderly dog pined away after his owner moved out, refusing to eat.
After her best friend moved away, she began to pine away in her empty house.
He refused to pine away over lost opportunities and focused on the future instead.
Year after year, the old dog seemed to pine away waiting for its owner to return.

Related Terms

  • synonym: languish - to lose or lack vitality; grow weak or feeble
