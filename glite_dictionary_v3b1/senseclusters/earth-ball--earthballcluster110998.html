earth-ball

noun
edible subterranean fungus (genus Tuber)
Pronunciation: / ˈɜrθ ˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈɜːθ ˌbɔːl / (BrE)
a type of highly prized edible subterranean fungus of the genus Tuber, known for its culinary value and natural growth in southwestern Europe

Examples

The earth-ball fungus
Foragers in southwestern Europe often search for earth-balls in the forests during autumn.
Some chefs prefer to use earth-balls in gourmet dishes to achieve a rich, earthy flavor.
The aroma of a fresh earth-ball can enhance the taste of pasta and risotto.
