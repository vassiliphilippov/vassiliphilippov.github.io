exacerbation

noun
violent increase in bitterness or intensity
9.9 6.9
Pronunciation: /ɪg.zæs.ər.ˈbeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɪg.zæs.ə.ˈbeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of making something worse or more intense, such as a problem, conflict, or symptoms

Examples

An exacerbation of symptoms
A combination of stress and fatigue often leads to an exacerbation of workplace disputes.

Air pollution can lead to the exacerbation of asthma symptoms in sensitive individuals.
Economic crises often result in the exacerbation of social inequalities.
The physician noted a sudden exacerbation of the patient's chronic pain after surgery.
