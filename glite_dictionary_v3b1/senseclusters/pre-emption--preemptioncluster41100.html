pre-emption

noun
government right to appropriate property
6.9
Pronunciation: / priˈɛm(p)ʃən /; / priˈɛmpʃən / (AmE), / priːˈɛmpʃən /; / priˈɛmpʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the right or act of taking, claiming, or purchasing something before others, especially by law or government

Examples

Pre-emption rights
By law, pre-emption allows authorities to claim property before private buyers.
Legislation detailing the scope of pre-emption is often debated in parliament.
The government exercised its pre-emption rights to acquire the land for highway construction.
Many countries have laws granting government pre-emption in cases involving national security.
Shareholders were given pre-emption rights when the new shares became available.
The company exercised its pre-emption rights to acquire the land before it was put on the market.
Government pre-emption of the land prevented any private development in the area.
The pre-emption of tickets by sponsors left little for the general public.
The company's pre-emption of new technology gave it a strong competitive advantage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: priority - the state or right of coming before others
  • synonym: reservation - the act of keeping something back or setting it aside for future use
  • synonym: right of first refusal - right to buy something before it is sold to others
