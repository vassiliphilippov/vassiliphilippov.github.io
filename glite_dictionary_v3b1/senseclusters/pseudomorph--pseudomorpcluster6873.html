pseudomorph

noun
imitation form
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːdəˌmɔrf / (AmE), / ˈsjuːdəʊˌmɔːf / (BrE)
something that has the outward form or appearance of another thing but does not have its true nature or substance

Examples

Fake pseudomorph
The sculpture was a clever pseudomorph of an ancient artifact, fooling many visitors.
In geology, a mineral can become a pseudomorph by taking the shape of another mineral.
The new building is just a pseudomorph of the historic theater that once stood there.

Related Terms

  • synonym: imitation - a copy or reproduction of something
  • antonym: original - the first or real version of something
  • synonym: replica - an exact copy or model of something
