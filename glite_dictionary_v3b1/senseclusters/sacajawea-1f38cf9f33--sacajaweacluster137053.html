Sacajawea

proper noun
Shoshone guide for Lewis and Clark
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsækədʒəˈwiːə / (AmE), / ˌsækədʒəˈwiːə / (BrE)
(c. 1788–c. 1812) A Shoshone woman who served as a guide and interpreter for the Lewis and Clark expedition across the western United States.

Examples

Sacajawea guide woman
Sacajawea played a key role in the success of the Lewis and Clark expedition.
Many schools and statues across the United States are named in honor of Sacajawea.
Textbooks often highlight the contributions of Sacajawea to American history.
