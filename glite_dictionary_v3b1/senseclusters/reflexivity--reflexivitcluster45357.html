reflexivity

noun
self-examination process
8.2 0.32/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀêfl…õkÀàs…™v…ôti / (AmE), / ÀåriÀêfl…õkÀàs…™v…ôti / (BrE)
the process of examining or reflecting on one's own actions, thoughts, or role, especially in research or social contexts

Examples

Researcher reflexivity
Reflexivity is important for researchers to understand their own biases.
The teacher encouraged reflexivity among students to help them learn from their experiences.
In social sciences, reflexivity helps professionals recognize how their background influences their work.

Synonyms

  • self-examination
    4.6 0.76/m
    self-examination is careful personal checking of thoughts and behavior for insight, while reflexivity focuses on one's role, actions, and context.
  • introspection
    6.9 2.10/m
    introspection is noticing private thoughts and feelings inside yourself, while reflexivity attends also to your public actions and role.
  • self-contemplation
    7.9 0.02/m
    self-contemplation is long, quiet thinking about desires and feelings, while reflexivity examines how your actions affect situations and roles.
  • reflection
    3.7 20/m
    reflection is careful thought often before decisions, while reflexivity specifically examines your own actions, role, and impact in social or research contexts.
