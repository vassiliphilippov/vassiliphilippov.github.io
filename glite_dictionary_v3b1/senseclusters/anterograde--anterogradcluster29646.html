anterograde

adjective
affecting memory after trauma
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈæn.t̬ə.roʊˌɡreɪd / (AmE), / ˈæn.tərəˌɡreɪd / (BrE)
(of amnesia or memory loss) affecting the ability to form new memories after an event, such as a trauma.

Examples

Anterograde amnesia
After the accident, the patient exhibited anterograde amnesia and could not remember events occurring afterward.
Many films depict anterograde amnesia as a dramatic barrier to remembering the present.
The neurologist explained that anterograde memory loss prevented the formation of new memories.

Related Terms

  • antonym: retrograde - (of amnesia) affecting memories from before a trauma; the opposite of anterograde
