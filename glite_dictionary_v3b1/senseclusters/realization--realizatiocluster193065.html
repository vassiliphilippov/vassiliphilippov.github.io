realization

noun
conversion of assets into cash; money obtained from sale
6.5 4.8
Pronunciation: /riː.ə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /rɪə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /rɪə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /rɪə.laɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act of converting assets into cash, especially by selling stocks or property, or the money obtained from such a sale

Examples

Realization of assets
A sudden market downturn forced the realization of several large stock holdings.
After the realization of the estate, the creditors were finally paid.
The company's realization of its remaining assets helped pay off its debts.
After the realisation of his shares, he invested the money in real estate.
The administrator handled the realisation of the estate efficiently, ensuring all debts were settled.
The bankrupt company's properties were sold off in a realisation to pay creditors.
