apprehend

verb
anticipate with fear or anxiety
Pronunciation: / ˌæprɪˈhɛnd / (AmE), / ˌæprɪˈhɛnd / (BrE)
to anticipate or expect something with fear or anxiety

Examples

Apprehend danger
As the deadline approached, he began to apprehend the consequences of his actions.
Many people apprehend the results of the exam, fearing they will not pass.
She could not help but apprehend what might happen to her family during the storm.
