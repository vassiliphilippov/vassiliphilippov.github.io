over

noun
six balls bowled in cricket
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
In cricket, a set of six consecutive balls bowled by a single bowler from one end of the pitch to the batsman.

Examples

A cricket over
After bowling three overs, the player was replaced by another bowler.
The team scored fifteen runs in the last over of the match.
The umpire signaled the end of the over with a wave of his hand.
