regurge

verb
to bring up food
Pronunciation: / rɪˈɡɜrdʒ / (AmE), / rɪˈɡɜːdʒ / (BrE)
to bring food or liquid back up from the stomach into the mouth without vomiting forcefully

Examples

Babies regurge milk
The baby sometimes regurges a small amount of milk after feeding.
Some birds regurge food to feed their young in the nest.
If you eat too quickly, you might regurge some of your meal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: regurgitate - to bring swallowed food back up into the mouth
  • antonym: vomit - to forcefully expel stomach contents through the mouth
