single-channel

adjective
using one sound channel (audio)
6.2 4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪŋɡəl ˌtʃænəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋɡl ˌtʃænəl / (BrE)
relating to sound transmission, recording, or reproduction using only one channel, as opposed to multiple channels (such as stereo or surround sound)

Examples

Single-channel sound
Most early radio broadcasts were single-channel, providing monophonic sound only.
Some audio equipment still supports single-channel output for simplicity and compatibility.
The single-channel recording lacked the depth and directionality of modern stereo tracks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mono - using a single audio channel for recording or playback
  • antonym: stereo - using two or more audio channels for recording or playback
