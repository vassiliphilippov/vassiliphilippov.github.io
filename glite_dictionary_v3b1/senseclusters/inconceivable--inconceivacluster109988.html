inconceivable

adjective
impossible to imagine or believe
7.4 2.90/m
Pronunciation: / Àå…™nk…ônÀàsiÀêv…ôb…ôl / (AmE), / Àå…™nk…ônÀàsiÀêv…ôbl / (BrE)
Impossible to imagine or believe; extremely unlikely.

Examples

An inconceivable idea
It is inconceivable that such a small mistake could have caused the entire system to fail.
Some scientists once considered flight by heavier-than-air machines inconceivable, but the Wright brothers proved it possible.
The idea that he would lie to her was simply inconceivable to everyone who knew him well.

Synonyms

  • unimaginable
    5.4 3.83/m
    unimaginable is extremely hard to picture in your mind, while inconceivable sounds more formal and very unlikely.
  • unbelievable
    4.9 13/m
    unbelievable describes something so surprising or unlikely that people doubt it, while inconceivable is stronger and often more formal.
  • unthinkable
    5.7 5.78/m
    unthinkable means something people refuse to consider or accept as possible, while inconceivable focuses on being hard to imagine.
  • incredible
    3.5 48/m
    incredible is often used to show strong surprise or praise, while inconceivable is more formal and stresses impossibility.
