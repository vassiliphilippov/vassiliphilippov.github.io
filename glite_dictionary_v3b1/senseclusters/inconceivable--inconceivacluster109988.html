inconceivable

adjective
impossible to imagine or believe
6.0 5.9
Pronunciation: /ɪn.kən.ˈsiː.və.bəl/ (AmE), /ɪn.kən.ˈsiː.və.bəl/ (BrE)
Impossible to imagine or believe; extremely unlikely.

Examples

An inconceivable idea
It is inconceivable that such a small mistake could have caused the entire system to fail.
Some scientists once considered flight by heavier-than-air machines inconceivable, but the Wright brothers proved it possible.
The idea that he would lie to her was simply inconceivable to everyone who knew him well.
