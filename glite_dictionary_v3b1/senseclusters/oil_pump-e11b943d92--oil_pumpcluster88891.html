oil pump

noun
device that circulates oil in machinery
Pronunciation: / ɔɪl pʌmp / (AmE), / ɔɪl pʌmp / (BrE)
a mechanical device that circulates oil to lubricate and cool moving parts, especially in engines or machinery

Examples

The oil pump fails
If the oil pump fails, it can cause severe damage to the motor.
Modern cars are designed with an efficient oil pump for better engine longevity.
The mechanic checked the oil pump to ensure the engine was being properly lubricated.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pump - a device for moving fluids or gases
