roll over

verb
reinvest funds in a similar investment
Pronunciation: / roʊl ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / rəʊl ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to move or continue something financial, such as reinvesting funds or extending a loan to a new period

Examples

She decided to roll over her 401(k) into a new retirement account.

Many borrowers choose to roll over their loans rather than pay the full amount when due.
We had to roll over the loan to avoid defaulting on our payments.
