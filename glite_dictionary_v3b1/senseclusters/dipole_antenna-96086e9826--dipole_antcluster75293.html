dipole antenna

noun
a basic two-rod radio antenna
4.8 5.9
Pronunciation: /ˈdaɪ.poʊl æn.ˈten.ə/ (AmE), /ˈdaɪ.pəʊl æn.ˈten.ə/ (BrE)
a type of antenna consisting of two rods or conductive elements arranged in a straight line, fed in the center, and typically half a wavelength long

Examples

A dipole antenna rod
A properly tuned dipole antenna can greatly enhance the performance of a shortwave radio.
Many amateur radio enthusiasts build their own dipole antennas for transmitting and receiving signals.
The radio operator set up a dipole antenna to improve reception in the remote area.
