Pimpinella anisum

noun
herb with aromatic seeds used in cooking and medicine
Pronunciation: / pɪmˌpɪnɛlə əˈnaɪsəm / (AmE), / pɪmˌpɪnɛlə əˈnɪsəm / (BrE)
An annual herb native to Egypt, widely cultivated for its aromatic seeds and oil, which are used in cooking and traditional medicine.

Examples

Pimpinella anisum seeds
Pimpinella anisum is valued for its sweet, licorice-like flavor in baked goods and liqueurs.
The seeds of Pimpinella anisum are often added to teas for a distinctive aroma.
Traditional remedies sometimes use oil extracted from Pimpinella anisum to soothe digestive problems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anise - aromatic plant used as a spice and flavoring
