dearly

adverb
at great cost; with much suffering or loss
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪrli / (AmE), / ˈdɪəli / (BrE)
in a way that involves great loss, suffering, or cost; at a high price

Examples

Pay dearly for a mistake
He realized too late that he had paid dearly for his mistake.
If you betray their trust, you may suffer dearly as a result.
Many soldiers fought and dearly bought the freedom we enjoy today.

  • antonym: cheaply - at little cost or sacrifice
