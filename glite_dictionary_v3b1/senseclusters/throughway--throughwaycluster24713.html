throughway

noun
broad highway for high-speed traffic
6.6 3.4
Pronunciation: /ˈθruː.weɪ/ /; / /θruː.ˈweɪ/ (AmE), /ˈθruː.weɪ/ /; / /θruː.ˈweɪ/ (BrE)
A broad highway designed for high-speed traffic, typically allowing vehicles to travel without interruptions such as stoplights or intersections.

Examples

A throughway highway
Heavy trucks are not allowed on the throughway after 10 p.m.
The city built a new throughway to help ease congestion during rush hour.
We took the throughway to get across the state more quickly.
