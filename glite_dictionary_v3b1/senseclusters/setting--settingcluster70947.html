setting

noun
environment or place where something happens
1.0 2.7
Pronunciation: /ˈse.tɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈse.tɪŋ/ (BrE)
the place, environment, or circumstances in which something happens, including the time and context of an event or story

Examples

A school setting
Children often behave differently in a school setting compared to at home.
The conference took place in an academic setting that encouraged open discussion.
The novel's dramatic setting heightened the tension among the characters.
Before making a decision, consider the political setting at the time.
The setting of the novel is crucial to understanding its themes.
The conference provided a professional setting for networking and discussion.
Filmmakers carefully choose the setting to enhance the mood of each scene.
The author's vivid descriptions help readers imagine the setting of the novel.
The old castle provided a dramatic setting for their wedding ceremony.

Related Terms

  • synonym: background - the conditions or situation in which something takes place
  • synonym: context - the circumstances that form the setting for an event
  • antonym: foreground - the part of a scene that is nearest to and in front of the viewer, often contrasted with setting
  • synonym: situation - set of circumstances in which one finds oneself
