opulence

noun
great wealth or luxuriousness
7.7 1.00/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…ëÀê.pj…ô.l…ôns / (AmE), / Àà…íp.j ä.l…ôns / (BrE)
great wealth or luxuriousness; the state of having or displaying immense riches, often in a lavish or extravagant manner

Examples

Great opulence
Despite his opulence, he lived a surprisingly modest life among the wealthy elite.
Her wedding dress embodied opulence, adorned with precious jewels and intricate lacework.
The royal palace was a symbol of opulence with its gilded halls and marble staircases.

Synonyms

  • sumptuousness
    8.1 0.04/m
    sumptuousness shows rich, lavish texture and comfort, while opulence denotes great wealth and showy display of riches.
  • luxuriousness
    7.2 0.02/m
    luxuriousness means extreme comfort and rich materials, while opulence suggests very large wealth and public display.
  • lavishness
    6.6 0.07/m
    lavishness means giving or spending in large amounts, while opulence focuses on great wealth shown in rich style.
  • sumptuosity
    9.9 0.01/m
    sumptuosity is an old word for extreme costly luxury, while opulence is more common and means great wealth or lavish display.
  • affluence
    6.3 2.17/m
    affluence means having a large amount of money, while opulence emphasizes both vast wealth and its rich, showy display.
  • luxury
    4.2 20/m
    luxury refers to comfort, fine things, or rare pleasures, while opulence stresses great wealth and lavish public display.
  • splendor
    4.3 3.93/m
    splendor shows impressive beauty and grand sights, while opulence links those sights specifically to great wealth and richness.
  • magnificence
    5.0 1.23/m
    magnificence means grand, impressive beauty or scale, while opulence adds the idea of great wealth and lavish living.

Related Terms

  • synonym: luxury - a state of great comfort or elegance, often involving great expense
  • antonym: poverty - the state of being extremely poor, without sufficient wealth
