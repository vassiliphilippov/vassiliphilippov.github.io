creep

noun
animal enclosure allowing young but not adults
Pronunciation: / krip / (AmE), / kriːp / (BrE)
a small pen or enclosure designed so that young animals can enter to feed while adults are kept out

Examples

A calf creep
Farmers use a creep to ensure only calves get access to special feed.
The lambs happily entered the creep to feed without being disturbed by the older sheep.
The new creep was built near the barn to make feeding the piglets easier.
