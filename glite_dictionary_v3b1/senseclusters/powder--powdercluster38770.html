powder

noun
explosive mixture used in guns and fireworks
Pronunciation: / ˈpaʊdɚ / (AmE), / ˈpaʊdə / (BrE)
A mixture of potassium nitrate, charcoal, and sulfur, usually in a 75:15:10 ratio, used as an explosive in gunnery, time fuses, and fireworks; also known as gunpowder.

Examples

Gun powder
Black powder was a crucial component in early firearms and cannons.
Old time fuses were packed with powder to ensure a controlled detonation.
The fireworks display was powered by a carefully measured amount of powder.
