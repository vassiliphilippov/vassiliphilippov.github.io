ethnographer

noun
person who studies cultures and societies
9.9 6.8
Pronunciation: / ɛθˈnɑɡrəfər / (AmE), / ɛθˈnɒɡrəfə / (BrE)
a person who studies and describes the customs, social patterns, and cultures of different peoples, typically through detailed observation and fieldwork

Examples

An ethnographer studies cultures
A skilled ethnographer must remain objective while observing social interactions.
Her report, written as an ethnographer, documented the intricate family structures of the tribe.
The ethnographer spent months living with the community to understand their traditions and daily life.
