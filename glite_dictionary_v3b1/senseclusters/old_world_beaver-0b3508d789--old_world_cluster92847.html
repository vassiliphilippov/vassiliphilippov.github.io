Old World beaver

noun
beaver species native to Europe and Asia
6.1
Pronunciation: / oʊld wɝːld ˈbiːvɚ / (AmE), / əʊld wɜːld ˈbiːvə / (BrE)
a species of beaver native to Europe and western Asia, distinct from the North American beaver

Examples

The Old World beaver species
Conservation efforts have helped the Old World beaver recover in some parts of Europe.
Once nearly extinct, Old World beaver populations are steadily increasing due to protection laws.
The Old World beaver differs from its North American cousin in several physical traits.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beaver - a large semiaquatic rodent known for building dams
