telescoped

adjective
compressed by sliding parts or crushed together
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛlɪˌskoʊpt / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪˌskəʊpt / (BrE)
having been shortened or compressed by parts sliding into one another, or appearing so; crushed together or condensed, sometimes metaphorically

Examples

A telescoped schedule
Our busy schedule made the week feel completely telescoped, as if time had shrunk.
The train's passenger compartments were telescoped after the collision, making rescue operations extremely difficult.
The two vehicles became telescoped together in the violent impact on the highway.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compressed - pressed into less space; condensed
  • antonym: elongated - lengthened; made longer
