Catherine of Aragon

proper noun
first wife of Henry VIII of England
Pronunciation: / ˈkæθərɪn əv ˈærəɡɑn / (AmE), / ˈkæθərɪn əv ˈærəɡən / (BrE)
(1485–1536) the first wife of Henry VIII of England, whose divorce from her led to the beginning of the English Reformation.

Examples

Catherine of Aragon first wife
Catherine of Aragon was married to Henry VIII for over twenty years before their marriage was annulled.
Many historians study the influence of Catherine of Aragon on Tudor England.
The English Reformation began after Henry VIII sought to divorce Catherine of Aragon.
