salt tree

noun
spiny shrub with silvery foliage and pink-purple blossoms
Pronunciation: / sɔlt tri / (AmE), / sɒlt triː / (BrE)
a spiny shrub native to the Caspian salt plains and Siberia, with silvery downy young leaves and mildly fragrant pink-purple flowers

Examples

The salt tree plant
Botanists study the salt tree for its ability to withstand harsh, saline environments.
The salt tree thrives in salty soils where few other plants can survive.
We admired the silvery foliage and delicate blooms of the salt tree during our trip to Siberia.
