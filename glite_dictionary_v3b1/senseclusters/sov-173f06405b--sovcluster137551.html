SOV

sym
subject-object-verb order
9.6
Pronunciation: / ɛs oʊ vi / (AmE), / ɛs əʊ viː / (BrE)
an abbreviation for a type of language word order where the subject comes first, followed by the object, then the verb

Examples

SOV word order
Japanese is an example of an SOV language, where the verb comes at the end of the sentence.
In an SOV structure, a sentence like 'She cake eats' is grammatically correct.
Many languages in Central Asia use the SOV word order as their standard sentence structure.

Related Terms

  • antonym: SVO - subject-verb-object word order
  • antonym: VSO - verb-subject-object word order
