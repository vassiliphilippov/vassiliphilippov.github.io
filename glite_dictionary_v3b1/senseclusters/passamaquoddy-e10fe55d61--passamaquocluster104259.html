Passamaquoddy

noun
Native American people
Pronunciation: / ˌpæsəməˈkwɑːdi / (AmE), / ˌpæsəməˈkwɒdi / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people indigenous to northeastern Maine and southwestern New Brunswick

Examples

A Passamaquoddy elder
A Passamaquoddy elder shared traditional stories with the children at the cultural center.
The Passamaquoddy have lived in the region for thousands of years.
Many Passamaquoddy continue to preserve their language and customs today.
