hemodialysis

noun
blood filtration medical procedure
1.1 4.3
Pronunciation: /hiː.moʊ.daɪ.ˈæl.ə.sɪs/ /; / /hə.moʊ.daɪ.ˈæl.ə.sɪs/ (AmE), /hiː.məʊ.daɪ.ˈæl.ə.sɪs/ /; / /hə.məʊ.daɪ.ˈæl.ə.sɪs/ (BrE)
A medical procedure in which a machine filters waste products and excess fluids from the blood, used to treat kidney failure.

Examples

Hemodialysis treatment
Advances in hemodialysis have greatly improved survival rates for patients with chronic kidney disease.
After her kidneys failed, she was immediately started on hemodialysis.
The patient undergoes hemodialysis three times a week at the clinic.
Advances in haemodialysis technology have greatly improved the quality of life for those with chronic renal disease.
Her doctor explained that haemodialysis would help remove toxins from her bloodstream.
The patient underwent haemodialysis three times a week to manage his kidney failure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dialysis - the process of removing waste products from the blood
  • antonym: peritoneal dialysis - dialysis using the lining of the abdomen rather than a machine
