potlicker

noun
vegetable cooking broth
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑtˌlɪkər / (AmE), / ˈpɒtˌlɪkə / (BrE)
the liquid left in a pot after cooking greens or vegetables, often served as a flavorful broth, especially in the southern United States

Examples

A bowl of potlicker
She dipped her cornbread into the hot potlicker and savored the salty, flavorful broth.
After cooking collard greens, he poured the potlicker into a bowl to enjoy with his meal.
Many southern families consider potlicker a nutritious and traditional part of their dinner.

  • synonym: stock - a flavored liquid base for soups and sauces
