reappropriation

noun
giving new meaning
8.9 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀê…ôÀåpro äpriÀàe…™ É…ôn / (AmE), / ÀåriÀê…ôÀåpr…ô äpriÀàe…™ É…ôn / (BrE)
the act of taking cultural elements, images, or objects and giving them new meanings or contexts, often by artists or groups

Examples

Reappropriation of objects
The museum's exhibit featured the reappropriation of everyday objects as modern art.
Through reappropriation, the community reclaimed symbols that once had negative meanings.
Many artists use reappropriation to challenge traditional ideas about culture and ownership.

Synonyms

  • recontextualization
    9.8 0.08/m
    recontextualization places an object in a new setting to change its meaning, while reappropriation involves taking cultural elements and giving them new social meanings.
  • reinterpretation
    6.2 0.74/m
    reinterpretation gives a new explanation or meaning to something, while reappropriation means taking cultural elements and using them in new contexts.

Related Terms
