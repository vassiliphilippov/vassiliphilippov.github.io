send

verb
arrange delivery or transportation
Pronunciation: / sɛnd / (AmE), / sɛnd / (BrE)
to cause something (such as a letter, message, product, or email) to go or be delivered to another place or person

Examples

To send a letter
The company sends products all over the world.
They sent the package by courier to arrive quickly.
We will send your order within three business days.
He decided to send flowers to his friend in the hospital.
Please send me your contact information as soon as possible.
They are sending medical supplies to areas affected by the flood.
Please send the documents to my office as soon as possible.
She sent her children to school on the bus every morning.
We are sending an invitation to everyone in the department.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deliver - to bring or take something to a particular place or person
  • synonym: dispatch - to send off to a destination or for a purpose
  • synonym: forward - to send something onward to another destination
  • antonym: receive - to be given or presented with something sent
  • synonym: ship - to send goods by a commercial carrier
