Echinochloa frumentacea

noun
annual grass grown for seeds and forage
Pronunciation: / ɛˌkaɪnoʊˈkloʊ.ə fruˌmɛnˈteɪʃə / (AmE), / ɛˌkɪnəˈkləʊ.ə fruːˌmɛnˈteɪsɪə / (BrE)
A coarse annual grass cultivated in Asia for its edible seeds and as fodder; also important as a wildlife food source in the United States.

Examples

Echinochloa frumentacea millet crop
Farmers in India often grow Echinochloa frumentacea as a drought-resistant millet crop.
The seeds of Echinochloa frumentacea are harvested and eaten as a staple food in some Asian countries.
Wildlife biologists have noted that Echinochloa frumentacea provides valuable food for waterfowl in wetland areas.
