drinky

adjective
easy to drink
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈdrɪŋki / (AmE), / ˈdrɪŋki / (BrE)
pleasant and easy to drink, especially referring to a beverage that is smooth and not strong or complex.

Examples

A drinky wine
This white wine is so drinky that it is perfect for a summer afternoon.
The cocktail was light and drinky, making it easy to enjoy more than one.
He prefers a drinky beer that goes down smoothly with his meal.

Related Terms

  • antonym: harsh - having a strong, unpleasant taste or effect
  • synonym: smooth - having a pleasant, mild taste and texture
