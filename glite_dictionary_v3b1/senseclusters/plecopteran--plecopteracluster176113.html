plecopteran

noun
aquatic insect of order Plecoptera
8.0
Pronunciation: / plɛˈkɑptərən / (AmE), / plɛˈkɒptərən / (BrE)
A primitive winged insect of the order Plecoptera, having a flattened body and aquatic larvae, often used as bait by anglers.

Examples

A plecopteran insect
A decline in plecopteran populations can indicate worsening water quality in freshwater habitats.
Many fishermen use plecopteran larvae as live bait when angling for trout.
The biologist collected several plecopteran specimens from the stream to study their gill development.
