beaked salmon

noun
fish with angular snout for burrowing
Pronunciation: / ˈbikt ˈsæmən / (AmE), / ˈbiːkt ˈsæmən / (BrE)
A fish of sandy areas in the western Pacific and Indian oceans, notable for its angular snout used for burrowing into sand.

Examples

The beaked salmon fish
Marine biologists discovered a group of beaked salmon near the coral reefs of Indonesia.
The beaked salmon uses its pointed snout to dig into sandy seabeds for shelter.
The distinctive snout shape of the beaked salmon helps it thrive in shifting underwater sands.
