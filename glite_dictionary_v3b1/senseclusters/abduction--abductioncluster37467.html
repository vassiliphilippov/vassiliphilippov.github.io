abduction

noun
the act of carrying away someone by force
1.1
Pronunciation: / æbˈdʌk.ʃən / (AmE), / əbˈdʌk.ʃən / (BrE)
The act of illegally taking someone away by force, especially a family member, often as a crime.

Examples

Child abduction
Laws against abduction are particularly strict in cases involving minors or spouses.
She lived in fear after escaping an attempted abduction by a stranger.
The police responded quickly after the abduction of the child from her home.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kidnapping - unlawful taking and detaining of a person, usually for ransom
  • antonym: release - the act of setting someone free
