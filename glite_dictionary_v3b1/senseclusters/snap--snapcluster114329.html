snap

verb
make a sharp, cracking sound
1.0
Pronunciation: / snæp / (AmE), / snæp / (BrE)
to make a sudden, sharp sound or movement, such as breaking, closing, or moving something quickly (including snapping fingers, jaws, or objects)

Examples

To snap fingers
He snapped his fingers to get the waiter’s attention.
She heard a twig snap under her foot as she walked through the forest.
The dry branch snapped in half with a loud crack.
He snapped the lid shut, making a loud noise.
She tried to snap her fingers to get the child's attention.
You can snap a twig in half with little effort.
Small branches snapped under our boots as we hiked through the dry forest.
The fireworks snapped overhead, echoing through the night.
The whip snapped against the air, startling the horses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: click - to make a short, sharp sound
  • synonym: crack - to make a sudden sharp or explosive sound
  • antonym: gently close - to close something softly, not forcefully or quickly
  • synonym: pop - to make a short, sharp, explosive sound
  • synonym: slam - to shut forcefully and loudly
