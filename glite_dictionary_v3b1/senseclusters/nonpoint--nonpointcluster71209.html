nonpoint

adjective
diffuse pollution source
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑnˌpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈnɒnˌpɔɪnt / (BrE)
describing pollution or a source that does not originate from a single, identifiable location

Examples

Nonpoint pollution source
Rainwater runoff is often considered nonpoint pollution because it comes from many scattered sources.
Controlling nonpoint pollution requires cooperation across entire communities and regions.
Unlike factories, farms are usually classified as nonpoint sources of water pollution.

Related Terms

  • antonym: point source - a single, identifiable source of pollution
