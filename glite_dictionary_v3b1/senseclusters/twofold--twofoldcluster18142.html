twofold

adjective
having two distinct aspects or parts
Pronunciation: / ˈtuːˌfoʊld /; / ˈtuˌfoʊld / (AmE), / ˈtuːˌfəʊld /; / ˈtuːfəʊld / (BrE)
having two distinct parts, aspects, or features

Examples

Twofold purpose
Our responsibilities are twofold: to protect the environment and to support our employees.
The benefits of this new policy are twofold, addressing both efficiency and employee satisfaction.
The company's decision had a twofold impact on the community: economic growth and improved infrastructure.
Her responsibilities at work are twofold: managing the team and coordinating with clients.
His interest in the project is twofold; he wants to learn new skills and help the community.
The environmental impact of the policy is twofold, affecting both wildlife and air quality.
