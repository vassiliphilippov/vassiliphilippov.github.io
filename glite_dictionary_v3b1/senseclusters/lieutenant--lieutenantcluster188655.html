lieutenant

noun
police officer of mid-rank
Pronunciation: / luːˈtɛnənt / (AmE), / lɛfˈtɛnənt / (BrE)
a police officer of middle or junior rank, typically ranking below captain

Examples

Police lieutenant
After ten years on the force, she was promoted to lieutenant.
The lieutenant led the investigation into the robbery downtown.
The lieutenant supervised a team of ten detectives in the department.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: captain - senior police officer, typically ranks above lieutenant
  • degree lower: sergeant - police officer typically ranking just below lieutenant
