think

verb
imagine or visualize (something)
Pronunciation: / θɪŋk / (AmE), / θɪŋk / (BrE)
to imagine, visualize, intend, or direct your mind toward something

Examples

To think of a plan
Think how different your life would be if you moved to another country.
Think of all the possibilities that could come from this new technology.
She likes to think about what her future might hold.
I only thought to help, not to interfere.
She didn't think to bring her umbrella when she left the house.
They hadn't thought to lock the door before leaving.
If you want to get fit, you have to think healthy and change your mindset.
She encourages her students to think big and pursue ambitious dreams.
The company told its designers to think outside the box when approaching the new project.

Related Terms

  • synonym: envision - to picture in the mind; to imagine the future
  • synonym: imagine - form a mental image or concept of something
  • synonym: intend - to have as a plan or purpose
  • synonym: mean - to have as a purpose, intention, or result in mind
