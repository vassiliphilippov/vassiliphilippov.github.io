taconite

noun
a low-grade iron ore rock
Pronunciation: / ˈtæk.əˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈtæk.ə.naɪt / (BrE)
a low-grade iron ore consisting of a siliceous rock containing layers of magnetite and hematite, mined for its iron content

Examples

Low-grade taconite ore
After the depletion of high-grade ores, companies began extracting iron from taconite.
The processing of taconite requires crushing and separating the iron minerals from the silica.
The steel mill relies on nearby taconite mines for its primary source of iron.
