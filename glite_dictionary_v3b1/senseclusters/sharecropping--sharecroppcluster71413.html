sharecropping

noun
tenant farming system
5.7
Pronunciation: /ˈʃeər.krɑː.pɪŋ/ /; / /ˈʃeɪr.krɑː.pɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈʃeə.krɒ.pɪŋ/ (BrE)
a system in which a farmer works land owned by someone else and gives a portion of the crops as rent

Examples

Sharecropping system
After the Civil War, sharecropping became widespread in the southern United States.
Many families relied on sharecropping as their main source of income during the early 20th century.
The disadvantages of sharecropping often kept farmers in a cycle of debt and poverty.

Related Terms

  • antonym: landownership - the state of owning land
  • synonym: tenant farming - a system where a farmer rents land to grow crops
