ramet

noun
independent plant part
Pronunciation: / ˈræmɛt / (AmE), / ˈræmɛt / (BrE)
an individual plant (or part of a plant) that is part of a larger clonal colony and can grow independently, genetically identical to others in the group

Examples

A ramet plant
Each ramet separated from the main plant can develop roots and grow on its own.
Scientists studied how a ramet responds to different soil conditions compared to the parent plant.
The gardener carefully transplanted a ramet to propagate the rare species.
Each ramet in the forest shares the same genetic material as the others.
Scientists studied how a single ramet responds to environmental changes within the colony.
The clonal colony expanded as new ramets sprouted from the original plant.
Each ramet in the forest shares the same genetic material as the others in the colony.
Scientists studied how a single ramet responds to environmental changes compared to the whole group.
When a ramet is separated from the colony, it can often survive and grow independently.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clone - a genetically identical organism produced from another
  • antonym: genet - a genetic individual in plants, often made up of several ramets
