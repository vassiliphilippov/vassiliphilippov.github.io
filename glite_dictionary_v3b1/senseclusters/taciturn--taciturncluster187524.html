taciturn

adjective
habitually quiet and uncommunicative
Pronunciation: / ˈtæsɪˌtɝn / (AmE), / ˈtæsɪˌtɜːn / (BrE)
(of a person) habitually quiet, reserved, and uncommunicative; tending not to speak much.

Examples

A taciturn person
Despite his taciturn nature, he was well-liked and respected by his colleagues.
During the meeting, her taciturn demeanor made it hard to gauge her opinions.
The new student was so taciturn that many thought he was shy or unfriendly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reticent - reluctant to speak; reserved
  • antonym: talkative - fond of talking, especially habitually
