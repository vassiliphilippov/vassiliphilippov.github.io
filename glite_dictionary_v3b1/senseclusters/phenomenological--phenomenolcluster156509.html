phenomenological

adjective
about personal experience
9.9
Pronunciation: / fəˌnɑːməˌnɑːˈlɑːdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / fəˌnɒmɪnəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the study or description of personal experience and consciousness as it is perceived

Examples

Phenomenological method
The psychologist adopted a phenomenological method to explore how people experience grief.
A phenomenological analysis focuses on how individuals perceive and interpret their own reality.
Her thesis provided a phenomenological account of childhood memories and their impact on identity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: experiential - relating to experience, especially as opposed to theoretical knowledge
  • antonym: objective - not influenced by personal feelings or opinions
