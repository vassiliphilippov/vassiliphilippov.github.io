stream

noun
small natural flowing watercourse
1.0 26/m
Pronunciation: / striÀêm / (AmE), / striÀêm / (BrE)
a small, narrow, natural body of running water flowing on or under the earth's surface

Examples

A mountain stream
A narrow stream of water ran through the valley, creating a peaceful atmosphere.
After the storm, the stream overflowed its banks and flooded the path.
The children spent the afternoon playing by the stream in the woods.

Synonyms

  • brook
    1.0 2.09/m
    brook is a small natural stream often found in countryside and as a tributary, while stream is the general small flowing watercourse.
  • creek
    1.4 15/m
    creek is a small waterway often wider and used in everyday speech locally, while stream names any narrow flowing natural watercourse.
  • run
    4.5 2.85/m
    run is a small fast-flowing stream in narrow channels, while stream covers any small or medium flowing natural watercourse.
  • streamlet
    7.3 0.03/m
    streamlet is a very tiny narrow flow like a rivulet in woods, while stream is a broader term for flowing water.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: brook - a small stream; a creek
  • synonym: creek - a small stream, often a tributary to a river
  • degree higher: river - a large natural stream of water flowing to the sea, a lake, or another river
