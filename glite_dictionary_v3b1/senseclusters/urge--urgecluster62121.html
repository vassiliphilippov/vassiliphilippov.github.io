urge

verb
strongly encourage or persuade
3.2 44/m
Pronunciation: / …úrd í /; / …ùd í / (AmE), / …úÀêd í / (BrE)
to strongly encourage, persuade, or advise someone to do something

Examples

To urge someone to act
His friends tried to urge him to apply for the job before the deadline.
Parents often urge their children to behave responsibly.
She urged us to remain calm during the emergency.
At the meeting, the manager urged everyone to share their ideas openly.
She urged her friend to apply for the job before the deadline.
The doctor urged him to quit smoking for his health.
She urged her friends to audition for the play, shouting encouragement from the seats.
Spectators urged the cyclists on as they climbed the steep hill.
The coach urged his team to keep fighting until the final whistle.

Synonyms

  • exhort
    7.7 2.27/m
    exhort is a strong, formal call to act in public or writing, while urge is a common word for encouraging someone.
  • encourage
    2.3 100/m
    encourage gives support and hope to try, while urge implies stronger pressure to act or immediate advice.
  • persuade
    2.7 27/m
    persuade wins belief or agreement with reasons, while urge pressures more by strong advice or emotional push.
  • push
    1.1 41/m
    push encourages with force or insistence to reach a goal, while urge can be less forceful or more polite advice.
  • press
    3.9 15/m
    press repeatedly asks or demands action, while urge often means a strong suggestion rather than a demand.
  • spur
    4.1 7.08/m
    spur inspires someone to act quickly or to improve, while urge stresses giving strong advice or insistence to do something.
  • urge on
    3.4 0.50/m
    urge on cheers someone to keep going or try harder, while urge is a general act of strongly encouraging action.
  • prompt
    3.6 23/m
    prompt gives a quick cue or reason to act or reply, while urge is stronger and wants the person to take clear action.
  • induce
    4.9 6.33/m
    induce convinces or causes someone to act often by influence or situation, while urge is direct advice or strong encouragement.
  • impel
    7.2 0.68/m
    impel drives someone from inside, often by strong feeling or duty, whereas urge is usually an external encouragement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: discourage - to try to prevent or dissuade someone from doing something
  • antonym: dissuade - to persuade someone not to take a particular course of action
  • synonym: encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope to someone
