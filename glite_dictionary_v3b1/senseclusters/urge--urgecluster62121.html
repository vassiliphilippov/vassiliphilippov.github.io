urge

verb
strongly encourage or persuade
Pronunciation: / ɜrdʒ /; / ɝdʒ / (AmE), / ɜːdʒ / (BrE)
to strongly encourage, persuade, or advise someone to do something

Examples

To urge someone to act
His friends tried to urge him to apply for the job before the deadline.
Parents often urge their children to behave responsibly.
She urged us to remain calm during the emergency.
At the meeting, the manager urged everyone to share their ideas openly.
She urged her friend to apply for the job before the deadline.
The doctor urged him to quit smoking for his health.
She urged her friends to audition for the play, shouting encouragement from the seats.
Spectators urged the cyclists on as they climbed the steep hill.
The coach urged his team to keep fighting until the final whistle.

Related Terms

  • antonym: discourage - to try to prevent or dissuade someone from doing something
  • antonym: dissuade - to persuade someone not to take a particular course of action
  • synonym: encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope to someone
