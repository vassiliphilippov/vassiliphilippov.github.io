pyriform lobe

noun
pear-shaped brain structure in the rhinencephalon
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈpɪr.ə.fɔːrm loʊb/ (AmE), /ˈpɪr.ɪ.fɔːm ləʊb/ (BrE)
a pear-shaped region on each side of the brain, part of the rhinencephalon, involved in olfactory processing

Examples

The pyriform lobe region
Damage to the pyriform lobe can result in an impaired sense of smell.
Neuroscientists are studying the function of the pyriform lobe in olfactory perception.
The pyriform lobe is located in the lower part of the cerebral hemisphere.
