spare

verb
save from something unpleasant or unnecessary
Pronunciation: / spɛr / (AmE), / speə / (BrE)
to save someone from having to experience or do something unpleasant or unnecessary

Examples

To spare someone trouble
He spared her having to work overtime by volunteering himself.
I will spare you the details of the argument.
She tried to spare him from embarrassment.
