Keplerian

adjective
of Kepler's laws
8.6
Pronunciation: / kɛˈplɪriən / (AmE), / kɛˈplɪəriən / (BrE)
relating to the laws or theories of planetary motion described by Johannes Kepler

Examples

Keplerian orbit
The planet's Keplerian orbit was calculated using precise astronomical data.
Scientists use Keplerian models to predict the movement of planets around the sun.
A Keplerian telescope is designed based on principles described by Kepler.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Newtonian - relating to the laws or theories of Isaac Newton
