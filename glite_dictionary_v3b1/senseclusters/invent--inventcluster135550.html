invent

verb
to create something new
3.1
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈvɛnt / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɛnt / (BrE)
to create or design something new that has not existed before, especially a device or process

Examples

To invent a machine
Thomas Edison managed to invent the electric light bulb after many experiments.
She wants to invent a new app that helps people learn languages.
Many people dream of becoming famous by being the first to invent something useful.
Thomas Edison managed to invent the electric light bulb after many experiments.
She wants to invent a new app that will help people learn languages more easily.
It takes creativity and persistence to invent something truly original.
Alexander Graham Bell invented the telephone, changing the way people communicate forever.
She wants to invent a new type of eco-friendly packaging for food products.
Many useful household items were invented in the twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • synonym: create - to bring something into existence
  • synonym: design - to plan and make something for a specific purpose
  • synonym: devise - to plan or invent by careful thought
  • antonym: discover - to find something that already exists
  • synonym: innovate - to introduce new ideas or methods
