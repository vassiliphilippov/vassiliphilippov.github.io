boilerplate

noun
standardized wording in documents
9.9 5.4
Pronunciation: /ˈbɔɪ.lər.pleɪt/ (AmE), /ˈbɔɪ.lə.pleɪt/ (BrE)
standardized language or passages used repeatedly in documents such as contracts, legal notices, or news articles.

Examples

Boilerplate text in contracts
Journalists often rely on boilerplate paragraphs when writing routine news stories.
Much of the document is just legal boilerplate and can be skimmed over.
The contract was filled with so much boilerplate that it was hard to find the important information.

Related Terms

  • synonym: template - a preset format used as a starting point for a particular application
