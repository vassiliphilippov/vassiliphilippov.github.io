liquid

adjective
(economics) easily convertible to cash
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪkwɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪd / (BrE)
(of assets) consisting of cash or easily convertible to cash, readily available for use.

Examples

Liquid assets
Banks must hold sufficient liquid reserves to ensure financial stability.
She prefers to keep her investments liquid in case of emergencies.
The company maintains a high level of liquid assets to meet short-term obligations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: illiquid - (of assets) not easily convertible to cash
