Spanish oak

noun
oak tree native to Texas and Oklahoma
Pronunciation: / ˈspænɪʃ oʊk / (AmE), / ˈspænɪʃ əʊk / (BrE)
A small deciduous oak tree with a trunk that branches near the base and spreading limbs, found in Texas and southern Oklahoma.

Examples

A Spanish oak tree
Many parks in southern Oklahoma feature beautiful groves of Spanish oak.
The bright red leaves of the Spanish oak make it a popular choice for landscaping in Texas.
Wildlife often use the spreading branches of the Spanish oak for shelter and nesting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oak - a large tree with lobed leaves and acorns
