Ionic order

noun
Greek architectural style with scroll capitals
8.3
Pronunciation: / aɪˈɑnɪk ˈɔrdər / (AmE), / aɪˈɒnɪk ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
A classical order of Greek architecture characterized by columns with spiral scroll-shaped ornaments (volutes) on the capital.

Examples

The Ionic order columns
Many ancient Greek temples were built in the Ionic order, giving them an elegant and graceful appearance.
The Parthenon combines elements of the Doric and Ionic order in its structure.
The columns in the Ionic order are easily recognized by their spiral volutes.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Corinthian order - the most ornate of the Greek architectural orders, characterized by elaborate capitals
  • synonym: Doric order - the oldest and simplest of the classical Greek architectural orders
