horsewhipping

noun
whipping with a horsewhip
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈhɔrsˌwɪpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɔːswɪpɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of whipping someone or something with a horsewhip as a punishment or reprimand

Examples

A horsewhipping punishment
In Victorian novels, a horsewhipping was often depicted as a harsh but acceptable form of discipline.
Some considered the horsewhipping of wrongdoers a cruel and outdated practice.
The judge condemned the prisoner to a severe horsewhipping for his crimes.
