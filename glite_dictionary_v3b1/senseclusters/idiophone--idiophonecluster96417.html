idiophone

noun
self-vibrating instrument
Pronunciation: / ˈɪdiəˌfoʊn / (AmE), / ˈɪdiəfəʊn / (BrE)
a musical instrument that produces sound by vibrating its own body, without strings or membranes

Examples

A bell idiophone
A bell is a classic example of an idiophone used in many musical traditions.
The orchestra featured several idiophones, including cymbals and maracas.
Unlike drums, an idiophone creates sound by the vibration of its own material.

Related Terms

  • antonym: membranophone - a musical instrument that produces sound by vibrating a stretched membrane
  • synonym: xylophone - a percussion instrument with wooden bars struck by mallets
