Scincidae

noun
family of lizards known as skinks
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪn.sɪˌdiː / (AmE), / ˈsɪn.sɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of lizards commonly known as skinks, characterized by their smooth, shiny scales and often elongated bodies.

Examples

Scincidae lizard family
Many lizards in the Scincidae family adapt to a variety of habitats, from deserts to rainforests.
Researchers recently discovered a new genus within the Scincidae from Southeast Asia.
The family Scincidae consists of more than 1,500 species of skinks found worldwide.
