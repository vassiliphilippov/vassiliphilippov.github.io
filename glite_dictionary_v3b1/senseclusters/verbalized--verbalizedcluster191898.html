verbalized

adjective
expressed in words
Pronunciation: / ˈvɝː.bə.laɪzd /; / ˈvɜrbəlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈvɜː.bə.laɪzd /; / ˈvɜːbəlaɪzd / (BrE)
expressed or communicated in words rather than in other forms.

Examples

Strongly verbalized support
At the meeting, his support for the plan was strongly verbalized.
Her fears were never verbalized, but they were clear in her actions.
Sometimes, feelings are better verbalized than left unsaid.
His verbalised anger helped others understand his frustration.
She appreciated when her concerns were verbalised clearly by the spokesperson.
The policy was first verbalised during the press conference.

Related Terms

  • synonym: articulated - clearly expressed or stated in words
  • antonym: unspoken - not communicated in words
  • synonym: verbalize - to express in words
