entrust

verb
give responsibility or care to someone
4.1 5.33/m
Pronunciation: / ÉªnËˆtrÊŒst / (AmE), / ÉªnËˆtrÊŒst / (BrE)
to give someone the responsibility to take care of something or someone

Examples

To entrust a task
She felt confident enough to entrust her children to the new babysitter.
The company will entrust you with confidential information after your training.
We decided to entrust the keys to our neighbor while we were on holiday.
Hospitals often entrust important medical equipment to senior staff only.
She decided to entrust her car keys to her best friend while she was on vacation.
The company entrusted the new manager with leading the expansion project.

Synonyms

  • commit
    3.3 6.69/m
    commit means to give someone formal charge or care of something, while entrust is a general, neutral way to hand over responsibility.
  • intrust
    7.7 0.00/m
    intrust is an older, rare form used to place trust or things in someone's care, while entrust is the common modern verb.
  • commend
    6.3 0.59/m
    commend is a formal word used to give someone into another's care or protection, while entrust is more neutral everyday language.
  • leave
    1.7 63/m
    leave means to put something or someone with another person when you go away, while entrust stresses giving responsibility.
