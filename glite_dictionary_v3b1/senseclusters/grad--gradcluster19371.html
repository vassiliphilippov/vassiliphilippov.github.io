grad

noun
unit of angle (1/100 of a right angle)
8.0
Pronunciation: / ɡræd / (AmE), / ɡræd / (BrE)
a unit of angle equal to one-hundredth of a right angle, or 0.9 degrees, used especially in surveying and navigation.

Examples

A grad angle
Mapping systems sometimes use grad instead of degrees for precision.
One grad is equivalent to 0.9 degrees in the metric system of angular measurement.
The horizontal angle was measured as 50 grads by the surveyor.
