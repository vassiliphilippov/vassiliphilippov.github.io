Aster macrophyllus

noun
species of woodland aster
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˌæstər ˌmækrəˈfɪləs / (AmE), / ˌæstə ˌmakrəˈfɪləs / (BrE)
A species of perennial woodland aster native to North America, known for its tufted clusters of pale flowers and broad leaves; widely naturalized in Europe.

Examples

Aster macrophyllus plant
Botanists often reference Aster macrophyllus when discussing native woodland flora.
Gardeners in Europe plant Aster macrophyllus for its attractive foliage and pale purple flowers.
The Aster macrophyllus blooms late in the summer, adding color to shaded gardens.
