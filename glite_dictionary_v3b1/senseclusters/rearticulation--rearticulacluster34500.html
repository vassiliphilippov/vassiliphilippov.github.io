rearticulation

noun
restoring a joint
8.5
Pronunciation: /riː.ɑːr.tɪ.kjʊ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /rɪ.ɑːr.tɪ.kjʊ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /riː.ɑː.tɪ.kjʊ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /rɪ.ɑː.tɪ.kjʊ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of rejoining or restoring a joint or connection between parts, especially by surgical or mechanical means

Examples

Surgical rearticulation
The surgeon performed a successful rearticulation of the patient's shoulder after the accident.
Mechanical engineers designed a system for the rearticulation of robotic limbs.
Physical therapy was needed after the rearticulation to help restore full movement.
