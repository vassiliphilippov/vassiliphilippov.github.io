breakdown

noun
chemical separation into component substances
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈbreɪkˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈbreɪk.daʊn /; / ˈbreɪkdaʊn / (BrE)
the process of something being separated or decomposed into simpler parts, especially by chemical or biological means

Examples

Chemical breakdown
Exposure to light accelerates the breakdown of certain chemicals in the solution.
Scientists studied the breakdown of proteins during digestion.
The breakdown of water produces hydrogen and oxygen gases.
After death, the breakdown of tissue is accelerated by bacteria and fungi.
Proper storage can slow the breakdown of fruits and vegetables.
The rapid breakdown of organic material enriches the soil with nutrients.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decomposition - the process of breaking down into simpler substances, especially by chemical means
  • synonym: putrefaction - the process of decay or rotting in a body or other organic matter
