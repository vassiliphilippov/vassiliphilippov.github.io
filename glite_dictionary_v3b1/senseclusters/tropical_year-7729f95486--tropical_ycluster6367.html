tropical year

noun
period of one revolution of Earth around the Sun
6.7 0.03/m

The period of time it takes Earth to complete one revolution around the Sun, measured from one vernal equinox to the next, approximately 365.24 days.

Examples

The tropical year length
Astronomers often refer to the tropical year when discussing the Earth's climate and seasons.
Leap years are used to keep our calendar in sync with the tropical year.
The tropical year is slightly shorter than the sidereal year due to the precession of the equinoxes.
