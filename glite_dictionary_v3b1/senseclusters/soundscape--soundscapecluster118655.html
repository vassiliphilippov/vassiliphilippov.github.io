soundscape

noun
audio environment
Pronunciation: / ˈsaʊndˌskeɪp / (AmE), / ˈsaʊndˌskeɪp / (BrE)
the combination of sounds that make up the overall atmosphere of a place or scene

Examples

A soundscape of nature
The film's soundscape included gentle rain and distant thunder to create a calming mood.
Artists often use a soundscape to transport listeners to a different place or time.
The natural soundscape of the forest was filled with birdsong and rustling leaves.
The soundscape of the city includes car horns, people talking, and distant music.
Birdsong and rustling leaves create a relaxing soundscape in the park each morning.
The film's soundscape was carefully designed to immerse the audience in the story.
The city soundscape was filled with car horns, footsteps, and distant music.
She recorded the natural soundscape of the rainforest for her research project.
At night, the soundscape of the countryside is peaceful, with only crickets and the occasional owl.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ambience - the character and atmosphere of a place
  • synonym: landscape - the visible features of an area of land
  • antonym: silence - the absence of sound
  • synonym: soundtrack - music or audio accompanying a film or performance
