endosymbiosis

noun
organism living inside another
9.5
Pronunciation: /en.doʊ.sɪm.ˈbaɪ.oʊ.sɪs/ (AmE), /en.dəʊ.sɪm.ˈbaɪ.əʊ.sɪs/ (BrE)
a biological process or relationship in which one organism lives inside the cells or body of another organism

Examples

Endosymbiosis theory
The theory of endosymbiosis explains how complex cells evolved from simpler ones.
Scientists believe that mitochondria originated through endosymbiosis millions of years ago.
Chloroplasts in plants are thought to have formed by endosymbiosis with photosynthetic bacteria.
