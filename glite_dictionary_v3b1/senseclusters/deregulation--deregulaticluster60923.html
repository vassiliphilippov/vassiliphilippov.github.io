deregulation

noun
removal of government regulations
6.0 4.40/m
Pronunciation: / ÀådiÀêÀår…õ…°j…ôÀàle…™ É…ôn / (AmE), / ÀådiÀêÀår…õ…°j äÀàle…™ É…ôn / (BrE)
The process of removing regulations or restrictions, especially those set by a government, from an industry, business, or activity.

Examples

Deregulation of markets
Many argue that deregulation of financial markets contributed to the economic crisis.
The deregulation of the airline industry led to greater competition and lower fares.
The energy sector has undergone significant deregulation over the past few decades.

Synonyms

  • liberalization
    6.7 4.78/m
