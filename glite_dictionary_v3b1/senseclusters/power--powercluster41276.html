power

noun
electrical energy supply
2.3
Pronunciation: / ˈpaʊ.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈpaʊ.ə / (BrE)
energy that is used to make machines, lights, or devices work; or the rate at which work is done (in physics)

Examples

Electric power
The house uses solar panels to generate its own power.
The storm caused the power to go out in several neighborhoods.
We need more power to run all the equipment in the lab.
In physics, power is measured as the amount of work done per unit time.
The electric motor has a power output of 200 watts.
The machine was upgraded to increase its power and efficiency.

Related Terms

  • synonym: electricity - energy resulting from the movement of charged particles
  • synonym: energy - capacity to do work; a related concept in physics
