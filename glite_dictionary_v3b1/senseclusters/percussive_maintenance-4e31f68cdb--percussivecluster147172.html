percussive maintenance

noun
fixing a device by hitting it
Pronunciation: / pərˈkʌsɪv meɪnˈteɪnəns / (AmE), / pəˈkʌsɪv meɪnˈteɪnəns / (BrE)
the act of attempting to fix a mechanical or electronic device by hitting or tapping it, often humorously or when other fixes have failed

Examples

Percussive maintenance technique
Engineers sometimes resort to percussive maintenance when a stubborn machine refuses to start.
She gave the printer a dose of percussive maintenance, and surprisingly, it began working again.
The old television finally worked after a bit of percussive maintenance from my dad.
