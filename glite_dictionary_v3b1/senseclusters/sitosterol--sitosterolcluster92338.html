sitosterol

noun
plant cholesterol compound
7.3
Pronunciation: / saɪˈtɑstərɒl / (AmE), / sɪˈtɒstərɒl / (BrE)
a plant-derived compound similar to cholesterol, found in many vegetables, nuts, and seeds, sometimes used as a dietary supplement to help lower cholesterol

Examples

sitosterol in plants
Many nuts and seeds are rich in sitosterol, which may help support heart health.
Researchers are studying how sitosterol can lower cholesterol levels in the body.
Vegetable oils often contain significant amounts of sitosterol.
Many health foods are fortified with sitosterol to support heart health.
Her doctor recommended sitosterol as part of a plan to lower her cholesterol levels.
Research suggests that sitosterol may help reduce the absorption of cholesterol from food.
