snail

verb
to move very slowly
6.7
Pronunciation: / sneɪl / (AmE), / sneɪl / (BrE)
to move very slowly, in a way that resembles the pace of a snail

Examples

To snail up hills
The old bus was snailing up the steep hill, barely making any progress.
We were snailing through the museum because my little brother wanted to look at every exhibit.
After the injury, he could only snail across the room, taking one slow step at a time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crawl - to move slowly on hands and knees or at a very slow pace
  • synonym: creep - to move slowly and carefully, often to avoid being noticed
  • antonym: rush - to move with great speed
