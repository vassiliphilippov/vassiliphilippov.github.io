round trip

noun
network request–response time
Pronunciation: / raʊnd trɪp / (AmE), / raʊnd trɪp / (BrE)
(computing, networking) the complete journey of a network request from a host to a remote system and back, often measured as the time taken (also called round-trip time or RTT).

Examples

Round trip time
During the load test, the average round trip time to the database was under fifty milliseconds.
Network engineers measured the round trips for each packet to diagnose intermittent connectivity problems.
When she pinged the remote host, the round trip varied between twenty and eighty milliseconds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: latency - delay before data begins to transfer, often discussed alongside round-trip measurements
  • synonym: round-trip time (RTT) - the time it takes for a signal or packet to travel to a destination and return to the origin
