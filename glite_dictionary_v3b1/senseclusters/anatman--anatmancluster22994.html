anatman

noun
Buddhist no-self doctrine
Pronunciation: / əˈnɑːtmən / (AmE), / əˈnɑːtmən / (BrE)
the Buddhist doctrine that there is no permanent, unchanging self or soul in living beings

Examples

The anatman doctrine
The teaching of anatman is central to many Buddhist philosophies.
Understanding anatman can help people let go of attachment to their ego.
Many students find the idea of anatman challenging when first studying Buddhism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anatta - the Pali term for the doctrine of no-self in Buddhism
  • antonym: atman - the concept of a permanent self or soul in Hindu philosophy
