swamp plant

noun
plant growing in wet, swampy areas
Pronunciation: / swɑmp plænt / (AmE), / swɒmp plɑːnt / (BrE)
A plant that grows in soft, wet, swampy ground, typically semiaquatic in nature.

Examples

A swamp plant species
Botanists studied the unique adaptations of the swamp plant to survive in waterlogged soils.
Cattails are a common type of swamp plant found in wetlands across the country.
The swamp plant attracted many birds that nested in its thick foliage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: marsh plant - a plant that grows in marshes or wetlands
