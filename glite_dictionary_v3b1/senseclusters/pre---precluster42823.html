a prefix meaning 'before' in time, order, or position

Examples

The pre- prefix
The pre- in 'prehistoric' means before recorded history.
In 'prewar', the pre- prefix shows something happened before the war.
Doctors use the term 'preoperative' to describe the period pre- surgery.
You should pre-heat the oven before baking the cake.
The company offered a pre-order option for the new phone.
Students attended a pre-exam workshop to prepare for the test.
