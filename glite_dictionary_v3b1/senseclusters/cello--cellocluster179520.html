cello

noun
large stringed instrument played upright
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɛloʊ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛləʊ / (BrE)
A large stringed musical instrument of the violin family, held upright on the floor between the knees of the seated player and played with a bow.

Examples

To play the cello
She practiced the cello every day to prepare for the orchestra concert.
The deep, rich sound of the cello filled the concert hall.
Yo-Yo Ma is renowned worldwide for his exceptional cello performances.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: double bass - the largest and lowest-pitched bowed string instrument
  • degree lower: viola - a string instrument slightly larger than a violin
  • synonym: violin - a bowed string instrument, smaller than cello
