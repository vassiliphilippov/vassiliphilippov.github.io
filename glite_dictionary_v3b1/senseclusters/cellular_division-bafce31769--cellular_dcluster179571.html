cellular division

noun
process of a cell splitting to form new cells
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛljələr dɪˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / ˈsɛljʊlə dɪˈvɪʒən / (BrE)
the process in which a cell splits to form two or more daughter cells, important in reproduction and growth

Examples

Cellular division process
During cellular division, the genetic material is equally distributed to the daughter cells.
Errors in cellular division can lead to genetic mutations or diseases.
Scientists study cellular division to understand how organisms grow and heal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: meiosis - cell division producing gametes with half the original chromosome number
  • synonym: mitosis - process of cell division producing identical cells
