at daggers drawn

adverb
in open hostility or conflict
9.2
Pronunciation: / ət ˈdæɡɚz drɔn / (AmE), / ət ˈdæɡəz drɔːn / (BrE)
in a state of open hostility or sharp conflict with someone

Examples

At daggers drawn with rivals
During the negotiations, the unions and management were at daggers drawn over pay increases.
Ever since the merger, the departments have been at daggers drawn about their roles.
The two family members have been at daggers drawn since the argument last year.
