X-ray photography

noun
photography using X-rays for imaging
1.0 3.2
Pronunciation: /ˈeks.reɪ fə.ˈtɑː.grə.fiː/ (AmE), /ˈeks.reɪ fə.ˈtɒ.grə.fiː/ (BrE)
The process of taking photographs using X-rays, especially to produce images of the inside of the body for medical examination.

Examples

X-ray photography of bones
X-ray photography played a crucial role in diagnosing the patient's broken bone.
Advances in X-ray photography have greatly improved the detection of lung diseases.
The doctor explained how X-ray photography helps to visualize internal organs without surgery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: X-ray - a form of electromagnetic radiation used for imaging
  • synonym: radiography - imaging technique using X-rays to view internal structures
