anthropomorphic

adjective
having human characteristics (applied to non-humans)
Pronunciation: / ˌænθrəpəˈmɔːrfɪk / (AmE), / ˌænθrəpəˈmɔːfɪk / (BrE)
(of animals, objects, or deities) described or thought of as having human attributes, form, or characteristics.

Examples

Anthropomorphic animals
Ancient myths often describe gods in anthropomorphic terms, giving them human emotions and desires.
Some cartoons depict anthropomorphic objects with faces and personalities.
The movie features anthropomorphic animals who talk and wear clothing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: personification - attribution of human qualities to non-human things
