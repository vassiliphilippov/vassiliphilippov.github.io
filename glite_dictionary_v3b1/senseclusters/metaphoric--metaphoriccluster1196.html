metaphoric

adjective
expressing one thing in terms of another
5.9 5.4
Pronunciation: /met.ə.ˈfɔːr.ɪk/ (AmE), /met.ə.ˈfɔːr.ɪk/ (BrE)
(of language or a phrase) expressing one thing in terms normally denoting another; metaphorical.

Examples

Metaphoric language
Her explanation was so metaphoric that some listeners were confused by her meaning.
That phrase was not meant literally but was intended to be metaphoric.
The poet often uses metaphoric language to convey deeper meanings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: literal - using words in their usual or most basic sense
  • synonym: metaphorical - (of language) relating to or using metaphors
