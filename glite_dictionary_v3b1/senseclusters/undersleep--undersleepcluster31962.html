undersleep

noun
insufficient sleep
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndərsliːp / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌsliːp / (BrE)
an insufficient amount or period of sleep, especially less than what is needed for good health

Examples

Chronic undersleep
Prolonged undersleep can negatively affect your mood and memory.
Doctors warn that undersleep may weaken the immune system over time.
Many students struggle with undersleep during exam periods due to late-night studying.

Related Terms

  • antonym: oversleep - to sleep for longer than intended or needed
  • synonym: sleep deprivation - the condition of not having enough sleep
