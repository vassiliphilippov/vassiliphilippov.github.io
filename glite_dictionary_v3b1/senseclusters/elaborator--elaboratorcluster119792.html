elaborator

noun
one who adds detail
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlæbəˌreɪtər / (AmE), / ɪˈlæbəˌreɪtə / (BrE)
a person or thing that adds detail, develops, or clarifies something to make it clearer or more complete

Examples

Group elaborator
The elaborator in the group discussion helped clarify complex ideas for everyone.
As an elaborator, she always provided extra details to support her arguments.
A good elaborator can make technical reports easier to understand.
As the team's main elaborator, he always clarified complex ideas for everyone.
The elaborator in the group made sure every detail was discussed thoroughly.
During the presentation, the elaborator added useful background information to support the main points.

Related Terms

  • synonym: expounder - a person who explains or interprets something in detail
  • antonym: summarizer - a person who gives a brief statement of the main points
