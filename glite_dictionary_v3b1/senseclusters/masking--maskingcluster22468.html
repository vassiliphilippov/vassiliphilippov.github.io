masking

noun
blocking of one sensation by another
Pronunciation: / ˈmæskɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmɑːskɪŋ / (BrE)
the process by which the perception of one sensation is prevented or diminished by the presence of another sensation, typically used in psychology and acoustics

Examples

Auditory masking
Research in auditory masking helps improve hearing aid technology.
The phenomenon of masking makes it difficult to hear faint sounds in a noisy environment.
Visual masking can occur when one image disrupts the perception of another.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interference - the process in which two or more sensory inputs overlap or affect each other
