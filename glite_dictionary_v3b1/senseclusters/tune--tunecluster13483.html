tune

verb
adjust for better functioning
3.2 9/m
Pronunciation: / tun / (AmE), / tjuÀên / (BrE)
to adjust (a machine, system, or device) so that it works more efficiently or performs as desired

Examples

To tune a machine
Technicians regularly tune the broadcasting equipment to ensure clear transmission.
The mechanic will tune your car to improve its fuel efficiency.
You may need to tune the air conditioner for optimal performance in hot weather.

Synonyms

    3.9 0.08/m

  • adjust
    2.7 30/m
    adjust means to change or position something to be correct or suitable, while tune focuses on improving machine performance.
  • fine-tune
    4.2 3/m
    fine-tune means to make very small, exact changes to improve performance, while tune is a general adjustment for efficiency.
  • tweak
    3.8 6/m
    tweak means to make small experimental changes to improve a system quickly, while tune implies a fuller adjustment for smooth operation.

    6.5 0.5/m
