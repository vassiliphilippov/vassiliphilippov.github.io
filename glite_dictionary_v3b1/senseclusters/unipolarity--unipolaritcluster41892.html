unipolarity

noun
depressive episodes only
Pronunciation: / ˌjuːnɪˈpoʊlərəti / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪˈpəʊlærɪti / (BrE)
the clinical condition of experiencing only depressive episodes, without alternating between depression and mania

Examples

Depressive unipolarity
The psychiatrist explained that unipolarity means the patient only experiences depressive episodes.
Researchers are studying the genetic factors that contribute to unipolarity in mood disorders.
Effective treatment for unipolarity often involves a combination of medication and therapy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bipolarity - the condition of experiencing both depressive and manic episodes
  • synonym: depression - a mental health disorder marked by persistent sadness and loss of interest
