Pterocarpus santalinus

noun
tree yielding red dyewood and fine timber
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛrəˈkɑrpəs sænˈteɪlɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌtɛrəˈkɑːpəs sænˈtalɪnəs / (BrE)
a tree native to India and Southeast Asia that produces a hard, fragrant timber prized for fine woodworking and a dark red heartwood used as dye

Examples

Pterocarpus santalinus tree
Due to over-harvesting, Pterocarpus santalinus is now considered a vulnerable species.
Furniture made from Pterocarpus santalinus is valued for its rich color and durability.
The dye extracted from Pterocarpus santalinus heartwood was once widely used in textiles.
