pull

verb
steer a vehicle or animal in a direction
1.6 2.7
Pronunciation: /pʊl/ (AmE), /pʊl/ /; / /pəl/ (BrE)
to steer a vehicle, animal, or similar thing into a particular direction, especially to the side or to a stop; (of a vehicle or object) to move or veer in a particular direction, often unintentionally

Examples

To pull over
He tried to pull his horse to the right at the intersection.
If you pull too sharply, you might miss the driveway.
The police officer signaled her to pull the car over to the curb.
If your car pulls when braking, you may have a problem with the alignment.
My bike started to pull to one side after I hit the curb.
The old truck tends to pull to the left when you let go of the wheel.
