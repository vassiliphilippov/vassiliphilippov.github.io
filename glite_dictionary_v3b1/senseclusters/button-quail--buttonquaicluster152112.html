button-quail

noun
small terrestrial bird resembling a quail
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌtən kweɪl / (AmE), / ˈbʌtən kweɪl / (BrE)
A small, quail-like terrestrial bird found in southern Eurasia and northern Africa, lacking a hind toe and inhabiting grassy plains.

Examples

A button-quail bird
Researchers studied the breeding habits of the button-quail in the wild.
The button-quail is difficult to spot due to its camouflaged plumage.
Unlike true quails, the button-quail lacks a hind toe.
