ignitor

noun
a substance that starts a fire
4.5
Pronunciation: / ɪɡˈnaɪtər / (AmE), / ɪɡˈnaɪtə / (BrE)
A substance that is used to start or kindle a fire; a material that initiates combustion.

Examples

Chemical ignitor
Always handle the ignitor carefully, as it is highly flammable.
The technician added a small amount of ignitor to the mixture to ensure it would burn evenly.
This type of ignitor is commonly used in model rocket engines.
