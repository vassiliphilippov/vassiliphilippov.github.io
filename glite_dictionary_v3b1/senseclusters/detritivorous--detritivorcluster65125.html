detritivorous

adjective
feeding on dead matter
9.9
Pronunciation: / dɪˌtraɪtəˈvɔrəs / (AmE), / dɪˌtraɪtɪˈvɔːrəs / (BrE)
feeding on dead organic matter, especially as part of an ecological process

Examples

Detritivorous soil animals
Many soil organisms are detritivorous, helping to recycle nutrients in the ecosystem.
The detritivorous habits of earthworms improve soil fertility by breaking down organic debris.
Some aquatic insects are detritivorous, feeding on decaying plant material in streams.

Related Terms

  • antonym: carnivorous - feeding on other animals
  • antonym: herbivorous - feeding on plants
  • synonym: saprophagous - feeding on decaying organic matter
