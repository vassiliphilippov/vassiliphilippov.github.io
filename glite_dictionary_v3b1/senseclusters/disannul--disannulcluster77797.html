disannul

verb
to reverse annulment
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsəˈnʌl / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈnʌl / (BrE)
to officially cancel or make something legally invalid

Examples

To disannul a ruling
The court decided to disannul the previous ruling, making the contract valid again.
After reviewing new evidence, the committee voted to disannul the annulment of the membership.
The bishop chose to disannul the marriage annulment, allowing the couple to be recognized by the church.
The judge decided to disannul the contract due to a legal error.
If the evidence is found to be false, the court may disannul the verdict.
The agreement was disannulled after both parties failed to meet the terms.

Related Terms

  • antonym: annul - to declare something invalid or void
  • antonym: confirm - to make something definite or officially valid
  • synonym: invalidate - to make something not legally acceptable
  • synonym: reinstate - to restore something to its previous state or position
