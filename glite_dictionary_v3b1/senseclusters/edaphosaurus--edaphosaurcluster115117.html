edaphosaurus

noun
extinct reptile with a dorsal sail
9.9
Pronunciation: /e.də.foʊ.ˈsɔːr.əs/ /; / /e.də.fə.ˈsɔːr.əs/ (AmE), /e.də.fə.ˈsɔː.rəs/ /; / /e.də.fəʊ.ˈsɔː.rəs/ (BrE)
an extinct heavy-bodied reptile of the late Paleozoic era, characterized by a distinctive dorsal sail or crest

Examples

An edaphosaurus fossil
Paleontologists discovered the fossil remains of an edaphosaurus in the Texas Red Beds.
The edaphosaurus is notable for the large sail on its back, thought to be used for thermoregulation.
Unlike Dimetrodon, edaphosaurus is more distantly related to modern mammals.
