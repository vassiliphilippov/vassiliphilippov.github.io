pontifical

adjective
relating to a bishop or bishops
6.9
Pronunciation: / pɑnˈtɪfɪkəl / (AmE), / pɒnˈtɪfɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to a bishop, bishops, or the pope; connected with church authority or ceremonies

Examples

Pontifical ceremony
He wore the ornate vestments reserved for pontifical ceremonies.
The pontifical authority was central to organizing the diocese.
The cathedral still preserves its pontifical traditions dating back centuries.
He questioned whether the pontifical authority extended to secular matters.
The cardinals awaited a pontifical decision before taking further action.
The new law was issued as a pontifical decree from the Vatican.
