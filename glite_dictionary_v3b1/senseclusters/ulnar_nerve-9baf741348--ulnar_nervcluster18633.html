ulnar nerve

noun
main nerve of the inner arm and hand
4.8 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆÊŒlnÉš nÉv / (AmE), / ËˆÊŒlnÉ™ nÉœËv / (BrE)
a nerve that runs along the inner side of the arm, passing close to the elbow and supplying muscles and skin of the medial side of the hand

Examples

Ulnar nerve injury
Injury to the ulnar nerve may result in weakness of the hand muscles.
She experienced tingling in her ring and little fingers due to an irritated ulnar nerve.
The ulnar nerve can sometimes be compressed at the elbow, causing numbness in the fingers.

Synonyms
