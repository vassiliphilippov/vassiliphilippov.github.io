pipul

noun
sacred fig tree of India
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈpiːpəl / (AmE), / ˈpiːpəl / (BrE)
A large fig tree (Ficus religiosa) native to India and Southeast Asia, sacred in Buddhism and noted for its longevity and size; unlike the banyan, it lacks prop roots.

Examples

The pipul tree
Botanists admire the pipul for its remarkable height and the shade it provides during India’s hot summers.
Many pilgrims tie ribbons around the trunk of the pipul as a sign of respect for its religious significance.
The ancient temple was built beneath a sprawling pipul tree, considered holy by local Buddhists.
