fireproof

verb
make resistant to fire
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪrˌpruf /; / ˈfaɪərˌpruːf / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌpruːf / (BrE)
to make something resistant to fire by treating or constructing it with special materials

Examples

To fireproof curtains
All the curtains in the theater were fireproofed as a safety precaution.
Modern construction methods often fireproof buildings to comply with safety regulations.
They hired a company to fireproof the old warehouse before storing flammable materials.
The company is fireproofing the new office building to meet safety standards.
Workers spent several days fireproofing the theater's curtains and stage materials.
It is important to fireproof your home if you live in an area prone to wildfires.

  • synonym: insulate - to protect something by surrounding it with material that reduces the passage of heat or sound
