bit

noun
short act within a longer show
4.6 2.12/m
Pronunciation: / bÉªt / (AmE), / bÉªt / (BrE)
a short performance or piece of entertainment that is part of a longer show, such as a skit or routine

Examples

Comedy bit
She performed a clever magic bit as part of the gala.
The comedian's opening bit had the audience laughing within seconds.
They rehearsed the dance bit before the main act began.

Synonyms

  • skit
    3.5 2.15/m
    a skit is a short, often funny performance by a small group, while a bit is a short piece within a larger show.
  • routine
    3.2 3.92/m
    a routine is a prepared sequence meant to entertain on stage, while a bit is a brief portion inside a longer performance.
  • turn
    5.4 2.06/m

  • act
    1.8 13/m

  • number
    6.5 6.33/m
    a number is often a musical or dance piece in a show, while a bit usually refers to a short comic or small sketch.
