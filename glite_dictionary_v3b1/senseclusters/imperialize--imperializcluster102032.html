imperialize

verb
to extend imperial influence
8.8
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɪriəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɪəriəlaɪz / (BrE)
to extend the influence or control of an empire or powerful nation over others, or to make something part of an empire

Examples

To imperialize a country
Some critics argue that global media companies imperialize local cultures by promoting foreign values.
The government feared that foreign investment would imperialize its key industries over time.
Activists protested against policies that seemed to imperialize education with outside standards.
The powerful nation sought to imperialize smaller countries in the region.
Throughout history, many empires have tried to imperialize distant lands for resources.
Some leaders resisted efforts to imperialize their homeland and fought for independence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: colonize - to establish control over another country or area and settle it with people from the controlling country
  • antonym: liberate - to set free from control or oppression
