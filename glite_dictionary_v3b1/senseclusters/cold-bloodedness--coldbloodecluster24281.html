cold-bloodedness

noun
calm self-control
Pronunciation: / ˌkoʊldˈblʌdɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˌkəʊldˈblʌdɪdnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being calm and not easily excited or upset, showing deliberate self-control in stressful situations

Examples

Cold-bloodedness under pressure
Her cold-bloodedness during the emergency helped everyone stay calm and focused.
The chess player's cold-bloodedness made him a difficult opponent in high-pressure matches.
Despite the chaos around him, his cold-bloodedness never wavered.

Related Terms

  • synonym: composure - the state of being calm and in control of oneself
  • antonym: nervousness - the state of being anxious or easily upset
