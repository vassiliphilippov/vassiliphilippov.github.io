fermentative

adjective
causing unrest or change
9.1
Pronunciation: / fərˈmɛntətɪv / (AmE), / fəˈmɛntətɪv / (BrE)
causing or promoting social or political unrest, agitation, or rapid change, especially in a figurative sense

Examples

Fermentative speech
The activist's fermentative speeches inspired many people to demand reform.
A fermentative atmosphere swept through the city after the controversial law was announced.
The novel's fermentative ideas challenged traditional beliefs and sparked debate.

Related Terms

  • antonym: stabilizing - making something steady or less likely to change
