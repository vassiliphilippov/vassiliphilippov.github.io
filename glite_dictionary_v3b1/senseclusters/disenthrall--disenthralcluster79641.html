disenthrall

verb
to set free
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsɪnˈθrɔl / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈθrɔːl / (BrE)
to set someone free from bondage, control, or illusion

Examples

To disenthrall the mind
The leader's words helped disenthrall the people from years of oppressive thinking.
Education can disenthrall individuals from ignorance and prejudice.
The novel aims to disenthrall readers from traditional views on society.

Related Terms

  • antonym: enslave - to make someone a slave or bring under control
  • synonym: liberate - to set someone free from a situation or imprisonment
