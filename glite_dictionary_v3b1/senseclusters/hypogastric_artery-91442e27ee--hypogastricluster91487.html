hypogastric artery

noun
artery supplying the pelvis and gluteal area
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪ.poʊˈɡæs.trɪk ˈɑːr.t̬ɚ.i / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ.pəʊˈɡæs.trɪk ˈɑː.tər.i / (BrE)
the internal branch of the common iliac artery that supplies blood to the pelvic and gluteal regions

Examples

The hypogastric artery branch
Damage to the hypogastric artery can lead to complications in pelvic surgeries.
The hypogastric artery divides into several branches that supply the pelvic organs.
The surgeon carefully isolated the hypogastric artery before proceeding with the operation.
