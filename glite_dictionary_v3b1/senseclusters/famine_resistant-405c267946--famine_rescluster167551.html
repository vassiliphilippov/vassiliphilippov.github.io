famine resistant

adjective
able to withstand famine
6.9 5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈfæmɪn rɪˈzɪstənt / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪn rɪˈzɪstənt / (BrE)
(of a plant, crop, or organism) able to withstand or survive conditions of food scarcity or famine.

Examples

Famine resistant crops
Cassava is considered highly famine resistant due to its ability to survive in harsh climates.
Developing famine resistant crops is crucial for food security in drought-prone regions.
This new variety of wheat is famine resistant and yields well in poor soil.
