myelocyte

noun
immature white blood cell in bone marrow
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪəloʊˌsaɪt / (AmE), / ˈmaɪələʊsaɪt / (BrE)
an immature white blood cell normally found in bone marrow, which develops into a mature granulocyte

Examples

A myelocyte cell
A high count of myelocytes in the blood may indicate a bone marrow disorder.
Doctors examined the bone marrow and noted the presence of numerous myelocytes.
The transition from myelocyte to mature neutrophil is crucial in immune response.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: leukocyte - a general term for a white blood cell
