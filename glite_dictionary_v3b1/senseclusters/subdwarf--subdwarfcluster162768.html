subdwarf

noun
dim small star
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌdwɔrf / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌdwɑːf / (BrE)
a star that is smaller and less luminous than a typical dwarf star

Examples

A faint subdwarf
The astronomer identified a faint subdwarf at the edge of the galaxy.
Unlike our Sun, a subdwarf emits much less light and heat.
Researchers are studying how subdwarfs form in different regions of space.
