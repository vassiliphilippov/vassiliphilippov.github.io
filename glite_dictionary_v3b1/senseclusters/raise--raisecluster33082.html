Synonyms

  • elevate
    3.9 11/m
    elevate is a more formal verb for moving something to a higher level, while raise is common everyday speech.
  • lift
    1.0 50/m
    lift is a direct physical action often done with hands or machines, while raise is more general and can be used figuratively.
  • lift up
    1.1 4.06/m
    lift up is a casual phrasal verb for moving something higher by lifting, while raise is neutral and appears in many contexts.
  • push up
    2.1 1.28/m
    push up means move something higher by pushing from below, while raise does not focus on the exact method used.
  • heave up
    4.5 0.27/m
    heave up means lift something with great effort or force, while raise can be simple or require no heavy strain.
  • heft up
    6.1 0.05/m
    heft up means lift briefly or test an object's weight with effort, while raise means simply move something to a higher place.
  • boost
    2.5 0.98/m
    boost often means help or increase something, sometimes more figurative, while raise focuses on physically moving or making something stand out.
