underpaid

verb
to receive too little pay
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈpeɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈpeɪd / (BrE)
received less money for work than is deserved or required by agreement.

Examples

Underpaid staff
Many teachers feel they are underpaid for the important work they do.
After reviewing her contract, she realized she had been underpaid for several months.
The workers protested because they were consistently underpaid compared to industry standards.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overpaid - received more money for work than is deserved or required
  • synonym: underpay - to pay someone less than they deserve
