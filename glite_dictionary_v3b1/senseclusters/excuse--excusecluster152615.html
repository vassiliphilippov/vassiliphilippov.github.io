excuse

verb
to explain or justify a fault or mistake
2.9 2.2
Pronunciation: /ɪk.ˈskjuːz/ /; / /ɪk.ˈskjuːs/ (AmE), /ɪk.ˈskjuːz/ /; / /ɪk.ˈskjuːs/ /; / /ek.ˈskjuːz/ /; / /ek.ˈskjuːs/ (BrE)
to try to justify or explain (a fault, mistake, or failure) so as to make it seem less serious or more acceptable; to give reasons to defend one's actions or behavior

Examples

To excuse bad behavior
He tried to excuse his lateness by blaming the traffic.
No amount of pressure can excuse cheating on the exam.
She refused to excuse her brother's rude behavior to their parents.

Related Terms

  • antonym: condemn - to express strong disapproval of; to declare to be wrong
  • synonym: justify - to show or prove to be right or reasonable
  • synonym: rationalize - to attempt to explain or justify with logical reasons, even if not true
