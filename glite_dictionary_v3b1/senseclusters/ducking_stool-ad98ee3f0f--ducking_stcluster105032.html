ducking stool

noun
punishment chair for dunking offenders
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌkɪŋ stuːl / (AmE), / ˈdʌkɪŋ stuːl / (BrE)
a historical instrument of punishment consisting of a chair attached to a lever, used to plunge offenders into water as a form of public humiliation.

Examples

The ducking stool punishment
Fear of the ducking stool kept many from openly criticizing local authorities.
In colonial America, women accused of gossiping were sometimes punished with the ducking stool.
The ducking stool was displayed in the museum as a reminder of past judicial practices.
