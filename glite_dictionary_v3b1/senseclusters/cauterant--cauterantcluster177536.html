cauterant

noun
substance or tool to destroy tissue (medically)
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔtɚənt / (AmE), / ˈkɔːtərənt / (BrE)
a substance or instrument used to destroy tissue, especially to stop bleeding or remove growths, by burning, freezing, or chemical action

Examples

A cauterant substance
Liquid nitrogen is a commonly used cauterant for removing skin lesions.
The doctor applied a cauterant to the wound to prevent further bleeding.
Using a chemical cauterant, the surgeon sealed the blood vessels quickly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cautery - the process of burning tissue for medical reasons
