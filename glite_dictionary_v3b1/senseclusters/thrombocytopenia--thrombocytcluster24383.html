thrombocytopenia

noun
low platelet blood disorder
6.7 9.4
Pronunciation: /θrɑːm.boʊ.saɪ.tə.ˈpiː.n.jə/ /; / /θrɑːm.boʊ.saɪ.toʊ.ˈpiː.n.jə/ (AmE), /θrɒm.bə.saɪ.tə.ˈpiː.n.jə/ (BrE)
a medical condition in which there is an abnormally low number of platelets in the blood, increasing the risk of bleeding

Examples

Low thrombocytopenia count
Chemotherapy can sometimes cause thrombocytopenia as a side effect.
People with thrombocytopenia may experience easy bruising and frequent nosebleeds.
The patient was diagnosed with thrombocytopenia after routine blood tests revealed low platelet counts.
