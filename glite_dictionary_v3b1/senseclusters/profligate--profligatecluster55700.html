profligate

adjective
recklessly wasteful

Examples

Profligate spending habits
His profligate lifestyle eventually caught up with him, leaving him in debt.
She was criticized for her profligate use of electricity during the energy crisis.
The company's profligate spending led to its eventual bankruptcy.
He squandered his fortune in profligate spending and endless parties.
The profligate lifestyle of the wealthy elite was condemned by journalists.
Their profligate disregard for the law shocked their more traditional neighbors.

Synonyms

    8.4 0.88/m
    prodigal spends money or resources freely and without care, while profligate suggests reckless excess and lack of moral restraint.
  • extravagant
    7.1 1.56/m
    extravagant describes spending or using far more than needed, while profligate implies reckless excess and moral carelessness.
  • spendthrift
    8.4 0.04/m
    spendthrift is a person who spends money recklessly, while profligate often describes extreme waste and lack of moral restraint.
  • wasteful
    3.0 3.71/m
    wasteful uses more of something than needed and ignores efficiency, while profligate implies reckless, often immoral, excess.
  • thriftless
    8.5 0.01/m
    thriftless is careless with money or resources, while profligate adds active extravagance and a strong sense of moral fault.
  • unthrifty
    8.6 0.00/m
    unthrifty is not careful with money or resources and sounds plain, while profligate suggests dramatic waste and moral blame.
