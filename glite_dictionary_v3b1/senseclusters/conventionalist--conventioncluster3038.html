conventionalist

adjective
favoring conventions
7.7
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈven.ʃən.əl.ɪst/ /; / /kən.ˈven.ʃə.nəl.ɪst/ (AmE), /kən.ˈven.ʃən.əl.ɪst/ /; / /kən.ˈven.ʃə.nəl.ɪst/ (BrE)
supporting or relating to the idea that rules or principles are based on social conventions rather than inherent necessity.

Examples

Conventionalist view of rules
Her conventionalist approach to education emphasized established teaching methods over experimental ones.
The committee adopted a conventionalist stance, preferring to follow long-standing procedures.
Some critics argue that a conventionalist perspective limits creativity and progress.

Related Terms

  • antonym: innovative - introducing new ideas or methods
  • synonym: traditionalist - favoring established customs or traditions
