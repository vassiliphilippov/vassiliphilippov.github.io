frontality

noun
forward-facing quality
9.6
Pronunciation: / frʌnˈtæləti / (AmE), / frʌnˈtælɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being at the front or facing directly forward, especially in art or in a leading position

Examples

Statue's frontality
The frontality of the ancient statue makes it appear powerful and imposing.
Artists often use frontality to create a sense of directness in their portraits.
The painting's frontality draws the viewer's attention straight to the subject's face.
The frontality of the statue made it appear as if it was confronting the viewer directly.
Her frontality in the discussion ensured that her ideas were heard first.
The painting's frontality draws attention to the subject's face and expression.
