mysterious

adjective
difficult to explain or understand
2.9
Pronunciation: / mɪˈstɪriəs / (AmE), / mɪˈstɪəriəs / (BrE)
difficult or impossible to understand, explain, or identify; having an obscure or puzzling quality

Examples

A mysterious noise
She heard a mysterious noise coming from the old attic late at night.
The ancient ruins were covered with mysterious symbols that no one could decipher.
The plot of the novel grew more mysterious with every chapter, keeping readers guessing.
A mysterious message appeared on her phone late at night, with no sender listed.
The abandoned house at the end of the street has always been a mysterious place to the villagers.
The detective was drawn to the mysterious circumstances surrounding the victim's disappearance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clear - easy to perceive, understand, or interpret
  • synonym: enigmatic - difficult to interpret or understand; mysterious
  • synonym: mystical - having a spiritual or supernatural significance; inspiring a sense of mystery or awe
  • synonym: obscure - not discovered or known about; uncertain
  • antonym: obvious - easily perceived or understood; clear, self-evident
