social insurance

noun
state-provided financial support system
1.0 4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊʃəl ɪnˈʃʊrəns / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəl ɪnˈʃʊərəns / (BrE)
A government system providing financial support to people who are unemployed, injured, or elderly, typically funded by contributions from employers, employees, and government revenue.

Examples

Social insurance system
Employees are often required to pay into social insurance as part of their payroll deductions.
Many countries have established social insurance programs to protect workers from loss of income.
Universal social insurance reduced poverty among the elderly and disabled.

Related Terms

  • antonym: private insurance - insurance provided by private companies rather than government
  • synonym: welfare - government support for the well-being of citizens
