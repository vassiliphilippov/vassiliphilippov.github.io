order Artiodactyla

noun
order of even-toed hoofed mammals
9.8
Pronunciation: /ˈɔːr.dər ɑːr.tiː.ə.ˈdæk.tɪ.lə/ /; / /ˈɔːr.dər ɑːr.tiː.oʊ.ˈdæk.tɪ.lə/ (AmE), /ˈɔː.də ɑː.tiː.ə.ˈdæk.tɪ.lə/ (BrE)
a taxonomic order of hoofed mammals with an even number of functional toes, including pigs, hippopotamuses, and all ruminants

Examples

Order Artiodactyla mammals
Members of the order Artiodactyla are characterized by having an even number of toes on each foot.
Researchers studied the evolution of limb structure in the order Artiodactyla.
The order Artiodactyla includes animals such as cattle, pigs, and deer.

Related Terms

  • antonym: order Perissodactyla - order of odd-toed ungulates, such as horses and rhinoceroses
  • synonym: ruminant - a mammal of the suborder Ruminantia, including cattle, sheep, and deer
