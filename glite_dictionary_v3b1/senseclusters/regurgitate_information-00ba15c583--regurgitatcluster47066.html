regurgitate information

verb
repeat information without understanding
Pronunciation: / rɪˈɡɝːdʒəˌteɪt ˌɪnfɚˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈɡɜːdʒɪteɪt ˌɪnfəˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
to repeat information, especially facts or ideas, without understanding or original thought

Examples

To regurgitate information quickly
He was able to regurgitate information from the textbook but could not apply it to real-world problems.
Many students simply regurgitate information on exams instead of demonstrating true understanding.
Our goal is to encourage critical thinking rather than having students just regurgitate information.
