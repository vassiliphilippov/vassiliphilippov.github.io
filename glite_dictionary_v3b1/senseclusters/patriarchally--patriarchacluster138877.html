patriarchally

adverb
in a patriarch's manner
8.2
Pronunciation: /peɪ.trɪ.ˈɑːr.kə.liː/ /; / /pə.trɪ.ˈɑːr.kə.liː/ (AmE), /peɪ.trɪ.ˈɑː.kə.liː/ /; / /pə.trɪ.ˈɑː.kə.liː/ (BrE)
in a way that reflects or supports the authority or leadership of men, especially as heads of families, groups, or society

Examples

Governed patriarchally
The church was governed patriarchally, with all major decisions made by the senior bishop.
Traditions were maintained patriarchally, following the customs set by previous leaders.
In some regions, authority is still exercised patriarchally within religious communities.
The family was governed patriarchally, with the eldest man making all major decisions.
Traditions were passed down patriarchally from father to son for generations.
In some communities, property is still distributed patriarchally rather than equally among all children.
The company was managed patriarchally, with men making all the important decisions.
In some societies, families are still organized patriarchally, giving fathers the most authority.
The rules were enforced patriarchally, favoring male members over female ones.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hierarchically - in a manner relating to a hierarchy or ranking system
  • antonym: matriarchally - in a manner showing the authority of a female head
  • synonym: paternally - in a fatherly manner
