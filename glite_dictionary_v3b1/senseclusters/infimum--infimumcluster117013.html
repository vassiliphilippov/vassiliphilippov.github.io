infimum

noun
greatest lower bound
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfɪməm / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɪməm / (BrE)
the greatest value that is less than or equal to every element of a set in mathematics

Examples

The infimum value
In this sequence, the infimum is 2, even though 2 is not a member of the set.
Mathematicians often use the infimum to describe the lower limit of a group of numbers.
If a set has a minimum, then the infimum and minimum are the same.
