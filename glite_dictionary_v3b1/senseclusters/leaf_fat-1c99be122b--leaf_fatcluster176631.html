leaf fat

noun
fat from around hog kidneys, used for lard
Pronunciation: / lif fæt / (AmE), / liːf fæt / (BrE)
the layer of fat found around the kidneys and abdomen of a hog, commonly used to make lard

Examples

Leaf fat layer
Leaf fat has a neutral flavor, making it ideal for both savory and sweet pastries.
After butchering the hog, they rendered the leaf fat into pure lard.
The baker prefers to use leaf fat when making traditional pie crusts for its superior texture.
