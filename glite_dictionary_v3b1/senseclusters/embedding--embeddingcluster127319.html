embedding

noun
structure-preserving mapping
7.6
Pronunciation: /ɪm.ˈbed.ɪŋ/ /; / /ə.ˈbed.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ɪm.ˈbed.ɪŋ/ /; / /ə.ˈbed.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
a mathematical function or mapping that inserts one object into another while preserving its structure

Examples

Mathematical embedding
The embedding of the circle into the plane preserves its geometric properties.
In algebra, an embedding allows one group to be viewed as a subgroup of another.
The concept of embedding is fundamental in many areas of mathematics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: injection - a function that maps distinct elements to distinct elements
  • synonym: isomorphism - a mapping that preserves structure and is invertible
