spit

noun
narrow projecting strip of land
Pronunciation: / spɪt / (AmE), / spɪt / (BrE)
A narrow strip of land projecting into the sea or another body of water.

Examples

A sandy spit
A narrow spit of land separated the lagoon from the open sea.
Birds nested on the spit, safe from mainland predators.
Waves had deposited a long sandy spit that curved along the coast.
