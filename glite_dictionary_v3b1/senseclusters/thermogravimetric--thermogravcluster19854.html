thermogravimetric

adjective
relating to weight changes with temperature
Pronunciation: / ˌθɜːrmoʊˌɡrævəˈmɪtrɪk / (AmE), / ˌθɜːməʊˌɡrævɪˈmɛtrɪk / (BrE)
Relating to the measurement of changes in a material's mass as it is heated or cooled.

Examples

Thermogravimetric analysis
A thermogravimetric curve was plotted to show how the sample's weight changed during heating.
Researchers used thermogravimetric methods to study the decomposition of the polymer.
The thermogravimetric analysis revealed a significant loss of mass at high temperatures.
