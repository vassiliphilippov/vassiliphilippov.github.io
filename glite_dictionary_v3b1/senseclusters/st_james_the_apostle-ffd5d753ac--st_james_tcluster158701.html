St. James the Apostle

proper noun
disciple of Jesus; author in the New Testament
7.5
Pronunciation: / seɪnt ˈdʒeɪmz ði əˈpɑsəl / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈdʒeɪmz ði əˈpɒsl̩ / (BrE)
One of the twelve disciples of Jesus, brother of John, and traditionally regarded as the author of the Epistle of James in the New Testament.

Examples

St. James the Apostle disciple
According to tradition, St. James the Apostle was the first of Jesus's disciples to be martyred.
Many pilgrims travel the Camino de Santiago in honor of St. James the Apostle.
The feast day of St. James the Apostle is celebrated on July 25th.

Related Terms

  • synonym: James the Greater - another name for St. James the Apostle, distinguishing him from James the Less
