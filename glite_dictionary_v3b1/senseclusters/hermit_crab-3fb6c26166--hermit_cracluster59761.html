hermit crab

noun
small crustacean living in empty shells
4.9 3.0
Pronunciation: / ˈhɝː.mɪt kræb / (AmE), / ˈhɜː.mɪt krab / (BrE)
a small marine crustacean with a soft body that lives in the empty shells of gastropods for protection

Examples

A hermit crab in shell
A hermit crab protects its soft abdomen by living in discarded shells from other sea creatures.
The children watched a hermit crab scuttle across the tide pool, searching for a new shell.
When threatened, the hermit crab quickly retreats into its borrowed shell for safety.
