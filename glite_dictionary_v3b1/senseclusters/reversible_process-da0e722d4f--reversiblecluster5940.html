reversible process

noun
process that can be exactly reversed
3.2 4.8
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈvɜːr.sə.bəl ˈprɑː.ses/ /; / /riː.ˈvɜːr.sə.bəl ˈprɔː.ses/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈvɜː.sɪ.bəl ˈprəʊ.ses/ /; / /rɪ.ˈvɜː.sə.bəl ˈprɒ.ses/ (BrE)
a process in which a system can return to its original state by reversing the sequence of changes, with all states retraced in reverse order

Examples

A reversible process in physics
A Carnot engine operates through a series of reversible processes to reach maximum efficiency.
In thermodynamics, a reversible process is an idealization that cannot be fully achieved in practice.
The melting of ideal ice at 0°C in contact with water is considered a reversible process under controlled conditions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: irreversible process - a process that cannot be exactly reversed to restore the original state
