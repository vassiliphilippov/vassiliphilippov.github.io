noreaster

noun
storm from the northeast, often severe
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˌnɔːrˈiːstər / (AmE), / ˌnɔːˈiːstə / (BrE)
A strong storm or wind blowing from the northeast, often bringing heavy rain or snow along coastal areas of North America.

Examples

A strong noreaster
A powerful noreaster battered the coastline, causing flooding and power outages.
Last winter, three noreasters hit the region in quick succession.
Meteorologists are tracking another noreaster expected to bring heavy snowfall this weekend.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - the absence of wind or storm
