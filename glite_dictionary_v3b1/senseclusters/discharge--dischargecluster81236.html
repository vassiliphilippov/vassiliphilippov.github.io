discharge

verb
declare not guilty (law)
1.6 5.8
Pronunciation: /dɪs.ˈtʃɑːrdʒ/ (AmE), /dɪs.ˈtʃɑːdʒ/ (BrE)
to formally state that someone is not guilty of a criminal charge; to clear from legal accusation

Examples

To discharge a defendant
After a lengthy trial, the jury discharged the defendant of all charges.
He was finally discharged after the prosecution failed to present enough evidence.
The court discharged her after reviewing new evidence proving her innocence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acquit - to officially declare someone not guilty of a crime
  • antonym: convict - to declare someone guilty of a crime by a verdict
