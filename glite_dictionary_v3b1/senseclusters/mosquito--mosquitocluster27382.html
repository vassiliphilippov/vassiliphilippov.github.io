mosquito

noun
bloodsucking flying insect
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /mə.ˈskiː.təʊ/ /; / /mə.ˈskiː.toʊ/ (AmE), /mə.ˈskiː.təʊ/ /; / /mɒ.ˈskiː.təʊ/ (BrE)
A small flying insect with two wings, whose female has a long proboscis for piercing the skin and sucking the blood of humans and animals.

Examples

A mosquito bite
A single mosquito can transmit diseases such as malaria or dengue fever.
She was bitten by a mosquito while camping in the woods.
You should wear long sleeves to avoid mosquito bites in this area.
