Mediterranean fever

noun
bacterial infectious disease with fever and headache
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛdəˌteɪrəˈniən ˈfiːvɚ / (AmE), / ˌmɛdɪtəˈreɪnɪən ˈfiːvə / (BrE)
an infectious bacterial disease transmitted to humans by contact with infected animals or consumption of infected meat or dairy, characterized by fever and headache

Examples

Mediterranean fever symptoms
Brucella bacteria are responsible for causing Mediterranean fever in humans.
Outbreaks of Mediterranean fever are often linked to contact with infected livestock.
She was diagnosed with Mediterranean fever after consuming unpasteurized cheese.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brucellosis - another term for Mediterranean fever, a disease caused by Brucella bacteria
