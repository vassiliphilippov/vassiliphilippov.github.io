star-shaped fortress

noun
fort with projecting bastions; star-like shape
7.2 8.2
Pronunciation: /stɑːr ʃeɪpt ˈfɔːr.trəs/ /; / /stɑːr ʃeɪpt ˈfɔːr.trɪs/ (AmE), /stɑː ʃeɪpt ˈfɔː.trəs/ /; / /stɑː ʃeɪpt ˈfɔː.trɪs/ (BrE)
A fortification designed with multiple projecting triangular bastions, usually five to eight, arranged so each can cover the others, first developed in 15th-century Italy.

Examples

A star-shaped fortress
Dutch engineers built a star-shaped fortress to protect the vital harbor entrance.
The museum contains detailed models of a star-shaped fortress used during the Renaissance.
The old city walls were reinforced with a massive star-shaped fortress to withstand artillery attacks.
