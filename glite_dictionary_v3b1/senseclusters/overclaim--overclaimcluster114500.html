overclaim

verb
to claim too much
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈkleɪm / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈkleɪm / (BrE)
to claim more than is justified or allowed, especially in relation to money, benefits, or effectiveness

Examples

To overclaim expenses
The auditor discovered that the employee had overclaimed his travel expenses for several months.
If you overclaim benefits, you may be required to pay them back with a penalty.
She was careful not to overclaim on her tax return to avoid any legal issues.
The advertisement was found to overclaim the effectiveness of the new cleaning product.
Researchers warned not to overclaim the results of the preliminary study.
Politicians sometimes overclaim their achievements during election campaigns.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exaggerate - to make something seem larger or more important than it is
  • antonym: underclaim - to claim less than one is entitled to
  • antonym: understate - to describe something as less important or smaller than it really is
