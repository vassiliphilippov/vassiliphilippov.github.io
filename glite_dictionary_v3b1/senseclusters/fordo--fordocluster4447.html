fordo

verb
to destroy or ruin
Pronunciation: / fɔrˈdoʊ / (AmE), / fɔːˈdəʊ / (BrE)
to destroy, ruin, or kill, especially in an archaic or literary context

Examples

To fordo an enemy
The villain's greed would ultimately fordo him in the end.
In the old tale, the city was fordone by a terrible curse.
She feared that her own doubts might fordo her chances of success.

Related Terms

  • synonym: destroy - to damage something so badly that it no longer exists or cannot be repaired
  • synonym: ruin - to spoil or destroy something completely
  • antonym: save - to keep someone or something safe from harm or destruction
