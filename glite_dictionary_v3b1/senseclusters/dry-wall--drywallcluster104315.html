dry-wall

verb
to cover with drywall
Pronunciation: / ˈdraɪ ˌwɔl / (AmE), / ˈdraɪ ˌwɔːl / (BrE)
to construct or cover walls with sheets of drywall (plasterboard)

Examples

To dry-wall a room
She hired a contractor to dry-wall the new extension over the weekend.
The team spent two days dry-walling the office space to prepare it for finishing.
We decided to dry-wall the entire basement before painting.
