reflected

adjective
bent or sent back (of light, sound, etc.)
Pronunciation: / rɪˈflɛktɪd / (AmE), / rɪˈflɛktɪd / (BrE)
(especially of light, sound, or heat) bent or sent back from a surface rather than passing through it or being absorbed.

Examples

Reflected light
Her painting captured the soft glow of reflected light on the wall.
The reflected sunlight made the water sparkle beautifully.
We measured the reflected sound to determine the size of the room.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reflected - past participle of 'reflect' (to bounce back, as of light or sound)
  • antonym: transmitted - (of energy, especially light or sound) passed through a medium rather than being reflected or absorbed
