whitelash

noun
backlash by Whites against civil rights progress
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪtˌlæʃ / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtˌlæʃ / (BrE)
A hostile or negative reaction by some White individuals to advances in civil rights for Black people or other minorities.

Examples

A whitelash reaction
Historians debate whether the policy changes were prompted by economic issues or by whitelash.
Some commentators described the election results as a clear case of whitelash.
The protest was seen by many as a whitelash against new diversity initiatives.
