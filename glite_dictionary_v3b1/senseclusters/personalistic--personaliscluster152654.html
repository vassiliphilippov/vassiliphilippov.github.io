personalistic

adjective
focused on one leader
8.0
Pronunciation: /pɜːr.sə.nəˈlɪs.tɪk/ (AmE), /pɜː.sə.nəˈlɪs.tɪk/ (BrE)
relating to or emphasizing the importance of individual persons or personal relationships, rather than abstract systems or rules

Examples

Personalistic style
The new government adopted a personalistic style, with all decisions made by the president.
Critics argue that personalistic regimes often undermine democratic institutions.
A personalistic approach to leadership can lead to instability if the leader leaves office.
The manager's personalistic approach made employees feel valued and understood.
Some political systems are criticized for being too personalistic and centered on one leader.
Her teaching style is highly personalistic, focusing on each student's unique needs.
Her personalistic approach to teaching made every student feel valued and respected.
The movement promoted a personalistic philosophy that centered on human dignity.
Critics argued that the policy was too personalistic and ignored broader social needs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authoritarian - favoring strict obedience to a single authority
  • antonym: collectivist - emphasizing the group over the individual
  • antonym: impersonal - not influenced by, showing, or involving personal feelings
  • synonym: individualistic - emphasizing the importance of individual people
  • antonym: institutional - based on established systems or formal organizations
