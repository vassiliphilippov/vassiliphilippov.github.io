active birth

noun
childbirth method with mother's active participation
Pronunciation: / ˈæktɪv bɝθ / (AmE), / ˈæktɪv bɜːθ / (BrE)
a method of childbirth in which the mother is encouraged to move around and take an active role in the labor process, rather than remaining passive or confined to bed

Examples

Active birth method
Many women find active birth empowering, as it gives them more control during childbirth.
Some midwives recommend active birth because movement can help ease pain and speed up labor.
The hospital offers active birth classes to help mothers prepare for labor.
