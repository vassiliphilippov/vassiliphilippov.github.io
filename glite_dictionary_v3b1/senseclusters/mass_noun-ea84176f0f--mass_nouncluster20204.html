mass noun

noun
noun that has no plural form
Pronunciation: / mæs naʊn / (AmE), / mæs naʊn / (BrE)
a noun that refers to something that cannot usually be counted and does not normally have a plural form, such as 'water', 'furniture', or 'information'

Examples

A mass noun example
'Water' is a classic example of a mass noun in English.
In grammar lessons, students often confuse mass nouns with count nouns.
The teacher explained that 'advice' is a mass noun and does not take a plural.

Related Terms

  • antonym: count noun - a noun that refers to something that can be counted and has both singular and plural forms
