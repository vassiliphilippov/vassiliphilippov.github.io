bail bond

noun
legal guarantee for court appearance
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪl ˌbɑnd / (AmE), / ˈbeɪl ˌbɒnd / (BrE)
A written promise or agreement by which a person pays or promises to pay an amount of money as security to ensure that an accused person returns to court, which is forfeited if the person fails to appear.

Examples

A bail bond company
Many people rely on a bail bond company to cover their bail amount.
She was released from jail after posting a bail bond of $5,000.
The bail bond will be forfeited if the defendant fails to appear in court.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bail - money paid as security for release before trial
  • synonym: bond - a written promise to pay or perform a duty
