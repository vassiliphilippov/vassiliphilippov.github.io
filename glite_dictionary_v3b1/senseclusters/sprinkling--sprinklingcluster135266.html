sprinkling

noun
a light, patchy rain shower
3.5 2.2
Pronunciation: /ˈsprɪŋ.klɪŋ/ /; / /ˈsprɪŋ.kəl.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈsprɪŋ.klɪŋ/ /; / /ˈsprɪŋ.kəl.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
a small amount of liquid or things lightly scattered over an area; a light fall or act of scattering drops (such as rain, water, or in baptism)

Examples

A light sprinkling of rain
A light sprinkling fell as we walked to the station, but the sun was shining a block away.
Farmers hoped for more than a sprinkling, as their fields were still too dry.
There was just a sprinkling of rain last night, enough to wet the ground but not much more.
Historical records indicate that sprinkling became more common in Europe after the Middle Ages.
Some denominations accept sprinkling while others insist on full immersion during baptism.
The church practiced sprinkling as the preferred form of baptism for infants.
A gentle sprinkling of rain refreshed the dry earth.
The priest performed a sprinkling of holy water during the ceremony.
There was a sprinkling of water on the leaves after the morning dew.

Related Terms

  • synonym: baptism - Christian rite of admission and adoption often by water
  • degree higher: dousing - the act of wetting thoroughly
  • degree higher: downpour - a very heavy, sudden rain shower
  • degree higher: drizzle - very light rain, finer than a sprinkling
  • antonym: immersion - baptism by fully submerging in water
  • synonym: spray - a fine stream of liquid dispersed in small drops
