on the beat

adverb
actively working in an assigned area
Pronunciation: / ɑn ðə bit / (AmE), / ɒn ðə biːt / (BrE)
actively engaged in one's assigned area of work, especially in journalism or police patrol duties.

Examples

Walk on the beat
New recruits often walk on the beat to gain experience in the community.
The reporter spent years on the beat covering city hall politics.
While the detective was on the beat, he noticed something unusual in the alley.
