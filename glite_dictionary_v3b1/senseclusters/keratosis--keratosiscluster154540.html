keratosis

noun
thickened overgrowth of outer skin
Pronunciation: / ˌkɛrəˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌkɛrəˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a skin condition characterized by thickening and overgrowth of the outer layer of the skin

Examples

Actinic keratosis
Actinic keratosis is common among people with prolonged sun exposure.
Certain types of keratosis can be removed with minor surgery.
The doctor diagnosed the rough patch on her arm as keratosis.
