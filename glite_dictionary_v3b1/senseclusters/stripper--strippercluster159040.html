stripper

noun
low-producing oil well
Pronunciation: / ˈstrɪpɚ / (AmE), / ˈstrɪpə / (BrE)
an oil well producing less than ten barrels of oil per day, typically near the end of its productive life

Examples

Oil well stripper
Maintaining a stripper can still be profitable if operating costs are kept low.
Many rural communities rely on stripper wells for local employment and revenue.
The company plans to sell some of its older strippers to independent operators.
