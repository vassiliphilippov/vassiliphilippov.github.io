magnetic intensity

noun
strength of a magnetic field
1.7 5.4
Pronunciation: /mæɡ.ˈnet.ɪk ɪn.ˈten.sɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /mæɡ.ˈnet.ɪk ɪn.ˈten.sɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
the strength of a magnetic field in a given area, measured as the amount of magnetic flux per unit area perpendicular to the field's direction

Examples

Magnetic intensity strength
Changes in magnetic intensity can affect sensitive electronic instruments.
Scientists measured the magnetic intensity at various points around the electromagnetic coil.
The magnetic intensity near the poles of the magnet is significantly higher than at the center.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: magnetic flux - the total number of magnetic field lines passing through a surface
