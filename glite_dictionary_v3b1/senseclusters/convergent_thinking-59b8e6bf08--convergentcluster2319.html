convergent thinking

noun
problem-solving method with single correct answer
5.8 5.9
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈvɜːr.dʒənt ˈθɪŋ.kɪŋ/ (AmE), /kən.ˈvɜː.dʒənt ˈθɪŋ.kɪŋ/ (BrE)
the process of bringing together focused information to solve a problem, especially where there is a single correct solution

Examples

Convergent thinking process
In science, convergent thinking is valuable for analyzing facts and reaching logical conclusions.
Standardized tests often require convergent thinking to choose the single best answer.
Teachers encourage convergent thinking when they assign problems with only one correct solution.

Related Terms

  • antonym: divergent thinking - thinking that generates multiple possible solutions or ideas
