wolf pack

noun
group of submarines operating together
Pronunciation: / wʊlf pæk / (AmE), / wʊlf pæk / (BrE)
A group of submarines operating together to attack enemy convoys, especially used during World War II.

Examples

Wolf pack attack
During the Battle of the Atlantic, a wolf pack coordinated a devastating attack on an Allied convoy.
Intelligence reports revealed that a wolf pack was patrolling the North Atlantic shipping lanes.
The German navy perfected the use of wolf packs to intercept and sink merchant ships.

Related Terms

  • synonym: convoy - a group of ships or vehicles traveling together, often for protection
  • synonym: submarine - a naval vessel designed to operate underwater
