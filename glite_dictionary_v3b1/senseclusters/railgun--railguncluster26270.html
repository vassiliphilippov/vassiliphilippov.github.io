railgun

noun
electromagnetic launcher
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪlˌɡʌn / (AmE), / ˈreɪlˌɡʌn / (BrE)
a device or weapon that uses electromagnetic force to launch objects or projectiles at extremely high speeds

Examples

A railgun weapon
The laboratory's railgun can launch metal projectiles at speeds exceeding several kilometers per second.
Scientists used a railgun to study the effects of high-velocity impacts on spacecraft materials.
A prototype railgun was demonstrated at the technology exhibition, impressing the audience with its power.
The military is developing a new railgun that can fire projectiles at hypersonic speeds.
A prototype railgun was tested successfully at the research facility last month.
Unlike traditional artillery, a railgun uses electricity to accelerate its ammunition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cannon - a large gun that fires heavy projectiles
  • synonym: coilgun - a device that uses electromagnetic coils to launch projectiles
  • synonym: firearm - a portable gun that launches projectiles
