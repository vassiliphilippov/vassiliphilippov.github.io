Carya glabra

noun
a North American hickory tree
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛr.i.ə ˈɡleɪ.brə / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.rɪə ˈɡleɪ.brə / (BrE)
A species of hickory tree native to eastern North America, noted for its hard wood and bitter nuts.

Examples

Carya glabra tree
Although the nuts of Carya glabra are bitter, some wildlife species feed on them.
The Carya glabra can be found in forests throughout the eastern United States.
The wood of Carya glabra is highly valued for tool handles and flooring.
