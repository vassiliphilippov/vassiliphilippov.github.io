mudbank

noun
raised mud area
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌdˌbæŋk / (AmE), / ˈmʌdˌbæŋk / (BrE)
a raised area or accumulation of mud in or near a river, lake, or sea, sometimes creating a hazard for boats

Examples

The mudbank near river
At low tide, the children played on the exposed mudbank by the river.
The boat became stuck on a mudbank during the early morning fog.
Wading birds gathered on the mudbank to search for insects and small fish.
The old ferry became stuck on a mudbank during low tide and could not move until morning.
Birds gathered on the exposed mudbank to search for food as the water receded.
Sailors must be careful to avoid the mudbank near the river's mouth, especially at night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sandbank - a raised area of sand in a river or sea
  • synonym: sandbar - a ridge of sand built up by currents, often just below the surface of the water
  • synonym: shoal - a shallow place in a body of water
