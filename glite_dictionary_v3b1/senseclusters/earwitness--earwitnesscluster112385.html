earwitness

noun
person who heard event
8.7
Pronunciation: /ˈɪr.wɪt.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈɪə.wɪt.nəs/ (BrE)
a person who gives information or testimony about something they heard, especially in legal or investigative situations

Examples

The earwitness testimony

Police relied on an earwitness to confirm the identity of the person speaking in the recording.
As an earwitness, he was able to describe the sounds he heard at the scene.
