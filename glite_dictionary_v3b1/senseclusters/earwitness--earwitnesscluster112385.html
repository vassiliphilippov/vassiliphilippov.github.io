earwitness

noun
person who heard event
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈɪrˌwɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈɪəˌwɪtnəs / (BrE)
a person who gives information or testimony about something they heard, especially in legal or investigative situations

Examples

The earwitness testimony
The court relied on the earwitness who heard the suspect shouting before the incident.
An earwitness described the sound of breaking glass during the robbery.
Police interviewed an earwitness who overheard the argument from the next apartment.
The earwitness testified that she recognized the suspect's voice during the phone call.
Police relied on an earwitness to confirm the identity of the person speaking in the recording.
As an earwitness, he was able to describe the sounds he heard at the scene.

Related Terms

  • antonym: eyewitness - a person who gives testimony about something they saw happen
  • synonym: witness - a person who gives evidence about an event they experienced
