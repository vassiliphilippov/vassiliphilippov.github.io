embankment

noun
artificial mound for support or protection
9.0
Pronunciation: / ɛmˈbæŋkmənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈbaŋkmənt / (BrE)
a long, raised structure of earth or stone built to hold back water, support a road or railway, or provide protection

Examples

A embankment by river
Children played on the grassy embankment beside the busy road.
Engineers reinforced the embankment to prevent flooding during the rainy season.
The train traveled along the embankment overlooking the river.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dam - a barrier built to hold back water and raise its level
  • synonym: levee - an embankment built to prevent the overflow of a river
