pot cheese

noun
mild, soft white cheese from soured milk curds
7.6 5.6
Pronunciation: /pɑːt tʃiːz/ (AmE), /pɒt tʃiːz/ (BrE)
A mild, soft white cheese made from curds of soured skim milk, often slightly crumbly and used in cooking or spreads.

Examples

A pot cheese spread
At the local market, pot cheese is sold alongside cream cheese and ricotta.
She spread pot cheese on her toast and topped it with fresh herbs.
The recipe calls for one cup of pot cheese mixed with eggs and sugar.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cottage cheese - a soft, white, creamy cheese made from curds of skimmed milk
  • degree higher: farmer cheese - a type of pressed cottage cheese that is drier and firmer than pot cheese
