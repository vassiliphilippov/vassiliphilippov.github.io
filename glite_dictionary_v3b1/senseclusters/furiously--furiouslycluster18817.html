furiously

adverb
very angrily or with great energy
Pronunciation: / ˈfjʊriəsli / (AmE), / ˈfjʊəriəsli / (BrE)
in a very angry, energetic, or emotional way; with great intensity

Examples

The crowd protested furiously when the referee made a controversial call.
She typed furiously to meet her deadline.
The crowd cheered furiously as the team scored a last-minute goal.
Waves crashed furiously against the rocky shore during the storm.
