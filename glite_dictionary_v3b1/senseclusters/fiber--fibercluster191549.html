fiber

noun
elongated threadlike cell (e.g. muscle, nerve)
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪbɚ / (AmE), / ˈfaɪbə / (BrE)
any of several elongated, threadlike cells such as a muscle fiber or a nerve fiber in living tissue

Examples

Muscle fiber
Damage to a muscle can cause the fibers to tear, resulting in pain and weakness.
Nerve fibers transmit signals quickly throughout the body to coordinate movement.
Under the microscope, each fiber appeared as a long, thin strand within the tissue.
Damage to nerve fibres can lead to loss of sensation in the affected area.
Scientists examined the plant's vascular fibres under a microscope.
The heart is composed of cardiac muscle fibres that contract rhythmically.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fiber - standard American English spelling for 'fibre'
  • synonym: filament - a slender threadlike object or structure, especially in biology
