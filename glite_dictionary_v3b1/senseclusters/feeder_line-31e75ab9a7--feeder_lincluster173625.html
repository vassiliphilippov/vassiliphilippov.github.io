feeder line

noun
secondary route branching from a main line
9.7 4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈfidər laɪn / (AmE), / ˈfiːdə laɪn / (BrE)
a secondary route that branches off from a main transportation line, especially in airlines or railways, to connect outlying areas

Examples

A feeder line route
Smaller towns depend on the feeder line for access to larger cities and beyond.
The new railway feeder line will improve connectivity in remote areas.
The regional airport is serviced by a feeder line that connects to several major hubs.
