throttle

verb
to limit or restrict
Pronunciation: / ˈθrɑː.t̬əl / (AmE), / ˈθrɒ.tl̩ / (BrE)
to restrict or limit the amount, extent, or access to something, often to control speed, growth, or progress

Examples

To throttle growth
Environmental concerns have throttled expansion plans for the new airport.
Government regulations may throttle the growth of small businesses.
The company decided to throttle access to non-essential services during peak hours.

Related Terms

  • antonym: allow - to permit or give access to
  • synonym: limit - to set a fixed maximum amount or boundary
  • synonym: restrict - to limit the size, amount, or range of something
