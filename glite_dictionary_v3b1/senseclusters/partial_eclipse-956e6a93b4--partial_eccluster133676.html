partial eclipse

noun
eclipse partly covering a celestial body
3.1 4.8
Pronunciation: /ˈpɑːr.ʃəl ɪ.ˈklɪps/ /; / /ˈpɑːr.ʃəl ə.ˈklɪps/ /; / /ˈpɑːr.ʃəl iː.ˈklɪps/ (AmE), /ˈpɑː.ʃəl ɪ.ˈklɪps/ /; / /ˈpɑː.ʃəl iː.ˈklɪps/ (BrE)
An eclipse in which the eclipsed body is only partly obscured by another object, so that some portion remains visible.

Examples

A partial eclipse of the Moon
A partial eclipse of the Moon occurred last night, visible from most of Europe.
During a partial eclipse, the sky darkens but sunlight can still be seen around the edges of the Moon.
Many people used special glasses to safely view the partial eclipse yesterday.

Related Terms

  • antonym: total eclipse - an eclipse in which the eclipsed body is completely covered
