neostriatum

noun
avian brain region
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌniːoʊˈstraɪətəm / (AmE), / ˌniːəʊˈstraɪətəm / (BrE)
an outdated term for a region of the avian forebrain once thought to be involved in sensory processing and learning

Examples

Avian neostriatum
The term neostriatum is no longer used in modern avian brain anatomy.
Researchers once believed the neostriatum played a key role in bird learning.
Textbooks from the 20th century often referenced the neostriatum when describing avian brains.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pallium - the outer layer of the brain in birds and mammals
  • synonym: striatum - a region of the brain involved in movement and reward
