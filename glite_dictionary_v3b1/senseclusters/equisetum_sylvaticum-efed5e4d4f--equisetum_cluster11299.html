Equisetum sylvaticum

noun
species of horsetail plant
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɛkwəˈsitəm sɪlˈvætɪkəm / (AmE), / ˌɛkwɪˈsiːtəm sɪlˈvætɪkəm / (BrE)
A species of horsetail plant found in Eurasia (except southern Russia) and northern North America, recognized for its feathery, branching shoots.

Examples

Equisetum sylvaticum is commonly known as wood horsetail and thrives in damp woodland areas.
Botanists often study Equisetum sylvaticum to understand the evolution of ancient vascular plants.
