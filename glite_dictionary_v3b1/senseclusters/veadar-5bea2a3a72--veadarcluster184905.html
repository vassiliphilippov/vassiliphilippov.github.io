Veadar

noun
extra month in the Hebrew calendar
9.9
Pronunciation: / veɪˈɑːdər / (AmE), / veɪˈɑːdə / (BrE)
An additional month inserted in the Hebrew calendar seven times every nineteen years, following Adar, to align the lunar calendar with the solar year.

Examples

The Veadar month
In leap years, Veadar is observed after the month of Adar.
The Hebrew calendar occasionally includes Veadar to align with the solar cycle.
The addition of Veadar ensures the Jewish festivals remain in the correct season.
