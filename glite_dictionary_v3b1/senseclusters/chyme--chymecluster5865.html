chyme

noun
semifluid digestive mass from stomach
Pronunciation: / kaɪm / (AmE), / kaɪm / (BrE)
The thick, semifluid mass of partially digested food that is passed from the stomach to the small intestine.

Examples

Thick chyme mass
After leaving the stomach, the chyme enters the small intestine for further digestion.
Enzymes break down the nutrients in chyme as it moves through the digestive tract.
The doctor explained how chyme is produced during the digestive process.
