which

pronoun
relative pronoun for things
Pronunciation: / wɪtʃ / (AmE), / wɪtʃ / (BrE)
a relative pronoun used to introduce a clause giving more information about a thing or animal mentioned earlier

Examples

The book which I read
The book which you lent me was very interesting.
She adopted a dog which had been abandoned by its previous owner.
The house, which was built in 1920, needs some repairs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: that - a relative pronoun used to introduce a clause about a person or thing
  • antonym: who - a relative pronoun used to refer to people
