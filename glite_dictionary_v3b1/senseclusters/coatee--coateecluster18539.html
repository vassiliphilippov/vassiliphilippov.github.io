coatee

noun
short, close-fitting coat
9.1
Pronunciation: / koʊˈti / (AmE), / kəʊˈtiː / (BrE)
A short, close-fitting coat, especially one worn as part of a uniform or formal dress in the 18th or 19th centuries.

Examples

A coatee uniform
In many period dramas, characters are seen dressed in a coatee and breeches.
The officer wore a blue coatee decorated with gold braid for the ceremony.
The tailors specialized in reproducing historic coatees for reenactments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coat - an outer garment with sleeves, worn outdoors
