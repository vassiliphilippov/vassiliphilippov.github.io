resist

verb
oppose or withstand
2.2 19/m
Pronunciation: / rÉªËˆzÉªst / (AmE), / rÉªËˆzÉªst / (BrE)
to oppose, refuse, or act against something such as authority, rules, or change

Examples

To resist new rules
Despite heavy pressure, the council resisted changing its environmental policy.
She could not resist the urge to check her phone during the meeting.
The community decided to resist any attempt to close their local library.
He tried to resist arrest, but the police quickly restrained him.
Protesters gathered to resist the government's controversial decision.
She decided to resist the new company policy despite pressure from management.
Citizens gathered to resist the new government policies through peaceful protests.
She tried to resist the pressure to conform to popular opinion.
They openly resisted the proposed changes by voicing their concerns at the meeting.

Synonyms

  • oppose
    2.9 91/m
    oppose is a direct formal verb used to argue against ideas or plans, while resist means to act against them.
  • go against
    3.3 8.20/m
    go against is a casual phrase for opposing rules or ideas, while resist implies more general or active opposition.
  • defy
    3.7 8.02/m
    defy is to openly challenge or refuse orders or rules, while resist covers both quiet refusal and active opposition.
  • stand up against
    3.7 1.13/m
    stand up against is to oppose someone openly, often for fairness, while resist is a broader act of opposing.
  • fight
    1.0 88/m
    fight is to try hard with energy to oppose or win, while resist can mean oppose without strong attack.
  • fight back
    3.3 7.42/m
    fight back is to resist by returning force or words, while resist can be passive or nonviolent opposition.
  • disobey
    3.7 3.75/m
    disobey is to refuse to follow orders or rules, usually by action, while resist may include wider opposition beyond rules.
  • rebel
    4.1 2.58/m
    rebel is to openly reject authority or rules, often in groups or politics, while resist can be personal or small scale.
  • stand firm
    3.7 1.35/m
    stand firm is to refuse to change your decision or view, showing steady resistance, while resist covers many kinds of opposition.
  • hold out
    3.7 4.46/m
    hold out is to refuse to give in and keep resisting over time, while resist can be sudden or short-lived opposition.
  • dissent
    6.8 3.82/m
    dissent is to publicly disagree or refuse to accept an official view, while resist may include private or physical opposition.
  • protest
    3.2 18/m
    protest is to show strong public disapproval, usually by action or speech, while resist can be quiet or personal opposition.
  • protest against
    3.6 3.37/m
    protest against is to publicly object to a specific action or policy, while resist is a wider act of opposing or refusing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defy - to openly resist or refuse to obey
  • synonym: oppose - to actively resist or work against something
  • antonym: submit - to yield or comply with a request or authority
  • antonym: yield - to give way to arguments, demands, or pressure
