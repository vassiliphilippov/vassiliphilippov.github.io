exegetically

adverb
in an interpretive way
Pronunciation: / ˌɛksəˈdʒɛtɪkli / (AmE), / ˌɛksɪˈdʒɛtɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to the interpretation or explanation of texts, especially religious scriptures

Examples

To study exegetically
The professor discussed the poem exegetically, focusing on its deeper meanings.
Biblical passages are often studied exegetically in theology courses.
She approached the ancient text exegetically to uncover its historical context.

Related Terms

  • synonym: analytically - in a way that involves detailed examination
  • antonym: literally - in a way that uses the exact meaning of words
