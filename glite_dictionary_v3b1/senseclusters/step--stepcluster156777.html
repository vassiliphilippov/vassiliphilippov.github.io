step

verb
measure by pacing (verb)
Pronunciation: / stɛp / (AmE), / stɛp / (BrE)
to move by lifting and putting down your foot, or to move in this way (walking, treading, pacing, etc.)

Examples

To step forward
He stepped off twenty feet to find the property line.
The surveyor stepped the length of the field to estimate its size.
They step out the distance before setting up the stakes.
Please step carefully over the wet floor.
She stepped aside to let him pass through the doorway.
The dancers stepped in time to the music.
He quickly stepped aside to let her pass through the doorway.
Please step forward when your name is called.
The cat stepped onto the windowsill, looking outside.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pace - to measure by walking a number of steps
  • antonym: stand - to be in an upright position on the feet
  • degree higher: stomp - to walk with heavy, forceful steps
  • degree higher: stride - to walk with long, decisive steps
  • degree lower: tiptoe - to walk quietly and carefully with one's heels raised
  • synonym: tread - to put your foot down while walking
  • synonym: walk - to move at a regular pace by lifting and setting down each foot in turn
