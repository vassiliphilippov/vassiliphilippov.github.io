banian

noun
loose-fitting jacket, originally from India
Pronunciation: / ˈbæniən / (AmE), / ˈbæniən / (BrE)
a loose-fitting jacket or gown, originally of cotton and worn in India, later adopted in Europe as informal indoor wear in the 18th century

Examples

A silk banian
Elegant gentlemen in the 18th century sometimes chose a silk banian for casual mornings at home.
In colonial portraits, the scholar was often depicted wearing a comfortable banian.
The museum exhibit displayed a finely embroidered banian once owned by a British officer in India.
