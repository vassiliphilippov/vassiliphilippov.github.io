micaceous soil

noun
soil containing mica
9.9
Pronunciation: / maɪˈkeɪʃəs sɔɪl / (AmE), / maɪˈkeɪʃəs sɔɪl / (BrE)
soil that contains significant amounts of mica, often improving drainage and affecting fertility

Examples

Micaceous soil drainage
In regions with micaceous soil, farmers often see better drainage after heavy rains.
The micaceous soil helped the plants thrive.
The garden's micaceous soil sparkled in the sunlight due to the mica particles.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clay soil - soil with a high proportion of clay particles
  • synonym: sandy soil - soil with a high proportion of sand particles
