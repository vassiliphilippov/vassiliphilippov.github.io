unbalance

verb
make mentally unstable or insane
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈbæl.əns / (AmE), / ʌnˈbæl.əns / (BrE)
to cause someone to lose their mental stability or become insane

Examples

To unbalance a person
Constant stress can unbalance even the strongest minds.
It was feared that the shock might unbalance him permanently.
The tragic accident seemed to unbalance her for years afterward.

Related Terms

  • synonym: derange - to disturb the order or arrangement of; to make insane
  • antonym: stabilize - to make stable or restore to normal mental balance
  • synonym: unhinge - to make mentally unbalanced or unstable
