paper mulberry

noun
Asian tree with bark used for cloth; shade tree
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪpər ˈmʌlˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈpeɪpə ˈmʌlbəri / (BrE)
a shrubby tree native to Asia, with bark used for making cloth (tapa), cultivated as a shade tree and known for its pendulous male catkins and urn-shaped female flowers followed by small orange-red berries

Examples

A large paper mulberry tree
A large paper mulberry tree provides shade in the center of the garden.
The paper mulberry has been cultivated in Hawaii for centuries for its fibrous bark.
The unusual berries of the paper mulberry attract various songbirds in the spring.
