doomful

adjective
deserving condemnation
Pronunciation: / ˈduːmfəl / (AmE), / ˈduːmfʊl / (BrE)
deserving or likely to receive severe judgment, punishment, or condemnation, especially in an archaic or literary context

Examples

Doomful punishment
The traitor faced a doomful fate at the hands of the angry villagers.
In the old legend, a doomful prophecy warned of disaster for the kingdom.
His doomful actions led to his exile from the community.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blameless - free from guilt or blame
  • synonym: condemned - judged to be guilty or unfit
