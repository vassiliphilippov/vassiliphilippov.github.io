boonies

noun
remote rural area
9.3 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàbuÀêniz / (AmE), / ÀàbuÀêniz / (BrE)
a remote or rural area far from cities or towns, often considered isolated or undeveloped

Examples

The boonies area
After getting lost, we realized we were deep in the boonies with no cell service.
My grandparents live out in the boonies, surrounded by forests and farmland.
He complained that moving to the boonies meant driving an hour to the nearest store.

Synonyms

  • boondocks
    9.1 0.05/m
    boondocks is a casual word for a very distant rural area, while boonies is an informal term for remote countryside.
  • in the sticks
    9.1 0.23/m
    in the sticks describes being in a small, quiet rural place far from shops, while boonies means any remote countryside.
  • backwoods
    7.0 0.00/m
    backwoods suggests a wild, forested area with few people, while boonies means remote rural places in general.
  • backcountry
    5.4 0.00/m
    backcountry refers to remote, often rugged land used for hiking and outdoors, while boonies means isolated rural regions.
  • middle of nowhere
    4.6 2.50/m
    middle of nowhere feels empty and extremely far from towns, while boonies implies rural isolation rather than complete emptiness.
  • hinterland
    8.8 1.68/m
    hinterland refers to land away from coasts or cities often in maps or studies, while boonies is casual talk about remote countryside.
