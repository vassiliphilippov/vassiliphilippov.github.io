oxyacid

noun
an acid containing oxygen
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɑksiˌæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈɒksɪˌæsɪd / (BrE)
a chemical acid that contains oxygen, typically composed of hydrogen, oxygen, and another element

Examples

An oxyacid compound
Chemists classify nitric acid as an oxyacid because it contains both hydrogen and oxygen.
Many industrial processes rely on oxyacids for their chemical properties.
Sulfuric acid is an example of an oxyacid commonly used in laboratories.
