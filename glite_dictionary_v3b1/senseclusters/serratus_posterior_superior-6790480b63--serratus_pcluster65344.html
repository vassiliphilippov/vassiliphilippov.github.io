serratus posterior superior

noun
muscle of upper back involved in rib elevation
Pronunciation: / səˌreɪtəs pəˈstɪriɚ suːˈpɪriɚ / (AmE), / səˌreɪtəs pɒˈstɪəriə suːˈpɪəriə / (BrE)
a thin, quadrilateral muscle located at the upper and back part of the thorax, which functions to elevate the upper ribs

Examples

The serratus posterior superior muscle
Anatomy textbooks provide detailed diagrams of the serratus posterior superior for medical students.
Injuries to the serratus posterior superior can cause pain in the upper back and limit breathing movements.
The serratus posterior superior assists in the process of inhalation by lifting the upper ribs.
