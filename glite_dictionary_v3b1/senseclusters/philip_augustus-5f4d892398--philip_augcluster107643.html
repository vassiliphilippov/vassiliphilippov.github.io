Philip Augustus

proper noun
king of France (1180–1223)
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪlɪp əˈɡʌstəs / (AmE), / ˈfɪlɪp ɔːˈɡʌstəs / (BrE)
(1165–1223) King of France from 1180 to 1223, whose reign saw the expansion of French royal power and recovery of territories from the English.

Examples

Philip Augustus king of France
Philip Augustus played a significant role in shifting the balance of power between France and England.
Historians credit Philip Augustus with strengthening royal authority in medieval France.
The reign of Philip Augustus marked an era of growth for the French monarchy.
