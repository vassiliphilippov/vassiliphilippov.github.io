roundwood

noun
cylindrical wood pieces
Pronunciation: / ˈraʊndˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈraʊndˌwʊd / (BrE)
wood in the form of whole, unprocessed logs, stems, or branches, used as raw material or for construction (such as poles or posts)

Examples

Stacked roundwood logs

Farmers often use roundwood as fence posts because of its strength and durability.
The bridge was supported by large roundwood logs driven deep into the riverbed.
The factory uses roundwood as the main raw material for making paper.
After the storm, workers collected roundwood from fallen trees in the forest.
Export regulations require that all roundwood shipments be inspected for pests.
The paper mill relies on a steady supply of roundwood from local forests.
Farmers often collect roundwood to use as fuel during the winter months.
Unlike sawn timber, roundwood is used in its natural, uncut form.

Related Terms

  • synonym: firewood - wood used as fuel for burning
  • synonym: log - a section of the trunk or a large branch of a tree that has been cut
  • synonym: lumber - wood that has been processed into beams and planks
  • antonym: sawnwood - wood that has been cut into planks or boards
  • synonym: timber - wood prepared for use in building and carpentry
