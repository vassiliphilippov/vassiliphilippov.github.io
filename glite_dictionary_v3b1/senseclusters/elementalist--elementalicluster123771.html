elementalist

noun
believer in basic elements
9.9
Pronunciation: /el.ə.ˈmen.tə.lɪst/ /; / /el.ɪ.ˈmen.tə.lɪst/ (AmE), /el.ə.ˈmen.tə.lɪst/ /; / /el.ɪ.ˈmen.tə.lɪst/ (BrE)
a person who believes in or supports the theory that the world is made up of basic elements such as earth, air, fire, and water

Examples

Ancient elementalist beliefs
The ancient philosopher was known as an elementalist for his belief in earth, air, fire, and water.
In some mystical traditions, an elementalist seeks to understand the powers of the four elements.
The debate between the elementalist and the atomist shaped early scientific thought.
