paternal

adjective
of or from one's father
4.1 1.26/m
Pronunciation: / p…ôÀàt…úrn…ôl /; / p…ôÀàt…ùÀên…ôl / (AmE), / p…ôÀàt…úÀên…ôl / (BrE)
belonging to, inherited from, or related to one's father or the father's side of the family

Examples

Paternal grandfather
Certain diseases can be traced through the paternal line of ancestry.
She grew up on her family's paternal estate in the countryside.
The paternal surname has been passed down for generations.
All the paternal relatives came together for the family reunion.
Her paternal grandfather immigrated from Italy in the 1920s.
She inherited the ring from her paternal aunt.

Synonyms

  • agnate
    9.9 0.00/m
    agnate means a blood relative through the male line in family terms, while paternal refers to something from or related to one's father.
  • agnatic
    9.9 0.10/m
    agnatic describes descent traced through male ancestors in legal or family records, while paternal describes what belongs to one's father.
  • patrilineal
    8.5 0.54/m
    patrilineal describes a system of descent or inheritance through the father's line, while paternal describes things related to a single father.
