mopani

noun
African hardwood tree
Pronunciation: / moʊˈpɑːni / (AmE), / məʊˈpɑːni / (BrE)
a tree native to southern Africa, valued for its hard wood and as a source of food and shelter for animals

Examples

Mopani tree
The mopani is an important source of food and shelter for many animals in the savanna.
Local communities harvest caterpillars from the mopani tree as a traditional food.
The hard wood of the mopani is often used for building and making furniture.
The craftsman selected mopani for the garden table because of its resistance to weather.
Outdoor decks made from mopani can last for decades with minimal maintenance.
They admired the rich color of the mopani used in the lodge's construction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acacia - a type of tree or shrub common in Africa and Australia
  • synonym: baobab - a large African tree with a thick trunk and edible fruit
  • antonym: pine - a softwood commonly used in construction and furniture
  • synonym: teak - a durable tropical hardwood used for furniture and shipbuilding
