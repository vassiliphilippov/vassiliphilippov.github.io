thistle

noun
a prickly wild plant with purple flowers
9.9 4.5
Pronunciation: /ˈθɪs.əl/ (AmE), /ˈθɪs.əl/ (BrE)
a wild plant with prickly leaves and typically purple flowers, belonging to the family Compositae, especially of the genera Carduus, Cirsium, and Onopordum

Examples

A thistle plant
A thistle grew at the edge of the field, its purple blooms standing out among the grass.
Be careful not to touch the thistle—the leaves are extremely prickly.
The Scottish emblem features a stylized thistle as a symbol of resilience.
