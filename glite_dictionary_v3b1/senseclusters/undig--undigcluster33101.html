undig

verb
to reverse digging
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnˈdɪɡ / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈdɪɡ / (BrE)
to remove earth or material that was previously dug, or to reverse the process of digging.

Examples

To undig a hole
After realizing the mistake, the team had to undig the foundation they had started.
The gardener decided to undig the flower bed and move the plants elsewhere.
When the pipe was found in the wrong place, workers needed to undig the area to fix it.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dig - to break up and move earth with a tool or machine
  • synonym: fill in - to put material back into a hole or trench
