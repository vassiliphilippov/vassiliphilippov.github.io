lorica

noun
Roman body armor
Pronunciation: / lɔːˈriːkə / (AmE), / lɒˈriːkə / (BrE)
a type of body armor made of metal strips or plates, worn by soldiers in ancient Rome

Examples

Roman lorica
The Roman soldier wore a lorica to protect himself in battle.
Archaeologists discovered a well-preserved lorica at the ancient military site.
The museum's exhibit features a replica of a lorica used by Roman legionaries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: armor - protective covering worn to prevent injury in battle
  • synonym: cuirass - a piece of armor covering the body from neck to waist
