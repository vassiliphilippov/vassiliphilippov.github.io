white bread

noun
bread made from refined wheat flour
Pronunciation: / waɪt brɛd / (AmE), / waɪt brɛd / (BrE)
bread made from refined and usually bleached wheat flour, with a light color and soft texture

Examples

A slice of white bread
Many doctors recommend reducing white bread intake due to its lower fiber content.
She prefers white bread for her sandwiches instead of wholemeal varieties.
The bakery sells fresh white bread every morning.
Many children prefer the taste of white bread over whole wheat varieties.
She bought a loaf of white bread at the grocery store for breakfast.
Toast made with white bread is often served with jam and butter.

  • antonym: wholemeal bread - bread made from whole wheat flour
