jumper cable

noun
insulated wire with clamps for electrical connections
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʌmpɚ ˌkeɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌmpə ˌkeɪbəl / (BrE)
A short insulated wire with clamps at each end, used to connect two pieces of electrical equipment, especially for starting a vehicle by transferring power from another vehicle's battery.

Examples

A jumper cable set
Before connecting the jumper cable, make sure both vehicles are turned off.
She kept a set of jumper cables in the trunk of her car for emergencies.
The mechanic attached the jumper cable to the car's battery to get it started.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jumper lead - British term for a similar cable
