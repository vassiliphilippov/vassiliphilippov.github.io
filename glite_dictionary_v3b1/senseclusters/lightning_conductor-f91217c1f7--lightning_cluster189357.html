lightning conductor

noun
rod protecting buildings from lightning
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪtnɪŋ kənˈdʌktər / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtnɪŋ kənˈdʌktə / (BrE)
a metal rod or wire fixed to the highest point of a building and connected to the ground to protect it from damage by lightning

Examples

A lightning conductor rod
After the storm, the lightning conductor showed signs of a recent strike.
Our old country house was fitted with a lightning conductor to prevent fire during storms.
The engineer explained how the lightning conductor channels electricity safely into the earth.
