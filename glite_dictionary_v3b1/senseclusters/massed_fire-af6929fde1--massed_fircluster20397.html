massed fire

noun
coordinated firing by multiple weapons
7.9
Pronunciation: / mæst ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / mæst ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
coordinated firing of two or more weapons simultaneously at a single target or area, especially in military operations

Examples

Massed fire attack
During the naval battle, massed fire from the destroyers overwhelmed the target ship.
Tanks employed massed fire to break through the fortified lines.
The artillery unit coordinated their massed fire to suppress enemy defensive positions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barrage - a concentrated artillery bombardment over a wide area
  • synonym: volley - a number of bullets, arrows, or other projectiles discharged at one time
