summer damask rose

noun
a fragrant pink rose cultivated for attar of roses
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌmɚ dəˈmæsk roʊz / (AmE), / ˈsʌmə dəˈmæsk rəʊz / (BrE)
a large, hardy, and highly fragrant pink rose, cultivated especially in Asia Minor for producing attar of roses; parent of many hybrid varieties

Examples

The summer damask rose flower
Fields of summer damask rose bloom in early summer across parts of Turkey and Bulgaria.
Many hybrid roses today trace their lineage back to the summer damask rose.
The summer damask rose is prized for its intense fragrance in both gardens and the perfume industry.
