submaxillary salivary gland

noun
salivary gland beneath the lower jaw
6.8
Pronunciation: /sʌb.ˈmæk.sɪ.lər.iː ˈsæl.ɪ.vər.iː glænd/ /; / /sʌb.ˈmæk.sɪ.lər.iː sə.ˈlaɪ.vər.iː glænd/ (AmE), /səb.ˈmæk.sɪ.lə.riː ˈsæl.ɪ.və.riː glænd/ /; / /səb.ˈmæk.sɪ.lə.riː sə.ˈlaɪ.və.riː glænd/ (BrE)
a salivary gland located beneath the lower jaw that produces saliva, especially during the night, and empties it into the mouth under the tongue

Examples

The submaxillary salivary gland
During surgery, extreme care must be taken not to damage the submaxillary salivary gland.
Swelling of the submaxillary salivary gland can indicate an underlying infection.
The submaxillary salivary gland is responsible for producing much of the saliva when you sleep.
