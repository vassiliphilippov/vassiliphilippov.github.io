revoice

verb
to rearrange musical notes
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈvɔɪs / (AmE), / ˌriːˈvɔɪs / (BrE)
to change the arrangement of notes in a musical part or chord by redistributing them among voices or instruments

Examples

To revoice a chord
The composer decided to revoice the chord to create a richer sound in the orchestra.
After feedback from the choir, the director chose to revoice several sections for better balance.
Jazz musicians often revoice chords to add variety and interest to their performances.
