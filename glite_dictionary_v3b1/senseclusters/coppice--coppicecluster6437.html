coppice

noun
small woodland or dense bushy growth
9.1 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëÀê.p…™s / (AmE), / Ààk…íp.…™s / (BrE)
A small area of woodland where trees or shrubs are periodically cut back to ground level to encourage new growth; also, a dense growth of bushes or small trees.

Examples

A coppice of trees
Birds often nest in the shelter provided by a coppice.
The hikers pushed their way through the dense coppice at the edge of the field.
We came upon a shady coppice filled with wildflowers during our walk.

Synonyms

  • copse
    7.5 0.36/m
    copse is a small group of trees that often grow naturally, while coppice is an area people cut back regularly to encourage new growth.
  • thicket
    5.1 3.40/m
    thicket is a dense group of bushes or small trees that often blocks paths, while coppice is an area people cut back to encourage regrowth.
