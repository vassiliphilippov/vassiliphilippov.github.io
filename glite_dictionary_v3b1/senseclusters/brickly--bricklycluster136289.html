brickly

adjective
easily broken or brittle (dialect)
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɪk.li / (AmE), / ˈbrɪk.li / (BrE)
(dialectal or archaic) Easily broken or cracked; brittle.

Examples

A brickly surface
After years in the attic, the pages of the book had become brickly and yellowed.
Her grandmother warned that the heirloom vase was brickly and should be handled with care.
The old plate was so brickly that it snapped in two with barely any pressure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brittle - easily broken, cracked, or snapped; fragile
  • antonym: tough - strong and not easily broken or damaged
