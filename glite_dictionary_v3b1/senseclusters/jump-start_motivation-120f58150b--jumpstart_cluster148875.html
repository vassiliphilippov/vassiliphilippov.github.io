jump-start motivation

verb
renew or boost enthusiasm
3.3 6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʌmp stɑrt moʊˌtɪˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌmp stɑːt məʊˌtɪˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
to cause someone's enthusiasm or drive to become active, intense, or renewed, especially after a period of low motivation

Examples

To jump-start motivation quickly
A new project can often jump-start motivation among employees who feel stuck in a routine.
Attending a workshop helped her jump-start motivation for her creative writing.
The coach planned a surprise event to jump-start motivation before the big game.
