crankshaft

noun
shaft with cranks in an engine
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈkræŋkˌʃæft / (AmE), / ˈkraŋkʃɑːft / (BrE)
A shaft with one or more cranks, typically used in an engine to convert reciprocating motion into rotational motion or vice versa.

Examples

An engine crankshaft
A broken crankshaft can cause serious damage to an engine's internal parts.
Engineers designed a lighter crankshaft to improve fuel efficiency.
The crankshaft in my car needed to be replaced after the engine failed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: camshaft - shaft in an engine controlling valve openings
