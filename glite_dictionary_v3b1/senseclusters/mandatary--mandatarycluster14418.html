mandatary

noun
recipient of a mandate
4.7 8.5
Pronunciation: /mæn.ˈdeɪ.tər.iː/ /; / /ˈmæn.də.tər.iː/ (AmE), /mæn.ˈdeɪ.tə.riː/ /; / /ˈmæn.də.tə.riː/ (BrE)
A person or organization to whom a mandate is given; one who is authorized to act on behalf of another, especially in legal matters.

Examples

A mandatary signs documents
As a mandatary, she is authorized to sign documents on behalf of the organization.
The mandatary must carry out the tasks as specified in the legal agreement.
The responsibilities of a mandatary are strictly defined by law.
