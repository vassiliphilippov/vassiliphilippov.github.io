overstock

verb
stock excessively
2.9
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈstɑk / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈstɒk / (BrE)
to supply or fill a place with more goods or stock than is needed or can be sold

Examples

To overstock goods
If you overstock on perishable goods, you might end up with a lot of waste.
The store tends to overstock its shelves before the holiday season, hoping for high demand.
They realized they had overstocked their warehouse after sales dropped unexpectedly.
The store overstocked on winter coats and had to offer big discounts.
If you overstock your shelves, you might end up with unsold products.
Last year, they accidentally overstocked the warehouse with electronics.
