interwar

noun
period between wars
5.8
Pronunciation: /ɪn.tər.ˈwɔːr/ /; / /ɪn.tər.ˈwɑːr/ (AmE), /ɪn.tə.ˈwɔː/ (BrE)
the period of time between two wars, especially the years between World War I and World War II

Examples

The interwar period
Many artists flourished during the interwar as new styles emerged across Europe.
The interwar was marked by significant political and economic changes in many countries.
Historians often study the interwar to understand the causes of World War II.

Related Terms

  • antonym: postwar - the period after a war has ended
  • antonym: prewar - the period before a war begins
