counterfeit

adjective
not genuine; fake or imitative
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntərˌfɪt / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəfɪt / (BrE)
made to look exactly like something else, usually to deceive; not genuine.

Examples

Counterfeit goods
A counterfeit passport can be difficult for officials to detect without close inspection.
He was arrested for paying with counterfeit bills at the restaurant.
The store was fined for selling counterfeit designer handbags to unsuspecting customers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: authentic - of undisputed origin; genuine
  • synonym: fake - not genuine; made to look like something else
  • antonym: genuine - truly what something is said to be; authentic
