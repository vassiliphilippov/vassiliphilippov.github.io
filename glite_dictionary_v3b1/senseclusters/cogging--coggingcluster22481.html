cogging

noun
jerky machine motion
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑɡɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkɒɡɪŋ / (BrE)
a jerky or uneven motion in machinery caused by irregular magnetic forces or gear teeth engagement

Examples

Cogging in motors
The electric motor experienced cogging when operating at low speeds.
Engineers redesigned the gears to reduce cogging and improve performance.
Persistent cogging can lead to increased wear and noise in machinery.

Related Terms

  • antonym: smooth running - operation of machinery without interruptions or jerks
  • synonym: sticking - a situation where movement is hindered or stops suddenly
