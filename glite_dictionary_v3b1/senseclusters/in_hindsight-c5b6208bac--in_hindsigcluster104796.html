in hindsight

adverb
looking back with greater understanding
Pronunciation: / ɪn ˈhaɪndsaɪt / (AmE), / ɪn ˈhaɪndsaɪt / (BrE)
when considering or thinking about a past event after it has happened, with fuller knowledge or clearer understanding than was available at the time.

Examples

In hindsight now
In hindsight, investing in that company was a mistake.
In hindsight, we should have planned the trip more carefully.
She realized in hindsight that the warning signs were obvious.
