agentive

adjective
indicating the doer
7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈeɪ.dʒən.tɪv/ (AmE), /ˈeɪ.dʒən.tɪv/ (BrE)
describing or relating to the person or thing that performs an action, especially in grammar

Examples

Agentive suffix
The suffix '-er' in 'teacher' is an agentive ending that shows who does the action.
In many languages, agentive forms are used to create nouns for people who perform certain jobs.
The word 'runner' is an example of an agentive noun formed from a verb.
In the sentence 'She opened the door', 'she' is the agentive subject.
Linguists often discuss the agentive role when analyzing sentence structure.
The word 'runner' is formed with an agentive suffix to indicate someone who runs.

  • antonym: passive - indicating the receiver of an action
  • antonym: patient - the person or thing affected by an action
