assumedly

adverb
supposedly; without proof
6.7
Pronunciation: / əˈsuːmɪdli / (AmE), / əˈsjuːmɪdli / (BrE)
in a way that is believed to be true without definite proof

Examples

The meeting assumedly starts
The meeting will assumedly start at 10 a.m., but the schedule may change.
He is assumedly the most experienced member of the team, though no one has checked his background.
The package was assumedly delivered yesterday, but I have not received any confirmation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allegedly - according to what is claimed but not proven
  • antonym: certainly - without any doubt; definitely
  • synonym: supposedly - in a way that is thought to be true but not certain
