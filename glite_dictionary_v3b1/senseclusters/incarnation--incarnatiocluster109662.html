incarnation

noun
existence in a particular bodily form
4.5
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnˌkɑːrˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnˌkɑːˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a period of existence in a bodily form, especially as believed in some religions and philosophies involving rebirth or life after death

Examples

A new incarnation
According to tradition, the god returned in a new incarnation every millennium.
In his second incarnation, the philosopher believed he would become a wiser person.
She wondered what her next incarnation might hold if she believed in rebirth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reincarnation - the belief that the soul is reborn in another body after death
