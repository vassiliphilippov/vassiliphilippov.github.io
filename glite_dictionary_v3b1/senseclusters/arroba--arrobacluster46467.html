arroba

noun
unit of weight in Spanish-speaking countries
Pronunciation: / əˈroʊbə / (AmE), / əˈrəʊbə / (BrE)
A unit of weight used in Spain and some Spanish-speaking countries, traditionally equal to about 25 pounds (11.5 kilograms) but varying locally.

Examples

A arroba of olives
Local farmers still price their olives by the arroba at the regional market.
The shipment weighed over fifty arrobas, making it difficult to transport by hand.
Traditionally, an arroba was used to measure both liquids and dry goods in Spain.
