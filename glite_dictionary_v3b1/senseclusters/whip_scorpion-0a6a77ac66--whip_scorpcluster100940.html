whip scorpion

noun
tailed arachnid resembling a scorpion
Pronunciation: / wɪp ˈskɔːrpiən / (AmE), / wɪp ˈskɔːpiən / (BrE)
A nonvenomous arachnid similar to a scorpion but with a long, thin, whip-like tail and no stinger.

Examples

The whip scorpion animal
Despite its fierce appearance, the whip scorpion poses no threat to humans.
Researchers observed the whip scorpion using its tail to sense its environment.
The whip scorpion is often found in tropical forests, hiding under logs and rocks.
