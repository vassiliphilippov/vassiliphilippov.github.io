kettledrum

noun
large tunable orchestral drum
6.0 0.09/m

A large hemispherical drum, usually made of brass or copper, with a tunable drumhead that produces a definite pitch, commonly used in orchestras.

Examples

A kettledrum in orchestra
Each kettledrum in the orchestra is tuned to a specific note for the performance.
He marveled at the rich, deep sound of the kettledrum during the concert.
The percussionist struck the kettledrum with precision during the symphony's dramatic crescendo.

Synonyms

  • kettle
    8.6 0.19/m
    a kettle is the short, informal orchestral name for the tunable bowl-shaped drum, while a kettledrum is the full, standard name.
  • tympani
    8.4 0.09/m
    tympani is the orchestral name often used in scores for multiple tuned drums, while a kettledrum often names a single tunable bowl-shaped drum.
  • tympanum
    9.9 0.00/m
    a tympanum is a rare or technical singular name for the bowl-shaped tuned drum, while a kettledrum is the common orchestral term.
