potato mildew

noun
fungal disease of potatoes
Pronunciation: / pəˈteɪtoʊ ˈmɪlduː / (AmE), / pəˈteɪtəʊ ˈmɪldjuː / (BrE)
A disease affecting potato plants, caused by a type of fungus or mold, often resulting in blight or decay.

Examples

Potato mildew disease
Applying fungicides early in the season can help control the spread of potato mildew.
Farmers monitor their fields closely to prevent outbreaks of potato mildew.
The crops suffered extensive damage from potato mildew during the unusually damp summer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: potato blight - a disease of potatoes caused by Phytophthora infestans or similar fungi
