nationalization

noun
transfer of private assets to state ownership
Pronunciation: / ˌnæʃənələˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌnæʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnaʃnəlaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌnæʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of transferring a business, industry, or asset from private to state ownership or control

Examples

Nationalization of banks
After the nationalization of the banks, all financial transactions came under government control.
Many workers supported the nationalization of the coal industry in the 20th century.
The nationalization of the railway system was a major political decision.
He opposed the government's policy of nationalisation in key industries.
Many workers were uncertain about their futures after the sudden nationalisation of the factories.
The nationalisation of the railway system was completed in 1948.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nationalize - to transfer a business or industry from private to state ownership (verb)
  • antonym: privatisation - the transfer of ownership from the state to private individuals
  • antonym: privatization - the transfer of ownership from state to private hands, opposite of nationalization
