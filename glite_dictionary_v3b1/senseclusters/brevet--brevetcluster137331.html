brevet

verb
promote (military) without pay/privileges
9.8 8.3
Pronunciation: /ˈbre.vɪt/ /; / /ˈbre.vət/ (AmE), /ˈbre.vɪt/ (BrE)
(of a military authority) to promote a person to a higher military rank, usually without the corresponding pay or privileges of the new grade.

Examples

To brevet an officer
He was breveted to major for his exceptional service during the war.
The army often brevets junior officers in recognition of gallantry.
The general decided to brevet several officers after the successful campaign.

Related Terms

  • synonym: promote - to raise someone to a higher rank or position
