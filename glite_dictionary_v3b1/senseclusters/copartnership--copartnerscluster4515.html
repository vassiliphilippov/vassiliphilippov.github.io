copartnership

noun
business partnership with employee profit sharing
Pronunciation: / koʊˈpɑːrt.nər.ʃɪp / (AmE), / kəʊˈpɑːt.nə.ʃɪp / (BrE)
a business arrangement in which employees receive a share of the profits in addition to their regular wages

Examples

A copartnership business
After the introduction of copartnership, worker morale improved and staff turnover decreased.
Some business leaders believe that copartnership increases productivity and employee satisfaction.
The company operates under a copartnership system, allowing employees to benefit directly from its success.
