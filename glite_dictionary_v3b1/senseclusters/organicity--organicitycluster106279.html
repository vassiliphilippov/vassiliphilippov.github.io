organicity

noun
quality of being organic
Pronunciation: / ˌɔːrɡəˈnɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌɔːɡəˈnɪsəti / (BrE)
the quality of being organic or having the characteristics of a living organism

Examples

Organicity of life
The organicity of the ecosystem allows it to adapt to environmental changes.
Researchers discussed the organicity of the new artificial intelligence model.
Some critics questioned the organicity of the community's social structure.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inorganic - not consisting of or deriving from living matter
  • synonym: organism - a living thing with organized structure
