assortative

adjective
grouping by similarity
Pronunciation: / əˈsɔrtətɪv / (AmE), / əˈsɔːtətɪv / (BrE)
showing a tendency to group, pair, or associate with similar individuals or items

Examples

Assortative mating
The study found that assortative friendships are common among students with similar interests.
In genetics, assortative mating can influence the distribution of traits in a population.
Some neighborhoods show assortative patterns based on income or education levels.

Related Terms

  • antonym: random - without a specific pattern or preference
  • synonym: selective - choosing or preferring certain types or groups
