detainable

adjective
can be held in custody
Pronunciation: / dɪˈteɪnəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈteɪnəbəl / (BrE)
able to be kept in custody or held by authorities, especially by law enforcement.

Examples

Detainable by police
The new regulations define which offenses are considered detainable by the police.
Under the law, only certain crimes are detainable without a court order.
The judge ruled that the individual was not detainable based on the available evidence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arrestable - able to be arrested by authorities
  • antonym: releasable - able to be released from custody
