Able to dissolve in fats or oils, rather than in water.

Examples

Fat-soluble vitamin
A fat-soluble substance tends to accumulate in body fat rather than being excreted quickly.
Because it is fat-soluble, the medication should be taken with meals containing some oil.
Vitamin D is a fat-soluble vitamin essential for healthy bones.

Synonyms

  • lipophilic
    8.6 0.07/m
    lipophilic describes a chemical that dissolves or mixes easily in fats and oils, while fat-soluble names this everyday property.
  • oil-soluble
    5.8 0.01/m
    oil-soluble means a substance will dissolve in oil for creams or paints, while fat-soluble describes dissolving in fats.
  • oleophilic
    9.9 0.00/m
    oleophilic describes a strong attraction to oils, often meaning it soaks up or holds oil, while fat-soluble means it dissolves in fats.
