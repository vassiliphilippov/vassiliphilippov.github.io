hammer away at

verb
work persistently on something
Pronunciation: / ˈhæmɚ əˈweɪ æt / (AmE), / ˈhamə əˈweɪ at / (BrE)
to work persistently and with great effort on a task or problem, often over a long period

Examples

To hammer away at a problem
Despite setbacks, the team kept hammering away at the project until they succeeded.
Every morning, he would hammer away at the piano, practicing for hours.
She continued to hammer away at her thesis until every chapter was perfect.

Related Terms

  • antonym: give up - to stop making an effort; to quit
  • synonym: persevere - to continue in a course of action despite difficulty
