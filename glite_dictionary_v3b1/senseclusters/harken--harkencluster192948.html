harken

verb
(archaic) listen; pay attention
9.9 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / Ààh…ërk…ôn / (AmE), / Ààh…ëÀêk…ôn / (BrE)
(archaic) To listen attentively or give heed; often used in the imperative.

Examples

Harken to my words
Harken to the story I am about to tell.
If you would harken to his advice, you might succeed.
The leader called, "Harken!" and the crowd fell silent.
Hearken to my words, for they are spoken in wisdom.
If you hearken to the wind, you may learn secrets untold.
The people were asked to hearken unto the king's commands.

Synonyms

  • hark
    9.9 1.16/m
    hark is a quick command used to draw attention to a sound, while harken is an older, more formal imperative.
  • give heed
    6.4 0.11/m
    give heed is a formal phrase for paying careful attention in writing or speech, while harken is an archaic imperative.
  • hear
    1.0 156/m
    hear means to listen or perceive sound in general, while harken is an old-fashioned call to listen closely or pay heed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hearken - listen attentively (archaic spelling/variant)
  • synonym: listen - to give attention with the ear; to hear
