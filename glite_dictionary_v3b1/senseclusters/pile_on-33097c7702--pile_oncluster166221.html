pile on

verb
to give many excuses or reasons excessively
4.8 4.2
Pronunciation: / paɪl ɑn / (AmE), / paɪl ɒn / (BrE)
to add or increase something in an excessive or overwhelming way (such as excuses, remarks, or decorations)

Examples

To pile on excuses
It's pointless to pile on excuses when the mistake is obvious.
They piled on excuses for being late to the meeting.
Whenever he doesn't finish his work, he tends to pile on excuses.
During the holiday season, many people pile on more decorations than usual.
Management continued to pile on responsibilities without increasing salaries.
The coach decided to pile on extra drills before the big game.

Related Terms

  • synonym: heap on - to add or give a large amount of something
  • synonym: make excuses - to give reasons to justify or explain one's behavior, often insincerely
  • synonym: overload - to give someone too much of something, such as work or responsibility
