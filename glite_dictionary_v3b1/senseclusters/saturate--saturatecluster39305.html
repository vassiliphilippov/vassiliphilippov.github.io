saturate

verb
to soak or fill completely
Pronunciation: / ˈsætʃ.əˌreɪt / (AmE), / ˈsatʃ.əˌreɪt / (BrE)
to soak, fill, or infuse something so thoroughly that no more can be absorbed or added

Examples

To saturate a sponge
Please saturate the sponge with water before cleaning the table.
The artist likes to saturate her canvases with vivid colors.
The heavy rain began to saturate the soil, making the ground muddy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dry - make or become free from moisture
  • synonym: soak - to make thoroughly wet by immersing
