desolate

verb
to make a place empty or sparsely populated
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛsəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛsələt /; / ˈdɛsəleɪt / (BrE)
to make a place empty, lifeless, or ruined, especially by destroying or abandoning it

Examples

To desolate a village
Repeated droughts would eventually desolate the once thriving farmland.
The pandemic began to desolate the remote province, leaving it almost deserted.
The war threatened to desolate entire villages, driving many residents away.
Many forests were desolated by the wildfire, transforming green landscapes into barren land.
The invading army desolated the once-thriving villages, leaving nothing but ruins behind.
War not only displaces people but can also desolate entire regions for generations.

  • synonym: devastate - to destroy or ruin something completely
