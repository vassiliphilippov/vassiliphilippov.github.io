roundly

adverb
in a blunt or direct way
7.6 4.7
Pronunciation: /ˈraʊnd.liː/ /; / /ˈraʊnd.lɪ/ (AmE), /ˈraʊnd.liː/ /; / /ˈraʊnd.lɪ/ (BrE)
in a direct and thorough way, often with strong criticism or praise

Examples

To roundly condemn
He roundly condemned the proposal as impractical and expensive.
The author roundly dismissed the allegations in his statement.
The mayor was roundly criticized for her remarks during the debate.
The new policy was roundly criticized by experts across the country.
Her performance was roundly praised by the judges after the competition.
The plan was roundly rejected by almost everyone at the meeting.
