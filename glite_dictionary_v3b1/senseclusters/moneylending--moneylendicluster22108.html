moneylending

noun
lending money for interest
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌniˌlɛndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmʌniˌlɛndɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or business of lending money to others, usually for interest

Examples

Moneylending business
Moneylending was strictly regulated by the government.
He started a small moneylending business to help local shop owners.
Traditional moneylending often involved personal relationships and trust within the community.
The government introduced new regulations to control moneylending and protect vulnerable borrowers.
Illegal moneylending can lead to serious financial problems for those who cannot repay their debts.
She started a small moneylending business to help local entrepreneurs access quick loans.

Related Terms

  • synonym: banking - the business of managing money, including lending and borrowing
  • antonym: borrowing - the act of receiving something, especially money, with the intention of returning it
  • synonym: lending - the act of giving something to someone for a period of time
  • synonym: usury - the practice of lending money at unreasonably high interest rates
