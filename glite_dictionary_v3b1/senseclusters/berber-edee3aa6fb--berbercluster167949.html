Berber

noun
group of North African languages
Pronunciation: / ˈbɝːbər / (AmE), / ˈbɜːbə / (BrE)
a group of closely related languages and dialects historically spoken across North Africa, especially in Morocco and Algeria

Examples

The Berber language
He is fluent in both Berber and Arabic, reflecting the region's cultural diversity.
Linguists have studied the Berber languages to trace the history of North Africa.
Many traditional tales are still told in Berber across remote mountain villages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Afro-Asiatic - a large language family to which Berber languages belong
  • antonym: Arabic - the dominant language replacing Berber in much of North Africa
