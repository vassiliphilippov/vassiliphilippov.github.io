foggara

noun
underground water channel
Pronunciation: / fəˈɡɑːrə / (AmE), / fəˈɡɑːrə / (BrE)
an underground channel for transporting water in arid regions, especially in North Africa

Examples

An underground foggara
The ancient foggara provided a steady water supply to the desert village.
Farmers maintained the foggara to ensure their crops received enough irrigation during the dry season.
Tourists visited the oasis to see the impressive foggara system built centuries ago.

Related Terms

  • synonym: well - a deep hole or shaft dug to obtain water
