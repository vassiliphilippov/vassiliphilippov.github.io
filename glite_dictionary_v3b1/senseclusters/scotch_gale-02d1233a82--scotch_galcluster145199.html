Scotch gale

noun
fragrant bog shrub of the north temperate zone
7.2
Pronunciation: / skɑtʃ ɡeɪl / (AmE), / skɒtʃ ɡeɪl / (BrE)
a bog shrub native to the north temperate zone, known for its bitter-tasting, fragrant leaves (Myrica gale)

Examples

Scotch gale shrub
Herbalists have used Scotch gale for centuries because of its fragrant leaves.
The wetland was filled with the distinctive aroma of Scotch gale.
We spotted several bushes of Scotch gale growing by the lakeside.
