war cry

noun
yell to rally troops in battle
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔr ˌkraɪ / (AmE), / ˈwɔː ˌkraɪ / (BrE)
a loud shout, phrase, or slogan used to encourage or rally a group, especially before a battle or to support a cause

Examples

A war cry shouted
As the battle began, their war cry echoed across the valley.
Each tribe had its own distinctive war cry to rally the warriors.
The general's fierce war cry inspired his troops before the final charge.
Freedom was the war cry of the movement's supporters.
The candidate adopted 'Change Now!' as her war cry during the election campaign.
Their team's war cry echoed through the stadium, energizing the fans.

Related Terms

  • synonym: slogan - a short, memorable phrase used in advertising or publicity
