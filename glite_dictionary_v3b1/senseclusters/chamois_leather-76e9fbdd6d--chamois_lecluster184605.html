chamois leather

noun
soft leather for cleaning or polishing
7.5 5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈʃæm.iː ˈleð.ər/ /; / /ˈʃæm.ɪz ˈleð.ər/ /; / /ˈʃæm.wɑː ˈleð.ər/ (AmE), /ˈʃæm.iː ˈleð.ə/ /; / /ˈʃæm.ɪz ˈleð.ə/ /; / /ˈʃæm.wɑː ˈleð.ə/ (BrE)
A soft, pliable suede leather originally made from the skin of the chamois antelope but now usually from sheepskin, valued for its absorbency and gentle texture, commonly used for cleaning and polishing surfaces.

Examples

A chamois leather cloth
Always dry your hands with a chamois leather after washing to avoid water spots.
He used a chamois leather to polish the car until it gleamed.
The craftsman carefully wiped the lens with a clean chamois leather to prevent scratches.
