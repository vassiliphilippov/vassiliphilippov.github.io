spark gap

noun
device with electrodes for electrical spark
8.1
Pronunciation: / spɑrk gæp /; / spɑrk ɡæp / (AmE), / spɑːk ɡap / (BrE)
a device or space between two electrical conductors or electrodes where a spark can jump, used to control or measure electrical discharges

Examples

A spark gap device
A worn spark gap can cause misfiring in automobile engines.
Modern radios sometimes use a spark gap for high-voltage discharge protection.
The engineer inspected the spark gap to ensure it was clean for proper ignition.
A lightning strike can jump across a spark gap and damage equipment if not properly grounded.
Engineers inspected the spark gap in the old radio to ensure proper function.
When a spark gap is used, it allows excess voltage to safely discharge.
