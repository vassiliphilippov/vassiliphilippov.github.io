secondary emission

noun
electron emission caused by bombardment
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛkənˌdɛri ɪˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɛkəndəri ɪˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
the emission of electrons from a surface when it is struck by high-energy primary electrons or other charged particles

Examples

Secondary emission of electrons
Television tubes make use of secondary emission to amplify signals within certain components.
The phenomenon of secondary emission is important in the design of photomultiplier tubes.
When the metal target is struck by high-energy particles, secondary emission of electrons is observed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: electron emission - release of electrons from a material's surface
