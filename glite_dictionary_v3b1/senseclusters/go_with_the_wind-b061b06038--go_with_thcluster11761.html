go with the wind

verb
be flexible and adapt to changes
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ wɪð ðə wɪnd / (AmE), / ɡəʊ wɪð ðə wɪnd / (BrE)
to be flexible and adaptable; to act according to changing circumstances or let events take their course

Examples

To go with the wind
During difficult projects, it's often wise to go with the wind and adjust your plans as needed.
Rather than resist the changes at work, she decided to go with the wind.
When traveling in unfamiliar places, it's helpful to go with the wind and keep an open mind.

Related Terms
