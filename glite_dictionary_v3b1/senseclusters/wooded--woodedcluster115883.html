wooded

adjective
covered with trees
2.4 2.82/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆwÊŠdÉªd / (AmE), / ËˆwÊŠdÉªd / (BrE)
(of an area) covered with trees or woods; having many trees growing on it.

Examples

A wooded area
The wooded hills were especially beautiful in autumn.
They bought a house on a wooded lot outside the city.
We walked for miles through wooded countryside.

Synonyms

  • timbered
    4.2 0.04/m
    timbered is land that has growing trees and small woods, while wooded simply means covered with trees.
  • forested
    2.3 0.06/m
    forested describes land with large, often dense forests, while wooded can mean any area with trees but may be less dense.
  • woody
    2.0 0.29/m
    woody describes places or plants that have much wood or shrub growth, while wooded focuses on areas covered with trees.

Related Terms

  • antonym: barren - land with little or no vegetation
  • synonym: forest - large area covered chiefly with trees and undergrowth
