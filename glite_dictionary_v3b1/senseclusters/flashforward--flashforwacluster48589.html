flashforward

verb
to show future scene
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈflæʃˌfɔrwɚd / (AmE), / ˈflæʃˌfɔːwəd / (BrE)
to show or insert a brief scene that jumps ahead to future events in a film, story, or broadcast

Examples

To flashforward in time
The director decided to flashforward to the hero's old age in the middle of the movie.
Writers sometimes flashforward to create suspense about what will happen to the characters.
In the novel, the author flashforwards to reveal the outcome before returning to the present timeline.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flashback - to show a scene set in an earlier time than the main story
  • synonym: foreshadow - to give a hint or indication of a future event
