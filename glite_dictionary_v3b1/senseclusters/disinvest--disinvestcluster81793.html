disinvest

verb
withdraw investment; sell assets
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsɪnˈvɛst /; / dɪsɪnˈvɛst / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈvɛst /; / dɪsɪnˈvɛst / (BrE)
to withdraw or reduce investment, usually by selling shares or assets

Examples

To disinvest in companies
Activists urged the city council to disinvest in corporations tied to deforestation.
Several shareholders chose to disinvest their stakes during the financial downturn.
The university voted to disinvest from companies involved in fossil fuels.
After a detailed review, they decided to disinvest their money from underperforming stocks.
Following the new regulations, the company chose to disinvest from the fossil fuel sector.
Some pension funds are pressured to disinvest in companies that do not meet environmental standards.

Related Terms

  • synonym: divest - to sell off assets, especially for ethical reasons
  • antonym: invest - to put money into something in order to earn a profit
