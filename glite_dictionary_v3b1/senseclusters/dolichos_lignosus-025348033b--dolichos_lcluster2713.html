Dolichos lignosus

noun
South African vine with edible pods
9.9
Pronunciation: / doʊˈlaɪkoʊs lɪgˈnoʊsəs / (AmE), / dəˈlɪkəs lɪgˈnəʊsəs / (BrE)
A South African evergreen, partly woody vine cultivated for its clusters of rosy-purple flowers and edible bean-like pods; also used as green manure.

Examples

Dolichos lignosus plant
Dolichos lignosus is grown in many regions for its attractive flowers and edible pods.
Gardeners sometimes plant Dolichos lignosus as green manure to enrich the soil.
The glossy purple pods of Dolichos lignosus resemble snap beans when ripe.
