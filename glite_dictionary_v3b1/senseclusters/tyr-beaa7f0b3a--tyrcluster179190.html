Tyr

proper noun
Norse god of war and strife
9.9
Pronunciation: /tiːr/ /; / /tɪr/ (AmE), /tɪə/ /; / /tiː/ (BrE)
In Norse mythology, the god of war and strife, son of Odin, identified with the Anglo-Saxon god Tiu.

Examples

Tyr god of war
In Norse legends, Tyr is known for sacrificing his hand to bind the wolf Fenrir.
Many ancient warriors prayed to Tyr before going into battle.
Tuesday is named after Tyr in several Germanic languages.
