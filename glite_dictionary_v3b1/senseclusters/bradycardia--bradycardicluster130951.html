bradycardia

noun
abnormally slow heartbeat
Pronunciation: / ˌbræd.iˈkɑːr.di.ə / (AmE), / ˌbræd.ɪˈkɑː.di.ə / (BrE)
a condition in which the heart beats abnormally slowly, usually fewer than 60 beats per minute

Examples

Slow bradycardia heartbeats
Athletes sometimes experience bradycardia because their hearts are more efficient.
Some medications can cause bradycardia as a side effect, requiring regular heart monitoring.
The patient was diagnosed with bradycardia after his heart rate dropped below 60 beats per minute.

Related Terms

  • antonym: tachycardia - abnormally fast heartbeat
