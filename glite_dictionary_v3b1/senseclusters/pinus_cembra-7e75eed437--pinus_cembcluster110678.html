Pinus cembra

noun
European five-needled pine species
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪnəs ˈsɛmbrə / (AmE), / ˈpaɪnəs ˈsɛmbrə / (BrE)
A large European pine tree with clusters of five needles, producing edible seeds known as cembra nuts and a resinous exudate.

Examples

Pinus cembra tree
Locals gather cembra nuts from the Pinus cembra for culinary and ornamental uses.
The Swiss stone pine, known botanically as Pinus cembra, thrives in the Alps and Carpathians.
The durable wood of Pinus cembra has been valued for centuries in construction and carving.
