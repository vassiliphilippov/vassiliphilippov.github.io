interactionist

noun
social theory supporter
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈækʃənɪst / (AmE), / ˌɪntərˈækʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who studies or supports the theory that social interactions are key to understanding behavior or society

Examples

A interactionist theory
The interactionist argued that language development depends on social engagement.
As an interactionist, she focused on how group dynamics influence individual choices.
Many interactionists believe that everyday conversations shape our understanding of society.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sociologist - a person who studies society and social behavior
  • antonym: structuralist - a person who supports the theory that social structures shape behavior
