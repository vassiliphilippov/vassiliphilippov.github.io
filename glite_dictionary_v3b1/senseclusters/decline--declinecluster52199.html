decline

verb
become worse over time
1.8 3.1
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈklaɪn/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈklaɪn/ (BrE)
to become less, smaller, or worse in amount, quality, value, or health

Examples

Decline in health
After years of neglect, the old building began to decline rapidly.
Her health continued to decline despite the doctors' best efforts.
If economic conditions decline, unemployment rates may rise.
After the scandal, public trust in the company declined significantly.
Attendance at the conference is expected to decline next year if fees are not reduced.
Sales of traditional newspapers have declined steadily over the past decade.
Housing prices are expected to decline over the next year.
If demand falls, the value of the currency will decline accordingly.
The company's profits began to decline sharply in the second quarter.

  • antonym: improve - to become or make better
  • antonym: increase - to become larger in amount, number, or degree
  • antonym: rise - to increase in amount or value
  • synonym: worsen - to become worse
