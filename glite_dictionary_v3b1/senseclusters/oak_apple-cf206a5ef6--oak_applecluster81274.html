oak apple

noun
round gall on oaks caused by wasps
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊk ˌæpəl / (AmE), / əʊk ˈæpəl / (BrE)
a round gall that grows on oak trees, caused by the larvae of certain gall wasps

Examples

An oak apple gall
An oak apple can often be seen hanging from the branches of English oak trees in early summer.
Historically, ink was sometimes made from crushed oak apples mixed with iron salts.
She pulled a small, spongy oak apple from the tree to show her students.
