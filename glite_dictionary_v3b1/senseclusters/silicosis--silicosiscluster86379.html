silicosis

noun
lung disease from inhaling silica dust
5.8 7.1
Pronunciation: /sɪl.ɪ.ˈkoʊ.sɪs/ /; / /sɪl.ə.ˈkoʊ.sɪs/ (AmE), /sɪl.ɪ.ˈkəʊ.sɪs/ /; / /sɪl.ə.ˈkəʊ.sɪs/ (BrE)
A lung disease caused by inhaling fine particles of silica, quartz, or slate, leading to inflammation and scarring of the lungs.

Examples

Lung silicosis disease
Chronic exposure to silica dust in construction can lead to silicosis over time.
He attended a seminar on occupational hazards, focusing on diseases like silicosis.
The miner was diagnosed with silicosis after years of working without proper protective equipment.

  • synonym: pneumoconiosis - lung disease caused by inhalation of various dusts
