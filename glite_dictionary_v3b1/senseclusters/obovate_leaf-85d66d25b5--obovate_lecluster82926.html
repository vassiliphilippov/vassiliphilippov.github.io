obovate leaf

noun
egg-shaped leaf, narrow end at base
Pronunciation: / oʊˈboʊ.veɪt lif / (AmE), / əʊˈvəʊ.veɪt liːf / (BrE)
A leaf that is egg-shaped with the narrower end at the base.

Examples

An obovate leaf shape
Several shrubs in this region have a distinctly obovate leaf form.
The obovate leaf of the plant helps distinguish it from related species with other leaf shapes.
You can identify the tree by observing its obovate leaf and smooth bark.

Related Terms
