unhusked

adjective
with husk intact
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈhʌskt / (AmE), / ʌnˈhʌskt / (BrE)
not having had the outer covering or husk removed, especially from grains or seeds

Examples

The unhusked rice
The farmer stored the unhusked rice in large sacks after the harvest.
You can buy unhusked barley at the local organic market.
Some traditional recipes call for unhusked grains to add extra texture.

Related Terms

  • antonym: husked - having had the outer covering or husk removed
  • synonym: unprocessed - not having been processed or refined
