dogger

noun
Jurassic rock layer
Pronunciation: / ˈdɔɡər / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡə / (BrE)
the middle division of the Jurassic period in European geology, or the sedimentary rocks formed during that time

Examples

Dogger rock layer
The quarry revealed a thick layer of dogger rich in ironstone fossils.
Geologists study the dogger to understand the environment of the middle Jurassic period.
Many important minerals have been discovered in the dogger formations across Europe.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: liassic - the lower division of the Jurassic period
  • degree higher: malm - the upper division of the Jurassic period
