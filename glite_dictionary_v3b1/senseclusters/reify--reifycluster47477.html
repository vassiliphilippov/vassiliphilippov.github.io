reify

verb
treat as real or concrete
Pronunciation: / ˈriˌaɪfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈriːɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to consider or treat an abstract idea or concept as if it were real or concrete

Examples

To reify an idea
Critics argue that such models reify subjective experiences, limiting our understanding.
It's dangerous to reify emotions and treat them as fixed objects.
The theory tends to reify social constructs, making them seem unchangeable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: objectify - to treat something abstract as a physical object
