awkwardness

noun
lack of grace or ease in movement
4.9 3.0
Pronunciation: /ˈɔː.kwərd.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈɔː.kwəd.nəs/ (BrE)
the quality of being clumsy, unskillful, or lacking grace in movement or actions

Examples

Physical awkwardness
He tried to hide his awkwardness by moving slowly and carefully across the room.
Her awkwardness on the dance floor was obvious to everyone watching.
Teenagers often struggle with physical awkwardness as their bodies change and grow.
Her awkwardness with the tools made the job take twice as long as expected.
The awkwardness of the new dancers was obvious during their first performance.
There was a certain awkwardness in the way he handled the paintbrush for the first time.
His awkwardness made it difficult for him to start a conversation at the party.
She could sense the awkwardness in the room after she mentioned the topic.
There was an obvious awkwardness in his posture during the interview.
