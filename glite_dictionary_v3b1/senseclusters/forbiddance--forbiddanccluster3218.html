forbiddance

noun
the act of forbidding; a prohibition
5.3 6.6
Pronunciation: /fər.ˈbɪd.əns/ /; / /fɔːr.ˈbɪd.əns/ (AmE), /fə.ˈbɪd.əns/ /; / /fɔː.ˈbɪd.əns/ (BrE)
the act or state of officially forbidding something; a prohibition or order not to do something

Examples

Official forbiddance
Despite his father's forbiddance, Tim snuck out to join his friends.
Her forbiddance was clear: no one was to enter the laboratory after hours.
The school's forbiddance of mobile phones was strictly enforced.
Despite the forbiddance, many people continued to access the restricted site.
The government issued a forbiddance on all unauthorized gatherings in the city.
The priest's stern forbiddance of alcohol was respected by the entire community.
