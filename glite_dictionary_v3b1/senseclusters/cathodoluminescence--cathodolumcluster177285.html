cathodoluminescence

noun
electron-induced light emission
9.9
Pronunciation: /kæ.θə.doʊ.luː.mɪ.ˈnes.əns/ (AmE), /kæ.θə.dəʊ.luː.mɪ.ˈnes.əns/ (BrE)
the emission of light from a material when it is struck by an electron beam, often used to study minerals or materials

Examples

Cathodoluminescence emission
Researchers observed cathodoluminescence to analyze the composition of the gemstone.

Researchers rely on cathodoluminescence to study defects in semiconductor materials.
