caller

noun
investor buying a call option
1.7 7.0
Pronunciation: /ˈkɔː.lər/ /; / /ˈkɑː.lər/ (AmE), /ˈkɔː.lə/ (BrE)
an investor who buys a call option on a security or commodity, giving them the right to purchase it at a specified price within a certain period

Examples

Caller buys options
A caller profits if the price of the underlying asset increases beyond the strike price.
Options trading can be risky for a caller if the asset's price does not rise as anticipated.
The caller exercised his option to buy shares at a lower price when the market rose.
