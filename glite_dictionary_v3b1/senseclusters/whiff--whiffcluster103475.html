whiff

verb
strike out in baseball by missing the pitch
6.5
Pronunciation: / wɪf / (AmE), / wɪf / (BrE)
to miss or fail to hit something, especially by swinging and missing (often in sports, especially baseball)

Examples

To whiff the ball
He has a tendency to whiff under pressure, striking out at key moments.
She whiffed three times in the game, missing every third pitch she faced.
The batter whiffed on the final pitch and the game was over.
The batter whiffed on the last pitch and lost the game for his team.
He swung hard but completely whiffed the tennis ball.
During practice, she kept whiffing and grew frustrated with her performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: miss - to fail to hit, catch, or reach something
  • antonym: strike - to hit something forcefully
  • synonym: strike out - (in baseball) to be put out by failing to hit three pitches
