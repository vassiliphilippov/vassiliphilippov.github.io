tract of land

noun
large area of land for a specific use
Pronunciation: / trækt əv lænd / (AmE), / trakt əv land / (BrE)
A large area of land, especially one intended for a particular use or purpose.

Examples

A tract of land for housing
Developers bought a tract of land on the city's outskirts for new housing.
The farmer owns a vast tract of land used exclusively for growing wheat.
The wildlife reserve covers a protected tract of land rich in biodiversity.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: estate - a large area of land, often with a grand house
  • synonym: parcel of land - a distinct, often smaller, piece of land
  • degree lower: plot - a small piece of land, especially for building or gardening
