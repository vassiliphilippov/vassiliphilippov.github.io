catalysis

noun
acceleration of chemical reaction by a catalyst
3.2 5.5
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈtæl.ə.sɪs/ (AmE), /kə.ˈtæl.ə.sɪs/ (BrE)
the increase in the rate of a chemical reaction caused by a substance (the catalyst) that is not consumed in the reaction

Examples

Biological catalysis
Biological catalysis by enzymes is essential for life.
Industrial chemists rely on catalysis to speed up processes that would otherwise occur too slowly.
The discovery of new methods of catalysis has revolutionized pharmaceutical manufacturing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: enzyme - a protein that acts as a biological catalyst
  • antonym: inhibition - decrease in the rate of a chemical reaction
