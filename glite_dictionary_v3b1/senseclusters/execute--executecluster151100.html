execute

verb
carry out; put into effect
Pronunciation: / ˈɛksɪˌkjut /; / ˈɛksɪˌkjuːt / (AmE), / ˈɛksɪkjuːt / (BrE)
to carry out or perform a plan, task, or action

Examples

To execute a plan
She was chosen to execute the board's decision with great precision.
The company will execute the plan next month after all preparations are complete.
The contractor executed the project exactly as specified in the contract.
The chef carefully executes each step of the recipe to ensure perfection.
The engineer executed the project plan exactly as designed.
The gymnast executed a flawless routine during the competition.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abandon - to stop carrying out a plan, order, or course of action
  • synonym: accomplish - to achieve or complete successfully
  • antonym: fail - to not achieve or perform as planned
  • synonym: implement - to put a plan or decision into effect
  • synonym: perform - to carry out an action or task
