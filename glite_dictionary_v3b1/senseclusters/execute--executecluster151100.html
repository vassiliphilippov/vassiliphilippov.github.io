to carry out or perform a plan, task, or action

Examples

To execute a plan
She was chosen to execute the board's decision with great precision.
The company will execute the plan next month after all preparations are complete.
The contractor executed the project exactly as specified in the contract.
The chef carefully executes each step of the recipe to ensure perfection.
The engineer executed the project plan exactly as designed.
The gymnast executed a flawless routine during the competition.

Synonyms

  • carry out
    1.8 31/m
    carry out means doing a plan or task step by step, while execute sounds more formal and often means to perform a planned action.
  • perform
    1.3 108/m
    perform means doing an action, often showing skill or in public, while execute is a neutral, formal word for carrying out a plan.
  • implement
    3.7 78/m
    implement means putting a plan or decision into effect in practice, while execute means to carry out or perform a specific action.
  • fulfill
    2.8 18/m
    fulfill means meeting a requirement or promise, while execute means carrying out an action or plan, not just meeting expectations.
  • accomplish
    2.6 64/m
    accomplish means successfully finish something, often after effort, while execute means to carry out or perform the steps of a plan.
  • carry through
    3.7 0.09/m
    carry through means completing something despite difficulty or doubt, while execute means carrying out a plan or action without that struggle.
  • do
    1.0 1183/m
    do is a very general verb for performing actions in everyday speech, while execute is more formal and often used for planned tasks.

Related Terms
