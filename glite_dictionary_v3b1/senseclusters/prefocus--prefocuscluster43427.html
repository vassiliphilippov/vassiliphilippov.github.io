prefocus

verb
to set focus in advance
6.9
Pronunciation: /priː.ˈfoʊ.kəs/ /; / /prɪ.ˈfoʊ.kəs/ (AmE), /priː.ˈfəʊ.kəs/ /; / /prɪ.ˈfəʊ.kəs/ (BrE)
to adjust the focus of a camera or optical device in advance, before taking a photograph or before the subject arrives

Examples

To prefocus the lens
The photographer prefocused the lens to capture the perfect moment as soon as the bird landed.
Before the ceremony began, she was prefocusing her camera on the aisle.
He always prefocuses his equipment to avoid missing any action during sports events.

Related Terms

  • synonym: focus - to adjust a camera or device to make an image clear
