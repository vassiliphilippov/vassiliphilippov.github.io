Levant cotton

noun
species of Old World cotton plant
9.8
Pronunciation: /lə.ˈvænt ˈkɑː.tən/ /; / /lə.ˈvænt ˈkɔː.tən/ /; / /lɪ.ˈvænt ˈkɑː.tən/ (AmE), /lɛ.ˈvænt ˈkɒt.ən/ /; / /lɪ.ˈvænt ˈkɒt.ən/ (BrE)
A species of cotton plant native to the Old World, with heart-shaped leaves and large seeds covered in short, greyish lint that is hard to remove; considered an ancestor of modern short-staple cottons.

Examples

Levant cotton plant
Levant cotton is noted for its short-staple fibers that are difficult to remove from the seeds.
Farmers in the Mediterranean region used to grow Levant cotton before the introduction of new varieties.
The short greyish lint of Levant cotton distinguishes it from other cotton species.
