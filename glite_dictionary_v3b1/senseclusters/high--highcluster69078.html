high

adjective
of considerable height or elevation
1.0 272/m
Pronunciation: / haÉª / (AmE), / haÉª / (BrE)
at or having a considerable distance from the ground or base to the top; having great elevation or rising far above the ground or level surface

Examples

A high mountain
The high mountain was covered in snow even during the summer months.
The room felt spacious thanks to its high ceilings and large windows.
Their apartment is on a high floor, offering an impressive view of the city.

Synonyms

  • tall
    1.0 71/m
    tall is used for people or buildings with greater than average height, while high describes general distance above the ground.
  • elevated
    3.9 0.04/m
    elevated is often a formal word for something placed above ground level, while high is more common and general.
  • towering
    4.0 0.02/m
    towering suggests something impressively tall and standing above surrounding things, while high only means raised above the ground.
  • soaring
    3.9 0.00/m
    soaring describes a thing that rises smoothly or flies far above others and feels grand, while high is a simple height word.
  • lofty
    5.6 1.20/m
    lofty sounds formal and noble and often names grand buildings or ideas, while high only indicates height above the ground.
  • skyscraping
    4.4 0.00/m
    skyscraping is an extreme, sometimes figurative word for very great height or amount, while high is an everyday height term.

Related Terms

  • antonym: low - of little height from bottom to top or to the ground
  • synonym: tall - of more than average height, especially of a person or structure
