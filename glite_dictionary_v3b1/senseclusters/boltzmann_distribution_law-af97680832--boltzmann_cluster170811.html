Boltzmann distribution law

noun
energy distribution law in gases (physics)
Pronunciation: / ˈboʊltsmən dɪˌstrɪbjuˈʃən lɑ / (AmE), / ˈbɒltsmən dɪsˌtrɪbjuːˈʃən lɔː / (BrE)
a law in physics stating how energy is distributed among the molecules of a gas in thermal equilibrium, with higher energies being less probable than lower ones

Examples

The Boltzmann distribution law explains energy
In statistical mechanics, the Boltzmann distribution law is essential for describing particle energy probabilities.
Students must understand the Boltzmann distribution law to solve problems related to heat and energy distribution in gases.
The Boltzmann distribution law explains why most molecules in a gas have average energies rather than extremely high or low energies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Maxwell–Boltzmann distribution - the specific probability distribution of particle energies in a gas
