fibromatosis

noun
fibrous tissue tumor
Pronunciation: / ˌfaɪbroʊməˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌfɪbrəʊməˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a medical condition where benign (non-cancerous) tumors made of fibrous tissue grow in the body, which can invade nearby tissue and may return after treatment

Examples

Fibromatosis tumor growth
The patient was diagnosed with fibromatosis after a biopsy of the growth in his arm.
Treatment for fibromatosis often involves surgery, but the tumor can sometimes come back.
Doctors monitor cases of fibromatosis closely because of its tendency to invade nearby tissues.
The surgeon diagnosed the lump as fibromatosis after reviewing the biopsy results.
Some types of fibromatosis can recur even after surgical removal.
Patients with fibromatosis may experience swelling or discomfort in the affected area.

Related Terms

  • synonym: desmoid tumor - a type of fibromatosis that is benign but locally aggressive
  • synonym: fibroma - a benign tumor made of fibrous tissue
  • antonym: fibrosarcoma - a malignant tumor of fibrous tissue
  • antonym: sarcoma - a malignant tumor of connective or other non-epithelial tissue
