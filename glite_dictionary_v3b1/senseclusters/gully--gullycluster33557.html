gully

verb
to erode into channels
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈɡʌli / (AmE), / ˈɡʌli / (BrE)
to form deep channels or grooves in land by the action of flowing water

Examples

Rain gullies the land
Heavy rainfall can cause hillsides to start gullying rapidly, threatening nearby roads.
If the land is not protected, water will gully the fields and damage crops.
The old path was gullied so deeply that it became difficult to walk along.
After the storm, the heavy rain gullied the dirt road, making it difficult to drive.
Years of flooding have gullied the farmland, leaving deep trenches in the fields.
If water is not managed properly, it can gully the hillside and cause soil loss.

Related Terms

  • antonym: build up - to accumulate or increase in amount
  • synonym: erode - to gradually wear away soil, rock, or land
  • synonym: wash away - to remove or destroy by the action of water
