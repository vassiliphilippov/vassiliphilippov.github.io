contingently

adverb
by chance
7.3
Pronunciation: / kənˈtɪndʒəntli / (AmE), / kənˈtɪndʒəntli / (BrE)
in a way that depends on something else happening, on chance, or on circumstances

Examples

Decide contingently on results
The outcome of the experiment was determined contingently on several unpredictable factors.
Their meeting occurred contingently when both visited the museum on the same day.
Funding for the project was provided contingently, depending on the results of the initial phase.
The bonus will be paid contingently upon the project's successful completion.
Access to the restricted area is contingently granted based on security clearance.
The agreement is contingently valid if both parties sign before the deadline.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accidentally - in a way that happens by chance, not on purpose
  • synonym: conditionally - in a way that depends on certain conditions
  • antonym: necessarily - in a way that must happen or is required
