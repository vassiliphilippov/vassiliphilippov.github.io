close call

noun
a narrow escape or near miss
Pronunciation: / kloʊs kɔl / (AmE), / kləʊs kɔːl / (BrE)
a situation where something bad or dangerous almost happens but is narrowly avoided

Examples

A close call
I made my flight, but it was a close call after the heavy traffic.
That was a close call; the car almost hit us at the intersection.
The team won the game, but it was a close call in the final minutes.
It was a close call when the ball just missed breaking the window.
That car almost hit us—it was a close call but everyone is okay.
We had a close call with the storm, but the worst of it passed us by.

Related Terms

  • synonym: near miss - a situation in which something almost happened, especially something dangerous
