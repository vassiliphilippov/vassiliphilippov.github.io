blocked or heavily obstructed, especially so that nothing can pass through; clogged up.

Examples

A choked drain
A drain choked with leaves can easily overflow during heavy rain.
He noticed the machine had stopped working because the filter was choked with dust.
The highway was choked with rush hour traffic this morning.

Synonyms

  • blocked
    1.7 0.00/m
    blocked is completely shut so movement cannot pass, while choked suggests a heavy, sudden obstruction that blocks flow.
  • clogged
    2.8 0.27/m
    clogged means filled with material so flow is slow or stopped, while choked describes extreme blockage that prevents passage.
  • plugged
    2.6 0.00/m
    plugged is blocked often by a single object or plug, while choked implies a broad, heavy obstruction of the whole passage.
  • stopped up
    5.1 0.02/m
    stopped up is used for noses or pipes when air or water cannot pass, while choked sounds stronger and more complete.
  • occluded
    8.0 0.00/m
    occluded is a formal medical term for a closed passage, while choked is a common word for severe blockage.
