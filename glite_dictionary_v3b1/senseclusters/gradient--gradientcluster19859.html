Examples

Synonyms

  • slope
    3.2 9.62/m
    a slope is a surface that rises or falls across the land, while a gradient refers to the measured steepness of that surface.
  • steepness
    3.3 0.23/m
    steepness describes how sharply a hill or road rises, while a gradient is the technical measure often used by engineers.
  • grade
    4.6 2.07/m
    a grade is the percent or rise used on roads and maps, while a gradient is the more general measure of steepness.
  • inclination
    6.3 0.93/m
    an inclination is the angle from a reference plane in math or physics, while a gradient names a surface's steepness in general.
  • slant
    3.4 1.18/m
    a slant is a tilt or leaning often used for objects or views, while a gradient is the measured steepness of a slope.
