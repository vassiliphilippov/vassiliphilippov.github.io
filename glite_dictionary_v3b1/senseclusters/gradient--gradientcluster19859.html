gradient

noun
slope or incline of a surface or line
Pronunciation: / ˈɡreɪdiənt / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪdiənt / (BrE)
the degree to which a surface or a line slopes away from the horizontal; a measure of steepness

Examples

Steep gradient
Engineers measured the gradient of the railway track before construction began.
The hikers climbed a steep gradient to reach the top of the hill.
The road has a gentle gradient, making it suitable for cyclists.

Related Terms

  • synonym: incline - an upward or downward slope
  • synonym: slope - the steepness of a line or surface
