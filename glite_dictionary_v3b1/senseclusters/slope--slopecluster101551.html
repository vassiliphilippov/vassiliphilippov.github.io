slope

verb
to be at an angle
5.4 1.5
Pronunciation: /sloʊp/ (AmE), /sləʊp/ (BrE)
(of a surface or line) to be at an angle away from a horizontal or vertical position.

Examples

The ground slopes down
Beyond the garden, the land slopes down to a river.
The driveway slopes gently toward the street.
The roof sloped so that rainwater could run off easily.

Related Terms

  • antonym: level - to make or become flat or even
  • synonym: tilt - to move or cause to move into a sloping position
