reboot

noun
restart or new version
3.7 0.70/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàriÀêÀåbuÀêt / (AmE), / ÀàriÀêÀåbuÀêt / (BrE)
an act of starting something again, especially a computer or a television or film series

Examples

A reboot of the show
After several years off the air, the network announced a reboot of the classic sitcom.
A quick reboot of your computer can often solve minor technical issues.
Fans were excited when the movie franchise received a modern reboot with new actors.

Synonyms

  • restart
    2.3 4.53/m
    restart is the usual verb for starting a computer or program again after it stops, while reboot often names that same act.
  • relaunch
    3.9 0.59/m
    relaunch is used to start a product, service, or campaign again after failure or change, while reboot usually means restarting a system or series.
