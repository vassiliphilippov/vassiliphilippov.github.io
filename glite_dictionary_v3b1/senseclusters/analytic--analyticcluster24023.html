analytic

adjective
using algebra/calculus to solve problems
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.əˈlɪt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌan.əˈlɪt.ɪk / (BrE)
(of a method, especially in mathematics) using algebra and calculus to solve problems rather than geometric or numerical methods.

Examples

Analytic method in math
His expertise lies in analytic number theory, which relies heavily on calculus.
In analytic geometry, problems are approached using analytic techniques rather than purely geometric constructions.
The analytic method allows us to solve these equations systematically.

Related Terms

  • antonym: synthetic - (of a mathematical method) proceeding by combining separate elements; opposed to analytic in mathematics
