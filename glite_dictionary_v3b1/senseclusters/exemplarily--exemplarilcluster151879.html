exemplarily

adverb
as a warning
7.7
Pronunciation: / ɪɡˈzɛmpləˌrɪli / (AmE), / ɪɡˈzɛmpləlɪli / (BrE)
in a way that serves as a warning or example to others, often to deter similar actions.

Examples

Exemplarily punished worker
The company fined the employee exemplarily to show that dishonesty would not be tolerated.
He was punished exemplarily so that others would think twice before breaking the rules.
The court acted exemplarily to set a strong precedent for future cases.

Related Terms

  • antonym: leniently - in a way that is not strict or harsh
  • synonym: punitively - in a way intended as punishment
