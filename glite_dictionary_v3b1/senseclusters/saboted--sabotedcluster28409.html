saboted

adjective
with a sabot
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪboʊtɪd / (AmE), / ˈsæbətɪd / (BrE)
fitted with or using a sabot, especially referring to ammunition designed to be fired with a supporting device

Examples

Saboted round
The saboted round allowed the tank to fire lighter projectiles at higher speeds.
Engineers tested several saboted bullets to improve the weapon's performance.
A saboted shell can travel farther and penetrate armor more effectively than a standard shell.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unsaboted - not fitted with a sabot
