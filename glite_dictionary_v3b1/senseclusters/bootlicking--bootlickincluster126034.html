bootlicking

noun
act of sycophantic flattery
8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈbuːt.lɪk.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈbuːt.lɪk.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
the act, practice, or culture of behaving obsequiously or with excessive flattery toward people in authority in order to gain favor.

Examples

Office bootlicking
The report criticized the company's pervasive bootlicking culture that rewarded flattery over actual skill.
During the meeting, his constant bootlicking toward the director made colleagues roll their eyes.
She refused to join the bootlicking and insisted promotions should be based on merit alone.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sycophancy - excessive flattery or obsequious behaviour toward someone to gain advantage
