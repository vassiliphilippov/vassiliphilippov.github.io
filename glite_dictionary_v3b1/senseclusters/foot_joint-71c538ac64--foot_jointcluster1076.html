foot joint

noun
end section of a concert flute
6.8
Pronunciation: / fʊt dʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / fʊt dʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
the last of the three sections of the Western concert flute, typically containing the lowest tone holes and keys

Examples

The foot joint of flute
She noticed a small dent on the foot joint that was affecting her instrument's sound.
Some flute models allow players to use a B foot joint for extra low notes.
The technician carefully dismantled the foot joint to clean the inside of the flute.
