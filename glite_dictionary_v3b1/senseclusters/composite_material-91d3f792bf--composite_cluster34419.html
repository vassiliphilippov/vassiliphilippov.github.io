composite material

noun
material of bonded mixed fibers
3.7
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpɑzɪt məˈtɪriəl / (AmE), / kəmˈpɒzɪt məˈtɪəriəl / (BrE)
a strong, lightweight substance made by chemically bonding together fibers of more than one kind, used in engineering and manufacturing

Examples

Lightweight composite material
Aircraft manufacturers increasingly use composite material to build lightweight, fuel-efficient airplanes.
Engineers selected a composite material for the bridge to reduce weight without sacrificing durability.
The tennis racket is made from a composite material that combines carbon fiber and resin for extra strength.
