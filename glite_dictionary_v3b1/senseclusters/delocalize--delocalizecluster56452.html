delocalize

verb
to spread out electrons
8.6
Pronunciation: / diˈloʊkəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / diˈləʊkəlaɪz / (BrE)
to cause something, especially electrons, to become spread out rather than confined to a specific location

Examples

To delocalize electrons
In aromatic compounds, chemists often delocalize electrons to explain their stability.
The process can delocalize charge across the entire molecule, affecting its reactivity.
Advanced materials may delocalize electrons to improve their electrical conductivity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: localize - to confine something to a specific place
