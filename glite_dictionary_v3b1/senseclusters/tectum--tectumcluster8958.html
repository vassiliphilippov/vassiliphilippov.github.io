tectum

noun
dorsal protective plate
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛktəm / (AmE), / ˈtɛktəm / (BrE)
a protective dorsal plate or covering on an animal, often forming part of an exoskeleton

Examples

Crab's tectum
The crab's armored tectum protected it from predators along the rocky shore.
Scientists studied the tectum of the insect to understand how its exoskeleton developed.
Damage to the tectum can leave some animals vulnerable to environmental hazards.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carapace - the hard upper shell of a turtle, crustacean, or arachnid
  • antonym: plastron - the nearly flat part of the shell structure of a turtle, what it rests on
