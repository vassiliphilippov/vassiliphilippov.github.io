annual

noun
plant that lives for one year
Pronunciation: / ˈænjuəl / (AmE), / ˈanjʊəl / (BrE)
A plant that completes its entire life cycle, from germination to seed production, within one year and then dies.

Examples

A garden annual
Because annuals complete their life cycle in one season, they need to be replanted each year.
Many gardeners plant annuals like marigolds and petunias for vibrant summer color.
She prefers annuals because they bloom more profusely than most perennials.

Related Terms

  • antonym: biennial - a plant that completes its life cycle in two years
  • antonym: perennial - a plant that lives for more than two years
