gall-berry

noun
evergreen North American holly shrub
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɔlˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈɡɔːlˌbɛri / (BrE)
An evergreen holly native to eastern North America, with oblong, leathery leaves and small black berries.

Examples

The gall-berry plant
Bees often collect nectar from gall-berry flowers to produce flavorful honey.
The gall-berry is a common shrub in the pine forests of the southeastern United States.
The leathery leaves of the gall-berry make it easy to identify in the wild.
