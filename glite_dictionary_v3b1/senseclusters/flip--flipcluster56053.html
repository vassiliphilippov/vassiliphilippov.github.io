flip

verb
suddenly lose temper or composure
3.9
Pronunciation: / flɪp / (AmE), / flɪp / (BrE)
(informal) to suddenly lose control of your emotions, become very angry, excited, or surprised

Examples

To flip out
He flipped at the meeting when he found out about the changes.
If you keep provoking him, he's going to flip sooner or later.
She completely flipped when she saw the mess in the kitchen.
I nearly flipped when I found out I had won the competition.
She flipped when she saw her favorite band walking down the street.
They flipped with joy upon receiving the good news.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm down - to become less excited or upset; to relax after being emotional
  • synonym: explode - suddenly lose one's temper violently
  • synonym: freak out - to become or cause someone to become extremely emotional or excited
  • antonym: keep one's cool - to remain calm
  • synonym: lose it - to suddenly become very angry or out of control
