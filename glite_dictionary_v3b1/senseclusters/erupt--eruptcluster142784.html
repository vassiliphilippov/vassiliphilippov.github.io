erupt

verb
release suddenly and forcefully (emotion, energy)
1.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˈrʌpt / (AmE), / ɪˈrʌpt / (BrE)
to start or break out suddenly and forcefully, especially of emotion, violence, or events

Examples

Erupt in anger
After hours of tension, he erupted in anger at the unfair decision.
Grief can erupt unexpectedly, catching you off guard in quiet moments.
She suddenly erupted in laughter at his joke, startling everyone in the room.
A heated argument erupted between the two colleagues during the meeting.
Laughter erupted from the audience as the comedian delivered the punchline.
Violence erupted on the streets after the controversial verdict was announced.
Tensions erupt between the two countries whenever there is a border dispute.
The volcano began to erupt, sending ash into the sky.
When the baby's first tooth started to erupt, she became fussy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: break out - to start suddenly (especially of war, violence, or trouble)
  • synonym: explode - to burst or shatter violently, especially with noise
  • synonym: flare up - to suddenly begin or become more intense
  • antonym: subside - to become less intense or violent
