Pinus sylvestris

noun
species of pine tree native to Europe and Asia
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪ.nəs sɪlˈvɛs.trɪs / (AmE), / ˈpaɪ.nəs sɪlˈvɛs.trɪs / (BrE)
A species of pine tree native to northern Europe and Asia, having two needles per bundle and distinctive flaky red-brown bark.

Examples

Pinus sylvestris tree
Botanists studied the unique bark patterns of Pinus sylvestris trees found in Siberia.
The timber from Pinus sylvestris is prized for its strength and durability.
The vast forests of northern Europe are dominated by Pinus sylvestris.
