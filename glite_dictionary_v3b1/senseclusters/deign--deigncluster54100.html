deign

verb
do something beneath one's dignity

to do something that one considers to be beneath one's dignity, often reluctantly or with condescension

Examples

To deign to help
He wouldn't deign to eat at such an ordinary restaurant.
She barely deigned to glance at the documents handed to her.

Synonyms

  • condescend
    8.1 0.16/m
    condescend shows clear superiority and scorn, while deign means doing something you see as beneath your dignity, often reluctantly.
  • lower oneself
    6.5 0.14/m
    lower oneself means doing something that makes you lose respect, while deign means doing it reluctantly and with a feeling of superiority.
  • descend
    7.4 0.66/m
    descend describes lowering your standards to do something you think is beneath you, while deign adds an idea of proud reluctance.
  • stoop
    8.1 0.70/m
    stoop means to lower yourself morally by doing something dishonorable, while deign emphasizes reluctant, condescending action rather than moral failure.
