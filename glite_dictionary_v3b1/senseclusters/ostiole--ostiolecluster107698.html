ostiole

noun
small pore for spore release in algae and fungi
8.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɑstiˌoʊl / (AmE), / ˈɒstɪəʊl / (BrE)
a small opening or pore, especially one in the reproductive structures of certain algae and fungi, through which spores are released

Examples

A ostiole pore
In many types of mold, the ostiole allows spores to escape into the air.
Scientists observed the algae reproducing by releasing spores from each ostiole.
The fungal fruiting body discharged its spores through an ostiole at the top.
