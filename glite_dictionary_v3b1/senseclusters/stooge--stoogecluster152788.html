stooge

verb
act as a subordinate or comic assistant
8.5 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / stuÀêd í / (AmE), / stuÀêd í / (BrE)
to act as a subordinate or assistant, often for someone else's benefit (especially in a comic or obedient role)

Examples

To stooge for others
He grew tired of always having to stooge for the stars without getting much credit.
In the sketch, Mike had to stooge along with the main performer, drawing laughs from the audience.
She was often asked to stooge for the lead comedian during live shows.
He didn't mind stooging for the director as long as he gained experience.
She refused to stooge for anyone, insisting on making decisions herself.
Some politicians are accused of stooging for lobbyists behind the scenes.

Synonyms

  • support
    4.5 4.83/m
    support is a professional helper on stage who backs another performer, while stooge is an obedient, comic assistant serving someone else's benefit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assist - help someone, especially in a performance
  • antonym: independent - acting on one's own authority, not subordinate or compliant
  • synonym: support - to act in a supporting role, especially in performance
  • synonym: toady - to act in an overly obedient or servile way to someone important
