splat

verb
to hit and flatten on impact, making a wet noise
7.8
Pronunciation: / splæt / (AmE), / splæt / (BrE)
to make a flat, soft, and often wet noise or mark when hitting a surface and flattening on impact

Examples

To splat on the ground
He slipped on the wet floor and splatted onto his back.
The paintball splatted against the window, leaving a colorful smear.
Water balloons often splat on the pavement, bursting as they hit the ground.
Raindrops splat loudly on the windowpane during the storm.
She accidentally stepped on a grape and it splat beneath her shoe.
The water balloon splat against the sidewalk, spraying everyone nearby.

Related Terms

  • synonym: smack - to make a sharp slap or strike noise
  • synonym: squash - to crush something so that it becomes flat or soft
  • synonym: squish - to make a soft, squelching sound
  • degree lower: thud - a dull sound as of a heavy object falling or striking
