underexposure

noun
insufficient publicity or attention
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndɚɪkˈspoʊʒɚ / (AmE), / ˌʌndərɪkˈspəʊʒə / (BrE)
insufficient attention or publicity given to something or someone, causing them to be little known

Examples

Media underexposure
Media underexposure meant that the event failed to attract a large audience.
Some musicians complain that underexposure is a bigger problem than bad reviews.
The artist struggled with underexposure early in her career, despite her considerable talent.
