demultiplexer

noun
data stream separator
9.5
Pronunciation: /diː.mʌl.tɪ.ˈplek.sər/ /; / /dɪ.mʌl.tɪ.ˈplek.sər/ (AmE), /dɪ.mʌl.tɪ.ˈplek.sə/ /; / /diː.mʌl.tɪ.ˈplek.sə/ (BrE)
a device or program that takes a single input and splits it into multiple separate outputs or channels

Examples

A demultiplexer device
The demultiplexer splits the incoming signal into separate audio and video channels for further processing.
Engineers installed a demultiplexer to manage the distribution of data streams in the network.
A demultiplexer is essential for separating combined signals in digital communication systems.
The demultiplexer is essential for routing signals to different parts of the circuit.
Engineers used a demultiplexer to separate the data stream into individual channels.
A demultiplexer can direct a single input to one of many outputs based on control signals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decoder - a device that converts coded data into its original form
  • antonym: multiplexer - a device that combines multiple signals into one
  • synonym: switch - a device for making and breaking the connection in an electric circuit
