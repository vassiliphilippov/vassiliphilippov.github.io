crane

verb
stretch the neck to see better
Pronunciation: / kreɪn / (AmE), / kreɪn / (BrE)
to stretch or extend your neck in order to see something more clearly or from a better position

Examples

To crane your neck
I had to crane my neck to see the parade floats coming down the street.
She craned over the heads of the crowd to catch a glimpse of her favorite actor.
The children were craning their necks to look out the classroom window.
He was craning his neck to catch a glimpse of the parade over the crowd.
The children craned forward to see what was happening on the stage.
She cranes her neck every morning to look out the high window.

Related Terms

  • synonym: peer - to look closely or with difficulty
  • synonym: strain - to make a great effort to see or hear something
