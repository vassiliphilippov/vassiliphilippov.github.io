Avalokitesvara

noun
bodhisattva of compassion in Buddhism
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˌvoʊloʊˌkitɛsˈvɑrə / (AmE), / əˌvɒləkɪˌteɪsˈvɑːrə / (BrE)
In Mahayana Buddhism, a celestial bodhisattva embodying the compassion of all Buddhas, often depicted as male in Indian traditions and widely venerated throughout Asia.

Examples

Avalokitesvara bodhisattva
Legends describe Avalokitesvara as a compassionate figure listening to the world's cries for help.
Many followers pray to Avalokitesvara for guidance and protection.
The statue of Avalokitesvara stood at the entrance to the temple, garlanded with flowers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Guanyin - the female form of Avalokitesvara in Chinese Buddhism
