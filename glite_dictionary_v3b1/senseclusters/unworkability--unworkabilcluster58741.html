unworkability

noun
difficulty to process
8.7
Pronunciation: /ʌn.wɜːr.kə.ˈbɪl.ə.tiː/ /; / /ən.wɜːr.kə.ˈbɪl.ə.tiː/ (AmE), /ʌn.wɜːk.ə.ˈbɪl.ə.tiː/ /; / /ən.wɜːk.ə.ˈbɪl.ə.tiː/ (BrE)
the quality of being difficult or impossible to shape, cut, or process, especially in materials or substances

Examples

Unworkability of metal
The unworkability of the new alloy made it unsuitable for manufacturing car parts.
Engineers had to find a solution to the unworkability of the material before production could continue.
Due to its unworkability, the substance was rarely used in industrial applications.
