vitalistic

adjective
belief in vital force
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌvaɪtəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌvaɪtəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to the belief that living things are driven by a special vital force distinct from physical or chemical processes

Examples

Vitalistic theory
The scientist rejected the vitalistic explanation for the origin of life.
Some alternative medicine practices are based on vitalistic principles.
Her vitalistic worldview influenced her approach to health and wellness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mechanistic - relating to the view that natural processes are mechanically determined
  • synonym: vitalism - the doctrine that life is driven by a vital force
