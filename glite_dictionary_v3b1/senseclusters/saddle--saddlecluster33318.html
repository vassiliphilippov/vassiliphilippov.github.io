saddle

verb
assign a burdensome responsibility
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈsæd.əl /; / ˈsædəl / (AmE), / ˈsæd.əl /; / ˈsædəl / (BrE)
to give someone a difficult or unwelcome responsibility or burden

Examples

To saddle someone with work
I don't want to saddle you with extra work just before your vacation.
She was unfairly saddled with all the blame for the failed event.
They saddled me with the project after my manager resigned.
He was saddled with his brother's debts after the business failed.
She felt saddled by expectations she could never meet.
The new manager didn't want to saddle his team with unnecessary paperwork.

Related Terms

  • synonym: burden - to load with a heavy responsibility or duty
  • synonym: encumber - to burden or weigh down
  • antonym: unburden - to relieve of a burden or responsibility
