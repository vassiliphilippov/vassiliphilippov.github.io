teer

noun
tar-like substance
Pronunciation: / tɪr / (AmE), / tɪə / (BrE)
a dark, sticky substance similar to tar or pitch, used for waterproofing or sealing, especially in dialectal speech

Examples

Black teer coating
The workers applied teer to the wooden planks to protect them from water damage.
Old boats were often coated with teer to keep them from leaking.
A strong smell of teer filled the air as the roof was being repaired.
