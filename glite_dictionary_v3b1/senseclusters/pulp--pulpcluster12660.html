pulp

noun
inexpensive magazine on poor-quality paper
Pronunciation: / pʌlp / (AmE), / pʌlp / (BrE)
a magazine or publication printed on cheap, low-quality paper, typically featuring sensational or popular stories

Examples

Pulp magazine
Collecting rare pulps has become a hobby for some enthusiasts.
He was a fan of old science fiction pulps from the 1930s.
The detective stories were first serialized in popular pulp magazines.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tabloid - a newspaper with a compact format, often with sensational content
