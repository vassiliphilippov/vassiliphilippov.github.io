semiconducting material

noun
substance with intermediate electrical conductivity
6.9 3.8
Pronunciation: /se.mɪ.kən.ˈdʌk.tɪŋ mə.ˈtɪr.iː.əl/ (AmE), /se.mɪ.kən.ˈdʌk.tɪŋ mə.ˈtɪə.ri.əl/ (BrE)
A substance, such as germanium or silicon, whose electrical conductivity is between that of a metal and an insulator, and increases with temperature and the presence of impurities.

Examples

Semiconducting material properties
Adding impurities to a semiconducting material can significantly change its electrical properties.
Researchers are developing new semiconducting materials that could improve solar cell efficiency.
Silicon is the most commonly used semiconducting material in electronic devices.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conductor - A material with high electrical conductivity
  • antonym: insulator - A material with very low electrical conductivity
  • synonym: semiconductor - A substance with electrical conductivity between that of a conductor and an insulator
