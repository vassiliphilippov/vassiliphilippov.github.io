transcortical aphasia

noun
type of brain-induced language disorder
6.3 8.4
Pronunciation: /træn.ˈskɔːr.tɪ.kəl ə.ˈfeɪ.ʒə/ /; / /træn.ˈskɔːr.tɪ.kəl ə.ˈfeɪ.zɪ.ə/ (AmE), /træn.ˈskɔː.tɪ.kəl ə.ˈfeɪ.ʒə/ /; / /træn.ˈskɔː.tɪ.kəl ə.ˈfeɪ.zɪ.ə/ (BrE)
a type of aphasia caused by damage to areas of the brain outside Broca's or Wernicke's areas, affecting language abilities

Examples

Transcortical aphasia symptoms
Studying cases of transcortical aphasia has deepened our understanding of brain networks involved in language processing.
The patient was diagnosed with transcortical aphasia after suffering a stroke that spared Broca's and Wernicke's areas.
Unlike other forms, transcortical aphasia often preserves repetition ability despite impairments in spontaneous speech.
