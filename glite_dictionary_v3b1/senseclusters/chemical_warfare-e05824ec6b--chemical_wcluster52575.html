chemical warfare

noun
use of chemicals as weapons in war
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˌkɛmɪkəl ˈwɔrˌfɛr / (AmE), / ˌkɛmɪkəl ˈwɔːˌfeə / (BrE)
the use of poisonous chemical substances as weapons in war to kill, injure, or incapacitate people

Examples

Chemical warfare weapons
During World War I, chemical warfare caused devastating injuries to thousands of soldiers.
Strict treaties now ban chemical warfare due to its indiscriminate and inhumane effects.
The international community has condemned the use of chemical warfare in recent conflicts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biological warfare - use of bacteria, viruses, or other organisms as weapons in war
  • antonym: conventional warfare - use of traditional weapons in war (not chemical, biological, or nuclear)
