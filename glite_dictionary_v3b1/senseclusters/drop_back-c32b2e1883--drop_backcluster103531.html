drop back

verb
move to a position at the rear
3.7 3.0
Pronunciation: /drɑːp bæk/ /; / /drɔːp bæk/ (AmE), /drɒp bæk/ (BrE)
to move to a position further behind others, either deliberately (as in sports or formations) or by losing ground

Examples

To drop back in football
The quarterback will drop back to pass after the snap.
The striker decided to drop back and help in defense.
When the team was attacked, the soldiers dropped back to regroup.
After leading the race for two laps, he began to drop back and finished in third place.
If you drop back too far, you might lose sight of the others.
When hiking with the group, Sarah would often drop back to take photos of the scenery.

Related Terms

  • antonym: catch up - to move faster in order to reach the same position as others
  • synonym: fall back - to retreat or move backward, especially in a military sense
  • synonym: fall behind - to fail to keep up with others; to lag or be left behind
