high-density lipoprotein

noun
cholesterol-transporting blood protein
4.1 0.12/m
Pronunciation: / ha…™ Ààd…õns…ôti Àåla…™p…ôÀàpro ätin / (AmE), / ha…™ Ààd…õns…™ti Àål…™p…ôÀàpr…ô ätiÀên / (BrE)
a substance in the blood that transports cholesterol, composed of a high proportion of protein and associated with lower risk of heart disease

Examples

Increase high-density lipoprotein
A diet rich in healthy fats can increase high-density lipoprotein in the bloodstream.
Doctors recommend monitoring your high-density lipoprotein levels to assess your heart health.
Researchers are studying how high-density lipoprotein helps remove cholesterol from arteries.

Synonyms

  • HDL
    3.2 3.30/m

  • alpha-lipoprotein
    7.2 0.00/m
    alpha-lipoprotein is an older scientific name found in some texts, whereas high-density lipoprotein is the modern, common term.
