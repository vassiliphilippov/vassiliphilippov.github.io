cosmologically

adverb
in relation to the universe
9.2
Pronunciation: /kɑːz.mə.ˈlɑː.dʒɪ.kəl.i/ (AmE), /kɒz.mə.ˈlɒdʒ.ɪ.kəl.i/ (BrE)
in a way that relates to the universe as a whole, its origin, structure, or history

Examples

The universe cosmologically vast
The universe is cosmologically vast, containing billions of galaxies.
Some questions can only be answered cosmologically, not by everyday science.
The model is cosmologically important for explaining the early stages of the universe.
The scientist explained the phenomenon cosmologically, considering the origins of the entire universe.
Some questions can only be answered cosmologically, not by looking at individual planets.
When viewed cosmologically, the lifespan of a star is just a brief moment.
The age of the solar system is quite young cosmologically compared to the universe itself.
Some changes on Earth are insignificant cosmologically but important on a human timescale.
Black holes are fascinating objects to study cosmologically because they reveal much about the universe's history.
