exclusionist

noun
one who excludes others
8.2
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈskluːʒənɪst / (AmE), / ɪkˈskluːʒənɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports excluding certain groups or individuals from a community, organization, or activity

Examples

An exclusionist leader
The new law was supported by every exclusionist in the city council.
Critics argued that the policy favored exclusionist attitudes toward immigrants.
As an exclusionist, he opposed allowing outsiders to join the club.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inclusionist - a person who supports including all groups or individuals
  • synonym: segregationist - a person who supports separating people based on group differences
