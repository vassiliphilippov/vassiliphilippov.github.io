totalize

verb
combine parts into a total
Pronunciation: / ˈtoʊtəˌlaɪz /; / ˈtoʊ.t̬ə.laɪz / (AmE), / ˈtəʊtəlaɪz /; / ˈtəʊ.tə.laɪz / (BrE)
to add up or combine different parts to form a total, or to combine or sum up elements into a single amount, entity, or concept.

Examples

To totalize the numbers
It's difficult to totalize all the financial data from last year.
The philosopher aimed to totalize competing concepts into a single theory.
We need to totalize our efforts if we want to achieve these results.
The accountant is totalizing all the expenses for the annual report.

Can we totalise these different perspectives into one coherent philosophy?
It is difficult to totalise the data when the sources are so varied.
The theory seeks to totalise individual experiences into a unified system.
