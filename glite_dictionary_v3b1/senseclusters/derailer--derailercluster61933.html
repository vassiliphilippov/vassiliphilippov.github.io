derailer

noun
one who disrupts progress
Pronunciation: / dɪˈreɪlər / (AmE), / dɪˈreɪlə / (BrE)
a person who causes a plan, discussion, or process to fail or become distracted, either intentionally or unintentionally

Examples

A meeting derailer
The team struggled to stay on topic because of a persistent derailer in the group.
Every project has at least one derailer who distracts others with unrelated issues.
As a manager, she learned how to handle a derailer during important meetings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disruptor - a person who interrupts or disturbs an activity or process
  • antonym: facilitator - a person who makes a process easier or helps it progress
  • synonym: obstructor - a person who blocks or hinders progress
