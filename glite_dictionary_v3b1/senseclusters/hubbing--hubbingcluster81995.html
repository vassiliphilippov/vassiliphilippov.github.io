hubbing

noun
centralized routing
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌbɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhʌbɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of organizing transportation or services so that they pass through a central hub or main point

Examples

Hubbing in transport
Many airlines rely on hubbing to efficiently connect passengers through major airports.
The company improved delivery times by implementing a hubbing system for its logistics network.
Public transportation systems often use hubbing to coordinate routes through central stations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: distribution - the process of delivering goods or services to various locations
  • synonym: networking - the act of connecting different points or people in a system
