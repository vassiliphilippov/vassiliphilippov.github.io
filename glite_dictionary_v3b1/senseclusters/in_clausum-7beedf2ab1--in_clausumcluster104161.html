in clausum

noun
entry into private property
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪn ˈklaʊzəm / (AmE), / ɪn ˈklaʊzəm / (BrE)
a Latin legal term meaning 'into a closed place', used in property law to refer to entering private property without permission

Examples

In clausum principle
The judge cited the principle of in clausum when discussing the boundaries of private land.
Understanding in clausum is important for students studying historical property law.
The old statute referenced in clausum to define unlawful entry onto another's land.

Related Terms

  • synonym: trespass - the act of entering someone's property without permission
