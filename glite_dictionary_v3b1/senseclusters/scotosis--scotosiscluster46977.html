scotosis

noun
mental blindness
Pronunciation: / skoʊˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / skəʊˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a mental blindness or inability to understand or perceive certain ideas or truths

Examples

Intellectual scotosis
The philosopher discussed how scotosis can hinder people from accepting new scientific ideas.
Overcoming scotosis is essential for personal growth and understanding.
His scotosis made it difficult for him to see the flaws in his own argument.
