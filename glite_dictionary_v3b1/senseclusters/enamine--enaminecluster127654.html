enamine

noun
chemical compound with -C=C-N- linkage
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɛnəˌmiːn / (AmE), / ˈiːnəmiːn / (BrE)
a chemical compound containing an amine group attached to a carbon-carbon double bond, with the linkage -C=C-N-.

Examples

Enamines in chemistry
Enamines are commonly used in organic synthesis due to their reactivity.
Reaction with an aldehyde can produce an enamine as an intermediate.
The chemist synthesized a new enamine as part of the experiment.
