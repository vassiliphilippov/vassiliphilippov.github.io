symmetry

noun
exact correspondence of parts on opposite sides
1.8 3.3
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪm.ə.triː/ /; / /ˈsɪm.ɪ.triː/ (AmE), /ˈsɪm.ə.triː/ /; / /ˈsɪm.ɪ.triː/ (BrE)
the quality of having parts that are identical or match each other in shape, size, or arrangement, especially on opposite sides of a line or around a center

Examples

Perfect symmetry
Architects often use symmetry to create aesthetically pleasing building designs.
In mathematics, a circle has complete symmetry about its center.
The butterfly's wings display remarkable symmetry on each side of its body.
Artists often strive for symmetry when designing patterns or sculptures.
Butterfly wings are known for their almost perfect symmetry.
The symmetry of the building made it especially pleasing to the eye.

Related Terms

  • antonym: asymmetry - lack of symmetry; absence of equal arrangement on opposite sides
  • synonym: balance - an even distribution of weight or elements
  • synonym: proportion - the relationship of sizes or amounts between parts
