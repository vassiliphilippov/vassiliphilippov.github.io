palmitin

noun
an ester of glycerol and palmitic acid
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈpæl.mɪ.tɪn / (AmE), / ˈpæl.mɪ.tɪn / (BrE)
an ester formed from glycerol and palmitic acid, commonly found in animal and vegetable fats

Examples

Palmitin in fats
Palmitin is the major component of palm oil and contributes to its solid texture at room temperature.
During soap making, palmitin reacts with lye to form soap and glycerol.
Researchers analyzed the palmitin content in various food products to assess their nutritional profiles.
