clownish

adjective
silly or comical like a clown
Pronunciation: / ˈklaʊnɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈklaʊnɪʃ / (BrE)
acting or appearing in a silly, foolish, or comical way like a clown

Examples

Clownish behavior
His clownish behavior at the meeting made everyone laugh but annoyed his boss.
She wore a clownish costume with oversized shoes and a red nose to the party.
The actor's clownish expressions made the children giggle in delight.

Related Terms

  • synonym: buffoonish - acting in a foolish or clown-like manner
  • antonym: serious - not joking or playful, thoughtful and earnest
  • synonym: zany - very silly or foolish, absurdly comical
