inspiration

noun
divine influence in religious context
3.4
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnspəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnspɪˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
(Theology) A special influence of a divine power on the minds of people, especially as believed to occur in the writing of sacred texts.

Examples

Divine inspiration
Early church leaders debated the nature of inspiration in religious writings.
He prayed for inspiration before composing his sermon.
Many theologians regard the Bible as the product of divine inspiration.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: intuition - the act of knowing without rational reasoning
  • synonym: revelation - the making known of divine truth or will
