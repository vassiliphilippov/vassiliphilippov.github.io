musical drama

noun
opera genre with equal emphasis on music and drama
2.6 5.4
Pronunciation: / ˈmjuːzɪkəl ˈdrɑːmə / (AmE), / ˈmjuːzɪkəl ˈdrɑːmə / (BrE)
An opera genre in which music and dramatic elements are of equal importance, and the music closely reflects the action.

Examples

The musical drama genre
Many scholars argue that the musical drama reached its peak during the late 19th century.
The concept of musical drama emphasizes that neither the music nor the drama should dominate.
Wagner revolutionized opera by creating the musical drama, blending music and narrative seamlessly.
