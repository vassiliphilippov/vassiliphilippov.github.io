Dirac

proper noun
English physicist who predicted antimatter
Pronunciation: / dɪˈræk / (AmE), / dɪˈræk / (BrE)
(Paul Adrien Maurice Dirac, 1902–1984) An English theoretical physicist known for his fundamental contributions to quantum mechanics and for predicting the existence of antimatter.

Examples

Dirac equation
Dirac was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1933 for his work on quantum theory.
Many advanced physics lectures refer to equations originally developed by Dirac.
The prediction of the positron by Dirac profoundly influenced the development of modern physics.
