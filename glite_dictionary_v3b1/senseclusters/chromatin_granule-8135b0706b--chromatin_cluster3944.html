chromatin granule

noun
small structure in cell nucleus with DNA and proteins
Pronunciation: / ˈkroʊ.mə.tɪn ˈɡræn.jul / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊ.mə.tɪn ˈɡræn.juːl / (BrE)
A small, stainable structure found in the cell nucleus, composed of DNA, RNA, and proteins, which condenses into chromosomes during cell division.

Examples

A chromatin granule in nucleus
During mitosis, each chromatin granule condenses to help form chromosomes.
The scientist stained the sample to observe the chromatin granule structures more clearly.
Under the microscope, a chromatin granule is visible within the nucleus of the cell.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chromatin - the material of which chromosomes are made; consists of DNA, RNA, and proteins
  • degree higher: chromosome - a threadlike structure of nucleic acids and protein carrying genetic information
