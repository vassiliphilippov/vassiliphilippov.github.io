Tsouic language

noun
group of Formosan languages in Taiwan
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtsoʊ.ɪk ˈlæŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈtsəʊ.ɪk ˈlaŋ.ɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
A group of Formosan languages spoken in Taiwan, comprising Tsou, Kanakanabu, and Saaroa.

Examples

Tsouic language family
Kanakanabu is a member of the Tsouic language family, along with Tsou and Saaroa.
Many speakers of the Tsouic language group live in the mountainous regions of central Taiwan.
The Tsouic language group is studied extensively by linguists specializing in Austronesian languages.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Formosan language - any indigenous language of Taiwan
