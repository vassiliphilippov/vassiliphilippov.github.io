Passamaquody

noun
member of an Algonquian people of Maine and New Brunswick
Pronunciation: / ˌpæsəməˈkwɑːdi / (AmE), / ˌpæsəməˈkwɒdi / (BrE)
a member of an Algonquian Indigenous people living in northeastern Maine, USA, and New Brunswick, Canada, closely related to the Maliseet.

Examples

A Passamaquody elder
A Passamaquody elder shared stories of his ancestors with the local community.
The Passamaquody have a long tradition of basket weaving and fishing.
Today, many members of the Passamaquody nation continue to preserve their heritage and language.
