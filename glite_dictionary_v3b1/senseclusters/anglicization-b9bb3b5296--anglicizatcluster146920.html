Anglicization

noun
the process of making English in form or character
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˌæŋɡlɪsəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌæŋɡlɪsaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something English in form or character, especially in language, custom, or appearance

Examples

The Anglicization of names
His accent showed the Anglicization that came from years of living in London.
The Anglicization of foreign names often leads to changes in pronunciation and spelling.
The Anglicization of local traditions has been a subject of debate among historians.
