flickery

adjective
unsteady or fluttering
6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈflɪ.krə.iː/ /; / /ˈflɪk.ə.riː/ (AmE), /ˈflɪ.krə.iː/ /; / /ˈflɪk.ə.riː/ (BrE)
moving or shining in a quick, unsteady, or wavering way, especially describing light that changes rapidly

Examples

A flickery light
The candlelight was flickery as the wind blew through the open window.
She watched the flickery shadows dance across the wall in the evening.
The old television screen had a flickery image that made it hard to watch.
The old television had a flickery screen that made it hard to watch movies.
A flickery streetlamp cast moving shadows on the empty road at night.
She read her book by the flickery light of a candle during the power outage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flickering - shining with a quick, unsteady movement
  • synonym: fluttering - moving with quick, light, and unsteady motions
  • antonym: steady - not changing, moving, or shaking
