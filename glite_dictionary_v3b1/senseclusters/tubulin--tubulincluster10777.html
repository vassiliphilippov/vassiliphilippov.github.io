tubulin

noun
cellular protein
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtuːbjəlɪn / (AmE), / ˈtjuːbjʊlɪn / (BrE)
a protein that forms the main structural component of microtubules in cells

Examples

Tubulin protein structure
Scientists discovered that tubulin is a key building block of microtubules in animal cells.
The stability of tubulin affects how cells divide and maintain their shape.
Researchers use fluorescent markers to observe tubulin dynamics during cell division.
Scientists study how tubulin assembles to form the microtubules inside living cells.
The drug works by binding to tubulin and preventing cell division in cancer cells.
Mutations in tubulin genes can lead to problems with nerve cell development.

Related Terms

  • synonym: actin - a protein that forms filaments in cells, important for cell movement
  • synonym: microtubule - a cylindrical structure in cells made of tubulin proteins
