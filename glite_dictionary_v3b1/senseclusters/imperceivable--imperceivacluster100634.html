imperceivable

adjective
impossible to detect
Pronunciation: / ˌɪmpərˈsiːvəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪmpəˈsiːvəbəl / (BrE)
impossible to be seen, noticed, or detected

Examples

An imperceivable difference
The difference between the two paintings was imperceivable to most viewers.
Any movement in the quiet room was so slight it was almost imperceivable.
The sound was so faint that it became imperceivable to the human ear.

Related Terms

  • synonym: imperceptible - impossible or very difficult to notice or sense
  • antonym: obvious - easily seen, noticed, or understood
