coronal consonant

noun
consonant pronounced with front of tongue
7.9
Pronunciation: / kəˈroʊnəl ˈkɑnsənənt / (AmE), / kəˈrəʊnəl ˈkɒnsənənt / (BrE)
(phonetics) a consonant sound articulated with the flexible front part of the tongue, such as t, d, s, or n.

Examples

Coronal consonant sounds
Children often master coronal consonant sounds like /n/ and /s/ early in language development.
Linguists study the differences between coronal consonant sounds and those produced farther back in the mouth.
The English sound /t/ is a typical coronal consonant pronounced with the tip of the tongue near the teeth.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: apical consonant - coronal consonant produced with the tongue tip
  • antonym: dorsal consonant - consonant articulated with the back of the tongue
  • antonym: labial consonant - consonant articulated with the lips
  • degree higher: laminal consonant - coronal consonant produced with the tongue blade
