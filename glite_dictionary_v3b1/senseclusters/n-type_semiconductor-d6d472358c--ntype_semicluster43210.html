A semiconductor in which electrons are the primary charge carriers responsible for electrical conduction, typically created by doping with elements that have more valence electrons than the host material.

Examples

n-type semiconductor material
Most electronic devices use both p-type and n-type semiconductors to function properly.
The n-type semiconductor layer is crucial for forming a diode with a p-type layer.
When a silicon crystal is doped with phosphorus, it becomes an n-type semiconductor.
