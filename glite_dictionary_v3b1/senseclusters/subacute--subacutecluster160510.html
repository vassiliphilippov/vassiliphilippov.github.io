subacute

adjective
moderately severe (medical term)
Pronunciation: / sʌbəˈkjuːt / (AmE), / sʌbəˈkjuːt / (BrE)
(in medicine) less severe than acute but not as prolonged as chronic; describing a disease or condition that is present with mild or no symptoms.

Examples

A subacute illness
After an acute phase, the illness often progresses to a subacute stage with subtler signs.
The doctor considered the possibility of subacute bacterial endocarditis due to the patient's persistent fatigue.
The patient was diagnosed with a subacute infection, showing only mild symptoms over a few weeks.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: acute - sudden and severe (medical term)
  • degree lower: chronic - persisting for a long time or constantly recurring (medical term)
