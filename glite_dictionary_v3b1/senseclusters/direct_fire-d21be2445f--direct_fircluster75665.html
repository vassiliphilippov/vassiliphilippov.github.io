direct fire

noun
military fire aimed at visible target
Pronunciation: / dəˈrɛkt ˈfaɪɚ / (AmE), / dəˈrɛkt ˈfaɪə / (BrE)
Military fire delivered at a target within the shooter's line of sight, usually using flat-trajectory weapons.

Examples

Direct fire weapons
In urban combat, direct fire provides immediate and accurate support against threats.
Soldiers are trained to identify targets suitable for direct fire.
The tank used direct fire to destroy the enemy bunker.

Related Terms

  • antonym: indirect fire - fire delivered at targets not directly visible to the shooter
