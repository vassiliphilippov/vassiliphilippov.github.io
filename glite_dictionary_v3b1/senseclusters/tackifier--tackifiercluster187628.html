tackifier

noun
adhesive additive
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈtækɪˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈtækɪˌfaɪə / (BrE)
a substance added to adhesives to increase their stickiness or ability to bond surfaces

Examples

A tackifier additive
The manufacturer added a tackifier to the glue to improve its performance on smooth surfaces.
Without a tackifier, the adhesive would not stick well to plastic or metal.
Scientists are developing a new tackifier that is both strong and environmentally friendly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adhesive - a substance used for sticking objects together
  • antonym: solvent - a substance that dissolves another to form a solution
