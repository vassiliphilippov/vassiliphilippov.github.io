pasteurization

noun
heat process to kill microbes in food/drink
Pronunciation: / ˌpæstɚɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌpæs.tʃə.raɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɑːstjʊəraɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌpɑːs.tjʊ.raɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a process of partially sterilizing food or liquids by heating them to a specific temperature to destroy harmful microorganisms, without significantly altering their composition

Examples

Pasteurization of milk
Pasteurization of milk is required by law in many countries to ensure it is safe to drink.
Most fruit juices on supermarket shelves have undergone pasteurization to extend their shelf life.
The pasteurization process kills bacteria while preserving the flavor and nutrients of the beverage.
All milk sold in stores is pasteurisation is carefully monitored to guarantee safety.
Modern pasteurisation techniques help extend the shelf life of fruit juices.
Without pasteurisation, the risk of contamination in dairy products would be much higher.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: disinfection - removal or reduction of harmful microorganisms
  • degree higher: sterilization - complete elimination of microorganisms
