holm

noun
riverside meadow
Pronunciation: / hoʊm / (AmE), / həʊm / (BrE)
a low, flat area of land beside a river, often used as a meadow and sometimes subject to flooding

Examples

River holm meadow
The cattle grazed peacefully on the lush holm after the river receded.
In spring, wildflowers covered the holm along the riverbank.
After heavy rains, the holm was partly underwater but still green and fertile.
