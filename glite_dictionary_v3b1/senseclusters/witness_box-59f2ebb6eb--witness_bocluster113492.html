witness box

noun
enclosure for witness in court
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪtnəs bɑks / (AmE), / ˈwɪtnəs bɒks / (BrE)
an enclosed area in a courtroom where a witness stands or sits while giving evidence

Examples

The witness box
Nervously, she entered the witness box and answered the judge's questions.
The witness box was placed to the left of the judge's bench in the courtroom.
The lawyer called the next person to the witness box to testify about the incident.
