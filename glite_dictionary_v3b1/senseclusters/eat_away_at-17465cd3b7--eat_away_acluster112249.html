eat away at

verb
gradually damage or erode
Pronunciation: / it əˈweɪ æt / (AmE), / iːt əˈweɪ æt / (BrE)
to gradually damage, erode, or destroy something over time, often used figuratively

Examples

To eat away at confidence
Constant criticism can eat away at a person's self-esteem over time.
Decades of neglect began to eat away at the old wooden bridge.
Doubt continued to eat away at his sense of certainty.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: destroy - to damage something so badly that it no longer exists or works
  • synonym: erode - to gradually wear away or be worn away
  • synonym: wear away - to gradually make something disappear or become thinner by continuously using or rubbing it
