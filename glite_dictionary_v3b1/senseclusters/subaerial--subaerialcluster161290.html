subaerial

adjective
exposed to air
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌb.eɪˈɪriəl /; / ˌsʌb.eɪˈɪri.əl / (AmE), / ˌsʌb.eəˈrɪəl / (BrE)
existing, occurring, or growing on or near the surface and exposed to the air (not underground or underwater)

Examples

Subaerial eruption
The volcano's subaerial eruption was visible from miles away.
Many plants develop subaerial roots to absorb moisture directly from the air.
Erosion is often more rapid in subaerial environments than underwater.
The subaerial weathering of rocks is a key process in soil formation.
Many volcanic eruptions are subaerial, occurring above the surface rather than underwater.
Scientists study subaerial processes to understand how landscapes change over time.
