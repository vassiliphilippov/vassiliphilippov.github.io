demurral

noun
formal legal objection
Pronunciation: / dɪˈmɝːəl /; / dɪˈmɜrəl / (AmE), / dɪˈmʌrəl / (BrE)
an act of objecting or refusing, especially as a formal protest (sometimes in law)

Her polite demurral made it clear she was not interested in the offer.
There was no demurral from the committee when the proposal was presented.
