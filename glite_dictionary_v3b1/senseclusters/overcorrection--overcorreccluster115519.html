overcorrection

noun
excessive correction error
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərkəˈrɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəkəˈrɛkʃən / (BrE)
the act of applying a rule or correction too strictly, resulting in a new error, especially in language use

Examples

Grammar overcorrection
Her attempt to sound formal resulted in an overcorrection, using 'whom' where 'who' was required.
The teacher warned students that overcorrection can lead to unnatural language in their essays.
Sometimes, overcorrection happens when people try too hard to avoid common mistakes.

  • synonym: hypercorrection - an error caused by trying too hard to be correct, especially in language
