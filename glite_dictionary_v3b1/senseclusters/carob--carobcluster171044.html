carob

noun
powder substitute for chocolate
Pronunciation: / ˈkærəb / (AmE), / ˈkærəb / (BrE)
a powder made from the ground seeds and pods of the carob tree, often used as a substitute for chocolate

Examples

Carob powder
Many health food recipes use carob instead of cocoa powder.
Some people prefer carob because it is naturally sweeter than chocolate.
The bakery sells carob cookies for those avoiding caffeine.

Related Terms

  • antonym: chocolate - food made from roasted cacao seeds, usually sweetened and eaten as candy or used as a flavoring
