charcoal

noun
black carbon material from burned wood
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɑrˌkoʊl / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːkəʊl / (BrE)
a black, carbon-rich material made from burned wood, used as fuel or for drawing

Examples

Burned wood charcoal
Artists often use charcoal for sketching because of its rich, dark lines.
He drew a portrait using only charcoal and paper.
The steaks were cooked over a charcoal grill, giving them a smoky flavor.
Artists often prefer charcoal for its rich, deep black shading.
She used a charcoal to sketch the outline of the old house.
The drawing was created entirely with charcoal on textured paper.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coal - a combustible black or dark brown rock formed from fossilized plant matter, used as fuel
  • synonym: pastel - a stick of powdered pigment used for drawing
  • synonym: pencil - a tool for writing or drawing with a graphite core
