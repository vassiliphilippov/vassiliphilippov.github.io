homologation

noun
recognition of homology
Pronunciation: / həˌmɑləˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / hɒˌmɒləˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of establishing or recognizing that biological structures, genes, or organs share a common evolutionary origin

Examples

Gene homologation
The homologation of certain genes in humans and mice helps scientists study genetic diseases.
Debates about the homologation of insect wings and crustacean limbs continue among evolutionary biologists.
Accurate homologation of anatomical features is essential for reconstructing evolutionary relationships.

Related Terms

  • antonym: analogy - similarity due to convergent evolution, not common ancestry
  • synonym: homology - similarity due to shared ancestry
