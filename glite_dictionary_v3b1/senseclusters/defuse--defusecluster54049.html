defuse

verb
to calm a situation
4.5
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈfjuːz/ /; / /diː.ˈfjuːz/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈfjuːz/ /; / /diː.ˈfjuːz/ (BrE)
to make a tense, dangerous, or difficult situation calmer or less likely to cause harm

Examples

To defuse tension
The teacher managed to defuse the argument between the two students before it became physical.
She spoke calmly in an effort to defuse the tense atmosphere in the meeting.
His joke helped defuse the situation and made everyone laugh.
The teacher quickly defused the tension between the two students before it became a bigger problem.
He tried to defuse the situation by making a joke and changing the subject.
Their calm response defused what could have turned into a heated argument.
