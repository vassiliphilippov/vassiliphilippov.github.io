unibody

adjective
single integrated structure
Pronunciation: / ˈjuːnɪˌbɑːdi / (AmE), / ˈjuːnɪˌbɒdi / (BrE)
constructed as a single unit with the body and frame integrated, rather than made from separate parts

Examples

Unibody construction
The manufacturer switched to a unibody design to make the vehicle lighter and more fuel-efficient.
Many modern laptops use a unibody chassis for increased durability and a sleek appearance.
A unibody structure can improve safety by distributing impact forces more evenly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: body-on-frame - a construction method where the body and frame are separate
  • synonym: monocoque - a structural system where loads are supported through an object's external skin
