the act of separating or removing something from association or connection with something else.

Examples

Dissociation from a group
The dissociation of the company from its former partner was announced publicly last week.
The new evidence led to a dissociation of the artist's name from the controversial movement.
There was a clear dissociation between his opinions and those of the group.

Synonyms

  • disengagement
    6.5 0.49/m
    disengagement is the act of releasing involvement or attachment, while dissociation means removing something from its association or connection.
  • detachment
    6.1 0.36/m
    detachment describes separating or freeing something or someone, often implying emotional distance, while dissociation focuses on removing an association.
  • disconnection
    4.2 0.85/m
    disconnection is the loss or stopping of a link, often physical or in communication, while dissociation stresses removing associations.
  • uncoupling
    5.0 0.03/m
    uncoupling is separating paired or joined things, often in machines or systems, while dissociation means removing a general association.
  • withdrawal
    4.1 13/m
    withdrawal is pulling back or taking something away from a place or person, while dissociation means severing an association or connection.
  • nonassociation
    7.4 0.00/m
    nonassociation is the state of not being connected or involved, while dissociation refers to the action of removing that association.
