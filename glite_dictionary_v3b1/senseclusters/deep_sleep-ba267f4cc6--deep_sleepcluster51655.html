deep sleep

noun
profound, restorative sleep
2.8 1.19/m
Pronunciation: / dip slip / (AmE), / diÀêp sliÀêp / (BrE)
a state of sleep in which the body and mind are fully at rest, with little or no awareness of the surroundings, typically associated with the most restorative part of the sleep cycle

Examples

Fall into deep sleep
After the long hike, he finally drifted into a deep sleep.
Doctors say that deep sleep is essential for physical and mental recovery.
Thunderstorms couldn't wake her once she was in deep sleep.

Synonyms

  • deep slumber
    4.0 0.10/m
    deep slumber is a very hard-to-wake, profoundly restful sleep, while deep sleep is the stage when the body and mind restore most.
  • sound asleep
    3.2 0.97/m
    sound asleep feels peaceful and not easily woken, while deep sleep refers to the specific deepest, most restorative stage.
  • fast asleep
    2.5 1.04/m
    fast asleep describes someone sleeping deeply and soundly, while deep sleep describes the restorative sleep stage in the cycle.
  • dead asleep
    3.5 0.09/m
    dead asleep means completely unconscious and very hard to wake, while deep sleep names the restorative sleep stage in the cycle.
