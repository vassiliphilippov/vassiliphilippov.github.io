conjugate

noun
each of two distinct liquid phases formed from partially miscible liquids
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑndʒəˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈkɒndʒʊɡeɪt / (BrE)
either of two solutions formed when partially miscible liquids separate into two distinct phases, each containing some of the other component dissolved in it.

Examples

Conjugate solution
Each conjugate contains both liquids, but in different proportions.
In lab experiments, scientists often separate conjugate phases to analyze their properties.
When oil and water are shaken together, two conjugate layers eventually form.
