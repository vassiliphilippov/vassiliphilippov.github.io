foliate

adjective
having thin leaflike layers (geology)
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊ.li.eɪt / (AmE), / ˈfəʊ.li.eɪt / (BrE)
(especially of metamorphic rock) having thin, leaflike layers or strata.

Examples

Foliate rock layers
Geologists study the foliate structure to understand metamorphic processes.
The schist is foliate, clearly showing distinct mineral layers.
Under the microscope, the rock's foliate texture becomes apparent.

Related Terms

  • synonym: laminated - formed of thin layers; composed of or arranged in layers
  • antonym: massive - (of rock) not layered or stratified
