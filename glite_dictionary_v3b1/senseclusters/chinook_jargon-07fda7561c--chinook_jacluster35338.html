Chinook Jargon

noun
historical pidgin language of Pacific Northwest
Pronunciation: / tʃɪˈnʊk ˈʤɑrɡən / (AmE), / tʃɪˈnʊk ˈdʒɑːɡən / (BrE)
a pidgin language that combined Chinook, French, and English words, formerly used as a lingua franca in the Pacific Northwest of North America

Examples

Chinook Jargon language
Many settlers in the 19th century communicated in Chinook Jargon to trade with indigenous peoples.
The vocabulary of Chinook Jargon reflects influences from several languages, including English, French, and native tongues.
Use of Chinook Jargon declined as English became dominant in the region.
