spinal nerve

noun
nerve emerging from spinal cord
4.1 0.06/m
Pronunciation: / Ààspa…™n…ôl n…úrv / (AmE), / Ààspa…™n…ôl n…úÀêv / (BrE)
any of the paired nerves that emerge from the spinal cord, each connecting to the cord by two roots, and transmitting signals between the spinal cord and the body.

Examples

A spinal nerve root
After the accident, the patient experienced pain along the pathway of a spinal nerve.
Each spinal nerve is responsible for transmitting sensory and motor signals between the spinal cord and specific body regions.
The doctor explained that damage to a spinal nerve could cause weakness in the limbs.

Synonyms

  • nervus spinalis
    8.2 0.00/m
