Melanchthon

proper noun
German theologian and Reformation leader
Pronunciation: / məˈlæŋkθɑn / (AmE), / məˈlaŋkθən / (BrE)
(1497–1560) German theologian and reformer, known as Martin Luther's successor and a leading figure of the Reformation in Germany.

Examples

Melanchthon the reformer
Melanchthon played a crucial role in consolidating the teachings of the Protestant Reformation in Germany.
Many historians regard Melanchthon as the intellectual architect of Lutheran education.
The partnership between Luther and Melanchthon was vital for the progress of the Reformation.
