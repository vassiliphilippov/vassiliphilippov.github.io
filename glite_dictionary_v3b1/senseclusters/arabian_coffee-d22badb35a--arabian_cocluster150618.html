Arabian coffee

noun
main coffee-producing shrub
Pronunciation: / əˈreɪ.bi.ən ˈkɑː.fi / (AmE), / əˈreɪ.bi.ən ˈkɒf.i / (BrE)
a tropical evergreen shrub native to northeastern Africa, widely cultivated for its seeds, which provide most of the world's coffee

Examples

Arabian coffee beans
Arabian coffee is grown throughout Central and South America for its high-quality beans.
Farmers in Ethiopia cultivate Arabian coffee, the source of most commercial coffee.
The delicate flavor of Arabian coffee makes it the preferred choice for many coffee drinkers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coffee plant - plant of genus Coffea that produces coffee beans
