computerized tomography

noun
medical imaging using computer-processed X-rays
2.2
Pronunciation: / kəmˌpjuːtəˌraɪzd təˈmɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / kəmˌpjuːtəˌraɪzd təˈmɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
a medical imaging technique in which cross-sectional images of the body are created using X-ray scans processed by a computer

Examples

A computerized tomography scanner
A computerized tomography scanner produces detailed images of tissues and organs inside the body.
Thanks to computerized tomography, tumors can now be detected much earlier than before.
The doctor ordered a computerized tomography to determine the extent of the injury.
