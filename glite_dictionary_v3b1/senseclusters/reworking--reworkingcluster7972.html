reworking

noun
new adaptation
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈwɜrkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈwɜːkɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of changing or improving something, or the new version that results from this

Examples

A reworking of a novel
The author published a reworking of her earlier novel with a new ending.
Many modern movies are reworkings of classic stories from the past.
The chef's reworking of the traditional recipe gave it a fresh and exciting flavor.
The reworking of the script made the story much more engaging for the audience.
After several rounds of reworking, the design finally met the client's expectations.
The museum displayed a reworking of the artist's famous painting with new colors and details.

Related Terms

  • synonym: modification - a change made to improve or alter something
  • antonym: original - the first or earliest version of something
  • synonym: revision - a corrected or improved version of something
