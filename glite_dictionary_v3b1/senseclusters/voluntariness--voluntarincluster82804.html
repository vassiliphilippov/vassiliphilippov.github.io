voluntariness

noun
state of acting freely
7.5
Pronunciation: /vɑː.lən.ˈter.ɪ.nəs/ /; / /vɑː.lən.ˈtɪr.ɪ.nəs/ (AmE), /vɒl.ən.ˈteə.rɪ.nəs/ /; / /vɒl.ən.ˈtɪə.rɪ.nəs/ (BrE)
the quality of being done by free will or choice, without being forced or pressured

Examples

The voluntariness of choice
The voluntariness of the donation made it even more meaningful to the charity.
Courts must ensure the voluntariness of a confession before accepting it as evidence.
The project relied on the voluntariness of community members to succeed.
The judge examined the voluntariness of the agreement before approving it.
Questions were raised about the voluntariness of her decision to resign.
Lawyers debated whether the voluntariness of the statement could be proven.
The voluntariness of his decision was clear, as no one pressured him to participate.
Informed consent requires the voluntariness of the patient before any procedure.
Her voluntariness to help with extra tasks made her a valued team member.

Related Terms

  • antonym: coercion - the practice of persuading someone to do something by force or threats
  • antonym: compulsion - the state of being forced to do something
  • antonym: reluctance - unwillingness or hesitation to do something
  • synonym: voluntarism - the principle of relying on voluntary action
  • synonym: willingness - the state of being prepared or eager to do something
