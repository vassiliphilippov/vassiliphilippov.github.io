parthenocarpic

adjective
seedless fruit-producing
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːrθənoʊˈkɑːrpɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɑːθənəʊˈkɑːpɪk / (BrE)
describing a fruit or plant that produces fruit without fertilization, usually resulting in seedless fruit

Examples

Parthenocarpic fruit

Farmers prefer parthenocarpic plants for greenhouse production since they yield seedless fruit.
A parthenocarpic tomato can develop even when pollinators are scarce.
Many commercial bananas are parthenocarpic, which is why they do not have seeds.
The scientist explained that the cucumber variety was parthenocarpic and would produce fruit without pollination.
Grapes labeled as parthenocarpic are popular because they are naturally seedless.
