capture

verb
cause particle or body to be trapped or enter new orbit
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkæp.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈkæp.tʃə / (BrE)
to cause an elementary particle or celestial body to be taken and to enter a new orbit or state

Examples

To capture a particle
A neutron can be captured by a nucleus during a fusion reaction.
The magnetic field can capture charged particles from the solar wind.
The planet captured a passing asteroid, turning it into one of its moons.

Related Terms

  • synonym: trap - to catch or confine, especially in physics or science
