volcanic rock

noun
rock formed from cooled lava on Earth's surface
1.7
Pronunciation: / vɑlˈkænɪk rɑk / (AmE), / vɒlˈkænɪk rɒk / (BrE)
An igneous rock formed by the cooling and solidification of lava or magma at or near the Earth's surface.

Examples

Volcanic rock samples
Geologists collected samples of volcanic rock to study the region’s eruptive history.
Many mountain landscapes feature dramatic cliffs of hardened volcanic rock.
The island is composed mostly of volcanic rock formed during ancient eruptions.
