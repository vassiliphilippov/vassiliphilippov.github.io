transfer payment

noun
government payment not for goods/services
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtræns.fɚ ˌpeɪ.mənt / (AmE), / ˈtrɑːns.fə ˌpeɪ.mənt / (BrE)
a payment made by the government to individuals, such as unemployment benefits or pensions, where no goods or services are received in return

Examples

Government transfer payments
Government transfer payments help reduce poverty and support people who are unemployed.
Social security is a common example of a transfer payment in many countries.
Unlike salaries, transfer payments do not reflect an exchange of goods or services.
