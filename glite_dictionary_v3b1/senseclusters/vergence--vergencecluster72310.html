vergence

noun
direction of fold tilt
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈvɜrdʒəns / (AmE), / ˈvɜːdʒəns / (BrE)
the direction in which geological folds or structures are tilted or overturned, indicating the sense of movement during deformation

Examples

Fold vergence
Geologists measured the vergence of the folds to determine the direction of tectonic movement.
The vergence of the rock layers suggested a history of intense geological pressure.
Accurate mapping of vergence helps scientists reconstruct the formation of mountain ranges.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dip - the angle at which a rock layer or fault is inclined from the horizontal
