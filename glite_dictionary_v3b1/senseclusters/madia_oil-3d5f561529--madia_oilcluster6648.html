madia oil

noun
oil from madia seeds, used in cooking
8.9 8.1
Pronunciation: /ˈmæ.dɪ.ə ɔɪl/ /; / /mə.ˈdiː.ə ɔɪl/ (AmE), /ˈmæ.dɪ.ə ˈɔɪ.əl/ /; / /mə.ˈdiː.ə ˈɔɪ.əl/ (BrE)
An edible oil obtained from the seeds of the madia plant (genus Madia), sometimes used as a substitute for olive oil.

Examples

Madia oil substitute
In certain regions, madia oil was once commonly used when olive oil was too expensive or unavailable.
Some chefs recommend madia oil as a flavorful substitute for olive oil in salad dressings.
The delicate taste of madia oil makes it suitable for light cooking or drizzling over vegetables.
