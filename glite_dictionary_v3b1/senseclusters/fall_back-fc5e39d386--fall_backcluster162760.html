fall back

verb
retreat or move backward
5.0 3.1
Pronunciation: /fɑːl bæk/ /; / /fɔːl bæk/ (AmE), /fɔːl bæk/ (BrE)
to move back or retreat, especially in the face of opposition or danger

Examples

Fall back from danger
If things get too risky, we’ll fall back and reconsider our plan.
The soldiers were forced to fall back when they were outnumbered by the enemy.
When the storm intensified, the hikers decided to fall back to a safer location.
As the storm intensified, the hikers decided to fall back to camp for safety.
If the lead runner gets tired, she might fall back into the main group.
When the troops were overwhelmed, they had no choice but to fall back.

Related Terms

  • antonym: advance - to move forward, especially toward an enemy
  • synonym: retreat - to withdraw from a position, often to avoid danger
