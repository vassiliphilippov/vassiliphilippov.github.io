hydrobromic acid

noun
strong acid; solution of hydrogen bromide in water
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪdroʊˈbroʊmɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrəˈbrəʊmɪk ˈasɪd / (BrE)
A strong acid formed by dissolving hydrogen bromide gas in water, used in chemical synthesis.

Examples

Strong hydrobromic acid
Handle hydrobromic acid with extreme care, as it is highly corrosive.
The laboratory required a fresh bottle of hydrobromic acid for the next experiment.
When hydrobromic acid is added to certain metals, it produces bromide salts and hydrogen gas.
