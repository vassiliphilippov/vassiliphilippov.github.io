Gorgon

noun
mythological monster with snake hair
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɔrɡən / (AmE), / ˈɡɔːɡən / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) any of three monstrous sisters with snakes for hair, whose glance could turn people to stone, most famously Medusa.

Examples

The Gorgon Medusa
According to myth, Perseus defeated the Gorgon Medusa using a mirrored shield.
In ancient Greek art, the face of a Gorgon often appears on shields and armor.
The stories of the Gorgons have inspired countless works of literature and sculpture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Medusa - the most famous of the Gorgons; mortal, slain by Perseus
