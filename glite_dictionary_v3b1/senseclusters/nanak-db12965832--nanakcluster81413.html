Nanak

proper noun
founder of Sikhism, Indian religious leader
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑːnək / (AmE), / ˈnænək / (BrE)
(1469–1539) Indian religious leader who founded Sikhism, teaching the equality of all people and the importance of meditation on God.

Examples

Nanak the founder
Nanak is revered as the founder of Sikhism in India and around the world.
Nanak's teachings have inspired generations to pursue spiritual harmony and social justice.
Followers of Nanak believe in the equality of all people and reject the caste system.
