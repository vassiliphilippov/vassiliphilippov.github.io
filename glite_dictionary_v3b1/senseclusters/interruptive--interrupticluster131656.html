interruptive

adjective
causing interruption
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntəˈrʌptɪv / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈrʌptɪv / (BrE)
causing or relating to interruption; tending to break the flow or continuity of something

Examples

An interruptive phone call
The interruptive phone calls made it difficult to finish my work on time.
He found the interruptive advertisements during the movie very annoying.
Teachers try to minimize interruptive behavior in the classroom to maintain focus.
The interruptive signal forced the computer to stop its current task and respond immediately.
Engineers designed the system to minimize interruptive events during critical operations.
An interruptive process can affect the performance of real-time applications.

Related Terms

  • antonym: continuous - without interruption or break
  • synonym: disruptive - causing disturbance or problems
