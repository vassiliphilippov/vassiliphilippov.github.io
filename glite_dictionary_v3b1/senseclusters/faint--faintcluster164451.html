faint

noun
temporary loss of consciousness
4.4 0.62/m
Pronunciation: / feÉªnt / (AmE), / feÉªnt / (BrE)
a sudden and brief loss of consciousness, usually caused by a decrease in blood flow to the brain

Examples

A sudden faint
After standing in the hot sun for hours, he experienced a sudden faint.
She suffered a faint in the crowded subway car, startling the people around her.
The doctor explained that a faint is usually harmless but can occasionally signal a more serious condition.

Synonyms

  • syncope
    9.9 0.21/m
    syncope is the medical term for a short loss of consciousness from low brain blood flow, while faint is the everyday word.
  • swoon
    6.8 0.60/m
    swoon is a poetic or old-fashioned word for a brief fainting spell, while faint is common everyday language.
  • loss of consciousness
    5.3 0.26/m
    loss of consciousness is any state of being unaware and unresponsive, while faint means a sudden, brief loss usually from low brain blood flow.
