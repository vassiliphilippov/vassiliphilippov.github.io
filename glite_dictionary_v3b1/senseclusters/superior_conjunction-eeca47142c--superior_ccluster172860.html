superior conjunction

noun
planet opposite Sun from Earth (astronomy)
9.3
Pronunciation: / səˈpɪriɚ kənˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / suːˈpɪəriə kənˈdʒʌŋkʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
(Astronomy) A position in which a planet is aligned on the opposite side of the Sun from the Earth.

Examples

Superior conjunction of planet
Astronomers observed Venus near superior conjunction during last night’s sky survey.
Mercury is at superior conjunction when it passes behind the Sun as seen from Earth.
The planet will not be visible for several weeks while it is in superior conjunction.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inferior conjunction - alignment of Earth and planet on same side of Sun
