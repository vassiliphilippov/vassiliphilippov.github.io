order Polemoniales

noun
former order of flowering plants
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrdɚ poʊˌlɛməˈnaɪəliˌiz / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌpəʊlɛmɒˈnaɪəliːz / (BrE)
a former order of flowering plants that included families such as the Polemoniaceae, Solanaceae, Boraginaceae, among others; no longer widely used in modern classification.

Examples

The order Polemoniales group
Researchers used to group certain plant families under the order Polemoniales before modern genetic analysis became standard.
The order Polemoniales was once considered to include a wide range of diverse flowering plant families.
With revisions in plant classification, the order Polemoniales is no longer commonly recognized.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Lamiales - an order of flowering plants that includes mints; some families formerly in Polemoniales are now placed here
  • synonym: Solanales - an order of flowering plants that includes nightshades and used to encompass some families formerly in Polemoniales
