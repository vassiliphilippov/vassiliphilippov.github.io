ectopia

noun
abnormal position of an organ or part
Pronunciation: / ɛkˈtoʊpiə / (AmE), / ɛkˈtəʊpiə / (BrE)
the abnormal position or placement of an organ or body part, especially present from birth

Examples

Kidney ectopia
Doctors detected ectopia in the patient's kidney position during a routine scan.
Genetic factors can sometimes contribute to ectopia of various organs.
The surgeon diagnosed the newborn with ectopia of the heart, a rare and serious condition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: displacement - the movement of something from its usual place, especially in anatomy
