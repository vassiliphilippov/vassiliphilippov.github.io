Rydberg constant

noun
spectroscopy constant for hydrogen atom
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈraɪdˌbɜrɡ ˈkɑnstənt / (AmE), / ˈrɪdˌbɜːɡ ˈkɒnstənt / (BrE)
a physical constant representing the limiting value of the highest wavenumber of any photon emitted from the hydrogen atom, used in spectroscopy
