unshowy

adjective
modest; not flashy
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈʃoʊi / (AmE), / ʌnˈʃəʊi / (BrE)
modest, simple, and not attracting attention; not trying to impress others

Examples

An unshowy style
Her unshowy style made her approachable and easy to talk to.
The restaurant is unshowy but serves excellent food at reasonable prices.
He gave an unshowy performance that still impressed the judges.
She preferred an unshowy style of dress that did not draw attention.
The restaurant's unshowy exterior hid a warm and welcoming atmosphere inside.
His unshowy achievements were appreciated by those who knew his true value.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flashy - attracting attention by being bright, expensive, or showy
  • synonym: humble - having a low opinion of one's own importance; not proud
  • synonym: modest - not showing off or drawing attention to oneself
  • synonym: plain - simple in appearance; not decorated
