malignant melanoma

noun
a dangerous skin cancer
4.1
Pronunciation: / məˈlɪɡnənt ˌmɛloʊˈnoʊmə / (AmE), / məˈlɪɡnənt ˌmɛlənˈəʊmə / (BrE)
a serious form of skin cancer originating from melanocytes, often characterized by dark, irregularly shaped spots on the skin

Examples

Dark malignant melanoma spot
Early detection of malignant melanoma greatly increases the survival rate for patients.
She noticed a suspicious mole that turned out to be a malignant melanoma after a biopsy.
The doctor explained that a malignant melanoma requires immediate treatment to prevent it from spreading.

Related Terms
