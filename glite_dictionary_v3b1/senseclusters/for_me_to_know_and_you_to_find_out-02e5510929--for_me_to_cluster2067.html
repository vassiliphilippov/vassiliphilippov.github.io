for me to know and you to find out

x
speaker withholding information
Pronunciation: / fər miː tə noʊ ənd juː tə faɪnd aʊt / (AmE), / fə miː tʊ nəʊ ənd juː tʊ faɪnd aʊt / (BrE)
used to indicate that the speaker does not wish to reveal information, suggesting the listener must discover it themselves

Examples

For me to know and you to find out
"Where are we going tonight?" "That's for me to know and you to find out."
She only smiled and said it was for me to know and you to find out when I asked her secret.
When pressed for details, he replied, "It's for me to know and you to find out."
