Caucasus

noun
mountain range between Black and Caspian Seas
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔkəsəs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːkəsəs / (BrE)
a mountain range and region in Eurasia between the Black Sea and the Caspian Sea, forming part of the traditional border between Europe and Asia

Examples

Caucasus Mountains
Mount Elbrus, the highest peak in the Caucasus, attracts climbers from all over the world.
Tensions in the Caucasus region have shaped much of its modern political landscape.
The Caucasus has long been considered a crossroads of various cultures and empires.
