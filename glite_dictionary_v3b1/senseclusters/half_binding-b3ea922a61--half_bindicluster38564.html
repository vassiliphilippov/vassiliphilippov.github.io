half binding

noun
bookbinding using two materials for covers and spine
5.8 7.6
Pronunciation: /hæf ˈbaɪn.dɪŋ/ /; / /hɑːf ˈbaɪn.dɪŋ/ (AmE), /hɑːf ˈbaɪn.dɪŋ/ /; / /hæf ˈbaɪn.dɪŋ/ (BrE)
a method of bookbinding in which the spine and part of the covers are bound in one material and the rest of the covers in another, typically of different quality or texture

Examples

Half binding book
Collectors often appreciate half binding for its aesthetic contrast and durability.
The rare volume featured a half binding with a leather spine and marbled paper sides.
The restoration included a new half binding that matched the book’s original design.
