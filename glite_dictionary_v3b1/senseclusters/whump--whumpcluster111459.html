whump

verb
to harm a character
9.9
Pronunciation: / wʌmp / (AmE), / wʌmp / (BrE)
to subject a fictional character to physical or emotional suffering, injury, or distress, often for dramatic effect, especially in fan fiction.

Examples

To whump a character
The author decided to whump the main character in the next chapter to increase the tension.
Fans often debate how much a writer should whump their favorite hero in a story.
After several chapters of whumping, the character finally found some peace.
