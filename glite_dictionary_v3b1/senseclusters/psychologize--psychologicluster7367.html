psychologize

verb
to explain psychologically
Pronunciation: / saɪˈkɑlədʒaɪz / (AmE), / saɪˈkɒlədʒaɪz / (BrE)
to explain or interpret something using psychological theories or ideas

Examples

To psychologize behavior
It is easy to psychologize someone's actions without knowing the facts.
Some critics argue that we should not psychologize every decision people make.
She tends to psychologize her friends' problems instead of just listening.
Some critics argue that the novel psychologizes its characters too much, focusing on their inner thoughts.
It is tempting to psychologize historical figures based on modern theories.
She tends to psychologize every disagreement, searching for hidden motives.
It is tempting to psychologize every decision people make, but sometimes the reasons are simple.
The author tends to psychologize historical events, focusing on the motives of key figures.
We should avoid psychologizing her behavior without understanding her background.
