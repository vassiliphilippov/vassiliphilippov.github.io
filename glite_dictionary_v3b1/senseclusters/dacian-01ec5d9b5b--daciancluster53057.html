Dacian

noun
ancient language
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈdeɪsiən / (BrE)
the ancient Indo-European language once spoken by the Dacian people in what is now Romania and Moldova

Examples

Dacian language
Few written records of the Dacian language have survived to the present day.
Linguists study place names to learn more about Dacian and its relationship to other ancient languages.
The Dacian language became extinct after the Roman conquest of Dacia.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Latin - the ancient language of the Romans
  • synonym: Thracian - the ancient language spoken by the Thracian people
