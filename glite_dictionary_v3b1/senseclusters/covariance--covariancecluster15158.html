covariance

noun
statistical measure of association
9.0
Pronunciation: / kəˈvɛriəns / (AmE), / kəʊˈvɛəriəns / (BrE)
(Statistics) A measure of the relationship between two random variables, equal to the mean value of the product of their deviations from their respective means.

Examples

Positive covariance value
A positive covariance indicates that the variables increase together, while a negative value suggests the opposite.
The covariance between the two variables was used to determine their statistical relationship.
When analyzing the data set, the scientist calculated the covariance to assess the strength of the association.

Related Terms

  • synonym: correlation - a statistical measure that expresses the extent to which two variables are linearly related
