lubber's mark

noun
mark on a ship's compass for direction
Pronunciation: / ˈlʌbɚz mɑrk / (AmE), / ˈlʌbəz mɑːk / (BrE)
a fixed line on a ship's compass that indicates the direction in which the vessel is heading

Examples

The lubber's mark line
A worn lubber's mark can make navigation more difficult for the crew.
He checked the lubber's mark before making any adjustments to the ship's course.
The helmsman kept the lubber's mark aligned with the compass heading throughout the journey.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lubber's line - the line or mark on a compass showing the fore-and-aft direction of a ship
