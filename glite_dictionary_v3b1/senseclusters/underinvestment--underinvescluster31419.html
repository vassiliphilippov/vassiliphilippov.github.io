underinvestment

noun
insufficient funding
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərɪnˈvɛstmənt / (AmE), / ˌʌndərɪnˈvɛstmənt / (BrE)
a situation where not enough money is spent or invested to maintain, improve, or grow something

Examples

Years of underinvestment
The hospital's outdated equipment is a result of years of underinvestment in healthcare.
Many public schools struggle because of chronic underinvestment in their infrastructure.
Experts warn that underinvestment in renewable energy could slow progress on climate goals.
Years of underinvestment in public transport have led to outdated infrastructure and frequent delays.
The school suffered from underinvestment, resulting in a lack of modern equipment and resources.
Analysts warned that underinvestment in technology could hurt the company's long-term competitiveness.
Years of underinvestment in public transport have led to crowded and unreliable services.
The company suffered from underinvestment in research and development, falling behind its competitors.
Government officials warned that continued underinvestment could harm the country's long-term economic prospects.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disinvestment - the process of withdrawing invested funds
  • antonym: investment - the act of putting money into something to gain a benefit
  • synonym: neglect - the failure to care for or give proper attention to something
