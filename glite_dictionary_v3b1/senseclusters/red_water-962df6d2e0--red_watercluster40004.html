red water

noun
cattle disease causing blood in urine
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛd ˌwɔtər / (AmE), / ˈrɛd ˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
a disease of cattle marked by the passing of blood in the urine (hematuria), typically caused by infection with certain parasites or bacteria.

Examples

Red water disease
Farmers are advised to check for red water during the high-risk season for cattle.
The veterinarian diagnosed the cow with red water after observing blood in its urine.
Vaccination can help prevent red water outbreaks in areas where the disease is prevalent.
