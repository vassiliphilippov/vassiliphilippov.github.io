thickening

noun
substance used to make liquids thicker
Pronunciation: / ˈθɪkənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈθɪkənɪŋ / (BrE)
a substance added to make a liquid thicker or less runny, especially in cooking or manufacturing

Examples

Cornstarch thickening
Cornstarch is commonly used as a thickening in soups and sauces.
Egg yolk can serve as a natural thickening in custards.
Gelatin is sometimes added as a thickening for desserts and jellies.
