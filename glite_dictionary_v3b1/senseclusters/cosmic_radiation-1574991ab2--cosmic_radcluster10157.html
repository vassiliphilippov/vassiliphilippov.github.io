cosmic radiation

noun
high-energy radiation from outside the solar system
2.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑzmɪk ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈkɒzmɪk ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
High-energy radiation originating from outside the solar system and reaching Earth.

Examples

Cosmic radiation exposure
Astronauts face increased exposure to cosmic radiation outside the Earth's protective atmosphere.
Scientists study cosmic radiation to learn about high-energy processes in the universe.
The Earth's magnetic field helps shield us from harmful cosmic radiation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cosmic ray - A highly energetic atomic nucleus or other particle from space
