chirality

noun
mirror-image property
9.8
Pronunciation: /kaɪ.ˈræl.ɪ.tɪ/ (AmE), /kaɪ.ˈræl.ɪ.tɪ/ (BrE)
the property of an object, molecule, or particle that makes it different from its mirror image (not superimposable; handedness)

Examples

Chirality in molecules
In chemistry, chirality explains why some molecules behave differently from their mirror images.
The concept of chirality is important in understanding the structure of biological molecules.
A left hand and a right hand are a simple example of chirality in everyday life.
In chemistry, molecules with chirality can have very different effects in biological systems.
The concept of chirality is important in both physics and mathematics.
Scientists study chirality to understand why some particles behave differently from their mirror images.

Related Terms

  • synonym: asymmetry - lack of equality or equivalence between parts or aspects
  • synonym: handedness - the quality of having a left or right orientation
  • antonym: symmetry - the quality of being made up of exactly similar parts facing each other
