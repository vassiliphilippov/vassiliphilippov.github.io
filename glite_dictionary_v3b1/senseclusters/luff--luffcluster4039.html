luff

noun
sailing close to the wind
9.2
Pronunciation: / lʌf / (AmE), / lʌf / (BrE)
the act of sailing a boat as close to the wind as possible without losing forward movement

Examples

A sudden luff
An unexpected luff caught the inexperienced crew off guard, causing the sails to flap.
During the regatta, the skipper executed a sharp luff to gain a better position.
Proper timing of the luff can help sailors avoid losing speed while changing direction.
