hole up

verb
stay hidden or secluded
Pronunciation: / hoʊl ʌp / (AmE), / həʊl ʌp / (BrE)
to stay in a place and avoid leaving it, especially to hide or escape from something

Examples

Thieves hole up inside
After the robbery, the thieves decided to hole up in an abandoned warehouse.
During the snowstorm, we had to hole up at a roadside motel.
She likes to hole up in her cabin and write all weekend.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hide out - to stay somewhere secretly, often to avoid trouble
