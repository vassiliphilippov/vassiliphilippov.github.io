Sophocles

proper noun
ancient Greek tragedian (playwright)
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑː.fə.kliːz / (AmE), / ˈsɒf.ə.kliːz / (BrE)
(c. 497–406 BC) An ancient Greek playwright and one of the three great tragedians whose surviving works include 'Oedipus Rex' and 'Antigone'.

Examples

Sophocles Greek playwright
Sophocles wrote tragedies that are still performed on modern stages today.
In my literature class, we discussed the themes present in Sophocles' play Antigone.
The works of Sophocles have influenced countless generations of writers and thinkers.
