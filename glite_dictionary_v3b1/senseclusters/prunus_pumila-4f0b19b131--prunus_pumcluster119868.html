Prunus pumila

noun
a North American cherry shrub
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpruːnəs ˈpjuːmɪlə / (AmE), / ˈpruːnəs ˈpjuːmɪlə / (BrE)
A small, straggling North American shrub in the cherry family, growing on sandy soil and producing small, purplish-black fruit that are barely edible.

Examples

Prunus pumila shrub
Birds sometimes feed on the tiny fruits of Prunus pumila, despite their tart taste.
Gardeners value Prunus pumila for its ability to prevent soil erosion in sandy habitats.
The Prunus pumila is often found growing along the sandy shores of the Great Lakes.
