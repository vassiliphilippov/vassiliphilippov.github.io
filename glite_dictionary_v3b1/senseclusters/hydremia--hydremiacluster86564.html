hydremia

noun
excess fluid in blood (medical)
9.9
Pronunciation: / haɪˈdriːmiə / (AmE), / hɪˈdriːmiə / (BrE)
A medical condition in which there is an excess of fluid in the blood relative to the number of blood cells.

Examples

Excess hydremia fluid
Doctors monitored the patient's fluid intake to prevent hydremia from worsening.
Excessive intravenous fluids can sometimes lead to hydremia in hospitalized patients.
