Artemis

proper noun
Greek goddess of hunt and moon
8.0 11/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…ëÀêr.t…ô.m…™s / (AmE), / Àà…ëÀê.t…™.m…™s / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) the virgin goddess of the hunt, wilderness, and the moon; daughter of Leto and twin sister of Apollo; identified with the Roman goddess Diana.

Examples

Artemis the huntress
Artemis was revered as a protector of wildlife and young women.
In classical art, Artemis is often depicted carrying a bow and arrows.
Many ancient temples were dedicated to Artemis, including the famous one at Ephesus.

Synonyms

  • Cynthia
    9.6 18/m
    Cynthia is a poetic name for the moon goddess used in poems and songs, while Artemis is the original Greek goddess.
