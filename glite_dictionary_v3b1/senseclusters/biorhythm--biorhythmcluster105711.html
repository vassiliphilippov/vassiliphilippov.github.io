biorhythm

noun
biological cycle theory
7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈbaɪ.ə.rɪ.ðəm/ /; / /ˈbaɪ.oʊ.rɪ.ðəm/ (AmE), /ˈbaɪ.ə.rɪ.ðəm/ (BrE)
a supposed regular cycle in physical, emotional, or mental activity that is believed to affect a person's mood or energy

Examples

Personal biorhythm chart
Some people track their biorhythm to predict days of high or low energy.
He blamed his poor performance on a low point in his biorhythm cycle.
The idea of biorhythm suggests that our abilities fluctuate in regular patterns.
Some people track their biorhythm to predict days when they might feel more energetic.
He blamed his lack of concentration on his biorhythm being out of sync.
Books about biorhythm claim that our moods follow predictable cycles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: circadian rhythm - the natural, internal process that regulates the sleep-wake cycle
  • synonym: cycle - a series of events that are regularly repeated in the same order
