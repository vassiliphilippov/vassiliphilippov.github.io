impersonalism

noun
doctrine of impersonal divinity
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɜrsənəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɜːsənəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief or doctrine that ultimate reality or the divine lacks personal will, personality, or individual consciousness

Examples

Philosophical impersonalism
The philosopher discussed impersonalism as a key concept in certain Eastern religions.
Some people find comfort in impersonalism, believing that the universe operates without a personal deity.
His research paper compared impersonalism and personalism in the context of modern theology.
