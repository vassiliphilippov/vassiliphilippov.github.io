noncivilized

adjective
lacking advanced culture or development
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˈsɪvəˌlaɪzd /; / nɑnˈsɪv.ə.laɪzd / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈsɪvəˌlaɪzd /; / nɒnˈsɪv.ɪ.laɪzd / (BrE)
not having a high level of culture or social development; lacking the characteristics of a civilized society.

Examples

A noncivilized society
Many early explorers wrongly described unfamiliar cultures as noncivilized.
Some argued that the absence of written language made a society noncivilized in Western eyes.
The documentary explored the traditions of a noncivilized tribe deep in the rainforest.
It is unfair and inaccurate to label cultures as noncivilised simply because they are different.
Some early explorers described remote tribes as noncivilised societies, though such views are now considered outdated.
The ancient ruins suggest that the people who built them were anything but noncivilised.
