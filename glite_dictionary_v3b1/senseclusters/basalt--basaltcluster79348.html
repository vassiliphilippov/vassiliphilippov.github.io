basalt

noun
a common dark volcanic rock
5.4 5.7
Pronunciation: / bəˈsɔlt / (AmE), / ˈbæs.ɔːlt / (BrE)
a dense, dark grey, fine-grained igneous rock formed from solidified lava, composed chiefly of plagioclase feldspar and pyroxene

Examples

Dark basalt rock
A layer of basalt covers much of the ocean floor, giving it a characteristic dark appearance.
Ancient columns made of basalt can still be seen in some Roman ruins.
The Hawaiian islands are primarily formed from basalt produced by volcanic eruptions.
