armguard

noun
protective covering for wrist or arm in sports
Pronunciation: / ˈɑrmˌɡɑrd / (AmE), / ˈɑːmˌɡɑːd / (BrE)
A protective covering worn on the wrist or arm, especially in archery, fencing, and other sports, to shield against injury.

Examples

An armguard for archery
Fencers wear an armguard to protect themselves from accidental strikes.
She bought a new armguard for her archery lessons at the sports shop.
The archer adjusted his armguard before stepping up to take the shot.
