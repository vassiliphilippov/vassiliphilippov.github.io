English Revolution

noun
the 1688-1689 revolution against James II in England
6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈɪŋ.ɡlɪʃ rɛv.ə.ˈluː.ʃən/ /; / /ˈɪŋ.lɪʃ rɛv.ə.ˈluː.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈɪŋ.ɡlɪʃ rɛv.ə.ˈluː.ʃən/ /; / /ˈɪŋ.lɪʃ rɛv.ə.ˈluː.ʃən/ (BrE)
the revolution of 1688–1689 in England that resulted in the overthrow of James II and the accession of William III and Mary II, with little armed resistance in England itself; also known as the Glorious Revolution.

Examples

The English Revolution 1688
After the English Revolution, the monarchy's power was limited by law.
Many historians consider the English Revolution a turning point for parliamentary democracy.
The English Revolution of 1688 changed the course of British constitutional history.
