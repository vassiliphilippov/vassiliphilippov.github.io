refractive

adjective
relating to refraction of light or waves
Pronunciation: / rɪˈfræktɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈfraktɪv /; / rɪˈfræktɪv / (BrE)
relating to or able to bend light or other waves as they pass through something

Examples

Refractive power
Changes in the eye's refractive power can cause vision problems like nearsightedness.
Scientists studied the refractive properties of crystals to develop better lasers.
The lens has a high refractive index, making it ideal for precise optics.
Glass is highly refractive, which is why it bends light passing through it.
Scientists measured the refractive index of water to better understand how light travels in the ocean.
The lens's refractive properties are essential for focusing images onto the retina.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reflective - relating to the reflection of light or waves
  • synonym: refractile - capable of refracting, alternative form; used in optics
