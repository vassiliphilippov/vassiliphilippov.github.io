suck

verb
attract irresistibly; draw in
Pronunciation: / sʌk / (AmE), / sʌk / (BrE)
to attract or draw in irresistibly, often by an overwhelming or compelling force

Examples

To suck you in
He warned that the risky investment could suck you in before you realize the dangers.
The bright lights of the city often suck young dreamers away from their hometowns.
The thriving job market sucked thousands of people into the city.

Related Terms

  • synonym: draw in - to cause someone or something to become involved or engaged
  • antonym: repel - to drive or force something away
