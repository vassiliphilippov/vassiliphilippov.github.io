odd

adjective
not divisible by two (math)
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɑd / (AmE), / ɒd / (BrE)
(of a number) not divisible by two, leaving a remainder of one when divided by two.

Examples

Odd numbers only
The teacher asked the students to circle all the odd numbers on the worksheet.
Three is an odd number, while four is even.
When you add an odd number to an even number, the result is always odd.

Related Terms

  • antonym: even - (of a number) divisible by two with no remainder
