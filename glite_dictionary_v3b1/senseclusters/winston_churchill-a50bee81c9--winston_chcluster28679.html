Winston Churchill

proper noun
British statesman and writer
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪnstən ˈtʃɜːrtʃɪl / (AmE), / ˈwɪnstən ˈtʃɜːtʃɪl / (BrE)
British statesman, Prime Minister during World War II, and Nobel Prize-winning author (1874–1965).

Examples

Winston Churchill speeches
Winston Churchill delivered some of the most memorable speeches of the twentieth century.
In addition to his political career, Winston Churchill won the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1953.
The leadership of Winston Churchill was critical during Britain's darkest hours in World War II.
