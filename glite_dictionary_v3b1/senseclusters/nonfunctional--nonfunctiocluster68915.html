nonfunctional

adjective
relating to software quality attributes
6.5
Pronunciation: /nɑːn.ˈfʌŋk.ʃən.əl/ /; / /nə.ˈfʌŋk.ʃən.əl/ (AmE), /nɒn.ˈfʌŋk.ʃən.əl/ /; / /nə.ˈfʌŋk.ʃən.əl/ (BrE)
(in computing, especially software engineering) relating to quality attributes of a system — such as performance, reliability, security, or usability — rather than to specific functions or features.

Examples

Nonfunctional requirements
The project list includes several nonfunctional requirements, such as fast startup and robust security.
During testing they focused on nonfunctional aspects like latency and memory use before release.
Managers discussed nonfunctional metrics in the review to ensure the app met user expectations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: functional - relating to the specific functions or features a system performs (often contrasted with nonfunctional in software contexts)
  • synonym: quality attribute - a measurable property of a system (for example performance or security) that affects its quality
