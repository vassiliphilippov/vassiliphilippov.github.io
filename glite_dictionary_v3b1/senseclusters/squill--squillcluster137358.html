squill

noun
medicinal Mediterranean plant with white flowers
Pronunciation: / skwɪl / (AmE), / skwɪl / (BrE)
a bulbous plant with narrow leaves and spikes of white, blue, or pink flowers, sometimes used in traditional medicine

Examples

Wild squill flowers
In spring, the fields near the coast bloom with wild squill.
Several traditional remedies use extracts from the squill plant.
The pharmacist recommended a syrup containing squill for his persistent cough.
The squill produces beautiful blue flowers in the early spring.
Traditional remedies sometimes used the dried bulb of the squill for medicinal purposes.
We planted several squill bulbs in the rock garden to add color.
