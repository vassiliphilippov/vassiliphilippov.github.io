chirr

verb
make a vibrant buzzing sound (insect)
9.9 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / t É…™r / (AmE), / t É…úÀê / (BrE)
of an insect such as a grasshopper or cicada: to make a continuous, vibrant, buzzing or trilling sound.

Examples

To chirr loudly
At dusk, grasshoppers began to chirr loudly all around us.
Cicadas chirr in the summer heat, filling the garden with their persistent song.
The night air was alive with insects that chirred from the tall grass.

Synonyms

  • chir
    9.3 0.00/m
    chir makes a short trilling or vibrating sound for birds or small insects, while chirr is a longer, continuous trilling.
  • churr
    9.9 0.00/m
    churr is a low, vibrant trilling sound often heard from birds or insects, while chirr is a more steady, buzzing trill.
  • buzz
    1.1 2.81/m
    buzz is a low, continuous vibrating sound like bees, while chirr is a brighter, trilling insect sound that can be more musical.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chirp - to make a short, sharp, high-pitched sound (insects or birds)
