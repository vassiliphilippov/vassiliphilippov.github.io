putatively

adverb
supposedly, assumedly
8.1 0.39/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàpjuÀêt…ôt…™vli / (AmE), / ÀàpjuÀêt…ôt…™vli / (BrE)
as is generally believed or assumed, but not proven to be true

Examples

Is putatively true
The artifact was putatively created in the 12th century, though experts are still debating its origin.
He is putatively the author of the anonymous letter that caused the controversy.
The new species is putatively related to the extinct animal found in the same region.

Synonyms

  • supposedly
    3.4 24/m
    supposedly is what people say when something is commonly believed, while putatively is a formal way to say it is assumed.
  • purportedly
    7.2 2.08/m
    purportedly often marks a claim made by someone, while putatively suggests a general or scholarly assumption without firm proof.
  • presumptively
    7.4 0.40/m
    presumptively is used for a reasonable legal or formal guess, while putatively is more neutral and often academic in tone.
  • assumedly
    6.7 0.02/m
    assumedly means someone accepts something without proof in speech, while putatively sounds more formal and appears in writing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allegedly - as is claimed to be the case, but without proof
  • antonym: definitely - without doubt; certainly
  • synonym: supposedly - as is thought or believed, but not known for certain
