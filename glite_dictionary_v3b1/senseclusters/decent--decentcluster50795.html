decent

adjective
conforming to standards of modesty
2.7 2.44/m
Pronunciation: / Ààdis…ônt /; / ÀàdiÀês…ônt / (AmE), / ÀàdiÀês…ônt / (BrE)
conforming to standards of sexual propriety; not obscene or lewd; modest in speech, behavior, or dress

Examples

Decent behavior
Although her dress was slightly torn, she remained decent in public.
He made sure the actors' costumes were decent for the family show.
Please check if I'm decent before coming in.
Most parents hope their children will use decent language in public.
She wore a decent dress that covered her shoulders and knees.
The school enforces a decent standard of behavior at all events.

Synonyms

  • clean
    2.8 6.30/m

  • respectable
    3.0 7.72/m
    respectable describes a person seen as socially proper and worthy of respect, while decent focuses on modest behavior and avoiding lewdness.
