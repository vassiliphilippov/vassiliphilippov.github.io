respite

verb
postpone a criminal's punishment
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛs.paɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛs.pɪt / (BrE)
(law) To postpone the punishment of a convicted person, especially a death sentence.

Examples

To respite a sentence
The governor decided to respite the execution, granting the prisoner more time to appeal.
The judge may respite a sentence in cases of new evidence.
The president has the power to respite the punishment of convicted criminals under certain circumstances.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reprieve - to cancel or postpone the punishment of someone, especially a criminal sentenced to death
