phantasma

noun
ghostly figure; specter
Pronunciation: / fænˈtæzmə / (AmE), / fanˈtazmə / (BrE)
a ghostly figure, apparition, or something imagined that seems unreal or illusory

Examples

A phantasma appears
In his fevered dreams, he saw a phantasma gliding through the shadows of his room.
She dismissed his story of a phantasma as a trick of the light and an overactive imagination.
The ancient castle was said to be haunted by a phantasma that roamed its halls at midnight.
At midnight, a phantasma seemed to glide silently through the old house.
The specter was dismissed as nothing more than a phantasma of the mind.
The writer described memories as fleeting phantasmas haunting the edges of consciousness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: apparition - a ghostly figure or unexpected appearance
  • synonym: phantasm - an illusory perception, ghost, or figment of the imagination
  • synonym: phantom - a ghost or illusory figure
  • antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist; not imagined
  • synonym: wraith - a ghost or spectral figure, especially seen before or after death
