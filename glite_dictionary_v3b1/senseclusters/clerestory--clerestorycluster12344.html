clerestory

noun
upper wall with windows letting in light
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈklɪrˌstɔri / (AmE), / ˈklɪəstəri / (BrE)
the upper part of a wall in a large building, such as a church or hall, that rises above adjacent roofs and contains windows to admit light into the interior

Examples

The clerestory windows
Restoration work focused on repairing the stained glass windows in the clerestory.
The architect designed the hall with a modern clerestory to provide natural illumination.
The cathedral’s clerestory allowed sunlight to stream down into the nave.
