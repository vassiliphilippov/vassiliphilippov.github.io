neocriticism

noun
philosophical movement revising Kantianism
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Àånio äÀàkr…™t…™Àås…™z…ôm / (AmE), / ÀåniÀê…ô äÀàkr…™t…™s…™z(…ô)m / (BrE)
A school of philosophy, especially associated with C. B. Renouvier, that revised and extended Kant's critical philosophy by rejecting the existence of noumena and limiting knowledge to phenomena shaped by a priori categories.

Examples

Neocriticism philosophy school
C. B. Renouvier played a central role in the development of neocriticism.
Modern debates about knowledge and perception often reference the key tenets of neocriticism.
The influence of neocriticism can be traced in many works of twentieth-century philosophy.
