progressive

adjective
tax rate that increases with income
2.7 4.9
Pronunciation: / prəˈɡrɛsɪv / (AmE), / prəˈɡrɛsɪv / (BrE)
happening, increasing, or developing gradually or in stages; advancing or becoming more severe over time (such as a disease, tax, or other process)

Examples

Progressive disease
Many argue that a progressive tax system is more equitable than a flat tax.
Some politicians advocate for making the property tax more progressive to reduce inequality.
Under the progressive tax law, those with higher incomes pay a higher percentage in taxes.
Doctors were concerned by the progressive nature of her symptoms, which worsened each week.
Research focuses on finding treatments to slow progressive forms of multiple sclerosis.
The patient was diagnosed with progressive neurological deterioration that required ongoing monitoring.
Her illness showed progressive improvement after the new treatment began.
Technological change in the industry has been progressive, influencing every stage of production.
The company adopted a progressive approach to workforce training, implementing changes step by step.
