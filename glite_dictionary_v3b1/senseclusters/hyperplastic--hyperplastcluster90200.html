hyperplastic

adjective
abnormal cell increase
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˈplæstɪk / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəˈplæstɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by an abnormal increase in the number of cells in a tissue or organ

Examples

Hyperplastic tissue growth
The doctor noted a hyperplastic response in the patient's thyroid gland.
A hyperplastic polyp was discovered during the routine colonoscopy.
Researchers studied the hyperplastic changes in the tissue samples under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • antonym: atrophic - relating to the wasting away or decrease in size of a body part or tissue
  • synonym: hypertrophic - relating to the enlargement of an organ or tissue from the increase in size of its cells
