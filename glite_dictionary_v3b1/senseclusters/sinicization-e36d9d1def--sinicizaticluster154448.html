Sinicization

noun
adapting to Chinese forms
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪnɪsɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪnɪsaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of adapting or converting foreign words, names, or scripts into Chinese language forms, including characters and pronunciation

Examples

Sinicization of names
The Sinicization of foreign place names often involves changing both their spelling and pronunciation.
Many loanwords undergo Sinicization before being widely used in Chinese society.
The Sinicization of scripts has played a key role in the history of East Asian languages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anglicization - the process of making something English in form or character
  • synonym: romanization - the process of converting a writing system to the Roman alphabet
