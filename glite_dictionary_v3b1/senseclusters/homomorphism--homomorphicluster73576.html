homomorphism

noun
mapping preserving structure (math, abstract)
9.9 9.7
Pronunciation: /hoʊ.mə.ˈmɑːr.fɪ.zəm/ /; / /hoʊ.mə.ˈmɔːr.fɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /hɒ.mə.ˈmɔː.fɪ.zəm/ /; / /həʊ.mə.ˈmɔː.fɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
A transformation or mapping between two mathematical or abstract structures that preserves the relevant operations or relations of those structures.

Examples

A homomorphism between groups
A homomorphism between two groups ensures that the product of elements is preserved under the mapping.
In computer science, a homomorphism can describe a function preserving properties in data transformations.
The concept of homomorphism is fundamental in algebra for comparing different structures.
