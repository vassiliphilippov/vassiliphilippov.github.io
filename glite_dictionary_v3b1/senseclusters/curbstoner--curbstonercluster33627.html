curbstoner

noun
street securities trader
Pronunciation: / ˈkɜːrbˌstoʊnər / (AmE), / ˈkɜːbstəʊnə / (BrE)
a broker or trader who conducted securities transactions on the street curb outside an official exchange, especially in the past

Examples

Wall Street curbstoner
In the early 1900s, a curbstoner would shout prices to attract buyers on Wall Street.
The rise of formal exchanges led to the decline of the curbstoner profession.
Many early investors relied on a curbstoner to handle their stock trades before regulations were established.

Related Terms

  • synonym: broker - a person who buys and sells assets for others
  • synonym: dealer - a person who buys and sells goods or assets
