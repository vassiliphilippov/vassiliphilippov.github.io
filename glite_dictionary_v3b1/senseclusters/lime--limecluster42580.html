lime

noun
green, acidic citrus fruit
Pronunciation: / laɪm / (AmE), / laɪm / (BrE)
a small, green, acidic citrus fruit or the tree that bears it, commonly used for its juice and flavoring

Examples

A slice of lime
A wedge of lime is often served with cocktails like a gin and tonic.
She squeezed fresh lime juice over the salad for extra flavor.
The key ingredient in this pie is freshly grated lime zest.
A lime tree requires a warm climate and plenty of sunshine to thrive.
Many commercial orchards grow both lemon and lime trees for the market.
The farmer planted several lime trees to supply his restaurant with fresh fruit.
