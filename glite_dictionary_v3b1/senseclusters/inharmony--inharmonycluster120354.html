inharmony

noun
lack of harmony
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈhɑːrməni / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɑːməni / (BrE)
a lack of harmony or agreement, especially between musical notes or sounds; discord or dissonance

Examples

Musical inharmony
The inharmony in the choir's performance was obvious to everyone in the audience.
Years of inharmony between the band members eventually led to their breakup.
A sudden inharmony in the music made the conductor stop and correct the orchestra.

Related Terms

  • synonym: discord - a lack of harmony between notes or people
  • synonym: dissonance - a harsh, disagreeable combination of sounds
  • antonym: harmony - a pleasing combination of musical notes or agreement
