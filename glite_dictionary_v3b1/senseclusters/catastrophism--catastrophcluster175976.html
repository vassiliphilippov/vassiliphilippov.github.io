catastrophism

noun
emphasis on disaster
9.1
Pronunciation: / kəˈtæstrəˌfɪzəm / (AmE), / kəˈtæstrəˌfɪzəm / (BrE)
the tendency to predict, emphasize, or exaggerate the likelihood of catastrophic events or disasters, especially in discussions or theories.

Examples

Media catastrophism
The scientist was criticized for his catastrophism when discussing the effects of climate change.
Some media outlets have been accused of catastrophism in their coverage of global events.
Her catastrophism made it difficult for her to see any positive outcomes in the situation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alarmism - the tendency to exaggerate dangers or risks
  • antonym: optimism - the tendency to expect the best possible outcome
