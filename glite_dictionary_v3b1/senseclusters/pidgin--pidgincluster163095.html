pidgin

noun
simplified language for communication between groups
8.2 5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈpɪdʒ.ɪn/ /; / /ˈpɪdʒ.ən/ (AmE), /ˈpɪdʒ.ɪn/ (BrE)
A simplified language that develops as a means of communication between speakers of different native languages, especially for trade.

Examples

A pidgin language
A new pidgin developed along the coast as a result of expanding trade.
Many workers from different countries communicated using a pidgin that allowed them to do business.
This book explores how a simple pidgin can evolve into a more complex language over time.
