quench

verb
suppress or crush completely
Pronunciation: / kwɛntʃ / (AmE), / kwɛntʃ / (BrE)
to suppress or crush completely, especially something such as a feeling, action, or movement

Examples

To quench your fears
She struggled to quench her fears in front of the team.
The government quickly moved to quench the uprising before it gained momentum.
The new laws are designed to quench any attempt at opposition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: extinguish - to put an end to or destroy something
  • synonym: suppress - to put an end to something, such as an activity or feeling, forcibly
