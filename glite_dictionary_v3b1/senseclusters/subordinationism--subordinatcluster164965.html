subordinationism

noun
belief in hierarchy
Pronunciation: / səˌbɔːrdɪˈneɪʃənɪzəm / (AmE), / səˌbɔːdɪˈneɪʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief or doctrine that one person or group should be subordinate to another in social, legal, or political status

Examples

Subordinationism in society
The new law was criticized for promoting subordinationism and reinforcing social inequality.
Many activists oppose subordinationism in any form, arguing for equal treatment of all groups.
Historical systems of subordinationism have left lasting effects on modern society.

Related Terms

  • antonym: egalitarianism - the belief that all people should have equal rights and opportunities
  • synonym: hierarchism - the belief in or support of a hierarchical system
