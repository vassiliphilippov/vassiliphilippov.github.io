impulse relay

noun
relay holding contact without power
7.4 8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈɪm.pʌls rɪˈleɪ / (AmE), / ˈɪm.pʌls rɪˈleɪ / (BrE)
a relay that maintains its switched contact position indefinitely without power applied to the coil, typically used in electrical circuits for switching operations

Examples

An impulse relay switch
Technicians installed an impulse relay to reduce energy waste caused by constantly powered coils.
The building's lighting system uses an impulse relay to allow lights to be controlled from multiple locations.
When the button is pressed, the impulse relay changes state and remains in position until triggered again.

Related Terms

  • synonym: latching relay - a similar relay that holds its state after an input pulse
