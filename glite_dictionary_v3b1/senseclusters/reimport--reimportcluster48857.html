reimport

verb
to bring goods back
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪmˈpɔrt / (AmE), / ˌriːɪmˈpɔːt / (BrE)
to bring goods or products back into a country after they have previously been exported

Examples

To reimport goods
Due to new regulations, the company had to reimport its own products from overseas.
After exporting the artwork, the museum decided to reimport it for a special exhibition.
Sometimes businesses reimport goods when domestic demand unexpectedly increases.
Due to new regulations, the company had to reimport several batches of its products.
After being exported for processing, the materials were reimported for final assembly.
The government imposed a tax on reimporting luxury vehicles that were previously sold abroad.

Related Terms

  • antonym: export - to send goods to another country for sale
  • synonym: import - to bring goods into a country from abroad
