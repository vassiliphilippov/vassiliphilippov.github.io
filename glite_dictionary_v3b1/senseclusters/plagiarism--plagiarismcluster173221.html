plagiarism

noun
taking and passing off others' work as one's own
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈpleɪdʒəˌrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈpleɪdʒərɪzəm / (BrE)
the act of copying someone else's work or ideas and pretending they are your own, or something that has been copied in this way

Examples

Plagiarism in writing
Academic institutions take plagiarism very seriously and often use software to detect it.
He apologized after being accused of plagiarism in his research paper.
The student was expelled for plagiarism after submitting an essay copied from the internet.
Many universities have strict policies to prevent plagiarism in academic work.
She was accused of plagiarism after it was discovered her speech was copied from another author.
The essay you submitted is clear plagiarism and will not be accepted for credit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: copying - the act of reproducing someone else's work
  • antonym: originality - the quality of being novel and unique; not derived from something else
