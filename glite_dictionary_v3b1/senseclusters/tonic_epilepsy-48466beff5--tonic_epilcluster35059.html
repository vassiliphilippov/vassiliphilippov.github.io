tonic epilepsy

noun
epilepsy with rigid body seizures
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑːnɪk ˌɛpɪˈlɛpsi / (AmE), / ˈtɒnɪk ˌɛpɪˈlɛpsi / (BrE)
A form of epilepsy in which a seizure causes the body to become rigid due to sustained muscle contraction.

Examples

tonic epilepsy seizure
During a tonic epilepsy episode, the patient's muscles became extremely stiff and immobile.
The neurologist explained that tonic epilepsy is characterized by sudden, rigid seizures rather than convulsions.
Treatment for tonic epilepsy often involves medication to help control the frequency of rigid seizures.

  • synonym: epilepsy - A neurological disorder marked by recurrent seizures
