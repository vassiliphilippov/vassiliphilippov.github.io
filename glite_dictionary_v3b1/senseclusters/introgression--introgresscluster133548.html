introgression

noun
gene transfer between species
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪn.trə.ˈgreʃ.ən/ (AmE), /ɪn.trə.ˈgreʃ.ən/ (BrE)
the movement of genes from one species into the gene pool of another through repeated backcrossing

Examples

Introgression of genes
Researchers observed introgression of disease-resistant genes from wild plants into cultivated crops.
The study focused on the introgression of genetic material between closely related animal species.
Conservationists monitor introgression to understand how hybridization affects endangered populations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hybridization - the process of combining different varieties or species to create a hybrid
  • antonym: speciation - the formation of new and distinct species in the course of evolution
