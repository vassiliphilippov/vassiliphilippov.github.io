motor fiber

noun
nerve fiber carrying impulses to muscles or glands
9.9 5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈmoʊtər ˈfaɪbər / (AmE), / ˈməʊtə ˈfaɪbə / (BrE)
a nerve fiber that carries nerve impulses from the central nervous system to muscles or glands, causing them to act

Examples

Motor fiber nerve
Diseases affecting the motor fiber can result in difficulties with movement or coordination.
Each motor fiber transmits signals from the spinal cord to a specific group of muscle fibers.
The doctor explained that damage to the motor fiber could lead to muscle weakness in the affected limb.
