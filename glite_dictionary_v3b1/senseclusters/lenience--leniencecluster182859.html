lenience

noun
quality of being merciful or tolerant
Pronunciation: / ˈliːniəns / (AmE), / ˈliːniəns / (BrE)
the quality of being merciful, tolerant, or not strict in judging or punishing others

Examples

Show lenience to mistakes
He pleaded for lenience, hoping the committee would forgive his mistake.
Her parents' lenience allowed her more freedom than most children her age.
The judge showed lenience and reduced the sentence for the first-time offender.
Parents sometimes offer lenience to children who admit their mistakes.
The judge showed unexpected lenience and reduced the defendant’s sentence.
The teacher’s lenience allowed several students to retake the exam.
The judge’s lenience surprised both the defendant and the attorneys.
The principal showed lenience toward the students who confessed their mistakes.
Too much lenience from parents can lead to spoiled children.
