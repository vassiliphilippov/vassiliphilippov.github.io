oversimplify

verb
simplify excessively, losing accuracy
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈsɪmplɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈsɪmplɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to simplify something so much that a distorted impression or misunderstanding results

Examples

To oversimplify complex issues
It's easy to oversimplify complex scientific issues when talking to a general audience.
She tends to oversimplify problems, ignoring critical details.
We must not oversimplify the history of the conflict for the sake of convenience.
