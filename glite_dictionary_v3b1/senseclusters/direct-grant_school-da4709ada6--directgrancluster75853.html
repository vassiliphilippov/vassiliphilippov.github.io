direct-grant school

noun
historic UK school with fees and government grants
Pronunciation: / dəˈrɛkt ɡrænt skuːl / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛkt ɡrɑːnt skuːl / (BrE)
formerly in the UK, a school that charged tuition fees but also received government funding in exchange for admitting a quota of non-fee-paying students selected by local authorities.

Examples

A direct-grant school
Admission to a direct-grant school could be gained through a local authority nomination or by paying fees.
Many prominent politicians attended direct-grant schools before the scheme ended in the 1970s.
The direct-grant school system offered a mix of private and publicly funded education.
