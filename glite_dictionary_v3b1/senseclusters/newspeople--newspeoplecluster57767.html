newspeople

noun
journalists and reporters
3.1 0.13/m
Pronunciation: / ÀànuÀêzÀåpip…ôl / (AmE), / ÀànjuÀêzÀåpiÀêp…ôl / (BrE)
people who work in gathering, reporting, or presenting news for newspapers, television, radio, or online media

Examples

Newspeople report stories
Newspeople gathered outside the courthouse to cover the trial.
Many newspeople work long hours to deliver breaking stories to the public.
The conference room was filled with newspeople eager to ask questions at the press event.

Synonyms

  • newsperson
    2.8 0.03/m
    newsperson refers to one person who gathers, reports, or edits news across media, while newspeople means that group collectively.
  • journalist
    2.3 54/m
    journalist often writes or investigates stories in print or online and may edit features, while newspeople covers all who work in news.
  • reporter
    1.1 112/m
    reporter investigates and presents news for broadcasts or papers, often with on-the-scene work, while newspeople include that role and others.

Related Terms

  • antonym: audience - people who watch, read, or listen to news
  • synonym: journalists - people who write or report news for media
  • synonym: reporters - people who gather and report news stories
