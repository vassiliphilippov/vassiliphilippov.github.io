plethysmograph

noun
device for measuring body or organ volume changes
9.9
Pronunciation: / pləˈθɪz.məˌɡræf / (AmE), / plɛˈθɪz.məˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
an instrument used for measuring changes in the volume of a part or organ of the body, usually as a result of fluctuations in the amount of blood it contains

Examples

Modern plethysmograph device
Modern plethysmograph devices provide highly accurate readings of blood flow in the limbs.
Researchers employed a plethysmograph to track changes in finger volume during the experiment.
The technician used a plethysmograph to measure the patient's lung volume.
