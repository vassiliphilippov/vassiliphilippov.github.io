suppositious

adjective
Based on supposition, not evidence

Based primarily on supposition or surmise rather than on adequate evidence; not supported by facts.

Examples

A suppositious idea
Critics argued that her conclusions were merely suppositious and not grounded in reality.
Much of the early history of the region is suppositious, reconstructed from scarce archaeological evidence.
The scientist dismissed the suppositious theory as lacking sufficient data to support its claims.

Synonyms

  • supposititious
    9.9 0.01/m
    supposititious is a variant form seen in some texts, while suppositious is the original adjective with the same meaning.
  • suppositional
    9.3 0.00/m
    suppositional describes ideas based on assumption, while suppositious means the idea is based on mere surmise without solid proof.
  • conjectural
    8.2 0.27/m
    conjectural refers to guesses from little information or evidence, while suppositious implies conclusions formed mainly from mere supposition.
  • hypothetical
    6.9 8.97/m
    hypothetical suggests a proposed idea for testing or discussion, while suppositious implies it rests on weak or no evidence.
  • unsubstantiated
    7.5 1.91/m
    unsubstantiated describes a claim with no proof, while suppositious stresses that the claim is based on guesswork or surmise.
  • unsupported
    5.0 0.79/m
    unsupported means lacking evidence or backing, while suppositious focuses on the idea coming from assumption rather than facts.
