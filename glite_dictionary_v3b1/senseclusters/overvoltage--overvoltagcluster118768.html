overvoltage

noun
sudden voltage surge
7.2
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ˈvoʊl.tɪdʒ/ /; / /ˈoʊ.vər.voʊl.tɪdʒ/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ˈvɒl.tɪdʒ/ /; / /ˈəʊ.və.vɒl.tɪdʒ/ (BrE)
a voltage in an electrical system that is higher than the normal or safe value, which can cause damage

Examples

An overvoltage event
A sudden overvoltage can damage sensitive electronic equipment in your home.
The technician installed a device to protect the circuit from overvoltage events.
Lightning strikes are a common cause of overvoltage in power lines.
The equipment failed after an unexpected overvoltage occurred during the storm.
Surge protectors are used to guard devices against overvoltage in power lines.
An overvoltage can damage sensitive electronic components if not properly managed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: surge - a sudden increase in electrical voltage
  • antonym: undervoltage - a drop in voltage below the normal level in an electrical system
