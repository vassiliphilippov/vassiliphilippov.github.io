blubber

noun
thick fat layer on marine mammals
5.5 0.95/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆblÊŒbÉš / (AmE), / ËˆblÊŒbÉ™ / (BrE)
a thick layer of fat under the skin of whales and other large marine mammals, used to keep them warm in cold water and as a source of oil

Examples

Whale blubber
In the past, people hunted whales for their blubber to produce oil.
Researchers analyzed the blubber of seals to monitor pollution levels in the ocean.
The whale's blubber helps it survive in freezing temperatures.
