skiagram

noun
photographic image produced by X-rays or gamma rays
Pronunciation: / ˈskaɪ.əˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈskaɪ.əˌɡræm / (BrE)
a photographic image produced on a radiosensitive surface by radiation other than visible light, especially X-rays or gamma rays; an X-ray image

Examples

A skiagram of chest
A clear skiagram of the chest is essential for diagnosing certain lung diseases.
Early clinicians relied on the skiagram to detect hidden injuries.
The doctor examined the skiagram to identify the fracture in the patient's arm.
