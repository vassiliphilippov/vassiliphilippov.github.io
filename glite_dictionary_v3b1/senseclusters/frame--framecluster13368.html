frame

verb
devise or construct plans or details
Pronunciation: / freɪm / (AmE), / freɪm / (BrE)
to create or formulate the basic structure or details of something, such as a plan, question, or idea

Examples

To frame a question
Lawmakers spent weeks framing regulations to address the industry crisis.
She was asked to frame the initial proposal for the company's expansion.
The committee met to frame the new environmental policy.
Can you frame the question more clearly for the audience?
She carefully framed her criticism so as not to offend anyone.
The proposal was framed in diplomatic language to avoid misunderstanding.

Related Terms

  • synonym: draft - to prepare a preliminary version of a document or plan
  • synonym: formulate - to create or devise methodically (a strategy or proposal)
  • synonym: phrase - to express in a particular way or wording
  • synonym: word - to express or choose the specific words for something
