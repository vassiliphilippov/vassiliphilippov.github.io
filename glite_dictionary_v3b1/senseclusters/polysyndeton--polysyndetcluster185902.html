polysyndeton

noun
multiple conjunctions in succession
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːlɪˈsɪndəˌtɑn / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈsɪndɪtən / (BrE)
The use of several conjunctions in close succession, especially where some could be omitted, as a stylistic device.

Examples

Polysyndeton in writing
In the sentence 'she laughed and danced and sang,' polysyndeton is clearly present.
Some writers employ polysyndeton for dramatic or rhetorical effect.
The author used polysyndeton to create a sense of excitement in the passage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: asyndeton - omission of conjunctions where they would normally be used
