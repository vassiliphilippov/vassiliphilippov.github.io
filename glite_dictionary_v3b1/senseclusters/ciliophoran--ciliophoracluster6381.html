ciliophoran

noun
protozoan with hair-like cilia
4.3
Pronunciation: / sɪlɪˈɑːfərən / (AmE), / sɪlɪˈɒfərən / (BrE)
A type of protozoan distinguished by the presence of cilia, microscopic hair-like appendages that extend from the surface of the cell and aid in movement and feeding.

Examples

A ciliophoran protozoan
Paramecium is a well-known example of a ciliophoran found in freshwater environments.
Scientists observed the ciliophoran under a microscope to study its unique movement.
The ciliophoran uses its cilia to propel itself and capture food particles.
