adoxography

noun
skilled praise of trivial things
Pronunciation: / ˌæd.ɑːkˈsɑː.ɡrə.fi / (AmE), / ˌad.ɒkˈsɒɡ.rə.fi / (BrE)
fine or skilled writing in praise of trivial, unimportant, or base subjects

Examples

Fine adoxography writing
His speech was a classic case of adoxography, extolling the virtues of an utterly useless artifact.
The professor admired her adoxography when she eloquently praised the humble potato in her essay.
Victorian writers sometimes engaged in adoxography to demonstrate their rhetorical skills in debating societies.
