look like a walrus

verb
resemble a walrus in appearance
Pronunciation: / lʊk laɪk ə ˈwɔlɹəs / (AmE), / lʊk laɪk ə ˈwɔːl.rəs / (BrE)
to have an appearance that resembles a walrus, often implying bulkiness or prominent facial features such as a large mustache or whiskers

Examples

To look like a walrus
After not shaving for a month, he started to look like a walrus.
Bundled up in my oversized coat, I look like a walrus in the mirror.
He laughed and said his new mustache made him look like a walrus.
