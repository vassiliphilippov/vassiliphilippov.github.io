limp

verb
walk with difficulty due to injury
2.6 5.22/m
Pronunciation: / lÉªmp / (AmE), / lÉªmp / (BrE)
to walk or move with difficulty, usually because of an injury or weakness

Examples

To limp along
Despite his injury, Thomas was determined and limped to the finish line.
He started to limp after twisting his ankle during the football match.
She limped across the parking lot, wincing at every step.
After the accident, the old truck limped along the highway until it finally broke down.
Despite a damaged engine, the fishing boat limped back to shore.
The project limped to completion after facing several major setbacks.

Synonyms

  • walk with a limp
    3.7 0.22/m
    walk with a limp is walking unevenly because of leg or foot pain, while limp means moving with difficulty.
  • hitch
    5.6 0.61/m
    hitch is a short, uneven step often from injury, while limp is moving slowly and with difficulty.
  • hobble
    5.6 2.32/m
    hobble is walking with short, stiff steps and sometimes using support, while limp is weaker, uneven walking.
