deer fly

noun
fishing lure resembling insect
6.1
Pronunciation: /dɪr flaɪ/ /; / /diːr flaɪ/ (AmE), /dɪə flaɪ/ /; / /diːə flaɪ/ (BrE)
an artificial fishing lure made to resemble a deer fly insect, used in fly-fishing to attract fish

Examples

Fishing deer fly
He tied a bright deer fly to his line to attract trout near the stream.
The angler selected a deer fly from his tackle box for the afternoon fishing trip.
Using a deer fly can be effective when fish are feeding on insects near the water's surface.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dry fly - an artificial fly that floats on the water surface
  • antonym: wet fly - an artificial fly designed to sink below the water surface
