Nelson

proper noun
British admiral, hero of Trafalgar
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛlsən / (AmE), / ˈnɛlsən / (BrE)
Horatio Nelson (1758–1805), an English admiral celebrated for his victories over the French fleets during the Napoleonic Wars, notably at the Battle of Trafalgar, where he was mortally wounded.

Examples

Nelson statue Trafalgar
Nelson is honored with a statue in Trafalgar Square, London, for his naval achievements.
Many books and films recount the heroic last moments of Admiral Nelson.
Students in Britain often learn about Nelson and his role in the Battle of Trafalgar.
