cinchonine

noun
antimalarial alkaloid from cinchona bark
Pronunciation: / sɪnˈkoʊniːn / (AmE), / sɪnˈkəʊniːn / (BrE)
an alkaloid found in the bark of cinchona trees, formerly used as an antimalarial drug

Examples

cinchonine alkaloid bark
Modern medicine has replaced cinchonine with more effective synthetic drugs for malaria.
Researchers studied the effects of cinchonine on malaria parasites in the laboratory.
The pharmacist explained that cinchonine was once a popular treatment for fevers.
