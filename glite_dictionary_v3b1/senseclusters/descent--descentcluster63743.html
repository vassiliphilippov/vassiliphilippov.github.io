descent

noun
movement downward
4.2 3.7
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsɛnt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛnt / (BrE)
the act or process of moving downward from a higher to a lower place, or a slope that goes down

Examples

Steep descent
He lost his footing during the steep descent of the mountain trail.
The hikers began their descent into the valley as the sun set.
The plane's descent was smooth, and we landed safely.
After hours of climbing, the hikers enjoyed a swift descent down the mountain.
During our descent, we could feel the airplane rapidly losing altitude.
The pilots began their descent toward the airport as they prepared to land.
After a long climb, the hikers finally enjoyed an easy descent into the valley.
Be careful on this part of the trail—the descent is sharp and covered in loose stones.
The steep descent made walking difficult in the rain.
