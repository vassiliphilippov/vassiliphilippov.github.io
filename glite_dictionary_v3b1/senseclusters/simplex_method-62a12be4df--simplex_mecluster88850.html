simplex method

noun
optimization method in linear programming
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪmplɛks ˈmɛθəd / (AmE), / ˈsɪmplɛks ˈmɛθəd / (BrE)
a method in linear programming for finding the optimal solution by systematically moving between adjacent vertices of the feasible region so that the objective function value improves or remains unchanged at each step

Examples

The simplex method algorithm
Applying the simplex method, the economist identified the most efficient way to allocate the resources.
Many business analysts learn the simplex method to solve complex supply chain issues.
The simplex method is widely used in operations research for solving large optimization problems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: linear programming - mathematical method for optimizing a linear objective function subject to linear constraints
