genius

noun
person with exceptional intellectual ability
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒiːniəs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːniəs / (BrE)
a person with exceptional intellectual or creative ability

Examples

A genius scientist
Einstein is widely regarded as a genius for his groundbreaking theories.
She has the genius to solve problems that leave others puzzled.
The company's founder was a marketing genius who transformed the industry.
Einstein is often cited as the quintessential genius of the twentieth century.
Her colleagues regarded her as a genius in the field of molecular biology.
Only a true genius could have solved that complex problem so quickly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dunce - a person who is slow to learn; a person lacking intellectual ability
  • antonym: idiot - a person of very low intelligence (offensive, historical sense)
  • synonym: mastermind - a person with outstanding intellect, often planning or directing projects
  • synonym: prodigy - a young person with exceptional abilities or talents
  • degree lower: talent - natural aptitude or skill
