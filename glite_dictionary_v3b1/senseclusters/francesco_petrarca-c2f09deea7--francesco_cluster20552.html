Francesco Petrarca

proper noun
Italian poet and scholar (1304–1374)
Pronunciation: / frænˈtʃɛs.koʊ peɪˈtrɑːrkə / (AmE), / franˈtʃɛs.kəʊ peˈtrɑːkə / (BrE)
(1304–1374) An Italian poet and humanist, renowned for his love sonnets and regarded as one of the earliest Renaissance scholars.

Examples

Francesco Petrarca the poet
Francesco Petrarca is often credited as the 'father of Humanism' due to his revival of classical literature.
Many of Francesco Petrarca's poems express his intense admiration for Laura, his muse.
Students of literature still study the profound influence of Francesco Petrarca on Renaissance poetry.
