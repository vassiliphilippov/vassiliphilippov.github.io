cutwater

noun
ship's bow projection
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌtˌwɔtər / (AmE), / ˈkʌtˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
a projecting part at the front of a ship or boat designed to divide and cut through water as it moves forward

Examples

Ship's cutwater
The cutwater sliced cleanly through the waves as the ship sailed forward.
Barnacles had collected on the cutwater after months at sea.
Engineers designed the cutwater to improve the boat's speed and stability.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bow - the front part of a ship or boat
