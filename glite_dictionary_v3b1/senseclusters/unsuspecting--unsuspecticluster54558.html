unsuspecting

adjective
not suspicious or aware of danger
Pronunciation: / ˌʌn.səˈspɛk.tɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌn.səˈspɛk.tɪŋ / (BrE)
not aware that something bad, dangerous, or dishonest might happen; not expecting harm or deceit

Examples

An unsuspecting person
She smiled warmly, her expression entirely unsuspecting of any deception.
The package was left outside the unsuspecting victim’s home.
Tourists are often easy targets for scammers because they are unsuspecting.
He entered the dark room, unsuspecting of the surprise waiting for him.
She handed the envelope to her unsuspecting colleague, who had no idea it was a birthday card.
The unsuspecting tourist was easily tricked by the street magician.
