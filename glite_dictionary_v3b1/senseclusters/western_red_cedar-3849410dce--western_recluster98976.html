western red cedar

noun
large Pacific Northwest coniferous tree
8.5 4.7
Pronunciation: /ˈwɛs.tərn rɛd ˈsiː.dər/ /; / /ˈhwɛs.tərn rɛd ˈsiː.dər/ (AmE), /ˈwɛs.tən rɛd ˈsiː.də/ (BrE)
a large valuable coniferous tree (Thuja plicata) native to the northwestern United States and Canada, known for its durable, reddish wood used in construction and furniture

Examples

A western red cedar tree
Many Native American tribes have traditionally used western red cedar to make canoes and totem poles.
The lumber from western red cedar is prized for its resistance to decay.
We planted a row of western red cedar trees to serve as a natural fence.
