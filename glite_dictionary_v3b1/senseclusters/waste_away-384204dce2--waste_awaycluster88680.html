waste away

verb
gradually weaken and decline
9.3 4.4
Pronunciation: /weɪst ə.ˈweɪ/ (AmE), /weɪst ə.ˈweɪ/ (BrE)
to gradually become weak, thin, or diminished in strength, often due to illness, neglect, or lack of nourishment

Examples

To waste away slowly
If you don't water your houseplants, they'll waste away in a matter of weeks.
She felt helpless watching her friend slowly waste away in the hospital.
The elderly man continued to waste away after he lost his appetite for food.

Related Terms

  • synonym: atrophy - to waste away, especially of a body part due to degeneration of cells
  • antonym: thrive - to grow, develop, or be successful
  • synonym: wither - to become dry and weak, often through neglect or lack of sustenance
