foxing

noun
paper stains
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑksɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɒksɪŋ / (BrE)
brownish spots or stains that develop on old paper, especially in books, due to age or moisture

Examples

Old foxing stains
The rare first edition had noticeable foxing on several of its pages.
Collectors often try to prevent foxing by storing books in dry conditions.
Despite the foxing, the old letter remained legible and valuable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mold - a growth of fungi that can cause spots or damage on paper
  • synonym: staining - the process of becoming marked or discolored
