reordain

verb
to ordain again
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɔrˈdeɪn / (AmE), / ˌriːɔːˈdeɪn / (BrE)
to officially make someone a priest or minister again after they have previously been ordained

Examples

To reordain a priest
After years away from the church, the bishop agreed to reordain him as a minister.
The council voted unanimously to reordain the priest following his return to the community.
She was reordained after completing additional theological studies.
