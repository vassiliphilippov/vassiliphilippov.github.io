premarket

noun
before market opens
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːˌmɑrkɪt / (AmE), / ˈpriːˌmɑːkɪt / (BrE)
the period before the official opening of a stock market when trading or activity may occur

Examples

Premarket session
Traders analyzed premarket data to predict how stocks might perform during the day.
Significant price changes can sometimes happen in the premarket session before regular trading begins.
News released overnight often affects premarket activity and investor sentiment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aftermarket - the period after the official closing of a stock market
