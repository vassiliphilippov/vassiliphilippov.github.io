consensus

noun
general agreement within a group
3.5
Pronunciation: / kənˈsɛnsəs / (AmE), / kənˈsɛnsəs / (BrE)
a general agreement among a group of people, especially in terms of beliefs, judgments, or decisions

Examples

Reach a consensus
After hours of discussion, the committee finally reached a consensus on the best course of action.
It is difficult to achieve consensus when everyone has different priorities.
There was a growing consensus that changes needed to be made to the policy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agreement - a negotiated and typically legally binding arrangement between parties
  • antonym: disagreement - a lack of consensus or harmony, especially in opinion
