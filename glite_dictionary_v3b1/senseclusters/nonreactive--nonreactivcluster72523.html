nonreactive

adjective
no reaction (test)
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnriˈæktɪv / (AmE), / ˌnɒnriˈæktɪv / (BrE)
showing no reaction, especially in a medical test, indicating a negative or absent result

Examples

Nonreactive test result
The blood sample was nonreactive, so no further testing was needed.
If the result is nonreactive, it means the patient does not have the infection.
A nonreactive test result can provide reassurance to patients awaiting a diagnosis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: negative - showing no evidence of a condition or substance in a test
  • antonym: reactive - showing a response or reaction, especially in a test
