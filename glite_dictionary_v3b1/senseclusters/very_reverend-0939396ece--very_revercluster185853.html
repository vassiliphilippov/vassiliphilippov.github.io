Very Reverend

noun
ecclesiastical title for some clergy
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛri ˈrɛv(ə)rənd / (AmE), / ˈvɛri ˈrɛv(ə)rənd / (BrE)
a formal title of respect given to certain clerical officials such as cathedral deans and canons in some Christian churches

Examples

The Very Reverend title
As the Very Reverend, she addressed the congregation during the special ceremony.
The cathedral appointed a new Very Reverend to oversee its daily operations.
The invitation was formally sent to the Very Reverend John Ellis, Dean of the college.
