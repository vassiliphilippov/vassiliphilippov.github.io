recontextualize

verb
to change context
8.3 0.19/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀêk…ônÀàt…õkst Éu…ôÀåla…™z / (AmE), / ÀåriÀêk…ônÀàt…õkstj ä…ôla…™z / (BrE)
to place something in a new or different context in order to change or clarify its meaning

Examples

To recontextualize old photos
The artist recontextualized old photographs to create a powerful new message.
Historical events are often recontextualized in films to appeal to modern audiences.
By quoting the poem in her speech, she recontextualized its meaning for a different purpose.

Synonyms

  • resituate
    7.7 0.07/m
    resituate places something in a new setting or framework, while recontextualize aims to change its meaning by altering context.
  • reframe
    4.8 1.16/m
    reframe changes how you present or think about an idea to make it more useful or positive, while recontextualize moves it into a different context.
  • reinterpret
    5.8 1.93/m
    reinterpret explains something in a new way, while recontextualize places it in a different context to change or clarify its meaning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contextualize - to place something in a particular context for understanding
  • synonym: reinterpret - to explain or understand something in a new or different way
