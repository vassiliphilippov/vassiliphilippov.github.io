recontextualize

verb
to change context
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkənˈtɛkstʃuəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˈtɛkstjʊəlaɪz / (BrE)
to place something in a new or different context in order to change or clarify its meaning

Examples

To recontextualize old photos
The artist recontextualized old photographs to create a powerful new message.
Historical events are often recontextualized in films to appeal to modern audiences.
By quoting the poem in her speech, she recontextualized its meaning for a different purpose.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contextualize - to place something in a particular context for understanding
  • synonym: reinterpret - to explain or understand something in a new or different way
