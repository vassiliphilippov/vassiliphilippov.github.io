compound leaf

noun
botanical leaf made of leaflets on one stalk
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑm.paʊnd liːf / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.paʊnd liːf / (BrE)
a leaf consisting of several distinct leaflets joined to a single stalk.

Examples

A compound leaf with leaflets
In textbooks, the distinction between a simple and a compound leaf is often illustrated with clear diagrams.
The rose plant has a compound leaf structure with multiple leaflets attached along the stem.
Unlike a simple leaf, a compound leaf is divided into several segments called leaflets.

Related Terms

  • antonym: simple leaf - a leaf that is not divided into leaflets
