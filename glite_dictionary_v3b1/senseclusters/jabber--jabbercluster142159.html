jabber

verb
talk rapidly and excitedly
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒæb.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʒæb.ə / (BrE)
to talk or speak rapidly and excitedly, often in a way that is hard to understand

Examples

To jabber quickly
He tends to jabber on the phone when he's nervous, barely stopping to breathe.
The children began to jabber excitedly as soon as the bell rang for recess.
They were jabbering so loudly in the café that I couldn't concentrate on my work.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: chat - to talk in a friendly and informal way
  • synonym: mutter - to speak in a low or barely audible voice
  • synonym: prattle - to talk at length in a foolish or inconsequential way
