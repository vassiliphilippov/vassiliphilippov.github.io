recidivistic

adjective
prone to relapse
Pronunciation: / rɪˌsɪdəˈvɪstɪk / (AmE), / rɪˌsɪdɪˈvɪstɪk / (BrE)
tending to relapse into previous bad behavior or criminal activity, especially after punishment or treatment

Examples

Recidivistic behavior
The court was concerned about his recidivistic tendencies after several repeated offenses.
Programs aim to reduce recidivistic behavior among former inmates through education and support.
Her recidivistic actions made it difficult for her to avoid returning to prison.

Related Terms

  • synonym: habitual - done regularly or repeatedly
  • synonym: incorrigible - unable to be corrected or reformed
  • antonym: reformed - improved in behavior; no longer engaging in bad habits
