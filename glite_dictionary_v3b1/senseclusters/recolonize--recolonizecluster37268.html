recolonize

verb
to establish again
6.3 0.09/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀêÀàk…ëÀêl…ôÀåna…™z / (AmE), / ÀåriÀêÀàk…íl…ôna…™z / (BrE)
to establish a population or colony again in a place after it was abandoned, destroyed, or cleared

Examples

To recolonize an area
After the fire, birds began to recolonize the forest.
Scientists observed how plants slowly recolonized the barren land after the volcanic eruption.
It can take decades for coral reefs to recolonize areas damaged by storms.
After the wildfire, native plants slowly began to recolonize the damaged hillside.
Scientists observed that fish species started to recolonize the restored river within a year.
Conservation efforts helped endangered animals recolonize their former habitats.
After the glacier melted, new plant species began to recolonize the valley.
Scientists observed how quickly the fish could recolonize the restored river habitat.
The abandoned island was recolonized by seabirds within a few years.

Synonyms

  • repopulate
    4.1 0.54/m
    repopulate fills a place again with people, animals, or plants, while recolonize involves settling and creating a new colony there.
  • reinhabit
    7.6 0.02/m
    reinhabit means people return to live in a place, while recolonize involves establishing a new, organized population or colony there.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abandon - to leave a place or thing permanently
  • degree lower: colonize - to establish a population in a new area
  • synonym: repopulate - to fill an area with people or animals again
  • synonym: resettle - to settle again in a new or former place
