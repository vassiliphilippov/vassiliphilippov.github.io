Sauterne

noun
semisweet golden dessert wine from Bordeaux
Pronunciation: / soʊˈtɜrn / (AmE), / sɔːˈtɜːn / (BrE)
A semisweet, golden-colored table or dessert wine, originally from the Bordeaux region of France; also refers to similar wines produced elsewhere, such as California.

Examples

A bottle of Sauterne
A bottle of chilled Sauterne complements the sweetness of the cake.
He enjoyed a glass of Sauterne with his dessert.
They served Sauterne at the wedding reception, pairing it with cheese and fruit.
