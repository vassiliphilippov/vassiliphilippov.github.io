primitivity

noun
polynomial property
Pronunciation: / ˌprɪməˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌprɪmɪˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
the property of a polynomial whose coefficients have no common factor greater than 1

Examples

Primitivity of a polynomial
The mathematician verified the primitivity of the equation before proceeding with the proof.
Ensuring primitivity is important when simplifying polynomials in number theory.
The software automatically checks for primitivity in all input polynomials.
