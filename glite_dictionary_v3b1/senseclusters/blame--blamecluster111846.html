blame

verb
to hold responsible for something bad
Pronunciation: / bleɪm / (AmE), / bleɪm / (BrE)
to say or think that someone or something is responsible for something bad or wrong

Examples

To blame a person
Don't blame me if you arrive late to the meeting.
He always tries to blame others for his own mistakes.
It's easy to blame the weather when things go wrong.
It’s unfair to blame others for mistakes you made yourself.
She tried to blame her lateness on the traffic, but no one believed her.
The manager blamed the poor results on lack of preparation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accuse - to say someone has done something wrong
  • antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration of someone or something
