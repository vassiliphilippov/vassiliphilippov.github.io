shotgun approach

noun
strategy using many simultaneous attempts
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɑtˌɡʌn əˈproʊtʃ / (AmE), / ˈʃɒtˌɡʌn əˈprəʊtʃ / (BrE)
a strategy in which many different attempts or methods are used simultaneously, rather than focusing narrowly on one

Examples

A shotgun approach strategy
Researchers used a shotgun approach when screening hundreds of compounds for effectiveness.
The marketing team adopted a shotgun approach to reach as many potential customers as possible.
While the shotgun approach may cover more ground, it can be less efficient than a targeted strategy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scattergun approach - strategy using many different, non-targeted attempts
