fast lane

noun
hectic, high-pressure lifestyle
4.9
Pronunciation: / fæst leɪn / (AmE), / fɑːst leɪn / (BrE)
a way of living or working that is exciting, active, and sometimes risky, often involving glamour, high energy, or pressure

Examples

Life in the fast lane
After her promotion, she found herself living in the fast lane and rarely had time to relax.
Many celebrities are drawn to the fast lane, but not everyone can handle the pace.
Tired of the fast lane, he decided to move to the countryside for a quieter life.
After his band became famous, he started living in the fast lane.
Not everyone can handle the risks and pressure of the fast lane.
She was drawn to the excitement of the fast lane in New York City.
