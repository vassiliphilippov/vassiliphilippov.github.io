jackboot

noun
high, sturdy knee-length boot
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒækˌbuːt / (AmE), / ˈdʒæk.buːt / (BrE)
a high, sturdy leather boot reaching above the knee, often with a tassel, worn historically by men, especially soldiers or police officers

Examples

A jackboot with tassel
Collectors often seek out antique military jackboots for their historical value.
In old portraits, noblemen are often depicted with tall jackboots and ornate uniforms.
The cavalry officer wore a pair of polished jackboots during the parade.
