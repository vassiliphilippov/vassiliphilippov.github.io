self-consciously

adverb
in an embarrassed or uneasy manner
2.7
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlfˈkɑːnʃəsli / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈkɒnʃəsli / (BrE)
In a way that shows embarrassment or awareness of being observed, often leading to discomfort.

Examples

Speak self-consciously in public
He spoke self-consciously in front of the large audience.
She laughed self-consciously when she realized everyone was watching her.
The actor adjusted his tie self-consciously before stepping onto the stage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: confidently - in a manner that shows confidence
