gum

verb
chew food with the gums only
Pronunciation: / ɡʌm / (AmE), / ɡʌm / (BrE)
to chew food slowly and with difficulty using the gums instead of teeth, especially because of tooth loss

Examples

To gum bread
After losing his dentures, Harold could only gum his bread at the table.
The baby tried to gum the soft fruit pieces, making a mess at lunchtime.
The elderly dog would gum his food before swallowing it, since he had very few teeth left.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chew - to bite and work food in the mouth with the teeth, especially in order to make it easier to swallow
