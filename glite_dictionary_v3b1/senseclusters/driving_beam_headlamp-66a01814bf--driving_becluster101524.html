driving beam headlamp

noun
car headlamp with a bright, centre-focused beam

A car headlamp designed to provide a bright, centre-focused beam of light without controlling light emitted towards other drivers, suitable only for use when no other vehicles are present due to potential glare.

Examples

A driving beam headlamp shines
He switched on the driving beam headlamp once the road was empty, illuminating the highway ahead.
It's illegal to use driving beam headlamps when approaching oncoming traffic, as the glare can blind other drivers.
Modern vehicles are often equipped with driving beam headlamps for enhanced nighttime visibility on rural roads.

    5.0 0.00/m
    full beam headlamp gives a very bright, focused beam that can dazzle other drivers, while driving beam headlamp is used only when roads are clear.
  • main beam headlamp
    5.0 0.00/m
    main beam headlamp provides a strong centre-weighted beam that can dazzle oncoming drivers, while driving beam headlamp is used only on roads with no other vehicles.
  • high beam headlamp
    4.1 0.00/m
    high beam headlamp produces a bright, far-reaching beam that does not dip for others, while driving beam headlamp focuses centre light and must be used only with no other vehicles present.
  • high beam
