micronutrient

noun
substance needed in small amounts for health
1.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪ.kroʊˈnuː.tri.ənt / (AmE), / ˌmʌɪ.krəʊˈnjuː.tri.ənt / (BrE)
a substance, such as a vitamin or mineral, required by the body in very small amounts for normal growth, development, and health

Examples

Essential micronutrient intake
A deficiency of any essential micronutrient can lead to serious health problems.
Dark leafy greens are an excellent source of several important micronutrients.
Nutritionists recommend a varied diet to ensure all necessary micronutrients are consumed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: macronutrient - a substance required in relatively large amounts for body function (such as protein, fat, or carbohydrate)
