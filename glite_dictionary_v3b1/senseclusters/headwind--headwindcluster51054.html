headwind

noun
wind blowing against movement
3.9 4.3
Pronunciation: /ˈhe.dwɪnd/ (AmE), /ˈhe.dwɪnd/ (BrE)
a wind blowing directly against the direction of travel of a ship, aircraft, or other vehicle, making progress more difficult

Examples

Strong headwinds
Despite the headwinds, the cyclists managed to maintain a steady pace throughout the race.
Sailing against a headwind requires more skill and effort from the crew.
The airplane faced a strong headwind during takeoff, extending the time needed to leave the ground.

Related Terms

  • antonym: tailwind - a wind blowing in the same direction as movement
