pepper

noun
woody vine with peppercorns (Piper nigrum)
1.0 3.59/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆpÉ›pÉ™r / (AmE), / ËˆpÉ›pÉ™ / (BrE)
A climbing woody vine (Piper nigrum) native to southern India and Sri Lanka, cultivated for its dark red berries, known as peppercorns, which are the source of black, white, and green pepper used as a spice.

Examples

Pepper plant berries
Farmers harvest the ripe pepper berries before they are dried to make black pepper.
The pepper vine thrives in the humid climate of southern India.
Wild pepper can be found growing in parts of Sri Lanka and Burma.

Synonyms

  • Piper nigrum
    6.1 0.01/m
    Piper nigrum is the scientific name used by botanists, while pepper is the common kitchen name for the same vine.
  • black pepper
    1.0 0.01/m
    black pepper is the ground spice made from dried berries, while pepper is the plant whose berries make black, white, and green spices.
  • common pepper
    2.3 0.00/m
    common pepper is the everyday cooking name for the plant's dried berries, while pepper is the general word for the plant and its spices.
