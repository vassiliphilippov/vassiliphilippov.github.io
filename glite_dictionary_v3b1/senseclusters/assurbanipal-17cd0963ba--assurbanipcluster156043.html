Assurbanipal

proper noun
king of Assyria (668–627 BC)
Pronunciation: / ɑːs.ʊrˈbæn.ɪ.pɑːl / (AmE), / ˌæs.jʊrˈbæn.ɪ.pæl / (BrE)
King of Assyria from 668 to 627 BC, known for building a magnificent palace and establishing a famous library at Nineveh.

Examples

Assurbanipal king of Assyria
Assurbanipal is remembered for establishing one of the ancient world’s greatest libraries in Nineveh.
Artifacts from Assurbanipal’s palace have provided valuable insights into Assyrian art and society.
Historians often study the reign of Assurbanipal to understand the cultural achievements of Assyria.
