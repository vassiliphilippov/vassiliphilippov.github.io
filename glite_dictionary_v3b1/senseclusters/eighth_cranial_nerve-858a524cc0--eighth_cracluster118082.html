eighth cranial nerve

noun
nerve transmitting sound and balance information
6.6
Pronunciation: / eɪθ ˈkreɪniəl nɜrv / (AmE), / eɪtθ ˈkreɪniəl nɜːv / (BrE)
the sensory nerve that transmits sound and balance information from the inner ear to the brain; also called the vestibulocochlear nerve

Examples

The eighth cranial nerve function
Damage to the eighth cranial nerve can result in hearing loss or dizziness.
Surgeons must be careful not to injure the eighth cranial nerve during procedures involving the inner ear.
The eighth cranial nerve is vital for both hearing and balance functions in humans.
