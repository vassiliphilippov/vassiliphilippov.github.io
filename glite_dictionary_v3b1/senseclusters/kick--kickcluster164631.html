kick

verb
stop a habit or addiction
Pronunciation: / kɪk / (AmE), / kɪk / (BrE)
to stop doing or consuming something, especially a harmful habit, usually after a period of dependence

Examples

To kick a habit
After ten years, he finally managed to kick his smoking habit for good.
Many people struggle to kick their reliance on caffeine.
She decided it was time to kick sugar and start a healthier lifestyle.
