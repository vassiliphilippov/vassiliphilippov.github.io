diesel

noun
petroleum-based fuel
2.4 3.41/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàdiÀêz…ôl / (AmE), / ÀàdiÀêz…ôl / (BrE)
a type of fuel made from petroleum, commonly used in engines of trucks, buses, and some cars

Examples

Diesel fuel
The bus runs on diesel instead of gasoline to save on fuel costs.
Many large trucks use diesel because it provides more power for heavy loads.
She filled the tank with diesel before starting her long journey.

Synonyms

  • diesel fuel
    2.8 0.97/m
    diesel fuel is the common vehicle fuel term used at pumps and garages, while diesel is the general name for that fuel.
  • diesel oil
    2.8 0.07/m
    diesel oil is a heavier, more technical name used in industry or older texts, while diesel is the common everyday fuel name.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gasoline - a liquid fuel made from petroleum, used in most cars
  • antonym: petrol - the British term for gasoline, a fuel for cars
