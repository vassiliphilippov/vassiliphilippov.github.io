infest

verb
to overrun or live on/in as parasites
7.2 4.4
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfɛst / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɛst / (BrE)
for animals, insects, or parasites to be present in large numbers in a place, causing harm or annoyance

Examples

Rats infest a house
Parasitic worms infest the intestines of their hosts, leading to health problems.
The old house was infested with termites, requiring immediate attention.
Ticks often infest dogs and can cause discomfort or disease.
Certain parasites infest the fur of wild animals in this region.
If left unchecked, weeds can quickly infest a garden, choking out other plants.
The kitchen became infested with ants after we left food out overnight.
After the flood, mosquitoes began to infest the area, making it difficult to go outside.
If you don't keep food sealed, ants will quickly infest your kitchen.
The old barn was infested with rats and had to be cleaned thoroughly.
