Gram stain

noun
bacterial classification stain technique
8.3
Pronunciation: / ɡræm steɪn / (AmE), / ɡram steɪn / (BrE)
a laboratory staining technique used to classify bacteria as either Gram-positive or Gram-negative, based on their reaction to gentian violet and other reagents

Examples

Gram stain test
A Gram stain helps doctors quickly distinguish between Gram-positive and Gram-negative organisms.
Technicians performed a Gram stain on the sample to aid in identifying the infection.
The laboratory confirmed the bacterial type with a Gram stain before starting treatment.
