quick

adjective
easily aroused or excitable (especially emotionally)
Pronunciation: / kwɪk / (AmE), / kwɪk / (BrE)
(of a person) easily aroused or excited, especially emotionally; reacting strongly or swiftly, particularly with emotions such as anger.

Examples

Quick to anger
Children can be quick to anger if they feel misunderstood.
He has a quick temper and sometimes overreacts to criticism.
The coach is quick to praise players who show improvement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - not easily aroused or excited
  • synonym: excitable - easily excited or aroused
