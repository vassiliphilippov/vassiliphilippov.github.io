stratus cloud

noun
a low, gray, layered cloud
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈstreɪtəs klaʊd / (AmE), / ˈstrætəs klaʊd / (BrE)
a continuous, extensive, low-lying, gray cloud layer, typically covering the sky and often producing drizzly or misty weather

Examples

A thick stratus cloud
A thick stratus cloud hid the sunrise from view this morning.
As the plane descended, we entered a sheet of stratus cloud just above the city.
The weather forecast mentioned low stratus clouds bringing a persistent drizzle across the region.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cumulus cloud - a puffy, white, often towering cloud
  • degree higher: nimbus cloud - a dense, dark rain cloud
