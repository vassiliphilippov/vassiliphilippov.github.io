radio-opacity

noun
opacity to X-rays or radiation
7.9 6.4
Pronunciation: /reɪ.di.oʊ.əˈpæs.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /ˈreɪ.di.oʊ oʊˈpæs.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /reɪ.di.əʊ.əˈpæs.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /ˈreɪ.di.əʊ əʊˈpæs.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
The property of being opaque to X-rays or other types of radiation, preventing their passage through a material or object.

Examples

High radio-opacity material
A higher degree of radio-opacity is desirable in dental fillings for easy identification.
The radio-opacity of the implant material allows it to be clearly seen on X-rays.
The doctor evaluated the radio-opacity of the mass to help determine its composition.

Related Terms

  • antonym: radiolucency - property of being permeable to X-rays or other radiation
