eaglehawk

noun
large bird of prey
Pronunciation: / ˈiːɡəlˌhɔk / (AmE), / ˈiːɡəlˌhɔːk / (BrE)
a large bird of prey, especially one that has features of both an eagle and a hawk

Examples

The eaglehawk soars
The eaglehawk soared high above the forest searching for food.
Farmers sometimes spot a eaglehawk circling their fields in the early morning.
With its sharp eyesight, the eaglehawk can spot prey from great distances.

Related Terms

  • synonym: eagle - a large, powerful bird of prey with a hooked beak
  • synonym: hawk - a medium-sized bird of prey with sharp talons and beak
