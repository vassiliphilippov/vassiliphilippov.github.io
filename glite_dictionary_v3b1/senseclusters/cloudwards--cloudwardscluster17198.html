cloudwards

adverb
toward more clouds
Pronunciation: / ˈklaʊdwɚdz / (AmE), / ˈklaʊdwədz / (BrE)
toward increased cloud cover or in the direction of becoming more cloudy or overcast

Examples

Sky turns cloudwards
As the morning progressed, the sky shifted cloudwards and the sun disappeared.
The weather forecast predicted that conditions would move cloudwards by the evening.
Sailors watched the horizon turn cloudwards, preparing for a possible storm.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: clear - with no clouds in the sky
  • degree higher: overcast - with a sky covered by clouds
