Umbellales

noun
order of flowering plants with umbels or corymbs
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌʌmbəˈleɪliːz / (AmE), / ˌʌmbɪˈleɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of flowering plants characterized by umbels or corymbs of uniovulate flowers, including families such as the Umbelliferae and Cornaceae

Examples

Umbellales plant order
Botanists study the Umbellales to understand the evolutionary relationships among different plant families.
Many herbs commonly used in cooking, such as parsley and carrot, belong to the Umbellales.
The Umbellales are distinguished by their characteristic umbrella-shaped clusters of flowers.
