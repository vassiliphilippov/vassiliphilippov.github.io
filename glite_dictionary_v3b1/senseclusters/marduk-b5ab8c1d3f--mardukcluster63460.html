Marduk

proper noun
chief god of Babylonian mythology
9.9 0.17/m
Pronunciation: / Ààm…ër.duÀêk / (AmE), / Ààm…ëÀê.duÀêk / (BrE)
the chief god of Babylonian mythology, regarded as the patron deity of the city of Babylon

Examples

Marduk the god
During the festival of Akitu, the Babylonians honored Marduk with elaborate rituals.
Many ancient texts describe Marduk as the creator of the world and the leader of the gods.
The myth of Marduk defeating Tiamat symbolizes the victory of order over chaos in Babylonian lore.

Synonyms

  • Merodach
    9.9 0.01/m
    Merodach is the same name used in many ancient texts for the chief Babylonian god, while Marduk is the familiar English name.
  • Bel-Merodach
    9.9 0.00/m
    Bel-Merodach is the form that adds the title 'Bel' to the god's name, while Marduk is the god's basic personal name.
  • Baal Merodach
    9.9 0.00/m
    Baal Merodach appears in some sources using 'Baal' as a lord title, while Marduk is the standard Babylonian name.
