exceptionality

noun
formal exemption
7.9
Pronunciation: / ɪkˌsɛpʃəˈnæləti / (AmE), / ɪkˌsɛpʃəˈnæləti / (BrE)
a formal exemption or exclusion from a general rule, requirement, or regulation

Examples

Exceptionality from rules
The committee granted exceptionality to the project, allowing it to proceed despite the usual restrictions.
Due to its historical value, the building received exceptionality from the new zoning laws.
They argued that exceptionality should only be given in rare and justified cases.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exemption - the act of freeing someone or something from an obligation or rule
  • antonym: inclusion - the act of including or being included within a group or structure
