exposure

noun
a photographic image or print
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈspoʊʒɚ / (AmE), / ɪkˈspəʊʒə / (BrE)
the process in photography of allowing light to reach film or a sensor, or the photograph created by this process

Examples

Camera exposure
Each exposure was carefully catalogued in her digital archive.
He developed the film and chose the best exposure for the exhibition.
The photographer adjusted the settings to get the perfect exposure of the sunset.
Proper exposure is crucial for capturing the right amount of detail in a picture.
She adjusted the camera settings to ensure the exposure was just right.
The exposure was too long, resulting in a washed-out photograph.
A long exposure is necessary to capture the stars at night.
He adjusted the camera to get the correct exposure for the low light.
The photo came out too dark because the exposure was too short.
