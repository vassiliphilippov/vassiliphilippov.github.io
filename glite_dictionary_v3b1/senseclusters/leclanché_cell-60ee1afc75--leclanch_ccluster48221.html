Leclanché cell

noun
type of electrical cell, 1.5 volts
Pronunciation: / lɛˈklɑn(t)ʃeɪ sɛl / (AmE), / lɛˈklɑ̃ʃeɪ sɛl / (BrE)
a primary voltaic cell producing about 1.5 volts, historically used in early batteries

Examples

A Leclanché cell battery
Although largely replaced today, the Leclanché cell played a key role in battery development.
Many early telegraphs were powered by the Leclanché cell, appreciated for its reliability.
The original flashlight used a Leclanché cell as its power source.

  • synonym: dry cell - a type of electric battery with a paste electrolyte
