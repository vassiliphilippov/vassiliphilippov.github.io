supersede

verb
to replace or take the place of
7.2 1.27/m
Pronunciation: / suÀê.p…öÀàsiÀêd / (AmE), / ÀåsuÀê.p…ôÀàsiÀêd / (BrE)
to take the place of (someone or something previously in authority or use); to replace or displace.

Examples

To supersede old technology
Digital cameras have almost completely superseded film photography in recent decades.
Many believe that online learning will supersede traditional classroom education in the future.
The older regulations were superseded by a more comprehensive policy last year.

Synonyms

  • replace
    1.8 89/m
    replace means to put a new thing or person in the place of another, while supersede suggests taking over former authority or use.
  • supplant
    7.5 3.15/m
    supplant often means to take someone's place by force or by being seen as better, while supersede is a neutral formal replacement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: displace - to cause to move from its usual place or position
  • synonym: replace - to take the place of something or someone
