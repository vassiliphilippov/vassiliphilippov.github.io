novelty

noun
small inexpensive mass-produced item
8.4 3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑːvəlti / (AmE), / ˈnɒvəlti / (BrE)
a small, inexpensive, or showy object, often bought as a gift, souvenir, or decoration

Examples

A novelty toy
Every year, the company gives away holiday novelties to its employees as a token of appreciation.
He bought a silly novelty hat for the party, which made everyone laugh.
The shop sells all sorts of novelties for tourists, including keychains and magnets.
She loved to decorate her bag with every new novelty she could find at the market.
Some people enjoy collecting novelty items, even if they are cheap and flashy.
The stall was filled with colorful novelties and trinkets for children.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bauble - a small, showy trinket or decoration
  • synonym: souvenir - an object kept as a reminder of a person, place, or event
  • synonym: trinket - a small ornament or item of jewelry that is of little value
