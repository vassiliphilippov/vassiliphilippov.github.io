carnage

noun
mass killing; slaughter
8.2 4/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ërn…™d í / (AmE), / Ààk…ëÀên…™d í / (BrE)
the killing of a large number of people, especially in a violent or brutal way

Examples

Mass carnage
Newspapers reported the scale of carnage in the aftermath of the terrorist attack.
The battlefield was littered with the evidence of carnage after the brutal conflict.
The city tried to recover from the carnage caused by the devastating explosion.

Synonyms

  • massacre
    7.0 12/m
    massacre is often an indiscriminate killing of many people, while carnage highlights the brutal, bloody scene and wide destruction.
  • slaughter
    5.7 4/m
    slaughter is violent, excessive killing often used for animals or people, while carnage stresses mass human deaths and brutality.
  • butchery
    8.1 0.1/m
    butchery shows savage, cruel killing and a messy scene, while carnage refers to widespread violent killing of people.
  • bloodbath
    7.0 1/m
    bloodbath describes a scene where many people are killed in a short time, while carnage focuses on the brutal violence.
  • mass murder
    6.7 2/m
    mass murder is the deliberate killing of many people, while carnage can include both deliberate and chaotic violent deaths.
  • bloodshed
    4.6 5/m
    bloodshed means killing or wounding in fights or war over time, while carnage usually means sudden, large-scale deadly violence.
  • bloodletting
    9.3 0.5/m
    bloodletting refers to political or large-scale killing often as policy, while carnage is the violent killing seen at scenes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bloodshed - the killing or wounding of people, usually during a violent event
  • synonym: massacre - the deliberate and brutal killing of many people
  • synonym: slaughter - the killing of a large number of people or animals violently
