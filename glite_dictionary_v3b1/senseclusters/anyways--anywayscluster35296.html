anyways

adverb
used to support or resume a statement (informal)
Pronunciation: / ˈɛniˌweɪz / (AmE), / ˈɛniˌweɪz / (BrE)
(informal) Used to emphasize a statement or to change the subject, often to support or explain a previous statement or to resume a topic.

Examples

Anyways, I say
Anyways, let me know if you change your mind about joining us tonight.
I didn't like that movie, but anyways, what do you want to do now?
They said the weather would be bad, but we decided to go hiking anyways.
