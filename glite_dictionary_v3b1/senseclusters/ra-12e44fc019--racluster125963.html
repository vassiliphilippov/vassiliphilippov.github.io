Ra

proper noun
ancient Egyptian sun god
9.9 1.01/m
Pronunciation: / r…ëÀê / (AmE), / r…ëÀê / (BrE)
the ancient Egyptian sun god, often depicted with the head of a hawk, regarded as a universal creator and later merged with Amen to become Amen-Ra, king of the gods.

Examples

Egyptian god Ra
In ancient mythology, Ra was worshipped as the supreme creator and giver of life.
Many pharaohs claimed to be the earthly sons of Ra, solidifying their divine right to rule.
The priests of Ra held great influence in the city of Heliopolis.
