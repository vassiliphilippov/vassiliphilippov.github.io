currawong

noun
Australian fruit-eating songbird
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈkʊrəˌwɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈkʌrəwɒŋ / (BrE)
a large, dark-plumaged Australian songbird with a resonant, bell-like call, belonging to the genus Strepera

Examples

A currawong bird
Some farmers consider currawongs a nuisance because they eat fruit from orchards.
The distinctive call of the currawong echoed through the Australian forest at dusk.
We saw a flock of currawongs feeding in the trees near the picnic area.

Related Terms

  • synonym: magpie - a large, black-and-white songbird closely related to the currawong
