palatalization

noun
softening of consonants
9.5
Pronunciation: /pæl.ə.təl.ɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /pæl.ə.təl.ɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a change in pronunciation where a consonant is pronounced closer to the hard palate or with a 'y'-like quality

Examples

Palatalization of consonants
In Russian, palatalization distinguishes many pairs of consonants, making the language sound softer.
The palatalization of certain consonants is common in the pronunciation of some English words.
Linguists study palatalization to understand how sounds change in different languages.
The palatalization of certain consonants is a common feature in Slavic languages.
In the history of English, palatalization affected the pronunciation of some words borrowed from Latin.
Linguists study palatalization to understand how languages evolve over time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assimilation - a process where a sound becomes more like a neighboring sound
  • antonym: depalatalization - a process where a palatalized sound loses its palatal quality
  • synonym: lenition - a phonological process where consonants become softer or weaker
