yanquapin

noun
North American water lily with edible seeds
Pronunciation: / jæn.kwə.pɪn / (AmE), / jæn.kwə.pɪn / (BrE)
A water lily native to eastern North America with pale yellow flowers and edible, globular, nutlike seeds.

Examples

Yellow yanquapin flower
Botanists surveyed the wetlands for healthy yanquapin populations.

You may see yanquapin growing in shallow ponds during late spring.
