fault

verb
blame or criticize
4.5
Pronunciation: / fɔlt / (AmE), / fɔːlt / (BrE)
to blame or criticize someone for a mistake or problem

Examples

To fault her actions
It's unfair to fault her for something that was beyond her control.
Many people fault the government for the current economic crisis.
The coach refused to fault the team after their narrow loss.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blame - to consider responsible for a fault or wrong
  • antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration of someone or something
