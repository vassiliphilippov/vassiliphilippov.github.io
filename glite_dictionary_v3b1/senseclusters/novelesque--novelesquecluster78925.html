novelesque

adjective
like a novel
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑːvəlˈɛsk / (AmE), / ˌnɒvəlˈɛsk / (BrE)
having qualities typical of a novel, such as being long, detailed, imaginative, or involving multiple characters and dramatic events

Examples

A novelesque story
The director gave the film a novelesque feel by focusing on several intertwined storylines.
Her memoir is so novelesque that readers often forget it is nonfiction.
The series' novelesque structure allows for deep character development and complex plots.
The film's novelesque plot kept the audience guessing until the very end.
He described his childhood as novelesque, filled with unexpected adventures and colorful characters.
The city at night had a novelesque atmosphere, mysterious and full of possibilities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: episodic - divided into separate or loosely connected parts
  • synonym: narrative - having the form or style of a story
  • antonym: realistic - representing things as they are, not imaginative or dramatic
  • synonym: storylike - having qualities similar to a story
