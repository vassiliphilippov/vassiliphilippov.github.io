dyslogistic

adjective
expressing disapproval or criticism
Pronunciation: / dɪsˌlɔˈɡɪstɪk / (AmE), / dɪsˌlɒˈɡɪstɪk / (BrE)
expressing or conveying disapproval or censure; disparaging.

Examples

dyslogistic words
He deliberately used dyslogistic language to belittle his opponent during the debate.
The critique was full of dyslogistic remarks about the author's writing style.
Their report was far from neutral; it contained several dyslogistic observations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: laudatory - expressing praise; complimentary
  • synonym: pejorative - expressing contempt or disapproval
