overline

noun
horizontal line above text
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvərˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəlaɪn / (BrE)
a horizontal line drawn above a letter, symbol, or group of characters to indicate a special meaning

Examples

A math overline
In mathematics, an overline is used to show that a decimal repeats indefinitely.
The teacher asked students to place an overline above the variable to indicate its average value.
Some dictionaries use an overline to mark stressed syllables in pronunciation guides.

Related Terms

  • antonym: underline - a line drawn below text for emphasis or distinction
