order of the day

noun
most important issue or practice at a time
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrdər əv ðə deɪ / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə əv ðə deɪ / (BrE)
the most important or prevailing issue, practice, or activity at a particular time

Examples

Teamwork is the order of the day
At the annual meeting, transparency was clearly the order of the day.
During the crisis, swift action became the order of the day.
Innovation is the order of the day in the technology industry.
