sen

noun
fractional currency unit in Japan, Indonesia, Cambodia
Pronunciation: / sɛn / (AmE), / sen /; / sɛn / (BrE)
a monetary unit equal to one hundredth of the main currency in several Asian countries (such as Japan, Indonesia, Malaysia, and Cambodia)

Examples

One sen coin
In Indonesia, one hundred sen make up one rupiah.
The coin was worth five sen before it was discontinued in Japan.
The collector displayed several rare Cambodian sen from the early twentieth century.
The price of the snack was fifty sen.
He found a few sen coins at the bottom of his bag.
A single sen is not enough to buy anything these days.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cent - a monetary unit equal to one hundredth of a basic unit of currency
  • degree higher: ringgit - the basic monetary unit of Malaysia
