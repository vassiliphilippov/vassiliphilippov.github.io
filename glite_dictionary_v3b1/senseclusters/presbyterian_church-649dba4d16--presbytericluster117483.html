Presbyterian Church

noun
Protestant Christian denomination (Calvinist)
Pronunciation: / ˌprɛz.bɪˈtɪr.i.ən tʃɝːtʃ / (AmE), / ˌprɛz.bɪˈtɪə.ri.ən tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
A Protestant Christian denomination following the teachings and organizational structure inspired by John Calvin, characterized by a system of church government by elders.

Examples

The Presbyterian Church minister
He became a minister in the local Presbyterian Church after completing his theology studies.
She grew up attending a Presbyterian Church with her family every Sunday.
The Presbyterian Church played an important role in Scottish history and education.
