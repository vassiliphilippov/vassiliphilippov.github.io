monohull

noun
single-hulled boat
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑnoʊˌhʌl / (AmE), / ˈmɒnəʊˌhʌl / (BrE)
a boat or ship that has a single hull, rather than two or more hulls

Examples

A monohull boat
The experienced sailor chose a monohull for the solo ocean crossing.
Many traditional yachts are built as monohulls for their classic design and handling.
He compared the stability of a monohull to that of a catamaran before making his purchase.

Related Terms

  • antonym: catamaran - a boat with two parallel hulls
  • antonym: trimaran - a boat with three hulls
