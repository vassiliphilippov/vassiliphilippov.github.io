interphase

noun
boundary layer in materials
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɪntərˌfeɪz / (AmE), / ˈɪntəˌfeɪz / (BrE)
a region or layer between two different phases or materials that has properties distinct from the surrounding phases

Examples

Interphase layer in materials
The interphase in composite materials plays a crucial role in determining mechanical properties.
Scientists studied the interphase to understand how the two materials interact at the microscopic level.
A well-designed interphase can improve the durability and strength of advanced coatings.
