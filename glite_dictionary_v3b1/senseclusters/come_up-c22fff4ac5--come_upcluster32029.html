come up

verb
produce or suggest (idea, plan, etc.)
3.2 43/m
Pronunciation: / kÊŒm ÊŒp / (AmE), / kÊŒm ÊŒp / (BrE)
to produce or bring forth (an idea, suggestion, plan, etc.), especially something desirable or useful

Examples

Come up with an idea
Can you come up with a better way to organize the files?
During the meeting, several employees came up with suggestions to improve productivity.
She always manages to come up with creative solutions to difficult problems.

Synonyms

  • come up with
    2.7 59/m
    come up with is the full phrase people use to produce ideas or plans, while come up alone usually does not mean that.
  • think up
    3.2 2.47/m
    think up means to invent something by active effort, while come up often refers to producing a needed idea or suggestion.
  • think of
    2.1 32/m
    think of can mean invent or recall an idea in thought, while come up focuses on producing useful new ideas.
  • dream up
    3.9 2.41/m
    dream up describes imagining a playful or unusual idea, while come up usually means producing a practical or useful suggestion.
  • conjure up
    6.2 3.29/m
    conjure up calls up images or ideas as if by magic, while come up means producing an idea in normal speech.
  • create
    1.0 400/m
    create means making or bringing something new into existence, while come up refers specifically to producing ideas or suggestions.
  • bring forth
    4.1 1.37/m
    bring forth sounds formal and means to produce or present something, while come up is more common for new ideas.
