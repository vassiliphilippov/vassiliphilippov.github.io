rev

noun
engine revolution; unit of motor speed
5.8 2.70/m
Pronunciation: / rÉ›v / (AmE), / rÉ›v / (BrE)
a revolution of an engine per minute or a unit for measuring the rotational speed of a motor

Examples

High revs
Let the engine idle at low revs before you start driving.
Racing cars often reach higher rev limits than standard automobiles.
The engine was running at 3000 revs per minute on the highway.

Synonyms

  • revolutions per minute
    3.5 0.16/m
    revolutions per minute names the full unit used in manuals and displays, while rev is a short, informal label for the same measure.
  • rpm
    3.1 4.60/m
    rpm is the common abbreviation seen on dials and manuals, while rev is a looser short form sometimes used in speech.
  • revolution
    3.5 4.92/m
    revolution is a single complete turn of an object, while rev is the unit name often used to measure turns per minute.
