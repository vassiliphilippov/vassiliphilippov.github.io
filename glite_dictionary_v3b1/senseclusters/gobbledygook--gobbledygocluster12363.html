gobbledygook

noun
unintelligible jargon; language full of jargon
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑbəldiˌɡʊk / (AmE), / ˈɡɒbəldɪˌɡuːk / (BrE)
language that is meaningless or hard to understand because it is full of jargon or technical terms; pompous or unintelligible speech or writing.

Examples

Gobbledygook language
During the meeting, the consultant's gobbledygook left everyone confused about the real issues.
Please avoid using gobbledygook in your presentation and stick to plain English.
The report was full of gobbledygook that ordinary readers couldn't understand.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jargon - special words or expressions used by a profession or group
  • synonym: nonsense - language or ideas that are absurd or meaningless
