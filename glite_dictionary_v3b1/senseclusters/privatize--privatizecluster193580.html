privatize

verb
transfer from public to private ownership
3.1 5.2
Pronunciation: /ˈpraɪ.və.taɪz/ /; / /ˈprɪ.və.taɪz/ (AmE), /ˈprɪ.və.taɪz/ /; / /ˈpraɪ.və.taɪz/ (BrE)
to transfer a business, industry, or service from public (government) ownership or control to private ownership or control

Examples

To privatize a company
Several countries have privatized their water utilities over the last decade.
The debate about whether to privatize healthcare continues among policymakers.
The government decided to privatize the national airline to improve efficiency.
Many workers were worried about job security when the company was privatised.
Some argue that it is not always beneficial to privatise public services.
The government decided to privatise several major utilities to encourage competition.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nationalise - transfer from private to government ownership
  • antonym: nationalize - to transfer from private to state ownership or control
  • synonym: privatize - transfer from public to private ownership (spelling variant, mainly US)
  • synonym: publicise - to make public, share with the public (different meaning but similar form)
