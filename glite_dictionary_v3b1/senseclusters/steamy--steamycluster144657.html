steamy

adjective
hot and humid (weather or place)
1.0 2.5
Pronunciation: / ˈsti.mi / (AmE), / ˈstiː.mi / (BrE)
(of weather or a place) hot or warm and humid, often uncomfortably so.

Examples

Steamy weather
It was so steamy outside that my clothes stuck to my skin.
The weather turned steamy after the afternoon rainstorm.
We walked through the steamy jungle, sweating within minutes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dry - without moisture or humidity; not wet
  • synonym: humid - having a high level of moisture in the air
  • synonym: muggy - (of weather) unpleasantly warm and humid
