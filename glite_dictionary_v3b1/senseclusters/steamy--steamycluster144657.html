steamy

adjective
hot and humid (weather or place)
3.0 1.0/m
Pronunciation: / Ààsti.mi / (AmE), / ÀàstiÀê.mi / (BrE)
(of weather or a place) hot or warm and humid, often uncomfortably so.

Examples

Steamy weather
It was so steamy outside that my clothes stuck to my skin.
The weather turned steamy after the afternoon rainstorm.
We walked through the steamy jungle, sweating within minutes.

Synonyms

  • muggy
    2.6 0.8/m
    muggy is unpleasantly warm and humid and makes the air feel heavy, while steamy often adds a wet, clammy heat.
  • sultry
    5.3 0.6/m
    sultry is oppressively hot and humid with a heavy, almost sensual feel, while steamy simply means hot and humid.
  • sticky
    1.0 1/m
    sticky is hot and humid in a way that makes your skin sweat and cling, while steamy is more general warmth.
  • sweltering
    4.8 0.01/m
    sweltering is intensely hot and often causes heavy sweating, while steamy usually focuses on heat combined with humidity.
  • sweltry
    9.1 0.00/m
    sweltry is an older, strong word for oppressive heat that causes sweating, while steamy is commonly used for humid warmth.
  • close
    3.4 2/m
    close is warm and lacking fresh air, often feeling stuffy indoors, while steamy highlights wet heat and humidity.
  • humid
    1.0 4/m
    humid means air that contains much moisture and can be simply damp, while steamy suggests an uncomfortably hot, humid feeling.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dry - without moisture or humidity; not wet
  • synonym: humid - having a high level of moisture in the air
  • synonym: muggy - (of weather) unpleasantly warm and humid
