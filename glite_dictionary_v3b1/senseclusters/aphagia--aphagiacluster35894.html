aphagia

noun
loss of ability to swallow
Pronunciation: / əˈfeɪdʒiə / (AmE), / əˈfeɪdʒiə / (BrE)
the loss or absence of the ability to swallow

Examples

aphagia from nerve damage
Doctors diagnosed her with aphagia due to nerve damage in her throat.
The patient developed aphagia after his stroke, requiring a feeding tube for nutrition.
Treatment for aphagia often involves a team of specialists including speech therapists.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: dysphagia - difficulty or discomfort in swallowing
