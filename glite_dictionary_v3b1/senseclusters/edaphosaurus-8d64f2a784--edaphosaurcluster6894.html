Edaphosaurus

proper noun
prehistoric genus of sail-backed reptiles
Pronunciation: / ˌɛdəfoʊˈsɔrəs / (AmE), / ˌɛdəfəˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
a genus of large, early synapsid reptiles from the Late Carboniferous to Early Permian periods, known for their distinctive sail-like back structure

Examples

Edaphosaurus sail back
Researchers believe that the sail of Edaphosaurus played a role in regulating body temperature.
The fossilized remains of Edaphosaurus have been found in North America and Europe.
Unlike Dimetrodon, Edaphosaurus had small, herbivorous teeth and a distinctive crossbarred sail.
