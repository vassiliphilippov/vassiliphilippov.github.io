rod

noun
unit of length equal to 16.5 feet
9.6 0.12/m
Pronunciation: / rÉ‘d / (AmE), / rÉ’d / (BrE)
a former unit of length equal to 16.5 feet (approximately 5.03 meters), used especially in surveying land.

Examples

A rod of land
A fence post was placed every rod along the property line.
Maps from the 1800s sometimes use the rod as a unit of distance.
The land was measured as ten rods wide by twenty rods long.

Synonyms

  • pole
    9.9 0.37/m
    pole is an old unit of length often named in countryside records, while rod is the surveying term for land measurement.
  • perch
    9.9 0.03/m
    perch is a historical unit used for land area or length in old records, while rod is the surveying unit used by mapmakers.
