crush

verb
become damaged by pressure
1.0 1.4
Pronunciation: /krʌʃ/ (AmE), /krʌʃ/ (BrE)
to press or squeeze something with force so that it breaks, becomes deformed, or is destroyed

Examples

To crush a can
If you squeeze the fruit too hard, it will crush and leak juice everywhere.
The empty can crushed under my foot with a loud pop.
The fragile box crushed easily when more weight was placed on top of it.
He used his foot to crush the empty can before throwing it away.
She accidentally crushed the crackers while trying to open the package.
The weight of the boulder threatened to crush the fragile flowers underneath.
Be careful not to crush the biscuits when packing them into the tin.
She used a hammer to crush the garlic cloves before cooking.
The machine can crush old cars into tiny cubes for recycling.

Related Terms

  • antonym: expand - to make or become larger or more extensive
  • synonym: flatten - to make or become flat
  • synonym: grind - reduce something to small particles or powder by crushing it
  • synonym: smash - break into pieces suddenly and violently
  • synonym: squash - to compress or squeeze something so that it becomes flat or loses its shape
  • synonym: squeeze - to compress something firmly, especially with the hands
