soak up

verb
absorb (liquid or experience)
Pronunciation: / soʊk ʌp / (AmE), / səʊk ʌp / (BrE)
to take in information, experience, or atmosphere completely; to become deeply absorbed in something

Examples

Soak up knowledge
He soaked up every word of advice his mentor gave him.
She likes to soak up the atmosphere at local cafes.
The towel will soak up any spills on the counter.
During the internship, she tried to soak up as much experience as possible.
He loves to soak up knowledge in every book he reads.
She decided to soak up everything she could about the new culture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absorb - to take in or soak up (liquid, energy, or information)
  • synonym: immerse oneself - to involve oneself deeply in a particular activity or interest
