Synonyms

  • join in
    1.1 9.68/m
    join in means taking part in an activity already happening, while join can mean becoming a member or simply taking part.
  • participate
    2.5 99/m
    participate means taking part or being involved in an activity, while join also implies becoming a member or joining a group.
  • take part
    1.4 13/m
    take part means to participate in an event with others, while join can also mean becoming a member of a group.
  • partake in
    4.3 1.04/m
    partake in sounds more formal and often refers to joining an experience, while join is more common and general in speech.
  • enroll
    2.7 29/m
    enroll means to officially register as a member or student, while join can be informal and may not require formal registration.
  • sign up
    1.3 20/m
    sign up means to register or agree to take part, while join can mean simply becoming a member without formal registration.
  • enter
    1.1 41/m
    enter means to register for or begin taking part in an event, while join more broadly means to become a member or take part.
  • fall in
    3.7 9.99/m
    fall in means to become part of a group or movement, while join is a neutral, general word for becoming a member or taking part.
