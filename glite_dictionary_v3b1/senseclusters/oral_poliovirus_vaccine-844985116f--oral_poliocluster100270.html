oral poliovirus vaccine

noun
vaccine for polio taken orally
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrəl ˌpoʊlioʊˌvaɪrəs vækˈsin / (AmE), / ˈɔːrəl ˌpəʊliəʊˌvaɪrəs vakˈsiːn / (BrE)
a vaccine administered by mouth containing live but weakened poliovirus, used to provide immunity against poliomyelitis

Examples

The oral poliovirus vaccine
Health officials distributed the oral poliovirus vaccine during a national immunization campaign.
Some countries have switched from the oral poliovirus vaccine to the inactivated version for safety reasons.
The oral poliovirus vaccine played a crucial role in reducing polio cases worldwide.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: inactivated poliovirus vaccine - a polio vaccine given by injection containing killed poliovirus
