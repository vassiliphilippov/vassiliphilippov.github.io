idealistically

adverb
in an idealistic way
Pronunciation: / aɪˌdiːəˈlɪstɪkli / (AmE), / aɪˌdɪəˈlɪstɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that is guided by ideals or high standards, often ignoring what is practical or realistic

Examples

To speak idealistically
She spoke idealistically about creating a world without poverty or war.
The team approached the project idealistically, believing they could solve every problem.
He idealistically believed that honesty would always be rewarded in business.
He idealistically hoped that the new law would solve all the city's problems.
The team idealistically believed they could finish the project in just one week.
She idealistically imagined a world without any conflict or injustice.

Related Terms

  • synonym: optimistically - in a way that expects the best possible outcome
  • antonym: realistically - in a way that accepts and deals with things as they really are
