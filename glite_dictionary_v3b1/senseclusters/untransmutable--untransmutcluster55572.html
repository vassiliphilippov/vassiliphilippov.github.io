untransmutable

adjective
not able to be changed or converted
3.5 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌʌntrænzˈmjuːtəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˌʌntrænzˈmjuːtəbl̩ / (BrE)
not capable of being changed or converted into a different form, nature, or substance.

Examples

An untransmutable substance
According to the laws of physics, matter is neither truly created nor destroyed, but nothing is entirely untransmutable.
He regarded traditions as untransmutable, refusing to accept any form of innovation.
Some elements are considered untransmutable and cannot be changed into other elements by chemical means.

  • synonym: unchangeable - not able to be changed
