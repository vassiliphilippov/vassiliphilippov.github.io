nonpolarity

noun
absence of polarity
9.1
Pronunciation: / nɑnpoʊˈlærəti / (AmE), / nɒnpəˈlærəti / (BrE)
the state of lacking polarity or not having distinct positive and negative ends, especially in molecules

Examples

Nonpolarity of molecules
The nonpolarity of oil explains why it does not mix with water.
Because of its nonpolarity, the substance cannot conduct electricity in solution.
Scientists studied the nonpolarity of the compound to understand its chemical behavior.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nonpolar - not having distinct positive and negative ends
  • antonym: polarity - the presence of distinct positive and negative ends in a molecule
