occur

verb
happen; take place
2.0 64/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàk…ùÀê / (AmE), / …ôÀàk…úÀê / (BrE)
to happen or take place, especially without being planned or expected

Examples

An accident occurs
An earthquake may occur at any time without warning.
It never occurred to me that he might not want the promotion.
Several minor problems occurred during the experiment, but they were quickly resolved.

Synonyms

  • happen
    1.0 745/m
    happen is a general, everyday verb for things that start by chance or suddenly, while occur is slightly more formal.
  • come about
    4.0 6.79/m
    come about often suggests events happen slowly or by chance in a story or history, while occur is neutral and formal.
  • go on
    1.3 159/m
    go on is common speech for things that happen now or continue, while occur sounds more formal and neutral.
  • take place
    1.8 68/m
    take place often describes planned or scheduled events like meetings or shows, while occur often implies unplanned or sudden events.
  • transpire
    6.9 3.45/m
    transpire is formal and often means news or facts become known, while occur simply means to happen.
