walk

verb
accompany or escort on foot
Pronunciation: / wɑk / (AmE), / wɔːk / (BrE)
to accompany or escort (someone) on foot, often as a courtesy or for safety

Examples

To walk someone home
Can I walk you home after the party?
He walked me to my car so I wouldn’t be alone in the dark.
She insisted on walking her friend to the train station late at night.
