forest fire

noun
uncontrolled fire in a forest
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔr.əst ˌfaɪr / (AmE), / ˈfɒr.ɪst ˌfaɪə / (BrE)
An uncontrolled fire burning in a forest or wooded area, often causing widespread damage.

Examples

A forest fire spreads
After the forest fire, many animals lost their natural habitat.
Firefighters battled the forest fire for several days before bringing it under control.
The forest fire spread quickly because of dry conditions and strong winds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: wildfire - a large, destructive, uncontrolled fire in an area of vegetation
