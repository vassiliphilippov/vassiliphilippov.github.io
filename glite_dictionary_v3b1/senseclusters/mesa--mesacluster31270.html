mesa

noun
flat-topped hill with steep sides
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪsə / (AmE), / ˈmeɪsə / (BrE)
A flat-topped natural elevation with steep sides, found especially in arid and semi-arid regions.

Examples

A mesa landform
From the air, the isolated mesa looked like an island in a sea of sand.
Hikers climbed the steep path to reach the flat mesa above the canyon.
The village was built atop a mesa overlooking the desert.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plateau - an area of fairly level high ground
