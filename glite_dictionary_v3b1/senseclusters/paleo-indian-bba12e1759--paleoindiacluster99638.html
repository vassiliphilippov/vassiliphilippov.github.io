Paleo-Indian

noun
early inhabitant of the Americas
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpeɪlioʊˈɪndiən / (AmE), / ˌpeɪlɪəʊˈɪndiən / (BrE)
a member of the earliest human group to inhabit North and South America during the late Pleistocene epoch

Examples

Early Paleo-Indian people
Archaeologists have discovered Paleo-Indian artifacts in several locations across North America.
Many theories exist about how the Paleo-Indian peoples migrated to the Americas.
The Paleo-Indian period is characterized by large stone spear points used for hunting megafauna.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Clovis people - a specific Paleo-Indian culture known for distinctive stone tools
  • synonym: Paleo-American - another term for earliest humans in the Americas
