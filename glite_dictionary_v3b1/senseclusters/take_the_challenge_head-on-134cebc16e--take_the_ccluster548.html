take the challenge head-on

verb
confront a challenge directly
1.9
Pronunciation: / teɪk ðə ˈtʃælɪndʒ hɛd ɑn / (AmE), / teɪk ðə ˈtʃælɪndʒ hɛd ɒn / (BrE)
to deal with a difficult situation or problem in a direct and determined way, without avoiding it

Examples

Athletes must take the challenge head-on if they want to be champions in their sport.

When a new project arrived unexpectedly, Sarah decided to take the challenge head-on and led her team to success.
