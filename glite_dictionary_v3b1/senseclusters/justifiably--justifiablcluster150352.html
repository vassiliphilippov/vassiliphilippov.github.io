justifiably

adverb
for a good reason
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʌstəˌfaɪəbli / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌstɪˌfaɪəbli / (BrE)
For a good reason; in a way that can be defended as right or reasonable.

Examples

To be justifiably harsh
Critics were justifiably harsh about the film's weak storyline.
She was justifiably upset when her luggage was lost again.
The company was justifiably proud of its achievements last year.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unjustifiably - without a good reason
