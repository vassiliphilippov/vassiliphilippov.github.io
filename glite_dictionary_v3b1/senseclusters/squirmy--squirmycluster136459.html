squirmy

adjective
restless and wiggly
3.5 0.08/m
Pronunciation: / Ààskw…úÀêrmi / (AmE), / Ààskw…úÀêmi / (BrE)
moving or behaving in a restless, wiggly, or twisting way

Examples

Squirmy child
The squirmy toddler could not sit still during the long ceremony.
She tried to hold the squirmy kitten while the vet examined it.
After eating too much sugar, the children became especially squirmy in their seats.

Synonyms

  • wriggly
    4.3 0.07/m
    wriggly moves with quick twisting like a small animal, while squirmy describes restless, wiggly movements often showing discomfort.
  • wiggly
    3.5 0.35/m
    wiggly is bent or moves in curving ways, while squirmy focuses on restless, twisting motion that looks uncomfortable.
  • fidgety
    4.8 0.64/m
    fidgety means unable to sit still from nervousness, while squirmy describes twisting, wiggly movement rather than just anxious restlessness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - not moving or agitated; peaceful
  • synonym: fidgety - unable to remain still, often moving nervously
  • synonym: restless - unable to stay still or quiet
