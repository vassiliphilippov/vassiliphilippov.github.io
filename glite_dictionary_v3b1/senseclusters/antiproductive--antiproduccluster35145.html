antiproductive

adjective
reducing productivity
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌæntiprəˈdʌktɪv /; / ˌæntiˌprəˈdʌktɪv / (AmE), / ˌæntiprəˈdʌktɪv /; / ˌæntiˌprəˈdʌktɪv / (BrE)
having the effect of reducing or preventing productivity or the production of something (such as goods, services, or desired results)

Examples

Antiproductive policy
The new policy turned out to be antiproductive and slowed down the entire team.

Using outdated software is antiproductive and leads to unnecessary delays.
The new regulations were criticized as antiproductive by several business leaders.
Some experts argue that excessive bureaucracy can be antiproductive for economic growth.
His antiproductive attitude made it difficult for the team to meet their goals.
The presence of certain impurities had an antiproductive effect on the chemical reaction.
Researchers found that high temperatures were antiproductive for the growth of the desired bacteria.
Adding too much solvent can be antiproductive and reduce the overall yield of the process.

Related Terms

  • synonym: counterproductive - having the opposite effect to what was intended, making things worse
  • synonym: inhibitory - tending to slow down or prevent a process
  • antonym: productive - achieving or producing a lot; effective
  • synonym: unproductive - not producing or achieving much
