Olympus

proper noun
mountain in Greece; mythological home of the gods
Pronunciation: / əˈlɪmpəs / (AmE), / əˈlɪmpəs / (BrE)
a mountain in northeast Greece, regarded in ancient Greek mythology as the home of the gods; the highest mountain in Greece, rising to about 9,570 feet (2,917 meters) near the Aegean coast.

Examples

Olympus mountain Greece
According to legend, the gods and goddesses once lived atop Olympus.
Climbers from around the world travel to Greece to ascend Olympus, the country's highest peak.
Mount Olympus is famous both for its natural beauty and its place in Greek mythology.
