pipe

noun
tube for carrying fluids
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /paɪp/ (AmE), /paɪp/ (BrE)
a long, hollow tube used to carry water, gas, oil, or other substances from one place to another

Examples

Water pipe
Gas flows through this pipe to reach the stove.
The plumber replaced the old pipe under the kitchen sink.
They had to dig up the street to repair a broken water pipe.
A broken pipe under the road caused flooding in the street.
Steam poured out of the factory’s main pipe into the cold air.
The plumber replaced the old copper pipe with a new plastic one.
