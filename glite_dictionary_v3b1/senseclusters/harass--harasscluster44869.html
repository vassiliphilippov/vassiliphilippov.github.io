harass

verb
to attack repeatedly to exhaust
2.7 3.6
Pronunciation: /hə.ˈræs/ (AmE), /hə.ˈræs/ (BrE)
To repeatedly attack or pressure an enemy or opponent in order to exhaust or wear them down.

Examples

To harass enemy forces
Smaller ships were sent to harass the larger vessels throughout the night.
The cavalry would harass the enemy from the flanks during the battle.
They continued to harass the supply lines until reinforcements arrived.

  • synonym: pester - to trouble or annoy persistently
