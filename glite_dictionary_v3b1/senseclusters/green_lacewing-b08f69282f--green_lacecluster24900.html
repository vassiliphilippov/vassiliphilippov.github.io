green lacewing

noun
a pale green insect with lace-like wings
Pronunciation: / ɡrin ˈleɪsˌwɪŋ / (AmE), / ɡriːn ˈleɪs.wɪŋ / (BrE)
a pale green insect with delicate, lace-like wings, known for its unpleasant smell and carnivorous larvae, often used in biological pest control

Examples

The green lacewing insect
A distinct odor is emitted by the green lacewing as a form of defense.
Scientists studied the behavior of green lacewings in agricultural environments.
The green lacewing is often introduced into gardens to control aphid populations.
