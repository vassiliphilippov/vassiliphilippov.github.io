obliquus

adjective
indirect or evasive
Pronunciation: / oʊˈbliːkwəs / (AmE), / əʊˈbliːkwəs / (BrE)
indirect or not stated clearly; expressed in a roundabout or evasive way

Examples

Obliquus answer
Her explanation was so obliquus that no one understood her true intentions.
The politician gave an obliquus answer instead of addressing the question directly.
His apology was oddly obliquus, avoiding direct responsibility for the mistake.

Related Terms

  • antonym: direct - stated clearly and openly
  • synonym: evasive - tending to avoid commitment or self-revelation
  • synonym: indirect - not stated directly or clearly
