inversion

noun

(archaic) The condition of taking on the gender role of the opposite sex; formerly used to refer to individuals whose gender expression or identity differed from their sex assigned at birth.

Examples

Gender-role inversion
Historical documents sometimes incorrectly attributed inversion to moral failings or illness.
In early psychological literature, inversion was often used to describe nonconforming gender roles.
The term inversion has been largely replaced by more specific and respectful language today.
