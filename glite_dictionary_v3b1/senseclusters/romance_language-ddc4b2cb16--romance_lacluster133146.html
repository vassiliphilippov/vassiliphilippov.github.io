Romance language

noun
language descended from Latin
Pronunciation: / roʊˈmæns ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / rəʊˈmæns ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
any of the languages derived from Latin, including French, Spanish, Italian, Portuguese, and Romanian.

Examples

Romance language family
French and Spanish are both considered Romance languages because they originated from Latin.
Learning a Romance language can make it easier to learn others in the same group.
Portuguese is a Romance language spoken widely in South America and parts of Europe.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Germanic language - languages descended from Proto-Germanic, such as English and German
  • antonym: Slavic language - languages descended from Proto-Slavic, such as Russian and Polish
