public school

noun
US state-funded school
Pronunciation: / ˈpʌblɪk skuːl / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪk skuːl / (BrE)
In the United States, a school that is free to attend, supported by public taxes, and controlled by a local school board.

Examples

Local public school
After moving to a new city, Mia enrolled her daughter in the local public school.
Most children in the United States attend a public school near their homes.
The budget for the public school district was increased this year.

Related Terms

  • antonym: private school - a school supported by a private organization or private individuals rather than by the government
