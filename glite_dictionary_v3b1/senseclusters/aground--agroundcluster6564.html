aground

adjective, adverb
stuck on the bottom (of a ship/boat)
Pronunciation: / əˈɡraʊnd / (AmE), / əˈɡraʊnd / (BrE)
resting on or stuck on the bottom of a body of water and unable to move (used for ships or boats)

Examples

Ship aground
If we aren't careful with the map, we could end up aground before sunrise.
Several ships went aground on the rocky shoreline after the tide went out.
The fishing boat ran aground during the storm and couldn't be freed until morning.
Several ships went aground after the tide went out unexpectedly.
The captain managed to steer clear of the rocks so the ferry did not go aground.
The fishing boat ran aground on a sandbar during the storm.

Related Terms

  • antonym: afloat - floating or not grounded (of a vessel)
