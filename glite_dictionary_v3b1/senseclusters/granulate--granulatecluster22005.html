granulate

verb
form into grains or granules
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɡræn.jə.leɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡran.jʊ.leɪt / (BrE)
to form into small grains or particles, or to become grainy in texture or appearance

Examples

To granulate sugar
As the sugar cooled, it began to granulate and turn into crystals.
Some medications granulate during manufacturing to aid in controlled release.
The machine is used to granulate the raw plastic before it is molded.
As the syrup cools, it will granulate if left undisturbed.
Certain salts granulate when moisture is removed from them.
The soap mixture began to granulate as it hardened in the mold.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: coagulate - to change from a fluid into a thickened mass
  • antonym: powder - reduce to fine particles
