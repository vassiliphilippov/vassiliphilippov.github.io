Tridentine

noun
traditional Catholic Mass
9.9
Pronunciation: / traɪˈdɛntaɪn / (AmE), / traɪˈdɛntaɪn / (BrE)
the traditional form of the Roman Catholic Mass or liturgy established by the Council of Trent in the 16th century

Examples

The Tridentine liturgy
Many traditionalists prefer the Tridentine for its solemn rituals and Latin prayers.
The bishop announced a special Tridentine to celebrate the anniversary of the Council of Trent.
Some churches offer both the modern Mass and the Tridentine to accommodate different preferences.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Latin Mass - the Roman Catholic Mass celebrated in Latin according to traditional rites
  • antonym: Novus Ordo - the modern form of the Roman Catholic Mass introduced after the Second Vatican Council
