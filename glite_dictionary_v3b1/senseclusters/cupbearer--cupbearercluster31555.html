cupbearer

noun
attendant serving wine in noble households
6.5 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌpˌberər / (AmE), / ˈkʌpˌbeərə / (BrE)
an attendant, often an officer in a noble household, whose duty is to fill and serve cups of wine, especially to a ruler or noble

Examples

The cupbearer serves wine
As the cupbearer, he was always present at royal banquets, ensuring the king’s wine was safe.
In ancient Persia, the trustworthiness of the cupbearer was essential, as he could protect the king from poison.
The cupbearer handed the goblet to the queen with a respectful bow.

Related Terms

  • synonym: butler - chief manservant of a household, especially in charge of wine
