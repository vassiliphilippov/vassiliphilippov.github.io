work

noun
activity done to achieve something
1.0 463/m
Pronunciation: / w…ùk /; / w…ùÀêk / (AmE), / w…úÀêk / (BrE)
the activity of doing a job or task, or the job or occupation itself; mental or physical effort to achieve something

Examples

Hard work
She put in hours of work to perfect her project.
The report required a lot of work before it was ready for publication.
There is still much work to be done before the conference.
After months of intense work in the library, she became an expert on medieval history.
Improving your vocabulary will require dedicated work and focus.
The professor emphasized that consistent work outside the classroom is essential for success.
After graduating, Alice began work at a law firm downtown.
He lost his work during the economic downturn and is looking for a new job.
Many people commute to work by train every morning.

Synonyms

  • labor
    1.4 50/m
    labor is physical, often hard work done with the body, while work covers any job, task, or effort for a result.
  • effort
    1.1 31/m
    effort is the energy put into a single task or attempt, while work is a regular job, task, or sustained activity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: employment - the state of having paid work
  • synonym: labor - physical or mental effort; work, especially hard work
  • antonym: rest - a state of relaxation or pause from activity
  • synonym: study - the devotion of time and attention to acquiring knowledge, especially from books
  • antonym: unemployment - the state of not having paid work
