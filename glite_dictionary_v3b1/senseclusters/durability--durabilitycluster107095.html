durability

noun
ability to withstand wear or damage
1.3
Pronunciation: / ˌdʊrəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌdjʊərəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
The ability of something to withstand wear, pressure, or damage; the quality of lasting for a long time without deteriorating.

Examples

The durability of materials
The durability of this smartphone is impressive even after a year of heavy use.
The company conducts tests to ensure the durability of its safety equipment.
When choosing furniture, it's important to consider the durability of the materials.

Related Terms

  • synonym: endurance - the ability to withstand hardship or stress
  • antonym: fragility - the quality of being easily broken or damaged
