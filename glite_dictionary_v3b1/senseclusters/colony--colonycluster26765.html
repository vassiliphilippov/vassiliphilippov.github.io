colony

noun
area controlled by a distant country
1.8
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːləni / (AmE), / ˈkɒləni / (BrE)
a country or area that is governed by a more powerful country at a distance, and is typically settled or controlled by people from that country

Examples

A British colony
India was once a colony of the British Empire until it gained independence in 1947.
Many countries around the world were former colonies of European powers.
The settlers established a colony on the remote island in the eighteenth century.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dependent territory - a region that belongs to another state but is not fully part of it
  • antonym: metropolis - the parent state of a colony
  • synonym: territory - an area of land under the jurisdiction of a ruler or state
