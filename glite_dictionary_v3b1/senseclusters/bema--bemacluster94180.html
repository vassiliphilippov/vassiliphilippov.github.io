bema

noun
area near altar for clergy and choir
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbiːmə / (AmE), / ˈbiːmə / (BrE)
a raised platform or special area in a church or synagogue, often used for conducting services, giving speeches, or reserved for clergy

Examples

The bema platform
A decorative railing separates the bema from the nave of the church.
Only clergy are permitted to enter the bema in this cathedral.
The choir stood in the bema during the morning service.
The rabbi stood on the bema to address the congregation.
During the ceremony, the speaker walked up to the bema to deliver his message.
In many synagogues, the Torah is read from the central bema.

Related Terms

  • synonym: altar - a table or flat-topped block used as the focus for a religious ritual
  • synonym: pulpit - a raised platform in a church from which the sermon is delivered
