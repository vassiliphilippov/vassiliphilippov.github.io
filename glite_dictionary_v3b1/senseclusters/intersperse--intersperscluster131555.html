intersperse

verb
to scatter or insert at intervals
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈspɜrs / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈspɜːs / (BrE)
to scatter or place things at intervals among or between other things

Examples

To intersperse stories
She interspersed her presentation with humorous anecdotes to keep the audience engaged.
The author has a habit of interspersing her narrative with vivid descriptions of the countryside.
The writer likes to intersperse his essays with quotes from famous thinkers.
She likes to intersperse her lectures with humor to keep the audience engaged.
The director chose to intersperse flashbacks throughout the movie to reveal the character's past.
Wildflowers are interspersed among the tall grasses on the hillside.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cluster - to group together closely
  • synonym: interleave - to insert something alternately with something else
  • synonym: scatter - to throw or spread things randomly over an area
