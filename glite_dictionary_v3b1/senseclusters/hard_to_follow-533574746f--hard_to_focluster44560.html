hard to follow

adjective
difficult to understand
1.0
Pronunciation: / hɑrd tə ˈfɑloʊ / (AmE), / hɑːd tə ˈfɒləʊ / (BrE)
difficult to understand or comprehend; not easy to make sense of.

Examples

Hard to follow handwriting
His handwriting is hard to follow, making it difficult to grade his essays.
Some novels can be extremely hard to follow if the plot jumps around too much.
The professor's lecture was so hard to follow that many students gave up taking notes.
