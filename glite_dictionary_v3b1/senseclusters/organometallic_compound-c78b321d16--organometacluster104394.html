organometallic compound

noun
compound with metal–carbon bonds
5.5 6.6
Pronunciation: /ɔːr.gə.noʊ.mə.ˈtæl.ɪk ˈkɑːm.paʊnd/ (AmE), /ɔː.gə.nəʊ.mə.ˈtæl.ɪk ˈkɒm.paʊnd/ (BrE)
a chemical compound in which a metal is directly bonded to a carbon atom of an organic group, such as an alkyl or aryl group.

Examples

An organometallic compound with metal
Ferrocene is a classic example of an organometallic compound featuring iron bonded between two cyclopentadienyl rings.
Research in catalysis often focuses on the synthesis of new organometallic compounds.
The unique properties of each organometallic compound depend on the metal and the organic groups involved.
