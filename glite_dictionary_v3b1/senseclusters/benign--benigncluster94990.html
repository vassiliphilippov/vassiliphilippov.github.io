benign

adjective
not harmful; especially of tumors
6.5
Pronunciation: / bɪˈnaɪn / (AmE), / bɪˈnaɪn / (BrE)
(of a disease or medical condition, especially a tumor) not harmful to health and not likely to recur or become worse.

Examples

Benign tumor
Although the lump appeared suspicious, it turned out to be a benign cyst.
Doctors confirmed the tumor was benign, providing great relief to the patient.
The test results showed that the growth was benign and not cancerous.

Related Terms

  • antonym: malignant - (of a tumor or disease) very virulent or infectious; likely to recur or worsen
