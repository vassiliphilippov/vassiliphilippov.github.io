underdiagnose

verb
to miss full diagnosis
7.5
Pronunciation: /ʌn.dər.daɪ.əg.ˈnoʊz/ (AmE), /ʌn.də.daɪ.əg.ˈnəʊz/ (BrE)
to not diagnose a medical condition fully or accurately, often missing cases or the true severity

Examples

To underdiagnose diseases
Doctors may underdiagnose certain diseases if the symptoms are mild or unusual.
It is a problem when mental health conditions are underdiagnosed in children.
Some rare illnesses are often underdiagnosed due to lack of awareness among healthcare professionals.
Doctors sometimes underdiagnose mental health conditions in teenagers due to subtle symptoms.
Certain rare diseases are often underdiagnosed because their signs are mistaken for more common illnesses.
If symptoms are mild, physicians may unintentionally underdiagnose the underlying problem.

Related Terms

  • synonym: misdiagnose - to diagnose incorrectly
  • antonym: overdiagnose - to diagnose a condition as more severe or more frequent than it is
