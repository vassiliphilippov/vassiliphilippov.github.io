Synonyms

  • click
    1.0 6/m
    click is a short, sharp single sound used for latches or fingers, while clack describes repeated sharp clicking or rattling.
  • clatter
    3.9 3/m
    clatter is a loud, noisy rattling of hard things hitting, while clack is shorter, sharper, and often more repeated clicks.
  • rattle
    2.2 10/m
    rattle is a shaking sound of many quick knocks or loose parts, while clack is sharper, shorter, and more click-like.
  • chatter
    3.4 1/m
    chatter is a rapid, high-pitched series of small sounds often from teeth or birds, while clack is sharper and harder.
  • brattle
    9.9 0.00/m
    brattle is a loud, rattling clatter often used for metal or crockery, while clack is a shorter, sharper repeated click.
