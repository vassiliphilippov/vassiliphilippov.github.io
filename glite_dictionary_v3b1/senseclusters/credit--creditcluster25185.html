an arrangement or amount of money that allows you to buy now and pay later, or a record of money added to an account; can also mean a unit of virtual money in games or apps

Examples

Buy on credit
She bought the television on credit and will pay for it over six months.
Some stores offer interest-free credit to encourage people to buy more expensive items.
Your credit limit determines how much you can borrow from the bank.
Every deposit results in a credit to your bank statement.
The accountant noticed a large credit under 'Miscellaneous Income.'
The payment was recorded as a credit in the company's account.
He was denied credit because of his poor financial history.
The bank offered her a line of credit to help start her business.
Using a credit card allows you to pay for purchases at a later date.

Synonyms

  • deferred payment
    4.3 0.07/m

  • credit entry
    5.4 0.00/m
    credit entry records an amount added to an account in accounting, whereas credit also means buying now and paying later or virtual money.
