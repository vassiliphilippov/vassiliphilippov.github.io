refractory

adjective
not responding to treatment
6.8
Pronunciation: / rɪˈfræk.tə.ri / (AmE), / rɪˈfræk.tər.i / (BrE)
not responding to treatment, especially when a disease or condition resists medical intervention

Examples

Refractory disease
Doctors described her epilepsy as refractory after several treatments failed to control it.
Some cancers are refractory to conventional chemotherapy.
The patient had a refractory infection that did not improve with antibiotics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: resistant - able to withstand something, not affected by treatment
  • antonym: responsive - reacting positively to treatment
