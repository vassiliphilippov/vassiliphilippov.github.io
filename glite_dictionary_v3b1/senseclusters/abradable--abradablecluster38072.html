abradable

adjective
can be worn away
Pronunciation: / əˈbreɪdəbl / (AmE), / əˈbreɪdəbl / (BrE)
able to be worn away or removed by friction or mechanical action

Examples

An abradable layer
The abradable layer is designed to wear away gradually, protecting the underlying surface.
Engineers selected an abradable material to reduce damage during contact between moving parts.
The abradable coating must be replaced after a certain amount of use to maintain performance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: durable - able to withstand wear or damage
  • synonym: wearable - able to be worn or eroded by use
