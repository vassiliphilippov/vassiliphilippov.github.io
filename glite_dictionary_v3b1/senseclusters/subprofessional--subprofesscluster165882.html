subprofessional

adjective
below professional standard
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌbprəˈfɛʃənəl / (AmE), / ˌsʌbprəˈfɛʃənəl / (BrE)
not at the level or standard of a professional; below professional quality or status

Examples

Subprofessional work quality
The company received complaints about the subprofessional quality of its customer service.
His subprofessional approach to the project led to several costly mistakes.
Inspectors found the construction work to be subprofessional and not up to code.
The hospital employs many subprofessional staff to support the medical team.
He started in a subprofessional role before gaining the qualifications needed to advance.
Routine administrative tasks are often handled by subprofessional workers in large companies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: paraprofessional - relating to workers who assist professionals but are not fully qualified
  • antonym: professional - meeting the standards or skill expected of a profession
  • synonym: unskilled - lacking professional skill or training
