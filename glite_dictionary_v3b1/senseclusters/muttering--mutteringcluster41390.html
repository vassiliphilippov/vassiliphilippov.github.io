muttering

noun
low, indistinct complaint or remark
5.5 4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌt̬ɚɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmʌtəɹɪŋ / (BrE)
a quiet or indistinct complaint or remark, typically made in a low voice and often expressing discontent

Examples

Muttering about rules
His constant muttering about the new policy annoyed his coworkers.
The teacher ignored the muttering coming from the back of the classroom.
There was a low muttering among the audience as the speaker took the stage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grumble - a complaint spoken in a low voice; to complain quietly
  • synonym: murmur - a soft, indistinct sound or whisper, often expressing dissatisfaction
