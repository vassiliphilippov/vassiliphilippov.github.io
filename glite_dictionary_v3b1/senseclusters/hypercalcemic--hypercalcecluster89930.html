hypercalcemic

adjective
high blood calcium
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərkælˈsiːmɪk / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəkælˈsiːmɪk / (BrE)
having an abnormally high level of calcium in the blood

Examples

Hypercalcemic patient
The doctor explained that a hypercalcemic condition can cause muscle weakness and confusion.
After reviewing the lab results, she noticed the patient was hypercalcemic and needed immediate treatment.
Certain cancers can make people hypercalcemic due to changes in hormone levels.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hypocalcemic - having an abnormally low level of calcium in the blood
