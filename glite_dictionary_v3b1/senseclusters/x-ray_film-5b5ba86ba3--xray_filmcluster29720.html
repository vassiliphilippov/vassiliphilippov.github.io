X-ray film

noun
film for recording X-ray images
Pronunciation: / ˈɛks reɪ fɪlm / (AmE), / ˈɛks reɪ fɪlm / (BrE)
Photographic film that is specially designed to record images produced by X-rays, used in medical diagnosis and industrial testing.

Examples

X-ray film image
Digital imaging has begun to replace traditional X-ray film in many hospitals.
Technicians carefully stored old X-ray film in the archive for future reference.
The X-ray film clearly showed a fracture in the patient's wrist.
