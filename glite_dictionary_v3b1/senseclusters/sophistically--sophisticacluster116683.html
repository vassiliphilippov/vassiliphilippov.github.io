sophistically

adverb
with deceptive reasoning
Pronunciation: / səˈfɪstɪkli / (AmE), / səˈfɪstɪkli / (BrE)
in a manner that uses clever but misleading or false arguments

Examples

Sophistically avoid the truth
The politician responded sophistically to avoid giving a direct answer to the question.
She defended her actions sophistically, making the situation seem more complicated than it was.
During the debate, he argued sophistically to distract from the real issue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deceptively - in a way that misleads or tricks
  • antonym: honestly - in a truthful and straightforward way
