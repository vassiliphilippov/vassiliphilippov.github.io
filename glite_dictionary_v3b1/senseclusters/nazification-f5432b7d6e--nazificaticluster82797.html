Nazification

noun
the process of adopting Nazism
9.9
Pronunciation: /nɑː.zɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /neɪ.zɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /nɑː.zɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /neɪ.zɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
The process of adopting or imposing the principles and policies of Nazism, especially as carried out in Germany and occupied territories before and during World War II.

Examples

Nazification of schools
Schools underwent Nazification, with new curricula promoting Nazi ideology.
The Allies aimed to reverse the Nazification of German society after the war.
The rapid Nazification of public institutions led to widespread changes in German society.

Related Terms

  • antonym: denazification - the process of removing Nazi ideology, influence, or members from positions of authority
