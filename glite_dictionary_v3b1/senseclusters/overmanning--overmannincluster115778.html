overmanning

noun
excess staff
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈmænɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈmænɪŋ / (BrE)
the situation in which more workers are employed than are necessary for a particular job or task

Examples

Reduce overmanning levels
The company had to reduce overmanning to improve its financial performance.
Union leaders discussed the issue of overmanning at the annual meeting.
Due to overmanning, the project became less efficient and more costly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: redundancy - the state of being no longer needed at work, often due to staff cuts
  • antonym: understaffing - the situation of having too few workers for a job
