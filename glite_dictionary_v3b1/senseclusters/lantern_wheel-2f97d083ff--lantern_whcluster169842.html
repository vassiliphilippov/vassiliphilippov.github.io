lantern wheel

noun
type of clock gear with cylindrical bars
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈlæntɚn wiːl / (AmE), / ˈlæntən wiːl / (BrE)
a small gear with cylindrical bars instead of traditional teeth, mostly used in inexpensive clocks

Examples

A lantern wheel gear
Horologists often prefer pinions, but the lantern wheel design is still found in many antique timepieces.
The cylindrical bars of a lantern wheel distinguish it from other types of clock gears.
The old clock was repaired with a new lantern wheel to restore its function.
