securitization

noun
turning loans into securities
8.4
Pronunciation: /sɪ.kjʊr.ə.tə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /sɪ.kjʊr.ɪ.tɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /sɪ.kjʊə.rɪ.tɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /sɪ.kjʊə.rə.tə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of turning loans or other assets into tradable financial securities sold to investors

Examples

Securitization of loans
The bank used securitization to convert its mortgage loans into investment products.
After the financial crisis, regulations on securitization became much stricter.
Many investors are attracted to securitization because it offers a way to diversify risk.
Banks often use securitization to raise additional capital.
The financial crisis highlighted the risks associated with securitization of subprime mortgages.
Through securitization, lenders can convert loans into securities that are sold to investors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: asset-backed security - a financial security backed by a pool of assets
  • synonym: bond - a fixed income investment representing a loan made by an investor to a borrower
  • synonym: financing - the act of providing funds for business activities
  • antonym: loan - money that is borrowed and expected to be paid back with interest
