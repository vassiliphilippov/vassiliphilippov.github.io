come through

verb
penetrate or pass through
1.0
Pronunciation: / kʌm θru / (AmE), / kʌm θruː / (BrE)
to penetrate or pass through something from one side to the other

Examples

Rain comes through the roof
After hours of heavy rain, water began to come through the ceiling.
Sunlight managed to come through the dense canopy of the forest.
The music could barely come through the thick walls of the old building.
