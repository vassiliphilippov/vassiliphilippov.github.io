Synonyms

  • clear out
    3.1 2/m
    clear out means removing everything from a place until it is empty, while clean out often implies removing unwanted items.
  • empty
    1.0 12/m
    empty means taking all contents away so nothing remains, while clean out often suggests clearing unwanted items from a space.
  • clean
    1.0 9/m
    clean means removing dirt or tidying a space, while clean out means emptying completely, often removing things rather than washing.
  • void
    5.8 0.09/m
    void means formally emptying a place of its contents or people, while clean out is more casual removal of items.
  • purge
    6.3 2/m
    purge means forcefully removing unwanted substances or people, while clean out usually means clearing things from a place for tidying.
  • evacuate
    5.0 0.2/m
    evacuate means moving people or contents out for safety, while clean out means emptying a place by removing items.
