deselection

noun
removal by selection
8.8
Pronunciation: /diː.sɪ.ˈlek.ʃən/ /; / /dɪ.sɪ.ˈlek.ʃən/ (AmE), /diː.sɪ.ˈlek.ʃən/ /; / /dɪ.sɪ.ˈlek.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process by which a gene variant or trait is selected against, causing its frequency to decrease in a population

Examples

Gene deselection
The researchers observed deselection of the harmful gene over several generations.
Environmental changes led to the deselection of traits that were once advantageous.
In breeding programs, deselection can help remove unwanted characteristics from a population.

Related Terms

  • synonym: elimination - the act of removing or getting rid of something
  • antonym: selection - the process by which certain traits become more common in a population
