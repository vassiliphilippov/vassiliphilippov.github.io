employee stock ownership plan

noun
plan allowing employees to own company shares
4.2 3.5
Pronunciation: /ɪm.ˈplɔɪ.iː stɑːk ˈoʊ.nər.ʃɪp plæn/ /; / /əm.ˈplɔɪ.iː stɑːk ˈoʊ.nər.ʃɪp plæn/ (AmE), /ɪm.ˈplɔɪ.iː stɒk ˈəʊ.nə.ʃɪp plæn/ /; / /əm.ˈplɔɪ.iː stɒk ˈəʊ.nə.ʃɪp plæn/ (BrE)
a program that allows employees to regularly acquire shares in their employer and possibly gain control of the company

Examples

An employee stock ownership plan program
An employee stock ownership plan can boost morale by making employees part-owners of the company.
The company introduced an employee stock ownership plan to encourage staff loyalty and investment in the business.
Through its employee stock ownership plan, workers slowly acquired a significant stake in the organization.
