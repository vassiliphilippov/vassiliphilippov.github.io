hematochezia

noun
passing blood in stool (medical)
9.9
Pronunciation: /hiː.mə.tə.ˈkiː.zi.ə/ /; / /hə.mə.tə.ˈkiː.zi.ə/ (AmE), /hiː.mə.tə.ˈkiː.zi.ə/ /; / /hə.mə.tə.ˈkiː.zi.ə/ (BrE)
The passage of stools containing fresh blood, typically caused by bleeding in the lower digestive tract.

Examples

Hematochezia with stools
Patients presenting with hematochezia are often tested for colon polyps or cancer.
Sudden hematochezia can be a sign of a serious gastrointestinal condition.
The doctor suspected hematochezia after noticing blood in the patient's stool.
Chronic haematochezia often requires a colonoscopy to identify the bleeding source.
Sudden onset of haematochezia can indicate significant lower gastrointestinal bleeding.
The doctor suspected haematochezia after observing fresh blood in the patient's stool.
