speech

noun
a passage spoken by an actor in a play
3.9 2.90/m
Pronunciation: / spiÀêt É / (AmE), / spiÀêt É / (BrE)
the part or passage spoken by a character in a play, or the words making up a dialogue or monologue

Examples

Actor's speech
During rehearsals, he struggled with a particularly long speech in the second act.
She memorized her speech the night before the school play.
The actor delivered his opening speech flawlessly in front of a packed audience.

Synonyms

  • actor's line
    3.5 0.01/m
    an actor's line is a short set of words an actor says in a scene, while speech refers to any passage a character speaks in a play.
  • monologue
    4.8 5.33/m
    a monologue is a long speech by one person on stage, while speech can mean any spoken passage or dialogue in a play.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dialogue - a conversation between two or more people as a feature of a book, play, or film
  • synonym: monologue - a long speech by one actor in a play or film
