compromise

verb
to settle by mutual concessions
2.2
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːmprəˌmaɪz /; / ˈkɑːm.prəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkɒmprəˌmaɪz /; / ˈkɒm.prə.maɪz / (BrE)
to settle a dispute by each side making concessions; to accept less than you want to reach an agreement

Examples

To compromise on price
If everyone is willing to compromise, we can find a solution that works for all.
Rather than argue all night, they decided to compromise and split the bill.
The two sides finally agreed to compromise after hours of negotiation.
If neither side is willing to compromise, the conflict may continue indefinitely.
Negotiators failed to compromise, so the talks broke down.
Sometimes you have to compromise when working on a team project.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agree - to have the same opinion or to consent
  • synonym: concede - to yield or give up something in an argument
  • synonym: negotiate - to discuss and settle an agreement
  • antonym: refuse - to indicate or show that one is not willing to do something
