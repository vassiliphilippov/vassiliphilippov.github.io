matrilineal

adjective
tracing descent through the female line
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌmætrəˈlɪniəl / (AmE), / ˌmætrɪˈlɪniəl / (BrE)
(of descent, inheritance, or kinship) relating to or based on descent through the female line.

Examples

Matrilineal inheritance
Anthropologists study how matrilineal inheritance affects family roles and social structure.
Many indigenous societies are matrilineal, passing property and names through the mother's family.
The tribe's matrilineal system means children belong to their mother's clan.

Related Terms

  • antonym: patrilineal - relating to or based on descent through the male line
