tobacco wilt

noun
a plant disease caused by Pseudomonas solanacearum
7.9 8.3
Pronunciation: / təˈbækoʊ wɪlt / (AmE), / təˈbækəʊ wɪlt / (BrE)
a disease of tobacco, tomatoes, potatoes, and related plants caused by the bacterium Pseudomonas solanacearum, leading to wilting and yellowing of leaves

Examples

Tobacco wilt disease
Tobacco wilt can devastate entire fields if not treated in time.
Farmers use disease-resistant plants to prevent tobacco wilt outbreaks.
Symptoms of tobacco wilt include wilting and yellowing of the lower leaves.
