benzocaine

noun
local anesthetic compound
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛnzəˌkeɪn / (AmE), / ˈbɛnzəkeɪn / (BrE)
a white, crystalline compound used chiefly as a local anesthetic to relieve pain and itching

Examples

Benzocaine dental spray
Dental sprays with benzocaine can provide temporary relief from toothache.
Over-the-counter gels containing benzocaine are often used to soothe mouth ulcers.
The doctor applied benzocaine to numb the area before stitching the wound.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lidocaine - a synthetic compound used as a local anesthetic and to treat heart arrhythmia
