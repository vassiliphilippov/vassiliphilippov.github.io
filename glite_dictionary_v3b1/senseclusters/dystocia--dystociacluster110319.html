dystocia

noun
difficult childbirth
Pronunciation: / dɪˈstoʊʃə / (AmE), / dɪˈstəʊsiə / (BrE)
difficult or abnormal labor during childbirth, often requiring medical or veterinary intervention

Examples

Difficult dystocia labor
The doctor recognized signs of dystocia and prepared for an emergency procedure.
Proper monitoring can help detect dystocia early during labor.
Veterinarians also treat dystocia in animals, especially in large livestock.
The veterinarian was called to assist with a case of dystocia in the mare.
Prompt recognition of dystocia can help prevent complications for both the mother and offspring.
Farmers are trained to identify signs of dystocia in their livestock during calving season.

Related Terms

  • antonym: eutocia - normal or easy childbirth
  • synonym: obstructed labor - labor in which the baby cannot progress through the birth canal
