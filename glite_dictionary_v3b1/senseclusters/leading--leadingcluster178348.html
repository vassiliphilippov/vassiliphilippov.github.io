leading

noun
thin metal strip for spacing in printing
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɛdɪŋ / (BrE)
the space between lines of text in printing or graphic design, or the thin strip of metal used to create this space

Examples

Leading in text
The printer adjusted the leading to create more space between the lines.
Too much leading can make a page look sparse and difficult to read.
Traditional letterpress printing required manual insertion of leading between each row of type.
Increasing the leading can make paragraphs easier to read in a printed book.
Graphic designers often adjust the leading to improve the appearance of text layouts.
If the leading is too tight, the lines of text may look crowded and hard to follow.

Related Terms

  • antonym: kerning - the spacing between individual letters or characters
  • synonym: line spacing - the vertical distance between lines of text
