tricyclic antidepressant drug

noun
type of antidepressant medication
2.2
Pronunciation: / ˌtraɪˈsaɪklɪk ˌæntɪˈdeprəˌsɛnt drʌɡ / (AmE), / ˌtraɪˈsɪklɪk ˌantɪˈdɛprəsənt drʌɡ / (BrE)
A type of antidepressant medication that works by blocking the reuptake of norepinephrine and serotonin, increasing their levels in the brain.

Examples

A tricyclic antidepressant drug prescription
Doctors may prescribe tricyclic antidepressant drugs for certain types of nerve pain as well as depression.
Many patients with chronic depression respond well to tricyclic antidepressant drugs.
The use of tricyclic antidepressant drugs has declined since the introduction of SSRIs.
