veto

verb
formally reject a proposal or law
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈviːtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈviːtəʊ / (BrE)
to officially reject or refuse to approve a decision, proposal, or law

Examples

To veto a law
If the committee members disagree, they can veto the plan.
The President threatened to veto the proposed tax legislation.
The mayor vetoed the new city ordinance yesterday.
She decided to veto the idea of getting a dog after thinking it over.
The committee can veto any proposal that doesn't meet their criteria.
The president threatened to veto the new law if it passed both houses of Congress.

Related Terms

  • antonym: approve - to officially accept or agree to something
  • synonym: forbid - to command someone not to do something
  • synonym: reject - to refuse to accept, allow, or consider something
