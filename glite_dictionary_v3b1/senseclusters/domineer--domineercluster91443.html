domineer

verb
assert control in an overbearing or autocratic way
8.8 0.13/m
Pronunciation: / Àåd…ëÀê.m…ôÀàn…™r / (AmE), / Àåd…í.m…™Ààn…™…ô / (BrE)
to assert one's will over others in an arrogant, overbearing, or autocratic way, often in a harsh manner

Examples

To domineer over others
He tends to domineer over his colleagues, making every decision unilaterally.
She refused to let anyone domineer her at work, standing up for her opinions.
Throughout the meeting, he domineered the discussion, not allowing others to speak.

Synonyms

  • lord over
    7.5 0.35/m
    lord over acts as if someone is superior and controls others openly, while domineer suggests forceful, arrogant control, often harsh.
  • lord it over
    8.2 0.39/m
    lord it over sounds more informal and petty, showing superiority in speech, while domineer implies a stronger, often harsh, controlling attitude.
  • hector
    8.4 0.18/m
    hector means to bully or threaten someone to get your way, while domineer covers broad, arrogant control often by harsh orders.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bully - to seek to harm, intimidate, or coerce someone perceived as vulnerable
  • synonym: tyrannize - to rule or treat (someone) despotically or cruelly
