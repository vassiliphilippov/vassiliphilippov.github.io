debriefing

noun
report or discussion after a mission or task
3.0
Pronunciation: / diˈbɹifɪŋ / (AmE), / diːˈbriːfɪŋ / (BrE)
a meeting or report in which people discuss and analyze a completed mission, task, or event, especially to evaluate what happened and what can be improved

Examples

A debriefing meeting
After the flight, the crew attended a debriefing to discuss the mission's results.
Every rescue operation is followed by a detailed debriefing to identify areas for improvement.
The manager scheduled a debriefing with the team to review the project's challenges.

Related Terms

  • antonym: briefing - meeting or session where instructions or information are given before an event
