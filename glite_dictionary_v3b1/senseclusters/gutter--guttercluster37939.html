gutter

verb
cut channels into a surface
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈɡʌtər /; / ˈɡʌtɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡʌtə / (BrE)
to erode, cut, or flow in a thin, irregular stream or channel, especially by water or other forces

Examples

Water guttered the path
Over years, flowing water slowly guttered the stone path.
The melting ice guttered the pavement, leaving irregular grooves.
The strong winds and rain guttered deep channels into the hillside.
Rainwater guttered along the cracked windowpane during the storm.
Tears guttered down her face as she remembered the tragedy.
Wax guttered down the candle, leaving uneven trails on the table.

Related Terms

  • synonym: trickle - to flow or fall in a thin, slow, or irregular stream
