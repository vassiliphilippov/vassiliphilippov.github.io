dragline

noun
excavating machine
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈdræɡˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈdræɡˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a large machine with a long arm or boom and bucket used for digging or moving earth, especially in construction or mining

Examples

A dragline excavator
The workers operated a dragline to remove soil from the construction site.
A massive dragline was used in the open-pit mine to extract minerals.
After the flood, a dragline helped clear debris from the riverbank.
The construction crew operated a dragline to excavate the site.
A massive dragline was used to remove the top layer of soil in the mining operation.
Engineers inspected the dragline before starting the day's work at the quarry.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crane - a large machine for lifting and moving heavy objects
  • synonym: excavator - a machine used for digging and moving large amounts of earth
