fascine

noun
bundle of sticks
9.9
Pronunciation: / fəˈsin / (AmE), / fəˈsiːn / (BrE)
a bundle of sticks or brushwood bound together, used in construction or military fortifications

Examples

A fascine bridge
The soldiers laid a fascine over the muddy trench to create a stable path.
Engineers used a large fascine to reinforce the riverbank during the flood.
In ancient warfare, a fascine was often used to fill ditches and support siege equipment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gabion - a cage or box filled with earth or stones, used in construction or fortification
