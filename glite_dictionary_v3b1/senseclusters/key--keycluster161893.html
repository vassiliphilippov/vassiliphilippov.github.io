key

noun
a winged dry fruit (botany)
9.1 2.28/m
Pronunciation: / kiÀê / (AmE), / kiÀê / (BrE)
A winged, often one-seeded dry fruit that does not split open when mature, as found in trees such as ash, elm, or maple.

Examples

Maple key
Children like to collect the keys from elm trees in autumn.
Each key on the ash tree contains a single seed with a papery wing.
The maple tree produces keys that spin as they fall to the ground.

Synonyms

  • samara
    9.9 0.02/m
    samara is a botanical term for a dry, winged, one-seeded fruit carried by the wind, while key is the common name.
  • key fruit
    9.3 0.00/m
    key fruit is a descriptive phrase for a winged, usually one-seeded indehiscent fruit on trees, while key is the shorter common name.

Related Terms

  • synonym: samara - a winged, indehiscent fruit (such as of the maple)
