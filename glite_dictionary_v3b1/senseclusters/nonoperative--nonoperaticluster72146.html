nonoperative

adjective
not in effect
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈɑpəˌreɪtɪv / (AmE), / nɒnˈɒpərətɪv / (BrE)
not in effect or having no legal or practical force, especially referring to a law, rule, or regulation

Examples

Nonoperative law
The court declared the old contract nonoperative after the new law was passed.
Because the policy is now nonoperative, employees are no longer required to follow it.
Several clauses in the agreement became nonoperative due to recent regulatory changes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: invalid - not legally recognized or in force
  • antonym: operative - having effect or force; in operation
