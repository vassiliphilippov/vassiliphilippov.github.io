a prefix meaning bad, difficult, or abnormal (often used in medical or scientific terms)

Examples

The prefix dys- in words
The prefix dys- in 'dysfunction' means abnormal or impaired function.
Words like 'dyslexia' use dys- to indicate a difficulty with reading.
Adding dys- to 'topia' creates 'dystopia', meaning a bad or unpleasant place.
The term dys-function refers to abnormal or impaired functioning of an organ.
In medical language, dys- is often used to describe painful or difficult conditions.
Words like dys-phagia indicate difficulty in swallowing.
