aleatoric music

noun
music with elements determined by chance
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪliəˈtɔrɪk ˈmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / ˌeɪliəˈtɒrɪk ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
Music in which certain elements are left to chance or to the performer's discretion, especially characteristic of some 20th-century compositions.

Examples

Aleatoric music style
John Cage is well known for his pioneering work in aleatoric music during the twentieth century.
Performers of aleatoric music often make creative decisions that shape each rendition.
The composer's score incorporated aspects of aleatoric music, allowing randomness in the final performance.
