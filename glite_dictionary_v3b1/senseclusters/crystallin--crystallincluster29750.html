crystallin

noun
eye lens protein
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪstəˌlɪn / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstəˌlɪn / (BrE)
a type of protein found in the lens of the eye that helps keep it transparent

Examples

Eye lens crystallin
Researchers discovered that crystallin plays a crucial role in maintaining the clarity of the eye lens.
A mutation in the crystallin gene can lead to cataracts and vision problems.
The structure of crystallin has been studied to understand how proteins prevent cloudiness in the lens.
