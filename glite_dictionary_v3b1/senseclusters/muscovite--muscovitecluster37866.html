muscovite

noun
colorless or pale brown mica mineral
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌskoʊˌvaɪt / (AmE), / ˈmʌskəˌvaɪt / (BrE)
A colorless or pale brown mineral of the mica group, containing potassium, commonly found in thin, transparent sheets.

Examples

Muscovite mineral sheet
Muscovite is often used in electrical insulators due to its heat resistance.
Geologists identified the shiny mineral as muscovite during the survey.
The thin layers of muscovite in the rock reflected light like tiny mirrors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mica - A group of silicate minerals that split into thin flexible sheets
