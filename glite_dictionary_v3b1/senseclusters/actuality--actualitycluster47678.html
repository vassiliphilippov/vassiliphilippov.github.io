actuality

noun
state of being actual or real
Pronunciation: / ˌæk.tʃuˈæl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌæk.tʃuˈæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the state of fact or reality; the condition of actually existing or occurring, especially as opposed to possibility or appearance

Examples

In actuality
Although the idea sounded promising, its actuality proved to be far more complex.
In actuality, the project faced more challenges than initially expected.
The scientist was amazed when the theoretical concept became an actuality after years of research.

Related Terms

  • antonym: possibility - a thing that may be true or possible, but is not certain
  • synonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist
