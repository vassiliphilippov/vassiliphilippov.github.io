contra

preposition
in opposition to
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːntrə / (AmE), / ˈkɒntrə / (BrE)
used to indicate opposition or contrast, especially in formal or academic contexts

Examples

Ruling contra argument
The judge ruled contra the defendant's argument, siding with the prosecution.
His theory stands contra the widely accepted scientific consensus.
The law was passed contra the wishes of many citizens.

Related Terms

  • synonym: against - in opposition to something
  • antonym: pro - in favor of something
