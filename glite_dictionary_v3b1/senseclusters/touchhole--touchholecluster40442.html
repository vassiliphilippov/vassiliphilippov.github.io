touchhole

noun
firearm ignition hole
Pronunciation: / ˈtʌtʃˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈtʌtʃˌhəʊl / (BrE)
a small hole in an old firearm through which the charge is ignited

Examples

The touchhole of cannon
The soldier made sure the touchhole was clear before firing the cannon.
Powder was poured into the touchhole to prepare the musket for use.
If the touchhole became blocked, the firearm would not discharge properly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: vent - an opening for the escape of gas or air, especially in firearms
