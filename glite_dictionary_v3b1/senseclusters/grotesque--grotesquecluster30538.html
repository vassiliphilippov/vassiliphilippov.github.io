grotesque

noun
decorative art with fantastic mixed forms
Pronunciation: / ɡroʊˈtɛsk / (AmE), / ɡrəʊˈtɛsk / (BrE)
a decorative style or figure featuring strange, distorted, or fantastical forms, often combining human, animal, and plant elements

Examples

A stone grotesque
He was fascinated by the intricate grotesque found on the ancient manuscript's border.
Renaissance artists often painted grotesques to decorate the walls and vaults of palaces.
The cathedral's ceiling was adorned with elaborate grotesques of mythical creatures and vines.
The ancient church was adorned with several stone grotesques peering down from the roof.
Artists in the Middle Ages often created grotesques to decorate cathedrals and public buildings.
Visitors admired the detailed grotesques carved into the facade of the historic library.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gargoyle - a carved stone figure, often with a spout to carry water from a roof
  • degree lower: statue - a carved or cast figure of a person or animal
