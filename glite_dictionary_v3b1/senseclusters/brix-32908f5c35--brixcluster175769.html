Brix

noun
sugar content scale
8.8
Pronunciation: / brɪks / (AmE), / brɪks / (BrE)
a scale used to measure the sugar content of a liquid solution, especially in fruit juices and wine

Examples

Brix scale reading
The winemaker checked the Brix of the grape juice before fermentation began.
A reading of 15 Brix indicates a high sugar content in the fruit sample.
Scientists use the Brix scale to ensure consistency in soft drink production.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Baumé - a scale for measuring the density of liquids
  • synonym: specific gravity - the ratio of the density of a substance to the density of water
