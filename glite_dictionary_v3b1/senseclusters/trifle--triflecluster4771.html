trifle

verb
treat lightly or without seriousness
8.2 5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈtraɪ.fəl/ (AmE), /ˈtraɪ.fəl/ (BrE)
to treat someone or something without seriousness or proper respect

Examples

To trifle with feelings
He was warned not to trifle with her emotions.
I wish you wouldn't trifle with such a serious decision.
She accused him of trifling with her trust.
Do not trifle with matters of great importance.
He tends to trifle when the discussion turns serious.
She was accused of trifling away her time instead of focusing on her work.

  • synonym: flirt - to behave as though attracted to someone, without serious intentions
  • synonym: play - to behave in a lighthearted or frivolous way
  • synonym: toy - to behave in a playful or casual way with something or someone
  • synonym: trifle with - to treat someone or something without respect or seriousness
