upbound

adjective
heading in an 'up' direction (e.g. upriver)
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpˌbaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌbaʊnd / (BrE)
(of a vehicle, ship, etc.) heading in a direction that is conventionally considered 'up', such as upriver or toward the interior of a country.

Examples

All upbound trains
All upbound trains are delayed due to maintenance work.
The upbound freighter passed us just before dawn.
Traffic in the upbound shipping lanes increased during the holiday period.

Related Terms

  • antonym: downbound - heading in a direction conventionally considered 'down', such as downriver
