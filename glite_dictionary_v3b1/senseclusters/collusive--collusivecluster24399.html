collusive

adjective
secretly acting in deceitful cooperation
8.4
Pronunciation: / kəˈluːsɪv / (AmE), / kəˈluːsɪv / (BrE)
acting in secret agreement with others, especially for a deceitful or illegal purpose

Examples

Collusive agreement
His collusive actions with the bidder undermined the integrity of the auction.
The companies were found guilty of engaging in collusive price-fixing to eliminate competition.
There was evidence of a collusive agreement among the contractors to rig the bids.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conspiratorial - suggesting involvement in a secret plan by a group
  • antonym: honest - free of deceit and untruthfulness
