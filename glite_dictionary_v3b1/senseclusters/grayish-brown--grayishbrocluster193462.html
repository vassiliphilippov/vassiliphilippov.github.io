grayish-brown

adjective
brown tinged with grey
Pronunciation: / ˌɡreɪɪʃˈbraʊn /; / ˈɡreɪɪʃˌbraʊn / (AmE), / ˌɡreɪɪʃˈbraʊn /; / ˈɡreɪɪʃˌbraʊn / (BrE)
Of a brown color that is tinged with grey; having characteristics of both brown and grey.

Examples

A grayish-brown coat
Her new coat is a grayish-brown that matches nearly everything in her wardrobe.
The bird has a distinctive grayish-brown plumage that helps it blend into the forest floor.
The old book's cover was a grayish-brown, worn by years of handling.
After the rain, the path turned a muddy greyish-brown under our feet.
She chose a greyish-brown paint for the living room to create a cozy atmosphere.
The fur of the animal was a soft greyish-brown that blended into the forest floor.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: brown - a color resembling that of wood or earth
  • degree lower: grey - a color between black and white
  • synonym: taupe - a grayish-brown color
