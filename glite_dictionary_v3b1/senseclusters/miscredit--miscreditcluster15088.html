miscredit

verb
to wrongly attribute
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪsˈkrɛdɪt / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈkrɛdɪt / (BrE)
to give credit or acknowledgment to the wrong person or source, or fail to give proper credit

Examples

To miscredit an author
The film mistakenly miscredited the composer in the closing titles.
She was upset when the report miscredited her work to someone else.
If you miscredit the source, it can lead to confusion and misinformation.
The article miscredited the discovery to the wrong scientist, causing confusion in the academic community.
She felt disappointed when the team miscredited her ideas to someone else during the meeting.
If you miscredit the source, it can lead to misunderstandings about who did the work.

Related Terms

  • antonym: credit - to acknowledge someone as the source of something
  • synonym: misattribute - to assign something to the wrong person or source
