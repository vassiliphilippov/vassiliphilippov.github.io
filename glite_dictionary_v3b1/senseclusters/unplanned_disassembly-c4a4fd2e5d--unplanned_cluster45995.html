unplanned disassembly

noun
catastrophic failure or destruction (technical slang)
6.8
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈplænd dɪsəˈsembli / (AmE), / ʌnˈplænd dɪsəˈsembli / (BrE)
a catastrophic and unexpected breakdown or destruction of a structure or device, often used humorously in engineering and aerospace contexts

Examples

An unplanned disassembly event
After hours of testing, the prototype underwent an abrupt unplanned disassembly due to a design flaw.
Engineers dubbed the catastrophic failure as an unplanned disassembly in the incident report.
The rocket experienced a unplanned disassembly soon after liftoff, scattering debris across the launch site.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: explosion - a violent bursting and destruction with force and noise
  • synonym: failure - the state or condition of not meeting a desirable or intended objective
