weak interaction

noun
a fundamental force in physics causing certain radioactive decays
8.1
Pronunciation: /wiːk ɪn.tə.ˈræk.ʃən/ (AmE), /wiːk ɪn.tə.ˈræk.ʃən/ (BrE)
a fundamental force in physics that is responsible for certain types of radioactive decay and involves interactions between elementary particles, especially neutrinos and antineutrinos, mediated by intermediate vector bosons

Examples

The weak interaction force
Neutrinos interact primarily via the weak interaction, making them extremely difficult to detect.
Scientists study the weak interaction to better understand the fundamental forces of nature.
The weak interaction plays a crucial role in the process of beta decay in atomic nuclei.

Related Terms

  • synonym: electromagnetic interaction - the interaction responsible for electric and magnetic forces between particles
  • antonym: strong interaction - the fundamental force responsible for binding protons and neutrons in the atomic nucleus
  • synonym: weak force - another name for the weak interaction
