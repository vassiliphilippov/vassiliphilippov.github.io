repurchase

noun
the act of buying something back
1.6
Pronunciation: / riˈpɜrtʃəs / (AmE), / ˌriːˈpɜːtʃəs / (BrE)
the act of buying back something that was previously sold

Examples

A repurchase agreement
A repurchase agreement is commonly used in financial markets to raise short-term capital.
After the sale, there was an option for the repurchase of the property within two years.
The company announced a repurchase of its own shares to boost their value.

Related Terms

  • synonym: buyback - the act of buying something back, especially shares or stock
