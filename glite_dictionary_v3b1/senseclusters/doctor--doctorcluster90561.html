doctor

noun
holder of a doctorate degree
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑktər / (AmE), / ˈdɒktə / (BrE)
a person who holds a doctorate degree, such as a Ph.D., from a university or equivalent institution

Examples

PhD doctor
After years of research, she finally became a doctor of philosophy in chemistry.
Many doctors in academia spend their careers teaching and publishing research papers.
The university awarded him the title of doctor for his outstanding dissertation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: professor - a senior academic who teaches at a university
  • antonym: student - a person who is studying at a university or other place of higher education
