hemorrhagic

adjective
relating to bleeding or hemorrhage
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛm.əˈrædʒ.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɛm.əˈrædʒ.ɪk /; / ˌhiː.məˈrædʒ.ɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or involving bleeding or a hemorrhage (the escape of blood from a ruptured blood vessel).

Examples

Hemorrhagic fever
A hemorrhagic fever outbreak prompted a rapid public health response.
Doctors warned that the infection could lead to hemorrhagic complications.
The patient suffered a hemorrhagic stroke that required immediate attention.
A haemorrhagic fever can be life-threatening without prompt treatment.
Doctors monitored her closely for any signs of haemorrhagic complications.
The patient developed a haemorrhagic stroke following the surgery.
