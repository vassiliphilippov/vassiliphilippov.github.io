toll line

noun
long-distance telephone line
4.0
Pronunciation: / toʊl laɪn / (AmE), / təʊl laɪn / (BrE)
a telephone line used for making long-distance calls, typically incurring extra charges beyond local service.

Examples

A toll line call
In the early days of telephony, businesses relied on dedicated toll lines for long-distance communication.
Making a call on the toll line was much more expensive than using a local line.
The company installed a toll line to connect its headquarters and regional office.

Related Terms

  • antonym: local line - a telephone line for local calls within a limited area
