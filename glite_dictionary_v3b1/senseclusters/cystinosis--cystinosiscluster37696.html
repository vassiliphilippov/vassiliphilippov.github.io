cystinosis

noun
rare genetic disorder
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪstəˈnoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌsɪstəˈnəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a rare inherited disorder in which the amino acid cystine accumulates in the body's cells, leading to organ damage

Examples

cystinosis disease
Children diagnosed with cystinosis often need lifelong medical care to prevent organ damage.
Doctors monitor kidney function closely in patients with cystinosis due to the risk of early kidney failure.
Research into new treatments offers hope for those living with cystinosis and their families.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cystinuria - a genetic disorder causing excess cystine in the urine
  • synonym: lysosomal storage disease - a group of inherited disorders caused by the buildup of substances in cells
