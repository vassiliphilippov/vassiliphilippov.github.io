confutative

adjective
able to be refuted
9.9
Pronunciation: / kənˈfjuːtətɪv / (AmE), / kənˈfjuːtətɪv / (BrE)
Able to be refuted or disproved; capable of being shown false by argument or evidence.

Examples

A confutative claim
A confutative claim is one that can be logically disproved or challenged.
Her statement was easily confutative by the facts presented in the meeting.
The scientist argued that the theory was inherently confutative and required further evidence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: irrefutable - impossible to refute or disprove
  • synonym: refutable - able to be refuted or disproved
