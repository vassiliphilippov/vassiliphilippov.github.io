scarabaeid

noun
a beetle of the scarab family
Pronunciation: / skəˈræbiːɪd / (AmE), / skəˈræbiːɪd / (BrE)
any of numerous species of stout-bodied beetles, especially those with heads bearing horny spikes; a scarab beetle.

Examples

The scarabaeid beetle
Ancient Egyptians revered the scarabaeid as a symbol of rebirth and protection.
Many scarabaeids are renowned for their flashy, metallic colors and unusual hornlike structures.
The entomologist collected several scarabaeids during his research in the rainforest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scarab - a large beetle of the family Scarabaeidae, often sacred in ancient Egypt
