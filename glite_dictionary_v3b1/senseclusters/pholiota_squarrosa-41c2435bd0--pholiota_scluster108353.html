Pholiota squarrosa

noun
species of scaly-capped mushroom
Pronunciation: / foʊˈlaɪ.ətə skwəˈroʊsə / (AmE), / fəʊˈlɪ.ətə skwəˈrəʊsə / (BrE)
a species of gilled mushroom with a cap and stem covered in upright, rough, yellowish scales, commonly known as the shaggy scalycap; its gills turn greenish with age

Examples

Pholiota squarrosa mushroom
Many people confuse Pholiota squarrosa with edible species, but it is not recommended for consumption.
The distinctive scales on the Pholiota squarrosa make it easy to identify during mushroom foraging.
The gills of Pholiota squarrosa develop a greenish tint as the mushroom ages.
