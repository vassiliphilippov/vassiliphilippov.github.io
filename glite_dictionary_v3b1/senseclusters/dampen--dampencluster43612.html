dampen

verb
make less distinct or clear
7.9 3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈdæm.pən/ (AmE), /ˈdæm.pən/ (BrE)
to make something less strong, intense, active, or noticeable (such as enthusiasm, sound, effects, or vibrations)

Examples

To dampen excitement
Noise-cancelling headphones are designed to dampen unwanted background sounds.
The fog began to dampen the outlines of the buildings, making them hard to see.
Too many technical details can dampen the message of a speech and make it difficult to understand.
Her disappointment did not dampen her determination to try again.
Strict rules are intended to dampen students' enthusiasm for risky behavior.
The news did little to dampen their excitement about the trip.
His refusal to participate did little to dampen their enthusiasm for the project.
The heavy curtains dampen the noise coming from outside, making the room quieter.
The new safety regulations helped to dampen the impact of the accident.

Related Terms

  • antonym: amplify - to increase the amplitude of oscillations, vibrations, or waves
  • synonym: attenuate - to reduce the force, effect, or value of something, especially waves or signals
  • synonym: deaden - to make a sound or feeling less strong or intense
  • antonym: intensify - to make or become more intense
  • synonym: moderate - to make something less extreme or intense
  • synonym: muffle - make a sound or message less clear or distinct
  • synonym: obscure - make less visible or unclear
  • synonym: quench - to extinguish or put out, especially a fire
  • synonym: restrain - to hold something back or limit it
  • synonym: soften - to make something less hard or severe
