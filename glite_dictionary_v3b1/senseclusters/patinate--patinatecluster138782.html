patinate

verb
apply a patina to a surface
Pronunciation: / ˈpætɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈpætɪneɪt / (BrE)
to cause a surface, especially metal, to develop a patina (an aged, colored, or decorative finish), either naturally or by treatment

Examples

To patinate metal surface
Over time, exposure to air and moisture can naturally patinate copper roofs.
Special chemicals are often used to patinate metal surfaces in restoration work.
The sculptor decided to patinate the bronze statue for a rich green finish.
The artist patinated the bronze statue to give it an antique look.
After years outdoors, the copper roof naturally patinated to a greenish color.
They patinated the metal handles so they would match the old furniture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corrode - to destroy or damage slowly by chemical action
  • synonym: oxidize - to undergo or cause to undergo a reaction with oxygen
  • antonym: polish - to make a surface smooth and shiny
