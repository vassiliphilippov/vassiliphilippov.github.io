precultural

adjective
before culture
9.5
Pronunciation: / priːˈkʌltʃərəl / (AmE), / priːˈkʌltʃərəl / (BrE)
existing or happening before the development or influence of culture

Examples

A precultural period
Scientists analyzed precultural tool use among early hominids to understand the origins of human behavior.
The study focused on precultural communication methods in animal groups before the rise of complex societies.
Some researchers debate whether certain instincts are truly precultural or shaped by early forms of social learning.
Some scientists argue that language ability is partly precultural and not entirely shaped by environment.
The study focused on precultural behaviors observed in newborn animals across different species.
Researchers debate whether certain moral instincts are precultural or learned through socialization.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acquired - learned or developed after birth
  • antonym: cultural - relating to the ideas, customs, and social behavior of a society
  • synonym: innate - existing from birth; not learned
  • synonym: prehistoric - relating to the period before written history
