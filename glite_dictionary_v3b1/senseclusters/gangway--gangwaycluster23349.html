gangway

noun
temporary bridge for boarding a ship
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæŋˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡæŋweɪ /; / ˈɡæŋˌweɪ / (BrE)
a temporary bridge or passageway used for boarding or leaving a ship, or for crossing over mud or uneven ground

Examples

Ship gangway
As soon as the ship docked, the crew lowered the gangway.
For safety, children should be accompanied when walking on the gangway.
The passengers crossed the gangway to board the ship.
A temporary gangway connected the building to the road during construction.
Passengers used the gangway to board the ship from the dock.
The workers laid a gangway over the mud to move equipment safely across the site.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gangplank - a movable plank used as a ramp to board or leave a ship
  • synonym: walkway - a path for walking, especially one constructed for use by pedestrians
