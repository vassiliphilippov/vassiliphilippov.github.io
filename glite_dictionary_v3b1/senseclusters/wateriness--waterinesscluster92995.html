wateriness

noun
quality of being watery or thin
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔːtɚinəs /; / ˈwɔt̬ɚi nɪs /; / ˈwɔtəri.nəs / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtərɪnɪs /; / ˈwɔːtəri nɪs /; / ˈwɔːtəri.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being watery, thin, or containing too much water, either literally (like food, soil, or other substances) or figuratively (like an argument or idea)

Examples

The wateriness of sauce
She complained about the wateriness in the texture of the sauce.
The wateriness of the soup disappointed everyone at the dinner table.
Too much broth can add wateriness to the stew, making it less flavorful.
Critics complained about the wateriness of the novel's arguments.
His eyes reflected a certain wateriness after hours of crying.
The soup's wateriness disappointed everyone at the table.
Excessive wateriness in the soil can harm the roots of many plants.
The wateriness of the field made it impossible to play soccer after the storm.
They complained about the wateriness of the newly paved road, which remained muddy for days.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diluteness - the quality of being diluted or less concentrated
  • antonym: dryness - the state of being dry
  • antonym: richness - the quality of having depth, substance, or abundance (often as antonym to thinness or wateriness)
  • antonym: thickness - the quality of being thick or viscous
  • synonym: watery - containing or resembling water; dilute or thin
  • synonym: wetness - the state of being wet or moist
