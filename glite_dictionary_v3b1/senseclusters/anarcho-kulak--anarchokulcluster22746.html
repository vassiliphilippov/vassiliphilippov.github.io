anarcho-kulak

noun
wealthy peasant opposing collectivization
Pronunciation: / əˌnɑrkoʊˈkuːlæk / (AmE), / əˌnɑːkəʊˈkuːlæk / (BrE)
A wealthy peasant who opposes collectivization or supports small-scale capitalism, especially in the context of Soviet-era agrarian policies.

Examples

An anarcho-kulak farmer
He identified as an anarcho-kulak and opposed government interference in farming.
Some historians discuss the role of the anarcho-kulak in resisting Soviet agricultural reforms.
The newspaper accused the local landowner of being an anarcho-kulak who undermined collectivist ideals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kulak - a wealthy Russian peasant, especially one opposed to collectivization
