phish

noun
fraudulent message
Pronunciation: / fɪʃ / (AmE), / fɪʃ / (BrE)
a fraudulent email, message, or website designed to trick someone into revealing personal information or installing malware

Examples

A fake phish email
She recognized the phish and reported it to her bank before clicking any link.
The company warned employees not to respond to any suspicious phish they might receive.
Many people fall victim to a phish that looks like a real message from their bank.

Related Terms

  • antonym: legitimate message - a genuine email or message not intended to deceive
  • synonym: scam - a dishonest scheme or fraud
