Aga

noun
title for Turkish leader
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːɡə / (AmE), / ˈɑːɡə / (BrE)
a title of respect for a civil or military leader, especially in Turkey, the former Ottoman Empire, and some other Islamic countries, used chiefly in historical contexts

Examples

Ottoman Aga
During Ottoman times, an Aga often commanded military units.
The respected Aga presided over the local council meeting.
The villagers gathered to seek the advice of their Aga on important matters.
During the Ottoman era, the Agha was responsible for maintaining order in the barracks.
He was addressed as Agha out of respect for his leadership.
The local Agha offered guidance to villagers on many matters.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Bey - title for a chieftain or leader in Turkish and Ottoman contexts
  • synonym: Pasha - high-ranking title for an official in the Ottoman Empire
