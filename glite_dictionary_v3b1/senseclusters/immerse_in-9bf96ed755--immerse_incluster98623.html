immerse in

verb
become deeply involved or absorbed
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmɜrs ɪn / (AmE), / ɪˈmɜːs ɪn / (BrE)
to become deeply involved or completely absorbed in an activity, subject, or environment

Examples

To immerse in culture
During their travels, they tried to immerse in the local culture as much as possible.
He would often immerse in fantasy novels to escape reality.
She loves to immerse in her work and often loses track of time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: engage in - to participate or become involved in
  • antonym: withdraw from - to stop being involved in something
