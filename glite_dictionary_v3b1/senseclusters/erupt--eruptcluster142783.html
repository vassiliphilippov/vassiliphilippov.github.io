erupt

verb
to spew forth lava, rocks, or ash suddenly
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɪˈrʌpt / (AmE), / ɪˈrʌpt / (BrE)
(of a volcano or geyser) to become active and eject lava, ash, rocks, or steam suddenly and violently.

Examples

A volcano erupts
Mount Etna can erupt several times in a single year.
Scientists are monitoring the volcano in case it erupts again soon.
The island was evacuated when the volcano began to erupt violently.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - state of inactivity or tranquility (opposite of erupting)
  • synonym: explode - to burst violently as a result of internal pressure
