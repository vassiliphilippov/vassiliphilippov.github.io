erupt

verb
to spew forth lava, rocks, or ash suddenly
4.0 3.44/m
Pronunciation: / ÉªËˆrÊŒpt / (AmE), / ÉªËˆrÊŒpt / (BrE)
(of a volcano or geyser) to become active and eject lava, ash, rocks, or steam suddenly and violently.

Examples

A volcano erupts
Mount Etna can erupt several times in a single year.
Scientists are monitoring the volcano in case it erupts again soon.
The island was evacuated when the volcano began to erupt violently.

Synonyms

  • belch
    6.4 0.78/m
    belch means a volcano sends out smoke or lava in a sudden forceful blast, while erupt means it ejects lava, ash, rocks.
  • explode
    2.8 24/m
    explode means something bursts with great force and noise, while erupt describes a volcano releasing lava, ash, or steam suddenly.
  • blow up
    2.7 13/m
    blow up is a casual phrasal verb for something exploding suddenly and violently, while erupt is the word used for volcanoes ejecting lava.
  • burst forth
    5.4 0.39/m
    burst forth means to appear or rush out with energy, often in nonviolent or poetic ways, while erupt is violent volcanic activity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - state of inactivity or tranquility (opposite of erupting)
  • synonym: explode - to burst violently as a result of internal pressure
