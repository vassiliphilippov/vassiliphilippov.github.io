mealybug

noun
sap-feeding insect pest with waxy coating
4.2 6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈmiː.li.bʌg/ (AmE), /ˈmiː.li.bʌg/ (BrE)
A small, soft-bodied insect that feeds on plant sap and is covered with a white, powdery or waxy coating; often a destructive pest to fruit trees and other plants.

Examples

A mealybug insect
A mealybug secretes a waxy substance that protects it from many predators.
Our orange trees were infested by mealybugs that damaged the fruit.
The gardener applied insecticidal soap to control the mealybug population on his houseplants.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scale insect - any small, sap-sucking insect from the superfamily Coccoidea, often having a waxy or scale-like covering
