radio wave

noun
electromagnetic wave used for communication
1.5
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪdioʊ weɪv / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəʊ weɪv / (BrE)
an electromagnetic wave with a wavelength between 0.5 centimeters and 30,000 meters, used especially for communication signals

Examples

Radio waves for communication
Lightning storms naturally produce radio waves that can be picked up by radio receivers.
Radio telescopes detect radio waves emitted by distant galaxies.
Your phone receives radio waves that carry voice and data signals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: electromagnetic wave - a wave of electric and magnetic fields that propagates through space
