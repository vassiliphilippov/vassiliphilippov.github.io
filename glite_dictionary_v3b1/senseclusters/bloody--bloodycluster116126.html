bloody

adjective, adverb
informal intensifier (esp. British)
Pronunciation: / ˈblədi /; / ˈblʌdi / (AmE), / ˈblʌdi / (BrE)
(informal, chiefly British) used to emphasize a statement or to express annoyance, anger, or surprise; extremely (used before adjectives or adverbs)

Examples

Bloody difficult
I can't find my keys—this is bloody annoying!
It's bloody cold out here, isn't it?
That was a bloody brilliant performance from the whole team.
It's bloody cold outside tonight, so grab a coat before leaving.
She was bloody furious when she heard the news.
That was a bloody brilliant performance in last night's match.

Related Terms

  • synonym: damn - used for emphasis, as an intensifier (informal, sometimes offensive)
  • antonym: slightly - to a small degree; not very
  • synonym: very - to a high degree; extremely
