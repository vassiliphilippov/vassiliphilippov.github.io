catch

noun
act of apprehending a criminal
Pronunciation: / kætʃ / (AmE), / katʃ /; / kætʃ / (BrE)
the act or result of catching something, or the amount or thing that is caught (especially fish or animals)

Examples

The children were laughing as they tossed the ball during their game of catch.
He divided the catch among the crew members after a long day at sea.
The fishermen displayed their catch proudly at the market.
Yesterday's catch was much larger than usual, thanks to the good weather.
