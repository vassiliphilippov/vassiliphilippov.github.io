spruce pine

noun
a pine tree species of the northwest U.S.
Pronunciation: / ˈsprus ˌpaɪn / (AmE), / ˈspruːs ˌpaɪn / (BrE)
A shrubby pine tree native to the coastal northwestern United States, with two needles per bundle and red to yellow-brown bark fissured into small squares.

Examples

Shrubby spruce pine
The bark of the spruce pine peels away in small, square plates revealing its characteristic pattern.
Unlike most pines, the spruce pine has a shrubby form and is adapted to coastal conditions.
We spotted a tall spruce pine on the rocky shoreline during our hike.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pine - any coniferous tree of the genus Pinus
