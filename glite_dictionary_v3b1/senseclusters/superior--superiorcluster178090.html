of high quality or performance

Examples

Superior quality
Her superior skills made her the top candidate for the job.
The chef only uses ingredients of superior quality in his dishes.
This computer model offers superior performance compared to its competitors.

Synonyms

  • better

    better is more common in everyday speech to compare two things, while superior sounds more formal and general.
  • top-quality
    3.8 1.04/m
    top-quality describes the very best level of goods, while superior simply means higher in quality than others.
  • top-grade
    3.7 0.14/m
    top-grade labels items of the highest standard, while superior means higher quality but not always the absolute best.
  • outstanding
    3.4 18/m
    outstanding is clearly excellent and easy to notice, while superior is a more general higher-quality word.
  • surpassing
    5.8 0.00/m
    surpassing means far better than what is usual or expected, while superior simply indicates higher quality among peers.
  • select
    3.7 0.61/m
    select refers to things chosen for their fine quality or exclusiveness, while superior describes any item higher in quality.
  • unrivaled
    5.3 0.81/m
    unrivaled means having no equal and being the single best, while superior only means higher in quality than others.
  • unequaled
    6.2 0.34/m
    unequaled claims that nothing matches it in quality or skill, while superior simply compares one thing as better.
