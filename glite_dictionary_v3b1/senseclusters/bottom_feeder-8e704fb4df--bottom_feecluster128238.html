bottom feeder

noun
person profiting from others' misfortune
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑtəm ˌfidər / (AmE), / ˈbɒtəm ˌfiːdə / (BrE)
a person who tries to gain from the misfortune or problems of others, often in an unethical or opportunistic way

Examples

Bottom feeders in business
Many consider ticket scalpers to be bottom feeders in the entertainment industry.
Some people see bankruptcy lawyers as bottom feeders taking advantage of others' problems.
The tabloids are full of bottom feeders who thrive on celebrity scandals.
After the scandal, only a few bottom feeders tried to take advantage of the chaos left behind.
Many consider corporate lobbyists to be bottom feeders who manipulate the system for profit.
Some tabloids are run by bottom feeders who don't hesitate to invade people's privacy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: benefactor - a person who gives help, often with no expectation of personal gain
  • synonym: opportunist - a person who takes advantage of opportunities, often without regard to principles
  • synonym: parasite - a person who exploits others for personal gain
  • synonym: scavenger - a person who searches for and collects discarded items or profits from the misfortune of others
