computer peripheral

noun
external computer device
7.7 2.6
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpjuːtər pəˈrɪfərəl / (AmE), / kəmˈpjuːtə pəˈrɪfərəl / (BrE)
A piece of electronic equipment that is connected to a computer but not part of the main central processing unit, such as a printer, mouse, or external drive.

Examples

Common computer peripherals
Printers, scanners, and webcams are common computer peripherals used in most offices.
The company upgraded all their old computer peripherals to improve productivity.
You can easily add more storage by attaching a new computer peripheral to your laptop.
