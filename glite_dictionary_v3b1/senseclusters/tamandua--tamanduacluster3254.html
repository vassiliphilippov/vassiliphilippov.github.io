tamandua

noun
Central/South American small anteater
8.5
Pronunciation: / təˈmænduə / (AmE), / təˈmændjʊə /; / təˈmandjʊə / (BrE)
a small anteater from Central and South America with a prehensile tail and four large claws, feeding mainly on insects

Examples

A tamandua anteater
A tamandua relies on its strong claws to tear open insect nests.
During our hike in the rainforest, we spotted a tamandua climbing a branch.
The tamandua used its long tongue to extract ants from a hollow tree.
Both species of tamandua are adapted to a life spent partly in trees.
The tamandua uses its long, sticky tongue to catch ants and termites in the rainforest.
We saw a tamandua climbing a tree during our trip to Brazil.

Related Terms
