dyers' chamomile

noun
Eurasian herb used for dye; yellow flowers
9.5 9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪərz ˈkæməˌmaɪl / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəz ˈkæməˌmʌɪl / (BrE)
A Eurasian perennial herb (Anthemis tinctoria) with hairy, divided leaves and yellow flowers, traditionally used as a source of yellow dye.

Examples

Dyers' chamomile plant
She planted dyers' chamomile in her garden to attract pollinators and use for her dye projects.
Textile artists have used dyers' chamomile for centuries to produce beautiful natural yellow dyes.
