polliwog

noun
tadpole; larval frog or toad
8.6 0.06/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…ëÀêliÀåw…ëg / (AmE), / Ààp…íliÀåw…íg / (BrE)
a tadpole; the aquatic larval stage of a frog or toad, which gradually develops legs and lungs

Examples

A polliwog in pond
As a child, we would catch polliwogs in the pond every spring.
The polliwog slowly transformed into a tiny frog over several weeks.
You can see hundreds of polliwogs swimming near the water's edge in April.

Synonyms

  • tadpole
    1.6 1.40/m
    a tadpole is the standard, widely used name for the larval frog stage, while a polliwog is a less common regional variant.
  • pollywog
    9.9 0.02/m
    a pollywog is an alternative, informal spelling used by some speakers, while a polliwog is the other variant seen in writing and speech.
