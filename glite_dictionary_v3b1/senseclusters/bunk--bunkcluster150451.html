bunk

verb
to skip school
8.5
Pronunciation: /bʌŋk/ (AmE), /bʌŋk/ (BrE)
to skip school or work without permission

Examples

To bunk school
They bunked school to spend the day at the beach with their friends.
If you bunk class too often, you might get into trouble with your teachers.
She regretted having bunked the important lecture when she saw the exam questions.
They decided to bunk school and spend the day at the park instead.
If you bunk work too often, your boss will notice and might take action.
She regretted bunking class when she missed an important test.

  • synonym: play truant - to stay away from school without permission
  • synonym: skip - to not attend something you are expected to
  • synonym: skive - to avoid work or school by staying away without permission
