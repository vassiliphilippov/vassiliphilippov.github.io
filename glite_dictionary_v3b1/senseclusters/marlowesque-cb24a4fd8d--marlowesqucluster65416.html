Marlowesque

adjective
like Philip Marlowe
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːrloʊˈɛsk / (AmE), / ˌmɑːləʊˈɛsk / (BrE)
resembling the style, tone, or character of Philip Marlowe, especially in being tough, cynical, and morally ambiguous

Examples

Marlowesque detective
The detective's Marlowesque attitude made him both admired and feared by his colleagues.
Critics praised the film's Marlowesque dialogue and gritty urban setting.
Her writing style is distinctly Marlowesque, filled with sharp wit and moral complexity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hardboiled - tough and unsentimental, especially in detective fiction
  • synonym: noir - relating to a style of crime fiction with a cynical, dark tone
