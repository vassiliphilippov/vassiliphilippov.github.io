rheumatogenic

adjective
causing rheumatism
Pronunciation: / ˌruːmətəˈdʒɛnɪk / (AmE), / ˌruːmətəˈdʒɛnɪk / (BrE)
able to cause or promote the development of rheumatism or rheumatic disease

Examples

Rheumatogenic bacteria
Certain bacteria are considered rheumatogenic because they can trigger joint inflammation.
Doctors study which environmental factors are most rheumatogenic in patients with a family history of rheumatism.
The rheumatogenic properties of the virus were confirmed in several clinical studies.
