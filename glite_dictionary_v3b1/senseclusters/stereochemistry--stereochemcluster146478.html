stereochemistry

noun
3D arrangement in molecules
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˌstɛrioʊˈkɛmɪstri / (AmE), / ˌstɪəriəʊˈkɛmɪstri / (BrE)
the branch of chemistry that studies the three-dimensional arrangement of atoms in molecules and how this affects their properties and reactions

Examples

Study of stereochemistry
A deep understanding of stereochemistry is essential for designing effective new drugs.
The difference in stereochemistry can make two molecules act very differently in the body.
Students often find stereochemistry challenging because it requires visualizing molecules in three dimensions.
Understanding stereochemistry is essential for designing effective pharmaceutical drugs.
The stereochemistry of a molecule can determine how it interacts with biological systems.
Researchers analyzed the stereochemistry of the compound to explain its unique reactivity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chirality - the property of a molecule having a non-superimposable mirror image
  • synonym: isomerism - the existence of compounds with the same formula but different structures
  • synonym: structural chemistry - the study of the structure of chemical compounds
