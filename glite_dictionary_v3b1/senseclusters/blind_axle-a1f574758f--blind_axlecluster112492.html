blind axle

noun
an axle that turns without transmitting motion
Pronunciation: / blaɪnd ˈæksəl / (AmE), / blaɪnd ˈæksl̩ / (BrE)
An axle that rotates but does not transmit motion to other components.

Examples

A blind axle rotates
In some designs, a blind axle is used to improve load distribution without adding complexity to the drivetrain.
The trailer is supported by a blind axle that carries weight but does not drive the wheels.
Unlike the drive axle, a blind axle only serves to support the vehicle's structure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dead axle - an axle that supports wheels but does not transmit power
