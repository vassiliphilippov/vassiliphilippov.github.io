Haredim

noun
ultra-Orthodox Jews
Pronunciation: / xɑːˈreɪdɪm / (AmE), / xɑːˈreɪdɪm / (BrE)
members of the ultra-Orthodox Jewish community who strictly follow traditional Jewish laws and customs, or the community as a whole

Examples

The Haredim community
Many Haredim live in neighborhoods with their own schools and shops.
The Haredim are known for their distinctive dress and strict religious observance.
Some Haredim avoid using modern technology to maintain their traditional lifestyle.
The Haredim have distinct traditions and often live in close-knit communities.
In recent years, the political influence of the Haredim has increased in Israel.
Many Haredim choose to send their children to religious schools rather than secular ones.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Orthodox Jews - Jews who strictly observe traditional Jewish law and customs
  • antonym: secular Jews - Jews who do not strictly follow religious laws and customs
  • synonym: ultra-Orthodox Jews - members of the strictest branch of Orthodox Judaism
