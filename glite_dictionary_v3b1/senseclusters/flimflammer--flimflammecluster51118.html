flimflammer

verb
to cheat by trickery
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈflɪmˌflæmər / (AmE), / ˈflɪmˌflæmə / (BrE)
to cheat or swindle someone by using deceit or trickery

Examples

To flimflammer people
The salesman tried to flimflammer the tourists with a fake antique vase.
Be careful not to let anyone flimflammer you when buying things online.
They accused him of trying to flimflammer elderly customers out of their savings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defraud - to illegally obtain money from someone by deception
  • antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere
  • synonym: swindle - to cheat someone out of money or property
