common caper

noun
Mediterranean shrub grown for pickled flower buds
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmən ˈkeɪpər / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən ˈkeɪpə / (BrE)
a sprawling, spiny shrub of the Mediterranean region, cultivated for its edible flower buds, which are pickled and used in cooking

Examples

The common caper bush
Farmers in southern Italy cultivate common caper bushes to harvest capers each spring.
The common caper is often used in Mediterranean cuisine for its tangy pickled buds.
The prickly stems of the common caper spread along dry, rocky hillsides.
