sleep

noun
euphemism for death
Pronunciation: / slip /; / sliːp / (AmE), / sliːp / (BrE)
a state of inactivity or rest that resembles sleep, such as hibernation in animals or (figuratively) death

Examples

Eternal sleep
He hoped that when his time came, he would find peaceful sleep.
The phrase "put to sleep" is often used when discussing euthanasia for pets.
The poem spoke of eternal sleep as a release from pain.
During winter, the bear enters a deep sleep that lasts for months.
Some seeds remain in a kind of sleep until the right conditions for growth appear.
The frog's sleep during colder months helps it survive harsh weather.

Related Terms

  • synonym: death - the end of life; the permanent cessation of vital functions
  • synonym: dormancy - a state in which something is not active but able to become active
  • synonym: hibernation - the state of inactivity and metabolic depression in animals during winter
