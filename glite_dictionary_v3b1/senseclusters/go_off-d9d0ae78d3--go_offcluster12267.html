go off

verb
stop running or functioning (machine or device)
2.5 1.42/m
Pronunciation: / É¡oÊŠ É”f / (AmE), / É¡É™ÊŠ É’f / (BrE)
(of a machine, light, or device) to stop running, functioning, or operating; to become disconnected or cease activity.

Examples

The lights went off
Her computer went off in the middle of her work, causing her to lose important files.
If the alarm goes off, it means there's a power failure in the system.
The lights suddenly went off during the storm, plunging the house into darkness.

Synonyms

  • go dead
    3.9 1.35/m
    go dead is used when a device suddenly stops working or becomes silent, while go off means a device stops running.
  • cut out
    4.1 1.69/m
    cut out happens when a machine stops working suddenly, while go off describes any device or light ceasing to function.
  • go out
    1.0 7.52/m
    go out describes a light or fire that stops burning or shining, while go off refers to a device stopping or disconnecting.
  • go down
    3.4 6.18/m
    go down is used for systems or services that stop operating, often temporarily, while go off applies to machines or lights stopping.
  • shut off
    2.6 3.79/m
    shut off means someone stops a flow or machine with a switch or control, while go off usually means it stops by itself.
  • shut down
    2.9 18/m
    shut down means to turn off a machine or system, often in a planned way, while go off suggests it stops unexpectedly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: break down - to stop working due to a fault or malfunction (of machines)
  • synonym: shut down - to stop operating, especially in a controlled or deliberate way
