grass widower

noun
man separated from his wife
7.8 8.2
Pronunciation: /græs ˈwɪd.oʊ.ər/ /; / /grɑːs ˈwɪd.oʊ.ər/ (AmE), /grɑːs ˈwɪd.əʊ.ə/ /; / /græs ˈwɪd.əʊ.ə/ (BrE)
A man who is divorced or separated from his wife, or whose wife is temporarily away.

Examples

A grass widower man
After his divorce, Tom often joked that he was a grass widower rather than truly single.
Feeling like a grass widower, he dined alone every night during his wife's trip abroad.
In Victorian novels, the term grass widower was sometimes used for men whose wives were away for extended periods.
