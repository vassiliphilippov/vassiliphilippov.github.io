Pavlov

proper noun
Russian physiologist, studied conditioned reflexes
5.2
Pronunciation: /ˈpæv.lɑːv/ /; / /ˈpæv.ləv/ (AmE), /ˈpæv.lɒv/ /; / /ˈpæv.ləv/ (BrE)
Ivan Pavlov (1849–1936), a Russian physiologist famous for his research on conditioned reflexes, especially through experiments with dogs.

Examples

The theory of classical conditioning is directly linked to the work of Pavlov.
