fatty acid

noun
organic acid found in fats and oils
1.8
Pronunciation: / ˈfæti ˌæsɪd / (AmE), / ˈfæti ˌæsɪd / (BrE)
an organic acid consisting of a long chain of carbon and hydrogen atoms, commonly forming part of fats and oils

Examples

Fatty acid chain
During digestion, fats are broken down into fatty acids and glycerol.
Many vegetable oils are rich in unsaturated fatty acids.
Omega-3 is a type of fatty acid that is important for heart health.
