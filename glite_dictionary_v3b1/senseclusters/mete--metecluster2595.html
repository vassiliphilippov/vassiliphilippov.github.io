mete

verb
to distribute punishment
8.1
Pronunciation: /miːt/ (AmE), /miːt/ (BrE)
to give or distribute something, especially in measured amounts (often used with 'out')

Examples

To mete out punishment
The principal will mete out consequences to students who break the rules.
It is not our place to mete justice without understanding the full story.
The coach meted out praise to the players after their hard work paid off.
The teacher meted out praise to students who completed their assignments on time.
Justice was meted out fairly to everyone involved in the dispute.
The organization metes out food supplies to families in need every week.
The teacher will mete out rewards to the students who performed best.
It is not his place to mete justice without understanding the full story.
The organization metes food supplies to families in need every month.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allocate - to assign or give a share of something
  • synonym: dispense - to give out or distribute something
  • synonym: distribute - to give out or share something among people
  • antonym: withhold - to refuse to give something
