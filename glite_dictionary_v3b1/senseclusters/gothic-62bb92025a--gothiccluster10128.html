Gothic

noun
heavy black-letter typeface (15th–18th c.)
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑːθɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡɒθɪk / (BrE)
a style of heavy black-letter typeface, used especially from the 15th to the 18th centuries

Examples

Printed in Gothic
He studied the evolution of Gothic in early European books.
Many old manuscripts were written in Gothic typefaces that can be hard to read today.
The printer offered several fonts, including Roman and Gothic styles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: black letter - a script style with dense, angular letters used in medieval manuscripts and printing
