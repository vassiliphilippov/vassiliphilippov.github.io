show-stopping

adjective
causing interruption
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈʃoʊ ˌstɑːpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊ ˌstɒpɪŋ / (BrE)
causing a performance, process, or project to be interrupted or halted due to a serious problem or defect

Examples

Show-stopping error
A show-stopping error forced the team to delay the product launch until the issue was fixed.
The discovery of a show-stopping flaw in the design halted construction immediately.
Developers worked overnight to resolve the show-stopping bug before the software release.

Related Terms

  • synonym: critical - extremely important or decisive
  • antonym: minor - not serious or significant
