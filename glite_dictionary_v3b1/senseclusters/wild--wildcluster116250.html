wild

adjective
lacking restraint or control
1.0 3.4
Pronunciation: /waɪld/ (AmE), /waɪld/ (BrE)
(of behavior, actions, or celebrations) lacking control or restraint; excessive, unrestrained, or frantic

Examples

Wild party
He threw a wild party that lasted until dawn on Saturday.
Her wild laughter echoed through the silent corridors.
The crowd erupted in wild applause as the band appeared on stage.
After hearing the news, he became so wild that no one could calm him down.
In his wild state, he started shouting nonsense at everyone in the room.
She gave him a wild look, her eyes wide and unfocused.
He became wild with grief when he lost his beloved pet.
She was wild with anger after hearing the news.
The crowd grew wild with excitement as the band took the stage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - not showing or feeling strong emotion
  • synonym: frenzied - in a wild, uncontrolled, and excited state
  • synonym: raving - behaving or talking wildly, often in an uncontrolled or irrational way
  • antonym: tame - domesticated or under control
  • synonym: unruly - difficult to control or discipline
