eschatologist

noun
theologian specializing in eschatology
Pronunciation: / ˌɛskəˈtɑlədʒɪst / (AmE), / ˌɛskəˈtɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
A scholar or theologian who specializes in the study of eschatology, the part of theology concerned with death, judgment, and the final destiny of the soul and of humankind.

Examples

eschatologist studies death
As an eschatologist, she has published extensively on interpretations of the apocalypse in different religions.
The debate brought together a philosopher, historian, and eschatologist to discuss the concept of human destiny.
The university hired an eschatologist to teach about various beliefs regarding the end times.

Related Terms

  • synonym: theologian - a person who engages in the study of theology
