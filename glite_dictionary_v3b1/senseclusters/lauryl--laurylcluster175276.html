lauryl

noun
twelve-carbon alkyl group
Pronunciation: / ˈlɔːriːl / (AmE), / ˈlɔːrɪl / (BrE)
the alkyl group with twelve carbon atoms (dodecyl), often found as part of chemical compounds

Examples

Lauryl group structure
The detergent contains a lauryl group to help break down oils and grease.
Scientists often use lauryl sulfate in laboratory experiments as a surfactant.
Replacing the side chain with a lauryl can change the solubility of the compound.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: cetyl - an alkyl group with sixteen carbon atoms
  • synonym: dodecyl - an alkyl group with twelve carbon atoms
  • degree lower: methyl - an alkyl group with one carbon atom
