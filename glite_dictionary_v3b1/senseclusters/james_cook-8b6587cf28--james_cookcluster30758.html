James Cook

proper noun
English explorer and navigator, 18th century
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒeɪmz kʊk / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪmz kʊk / (BrE)
(1728–1779) An English navigator and explorer who claimed the east coast of Australia for Britain and charted many Pacific islands.

Examples

James Cook explorer
James Cook is celebrated for his detailed maps and exploration of the Pacific Ocean.
Many historians credit James Cook with expanding European knowledge of Australia and the South Pacific.
The voyages of James Cook had a lasting impact on navigation and global trade.
