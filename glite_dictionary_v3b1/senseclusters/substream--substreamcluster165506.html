substream

noun
smaller data flow
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbstriːm / (AmE), / ˈsʌbstriːm / (BrE)
a smaller stream or data flow that forms part of a larger stream or system

Examples

A substream of data
Each substream carries a portion of the total data in the network.
The video was divided into several substreams to improve playback quality.
Engineers monitored each substream to ensure no data was lost during transmission.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mainstream - the principal or primary stream or flow
  • synonym: stream - a continuous flow of data or water
