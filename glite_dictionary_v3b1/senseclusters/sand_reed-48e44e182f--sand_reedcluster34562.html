sand reed

noun
European sedge with sarsaparilla-like rootstock
6.0
Pronunciation: / sænd riːd / (AmE), / sand riːd / (BrE)
A European maritime sedge naturalized along the Atlantic coast of the United States, with a rootstock known for its sarsaparilla-like properties.

Examples

The sand reed plant
Scientists studied the spread of sand reed in its new habitats along the eastern seaboard.
Some herbalists use extracts from the rootstock of sand reed for medicinal purposes.
The sand reed is often found stabilizing dunes along the coast.
