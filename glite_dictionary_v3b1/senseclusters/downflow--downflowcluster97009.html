downflow

noun
downward flow
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊnˌfloʊ / (AmE), / ˈdaʊn.fləʊ / (BrE)
a downward movement or flow of a liquid or gas, especially in a controlled system or environment

Examples

A downflow of water
The downflow of water in the pipe was measured to ensure proper drainage.
Engineers designed the system to control the downflow of air in the cleanroom.
A sudden downflow of gas caused the pressure to drop in the chamber.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inflow - the movement of liquid or gas into a place
  • synonym: outflow - the movement of liquid or gas out of a place
  • antonym: upflow - an upward movement or flow of a liquid or gas
