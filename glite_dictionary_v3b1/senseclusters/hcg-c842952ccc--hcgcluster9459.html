HCG

noun
pregnancy hormone produced by the placenta
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪtʃ si ˈdʒi / (AmE), / ˌeɪtʃ siː ˈdʒiː / (BrE)
a hormone produced early in pregnancy by the placenta, the presence of which is used as the basis for certain pregnancy tests

Examples

HCG hormone test
A blood test can measure the levels of HCG to confirm pregnancy.
After implantation, the body begins to produce HCG rapidly.
Some pregnancy tests detect the presence of HCG in urine.

  • synonym: hormone - a chemical substance that regulates physiology or behavior
