pseudoscientist

noun
false scientist
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːdoʊˈsaɪəntɪst / (AmE), / ˌsjuːdəʊˈsaɪəntɪst / (BrE)
a person who pretends to be a scientist or promotes ideas that seem scientific but are not supported by real evidence

Examples

A pseudoscientist spreads false ideas
The media criticized the pseudoscientist for spreading misinformation about health treatments.
Many people were misled by the claims of the self-proclaimed pseudoscientist.
After his theories were debunked, he was labeled a pseudoscientist by the academic community.
The pseudoscientist claimed to have discovered a new energy source without any credible proof.
Many people were misled by the pseudoscientist who promoted untested medical treatments.
Despite criticism from experts, the pseudoscientist continued to publish his theories online.
The pseudoscientist claimed to have discovered a cure without any real evidence.
Many experts warned that the theories promoted by the pseudoscientist were misleading and unproven.
A true scientist relies on data, while a pseudoscientist often ignores facts that contradict their beliefs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: charlatan - a person who pretends to have knowledge or skills
  • antonym: scientist - a person who studies or practices science
