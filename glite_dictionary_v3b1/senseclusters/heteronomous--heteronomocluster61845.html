heteronomous

adjective
under external control
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛtəˈrɑːnəməs / (AmE), / ˌhɛtəˈrɒnəməs / (BrE)
subject to external laws, authority, or control rather than being self-governing or independent

Examples

Heteronomous rule
The colony remained heteronomous, governed by laws imposed from abroad.
In some ethical theories, human actions are considered heteronomous when guided by external rules.
The region's heteronomous status limited its ability to make independent decisions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: autonomous - self-governing; acting independently
  • synonym: dependent - relying on or controlled by someone or something else
