final judgment

noun
court decision settling a case
1.0 4.5
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪnəl ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (AmE), / ˈfaɪnəl ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (BrE)
a legal decision by a court that settles all issues in a case, leaving nothing but the enforcement of the decision

Examples

The final judgment of court
After two years of litigation, the court finally issued its final judgment on the matter.
Once a final judgment is entered, the case is officially closed except for enforcement.
The losing party can appeal the final judgment to a higher court within thirty days.

Related Terms

  • antonym: interlocutory judgment - a judgment that does not dispose of the entire case
  • synonym: verdict - the formal decision made by a jury in a trial
