theatrology

noun
study of theater and theatrical performance
Pronunciation: / θiˈætɹɑlədʒi / (AmE), / θɪˈatɹɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the study of theater, focusing on theatrical performance and its literary, physical, psychological, sociological, and historical contexts

Examples

Theatrology study
His research in theatrology explores how stage performance reflects historical events and cultural movements.
Many universities now offer courses in theatrology, combining theory with practical analysis of drama.
She decided to specialize in theatrology for her graduate studies, focusing on the relationship between performance and society.
