chronographic

adjective
in time order
Pronunciation: / ˌkroʊnəˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˌkrɒnəˈɡræfɪk / (BrE)
arranged or presented in chronological order, or relating to the ordering of events in time

Examples

Chronographic order
The documentary followed a chronographic structure, starting with the earliest events and moving forward in time.
She organized her research notes in a chronographic order to make the timeline clear.
The museum's exhibit used a chronographic display to help visitors understand the sequence of historical events.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chronological - arranged in the order in which events happened
  • antonym: random - lacking any definite plan or order
