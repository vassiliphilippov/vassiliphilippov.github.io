eternalism

noun
belief in eternal soul
Pronunciation: / ɪˈtɜrnəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪˈtɜːnəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief or doctrine that the soul or self continues to exist forever after death

Examples

Religious eternalism
Some religions teach eternalism, promising that the soul will live on forever.
The philosopher wrote extensively about eternalism and its implications for personal identity.
Debates between eternalism and annihilationism have shaped many spiritual traditions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: annihilationism - the belief that the soul ceases to exist after death
  • synonym: immortality - the state of living forever or never dying
