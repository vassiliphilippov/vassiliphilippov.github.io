unban

verb
to remove a ban
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈbæn / (AmE), / ʌnˈbæn / (BrE)
to remove a ban or restriction, allowing someone or something to participate or be used again

Examples

To unban a player
After a year of suspension, the committee decided to unban the athlete from competition.
The government may unban certain websites if they meet new regulations.
Fans celebrated when the club finally unbanned the popular player.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ban - to officially prohibit or forbid something
  • synonym: reinstate - to restore someone or something to a previous position or status
