brown rot gummosis

noun
fungal disease of citrus trees
9.4
Pronunciation: / braʊn rɑt ɡʌˈmoʊsɪs / (AmE), / braʊn rɒt ɡʌˈməʊsɪs / (BrE)
A disease of citrus trees caused by the fungus Phytophthora citrophthora, characterized by brown, rotting areas and gum exudation.

Examples

brown rot gummosis disease
Early detection of brown rot gummosis can help prevent the spread of the disease among trees.
Symptoms of brown rot gummosis include brown lesions and gum oozing from the bark.
The orchard was devastated by brown rot gummosis, leading to significant losses in citrus production.
