retransmission

noun
broadcasting again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːtrænzˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːtrænzˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of sending or broadcasting something again, such as a program, message, or data

Examples

Unauthorized retransmission
The cable company was fined for unauthorized retransmission of the popular TV series.
International retransmission of live sports events often requires special licensing agreements.
The retransmission of the news program allowed viewers in different time zones to watch it.
The system automatically initiates a retransmission if a packet is not received correctly.
Network congestion can increase the number of retransmissions required for successful communication.
After detecting an error, the protocol requests a retransmission of the missing data.
The retransmission of the emergency alert ensured everyone received the warning.
Due to technical issues, a retransmission of the data was necessary.
The station scheduled a retransmission of the popular documentary for viewers who missed it.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: broadcast - the act of transmitting a program by radio or television
  • synonym: rebroadcast - the act of broadcasting a program again
  • synonym: transmission - the act of sending data or a message from one place to another
