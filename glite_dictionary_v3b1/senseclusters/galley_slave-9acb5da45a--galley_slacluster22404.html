galley slave

noun
slave forced to row a galley
8.5 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæli sleɪv / (AmE), / ˈɡæli sleɪv / (BrE)
a person, especially in the past, who was forced to row a galley ship as a slave or prisoner; also used figuratively for someone forced to do very hard, menial work

Examples

A galley slave rower
He compared his monotonous office work to the toil of a galley slave.
In ancient times, a galley slave endured harsh conditions and little freedom aboard the ship.
Many galley slaves perished due to brutal treatment and exhaustion.
In ancient times, a galley slave endured brutal conditions rowing ships for hours at a time.
Many novels depict the harsh lives of galley slaves in the Mediterranean.
She felt like a galley slave after working twelve hours straight on the project.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drudge - person who does hard, menial, or tedious work
  • antonym: free man - a person who is not a slave or prisoner
  • synonym: oarsman - someone who rows a boat
  • degree higher: slave - a person who is the legal property of another and is forced to obey them
