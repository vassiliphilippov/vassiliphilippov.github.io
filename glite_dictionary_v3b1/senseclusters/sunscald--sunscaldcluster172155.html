sunscald

noun
plant sunlight damage
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌnˌskɔld / (AmE), / ˈsʌnˌskɔːld / (BrE)
damage to plant tissue or fruit caused by excessive exposure to direct sunlight, often resulting in discoloration or injury

Examples

Apple sunscald
The apples showed signs of sunscald after being exposed to the hot afternoon sun.
Gardeners often use shade cloth to protect young plants from sunscald during heat waves.
Peppers left unshaded in the field can develop sunscald on their fruit.

Related Terms

  • antonym: frostbite - damage to tissue caused by extreme cold
  • synonym: sunburn - skin or plant tissue damage from too much sun exposure
