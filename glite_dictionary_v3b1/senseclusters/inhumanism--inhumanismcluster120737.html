inhumanism

noun
non-human-centered philosophy
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈhjuːmənɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪnˈhjuːmənɪzəm / (BrE)
a belief or philosophy that rejects human-centered values and emphasizes perspectives beyond humanity

Examples

Inhumanism in philosophy
The poet's philosophy of inhumanism challenges the idea that humans are central to the universe.
Some environmental thinkers adopt inhumanism to emphasize the importance of nature over human interests.
His essays explore inhumanism as a way to rethink our relationship with the natural world.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anthropocentrism - the belief that humans are the most important beings
  • antonym: humanism - a philosophy that centers on human values and concerns
