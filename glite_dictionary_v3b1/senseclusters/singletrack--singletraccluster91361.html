singletrack

adjective
one-lane or one-track
6.6
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪŋ.ɡəl.træk/ (AmE), /ˈsɪŋ.ɡəl.træk/ (BrE)
having only one track or lane, allowing traffic to move in a single line in each direction or only one direction at a time

Examples

Singletrack road
The mountain road is a singletrack route, so drivers must pull over to let others pass.
Cyclists enjoy riding on singletrack trails that wind through the forest.
The old railway line is singletrack, which means trains must wait for each other at passing points.

Related Terms

  • antonym: doubletrack - having two tracks or lanes for traffic in both directions
  • synonym: narrow - not wide; limited in width
