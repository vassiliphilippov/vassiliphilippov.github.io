bicultural

noun
person of two cultures
6.0
Pronunciation: / baɪˈkʌltʃərəl / (AmE), / baɪˈkʌltʃərəl / (BrE)
a person who identifies with or belongs to two different cultures.

Examples

A bicultural person
Growing up as a bicultural gave him a unique perspective on both his parents' traditions.
Many biculturals find it easy to adapt to new environments and customs.
As a bicultural, she often navigates between family traditions and mainstream culture.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: monocultural - a person who identifies with only one culture
  • degree higher: multicultural - a person who identifies with more than one culture
