MSH

noun
hormone regulating skin pigmentation
Pronunciation: / ɛm ɛs eɪtʃ / (AmE), / ɛm ɛs eɪtʃ / (BrE)
a hormone produced by the anterior pituitary gland that regulates pigmentation in melanocytes

Examples

MSH hormone
An imbalance in MSH can result in unusual changes to skin color.
Doctors measured his MSH levels to investigate the cause of his skin discoloration.
The function of MSH is essential in regulating skin and hair pigmentation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: melanocyte-stimulating hormone - a hormone that regulates the degree of pigmentation in melanocytes
