get

verb
persuade or cause to do
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt / (AmE), / ɡɛt / (BrE)
to cause or persuade someone to do something, or to cause a state or condition

Examples

It took hours to get the kids to go to bed.
She finally got him to try the new restaurant.
We couldn't get our neighbor to join us for dinner.

The coach managed to get his team into the finals.
We need to get everyone out of the building quickly.
Her kind words got us feeling much better.
The unexpected news got me worried about the future.
Try not to get him angry by bringing up that topic.
