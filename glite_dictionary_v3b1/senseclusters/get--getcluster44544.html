Synonyms

  • induce
    4.9 6.33/m
    induce is a formal verb that often means to cause or persuade someone to act, while get is more informal and general.
  • persuade
    2.7 27/m
    persuade uses reasons or argument to change someone's choice, while get is more casual and can mean cause in many ways.
  • convince
    2.7 73/m
    convince makes someone believe something is true, often before they act, while get focuses on causing action or a state.
  • have
    1.0 858/m
    have means arrange for someone to do something or cause it indirectly, while get more often means persuade or obtain directly.
  • make
    1.0 877/m
    make causes something to happen or forces a change, often by direct action, while get can imply persuasion or informal causing.
  • cause
    1.0 262/m
    cause brings about an event or effect generally and often without intent, while get can mean persuade someone or bring about a state.
