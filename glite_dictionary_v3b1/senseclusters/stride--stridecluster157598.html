stride

verb
to walk with long, purposeful steps
4.4
Pronunciation: / straɪd / (AmE), / straɪd / (BrE)
to walk with long, decisive steps in a particular direction, often with confidence or purpose

Examples

To stride across the room
He strode confidently into the meeting, making an immediate impression.
She strides along the beach every morning, enjoying the fresh air.
The general strode across the battlefield, issuing commands to his troops.

Related Terms

  • synonym: march - to walk quickly with regular steps, especially in a group or with determination
  • degree lower: stroll - to walk in a leisurely way
