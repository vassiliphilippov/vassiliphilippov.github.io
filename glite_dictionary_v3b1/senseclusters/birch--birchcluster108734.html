birch

noun
bundle of birch twigs used for punishment
4.2
Pronunciation: / bɝːtʃ / (AmE), / bɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a bundle of twigs from a birch tree, or a single twig, used as an instrument for punishing people by beating them, especially in former times

Examples

A birch rod
In Victorian schools, the birch was often used to discipline unruly pupils.
Stories of the birch as a punishment tool appear frequently in old English literature.
The judge sentenced him to six strokes with the birch for his crime.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cane - a stiff stick used for corporal punishment
  • synonym: rod - a stick or thin pole used for hitting people as punishment
