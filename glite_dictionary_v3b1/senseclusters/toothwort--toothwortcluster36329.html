toothwort

noun
a North American herb with toothed roots
Pronunciation: / ˈtuːθˌwɜrt / (AmE), / ˈtuːθˌwɜːt / (BrE)
A North American herb of the mustard family, with pungent, scaly or toothed roots; several species in the genus Cardamine or Dentaria.

Examples

The toothwort plant
Early settlers used the roots of toothwort as a medicinal herb.
In spring, toothwort carpets the forest floor with delicate white flowers.
The botanist studied a rare species of toothwort growing near the riverbank.
