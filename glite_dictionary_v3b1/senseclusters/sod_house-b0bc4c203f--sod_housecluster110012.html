sod house

noun
house built from sod or adobe blocks
6.9
Pronunciation: / sɑd haʊs / (AmE), / sɒd haʊs / (BrE)
a house built from blocks of sod or adobe, typically laid in horizontal layers, historically common on the prairies.

Examples

A sod house building
Building a sod house required cutting and stacking blocks of earth from the prairie.
Pioneers on the Great Plains often lived in sod houses to cope with the lack of timber.
The museum features a restored sod house from the nineteenth century.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adobe house - a house built with sun-dried bricks made from earth and organic materials
