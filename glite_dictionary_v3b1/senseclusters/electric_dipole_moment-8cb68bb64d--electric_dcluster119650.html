electric dipole moment

noun
measure of charge separation in a system
9.8 6.1
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈlek.trɪk ˈdaɪ.poʊl ˈmoʊ.mənt/ /; / /ə.ˈlek.trɪk ˈdaɪ.poʊl ˈmoʊ.mənt/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈlek.trɪk ˈdaɪ.pəʊl ˈməʊ.mənt/ /; / /ə.ˈlek.trɪk ˈdaɪ.pəʊl ˈməʊ.mənt/ (BrE)
A measure of the separation of positive and negative electrical charges in a system, defined as the product of the magnitude of the charge and the distance between the charges.

Examples

An electric dipole moment measure
A molecule with a large electric dipole moment tends to have stronger interactions with external electric fields.

The electric dipole moment of a water molecule is responsible for its unique solvent properties.
