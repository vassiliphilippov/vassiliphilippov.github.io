aspirational

adjective
desiring higher status
Pronunciation: / ˌæspəˈreɪʃənəl / (AmE), / ˌæspɪˈreɪʃənəl / (BrE)
having or showing a strong desire to achieve success, improve one's status, or reach a higher standard; designed to appeal to people with such desires

Examples

Aspirational lifestyle
The company created an aspirational brand image to attract young professionals.
Many people are drawn to aspirational lifestyles shown in magazines and advertisements.
Their new product line is marketed as aspirational, targeting those who want to appear successful.
Many young professionals have aspirational goals to advance quickly in their careers.
The brand markets itself as an aspirational choice for people seeking a luxurious lifestyle.
Her aspirational attitude inspired others to work harder and dream bigger.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ambitious - having a strong desire for success or achievement
  • antonym: content - satisfied with what one has; not desiring more
