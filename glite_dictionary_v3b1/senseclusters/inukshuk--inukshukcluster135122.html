inukshuk

noun
stone landmark sculpture
Pronunciation: / ɪˈnʊkʃʊk / (AmE), / ɪˈnʊkʃʊk / (BrE)
a stone landmark or sculpture, often shaped like a human, originally built by Inuit people as a marker or for navigation, and also used today as a symbol or artwork

Examples

A large inukshuk
The city installed a large inukshuk at the entrance to welcome visitors.
Many tourists took photos beside the inukshuk by the waterfront.
The Olympic logo featured a stylized inukshuk to represent unity and friendship.
During our hike in northern Canada, we spotted a large inukshuk on the hilltop.
The inukshuk served as a guide for travelers crossing the vast tundra.
Many visitors take photos with the inukshuk near the shore as a symbol of friendship.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cairn - a pile of stones built as a landmark or memorial
  • synonym: monument - a structure built to commemorate a person or event
  • synonym: statue - a carved or cast figure of a person or animal
