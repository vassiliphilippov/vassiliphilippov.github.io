Gorbachev

proper noun
Soviet leader, 1985–1991, Cold War reformer
6.5
Pronunciation: /gɔːr.ˈbə.tʃev/ /; / /gɔːr.ˈbə.tʃɔːf/ (AmE), /gɔː.ˈbə.tʃev/ /; / /gɔː.ˈbə.tʃɔːf/ (BrE)
Mikhail Sergeyevich Gorbachev (born 1931), Soviet statesman who led the USSR from 1985 to 1991, introducing reforms that contributed to the end of the Cold War.

Examples

Gorbachev reforms
Gorbachev introduced policies of glasnost and perestroika to reform the Soviet Union.
Historians debate whether Gorbachev intended to dissolve the Soviet Union or simply reform it.
The leadership of Gorbachev played a vital role in ending the Cold War.
