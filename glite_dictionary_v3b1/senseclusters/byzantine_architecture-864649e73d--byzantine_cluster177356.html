Byzantine architecture

noun
distinctive architectural style of Byzantine Empire
5.5 7.0
Pronunciation: /ˈbɪz.ən.taɪn ˈɑːr.kɪ.tek.tʃər/ /; / /ˈbɪz.ən.tiːn ˈɑːr.kɪ.tek.tʃər/ /; / /ˈbɪz.ən.taɪn ˈɑːr.kə.tek.tʃər/ (AmE), /ˈbɪz.ən.taɪn ˈɑː.kɪ.tek.tʃə/ /; / /ˈbaɪ.zæn.taɪn ˈɑː.kɪ.tek.tʃə/ /; / /ˈbɪz.ən.tiːn ˈɑː.kɪ.tek.tʃə/ (BrE)
the style of architecture that developed in the Byzantine Empire after the 5th century, characterized by large domes set on square bases, rounded arches, spires, and extensive use of mosaics

Examples

Byzantine architecture style
Art historians often study Byzantine architecture to understand the cultural influences of the medieval world.
Many churches in eastern Europe display characteristics of Byzantine architecture.
The Hagia Sophia is a masterpiece of Byzantine architecture, renowned for its massive dome and mosaics.
