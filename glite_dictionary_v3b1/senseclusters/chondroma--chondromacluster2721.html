chondroma

noun
benign tumor of cartilage
Pronunciation: / kɑnˈdroʊmə / (AmE), / kɒnˈdrəʊmə / (BrE)
A benign tumor composed of cartilage cells.

Examples

A chondroma tumor
Surgical removal is often recommended if a chondroma causes pain or impairs movement.
The doctor diagnosed the mass in her hand as a chondroma, reassuring her it was noncancerous.
X-rays revealed a small chondroma growing in the patient's finger bone.

  • degree higher: sarcoma - malignant tumor of connective tissue
