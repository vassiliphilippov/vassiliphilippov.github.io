Molucella laevis

noun
an aromatic plant with bell-shaped calyces
9.9
Pronunciation: / moʊˌluːˈsɛlə ˈliːvɪs / (AmE), / məˌlʊˈsɛlə ˈliːvɪs / (BrE)
An aromatic annual plant with tall stems, bearing small whitish flowers surrounded by conspicuous bell-shaped calyces, commonly called 'Bells of Ireland'.

Examples

Molucella laevis plant
Molucella laevis is often grown in gardens for its striking green bell-shaped calyces.
Florists use Molucella laevis in bouquets because of its long-lasting decorative appeal.
The unique appearance of Molucella laevis makes it a favorite in floral arrangements.
