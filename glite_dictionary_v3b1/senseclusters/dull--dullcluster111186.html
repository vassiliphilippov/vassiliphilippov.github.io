dull

adjective
not having a sharp edge or point
3.4 1.0
Pronunciation: /dʌl/ (AmE), /dʌl/ (BrE)
(of a blade or edge) not having a sharp edge or point; blunt.

Examples

Dull knife
It's dangerous to cut bread with a dull blade.
The chef refused to use the dull knife because it wouldn't slice cleanly.
You should replace your razor blade when it becomes dull.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sharp - having an edge or point that is able to cut easily
