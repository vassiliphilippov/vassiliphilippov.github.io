hesitancy

noun
uncertainty or reluctance to act
5.6 3.4
Pronunciation: /ˈhe.zɪ.tən.siː/ (AmE), /ˈhe.zɪ.tən.siː/ (BrE)
the state of being uncertain, indecisive, or unwilling to act quickly

Examples

His hesitancy to decide
Despite his hesitancy, he eventually agreed to join the project.
Her hesitancy to speak in public was obvious during the meeting.
There was a moment of hesitancy before he accepted the new position.
Despite his hesitancy, he eventually shared his opinion with the team.
The doctor observed some hesitancy in patients about receiving the new vaccine.
There was a noticeable hesitancy in her voice when she answered the question.

Related Terms

  • antonym: confidence - a feeling of certainty or assurance, opposite of hesitancy
  • antonym: eagerness - keen or enthusiastic willingness
  • synonym: hesitation - the act of pausing or delaying due to uncertainty or indecision
  • synonym: reluctance - unwillingness or disinclination to do something
