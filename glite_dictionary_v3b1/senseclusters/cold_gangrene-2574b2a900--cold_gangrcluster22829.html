cold gangrene

noun
dry gangrene from arterial obstruction
7.6
Pronunciation: /koʊld ˈgæŋ.griːn/ /; / /koʊld ˈgæŋ.grɪn/ (AmE), /kəʊld ˈgæŋ.griːn/ /; / /kəʊld ˈgæŋ.grɪn/ (BrE)
A form of gangrene caused by arterial obstruction, marked by dry, dark brown dead tissue.

Examples

Cold gangrene tissue
Cold gangrene typically occurs in individuals with severe arterial disease, especially in the lower limbs.
The surgeon diagnosed the patient with cold gangrene after noting the dry, dark brown tissue on his foot.
Unlike wet gangrene, cold gangrene is not associated with significant infection or pus formation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dry gangrene - gangrene due to loss of blood supply, tissue dries and shrinks
  • antonym: wet gangrene - gangrene with bacterial infection and moist, swollen tissue
