distressed

adjective
suffering anxiety or sorrow
Pronunciation: / dɪˈstrɛst / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɛst / (BrE)
feeling or showing emotional or physical pain, anxiety, or discomfort; suffering mentally or physically

Examples

Distressed face
His distressed expression told us something was wrong.
Many distressed people sought comfort from their friends.
She looked distressed after hearing the bad news.
After running for hours in the heat, Mark looked distressed and struggled to catch his breath.
She appeared visibly distressed after hearing about the accident.
The paramedics quickly attended to the distressed hikers at the mountain summit.
During the interview, he appeared visibly distressed by the difficult questions.
She looked distressed when she heard the bad news and sat down in silence.
The distressed family waited anxiously for any information from the hospital.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - not showing or feeling worry, anger, or anxiety
  • antonym: comfortable - free from physical or emotional discomfort
  • synonym: strained - showing signs of being physically or emotionally stressed
  • synonym: upset - feeling unhappy or worried
  • synonym: worried - anxious or concerned about actual or potential problems
