flagellum

noun
artificial tail-like propulsor for microrobots
9.4
Pronunciation: /flə.ˈdʒe.ləm/ (AmE), /flə.ˈdʒe.ləm/ (BrE)
An artificial, often motor-driven tail-like structure attached to microrobots or nanoscale devices that produces propulsion by rotating or beating, modelled on biological flagella.

Examples

Robotic flagellum
Researchers developed an artificial flagellum to propel the microrobot through fluids.
The team attached a rotating flagellum to the micro-swimmer to increase its forward speed.
Using magnetic fields, engineers steered multiple microbots equipped with tiny flagella during experiments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flagellum (biological) - the natural whip-like cellular appendage that produces locomotion in certain cells and microorganisms
  • synonym: micropropeller - a small rotating device that provides thrust to a microrobot or micro-swimmer
