pickaxe

noun
heavy pointed tool for breaking rock or soil
1.1 4.1
Pronunciation: /ˈpɪk.æks/ (AmE), /ˈpɪk.æks/ (BrE)
A heavy iron tool with a wooden handle and a curved, pointed head, used for breaking up hard surfaces such as rocks or soil.

Examples

A pickaxe tool
A rusty pickaxe lay abandoned by the old railway tracks.
She used a pickaxe to break up the frozen ground in the garden.
The miners swung their pickaxe against the rock, sending sparks flying.
