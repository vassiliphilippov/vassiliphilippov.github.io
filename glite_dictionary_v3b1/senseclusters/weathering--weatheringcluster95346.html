weathering

noun
damage from weather
3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈweð.ər.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈweð.ə.rɪŋ/ (BrE)
the gradual damage or change to materials, surfaces, or structures caused by exposure to weather and atmospheric conditions

Examples

Weathering of buildings
The stone statues showed signs of weathering after many years in the open air.
Regular maintenance can help protect buildings from severe weathering caused by rain and wind.
The paint on the old barn faded due to constant weathering from sun and storms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decay - the process of rotting or breaking down over time
  • synonym: erosion - the gradual wearing away of land or soil by natural forces
