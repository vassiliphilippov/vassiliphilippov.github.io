resorption

noun
breakdown and assimilation of body material
8.1
Pronunciation: / rɪˈsɔrpʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈzɔːpʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a structure produced by the body is broken down and its components are assimilated, especially by cells

Examples

Bone resorption
Bone resorption is increased in osteoporosis, leading to weaker bones.
During healing, the body performs resorption of blood clots at the injury site.
The dentist explained how resorption of the tooth root could occur after trauma.
