nefariously

adverb
in a wicked or criminal manner
Pronunciation: / nɪˈfɛriəsli / (AmE), / nɪˈfɛːrɪəsli / (BrE)
In a way that is wicked, criminal, or morally wrong.

Examples

To nefariously break rules
He nefariously manipulated the results to benefit himself.
The company acted nefariously to avoid paying taxes.
They were nefariously involved in the cyber-attack.
