dimply

adjective
with small dimples
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪmpli / (AmE), / ˈdɪmpli / (BrE)
having small natural indentations or dimples, especially on the skin or surface of something

Examples

Dimply cheeks
Her dimply cheeks made her smile even more charming.
The golf ball's dimply surface helps it travel farther through the air.
He noticed the dimply texture of the orange peel as he peeled it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dimpled - having dimples or small indentations
  • antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface; without indentations
