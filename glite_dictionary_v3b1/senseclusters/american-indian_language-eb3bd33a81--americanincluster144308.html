American-Indian language

noun
a language spoken by Native Americans
4.4
Pronunciation: / əˌmɛrɪkən ˈɪndiən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / əˌmɛrɪkən ˈɪndiən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
any of the languages spoken by the indigenous peoples of North, Central, and South America before European colonization.

Examples

An American-Indian language family
Preserving an American-Indian language is crucial for maintaining cultural heritage.
She is fluent in an American-Indian language spoken by her ancestors.
Some universities offer courses in at least one American-Indian language.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Indigenous language - a language native to a region, often referring to the Americas
  • synonym: Native American language - language spoken by the native peoples of the Americas
