denaturation

noun
making alcohol undrinkable
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːˌneɪtʃəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːˌneɪtʃəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of adding chemicals to ethanol or other alcohols to make them poisonous or bitter and unfit for drinking

Examples

Denaturation of alcohol
The denaturation of industrial alcohol prevents people from consuming it as a beverage.
Government regulations require the denaturation of ethanol used in cleaning products.
After denaturation, the alcohol can be safely used as a solvent but not for drinking.

Related Terms

  • synonym: denatured alcohol - alcohol that has been made unfit for drinking by adding chemicals
