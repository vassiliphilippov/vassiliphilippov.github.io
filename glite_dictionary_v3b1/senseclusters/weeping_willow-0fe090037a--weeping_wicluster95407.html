weeping willow

noun
ornamental tree with drooping branches
5.6 2.6
Pronunciation: /ˈwiː.pɪŋ ˈwɪl.oʊ/ (AmE), /ˈwiː.pɪŋ ˈwɪl.əʊ/ (BrE)
A species of willow tree with long, drooping branches and slender leaves, originally native to China and widely grown for ornamental purposes.

Examples

A weeping willow tree

Artists often choose the weeping willow as a subject because of its graceful, drooping form.
The weeping willow by the pond created a peaceful, shady spot in the garden.
