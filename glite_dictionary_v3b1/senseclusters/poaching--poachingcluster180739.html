poaching

noun
recruiting from competitors
6.3 0.93/m
Pronunciation: / \u02c8po\u028a.t\u0283\u026a\u014b / (AmE), / \u02c8p\u0259\u028a.t\u0283\u026a\u014b / (BrE)
The act of recruiting or enticing employees, clients, or customers away from a rival organisation, often seen as aggressive or unethical.

Examples

Employee poaching
The startup blamed the larger company for poaching its best engineers with higher salaries and benefits.
Industry groups warned against aggressive poaching, arguing it damages smaller firms and reduces long-term innovation.
After the announcement, the CEO accused a rival of poaching clients by copying their pricing and promises.

Synonyms

  • poach
    7.0 0.39/m
    poach is a verb for taking others' employees, ideas, or work unfairly, while poaching names the aggressive, often unethical act of recruiting from rivals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: headhunting - the practice of actively recruiting talented employees from other organisations
  • synonym: recruit - to find and hire new employees; a more general term for hiring
