lapping

noun
overlapping arrangement in construction or design
5.9 4.9
Pronunciation: /ˈlæp.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈlæp.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
an arrangement in which one element covers part of another, as in tiles or shingles that overlap for protection or decoration

Examples

Lapping tiles
In mosaic art, lapping tiles create intricate patterns and add depth to the design.
The careful lapping of the roof shingles ensures that rainwater flows off without leaking through.
Traditional Japanese architecture often features lapping wooden panels to protect structures from the elements.
