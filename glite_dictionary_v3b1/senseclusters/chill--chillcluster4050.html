chill

verb
to discourage or depress
Pronunciation: / tʃɪl / (AmE), / tʃɪl / (BrE)
to discourage, depress, or make someone feel afraid or less enthusiastic

Examples

Chill the mood
His harsh words chilled the cheerful mood of the gathering.
Rumors of layoffs chilled the previously optimistic staff.
The sudden criticism chilled her enthusiasm for the project.
The sudden announcement chilled the enthusiasm of the entire team.
Rumors of budget cuts have chilled many employees' willingness to propose new ideas.
The threat of legal action chilled further discussion on the controversial topic.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deter - to prevent or discourage someone from doing something
  • synonym: discourage - to make someone less confident or enthusiastic
  • antonym: encourage - to give someone confidence or hope to do something
  • antonym: hearten - to encourage or give hope to someone
