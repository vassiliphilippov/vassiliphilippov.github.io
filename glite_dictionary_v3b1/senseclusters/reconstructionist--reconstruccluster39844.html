reconstructionist

noun
restorer of history
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkənˈstrʌkʃənɪst / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˈstrʌkʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who rebuilds or restores historical buildings, artifacts, or sites, especially in archaeology or conservation

Examples

Historic reconstructionist
The museum hired a skilled reconstructionist to restore the ancient mosaic floor.
As a reconstructionist, she carefully studied old photographs before rebuilding the historic bridge.
The team relied on a reconstructionist to piece together the damaged artifacts from the excavation site.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conservator - a person responsible for the preservation of works of art or historical items
  • synonym: restorer - a person who repairs or renovates something to its original condition
