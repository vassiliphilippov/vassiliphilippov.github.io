downshore

noun
farther part of coast
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈdaʊn.ʃɔːr/ (AmE), /ˈdaʊn.ʃɔː/ (BrE)
the part of a coast that is farther along or lower down from a particular point of reference

Examples

A quiet downshore

The fishermen moved downshore to find a better spot for their nets.
After the storm, debris was scattered all downshore from the pier.
