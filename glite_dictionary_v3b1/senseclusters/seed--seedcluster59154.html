seed

verb
to plant seeds
7.1
Pronunciation: / sid / (AmE), / siːd / (BrE)
to plant seeds, or for a plant to produce or shed seeds

Examples

To seed a field
Farmers often seed the fields in early spring to prepare for the summer harvest.
In late autumn, it's common to seed new grass on bare patches of lawn.
She carefully seeded her garden with wildflowers to attract bees and butterflies.
As soon as the flowers seed, the garden takes on a wild, overgrown look.
If you wait too long to harvest lettuce, it will seed and become bitter.
The wild grasses in the meadow seed every summer, covering the ground with new life.
If the plant does not seed this summer, it will not survive the winter.
Some herbs only seed after their second year of growth.
The dandelions seed in late spring, spreading throughout the lawn.
