group Pteridospermae

noun
extinct group of seed ferns (gymnosperms)
Pronunciation: / ɡruːp tɛrɪdoʊˈspɜːrmiː / (AmE), / ɡruːp tɛrɪdəʊˈspɜːmiː / (BrE)
a group of extinct fossil gymnosperms, also known as seed ferns, coextensive with the order Cycadofilicales, formerly used in some classification systems

Examples

Group Pteridospermae fossils
Fossils belonging to the group Pteridospermae are crucial for understanding the evolution of seed plants.
Many paleobotanists have studied the group Pteridospermae for clues about prehistoric flora.
The extinction of the group Pteridospermae marked a major change in plant diversity during the late Paleozoic era.
