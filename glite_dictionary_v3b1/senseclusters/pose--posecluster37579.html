pose

noun
affected manner to impress others
1.5 5.6
Pronunciation: /poʊz/ (AmE), /pəʊz/ (BrE)
a way of behaving or acting that is not genuine, intended to impress or deceive others

Examples

An arrogant pose
Everyone saw through his arrogant pose at the party.
His friendliness was just a pose, hiding his true intentions.
She dropped her pose of confidence once the meeting was over.
He dropped the tough-guy pose once he was among friends.
Her confidence was, in reality, merely a pose she adopted for interviews.
Some saw his environmentalism as just a pose to win support.

Related Terms

  • synonym: affectation - behavior, speech, or writing that is artificial and designed to impress
  • synonym: pretense - an attempt to make something that is not the case appear true
  • antonym: sincerity - the quality of being free from pretense or deceit
