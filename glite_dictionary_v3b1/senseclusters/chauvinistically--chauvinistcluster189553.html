chauvinistically

adverb
with extreme bias
8.6
Pronunciation: /ʃoʊ.və.ˈnɪs.tɪ.kəl.iː/ /; / /ʃoʊ.vɪ.ˈnɪs.tɪ.kəl.iː/ (AmE), /ʃəʊ.və.ˈnɪs.tɪ.kəl.iː/ /; / /ʃəʊ.vɪ.ˈnɪs.tɪ.kəl.iː/ (BrE)
in a way that shows excessive or prejudiced loyalty or belief in the superiority of one's own group, often with a sense of superiority

Examples

Argue chauvinistically
He argued chauvinistically that his team was always the best, ignoring their recent losses.
The article was written chauvinistically, dismissing all foreign achievements.
She spoke chauvinistically about her city's culture, claiming it was superior to all others.
He spoke chauvinistically about why only men should lead the team.
The article was criticized for being written chauvinistically and ignoring women's achievements.
Some people still behave chauvinistically in professional environments, which can discourage others.

Related Terms

  • antonym: egalitarian - believing in the equality of all people
  • antonym: impartially - in a way that is fair and unbiased
  • degree lower: patriotically - in a way that shows love for one's country
  • synonym: sexist - showing prejudice based on sex, especially against women
