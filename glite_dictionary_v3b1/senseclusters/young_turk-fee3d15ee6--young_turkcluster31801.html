Young Turk

noun
member of Turkish reformist movement (late 19th/early 20th c.)
Pronunciation: / jʌŋ tɜrk / (AmE), / jʌŋ tɜːk / (BrE)
a member of a group of Turkish reformists in the late 19th and early 20th centuries who sought to modernize and liberalize the Ottoman Empire by overthrowing absolute rule

Examples

The Young Turk revolution
He studied the impact of the Young Turk revolution on Ottoman society.
Several prominent Young Turks were later instrumental in establishing the Turkish Republic.
The Young Turk movement played a crucial role in shaping modern Turkey.
