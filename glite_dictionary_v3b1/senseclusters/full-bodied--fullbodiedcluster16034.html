full-bodied

adjective
rich and strong in flavor or character
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˌfʊlˈbɑdiːd / (AmE), / ˌfʊlˈbɒdid / (BrE)
having a strong, rich, and satisfying flavor or character, especially used to describe wine and other drinks

Examples

Full-bodied wine
She prefers full-bodied teas that have a strong and satisfying taste.
The coffee was rich and full-bodied, filling the room with its deep aroma.
This full-bodied red wine pairs perfectly with roasted meats and strong cheeses.

Related Terms

  • antonym: light-bodied - having a delicate or less intense flavor or character, especially in wine or drinks
  • synonym: robust - having a strong, rich flavor; vigorous in taste or character
