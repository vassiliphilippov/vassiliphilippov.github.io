MTN

noun
medium-term debt note
8.8
Pronunciation: /ˈem.tiː.en/ (AmE), /ˈem.tiː.en/ (BrE)
a medium-term note, a debt instrument issued by a company or bank with a maturity typically between one and ten years

Examples

Corporate MTN issue
The company issued an MTN to raise capital for its expansion projects.
Investors often choose an MTN for its flexible maturity options and steady returns.
The bank's five-year MTN was quickly purchased by institutional investors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bond - a debt security issued by a government or corporation
  • degree lower: commercial paper - a short-term unsecured debt instrument issued by companies
  • degree higher: long-term bond - a debt instrument with a maturity longer than ten years
