fascinate

verb
attract intense interest or curiosity
2.6 2.8
Pronunciation: /ˈfæs.ɪ.neɪt/ /; / /ˈfæs.ə.neɪt/ (AmE), /ˈfæs.ɪ.neɪt/ /; / /ˈfæs.ə.neɪt/ (BrE)
to attract and hold someone's intense interest or attention

Examples

To fascinate people
New technology continues to fascinate people of all ages.
She was endlessly fascinated by the mysteries of the universe.
The magician's tricks always fascinate the children in the audience.
Ancient history continues to fascinate scholars and students around the world.
She fascinates everyone she meets with her charm and wit.
The magician's tricks fascinated the children for hours.
Stories of haunted castles fascinate the children, making them too scared to move.
The magician's intense gaze seemed to fascinate his volunteer, who stood perfectly still on stage.
The movements of the snake fascinate the small mouse, leaving it frozen in place.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bore - to cause someone to feel uninterested
  • synonym: captivate - to attract and hold the interest and attention of; charm
  • synonym: enchant - to attract and hold by charm, delight, or wonder
  • synonym: hypnotize - to capture and hold the attention as if by a spell
  • synonym: intrigue - to arouse curiosity or interest
  • antonym: repel - to drive or keep away, the opposite of fascinate
