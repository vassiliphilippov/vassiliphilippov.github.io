immunosuppressive drug

noun
drug that suppresses immune response
7.6 3.2
Pronunciation: / ɪˌmjuːnoʊsəˈprɛsɪv drʌɡ / (AmE), / ɪˌmjuːnəʊsəˈprɛsɪv drʌɡ / (BrE)
a drug that reduces the strength of the body's immune response, often used to prevent rejection in organ transplants or to treat autoimmune diseases

Examples

An immunosuppressive drug treatment
After her kidney transplant, she had to take an immunosuppressive drug every day to prevent rejection.
Doctors carefully monitor patients on immunosuppressive drugs for potential side effects.
Prolonged use of an immunosuppressive drug can increase the risk of infections.

  • synonym: immunosuppressant - a substance that suppresses the immune response
