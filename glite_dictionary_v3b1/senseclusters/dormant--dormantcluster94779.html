dormant

adjective
temporarily inactive (esp. volcano)
4.0 1.32/m
Pronunciation: / Ààd…îÀêrm…ônt /; / Ààd…îrm…ônt / (AmE), / Ààd…îÀêm…ônt / (BrE)
(of a volcano, plant, animal, or other natural thing) temporarily inactive, but not extinct or dead; able to become active again

Examples

Dormant volcano
Seeds can remain dormant for years before conditions are right for them to sprout.
The dormant volcano hasn't erupted in over a century, but scientists believe it could become active again.
The park is home to several dormant geysers that occasionally spring back to life.
During the winter, the volcano appeared dormant but was still monitored closely.
Many trees become dormant in cold weather to survive harsh conditions.
The seeds remain dormant until conditions are right for growth.

Synonyms

  • inactive
    2.7 0.15/m
    inactive describes something not currently working and often longer or general, while dormant means temporarily inactive but able to become active again.

Related Terms

  • antonym: active - currently showing activity (e.g. of a volcano)
  • antonym: extinct - no longer active or able to erupt (e.g. of a volcano)
  • synonym: inactive - not active; not engaging in activity
