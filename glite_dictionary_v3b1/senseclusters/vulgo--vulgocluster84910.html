vulgo

adverb
commonly known as
Pronunciation: / ˈvʌlɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˈvʌlɡəʊ / (BrE)
used before an alternative name or alias to indicate 'commonly known as', especially in legal or historical contexts

Examples

Vulgo 'The Fox'
The document referred to the suspect as John Smith, vulgo 'The Fox'.
In the old records, she is listed as Mary Brown, vulgo 'Molly'.
The defendant, vulgo 'Shorty', was well known in the neighborhood.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alias - an assumed or additional name
