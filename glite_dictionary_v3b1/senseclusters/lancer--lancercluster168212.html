lancer

noun
historical cavalryman with a lance
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˈlænsər / (AmE), / ˈlɑːnsə / (BrE)
(historical) a cavalry soldier who is armed with a lance, especially in European armies of the past.

Examples

A lancer soldier
During the parade, actors dressed as lancers reenacted a historic charge.
Many European armies once included regiments of lancers among their cavalry.
The Polish lancer was renowned for his skill and bravery in battle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cavalryman - a soldier who fights on horseback
  • antonym: infantryman - a soldier who fights on foot
