incurably

adverb
in a way that cannot be cured
1.4 5.6
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈkjʊr.ə.bliː/ /; / /ɪn.ˈkjuːr.ə.bliː/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈkjʊə.rə.bliː/ /; / /ɪn.ˈkjuːə.rə.bliː/ (BrE)
in a way that cannot be cured or made better, especially about diseases or conditions

Examples

To be incurably ill
Doctors said he was incurably ill and could not recover.
She was incurably curious about the world around her.
Some people are incurably optimistic, no matter what happens.
Despite the doctor's efforts, his illness proved incurably resistant to treatment.
She remains incurably optimistic, even in the face of challenges.
The garden was incurably overrun with weeds after years of neglect.
