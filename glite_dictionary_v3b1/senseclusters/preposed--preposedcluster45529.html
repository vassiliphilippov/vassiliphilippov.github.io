preposed

adjective
placed before
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːpoʊzd / (AmE), / ˈpriːpəʊzd / (BrE)
placed before another word or element in a sentence

Examples

Preposed adjective position
In English, most adjectives are preposed and come before the noun they describe.
The preposed modifier in the sentence changes the meaning significantly.
Some languages allow both preposed and postposed adjectives depending on the context.
