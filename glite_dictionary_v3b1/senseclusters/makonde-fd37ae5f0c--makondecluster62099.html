Makonde

proper noun
ethnic group or language
Pronunciation: / məˈkɒndeɪ / (AmE), / məˈkɒndeɪ / (BrE)
a member of a Bantu-speaking ethnic group from southeast Tanzania and northern Mozambique, or the language they speak

Examples

Makonde people
The Makonde are famous for their detailed wood carvings and sculptures.
She studied the Makonde language as part of her research on African cultures.
Many Makonde people live in villages near the border of Tanzania and Mozambique.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Yao - a Bantu-speaking ethnic group in East Africa
