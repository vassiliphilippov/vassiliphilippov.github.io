heir-at-law

noun
person legally entitled to inherit without a will
9.6 7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈer.ət.lɑː/ /; / /ˈer.ət.lɔː/ (AmE), /ˈeə.ət.lɔː/ (BrE)
the person who is legally entitled to inherit the property of someone who dies without a valid will

Examples

The heir-at-law inherits
As the only child, John became the heir-at-law to his father's estate.
If a person dies without a will, the heir-at-law is entitled to inherit the property by default.
The court had to determine who was the rightful heir-at-law under state statutes.
