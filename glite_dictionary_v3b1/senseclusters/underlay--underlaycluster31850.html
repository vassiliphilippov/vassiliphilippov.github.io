underlay

verb
to provide with a base or lining beneath
7.0 3.9
Pronunciation: /ʌn.dər.ˈleɪ/ /; / /ˈʌn.dər.leɪ/ (AmE), /ʌn.də.ˈleɪ/ /; / /ˈʌn.də.leɪ/ (BrE)
to put or provide a supporting or protective layer beneath something

Examples

To underlay the floor
For extra insulation, they underlay the floorboards with cork sheets.
The craftsman underlaying the tiles ensures a smoother finish and greater durability.
The workers underlaid the carpet with a thick foam pad.
Before starting the run, the technician meticulously underlaid the plate to correct for the imperfect press bed.
Carefully underlaying the form with thin paper can solve minor alignment issues in traditional printing.
The printer had to underlay the type to ensure an even impression across the whole page.
A waterproof membrane was underlaid beneath the tiles to prevent leaks.
Before installing the hardwood floor, workers underlay it with soundproofing material.
They underlaid the carpet with foam padding for extra comfort.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overlay - to cover the surface of something with a layer, often placed on top
