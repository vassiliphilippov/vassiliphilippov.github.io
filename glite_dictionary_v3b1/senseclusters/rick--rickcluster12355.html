rick

verb
sprain or twist (body part) suddenly
Pronunciation: / rɪk / (AmE), / rɪk / (BrE)
to twist or sprain suddenly, especially a part of the body such as the neck or ankle

Examples

To rick your neck
After she ricked her shoulder during tennis, she couldn't play for weeks.
Be careful on the uneven path or you might rick your ankle.
He ricked his neck while trying to lift the heavy suitcase off the floor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sprain - to injure a ligament by a sudden wrench or twist
  • synonym: twist - to turn something suddenly, causing pain or injury
  • synonym: wrench - to twist or pull suddenly and violently
