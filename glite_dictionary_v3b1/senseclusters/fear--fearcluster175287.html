fear

verb
regard with reverence or awe
3.1 6.1
Pronunciation: / fɪr / (AmE), / fɪə / (BrE)
to regard someone or something with deep respect, reverence, or awe, especially in a religious context

Examples

They fear the king
In some cultures, elders are feared for their wisdom and honored with great respect.
Many people fear God as a sign of profound respect.
She taught her children to fear the law, not out of terror, but out of reverence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: revere - to feel deep respect or admiration for
  • synonym: venerate - to regard with great respect or reverence
