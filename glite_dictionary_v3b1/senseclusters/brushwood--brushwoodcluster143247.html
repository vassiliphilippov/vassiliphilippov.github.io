brushwood

noun
dense growth of bushes or twigs
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈbrʌʃ.wʊd / (AmE), / ˈbrʌʃ.wʊd / (BrE)
small branches, twigs, or undergrowth from bushes and shrubs, especially used as firewood, kindling, or for fencing

Examples

Dead brushwood
Dead brushwood can be a major fire hazard during dry months.
Farmers used brushwood to build simple fences around their fields.
The hikers had to push through thick brushwood to reach the old path.
During the drought, piles of dry brushwood increased the risk of wildfires.
The cottage was surrounded by thick brushwood that made it hard to see the path.
We gathered brushwood from the forest to make a campfire.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kindling - small pieces of dry wood or other material used to start a fire
  • synonym: thicket - a dense group of bushes or small trees
