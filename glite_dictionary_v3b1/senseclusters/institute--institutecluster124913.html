institute

verb
to formally begin or start (esp. legal action)
6.8 3.2
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈstɪ.tuːt/ /; / /ɪn.ˈstɪ.tjuːt/ /; / /ɪn.ˈstɪ.tʃuːt/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈstɪ.tjuːt/ /; / /ɪn.ˈstɪ.tʃuːt/ (BrE)
to officially start or establish something, such as a process, rule, or legal action

Examples

To institute new rules
After months of investigation, the government finally instituted criminal charges against the organization.
She threatened to institute a lawsuit if the issues were not resolved.
The company decided to institute legal proceedings against its former employee.
After the scandal, the organization instituted a strict code of conduct for all employees.
The government plans to institute new regulations to ensure workplace safety.
The university will institute an annual award for outstanding research.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abolish - to formally put an end to a system or practice
  • synonym: commence - to begin; to start
  • synonym: establish - to set up or found something
  • synonym: initiate - to begin or start something
  • antonym: terminate - to bring to an end
