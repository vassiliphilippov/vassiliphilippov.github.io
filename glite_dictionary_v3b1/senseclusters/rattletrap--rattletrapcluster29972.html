rattletrap

noun
dilapidated but working vehicle or machine
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈrætlˌtræp / (AmE), / ˈrætlˌtræp / (BrE)
A vehicle or machine that is in poor condition and makes a lot of noise, but still functions.

Examples

Old rattletrap car
Despite being a rattletrap, the car survived another winter.
My grandfather's truck is a rattletrap, but it still gets him around town.
We could hear the rattletrap coming down the road long before we saw it.
