quote

verb
repeat or copy a passage from someone else
Pronunciation: / kwoʊt / (AmE), / kwəʊt / (BrE)
to repeat or copy words from a book, speech, or person, often to support an argument or show the source

Examples

To quote a book
He quoted a line from Shakespeare in his speech.
She likes to quote famous philosophers during her lectures.
Writers often quote statistics to support their arguments.
During her presentation, she quoted several studies to back up her claims.
He quotes historical events to support his arguments in debates.
The lawyer quoted previous cases to illustrate his point in court.
Be sure to quote the passage exactly as it appears in the original text.
He accidentally failed to quote the source when copying the paragraph.
The editor asked her to quote the author correctly in the article.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cite - to mention or refer to as evidence for justification of an argument or statement
