evidence

verb
serve as proof; show evidence
Pronunciation: / ˈɛvɪdəns / (AmE), / ˈɛvɪdəns / (BrE)
to show, provide, or serve as evidence or proof of something

Examples

Data evidences success
Documented data evidences the effectiveness of this treatment.
His calm tone evidenced his confidence in the plan.
The torn fabric evidenced a struggle had taken place.
Her nervous laughter evidenced her discomfort with the situation.
The company's financial results evidence a significant improvement over the previous year.
The repairs evidence the homeowner's commitment to preserving the historic character of the building.
Several experts evidenced for the prosecution in the high-profile case.
She is willing to evidence on behalf of her colleague if called by the court.
The witness evidenced calmly during the trial, answering each question clearly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attest - to be or give evidence of something; to testify
  • synonym: demonstrate - to show clearly by giving proof or evidence
  • antonym: disprove - to show that something is not true
  • synonym: prove - to show the truth or existence of something by evidence or argument
  • antonym: refute - to prove something to be wrong or false
  • synonym: testify - to give evidence as a witness in a law court
