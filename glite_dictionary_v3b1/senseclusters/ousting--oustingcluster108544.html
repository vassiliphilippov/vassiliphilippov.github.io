ousting

noun
removal from a position or place
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈaʊstɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of removing someone from a position or place, especially by force or authority

Examples

The ousting of leader
After weeks of protest, the ousting of the dictator was celebrated in the streets.
The board discussed the possible ousting of the coach after another disappointing season.
The sudden ousting of the company president shocked the entire staff.

Related Terms

  • antonym: installing - the act of putting someone into office or a position
  • synonym: removal - the act of taking something or someone away
