encyclopedism

noun
profound scholarly knowledge
Pronunciation: / ɛnˌsaɪkləˈpidɪzəm /; / ɪnˌsaɪkləˈpidɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪnˌsaɪkləˈpiːdɪzəm /; / ɪnˌsʌɪklɪəˈpiːdɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the possession of profound, comprehensive scholarly knowledge; encyclopedic learning

Examples

Profound encyclopedism
His encyclopedism impressed the entire faculty at the university.

Striving for encyclopaedism, he spent years absorbing information from countless books and sources.
The scholar's encyclopaedism impressed the academic community during his keynote address.
