outer jib

noun
second outermost sail on a square-rigged ship
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtɚ dʒɪb / (AmE), / ˈaʊtə dʒɪb / (BrE)
the second outermost triangular sail set forward of the foremast on a large square-rigged ship

Examples

The outer jib sail
He studied the rigging to learn the position of every sail, including the outer jib.
The crew climbed onto the bowsprit to adjust the outer jib during the storm.
With the outer jib unfurled, the ship gained extra speed as it caught the wind.
