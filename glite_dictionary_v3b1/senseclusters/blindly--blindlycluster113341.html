blindly

adverb
without seeing or looking
Pronunciation: / ˈblaɪndli / (AmE), / ˈblaɪndli / (BrE)
Without being able to see or without looking; in a way that involves no use of sight.

Examples

Walk blindly
He stumbled blindly down the hallway after the lights went out.
She reached blindly for her glasses in the dark.
The cat pawed blindly at the toy under the couch.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sightlessly - without seeing; lacking sight
  • antonym: visually - in a way that relates to sight or vision
