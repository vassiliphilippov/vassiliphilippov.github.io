Tudor

adjective
English architectural style from Tudor period
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈtuː.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈtjuː.də / (BrE)
Relating to or characteristic of the style of architecture and design in England during the period of the Tudor dynasty (late 15th to early 17th centuries), often featuring half-timbering and steeply pitched roofs.

Examples

Tudor style house
Several Tudor cottages still stand in the village, displaying the distinctive architectural style.
She prefers Tudor furniture for its intricate carvings and historical charm.
The old manor features a classic Tudor façade with exposed wooden beams.
