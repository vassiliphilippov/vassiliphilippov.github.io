foot

noun
unit of length (12 inches)
Pronunciation: / fʊt / (AmE), / fʊt / (BrE)
A unit of linear measurement equal to 12 inches or one third of a yard, used primarily in the United States and Britain.

Examples

Six feet tall
He is six feet tall.
The pool is eight feet deep at the deepest point.
The table measures five feet in length.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: inch - a unit of length equal to 1/12 of a foot
  • degree higher: meter - the basic unit of length in the metric system, about 3.28 feet
  • degree higher: yard - a unit of length equal to 3 feet
