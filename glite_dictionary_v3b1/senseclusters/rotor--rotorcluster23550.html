rotor

noun
rotating part of a motor or generator
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊ.tər / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.tə / (BrE)
the rotating part of the armature in a motor or generator that turns inside a stationary part (the stator) to produce or use electrical energy

Examples

Motor rotor
Engineers inspected the rotor for cracks after the machine malfunctioned.
If the rotor is misaligned, the motor will run inefficiently and may overheat.
The rotor spins inside the stator to generate electricity in the wind turbine.

Related Terms

  • antonym: stator - the stationary part of an electric motor or generator
