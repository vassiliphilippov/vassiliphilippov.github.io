radiography

noun
production of internal images using radiation
1.2
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪdiˈɑɡrəfi /; / ˌreɪdiˈɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiˈɒɡrəfi /; / ˌreɪdɪˈɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the process of producing images of the inside of objects or the body using X-rays or other forms of radiation, especially for medical or scientific purposes

Examples

Digital radiography images
Advances in digital radiography have greatly improved the clarity and accuracy of diagnostic images.
Modern hospitals rely heavily on radiography to help diagnose broken bones and other internal injuries.

Advancements in radiography have greatly improved our ability to detect bone fractures.
Modern medicine relies heavily on radiography to diagnose and monitor a wide range of conditions.
She specialized in veterinary radiography to help diagnose illnesses in animals.
