side dish

noun
food served with the main course
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪd dɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈsaɪd dɪʃ / (BrE)
A food item served in addition to the main course, usually in a smaller portion.

Examples

A side dish of rice
Rice is a common side dish in many Asian meals.
She brought a homemade salad as her side dish to the potluck.
The grilled vegetables were served as a side dish with the steak.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accompaniment - something that is served or used with the main item to complement it
  • antonym: main course - the primary or most substantial dish in a meal
