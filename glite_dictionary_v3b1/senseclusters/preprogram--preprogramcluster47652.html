preprogram

verb
to influence in advance
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈproʊɡræm / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈprəʊɡræm / (BrE)
to influence or determine someone's thoughts, behavior, or actions in advance through conditioning, training, or prior information

Examples

To preprogram a mind
The teacher tried to preprogram students' attitudes toward the new subject before the course began.
Some companies attempt to preprogram employee responses through extensive training sessions.
Media can preprogram public opinion by repeatedly presenting certain viewpoints.

Related Terms

  • synonym: condition - to train or influence someone to behave in a certain way
  • antonym: free - to allow someone to act or think without restriction
  • synonym: influence - to have an effect on someone's behavior or thinking
