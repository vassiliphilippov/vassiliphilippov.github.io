encyclical

noun
letter from pope to bishops
7.3
Pronunciation: / ɛnˈsɪklɪkəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɪklɪkəl / (BrE)
A formal letter sent by the pope to all bishops of the Roman Catholic Church, usually concerning church doctrine or policy.

Examples

A encyclical letter
Historians often study encyclicals to understand changes in Catholic doctrine over time.
Pope Francis issued an important encyclical addressing environmental issues and climate change.
The bishops gathered to discuss the latest encyclical and its implications for church teaching.
