Ascension

noun

(in Christian belief) the bodily rising of Jesus Christ into heaven on the 40th day after the Resurrection.

Examples

The Ascension of Jesus
Believers regard the Ascension as a key moment in the life of Jesus.
Many paintings depict the Ascension with Jesus rising above his disciples.
The church celebrates the Ascension forty days after Easter.

Synonyms

  • Ascension of Christ
    7.5 0.01/m
    Ascension of Christ names Jesus' bodily rising into heaven on the 40th day, while Ascension is the general name for that event.
  • Ascension of Jesus
    6.7 0.02/m
    Ascension of Jesus refers to Jesus leaving Earth to go to heaven on the 40th day, while Ascension names the event itself.
  • Ascension of the Lord
    8.6 0.00/m
    Ascension of the Lord is often used for the church feast that celebrates Jesus' rise, while Ascension describes the actual rising event.
