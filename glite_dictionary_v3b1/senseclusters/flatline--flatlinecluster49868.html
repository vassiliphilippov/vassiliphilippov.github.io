flatline

verb
to stop progressing
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈflætˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈflætˌlaɪn / (BrE)
to stop changing or progressing and remain at a constant, unchanging level

Examples

Profits flatlined
After several years of rapid expansion, the company's profits flatlined and showed no further growth.
If your motivation has flatlined, it might be time to set new goals.
The number of new subscribers has flatlined since the start of the year.

Related Terms

  • antonym: grow - to increase or develop over time
  • synonym: stagnate - to stop developing or making progress
