blush

verb
become rosy or reddish in color
4.8
Pronunciation: / blʌʃ / (AmE), / blʌʃ / (BrE)
to become red or pink in the face, especially from emotion, embarrassment, or cold

Examples

To blush with shame
As she stepped outside, her cheeks began to blush in the cold morning breeze.
The apple's skin seemed to blush as it ripened on the branch.
When exposed to the wind, his nose would quickly blush a vivid pink.
During the awkward conversation, her cheeks began to blush deeply.
He tends to blush whenever someone compliments his work.
She couldn't help but blush when everyone sang 'Happy Birthday' to her.
