entoparasite

noun
internal parasite of animals
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˌɛn.toʊˈpær.ə.saɪt / (AmE), / ˌɛntəʊˈpærəsaɪt / (BrE)
A parasite that lives inside the internal organs or tissues of an animal, especially intestinal worms.

Examples

An entoparasite inside organs
Roundworms are a common type of entoparasite found in dogs and cats.
Studying entoparasites helps scientists understand diseases transmitted by internal parasites.
The veterinarian explained how an entoparasite can affect an animal’s digestive system.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ectoparasite - a parasite that lives on the external surface of a host
