fictionally

adverb
in an imagined way
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪkʃənəli / (AmE), / ˈfɪkʃənəli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to or exists only in fiction or imagination, not in reality

Examples

Exists fictionally
The city described in the novel exists only fictionally and not on any real map.
He argued fictionally about what might happen if time travel were possible.
The character was fictionally based on several historical figures combined.
The city was fictionally portrayed as a place where anything could happen.
In the novel, the events are fictionally exaggerated to create more drama.
The characters were fictionally connected, even though they never met in real life.
The town is fictionally set in the mountains, though no such place exists in real life.
He was fictionally portrayed as a hero in the novel, despite his flaws.
The events in the movie are fictionally inspired by ancient legends.

Related Terms

  • antonym: actually - in fact or really, as opposed to fiction or imagination
  • antonym: factually - in a way that is based on facts or reality
  • synonym: hypothetically - in a way that is based on a suggested idea or theory
  • synonym: imaginarily - in a way that exists only in the imagination
  • synonym: imaginatively - in a creative or inventive manner
  • antonym: realistically - in a way that is true to life or reality
