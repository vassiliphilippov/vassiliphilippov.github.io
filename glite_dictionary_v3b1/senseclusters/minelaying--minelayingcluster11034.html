minelaying

noun
the act of placing explosive mines
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪnˌleɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnˌleɪɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of placing explosive mines in concealed locations to damage or destroy enemy personnel or equipment.

Examples

Effective minelaying operation
Effective minelaying can significantly hinder enemy movement and supply routes.
The navy specialized in minelaying operations along the coast during the conflict.
The unit received extensive training in minelaying before their deployment abroad.
