close season

noun
period when hunting or fishing is forbidden
Pronunciation: / kloʊs ˈsizən / (AmE), / kləʊs ˈsiːzn̩ / (BrE)
A period during which certain activities, especially hunting or fishing, are legally prohibited in order to protect wildlife or allow stocks to recover.

Examples

The close season for fishing
Fishing is strictly prohibited during the close season to protect breeding stocks.
Local anglers must wait until the close season ends before they can fish in the river again.
The national park enforces a close season for deer hunting each spring.

Related Terms

  • antonym: open season - period when hunting or fishing is permitted
