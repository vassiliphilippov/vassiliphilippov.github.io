concessional

adjective
willing to compromise
6.8
Pronunciation: / kənˈsɛʃənəl / (AmE), / kənˈsɛʃənəl / (BrE)
showing a willingness to make concessions or compromise, especially in negotiations or discussions

Examples

Concessional attitude
The manager took a concessional approach to resolve the dispute with the employees.
Her concessional attitude helped the two sides reach an agreement more quickly.
During the meeting, he made several concessional offers to encourage cooperation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conciliatory - intended to make peace or reduce hostility
  • antonym: stubborn - unwilling to change or compromise
