dominion

noun
self-governing territory or administrative region
4.4 6.4
Pronunciation: /də.ˈmɪn.jən/ /; / /doʊ.ˈmɪn.jən/ (AmE), /də.ˈmɪn.jən/ /; / /dəʊ.ˈmɪn.jən/ (BrE)
a self-governing territory, especially one that was part of the British Empire; a region or area under administrative control

Examples

British dominion
Canada was once a dominion within the British Empire, having its own government but recognizing the British monarch.
Each dominion had a governor who was responsible for administration and order in the region.
The new constitution granted greater autonomy to the dominion and its people.
