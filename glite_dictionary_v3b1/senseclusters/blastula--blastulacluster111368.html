blastula

noun
early hollow stage of an embryo
Pronunciation: / ˈblæstjələ / (AmE), / ˈblastjʊlə / (BrE)
an early stage of an embryo, consisting of a hollow, fluid-filled sphere with a single layer of cells, formed after the fertilized egg divides

Examples

The blastula stage
Scientists observed the development of the blastula under a high-powered microscope.
The cells of the blastula eventually rearrange to form the tissues of the embryo.
When the fertilized egg reaches the blastula stage, it is ready for implantation.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: gastrula - later embryonic stage following the blastula, with cell layers forming
