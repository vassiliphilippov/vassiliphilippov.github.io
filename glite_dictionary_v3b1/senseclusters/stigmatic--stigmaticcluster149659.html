stigmatic

adjective
having no astigmatism (in optics)
5.2
Pronunciation: / stɪgˈmætɪk / (AmE), / stɪgˈmatɪk /; / stɪgˈmætɪk / (BrE)
(of an optical system or lens) free from astigmatism; able to focus light to a single point

Examples

A stigmatic optical system is essential for high-quality photography.
The lens is stigmatic and produces a clear, sharp image.
Unlike astigmatic mirrors, stigmatic mirrors focus all rays to a single point.
A perfectly stigmatic focus is essential in high-precision microscopy.
The new telescope features a stigmatic lens system for clearer, sharper images.
To correct for distortion, the optical engineer designed a highly stigmatic camera lens.
