trough

noun
long, narrow container
6.7
Pronunciation: / trɔf /; / trɑf / (AmE), / trɒf / (BrE)
a long, narrow open container or channel, often used for holding or carrying water, food for animals, or other materials

Examples

Animal trough
Rainwater collected in the stone trough beside the barn.
The farmer filled the trough with fresh water for the cows.
They planted herbs in a wooden trough on the windowsill.
After cleaning the trough, the farmer filled it with fresh water.
Hay was piled high in the wooden trough inside the stable.
The cows gathered around the trough for their morning feed.
A metal trough was used to direct the flow of water into the reservoir.
During the experiment, the chemicals were mixed in a glass trough to observe the reaction.
The wave formed a distinct trough between two crests as it moved across the tank.

Related Terms

  • synonym: basin - a wide, open container for holding liquids
  • synonym: gutter - a channel at the edge of a roof for carrying off rainwater
  • synonym: manger - an open box in a stable from which horses or cattle feed
  • antonym: ridge - a long narrow elevated land formation or surface
