clean

adjective
morally pure; virtuous
Pronunciation: / klin / (AmE), / kliːn / (BrE)
free from immoral, improper, or offensive behavior or content; having high moral standards or suitable for all audiences

Examples

A clean joke
Despite being surrounded by temptation, she always tried to lead a clean life.
He has a reputation for being completely clean in both his personal and professional conduct.
The organization aims to promote clean living among young people.
Both teams agreed to keep the competition clean and respectful.
She believes in playing a clean game, even if it means losing.
The referee ensured it was a clean match with no fouls or unsportsmanlike behavior.
Her stand-up routine was entirely clean, with no swearing or adult themes.
The comedian is known for his clean jokes that everyone can enjoy.
We want this movie to be clean enough for children to watch.

Related Terms

  • antonym: corrupt - showing a lack of moral principles; dishonest or immoral
  • antonym: dirty - not showing good sportsmanship, involving cheating or unfair tactics
  • synonym: pure - free from moral fault
