mog

verb
to boast or brag
Pronunciation: / mɑɡ / (AmE), / mɒɡ / (BrE)
to boast or brag about one's achievements or abilities, often in a showy way

Examples

To mog about success
He likes to mog about his skills whenever he gets the chance.
After winning the contest, she couldn't help but mog in front of her friends.
Try not to mog too much about your promotion at the party.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brag - to talk with excessive pride about one's achievements
  • antonym: humble - to be modest and not boastful
  • synonym: show off - to display one's abilities or achievements in a way meant to attract attention
