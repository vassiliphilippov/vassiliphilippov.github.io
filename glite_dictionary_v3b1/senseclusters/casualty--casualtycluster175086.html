casualty

noun
a person injured, killed, or missing in an event
Pronunciation: / ˈkæʒuˌælti / (AmE), / ˈkæʒjʊəlti /; / ˈkæʒjuəlti / (BrE)
a person who is injured, killed, or missing as a result of an accident, disaster, or war

Examples

War casualty
After the explosion, the medic rushed to help every casualty on the field.
Reports confirmed that there was at least one casualty during the night raid.
The battle resulted in heavy casualty numbers on both sides.
The first responders worked quickly to help the casualty at the scene of the accident.
The storm last night caused several casualties in the small village.
There were no casualties reported after the explosion in the factory.
