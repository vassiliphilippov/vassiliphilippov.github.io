low beam headlamp

noun
headlamp setting for minimal glare
3.3 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / lo ä bim Ààh…õdÀål√¶mp / (AmE), / l…ô ä biÀêm Ààh…õdÀålamp / (BrE)
A headlamp setting on a motor vehicle that provides adequate forward and lateral illumination without causing excessive glare to other road users; used when other vehicles are present.

Examples

Use low beam headlamp in fog
During rain or fog, it's safer to use your low beam headlamp instead of the high beams.
Please remember to switch to low beam headlamps when driving in traffic at night.
The driver forgot to turn off his low beam headlamps when he parked the car.

Synonyms

    5.3 0.00/m

  • meeting beam headlamp
    6.3 0.00/m
    meeting beam headlamp is used when other vehicles are present to avoid dazzling drivers, while low beam headlamp describes this safer lighting choice.
  • passing beam headlamp
    5.6 0.00/m
    passing beam headlamp gives a dipped low beam to light the road without glare, while low beam headlamp refers to that simple safety setting.
  • low-beam
