false labor

noun
non-birth uterine contractions in pregnancy
5.1 0.08/m
Pronunciation: / f…îls Ààle…™b…ö / (AmE), / f…îÀêls Ààle…™b…ô / (BrE)
Painless contractions of the uterus that occur during pregnancy but do not result in childbirth and are distinguished from true labor.

Examples

Painless false labor
Doctors often reassure patients that false labor is normal and not a cause for concern.
Some women experience false labor in the weeks leading up to their due date.
Unlike true contractions, false labor does not indicate that childbirth is imminent.

Synonyms

  • Braxton-Hicks contraction
    5.1 0.01/m
    A Braxton-Hicks contraction is a painless, irregular tightening of the uterus in pregnancy, while false labor means contractions that do not lead to childbirth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: true labor - contractions leading to childbirth
