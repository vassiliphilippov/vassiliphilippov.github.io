snowmelt

noun
melting of snow
3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈsnoʊˌmɛlt / (AmE), / ˈsnəʊˌmɛlt / (BrE)
the process or period when snow melts, especially in spring, often leading to increased water flow in rivers and streams

Examples

Spring snowmelt
The rapid snowmelt in the mountains caused the river to overflow its banks.
Farmers prepared for possible flooding as the annual snowmelt approached.
Scientists monitor snowmelt to predict water availability for the summer months.

Related Terms

  • antonym: freeze - the process of turning into ice or becoming solid due to cold
  • synonym: thaw - the process of ice or snow melting
