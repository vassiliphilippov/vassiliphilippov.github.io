prepositional object

noun
object noun or pronoun of a preposition
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˌprɛpəˈzɪʃənl ˈɑbdʒɛkt / (AmE), / ˌprɛpəˈzɪʃənəl ˈɒbdʒɪkt / (BrE)
a noun or pronoun that is governed by a preposition in a sentence

Examples

A prepositional object noun
In the sentence 'She sat on the chair,' 'chair' is a prepositional object.
The prepositional object typically answers the question 'what?' or 'whom?' after the preposition.
You must identify the prepositional object in each sentence for this exercise.

Related Terms

  • synonym: direct object - a noun or pronoun that receives the action of a verb directly
  • synonym: indirect object - a noun or pronoun affected by the action of a verb, but not directly acted on
