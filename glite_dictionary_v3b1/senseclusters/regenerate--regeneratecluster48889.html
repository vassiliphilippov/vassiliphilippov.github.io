regenerate

verb
to reform morally or spiritually
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdʒɛnəˌreɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒɛnəreɪt / (BrE)
to cause someone to abandon an immoral way of life and adopt a better, more responsible one; to reform morally or spiritually

Examples

To regenerate a person
She believed that faith could regenerate even the most troubled individuals.
The community center works to regenerate the lives of those struggling with addiction.
The program aims to regenerate young offenders and help them reintegrate into society.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reform - to improve someone's behavior morally or spiritually
