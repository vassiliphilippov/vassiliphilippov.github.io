calamus

noun
reed or quill pen
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkæləməs / (AmE), / ˈkæləməs / (BrE)
a reed or quill, especially one used as a pen in ancient times

Examples

Ancient calamus pen
The ancient scribe wrote with a calamus on a sheet of papyrus.
Archaeologists discovered a well-preserved calamus in the ruins of the old library.
Before metal pens, people often used a calamus to record important documents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: quill - a writing pen made from a bird's feather
  • synonym: reed - a tall, slender-leaved plant of the grass family
