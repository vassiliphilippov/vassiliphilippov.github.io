Cynoglossum officinale

noun
a European and Asian biennial plant (hound's-tongue)
9.9
Pronunciation: / saɪnoʊˈɡlɒsəm ˌɒfɪˈsaɪnəli / (AmE), / sɪˌnɒɡlɒsəm ˌɒfɪˈsiːnəli / (BrE)
A biennial plant of Europe and western Asia with rough, tongue-shaped leaves and dark reddish-purple flowers, also known as hound's-tongue.

Examples

Cynoglossum officinale plant
Cynoglossum officinale is often found growing along roadsides and in disturbed soils across Europe.
Herb gardeners sometimes cultivate Cynoglossum officinale despite its reputation as a weed.
The leaves of Cynoglossum officinale are rough and shaped like a dog's tongue, inspiring its common name.
