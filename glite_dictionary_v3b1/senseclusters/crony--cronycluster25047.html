crony

noun
close companion or friend
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈkroʊni / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊni / (BrE)
a close friend, especially as part of a group (often with a negative or exclusive connotation)

Examples

Cronies meet often
He and his cronies meet every Friday for a game of cards.
Since college, she’s stayed in touch with a loyal group of cronies.
The mayor was often criticized for awarding contracts to his old cronies.
The politician was often seen dining with his trusted crony at the local restaurant.
Many believed the company only hired people who were cronies of the CEO.
She was accused of favoring her crony when making important decisions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ally - a person who supports or cooperates with another
  • synonym: companion - a person who spends time with another
  • antonym: enemy - a person who is actively opposed or hostile
  • synonym: friend - a person with whom one has a bond of mutual affection
