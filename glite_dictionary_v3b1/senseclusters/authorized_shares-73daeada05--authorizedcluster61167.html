authorized shares

noun
maximum shares allowed by a corporation's charter
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˈɔθəraɪzd ʃɛrz / (AmE), / ˈɔːθəraɪzd ʃeəz / (BrE)
The maximum number of shares a corporation is legally permitted to issue, as specified in its articles of incorporation.

Examples

The authorized shares limit
Investors should note that authorized shares differ from shares that have actually been issued.
The board proposed an amendment to the articles to raise the number of authorized shares.
The company's authorized shares were increased to accommodate future growth.
