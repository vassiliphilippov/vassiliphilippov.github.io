flabby

adjective
soft and loose, lacking firmness
Pronunciation: / ˈflæbi / (AmE), / ˈflæbi / (BrE)
(of a part of the body) soft, loose, and lacking firmness or muscle; physically weak or out of condition.

Examples

A flabby arm
After months without exercise, his arms became noticeably flabby.
His flabby handshake made a weak impression during the interview.
She was determined to tighten her flabby stomach muscles before summer.

Related Terms

  • antonym: firm - solid and not yielding when pressed; strong and taut
  • synonym: flaccid - soft and hanging loosely or limply
