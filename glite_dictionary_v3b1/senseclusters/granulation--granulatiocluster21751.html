granulation

noun
new tissue in wound healing
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˌɡræn.jəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɡran.jʊˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the growth of new tissue and tiny blood vessels on a wound as it heals

Examples

Wound granulation
Delayed granulation can indicate an underlying infection or poor circulation.
Healthy granulation is a good sign that the patient's wound is healing properly.
The surgeon noted the presence of well-formed granulation during the examination.
The surgeon observed healthy granulation in the wound, indicating good progress.
Proper cleaning of the injury helps promote granulation and faster recovery.
Red, bumpy granulation tissue often appears as a wound begins to heal.
