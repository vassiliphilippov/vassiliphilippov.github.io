dialogism

noun
use of multiple viewpoints or voices in a text
5.3
Pronunciation: / daɪˈɑl.əˌdʒɪz.əm / (AmE), / daɪˈæl.əˌdʒɪz.əm / (BrE)
the use or presence in a text of multiple voices, tones, or viewpoints whose interplay or contradiction is significant to the text's interpretation, especially as discussed in literary theory.

Examples

Theory of dialogism
Bakhtin’s theory of dialogism has influenced the study of modern fiction.
The novel's structure is notable for its dialogism, blending the perspectives of several narrators.
The poem’s dialogism allows conflicting voices to shape its meaning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: polyphony - the use of multiple distinct voices or perspectives in literature
