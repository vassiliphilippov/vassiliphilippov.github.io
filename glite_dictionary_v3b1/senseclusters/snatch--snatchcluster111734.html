snatch

noun
quick act of seizing or catching
2.7
Pronunciation: / snætʃ / (AmE), / snætʃ / (BrE)
an act of quickly seizing or catching something, especially with the hands

Examples

A quick snatch
His snatch at the keys was too late—they had already slipped through the grate.
The goalkeeper made a brilliant snatch to stop the ball from entering the net.
With a swift snatch, she grabbed the falling book before it hit the floor.
