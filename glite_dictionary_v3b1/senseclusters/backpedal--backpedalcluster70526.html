backpedal

verb
move or step backward (often in sports), or retract a stance
1.3 5.2
Pronunciation: /ˈbæk.pə.dəl/ /; / /ˈbæk.pe.dəl/ (AmE), /ˈbæk.pə.dəl/ /; / /ˈbæk.pe.dəl/ (BrE)
to move or step backward, or to reverse or weaken your opinion, position, or statement (often to avoid criticism or after realizing a mistake)

Examples

To backpedal on a statement
She tried to backpedal after realizing her comments had caused offense.
The boxer began to backpedal when his opponent pressed forward aggressively.
Under tough questioning, the politician tried to backpedal from his previous statements.
After facing public criticism, the senator began to backpedal on his earlier statements.
She quickly backpedaled when she realized her comments had offended her colleagues.
The company tried to backpedal after their controversial policy received backlash.
He quickly backpedaled when he realized his statement was unpopular.
The company CEO had to backpedal after the controversial announcement.
When faced with tough questions, she tends to backpedal rather than stand her ground.

  • antonym: dig in - to refuse to change one's opinion or stance
  • antonym: double down - to reinforce or continue a previous position or effort
  • synonym: retract - to withdraw a statement or accusation
  • synonym: retreat - to move back or withdraw, especially in a difficult situation
  • synonym: withdraw - to take back an opinion or statement
