pi-meson

noun
type of meson particle in nuclear physics
Pronunciation: / paɪˈmɛsɑn / (AmE), / paɪˈmiːsɒn / (BrE)
a type of meson, also called a pion, that plays a key role in holding atomic nuclei together; produced during high-energy particle collisions

Examples

A pi-meson particle
High-energy collisions in particle accelerators can produce a pi-meson.
The pi-meson plays a vital role in the nuclear forces that bind protons and neutrons together.
The decay of a pi-meson provides valuable information about fundamental particle interactions.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: kaon - another type of meson, heavier than a pion
  • synonym: pion - another name for pi-meson
