oospore

noun
thick-walled spore in some algae and fungi
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊ.əˌspɔr / (AmE), / ˈəʊ.əˌspɔː / (BrE)
A thick-walled sexual spore formed from a fertilized oosphere, occurring in certain algae and fungi.

Examples

A oospore in algae
Algae often reproduce sexually by generating oospores within specialized cells.
Researchers observed the formation of oospores under the microscope during the fungus's life cycle.
The fungus survives harsh conditions by producing oospores that can remain dormant for years.
