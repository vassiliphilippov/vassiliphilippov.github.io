prolate cycloid

noun
curve traced by a point outside a rolling circle
9.9
Pronunciation: / proʊˌleɪt ˈsaɪ.k.lɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈprəʊ.leɪt ˈsaɪ.klɔɪd / (BrE)
A type of cycloid generated by a point outside the circumference of a rolling circle.

Examples

The prolate cycloid curve
Mathematicians study the properties of the prolate cycloid to understand its unique geometric features.
The path traced by the tip of a spoke on a rolling bicycle wheel is a prolate cycloid.
When the tracing point is set outside the rolling circle's circumference, the resulting curve is a prolate cycloid.
