teardown

noun
ending a connection
6.9
Pronunciation: /ˈter.daʊn/ (AmE), /ˈteə.daʊn/ (BrE)
the process of ending a computer connection, shutting down a service, or releasing resources after a test or session

Examples

System teardown
After the test finished, the system performed a teardown to close connections and free memory.
The teardown process ensures that all resources are properly released after a session ends.
Engineers scheduled a teardown to safely disconnect the network services after the demonstration.

Related Terms

  • antonym: initialization - the process of starting up a system or service
  • synonym: shutdown - the process of turning off a computer or service
