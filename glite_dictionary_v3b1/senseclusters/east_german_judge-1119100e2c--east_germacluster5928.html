East German judge

noun
a notably harsh or unfair judge, especially in competitions
6.9
Pronunciation: / ist ˈdʒɝːmən dʒʌdʒ / (AmE), / iːst ˈdʒɜːmən dʒʌdʒ / (BrE)
a person who is notably harsh or unfair in judgment, especially in competitions or evaluations, often used humorously with reference to the stereotypically strict scoring by judges from East Germany in international events

Examples

The East German judge role
After my presentation, my boss played the role of the East German judge with his tough critiques.
The scores from the East German judge were noticeably lower than everyone else's.
Whenever someone is overly critical during family game night, we joke that they're the East German judge.

Related Terms

  • synonym: harsh judge - a judge known for severe or critical evaluations
  • antonym: lenient judge - a judge who tends to be tolerant or gentle in evaluations
