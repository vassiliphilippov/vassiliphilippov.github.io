backhaul

verb
to transport back
7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈbæk.hɑːl/ /; / /ˈbæk.hɔːl/ (AmE), /ˈbæk.hɔːl/ (BrE)
to transport goods or data from a remote location back to a central site or origin, often as a return trip

Examples

To backhaul goods
The company is backhauling empty containers to the main warehouse after deliveries.
Telecom providers are backhauling large amounts of data from rural towers to the city center.
After dropping off supplies, the truck will be backhauling recycled materials to the plant.
After dropping off the shipment, the truck was used to backhaul empty pallets to the warehouse.
Telecommunications companies often backhaul data from remote sites to their main network centers.
The company saves costs by arranging to backhaul supplies on return trips.
