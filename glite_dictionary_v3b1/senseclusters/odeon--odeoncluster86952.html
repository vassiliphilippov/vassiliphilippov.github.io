odeon

noun
ancient concert hall
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊdiˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈəʊdiːɒn / (BrE)
a theater or concert hall, especially one from ancient Greece or Rome, used for music and poetry performances

Examples

Ancient odeon hall
The ancient city still has a well-preserved odeon where musicians once played.
Tourists gathered at the odeon to imagine the performances that took place centuries ago.
Archaeologists discovered new mosaics beneath the old odeon during their excavation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amphitheater - an open circular or oval building with seats rising around a central open area
  • synonym: theater - a building where plays, shows, or concerts are performed
