electrovalent bond

noun
a bond formed by electron transfer between ions
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktroʊˈveɪlənt ˌbɑnd / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊˈveɪlənt ˌbɒnd / (BrE)
a chemical bond formed when one atom transfers an electron to another, resulting in the formation of positive and negative ions held together by electrostatic attraction

Examples

An electrovalent bond forms
An electrovalent bond forms when sodium donates an electron to chlorine, creating sodium chloride.
Table salt is an example of a compound held together by an electrovalent bond.
The stability of the crystal structure is due to the strong electrovalent bonds between the ions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ionic bond - a chemical bond formed between ions of opposite charge
