wax begonia

noun
a hybrid ornamental begonia plant
Pronunciation: / wæks bɪˈgoʊniə / (AmE), / waks bɪˈgəʊniə / (BrE)
A hybrid begonia plant with waxy leaves and small clusters of white, pink, or red flowers, commonly grown as a bedding plant.

Examples

The wax begonia plant
Many landscapers use the wax begonia for its durability and continuous blooms.
She planted a border of wax begonias along the walkway, adding vibrant color all summer.
The wax begonia thrives in both sun and shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.
