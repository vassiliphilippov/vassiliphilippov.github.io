degust

verb
taste and enjoy carefully
Pronunciation: / dɪˈɡʌst / (AmE), / dɪˈɡʌst / (BrE)
To taste and enjoy something carefully and appreciatively.

Examples

To degust fine food
At the tasting event, participants were encouraged to degust every course with attention to detail.
He invited his guests to degust each cheese slowly, appreciating the different flavors.
The food critic took a moment to degust the fine wine before writing her review.

Related Terms

  • antonym: devour - to eat hungrily or quickly
  • synonym: savor - to taste and enjoy something fully
