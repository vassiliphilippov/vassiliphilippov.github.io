umbrella arum

noun
tropical Asian plant with showy spathe and edible corms
9.9
Pronunciation: / ʌmˈbɹɛl.ə ˈɛɹ.əm / (AmE), / ʌmˈbɹɛl.ə ˈɑː.rəm / (BrE)
A foul-smelling, somewhat fleshy tropical plant of southeastern Asia, cultivated for its edible corms, its large leaves, and its showy dark red spathe that surrounds a large spadix.

Examples

Large umbrella arum leaves
Gardeners appreciate the umbrella arum for its large, decorative leaves.
Some Southeast Asian cuisines feature the corms of the umbrella arum as a delicacy.
The umbrella arum is often grown in greenhouses for its striking, dark red spathe.
