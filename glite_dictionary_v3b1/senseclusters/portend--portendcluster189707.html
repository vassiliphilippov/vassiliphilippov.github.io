portend

verb
be a sign or warning of future event
8.4 1.30/m
Pronunciation: / p…îrÀàt…õnd / (AmE), / p…îÀêÀàt…õnd / (BrE)
to be a sign or warning that something, especially something momentous or calamitous, is likely to happen

Examples

Dark clouds portend storm
Dark clouds portend an approaching storm.
Some believe that a comet sighting can portend disaster.
The rise in unemployment may portend further economic troubles.

Synonyms

  • foreshadow
    6.2 2.02/m
    foreshadow gives a clear warning of future events in stories or speech, while portend sounds more formal and ominous.
  • forebode
    8.4 0.01/m
    forebode often predicts something bad or worrying in a feeling or sign, while portend signals a major or calamitous future event.
  • presage
    8.8 0.83/m
    presage acts as a literary or old-fashioned sign of what will come, while portend is used for strong, ominous warnings.
  • augur
    8.6 0.45/m
    augur suggests future outcomes often from signs or events and is somewhat formal, while portend stresses a warning of something important.
  • bode
    7.2 0.84/m
    bode usually appears in phrases like 'bode ill' to show a result, while portend more directly warns of a coming event.
  • betoken
    9.3 0.14/m
    betoken is a formal word that indicates or shows something, while portend implies a stronger ominous warning about future events.
  • prefigure
    8.0 0.38/m
    prefigure often means to show the form or idea of something before it happens, while portend warns of an event or danger.
  • signal
    3.0 13/m
    signal can simply point to a possible change or fact, while portend carries a weighty, often ominous warning about the future.
  • auspicate
    9.9 0.00/m
    auspicate is a rare, technical term for predicting by omens, while portend is the common literary verb that warns of future events.
