Lilium columbianum

noun
a lily species of western North America
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪliəm ˌkoʊləmˈbiːənəm / (AmE), / ˈlɪlɪəm ˌkɒləmˈbiːənəm / (BrE)
A species of lily native to western North America, having showy orange-red flowers with purple spots.

Examples

Lilium columbianum orange-red flowers
Lilium columbianum is admired for its vibrant orange-red blooms in many gardens across the Pacific Northwest.
Botanists observed that Lilium columbianum prefers moist but well-drained forest habitats.
I spotted a cluster of Lilium columbianum growing along the hiking trail last summer.
