tucker-bag

noun
Australian food-carrying bag
Pronunciation: / ˈtʌk.ɚ.bæɡ / (AmE), / ˈtʌk.ə.bæɡ / (BrE)
An Australian term for a bag used to carry food, especially by travellers or workers in the bush.

Examples

An Australian tucker-bag
In traditional Australian folk songs, the tucker-bag is often mentioned as an essential companion.
She packed sandwiches and fruit into her tucker-bag for the long day ahead.
The old swagman carried his tucker-bag over his shoulder as he walked through the outback.

Related Terms

  • synonym: swag - a traveller's bundle or pack, especially in Australia
