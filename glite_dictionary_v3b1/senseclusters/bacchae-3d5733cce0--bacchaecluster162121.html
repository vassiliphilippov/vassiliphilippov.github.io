Bacchae

proper noun
followers of Bacchus
Pronunciation: / ˈbækiˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˈbækiː / (BrE)
the female followers of Bacchus (Dionysus) in Greek mythology, known for their ecstatic and frenzied worship

Examples

The Bacchae in myth
The Bacchae are central figures in the ancient Greek play by Euripides.
Artists often depict the Bacchae dancing wildly in celebration of their god.
Legends tell of the Bacchae roaming the mountains in ecstatic rituals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: maenads - female followers of Dionysus in Greek mythology
