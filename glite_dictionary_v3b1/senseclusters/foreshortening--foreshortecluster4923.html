foreshortening

noun
art depth technique
Pronunciation: / ˌfɔrˈʃɔrtənɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌfɔːˈʃɔːtənɪŋ / (BrE)
a technique or visual effect in art where an object appears shorter than it really is because of the angle or perspective, creating the illusion of depth

Examples

Artistic foreshortening
The painter used foreshortening to make the arm appear to reach out toward the viewer.
Learning foreshortening helps artists create more realistic and dynamic figures in their drawings.
In the mural, the clever use of foreshortening gives the illusion that the objects are coming out of the wall.
The artist used foreshortening to make the figure's arm appear to reach out of the painting.
In anatomy classes, students learn how foreshortening affects the way bones look in X-rays.
Photographers often use foreshortening to create dramatic effects in their images.

Related Terms

  • synonym: distortion - a change in the appearance or shape of something
  • synonym: perspective - the art technique of representing three-dimensional objects on a two-dimensional surface
