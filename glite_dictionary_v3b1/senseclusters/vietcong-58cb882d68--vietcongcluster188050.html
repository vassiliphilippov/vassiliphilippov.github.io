Vietcong

proper noun
Vietnam War guerrilla
8.3
Pronunciation: /ˈviː.et.kɔːŋ/ /; / /ˈviː.et.kɑːŋ/ (AmE), /ˈviː.et.kɒŋ/ (BrE)
a member of the communist guerrilla force, or the force itself, that fought against South Vietnam and the United States during the Vietnam War

Examples

A Vietcong fighter
The Vietcong played a major role in the outcome of the Vietnam War.
Many villages were caught between the Vietcong and government forces.
The tactics used by the Vietcong were difficult for the US military to counter.
The Vietcong used underground tunnels to move supplies and troops during the conflict.
Many villages in South Vietnam were influenced by the Vietcong during the war.
The tactics of the Vietcong made it difficult for opposing forces to predict their movements.

Related Terms

  • synonym: National Liberation Front - the official name of the Vietcong organization
  • synonym: North Vietnamese Army - the regular army of North Vietnam during the Vietnam War
  • antonym: South Vietnamese - people or forces supporting South Vietnam during the Vietnam War
