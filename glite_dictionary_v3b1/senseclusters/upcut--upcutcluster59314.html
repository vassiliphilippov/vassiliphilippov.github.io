upcut

noun
upward-directed cut
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpˌkʌt / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌkʌt / (BrE)
a cut made in an upward direction, especially in machining or editing processes

Examples

An upcut groove
The machinist used an upcut to remove material from the workpiece.
An upcut is often preferred when you want to clear chips away from the cutting area.
In video editing, an upcut can refer to trimming footage from the beginning of a scene.

Related Terms

  • antonym: downcut - a cut made in a downward direction
