Guinea worm disease

noun
tropical parasitic infection caused by Guinea worm
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɪni wɜrm dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈɡɪni wɜːm dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A parasitic infection caused by drinking water contaminated with larvae of the Guinea worm, leading to painful blisters as the mature worm emerges from the skin.

Examples

Guinea worm disease infection
Contaminated drinking water is the main cause of Guinea worm disease outbreaks in rural regions.
Eradication efforts have successfully reduced Guinea worm disease to only a handful of countries.
People who contract Guinea worm disease often suffer weeks of incapacitating pain.
