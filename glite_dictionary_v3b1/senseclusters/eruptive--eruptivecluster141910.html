eruptive

adjective
formed by fire or intense heat (geology)
5.1 6.3
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈrʌp.tɪv/ /; / /iː.ˈrʌp.tɪv/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈrʌp.tɪv/ (BrE)
(of rocks or geological formations) formed by the action of fire or intense heat, especially during volcanic activity.

Examples

Eruptive rock

Some minerals are only found in eruptive environments where intense heat alters their structure.
The island was composed mainly of eruptive rocks, evidence of its volcanic origins.
