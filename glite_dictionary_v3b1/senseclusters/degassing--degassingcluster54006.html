degassing

noun
release of gas
6.4
Pronunciation: / diˈɡæsɪŋ / (AmE), / diˈɡasɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of gas being released or removed from a material, such as a liquid, solid, or geological formation

Examples

The degassing of magma
The degassing of magma can lead to explosive volcanic eruptions.
Proper degassing is important in coffee production to ensure the best flavor.
Engineers monitor degassing in chemical tanks to prevent dangerous pressure buildup.
The degassing of water is essential before using it in sensitive laboratory experiments.
Engineers performed degassing to ensure the metal casting was free of air bubbles.
Proper degassing improves the quality of beverages by removing unwanted dissolved gases.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aeration - the process of introducing air into a substance
  • synonym: outgassing - the release of gas, especially from rocks or materials
  • synonym: venting - the act of allowing gas or air to escape
