Buddha

noun
enlightened person in Buddhism; the historical Siddhartha Gautama
4.2 0.18/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàbuÀêd…ô / (AmE), / Ààb äd…ô / (BrE)
a person who has achieved a state of perfect enlightenment, especially Siddhartha Gautama, the founder of Buddhism

Examples

An enlightened Buddha
In some traditions, anyone who achieves enlightenment can be called a Buddha.
Many people meditate in hopes of reaching the understanding attained by the Buddha.
The teachings of the Buddha have inspired millions around the world.

Synonyms

  • Gautama Buddha
    4.4 0.00/m
    Gautama Buddha names the historical founder and teacher, while Buddha is the title for anyone who reaches full enlightenment.
  • Siddhartha
    8.6 0.34/m
    Siddhartha is the personal name used in stories, while Buddha is the title that emphasizes his enlightened role.
  • Gautama
    8.5 0.10/m
    Gautama is a family or clan name for that teacher, while Buddha highlights his state of full spiritual awakening.
  • Gautama Siddhartha
    9.4 0.00/m
    Gautama Siddhartha is a less common ordering of his full name, while Buddha is the respectful title used for the enlightened person.
  • Shaka
    9.7 0.00/m
    Shaka is the East Asian name used in Japan and nearby regions, while Buddha is the general title in English.
