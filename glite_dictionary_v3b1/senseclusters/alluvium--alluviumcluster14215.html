alluvium

noun
sediment deposited by water
8.9 0.1/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàluÀêvi…ôm / (AmE), / …ôÀàljuÀêv…™…ôm / (BrE)
Loose clay, silt, sand, or gravel that has been transported by running water and deposited, especially in a riverbed, floodplain, or delta.

Examples

River alluvium layer
After the flooding, a thick layer of alluvium covered the crops in the valley.
Geologists collected samples of alluvium to study changes in the stream's flow over time.
The fertile soils of the Nile Delta are composed largely of alluvium deposited by the river.

Synonyms

  • alluvial sediment
    6.0 0.00/m
    alluvial sediment is loose sand, silt, and clay carried and laid down by rivers, while alluvium names the same deposited material.
  • alluvial deposit
    5.6 0.03/m
    alluvial deposit is the material left when flowing water slows and drops its load, while alluvium is the standard geological term.
  • alluvion
    9.9 0.00/m
    alluvion is soil or sand carried by water and left on river banks or plains, while alluvium is the more common modern term.
