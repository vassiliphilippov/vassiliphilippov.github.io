C1

noun
CEFR advanced language proficiency level
4.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / si w…ôn / (AmE), / siÀê w…ôn / (BrE)
A level on the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR) indicating advanced proficiency in a language, able to use language flexibly and effectively for social, academic, and professional purposes.

Examples

C1 language level
She achieved a C1 level in English after studying abroad.
To teach advanced students, the course requires applicants to demonstrate C1 proficiency.
Employers often list C1 as a preferred level for roles needing strong written and spoken English.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: B2 - a CEFR level indicating upper-intermediate proficiency; below C1 in the CEFR scale
  • degree higher: C2 - the highest CEFR level indicating near-native or mastery proficiency; above C1
