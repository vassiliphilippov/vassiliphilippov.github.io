absolute majority

noun
more than half the votes
3.0 3.2
Pronunciation: /ˈæb.sə.luːt mə.ˈdʒɔː.rɪ.tiː/ /; / /ˈæb.sə.lət mə.ˈdʒɔː.rɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /ˈæb.sə.luːt mə.ˈdʒɒ.rɪ.tiː/ /; / /ˈæb.səl.juːt mə.ˈdʒɒ.rɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
More than half of all the votes cast in an election or decision-making process.

Examples

An absolute majority of votes
A party needs an absolute majority in parliament to form a government without coalition partners.
The candidate won an absolute majority in the first round of voting, securing over 50% of the ballots.
When no side achieves an absolute majority, a second round of elections may be required.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: plurality - the largest number of votes, not necessarily more than half
  • degree lower: simple majority - more votes than any other candidate or option, but not necessarily more than half
