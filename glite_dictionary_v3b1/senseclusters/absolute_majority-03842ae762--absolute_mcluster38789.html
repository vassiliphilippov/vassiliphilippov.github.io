More than half of all the votes cast in an election or decision-making process.

Examples

An absolute majority of votes
A party needs an absolute majority in parliament to form a government without coalition partners.
The candidate won an absolute majority in the first round of voting, securing over 50% of the ballots.
When no side achieves an absolute majority, a second round of elections may be required.
