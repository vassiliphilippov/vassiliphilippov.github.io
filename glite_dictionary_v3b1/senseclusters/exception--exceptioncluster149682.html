exception

noun
person or thing not following a rule
3.8
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈsɛpʃən /; / ɪkˈsɛp.ʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɛpʃən /; / ɪkˈsɛp.ʃən / (BrE)
a person or thing that does not follow a rule or is not included; the act of leaving someone or something out

Examples

An exception to the rule
Every rule has its exception, making it wise to stay flexible.
He was the rare exception in a family of doctors, choosing to become an artist instead.
Usually, students must submit homework on time, but Sarah was granted an exception because she was ill.
No one is allowed in this area, with the exception of authorized personnel.
She eats fruit every day, with the exception of Mondays.
With the exception of Mark, everyone arrived on time to the meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anomaly - something that deviates from what is standard, normal, or expected
  • synonym: exclusion - the act of deliberately not including something
  • antonym: inclusion - the act of including something or someone
  • antonym: rule - a prescribed guide for conduct or action
