secretin

noun
intestinal hormone affecting digestive secretions
7.1
Pronunciation: / sɪˈkritn̩ / (AmE), / sɪˈkriːtɪn / (BrE)
a hormone produced in the small intestine that stimulates the secretion of water and bicarbonate from the pancreas and bile ducts, especially in response to excess stomach acid

Examples

Secretin hormone levels
Researchers measured secretin levels in patients with digestive disorders.
The release of secretin helps neutralize stomach acid entering the small intestine.
When the stomach contents are very acidic, secretin triggers the pancreas to produce bicarbonate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gastrin - a hormone that stimulates secretion of gastric acid
  • synonym: hormone - a signaling molecule produced in the body that regulates physiology and behavior
