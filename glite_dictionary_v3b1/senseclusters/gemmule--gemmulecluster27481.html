gemmule

noun
Darwin's hypothetical hereditary particle
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛm.juːl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛm.juːl / (BrE)
A hypothetical microscopic particle proposed by Darwin as the agent responsible for heredity in his theory of pangenesis.

Examples

Darwin's gemmule theory
Although later disproved, the idea of the gemmule was influential in early genetics.
Darwin theorized that gemmules gathered from all parts of the body and transmitted heredity.
Some historians of science discuss gemmules when examining the origins of heredity theories.
