make a meal of

verb
exaggerate complexity or importance
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˌmeɪk ə ˈmiːl əv / (AmE), / ˌmeɪk ə ˈmiːl əv / (BrE)
to treat something as more complicated, difficult, or important than it really is; to exaggerate the significance of something

Examples

To make a meal of a problem
Don't make a meal of this small problem; just fix it and move on.
Instead of simply apologizing, he made a meal of his explanation and went on for ages.
She really made a meal of completing the application form—it should have taken five minutes, not an hour.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overdo - to do something to an excessive degree; to exaggerate
  • antonym: understate - to present something as being smaller or less important than it actually is
