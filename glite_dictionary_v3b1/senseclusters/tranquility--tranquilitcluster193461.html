tranquility

noun
calm and peacefulness
Pronunciation: / træŋˈkwɪləti /; / træŋˈkwɪl.ə.ti /; / træŋˈkwɪl.ə.t̬i / (AmE), / træŋˈkwɪlɪti /; / træŋˈkwɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
a state of calm and peacefulness, free from disturbance or stress

Examples

The tranquility of nature
After a busy day at work, she enjoyed the tranquility of her garden in the evening.
He meditated every morning to find a sense of inner tranquility before starting his day.
The tranquility of the mountain lake was breathtaking, with only the sound of birds to be heard.
He sought tranquility in the mountains, far from the city's chaos.
Meditation helps many people achieve inner tranquility despite busy lives.
The gentle sound of the waves restored her sense of tranquility.
After the storm passed, a sense of tranquility settled over the lake.
She values tranquility above all else when choosing a place to live.
Yoga and meditation help him achieve inner tranquility despite life's pressures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agitation - a state of nervousness or restlessness
  • synonym: calmness - the quality of being calm
  • synonym: serenity - the state of being calm, peaceful, and untroubled
  • antonym: turmoil - a state of great disturbance, confusion, or uncertainty
