junction transistor

noun
semiconductor device with two p-n junctions
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʌŋkʃən trænˈzɪstər / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌŋkʃən trænˈzɪstə / (BrE)
A semiconductor device with two p-n junctions, used to amplify or switch electronic signals.

Examples

A junction transistor device
A junction transistor has three layers of semiconductor material joined together.
Engineers replaced vacuum tubes with junction transistors to improve reliability.
The junction transistor revolutionized electronics by making portable radios possible.
