sash line

noun
cord connecting sash weight to window sash
7.1 7.1
Pronunciation: /sæʃ laɪn/ (AmE), /sæʃ laɪn/ (BrE)
a strong cord used to connect a sash weight to a sliding sash in a sash window, allowing the sash to move up and down

Examples

A sash line cord
Old houses often need their sash lines replaced due to wear and tear over time.
She called a handyman to repair the sash line in her Victorian window.
The sash line broke, making it impossible to open the window smoothly.
