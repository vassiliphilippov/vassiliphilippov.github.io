pom

noun
(slang) British person (disparaging)
8.7
Pronunciation: / pɑm /; / ˈpɑmi / (AmE), / pɒm /; / ˈpɒmi / (BrE)
(informal, slang; sometimes offensive or disparaging) A British person, especially as referred to by Australians or New Zealanders.

Examples

A pom in Australia
He didn't mind being called a pom after moving to Sydney.
Some Australians refer to British tourists as poms during cricket matches.
The word pom can sometimes be used affectionately, but is often considered offensive.
He didn’t mind the nickname pommy and often joked about his British roots.
Some Australians use the term pommy to refer to people from England, sometimes in jest.
They always called the new teacher a pommy, even though she had been living in Australia for years.
