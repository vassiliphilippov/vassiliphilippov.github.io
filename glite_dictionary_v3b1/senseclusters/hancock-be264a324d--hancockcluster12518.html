Hancock

proper noun
president of the Continental Congress; American revolutionary leader
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈhæn.kɑk / (AmE), / ˈhan.kɒk / (BrE)
an American revolutionary patriot (1737–1793), president of the Continental Congress and the first signer of the Declaration of Independence.

Examples

John Hancock signature
John Hancock was famous for his bold signature on the Declaration of Independence.
Many people refer to a person's signature as their 'Hancock' in American English.
The legacy of Hancock is celebrated in U.S. history classes across the country.
