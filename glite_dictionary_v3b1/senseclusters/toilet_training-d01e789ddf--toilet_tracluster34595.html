toilet training

noun
teaching a child to use the toilet
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈtɔɪlɪt ˌtreɪnɪŋ /; / ˈtɔɪlət ˌtreɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɔɪlɪt ˌtreɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of teaching a young child or pet to use the toilet instead of diapers or a designated spot

Examples

Toilet training process
Consistency and patience are important during the toilet training process.
Most parents begin toilet training when their child is between 18 months and three years old.
Some children pick up toilet training quickly, while others may take a little longer.
Toilet training can be a significant milestone in a child's development.
Many parents find that patience is essential during toilet training.
Puppies often require consistent routines for successful toilet training.
