bathyscaphe

noun
deep-diving underwater vessel
9.9 9.4
Pronunciation: / ˈbæθiˌskæf / (AmE), / ˈbæθɪˌskɑːf / (BrE)
a navigable deep-sea vessel designed for underwater exploration at great depths

Examples

A bathyscaphe vessel
A bathyscaphe allows scientists to explore parts of the ocean previously unreachable by traditional submarines.
Engineers worked for years to design a reliable bathyscaphe capable of withstanding immense ocean pressure.
The Trieste was a famous bathyscaphe that reached the Challenger Deep in 1960.
