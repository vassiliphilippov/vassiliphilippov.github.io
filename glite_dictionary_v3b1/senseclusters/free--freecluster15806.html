free

verb
release gas or energy (scientific use)
Pronunciation: / fri / (AmE), / friː / (BrE)
(of a chemical or physical process) to release gas or energy as a result of a reaction or decomposition.

Examples

To free energy
Combustion of the fuel will free large amounts of energy.
Heat can free gases that are trapped in certain minerals.
When the bonds break, the molecules free oxygen into the air.

Related Terms

  • synonym: release - to set free or allow to escape; often used for energy or gases
