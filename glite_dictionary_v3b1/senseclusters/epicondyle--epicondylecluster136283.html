epicondyle

noun
bony projection above a condyle
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˈkɑnˌdaɪl / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈkɒndaɪl / (BrE)
a projection on a bone above a condyle that serves as a point for the attachment of muscles and ligaments

Examples

The epicondyle bone
An injury to the lateral epicondyle often results from repetitive strain in tennis players.
The doctor explained that the pain was coming from inflammation near the epicondyle of the elbow.
The surgeon identified the fractured epicondyle before beginning the repair.
