ganglion cell

noun
nerve cell outside central nervous system
5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈgæŋ.ɡlɪ.ən sel/ (AmE), /ˈgæŋ.ɡlɪ.ən sel/ (BrE)
a type of nerve cell whose cell body is located outside the central nervous system, often forming part of the peripheral nervous system or the retina.

Examples

Ganglion cells in retina
Damage to ganglion cells can impair vision if it affects the retina.
Each ganglion cell processes information before sending it to the brain.
Scientists are studying how ganglion cell degeneration contributes to certain neurological disorders.

Related Terms

  • antonym: glial cell - a type of cell in the nervous system that is not a neuron
  • synonym: neuron - a nerve cell; the basic unit of the nervous system
