syncrisis

noun
rhetorical comparison
Pronunciation: / sɪnˈkraɪsɪs / (AmE), / sɪnˈkraɪsɪs / (BrE)
a rhetorical device in which two things or ideas are compared and contrasted to highlight their differences

Examples

Syncrisis in writing
The author used syncrisis to emphasize the contrast between the hero and the villain.
In her essay, she employed syncrisis to compare the two political systems.
A well-crafted syncrisis can make an argument more persuasive by highlighting key differences.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antithesis - a rhetorical device that presents contrasting ideas in parallel structures
  • synonym: comparison - the act of examining similarities and differences
