one-upmanship

noun
practice of outdoing others
Pronunciation: / ˌwʌnˈʌp.mənˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˌwʌnˈʌp.mən.ʃɪp / (BrE)
the practice of trying to outdo or surpass someone, especially in a competitive or boastful way

Examples

A game of one-upmanship
Dinner with them always turns into a game of one-upmanship about travel and achievements.
His constant one-upmanship at work made him unpopular with colleagues.
I grew tired of the petty one-upmanship between the two siblings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: competition - rivalry between individuals or groups
  • synonym: rivalry - competition for the same objective or superiority
