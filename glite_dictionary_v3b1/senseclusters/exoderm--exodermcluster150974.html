exoderm

noun
outer embryonic germ layer

the outer germ layer in an embryo that develops into the skin and nervous system

Examples

The exoderm layer
Studying the exoderm is essential to understanding early stages of human development.
The exoderm gives rise to both the epidermis and the nervous system in vertebrate embryos.
The cells of the exoderm begin to differentiate soon after fertilization.

Synonyms

  • ectoderm
    9.9 0.07/m
    ectoderm is the modern, commonly used name for the embryo's outer cell layer that forms skin and the nervous system, while exoderm is an older term.
  • ectoblast
    9.9 0.00/m
    ectoblast is a term for the embryo's outer cells that give rise to skin and nerves, while exoderm names the whole outer germ layer.
