polyvalent

adjective
having more than one valence (chemistry)
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːliˈveɪlənt / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈveɪlənt / (BrE)
(of a chemical element or ion) having more than one valence, or a valence of three or higher.

Examples

Polyvalent ion
Copper is considered a polyvalent element because it can have more than one valence state.
In the lab, a polyvalent ion can participate in several different types of chemical bonds.
The vaccine is polyvalent, protecting against multiple strains of the virus.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bivalent - having a valence of two
  • antonym: monovalent - having a valence of one
