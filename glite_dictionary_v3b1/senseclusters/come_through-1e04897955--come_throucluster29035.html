come through

verb
survive an adversity
Pronunciation: / kʌm θru / (AmE), / kʌm θruː / (BrE)
to survive, succeed, or obtain something after difficulty or adversity

Examples

Despite the serious accident, he managed to come through with only minor injuries.
She came through her illness stronger than ever.
The company had to lay off workers, but it eventually came through the crisis.
After months of hard work, she finally came through and graduated at the top of her class.
Even though the team faced many obstacles, they always come through in the end.
We were counting on you to organize the event, and you really came through for us.
After weeks of uncertainty, our funding finally came through just in time.
Despite the difficult circumstances, she managed to come through with excellent results.
Many people doubted him, but he always seems to come through when it matters most.
