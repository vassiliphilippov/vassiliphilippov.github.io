postfire

noun
period after a fire
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊstˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈpəʊstˌfaɪə / (BrE)
the period or state after a fire, especially when recovery or new plant growth happens

Examples

Postfire period
The postfire landscape was carefully studied to understand how the ecosystem would recover.
Emergency teams arrived during the postfire phase to assess the damage and begin cleanup.
Researchers collected data throughout the postfire months to monitor changes in soil quality.
The forest showed rapid postfire recovery, with new shoots emerging just weeks after the blaze.
Scientists studied the types of postfire plants that colonized the burned grassland.
Within a year, postfire vegetation had transformed the blackened landscape into a patchwork of green.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aftermath - the period following a significant event, especially one with negative effects
  • antonym: pre-fire - the period before a fire occurs
  • synonym: regrowth - new growth of plants after damage or loss
  • synonym: succession - the process of change in plant species in an area over time
