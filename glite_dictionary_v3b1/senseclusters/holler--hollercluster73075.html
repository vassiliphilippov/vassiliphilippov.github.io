holler

noun
small valley between mountains (US regional)
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑlər / (AmE), / ˈhɒlə / (BrE)
(US, regional) A small, narrow valley between mountains, especially in Appalachia; a hollow.

Examples

Mountain holler
After a long hike, they camped in a cool holler by a clear stream.
He grew up in a remote holler deep in the Appalachian foothills.
The family settled in a quiet holler surrounded by dense forest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hollow - a small valley or depression in the land
