character

noun
a person's moral and ethical qualities
1.0 1.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkær.ək.tər/ /; / /ˈkær.ɪk.tər/ (AmE), /ˈkær.ək.tə/ /; / /ˈkær.ɪk.tə/ (BrE)
the combination of moral qualities, such as integrity and honesty, that form a person's reputation

Examples

Strong character
Even under pressure, his character remained unshaken and admirable.
Her honesty and kindness are essential parts of her character.
The school aims to develop both the intellect and character of its students.
Everyone trusts her because she is a woman of strong character.

When faced with difficult decisions, his character shone through.

  • synonym: honor - high respect or esteem; a sense of principled uprightness
  • degree higher: integrity - the quality of being honest and having strong moral principles
  • synonym: personality - the combination of characteristics or qualities that form an individual's distinctive character
  • degree lower: reputation - the beliefs or opinions generally held about someone
