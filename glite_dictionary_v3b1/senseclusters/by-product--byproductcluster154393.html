by-product

noun
a secondary product of a process
6.1 2.8
Pronunciation: /baɪ ˈprɑː.dʌkt/ /; / /baɪ ˈprɑː.dəkt/ (AmE), /baɪ ˈprɒd.ʌkt/ /; / /baɪ ˈprɒd.əkt/ (BrE)
A substance or item produced incidentally during the manufacture or synthesis of another main product.

Examples

Industrial by-product
Economic growth often has pollution as an unintended by-product.
Glycerin is a by-product of soap manufacturing.
Steel production creates slag as a by-product.

Related Terms

  • antonym: main product - the primary product intended by a process
  • degree lower: waste - material no longer of use from a process; discarded material
