ground sloth

noun
extinct giant terrestrial sloth-like mammal
Pronunciation: / ɡraʊnd sloʊθ / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd slɒθ / (BrE)
a large extinct terrestrial sloth-like mammal that lived in the Americas during the Pliocene and Pleistocene epochs

Examples

The ground sloth fossil
Many paleontologists specialize in studying extinct creatures such as the ground sloth.
Scientists have discovered fossils of the ground sloth in several parts of South America.
The ground sloth was one of the largest mammals of its time, some reaching the size of elephants.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sloth - a slow-moving arboreal mammal of Central and South America
