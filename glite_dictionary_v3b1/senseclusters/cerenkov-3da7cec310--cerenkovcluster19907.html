Cerenkov

adjective
relating to Cerenkov radiation
9.9
Pronunciation: /tʃə.ˈren.kəv/ /; / /tʃə.ˈren.kəf/ (AmE), /tʃə.ˈren.kɒf/ /; / /tʃə.ˈren.kɒv/ (BrE)
relating to or produced by Cerenkov radiation, the light emitted when a charged particle moves faster than light in a medium

Examples

Cerenkov radiation
The Cerenkov effect is often observed in nuclear reactors submerged in water.
Scientists use Cerenkov detectors to identify high-speed charged particles in experiments.
A faint blue glow, known as Cerenkov radiation, can be seen in certain laboratory conditions.
