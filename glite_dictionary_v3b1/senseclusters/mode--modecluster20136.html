mode

noun
classification of propositions by necessity, possibility, or impossibility
Pronunciation: / moʊd / (AmE), / məʊd / (BrE)
(Logic) A category used to classify propositions according to whether they express necessity, possibility, or impossibility.

Examples

Possible mode in logic
In modal logic, each mode determines whether a proposition is necessary, possible, or impossible.
Philosophers often debate which mode best describes a hypothetical scenario.
Understanding the mode of a statement helps clarify its logical status.
