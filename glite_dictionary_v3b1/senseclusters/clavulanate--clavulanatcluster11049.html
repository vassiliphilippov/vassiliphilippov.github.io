clavulanate

noun
antibiotic resistance inhibitor
8.9
Pronunciation: / kləˈvjuːləˌneɪt / (AmE), / kləˈvjuːləneɪt / (BrE)
a chemical compound used to block bacterial resistance to certain antibiotics, often combined with penicillins

Examples

Clavulanate antibiotic combination
The doctor prescribed an antibiotic containing clavulanate to treat the infection.
Some bacteria are resistant to penicillin unless clavulanate is added to the medication.
Pharmacists often combine amoxicillin with clavulanate to make the treatment more effective.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antibiotic - a medicine that kills or stops the growth of bacteria
  • synonym: clavulanic acid - a compound that inhibits beta-lactamase enzymes in bacteria
