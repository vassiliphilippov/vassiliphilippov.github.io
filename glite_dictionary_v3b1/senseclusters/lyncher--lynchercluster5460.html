lyncher

noun
person in a mob killing
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪntʃər / (AmE), / ˈlɪntʃə / (BrE)
a person who takes part in or organizes the illegal killing or punishment of someone by a mob, usually without a legal trial

Examples

Mob lyncher
The police identified the main lyncher from the video footage of the attack.
After the incident, the community condemned the actions of every lyncher involved.
Laws were strengthened to ensure that a lyncher would face serious legal consequences.
The angry crowd included at least one known lyncher who had organized previous attacks.
Historians studied the motives of each lyncher involved in the tragic event.
Calling someone a lyncher is a serious accusation that implies involvement in violent, illegal acts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: defender - a person who protects or supports someone
  • synonym: mobster - a member of a group involved in organized crime or violence
  • antonym: victim - a person harmed or killed as a result of a crime or accident
  • synonym: vigilante - a person who takes the law into their own hands
