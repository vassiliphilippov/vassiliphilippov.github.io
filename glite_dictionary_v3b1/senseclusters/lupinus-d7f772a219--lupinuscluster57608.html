Lupinus

proper noun
genus of flowering plants (lupin)
Pronunciation: / luˈpaɪnəs / (AmE), / luˈpiːnəs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants in the pea family, commonly known as lupins, which includes herbaceous plants and shrubs with attractive, often colorful, flowers.

Examples

Lupinus genus
Botanists have identified several new species within the Lupinus genus.
Many gardens feature Lupinus varieties for their tall spikes of colorful blooms.
The seeds of some Lupinus species are used as a food source in Mediterranean countries.
