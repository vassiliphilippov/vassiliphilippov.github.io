underinflated

adjective
not enough air
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərɪnˈfleɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌndərɪnˈfleɪtɪd / (BrE)
not filled with enough air or gas to reach the proper or recommended level

Examples

Underinflated tires
The mechanic warned that underinflated tires could cause uneven wear and reduce fuel efficiency.
Always check that your bicycle tires are not underinflated before going for a long ride.
An underinflated football is harder to throw accurately during a game.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deflated - having lost air or gas and become flat
  • antonym: inflated - filled with air or gas to the proper level
  • antonym: overinflated - filled with too much air or gas
