accommodating IOL

noun
hinged lens implant for near and far vision
5.8
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈkɑː.mə.deɪ.tɪŋ aɪ oʊ el/ (AmE), /ə.ˈkɒm.ə.deɪ.tɪŋ aɪ əʊ el/ (BrE)
a type of intraocular lens implant designed with a hinge mechanism to allow the eye to focus on both near and far objects, mimicking the natural lens's ability.

Examples

An accommodating IOL implant
After her cataract surgery, the doctor implanted an accommodating IOL to help restore her range of vision.
Patients with an accommodating IOL often report improved ability to read and see distant objects.
The development of the accommodating IOL represents a major advancement in eye care technology.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monofocal IOL - an intraocular lens with a single fixed focus
