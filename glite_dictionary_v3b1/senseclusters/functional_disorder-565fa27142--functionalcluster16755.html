functional disorder

noun
medical disorder with no clear physical cause
Pronunciation: / ˈfʌŋkʃənl dɪsˈɔrdɚ / (AmE), / ˈfʌŋkʃənl dɪsˈɔːdə / (BrE)
a medical condition showing symptoms without an identifiable physiological or anatomical cause

Examples

A functional disorder diagnosis
A functional disorder can cause real distress despite the absence of structural disease.
Doctors often find it challenging to diagnose a functional disorder due to the lack of visible abnormalities.
Some patients may suffer from a functional disorder even when all test results are normal.
