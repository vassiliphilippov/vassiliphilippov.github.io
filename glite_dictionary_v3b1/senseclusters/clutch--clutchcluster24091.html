clutch

noun
the act of grasping tightly
4.4
Pronunciation: / klʌtʃ / (AmE), / klʌtʃ / (BrE)
an act of grasping or holding something tightly

Examples

A clutch at hope
In his final clutch at the trophy, he nearly knocked it off the table.
The child escaped from the clutch of his parent's hand and ran across the yard.
With a desperate clutch, she caught the falling book just in time.
He escaped from the clutches of poverty through hard work and determination.
She managed to free her arm from his clutches at the last moment.
The frightened bird escaped the clutches of the predator.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grip - a firm hold; act of grasping tightly
  • antonym: release - the act of letting go; ceasing to hold
