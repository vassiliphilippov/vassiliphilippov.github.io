conform

verb
adapt to fit in with conditions or norms
3.6 3.2
Pronunciation: / kənˈfɔrm / (AmE), / kənˈfɔːm / (BrE)
to behave or think in the same way as others or as required by rules or expectations

Examples

To conform to rules
He found it difficult to conform after moving to such a different culture.
New employees are expected to conform to the company's dress code.
Students often feel pressure to conform to the expectations of their peers.
All recommendations should conform with the official guidelines provided by the committee.
Employees are expected to conform to the company dress code at all times.
The new design must conform to international safety standards.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adapt - to change to suit new conditions
  • synonym: comply - to act in accordance with a wish or command
  • antonym: deviate - to depart from an established course or norm
  • antonym: resist - to refuse to comply or adapt to changes
