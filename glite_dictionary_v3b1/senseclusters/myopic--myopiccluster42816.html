myopic

adjective
lacking foresight or broad view
Pronunciation: / maɪˈɑpɪk / (AmE), / maɪˈɒpɪk / (BrE)
Lacking foresight or the ability to think or plan for the future; having a narrow or limited perspective.

Examples

Myopic thinking
Critics accused the government of making myopic decisions regarding environmental policy.
His myopic approach to business ignored long-term consequences.
We cannot afford to be myopic in planning the city's future.

Related Terms

  • antonym: far-sighted - having the ability to imagine or plan for the future; showing foresight
  • synonym: shortsighted - lacking foresight or scope; unable to plan for the future
