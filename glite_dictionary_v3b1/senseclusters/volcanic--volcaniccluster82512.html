volcanic

adjective
formed from erupted magma (rock)
Pronunciation: / vəˈkænɪk /; / vɑlˈkænɪk / (AmE), / vɒlˈkænɪk / (BrE)
relating to volcanoes or material produced by volcanoes, such as lava, ash, or volcanic rocks

Examples

Volcanic ash cloud
Geologists collected samples of volcanic basalt from the mountain slope.
Obsidian is a type of volcanic glass often used for tools and ornaments.
The island is largely composed of volcanic rock formed by ancient eruptions.
Scientists measured the levels of volcanic ash in the atmosphere after the eruption.
The island was formed from volcanic eruptions millions of years ago.
The landscape is dotted with volcanic cones and craters.

Related Terms

  • synonym: igneous - (of rocks) formed by solidification of molten magma
  • antonym: plutonic - (of rock) formed by solidification of magma deep underground
