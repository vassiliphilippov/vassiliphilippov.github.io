pawnbroker's shop

noun
shop providing loans against personal property
3.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…înÀåbro äk…ôrz  É…ëp / (AmE), / Ààp…îÀênÀåbr…ô äk…ôz  É…íp / (BrE)
A shop where people can obtain loans by leaving their personal property as security, which may be sold if the loan is not repaid.

Examples

The pawnbroker's shop
He took his old watch to the pawnbroker's shop to get a quick loan.
She reclaimed her necklace from the pawnbroker's shop after paying back the loan.
There are several pawnbroker's shops on this street, catering to people in need of cash.

Synonyms

  • pawnshop
    3.0 0.76/m
    a pawnshop lends money for items left as security or buys goods, while a pawnbroker's shop is the same traditional business.
  • pawnbrokerage
    8.4 0.01/m
    a pawnbrokerage is the business or company that lends money against goods, while a pawnbroker's shop is the physical shop people visit.
  • loan office
    2.7 0.00/m
    a loan office lends money against personal property in a formal setting, while a pawnbroker's shop feels like a small local pawn shop.
  • hockshop
    9.3 0.01/m
    a hockshop is a low-price place where people pawn or sell items for cash, while a pawnbroker's shop is the usual pawn business.
