air sac

noun
small sac in lungs holding air
Pronunciation: / ˈɛr ˌsæk /; / ˈɛr sæk / (AmE), / ˈeə ˌsæk /; / ˈeə sæk / (BrE)
a small, thin-walled sac in the lungs or respiratory system of birds and some other animals that holds air and helps with breathing

Examples

Bird air sac
Birds have a system of air sacs that helps them breathe efficiently during flight.
Oxygen passes through the walls of the air sac and into the bloodstream.
The infection caused several air sacs in her lungs to become inflamed.
Birds rely on their air sacs to increase oxygen exchange during flight.
The anatomy of a bird includes several air sacs connected to the lungs.
When studying respiration in birds, the function of air sacs is crucial to understand.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alveolus - a tiny air sac in the lungs where gas exchange occurs
