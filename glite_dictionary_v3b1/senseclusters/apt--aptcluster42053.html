apt

adjective
having a tendency; likely to do something
6.9
Pronunciation: / æpt / (AmE), / æpt / (BrE)
likely or having a tendency to do something

Examples

Apt to forget
He is apt to forget names if he is not reminded.
People living near rivers are apt to experience flooding during heavy rains.
Young children are apt to imitate the behavior they see in adults.
Cars left unlocked in this area are apt to be stolen.
Children are apt to make mistakes when they are learning something new.
He is apt to forget important dates unless he writes them down.

Related Terms

  • synonym: liable - likely to do or to be something, often negative
  • synonym: likely - probable or having a good chance of happening
  • synonym: prone - having a tendency or inclination; likely to do something
  • antonym: unlikely - not probable; not likely to happen
