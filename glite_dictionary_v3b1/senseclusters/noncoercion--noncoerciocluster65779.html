noncoercion

noun
absence of force
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnkoʊˈɜːrʒən /; / ˌnɑnkoʊˈɜrʒən / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkəʊˈɜːʃən / (BrE)
the practice or state of not using force, pressure, or compulsion to make someone do something

Examples

Principle of noncoercion

Advocates of noncoercion believe that voluntary cooperation leads to better outcomes for society.

The contract was valid because it was signed in a spirit of noncoercion.
A healthy relationship is based on trust and noncoercion between both partners.
The organization promotes noncoercion in all its negotiations and agreements.

Related Terms

  • antonym: coercion - the practice of forcing someone to act in a certain way by use of threats or force
  • synonym: voluntariness - the quality of being done by free choice
  • synonym: voluntarism - the belief that actions should be voluntary and not forced
