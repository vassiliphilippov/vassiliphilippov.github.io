C

noun
unit of electric charge (coulomb)
4.6
Pronunciation: / siː / (AmE), / siː / (BrE)
the symbol for the coulomb, a unit of electrical charge equal to the charge transferred by a current of 1 ampere in 1 second

Examples

5 C charge
A charge of 5 C flows through the circuit in five seconds.
The battery capacity is measured in ampere-hours, which can be converted to C.
The official SI unit for electric charge is the C or coulomb.
