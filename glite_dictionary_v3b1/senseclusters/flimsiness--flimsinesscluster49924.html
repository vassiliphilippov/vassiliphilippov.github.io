flimsiness

noun
the state of being flimsy or weak
Pronunciation: / ˈflɪmzinəs / (AmE), / ˈflɪmzinɪs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being weak, fragile, or poorly constructed.

Examples

The flimsiness of argument
Critics pointed out the flimsiness of his argument.
The flimsiness of the chair made me hesitate to sit down.
They were disappointed by the flimsiness of the tent in harsh weather.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fragility - the quality of being easily broken or damaged
  • antonym: sturdiness - the quality of being strong and solid
