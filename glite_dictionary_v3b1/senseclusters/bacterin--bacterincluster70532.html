bacterin

noun
killed bacteria preparation
9.5
Pronunciation: /ˈbæk.tə.rɪn/ (AmE), /ˈbæk.tə.rɪn/ (BrE)
a preparation of killed bacteria used as an antigen in laboratory tests or immunological research, not for vaccination

Examples

Laboratory bacterin sample
The researchers prepared a bacterin from the killed bacteria for use in their experiment.
A bacterin can help detect specific antibodies in a patient's blood sample.
Unlike a vaccine, a bacterin is not intended to provide immunity but to aid laboratory testing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antigen - a substance that stimulates an immune response
  • antonym: vaccine - a preparation used to stimulate immunity to a disease
