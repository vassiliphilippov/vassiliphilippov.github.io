lipogenic

adjective
promoting fat formation
Pronunciation: / ˌlɪpoʊˈdʒɛnɪk / (AmE), / ˌlɪpəˈdʒɛnɪk / (BrE)
relating to or promoting the formation or production of fat in the body

Examples

Lipogenic diet
A diet high in sugar is considered lipogenic because it encourages fat storage.
Researchers studied the lipogenic effects of different types of carbohydrates on metabolism.
Some medications have lipogenic properties that can lead to weight gain.
A lipogenic diet can lead to increased fat storage in the body.
Researchers studied the lipogenic activity of certain hormones in liver cells.
Some medications have a lipogenic effect, causing patients to gain weight.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adipogenic - promoting the formation of fat cells
  • antonym: lipolytic - promoting the breakdown of fat
