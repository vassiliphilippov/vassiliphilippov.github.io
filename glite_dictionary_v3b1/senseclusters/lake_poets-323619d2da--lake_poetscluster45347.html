Lake Poets

proper noun
group of 19th-century poets from England's Lake District
8.1
Pronunciation: / leɪk ˈpoʊɪts / (AmE), / leɪk ˈpəʊɪts / (BrE)
A group of English poets in the early 19th century who lived in the Lake District and whose work was inspired by its landscape; chiefly Wordsworth, Coleridge, and Southey.

Examples

Lake Poets group
Many literary scholars have written extensively about the influence of nature on the Lake Poets.
The beauty of the Lake District served as the central inspiration for the works of the Lake Poets.
William Wordsworth is often regarded as the most prominent member of the Lake Poets.
