ambisextrous nature

noun
adaptability to both roles or sides
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˌæmbɪˈsɛkstrəs ˈneɪtʃɚ / (AmE), / ˌæmbɪˈsɛkstrəs ˈneɪtʃə / (BrE)
The quality of being able to adapt to both roles, sides, or functions, especially in a flexible or versatile way.

Examples

The ambisextrous nature
Because of her ambisextrous nature, she excelled in both leadership and support roles.
Her ambisextrous nature helped her connect easily with people from different backgrounds.
The coach valued his ambisextrous nature when assigning positions on the team.
