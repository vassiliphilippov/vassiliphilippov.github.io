fiduciary duty

noun
legal obligation to act in another's best interests
5.9
Pronunciation: / fɪˈduːʃiˌɛri ˈduːti / (AmE), / fɪˈdjuːʃiəri ˈdjuːti / (BrE)
The legal obligation of one party to act in the best interests of another, typically arising when one party is entrusted with care or management of another's assets.

Examples

Owe a fiduciary duty
An attorney owes a fiduciary duty to act in the best interest of his client.
Breach of fiduciary duty can result in legal consequences for trustees or directors.
The board members have a fiduciary duty to protect the interests of the company’s shareholders.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conflict of interest - a situation where a person's duty conflicts with personal interests
  • synonym: duty of care - a legal obligation to avoid acts or omissions likely to cause harm to others
