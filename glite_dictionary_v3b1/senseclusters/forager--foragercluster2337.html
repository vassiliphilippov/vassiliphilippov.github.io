forager

noun
one who searches for food in the wild
9.9 4.3
Pronunciation: /ˈfɑːr.ə.dʒər/ /; / /ˈfɑːr.ɪ.dʒər/ (AmE), /ˈfɒr.ə.dʒə/ /; / /ˈfɒr.ɪ.dʒə/ (BrE)
a person or animal that searches widely for food or provisions, especially by gathering or hunting in the wild

Examples

A forager in woods
In the remote woods, the forager collects edible mushrooms and berries for her family's meals.
Some modern restaurants hire expert foragers to supply them with rare wild ingredients.
Wild boars are natural foragers, using their snouts to root around for food.
