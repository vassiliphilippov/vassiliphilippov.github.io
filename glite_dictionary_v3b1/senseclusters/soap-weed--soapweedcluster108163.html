soap-weed

noun
tall yucca plant of the southwestern U.S.
5.4
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊp ˌwid / (AmE), / ˈsəʊp ˌwiːd / (BrE)
A tall, tree-like yucca native to the southwestern United States, often with sword-shaped leaves and large clusters of white flowers.

Examples

The soap-weed plant
Early settlers used the roots of soap-weed for cleaning because of their soapy properties.
The ranch was surrounded by clusters of soap-weed stretching toward the clear sky.
We saw a field dotted with tall, flowering soap-weed during our hike in New Mexico.
