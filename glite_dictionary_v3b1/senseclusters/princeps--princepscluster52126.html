princeps

noun
chief Roman leader
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪnˌsɛps / (AmE), / ˈprɪnˌsɛps / (BrE)
the leading person or first in rank, especially a title for the chief citizen in ancient Rome

Examples

Roman princeps
Augustus was honored as the first princeps of the Roman Empire.
The title of princeps signified supreme authority in ancient Rome.
Historians often debate how much power the princeps truly held.

Related Terms

  • antonym: follower - a person who follows the lead of another
  • synonym: leader - a person who leads or commands a group
