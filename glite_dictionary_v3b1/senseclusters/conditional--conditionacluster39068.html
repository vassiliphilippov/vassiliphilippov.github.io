conditional

noun
if-then statement
Pronunciation: / kənˈdɪʃənəl / (AmE), / kənˈdɪʃənəl / (BrE)
a statement or sentence that expresses a condition and its possible result, often using 'if'

Examples

An conditional sentence
In English grammar, a conditional often starts with the word 'if'.
The teacher explained how to form a conditional to talk about possible future events.
A common conditional is 'If you study hard, you will pass the exam.'

Related Terms

  • antonym: declarative - a sentence that makes a statement or declaration
  • synonym: hypothetical - a statement based on possible or imagined situations
