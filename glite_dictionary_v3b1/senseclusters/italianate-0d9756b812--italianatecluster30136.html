Italianate

noun
Italian-style building
8.7
Pronunciation: / ɪˈtæliəˌneɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈtæliəneɪt / (BrE)
a building, especially a house, designed in the Italianate architectural style, characterized by features such as low-pitched roofs and decorative brackets.

Examples

An Italianate house
The old mansion was an impressive Italianate with ornate window frames and a tall cupola.
Many 19th-century neighborhoods feature at least one Italianate among the rows of historic homes.
They restored the Italianate to its former glory, keeping the original decorative brackets and cornices.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Victorian - a building in the architectural style popular during the reign of Queen Victoria
  • synonym: villa - a large and luxurious country house in Italy or similar style
