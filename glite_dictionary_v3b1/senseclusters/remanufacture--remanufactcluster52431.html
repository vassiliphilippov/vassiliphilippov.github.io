remanufacture

noun
restoring used products
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌmænjuˈfæktʃər / (AmE), / ˌriːˌmænjuˈfæktʃə / (BrE)
the process of restoring a used product to a like-new condition through rebuilding or repairing

Examples

Remanufacture of car parts
The remanufacture of car parts helps reduce environmental impact and saves money for consumers.
Many companies now offer remanufacture services for electronics to extend their useful life.
By supporting the remanufacture industry, we can help decrease the amount of waste in landfills.

Related Terms

  • antonym: recycling - the process of converting waste into reusable material
  • synonym: refurbishment - the process of cleaning and repairing something to improve its condition
