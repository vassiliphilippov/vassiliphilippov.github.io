coincidentally

adverb
by chance; at the same time
1.0 4.0
Pronunciation: /koʊ.ɪn.ˈsɪd.ənt.liː/ (AmE), /kəʊ.ɪn.ˈsɪd.ənt.liː/ (BrE)
In a way that happens by chance, especially in a surprising or unusual way; happening at the same time or in connection without prior arrangement.

Examples

Happened coincidentally
Coincidentally, the two events happened on the same day but were not related.
Coincidentally, we were both assigned to the same project without knowing it.
I bumped into her at the café, and coincidentally, she had just been talking about me.
