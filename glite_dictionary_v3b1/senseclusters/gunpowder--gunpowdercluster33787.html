gunpowder

noun
an explosive powder used in firearms and fireworks
1.3
Pronunciation: / ˈɡʌnˌpaʊdɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡʌnˌpaʊdə / (BrE)
an explosive powder made from a mixture of potassium nitrate, charcoal, and sulfur, used in firearms, fireworks, and blasting

Examples

Explosive gunpowder
Early cannons relied on a blast of gunpowder to propel their heavy projectiles.
She watched as the fireworks filled the air with colored sparks and the smell of gunpowder.
The invention of gunpowder changed the nature of warfare forever.
