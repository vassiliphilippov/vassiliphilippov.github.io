crunch

verb
chew noisily with a cracking sound
1.0
Pronunciation: / krʌntʃ / (AmE), / krʌntʃ / (BrE)
to chew or crush something with your teeth or underfoot, making a loud, crackling or grinding sound

Examples

To crunch an apple
He crunched on the ice cubes absentmindedly during the meeting.
She likes to crunch apples while watching TV.
The dog was crunching a bone in the backyard.
He absentmindedly crunched the ice cubes in his glass while waiting.
She likes to crunch on fresh carrots during lunch.
The gravel crunched beneath their boots as they walked down the path.
He crunched the ice cubes loudly between his teeth.
Her tires crunched over the gravel driveway as she arrived.
The dry leaves crunch underfoot as you walk through the park.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crackle - make a series of short, sharp, snapping sounds
  • synonym: crush - to press something so hard it breaks or is damaged
  • synonym: grind - to crush or break into small pieces, especially between two hard surfaces
  • synonym: munch - to chew steadily or vigorously, especially with a loud noise
