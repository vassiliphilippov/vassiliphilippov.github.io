blackish

adjective
somewhat black in color
Pronunciation: / ˈblækɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈblækɪʃ / (BrE)
Somewhat black in color; having a dark hue that is close to black but not fully black.

Examples

A blackish mark
She noticed a blackish mark on the old photograph.
The river looked blackish in the fading evening light.
The sky was filled with blackish clouds before the storm began.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: black - of the darkest color, like coal or soot
  • degree lower: gray - of a color intermediate between black and white
