centralist

noun
supporter of central authority
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛntrəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈsɛntrəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports the concentration of power and authority in a central government or organization

Examples

A centralist leader
The centralist argued that a strong national government would ensure stability.
During the debate, she identified herself as a centralist who opposed local autonomy.
Many centralists believe that unified policies are more effective than regional ones.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decentralist - a person who favors distributing power away from a central authority
  • antonym: federalist - a person who supports a federal system of government
