gallon

noun
British unit of capacity (approx. 4.545 liters)
3.2 1.0
Pronunciation: /ˈgæl.ən/ (AmE), /ˈgæl.ən/ (BrE)
a unit for measuring liquid volume; the exact amount is different in the UK (about 4.5 liters) and the US (about 3.8 liters)

Examples

A gallon of milk
A British gallon of petrol is larger than its American counterpart.
Milk was once delivered in glass bottles holding exactly one gallon.
The recipe called for half a gallon of water, but I used less.
A gallon of milk usually lasts us a week.
He bought a five-gallon container for water storage.
The car can travel over thirty miles on a single gallon of gas.

  • synonym: liter - metric unit of volume, about a quarter of a gallon
  • degree lower: quart - a unit of liquid capacity equal to a quarter of a gallon
