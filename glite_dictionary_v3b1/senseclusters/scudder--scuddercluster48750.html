scudder

noun
fast runner (archaic)
Pronunciation: / ˈskʌdər / (AmE), / ˈskʌdə / (BrE)
an old term for a person who moves quickly or runs fast

Examples

A fast scudder
In his youth, Tom was called a scudder for his speed on the football field.
The children watched as the scudder dashed across the meadow, leaving everyone behind.
Long ago, a scudder would deliver urgent messages between villages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: runner - a person who runs
  • synonym: sprinter - a person who runs very fast, especially in short races
