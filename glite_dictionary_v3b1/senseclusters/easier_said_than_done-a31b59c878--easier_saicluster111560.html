difficult to do despite sounding simple

Pronunciation: / ˌiːziɚ ˈsɛd ðən ˈdʌn / (AmE), / ˌiːziə ˈsɛd ðən ˈdʌn / (BrE)
used to say that something sounds simple or easy to do, but is actually difficult to achieve in practice

Examples

Easier said than done phrase
Balancing work and family life is easier said than done.
Forgiving someone who hurt you deeply can be easier said than done.
Moving to a new country is easier said than done for most people.
