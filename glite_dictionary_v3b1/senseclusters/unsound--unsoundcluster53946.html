unsound

adjective
not based on valid reasoning
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈsaʊnd / (AmE), / ʌnˈsaʊnd / (BrE)
not based on valid or reliable reasoning or evidence; containing a fallacy, error, or being unreliable or faulty.

Examples

Unsound argument
Critics noted that the report was unsound due to several logical fallacies.
His theory was quickly discredited because it was built on unsound assumptions.
The judge dismissed the case, citing unsound arguments presented by the defense.
His unsound advice led to several costly mistakes for the company.
She realized later that following such unsound reasoning was a serious error.
The theory was criticized as unsound by most experts in the field.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fallacious - based on mistaken belief or reasoning
  • synonym: flawed - having faults or weaknesses
  • antonym: sound - based on valid reasoning or evidence
