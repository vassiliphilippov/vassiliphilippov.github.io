common St John's wort

noun
a Eurasian shrub with yellow flowers, used medicinally
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmən sɛnt dʒɑnz wɝt / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən sənt ˈdʒɒnz wɜːt / (BrE)
A deciduous, bushy Eurasian shrub (Hypericum perforatum) with golden yellow flowers and reddish-purple fruits, used in herbal remedies.

Examples

Common St John's wort plant
Common St John's wort is widely used in herbal medicine to treat mild depression.
Spanish farmers make a soothing salve from the oil extracted from common St John's wort.
The fields behind the house were filled with blooming common St John's wort.
