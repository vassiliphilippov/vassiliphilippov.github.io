blurredly

adverb
unclearly
7.5 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààbl…úrdli / (AmE), / Ààbl…úÀêdli / (BrE)
in a way that is unclear or difficult to see or understand

Examples

Blurredly seen lights
Through the foggy window, the city lights appeared blurredly in the distance.
She remembered the events of that night only blurredly, as if in a dream.
The message was written so blurredly that it was almost impossible to read.

Synonyms

  • unclearly
    4.4 0.04/m
    unclearly describes something hard to understand or notice, while blurredly suggests a visual soft or out-of-focus appearance.
  • fuzzily
    5.4 0.05/m
    fuzzily means seen with soft, unclear edges like cotton, while blurredly often points to a smeared or out-of-focus view.
  • indistinctly
    6.3 0.40/m
    indistinctly describes things heard or seen without clear detail, while blurredly emphasizes a blurry, visually out-of-focus look.
  • foggily
    5.6 0.03/m
    foggily describes something as if hidden by fog or mist, while blurredly stresses soft focus or an unclear shape.
  • opaquely
    8.0 0.08/m
    opaquely means hard to understand or see through, often in a formal way, while blurredly suggests a soft, visual or vague impression.
