dunning

noun
debt collection process
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʌnɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of persistently demanding payment of a debt, especially through repeated requests or notices

Examples

A dunning letter
The company sent several dunning letters to remind the customer about the overdue bill.
Ignoring dunning notices can lead to further legal action from creditors.
After months of dunning, the agency finally received payment from the client.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collection - the act of pursuing payment of debts
  • antonym: forgiveness - the act of canceling a debt or obligation
