Choloepus

noun
genus of two-toed sloths
9.9
Pronunciation: / koʊˈloʊəpəs / (AmE), / kɒˈlɔːɪpəs / (BrE)
a genus of sloths in the family Megalonychidae, known as the two-toed sloths, native to Central and South America

Examples

Choloepus sloth species
Researchers studied the behavior of Choloepus in their native rainforest habitats.
The genus Choloepus includes two species of two-toed sloths found in Central and South America.
Unlike the three-toed sloth, animals in the genus Choloepus have only two toes on their front limbs.
