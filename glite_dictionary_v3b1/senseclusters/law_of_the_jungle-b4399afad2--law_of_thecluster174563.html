law of the jungle

noun
principle that only the strong survive
3.5 5.1
Pronunciation: /lɔː əv ðə ˈdʒʌŋ.gəl/ /; / /lɑː əv ðə ˈdʒʌŋ.gəl/ (AmE), /lɔː əv ðə ˈdʒʌŋ.gəl/ (BrE)
the idea that in a competitive environment, only the strongest or most ruthless individuals succeed, without regard for fairness or moral principles

Examples

The law of the jungle
In the absence of clear rules, it became a case of law of the jungle at the negotiating table.
Many believe that in politics, it’s often the law of the jungle that determines who rises to the top.
When the market crashed, law of the jungle conditions took over and many companies went bankrupt.

Related Terms

  • synonym: survival of the fittest - the concept that those best adapted to their environment survive and prosper
