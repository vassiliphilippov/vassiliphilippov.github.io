GiB

noun
unit of digital information (1,073,741,824 bytes)
Pronunciation: / d͡ʒiː aɪ biː / (AmE), / d͡ʒiː aɪ biː / (BrE)
A unit of digital information equal to 1,073,741,824 bytes or 1,024 mebibytes, commonly used in computing.

Examples

GiB of memory
A modern graphics card may have up to 24 GiB of video memory.
Many operating systems display hard drive capacities in GiB instead of gigabytes.
The file size was listed as 8 GiB, which is larger than a standard DVD.
