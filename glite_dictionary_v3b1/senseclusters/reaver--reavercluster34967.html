reaver

noun
raider or plunderer
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈriːvər / (AmE), / ˈriːvə / (BrE)
a person who raids or plunders, often violently, especially in stories, legends, or fantasy

Examples

A reaver raids villages
In the old tales, a reaver would strike villages along the coast without warning.
The castle was built to protect the land from reavers who came from the north.
Legends describe the reaver as both feared and respected by his enemies.
The coastal town built walls to protect itself from reavers who came by sea.
In the old legends, a reaver would strike fear into the hearts of villagers.
The story describes how the reaver took everything of value from the settlement.
The villagers lived in fear of the reaver who attacked their homes at night.
Legends tell of a monstrous reaver haunting the northern forests.
The hero faced the reaver in a final, desperate battle to save the town.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bandit - a robber, especially one who attacks travelers
  • antonym: defender - a person who protects against attack
  • synonym: marauder - a person who raids or plunders
  • synonym: plunderer - someone who steals goods, typically using force
  • synonym: raider - a person who attacks or invades a place suddenly
