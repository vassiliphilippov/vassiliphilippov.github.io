reaver

noun
raider or plunderer
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈriː.vər/ (AmE), /ˈriː.və/ (BrE)
a person who raids or plunders, often violently, especially in stories, legends, or fantasy

Examples

A reaver raids villages
In the old tales, a reaver would strike villages along the coast without warning.
The castle was built to protect the land from reavers who came from the north.
Legends describe the reaver as both feared and respected by his enemies.
The coastal town built walls to protect itself from reavers who came by sea.

The story describes how the reaver took everything of value from the settlement.

The hero faced the reaver in a final, desperate battle to save the town.
