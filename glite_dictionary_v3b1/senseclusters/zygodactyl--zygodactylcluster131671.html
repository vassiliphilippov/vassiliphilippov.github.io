zygodactyl

adjective
bird's toes: two forward, two back
9.9 9.8
Pronunciation: /zaɪ.ɡə.ˈdæk.tɪl/ /; / /zɪ.ɡə.ˈdæk.tɪl/ (AmE), /zɪ.ɡə.ˈdæk.tɪl/ /; / /zaɪ.ɡə.ˈdæk.tɪl/ (BrE)
(of bird feet) having two toes pointing forward and two pointing backward, specifically the first and fourth toes directed backward and the second and third forward.

Examples

Zygodactyl feet
Ospreys are among the few birds with zygodactyl feet adapted for catching fish.
Parrots have zygodactyl feet, which help them grasp branches and climb.
The woodpecker's zygodactyl toes enable it to cling securely to tree trunks.
