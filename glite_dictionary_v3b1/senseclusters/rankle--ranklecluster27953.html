rankle

verb
persist and cause irritation or resentment
Pronunciation: / ˈræŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈræŋkəl / (BrE)
(of a wound, emotion, or comment) to continue to cause irritation or bitter resentment over a period of time.

Examples

To rankle a wound
Even years later, the unfair decision continued to rankle in his mind.
Her critical remarks still rankle with me after all this time.
The memory of that insult would rankle him for decades.

Related Terms

  • antonym: delight - to bring great pleasure (opposite of irritate or anger)
  • synonym: fester - to become worse or more intense, especially through long-term neglect or irritation
  • synonym: smolder - to show suppressed anger or resentment
