subclause

noun
dependent clause
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌklɔz / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌklɔːz / (BrE)
a part of a sentence that cannot stand alone and depends on the main clause to complete its meaning

Examples

Sentence subclause
The teacher asked us to underline every subclause in the paragraph.
In the sentence 'She left when it started raining,' 'when it started raining' is a subclause.
A subclause often begins with words like 'because', 'if', or 'when'.
The subclause in this sentence explains why she left early.
If you look closely, you will see the subclause starts with 'because'.
A subclause often adds extra information to the main idea of a sentence.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: clause - a group of words with a subject and verb
  • synonym: dependent clause - a clause that cannot stand alone as a sentence
  • antonym: main clause - a clause that can stand alone as a sentence
  • synonym: sentence - a set of words expressing a complete thought
