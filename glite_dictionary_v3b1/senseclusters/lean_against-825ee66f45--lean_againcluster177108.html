lean against

verb
rest for support or balance
Pronunciation: / lin əˈgɛnst / (AmE), / liːn əˈɡɛnst / (BrE)
to rest your body lightly on something for support or balance

Examples

To lean against a wall
He paused to lean against the old stone wall and catch his breath.
She likes to lean against the window while reading a book.
You shouldn't lean against the glass door because it's not very strong.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lean on - to rely physically or figuratively for support
