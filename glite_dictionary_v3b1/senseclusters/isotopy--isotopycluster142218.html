isotopy

noun
repetition of themes
Pronunciation: / aɪˈsɑːtəpi / (AmE), / aɪˈsɒtəpi / (BrE)
the repetition of semantic traits or themes within a text, creating coherence in its meaning

Examples

Textual isotopy
The literary critic identified an isotopy of journey metaphors throughout the novel.
By analyzing the isotopy in the speech, the researcher uncovered its underlying message.
A strong isotopy of hope can unify the themes in a collection of poems.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ambiguity - the quality of having more than one possible meaning
  • synonym: cohesion - the quality of being logically connected in a text
