kinematics

noun
study of motion without regard to forces
7.6 6.7
Pronunciation: /kɪ.nə.ˈmæt.ɪks/ (AmE), /kɪ.nə.ˈmæt.ɪks/ (BrE)
the branch of mechanics that deals with the motion of objects without considering the forces or masses involved

Examples

Study of kinematics
A basic understanding of kinematics is essential in fields like automotive design and biomechanics.
In classical mechanics, kinematics is often the first topic introduced to explain how objects move.
The engineer used kinematics to model the robot's arm movements precisely.
