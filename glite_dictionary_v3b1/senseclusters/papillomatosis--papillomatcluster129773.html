papillomatosis

noun
benign papilloma disease
9.9
Pronunciation: /pə.pɪl.ə.mə.ˈtoʊ.sɪs/ /; / /pæ.pɪl.ə.mə.ˈtoʊ.sɪs/ (AmE), /pə.pɪl.ə.mə.ˈtəʊ.sɪs/ /; / /pæ.pɪl.ə.mə.ˈtəʊ.sɪs/ (BrE)
a disease or medical condition where many benign papillomas (wart-like growths) form on the skin or mucous membranes, in humans or animals

Examples

Papillomatosis disease
The doctor diagnosed the child with papillomatosis after finding multiple growths in the airway.
Surgical removal is often required to treat papillomatosis in the larynx.
Patients with papillomatosis may experience recurring voice changes and breathing problems.
The veterinarian diagnosed the calf with papillomatosis after noticing several warts on its skin.
Dogs affected by papillomatosis may develop clusters of warts around their mouths or paws.
Treatment for papillomatosis often involves removing the warts and supporting the animal's immune system.
The patient was diagnosed with papillomatosis after several growths appeared in his throat.
Veterinarians often treat papillomatosis in young cattle and dogs.
Surgical removal is sometimes necessary to manage severe cases of papillomatosis.
