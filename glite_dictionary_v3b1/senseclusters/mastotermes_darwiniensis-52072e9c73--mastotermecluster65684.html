Mastotermes darwiniensis

noun
a primitive Australian termite species
Pronunciation: / ˌmæstoʊˈtɜrmiːz dɑːrˈwaɪniːˌɛnɪsɪs / (AmE), / ˌmæstəˈtɜːmiːz dɑːˈwɪniːˌɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a large Australian termite, the only living species of its genus, known as a living fossil and believed to show traits linking cockroaches and termites

Examples

The Mastotermes darwiniensis termite
Researchers discovered a colony of Mastotermes darwiniensis in northern Australia.
The Mastotermes darwiniensis is often studied for its primitive characteristics among termites.
Unlike most termites, Mastotermes darwiniensis retains several ancestral traits reminiscent of cockroaches.
