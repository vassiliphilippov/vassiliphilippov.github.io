flatter

verb
to praise insincerely
Pronunciation: / ˈflætər / (AmE), / ˈflætə / (BrE)
to praise someone, often insincerely or too much, usually to please them or gain an advantage

Examples

To flatter someone
She tried to flatter her boss with exaggerated compliments.
They flattered him, hoping he would give them what they wanted.
You don't need to flatter me; just tell the truth.
He tried to flatter his boss by praising her leadership skills.
She felt genuinely flattered by the artist's kind words about her work.
People sometimes flatter others to get what they want.

Related Terms

  • antonym: insult - to speak or treat with disrespect or scorn
  • synonym: praise - to express approval or admiration for someone or something
