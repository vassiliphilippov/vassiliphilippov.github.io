nightglow

noun
faint nighttime light
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈnaɪtˌɡloʊ / (AmE), / ˈnaɪtˌɡləʊ / (BrE)
a faint light visible at night, often produced by living organisms or chemical reactions, especially in seawater or on wet surfaces

Examples

Ocean nightglow
The beach was illuminated by the mysterious nightglow of tiny plankton in the water.
Sailors often marvel at the nightglow that appears on the ocean's surface during warm nights.
After the rain, a soft nightglow could be seen shimmering on the wet pavement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bioluminescence - the production of light by living organisms
  • synonym: phosphorescence - a type of light emitted without heat, often after exposure to radiation
