poeticize

verb
to make poetic
7.6
Pronunciation: / poʊˈɛtɪˌsaɪz / (AmE), / pəʊˈɛtɪsaɪz / (BrE)
to express something in a poetic way or make it seem more poetic or beautiful than it really is

Examples

To poeticize harsh realities
The author tried to poeticize the harsh realities of war in her latest novel.
He tends to poeticize everyday moments, turning simple events into beautiful stories.
Critics argued that the film poeticized the struggles of the main character too much.
The filmmaker chose to poeticize the struggles of the main character, making the story feel more hopeful.
Historians warn not to poeticize the past and ignore its harsh realities.
She tends to poeticize everyday moments, turning simple events into beautiful stories.

Related Terms

  • antonym: downplay - to make something seem less important or serious
  • synonym: idealize - to regard or represent as perfect or better than reality
  • antonym: prosaic - lacking poetic beauty; straightforward or dull
  • synonym: romanticize - to make something seem more attractive or ideal than it really is
