propitiatory

adjective
intended to appease or reconcile
Pronunciation: / prəˈpɪʃ.iəˌtɔr.i /; / proʊˈpɪʃiəˌtɔri / (AmE), / prəˈpɪʃ.i.ə.tər.i /; / prəˈpɪʃiətəri / (BrE)
intended to appease, reconcile, or win the favor of someone, especially a god or someone who is offended

Examples

A propitiatory sacrifice
A propitiatory sacrifice was performed to calm the angry spirits.
He offered a propitiatory gift to make amends for his earlier behavior.
The ambassador made a propitiatory speech to ease tensions between the countries.
Many ancient rituals included propitiatory ceremonies to appease the gods after a natural disaster.
She brought a propitiatory gift to mend the strained relationship with her colleague.
The priests offered a propitiatory sacrifice to ensure a good harvest that year.

Related Terms

  • antonym: antagonistic - showing or feeling active opposition or hostility
  • synonym: conciliatory - intended to placate or pacify
  • synonym: expiatory - intended to atone for wrongdoing or sin
