peperino

noun
volcanic building stone
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛpəˈriːnoʊ / (AmE), / ˌpɛpəˈriːnəʊ / (BrE)
a type of volcanic rock made mostly of tuff and lava fragments, often used as building stone

Examples

Volcanic peperino rock
The ancient Romans often used peperino to construct temples and public buildings.
A layer of peperino was discovered beneath the ruins during the archaeological dig.
The city walls were built from locally quarried peperino, giving them a distinctive look.

Related Terms

  • antonym: basalt - a dark, fine-grained volcanic rock
  • synonym: tuff - a porous rock formed from volcanic ash
