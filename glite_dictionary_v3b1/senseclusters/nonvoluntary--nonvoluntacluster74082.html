nonvoluntary

adjective
not under conscious control
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˈvɑlənˌtɛri / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈvɒlənˌtəri / (BrE)
not subject to one's conscious will; done or occurring without intentional control or decision

Examples

Nonvoluntary action
His actions were nonvoluntary, resulting from a reflex rather than a decision.
Some legal distinctions depend on whether an act is nonvoluntary or done of free choice.
The patient exhibited nonvoluntary muscle movements after the seizure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: involuntary - done without will or conscious control
  • antonym: voluntary - done by one's own will
