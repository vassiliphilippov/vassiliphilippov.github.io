trephining

noun
surgical skull opening
9.9
Pronunciation: / trəˈfaɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / trɪˈfaɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
a hole made in the skull by surgical removal of a circular piece of bone, or the bone piece itself, during trephination.

Examples

A trephining in bone
The X-ray revealed a small trephining where the surgeon had removed a section of bone.
After the operation, the patient had a visible trephining in the skull.
Archaeologists discovered an ancient skull with evidence of trephining performed thousands of years ago.

Related Terms

  • synonym: trepanation - the surgical procedure of drilling or scraping a hole into the skull
