heterozygote

noun
organism with two different gene alleles
5.5 7.8
Pronunciation: /he.tə.rə.ˈzaɪ.goʊt/ /; / /hə.tə.rə.ˈzaɪ.goʊt/ (AmE), /he.tə.rə.ˈzaɪ.gəʊt/ /; / /hə.tə.rə.ˈzaɪ.gəʊt/ (BrE)
(genetics) an organism that has two different alleles of a particular gene, resulting in genetic variation in its offspring.

Examples

A heterozygote organism
A heterozygote for a recessive disease may not show symptoms but can pass the gene to offspring.
Plants that are heterozygote for flower color often display intermediate hues.
The study examined how heterozygote mice differed from homozygotes in their response to treatment.
