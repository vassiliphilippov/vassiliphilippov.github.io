Muse

proper noun
goddess presiding over arts in Greek myth
3.7
Pronunciation: / mjuz / (AmE), / mjuːz / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, any of the nine goddesses, daughters of Zeus and Mnemosyne, who preside over the arts and sciences.

Examples

The Muse of arts
Each Muse was believed to inspire a different art, such as music or poetry.
In ancient tales, Clio is celebrated as the Muse of history.
The poet prayed to the Muses for inspiration before beginning his epic.

Related Terms

  • synonym: muse - a source of inspiration, especially for artists and poets
