ectozoan

noun
external parasitic organism
6.0 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɛk.toʊˈzoʊ.ən / (AmE), / ˌɛk.təʊˈzəʊ.ən / (BrE)
an organism that lives on the body surface of another organism and acts as a parasite, such as a flea or tick

Examples

Ectozoan parasite
Fleas are a classic example of an ectozoan infesting household pets.
Researchers studied several types of ectozoans affecting livestock in rural areas.
The tick, as an ectozoan, remains attached to the skin of its host for a prolonged period.

Related Terms

  • antonym: endoparasite - a parasite that lives inside the host's body
