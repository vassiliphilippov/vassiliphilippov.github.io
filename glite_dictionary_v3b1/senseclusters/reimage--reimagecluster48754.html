reimage

verb
to change reputation
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈɪmɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɪmɪdʒ / (BrE)
to give a person, organization, or product a new public image or reputation

Examples

To reimage a brand
The company decided to reimage itself after a series of scandals hurt its reputation.
A new logo and advertising campaign helped the brand reimage in the eyes of consumers.
The politician worked hard to reimage his public persona before the election.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rebrand - to change the image or identity of a company or product
  • synonym: refresh - to update or improve the appearance or image of something
  • antonym: tarnish - to damage the reputation or image of someone or something
