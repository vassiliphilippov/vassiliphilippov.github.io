prison

noun
situation or place of seeming confinement
1.1
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪzən / (AmE), / ˈprɪzən / (BrE)
A place or situation in which someone feels very confined or restricted, often used metaphorically.

Examples

A mental prison
For some, a small town can feel like a prison when they long for adventure.
He described his cubicle as a prison from which he couldn't escape.
Her strict schedule was a self-imposed prison that left little room for spontaneity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: confinement - the state of being restricted within certain limits
  • antonym: freedom - the condition of being free or unrestricted
