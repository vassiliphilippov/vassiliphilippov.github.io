coracoid

noun
shoulder bone
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrəˌkɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɒrəkɔɪd / (BrE)
a bone or bony projection in the shoulder region of vertebrates, helping to connect the shoulder to the body

Examples

Bird coracoid bone
In birds, the coracoid is essential for supporting the wings during flight.
The doctor explained that the injury was near the coracoid of the shoulder.
Fossil evidence shows that dinosaurs also had a prominent coracoid bone.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scapula - the shoulder blade bone in vertebrates
