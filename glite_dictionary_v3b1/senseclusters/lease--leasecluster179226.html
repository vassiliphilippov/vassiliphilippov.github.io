lease

verb
obtain use of something by contract
6.5
Pronunciation: / lis / (AmE), / liːs / (BrE)
to obtain or use property, land, or an item by paying rent (as the renter)

Examples

To lease an apartment
They decided to lease an apartment downtown for a year.
We need to lease office space for our new company.
You can lease a car instead of buying one outright.
They have leased their apartment for another year.
We lease the copier for our office rather than buying it outright.
You can lease a car for three years with this contract.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rent - to pay for the use of something, such as a house or car, for a set period
