kelvin

noun
SI unit of temperature
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛl.vɪn / (AmE), / ˈkɛl.vɪn / (BrE)
the SI base unit of thermodynamic temperature, equal in magnitude to the degree Celsius

Examples

Zero kelvin
Absolute zero is defined as zero kelvin, the lowest possible temperature.
Astronomers often report the surface temperature of stars in kelvin rather than Celsius or Fahrenheit.
The temperature was measured at 300 kelvin in the laboratory experiment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: celsius - scale and unit of temperature where 0 is the freezing point of water
  • antonym: fahrenheit - non-metric temperature scale commonly used in the US
