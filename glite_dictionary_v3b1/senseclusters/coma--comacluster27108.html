coma

noun
terminal tuft of bracts or hairs in plants
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊmə / (AmE), / ˈkəʊmə / (BrE)
(Botany) A tuft of bracts or hairs, especially one at the tip of a plant organ such as a seed or fruit.

Examples

Seed coma
In pineapples, the coma consists of a tuft of leafy bracts at the fruit's apex.
Many seeds disperse more easily because of the silky coma attached to them.
The dandelion seed's coma helps it to float gracefully on the wind.
