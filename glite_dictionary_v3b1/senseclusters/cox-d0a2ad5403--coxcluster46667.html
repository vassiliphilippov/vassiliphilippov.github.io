Cox

noun
enzyme controlling prostaglandin production
7.9
Pronunciation: / kɑks / (AmE), / kɒks / (BrE)
either of two related enzymes (COX-1 and COX-2) that control the production of prostaglandins and are inhibited by drugs such as aspirin

Examples

Cox enzymes
Aspirin works by inhibiting the action of Cox enzymes in the body.
Researchers are studying the differences between COX-1 and COX-2 to develop better Cox inhibitors.
Selective Cox inhibitors can relieve pain without causing as many digestive side effects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cyclooxygenase - another name for Cox enzymes
