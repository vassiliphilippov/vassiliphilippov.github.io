enslaver

noun
person who enslaves others
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈsleɪvər / (AmE), / ɪnˈsleɪvə / (BrE)
a person who forces others into slavery or keeps them in a state of slavery

Examples

A cruel enslaver
The enslaver profited from the suffering of others.
History books describe how the enslaver controlled every aspect of the captives' lives.
The legacy of the enslaver still affects society today.
The enslaver forced hundreds of people to work without pay on his plantation.
Historians study the lives of both the enslaver and the enslaved to understand the past.
Laws were eventually passed to punish any enslaver who tried to keep people in bondage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abolitionist - a person who works to end slavery
  • synonym: slaveholder - a person who owns slaves
