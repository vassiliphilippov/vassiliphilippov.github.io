pole weapon

noun
long-shafted close combat weapon
8.7 6.4
Pronunciation: /poʊl ˈwep.ən/ (AmE), /pəʊl ˈwep.ən/ (BrE)
A weapon designed for close combat, consisting of a main fighting part attached to the end of a long shaft, typically made of wood, to increase the user's reach.

Examples

A pole weapon attack
He studied the techniques of using a pole weapon in historical reenactments.
Many armies equipped their foot soldiers with some form of pole weapon for better reach in battle.
The museum displayed a rare medieval pole weapon used by infantry soldiers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: polearm - a weapon with a long shaft and a blade or pointed end, used in combat
