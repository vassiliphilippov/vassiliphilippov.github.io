Japanese pink

noun
flowering plant with jagged petals
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈpæn ɪs pɪŋk / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæn ɪs pɪŋk / (BrE)
a variety of China pink (Dianthus chinensis) with jagged-edged petals, cultivated for its bright, ornamental flowers

Examples

Bright Japanese pink flowers
Gardeners often choose Japanese pink for its long-lasting blooms and attractive appearance.
She planted Japanese pink in the border to add a splash of vibrant color to the garden.
The jagged petals of the Japanese pink make it easily distinguishable from other Dianthus varieties.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: China pink - a related species of Dianthus with fringed petals
  • synonym: carnation - a popular garden flower in the genus Dianthus
