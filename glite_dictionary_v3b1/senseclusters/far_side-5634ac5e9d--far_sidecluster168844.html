far side

noun
most distant side
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑr saɪd / (AmE), / ˈfɑː saɪd / (BrE)
the side of something that is most distant from the observer or a reference point

Examples

The far side of park
The picnic table was set up on the far side of the park, away from the playground.
She waved to her friend standing on the far side of the busy street.
The cat disappeared behind the far side of the sofa and would not come out.

Related Terms

  • antonym: near side - the side of something closest to the observer
  • synonym: opposite side - the side directly across from a given point
