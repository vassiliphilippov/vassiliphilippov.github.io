newsie

noun
newspaper seller
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈnuːzi / (AmE), / ˈnjuːzi / (BrE)
a person, especially a child, who sells newspapers on the street

Examples

A newsie selling papers
The newsie stood on the corner, calling out the day's headlines to passersby.
In old movies, you often see a newsie selling papers on busy city streets.
The young newsie worked every morning before school to help his family.
The newsie shouted the latest headlines to attract customers on the busy street.
In the early 1900s, many newsies worked long hours to help support their families.
A friendly newsie handed me a newspaper as I walked to work this morning.
