division

noun
US Air Force unit comprising several wings
Pronunciation: / dɪˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / dɪˈvɪʒən /; / dɪˈvɪʒ.ən / (BrE)
a large group or unit within the military, such as the army, air force, or navy

Examples

Army division
After a major reorganization, the division was tasked with oversight of several new wings.
He served as an officer in the 5th Air Force division for three years.
The Air Force announced that the division would relocate its headquarters to Texas.
Each division reported to the squadron commander for further orders.
The destroyers were organized into a division for the upcoming operation.
The navy assigned a new commanding officer to oversee the battleship division.
After months of training, the division was finally deployed overseas.
Several armored divisions participated in the military exercise.
The general ordered the entire division to advance at dawn.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: brigade - a smaller military unit than a division, typically part of a division
  • degree higher: corps - a larger military unit than a division, composed of several divisions
  • synonym: squadron - a principal division of a fleet, consisting of a group of ships
