eq

noun
abbreviation for 'equivalent', a unit in chemistry
9.9
Pronunciation: / iː kjuː / (AmE), / iː kjuː / (BrE)
an abbreviation for 'equivalent' or 'equivalent weight', the quantity of a substance that will react with or supply one mole of hydrogen ions or electrons in a chemical reaction

Examples

One eq of sodium
One eq of sodium will react completely with one eq of chlorine.
The chemist calculated the number of eq of acid consumed in the reaction.
The report specifies concentrations in terms of eq per liter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: equivalent weight - the amount of a substance that reacts with a fixed quantity of another substance
