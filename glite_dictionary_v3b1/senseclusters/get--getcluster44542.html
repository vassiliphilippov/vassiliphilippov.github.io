get

verb
buy or purchase
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt / (AmE), / ɡɛt / (BrE)
to obtain, receive, or acquire something (including buying, catching an illness, or begetting children)

Examples

I get a prize
Did you get the tickets for the concert yesterday?
I need to get some milk from the store on my way home.
She got a new jacket during the holiday sales.
After several attempts, she finally got the hotel receptionist.
I tried to get him all morning, but his line was busy.
You can get the emergency services by dialing 911.
Did you get my email yesterday?
It's difficult to get fresh fruit during the winter here.
She worked hard to get a promotion at the company.

  • synonym: receive - to be given or presented with something
