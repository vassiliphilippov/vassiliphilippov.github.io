net

noun
amount of profit after expenses
Pronunciation: / nɛt / (AmE), / nɛt / (BrE)
the amount by which revenue exceeds expenses in a particular period, after accounting for depreciation and other non-cash charges

Examples

The net was positive
After accounting for depreciation, the net was still positive despite lower sales.
The company's net for the quarter increased by 10 percent compared to last year.
We need to improve our net to stay competitive in the market.

Related Terms

  • antonym: loss - the excess of expenses over revenues
  • synonym: profit - financial gain; the difference between revenue and expenses
