deleveraging

verb
to reduce debt
Pronunciation: / diːˈlɛvərɪdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / diːˈlɛvərɪdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
to reduce the level of debt by repaying loans, selling assets, or lowering financial risk

Examples

To deleveraging a company
After the financial crisis, many companies began deleveraging to strengthen their balance sheets.
The government encouraged banks to start deleveraging by selling risky assets.
Investors worry that rapid deleveraging could slow economic growth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: borrow - to take and use money with the intention of returning it
  • synonym: repay - to pay back money that is owed
