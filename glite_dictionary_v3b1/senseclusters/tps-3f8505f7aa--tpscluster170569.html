TPS

noun
throttle position sensor
Pronunciation: / ti pi ɛs / (AmE), / tiː piː ɛs / (BrE)
a throttle position sensor, a device in a vehicle's engine that measures the position of the throttle valve and sends data to the engine control unit

Examples

Car TPS sensor
The mechanic diagnosed a faulty TPS as the cause of the engine's irregular idling.
A malfunctioning TPS can lead to poor fuel efficiency and engine performance.
After replacing the TPS, the car accelerated smoothly without hesitation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: actuator - a device that moves or controls a mechanism or system
  • synonym: sensor - a device that detects or measures a physical property
