criollo

noun
person of Spanish descent born in Spanish America
Pronunciation: / kriˈoʊjoʊ / (AmE), / krɪˈɒljəʊ / (BrE)
A person of pure or mostly Spanish descent born in Spanish America, especially in colonial times.

Examples

Criollo elite
During the colonial period, criollo elites often held significant power in Spanish America.
Many criollo families traced their ancestry directly to Spanish settlers.
The revolution was largely led by criollo leaders seeking independence from Spain.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mestizo - a person of mixed European and Indigenous descent, especially in Latin America
  • antonym: peninsular - a person born in Spain residing in the Spanish colonies
