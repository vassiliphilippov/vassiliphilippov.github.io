friction between

noun
conflict or tension between groups
Pronunciation: / ˈfrɪkʃən bɪˈtwin / (AmE), / ˈfrɪkʃən bɪˈtwiːn / (BrE)
conflict, tension, or lack of harmony between two or more people or groups

Examples

Friction between team members
Miscommunication led to friction between team members during the project.
The recent policy changes have caused significant friction between management and staff.
There is growing friction between the neighboring countries over trade regulations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conflict - a serious disagreement or argument, typically a protracted one
  • antonym: harmony - agreement or concord between people or groups
