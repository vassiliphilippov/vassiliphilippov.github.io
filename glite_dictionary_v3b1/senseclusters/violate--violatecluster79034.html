violate

verb
destroy and strip of possessions
Pronunciation: / ˈvaɪ.ə.leɪt / (AmE), / ˈvaɪ.ə.leɪt / (BrE)
to destroy and strip something of its possessions, especially by force or violence

Examples

To violate a village
During the war, several villages were violated and left deserted by fleeing residents.
Many felt that the forest had been violated by the unchecked construction and deforestation.
The invading army violated the ancient city, leaving nothing but ruins.
