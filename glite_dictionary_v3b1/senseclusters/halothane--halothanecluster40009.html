halothane

noun
inhalation anesthetic (medical)
3.2
Pronunciation: / ˈhæləˌθeɪn / (AmE), / ˈhæl.ə.θeɪn / (BrE)
A nonflammable inhalation anesthetic used to induce general anesthesia, especially during surgical procedures.

Examples

Halothane anesthetic gas
Doctors replaced halothane with newer anesthetics due to safety concerns.
Inhalation of halothane rapidly induces unconsciousness in surgical patients.
The patient was administered halothane before the operation began.
