mood

noun
general emotional state or atmosphere
2.1 29/m
Pronunciation: / mud / (AmE), / muÀêd / (BrE)
the general emotional feeling or psychological atmosphere of a person, group, or situation at a particular time

Examples

Office mood
Her cheerful mood lifted the spirits of everyone in the office.
The country's political mood was tense following the sudden resignation of the prime minister.
There was a festive mood in the city as people celebrated the holiday.

Synonyms

  • state of mind
    3.4 4.91/m
    state of mind describes a person's mental or emotional condition at a time, while mood covers feelings or atmosphere broadly.
  • emotional state
    3.6 1.19/m
    emotional state names a person's feelings at a particular moment, while mood also includes the general atmosphere around people.
  • frame of mind
    3.7 1.54/m
    frame of mind means the temporary way someone thinks or feels, while mood can mean both a person's feelings and scene atmosphere.
  • feeling
    1.4 15/m
    feeling often names an inner emotion or reaction, while mood refers to a broader lasting personal or group atmosphere.
  • humor
    2.1 1.87/m
    humor describes a short, often old-fashioned state like 'in a good humor', while mood is the common modern word for feelings.
  • atmosphere
    3.2 23/m
    atmosphere describes the character or feeling of a place, while mood also applies to a person's inner emotional state.
  • ambiance
    5.3 1.30/m
    ambiance focuses on the sensory mood of a room or place, while mood can mean both a person's feelings and wider atmosphere.
  • vibe
    6.4 8.23/m
    vibe names the informal, often quick impression you get from a place or person, while mood is more general and standard.
  • spirit
    3.1 42/m
    spirit means lively energy or positive feeling in someone or a group, while mood covers any emotional atmosphere or state.
  • climate
    3.8 21/m
    climate refers to the general attitudes or social mood over time, whereas mood points to a current feeling or atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • synonym: spirit - the prevailing or typical quality, mood, or attitude of a person, group, or period
