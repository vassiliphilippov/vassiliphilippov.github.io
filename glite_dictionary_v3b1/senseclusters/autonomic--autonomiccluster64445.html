autonomic

adjective
relating to the autonomic nervous system
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˌɑː.təˈnɑː.mɪk /; / ˌɔtəˈnɑmɪk / (AmE), / ˌɔː.təˈnɒm.ɪk /; / ˌɔːtəˈnɒmɪk / (BrE)
relating to automatic or involuntary processes, especially those controlled by the autonomic nervous system (such as heartbeat and digestion)

Examples

Autonomic nervous system
His doctor explained that his fainting episodes were related to an autonomic dysfunction.
Some medications can affect autonomic responses, causing changes in blood pressure.
The autonomic nervous system regulates involuntary functions like breathing and heart rate.
The autonomic nervous system regulates heart rate and digestion without conscious effort.
Some diseases affect the autonomic functions of the body, leading to irregular sweating or blood pressure.
Breathing is partly controlled by autonomic processes, especially when you are asleep.

Related Terms

  • synonym: automatic - working by itself with little or no direct human control
  • antonym: voluntary - controlled by conscious will rather than by the autonomic nervous system
