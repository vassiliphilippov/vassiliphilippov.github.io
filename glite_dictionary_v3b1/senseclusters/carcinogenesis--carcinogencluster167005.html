carcinogenesis

noun
formation of cancer
7.5
Pronunciation: / kɑːrsɪnoʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / kɑːsɪnəʊˈdʒɛnɪsɪs / (BrE)
the process by which normal cells are transformed into cancer cells

Examples

Carcinogenesis process
Researchers are studying the steps involved in carcinogenesis to develop better cancer treatments.
Certain chemicals in the environment can trigger carcinogenesis in human tissues.
Understanding carcinogenesis is essential for creating effective cancer prevention strategies.
