ortholog

noun
gene with shared ancestry
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrθəˌlɔɡ / (AmE), / ˈɔːθəlɒɡ / (BrE)
a gene in different species that evolved from a common ancestral gene and retains the same function

Examples

The ortholog gene
Scientists compared the ortholog of this gene in humans and chimpanzees to study evolution.
The mouse ortholog of the human gene is often used in medical research.
Researchers identified an ortholog in fruit flies that performs a similar function.

Related Terms

  • synonym: homolog - a gene related to another by descent from a common ancestor
  • antonym: paralog - a gene related by duplication within a genome
