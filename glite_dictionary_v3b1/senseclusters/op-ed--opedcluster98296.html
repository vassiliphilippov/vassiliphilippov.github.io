op-ed

noun
opinion article
Pronunciation: / ˌɑpˈɛd / (AmE), / ˌɒpˈɛd / (BrE)
a newspaper or magazine article that expresses the opinion of a named writer, not the publication's editorial board

Examples

A newspaper op-ed
She published an op-ed about climate change in the local newspaper.
The politician wrote an op-ed to explain his position on the new law.
Many readers responded to the op-ed with letters to the editor.
She published an op-ed in the newspaper to share her views on climate change.
The magazine's latest op-ed challenges the government's new education policy.
After reading the op-ed, many readers wrote in to express their agreement or disagreement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: column - a regular article or feature written by the same person
  • antonym: editorial - an article expressing the official opinion of a newspaper
  • synonym: letter to the editor - a letter written by a reader expressing opinions or feedback
