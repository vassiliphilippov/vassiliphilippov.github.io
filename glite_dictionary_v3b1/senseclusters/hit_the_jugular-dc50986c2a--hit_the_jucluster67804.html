hit the jugular

verb
address the most critical point
Pronunciation: / hɪt ðə ˈdʒʌɡjələr / (AmE), / hɪt ðə ˈdʒʌɡjʊlə / (BrE)
to address or attack the most vital or critical point of an issue or situation

Examples

To hit the jugular point
If you want to resolve this argument, you need to hit the jugular instead of skirting the issue.
In her critique, she hit the jugular by identifying the fundamental flaw in the proposal.
The prosecutor's questions hit the jugular and left the witness visibly shaken.
