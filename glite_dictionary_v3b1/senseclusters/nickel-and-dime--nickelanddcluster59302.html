nickel-and-dime

adjective
of little importance or value
Pronunciation: / ˌnɪkəl ənd ˈdaɪm /; / ˈnɪkl ənd daɪm / (AmE), / ˌnɪkəl ənd ˈdaɪm /; / ˈnɪkl ənd daɪm / (BrE)
of little importance or value; involving only small or trivial amounts of money

Examples

Nickel-and-dime problems
Don't waste time on those nickel-and-dime issues when bigger problems need attention.
It's just a nickel-and-dime project with no real impact.
They were running a nickel-and-dime business that barely broke even.
He decided it was time to leave behind the nickel-and-dime work and pursue a real career.
Most nickel-and-dime gigs offer no benefits or job security.
She was tired of working nickel-and-dime jobs that barely covered her rent.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lucrative - producing a great deal of profit; highly paid
  • antonym: major - of great importance or significance
  • synonym: menial - (of work) not requiring much skill and lacking prestige
  • synonym: petty - of secondary or lesser importance, minor
  • synonym: trivial - of little value or importance
