zanjero

noun
water official
Pronunciation: / sɑnˈhɛroʊ / (AmE), / sænˈjɛərəʊ / (BrE)
a person (often an official or worker) who manages, oversees, or maintains irrigation ditches or water supply, especially in the southwestern United States

The zanjero checked the flow of water to each field every morning.
Farmers relied on the zanjero to resolve disputes over water distribution.
Becoming a zanjero required knowledge of both the land and the irrigation system.
The farm employed a skilled zanjero to keep the irrigation channels clear.
After the storm, the zanjero worked long hours repairing the damaged ditches.
A zanjero is essential for maintaining water flow in agricultural fields.
