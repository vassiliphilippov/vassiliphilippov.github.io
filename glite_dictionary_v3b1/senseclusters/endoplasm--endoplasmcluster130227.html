endoplasm

noun
inner portion of a cell's cytoplasm
4.8 8.0
Pronunciation: /en.ˈdoʊ.plæ.zəm/ (AmE), /en.ˈdəʊ.plæ.zəm/ (BrE)
the inner, often more fluid part of the cytoplasm of a cell, distinguished from the outer ectoplasm

Examples

The endoplasm inside cells
Amoebas move by streaming their endoplasm forward to create pseudopodia.
The endoplasm contains most of the cell's organelles and plays a crucial role in cell metabolism.
When viewed under a microscope, the distinction between the endoplasm and ectoplasm is often visible in some protozoa.
