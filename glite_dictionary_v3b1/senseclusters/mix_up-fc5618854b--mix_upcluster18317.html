mix up

verb
confuse or perplex
1.0
Pronunciation: / mɪks ʌp / (AmE), / mɪks ʌp / (BrE)
to confuse people or things, or to make things disorganized or unclear

Examples

To mix up names

Please do not mix up the documents on my desk.
The teacher warned the students not to mix up the answer sheets.
Don't mix up the documents, or we will lose track of the originals.
I often mix up the twins because they look so alike.
She accidentally mixed up the salt and sugar when baking the cake.
