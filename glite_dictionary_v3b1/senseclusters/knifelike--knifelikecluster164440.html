knifelike

adjective
extremely sharp or penetrating
2.7
Pronunciation: / ˈnaɪfˌlaɪk / (AmE), / ˈnaɪfˌlaɪk / (BrE)
(of reasoning, remarks, or qualities) extremely sharp, keen, or penetrating, as if cutting like a knife.

Examples

He possessed a knifelike intellect that saw through every deception.
Her knifelike comments quickly silenced the opposition.
The judge's knifelike reasoning exposed flaws in both sides of the argument.
