woo

verb
try to win someone's love or affection
4.6 1.38/m
Pronunciation: / wuÀê / (AmE), / wuÀê / (BrE)
to seek the affection or love of someone, especially with the intention of marriage; to try to win someone's romantic interest

Examples

To woo a girl
He tried to woo her with flowers and romantic poems.
She is clearly being wooed by several suitors at once.
The prince spent months wooing the princess before she agreed to marry him.

Synonyms

  • court
    4.2 1.87/m
    court is a formal, steady way to try to win someone's love with marriage in mind, while woo is less formal and more personal.
  • romance
    5.3 0.53/m
    romance means acting in a flirtatious, charming way to attract someone, while woo focuses on seeking lasting affection or marriage.
  • chase
    4.0 5.36/m
    chase is to pursue someone, often aggressively or for sexual interest, while woo implies gentle courtship with marriage intent.

Related Terms
