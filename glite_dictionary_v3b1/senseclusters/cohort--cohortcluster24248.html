cohort

noun
group of companions or supporters
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊ.hɔrt / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.hɔːt / (BrE)
A group of people who share a common characteristic or are supporters of a particular person, cause, or activity.

Examples

Cohort of supporters
A small cohort of fans waited outside the venue to catch a glimpse of the band.
She and her cohort worked tirelessly to complete the project on time.
The senator arrived with a loyal cohort of advisors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: group - a number of people or things that are together or share something in common
