whipstaff

noun
old ship steering lever
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪpˌstæf / (AmE), / ˈwɪpˌstɑːf / (BrE)
a long wooden lever used to steer a ship before the invention of the ship's wheel

Examples

The whipstaff lever
The captain ordered the sailor to hold the whipstaff steady during the storm.
Before ship's wheels were invented, steering was done with a whipstaff connected to the rudder.
The museum displayed an old whipstaff from a seventeenth-century sailing ship.

Related Terms

  • synonym: helm - the equipment or position used to steer a ship
  • synonym: tiller - a bar used to turn a boat's rudder
