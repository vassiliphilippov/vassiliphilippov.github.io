keelhauling

noun
naval punishment
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkiːlˌhɔlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkiːlˌhɔːlɪŋ / (BrE)
a former naval punishment in which a person was dragged under the keel of a ship as a severe penalty

Examples

Naval keelhauling
In the age of sail, keelhauling was one of the harshest punishments a sailor could face.
The threat of keelhauling kept the crew in strict discipline aboard the old warship.
Historical records describe keelhauling as both dangerous and often fatal for those subjected to it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flogging - a punishment involving whipping or beating
  • antonym: pardon - the act of forgiving or excusing an offense
