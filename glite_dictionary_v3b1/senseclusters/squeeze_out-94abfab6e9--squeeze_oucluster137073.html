squeeze out

verb
extract liquid by pressing
1.0 2.7
Pronunciation: /skwiːz aʊt/ (AmE), /skwiːz aʊt/ (BrE)
to extract a liquid or soft substance from something by pressing or squeezing it

Examples

To squeeze out juice
He carefully squeezed out the lemon to add juice to the recipe.
She squeezed out the towel after rinsing it in cold water.
Try to squeeze out as much water as possible from the sponge before cleaning the surface.
He squeezed out some ketchup from the bottle onto his fries.
She squeezed out a small amount of toothpaste onto her brush.
The artist squeezes out paint from the tube before mixing the colors.
