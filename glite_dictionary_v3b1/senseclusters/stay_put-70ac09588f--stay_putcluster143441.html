stay put

verb
remain in one place
Pronunciation: / steɪ pʊt / (AmE), / steɪ pʊt / (BrE)
to remain in the same place or position and not move or leave

Examples

To stay put safely
If lost in the woods, it's often safest to stay put and wait for help.
We decided to stay put during the storm rather than seek shelter elsewhere.
You should stay put while the doctor examines you.
During the storm, everyone was advised to stay put indoors.
I told the children to stay put while I went to get the car.
If there's an emergency, just stay put until help arrives.

  • antonym: move - to change location or position
  • synonym: remain - to stay in the same place or condition
