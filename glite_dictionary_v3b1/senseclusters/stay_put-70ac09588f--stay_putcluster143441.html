stay put

verb
remain in one place

to remain in the same place or position and not move or leave

Examples

To stay put safely
If lost in the woods, it's often safest to stay put and wait for help.
We decided to stay put during the storm rather than seek shelter elsewhere.
You should stay put while the doctor examines you.
During the storm, everyone was advised to stay put indoors.
I told the children to stay put while I went to get the car.
If there's an emergency, just stay put until help arrives.

Synonyms

  • stay in place
    1.5 0.78/m
    stay in place describes staying physically still in one spot, while stay put emphasizes not moving or leaving that position.
  • stay
    1.0 422/m
    stay often means remaining in a place or condition for any time, while stay put means not moving from a spot.
  • remain
    1.0 316/m
    remain is more formal and can mean staying in a state or continuing to exist, while stay put means not moving physically.
  • stick around
    2.9 7.31/m
    stick around means stay somewhere, often while waiting or casually, whereas stay put stresses staying still and not leaving at all.
