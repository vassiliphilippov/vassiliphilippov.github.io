lovastatin

noun
cholesterol-lowering drug
Pronunciation: / loʊˈvæstətɪn / (AmE), / ləʊˈvæstətɪn / (BrE)
A drug taken orally to lower cholesterol levels in the blood, especially when dietary changes are insufficient.

Examples

Lovastatin tablets
After her cholesterol levels stayed high, the doctor prescribed lovastatin to help reduce her risk of heart disease.
Despite a healthier diet, he required lovastatin to bring his cholesterol down to a safe range.
Patients taking lovastatin should have their liver function monitored periodically by their physician.

Related Terms

  • synonym: atorvastatin - a statin drug used to lower cholesterol
  • synonym: simvastatin - another statin drug used to lower cholesterol
