porcupine fish

noun
spiny fish that inflates its body when threatened
Pronunciation: / ˈpɔːrkjupaɪn fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈpɔːkjʊpʌɪn fɪʃ / (BrE)
A warm-water marine fish with spines that become erect when its body is inflated as a defense mechanism.

Examples

A porcupine fish inflates
Divers encountered a porcupine fish hiding among the coral reefs.
Many species of porcupine fish are found in tropical and subtropical seas around the world.
The porcupine fish inflates its body and raises its spines when threatened by predators.
