subhedral

adjective
partly formed faces
Pronunciation: / sʌbˈhiːdrəl / (AmE), / sʌbˈhiːdrəl / (BrE)
describing a mineral crystal that is partly but not fully bounded by its natural crystal faces

Examples

Subhedral mineral crystal
The geologist identified several subhedral grains in the thin section of the rock.
Under the microscope, the mineral appeared subhedral rather than perfectly shaped.
Most crystals in the sample were subhedral, showing only some natural faces.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: anhedral - lacking well-formed crystal faces
  • degree higher: euhedral - having well-formed, fully developed crystal faces
