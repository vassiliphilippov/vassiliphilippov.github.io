bay grass

noun
a grass of the genus Eragrostis, used for forage and erosion control
Pronunciation: / beɪ ɡræs / (AmE), / beɪ ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
Any of several grasses of the genus Eragrostis, especially valued for forage and for preventing soil erosion.

Examples

Bay grass plants
Efforts to reduce erosion along the riverbank involved seeding the area with bay grass.
Farmers often use bay grass as a nutritious forage for livestock.
The wetland restoration project included planting bay grass to stabilize the shoreline.
