off-axis reflector

noun
tilted-mirror reflecting telescope
9.9 7.4
Pronunciation: /ɔːf ˈæk.sɪs rɪ.ˈflek.tər/ /; / /ɑːf ˈæk.sɪs rɪ.ˈflek.tər/ (AmE), /ɒf ˈæk.sɪs rɪ.ˈflek.tə/ /; / /ɔːf ˈæk.sɪs rɪ.ˈflek.tə/ (BrE)
A reflecting telescope with its primary mirror tilted so that the image is directed to the side, allowing it to be observed without obstruction.

Examples

An off-axis reflector telescope
Astronomers chose an off-axis reflector to improve the clarity of their observations.
Light enters the off-axis reflector and is directed to a side-mounted eyepiece.
The off-axis reflector design reduces optical obstruction from the secondary mirror.
