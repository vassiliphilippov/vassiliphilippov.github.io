affinity

noun
natural liking or close connection
7.0
Pronunciation: / əˈfɪnəti / (AmE), / əˈfɪnɪti /; / əˈfɪnəti / (BrE)
a natural liking, attraction, or connection between people, things, or ideas

Examples

Strong affinity for music
Many people feel a strong affinity with nature when spending time outdoors.
She has always felt an affinity for classical music, even as a child.
There is a deep affinity between the two artists that is reflected in their work.
Researchers discovered an unexpected affinity between the two species based on shared behaviors.
She felt an immediate affinity with her new colleagues, as if they had known each other for years.
There is a strong affinity between classical music and poetry in their expressive power.
Her affinity for classical art is evident in her paintings.
Some animals share an affinity that is reflected in their behavior and appearance.
There was an immediate affinity between the two musicians when they met.
