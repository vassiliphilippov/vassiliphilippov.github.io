adactylous

adjective
lacking fingers or toes
Pronunciation: / əˈdæktɪləs / (AmE), / əˈdaktɪləs / (BrE)
Lacking fingers and/or toes.

Examples

Adactylous animals
Several species of salamanders are naturally adactylous, lacking digits on their limbs.
The medical report confirmed that the patient was adactylous as a result of a congenital anomaly.
The rare genetic disorder caused the child to be adactylous on both hands.

Related Terms

  • antonym: polydactylous - having more than the usual number of fingers or toes
  • antonym: syndactylous - having fused or webbed fingers or toes
