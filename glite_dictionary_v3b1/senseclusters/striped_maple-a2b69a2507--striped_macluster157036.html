striped maple

noun
a North American maple with striped bark
4.3 5.9
Pronunciation: /straɪpt ˈmeɪ.pəl/ (AmE), /straɪpt ˈmeɪ.pəl/ (BrE)
a maple tree native to eastern North America, recognized by its distinctive striped bark and large two-lobed leaves that turn clear yellow in autumn

Examples

Striped maple tree
Hikers often spot striped maple saplings growing in the shady understory of eastern forests.
In autumn, the leaves of the striped maple turn a brilliant clear yellow.
The striped maple is easily identified by the white stripes on its green bark.
