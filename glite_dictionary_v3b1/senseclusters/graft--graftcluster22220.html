graft

noun
dishonest or illegal gain, especially through bribery
9.2
Pronunciation: / ɡræft / (AmE), / ɡrɑːft / (BrE)
the use of dishonest or illegal methods, especially bribery, to gain an advantage, usually in politics or business

Examples

Political graft
Many citizens lost faith in the government due to reports of graft and corruption.
She refused to participate in any graft, even when pressured by her colleagues.
The investigation uncovered widespread graft among local officials.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bribery - the offering of money or favors to influence someone in authority
  • synonym: corruption - dishonest or fraudulent conduct by those in power
