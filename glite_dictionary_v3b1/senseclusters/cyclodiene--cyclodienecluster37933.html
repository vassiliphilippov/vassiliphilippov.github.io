cyclodiene

noun
cyclic diene compound
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪkloʊˈdaɪiːn / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkləʊˈdaɪiːn / (BrE)
a cyclic hydrocarbon molecule containing two double bonds within the ring structure

Examples

A cyclodiene molecule
The chemist isolated a cyclodiene as an intermediate during the reaction.
Many pesticides are based on the cyclodiene structure due to its chemical stability.
A cyclodiene can participate in Diels-Alder reactions to form complex ring systems.
