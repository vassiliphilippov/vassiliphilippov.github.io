Amphibolips

proper noun
genus of gall wasps causing oak-apple galls
Pronunciation: / ˌæmfəˈboʊlɪps / (AmE), / ˌamfɪˈbəʊlɪps / (BrE)
A genus of gall wasps (family Cynipidae) known for causing oak-apple galls, particularly on oak trees.

Examples

Amphibolips on oaks
Researchers studied the impact of Amphibolips infestations on local oak populations.
Several species of Amphibolips are known to induce large, round galls on oak trees.
The life cycle of Amphibolips includes a larval stage that develops within the gall.
