focal length

noun
distance from lens to focus
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊkəl lɛŋkθ / (AmE), / ˈfəʊkəl lɛŋkθ / (BrE)
the distance between the center of a lens or curved mirror and its focus, where parallel rays converge.

Examples

A focal length distance
A lens with a short focal length provides a wider field of view than one with a longer focal length.
Changing the focal length affects the magnification and perspective of your photographs.
The camera's focal length determines how much of the scene will be captured in the frame.

Related Terms

  • synonym: focus - the point where rays converge after passing through a lens
