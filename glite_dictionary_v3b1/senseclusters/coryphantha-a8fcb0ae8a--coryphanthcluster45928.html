Coryphantha

noun
genus of globular southwestern cacti
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkɔːrɪˈfænθə / (AmE), / ˌkɒrɪˈfanθə / (BrE)
A genus of mostly globular cacti native to the southwestern United States and Mexico, characterized by many tubercles and formerly included in the genus Mammillaria.

Examples

The Coryphantha cactus
Botanists often distinguish Coryphantha from Mammillaria by the structure of their tubercles.

The Coryphantha species thrive in arid regions of Mexico and the American Southwest.
