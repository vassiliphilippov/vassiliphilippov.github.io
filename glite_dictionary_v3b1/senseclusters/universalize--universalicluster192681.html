universalize

verb
make universal; generalize widely
Pronunciation: / juˌnɪvərsəˌlaɪz /; / juˌnɪvərsəˈlaɪz / (AmE), / juːˈnɪːvəsəlaɪz /; / juːˌnɪvəːsəˈlaɪz / (BrE)
To make something universal; to give a general or all-encompassing quality or application to.

Examples

To universalize ideas
Her films universalize personal experiences, making them relatable to audiences worldwide.
Many attempts to universalize Western values have met with resistance in different cultures.
The philosopher sought to universalize basic human rights in his treatise.
Great philosophers often try to universalise specific human experiences in their theories.
She hopes her novel will universalise the struggles faced by people in her community.
The company attempted to universalise its standards across all international branches.

Related Terms

  • synonym: generalize - make something more general or widespread
  • antonym: particularize - to make particular or specific
