prestige

noun
respect or admiration from success or status
5.1 7.86/m
Pronunciation: / pr…õÀàsti í / (AmE), / pr…õÀàstiÀê í / (BrE)
the respect and admiration that someone or something receives because of their success, influence, or wealth

Examples

High prestige
He accepted the position mainly for the prestige it brought, not for the salary.
The company enjoys considerable prestige among tech enthusiasts.
Winning the award gave the scientist great prestige in her field.

Synonyms

  • high standing
    4.3 0.14/m
    high standing means a recognized position of respect within a group, while prestige is broader public admiration linked to success or wealth.
  • renown
    5.5 0.95/m
    renown means being widely known and admired for achievements, while prestige covers respect from success, influence, or wealth more generally.
  • eminence
    7.9 0.77/m
    eminence is high distinction within a field or profession, while prestige is wider social respect tied to status or wealth.
  • stature
    5.9 3.81/m
    stature means a person's perceived importance or reputation after achievement, while prestige focuses on admired status from success or influence.
  • repute
    6.7 0.51/m
    repute is the general opinion people hold about someone, while prestige is the respected, admired status from success or wealth.
