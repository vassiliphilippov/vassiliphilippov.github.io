aeromechanical

adjective
aerodynamics and mechanics
Pronunciation: / ˌɛroʊməˈkænɪkəl /; / ˌɛəroʊməˈkænɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌeərəʊmɪˈkænɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the mechanical properties or behavior of air or gases, especially as it affects structures or engineering (such as in aircraft or turbines)

Examples

Aeromechanical properties
The team analyzed the aeromechanical properties of the new wind turbine blades.
Understanding aeromechanical effects is crucial for designing safe helicopters.
The failure was traced to an unexpected aeromechanical interaction in the engine system.
The aeromechanical properties of the new material make it ideal for use in aircraft construction.
Engineers studied the aeromechanical effects of wind on the bridge's structure.
Advances in aeromechanical engineering have led to more efficient wind turbines.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aerodynamic - relating to the movement of air and its interaction with solid objects
  • synonym: mechanical - relating to machines or the movement of objects
