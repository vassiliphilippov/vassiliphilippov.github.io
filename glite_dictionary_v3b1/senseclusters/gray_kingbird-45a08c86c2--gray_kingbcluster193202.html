gray kingbird

noun
a large flycatcher bird of the Americas
4.7
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪ ˈkɪŋˌbɝd / (AmE), / ɡreɪ ˈkɪŋˌbɜːd / (BrE)
a large kingbird (Tyrannus dominicensis) of the tyrant flycatcher family, native to the southeastern United States and tropical America (including the Caribbean); known for its gray plumage and aggressive behavior; breeds in the southeastern US and winters in tropical America, similar to but larger than the eastern kingbird

Examples

The gray kingbird migrates
The gray kingbird migrates from the southeastern United States to the Caribbean each winter.
Unlike the eastern kingbird, the gray kingbird has a slightly larger body and a heavier bill.
We spotted a gray kingbird perched on a wire near the beach.
During migration season, we spotted several grey kingbirds along the coast.
The grey kingbird is often seen perched on telephone wires in southern Florida.
Unlike the eastern kingbird, the grey kingbird has a stouter beak and a more robust body.
