perjurious

adjective
deceitful; untrustworthy
9.1
Pronunciation: / pərˈdʒʊriəs / (AmE), / pəˈdʒʊəriəs / (BrE)
relating to lying or making false statements, especially under oath

Examples

Perjurious statement
The witness gave a perjurious statement, misleading the entire court.
His perjurious actions destroyed the trust his friends had in him.
Voters were disappointed by the candidate's perjurious campaign promises.
The lawyer accused the defendant of making a perjurious statement during the hearing.
A perjurious witness can undermine the entire case in court.
Giving perjurious testimony is a serious crime with severe penalties.

Related Terms

  • antonym: faithful - loyal and trustworthy
  • synonym: perjured - having lied under oath
  • antonym: truthful - telling the truth; honest
