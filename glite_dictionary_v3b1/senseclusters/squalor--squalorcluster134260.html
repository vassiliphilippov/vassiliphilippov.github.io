squalor

noun
extreme dirtiness and neglect
Pronunciation: / ˈskwɑːlər / (AmE), / ˈskwɒlə / (BrE)
a state of being extremely dirty, unpleasant, and often connected with poverty or neglect

Examples

Living in squalor
He was shocked by the squalor he witnessed in the city's poorest neighborhoods.
The refugees lived in unbearable squalor, with trash piling up in every corner.
Years of neglect had reduced the once-grand mansion to a state of squalor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cleanliness - the state of being clean
  • synonym: filth - extreme dirt or foul matter
