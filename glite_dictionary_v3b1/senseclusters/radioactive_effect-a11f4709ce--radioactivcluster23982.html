radioactive effect

noun
significant, potentially harmful impact (figurative)
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪdioʊˈæktɪv ɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊˈæktɪv ɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
a significant and potentially harmful or explosive impact, especially in a figurative or metaphorical sense

Examples

Her resignation had a radioactive effect on the company's reputation.
The leaked memo created a radioactive effect throughout the department, making everyone uneasy.
The politician's speech had a radioactive effect on the upcoming election campaign.
