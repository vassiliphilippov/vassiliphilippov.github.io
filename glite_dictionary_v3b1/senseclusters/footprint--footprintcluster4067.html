footprint

noun
measure of greenhouse-gas emissions
3.8
Pronunciation: /ˈfʊt.prɪnt/ (AmE), /ˈfʊt.prɪnt/ (BrE)
the total greenhouse-gas emissions caused directly or indirectly by a person, organisation, product, or activity; often expressed as carbon dioxide equivalent and called a 'carbon footprint'.

Examples

Carbon footprint
Companies monitor their carbon footprint to identify emissions reduction opportunities.
The city's new policy reduced the industrial footprint by investing in clean technology.
Calculating your household's footprint helped them choose greener travel and energy options.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carbon footprint - the amount of carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gases emitted by an activity or product
  • synonym: environmental impact - the effect that an activity or product has on the natural environment
