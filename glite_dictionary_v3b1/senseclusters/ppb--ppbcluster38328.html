parts per billion

Pronunciation: / pi pi ˈbi / (AmE), / piː piː ˈbiː / (BrE)
an abbreviation for parts per billion, a unit used to measure the concentration of a substance in a solution or mixture

Examples

ppb measurement
The laboratory detected only 2 ppb of mercury in the fish sample.
Regulations limit the amount of arsenic in drinking water to 10 ppb.
Scientists measured the pollutant at 0.5 ppb in the atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: ppm - parts per million, a unit for measuring concentration
  • degree lower: ppt - parts per trillion, a unit for measuring very low concentrations
