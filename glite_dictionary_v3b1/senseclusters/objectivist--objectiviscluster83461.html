objectivist

noun
believer in objective truth
9.4
Pronunciation: / əbˈdʒɛktɪvɪst / (AmE), / əbˈdʒɛktɪvɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that truths and values exist independently of personal feelings or opinions, especially an adherent of Ayn Rand's philosophy

Examples

Ayn Rand objectivist
As an objectivist, she argued that moral values are not just personal preferences.
The debate between the objectivist and the subjectivist became quite intense during the philosophy seminar.
He identified as an objectivist after reading several books by Ayn Rand.

Related Terms

  • synonym: realist - a person who accepts things as they are and deals with them practically
  • antonym: subjectivist - a person who believes that knowledge and values depend on personal feelings or opinions
