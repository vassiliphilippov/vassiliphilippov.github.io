fossil fuel

noun
fuel from ancient organic remains
1.0 2.8
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑsəl ˌfjuːəl / (AmE), / ˈfɒsl ˌfjʊəl / (BrE)
A fuel such as coal, oil, or natural gas, formed from the remains of ancient plants and animals, used as an energy source.

Examples

Common fossil fuels
Coal, oil, and natural gas are common types of fossil fuels.
Many power plants still rely on fossil fuels to generate electricity.
The shift away from fossil fuels is essential to combat climate change.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coal - a combustible black or brownish-black sedimentary rock used as fuel
  • synonym: natural gas - a flammable gas, consisting largely of methane, occurring naturally underground and used as fuel
  • synonym: oil - a viscous liquid derived from petroleum, especially for use as a fuel
  • antonym: renewable energy - energy from a source that is not depleted when used, such as wind or solar power
