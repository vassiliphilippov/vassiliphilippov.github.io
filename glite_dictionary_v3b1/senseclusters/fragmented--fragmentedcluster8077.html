fragmented

adjective
broken into parts; not unified
Pronunciation: / ˈfræɡ.mən.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈfræɡ.mənt.ɪd / (BrE)
broken into separate parts or sections; no longer whole or unified

Examples

A fragmented system
After the company reorganization, communication became more fragmented across the various departments.
Her memories of childhood were fragmented and difficult to piece together.
The nation was politically fragmented following the long period of conflict.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disjointed - lacking a coherent sequence or connection.
  • antonym: unified - formed or united into a whole.
