Shuha Shinto

noun
sectarian branches of Shinto
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈʃuːhɑː ˈʃɪnˌtoʊ / (AmE), / ˈʃuːhɑː ˈʃɪntəʊ / (BrE)
Any branch of Shinto other than State Shinto (Kokka Shinto); refers to the sectarian forms of Shinto in Japan.

Examples

Shuha Shinto sects
Many scholars have studied the differences between Shuha Shinto and Kokka Shinto.
Practices within Shuha Shinto often emphasize personal faith and local traditions.
Religious festivals in Japan can be influenced by various branches, including Shuha Shinto.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Kokka Shinto - State Shinto; government-endorsed form of Shinto in Japan
