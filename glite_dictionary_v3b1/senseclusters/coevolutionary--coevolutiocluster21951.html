coevolutionary

adjective
mutual evolutionary influence
8.9
Pronunciation: /koʊ.iː.və.ˈluː.ʃə.riː/ (AmE), /kəʊ.iː.və.ˈluː.ʃə.riː/ (BrE)
relating to the process where two or more species, systems, or groups change and adapt in response to each other over time

Examples

A coevolutionary process
The coevolutionary arms race between predators and prey can lead to remarkable adaptations.
Scientists study coevolutionary dynamics to understand how species interactions shape biodiversity.
A coevolutionary process can be seen in the relationship between certain birds and the plants they pollinate.
Scientists observed coevolutionary changes between flowering plants and their pollinators over many generations.
The study focused on coevolutionary dynamics in competing technological platforms.
Researchers developed a coevolutionary algorithm to solve complex optimization problems.

Related Terms

  • antonym: independent - not influenced by others
  • synonym: reciprocal - involving mutual action or influence
