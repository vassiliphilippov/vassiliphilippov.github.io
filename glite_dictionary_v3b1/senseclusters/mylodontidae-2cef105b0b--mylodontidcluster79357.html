Mylodontidae

noun
extinct family of South American sloths
Pronunciation: / maɪˌloʊdɒnˈtɪdiː / (AmE), / maɪˌləʊdɒnˈtɪdiː / (BrE)
an extinct family of large, ground-dwelling South American sloths belonging to the order Pilosa

Examples

The Mylodontidae family
Fossil evidence suggests that Mylodontidae survived in South America until the end of the Pleistocene.
Researchers have found remains of Mylodontidae in caves throughout Argentina and Brazil.
The Mylodontidae were among the largest ground sloths to ever roam the continent.
