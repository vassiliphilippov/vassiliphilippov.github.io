awry

adjective, adverb
not working properly; out of order
9.8
Pronunciation: / əˈraɪ / (AmE), / əˈraɪ / (BrE)
not as planned or intended; in a state of disorder or failure

Examples

Plans go awry
Something must be awry with the computer because it keeps restarting on its own.
The negotiations went awry after a series of misunderstandings.
When the plan went awry, the team had to improvise quickly.
Despite careful preparation, the project still went awry in the final stages.
If anything goes awry during the event, please let me know immediately.
When the weather suddenly changed, our camping trip plans went awry.

  • synonym: wrong - not functioning properly; incorrect or faulty
