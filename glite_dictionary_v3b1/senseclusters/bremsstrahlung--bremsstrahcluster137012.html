bremsstrahlung

noun
radiation from deceleration

electromagnetic radiation produced when a charged particle is rapidly slowed down or deflected, especially as it passes near an atomic nucleus

Examples

Bremsstrahlung radiation
The X-ray tube produces bremsstrahlung as electrons are decelerated by the metal target.
Physicists study bremsstrahlung to understand how high-energy particles lose energy in matter.
Medical imaging devices often rely on bremsstrahlung to generate X-rays for diagnostic purposes.
X-ray tubes generate bremsstrahlung as electrons hit the metal target.
Scientists studied the bremsstrahlung produced by high-energy particles in the laboratory.
The intensity of bremsstrahlung depends on the speed and charge of the incoming particle.

Related Terms
