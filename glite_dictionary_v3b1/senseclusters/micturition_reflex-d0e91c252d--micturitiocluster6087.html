micturition reflex

noun
bladder reflex for urination
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪktʃəˈrɪʃ(ə)n rɪˈflɛks / (AmE), / ˌmɪktjʊˈrɪʃ(ə)n rɪˈflɛks / (BrE)
the reflex by which increased pressure in the bladder causes relaxation of the urethral sphincter, allowing urination

Examples

The micturition reflex action
Damage to the nerves controlling the micturition reflex can lead to urinary retention or incontinence.
During infancy, urination is primarily regulated by the micturition reflex rather than voluntary control.
The micturition reflex is activated when the bladder becomes full, resulting in a strong urge to urinate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: urination - the act of passing urine from the bladder
