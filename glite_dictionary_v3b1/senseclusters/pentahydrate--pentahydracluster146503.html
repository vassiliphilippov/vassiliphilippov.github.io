pentahydrate

adjective
with five water molecules
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛntəˈhaɪdreɪt / (AmE), / ˌpɛntəˈhaɪdreɪt / (BrE)
containing five molecules of water of crystallization in its structure, especially in a chemical compound

Examples

Pentahydrate salt crystals
Copper(II) sulfate pentahydrate is a common blue chemical used in laboratories.
The label indicated that the compound was a pentahydrate rather than an anhydrous form.
When heated, the pentahydrate loses its water molecules and changes color.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: dihydrate - containing two molecules of water of crystallization
  • degree higher: hexahydrate - containing six molecules of water of crystallization
  • degree lower: monohydrate - containing one molecule of water of crystallization
