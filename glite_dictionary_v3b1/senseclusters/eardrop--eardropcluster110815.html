eardrop

noun
medicine for ears
Pronunciation: / ˈɪrˌdrɑp / (AmE), / ˈɪəˌdrɒp / (BrE)
a liquid medicine that is put into the ear to treat pain, infection, or other ear problems

Examples

Use eardrops for infection
The doctor recommended eardrops to help clear up the infection.
She used eardrops every morning to relieve the pain in her ear.
After swimming, he put eardrops in his ears to prevent discomfort.
