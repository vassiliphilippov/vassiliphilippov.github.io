durable

adjective
persisting through failures (computing)
5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈdʊr.ə.bəl/ /; / /ˈdjʊr.ə.bəl/ (AmE), /ˈdjʊə.rə.bəl/ /; / /ˈdʊə.rə.bəl/ (BrE)
(in computing) remaining intact and retrievable after hardware or software failures; referring to data, storage, or messages that are guaranteed to persist.

Examples

Durable data storage
Cloud providers advertise durable object storage that keeps files intact despite hardware failures.
In distributed databases, a commit is considered durable when it survives node crashes and restarts.
Developers choose synchronous replication to ensure messages remain durable even if a server fails.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ephemeral - lasting for only a short time; not stored long-term
  • synonym: persistent - continuing to exist or be accessible over time; surviving failures or restarts
