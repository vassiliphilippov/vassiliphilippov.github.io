promachos

noun
frontline champion
Pronunciation: / proʊˈmɑːkɒs / (AmE), / prəʊˈmækɒs / (BrE)
an ancient Greek champion or warrior who fought at the front of the army

Examples

Greek promachos statue
The ancient city erected a statue of a promachos to honor its bravest defender.
In Greek mythology, Athena was sometimes called a promachos for leading warriors into battle.
The promachos inspired the troops by standing at the very front of the formation.

  • synonym: warrior - a person who fights in battles or wars
