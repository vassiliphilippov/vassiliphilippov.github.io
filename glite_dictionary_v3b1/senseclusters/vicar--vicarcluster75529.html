vicar

noun
priest in charge of a parish (Church of England)
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪkər / (AmE), / ˈvɪkə / (BrE)
a Christian priest or clergyman, especially one in charge of a church or acting as a representative of a higher authority

Examples

The vicar of church
After the retirement of the previous priest, a new vicar was appointed to the parish.
Our local vicar organizes community events throughout the year.
The vicar delivered an inspiring sermon at Sunday’s service.
As a vicar, he performed many pastoral duties on behalf of the cardinal.
The vicar administered the parish while the bishop was away.
The vicar reported directly to the archbishop during his service.
After the previous vicar retired, the church appointed a new leader for the chapel.
Many parishioners sought counsel from their vicar during difficult times.
The vicar delivered a thoughtful sermon to the congregation last Sunday.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: bishop - a senior member of the Christian clergy, often responsible for a diocese
  • degree lower: curate - assistant clergyman in a parish church
  • synonym: deputy - a person who acts as a substitute for someone else
  • synonym: rector - priest in charge of a parish, especially one entitled to tithes (in Anglican tradition)
