law of equivalent proportions

noun
chemistry law about combining proportions of elements
8.1
Pronunciation: / lɔ əv ɪˈkwɪvələnt pɹəˈpɔrʃənz / (AmE), / lɔː əv ɪˈkwɪvələnt prəˈpɔːʃənz / (BrE)
(Chemistry) A law stating that if two elements separately combine with a third element, the ratio in which they do so will also be the ratio in which they combine with each other.

Examples

The law of equivalent proportions
According to the law of equivalent proportions, the ratio in which two elements combine with a third will also apply when those two combine with each other.
Dalton cited the law of equivalent proportions as evidence supporting atomic theory.
The law of equivalent proportions helped chemists understand relationships between elements in the early 19th century.

Related Terms

  • synonym: law of definite proportions - the law stating chemical compounds have constant composition by mass
  • synonym: law of multiple proportions - the law concerning ratios of masses in compounds formed by the same elements
