submarining

noun
seat belt sliding
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌbməˈriːnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌsʌbməˈriːnɪŋ / (BrE)
the forward sliding of a vehicle occupant under the lap part of a seat belt during a collision, increasing injury risk

Examples

Seat belt submarining
Proper seat belt positioning can help prevent submarining in the event of a car crash.
Engineers redesigned the seat to reduce the risk of submarining during collisions.
Crash tests revealed frequent submarining of the dummy when the belt was positioned too high.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: ejection - being thrown from a vehicle during a crash
  • synonym: whiplash - neck injury caused by sudden movement in a collision
