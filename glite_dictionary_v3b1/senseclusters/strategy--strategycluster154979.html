strategy

noun
military science of war planning
Pronunciation: / ˈstræt̬.ə.dʒi / (AmE), / ˈstræt.ə.dʒi / (BrE)
The branch of military science concerned with the planning, directing, and execution of large-scale operations and the overall conduct of a war.

Examples

Military strategy
Military academies often teach the theory and application of strategy in warfare.
The general was renowned for his innovative strategy during the campaign.
Understanding the strategy of opposing forces was critical to the outcome of the battle.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: tactics - the art of arranging and directing immediate military operations
