chaulmoogra

noun
East Indian tree, source of medicinal oil
8.2
Pronunciation: / tʃɔlˈmuːɡrə / (AmE), / tʃɔːlˈmuːɡrə / (BrE)
An East Indian tree (Hydnocarpus species) whose oily seeds produce chaulmoogra oil, historically used to treat leprosy and other skin diseases.

Examples

A chaulmoogra tree
A plantation of chaulmoogra trees grew along the riverbank.
Researchers studied the therapeutic uses of chaulmoogra in traditional medicine.
The seeds of the chaulmoogra were once the primary source of oil for treating leprosy.
