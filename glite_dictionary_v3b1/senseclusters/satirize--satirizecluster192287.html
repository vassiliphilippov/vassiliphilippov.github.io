satirize

verb
criticize with satire
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈsæt̬əˌraɪz /; / ˈsætəˌraɪz / (AmE), / ˈsætəraɪz / (BrE)
to use satire to criticize, ridicule, or mock someone or something, often by exaggeration or irony and usually in a humorous way

Examples

To satirize celebrity culture
Her novel satirizes celebrity culture with biting wit and clever observations.
Many cartoons satirize aspects of modern society through exaggeration and humor.
The comedian skillfully satirized the government's new policy in his latest show.
Cartoonists often satirise political figures to highlight their flaws.
The novel skillfully satirises the excesses of celebrity culture.
The play satirised the government’s response to the crisis with sharp wit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mock - to tease or make fun of someone or something
  • synonym: parody - to imitate in a humorous or satirical way
  • antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration
  • synonym: satirize - to ridicule with satire (American spelling)
