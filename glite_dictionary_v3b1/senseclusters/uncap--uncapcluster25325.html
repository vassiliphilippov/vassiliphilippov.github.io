uncap

verb
to remove a limit
4.8
Pronunciation: /ʌn.ˈkæp/ (AmE), /ʌn.ˈkæp/ (BrE)
to remove a cap, limit, or maximum restriction from something, such as prices, fees, or salaries

Examples

To uncap prices
The government decided to uncap tuition fees, allowing universities to charge more.
After years of restrictions, the company will uncap employee bonuses next year.
Some argue that to encourage competition, we should uncap the number of available licenses.
