humbuggery

noun
deceptive behavior
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌmbʌɡəri / (AmE), / ˈhʌmbʌɡəri / (BrE)
deceptive or dishonest behavior intended to mislead or trick people

Examples

False humbuggery
The audience quickly saw through the magician's humbuggery and demanded a real performance.
She accused the salesman of humbuggery when his product did not work as promised.
Many people are tired of political humbuggery during election campaigns.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deceit - the act of deceiving or misleading someone
  • synonym: fraud - wrongful or criminal deception intended to result in financial or personal gain
  • antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and free from deceit
