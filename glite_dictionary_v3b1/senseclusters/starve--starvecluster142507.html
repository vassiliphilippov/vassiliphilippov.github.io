starve

verb
crave intensely; have a great desire
4.3 1.78/m
Pronunciation: / st…ërv / (AmE), / st…ëÀêv / (BrE)
to have a strong craving or intense desire for something, especially in a figurative sense

Examples

Starving for attention
After weeks without a vacation, I'm starving for a break from work.
She was starving for affection in that lonely new city.
The children were starving for attention after their parents got home.

Synonyms

  • crave
    3.8 7.11/m
    crave is to want something very strongly and often briefly, while starve suggests a much more desperate, long-lasting desire.
  • thirst
    5.2 0.33/m
    thirst is a strong figurative longing often used for knowledge or goals, while starve feels like urgent lack.
  • hunger
    3.9 0.69/m
    hunger is a general strong need or desire that can be a noun, while starve implies an extreme, often figurative craving.
