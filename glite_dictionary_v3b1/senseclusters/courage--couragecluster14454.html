courage

noun
bravery; ability to face danger or pain
1.4 40/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ùÀê…™d í / (AmE), / Ààk år…™d í / (BrE)
The ability to do something that frightens one or face pain or danger without showing fear.

Examples

Show courage in danger
Despite his fear, he found the courage to speak in front of the large audience.
It takes a lot of courage to admit when you are wrong.
The firefighters demonstrated remarkable courage during the rescue operation.

Synonyms

  • bravery
    2.3 4.47/m
    bravery is the visible willingness to face danger or pain openly, while courage is the inner ability to act despite fear.
  • dauntlessness
    8.1 0.01/m
    dauntlessness is fearless, steady determination under pressure, while courage is the broader ability to act despite feeling afraid.
  • intrepidity
    9.9 0.06/m
    intrepidity is bold, often formal bravery used in stories or reports, while courage is the everyday power to face fear.
  • valiance
    8.9 0.00/m
    valiance is great, heroic bravery seen in battle or stories, while courage covers any act of facing fear.
