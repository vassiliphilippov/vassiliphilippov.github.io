rheumatoid spondylitis

noun
chronic spinal inflammation disease
5.4 7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈruː.mə.tɔɪd spɑn.dɪ.ˈlaɪ.tɪs/ (AmE), /ˈruː.mə.tɔɪd spɒn.dɪ.ˈlaɪ.tɪs/ (BrE)
A chronic inflammatory disease, chiefly affecting men, characterized by reduced mobility of the spine and sometimes leading to spinal fusion (ankylosis).

Examples

Chronic rheumatoid spondylitis disease
Early diagnosis of rheumatoid spondylitis can delay progression and preserve spinal flexibility.
His limited movement was due to advanced rheumatoid spondylitis affecting his vertebrae.
Patients with rheumatoid spondylitis often report stiffness and pain in the lower back.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ankylosing spondylitis - a type of arthritis featuring chronic spinal inflammation and potential fusion
