ruse

noun
deceptive trick or tactic
7.7 2.33/m
Pronunciation: / ruÀêz / (AmE), / ruÀêz / (BrE)
A clever trick or plan intended to deceive someone.

Examples

A clever ruse
She saw through his ruse and refused to give him the information he wanted.
The thief used a clever ruse to distract the guards and escape.
Their apparent disagreement was actually a ruse to trick the competition.

Synonyms

  • artifice
    8.1 1.70/m
    artifice is a formal, clever trick used to hide intent or gain an edge, while ruse is a general trick to deceive.
  • stratagem
    8.5 0.64/m
    stratagem is a planned clever move used to outwit an opponent in strategy, while ruse is a simpler trick to deceive.
  • subterfuge
    9.5 1.11/m
    subterfuge is a secret or dishonest plan used to hide true purpose or excuse actions, while ruse is a straightforward trick.
  • cunning plan
    5.0 0.10/m
    cunning plan is an informal phrase for a clever scheme often said with admiration, while ruse stresses deception.

Related Terms

  • antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest and free of deceit
  • synonym: stratagem - a plan or scheme, especially one used to outwit an opponent
  • synonym: trick - a cunning or deceitful act or scheme
