grumbly

adjective
low rumbling sound
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrʌmbli / (AmE), / ˈɡrʌmbli / (BrE)
making or characterized by a low, deep, rumbling sound, often suggesting discontent or vibration.

Examples

Grumbly engine noise
The old truck's grumbly engine warned us it needed maintenance before the trip.
After skipping breakfast, his stomach became noticeably grumbly during the meeting.
The grumbly voice on the phone made it hard to understand the message.

Related Terms

  • antonym: quiet - making little or no noise
  • synonym: rumbling - making a deep, continuous sound
