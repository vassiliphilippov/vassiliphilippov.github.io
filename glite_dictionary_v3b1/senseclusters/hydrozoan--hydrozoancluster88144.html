hydrozoan

noun
aquatic animal of class Hydrozoa
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪdrəˈzoʊən / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪdrəʊˈzəʊən / (BrE)
a small, usually colonial aquatic animal of the class Hydrozoa, characterized by a dominant polyp stage in its life cycle

Examples

A hydrozoan colony
Scientists discovered a new species of hydrozoan in the deep ocean.
The hydrozoan colony formed a delicate branching structure on the coral.
Unlike jellyfish, the hydrozoan spends most of its life in the polyp stage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coelenterate - an aquatic invertebrate of the phylum Cnidaria
