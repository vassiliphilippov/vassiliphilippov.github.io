ESP

noun
supposed paranormal perception
8.8 0.77/m
Pronunciation: / Àåi Àå…õs Ààpi / (AmE), / ÀåiÀê Àå…õs ÀàpiÀê / (BrE)
the ability to gain information about objects, people, or events without using the known senses; extrasensory perception

Examples

ESP ability
Some people claim to have ESP and can sense things others cannot.
Television shows about the paranormal often feature characters with ESP.
The scientist remained skeptical about the existence of ESP despite repeated experiments.

Synonyms

  • extrasensory perception
    8.7 0.08/m
    extrasensory perception is the full phrase used in formal writing, while ESP is its common abbreviation used in speech and headlines.
  • clairvoyance
    9.8 0.35/m
    clairvoyance is the claimed power to learn about distant events or objects, while ESP names this general extrasensory ability.
  • second sight
    9.9 0.14/m
    second sight is a folk term often for foreseeing the future, while ESP is the broader, modern label for extrasensory ability.
  • sixth sense
    7.6 0.41/m
    sixth sense is a casual phrase for instinct or intuition, while ESP refers to a claimed paranormal extrasensory ability.

Related Terms

  • synonym: telepathy - the supposed communication of thoughts or ideas by means other than the known senses
