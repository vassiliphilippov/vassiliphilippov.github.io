pellucidity

noun
the quality of being transparent or clear
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / p…ôÀåluÀêÀàs…™d…ôti / (AmE), / p…õljuÀêÀàs…™d…ôti / (BrE)
the quality of allowing light to pass through without diffusion or distortion; transparency or clearness

Examples

Pellucidity of water
Scientists measured the pellucidity of the water to determine its purity.
The pellucidity of the crystal allowed us to see right through to the other side.
The painting's colors were enhanced by the pellucidity of the glass overlay.

Synonyms

  • limpidity
    8.6 0.00/m
    limpidity is clear and transmits light without distortion, while pellucidity names the same transparent quality in formal contexts.
  • lucency
    8.5 0.00/m
    lucency lets light pass and can be slightly translucent, while pellucidity stresses full, undistorted transparency.
  • diaphany
    9.9 0.00/m
    diaphany is a poetic or technical word for being translucent or clear, while pellucidity focuses on undistorted, pure transparency.
  • diaphaneity
    9.9 0.00/m
    diaphaneity names how much light a material allows through, while pellucidity describes the clear, undistorted quality itself.
  • transparency
    4.6 0.87/m
    transparency means things let light pass so you can see through, while pellucidity stresses no diffusion or distortion in that light.
  • clearness
    2.2 0.02/m
    clearness often describes liquids or simple visibility, while pellucidity highlights perfect, undistorted light transmission and exact transparency.

Related Terms

  • antonym: opacity - the quality of not allowing light to pass through; lack of transparency
  • synonym: transparency - the quality of being able to see through something; lack of opacity
