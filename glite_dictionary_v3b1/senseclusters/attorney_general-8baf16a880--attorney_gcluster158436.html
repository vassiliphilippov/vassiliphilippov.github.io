Attorney General

noun
principal legal officer of a government
4.0 5.5
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈtɜːr.niː ˈdʒen.ə.rəl/ /; / /ə.ˈtɜːr.niː ˈdʒen.rəl/ (AmE), /ə.ˈtɜː.niː ˈdʒen.ə.rəl/ /; / /ə.ˈtɜː.niː ˈdʒen.rəl/ (BrE)
the highest-ranking legal officer who represents a government (country or state) in legal matters and is usually the head of the Department of Justice

Examples

The Attorney General filed charges
After a long investigation, the Attorney General filed charges against several officials.
Each state has its own Attorney General responsible for upholding the law within its jurisdiction.
The Attorney General announced new measures to combat cybercrime today.
As Attorney General, she led the investigation into government corruption.
Each state in the U.S. has its own Attorney General responsible for legal affairs.
The Attorney General announced a new policy on cybercrime enforcement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: prosecutor - a legal officer who conducts criminal prosecutions on behalf of the state
  • synonym: solicitor general - a law officer below the attorney general, often representing the government in court
