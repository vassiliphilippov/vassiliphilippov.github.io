surveil

verb
to watch or monitor closely
7.5 8.4
Pronunciation: / sɚˈveɪl / (AmE), / sɜːˈveɪl / (BrE)
to watch or monitor someone or something closely, especially in order to gather information or prevent wrongdoing

Examples

To surveil private communications
Activists expressed concern that government agencies might surveil private communications without proper oversight.
New technologies make it easier to surveil large crowds during public events.
The detectives decided to surveil the warehouse for any suspicious activity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ignore - to intentionally pay no attention to something
  • synonym: monitor - to observe and check the progress or quality of something over time
  • synonym: observe - to watch carefully and attentively
