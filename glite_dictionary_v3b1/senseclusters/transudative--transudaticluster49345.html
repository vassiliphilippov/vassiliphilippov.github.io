transudative

adjective
of transudate fluid
9.8
Pronunciation: / trænˈsuːdeɪtɪv / (AmE), / trænˈsjuːdeɪtɪv / (BrE)
describing or relating to a transudate (a clear fluid that passes through a membrane, usually low in protein and caused by systemic factors, not local inflammation)

Examples

Transudative fluid
The doctor explained that the transudative fluid in the lungs was due to heart failure.
Laboratory tests confirmed the pleural effusion was transudative rather than exudative.
A transudative ascites is often seen in patients with advanced liver disease.
The laboratory analysis confirmed the fluid was transudative rather than exudative.
Doctors often distinguish between transudative and exudative effusions to determine the underlying cause.
A transudative process usually indicates a non-inflammatory condition such as heart failure.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exudative - describing a fluid or effusion that is an exudate, usually high in protein and caused by inflammation
