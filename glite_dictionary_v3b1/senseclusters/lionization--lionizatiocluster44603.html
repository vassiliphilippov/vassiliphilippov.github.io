lionization

noun
celebrity treatment
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌlaɪənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌlaɪənʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of treating someone as a celebrity or giving them a lot of public attention and admiration

Examples

The lionization of celebrities
The lionization of the young actor made it difficult for him to live a normal life.
After her discovery, the scientist was unprepared for the sudden lionization by the media.
Some critics argue that the lionization of celebrities distracts from more important issues.
The sudden lionization of the young actor surprised even his closest friends.
After her invention went viral, she experienced a period of intense lionization in the media.
Critics warned that the lionization of political figures can lead to unrealistic expectations.
