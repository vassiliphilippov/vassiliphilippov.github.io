aciduria

noun
highly acidic urine
Pronunciation: / ˌæsɪˈdʊriə /; / ˌæsɪˈdjʊriə / (AmE), / ˌæsɪˈdjʊəriə / (BrE)
a medical condition in which urine contains an unusually high level of acids

Examples

Urine aciduria test
The laboratory test confirmed the presence of aciduria in the patient's urine sample.
Dietary changes can sometimes help reduce aciduria caused by excessive protein intake.
Doctors monitor aciduria in patients with certain metabolic disorders.
The laboratory confirmed the diagnosis of aciduria after analyzing the urine sample.
Children with certain metabolic disorders may develop aciduria as a symptom.
Treatment for aciduria often involves managing the underlying metabolic condition.

Related Terms

  • antonym: alkaluria - a condition in which urine is unusually alkaline
