racialism

noun
belief or behavior supporting racial discrimination
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪʃəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈreɪʃəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
the belief that races have distinctive characteristics, abilities, or qualities, which can justify discrimination or prejudice against people of other races

Examples

Racialism belief
He actively spoke out against racialism in sports and education.
The book provides a detailed history of racialism during the colonial era.
The government's policies were influenced by a dangerous racialism that divided communities.
