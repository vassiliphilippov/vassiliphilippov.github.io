phish

verb
to deceive for information
7.5
Pronunciation: /fɪʃ/ (AmE), /fɪʃ/ (BrE)
to try to trick people online into giving away private or sensitive information, such as passwords or credit card numbers

Examples

To phish for passwords
Cybercriminals often phish by sending emails that look like they are from real companies.
If you receive a suspicious message asking for your password, someone may be trying to phish you.
The IT department warned employees not to click on links in emails that could be used to phish sensitive data.
Cybercriminals often phish by sending emails that look like they are from your bank.
If you receive a suspicious message asking for your password, someone may be trying to phish you.
Many companies train employees to recognize attempts to phish sensitive information.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defraud - to illegally obtain money or information from someone by deception
  • synonym: hack - to gain unauthorized access to data in a system
  • antonym: protect - to keep someone or something safe from harm or danger
  • synonym: scam - to trick someone in order to get money or information
