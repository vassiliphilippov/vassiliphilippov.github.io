racialize

verb
to assign racial identity
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪʃəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈreɪʃəlaɪz / (BrE)
to make something about race, by assigning a racial identity, emphasizing racial differences, or interpreting/treating people or issues in terms of race

Examples

To racialize a group
Some media outlets unintentionally racialize stories by focusing on the background of those involved.
Policies that racialize certain groups can lead to discrimination and social division.
Educators should avoid materials that racialize students or reinforce harmful stereotypes.
Some policies can unintentionally racialize access to education and healthcare.
Activists argued that the new law would further racialize the criminal justice system.
Media coverage sometimes racializes certain events, influencing public perception.
It is important not to racialize every disagreement in public discussions.
The media coverage tended to racialize the incident, focusing on the backgrounds of those involved.
Some policies can unintentionally racialize social issues by emphasizing ethnic differences.

Related Terms

  • synonym: categorize - to place into a particular group or class
  • antonym: depersonalize - to treat as an impersonal object rather than as an individual
  • antonym: desegregate - to end the separation of people by race
  • synonym: discriminate - to treat people differently based on group characteristics
  • synonym: stereotype - to assign a fixed set of characteristics to a group
