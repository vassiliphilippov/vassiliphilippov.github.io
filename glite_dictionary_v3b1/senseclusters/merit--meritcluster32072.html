merit

noun
quality of deserving praise or reward
1.3 2.9
Pronunciation: /ˈmer.ɪt/ /; / /ˈmer.ət/ (AmE), /ˈme.rɪt/ /; / /ˈme.rət/ (BrE)
the quality or feature of being good or deserving praise, reward, or approval

Examples

Academic merit
Scholarships are awarded solely on academic merit rather than financial need.
The judges considered the merit of each proposal before making a decision.
We should evaluate candidates by their abilities and merit, not by who they know.
Each candidate was evaluated on the merit of their proposal.
That suggestion has real merit and deserves further discussion.
The museum's collection is of considerable merit, attracting visitors from around the world.
