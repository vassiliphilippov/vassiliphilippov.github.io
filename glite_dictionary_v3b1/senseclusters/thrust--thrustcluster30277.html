thrust

verb
force molten rock into existing rock
Pronunciation: / θrʌst / (AmE), / θrʌst / (BrE)
(Geology) To force molten rock into pre-existing rock formations.

Examples

To thrust magma
Engineers studied how volcanic forces had thrust new rock into the surrounding strata.
Geological activity can thrust magma through cracks in the Earth's crust.
Molten material thrusts upward during some types of intrusive igneous events.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intrude - (Geology) To force molten rock or magma into a pre-existing rock mass
