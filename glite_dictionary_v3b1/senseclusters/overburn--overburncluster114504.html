overburn

noun
excess disc writing
9.2
Pronunciation: /ˈoʊ.vər.bɜːrn/ (AmE), /ˈəʊ.və.bɜːn/ (BrE)
the process or result of writing more data to an optical disc than its official capacity allows

Examples

Overburn a disc
Attempting an overburn can sometimes damage the disc or make it unreadable.
The software supports overburn for users who need to store extra data on CDs.
He tried an overburn to fit the large video file onto a single disc.
