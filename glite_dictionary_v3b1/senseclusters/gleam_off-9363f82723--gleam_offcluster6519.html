gleam off

verb
to reflect light from a surface
Pronunciation: / ɡlim ɔf / (AmE), / ɡliːm ɒf / (BrE)
(of light) to be reflected brightly from a surface.

Examples

Light gleams off metal
Jewelry on the table gleamed off the light, making the room sparkle.
The wet pavement gleamed off the headlights of passing cars.
The windows of the skyscraper gleamed off the sunlight, blinding everyone below.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reflect - to throw back light, heat, sound, or an image from a surface
