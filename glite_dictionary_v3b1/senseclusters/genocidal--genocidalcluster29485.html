genocidal

adjective
extremely destructive
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒɛnəˈsaɪdəl / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛnəˈsaɪdəl / (BrE)
extremely destructive or intended to eliminate something completely, often used figuratively for emphasis rather than literal genocide

Examples

Genocidal policy
The new policy was described as genocidal because it threatened to erase entire communities.
Critics called the environmental damage from the project genocidal in its scale and impact.
Some activists used the term genocidal to emphasize the severity of the proposed budget cuts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: constructive - helpful or intended to improve
  • synonym: devastating - causing great destruction or damage
