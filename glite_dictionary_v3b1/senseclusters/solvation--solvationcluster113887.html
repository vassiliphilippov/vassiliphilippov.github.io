solvation

noun
process where solvent molecules interact with solute
Pronunciation: / sɑlˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / sɒlˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the process in which molecules of a solvent surround and interact with molecules or ions of a solute to form a solution or a compound

Examples

The solvation process
During the experiment, the scientist carefully observed the solvation process of the solute.
Proper solvation is necessary to stabilize charged particles in solutions.
The solvation of ions in water is critical for many biochemical reactions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dissolution - process of dissolving a substance in a solvent to form a solution
  • antonym: precipitation - process where a solute comes out of solution and forms a solid
