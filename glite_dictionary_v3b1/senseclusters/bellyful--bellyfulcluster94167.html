bellyful

noun
more than enough of something unpleasant
5.9 5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈbel.i.fəl/ (AmE), /ˈbel.i.fəl/ (BrE)
An amount of something unpleasant or unwanted that is more than one wants or can tolerate.

Examples

A bellyful of trouble
By the end of the day, she had a bellyful of complaints from customers.
He stormed out after getting a bellyful of criticism from his colleagues.
I've had a bellyful of their pointless arguments this week.
