photocatalytic

adjective
light-driven chemical
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊtoʊˌkætəˈlɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊˌkætəˈlɪtɪk / (BrE)
relating to the acceleration of a chemical reaction by the presence of light, often using a catalyst

Examples

The photocatalytic process
The photocatalytic process uses sunlight to break down harmful chemicals in water.
Researchers developed a photocatalytic material that can clean the air in urban environments.
A photocatalytic reaction can help remove pollutants from industrial waste.

Related Terms

  • synonym: catalytic - causing or accelerating a chemical reaction
