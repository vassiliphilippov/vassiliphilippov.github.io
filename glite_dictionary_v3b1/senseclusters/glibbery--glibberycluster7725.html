glibbery

adjective
slippery or slick
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlɪbəri / (AmE), / ˈɡlɪbəri / (BrE)
slippery or slick, especially in an unpleasant or slimy way

Examples

Glibbery surface
The path was covered in glibbery mud after the heavy rain.
She slipped on the glibbery floor near the kitchen sink.
The fish felt glibbery in his hands as he tried to hold it.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rough - having an uneven or coarse surface
  • synonym: slick - smooth and slippery, often because of being wet or oily
  • synonym: slippery - difficult to hold or stand on because it is smooth or wet
