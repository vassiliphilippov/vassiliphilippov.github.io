fiddleback

noun
wood grain or spider
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪdəlˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈfɪdəlˌbæk / (BrE)
a type of wood grain pattern with wavy lines, often used in making musical instruments, or a spider with a violin-shaped mark on its back

Examples

Fiddleback grain
The craftsman selected a piece of fiddleback maple for the violin.
She admired the beautiful fiddleback pattern on the back of the guitar.
The fiddleback spider is easily recognized by the violin-shaped mark on its back.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brown recluse - a spider known for its violin-shaped marking
  • synonym: curly maple - a type of maple wood with a wavy grain pattern
