convolution

noun
fold or ridge on the brain's surface
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːn.vəˈluː.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.vəˈluː.ʃən / (BrE)
a fold or ridge in the surface of the brain, especially one of the rounded prominences on the cerebral cortex

Examples

Brain convolution
Damage to certain convolutions of the cerebral cortex can affect memory and language abilities.
Neurosurgeons must navigate the complex convolutions of the brain during delicate procedures.
The intricate convolutions of the brain help increase its surface area and cognitive capacity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gyrus - a ridge or fold between two clefts on the cerebral surface
  • antonym: sulcus - a groove or furrow on the surface of the brain
