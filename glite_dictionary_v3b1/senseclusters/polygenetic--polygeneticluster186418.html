polygenetic

adjective
involving many genes
9.4
Pronunciation: /pɑː.lɪ.dʒə.ˈnet.ɪk/ /; / /pə.lɪ.dʒə.ˈnet.ɪk/ (AmE), /pɒl.ɪ.dʒə.ˈnet.ɪk/ /; / /pə.lɪ.dʒə.ˈnet.ɪk/ (BrE)
produced or influenced by the combined action of several different genes

Examples

Polygenetic disease
Diabetes is often considered a polygenetic disease because many genes contribute to its development.
The scientists studied the polygenetic nature of height in human populations.
Some mental health disorders are now understood to be polygenetic rather than caused by a single gene.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monogenic - caused by a single gene
  • synonym: multifactorial - involving many factors, including genes and environment
