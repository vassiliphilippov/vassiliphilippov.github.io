encoder

noun
data converter
4.5
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkoʊdər / (AmE), / ɪnˈkəʊdə / (BrE)
a person or device that converts information or data into a coded form, especially for processing or storage

Examples

Medical encoder
The hospital hired an encoder to enter patient records into the new electronic system each morning.
A video encoder compresses raw footage so it can be streamed online efficiently.
Every barcode scanner contains an encoder that translates the scanned data into digital information.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decoder - a person or device that converts coded data back into its original form
  • synonym: transcriber - a person who converts spoken or written information into another format
