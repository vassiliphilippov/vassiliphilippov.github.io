groundedness

noun
electrical earth connection
Pronunciation: / ˈɡraʊndɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊndɪdnəs / (BrE)
the state of being electrically connected to the earth to prevent electric shock or safely discharge current

Examples

Electrical groundedness
The engineer verified the groundedness of the equipment before starting the repairs.
Proper groundedness is essential to ensure safety in electrical installations.
A lack of groundedness can result in dangerous electric shocks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: earthing - the process of connecting electrical equipment to the earth
  • antonym: insulation - the state of being protected from electric shock by non-conductive material
