toccata

noun
baroque keyboard musical piece with fast runs
Pronunciation: / toʊˈkɑtə / (AmE), / təˈkɑːtə / (BrE)
a musical composition, typically for a keyboard instrument, with fast, elaborate passages and a free, improvisatory style, especially popular in the Baroque period

Examples

A toccata piece
Bach's toccata and fugue in D minor is one of the most famous organ works ever written.
Composers in the Baroque era often wrote a toccata to highlight the expressive possibilities of the harpsichord.
The pianist opened the concert with a dazzling toccata that showcased her technical skill.
