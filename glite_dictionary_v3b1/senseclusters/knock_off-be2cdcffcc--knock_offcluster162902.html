knock off

verb
stop doing something; cease an activity
7.4 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / nÉ‘k É‘f / (AmE), / nÉ’k É’f / (BrE)
(informal) to stop doing something or to cease an activity, especially one that is annoying or inappropriate.

Examples

Knock off the noise
Hey, knock off with the complaining and do your work.
If you don't knock off that noise, I'll call security.
The teacher told the students to knock off the chatter and focus on their assignments.

Video Examples

Synonyms

  • cut it
    7.2 4/m
    cut it is an informal order to stop a specific action immediately, while knock off tells someone to stop annoying behaviour.
  • lay off
    3.4 3/m
    lay off is used to tell someone to stop bothering another person, while knock off targets annoying actions more broadly.
  • leave off
    4.1 4/m
    leave off suggests pausing or stopping at a certain point, while knock off usually demands stopping annoying or inappropriate actions.
  • drop
    3.1 60/m
    drop means to stop or omit something or remove it from a list, while knock off focuses on stopping annoying behaviour.

Related Terms

  • antonym: continue - to keep doing something without stopping
  • synonym: quit - to stop or cease doing something
