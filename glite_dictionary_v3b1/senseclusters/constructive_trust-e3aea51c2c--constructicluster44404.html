constructive trust

noun
court-imposed trust for legal remedy
2.6
Pronunciation: / kənˈstrʌktɪv trʌst / (AmE), / kənˈstrʌktɪv trʌst / (BrE)
A trust imposed by a court to benefit a party who has been wrongfully deprived of their rights, regardless of the original intent of the parties involved.

Examples

A constructive trust
A constructive trust was granted to restore property taken in breach of confidence.
Lawyers argued that a constructive trust was the appropriate remedy for the unjust enrichment.
The judge imposed a constructive trust to prevent the company from benefiting from fraudulent behavior.
