obligor

noun
person with legal duty
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːblɪˌɡɔr / (AmE), / ˈɒblɪˌɡɔː / (BrE)
a person or organization that is legally required to pay money or do something under a contract or agreement

Examples

The obligor pays money
The obligor failed to deliver the goods as promised in the contract.
If the obligor does not pay on time, penalties may apply.
Each obligor is responsible for fulfilling their part of the agreement.
The obligor failed to deliver the goods on time, breaching the contract.
Each obligor is responsible for making payments as outlined in the agreement.
If the obligor does not fulfill their duty, legal action may be taken.

Related Terms

  • synonym: debtor - a person who owes money
  • antonym: obligee - a person to whom an obligation is owed
