Constantine the Great

proper noun
Roman Emperor, promoted Christianity
8.2
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑːn.stən.taɪn ðə greɪt/ /; / /ˈkɑːn.stən.tiː ðə greɪt/ (AmE), /ˈkɒn.stən.taɪn ðə greɪt/ /; / /ˈkɒn.stən.tiː ðə greɪt/ (BrE)
(c. 280–337) Roman Emperor who stopped the persecution of Christians, made Christianity the official religion of the Roman Empire, and moved the capital to Byzantium (renamed Constantinople).

Examples

Constantine the Great Roman Emperor
Constantine the Great is credited with ending the persecution of Christians in the Roman Empire.
Many historians believe that Constantine the Great's conversion to Christianity changed the course of Western history.
The city of Constantinople was named by Constantine the Great after moving the capital from Rome.
