negligible

adjective
too small to be important
3.3
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛɡlɪdʒəbəl /; / ˈnɛɡlədʒəbəl / (AmE), / ˈnɛɡlɪdʒɪbəl /; / ˈnɛɡlɪdʒəbl̩ / (BrE)
so small or unimportant as to be not worth considering; insignificant

Examples

A negligible amount
Any risks associated with the procedure are negligible.
The change in temperature was so negligible that nobody noticed.
The extra cost is negligible compared to the overall budget.
Any risk associated with this procedure is considered negligible by experts.
The cost of the extra materials was so negligible that it barely affected the budget.
The temperature change overnight was negligible and went almost unnoticed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: insignificant - too small or unimportant to be worth consideration
  • synonym: paltry - insignificantly small; minor
  • antonym: substantial - of considerable importance, size, or worth
