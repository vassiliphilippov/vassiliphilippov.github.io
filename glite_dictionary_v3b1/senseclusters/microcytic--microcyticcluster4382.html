microcytic

adjective
smaller than normal cells
8.6
Pronunciation: /maɪ.krə.ˈsɪ.tɪk/ /; / /mɪ.krə.ˈsɪ.tɪk/ (AmE), /maɪ.krə.ˈsɪ.tɪk/ /; / /mɪ.krə.ˈsɪ.tɪk/ (BrE)
describing cells, especially red blood cells, that are smaller than normal in size

Examples

Microcytic red cells
The laboratory report showed a microcytic anemia, indicating the red blood cells were unusually small.
Iron deficiency is a common cause of microcytic red blood cells in patients.
Doctors look for microcytic patterns in blood tests to help diagnose certain types of anemia.

Related Terms

  • antonym: macrocytic - describing cells that are larger than normal
  • antonym: normocytic - describing cells of normal size
