bite

noun
wound caused by biting
1.0 8.14/m
Pronunciation: / baÉªt / (AmE), / baÉªt / (BrE)
the act of using teeth or jaws to grip, cut, or tear something, or the wound or mark caused by this action (by a person, animal, or insect)

Examples

A dog bite
After the fight, he had several scratches and a human bite on his shoulder.
He went to the doctor to have his spider bite checked for infection.
The dog left a deep bite on his arm during the attack.
I felt the bite of the cold air on my skin after stepping outside.
She took a big bite of the sandwich, savoring the fresh flavors.
The dog gave my shoe a sharp bite before running away.
A bee bite can sometimes cause an allergic reaction.
He couldn't stop scratching the itchy bite on his arm.
The mosquito's bite left a swollen red mark on her leg.

Synonyms

  • nibble
    3.2 0.63/m
    nibble is a small, gentle bite often taken carefully, while bite is the general action or mark made by teeth.
  • chomp
    5.3 0.26/m
    chomp is a loud, vigorous chewing or biting action, while bite is the basic act of using teeth to grip or tear.
  • insect bite
    2.5 0.36/m
    insect bite is a wound from an insect that can itch or hurt, while bite is any mark or wound made by teeth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lick - to pass the tongue over something, as the opposite of bite
  • antonym: sting - a wound caused by the stinger of an insect or animal
