well

verb
rise or flow up (liquid or emotion)
4.3
Pronunciation: / wɛl / (AmE), / wɛl / (BrE)
(of a liquid or emotion) to rise up to the surface and flow out or become visible, often gradually or in a steady stream.

Examples

Tears well up
A sense of pride will often well up during moments of achievement.
Blood could be seen welling from the small cut on his arm.
Tears began to well in her eyes as she listened to the sad news.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overflow - (of liquid) to spill over the edge or brim
