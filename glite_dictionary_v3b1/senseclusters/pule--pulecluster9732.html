pule

verb
cry or whimper weakly
Pronunciation: / pjuːl / (AmE), / pjuːl / (BrE)
to cry or whimper softly and weakly, often in a high-pitched or plaintive way

Examples

To pule softly
Listening to the baby pule made his older sister rush to comfort him.
She would pule for hours whenever she was scolded by her mother.
The puppy began to pule softly when left alone in the dark room.
The lost kitten began to pule when it could not find its mother.
He would pule about every small inconvenience, annoying his friends.
Children sometimes pule when they are tired or want attention.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cheer - to shout for joy or encouragement
  • degree higher: sob - to cry noisily, making loud, convulsive gasps
  • degree higher: wail - cry out loudly and mournfully
  • synonym: whimper - make weak, soft crying sounds
  • synonym: whine - to complain or cry in a high-pitched, annoying way
