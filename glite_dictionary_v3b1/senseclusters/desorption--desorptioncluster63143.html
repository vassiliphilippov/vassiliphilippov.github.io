desorption

noun
release of a substance from a surface
4.2 8.4
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈsɑːr.p.ʃən/ /; / /diː.ˈsɑːr.p.ʃən/ /; / /dɪ.ˈsɔːr.p.ʃən/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈsɔːp.ʃən/ /; / /diː.ˈsɔːp.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process by which a substance is released from or through a surface, usually changing from an adsorbed state to a gaseous or liquid state

Examples

Desorption process

High temperatures can accelerate the desorption process in catalytic reactions.
