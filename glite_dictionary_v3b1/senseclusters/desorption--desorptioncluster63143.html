desorption

noun
release of a substance from a surface
4.2
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsɔɹpʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈsɔːpʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a substance is released from or through a surface, usually changing from an adsorbed state to a gaseous or liquid state

Examples

Desorption process
During cleaning, desorption removes contaminants from metal surfaces.
High temperatures can accelerate the desorption process in catalytic reactions.
The desorption of water from the surface was measured during the experiment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: adsorption - the process by which atoms, ions, or molecules adhere to a surface
