paving

noun
the act of laying paving materials
5.2 1.9
Pronunciation: /ˈpeɪ.vɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈpeɪ.vɪŋ/ (BrE)
a hard surface or the material used for making roads, paths, or areas suitable for walking or driving

Examples

Road paving
After the paving was finished, the road was much smoother.
Extensive paving is required to create new parking lots.
The city started paving the streets in early spring.
Loose stones in the paving made it difficult to walk in high heels.
The paving on the main road was recently replaced with smooth asphalt.
The city council allocated funds to repair the damaged paving downtown.
High-quality paving can significantly enhance the appearance of a public plaza.
The construction crew unloaded stacks of paving beside the road.
They decided to replace the old gravel with new paving in the backyard.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flagstone - a flat stone used for paving paths or floors
  • synonym: pavement - a hard surface laid down to form a road or path
  • synonym: tile - a flat piece of baked clay or stone used for covering surfaces
