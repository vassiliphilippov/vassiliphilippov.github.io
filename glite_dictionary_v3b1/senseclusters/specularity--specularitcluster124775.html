specularity

noun
reflected light spot
9.3
Pronunciation: /spek.jə.ˈlær.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /spek.jʊ.ˈlær.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /spek.jə.ˈlær.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /spek.jʊ.ˈlær.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
a bright spot or highlight on a surface caused by the reflection of light, especially from a shiny or smooth area

Examples

A specularity on glass
The photographer adjusted the lights to reduce the specularity on the model's face.
A sudden specularity on the car's hood revealed the nearby streetlight's reflection.
Artists often paint subtle specularity to make objects appear more realistic and three-dimensional.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glare - a strong, harsh light reflected from a surface
  • synonym: highlight - a bright area on a surface where light is reflected
  • antonym: matte - a surface that does not reflect light and lacks shine
