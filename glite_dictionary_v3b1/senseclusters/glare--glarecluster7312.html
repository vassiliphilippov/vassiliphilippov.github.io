glare

verb
look angrily or fiercely at something
6.8 2.7
Pronunciation: /gler/ (AmE), /gleə/ (BrE)
to stare at someone or something in an angry or fierce way

Examples

To glare at someone
During the meeting, she glared at her colleague, making her displeasure very obvious.
He glared so intensely that everyone in the room fell silent.
The teacher glared at the students who were talking during the lesson.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glower - to look or stare with sullen dislike, discontent, or anger
  • antonym: smile - to form one's features into a pleased, kind, or amused expression
  • synonym: stare - to look fixedly or vacantly at someone or something
