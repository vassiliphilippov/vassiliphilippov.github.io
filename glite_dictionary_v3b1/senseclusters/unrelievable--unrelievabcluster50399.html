unrelievable

adjective
cannot be eased
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnrɪˈliːvəbəl / (AmE), / ˌʌnrɪˈliːvəbəl / (BrE)
not able to be eased, reduced, or made less severe

Examples

Unrelievable pain
The patient described his headache as unrelievable, even after taking medication.
She faced unrelievable stress during the long months of uncertainty.
After the surgery, he was left with unrelievable discomfort in his leg.

Related Terms

  • synonym: incurable - not able to be cured or healed
  • antonym: relievable - able to be eased or made less severe
