article of faith

noun
a principle or section of a creed or doctrine
3.4 5.9
Pronunciation: /ˈɑːr.tɪ.kəl əv feɪθ/ /; / /ˈɑːr.tə.kəl əv feɪθ/ (AmE), /ˈɑː.tɪ.kəl əv feɪθ/ /; / /ˈɑː.tə.kəl əv feɪθ/ (BrE)
a statement or principle, often from a creed or doctrine, regarded as an essential belief, especially in Christianity; one of the distinct sections into which such a creed is divided

Examples

Christian article of faith
Accepting certain teachings as articles of faith is essential in belonging to the church.
Belief in the resurrection of Jesus is an article of faith for many Christians.
The Nicene Creed contains several articles of faith that are central to Christian doctrine.
