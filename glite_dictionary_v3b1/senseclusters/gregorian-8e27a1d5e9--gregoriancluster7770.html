Gregorian

adjective
relating to Pope Gregory XIII’s calendar
7.9
Pronunciation: / ɡrəˈɡɔriən / (AmE), / ɡrɪˈɡɔːrɪən / (BrE)
of or relating to Pope Gregory XIII or the calendar he introduced in 1582, which is the basis of the modern Western calendar.

Examples

Gregorian calendar system
The Gregorian calendar is now used in most countries around the world.
The adoption of the Gregorian reform caused confusion in some regions.
They celebrated New Year's Day according to the Gregorian system.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Julian - relating to the earlier Julian calendar, which the Gregorian replaced
