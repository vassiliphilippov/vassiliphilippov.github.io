groat

noun
hulled grain
9.8
Pronunciation: / ɡroʊt / (AmE), / ɡrəʊt / (BrE)
a grain, especially oat or barley, with the outer husk removed but the bran layer left intact

Examples

Oat groat
The recipe called for a cup of groat to make a nutritious breakfast.
Farmers often sell groats to local markets for use in porridge and soups.
She prefers groat over processed grains because it retains more nutrients.
