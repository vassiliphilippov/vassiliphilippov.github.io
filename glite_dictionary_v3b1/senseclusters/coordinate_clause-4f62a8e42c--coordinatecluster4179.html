coordinate clause

noun
a clause parallel to another in structure and rank

a clause in a sentence that is of equal importance and grammatical status to another clause, often joined by a coordinating conjunction such as 'and' or 'but'

Examples

A coordinate clause with and
In the phrase 'It rained, but we went outside,' each part is a coordinate clause.
The sentence 'She played the piano and he sang' contains two coordinate clauses.
You can link coordinate clauses with conjunctions like 'or', 'and', or 'but'.

Related Terms

  • synonym: main clause - an independent clause that can form a complete sentence
  • antonym: subordinate clause - a clause that depends on a main clause and cannot stand alone
