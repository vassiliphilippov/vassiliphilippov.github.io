repulsive force

noun
force causing objects to repel
1.7 5.4
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈpʌl.sɪv fɔːrs/ /; / /rə.ˈpʌl.sɪv fɔːrs/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈpʌl.sɪv fɔːs/ /; / /rə.ˈpʌl.sɪv fɔːs/ (BrE)
a force that causes bodies to push away from each other

Examples

A repulsive force between charges
Electric charges of the same sign experience a repulsive force when brought close together.
Scientists study repulsive force to better understand atomic and molecular interactions.
The repulsive force between the two magnets prevented them from touching each other.
