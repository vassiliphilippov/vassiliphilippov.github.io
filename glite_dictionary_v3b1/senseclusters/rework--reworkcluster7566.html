rework

verb
use or adapt again in altered form
1.0
Pronunciation: / riˈwɝk /; / ˌriˈwɜrk / (AmE), / ˌriːˈwɜːk / (BrE)
to change, adapt, or improve something by working on it again

Examples

To rework old ideas
Many designers rework old concepts to fit current trends.
She decided to rework her essay after receiving feedback from her teacher.
The director plans to rework the script before filming begins.
The author decided to rework the ending of her novel after receiving feedback from her editor.
Engineers had to rework the design to meet the new safety standards.
He spent the weekend reworking his presentation to make it clearer and more engaging.

  • antonym: finalize - to complete the last details of something
  • degree lower: reuse - to use something again
