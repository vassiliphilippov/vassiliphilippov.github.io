floodway

noun
protected floodplain area
5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈflʌd.weɪ/ (AmE), /ˈflʌd.weɪ/ (BrE)
an area of a river's floodplain that must be kept clear of development to allow floodwaters to flow safely during floods

Examples

Floodway zone rules
The city council voted to keep the floodway free of new construction to reduce flood risks.
Engineers studied the floodway to ensure it could handle heavy rainfall during the storm season.
Building in the floodway is prohibited to protect both people and property from flooding.

Related Terms

  • synonym: floodplain - the flat land near a river that is prone to flooding
  • antonym: levee - an embankment built to prevent the overflow of a river
