pulsator

noun
device producing pulses
Pronunciation: / ˈpʌlseɪtər / (AmE), / ˈpʌlseɪtə / (BrE)
a device or organism that produces rhythmic pulses or beats, often to control a mechanical or biological process

Examples

A pulsator device
The milking machine uses a pulsator to control suction and release cycles.
Technicians checked the pulsator to ensure the device was producing regular beats.
Some aquarium filters include a pulsator to simulate natural water movement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oscillator - a device that produces regular oscillations or waves
  • synonym: pump - a device that moves fluids by mechanical action
