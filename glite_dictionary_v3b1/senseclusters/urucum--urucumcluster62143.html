urucum

noun
natural food coloring
9.9
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈruː.kəm/ /; / /ʊ.ˈruː.kəm/ (AmE), /ə.ˈruː.kəm/ /; / /ʊ.ˈruː.kəm/ (BrE)
a reddish-orange dye or seasoning made from the seeds of the urucum plant, used as a natural food coloring or spice

Examples

Urucum spice color
The chef added urucum to the rice to give it a vibrant color.
Some traditional cheeses are colored with urucum instead of artificial dyes.
In Brazil, urucum is a common ingredient in regional dishes for both flavor and color.
