matter of life and death

noun
extremely important or critical situation
1.4
Pronunciation: / ˈmæt̬ɚ əv ˈlaɪf ənd ˈdɛθ / (AmE), / ˈmætə əv ˈlaɪf ən ˈdɛθ / (BrE)
a situation of extreme importance, often involving the difference between survival and death

Examples

A matter of life and death
For many refugees, safe passage is literally a matter of life and death.
In certain medical emergencies, time can be a matter of life and death.
The rescuers treated the situation as a matter of life and death.

Related Terms

  • antonym: trivial - of little value or importance
