sugar cane

noun
tropical grass used as a sugar source
Pronunciation: / ˈʃuːɡər keɪn /; / ˈʃʊɡɚ keɪn / (AmE), / ˈʃʊɡə keɪn / (BrE)
a tall tropical plant with thick, sweet stalks that is grown for its juice, which is used to make sugar and molasses

Examples

Fresh sugar cane juice
Freshly cut sugar cane juice is a popular drink in many tropical countries.
Molasses is often produced as a byproduct when sugar cane is processed for sugar.
The farmer planted acres of sugar cane to meet the growing demand for sweeteners.
After extracting the juice from sugar cane, the remaining fiber is used as animal feed.
In Brazil, large fields of sugar cane are harvested to produce ethanol fuel.
The vendor sold fresh sticks of sugar cane that customers chewed for the sweet juice.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sugar beet - a plant whose root contains a high concentration of sucrose; another major source of sugar
