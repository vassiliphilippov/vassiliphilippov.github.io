wheel around

verb
to turn or move on wheels, or turn suddenly
4.2 0.46/m
Pronunciation: / wil …ôÀàra änd /; / wiÀêl …ôÀàra änd / (AmE), / wiÀêl …ôÀàra änd / (BrE)
to turn around quickly or change direction suddenly, often as if revolving on a pivot or with wheels

Examples

To wheel around quickly
He wheeled around and glared at me when I called his name.
Startled by the noise, the dog wheeled around to see what had happened.
The nurse wheeled around the patient to face the window.
At the sound of his name, she wheeled around to see who had called her.
The general wheeled around on his horse to address the gathered soldiers.
The startled bird suddenly wheeled around and flew off in the opposite direction.

Synonyms

  • wheel
    4.1 2.73/m
    wheel is a simple verb for turning or changing direction like a vehicle, while wheel around means turning suddenly.
  • swivel
    4.7 3.51/m
    swivel turns smoothly on a fixed pivot, often for chairs or screens, while wheel around means a sudden, wheel-like turn.
  • pivot
    4.2 1.28/m
    pivot means to turn slowly or on a central point in many contexts, while wheel around implies a quick, wheeled turn.
  • swing around
    3.2 1.15/m
    swing around means to turn abruptly to face another way, while wheel around suggests a sharp turn like a vehicle.
  • spin around
    2.1 3.16/m
    spin around means to turn quickly in place, often repeatedly, while wheel around suggests a single sudden directional change.
  • spin
    1.0 14/m
    spin means to rotate fast, often many times or out of control, while wheel around implies a controlled sudden turn.
  • whirl around
    3.7 1.39/m
    whirl around means to move quickly in a spinning, continuous way, while wheel around suggests a quick, pivot-like direction change.

Related Terms
