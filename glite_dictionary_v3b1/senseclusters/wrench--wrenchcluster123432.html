wrench

noun
a jerky pulling movement
5.7
Pronunciation: / rɛntʃ / (AmE), / rɛntʃ / (BrE)
a sudden, forceful twisting or pulling movement, often causing pain or injury

Examples

A sudden wrench
She felt a sudden wrench in her shoulder as she tried to lift the heavy box.
The wrench of leaving her hometown was almost too much to bear.
With a wrench, he pulled the door open against the wind.
After the fall, he felt a painful wrench in his shoulder.
She twisted her ankle in a wrench during the hike.
The football player's wrench kept him off the field for several weeks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jerk - a quick, sharp pull or twist
  • synonym: sprain - an injury to a ligament caused by being stretched or twisted
  • synonym: strain - an injury to a muscle or tendon from stretching or overuse
  • synonym: tug - a sudden or forceful pull
