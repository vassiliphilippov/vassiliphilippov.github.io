implicature

noun
implied meaning
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈplɪkətʃɚ / (AmE), / ɪmˈplɪkətʃə / (BrE)
a meaning that is implied by a speaker but not directly stated in words

Examples

The implicature of comment
The implicature of his comment was that he did not trust the new policy.
In conversation, listeners often rely on implicature to understand what is really meant.
Her refusal to answer carried an implicature that she disagreed with the proposal.

Related Terms

  • antonym: explicit statement - something clearly and directly stated
  • synonym: implication - a suggestion that is not directly expressed
