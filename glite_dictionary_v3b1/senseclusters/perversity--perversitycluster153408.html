perversity

noun
deliberate unreasonable or immoral behavior
8.6 0.98/m
Pronunciation: / p…ôrÀàv…ùÀês…ôti /; / p…ôrÀàv…úrs…ôti / (AmE), / p…ôÀàv…úÀês…ôti / (BrE)
the quality of deliberately behaving in a way that is unreasonable, wrong, or stubbornly against accepted standards

Examples

Her perversity showed
Despite knowing the right thing to do, her perversity led her to act otherwise.
He seemed driven by a kind of perversity that delighted in breaking the rules.
Her perversity shocked everyone at the meeting.
Her perversity made every attempt to help her feel like an uphill battle.
His perversity in ignoring good advice eventually led to his downfall.
Sometimes, children show perversity simply to test boundaries set by adults.

Synonyms

  • perverseness
    8.4 0.07/m

  • contrariness
    7.7 0.08/m
    contrariness means being deliberately difficult and opposing others, while perversity means resisting accepted standards by being stubbornly wrong or unreasonable.
