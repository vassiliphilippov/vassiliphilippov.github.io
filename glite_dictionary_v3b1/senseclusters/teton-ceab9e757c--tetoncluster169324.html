Teton

noun
member of a western Sioux group
9.6 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàtiÀêÀåt…ën / (AmE), / ÀàtiÀêt…ín / (BrE)
A member of the largest western division of the Sioux people, made up of several groups who traditionally lived on the Great Plains.

Examples

Teton Sioux tribe
Anthropologists have studied the social structure of the Teton Sioux extensively.
Many Teton traditions are still practiced by their descendants today.
The Teton people played a significant role in the history of the Great Plains.

Synonyms

  • Teton Sioux
    9.4 0.04/m
    Teton Sioux refers clearly to the western branch of the Sioux people, while Teton is the shorter name used for the same group.
  • Lakota
    8.8 2.21/m
    Lakota is the widely used name on maps and in writing for the western Sioux, while Teton is another term for that group.
  • Teton Dakota
    9.9 0.00/m
    Teton Dakota sounds like a mixed label and can confuse learners, while Teton is the standard term for the western Sioux branch.
