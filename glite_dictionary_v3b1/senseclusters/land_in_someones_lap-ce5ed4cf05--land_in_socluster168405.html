land in someone's lap

verb
receive something unexpectedly or easily
4.2
Pronunciation: / lænd ɪn sʌmwʌnz læp / (AmE), / lænd ɪn sʌmwʌnz læp / (BrE)
to be given or to receive something valuable, opportunity, or responsibility unexpectedly and without effort

Examples

To land in someone's lap unexpectedly
A job offer landed in his lap just when he needed it most.
Sometimes good fortune lands in your lap when you least expect it.
The inheritance landed in her lap after a distant relative passed away.
