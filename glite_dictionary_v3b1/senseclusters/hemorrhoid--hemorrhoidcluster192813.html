hemorrhoid

noun
swollen vein near the anus
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛm.əˌrɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈhɛm.ər.ɔɪd /; / ˈhiː.mə.rɔɪd / (BrE)
A swollen and inflamed vein in the region of the anus, which may be internal or external to the anal sphincter and can cause discomfort, pain, or bleeding.

Examples

Painful hemorrhoid swelling
Chronic constipation can increase the risk of developing hemorrhoids.
Many adults experience hemorrhoids at some point in their lives, especially with prolonged sitting.
The doctor prescribed a cream to relieve the discomfort caused by hemorrhoids.
After the surgery, she finally found relief from her chronic haemorrhoid problem.
Many people are reluctant to seek treatment for a haemorrhoid due to embarrassment.
The patient complained of severe pain caused by a haemorrhoid.
