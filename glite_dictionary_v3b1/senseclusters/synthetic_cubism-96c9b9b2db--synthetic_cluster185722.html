synthetic cubism

noun
late phase of the Cubist art movement
7.4 7.7
Pronunciation: / sɪnˈθɛtɪk ˈkjuːbɪzəm / (AmE), / sɪnˈθɛtɪk ˈkjuːbɪzəm / (BrE)
the later phase of the Cubist art movement, characterized by simpler shapes, brighter colors, and the introduction of collage elements

Examples

Synthetic cubism art style
Synthetic cubism introduced collage and brighter colors into the Cubist style.
Art historians often distinguish between analytic and synthetic cubism when discussing early twentieth-century paintings.
Many of Picasso's works from 1912 demonstrate the features of synthetic cubism.

Related Terms

  • antonym: analytic cubism - the earlier phase of Cubism, focusing on breaking down forms into geometric parts
