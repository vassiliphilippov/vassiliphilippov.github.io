wrymouth

noun
an eel-like fish with a vertical mouth
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈraɪˌmaʊθ / (AmE), / ˈraɪˌmaʊθ / (BrE)
An eel-like fish of the North Atlantic, known for its elongated body and large, nearly vertical mouth.

Examples

The wrymouth fish
Fishermen sometimes catch a wrymouth by accident when trawling near the seabed.
The wrymouth has a distinctive mouth that gives the fish its name.
The wrymouth is seldom seen by divers due to its burrowing habits.
