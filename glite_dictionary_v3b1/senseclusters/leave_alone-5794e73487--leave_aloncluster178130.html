leave alone

verb
not disturb or interfere with
2.3 30/m
Pronunciation: / liv …ôÀàlo än / (AmE), / liÀêv …ôÀàl…ô än / (BrE)
to not disturb, alter, or interfere with someone or something; to refrain from taking action affecting them or it.

Examples

To leave alone a wild animal
If you see a wild animal in the park, it's best to leave alone and not approach it.
She decided to leave alone her old diaries, knowing some memories are best left untouched.
Sometimes it's wiser to leave alone issues that don't directly affect you.

Synonyms

  • leave it be
    5.1 0.05/m
    leave it be is a casual phrase used to tell someone not to change something, while leave alone is broader.
  • let it alone
    3.0 0.06/m
    let it alone is a set phrase that asks people to stop touching something, while leave alone is a general instruction not to interfere.
  • leave
    1.6 200/m
    leave often means causing something to remain or to go away, while leave alone specifically tells someone not to disturb.
  • leave behind
    2.1 0.9/m
    leave behind means to not take something with you or forget it somewhere, while leave alone means to avoid touching or interfering.
