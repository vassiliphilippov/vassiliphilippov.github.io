trespass

noun
wrongful interference with property
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɛspəs /; / ˈtrɛs.pæs / (AmE), / ˈtrɛspəs /; / ˈtrɛs.pæs / (BrE)
unauthorized interference with property or digital systems, especially in a legal or technical sense

Examples

A trespass on systems
If you damage someone's car while committing a trespass, you could be held liable.
The company filed a lawsuit for trespass after repeated unauthorized entry onto their land.
The legal definition of trespass includes both interference with land and with personal property.
The company faced accusations of cyber trespass after a data breach exposed employee records.
Lawyers argued the hacker's actions amounted to digital trespass under the new computer-crime laws.
Following the breach, regulators investigated whether the firm committed trespass by accessing users' private files.

Related Terms

  • antonym: authorization - official permission to access data, systems, or property
  • synonym: encroachment - an intrusion on another's rights or territory
  • synonym: intrusion - the act of wrongfully entering or accessing someone else's property or systems
  • synonym: invasion - an unwelcome entry into someone else's domain or property
  • synonym: unauthorized access - the act of accessing a computer system, network, or data without permission
  • antonym: withdrawal - the action of ceasing to occupy or interfere with property
