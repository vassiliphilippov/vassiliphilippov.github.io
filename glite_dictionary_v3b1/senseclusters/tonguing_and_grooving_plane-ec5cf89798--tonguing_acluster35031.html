tonguing and grooving plane

noun
tool for cutting tongue and groove in wood
9.5 8.9
Pronunciation: /ˈtʌŋ.ɪŋ ənd ˈgruː.vɪŋ pleɪn/ /; / /ˈtʌŋ.ɪŋ ænd ˈgruː.vɪŋ pleɪn/ (AmE), /ˈtʌŋ.ɪŋ ənd ˈgruː.vɪŋ pleɪn/ /; / /ˈtʌŋ.ɪŋ ænd ˈgruː.vɪŋ pleɪn/ (BrE)
A woodworking hand tool fitted with cutters for making the tongue and groove on the edges of boards.

Examples

A tonguing and grooving plane tool
A traditional tonguing and grooving plane creates precise edges for paneling and wainscoting.
Before power tools, craftsmen relied on the tonguing and grooving plane for parquet flooring.
The joiner used a tonguing and grooving plane to fit the floorboards tightly together.
