Hedjaz

proper noun
region in western Saudi Arabia, includes Mecca and Medina
Pronunciation: / hɛˈdʒæz / (AmE), / hɛˈdʒæz / (BrE)
a coastal region in western Saudi Arabia along the Red Sea, historically important as the location of Mecca and Medina and formerly an independent kingdom

Examples

Hedjaz region
In the early 20th century, Hedjaz was briefly an independent kingdom before joining with Nejd.
Many pilgrims travel through Hedjaz each year to visit the holy cities of Mecca and Medina.
The historic region of Hedjaz played a crucial role in the early development of Islam.
