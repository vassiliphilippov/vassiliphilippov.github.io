appeaser

noun
one who seeks peace by acceding to demands
7.6 0.2/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàpiz…ôr / (AmE), / …ôÀàpiÀêz…ô / (BrE)
a person who seeks to bring about peace by agreeing to others' demands, often in the hope of avoiding conflict

Examples

An appeaser in politics
Calling someone an appeaser can be controversial in political discussions.
Historians debate whether the policy was that of an appeaser or a pragmatic diplomat.
The leader was criticized as an appeaser who made too many concessions to foreign powers.

Synonyms

  • accommodationist
    8.4 0.2/m
    an accommodationist adapts or seeks compromise to avoid conflict, while an appeaser agrees to demands mainly to keep peace.
  • compromiser
    4.8 0.2/m
    a compromiser makes give-and-take deals in negotiation, while an appeaser often gives in to others' demands to prevent conflict.

Related Terms

  • antonym: opponent - person who resists or acts against something
  • synonym: pacifist - person who believes in or promotes peace
