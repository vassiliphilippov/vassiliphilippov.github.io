Ferdinand of Aragon

proper noun
King of Aragon and Castile, unified Spain
9.4
Pronunciation: /ˈfɜːr.dɪ.nænd əv ˈær.ə.ɡɑːn/ /; / /ˈfɜːr.dɪ.nænd əv ˈær.ə.ɡən/ (AmE), /ˈfɜː.dɪ.nænd əv ˈær.ə.ɡən/ /; / /ˈfɜː.dɪ.nænd əv ˈær.ə.ɡɒn/ (BrE)
(1452–1516) King of Aragon and Castile, who ruled jointly with his wife Isabella I. Their reign marked the unification of Spain, the completion of the Reconquista, the institution of the Spanish Inquisition, and support for Columbus's expedition.

Examples

Ferdinand of Aragon and Isabella
Ferdinand of Aragon and Isabella I played crucial roles in the unification of Spain.
Many history books discuss how Ferdinand of Aragon supported Columbus's voyage to the New World.
The marriage between Ferdinand of Aragon and Isabella I marked a turning point in European history.
