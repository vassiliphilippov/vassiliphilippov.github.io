final cause

noun
purpose or goal as a type of cause (philosophy)
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪnəl kɔz / (AmE), / ˈfaɪnəl kɔːz / (BrE)
(Philosophy) The end, goal, or purpose for which something exists or is done; one of Aristotle's four types of causes.

Examples

The final cause of action
Aristotle included the final cause among his four explanations for natural phenomena.
In biology, the function of an organ can be considered its final cause.
The final cause of the clock is to measure and indicate time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: efficient cause - the cause that brings something into being (philosophy)
  • synonym: formal cause - the form or pattern of a thing (philosophy)
  • synonym: material cause - the matter from which something is made (philosophy)
