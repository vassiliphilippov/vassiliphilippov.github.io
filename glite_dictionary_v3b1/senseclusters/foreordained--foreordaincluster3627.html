foreordained

adjective
predetermined, inevitable
Pronunciation: / ˌfɔːrɔrˈdeɪnd / (AmE), / ˌfɔːrɔːˈdeɪnd / (BrE)
(of an event or outcome) determined or established in advance, especially by fate or divine will; inevitable or unalterable.

Examples

A foreordained outcome
Many believed his victory was foreordained by destiny.
She felt every moment of the journey had been foreordained and could not be changed.
The ancient prophecy seemed to make their meeting foreordained.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inevitable - certain to happen; unavoidable
  • synonym: predestined - having one's fate or outcome decided in advance
  • antonym: random - made or done without method or conscious decision; not predetermined
