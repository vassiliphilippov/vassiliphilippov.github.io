tetrameter

noun
verse line with four metrical feet

Pronunciation: /te.ˈtræ.mɪ.tər/ (AmE), /te.ˈtræ.mɪ.tə/ (BrE)
A line of verse consisting of four metrical feet.

Examples

A tetrameter line
Many English ballads are written in tetrameter, giving them a rhythmic, sing-song quality.
The poet chose tetrameter for his verses to create a brisk and lively pace.
You can identify tetrameter by counting four metrical feet in each line.
