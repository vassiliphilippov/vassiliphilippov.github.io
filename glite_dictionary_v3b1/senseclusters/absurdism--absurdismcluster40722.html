absurdism

noun
belief in life's meaninglessness
8.8
Pronunciation: / əbˈsɝːdɪzəm / (AmE), / əbˈsɜːdɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief or philosophical view that human life is inherently meaningless and that efforts to find meaning are ultimately futile

Examples

Absurdism in philosophy
The play is a classic example of absurdism, challenging audiences to question the purpose of existence.
Many modern artists use absurdism to express feelings of confusion and alienation.
He became interested in absurdism after reading works by Albert Camus.

Related Terms

  • synonym: existentialism - a philosophy emphasizing individual existence and freedom
  • synonym: nihilism - the rejection of all religious and moral principles, often believing life is meaningless
  • antonym: optimism - the belief that good ultimately prevails and life has meaning
