massacre

verb
kill a large number of people indiscriminately
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈmæsəkər /; / ˈmæsəkɚ / (AmE), / ˈmæsəkə / (BrE)
to deliberately and violently kill a large number of people or animals, especially in a cruel or indiscriminate way

Examples

To massacre villagers
During the war, invading forces massacred hundreds of villagers.
International media reported that government troops had massacred civilians in the city.
The rebels threatened to massacre anyone who resisted their rule.
The invading army massacred the villagers without mercy.
Poachers massacred hundreds of elephants for their ivory last year.
The rebels threatened to massacre anyone who opposed their rule.

  • synonym: murder - to kill someone intentionally and unlawfully
  • antonym: save - to rescue or prevent from being killed
  • synonym: slaughter - to kill a large number of people or animals cruelly
  • antonym: spare - to refrain from killing or harming
