atraumatic

adjective
not caused by injury
7.1
Pronunciation: /ə.trɑː.ˈmæt.ɪk/ /; / /eɪ.trɑː.ˈmæt.ɪk/ (AmE), /ə.trɔː.ˈmæt.ɪk/ /; / /eɪ.trɔː.ˈmæt.ɪk/ (BrE)
not caused by or not causing injury or trauma, especially in a medical context

Examples

An atraumatic fracture
The patient presented with an atraumatic fracture, which developed without any obvious injury.
Doctors noted an atraumatic swelling in the joint, suggesting a medical cause rather than an accident.
An atraumatic subdural hematoma can occur in elderly patients without any history of a fall.

Using atraumatic instruments helps protect delicate tissues during surgery.
Nurses are trained to use atraumatic methods when caring for young patients.
