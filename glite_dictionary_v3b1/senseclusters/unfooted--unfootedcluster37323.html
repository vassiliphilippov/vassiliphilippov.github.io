unfooted

adjective
without metrical feet
9.1
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈfʊtɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈfʊtɪd / (BrE)
not arranged in metrical feet; lacking a regular rhythmic meter in a line of poetry

Examples

Unfooted verse
The poet chose an unfooted structure to emphasize the unpredictability of the theme.
Critics noted that the unfooted lines gave the poem a sense of freedom and spontaneity.
Some readers found the unfooted verse challenging but appreciated its break from tradition.

Related Terms

  • antonym: metrical - having a regular rhythmic structure in poetry
  • synonym: unrhymed - not having rhyme, especially in poetry
