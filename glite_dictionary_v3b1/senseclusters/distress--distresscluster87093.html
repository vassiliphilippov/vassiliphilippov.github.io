distress

noun
extreme physical pain or discomfort
3.9
Pronunciation: / dɪˈstrɛs / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɛs / (BrE)
a state of great pain, anxiety, sadness, or serious difficulty; extreme suffering or danger

Examples

Emotional distress
After the accident, his cries of distress could be heard throughout the building.
Emergency staff are trained to identify when someone is in severe physical distress.
The patient showed clear signs of distress during the examination.
A red flare is used to indicate that a boat is in distress.
The government sent aid to regions suffering severe distress after the storm.
The stranded hikers signaled for help, showing they were in distress.
After losing his job, he experienced significant distress and had trouble sleeping.
She called her friend to talk about her distress during a difficult time in her life.
The news of the accident caused widespread distress in the community.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agony - intense pain or suffering
  • synonym: anguish - severe mental or physical pain or suffering
  • antonym: comfort - a state of physical ease and freedom from pain
  • synonym: danger - the possibility of harm or injury
  • antonym: relief - alleviation or removal of distress or discomfort
