proton

noun
positively charged subatomic particle
5.0 3.7
Pronunciation: /ˈproʊ.tən/ /; / /ˈproʊ.tɑːn/ (AmE), /ˈprəʊ.tɒn/ /; / /ˈprəʊ.tən/ (BrE)
a stable subatomic particle with a positive electric charge, equal in magnitude to the negative charge of an electron, found in the nucleus of an atom

Examples

A proton particle
A hydrogen atom consists of a single proton and one electron.
Scientists accelerated a beam of protons to study particle collisions.
The number of protons in the nucleus determines the element's atomic number.

Related Terms

  • antonym: electron - a stable subatomic particle with a negative charge
  • synonym: neutron - a subatomic particle with no electric charge, found in the nucleus of an atom
