margin of error

noun
allowed degree of error
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑrdʒɪn əv ˈɛrər / (AmE), / ˈmɑːdʒɪn əv ˈɛrər / (BrE)
an allowed or calculated degree of error, especially to ensure safety or accuracy

Examples

A margin of error allowed
Engineers include a margin of error in their calculations to prevent structural failures.
The poll's margin of error suggests the actual results could vary by several points.
When reporting survey results, researchers always mention the margin of error.
