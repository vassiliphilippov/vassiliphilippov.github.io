atomic

adjective
indivisible operation; all-or-nothing (computing)
Pronunciation: / 59c8t51d0m6ak / (AmE), / 59c8t52m6ak / (BrE)
(in computing and distributed systems) indivisible; describing an operation or transaction that either completes fully or has no effect (all-or-nothing).

Examples

Atomic operation
They used an atomic swap to exchange tokens without risk of partial settlement.
The database applies atomic transactions so either every change is saved or none is.
Engineers ensure each payment operation is atomic to prevent inconsistent balances across nodes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: all-or-nothing - a property meaning complete success or complete failure with no partial effect
  • synonym: atomic transaction - a transaction that is indivisible and either commits fully or has no effect
