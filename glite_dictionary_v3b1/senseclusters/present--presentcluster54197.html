present

noun
grammatical tense for current actions or states
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛzənt / (AmE), / ˈprɛzənt / (BrE)
the verb tense used to express actions or states happening at the time of speaking or regularly

Examples

Present simple tense
In English, the present is often formed by using the base form of the verb.
She explains grammar rules using the present tense.
The teacher asked us to rewrite the story in the present.

Related Terms

  • antonym: future - the verb tense used to describe actions or states that will happen in the future
  • antonym: past - the verb tense used to describe actions or states that happened in the past
