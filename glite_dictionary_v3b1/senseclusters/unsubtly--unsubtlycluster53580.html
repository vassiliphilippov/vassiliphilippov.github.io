unsubtly

adverb
obviously, not delicately
5.9
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈsʌtli / (AmE), / ʌnˈsʌtli / (BrE)
in a way that is obvious, lacking subtlety, or not at all delicate or indirect

Examples

To unsubtly hint
He unsubtly hinted that he wanted a promotion during the meeting.
The advertisement unsubtly targeted young people with its flashy graphics and loud music.
She unsubtly rolled her eyes when she disagreed with the suggestion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blatantly - in an open and unashamed manner
  • synonym: obviously - in a way that is easy to see or understand
  • antonym: subtly - in a delicate or not obvious way
