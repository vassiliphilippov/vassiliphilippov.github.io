corpuscle

noun
blood cell; especially red or white blood cell
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔːrˌpʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈkɔː.pʌs.l̩ / (BrE)
a cell, especially a blood cell such as an erythrocyte (red blood cell) or leukocyte (white blood cell), sometimes also including platelets

Examples

A blood corpuscle
A decrease in white corpuscles can weaken the immune system.
Red blood corpuscles are responsible for transporting oxygen throughout the body.
Scientists examined the corpuscles under a microscope to diagnose the disease.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cell - the basic structural and functional unit of all organisms
  • degree lower: red blood cell - an erythrocyte; a cell that carries oxygen in the blood
  • degree higher: white blood cell - a leukocyte; a cell that combats infection in the blood
