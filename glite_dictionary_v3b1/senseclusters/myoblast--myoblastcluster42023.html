myoblast

noun
muscle precursor cell
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪəˌblæst / (AmE), / ˈmaɪəʊˌblɑːst / (BrE)
an immature cell that develops into muscle tissue

Examples

A myoblast cell
A myoblast is essential for the formation of new muscle fibers.
Scientists study myoblasts to understand how muscles grow and repair themselves.
During development, each myoblast fuses with others to create mature muscle cells.
A myoblast is essential for the early stages of muscle development in embryos.
Scientists study myoblasts to understand how muscle tissue repairs itself after injury.
During growth, each myoblast can fuse with others to form new muscle fibers.
