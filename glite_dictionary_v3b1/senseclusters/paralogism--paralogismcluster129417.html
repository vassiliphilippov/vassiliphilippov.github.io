paralogism

noun
unintentionally invalid argument
Pronunciation: / pəˈɹælədʒɪzəm / (AmE), / pəˈralədʒɪzəm / (BrE)
An argument that is invalid due to faulty reasoning, made unintentionally rather than through deliberate deception.

Examples

A paralogism in reasoning
Critical thinking helps to identify a paralogism before accepting an argument as valid.
Her essay contained a subtle paralogism that most readers failed to recognize.
The philosopher pointed out the paralogism in his opponent’s argument during the debate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fallacy - a mistaken belief or argument based on unsound reasoning
  • antonym: sophism - a deliberately deceptive argument
