compartmentalize

verb
separate into parts or categories
Pronunciation: / kəmˌpɑrtˈmɛntəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / kəmˌpɑːtˈmentəlaɪz / (BrE)
to separate something into distinct parts, categories, or sections, often to keep them isolated from one another

Examples

To compartmentalize life and work
He tends to compartmentalize his work and personal life to avoid stress.
It's not healthy to compartmentalize your emotions and never talk about them.
Some managers compartmentalize tasks to increase efficiency within their teams.
It's not healthy to compartmentalise your emotions and refuse to discuss your feelings.
Managers often compartmentalise tasks to improve efficiency within teams.
She tends to compartmentalise her work life and personal life to avoid stress.

  • antonym: integrate - to combine into a whole; to make unified
