inquisition

noun
intense or intrusive interrogation
8.0
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌkwɪzɪˈʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌkwɪzɪˈʃən / (BrE)
a severe or prolonged questioning, often in a way that invades someone's privacy or rights

Examples

A harsh inquisition
After hours of inquisition, the suspect finally confessed.
She dreaded the inquisition that would follow her late arrival home.
The lawyer objected to the prosecutor's relentless inquisition of his client.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interrogation - an act of questioning, especially formally or thoroughly
  • degree lower: interview - a formal meeting in which questions are asked
