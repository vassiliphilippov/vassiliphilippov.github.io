nitroprusside

noun
chemical test reagent
Pronunciation: / ˌnaɪtroʊˈpruːsaɪd / (AmE), / ˌnaɪtrəʊˈpruːsaɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound used in laboratory tests to detect substances like ketone bodies or sulfhydryl compounds by causing a color change

Examples

Nitroprusside test
The doctor ordered a nitroprusside test to check for ketones in the patient's urine.
A positive result with nitroprusside indicates the presence of certain compounds in the sample.
Laboratories often use nitroprusside because it produces a clear color change when reacting with specific chemicals.

Related Terms
