anamnesis

noun
recollection of past events
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˌænəmˈnisɪs / (AmE), / ˌan.amˈniː.sɪs / (BrE)
the ability to recall or remember past events or experiences; recollection

Examples

Philosophical anamnesis
Philosophers often discuss anamnesis as the soul's recollection of knowledge from previous lives.
The therapist encouraged anamnesis to help the patient process traumatic memories.
Through deep meditation, she experienced a sudden anamnesis of her childhood.

Related Terms

  • antonym: amnesia - loss of memory
  • synonym: recollection - the act of remembering something
