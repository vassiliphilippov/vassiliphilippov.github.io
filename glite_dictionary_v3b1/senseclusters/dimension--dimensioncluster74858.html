dimension

noun
measurable extent (length, width, height)
1.0
Pronunciation: / dəˈmɛnʃən /; / dɪˈmɛnʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈmɛnʃən / (BrE)
a measurable extent such as length, width, or height; a physical measurement or coordinate

Examples

Dimension of the box
Accurately measuring each dimension of the box is important before shipping.
Engineers must consider every dimension to ensure the structure is safe and stable.
The room's largest dimension is its height, reaching almost five meters.
Each point in three-dimensional space can be specified by three dimensions: length, width, and height.
In physics class, we learned how each dimension represents a different direction in space.
The robot calculates its location using sensors to determine its exact dimension coordinates.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coordinate - a set of numbers determining the position of a point
  • synonym: extent - the area covered by something; scope or range
  • synonym: measurement - the size, length, or amount of something, as established by measuring
