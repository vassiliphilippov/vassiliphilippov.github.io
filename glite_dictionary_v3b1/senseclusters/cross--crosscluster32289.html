cross

verb
move across or pass over
1.0 83/m
Pronunciation: / krÉ‘s / (AmE), / krÉ’s / (BrE)
to move, travel, or extend across, over, or past something; or to meet/intersect with something or someone

Examples

To cross the street
Hundreds of people cross the river every day to reach the city.
She plans to cross the desert alone next year.
The hikers crossed the bridge at dawn, enjoying the view.
The festival crosses over two weekends in July.
The railway line crosses the entire city from east to west.
This tradition crosses several generations in my family.
Our paths crossed as I was leaving and she was arriving.
The ships crossed each other in the foggy channel.
The two trains crossed just outside the station.

Synonyms

  • traverse
    5.6 2.14/m
    traverse is a formal verb meaning to travel across an area or region, while cross is the common general term.
  • get across
    1.8 1.02/m
    get across means moving from one side to the other, while cross is used more broadly for many crossing actions.
  • pass
    1.0 108/m
    pass means to move past or through something on the way, while cross can mean to go from one side to another.
  • move through
    2.1 7.02/m
    move through means to pass inside an area as part of travel, while cross often suggests going over or across that area.
  • get over
    3.3 2.24/m
    get over means to pass above or across an obstacle or distance, while cross is more general for going from side to side.
  • cover
    3.3 11/m
    cover means to travel across a set distance or area, often measured, while cross focuses on going from one side to another.
  • span
    3.4 10/m
    span means to stretch across a space or time, often without moving, while cross implies active movement or meeting.
  • cut across
    3.2 0.68/m
    cut across means to go through as a shortcut, often quickly and directly, while cross simply means to go from side to side.
  • cut through
    3.0 3.41/m
    cut through means to force or move straight through something solid or crowded, while cross can mean moving over or between areas.
  • intersect
    3.9 3.95/m
    intersect means two lines or paths meet at a point, while cross can mean moving across or meeting in a wider range of situations.
  • intercross
    6.5 0.02/m
    intercross means to cross each other, often repeatedly or in patterns, while cross is the common general word for meeting or crossing once.
  • pass over
    3.3 2.93/m
    pass over means to move across or above something, sometimes skipping it, while cross usually means to go from one side to the other.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crossing - the act of going across something
  • antonym: diverge - to move or extend in different directions from a common point
  • synonym: intersect - to cross or pass through each other, especially of lines or paths
  • synonym: span - to extend across a period or area
  • synonym: traverse - to travel across or through an area
