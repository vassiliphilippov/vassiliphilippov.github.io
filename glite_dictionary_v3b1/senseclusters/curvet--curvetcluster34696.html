curvet

noun
a horse's graceful leap
9.6 8.8
Pronunciation: /kər.ˈveɪ/ /; / /kər.ˈvet/ (AmE), /kə.ˈveɪ/ /; / /kə.ˈvet/ (BrE)
a light leap made by a horse in which both hind legs leave the ground before the forelegs come down, especially as a showy movement in classical horsemanship

Examples

A horse's curvet
In classical dressage, the curvet is a challenging maneuver for both horse and rider.
The horse’s sudden curvet startled those watching from the fence.
The stallion performed a perfect curvet, delighting the audience with its grace.
