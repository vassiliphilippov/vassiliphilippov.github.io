chip

noun
triangular wooden float on a log line
Pronunciation: / tʃɪp / (AmE), / tʃɪp / (BrE)
A triangular wooden float attached to the end of a log line, used especially in navigation for measuring a vessel's speed through water.

Examples

A log line chip
Every log line had a chip secured at its end for accurate readings.
The old chip was replaced after years of service on the vessel.
The sailor tossed the chip overboard to measure the ship's speed.
