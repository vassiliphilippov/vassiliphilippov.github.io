turn up

verb
find by digging in the ground
2.2
Pronunciation: / tɝn ʌp / (AmE), / tɜːn ʌp / (BrE)
to find, discover, or have something be revealed, especially by searching or by chance

Examples

To turn up evidence
During the excavation, the archaeologists turned up several ancient coins.
Sometimes, you might turn up unexpected items when digging in old gardens.
They managed to turn up a rusty key while planting flowers in the yard.
I searched everywhere but nothing turned up.
Sometimes lost objects turn up in the most unexpected places.
The police investigation turned up several important clues.
After weeks of searching, the missing painting finally turned up in an attic.
He turned up positive for the virus after the second test.
The documents turned up to be forgeries during the investigation.
