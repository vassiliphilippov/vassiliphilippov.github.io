southern cypress

noun
cypress tree native to southeastern U.S. swamps
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌðərn ˈsaɪprəs / (AmE), / ˈsʌðən ˈsʌɪprəs / (BrE)
a common cypress tree native to the southeastern United States, especially found in coastal swamps and river bottoms, with a trunk expanded at the base

Examples

A southern cypress tree
Many swamps in the southeastern United States are dominated by southern cypress trees.
The southern cypress thrives in the wetlands of Louisiana and Mississippi.
The distinctive flared trunk of the southern cypress helps it adapt to flooded environments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bald cypress - another term for the southern cypress tree, Taxodium distichum
