pour one out

verb
spill or drink in tribute to someone
3.6 7.2
Pronunciation: /pɔːr wʌn aʊt/ /; / /pɔːr wən aʊt/ (AmE), /pɔː wʌn aʊt/ /; / /pɔː wɒn aʊt/ (BrE)
to honor the memory of someone, especially one who has died, by spilling a small amount of drink (usually alcohol) on the ground or by drinking in tribute

Examples

To pour one out for friends
Every year, we pour one out for our classmates who have passed away.
He stopped at the cemetery to pour one out for his uncle.
They gathered after the game and poured one out for their former coach.
