platonically

adverb
in an idealistic way
Pronunciation: / pləˈtɑːnɪkli / (AmE), / pləˈtɒnɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to Plato's ideas, especially focusing on abstract or ideal concepts rather than physical reality

Examples

Think platonically
The artist spoke platonically about the concept of perfect beauty, ignoring real-world imperfections.
They debated platonically about justice, focusing on ideals rather than specific laws.
In philosophy class, we often discuss topics platonically before considering their practical applications.

Related Terms

  • antonym: practically - in a way that relates to real situations or actions
  • synonym: theoretically - in a way that is based on theory or ideas
