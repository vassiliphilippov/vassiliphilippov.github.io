paraldehyde

noun
a sedative and solvent chemical compound
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəˈældəˌhaɪd / (AmE), / ˌparəˈaldɪhaɪd / (BrE)
a colorless, volatile liquid (a cyclic trimer of acetaldehyde) used as a sedative and a solvent, particularly in medicine and chemistry

Examples

Paraldehyde liquid
Due to its distinct odor, the presence of paraldehyde can be detected even in small amounts.
Laboratory technicians often use paraldehyde as a solvent for various chemical reactions.
The doctor administered paraldehyde to calm the patient during the seizure.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: acetaldehyde - a colorless volatile liquid aldehyde; precursor to paraldehyde formation
  • synonym: chloral hydrate - a sedative and hypnotic medication
