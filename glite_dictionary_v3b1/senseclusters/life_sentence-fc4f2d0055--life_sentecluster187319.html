life sentence

noun
prison term for a convict's natural life
5.4 4.6
Pronunciation: /laɪf ˈsen.təns/ (AmE), /laɪf ˈsen.təns/ (BrE)
a prison term that lasts for the prisoner's natural life, or for a very long period, sometimes with the possibility of parole

Examples

A life sentence without parole
After his third conviction, he was given a life sentence without the possibility of parole.
She campaigned for reform, arguing that a life sentence should not be automatic for all serious crimes.
The judge explained why a life sentence was appropriate in this murder case.

Related Terms

  • antonym: parole - release of a prisoner before the sentence is complete
  • synonym: sentence - judicial punishment assigned to a convicted person
