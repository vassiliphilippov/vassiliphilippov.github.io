digestion

noun
decomposition of organic matter (e.g. sewage)
Pronunciation: / daɪˈdʒɛstʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈdʒɛstʃən / (BrE)
the process of breaking down organic matter, such as sewage, by bacteria, chemical action, or heat, often for waste treatment or biogas production

Examples

Waste digestion
Accelerating the digestion process can increase the overall efficiency of waste management systems.
Modern sewage treatment plants use bacterial digestion to reduce waste volume and odors.
The digestion of organic matter produces methane that can be used as a fuel source.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decomposition - the process of decaying or rotting
  • synonym: fermentation - the process of breaking down substances by bacteria, yeasts, or other microorganisms
