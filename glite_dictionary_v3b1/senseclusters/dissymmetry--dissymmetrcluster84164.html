dissymmetry

noun
absence of symmetry; imbalance
7.7 7.3
Pronunciation: /dɪs.ˈɪm.ə.triː/ (AmE), /dɪs.ˈɪm.ə.triː/ (BrE)
A lack of symmetry or proportion; an imbalance among the parts of something.

Examples

A dissymmetry between parts
A certain dissymmetry between the two sides of the argument was obvious to the observers.
Architects must be careful that dissymmetry in their designs does not create a sense of imbalance.
The painting’s dissymmetry gives it a dynamic and unconventional beauty.
