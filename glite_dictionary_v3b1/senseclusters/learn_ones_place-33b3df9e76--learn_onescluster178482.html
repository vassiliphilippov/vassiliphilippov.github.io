learn one's place

verb
understand one's role or position
6.6
Pronunciation: / lɝn wʌnz pleɪs /; / lɝːn wʌnz pleɪs / (AmE), / lɜːn wʌnz pleɪs / (BrE)
to understand and accept your proper role or position within a group, organization, or society, especially in relation to hierarchy or status

Examples

To learn one's place in a group
After missing an important deadline, he was told to learn his place in the company.
She had to learn her place when she joined the strict boarding school.
Some people need to learn their place before they try to challenge authority.
After being reprimanded by her manager, she finally learned her place in the company.
He tried to speak out, but soon learned his place among the senior staff.
Moving to a new school, she quickly learned her place in the social hierarchy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: know one's place - be aware of one's proper position or role, especially in a hierarchy
