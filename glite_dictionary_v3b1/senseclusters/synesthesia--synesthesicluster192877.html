synesthesia

noun
condition where senses combine involuntarily
8.5 8.0
Pronunciation: /sɪn.əs.ˈθiː.zɪ.ə/ /; / /sɪn.ɛs.ˈθiː.zɪ.ə/ (AmE), /sɪn.ɛs.ˈθiː.zɪ.ə/ /; / /sɪn.əs.ˈθiː.zɪ.ə/ (BrE)
A condition in which the stimulation of one sense leads to the automatic and involuntary experience of another sense, such as seeing colors when hearing music.

Examples

Synesthesia condition
Her synesthesia made the days of the week appear as different colors in her mind.
Scientists are researching how synesthesia might enhance memory and creativity.
Some people with synesthesia perceive specific colors when they hear certain musical notes.
His experience of synaesthesia made reading letters appear as vibrant colors.
Some people with synaesthesia might taste flavors when they hear certain words.
The artist claimed that her synaesthesia allowed her to paint music as colors on the canvas.
