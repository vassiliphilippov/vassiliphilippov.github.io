unshutter

verb
to remove shutters
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈʃʌtər / (AmE), / ʌnˈʃʌtə / (BrE)
to remove shutters from a window or building, usually to let in light or air

Examples

To unshutter a window
After the storm passed, they decided to unshutter the windows and let in the sunlight.
The caretaker will unshutter the old house before the family arrives for the summer.
Every spring, residents unshutter their cottages to prepare for the warmer months.

Related Terms

  • synonym: open - to make something no longer closed or covered
  • antonym: shutter - to close or cover with shutters
