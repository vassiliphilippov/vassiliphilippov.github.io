truss

noun
triangular architectural bracket
7.7 5.9
Pronunciation: /trʌs/ (AmE), /trʌs/ (BrE)
a strong framework or bracket used to support a roof, bridge, or other structure

Examples

Roof truss
An elegant truss was used as a bracket beneath the archway.
Architectural plans showed a decorative brick truss on each corner of the building.
The stone truss above the doorway added both support and decoration to the old church.
Engineers inspected the steel truss for signs of wear before opening the bridge to traffic.
The architect chose a triangular truss system to maximize both strength and aesthetic appeal.
The roof is supported by a wooden truss designed to bear heavy snow loads.

  • synonym: girder - a large beam that supports structures, especially bridges or buildings
