A person who frequently changes their opinions, loyalties, or public positions to match whatever is currently popular or advantageous.

Examples

Political weathervane
During the campaign the candidate acted like a weathervane, adjusting promises to win each region.
Colleagues labeled him a weathervane after he repeatedly adopted whatever policy was popular.
The board accused their CEO of being a weathervane who changed strategy every quarter.
