innominata

noun
old name for artery
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ə.ˈmɪn.ə.tə/ (AmE), /ɪn.ə.ˈmɪn.ə.tə/ (BrE)
an older term for the brachiocephalic artery, the large blood vessel from the aortic arch supplying the right arm and head

Examples

Innominata artery
The surgeon carefully examined the innominata before proceeding with the operation.
A blockage in the innominata can lead to serious complications affecting the right arm and head.
Older medical texts often refer to the brachiocephalic artery as the innominata.
