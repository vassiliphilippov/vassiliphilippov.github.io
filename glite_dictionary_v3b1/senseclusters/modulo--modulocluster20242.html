modulo

preposition
mathematical remainder
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːdʒəˌloʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɒdjʊˌləʊ / (BrE)
used to indicate the remainder when one number is divided by another in mathematics.

Examples

Modulo 5 division
When you divide 17 by 5, the modulo is 2.
In programming, the modulo operation is often used to check if a number is even or odd.
The clock uses a 12-hour system, so 15 modulo 12 equals 3.

Related Terms

  • antonym: divisor - the number by which another number is divided
  • synonym: remainder - the amount left after division
