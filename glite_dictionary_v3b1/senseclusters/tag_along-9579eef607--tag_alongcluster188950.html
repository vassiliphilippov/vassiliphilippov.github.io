tag along

verb
go with someone, often uninvited
Pronunciation: / tæɡ əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / tæɡ əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to go somewhere with someone, usually informally or without being specifically invited

Examples

To tag along quietly
Do you mind if I tag along to the market with you?
He didn't invite me, but I decided to tag along anyway.
Little kids often want to tag along with their older siblings wherever they go.
Do you mind if I tag along with you to the store?
My sister loves to tag along whenever I go to the park.
Some friends decided to tag along for the road trip at the last minute.

  • synonym: follow - to go or come after a person or thing
  • synonym: join - to go somewhere in order to be with someone
