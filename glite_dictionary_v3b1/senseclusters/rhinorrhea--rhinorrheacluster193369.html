rhinorrhea

noun
nasal fluid discharge
9.3
Pronunciation: /raɪ.nə.ˈriː.ə/ (AmE), /raɪ.nə.ˈriː.ə/ (BrE)
the discharge of fluid, especially cerebrospinal fluid, from the nose, often following injury or surgery to the head.

Examples

Head injury rhinorrhea
The patient developed rhinorrhoea after undergoing surgery for a head injury.
Doctors monitored her closely when persistent rhinorrhoea appeared following the accident.
Clear rhinorrhoea can sometimes indicate a serious underlying condition after trauma.

Related Terms

  • antonym: epistaxis - bleeding from the nose
  • synonym: runny nose - a condition where fluid drips from the nose
