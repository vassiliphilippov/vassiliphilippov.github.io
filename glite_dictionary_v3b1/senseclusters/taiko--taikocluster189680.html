taiko

proper noun
retired Japanese regent
Pronunciation: / taɪˈkoʊ / (AmE), / taɪˈkəʊ / (BrE)
a historical Japanese title for a retired regent, especially referring to Toyotomi Hideyoshi

Examples

Taiko title holder
Toyotomi Hideyoshi was given the title of taiko after retiring from his position as regent.
The term taiko is closely associated with powerful leaders in Japanese history.
After stepping down, he was honored as a taiko and continued to influence politics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: regent - a person appointed to administer a country because the monarch is a minor or absent
