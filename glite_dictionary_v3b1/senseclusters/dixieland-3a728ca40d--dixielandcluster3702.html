Dixieland

noun
style of jazz
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪksiˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈdɪksiˌlænd / (BrE)
a style of jazz music that developed in the Southern United States, known for its lively rhythms and group improvisation

Examples

Dixieland jazz music
The lively sound of Dixieland filled the streets during the parade.
She learned to play the trumpet by joining a local Dixieland band.
Many music festivals in New Orleans feature traditional Dixieland performances.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: bebop - a complex, fast-paced style of jazz
  • synonym: jazz - a genre of music characterized by swing and improvisation
  • degree lower: ragtime - an early style of jazz with syncopated rhythms
