structuration

noun
creation of social structures
9.9
Pronunciation: /strʌk.tʃəˈreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /strək.tʃəˈreɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /strʌk.tʃəˈreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /strək.tʃəˈreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process by which social structures are created and maintained through the interaction of individual actions, rules, and resources

Examples

Structuration of society
The theory of structuration explains how individual actions contribute to the formation of social institutions.
Researchers use structuration to analyze the ongoing relationship between people and the rules that shape society.
Understanding structuration helps sociologists see how social norms are both created and maintained over time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: institutionalization - the process of establishing something as a norm or standard in society
  • synonym: socialization - the process by which individuals learn and adopt the norms and values of society
