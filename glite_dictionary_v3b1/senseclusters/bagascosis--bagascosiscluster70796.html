bagascosis

noun
lung disease from inhaling bagasse dust
Pronunciation: / ˌbæɡəˈskoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌbaɡəˈskəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A lung disease (alveolitis) caused by inhaling sugarcane dust (bagasse), characterized by inflammation of the alveoli.

Examples

Chronic bagascosis disease
Pulmonary fibrosis was diagnosed as a complication of chronic bagascosis.
Regular use of protective masks can help prevent bagascosis among sugar factory employees.
The worker developed bagascosis after years of exposure to sugarcane dust in the mill.
