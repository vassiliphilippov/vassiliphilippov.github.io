glutamic acid

noun
an amino acid used in proteins and flavor enhancers
Pronunciation: / ɡluˈtæmɪk ˌæsɪd / (AmE), / ɡluːˈtæmɪk ˌæsɪd / (BrE)
an amino acid found in many proteins, important in plant metabolism, and used as a flavor enhancer in the form of monosodium glutamate

Examples

Glutamic acid flavor enhancer
Many fermented foods naturally contain glutamic acid, which contributes to their umami flavor.
Researchers study how glutamic acid functions as a neurotransmitter in the human brain.
The label lists glutamic acid as one of the protein components in the supplement.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: amino acid - organic compounds forming the building blocks of proteins
  • synonym: monosodium glutamate - the sodium salt of glutamic acid used as a flavor enhancer
