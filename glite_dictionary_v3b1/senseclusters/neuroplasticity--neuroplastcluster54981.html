neuroplasticity

noun
brain's adaptability
7.1
Pronunciation: /nʊr.oʊ.plæ.ˈstɪs.ɪ.tɪ/ /; / /njuː.roʊ.plæ.ˈstɪs.ɪ.tɪ/ (AmE), /njʊə.rəʊ.plæ.ˈstɪs.ɪ.tɪ/ /; / /njuː.rəʊ.plæ.ˈstɪs.ɪ.tɪ/ (BrE)
the brain's ability to change and adapt by forming new neural connections throughout life

Examples

Brain neuroplasticity
Recent studies show that neuroplasticity allows adults to learn new skills even later in life.
After his stroke, doctors relied on neuroplasticity to help him regain lost abilities.
Therapists design activities to stimulate neuroplasticity in patients recovering from brain injuries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brain plasticity - the capacity of the brain to change its structure and function
  • synonym: neurogenesis - the process of forming new neurons in the brain
