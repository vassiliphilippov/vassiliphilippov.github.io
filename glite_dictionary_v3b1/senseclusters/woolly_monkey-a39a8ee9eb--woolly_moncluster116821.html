woolly monkey

noun
a large South American monkey with woolly fur
Pronunciation: / ˈwʊli ˌmʌŋki / (AmE), / ˈwʊli ˌmʌŋki / (BrE)
a large monkey native to the forests of the Amazon and Orinoco basins, characterized by dark skin and thick, woolly fur

Examples

A woolly monkey in forest
A woolly monkey uses its prehensile tail to swing through the rainforest canopy.
Researchers observed a group of woolly monkeys feeding in the treetops during their field study.
The woolly monkey is hunted for its meat in some parts of South America.
