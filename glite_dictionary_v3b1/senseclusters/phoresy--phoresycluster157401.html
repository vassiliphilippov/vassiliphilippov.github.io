phoresy

noun
organism transport relationship
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔːrəsi / (AmE), / ˈfɒrəsi / (BrE)
a relationship in which one organism attaches to another solely for transportation without harming it

Examples

Phoresy on animals
Certain mites rely on phoresy to move from one animal host to another.
The beetle's larvae use phoresy by hitching rides on adult insects to reach new food sources.
Researchers observed phoresy in action as tiny organisms traveled on the backs of larger animals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: commensalism - a relationship where one organism benefits and the other is unaffected
  • antonym: parasitism - a relationship where one organism benefits at the expense of another
