reductively

adverb
oversimplifying manner
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdʌktɪvli / (AmE), / rɪˈdʌktɪvli / (BrE)
in a way that oversimplifies something, ignoring important details or complexity

Examples

Reductively explain a topic
The article explained the complex topic reductively, leaving out many important aspects.
He described her achievements reductively, failing to recognize the full scope of her work.
Some debates treat cultural issues reductively, ignoring their deeper causes and effects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: simplistically - in a way that treats something as simpler than it really is
  • antonym: thoroughly - in a way that is complete and detailed
