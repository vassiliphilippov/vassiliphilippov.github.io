decoction

noun
extraction by boiling, esp. in medicine
7.6 7.7
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈkɑːk.ʃən/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈkɒk.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of extracting the active or water-soluble components of a substance by boiling, especially for medicinal purposes

Examples

A decoction of roots
The herbalist prepared a decoction of roots to treat the patient's illness.
To maximize potency, the dried bark was boiled into a strong decoction.
Traditional remedies often use a decoction rather than a simple infusion to extract ingredients.

Related Terms

  • synonym: infusion - the process of extracting flavors or active ingredients by soaking in liquid, usually at lower temperatures
