garnish

verb
legally withhold wages to pay a debt
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑrnɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːnɪʃ / (BrE)
(of an employer or authority) to legally withhold part of someone's wages in order to pay a debt, such as child support.

Examples

To garnish wages
Federal law limits how much of your paycheck can be garnished by creditors.
If you fail to pay child support, the state may garnish your income automatically.
The court ordered his employer to garnish his wages for unpaid taxes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: seize - to take hold of suddenly and forcibly, especially by legal right
