espouser

verb
to marry (archaic)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˈspaʊzər / (AmE), / ɪˈspaʊzə / (BrE)
to marry or take someone as one's spouse, especially in an archaic or literary context

Examples

To espouser a princess

It was once common for nobles to espouser for political alliances rather than love.
The poet wrote of his desire to espouser his beloved under the old oak tree.
