by-product

noun
secondary result or consequence
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪ ˌprɑdʌkt / (AmE), / ˈbaɪ ˌprɒdʌkt / (BrE)
A secondary result or consequence, often unintended, that accompanies the main outcome of an action or process.

Examples

A by-product of success
Improved efficiency can sometimes be a positive by-product of stricter regulations.
Increased traffic is a by-product of the new shopping mall.
Noise is often an unwelcome by-product of urban development.

Related Terms

  • antonym: main effect - the primary intended result or consequence
  • synonym: side effect - an additional, typically undesirable effect of something
