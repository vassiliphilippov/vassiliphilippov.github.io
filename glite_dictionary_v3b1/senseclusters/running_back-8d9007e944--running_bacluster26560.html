running back

noun
offensive football player who carries the ball
6.0 2.4
Pronunciation: /ˈrʌn.ɪŋ bæk/ (AmE), /ˈrʌn.ɪŋ bæk/ (BrE)
(in American football) a player on the offensive team, typically a fullback or halfback, whose main role is to advance the ball by running with it.

Examples

The running back runs
After fumbling the ball, the running back was quickly tackled by the defense.
He became a running back because of his speed and agility on the field.
The team's star running back scored two touchdowns in the final quarter.
