advance death benefit

noun
life insurance benefit paid before death
7.4 5.6
Pronunciation: /əd.ˈvæns deθ ˈben.ə.fɪt/ /; / /ˈæd.væns deθ ˈben.ə.fɪt/ (AmE), /əd.ˈvɑːns deθ ˈben.ɪ.fɪt/ /; / /ˈæd.vɑːns deθ ˈben.ɪ.fɪt/ (BrE)
a payment of a portion of the life insurance death benefit to a policyholder who is terminally ill and expected to die within a short period, usually six months

Examples

An advance death benefit payment
Her policy included an advance death benefit that provided financial support after she was diagnosed with a terminal illness.
Some insurance companies offer an advance death benefit to help with medical expenses during a policyholder's final months.
The advance death benefit allowed him to cover the cost of hospice care without waiting for the full payout.
