scope

verb
to examine closely
3.7
Pronunciation: / skoʊp / (AmE), / skəʊp / (BrE)
to look at or examine something carefully, often to assess or investigate it

Examples

To scope the area
The detective decided to scope the building before entering.
We should scope out the competition before launching our new product.
She scoped the room for any signs of danger before proceeding.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ignore - to pay no attention to something
  • synonym: inspect - to look at something carefully to check its condition
  • synonym: survey - to examine or look at all of something
