ell

noun
old length unit
Pronunciation: / ɛl / (AmE), / ɛl / (BrE)
an old unit of length, especially for measuring cloth, used in Britain and other countries, typically about 45 inches or 1.14 meters

Examples

Three ells of cloth
The merchant sold three ells of fine linen to the tailor.
In the past, cloth was often measured by the ell in British markets.
A bolt of fabric was said to be ten ells long in the old records.

Related Terms

  • synonym: meter - the basic unit of length in the metric system, equal to 100 centimeters
  • synonym: yard - a unit of length equal to 36 inches or 0.9144 meters
