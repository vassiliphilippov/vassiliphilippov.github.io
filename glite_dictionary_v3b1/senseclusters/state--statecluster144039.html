state

noun
condition or situation of something
1.6 121/m
Pronunciation: / steÉªt / (AmE), / steÉªt / (BrE)
the condition or situation of something or someone at a particular time, including mental or emotional condition

Examples

A happy state
His mental state improved after a good night's sleep.
She was surprised by the poor state of the hotel room.
The building was in a dangerous state after the fire.
Don't get yourself into a state over something so minor.
I found him in a terrible state when I arrived, clearly upset and confused.
She was in such a state after the argument that she couldn't stop crying.

Synonyms

    2.9 64/m

  • situation
    1.9 238/m
    situation means the set of surrounding circumstances or affairs, while state means the overall condition at a particular time.
  • condition
    2.1 70/m
    condition refers to quality, appearance, or functioning at a time, while state covers any condition or situation more broadly.
  • state of mind
    3.4 4.91/m
    state of mind describes a person's current mood and feelings, while state covers any condition of a person or thing at a time.
  • mental state
    4.0 1.83/m
    mental state means a person's lasting psychological condition or wellbeing, while state can mean any condition at a particular time.
