boycott

noun
organized protest by refusing to deal
4.0 3.5
Pronunciation: /ˈbɔɪ.kət/ /; / /ˈbɔɪ.kɑːt/ (AmE), /ˈbɔɪ.kɒt/ (BrE)
an organized refusal to buy, use, or participate in something as a way of protesting against policies or actions

Examples

A boycott of products
A national boycott led to a significant drop in sales for the brand.
Many activists organized a boycott of the store in response to its environmental record.
The workers called for a boycott of the company’s products until better labor practices were adopted.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embargo - an official ban on trade or other commercial activity
  • antonym: patronize - to support a business by being a customer
  • synonym: protest - an act of expressing strong objection or disapproval
