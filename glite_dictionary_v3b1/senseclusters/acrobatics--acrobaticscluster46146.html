acrobatics

noun
aerial stunts in aircraft
Pronunciation: / ˌækrəˈbætɪks / (AmE), / ˌækrəˈbætɪks / (BrE)
The performance of complex or daring stunts in flight, especially while piloting an aircraft.

Examples

Aerial acrobatics
Learning acrobatics requires exceptional skill and concentration from every pilot.
Modern fighter jets are designed to handle extreme acrobatics with ease.
The pilot impressed the crowd with his spectacular acrobatics during the air show.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aerobatics - the practice of flying maneuvers involving aircraft attitudes not used in normal flight
