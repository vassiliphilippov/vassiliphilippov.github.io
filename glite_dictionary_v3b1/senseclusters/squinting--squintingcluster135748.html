squinting

adjective
having eyes partly closed to see better
4.7 2.6
Pronunciation: /ˈskwɪn.tɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈskwɪn.tɪŋ/ (BrE)
having the eyes partly closed in order to see more clearly, usually because of bright light or difficulty seeing

Examples

Squinting eyes
His squinting gaze suggested the sunlight was too bright for comfort.
She looked up at the sky with squinting eyes, trying to spot the airplane.
The photographer asked the children to open their squinting eyes wider for the picture.
