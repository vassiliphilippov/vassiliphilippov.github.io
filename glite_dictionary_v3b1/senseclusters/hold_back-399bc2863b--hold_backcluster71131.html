hold back

verb
retain for future use
2.0 3.2
Pronunciation: / hoʊld bæk / (AmE), / həʊld bæk / (BrE)
to keep something in reserve for possible future use or application; to retain rather than release or use immediately

Examples

To hold back supplies
Governments sometimes hold back supplies in case of unexpected demand.
She chose to hold back her opinion until she knew more about the situation.
The company decided to hold back some funds for emergencies.
