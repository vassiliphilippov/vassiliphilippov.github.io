recalcitrance

noun
stubborn resistance to authority
8.1
Pronunciation: / rɪˈkælsɪtrəns / (AmE), / rɪˈkælsɪtrəns / (BrE)
the quality of being stubbornly uncooperative or resistant to authority or control

Examples

Student recalcitrance
Despite repeated warnings, his recalcitrance only seemed to grow stronger over time.
The organization's recalcitrance in adopting new regulations frustrated government officials.
The student's recalcitrance made classroom management particularly challenging for the new teacher.

Related Terms

  • antonym: compliance - the act of conforming or yielding to authority
  • synonym: obstinacy - stubborn refusal to change one's opinion or course of action
