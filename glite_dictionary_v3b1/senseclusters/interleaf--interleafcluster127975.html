interleaf

noun
blank sheet between pages of a book
Pronunciation: / ˈɪntərˌlif / (AmE), / ˈɪntəˌliːf / (BrE)
A blank page inserted between the pages of a book.

Examples

A blank interleaf
A printed interleaf separated the two chapters, marking a significant change in narrative.
Some albums use a thin interleaf to prevent photographs from sticking together.
The restored manuscript included a interleaf between each section to protect the fragile illustrations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: insert - a sheet or page placed into a book
