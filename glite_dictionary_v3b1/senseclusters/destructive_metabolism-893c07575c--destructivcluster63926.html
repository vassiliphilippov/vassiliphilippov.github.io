destructive metabolism

noun
breakdown of complex substances with energy release
Pronunciation: / dɪˈstrʌktɪv məˈtæbəlɪzəm / (AmE), / dɪˈstrʌktɪv məˈtæbəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the process in living organisms by which complex substances are broken down into simpler ones, releasing energy

Examples

Destructive metabolism process
Certain hormones regulate both constructive and destructive metabolism in the human body.
During periods of starvation, destructive metabolism accelerates to provide energy from stored body tissues.
The study examined how destructive metabolism contributes to muscle loss in elderly patients.
