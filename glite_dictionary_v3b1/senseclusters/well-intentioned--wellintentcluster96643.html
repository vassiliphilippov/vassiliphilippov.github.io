well-intentioned

adjective
having good intentions
5.0 4.6
Pronunciation: / ˌwɛl.ɪnˈtɛnʃənd / (AmE), / ˌwɛl.ɪnˈtɛn.ʃənd / (BrE)
Having good or sincere intentions, even if the results are not successful or are misguided.

Examples

A well-intentioned person
Her well-intentioned advice ended up causing more confusion than help.
The well-intentioned campaign failed to address the community's actual needs.
Though well-intentioned, his actions made the situation worse.

Related Terms

  • antonym: malicious - intending to do harm
  • synonym: well-meaning - having good intentions, often without the desired effect
