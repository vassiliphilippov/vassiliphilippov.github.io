viewing

noun
showing of a film or television program
5.7 2.0
Pronunciation: /ˈvjuː.ɪŋ/ /; / /ˈvuː.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈvjuː.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
an occasion or event when something (such as a film, TV program, house, or artwork) is shown for people to see

Examples

A viewing party
Due to high demand, there will be an extra viewing scheduled for the film this weekend.
The museum will host a private viewing of the documentary before its public release.
We attended a special viewing of the director's latest movie last night.
We attended a special viewing of the artist's latest paintings at the gallery.
The real estate agent arranged several viewings of the house for interested buyers.
Tickets for the first viewing of the new movie sold out within hours.
