accommodation

noun
adjustment to circumstances
4.3 4.3
Pronunciation: /ə.kɑː.mə.ˈdeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ə.kə.mə.ˈdeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ə.kɒ.mə.ˈdeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ə.kə.mə.ˈdeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of adjusting or adapting to new circumstances, conditions, or information

Examples

Accommodation to change
Biological accommodation allows an organism to survive changes in its environment.
Effective therapy often involves the accommodation of one's behavior to deal with stress.
The company showed accommodation in modifying its policies to suit the new regulations.
According to Piaget, accommodation allows children to adapt their thinking when they encounter new experiences.
An example of accommodation is when a child revises their idea of animals to include dolphins as mammals.
The process of accommodation helps explain how students modify concepts in response to new learning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adaptation - the process of changing to fit new conditions
  • synonym: adjustment - a small change to improve fit or function
  • antonym: assimilation - the process of incorporating new information into existing cognitive frameworks (Piagetian psychology)
