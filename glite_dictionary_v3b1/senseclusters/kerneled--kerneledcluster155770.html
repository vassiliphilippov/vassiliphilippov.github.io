kerneled

verb
to apply a kernel
Pronunciation: / ˈkɜrnəld / (AmE), / ˈkɜːnəld / (BrE)
to process data, especially images or signals, by applying a kernel or convolution function to filter or smooth it

Examples

Kerneled image data
The engineer kerneled the audio signal to eliminate background noise.
Before analysis, the satellite image was kerneled to enhance its clarity.
Researchers often use kerneled data to improve the accuracy of their models.

Related Terms

  • synonym: convolve - to combine two functions to produce a third, often for filtering data
  • synonym: filter - to remove unwanted parts from data or signals
