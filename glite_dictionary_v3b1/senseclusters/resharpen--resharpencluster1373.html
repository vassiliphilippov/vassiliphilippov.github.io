resharpen

verb
to make sharp again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈʃɑːrpən / (AmE), / ˌriːˈʃɑːpən / (BrE)
to make something sharp or effective again, especially a skill, ability, or tool.

Examples

To resharpen your skills
After months away from the piano, he needed to resharpen his playing skills.
The chef took a moment to resharpen his knife before preparing the vegetables.
She attended a workshop to resharpen her leadership abilities for the new project.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dull - to make less sharp or effective
  • synonym: refresh - to restore strength or effectiveness
