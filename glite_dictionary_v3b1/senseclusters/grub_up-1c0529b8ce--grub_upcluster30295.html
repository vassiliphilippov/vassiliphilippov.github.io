grub up

verb
dig up or remove (plants or roots)
8.2 6.1
Pronunciation: /grʌb ʌp/ (AmE), /grʌb ʌp/ (BrE)
to dig up or remove something, especially plants, roots, or stumps, from the ground by digging

Examples

To grub up old trees
Farmers often grub up old trees to make way for new crops.
The council decided to grub up the overgrown hedges along the road.
We had to grub up the roots before laying the new lawn.
