heavy hitter

noun
very frequent item in a data stream
6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈhev.iː ˈhɪt.ər/ /; / /ˈhiː.vi ˈhɪt.ər/ (AmE), /ˈhev.iː ˈhɪt.ə/ /; / /ˈhiː.vi ˈhɪt.ə/ (BrE)
(in computing and data streaming) an item, key, or element that appears with very high frequency in a stream of data, so that it dominates counts and is important to detect or track.

Examples

A data heavy hitter
Network engineers used an algorithm to detect the heavy hitter causing most of the traffic.
In the analytics dashboard, several heavy hitters stood out among millions of events.
Researchers implemented a sketch algorithm to estimate whether a given key is a heavy hitter in the stream.

  • synonym: top-k item - one of the k most frequent items in a dataset or stream
