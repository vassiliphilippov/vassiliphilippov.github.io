Renoir

proper noun
AMD mobile processor codename
7.1
Pronunciation: /rə.ˈnwɑːr/ /; / /reɪ.ˈnwɑːr/ (AmE), /rə.ˈnwɑː/ /; / /reɪ.ˈnwɑː/ (BrE)
A codename used by AMD for a family of mobile accelerated processing units (APUs) — notably the Ryzen 4000-series — based on the Zen 2 architecture, commonly mentioned in laptop specifications and reviews.

Examples

AMD Renoir chip
The new laptop uses an AMD Renoir APU to deliver strong performance and efficiency.
Many reviewers praised the battery life of laptops with Renoir-based processors during the tests.
Developers optimized drivers to improve graphics on laptops powered by Renoir APUs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: APU - a processor that combines a CPU and GPU on a single chip
  • synonym: Ryzen 4000 - AMD's series of mobile processors using the Zen 2 architecture
