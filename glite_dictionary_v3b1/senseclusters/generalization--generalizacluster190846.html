generalization

noun
model performance on unseen data
5.7
Pronunciation: /dʒen.ə.rəl.ɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /dʒen.rəl.ɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /dʒen.ə.rəl.ɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /dʒen.rəl.ɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
(in machine learning) the ability of a model or algorithm to perform well on new, unseen data rather than only on the data used for training.

Examples

Generalization to new data
Researchers aim to improve the generalization of neural networks on unseen test data.
High model complexity can harm generalization and cause poor performance on real-world examples.
They measured generalization by evaluating the classifier on a held-out validation set.
The model's generalisation to new customer reviews improved after adding dropout regularization.
Good generalisation means the classifier performs well on unseen test data from different sources.
We measured the network's generalisation by evaluating accuracy on a held-out validation set.

Related Terms

  • synonym: generalization - US spelling of the same machine-learning concept
  • synonym: generalize - to apply what was learned from training data to new, unseen cases; closely related verb form.
  • antonym: overfitting - when a model fits training data too closely and performs poorly on new data; the opposite of good generalization.
