forte-piano

noun
early piano keyboard instrument

An early type of piano, popular in the 18th and early 19th centuries, in which hammers strike strings to produce sound, allowing for variation in loudness.

Examples

An 18th century forte-piano
In her recital, she performed Mozart sonatas on an authentic forte-piano to capture the period sound.
The museum features a beautifully restored forte-piano from the late eighteenth century.
Unlike the modern piano, the forte-piano produces a lighter and more delicate tone.

Synonyms

  • pianoforte
    9.6 0.13/m
