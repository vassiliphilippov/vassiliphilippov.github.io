shopping center

noun
group of shops built together
1.0 1.4
Pronunciation: /ˈʃɑː.pɪŋ ˈsen.tər/ (AmE), /ˈʃɒp.ɪŋ ˈsen.tə/ (BrE)
A large building or group of connected buildings containing a variety of shops and often restaurants, with parking facilities, built as a single development and designed for shoppers to visit for retail and leisure purposes.

Examples

A shopping center building
She parked her car and walked into the shopping center to buy some clothes.
The new shopping center has over fifty stores and several restaurants.
They decided to meet at the shopping center for lunch and a bit of shopping.
Many families spend their weekends at the shopping centre browsing shops and enjoying meals together.
The shopping centre was decorated with festive lights during the holiday season.
The city recently opened a new shopping centre with over fifty stores and several cafes.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: marketplace - an open area or building where goods are bought and sold
