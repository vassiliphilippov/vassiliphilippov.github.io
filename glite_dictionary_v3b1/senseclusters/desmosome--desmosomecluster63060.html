desmosome

noun
cell-connecting structure
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛzməˌsoʊm / (AmE), / ˈdɛzməsəʊm / (BrE)
a specialized structure that connects adjacent animal cells, providing strong adhesion between them

Examples

A desmosome structure
A desmosome helps hold skin cells tightly together, making the tissue more resistant to stress.
Researchers observed that the desmosome was damaged in certain skin diseases.
The strength of heart muscle cells depends on the presence of desmosomes between them.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gap junction - a structure that allows communication between adjacent cells
  • synonym: tight junction - a structure that seals adjacent cells together in an epithelium
