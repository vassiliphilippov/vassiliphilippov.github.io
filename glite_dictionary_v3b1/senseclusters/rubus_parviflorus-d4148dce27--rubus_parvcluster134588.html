Rubus parviflorus

noun
a raspberry species with white flowers and orange berries
Pronunciation: / ˈruːbəs pɑːrˌvaɪˈflɔːrəs / (AmE), / ˈruːbəs pɑːvɪˈflɔːrəs / (BrE)
A species of raspberry native to western North America and northern Mexico, distinguished by its white flowers and thimble-shaped orange berries.

Examples

Rubus parviflorus white flowers
Native Americans traditionally used Rubus parviflorus for both food and medicinal purposes.
The Rubus parviflorus grows abundantly in moist woodlands throughout the Pacific Northwest.
The white flowers of Rubus parviflorus attract a variety of pollinators in early summer.
