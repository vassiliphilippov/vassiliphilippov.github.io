prehearing

adjective
before a hearing
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈhɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈhɪərɪŋ / (BrE)
occurring or done before a formal hearing, especially as a preliminary step in legal or official proceedings

Examples

Prehearing conference
The judge scheduled a prehearing conference to clarify the issues in the case.
A prehearing statement must be submitted by both parties before the main proceedings.
During the prehearing meeting, the lawyers discussed possible settlements.

Related Terms

  • antonym: posthearing - occurring or done after a formal hearing
  • synonym: preliminary - serving as an introduction or preparation for something more important
