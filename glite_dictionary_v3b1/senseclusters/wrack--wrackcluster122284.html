wrack

noun
dried seaweed cast ashore
Pronunciation: / ræk / (AmE), / ræk / (BrE)
seaweed or marine plants, especially those found along the shore or washed up on the beach

Examples

Beach wrack
Children searched among the wrack for shells and small sea creatures.
The shore was strewn with wrack left behind by the retreating tide.
The smell of drying wrack filled the coastal air.
At low tide, the shore was covered with wrack and other marine plants.
Scientists studied the diversity of wrack along the rocky coastline.
Seabirds often forage among the wrack washed up on the beach.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kelp - large brown seaweed of the order Laminariales
  • synonym: rockweed - a type of brown seaweed growing on rocks at the shore
  • synonym: seaweed - a general term for marine algae
