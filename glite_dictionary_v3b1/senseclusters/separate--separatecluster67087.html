separate

verb
break into pieces or fragments
1.5 1.7
Pronunciation: /ˈse.pə.reɪt/ /; / /ˈse.prət/ (AmE), /ˈse.pə.reɪt/ /; / /ˈse.prət/ (BrE)
to divide, split, or set something apart from something else; to break or move things into different parts or groups

Examples

To separate the pages
After years of use, the old book began to separate at the spine.
If you bend that brittle stick too far, it will separate into two pieces.
The biscuit was so crumbly that it would separate as soon as you picked it up.
A narrow corridor separates the main office from the storage area.
A tall fence separates the two gardens, giving each family some privacy.
The river separates the old city from the new developments on the western bank.
At the old oak tree, the road separates in different directions.
The path separates into two trails near the lake.
The river separates just beyond the bridge, forming a small island.

  • synonym: fragment - break or cause to break into pieces

  • synonym: split - divide or break into parts or pieces
