Irish spelling

noun
English orthographic style in Ireland
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪrɪʃ ˈspɛlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈaɪərɪʃ ˈspɛlɪŋ / (BrE)
The style of English spelling used in Ireland, resembling British spelling and differing from American spelling in certain words.

Examples

Irish spelling style
In schools across Dublin, Irish spelling is the standard taught to students.
Writers often choose between Irish spelling and American spelling depending on their audience.
You can spot Irish spelling in words like 'favour' and 'centre' on official documents.

Related Terms

  • antonym: American spelling - English orthography typical in the United States
  • synonym: British spelling - English orthography used in the UK and Commonwealth countries
