hurly

noun
noisy commotion
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhɜːrli / (AmE), / ˈhɜːli / (BrE)
a noisy disturbance or commotion, especially involving a crowd or confusion

Examples

A hurly outside
The hurly in the marketplace lasted all afternoon.
After the announcement, a sudden hurly broke out among the students.
Despite the hurly outside, she managed to concentrate on her work.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - a state of peace and quiet without disturbance
  • synonym: uproar - a loud and impassioned noise or disturbance
