African holly

noun
arborescent shrub with holly-like red berries
9.6
Pronunciation: /ˈæf.rɪ.kɑːn ˈhɑː.lɪ/ /; / /ˈæf.rɪ.kən ˈhɑː.lɪ/ (AmE), /ˈæf.rɪ.kən ˈhɒl.ɪ/ (BrE)
A woolly-stemmed biennial shrub of tropical Africa and southern Asia, with silvery-white prickly branches, clusters of blue or white flowers, and bright red berries that resemble those of holly.

Examples

The African holly shrub
Gardeners are drawn to the African holly for its silvery branches and striking flowers.
The African holly thrives in tropical climates and produces vibrant red berries.
Unlike European holly, the African holly is native to both Africa and southern Asia.
