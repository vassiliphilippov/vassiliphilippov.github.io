buckbean

noun
perennial wetland plant with bitter leaves
8.1 9.0
Pronunciation: /ˈbʌk.biːn/ (AmE), /ˈbʌk.biːn/ (BrE)
a perennial plant of wet areas in Europe and North America, with racemes of white or purplish flowers and bitter trifoliate leaves, often spreading along water margins

Examples

The buckbean plant
Clusters of buckbean can be seen spreading across the surface of shallow ponds.
Early settlers sometimes brewed a tonic from the leaves of buckbean, despite its bitter taste.
The buckbean thrives in marshy areas and produces striking white flowers in late spring.
