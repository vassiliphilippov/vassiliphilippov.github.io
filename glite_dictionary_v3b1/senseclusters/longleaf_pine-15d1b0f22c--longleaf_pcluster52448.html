longleaf pine

noun
a tall pine tree of the southeastern US
3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈlɔŋˌlif paɪn / (AmE), / ˈlɒŋˌliːf paɪn / (BrE)
a tall pine tree native to the southeastern United States, with very long needles and rough, deeply ridged red-brown bark; valued as a source of timber

Examples

The longleaf pine tree
Conservationists are working to restore longleaf pine habitats for native wildlife.
The longleaf pine forests once covered vast areas of the southeastern United States.
The wood of the longleaf pine is highly valued for its strength and durability.
