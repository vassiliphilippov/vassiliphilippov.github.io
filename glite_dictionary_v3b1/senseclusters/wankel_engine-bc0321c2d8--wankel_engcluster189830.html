Wankel engine

noun
rotary internal-combustion engine design
8.0
Pronunciation: /ˈwæŋ.kəl ˈen.dʒən/ /; / /ˈwæŋ.kəl ˈɪn.dʒən/ (AmE), /ˈvɑːŋ.kəl ˈen.dʒɪn/ /; / /ˈwæŋ.kəl ˈen.dʒɪn/ (BrE)
a rotary internal-combustion engine in which power is produced by the rotation of a triangular rotor inside an oval chamber, instead of moving pistons

Examples

Wankel engine design
Although the Wankel engine has certain advantages, it also faces challenges with emissions and fuel economy.
Engineers admire the compact and smooth operation of the Wankel engine.
The Mazda RX-7 was famous for using a Wankel engine instead of a traditional piston engine.
