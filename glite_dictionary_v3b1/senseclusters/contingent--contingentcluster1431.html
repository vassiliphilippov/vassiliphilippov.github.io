Synonyms

  • contingent on
    6.4 1/m
    contingent on is used to say something depends on a condition, while contingent refers to that uncertain possibility.
  • contingent upon
    6.4 0.8/m
    contingent upon sounds formal and is used in contracts, while contingent refers to a possible but not certain state.
  • conditional
    5.0 4/m
    conditional describes something that only happens if a condition is met, while contingent stresses the uncertain possibility.
  • dependent on
    3.2 17/m
    dependent on shows a clear reliance on another factor, while contingent suggests that the outcome may or may not happen.
  • dependent upon
    3.4 4/m
    dependent upon is a more formal way to show reliance on conditions, while contingent highlights the possible but uncertain result.
  • dependent
    2.8 15/m
    dependent means controlled or decided by something else, while contingent focuses on being possible but not yet certain.
