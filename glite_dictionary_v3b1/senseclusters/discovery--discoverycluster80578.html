discovery

noun
legal process of sharing case information
Pronunciation: / dɪˈskʌvəri / (AmE), / dɪˈskʌvəri / (BrE)
a formal process of revealing or locating information, especially in law or information technology

Examples

Discovery process in court
During discovery, both lawyers requested access to thousands of financial records.
Failure to comply with discovery requests can result in sanctions from the court.
The judge ruled that key emails must be produced during discovery before the trial.
In cloud-native architectures, discovery helps services locate each other at runtime.
The engineers implemented DNS-based discovery so containers could find backend databases automatically.
When the network was isolated, automatic discovery failed and devices could not register their services.

Related Terms

  • synonym: device discovery - the process by which devices identify each other on a network
  • synonym: service discovery - automatic locating and resolving of service instances in a distributed system
