go to the well

verb
visit a source for resources
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ tə ðə wɛl / (AmE), / ɡəʊ tə ðə wɛl / (BrE)
to visit a source, especially to obtain resources or support, often repeatedly or as a matter of routine

Examples

To go to the well often
During difficult times, the team would go to the well and seek advice from their mentor.
Every summer, villagers go to the well for fresh water.
She felt she couldn't go to the well for help again so soon after her last request.
