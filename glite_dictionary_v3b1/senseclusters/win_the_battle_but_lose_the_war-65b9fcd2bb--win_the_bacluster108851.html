win the battle but lose the war

verb
achieve small victory but fail overall
Pronunciation: / wɪn ðə ˈbæt̬l bəʔ luːz ðə wɔr / (AmE), / wɪn ðə ˈbætl bət luːz ðə wɔː / (BrE)
to achieve a small or temporary success, but fail to achieve a larger or long-term goal

Examples

To win the battle but lose the war
By focusing only on immediate gains, she risked winning the battle but losing the war in her career.
He tried to prove his point in the meeting and won the battle but lost the war when his coworkers lost trust in him.
If the company cuts safety to boost profits, it may win the battle but lose the war with unhappy employees and regulators.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pyrrhic victory - a victory that comes at such a great cost that it is nearly tantamount to defeat
