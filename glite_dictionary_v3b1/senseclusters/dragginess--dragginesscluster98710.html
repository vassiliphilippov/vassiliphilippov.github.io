dragginess

noun
degree of drag
9.2
Pronunciation: /ˈdræ.ɡɪ.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈdræ.ɡɪ.nəs/ (BrE)
the quality or degree to which something creates drag or resistance as it moves through air or water

Examples

Car's dragginess
The engineers tested the car's dragginess in a wind tunnel to improve its speed.
Reducing the dragginess of the airplane's wings made it more fuel-efficient.
Swimmers wear special suits to minimize dragginess in the water during races.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drag - the force that slows an object moving through air or water
  • antonym: streamlined - designed to reduce resistance to motion through air or water
