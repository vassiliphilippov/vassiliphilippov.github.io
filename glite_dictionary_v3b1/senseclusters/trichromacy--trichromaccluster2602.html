trichromacy

noun
normal color vision; three color receptors
8.8
Pronunciation: / traɪˈkroʊməsi / (AmE), / traɪˈkrəʊməsi / (BrE)
the condition of having normal color vision due to the presence of three types of color receptors in the eyes

Examples

Normal trichromacy vision
Most humans possess trichromacy, enabling them to perceive a full spectrum of colors.
Researchers are studying the genetic basis of trichromacy in primates.
Some animals lack trichromacy and can only distinguish a limited range of colors.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: dichromacy - color vision based on two types of receptors
  • degree lower: monochromacy - color vision based on only one receptor type; total color blindness
  • degree higher: tetrachromacy - color vision based on four types of receptors; enhanced color discrimination
