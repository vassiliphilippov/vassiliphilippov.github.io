repair

verb
to correct or remedy a problem or error
Pronunciation: / rɪˈpɛr / (AmE), / rɪˈpeə / (BrE)
to correct, improve, or make amends for a problem, relationship, health, or a wrong

Examples

To repair a friendship
She tried to repair their strained relationship by apologizing for her mistake.
The company is working hard to repair its damaged reputation after the scandal.
The government pledged to repair social injustices through new legislation.
A weekend at the spa helped to repair her exhausted body and mind.
After his illness, a nutritious diet was necessary to repair his strength.
The cheerful music did much to repair our spirits after a long, dreary week.
Although years had passed, nothing could fully repair the injustice done to the victims.
He apologized to the community, hoping his actions would repair the damage his mistake had caused.
The company tried to repair its reputation by compensating those who were affected.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rectify - to correct or put right a situation or error
  • synonym: redress - to remedy or set right a wrong or grievance
  • synonym: rejuvenate - to make someone or something look or feel younger, fresher, or more lively
  • synonym: remedy - to set right or cure a problem or deficiency
  • synonym: revive - to give new life, energy, or strength to
