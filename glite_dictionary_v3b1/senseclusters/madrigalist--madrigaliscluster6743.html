madrigalist

noun
singer or composer of madrigals
Pronunciation: / ˈmædrɪɡəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈmædrɪɡəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who sings or composes madrigals, especially during the Renaissance period

Examples

A madrigalist sings songs
As a talented madrigalist, she performed Renaissance songs at the festival.
Several famous madrigalists composed works that are still performed today.
The choir included a renowned madrigalist who specialized in sixteenth-century music.

Related Terms

  • synonym: madrigal - a secular vocal music composition, usually from the Renaissance
