stock saddle

noun
ornamented cowboy saddle with high horn
6.5 5.9
Pronunciation: /stɑːk ˈsæd.əl/ /; / /stɔːk ˈsæd.əl/ (AmE), /stɒk ˈsæd.əl/ (BrE)
a type of saddle, usually ornamented and with a high horn, designed for use by cowboys and suited for securing a lariat

Examples

A stock saddle with horn
He rode all day using a comfortable stock saddle that fit his horse perfectly.
The cowboy strapped his lariat onto the horn of his stock saddle before heading out to the range.
Unlike an English saddle, the stock saddle provides a high pommel and cantle for better support while herding cattle.

Related Terms

  • antonym: English saddle - a lighter, flatter saddle used in English-style riding
  • synonym: western saddle - a type of saddle developed for working cattle in the western United States
