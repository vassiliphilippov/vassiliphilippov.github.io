demagogue

verb
to stir emotions politically
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛməˌɡɑɡ / (AmE), / ˈdɛməɡɒɡ / (BrE)
to speak or act in a way that stirs up emotions, often for political gain or to influence people unfairly

Examples

To demagogue an issue
The politician was accused of demagoguing the issue to gain more support.
Critics say he tends to demagogue sensitive topics during his speeches.
Instead of offering solutions, she kept demagoguing to appeal to voters' fears.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - to make someone less excited or emotional
  • synonym: manipulate - to control or influence someone cleverly or unfairly
  • degree lower: persuade - to cause someone to do or believe something through reasoning or argument
