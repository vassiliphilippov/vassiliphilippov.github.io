skid road

noun
road of logs for hauling timber
6.3 6.9
Pronunciation: /skɪd roʊd/ (AmE), /skɪd rəʊd/ (BrE)
a rough road made by placing logs side by side, used for dragging freshly cut timber in logging operations

Examples

A skid road for logs
Early logging operations often relied on a skid road for moving timber.
The workers used a skid road to haul the giant tree trunks out of the forest.
Without a skid road, transporting the felled logs would be much more difficult.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corduroy road - a road made of logs laid side by side, especially over swampy ground
