lift

verb
steal (informal sense)
8.6
Pronunciation: / lɪft / (AmE), / lɪft / (BrE)
to steal something, especially secretly or by sleight of hand

Examples

To lift a wallet
She was caught trying to lift a necklace from the store.
Someone tried to lift my wallet on the subway this morning.
The gang would often lift merchandise from the warehouse at night.
She quietly lifted a book from the store without anyone noticing.
Someone lifted my wallet while I was on the train.
Tourists are often warned not to leave their bags unattended in case someone tries to lift them.

Related Terms

  • antonym: return - to give something back to its owner
  • synonym: rob - to steal from a person or place, often using force or threat
  • synonym: steal - to take something that does not belong to you without permission
