Synonyms

  • rise
    1.0 40/m
    rise describes an increase in amount or level in many contexts, while go up is more informal and common in speech.
  • move up
    2.0 2/m
    move up means to shift or rise to a higher position or level, while go up is the everyday phrasal verb for increases.
  • climb
    2.2 7/m
    climb describes a steady or often gradual increase in value or level, while go up can be sudden or slow in use.
  • gain
    1.1 20/m
    gain means to become larger or to get more in amount, often implying result, while go up focuses on the action of increasing.
  • bump up
    3.4 1/m
    bump up usually means to raise something noticeably, often by action, while go up simply says that value or level increases.
