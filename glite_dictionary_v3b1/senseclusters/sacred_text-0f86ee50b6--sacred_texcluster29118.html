sacred text

noun
writing venerated in religion
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪ.krɪd tɛkst / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.krɪd tɛkst / (BrE)
a book or writing regarded with great reverence, especially one central to a religion, and used in the worship of a deity

Examples

A sacred text book
Many people look to their sacred texts for guidance and inspiration.
The Torah is considered a sacred text in Judaism.
The scholar compared several sacred texts to understand different religious traditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: holy scripture - another term for a sacred text; revered religious writing
  • antonym: secular text - a non-religious written work
