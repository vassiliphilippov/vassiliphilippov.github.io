maniple

noun
liturgical vestment
Pronunciation: / ˈmænɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈmænɪpəl / (BrE)
a band or strip of cloth worn on the left arm by clergy during Mass as part of liturgical vestments

Examples

Priest's maniple
The priest placed the maniple on his left arm before the ceremony began.
During the traditional service, each deacon wore a maniple as part of their vestments.
The ornate embroidery on the maniple reflected the importance of the occasion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chasuble - the outermost liturgical vestment worn by clergy during Mass
  • synonym: stole - a long, narrow strip of cloth worn by clergy as a vestment
