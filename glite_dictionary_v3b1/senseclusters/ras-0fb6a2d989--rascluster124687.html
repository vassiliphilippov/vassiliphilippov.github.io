RAS

noun
brain system regulating arousal and wakefulness
Pronunciation: / ɑr eɪ ɛs / (AmE), / ɑː r iː ɛs / (BrE)
the network in the brainstem reticular formation involved in regulating wakefulness and arousal (reticular activating system)

Examples

RAS brain network
Damage to the RAS can result in a coma or persistent sleepiness.
Stimulants like caffeine are thought to affect the RAS to increase wakefulness.
The RAS plays a key role in maintaining alertness throughout the day.
