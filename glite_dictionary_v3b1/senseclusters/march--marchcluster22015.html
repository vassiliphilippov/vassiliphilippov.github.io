march

verb
lie adjacent to or share a boundary with
8.6 0.56/m
Pronunciation: / m…ërt É / (AmE), / m…ëÀêt É / (BrE)
(of land, countries, or regions) to lie adjacent to another or to share a boundary with.

Examples

Counties march with
Many old English counties marched with Scotland before the borders were fixed.
The duchy marches upon the lands of its ancient rivals.
The region of Lorraine marches with Germany to the east.

Synonyms

  • abut
    6.9 0.69/m
    abut describes land or a building that touches directly along a boundary, while march refers to regions lying next to each other.
  • adjoin
    5.2 3.56/m
    adjoin means to be next to and in direct contact, while march is used for regions or lands that share a boundary.
  • border
    2.3 4.77/m
    border means to be next to or surround something, while march specifically describes countries or regions lying beside each other.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adjoin - to be next to or joined with something
