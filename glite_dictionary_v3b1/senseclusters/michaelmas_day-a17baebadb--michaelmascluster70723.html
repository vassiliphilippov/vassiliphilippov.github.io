Michaelmas Day

noun
September 29th, feast of St Michael
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪkəlməs deɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɪkəlməs deɪ / (BrE)
September 29th, the feast day of the archangel Michael, formerly one of the quarter days in England, Wales, and Ireland.

Examples

Michaelmas Day feast
Celebrations for Michaelmas Day often include feasts and church services.
Many universities in the UK begin their academic year after Michaelmas Day.
Rent payments were traditionally due on Michaelmas Day during the medieval period.

Related Terms

  • synonym: quarter day - a day on which rents and other payments were traditionally due in Britain and Ireland
