distortion

noun
image defect from imperfect optics
Pronunciation: / dɪˈstɔrʃən /; / dɪˈstɔɹʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈstɔːʃən / (BrE)
a change in the original form, appearance, or quality of something, making it less accurate or true

Examples

Image distortion
Astronomers work to minimize optical distortion in telescope mirrors.
Barrel distortion can make straight lines appear curved in wide-angle photographs.
The image suffered from noticeable distortion near the edges due to the camera lens.
Environmental distortion has made the area uninhabitable for wildlife.
He regretted the distortion of his original statement by his opponents.
The report criticized the distortion of facts in the media.
Reflections on the uneven glass created a distortion of the scenery outside.
The earthquake caused a noticeable distortion in the building's foundation.
The image was so badly edited that it showed a clear distortion of the person's face.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aberration - a departure from what is normal or expected, especially in optics
  • synonym: deformation - the act of deforming or distorting
  • antonym: improvement - the act of improving or making better
  • synonym: warping - the action or result of bending or twisting out of shape
