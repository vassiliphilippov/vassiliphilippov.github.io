draggle

verb
make wet and dirty by dragging
Pronunciation: / ˈdræɡəl / (AmE), / ˈdræɡəl / (BrE)
to make (something) wet and dirty by dragging it through mud or water

Examples

To draggle with mud
After the heavy rain, her skirt was draggled with mud from the path.
If you draggle your coat in the garden, it will be very hard to clean.
The dog's fur became draggled after it ran through the puddles.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clean - to remove dirt from; make clean
  • synonym: soil - to make dirty
