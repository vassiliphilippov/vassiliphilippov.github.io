physical

adjective
relating to material things
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪzɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈfɪzɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to material things, matter, or things that are not mental, spiritual, or abstract

Examples

Physical objects
Computers today are much smaller in physical size than they were in the past.
She was attracted by his physical appearance, not just his charm.
The researcher measured the physical properties of the new alloy.
A sculpture is a physical object that you can touch and see.
Some philosophers debate whether consciousness has a physical basis.
The physical evidence at the crime scene was crucial to the investigation.
A reflex is a physical response to a stimulus, not a conscious action.
Physics is the study of the physical properties of matter and energy.
Their work focuses on the physical characteristics of minerals rather than their chemical composition.

Related Terms

  • antonym: immaterial - not having physical substance; not tangible
  • synonym: material - relating to physical objects or substances
  • antonym: nonphysical - not relating to material things; immaterial or abstract
  • antonym: spiritual - relating to the mind or spirit, not material things
