alkalinization

noun
making more alkaline
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˌæl.kə.laɪ.nəˈzeɪ.ʃən /; / ˌæl.kə.lə.nəˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌæl.kə.laɪ.nʌɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something more alkaline or less acidic, usually by increasing its pH

Examples

The alkalinization of water
The alkalinization of drinking water is sometimes used to reduce acidity and improve taste.
Doctors may recommend alkalinization of urine to help treat certain types of kidney stones.
Excessive alkalinization of soil can make it difficult for some plants to absorb nutrients.
The alkalinization of soil can help certain crops grow better in acidic regions.
Doctors monitored the patient's blood for signs of alkalinization after the treatment.
Industrial waste can cause the alkalinization of nearby rivers, affecting aquatic life.
