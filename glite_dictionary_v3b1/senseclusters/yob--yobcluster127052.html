yob

noun
rude or violent young man (British)
9.9 0.06/m
Pronunciation: / jÉ‘b / (AmE), / jÉ’b / (BrE)
A young man who is rude, noisy, and sometimes violent, especially in public places.

Examples

A yob in street
She was frightened by the yob who shouted insults as he passed by.
The yobs were arrested for vandalizing the bus stop last night.
The police were called after a group of yobs started causing trouble outside the pub.

Synonyms

    9.9 0.01/m
    yobbo names a slangy, loud young man who acts rowdy in public, while yob is the broader informal label for such behaviour.
  • hooligan
    6.3 1.01/m

  • roughneck
    8.6 0.20/m

  • ruffian
    8.1 0.88/m
    ruffian is a violent, often criminal person known for cruelty, while yob usually means a younger, noisy or rude person in public.
