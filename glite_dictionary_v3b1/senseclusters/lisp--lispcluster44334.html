lisp

noun
speech defect mispronouncing sibilants as 'th'
4.9
Pronunciation: / lɪsp / (AmE), / lɪsp / (BrE)
a speech defect in which sibilant sounds, such as 's' and 'z', are pronounced like 'th' sounds

Examples

A child with a lisp
Because of her lisp, she sometimes struggled to pronounce certain words clearly.
He attended speech therapy to help correct his lisp before the school play.
Many people with a lisp can improve their pronunciation with regular practice.
