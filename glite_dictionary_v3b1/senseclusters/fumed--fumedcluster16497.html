fumed

adjective
wood darkened by ammonia fumes
7.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / fjuÀêmd / (AmE), / fjuÀêmd / (BrE)
(of wood) darkened or colored by exposure to ammonia fumes, especially as a finish for furniture or flooring.

Examples

Fumed wood finish
Many antique Arts and Crafts furniture pieces are made from fumed wood.
She admired the beautiful, fumed surface of the new hardwood floors.
The craftsman used fumed oak to give the table a rich, dark finish.

Synonyms

  • ammoniated
    8.8 0.01/m
    ammoniated means treated or combined with ammonia in general, while fumed describes wood darkened specifically by ammonia fumes.
