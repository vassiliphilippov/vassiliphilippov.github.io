mixed-blood

noun
person of mixed racial ancestry
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪkst ˌblʌd / (AmE), / ˈmɪkst ˌblʌd / (BrE)
a person whose ancestry includes members of two or more different racial groups, especially in contexts historically describing Indigenous peoples and settlers.

Examples

A mixed-blood person
He identified as a mixed-blood, with both European and Indigenous heritage.
Many policies discriminated against mixed-blood individuals in certain communities.
The novel tells the story of a mixed-blood growing up in the early twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • antonym: full-blood - a person whose ancestors all belong to the same racial group
  • synonym: half-blood - a person with one parent of one race and the other of another
