Passover supper

noun
ceremonial Passover meal in Judaism
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpæs.oʊ.vɚ ˈsʌp.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːs.əʊ.və ˈsʌp.ə / (BrE)
the ceremonial meal held on the first night (or both nights) of Passover, commemorating the Israelites' exodus from Egypt, observed by Jewish families with traditional foods and rituals

Examples

The Passover supper meal
Our family gathers for the Passover supper every year to celebrate our traditions.
The children asked the Four Questions during the Passover supper.
We invited friends to join us for the Passover supper on the first night of the festival.
