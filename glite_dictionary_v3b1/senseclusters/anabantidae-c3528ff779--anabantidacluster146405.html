Anabantidae

noun
family of spiny-finned freshwater fish
Pronunciation: / ˌænəˈbæntɪˌdi /; / ænəˌbænˈtɪdi / (AmE), / ˌanəˈbantɪdiː /; / ænəˌbænˈtɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of small freshwater spiny-finned fishes from Africa and southern Asia, known for their ability to breathe air; also called climbing gouramies or climbing perches
