accounting ratio

noun
numerical measure from financial statements
4.3
Pronunciation: / əˈkaʊntɪŋ ˈreɪʃioʊ / (AmE), / əˈkaʊntɪŋ ˈreɪʃɪəʊ / (BrE)
A numerical relationship between two figures taken from a company's financial statements, used to assess financial performance.

Examples

Key accounting ratios
Improving key accounting ratios can make a business more attractive to lenders.
Investors often compare accounting ratios between firms in the same industry.
The company monitors its accounting ratios to evaluate its overall financial health.

Related Terms

  • synonym: financial ratio - a ratio that expresses a relationship between selected financial statement items
