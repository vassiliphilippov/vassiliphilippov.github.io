bone fat

noun
fat extracted from animal bones
6.7 6.7
Pronunciation: /boʊn fæt/ (AmE), /bəʊn fæt/ (BrE)
Fatty substance derived from animal bones, used mainly in making candles, inexpensive soaps, and lubricating greases.

Examples

Bone fat for candles
After boiling the bones, they extracted bone fat for industrial use.
Lubricating greases made from bone fat are valued for their viscosity and stability.
The company uses bone fat in the production of inexpensive candles and soaps.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: bone marrow - the soft, fatty tissue inside bones
  • synonym: tallow - rendered fat from animals, used similarly in candles and soaps
