releasable

adjective
able to be released
Pronunciation: / rɪˈliːsəbəl / (AmE), / rɪˈliːsəbəl / (BrE)
able to be released, made available, or set free

Examples

Releasable information
The confidential documents are not releasable until the investigation is complete.
Only certain information is releasable to the public under the new policy.
The prisoner was considered releasable after serving half of his sentence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: restricted - not allowed to be released or made available
