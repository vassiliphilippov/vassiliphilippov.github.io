sling

verb
throw or hurl forcefully
1.2
Pronunciation: / slɪŋ / (AmE), / slɪŋ / (BrE)
to throw, hurl, or toss something forcefully or casually

Examples

To sling a ball
After the argument, he slung the phone onto the couch and walked away.
He slung his backpack across the room without looking.
She slung the ball toward the basket with surprising accuracy.
Could you sling those keys to me from the countertop?
He decided to sling the ball to his teammate across the field.
She slinged her backpack to her brother before heading out the door.
Don't sling the keys like that—you might lose them under the sofa.
He would often sling the keys onto the kitchen counter after coming home from work.
She asked him to sling the keys over to her from across the patio.
