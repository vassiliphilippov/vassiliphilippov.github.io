preachily

adverb
in a sermon-like way
8.2
Pronunciation: /ˈpriː.tʃə.liː/ /; / /ˈpriː.tʃə.lɪ/ (AmE), /ˈpriː.tʃə.liː/ /; / /ˈpriː.tʃə.lɪ/ (BrE)
in a way that is like a preacher, giving advice or moral lessons in a self-righteous or overly moralizing manner

Examples

Speak preachily to others
He spoke preachily about the importance of honesty, making the conversation feel like a moral lesson.
Her preachily delivered advice sometimes annoyed her friends, who preferred a more relaxed tone.
The teacher addressed the class preachily, urging everyone to always do the right thing.
She answered the question preachily, making everyone feel uncomfortable with her moral advice.
Instead of offering support, he spoke preachily about what others should do.
The article was written preachily, as if the author believed they knew best.

Related Terms

  • antonym: casually - in a relaxed or informal way
  • synonym: didactically - in a way that is intended to teach, often with a moral lesson
  • antonym: humbly - in a modest or unassuming way
  • synonym: lecturingly - in a way that gives a lecture or moral advice
  • synonym: sermonically - in a way that resembles a sermon
  • synonym: sermonizingly - in a manner that resembles giving a sermon or moral lecture
