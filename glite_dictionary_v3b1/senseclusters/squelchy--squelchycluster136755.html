squelchy

adjective
wet, resonant sound
Pronunciation: / ˈskwɛltʃi / (AmE), / ˈskwɛltʃi / (BrE)
making or having a wet, squishy, or sucking sound or feeling, like walking on soft, muddy ground

Examples

A squelchy sound
The rain made the ground squelchy as we walked through the muddy field.
He created a squelchy bassline that gave the song a unique electronic feel.
Her shoes made a squelchy noise after she stepped in the puddle.
After the rain, the squelchy ground made walking difficult.
Her boots made a squelchy noise as she crossed the muddy field.
The children laughed at the squelchy feeling of the wet sand between their toes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: crisp - having a clear, sharp sound
  • antonym: dry - not wet or moist
  • synonym: soggy - wet and soft, often unpleasantly so
  • synonym: squelching - making a squishy or sucking sound
