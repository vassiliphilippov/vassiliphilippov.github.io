innominate artery

noun
major artery from aorta to right side of head and arm
7.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˈnɑmənət ˈɑrtəri / (AmE), / ɪˈnɒmɪnət ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
the large artery arising from the aortic arch that divides into the right subclavian and right common carotid arteries, supplying blood to the right side of the head, neck, and arm

Examples

The innominate artery branch
A blockage in the innominate artery can severely affect blood flow to the brain.
Imaging tests revealed an aneurysm of the innominate artery.
The surgeon carefully tied off the innominate artery during the procedure.
