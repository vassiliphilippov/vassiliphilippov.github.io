nonconductive

adjective
not conducting heat, electricity, or sound
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnkənˈdʌktɪv / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkənˈdʌktɪv / (BrE)
not able to conduct heat, electricity, or sound.

Examples

Nonconductive material
Because glass is nonconductive, it is often used in high-voltage applications.
Plastic is a nonconductive material, making it useful for insulating electrical wires.
The laboratory gloves are designed to be nonconductive to protect against electrical hazards.
