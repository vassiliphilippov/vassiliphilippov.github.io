scolex

noun
tapeworm head segment
Pronunciation: / ˈskoʊlɛks / (AmE), / ˈskəʊlɛks / (BrE)
the head segment of a tapeworm, usually equipped with suckers or hooks for attachment to the host

Examples

The scolex of tapeworm
The scolex of the tapeworm attaches firmly to the wall of the host's intestine.
Scientists examined the scolex under a microscope to identify the species of tapeworm.
Without a functioning scolex, the tapeworm cannot remain anchored inside its host.

Related Terms

  • antonym: proglottid - a segment of a tapeworm containing reproductive organs
