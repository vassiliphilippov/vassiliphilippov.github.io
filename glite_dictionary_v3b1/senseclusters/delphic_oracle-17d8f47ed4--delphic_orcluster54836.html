Delphic oracle

noun
oracle of ancient Delphi
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛlfɪk ˈɔrəkəl / (AmE), / ˈdɛlfɪk ˈɒrəkəl / (BrE)
(in ancient Greece) the oracle at Delphi, where a priestess delivered cryptic or ambiguous messages from Apollo to those seeking guidance.

Examples

The Delphic oracle priestess
Many leaders traveled to the Delphic oracle in search of advice before making important decisions.
The pronouncements of the Delphic oracle were famous for being mysterious and open to interpretation.
Today, people sometimes refer to a cryptic statement as being worthy of the Delphic oracle.
