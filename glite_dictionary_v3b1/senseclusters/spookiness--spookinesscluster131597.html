spookiness

noun
eerie or scary quality
5.3 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàspuÀêkin…ôs / (AmE), / ÀàspuÀêkin…ôs / (BrE)
the quality of being strange, eerie, or frightening in a way that causes unease

Examples

Haunted house spookiness
The spookiness of the abandoned hospital made it a popular spot for ghost hunters.
A thick fog added to the spookiness of the forest at night.
The movie's soundtrack increased the spookiness of every scene.

Synonyms

  • eeriness
    7.2 0.15/m
    eeriness is a quiet, mysterious feeling that makes you uneasy and alert, while spookiness causes more direct fright.
  • ghostliness
    8.4 0.03/m
    ghostliness looks or sounds like a spirit and suggests hauntings, while spookiness is a general eerie feeling not limited to ghosts.
  • uncanniness
    7.8 0.05/m
    uncanniness feels oddly familiar yet strange and makes you unsettled, while spookiness is more plainly eerie and frightening.
  • creepiness
    5.3 0.34/m
    creepiness gives an unpleasant skin-crawling feeling and makes you recoil, while spookiness is more about eerie fear and mystery.
  • freakiness
    6.1 0.02/m
    freakiness is strange or very odd and often playful or shocking, while spookiness is darker, eerie, and fear-inducing.

  • synonym: scariness - the quality of causing fear
