brutalism

noun
raw design style
7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈbruː.tə.lɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈbruː.tə.lɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
a style in architecture, art, or design that uses raw, unadorned materials (especially concrete) and has a stark, functional appearance

Examples

Brutalism style
The museum's brutalism is evident in its exposed concrete walls and minimal decoration.
Many people find brutalism cold, but others appreciate its honesty and simplicity.
In web design, brutalism often means using basic layouts and visible grids for a bold effect.
The new library is a striking example of brutalism with its exposed concrete walls.
Many people find brutalism cold, but others appreciate its honesty and strength.
Several government buildings from the 1960s were designed in the brutalism style.

  • antonym: ornamentalism - a style characterized by decorative details
