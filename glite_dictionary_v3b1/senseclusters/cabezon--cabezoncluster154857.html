cabezon

noun
Pacific marine fish
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkæbəˈzoʊn / (AmE), / ˌkæbəˈzɒn / (BrE)
a large-headed marine fish found along the Pacific coast of North America, known for its broad head and mottled coloring

Examples

Pacific cabezon fish
The chef prepared a delicious meal using freshly caught cabezon from the local market.
Divers often spot the distinctive cabezon resting among the rocks on the ocean floor.
A large cabezon can weigh over ten pounds and is prized by anglers.
