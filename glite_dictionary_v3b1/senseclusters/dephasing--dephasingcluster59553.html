dephasing

noun
loss of phase coherence
9.9
Pronunciation: /diː.ˈfeɪ.zɪŋ/ /; / /dɪ.ˈfeɪ.zɪŋ/ (AmE), /diː.ˈfeɪ.zɪŋ/ /; / /dɪ.ˈfeɪ.zɪŋ/ (BrE)
the process by which the phases of waves or particles lose correlation, often leading to loss of coherence in a system

Examples

Dephasing process
In quantum mechanics, dephasing can limit the performance of sensitive experiments.
Researchers are developing new materials to reduce dephasing in quantum devices.
The rapid dephasing of particles makes it difficult to maintain quantum information.

Related Terms

  • antonym: coherence - the property of waves or particles maintaining a constant phase relationship
  • synonym: decoherence - the loss of quantum coherence in a system
