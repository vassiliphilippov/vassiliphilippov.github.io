reapportion

verb
distribute or allocate again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːəˈpɔːrʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈpɔːʃən / (BrE)
to allocate or distribute again in a new way, especially after a change in circumstances

Examples

To reapportion districts
After each census, the government must reapportion congressional districts to reflect changes in population.
If resources are limited, it may be necessary to reapportion them among the various departments.
The company decided to reapportion its budget to prioritize research and development.

Related Terms

  • synonym: apportion - to divide and allocate proportionally
