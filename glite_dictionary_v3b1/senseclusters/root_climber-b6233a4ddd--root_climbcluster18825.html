root climber

noun
plant climbing using roots
Pronunciation: / ruːt ˈklaɪmər / (AmE), / ruːt ˈklaɪmə / (BrE)
A climbing plant that ascends surfaces by means of adventitious roots, such as ivy.

Examples

A root climber plant
Botanists identify the trumpet vine as a vigorous root climber in warm climates.
English ivy is a common example of a root climber found in many gardens.
Several types of root climbers can attach themselves firmly to brick or stone walls.
