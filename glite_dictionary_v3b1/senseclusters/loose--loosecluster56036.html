loose

adjective
not strictly controlled or official
5.9
Pronunciation: / luːs / (AmE), / luːs / (BrE)
not strictly controlled, organized, or exact; lacking structure, discipline, or precision

Examples

Loose rules
It's a loose network of volunteers rather than an official organization.
Many activists join loose coalitions to work on specific issues.
The town council formed a loose alliance with neighboring communities to improve services.
Her loose interpretation of the data led to some controversy.
That film is a loose adaptation of the bestselling novel.
The professor gave a loose summary of the complex theory.

  • antonym: literal - using or taking words in their usual or most basic sense
