carvel

adjective
smooth-planked hull
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːrvəl / (AmE), / ˈkɑːvəl / (BrE)
built with planks laid edge to edge, creating a smooth hull surface on a boat or ship

Examples

Carvel hull design
The shipbuilders chose a carvel design for its strength and smooth appearance.
A carvel hull allows the boat to move more efficiently through the water.
Many historic sailing vessels were constructed using the carvel method.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clinker - built with overlapping planks on a boat or ship
