Dipsosaurus dorsalis

noun
species of desert lizard
9.9
Pronunciation: / dɪp.səˈsɔːr.əs dɔːrˈsæl.ɪs / (AmE), / dɪp.səˈsɔː.rəs dɔːˈsɑː.lɪs / (BrE)
A species of small, long-tailed lizard found in arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico, commonly known as the desert iguana.

Examples

The Dipsosaurus dorsalis lizard
Scientists observed a Dipsosaurus dorsalis basking on a rock near the edge of the sand dunes.
The Dipsosaurus dorsalis is well adapted to the extreme temperatures of desert habitats.
The diet of Dipsosaurus dorsalis primarily consists of leaves, flowers, and fruit.
