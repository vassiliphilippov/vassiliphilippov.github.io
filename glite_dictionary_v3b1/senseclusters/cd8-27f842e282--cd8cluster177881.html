CD8

noun
immunological glycoprotein on cytotoxic T cells
Pronunciation: / si di ˈeɪt / (AmE), / siː diː ˈeɪt / (BrE)
a membrane glycoprotein found mainly on the surface of cytotoxic T cells, involved in immune response recognition

Examples

High CD8 count
A high CD8 count is often associated with a stronger immune defense.
Researchers use CD8 as a marker to identify cytotoxic T lymphocytes in blood samples.
The number of CD8 cells increased significantly after vaccination.

Related Terms

  • synonym: CD4 - a membrane glycoprotein found on helper T cells
