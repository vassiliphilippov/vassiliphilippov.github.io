hormesis

noun
beneficial low-dose effect
9.9
Pronunciation: / hɔːrˈmiːsɪs / (AmE), / hɔːˈmiːsɪs / (BrE)
a biological effect in which a low dose of a harmful agent produces beneficial results, while higher doses are harmful

Examples

Hormesis effect
Researchers found that hormesis can explain why small amounts of certain toxins may improve cell health.
The concept of hormesis is important in understanding how the body responds to stress.
Some exercise scientists believe hormesis helps explain the benefits of moderate physical stress.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adaptation - the process of adjusting to new conditions
  • antonym: toxicity - the quality of being poisonous or harmful
