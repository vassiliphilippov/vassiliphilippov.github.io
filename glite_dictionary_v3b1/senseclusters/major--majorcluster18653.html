major

adjective
music scale/key with specific intervals
6.5 6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪdʒər / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdʒə / (BrE)
(of a musical scale or key) having intervals of a semitone between the third and fourth degrees and the seventh and eighth degrees, often associated with a brighter or happier sound.

Examples

Major key music
Beethoven's Symphony No. 9 begins in D major.
She practiced her major scales every morning before school.
The piece is written in major key, giving it a joyful character.

Related Terms

  • antonym: minor - (of a musical scale or key) with a different arrangement of intervals creating a generally sadder tone
