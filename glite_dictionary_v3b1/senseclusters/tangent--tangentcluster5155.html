tangent

noun
sudden topic change
Pronunciation: / ˈtændʒənt / (AmE), / ˈtændʒənt / (BrE)
a sudden change of topic or line of thought that is only loosely related to the main subject

Examples

Go off on a tangent
During the meeting, he went off on a tangent about his vacation plans.
The professor's lecture was full of interesting tangents that made the class more engaging.
She tried to stay on topic but often found herself on a tangent.

Related Terms

  • synonym: digression - a temporary departure from the main subject in speech or writing
  • antonym: focus - the main point of attention or interest
