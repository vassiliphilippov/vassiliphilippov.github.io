respite

noun
temporary postponement of punishment
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛspɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛspɪt / (BrE)
a temporary delay or postponement of a punishment, especially an official order that allows a punishment to be carried out at a later time

Examples

A legal respite
His lawyer appealed for a respite so further evidence could be reviewed.
The court's decision provided a brief respite from the execution of the sentence.
The governor granted a respite of thirty days to the prisoner on death row.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reprieve - to cancel or postpone the punishment of someone, especially a person condemned to death
