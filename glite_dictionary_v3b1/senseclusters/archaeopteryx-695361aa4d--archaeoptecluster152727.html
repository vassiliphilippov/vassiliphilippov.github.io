Archaeopteryx

noun
extinct primitive Jurassic bird
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɑːr.ki.ˈɑːp.tər.ɪks/ (AmE), /ɑː.ki.ˈɒp.tə.rɪks/ (BrE)
an extinct early bird from the Jurassic period with both bird and dinosaur features; considered a key transitional fossil in evolution

Examples

Fossil of Archaeopteryx
Fossils of Archaeopteryx show both bird and dinosaur features, making it a key species in evolutionary studies.
Natural history museums often display models of Archaeopteryx to illustrate early avian evolution.
The discovery of Archaeopteryx helped scientists understand the transition from reptiles to birds.
Many paleontologists consider Archaeopteryx one of the most important fossils ever unearthed.
The discovery of Archaeopteryx fossils has provided key evidence for the evolutionary link between birds and dinosaurs.
The feather impressions found alongside Archaeopteryx bones show that it was capable of some form of flight or gliding.
