precharge

noun
initial charging process
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːˌtʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / ˈpriːˌtʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
the action or process of applying an initial charge to a device or system before it is used

Examples

Device precharge
The technician checked the precharge of the hydraulic accumulator before starting the machine.
Proper precharge is essential to ensure the safe operation of electrical capacitors.
If the precharge is too low, the system may not function correctly during startup.

Related Terms

  • synonym: charging - the process of adding electrical energy to a device
  • antonym: discharge - the process of releasing stored energy from a device
