equalizer

noun
measure that reduces inequality
4.1
Pronunciation: /ˈiː.kwə.laɪ.zər/ (AmE), /ˈiː.kwə.laɪ.zə/ (BrE)
a policy, program, or factor that reduces social or economic inequalities and helps give disadvantaged people more equal opportunities.

Examples

Tax equalizer
Many economists argue that progressive taxation can be an important equalizer in reducing income disparities.
She believes universal childcare would act as an effective equalizer for working mothers returning to employment.
Access to quality education remains the best-known equalizer across generations and social backgrounds.
