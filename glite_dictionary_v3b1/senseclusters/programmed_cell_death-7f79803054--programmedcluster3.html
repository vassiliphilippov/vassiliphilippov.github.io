programmed cell death

noun
intentional cell self-destruction process
Pronunciation: / ˈproʊ.ɡræmd sɛl dɛθ / (AmE), / ˈprəʊ.ɡræmd sɛl dɛθ / (BrE)
a biological process in which a cell intentionally destroys itself using specialized mechanisms, often to maintain health or development

Examples

Programmed cell death process
Certain diseases can result from defects in programmed cell death within the body.
Researchers study programmed cell death to understand how tissues develop and maintain balance.
Some cancer cells evade programmed cell death, which allows them to grow uncontrollably.
