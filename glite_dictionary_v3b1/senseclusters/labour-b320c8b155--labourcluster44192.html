Labour

proper noun
British political party representing workers' interests
2.9
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪbɚ ˈpɑːrti / (AmE), / ˈleɪbə ˈpɑːti / (BrE)
a political party in Great Britain, founded in 1900, traditionally associated with representing workers' interests and formerly promoting socialization of key industries

Examples

Labour Party
Many young voters expressed support for Labour in the recent general election.
Several members of Parliament resigned from Labour following internal disagreements.
The Labour government introduced new social welfare policies after the election.
