Northwest Passage

noun
Arctic sea route between Atlantic and Pacific
7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈnɔːrθ.west ˈpæs.ɪdʒ/ /; / /ˈnɔːrθ.west ˈpæs.ə.dʒ/ (AmE), /ˈnɔːθ.west ˈpæs.ɪdʒ/ (BrE)
a sea route connecting the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans along the northern coast of North America, sought for centuries as a shortcut between Europe and Asia

Examples

The Northwest Passage route
For centuries, explorers risked their lives searching for the Northwest Passage to Asia.
Melting ice in the Arctic has made the Northwest Passage more navigable for ships in recent years.
Roald Amundsen was the first person to successfully navigate the Northwest Passage from 1903 to 1906.
