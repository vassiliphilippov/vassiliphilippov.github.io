intercorrelate

verb
to relate mutually
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərkəˈreɪt / (AmE), / ˌɪntəkɒˈrɛlɛɪt / (BrE)
to have a mutual or reciprocal relationship or connection with each other, where two or more things affect each other

Examples

To intercorrelate factors
The researchers found that the two factors intercorrelate significantly in the study results.
In complex systems, many variables can intercorrelate and influence each other.
The survey data showed that age and income intercorrelate in interesting ways.
The results show that the two factors intercorrelate across different age groups.
Researchers found that several symptoms intercorrelate in patients with the condition.
In this analysis, the variables intercorrelate more strongly than previously thought.

Related Terms

  • synonym: correlate - to have a relationship or connection between two or more things
  • antonym: disconnect - to have no relationship or connection
