paternalistic

adjective
showing fatherly but controlling care
6.8 6.5
Pronunciation: /pə.tɜːr.nə.ˈlɪs.tɪk/ /; / /pə.tɜːr.nəl.ˈɪs.tɪk/ (AmE), /pə.tɜː.nə.ˈlɪs.tɪk/ /; / /pə.tɜː.nəl.ˈɪs.tɪk/ (BrE)
having or showing a tendency to protect people in a manner resembling that of a father, often by limiting their freedom or making decisions for them for their own good, sometimes in a condescending or intrusive way.

Examples

Paternalistic attitude
Critics accused the government of being paternalistic in trying to control citizens' personal choices.
She rejected his paternalistic advice, wanting to make her own decisions.
The manager's paternalistic approach made employees feel cared for, but also restricted their independence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: authoritarian - favoring or enforcing strict obedience to authority
  • synonym: maternalistic - having or showing motherly care or authority, sometimes in a controlling way
