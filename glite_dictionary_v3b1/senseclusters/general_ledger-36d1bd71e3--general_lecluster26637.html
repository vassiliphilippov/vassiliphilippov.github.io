general ledger

noun
main accounting record of a business
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛnɚl ˈlɛdʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛn(ə)rəl ˈlɛdʒə / (BrE)
a main accounting record that contains all financial accounts of a business, including both credit and debit entries

Examples

The general ledger account
Entries in the general ledger must be balanced at the end of each month.
Every company is required to maintain a general ledger for accurate financial reporting.
She spent the afternoon reconciling invoices with the general ledger records.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: subsidiary ledger - a ledger providing details supporting a control account in the general ledger
  • synonym: trial balance - a statement of all debits and credits in a double-entry account book to test their equality
