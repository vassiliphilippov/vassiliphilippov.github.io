dissolve

verb
gradually disappear or fade away
Pronunciation: / dɪˈzɑlv / (AmE), / dɪˈzɒlv / (BrE)
to gradually disappear or become less visible or distinct

Examples

Clouds dissolve away
Her worries seemed to dissolve after she heard the good news.
The image on the movie screen slowly dissolved into darkness.
The mist began to dissolve as the sun rose higher in the sky.
Her anxiety seemed to dissolve as the good news sank in.
The director used a special effect to dissolve the scene into darkness.
You can dissolve the image gradually on the computer screen.
