assume

verb
pretend to have a quality or feeling
Pronunciation: / əˈsum / (AmE), / əˈsjuːm / (BrE)
to pretend to have a particular quality, feeling, or appearance

Examples

To assume an attitude
He tried to assume an air of confidence, though he felt nervous.
In the meeting, he assumed a friendly attitude despite their argument.
She managed to assume indifference, hiding her true feelings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: affect - to put on a false display of a quality, feeling, or attitude
  • synonym: feign - to pretend to be affected by a feeling, state, or injury
