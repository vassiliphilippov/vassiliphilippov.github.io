decompressing

noun
process of relieving pressure
2.4 5.1
Pronunciation: /diː.kəm.ˈpres.ɪŋ/ /; / /də.kəm.ˈpres.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /diː.kəm.ˈpres.ɪŋ/ /; / /də.kəm.ˈpres.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
the process of relieving pressure, especially the gradual return of a person or object to normal atmospheric pressure after being under higher pressure

Examples

Slow decompressing after diving
Gradual decompressing can help prevent injuries associated with rapid pressure changes.
Proper decompressing is vital for divers to avoid decompression sickness.
The patient underwent careful decompressing in the hyperbaric chamber after the accident.
