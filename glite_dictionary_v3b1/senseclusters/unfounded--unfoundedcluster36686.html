unfounded

adjective
having no basis in fact or evidence
5.6 3.01/m
Pronunciation: / ÊŒnËˆfaÊŠndÉªd / (AmE), / ÊŒnËˆfaÊŠndÉªd / (BrE)
(of a claim, belief, or feeling) having no basis in fact or evidence; not justified.

Examples

Unfounded fears
His fears of being rejected turned out to be unfounded as everyone welcomed him warmly.
Many rumors about the company were completely unfounded and caused unnecessary panic.
The police concluded that the accusations were unfounded after a thorough investigation.

Synonyms

  • baseless
    5.0 1.60/m
    baseless is a blunt, strong word used in headlines to deny claims, while unfounded is more formal and neutral.
  • unsubstantiated
    7.5 1.91/m
    unsubstantiated describes a claim lacking evidence or proof, while unfounded is a general term for having no basis.
  • unsupported
    5.0 0.79/m
    unsupported means not backed by evidence or proof, while unfounded focuses on having no factual basis.
  • groundless
    6.7 0.79/m
    groundless feels like a strong claim with no facts or reason, while unfounded is a neutral way to say the same.
  • uncorroborated
    8.1 0.16/m
    uncorroborated means a statement lacks other confirming evidence, while unfounded stresses that the claim has no factual basis.
  • unjustified
    4.8 1.61/m
    unjustified says there is no good reason or excuse for something, while unfounded says it has no factual basis.
  • unwarranted
    7.0 2.62/m
    unwarranted describes actions or comments that are not deserved or needed, while unfounded means lacking any factual support.

Related Terms

  • synonym: baseless - without foundation in fact
  • synonym: groundless - not based on any good reason or evidence
  • antonym: justified - having a good or legitimate reason
