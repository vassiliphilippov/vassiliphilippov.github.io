unfounded

adjective
having no basis in fact or evidence
1.1 4.5
Pronunciation: /ən.ˈfaʊn.dɪd/ /; / /ʌn.ˈfaʊn.dɪd/ (AmE), /ʌn.ˈfaʊn.dɪd/ /; / /ən.ˈfaʊn.dɪd/ (BrE)
(of a claim, belief, or feeling) having no basis in fact or evidence; not justified.

Examples

Unfounded fears
His fears of being rejected turned out to be unfounded as everyone welcomed him warmly.
Many rumors about the company were completely unfounded and caused unnecessary panic.
The police concluded that the accusations were unfounded after a thorough investigation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: baseless - without foundation in fact
  • synonym: groundless - not based on any good reason or evidence
  • antonym: justified - having a good or legitimate reason
