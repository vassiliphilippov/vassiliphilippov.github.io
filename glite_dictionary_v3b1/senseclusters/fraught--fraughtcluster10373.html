fraught

adjective
filled with (especially something undesirable)
8.6 5.6
Pronunciation: /frɔːt/ /; / /frɑːt/ (AmE), /frɔːt/ (BrE)
full of or filled with something negative, such as worry, tension, or strong emotion

Examples

Fraught with tension
Her voice was fraught with emotion when she delivered the news.
Negotiations became increasingly fraught as the deadline approached.
The expedition was fraught with dangers they had not anticipated.
Negotiations between the two countries have been fraught since the incident.
The discussions became increasingly fraught as the deadline approached.
Their marriage was often fraught with misunderstandings and arguments.
