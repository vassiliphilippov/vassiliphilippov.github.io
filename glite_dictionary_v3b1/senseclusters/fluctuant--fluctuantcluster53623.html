fluctuant

adjective
unstable or shifting
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈflʌktʃuənt / (AmE), / ˈflʌktjʊənt / (BrE)
soft, unstable, and able to change shape or move when pressed, especially describing a swelling filled with fluid

Examples

A fluctuant swelling
The doctor noted that the swelling was fluctuant to the touch.
The patient's symptoms were fluctuant, changing from mild to severe throughout the day.
The market remained fluctuant during the uncertain economic period.
The doctor noticed a fluctuant mass on the patient's arm, suggesting it was filled with fluid.
A fluctuant swelling often indicates the presence of an abscess that may need to be drained.
If a lump feels fluctuant when pressed, it is likely not solid but contains liquid.

Related Terms

  • antonym: firm - hard and not easily pressed or compressed
  • antonym: steady - firmly fixed, not changing or moving
  • synonym: unstable - not steady or firm; likely to change
