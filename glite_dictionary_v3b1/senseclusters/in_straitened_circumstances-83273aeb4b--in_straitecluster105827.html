in straitened circumstances

adjective
lacking enough money for necessities
Pronunciation: / ɪn ˈstreɪtənd ˈsɝkəmˌstænsɪz / (AmE), / ɪn ˈstreɪtənd ˈsɜːkəmˌstɑːnsɪz / (BrE)
having little money and unable to afford basic necessities or comforts

Examples

Living in straitened circumstances
After losing his job, he found himself in straitened circumstances and struggled to pay his rent.
Even though they once lived comfortably, they now find themselves in straitened circumstances due to the economic downturn.
The charity provides support to families who are in straitened circumstances.

Related Terms

  • synonym: destitute - without the basic necessities of life
  • antonym: well-off - having plenty of money or wealth
