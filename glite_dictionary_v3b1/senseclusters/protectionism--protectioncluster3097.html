protectionism

noun
policy of restricting imports to protect home industries
6.9
Pronunciation: / prəˈtɛkʃəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / prəˈtɛkʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
the policy of protecting domestic industries by restricting imports through tariffs, quotas, or other trade barriers

Examples

Protectionism policy
Many countries turned to protectionism during times of economic crisis.
Some economists argue that protectionism ultimately harms both producers and consumers.
The government's protectionism angered international trading partners.

Related Terms

  • antonym: free trade - international trade left to its natural course without tariffs or other restrictions
