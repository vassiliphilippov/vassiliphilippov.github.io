forb

noun
non-grass herbaceous plant
Pronunciation: / fɔrb / (AmE), / fɔːb / (BrE)
a herbaceous flowering plant that is not a grass, sedge, or rush

Examples

Wild forbs plants
The prairie was dominated by forbs such as sunflowers and clover.
Ecologists study the diversity of forbs in grassland ecosystems.
Many pollinators rely on forbs for nectar throughout the summer.
The field was covered in colorful forbs that attracted many butterflies.
Ecologists studied the diversity of forbs in the restored prairie.
Unlike grasses, forbs often have broad leaves and showy flowers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: grass - a plant with narrow leaves growing from the base, often forming lawns or pastures
  • synonym: herb - a plant with leaves, seeds, or flowers used for flavoring, food, or medicine
