embellisher

noun
person who exaggerates
7.1
Pronunciation: / ɛmˈbɛlɪʃər / (AmE), / ɪmˈbɛlɪʃə / (BrE)
a person who adds extra or exaggerated details to a story, report, or statement to make it more interesting or dramatic

Examples

A story embellisher
The embellisher in the group always made our adventures sound more exciting than they really were.
As a known embellisher, he could turn a simple walk in the park into a thrilling tale.
Her reputation as an embellisher made people question the truth of her stories.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exaggerator - a person who makes things seem larger or more important than they are
  • antonym: realist - a person who describes things as they really are, without exaggeration
  • synonym: storyteller - a person who tells stories, often adding imaginative details
