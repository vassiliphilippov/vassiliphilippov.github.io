cat-o'-nine-tails

noun
whip with nine knotted cords
Pronunciation: / ˌkæt ə ˈnaɪn teɪlz / (AmE), / ˌkat ə ˈnaɪn teɪlz / (BrE)
A whip made of nine knotted cords, traditionally used for flogging, especially as a punishment on ships and in prisons.

Examples

The cat-o'-nine-tails whip
Stories about the cat-o'-nine-tails are common in naval history books.
The captain ordered the use of the cat-o'-nine-tails as punishment for the sailors' disobedience.
The museum displayed an old cat-o'-nine-tails used during the 18th century.
