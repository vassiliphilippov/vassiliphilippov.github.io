carrageenin

noun
gelatinous extract from red seaweed
Pronunciation: / ˌkærəˈgiːnən / (AmE), / ˌkærəˈgiːnɪn / (BrE)
a gelatinous extract from red seaweed, used especially as a thickener and stabilizer in food and pharmaceuticals

Examples

Carrageenin in food
Food labels often list carrageenin among the ingredients in dairy alternatives.
Some ice creams use carrageenin as a thickening agent instead of gelatin.
The laboratory conducted an experiment to observe the effects of carrageenin on muscle tissue.
