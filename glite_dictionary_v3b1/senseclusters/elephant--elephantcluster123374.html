elephant

noun
large mammal with trunk and tusks
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /ˈe.lə.fənt/ (AmE), /ˈe.lɪ.fənt/ (BrE)
A very large herbivorous mammal with a trunk and ivory tusks, native to Africa and southern Asia.

Examples

A wild elephant
A single elephant can drink up to 50 gallons of water a day.
The elephant is the largest land animal on Earth.
We watched the elephant use its trunk to pick leaves from the tree.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mammoth - extinct large, hairy elephant-like mammal
  • antonym: mouse - small rodent, sometimes contrasted humorously with elephant
