simple phobia

noun
phobia of a specific thing or situation
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪmpəl foʊˈbiːə / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpəl fəʊˈbɪə / (BrE)
A phobia involving an intense, irrational fear of a specific object or situation (other than agoraphobia), such as animals, heights, or specific environments.

Examples

A simple phobia of heights
A simple phobia can significantly impact daily life if the stimulus is commonly encountered.
Her simple phobia of spiders made it difficult for her to enter the basement.
Treatment for a simple phobia often involves gradual exposure to the feared stimulus.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agoraphobia - fear of open or public spaces
  • synonym: specific phobia - another term for simple phobia; fear of a specific object or situation
