pull quote

noun
highlighted quotation
Pronunciation: / pʊl kwoʊt / (AmE), / pʊl kwəʊt / (BrE)
a short quotation from an article or document that is highlighted or displayed separately for emphasis

Examples

A pull quote box
The editor chose a powerful pull quote to draw readers' attention to the main argument.
A large pull quote in the center of the page highlighted the author's key message.
Designers often use a pull quote to break up long sections of text in magazines.

Related Terms

  • antonym: caption - a brief explanation or description accompanying an illustration or photo
  • synonym: quotation - a group of words taken from a text or speech and repeated by someone other than the original author
