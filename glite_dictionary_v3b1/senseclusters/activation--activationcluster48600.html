activation

noun
process of making active; stimulation
Pronunciation: / ˌæktɪˈveɪʃən /; / ˌæk.təˈveɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌæktɪˈveɪʃən /; / ˌæk.tɪˈveɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something active or starting activity, especially in a scientific, biological, or technical context

Examples

Activation of enzymes
Exposure to sunlight leads to the activation of vitamin D in the skin.
Recent studies focus on the activation of neural pathways during learning.
The activation of certain enzymes is essential for digestion.
Scientists are studying the activation of certain areas of the brain during sleep.
The activation of the alarm system is automatic when motion is detected.
The software requires activation before it can be used.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deactivation - the process of making something inactive
  • antonym: inhibition - the process of restraining or preventing activity (in biology or chemistry)
  • synonym: stimulate - to encourage something to become active or more active
  • synonym: stimulation - the action of arousing activity, especially in biological contexts
