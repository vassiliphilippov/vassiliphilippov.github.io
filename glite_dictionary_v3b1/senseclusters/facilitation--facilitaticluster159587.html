facilitation

noun
neural impulses combining to trigger action
7.7 6.2
Pronunciation: /fə.sɪ.lə.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /fə.sɪ.lɪ.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /fə.sɪ.lə.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /fə.sɪ.lɪ.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
In neurophysiology, a phenomenon in which two or more neural impulses, each insufficient alone to trigger a response, combine to produce an action potential.

Examples

Neural facilitation
Researchers studied the facilitation that occurs when subthreshold stimuli combine in the nervous system.
Synaptic facilitation is crucial for understanding how information is processed in the brain.
The facilitation of nerve impulses can lead to more efficient signal transmission in neural pathways.
