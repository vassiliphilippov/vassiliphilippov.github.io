label

verb
mark a molecule with a detectable atom
2.8 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈleɪ.bəl/ (AmE), /ˈleɪ.bəl/ (BrE)
to introduce a labeled atom or isotope into a compound for detection or tracing in scientific experiments

Examples

To label a compound
By labeling the compound, they could easily detect its presence in the sample.
Researchers label the protein with a radioactive isotope to track its movement in the cell.
The scientists labeled the glucose molecules to observe metabolic pathways.
For this experiment, researchers label the nitrogen atoms with a radioactive isotope.
Scientists often label molecules to follow their pathway through a metabolic process.
The enzyme can be tracked if you label it with carbon-14 before the reaction begins.
