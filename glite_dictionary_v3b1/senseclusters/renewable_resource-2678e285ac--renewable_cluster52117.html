renewable resource

noun
resource naturally replenished over time
4.8 2.5
Pronunciation: / rɪˈnuːəbl rɪˈsɔrs / (AmE), / rɪˈnjuːəbl rɪˈsɔːs / (BrE)
a natural resource that can be replenished naturally over time, such as wood or solar energy

Examples

A renewable resource like wood
Forests are considered a renewable resource because they can regrow after being harvested.
Managing fisheries sustainably ensures that fish remain a renewable resource for future generations.
Solar power is a renewable resource that can help reduce our reliance on fossil fuels.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nonrenewable resource - a resource that cannot be replenished within a short period
  • degree higher: resource - a supply of something that can be used as needed
