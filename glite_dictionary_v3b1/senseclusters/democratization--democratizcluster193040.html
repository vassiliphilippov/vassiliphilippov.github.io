democratization

noun
process of making something democratic
Pronunciation: / dɪˌmɑː.krə.t̬əˈzeɪ.ʃən /; / dɪˌmɑːkrətaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌmɒk.rə.taɪˈzeɪ.ʃən /; / dɪˌmɒkrətaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making a country, organization, or system more democratic by expanding participation, rights, or freedoms

Examples

Democratization of a country
Many countries have experienced democratization after decades of authoritarian rule.
The democratization of information has transformed how people access news around the world.
The internet played a key role in the democratization of education.
Education plays a vital role in the democratisation of society.
Technological advances have contributed to the democratisation of information worldwide.
The recent democratisation of the country has led to freer elections and more public participation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: authoritarianism - the enforcement or advocacy of strict obedience to authority
  • synonym: democracy - a system of government by the whole population; democratic system
  • synonym: liberalization - the process of making policies or systems less strict or more open
