pally

adjective
overly friendly or familiar
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈpæli / (AmE), / ˈpæli / (BrE)
(informal) Friendly in an overly familiar or intimate way; behaving as if one is a close friend.

Examples

To be pally with someone
Don't get too pally with the suspects, warned the detective sergeant.
I wish my boss wouldn't get so pally with the staff at office parties.
They became quite pally after just a few meetings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aloof - not friendly or forthcoming; cool and distant
  • synonym: friendly - showing kindness and pleasantness toward others
