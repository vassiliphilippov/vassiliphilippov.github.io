lymph gland

noun
small immune system organ producing lymph
3.1
Pronunciation: / lɪmf ˈɡlænd / (AmE), / lɪmf ˈɡlɑːnd / (BrE)
a small organ in the lymphatic system that produces lymph and lymphocytes and helps defend the body against infection

Examples

A lymph gland biopsy
Biopsies are sometimes performed on a suspicious lymph gland to check for cancer.
Infections often cause a lymph gland to enlarge temporarily.
The doctor examined the swollen lymph gland in my neck.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lymph node - small structure that filters lymph and stores lymphocytes
