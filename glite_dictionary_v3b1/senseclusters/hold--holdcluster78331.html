hold

noun
cell or place for keeping prisoners
Pronunciation: / hoʊld / (AmE), / həʊld / (BrE)
a place for storage or confinement, such as a ship's hold, jail cell, or fortress

Examples

A ship's hold
After his arrest, he spent several hours in a hold before being questioned.
Police escorted the detainees to the hold at the county jail.
The suspect was placed in the hold overnight until bail was posted.
Some containers were damaged during rough seas in the vessel's hold.
The aircraft's hold was filled with passengers' luggage and commercial freight.
The crew loaded the crates into the ship's hold before departure.
Legends spoke of a hidden hold deep within the northern forests.
The enemy retreated to their mountain hold, preparing for the final siege.
The old stone hold overlooked the valley, guarding it for centuries.

  • synonym: castle - a large building fortified against attack; typical medieval stronghold
  • synonym: cell - a small room in a prison or jail where a prisoner is kept
  • synonym: fortress - a large fortified building or complex; stronghold
