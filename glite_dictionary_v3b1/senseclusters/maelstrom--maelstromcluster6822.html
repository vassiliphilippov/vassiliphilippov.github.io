maelstrom

noun
powerful circular water current; whirlpool
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪlstrəm / (AmE), / ˈmeɪlstrəm / (BrE)
a powerful, violent whirlpool in the sea or a river, especially one caused by opposing currents

Examples

A maelstrom in sea
Legends spoke of a massive maelstrom off the northern coast that swallowed ships whole.
Sailors tried to steer clear of the maelstrom that formed at the river's mouth.
The powerful maelstrom threatened to pull the small boat under the swirling water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: whirlpool - a rapidly rotating mass of water in a river or sea
