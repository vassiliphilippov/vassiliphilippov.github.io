rhetorical

adjective
relating to style over substance in speech or writing
Pronunciation: / rəˈtɔːrɪkəl /; / ɹəˈtɔɹɪkəl / (AmE), / rɪˈtɒrɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the art or style of using language to persuade or impress, often focusing on effect rather than substance

Examples

Rhetorical question
Her argument was criticized as being merely rhetorical rather than substantive.
Many politicians give rhetorical speeches that lack concrete plans or details.
The essay was full of rhetorical flourishes but said very little of substance.
He asked a rhetorical question to emphasize his point.
Her speech was full of rhetorical flourishes that impressed the audience.
The editor made only minor rhetorical changes to the original text.

Related Terms

  • antonym: literal - taking words in their usual or most basic sense without metaphor or exaggeration
  • synonym: oratorical - relating to the skill or art of public speaking
  • antonym: substantive - focused on important or meaningful content
