having a tendency to do something, especially something undesirable; likely to experience or suffer from something

Examples

Prone to mistakes
Children are often prone to catching colds during the winter months.
He is prone to forgetting important meetings if he doesn't set a reminder.
Older buildings are more prone to damage during earthquakes.

Synonyms

  • liable
    5.0 2.59/m
    liable is often used for being likely to suffer or be blamed, while prone describes a personal tendency to suffer or act.
  • inclined
    3.8 14/m
    inclined means having a preference or tendency to act, while prone stresses a habitual or likely tendency, often unwanted.
  • susceptible
    5.5 8.82/m
    susceptible means easily affected or harmed by something outside, while prone focuses on an inner tendency or likelihood to suffer.
