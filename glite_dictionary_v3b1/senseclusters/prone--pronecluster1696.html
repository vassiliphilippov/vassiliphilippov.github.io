prone

adjective
likely to experience or do something
Pronunciation: / proʊn / (AmE), / prəʊn / (BrE)
having a tendency to do something, especially something undesirable; likely to experience or suffer from something

Examples

Prone to mistakes
Children are often prone to catching colds during the winter months.
He is prone to forgetting important meetings if he doesn't set a reminder.
Older buildings are more prone to damage during earthquakes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: immune - not affected by or not likely to experience something
  • synonym: inclined - having a tendency to do something
  • synonym: liable - likely to do or experience something, especially something undesirable
