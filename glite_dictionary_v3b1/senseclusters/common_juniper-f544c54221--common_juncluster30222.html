common juniper

noun
an evergreen shrub or tree; source of gin flavoring
7.2 3.1
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑː.mən ˈdʒuː.nɪ.pər/ (AmE), /ˈkɒ.mən ˈdʒuː.nɪ.pə/ (BrE)
a small evergreen shrub or tree (Juniperus communis) with needle-like leaves and blue, berry-like cones, widely found in the Northern Hemisphere and used to flavor gin

Examples

The common juniper shrub
A common juniper can survive in harsh, rocky environments where few other trees grow.

The berries of the common juniper are an essential ingredient in many traditional gin recipes.
