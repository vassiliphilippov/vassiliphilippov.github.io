deprogrammer

noun
cult exit specialist
8.5
Pronunciation: / diˈproʊɡræmər /; / diːˈproʊɡræmər / (AmE), / diˈprəʊɡræmə /; / diːˈprəʊɡræmə / (BrE)
a person who helps someone leave a cult or extreme group by reversing their beliefs or indoctrination

Examples

Cult deprogrammer
The family hired a deprogrammer to help their son leave the group.
A skilled deprogrammer can help individuals question and move away from harmful organizations.
After months in the cult, she finally agreed to meet with a deprogrammer.
The family hired a deprogrammer to help their daughter leave the cult.
A skilled deprogrammer can support individuals in questioning extreme beliefs.
After months of effort, the deprogrammer succeeded in helping the young man reconnect with his family.

Related Terms

  • synonym: counselor - a person trained to give guidance on personal or psychological problems
  • antonym: cult leader - a person who leads a cult and influences its members
