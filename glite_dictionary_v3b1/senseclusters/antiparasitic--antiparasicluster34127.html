antiparasitic

noun
parasite-killing drug
Pronunciation: / ˌæntiˌpærəˈsɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌæntiˌpærəˈsɪtɪk / (BrE)
a drug or agent used to kill, remove, or stop the growth of parasites in humans or animals

Examples

An antiparasitic drug
The doctor prescribed an antiparasitic to treat the patient's intestinal infection.
Farmers often use antiparasitics to protect their livestock from harmful worms.
After traveling abroad, she needed an antiparasitic to recover from a tropical disease.
