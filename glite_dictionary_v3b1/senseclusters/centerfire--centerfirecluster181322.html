centerfire

noun
cartridge with central primer
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛntərˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈsɛntəˌfaɪə / (BrE)
a type of cartridge for firearms in which the primer is located at the center of the base

Examples

Centerfire ammunition
The hunter loaded his rifle with centerfire ammunition before heading into the woods.
Most modern handguns use centerfire cartridges for better reliability and power.
He compared the performance of centerfire and rimfire rounds at the shooting range.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rimfire - a cartridge with the primer located in the rim of the base
