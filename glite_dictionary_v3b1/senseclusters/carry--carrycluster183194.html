carry

verb
hold or support oneself in a certain way
1.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkæri / (AmE), / ˈkæri /; / ˈkari / (BrE)
to conduct, present, or carry oneself in a particular way; to have a certain manner or bearing

Examples

She always carries herself with great confidence at public events.
The way he carried his shoulders made him look taller than he really was.
You should carry your head high and be proud of your achievements.
Even when nervous, she always carries herself with grace and confidence.
He tried to carry himself like a professional during the interview.
The way you carry yourself can influence how others perceive you.
