consider

verb
think carefully about something
Pronunciation: / kənˈsɪdɚ /; / kənˈsɪdər / (AmE), / kənˈsɪdə / (BrE)
to think about something carefully, especially in order to make a decision or to take something into account

Examples

To consider your options
Before making a decision, you should consider all possible outcomes.
Have you considered moving to a different city for work?
We will consider your request and reply as soon as possible.
I hoped my boss would consider my request with respect and understanding.
It is important to consider the feelings of others before making a decision.
She always considers her team's needs before her own.
She always considers others' feelings before speaking.
The committee will consider your request at their next meeting.
When making your decision, please consider all of the available information.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contemplate - to think deeply and at length
  • antonym: disregard - to pay no attention to; ignore
  • synonym: esteem - to respect and admire deeply
  • antonym: ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to something
  • synonym: ponder - to think about or consider something deeply and thoroughly
