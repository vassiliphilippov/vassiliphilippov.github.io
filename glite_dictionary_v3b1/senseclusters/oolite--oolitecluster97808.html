oolite

noun
sedimentary rock grains
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊəˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈuːəˌlaɪt /; / ˈəʊəˌlaɪt / (BrE)
a sedimentary rock made of many small, rounded grains of calcium carbonate (the grains themselves are also called oolites)

Examples

Blocks of oolite
The ancient temple was built using blocks of oolite quarried nearby.
Geologists studied the oolite to learn about the region's prehistoric environment.
Many famous buildings in the city feature walls made from local oolite stone.
The geologist examined the oolite under a microscope to study its concentric layers.
Many ancient buildings were constructed using stone rich in oolite.
A beach with abundant oolite grains often has a unique, soft texture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: limestone - a sedimentary rock mainly composed of calcium carbonate
  • synonym: ooid - a small, spherical grain formed by mineral precipitation
  • synonym: oolitic limestone - a sedimentary rock composed mainly of oolites
  • antonym: sandstone - a sedimentary rock made of sand-sized mineral particles
