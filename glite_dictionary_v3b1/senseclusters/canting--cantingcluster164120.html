canting

adjective
hypocritically moralizing
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkæntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkæntɪŋ / (BrE)
speaking or behaving in a way that is insincerely moral or hypocritically pious

Examples

Canting speech
The politician's canting speeches failed to convince the public of his honesty.
She grew tired of her colleague's canting remarks about ethics in the workplace.
The film satirizes the canting attitudes of those who pretend to care about social issues.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sanctimonious - making a show of being morally superior to others
  • antonym: sincere - genuine and honest in feelings or actions
