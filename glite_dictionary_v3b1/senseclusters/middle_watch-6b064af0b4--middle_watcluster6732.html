middle watch

noun
nautical watch from midnight to 4 a.m.
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪd.əl wɑtʃ / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.əl wɒtʃ / (BrE)
the period of duty or watch on a ship between midnight and 4 a.m., traditionally assigned to a rotating crew

Examples

The middle watch shift
During the middle watch, the seas grew unexpectedly rough.
He often found it hard to stay awake through the middle watch on long voyages.
The crew changed shifts at midnight for the middle watch.
