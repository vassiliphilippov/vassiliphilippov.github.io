noncentral

adjective
not centered at zero
9.1
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈsɛntrəl / (AmE), / nɒnˈsɛntrəl / (BrE)
in statistics, describing a probability distribution that is not centered at zero and has a nonzero noncentrality parameter

Examples

Noncentral t-distribution
The noncentral t-distribution is used when the population mean is not zero.
Statisticians often compare central and noncentral distributions in hypothesis testing.
A noncentral chi-square distribution arises when the null hypothesis is not true.

Related Terms
