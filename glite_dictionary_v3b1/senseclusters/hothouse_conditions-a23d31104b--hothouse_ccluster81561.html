hothouse conditions

noun
intense, artificially accelerated environment
5.7
Pronunciation: /ˈhɑːt.haʊs kən.ˈdɪʃ.ənz/ (AmE), /ˈhɒt.haʊs kən.ˈdɪʃ.ənz/ (BrE)
A metaphorical environment that promotes unusually rapid development or achievement, often through intense pressure, close supervision, or artificial support.

Examples

Hothouse conditions for growth
The startup accelerator created hothouse conditions that pushed teams to ship products within weeks.
The conservatory's intense program produced artists faster because its hothouse conditions demanded constant experimentation.
Critics said the political campaign's secrecy and pressure created hothouse conditions for scandal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accelerated development - rapid progress or maturation produced in a shorter-than-normal time
  • synonym: pressure-cooker environment - a situation in which people face intense stress and urgency to perform
