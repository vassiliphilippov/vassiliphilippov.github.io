remunerate

verb
pay or compensate (formally)
8.2 0.15/m
Pronunciation: / r…ôÀàmjun…ôÀåre…™t / (AmE), / r…™ÀàmjuÀên…ôre…™t / (BrE)
to pay someone for work or services; to compensate financially

Examples

To remunerate employees
All employees will be remunerated fairly for their overtime work.
She was generously remunerated for her consulting services.
The company failed to remunerate him for his contributions to the project.

Synonyms

  • compensate
    4.2 7.55/m
    compensate means paying someone for loss, injury, or work, while remunerate means paying specifically for regular services or job work.
  • recompense
    7.4 0.08/m
    recompense means to pay or reward someone for loss or effort, while remunerate means to pay people for services or work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compensate - to give someone something, usually money, in recognition of loss, suffering, or work
  • synonym: reward - to give something to someone in recognition of service, effort, or achievement
