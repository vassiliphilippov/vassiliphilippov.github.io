gopher turtle

noun
burrowing land tortoise of the Southeast US
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡoʊfər ˌtɜr.təl / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊ.fə ˌtɜː.tl̩ / (BrE)
A large, burrowing land tortoise of the southeastern United States, known for making extensive burrows; also called gopher tortoise.

Examples

A gopher turtle burrow
A gopher turtle can live for more than 40 years in its natural habitat.
It is illegal to disturb or relocate a gopher turtle in some southeastern states.
The gopher turtle creates extensive burrows that provide shelter for many other animal species.
