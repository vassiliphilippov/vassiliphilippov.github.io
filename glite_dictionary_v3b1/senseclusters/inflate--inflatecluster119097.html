inflate

verb
exaggerate or make bigger (figurative)
5.6 3.5
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfleɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈfleɪt / (BrE)
to make something seem more important or impressive than it really is, especially by exaggerating its size, value, or significance

Examples

To inflate the story
Reporters accused the government of inflating the statistics to make the situation look better.
Some companies inflate their earnings reports to attract investors.
The coach warned the players not to inflate their achievements in interviews.
