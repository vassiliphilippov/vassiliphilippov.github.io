rainstorm

noun
storm with heavy rain
1.0 1.74/m

A period of bad weather in which a lot of rain falls, often with wind.

Examples

A heavy rainstorm
After the rainstorm, the air felt cleaner and the grass looked greener.
The rainstorm last night flooded the streets and caused several power outages.
We had to cancel the picnic because a sudden rainstorm started in the afternoon.

Synonyms

  • downpour
    2.7 1.90/m
    a downpour is a short, very heavy fall of rain that soaks quickly, while a rainstorm lasts longer and may bring wind.
  • soaker
    4.2 0.01/m
    a soaker is an informal word for heavy rain that soaks you, while a rainstorm describes a longer bad-weather event with wind.
  • torrent
    4.4 0.56/m
    a torrent is a sudden, fast outpouring of heavy rain and water, while a rainstorm is a period of bad wet weather.
  • deluge
    6.3 0.84/m
    a deluge is an extreme, flooding rain that overwhelms places, while a rainstorm is heavy rain but may not cause flood.
