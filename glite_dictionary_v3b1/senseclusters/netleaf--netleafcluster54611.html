netleaf

noun
plant with netted veins
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛtˌliːf / (AmE), / ˈnɛtˌliːf / (BrE)
a plant whose leaves have a net-like pattern of veins

Examples

A netleaf plant
The netleaf is admired for its beautiful, patterned foliage in many gardens.
Botanists often study the netleaf to understand how leaf veins develop.
She chose a netleaf for her collection because of its unique vein structure.

Related Terms

  • antonym: parallel-veined - having veins that run parallel to each other in leaves
  • synonym: reticulate - having a net-like pattern, especially in leaves
