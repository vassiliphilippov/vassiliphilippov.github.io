box elder

noun
a North American maple tree (shade tree)
9.9 4.9
Pronunciation: /bɑːks ˈel.dər/ (AmE), /bɒks ˈel.də/ (BrE)
A fast-growing North American maple tree (Acer negundo) with divided leaves, commonly used as a shade tree in the eastern and central United States.

Examples

A large box elder tree
A large box elder provided shade for the picnic area by the river.
The leaves of the box elder distinguish it from other maple species.
We planted a box elder in our yard for its rapid growth and lush foliage.
