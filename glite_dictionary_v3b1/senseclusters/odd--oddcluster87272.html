odd

adjective
strange or difficult to explain
1.0 41/m
Pronunciation: / É‘d / (AmE), / É’d / (BrE)
strange, unusual, or different from what is expected

Examples

An odd person
I found his reaction rather odd given the circumstances.
It's odd that no one else noticed the mistake.
There's something odd about this whole situation that I can't quite explain.
Her odd behavior worried her friends.
It's odd that he didn't call to say he would be late.
There was something odd about the way he looked at us.

Synonyms

  • strange
    2.3 84/m
    strange describes something unusual and hard to understand, while odd often means different from what people expect.
  • unusual
    2.2 54/m
    unusual means not common or rare, while odd often suggests being different in a small or surprising way.
  • peculiar
    4.6 11/m
    peculiar feels slightly strange or marked, while odd is more general and can be used for many different surprises.
  • curious
    4.0 9.88/m
    curious sounds a bit old-fashioned or formal for strange things, while odd is common in everyday speech for surprises.
  • weird
    3.2 50/m
    weird feels very strange and unsettling, often with strong surprise, while odd is milder and more neutral in tone.
  • singular
    7.0 1.72/m
    singular sounds formal and means strikingly unusual or remarkable, while odd is more common and used in everyday conversation.
  • funny
    2.0 20/m
    funny often means amusing or humorous, which can confuse learners, while odd usually means simply different or unexpected.
