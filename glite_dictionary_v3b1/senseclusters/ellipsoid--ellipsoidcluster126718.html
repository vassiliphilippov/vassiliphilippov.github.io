ellipsoid

noun
three-dimensional surface with elliptical sections
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɪp.sɔɪd / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪp.sɔɪd / (BrE)
A three-dimensional geometric surface, all of whose plane sections are ellipses or circles.

Examples

A solid ellipsoid
Mathematicians use the term ellipsoid when describing certain three-dimensional surfaces in geometry.
Stretching a balloon can sometimes create the shape of an ellipsoid.
The Earth is often modeled as an ellipsoid rather than a perfect sphere.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ellipse - a plane curve surrounding two focal points
  • degree lower: sphere - a perfectly round geometrical surface, every point equidistant from the center
