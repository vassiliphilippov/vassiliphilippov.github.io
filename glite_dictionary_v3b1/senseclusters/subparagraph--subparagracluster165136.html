subparagraph

noun
section of a paragraph
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌbˈpærəˌɡræf /; / ˈsʌbˌpærəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˈpærəˌɡrɑːf /; / ˈsʌbˌpærəɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a smaller section within a paragraph, especially in legal or formal documents

Examples

A subparagraph in law
The instructions are detailed in subparagraph (c) of the document.
Each subparagraph must be clearly labeled for easy reference.
Please read subparagraph 2 before continuing to the next section.
Each subparagraph must be addressed separately in your response.
The contract includes several subparagraphs outlining the responsibilities of each party.
Please refer to subparagraph (b) for the specific requirements.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clause - a separate section of a legal document or contract
  • degree higher: paragraph - a distinct section of a piece of writing, usually dealing with a single theme
