reefing line

noun
rope in a sail for tying a reef (nautical)
5.3 7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈriː.fɪŋ laɪn/ /; / /ˈriː.fəŋ laɪn/ (AmE), /ˈriː.fɪŋ laɪn/ /; / /ˈriː.fəŋ laɪn/ (BrE)
(Nautical) A short length of rope or line stitched through a sail, used for tying part of the sail down when taking in a reef.

Examples

Reefing line on sail
It's important to check every reefing line for wear before heading out to sea.
The sailor tightened the reefing line to reduce the sail's area during high winds.
With a quick tug on the reefing line, she secured the mainsail against the strong breeze.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reef - reduce the area of a sail by folding or rolling part of it and securing it, typically in a storm
