court

noun
room or building for legal proceedings
1.4 186/m
Pronunciation: / k…îrt / (AmE), / k…îÀêt / (BrE)
a place or official assembly where legal cases are heard and decided by a judge or judges

Examples

Court hearing
Reporters waited outside the court for a statement after the trial concluded.
The judge entered the court and everyone stood in respect.
The trial was adjourned because there was a problem with the ventilation system in the court.
After months of preparation, the case finally went to court last week.
The Supreme Court is the highest judicial authority in the country.
The defendant appeared before the court to hear the charges against him.

Synonyms

  • court of law
    3.2 2.64/m
    court of law is a formal tribunal where judges hear cases under statutory rules, while court is the general place where legal cases are decided.
  • law court
    3.1 0.24/m
    law court is a tribunal where judges hear and decide legal cases, while court is the standard word for that place or assembly.
  • court of justice
    3.8 0.14/m
    court of justice often serves to decide important or high-level cases, while court refers to any place where legal cases are heard.
  • tribunal
    7.4 7.07/m
    tribunal is any adjudicating body that may be less formal or specialized, while court is an official place where judges hear legal cases.

Related Terms

  • synonym: courtroom - a room where a law court is held
  • degree lower: jury - a group of people sworn to render a verdict in a legal case
  • synonym: tribunal - an official body or assembly with authority to judge or adjudicate
