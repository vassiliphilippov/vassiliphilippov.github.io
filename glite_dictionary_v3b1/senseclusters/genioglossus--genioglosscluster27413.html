genioglossus

noun
tongue muscle
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒiniəˈɡlɑsəs / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːniəˈɡlɒsəs / (BrE)
a muscle in the tongue that is responsible for moving the tongue forward and aiding in speech and swallowing

Examples

The genioglossus muscle
The genioglossus plays a key role in sticking out your tongue.
Damage to the genioglossus can affect speech and swallowing abilities.
Doctors examined the patient's genioglossus to assess tongue movement after the injury.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hyoglossus - a muscle of the tongue that depresses its sides
  • synonym: styloglossus - a muscle of the tongue that retracts and elevates it
