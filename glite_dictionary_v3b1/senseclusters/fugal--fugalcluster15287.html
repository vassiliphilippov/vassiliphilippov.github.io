fugal

adjective
related to a musical fugue
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈfjuːɡəl / (AmE), / ˈfjuːɡəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a musical fugue, especially in style or structure.

Examples

Fugal style music
Her improvisation developed into a fugal passage, echoing the theme in overlapping voices.
Many Baroque pieces are noted for their fugal structures, especially those by Bach.
The composer wrote a fugal finale for the symphony, showcasing intricate counterpoint.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fugue - a musical composition in which a short melody or phrase is introduced and then developed by interweaving parts
