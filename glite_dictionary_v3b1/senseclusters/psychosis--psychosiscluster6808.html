psychosis

noun
severe mental disorder; loss of contact with reality
3.7
Pronunciation: / saɪˈkoʊsɪs / (AmE), / saɪˈkəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A severe mental disorder in which a person loses contact with reality, often experiencing delusions or hallucinations.

Examples

Severe psychosis symptoms
Early treatment of psychosis can improve long-term outcomes for individuals with mental illness.
Symptoms of psychosis include delusions, disorganized thinking, and a loss of touch with reality.
The patient was diagnosed with psychosis after displaying severe hallucinations and paranoia.

Related Terms

  • antonym: neurosis - a relatively mild mental disorder not involving loss of contact with reality
