emmetropia

noun
normal optical condition of the eye
9.9 8.7
Pronunciation: /em.ə.ˈtroʊ.piː.ə/ (AmE), /em.ə.ˈtrəʊ.piː.ə/ (BrE)
The normal condition of the eye in which light is focused directly on the retina, allowing for clear vision without corrective lenses.

Examples

Normal emmetropia vision
A person with emmetropia can focus on objects at varying distances without visual aid.
After undergoing corrective surgery, her eyes achieved emmetropia and she no longer needed glasses.
The doctor explained that emmetropia is the ideal state of vision.
