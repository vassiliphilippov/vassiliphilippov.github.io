myxobacteria

noun
slime-producing soil bacteria
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪk.soʊ.bækˈtɪr.i.ə / (AmE), / ˌmɪk.səʊ.bækˈtɪə.ri.ə / (BrE)
A group of bacteria that form colonies in self-produced slime and are found in moist soils, decaying plant matter, or animal waste.

Examples

Myxobacteria form colonies
Myxobacteria thrive in soils rich with decaying plant matter.
Scientists study myxobacteria for their ability to produce unique antibiotics.
Some myxobacteria can move collectively through a process called swarming motility.
