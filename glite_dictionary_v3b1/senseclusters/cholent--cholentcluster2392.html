cholent

noun
Jewish Sabbath stew
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃoʊlənt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɒlənt / (BrE)
a traditional Jewish stew of meat, beans, and vegetables, slow-cooked and eaten on the Sabbath

Examples

Traditional cholent stew
Every Friday night, the family prepares cholent to enjoy for their Sabbath lunch.
The aroma of cholent filled the house as it simmered overnight.
Many Jewish communities have their own special recipes for cholent passed down through generations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stew - a dish of meat and vegetables cooked slowly in liquid
