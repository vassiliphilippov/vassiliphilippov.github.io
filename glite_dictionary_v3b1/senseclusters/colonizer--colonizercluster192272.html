colonizer

noun
person who establishes a colony
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑː.ləˌnaɪ.zɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɒl.əˌnaɪ.zə /; / ˈkɒl.ə.naɪ.zə / (BrE)
a person who establishes or helps to establish a colony in another territory

Examples

A colonizer in history
He wrote an essay about the impact of colonizers on native societies.
Many indigenous communities resisted the arrival of the European colonizers.
The British colonizer brought dramatic changes to the region's culture and economy.
As a coloniser, he played a crucial role in establishing the first settlements.
Many European colonisers arrived in the Americas during the 16th century.
The impact of colonisers on indigenous cultures was profound and lasting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: colonist - a person who lives in or helps establish a colony
  • antonym: colonized - the state of being under the control of a colonizer
  • synonym: settler - a person who moves to a new area and settles there
