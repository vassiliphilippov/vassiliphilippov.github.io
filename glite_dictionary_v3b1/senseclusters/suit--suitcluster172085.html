suit

noun
legal case or action
5.6 21/m
Pronunciation: / sut / (AmE), / sjuÀêt / (BrE)
a legal action in which one party brings a case against another in a court of law to seek a legal remedy

Examples

File a suit
After months of negotiation failed, she decided to bring a suit in civil court.
The company filed a suit against its competitor for copyright infringement.
The victims plan to join together in a class action suit.

Synonyms

  • lawsuit
    4.8 30/m
    a lawsuit is the common everyday word for a court case seeking remedy, while a suit is the formal legal term for that case.
  • legal action
    3.8 2.60/m
    a legal action is a formal process taken in court to resolve disputes, while a suit is the specific legal case name.
  • action at law
    7.9 0.03/m
    an action at law is a formal, older legal phrase used in courts, while a suit is the modern general term for such cases.
  • civil action
    5.3 0.67/m
    a civil action is a lawsuit filed to protect private rights in court, while a suit can mean any legal case, civil or otherwise.
