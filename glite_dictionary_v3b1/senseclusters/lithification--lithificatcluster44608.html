lithification

noun
sediment becoming rock
9.6
Pronunciation: /lɪθ.ɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /lɪθ.ə.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /lɪθ.ɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /lɪθ.ə.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process by which loose sediment is transformed into solid rock through compaction and cementation

Examples

Lithification of sediment
Over time, layers of mud and sand undergo lithification to form sedimentary rocks.
Geologists study lithification to understand how ancient rock layers were created.
The lithification process can take thousands or even millions of years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cementation - the process of binding sediment particles together with minerals
  • synonym: compaction - the process of pressing sediments together to reduce space
