sensibleness

noun
quality of being sensible or prudent
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛnsɪbəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈsɛnsɪb(ə)lnəs / (BrE)
the quality of showing good sense or practical judgment

Examples

The sensibleness of advice
Her sensibleness was evident in the way she handled the emergency.
The sensibleness of her remarks won the respect of the entire team.
We admired his sensibleness when confronted with difficult choices.

Related Terms

  • antonym: foolishness - the lack of good sense or judgment
  • synonym: prudence - the quality of being prudent or cautious
