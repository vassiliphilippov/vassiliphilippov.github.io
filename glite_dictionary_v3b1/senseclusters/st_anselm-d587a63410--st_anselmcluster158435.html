St. Anselm

proper noun
medieval philosopher and theologian
Pronunciation: / seɪnt ænˈsɛlm / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈæn.selm / (BrE)
(c. 1033–1109) An Italian philosopher and theologian, Archbishop of Canterbury, regarded as a founder of scholasticism and known for formulating the ontological argument for the existence of God.

Examples

St. Anselm philosopher
St. Anselm is famous for formulating the ontological argument for the existence of God.

The philosophical writings of St. Anselm had a profound influence on medieval thought.
