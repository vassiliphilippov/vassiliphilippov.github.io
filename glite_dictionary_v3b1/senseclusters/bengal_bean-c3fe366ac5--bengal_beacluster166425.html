Bengal bean

noun
Asian vine with purplish flowers and hairy pods

an annual climbing vine native to Asia, with long clusters of purplish flowers and hairy pods, cultivated in the southern United States for green manure and grazing

Examples

Bengal bean plant
Bengal bean produces attractive clusters of purple flowers in late summer.

Synonyms

  • Florida bean
    8.9 0.00/m
    Florida bean is the local name in the southern US for this climbing plant used as green manure, while Bengal bean is the broader name.
  • velvet bean
    7.9 0.02/m
    velvet bean has densely hairy pods and is often grown for green manure, while Bengal bean has similar use but less dense pod hairs.
  • cowage
    9.9 0.00/m
    cowage is a common name for the purple-flowered vine used as green manure, while Bengal bean refers to the same crop in southern US farming.
  • Benghal bean
    9.1 0.00/m
    Benghal bean is called an annual woody vine grown in some regions, while Bengal bean is the climbing Asian plant grown in southern United States.
