Examples

Synonyms

  • impecunious
    9.9 0.08/m
    impecunious is a formal term for having very little money over time, while impoverished describes being poor and lacking resources.
  • penurious
    9.9 0.03/m
    penurious describes extreme lack of money and means, while impoverished refers to being poor but not always destitute.
  • indigent
    8.7 1.23/m
    indigent refers to people who need outside help and support, while impoverished means very poor without specifying help.
  • destitute
    6.5 1.68/m
    destitute means lacking basic necessities and shelter, while impoverished means poor but can cover less extreme need.
  • poverty-stricken
    5.1 0.80/m
    poverty-stricken describes whole communities with deep need, while impoverished describes a person or area that is very poor.
  • penniless
    4.3 1.02/m
    penniless means having no money at all, while impoverished refers more broadly to lacking money or other resources.
  • needy
    1.5 3.19/m
    needy describes people who require help or support, while impoverished simply means very poor or lacking resources.
