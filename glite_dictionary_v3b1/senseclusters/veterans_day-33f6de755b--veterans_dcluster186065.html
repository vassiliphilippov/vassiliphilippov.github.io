Veterans' Day

noun
US holiday honoring military veterans
1.8
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛt̬ɚrənz deɪ / (AmE), / ˈvɛtərənz deɪ / (BrE)
A legal holiday in the United States, observed on November 11, honoring military veterans; formerly known as Armistice Day until 1954.

Examples

Veterans' Day holiday
Federal offices are typically closed on Veterans' Day each year.
Many cities hold parades on Veterans' Day to honor those who have served in the military.
My grandfather, a World War II veteran, proudly displays his medals on Veterans' Day.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Armistice Day - The original name for Veterans' Day, commemorating the end of World War I
