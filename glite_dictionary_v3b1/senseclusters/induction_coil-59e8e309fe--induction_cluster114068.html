induction coil

noun
device generating high voltage from low voltage
1.4
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdʌkʃən kɔɪl / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌkʃən kɔɪl / (BrE)
A coil of wire used for generating a high voltage from a low-voltage direct current by means of electromagnetic induction.

Examples

An induction coil device
Engine ignition systems often rely on an induction coil to create the necessary spark.
The old radio used an induction coil to amplify the signal.
When the induction coil failed, the device could no longer generate high voltage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: transformer - an electrical device that transfers energy between circuits by electromagnetic induction
