armonica

noun
glass musical instrument
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɑːrˈmɑːnɪkə / (AmE), / ɑːˈmɒnɪkə / (BrE)
a musical instrument consisting of glass bowls or goblets that produce tones when their rims are rubbed with wet fingers

Examples

Glass armonica music
The musician played a haunting melody on the armonica during the concert.
Benjamin Franklin invented the armonica, which fascinated audiences in the 18th century.
The delicate sound of the armonica filled the hall with an ethereal atmosphere.
