process

verb
institute legal proceedings
8.5 1.59/m
Pronunciation: / Ààpr…ëÀê.ses /; / Ààpr…ëÀê.s…õs / (AmE), / Ààpr…ô ä.ses /; / Ààpr…ô ä.s…õs / (BrE)
to start legal proceedings against someone or to officially deliver legal documents

Examples

To process a lawsuit
He was surprised to learn that he had been processed for fraud.
If you fail to pay the fine, the authorities may process you in court.
The company decided to process the supplier for breach of contract.
Before the hearing, all parties had been properly processed.
She was processed with a summons to appear in court.
The defendant was processed by the deputy at his home.

Synonyms

  • take legal action
    4.8 0.43/m
    take legal action means beginning formal court proceedings to complain against someone, while process can mean starting those proceedings or serving legal papers.
  • file a lawsuit
    4.6 1.65/m
    file a lawsuit means formally filing a complaint in court to begin a case, while process can mean starting proceedings or serving legal documents.
  • serve
    7.5 4.25/m
    serve means formally delivering court papers to someone in line with procedures, while process can mean beginning legal action or officially delivering documents.
