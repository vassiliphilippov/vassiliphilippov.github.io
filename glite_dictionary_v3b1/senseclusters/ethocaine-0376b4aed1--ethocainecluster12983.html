Ethocaine

noun
local anesthetic used in medicine and dentistry
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɛθoʊˌkeɪn / (AmE), / ˈiːθəˌkeɪn / (BrE)
A white crystalline powder used as a local anesthetic in dentistry and medicine, known by the trade name Ethocaine.

Examples

Ethocaine local anesthetic
Local anesthetics such as Ethocaine are commonly used in oral surgeries.
Some patients may be allergic to Ethocaine, requiring alternative medications.
The dentist injected Ethocaine before starting the procedure to ensure the patient felt no pain.
