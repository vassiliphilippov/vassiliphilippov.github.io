denervation

noun
loss of nerve supply
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːnɚˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːnəˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the loss or removal of nerve supply to a part of the body

Examples

Denervation of muscle
The doctor discussed the possible effects of denervation on muscle function after surgery.
Chronic pain in the joint was treated with a procedure involving denervation of the affected nerves.
Researchers studied how denervation impacts the healing process in injured tissues.

Related Terms

  • antonym: innervation - the supply of nerves to a body part
