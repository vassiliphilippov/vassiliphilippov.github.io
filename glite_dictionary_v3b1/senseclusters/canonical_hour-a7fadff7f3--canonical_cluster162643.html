canonical hour

noun
one of seven fixed times for prayer
8.6
Pronunciation: / kəˈnɑnɪkəl ˈaʊər / (AmE), / kəˈnɒnɪkəl ˈaʊə / (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic Church) any of the seven fixed times of the day set aside for prayer and devotion.

Examples

The canonical hour prayer
Lauds is one of the most important canonical hours in the liturgical schedule.
Monks would assemble in the chapel to observe each canonical hour throughout the day.
The tradition of chanting at a canonical hour dates back to early Christianity.
