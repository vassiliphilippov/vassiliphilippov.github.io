bank discount

noun
interest deducted in advance on a loan
5.4
Pronunciation: / bæŋk ˈdɪskaʊnt / (AmE), / bæŋk ˈdɪskaʊnt / (BrE)
an amount of interest, calculated annually, that is deducted in advance by a bank when providing a loan or purchasing a bill of exchange

Examples

A bank discount rate
Companies sometimes sell promissory notes at a bank discount to raise quick capital.
She calculated the bank discount to determine the net amount required for the project.
The bank discount is subtracted before the borrower receives the loan proceeds.
