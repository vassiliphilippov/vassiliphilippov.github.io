Bodhisattva

noun
enlightened being in Buddhism who helps others attain nirvana
Pronunciation: / boʊ.diˈsɑt.və /; / boʊˌdiːˈsɑːtvə / (AmE), / ˌbəʊ.dɪˈsʌt.və /; / ˌbəʊdiːˈsʌtvə / (BrE)
in Buddhism, a person who could reach enlightenment but chooses to stay and help others achieve it first

Examples

The Bodhisattva ideal
In Mahayana Buddhism, the Bodhisattva ideal is highly revered.
Many devotees pray to a Bodhisattva for guidance and compassion.
The Bodhisattva vowed to help all sentient beings achieve enlightenment before entering nirvana.
Many Mahayana Buddhists aspire to follow the path of a Bodhisattva.
The Dalai Lama is often considered a Bodhisattva because of his compassion and dedication to others.
The statue in the temple depicts a serene Bodhisattva offering blessings to visitors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arhat - one who has attained nirvana in Buddhism
  • synonym: saint - a person acknowledged as holy or virtuous
