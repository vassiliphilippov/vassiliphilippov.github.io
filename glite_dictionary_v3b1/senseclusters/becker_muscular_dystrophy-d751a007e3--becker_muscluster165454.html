Becker muscular dystrophy

noun
hereditary muscle-wasting disease, milder than Duchenne
6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈbek.ər ˈmʌs.kjə.lər ˈdɪs.trə.fiː/ (AmE), /ˈbek.ə ˈmʌs.kjʊ.lə ˈdɪs.trə.fiː/ (BrE)
A form of muscular dystrophy that usually begins in adolescence or adulthood, progresses slowly, and causes generalized weakness and wasting of voluntary muscles, especially in the limbs and trunk; less severe than Duchenne muscular dystrophy and inherited in an X-linked recessive manner, typically affecting males.

Examples

Becker muscular dystrophy diagnosis
A diagnosis of Becker muscular dystrophy can be confirmed through genetic testing.
Children with Becker muscular dystrophy may not show symptoms until their teenage years.
Unlike Duchenne muscular dystrophy, Becker muscular dystrophy progresses at a slower rate.
