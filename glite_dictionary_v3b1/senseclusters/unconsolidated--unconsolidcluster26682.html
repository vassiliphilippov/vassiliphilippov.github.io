unconsolidated

adjective
not compacted or cemented; loose (esp. soils, sediments)
7.6
Pronunciation: / ʌn.kənˈsɑː.ləˌdeɪ.tɪd / (AmE), / ʌn.kənˈsɒl.ɪ.deɪ.tɪd / (BrE)
(of materials, especially soils or sediments) not compacted, cemented, or stratified; loose or loosely arranged.

Examples

The unconsolidated soil
Engineers tested the stability of the unconsolidated soil before starting construction.
Over time, these unconsolidated sediments may harden into rock.
The river deposited layers of unconsolidated sand and gravel along its banks.

Related Terms

  • antonym: consolidated - compacted, cemented, or made firm, especially in geology
