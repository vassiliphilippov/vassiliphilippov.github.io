anestrous

adjective
not in estrus (animal fertility cycle)
Pronunciation: / əˈnɛstrəs /; / ˈænɛstrəs /; / ˌæn.oʊˈɛs.trəs / (AmE), / əˈniːstrəs /; / ˈænɪstrəs /; / ˌan.ɪʊs.trəs / (BrE)
(of female mammals) not in a period of sexual receptivity or fertility; not in estrus or 'in heat'

Examples

An anestrous female
Breeders often keep a record of how long each female remains anestrous after mating season.
Hormonal changes cause the animal to become anestrous during certain months of the year.
The veterinarian confirmed that the dog was currently anestrous and would not come into heat for several weeks.
During the anestrous phase, hormone levels remain low and reproductive cycles are suspended.
Seasonally anestrous animals do not display sexual behavior during the off-season.
The veterinarian explained that the mare was currently anestrous and would not be receptive to breeding.
Hormonal treatments may be used to induce estrus in anoestrous cattle.
The ewe was anoestrous throughout the winter months, showing no signs of heat.
Veterinarians monitor anoestrous animals to determine the best time for breeding.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anestrous - of or relating to anestrus; the American English spelling
  • antonym: estrous - (of certain female mammals) in a period of sexual receptivity or fertility; in heat
