crouch

verb
bend down low by lowering body
1.8 8.82/m
Pronunciation: / kraÊŠtÊƒ / (AmE), / kraÊŠtÊƒ / (BrE)
to lower your body close to the ground by bending your knees and leaning forward

Examples

To crouch down low
She crouched down to tie her shoelaces on the busy sidewalk.
Soldiers crouched in the trench as bullets flew overhead.
The cat crouched behind the bush, waiting to pounce on its prey.
She crouched behind the sofa when she heard a noise in the hall.
The cat crouched low in the grass, waiting to pounce on its prey.
The children crouched down to look at the tiny insects under the rock.

Synonyms

  • hunker
    7.5 2.62/m
    hunker is to squat low and stay on your heels, while crouch is to bend forward and lower your body close to the ground.
  • scrunch
    6.0 0.44/m
    scrunch means bend your knees and sit close to your heels, while crouch usually involves leaning forward as you lower your body.
  • squat
    3.4 2.27/m
    squat is a deliberate up-and-down exercise or movement, while crouch is a single forward-leaning lower position often held still.
  • get down
    1.8 1.91/m
    get down means lower your body all the way to the ground or kneel, while crouch means bend knees and lean forward slightly.
  • duck down
    4.2 0.27/m
    duck down is a quick move to avoid being seen or hit, while crouch is a slower bend forward to lower your body.

Related Terms

  • synonym: squat - to sit with one's knees bent and one's heels close to or touching the buttocks or the back of the thighs
  • antonym: stand - to be in an upright position on the feet
  • synonym: stoop - to bend the body forward and downward
