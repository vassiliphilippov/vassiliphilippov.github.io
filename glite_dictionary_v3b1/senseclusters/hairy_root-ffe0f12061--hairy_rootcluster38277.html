hairy root

noun
abnormally fibrous root growth in plants
3.9 7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛri ˌrut / (AmE), / ˈheəri ˌruːt / (BrE)
an abnormal proliferation of fine, fibrous roots, especially occurring as a phase of crown gall disease in plants such as apples

Examples

The hairy root disease

The appearance of hairy root signals a possible crown gall infection in fruit crops.
The apple tree showed signs of hairy root near its base, alarming the orchardist.
