namban

noun
Japanese-European art style
9.9
Pronunciation: / nɑːmˈbɑːn / (AmE), / næmˈban / (BrE)
a Japanese artwork or object made in a style influenced by early European contact, especially from the 16th and 17th centuries

Examples

A namban screen
The museum displayed a beautiful namban screen showing Portuguese traders arriving in Japan.
Collectors value namban art for its unique blend of Japanese and European styles.
He studied the history of namban lacquerware created during the period of early European contact.
