understood

adjective
implied; tacit; not expressed
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndɚˈstʊd /; / ˌʌndərˈstʊd / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈstʊd / (BrE)
implied, accepted, or known without being openly stated; tacit or implicit

Examples

An understood rule
Attendance at the meeting was understood to be mandatory, even though it wasn't made explicit.
Her understood role was to manage the team quietly, without a formal title.
There was an understood agreement between the partners, even though nothing was written down.
In English grammar, the subject can sometimes be understood rather than stated.
The understood agreement between the parties was not written down but respected by all.
There are several understood rules in professional etiquette that everyone follows.

Related Terms

  • antonym: explicit - stated clearly and in detail, leaving no room for confusion or doubt
  • synonym: tacit - understood or implied without being stated
