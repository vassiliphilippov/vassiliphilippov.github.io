thrill

verb
tremble with excitement or fear
Pronunciation: / θrɪl / (AmE), / θrɪl / (BrE)
to tremble or shiver with a sudden feeling of excitement or fear

Examples

To thrill with fear
As the roller coaster descended, she couldn't help but thrill with both excitement and fear.
He would thrill at the sound of thunder rumbling through the mountains.
The audience seemed to thrill at every twist in the suspenseful story.

Related Terms

  • synonym: quiver - to shake with slight rapid motion
  • synonym: tremble - to shake involuntarily, typically as a result of anxiety or excitement
