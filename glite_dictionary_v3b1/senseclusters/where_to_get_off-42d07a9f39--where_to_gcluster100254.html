Pronunciation: /weər tə get ɔːf/ /; / /weər tə get ɑːf/ (AmE), /weə tə get ɒf/ /; / /weə tə get ɔːf/ (BrE)
(informal) Used especially in the phrase 'tell someone where to get off', meaning to angrily tell someone to mind their own business or to stop bothering or criticizing you.

Examples

To tell where to get off
After listening to his insults, she finally told him where to get off.
He thought he could boss me around, but I showed him where to get off.
If you keep criticizing her decisions, she's going to tell you where to get off.
