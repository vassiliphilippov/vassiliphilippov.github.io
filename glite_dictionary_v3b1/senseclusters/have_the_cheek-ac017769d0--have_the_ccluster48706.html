have the cheek

verb
to act with audacity or impudence
Pronunciation: / hæv ðə tʃik / (AmE), / hæv ðə tʃiːk / (BrE)
to have the audacity or impudence to do something, often in a way that is surprising or disrespectful

Examples

To have the cheek to complain
He had the cheek to complain about the service after causing so much trouble himself.
I can't believe she had the cheek to borrow my dress without asking.
You have the cheek to criticize my work after missing every deadline.

Related Terms

  • synonym: have the gall - to have the impudence or rudeness to do something
  • synonym: have the nerve - to have the audacity or boldness
