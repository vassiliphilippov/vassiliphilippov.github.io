camp

noun
faction united by a common cause
Pronunciation: / kæmp / (AmE), / kamp / (BrE)
a group of people who share a common purpose, belief, or cause, often forming a distinct faction within a larger group

Examples

The rival camp
Each camp presented its arguments with great conviction.
She clearly belongs to the conservationist camp in this debate.
The two political camps could not agree on the best way forward.

Related Terms

  • synonym: faction - a small organized dissenting group within a larger one
  • antonym: opponent - a person or group that opposes another
  • synonym: party - an organized political group or group with a cause
