suited

adjective
adapted or appropriate for a use
2.8 1.16/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàsuÀê.t…™d / (AmE), / ÀàsuÀê.t…™d / (BrE)
Adapted or appropriate for a particular purpose, situation, or person.

Examples

Suited for work
She wore a dress suited for the formal dinner.
The movie isn't suited for young children because of its violent scenes.
This job is suited to someone with excellent communication skills.

Synonyms

  • suitable
    1.8 20/m
    suitable is the common everyday word for being right for something, while suited feels more about being adapted to a person.
  • appropriate
    3.5 109/m
    appropriate is more formal and often used in rules or speeches, while suited is more neutral and describes fitting use.
  • fitting
    2.6 13/m
    fitting is slightly literary and shows a natural match in style or tone, while suited simply means appropriate for use.
  • fit
    1.0 3.96/m
    fit is a shorter, more informal word often used in speech or British English, while suited emphasizes being adapted for purpose.
  • befitting
    4.6 0.08/m
    befitting sounds formal and is used in ceremonies or old texts, while suited is neutral and used for practical situations.
