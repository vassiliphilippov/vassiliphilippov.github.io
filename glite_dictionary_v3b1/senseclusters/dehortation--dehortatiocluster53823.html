dehortation

noun
advising against action
9.9
Pronunciation: / diˌhɔːrˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌhɔːˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of advising or urging someone not to do something

Examples

Repeated dehortation
Despite his repeated dehortation, she decided to invest in the risky venture.
The teacher's dehortation against cheating was clear and firm.
Her dehortation made him reconsider his plan to drop out of school.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dissuasion - the act of persuading someone not to do something
  • antonym: exhortation - the act of strongly encouraging someone to do something
