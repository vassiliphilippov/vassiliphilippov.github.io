mortify

verb
cause deep embarrassment or shame
3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔːrtɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɔːtɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to cause someone to feel great embarrassment or shame; to hurt someone's pride deeply

Examples

To mortify a friend
I didn't mean to mortify you in front of your friends.
It would mortify him to admit that he was wrong.
She was mortified when everyone heard about her mistake.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embarrass - to make someone feel awkward or self-conscious
  • antonym: honor - to show great respect or admiration for someone
  • synonym: humiliate - to make someone feel ashamed or foolish
