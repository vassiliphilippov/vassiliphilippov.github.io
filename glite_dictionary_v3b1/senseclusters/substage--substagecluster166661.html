substage

noun
space under stage
8.1
Pronunciation: /ˈsʌb.steɪdʒ/ (AmE), /ˈsʌb.steɪdʒ/ (BrE)
the area or space beneath a theater stage used for machinery, storage, or moving scenery and equipment

Examples

Theater substage storage
The crew stored lighting rigs and props in the dark substage beneath the main performance area.
Access to the substage is restricted to technical staff during the show.
The new theater design includes a spacious substage for moving large set pieces easily.
