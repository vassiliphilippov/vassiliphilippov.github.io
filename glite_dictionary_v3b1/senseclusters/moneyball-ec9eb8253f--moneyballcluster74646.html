Moneyball

proper noun
book on baseball analytics
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌniˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈmʌniˌbɔːl / (BrE)
the title of a 2003 non-fiction book by Michael Lewis about using statistical analysis to build successful baseball teams, also adapted into a 2011 film

Examples

Moneyball book and film
After reading Moneyball, he became fascinated by the use of data in sports management.
The film adaptation of Moneyball brought the story of baseball analytics to a wider audience.
Many teams adopted Moneyball strategies to improve their performance on a limited budget.
