Pinkerton syndrome

noun
excessive distrust due to past trauma
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪŋkɚtən ˈsɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / ˈpɪŋkətən ˈsɪndrəʊm / (BrE)
a psychological condition characterized by excessive suspicion or distrust, often resulting from negative past experiences

Examples

Pinkerton syndrome distrust
After her last relationship ended badly, she developed Pinkerton syndrome and found it hard to trust again.
His Pinkerton syndrome made it difficult for him to maintain friendships.
Therapists recognize Pinkerton syndrome in patients who consistently fear betrayal.
