load

noun
power output of a generator or plant
2.8
Pronunciation: / loʊd / (AmE), / ləʊd / (BrE)
the amount of electrical power delivered or used, or a device that uses electrical power

Examples

Electrical load
A sudden drop in load can cause instability in the power grid.
The engineers monitor the load to ensure the generator is not overloaded during peak hours.
When the electricity load increases, the plant must generate more power to meet the demand.
Each load in the system is protected by its own circuit breaker.
The generator must be able to supply sufficient power for the connected load.
When the load increases, the current in the circuit rises as well.

Related Terms

  • synonym: output - the amount of something produced by a person, machine, or industry
  • synonym: resistor - component serving as a simple electrical load by resisting electric current
  • antonym: source - device or component that supplies electrical power
