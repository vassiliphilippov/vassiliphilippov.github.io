transcriptome

noun
all RNA in a cell
9.6
Pronunciation: / trænˈskrɪptoʊm / (AmE), / trænˈskrɪptəʊm / (BrE)
the complete set of RNA molecules produced in a cell or organism at a specific time

Examples

The transcriptome profile
Scientists compared the transcriptome of healthy and diseased cells to identify differences in gene expression.
The transcriptome provides valuable information about which genes are active in a particular tissue.
Advances in technology have made it easier to analyze the entire transcriptome of an organism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: genome - the complete set of genes or genetic material in an organism
  • synonym: proteome - the complete set of proteins expressed by a cell or organism
