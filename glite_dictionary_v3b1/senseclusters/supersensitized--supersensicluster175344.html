supersensitized

adjective
abnormally or excessively sensitive
Pronunciation: / suː.pɚˈsɛn.sə.taɪzd /; / suː.pɚˈsɛnsɪ.taɪzd / (AmE), / suː.pəˈsɛn.sɪ.taɪzd /; / suː.pəˈsɛnsɪ.taɪzd / (BrE)
having an unusually high or excessive sensitivity or susceptibility, especially to allergens or specific factors

Examples

Be supersensitized to allergens
After years of pollution, the local population grew supersensitized to environmental irritants.
Some patients become supersensitized to certain medications after repeated exposure.
The study focused on individuals who were supersensitized to common airborne allergens.
Children who are supersensitized to dust may develop more severe allergic reactions.
She became supersensitized to certain chemicals after years of exposure at work.
The patient's immune system is supersensitized, requiring special precautions with medications.

Related Terms

  • antonym: desensitized - having reduced sensitivity or responsiveness
  • synonym: hypersensitive - having extreme sensitivity to stimuli
  • antonym: insensitive - not sensitive; showing lack of response or feeling
