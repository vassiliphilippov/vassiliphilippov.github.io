astaxanthin

noun
red antioxidant pigment
Pronunciation: / ˌæstəˈzænθɪn / (AmE), / ˌæstəˈzanθɪn / (BrE)
a red pigment used as a colorant and dietary supplement, especially to enhance the color of farmed fish and seafood

Examples

Astaxanthin pigment
Farmed salmon are often fed astaxanthin to enhance their pink color.
Researchers study the antioxidant properties of astaxanthin for potential health benefits.
Shrimp and krill naturally contain high levels of astaxanthin in their bodies.
Many fish farmers add astaxanthin to salmon feed to achieve a vibrant pink color.
Some dietary supplements contain astaxanthin because of its antioxidant properties.
The shrimp's reddish hue is due to the presence of astaxanthin in its shell.
