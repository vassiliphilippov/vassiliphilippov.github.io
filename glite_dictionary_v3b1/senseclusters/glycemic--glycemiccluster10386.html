glycemic

adjective
affecting blood sugar
Pronunciation: / ɡlaɪˈsiːmɪk / (AmE), / ɡlaɪˈsiːmɪk /; / ɡlʌɪˈsiːmɪk / (BrE)
relating to the level or presence of glucose (sugar) in the blood, especially as affected by food or diet

Examples

Glycemic index
Nutritionists recommend choosing glycemic foods that help maintain steady energy throughout the day.
The doctor explained how a low-glycemic diet could benefit people with diabetes.
Athletes often monitor the glycemic impact of their meals to optimize performance.
The doctor explained the importance of monitoring glycemic levels.
A low glycemic diet can help manage diabetes more effectively.
Foods with a high glycemic index cause blood sugar to rise quickly.
Doctors often recommend a glycemic index diet to help manage blood sugar levels.
The glycemic response to different foods can vary greatly between individuals.
Athletes sometimes monitor their glycemic load to optimize energy during training.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glycaemic - relating to the presence of glucose in the blood (British spelling)
  • synonym: glycemic index - a measure of how quickly a food raises blood sugar levels
  • degree higher: hyperglycemic - having excessively high blood sugar levels
  • degree lower: hypoglycemic - having abnormally low blood sugar levels
