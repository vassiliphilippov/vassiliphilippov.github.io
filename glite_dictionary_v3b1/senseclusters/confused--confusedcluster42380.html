confused

adjective
mentally unclear; disoriented in time, place, or self
Pronunciation: / kənˈfjuzd / (AmE), / kənˈfjuːzd / (BrE)
unable to think clearly or to understand what is happening; feeling bewildered or uncertain

Examples

Feel confused
I felt completely confused when I stepped off the train into the unfamiliar city.
She appeared confused after waking from surgery and could not remember where she was.
The medication made him so confused that he couldn't recognize his own family.
After the sudden announcement, many employees felt confused about what to do next.
His confused explanation only made the situation more unclear.
She looked confused when she read the complex instructions on the form.
His confused expression showed he had not understood the instructions.
She felt confused after hearing all the conflicting stories about the event.
The sudden changes in schedule left everyone feeling confused and anxious.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bewildered - very confused and unable to understand something
  • antonym: clear-headed - able to think clearly and sensibly
  • antonym: clearheaded - able to think clearly; not confused
  • synonym: disoriented - unable to determine direction or position; confused about time, place, or identity
  • synonym: perplexed - unable to understand something clearly; puzzled
