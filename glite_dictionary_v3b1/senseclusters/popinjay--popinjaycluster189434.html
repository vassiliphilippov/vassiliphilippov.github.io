popinjay

noun
archaic term for a parrot
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑpɪnˌdʒeɪ / (AmE), / ˈpɒpɪnˌdʒeɪ / (BrE)
An archaic term for a parrot; sometimes used figuratively for a vain or talkative person.

Examples

An old popinjay
He was called a popinjay in jest, for his loud clothes and boastful manner.
In the king’s court, a brightly colored popinjay sat on its perch, mimicking the guests.
Medieval tapestries often feature a popinjay among other exotic animals.
