cooped up

adjective
confined in a small space
5.5 0.66/m
Pronunciation: / kupt  åp / (AmE), / kuÀêpt  åp / (BrE)
confined or restricted in a small or enclosed space, often resulting in discomfort or restlessness

Examples

Cooped up at home
After spending a week cooped up at home, she finally went for a walk in the park.
He felt like a prisoner, cooped up in his tiny office all day.
The children grew restless after being cooped up inside all afternoon because of the rain.

Synonyms

  • confined
    3.7 0.00/m
    confined means kept within a space and unable to leave, while cooped up suggests a small, stuffy place making someone restless.
  • boxed in
    6.3 0.10/m
    boxed in means trapped as if inside a box, while cooped up focuses on being kept in a small place and feeling restless.
  • cramped
    3.2 2.59/m
    cramped describes a space that is very small with little room, while cooped up describes a person kept confined and feeling restless.
