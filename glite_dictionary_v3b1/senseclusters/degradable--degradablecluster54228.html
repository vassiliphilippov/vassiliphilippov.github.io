degradable

adjective
able to break down
Pronunciation: / dɪˈɡreɪdəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈɡreɪdəbəl / (BrE)
able to break down or decompose naturally, especially by biological or chemical processes

Examples

Degradable material
This packaging is degradable and will break down in a landfill within a few years.
The company switched to degradable materials to reduce environmental impact.
Unlike plastic, paper bags are more degradable and safer for the environment.
The packaging is degradable, so it will not remain in the environment for years.
Scientists are developing more degradable materials to reduce plastic waste.
These plates are degradable and can be composted after use.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biodegradable - able to be broken down by living organisms
  • antonym: durable - able to withstand wear or decay; not easily broken down
  • antonym: nondegradable - not able to break down naturally
