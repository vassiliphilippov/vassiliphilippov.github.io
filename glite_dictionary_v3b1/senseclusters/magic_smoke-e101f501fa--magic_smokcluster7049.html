magic smoke

noun
humorous term for smoke from failed electronics
Pronunciation: / ˈmædʒɪk smoʊk / (AmE), / ˈmædʒɪk sməʊk / (BrE)
a humorous term for the smoke released when an electronic component fails, suggesting that the device functioned because of the smoke inside it

Examples

The magic smoke escapes
Don't let out the magic smoke, or the circuit board will never work again.
We knew the old computer was finished as soon as the magic smoke started billowing out.
When the capacitor blew, the distinctive smell of magic smoke filled the lab.
