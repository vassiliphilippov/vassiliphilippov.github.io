whole tone

noun
musical interval of two semitones
Pronunciation: / hoʊl toʊn / (AmE), / həʊl təʊn / (BrE)
A musical interval equal to two semitones, often used in the construction of scales.

Examples

A whole tone interval
Debussy frequently used the whole tone scale in his compositions.
Moving up a whole tone from C will get you to D.
The scale consists entirely of whole tones and lacks semitones.
