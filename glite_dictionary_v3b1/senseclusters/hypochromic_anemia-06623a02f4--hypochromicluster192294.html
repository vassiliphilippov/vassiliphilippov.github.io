hypochromic anemia

noun
anemia with reduced hemoglobin in red blood cells
4.1 5.9
Pronunciation: /haɪ.poʊ.ˈkroʊ.mɪk ə.ˈniː.mi.ə/ (AmE), /haɪ.pəʊ.ˈkrɒm.ɪk ə.ˈniː.mi.ə/ (BrE)
a type of anemia characterized by red blood cells that have less than the normal amount of hemoglobin, resulting in paler cells

Examples

Hypochromic anemia symptoms
Doctors monitor patients with hypochromic anemia for symptoms, including fatigue and pale skin.

Patients with hypochromic anaemia often experience fatigue, weakness, and pale skin.
