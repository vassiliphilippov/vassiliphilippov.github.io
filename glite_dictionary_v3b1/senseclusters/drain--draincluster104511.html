drain

noun
tube for removing fluid from body
2.5 4.4
Pronunciation: /dreɪn/ (AmE), /dreɪn/ (BrE)
a tube inserted into a body cavity to remove fluid or other unwanted material, especially during or after surgery

Examples

Surgical drain
After her procedure, the nurse checked the drain to ensure it was functioning properly.
In some cases, a drain is necessary to aid healing and reduce the risk of infection.
The surgeon inserted a drain to prevent fluid from building up after the operation.
