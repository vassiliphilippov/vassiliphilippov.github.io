paulownia

noun
light wood for crafts
9.6
Pronunciation: / pɔːˈloʊniə / (AmE), / pɔːˈləʊniə / (BrE)
the light, stable wood from the paulownia tree, often used for furniture, musical instruments, and crafts

Examples

Paulownia wood furniture
The craftsman selected paulownia for its lightness when building the traditional Japanese chest.
Many guitar makers prefer paulownia because it produces a clear, resonant sound.
She carved a delicate jewelry box from a single piece of paulownia to keep her mother's letters.

Related Terms

  • synonym: balsa - a very light wood used for crafts and model making
  • antonym: oak - a strong, heavy wood commonly used for furniture
