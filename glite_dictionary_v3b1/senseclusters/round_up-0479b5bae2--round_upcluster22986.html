round up

verb
gather together; collect
3.2 5.07/m
Pronunciation: / raÊŠnd ÊŒp / (AmE), / raÊŠnd ÊŒp / (BrE)
to gather or collect people, animals, or things together in one place

Examples

To round up cattle
Every autumn, the farmers round up their cattle for vaccination.
The police managed to round up all the suspects before dawn.
The volunteers will round up supplies for the disaster relief effort.
Before starting the meeting, please round up everyone from the office.
The teacher had to round up all the students after recess.
They worked together to round up stray dogs in the neighborhood.
