light time

noun
distance covered by light in a time period
8.5 6.8
Pronunciation: /laɪt taɪm/ (AmE), /laɪt taɪm/ (BrE)
The distance that light or radio waves travel in a given period, especially used as a measure in astronomy.

Examples

Light time distance
Astronomers calculated the light time from Earth to Mars to determine the delay in communication signals.
Measuring the distance in light time helps scientists track changes in positions of celestial objects.
The light time between the spacecraft and mission control was about eight minutes.
