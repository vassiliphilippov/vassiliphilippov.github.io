Irvingia gabonensis

noun
African tree with edible mango-like fruit
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɜrˈvɪndʒiə ɡæbəˈnɛnsɪs / (AmE), / ɜːˈvɪndʒiə ɡabəˈnɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a species of African tree with edible yellow fruit resembling mangoes, valued for its oil-rich seeds and hard, termite-resistant wood

Examples

Irvingia gabonensis tree wood
Furniture made from Irvingia gabonensis wood is resistant to termites and highly durable.
Local farmers harvest Irvingia gabonensis for its nutritious fruit and valuable oil-rich seeds.
The seeds of Irvingia gabonensis are often used to thicken soups in West African cuisine.
