viral

adjective
spreading widely online
3.7
Pronunciation: /ˈvaɪ.rəl/ (AmE), /ˈvaɪ.rəl/ (BrE)
spreading quickly and widely among many people, especially used of online content shared rapidly through social media and websites.

Examples

Viral video
The video went viral after influencers shared it, quickly flooding social media feeds worldwide.
Their fundraising campaign became viral, attracting donations from thousands of people in just days.
A catchy tune and funny lyrics helped the song turn viral across multiple streaming platforms.

Related Terms

  • antonym: obscure - not well known or rarely seen; opposite of widely spread
  • synonym: popular - liked or enjoyed by many people
  • synonym: trending - currently popular and widely discussed online
