scaly polypore

noun
a fungus with a scaly, darkening cap
Pronunciation: / ˈskeɪli ˈpɑliˌpɔr / (AmE), / ˈskeɪli ˈpɒliˌpɔː / (BrE)
a fungus with a scaly cap, often with a lateral stalk, that turns nearly black at maturity and is found widely in the Northern Hemisphere

Examples

The scaly polypore fungus
As the scaly polypore matures, its cap darkens and becomes almost black.
Some hikers mistakenly identify the scaly polypore as an edible mushroom, but it is generally inedible.
The scaly polypore is often found growing on dead hardwood trees in temperate forests.
