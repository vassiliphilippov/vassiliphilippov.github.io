damned

noun
people condemned to eternal punishment
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈdæmd / (AmE), / ˈdamd / (BrE)
(in religious belief) people condemned to eternal punishment in hell or the afterlife.

Examples

The damned suffer
He wrote a novel about the struggles and regrets of the damned.
In medieval art, the damned are often depicted suffering in the fires of hell.
The preacher warned of the fate that awaited the damned.

Related Terms

  • antonym: saved - people believed to be delivered from sin and its consequences, especially in a religious context
