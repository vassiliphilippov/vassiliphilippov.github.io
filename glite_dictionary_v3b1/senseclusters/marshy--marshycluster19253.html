marshy

adjective
soft, wet, waterlogged (land/soil)
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːrʃi / (AmE), / ˈmɑːʃi / (BrE)
(of land or soil) soft, wet, and often covered with water, like a marsh.

Examples

Marshy land
After heavy rain, the fields became marshy and difficult to walk through.
Several bird species nest in the marshy areas near the riverbank.
The construction crew had trouble building on the marshy ground.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boggy - similar in meaning to marshy; land that is soft, wet, and spongy
  • antonym: dry - opposite of wet or marshy, not wet or waterlogged
  • synonym: swampy - land or area similar to a swamp, wet and soft
