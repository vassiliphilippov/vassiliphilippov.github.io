cruiser

noun
police patrol car
3.2 2.21/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàkruÀêz…ôr / (AmE), / ÀàkruÀêz…ô / (BrE)
a police car used for patrolling streets, typically equipped with radio communication

Examples

Police cruiser car
He watched as a cruiser passed by, its lights flashing in the night.
Several police cruisers arrived at the scene within minutes of the call.
The officer drove the cruiser slowly down Main Street, keeping an eye out for any trouble.

Synonyms

  • police cruiser
    3.0 0.68/m
    police cruiser is the full, clear name used in reports and signs, while cruiser is the shorter everyday term for the same patrol vehicle.
  • police car
    1.0 5.04/m
    a police car is the general vehicle police use for many tasks, while cruiser refers specifically to a patrol car with radio and lights.
  • patrol car
    2.8 1.78/m
    a patrol car is used mainly for street patrols and shows police presence, while cruiser highlights a vehicle fitted for patrolling.
  • squad car
    2.7 1.71/m
    a squad car is an American informal name often used by officers, while cruiser is the more general patrol car term.
  • prowl car
    7.4 0.00/m
    a prowl car is an older or regional word heard in some places, while cruiser is the standard modern term for a patrol vehicle.
