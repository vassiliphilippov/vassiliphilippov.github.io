Maccabees

proper noun
Jewish rebel group
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈmækəˌbiz / (AmE), / ˈmækəˌbiːz / (BrE)
the Jewish family and their followers who led a revolt against foreign rule in ancient Judea, celebrated during Hanukkah

Examples

The Maccabees revolt
The story of the Maccabees is an important part of Jewish history and tradition.
During Hanukkah, families remember the courage of the Maccabees who fought for religious freedom.
The Maccabees led a successful revolt that restored the Temple in Jerusalem.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Hasmoneans - the dynasty established by the Maccabees after their revolt
