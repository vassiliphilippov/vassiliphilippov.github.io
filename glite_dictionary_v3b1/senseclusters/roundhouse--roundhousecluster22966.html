roundhouse

noun
a sweeping, wide hook punch
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈraʊn(d)ˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈraʊn(d)ˌhaʊs / (BrE)
A punch thrown with a broad, sweeping motion, typically coming from the side rather than straight on.

Examples

Roundhouse punch
He had a reputation for knocking out his rivals with a single, devastating roundhouse.
She dodged the roundhouse just in time and countered with a quick jab.
The fighter surprised his opponent with a powerful roundhouse that sent him reeling.
