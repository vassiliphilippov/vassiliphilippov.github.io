respect

noun
courteous expression of esteem
Pronunciation: / rɪˈspɛkt / (AmE), / rɪˈspɛkt / (BrE)
polite actions, expressions, or attitudes that show regard or consideration for others

Examples

Show respect
As a sign of respect, she stood when the judge entered the courtroom.
He paid his respects at the funeral service yesterday.
Please give my respects to your parents when you see them.
He declined to comment out of respect for the family's privacy.
She always treats her colleagues with respect, no matter their position.
The children showed great respect for their teacher during the ceremony.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contempt - lack of respect or reverence
  • synonym: deference - polite submission and respect
  • antonym: disregard - lack of consideration or respect
  • synonym: regard - a feeling of respect or admiration for someone
