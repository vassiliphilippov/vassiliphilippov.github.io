pinfold

noun
enclosure for stray animals
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪnˌfoʊld / (AmE), / ˈpɪnˌfəʊld / (BrE)
an enclosure or pen where stray animals are kept until reclaimed by their owners, especially in historical contexts

Examples

A pinfold for cattle
In old English towns, a pinfold was used to keep stray cattle until their owners paid a fine.
The ancient stone pinfold still stands as a reminder of medieval livestock management.
The farmer found his lost sheep in the pinfold near the village green.
