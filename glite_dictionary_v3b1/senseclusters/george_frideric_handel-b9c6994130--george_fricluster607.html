George Frideric Handel

proper noun
British composer, Baroque period, 'Messiah'
5.3
Pronunciation: /dʒɔːrdʒ frɪ.ˈde.rɪk ˈhæn.dəl/ (AmE), /dʒɔːdʒ frɪ.ˈde.rɪk ˈhæn.dəl/ (BrE)
(1685–1759) A German-born British composer of the Baroque period, renowned for his oratorio 'Messiah' and numerous other influential works.

Examples

George Frideric Handel composer
George Frideric Handel composed some of the most enduring choral works of the Baroque era.
Many consider George Frideric Handel one of the greatest musicians of the eighteenth century.
The annual performance of 'Messiah' celebrates the legacy of George Frideric Handel.

  • synonym: oratorio - type of large-scale musical composition for orchestra and voices, example: Handel's Messiah
