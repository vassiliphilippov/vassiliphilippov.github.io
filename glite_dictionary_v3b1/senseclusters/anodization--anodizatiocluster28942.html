anodization

noun
metal surface treatment
7.9
Pronunciation: /ə.noʊ.də.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /æn.oʊ.dɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ə.nəʊ.də.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /æn.əʊ.dɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of creating a protective or decorative oxide layer on a metal surface (especially aluminum) using an electrolytic method

Examples

The anodization process
The anodization of aluminum parts makes them more resistant to corrosion and wear.
Different colors can be achieved through anodization during the manufacturing process.
Many bicycle frames undergo anodization to improve their durability and appearance.

Different colors can be achieved through anodization, giving products a unique appearance.

  • synonym: electroplating - a process of coating a metal object with a thin layer of another metal using electricity
