obeisant

adjective
submissive or respectful
Pronunciation: / oʊˈbiːsənt / (AmE), / əʊˈbiːsənt / (BrE)
showing respect, deference, or willingness to obey, often in a submissive or formal way

Examples

To be obeisant to leaders
The new employee was extremely obeisant toward her supervisors, always eager to please.
He gave an obeisant bow as he entered the grand hall.
Some leaders expect their followers to be completely obeisant at all times.
The staff gave an obeisant greeting as the ambassador entered the hall.
She offered an obeisant nod to her elders during the ceremony.
His obeisant manner impressed the traditional members of the committee.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compliant - willing to agree with others or obey rules
  • synonym: deferential - showing polite submission or respect
  • antonym: defiant - openly resisting or refusing to obey
  • antonym: insolent - showing a rude and arrogant lack of respect
  • synonym: respectful - showing respect or esteem
  • synonym: submissive - willing to obey or yield to others
