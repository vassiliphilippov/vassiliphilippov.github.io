glomalin

noun
soil-binding protein
Pronunciation: / ɡloʊˈmælɪn / (AmE), / ɡləʊˈmælɪn / (BrE)
a glycoprotein produced by certain soil fungi that helps bind soil particles together and improve soil structure

Examples

Glomalin in soil
Scientists discovered that glomalin plays a key role in maintaining healthy soil structure.
The presence of glomalin in the soil can increase its ability to retain water and nutrients.
Farmers are interested in how glomalin produced by fungi can improve crop yields.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glycoprotein - a protein with attached sugar molecules
