fluidics

noun
fluid control technology
Pronunciation: / fluˈɪdɪks / (AmE), / fluˈɪdɪks / (BrE)
the study and technology of using fluids (liquids or gases) to control or operate devices and systems, especially in machinery or microchips

Examples

Fluidics technology
Advances in fluidics have made it possible to perform complex tests on a single microchip.
Researchers use fluidics to precisely control the flow of liquids in medical devices.
Modern lab equipment often relies on fluidics to handle very small amounts of chemicals.
Recent developments in fluidics have led to more efficient microchip cooling systems.
The laboratory specializes in research on fluidics for medical and industrial applications.
Understanding fluidics is essential for designing advanced inkjet printers and lab-on-a-chip devices.
The engineer checked the fluidics to ensure the machine was operating smoothly.
Modern printers rely on advanced fluidics to control the flow of ink.
Proper maintenance of the fluidics is essential for the safe operation of medical devices.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hydraulics - the science of using liquids to transmit force or motion
  • synonym: microfluidics - the science of controlling fluids in tiny channels, often in lab-on-a-chip devices
  • antonym: pneumatics - the science of using gases to transmit force or motion
