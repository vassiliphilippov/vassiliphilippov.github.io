puristically

adverb
in a strict, pure way
Pronunciation: / pjʊˈrɪstɪkli / (AmE), / pjʊˈrɪstɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that strictly follows purity or traditional rules, especially in language or style

Examples

Puristically judged entries
The committee judged the entries puristically, accepting only those that followed the original guidelines exactly.
She writes puristically, avoiding any slang or borrowed words in her essays.
The chef prepared the dish puristically, using only traditional ingredients and methods.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conservatively - in a way that resists change and favors tradition
  • antonym: liberally - in a way that is open to new ideas and not strict about rules
