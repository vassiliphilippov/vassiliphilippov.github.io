root hair

noun
hairlike outgrowth on plant root absorbing nutrients
5.3 3.3
Pronunciation: / ruːt hɛr / (AmE), / ruːt heə / (BrE)
a thin, hairlike projection from an epidermal cell of a plant root, just behind the root tip, that absorbs water and nutrients from the soil

Examples

Root hair cell
Damage to the root hair can reduce the plant's ability to absorb nutrients efficiently.
The root hair increases the surface area available for water absorption.
Under the microscope, several root hairs could be seen extending into the soil.
