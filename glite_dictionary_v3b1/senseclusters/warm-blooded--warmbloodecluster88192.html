Examples

warm-blooded animals
Birds are warm-blooded, meaning their body temperature does not change much with the environment.
The warm-blooded nature of humans allows us to survive in a wide range of conditions.
Unlike reptiles, mammals are warm-blooded and can thrive in colder climates.

Whales are warm-blooded mammals that live in the ocean.
