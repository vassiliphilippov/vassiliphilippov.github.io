Galiella rufa

noun
a cup-shaped fungus of the Galiella genus
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɡəˈlaɪələ ˈruːfə / (AmE), / ɡæliˈɛlə ˈruːfə / (BrE)
A species of fungus with cup-shaped fruiting bodies that have a jellylike interior and a short stalk, found on decaying wood.

Examples

Galiella rufa fungus
Researchers documented several specimens of Galiella rufa growing on decaying logs in the forest.
The Galiella rufa can be identified by its dark, cup-like cap and gelatinous texture.
The jellylike interior of Galiella rufa distinguishes it from other similar fungi.
