constructionism

noun
theory of knowledge creation
8.8
Pronunciation: / kənˈstrʌkʃənɪzəm / (AmE), / kənˈstrʌkʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
the theory that knowledge or meaning is created by individuals or groups rather than discovered objectively

Examples

Social constructionism
Many educators use constructionism to design classroom activities that encourage students to build their own understanding.
Debates about reality often reference constructionism as a way to explain how societies shape knowledge.
The rise of constructionism has influenced research in psychology, sociology, and education.

Related Terms

  • synonym: constructivism - the theory that people construct knowledge through experiences and interactions
  • antonym: objectivism - the belief that knowledge exists independently of the mind
