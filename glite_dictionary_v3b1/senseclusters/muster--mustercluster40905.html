muster

verb
gather or summon up
5.4 6.14/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆmÊŒstÉ™r /; / ËˆmÊŒstÉš / (AmE), / ËˆmÊŒstÉ™ / (BrE)
to gather or bring together people, resources, or qualities, especially for a particular purpose

Examples

To muster support
He couldn't muster enough support for his proposal.
She tried to muster the confidence to speak in front of the audience.
The general had to muster his troops before the sunrise attack.
At a moment's notice, the team can be mustered in case of emergency.
Citizens were mustered for jury duty by official summons.
The sergeant will muster the soldiers at dawn for inspection.

Synonyms

  • summon
    4.5 3.70/m
    summon calls a person or inner strength with effort, while muster gathers people, resources, or qualities together for a purpose.
  • gather
    1.0 65/m
    gather brings people, things, or information into one place, while muster is used when people or resources are gathered for a clear purpose.
  • assemble
    3.3 11/m
    assemble brings people or things together in a planned way, while muster often adds a sense of formal readiness or purpose.
  • round up
    3.2 5.07/m
    round up gathers people or animals roughly and quickly, while muster usually means a more organized, purposeful collection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assemble - to gather together in one place
  • antonym: dismiss - to send away or release from duty or service
  • antonym: disperse - to scatter or break up a group
  • synonym: summon - to call people to appear or be present, especially officially
