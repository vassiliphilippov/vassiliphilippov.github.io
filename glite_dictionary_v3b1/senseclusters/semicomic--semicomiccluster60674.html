semicomic

noun
partly comic character
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmiˈkɑmɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈkɒmɪk / (BrE)
a performer or character whose role is only partly comic and provides limited comic relief in a play or performance

Examples

Semicomic character
The director assigned the semicomic role to an actor known for subtle humor.
As the semicomic, she brought a touch of lightness to the otherwise serious drama.
Audiences appreciated the semicomic for breaking the tension with occasional jokes.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: comic - a performer or character whose main role is to provide humor
  • degree lower: straight man - a performer who sets up jokes for the comic and does not act humorously
