palpable

adjective
so intense as to seem tangible
6.3 4.9
Pronunciation: /ˈpæl.pə.bəl/ (AmE), /ˈpæl.pə.bəl/ (BrE)
(of a feeling, atmosphere, or fact) so strong or obvious that it seems almost touchable or is easily noticed

Examples

Palpable tension
A palpable tension filled the courtroom as the verdict was about to be read.
After the announcement, the excitement in the room was palpable and everyone started cheering.
Her relief was palpable when she saw her missing cat at the door.
His frustration was so palpable that everyone in the meeting noticed.
The tension during the negotiations was almost palpable.
There was a palpable sense of relief in the room after the announcement.
After the storm, a palpable sense of relief swept through the town.
The difference between the two proposals was palpable to everyone present.
There was a palpable tension in the room as the results were announced.
