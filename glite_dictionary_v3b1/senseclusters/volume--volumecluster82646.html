volume

noun
amount of 3D space occupied
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈvɑːl.jum /; / ˈvɑljum / (AmE), / ˈvɒl.juːm /; / ˈvɒljuːm / (BrE)
the amount of three-dimensional space that something occupies; capacity or measurement of space

Examples

Water volume
Heating the air causes it to expand and increase its volume.
The architect calculated the volume of the building for heating estimates.
This container has a volume of two liters.
Mix one volume of juice with three volumes of water for a lighter drink.
The recipe calls for equal volume of flour and sugar to achieve the right texture.
The technician measured out a precise volume of solution for the experiment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: capacity - the maximum amount that something can contain
  • antonym: surface area - the total area that the surface of an object occupies
