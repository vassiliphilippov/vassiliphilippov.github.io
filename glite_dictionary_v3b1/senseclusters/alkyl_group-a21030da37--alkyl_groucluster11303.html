alkyl group

noun
univalent group from an aliphatic hydrocarbon
6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈæl.kɪl gruːp/ /; / /ˈæl.kaɪl gruːp/ (AmE), /ˈæl.kɪl gruːp/ /; / /ˈæl.kaɪl gruːp/ (BrE)
a univalent group of atoms, with the general formula CnH2n+1, derived from an aliphatic hydrocarbon by removal of one hydrogen atom

Examples

A alkyl group in chemistry
In organic chemistry, methyl is the simplest alkyl group.
Substituting an alkyl group for a hydrogen atom can increase the compound's hydrophobicity.
The addition of an alkyl group can alter the properties of a molecule.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alkyl - an organic radical derived from an alkane
  • antonym: aryl group - a univalent group derived from an aromatic hydrocarbon
