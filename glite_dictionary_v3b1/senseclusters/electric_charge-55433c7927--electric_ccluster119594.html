electric charge

noun
amount of electricity in a body
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɛktrɪk tʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪk tʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
a physical property of matter that causes it to experience a force when placed in an electromagnetic field, measured as an excess or deficiency of electrons, and classified as positive or negative

Examples

An electric charge in matter
Lightning is caused by the sudden movement of an electric charge in the atmosphere.
Scientists measured the electric charge on the particles to determine their properties.
The balloon had an electric charge after being rubbed on the sweater.

Related Terms

  • synonym: electric current - the flow of electric charge
  • antonym: neutral - having no net electric charge
