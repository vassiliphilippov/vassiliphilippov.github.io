Old Catholic Church

proper noun
Catholic churches separated from Rome in the 18th century
Pronunciation: / oʊld ˈkæθlɪk tʃɝːtʃ / (AmE), / əʊld ˈkæθlɪk tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
A group of Catholic churches which separated from the Roman Catholic Church in the 18th century, mainly over issues of doctrine and church authority.

Examples

The Old Catholic Church group
Bishops from the Old Catholic Church attended the ecumenical conference in Geneva.
In the 19th century, several communities joined the Old Catholic Church after disputes over doctrine.
The Old Catholic Church maintains many traditional beliefs but does not recognize papal infallibility.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Roman Catholic Church - the main body of the Catholic Church headed by the Pope
