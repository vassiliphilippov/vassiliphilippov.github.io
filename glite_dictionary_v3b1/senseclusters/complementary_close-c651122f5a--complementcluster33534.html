complementary close

noun
formal closing phrase in a letter
3.1 5.3
Pronunciation: / kəmˌplɛmənˈtɛri kloʊs / (AmE), / kəmˌplɪmənˈtɛəri kləʊz / (BrE)
the formal phrase at the end of a letter, just before the signature, that expresses the sender's regard for the recipient, such as 'Sincerely yours' or 'Best regards'

Examples

A complementary close phrase
Every business letter should include a complementary close before the sender's name.
She signed off her letter with a traditional complementary close to show respect.
The teacher explained where to place the complementary close in a formal letter.
