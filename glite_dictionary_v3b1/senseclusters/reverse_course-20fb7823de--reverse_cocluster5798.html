reverse course

verb
change direction or decision
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvɝːs kɔrs / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːs kɔːs / (BrE)
to change direction, strategy, or decision, especially after having started along a particular path

Examples

To reverse course quickly
After several failed attempts, the company decided to reverse course and pursue a different approach.
She realized her mistake halfway through and chose to reverse course before it was too late.
When public opinion turned against the bill, lawmakers were forced to reverse course.

Related Terms

  • synonym: backtrack - to go back over the same route or reconsider decisions
  • antonym: stick to one's guns - to refuse to change one's decision or opinion
