stoop

verb
bend forward, often habitually
2.6 4.08/m
Pronunciation: / stuÀêp / (AmE), / stuÀêp / (BrE)
to bend your body forward and downward, often at the waist

Examples

To stoop down
After years of working in the fields, he began to stoop as he walked.
The elderly woman stooped to pick up the letter lying on the floor.
You could tell he was tired because he would stoop over his desk for hours.
He had to stoop to enter the low doorway.
She stooped down to pick up a coin from the ground.
The old woman stooped to tie her shoes.
He had to stoop to avoid hitting his head on the low doorway.
She stooped down to pick up the coin from the ground.
The elderly woman stooped as she walked slowly down the street.

Synonyms

  • bend
    1.0 21/m
    bend means leaning your upper body forward and down at the waist, while stoop often suggests a lower, more fixed posture.
  • hunch over
    4.2 2.17/m
    hunch over means bending forward with rounded shoulders from tiredness or focus, while stoop is a simpler forward bend at the waist.
  • hunch forward
    4.4 0.30/m
    hunch forward means bending with rounded shoulders to hide or protect yourself or from tiredness, while stoop is a plain waist bend.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crouch - to bend low with the limbs close to the body
  • synonym: slouch - to stand, sit, or move in a lazy, drooping way
  • antonym: straighten - to make or become straight
