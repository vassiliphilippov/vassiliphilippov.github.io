tithe barn

noun
barn for storing tithes paid in kind
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪð bɑrn / (AmE), / ˈtaɪð bɑːn / (BrE)
a barn, usually found in England, originally built to store tithes paid in the form of crops or produce

Examples

Historic tithe barns
Historic tithe barns can still be seen in some rural parts of England.
The harvest was collected in the tithe barn before being distributed to the church.
The old tithe barn at the edge of the village is now a museum about medieval agriculture.
