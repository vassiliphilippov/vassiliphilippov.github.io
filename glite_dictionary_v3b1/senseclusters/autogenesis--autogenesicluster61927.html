autogenesis

noun
generation of life from nonliving matter
Pronunciation: / ˌɔːtoʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəʊˈdʒɛnɪsɪs / (BrE)
the hypothetical process by which living organisms are generated from nonliving matter

Examples

Autogenesis from nonliving matter
Many scientists now reject autogenesis in favor of biogenesis to explain life's beginnings.
Some early experiments attempted to prove autogenesis by observing the formation of microbes from nonliving materials.
The theory of autogenesis was once proposed to explain the spontaneous origin of life.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abiogenesis - the original evolution of life from inorganic or inanimate substances
