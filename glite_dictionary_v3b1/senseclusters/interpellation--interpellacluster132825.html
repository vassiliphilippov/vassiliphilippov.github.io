interpellation

noun
act of interrupting with a remark or question
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərpəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəpəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of interrupting a speaker with a question, remark, or objection, especially in a formal assembly or discussion.

Examples

Debate interpellation
During the heated debate, the senator's interpellation caught everyone off guard.
Her sudden interpellation shifted the direction of the conversation entirely.
The chairman grew frustrated by the constant interpellation from the opposition benches.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interjection - a sudden remark, often interrupting someone
  • synonym: interruption - the action of stopping something from continuing
