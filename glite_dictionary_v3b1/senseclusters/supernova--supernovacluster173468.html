supernova

noun
exploding star; astronomical event
5.4 6.0
Pronunciation: /suː.pər.ˈnoʊ.və/ (AmE), /suː.pə.ˈnəʊ.və/ (BrE)
An astronomical event in which a star suddenly explodes, greatly increasing in brightness for a short time before fading.

Examples

A supernova explosion
A supernova can outshine an entire galaxy for a brief period.
Scientists studied the remnants left behind by the supernova.
The supernova was visible to the naked eye for several weeks.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: nova - a star suddenly becoming much brighter, not as extreme as a supernova
