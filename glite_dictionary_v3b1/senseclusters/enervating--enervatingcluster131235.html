enervating

adjective
causing loss of energy or strength
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɛnərˌveɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɛnəˌveɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
making someone feel drained of energy or vitality; causing debilitation.

Examples

An enervating effect
Running a marathon in high humidity can have an enervating effect on even experienced athletes.
She found the endless meetings to be mentally enervating and struggled to stay focused.
The oppressive heat was enervating, leaving everyone feeling exhausted by midday.

Related Terms

  • synonym: draining - tiring or exhausting; using up energy
  • antonym: invigorating - making someone feel stronger, healthier, and more full of energy
