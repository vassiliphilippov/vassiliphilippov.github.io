sporulation

noun
production and release of spores
Pronunciation: / ˌspɔr.jəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌspɔːr.jʊˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process of producing and releasing spores, especially as a form of asexual reproduction in certain organisms such as fungi, bacteria, and plants

Examples

sporulation process
Certain bacteria undergo sporulation when faced with harsh environmental conditions.
Researchers observed sporulation under the microscope to study the life cycle of the mold.
The process of sporulation allows fungi to spread and colonize new areas efficiently.

Related Terms

  • synonym: spore - a reproductive cell capable of developing into a new individual without fusion with another cell
