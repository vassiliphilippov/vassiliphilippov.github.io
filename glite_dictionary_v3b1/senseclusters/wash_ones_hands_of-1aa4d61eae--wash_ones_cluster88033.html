wash one's hands of

verb
stop being involved or responsible
Pronunciation: / wɑʃ wʌnz hændz ʌv / (AmE), / wɒʃ wʌnz hændz ɒv / (BrE)
to intentionally stop being involved with or accepting responsibility for someone or something

Examples

To wash hands of responsibility
After hearing about the repeated problems, the manager decided to wash his hands of the project.
Once the evidence was presented, the witness washed his hands of the entire case.
She finally washes her hands of the constant family drama and focuses on her own life.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disown - refuse to be connected with or responsible for
  • antonym: take responsibility for - to accept responsibility for something
