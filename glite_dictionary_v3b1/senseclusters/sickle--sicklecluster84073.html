sickle

verb
to cut with sickle
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈsɪkəl / (BrE)
to cut or harvest plants, grain, or stalks using a sickle, or to shape something into a curved, sickle-like form

Examples

To sickle the grass
The farmers sickled the wheat early in the morning before the sun became too hot.
She carefully sickled the tall grass along the edge of the field.
The gardener sickled the weeds growing around the vegetable patch.

Related Terms

  • synonym: harvest - to gather crops from the fields
  • synonym: reap - to cut and collect crops, especially grain
