unstrung

adjective
emotionally upset or unnerved
7.6
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈstrʌŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈstrʌŋ / (BrE)
extremely nervous, upset, or unable to think calmly, especially as a result of shock or stress.

Examples

Completely unstrung after shock
After receiving the tragic news, she was completely unstrung for days.
He became unstrung by the constant criticism and began to doubt his abilities.
The pressure of the trial left the witness visibly unstrung in the courtroom.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other emotions
  • synonym: upset - unhappy, worried, or disturbed
