sulcate

adjective
having deep, narrow grooves
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌl.keɪt / (AmE), / ˈsʌl.keɪt / (BrE)
(of a surface) having deep, narrow grooves or furrows.

Examples

A sulcate shell
The sulcate shell of the sea snail distinguishes it from other smooth-shelled species.
The leaf was sulcate, with deep grooves running lengthwise along its surface.
Under the microscope, the pollen grains appeared sulcate and ridged.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grooved - having grooves or channels; furrowed
  • antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface; not grooved
