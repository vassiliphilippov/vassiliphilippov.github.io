Cape Hatteras

proper noun
prominent cape on North Carolina's Atlantic coast
Pronunciation: / keɪp ˈhætərəs / (AmE), / keɪp ˈhætərəs / (BrE)
a promontory located on Hatteras Island, off the Atlantic coast of North Carolina, known for its treacherous waters and frequent storms

Examples

Cape Hatteras promontory
Many sailors have feared the waters near Cape Hatteras because of its dangerous currents.
Strong hurricanes often pass close to Cape Hatteras during the Atlantic storm season.
The lighthouse at Cape Hatteras is one of the most recognizable on the east coast.
