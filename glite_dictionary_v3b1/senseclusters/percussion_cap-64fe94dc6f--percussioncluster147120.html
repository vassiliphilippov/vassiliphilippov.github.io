percussion cap

noun
small detonator for igniting firearms
Pronunciation: / pərˈkʌʃən kæp / (AmE), / pəˈkʌʃən kap / (BrE)
a small device containing an explosive compound, which detonates when struck, used especially in firearms to ignite the main charge

Examples

A percussion cap device
After replacing the spent percussion cap, he reloaded the antique pistol.
During the 19th century, percussion caps revolutionized firearm technology.
The rifle uses a percussion cap to fire its charge.
