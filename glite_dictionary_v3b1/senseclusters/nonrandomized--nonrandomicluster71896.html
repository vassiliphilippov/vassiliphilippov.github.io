nonrandomized

adjective
not chosen randomly
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈrændəˌmaɪzd /; / ˌnɑnˈrændəˌmaɪzd / (AmE), / nɒnˈrændəmaɪzd /; / ˌnɒnˈrændəmaɪzd / (BrE)
not using random methods or procedures, especially in research or experiments

Examples

A nonrandomized trial
The researchers conducted a nonrandomized trial to compare the effects of two treatments.
A nonrandomized sample may introduce bias into the study results.
In a nonrandomized experiment, participants are assigned to groups based on specific criteria.
The study used a nonrandomized design, so the results may be less reliable.
A nonrandomized trial assigns participants to groups without using chance.
The nonrandomized process ensures the same outcome every time the experiment is run.

Related Terms

  • antonym: randomized - assigned or selected by a random process
