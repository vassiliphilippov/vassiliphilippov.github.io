scarring

noun
surface damage marks
4.1
Pronunciation: /ˈskɑː.rɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈskɑː.rɪŋ/ (BrE)
visible marks or damage left on a surface, such as land or buildings, after being affected by force, erosion, or impact

Examples

Land scarring
The construction work caused significant scarring on the once-pristine landscape.
Years of mining left visible scarring on the hills surrounding the town.
Heavy machinery can result in scarring that takes decades for nature to heal.
