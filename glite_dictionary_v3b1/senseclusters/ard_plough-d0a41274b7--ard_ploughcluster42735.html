ard plough

noun
simple type of early plough
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / …ërd pla ä / (AmE), / …ëÀêd pla ä / (BrE)
a simple, light plough without a mouldboard, used mainly in ancient and early agriculture to break up soil without turning it completely

Examples

The ard plough tool
Historians have found remnants of the ard plough in several archaeological sites.
The farmer demonstrated how the ard plough was used in ancient agriculture.
Unlike modern ploughs, the ard plough simply scratched the surface of the soil.
