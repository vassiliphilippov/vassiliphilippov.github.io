sublethal

adjective
not deadly but harmful
7.6
Pronunciation: / sʌbˈliːθəl / (AmE), / sʌbˈliːθəl / (BrE)
not strong enough to cause death but still capable of causing harm or damage

Examples

Sublethal concentration
The pesticide was applied at a sublethal concentration to minimize environmental impact.
Researchers observed behavioral changes in animals exposed to sublethal levels of the toxin.
Even a sublethal dose of the drug can have serious side effects over time.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: harmless - not causing any harm or damage
  • degree higher: lethal - causing death
