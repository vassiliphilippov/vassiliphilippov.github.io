incendiary bomb

noun
bomb designed to start fires
4.5
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈsɛndiˌɛri bɑm / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɛndiəri bɒm / (BrE)
a bomb designed to start fires, especially effective against flammable targets such as fuel or buildings

Examples

An incendiary bomb
Authorities discovered an unexploded incendiary bomb in the abandoned building.
During World War II, entire cities were devastated by incendiary bombs.
The warehouse was destroyed when an incendiary bomb detonated near the fuel tanks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: firebomb - a bomb that causes fire when it explodes
