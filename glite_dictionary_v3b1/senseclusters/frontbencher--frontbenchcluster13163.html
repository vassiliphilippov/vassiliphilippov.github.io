frontbencher

noun
senior member of UK Parliament seated at the front
Pronunciation: / ˌfrʌntˈbɛntʃər / (AmE), / ˌfrʌntˈbɛntʃə / (BrE)
a member of the House of Commons who is a government minister or an official spokesperson for the opposition, typically seated on the front benches of Parliament

Examples

A frontbencher in Parliament
As a new frontbencher, he was expected to take on more responsibilities in Parliament.
Opposition frontbenchers challenged the government's proposal during the heated debate.
The Prime Minister spoke while several frontbenchers nodded in agreement behind her.

Related Terms

  • antonym: backbencher - a member of Parliament who does not hold governmental office or shadow office and sits behind the frontbenchers
