ejector

noun
person who ousts or supplants another
6.7
Pronunciation: / ɪˈdʒɛktər / (AmE), / ɪˈdʒɛktə / (BrE)
a person who forces someone else out of a position, place, or role; someone who ousts or supplants another

Examples

The ejector of a leader
He became the ejector of his predecessor, replacing her as CEO.
In ancient times, the victor often acted as the ejector of the previous ruler.
The board relied on an outside ejector to resolve the leadership dispute.

Related Terms

  • synonym: evictor - person who evicts someone, especially from property
  • synonym: oust - to remove or force out of a position
