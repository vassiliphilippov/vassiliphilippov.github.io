heterodiegetic

adjective
external narrator
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛt̬əroʊdaɪəˈdʒɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəʊdaɪəˈdʒɛtɪk / (BrE)
describing a narrator who is not a character in the story being told

Examples

A heterodiegetic narrator
The story is told by a heterodiegetic narrator who observes the characters from the outside.
Many classic novels use a heterodiegetic perspective to give readers a broader view of events.
A heterodiegetic narrator can describe scenes the main characters never witness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: homodiegetic - describing a narrator who is also a character in the story
  • synonym: omniscient - knowing everything; often used for narrators who know all characters' thoughts
