Ceiba pentandra

noun
species of tropical tree; source of kapok fiber
Pronunciation: / ˌseɪbə pɛnˈtændrə / (AmE), / ˌsiːbə pɛnˈtændrə / (BrE)
A massive tropical tree with deeply ridged trunk, known for producing large pods containing seeds covered with silky floss, used as the source of kapok fiber.

Farmers harvest the floss from Ceiba pentandra trees to use as stuffing for pillows and mattresses.
Many tropical animals rely on the Ceiba pentandra for shelter and food throughout the year.
The towering Ceiba pentandra dominates the rainforest canopy with its impressive height and sprawling branches.
