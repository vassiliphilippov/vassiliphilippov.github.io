photosphere

noun
luminous surface layer of a star
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊtəˌsfɪr / (AmE), / ˈfəʊtəˌsfɪə / (BrE)
the intensely bright outer layer of a star, especially the sun, from which most of its visible light is emitted

Examples

Sun's photosphere
Astronomers study the photosphere to understand solar activity.
Sunspots appear as dark patches on the photosphere.
The photosphere of the sun emits most of its visible light.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chromosphere - the layer of a star's atmosphere above the photosphere
  • degree higher: corona - the outermost layer of a star's atmosphere
