medial rectus muscle

noun
eye muscle that turns eyeball inward
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈmiːdiəl ˈrɛktəs ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈmiːdiəl ˈrɛktəs ˈmʌsl̩ / (BrE)
the muscle located on the medial side of the eyeball whose contraction moves the eye inward toward the nose

Examples

The medial rectus muscle inside eye
An injury to the medial rectus muscle may cause difficulty in turning the eye inward.
During the eye exam, the doctor assessed the function of the medial rectus muscle.
The medial rectus muscle is responsible for moving the eye toward the nose.
