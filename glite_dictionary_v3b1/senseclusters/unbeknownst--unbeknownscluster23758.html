unbeknownst

adjective, adverb
without someone's knowledge
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnbɪˈnoʊnst / (AmE), / ˌʌnbɪˈnəʊnst / (BrE)
happening or existing without someone knowing (usually used as 'unbeknownst to [someone]')

Examples

Unbeknownst to parents
Unbeknownst to her parents, she had already bought the ticket.
Unbeknownst to the public, the company was planning a major merger.
The keys had, unbeknownst to me, fallen behind the sofa.
Unbeknownst to her parents, she had applied for colleges abroad.
Unbeknownst to the manager, the team had already finished the project ahead of schedule.
She left a gift on his desk, unbeknownst to anyone else in the office.

Related Terms

  • antonym: known - understood or recognized by someone
  • synonym: unbeknown - without the knowledge of; unknown to
