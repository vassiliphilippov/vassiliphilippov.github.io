Hamiltonian

adjective
of Hamilton's mathematics
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌhæməlˈtoʊniən / (AmE), / ˌhæmɪlˈtəʊniən / (BrE)
relating to the mathematical work or theories of Sir William Rowan Hamilton, especially in graph theory or physics.

Examples

Hamiltonian path
A Hamiltonian path visits every vertex in a graph exactly once.
The Hamiltonian operator is central to quantum mechanics.
Researchers studied Hamiltonian cycles to solve complex network problems.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Eulerian - relating to the mathematical work of Leonhard Euler, especially in graph theory
