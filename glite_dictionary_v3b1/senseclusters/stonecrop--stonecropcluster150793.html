stonecrop

noun
plant of the genus Sedum with fleshy leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈstoʊnˌkrɑp / (AmE), / ˈstəʊnˌkrɒp / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Sedum, typically found in northern temperate regions, with fleshy leaves and red, yellow, or white flowers

Examples

Stonecrop plant leaves
Stonecrop is known for thriving in poor, rocky soils where few other plants can survive.
In late summer, the stonecrop produced clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers.
The gardener planted several stonecrop varieties to fill the gaps between rocks in the garden.
