shock

noun
sudden distress or disbelief from bad news
1.9 2.0
Pronunciation: /ʃɑːk/ /; / /ʃɔːk/ (AmE), /ʃɒk/ (BrE)
a sudden strong feeling of surprise, distress, or disbelief caused by an unexpected event

Examples

In shock after news
He was so overwhelmed by the accident that he sat in shock for hours.
The news of her sudden resignation left the whole team in shock.
They reacted with shock when they heard about the unexpected layoffs.
He tried to hide his disappointment, but the defeat was clearly a shock to him.
It was a shock to discover that the store had already closed for the day.
The news of her sudden resignation was a complete shock to everyone at the office.

Related Terms

  • antonym: delight - great pleasure or joy, an opposite feeling to shock
  • synonym: disappointment - sadness or displeasure caused by the non-fulfillment of one's hopes or expectations
  • degree lower: surprise - a feeling of mild astonishment or amazement
  • degree higher: trauma - a deeply distressing or disturbing experience
