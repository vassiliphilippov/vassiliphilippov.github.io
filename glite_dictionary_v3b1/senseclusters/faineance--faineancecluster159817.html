faineance

noun
idleness; reluctance to work
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààfe…™n.i.…ôns / (AmE), / Ààfe…™n.i.…ëÀêns / (BrE)
the quality of being idle or unwilling to work; habitual laziness or indolence.

Examples

Total faineance
His total faineance made progress on the project nearly impossible.
She was criticized for her faineance during a time that required decisive action.
The culture of faineance threatened to undermine the company's formerly strong work ethic.

Synonyms

  • indolence
    8.1 0.46/m
    indolence describes a steady avoidance of work used in formal contexts, while faineance denotes the same habitual unwillingness to work.
  • laziness
    1.4 2.50/m
    laziness means a common, everyday lack of energy or effort, while faineance labels a habitual unwillingness to work.
  • sloth
    4.6 0.90/m
    sloth suggests a deep, often moral laziness or slow lack of effort, while faineance means habitual unwillingness to work.
  • slothfulness
    8.1 0.04/m
    slothfulness names the state of being lazy or inactive, while faineance stresses a habitual unwillingness to work.
  • idleness
    4.5 1.01/m
    idleness is the state of being inactive or having nothing to do, while faineance implies unwillingness to work.
