retreatism

noun
withdrawal from society
Pronunciation: / rɪˈtriːtɪzəm / (AmE), / rɪˈtriːtɪzəm / (BrE)
the rejection of both societal goals and the means to achieve them, often by withdrawing from social life

Examples

Sociological retreatism
The sociologist explained retreatism as a way some people respond to overwhelming social expectations.
After years of pressure, he adopted retreatism and withdrew from most social activities.
Some subcultures are formed around shared retreatism and rejection of mainstream values.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alienation - a feeling of being isolated or estranged from society
  • antonym: conformity - behavior in accordance with accepted social standards
