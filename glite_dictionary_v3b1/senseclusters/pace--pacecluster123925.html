pace

verb
walk back and forth, often thoughtfully or anxiously
4.0 2.5
Pronunciation: /peɪs/ (AmE), /peɪs/ (BrE)
to walk steadily or repeatedly with measured steps, especially back and forth, usually as a sign of anxiety or deep thought

Examples

To pace nervously
She began to pace nervously around the waiting room, glancing at her phone every few minutes.
The teacher watched as the student paced back and forth, clearly anxious about the exam.
While thinking about his next move, he would pace the length of his office.

Related Terms

  • synonym: march - walk with regular, measured steps, usually in a group
  • synonym: stroll - walk in a slow, relaxed way
