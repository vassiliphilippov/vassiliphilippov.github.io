Tarsius

noun
genus of small Southeast Asian primates
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑr.si.əs / (AmE), / ˈtɑː.si.əs / (BrE)
A genus of small, nocturnal primates known as tarsiers, native to Southeast Asia.

Examples

The Tarsius genus
Members of Tarsius are known for their ability to leap great distances between trees.
Scientists have discovered new species within the Tarsius genus on various Indonesian islands.
The Tarsius genus is recognized for its unusually large eyes adapted for night vision.
