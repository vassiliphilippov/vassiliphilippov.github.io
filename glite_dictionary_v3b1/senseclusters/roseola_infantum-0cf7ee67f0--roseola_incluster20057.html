roseola infantum

noun
viral illness in infants with fever and rash
Pronunciation: / roʊˌziːoʊlə ɪnˈfæntəm / (AmE), / rəʊˌziːələ ɪnˈfæntəm / (BrE)
A viral illness affecting infants and young children, marked by sudden high fever followed by a faint pinkish rash.

Examples

A roseola infantum rash
After several days of high temperature, a pale rash signaled the end of her roseola infantum infection.
Babies with roseola infantum often recover without complications within a week.
The pediatrician diagnosed the sudden fever and rash as roseola infantum.
