gribble

verb
to nibble persistently
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈgrɪ.bəl/ (AmE), /ˈgrɪ.bəl/ (BrE)
to chew or gnaw something repeatedly or persistently, often in a small or nibbling way

Examples

To gribble a shoe
The puppy began to gribble the old slipper until it fell apart.
She watched the mouse gribble on a piece of cheese in the corner.
Children sometimes gribble their pencils when they are nervous or bored.

Related Terms

  • antonym: devour - to eat something quickly and eagerly
  • synonym: munch - to chew steadily or vigorously
  • synonym: nibble - to take small bites repeatedly
