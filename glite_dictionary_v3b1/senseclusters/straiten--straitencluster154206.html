straiten

verb
to put into difficulty or hardship
8.8 7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈstreɪ.tən/ (AmE), /ˈstreɪ.tən/ (BrE)
to put someone or something into a difficult or distressed situation, especially one involving a lack of money

Examples

To straiten a family
A weak harvest can straiten even the most diligent of farmers.
Heavy debts have straitened the company, forcing it to cut costs drastically.
The sudden loss of income will straiten many families in the community.

Related Terms

  • synonym: impoverish - to make someone poor
  • antonym: relieve - to make a difficult situation less severe
