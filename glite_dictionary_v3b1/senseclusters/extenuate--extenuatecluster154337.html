extenuate

verb
make something seem less serious
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstɛnjuˌeɪt / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɛnjueɪt / (BrE)
to make (a fault or offense) seem less serious or more forgivable

Examples

To extenuate a fault
He tried to extenuate his actions by explaining the difficult circumstances.
Nothing can extenuate the seriousness of the offense he committed.
The lawyer sought to extenuate the client's guilt by presenting evidence of hardship.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aggravate - to make a situation or problem worse
  • synonym: mitigate - to make less severe or intense
