sporophyte

noun
spore-producing stage in plant life cycle
Pronunciation: / ˈspɔrəˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˈspɔːrəʊˌfaɪt / (BrE)
The spore-producing phase in the life cycle of a plant that undergoes alternation of generations, typically producing spores through meiosis.

Examples

The sporophyte phase
In ferns, the sporophyte is the dominant, leafy plant that releases spores for reproduction.
Mosses have a distinct sporophyte that grows out of the gametophyte and produces spores.
The lifecycle alternates between sporophyte and gametophyte generations in many plants.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gametophyte - the gamete-producing phase in the plant life cycle
