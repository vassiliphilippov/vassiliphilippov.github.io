implication

noun
logical relationship: if p then q

Pronunciation: /ɪm.plə.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ɪm.plɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɪm.plɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ɪm.plə.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
(Logic) A relationship between two propositions in which if the first (p) is true, the second (q) must also be true; expressed as 'if p, then q.'

Examples

Logical implication
In formal proofs, implication is often denoted by an arrow symbol.
Mathematicians carefully distinguish between implication and other logical relations, such as equivalence.
