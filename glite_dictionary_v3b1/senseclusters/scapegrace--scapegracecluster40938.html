scapegrace

noun
reckless, unprincipled person
Pronunciation: / ˈskeɪpˌɡreɪs / (AmE), / ˈskeɪpɡreɪs / (BrE)
a person who is mischievous, reckless, and habitually behaves in an irresponsible or unprincipled way

Examples

A scapegrace youth
As a young man, Henry was considered the scapegrace of the family, always in trouble and ignoring advice.
The old novel tells the tale of a charming scapegrace who eventually redeems himself through courage and love.
Though he was called a scapegrace in his youth, he grew to be a respected member of society.
