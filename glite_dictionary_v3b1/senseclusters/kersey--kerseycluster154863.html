kersey

noun
coarse woolen cloth
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɜrzi / (AmE), / ˈkɜːzi / (BrE)
a coarse, durable woolen cloth traditionally used for work clothes or uniforms

Examples

A kersey cloth
The tailor recommended kersey for making a durable pair of work trousers.
In the past, soldiers' uniforms were often made from thick kersey to withstand harsh conditions.
She found an old blanket woven from kersey in her grandmother's attic.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tweed - a rough, woolen fabric often used for jackets and suits
