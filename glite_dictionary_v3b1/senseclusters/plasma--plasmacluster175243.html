plasma

noun
ionized gas; the fourth state of matter
Pronunciation: / ˈplæz.mə / (AmE), / ˈplɑːz.mə / (BrE)
A state of matter distinct from solid, liquid, or gas, consisting of an ionized gas with free electrons and ions, found in stars and fusion reactors.

Examples

Hot plasma in stars
In fusion reactors, hydrogen gas is heated until it becomes a plasma.
Scientists study plasma to learn more about fusion energy.
The sun, like other stars, is made almost entirely of plasma.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gas - state of matter with no fixed shape or volume
  • antonym: liquid - state of matter with fixed volume but no fixed shape
  • antonym: solid - state of matter with fixed shape and volume
