articulatio radiocarpea

noun
the wrist joint
Pronunciation: / ɑːrˌtɪkjʊˈleɪʃioʊ ˌreɪdioʊˈkɑːrpiə / (AmE), / ɑːˌtɪkjʊˈleɪsiəʊ ˌreɪdɪəʊˈkɑːpiə / (BrE)
the joint between the distal end of the radius and the proximal row of carpal bones, commonly known as the wrist joint

Examples

The articulatio radiocarpea joint
Anatomy students are required to identify the articulatio radiocarpea during practical examinations.
In cases of trauma, injury to the articulatio radiocarpea may require surgical intervention.
The articulatio radiocarpea allows for a wide range of wrist movements essential for hand function.
