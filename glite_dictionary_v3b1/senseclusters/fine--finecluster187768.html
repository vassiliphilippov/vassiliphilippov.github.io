fine

adjective
smooth or made of very small particles
1.0
Pronunciation: / faɪn / (AmE), / faɪn / (BrE)
smooth, thin, or made of very small or closely packed parts

Examples

Fine sand
A layer of fine dust settled over the old books in the attic.
After sanding, the wood's surface felt incredibly fine and smooth.
The baker prefers to use fine flour for a lighter texture in cakes.
He used a fine brush to paint the delicate details.
Make sure you read the fine print before signing any contract.
The sand on this beach is extremely fine and soft to the touch.
