commemorate

verb
honor or remember with a ceremony
Pronunciation: / kəˈmɛməˌreɪt /; / kəˈmɛm.əˌreɪt / (AmE), / kəˈmɛm.ə.reɪt /; / kəˈmɛm.əˌreɪt / (BrE)
to honor or remember a person or event, especially with a ceremony, memorial, or special activity

Examples

To commemorate a person
A plaque was installed to commemorate those who lost their lives in the disaster.
Each year, the city holds a parade to commemorate its founding.
The school organized an event to commemorate the achievements of its former students.
A special ceremony will commemorate the anniversary of her remarkable achievement.
Every year, we commemorate the founding of the city with a grand parade.
The plaque was placed there to commemorate those who lost their lives in the war.
A statue was erected to commemorate those who lost their lives in the conflict.
Each year, the community gathers to commemorate the founding of the town with a special ceremony.
We lit candles to commemorate the anniversary of the historic event.

Related Terms

  • synonym: celebrate - to mark a special occasion or event, usually with festivities
  • antonym: forget - to fail to remember or honor
  • antonym: ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to something or someone
  • synonym: memorialize - to preserve the memory of; to commemorate
