follow-up

noun
work continuing previous work
1.0 2.6
Pronunciation: /ˈfɑː.loʊ.ʌp/ (AmE), /ˈfɒl.əʊ.ʌp/ (BrE)
a work or activity (such as a book, project, or effort) that continues or builds on something done earlier

Examples

A follow-up book
Her latest novel is a follow-up to her bestseller.
The study was a follow-up to the research done last year.
We are planning a follow-up to our previous investigation.
Her bestselling novel has a highly anticipated follow-up coming out next year.
The new guidelines are a follow-up to the previous safety recommendations.
The researchers conducted a follow-up study to verify their initial findings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sequel - a published, broadcast, or recorded work that continues the story or develops the theme of an earlier one
