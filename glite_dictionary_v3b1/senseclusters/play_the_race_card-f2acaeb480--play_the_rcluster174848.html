play the race card

verb
invoke racial issues for advantage
Pronunciation: / pleɪ ðə reɪs kɑrd / (AmE), / pleɪ ðə reɪs kɑːd / (BrE)
to bring up racial issues or accusations of racism in order to gain an advantage, often to influence opinion or outcome

Examples

To play the race card in politics
He denied that he was attempting to play the race card in the dispute.
She was criticized for trying to play the race card during the heated debate.
Some politicians have been accused of playing the race card to rally support from certain groups.
