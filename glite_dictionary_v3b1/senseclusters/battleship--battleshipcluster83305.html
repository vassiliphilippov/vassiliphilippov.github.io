battleship

noun
large, heavily armored warship
Pronunciation: / ˈbæt̬.əlˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈbæt.əl.ʃɪp / (BrE)
a large, heavily armored warship equipped with powerful guns, used especially in the late 19th and early 20th centuries

Examples

A battleship at war
During the Second World War, the battleship played a crucial role in many naval battles.
The navy commissioned a new battleship equipped with state-of-the-art weaponry.
Tourists visited the decommissioned battleship now serving as a floating museum.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: cruiser - a fast warship less heavily armed and armored than a battleship
  • degree higher: dreadnought - a type of battleship with heavy armor and large-caliber guns, first launched in 1906
