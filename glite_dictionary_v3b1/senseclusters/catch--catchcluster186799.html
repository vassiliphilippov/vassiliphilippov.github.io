catch

noun
break or hesitation in the voice
3.7
Pronunciation: / kætʃ / (AmE), / katʃ / (BrE)
a slight break or hesitation in a person's voice, often caused by emotion or nervousness

Examples

A catch in her voice
A sudden catch betrayed her true feelings despite her calm appearance.
Her voice had a catch in it as she tried not to cry.
There was a noticeable catch in his voice when he spoke about his late friend.
