impound

verb
to take possession by legal authority
3.1 5.8
Pronunciation: /ɪm.ˈpaʊnd/ (AmE), /ɪm.ˈpaʊnd/ (BrE)
to take possession of or seize something, especially by legal authority, often for evidence, security, or safekeeping

Examples

To impound illegal goods
Customs officials may impound goods suspected of being smuggled.
The animal shelter has the power to impound stray dogs found in public areas.
The authorities decided to impound his car after he failed to provide proper registration.
During the investigation, authorities impounded several documents as evidence.
Farmers sometimes impound stray cattle to prevent damage to crops.
The police had to impound his car after the accident.

Related Terms

  • synonym: confine - to keep or restrict someone or something within certain limits
  • synonym: confiscate - to take away something, especially by authority
  • antonym: release - to set something free, to allow to leave impoundment
  • synonym: seize - to take hold of something suddenly and forcibly, often by legal authority
