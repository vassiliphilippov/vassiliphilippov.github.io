forget

verb
remove stored credentials or settings
4.1
Pronunciation: / fərˈɡet / (AmE), / fəˈɡet / (BrE)
(in computing) to remove or clear stored data, credentials, or configuration from a device or service so it is no longer remembered or used automatically.

Examples

Forget a Wi-Fi network
Tap the network name and choose forget so your phone will not reconnect automatically.
After changing routers, she had to forget the old Wi‑Fi network on each device.
Because of a bug, the laptop forgot saved credentials and prompted him to log in again.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clear - to remove saved information or settings
  • synonym: delete - to remove data so it is no longer available
  • antonym: remember - to keep something stored so it can be used or accessed automatically
