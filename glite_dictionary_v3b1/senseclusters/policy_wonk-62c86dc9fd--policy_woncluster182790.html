policy wonk

noun
an expert in policy analysis or politics
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːləsi wɑŋk / (AmE), / ˈpɒləsi wɒŋk / (BrE)
an expert or enthusiast who is highly knowledgeable about government policy or politics, often focusing on detailed analysis and technical aspects

Examples

Although some find politics boring, he's a proud policy wonk who loves dissecting every new reform.
During the debate, the policy wonk on the panel impressed everyone with his data-driven arguments.
The senator is considered a policy wonk because of her detailed understanding of complex legislation.
