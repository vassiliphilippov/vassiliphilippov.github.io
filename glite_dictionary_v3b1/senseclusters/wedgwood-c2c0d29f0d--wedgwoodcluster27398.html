Wedgwood

noun
type of fine pottery with white decoration
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛdʒ.wʊd / (AmE), / ˈwɛdʒ.wʊd / (BrE)
a type of fine pottery, typically with white classical decoration on a blue background, made by Josiah Wedgwood and his successors

Examples

Wedgwood pottery set
A set of Wedgwood plates was displayed in the museum's English ceramics collection.
Collectors often seek out rare pieces of Wedgwood to add to their antique pottery collections.
She cherished her grandmother's Wedgwood vase, which displayed delicate white figures against a pale blue background.
