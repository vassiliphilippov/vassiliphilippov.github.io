positive magnetic pole

noun
end of magnet pointing north
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑzətɪv məɡˈnætɪk poʊl / (AmE), / ˈpɒzətɪv mæɡˈnætɪk pəʊl / (BrE)
the end of a magnet that points toward the north when the magnet is freely suspended

Examples

The positive magnetic pole
Magnets always have both a positive magnetic pole and a negative magnetic pole.
The scientist marked the positive magnetic pole with red paint for clarity during the experiment.
When a compass needle is allowed to spin freely, its positive magnetic pole aligns with the Earth's north.

Related Terms
