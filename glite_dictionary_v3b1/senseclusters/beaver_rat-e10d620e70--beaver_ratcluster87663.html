beaver rat

noun
amphibious rodent of Australia and New Guinea
Pronunciation: / ˈbiːvər ræt / (AmE), / ˈbiːvə ræt / (BrE)
an aquatic rodent found in Australia and New Guinea, resembling a beaver in appearance

Examples

A beaver rat swimming
A beaver rat can often be seen swimming in rivers and lakes in New Guinea.
The scientist studied the habits of the beaver rat in its natural wetland habitat.
Unlike true beavers, the beaver rat has a more slender tail.
