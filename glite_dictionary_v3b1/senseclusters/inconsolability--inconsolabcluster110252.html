inconsolability

noun
unable to be comforted
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnkənˌsoʊləˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌɪnkənˌsəʊləˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the state of being unable to be comforted or made less sad

Examples

The inconsolability of family
The inconsolability of the family was clear after the tragic news.
Despite their efforts, her inconsolability lasted for weeks after the breakup.
He was overwhelmed by a sense of inconsolability when his dog passed away.

Related Terms

  • antonym: comfort - a state of physical or emotional ease
  • synonym: grief - deep sorrow, especially caused by someone's death
