modal

adjective
relating to a musical mode
6.2 6.9
Pronunciation: /ˈmoʊ.dəl/ (AmE), /ˈməʊ.dəl/ (BrE)
relating to a musical mode, especially as used in early church (ecclesiastical) music.

Examples

Modal music style
Many Gregorian chants are written in a modal style rather than following modern major or minor keys.
The composer experimented with modal harmony to create an ancient, mystical atmosphere.
This piece exemplifies the use of modal melodies in Renaissance choral music.
