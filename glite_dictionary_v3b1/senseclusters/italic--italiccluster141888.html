italic

noun
style of slanting typeface
Pronunciation: / ɪˈtælɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈtælɪk / (BrE)
A style of typeface in which the letters slant to the right, often used for emphasis or distinction.

Examples

Italic typeface
Please print the book titles in italic for the bibliography.
Scientific names are traditionally written in italic in academic articles.
The word was set in italic to emphasize its importance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oblique - style of typeface similar to italic but sloped without script features
  • antonym: roman - upright style of typeface, as opposed to italic
