A tropical climbing plant native to southern India and Sri Lanka, cultivated for its small, dark red berries (peppercorns) used as the source of black and white pepper.

Examples

Piper nigrum plant
Piper nigrum is the main source of both black and white pepper used in cooking worldwide.
Botanists study Piper nigrum to understand its medicinal properties and global trade significance.
The cultivation of Piper nigrum requires a warm, humid climate and careful harvesting of its berries.
