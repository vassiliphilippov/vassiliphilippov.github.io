progressive

adjective
changing places in a series (in games or dances)
Pronunciation: / prəˈɡrɛsɪv / (AmE), / prəˈɡrɛsɪv / (BrE)
(of a card game or a dance) involving a format in which participants move or change places or positions in a series of rounds or sections.

Examples

Progressive dance
A progressive square dance keeps everyone moving to a new partner with each sequence.
At the club, they organized a progressive euchre night, so players switched tables after each round.
The progressive dinner party allowed guests to mingle at a different host's house for each course.
