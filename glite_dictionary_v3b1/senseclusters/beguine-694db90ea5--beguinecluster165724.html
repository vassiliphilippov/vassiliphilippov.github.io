Beguine

noun
member of medieval lay religious sisterhood
8.2 9.9
Pronunciation: /bə.ˈgiːn/ /; / /beɪ.ˈgiːn/ (AmE), /bə.ˈgiːn/ /; / /beɪ.ˈgiːn/ (BrE)
A member of a lay religious sisterhood, especially one of several founded in the Netherlands in the 12th and 13th centuries, whose members lived communally under religious discipline but without taking formal vows.

Examples

A Beguine sisterhood
During the Middle Ages, the Beguine communities offered women an alternative to marriage or convent life.
She researched the lives of Beguines and their contributions to medieval society.
The Beguine movement emphasized both spiritual devotion and charitable work among the poor.
