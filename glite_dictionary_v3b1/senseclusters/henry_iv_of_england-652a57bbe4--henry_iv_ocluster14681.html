Henry IV of England

proper noun
King of England 1399–1413, first Lancastrian monarch
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛnri ðə ˈfɔrθ əv ˈɪŋɡlənd / (AmE), / ˈhɛnri ði ˈfɔːθ əv ˈɪŋɡlənd / (BrE)
the King of England from 1399 to 1413, the first monarch of the House of Lancaster, who deposed Richard II and dealt with several uprisings during his reign.

Examples

Henry IV of England king
Henry IV of England ascended the throne after deposing Richard II in 1399.

The reign of Henry IV of England was marked by political turmoil and rebellion.
