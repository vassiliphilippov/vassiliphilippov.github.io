sectile

adjective
easily cuttable
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛktaɪl / (AmE), / ˈsɛktaɪl / (BrE)
able to be cut smoothly with a knife, especially referring to minerals or metals

Examples

Sectile metal
Gold is a sectile metal because it can be sliced easily with a knife.
The mineral was identified as sectile after it was smoothly cut during testing.
Unlike quartz, which is brittle, copper is sectile and can be cut without shattering.

Related Terms

  • antonym: brittle - easily broken or cracked, not easily cut
  • synonym: malleable - able to be shaped or bent easily
