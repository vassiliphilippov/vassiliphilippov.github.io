purge

noun
removal of unwanted elements by cleansing
7.1 0.34/m
Pronunciation: / p…ùd í / (AmE), / p…úÀêd í / (BrE)
an act of removing unwanted substances, impurities, or people from a place, organization, or substance, especially by a process of cleansing or elimination

Examples

A company purge
After the scandal, there was a massive purge within the organization to restore public trust.
The new government ordered a purge of corrupt officials from the administration.
The water treatment process includes a purge to remove harmful bacteria and sediment.

Synonyms

  • elimination
    3.5 7.49/m
    elimination means getting rid of something or someone, while purge often suggests a forceful cleansing of people or impurities.
  • cleansing
    3.9 0.50/m
    cleansing is the process of removing dirt or impurities, while purge often means a stronger, broader removal that can target people.
  • cleaning
    1.0 16/m
    cleaning is the everyday act of removing dirt to make things tidy, while purge implies a more thorough or forced removal.
  • purification
    4.6 1.38/m
    purification is making a substance pure by removing chemical impurities, while purge can also mean removing people or cleansing organizations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cleanse - to clean thoroughly; to remove impurities
  • antonym: contaminate - to make something impure by adding harmful substances
