callous

adjective
having tough, thickened skin
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkæl.əs / (AmE), / ˈkæl.əs / (BrE)
(of skin) having become tough and thick, especially through repeated use or friction; having calluses.

Examples

Callous hands
She barely noticed the pain because her feet were already callous from years of dancing barefoot.
The gardener's callous fingers showed the signs of a lifetime spent with tools.
Years of working outdoors gave him callous hands that felt like leather.

Related Terms

  • synonym: calloused - having calluses; made hard and thick by use
