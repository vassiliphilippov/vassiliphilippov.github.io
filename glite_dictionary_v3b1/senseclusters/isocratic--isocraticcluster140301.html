isocratic

adjective
constant composition
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪsəˈkrætɪk / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəˈkrætɪk / (BrE)
having a constant composition or condition throughout a process, especially in scientific or technical contexts

Examples

Isocratic process
The chemist used an isocratic method to keep the solvent mixture unchanged during the experiment.
In chromatography, an isocratic system maintains the same mobile phase composition throughout the process.
The results were more reliable because the procedure was strictly isocratic from start to finish.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gradient - a gradual change in composition or concentration
