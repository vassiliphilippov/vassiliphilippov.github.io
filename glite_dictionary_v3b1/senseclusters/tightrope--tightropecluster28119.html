tightrope

noun
a rope or wire for acrobatic balancing acts
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪtˌroʊp / (AmE), / ˈtaɪtrəʊp / (BrE)
a tightly stretched rope or wire, often suspended high above the ground, on which acrobats or performers walk or balance

Examples

A tightrope walk
During the performance, she danced gracefully on the tightrope, amazing the audience.
Learning to master the tightrope requires intense concentration and balance.
The acrobat skillfully walked across the tightrope without a safety net beneath him.

Related Terms

  • antonym: slackrope - a loosely suspended rope or wire used for balancing acts
