Caesar's agaric

noun
edible mushroom Amanita caesarea
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːzɚz əˈgærɪk / (AmE), / ˈsiːzəz əˈɡærɪk / (BrE)
an edible mushroom (Amanita caesarea) widely distributed in southern Europe, resembling the fly agaric but with a distinctive orange cap and yellow gills and stem

Examples

Caesar's agaric mushroom
Many foragers value Caesar's agaric for its delicate flavor and culinary uses.
The bright orange cap of the Caesar's agaric makes it easy to identify in the wild.
Unlike its toxic relatives, the Caesar's agaric is a prized edible mushroom in Mediterranean cuisine.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fly agaric - toxic mushroom with red cap and white spots (Amanita muscaria)
