carvel

noun
shipbuilding method
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːrvəl / (AmE), / ˈkɑːvəl / (BrE)
a method of shipbuilding in which planks are laid edge to edge to form a smooth hull surface

Examples

Carvel construction method
The ancient ship was constructed using the carvel technique for a smoother hull.
Many traditional fishing boats in the region are built with the carvel method.
Shipbuilders preferred the carvel style for its strength and sleek appearance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clinker - a shipbuilding method where planks overlap each other
