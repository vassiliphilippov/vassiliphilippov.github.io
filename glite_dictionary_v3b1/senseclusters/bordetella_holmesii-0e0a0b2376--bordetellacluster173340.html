Bordetella holmesii

noun
bacterium causing sepsis
8.8
Pronunciation: / bɔːrdəˈtɛlə hoʊlˈmziːaɪ / (AmE), / bɔːdəˈtɛlə hɒlˈmziːaɪ / (BrE)
a species of bacterium that can cause bloodstream infections and sepsis, especially in people with weakened immune systems

Examples

Bordetella holmesii sepsis
After her transplant, the patient was diagnosed with a Bordetella holmesii infection.
Doctors identified Bordetella holmesii as the cause of the sudden onset of sepsis.
Immunocompromised individuals are at higher risk for Bordetella holmesii bacteremia.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Bordetella pertussis - a related bacterium that causes whooping cough
  • synonym: Escherichia coli - a common bacterium that can also cause bloodstream infections
