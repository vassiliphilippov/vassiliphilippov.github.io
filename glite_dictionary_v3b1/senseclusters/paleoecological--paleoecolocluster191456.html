paleoecological

adjective
ancient ecosystems
Pronunciation: / ˌpeɪlioʊˌikəˈlɑdʒɪkəl /; / ˌpæliˌoʊˌikəˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌpæliəʊˌiːkəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the study of ancient ecosystems and their environments

Examples

Paleoecological evidence
Researchers used paleoecological evidence to reconstruct the vegetation of the Ice Age.
A paleoecological approach helps scientists understand how ancient species interacted with their environment.
The paleoecological record shows dramatic shifts in climate over millions of years.
The palaeoecological evidence suggests that the region was once covered by dense forests.
Researchers used palaeoecological data to reconstruct the climate of the last ice age.
A palaeoecological approach helps scientists understand how ancient species interacted with their environment.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: ecological - relating to the relationships between living things and their environment
  • synonym: paleoecology - the study of ancient ecosystems and their interactions
  • synonym: paleontological - relating to the study of ancient life forms through fossils
