subvocal

adjective
inaudible speech signals
Pronunciation: / sʌbˈvoʊkəl / (AmE), / sʌbˈvəʊkəl / (BrE)
relating to very slight movements or signals of the speech organs that do not produce audible sound, often detected by technology.

Examples

Subvocal signals
The new device can detect subvocal commands by monitoring tiny throat movements.
Researchers studied subvocal activity to understand how people read silently.
Some advanced computers use subvocal signals to allow hands-free typing.

Related Terms

  • antonym: audible - able to be heard
  • synonym: silent - not making any sound
