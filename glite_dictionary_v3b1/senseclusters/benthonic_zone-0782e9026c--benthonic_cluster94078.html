benthonic zone

noun
seabed and shore region of an ocean
9.9
Pronunciation: / bɛnˈθɑnɪk zoʊn / (AmE), / bɛnˈθɒnɪk zəʊn / (BrE)
The region of an ocean that includes the seabed and the areas near the shore (littoral zones).

Examples

The benthonic zone area
Many species in the benthonic zone have adapted to life without sunlight.
Pollutants on the ocean floor can seriously impact the delicate ecosystem of the benthonic zone.
Researchers collected sediment samples from the benthonic zone to study microplastics in marine environments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: benthic zone - the ecological region at the lowest level of a body of water such as an ocean or a lake
