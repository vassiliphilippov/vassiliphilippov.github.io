loosey

adjective
not strict
9.1 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàluÀêsi / (AmE), / ÀàluÀêsi / (BrE)
informal; not strict, precise, or tightly controlled; lax in discipline or enforcement.

Examples

Loosey rules
The manager's loosey approach to deadlines made the team less productive.
Parents worried that the school's loosey rules would lead to chaos.
His loosey attitude toward safety regulations concerned his coworkers.

Synonyms

  • loose
    3.0 8.47/m
    loose is informal and means not tightly controlled or exact in general situations, while loosey implies lax discipline or enforcement.
  • lax
    4.6 2.00/m
    lax is used about poor care or weak rules, while loosey is more informal and focused on lax discipline or enforcement.
  • lenient
    4.4 1.58/m
    lenient describes a person who shows mercy or gentleness with rules, while loosey suggests informal, lax enforcement rather than mercy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lax - not strict or severe; careless
  • antonym: strict - demanding that rules are closely followed
