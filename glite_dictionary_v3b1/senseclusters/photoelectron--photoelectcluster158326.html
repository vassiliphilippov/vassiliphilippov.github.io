photoelectron

noun
electron emitted by photon absorption
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊtoʊɪˈlɛktrɑn / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊɪˈlɛktrɒn / (BrE)
An electron that is emitted from an atom or molecule as a result of absorbing a photon.

Examples

A photoelectron emitted
A single photon can release a photoelectron from the metal surface.
Measuring the energy of a photoelectron helps scientists study atomic structure.
The photoelectron was detected using a sophisticated sensor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: electron - a subatomic particle with a negative electric charge
