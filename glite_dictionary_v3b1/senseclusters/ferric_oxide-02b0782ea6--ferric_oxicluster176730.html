ferric oxide

noun
red oxide of iron; rust
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛrɪk ˈɑksaɪd / (AmE), / ˈfɛrɪk ˈɒksaɪd / (BrE)
A red oxide of iron, commonly found as rust, used in pigments and polishing agents.

Examples

Red ferric oxide
Artists have used ferric oxide as a pigment since ancient times.
The formation of ferric oxide on iron surfaces is commonly known as rusting.
The reddish color of the soil is due to the presence of ferric oxide.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: ferrous oxide - a black oxide of iron
  • synonym: iron oxide - any compound of iron and oxygen
