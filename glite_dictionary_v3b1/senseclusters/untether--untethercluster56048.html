untether

verb
to free from limits
6.2
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈtɛðər / (AmE), / ʌnˈtɛðə / (BrE)
to free someone or something from restrictions, limits, or attachments

Examples

To untether workers
The new policy will untether employees from strict office hours, allowing more flexibility.
She decided to untether herself from old habits and start fresh in a new city.
Innovative leaders often untether their teams from traditional thinking to inspire creativity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: release - to set free from confinement or restraint
  • antonym: restrict - to limit the movement or freedom of someone or something
  • synonym: unbind - to remove bindings or restraints
