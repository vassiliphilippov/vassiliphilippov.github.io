humble

adjective
of low social rank or birth
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌmbəl / (AmE), / ˈhʌmbəl / (BrE)
of low social rank, position, or status; not grand or impressive; modest or lowly (such as origins, jobs, or things)

Examples

Humble beginnings
Despite her humble origins, she became a renowned scientist.
He came from a humble background but achieved great success.
The king chose a humble servant to receive the honor.
Despite their humble origins, the company rose to international success.
He began his career with humble beginnings, working in a small workshop.
The family lived in a humble cottage at the edge of the village.
Despite his humble job as a dishwasher, he always took pride in his work.
Many people with humble beginnings have achieved great success.
She worked in a humble position as a housekeeper when she first arrived in the city.

Related Terms

  • synonym: baseborn - of low or unprivileged birth
  • antonym: grand - impressive in size or appearance, opposite of humble
  • synonym: lowly - of low rank; humble in status or position
  • synonym: menial - (of work) not requiring much skill and lacking prestige
  • synonym: modest - unassuming in quality or importance
  • antonym: noble - of high social or moral rank
