arachnoid membrane

noun
middle membrane covering brain and spinal cord
Pronunciation: / əˈræk.nɔɪd ˈmɛm.breɪn / (AmE), / əˈræk.nɔɪd ˈmɛm.breɪn / (BrE)
the thin, web-like middle layer of the three membranes (meninges) covering the brain and spinal cord

Examples

The arachnoid membrane layer
During the surgery, the doctor carefully avoided damaging the arachnoid membrane.
Inflammation of the arachnoid membrane, known as arachnoiditis, can cause severe neurological symptoms.
The arachnoid membrane separates the dura mater from the pia mater in the central nervous system.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: dura mater - the tough outermost membrane covering the brain and spinal cord
  • degree lower: pia mater - the delicate innermost membrane covering the brain and spinal cord
