tender

adjective
very young or immature
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛndɚ / (AmE), / ˈtɛndə / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) very young and inexperienced; not fully mature.

Examples

Tender age
At such a tender age, children can be easily influenced by their surroundings.
He showed musical talent from a tender age.
The calf was separated from its mother at a very tender age.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mature - fully grown or developed
