disemployment

noun
loss of employment
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsɪmˈplɔɪmənt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪmˈplɔɪmənt / (BrE)
the state of being removed from employment or losing one's job

Examples

High disemployment rate
Rapid technological changes have caused significant disemployment in the manufacturing sector.
Government policies aim to reduce disemployment during economic downturns.
The rise of automation may increase the risk of disemployment for some workers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: employment - the state of having a paid job
  • synonym: unemployment - the state of not having a job
