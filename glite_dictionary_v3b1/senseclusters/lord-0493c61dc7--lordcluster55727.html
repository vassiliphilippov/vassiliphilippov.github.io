Lord

noun
nobleman with the title 'Lord'
4.8
Pronunciation: / lɔrd / (AmE), / lɔːd / (BrE)
a man who has the title of a peer in certain systems of nobility, especially in the United Kingdom

Examples

British Lord
He was made a Lord in recognition of his service to the country.
In the House of Lords, each Lord has a seat by right of their title.
The Lord presided over the estate with grace and responsibility for many years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: baron - a lower rank of nobility, often a synonym for Lord
  • antonym: lady - the female equivalent of a lord in nobility
