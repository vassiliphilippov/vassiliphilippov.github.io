Lord

noun
nobleman with the title 'Lord'
4.8 7.51/m
Pronunciation: / l…îrd / (AmE), / l…îÀêd / (BrE)
a man who has the title of a peer in certain systems of nobility, especially in the United Kingdom

Examples

British Lord
He was made a Lord in recognition of his service to the country.
In the House of Lords, each Lord has a seat by right of their title.
The Lord presided over the estate with grace and responsibility for many years.

Synonyms

  • peer
    8.6 4.83/m
    peer is a noble title holder in the British peerage, while Lord names a man who holds that peer title.
  • peer of the realm
    9.9 0.04/m
    peer of the realm is a formal member with a seat in the House of Lords, while Lord is a man with peerage title.
  • nobleman
    5.6 1.10/m
    nobleman is any man of noble birth or title, while Lord specifically means a man who holds a peerage title.

Related Terms

  • synonym: baron - a lower rank of nobility, often a synonym for Lord
  • antonym: lady - the female equivalent of a lord in nobility
