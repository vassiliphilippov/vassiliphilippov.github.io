permeable

adjective
allowing fluids or gases to pass through
5.8 0.94/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…ùÀê.mi.…ô.b…ôl / (AmE), / Ààp…úÀê.mi.…ô.b…ôl / (BrE)
allowing liquids or gases to pass through it; capable of being penetrated by fluids or gases.

Examples

Permeable membrane
A permeable membrane allows certain substances to pass through while blocking others.
Rainwater can easily soak through the permeable pavement, reducing surface runoff.
The soil in this area is highly permeable, making it ideal for groundwater recharge.

Synonyms

  • pervious
    7.8 0.02/m
    pervious allows water, light, and air to pass through small openings, while permeable is a broader word for letting fluids through.
  • porous
    4.6 2.19/m
    porous has many tiny holes that hold and let fluids pass, while permeable simply means able to let liquids or gases through.

Related Terms

  • antonym: impermeable - not allowing fluids or gases to pass through
