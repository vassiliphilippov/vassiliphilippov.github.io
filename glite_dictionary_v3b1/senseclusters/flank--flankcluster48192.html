flank

verb
be at the side of something
Pronunciation: / flæŋk / (AmE), / flæŋk / (BrE)
to be at or move to the side of something or someone (sometimes to gain an advantage)

Examples

Statues flank the door
A pair of statues flanks the entrance to the old library.
Tall pines flank the driveway leading to the mansion.
Two guards flanked the politician as he walked toward the stage.
The soldiers attempted to flank the enemy by moving quietly through the forest.
During the parade, two guards flanked the president as he walked down the street.
The hikers decided to flank the mountain to avoid the steepest path.

Related Terms

  • synonym: border - to form the outer edge of something
  • antonym: confront - to face someone or something directly
  • synonym: outflank - to move around the side of an enemy to gain an advantage
  • synonym: surround - to encircle or be all around something
