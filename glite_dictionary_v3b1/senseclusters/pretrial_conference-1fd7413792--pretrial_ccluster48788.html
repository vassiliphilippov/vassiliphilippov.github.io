pretrial conference

noun
meeting before a trial to organize legal proceedings
9.6 5.9
Pronunciation: /priː.ˈtraɪ.əl ˈkɑːn.fər.əns/ /; / /prɪ.ˈtraɪ.əl ˈkɑːn.frəns/ (AmE), /prɪ.ˈtraɪ.əl ˈkɒn.fə.rəns/ /; / /prɪ.ˈtraɪ.əl ˈkɒn.frəns/ (BrE)
a meeting held before a trial to discuss procedural matters, clarify the issues, and plan the conduct of the case, especially in civil law.

Examples

A pretrial conference meeting
Attending a pretrial conference can often expedite the resolution of civil disputes.
During the pretrial conference, the lawyers agreed on several key aspects of the discovery process.
The judge scheduled a pretrial conference to outline the proceedings for both parties.
