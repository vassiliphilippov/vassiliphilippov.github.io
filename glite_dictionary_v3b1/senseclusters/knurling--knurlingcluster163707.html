knurling

noun
grip-enhancing pattern
Pronunciation: / ˈnɜrlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnɜːlɪŋ / (BrE)
a pattern of small ridges or bumps on a surface, usually added to improve grip

Examples

Knurling on a handle
The knurling on the metal tool prevents it from slipping out of your hand.
Bicycle handlebars often feature knurling to provide a better grip for riders.
He admired the detailed knurling on the antique doorknob.

Related Terms

  • antonym: smoothness - the quality of having an even, flat surface
  • synonym: texture - the feel or appearance of a surface
