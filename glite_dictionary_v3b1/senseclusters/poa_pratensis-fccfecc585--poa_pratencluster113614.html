Poa pratensis

noun
species of grass; Kentucky bluegrass
Pronunciation: / poʊ.ə præˈtɛn.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈpəʊ.ə præˈtɛn.sɪs / (BrE)
A species of grass native to Europe and Asia, widely cultivated in North America as a valuable meadow and pasture grass, known for its tall stalks and slender bright green leaves; also called Kentucky bluegrass.

Examples

Poa pratensis grass
Farmers prefer Poa pratensis for pastures due to its resilience and nutritional value.
Golf courses often use Poa pratensis because it produces a dense, attractive turf.
The field was covered with lush Poa pratensis after the spring rains.

Related Terms
