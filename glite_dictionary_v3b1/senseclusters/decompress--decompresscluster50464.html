decompress

verb
reduce or release pressure
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːkəmˈprɛs / (AmE), / ˌdiːkəmˈprɛs / (BrE)
to reduce or release the pressure in something, especially air or gas pressure.

Examples

To decompress a chamber
Engineers needed to decompress the chamber before entering it safely.
Scuba divers must decompress gradually to avoid getting the bends.
The pilot warned the passengers that he had to decompress the cabin due to a technical issue.

Related Terms

  • antonym: pressurize - to increase the pressure of something
