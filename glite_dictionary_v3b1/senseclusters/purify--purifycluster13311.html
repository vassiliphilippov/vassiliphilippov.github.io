purify

verb
make morally or spiritually pure
3.0 4.1
Pronunciation: /ˈpjʊr.ə.faɪ/ /; / /ˈpjʊr.ɪ.faɪ/ /; / /ˈpjɜːr.ɪ.faɪ/ (AmE), /ˈpjʊə.rɪ.faɪ/ /; / /ˈpjɔː.rɪ.faɪ/ (BrE)
to make someone or something morally or spiritually pure; to free from sin, guilt, or negative qualities

Examples

To purify the soul
Many believe that acts of charity can purify the heart.
She prayed that the ritual would purify her soul.
The confession was intended to purify him of his guilt.
Some people believe that meditation can purify the soul of negative thoughts.
The monks fasted to purify their minds before the ceremony.
The river water began to purify after months of environmental efforts.
