Actinopoda

noun
group of protozoan organisms
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌæktɪˈnɑːpədə /; / ˌæktɪˈnɑpədə / (AmE), / ˌæktɪˈnɒpədə / (BrE)
a group of single-celled aquatic organisms (protozoans), including heliozoans and radiolarians, that have slender, ray-like extensions for movement and feeding

Examples

Actinopoda aquatic organisms
Actinopoda play an important role in the ocean's food web by feeding on smaller plankton.
Many marine biologists study Actinopoda to understand microscopic aquatic ecosystems.
Radiolarians, a type of Actinopoda, have intricate mineral skeletons visible under a microscope.
