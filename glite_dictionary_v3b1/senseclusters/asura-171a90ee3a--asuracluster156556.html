Asura

noun
demon or deity in Hindu mythology
9.9
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈsuː.rə/ /; / /ˈɑː.sʊ.rə/ (AmE), /ə.ˈsuː.rə/ /; / /ˈɑː.sʊ.rə/ (BrE)
In Hindu mythology, a member of a class of powerful beings who were originally considered gods but later became regarded as demons and adversaries of the gods.

Examples

The Asura demon
Ancient texts often describe the Asura as rivals to the gods.
In later Hindu mythology, an Asura is typically depicted as a demon.
The battle between Devas and Asuras is a central theme in many epics.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deva - a deity or god in Hinduism, often an opponent of the asura
