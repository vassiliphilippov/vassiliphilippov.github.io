ray

verb
to decorate with lines
8.2
Pronunciation: / reɪ / (AmE), / reɪ / (BrE)
to mark, decorate, or cover something with lines or beams that radiate outward from a center.

Examples

To ray a shield
The artist rayed the shield with golden lines to suggest morning sun.
They rayed the cake with chocolate stripes for a festive look.
The designer rays the fabric with bright colors to create a sunburst effect.

Related Terms

  • antonym: plain - not decorated or marked with lines or patterns
  • synonym: stripe - to mark with long, narrow bands of color or texture
