actinic radiation

noun
radiation that causes photochemical reactions
Pronunciation: / ækˈtɪnɪk reɪdiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ækˈtɪnɪk reɪdiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
electromagnetic radiation capable of causing photochemical reactions, such as ultraviolet or visible light.

Examples

Some types of actinic radiation are used in sterilization because they destroy microorganisms.
The laboratory uses filters to block harmful actinic radiation during certain chemical experiments.
