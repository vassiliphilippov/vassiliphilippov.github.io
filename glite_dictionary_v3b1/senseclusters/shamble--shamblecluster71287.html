shamble

noun
a slow, dragging walk
Pronunciation: / ˈʃæmbəl / (AmE), / ˈʃæmbəl / (BrE)
a slow, awkward way of walking in which you drag your feet along the ground without lifting them fully

Examples

A tired shamble
After hours of hiking, his shamble revealed his exhaustion.
His characteristic shamble was easy to spot from a distance.
The old man's shamble across the street drew concerned looks from passersby.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shuffle - to walk by dragging one's feet along the ground
