contrast medium

noun
substance for medical imaging
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑnˌtræst ˈmiːdiəm / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.trɑːst ˈmiːdiəm / (BrE)
a substance used in medical imaging that is opaque to x-rays, allowing the visualization of organs or tissues when administered into the body

Examples

A contrast medium injection
A contrast medium can improve the detail of X-ray images in a CT scan.
Some patients may be allergic to a contrast medium used in imaging procedures.
The radiologist injected a contrast medium to help visualize the patient's blood vessels.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contrast agent - another term for contrast medium, used in medical imaging
