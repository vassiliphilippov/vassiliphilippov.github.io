parasitism

noun
biological relationship where one organism harms another for benefit
2.9 6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈpær.ə.sɪ.tɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈpær.ə.sɪ.tɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
A relationship between two different kinds of organisms in which one benefits at the expense of the other, often causing harm but rarely death.

Examples

Parasitism in nature
In nature, parasitism can be observed between ticks and mammals, with ticks feeding on their hosts' blood.
Many diseases in humans are caused by parasitism, where microscopic organisms exploit the body for their own gain.
The balance between parasitism and the host's defenses affects how much harm is done to the host.

Related Terms

  • antonym: commensalism - a relationship where one organism benefits and the other is neither helped nor harmed
  • antonym: mutualism - a relationship where both organisms benefit
