osteopenia

noun
mild bone loss
Pronunciation: / ˌɑstioʊˈpiniə / (AmE), / ˌɒstiəʊˈpiːniə / (BrE)
a medical condition in which bone mineral density is lower than normal but not low enough to be classified as osteoporosis

Examples

Bone osteopenia
The doctor explained that osteopenia is an early sign of bone loss that should be monitored.
Regular exercise and calcium intake can help slow the progression of osteopenia.
Her bone scan revealed osteopenia, so she started a new diet and exercise plan.
The doctor diagnosed her with osteopenia after reviewing her bone density scan.
Regular exercise and a healthy diet can help prevent osteopenia from progressing to osteoporosis.
Men and women over fifty are often screened for osteopenia during routine checkups.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: healthy bone density - normal levels of bone mineral density
  • degree higher: osteoporosis - a condition of severe bone loss and fragility
