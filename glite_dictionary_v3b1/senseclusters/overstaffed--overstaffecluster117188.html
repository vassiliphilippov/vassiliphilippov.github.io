overstaffed

adjective
too many employees
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈstæft / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈstɑːft / (BrE)
having more employees than are necessary for the amount of work to be done

Examples

An overstaffed office
The office was overstaffed, so some employees had very little to do.
During the holiday season, the store became overstaffed and shifts were shortened.
Management realized the factory was overstaffed after reviewing the production numbers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overmanned - having more workers than necessary
  • antonym: understaffed - having fewer employees than are needed
