right brain

noun
right hemisphere of the brain
Pronunciation: / ˈraɪt ˌbreɪn / (AmE), / ˈraɪt ˌbreɪn / (BrE)
the right cerebral hemisphere of the brain, which controls the movements and sensations of the left side of the body and is associated with creativity and spatial ability

Examples

The right brain hemisphere
Injury to the right brain can affect spatial awareness and the ability to recognize faces.
Some theories suggest that the right brain is more involved in creative tasks like art and music.
The left hand is typically controlled by the right brain hemisphere.

Related Terms

  • antonym: left brain - the left cerebral hemisphere of the brain, controlling the right side of the body and linked to logical and analytical tasks
