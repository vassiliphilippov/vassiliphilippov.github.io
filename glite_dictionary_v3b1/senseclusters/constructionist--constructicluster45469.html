constructionist

noun
theory supporter
8.5
Pronunciation: / kənˈstrʌkʃənɪst / (AmE), / kənˈstrʌkʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports or practices a particular theory of interpretation, especially in law or philosophy

Examples

Constructionist view in law
The constructionist approach to law emphasizes the original meaning of the text.
As a constructionist, she believed in interpreting the constitution strictly.
Many philosophers debate with constructionists about the best way to understand legal documents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interpreter - a person who explains or gives meaning to something
  • synonym: literalist - a person who interprets words or texts in their most basic sense
