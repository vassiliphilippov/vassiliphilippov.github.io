radical chic

noun
fashionable display of radical left-wing views
Pronunciation: / ˌrædɪkəl ˈʃik / (AmE), / ˌrædɪkəl ˈʃiːk / (BrE)
The adoption of radical left-wing political views and associated fashionable dress or lifestyle, often as a trend among the wealthy or elite.

Examples

Radical chic lifestyle
Critics accused him of adopting radical chic without any real commitment to the cause.
In the 1970s, radical chic became popular among certain fashionable circles in New York.
The party was full of celebrities indulging in radical chic to appear socially conscious.
