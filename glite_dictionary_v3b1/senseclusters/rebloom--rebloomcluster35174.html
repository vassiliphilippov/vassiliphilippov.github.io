rebloom

verb
to bloom again
4.4
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈbluːm/ /; / /rə.ˈbluːm/ /; / /riː.ˈbluːm/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈbluːm/ /; / /rə.ˈbluːm/ /; / /riː.ˈbluːm/ (BrE)
to flower or bloom again after the first blooming period

Examples

To rebloom in autumn
Some varieties of irises can rebloom in the fall after their initial spring display.
Gardeners appreciate plants that rebloom because they provide color throughout the season.
If you prune the bushes carefully, they may rebloom before the first frost arrives.
Certain orchids can rebloom if they receive enough light and proper care.
Gardeners hope their lilies will rebloom later in the season after the first flowers fade.
With the right conditions, some plants may rebloom multiple times each year.
