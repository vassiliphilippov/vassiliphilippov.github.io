genus Canis

noun
genus of dogs and related animals
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈkeɪ.nɪs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs ˈka.nɪs / (BrE)
The genus of mammals in the family Canidae that includes domestic dogs, wolves, jackals, and closely related animals.

Examples

Genus Canis animals
Domestic dogs were classified in the genus Canis by early taxonomists.
Scientists have discovered several extinct species within the genus Canis.
The gray wolf is a member of the genus Canis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: canid - any member of the biological family Canidae, including dogs, wolves, and foxes
