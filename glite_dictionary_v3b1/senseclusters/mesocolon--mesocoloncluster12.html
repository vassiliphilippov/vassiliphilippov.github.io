mesocolon

noun
a membrane attaching colon to the abdominal wall
8.0 9.1
Pronunciation: /me.soʊ.ˈkoʊ.lən/ /; / /miː.soʊ.ˈkoʊ.lən/ (AmE), /me.sə.ˈkəʊ.lən/ /; / /miː.sə.ˈkəʊ.lən/ (BrE)
a fold of the peritoneum that attaches the lower part of the colon to the posterior abdominal wall

Examples

The mesocolon fold
Anatomical diagrams often highlight the mesocolon to show its connection to the colon and abdominal wall.
Inflammation of the mesocolon can cause severe abdominal pain.
The surgeon carefully separated the mesocolon from the surrounding tissue during the operation.
