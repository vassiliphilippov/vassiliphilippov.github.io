float

noun
an object that floats on water
1.7 1.0
Pronunciation: / floʊt / (AmE), / fləʊt / (BrE)
an object that floats on the surface of water, often used for supporting things or indicating position in fishing or swimming activities

Examples

Fishing float
A small wooden float drifted slowly across the pond.
Children played in the pool with a bright pink float.
The fisherman attached a float to his line to help him see when he had a bite.

Related Terms

  • synonym: buoy - an anchored floating object as a navigation marker
  • antonym: sink - to go below the surface of water and not float
