noninterventionist

adjective
avoiding involvement abroad
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˌɪntərˈvɛnʃənɪst / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌɪntəˈvɛnʃənɪst / (BrE)
preferring not to become involved in the affairs or conflicts of other countries or people

Examples

Noninterventionist policy
The government maintained a noninterventionist stance during the international crisis.
Many citizens supported a noninterventionist approach to foreign policy.
Her noninterventionist views influenced the country's decision to stay out of the conflict.
The country's noninterventionist policy kept it out of international disputes.
Her noninterventionist attitude allowed her children to learn from their own mistakes.
Many leaders debated whether a noninterventionist approach was best for the situation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: interventionist - favoring involvement in the affairs of other countries
  • synonym: isolationist - favoring a policy of remaining apart from the affairs of other countries
  • synonym: neutral - not supporting or helping either side in a conflict
