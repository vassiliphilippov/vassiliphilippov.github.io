cringle

noun
metal eyelet for attaching lines
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈkrɪŋɡl / (BrE)
a metal ring or eyelet for reinforcing a hole in a sail or other fabric, used especially for attaching cords or lines

Examples

A cringle in sail
Make sure each cringle is firmly fastened to prevent tearing in strong winds.
The canvas cover had a reinforced cringle at each corner for attaching lines.
The sailor threaded the rope through the cringle to secure the sail.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grommet - a metal or plastic ring inserted in a hole to protect or reinforce the hole
