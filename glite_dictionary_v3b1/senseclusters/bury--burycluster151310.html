bury

verb
put in the ground and cover with soil
1.7 33/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆbÉ›ri / (AmE), / ËˆbÉ›ri / (BrE)
to put something in the ground and cover it with soil, especially as part of a funeral or to hide it

Examples

To bury a body
After the storm, we had to bury the broken fence posts in the soil.
Farmers sometimes bury seeds to help them sprout.
The pirates decided to bury their treasure on the remote island.
According to tradition, the pharaohs were buried with treasures for the afterlife.
The family gathered at the cemetery to bury their grandmother.
They decided to bury their beloved dog under the old oak tree in the garden.

Synonyms

  • inter
    8.7 0.11/m
    inter means placing a dead body in a grave or tomb during a funeral, while bury means putting something under soil.
  • inhume
    9.9 0.00/m
    inhume is a formal, less common word for placing a body in a grave, while bury is the everyday, general term.
  • entomb
    8.1 0.65/m
    entomb means placing a body inside a tomb or stone enclosure, while bury usually means covering something with soil in the ground.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cremate - to dispose of a dead body by burning
  • synonym: entomb - to place a dead body in a tomb
  • synonym: inter - to bury a dead body, usually with formal ceremony
