B.T.U.

noun
unit of energy measurement
5.5 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / bi ti ju / (AmE), / biÀê tiÀê juÀê / (BrE)
a unit of energy equal to about 1,055 joules, commonly used to measure the energy content of fuels or the power of heating and cooling systems

Examples

One B.T.U. unit
Natural gas is often priced by the B.T.U. in energy markets.
The air conditioner is rated at 10,000 B.T.U. per hour.
They used a calculator to estimate how many B.T.U. are needed to heat the room.

Synonyms

  • British thermal unit
    6.8 0.08/m
    British thermal unit names the heat to raise one pound of water by one degree Fahrenheit at atmospheric pressure, while B.T.U. is the common abbreviation used in technical specs.
