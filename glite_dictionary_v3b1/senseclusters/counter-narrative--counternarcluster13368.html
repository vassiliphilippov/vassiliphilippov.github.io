counter-narrative

adjective
opposing dominant narrative
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntər ˌnærətɪv / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntə ˌnærətɪv / (BrE)
opposing or challenging a dominant or widely accepted narrative, especially in actions, texts, or messages

Examples

Counter-narrative campaign
The organization launched a counter-narrative campaign to challenge stereotypes in the media.
Many activists use counter-narrative strategies to question official government statements.
A strong counter-narrative can help communities resist misinformation and promote critical thinking.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alternative - offering a different option or perspective
  • antonym: mainstream - representing the dominant or widely accepted view
