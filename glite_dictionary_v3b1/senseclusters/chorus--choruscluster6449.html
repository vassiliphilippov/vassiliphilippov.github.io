chorus

verb
to sing together in a choir
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔːrəs /; / ˈkɔrəs / (AmE), / ˈkɔːrəs / (BrE)
to sing or say something together as a group, at the same time

Examples

Birds chorus at dawn
Birds chorused at dawn, filling the air with music.
The children chorused joyfully during the school concert.
The crowd chorused the birthday song together.
The crowd chorused their approval when the speaker finished.
“Let’s go!” the children chorused excitedly as the bus arrived.
“We want ice cream!” they all chorused at once.
The children began chorusing the song during the school assembly.
Fans in the stadium were chorusing the team's anthem after the big win.
Protesters chorused their demands in unison outside the city hall.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chant - to repeat words or phrases together in a rhythmic way
  • synonym: echo - to repeat a sound or phrase, often after another
  • synonym: sing - to produce musical tones with the voice
  • antonym: solo - to sing or perform alone
