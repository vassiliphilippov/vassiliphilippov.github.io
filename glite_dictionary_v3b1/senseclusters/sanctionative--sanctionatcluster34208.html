sanctionative

adjective
serving to sanction or authorize
9.4
Pronunciation: / sæŋkʃəˌneɪtɪv / (AmE), / sæŋkʃəˌneɪtɪv / (BrE)
serving to authorize, approve, or give official permission to something; implying or imposing a sanction.

Examples

A sanctionative role
Her role in the process was primarily sanctionative, ensuring all actions complied with regulations.
The committee granted sanctionative approval for the new policy.
The power of the board of directors is mostly sanctionative rather than advisory.
