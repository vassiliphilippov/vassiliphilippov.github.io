paroicous

adjective
having both male and female organs in one moss structure
7.8
Pronunciation: / pəˈrɔɪɪkəs / (AmE), / pəˈrɔɪɪkəs / (BrE)
(of mosses and liverworts) having male and female reproductive organs present separately within the same reproductive structure (gametoecium).

Examples

Paroicous moss species
Some moss species are paroicous, with both antheridia and archegonia in the same cluster.
The paroicous arrangement can facilitate self-fertilization in certain bryophytes.
Unlike dioicous forms, paroicous mosses produce eggs and sperm on the same shoot.

Related Terms

  • synonym: autoicous - having male and female organs on the same moss plant but in separate structures
  • antonym: dioicous - having male and female organs on separate plants
