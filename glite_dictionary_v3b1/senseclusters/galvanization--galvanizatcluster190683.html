galvanization

noun
stimulation into action or energy
8.8 6.3
Pronunciation: /ɡæl.və.naɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ɡæl.və.nə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɡæl.və.naɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ɡæl.və.nə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
The act of stimulating someone or something into sudden activity or heightened energy.

Examples

Galvanization of a team
The leader's speech was a galvanization for the movement.
The surprise announcement led to an immediate galvanization of public opinion.
There was a noticeable galvanization of the team after their early setback.
After months of inaction, the crisis served as a galvanisation for change.
Her words were a much-needed galvanisation that revived our enthusiasm for the project.
The unexpected speech acted as a galvanisation for the movement's supporters.
