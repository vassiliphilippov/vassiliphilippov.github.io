microaggression

noun
subtle prejudice
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪkroʊəˈɡrɛʃən / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəʊəˈɡrɛʃən / (BrE)
a subtle or indirect comment or action that unintentionally expresses prejudice or discrimination toward a marginalized group

Examples

A microaggression comment
He was unaware that his comment was a microaggression that made his colleague uncomfortable.
Many people experience microaggressions in the workplace without realizing their impact.
Teachers are learning to recognize and address microaggressions in the classroom.
Repeated microaggressions in the classroom made the student feel unwelcome and isolated.
She was unaware that her comment was a microaggression until someone explained its impact.
Workshops on microaggression help employees recognize and avoid subtle forms of bias.
