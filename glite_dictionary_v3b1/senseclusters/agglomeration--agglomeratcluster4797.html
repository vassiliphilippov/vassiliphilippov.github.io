agglomeration

noun
a jumbled collection or mass
Pronunciation: / əˌɡlɑːməˈreɪʃən /; / əˌɡlɑː.məˈreɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / əˌɡlɒməˈreɪʃən /; / əˌɡlɒm.əˈreɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
a mass or collection of things that have been gathered together, or the process of gathering them

Examples

An agglomeration of objects
On his desk was an agglomeration of papers, books, and old coffee mugs.
The city is actually an agglomeration of several once-separate towns.
The region is known for its agglomeration of tech companies and startups.
An agglomeration of dust and fibers collected in the corner of the room.
Economic development has led to the agglomeration of industries in urban areas.
The city is actually an agglomeration of several small towns and villages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aggregation - a group, body, or mass composed of many distinct parts or individuals
  • antonym: dispersal - the act or result of dispersing; distribution in various directions
  • antonym: dispersion - the process of distributing things over a wide area
