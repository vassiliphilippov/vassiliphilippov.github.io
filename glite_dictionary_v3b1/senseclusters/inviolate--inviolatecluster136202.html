inviolate

adjective
not violated; kept pure or sacred
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈvaɪ.ə.lət / (AmE), / ɪnˈvʌɪ.ələt / (BrE)
not harmed or changed; kept pure or untouched, especially in the sense of remaining sacred or unbroken.

Examples

Inviolate tradition
A sense of tradition kept the historic site inviolate despite pressures for modernization.
Her privacy was considered inviolate by everyone in the family.
The ancient treaty remained inviolate for centuries, respected by all parties involved.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sacrosanct - regarded as too important to be interfered with
  • antonym: violated - having been harmed or treated disrespectfully
