social stratification

noun
hierarchical division of society into social classes
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊʃəl ˌstræt̬əfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈsəʊʃəl ˌstrætɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the hierarchical arrangement of people in society into different social classes or strata based on factors such as wealth, education, and occupation

Examples

Social stratification system
Education is often a key factor influencing social stratification within a country.
Many sociologists study social stratification to understand inequality in modern societies.
The research paper explores how social stratification affects access to healthcare.
