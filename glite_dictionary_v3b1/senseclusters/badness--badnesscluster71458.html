badness

noun
mischievous or naughty behavior, esp. in children
Pronunciation: / ˈbædnəs / (AmE), / ˈbadnəs / (BrE)
(especially in children) the quality of being mischievous, naughty, or behaving badly.

Examples

Child's badness
His badness often made his parents laugh, even when they tried to scold him.
She blamed her son's badness on the influence of his older cousins.
The teacher was frustrated by the constant badness of the children at the back of the class.

Related Terms

  • antonym: goodness - the quality of being good; virtue or kindness
  • synonym: naughtiness - the quality or state of being naughty or disobedient, especially in children
