impositional

adjective
relating to imposing
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɪm.pəˈzɪʃ.ən.əl / (AmE), / ˌɪm.pəˈzɪʃ.ən.əl / (BrE)
relating to or involving the act of imposing something on others

Examples

Impositional rules
Many employees found the new rules to be overly impositional and difficult to follow.
The teacher's impositional style left little room for student input.
Critics argued that the law was unnecessarily impositional on personal freedoms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authoritative - demanding obedience or control
  • antonym: permissive - allowing or tolerant, not imposing
