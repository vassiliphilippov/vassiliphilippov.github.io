bridegroom

noun
man recently married
1.1
Pronunciation: / ˈbraɪdˌɡruːm /; / ˈbraɪdˌɡrum / (AmE), / ˈbraɪdɡruːm /; / ˈbraɪdˌɡruːm / (BrE)
a man who is getting married or has just been married, especially on his wedding day

Examples

The bridegroom at wedding
After the ceremony, the bridegroom was congratulated by his friends and family.
The bridegroom gave a heartfelt speech at the reception.
The bridegroom wore a classic black tuxedo for the wedding.
Everyone cheered as the bridegroom kissed his new wife.
The bridegroom waited nervously at the altar for his bride to arrive.
Traditionally, the bridegroom gives a speech at the wedding reception.
