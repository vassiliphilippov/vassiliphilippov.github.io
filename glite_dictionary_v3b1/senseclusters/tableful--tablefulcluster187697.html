tableful

noun
amount filling a table
Pronunciation: / ˈteɪbəlˌfʊl / (AmE), / ˈteɪbəlˌfʊl / (BrE)
the amount of people or things (especially food or guests) that fills a table

Examples

A tableful of food
The family enjoyed a tableful of homemade dishes during the holiday dinner.
There was a tableful of guests laughing and sharing stories at the reunion.
She prepared a tableful of snacks for the children’s birthday party.
A cheerful tableful of guests laughed and shared stories during the holiday dinner.
The restaurant was busy, with every tableful enjoying their meals and conversations.
She prepared enough food to serve a full tableful at the family reunion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: armful - an amount that can be carried in one armful
  • synonym: handful - an amount that can be held in one hand
  • synonym: roomful - the number of people or things that fill a room
