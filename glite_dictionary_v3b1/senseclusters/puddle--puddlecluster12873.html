puddle

verb
dip plant roots in mud before planting
9.0 8.2
Pronunciation: /ˈpʌd.əl/ (AmE), /ˈpʌd.əl/ (BrE)
(of a plant, especially young ones) to dip into mud or a muddy mixture before planting, typically to protect the roots or help with transplantation.

Examples

Puddle tree roots
Gardeners often puddle young trees to give their roots additional protection before placing them in the ground.
It is common practice to puddle seedlings, especially in heavy soils, to ensure firm contact with the earth.
The nursery workers puddled the saplings in a clay slurry before transporting them to the planting site.
