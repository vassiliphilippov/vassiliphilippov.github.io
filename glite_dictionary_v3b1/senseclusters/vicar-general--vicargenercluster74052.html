vicar-general

noun
administrative deputy to a bishop
9.9 8.1
Pronunciation: /vɪk.ər ˈdʒen.ər.əl/ /; / /ˈvɪk.ər ˈdʒen.ər.əl/ (AmE), /vɪk.ə ˈdʒen.ər.əl/ /; / /ˈvɪk.ə ˈdʒen.ər.əl/ (BrE)
A priest who acts as the administrative deputy of a bishop, assisting in the governance of a diocese, especially in the Roman Catholic Church.

Examples

The vicar-general assists bishop
After consulting with the vicar-general, the bishop made his final decision on the issue.
The vicar-general oversees the administrative tasks of the diocese when the bishop is unavailable.
The vicar-general was responsible for implementing new church policies across the parishes.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: bishop - a senior member of the Christian clergy, typically in charge of a diocese
  • degree lower: vicar - a representative or deputy of a bishop or parish priest
