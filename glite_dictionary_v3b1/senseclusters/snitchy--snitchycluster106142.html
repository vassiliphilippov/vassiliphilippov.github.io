snitchy

adjective
inclined to inform
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsnɪtʃi / (AmE), / ˈsnɪtʃi / (BrE)
tending to inform on others, especially in a way that is disloyal or disliked

Examples

A snitchy teammate
Nobody wanted to share secrets with their snitchy teammate after he told the coach everything.
Her snitchy attitude made her unpopular among her classmates.
He was careful not to sound snitchy when reporting the problem to his boss.

Related Terms

  • antonym: loyal - showing firm and constant support to someone or something
  • synonym: tattletale - a person who tells on others, especially to authority
