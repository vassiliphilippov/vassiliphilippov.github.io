untrue

adjective
not faithful or loyal; betraying trust
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈtru / (AmE), / ʌnˈtruː / (BrE)
not loyal or faithful; betraying trust or not dependable

Examples

Untrue to a friend
Being untrue to one's principles can lead to regret and disappointment.
He realized he had been untrue to his promise and tried to make amends.
She felt hurt when her closest friend was untrue to her during a difficult time.
He was accused of being untrue to his promises and letting down his friends.
In the old tale, the knight who was untrue to his love was cast out of the kingdom.
She felt betrayed when her boyfriend proved untrue to her.

Related Terms

  • synonym: betray - to be disloyal to a person, group, or principle
  • antonym: faithful - loyal and constant to a person, cause, or belief
  • antonym: loyal - showing firm and constant support or allegiance
  • synonym: unfaithful - not loyal, especially in sexual relationships
