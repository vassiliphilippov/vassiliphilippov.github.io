pulley block

noun
wheel device for redirecting a rope's force
Pronunciation: / ˈpʊli ˌblɑk / (AmE), / ˈpʊli ˌblɒk / (BrE)
A device made up of a wheel with a groove for a rope or cable, used to change the direction or point of application of a force.

Examples

A pulley block device
He explained how a pulley block reduces the effort needed to raise a load.
Sailboats often have more than one pulley block to help manage the rigging.
The workers used a pulley block to lift the heavy beams onto the roof.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pulley - a wheel on an axle designed to support movement of a cable or belt
