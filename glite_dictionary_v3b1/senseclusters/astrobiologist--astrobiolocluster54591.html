astrobiologist

noun
life-in-universe scientist
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌæstroʊbaɪˈɑːlədʒɪst / (AmE), / ˌæstrəʊbaɪˈɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a scientist who studies the origin, existence, and possibility of life in the universe, especially beyond Earth

Examples

The astrobiologist studies life
The astrobiologist presented her findings about possible life on Mars at the international conference.
As an astrobiologist, he studies how life could survive in extreme environments beyond Earth.
A team led by an astrobiologist discovered new evidence of water on a distant moon.
The astrobiologist analyzed soil samples from Mars for signs of microbial life.
As an astrobiologist, she collaborates with NASA to design experiments for space missions.
Many astrobiologists are interested in the extreme environments on Earth as analogs for other planets.
The astrobiologist examined samples from a Mars rover for signs of microbial life.
Many astrobiologists collaborate with NASA to search for habitable planets.
As an astrobiologist, she is fascinated by the possibility of life on Europa.

Related Terms

  • synonym: astrobiology - the scientific study of life in the universe
  • synonym: astronomer - a scientist who studies stars, planets, and space
  • synonym: biologist - a scientist who studies living organisms
