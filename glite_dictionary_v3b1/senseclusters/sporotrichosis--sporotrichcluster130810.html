sporotrichosis

noun
chronic fungal skin infection
Pronunciation: / ˌspɔːroʊtrɪˈkoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌspɔːrətrɪˈkəʊsɪs / (BrE)
a chronic fungal infection of the skin and lymph nodes, caused by fungi of the genus Sporothrix

Examples

Chronic sporotrichosis infection
Gardeners using contaminated soil have a higher risk of developing sporotrichosis.
The veterinarian diagnosed the cat with sporotrichosis after noticing lesions on its skin.
Treatment for sporotrichosis usually involves a prolonged course of antifungal medication.
