sandarac

noun
a coniferous tree of North Africa and Spain
Pronunciation: / ˈsændəræk / (AmE), / ˈsændərək / (BrE)
a large evergreen coniferous tree (Tetraclinis articulata) native to North Africa and Spain, known for its fragrant wood and resin used in varnishes.

Examples

Sandarac tree grove
The sandarac tree grows abundantly in certain regions of Morocco and Algeria.
The fragrant wood of the sandarac is highly valued in woodworking.
Varnishes made from sandarac resin are prized for their clear, hard finish.
