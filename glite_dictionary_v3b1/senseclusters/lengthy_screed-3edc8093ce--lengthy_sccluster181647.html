lengthy screed

noun
long, angry written or spoken piece
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛŋθi skrid / (AmE), / ˈlɛŋθi skriːd / (BrE)
a long and often angry piece of writing or speech, usually expressing strong opinions

Examples

A lengthy screed of complaints
He delivered a lengthy screed at the meeting, criticizing every aspect of the proposal.
Her letter to the editor was a lengthy screed denouncing local government policies.
The professor's email became a lengthy screed about the flaws in the curriculum.
