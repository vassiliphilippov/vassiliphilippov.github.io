overinvestment

noun
excessive investment
6.2
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ɪn.ˈvest.mənt/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ɪn.ˈvest.mənt/ (BrE)
the act of investing more money or resources than is necessary, sustainable, or profitable

Examples

Business overinvestment
The country's rapid overinvestment in infrastructure led to many unused buildings and financial losses.
Experts believe that overinvestment in the tech sector could result in a market correction.
After years of overinvestment, the company struggled to achieve a reasonable return on its assets.
The real estate market crashed after years of overinvestment by developers.
Analysts warned that overinvestment in unproven technologies could lead to financial losses.
Due to overinvestment in advertising, the company exceeded its marketing budget.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: investment - the act of putting money or resources into something to gain a benefit
  • synonym: overspending - spending more money than is necessary or available
  • antonym: underinvestment - insufficient investment in a sector or project
