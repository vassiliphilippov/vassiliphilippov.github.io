unharmed

adjective
not injured or harmed
2.1 1.36/m
Pronunciation: /  ånÀàh…ërmd / (AmE), /  ånÀàh…ëÀêmd / (BrE)
not injured, damaged, or hurt; remaining safe and without harm

Examples

Remain unharmed after accident
Despite the accident, all the passengers remained unharmed.
She managed to escape the fire unharmed, thanks to the quick response of firefighters.
The precious artwork was found unharmed in the ruins after the earthquake.

Synonyms

  • unhurt
    1.0 0.53/m
    unhurt describes a person with no physical wounds, while unharmed is broader and can apply to people, objects, or situations.
  • uninjured
    1.8 0.47/m
    uninjured is used in reports and medical talk for someone with no wounds, while unharmed sounds more general and everyday.
  • unscathed
    6.3 1.87/m
    unscathed suggests escaping damage or harm completely, while unharmed is neutral and simply says no injury or damage occurred.
  • safe and sound
    1.5 1.19/m
    safe and sound feels warm and idiomatic when someone returns okay, while unharmed is a plain label for no injury.
  • unmolested
    6.5 0.40/m
    unmolested says something was left alone and not disturbed, while unharmed focuses on having no injury or damage.
