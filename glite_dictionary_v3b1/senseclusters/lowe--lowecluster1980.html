lowe

verb
to glow or blaze
Pronunciation: / loʊ / (AmE), / ləʊ / (BrE)
to burn with a flame or to glow brightly, especially in a poetic or archaic sense

Examples

To lowe in darkness
The campfire began to lowe as the night grew colder around us.
In the old tale, the hearth would lowe with a welcoming light each evening.
As dawn approached, the embers continued to lowe softly in the fireplace.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blaze - to burn brightly and strongly
  • antonym: smolder - to burn slowly with smoke but no flame
