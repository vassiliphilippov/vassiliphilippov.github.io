accrue

verb
to accumulate or increase over time
Pronunciation: / əˈkru / (AmE), / əˈkruː / (BrE)
to increase or accumulate over time, especially as a result of regular additions or the passage of time

Examples

Benefits accrue over time
Benefits will accrue to employees who stay with the company longer.
Interest will accrue on your savings account monthly.
Over the years, a considerable sum has accrued in her retirement fund.
Interest will accrue on your savings account every month.
The rights to the estate will accrue to her after the legal process is complete.
Upon the founder's death, control of the company accrued to his eldest daughter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accumulate - to gather or build up gradually
  • antonym: diminish - to become less, decrease
  • synonym: inherit - to receive property or money after someone's death
  • antonym: lose - to cease to have something, often as an antonym to 'acquire' or 'accrue'
