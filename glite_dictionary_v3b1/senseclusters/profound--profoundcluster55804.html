profound

adjective
very deep; extending far down
Pronunciation: / prəˈfaʊnd / (AmE), / prəˈfaʊnd / (BrE)
(of a place or thing) situated or extending to a great depth; very deep or unfathomable.

Examples

Profound depth
Divers explored the profound caves hidden beneath the coastline.
He gazed into the profound darkness of the old well, unable to see the bottom.
The submarine disappeared into the profound depths of the ocean.

Related Terms

  • antonym: shallow - of little depth; not deep
