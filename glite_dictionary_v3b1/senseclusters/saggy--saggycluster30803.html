saggy

adjective
loose and drooping
3.5
Pronunciation: /ˈsæ.giː/ (AmE), /ˈsæ.giː/ (BrE)
hanging down loosely or lacking firmness and shape, often due to age or wear

Examples

A saggy mattress
The old mattress became saggy after years of use and no longer supported his back.
She wore a saggy sweater that hung loosely from her shoulders.
The tent roof looked saggy after the heavy rain collected on top.

Related Terms

  • synonym: droopy - hanging down or bending limply
  • antonym: firm - solid and not easily bent or shaped
