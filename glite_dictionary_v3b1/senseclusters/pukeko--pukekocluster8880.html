pukeko

noun
New Zealand swamphen
9.9
Pronunciation: / puːˈkɛkoʊ / (AmE), / puːˈkɛkəʊ / (BrE)
a purple swamphen native to New Zealand, recognized for its bright blue and black feathers and red bill

Examples

A pukeko bird
We watched a pukeko wade through the reeds by the lake.
The bright blue feathers of the pukeko stood out against the green grass.
A group of pukeko gathered near the wetland at sunset.

Related Terms

  • synonym: moorhen - a small water bird related to the swamphen
  • synonym: swamphen - a large water bird found in wetlands
