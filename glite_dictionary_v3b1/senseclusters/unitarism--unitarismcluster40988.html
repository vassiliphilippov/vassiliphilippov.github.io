unitarism

noun
political centralization favoring unity
9.9 7.8
Pronunciation: /ˈjuː.nɪ.tə.rɪ.zəm/ /; / /juː.ˈnɪt.ə.rɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈjuː.nɪ.tə.rɪ.zəm/ /; / /juː.ˈnɪt.ə.rɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
The political principle or policy supporting the unification of a political entity by centralizing authority, often reducing the autonomy of constituent regions.

Examples

Central unitarism policy
Critics argue that unitarism can undermine local identities and governance.
Many national leaders advocated unitarism to ensure stronger governmental control over the provinces.
The debate between federalism and unitarism has shaped the country's constitution.
