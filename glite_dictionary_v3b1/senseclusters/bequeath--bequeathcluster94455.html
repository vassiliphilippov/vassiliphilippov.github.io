bequeath

verb
leave property by will after death
Pronunciation: / bɪˈkwið / (AmE), / bɪˈkwiːð / (BrE)
to leave or give (property, money, etc.) to someone by will after one's death

Examples

To bequeath property
In her will, she bequeathed all her paintings to the art museum.
She decided to bequeath her rare book collection to the local library.
Upon his death, Mr. Turner bequeathed his fortune to his three children.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inherit - to receive (property, title, etc.) as an heir at the death of the previous holder
