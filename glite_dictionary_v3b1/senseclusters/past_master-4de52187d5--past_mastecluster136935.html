past master

noun
person previously holding the position of master
Pronunciation: / pæst ˈmæstər / (AmE), / pɑːst ˈmɑːstə / (BrE)
a person who was formerly a master, especially one who has held the post of master in a society, organization, or profession

Examples

A past master of the lodge
After serving as chair for a decade, he became a past master of the organization.
As a past master of the lodge, she advised new members on traditions.
He was honored at the reunion as a respected past master.
