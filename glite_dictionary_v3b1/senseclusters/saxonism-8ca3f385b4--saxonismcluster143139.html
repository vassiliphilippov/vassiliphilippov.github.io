Saxonism

noun
Saxon language feature
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsæksənɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsæksənɪzəm / (BrE)
a word, phrase, or feature that is characteristic of the Saxons or their language

Examples

A Saxonism word
The preference for short, direct words in English is often seen as a Saxonism.
Some linguists study Saxonisms to understand the influence of Germanic languages on English.
The use of the word 'house' instead of 'domicile' is an example of a Saxonism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Anglicism - a word or feature characteristic of English
  • antonym: Latinism - a word or feature characteristic of Latin
