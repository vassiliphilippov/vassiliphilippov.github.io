stopband

noun
blocked frequency range
Pronunciation: / ˈstɑpˌbænd / (AmE), / ˈstɒpˌbænd / (BrE)
a range of frequencies that a filter blocks or greatly reduces in strength

Examples

The stopband range
The engineer adjusted the filter to widen the stopband and block more unwanted noise.
Signals within the stopband are significantly attenuated by the electronic circuit.
A well-designed stopband is essential for clear audio in communication systems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: notch filter - a filter that blocks a narrow range of frequencies
  • antonym: passband - a range of frequencies that a filter allows to pass through
