titanothere

noun
extinct horned mammal
Pronunciation: / taɪˈtænəˌθɪr / (AmE), / taɪˈtænəˌθɪə / (BrE)
an extinct large, horned mammal related to rhinoceroses that lived during the Eocene and Oligocene epochs

Examples

A titanothere skull
The paleontologist carefully examined the massive skull of a titanothere found in the desert.
Fossils of the titanothere reveal that it had two large horns on its head.
During the Eocene epoch, herds of titanothere roamed the ancient plains.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brontothere - an extinct mammal closely related to titanothere
  • synonym: rhinoceros - a large, thick-skinned, horned mammal living today
