A foot with a fallen arch resulting in an abnormally flattened and widened shape.

Examples

A splayfoot problem
A splayfoot can lead to discomfort and difficulty walking if not treated.
After years of wearing the wrong shoes, he developed a noticeable splayfoot.
Children with splayfoot may need special insoles for support.

Synonyms

  • flatfoot
    6.5 0.00/m
    flatfoot is a common everyday term for a flattened sole, while splayfoot highlights a flattened, widened foot shape.
  • fallen arch
    7.4 0.01/m
    fallen arch describes an arch collapse when standing, while splayfoot adds that the foot becomes unusually wide as well.
  • pes planus
    7.3 0.00/m
    pes planus is the medical name used by doctors, while splayfoot sounds descriptive and points to flattening plus widening.
  • sunken arch
    6.1 0.00/m
    sunken arch refers to an arch that has flattened inward, while splayfoot stresses both flattening and a noticeably widened sole.
