rehearsal

noun
practice session before a performance
3.0 13/m
Pronunciation: / r…™Ààh…úrs…ôl / (AmE), / r…™Ààh…úÀês…ôl / (BrE)
a session in which a play, piece of music, or other performance is practiced before being performed in front of an audience

Examples

A rehearsal before the show
Her anxiety eased after several rehearsals with the cast.
The choir met every week for rehearsal at the community center.
We have a final rehearsal tonight before the opening show.

Synonyms

  • run-through
    4.2 0.44/m
    a run-through is an uninterrupted practice of the whole show, while a rehearsal often includes shorter, stopped sessions.
  • playthrough
    4.4 0.00/m
    a playthrough is a complete run of a piece from start to finish, while a rehearsal often stops and repeats parts.
  • walk-through
    5.1 0.05/m
    a walk-through is an informal read-through with scripts and notes, while a rehearsal normally aims to perform without scripts.
  • dry run
    4.1 0.27/m
    a dry run is a practice used to test systems or timings in any event, while a rehearsal focuses on performing arts.
