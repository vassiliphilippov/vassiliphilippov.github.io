sick-out

noun
mass illness absence
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪkˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈsɪkˌaʊt / (BrE)
an event in which a large number of people are absent from work or school due to illness, causing significant disruption

Examples

Flu sick-out at school
A severe flu season led to a sick-out at the local high school, forcing classes to be canceled.
The company struggled to operate during the unexpected sick-out that affected most of its staff.
After the virus spread quickly, the hospital experienced a sick-out among its nurses and doctors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: epidemic - a widespread occurrence of an infectious disease in a community
  • synonym: outbreak - a sudden occurrence of disease affecting many people
