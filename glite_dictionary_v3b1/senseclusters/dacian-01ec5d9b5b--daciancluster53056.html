Dacian

noun
ancient Dacia inhabitant
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈdeɪsiən / (BrE)
a person who belonged to the ancient people of Dacia, a region in present-day Romania and Moldova

Examples

A Dacian warrior
The museum displayed artifacts created by a skilled Dacian craftsman over two thousand years ago.
Historians study the language and culture of the Dacian people to learn about ancient Eastern Europe.
A Dacian helmet was discovered during the archaeological dig near the Carpathian Mountains.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Roman - a person from ancient Rome
  • synonym: Thracian - a person from ancient Thrace, a neighboring region to Dacia
