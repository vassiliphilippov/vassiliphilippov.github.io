counter-reform

noun
opposition to reforms
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntər rɪˌfɔrm / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntə rɪˌfɔːm / (BrE)
an action or movement intended to oppose or reverse previous reforms

Examples

A counter-reform movement
The new administration introduced a counter-reform to reverse the previous education policies.
Many citizens protested against the counter-reform that threatened to undo years of progress.
Historians studied the effects of the counter-reform on social and political structures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reaction - opposition to progress or change
  • antonym: reform - the process of making changes to improve something
