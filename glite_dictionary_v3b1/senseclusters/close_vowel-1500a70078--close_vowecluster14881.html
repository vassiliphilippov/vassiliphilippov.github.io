close vowel

noun
vowel pronounced with tongue near roof of mouth
Pronunciation: / kloʊs ˈvaʊəl / (AmE), / kləʊs ˈvaʊəl / (BrE)
a vowel sound pronounced with the tongue positioned close to the roof of the mouth, as in the vowel of 'see'

Examples

A close vowel sound
Linguists classify the sound in 'see' as a high or close vowel.
The vowel in 'machine' is considered a close vowel in phonetic studies.
When learning French, many students struggle with the difference between a close vowel and an open vowel.

Related Terms

  • antonym: open vowel - a vowel sound made with the tongue far from the roof of the mouth
