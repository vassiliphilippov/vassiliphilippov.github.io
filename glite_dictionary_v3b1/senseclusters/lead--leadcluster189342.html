lead

verb
to cause or result in something
2.1
Pronunciation: / liːd /; / lid / (AmE), / liːd / (BrE)
to cause something to happen or be the result; to have something as a consequence

Examples

To lead to trouble
A lack of communication can lead to misunderstandings in relationships.
His poor decisions often lead to unnecessary complications.
The discovery of evidence led to the reopening of the case.
Her efforts to save money eventually led to the purchase of a new car.
Increased rainfall can lead to flooding in the area.
The team's careful planning leads to successful events each year.
Her sense of duty led her to volunteer for the mission.
Jealousy can sometimes lead people to make poor choices.
The evidence may lead the jury to a different conclusion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deter - to prevent someone from acting by discouraging them
  • synonym: influence - to affect how someone behaves or thinks
  • synonym: persuade - to cause someone to do something by giving them good reasons
  • synonym: result in - to have a particular outcome; to cause something as a result
