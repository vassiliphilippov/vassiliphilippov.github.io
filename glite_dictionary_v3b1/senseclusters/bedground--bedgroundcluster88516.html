bedground

noun
area where livestock sleep overnight
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛdˌɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈbɛdˌɡraʊnd / (BrE)
an area of ground where a herd of cattle or sheep sleeps for the night

Examples

The bedground for cattle
After a long day on the trail, the sheep flocked to their bedground for rest.
Predators often try to approach the bedground at night, so vigilance is necessary.
The cowboys selected a sheltered bedground to keep the cattle safe from the wind.
