wingover

noun
aerobatic climbing turn
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪŋˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈwɪŋˌəʊvə / (BrE)
an aerobatic maneuver in which an aircraft climbs steeply and then turns sharply to reverse direction

Examples

A wingover maneuver
The pilot executed a perfect wingover to impress the crowd at the air show.
During training, students learn how to perform a wingover safely and smoothly.
Spectators cheered as the vintage plane completed several wingovers in quick succession.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barrel roll - an aerobatic maneuver in which an aircraft rolls around its longitudinal axis
  • synonym: loop - an aerobatic maneuver where an aircraft makes a complete vertical circle
