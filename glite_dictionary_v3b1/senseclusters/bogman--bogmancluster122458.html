bogman

noun
body preserved in bog
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑɡˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈbɒɡˌmæn / (BrE)
a human body that has been naturally preserved in a peat bog, often for thousands of years

Examples

Ancient bogman discovery
The ancient bogman was discovered by archaeologists during a dig in northern Europe.
Visitors to the museum were fascinated by the well-preserved features of the bogman on display.
Scientists studied the clothing and hair of the bogman to learn about life in the Iron Age.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mummy - a body preserved by natural or artificial means
  • degree lower: skeleton - the bones of a dead person or animal
