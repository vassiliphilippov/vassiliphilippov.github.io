incest

noun
overlapping information causing bias
9.9 0.08/m
Pronunciation: / \u02c8\u026an.s\u025bst / (AmE), / \u02c8\u026an.s\u025bst / (BrE)
(in research, data analysis, and journalism) overlapping or recycled information from related sources that creates redundancy and can bias conclusions.

Examples

Data incest
Researchers warned of data incest in the social network study, which distorted the model's conclusions.
The analyst removed duplicated sources to prevent incest that could inflate perceived support for the theory.
When journalists rely on press releases without verification, incest can spread false narratives rapidly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: circularity - the situation where information or evidence repeatedly refers back to the same source, creating false confirmation
  • synonym: redundancy - the presence of similar or duplicated information across sources
