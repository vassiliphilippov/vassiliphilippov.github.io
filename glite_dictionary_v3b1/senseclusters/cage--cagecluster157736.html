cage

noun
something that restricts freedom
2.6
Pronunciation: / keɪdʒ / (AmE), / keɪdʒ / (BrE)
A situation, condition, or thing that limits or restricts freedom or movement, similar to how a physical cage confines.

Examples

A mental cage
His fear acted as a cage, preventing him from experiencing new things.
Many people yearn to break free from the invisible cage of social expectations.
She felt that her job was a cage, keeping her from pursuing her true passions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: freedom - the power or right to act, speak, or think as one wants
  • synonym: prison - a building or place where people are legally held as a punishment or while awaiting trial
