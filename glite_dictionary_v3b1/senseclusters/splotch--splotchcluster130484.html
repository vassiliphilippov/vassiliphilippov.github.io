splotch

verb
to mark with blotches or spots
6.7 0.28/m
Pronunciation: / splÉ‘tÊƒ / (AmE), / splÉ’tÊƒ / (BrE)
to mark or cover with irregular spots or blotches, typically of color or liquid

Examples

To splotch the wall
He splotched blue ink across the paper, leaving messy marks everywhere.
She accidentally splotched paint on her new jeans while working on the mural.
The children splotched mud all over the clean kitchen floor after playing outside.

Synonyms

  • blotch
    5.3 0.25/m
    blotch is an irregular stained patch on a surface, while splotch means marking with bigger, wet-looking blobs of color.
  • mottle
    8.0 0.62/m
    mottle shows many small spots or streaks of different shades across a surface, while splotch refers to fewer larger blotches.
  • splash
    4.1 1.33/m
    splash creates scattered drops or wet marks from liquid, while splotch describes larger, often thicker spots or blobs of color.
