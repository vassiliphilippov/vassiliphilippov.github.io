wing

noun
airfoil on the side of an aircraft
Pronunciation: / wɪŋ / (AmE), / wɪŋ / (BrE)
one of the horizontal structures on either side of an aircraft's fuselage that provide lift during flight

Examples

An airplane wing
Maintenance crews inspected the wing for any signs of damage.
Passengers sitting by the wing often feel less turbulence during flights.
The pilot noticed ice forming on the right wing before takeoff.
