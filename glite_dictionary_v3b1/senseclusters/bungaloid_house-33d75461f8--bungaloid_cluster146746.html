bungaloid house

noun
house similar to a bungalow
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌŋɡəˌlɔɪd haʊs / (AmE), / ˈbʌŋɡələʊɪd haʊs / (BrE)
A house that resembles a bungalow in appearance or style, but may not meet all the criteria of a true bungalow.

Examples

A bungaloid house style
Architects often classify certain suburban homes as bungaloid houses due to their low, wide profile.
Our neighborhood mostly consists of bungaloid houses built after World War II.
The couple bought a bungaloid house just outside the city, drawn by its charming design.
