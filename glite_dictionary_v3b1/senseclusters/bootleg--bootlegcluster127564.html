bootleg

verb
produce or distribute illegally
Pronunciation: / ˈbuːtˌlɛɡ / (AmE), / ˈbuːtˌlɛɡ / (BrE)
to make, sell, or distribute something illegally, especially alcohol or recordings

Examples

To bootleg music
He was arrested for trying to bootleg alcohol across state borders.
It's illegal to bootleg movies and sell them online.
The band discovered that someone had bootlegged their concert and was selling recordings.
During Prohibition, many people would bootleg whiskey to make money.
He was arrested for trying to bootleg counterfeit DVDs on the street.
They were caught attempting to bootleg pills across the border.

Related Terms

  • antonym: legalize - to make something legal that was previously illegal
  • synonym: pirate - to use or reproduce another's work illegally
  • synonym: smuggle - to move goods illegally into or out of a country
  • synonym: traffick - to deal or trade in something illegal, especially drugs
