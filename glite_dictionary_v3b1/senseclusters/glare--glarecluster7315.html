glare

noun
intense public attention or scrutiny
Pronunciation: / ɡlɛr / (AmE), / ɡlɛə / (BrE)
Intense public attention or scrutiny, especially in a way that is difficult to avoid.

Examples

Glare of publicity
After the scandal, every aspect of his life was under the glare of the media.
Some celebrities struggle with the constant glare of public attention.
The new policy came into the glare of political debate almost immediately.

Related Terms

  • synonym: limelight - the focus of public attention
  • antonym: obscurity - the state of being unknown or unnoticed
  • synonym: spotlight - public attention or notice
