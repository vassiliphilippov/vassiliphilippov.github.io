stick

noun
long, thin object; length of wood
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /stɪk/ (AmE), /stɪk/ (BrE)
a long, thin object or implement, often made of wood, used as a tool, support, or for other purposes (including sports equipment, levers, chalk, butter, or a USB drive)

Examples

A stick of wood
He found a stick of chalk on the teacher's desk.
She stirred her coffee with a cinnamon stick for extra flavor.
The magician broke the stick in half during his performance.
He bought a new stick for the upcoming hockey season.
Her stick broke during the final minutes of the polo match.
The player swung his stick and sent the puck across the ice.
A stick of butter is equivalent to half a cup.
She bought two sticks of margarine at the store.
The recipe calls for one stick of butter, melted.
