bottom out

verb
reach lowest point before improving
5.8 0.61/m
Pronunciation: / Ààb…ëÀêt…ôm a ät /; / Ààb…ët…ôm a ät / (AmE), / Ààb…ít…ôm a ät / (BrE)
to reach the lowest or worst point before starting to improve (used for emotions, prices, activity, etc.)

Examples

Prices bottom out
His mood bottomed out in late winter but soon improved with the arrival of spring.
Many experts believe the economy will bottom out before the end of the year.
Shares finally bottomed out after months of decline and began to recover.
After months of declining sales, the company finally bottomed out in March.
Experts predict that unemployment will bottom out in the second quarter of this year.
Housing prices continued to fall, but many analysts believe they will soon bottom out.

Synonyms

  • hit bottom
    5.1 0.75/m
    hit bottom describes reaching the lowest point after a long decline, while bottom out stresses starting to improve afterward.
  • bottom
    4.2 1.18/m
    bottom means reaching the lowest point before recovery, while bottom out is a phrasal verb used especially for prices and activity.
  • hit rock bottom
    4.8 0.83/m
    hit rock bottom means reaching a very bad or crisis point, while bottom out suggests the low point before things begin improving.

Related Terms

  • antonym: peak - to reach the highest point (opposite meaning)
  • degree higher: plateau - to reach and stay at a steady level
