repudiation

noun
denial of participation in a digital transaction
9.1
Pronunciation: /rɪ.pjuː.diː.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /rə.pjuː.dɪ.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /rɪ.pjuː.dɪ.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /rə.pjuː.dɪ.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
(in information security) the act or risk of a party denying that they sent or participated in an electronic communication or transaction, especially one that is digitally signed, creating uncertainty about its origin or authenticity.

Examples

Repudiation in messages
To prevent repudiation, the company logs timestamps and uses cryptographic signatures for every message.
The audit found a risk of repudiation because the system lacked secure record-keeping for user actions.
After the dispute, the customer claimed repudiation of the contract despite the digital signatures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: denial - the act of saying that something is not true or that one did not do something
  • antonym: non-repudiation - a security property that ensures a party cannot deny the authenticity or origin of a communication or transaction
