regressiveness

noun
tendency to revert
8.8
Pronunciation: / rɪˈɡrɛsɪvnəs / (AmE), / rɪˈɡrɛsɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of returning to an earlier or less mature stage of development or behavior, especially in response to stress

Examples

Regressiveness under stress
The regressiveness of his actions surprised everyone who knew his usual maturity.
Therapists often look for signs of regressiveness in patients under extreme stress.
Her regressiveness became apparent when she started avoiding adult responsibilities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: progressiveness - the quality of moving forward or advancing
  • synonym: regression - a return to an earlier or less developed state
