white zinnia

noun
zinnia with white flowers
Pronunciation: / waɪt zɪn.i.ə / (AmE), / waɪt ˈzɪn.i.ə / (BrE)
A type of zinnia plant with white flowers, having slender woolly stems and long narrow leaves, native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

Examples

A white zinnia flower
Gardeners in Texas often choose white zinnia for its resilience and attractive appearance.
She planted white zinnia alongside pink and yellow varieties for a colorful display.
The white zinnia brightened up the flower bed with its delicate blooms.
