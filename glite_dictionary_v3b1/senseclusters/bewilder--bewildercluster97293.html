bewilder

verb
confuse greatly; puzzle
5.7
Pronunciation: / bɪˈwɪldər / (AmE), / bɪˈwɪldə / (BrE)
to confuse or puzzle someone greatly, making them unable to understand or think clearly

Examples

To bewilder someone
Her sudden change in attitude completely bewildered him.
The complex instructions bewildered the students, leaving them unsure of what to do next.
The strange customs of the new country can bewilder visitors.
Her sudden change of attitude completely bewildered him.
The complex instructions bewildered the new employees.
The wide variety of choices can bewilder first-time visitors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: confuse - to make (someone) unable to think clearly or understand something
  • antonym: enlighten - to give someone greater knowledge or understanding about a subject
  • synonym: perplex - to make (someone) feel completely baffled
