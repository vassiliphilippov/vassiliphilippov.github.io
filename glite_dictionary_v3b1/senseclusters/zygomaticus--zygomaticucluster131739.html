zygomaticus

noun
facial muscle for smiling
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌzaɪɡəˈmætɪkəs / (AmE), / ˌzaɪɡəˈmætɪkəs / (BrE)
a muscle in the face that helps move the mouth and lips, especially when smiling

Examples

The zygomaticus muscle
The zygomaticus muscle contracts when you smile or laugh.
Damage to the zygomaticus can affect a person's ability to express emotions with their face.
During anatomy class, we learned about the function of the zygomaticus in facial expressions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: masseter - a facial muscle that helps with chewing
  • synonym: orbicularis oris - a muscle around the mouth that controls lip movements
