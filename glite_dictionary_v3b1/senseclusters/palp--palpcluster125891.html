palp

noun
insect sensory organ
9.9
Pronunciation: /pælp/ (AmE), /pælp/ (BrE)
a small, sensitive organ near the mouth of some insects and other animals, used for touching, feeling, or tasting

Examples

Insect palp
The spider uses its palps to sense vibrations and locate prey.
Scientists observed the movement of the insect's palp under the microscope.
Many butterflies have long palps that help them taste food before eating.
The spider uses its palp to sense its environment.
Insects often have a palp near their mouths to help them taste food.
A biologist examined the palp of the beetle under a microscope.
