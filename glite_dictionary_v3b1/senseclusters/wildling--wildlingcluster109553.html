wildling

noun
self-sown plant
8.2
Pronunciation: /ˈwaɪld.lɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈwaɪld.lɪŋ/ (BrE)
a plant that grows naturally from seed without being deliberately planted or cultivated by people

Examples

Wildling rose bush
A wildling rose bush appeared behind the fence, its flowers bright against the wall.
Gardeners sometimes leave a wildling to see how it develops naturally.
The forest floor was dotted with wildlings that had sprouted after the fire.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cultivar - a plant variety that has been produced in cultivation by selective breeding
  • synonym: sapling - a young tree, especially one with a slender trunk
  • synonym: seedling - a young plant grown from a seed
