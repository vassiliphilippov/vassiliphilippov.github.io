guiltiness

noun
the state of being guilty
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɪltiˌnəs / (AmE), / ˈɡɪltinɪs / (BrE)
the state or quality of having committed an offense or crime; guilt.

Examples

The guiltiness of crime
He was burdened by the guiltiness of his undisclosed actions.
Her sense of guiltiness grew as she considered confessing to her mistake.
The defendant's obvious guiltiness was evident from the evidence presented in court.

Related Terms

  • synonym: guilt - the fact of having committed a specified or implied offense or crime
  • antonym: innocence - the state of not having committed an offense
