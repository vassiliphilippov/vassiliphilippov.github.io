Synonyms

    1.0 200/m
    return is a neutral, slightly formal verb for coming to a previous place or state, while back is common and phrasal.
  • go back
    1.0 110/m
    go back is a common phrasal verb used when you return to an earlier place, topic, or time, while back is shorter and more flexible.
  • come back
    1.3 20/m
    come back means returning toward the speaker or an earlier topic, while back is more general about going or replying.
  • revert
    4.2 5/m
    revert is a formal verb meaning to return to an earlier state or method, while back is everyday and suits actions or replies.
  • turn back
    2.1 11/m
    turn back means to change direction and go the other way, while back covers returning in many forms and contexts.
  • reenter
    3.2 1/m
    reenter means to go into a place or situation again after leaving, while back covers returning in place, time, or reply.
