Auriculariaceae

noun
family of fungi with jelly-like bodies
Pronunciation: / ɔːˌrɪkjəˌlɛriˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / ɔːˌrɪkjʊˌlɛəriˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
a family of fungi with gelatinous fruiting bodies, belonging to the order Auriculariales

Examples

Auriculariaceae fungi family
Members of the Auriculariaceae are known for their jelly-like fruiting bodies that grow on wood.
Researchers studied the ecological roles of Auriculariaceae species in forest environments.
The wood ear mushroom, a popular edible fungus, belongs to the Auriculariaceae family.
