affordance

noun
possibility for action
Pronunciation: / əˈfɔrdəns / (AmE), / əˈfɔːdəns / (BrE)
a feature or property of an object or environment that makes a particular action possible or suggests how it can be used

Examples

The affordance of a handle
The handle on the door is an affordance that allows people to pull it open easily.
Designers consider every affordance to ensure their products are intuitive to use.
A flat surface provides an affordance for placing objects on it.
The affordance of a button on a website is that it looks clickable to users.
Designers consider the affordance of objects to make them easier to use.
A chair's affordance is that it invites people to sit on it.

Related Terms

  • antonym: constraint - a limitation or restriction that prevents an action
  • synonym: function - the purpose or use of something
  • synonym: opportunity - a situation that makes it possible to do something
