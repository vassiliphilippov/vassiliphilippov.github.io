barrel

verb
to move rapidly
Pronunciation: / ˈbærəl / (AmE), / ˈbærəl / (BrE)
to move very quickly and often in a careless, forceful, or uncontrolled way

Examples

To barrel down the road
The truck barreled down the narrow street, barely missing the parked cars.
She barreled into the room, not noticing the surprised looks on everyone's faces.
The children barreled through the hallway, laughing and shouting as they played.
The truck barrelled down the highway at high speed.
Children barrelled through the hallway, laughing and shouting.
He barrelled into the room without knocking, startling everyone inside.
The car barreled around the corner, barely missing the pedestrians.
He barreled through the crowd, not stopping to apologize.
A cyclist barreled down the hill, gaining speed with every second.

Related Terms

  • synonym: careen - to move swiftly and in an uncontrolled way
  • antonym: crawl - to move very slowly
  • antonym: creep - to move slowly and carefully
  • synonym: rush - to move quickly with urgency
  • synonym: speed - to move quickly
