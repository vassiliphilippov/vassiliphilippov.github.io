basal vein

noun
large vein in the brain's temporal lobe
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪsəl veɪn / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsəl veɪn / (BrE)
a large vein located along the medial surface of the temporal lobe of the brain, which drains into the great cerebral vein.

Examples

The basal vein of brain
A blockage in the basal vein can lead to serious neurological symptoms.
During the surgery, the neurosurgeon carefully identified the basal vein to avoid complications.
The basal vein is important for draining blood from deep structures of the brain.
