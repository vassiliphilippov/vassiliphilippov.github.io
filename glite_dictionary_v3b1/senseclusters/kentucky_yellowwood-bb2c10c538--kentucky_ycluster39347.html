Kentucky yellowwood

noun
a North American deciduous tree with yellow dye-yielding wood
9.3
Pronunciation: / kənˈtʌki ˈjɛloʊˌwʊd / (AmE), / kənˈtʌki ˈjɛləʊˌwʊd / (BrE)
a small deciduous tree with a rounded head, showy white flowers in terminal clusters, and heavy hardwood that yields a yellow dye

Examples

The Kentucky yellowwood tree
Gardeners often choose Kentucky yellowwood for ornamental planting due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
Its heavy hardwood has been traditionally used for making furniture, and the Kentucky yellowwood also supplies a natural yellow dye.
The Kentucky yellowwood produces elegant white flowers that brighten up the spring landscape.
