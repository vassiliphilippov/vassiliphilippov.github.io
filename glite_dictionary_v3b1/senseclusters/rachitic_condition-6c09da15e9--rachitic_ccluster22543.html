rachitic condition

noun
medical condition related to rickets
Pronunciation: / rəˈkɪtɪk kənˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / rəˈkɪtɪk kənˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
A medical condition related to rickets, often resulting from vitamin D deficiency, and typically causing weak or deformed bones.

Examples

A rachitic condition diagnosis
Many 19th-century medical texts describe various rachitic conditions associated with poor nutrition.
The doctor diagnosed the child with a rachitic condition after seeing the bone deformities.
Vitamin D supplements can help prevent rachitic conditions in young children.
