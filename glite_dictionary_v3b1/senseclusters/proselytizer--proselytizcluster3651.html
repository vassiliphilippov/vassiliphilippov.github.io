proselytizer

noun
one who converts others
8.9
Pronunciation: /ˈprɑː.sə.laɪ.zər/ (AmE), /ˈprɒs.ə.laɪ.zə/ (BrE)
a person who tries to persuade others to adopt a particular belief, religion, cause, or group

Examples

A proselytizer speaks
The proselytizer spoke passionately about his beliefs at the community center.
Some people feel uncomfortable when a proselytizer approaches them in public places.
As a dedicated proselytizer, she traveled to many countries to share her faith.
The proselytizer handed out pamphlets about the new community group in the city square.
As a passionate proselytizer, she often spoke to friends about joining her environmental cause.
Some people avoid conversations with a proselytizer because they dislike being persuaded to change their views.

  • antonym: skeptic - a person who doubts or questions accepted beliefs
