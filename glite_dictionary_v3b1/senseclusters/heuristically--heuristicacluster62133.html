heuristically

adverb
by practical methods
Pronunciation: / hjuˈrɪstɪkli / (AmE), / hjʊəˈrɪstɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that uses practical methods, intuition, or experience to solve problems or learn, rather than following strict rules or logic

Examples

Solved heuristically
The problem was solved heuristically because there was no time for a detailed analysis.
Engineers often approach complex systems heuristically to find workable solutions quickly.
The theory was developed heuristically before being confirmed by experiments.
The engineer approached the problem heuristically, using past experience to guide her decisions.
Many puzzles can be solved heuristically when there is no clear formula.
The software was improved heuristically by testing different solutions and keeping what worked best.

Related Terms

  • synonym: experimentally - in a way based on trial and error or testing
  • synonym: intuitively - in a way based on instinctive understanding
  • antonym: systematically - in a methodical and organized way
