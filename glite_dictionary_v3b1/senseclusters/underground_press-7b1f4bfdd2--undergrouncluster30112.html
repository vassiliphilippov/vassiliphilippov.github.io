underground press

noun
clandestine publishing of banned or dissident literature
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndɚˌɡraʊnd ˈprɛs / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌɡraʊnd ˈprɛs / (BrE)
a system of clandestine publishing and distribution of literature, especially that which is dissident or banned by authorities

Examples

The underground press system
During the dictatorship, the underground press played a crucial role in keeping dissenting voices alive.
Historians have found evidence of an extensive underground press network operating in the city.
Many writers risked imprisonment for contributing to the underground press during the era of censorship.

Related Terms

  • synonym: samizdat - the clandestine copying and distribution of literature banned by the state, especially in the former Soviet Union
