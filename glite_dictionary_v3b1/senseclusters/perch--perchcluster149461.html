perch

noun
unit of land area (square rod)
9.9 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / p…ùt É / (AmE), / p…úÀêt É / (BrE)
an old unit of measurement for land area or length (historical)

Examples

A land perch
Historically, land was often measured in perches and acres rather than modern metric units.
Surveyors marked out each perch using wooden poles as a guide.
The old deed listed the property size as twenty perches.
A length of fifteen perches was marked out along the boundary.
In historical documents, land was sometimes sold by the perch.
The surveyor used a perch as a standard unit when measuring the old field.

Synonyms

    9.9 0.37/m

  • rod

  • rod
    9.1 0.28/m
    rod is a unit forming a square plot about five yards on each side, while perch is the older name for that area.
