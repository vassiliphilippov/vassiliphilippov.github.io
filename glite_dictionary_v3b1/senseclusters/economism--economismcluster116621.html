economism

noun
economic focus in non-economic areas
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈkɑːn.ə.mɪz.əm/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈkɒn.ə.mɪz.əm/ (BrE)
the practice of applying economic principles or market reasoning to areas not traditionally considered economic, such as education or health care

Examples

Economism in education
Some educators criticize economism for treating schools as businesses rather than places of learning.
The government's approach to health care has been accused of economism by focusing mainly on cost efficiency.
Debates about public policy often reveal tensions between economism and social or ethical considerations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: humanism - a system of thought that focuses on human values and concerns rather than economic or material interests
  • synonym: materialism - the belief that material possessions and physical comfort are more important than spiritual values
