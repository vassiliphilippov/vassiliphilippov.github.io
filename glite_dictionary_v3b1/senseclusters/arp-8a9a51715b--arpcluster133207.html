ARP

noun
broken chord sequence
6.9
Pronunciation: / ɑːr piː / (AmE), / ɑː pɪ / (BrE)
a broken chord in music played as a sequence of individual notes, also called an arpeggio

Examples

A shimmering ARP
The synthesizer's ARP added a shimmering texture to the electronic track.
He practiced the ARP pattern slowly to improve his finger strength and accuracy.
Many pop songs use a simple ARP to create a catchy introduction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arpeggio - a chord whose notes are played in sequence rather than together
  • degree higher: chord - a group of notes played together in music
