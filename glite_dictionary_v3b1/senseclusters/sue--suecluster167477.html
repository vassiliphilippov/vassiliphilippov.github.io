sue

verb
start legal proceedings in court
Pronunciation: / suː / (AmE), / suː / (BrE)
to start legal proceedings against a person or organization, especially by bringing a case to court

Examples

To sue a company
After the accident, she decided to sue the company for negligence.
The actor plans to sue several publications for defamation of character.
They are suing their landlord for failing to make necessary repairs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: defend - to argue or act in support of someone in court against legal claims
  • synonym: prosecute - to officially charge someone with a crime in a court of law
