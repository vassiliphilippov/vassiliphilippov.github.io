sue

verb
start legal proceedings in court
3.7 35/m
Pronunciation: / suÀê / (AmE), / suÀê / (BrE)
to start legal proceedings against a person or organization, especially by bringing a case to court

Examples

To sue a company
After the accident, she decided to sue the company for negligence.
The actor plans to sue several publications for defamation of character.
They are suing their landlord for failing to make necessary repairs.

Synonyms

  • file a lawsuit
    4.6 1.65/m
    file a lawsuit is the act of officially starting a court case, while sue means to bring that formal case against someone.
  • take legal action
    4.8 0.43/m
    take legal action can mean many official steps like suing or negotiating, while sue specifically means starting a court case.
  • litigate
    8.2 1.59/m
    litigate means to argue a case in court over time, whereas sue means to begin the legal case against someone.

Related Terms

  • antonym: defend - to argue or act in support of someone in court against legal claims
  • synonym: prosecute - to officially charge someone with a crime in a court of law
