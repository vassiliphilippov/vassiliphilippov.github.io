know

verb
be familiar or acquainted with
1.0
Pronunciation: / noʊ / (AmE), / nəʊ / (BrE)
to be familiar with or have experience of a person, place, thing, or situation

Examples

To know a person
Have you ever known anyone who climbed Mount Everest?
I know most of the people in my neighborhood.
She doesn't know this song, so she can't sing along.
I know him as someone who always keeps his promises.
Many people knew her as a talented musician in college.
We know this restaurant for its excellent service and friendly atmosphere.
After his illness, he knows weakness and pain in a way he never had before.
Only someone who has lived in war can truly know the fear that comes with it.
She doesn't just sympathize with your struggle—she genuinely knows what it's like.
