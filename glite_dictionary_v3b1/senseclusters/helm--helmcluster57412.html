helm

verb
to steer or lead; control

Pronunciation: / hÉ›lm / (AmE), / hÉ›lm / (BrE)
to steer or direct (a ship, boat, or, figuratively, an organization or enterprise); to be in control or leadership

Examples

To helm a project
After years of hard work, she was finally chosen to helm the project.
He was brought in to helm the struggling company and restore its reputation.
The captain asked him to helm the ship in rough waters.

Synonyms

  • head up
    3.9 2.60/m

  • head
    2.9 28/m

  • hold the reins

  • skipper
