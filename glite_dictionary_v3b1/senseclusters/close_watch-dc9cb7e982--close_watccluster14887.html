close watch

noun
careful and continuous observation
Pronunciation: / kloʊs wɑtʃ / (AmE), / kləʊs wɒtʃ / (BrE)
continuous and careful observation of someone or something, especially to notice any changes or problems

Examples

A close watch
Parents should maintain a close watch on their children when playing near water.
The company is keeping a close watch on market trends to stay competitive.
The police kept a close watch on the suspect's house throughout the night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scrutiny - careful and thorough examination
  • synonym: surveillance - close monitoring of people or activities
