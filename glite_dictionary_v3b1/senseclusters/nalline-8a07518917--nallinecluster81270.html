Nalline

noun
drug counteracting narcotic effects
Pronunciation: / ˈnæliːn / (AmE), / ˈnal.iːn / (BrE)
A trademarked drug used as a narcotic antagonist to counteract the effects of narcotics, especially in cases of morphine poisoning.

Examples

Nalline injection
After the injection of Nalline, the patient's breathing improved noticeably.
Emergency staff carry Nalline for rapid treatment of opioid poisoning.
The doctor administered Nalline to reverse the patient's morphine overdose.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nalorphine - a narcotic antagonist used to reverse opioid effects
