cheddar cheese

noun
hard, smooth-textured cheese
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɛdər tʃiz / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛdə ˈtʃiːz / (BrE)
A hard, smooth-textured cheese originally made in the Cheddar region of southwestern England, now produced worldwide in various styles.

Examples

Aged cheddar cheese
In England, traditional cheddar cheese is often aged for several months.

The recipe calls for grated cheddar cheese sprinkled on top before baking.
