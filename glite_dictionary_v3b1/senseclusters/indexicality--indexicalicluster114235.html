indexicality

noun
property of pointing
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndɛksɪˈkæləti / (AmE), / ˌɪndɛksɪˈkælɪti / (BrE)
the quality of a word or expression whose meaning depends on the context or situation in which it is used (such as 'here', 'now', or 'you')

Examples

Indexicality of 'here'
In linguistics, the indexicality of words like 'here' and 'now' depends on the context in which they are used.
Photographs are often discussed in terms of their indexicality, as they directly record what was in front of the camera.
The indexicality of smoke makes it a sign of fire in many cultures.
The indexicality of the word 'you' means it refers to different people in different situations.
Linguists study indexicality to understand how context shapes the meaning of certain expressions.
Because of its indexicality, the meaning of 'today' changes depending on when it is spoken.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deixis - words or phrases whose meaning depends on context, such as 'this' or 'now'
  • synonym: reference - the act of mentioning or alluding to something
  • antonym: symbolism - the use of symbols to represent ideas or qualities
