Wobbly

noun
member of the Industrial Workers of the World
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑbli / (AmE), / ˈwɒbli / (BrE)
A member of the Industrial Workers of the World, an international labor union founded in 1905.

Examples

Wobblies union members
In the early 20th century, many Wobblies organized strikes demanding better conditions for workers.
She became a Wobbly after joining the Industrial Workers of the World in Chicago.
The Wobblies believed in uniting all workers into one big union.
