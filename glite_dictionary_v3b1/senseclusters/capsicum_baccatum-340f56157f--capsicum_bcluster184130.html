Capsicum baccatum

noun
a species of hot pepper plant
Pronunciation: / ˈkæpsɪkəm bəˈkætəm / (AmE), / ˈkæpsɪkəm bəˈkætəm / (BrE)
a species of pepper plant native to tropical America, bearing small, very hot, oblong red fruits; includes wild forms and is ancestral to many cultivated hot and sweet peppers

Examples

Capsicum baccatum plant
Farmers have cultivated Capsicum baccatum for generations, selecting the hottest fruits for seed.
Many South American dishes feature the Capsicum baccatum for its unique heat and flavor.
The botanist studied a wild variety of Capsicum baccatum in the rainforest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chili pepper - a generic term for hot peppers produced by various Capsicum species
