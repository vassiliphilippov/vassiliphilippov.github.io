Crataegus calpodendron

noun
American hawthorn species with pear-shaped berries
Pronunciation: / krəˈtiːɡəs ˌkælpəˈdɛndrən / (AmE), / krəˈtiːɡəs ˌkalpəˈdɛndrən / (BrE)
A North American species of hawthorn tree or shrub, almost thornless, with upright form and somewhat pear-shaped berries.

Examples

North American Crataegus calpodendron
Botanists admire the almost thornless branches of Crataegus calpodendron compared to other hawthorns.
The Crataegus calpodendron grows best in well-drained soils and sunny locations.
The berries of Crataegus calpodendron are pear-shaped and turn reddish as they ripen.
