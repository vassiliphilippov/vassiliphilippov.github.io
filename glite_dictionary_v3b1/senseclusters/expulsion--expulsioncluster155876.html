expulsion

noun
forcing out by pressure
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈspʌlʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˈspʌlʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of forcing out something by applying pressure, especially from a container or body

Examples

Expulsion of air
An accidental expulsion of toothpaste can make a mess in the bathroom.
The dentist explained that the expulsion of pus from the abscess indicated an infection.
The steady expulsion of air from the balloon caused it to deflate quickly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: extrusion - the act of pushing something out by force
