molluscum contagiosum

noun
viral skin infection with white bumps
9.9 9.1
Pronunciation: / məˌlʌskəm kənˌteɪdʒiˈoʊsəm / (AmE), / məˌlʌskəm kənˌtadʒiˈəʊsəm / (BrE)
a viral skin infection characterized by small, round, white or flesh-colored bumps, most commonly affecting children or people with weakened immune systems

Examples

molluscum contagiosum skin bumps
People with weakened immune systems are more likely to develop molluscum contagiosum.
The doctor diagnosed the rash as molluscum contagiosum, a common infection in young children.
Treatment for molluscum contagiosum is often unnecessary, as the bumps can disappear on their own.
