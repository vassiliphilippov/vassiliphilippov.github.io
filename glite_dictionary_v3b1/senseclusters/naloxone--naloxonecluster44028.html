naloxone

noun
drug counteracting opioid overdose
Pronunciation: / nəˈlɑkˌsoʊn / (AmE), / nəˈlɒksəʊn / (BrE)
a potent drug used to counter the effects of opioid overdose, especially effective against narcotics like morphine and heroin

Examples

naloxone injection
Doctors recommend having naloxone readily available where opioid medications are present.
Emergency responders administered naloxone to revive the patient who had overdosed on heroin.
The city distributed free naloxone kits to help prevent deaths from opioid overdose.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Narcan - brand name for naloxone; opioid overdose antidote
