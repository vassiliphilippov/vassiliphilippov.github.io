disaccord

verb
be in disagreement or not correspond
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪs.əˈkɔrd / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.əˈkɔːd / (BrE)
(of two or more things) to be different or not in agreement; to fail to correspond.

Examples

They disaccord strongly
His personal beliefs disaccord with the values upheld by the organization.
The accounts from different sources disaccorded, making it hard to establish the facts.
The evidence presented in court disaccorded with the witness's statement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accord - to be in agreement or harmony
  • synonym: disagree - to have or express a different opinion; to not agree
