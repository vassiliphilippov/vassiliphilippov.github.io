undersaturation

noun
lack of color intensity
7.7
Pronunciation: /ʌn.dər.sætʃ.ə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ʌn.də.sætʃ.ə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a condition in which colors or images lack intensity or vividness due to low saturation

Examples

Undersaturation of colors
The undersaturation in the image made the landscape appear dull and lifeless.
Graphic designers often adjust undersaturation to achieve a more vibrant look in their work.
If there is too much undersaturation, the colors in a photo can seem faded or washed out.

Related Terms

  • synonym: desaturation - the process of reducing color intensity
  • antonym: oversaturation - a condition in which colors are excessively intense or vivid
