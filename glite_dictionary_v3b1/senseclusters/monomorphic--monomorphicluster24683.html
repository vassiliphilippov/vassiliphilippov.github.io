monomorphic

adjective
injective or left-cancellative
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑnəˈmɔrfɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈmɔːfɪk / (BrE)
having the property of being a monomorphism; in mathematics, describing a function or mapping that is injective or left-cancellative.

Examples

Monomorphic mapping
A function is called monomorphic if it is injective in the context of set theory.
In category theory, an arrow is monomorphic when it can be left-cancelled in compositions.
The professor explained that monomorphic mappings preserve the uniqueness of elements.

Related Terms

  • antonym: epimorphic - having the property of being an epimorphism; right-cancellative in category theory
  • synonym: injective - describing a function that maps distinct inputs to distinct outputs
