countercurrent

noun
a turbulent area where two water currents meet
8.4 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààka änt…ôrÀåk…ùr…ônt / (AmE), / Ààka änt…ôk år…ônt / (BrE)
A stretch of turbulent water in a river or the sea where one current flows into or across another current, often resulting in swirling motion or disruption.

Examples

River countercurrent
A countercurrent developed as the ocean's main flow collided with a coastal stream.
Swimmers should be careful of the strong countercurrent near the rocks.
The boat struggled against the countercurrent where the river met the incoming tide.
