cheapness

noun
lack of taste due to being cheap or vulgar
3.5 0.11/m
Pronunciation: / Ààt Éip.n…ôs / (AmE), / Ààt ÉiÀêp.n…ôs / (BrE)
the quality of lacking taste, refinement, or value, especially due to being inexpensive or vulgar

Examples

The cheapness of style
Her flashy jewelry added an air of cheapness to her otherwise lovely attire.
Many critics commented on the cheapness of the decor, saying it lacked any sense of elegance.
The movie's cheapness was evident in its tacky sets and costumes.

Synonyms

  • tackiness
    6.6 0.03/m
    tackiness is a low-quality, showy lack of taste often in objects or decor, while cheapness is simple lack of value or refinement.
  • trashiness
    7.2 0.01/m
    trashiness is a rough, cheaply made or imitative quality that looks low-class, while cheapness is being inexpensive and lacking value or taste.
  • tawdriness
    8.7 0.05/m
    tawdriness is cheap and gaudy in a showy way that seeks attention, while cheapness is simply low value or taste.
  • tastelessness
    6.8 0.04/m
    tastelessness means lacking aesthetic judgment or subtlety, often in style or art, while cheapness highlights low value or vulgarity.
  • cheesiness
    4.9 0.07/m
    cheesiness is corny, sentimental, or cheaply silly in tone or style, while cheapness stresses low value or vulgar appearance.
  • tat
    9.6 0.29/m
    tat refers to cheap, gaudy objects people sell or wear, while cheapness describes the general quality of low value or poor taste.
  • classlessness
    8.1 0.00/m
    classlessness shows lack of social grace or manners and can mean rude behavior, while cheapness focuses on low value, taste, or vulgar style.
