Pinot blanc

noun
variety of white wine grape
Pronunciation: / piːnoʊ ˈblɑːŋk /; / piːˌnoʊ ˈblɑːŋk / (AmE), / ˈpiːnəʊ ˈblɒ̃ /; / ˈpiːnəʊ ˌblɒ̃ / (BrE)
a type of white wine grape, or the dry white wine made from it, especially grown in regions like California and France

Examples

Pinot blanc wine

The winery specializes in Pinot blanc and other crisp white wines.

She prefers Pinot blanc over Chardonnay for its lighter body and subtle fruit notes.
The Pinot blanc from Alsace has a crisp, refreshing flavor.
We ordered a bottle of Pinot blanc to start our meal.
