categoreme

noun
logical term usable as subject or predicate
8.5 9.6
Pronunciation: /kæt.ə.ˈgɔː.r.iːm/ /; / /kæt.ə.ˈgɔː.r.əm/ (AmE), /kæt.ə.ˈgɒ.r.iːm/ /; / /kæt.ə.ˈgɔː.r.iːm/ (BrE)
A term that can stand alone as the subject or predicate in a logical proposition; a categorematic expression.

Examples

A categoreme term
Articles like 'the' or 'and' are not categoremes because they cannot stand alone in propositions.
In classical logic, the word 'man' is a categoreme because it can function as a subject or predicate.
The distinction between categoremes and syncategoremes is important in medieval logic.
