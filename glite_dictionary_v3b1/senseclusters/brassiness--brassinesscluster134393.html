brassiness

noun
harsh metallic sound
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈbræsinəs / (AmE), / ˈbrɑːsinəs / (BrE)
a bright, harsh, and metallic quality in sound, especially similar to that of brass instruments

Examples

Brassiness of the trumpet
The brassiness of the saxophone solo made the performance stand out in the concert.
Some people enjoy the brassiness of certain jazz bands, while others prefer a softer sound.
The brassiness in her voice gave the song a bold and energetic feel.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mellowness - a soft, smooth, and pleasant quality of sound
  • synonym: shrillness - a high-pitched and piercing quality of sound
