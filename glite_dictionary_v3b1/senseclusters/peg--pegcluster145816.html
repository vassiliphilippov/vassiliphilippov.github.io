peg

verb
fix or stabilize a price or rate
2.5 5.3
Pronunciation: / pɛɡ / (AmE), / pɛɡ / (BrE)
to fix or stabilize the price of a commodity or an exchange rate, usually by law or market intervention

Examples

To peg the price
By pegging prices, the authorities tried to prevent inflation.
Some countries peg their exchange rates to the US dollar to avoid market fluctuations.
The government decided to peg the currency to the euro for greater stability.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fix - to set or make stable (a price, rate, etc.)
  • antonym: float - to allow prices or rates to vary freely rather than fix them
