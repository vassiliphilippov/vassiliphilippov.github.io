sea moss

noun
aquatic animal forming moss-like colonies
2.9 7.1
Pronunciation: /siː mɔːs/ /; / /siː mɑːs/ (AmE), /siː mɒs/ (BrE)
a sessile aquatic animal that forms moss-like colonies of small polyps, each with a curved or circular ridge bearing tentacles, typically attached to stones or seaweed and reproducing by budding

Examples

Rocky sea moss colony
Divers discovered a colony of sea moss attached to the rocky seabed.
Marine biologists study how sea moss reproduces through budding and forms new colonies.
The delicate structure of sea moss colonies resembles underwater gardens.
