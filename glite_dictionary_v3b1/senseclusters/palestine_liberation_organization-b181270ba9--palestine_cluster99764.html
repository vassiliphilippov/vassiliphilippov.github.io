Palestine Liberation Organization

proper noun
Palestinian political and representative organization
4.2
Pronunciation: / pæl.ə.stin ˌlɪ.bəˈreɪ.ʃən ˌɔr.ɡə.nəˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌpæl.ɪˈstiːn ˌlɪb.əˈreɪ.ʃən ˌɔː.ɡə.naɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
an organization established in 1964 to represent Palestinian Arabs and promote the creation of an independent state of Palestine; recognized as the official representative of the Palestinian people by the United Nations and many states.

Examples

The Palestine Liberation Organization leader
After years of negotiation, the Palestine Liberation Organization gained observer status at the United Nations.
The Palestine Liberation Organization has been a key player in Middle Eastern politics since the 1960s.
Yasser Arafat was the chairman of the Palestine Liberation Organization for several decades.
