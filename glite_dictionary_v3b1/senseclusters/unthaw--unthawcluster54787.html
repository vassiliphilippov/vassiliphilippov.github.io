unthaw

verb
(nonstandard) thaw or defrost
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈθɔ / (AmE), / ʌnˈθɔː / (BrE)
To cause to become soft or liquid, especially by warming after being frozen; to thaw or defrost (nonstandard).

Examples

To unthaw frozen food
I took the chicken out of the freezer to unthaw it for dinner.
Remember to unthaw the vegetables before cooking them.
The meat needs to unthaw overnight in the refrigerator.

Related Terms

  • antonym: freeze - to turn into ice or another solid as a result of extreme cold
  • synonym: thaw - to become or cause to become unfrozen; to melt
