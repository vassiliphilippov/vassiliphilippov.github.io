HSV-1

noun
herpes simplex virus type 1; causes oral herpes
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪtʃ ˌɛs ˈvi wʌn / (AmE), / ˌeɪtʃ ˌɛs ˈvi wʌn / (BrE)
a herpes simplex virus that primarily causes oral herpes, characterized by cold sores or fever blisters around the mouth

Examples

HSV-1 virus infection
Doctors often test for HSV-1 when a patient presents with cold sores.
Most cases of oral herpes are caused by HSV-1 infection.
The spread of HSV-1 can be reduced by avoiding direct contact during outbreaks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: herpes simplex virus - group of viruses causing herpes infections
