outlawry

noun
state of being an outlaw; act of outlawing
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtˌlɔːri / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌlɑːri / (BrE)
the state of being an outlaw or the act of declaring someone an outlaw, especially as punishment for criminal activity; defiance of the law or legal prohibition

Examples

The outlawry of criminals
After his outlawry, he lived in hiding to evade capture by the authorities.
The act of outlawry was once a common punishment for fugitives in medieval England.
The novel explores a life of outlawry on the fringes of society.

Related Terms

  • antonym: legality - the quality or state of being in accordance with the law
  • synonym: outlaw - a person who has been declared outside the protection of the law
