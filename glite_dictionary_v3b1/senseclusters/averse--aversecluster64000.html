averse

adjective
strongly opposed; having a dislike to
6.4 2.04/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàv…ùs / (AmE), / …ôÀàv…úÀês / (BrE)
strongly opposed or having a strong dislike (especially when followed by 'to')

Examples

To be averse to risk
He is not averse to trying new foods, despite his reputation as a picky eater.
Many people are averse to change, preferring routines they know.
She was averse to the idea of traveling alone at night.

Synonyms

  • antipathetic
    8.4 0.07/m

  • antipathetical
    9.2 0.00/m
    antipathetical means being directly opposed or incompatible in nature, while averse describes a strong dislike often followed by 'to'.
