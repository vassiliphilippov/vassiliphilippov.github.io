factorage

noun
fee to selling agent
Pronunciation: / ˈfæktərɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈfæktərɪdʒ / (BrE)
a commission or fee paid to a factor for selling goods on someone else's behalf

Examples

Factorage fee
The merchant deducted the factorage before sending the profits to the owner.
After paying the factorage, the exporter received the remaining balance from the sale.
The contract specified the amount of factorage owed to the agent for each transaction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brokerage - the fee charged by a broker for acting as an agent
  • synonym: commission - a fee paid to an agent for services, especially selling goods
