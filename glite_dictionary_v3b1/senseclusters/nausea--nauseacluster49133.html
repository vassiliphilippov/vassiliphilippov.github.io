nausea

noun
sense of disgust causing sickness
3.0 5.55/m
Pronunciation: / Ààn…îzi…ô / (AmE), / Ààn…îÀêzi…ô / (BrE)
a feeling of sickness in your stomach that makes you feel like you might vomit, often caused by illness, motion, or disgust

Examples

A wave of nausea
He felt a wave of nausea at the sight of the spoiled food.
His comments induced a sense of nausea in everyone present.
The corruption in the organization filled her with nausea.
He struggled with nausea throughout the long bus ride.
She felt a sudden wave of nausea after the boat started rocking.
The medication may help reduce nausea caused by chemotherapy.

Synonyms

  • sickness
    1.0 2.74/m
    sickness often means a general illness or feeling of wanting to vomit, while nausea is the specific queasy stomach sensation before vomiting.
  • queasiness
    5.8 0.26/m
    queasiness is a mild, uneasy stomach feeling, while nausea is a stronger, more intense sensation that often makes you want to vomit.

Related Terms

  • antonym: delight - a feeling of great pleasure and satisfaction, the opposite of disgust
  • degree lower: dizziness - a sensation of spinning or losing one's balance
  • synonym: revulsion - a strong feeling of disgust or repulsion
  • degree higher: vomiting - the act of ejecting stomach contents through the mouth
