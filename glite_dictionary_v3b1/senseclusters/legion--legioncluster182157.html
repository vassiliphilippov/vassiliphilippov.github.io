legion

noun
large military unit
6.7 2.30/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàliÀê.d í…ôn /; / ÀàliÀêd í…ôn / (AmE), / ÀàliÀê.d í…ôn /; / ÀàliÀêd í…ôn / (BrE)
a large unit of the army, especially in ancient Rome

Examples

Roman legion
During ancient times, a single legion could control vast territories.
The Roman legion was renowned for its discipline and training.
The general led his legion into battle with great confidence.
In ancient Rome, a legion could comprise up to six thousand soldiers.
Medieval chronicles often use the word legion for any large army.
The commander led his entire legion into battle at dawn.
