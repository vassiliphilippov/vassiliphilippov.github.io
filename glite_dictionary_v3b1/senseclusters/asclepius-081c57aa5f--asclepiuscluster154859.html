Asclepius

proper noun
god of medicine in mythology
9.9 0.09/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàskliÀêpi.…ôs / (AmE), / …ôÀàskliÀêpi.…ôs / (BrE)
In Greek and Roman mythology, the god of medicine and healing, son of Apollo.

Examples

Asclepius god of medicine
Legends say that Asclepius could even bring the dead back to life.
Many ancient temples were dedicated to Asclepius, where people sought healing.
The staff of Asclepius remains a symbol of medicine today.

Synonyms

  • Asklepios
    9.9 0.01/m
    Asklepios is the original Greek name for the god of healing, while Asclepius is the common English form used in modern texts.
  • Aesculapius
    9.9 0.03/m
    Aesculapius is the Roman form of the medicine god, while Asclepius is the Greek figure he is based on.
