-izing

x
gerund-forming suffix
7.0
Pronunciation: / aɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / aɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
a suffix used to form nouns (gerunds) from verbs ending in -ize, indicating the action or process of the verb

Examples

The -izing of change
In the word 'organizing', the -izing ending shows the action of making something organized.
The process of 'modernizing' is turned into a noun by adding -izing to the verb 'modernize'.
Linguists study how -izing creates new words that describe actions or processes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: -ing - a suffix forming present participles and gerunds from verbs
