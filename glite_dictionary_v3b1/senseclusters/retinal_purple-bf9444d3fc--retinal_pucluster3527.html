retinal purple

noun
red photopigment in vertebrate retinas
3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛtənəl ˈpɜːrpəl / (AmE), / ˈrɛtɪnəl ˈpɜːpl / (BrE)
a red photopigment found in the rods of vertebrate retinas, responsible for the first step in the perception of light; also known as rhodopsin.

Examples

Retinal purple pigment
Exposure to bright light causes retinal purple in the eye to break down rapidly.
Scientists studied how retinal purple regenerates after being bleached by intense illumination.
The discovery of retinal purple was crucial in understanding how vision works at night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rhodopsin - the visual pigment found in the rods of the retina; identical to retinal purple
