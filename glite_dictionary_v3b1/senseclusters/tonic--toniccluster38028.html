tonic

adjective
using tone to distinguish word meanings (linguistics)
9.3 7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈtɑː.nɪk/ (AmE), /ˈtɒ.nɪk/ (BrE)
(of a language) employing variations in pitch or tone to distinguish meanings of otherwise similar words.

Examples

Tonic language
In tonic languages, the same syllable pronounced with different pitches can have different meanings.
Mandarin is a tonic language, meaning the pitch or intonation changes the meaning of words.
Many African languages are classified as tonic because their word meanings depend on tone.
