apodictic proposition

noun
a logically certain proposition
9.9
Pronunciation: / æpəˈdɪktɪk ˌprɑpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌapəˈdɪktɪk ˌprɒpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
A statement or proposition that is necessarily true or logically certain, leaving no room for doubt or contradiction.

Examples

An apodictic proposition statement
He argued that the law of non-contradiction is an example of an apodictic proposition.
Mathematical axioms are often considered apodictic propositions because they are self-evidently true.
The philosopher distinguished an apodictic proposition from one that was merely probable.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: assertoric proposition - a statement that asserts something as factual but is not necessarily logically certain
  • degree lower: problematic proposition - a proposition that is possible but neither necessary nor certain
