preventively

adverb
to avoid problems
Pronunciation: / prɪˈvɛntɪvli / (AmE), / prɪˈvɛntɪvli / (BrE)
in a way that is intended to stop something from happening before it occurs

Examples

Act preventively early
The government acted preventively to stop the spread of the disease.
She took medicine preventively before traveling to a high-risk area.
Engineers inspected the bridge preventively to ensure its safety.

Related Terms

  • synonym: proactively - in a way that takes action in advance to deal with an expected difficulty
  • antonym: reactively - in a way that responds to events after they have happened
