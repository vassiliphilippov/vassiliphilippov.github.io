spumy

adjective
full of foam or bubbles
9.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàspjuÀêmi / (AmE), / ÀàspjuÀêmi / (BrE)
full of or producing foam or froth; emitting or covered with bubbles, as from carbonation or agitation

Examples

Spumy beer head
He poured the beer quickly, resulting in a spumy head overflowing the glass.
The spumy surface of the river glistened in the afternoon sunlight.
The waves crashed against the rocks, sending spumy spray into the air.

Synonyms

  • frothy
    3.2 0.80/m
    frothy is light and full of foam on drinks or beer, while spumy means covered with rough froth after strong agitation.
  • foamy
    2.2 0.49/m
    foamy is thick and covered with a mass of bubbles in many liquids, while spumy often suggests froth from strong agitation.
  • bubbly
    3.2 1.48/m
    bubbly contains many rising bubbles, often in carbonated drinks, while spumy emphasizes foam or froth from churning.
