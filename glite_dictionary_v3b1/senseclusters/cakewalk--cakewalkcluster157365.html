cakewalk

noun
strutting dance for a cake prize
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪkˌwɔk / (AmE), / ˈkeɪkˌwɔːk / (BrE)
a lively, strutting dance developed in the southern United States, originally performed by Black dancers in competitions to win a cake, and later popularized in minstrel shows

Examples

A lively cakewalk
In the late 1800s, the cakewalk became a popular feature of minstrel shows.
The performers delighted the audience with an energetic cakewalk during the final act.
Their cakewalk costumes were flamboyant, reflecting the dance’s origins in showmanship and competition.
