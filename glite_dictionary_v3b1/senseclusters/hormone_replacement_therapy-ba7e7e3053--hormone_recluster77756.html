hormone replacement therapy

noun
treatment using hormones after menopause
3.9 3.5
Pronunciation: /ˈhɔːr.moʊn rɪ.ˈpleɪs.mənt ˈθer.ə.piː/ /; / /ˈhɔːr.mən rɪ.ˈpleɪs.mənt ˈθer.ə.pɪ/ (AmE), /ˈhɔː.məʊn rɪ.ˈpleɪs.mənt ˈθe.rə.piː/ (BrE)
a medical treatment in which hormones, especially estrogen and progestin, are given to postmenopausal women to alleviate symptoms of menopause or reduce health risks such as osteoporosis

Examples

Hormone replacement therapy for menopause
After beginning hormone replacement therapy, she noticed a significant decrease in her menopausal symptoms.
Concerns about long-term side effects have led some women to avoid hormone replacement therapy.
Some doctors discuss the risks and benefits of hormone replacement therapy with their patients before starting the treatment.
