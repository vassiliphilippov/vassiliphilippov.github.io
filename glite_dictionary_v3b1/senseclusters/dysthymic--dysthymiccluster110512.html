dysthymic

noun
person with mild depression
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈθaɪmɪk / (AmE), / dɪsˈθaɪmɪk / (BrE)
a person who has dysthymia, a chronic, mild form of depression

Examples

A dysthymic patient
The dysthymic found it difficult to stay motivated at work despite ongoing treatment.
Support groups can help a dysthymic feel less isolated and more understood.
Her doctor explained that a dysthymic may experience symptoms for many years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: depressive - a person suffering from depression
  • antonym: optimist - a person who tends to be hopeful and confident
