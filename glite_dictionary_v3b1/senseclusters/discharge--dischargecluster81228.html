discharge

noun
release or pouring out of a fluid
2.0 3.6
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈtʃɑrdʒ /; / ˈdɪs.tʃɑːrdʒ /; / ˈdɪs.tʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / dɪsˈtʃɑːdʒ /; / ˈdɪs.tʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
a substance or material that comes out from something, especially as waste or as a result of a process

Examples

A thick discharge
After the infection, the wound began to show a thick discharge.
Eye irritation can be caused by an abnormal discharge from the tear ducts.
The factory was fined for the illegal discharge of chemicals into the river.
After the heated argument, she felt a discharge of pent-up frustration.
Safety procedures were put in place to control the discharge of pressure from the equipment.
The discharge of toxic fumes from the factory was quickly detected by nearby residents.
After the surgery, she noticed a small amount of clear discharge near the incision.
Excessive vaginal discharge can be a sign of infection.
The doctor examined the unusual discharge from the patient's ear.

  • synonym: emission - the act of sending out something such as gas or liquid
  • synonym: release - the action of letting something out
  • antonym: restraint - the act of holding something back or controlling it
  • synonym: secretion - the process by which substances are produced and discharged by cells or glands
