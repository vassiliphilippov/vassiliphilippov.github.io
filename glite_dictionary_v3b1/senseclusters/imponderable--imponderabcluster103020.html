imponderable

adjective
difficult to measure or estimate precisely
8.2
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɑːnd(ə)rəbl̩ / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɒnd(ə)rəbl̩ / (BrE)
difficult or impossible to estimate, assess, or evaluate with precision due to being unknown or indeterminate.

Examples

Imponderable risks
Investors must account for imponderable risks that cannot be precisely calculated.
The outcome of the experiment depended on several imponderable variables.
There are always imponderable factors that make predictions uncertain in such situations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: measurable - able to be measured or precisely assessed
