impossible

adjective
not able to happen or exist
1.9
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɑːsəbəl /; / ɪmˈpɑsəbl̩ / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɒsəbl /; / ɪmˈpɒsəbl̩ / (BrE)
not able to happen, exist, or be done; totally not possible

Examples

An impossible task
It seemed impossible to finish the project on time, but the team persevered.
The mountain looked impossible to climb, but she reached the summit after days of effort.
Winning the lottery twice is almost impossible for the average person.
Climbing that mountain in one hour is impossible for most people.
It seemed impossible to finish the project by Friday, but they managed it.
She faced an impossible decision and didn't know what to do.
