Gestalt psychology

noun
psychological theory focusing on holistic perception
7.0
Pronunciation: / ɡəˈstɑlt saɪˈkɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ɡəˈʃtælt saɪˈkɒlədʒi / (BrE)
a theory of psychology that emphasizes the importance of perceiving whole structures, rather than only their individual parts

Examples

Gestalt psychology theory
Her professor recommended several texts on Gestalt psychology for deeper understanding of human cognition.
Many principles from Gestalt psychology are applied in modern design to enhance user experience.
The study of perception is greatly influenced by Gestalt psychology and its holistic approach.
