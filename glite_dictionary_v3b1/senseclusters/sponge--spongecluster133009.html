sponge

noun
aquatic animal with porous body
Pronunciation: / spʌndʒ / (AmE), / spʌndʒ / (BrE)
A primitive multicellular aquatic animal with a porous body supported by a fibrous skeleton, typically living in sessile colonies in the sea.

Examples

Sea sponge colony
Divers found a large sponge attached to the coral reef.
Scientists study the structure of the sponge to understand early animal evolution.
The sponge filters water to obtain its food from plankton and other particles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: poriferan - animal phylum including sponges
