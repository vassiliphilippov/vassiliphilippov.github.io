deregulatory

adjective
reducing regulations
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːrɛɡjəˈleɪtəri / (AmE), / ˌdiːrɪˈɡjʊlətəri / (BrE)
relating to the reduction or removal of government rules or controls

Examples

Deregulatory policies
The new deregulatory policies made it easier for small businesses to enter the market.
Critics argue that deregulatory actions can sometimes lead to less consumer protection.
A deregulatory approach was adopted to stimulate economic growth in the region.

Related Terms

  • synonym: liberalizing - making rules or restrictions less strict
  • antonym: regulatory - relating to the creation or enforcement of rules and laws
