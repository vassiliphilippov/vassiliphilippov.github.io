Dutch hoe

noun
garden hoe designed to be pushed
Pronunciation: / dʌtʃ hoʊ / (AmE), / dʌtʃ həʊ / (BrE)
A type of gardening hoe with a flat blade, designed to be pushed rather than pulled to cut weeds just below the soil surface.

Examples

A Dutch hoe tool
She prefers to use a Dutch hoe for light surface weeding in her flower beds.
The Dutch hoe makes it easy to remove weeds from between vegetable rows.
With a sharp Dutch hoe, you can quickly slice through weeds without disturbing the soil too much.

Related Terms

  • antonym: draw hoe - a hoe designed to be pulled toward the user
