ghost weed

noun
a toxic western US spurge plant
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊst wid / (AmE), / ɡəʊst wiːd / (BrE)
A species of spurge native to the western United States, known for its showy white-bracted flower clusters and highly poisonous milky sap.

Examples

Ghost weed plant
Hikers are warned not to touch ghost weed because of its poisonous properties.
The milky sap of the ghost weed can be highly toxic to livestock.
We found a patch of ghost weed near the canyon, blooming with pale white bracts.
