objectionable

adjective
causing offense or disapproval
7.5 5.4
Pronunciation: / əbˈdʒɛkʃənəbəl /; / əbˈdʒɛkʃənəbl / (AmE), / əbˈdʒɛkʃənəbl̩ /; / əbˈdʒɛkʃ(ə)nəbl / (BrE)
causing offense, disapproval, or protest; unpleasant or unacceptable

Examples

Objectionable remarks
He made some objectionable remarks during the meeting, which caused a heated discussion.
Many parents found the language in the song objectionable for young children.
The movie contained several objectionable scenes that were edited out for television.
It is important to speak up if you witness objectionable behavior in the workplace.
Some viewers found the comedian's jokes objectionable and complained to the television station.
The committee removed all objectionable material from the final report before publication.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acceptable - able to be tolerated or allowed; not objectionable
  • synonym: offensive - causing someone to feel deeply hurt, upset, or angry
  • antonym: unobjectionable - not likely to cause objection; acceptable
  • synonym: unpleasant - not enjoyable or nice; causing discomfort
