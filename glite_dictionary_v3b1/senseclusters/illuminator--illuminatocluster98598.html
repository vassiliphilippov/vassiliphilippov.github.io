illuminator

noun
manuscript artist
8.6
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈluː.mə.neɪ.tər/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈluː.mə.neɪ.tə/ (BrE)
an artist, especially in the Middle Ages, who decorates manuscripts with elaborate illustrations and gold leaf

Examples

Medieval illuminator
The illuminator added vibrant colors and gold leaf to the medieval manuscript.
Each page was carefully decorated by a skilled illuminator in the monastery.
Historians admire the detailed work of the illuminator in ancient religious texts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scribe - a person who copies out documents, especially in the past
