phenomenism

noun
philosophy of appearances
Pronunciation: / fəˈnɑːməˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / fəˈnɒmɪˌnɪzəm / (BrE)
the philosophical theory that only sensory experiences (phenomena) exist, and that physical objects have no existence or meaning independent of perception

Examples

Phenomenism in philosophy
According to phenomenism, the world we know is only a collection of our sensory experiences.
Many philosophers have debated whether phenomenism can fully explain the existence of physical objects.
His essay argues that phenomenism challenges the idea of a mind-independent reality.
In philosophy class, we discussed how phenomenism challenges the idea of an objective reality.
Some thinkers argue that phenomenism cannot fully explain the existence of the external world.
According to phenomenism, our knowledge of objects is limited to what we perceive through our senses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: phenomenalism - the view that physical objects exist only as perceptual phenomena
  • antonym: realism - the belief that objects exist independently of perception
