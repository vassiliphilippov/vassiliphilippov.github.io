carotin

noun
plant pigment converted to vitamin A
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛrəˌtɪn / (AmE), / ˈkærətɪn / (BrE)
an orange-colored compound found in plants that is converted to vitamin A in the liver

Examples

Orange carotin compound
Deficiency in carotin may lead to vision problems if not supplemented with other sources of vitamin A.
Scientists are studying the way carotin is metabolized in different plant species.
Spinach contains high levels of carotin, which the body can convert into vitamin A.

  • synonym: carotene - pigment in plants and precursor to vitamin A
