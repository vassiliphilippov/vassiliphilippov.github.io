Worn out or ruined because of age or neglect; in poor condition due to long use or lack of maintenance.

Examples

Decrepit building
He drove a decrepit car that barely made it up the hill.

Synonyms

    6.2 1.62/m
    dilapidated is in a state of ruin and disrepair, while decrepit is older and more damaged from long neglect.
  • tumbledown
    5.6 0.00/m
    tumbledown describes a building that looks ready to fall, while decrepit describes something very worn or ruined by age or neglect.
  • run-down
    4.5 1.04/m
    run-down describes buildings or areas that are neglected and shabby, while decrepit stresses severe decay from long use or lack of care.
  • broken-down
    4.4 1.08/m
    broken-down describes machines or vehicles that no longer work, while decrepit usually refers to extreme age or neglect in objects or buildings.
  • worn-out
    3.1 1.46/m
    worn-out means too used or tired to be useful, while decrepit suggests damage from age or long neglect beyond simple wear.
  • outworn
    5.2 0.00/m
    outworn describes things frayed or no longer useful from long use, while decrepit implies serious ruin from age or neglect.
