scintillate

verb
be lively or brilliant; sparkle
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪn.tɪˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪn.tɪ.leɪt / (BrE)
to be lively, brilliant, or exceptionally skillful; to sparkle intellectually or artistically

Examples

Her ideas scintillate
Her wit tends to scintillate at dinner parties, making everyone laugh and think.
The pianist’s fingers scintillated across the keys in a dazzling display of technique.
Their conversation truly scintillated, keeping guests engaged late into the night.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dull - lacking liveliness, brilliance, or interest
  • synonym: sparkle - to be lively or brilliant; to shine brightly
