philologically

adverb
in a linguistic manner
Pronunciation: / ˌfɪləˈlɑdʒɪkli / (AmE), / ˌfɪləˈlɒdʒɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to the study of language, its history, and development

Examples

Studied philologically
The ancient text was examined philologically to understand its original meaning.
She approached the translation philologically, considering the historical context of each word.
The professor discussed the poem philologically, focusing on its linguistic evolution.

Related Terms

  • synonym: linguistically - in a way that relates to language or linguistics
