diver

noun
large Northern Hemisphere diving bird; loon
8.3 4.6
Pronunciation: /ˈdaɪ.vər/ (AmE), /ˈdaɪ.və/ (BrE)
a large, somewhat primitive, fish-eating diving bird of the Northern Hemisphere, with webbed feet placed far back on the body; related to the grebes, also known as a loon

Examples

Great northern diver
In Europe, the great northern diver is a rare but impressive visitor.
Naturalists watched a diver disappear beneath the surface in search of fish.
The common diver can be seen on remote northern lakes during the breeding season.
