sloth

noun
laziness; deadly sin of inactivity
4.8
Pronunciation: / sloʊθ / (AmE), / sləʊθ / (BrE)
laziness or unwillingness to work or make an effort

Examples

Deadly sloth
In medieval times, sloth was condemned as a serious moral failing.
Overcoming sloth requires both discipline and a sense of purpose.
The church warns that sloth can be just as destructive as anger or greed.
Despite his intelligence, his sloth prevented him from achieving more.
His sloth made it difficult for him to keep a steady job.
Teachers often warn against sloth as a barrier to academic success.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diligence - careful and persistent work or effort
  • antonym: industriousness - the quality of being diligent and hardworking
  • synonym: laziness - lack of willingness to work or use energy
