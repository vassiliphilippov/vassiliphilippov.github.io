involuntarily

adverb
without choice or conscious intention
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌvɑlənˈtɛrəli / (AmE), / ɪnˌvɒlənˈtɛərəli / (BrE)
in a way that is not done by choice or conscious intention; against one's will or automatically

Examples

To involuntarily flinch
He involuntarily flinched when the balloon burst nearby.
She involuntarily smiled at the memory of her childhood.
The suspect was involuntarily committed to a psychiatric hospital.

Related Terms

  • antonym: voluntarily - by choice or of one's own free will
