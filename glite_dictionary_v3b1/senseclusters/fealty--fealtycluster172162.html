fealty

noun
formal loyalty or allegiance
9.9 0.69/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàfiÀê…ôlti / (AmE), / ÀàfiÀê…ôlti / (BrE)
loyalty and duty owed by a subject to a sovereign or by a citizen to a country, especially in a formal or historical context

Examples

Fealty to a king
During the ceremony, citizens affirmed their fealty to the new government.
Many nobles displayed their fealty through acts of service and sacrifice.
The knight swore fealty to his king, pledging loyalty above all else.

Synonyms

  • allegiance
    5.6 9.44/m
    allegiance shows a person's pledge to a leader or country, while fealty is a formal, often historical duty to a sovereign.
  • fidelity
    4.9 2.82/m
    fidelity means strict faithfulness to promises or duties, while fealty is the pledged loyalty and duty to a ruler or country.
  • loyalty
    1.3 23/m
    loyalty is a general feeling of devotion to people or ideas, while fealty is a formal duty pledged to a sovereign.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allegiance - loyalty or commitment to a group or cause
  • antonym: treason - betrayal of one's country or sovereign
