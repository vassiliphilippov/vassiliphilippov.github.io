fealty

noun
formal loyalty or allegiance
Pronunciation: / ˈfiːəlti / (AmE), / ˈfiːəlti / (BrE)
loyalty and duty owed by a subject to a sovereign or by a citizen to a country, especially in a formal or historical context

Examples

Fealty to a king
During the ceremony, citizens affirmed their fealty to the new government.
Many nobles displayed their fealty through acts of service and sacrifice.
The knight swore fealty to his king, pledging loyalty above all else.

Related Terms

  • antonym: treason - betrayal of one's country or sovereign
