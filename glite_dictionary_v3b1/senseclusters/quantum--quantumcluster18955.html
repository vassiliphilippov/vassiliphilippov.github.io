quantum

noun
smallest unit in quantum theory
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɑn.təm / (AmE), / ˈkwɒn.təm / (BrE)
the smallest discrete quantity of a physical property that a system can possess, as defined in quantum theory

Examples

Quantum theory unit
In physics, energy is often transferred in discrete quantum units known as photons.
Scientists discovered that light behaves as both a wave and a collection of quantum particles.
The quantum of action in quantum mechanics is represented by Planck’s constant.
