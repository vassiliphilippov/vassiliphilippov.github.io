constitution

noun
fundamental law or principles of a government
5.1 33/m
Pronunciation: / Àåk…ëÀênst…™ÀàtuÀê É…ôn / (AmE), / Àåk…ínst…™ÀàtjuÀê É…ôn / (BrE)
The system of fundamental principles or established precedents according to which a state or organization is governed, especially as embodied in a written document.

Examples

The constitution of a country
Amending a constitution often requires a special legislative process.
Many countries have written their own constitutions to outline the structure of their governments.
The United States Constitution established the framework for the federal government.

Synonyms

  • fundamental law
    5.6 0.15/m
    fundamental law is the set of core legal rules that set a state's framework, while constitution is the formal written document that records them.
  • organic law
    7.9 0.03/m
    organic law is a legal rule that creates a government's main structure, while constitution is the complete written charter used to govern the state.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: amendment - a change or addition to a legal or statutory document, such as a constitution
  • synonym: charter - a formal written document that defines rights and privileges, especially of an organization or institution
  • degree lower: regulation - a rule or directive made and maintained by an authority
  • synonym: statute - a written law passed by a legislative body
