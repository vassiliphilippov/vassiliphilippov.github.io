dirgy

adjective
mournful or solemn
Pronunciation: / ˈdɜrdʒi / (AmE), / ˈdɜːdʒi / (BrE)
having a mournful, solemn, or funeral-like quality, especially in sound or atmosphere

Examples

A dirgy melody
The slow, dirgy melody filled the room with a sense of sorrow.
After the loss, their conversations took on a dirgy tone.
The film's soundtrack was so dirgy that it made the scenes even more somber.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic
  • synonym: funereal - having the mournful, somber character of a funeral
