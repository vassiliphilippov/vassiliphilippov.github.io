floppy

adjective
hanging loosely; limp
3.7 2.71/m
Pronunciation: / Ààfl…ëÀêpi / (AmE), / Ààfl…ípi / (BrE)
soft and hanging down loosely, often in a way that appears limp or flexible.

Examples

A floppy hat
After being washed, the old stuffed rabbit became even more floppy.
She wore a large, floppy hat to protect herself from the sun.
The dog's floppy ears bounced as it ran.

Synonyms

  • dangly
    3.9 0.13/m
    dangly is hanging loosely and can swing freely from its support, while floppy is soft and hangs down without shape.
  • saggy
    3.5 0.41/m
    saggy is drooping and loose, often from age or wear, while floppy is soft and hangs down with a limp look.
  • limpy
    7.9 0.01/m
    limpy is weak or lacking firmness, often about movement or ideas, while floppy is soft and hangs down physically.
