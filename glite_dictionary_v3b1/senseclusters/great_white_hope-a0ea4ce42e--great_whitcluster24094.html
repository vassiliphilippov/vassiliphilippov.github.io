great white hope

noun
person/thing expected to succeed greatly
5.9
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪt waɪt hoʊp / (AmE), / ɡreɪt waɪt həʊp / (BrE)
a person or thing that is expected to achieve great success or bring significant progress in a particular field, often where others have not succeeded

Examples

The great white hope emerges
After years of mediocre results, the new CEO was seen as the great white hope for the struggling company.
Many consider the young scientist the great white hope of cancer research.
The team's promising rookie has quickly become their great white hope for a championship title.
