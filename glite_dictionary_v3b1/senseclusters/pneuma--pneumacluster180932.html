pneuma

noun
breath or spirit
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈnuː.mə/ /; / /ˈn.juː.mə/ (AmE), /ˈn.juː.mə/ (BrE)
the vital spirit, soul, breath, or animating force that gives life, especially in ancient philosophy and medicine

Examples

The vital pneuma
In ancient philosophy, the pneuma was believed to be the vital force that animated living beings.
The healer spoke of the pneuma as the breath of life connecting body and soul.
He could feel the cool pneuma of the sea breeze against his face at dawn.
In ancient medical texts, pneuma was believed to be the force that animated the body.
The Stoics considered pneuma to be the principle that organized and sustained the universe.
Some philosophers debated whether pneuma was distinct from the soul or simply another name for it.
