Polonius

proper noun
Hamlet character
9.5
Pronunciation: / pəˈloʊniəs / (AmE), / pəˈləʊniəs / (BrE)
a character in Shakespeare's play Hamlet, known for being talkative, meddling, and giving advice

Examples

Shakespeare's Polonius
In Shakespeare's play, Polonius is often seen giving long speeches and advice to his children.
Many readers remember Polonius for his famous line, 'To thine own self be true.'
The character of Polonius is both comic and tragic in the story of Hamlet.
