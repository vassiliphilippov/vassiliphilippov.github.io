degenerative arthritis

noun
type of arthritis with cartilage breakdown
5.0 4.4
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈdʒen.ə.rə.tɪv ɑːr.ˈθraɪ.tɪs/ /; / /dɪ.ˈdʒen.ə.rə.tɪv ɑːr.ˈθraɪ.təs/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈdʒen.ə.rə.tɪv ɑː.ˈθraɪ.tɪs/ /; / /dɪ.ˈdʒen.ə.rə.tɪv ɑː.ˈθraɪ.təs/ (BrE)
A chronic joint disorder characterized by the breakdown of cartilage, typically occurring after middle age; also called osteoarthritis.

Examples

Degenerative arthritis pain
Her doctor diagnosed her with degenerative arthritis after she began experiencing persistent knee pain.
People with degenerative arthritis may find it difficult to perform everyday activities such as walking or climbing stairs.
Treatment for degenerative arthritis often focuses on pain management and improving joint function.
