purchasable

adjective
capable of being corrupted or bribed
Pronunciation: / ˈpɜrtʃəsəbəl / (AmE), / ˈpɜːtʃəsəbl / (BrE)
(of a person, position, or honor) susceptible to being bribed or corrupted; able to be bought in a dishonorable way.

Examples

Purchasable officials
Critics accused the entire committee of being purchasable and lacking integrity.
It was widely believed that the purchasable official had accepted bribes to overlook the violations.
She refused to work in a system where judgments were purchasable rather than based on justice.
