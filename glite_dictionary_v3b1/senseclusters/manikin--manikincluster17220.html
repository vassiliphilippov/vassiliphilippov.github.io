manikin

noun
very small person (not deformed)
Pronunciation: / ˈmænɪkɪn / (AmE), / ˈmanɪkɪn / (BrE)
a person who is very small in stature but is not otherwise deformed or abnormal

Examples

A small manikin
Despite being a manikin, she commanded respect with her presence.
In the crowd, the manikin struggled to see over the heads of others.
The character was described as a manikin, hardly reaching the height of the table.

  • degree higher: dwarf - a person of unusually small stature, often due to a medical condition
