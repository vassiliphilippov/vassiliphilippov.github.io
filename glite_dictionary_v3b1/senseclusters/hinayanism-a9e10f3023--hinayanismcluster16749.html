Hinayanism

noun
doctrine of Hinayana Buddhism
9.9
Pronunciation: / hɪˈnɑːjənɪzəm / (AmE), / hɪˈnɑːjənɪzəm / (BrE)
The religious doctrine or system of Hinayana Buddhism, characterized by its focus on personal salvation and monastic discipline, traditionally contrasted with Mahayana Buddhism.

Examples

Hinayanism religious doctrine
He wrote a thesis on the rituals practiced in Hinayanism during the early centuries CE.
Many scholars no longer use the term Hinayanism due to its negative connotations.
The comparative study of Hinayanism and Mahayanism reveals important doctrinal differences.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Mahayana - another major school of Buddhist thought
  • synonym: Theravada - major surviving form of Hinayana Buddhism
