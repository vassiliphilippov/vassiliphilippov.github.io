Navy Department

proper noun
former U.S. government department for naval affairs
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈneɪvi dɪˌpɑrtmənt / (AmE), / ˈneɪvi dɪˌpɑːtmənt / (BrE)
the former executive department of the United States government responsible for naval affairs, created in 1798 and merged with the Department of War to form the Department of Defense in 1947

Examples

The Navy Department archives
He found archived documents from the Navy Department while researching American military history.
The Navy Department played a crucial role during World War II before its functions were merged in 1947.
Until 1947, the Navy Department oversaw the development of the U.S. Navy.
