Plimsoll

noun
ship's load line marking
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈplɪmˌsɔl / (AmE), / ˈplɪmsəl / (BrE)
a line marked on the hull of a ship to indicate the maximum safe draft and therefore the legal limit to which a ship may be loaded

Examples

The Plimsoll line
If the water rises above the Plimsoll, the ship could be dangerously overloaded.
Inspectors checked the Plimsoll before allowing the vessel to depart the port.
Marine law requires every cargo vessel to have a visible Plimsoll marked on its hull.

Related Terms

  • synonym: draft mark - marks showing the depth of a ship in the water
  • synonym: load line - the line on a ship's hull indicating the maximum safe draft
