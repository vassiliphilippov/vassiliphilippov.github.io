fixed

adjective
unchanging; not variable
1.0
Pronunciation: / fɪkst / (AmE), / fɪkst / (BrE)
not changing or able to be changed; remaining the same (such as a value, rate, or condition)

Examples

Fixed price
A fixed interest rate does not change throughout the term of the loan.
In this experiment, we kept the temperature fixed while varying the pressure.
The company pays employees a fixed monthly salary regardless of hours worked.
Many retirees rely on their fixed incomes to cover daily expenses.
The company offered a fixed price for the entire construction project.
The terms of the contract are fixed and cannot be negotiated.

Related Terms

  • synonym: constant - a value or quantity that does not change
  • antonym: flexible - able to change or be modified easily
  • antonym: variable - (of a number, value, or quantity) able to change or vary
