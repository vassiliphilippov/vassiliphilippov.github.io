tolerance

noun
acceptance of differing beliefs or practices
1.0 3.1
Pronunciation: /ˈtɑː.lə.rəns/ (AmE), /ˈtɒ.lə.rəns/ (BrE)
the willingness or ability to accept or allow beliefs, opinions, or behaviors that are different from your own

Examples

Religious tolerance
A society that values tolerance encourages open discussion of different viewpoints.
Her tolerance of other religions made her a respected community leader.
Schools often promote tolerance to help students understand and appreciate diversity.
Her tolerance of different cultures made her a valued member of the community.
His level of tolerance towards noise surprised his friends.
There should be zero tolerance for bullying in schools.
A society that values tolerance encourages people to express diverse opinions.
Her tolerance of others' differences made her well respected in the community.
Teaching children about tolerance can help prevent prejudice and discrimination.
