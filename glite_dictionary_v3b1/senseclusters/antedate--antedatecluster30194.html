antedate

verb
occur or exist before a certain date
9.0 6.9
Pronunciation: /æn.tɪ.ˈdeɪt/ /; / /ˈæn.tɪ.deɪt/ (AmE), /æn.tɪ.ˈdeɪt/ /; / /ˈæn.tɪ.deɪt/ (BrE)
to happen or exist before something else in time, or to assign an earlier date to something

Examples

To antedate an event
Bronze artifacts in the area antedate those found elsewhere by several centuries.
The ruins clearly antedate the written records of the city.
This discovery may antedate what we believed about human migration.
Experts antedated the manuscript to the 15th century, earlier than previously believed.
Recent discoveries antidate the appearance of the species by thousands of years.
The researchers were able to antedate the earliest known use of the tool by several centuries.
