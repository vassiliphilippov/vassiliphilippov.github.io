liquefaction

noun
conversion of a solid or gas into liquid
6.1 7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌlɪkwəˈfækʃən / (AmE), / ˌlɪkwɪˈfækʃən / (BrE)
the process of converting a solid or a gas into a liquid

Examples

Liquefaction of gas
During the industrial process, the liquefaction of natural gas requires extremely low temperatures.
Scientists observed the liquefaction of ice as the temperature increased above freezing.
The liquefaction of carbon dioxide is used in making dry ice for shipping perishables.
