oblate

noun
lay person dedicated to religious life
Pronunciation: / ˈɑbleɪt / (AmE), / ˈɒbleɪt / (BrE)
A lay person who is formally dedicated to religious life or religious work, often affiliated with a monastery or religious community.

Examples

A monastery oblate
As an oblate, she participated in the daily prayers and work of the monastery.
He became an oblate to strengthen his spiritual practice without taking full monastic vows.
The monastery welcomed several new oblates who wished to dedicate their lives to service.

Related Terms

  • synonym: monk - a man who is a member of a religious community, especially one living under vows of poverty, chastity, and obedience
