marginal

adjective
barely profitable; just covering costs
6.2 4.65/m
Pronunciation: / Ààm…ërd í…™n…ôl / (AmE), / Ààm…ëÀêd í…™n…ôl / (BrE)
(of a business or activity) producing just enough income to cover production costs, with little or no profit.

Examples

Marginal business
Farmers operating marginal businesses often struggle to survive market changes.
Many marginal industries shut down during the economic downturn.
The company remained marginal until demand for its products increased.

Related Terms

  • antonym: profitable - making a profit; financially beneficial
  • synonym: unprofitable - not yielding profit or financial gain
