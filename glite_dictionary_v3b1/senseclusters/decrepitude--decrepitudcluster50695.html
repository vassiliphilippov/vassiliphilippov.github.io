decrepitude

noun
weakness or decay from old age
Pronunciation: / dɪˈkrɛpɪˌtud / (AmE), / dɪˈkrɛpɪtjuːd /; / dɪˈkrɛpɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
the state of being worn out, weak, or broken down, especially because of old age or long use

Examples

Signs of decrepitude
After decades of hard labor, his body showed signs of decrepitude.
Despite her decrepitude, she maintained a sharp and lively mind.
The abandoned mansion had fallen into a state of decrepitude over the years.
After decades of loyal service, the grand old piano showed clear signs of decrepitude.
The film portrayed the decrepitude that often accompanies advanced age with remarkable sensitivity.
The mansion's air of faded elegance was overshadowed by the decrepitude of its crumbling walls.

Related Terms

  • synonym: frailty - the condition of being weak or delicate
  • synonym: senility - the condition of being senile or showing the weaknesses of old age
  • antonym: vigor - physical strength and good health
