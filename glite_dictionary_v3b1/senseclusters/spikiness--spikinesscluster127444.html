spikiness

noun
sharp peaks in data
7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈspaɪ.kɪ.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈspaɪ.kɪ.nəs/ (BrE)
the quality of having sudden sharp peaks or brief extreme values, especially in data, signals, or measurements

Examples

Spikiness in data
The spikiness of the temperature readings made it difficult to identify a clear trend.
Engineers worked to reduce the spikiness in the signal for more accurate results.
The financial report highlighted the spikiness in revenue during the holiday season.
