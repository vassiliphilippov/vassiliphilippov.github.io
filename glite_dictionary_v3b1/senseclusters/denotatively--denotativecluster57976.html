denotatively

adverb
in literal sense
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːnoʊˈteɪtɪvli / (AmE), / ˌdiːnəʊˈteɪtɪvli / (BrE)
in a manner that refers to the explicit or direct meaning of a word or expression

Examples

Used denotatively in language
The term 'home' is used denotatively to mean a place where someone lives.
When interpreting the law, judges often read the words denotatively rather than figuratively.
In scientific writing, words are chosen denotatively to avoid confusion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: connotatively - in a way that relates to the suggested or associated meaning
  • synonym: literally - in a literal or exact sense
