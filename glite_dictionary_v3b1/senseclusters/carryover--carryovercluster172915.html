carryover

noun
transferred amount
Pronunciation: / ˈkæriˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈkæriˌəʊvə / (BrE)
something (such as an amount, item, or effect) that is transferred or continues from a previous period, situation, or activity to the present

Examples

Budget carryover
The budget committee discussed the carryover from last year's unspent funds.
There was a significant carryover of vacation days into the new year.
Some traditions are a carryover from earlier generations.
The carryover from last year's budget allowed the school to fund new computers.
Researchers must account for any carryover effects when designing clinical trials.
There was a noticeable carryover of stress from work into his personal life.
The budget included a carryover of funds that were not spent last year.
Some traditions are a carryover from ancient times and still influence us today.
There was a carryover of stress from work that affected his weekend.
