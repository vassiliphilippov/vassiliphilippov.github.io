compactification

noun
making space compact
9.9
Pronunciation: / kəmˌpæktɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / kəmˌpæktɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process in mathematics or physics of making a space or structure compact, often by adding points or limiting dimensions

Examples

The compactification process
The compactification of the real line adds a point at infinity to make it compact.
Mathematicians often use compactification to study spaces with better properties.
The Stone–Čech compactification is a common technique in topology.
In advanced mathematics, compactification helps to simplify the study of infinite spaces.
Physicists use compactification to explain how extra dimensions in string theory can be hidden from observation.
The compactification of the data set made it easier to analyze and visualize.
