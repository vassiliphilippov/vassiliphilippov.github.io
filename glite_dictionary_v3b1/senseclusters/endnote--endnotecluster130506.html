endnote

noun
final remark or comment
3.9
Pronunciation: /ˈend.noʊt/ (AmE), /ˈend.nəʊt/ (BrE)
a note or comment placed at the end of a book, article, or document to give extra information, references, or remarks

Examples

A endnote at book end
The author included a personal endnote to thank her mentors and supporters.
In his lecture, he finished with an inspiring endnote about the importance of perseverance.
The article's endnote summarized the main findings and suggested areas for future research.
The endnote provided a detailed explanation of the historical context mentioned in the chapter.
Readers can find all the references listed in the endnote section at the back of the book.
She added an endnote to clarify the source of her statistics.
The author included an endnote to explain the historical background of the event.
You can find the full list of references in the endnote section of the report.
Each chapter ends with an endnote that provides further reading suggestions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conclusion - the final part of something, especially a speech or piece of writing
  • synonym: footnote - a note at the bottom of a page giving extra information
  • antonym: introduction - the beginning section of a speech or written work
