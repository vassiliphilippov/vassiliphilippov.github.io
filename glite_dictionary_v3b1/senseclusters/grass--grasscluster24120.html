grass

verb
to cover land with grass
Pronunciation: / ɡræs / (AmE), / ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
to cover an area with grass, either by planting or by allowing it to grow naturally

Examples

To grass the yard
After construction ended, they grassed the site to prevent erosion.
The landscaper will grass the new playground before summer.
We need to grass the bare patches in the backyard to improve its appearance.
After the field was left fallow for two years it grassed quickly.
The hillside grassed over after the heavy rains in spring.
If you leave the garden unattended, it will soon become grassed and overgrown.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bare - to make or remain without vegetation
  • synonym: sod - to cover with turf (grass and soil together)
