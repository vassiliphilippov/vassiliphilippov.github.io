nondecision

noun
ruling without judgment
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑn.dɪˈsɪʒ.ən / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.dɪˈsɪʒ.ən / (BrE)
an official ruling in which a court or authority declines to decide the merits of a case, often for procedural or jurisdictional reasons

Examples

Court nondecision
The court issued a nondecision because it lacked jurisdiction over the matter.
After months of hearings, the regulatory agency's nondecision left both parties frustrated.
A nondecision can occur when procedural errors prevent a case from being fully considered.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dismissal - a court's decision to end a case without a judgment on the merits
  • antonym: judgment - a formal decision by a court on the merits of a case
