conopid

noun
thick-headed fly
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑnəpɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɒnəpɪd / (BrE)
a fly of the family Conopidae, commonly known as thick-headed flies, often parasitic on bees and wasps

Examples

A conopid fly
The entomologist identified the insect as a conopid based on its distinctive head shape.
Many species of conopid are known to parasitize bees and wasps.
A conopid can often be recognized by its narrow waist and thick head.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fly - an insect of the order Diptera with a single pair of wings
