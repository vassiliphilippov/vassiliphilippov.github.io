recompense

verb
pay or compensate
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛk.əmˌpɛns /; / ˈrɛkəmˌpɛns / (AmE), / ˈrɛk.əm.pɛns /; / ˈrɛkəmˌpɛns / (BrE)
to pay or reward someone for loss, harm, or effort; to compensate or make amends

Examples

To recompense someone
He hoped his honesty would recompense for his earlier mistakes.
She was recompensed for the damages caused by the storm.
The company agreed to recompense the workers for their extra hours.
After the mistake was discovered, the company agreed to recompense the affected customers.
He sought to recompense her for the damage he had accidentally caused.
The government promised to recompense the farmers for their losses caused by the drought.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compensate - to give payment or reward for loss or effort
  • antonym: punish - to subject to a penalty for wrongdoing
  • synonym: reward - to give something in recognition of service, effort, or achievement
