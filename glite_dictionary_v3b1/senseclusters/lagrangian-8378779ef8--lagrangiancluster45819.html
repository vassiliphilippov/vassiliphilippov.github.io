Lagrangian

noun
function in physics
9.9
Pronunciation: / ləˈɡrændʒiən / (AmE), / ləˈɡrɑːndʒiən / (BrE)
a function in physics or mathematics that summarizes the dynamics of a system, often used to derive equations of motion

Examples

The Lagrangian function
The Lagrangian is a key concept in classical mechanics and field theory.
Physicists use the Lagrangian to derive the equations that govern a system's motion.
In advanced physics courses, students learn how to construct a Lagrangian for different physical systems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Hamiltonian - a function used in physics to describe the total energy of a system
