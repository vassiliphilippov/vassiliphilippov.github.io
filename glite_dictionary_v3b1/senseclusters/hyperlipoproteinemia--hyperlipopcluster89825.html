hyperlipoproteinemia

noun
abnormally high blood lipoproteins and cholesterol
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərlɪpoʊˌproʊtiːˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəlɪpəʊˌprəʊtiːˈniːmiə / (BrE)
any of several disorders marked by abnormally high levels of lipoproteins and cholesterol in the blood

Examples

High hyperlipoproteinemia levels
Genetic factors may predispose individuals to develop hyperlipoproteinemia at an early age.
The patient was diagnosed with hyperlipoproteinemia after several blood tests showed elevated cholesterol levels.
Treating hyperlipoproteinemia often involves both dietary changes and medication.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hyperlipidemia - abnormally high levels of lipids in the blood
  • antonym: hypolipoproteinemia - abnormally low levels of lipoproteins in the blood
