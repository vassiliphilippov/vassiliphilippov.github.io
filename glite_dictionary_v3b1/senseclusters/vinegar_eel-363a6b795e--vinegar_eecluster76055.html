vinegar eel

noun
microscopic worm found in vinegar
9.1 9.3
Pronunciation: /ˈvɪn.ə.gər iːl/ /; / /ˈvɪn.ɪ.gər iːl/ (AmE), /ˈvɪn.ə.gə iːl/ /; / /ˈvɪn.ɪ.gə iːl/ (BrE)
a minute, free-living nematode (Turbatrix aceti) found in vinegar and other fermenting solutions, feeding on microorganisms involved in fermentation

Examples

Vinegar eels in vinegar
Despite their name, vinegar eels are harmless to humans and are sometimes used in biology classrooms for study.
Some home-brew enthusiasts may encounter vinegar eels if natural vinegar is left to ferment too long.
The laboratory technician examined the sample under a microscope and noticed several vinegar eels swimming actively.
