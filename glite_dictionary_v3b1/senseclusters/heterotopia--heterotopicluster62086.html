heterotopia

noun
alternative space concept
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛtərəˈtoʊpiə / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəˈtəʊpiə / (BrE)
a real or imagined place that exists outside or in contrast to normal social or cultural spaces, often used in philosophy and cultural studies

Examples

A cultural heterotopia
The museum was designed as a heterotopia, offering visitors a space apart from daily routines.
In his essay, Foucault describes the cemetery as a classic example of heterotopia in society.
Artists often create a heterotopia in their work to challenge conventional ideas of space and meaning.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dystopia - an imagined place where everything is unpleasant or bad
  • synonym: utopia - an imagined perfect or ideal place
