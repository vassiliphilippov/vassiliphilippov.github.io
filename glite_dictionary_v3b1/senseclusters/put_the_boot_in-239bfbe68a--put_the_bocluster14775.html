put the boot in

verb
attack or criticize someone already vulnerable
Pronunciation: / pʊt ðə buːt ɪn / (AmE), / pʊt ðə buːt ɪn / (BrE)
to attack or criticize someone, especially when they are already weak, vulnerable, or in a difficult situation

Examples

To put the boot in hard
Despite her apology, the critics continued to put the boot in during the interview.
It's unfair to put the boot in now that he's already facing so much trouble.
When their team was losing badly, the fans really put the boot in with boos and jeers.
