gasper

noun
sudden cold draft
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæspər / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːspə / (BrE)
a sudden draft or gust of cold air that causes a person to gasp or shiver

Examples

A cold gasper
A sudden gasper blew out the candles during the stormy night.
He felt a sharp gasper as he stepped into the chilly hallway.
Opening the cellar door let a gasper rush through the old house.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: breeze - a gentle wind
  • synonym: draught - a current of air, especially one that is cold or unpleasant
  • degree higher: gust - a strong, sudden rush of wind
