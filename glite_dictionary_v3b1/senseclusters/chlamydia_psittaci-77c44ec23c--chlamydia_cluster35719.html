Chlamydia psittaci

noun
a bacterial species infecting birds and humans
9.1
Pronunciation: / kləˌmɪdiə sɪˈtɑːsaɪ / (AmE), / kləˌmɪdɪə sɪˈtæsaɪ / (BrE)
A species of bacteria that can cause disease in birds and humans; in humans, it can cause psittacosis and is sometimes associated with other infections.

Examples

Chlamydia psittaci infection
Infection with Chlamydia psittaci can lead to flu-like symptoms in humans.
Researchers isolated Chlamydia psittaci from the infected parrot.
Veterinarians were concerned about Chlamydia psittaci outbreaks in captive bird populations.
