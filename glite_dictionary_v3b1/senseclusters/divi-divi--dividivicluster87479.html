divi-divi

noun
tropical American tree with tannin-rich pods
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːviːˈdiːviː / (AmE), / ˌdiːviːˈdiːviː / (BrE)
a small thornless tree or shrub (Caesalpinia coriaria) native to tropical America, whose curved seed pods are a source of tannin

Examples

A divi-divi tree
On the beach, the shade from a divi-divi provided a cool spot to rest.
Tannins extracted from the seed pods of divi-divi are used in leather production.
The divi-divi tree is easily recognized by its distinctive, wind-shaped appearance on Caribbean islands.
