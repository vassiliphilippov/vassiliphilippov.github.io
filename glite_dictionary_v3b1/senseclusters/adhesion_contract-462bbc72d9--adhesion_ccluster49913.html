A contract drafted by one party, usually a business with stronger bargaining power, and signed by another party who has little ability to negotiate the terms.

Examples

An adhesion contract signed
If a court finds an adhesion contract to be unconscionable, it may refuse to enforce it.
Many consumer agreements, such as click-through licenses, are considered adhesion contracts.
The gym membership agreement was an adhesion contract that offered no room to negotiate its strict terms.
