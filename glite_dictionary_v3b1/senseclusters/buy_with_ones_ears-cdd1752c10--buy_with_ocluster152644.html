buy with one's ears

verb
be easily persuaded by spoken words
3.4 7.7
Pronunciation: / baɪ wɪð wʌnz ɪrz / (AmE), / baɪ wɪð wʌnz ɪəz / (BrE)
to be easily persuaded or influenced by what one hears, especially by persuasive speech or promises

Examples

To buy with your ears easily
It's risky to buy with your ears when making big decisions; always check the facts.
Mark was warned not to buy with his ears when dealing with smooth-talking advertisers.
She tends to buy with her ears, believing every sales pitch.
