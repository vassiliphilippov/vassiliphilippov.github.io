skimp

verb
provide less than enough; be sparing
5.8 0.91/m
Pronunciation: / skÉªmp / (AmE), / skÉªmp / (BrE)
to provide or use less of something than is needed, often to save money, time, or effort

Examples

To skimp on sleep
If you skimp on sleep, you'll feel tired all day.
The company decided to skimp on employee benefits to save money.
You shouldn't skimp on quality when buying safety equipment.
If you skimp on studying, you may not do well on the test.
She often skimps when cooking, using fewer ingredients than needed.

Don't skimp on the ingredients if you want the dish to taste good.

Synonyms

  • scrimp
    7.4 0.41/m
    scrimp means living very cheaply and cutting costs, while skimp means giving or using less than is needed to save.
  • stint
    7.4 0.25/m
    stint means to give or use only small amounts, often in a formal way, while skimp means to provide too little to meet needs.
  • scant
    7.3 0.03/m
    scant describes a small or barely enough amount, often describing things, while skimp means to use or give less to save.

Related Terms
