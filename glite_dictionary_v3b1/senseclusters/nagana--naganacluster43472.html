nagana

noun
parasitic animal disease
9.9
Pronunciation: / nəˈɡɑːnə / (AmE), / nəˈɡɑːnə / (BrE)
a disease of cattle and other animals in Africa caused by trypanosome parasites, leading to fever, weakness, and anemia

Examples

nagana disease
The veterinarian diagnosed the sick cow with nagana after observing the symptoms.
Farmers in some African regions vaccinate their cattle to prevent nagana outbreaks.
Losses from nagana can have a serious impact on local livestock production.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sleeping sickness - a disease in humans caused by trypanosome parasites
  • synonym: trypanosomiasis - a disease caused by trypanosome parasites
