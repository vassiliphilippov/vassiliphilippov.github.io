palisading

adjective
row-like arrangement
Pronunciation: / ˈpælɪˌseɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpælɪseɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
arranged in a row or fence-like formation, especially referring to cells or tissues in biology.

Examples

Palisading cells
Under the microscope, the cells showed a distinct palisading pattern along the edge of the tissue.
The tumor was characterized by palisading nuclei, which helped confirm the diagnosis.
Researchers noted the palisading arrangement of fibers in the sample they examined.

  • antonym: scattered - spread out irregularly
