rate

noun
frequency or amount per time unit
Pronunciation: / ɹeɪt /; / reɪt / (AmE), / ɹeɪt /; / reɪt / (BrE)
the amount, speed, or frequency at which something happens, measured in relation to something else (such as time or quantity)

Examples

Heart rate
Doctors monitor the heart rate to assess cardiovascular health.
The rate of inflation has decreased significantly this year.
The plant grew at an impressive rate during the summer.
Experts analyzed the graduation rate to assess the effectiveness of the program.
Infant mortality rate is a critical indicator of a country's health care system.
The unemployment rate has decreased significantly over the past year.
At this rate, we'll finish the work much sooner than expected.
The birth rate in the city has increased steadily over the last decade.
The company is growing at a remarkable rate compared to last year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pace - the rate of progress or movement
  • synonym: ratio - the relation between two numbers indicating how many times the first number contains the second
  • synonym: speed - the rate at which someone or something moves or operates
