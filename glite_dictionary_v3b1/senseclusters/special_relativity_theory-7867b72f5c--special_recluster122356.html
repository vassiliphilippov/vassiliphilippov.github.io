special relativity theory

noun
physical theory; speed of light is constant
Pronunciation: / ˈspɛʃəl rɪˌlæt̬əˈvɪɾi ˌθɪəri / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəl rɪˌlatɪˈvɪɾɪ ˌθɪəri / (BrE)
The physical theory stating that the speed of light in a vacuum is constant and that the laws of physics are the same in all inertial reference frames.

Examples

Einstein's special relativity theory
Einstein's special relativity theory revolutionized twentieth-century physics.
Many experiments have confirmed the predictions made by special relativity theory.
The Global Positioning System relies on the corrections predicted by special relativity theory.

Related Terms

  • antonym: classical mechanics - The branch of physics dealing with the motion of bodies under forces before relativity or quantum mechanics.
  • degree higher: general relativity - The theory of gravitation extending special relativity to non-inertial frames and gravitation.
