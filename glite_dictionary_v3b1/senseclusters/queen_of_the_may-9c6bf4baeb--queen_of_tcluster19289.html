queen of the May

noun
girl selected as queen for May Day festival
Pronunciation: / kwiːn əv ðə meɪ / (AmE), / kwiːn ɒv ðə meɪ / (BrE)
the girl who is chosen to preside as queen during a May Day festival, often crowned and celebrated as part of traditional festivities

Examples

Queen of the May crowned
As queen of the May, she wore a crown of flowers and a flowing white dress.
Every year the village selects a queen of the May to lead the procession.
The tradition of crowning a queen of the May dates back centuries in this region.
