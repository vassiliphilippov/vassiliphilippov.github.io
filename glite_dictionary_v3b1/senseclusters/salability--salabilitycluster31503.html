salability

noun
quality of being saleable
Pronunciation: / ˌsæləˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌseɪləˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being capable of being sold or marketed

Examples

The salability of products
An item's salability can be affected by changes in consumer trends.
The salability of the new product depends largely on its price and packaging.
They improved the house to increase its salability before putting it on the market.

Related Terms

  • synonym: marketability - the quality of being easy to market or sell
  • antonym: unsalability - the quality of not being able to be sold
