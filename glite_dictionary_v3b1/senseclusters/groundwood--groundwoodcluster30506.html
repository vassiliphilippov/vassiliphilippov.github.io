groundwood

noun
mechanically ground wood pulp
Pronunciation: / ˈɡraʊndˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊndˌwʊd / (BrE)
wood pulp made by mechanically grinding logs or wood chips, used as a raw material in papermaking

Examples

Groundwood pulp
The factory uses groundwood to produce newsprint and other types of paper.
Because groundwood contains more lignin, paper made from it tends to yellow over time.
Modern mills often blend groundwood with chemical pulp to improve paper quality.

Related Terms

  • antonym: chemical pulp - wood pulp produced by chemical processes
  • synonym: wood pulp - fibrous material from wood used to make paper
