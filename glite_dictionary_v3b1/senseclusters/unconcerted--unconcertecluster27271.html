unconcerted

adjective
not single-step (chemistry)
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnkənˈsɜːrtɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnkənˈsɜːtɪd / (BrE)
in chemistry, describing a reaction that does not occur in a single step but proceeds through separate, sequential steps or intermediates

Examples

Unconcerted reaction steps
The chemist explained that the reaction was unconcerted, involving several distinct intermediates.
Unlike a concerted process, an unconcerted reaction proceeds through multiple steps.
Researchers observed an unconcerted mechanism with a clear separation between the two main stages.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concerted - in chemistry, occurring in a single, simultaneous step
