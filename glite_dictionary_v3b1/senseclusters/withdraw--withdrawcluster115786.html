withdraw

verb
retire gracefully from a position
2.8 2.4
Pronunciation: / wɪðˈdrɔ / (AmE), / wɪðˈdrɔː / (BrE)
to leave, remove oneself, or stop participating in a place, group, activity, or situation (including becoming less involved or distant)

Examples

To withdraw from a group
After 30 years as president, she chose to withdraw from public life and focus on her family.
He decided to withdraw from the competition rather than risk further injury.
The CEO withdrew when it became clear the company needed new leadership.
After years of volunteering, Maria decided to withdraw from the committee to focus on her family.
Due to health reasons, the athlete had to withdraw from the tournament at the last minute.
Once he felt overwhelmed by the workload, he chose to withdraw from the project team.
He tried to withdraw himself from the complicated situation.
She carefully withdrew her hand from the thorny bush.
The mechanic withdrew the gears to inspect for damage.
