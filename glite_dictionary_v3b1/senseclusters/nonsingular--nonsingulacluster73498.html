nonsingular

adjective
invertible (math)
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˈsɪŋɡjələr / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈsɪŋɡjʊlə / (BrE)
not singular; in mathematics, having a unique solution or being invertible, especially of a matrix or equation

Examples

Nonsingular matrix
The matrix is nonsingular, so it can be inverted to solve the system of equations.
A nonsingular transformation preserves the structure of the mathematical space.
If the determinant is not zero, the matrix is nonsingular and has a unique solution.

Related Terms

  • synonym: invertible - able to be inverted, especially referring to a matrix in mathematics
  • antonym: singular - having only one solution or not invertible, especially in mathematics
