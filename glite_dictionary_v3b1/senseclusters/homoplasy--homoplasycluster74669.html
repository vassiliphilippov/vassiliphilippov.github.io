homoplasy

noun
similar traits, no ancestry
Pronunciation: / ˈhoʊməˌpleɪzi / (AmE), / ˈhɒməˌpleɪzi / (BrE)
the occurrence of similar traits in different species that are not inherited from a common ancestor

Examples

Evolutionary homoplasy
The evolution of wings in both bats and birds is a classic example of homoplasy.
Scientists study homoplasy to understand how similar features can arise independently in different lineages.
Convergent evolution often leads to homoplasy in unrelated species facing similar environmental challenges.

Related Terms

  • synonym: analogy - similarity in function or appearance not due to common ancestry
  • antonym: homology - similarity in traits due to shared ancestry
