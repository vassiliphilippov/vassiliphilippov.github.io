actinomorphic

adjective
having radial symmetry (botany)
6.5 9.0
Pronunciation: /æk.tɪ.noʊ.ˈmɔːr.fɪk/ /; / /æk.tɪ.nə.ˈmɔːr.fɪk/ (AmE), /æk.tɪ.nəʊ.ˈmɔː.fɪk/ (BrE)
(of a flower) having radial symmetry, so it can be divided into similar halves by any longitudinal plane through its axis.

Examples

Actinomorphic flower symmetry
In botany class, we examined how actinomorphic flowers differ from bilaterally symmetrical ones.
Most wildflowers in this region exhibit actinomorphic symmetry, unlike the zygomorphic ones in tropical areas.
The lily's actinomorphic flowers make it easy to identify among other species.

Related Terms

  • antonym: zygomorphic - having bilateral symmetry (in flowers)
