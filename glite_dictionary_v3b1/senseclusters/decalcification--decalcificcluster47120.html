decalcification

noun
loss of calcium from bones or teeth
7.8 6.6
Pronunciation: /diː.kæl.sɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /də.kæl.sɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /diː.kæl.sɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /də.kæl.sɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process or result of the loss of calcium from bones, teeth, or other tissues, often leading to weakening or disease

Examples

Decalcification of bones
Dentists are concerned about decalcification in patients who neglect their oral hygiene.
Osteoporosis involves the gradual decalcification of the skeletal system.
Prolonged use of certain medications can cause decalcification of the bones.
