tall oil

noun
oily byproduct from wood pulp used in soaps and lubricants
Pronunciation: / tɔl ɔɪl / (AmE), / tɔːl ɔɪl / (BrE)
an oily byproduct obtained from wood pulp during the manufacture of paper, used in making soaps, lubricants, and other products

Examples

Tall oil byproduct
After extracting cellulose, tall oil is separated as a valuable byproduct from wood pulp.
Lubricants derived from tall oil have become more common in industrial applications.
The soap manufacturer used tall oil as a key ingredient in their production process.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rosin - solid form of resin obtained from pines and other plants, related by origin and use
