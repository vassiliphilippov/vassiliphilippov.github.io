heliozoan

noun
aquatic protozoan with radiating pseudopods
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˌhiːlioʊˈzoʊən / (AmE), / ˌhiːlɪəˈzəʊən / (BrE)
a single-celled aquatic organism with a spherical body and stiff radiating pseudopods, belonging to a group of protozoa typically found in freshwater

Examples

A heliozoan organism
A heliozoan uses its long, radiating pseudopods to capture tiny prey in pond water.
Many biology students observe heliozoans during experiments on protists.
Under the microscope, the heliozoan appeared to have rays stretching out from a central sphere.
