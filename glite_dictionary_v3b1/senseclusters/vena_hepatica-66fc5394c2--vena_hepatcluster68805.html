vena hepatica

noun
vein draining blood from the liver
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈveɪnə hɪˈpætɪkə / (AmE), / ˈviːnə hɪˈpætɪkə / (BrE)
A vein that drains blood from the liver and empties into the inferior vena cava.

Examples

Vena hepatica vein
Damage to a vena hepatica can result in severe internal bleeding.
During surgery, careful attention is paid to the location of each vena hepatica.
The vena hepatica carries deoxygenated blood from the liver to the inferior vena cava.
