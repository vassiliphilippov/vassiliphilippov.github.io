occlusive

adjective
tending to block or close off
8.2 6.5
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈkluː.sɪv/ (AmE), /ə.ˈkluː.sɪv/ (BrE)
tending to block or close off a passage or opening, especially in a medical context.

Examples

Occlusive blood clot

Some skin creams have occlusive properties to lock in moisture.
The doctor used an occlusive dressing to help the wound heal.
