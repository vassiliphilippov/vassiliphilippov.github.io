screed

noun
long, tedious piece of writing or speech
Pronunciation: / skrid / (AmE), / skriːd / (BrE)
a long, tedious, and often critical or ranting piece of writing or speech

Examples

A long screed
After reading his endless screed on bureaucracy, I lost interest halfway through.
She published a lengthy screed criticizing the government’s handling of the crisis.
The internet is full of angry screeds about recent political events.
After ten minutes of her screed, most of the audience had tuned out.
He delivered a lengthy screed about the government's failures that bored everyone.
The editor returned my article, calling it a pointless screed rather than a news piece.

  • synonym: tirade - a long, angry speech of criticism or accusation
