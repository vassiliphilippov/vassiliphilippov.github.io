prophase

noun
first stage of mitosis in cell division
6.5 7.1
Pronunciation: /ˈproʊ.feɪz/ (AmE), /ˈprəʊ.feɪz/ (BrE)
the first stage of cell division (mitosis or meiosis) when chromosomes condense and become visible

Examples

Prophase stage
Biology students often identify the cell's stage by recognizing the features of prophase.
During prophase, the chromosomes become visible under a microscope as they condense.
The breakdown of the nuclear envelope occurs in prophase before the chromosomes align.
During prophase, chromosomes become visible under a light microscope.
Errors that occur in prophase can lead to problems in genetic division.
The nuclear envelope starts to break down in prophase of meiosis.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: metaphase - second stage of mitosis when chromosomes align at the center of the cell
