moth-eaten

adjective
old and worn; shabby or damaged
Pronunciation: / mɔθ ˌitən /; / ˈmɔθ ˌitn̩ /; / ˈmɔθˌitn̩ / (AmE), / mɒθ ˌiːtən /; / ˈmɒθ ˌiːtn̩ /; / ˈmɒθˌiːtn̩ / (BrE)
old, worn, or shabby in appearance, especially as if damaged by moths

Examples

A moth-eaten coat
His old moth-eaten jacket barely kept out the cold.
She refused to throw away her grandmother's moth-eaten quilt.
The theater’s curtains were faded and moth-eaten from years of use.
He wore a moth-eaten sweater that looked decades old.
Stacks of moth-eaten books lined the dusty shelves.
The old mansion was filled with moth-eaten carpets and faded wallpapers.
Her grandmother's moth-eaten shawl reminded her of childhood winters.
The play took place in a moth-eaten theater that had clearly seen better days.
We discovered a moth-eaten carpet in the attic, full of holes and faded colors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dilapidated - in a state of disrepair or ruin
  • synonym: shabby - in poor condition through long use or lack of care
  • synonym: tattered - old and torn; in poor condition
  • synonym: tatty - worn and shabby; in poor condition
  • synonym: threadbare - (of cloth, clothing, or soft furnishings) becoming thin and tattered with age
