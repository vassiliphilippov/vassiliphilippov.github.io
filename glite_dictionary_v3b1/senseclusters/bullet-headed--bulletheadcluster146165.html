bullet-headed

adjective
stubborn and unintelligent (person)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊlət ˌhɛdɪd / (AmE), / ˈbʊlɪt ˌhɛdɪd / (BrE)
(of a person) stubborn and unintelligent; obstinate and foolish.

Examples

Bullet-headed boss
Despite all evidence to the contrary, the bullet-headed manager refused to change his mind.
He can be so bullet-headed that working with him is exhausting.
Her bullet-headed insistence on a single approach led to the project's failure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: obstinate - stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion or chosen course of action
  • antonym: open-minded - willing to consider new ideas; unprejudiced
  • synonym: stupid - lacking intelligence or common sense
