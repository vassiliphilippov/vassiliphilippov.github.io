NRTI

noun
antiviral drug used against HIV
Pronunciation: / ˌɛn ˌɑr ˌti ˈaɪ / (AmE), / ˌɛn ˌɑː ˌti ˈʌɪ / (BrE)
a type of antiviral drug used to treat HIV, which prevents the virus from replicating by incorporating into its DNA and causing incomplete strands

Examples

An NRTI drug
Lamivudine is a commonly used NRTI in antiretroviral therapy.
The doctor prescribed two NRTIs in combination with a protease inhibitor.
Treatment regimens for HIV often include an NRTI to inhibit viral replication.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antiretroviral - a drug used to treat retrovirus infections, especially HIV
