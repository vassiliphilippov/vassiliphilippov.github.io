isotropic

adjective
having identical properties in all directions
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪ.səˈtroʊ.pɪk / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.səˈtrɒ.pɪk / (BrE)
Having identical properties in all directions; not varying with direction.

Examples

An isotropic medium
In physics, an isotropic medium transmits waves at the same speed regardless of direction.
Scientists tested whether the material was isotropic or had directional differences in strength.
The crystal is isotropic, so its optical properties are the same in every direction.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anisotropic - having properties that vary with direction
