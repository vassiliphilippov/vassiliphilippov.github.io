put an end to

verb
bring something to a stop or conclusion
4.1
Pronunciation: / pʊt ən ɛnd tu / (AmE), / pʊt ən end tʊ / (BrE)
to cause something to stop or to bring something to a definite conclusion

Examples

To put an end to problems
Strict laws were introduced to put an end to illegal dumping in the area.
The new policy aims to put an end to tax evasion in the country.
The teacher tried to put an end to the argument between the students.

Related Terms

  • antonym: continue - to keep something going; not stop
  • synonym: stop - to bring to an end; cease
