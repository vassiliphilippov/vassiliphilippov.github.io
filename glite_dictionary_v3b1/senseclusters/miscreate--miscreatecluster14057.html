miscreate

verb
create or make badly
5.6
Pronunciation: / mɪsˈkriː.eɪt / (AmE), / mɪsˈkriː.eɪt / (BrE)
to create, form, or make badly; to bring into existence in a flawed or defective way

Examples

To miscreate a plan
If you miscreate your business plan, the whole project could be at risk.
Some scientists fear we might miscreate life forms through careless experimentations in biotechnology.
The author seemed to miscreate the main character, making him both confusing and unconvincing.

  • antonym: create - to bring something into existence
