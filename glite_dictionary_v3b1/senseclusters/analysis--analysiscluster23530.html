analysis

noun
Freudian method for treating mental disorders
1.7
Pronunciation: / əˈnæl.ə.sɪs / (AmE), / əˈnæl.ə.sɪs / (BrE)
A set of techniques for exploring unconscious motives and treating certain mental disorders, especially as developed by Sigmund Freud.

Examples

Freud's analysis method
Freud's method of analysis has influenced many modern therapies.
She studied analysis as part of her training to become a therapist.
The patient began analysis to explore childhood memories and unconscious conflicts.
