drop the gloves

verb
prepare for a fight or end conflict
3.6 6.9
Pronunciation: / drɑp ðə ɡlʌvz / (AmE), / drɒp ðə ɡlʌvz / (BrE)
(informal) To prepare for a fight or confrontation, originally from ice hockey where players physically drop their gloves before fighting. Sometimes also used to mean to end hostilities and begin cooperation or negotiation.

Examples

To drop the gloves in hockey
After months of arguing, both sides agreed to drop the gloves and start negotiating.
The manager told the team it was time to drop the gloves and work together.
When their tempers flared on the ice, the players were quick to drop the gloves.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fight - to engage in a physical or verbal confrontation
  • antonym: make peace - to end hostilities and cooperate
