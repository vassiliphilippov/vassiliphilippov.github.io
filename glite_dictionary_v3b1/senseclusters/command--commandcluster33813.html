command

noun
power or authority to give orders
Pronunciation: / kəˈmænd / (AmE), / kəˈmɑːnd / (BrE)
the power, authority, or position to control people or things, especially in a military or organizational context

Examples

In command of the team
After years of service, she finally achieved command of the entire fleet.
He has command over a large team of engineers.
The general's command was respected by everyone under him.
He used every tool at his command to solve the difficult puzzle.
She had a vast library at her command for her research.
With all resources at their command, the engineers finished the project early.
After the general retired, she assumed command of the entire division.
The company faced new challenges under his command.
There was confusion during the transition to a new command.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authority - the power or right to give orders, make decisions, and enforce obedience
  • synonym: control - the power to direct or manage
  • synonym: division - a large military unit or formation
  • synonym: leadership - the action or position of leading a group or organization
  • antonym: subordination - the condition of being lower in rank or authority
  • synonym: unit - a group forming part of an organization, especially military
