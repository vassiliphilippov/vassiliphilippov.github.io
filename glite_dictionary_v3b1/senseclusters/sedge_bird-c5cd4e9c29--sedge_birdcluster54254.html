sedge bird

noun
a small European warbler
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛdʒ bɜrd / (AmE), / ˈsɛdʒ bɜːd / (BrE)
a small European warbler (Acrocephalus schoenobaenus) that breeds among reeds and sedges and migrates to Africa for the winter

Examples

The sedge bird migrates
During migration, the sedge bird travels thousands of miles to reach its wintering grounds in Africa.
The melodious song of the sedge bird is a sure sign of spring in the marshes.
We spotted a sedge bird flitting through the reeds by the lake.
