sclerose

verb
to become inflexible
9.5
Pronunciation: / skləˈroʊs / (AmE), / skləˈrəʊz / (BrE)
to become rigid, inflexible, or resistant to change, especially in organizations, systems, or habits (figurative use)

Examples

Systems sclerose
If a company fails to innovate, its culture may slowly sclerose and resist necessary changes.
Over the years, the committee began to sclerose, making it difficult to introduce new policies.
Without fresh ideas, even creative teams can sclerose and lose their edge.

Related Terms

  • antonym: adapt - to change or adjust to new conditions
  • synonym: harden - to become less flexible or more rigid
  • synonym: ossify - to become rigid or fixed in attitude or position
