worldly possessions

noun
material belongings owned by a person

Examples

Synonyms

  • worldly belongings
    3.8 0.02/m
    worldly belongings means all the material things a person owns, while worldly possessions is used the same way but sounds more formal.
  • worldly goods
    5.0 0.1/m
    worldly goods refers to a person's material items and sometimes tradeable stuff, while worldly possessions highlights general ownership.
  • material possession
    3.0 0.5/m
    material possession names a single physical object someone owns, while worldly possessions means all their material property together.
  • tangible possession
    3.5 0.00/m
    tangible possession is an item you can touch and hold, while worldly possessions covers all your physical property.
  • personal property
    3.1 1/m
    personal property is movable property like furniture or cars in law, while worldly possessions can include real estate too.
  • belongings
    1.0 0.01/m
    belongings are the things someone carries or keeps near them, while worldly possessions means all their material assets.
  • possession
    1.7 12/m
    possession means a single thing owned by someone, while worldly possessions refers to the full set of their material belongings.
