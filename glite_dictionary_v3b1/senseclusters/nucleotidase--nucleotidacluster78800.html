nucleotidase

noun
enzyme breaking nucleotides
9.9
Pronunciation: / nuːkliˈoʊtəˌdeɪs / (AmE), / njuːkliˈəʊtɪˌdeɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that breaks down nucleotides into smaller molecules by removing phosphate groups

Examples

nucleotidase enzyme activity
The liver produces nucleotidase to help process nucleotides during metabolism.
Researchers measured the activity of nucleotidase in different tissue samples.
A deficiency in nucleotidase can affect the breakdown of nucleotides in the body.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nuclease - an enzyme that breaks down nucleic acids
  • synonym: phosphatase - an enzyme that removes phosphate groups from molecules
