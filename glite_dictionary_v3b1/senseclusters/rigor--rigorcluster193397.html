rigor

noun
severe hardship or adversity
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪɡər / (AmE), / ˈrɪɡə / (BrE)
a severe or harsh condition that is difficult to endure, especially relating to hardship or adversity

Examples

The rigors of winter
Doctors must endure the rigors of long, exhausting shifts in the emergency room.
New recruits often underestimate the rigor of military training.
The explorers braved the rigors of the Arctic winter for months on end.
During the long war, civilians endured the rigour of daily shortages and curfews.
The rigour of the high-altitude climate challenged even the most experienced explorers.
Training for the marathon exposed him to the rigour of early morning runs in the winter.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ease - absence of difficulty or effort
  • synonym: hardship - severe suffering or privation
  • synonym: harshness - the quality of being unpleasantly rough or severe
  • antonym: mildness - the quality of being not severe or harsh (contrasting property)
