hypertrophic

adjective
enlarged tissue or organ
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˈtrɑfɪk /; / ˌhaɪpərˈtroʊfɪk / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəˈtrɒfɪk / (BrE)
abnormally or excessively enlarged or developed (used for body parts, tissues, or sometimes for things like organizations or systems)

Examples

Hypertrophic heart muscle
The doctor observed hypertrophic changes in the patient's heart muscle.
A hypertrophic scar can form after a deep cut heals.
The ultrasound revealed hypertrophic tissue in the patient's liver.
The company developed a hypertrophic management structure that slowed down decision-making.
Critics argue that the new law is a result of a hypertrophic legal system.
The plant's hypertrophic leaves made it stand out among the others in the garden.

Related Terms

  • antonym: atrophic - describing tissue or an organ that has decreased in size
  • synonym: enlarged - increased in size
  • synonym: overgrown - grown excessively large or beyond normal size
