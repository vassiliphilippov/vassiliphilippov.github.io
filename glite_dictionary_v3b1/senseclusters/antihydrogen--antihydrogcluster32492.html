antihydrogen

noun
antimatter hydrogen atom
Pronunciation: / ˌæntiˈhaɪdrədʒən / (AmE), / ˌæntiˈhaɪdrɪdʒən / (BrE)
a form of antimatter made of an antiproton and a positron, the antiparticle equivalent of hydrogen

Examples

Antihydrogen atom
Scientists have successfully produced antihydrogen atoms in a controlled laboratory environment.
The study of antihydrogen helps researchers understand the fundamental differences between matter and antimatter.
Trapping antihydrogen for extended periods is a major achievement in particle physics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antimatter - matter composed of antiparticles, opposite to normal matter
  • antonym: hydrogen - the lightest chemical element, with one proton and one electron
