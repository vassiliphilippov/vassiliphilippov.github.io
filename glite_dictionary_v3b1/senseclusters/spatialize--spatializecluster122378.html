spatialize

verb
to take spatial form
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈspeɪʃəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈspeɪʃəlaɪz / (BrE)
to give something spatial qualities or make it seem arranged in space

Examples

To spatialize sound
The new audio technology can spatialize sound, making it feel like it surrounds the listener.
As the artist worked, the abstract shapes began to spatialize on the canvas.
Virtual reality games often spatialize objects to create a more immersive experience.
The new audio system can spatialize music to make it sound like it is coming from all directions.
Architects often spatialize their ideas using 3D models to show how a building will fit in its environment.
The artist tried to spatialize emotions by arranging objects throughout the gallery space.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abstract - to remove spatial or concrete qualities
  • synonym: arrange - to put things in a particular order or position
  • antonym: flatten - to make or become flat, lacking spatial depth
  • synonym: visualize - to form a mental image of something
