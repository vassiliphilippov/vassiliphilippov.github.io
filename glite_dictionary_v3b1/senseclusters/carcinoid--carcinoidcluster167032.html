carcinoid

noun
type of gastrointestinal tumor
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːr.sɪ.nɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈkɑː.sɪ.nɔɪd / (BrE)
A small tumor, either benign or malignant, that arises from the mucous membranes of the gastrointestinal tract and is often associated with increased secretion of serotonin.

Examples

A carcinoid tumor
Doctors discovered a carcinoid during the patient's routine endoscopy.
Symptoms of a carcinoid may include flushing and diarrhea due to excess serotonin.
The surgeon explained that the carcinoid could be benign or malignant, depending on further tests.
