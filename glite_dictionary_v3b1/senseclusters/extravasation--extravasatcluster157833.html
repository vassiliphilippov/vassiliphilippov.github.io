extravasation

noun
accidental leakage of body fluid into tissue
Pronunciation: / ɛkˌstrævəˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ɛkˌstrævəˈseɪʃən /; / ɪkˌstrævəˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
the abnormal escape of bodily fluid (such as blood, lymph, or urine) from a vessel into surrounding tissue

Examples

Blood extravasation
Extravasation of blood can cause bruising and tissue damage around the injury.
The doctor explained that the extravasation observed was likely due to a ruptured blood vessel.
The nurse monitored the IV site for any signs of extravasation.
Extravasation of urine was observed following the injury to the bladder.
Prompt recognition of extravasation can prevent severe tissue damage in chemotherapy patients.
The intravenous drug caused extravasation at the injection site, resulting in localized swelling.

Related Terms

  • synonym: leakage - the accidental escape of fluid or gas from a container
