platyrrhine

noun
New World monkey with a flat nose
9.9 9.6
Pronunciation: /ˈplæt.ɪ.raɪn/ /; / /plə.ˈtɪ.raɪn/ (AmE), /ˈplæt.ɪ.raɪn/ /; / /plə.ˈtɪ.raɪn/ (BrE)
A monkey of a group native to Central and South America, characterized by a broad, flat nose with widely separated nostrils and usually a prehensile tail.

Examples

A platyrrhine monkey
Researchers observed the social behaviors of several platyrrhine species in their natural habitat.
The spider monkey is a well-known platyrrhine found in South American rainforests.
Unlike catarrhines, platyrrhines have prehensile tails and broad, flat noses.
