multigrain

adjective
containing several grains
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌltiˌɡreɪn / (AmE), / ˈmʌltiˌɡreɪn / (BrE)
made from or containing more than one type of grain

Examples

Multigrain bread
She prefers multigrain bread because it is healthier than white bread.
The bakery offers a delicious multigrain loaf packed with seeds and oats.
For breakfast, he made toast using multigrain slices topped with avocado.

Related Terms

  • antonym: white - made from refined grains with the bran and germ removed
  • synonym: wholegrain - made from the entire grain, including the bran, germ, and endosperm
