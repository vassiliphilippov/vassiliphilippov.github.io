covenant of salt

noun
a solemn, binding promise of loyalty
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌvənənt əv sɔlt / (AmE), / ˈkʌvənənt əv sɒlt / (BrE)
a solemn agreement or promise that is regarded as unbreakable and symbolizes lasting loyalty, especially as referenced in ancient traditions or religious texts

Examples

A covenant of salt
Biblical references often mention a covenant of salt to denote an everlasting agreement.
The friends swore a covenant of salt as a sign of their unwavering loyalty.
The treaty was described as a covenant of salt between the two nations, meant to last for generations.
