doggone

adjective, adverb
informal intensifier
9.4
Pronunciation: /ˈdɔː.gɔːn/ /; / /ˈdɑː.gən/ (AmE), /ˈdɒ.gən/ /; / /ˈdɒ.gɒn/ (BrE)
used informally to emphasize something or to express annoyance, especially before a noun or in a statement

Examples

A doggone mess
I can't believe I lost my doggone keys again this morning.
That doggone computer keeps freezing every time I try to print something.
It's a doggone shame that we all missed the concert last night.
That was a doggone impressive performance by the young athlete.
I can't find my keys, and it's doggone frustrating.
She was doggone surprised to see her old friend at the party.
