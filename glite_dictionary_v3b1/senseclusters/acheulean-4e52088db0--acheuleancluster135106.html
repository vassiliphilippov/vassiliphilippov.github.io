Acheulean

adjective
prehistoric stone tools
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈʃuːliən / (AmE), / əˈʃuːliən / (BrE)
relating to a prehistoric stone tool industry marked by large hand axes, used by early humans in the Lower Paleolithic period

Examples

Acheulean hand axe
Archaeologists discovered Acheulean hand axes at the ancient site.
The Acheulean period is known for its distinctive stone tools.
Researchers studied Acheulean artifacts to learn about early human technology.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Mousterian - relating to a later prehistoric stone tool industry
  • synonym: Paleolithic - relating to the early phase of the Stone Age
