almond moth

noun
a moth pest of stored plant products
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːlmənd mɑθ / (AmE), / ˈɑːmənd mɒθ / (BrE)
a small moth (Ephestia cautella) whose larvae infest and web together various stored plant products, such as nuts and grains

Examples

The almond moth larvae
Almond moth infestations can cause significant economic losses to stored products.
The larvae of the almond moth produce webbing that contaminates nuts and dried fruit.
The warehouse manager discovered an infestation of almond moth larvae in the grain storage area.
