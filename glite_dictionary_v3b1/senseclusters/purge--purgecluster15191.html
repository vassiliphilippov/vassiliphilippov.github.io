purge

verb
to vomit; to eject stomach contents
5.4
Pronunciation: / pɝdʒ / (AmE), / pɜːdʒ / (BrE)
to make someone vomit or empty their bowels, or to cleanse the body of impurities, especially as a medical treatment

Examples

To purge the stomach
Doctors sometimes induce patients to purge if they have ingested poison.
She tried to purge herself after eating something that upset her stomach.
The patient began to purge after receiving the medication.
After eating contaminated food, he needed treatment to purge his digestive tract.
The doctor prescribed a laxative to purge the patient's system before surgery.
The herbal remedy was once used to purge the body of toxins.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cleanse - to make clean; to free from dirt or impurities
  • synonym: regurgitate - to bring swallowed food back up into the mouth
  • synonym: vomit - to eject matter from the stomach through the mouth
