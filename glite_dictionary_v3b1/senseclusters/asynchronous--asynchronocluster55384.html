asynchronous

adjective
not requiring a common clock in digital communication
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈsɪŋkrənəs / (AmE), / əˈsɪŋkrənəs / (BrE)
(of digital communication) not requiring a common clock between communicating devices, with timing signals derived from special characters within the data stream.

Examples

Asynchronous data transfer
In an asynchronous transmission, data is sent without the need for both devices to share a clock signal.
Many modern network protocols use asynchronous communication to increase flexibility and reduce complexity.
The company upgraded its equipment to support asynchronous data transfer, allowing for more efficient device communication.

Related Terms

  • antonym: synchronous - requiring a common timing signal or clock between communicating devices
