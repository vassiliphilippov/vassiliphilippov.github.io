causality

noun
relationship between cause and effect
Pronunciation: / kɔˈzæləti / (AmE), / kɔːˈzæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the relationship between a cause and its effect

Examples

Cause and causality
Scientists debate whether there is causality or just correlation between the two variables.
The experiment was designed to demonstrate causality rather than just association.
Understanding causality is crucial in designing effective public health interventions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: causation - the act or process of causing something to happen
  • antonym: correlation - a connection or relationship between two things, often without implying causation
