mono

noun
informal term for mononucleosis (disease)
8.3 5.9
Pronunciation: /ˈmoʊ.noʊ/ /; / /ˈmɑː.noʊ/ (AmE), /ˈmɒ.nəʊ/ (BrE)
An infectious disease, especially among young people, that causes fever, sore throat, fatigue, and swollen glands; an informal term for mononucleosis.

Examples

Catch mono at school
After catching mono during her first semester, she missed several classes.
He was surprised how tired he felt when he had mono as a teenager.
My doctor said I have mono, so I need to rest for a few weeks.
