deflate

verb
make less important or confident; reduce
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfleɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈfleɪt / (BrE)
to make someone or something feel or seem less important, confident, or impressive; to reduce in size, value, or importance

Examples

To deflate someone's ego
A series of poor sales figures quickly deflated the company's reputation for success.
He tried to deflate their excitement by revealing the project's many challenges.
The teacher's criticism seemed to deflate her enthusiasm for the project.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diminish - to make or become less
  • antonym: inflate - to increase in size or importance
