fatted

adjective
well-fed for purpose
Pronunciation: / ˈfætɪd / (AmE), / ˈfætɪd / (BrE)
made fat or especially well-fed, often in preparation for a particular purpose such as eating or sacrifice

Examples

Fatted calf feast
The villagers celebrated with a fatted lamb during the festival.
He saved the fatted goose for a special holiday meal.
In the story, the father welcomed his son by preparing the fatted calf.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lean - having little fat; not well-fed
  • synonym: plump - having a full, rounded shape from being well-fed
