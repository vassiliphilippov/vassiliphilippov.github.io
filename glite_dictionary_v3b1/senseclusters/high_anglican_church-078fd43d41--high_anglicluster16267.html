High Anglican Church

noun
Anglican group emphasizing Catholic traditions
Pronunciation: / haɪ æŋˈɡlɪkən tʃɝːtʃ / (AmE), / haɪ æŋˈɡlɪkən tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
A branch of the Anglican Church that emphasizes Catholic tradition, particularly in sacraments, rituals, and adherence to church authority.

Examples

The High Anglican Church
Debate over liturgy often arises between the High Anglican Church and other branches of Anglicanism.
She was drawn to the formal ceremonies held by the High Anglican Church.
The High Anglican Church retains many rituals that are similar to those of the Catholic tradition.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Low Church - a branch of the Anglican Church with less emphasis on ritual and authority
