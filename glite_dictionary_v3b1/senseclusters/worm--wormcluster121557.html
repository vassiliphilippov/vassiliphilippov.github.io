worm

verb
move with twisting motions
4.6 0.24/m
Pronunciation: / w…úrm / (AmE), / w…úÀêm / (BrE)
to move by twisting, wriggling, or sliding slowly, like a worm

Examples

To worm through
She tried to worm out of the tight corner by twisting her body.
The child managed to worm his way through the gap in the fence.
The snake began to worm its way across the forest floor, searching for food.
The snake kept worming along the damp bank until it vanished under the reeds.
He managed to worm through the narrow gap in the fence without getting stuck.
The child was worming his way across the floor, pretending to be a spy.
