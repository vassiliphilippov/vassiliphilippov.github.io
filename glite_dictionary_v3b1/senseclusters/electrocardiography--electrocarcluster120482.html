electrocardiography

noun
recording of heart's electrical activity
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktroʊˌkɑrdiˈɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊˌkɑːdiˈɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the process of recording the electrical activity of the heart using a cardiograph to produce a cardiogram, used especially to diagnose heart conditions

Examples

Electrocardiography test
Modern hospitals use advanced equipment for electrocardiography during routine checkups.
The doctor decided to perform electrocardiography to assess her heart function.
Training in electrocardiography is essential for cardiologists and other healthcare professionals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: electrocardiogram - a record or display of a person's heartbeat produced by electrocardiography
