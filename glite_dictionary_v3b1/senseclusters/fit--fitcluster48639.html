fit

noun
sudden outburst of anger
Pronunciation: / fɪt / (AmE), / fɪt / (BrE)
a sudden episode of uncontrollable activity, emotion, or a medical attack, such as anger, laughter, coughing, or convulsions

Examples

A fit of anger
Don't have a fit—I'll fix the problem right away.
The little boy threw a fit when he was told to share his toys.
When her plans were cancelled, she had a fit and stormed out of the room.
He flew into a fit of laughter when he heard the joke.
In a fit of anger, she slammed the door and left the room.
She cleaned the entire house in a sudden fit of energy.
He broke into a fit of laughter during the meeting.
She had a fit of sneezing that lasted several minutes.
The child was prone to having a fit when upset.

Related Terms

  • synonym: burst - a sudden outbreak or effort
  • synonym: paroxysm - a sudden attack or outburst of emotion or activity
  • degree higher: rage - violent, uncontrollable anger
  • synonym: tantrum - a sudden outburst of anger, especially by a child
