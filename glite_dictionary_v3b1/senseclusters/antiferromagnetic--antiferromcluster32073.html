antiferromagnetic

adjective
relating to antiferromagnetism in materials
9.9
Pronunciation: /æn.tɪ.fer.oʊ.mæg.ˈne.tɪk/ (AmE), /æn.tɪ.fer.əʊ.mæg.ˈne.tɪk/ (BrE)
relating to or exhibiting antiferromagnetism, a phenomenon in which adjacent ions in a material have opposing magnetic moments resulting in no net magnetization.

Examples

Antiferromagnetic materials
Researchers are investigating antiferromagnetic materials for use in next-generation memory devices.
The antiferromagnetic properties of the compound were apparent at low temperatures.
When cooled below a certain temperature, manganese oxide becomes antiferromagnetic.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ferromagnetic - showing ferromagnetism, i.e., strong magnetic properties due to parallel alignment of magnetic moments
