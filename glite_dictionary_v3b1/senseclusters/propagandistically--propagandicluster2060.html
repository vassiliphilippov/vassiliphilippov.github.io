propagandistically

adverb
in a propaganda manner
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˌprɑːpəˌɡændəˈstɪkli / (AmE), / ˌprɒpəˌɡændəˈstɪkli /; / ˌprɒpəˌɡændɪˈstɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that is intended to spread propaganda or influence people's opinions, often using biased or manipulative techniques

Examples

To speak propagandistically
The speech was delivered propagandistically to persuade the audience to support the new policy.
Some news outlets report propagandistically rather than presenting unbiased facts.
The film was criticized for presenting history propagandistically instead of accurately.
The article was written propagandistically, aiming to sway readers rather than inform them.
Some documentaries present information propagandistically to support a particular viewpoint.
The politician spoke propagandistically, using emotional language to influence the audience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: manipulatively - in a way that tries to control or influence others unfairly
  • antonym: objectively - in a way that is not influenced by personal feelings or opinions
