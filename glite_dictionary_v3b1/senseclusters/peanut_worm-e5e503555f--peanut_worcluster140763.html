peanut worm

noun
a marine worm resembling a peanut when retracted
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpiːnʌt wɜrm / (AmE), / ˈpiːnʌt wɜːm / (BrE)
a small, unsegmented marine worm that retracts its front part into its body when disturbed, resembling a peanut in shape

Examples

The peanut worm animal
Marine biologists studied the feeding habits of the peanut worm in coastal waters.
On the seafloor, peanut worms burrow into mud and sand for protection.
The peanut worm quickly retracts its head when threatened by predators.
