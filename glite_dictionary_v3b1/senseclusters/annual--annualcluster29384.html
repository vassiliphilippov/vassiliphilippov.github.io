annual

adjective
plant living for one year or season
Pronunciation: / ˈænjuəl / (AmE), / ˈanjʊəl / (BrE)
(of a plant) living for only one year or season, completing its entire life cycle from germination to death within that time.

Examples

Annual flowers
After the first frost, most annual flowers will die off completely.
Many gardeners prefer annual plants for their vibrant, long-lasting blooms throughout the summer.
The marigold is a classic example of an annual that must be replanted each year.

Related Terms

  • antonym: biennial - (of a plant) living for two years
  • antonym: perennial - (of a plant) living for more than two years
