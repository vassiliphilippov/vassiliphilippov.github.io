unpoliced

adjective
without supervision
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnpəˈliːst / (AmE), / ˌʌnpəˈliːst / (BrE)
not supervised, watched, or controlled by police or other authorities

Examples

An unpoliced area
The alley remained unpoliced for years, leading to frequent disturbances.
Many online spaces are unpoliced, which can result in the spread of false information.
The event was largely unpoliced, relying on attendees to follow the rules themselves.
The remote border crossing remained unpoliced for several months.
Many small villages are almost completely unpoliced due to a lack of resources.
After the festival, the park was left unpoliced overnight.
