colonia

noun
Roman settlement
Pronunciation: / koʊˈloʊniə / (AmE), / kəˈləʊniə / (BrE)
an outpost or settlement established by ancient Rome, often for military or administrative purposes

Examples

Roman colonia
The ancient Roman colonia was strategically located near the river for defense.
Archaeologists discovered the remains of a colonia with well-preserved streets and buildings.
Many veterans of the Roman army were given land in a new colonia after their service.

Related Terms

  • synonym: colony - a territory settled and ruled by people from another land
  • antonym: metropolis - the parent city of a colony
