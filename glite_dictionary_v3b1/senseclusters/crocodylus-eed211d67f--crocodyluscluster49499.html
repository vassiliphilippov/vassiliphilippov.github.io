Crocodylus

proper noun
genus of true crocodiles
Pronunciation: / ˌkrɑː.kəˈdaɪ.ləs / (AmE), / ˌkrɒ.kəˈdaɪ.ləs / (BrE)
the genus of large reptiles comprising the true crocodiles, found in tropical regions of Africa, Asia, the Americas, and Australia

Examples

Crocodylus niloticus
Crocodylus includes several species found in both the Old and New Worlds.
Researchers compared skulls from various members of the Crocodylus genus.
The species Crocodylus niloticus is known as the Nile crocodile.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crocodile - large aquatic reptile with a long snout and powerful jaws
