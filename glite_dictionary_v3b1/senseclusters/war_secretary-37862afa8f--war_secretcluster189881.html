War Secretary

noun
historical head of the U.S. Department of War
8.9
Pronunciation: /wɔːr ˈse.krə.tə.riː/ (AmE), /wɔː ˈse.krə.trɪ/ /; / /wɔː ˈse.krə.tə.riː/ (BrE)
the head of the former U.S. Department of War, a position that was merged into the Secretary of Defense in 1947

Examples

The War Secretary position
Before 1947, the War Secretary was responsible for overseeing the U.S. Army.
President Lincoln appointed Edwin Stanton as War Secretary during the American Civil War.
The position of War Secretary was abolished when the Department of Defense was created.
