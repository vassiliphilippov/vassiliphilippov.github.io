cryolite

noun
a rare white mineral; source of fluorine
Pronunciation: / ˈkraɪəˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈkraɪəlaɪt / (BrE)
A rare white mineral consisting of sodium aluminum fluoride, used as a source of fluorine and in aluminum production.

Examples

A sample of cryolite
Chemists analyzed a sample of cryolite to determine its fluoride content.
Natural deposits of cryolite are now largely depleted.
The discovery of cryolite in Greenland was crucial for the aluminum industry.
