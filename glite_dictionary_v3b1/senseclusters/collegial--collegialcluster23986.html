collegial

adjective
shared authority among colleagues
Pronunciation: / kəˈliːdʒiəl / (AmE), / kəˈliːdʒiəl / (BrE)
characterized by shared authority and responsibility among colleagues or members of a group

Examples

A collegial relationship
A collegial relationship between teachers creates a positive learning environment for students.
Faculty members value the collegial atmosphere in their department.
The board maintained a collegial approach to decision-making, ensuring all voices were heard.

Related Terms

  • antonym: authoritarian - favoring or enforcing strict obedience to authority
  • synonym: collaborative - working together on a task or project
