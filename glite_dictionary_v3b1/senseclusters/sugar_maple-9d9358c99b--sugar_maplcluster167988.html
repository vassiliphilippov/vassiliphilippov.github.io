sugar maple

noun
North American maple tree used for syrup and wood
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˈʃuɡɚ ˈmeɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈʃʊɡə ˈmeɪpəl / (BrE)
a North American maple tree (Acer saccharum) known for its hard wood and sap, the main source of maple syrup and sugar

Examples

The sugar maple tree
Farmers tap the sugar maple in early spring to collect sap for syrup.
The furniture was crafted from durable sugar maple wood.
The leaves of the sugar maple turn a brilliant red and orange in autumn.
