speech sound

noun
an individual unit of speech sound
1.7
Pronunciation: / spiːtʃ saʊnd / (AmE), / spiːtʃ saʊnd / (BrE)
an individual audible unit of speech, considered without regard to whether it functions as a phoneme in a particular language

Examples

A speech sound unit
A linguist studies every speech sound in detail to understand how languages work.
Children gradually learn to pronounce each speech sound correctly as they develop.
The English 'th' is a speech sound that does not exist in some other languages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: phoneme - smallest unit of sound that can distinguish meaning in a language
