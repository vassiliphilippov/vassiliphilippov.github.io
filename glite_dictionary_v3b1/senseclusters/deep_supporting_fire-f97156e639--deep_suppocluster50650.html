deep supporting fire

noun
military fire targeting enemy rear areas
8.8
Pronunciation: /diːp sə.ˈpɔːr.tɪŋ faɪr/ /; / /diːp sə.ˈpɔːr.tɪŋ ˈfaɪ.ər/ (AmE), /diːp sə.ˈpɔː.tɪŋ ˈfaɪ.ə/ (BrE)
artillery or other fire directed at targets away from friendly forces, intended to disrupt enemy reserves, command, supplies, and communications

Examples
