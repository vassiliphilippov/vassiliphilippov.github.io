stormwater

noun
runoff rainwater
3.5
Pronunciation: /ˈstɔːrm.wɔː.tər/ /; / /ˈstɔːrm.wɑː.tər/ (AmE), /ˈstɔːm.wɔː.tə/ (BrE)
rainwater that flows or runs off streets, roofs, and other surfaces instead of soaking into the ground, often managed by drainage systems

Examples

Stormwater drainage system
Cities build special drains to manage stormwater and prevent flooding after heavy rain.
Uncontrolled stormwater can carry pollution into rivers and lakes.
Engineers designed a new system to collect and reuse stormwater for irrigation.
Heavy rain caused stormwater to flood several city streets last night.
The new park includes a system to collect and filter stormwater before it reaches the river.
Engineers designed special drains to manage stormwater from the shopping center's parking lot.

Related Terms

  • synonym: runoff - water from rain or melted snow that flows over the ground surface
  • antonym: wastewater - used water from homes, businesses, or industry that is not clean
