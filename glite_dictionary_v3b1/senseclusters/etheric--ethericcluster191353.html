etheric

adjective
of the ether (physics)
9.9
Pronunciation: /iː.ˈθe.rɪk/ /; / /ɪ.ˈθe.rɪk/ (AmE), /iː.ˈθe.rɪk/ /; / /ɪ.ˈθe.rɪk/ (BrE)
relating to the luminiferous ether, the hypothetical substance once thought to fill space and transmit light.

Examples

Etheric substance
In the 19th century, scientists proposed an etheric substance to explain how light traveled through space.
The old textbooks described light as waves moving through an etheric medium.
Many early theories of physics included the idea of an etheric field filling the universe.
