transgress

verb
violate a law or moral rule
Pronunciation: / trænzˈɡrɛs / (AmE), / trænzˈɡrɛs / (BrE)
to do something that is not allowed by law, rule, or moral principle; to go beyond what is acceptable

Examples

To transgress the law
He felt guilty after he transgressed against his own principles.
If you transgress the rules of the club, you may be asked to leave.
Those who continually transgress may eventually face serious consequences.
Anyone who transgresses the community’s rules faces suspension.
He apologized for having transgressed the company’s code of ethics.
She did not intend to transgress but found herself caught up in circumstances beyond her control.
Anyone who transgresses these boundaries will face serious consequences.
He warned the students not to transgress the rules of the school.
The company transgressed environmental regulations and was fined heavily.

Related Terms

  • antonym: obey - to comply with the command, direction, or request of
  • synonym: overstep - to go beyond what is acceptable or allowed
  • synonym: violate - to break or fail to comply with a rule or law
