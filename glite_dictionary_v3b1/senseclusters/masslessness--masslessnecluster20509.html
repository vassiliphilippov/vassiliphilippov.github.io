masslessness

noun
having no mass
Pronunciation: / ˈmæs.ləs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmæs.ləs.nəs / (BrE)
the state or quality of having no mass

Examples

The masslessness of photons
The masslessness of photons allows them to travel at the speed of light.
Scientists study masslessness to better understand the fundamental forces of nature.
In certain theories, the concept of masslessness is essential for explaining particle behavior.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mass - the property of matter that measures its resistance to acceleration
  • synonym: weightlessness - the state of being without weight
