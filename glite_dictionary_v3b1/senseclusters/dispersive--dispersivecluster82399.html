dispersive

adjective
causing or involving spreading by diffusion
5.9
Pronunciation: / dɪˈspɜrsɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈspɜːsɪv / (BrE)
(of a process or material) tending to cause or involve the spreading of particles, waves, or substances by diffusion.

Examples

Dispersive effect
Light passing through a prism exhibits a dispersive phenomenon, separating into different colors.
Scientists studied the dispersive effects in the chemical solution to understand how it spread.
The dispersive properties of the material affected the behavior of the sound waves.
