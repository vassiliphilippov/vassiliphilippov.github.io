mackinaw

noun
short woolen plaid coat
Pronunciation: / ˈmækɪˌnɔ / (AmE), / ˈmækɪˌnɔː / (BrE)
A short, thick, woolen coat, typically with a plaid pattern, originally worn in North America for warmth.

Examples

A mackinaw coat
A heavy mackinaw is perfect for keeping you warm in the woods.

The vintage store had a red-and-black plaid mackinaw hanging by the door.
