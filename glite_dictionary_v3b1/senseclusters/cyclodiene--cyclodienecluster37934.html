cyclodiene

noun
persistent insecticide
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪkləˈdaɪiːn / (AmE), / ˌsaɪkləˈdaɪiːn / (BrE)
a synthetic chemical compound used as an insecticide, known for its persistence in the environment

Examples

Cyclodiene insecticide
The government banned the use of cyclodiene pesticides because of their environmental impact.
Farmers once relied on cyclodiene compounds to control insect pests in their crops.
Research has shown that cyclodiene residues can remain in soil for many years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: organochlorine - a class of synthetic organic compounds containing chlorine, often used as pesticides
  • antonym: pyrethroid - a synthetic chemical used as an insecticide, less persistent than cyclodienes
