glossiness

noun
smooth and shiny quality
4.1 0.05/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…°l…ëÀêsi.n…ôs / (AmE), / Àà…°l…ís.i.n…ôs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being smooth and shiny, often reflecting light

Examples

The glossiness of paint
After applying the new polish, the car regained its original glossiness.
The glossiness of the marble floor made the room appear brighter and more elegant.
The artist preferred paint with a high glossiness for a more dramatic effect.

Synonyms

  • gloss
    3.8 1.89/m
    gloss is a smooth, shiny look on a surface, while glossiness names the quality of being smooth and reflecting light.
  • shininess
    2.0 0.03/m
    shininess is the simple quality of reflecting light and looking bright, while glossiness adds surface smoothness to that shine.
  • luster
    5.2 0.03/m
    luster is a gentle, often deep shine on materials, while glossiness focuses on smooth, bright reflection from a surface.
  • polish
    3.7 0.34/m
    polish is the smooth, glossy finish made by rubbing, while glossiness describes the quality of smoothness and light reflection.
  • sheen
    4.2 3.78/m
    sheen is a soft, thin shine that looks attractive, while glossiness implies a stronger, more reflective smooth surface.
  • sleekness
    4.3 0.01/m
    sleekness is smooth, streamlined touch and shape often seen in objects, while glossiness focuses on shiny light reflection on a surface.
