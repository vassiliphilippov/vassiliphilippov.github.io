bellarmine

noun
decorated stoneware jug with a bearded face
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛl.ərˌmiːn / (AmE), / ˈbɛl.əˌmiːn / (BrE)
a stoneware jug from the 16th and 17th centuries with a long neck and a bearded face, often a caricature of Cardinal Bellarmine, used mainly for storing and serving drinks

Examples

A bellarmine jug
Collectors value the bellarmine not only for its craftsmanship but also for its historical associations.
She found an old bellarmine in the cellar, covered in layers of dirt but still remarkably intact.
The museum displayed a rare bellarmine from the seventeenth century, complete with its intricately detailed face.
