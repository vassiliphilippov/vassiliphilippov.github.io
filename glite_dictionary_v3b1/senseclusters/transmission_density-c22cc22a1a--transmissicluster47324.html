transmission density

noun
measure of a material's transparency to radiation
4.7 5.7
Pronunciation: / trænzˈmɪʃ.ən ˈdɛn.sə.ti / (AmE), / trænzˈmɪʃ.ən ˈden.sɪ.ti / (BrE)
(Physics) A measure of how much a substance allows light or other electromagnetic radiation to pass through it.

Examples

Transmission density in physics
A higher transmission density usually means the sample is more transparent to electromagnetic radiation.
Scientists measured the transmission density to assess the material's suitability for optical lenses.
The transmission density of the filter determines how much light can pass through.

Related Terms

  • antonym: opacity - the degree to which something is opaque or blocks light
  • synonym: transmittance - the ratio of the transmitted light to the incident light
