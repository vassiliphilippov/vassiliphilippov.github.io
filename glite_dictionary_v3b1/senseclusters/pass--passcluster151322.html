pass

verb
move or travel past something
Pronunciation: / pæs / (AmE), / pɑːs / (BrE)
to move or travel past, across, through, or beyond something

Examples

To pass the house
As we passed the old house, we saw some children playing outside.
She slowed down to let the ambulance pass on the narrow road.
The train will pass the station without stopping.
As we passed the old bridge, memories of childhood came flooding back.
She asked everyone to pass the checkpoint before sunset.
We watched the train pass through the station without stopping.
Her influence continues to pass beyond her lifetime and affect the organization today.
The mountain range passes across three different countries.
The newly built highway will pass through several small villages along its route.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bypass - to go around something instead of through or across it
  • synonym: cross - to go across something from one side to the other
  • synonym: extend - to stretch out over a distance, time, or space
  • synonym: overtake - to catch up with and move in front of (another vehicle or person)
  • synonym: span - to extend the full length of something
