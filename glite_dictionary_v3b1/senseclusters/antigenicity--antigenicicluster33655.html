antigenicity

noun
ability to trigger immunity
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˌæntɪdʒəˈnɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌæntɪdʒɪˈnɪsɪti /; / ˌæntɪdʒɪˈnɪsəti / (BrE)
the ability of a substance to cause the immune system to react or respond

Examples

The antigenicity of vaccine
Researchers tested the antigenicity of the new vaccine to ensure it would trigger a strong immune response.
Changes in protein structure can affect the antigenicity of a virus, making it harder for antibodies to recognize.
The antigenicity of the substance was measured by observing how well it bound to specific antibodies.
Scientists are studying the antigenicity of the virus to develop an effective vaccine.
Changes in protein structure can affect the antigenicity of a pathogen.
The antigenicity of the new drug was evaluated before clinical trials began.

Related Terms

  • synonym: immunogenicity - the ability to provoke an immune response
  • antonym: tolerance - the immune system's lack of response to a substance
