sympathetic strike

noun
a strike in support of other workers
6.5 5.7
Pronunciation: / sɪmˈpæθɛtɪk straɪk / (AmE), / sɪmˈpæθətɪk straɪk / (BrE)
a strike carried out by workers to support others who are striking, rather than due to their own direct grievances.

Examples

A sympathetic strike action
Management was surprised by the scale of the sympathetic strike that disrupted multiple industries.
The union called a sympathetic strike to back the teachers who were already picketing.
Workers at the factory joined a sympathetic strike out of solidarity with employees at a neighboring plant.
