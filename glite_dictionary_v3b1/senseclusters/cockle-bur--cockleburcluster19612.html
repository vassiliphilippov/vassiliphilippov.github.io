cockle-bur

noun
a coarse weed with spiny burrs (Xanthium genus)
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑkəlˌbɜr / (AmE), / ˈkɒkəlˌbɜː / (BrE)
a coarse, weedy plant of the genus Xanthium, known for its spiny, burred fruit that easily clings to animals and clothing

Examples

A cockle-bur plant
Dogs often come home with cockle-bur stuck in their fur after running through the fields.
The cockle-bur is notorious for its clinging burrs and rapid spread in disturbed soils.
The farmer spent hours pulling cockle-bur plants from the fence line.
