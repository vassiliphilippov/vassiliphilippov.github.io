In a way that shows a desire to resist authority or control; in a rebellious manner.

Examples

Answer rebelliously
Even when asked politely, he answered rebelliously, challenging every suggestion.
She rebelliously refused to follow her parents' strict rules.
The student glared rebelliously at the teacher after being scolded.

Synonyms

  • defiantly
    5.4 2.20/m
    defiantly shows bold, open challenge to authority in a clear action, while rebelliously means resisting control in a broader, often emotional way.
  • mutinously
    8.4 0.04/m
    mutinously describes a group's planned revolt against leaders at sea or in the army, while rebelliously can describe individual or general resistance.
  • disobediently
    5.1 0.00/m
    disobediently means refusing to follow rules or orders in a simple way, while rebelliously suggests active desire to challenge authority.
  • contumaciously
    9.9 0.00/m
    contumaciously is a formal, stubborn word for repeated defiance in law or court, while rebelliously is more general and used in everyday speech.
