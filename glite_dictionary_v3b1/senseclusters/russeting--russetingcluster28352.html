russeting

noun
brown fruit skin patch
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌsɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrʌsɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
a rough, brownish skin or patch that develops on the surface of some fruits (especially apples and pears), or the process of developing this skin

Examples

Apple russeting skin
The russeting on the apple did not affect its taste.
Some pear varieties are more prone to russeting than others.
Despite the russeting on its skin, the fruit was perfectly ripe and sweet.
The russeting on the apples made them look less appealing but did not affect their taste.
In autumn, the russeting of the leaves creates a beautiful landscape in the park.
Some fruit varieties are more prone to russeting due to weather conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: browning - the process of turning brown in color
  • antonym: greening - the process of becoming green in color
  • synonym: scab - a rough patch or crust on the surface of fruit or skin
