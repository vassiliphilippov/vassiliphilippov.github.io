vulnerable

adjective
susceptible to attack or harm
Pronunciation: / ˈvʌlnərəb(ə)l /; / ˈvʌlnərəbəl /; / ˈvʌlnɚəbəl / (AmE), / ˈvʌln(ə)rəb(ə)l /; / ˈvʌlnərəbl /; / ˈvʌln(ə)rəbl / (BrE)
able to be easily harmed, attacked, or influenced, either physically, emotionally, or in other ways

Examples

Vulnerable to illness
Children are often vulnerable to colds and other illnesses.
The company’s outdated software made it vulnerable to cyber threats.
The old fortress was vulnerable to enemy attack from the sea.
Children are especially vulnerable to diseases during winter.
She felt vulnerable sharing her story with the group.
The castle's walls were thick, but its gate was vulnerable to attack.
His theory seemed vulnerable to attack because it lacked supporting evidence.
The committee's proposal was vulnerable to sharp criticism from experts in the field.
Young people are often vulnerable to peer pressure and quick to make impulsive decisions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defenseless - without protection; unable to defend oneself
  • antonym: invulnerable - impossible to harm or damage
  • antonym: resistant - not easily affected by something; able to withstand
  • synonym: susceptible - likely to be influenced or harmed; easily affected
