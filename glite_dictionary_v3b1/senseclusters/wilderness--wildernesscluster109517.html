wilderness

noun
state of political disfavor or exclusion
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪl.dɚ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈwɪl.də.nəs / (BrE)
(politics) A metaphorical state of having lost influence, power, or favor, especially after defeat or exclusion from decision-making.

Examples

Political wilderness
After the election loss, the party spent years in the wilderness before regaining power.
His controversial views left him in the political wilderness for a decade.
She returned from the wilderness to lead her team to success.
