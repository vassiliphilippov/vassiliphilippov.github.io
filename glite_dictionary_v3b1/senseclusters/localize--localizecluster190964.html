localize

verb
adapt for a specific market or language
Pronunciation: / c8lo8ak59ccla6az /; / ˈloʊkəlaɪz / (AmE), / c8l598ak59la6az /; / ˈləʊkəlaɪz / (BrE)
(in computing, product development, and marketing) to adapt software, content, or a product so it fits the language, cultural conventions, formatting, and legal requirements of a particular locale or market.

Examples

To localize software
We plan to localize the app for the Japanese market to match language and cultural norms.
The company localized its website, changing images and payment options for local customers.
They are localizing the training materials so employees in each country understand procedures.
The company plans to localise its app for the Japanese market before launch next spring.
They hired translators and designers to localise product packaging for several European countries.
If you want more users, you must localise the website and adapt content to local customs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adapt - to change something so it is suitable for a new purpose or situation; general synonym for making products fit a market
  • synonym: localize - adapt for a particular country or region (American spelling of 'localise').
  • synonym: translate - to render text or speech from one language into another; often a component of localization
