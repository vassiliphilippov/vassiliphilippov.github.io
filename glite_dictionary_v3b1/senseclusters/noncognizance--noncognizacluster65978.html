noncognizance

noun
refusal to consider case
9.9
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈkɑɡnɪzəns / (AmE), / nɒnˈkɒɡnɪzəns / (BrE)
the failure or refusal of a court or official body to take jurisdiction over or consider a case or matter

Examples

Court's noncognizance
The court's noncognizance of the complaint forced the parties to seek another legal remedy.
Because of noncognizance, the tribunal did not review the evidence presented by the defense.
Legal scholars debated whether the judge's noncognizance was justified under the circumstances.

  • synonym: dismissal - the act of rejecting or refusing to consider a case
