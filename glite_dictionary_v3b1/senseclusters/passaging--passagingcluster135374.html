passaging

noun
cell transfer process
8.9
Pronunciation: /ˈpæs.ɪ.dʒɪŋ/ /; / /ˈpæs.ə.dʒɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈpæs.ɪ.dʒɪŋ/ /; / /ˈpæs.ə.dʒɪŋ/ (BrE)
the process of transferring cells from one culture medium to another to maintain or propagate them in laboratory work

Examples

Regular passaging of cells
Regular passaging is necessary to prevent cell cultures from becoming overcrowded.
The technician completed the passaging of stem cells to ensure their continued growth.
Improper passaging can lead to contamination or loss of cell viability.

Related Terms
