telocentric

adjective
chromosome with centromere at the end
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛloʊˈsɛntrɪk / (AmE), / ˌtiːləʊˈsɛntrɪk / (BrE)
(of a chromosome) having the centromere at the terminal end, resulting in a rod-like form.

Examples

Telocentric chromosome end
Humans do not generally have telocentric chromosomes, but they are found in some other species.
Identification of telocentric chromosomes can help scientists understand genetic organization in certain mammals.
Researchers observed several telocentric chromosomes during the experiment with mouse cells.
