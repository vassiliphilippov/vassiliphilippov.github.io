minuscule

noun
medieval small cursive script
8.1 8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪnəˌskjul / (AmE), / ˈmɪnəskjuːl / (BrE)
A small, cursive style of writing developed from uncial between the 7th and 9th centuries and used in medieval manuscripts.

Examples

Medieval minuscule script
Original documents written in minuscule are often hard for modern readers to decipher.
Scholars can distinguish Carolingian minuscule from later medieval handwriting styles.
The ancient monk copied the manuscript in elegant minuscule script.
