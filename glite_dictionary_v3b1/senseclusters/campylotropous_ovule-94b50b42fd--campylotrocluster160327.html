campylotropous ovule

noun
a curved ovule in plants
9.9
Pronunciation: /kæm.pɪ.lə.ˈtroʊ.pəs ˈəʊ.vjuːl/ /; / /kæm.pɪ.lə.ˈtroʊ.pəs ˈoʊ.vjuːl/ (AmE), /kæm.pɪ.lə.ˈtrəʊ.pəs ˈɒ.vjuːl/ /; / /kæm.pɪ.lə.ˈtrəʊ.pəs ˈəʊ.vjuːl/ (BrE)
an ovule in which the body is curved so that the micropyle is almost in contact with the funiculus

Examples

A campylotropous ovule structure
A campylotropous ovule differs from orthotropous and anatropous ovules in its structure.
Botanists use the term campylotropous ovule to describe this distinctive curvature.
The campylotropous ovule is common in members of the legume family.
