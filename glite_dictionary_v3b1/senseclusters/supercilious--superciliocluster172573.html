supercilious

adjective
arrogantly superior or disdainful
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərˈsɪliəs /; / ˌsuː.pɚˈsɪl.i.əs / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˈsɪlɪəs /; / ˌsuː.pəˈsɪl.i.əs / (BrE)
behaving or looking as if you think you are better than other people; showing arrogant or disdainful attitude

Examples

Supercilious tone
He spoke in a supercilious tone that offended the entire committee.
Her supercilious attitude made it difficult for her to make friends at her new school.
The waiter gave us a supercilious glance as we entered the expensive restaurant.
His supercilious attitude made it difficult for others to work with him.
She gave him a supercilious glance and walked away without answering.
The supercilious tone in her voice revealed her contempt for the guests.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arrogant - having an exaggerated sense of one's own importance or abilities
  • synonym: haughty - arrogantly superior and disdainful
  • antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's importance
