to form a mental image of something; to imagine or visualize in the mind

Examples

To picture a place
He tried to picture what life would be like in a new city.
It's hard to picture her being angry because she's usually so calm.
When you close your eyes, can you picture the house where you grew up?

Synonyms

  • visualize
    3.8 5.14/m
    visualize creates a clear mental image with sensory detail, while picture means to imagine something in the mind.
  • imagine
    1.3 133/m
    imagine forms a general idea or scene freely, while picture often suggests a detailed mental image you can see in your mind.
  • see
    1.0 114/m
    see can mean mentally picture something as if watching it, while picture focuses on deliberately forming that inner image.
  • envisage
    6.8 2.66/m
    envisage treats an idea as a real possibility to imagine, while picture focuses on forming a clear mental image.
  • envision
    4.1 15/m
    envision imagines a future scene or plan more actively, while picture simply means to form a mental image or idea.
  • fancy
    4.4 2.63/m
    fancy suggests an odd or wishful mental idea and is more informal, while picture means to form a clear mental image.
