Cassia acutifolia

noun
a shrub used medicinally for its cathartic leaves
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkæsi.ə ˌækjuˈtɪfəli.ə / (AmE), / ˌkæsi.ə ˌækjuːˈtɪfəliːə / (BrE)
an erect shrub native to northeast Africa and Arabia, with racemes of tawny yellow flowers; its dried leaves are used medicinally as a cathartic

Examples

Cassia acutifolia leaves
Researchers found that extracts from Cassia acutifolia leaves had notable cathartic effects in laboratory studies.
The active compounds in Cassia acutifolia are commonly used in herbal medicine as a natural laxative.
The dried leaves of Cassia acutifolia are sometimes brewed into a tea to relieve occasional constipation.

Related Terms
