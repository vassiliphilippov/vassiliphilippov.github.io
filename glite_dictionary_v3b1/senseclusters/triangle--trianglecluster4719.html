triangle

verb
to use triangulation
Pronunciation: / ˈtraɪæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈtraɪæŋɡl / (BrE)
to determine or fix a position or distance by triangulation, using measurements from at least three points

Examples

To triangle a location
The surveyors triangled the new bridge site to ensure accurate placement.
Early explorers triangled their position using distant mountain peaks as reference points.
Engineers triangled the area before starting construction to map the land precisely.

Related Terms

  • synonym: triangulate - to determine a location by measuring angles from known points
