unsaturation

noun
lack of color intensity
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnsætʃəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌʌnsætʃəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the quality of a color being less vivid or intense due to a lack of saturation

Examples

Color unsaturation
The unsaturation of the painting's colors created a calm and subdued mood.
Photographers sometimes use unsaturation to give images a vintage or dreamy effect.
The designer adjusted the unsaturation to make the background less distracting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: desaturation - the process of reducing the intensity of a color
  • antonym: saturation - the intensity or purity of a color
