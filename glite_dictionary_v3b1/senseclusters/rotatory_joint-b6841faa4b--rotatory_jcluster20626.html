rotatory joint

noun
joint permitting rotation only
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊtəˌtɔri dʒɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈrəʊtəˌtəri dʒɔɪnt / (BrE)
an anatomical joint that allows movement only around a single axis, enabling rotation

Examples

A rotatory joint movement
A rotatory joint permits limited movement, strictly in the form of rotation.
Doctors examined the condition of the patient's rotatory joint after the injury.
The elbow contains a rotatory joint between the radius and ulna, allowing the forearm to twist.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pivot joint - a freely moving joint that allows only rotary movement around a single axis
