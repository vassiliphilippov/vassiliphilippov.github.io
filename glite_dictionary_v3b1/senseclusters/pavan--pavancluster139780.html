pavan

noun
stately Renaissance court dance
4.8
Pronunciation: / pəˈvɑn / (AmE), / pəˈvɑːn / (BrE)
a slow, stately court dance popular in Europe during the 16th and 17th centuries

Examples

A courtly pavan
In the dance workshop, students learned the intricate steps of the pavan.
The composer wrote a beautiful pavan to evoke the elegance of the Renaissance.
The noble couples moved gracefully through the hall, performing the pavan.
