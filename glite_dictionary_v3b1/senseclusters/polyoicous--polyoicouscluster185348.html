polyoicous

adjective
having several forms of gametoecia on one plant
9.9
Pronunciation: /pɑː.lɪ.ˈɔɪ.kəs/ (AmE), /pɒ.lɪ.ˈɔɪ.kəs/ (BrE)
(of a plant) having several forms of reproductive structures (gametoecia) on the same individual plant.

Examples

Polyoicous plant forms
Researchers studied the reproductive biology of polyoicous plants to understand their evolutionary adaptations.
Some bryophytes display a polyoicous condition, producing several types of gametoecia.
The moss was identified as a polyoicous species due to its variety of reproductive organs on a single plant.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dioicous - having male and female reproductive organs on separate plants
  • degree lower: monoicous - having both male and female reproductive organs on the same plant
