testability

noun
degree of being testable
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛstəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌtɛstəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or degree to which something (such as a theory or hypothesis) can be tested or evaluated, especially by experiment or observation

Examples

The testability of a theory
The testability of a scientific hypothesis is crucial for its acceptance.
Engineers improved the testability of the product by adding clear diagnostic features.
High testability makes it easier to identify and fix problems in software.
Scientists debated the testability of the new theory before accepting it as a valid explanation.
A key requirement for a good hypothesis is its testability through experiments or observation.
The testability of a claim determines whether it can be supported or refuted by evidence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: falsifiability - the ability of a theory or statement to be proven false
  • antonym: unfalsifiability - the quality of not being able to be proven false
  • antonym: untestability - the quality of not being able to be tested
  • synonym: verifiability - the quality of being able to be proven true or false
