Judean

noun
person from Judea
Pronunciation: / dʒuˈdiːən / (AmE), / dʒuːˈdiːən / (BrE)
a person from Judea, especially in ancient or historical contexts

Examples

An ancient Judean
The ancient Judean city was known for its impressive architecture and bustling markets.
A Judean merchant traveled across the Mediterranean to sell olive oil and spices.
Many Judeans played important roles in the history of the region.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Galilean - a person from Galilee, a region near Judea
  • synonym: Jew - a member of the people and cultural community whose traditional religion is Judaism
