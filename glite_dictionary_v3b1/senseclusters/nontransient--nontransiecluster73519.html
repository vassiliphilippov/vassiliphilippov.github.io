nontransient

adjective
lasting; persistent
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˈtrænziənt / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈtrænziənt / (BrE)
lasting or persistent over time; not temporary or short-lived

Examples

A nontransient effect
The researchers observed a nontransient effect that persisted throughout the experiment.
A nontransient increase in temperature can indicate a serious problem in the system.
Unlike a temporary error, a nontransient issue requires immediate attention and repair.

Related Terms

  • synonym: enduring - continuing or long-lasting
  • synonym: permanent - lasting or intended to last indefinitely
  • antonym: transient - lasting only for a short time; temporary
