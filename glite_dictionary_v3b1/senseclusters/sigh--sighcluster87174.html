sigh

noun
audible exhalation expressing emotion
Pronunciation: / saɪ / (AmE), / saɪ / (BrE)
a long, deep, audible breath expressing emotion such as sadness, relief, or tiredness, or a sound resembling this

Examples

A deep sigh
She let out a sigh after hearing the disappointing news.
There was a long sigh of relief when the test was finally over.
With a sigh, he sat down and rested his tired feet.
A soft sigh of wind rustled the curtains in the evening light.
The old house seemed to breathe, each board creaking with a sigh as the temperature dropped.
With a sigh, she put the letter down and wiped away a tear.

Related Terms

  • synonym: groan - a deep, inarticulate sound expressing pain or despair
  • synonym: moan - a long, low sound made when expressing pain or unhappiness
