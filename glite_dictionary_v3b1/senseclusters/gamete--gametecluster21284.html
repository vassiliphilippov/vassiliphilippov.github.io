gamete

noun
mature reproductive cell (sperm or egg)
7.9 0.78/m

A mature sexual reproductive cell, such as a sperm or an egg, that has a single set of unpaired chromosomes and can unite with another cell to form a new organism.

Examples

A gamete cell
During fertilization, a male gamete combines with a female gamete to form a zygote.
Each gamete carries only half the genetic information needed to produce an offspring.
Scientists observed the movement of the gamete under a microscope.

Synonyms

  • sex cell
    4.1 0.06/m
    sex cell is the everyday name for a sperm or egg, while gamete is the scientific, biological term for the same cells.
  • reproductive cell
    3.4 0.09/m
    reproductive cell is a formal term for a sperm or egg in general biology, while gamete refers specifically to a mature haploid reproductive cell.
  • germ cell
    5.8 0.15/m
    germ cell is a reproductive-line cell that becomes sperm or eggs, while gamete is the mature sex cell that unites to form a new organism.
