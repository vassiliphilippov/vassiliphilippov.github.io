overactivate

verb
to activate too much
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈæktɪveɪt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈæktɪveɪt / (BrE)
to cause something to become active to an excessive or abnormal degree

Examples

To overactivate nerves
Some medications can overactivate certain parts of the nervous system, leading to side effects.
If you overactivate the alarm system, it may become too sensitive and trigger false alerts.
Researchers found that stress can overactivate the body's immune response.
