reformative

adjective
tending to bring about reform
4.0 6.8
Pronunciation: / rɪˈfɔrmətɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈfɔːmətɪv / (BrE)
Tending or intended to bring about reform or improvement, especially by correcting faults or deficiencies.

Examples

A reformative approach
Her approach to discipline is more reformative than punitive, focusing on positive change.
Many modern prisons aim to be reformative rather than simply punitive.
The government introduced reformative measures to address the issues in the education system.
