presession

noun
event before main session
Pronunciation: / priːˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / priːˈsɛʃən / (BrE)
a meeting, event, or period of time that takes place before the main session or conference, often used for preparation or introduction

Examples

A presession meeting
The organizers scheduled a presession to introduce the conference topics to new attendees.
We attended a helpful presession workshop before the main event started.
A short presession allowed participants to network before the official meeting began.
The organizers used the presession to finalize the agenda and test the microphones.
Arriving early for the presession allowed participants to network before the main conference started.
During the presession, volunteers arranged chairs and distributed materials to attendees.

Related Terms

  • antonym: postsession - an event or meeting held after the main session
  • synonym: preliminary - an event or action that comes before something more important
  • synonym: prelude - an introductory event or action before something more important
