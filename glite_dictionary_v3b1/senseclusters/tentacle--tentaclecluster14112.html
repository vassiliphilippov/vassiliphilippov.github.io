tentacle

noun
elongated flexible organ of animals
6.2 4.0
Pronunciation: /ˈten.tə.kəl/ /; / /ˈten.tɪ.kəl/ (AmE), /ˈten.tə.kəl/ /; / /ˈten.tɪ.kəl/ (BrE)
A long, flexible organ extending from the head or near the mouth of certain animals, used for touching, grasping, or movement.

Examples

Jellyfish tentacle
Jellyfish sting their prey with their tentacles.
Some plants, like the sundew, have sticky tentacles for catching insects.
The octopus used a tentacle to investigate the small opening in the rock.
