play off

verb
deliberately set in opposition
4.9
Pronunciation: / pleɪ ɔf / (AmE), / pleɪ ɒf / (BrE)
to deliberately cause people or groups to compete or be in opposition to each other, often to one's own advantage

Examples

To play off two teams

The manager was accused of playing off staff members against each other to boost productivity.
