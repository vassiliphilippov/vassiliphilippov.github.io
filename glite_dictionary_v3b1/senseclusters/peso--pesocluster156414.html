peso

noun
Uruguayan unit of currency
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪsoʊ / (AmE), / ˈpeɪsəʊ / (BrE)
the basic unit of currency in several countries, especially in Latin America and the Philippines

Examples

The peso currency
All transactions in Uruguay are settled in pesos.
She exchanged her dollars for Uruguayan pesos at the bank.
The price of lunch was just over 300 pesos.
He counted the pesos left in his wallet after dinner.
I exchanged my dollars for pesos at the airport before leaving for Manila.
The souvenir cost 500 pesos in the local market.
He exchanged his American money for Cuban pesos at the airport.
The Cuban peso has experienced significant inflation in recent years.
The menu listed prices in both dollars and pesos.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: centavo - a monetary unit equal to one hundredth of a peso
  • degree lower: centésimo - one hundredth of a peso (Uruguayan currency)
  • synonym: dollar - the basic monetary unit in several countries including the US, parallel to peso as a major currency
  • synonym: peso - the basic unit of money in several other countries, such as Mexico, Argentina, or the Philippines
