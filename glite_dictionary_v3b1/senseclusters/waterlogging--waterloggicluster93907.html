waterlogging

noun
flooding of areas
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtərˌlɔɡɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌlɒɡɪŋ / (BrE)
the accumulation of water in an area, such as streets or buildings, that disrupts normal use or causes damage

Examples

City waterlogging
Heavy rains caused severe waterlogging in several neighborhoods, making roads difficult to use.
The city installed better drainage systems to prevent waterlogging during the rainy season.
After the storm, waterlogging forced many businesses to close for repairs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: drought - a long period with little or no rain
  • synonym: flooding - an overflow of water that submerges land or property
