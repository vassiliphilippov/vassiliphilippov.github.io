rectifying tube

noun
thermionic tube used as a rectifier
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛktəˌfaɪɪŋ tuːb / (AmE), / ˈrɛktɪfaɪɪŋ tjuːb / (BrE)
A thermionic tube with two electrodes, used as a rectifier to convert alternating current to direct current.

Examples

A rectifying tube device
Advancements in technology have largely replaced the rectifying tube with semiconductor diodes.
Early radios used a rectifying tube to change AC into DC power.
The engineer replaced the faulty rectifying tube in the amplifier circuit.

Related Terms
