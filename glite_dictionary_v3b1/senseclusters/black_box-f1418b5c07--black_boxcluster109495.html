black box

noun
device that records aircraft flight data
1.4
Pronunciation: / blæk bɑks / (AmE), / blæk bɒks / (BrE)
an electronic device in an aircraft that records flight data and cockpit voice information, used to investigate accidents

Examples

Aircraft black box
Every commercial aircraft is equipped with a black box to record flight information.
Investigators recovered the black box to learn what happened before the crash.
The data stored in the black box helped explain the cause of the accident.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flight recorder - an instrument that records various aspects of an aircraft's operation
