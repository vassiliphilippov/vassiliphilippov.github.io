flash

verb
show briefly; expose quickly
5.5 2.8
Pronunciation: /flæʃ/ (AmE), /flæʃ/ (BrE)
to show or display something quickly or briefly; to show off or display in a way meant to attract attention

Examples

To flash a smile
He flashed a confident smile during the interview.
She flashed her ID badge at the security guard and walked in.
The thief flashed a knife before running away.
Celebrities often flash their wealth on social media.
He couldn't resist flashing his expensive watch at the party.
The young woman flashed her engagement ring to her friends.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conceal - to hide or keep something from being seen
  • synonym: display - to show something to people; to exhibit
  • synonym: flaunt - to display something ostentatiously, especially in order to provoke envy or admiration
  • synonym: show off - to behave in a way that is intended to attract attention or admiration, often by displaying possessions, skills, etc.
