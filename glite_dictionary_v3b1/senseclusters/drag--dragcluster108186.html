drag

verb
pull with effort or difficulty
Pronunciation: / dræɡ / (AmE), / dræɡ / (BrE)
to pull or move something or someone along the ground or through something with effort or difficulty, or to force someone into a situation unwillingly

Examples

To drag a suitcase
She dragged the suitcase behind her on the way to the train station.
The fisherman dragged his net slowly along the riverbed.
We had to drag the sofa across the room because it was too heavy to lift.
Divers used a large net to drag the riverbed for the missing jewelry.
The police had to drag the lake in search of evidence.
They dragged the harbor for several hours before finding the sunken boat.
She had to drag the heavy box up the stairs by herself.
The news seemed to drag him down, making him feel tired all day.
You shouldn't drag your feet when you walk; you'll wear out your shoes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coax - to gently persuade someone to do something
  • degree higher: coerce - to force someone to do something by use of threats or pressure
  • synonym: haul - to pull with force, especially something heavy
  • synonym: involve - to include someone in an activity or situation
  • synonym: lag - to fall behind in movement, progress, or development
  • antonym: lead - to be ahead or in front of others
  • degree lower: persuade - to cause someone to do something by advising or urging
  • synonym: pull - to move something towards yourself or in the direction you are going
  • antonym: push - to move something away by exerting force
  • synonym: trail - to move or lag slowly behind
