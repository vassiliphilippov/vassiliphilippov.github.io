let it be

verb
accept without interfering
Pronunciation: / ˌlɛt ɪt ˈbi / (AmE), / ˌlɛt ɪt ˈbiː / (BrE)
to accept a situation as it is without attempting to change or interfere with it

Examples

To let it be
If you can't fix the problem right now, you might as well let it be.
She wanted to argue, but chose to let it be and move on.
Sometimes it's best to just let it be rather than cause more trouble.

Related Terms

  • antonym: interfere - to involve oneself in a situation where one is not wanted or needed
  • synonym: leave alone - to not disturb or interfere with someone or something
