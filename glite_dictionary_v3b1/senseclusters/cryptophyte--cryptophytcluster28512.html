cryptophyte

noun
aquatic unicellular alga
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪptoʊˌfaɪt / (AmE), / ˈkrɪptəʊˌfaɪt / (BrE)
A unicellular aquatic alga found in fresh and salt water, often appearing along shores as algal blooms.

Examples

A cryptophyte alga
A sudden increase in cryptophyte populations led to extensive algal blooms in the lake.
Marine biologists are studying the role of cryptophyte algae in coastal ecosystems.
Under the microscope, the cryptophyte exhibited unique nucleomorphs within its cells.
