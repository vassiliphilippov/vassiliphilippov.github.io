localization

noun
assignment of functions to specific body parts
Pronunciation: / ˌloʊkəlaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌloʊkələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌləʊkəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the principle that specific functions are associated with particular, limited regions of an organ, especially in the brain or body

Examples

Brain localization
Early theories about localization of cognitive abilities led to the identification of specialized brain regions.
Modern imaging techniques have provided strong evidence supporting the localization of sensory functions in the brain.
The localization of speech production in Broca’s area is a classic finding in neuroscience.
Localisation of sensory functions allows neurologists to diagnose certain types of brain injuries.
Recent studies challenge the strict localisation of memory functions within the hippocampus.
The concept of localisation suggests that language abilities reside in specific areas of the brain.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diffusion - the opposite principle, with no specific functional regions
  • synonym: lateralization - the localization of function to one side of the brain or body
  • synonym: localization - US spelling of 'localisation', same meaning
