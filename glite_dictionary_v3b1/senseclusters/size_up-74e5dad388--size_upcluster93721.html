size up

verb
examine to form a judgment
4.8
Pronunciation: / saɪz ʌp / (AmE), / saɪz ʌp / (BrE)
to look at or think about someone or something carefully in order to form a judgment or opinion

Examples

To size up a person
As he walked into the room, everyone sized up the new manager with curiosity.
He took a moment to size up the competition before the race began.
She quickly sized up the situation and decided to call for help.
Before making his decision, he sized up all the available options.
She walked into the meeting and immediately sized up her opponents.
The manager quickly sized up the new applicant during the interview.
