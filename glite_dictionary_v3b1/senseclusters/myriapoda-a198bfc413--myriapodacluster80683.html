Myriapoda

noun
a group of segmented arthropods, e.g. millipedes
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪriˈæpədə / (AmE), / ˌmɪrɪˈapədə / (BrE)
a group of arthropods with many body segments and legs, especially millipedes and similar creatures

Examples

Myriapoda group
Although centipedes are not part of the Myriapoda, they are often confused with millipedes.
Scientists study the Myriapoda to better understand terrestrial arthropod evolution.
The Myriapoda include familiar creatures such as millipedes, which can be found in moist soil.
Members of the Myriapoda are easily recognized by their segmented, many-legged bodies.
Scientists recently discovered a new species in the Myriapoda class in the Amazon rainforest.
Unlike insects, organisms in the Myriapoda class have two pairs of legs per segment.
Research on the evolutionary origins of Myriapoda has provided new insights into arthropod relationships.
Some scientists once classified both Pauropoda and Symphyla within Myriapoda, though this usage has become less common.
The superclass Myriapoda includes creatures such as centipedes and millipedes in biological classification.
