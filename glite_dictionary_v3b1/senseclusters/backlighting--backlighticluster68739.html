backlighting

noun
lighting from behind
1.0 4.5
Pronunciation: /ˈbæk.laɪ.tɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈbæk.laɪ.tɪŋ/ (BrE)
illumination that comes from behind the subject, often used to create depth or highlight edges

Examples

Backlighting effect
Excessive backlighting can make it difficult to see what's on your computer screen.
In films, backlighting is often employed to create a sense of mystery or separation.
The photographer used backlighting to give the portrait a dramatic effect.
