osteomalacia

noun
softening of the bones (medical)
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːstiəʊməˈleɪʃə / (AmE), / ˌɒstiəʊməˈleɪʃə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by the softening of bones due to a deficiency of vitamin D or minerals such as calcium or phosphorus.

Examples

Osteomalacia bone softening
Laboratory tests confirmed that the cause of her bone pain was osteomalacia.
People who do not get enough sunlight are at risk of developing osteomalacia.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with osteomalacia due to long-term vitamin D deficiency.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rickets - a similar softening of bones in children caused by vitamin D deficiency
