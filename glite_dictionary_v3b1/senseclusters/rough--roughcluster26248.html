rough

verb
prepare in a preliminary or sketchy form
6.0 6.5
Pronunciation: / rʌf / (AmE), / rʌf / (BrE)
to make or treat something in a rough, uneven, or preliminary way; to sketch or prepare a draft

Examples

To rough a plan
Before painting, she likes to rough her ideas on canvas to visualize the composition.
I asked the architect to rough a plan of the new building before the meeting.
They roughed an outline of the proposal on a piece of paper.
The carpenter roughed the wood before applying the final finish.
She roughed the dough to give the bread a rustic texture.
The artist roughs the surface of the canvas to create interesting effects.

Related Terms

  • antonym: finalize - to complete an unfinished work
  • synonym: scrape - to rub or remove the surface of something harshly
  • synonym: sketch - to make a rough drawing or outline
  • antonym: smooth - to make something even or free from roughness
