go down south

verb
travel southward; journey to the south
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ daʊn saʊθ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ daʊn saʊθ / (BrE)
to travel to or toward a region further south, often for vacation or seasonal reasons.

Examples

To go down south for winter
Every winter, many retirees go down south to escape the cold weather.
She decided to go down south after college to start a new job.
We're planning to go down south for a beach holiday next month.
