isotonic exercise

noun
exercise involving moving muscles under constant tension
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪ.səˈtɑ.nɪk ˈɛk.sɚ.saɪz / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.səʊˈtɒn.ɪk ˈɛk.sə.saɪz / (BrE)
a form of physical exercise in which muscles contract and move under constant tension, causing the muscle length to change

Examples

Isotonic exercise with weights
Lifting free weights is a common form of isotonic exercise at the gym.
Performing squats and push-ups are both examples of isotonic exercise that help build strength.
Physical therapists often recommend isotonic exercise to improve joint mobility after injury.

Related Terms

  • antonym: isometric exercise - exercise with muscle contraction without changing length
