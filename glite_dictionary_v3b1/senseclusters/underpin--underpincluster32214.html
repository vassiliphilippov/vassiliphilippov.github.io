underpin

verb
support or strengthen with evidence
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈpɪn / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈpɪn / (BrE)
to support or strengthen something (especially an idea, argument, or conclusion) with evidence, reasoning, or a firm foundation

Examples

To underpin an argument
Her conclusions were underpinned by solid evidence and clear logic.
The report was carefully underpinned by statistical data.
These theories are underpinned by years of scientific research.
Her thesis is underpinned by extensive research and evidence.
Strong family values often underpin a stable society.
The workers had to underpin the building to prevent it from collapsing.
Her analysis underpins the entire argument of the book.
Recent data underpins the claims made in the research paper.
Sound reasoning is needed to underpin your position in a debate.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contradict - to assert the opposite, or deny the truth of something
  • synonym: support - to give assistance or backing to something
  • antonym: undermine - to weaken or lessen the effectiveness of something
