silt

verb
become blocked with fine sediment
5.9
Pronunciation: / sɪlt / (AmE), / sɪlt / (BrE)
to become filled or blocked with silt, or to cause a place to become filled with silt

Examples

Canals silt up
If the canals are not maintained, they may eventually silt and become unusable.
Many small harbors silt after heavy rains, making navigation difficult.
Over the years, the reservoir began to silt, reducing its water storage capacity.
Heavy rains are silting the river, making it difficult for boats to pass.
The harbor gradually silts up every year, requiring regular dredging.
If the channel silts over, large ships will not be able to enter the port.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clear - to remove obstructions or make free from blockage
  • synonym: clog - to block or become blocked with an accumulation of matter
  • synonym: silt up - become blocked with silt
