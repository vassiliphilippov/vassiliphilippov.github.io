Dravidian language

noun
a South Indian language family
Pronunciation: / drəˈvɪdiən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / drəˈvɪdɪən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
any of a family of languages spoken mainly in southern and central India, Sri Lanka, and parts of Southeast Asia, including Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, and Malayalam.

Examples

Dravidian language family
Linguists have traced the origins of many words in a Dravidian language back thousands of years.
Malayalam, another Dravidian language, is widely used in the Indian state of Kerala.
Tamil is a prominent Dravidian language spoken by millions in southern India and Sri Lanka.
