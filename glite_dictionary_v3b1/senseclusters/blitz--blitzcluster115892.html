blitz

verb
to attack intensively
4.2
Pronunciation: / blɪts / (AmE), / blɪts / (BrE)
to do something or carry out a campaign very quickly and with a lot of energy

Examples

To blitz social media
The marketing team blitzed social media with ads for the new product launch.
During the campaign, volunteers blitzed the neighborhood with flyers and posters.
Reporters blitzed the celebrity with questions as she left the building.
She decided to blitz through her homework before dinner.
We need to blitz this project if we want to finish by Friday.
He blitzed the cleaning, finishing the whole house in just an hour.
