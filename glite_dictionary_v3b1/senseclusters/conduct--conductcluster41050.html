conduct

verb
behave in a particular way

to behave in a particular way, especially in relation to others or in a specific situation

Examples

To conduct oneself well
He always tries to conduct himself with professionalism, even under pressure.
The student was praised for the way she conducted herself during the debate.
Tourists are expected to conduct themselves respectfully at cultural sites.

Synonyms

  • behave
    1.0 36/m
    behave means to act politely or properly toward others, while conduct describes how someone behaves in situations.
  • comport
    8.0 1.11/m
    comport means to behave according to expected rules or standards, while conduct is the general word for how someone behaves.
  • act
    1.0 68/m
    act means to behave in a certain way and can include pretending, while conduct means how someone behaves in situations.
  • carry
    3.0 2.70/m
    carry means to hold or show a manner or bearing in public, while conduct means to behave in a situation.
  • bear
    6.7 3.10/m
    bear means to hold or show oneself with a certain manner, while conduct means to behave toward others in situations.
  • do
    1.0 222/m
    do means to act or behave in some way in informal speech, while conduct is a more formal word for behavior.
  • deport
    6.1 0.09/m
    deport means to behave in a formal, old-fashioned way, while conduct is the common modern word for how someone behaves.
