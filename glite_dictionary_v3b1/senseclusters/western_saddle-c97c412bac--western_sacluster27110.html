Western saddle

noun
ornamented cowboy saddle with horn
7.1 3.3
Pronunciation: /ˈwe.stərn ˈsæd.əl/ /; / /ˈhwe.stərn ˈsæd.əl/ (AmE), /ˈwe.stən ˈsæd.əl/ (BrE)
A type of horseback riding saddle with a high horn at the front, designed for use by cowboys and commonly decorated.

Examples

A Western saddle horn
Her Western saddle was beautifully tooled with floral designs and silver accents.
Riders often prefer a Western saddle when competing in rodeo events for its comfort and security.
The cowboy expertly tightened the girth of his Western saddle before heading out on the trail ride.

Related Terms

  • antonym: English saddle - a lighter, flatter type of saddle used for English-style riding
  • synonym: saddle - a seat fastened on the back of a horse for riding
