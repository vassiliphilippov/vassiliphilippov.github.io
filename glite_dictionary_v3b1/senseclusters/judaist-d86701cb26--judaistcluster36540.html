Judaist

noun
Christian advocate of Jewish law
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒuːdəɪɪst / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːdəɪɪst / (BrE)
a person, especially in historical or theological contexts, who advocates that Christians should observe Jewish law

Examples

A Judaist teacher
The council debated whether the views of the Judaist should influence church policy.
Some early Christian communities were divided by the teachings of a prominent Judaist.
Letters from church leaders often addressed conflicts caused by Judaist doctrines.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Judaizer - a person who tries to make others adopt Jewish customs or beliefs
