dragline

noun
rope or cable for dragging
Pronunciation: / ˈdræɡˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈdræɡˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a rope, cable, or chain that is dragged along the ground or seafloor to move, anchor, dredge, or catch fish

Examples

A dragline cable
The workers used a dragline to pull heavy debris from the construction site.
Fishermen lowered the dragline into the water to catch fish near the riverbed.
A strong dragline is essential for safely moving large objects across rough terrain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anchor line - a rope or cable used to anchor a vessel
  • synonym: towline - a rope or cable used for towing
