degree Fahrenheit

noun
unit of temperature (Fahrenheit)
5.9 2.6
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈgriː ˈfær.ən.haɪt/ /; / /də.ˈgriː ˈfær.ən.haɪt/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈgriː ˈfær.ən.haɪt/ /; / /də.ˈgriː ˈfær.ən.haɪt/ (BrE)
A unit of temperature equal to one increment on the Fahrenheit scale, commonly used in the United States and some other countries.

Examples

350 degrees Fahrenheit
The oven should be preheated to 350 degrees Fahrenheit before baking the cake.
Tomorrow's high will reach 90 degrees Fahrenheit, so remember to stay hydrated.
Water freezes at 32 degrees Fahrenheit under standard conditions.
