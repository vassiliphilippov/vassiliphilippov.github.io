cuffy

proper noun
Guyanese rebel leader
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌfi / (AmE), / ˈkʌfi / (BrE)
the name of an 18th-century enslaved African leader who led the 1763 Berbice slave uprising in present-day Guyana, symbolizing resistance

Examples

Cuffy the leader
Many people in Guyana honor Cuffy as a national hero for his leadership in the 1763 uprising.
Statues of Cuffy can be found in Georgetown, symbolizing freedom and resistance.
The story of Cuffy inspires annual celebrations of emancipation in Guyana.

  • synonym: Toussaint Louverture - leader of the Haitian Revolution against French colonial rule
