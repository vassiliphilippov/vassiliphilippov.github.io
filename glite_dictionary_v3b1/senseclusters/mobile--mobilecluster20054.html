mobile

adjective
able to change or move quickly and easily
7.1 2.3
Pronunciation: /ˈmoʊ.bəl/ /; / /ˈmoʊ.baɪl/ /; / /ˈmoʊ.biːl/ (AmE), /ˈməʊ.baɪl/ /; / /ˈməʊ.biːl/ (BrE)
able to move or be moved easily or freely; able to change or travel easily

Examples

A mobile person
A child's mobile imagination can quickly shift from one idea to another.
Her mobile features expressed every emotion clearly.
The actor's mobile face was perfect for the role of a comedian.
Having a bicycle makes students much more mobile around campus.
In rural areas, being mobile often depends on access to public transportation.
Without a car, she no longer felt as mobile as she once did.
In a mobile society, people often relocate for work or lifestyle preferences.
Many birds are mobile during certain seasons, traveling great distances to find warmer climates.
The mobile nature of hunter-gatherer communities allowed them to adapt to changing environments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agile - able to move quickly and easily
  • antonym: immobile - unable to move or change quickly
  • synonym: migratory - moving from place to place, especially periodically
  • synonym: nomadic - tending to travel from place to place rather than settle permanently
  • antonym: stationary - not moving; fixed in one place
