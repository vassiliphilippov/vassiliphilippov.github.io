water shamrock

noun
aquatic plant with trifoliate leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtər ˈʃæm.rɑk / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˈʃæm.rɒk / (BrE)
A perennial aquatic plant found in Europe and America, known for its clusters of white or purplish flowers and bitter, three-lobed leaves.

Examples

Water shamrock plant
Gardeners often use water shamrock to add greenery to water features.
The intensely bitter taste of water shamrock leaves deters most animals from eating them.
The pond was surrounded by water shamrock with its distinctive three-part leaves.
