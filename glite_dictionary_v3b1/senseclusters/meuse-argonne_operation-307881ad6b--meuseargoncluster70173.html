Meuse-Argonne operation

noun
American World War I military campaign (1918)
9.9
Pronunciation: /mjuːz ˈɑːr.gɑːn ɑː.pər.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /mjuːz ɑː.ˈgɒn ɒp.ə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a major American-led military campaign in World War I (1918), in which Allied forces, under General Pershing, launched an offensive that ultimately pushed German armies back until the armistice of November 11, 1918.

Examples

The Meuse-Argonne operation offensive
General Pershing led U.S. forces in the Meuse-Argonne operation that helped end the war.
Historians often discuss the impact of the Meuse-Argonne operation on the final outcome of World War I.
The Meuse-Argonne operation was the largest American military offensive of World War I.
