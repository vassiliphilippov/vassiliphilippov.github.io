ritardando

noun
slowing of tempo
Pronunciation: / ˌriːtɑːrˈdɑːndoʊ / (AmE), / ˌrɪtɑːˈdændəʊ / (BrE)
a gradual slowing down of the tempo in a piece of music

Examples

A ritardando passage
The conductor signaled a ritardando as the orchestra approached the final bars.
A clear ritardando gives the ending of the song a dramatic effect.
Musicians must watch closely for the ritardando marked in the score.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accelerando - a gradual speeding up of the tempo in music
  • synonym: rallentando - a gradual slowing down of the tempo in music
