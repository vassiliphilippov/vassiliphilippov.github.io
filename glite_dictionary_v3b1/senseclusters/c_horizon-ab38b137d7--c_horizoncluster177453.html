the soil layer situated beneath the B horizon and above the bedrock, consisting mainly of weathered rock and little organic material

Examples

The C horizon layer
The C horizon contains mostly partially disintegrated and weathered rock fragments.
Unlike the upper layers, the C horizon holds little organic matter and few nutrients.
When digging deeper, geologists often analyze the C horizon to understand the origin of the soil.
