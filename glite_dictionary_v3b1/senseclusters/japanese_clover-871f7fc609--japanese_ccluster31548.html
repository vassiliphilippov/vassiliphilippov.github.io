Japanese clover

noun
tropical Asian plant, naturalized in US
4.9
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈpæniz ˈkloʊvɚ / (AmE), / dʒəˈpæniːz ˈkləʊvə / (BrE)
A tropical Asian annual plant (Lespedeza striata) with clover-like leaves, naturalized in the United States and used as ground cover or forage.

Examples

Japanese clover plant
After being introduced, Japanese clover spread rapidly across the southern United States.
Gardeners often plant Japanese clover to improve soil quality and prevent erosion.
The field was covered with Japanese clover, providing excellent forage for cattle.
