chassé

verb
gliding dance step in ballet
Pronunciation: / ʃæˈseɪ / (AmE), / ʃaˈseɪ / (BrE)
To perform a quick gliding dance step in which one foot appears to chase the other, commonly used in ballet.

Examples

To chassé quickly
After a quick spin, she chasséed gracefully to the left.
He learned how to chassé during his first ballet class.
The ballerinas chassé across the stage in perfect unison.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glide - move smoothly with little apparent effort, as in dance
