fess up

verb
admit a wrongdoing or error
8.0
Pronunciation: / fɛs ʌp / (AmE), / fɛs ʌp / (BrE)
to admit or confess to something, especially after being asked or when you did something wrong

Examples

To fess up the truth
After hours of questioning, he finally fessed up to breaking the window.
It's better to fess up now than to be caught in a lie later.
She fessed up about her mistake during the meeting.
After hours of questioning, he fessed up to taking the money.
If you broke the vase, you should just fess up and take responsibility.
She finally decided to fess up about what happened at the meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: confess - to admit that you have done something wrong or illegal
  • antonym: deny - to state that something is not true, or refuse to admit
  • synonym: own up - to admit or confess to something one has done
