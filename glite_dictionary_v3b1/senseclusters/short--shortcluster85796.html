short

verb
create a short circuit (verb)
Pronunciation: / ʃɔrt / (AmE), / ʃɔːt / (BrE)
to cause (an electrical circuit or device) to have a short circuit, often resulting in damage or malfunction.

Examples

To short a circuit
Be careful not to let water short the circuit board.
If you connect the wires incorrectly, you could short the entire system.
The faulty appliance shorted and caused the lights to go out.

Related Terms

  • synonym: short-circuit - to make a short circuit in something
