purdah

noun
curtain or practice secluding women (South Asia)
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…úrd…ô / (AmE), / Ààp…úÀêd…ô / (BrE)
a curtain or screen used in some South Asian cultures, especially in India, to separate women from men or from strangers, or the practice of secluding women from public observation

Examples

Purdah curtain
Behind the purdah, the women could observe the ceremonies without being seen.
The practice of purdah is less common in urban areas now.
The royal family maintained purdah in their household for generations.

Synonyms

  • purdah tradition
    9.9 0.00/m
    purdah tradition describes the social customs and practices that keep women separate from public life, while purdah is the physical curtain or broader practice.
