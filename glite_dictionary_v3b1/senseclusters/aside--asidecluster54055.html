aside

adverb
to or toward one side
Pronunciation: / əˈsaɪd / (AmE), / əˈsaɪd / (BrE)
to one side; out of the way, either physically or to ignore or not consider something

Examples

Step aside
He turned his head aside and pretended not to notice.
She stepped aside to let the children pass.
The chair was pushed aside to make space for more guests.
He brushed her comments aside and continued with his presentation.
She set her disappointment aside and congratulated the winner.
Try to put your worries aside for now and focus on the task at hand.
He put his feelings aside to focus on the task at hand.
Please step aside so everyone can see the board.
She swept the papers aside and began to write a letter.

  • synonym: away - to or at a different place or direction
