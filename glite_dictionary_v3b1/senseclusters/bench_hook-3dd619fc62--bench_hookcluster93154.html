bench hook

noun
workbench stop for holding wood steady
Pronunciation: / bɛntʃ hʊk / (AmE), / bɛntʃ hʊk / (BrE)
a device fixed to a workbench that acts as a stop to hold wood steady while it is being cut or shaped

Examples

A bench hook tool
She made her own bench hook as a beginner woodworking project.
The carpenter placed the board against the bench hook to keep it steady while sawing.
Using a bench hook ensures greater accuracy and safety when working with chisels.
