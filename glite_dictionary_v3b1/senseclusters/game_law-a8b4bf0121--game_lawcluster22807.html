game law

noun
law governing video games and digital platforms
6.1
Pronunciation: / ɡeɪm lɔ / (AmE), / ɡeɪm lɔː / (BrE)
The body of law and regulation that applies to video games and related digital platforms, including licensing, intellectual property, consumer protection, monetisation (e.g., loot boxes), and privacy.

Examples

Game law for video games
The startup hired a game law attorney to navigate loot-box regulations and IP issues.
Recent changes in game law require clearer age ratings and consumer refund protections for digital purchases.
European regulators are debating whether existing game laws adequately address in-game microtransactions and privacy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: video game law - legal rules and practice specifically concerning video games and interactive digital entertainment
