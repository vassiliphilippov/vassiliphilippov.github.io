pharyngobranchial

noun
gill arch bone
9.9
Pronunciation: / fəˌrɪŋɡoʊˈbræŋkiəl / (AmE), / fəˌrɪŋɡəʊˈbraŋkɪəl / (BrE)
a bone in the pharyngeal region that supports the gill arches in fishes and other gill-bearing vertebrates

Examples

A pharyngobranchial bone
The paleontologist identified a small pharyngobranchial among the fossilized gill-arch elements.
In bony fishes, the pharyngobranchial helps support the structure of the gills.
Researchers studied the development of the pharyngobranchial in different species of fish.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ceratobranchial - a lower bone of the gill arch in fishes
  • synonym: epibranchial - a bone above the gill arch in fishes
