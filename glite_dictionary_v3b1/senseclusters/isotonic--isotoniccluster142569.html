isotonic

adjective
of muscle contraction: constant tension, changing length
7.1 8.1
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪsəˈtɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəˈtɒnɪk / (BrE)
(of muscular contraction) involving constant tension while the length of the muscle changes.

Examples

Isotonic contraction
During an isotonic contraction, the muscle shortens as it lifts a constant load.
Physical therapists often recommend isotonic exercises to improve joint mobility and muscle strength.
The biceps undergoes an isotonic contraction when you lift a dumbbell.

Related Terms

  • antonym: isometric - (of muscular contraction) involving equal muscle length with varying tension
