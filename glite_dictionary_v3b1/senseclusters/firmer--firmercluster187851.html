firmer

adjective
more solid or secure
1.4
Pronunciation: / ˈfɜrmər / (AmE), / ˈfɜːmə / (BrE)
more solid, secure, or certain than something else; comparative form of firm.

Examples

A firmer grip
After the repairs, the table felt firmer and no longer wobbled when someone leaned on it.
She prefers a firmer mattress for better back support.
His voice grew firmer as he explained his decision to the team.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: firm - solid, stable, or securely fixed in place
  • degree higher: firmest - most solid, secure, or certain; superlative of firm
  • antonym: softer - less solid or less hard; comparative of soft
