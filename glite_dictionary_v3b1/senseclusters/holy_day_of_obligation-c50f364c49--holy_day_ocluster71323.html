holy day of obligation

noun
religious day requiring Mass or Communion
3.2 7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈhoʊ.liː deɪ əv ɑːb.lɪ.ˈgeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ˈhoʊ.liː deɪ əv ɑːb.lə.ˈgeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈhəʊ.liː deɪ əv ɒb.lɪ.ˈgeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ˈhəʊ.liː deɪ ə ɒb.lɪ.ˈgeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
A religious day on which Roman Catholics are required to attend Mass and refrain from servile work; for Episcopalians, a day when Communion must be taken.

Examples

A holy day of obligation Mass
Catholics are required to attend Mass on each holy day of obligation throughout the year.
Christmas is considered a holy day of obligation in many countries.
On a holy day of obligation, many churches see higher attendance than usual.
