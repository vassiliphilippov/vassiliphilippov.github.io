overcall

verb
to bid after another
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈkɔl / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈkɔːl / (BrE)
to make a call or bid in a card game after another player has already called or bid, often with a higher value

Examples

To overcall in bridge
In bridge, she chose to overcall with a strong hand after her opponent's opening bid.
Players must decide carefully when to overcall in competitive card games.
He regretted his decision to overcall after realizing his cards were not strong enough.

Related Terms

  • synonym: outbid - to offer a higher bid than another player
  • antonym: pass - to decline to make a bid or call
