Galois theory

noun
branch of algebra using group theory for equations
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɡælˈwɑː θiəri / (AmE), / ɡalˈwɑː θɪəri / (BrE)
A branch of abstract algebra that uses group theory to study the solutions of polynomial equations.

Examples

Galois theory in algebra
Many results in modern mathematics rely on Galois theory to determine whether a polynomial equation is solvable by radicals.
The development of Galois theory marked a turning point in the understanding of algebraic equations.
While studying field extensions, she became fascinated by the elegant structures revealed through Galois theory.
