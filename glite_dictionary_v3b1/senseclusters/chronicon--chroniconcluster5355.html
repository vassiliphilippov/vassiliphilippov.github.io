chronicon

noun
medieval historical record
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɑːnɪˌkɑn / (AmE), / ˈkrɒnɪkɒn / (BrE)
a historical record or chronicle, especially one written in Latin during the medieval period

Examples

Medieval chronicon
The historian studied the chronicon to learn about the political changes of the era.
Many details about medieval life are preserved in the chronicon written by monks.
The chronicon offers a year-by-year account of the kingdom's major events.

Related Terms

  • synonym: annals - a record of events year by year
  • synonym: chronicle - a factual written account of important or historical events
