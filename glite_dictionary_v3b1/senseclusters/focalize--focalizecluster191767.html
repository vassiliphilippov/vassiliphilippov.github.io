focalize

verb
concentrate on a particular spot
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊkəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈfəʊkəlaɪz / (BrE)
to concentrate or direct attention, activity, or an effect on a particular place or spot, especially within a scientific or medical context

Examples

To focalize energy
Researchers observed that the magnetic field began to focalize at the center of the chamber.
The artist decided to focalize the lighting on the main subject of the painting.
The disease tends to focalize in the lungs before spreading.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diffuse - to spread over a wide area or among a large number of people
  • synonym: focus - to concentrate attention or effort on something
