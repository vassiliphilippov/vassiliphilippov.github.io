retreat from

verb
withdraw from a position or place
Pronunciation: / rɪˈtrit frəm / (AmE), / rɪˈtriːt frəm / (BrE)
to withdraw from a position, location, or situation, often to avoid danger or difficulty

Examples

To retreat from danger
After the failed negotiations, the company had to retreat from its proposed merger.
He decided to retreat from public life after years in politics.
The soldiers were ordered to retreat from the city when the enemy advanced.

Related Terms

  • synonym: withdraw from - to leave or move back from a place or position
