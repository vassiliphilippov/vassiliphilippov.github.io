fob

verb
deceive or trick someone
9.4 0.05/m
Pronunciation: / fÉ‘b / (AmE), / fÉ’b / (BrE)
to deceive or trick (someone), especially by making them accept something false or less valuable

Examples

To fob off lies
She suspected he was trying to fob her with a false excuse.
The merchant tried to fob me off with a cheap imitation instead of the genuine article.
They attempted to fob the customer by promising a refund they never intended to give.

Synonyms

  • fob off
    8.8 0.05/m
    fob off is the phrasal form used to pass off bad goods or lies, while fob is the base verb meaning to deceive.
  • palm off
    7.9 0.02/m
    palm off means tricking someone into accepting a fake or poor item, while fob usually refers to passing off such goods.
  • foist
    8.4 1.57/m
    foist means forcing or tricking someone to accept something unwanted, while fob focuses on passing off false or low-value items.
  • con
    7.3 1.90/m
    con means tricking someone to give money or valuables, while fob more often means getting someone to accept a fake or poor item.
  • trick
    3.5 9.77/m
    trick means deceiving someone by cunning in many situations, while fob specifically involves passing off fake or low-value items.
  • pull a fast one on
    8.1 0.13/m
    pull a fast one on means secretly deceiving someone to gain an advantage, while fob usually means selling or passing off inferior goods.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dupe - to deceive someone, usually by trickery
  • synonym: trick - to deceive or cheat someone
