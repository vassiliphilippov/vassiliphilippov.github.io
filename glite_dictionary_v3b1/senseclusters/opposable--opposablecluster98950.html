opposable

adjective
able to be placed opposite something (esp. thumb)
4.7
Pronunciation: / əˈpoʊzəbəl / (AmE), / əˈpəʊzəbəl / (BrE)
(of a digit, especially a thumb) capable of being placed opposite to another digit on the same hand, allowing for grasping or manipulation.

Examples

Opposable thumb
Evolution of opposable digits was crucial for the development of tool use in early humans.
Humans are unique among primates because they have an opposable thumb that allows for precise gripping.
The panda uses its opposable thumb to grasp bamboo shoots efficiently.

Related Terms

  • synonym: opposite - situated or placed across from something else
