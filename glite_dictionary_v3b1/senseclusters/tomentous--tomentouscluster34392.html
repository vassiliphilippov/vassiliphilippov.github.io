tomentous

adjective
covered with matted woolly hairs (botany)
7.9
Pronunciation: / toʊˈmɛntəs / (AmE), / təʊˈmɛntəs / (BrE)
(of a surface, especially of a leaf or stem) densely covered with short, matted, woolly hairs.

Examples

Tomentous leaf surface
Several alpine plants possess tomentous stems to help protect them from cold winds.
The botanist noted the tomentous texture on the plant sample's surface under her microscope.
The underside of the leaf is tomentous, giving it a soft, silvery appearance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tomentose - covered with short, matted, woolly hairs; variant often preferred in botany
