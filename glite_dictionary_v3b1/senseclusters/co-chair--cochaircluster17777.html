co-chair

noun
joint chairperson
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊ tʃɛr / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ tʃeə / (BrE)
a person who shares the position of chairperson with one or more others in a group or committee

Examples

Committee co-chair
She was elected co-chair of the committee after years of dedicated service.
The conference had two co-chairs to ensure fair representation of both organizations.
As co-chair, he shared responsibilities and decision-making with his colleague.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chairperson - the leader or head of a meeting, committee, or organization
  • antonym: member - a person who belongs to a group or organization
