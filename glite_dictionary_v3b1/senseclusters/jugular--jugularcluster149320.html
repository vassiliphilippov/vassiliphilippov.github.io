jugular

noun
major neck vein carrying blood from the head
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʌɡjələr / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌɡjʊlə / (BrE)
Either of two large veins in the neck that carry blood from the head back to the heart.

Examples

Jugular vein injury
A cut to the jugular can be life-threatening due to massive blood loss.
The paramedic checked the patient's jugular for a pulse.
The surgeon carefully avoided damaging the jugular during the operation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: vein - a blood vessel carrying blood toward the heart
