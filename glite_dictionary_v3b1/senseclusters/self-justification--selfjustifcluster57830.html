self-justification

noun
the act of defending one's own behavior
Pronunciation: / sɛlf dʒʌstəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / sɛlf ˌdʒʌstɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of excusing or defending one's own actions, especially when they are wrong or considered unacceptable

Examples

Constant self-justification
Constant self-justification can damage trust in personal relationships.
His self-justification for missing the meeting did not convince his manager.
She resorted to self-justification instead of admitting her mistake.

Related Terms

  • antonym: admission - acknowledgment of a fault or wrongdoing (contrasts with defending oneself)
  • synonym: alibi - an explanation offered to avoid blame or justify failure
  • synonym: excuse - a reason given to justify a fault or offense
