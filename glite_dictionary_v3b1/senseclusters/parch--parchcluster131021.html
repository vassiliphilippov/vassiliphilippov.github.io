parch

verb
make very dry by heat
3.5
Pronunciation: / pɑrtʃ / (AmE), / pɑːtʃ / (BrE)
to make something very dry, especially by exposure to heat or sunlight

Examples

To parch the land
After hours of hiking, the wind and heat parched my skin.
The careless campfire parched the grass in a wide circle.
The relentless sun parched the fields, leaving them cracked and barren.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dry - to remove moisture from; to become dry
