hawk

verb
sell goods by calling out in public places
6.8 1.78/m
Pronunciation: / h…ëk / (AmE), / h…îÀêk / (BrE)
to sell or offer goods for sale by calling out and moving from place to place, especially in public areas

Examples

To hawk goods
He used to hawk homemade jewelry door to door when he was a teenager.
Street vendors hawk their wares in the busy market every morning.
Vendors hawking snacks shouted their offers as the train pulled into the station.

Synonyms

  • vend
    5.6 0.19/m
    vend is a neutral term for selling in public or at stalls, while hawk means loudly calling out while moving along streets.
  • peddle
    3.9 2.60/m
    peddle often means selling goods while traveling from house to house or along streets, while hawk means calling out loudly while moving.

Related Terms

  • synonym: peddle - to sell goods by going from place to place
