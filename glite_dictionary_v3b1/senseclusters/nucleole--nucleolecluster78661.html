nucleole

noun
small body in a cell nucleus containing RNA
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈnuːkliˌoʊl / (AmE), / ˈnjuːklɪəʊl / (BrE)
a small, rounded structure within the cell nucleus that contains RNA and is involved in protein synthesis

Examples

The nucleole in nucleus
Biology students learn that the nucleole is essential for protein production in eukaryotic cells.
The nucleole plays a crucial role in the synthesis of ribosomal RNA.
Under the microscope, the scientist identified a nucleole inside the nucleus of the cell.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nucleolus - the larger structure in a cell nucleus where ribosomes are produced and assembled
