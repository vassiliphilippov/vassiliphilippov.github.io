testudo

noun
Roman soldiers' protective shield formation
Pronunciation: / tɛˈstuːdoʊ / (AmE), / tɛˈstjuːdəʊ / (BrE)
A movable protective shelter formed by Roman soldiers with their shields held overhead, used to protect against missiles when approaching enemy fortifications.

Examples

Roman testudo formation
Historians described the testudo as an effective method of protecting troops under enemy fire.
Reenactments of ancient battles sometimes include the famous testudo formation for demonstration.
The Roman legionaries advanced in a testudo to shield themselves from arrows during the siege.
