Ephesians

noun
book of the New Testament; epistle to the Ephesians
Pronunciation: / ɪˈfiʒənz / (AmE), / ɪˈfiːʒənz / (BrE)
a book of the New Testament comprising an epistle written by Saint Paul to the Christians of Ephesus

Examples

The book of Ephesians
Many Christians study Ephesians to gain a deeper understanding of Paul's teachings on unity and love.
She cited Ephesians to support her argument about the importance of kindness.
The sermon focused on a passage from Ephesians about spiritual strength and faith.
