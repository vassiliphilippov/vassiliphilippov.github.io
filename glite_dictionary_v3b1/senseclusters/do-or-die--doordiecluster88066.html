do-or-die

adjective
desperately determined; willing to risk everything
1.1 5.8
Pronunciation: /duː.ɔːr.ˈdaɪ/ /; / /duː.ər.ˈdaɪ/ /; / /də.ər.ˈdaɪ/ (AmE), /duː.ɔː.ˈdaɪ/ /; / /dəʊ.ɔː.ˈdaɪ/ /; / /duː.ə.ˈdaɪ/ (BrE)
showing a willingness to make any sacrifice or take any risk in order to succeed, especially when the situation is urgent or desperate

Examples

A do-or-die effort
His do-or-die spirit inspired the rest of the group to persevere.
She made a do-or-die attempt to save the company from bankruptcy.
The team adopted a do-or-die attitude before the final match of the season.

Related Terms

  • synonym: desperate - feeling or showing a hopeless sense of urgency or need
  • synonym: determined - having made a firm decision and being resolved not to change it
