nicotinic

adjective
related to nicotine
Pronunciation: / ˌnɪkəˈtɪnɪk / (AmE), / ˌnɪkəˈtɪnɪk / (BrE)
relating to or resembling the effects of nicotine, especially in reference to certain receptors or drugs

Examples

Nicotinic receptors
The scientist studied the nicotinic receptors involved in muscle movement.
Some medications target nicotinic pathways to treat neurological disorders.
Exposure to certain chemicals can affect nicotinic acetylcholine receptors in the brain.

Related Terms

  • antonym: muscarinic - relating to or resembling the effects of muscarine, especially in reference to certain receptors
