commonplace

noun
a trite or obvious remark
6.2 3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmənˌpleɪs / (AmE), / ˈkɒmənpleɪs /; / ˈkɒmənˌpleɪs / (BrE)
something that is ordinary or unoriginal, such as a remark, idea, or thing

Examples

A tired commonplace
His speech was full of commonplace about hard work and determination.
It's disappointing when a novel only offers commonplace instead of original insights.
She tried to avoid making any commonplace during the discussion.
Electric cars are becoming a commonplace on city streets around the world.
It was once rare to travel abroad, but now it is a commonplace for many families.
The use of video calls has turned into a commonplace in modern business.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cliché - a phrase or opinion that is overused and betrays a lack of original thought
  • antonym: novelty - something new, original, or unusual
  • synonym: platitude - a remark or statement, especially one with a moral content, that has been used too often to be interesting or thoughtful
  • synonym: routine - a regular or ordinary event or activity
  • antonym: witticism - a clever or witty remark (opposite of dull or trite)
