contraindicative

adjective
not recommended
9.5
Pronunciation: /kɑːn.trə.ɪn.ˈdɪk.tɪv/ /; / /kən.trə.ɪn.ˈdɪk.tɪv/ (AmE), /kɒn.trə.ɪn.ˈdɪk.tɪv/ /; / /kən.trə.ɪn.ˈdɪk.tɪv/ (BrE)
indicating that something (such as a treatment or action) should not be used or done because of certain evidence or circumstances

Examples

Contraindicative for treatment
The presence of severe liver disease is contraindicative for this medication.
A known allergy to penicillin is contraindicative when considering this antibiotic.
Certain heart conditions are contraindicative for intense physical exercise.
The test results were contraindicative of continuing the current treatment plan.
His symptoms are contraindicative of a simple cold and suggest a more serious illness.
The weather forecast is contraindicative of holding the event outdoors this weekend.

Related Terms

  • antonym: advisable - recommended or sensible to do
  • synonym: contraindicated - not advised or recommended due to possible harm
  • antonym: recommended - advised as suitable or beneficial
