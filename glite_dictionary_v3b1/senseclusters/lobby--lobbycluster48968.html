lobby

verb
to influence officials
4.0 6.09/m
Pronunciation: / Ààl…ëÀêbi / (AmE), / Ààl…íbi / (BrE)
to try to influence politicians or officials to support or oppose a particular cause or policy

Examples

To lobby the government
Environmental groups often lobby lawmakers to pass stricter pollution controls.
The company hired experts to lobby for changes in trade regulations.
Citizens can lobby their representatives by writing letters or attending public meetings.
Environmental groups lobby the government to pass stricter pollution laws.
The company hired professionals to lobby for changes in tax regulations.
Citizens often lobby their representatives about important community issues.

Synonyms

  • advocate for
    3.9 4.57/m
    advocate for means publicly arguing for an idea to gain support, while lobby means influencing politicians directly, often behind the scenes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: advocate - to publicly support or recommend a particular cause or policy
  • synonym: campaign - to work in an organized way toward a particular goal, especially in politics
  • antonym: oppose - to actively resist or refuse to support something
