combustible material

noun
substance that can catch fire and burn
Pronunciation: / kəmˈbʌstəbəl məˈtɪriəl / (AmE), / kəmˈbʌstɪbəl məˈtɪəriəl / (BrE)
a substance that is capable of catching fire and burning to provide heat or power

Examples

Combustible material burns
Keep all combustible material away from open flames in the laboratory.
The storage area contained large amounts of combustible material, increasing the fire hazard.
Wood is considered a common example of a combustible material.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flammable material - substance that easily catches fire
  • antonym: noncombustible material - substance that does not burn
