consolidation

noun
process of forming a solid mass
Pronunciation: / kənˌsɑːlɪˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˌsɒlɪˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
a solid mass formed by the process of things coming together and compacting (such as in medicine or chemistry)

Examples

Lung consolidation
After surgery, the consolidation in his lungs gradually resolved.
Soil consolidation is an important factor in the stability of foundations.
The consolidation of sediment creates layers of solid rock over time.
A small consolidation had formed in the solution after several hours of standing.
After filtration, a consolidation of mineral deposits was left at the bottom of the flask.
The radiologist observed a consolidation in the patient's lung on the chest X-ray.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agglomerate - a mass formed by the collection of particles or fragments into a whole
  • synonym: clot - a thick, coagulated mass, especially of blood or other liquid
  • synonym: coalescence - the process of coming together to form one mass or whole
