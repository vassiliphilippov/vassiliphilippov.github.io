recalibration

noun
adjustment of strategy
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌkælɪˈbreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌkælɪˈbreɪʃən / (BrE)
a change or adjustment in plans, strategy, or approach to improve effectiveness or respond to new circumstances

Examples

Recalibration of strategy
The company announced a recalibration of its marketing strategy to reach younger customers.
After receiving feedback, the team began a recalibration of their project goals.
A sudden drop in sales led to a recalibration of the business plan.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adjustment - a small change to improve something
  • synonym: revision - the act of changing or updating something
  • antonym: stagnation - a lack of change or progress
