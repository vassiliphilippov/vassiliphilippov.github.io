outcast

noun
person rejected by society
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌkɑːst / (BrE)
a person who has been rejected by society or by their family and friends

Examples

A social outcast
After the scandal, he lived as an outcast in his own community.
Historically, lepers were treated as outcasts and forced to live outside the city walls.
She felt like an outcast after moving to a new city where she knew no one.
