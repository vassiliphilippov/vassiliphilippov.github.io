buckle

noun
a distorted shape caused by twisting or folding
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌkəl / (AmE), / ˈbʌkəl / (BrE)
A shape that has become distorted by twisting or folding, typically referring to a structural deformation.

Examples

A buckle in metal
After the car accident, there was a visible buckle in the door frame.
The metal beam developed a small buckle under the heavy load.
The ship’s hull showed a buckle where it struck the rock.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bend - a curve or angle formed by something bending
  • synonym: warp - a twist or distortion in something that was once flat or straight
