prothrombin

noun
blood protein precursor to thrombin
9.0 6.6
Pronunciation: /proʊ.ˈθrɑːm.bɪn/ /; / /proʊ.ˈθrɔːm.bɪn/ (AmE), /prəʊ.ˈθrɒm.bɪn/ /; / /prəʊ.ˈθrɒm.bən/ (BrE)
a protein in blood plasma that is an inactive precursor of thrombin, essential for blood clotting

Examples

Prothrombin level test

Vitamin K is essential for the synthesis of prothrombin in the liver.
