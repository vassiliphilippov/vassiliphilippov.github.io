stand still

verb
remain stationary; not move
Pronunciation: / stænd stɪl / (AmE), / stænd stɪl / (BrE)
to stand in one place without moving; to remain upright and still

Examples

To stand still quietly
Please stand still while I take your picture.
She tried to stand still, but she was too excited to keep from fidgeting.
The children were asked to stand still during the national anthem.
Please stand still so that I can adjust your tie properly.
She tried to stand still despite the biting wind.
The children were told to stand still during the national anthem.

Related Terms

  • synonym: freeze - to become motionless suddenly or completely
  • antonym: move - to change position or go from place to place
