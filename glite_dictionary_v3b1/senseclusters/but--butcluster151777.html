but

conjunction
contrasting conjunction
1.0 7192/m
Pronunciation: / bÊŒt / (AmE), / bÊŒt / (BrE)
used to introduce a statement that contrasts with or seems to contradict something that has been said previously

Examples

But it rained
I wanted to go for a walk, but it started to rain.
She studied hard for the exam, but she did not pass.
He is very talented, but he lacks confidence in his abilities.

Synonyms

  • yet
    1.0 43/m
    yet joins two clauses to show contrast in the same short way, while but is the common everyday connector.
  • however
    2.6 512/m
    however often starts a sentence or follows a semicolon to show contrast, whereas but links clauses directly in speech.
  • whereas
    3.5 48/m
    whereas sets two facts in contrast, often in formal writing, while but gives a more general, spoken contrast between ideas.
  • even so
    3.5 12/m
    even so introduces a result that surprises after the first idea, while but simply adds a direct contrast.
  • on the other hand
    2.8 26/m
    on the other hand presents an alternative view or choice in a discussion, while but gives a quick, direct contrast.

Related Terms

  • antonym: and - used to connect words or phrases of the same type
  • synonym: however - used to introduce a statement that contrasts with a previous one
  • synonym: yet - used to introduce a contrasting idea
