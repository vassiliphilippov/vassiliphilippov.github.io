but

conjunction
contrasting conjunction
1.0
Pronunciation: / bʌt / (AmE), / bʌt / (BrE)
used to introduce a statement that contrasts with or seems to contradict something that has been said previously

Examples

But it rained
I wanted to go for a walk, but it started to rain.
She studied hard for the exam, but she did not pass.
He is very talented, but he lacks confidence in his abilities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: and - used to connect words or phrases of the same type
  • synonym: however - used to introduce a statement that contrasts with a previous one
  • synonym: yet - used to introduce a contrasting idea
