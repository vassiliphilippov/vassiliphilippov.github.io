track

verb
carry and deposit (on the feet)
Pronunciation: / træk / (AmE), / træk / (BrE)
to make marks or paths on a surface by moving across it, or to carry and deposit substances (like mud) by walking

Examples

To track mud
After hiking in the rain, he accidentally tracked mud through the entire hallway.
Please wipe your shoes so you don't track dirt onto the carpet.
The children tracked sand into the living room after playing on the beach.
Hikers often track along the same path, gradually creating a visible trail.
Please don't track sand into the house after your day at the beach.
The muddy boots tracked dirt all over the floor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: trail - to leave marks or substances behind as one moves
  • synonym: trample - to tread heavily so as to crush or leave marks
