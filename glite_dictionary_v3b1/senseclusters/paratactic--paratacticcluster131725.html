paratactic

adjective
juxtaposed without links
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəˈtæktɪk / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈtæktɪk / (BrE)
arranged side by side without clear connections or transitions, especially in writing (such as sentences placed together without conjunctions)

Examples

Paratactic style writing
The author used a paratactic style, presenting events without clear transitions between them.
In her essay, the paratactic structure made each idea stand out on its own.
The documentary's paratactic editing left viewers to draw their own connections between scenes.
The author chose a paratactic style, presenting ideas in short, simple sentences.
In ancient literature, paratactic constructions are often used instead of complex sentences.
Her essay was criticized for being too paratactic, lacking clear connections between thoughts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hypotactic - arranged with clear connections or subordination between parts
  • synonym: juxtaposed - placed side by side for contrast or comparison
