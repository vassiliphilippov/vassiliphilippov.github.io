suffer in silence

verb
endure pain or distress quietly
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌfɚ ɪn ˈsaɪləns / (AmE), / ˈsʌfə ɪn ˈsaɪləns / (BrE)
to endure pain, hardship, or distress without expressing complaints or asking for help

Examples

To suffer in silence at work
Many employees suffer in silence rather than confronting workplace issues directly.
No one should have to suffer in silence when help and support are available.
She decided to suffer in silence, keeping her problems to herself.
