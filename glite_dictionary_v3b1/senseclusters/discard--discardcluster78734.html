discard

noun
the act of discarding a card in a card game
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪsˌkɑrd / (AmE), / dɪsˈkɑːd / (BrE)
an act of playing or removing a card from one's hand, especially in a card game when unable or unwilling to follow the suit led

Examples

A discard in poker
A strategic discard can sometimes give away information to opponents.
He made a clever discard that changed the course of the game.
Players debated whether his last discard was a mistake.

Related Terms

  • antonym: draw - an act of taking a card from the deck in a card game
