anorchidism

noun
absence of one or both testes
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈnɔrkɪˌdɪzəm / (AmE), / əˈnɔːkɪdɪzəm / (BrE)
The congenital or acquired absence of one or both testes.

Examples

Congenital anorchidism
Congenital anorchidism is extremely rare and often requires hormone replacement therapy.
Medical imaging can help differentiate anorchidism from other conditions affecting testicular development.
The patient was diagnosed with anorchidism after a series of tests confirmed the absence of both testes.
