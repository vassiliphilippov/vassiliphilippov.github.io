acentric chromosome

noun
chromosome without a centromere
9.5
Pronunciation: / eɪˈsɛntrɪk ˈkroʊməˌsoʊm / (AmE), / eɪˈsɛntrɪk ˈkrəʊməsəʊm / (BrE)
a chromosome that lacks a centromere, commonly resulting from chromosomal breakage or abnormal recombination

Examples

An acentric chromosome
During cell division, an acentric chromosome is often lost because it cannot attach to the spindle apparatus.
Genetic mutations can sometimes result in the formation of an acentric chromosome in plant cells.
Researchers identified an acentric chromosome in the sample, which explained the observed genetic instability.

Related Terms

  • antonym: centromere - the region of a chromosome where the spindle fibers attach during cell division
  • synonym: chromosome - a thread-like structure of nucleic acids and proteins carrying genetic information
