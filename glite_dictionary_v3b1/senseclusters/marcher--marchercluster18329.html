marcher

noun
person who marches in a group
3.4 1.13/m
Pronunciation: / Ààm…ërt É…ôr / (AmE), / Ààm…ëÀêt É…ô / (BrE)
A person who marches, especially as part of a procession, demonstration, or parade.

Examples

Parade marcher
Police escorted the group of marchers to ensure their safety.
The parade's lead marcher carried the national flag.
Thousands of marchers filled the streets calling for climate action.

Synonyms

  • demonstrator
    3.4 7.33/m

  • marchman
    6.5 0.00/m
    a marchman takes part in public marches, often as a protester, while a marcher can also be someone in parades or processions.
  • parader
    7.2 0.04/m
    a parader walks with a stately or showy step in parades to attract attention, while a marcher also includes protesters or people in processions.
