chromatic color

noun
color with hue (not grey, black, or white)
Pronunciation: / kroʊˈmætɪk ˈkʌlər / (AmE), / krəʊˈmatɪk ˈkʌlə /; / krəˈmatɪk ˈkʌlə / (BrE)
A color that possesses hue, such as red, green, or blue, as opposed to shades of grey, white, or black.

Examples

Chromatic color hues
Artists often use chromatic color to create vibrant and expressive paintings.
Green is an example of a chromatic color, while gray is not.
Lighting can affect how we perceive a chromatic color on a wall.
Artists often use a wide range of chromatic colour to create vibrant, realistic paintings.
Red, blue, and yellow are considered primary chromatic colours in traditional color theory.
Unlike black and white, every chromatic colour can be described in terms of its hue, saturation, and brightness.
