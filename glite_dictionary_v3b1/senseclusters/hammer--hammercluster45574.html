hammer

verb
shape by striking with a hammer
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˈhæmɚ / (AmE), / ˈhamə /; / ˈhæmə / (BrE)
to hit or shape something by striking it repeatedly, especially with a hammer

Examples

To hammer metal
She skillfully hammered the gold sheet into an elegant bracelet.
The artisan hammered the hot metal into a decorative plate.
They watched in awe as he hammered the piece of silver into a beautiful bowl.
She hammered her point home during the heated debate.
The blacksmith began to hammer the glowing iron on the anvil.
The rain hammered against the windows all night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: forge - to shape metal by heating and hammering it
  • degree lower: mold - to shape a substance by using a mold or by hand
  • synonym: pound - to strike repeatedly with heavy blows
  • degree lower: tap - to strike lightly, often repeatedly
