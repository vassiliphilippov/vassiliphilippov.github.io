tax lien

noun
a government claim on property for unpaid taxes
9.9 7.6
Pronunciation: / tæks lin / (AmE), / tæks liːn / (BrE)
a legal claim by a government against a property for unpaid taxes, giving the authority the right to seize the property if taxes remain unpaid

Examples

A tax lien claim
He sold the property only after clearing the tax lien attached to it.
If you don't pay the taxes you owe, a tax lien can be filed against your assets.
The government placed a tax lien on his house due to unpaid property taxes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lien - a legal right to keep possession of property until a debt is paid
