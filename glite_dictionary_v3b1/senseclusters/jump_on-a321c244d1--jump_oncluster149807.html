jump on

verb
get up on the back of
1.8 0.86/m
Pronunciation: / dÊ’ÊŒmp É‘n / (AmE), / dÊ’ÊŒmp É’n / (BrE)
to get up onto the back of something, especially quickly or suddenly

Examples

To jump on a horse
He quickly jumped on his horse and rode away.
She laughed as she jumped on the back of her friend's bike.
The children tried to jump on the donkey for a ride.

Synonyms

  • hop on
    2.7 1.61/m
    hop on means getting onto something quickly and lightly, while jump on stresses a sudden, stronger upward movement onto it.
  • hop up on
    5.2 0.16/m
    hop up on is a quick upward leap onto a surface like a table, while jump on means getting up suddenly onto something's back.
  • get on
    1.0 1.11/m
    get on means climbing onto a horse, bike, or bus in a neutral way, while jump on implies a quick, sudden mount.
  • mount
    3.0 5.47/m
    mount means getting up to ride a horse or bicycle formally, while jump on describes doing this quickly or suddenly.
  • mount up
    4.0 0.27/m
    mount up is used for getting up onto a horse or animal, often in commands, while jump on suggests a quick, sudden action.
  • climb on
    2.0 1.48/m
    climb on involves using hands and effort to get up onto something, while jump on implies a quick upward leap onto its back.
