brachioradialis

noun
forearm muscle
Pronunciation: / ˌbreɪkiˌoʊˌreɪdiˈælɪs / (AmE), / ˌbrækiəʊˌreɪdiˈælɪs / (BrE)
a muscle of the forearm that helps bend the elbow

Examples

The brachioradialis muscle
The brachioradialis is often used when lifting objects with your arm.
In anatomy class, we learned how the brachioradialis assists in bending the elbow.
After injuring his brachioradialis, he found it difficult to carry heavy bags.
