hacek

noun
diacritical mark (inverted circumflex)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑː.tʃɛk / (AmE), / ˈhæt.ʃɛk / (BrE)
a diacritical mark shaped like an inverted circumflex (ˇ), placed above certain letters, as in č, to indicate pronunciation change

Examples

A hacek mark
In Czech, the letter 'c' with a hacek is pronounced like 'ch' in English.
Many Slavic languages use the hacek in their alphabets.
To write the word 'Škoda' correctly, remember to add a hacek above the 'S'.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diacritic - a mark added to a letter to alter its pronunciation or distinguish it
