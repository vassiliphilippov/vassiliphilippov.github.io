light

adjective
not great in degree or amount
1.0
Pronunciation: / laɪt / (AmE), / laɪt / (BrE)
gentle, not strong or intense; small in amount or degree

Examples

Light rain
After the accident, the injuries to the crew were surprisingly light.
The chef recommends a light dressing for this salad to avoid overpowering the flavors.
We only received a light dusting of snow overnight, so driving conditions are fine.
After a night of light sleep, he felt tired and unrested.
Because he is a light sleeper, even the faintest noises will wake him up.
She drifted into a light doze while waiting for the phone call.
A light breeze made the curtains flutter gently.
Her voice was so light that it could barely be heard over the music.
The surgeon used a light touch to avoid damaging the tissue.
