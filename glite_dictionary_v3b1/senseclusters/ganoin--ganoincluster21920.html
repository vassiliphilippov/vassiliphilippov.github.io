ganoin

noun
enamel-like substance on ganoid fish scales
Pronunciation: / ˈɡænoʊɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡanɔɪn / (BrE)
a shiny, enamel-like substance secreted by certain fishes, especially ganoid fishes, forming the outer layer of their scales

Examples

A layer of ganoin
A layer of ganoin protects certain prehistoric fishes from predators and environmental damage.
Paleontologists study ganoin to understand the evolution of fish scales.
The scales of gars and sturgeons are coated with ganoin, giving them a hard, glossy appearance.
