ad-lib

verb
perform or speak without preparation
9.3
Pronunciation: / ædˈlɪb /; / ˌædˈlɪb / (AmE), / ædˈlɪb /; / ˌædˈlɪb / (BrE)
to speak or perform without preparation; to improvise

Examples

To ad-lib a joke
Comedians often ad-lib jokes based on the audience's reactions.
During the meeting, she managed to ad-lib an impressive presentation.
When the lead actor forgot his lines, he had to ad-lib his way through the scene.
She had to ad-lib her speech when she misplaced her notes.
Sometimes, comedians will ad-lib jokes based on the audience's reactions.
The actor skillfully ad-libbed a line that made the whole audience laugh.
