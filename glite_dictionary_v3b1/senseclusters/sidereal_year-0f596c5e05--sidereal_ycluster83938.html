sidereal year

noun
time for Earth's orbit relative to stars
Pronunciation: / saɪˈdɪriəl jɪr / (AmE), / saɪˈdɪəriəl jɪə / (BrE)
the time taken by the Earth to complete one full orbit around the Sun, measured relative to the fixed stars, approximately 365.256 days

Examples

A sidereal year
A sidereal year is slightly longer than a tropical year due to the precession of the equinoxes.
Astronomers use the sidereal year to measure Earth's true revolution period around the Sun.
Understanding the sidereal year helps explain the differences between astronomical and calendar years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tropical year - the time for the Earth to return to the same position relative to the vernal equinox
