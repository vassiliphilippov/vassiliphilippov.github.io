convection

noun
transfer of heat by fluid movement
4.2
Pronunciation: / kənˈvɛkʃən /; / kənˈvɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / kənˈvɛkʃən /; / kənˈvɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
the transfer of heat (or other properties) in a liquid or gas by the movement of the fluid itself

Examples

Heat convection in air
Most thunderstorms are powered by the convection of moist air from the ground upward.
The meteorologist explained how convection currents drive weather patterns.
Warm air rises in the atmosphere due to convection, leading to cloud formation.
Cooking soup relies on convection to circulate heat and cook the ingredients evenly.
The weather report explained how convection currents influence local wind patterns.
Warm air rises in a room due to convection, spreading heat throughout the space.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conduction - the transfer of heat through direct contact
  • antonym: radiation - the transfer of energy by electromagnetic waves
