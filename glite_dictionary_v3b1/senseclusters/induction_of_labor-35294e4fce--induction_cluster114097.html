induction of labor

noun
artificial initiation of childbirth
8.3
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdʌkʃən əv ˈleɪbɚ / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌkʃən əv ˈleɪbə / (BrE)
the artificial initiation of childbirth by medical means, such as administering oxytocin or rupturing the amniotic sac

Examples

Induction of labor by medication
Methods for induction of labor can include the use of medications or breaking the water.
Some women may require an induction of labor if there are health risks to the mother or baby.
The doctor recommended an induction of labor because the pregnancy had extended beyond forty-one weeks.
