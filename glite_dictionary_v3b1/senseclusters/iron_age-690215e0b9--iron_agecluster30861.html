Iron Age

noun
historical period when iron was in widespread use
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪ.ɚn eɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.ən eɪdʒ / (BrE)
the period in human history following the Bronze Age, marked by the widespread use of iron for tools and weapons

Examples

Early Iron Age tools
Archaeologists discovered iron tools dating back to the early Iron Age.
Many ancient civilizations reached new heights during the Iron Age.
The transition to the Iron Age marked a major advance in human technology.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: Bronze Age - the period before the Iron Age, characterized by the use of bronze tools
  • degree lower: Stone Age - the earliest period of human culture, marked by the use of stone tools
