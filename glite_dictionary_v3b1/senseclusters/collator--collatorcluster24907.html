collator

noun
machine for merging punch cards
Pronunciation: / kəˈleɪtɚ / (AmE), / kəˈleɪtə / (BrE)
a machine that selects, merges, and matches decks of punch cards, used especially in early computing and data processing

Examples

Card collator machine
Early computers used a collator to merge information from different card decks.
Technicians loaded decks of cards into the collator for sorting and matching.
The collator processed thousands of punch cards each hour in the data center.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sorter - a machine that arranges items, such as cards or mail, into a specific order
