white slave

noun
historical term for a trafficked woman
9.9
Pronunciation: / waɪt sleɪv / (AmE), / waɪt sleɪv / (BrE)
(historical, offensive) A woman, typically of European descent, who is forced or trafficked into prostitution.

Examples

The white slave trade
Reformers in the early 1900s campaigned against the so-called white slave trade.
The novel depicted the harrowing experiences of a white slave in the city.
The pamphlet warned parents about the dangers of their daughters becoming white slaves.
