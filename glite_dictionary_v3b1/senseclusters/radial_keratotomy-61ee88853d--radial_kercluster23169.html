radial keratotomy

noun
surgical procedure to correct myopia
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪdiəl ˌkɛrəˈtɑtəmi / (AmE), / ˈreɪdɪəl ˌkɛrəʊˈtɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which radial incisions are made in the cornea to correct nearsightedness (myopia) by allowing the cornea to change shape

Examples

Radial keratotomy surgery
Radial keratotomy was a popular procedure for correcting myopia before laser surgeries became common.
After undergoing radial keratotomy, his vision improved significantly and he no longer needed glasses.
Some patients experience long-term effects after radial keratotomy, especially as they age.
