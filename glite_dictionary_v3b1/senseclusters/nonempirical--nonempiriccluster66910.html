nonempirical

adjective
not based on observation
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnɛmˈpɪrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnɛmˈpɪrɪkəl / (BrE)
not based on observation, experience, or experiment, but rather on theory or reasoning

Examples

Nonempirical theory
His argument was criticized for being nonempirical and lacking supporting data.
Some philosophical claims are considered nonempirical because they cannot be tested by experiment.
The committee rejected the nonempirical approach in favor of one based on real-world evidence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: empirical - based on observation or experiment
  • synonym: theoretical - based on theory rather than practical observation
