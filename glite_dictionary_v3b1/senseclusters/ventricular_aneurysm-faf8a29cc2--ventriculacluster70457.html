ventricular aneurysm

noun
bulge in heart ventricle wall
8.8 6.5
Pronunciation: /ven.ˈtrɪ.kjə.lər ˈæn.jə.rɪz.əm/ /; / /vən.ˈtrɪ.kjə.lər ˈæn.jə.rɪz.əm/ (AmE), /ven.ˈtrɪ.kjə.lə ˈæn.jʊ.rɪ.zəm/ /; / /vən.ˈtrɪ.kjə.lə ˈæn.jʊ.rɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
a localized bulge or dilation in the wall of the left ventricle of the heart, often occurring after a heart attack (myocardial infarction)

Examples

A ventricular aneurysm after heart attack
A ventricular aneurysm can increase the risk of heart failure and arrhythmias.
After the heart attack, doctors discovered a ventricular aneurysm on the patient's left ventricle.
Surgical intervention may be necessary to repair a large ventricular aneurysm in high-risk cases.
