revenue tariff

noun
tariff aimed at raising revenue
3.0 7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛv.əˌnu ˈtær.ɪf / (AmE), / ˈrɛv.juː ˈtær.ɪf / (BrE)
a tariff imposed mainly to raise government income rather than to protect domestic industries

Examples

A revenue tariff imposed
Historically, many countries relied on the revenue tariff as a major source of government financing.
The government introduced a revenue tariff on imported goods to boost national income.
Unlike a protective tariff, a revenue tariff is primarily designed to generate funds for the state.

Related Terms

  • antonym: protective tariff - a tariff imposed to protect domestic industries from foreign competition
