Arapaho

proper noun
a member of a Native American people
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈræpəˌhoʊ / (AmE), / əˈrapəhəʊ / (BrE)
A member of a Native American people originally from the Plains, particularly eastern Colorado and Wyoming, now living mainly in Oklahoma and Wyoming.

Examples

An Arapaho chief
Many Arapaho now live on reservations in Wyoming and Oklahoma.
She is studying the language and traditions of the Arapaho people.
The Arapaho once roamed across the Great Plains alongside other tribes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Cheyenne - a member of another Plains Indian tribe closely associated with the Arapaho
