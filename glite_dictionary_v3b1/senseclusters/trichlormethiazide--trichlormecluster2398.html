trichlormethiazide

noun
diuretic drug for high blood pressure
Pronunciation: / traɪˌklɔrˌmɛˈθaɪəˌzaɪd / (AmE), / trɪˌklɔːˌmɛˈθaɪəzaɪd / (BrE)
a diuretic drug used to treat hypertension, often known by the trade name Naqua

Examples

Trichlormethiazide medicine
He experienced increased urination after starting trichlormethiazide for his hypertension.
People taking trichlormethiazide should have their electrolyte levels monitored regularly.
The doctor prescribed trichlormethiazide to help reduce her blood pressure.
