autologous

adjective
from the same individual (medical)
Pronunciation: / ɔːˈtɑl.əɡəs / (AmE), / ɔːˈtɒl.əɡəs / (BrE)
(of cells, tissues, or materials) obtained from the same individual, especially for use in medical treatment.

Examples

Autologous skin grafts
Doctors often prefer autologous skin grafts because they are less likely to be rejected by the body.
She donated her own blood for an autologous transfusion before surgery.
The patient received an autologous bone marrow transplant to reduce the risk of rejection.

Related Terms

  • antonym: homologous - (of biological materials) derived from a different individual of the same species
