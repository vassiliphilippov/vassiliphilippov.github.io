crowded

adjective
filled beyond comfortable capacity
1.0 14/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆkraÊŠdÉªd / (AmE), / ËˆkraÊŠdÉªd / (BrE)
containing more people or things than is comfortable or possible; filled to excess capacity.

Examples

A crowded room
His schedule was crowded with meetings and appointments all week.
She prefers to avoid crowded places because she feels anxious in large groups.
The subway was so crowded during rush hour that I could barely move.

Synonyms

  • overcrowded
    3.1 1.20/m
    overcrowded describes a place with too many people that causes discomfort and safety problems, while crowded means simply full.
  • packed
    1.3 1.82/m
    packed means a space is completely full with people or things and leaves no room, while crowded can be less full.
  • jammed
    2.3 0.01/m
    jammed means people or objects are squashed together and movement stops, while crowded means the place is simply very full.
  • congested
    4.4 0.93/m
    congested describes a place or road where movement is slow and blocked, while crowded simply means many people or things.
  • dense
    3.2 1.12/m
    dense means things or people are closely packed in a small area, while crowded focuses on being uncomfortably full.

Related Terms

  • antonym: empty - not containing anyone or anything
  • synonym: full - having no more space for additional people or things
  • synonym: packed - filled tightly; having as many people or things as possible
