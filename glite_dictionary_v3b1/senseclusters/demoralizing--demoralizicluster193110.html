demoralizing

adjective
causing loss of morale or confidence
Pronunciation: / dɪˈmɔrəˌlaɪzɪŋ /; / dɪˈmɔːrəˌlaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈmɒr.ə.laɪ.zɪŋ / (BrE)
Causing someone to lose confidence, hope, morale, or self-reliance.

Examples

A demoralizing defeat
Facing constant rejection can be incredibly demoralizing when looking for a job.
It was demoralizing to see all their hard work go unrecognized.
The team's third straight loss was particularly demoralizing for the players.
It can be demoralising to work hard and receive no recognition.
Losing three games in a row was utterly demoralising for the team.
The constant criticism had a demoralising effect on the staff's performance.
