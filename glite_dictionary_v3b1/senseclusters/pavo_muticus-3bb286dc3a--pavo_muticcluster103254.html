Pavo muticus

noun
a species of Southeast Asian peafowl
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪvoʊ mjuˈtaɪkəs / (AmE), / ˈpɑːvəʊ mjuˈtiːkəs / (BrE)
a species of peafowl native to Southeast Asia, known for its iridescent green plumage and long tail feathers

Examples

Pavo muticus bird
Many wildlife photographers travel to spot the elusive Pavo muticus in the wild forests of Myanmar.
The Pavo muticus is endangered due to habitat loss in Southeast Asia.
Unlike the Indian peafowl, the Pavo muticus has green plumage and a more slender build.
