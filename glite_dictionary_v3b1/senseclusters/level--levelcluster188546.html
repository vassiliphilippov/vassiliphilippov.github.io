level

noun
stage or layer of complexity
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛvəl /; / ˈlɛv.əl / (AmE), / ˈlɛvəl /; / ˈlɛv.əl / (BrE)
a particular stage, position, or amount in a system, scale, or measurement; a degree or rank

Examples

A high level
Critics discussed the film on several different levels during the panel.
Her argument can be understood at the political level as well as the philosophical.
The story operates on more than one level, offering both surface entertainment and deeper themes.
His job requires a high level of attention to detail.
The noise level in the restaurant was higher than usual last night.
The water level in the lake dropped after the long drought.
Her stress level increased during the busy week.
Please check the water level before using the machine.
The course is suitable for students at any level of experience.
