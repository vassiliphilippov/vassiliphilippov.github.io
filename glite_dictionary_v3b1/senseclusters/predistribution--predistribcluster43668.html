predistribution

noun
income policy before taxes
9.8
Pronunciation: /priː.dɪs.trɪ.ˈbjuː.ʃən/ /; / /prə.dɪs.trɪ.ˈbjuː.ʃən/ (AmE), /priː.dɪs.trɪ.ˈbjuː.ʃən/ /; / /prə.dɪs.trɪ.ˈbjuː.ʃən/ (BrE)
a policy approach that aims to influence the distribution of income before government taxes or transfers are applied

Examples

Predistribution policy reform
The government introduced predistribution policies to address income inequality at its source.
Economists discussed whether predistribution could be more effective than traditional welfare programs.
By focusing on predistribution, policymakers hope to reduce the need for later tax-based interventions.
