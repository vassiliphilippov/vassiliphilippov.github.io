yellow spruce

noun
North American spruce tree used for lumber
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˈjɛloʊ ˌsprus / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˌspruːs / (BrE)
A medium-sized spruce tree native to eastern North America, valued as a principal source of lumber and pulpwood in the region.

Examples

A yellow spruce tree
Forests of yellow spruce provide important habitat for wildlife in northern regions.
Many paper products in eastern Canada are made from yellow spruce pulpwood.
The lumber from yellow spruce is prized for its strength and versatility in construction.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Norway spruce - European species of spruce, also used for timber
  • synonym: Picea glauca - scientific name for yellow spruce (white spruce)
