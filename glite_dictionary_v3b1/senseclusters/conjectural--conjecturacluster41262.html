Based on or involving conjecture; formed without firm evidence or with little information.

Examples

A conjectural idea
Her explanation for the missing painting seemed highly conjectural to the art experts.
Much of the early research on the disease was conjectural and lacked empirical support.
The historian admitted that his account of the event was largely conjectural due to scarce evidence.

Synonyms

  • speculative
    6.7 3.33/m
    speculative is based on guesses and incomplete evidence and often suggests risk or testing ideas, while conjectural means formed without firm proof.
  • hypothetical
    6.9 8.97/m
    hypothetical presents an imagined or supposed scenario for argument or testing, while conjectural describes a claim made without solid evidence.
  • suppositional
    9.3 0.00/m
    suppositional rests on assumption or supposition used for reasoning, while conjectural highlights that an idea lacks firm proof or facts.
  • supposititious
    9.9 0.01/m
    supposititious is mainly a rare formal word for something assumed without proof, while conjectural is the more common term for such claims.
