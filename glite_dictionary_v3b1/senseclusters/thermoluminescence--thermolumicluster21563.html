thermoluminescence

noun
scientific dating method
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌθɜrmoʊˌluːməˈnɛsəns / (AmE), / ˌθɜːməʊˌluːmɪˈnɛsəns / (BrE)
the emission of light from a material when it is heated, especially as a scientific method for dating objects

Examples

Thermoluminescence dating method
The pottery fragment was analyzed using thermoluminescence to estimate when it was last fired.
Scientists rely on thermoluminescence to date ancient ceramics and minerals found at archaeological sites.
By applying thermoluminescence, researchers determined the artifact had not been exposed to sunlight for centuries.
Scientists used thermoluminescence to date the ancient pottery shards.
The mineral glowed with thermoluminescence when it was gently heated in the laboratory.
Archaeologists rely on thermoluminescence to determine the age of fired clay objects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dendrochronology - the scientific method of dating tree rings
  • synonym: fluorescence - light emitted by a substance while it is exposed to radiation
  • synonym: phosphorescence - light emitted by a substance without heat, after exposure to radiation
  • synonym: radiocarbon dating - a method for determining the age of organic materials using carbon-14
