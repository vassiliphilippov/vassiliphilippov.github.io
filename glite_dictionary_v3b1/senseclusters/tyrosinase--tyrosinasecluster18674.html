tyrosinase

noun
browning enzyme
9.8
Pronunciation: /taɪ.rə.sɪ.ˈneɪz/ /; / /ˈtaɪ.rə.sɪ.neɪz/ (AmE), /taɪ.rə.sɪ.ˈneɪz/ /; / /ˈtaɪ.rə.sɪ.neɪz/ (BrE)
an enzyme found in plants, fungi, and bacteria that causes browning in fruits and vegetables by oxidizing phenolic compounds when tissues are damaged

Examples

Tyrosinase browns apples
When apples are cut and exposed to air, tyrosinase causes them to turn brown.
Scientists study tyrosinase to understand how fruits and vegetables change color after being damaged.
The activity of tyrosinase can be slowed by adding lemon juice to sliced potatoes.
