lipidosis

noun
disorder with abnormal fat accumulation in the body
7.6
Pronunciation: / lɪˈpoʊdəsɪs / (AmE), / lɪˈpɪdəsɪs / (BrE)
A medical disorder in which abnormal levels of certain fats accumulate in the body's tissues due to problems in lipid metabolism.

Examples

Lipidosis disorder
Research into new treatments for lipidosis has shown promising results in animal models.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with lipidosis after abnormal fat deposits were found in her liver.
Untreated genetic disorders can sometimes lead to severe lipidosis in children.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lipid metabolism disorder - disorder affecting the body's processing of fats
  • synonym: steatosis - accumulation of fat in liver cells
