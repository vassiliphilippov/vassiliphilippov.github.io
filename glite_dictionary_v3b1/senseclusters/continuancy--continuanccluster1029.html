continuancy

noun
prolonged speech sound
Pronunciation: / kənˈtɪnjuənsi / (AmE), / kənˈtɪnjʊənsi / (BrE)
the quality of a speech sound that allows it to be prolonged without interruption, as in fricatives or vowels

Examples

Continuancy in speech sounds
In phonetics, continuancy distinguishes fricatives from stops based on airflow.
The teacher explained how continuancy affects the way certain sounds are produced in English.
Vowels and some consonants share the property of continuancy in speech.

Related Terms

  • synonym: continuant - a speech sound that can be prolonged without interruption
  • antonym: stop - a speech sound produced by blocking airflow completely
