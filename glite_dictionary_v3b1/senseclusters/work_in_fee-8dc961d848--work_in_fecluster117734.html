work in fee

verb
work for payment or compensation
7.3
Pronunciation: / wɝk ɪn fi / (AmE), / wɜːk ɪn fiː / (BrE)
To work for payment or financial compensation, rather than as a volunteer or unpaid.

Examples

To work in fee professionally
Doctors who work in fee typically charge clients directly for their services.
The attorney agreed to work in fee rather than handle the case for free.
Unlike volunteers, consultants usually work in fee for organizations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: work pro bono - do professional work voluntarily and without payment
