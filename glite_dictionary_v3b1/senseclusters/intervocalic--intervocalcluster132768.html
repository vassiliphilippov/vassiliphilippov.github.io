intervocalic

adjective
between vowels
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərvoʊˈkælɪk / (AmE), / ˌɪntəvəʊˈkælɪk / (BrE)
occurring between two vowels, especially within a word or phrase

Examples

Intervocalic position
The 'd' in 'ladder' is intervocalic and often pronounced softly in American English.
In some languages, an intervocalic consonant may be pronounced differently than at the start of a word.
The teacher explained how the 't' in 'water' is intervocalic and can sound like a quick 'd'.

Related Terms
