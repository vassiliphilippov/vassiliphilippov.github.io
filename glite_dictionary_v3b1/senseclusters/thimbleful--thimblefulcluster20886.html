thimbleful

noun
a very small amount (as much as a thimble holds)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈθɪmbəlfʊl / (AmE), / ˈθɪmbəlfʊl / (BrE)
A very small quantity, especially as much as can be held in a thimble.

Examples

A thimbleful of brandy
He poured just a thimbleful of brandy into the cake batter.
She offered me only a thimbleful of sherry, much to my disappointment.
There's hardly a thimbleful of milk left in the jug.

  • synonym: drop - a very small amount of liquid
