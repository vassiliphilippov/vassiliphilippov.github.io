Thomson's gazelle

noun
small East African gazelle species
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑmsənz ɡəˈzɛl / (AmE), / ˈtɒmsənz ɡəˈzɛl / (BrE)
a small species of gazelle native to East Africa, distinguished by its slender build and distinctive black side stripe

Examples

A Thomson's gazelle animal
During the safari, our guide pointed out a lone Thomson's gazelle near the waterhole.
The Thomson's gazelle is renowned for its speed and agility when escaping predators.
We spotted a group of Thomson's gazelles grazing on the Serengeti plains.
