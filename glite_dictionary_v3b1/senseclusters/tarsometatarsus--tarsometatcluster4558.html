tarsometatarsus

noun
bird leg bone
Pronunciation: / ˌtɑːrsoʊˌmɛtəˈtɑːrsəs / (AmE), / ˌtɑːsəʊˌmɛtəˈtɑːsəs / (BrE)
a bone in the lower leg of birds formed by the fusion of the tarsal and metatarsal bones

Examples

The tarsometatarsus bone
The tarsometatarsus is crucial for supporting a bird's weight while it stands on one leg.
Paleontologists identified the species by examining the fossilized tarsometatarsus found at the site.
In birds, the tarsometatarsus connects the lower leg to the toes, aiding in movement and balance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: femur - the thigh bone in vertebrates
  • synonym: tibia - the larger bone in the lower leg of vertebrates
