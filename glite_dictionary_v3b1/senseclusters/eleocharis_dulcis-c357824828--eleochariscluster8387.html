Eleocharis dulcis

noun
species of sedge (Chinese water chestnut)
Pronunciation: / ˌɛlioʊˈkɛrɪs ˈdʌl.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛliəʊˈkærɪs ˈdʌl.sɪs / (BrE)
A species of sedge native to Asia, cultivated for its edible, bulb-shaped tubers, commonly known as Chinese water chestnuts.

Examples

Eleocharis dulcis water chestnuts
Farmers in Southeast Asia cultivate Eleocharis dulcis in shallow ponds for its edible water chestnuts.
Researchers are studying the nutritional benefits of Eleocharis dulcis for use in healthy snacks.
The crisp tubers of Eleocharis dulcis are a popular ingredient in many Asian dishes.
