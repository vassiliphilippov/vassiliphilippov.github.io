deed

verb
to transfer property
Pronunciation: / diːd / (AmE), / diːd / (BrE)
to officially transfer property or rights to someone using a legal document

Examples

To deed a house
After years of renting, they finally deeded the land to their children.
The company deeded the building to a local charity as part of its community outreach.
He deeded his share of the property to his sister in the settlement.
She is deeding the house to her children to ensure they inherit it.
The landowner deeded a portion of his property to the city for a new park.
After the agreement, he will deed the apartment to his sister.
