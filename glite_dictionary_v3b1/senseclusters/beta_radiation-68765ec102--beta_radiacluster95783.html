beta radiation

noun
radiation of beta particles in nuclear decay
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪtə reɪdiˌeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈbiːtə reɪdiˌeɪʃən / (BrE)
high-energy radiation consisting of beta particles emitted during the radioactive decay of certain nuclei

Examples

Beta radiation emission
Nuclear reactors are a common source of beta radiation during operation.
Prolonged exposure to beta radiation can be harmful to living tissue.
Scientists measured the beta radiation emitted by the radioactive sample.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: alpha radiation - radiation of alpha particles during radioactive decay
  • degree higher: gamma radiation - high-energy electromagnetic radiation emitted during radioactive decay
