verifiable

adjective
able to be proven or confirmed
Pronunciation: / ˌvɛrɪˈfaɪəbəl /; / ˌvɛrəˈfaɪəbəl / (AmE), / ˌvɛrɪˈfaɪəbl /; / ˌvɛərɪˈfaɪəbl / (BrE)
able to be proven true or correct by checking facts, evidence, or observation

Examples

Verifiable facts
Scientific claims should be verifiable through observation and experimentation.
The data provided by the researcher was fully verifiable by independent experts.
We need verifiable evidence before making any accusations.
All financial transactions are recorded in a verifiable way for transparency.
The scientist presented only verifiable data to support her claims.
Your story is interesting, but do you have any verifiable evidence?

Related Terms

  • synonym: provable - able to be proved or demonstrated as true
  • antonym: unverifiable - not able to be confirmed or proven true
