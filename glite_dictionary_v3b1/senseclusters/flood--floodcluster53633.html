flood

verb
cover with water or other liquid
1.0 10/m
Pronunciation: / flÊŒd / (AmE), / flÊŒd / (BrE)
to cover or fill an area with water or another liquid, often causing it to overflow or become inundated

Examples

To flood a room
She accidentally left the tap running and flooded the kitchen.
Sudden tears flooded his eyes as he listened to the sad news.
The river burst its banks and flooded the entire neighborhood.
After hours of heavy rain, water began to flood the lower levels of the building.
Bright sunlight flooded the room when she opened the curtains.
Memories flood her mind every time she visits her childhood home.
After several hours of relentless rain, the river began to flood its banks.
The kitchen will flood if you forget to turn off the tap.
The parking lot flooded last night after the storm.

Synonyms

  • deluge
    6.0 0.12/m
    deluge pours large amounts of water over land very quickly, while flood means water covers or overwhelms an area.
  • inundate
    6.8 2.89/m
    inundate covers something completely with water or overwhelms it with too much, while flood means water fills an area.
  • swamp
    4.0 3.13/m
    swamp fills land with water or overwhelms people with work, while flood means water covers or fills an area.

Related Terms

  • antonym: drain - to remove liquid from something
  • synonym: inundate - to flood or cover with water
  • synonym: overflow - to spill over the bounds or limits, especially of liquid
