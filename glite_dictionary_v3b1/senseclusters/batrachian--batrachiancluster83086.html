batrachian

noun
frog or toad; tailless amphibian
Pronunciation: / bəˈtreɪkiən / (AmE), / bəˈtreɪkɪən / (BrE)
any amphibian of the group that includes frogs and toads, typically tailless and having long hind limbs for leaping

Examples

A batrachian leaps
The biologist spent years studying various species of batrachian in the Amazon rainforest.
The exhibition featured a rare batrachian specimen discovered in Madagascar.
Unlike most reptiles, the batrachian thrives in both aquatic and terrestrial environments.
