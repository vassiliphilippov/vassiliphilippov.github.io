log cabin

noun
house built from logs
1.8
Pronunciation: / lɔɡ ˈkæbɪn / (AmE), / lɒɡ ˈkabɪn / (BrE)
A small house made from logs, typically with a simple structure, often found in rural or wilderness areas.

Examples

A log cabin house
Abraham Lincoln was born in a humble log cabin in Kentucky.
The log cabin was warm inside despite the snowstorm raging outside.
They spent the weekend in a cozy log cabin deep in the woods.
