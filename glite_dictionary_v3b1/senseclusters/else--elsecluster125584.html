else

adverb
otherwise; if not
1.0 19/m
Pronunciation: / É›ls / (AmE), / É›ls / (BrE)
used after 'or' to mean 'otherwise' or 'if not', often introducing a negative consequence or alternative.

Examples

Or else trouble
You must leave now, else you will miss the train.
Study hard, else you might fail the exam.
Call me when you arrive, else I will worry.

Synonyms

  • otherwise
    3.2 54/m
    otherwise warns of what happens if not and goes in whole clauses, while else follows 'or' and marks simple alternatives.
  • or else
    2.0 8.34/m
    or else adds a threat or firm consequence after 'or', while else by itself often just gives a simple alternative.

  • synonym: otherwise - in a different way or if not
