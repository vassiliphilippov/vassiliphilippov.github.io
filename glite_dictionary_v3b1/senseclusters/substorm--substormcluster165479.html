substorm

noun
magnetospheric disturbance
9.3
Pronunciation: /ˈsʌb.stɔːrm/ /; / /ˈsʌb.stɑːrm/ (AmE), /ˈsʌb.stɔːm/ (BrE)
a brief disturbance in Earth's magnetosphere that often results in increased auroral activity

Examples

A substorm event
Scientists observed a sudden substorm that brightened the night sky with vivid auroras.
The satellite recorded data during the intense substorm over the polar region.
A substorm can cause temporary disruptions in radio communications at high latitudes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aurora - a natural light display in the Earth's sky, often caused by magnetospheric activity
  • degree higher: geomagnetic storm - a major disturbance of Earth's magnetosphere caused by solar wind
