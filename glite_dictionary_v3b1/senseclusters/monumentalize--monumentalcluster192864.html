monumentalize

verb
commemorate with a monument
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːn.jəˈmen.tl̩.aɪz /; / ˌmɑːn.jəˈmen.tə.laɪz / (AmE), / ˌmɒn.jəˈmen.tl.aɪz /; / ˌmɒn.jʊˈmen.tə.laɪz / (BrE)
To record or commemorate (a person, event, or thing) in a lasting way, especially by creating a monument or similar structure.

Examples

To monumentalize achievements
Her achievements were monumentalized in a grand memorial at the university.
The city decided to monumentalize the hero with a statue in the central square.
Writers often monumentalize significant events in history through powerful storytelling.
Many cultures monumentalise important events to ensure their stories are passed down through generations.
She hoped to monumentalise her father's achievements by commissioning a commemorative plaque in the museum.
The city council decided to monumentalise the sacrifice of healthcare workers with a bronze statue in the central square.

Related Terms

  • synonym: commemorate - to honor the memory of someone or something, especially with a ceremony or object
  • antonym: forget - to fail to remember; neglect in memory
  • synonym: memorialize - to preserve the memory of; to commemorate
