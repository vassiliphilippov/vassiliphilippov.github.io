impenetrably

adverb
in an impossible-to-pass way
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɛnɪtrəbli / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛnɪtrəbli / (BrE)
in a way that cannot be entered, passed through, or understood

Examples

Written impenetrably confusing
The instructions were written so impenetrably that no one could follow them.
Fog covered the valley impenetrably, making it impossible to see the road ahead.
The author’s style is sometimes impenetrably complex for new readers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clearly - in a way that is easy to understand
  • synonym: inaccessibly - in a way that is difficult or impossible to reach
