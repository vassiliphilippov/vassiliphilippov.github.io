soapstone

noun
soft, compact mineral used for ornaments, etc.
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊpˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈsəʊpstəʊn / (BrE)
a soft, heavy, compact mineral variety of talc with a soapy feel, used for hearths, tabletops, and ornaments

Examples

A soapstone hearth
Hearths made of soapstone retain heat remarkably well for hours.
Many artists prefer working with soapstone because of its smooth texture and ease of carving.
The sculpture was carefully carved from soapstone and displayed in the gallery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: steatite - another name for soapstone, especially in mineralogy
