combine

verb
to mix or join things together
4.1 2.3
Pronunciation: /kəm.ˈbaɪn/ (AmE), /kəm.ˈbaɪn/ (BrE)
to mix, join, or bring together two or more things to form a single unit or group

Examples

To combine ingredients
Their efforts combined to create a successful project.
When red and yellow are combined, they make orange.
You should combine the flour and eggs until the mixture is smooth.
For this recipe, simply combine all the ingredients in one large bowl.
Their voices combined beautifully in the choir's performance.
You should combine the flour and eggs before adding the milk.
The companies decided to combine their resources to develop a new product.
They are planning to combine their efforts to clean up the park.
When you combine these two groups, you get a total of fifty students.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blend - to mix two or more substances so that they combine smoothly
  • synonym: join - to come together or connect with another
  • synonym: merge - to join together to form a single entity
  • synonym: mix - to combine two or more substances or things so they become one
  • antonym: separate - to move or be moved apart; to break into pieces
  • synonym: unite - to bring or come together into a single unit
