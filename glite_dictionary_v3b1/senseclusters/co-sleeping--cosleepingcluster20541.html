co-sleeping

adjective
sharing a sleeping space
Pronunciation: / ˌkoʊˈsliːpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌkəʊˈsliːpɪŋ / (BrE)
describing an arrangement where people, often a parent and child, sleep together in the same bed or room.

Examples

Co-sleeping family bed
Many parents choose a co-sleeping arrangement to stay close to their newborn at night.
The co-sleeping practice is common in some cultures for bonding and convenience.
Some families find that co-sleeping helps their children feel more secure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: room-sharing - sleeping in the same room as another person
  • antonym: solo sleeping - sleeping alone in a separate bed or room
