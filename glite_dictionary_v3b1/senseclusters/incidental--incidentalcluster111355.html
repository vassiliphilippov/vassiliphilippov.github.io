incidental

adjective
happening as a secondary consequence
6.5 4.8
Pronunciation: /ɪn.sɪ.ˈden.təl/ /; / /ɪn.sə.ˈden.təl/ (AmE), /ɪn.sɪ.ˈden.təl/ /; / /ɪn.sə.ˈden.təl/ (BrE)
happening as a minor or secondary result or feature; not the main or primary thing

Examples

Incidental detail
Her promotion was incidental to the major changes happening in the company.
The damage to the fence was incidental and did not affect its function.
There were several incidental costs that we had not anticipated when we started the renovation.
Her interest in art was always incidental to her passion for science.
In the overall scheme of the investigation, his role was merely incidental.
The noise is incidental compared to the benefits the renovation will bring.
Any incidental damage resulting from the repairs will be fixed at no extra charge.
The noise was just an incidental effect of the construction next door.
There were several incidental expenses that we had not budgeted for during the trip.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accidental - happening by chance, not planned
  • synonym: consequential - resulting as an effect or outcome
  • antonym: essential - absolutely necessary; extremely important
  • antonym: primary - of first importance, main
  • synonym: resultant - occurring as a result or consequence
  • synonym: secondary - not primary, of minor importance
  • antonym: significant - important and meaningful
