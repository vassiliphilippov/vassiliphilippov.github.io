tineid moth

noun
small moth whose larvae eat wool or fur
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪniɪd mɑθ / (AmE), / ˈtaɪniːɪd mɒθ / (BrE)
a small yellowish moth of the family Tineidae, whose larvae feed on wool, fur, and other animal fibers

Examples

A tineid moth larva
A tineid moth infestation can ruin stored woolen garments over time.
Scientists studied the life cycle of the common tineid moth to develop better pest control.
The larvae of the tineid moth feed on animal fibers, damaging carpets and clothing in households.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clothes moth - a type of moth whose larvae consume animal fibers such as wool or fur
