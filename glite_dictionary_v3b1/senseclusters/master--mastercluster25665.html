master

verb
learn or control thoroughly
Pronunciation: / ˈmæstɚ /; / ˈmæs.tɚ /; / ˈmæstər / (AmE), / ˈmɑːstə /; / ˈmɑː.stər / (BrE)
to learn something thoroughly or become very skilled or proficient; to gain control of or overcome something

Examples

To master a skill
After months of practice, she finally mastered the piano piece.
He hopes to master several programming languages before graduating.
It takes years to master the art of traditional Japanese cooking.
After months of practice, she finally mastered the complicated piano piece.
He struggled with his fear of public speaking, but eventually mastered it.
Learning to master your emotions can make handling stressful situations much easier.
After years of practice, she finally mastered the violin and performed on stage.
It took him months to master his fear of heights and climb the tower.
The team quickly mastered the new software and streamlined their workflow.

Related Terms

  • synonym: excel - to be extremely good at something; to surpass others
  • degree lower: learn - to gain knowledge or skill in something
  • synonym: overcome - succeed in dealing with a problem or difficulty
  • antonym: struggle - to make a difficult or labored effort to do something, especially when lacking skill
  • antonym: succumb - fail to resist; to give way to something
