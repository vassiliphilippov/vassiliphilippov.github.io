organic light-emitting diode

noun
self-luminous electronic display component
6.9
Pronunciation: / ɔrˈɡænɪk laɪt ɪˈmɪtɪŋ daɪˌoʊd / (AmE), / ɔːˈɡanɪk laɪt ɪˈmɪtɪŋ dʌɪˌəʊd / (BrE)
a type of self-luminous electronic device made with organic compounds that emits light when an electric current is applied, commonly used in display screens and lighting without the need for separate backlighting.

Examples

Organic light-emitting diode display
Replacing traditional lights, organic light-emitting diode panels can offer energy efficiency and flexibility in design.
Televisions with organic light-emitting diode technology are admired for their thinness and picture quality.
The smartphone uses an organic light-emitting diode display for deeper blacks and vibrant colors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: LED - light-emitting diode, an electronic device that emits light when current flows through it
