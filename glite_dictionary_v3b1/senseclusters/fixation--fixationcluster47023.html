fixation

noun
preservation of tissue in histology
6.0 6.0
Pronunciation: / fɪkˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / fɪkˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
(Histology) The process of preserving and hardening a tissue sample so that its structure remains as close as possible to its natural state.

Examples

Tissue fixation
A common fixation method involves using formaldehyde to preserve cellular structures.
Proper fixation of the tissue is essential for accurate microscopic analysis.
The specimen underwent fixation before it was embedded in paraffin.
