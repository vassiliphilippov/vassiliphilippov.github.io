fiddle-shaped

adjective
having a shape like a fiddle: rounded ends, narrow center
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪd.əl ˌʃeɪpt / (AmE), / ˈfɪd.əl ˌʃeɪpt / (BrE)
(of a leaf or object) having rounded ends and a narrowed, contracted center, resembling the shape of a fiddle or violin.

Examples

Fiddle-shaped leaves
She chose a houseplant with large, fiddle-shaped leaves to decorate her living room.
Some violas have a fiddle-shaped body, giving them a unique appearance among instruments.
The fig tree's leaves are often described as fiddle-shaped due to their distinct outline.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: lyrate - lyre-shaped, having one large rounded section and smaller lobes (of leaves)
  • degree lower: spatulate - shaped like a spatula, broad and rounded at the tip, narrowing at the base (of leaves)
