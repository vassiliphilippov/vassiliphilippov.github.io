provide for

verb
make arrangements for future needs
Pronunciation: / prəˈvaɪd fɔr /; / prəˈvaɪd fər / (AmE), / prəˈvaɪd fə / (BrE)
to make arrangements or take steps in advance to ensure that needs are met or that something is possible or allowed

Examples

To provide for children
It is important to provide for your children's education by saving early.
The new policy was designed to provide for those who are unable to work.
They have carefully provided for any unexpected emergencies during the trip.
The law provides for emergency funding in case of natural disasters.
The new contract provides for annual salary increases based on performance.
The schedule provides for several breaks throughout the day.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allow for - to make allowances or plans for something
  • synonym: leave room for - to permit the possibility or opportunity for something
  • antonym: neglect - to fail to care for or attend to
  • synonym: prepare - to make ready or get ready for something
