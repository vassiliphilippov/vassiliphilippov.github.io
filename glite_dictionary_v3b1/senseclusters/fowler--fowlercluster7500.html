fowler

noun
hunter of wild birds
Pronunciation: / ˈfaʊlər / (AmE), / ˈfaʊlə / (BrE)
a person who hunts wild birds for food

Examples

A fowler hunts birds
Ancient fowlers used nets and decoys to lure wild birds.
In medieval times, a skilled fowler was highly valued for providing fresh game.
The fowler set traps at dawn, hoping to catch enough birds for the market.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hunter - a person who hunts wild animals
