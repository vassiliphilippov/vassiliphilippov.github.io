Synonyms

  • mount
    3.0 5.47/m
    mount means to get up onto a horse or bicycle to ride, while get on means to climb onto the back to use it.
  • mount up
    4.0 0.27/m
    mount up is a slightly old-fashioned or formal phrase for getting onto a horse to ride, while get on is everyday and neutral.
  • climb on
    2.0 1.48/m
    climb on means to go up onto a surface or animal with effort, while get on is used for mounting to ride.
  • hop on
    2.7 1.61/m
    hop on suggests getting on quickly and lightly, often for a bus or bike, while get on is neutral and general.
  • jump on
    1.8 0.86/m
    jump on implies getting on suddenly or forcefully, sometimes in a rough or unsafe way, while get on is a plain act of mounting.
