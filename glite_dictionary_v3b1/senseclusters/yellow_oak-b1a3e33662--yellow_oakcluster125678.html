yellow oak

noun
timber tree with yellow inner bark

A medium to large deciduous tree of the eastern United States and southeastern Canada, with dark outer bark, yellow inner bark used for tanning, and broad, bristle-tipped, five-lobed leaves.

Examples

The yellow oak tree
In autumn, the leaves of the yellow oak turn a bright color before falling.
Tanners once used the bark of the yellow oak as a valuable source for making leather.
The forester identified the tree as a yellow oak by its distinctive yellowish inner bark.
