deal

verb
behave toward someone in a particular way
Pronunciation: / diːl / (AmE), / diːl / (BrE)
to manage, handle, or take action to address a situation, problem, or person

Examples

To deal with problems
As a manager, he always tried to deal fairly with his staff.

Teachers must learn to deal patiently with difficult students.

Her latest novel deals honestly with themes of loss and forgiveness.
The documentary deals with the issue of climate change in great depth.
The professor's lecture dealt with the origins of modern democracy.
