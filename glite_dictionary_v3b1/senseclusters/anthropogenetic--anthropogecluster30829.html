anthropogenetic

adjective
caused by humans
Pronunciation: / ˌænθrəpəˈdʒənɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌænθrəpəˈdʒɛnɪtɪk / (BrE)
originating from or caused by human activity or influence

Examples

Anthropogenetic effects
Some scientists argue that climate change is largely anthropogenetic rather than a natural process.
The spread of certain invasive species is considered anthropogenetic due to human transportation.
Many environmental problems today are anthropogenetic and require changes in human behavior.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anthropogenic - caused by human activity
  • antonym: natural - existing in or caused by nature, not by humans
