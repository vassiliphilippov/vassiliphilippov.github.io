attestor

noun
one who certifies a document's authenticity
6.5 6.6
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈtes.tər/ /; / /ˈæt.es.tər/ (AmE), /ə.ˈtes.tə/ /; / /ˈæt.es.tə/ (BrE)
A person who formally certifies the authenticity of a document or signature by signing it, especially as a witness.

Examples

Document attestor
As an attestor, she confirmed that the signature was genuine.
Each document requires at least one attestor to validate its authenticity.
The attestor signed the will in the presence of both the lawyer and the client.

Related Terms

  • synonym: witness - a person who observes an event and can verify its occurrence, especially a legal document's signing
