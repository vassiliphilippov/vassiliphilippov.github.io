a person who believes that all events are predetermined and inevitable, and who accepts that they cannot change their destiny

Examples

A true fatalist
As a fatalist, she believed that no amount of effort could change what was meant to be.
Despite her friends' optimism, she remained a fatalist about her future.
The fatalist in him accepted bad luck without complaint.

Synonyms

  • determinist
    8.8 0.34/m
    a determinist says events follow prior causes and people lack power to change them, while a fatalist accepts that destiny is inevitable.
  • predestinationist
    9.9 0.00/m
    a predestinationist believes divine will fixes a person's destiny before life begins, while a fatalist simply accepts inevitable events.
  • necessitarian
    9.9 0.01/m
    a necessitarian denies free will and sees actions as necessary, while a fatalist believes events are fixed and accepts their helplessness.
  • predestinarian
    9.9 0.01/m
    a predestinarian sees salvation and key outcomes as set by divine power, while a fatalist accepts all life's events as inevitable.
