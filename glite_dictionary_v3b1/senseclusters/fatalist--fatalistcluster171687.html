fatalist

noun
person who believes fate controls events
4.5
Pronunciation: / ˈfeɪtəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈfeɪtəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that all events are predetermined and inevitable, and who accepts that they cannot change their destiny

Examples

A true fatalist
As a fatalist, she believed that no amount of effort could change what was meant to be.
Despite her friends' optimism, she remained a fatalist about her future.
The fatalist in him accepted bad luck without complaint.

Related Terms

  • synonym: determinist - one who believes that all events are determined by previously existing causes
  • antonym: optimist - someone who tends to be hopeful and confident about the future
  • antonym: pessimist - someone who expects the worst possible outcome
