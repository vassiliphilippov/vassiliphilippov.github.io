conditional response

noun
learned reaction to a stimulus
4.7 5.7
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈdɪʃ.ən.əl rɪ.ˈspɑːns/ /; / /kən.ˈdɪʃ.ən.əl riː.ˈspɑːns/ (AmE), /kən.ˈdɪʃ.ən.əl rɪ.ˈspɒns/ (BrE)
a learned reaction that occurs in response to a specific stimulus, typically after repeated association

Examples

A conditional response to sound
Eye blinking in response to a particular sound can be a conditional response.
Over time, the child developed a conditional response to the teacher's raised hand.
The dog salivates as a conditional response to the ringing bell.
