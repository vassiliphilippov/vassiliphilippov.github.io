infectiousness

noun
ability to spread
6.3
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈfek.ʃəs.nəs/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈfek.ʃəs.nəs/ (BrE)
the quality of causing emotions, ideas, or behaviors to spread quickly from person to person, like a contagion.

Examples

Infectiousness of laughter
Her laughter had a special infectiousness that made everyone in the room start smiling.
The infectiousness of his enthusiasm inspired the whole team to work harder.
The infectiousness of the new idea quickly spread throughout the company.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contagiousness - the ability to spread from one person to another
  • synonym: influence - the power to affect others' thoughts or actions
  • antonym: isolation - the state of being separated from others
