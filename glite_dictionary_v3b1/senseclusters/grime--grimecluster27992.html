grime

noun
dirt or soot making things dirty
Pronunciation: / ɡraɪm / (AmE), / ɡraɪm / (BrE)
dirt or soot that makes something look dirty or unclean.

Examples

Grime on the window
He wiped away the grime from the window to let the sunlight in.
The workers were covered in grime after a long day at the factory.
Years of grime had built up on the kitchen tiles.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cleanliness - the state of being clean
  • synonym: dirt - any substance that makes something unclean
  • synonym: filth - thick, unpleasant dirt
