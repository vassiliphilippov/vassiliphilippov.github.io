positron

noun
subatomic particle with positive charge
5.1 7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈpɑː.zə.trɑːn/ /; / /ˈpɑː.zɪ.trɑːn/ (AmE), /ˈpɒ.zɪ.trɒn/ (BrE)
a subatomic particle with the same mass as an electron and a positive charge; when a positron and an electron interact, they are annihilated.

Examples

A positron particle
A positron is the antiparticle counterpart of the electron in modern physics.
Scientists can detect positrons in certain types of radioactive decay.
When a positron and an electron meet, they annihilate each other and release energy.
