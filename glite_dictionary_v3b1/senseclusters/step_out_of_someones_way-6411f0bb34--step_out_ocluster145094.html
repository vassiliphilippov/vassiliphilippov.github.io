step out of someone's way

verb
move aside for someone
Pronunciation: / stɛp aʊt əv sʌmwʌnz weɪ / (AmE), / stɛp aʊt əv sʌmwʌnz weɪ / (BrE)
to move aside so that someone can pass or proceed unimpeded

Examples

To step out of his way
I stepped out of his way when I saw he was in a hurry.
Please step out of her way so she can get through the door.
They quickly stepped out of my way as I carried the heavy boxes.
