iron hand

noun
strict or harsh control
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪ.ɚn hænd / (AmE), / ˈaɪ.ən hænd / (BrE)
strict and absolute control, often exercised in a harsh or authoritarian manner

Examples

Rule with an iron hand
After years of chaos, the government imposed order by governing with an iron hand.
Some believe that raising children with an iron hand creates discipline, while others see it as too harsh.
The new CEO ruled the company with an iron hand, allowing no dissent from employees.

Related Terms

  • antonym: velvet glove - gentle or diplomatic approach, often contrasted with harshness
