radicalization

noun
formation of free radicals
8.0
Pronunciation: /ræ.dɪ.kə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ræ.dɪ.kə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the chemical process by which free radicals are formed, often during reactions that break chemical bonds

Examples

Chemical radicalization
The radicalization of the molecule occurred rapidly when exposed to ultraviolet light.
Scientists study radicalization to understand how certain chemical reactions proceed.
During combustion, radicalization can lead to the formation of reactive intermediates.

  • antonym: reduction - a chemical reaction involving the gain of electrons
