Synonyms

  • praiseworthy
    4.5 0.6/m
    praiseworthy is clearly deserving praise for an action, while admirable is broader and inspires respect or admiration in people.
  • laudable
    7.2 1/m
    laudable means deserving strong praise in formal settings, while admirable is more general and used in everyday admiration.
  • commendable
    4.6 2/m
    commendable describes actions worthy of praise and approval, while admirable can refer to people, actions, or qualities that inspire admiration.
  • meritorious
    7.5 0.7/m
    meritorious often shows deserving reward or formal recognition, while admirable focuses on inspiring public respect or personal admiration.
  • applaudable
    6.1 0.01/m
    applaudable suggests public praise or applause for an act, while admirable covers a wider range of traits that inspire respect.
  • estimable
    7.0 0.4/m
    estimable sounds formal and means worthy of esteem, while admirable is more common and implies people feel respect and wonder.
  • honorable
    3.7 11/m
    honorable describes high moral standards and fairness, while admirable can praise many traits, not only moral actions or choices.
  • worthy
    2.8 30/m

  • creditable
