nurture

verb
to provide nourishment; to feed
1.4
Pronunciation: / ˈnɝː.tʃɚ /; / ˈnɜrtʃər / (AmE), / ˈnɜː.tʃə /; / ˈnɜːtʃə / (BrE)
to care for, nourish, or encourage the growth or development of someone or something

Examples

To nurture a child
A healthy diet is essential to nurture a child’s growth.
The soil in this region helps to nurture the delicate grapevines.
The volunteers worked to nurture the rescued animals back to health.
Parents should nurture their children's creativity from a young age.
She did her best to nurture an interest in science in her students.
The mentor helped nurture his leadership skills over time.
Good teachers not only educate but also nurture young minds to help them reach their potential.
He was nurtured by his grandparents after the loss of his parents.
She devoted her life to nurturing her children through every stage of their development.

Related Terms

  • synonym: feed - to give food to; to supply with nourishment
  • synonym: foster - to encourage the development of something, especially something desirable
  • antonym: neglect - to fail to care for properly
  • synonym: raise - to bring up a child
  • antonym: starve - to suffer or die from lack of food
