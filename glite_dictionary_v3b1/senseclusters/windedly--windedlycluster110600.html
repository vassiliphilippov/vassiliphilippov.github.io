windedly

adverb
breathlessly, gasping
9.1
Pronunciation: /ˈwɪn.dɪd.liː/ /; / /ˈwɪn.dəd.liː/ (AmE), /ˈwɪn.dɪd.liː/ /; / /ˈwɪn.dəd.liː/ (BrE)
in a breathless or gasping way, as if out of breath from exertion or shock

Examples

Breathing windedly
He spoke windedly after running up the stairs to deliver the message.
The player collapsed windedly on the field after the intense match.
She answered the phone windedly, still catching her breath from her jog.
After being hit in the stomach, he walked windedly across the field, trying to catch his breath.
She answered windedly after the shocking news, struggling to process what had happened.
The boxer stood windedly in the ring, momentarily unable to defend himself.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calmly - in a relaxed and untroubled manner
  • synonym: dizzily - in a way that feels unsteady or confused
  • antonym: steadily - in a firm, controlled, or stable manner
