blade

noun
flat part of a propeller or fan
1.1 2.6
Pronunciation: /bleɪd/ (AmE), /bleɪd/ (BrE)
the flat, extended part of an object such as a propeller or fan that moves through air or water to create motion or force

Examples

Fan blade
Modern wind turbines have extremely long blades to capture more wind energy.
She cleaned the blades of the ceiling fan to improve its performance.
The helicopter's blades spun rapidly as it prepared to take off.

Related Terms

  • synonym: propeller - a device with rotating blades for moving a boat or aircraft
