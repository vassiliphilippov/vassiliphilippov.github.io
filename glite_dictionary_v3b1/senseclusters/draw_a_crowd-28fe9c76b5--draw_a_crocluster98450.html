draw a crowd

verb
attract a group of people
1.6
Pronunciation: / drɔ ə kraʊd / (AmE), / drɔː ə kraʊd / (BrE)
to attract a large group of people as an audience or gathering

Examples

To draw a crowd quickly
Her bold artwork managed to draw a crowd at the gallery opening.
The protest in the city center drew a crowd of several hundred people.
The street performer always draws a crowd during the summer festivals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attract attention - to make people notice something or someone
  • antonym: disperse a crowd - to cause a group of people to scatter or leave
