Jesuit order

noun
Roman Catholic religious order (Society of Jesus)
6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒe.zjuː.ɪt ˈɔːr.dər/ /; / /ˈdʒe.ʒuː.ɪt ˈɔːr.dər/ (AmE), /ˈdʒe.zjuː.ɪt ˈɔː.də/ /; / /ˈdʒe.ʒuː.ɪt ˈɔː.də/ (BrE)
A Roman Catholic religious order, officially known as the Society of Jesus, founded by Saint Ignatius of Loyola in 1534, noted for its work in education, scholarship, and missionary activity.

Examples

The Jesuit order society

Many members of the Jesuit order have played significant roles in the fields of science and philosophy.
The Jesuit order established some of the world's oldest and most respected universities.
