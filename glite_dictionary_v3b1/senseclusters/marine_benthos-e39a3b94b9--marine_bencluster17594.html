marine benthos

noun
sea-bottom-dwelling organisms
Pronunciation: / məˈrin ˈbɛnθoʊs / (AmE), / məˈriːn ˈbɛnθɒs / (BrE)
Organisms, including plants and animals, that live at or near the bottom of the sea.

Examples

Marine benthos organisms
Fishing practices can have a significant impact on the marine benthos.
Scientists study the marine benthos to understand environmental changes in the sea.
The marine benthos supports diverse ecosystems on the ocean floor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: benthos - organisms that live at the bottom of a body of water
  • antonym: pelagic - organisms living in the open sea rather than near the bottom
