borderline intelligence

noun
IQ just above intellectual disability
6.5 6.4
Pronunciation: /ˈbɔːr.dər.laɪn ɪn.ˈtel.ə.dʒəns/ (AmE), /ˈbɔː.də.laɪn ɪn.ˈtel.ɪ.dʒəns/ (BrE)
the level of intelligence just above intellectual disability, typically referring to an IQ range at the lower limit of normal, where a person can function independently without institutional assistance

Examples

Borderline intelligence level
Children with borderline intelligence may face challenges in school but can often live independently as adults.
Support services can help individuals with borderline intelligence develop life skills.
The psychologist determined that the patient had borderline intelligence and did not require institutional care.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: average intelligence - intelligence within the normal range for the population
  • degree lower: intellectual disability - significantly below-average cognitive ability and adaptive functioning
