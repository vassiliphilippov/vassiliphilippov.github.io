flusher

noun
toilet cleaning device
Pronunciation: / ˈflʌʃər / (AmE), / ˈflʌʃə / (BrE)
a device or mechanism that releases water to clean or flush a toilet or similar fixture

Examples

Toilet flusher
The flusher in the public restroom was not working, so the toilet could not be cleaned properly.
After pressing the flusher, a strong stream of water rinsed the toilet bowl.
Maintenance staff replaced the broken flusher to ensure the toilets functioned correctly.
The flusher in the public restroom was not working, so the toilet could not be used.
After years of use, the old flusher needed to be replaced by a plumber.
She pressed the flusher and the toilet quickly emptied and refilled with clean water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flush - to cause water to flow through and clean something
  • synonym: flush handle - the lever or button used to activate a toilet flush
  • synonym: flush valve - the mechanism that releases water to flush a toilet
  • antonym: plunger - a tool used to clear blockages in toilets or drains
