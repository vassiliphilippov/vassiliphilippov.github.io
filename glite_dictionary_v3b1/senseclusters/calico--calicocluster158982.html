calico

noun
coarse printed cotton cloth
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkælɪˌkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈkælɪkəʊ / (BrE)
A coarse, plain-woven cotton cloth, often printed with a bright pattern.

Examples

Calico fabric
Old-fashioned quilts were often pieced together from scraps of calico.
She bought several yards of calico to sew curtains for the kitchen.
The dress was made from a lovely calico with a bright floral print.
