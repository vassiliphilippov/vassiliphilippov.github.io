skelter

verb
to rush disorderly
Pronunciation: / ˈskɛltər / (AmE), / ˈskɛltə / (BrE)
to move quickly in a confused or disorderly way

Examples

To skelter away
The children skeltered out of the classroom as soon as the bell rang.
When the rain started, people skeltered for cover under the nearest awning.
Excited fans skeltered toward the stage when the band appeared.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dash - to run or move quickly and suddenly
  • antonym: saunter - to walk in a slow, relaxed manner
  • synonym: scurry - to move quickly with short, hurried movements
