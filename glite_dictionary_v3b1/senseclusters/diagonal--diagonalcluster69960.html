diagonal

adjective
having a slanted or oblique direction
7.7
Pronunciation: / daɪˈæɡənl̩ /; / daɪˈæɡənəl / (AmE), / daɪˈæɡənl̩ /; / daɪˈaɡən(ə)l / (BrE)
slanted or at an angle; not at a right angle to the sides of a shape

Examples

Diagonal stripes
He walked across the field in a diagonal path rather than straight across.
Sunlight streamed through the window at a diagonal angle, casting long shadows on the floor.
The painter arranged the lines in a diagonal pattern across the canvas.
Draw a diagonal line from the top right to the bottom left corner.
She measured the diagonal distance across the television screen.
The square tablecloth was placed so that its diagonal edges hung over the sides.
