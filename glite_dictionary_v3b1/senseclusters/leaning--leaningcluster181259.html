Synonyms

  • tendency
    2.8 31/m
    a tendency is a general likelihood to act or think a certain way, while leaning is a softer personal preference.
  • inclination
    4.2 6.09/m
    an inclination is a natural feeling or desire to choose something, while leaning is a broader tendency toward an idea.
  • preference
    2.1 24/m
    a preference is a clear liking for one thing over others, while leaning is a looser tendency or partial choice.
  • tilt
    4.4 1.49/m
    a tilt is a slight, visible bias toward one side or view, while leaning can be stronger or less noticeable.
  • propensity
    7.3 4.66/m
    a propensity is a natural habit or tendency to behave in certain ways, while leaning often names a personal opinion or choice.
  • predisposition
    7.1 1.27/m
    a predisposition is an underlying tendency often based on past factors or biology, while leaning usually means a simple present preference.
  • proclivity
    8.5 1.54/m
    a proclivity is a natural liking or habit, often for something negative, while leaning is a neutral tendency toward an idea or choice.
