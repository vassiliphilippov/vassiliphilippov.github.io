implacably

adverb
without being calmed
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈplækəbli / (AmE), / ɪmˈplækəbli / (BrE)
in a way that cannot be calmed, changed, or appeased

Examples

To be implacably opposed
The judge remained implacably opposed to any reduction in the sentence.
She pursued her goals implacably, never letting obstacles slow her down.
The enemy advanced implacably, showing no signs of retreat or hesitation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gently - in a kind or mild manner
  • synonym: mercilessly - in a way that shows no mercy or pity
  • synonym: relentlessly - in a way that does not stop or become less severe
