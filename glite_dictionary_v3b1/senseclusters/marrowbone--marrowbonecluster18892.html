marrowbone

noun
bone with edible marrow, used in cooking
Pronunciation: / ˈmæroʊˌboʊn / (AmE), / ˈmærəʊˌbəʊn / (BrE)
a bone from an animal, especially a beef or veal bone, that contains edible marrow, often used to flavor soups and stews

Examples

A marrowbone soup
She added a marrowbone to the pot to give the soup a richer flavor.
The chef served roasted marrowbone with freshly baked bread.
Traditionally, a marrowbone is prized for making hearty broths and stews.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bone marrow - soft tissue inside bones, often eaten as food
