Humulus lupulus

noun
plant used to flavor beer (common hop)
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈhjuːmjələs luːˈpjuːləs / (AmE), / ˈhjuːmjʊləs ljuːˈpjuːləs / (BrE)
A European climbing plant, also known as the common hop, whose flowers are used mainly to flavor beer and other malt liquors.

Examples

Humulus lupulus flowers
Farmers in the region cultivate Humulus lupulus for local craft breweries.
Hops, derived from Humulus lupulus, impart a distinctive bitterness and aroma to beer.
The flowers of Humulus lupulus are essential for brewing traditional ales and lagers.
