vent

noun
slit in a garment, esp. in a jacket
Pronunciation: / vɛnt / (AmE), / vɛnt /; / vent / (BrE)
an opening or passage that allows air, gas, liquid, or other substances to escape or enter (such as in clothing, buildings, the earth, or animals)

Examples

Air vent
She preferred jackets without a vent for a sleeker silhouette.
Single or double vents are common features in men's suit jackets.
The tailor added a vent to the back of my blazer for extra comfort.
During spawning, eggs and sperm are released from the vent into the water.
The biologist examined the turtle's vent to determine its sex.
The fish expels waste through its vent, located just behind the pelvic fins.
An underwater volcanic vent supports a unique ecosystem of marine life.
Molten lava spewed from the main vent during the eruption.
Scientists studied the gases emitted by the vent to learn more about the volcano's activity.
