overbuy

noun
excessive purchase
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈbaɪ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈbaɪ / (BrE)
the act or instance of buying more of something than is needed or wanted

Examples

A grocery overbuy
Her weekly overbuy left fresh vegetables rotting in the back of the fridge.
A sudden overbuy of supplies meant the company had to find extra storage space.
He regretted his overbuy when he realized most of the items would go unused.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overspend - to spend more money than intended or necessary
  • antonym: underbuy - to buy less than is needed or wanted
