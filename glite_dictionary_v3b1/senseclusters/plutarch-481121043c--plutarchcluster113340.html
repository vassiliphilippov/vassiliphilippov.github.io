Plutarch

proper noun
ancient Greek biographer and essayist
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈpluˌtɑrk / (AmE), / ˈpluːˌtɑːk / (BrE)
(c. 46–c. 120 AD) A Greek biographer and essayist, best known for his work 'Parallel Lives', a series of biographies of famous Greeks and Romans.

Examples

Plutarch wrote biographies
Plutarch wrote biographies that have informed our understanding of ancient Greece and Rome.
Many scholars rely on Plutarch as a source for the lives of classical figures.
The influence of Plutarch can be seen in later historians and writers.
