Greenwich Meridian

noun
prime meridian through Greenwich, London
4.9
Pronunciation: /ˈgren.ɪtʃ mə.ˈrɪd.ɪ.ən/ /; / /ˈgriːn.wɪtʃ mə.ˈrɪd.ɪ.ən/ (AmE), /ˈgren.ɪtʃ mə.ˈrɪd.ɪ.ən/ /; / /ˈgriːn.wɪtʃ mə.ˈrɪd.ɪ.ən/ (BrE)
the meridian (line of longitude) running through Greenwich, London, internationally adopted in 1884 as the earth's prime meridian (zero degrees longitude)

Examples

The Greenwich Meridian line
Since 1884, the Greenwich Meridian has been internationally recognized as the earth's prime meridian.
The Greenwich Meridian serves as the starting point for measuring longitude around the world.
Tourists can stand with one foot in each hemisphere at the Greenwich Meridian in London.

Related Terms

  • antonym: International Date Line - the line roughly following 180º longitude, where the date changes by one day when crossed
  • synonym: prime meridian - the earth's zero of longitude, from which other longitudes are calculated
