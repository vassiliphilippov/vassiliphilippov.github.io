loser

noun
person who consistently fails
4.8 20/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàluÀêz…ôr / (AmE), / ÀàluÀêz…ô / (BrE)
a person who is habitually unsuccessful or is called a 'loser' in a negative or insulting way

Examples

Called a loser
Despite practicing every day, he remained a loser in the tennis league.
In high school, some students cruelly labeled him a loser because he wasn't popular.
She refuses to think of herself as a loser, despite several setbacks.
Don't be such a loser; just go for it!
He felt like a loser after failing the exam for the third time.
Some people unfairly label others as losers because of their mistakes.

Synonyms

  • no-account loser
    9.6 0.00/m
    no-account loser is an insulting phrase for someone seen as worthless, while loser is a general insult for an unsuccessful person.
  • failure
    2.7 25/m
    failure names a person who does not succeed, while loser sounds more insulting and is used to mock someone's whole character.
  • born loser
    6.5 0.03/m
    born loser means someone who seems destined to fail from birth, while loser is a general insult for habitual failure.
  • nonstarter
    7.6 0.01/m
    nonstarter describes someone who never gains a chance or respect, while loser focuses on being mocked for repeated failure.
  • dud
    5.8 0.62/m
    dud describes a person who disappoints or fails expectations, while loser is a harsher insult about a person's overall worth.
