collision course

noun
likely path leading to conflict
9.9
Pronunciation: / kəˈlɪʒ.ən kɔrs /; / kəˈlɪʒ.ən kɔɹs / (AmE), / kəˈlɪʒ.ən kɔːs / (BrE)
a path, situation, or course of action that is likely to lead to conflict, confrontation, or a crash if not changed

Examples

On a collision course
Environmental regulations and economic growth often seem to be set on a collision course.
Her refusal to compromise put her on a collision course with management.
The two companies are on a collision course over the patent dispute.
His aggressive strategy put him on a collision course with management.
If the asteroid stays on its current collision course, it could hit Earth in ten years.
The two planes were on a collision course before air traffic control intervened.
If they do not compromise soon, both nations could be on a collision course.
The new policy put the company on a collision course with environmental groups.
Their opposing views set them on a collision course during the meeting.
