Plasmodiidae

noun
family of malaria parasites
Pronunciation: / plæzˌmoʊˈdɪiːdiː / (AmE), / plazˌməʊˈdɪɪdɪiː / (BrE)
A family of protozoan parasites which includes the malaria-causing Plasmodium species.

Examples

Plasmodiidae family parasites
One member of the Plasmodiidae family, Plasmodium falciparum, is especially dangerous to human health.
Researchers are studying the life cycle of Plasmodiidae to develop better treatments for malaria.
The Plasmodiidae family comprises several species responsible for malaria in humans and animals.
