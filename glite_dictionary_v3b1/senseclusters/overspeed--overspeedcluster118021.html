overspeed

noun
excessive engine speed
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvərˌspiːd / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌspiːd / (BrE)
a condition in which a machine or engine operates above its safe speed limit

Examples

Overspeed warning light
The pilot noticed an overspeed warning and immediately reduced the throttle.
Continuous overspeed can cause serious damage to the turbine blades.
The safety system is designed to shut down the engine in case of overspeed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overload - a condition where a machine is subjected to excessive load
  • antonym: stall - a condition where an engine or machine stops working suddenly
