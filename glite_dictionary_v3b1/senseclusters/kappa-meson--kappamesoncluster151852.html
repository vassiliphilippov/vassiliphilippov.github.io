kappa-meson

noun
unstable subatomic meson
Pronunciation: / ˈkæpə ˌmiːsɑn / (AmE), / ˈkapə ˌmiːsɒn / (BrE)
An unstable subatomic particle (meson) produced in high-energy particle collisions.

Examples

A kappa-meson particle
Physicists observed a kappa-meson in the aftermath of the accelerator collision.
Researchers debate whether the kappa-meson should be classified as an ordinary meson or as an exotic particle.
The properties of the kappa-meson remain a focus of theoretical particle physics.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: kaon - another type of meson with similar properties but longer-lived
  • synonym: meson - a hadronic subatomic particle composed of one quark and one antiquark
  • degree lower: pion - a common type of meson involved in mediating the strong nuclear force
