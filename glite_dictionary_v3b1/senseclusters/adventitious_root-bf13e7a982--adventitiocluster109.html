adventitious root

noun
a root growing from an unusual place on a plant
8.4 6.5
Pronunciation: /æd.vən.ˈtɪʃ.əs ruːt/ /; / /æd.vɛn.ˈtɪʃ.əs ruːt/ /; / /æd.vən.ˈtɪʃ.əs rʊt/ (AmE), /æd.vən.ˈtɪʃ.əs ruːt/ /; / /æd.vɛn.ˈtɪʃ.əs ruːt/ (BrE)
a root that grows from an unusual part of a plant, such as from a stem or leaf rather than from the primary root system

Examples

Adventitious roots on stem
After the stem was buried in soil, several adventitious roots began to appear along its length.
Gardeners often encourage adventitious roots to grow by cutting and planting stem sections.
Some plants develop adventitious roots from their stems when grown in moist conditions.
