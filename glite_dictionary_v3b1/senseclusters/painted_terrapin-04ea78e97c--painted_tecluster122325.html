painted terrapin

noun
freshwater turtle with colorful markings
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪntɪd təˈrɛpɪn / (AmE), / ˈpeɪntɪd təˈrɛpɪn / (BrE)
a freshwater turtle native to the eastern United States, known for its distinctive bright yellow and red markings

Examples

A painted terrapin turtle
A young painted terrapin basked on a log at the edge of the pond.
Many conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the painted terrapin in the wild.
The painted terrapin is easily recognized by its vivid yellow and red stripes along the shell and head.
