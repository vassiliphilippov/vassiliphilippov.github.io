privilege

verb
to give special advantage
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪvəlɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈprɪvɪlɪdʒ / (BrE)
to give special importance, advantage, or priority to someone or something over others

Examples

To privilege one group
The new policy tends to privilege urban residents over those in rural areas.
Some hiring practices may unintentionally privilege certain groups of applicants.
The curriculum was designed not to privilege one subject over another.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disadvantage - to put someone or something in a less favorable position
  • synonym: favor - to prefer or give special treatment to someone or something
