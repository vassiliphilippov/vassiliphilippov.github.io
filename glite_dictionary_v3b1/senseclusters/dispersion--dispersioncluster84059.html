dispersion

noun
mixture of particles
6.4
Pronunciation: / dɪˈspɝːʒən / (AmE), / dɪˈspɜːʃən / (BrE)
a mixture in which small particles are distributed throughout another substance

Examples

Milk dispersion
Milk is a classic example of a dispersion where fat droplets are spread in water.
Paint is often a dispersion of pigment particles in a liquid medium.
Scientists studied the stability of the dispersion to improve the product's shelf life.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emulsion - a mixture of two liquids that do not normally mix
  • antonym: solution - a liquid mixture where a substance is dissolved in another
