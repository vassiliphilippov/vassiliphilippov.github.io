freedom from discrimination

noun
protection against unfair treatment
2.3
Pronunciation: / ˈfriːdəm frəm dɪˌskrɪmɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈfriːdəm frəm dɪˌskrɪmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
The right not to be unfairly treated or prejudiced due to race, sex, nationality, religion, age, or other characteristics, often guaranteed by law.

Examples

Freedom from discrimination rights
Many fought for freedom from discrimination during the civil rights movement.
Schools strive to promote freedom from discrimination among students of different backgrounds.
The law ensures freedom from discrimination in the workplace for all employees.

Related Terms

  • antonym: discrimination - unfair or prejudicial treatment of people based on group membership
