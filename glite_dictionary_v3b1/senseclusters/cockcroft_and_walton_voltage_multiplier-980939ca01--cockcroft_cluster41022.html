Cockcroft and Walton voltage multiplier

noun
high-voltage apparatus for particle acceleration
9.7
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑːk.krɑːft ənd ˈwɔːl.tən ˈvoʊl.tɪdʒ ˈmʌl.tɪ.plaɪ.ər/ /; / /ˈkɑːk.krɑːft ənd ˈwɑːl.tən ˈvoʊl.tə.dʒ ˈmʌl.tə.plaɪ.ər/ (AmE), /ˈkɒk.krɒft ænd ˈwɒl.tən ˈvəʊl.tɪdʒ ˈmʌl.tɪ.plaɪ.ə/ /; / /ˈkɒk.krɒft ænd ˈwɔːl.tən ˈvɒl.tɪdʒ ˈmʌl.tə.plaɪ.ə/ (BrE)
an apparatus that produces high voltages by using a series of rectifiers and capacitors to step up voltage, commonly used to accelerate charged particles in physics experiments

Examples

A Cockcroft and Walton voltage multiplier device
Many high-energy physics labs still use a Cockcroft and Walton voltage multiplier for initial voltage boosting.
The Cockcroft and Walton voltage multiplier was instrumental in the early development of particle accelerators.
The design of the Cockcroft and Walton voltage multiplier helped Cockcroft and Walton win the Nobel Prize in Physics.
