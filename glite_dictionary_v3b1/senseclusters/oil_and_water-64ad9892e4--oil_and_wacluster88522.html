oil and water

noun
things or people that do not mix well
Pronunciation: / ɔɪl ənd ˈwɔtɚ / (AmE), / ɔɪl ənd ˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
used to describe people or things that are completely different and do not go together or get along well

Examples

Oil and water separate
Although they tried to work together, their approaches were like oil and water.
His conservative views and her liberal ideals are oil and water in political debates.
In group projects, some personalities mix well, but others are clearly oil and water.
