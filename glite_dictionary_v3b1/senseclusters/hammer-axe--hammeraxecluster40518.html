hammer-axe

noun
weapon with both hammer and axe heads
Pronunciation: / ˈhæmɚ ˌæks / (AmE), / ˈhamə ˌæks / (BrE)
A weapon featuring a hammer on one side of the head and an axe blade on the other, used especially in medieval combat.

Examples

A hammer-axe weapon
Museum displays often include a hammer-axe among other medieval weapons.
The knight carried a hammer-axe into battle for both striking and cleaving attacks.
With a powerful swing, he brought the hammer-axe down on his opponent's shield.

Related Terms

  • synonym: poleaxe - a weapon with an axe blade and a spike, mounted on a long pole
