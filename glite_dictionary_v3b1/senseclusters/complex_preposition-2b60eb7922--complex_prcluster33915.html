complex preposition

noun
multi-word prepositional phrase acting as one unit
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑm.plɛks ˌprɛp.əˈzɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.plɛks ˌprɛp.əˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
a preposition consisting of two or more words that function together as a single prepositional unit, such as 'in spite of' or 'according to'

Examples

A complex preposition phrase
In academic writing, it is important to use the appropriate complex preposition to clarify relationships between ideas.
The teacher explained the difference between a simple preposition and a complex preposition.
‘According to’ and ‘in front of’ are examples of a complex preposition in English.
