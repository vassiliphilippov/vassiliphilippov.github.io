literary agent

noun
person who represents writers to publishers
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪt̬əˌrɛri ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈlɪtərəri ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
a person who represents writers and their written works to publishers and helps negotiate publishing contracts

Examples

Literary agent helps writers
After finishing her novel, she contacted a literary agent to help find a publisher.
His literary agent secured him a lucrative book deal with a major company.
Many authors rely on a literary agent to negotiate favorable contractual terms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agent - someone who acts on behalf of another
