Rubicon

proper noun
ancient river marking Italy-Gaul boundary
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈruː.bɪ.kɔːn/ /; / /ˈruː.bɪ.kən/ /; / /ˈruː.bɪ.kɑːn/ (AmE), /ˈruː.bɪ.kɒn/ /; / /ˈruː.bɪ.kən/ (BrE)
a river in northern Italy which, in ancient times, marked the boundary between Italy and Gaul; famously crossed by Julius Caesar in 49 BC, signifying the start of civil war.

Examples

The Rubicon river
Historians debate the exact location of the ancient Rubicon.
Julius Caesar's crossing of the Rubicon is regarded as a turning point in Roman history.
The phrase 'crossing the Rubicon' refers to taking an irreversible step, just as Caesar did.
