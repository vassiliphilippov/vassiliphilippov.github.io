probit

noun
statistical unit or function
Pronunciation: / ˈproʊbɪt / (AmE), / ˈprəʊbɪt / (BrE)
a unit or function in statistics used to analyze binary or ordinal outcome data, especially in regression models

Examples

A probit model
The scientist applied a probit analysis to determine the effect of the drug on survival rates.
A probit model is often used when the response variable is binary, such as yes or no.
Students learned how to interpret the results of a probit regression in their statistics class.

Related Terms

  • synonym: logit - a function used in statistics for modeling binary outcomes
