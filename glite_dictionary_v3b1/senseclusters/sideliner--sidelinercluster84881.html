sideliner

noun
person with side job
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪdˌlaɪnər / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdˌlaɪnə / (BrE)
a person who has a secondary job or business in addition to their main employment, often to earn extra income

Examples

A sideliner with a shop
As a sideliner, he ran a small online store while working at the bank during the week.
Many sideliners use their extra income to save for vacations or pay off debts.
She became a sideliner by tutoring students in the evenings after her regular office job.
