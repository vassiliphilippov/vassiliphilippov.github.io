march

verb
force someone to walk (military/prisoner sense)
4.0 2.8
Pronunciation: /mɑːrtʃ/ (AmE), /mɑːtʃ/ (BrE)
to force or cause someone to walk somewhere in a steady, organized, or military way

Examples

To march someone out
Police officers marched the protesters out of the building.
The defeated soldiers were marched under heavy guard to the detention center.
The guards marched the prisoners through the snow to the camp.
She marched the children straight to the principal’s office after the incident.
The guards marched the prisoners down the hallway to their cells.
The officer marched the new recruits out to the drill ground early in the morning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drive - to move or urge forward by force
  • synonym: escort - to go with someone to protect or guard them
