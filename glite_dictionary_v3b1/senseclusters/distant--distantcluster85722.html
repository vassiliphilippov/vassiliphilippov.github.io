distant

adjective
far apart in time
2.5
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪstənt / (AmE), / ˈdɪstənt / (BrE)
Far removed in time; belonging to or occurring in the remote past or future.

Examples

Distant past
Historians often study distant periods to understand the roots of modern society.
She has vivid memories of events from her distant childhood.
The possibility of travel to Mars belongs to a distant future.

Related Terms

  • antonym: immediate - happening or done without delay, very near in time
  • synonym: remote - far apart in time or space
