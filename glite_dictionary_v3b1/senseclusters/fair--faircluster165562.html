fair

adjective, adverb
in accordance with rules or laws; without cheating
2.8 1.0
Pronunciation: /fer/ (AmE), /feə/ (BrE)
treating people equally and honestly; in a just or honest way

Examples

Fair play
He prefers to win fair rather than by deception.
The teams competed fair for the championship, respecting all the rules.
They insisted that the election be conducted fair and square.
He wanted to make a fair living by working honestly.
She believed her winnings were fair and deserved after months of hard work.
We all agreed the competition was fair and that nobody cheated.
Employees expect a fair evaluation of their performance each year.
It's important for teachers to be fair when grading all students' work.
The judge made a fair decision based solely on the evidence presented in court.

Related Terms

  • synonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere
  • synonym: impartial - treating all rivals or disputants equally; fair and just
  • synonym: justly - in a morally right or fair way
  • antonym: unfair - not following the rules of justice or equality; not honest or legitimate
  • antonym: unfairly - in a way that is not just or according to the rules; with cheating
