aramid

noun
synthetic fiber
Pronunciation: / ˈærəmɪd / (AmE), / ˈærəmɪd / (BrE)
a strong, heat-resistant synthetic fiber used in protective clothing and materials

Examples

Aramid fiber
The firefighter's jacket is made from aramid to protect against extreme heat.
Manufacturers use aramid fibers in the production of bulletproof vests.
Because of its strength, aramid is also used in aerospace engineering.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Kevlar - a well-known brand of aramid fiber used in bulletproof vests
  • synonym: nylon - a synthetic polymer fiber used in textiles
