howl

verb
emit a long, loud cry
Pronunciation: / haʊl / (AmE), / haʊl / (BrE)
to make a long, loud, emotional cry, especially in pain, grief, anger, or as an animal

Examples

To howl in pain
He howled in pain when he accidentally slammed the door on his finger.
The mourners began to howl with grief at the funeral.
Wolves howl at the moon in the darkness of the night.
At night, the dog would howl when left alone.
Crowds would howl with laughter at his jokes.
The wolves began to howl at the full moon.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sob - to cry noisily, making loud, convulsive gasps
  • synonym: wail - to make a prolonged, high-pitched cry of pain, grief, or anger
  • degree lower: whimper - to make a series of low, weak sounds expressing fear, pain, or unhappiness
  • antonym: whisper - to speak very softly or quietly
  • synonym: yowl - to utter a loud, distressed cry, especially as a cat or dog
