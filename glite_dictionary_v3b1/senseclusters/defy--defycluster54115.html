defy

verb
resist or challenge authority
3.2 3.1
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfaɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈfaɪ / (BrE)
to openly resist, challenge, or refuse to obey someone or something

Examples

To defy the rules
He was admired for his courage to defy the expectations of his peers.
She chose to defy the unjust law, risking punishment for her beliefs.
The monument appears to defy the effects of time and weather.
He looked straight at his teacher and boldly defied her instructions.
She chose to defy the new rules even though it risked punishment.
The group decided to defy the ban and hold the protest anyway.

Related Terms

  • synonym: challenge - to invite someone to contest or compete; to question authority
  • antonym: obey - to follow commands or rules
  • synonym: resist - to oppose or withstand something
  • antonym: yield - to give way or submit under pressure
