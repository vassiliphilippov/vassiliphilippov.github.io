police sergeant

noun
sergeant rank in the police force
Pronunciation: / pəˈlis ˈsɝdʒənt / (AmE), / pəˈliːs ˈsɑːdʒənt / (BrE)
A police officer holding the rank of sergeant, typically responsible for supervising officers or units within a police department.

Examples

A police sergeant supervises officers
A police sergeant must ensure that all officers follow department procedures.
After years of exemplary service, she was promoted to police sergeant.
The police sergeant led the morning briefing before officers began their patrols.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: police inspector - a higher rank than sergeant in many police forces
  • degree lower: police officer - a member of a police force
  • synonym: sergeant - a non-commissioned officer ranking above corporal
