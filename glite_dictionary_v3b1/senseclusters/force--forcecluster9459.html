force

noun
a power or influence affecting events
3.8 59/m
Pronunciation: / f…îrs / (AmE), / f…îÀês / (BrE)
a person, group, or thing that has power or influence and can affect events or people's behavior

Examples

A force for change
Many people believe that a mysterious force guides our destinies.
She was a driving force behind the new community project.
The company became a major force in the technology industry.
Her words carried such force that the audience remained silent and attentive.
The force of his personality inspired those around him to work harder.
The new law came into force at the beginning of the year.

Synonyms

  • influence
    3.4 34/m
    influence is a subtle power that changes how people think or act, while force is stronger and works more directly.
  • power
    3.4 329/m
    power is the formal ability or right to control people or events, while force is often a person or group using pressure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: influence - the capacity to have an effect on the character, development, or behaviour of someone or something
  • synonym: power - the ability or capacity to do something or act in a particular way
