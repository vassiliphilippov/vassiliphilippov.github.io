twittery

adjective
nervously excited
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtwɪtəri / (AmE), / ˈtwɪtəri / (BrE)
feeling or showing nervous excitement, restlessness, or talkativeness

Examples

Twittery before a test
He always gets twittery before important meetings at work.
The children were twittery with excitement as they waited for the show to start.
She felt twittery before giving her first public speech.
She became twittery whenever she was excited to meet new people.
His twittery mood made the whole group laugh and relax.
After hearing the good news, everyone felt a bit twittery and started chatting happily.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - not showing nervousness or excitement
  • synonym: chatty - talkative in a friendly or informal way
  • synonym: giddy - feeling silly, happy, and excited
  • synonym: jittery - feeling nervous or unable to relax
  • antonym: reserved - quiet and not talkative
