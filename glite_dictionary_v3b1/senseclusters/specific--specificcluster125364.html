specific

adjective
particular to a disease, organism, or condition
1.0 5.4
Pronunciation: /spə.ˈsɪ.fɪk/ /; / /spɪ.ˈsɪ.fɪk/ (AmE), /spə.ˈsɪ.fɪk/ /; / /spɪ.ˈsɪ.fɪk/ (BrE)
(of a medicine, remedy, stain, etc.) effective against or concerning only a particular disease, condition, or organism; not general.

Examples

Specific medicine for infection
Doctors prescribed a specific remedy for the infection caused by the rare fungus.
The antibiotic is highly specific and targets only certain bacteria.
This specific dye binds only to the nerve cells on the microscope slide.

Related Terms

  • antonym: general - not specific to a particular cause or organism
  • synonym: selective - affecting some things but not others, particularly in a targeted way
