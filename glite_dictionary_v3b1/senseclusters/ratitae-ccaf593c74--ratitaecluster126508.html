Ratitae

noun
former group of flightless birds
Pronunciation: / ˈræ.təˌti / (AmE), / ˈræt.ɪ.tiː / (BrE)
a former taxonomic group used to include all ratite birds, such as ostriches, emus, and kiwis

Examples

The Ratitae group
Many 19th-century zoologists included ostriches and emus in the group Ratitae.
Modern taxonomy has replaced Ratitae with more precise avian classifications.
The term Ratitae is seldom used in current ornithology, but may appear in historical texts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ratite - any of various large, flightless birds such as ostriches or emus
