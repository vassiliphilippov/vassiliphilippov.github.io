formative

noun
a morpheme or affix in linguistics
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔr.mə.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈfɔː.mə.tɪv / (BrE)
(Linguistics) a word part or affix that carries syntactic or morphological meaning, such as a prefix, suffix, or root.

Examples

A formative suffix
Each formative in the sentence carries grammatical meaning.
Suffixes like "-ing" are common formatives in English verbs.
The prefix "un-" serves as a formative in the word "unhappy".
