heterophile

adjective
reacting with foreign substances
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛtərəˌfaɪl / (AmE), / ˈhɛtərəˌfaɪl / (BrE)
reacting with or attracted to substances that are different or foreign, especially in a biological or immunological context

Examples

Heterophile reaction
A heterophile antibody can react with antigens from different species.
Doctors use tests that detect heterophile antibodies to help diagnose certain infections.
Some heterophile reactions can cause false positives in laboratory results.

Related Terms

  • antonym: homophile - reacting with or attracted to similar substances
