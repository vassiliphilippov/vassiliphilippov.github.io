duffel

noun
coarse woolen fabric
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌfəl / (AmE), / ˈdʌf(ə)l /; / ˈdʌfəl / (BrE)
A coarse, heavy woolen fabric, typically used for coats and blankets.

Examples

Duffel fabric
Early explorers favored duffel for its warmth and durability.
The blanket, woven from grey duffel, covered the old wooden bed.
The coat was made from thick duffel to withstand the cold winters.
Sailors often used duffle blankets during long voyages.
The winter coat was made from thick duffle to keep out the cold.
Traditional duffle provided both warmth and durability in harsh weather.
