soredium

noun
lichen reproductive structure
Pronunciation: / səˈriːdiəm / (AmE), / səˈriːdiəm / (BrE)
a small reproductive structure in lichens, made of algal cells surrounded by fungal filaments, used for dispersal

Examples

A soredium structure
Lichens reproduce by releasing soredia that can grow into new colonies.
Scientists observed the spread of soredia on the bark of old trees.
The presence of soredia helps lichens colonize new environments quickly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: spore - a reproductive cell capable of developing into a new individual
