Confederate

noun
supporter of the Confederate States (Civil War)
Pronunciation: / kənˈfɛdəɹət / (AmE), / kənˈfɛd(ə)ɹət / (BrE)
a supporter of the Confederate States of America, especially during the American Civil War (1861–65)

Examples

Confederate soldier
A monument in the town square honors the memory of Confederate veterans.
During the Civil War, many Confederate soldiers fought bravely for their cause.
He discovered that his great-great-grandfather had been a Confederate during the 1860s.
