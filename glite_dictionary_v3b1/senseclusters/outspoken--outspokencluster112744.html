outspoken

adjective
frank and direct in expression
6.6 3.5
Pronunciation: /aʊt.ˈspoʊ.kən/ (AmE), /aʊt.ˈspəʊ.kən/ (BrE)
expressing opinions or feelings freely and openly, without holding back; frank and direct

Examples

Outspoken about issues
He is known for being outspoken about environmental issues, even when it is unpopular.
Some people admire her for being so outspoken, while others find her bluntness intimidating.
The committee appreciated her outspoken views during the heated debate.
His outspoken comments about company policy surprised many of his colleagues.
She has always been outspoken about her political beliefs, never hesitating to share her views.
The journalist is known for being outspoken, often challenging powerful figures with tough questions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blunt - saying what you think without trying to be polite or considering other people's feelings
  • synonym: frank - open, honest, and direct in speech or expression
  • antonym: reserved - not revealing one's thoughts or feelings readily
  • antonym: reticent - not revealing one's thoughts or feelings readily
  • antonym: tactful - careful not to say or do anything that could upset someone
