China grass

noun
fiber-producing Asian herb (Boehmeria nivea)
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃaɪnə ɡræs / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪnə ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a tall perennial herb (Boehmeria nivea) native to tropical Asia, cultivated for the fiber from its woody stems, which resembles flax

Examples

China grass fiber
China grass fields thrive in the humid climates of Southeast Asia.
The farmers harvested China grass to process it into valuable fiber for textiles.
The strength of China grass makes it useful in making fishing nets and ropes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ramie - the strong fiber from Boehmeria nivea, also called China grass
