heteronomy

noun
being ruled by others
9.9 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / Àåh…õt…ôÀàr…ëÀên…ômi / (AmE), / Àåh…õt…ôÀàr…ín…ômi / (BrE)
the condition of being controlled or governed by external laws, rules, or influences rather than by oneself

Examples

Moral heteronomy
The philosopher contrasted heteronomy with autonomy in his lecture on moral responsibility.
Some argue that strict social rules can lead to heteronomy rather than personal freedom.
In certain societies, heteronomy is seen as necessary to maintain order and tradition.
