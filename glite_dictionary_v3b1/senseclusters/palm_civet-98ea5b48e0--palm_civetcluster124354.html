palm civet

noun
a tree-dwelling civet of Southeast Asia
8.5
Pronunciation: / pɑm ˈsɪvət / (AmE), / pɑːm ˈsɪvɪt / (BrE)
a small, spotted or striped, tree-dwelling civet native to Southeast Asia and the East Indies, known for its cat-like appearance and nocturnal habits

Examples

A palm civet animal
Farmers in Indonesia often spot a palm civet in their plantations at night.
The palm civet is known for its unique ability to climb trees in search of food.
The palm civet produces the coffee beans used in the famous kopi luwak.

Related Terms
