Moreton Bay chestnut

noun
Australian tree with chestnut-like seeds
7.7 8.3
Pronunciation: /ˈmɔːr.tən beɪ ˈtʃes.nʌt/ (AmE), /ˈmɔː.tən beɪ ˈtʃes.nʌt/ (BrE)
An Australian tree (Castanospermum australe) with pinnate leaves and orange-yellow flowers, producing large woody pods containing seeds resembling chestnuts; valued for its dark, durable wood.

Examples

The Moreton Bay chestnut tree
Aboriginal people traditionally used the seeds of the Moreton Bay chestnut after careful preparation to remove toxins.
The Moreton Bay chestnut is popular in Australian parks for its shade and ornamental pods.
The timber from the Moreton Bay chestnut is highly valued for furniture and construction due to its strength.
