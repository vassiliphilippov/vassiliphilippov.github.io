monopod

noun
one-footed mythical being
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑnəˌpɑd / (AmE), / ˈmɒnəˌpɒd / (BrE)
a mythical or legendary creature with a single large foot, often described in medieval stories and maps

Examples

Legendary monopod creature
Ancient maps sometimes depicted a monopod resting in the shade of its own foot.
The legend of the monopod fascinated medieval scholars and travelers alike.
In the story, a monopod used its single foot as a shield against the sun.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cyclops - a mythical giant with a single eye in the center of its forehead
  • synonym: giant - a being of great size and strength in legends and fairy tales
