solid-colored

adjective
of a single, uniform color
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑlɪd ˌkʌlɚd /; / ˈsɑː.lɪd ˌkʌl.ɚd / (AmE), / ˈsɒlɪd ˌkʌləd /; / ˈsɒl.ɪd ˌkʌl.əd / (BrE)
Having a single, uniform color with no pattern or variation.

Examples

A solid-colored shirt
He wore a solid-colored shirt to the interview instead of his usual patterned ones.
She prefers solid-colored dresses because they are easy to accessorize.
The living room was decorated with solid-colored pillows in bright shades of green and blue.
Many people prefer solid-coloured furnishings because they are easier to match with other decor.
She wore a solid-coloured dress to the party, which made her stand out amidst the floral prints.
The designer recommended a solid-coloured background for the company logo to keep it simple and professional.
