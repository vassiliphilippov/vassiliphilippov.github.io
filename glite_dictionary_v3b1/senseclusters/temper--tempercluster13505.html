temper

verb
restrain or moderate
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛmpɚ /; / ˈtɛmpər / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpə / (BrE)
to restrain, moderate, or make something less strong or extreme

Examples

To temper excitement
His enthusiasm was tempered by concern for safety.
She tried to temper her criticism with a few words of praise.
The report suggested new measures to temper the impact of the policy.
His anger was tempered by a sense of fairness.
She tried to temper her criticism with some positive feedback.
The chef will temper the spicy sauce with a touch of cream.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intensify - to make something stronger or more extreme
  • synonym: moderate - to make less extreme or intense
