Neoplatonism

noun
philosophical system blending Platonism and mysticism
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌniː.oʊˈpleɪ.t̬əˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌniː.əʊˈplæt.ə.nɪz.əm / (BrE)
A philosophical and theological system that combines elements of Platonism, Aristotelianism, and Eastern mysticism, centered on the belief in a transcendent, unknowable first principle as the source of all reality.

Examples

Neoplatonism philosophy system
Her research focuses on the role of Neoplatonism in the development of early Christian and Islamic philosophy.
Many Renaissance thinkers were greatly influenced by Neoplatonism and incorporated its ideas into their work.
The teachings of Neoplatonism emphasize the existence of a transcendent reality beyond the material world.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: Aristotelianism - the philosophy of Aristotle
  • synonym: Platonism - the philosophy or doctrines of Plato
  • synonym: mysticism - belief in the direct experience of the divine
