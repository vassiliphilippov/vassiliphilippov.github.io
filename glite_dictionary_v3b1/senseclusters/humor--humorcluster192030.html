humor

noun
mood; temporary state of mind
2.1 1.87/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàhjuÀêm…ôr /; / ÀàhjuÀêm…ö / (AmE), / ÀàhjuÀêm…ô / (BrE)
a temporary state of mind or feeling; mood (especially in phrases such as 'in a good/bad humor')

Examples

In a good humor
After the meeting, she was clearly in a bad humor and avoided conversation.
He left the party early because he wasn't in the right humor for company.
Her cheerful humor made everyone around her feel at ease.
Depending on his humour, he could be charming or irritable.
He was in no humour to listen to advice after the argument.
Her humour changed suddenly from cheerful to sullen.

Synonyms

  • mood
    1.0 22/m
    mood shows a person's current feelings in behavior or expression, while humor appears in fixed phrases meaning someone's temporary mood.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mood - a temporary state of mind or feeling
  • synonym: temper - a person's state of mind seen in terms of being angry or calm
