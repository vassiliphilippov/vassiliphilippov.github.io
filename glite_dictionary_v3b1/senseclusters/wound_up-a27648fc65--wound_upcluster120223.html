wound up

adjective
tense or nervous; highly excited
5.9
Pronunciation: / waʊnd ʌp / (AmE), / waʊnd ʌp / (BrE)
in a state of great tension, nervousness, or excitement

Examples

Very wound up person
He was so wound up that he couldn't sleep all night.
I'm feeling wound up after that stressful meeting.
She always gets wound up before an important exam.

Related Terms

  • antonym: relaxed - free from tension or anxiety
  • synonym: tense - showing nervous tension
