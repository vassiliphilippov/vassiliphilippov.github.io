Fallot's syndrome

noun
congenital heart defect causing cyanosis
8.1
Pronunciation: / fəˈloʊz ˌsɪnˈdroʊm / (AmE), / fəˈlɒz ˈsɪn.drəʊm / (BrE)
A congenital heart disease characterized by four anatomical abnormalities causing poor oxygenation of blood, resulting in cyanosis.

Examples

Fallot's syndrome heart disease
Children with Fallot's syndrome often appear blue due to reduced oxygen in the blood.
Doctors explained that surgery could correct the heart defects associated with Fallot's syndrome.
The child was diagnosed with Fallot's syndrome shortly after birth, requiring immediate medical attention.
