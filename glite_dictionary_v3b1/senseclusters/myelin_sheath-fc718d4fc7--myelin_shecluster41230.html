myelin sheath

noun
insulating layer around some nerve fibers
6.8 7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪəlɪn ʃiθ / (AmE), / ˈmaɪəlɪn ʃiːθ / (BrE)
a layer of fatty substance (myelin) that surrounds and insulates the axons of certain nerve fibers, aiding in the rapid transmission of nerve impulses

Examples

The myelin sheath layer
Damage to the myelin sheath can result in neurological disorders such as multiple sclerosis.
Scientists are studying how to repair the myelin sheath to treat nerve injuries.
The myelin sheath helps speed up the transmission of nerve signals in the brain.

  • synonym: neuron - a nerve cell that transmits nerve impulses
