trachea

noun
air tube in insects/arachnids
Pronunciation: / ˈtreɪkiə / (AmE), / ˈtreɪkɪə / (BrE)
each of the small tubes forming part of the respiratory system of insects and many arachnids, through which air passes to the tissues

Examples

Insect trachea
Oxygen enters an insect's body through openings connected to the trachea.
Scientists studied how dust particles move through the trachea of a beetle.
The trachea in arachnids delivers air directly to their internal organs.
