aerophilic

adjective
requiring free oxygen or air
6.2 9.5
Pronunciation: / ˌɛroʊˈfɪlɪk / (AmE), / ˌeərəʊˈfɪlɪk / (BrE)
(of an organism or process) requiring free oxygen or air in order to live, grow, or function.

Examples

Aerophilic microorganisms
Modern composting methods use aerophilic microorganisms to speed up decomposition.
Some fermentation processes are strictly aerophilic, needing constant oxygen flow.
The bacteria are aerophilic and thrive only when exposed to air.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anaerobic - not requiring oxygen or air
