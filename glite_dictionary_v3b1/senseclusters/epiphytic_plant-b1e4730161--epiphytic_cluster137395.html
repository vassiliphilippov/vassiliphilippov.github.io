epiphytic plant

noun
plant growing on another plant (not parasitic)
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˈfɪtɪk plænt / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈfɪtɪk plɑːnt / (BrE)
A plant that grows on another plant for physical support but is not parasitic, obtaining moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Examples

An epiphytic plant grows
Because of its unique adaptations, an epiphytic plant can absorb water directly from humid air.
Many ferns are epiphytic plants, living on branches without harming their supporting trees.
The orchid is a classic example of an epiphytic plant, thriving high in the rainforest canopy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: epiphyte - a plant that grows non-parasitically on another plant
  • antonym: parasite - an organism that lives on or in another and benefits by deriving nutrients at the host's expense
