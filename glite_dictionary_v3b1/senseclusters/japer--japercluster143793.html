japer

noun
person who jokes
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒeɪpər / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪpə / (BrE)
a person who jokes, mocks, or entertains others in a playful or mischievous way

Examples

A japer tells jokes

At school, she was known as a japer who could lighten any mood.
Although he was a skilled japer, he always made sure his jokes were kind.
The office japer always finds new ways to make his colleagues laugh.
At school, the japer was known for harmless pranks that cheered everyone up.
No one was surprised when the japer organized a surprise birthday party with silly games.
The japer delighted the audience with clever jokes and playful tricks during the festival.
In old stories, a japer often wore colorful clothes and made the king laugh.
No one could match the wit of the japer at the medieval banquet.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fool - a historical entertainer who amused people by acting silly or making jokes
  • synonym: jester - a professional entertainer who amuses by jokes and antics, especially at a royal court
  • synonym: joker - a person who tells jokes or acts playfully
  • synonym: prankster - someone who plays practical jokes on others
  • antonym: serious person - someone who does not joke or make fun of others
  • antonym: spoilsport - a person who spoils the fun of others
  • synonym: teaser - someone who makes fun of others in a playful way
