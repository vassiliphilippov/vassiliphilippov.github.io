drachm

noun
apothecary weight unit (1/8 ounce, 60 grains)
Pronunciation: / dræm / (AmE), / dræm / (BrE)
a small unit of weight, used in the apothecary system or in ancient Greece

Examples

A drachm of gold
A gold coin weighing a drachm was once standard in ancient Greece.
Old recipes sometimes call for a drachm of spice in their ingredient lists.
The apothecary prescribed two drachms of the powdered herb for each dose.
The merchant weighed the spices in drachms before selling them at the market.
In ancient Greece, a drachm could be used to buy a loaf of bread.
Archaeologists discovered a silver drachm among the ruins of the old city.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dram - a unit of weight, also called drachm, commonly used in Apothecaries' systems
  • degree higher: ounce - a unit of weight equal to one-sixteenth of a pound
