rudiments

noun
basic principles of a subject
5.1 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàruÀêd…ôÀåm…õnts / (AmE), / ÀàruÀêd…™m…ônts / (BrE)
the basic principles or elements of a subject or skill; the foundations upon which more complex ideas are built

Examples

The rudiments of learning
Children begin by learning the rudiments of reading and writing in school.
Even after a year of lessons, she only knew the rudiments of the piano.
He tried to explain the rudiments of chess to his nephew.
Before advanced mathematics, you must first learn the rudiments of algebra and geometry.
She taught her students the rudiments of music theory on their first day.
The guide outlined the rudiments of survival in the wilderness.

Synonyms

  • fundamentals
    3.0 0.01/m

  • rudiment
    4.3 0.63/m

  • basics
    1.0 0.93/m

  • first principles
    5.8 0.98/m
