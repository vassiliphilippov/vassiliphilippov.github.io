projective technique

noun
psychological test using ambiguous stimuli
Pronunciation: / prəˈdʒɛktɪv tɛkˈniːk / (AmE), / prəˈdʒɛktɪv tɛkˈniːk / (BrE)
a method in psychology in which a person is presented with ambiguous stimuli and asked to interpret or respond to them, revealing unconscious aspects of their personality

Examples

A projective technique test
In their research, the team employed a projective technique to assess the children's hidden anxieties.
The Rorschach inkblot test is a classic example of a projective technique in psychology.
Therapists sometimes use a projective technique to explore a patient's unconscious feelings and thoughts.
