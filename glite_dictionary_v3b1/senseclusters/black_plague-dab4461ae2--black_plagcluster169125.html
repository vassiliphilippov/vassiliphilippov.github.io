Black Plague

noun
devastating medieval epidemic of bubonic plague
6.4
Pronunciation: / blæk pleɪɡ / (AmE), / blæk pleɪɡ / (BrE)
the epidemic form of bubonic plague that devastated Europe in the Middle Ages, killing millions of people

Examples

The Black Plague epidemic
Art and literature from the period often reflect the horror inspired by the Black Plague.
Historians estimate that the Black Plague killed nearly half of western Europe's people.
The Black Plague devastated European populations in the 14th century.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Black Death - another common term for the same epidemic
  • synonym: bubonic plague - the specific disease caused by Yersinia pestis bacteria
