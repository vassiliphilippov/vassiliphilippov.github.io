Boethian

noun
follower of Boethius
9.9
Pronunciation: / boʊˈiːθiən / (AmE), / bəʊˈiːθiən / (BrE)
a follower or scholar of Boethius or someone who studies his philosophical writings

Examples

A Boethian scholar
The medieval scholar was known as a Boethian for his deep study of ancient philosophy.
Many Boethians contributed to the preservation of classical knowledge during the Middle Ages.
As a Boethian, she often referenced The Consolation of Philosophy in her lectures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Aristotelian - a follower or scholar of Aristotle's philosophy
  • synonym: Platonist - a follower or scholar of Plato's philosophy
