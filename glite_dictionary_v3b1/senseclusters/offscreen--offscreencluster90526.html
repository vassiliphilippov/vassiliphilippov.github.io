offscreen

adjective, adverb
not shown on screen
Pronunciation: / ˌɔfˈskrin / (AmE), / ˌɒfˈskriːn / (BrE)
not visible on the screen; happening or located outside the area shown by a camera or display (in film, TV, etc.)

Examples

Offscreen drama
The main character's actions offscreen had a big impact on the story.
Many important events occur offscreen and are only mentioned by other characters.
The director chose to keep the villain offscreen to build suspense.
The villain was defeated offscreen, so the audience never saw the final battle.
Important events sometimes occur offscreen to create mystery in a story.
She heard laughter offscreen while the main characters were talking.

Related Terms

  • antonym: onscreen - visible on the screen or display
