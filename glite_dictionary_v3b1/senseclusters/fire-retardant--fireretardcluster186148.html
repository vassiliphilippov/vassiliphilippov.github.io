(of a substance or material) difficult to burn or slow to catch fire, often specially treated to resist fire.

Examples

Fire-retardant material
All children's sleepwear must be made from fire-retardant materials to ensure safety.
The curtains in the theater are fire-retardant to reduce the risk of fire spreading.
They applied a fire-retardant coating to the wooden beams during construction.

Synonyms

  • flame-retardant
    4.9 0.78/m
    flame-retardant is treated to slow or stop flames in fabrics, while fire-retardant means a material made hard to burn.
  • fire-resistant
    4.1 0.20/m
    fire-resistant keeps its shape or function in high heat, while fire-retardant is treated to burn more slowly.
  • flameproof
    3.9 0.03/m
    flameproof does not catch fire easily and sounds very strong, while fire-retardant is treated to slow burning.
