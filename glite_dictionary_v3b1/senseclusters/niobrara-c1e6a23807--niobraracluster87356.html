Niobrara

proper noun
tributary of the Missouri River in the U.S.
Pronunciation: / ˌnaɪ.oʊˈbrær.ə / (AmE), / ˌnaɪ.əʊˈbrær.ə / (BrE)
A river in the central United States, a tributary of the Missouri River, flowing through Wyoming and Nebraska.

Examples

The Niobrara River
Historic settlements grew up along the Niobrara due to its access to water and fertile land.
Many canoeists travel down the Niobrara each summer to enjoy its natural beauty.
The Niobrara River is famous for its scenic bluffs and waterfalls in Nebraska.
