don't be a cow

x

6.3
Pronunciation: / doʊnt bi ə kaʊ / (AmE), / dəʊnt biː ə kaʊ / (BrE)
(informal, chiefly British) Used to tell someone not to be unreasonably difficult, unkind, or stubborn.

Examples

Please don't be a cow
Don't be a cow, just accept the changes.
If you want to join us, fine, but don't be a cow about the arrangements.
She told him, 'don't be a cow', when he refused to share the remote.

Related Terms

  • synonym: don't be a jerk - used to tell someone not to behave badly or irritatingly
  • synonym: don't be difficult - used to tell someone not to make things unnecessarily hard
