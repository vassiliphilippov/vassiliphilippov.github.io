trade

verb
buy and sell goods or services
2.3 19/m
Pronunciation: / treÉªd / (AmE), / treÉªd / (BrE)
to buy and sell goods or services as a business, including on financial markets

Examples

To trade stocks
He built his fortune by trading in textiles.
Many companies trade internationally to expand their markets.
She traded antiques for more than twenty years.
Gold traded above $1,900 an ounce during the session.
The company's shares traded at their highest point this year.
The new bond traded actively in early morning markets.
Many farmers trade their produce directly with local stores.
The company trades oil and natural gas internationally.
They trade in antiques at the weekend market.

Synonyms

  • transact
    5.1 0.65/m
    transact is used for formal financial or official business actions, while trade means buying and selling goods or services as a business.
  • deal
    3.0 13/m
    deal often means making a specific or informal transaction, sometimes illegal, while trade means buying and selling as an ongoing business activity.
