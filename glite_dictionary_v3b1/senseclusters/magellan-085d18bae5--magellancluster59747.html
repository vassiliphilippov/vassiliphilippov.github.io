Magellan

proper noun
Portuguese navigator, led first circumnavigation
Pronunciation: / məˈdʒɛlən / (AmE), / məˈdʒɛlən / (BrE)
Ferdinand Magellan (1480–1521), Portuguese navigator who led the first expedition to circumnavigate the globe while in the service of Spain.

Examples

Magellan expedition
Magellan's expedition faced incredible hardships as they sailed around the globe.
Many historians credit Magellan with proving that the Earth is round through his famous voyage.
The strait at the southern tip of South America is named after Magellan.
