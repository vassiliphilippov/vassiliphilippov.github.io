RIC

noun
U.S. investment fund
9.4
Pronunciation: /rɪk/ /; / /ɑːr aɪ siː/ (AmE), /rɪk/ /; / /ɑː aɪ siː/ (BrE)
a regulated investment company in the U.S. that meets tax requirements allowing it to pass income directly to shareholders

Examples

RIC mutual fund
The mutual fund registered as a RIC to receive favorable tax treatment for its shareholders.
Investors often choose a RIC because it allows income to be distributed without double taxation.
To qualify as a RIC, the company must meet specific IRS requirements each year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ETF - an exchange-traded fund, a type of investment fund traded on stock exchanges
  • synonym: mutual fund - an investment fund that pools money from many investors
