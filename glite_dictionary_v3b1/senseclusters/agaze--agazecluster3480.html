agaze

adjective
with wide, fixed eyes
5.7
Pronunciation: / əˈɡeɪz / (AmE), / əˈɡeɪz / (BrE)
(of eyes) open wide and fixed, often in fear, wonder, or astonishment.

Examples

Eyes agaze with shock
Her face was pale, her eyes agaze with shock as she heard the news.
The child stood agaze at the fireworks display, mesmerized by the colors.
They found him agaze, staring into the darkness, lost in thought.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agape - (of mouth or eyes) wide open, especially with surprise or wonder
  • synonym: staring - looking with wide open eyes; gaze fixed intently
