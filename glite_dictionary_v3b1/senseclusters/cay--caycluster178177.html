cay

noun
small sandy island on a coral reef
Pronunciation: / keɪ / (AmE), / keɪ / (BrE)
a small, low-elevation, sandy island formed on the surface of a coral reef, typically found in tropical regions

Examples

A small cay
Many species of seabirds nest on the remote sandy cays in the Caribbean.
The sailors discovered a tiny cay covered with palm trees off the coast.
We anchored our boat beside a beautiful cay with clear turquoise water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: islet - a very small island
  • degree lower: reef - a ridge of rock or coral near the surface of the sea
