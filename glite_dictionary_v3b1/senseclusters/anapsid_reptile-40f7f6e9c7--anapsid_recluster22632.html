anapsid reptile

noun
primitive reptile with solid skull structure
5.3
Pronunciation: / əˈnæp.sɪd ˈrɛp.taɪl / (AmE), / əˈnap.sɪd ˈrɛp.taɪl / (BrE)
a primitive reptile with no opening in the temporal region of the skull, now represented only by turtles

Examples

The anapsid reptile turtle
Fossil evidence suggests that anapsid reptiles flourished during the Permian period.
The solid skull structure is a key characteristic of all anapsid reptiles.
Turtles are the only surviving anapsid reptiles in the modern world.

Related Terms

  • antonym: synapsid - reptile with a single skull opening (mammal ancestors)
