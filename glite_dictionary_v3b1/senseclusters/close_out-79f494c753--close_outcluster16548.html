close out

verb
sell off remaining stock; end an operation
3.4 3.5
Pronunciation: /kloʊz aʊt/ /; / /kloʊs aʊt/ (AmE), /kləʊz aʊt/ /; / /kləʊs aʊt/ (BrE)
To terminate by selling off all remaining stock or assets, often at reduced prices, or to bring an operation or account to an end.

Examples

To close out a store
After 30 years, the store will close out its operations at the end of the month.
The company decided to close out its remaining merchandise at huge discounts.
They had to close out the warehouse inventory before relocating to a new facility.

Related Terms

  • synonym: liquidate - to sell assets to settle a debt or end a business
  • synonym: wind up - to bring a business to an end
