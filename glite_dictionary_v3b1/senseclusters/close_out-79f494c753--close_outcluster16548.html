Synonyms

  • sell off
    3.5 3/m
    sell off means selling goods quickly at reduced prices to clear stock, while close out means ending operations and selling remaining inventory.
  • liquidate
    7.3 1/m
    liquidate means selling assets to pay debts, often in formal or bankruptcy cases, while close out means ending an operation.
  • wind up
    3.7 0.8/m
    wind up means formally finishing affairs and distributing assets at the end of a business, while close out focuses on selling remaining stock.
  • close down
    2.9 4/m
    close down means permanently stop operating a business, often without noting stock sales, while close out stresses selling leftover stock.
  • close up shop
    3.9 0.5/m
    close up shop is an informal way to stop doing business, sometimes temporarily, while close out implies selling off to finish operations.
  • close
    1.0 80/m
    close means to bring something to an official end like a meeting or deal, while close out involves selling remaining stock to finish a business.
