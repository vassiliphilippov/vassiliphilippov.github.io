widdershins

adverb
counterclockwise direction
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪdərˌʃɪnz / (AmE), / ˈwɪdəʃɪnz / (BrE)
in a direction opposite to the usual, especially counterclockwise or against the direction of the sun

Examples

Move widdershins around
The dancers moved widdershins around the bonfire as part of the old ritual.
Superstitious people believed that walking widdershins around a church would bring bad luck.
The children ran widdershins around the playground, laughing at their own rebellion.
The children ran widdershins around the old oak tree during the festival.
According to tradition, walking widdershins around the church was considered unlucky.
The parade moved widdershins through the village square, surprising many onlookers.
