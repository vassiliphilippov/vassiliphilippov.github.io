Easter

noun
Christian festival celebrating the resurrection of Jesus
1.0 0.33/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàiÀêst…ö / (AmE), / ÀàiÀêst…ô / (BrE)
The most important Christian festival, celebrating the resurrection of Jesus Christ, observed on the Sunday following the first full moon after the vernal equinox.

Examples

The Easter festival
Children often hunt for eggs as part of traditional Easter festivities.
Churches hold sunrise services on Easter to mark the resurrection of Christ.
Many families gather for a special meal on Easter Sunday to celebrate together.

Synonyms

  • Easter Sunday
    1.0 0.76/m
    Easter Sunday is the single Sunday when Christians celebrate the resurrection, while Easter refers to the whole festival and season.
  • Easter Day
    1.0 0.10/m
    Easter Day is the specific calendar day many churches mark for celebration, while Easter names the wider festival and season.
  • Pascha
    9.6 0.01/m
    Pascha is the name used in Eastern churches for the celebration, while Easter is the common English term for the same festival.
  • Pasch
    9.9 0.01/m
    Pasch is an older term sometimes confused with Passover, whereas Easter is the modern English name for the Christian festival.
