myositis trichinosa

noun
infestation of muscles by trichina larvae
9.9
Pronunciation: /maɪ.oʊ.ˈsaɪ.tɪs trɪ.kɪ.ˈnoʊ.sə/ /; / /maɪ.ə.ˈsaɪ.tɪs trɪ.kɪ.ˈnoʊ.sə/ (AmE), /maɪ.ə.ˈsaɪ.tɪs trɪ.kɪ.ˈnəʊ.sə/ /; / /maɪ.oʊ.ˈsaɪ.tɪs trɪ.kɪ.ˈnəʊ.sə/ (BrE)
Infestation by trichina larvae, typically caused by eating undercooked meat, especially pork; the larvae migrate from the intestine to muscles and become encysted.

Examples

Myositis trichinosa infection
Doctors diagnosed the patient with myositis trichinosa after discovering larvae in his muscle tissue.
Improperly cooked pork can lead to myositis trichinosa in infected individuals.
Symptoms of myositis trichinosa include muscle pain and fever after eating contaminated meat.

Related Terms

  • synonym: trichinosis - disease caused by eating undercooked meat containing Trichinella larvae
