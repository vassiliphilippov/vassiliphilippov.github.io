wood spirit

noun
methanol; volatile, poisonous liquid alcohol
1.1
Pronunciation: / wʊd ˈspɪrɪt / (AmE), / wʊd ˈspɪrɪt / (BrE)
a light, volatile, flammable, and poisonous liquid alcohol (methanol), historically obtained by distilling wood, used as a solvent, antifreeze, fuel, and denaturant for ethyl alcohol

Examples

Wood spirit fuel
Engineers sometimes use wood spirit as a fuel in specialized internal combustion engines.
It is important to handle wood spirit with care due to its toxic properties.
Old scientific texts often refer to wood spirit as an essential solvent for laboratory procedures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: methanol - a colorless volatile poisonous liquid alcohol, the simplest alcohol
