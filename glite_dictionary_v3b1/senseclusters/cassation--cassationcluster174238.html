cassation

noun
annulment of legal decision
Pronunciation: / kæˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / kæˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of annulling or overturning a legal decision, especially by a higher court

Examples

Cassation of a ruling
The Supreme Court's cassation of the previous ruling set a new legal precedent.
After the cassation of the contract, both parties had to renegotiate the terms.
The lawyer filed for cassation to challenge the lower court's decision.

Related Terms

  • synonym: annulment - the act of declaring something invalid
  • synonym: reversal - the act of changing a decision to its opposite
  • antonym: upholding - the act of confirming or supporting a decision
