mania

noun
state of extreme mood or excitement
6.8 6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈmeɪ.niː.ə/ /; / /ˈmeɪ.n.jə/ (AmE), /ˈmeɪ.niː.ə/ /; / /ˈmeɪ.n.jə/ (BrE)
A mental disorder marked by periods of great excitement, euphoria, delusions, and overactivity.

Examples

Bipolar mania
During a period of mania, he barely slept and spent money recklessly.
Her doctor diagnosed her with bipolar disorder after observing symptoms of both mania and depression.
The patient's sudden burst of energy was a clear sign of mania.

Related Terms

  • antonym: depression - a mental state characterized by persistent sadness; opposite of mania in mood disorders
  • degree lower: hypomania - a mild form of mania, less severe symptoms
