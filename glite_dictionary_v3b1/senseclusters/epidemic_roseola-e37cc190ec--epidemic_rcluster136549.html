epidemic roseola

noun
mild viral disease, similar to measles
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˈdɛmɪk roʊˈziːələ / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈdɛmɪk rəʊˈziːələ / (BrE)
A contagious viral disease that is a milder form of measles, lasting three or four days, and can be harmful to a fetus during the first trimester.

Examples

The epidemic roseola virus
Although epidemic roseola is generally mild, it can be dangerous for unborn babies.
Pregnant women are advised to avoid exposure to epidemic roseola, especially in their first trimester.
The doctor diagnosed the child with epidemic roseola after observing the rash and mild fever.

Related Terms

  • synonym: measles - a highly contagious viral disease marked by red spots
  • synonym: rubella - a mild viral infection similar to measles, also known as German measles
