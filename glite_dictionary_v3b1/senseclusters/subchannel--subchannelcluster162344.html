subchannel

noun
secondary broadcast channel
6.6
Pronunciation: /səb.ˈtʃæn.əl/ /; / /ˈsʌb.tʃæn.əl/ (AmE), /səb.ˈtʃæn.əl/ /; / /ˈsʌb.tʃæn.əl/ (BrE)
a secondary or subsidiary channel within a main channel, especially in broadcasting or digital media

Examples

TV subchannel
The sports event was broadcast on a dedicated subchannel to avoid interrupting regular programming.
Each subchannel on the radio station offers a different genre of music.
You can watch local news on the main channel and weather updates on the subchannel.
The new digital receiver can access multiple subchannels from the same television station.
Each subchannel offers different types of content, such as news, sports, or movies.
Radio stations sometimes use a subchannel to broadcast special programs or music genres.
