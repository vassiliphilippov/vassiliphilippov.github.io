autolyze

verb
to self-digest cells
Pronunciation: / ˈɔtəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈɔːtəˌlaɪz / (BrE)
to undergo or cause the breakdown of cells or tissues by self-produced enzymes

Examples

Cells autolyze after death
After the cells die, they begin to autolyze due to their own enzymes.
Scientists observed that the tissue would autolyze if not preserved quickly.
During bread making, yeast cells can autolyze and release nutrients into the dough.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decompose - to break down into simpler substances
  • antonym: preserve - to keep something from decaying or being destroyed
