fain

adverb
(archaic) gladly; with pleasure
9.6
Pronunciation: / feɪn / (AmE), / feɪn / (BrE)
(archaic) Gladly; with pleasure or eagerness.

Examples

Gladly fain
He fain would help, were he able to do so.
In those days, people fain believed in the promise of adventure.
She would fain travel the world if given the chance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gladly - with pleasure; willingly
  • antonym: reluctantly - unwillingly; with hesitation or unwillingness
