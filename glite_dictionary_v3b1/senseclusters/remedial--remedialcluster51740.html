remedial

adjective
intended to rectify or improve
Pronunciation: / rɪˈmidiəl / (AmE), / rɪˈmiːdiəl / (BrE)
Intended to correct or improve something, especially skills or knowledge that are below the expected level.

Examples

Remedial reading class
She attended a remedial reading program to improve her literacy skills.
The school offers remedial classes in mathematics for students who are struggling.
There are special remedial measures in place to help employees meet company standards.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corrective - intended to correct or improve faults or deficiencies
