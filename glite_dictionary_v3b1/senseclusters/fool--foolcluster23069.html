fool

noun
professional entertainer for royals
5.4
Pronunciation: / fuːl / (AmE), / fuːl / (BrE)
(historical) A professional clown or entertainer employed to amuse a king or nobleman, especially in the Middle Ages.

Examples

A royal fool
During the banquet, the fool performed a hilarious routine that delighted the entire court.
In Shakespeare's plays, the fool often possesses surprising wisdom despite his comic role.
The king's fool made everyone laugh with his witty jokes and playful antics.
