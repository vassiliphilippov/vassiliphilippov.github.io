dusky

adjective
dimly lit as by twilight
4.7 0.8/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆdÊŒski / (AmE), / ËˆdÊŒski / (BrE)
Lit or appearing as if illuminated by twilight; dim or shadowy in a way reminiscent of dusk.

Examples

Dusky evening sky
A dusky glow lingered on the horizon long after the sun had set.
Mist hovered over the lake in the dusky stillness of early evening.
They walked through the dusky streets just as the city lights began to appear.

Synonyms

  • twilit
    8.4 0.03/m
    twilit shows a gentle, evening light on objects, while dusky often paints scenes with a deeper, dusk-like shadow.
  • crepuscular
    9.9 0.10/m
    crepuscular is a formal word for twilight-like light, while dusky is more common and used in everyday speech.
  • shadowy
    3.3 2/m
    shadowy is dark and covered in shade from any light source, while dusky refers specifically to twilight's soft, dim glow.
