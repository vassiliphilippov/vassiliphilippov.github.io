in deep thought

adjective
thinking seriously about something
1.0 3.5
Pronunciation: /ɪn diːp θɔːt/ /; / /ɪn diːp θɑːt/ (AmE), /ɪn diːp θɔːt/ (BrE)
concentrating intensely and seriously on something in one's mind; engaged in serious or reflective thinking.

Examples

Be in deep thought
He was in deep thought when I entered the room, so I didn't disturb him.
She sat in deep thought for several minutes, unaware of her surroundings.
Walking home, Maria found herself in deep thought about the day's events.

Related Terms

  • synonym: preoccupied - thinking so much about something that one overlooks other things
