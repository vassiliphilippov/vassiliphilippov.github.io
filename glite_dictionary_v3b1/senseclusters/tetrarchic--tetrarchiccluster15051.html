tetrarchic

adjective
of four rulers
Pronunciation: / tɛˈtrɑrkɪk / (AmE), / tɛˈtrɑːkɪk / (BrE)
relating to a tetrarch or to a system of government ruled by four leaders

Examples

The tetrarchic system
The tetrarchic system divided the Roman Empire among four co-rulers to improve stability.
Historians often debate the effectiveness of tetrarchic governance in ancient times.
A tetrarchic arrangement can help prevent the concentration of power in one individual.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monarchic - relating to a government ruled by a single person
  • synonym: oligarchic - relating to a government ruled by a small group
