papillary tumor

noun
benign epithelial tumor forming a rounded mass
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈpæpɪˌleri ˈtuːmər /; / ˈpæpəˌlɛri ˈtumər / (AmE), / ˈpapɪləri ˈtjuːmə /; / ˈpapɪləɹi ˈtjuːmə / (BrE)
A benign growth of epithelial tissue forming a rounded mass resembling a small, nipple-like projection.

Examples

A papillary tumor growth
A papillary tumor is typically benign but may require monitoring for changes.
The biopsy revealed that the mass was actually a papillary tumor rather than a malignant growth.
The surgeon removed a papillary tumor from the patient's bladder.
A papillary tumour typically appears as a rounded mass under microscopic examination.
The doctor diagnosed her with a papillary tumour after reviewing the biopsy results.
Treatment for a papillary tumour often depends on its size and location.
