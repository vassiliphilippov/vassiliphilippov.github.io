Tourette syndrome

noun
neurological disorder with tics
4.9
Pronunciation: / tuˈrɛt ˌsɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / tuˈrɛt ˌsɪndrəʊm / (BrE)
a neurological disorder characterized by involuntary movements and vocal sounds called tics

Examples

Tourette syndrome symptoms
The doctor explained that Tourette syndrome often begins in childhood and can improve with age.
People with Tourette syndrome may experience both motor and vocal tics.
Raising awareness about Tourette syndrome helps reduce misunderstanding and stigma.
