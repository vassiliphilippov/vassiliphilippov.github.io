thin-leaved stringybark

noun
Australian eucalyptus with white wood and stringy bark
Pronunciation: / θɪn livd ˈstrɪŋiˌbɑrk / (AmE), / θɪn liːvd ˈstrɪŋɡiˌbɑːk / (BrE)
a type of Australian eucalyptus tree with thin leaves and white wood, known for its stringy bark

Examples

The thin-leaved stringybark tree
The thin-leaved stringybark is commonly found in the forests of eastern Australia.
We spotted several thin-leaved stringybarks growing along the ridgeline during our hike.
Wood from the thin-leaved stringybark is valued for its durability and pale color.
