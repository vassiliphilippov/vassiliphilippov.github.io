transmissibility

noun
ability to transmit water
8.0
Pronunciation: / trænzˌmɪsəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / trænzˌmɪsəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the ability of a material, such as rock or soil, to allow water to pass through it, usually measured by the rate of flow under a hydraulic gradient

Examples

Transmissibility of soil
Engineers measured the transmissibility of the soil to determine if it was suitable for the new well.
High transmissibility in the aquifer means water can move quickly through the underground layers.
The project's success depended on the transmissibility of the rock beneath the construction site.

Related Terms

  • antonym: impermeability - the quality of not allowing liquids or gases to pass through
  • synonym: permeability - the quality of a material that allows liquids or gases to pass through it
