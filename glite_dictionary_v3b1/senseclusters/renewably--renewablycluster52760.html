renewably

adverb
in a renewable way
4.2
Pronunciation: / rɪˈnuːəbli / (AmE), / rɪˈnjuːəbli / (BrE)
in a manner that allows something, such as a contract or license, to be renewed or extended repeatedly.

Examples

Renewably extended contract
The magazine subscription is offered renewably, so you can continue it each year without interruption.
Some work visas are granted renewably, allowing employees to stay longer if needed.
The parking permit is issued renewably every six months, making it convenient for long-term residents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: periodically - at regular intervals of time
  • antonym: permanently - in a way that lasts forever without change
