illuminism

noun
Enlightenment philosophy
Pronunciation: / ɪˈluːmɪˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪˈluːmɪˌnɪzəm / (BrE)
a movement or belief system that emphasizes reason, scientific inquiry, and secular values, especially as seen in the Enlightenment period

Examples

Enlightenment illuminism
The spread of illuminism in Europe led to major advances in science and philosophy.
Many political reforms in the 18th century were inspired by the ideals of illuminism.
Historians often debate the long-term effects of illuminism on modern society.

Related Terms

  • synonym: enlightenment - an intellectual movement emphasizing reason and individualism in the 18th century
  • synonym: rationalism - the belief that reason is the chief source of knowledge
  • antonym: superstition - a belief or practice resulting from ignorance or fear of the unknown
