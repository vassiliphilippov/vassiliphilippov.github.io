radiosensitivity

noun
susceptibility to radiant energy
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪdioʊˌsɛnsəˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəʊˌsɛnsəˈtɪvəti / (BrE)
The degree to which cells, tissues, or organisms are susceptible to damage by radiant energy, such as X-rays or gamma rays.

Examples

The radiosensitivity of cells
Scientists are studying the radiosensitivity of different bacteria to improve sterilization methods.
Some cancer cells have a high radiosensitivity, making radiation therapy more effective against them.
The radiosensitivity of tissues can vary depending on the cell type and stage of development.

Related Terms

  • antonym: radioresistance - the ability to withstand the effects of radiation
