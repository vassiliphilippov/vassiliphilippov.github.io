antievolutionary

adjective
opposing evolutionary change
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˌæntiˌɛvəˈluːʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˌæntiˌiːvəˈluːʃənəri / (BrE)
acting to prevent or reduce evolutionary change in a population or species, favoring the stability of existing traits

Examples

Antievolutionary traits
The species exhibited antievolutionary traits that helped preserve its genetic makeup over centuries.
Some scientists argue that certain environmental pressures can have an antievolutionary effect on populations.
The antievolutionary forces in the ecosystem resisted the introduction of new genetic variations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: evolutionary - relating to or promoting evolutionary change
  • synonym: stabilizing - maintaining or promoting stability in a system or population
