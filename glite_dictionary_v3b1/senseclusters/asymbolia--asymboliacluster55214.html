asymbolia

noun
pain without distress
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪsɪmˈboʊliə / (AmE), / ˌæsɪmˈbəʊliə / (BrE)
a neurological condition in which a person perceives pain but does not experience the usual emotional discomfort associated with it

Examples

Asymbolia to pain
After his brain injury, the patient exhibited asymbolia and did not seem bothered by painful stimuli.
Doctors diagnosed her with asymbolia when she reported pain but showed no emotional reaction.
Researchers study asymbolia to better understand how the brain processes pain and emotion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: analgesia - absence of the sensation of pain
