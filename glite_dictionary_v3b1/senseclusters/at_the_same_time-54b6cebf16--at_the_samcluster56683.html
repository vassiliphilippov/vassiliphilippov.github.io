at the same time

adverb
simultaneously; concurrently
Pronunciation: / ət ðə seɪm taɪm /; / æt ðə seɪm taɪm / (AmE), / ət ðə seɪm taɪm / (BrE)
at the same moment or during the same period; simultaneously

Examples

Do two things at the same time
He tried to answer emails and take phone calls at the same time.
It’s hard to watch two movies at the same time.
She managed to work full-time and study at the same time.
All the lights in the building went out at the same time.
Both engines failed at the same time, causing a serious emergency.
She tried to talk and eat at the same time, but it proved difficult.

Related Terms

  • synonym: concurrently - happening at the same time
  • synonym: simultaneously - occurring at the same time
