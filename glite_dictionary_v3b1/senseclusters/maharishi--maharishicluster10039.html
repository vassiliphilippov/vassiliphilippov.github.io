maharishi

proper noun
Indian spiritual teacher
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːhəˈriːʃi / (AmE), / ˌmɑːhəˈriːʃi / (BrE)
a title for a great spiritual teacher or sage in India, often used for Maharishi Mahesh Yogi, founder of Transcendental Meditation

Examples

The maharishi speaks
The maharishi attracted thousands of followers from around the world.
Many people credit the maharishi with popularizing meditation in the West.
The teachings of the maharishi continue to influence spiritual seekers today.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disciple - a follower or student of a teacher, leader, or philosopher
  • synonym: guru - a spiritual teacher, especially in Indian religions
