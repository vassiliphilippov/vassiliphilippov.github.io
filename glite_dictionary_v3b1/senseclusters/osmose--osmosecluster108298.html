osmose

verb
to absorb gradually
9.0
Pronunciation: / ɑzˈmoʊz / (AmE), / ɒzˈməʊz / (BrE)
to gradually and unconsciously absorb or take in ideas, habits, or attitudes from others

Examples

To osmose new ideas
Children often osmose the behaviors and attitudes of their parents without realizing it.
After living abroad for years, he seemed to osmose the local culture and traditions.
New employees quickly osmose the company's informal rules by watching their colleagues.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absorb - to take in or soak up
  • antonym: ignore - to pay no attention to
  • synonym: learn - to gain knowledge or skill by studying or experience
