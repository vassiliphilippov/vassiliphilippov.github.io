semifeudal

adjective
partly feudal
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmiˈfjuːdəl / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈfjuːdəl / (BrE)
partly resembling or having some characteristics of a feudal system, especially in social or economic structure

Examples

A semifeudal system
The region's semifeudal land ownership system limited opportunities for small farmers.
Despite reforms, many villages remained under semifeudal control for decades.
Historians described the society as semifeudal due to its mix of old and new structures.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: feudal - relating to or characteristic of a feudal system
  • antonym: modern - relating to the present or recent times, not feudal
