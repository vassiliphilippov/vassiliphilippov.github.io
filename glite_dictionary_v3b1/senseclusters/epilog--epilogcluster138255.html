epilog

noun
concluding speech or section in a work
5.4 5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈe.pə.lɑːg/ /; / /ˈe.pɪ.lɑːg/ (AmE), /ˈe.pɪ.lɒg/ /; / /ˈe.pə.lɒg/ (BrE)
a short section or speech at the end of a play, book, or other literary work that comments on or concludes the story

Examples

The epilog of a play
In Shakespearean times, it was common for a character to speak an epilog directly to the audience.
The actor delivered the epilog with great emotion, bringing the play to a satisfying close.
The novel ends with an epilog that ties up all the remaining loose threads.
After the main play ended, the epilog offered the playwright's final thoughts.
Shakespeare often included a brief epilog in his dramatic works.
The novel's epilog reveals what happened to the characters years later.
