draw away

verb
move ahead of competitors
6.0
Pronunciation: / drɔ əˈweɪ / (AmE), / drɔː əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to move ahead of one's competitors in a race or competition

Examples

Draw away from rivals
As the final lap began, the runner managed to draw away from the rest of the field.
She started to draw away after the first mile, opening up a clear lead.
The team drew away in the second half, leaving their rivals far behind.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fall behind - to lose position and be overtaken in a race or competition
  • synonym: pull ahead - to move into the lead in a race or competition
