obliterative

adjective
causing total removal
Pronunciation: / əˈblɪt̬ɚˌeɪtɪv / (AmE), / əˈblɪtərətɪv / (BrE)
causing or relating to the complete destruction or removal of something

Examples

An obliterative effect
The surgeon recommended an obliterative approach to remove the damaged tissue.
Some diseases require obliterative treatments to prevent further complications.
The obliterative effects of the fire left nothing but ashes behind.

  • antonym: preservative - serving to preserve or protect from harm
