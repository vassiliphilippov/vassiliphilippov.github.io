hexyl

noun
six-carbon group
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛksɪl / (AmE), / ˈhɛksɪl / (BrE)
a chemical group or radical derived from hexane, consisting of six carbon atoms arranged in a chain

Examples

Hexyl group
The chemist added a hexyl group to the molecule to change its properties.
Many surfactants contain a hexyl chain as part of their structure.
Researchers studied how the hexyl radical reacts with other compounds in the solution.
