limping iamb

noun
iambic verse ending with a spondee or trochee
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈlɪm.pɪŋ ˈaɪ.æm/ /; / /ˈlɪm.pɪŋ ˈaɪ.əm/ (AmE), /ˈlɪm.pɪŋ ˈaɪ.æm/ /; / /ˈlɪm.pɪŋ ˈaɪ.əm/ (BrE)
an iambic verse in which the final foot is a spondee or trochee rather than an iamb

Examples

A limping iamb verse
A sudden limping iamb can disrupt the expected rhythm of a poem, drawing attention to the line's meaning.
Many classical scholars discuss the effect of the limping iamb in ancient Greek verse.
The poet cleverly employed a limping iamb to convey a sense of unease in the final line.
