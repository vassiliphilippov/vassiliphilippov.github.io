lotting

noun
grouping into lots
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈlɒtɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of grouping goods, securities, or items into standard lots for sale or trading

Examples

Lotting auction items
The auction house uses lotting to organize items before the sale begins.
Proper lotting of shares can make trading more efficient for investors.
The company improved its profits by changing its lotting strategy for bulk goods.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bundling - the act of grouping items together for sale
  • antonym: separating - the act of keeping items apart or ungrouped
