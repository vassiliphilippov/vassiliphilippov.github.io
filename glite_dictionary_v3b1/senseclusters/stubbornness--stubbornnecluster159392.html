stubbornness

noun
quality of being difficult to change or persuade
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈstʌbərnnəs / (AmE), / ˈstʌbənnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being unwilling to change your mind or behavior, even when there are good reasons to do so

Examples

His stubbornness in decisions
Despite his stubbornness, she eventually convinced him to try the new restaurant.
Her stubbornness made it hard for the team to agree on the best approach.
Sometimes, stubbornness can be a virtue when standing up for what is right.
Despite their stubbornness, the children eventually agreed to share their toys.
Her stubbornness made it impossible to persuade her to try a new approach.
His stubbornness was both admired and criticized by his colleagues.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flexibility - the quality of being adaptable or willing to change
  • synonym: obstinacy - the quality of being stubborn, refusing to change one's mind
