fainter

adjective
less intense
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈfeɪntər / (AmE), / ˈfeɪntə / (BrE)
less bright, loud, strong, or distinct than something else; weaker in intensity.

Examples

Fainter sound
The music became fainter as we walked away from the concert hall.
She could see a fainter outline of the mountain through the morning mist.
His voice grew fainter as he drifted off to sleep.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: brighter - giving out or reflecting more light
  • degree lower: faint - not strong, bright, or clear
  • degree higher: louder - making more noise or sound
  • degree higher: stronger - having more intensity or power
