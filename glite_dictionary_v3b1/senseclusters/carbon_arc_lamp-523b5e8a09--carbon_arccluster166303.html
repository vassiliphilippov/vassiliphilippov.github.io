carbon arc lamp

noun
lamp producing light via carbon arc
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑrbən ˈɑrk læmp / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbən ˈɑːk læmp / (BrE)
a type of electric lamp in which a bright light is produced by an arc between carbon electrodes.

Examples

A carbon arc lamp
A carbon arc lamp requires careful handling due to the extremely high temperatures involved.
Early film projectors relied on the bright illumination of the carbon arc lamp.
The old theater used a carbon arc lamp to create intense stage lighting.
