firstborn

noun
child born first among siblings
2.1
Pronunciation: / ˈfɝst.bɔrn / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst.bɔːn / (BrE)
the child who is born first in the order of siblings

Examples

The firstborn child
As the firstborn, she always felt a sense of responsibility for her younger siblings.
Parents often expect their firstborn to set a good example.
The firstborn usually receives the most attention from grandparents.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lastborn - the youngest child in the family
