Scirpus acutus

noun
North American sedge species
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪər.pəs əˈkjuːtəs / (AmE), / ˈsɪə.pəs əˈkjuːtəs / (BrE)
A widely distributed North American sedge with rigid olive green stems, commonly found in wetlands.

Examples

Scirpus acutus wetland plant
Scirpus acutus is often mistaken for common cattail due to its tall, slender stems.
Scientists monitor populations of Scirpus acutus as an indicator of marsh ecosystem health.
The wetland restoration project included planting Scirpus acutus to improve habitat for native birds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bulrush - a tall rush-like water plant
