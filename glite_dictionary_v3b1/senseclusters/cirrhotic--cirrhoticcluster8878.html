cirrhotic

noun
person with cirrhosis
Pronunciation: / səˈrɑtɪk / (AmE), / sɪˈrɒtɪk / (BrE)
a person who has cirrhosis, especially of the liver, resulting in chronic scarring and impaired function

Examples

A cirrhotic patient
The hospital provides special care for each cirrhotic admitted to the liver unit.
Doctors monitor the health of a cirrhotic closely to prevent further complications.
Support groups can help a cirrhotic cope with lifestyle changes after diagnosis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: healthy person - a person without disease or illness
  • synonym: patient - a person receiving medical care
