spider angioma

noun
type of dilated blood vessel on the skin
Pronunciation: / ˈspaɪdər ænˈdʒioʊmə / (AmE), / ˈspaɪdə ænˈdʒiəmə / (BrE)
a small, dilated blood vessel with a central red spot and radiating capillaries, resembling a spider, typically seen on the skin

Examples

A spider angioma spot
A spider angioma often fades when pressed in the center, then refills from the radiating vessels.
Liver disease can cause multiple spider angiomas to appear on the skin.
The dermatologist identified a spider angioma on the patient’s cheek during the examination.

Related Terms

  • synonym: telangiectasia - a condition involving dilated small blood vessels near the skin's surface
