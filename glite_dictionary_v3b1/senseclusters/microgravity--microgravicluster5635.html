microgravity

noun
very weak gravity
6.8
Pronunciation: /maɪ.kroʊ.ˈgræv.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /maɪ.krə.ˈgræv.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /maɪ.krə.ˈgræv.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /maɪ.krəʊ.ˈgræv.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
a condition in which gravity is extremely weak, causing objects to float or become weightless

Examples

Microgravity in space
Astronauts experience microgravity while orbiting the Earth on the International Space Station.
In microgravity, flames behave differently than they do on Earth.
Scientists study plant growth in microgravity to learn how space travel affects living things.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gravity - the force that attracts objects toward the center of the earth
  • synonym: weightlessness - the state of being without apparent weight, as in free fall
