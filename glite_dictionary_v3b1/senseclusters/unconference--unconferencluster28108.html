unconference

verb
to hold participant-led meeting
8.3
Pronunciation: /ʌn.ˈkɑːn.fɜːr.əns/ /; / /ʌn.ˈkɑːn.frəns/ (AmE), /ʌn.ˈkɒn.fɜː.əns/ /; / /ʌn.ˈkɒn.frəns/ (BrE)
to organize or hold a meeting in which participants set the agenda and lead sessions themselves, rather than following a fixed program.

Examples

To unconference a workshop
This year, the organizers chose to unconference the event, letting attendees decide the topics.
After years of traditional meetings, the team decided to unconference their annual retreat.
Many tech communities prefer to unconference so everyone can contribute ideas and lead sessions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: confer - to hold a formal meeting or discussion
  • synonym: convene - to bring people together for a meeting or activity
  • synonym: facilitate - to make an event or process easier or more effective
