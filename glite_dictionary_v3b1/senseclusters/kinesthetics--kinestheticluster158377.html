kinesthetics

noun
awareness of body movement
5.7 7.2
Pronunciation: /kɪn.əs.ˈθet.ɪks/ /; / /kaɪ.nə.ˈθet.ɪks/ (AmE), /kɪn.əs.ˈθet.ɪks/ /; / /kaɪ.nə.ˈθet.ɪks/ (BrE)
the ability to perceive and be aware of the movements and position of one's own body and limbs

Examples

Body kinesthetics sense
Dancers often have highly developed kinesthetics, allowing them to control their movements precisely.
Good kinesthetics is essential for athletes who need to react quickly to changes in motion.
Therapists use exercises to improve patients' kinesthetics after injuries affecting balance or coordination.

Related Terms

  • synonym: proprioception - the sense of the relative position of one's own body parts
