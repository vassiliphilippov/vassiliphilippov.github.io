orbital rotation

noun
motion around a central point in an orbit
4.4 5.6
Pronunciation: /ˈɔːr.bə.təl roʊˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ˈɔːr.bɪ.təl roʊˈteɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈɔː.bɪ.təl rəʊˈteɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ˈɔː.bə.təl rəʊˈteɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the motion of an object as it moves in a curved path around a fixed point or another body, especially in space

Examples

Orbital rotation of satellites
Engineers calculate the orbital rotation of satellites to ensure they remain in stable orbits.
Jupiter's rings maintain their shape due to the orbital rotation of countless tiny particles.
The Moon's orbital rotation around the Earth takes approximately 27 days.
