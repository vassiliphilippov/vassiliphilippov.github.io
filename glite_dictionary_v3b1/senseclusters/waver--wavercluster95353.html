waver

verb
emit unsteady or fluctuating sounds
3.4 3.9
Pronunciation: /ˈweɪ.vər/ (AmE), /ˈweɪ.və/ (BrE)
to move, sway, or shake with an unsteady or trembling motion (including sounds, objects, or people)

Examples

To waver in the wind
As the wind increased, the distant siren seemed to waver between high and low pitches.
Her voice began to waver as she sang the final notes of the song.
The old radio's signal would sometimes waver, making it hard to hear clearly.
Her voice seemed to waver with emotion as she spoke.
The candle flame began to waver in the breeze.
The tall grass wavered gently under the summer wind.
A shadow seemed to waver on the wall as the headlights passed by.
The flame of the candle began to waver in the breeze.
The old flag wavered in the wind, barely holding onto the pole.

Related Terms

  • synonym: falter - to lose strength or momentum
  • synonym: flicker - to move or shine with quick, unsteady movements or light
  • synonym: hesitate - to pause before doing something, often due to uncertainty
  • synonym: oscillate - to move or swing back and forth at a regular speed
  • synonym: quaver - (of a voice) to shake or tremble, often when nervous or emotional
  • antonym: steady - not shaking or moving; firmly fixed
  • synonym: vacillate - to move in a wavy or unsteady manner
