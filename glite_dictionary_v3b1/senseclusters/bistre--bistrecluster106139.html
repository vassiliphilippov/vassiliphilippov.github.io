bistre

noun
brownish-yellow pigment from wood soot
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪstər / (AmE), / ˈbɪstə / (BrE)
a brownish-yellow pigment made from the soot of burned wood, used especially in drawing and watercolor painting

Examples

Bistre pigment
She mixed bistre with water to achieve a subtle wash for her landscape painting.
The museum displayed drawings in ink, charcoal, and bistre from the 18th century.
The old master sketches were done in bistre to create warm brown tones.
