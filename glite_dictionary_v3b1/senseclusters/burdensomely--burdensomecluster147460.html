burdensomely

adverb
in a difficult way
Pronunciation: / ˈbɝːdənsəmli / (AmE), / ˈbɜːdn̩səmli / (BrE)
in a way that causes difficulty, hardship, or is hard to bear

Examples

Act burdensomely
The new taxes were implemented burdensomely on low-income families.
She found the paperwork requirements burdensomely complex and time-consuming.
The rules were enforced burdensomely, making it hard for anyone to comply.

Related Terms

  • antonym: easily - in a way that is not difficult or troublesome
  • synonym: oppressively - in a way that is harsh or difficult to endure
