detector

noun
device that detects signals or radioactivity
2.4 3.0
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈtek.tər/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈtek.tə/ (BrE)
a device that discovers or senses the presence of something, such as radio signals, radioactivity, or movement

Examples

Metal detector
Modern metal detectors can distinguish between different types of metals and objects.
The scientist used a detector to measure the level of radioactivity in the laboratory.
The technician adjusted the detector to pick up weak radio signals from space.
Motion detectors are commonly used in security systems to alert owners of unusual activity.
Scientists use a particle detector to observe high-energy collisions in the laboratory.
The smoke detector went off when it sensed a small fire in the kitchen.
