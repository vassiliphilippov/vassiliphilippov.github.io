follow up

verb
take further action to complete
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑloʊ ˌʌp /; / ˈfɑː.loʊ ʌp / (AmE), / ˈfɒləʊ ˌʌp /; / ˈfɒl.əʊ ʌp / (BrE)
to take further action after something has started, especially to ensure it is completed or successful

Examples

To follow up with email

If you don't hear back, it's important to follow up on your application.
The police promised to follow up any new leads in the investigation.
After the interview, the journalist decided to follow up the story with further investigation.
It's important for teachers to follow up feedback with practical suggestions.
The marketing team will follow up the email campaign with a series of phone calls.
