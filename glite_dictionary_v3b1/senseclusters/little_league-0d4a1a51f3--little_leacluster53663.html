Little League

noun
youth baseball league for ages 8–12
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪt̬l ˌliːɡ /; / ˈlɪɾl̩ liːɡ / (AmE), / ˈlɪtl ˌliːɡ /; / ˈlɪt.əl liːɡ / (BrE)
an organized baseball or softball league for children, usually with teams, competitions, and instruction

Examples

Little League baseball
My son plays shortstop in the local Little League team every summer.
She coached Little League for several years before moving on to high school baseball.
The Little League World Series attracts teams from all over the world.
Every Saturday morning, parents gather to watch their children play Little League.
Many professional athletes started their careers in Little League.
She still keeps the trophy her team won in Little League as a child.
