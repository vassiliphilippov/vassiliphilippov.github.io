immune reaction

noun

a bodily defense response in which the immune system identifies and attacks foreign substances such as viruses, bacteria, or transplanted organs

Examples

Mild immune reaction
After receiving the vaccine, she experienced a mild immune reaction as her body produced antibodies.
An immune reaction is triggered when the body detects harmful bacteria in the bloodstream.
Transplanted organs can sometimes cause a strong immune reaction in recipients.

Synonyms

  • immune response
    4.4 1.52/m
    immune response is the body's action that makes antibodies against invaders, while immune reaction describes the body's attack on foreign substances.
  • immunologic response
    5.3 0.01/m
    immunologic response is a technical term used in medicine for targeted immune activity, while immune reaction is the general bodily defense against invaders.
