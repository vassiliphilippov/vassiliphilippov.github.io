bowed down

adjective
weighed down by burden or worry
6.5 0.06/m

Heavily burdened with work, responsibilities, or cares; weighed down by difficulties.

Examples

Bowed down with worry
After months of bad news, he was bowed down with worry.
She felt bowed down by the constant demands of her job and family.
Social workers are sometimes bowed down with the weight of their responsibilities.

Synonyms

  • weighed down
    4.5 0.61/m
    weighed down is heavy with physical or mental load, while bowed down suggests being bent and crushed by many cares.
  • overburdened
    5.3 0.03/m
    overburdened has more work or worry than someone can cope with, while bowed down means heavily worn by difficulties.
  • burdened
    3.8 0.00/m
    burdened carries a heavy load of duties or worries, while bowed down suggests a deeper, almost crushing level of strain.
  • loaded down
    4.6 0.21/m
    loaded down often sounds informal and shows many things to carry, while bowed down paints a more emotional, heavy weariness.
  • heavy-laden
    6.5 0.01/m
    heavy-laden feels old-fashioned and poetic, while bowed down is modern and common to describe being weighed by troubles.
