on my tail

preposition
pursuing closely or persistently
Pronunciation: / ɑn maɪ teɪl / (AmE), / ɒn maɪ teɪl / (BrE)
following or pursuing someone closely or persistently.

Examples

On my tail closely
Ever since I started my new job, deadlines have been on my tail.
The police were on my tail as I sped down the highway.
Whenever I jog in the park, it feels like someone's always on my tail.

Related Terms

  • antonym: give up the chase - stop pursuing someone or something
  • synonym: on someone's heels - following someone closely; in close pursuit
