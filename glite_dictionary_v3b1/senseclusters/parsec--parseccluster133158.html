parsec

noun
astronomical distance unit (about 3.26 light years)
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑrˌsɛk / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.sɛk / (BrE)
a unit of astronomical distance equal to the distance at which a star would have a parallax of one arcsecond, approximately 3.26 light years or 3.086 × 10¹³ kilometers

Examples

One parsec distance
Astronomers often measure interstellar distances in parsecs rather than light years.
At a distance of ten parsecs, a star's parallax would be a tenth of an arcsecond.
The nearest star to Earth is just over one parsec away.

Related Terms

  • synonym: light year - distance that light travels in one year
