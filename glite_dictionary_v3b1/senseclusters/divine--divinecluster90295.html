divine

adjective
like or relating to a god
Pronunciation: / dəˈvaɪn /; / dɪˈvaɪn / (AmE), / dɪˈvaɪn / (BrE)
of, from, or relating to God or a deity; godlike; connected to worship or inspired by God

Examples

Divine power
His followers believed his wisdom came from a divine source.
Many cultures have myths about divine beings shaping the universe.
The pharaohs were considered to possess divine authority in ancient Egypt.
Many people seek divine guidance during difficult times.
The prophet believed his message was of divine origin.
They interpreted the rare event as a sign of divine intervention.
Many people seek a divine connection through prayer and meditation.
The ceremony took place in a divine temple built centuries ago.
The choir sang hymns as part of the divine liturgy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: godlike - having qualities similar to a god
  • synonym: godly - having the qualities or characteristics of God; devout or pious
  • antonym: mortal - not divine; relating to human beings
  • synonym: sacred - regarded with great respect and reverence, especially by a religious tradition
  • antonym: satanic - of or characteristic of Satan; wicked or devilish
  • antonym: secular - not connected with religious or spiritual matters
