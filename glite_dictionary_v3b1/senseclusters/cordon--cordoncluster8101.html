cordon

noun
ornamental cord or ribbon of honor
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrd(ə)n /; / ˈkɔrdən / (AmE), / ˈkɔːd(ə)n /; / ˈkɔːdən / (BrE)
an ornamental cord or ribbon used as a decoration or mark of honor, often in ceremonies or with medals

Examples

A gold cordon
A red silk cordon adorned the ambassador's uniform during the ceremony.
Receiving the official cordon is considered a great honor in many orders of chivalry.
The general wore a blue cordon across his chest, signifying his distinguished service.
A red silk cordon adorned the base of the ornate lamp.
She wore a blue cordon across her shoulder to signify her academic achievement.
The military officer was presented with a golden cordon as part of his ceremonial uniform.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sash - a long strip or loop of cloth worn over one shoulder or around the waist as a decoration or insignia
