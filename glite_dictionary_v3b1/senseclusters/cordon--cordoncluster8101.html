cordon

noun
ornamental cord or ribbon of honor
8.3 6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈkɔːr.dən/ (AmE), /ˈkɔː.dən/ (BrE)
an ornamental cord or ribbon used as a decoration or mark of honor, often in ceremonies or with medals

Examples

A gold cordon

Receiving the official cordon is considered a great honor in many orders of chivalry.

She wore a blue cordon across her shoulder to signify her academic achievement.
The military officer was presented with a golden cordon as part of his ceremonial uniform.
