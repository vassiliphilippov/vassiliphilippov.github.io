interchangeability

noun
ability to be exchanged or substituted
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntɚˌtʃeɪndʒəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˌtʃeɪndʒəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being able to be exchanged or substituted for one another without loss of function or value

Examples

Interchangeability of parts
In manufacturing, interchangeability is crucial for efficient mass production.
Language allows for some interchangeability of words with similar meanings.
The interchangeability of the parts makes repairs much easier and less costly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: uniqueness - the state of being unique or one of a kind
