rat

noun
despicable or contemptible person
Pronunciation: / ræt / (AmE), / ræt / (BrE)
(informal) a person who betrays, informs on others, or works against a group, especially during a strike

Examples

A rat betrays friends
Everyone turned on the rat who betrayed the team.
He acted like a rat and stole from his friends.
I can't believe you lied to me, you rat!
He was called a rat for taking over another worker's shift during the strike.
Some workers are accused of being a rat if they don't join the protest.
The union members were angry when a rat crossed the picket line.
After years in the gang, he became a rat and told the police everything.
No one in the group wanted to be labeled as a rat after the incident.
They discovered the cause of the arrests was a rat in their organization.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hero - a person admired for courage or noble qualities
  • synonym: informant - a person who gives information to the police or an authority
  • synonym: rotter - a thoroughly bad or unprincipled person
  • synonym: scab - a person who works during a strike; strikebreaker
  • synonym: scoundrel - a person who behaves dishonestly or unscrupulously
  • synonym: snitch - someone who betrays by informing to police or authorities
  • synonym: traitor - a person who betrays someone or something, such as a friend or principle
