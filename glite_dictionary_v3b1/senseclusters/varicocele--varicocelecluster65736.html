varicocele

noun
enlarged veins in the scrotum
5.4
Pronunciation: / ˈværɪkoʊˌsiːl / (AmE), / ˈværɪkəʊˌsiːl / (BrE)
A medical condition in which the veins within the scrotum, associated with the spermatic cord in the testes, become enlarged and dilated.

Examples

A varicocele diagnosis
A varicocele is identified by the presence of enlarged veins that may feel like a 'bag of worms' in the scrotum.
Surgical treatment is sometimes required if a varicocele causes pain or infertility.
The doctor diagnosed him with a varicocele after he complained of discomfort in his testicles.
