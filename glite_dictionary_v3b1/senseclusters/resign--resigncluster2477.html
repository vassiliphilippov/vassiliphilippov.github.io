to formally give up a right, possession, or claim; to relinquish or surrender something

Examples

To resign a claim
After years of dispute, he finally decided to resign his claim to the property.
She chose to resign her rights to the inheritance in favor of her brother.
The prince was forced to resign his title after the scandal.

Synonyms

  • relinquish
    7.2 5.71/m
    relinquish means to give up control or rights voluntarily and generally, while resign is often used for leaving a job or post.
  • renounce
    6.3 5.03/m
    renounce is a formal public refusal of a right, belief, or position, while resign usually refers to stepping down from a job.
  • release
    2.6 2.90/m
    release means to give up ownership or free something or someone, while resign focuses on giving up a role or legal claim.
  • cede
    8.2 3.28/m
    cede means to hand over land, power, or rights to another state or group, while resign is personal surrender or stepping down.

  • antonym: retain - to keep possession of something
