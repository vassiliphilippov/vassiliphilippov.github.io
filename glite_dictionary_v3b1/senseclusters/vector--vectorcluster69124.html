vector

noun
mathematical entity with magnitude and direction
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛk.tɚ /; / ˈvɛktɚ / (AmE), / ˈvɛk.tə /; / ˈvɛktə / (BrE)
a quantity in mathematics or physics that has both size (magnitude) and direction

Examples

A vector diagram
In physics, a vector is used to represent quantities like velocity and force.
The vector points north with a magnitude of five units.
You must add the vectors head-to-tail to find the resultant displacement.
In physics, a vector can represent velocity, which has both speed and direction.
The force acting on the object was described by a mathematical vector.
You can break a vector into horizontal and vertical components for easier calculation.
