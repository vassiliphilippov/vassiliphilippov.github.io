Fuchsia

noun
genus of ornamental tropical shrubs
Pronunciation: / ˈfjuːʃə / (AmE), / ˈfjuːʃə / (BrE)
A genus of decorative tropical shrubs with drooping, tubular, four-part flowers, commonly grown as ornamentals.

Examples

Bright fuchsias flowers
Gardeners often plant several fuchsias in hanging baskets for a vivid display all summer long.
The Fuchsia is admired for its bright, pendulous flowers that attract hummingbirds.
The vibrant blossoms of Fuchsia varieties add a splash of color to shaded corners of a garden.
