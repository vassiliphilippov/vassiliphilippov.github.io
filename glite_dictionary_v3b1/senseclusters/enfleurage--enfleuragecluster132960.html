enfleurage

verb
to extract fragrance
9.9
Pronunciation: /ən.ˈflʊr.ɑːʒ/ /; / /en.ˈflʊr.ɑːʒ/ (AmE), /ən.ˈflʊə.rɑːʒ/ /; / /ɒn.ˈflʊə.rɑːʒ/ (BrE)
to extract the fragrance of flowers by placing their petals in fat, allowing the scent to be absorbed.

Examples

To enfleurage petals
Traditional perfumers enfleurage delicate petals to capture their natural scent for luxury fragrances.
The laboratory team decided to enfleurage the rare blossoms to preserve their unique aroma.
Artisans still enfleurage flowers by hand in some regions to produce high-quality essential oils.
