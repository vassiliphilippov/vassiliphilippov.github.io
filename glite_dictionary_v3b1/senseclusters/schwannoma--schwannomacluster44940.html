schwannoma

noun
benign nerve tumor
9.9
Pronunciation: /ʃwɑː.ˈnoʊ.mə/ /; / /ʃwə.ˈnoʊ.mə/ (AmE), /ʃwɒ.ˈnəʊ.mə/ /; / /ʃwɒn.ˈəʊ.mə/ (BrE)
a usually benign tumor that develops from Schwann cells (cells that cover nerves) in the nervous system

Examples

A schwannoma tumor
The doctor confirmed that the growth was a schwannoma and recommended surgery.
After the schwannoma was removed, the patient recovered quickly.
A schwannoma can sometimes press on nerves and cause discomfort.
The surgeon removed a schwannoma from the patient's arm without damaging the nerve.
A schwannoma can cause pain or numbness if it presses on nearby nerves.
After the MRI, doctors confirmed that the growth was a benign schwannoma.

Related Terms

  • synonym: neurinoma - a tumor arising from nerve tissue, especially Schwann cells
  • synonym: neurofibroma - a benign tumor of nerve tissue
