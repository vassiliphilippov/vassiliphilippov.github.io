arc-boutant

noun
arch-supported buttress in Gothic architecture
6.3
Pronunciation: / aʁk bu.tɑ̃ / (AmE), / aʁk bu.tɑ̃ / (BrE)
an architectural support consisting of a buttress that stands apart from the main structure and is connected to it by an arch, chiefly used in Gothic architecture; also known as a flying buttress.

Examples

A arc-boutant support
Engineers studied the medieval arc-boutant to understand its structural advantages for tall buildings.
Restorers carefully repaired a damaged arc-boutant above the choir of the old church.
The cathedral's most remarkable feature is its series of arc-boutants supporting the high nave walls.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flying buttress - a buttress with an arch that supports a wall from a distance
