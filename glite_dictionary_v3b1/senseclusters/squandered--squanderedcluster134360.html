squandered

adjective
wasted; not used to good advantage
Pronunciation: / ˈskwɑːndərd / (AmE), / ˈskwɒndəd / (BrE)
(of something valuable) not used to good advantage or wasted, especially through careless or foolish actions.

Examples

Squandered opportunities
After years of squandered opportunities, the town finally invested in its future.
Many felt the squandered funds could have helped those in real need.
The charity warned that squandered resources could not be recovered.
