dispatchable

adjective
ready for quick use
Pronunciation: / dɪˈspætʃəbəl / (AmE), / dɪˈspætʃəbəl / (BrE)
able to be sent out, activated, or used quickly when needed, especially in technical or energy contexts

Examples

Dispatchable power plant
Natural gas plants are highly dispatchable and can respond quickly to changes in electricity demand.
Wind energy is less dispatchable because it depends on weather conditions.
The new system makes backup power more dispatchable during emergencies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: available - able to be used or obtained
  • antonym: unavailable - not able to be used or obtained
