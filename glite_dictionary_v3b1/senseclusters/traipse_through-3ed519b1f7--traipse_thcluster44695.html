traipse through

verb
walk or wander casually through
Pronunciation: / ˈtreɪps θru / (AmE), / ˈtreɪps θruː / (BrE)
to walk or wander casually, often without a clear purpose, through a place

Examples

To traipse through museums
They spent the afternoon traipsing through museums and art galleries in the city.
Tourists love to traipse through the old city streets, discovering hidden shops and cafes.
We traipsed through the park for hours, enjoying the autumn leaves.

Related Terms

  • synonym: wander through - to move slowly or aimlessly through a place
