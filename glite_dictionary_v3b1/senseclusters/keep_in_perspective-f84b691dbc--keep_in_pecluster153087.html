keep in perspective

verb
judge importance by considering context
Pronunciation: / kip ɪn pərˈspɛktɪv / (AmE), / kiːp ɪn pəˈspɛktɪv / (BrE)
to judge the true importance of something by considering it in relation to everything else

Examples

To keep in perspective risks
Amid the excitement, we must keep in perspective the potential risks involved.
It's important to keep in perspective that one mistake doesn't define your whole career.
Try to keep in perspective how much progress you've already made, even if it feels slow.
