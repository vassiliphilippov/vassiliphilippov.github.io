covenant

verb
enter into a formal agreement
8.2 6.4
Pronunciation: /ˈkʌv.ən.ənt/ /; / /ˈkʌv.nənt/ (AmE), /ˈkʌv.ən.ənt/ /; / /ˈkʌv.nənt/ (BrE)
to make or enter into a formal agreement or promise, especially in a legal or religious context

Examples

To covenant with others
In 1650, the Scottish nobles covenanted with the king for religious freedoms.
The landlord and tenant covenant to respect the terms set out in the lease.
The two companies covenanted not to compete with each other for five years.
The landlord covenants to repair the property according to the terms of the lease.
The two companies covenanted to share research findings for the duration of the project.
They covenanted together to preserve the ancient traditions of their community.
The two companies are covenanting to work together on the new project.
By covenanting with the organization, she agreed to uphold its values.
The members of the group are covenanting to support each other through difficult times.

  • synonym: contract - make a formal and legally binding agreement
  • synonym: pledge - to make a serious or formal promise
  • synonym: promise - to assure someone that you will do something
