pine fern

noun
ferns native to Florida and the West Indies
2.5
Pronunciation: / paɪn fɜrn / (AmE), / paɪn fɜːn / (BrE)
A tropical fern native to Florida, the West Indies, and Central America, characterized by a rhizome densely covered with brown hairs.

Examples

The pine fern plant
Researchers studied the unique brown-haired rhizome of the pine fern.
The pine fern thrives in the humid forests of southern Florida.
We saw several pine ferns growing beneath the shade of larger trees.
