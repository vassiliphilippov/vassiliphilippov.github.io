peaty flavor

noun
smoky taste in certain whiskies
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈpiːti ˈfleɪvər / (AmE), / ˈpiːti ˈfleɪvə / (BrE)
A smoky, earthy taste commonly found in certain whiskies, resulting from the use of peat during the malting process.

Examples

The peaty flavor of whisky
Connoisseurs can often identify the peaty flavor unique to Islay whiskies.
If you prefer a milder taste, avoid bottles with an intense peaty flavor.
Many Scotch whiskies are celebrated for their peaty flavor and lingering finish.
