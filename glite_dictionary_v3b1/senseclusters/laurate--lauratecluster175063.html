laurate

noun
salt of lauric acid
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈlɔːreɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɔːreɪt / (BrE)
a salt or ester derived from lauric acid, commonly used in soaps and cosmetics

Examples

Sodium laurate
Sodium laurate is commonly found in many cleansing products.
The soap contains potassium laurate as one of its main ingredients.
Cosmetic manufacturers often use laurate compounds for their emulsifying properties.

Related Terms

  • synonym: palmitate - a salt or ester of palmitic acid
  • synonym: stearate - a salt or ester of stearic acid
