extensive

adjective
large-scale, low-input farming
3.2
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstɛnsɪv / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɛnsɪv / (BrE)
(of agriculture) involving the production of crops or livestock across large areas with relatively minimal input of labor or capital per unit area.

Examples

Extensive farming
Cattle are often raised under extensive systems, roaming freely over vast grasslands.
Switching from intensive to extensive farming can help preserve soil fertility over time.
The region is known for its extensive agriculture, with large fields requiring little manual labor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intensive - (of agriculture) involving concentrated effort and inputs on a small area for higher productivity
