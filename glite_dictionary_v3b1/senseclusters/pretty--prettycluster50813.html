pretty

adjective
emphatic, ironic: unexpectedly bad
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪti / (AmE), / ˈprɪti / (BrE)
(used for emphasis, often ironically) used to describe something that is unexpectedly bad, awkward, or difficult.

Examples

A pretty big problem
Getting lost in the woods was a pretty problem to deal with after dark.
That was a pretty mess you made of the presentation.
We've gotten ourselves into a pretty situation now, haven't we?

Related Terms

  • synonym: awful - extremely bad or unpleasant
  • antonym: nice - pleasant or agreeable (contrasting meaning, not an antonym in this sense)
  • synonym: terrible - very bad; of poor quality
