unbranching

adjective
having no branches
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnˈbræn.tʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈbrɑːn.tʃɪŋ / (BrE)
Having no branches; not dividing into separate stems or limbs.

Examples

An unbranching river
An unbranching river flows straight across the plain without dividing.
She marveled at the plant's unbranching structure, which stood in contrast to the surrounding bushes.
The cactus has a tall, unbranching stem that stores water.

Related Terms

  • antonym: branched - dividing into branches or subdivisions
