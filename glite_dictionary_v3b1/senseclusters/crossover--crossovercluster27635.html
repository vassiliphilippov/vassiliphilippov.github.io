crossover

noun
exchange between homologous chromosomes (genetics)
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɑːsˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈkrɒsˌəʊvə / (BrE)
the exchange of sections between paired homologous chromosomes during prophase of meiosis, leading to genetic variation.

Examples

Genetic crossover
A mutation can occur if there is an error during the crossover process.
During meiosis, crossover increases genetic diversity among offspring.
Scientists observed the crossover of genetic material under the microscope.

Related Terms

  • synonym: recombination - the rearrangement of genetic material, especially by crossing over in chromosomes
