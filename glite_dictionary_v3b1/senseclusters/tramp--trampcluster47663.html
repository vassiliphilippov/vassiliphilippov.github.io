tramp

noun
commercial steamship without regular schedule
7.1
Pronunciation: / træmp / (AmE), / tramp / (BrE)
a commercial steamship that is hired out for cargo transport rather than operating on a regular schedule or route

Examples

A tramp steamer
In the early twentieth century, the shipping company operated a large fleet of tramps across the Atlantic.
The old tramp carried coal from port to port, never staying long in any harbor.
Unlike a luxury liner, a tramp takes any cargo available for transport.

Related Terms

  • antonym: liner - a ship sailing a regular route on a fixed schedule
