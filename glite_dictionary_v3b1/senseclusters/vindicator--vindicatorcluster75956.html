vindicator

noun
person who defends or justifies a cause
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪndɪˌkeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈvɪndɪkeɪtə / (BrE)
a person who defends or justifies a policy, cause, or institution against criticism or attack

Examples

A vindicator of rights
As a passionate vindicator of free speech, she often debated with her opponents.
He became the chief vindicator of the government's economic reforms.
Historians have sometimes acted as vindicators for unpopular regimes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: apologist - a person who offers an argument in defense of something controversial
  • antonym: critic - a person who expresses an unfavorable opinion of something
