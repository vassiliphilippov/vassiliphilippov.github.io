Coffea arabica

noun
main species of coffee plant
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːfi ə əˈræbɪkə / (AmE), / ˌkɒfi ə əˈrabɪkə / (BrE)
A species of coffee plant native to northeastern Africa, widely cultivated for its seeds, which produce most of the world's coffee.

Examples

Coffea arabica plant
Because Coffea arabica prefers temperate climates, it thrives in mountainous regions.
The finest specialty coffees are often derived from Coffea arabica beans grown at high altitudes.
The plantation specializes in cultivating Coffea arabica for export to premium markets.
