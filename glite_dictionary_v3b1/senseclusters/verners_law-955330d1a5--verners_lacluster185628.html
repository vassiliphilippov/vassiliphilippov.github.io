Verner's law

noun
historical linguistics principle modifying Grimm's law
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈvɜrnərz lɔ / (AmE), / ˈvɜːnəz lɔː / (BrE)
a principle in historical linguistics stating that certain consonant shifts in the Germanic languages, as described by Grimm's law, were affected by the position of the original accent, resulting in exceptions to Grimm's law.

Examples

Verner's law in linguistics
Verner's law explains certain exceptions to Grimm's law in the development of Germanic languages.
Students of philology often study Verner's law to understand historical sound shifts.
The application of Verner's law clarified patterns in consonant changes previously considered irregular.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Grimm's law - a set of sound changes describing consonant shifts in Proto-Germanic
