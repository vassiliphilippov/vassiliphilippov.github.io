starvation acidosis

noun
acidosis due to prolonged fasting or starvation
8.2
Pronunciation: /stɑːr.ˈveɪ.ʃən æs.ɪ.ˈdoʊ.sɪs/ (AmE), /stɑː.ˈveɪ.ʃən æs.ɪ.ˈdəʊ.sɪs/ (BrE)
a form of acidosis caused by lack of food, in which the body breaks down fat, leading to the production of acidic ketone bodies

Examples

Starvation acidosis condition
After several days without food, the patient developed starvation acidosis due to increased fat breakdown.
Clinicians should monitor for starvation acidosis in patients who are fasting or unable to eat.
Starved animals often display starvation acidosis as their bodies switch to using fat reserves.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ketoacidosis - acidosis caused by excess ketone bodies in the body
