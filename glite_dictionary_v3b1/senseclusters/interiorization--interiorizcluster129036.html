interiorization

noun
inward migration
9.0
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌtɪriərɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɪəriəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of moving into or settling the interior parts of a region or country

Examples

Regional interiorization policy
The government promoted interiorization to encourage development in less populated regions.
Policies of interiorization led to new towns being established far from the coast.
During the 19th century, interiorization changed the demographic landscape of the country.

Related Terms

  • synonym: migration - the movement of people from one place to another
  • synonym: settlement - the act of people establishing a community in a new area
  • antonym: urbanization - the process of people moving to cities
