quadric surface

noun
surface defined by a second-degree equation
9.9 8.4
Pronunciation: /ˈkwɑː.drɪk ˈsɜːr.fəs/ (AmE), /ˈkwɒd.rɪk ˈsɜː.fɪs/ (BrE)
a surface in three-dimensional space defined by a second-degree equation in Cartesian coordinates

Examples

A quadric surface equation
By changing the coefficients in the equation, you can represent different quadric surfaces such as ellipsoids and paraboloids.
The hyperboloid is a type of quadric surface commonly studied in advanced geometry.
The intersection of a plane with a quadric surface can produce an ellipse, parabola, or hyperbola.
