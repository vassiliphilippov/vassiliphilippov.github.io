monolithic

adjective
massive, uniform, and rigid
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːnəˈlɪθɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈlɪθɪk / (BrE)
large, powerful, and intractably indivisible and uniform, often referring to organizations, movements, or societies that are massive, rigid, and lack diversity or flexibility.

Examples

Monolithic organization
Many critics argue that the media is not as monolithic as it appears.
The company had a monolithic structure that made it difficult to implement change.
The government's monolithic approach left little room for regional differences.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flexible - capable of bending easily without breaking; easily modified
  • synonym: rigid - not flexible; unable to bend or be forced out of shape
