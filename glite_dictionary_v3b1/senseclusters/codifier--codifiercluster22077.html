codifier

noun
device for encoding
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊdəˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈkəʊdɪˌfaɪə / (BrE)
a device or computer program that converts information into a coded form, especially for encryption, transmission, or storage

Examples

Digital codifier device
The new codifier improved the security of sensitive data during transmission.
Engineers tested the codifier to ensure it could handle large volumes of information.
A reliable codifier is essential for protecting messages from unauthorized access.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decoder - a device or program that converts coded data back to its original form
  • synonym: encoder - a device or program that converts data into a coded format
