dyskinetic

adjective
abnormal movements
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪskɪˈnɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌdɪskɪˈnɛtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or affected by abnormal, involuntary muscle movements

Examples

Dyskinetic movements
The doctor noted several dyskinetic movements in the patient's arms and legs.
Some medications can cause dyskinetic side effects, especially with long-term use.
Children with certain neurological disorders may develop dyskinetic symptoms over time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dystonic - relating to abnormal muscle tone or spasms
  • antonym: normal - not affected by abnormal movements
