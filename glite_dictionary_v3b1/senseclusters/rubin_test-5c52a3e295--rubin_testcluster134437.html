Rubin test

noun
test for Fallopian tube patency
7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈruː.bɪn test/ /; / /ˈruː.bən test/ /; / /ˈrʊ.bɪn test/ (AmE), /ˈruː.bɪn test/ /; / /ˈruː.bən test/ (BrE)
a test used to determine whether the Fallopian tubes are open (patent) or blocked (occluded) by introducing gas into the uterus and observing its passage

Examples

The Rubin test procedure
After her Rubin test showed both tubes were blocked, further treatment options were discussed.
Many clinics now use less invasive methods than the traditional Rubin test for tubal assessment.
The Rubin test was commonly used in the past to assess infertility causes related to the Fallopian tubes.
