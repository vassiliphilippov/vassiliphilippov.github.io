elder

noun
shrub or small tree with white flowers and berries
Pronunciation: / ˈɛldɚ / (AmE), / ˈɛldə / (BrE)
a shrub or small tree of the genus Sambucus, found in temperate and subtropical regions of the Northern Hemisphere, which has white flowers and produces berrylike fruit

Examples

Elder tree berries
She harvested some berries from the elder behind the fence to make syrup.
The elder in the garden is covered with fragrant white blossoms every spring.
Wildlife are often attracted to the elder for its rich, dark berries.

  • degree lower: elderberry - the fruit of the elder tree
