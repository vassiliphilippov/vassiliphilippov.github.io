glycosylation

noun
adding sugar to molecules
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɡlaɪkəˌsɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɡlʌɪkəʊsɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a sugar molecule is attached to a protein or other molecule in a cell

Examples

Proper glycosylation of proteins
Proper glycosylation of proteins is essential for many biological functions in the body.
Scientists study glycosylation to understand how cells communicate and process information.
Defects in glycosylation can lead to serious health problems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: phosphorylation - the process of adding a phosphate group to a molecule
