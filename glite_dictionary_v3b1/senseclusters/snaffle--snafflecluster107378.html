snaffle

verb
seize or take quickly (especially opportunistically)
5.3 6.8
Pronunciation: /ˈsnæf.əl/ (AmE), /ˈsnæf.əl/ (BrE)
to take or grab something quickly, sometimes in a slightly sneaky or dishonest way

Examples

He snaffled a seat by the window as soon as the doors opened.
If you get there early, you can snaffle all the best bargains.
She managed to snaffle the last slice of cake before anyone else noticed.
He was always looking for a chance to snaffle a bargain at the market.
I tried to snaffle the last biscuit before anyone noticed.
She snaffled my headphones while I was out of the room.
