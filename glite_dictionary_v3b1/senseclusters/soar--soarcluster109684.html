soar

verb
to fly using a hang glider
5.2
Pronunciation: / sɔr / (AmE), / sɔː / (BrE)
to fly or glide high and freely in the air, often with little effort (like a bird, kite, or glider)

Examples

Birds soar high
He learned how to soar over the cliffs safely after taking a hang gliding course.
On weekends, many adventure seekers travel to the mountains to soar above the valleys in their hang gliders.
The group spent the afternoon soaring high over the beach, riding the wind currents in their gliders.
During the festival, colorful kites soared high into the blue sky.
The eagle began to soar above the mountains, gliding effortlessly on the wind.
Watching the paper airplane soar across the room made the children cheer.
Pilots must learn how to soar skillfully to stay airborne in a glider.
She loves to soar silently over the countryside in her sailplane.
The vintage plane began to soar after its tow rope was released.
