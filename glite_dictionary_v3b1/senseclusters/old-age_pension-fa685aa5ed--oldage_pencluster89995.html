a regular payment made by the government or an employer to a person who has retired from work, typically due to age

Examples

Receive an old-age pension
After working for forty years, she now receives an old-age pension from the government.
He applied for his old-age pension as soon as he reached the retirement age.
Many people worry that their old-age pension will not be enough to cover living expenses.

Synonyms

  • retirement pension
    2.7 0.13/m
    retirement pension is the usual payment from government or employer to someone who stops work, while old-age pension is for older people.
  • pension
    3.2 39/m
    pension is a general regular income for retired or non-working people, while old-age pension usually targets older retirees.
  • retirement benefit
    3.4 1.24/m
    retirement benefit is any support or payment after leaving work, while old-age pension is a specific age-based regular payment.
  • superannuation
    9.7 0.02/m
    superannuation is a fund-based payment from employers or plans, while old-age pension is a broader regular payment to retired people.
  • retirement check
    3.1 0.10/m
    retirement check is usually a monthly physical payment you receive by mail, while old-age pension is regular retirement income.
