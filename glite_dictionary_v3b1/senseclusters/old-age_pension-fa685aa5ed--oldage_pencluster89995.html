old-age pension

noun
retirement payment for the elderly
Pronunciation: / oʊld eɪdʒ ˈpɛnʃən / (AmE), / əʊld eɪdʒ ˈpɛnʃən / (BrE)
a regular payment made by the government or an employer to a person who has retired from work, typically due to age

Examples

Receive an old-age pension
After working for forty years, she now receives an old-age pension from the government.
He applied for his old-age pension as soon as he reached the retirement age.
Many people worry that their old-age pension will not be enough to cover living expenses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pension - a regular payment made during retirement from an investment fund to which a person has contributed
