financial audit

noun
official examination of financial records
Pronunciation: / fəˈnænʃəl ˈɔdɪt / (AmE), / faɪˈnænʃəl ˈɔːdɪt / (BrE)
An official examination of an organization's financial records and statements to ensure accuracy and compliance with laws and regulations.

Examples

A financial audit report
A financial audit is required by law for most publicly traded firms.
The company underwent a thorough financial audit to verify its annual report.
The external firm completed the financial audit ahead of schedule.
