twilight zone

noun
ambiguous or indeterminate area
9.9 6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈtwaɪ.laɪt zoʊn/ (AmE), /ˈtwaɪ.laɪt zəʊn/ (BrE)
a situation or place that is unclear, strange, or difficult to understand; an in-between or mysterious state

Examples

In a twilight zone state
Her emotions were in a twilight zone, neither happiness nor sadness, just a confusing in-between.
Science fiction often explores the twilight zone between reality and imagination.
The new law falls into a twilight zone between established regulations and recent technological changes.
After the announcement, the office entered a twilight zone of rumors and uncertainty.
The city at dawn felt like a twilight zone, empty and strangely silent.
When the rules changed without warning, we found ourselves in a twilight zone of confusion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gray area - an ill-defined situation or concept that is not clearly one thing or another
  • synonym: limbo - an intermediate or uncertain state
  • synonym: no man's land - an area of uncertainty, especially one not clearly belonging to any category
  • antonym: normality - a state of being normal or usual
