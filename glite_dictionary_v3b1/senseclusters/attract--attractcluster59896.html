attract

verb
cause to move toward; pull in (physics)
2.9
Pronunciation: / əˈtrækt / (AmE), / əˈtrækt / (BrE)
(of a force or object) to cause something to move toward itself or prevent it from moving away, especially as in physics.

Examples

Attracts a magnet
Charged objects can attract or repel each other depending on their polarity.
The Sun's gravity attracts the planets, keeping them in orbit.
The magnet will attract any pieces of metal nearby.

Related Terms

  • antonym: repel - to drive or force (something) away; to push away, especially in physics
