jack pine

noun
North American pine tree species
4.7 5.4
Pronunciation: /dʒæk paɪn/ (AmE), /dʒæk paɪn/ (BrE)
a slender, medium-sized pine tree of eastern North America, with paired yellow-green needles and scaly gray or red-brown fissured bark

Examples

A jack pine tree
The dense stands of jack pine provide ideal habitat for the endangered Kirtland’s warbler.
They planted several jack pines to help restore the natural forest ecosystem.
You can recognize a jack pine by its twisted needles and rough bark.
