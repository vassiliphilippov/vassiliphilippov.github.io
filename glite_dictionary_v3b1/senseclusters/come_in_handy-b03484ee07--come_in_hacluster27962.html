come in handy

verb
be useful or convenient
Pronunciation: / kʌm ɪn ˈhændi / (AmE), / kʌm ɪn ˈhændi /; / kʌm ɪn ˈhandi / (BrE)
to be useful or convenient in a particular situation or for a particular purpose

Examples

A notebook comes in handy
A notebook always comes in handy for jotting down ideas.
That flashlight will come in handy during a power outage.
The spare key came in handy when I locked myself out of the house.
A pocketknife can come in handy when you are hiking in the woods.
Her years of language study came in handy during her trip to Spain.
You never know when a spare battery might come in handy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: be useful - to serve a practical purpose or be of use
  • antonym: be useless - to have no utility or purpose; to be of no use
