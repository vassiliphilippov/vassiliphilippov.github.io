Marie Antoinette

proper noun
Queen of France executed during the Revolution
7.5
Pronunciation: /mə.ˈriː æn.twə.ˈnet/ (AmE), /mə.ˈriː æn.twə.ˈnet/ (BrE)
(1755–1793) The queen of France and wife of Louis XVI, known for her extravagance and unpopularity, whose actions contributed to the French Revolution; she was executed during the Revolution.

Examples

Marie Antoinette queen of France
Many historians study the influence of Marie Antoinette on the course of the French Revolution.
The dramatic downfall of Marie Antoinette is often cited as a symbol of the end of the monarchy in France.
The lavish lifestyle of Marie Antoinette fueled resentment among the people of France.
