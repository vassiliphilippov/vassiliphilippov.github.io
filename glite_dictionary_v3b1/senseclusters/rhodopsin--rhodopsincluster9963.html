rhodopsin

noun
pigment in retinal rods, aids vision
9.9 8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈroʊ.dəp.sɪn/ (AmE), /ˈrəʊ.dɒp.sɪn/ (BrE)
a red pigment in the retina's rod cells that is responsible for the first step in the perception of light

Examples

Rhodopsin in the retina
Rhodopsin plays a crucial role in enabling vision under low-light conditions.
Exposure to bright light causes rhodopsin molecules to break down and regenerate in the retina.
Scientists study rhodopsin to better understand night blindness and related disorders.
