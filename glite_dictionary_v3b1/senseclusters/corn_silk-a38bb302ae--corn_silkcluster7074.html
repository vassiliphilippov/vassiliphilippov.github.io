corn silk

noun
silky threads from an ear of corn
Pronunciation: / kɔrn sɪlk / (AmE), / kɔːn sɪlk / (BrE)
The long, silky threads that grow at the tip of an ear of corn.

Examples

The corn silk threads
Before cooking, she carefully removed all the corn silk from each cob.
Long strands of corn silk peeked out from the top of the fresh ears at the market.
The herbal tea was brewed from dried corn silk collected at harvest time.
