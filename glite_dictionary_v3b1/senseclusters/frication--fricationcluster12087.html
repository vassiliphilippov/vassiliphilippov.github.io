frication

noun
sound made by friction
9.8
Pronunciation: /frɪ.kə.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /frɪ.kə.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the production of a speech sound by forcing air through a narrow passage, creating audible friction

Examples

Frication in speech
The frication in the 's' sound is easy to hear in the word 'snake'.
Linguists study frication to understand how different languages use friction sounds.
Children often learn to produce frication correctly as their speech develops.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fricative - a consonant sound produced by frication
  • antonym: plosive - a speech sound produced by stopping and then releasing airflow
