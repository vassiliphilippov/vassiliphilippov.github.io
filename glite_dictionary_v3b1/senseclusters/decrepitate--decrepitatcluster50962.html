decrepitate

verb
crackle or burst noisily when heated
9.9 9.6
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈkrep.ɪ.teɪt/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈkrep.ɪ.teɪt/ (BrE)
to crackle or burst noisily when heated, especially when heating salts or minerals

Examples

To decrepitate salts
As the salt began to decrepitate, a series of tiny pops filled the laboratory.
During the experiment, he watched the mineral decrepitate in the flame.
When strongly heated, the crystals decrepitate and split apart noisily.
Heating the mineral until it decrepitates is a standard step in the analysis.
The chemist carefully decrepitated the sample to eliminate all moisture.
When salts are decrepitated, they emit a characteristic crackling noise.
