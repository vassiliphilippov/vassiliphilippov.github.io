sheet metal

noun
thin, flat piece of metal
Pronunciation: / ʃit ˈmɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ʃiːt ˈmɛtl̩ / (BrE)
Metal formed into thin, flat pieces, typically used in construction and manufacturing.

Examples

Sheet metal parts
Air ducts in buildings are often made from sheet metal due to its strength and flexibility.
He learned how to cut and bend sheet metal at the technical college.
The roof was repaired using galvanized sheet metal for added durability.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: foil - thin sheet of metal, much thinner than sheet metal
  • degree higher: plate - a sheet of metal thicker than sheet metal
