contravariant

adjective
opposite transformation
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːntrəˈvɛriənt / (AmE), / ˌkɒntrəˈvɛəriənt / (BrE)
describing a quantity or object that changes in the opposite way to another when the basis or coordinate system is changed

Examples

Contravariant vector
A contravariant vector reverses its direction when the coordinate axes are inverted.
In mathematics, contravariant components transform differently from covariant components under a change of basis.
The concept of contravariant tensors is important in advanced physics and engineering.

Related Terms

  • antonym: covariant - changing in the same way as another quantity under a change of basis
