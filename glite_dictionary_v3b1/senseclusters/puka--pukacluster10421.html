puka

noun
New Zealand tree with large leaves
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpuːkə / (AmE), / ˈpuːkə / (BrE)
a small New Zealand tree (Meryta sinclairii) with large, glossy, resinous leaves and clusters of greenish-white flowers

Examples

New Zealand puka tree
The puka thrives in sheltered coastal areas due to its sensitivity to frost.
The glossy leaves of the puka make it a popular choice for subtropical gardens.
We saw a rare puka in flower during our hike in northern New Zealand.
