by the way

adverb
introducing a different topic; adding information
Pronunciation: / baɪ ðə weɪ / (AmE), / baɪ ðə weɪ / (BrE)
used to introduce a new subject or add information that is not directly related to what was being discussed

Examples

By the way I ask
By the way, did you remember to call your sister?
I finished reading the report. By the way, the meeting has been moved to 3 PM.
She handed him his coffee and said, 'by the way, your friend dropped by earlier.'
By the way, did you hear about the new project at work?
By the way, your package arrived this morning.
I saw Sarah yesterday, by the way.

Related Terms

  • synonym: incidentally - used to introduce a new or separate point
