enclave

verb
to isolate by surrounding
7.9
Pronunciation: / ɛnˈkleɪv / (AmE), / ɛnˈkleɪv / (BrE)
to surround or isolate a person, group, or area by placing it within a different community or territory

Examples

To enclave a village
The government enclaved the village, cutting it off from neighboring towns.
During the conflict, the city was enclaved and supplies could not reach the residents.
Authorities enclaved the community, making it difficult for people to leave or enter.

Related Terms

  • antonym: integrate - to combine or bring together into a whole
  • synonym: isolate - to separate from others or keep apart
