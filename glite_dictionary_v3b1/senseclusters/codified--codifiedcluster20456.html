codified

adjective
enacted and written law
9.9 6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊdɪˌfaɪd, ˈkɑːdɪˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈkəʊdɪfaɪd / (BrE)
(of laws or rules) enacted and written down by a legislative authority, especially in the form of statutes or a legal code.

Examples

A codified constitution
A codified constitution clearly outlines the powers of government and the rights of citizens.
Many countries rely on codified laws rather than unwritten traditions.
The legal reforms resulted in a set of codified criminal statutes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: statutory - required or permitted by law; relating to statutes
  • antonym: uncodified - not written down or formalized into a code
