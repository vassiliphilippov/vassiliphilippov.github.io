look out on

verb
have a view of; face (place or scene)
Pronunciation: / lʊk aʊt ɑn / (AmE), / lʊk aʊt ɒn / (BrE)
(of a building, room, etc.) to face or have a view of (a particular place or scene)

Examples

To look out on a street
His office looked out on a busy street, which made it hard to concentrate sometimes.
My bedroom looks out on the garden, so I wake up to a lovely view every morning.
The hotel restaurant looks out on the bay, making dinner especially scenic at sunset.
