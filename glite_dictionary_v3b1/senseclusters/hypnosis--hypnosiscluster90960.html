hypnosis

noun
artificially induced trance-like state
Pronunciation: / hɪpˈnoʊsɪs / (AmE), / hɪpˈnəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A state of focused attention and increased suggestibility, often resembling sleep, that is artificially induced by suggestion.

Examples

Under hypnosis treatment
During hypnosis, she recalled memories from her early childhood.
Many people remain skeptical about whether hypnosis can really change behavior.
The therapist used hypnosis to help her patient overcome his fear of flying.

Related Terms

  • synonym: trance - a state of mind in which someone is not fully conscious and aware of the world around them
