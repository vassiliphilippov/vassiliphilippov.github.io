toss-up

noun
uncertain situation; equal chance outcome
2.5
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑs ˌʌp /; / ˈtɑs.ʌp /; / ˈtɑːs.ʌp / (AmE), / ˈtɒs ˌʌp /; / ˈtɒs.ʌp / (BrE)
a situation where the outcome is uncertain and either of two results is equally likely

Examples

A toss-up situation
It's a toss-up who will win the championship game this year.
The election was such a toss-up that the outcome surprised everyone.
Whether we'll get rain or sunshine tomorrow is really a toss-up.
It's a toss-up who will become the next chairman after the debates.
The election was such a toss-up that no one could confidently predict the winner.
Whether the weather will clear up in time for the game is still a toss-up.
It's a toss-up who will win the Best Actor award this year.
The weather is so unpredictable that planning a picnic is always a toss-up.
Whether we get the contract or not is really a toss-up at this point.

Related Terms

  • antonym: certainty - something that is sure to happen
  • synonym: coin flip - the action of tossing a coin to decide between two possibilities
  • synonym: coin toss - the act of throwing a coin to decide something randomly
  • synonym: fifty-fifty - used to mean that the odds for two possible outcomes are equal
