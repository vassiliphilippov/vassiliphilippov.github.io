bone age

noun
measure of skeletal development by X-ray
Pronunciation: / boʊn eɪdʒ / (AmE), / bəʊn eɪdʒ / (BrE)
the stage of skeletal development in a person, assessed by comparing X-rays of bones to standards for chronological age

Examples

Bone age assessment
A child with delayed bone age may need further testing to determine the underlying cause.
Pediatricians often use a bone age assessment to evaluate growth disorders in children.
The doctor's report stated that her bone age was significantly advanced for her chronological age.
