impersonalization

noun
making language impersonal
9.3
Pronunciation: /ɪm.pɜːr.sən.əl.aɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɪm.pɜː.sən.əl.aɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the use or creation of impersonal grammatical constructions so that a sentence does not specify a particular subject or agent

Examples

Impersonalization of sentences
The impersonalization of instructions can make them sound more objective and less direct.
Academic writing often relies on impersonalization to focus on facts rather than the writer.
In some languages, impersonalization is achieved by using passive voice or special verb forms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: passivization - the process of making a verb or sentence passive
  • antonym: personalization - the act of making something personal or specific to a person
