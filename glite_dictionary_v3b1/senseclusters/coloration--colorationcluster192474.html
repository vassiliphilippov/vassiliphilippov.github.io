coloration

noun
musical timbre or tone color
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˌkʌləˈreɪʃən /; / ˌkʌlərˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkʌləˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the character or quality of a musical sound, especially as determined by its timbre or tone color

Examples

Sound coloration
Some amplifiers add their own coloration to the sound, changing how instruments are perceived.
The engineer adjusted the settings to preserve the natural coloration of the orchestra.
The subtle coloration of her voice added warmth to the song's melody.
Different microphones can add a distinctive colouration to the recorded voice.
Some audiophiles prefer headphones with minimal colouration for a more natural listening experience.
The engineer adjusted the filters to reduce the colouration introduced by the speakers.
