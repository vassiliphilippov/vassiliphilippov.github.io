thiol

noun
sulfur-hydrogen compound
Pronunciation: / ˈθaɪˌɑl / (AmE), / ˈθaɪɒl / (BrE)
an organic chemical compound containing a sulfur-hydrogen (–SH) group, often with a strong odor

Examples

Chemists use a thiol to introduce sulfur atoms into organic molecules.
Some thiols are added to natural gas to help detect leaks by smell.

Chemists often detect the presence of a thiol by its strong odor.
