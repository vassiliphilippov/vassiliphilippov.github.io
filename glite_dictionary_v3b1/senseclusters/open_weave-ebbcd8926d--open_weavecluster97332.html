open weave

noun
fabric with widely spaced threads
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊpən wiːv / (AmE), / ˈəʊpən wiːv / (BrE)
a type of fabric weave with widely spaced threads, leaving visible gaps or holes in the fabric

Examples

An open weave fabric
The artisan demonstrated how to create an open weave pattern using basic weaving techniques.
The curtains were made from an open weave that allowed plenty of natural light to filter through.
This linen shirt has an open weave, making it lightweight and breathable for summer weather.

Related Terms

  • antonym: tight weave - a fabric weave with closely packed threads and no visible gaps
