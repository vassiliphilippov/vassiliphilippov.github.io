able to be disproved or challenged, especially by presenting evidence to the contrary.

Examples

A rebuttable presumption
The lawyer argued that the evidence was only a rebuttable presumption, not a final conclusion.
In some cases, the burden of proof is rebuttable by presenting new facts.
A rebuttable assumption means it can be overturned if sufficient evidence is provided.
