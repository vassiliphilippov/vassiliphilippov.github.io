subjunctive

noun
verb mood for wishes or hypotheticals
7.6
Pronunciation: / səbˈdʒʌŋktɪv / (AmE), / səbˈdʒʌŋktɪv / (BrE)
a verb mood used to express wishes, suggestions, demands, or situations that are hypothetical or not factual

Examples

The subjunctive mood
In English, the subjunctive often appears in phrases like 'If I were you'.
The French language uses the subjunctive much more frequently than English.
