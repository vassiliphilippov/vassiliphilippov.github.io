split decision

noun
boxing result where judges disagree
6.0 6.7
Pronunciation: / splɪt dɪˈsɪʒən / (AmE), / splɪt dɪˈsɪʒən / (BrE)
in boxing, a result in which two judges vote for one boxer and the third for the other, so the winner is not decided unanimously

Examples

A split decision in boxing
Fans were shocked by the split decision since most expected a unanimous verdict.
The boxer won the title after a close split decision victory.
The judges reached a split decision, making the outcome highly controversial.
