access time

noun
interval for data retrieval (computing)
4.6 3.9
Pronunciation: /ˈæk.ses taɪm/ (AmE), /ˈæk.ses taɪm/ (BrE)
(Computer science) The amount of time between a request for data by a computer system and the delivery of that data by a storage device.

Examples

Fast access time
An SSD typically offers much faster access time than a traditional HDD.
Engineers are constantly working to minimize access time in memory storage devices.
The new hard drive greatly reduced the access time for opening large files.

Related Terms

  • synonym: seek time - time required for a drive's head to locate the data
