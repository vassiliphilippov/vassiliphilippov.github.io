spondylolisthesis

noun
vertebra slippage (medical)
9.9
Pronunciation: /spɑːn.dɪ.loʊ.lɪs.ˈθiː.sɪs/ (AmE), /spɒn.dɪ.lə.lɪs.ˈθiː.sɪs/ (BrE)
A medical condition in which one vertebra slips forward over the vertebra below it, potentially causing nerve compression.

Examples

Spondylolisthesis diagnosis
After the accident, the doctor diagnosed him with spondylolisthesis in his lower back.
Chronic back pain can sometimes be caused by spondylolisthesis, especially in athletes.
Surgery may be required if spondylolisthesis leads to severe nerve compression.
