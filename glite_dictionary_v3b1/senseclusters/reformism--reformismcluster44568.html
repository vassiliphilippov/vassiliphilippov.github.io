reformism

noun
belief in gradual reform
Pronunciation: / rɪˈfɔrmɪzəm / (AmE), / rɪˈfɔːmɪzəm / (BrE)
The doctrine or belief in pursuing gradual reform rather than abrupt or revolutionary change, especially in social or political systems.

Examples

Reformism in politics
Many political parties advocate reformism as a means to achieve social progress.
The debate between reformism and revolutionism has shaped many political movements throughout history.
Those who support reformism believe in changing society through gradual improvements rather than radical shifts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: revolutionism - doctrine favoring abrupt political or social revolution
