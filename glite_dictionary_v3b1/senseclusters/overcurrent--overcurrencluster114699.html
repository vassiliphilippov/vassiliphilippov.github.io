overcurrent

noun
excess electrical current
7.2
Pronunciation: /ˈoʊ.vər.kɜːr.ənt/ (AmE), /ˈəʊ.və.kɜːr.ənt/ (BrE)
an electrical current that exceeds the safe or normal limit in a circuit

Examples

An overcurrent event
The technician installed a new fuse to protect the system from overcurrent situations.
An overcurrent can damage sensitive electronic components if not controlled properly.
Circuit breakers are designed to trip when they detect an overcurrent in the wiring.

Related Terms

  • synonym: short circuit - an abnormal connection causing excessive current flow
  • antonym: undercurrent - a current less than the normal or expected amount
