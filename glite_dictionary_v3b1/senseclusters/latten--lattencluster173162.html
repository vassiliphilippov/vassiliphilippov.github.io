latten

noun
historical thin sheet of brass or brass-like alloy
Pronunciation: / ˈlætən / (AmE), / ˈlætən / (BrE)
A type of brass or a yellowish alloy resembling brass, hammered into thin sheets; used in the past for making church utensils and other items.

Examples

Sheets of latten
Candlesticks made of latten were common in 15th-century European cathedrals.
Restoration experts discovered fragments of latten while excavating the old church site.
The medieval chalice was crafted from latten and decorated with intricate patterns.
