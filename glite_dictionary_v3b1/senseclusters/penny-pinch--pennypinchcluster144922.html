penny-pinch

verb
spend money very frugally
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛniˌpɪntʃ / (AmE), / ˈpɛniˌpɪntʃ / (BrE)
to spend money very carefully and avoid unnecessary expenses; to be extremely frugal

Examples

To penny-pinch money
He always penny-pinches when it comes to eating out, choosing the cheapest options available.
Her tendency to penny-pinch sometimes annoyed her friends.
They penny-pinched for years in order to afford a new home.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scrimp - to be thrifty or parsimonious, especially to economize severely
  • antonym: splurge - to spend money freely or extravagantly
