pragmatic

adjective
practical; focused on results
2.4
Pronunciation: / præɡˈmætɪk / (AmE), / præɡˈmatɪk / (BrE)
dealing with problems or situations in a practical, sensible, and realistic way rather than by using theory or abstract ideas

Examples

Pragmatic solution
His pragmatic attitude helped the team overcome many unexpected obstacles.
Instead of dreaming of ideal outcomes, the manager remained pragmatic about what was possible within the budget.
She offered a pragmatic solution to the complicated problem, focusing on what could actually be done.
His pragmatic approach to problem-solving helped the team find an effective solution quickly.
Rather than discussing theories all day, she preferred to take a pragmatic stance and get things done.
They admired the mayor's pragmatic leadership during the crisis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: idealistic - guided more by ideals or theory than by practical considerations
  • synonym: practical - concerned with actual use or practice
