scrape along

verb
barely manage to live or survive
Pronunciation: / skreɪp əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / skreɪp əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to manage to live or survive with difficulty, especially when you have very little money or resources

Examples

To scrape along on money
After losing his job, he had to scrape along on odd jobs for a few months.
Despite the tough economy, the family managed to scrape along without taking on debt.
Many artists scrape along until their work gains recognition.
After losing his job, he had to scrape along on unemployment benefits.
Many small businesses barely scrape along during tough economic times.
They managed to scrape along until things improved financially.

Related Terms

  • synonym: get by - to manage with difficulty to live or accomplish something
  • synonym: scrape by - to manage to survive with very little money or resources
  • synonym: struggle - to make strenuous or labored efforts to survive or cope
  • antonym: thrive - to prosper or flourish, especially financially or in well-being
