French loaf

noun
long, thin bread with a crisp crust
Pronunciation: / fɹɛntʃ loʊf / (AmE), / fɹɛntʃ ləʊf / (BrE)
a long, thin loaf of white bread with a crisp crust, originally made in France; a baguette

Examples

A French loaf bread
A crusty French loaf is perfect for making garlic bread.
He bought a fresh French loaf from the bakery for breakfast.
They sliced the French loaf and served it with cheese and wine.

Related Terms

  • synonym: baguette - a long, narrow loaf of French bread
