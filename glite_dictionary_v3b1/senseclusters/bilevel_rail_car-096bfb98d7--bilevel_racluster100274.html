bilevel rail car

noun
rail car with two levels of passenger seating
6.8 4.2
Pronunciation: /ˈbaɪ.lev.əl reɪl kɑːr/ /; / /ˈbaɪ.lə.vəl reɪl kɑːr/ (AmE), /ˈbaɪ.lev.əl reɪl kɑː/ /; / /ˈbaɪ.lə.vəl reɪl kɑː/ (BrE)
a type of train carriage or rail car with two levels of seating for passengers, designed to increase capacity

Examples

A bilevel rail car design
A bilevel rail car can carry almost twice as many passengers as a standard single-level car.
Commuters preferred the new bilevel rail car because it provided more seats during rush hour.
The city upgraded its fleet with bilevel rail cars to accommodate the growing number of travelers.
