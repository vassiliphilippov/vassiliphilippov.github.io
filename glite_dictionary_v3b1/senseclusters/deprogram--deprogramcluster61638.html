deprogram

verb
to erase settings
6.3
Pronunciation: /diː.ˈproʊ.græm/ /; / /dɪ.ˈproʊ.græm/ (AmE), /diː.ˈprəʊ.græm/ /; / /dɪ.ˈprəʊ.græm/ (BrE)
to remove or erase a program, settings, or pre-programmed instructions from a machine or electronic device

Examples

To deprogram a device
The engineer had to deprogram the security system before installing the new software.
If the device malfunctions, you may need to deprogram it and start over.
She carefully deprogrammed the old settings from the remote control to avoid interference.
