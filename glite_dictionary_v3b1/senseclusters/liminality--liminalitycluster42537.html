liminality

noun
state of transition
Pronunciation: / ˌlɪməˈnæləti / (AmE), / ˌlɪmɪˈnælɪti / (BrE)
the state of being in between two different stages, conditions, or places, often during a transition or period of uncertainty

Examples

A liminality period
Graduation marked a period of liminality between school and adult life.
The artist explored themes of liminality in her work, focusing on moments of change.
Traveling alone in a new country gave him a sense of liminality and self-discovery.
Graduating from university left her in a period of liminality as she searched for her first job.
The novel explores the liminality of adolescence, where characters are neither children nor adults.
During the move to a new country, he felt a strong sense of liminality and uncertainty about the future.
The abandoned playground at dusk had a feeling of liminality that made it both fascinating and unsettling.
Graduation is a time of liminality, when students are no longer children but not yet fully adults.
Artists often explore liminality in their work, focusing on moments of change and uncertainty.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ambiguity - the quality of being open to more than one interpretation
  • antonym: certainty - the state of being sure or confident
  • antonym: stability - the state of being steady and unchanging
  • synonym: transition - the process of changing from one state or condition to another
