ineffability

noun
impossibility to express
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˌnɛfəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ɪˌnɛfəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being impossible to express or describe fully in words

Examples

The ineffability of love
The ineffability of true love makes it difficult to capture in poetry or prose.
Many spiritual experiences are described as having a sense of ineffability.
Artists often struggle with the ineffability of emotions they wish to convey through their work.
The ineffability of the sunset made it impossible for her to describe the scene.
Many poets try to capture the ineffability of love in their verses.
He was moved by the ineffability of the music, which seemed beyond words.
The ineffability of her emotions left her unable to explain how she truly felt.
Many poets try to capture the ineffability of beauty in their verses.
The ineffability of the tragedy made it difficult for anyone to speak about it openly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: expressibility - the ability to be expressed in words
  • synonym: indescribability - the state of being impossible to describe
