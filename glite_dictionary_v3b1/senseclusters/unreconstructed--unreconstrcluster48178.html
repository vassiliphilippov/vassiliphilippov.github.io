unreconstructed

adjective
holding outdated attitudes or beliefs
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnˌriːkənˈstrʌktəd / (AmE), / ˌʌnriːkənˈstrʌktɪd / (BrE)
holding attitudes or beliefs considered outdated or no longer appropriate, and unwilling to change them.

Examples

An unreconstructed attitude
Even after decades of social change, his views were stubbornly unreconstructed.
She remains an unreconstructed supporter of the old regime, despite overwhelming criticism.
The novel portrays an unreconstructed aristocrat struggling to adapt to the modern world.

Related Terms

  • antonym: progressive - favoring or implementing social reform or new ideas
  • synonym: reactionary - opposing political or social progress or reform
