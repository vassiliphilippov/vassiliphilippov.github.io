(in Internet policy) the principle that internet service providers should treat all data and online services equally, without blocking, slowing (throttling), or giving paid priority to particular content.

Examples

Internet neutrality
Regulators debated neutrality rules to ensure equal treatment of online content across providers.
They campaigned for net neutrality to prevent internet service providers from blocking popular websites.
The company argued that strict neutrality regulations would reduce investment in network upgrades.

Related Terms

  • synonym: net neutrality - the same principle concerning equal treatment of Internet traffic; common multiword name for the concept
  • synonym: network neutrality - an alternative term for the policy principle that networks should not discriminate among data types or sources
  • antonym: paid prioritization - the practice of giving faster or better network access to data from parties that pay, often cited as opposite of neutrality
