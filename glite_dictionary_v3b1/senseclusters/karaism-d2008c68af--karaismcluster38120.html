Karaism

noun
Jewish movement rejecting oral tradition
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkærəˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈkɑːrəˌɪzəm / (BrE)
A Jewish religious movement that rejects oral tradition as a source of divine law, accepting only the Hebrew Bible as authoritative.

Examples

Karaism religious movement
Scholars have studied the influence of Karaism on medieval Jewish philosophy and literature.
The history of Karaism offers a unique perspective on the diversity of Jewish belief and practice.
Unlike Rabbinic Judaism, Karaism relies solely on the Hebrew Bible for religious law.
