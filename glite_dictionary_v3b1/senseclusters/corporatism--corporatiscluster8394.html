corporatism

noun
control by large interest groups
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔːrpərəˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈkɔːpərətɪzəm / (BrE)
the control of a state or organization by large interest groups, especially corporations, rather than individuals or the state alone

Examples

Corporatism in politics
Some critics argue that corporatism undermines democracy by giving too much power to private interests.
The policy changes reflect a shift away from pure capitalism toward corporatism.
The rise of corporatism shaped the politics of many twentieth-century European countries.

Related Terms

  • antonym: individualism - the belief in the primary importance of the individual
