(computing) a packet-writing standard for writable optical discs that enables drag-and-drop (background) writing, defect management, and improved compatibility (often abbreviated MRW).

Examples

Mount Rainier support
Many computers gained support for Mount Rainier to allow drag-and-drop burning on writable CDs.
After inserting the disc, the system automatically used Mount Rainier formatting to write files in the background.
Technicians still mention Mount Rainier when explaining packet-writing standards for optical media.

Related Terms

  • synonym: MRW - abbreviation commonly used for the Mount Rainier packet-writing standard
  • synonym: packet writing - a method of writing data to optical discs in small packets to allow random-access-style use (enables drag-and-drop)
