oxygen acid

noun
acid containing oxygen atoms
8.7 5.3
Pronunciation: /ˈɑːk.sə.dʒən ˈæs.ɪd/ /; / /ˈɑːk.sɪ.dʒən ˈæs.ɪd/ (AmE), /ˈɒk.sɪ.dʒən ˈæs.ɪd/ /; / /ˈɒk.sə.dʒən ˈæs.ɪd/ (BrE)
An acid that contains oxygen, especially one in which oxygen is bonded to another element as part of the acid's structure.

Examples

Oxygen acid compound
Some mineral acids, like nitric acid and sulfuric acid, are classified as oxygen acids.
The difference between a binary acid and an oxygen acid lies in the presence of oxygen atoms.
When dissolved in water, an oxygen acid often forms hydronium ions and a conjugate base.
