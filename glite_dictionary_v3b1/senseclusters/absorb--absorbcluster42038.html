absorb

verb
to take in or soak up; assimilate
1.9 2.5
Pronunciation: /əb.ˈzɔːr/ /; / /æb.ˈzɔːr/ (AmE), /əb.ˈzɔːb/ /; / /əb.ˈsɔːb/ (BrE)
to take in or soak up a substance (such as liquid, energy, or information), or to incorporate ideas or influences

Examples

To absorb water
Plants absorb sunlight during the process of photosynthesis.
She tried to absorb all the details of the painting during her visit.
The towel will absorb most of the spilled juice if you act quickly.
Children absorb languages more easily when they are young.
It usually takes time to absorb new information and make it your own.
She quickly absorbed the details of the complex case.
Dark colors tend to absorb more heat from sunlight than lighter ones.
She quickly absorbed the information presented in the meeting.
This sponge can absorb a large amount of water in seconds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assimilate - to take in and fully understand or integrate information or ideas
  • antonym: emit - to release or give off (opposite of absorb)
  • antonym: forget - to fail to remember information or ideas
  • synonym: imbibe - to absorb or take in (especially liquids)
  • synonym: incorporate - to combine or include as part of something
  • synonym: integrate - to combine with another to form a whole
  • antonym: release - to let go or allow to escape
  • antonym: separate - to set or keep apart, not combining into a whole
  • synonym: soak up - to take in a liquid, especially through a surface or material
  • synonym: understand - to grasp the meaning of something mentally
