maquis

noun
French WWII resistance guerrilla fighter
Pronunciation: / maˈki / (AmE), / maˈki / (BrE)
a member of the French underground resistance movement during World War II, especially one fighting as a guerrilla against the occupying forces

Examples

French maquis fighter
During the German occupation, he joined the maquis to fight for France's liberation.
Her grandfather was a maquis who aided Allied pilots in escaping occupied territory.
Many maquis operated in remote rural areas, using the land's cover to their advantage.
