choke

verb
reduce air supply, especially in engines
5.7 4.3
Pronunciation: /tʃoʊk/ (AmE), /tʃəʊk/ (BrE)
to reduce or restrict the flow of air, especially in an engine or mechanical device

Examples

To choke an engine
Before starting the cold engine, you need to choke the carburetor to ensure a rich fuel mixture.
If you choke the engine too much, it won't start properly.
The mechanic had to choke the air intake temporarily during maintenance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: strangle - to prevent something from getting enough air or from operating normally
  • antonym: unblock - to remove an obstruction; to clear the passage
