lighter-than-air craft

noun
aircraft supported by buoyancy, e.g. balloons, airships
3.0 4.8
Pronunciation: /ˈlaɪ.tər ðən er kræft/ /; / /ˈlaɪ.tər ðæn er kræft/ (AmE), /ˈlaɪ.tə ðən eə krɑːft/ /; / /ˈlaɪ.tə ðən eə kræft/ (BrE)
an aircraft, such as a balloon or airship, that is supported in the air by its own buoyancy rather than by aerodynamic lift

Examples

A lighter-than-air craft balloon
A hot air balloon is a classic example of a lighter-than-air craft that can float gently across the sky.
Developing a new lighter-than-air craft requires careful study of buoyancy and gas containment.
Zeppelins, a type of lighter-than-air craft, were once used for passenger travel and military observation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: airship - a powered lighter-than-air craft; dirigible
  • antonym: heavier-than-air craft - aircraft supported by aerodynamic lift, like airplanes
