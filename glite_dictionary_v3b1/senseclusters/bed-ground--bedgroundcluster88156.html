bed-ground

noun
land for livestock to sleep overnight
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛdˌɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈbɛdˌɡraʊnd / (BrE)
an area of land where a group of cattle or sheep settle to sleep for the night

Examples

A bed-ground for sheep
After a long day on the trail, the sheep gathered together at the bed-ground.
Coyotes had been spotted near the bed-ground, so the herders stayed alert through the night.
The rancher checked the bed-ground before sunrise to make sure the cattle were all accounted for.
