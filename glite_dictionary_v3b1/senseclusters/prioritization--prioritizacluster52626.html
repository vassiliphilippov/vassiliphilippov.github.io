prioritization

noun
arranging by importance
Pronunciation: / praɪˌɔːrətəˈzeɪʃən /; / praɪˌɔrətəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / praɪˌɒrɪtaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of deciding which tasks or items are most important and dealing with them first

Examples

Effective prioritization process
Effective prioritization helps managers focus on the most urgent tasks first.
The team's prioritization of customer feedback led to rapid improvements in the product.
Without clear prioritization, important projects may be delayed or overlooked.
Effective prioritization helps managers focus on the most important projects first.
The software uses prioritization to ensure urgent messages are delivered quickly.
Without proper prioritization, teams may waste time on less critical tasks.
Prioritization is essential for effective time management in both work and personal life.
Good prioritization helps students focus on the most important assignments first.
Without proper prioritization, it is easy to become overwhelmed by too many tasks.
