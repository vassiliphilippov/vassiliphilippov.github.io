coronary insufficiency

noun
inadequate blood supply to the heart
9.7 4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrəˌneri ɪnˌsʌfəˈʃɛnsi / (AmE), / ˈkɒr.ən.əri ɪnˌsʌf.ɪˈʃɛn.si / (BrE)
Inadequate blood flow to the muscles of the heart, which may result in symptoms such as chest pain (angina pectoris).

Examples

Coronary insufficiency symptoms
Early detection of coronary insufficiency can help prevent more serious cardiac events.
The patient was diagnosed with coronary insufficiency after experiencing severe chest pain during exercise.
Treatment for coronary insufficiency often includes lifestyle changes and medication.

Related Terms

  • synonym: angina pectoris - chest pain caused by reduced blood flow to the heart
