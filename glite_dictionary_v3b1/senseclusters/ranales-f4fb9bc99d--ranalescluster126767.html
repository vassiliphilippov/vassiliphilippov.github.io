Ranales

noun
historical order of flowering plants
9.9
Pronunciation: / ræˈneɪliːz / (AmE), / ræˈneɪliːz /; / rəˈneɪliːz / (BrE)
a former order of flowering plants (including families like Ranunculaceae and Magnoliaceae) used in older botanical classification systems

Examples

The Ranales order
Many older textbooks refer to water lilies as members of the Ranales.
The family Magnoliaceae was traditionally classified under the order Ranales.
With advances in plant taxonomy, Ranales has largely been replaced by newer classification systems.
Recent revisions in plant taxonomy have largely replaced the use of Ranales with more modern groupings.
Some botanists still refer to the Ranales when describing relationships among certain plant families.
The family Magnoliaceae was once classified under the Ranales order in traditional taxonomy.
