stump

noun
part left after removal of limb or tooth
9.9 2.6
Pronunciation: / stʌmp / (AmE), / stʌmp / (BrE)
the part of something (such as a tree, limb, or tooth) that remains after the rest has been cut or removed

Examples

Tree stump
After the surgery, the patient had difficulty adjusting to the stump of his leg.
Care must be taken to clean the stump daily to prevent any complications.
The dentist explained that the stump of the tooth needed to be removed to avoid infection.
After the tree was cut down, only a stump remained in the yard.
Moss grew thickly over the stump in the forest clearing.
The children sat on the old stump and watched the sunset.

Related Terms

  • synonym: residue - what is left after a part is removed
  • degree lower: shoot - a new growth from a plant or tree
  • degree higher: trunk - the main woody stem of a tree
