ciborium

noun
church bread container
9.9
Pronunciation: / sɪˈbɔriəm / (AmE), / sɪˈbɔːriəm / (BrE)
a covered container used in Christian churches to hold the consecrated bread during the Eucharist

Examples

A ciborium for bread
The golden ciborium was placed on the altar during the ceremony.
After Communion, the deacon carefully covered the ciborium and returned it to the tabernacle.
The church donated a new ciborium to replace the old, damaged one.
The priest placed the consecrated hosts in the ciborium after the service.
A golden ciborium stood on the altar, ready for the communion ceremony.
The church carefully cleaned the ciborium before each mass.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chalice - a cup used to hold wine during the Eucharist
  • synonym: pyx - a small container for carrying the consecrated host
  • synonym: tabernacle - a fixed, locked box in which the Eucharist is stored
