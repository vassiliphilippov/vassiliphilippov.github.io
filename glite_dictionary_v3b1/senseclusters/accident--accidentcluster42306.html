accident

noun
unexpected mishap causing harm or damage
2.2
Pronunciation: / ˈæksəˌdɛnt / (AmE), / ˈæksɪdənt / (BrE)
An unexpected event, especially one that causes damage, injury, or harm.

Examples

A car accident
She broke her arm in a skiing accident last winter.
The glass fell off the table by accident, so nobody was really to blame.
There was a terrible accident on the highway involving several cars.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: disaster - a sudden event that causes great damage or loss
  • degree lower: incident - an event or occurrence, often minor or insignificant
  • antonym: intentional - done on purpose, not accidental
  • synonym: mishap - an unlucky or unfortunate accident
