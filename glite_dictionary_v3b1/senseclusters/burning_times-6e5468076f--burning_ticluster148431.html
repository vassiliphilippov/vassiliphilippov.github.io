burning times

noun
period of witch hunt persecution
Pronunciation: / ˈbɝːnɪŋ taɪmz / (AmE), / ˈbɜːnɪŋ taɪmz / (BrE)
A historical period, especially in Europe, marked by widespread persecution and execution of alleged witches, often by burning at the stake.

Examples

The burning times period
Many historians regard the burning times as a tragic episode of mass hysteria.
Some modern pagans commemorate those who suffered during the burning times.
The fear and suspicion of the burning times had long-lasting effects on society.

Related Terms

  • synonym: witch hunt - campaign to find and punish supposed witches
