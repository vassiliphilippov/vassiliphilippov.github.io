claque

noun
group hired to applaud at performances
Pronunciation: / klæk / (AmE), / klæk / (BrE)
A group of people hired to applaud or show enthusiastic support at a performance, often to create the impression of popularity.

Examples

A claque group
In 19th-century Paris, it was common for a claque to be present at the opera.
Rumors spread that the politician's supporters were nothing but a claque hired for the rally.
The theater company secretly paid a claque to ensure a standing ovation each night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: applaud - to show approval by clapping hands
