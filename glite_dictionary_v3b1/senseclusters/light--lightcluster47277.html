light

noun
device emitting signal or warning
3.2 1.0
Pronunciation: /laɪt/ (AmE), /laɪt/ (BrE)
a device that produces light, such as a lamp, bulb, or traffic signal

Examples

Traffic light
Every intersection on the main street has a light to control the flow of traffic.
Suddenly, a flashing red light appeared on the dashboard, indicating a problem with the engine.
The ship's captain spotted a light in the distance, warning of dangerous rocks ahead.
Do you happen to have a light with you?
He asked the stranger for a light to ignite his cigarette.
My light went out, so I couldn't start the campfire.
She turned on the light in the hallway so she could see.
The lights in the office automatically switch off at night.
We bought a new ceiling light for the living room.
