Anomala orientalis

noun
a beetle species harmful to grasses
Pronunciation: / əˈnɑː.mələ ɔːˌriːənˈteɪlɪs / (AmE), / əˈnɒ.mələ ɔːˌrɪənˈteɪlɪs / (BrE)
A species of beetle native to Asia and introduced to the United States; its larvae feed on the roots of sugarcane and other grasses.

Examples

Anomala orientalis beetle
Farmers are concerned about the spread of Anomala orientalis in their fields.
Some entomologists study Anomala orientalis to understand invasive species in agriculture.
The larvae of Anomala orientalis can cause significant damage to sugarcane crops.
