muddied

adjective
color dulled or discolored by impurities
6.4 4.1
Pronunciation: /ˈmʌd.iːd/ /; / /ˈmʌd.ɪd/ (AmE), /ˈmʌd.ɪd/ /; / /ˈmʌd.iːd/ (BrE)
(of a color) made less bright or clear due to impurities or mixing with other substances; not pure or vivid.

Examples

Muddied brown color
After the storm, the river took on a muddied brown hue.
The artist mixed too many pigments, resulting in a muddied shade on the canvas.
With time, the bright blue curtains had faded to a muddied gray.
