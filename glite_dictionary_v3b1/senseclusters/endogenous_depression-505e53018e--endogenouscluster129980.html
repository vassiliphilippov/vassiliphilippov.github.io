endogenous depression

noun
depression without apparent external cause
7.3 7.8
Pronunciation: / ɛnˈdoʊdʒənəs dɪˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / ɛnˈdɒdʒɪnəs dɪˈprɛʃən / (BrE)
A type of depression thought to arise from internal or biological factors, without an obvious external cause.

Examples

Endogenous depression symptoms
Some patients with endogenous depression respond better to medication than to psychotherapy.
The psychiatrist diagnosed her condition as endogenous depression after ruling out recent significant life stressors.
Unlike reactive depression, endogenous depression is believed to stem primarily from biological or genetic factors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clinical depression - a general term for major depressive disorder
  • antonym: reactive depression - a type of depression resulting from external events or stress
