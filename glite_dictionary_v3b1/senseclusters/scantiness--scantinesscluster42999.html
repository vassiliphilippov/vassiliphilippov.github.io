scantiness

noun
the quality of being meager or insufficient
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈskæn.ti.nəs / (AmE), / ˈskæn.ti.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being insufficient in quantity or amount; meagerness.

Examples

Scantiness of food
Her dress attracted attention because of its scantiness.
The scantiness of available food made the journey extremely difficult for the explorers.
The scantiness of the evidence made it hard for the jury to reach a decision.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abundance - a very large quantity of something
  • synonym: meagerness - the state of being deficient in quantity or quality
