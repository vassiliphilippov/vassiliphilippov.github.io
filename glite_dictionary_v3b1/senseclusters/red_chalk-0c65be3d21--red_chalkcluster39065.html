red chalk

noun
reddish-brown chalk for drawing
Pronunciation: / rɛd tʃɔk / (AmE), / rɛd tʃɔːk / (BrE)
A reddish-brown chalk, often resembling dried blood, used especially for drawing and artistic purposes.

Examples

A piece of red chalk
He found a small piece of red chalk among his grandmother's old sketching supplies.
Renaissance drawings often feature the subtle tones made possible by red chalk.
The artist created a delicate portrait using red chalk on ivory paper.
