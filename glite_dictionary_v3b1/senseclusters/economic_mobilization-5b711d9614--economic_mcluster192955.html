economic mobilization

noun
organizing economy for a specific purpose
5.3 5.5
Pronunciation: / ɪˌkɑnəmɪk ˌmoʊbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ɪˌkɑnəmɪk ˌmoʊbələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / iːˌkɒnəmɪk ˌməʊbɪlaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / iˌkɒnəmɪk ˌməʊbɪlaɪˈzeɪʃn / (BrE)
the act or process of organizing and directing a country's economic resources towards a specific goal, especially in preparation for war or national emergency

Examples

Economic mobilization plan
Government leaders discussed strategies for economic mobilization in times of crisis.
Rapid economic mobilization helped the nation recover after the natural disaster.
The country began economic mobilization to support its military efforts during the conflict.
During World War II, the economic mobilisation of many countries led to rapid industrial growth.
Effective economic mobilisation requires cooperation between government, industries, and the workforce.
Governments may use economic mobilisation strategies in response to natural disasters or military threats.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mobilization - the act of assembling and readying troops and supplies for war
