optic disk

noun
point on retina where optic nerve enters
6.4 5.7
Pronunciation: /ˈɑːp.tɪk dɪsk/ (AmE), /ˈɒp.tɪk dɪsk/ (BrE)
the circular area on the retina where the optic nerve enters the eye and which lacks light-sensitive cells; sometimes called the blind spot

Examples

The optic disk area
During the eye exam, the doctor closely inspected the optic disk for signs of disease.
Swelling of the optic disk can indicate increased pressure inside the skull.
The optic disk is also known as the blind spot because it lacks photoreceptor cells.
Because the optic disc lacks photoreceptors, it is also known as the blind spot.
Changes in the appearance of the optic disc can indicate increased pressure inside the skull.
The ophthalmologist examined the optic disc for any signs of swelling or damage.
