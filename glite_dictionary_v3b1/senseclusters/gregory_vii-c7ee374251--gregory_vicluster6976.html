Gregory VII

proper noun
Italian pope, church reformer (1020–1085)
Pronunciation: / ˌɡrɛɡəri səˈvɛnθ / (AmE), / ˌɡrɛɡəri səˈvɛnθ / (BrE)
(1020–1085) Italian pope known for asserting papal authority over the Church and the state, initiating reforms that strengthened the position of the papacy.

Examples

Gregory VII pope
Gregory VII played a key role in the medieval struggle between the papacy and secular rulers.
Historians often discuss Gregory VII when analyzing the Investiture Controversy.
The reforms of Gregory VII had a lasting impact on the structure of the Catholic Church.
