pop off

verb
(informal) die; pass away
Pronunciation: / pɑp ɑf / (AmE), / pɒp ɒf / (BrE)
(informal) To die, usually used humorously or euphemistically.

Examples

To pop off suddenly
Everyone was shocked when the main character suddenly popped off halfway through the movie.
He joked that he wanted to travel the world before he popped off.
My old computer finally popped off after years of faithful service.
