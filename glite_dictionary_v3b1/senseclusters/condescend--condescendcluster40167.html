condescend

verb
act in a patronizing or superior manner
7.8 0.23/m
Pronunciation: / Àåk…ëÀên.d…ôÀàs…õnd /; / Àåk…ëÀênd…™Ààsend / (AmE), / Àåk…ín.d…™Ààsend /; / Àåk…índ…™Ààsend / (BrE)
to act as if you are superior to others and treat them in a patronizing way

Examples

To condescend to others
It is offensive when people condescend to others based on their intelligence or experience.
Please do not condescend to me as if I do not understand basic math.
She tends to condescend when explaining things to her coworkers, which annoys them.
Although she was the expert, she never condescends to her students.
It irritated him when people condescended to explain basic concepts to him.
She refused to condescend to her colleagues, always treating everyone equally.
