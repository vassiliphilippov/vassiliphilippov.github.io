reciprocality

noun
mutual dependence or influence
Pronunciation: / rɪˌsɪprəˈkæləti / (AmE), / rɪˌsɪprɪˈkælɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being reciprocal; a mutual dependence, action, or influence between parties or things.

Examples

The reciprocality of actions
Effective communication is based on the reciprocality of listening and responding.
International treaties often rely on the principle of reciprocality between nations.
The reciprocality of their friendship was evident in how they always supported one another.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reciprocity - the practice of exchanging things with others for mutual benefit
