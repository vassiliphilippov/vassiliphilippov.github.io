exponent

noun
advocate or leading representative
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈspoʊnənt / (AmE), / ɪkˈspəʊnənt / (BrE)
a person who supports, explains, or is a leading representative of an idea, cause, or movement

Examples

A leading exponent
As an exponent of modern art, he frequently gives public lectures and exhibitions.
She has become a leading exponent of renewable energy in her community.
The book's author is an outspoken exponent of education reform.
He became an enthusiastic exponent of free market principles.
She is a leading exponent of the new environmental philosophy.
The documentary featured an exponent who explained the origins of the tradition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: advocate - a person who publicly supports or recommends a particular cause or policy
  • antonym: opponent - a person who disagrees with and argues against a cause or idea
