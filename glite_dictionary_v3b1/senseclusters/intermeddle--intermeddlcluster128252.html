intermeddle

verb
to interfere unnecessarily
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈmɛdəl / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈmɛdəl / (BrE)
to interfere in matters that do not concern oneself, often in an unwanted or unnecessary way

Examples

To intermeddle in disputes
It is best not to intermeddle in disputes between colleagues unless asked for help.
She regretted her decision to intermeddle in her friend's personal matters.
The manager warned staff not to intermeddle in other departments' work.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ignore - to pay no attention to something or someone
  • synonym: interfere - to get involved in a situation where you are not wanted
  • synonym: meddle - to become involved in something that is not one's business
