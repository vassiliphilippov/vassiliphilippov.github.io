conceptually

adverb
in terms of concepts or ideas
3.4
Pronunciation: / kənˈsɛptʃuəli / (AmE), / kənˈsɛptjʊəli / (BrE)
In a way that is related to or based on abstract ideas or concepts, rather than physical things.

Examples

To conceptually understand
Conceptually, the theory offers a new way to understand human behavior.
She found it hard to grasp the idea conceptually without visual aids.
The problem is simple conceptually, but difficult to solve in practice.

Related Terms

  • synonym: theoretically - in a way based on theory rather than practice
