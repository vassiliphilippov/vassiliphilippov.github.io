call forth

verb
provoke or evoke a response
5.9 4.0
Pronunciation: /kɔːl fɔːrθ/ /; / /kɑːl fɔːrθ/ (AmE), /kɔːl fɔːθ/ (BrE)
to cause something (such as a reaction, feeling, or effect) to appear or happen

Examples

To call forth feelings
His bold actions called forth widespread admiration from his colleagues.
The leader's speech called forth a passionate response from the audience.
The solemn music seemed to call forth memories of loved ones lost.
Her poem called forth strong emotions from everyone who heard it.
The leader's speech was intended to call forth courage in his followers.
The legend says the ritual can call forth ancient spirits.
