familist

noun
member of religious sect
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfæmɪlɪst / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪlɪst / (BrE)
a member of the Family of Love, a mystical Christian sect active in the 16th and 17th centuries

Examples

A familist preacher
A familist in 16th-century Europe often faced suspicion from both church and state authorities.
The beliefs of a familist emphasized spiritual love and inner faith over outward rituals.
Historians have studied how the familist movement spread quietly across several countries.

Related Terms

  • antonym: heretic - a person holding beliefs contrary to official religious doctrine
  • synonym: sectarian - a member of a religious sect
