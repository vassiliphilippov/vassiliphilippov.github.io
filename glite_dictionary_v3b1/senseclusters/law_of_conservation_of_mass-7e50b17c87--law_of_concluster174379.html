law of conservation of mass

noun

A fundamental principle of classical physics stating that matter cannot be created or destroyed in an isolated system.

Examples

The law of conservation of mass
Because of the law of conservation of mass, scientists know that matter cannot simply disappear during physical or chemical changes.
Teachers often demonstrate the law of conservation of mass with closed-system experiments in the classroom.
The law of conservation of mass is fundamental in chemical reactions, ensuring the mass of reactants equals the mass of products.

Synonyms

  • law of conservation of matter
    4.8 0.01/m
    law of conservation of matter is the formal name using matter as the conserved quantity, while law of conservation of mass uses mass.
  • conservation of mass
    4.0 0.07/m
    conservation of mass states that total mass stays the same in an isolated system, while law of conservation of mass names that principle formally.
  • conservation of matter
    3.9 0.02/m
    conservation of matter says substance cannot be created or destroyed in an isolated system, while law of conservation of mass is the precise physical law.
