overspecialization

noun
excessive narrow focus
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˌspɛʃələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˌspɛʃəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of focusing too narrowly on a single area, skill, or subject, often at the expense of broader abilities or knowledge

Examples

The overspecialization of skills
The overspecialization of medical professionals can sometimes limit their ability to treat complex cases.
In the modern job market, overspecialization may reduce a worker's flexibility and career options.
Some educators warn that overspecialization in schools can hinder students' overall development.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: specialization - the process of concentrating on a specific area or skill
