unsuit

verb
to remove suit; make unfit
9.9
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈsuːt / (AmE), / ʌnˈsuːt / (BrE)
to remove a suit of clothes or to make someone or something unsuitable for a purpose; now rare or archaic

Examples

To unsuit clothes
After the formal dinner, he went upstairs to unsuit and relax.
The harsh climate may unsuit the land for growing delicate crops.
She began to unsuit as soon as she arrived home from the ball.
