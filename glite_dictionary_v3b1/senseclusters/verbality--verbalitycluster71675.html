verbality

noun
excessive wordiness
9.5
Pronunciation: / vərˈbæləti / (AmE), / vɜːˈbælɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of using too many words, especially in speech or writing

Examples

Excessive verbality
The professor's verbality made the lecture difficult to follow for many students.
Editors often reduce verbality to make articles clearer and more engaging.
Her writing style is marked by verbality, which sometimes obscures her main arguments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conciseness - the quality of being brief and to the point
  • synonym: verbosity - the use of more words than needed
