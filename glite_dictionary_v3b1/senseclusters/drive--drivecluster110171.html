drive

noun
the act of moving a herd of animals
Pronunciation: / draɪv / (AmE), / draɪv / (BrE)
an organized effort or campaign to achieve a goal, or the movement of a group of animals

Examples

Charity drive
Cowboys prepared for the spring drive to lead the herd to market.
During the great sheep drive, the animals were herded from the mountains to the valley.
The annual cattle drive lasted several weeks across the plains.
She volunteers in a drive to collect books for underprivileged children.
The company launched a drive to reduce plastic waste across all its offices.
There was a national drive to encourage people to donate blood after the disaster.

Related Terms

  • synonym: campaign - an organized course of action to achieve a goal
  • synonym: movement - a group effort or organized activity to achieve a goal
  • synonym: roundup - the act of gathering together livestock, especially cattle
