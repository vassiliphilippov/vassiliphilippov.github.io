smear

verb
spread a substance over a surface
Pronunciation: / smɪr / (AmE), / smɪə / (BrE)
to spread a substance over a surface in an untidy or uneven way, often making it dirty or marked

Examples

To smear paint
He smears sunscreen on his arms before going out in the sun.
She used a cloth to smear grease all over the bicycle chain.
The children smeared chocolate on the kitchen counter while baking.
I tried not to smear the paint, but it was still wet.
She accidentally smeared mud on her dress while gardening.
The child smeared chocolate all over his face and hands.
Rain smeared the ink, making the writing hard to read.
She accidentally smeared chocolate on her dress while eating.
The child smeared paint all over the paper with his fingers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: daub - to coat or smear a surface with a thick or sticky substance
  • synonym: smudge - to make a blurry or dirty mark
  • synonym: stain - to make something dirty or discolored
