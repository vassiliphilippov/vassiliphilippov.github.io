Nepeta cataria

noun
herb, commonly called catnip
7.0
Pronunciation: / nəˈpiːtə kəˈtɛəriə / (AmE), / nɪˈpiːtə kəˈtɛːrɪə / (BrE)
A perennial herb with aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small white, purple-spotted flowers, commonly known as catnip; valued for its effects on cats and formerly used as a home remedy.

Examples

Nepeta cataria plant
Many gardeners grow Nepeta cataria to attract cats to their yards.
The leaves and flowers of Nepeta cataria have long been used in herbal remedies.
When exposed to Nepeta cataria, most cats react with playful excitement.
