cantering rhythm

noun
cardiac rhythm with extra sound
8.3 8.0
Pronunciation: /ˈkæn.tər.ɪŋ ˈrɪð.əm/ (AmE), /ˈkæn.tər.ɪŋ ˈrɪð.əm/ (BrE)
A cardiac rhythm marked by the presence of an extra heartbeat sound, which may indicate a heart abnormality.

Examples

Heart cantering rhythm
A cantering rhythm is sometimes heard in patients with advanced heart failure.
After the patient complained of fatigue, the cardiologist listened for signs such as a cantering rhythm.
The doctor detected a cantering rhythm during the cardiac examination, suggesting a possible abnormality.
