vena pharyngeus

noun
vein from the pharyngeal plexus
Pronunciation: / ˈviːnə fəˈrɪndʒiˌəs / (AmE), / ˈviːnə fəˈrɪndʒɪˌəs / (BrE)
Any of the veins originating from the pharyngeal plexus that drain into the internal jugular vein.

Examples

The vena pharyngeus vein
During neck surgery, care must be taken to avoid damaging the vena pharyngeus.
Inflammation in the throat can sometimes affect the vena pharyngeus and cause complications.
The vena pharyngeus collects blood from the pharyngeal region and drains into the internal jugular vein.
