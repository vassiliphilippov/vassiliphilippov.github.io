rich

adjective
abundant in desirable qualities or substances
Pronunciation: / rɪtʃ / (AmE), / rɪtʃ / (BrE)
present in large quantities or providing a plentiful supply; abundant or productive

Examples

Rich history
Her writing is rich with cultural references and historical insight.
The region is rich in natural beauty and wildlife.
This area of the world is rich in minerals and other valuable resources.
They discovered a rich deposit of iron ore near the riverbank.
This fuel is a rich mixture, suitable for starting engines in cold weather.
You can tell the soil is rich in nutrients when plants thrive effortlessly.
Farmers benefited from the region's rich soil, which yielded abundant harvests every year.
The country boasts rich deposits of gold and silver in its mountains.
They discovered rich fishing grounds just off the coast.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abundant - existing or available in large quantities; plentiful
  • antonym: barren - (of land) too poor to produce much or any vegetation
  • synonym: concentrated - having a high amount of a substance in a mixture
  • synonym: fertile - (of soil or land) producing or capable of producing abundant vegetation or crops
  • synonym: plentiful - existing in large amounts; more than enough
  • antonym: poor - lacking in quality, value, or worth
  • antonym: scarce - insufficient for the demand; in short supply
  • synonym: valuable - having great value or worth
