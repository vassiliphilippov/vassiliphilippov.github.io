poise

verb
prepare oneself for action
3.9 5.0
Pronunciation: /pɔɪz/ (AmE), /pɔɪz/ (BrE)
to prepare oneself or remain ready and steady just before acting

Examples

To poise for action
He took a deep breath and poised himself to deliver the bad news.
She poised herself at the starting line, ready for the race to begin.
The soldiers poised themselves for the difficult journey ahead.
She poised at the edge of the diving board, gathering her courage.
The dancer poised gracefully before beginning her routine.
The eagle poised in mid-air before swooping down on its prey.
