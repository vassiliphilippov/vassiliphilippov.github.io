noble

adjective
relating to hereditary aristocracy

of or belonging to the hereditary aristocracy, especially as derived from feudal times; relating to people of high social rank by birth or title

Examples

Noble family title
Despite her noble birth, she supported reforms to aid the poor.
He was of noble descent, tracing his family back to medieval lords.
The palace was once home to many noble families.

Synonyms

  • aristocratic
    7.2 3.38/m
    aristocratic describes belonging to the ruling class with their manners and ways, while noble refers to hereditary rank and title.
  • highborn
    7.5 0.11/m
    highborn means born into an upper-class family by blood, while noble emphasizes hereditary rank and formal title.
  • wellborn
    9.1 0.01/m
    wellborn indicates coming from an upper-class family, while noble stresses inherited social rank and legal title.
  • highbred
    7.4 0.01/m
    highbred suggests good breeding and refined manners from an upper-class origin, while noble focuses on birth rank or title.
  • patrician
    8.6 0.68/m
    patrician means refined, polite, and like an upper-class person, while noble specifically names inherited aristocratic rank.
