inarguably

adverb
undisputedly, unquestionably
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈɑːrɡjuəbli / (AmE), / ɪnˈɑːɡjuəbli / (BrE)
In a way that cannot be argued against or disputed; unquestionably.

Examples

Is inarguably true
Shakespeare is inarguably one of the greatest playwrights in history.
The evidence presented in court was inarguably in her favor.
The team's victory was inarguably well-deserved after their impressive performance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: arguably - it may be argued (used for subjective claims)
  • synonym: indisputably - in a way that cannot be challenged or denied
  • antonym: questionably - in a way that may be doubted or disputed
