Examples

Synonyms

  • type I diabetes
    3.5 0.09/m
    type I diabetes is the common clinical term for pancreatic loss of insulin in youth, while insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus is the older full formal name used by doctors and texts.
  • IDDM
    7.7 0.15/m
    IDDM is the medical abbreviation often used in hospital notes and research papers, while insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus is the full phrase used in explanations and formal writing.
  • autoimmune diabetes
    4.5 0.00/m
    autoimmune diabetes highlights the immune system destroying insulin-producing cells over time, while insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus focuses on the need for regular insulin treatment to control blood sugar.
  • juvenile-onset diabetes
    4.1 0.03/m
    juvenile-onset diabetes points to diabetes that starts in childhood or youth with early symptoms, while insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus stresses lifelong insulin therapy to control glucose.
  • juvenile diabetes
    3.2 0.13/m
    juvenile diabetes names diabetes beginning in children or young adults and often treated early with insulin, while insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus underlines the need for regular insulin injections.
