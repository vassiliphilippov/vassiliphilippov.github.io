Arminian

noun
a follower of Arminianism
Pronunciation: / ɑrˈmɪniən / (AmE), / ɑːˈmɪniən / (BrE)
A person who adheres to Arminianism, a Christian theological movement emphasizing free will in salvation.

Examples

An Arminian believer
John identified as an Arminian and often discussed the emphasis on free will in his church.
She studied the writings of early Arminian theologians for her thesis.
The Arminians held a conference to debate theological differences with other groups.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Calvinist - a follower of Calvinism, which contrasts with Arminianism
