mismarry

verb
marry an unsuitable partner
Pronunciation: / mɪsˈmæri / (AmE), / mɪsˈmæri / (BrE)
to marry a person who is unsuitable, especially in the opinion of society or family

Examples

To mismarry someone
Families sometimes fear their children will mismarry due to social or financial differences.
He believed he had mismarried and regretted his decision for years.
She warned her daughter not to mismarry out of desperation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: divorce - legal dissolution of a marriage
