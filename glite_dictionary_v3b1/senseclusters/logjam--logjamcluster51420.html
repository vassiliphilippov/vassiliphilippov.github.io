logjam

noun
a mass of logs blocking a river
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑɡˌdʒæm / (AmE), / ˈlɒɡˌdʒæm / (BrE)
a large pile of logs that blocks the flow of a river or stream

Examples

River logjam
A massive logjam formed after the storm swept loose branches into the current.
The heavy rain caused a logjam that blocked the river for days.
We had to call for help to clear the logjam downstream.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blockage - something that stops movement through an area
  • synonym: obstruction - something that blocks a passage or flow
