backroom

noun
room or setting for private decision-making
2.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌruːm / (AmE), / ˈbæk.ruːm / (BrE)
A place, often figurative, where decisions are made privately by a group of leaders or influential people.

Examples

Backroom deals
Decisions about the merger were made in the backroom away from public scrutiny.
Some accused the council of making backroom deals instead of holding open discussions.
The party's real power lies with its backroom strategists, not the personalities on television.

Related Terms

  • antonym: front room - a room at the front of a building, sometimes indicating openness or public activities
