caseworm

noun
insect larva with a protective case
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪsˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈkeɪswɜːm / (BrE)
an insect larva that constructs a protective case or covering around its body, typically for protection and camouflage

Examples

A caseworm larva
A caseworm constructs its shelter from whatever materials are available in its environment.
Many fish feed on caseworms found on the undersides of rocks in streams.
The caseworm carefully glued bits of sand and leaves to its portable home.

Related Terms

  • synonym: caddisworm - larva of a caddisfly, which also constructs a case
  • synonym: larva - the juvenile form of an insect before metamorphosis
