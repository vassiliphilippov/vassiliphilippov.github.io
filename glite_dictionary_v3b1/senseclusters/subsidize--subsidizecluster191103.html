subsidize

verb
to support financially with a subsidy
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌb.sɪˌdaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsʌb.sɪ.daɪz / (BrE)
to support someone or something financially by giving money, especially as a subsidy, to help an organization, activity, or country continue operating, usually by a government or authority

Examples

To subsidize farmers
Farmers often rely on subsidized loans during difficult growing seasons.
Many public transportation systems are subsidized to keep ticket prices low for commuters.
The government decided to subsidize renewable energy sources to encourage their use.
During the war, the country chose to subsidize its allies' military efforts.
Several nations agreed to subsidize the peacekeeping mission to maintain regional stability.
The government decided to subsidize local farmers to ensure food security during the drought.
Many countries subsidise their national airlines to maintain international routes.
The government plans to subsidise local farmers to help increase food production.
The state agreed to subsidise the construction of new affordable housing units.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sponsor - to support someone or something financially or by providing resources

  • synonym: support - to give assistance to
  • antonym: tax - to impose a financial charge, usually contrasted with giving a subsidy
  • antonym: withdraw - to stop giving support, aid, or resources
  • antonym: withdraw support - to stop providing financial or other assistance
  • antonym: withhold - refuse to give financial support or aid
