plumule

noun
downy feather of young birds
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpluː.mjuːl / (AmE), / ˈpluː.mjuːl / (BrE)
a soft, downy feather of a young bird, sometimes remaining in adult birds

Examples

A bird's plumule
Scientists studied the plumule to understand how birds stay warm after hatching.
Some adult birds retain bits of plumule on their bodies throughout life.
The hatchling was covered in soft plumule instead of mature feathers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: down feather - a fine, soft feather found under the tougher exterior feathers
