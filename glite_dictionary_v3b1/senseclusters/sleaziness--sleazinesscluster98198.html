sleaziness

noun
dishonorable or sordid quality
Pronunciation: / ˈsliːzinəs / (AmE), / ˈsliːzinɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being morally dishonorable, corrupt, or sordid

Examples

Sleaziness in business
He tried to hide the sleaziness of his dealings, but the truth eventually came out.
She was uncomfortable with the sleaziness that surrounded the nightclub business.
The sleaziness of the politician's actions shocked even his loyal supporters.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corruption - dishonest or fraudulent conduct, especially by those in power
  • antonym: honesty - the quality of being honest and free of deceit
