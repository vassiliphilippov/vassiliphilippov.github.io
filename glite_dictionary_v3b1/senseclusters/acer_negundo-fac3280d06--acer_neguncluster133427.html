Acer negundo

noun

A species of maple tree native to eastern and central North America, commonly known as box elder, often planted for shade.

Examples

Botanists classified the box elder as Acer negundo due to its similarities with other maples.
In autumn, the leaves of Acer negundo turn a pale yellow before falling.
The Acer negundo is often planted in parks for its fast growth and shade.
