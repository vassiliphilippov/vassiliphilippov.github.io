discontinue

verb
cease; stop happening or existing
4.2 4.49/m
Pronunciation: / Àåd…™s.k…ônÀàt…™n.ju /; / Àåd…™sk…ônÀàt…™n.ju / (AmE), / Àåd…™s.k…ônÀàt…™n.juÀê /; / Àåd…™sk…ônÀàt…™n.juÀê / (BrE)
to stop doing, providing, or continuing something; to bring to an end

Examples

To discontinue service
Funding for the program will discontinue by the end of the year.
The medication will discontinue if your symptoms improve.
Their weekly meetings are set to discontinue after this month.
The company decided to discontinue their line of sports shoes due to low sales.
They announced plans to discontinue the bus service after next month.
You should not discontinue your medication without consulting your doctor.

Synonyms

  • cease
    3.6 23/m
    cease means to stop happening or being done, while discontinue often means to stop providing or using something.
  • stop
    1.0 539/m
    stop is the common verb for ending an action immediately, while discontinue is more formal and often used for services or products.
  • end
    1.0 299/m
    end means to bring something to a close in any way, while discontinue usually means stopping use or supply.
  • terminate
    4.6 8.77/m
    terminate is a formal word often used in contracts or jobs, while discontinue is used more for products or actions.
  • halt
    2.4 14/m
    halt means to stop movement or progress suddenly, while discontinue means to stop doing or providing something over time.
  • break off
    3.2 3.16/m
    break off means to stop or interrupt something suddenly, while discontinue often means ending a service or long activity.
  • cancel
    1.0 9.36/m
    cancel means to make an event or order no longer valid, while discontinue means to stop providing or continuing something.
  • withdraw
    2.8 26/m
    withdraw means to remove oneself or something from a place or agreement, while discontinue means to stop doing or supplying it.
