encryption

noun
conversion of information into code
3.2
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkrɪpʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈkrɪpʃən / (BrE)
the process of converting data or information into a coded form to prevent unauthorized access

Examples

Encryption of data
Many online services use encryption to protect your personal information from hackers.
Strong encryption makes it nearly impossible for unauthorized users to read the data.
The encryption of emails is now standard practice in many businesses.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decryption - the process of converting coded information back into its original form
