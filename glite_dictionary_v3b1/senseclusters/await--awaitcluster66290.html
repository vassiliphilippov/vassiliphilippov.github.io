Synonyms

  • wait
    1.0 482/m
    wait is a common, everyday verb used for staying or delaying, while await is more formal and often used in writing.
  • look forward to
    2.4 35/m
    look forward to shows eager pleasure about a future event and usually takes an -ing form, while await is more formal.
  • expect
    1.0 267/m
    expect is to believe that something will happen, often without action, while await implies actively waiting for its arrival.
  • anticipate
    3.6 8.30/m
    anticipate often means you expect something with excitement or plan for it, while await focuses on waiting passively.
  • look to
    3.2 9.23/m
    look to often means to rely on or expect someone to act in the future, while await means to wait for something.
