snarl

verb
make or move with a snarling noise
9.7 4.1
Pronunciation: /snɑːrl/ (AmE), /snɑːl/ (BrE)
to make a low, harsh sound or to move while making such a sound, like an animal showing anger or aggression

Examples

Engines snarl loudly
Angry engines snarled through the dark tunnel, startling the hikers.
In the silence, we heard something snarling behind the trees.
The wolves began to snarl when they sensed danger nearby.

Related Terms

  • synonym: growl - make a low, guttural sound, often expressing hostility
  • antonym: whisper - speak very softly using one's breath without vocal cords
