referendum

noun
popular vote on a single political issue
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛfəˈrɛndəm / (AmE), / ˌrɛfəˈrɛndəm / (BrE)
a general vote by the electorate on a single political question that has been referred to them for a direct decision

Examples

National referendum
The decision was postponed until after the national referendum next month.
The government held a referendum to let the people decide on the constitution.
Voters are being asked in a referendum whether to approve the new law.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plebiscite - a direct vote by the electorate on an important public question
