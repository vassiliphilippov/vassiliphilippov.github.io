apraxia

noun
inability to perform purposeful movements
Pronunciation: / əˈpræksiə / (AmE), / əˈpræksiə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by the inability to perform purposeful movements, despite having the desire and physical capacity to do so.

Examples

Apraxia disorder
Apraxia can make it difficult for individuals to use common objects, such as keys or utensils, correctly.
The patient was diagnosed with apraxia after struggling to carry out daily tasks like brushing his teeth.
Therapists worked with children affected by apraxia to help improve their motor planning skills.
