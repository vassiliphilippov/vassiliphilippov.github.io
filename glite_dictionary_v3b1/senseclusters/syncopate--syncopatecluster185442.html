syncopate

verb
omit sound or letter in a word
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪŋ.kəˌpeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋ.kə.peɪt / (BrE)
to omit a sound or letter from the middle of a word

Examples

To syncopate a word
It is common to syncopate words in casual speech, such as 'library' pronounced as 'libry'.
Linguists may study which syllables are typically syncopated in rapid conversation.
Some dialects syncopate certain vowels, making the words shorter and easier to say.

Related Terms

  • synonym: elide - to omit a sound or syllable when speaking
