auxesis

noun
growth by increase in cell size
Pronunciation: / ɔgˈziːsɪs / (AmE), / ɔːgˈziːsɪs / (BrE)
Growth due to an increase in the size of cells without cell division.

Examples

Auxesis in plants
Certain plant tissues exhibit auxesis as their cells grow larger without dividing.
In animals, muscle growth after birth is mainly due to auxesis.
The process of auxesis can be contrasted with growth through cell division, known as hyperplasia.
