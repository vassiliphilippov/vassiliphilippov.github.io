indigenization

noun
transfer to locals
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌdɪdʒənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌdɪdʒənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or policy of transferring ownership, control, or important roles to indigenous or local people, especially in politics or the economy

Examples

Indigenization of jobs
The government promoted indigenization to ensure more local participation in the mining sector.
Critics argued that indigenization could discourage foreign investment in the country.
After independence, many African nations adopted indigenization policies to empower their citizens.

Related Terms

  • antonym: colonization - the act of establishing control over indigenous people or areas
  • synonym: localization - the process of making something local in character or restricting it to a particular place
  • synonym: nationalization - the process of transferring private assets into public ownership
