blip

noun
a brief interruption or minor deviation
Pronunciation: / blɪp / (AmE), / blɪp / (BrE)
a sudden or brief interruption or minor deviation, often of little significance, in a process or activity

Examples

A small blip in progress
Her concentration suffered a brief blip before she regained focus.
Most experts agree that this is just a temporary blip and not a long-term trend.
There was a blip in the company's earnings last quarter, but overall growth remained steady.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hiccup - a minor, temporary difficulty or interruption
  • synonym: spike - a sudden sharp increase or fluctuation, usually temporary
