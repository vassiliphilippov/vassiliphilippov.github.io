bindingness

noun
obligatory quality
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪndɪŋnəs / (AmE), / ˈbaɪndɪŋnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being legally or morally obligatory or enforceable

Examples

Legal bindingness
The bindingness of the agreement was challenged by the defendant's lawyer.
Judges must consider the bindingness of previous court decisions when making a ruling.
Some rules lack the bindingness needed to ensure compliance from all parties.

Related Terms

  • synonym: enforceability - the quality of being able to be enforced by law
  • synonym: obligation - a duty or commitment
  • antonym: optionality - the quality of being optional or not required
