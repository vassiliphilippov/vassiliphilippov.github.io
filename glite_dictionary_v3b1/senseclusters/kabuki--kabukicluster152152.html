kabuki

noun
ritualized public display
9.5
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈbuː.kiː/ /; / /kə.ˈbuː.kɪ/ (AmE), /kə.ˈbuː.kiː/ /; / /kə.ˈbuː.kɪ/ (BrE)
a staged or ritualized public performance or display, especially one that is insincere or merely for show, often in politics or diplomacy

Examples

Political kabuki
The press conference turned into political kabuki, with officials avoiding direct answers to every question.
Many saw the negotiations as mere kabuki, with no real intention to reach an agreement.
Critics described the hearing as kabuki designed to impress the public rather than solve the problem.

Related Terms

  • synonym: charade - an act or event that is obviously false or insincere
  • synonym: show - a display or performance, often for effect rather than substance
  • antonym: sincerity - the quality of being genuine or honest
