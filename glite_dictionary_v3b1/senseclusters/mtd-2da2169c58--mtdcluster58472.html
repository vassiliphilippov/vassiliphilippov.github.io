MTD

noun
maximum tolerated dose
Pronunciation: / ɛm ti di / (AmE), / ɛm ti di / (BrE)
the highest dose of a drug or treatment that does not cause unacceptable side effects in patients

Examples

The MTD level
The clinical trial established the MTD for the experimental cancer drug.
Doctors monitor patients closely to ensure the MTD is not exceeded during treatment.
Researchers adjusted the dosage to stay below the MTD and minimize side effects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: maximum dose - the largest amount of a drug that can be given
  • antonym: minimum effective dose - the smallest amount of a drug that produces the desired effect
