Shabuoth

noun
Jewish festival commemorating the giving of the Ten Commandments
9.9
Pronunciation: /ʃə.ˈbuː.ət/ /; / /ʃə.ˈvuː.ət/ (AmE), /ʃə.ˈvuː.ət/ /; / /ʃə.ˈbuː.ət/ (BrE)
A Jewish festival celebrated on the sixth of Sivan, commemorating Moses receiving the Ten Commandments at Mount Sinai.

Examples

Shabuoth festival
During Shabuoth, it is customary to read the Book of Ruth in the synagogue.
Many people decorate their homes with greenery in honor of Shabuoth.
The family gathered together to celebrate Shabuoth with traditional foods and prayers.
