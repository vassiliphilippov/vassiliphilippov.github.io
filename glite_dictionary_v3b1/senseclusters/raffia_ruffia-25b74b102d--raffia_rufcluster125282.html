Raffia ruffia

noun
African palm yielding commercial fibers
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈræfi ə ˈrʌfi ə / (AmE), / ˈræfi ə ˈrʌfi ə / (BrE)
A large feather palm native to Africa and Madagascar, with very long divided fronds, producing strong fibers from its leafstalks used commercially.

Examples

Raffia ruffia palm tree
Farmers in Madagascar harvest Raffia ruffia for its durable leaf fibers.
The Raffia ruffia tree can reach impressive heights, towering over the surrounding vegetation.
The fibers of the Raffia ruffia palm are widely used in weaving baskets and mats.
