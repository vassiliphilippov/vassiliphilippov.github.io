tectum

noun
midbrain sensory region
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛktəm / (AmE), / ˈtɛktəm / (BrE)
the dorsal part of the midbrain in vertebrates, responsible for processing sensory information such as vision and hearing

Examples

Midbrain tectum
The tectum is essential for coordinating visual reflexes in many animals.
Damage to the tectum can affect an organism's ability to respond to sounds and sights.
Researchers studied the development of the tectum in embryonic vertebrates.

Related Terms

  • synonym: midbrain - the central part of the brainstem involved in vision, hearing, and motor control
