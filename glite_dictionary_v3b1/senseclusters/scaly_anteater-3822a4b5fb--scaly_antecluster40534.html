scaly anteater

noun
mammal with scales, also called pangolin
Pronunciation: / ˈskeɪli ˈænˌtitər / (AmE), / ˈskeɪli ˈænˌtiːtə / (BrE)
a mammal found in southern Africa and Asia, with a body covered in horny scales and a long snout, feeding mainly on ants and termites; also known as the pangolin.

Examples

The scaly anteater animal
Conservation efforts are underway to protect the endangered scaly anteater from poaching.
The scaly anteater rolls into a ball when threatened by predators.
The diet of a scaly anteater consists mostly of ants and termites.
