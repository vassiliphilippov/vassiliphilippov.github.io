fire sale

noun
sale of goods at very low prices under duress
9.9 3.5
Pronunciation: /ˈfaɪ.ər seɪl/ /; / /faɪr seɪl/ (AmE), /ˈfaɪ.ə seɪl/ /; / /faɪ seɪl/ (BrE)
a sale of goods or assets at very low prices, usually because the seller needs to sell quickly due to an emergency, such as bankruptcy or damage

Examples

A fire sale event
After the company declared bankruptcy, they held a fire sale to sell off remaining inventory.
Investors rushed to buy assets during the fire sale, hoping for bargains.
The struggling retailer announced a massive fire sale to clear its shelves before closing down.
After the warehouse blaze, the store announced a massive fire sale on all affected items.
Shoppers lined up early to take advantage of the fire sale discounts.
The company was in such financial trouble that it had to hold a fire sale to raise cash quickly.
After the company went bankrupt, its assets were sold off in a massive fire sale.
Many collectors wait for a fire sale to find rare items at bargain prices.
The electronics store held a fire sale to get rid of last season's products.

Related Terms

  • synonym: auction - public sale in which goods are sold to the highest bidder
  • synonym: clearance sale - sale to clear out inventory at discounted prices
