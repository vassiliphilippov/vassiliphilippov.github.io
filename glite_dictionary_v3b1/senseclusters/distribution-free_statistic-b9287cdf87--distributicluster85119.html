distribution-free statistic

noun
statistic not requiring distribution knowledge
7.9
Pronunciation: / dɪˌstrɪbjuˈʃən fri stəˈtɪstɪk / (AmE), / dɪˌstrɪbjuˈʃən fri stəˈtɪstɪk / (BrE)
a statistic calculated without requiring knowledge of the underlying probability distribution or its parameters of the data

Examples

A distribution-free statistic test
A distribution-free statistic is often used when the underlying distribution of the data is unknown.
Researchers chose a distribution-free statistic to analyze the data due to the small sample size.
The median is a classic example of a distribution-free statistic.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nonparametric statistic - a statistic that does not assume a specific form for the population distribution
  • antonym: parametric statistic - a statistic that assumes a specific form for the population distribution
