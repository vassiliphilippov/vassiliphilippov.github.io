feudal lord

noun
a man of rank in the feudal system
9.5 0.16/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàfjuÀêd…ôl l…îrd / (AmE), / ÀàfjuÀêd…ôl l…îÀêd / (BrE)
a man of high rank who held land from the king in return for military service and allegiance under the feudal system

Examples

A feudal lord in power
During the Middle Ages, a feudal lord had control over vast territories and armies.
The feudal lord demanded taxes from all the peasants living on his land.
The castle once belonged to a powerful feudal lord who ruled the region for decades.

Synonyms

  • liege lord
    9.9 0.03/m
    a liege lord demands loyalty and military service from vassals, while a feudal lord holds land from the king for service.
  • seigneur
    9.9 0.08/m
    a seigneur is a French feudal lord in pre-revolutionary regions, while a feudal lord is the general English term for such landholders.
  • seignior
    9.9 0.03/m
    seignior is an older or variant spelling seen in historical texts, while a feudal lord is the common modern English term.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: overlord - a ruler who has power over other lords or vassals, especially in the feudal system
  • degree lower: vassal - a person in a subordinate position under a feudal lord
