tierce

noun
old liquid measure
9.9
Pronunciation: / tɪrs / (AmE), / tɪəs / (BrE)
an old unit of liquid measure, or a large cask, especially one holding about 42 gallons

Examples

A tierce of wine
The merchant ordered a tierce of olive oil for his shop.
In the old cellar, they discovered a dusty tierce once used for storing wine.
A tierce was commonly used as a standard measure for shipping rum in the 18th century.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barrel - a large container for liquids, often used for storage or transport
  • degree higher: hogshead - a large cask or unit of liquid measure, larger than a tierce
