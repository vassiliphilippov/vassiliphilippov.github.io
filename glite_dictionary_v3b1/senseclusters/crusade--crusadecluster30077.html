crusade

verb
campaign vigorously for a cause
Pronunciation: / kruːˈseɪd /; / kruˈseɪd / (AmE), / kruːˈseɪd / (BrE)
to campaign vigorously and persistently for a cause or against something

Examples

To crusade for change
He is crusading to improve local education, speaking at events and meeting with officials.
Many citizens crusade against corruption in government by supporting transparency initiatives.
She has crusaded for animal rights her entire life, organizing rallies and petitions.
He is crusading against corruption in the local government.
She decided to crusade for environmental protection after witnessing widespread pollution.
Throughout history, many have crusaded against social injustice, hoping to bring about reform.

Related Terms

  • synonym: advocate - to publicly support or recommend a particular cause or policy
  • synonym: campaign - to work in an organized and active way toward a goal
  • antonym: oppose - to actively resist or refuse to comply with a cause or idea
