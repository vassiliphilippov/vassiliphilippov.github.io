chronotropic

adjective
affecting heart rate
Pronunciation: / ˌkroʊnəˈtroʊpɪk / (AmE), / ˌkrɒnəˈtrɒpɪk / (BrE)
affecting the rate or timing of a biological process, especially the heartbeat

Examples

Chronotropic effect
Doctors monitor the chronotropic response of patients during exercise stress tests.
Some medications have a negative chronotropic effect, slowing the heart rate.
The nurse explained how caffeine can have a positive chronotropic influence on the heartbeat.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dromotropic - affecting the speed of electrical conduction in the heart
  • synonym: inotropic - affecting the force of muscle contractions, especially in the heart
