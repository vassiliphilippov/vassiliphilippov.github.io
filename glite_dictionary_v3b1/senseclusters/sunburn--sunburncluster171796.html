sunburn

noun
redness of skin from sun exposure
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌnˌbɝn / (AmE), / ˈsʌnˌbɜːn / (BrE)
A condition in which skin becomes red and sore from excessive exposure to the sun's ultraviolet rays.

Examples

A bad sunburn
After a long day at the beach, his face was red from sunburn.
Severe sunburn can cause peeling and sensitivity for several days.
She forgot to wear sunscreen and ended up with a painful sunburn on her shoulders.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: tan - darker skin color after sun exposure, usually without soreness
