indifference

noun
lack of interest or concern
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdɪfɚəns /; / ɪnˈdɪfrəns /; / ɪnˈdɪf.ɚ.əns / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɪfərəns /; / ɪnˈdɪf.ər.əns / (BrE)
lack of interest, concern, or caring about something

Examples

Show indifference to problems
He tried to hide his excitement with a display of indifference.
Her indifference to the suffering of others was alarming.
The manager’s indifference resulted in low staff morale and poor performance.
Despite the shocking news, she showed complete indifference to the situation.
His indifference towards his job was evident in his poorly done work.
Public indifference can allow injustice to persist unchecked.
He tried to hide his excitement but could not maintain an air of indifference.
Her indifference to the outcome of the game surprised everyone who knew how competitive she usually is.
The company’s indifference toward customer complaints caused its reputation to suffer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: apathy - lack of feeling, emotion, or interest
  • antonym: concern - interest or worry
  • antonym: enthusiasm - intense and eager enjoyment, interest, or approval
  • antonym: interest - the feeling of wanting to give attention or care to something
