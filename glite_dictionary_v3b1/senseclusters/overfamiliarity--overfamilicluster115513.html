overfamiliarity

noun
being overly familiar or commonplace
5.4
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərfəˌmɪlˈjæɾɪti / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəfəˌmɪlˈjærɪti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being so familiar as to become unremarkable, unnoticed, or even tiresome.

Examples

Overfamiliarity with routine
She felt a sense of overfamiliarity with the routine, which dulled her enthusiasm.
Some jokes lose their impact through overfamiliarity and constant repetition.
The overfamiliarity of the theme made the film less engaging than expected.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: familiarity - close acquaintance with or knowledge of something
  • antonym: novelty - the quality of being new, original, or unusual
