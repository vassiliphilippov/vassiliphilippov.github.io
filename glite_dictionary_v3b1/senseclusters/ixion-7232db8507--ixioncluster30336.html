Ixion

proper noun
mythological figure
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈsiːən / (AmE), / ɪkˈsiːən / (BrE)
a figure from Greek mythology who was punished by the gods for his crimes and hubris

Examples

Myth of Ixion
The myth of Ixion warns against angering the gods through arrogance.
In ancient art, Ixion is often shown bound to a fiery wheel as punishment.
Teachers sometimes reference Ixion when discussing the consequences of hubris in literature.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Sisyphus - a Greek mythological figure punished with endless labor
  • synonym: hubris - excessive pride or self-confidence, often leading to downfall
