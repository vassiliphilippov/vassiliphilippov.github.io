Ricardian

noun
follower of Ricardo
8.9
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈkɑːr.dɪ.ən/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈkɑː.dɪ.ən/ (BrE)
a person who supports or follows the economic theories of David Ricardo, especially regarding free trade and comparative advantage

Examples

A Ricardian economist
As a dedicated Ricardian, he often referenced the principle of comparative advantage in his lectures.
Many 19th-century economists identified as Ricardians and promoted free trade policies.
The debate between the Ricardian and Keynesian schools shaped much of modern economic thought.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Keynesian - a person who supports the economic theories of John Maynard Keynes
