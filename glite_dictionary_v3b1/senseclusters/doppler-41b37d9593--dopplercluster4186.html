Doppler

noun
ultrasound blood flow test
4.9
Pronunciation: /ˈdɑː.plər/ (AmE), /ˈdɒ.plə/ (BrE)
an informal term for an ultrasound test or device that uses the Doppler effect to measure blood flow or movement in the body

Examples

Doppler ultrasound test
The doctor used a Doppler to check the blood flow in her leg after surgery.
A Doppler can help detect blockages in blood vessels quickly and painlessly.
During her pregnancy, the nurse performed a Doppler to listen to the baby's heartbeat.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ultrasound - a medical test using high-frequency sound waves to create images of the inside of the body
