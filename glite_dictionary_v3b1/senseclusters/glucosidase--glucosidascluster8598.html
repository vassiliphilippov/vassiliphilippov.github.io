glucosidase

noun
sugar-breaking enzyme
8.9
Pronunciation: / ɡluːˈkoʊsɪˌdeɪs / (AmE), / ɡluːˈkəʊsɪˌdeɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that breaks down complex sugars into simpler sugars by hydrolyzing glycosidic bonds

Examples

Glucosidase enzyme activity
The doctor explained that a deficiency in glucosidase can lead to metabolic disorders.
Researchers measured the glucosidase activity in different samples to study sugar digestion.
Certain medications work by inhibiting glucosidase to help control blood sugar levels.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amylase - an enzyme that helps digest starch into sugars
  • synonym: glycosidase - an enzyme that breaks glycosidic bonds in sugars
