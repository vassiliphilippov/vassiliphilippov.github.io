breach of promise

noun
failure to keep a marriage promise
Pronunciation: / briːtʃ əv ˈprɑmɪs / (AmE), / briːtʃ əv ˈprɒmɪs / (BrE)
the act of failing to keep a promise to marry someone, formerly recognized as grounds for legal action

Examples

A breach of promise case
In the nineteenth century, a woman could sue for breach of promise if her fiancé called off their engagement.
Literature from the era often features plots centered on a breach of promise lawsuit.
The court dismissed her breach of promise claim due to insufficient evidence.
