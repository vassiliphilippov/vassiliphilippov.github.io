frowzy

adjective
habitually dirty or untidy
Pronunciation: / ˈfraʊzi / (AmE), / ˈfraʊzi / (BrE)
Negligent of neatness, especially in dress or person; habitually dirty or unkempt.

Examples

A frowzy appearance
After a long journey, she looked utterly frowzy and disheveled.
His frowzy clothes made him stand out at the elegant dinner party.
The abandoned house had a frowzy feel, with dust and cobwebs everywhere.
Despite his frowsy appearance, he greeted everyone with a warm smile.
She felt embarrassed by her frowsy hair and wrinkled clothes at the meeting.
The frowsy room smelled of old papers and unwashed laundry.

Related Terms

  • synonym: slovenly - messy or careless in appearance or habits
  • antonym: tidy - neat and orderly in appearance or habits
