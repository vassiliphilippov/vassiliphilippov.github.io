holoplankton

noun
permanent plankton organisms
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhoʊloʊˈplæŋktən / (AmE), / ˌhɒləʊˈplæŋktən / (BrE)
small aquatic organisms that remain in the planktonic stage for their entire life cycle

Examples

Small holoplankton species
Many species of jellyfish are classified as holoplankton because they never leave the planktonic stage.
Unlike meroplankton, holoplankton spend their entire lives drifting in the water column.
Tiny copepods are a common type of holoplankton found in oceans worldwide.

Related Terms

  • antonym: meroplankton - organisms that are planktonic for only part of their life cycle
