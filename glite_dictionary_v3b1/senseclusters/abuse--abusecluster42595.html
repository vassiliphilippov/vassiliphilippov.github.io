abuse

verb
treat cruelly or unfairly
3.7 14/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàbjus / (AmE), / …ôÀàbjuÀêz / (BrE)
to treat a person or animal cruelly or unfairly, especially by using force or words

Examples

To abuse a child
If you abuse your privilege, it may be taken away.
Sadly, some people still abuse animals despite strict laws.
The manager would often abuse his authority by yelling at employees.

Synonyms

  • maltreat
    7.4 0.26/m
    maltreat is to treat a person or animal cruelly or unfairly, while abuse covers cruel treatment including force or hurtful words.
  • mistreat
    2.9 2.91/m
    mistreat means treating someone badly or cruelly, while abuse also emphasizes repeated harm or use of force and cruel words.
  • ill-treat
    5.2 0.01/m
    ill-treat describes being cruel or unfair to someone, while abuse often adds physical force or repeated verbal harm.
  • victimize
    6.0 3.61/m
    victimize means single out and hurt someone unfairly, while abuse is a broader term for cruel or forceful treatment.
  • brutalize
    7.7 1.61/m
    brutalize means use great cruelty and violence, while abuse can be less violent and also include harsh words or unfair acts.
