pre-emption

noun
supremacy of federal law over state law
8.3 7.2
Pronunciation: /priː.ˈemp.ʃən/ /; / /prɪ.ˈemp.ʃən/ (AmE), /priː.ˈemp.ʃən/ /; / /prɪ.ˈemp.ʃən/ (BrE)
(law) The judicial principle that federal law overrides or takes precedence over state laws on the same subject.

Examples

Federal pre-emption
The Supreme Court's ruling on pre-emption clarified the balance of power between federal and state authorities.
The doctrine of pre-emption was cited when the state law conflicted with federal regulations.
Under the principle of pre-emption, state governments cannot pass laws that undermine federal statutes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: supremacy clause - the clause in the U.S. Constitution establishing federal law as supreme
