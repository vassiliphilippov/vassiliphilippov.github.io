paralogistic

adjective
illogical reasoning
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəloʊˈdʒɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌpærələˈdʒɪstɪk / (BrE)
relating to or involving reasoning that is illogical or based on false logic

Examples

A paralogistic argument
The philosopher criticized the theory as being paralogistic and unsupported by evidence.
Her paralogistic conclusions made the debate confusing for everyone involved.
A paralogistic argument can easily mislead people who are not careful thinkers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fallacious - based on a mistaken belief or unsound reasoning
  • synonym: illogical - not logical or reasonable
  • antonym: logical - using clear and sound reasoning
