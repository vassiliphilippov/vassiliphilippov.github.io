pump

verb
supply in large quantities (e.g. money)
Pronunciation: / pʌmp / (AmE), / pʌmp / (BrE)
to supply or provide something, especially money or resources, in large or continuous amounts, often energetically or forcefully

Examples

To pump money in
During the crisis, banks were pumping funds into the market to stabilize it.
She plans to pump more resources into education reform next year.
The government decided to pump billions of dollars into the failing industry.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pour - to send liquid or another substance somewhere in a continuous flow or large quantity
