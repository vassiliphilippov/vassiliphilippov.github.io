Eocene epoch

noun
geological epoch with early modern mammals
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈiː.ə.siːn ˈep.ək/ /; / /ˈiː.ə.siːn ˈiːp.ək/ (AmE), /ˈiː.ə.siːn ˈep.ɒk/ /; / /ˈiː.ə.siːn ˈiːp.ɒk/ (BrE)
the geological epoch, lasting from about 56 to 34 million years ago, marked by the emergence of many modern mammal families

Examples

The Eocene epoch
During the Eocene epoch, Earth's climate was much warmer and forests covered large areas.
Fossils from the Eocene epoch provide important evidence about the development of modern animal groups.
The Eocene epoch was a period of significant evolutionary change, especially for mammals.
