encage

verb
to confine or restrict
Pronunciation: / ɛnˈkeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ɛnˈkeɪdʒ / (BrE)
to confine or restrict someone or something, often in a figurative or non-literal sense

Examples

To encage ideas
Strict rules at the summit seemed designed to encage any open discussion of new ideas.
She felt that her responsibilities at home would encage her dreams of traveling the world.
The artist refused to let tradition encage his creativity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: free - to release from confinement or restriction
  • synonym: imprison - to confine someone in a prison or similar place
