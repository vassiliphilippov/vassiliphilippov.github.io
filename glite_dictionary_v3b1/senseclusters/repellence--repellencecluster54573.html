repellence

noun
quality of repelling
7.0
Pronunciation: / rɪˈpɛləns / (AmE), / rɪˈpɛl.əns / (BrE)
the quality or property of causing something, such as liquids or insects, to be kept away or not penetrate

Examples

Water repellence
The repellence of the new coating keeps rainwater from soaking into the fabric.
Mosquito nets are designed for their repellence against insects in tropical regions.
The scientist measured the repellence of different materials to find the most effective barrier.

Related Terms

  • antonym: attraction - the quality of drawing or pulling something in
  • synonym: resistance - the ability to withstand or oppose something
