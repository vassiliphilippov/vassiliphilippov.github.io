blur

verb
make unclear or indistinct
1.1
Pronunciation: / blɝː /; / blɝ / (AmE), / blɜː / (BrE)
to make something unclear, indistinct, or difficult to see or understand

Examples

To blur the lines
Tears threatened to blur her vision as she read the letter.
The lines between truth and fiction often blur in historical novels.
The rain on the window began to blur the city lights outside.
As the evening went on, the details of the conversation began to blur in my memory.
The boundaries between work and leisure can easily blur when you work from home.
The image on the screen will blur if the projector is not focused correctly.
He tried to blur the facts to avoid admitting responsibility.
Stress can blur your ability to think clearly during an exam.
The rain began to blur the view through the window as we drove.
