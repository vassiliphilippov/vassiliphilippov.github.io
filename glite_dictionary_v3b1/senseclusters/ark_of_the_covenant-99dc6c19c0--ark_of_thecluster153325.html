Ark of the Covenant

proper noun
sacred chest of the Ten Commandments
9.5
Pronunciation: / ɑrk əv ðə ˈkʌvənənt / (AmE), / ɑːk əv ðə ˈkʌvənənt / (BrE)
In Judaism, a sacred chest that held the two stone tablets of the Ten Commandments, kept by the ancient Hebrews as a symbol of God's covenant with them.

Examples

The Ark of the Covenant chest
According to the Bible, the Ark of the Covenant was carried by the Israelites during their journey through the desert.
In popular culture, the Ark of the Covenant has appeared in several adventure films and novels.
The discovery of the Ark of the Covenant has fascinated archaeologists and religious scholars for centuries.
