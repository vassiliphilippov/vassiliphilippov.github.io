notionally

adverb
theoretically; for calculation
Pronunciation: / ˈnoʊʃənəli / (AmE), / ˈnəʊʃənəli / (BrE)
in theory or as an idea, but not necessarily in reality; for calculation or conceptual purposes, not based on something real

Examples

To notionally calculate interest
The interest payments are notionally calculated on a principal that does not actually change hands.
Although the funds are notionally allocated to the project, they have not yet been spent.
The two teams are notionally equal in strength, but one has more experience.
The funds are notionally allocated for emergencies, but may be used for other purposes if needed.
The project is notionally complete, though some minor tasks remain unfinished.
He is notionally in charge, but most decisions are made by the team.

Related Terms

  • antonym: actually - in fact; really existing or happening
  • antonym: practically - in practice or reality
  • synonym: theoretically - in a way that is based on theory or ideas rather than actual events
