collectability

noun
ability to be collected
5.7
Pronunciation: /kə.lek.tə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tɪ/ /; / /kə.lek.tə.ˈbɪl.ə.tɪ/ (AmE), /kə.lek.tə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tɪ/ /; / /kə.lek.tə.ˈbɪl.ə.tɪ/ (BrE)
the degree to which something, such as a debt or payment, can be collected or recovered successfully

Examples

Collectability of a debt
The collectability of the loan was questioned after the borrower declared bankruptcy.
Before approving the contract, the manager assessed the collectability of the outstanding payments.
Changes in the economy can affect the collectability of taxes owed to the government.
