inelastic

adjective
not elastic; unable to return to original shape
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnɪˈlæstɪk / (AmE), / ˌɪnɪˈlæstɪk / (BrE)
not able to return to its original shape after being stretched or compressed; not elastic.

Examples

An inelastic material
Rubber bands are flexible, but metal rods are usually inelastic.
The collision was nearly inelastic, causing the vehicles to remain stuck together.
When demand is inelastic, consumers buy almost the same amount regardless of price changes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: elastic - able to resume original shape after being stretched or compressed
