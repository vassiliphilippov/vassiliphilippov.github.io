tie rod

noun
rod linking steering gear and front wheels in vehicles
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪ ˌrɑd / (AmE), / ˈtaɪ ˌrɒd / (BrE)
either of two rods in a vehicle that connect the steering gear to the front wheels, enabling them to turn together.

Examples

A tie rod in vehicle
A broken tie rod can result in dangerous steering problems.
He inspected the tie rod ends for signs of excessive wear before the long trip.
The mechanic discovered that the tie rod was bent and needed to be replaced.
