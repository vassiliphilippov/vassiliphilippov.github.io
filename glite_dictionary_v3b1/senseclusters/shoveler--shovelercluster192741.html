shoveler

noun
freshwater duck with a broad bill
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˈʃʌvələr / (AmE), / ˈʃʌvələ / (BrE)
A freshwater duck found in the Northern Hemisphere, known for its broad, flat bill used for sifting food from the water.

Examples

A shoveler duck
Among the various ducks at the lake, the shoveler is easily recognized by its distinctive bill.
I watched a pair of shovelers filter food from the surface of the pond.
The northern shoveler is a common sight in wetlands during migration season.
A flock of shovellers was spotted feeding in the wetlands during migration season.
The male shoveller displays striking green and white plumage during the breeding period.
The northern shoveller is easily recognized by its distinctive spatula-shaped bill.

Related Terms

  • synonym: duck - a waterbird with a broad blunt bill, short legs, webbed feet, and a waddling gait
