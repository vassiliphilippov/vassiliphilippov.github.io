whutter

verb
to say 'what' dialectally
to say or ask 'what' or 'utter' in a dialectal or nonstandard way, especially in certain regional speech.

Examples

To whutter a question
He leaned over and whuttered something I couldn't quite understand.
In the old village, people would often whutter their questions across the street.
She whuttered a greeting as she passed by the open window.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ask - to request information by speaking
  • synonym: utter - to say or speak aloud
