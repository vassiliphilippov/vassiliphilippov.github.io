shadow

noun
dominant presence overshadowing someone
1.3
Pronunciation: / ˈʃædoʊ / (AmE), / ˈʃædəʊ /; / ˈʃadəʊ / (BrE)
a sign, feeling, or influence that is present but not always obvious, often making something seem less positive or showing the presence of something else

Examples

Shadow of doubt
He worked tirelessly, always in the shadow of his elder brother's achievements.
The company's innovative ideas remained in the shadow of its larger competitor.
The young artist felt trapped in the shadow of her famous predecessor.
His apology lacked even the shadow of sincerity.
Not a shadow of evidence was found at the scene of the crime.
There wasn't even a shadow of doubt in her mind about the decision.
Even at the party, a shadow of doubt lingered in her mind.
The news cast a shadow over the whole event.
Their joy was darkened by the shadow of impending loss.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blessing - something that brings happiness or is positive

  • synonym: hint - a slight or indirect indication or suggestion
  • synonym: influence - the power to affect someone or something
  • antonym: limelight - the center of public attention
  • synonym: omen - a sign of something to come, especially something bad
  • synonym: shelter - a place giving protection from danger or bad weather
  • synonym: threat - the possibility or danger that something unwanted will happen
  • synonym: trace - a very small amount or indication of something
