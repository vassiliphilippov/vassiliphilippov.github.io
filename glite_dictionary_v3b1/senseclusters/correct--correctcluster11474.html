Synonyms

  • put right
    3.6 0.1/m
    put right corrects mistakes or restores order in everyday speech, while correct is the general verb for making something accurate.
  • rectify
    6.0 3/m
    rectify is a formal verb used to fix a wrong or error, while correct is the common verb for making things accurate.
  • right
    4.4 3/m
    right means to put something back in a proper position or restore it, while correct is broader and makes things accurate.
  • adjust
    2.7 30/m
    adjust means to change or fine-tune something to fit or work better, while correct means to remove an error or fault.
  • repair
    2.1 4/m
    repair usually means to fix damage or restore an object or relationship, while correct focuses on making something accurate or right.
  • compensate
    3.7 8/m
    compensate means to balance or offset a loss or effect, while correct means to fix an error or make something accurate.

Related Terms
