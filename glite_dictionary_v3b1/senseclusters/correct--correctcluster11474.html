correct

verb
make right; remove errors
2.0
Pronunciation: / kəˈrɛkt / (AmE), / kəˈrɛkt / (BrE)
to make something right, accurate, or free from error; to fix, adjust, compensate, remedy, or make amends for a mistake or problem

Examples

To correct a mistake
She carefully corrected his grammar errors on the essay.
The teacher corrects students when they make mistakes.
You should correct any inaccuracies before submitting your report.
Please correct the time on the wall clock if it is wrong.
The technician had to correct the settings to ensure precise measurements.
You should correct the angle of the mirror for a clearer view.
It took years to correct the mistakes and bring peace to the divided community.
She made every effort to correct the harm caused by her earlier decisions.
The government tried to correct the injustices suffered by minority groups in the past.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adjust - to change something slightly so that it works better or fits better
  • synonym: compensate - to counterbalance or adjust for something, often an error or a negative effect
  • synonym: fix - to repair or mend something that is broken or incorrect
  • antonym: mistake - an action or opinion that is not correct; error
  • synonym: rectify - to put right; to correct, especially in a technical or precise sense
  • synonym: repair - to restore something that is broken or damaged to a good condition
  • synonym: right - to make something right, especially by reparations or amends
  • antonym: worsen - to make or become worse; the opposite of improving or correcting a condition
