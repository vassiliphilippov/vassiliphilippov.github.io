genetic disease

noun
disease inherited genetically
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈnɛtɪk dɪˈziz / (AmE), / dʒɪˈnɛtɪk dɪˈziːz / (BrE)
A disease or disorder caused by abnormalities in an individual's genetic material and inherited from one's parents.

Examples

A genetic disease
Advances in medicine have improved the diagnosis and treatment of many genetic diseases.
Cystic fibrosis is a well-known genetic disease that affects the lungs and digestive system.
Some couples undergo genetic counseling to assess their risk of passing on a genetic disease to their children.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hereditary disease - A disease passed from parents to offspring through the genes
