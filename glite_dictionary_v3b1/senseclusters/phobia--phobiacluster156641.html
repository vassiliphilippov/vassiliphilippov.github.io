phobia

noun
irrational fear; anxiety disorder
1.0 4.1
Pronunciation: /ˈfoʊ.bɪ.ə/ /; / /ˈfoʊ.biː.ə/ (AmE), /ˈfəʊ.bɪ.ə/ /; / /ˈfəʊ.biː.ə/ (BrE)
An extreme and irrational fear of a particular object, activity, or situation, especially as a mental disorder.

Examples

Extreme phobia of crowds
His phobia became so severe that he could not enter crowded public spaces.
Overcoming a phobia often requires therapy and gradual exposure to the feared object or situation.
She has a phobia of flying and avoids airplanes whenever possible.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anxiety - a feeling of worry, nervousness, or unease
  • synonym: fear - an unpleasant emotion caused by the threat of danger, pain, or harm
  • antonym: philia - an abnormal love or strong affinity for something (opposite of phobia)
