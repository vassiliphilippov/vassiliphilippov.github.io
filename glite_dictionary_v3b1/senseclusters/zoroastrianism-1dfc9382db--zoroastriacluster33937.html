Zoroastrianism

noun
ancient Persian religion
Pronunciation: / ˌzɔːroʊˈæstriəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌzɒrəʊˈæstriənɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
an ancient Persian religion founded by Zoroaster in the 6th century BC, emphasizing the duality of good and evil and set forth in the Zend-Avesta

Examples

Zoroastrianism religion
Fire temples play a significant role in the rituals of Zoroastrianism.
Many consider Zoroastrianism to be one of the world's oldest continuously practiced religions.
Some scholars believe that Zoroastrianism influenced later religious traditions, including Judaism and Christianity.
