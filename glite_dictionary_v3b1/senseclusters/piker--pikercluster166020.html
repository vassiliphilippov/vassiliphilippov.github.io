piker

noun
stingy person
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪkər / (AmE), / ˈpaɪkə / (BrE)
a person who is stingy or unwilling to spend money or take risks, especially in social or financial situations

Examples

A cheap piker
Everyone called him a piker because he never chipped in for lunch.
She was labeled a piker after refusing to join the group investment.
Don't be a piker; sometimes you have to take a chance to succeed.
Everyone called him a piker because he never contributed to group gifts.
She was labeled a piker for always avoiding high-stakes games at the casino.
Don't be a piker—join us for dinner and split the bill.
