penetrometer

noun
instrument for measuring firmness
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛnɪˈtrɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / ˌpɛnɪˈtrɒmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument used to measure how easily a material, such as soil, can be penetrated or compressed

Examples

A penetrometer device
The engineer used a penetrometer to determine the strength of the roadbed before construction began.
A penetrometer can help farmers assess whether their soil is compacted and needs to be loosened.
After heavy rain, the technician checked the field with a penetrometer to measure soil softness.

  • synonym: gauge - an instrument for measuring or testing
