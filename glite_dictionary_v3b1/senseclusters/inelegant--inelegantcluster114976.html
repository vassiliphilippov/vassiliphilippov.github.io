inelegant

adjective
lacking grace or good taste
5.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˈnɛlɪɡənt / (AmE), / ɪˈnɛlɪɡənt / (BrE)
lacking in refinement, grace, or good taste; not elegant.

Examples

An inelegant style
His handwriting was so inelegant that it was difficult to read.
She made an inelegant exit, tripping over the chair on her way out.
The solution, though effective, was considered inelegant by the design team.

Related Terms

  • antonym: elegant - graceful and stylish in appearance or manner
