reinstill

verb
to cause again
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪnˈstɪl / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈstɪl / (BrE)
to cause a feeling, idea, or quality to be felt or present again in someone or something

Examples

To reinstill confidence
The coach worked hard to reinstill a sense of teamwork after the team lost several games.
After the incident, the company tried to reinstill trust among its employees.
Parents often need to reinstill good habits in their children after a long vacation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: instill - to gradually cause someone to have a feeling or idea
  • antonym: remove - to take away a feeling or quality
  • synonym: restore - to bring back a feeling or quality that was lost
