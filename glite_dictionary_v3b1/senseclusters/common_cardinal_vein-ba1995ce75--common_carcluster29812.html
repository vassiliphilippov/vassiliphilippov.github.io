common cardinal vein

noun
major embryonic vein returning blood to heart
7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑː.mən ˈkɑːr.dɪ.nəl veɪn/ /; / /ˈkɑː.mɪn ˈkɑːr.də.nəl veɪn/ (AmE), /ˈkɒm.ən ˈkɑː.dɪ.nəl veɪn/ /; / /ˈkɒm.ən ˈkɑː.də.nəl veɪn/ (BrE)
Either of the paired major veins in the embryo that return blood to the heart, formed by the junction of anterior and posterior cardinal veins.

Examples

The common cardinal vein
Any disruption in the formation of the common cardinal vein can affect embryonic circulation.
During early development, the common cardinal vein carries blood from the embryo to the heart.
The common cardinal veins are essential for returning deoxygenated blood during embryogenesis.
