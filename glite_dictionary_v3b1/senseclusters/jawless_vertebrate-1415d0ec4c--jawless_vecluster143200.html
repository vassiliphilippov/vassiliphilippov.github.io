jawless vertebrate

noun
vertebrate animal without jaws or paired limbs
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɔləs ˈvɜrtɪˌbreɪt / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔːlɪs ˈvɜːtɪbrət / (BrE)
an eel-shaped vertebrate animal lacking jaws and paired appendages, including cyclostomes and some extinct species

Examples

A jawless vertebrate animal
Lampreys are a common example of a living jawless vertebrate found in freshwater streams.
The fossil record shows that several types of jawless vertebrates existed hundreds of millions of years ago.
Unlike most fish, a jawless vertebrate does not have paired fins or true jaws.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cyclostome - an animal in the group of jawless vertebrates including lampreys and hagfishes
