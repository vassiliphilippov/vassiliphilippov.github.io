feel the collar

verb
to check or discipline someone
9.9
Pronunciation: / fil ðə ˈkɑlər / (AmE), / fiːl ðə ˈkɒlə / (BrE)
informal: to check or rebuke someone, especially regarding their behavior or discipline.

Examples

To feel the collar of a student
After the students got too rowdy, the teacher had to feel the collar of the main troublemaker.
If you break the rules, you can expect someone to feel the collar pretty quickly.
The manager didn't hesitate to feel the collar of any employee caught slacking off.
