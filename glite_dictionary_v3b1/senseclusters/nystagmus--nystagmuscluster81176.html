nystagmus

noun
involuntary movements of the eyeballs
7.8 9.9
Pronunciation: / nɪˈstæɡməs / (AmE), / nɪˈstæɡməs / (BrE)
A condition characterized by involuntary, rapid, and repetitive movements of the eyeballs, often used as a clinical sign in the diagnosis of neurological and visual disorders.

Examples

nystagmus eye movement
Certain medications may cause nystagmus as a side effect.
The doctor noted nystagmus during the neurological examination.
Vertical nystagmus can indicate a serious brainstem disorder.
