firepower

noun
military weapon capacity or strength
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪrˌpaʊər / (AmE), / ˈfaɪəˌpaʊə / (BrE)
the amount or strength of weapons and ammunition available to a military force or group; the ability to deliver effective fire against a target.

Examples

Heavy firepower
Modern tanks bring significant firepower to ground operations.
The army relied on superior firepower to overwhelm the enemy forces.
With increased firepower, the navy could defend the harbor against any attack.

Related Terms

  • synonym: firepower - amount or strength of weapons and ammunition
  • synonym: weaponry - the weapons possessed by a group or country
