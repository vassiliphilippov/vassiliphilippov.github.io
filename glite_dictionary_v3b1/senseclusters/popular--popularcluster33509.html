popular

adjective
new and widely enjoyed music or art
1.0 20/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…ëÀêpj…ôl…ôr / (AmE), / Ààp…ípj äl…ô / (BrE)
(of music or art) relating to styles that are new and widely enjoyed or sought after by the general public, especially young people.

Examples

Popular music
She prefers popular music over classical or jazz.
That song became popular almost overnight among young audiences.
The band's popular sound resonated with teenagers across the country.

Synonyms

  • pop
    2.9 0.33/m
    pop is new music or art aimed at young people and wide audiences, while popular describes styles enjoyed by many.
  • all the rage
    5.5 1.39/m
    all the rage describes a fashion or style that everyone is wearing right now, while popular means widely liked.
  • hot
    3.4 17/m
    hot is an exciting, very current thing people want right now, while popular is a broader word for what many like.
  • in demand
    2.9 3.12/m
    in demand means many people want or buy something now, while popular refers to styles or items enjoyed by the public.
