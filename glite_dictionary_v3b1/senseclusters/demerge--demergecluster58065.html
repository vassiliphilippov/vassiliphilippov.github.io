demerge

verb
to separate companies
Pronunciation: / diːˈmɜrdʒ /; / diˈmɜrdʒ / (AmE), / diːˈmɜːdʒ /; / diˈmɜːdʒ / (BrE)
to separate things that were previously joined or merged, returning them to their original parts (often used for companies, organizations, or divisions)

Examples

To demerge a company
After years of operating together, the board voted to demerge the two companies.
The government encouraged the corporation to demerge its energy and transport sectors.
Shareholders supported the plan to demerge the business into separate entities.
The company decided to demerge its technology and retail divisions to focus on separate markets.
After the project ended, the teams were demerged and returned to their original departments.
It took several hours to demerge the combined data into individual files for each client.

Related Terms

  • antonym: merge - to combine two or more things into one
  • synonym: separate - to divide into different parts or groups
  • synonym: split - to divide something into parts
