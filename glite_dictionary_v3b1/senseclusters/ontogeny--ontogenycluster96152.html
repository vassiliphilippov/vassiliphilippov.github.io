ontogeny

noun
development of an individual organism
3.4 9.1
Pronunciation: /ɑːn.ˈtɑː.dʒə.niː/ (AmE), /ɒn.ˈtɒ.dʒə.niː/ (BrE)
(biology) the development of an individual organism from the earliest stage to maturity, involving the gradual changes in structure and complexity.

Examples

Ontogeny of organisms
Researchers examined how diet influences the ontogeny of immune cells in infants.
The study focused on the ontogeny of amphibian limb development.
Understanding the ontogeny of language aids in identifying developmental disorders.
