perleche

noun
disorder causing cracked mouth corners
Pronunciation: / pərˈlɛʃ / (AmE), / pɛəˈlɛʃ / (BrE)
A medical disorder marked by redness, scaling, and cracking at the corners of the mouth, often caused by a deficiency of riboflavin or by infection.

Examples

Perleche at corners
Nutritional deficiencies can contribute to the development of perleche in some individuals.
Persistent perleche may require both dietary changes and antifungal treatment.
The doctor diagnosed the painful cracks in the patient's mouth as perleche.
