pouch

verb
send by official or diplomatic mail
9.4
Pronunciation: / paʊtʃ / (AmE), / paʊtʃ / (BrE)
to send something by a special mail service, especially one used for official or diplomatic correspondence

Examples

To pouch a letter
Sensitive information is sometimes pouched to avoid interception by ordinary mail services.
The embassy will pouch your documents to the home office for secure delivery.
They are pouching critical letters to the capital instead of sending them electronically.
