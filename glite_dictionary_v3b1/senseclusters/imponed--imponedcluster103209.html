imponed

verb
to place ceremonially
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpoʊnd / (AmE), / ɪmˈpəʊnd / (BrE)
to place or set something on or into something, often in a formal or ceremonial way (archaic)

Examples

To imponed an object
During the ancient ritual, the priest imponed the sacred objects onto the altar.
The king imponed his seal upon the document as a sign of approval.
In old traditions, elders imponed gifts into the hands of the newlyweds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: place - to put something in a particular position
  • antonym: remove - to take something away from a place
