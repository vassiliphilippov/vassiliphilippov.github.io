free energy

noun
variational bound used in Bayesian inference
8.6 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / fri \u02c8\u025bn\u025ad\u0292i / (AmE), / fri \u02c8\u025bn\u0259d\u0292i / (BrE)
(in neuroscience and machine learning) a mathematical quantity (a variational functional) used to bound unexpected observations (surprise) and drive approximate Bayesian inference and learning.

Examples

Brain free energy
Researchers use free energy minimization to train predictive coding models of perception in neuroscience.
In machine learning, minimizing free energy is equivalent to maximizing a model's evidence under observed data.
The paper showed how agents can act to reduce long-term free energy and avoid surprising sensory states.
