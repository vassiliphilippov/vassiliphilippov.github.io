deltoideus

noun
shoulder muscle
Pronunciation: / dɛlˈtɔɪdiəs / (AmE), / dɛlˈtɔɪdiəs / (BrE)
the deltoid muscle of the shoulder, responsible for lifting the arm away from the body

Examples

Deltoideus muscle
The deltoideus plays a key role in raising your arm to the side.
Injury to the deltoideus can make it difficult to lift objects overhead.
Physical therapists often recommend exercises to strengthen the deltoideus after shoulder surgery.

Related Terms

  • antonym: biceps - the muscle on the front of the upper arm
  • synonym: deltoid - the large, triangular muscle covering the shoulder joint
