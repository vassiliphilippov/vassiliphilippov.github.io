Sylvanus

proper noun
Roman god of woods and flocks
9.9
Pronunciation: /sɪl.ˈvən.ɪs/ /; / /sɪl.ˈveɪ.nəs/ (AmE), /sɪl.ˈveɪ.nəs/ /; / /sɪl.ˈvɑː.nəs/ (BrE)
In Roman mythology, the god of woods, fields, and flocks, often associated with nature and rural life; equivalent to the Greek Pan.

Examples

Sylvanus god of woods
In ancient Roman religion, Sylvanus was honoured as the protector of forests and fields.
Statues of Sylvanus often depict him holding branches or surrounded by animals.
The early Romans would offer sacrifices to Sylvanus to ensure a fruitful harvest.
