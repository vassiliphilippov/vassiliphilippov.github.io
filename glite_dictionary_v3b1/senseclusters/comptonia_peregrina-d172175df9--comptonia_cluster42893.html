Comptonia peregrina

noun
a North American shrub with fern-like leaves
Pronunciation: / kɑmpˈtoʊniə ˌpɛrəˈɡrɪnə / (AmE), / kɒmpˈtəʊniə ˌpɛrɪˈɡrɪnə / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub of eastern North America with sweet-scented, fern-like leaves and small white flowers, commonly known as sweetfern.

Examples

Comptonia peregrina shrub
Botanists often recommend planting Comptonia peregrina to attract pollinators and enrich the soil.
The Comptonia peregrina adds a lovely fragrance to native gardens in the eastern United States.
The sweetfern, or Comptonia peregrina, thrives in sandy, well-drained soils near pine forests.
