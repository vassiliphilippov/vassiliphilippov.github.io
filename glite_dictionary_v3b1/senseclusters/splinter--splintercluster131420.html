splinter

noun
tiny sharp piece of material
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˈsplɪntɚ / (AmE), / ˈsplɪntə / (BrE)
a small, thin, sharp piece of material, such as wood, glass, or metal, broken off from a larger object

Examples

A wood splinter
Be careful with that glass; you might get a splinter if it shatters.
He got a splinter in his finger while fixing the fence.
The wooden crate broke apart, scattering splinters across the floor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chip - a small fragment of something broken off from the whole
  • synonym: sliver - a small, thin, narrow piece of something cut or split off from a larger piece
