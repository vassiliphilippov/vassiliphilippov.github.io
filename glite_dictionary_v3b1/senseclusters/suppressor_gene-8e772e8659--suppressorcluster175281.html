suppressor gene

noun
gene that inhibits another gene's effect
8.5
Pronunciation: / səˈprɛsər ˌdʒin / (AmE), / səˈprɛsə ˌdʒiːn / (BrE)
a gene that inhibits or reduces the phenotypic expression of another gene, particularly a mutant gene

Examples

Suppressor gene function
Cancer can develop if a suppressor gene becomes inactive or is deleted.
Researchers are studying how each suppressor gene functions to prevent abnormal cell growth.
The suppressor gene blocked the harmful effects of the mutated allele in laboratory tests.

Related Terms

  • antonym: oncogene - a gene that can cause cancer when mutated or expressed at high levels
