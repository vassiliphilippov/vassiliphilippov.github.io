Coprinus

noun
genus of mushrooms dissolving into ink-like fluid
9.9
Pronunciation: / koʊˈpraɪnəs / (AmE), / kɒˈprɪnəs / (BrE)
a genus of mushrooms whose caps dissolve into an inky fluid when mature (called inky caps)

Examples

Coprinus mushrooms
Many species in Coprinus are called 'inky caps' due to their dissolving caps.

Researchers studied the habitat preferences of various Coprinus species in temperate forests.
Some Coprinus mushrooms are commonly called inkcaps because of their unusual way of decomposing.
The distinctive feature of the Coprinus genus is that many species develop inky caps as they mature.
