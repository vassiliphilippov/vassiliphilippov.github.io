regather

verb
to collect oneself again
Pronunciation: / riˈɡæðər / (AmE), / riˈɡæðə / (BrE)
to collect or bring together one's thoughts, strength, or feelings again, especially after being disturbed or interrupted

Examples

To regather your thoughts
He took a deep breath to regather his composure before stepping onto the stage.
After the shocking news, she needed a moment to regather her thoughts.
The team paused to regather their energy before continuing the hike up the mountain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compose - to calm and control oneself
  • antonym: lose control - to fail to remain calm or collected
  • synonym: recover - to return to a normal state after difficulty
