bar billiards

noun
table game using cues and pegs
7.0 6.5
Pronunciation: /bɑːr ˈbɪl.jərdz/ (AmE), /bɑː ˈbɪl.ɪ.ədz/ /; / /bɑː ˈbɪl.jədz/ (BrE)
A tabletop cue sport in which players use short cues to strike balls into holes guarded by wooden pegs; knocking over pegs incurs penalties.

Examples

Bar billiards game
He spent the afternoon teaching his friends how to play bar billiards.
The pub installed a bar billiards table to attract more patrons.
Unlike pool, bar billiards uses wooden pegs and no side pockets.
