oil-water interface

noun
boundary surface between oil and water
Pronunciation: / ɔɪl ˈwɔtɚ ˈɪntɚˌfeɪs / (AmE), / ɔɪl ˈwɔːtə ˈɪntəfeɪs / (BrE)
the surface or boundary where oil and water, which do not mix, come into contact with each other

Examples

The oil-water interface
Emulsifiers help to stabilize the oil-water interface in salad dressings.
Scientists studied the behavior of surfactants at the oil-water interface to improve cleaning products.
Tiny droplets formed a visible oil-water interface in the laboratory experiment.
