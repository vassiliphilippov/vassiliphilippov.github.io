red-winged blackbird

noun
North American blackbird species with red wing patches
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛd ˌwɪŋd ˈblækˌbɝd / (AmE), / ˈrɛd ˌwɪŋd ˈblækˌbəːd / (BrE)
a North American blackbird (Agelaius phoeniceus) with distinctive scarlet and yellow wing patches, especially prominent in males

Examples

A red-winged blackbird flock
A flock of red-winged blackbirds filled the reeds by the pond.
During spring, the male red-winged blackbird displays his bright shoulder patches to attract a mate.
We spotted several red-winged blackbirds calling loudly in the marsh.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blackbird - a common Eurasian and North American songbird
  • degree lower: grackle - a different species of New World blackbird with iridescent plumage
