martingale

noun
strap on a horse's harness to control head position
6.8 8.1
Pronunciation: /ˈmɑːr.tɪn.geɪl/ /; / /ˈmɑːr.tɪŋ.geɪl/ (AmE), /ˈmɑː.tɪn.geɪl/ /; / /ˈmɑː.tɪŋ.geɪl/ (BrE)
a strap on a horse's harness that runs from the noseband to the girth, used to keep the horse from raising its head too high

Examples

Horse martingale
She checked that the martingale was properly fitted before riding.
The trainer attached a martingale to the horse's bridle before the competition.
Without a martingale, the horse kept tossing its head uncomfortably.
