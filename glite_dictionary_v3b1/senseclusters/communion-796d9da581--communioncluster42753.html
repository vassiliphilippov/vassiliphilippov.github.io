Communion

noun
participation in the Christian Eucharist
7.6 4.84/m
Pronunciation: / k…ôÀàmjunj…ôn / (AmE), / k…ôÀàmjuÀênj…ôn / (BrE)
The act of taking part in the Christian ceremony of the Eucharist, in which bread and wine are consecrated and consumed.

Examples

To take Communion
Many Christians attend church weekly to take Communion.
She received Communion for the first time on Easter Sunday.
The priest offered Communion to all baptized members of the congregation.

Synonyms

  • Holy Communion
    6.3 0.62/m
    Holy Communion is the formal name for receiving consecrated bread and wine in church, while Communion means taking part in that ceremony.
  • Eucharist
    9.3 3.75/m
    Eucharist is the church term for the sacrament of consecrated bread and wine, while Communion means taking part in it.
  • Lord's Supper
    7.7 0.45/m
    Lord's Supper is the name that Protestant churches often use for the shared meal, while Communion names the act of receiving it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Eucharist - the Christian sacrament commemorating the Last Supper
