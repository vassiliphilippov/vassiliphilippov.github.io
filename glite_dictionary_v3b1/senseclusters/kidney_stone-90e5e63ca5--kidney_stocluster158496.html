kidney stone

noun
hard mass formed in the kidney
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪdni stoʊn / (AmE), / ˈkɪdni stəʊn / (BrE)
a hard, solid mass that forms in the kidney from minerals or crystals, often causing pain and urinary problems

Examples

A kidney stone pain
Drinking plenty of water may help prevent kidney stones from forming.
She was hospitalized last year after passing a kidney stone.
The doctor explained that my severe pain was caused by a kidney stone.
After suffering from severe pain, she was diagnosed with multiple kidney stones.
Drinking plenty of water each day can help prevent the formation of a kidney stone.
The doctor explained that the kidney stone was blocking his urinary tract.
