transnationalism

noun
cross-border connections
Pronunciation: / trænzˈnæʃənəlɪzəm /; / trænzˈnæʃənəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / trænzˈnæʃənəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the practice, process, or principle of maintaining connections, activities, or relationships across national borders

Examples

Transnationalism across borders
Modern technology has made transnationalism more common among families living in different countries.
The rise of transnationalism challenges traditional ideas of national identity and citizenship.
Many migrant communities express their transnationalism through cultural festivals and international communication.
The rise of transnationalism has influenced how companies operate in the global economy.
Some scholars argue that transnationalism challenges traditional ideas of citizenship and identity.
Modern communication technologies have made transnationalism more common in social and political movements.
The rise of transnationalism has changed how families stay connected across continents.
Many scholars study transnationalism to understand migration and cultural exchange.
Modern technology has made transnationalism more common in business and education.

Related Terms

  • synonym: globalization - the process by which businesses or other organizations develop international influence
  • synonym: internationalism - the principle of cooperation among nations
  • antonym: nationalism - strong identification with and support for one's own nation
