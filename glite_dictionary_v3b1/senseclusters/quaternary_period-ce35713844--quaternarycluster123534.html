Quaternary period

noun
recent geological period (last 2 million years)
7.0
Pronunciation: /kwə.ˈtɜːr.nə.riː ˈpɪr.i.əd/ /; / /kwɑː.ˈtɜːr.nə.riː ˈpɪr.i.əd/ (AmE), /kwɒ.ˈtɜː.nə.riː ˈpɪə.ri.əd/ /; / /kwɔː.ˈtɜː.nə.ri ˈpɪə.ri.əd/ (BrE)
the most recent geological time period, covering the last 2 million years and including the present, characterized by cycles of glaciation and the evolution of humans

Examples

The Quaternary period ice ages
During the Quaternary period, the Earth experienced several major ice ages.
Many animal species became extinct at the end of the Quaternary period due to climate changes.
The Quaternary period saw the rise and spread of modern humans across the globe.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Holocene - the current epoch within the Quaternary period, starting around 11,700 years ago
  • synonym: Pleistocene - the earlier epoch of the Quaternary period, marked by repeated glaciations
