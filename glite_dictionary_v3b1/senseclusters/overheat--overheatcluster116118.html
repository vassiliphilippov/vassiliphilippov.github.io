overheat

verb
make or become excessively hot
1.1 3.7
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊ.vɚˈhiːt /; / ˌoʊvərˈhit / (AmE), / ˌəʊ.vəˈhiːt /; / ˌəʊvəˈhiːt / (BrE)
to make or become too hot, especially so that it may cause damage or problems

Examples

To overheat a computer
Don’t let your computer overheat during long gaming sessions.
If you overheat the soup, it might lose its flavor.
The engine can overheat on a hot day if not checked regularly.
During the race, his car overheated and forced him to stop.
If you leave your laptop running all day, it may overheat and shut down unexpectedly.
The engine began to overheat after the coolant leaked out onto the road.
