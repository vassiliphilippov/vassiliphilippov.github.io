mouthy

adjective
talkative or outspoken
Pronunciation: / ˈmaʊði / (AmE), / ˈmaʊði / (BrE)
tending to talk a lot, often in a lively or outspoken way

Examples

Mouthy friend
Her mouthy personality made her the center of attention at every party.
The teacher asked the mouthy student to let others have a turn to speak.
He can be a bit mouthy when he gets excited about a topic.

Related Terms

  • antonym: quiet - not talking much
  • synonym: talkative - liking to talk a lot
