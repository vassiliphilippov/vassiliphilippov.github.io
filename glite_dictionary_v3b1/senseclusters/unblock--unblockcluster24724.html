unblock

verb
remove an obstruction
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈblɑk / (AmE), / ʌnˈblɒk / (BrE)
to remove an obstruction from something so that it can function or move freely

Examples

To unblock a pipe
Doctors were able to unblock his arteries after the heart attack.
She used a special tool to unblock the printer when the paper jammed.
The plumber arrived promptly to unblock the kitchen sink.

Related Terms

  • antonym: block - to prevent movement or flow by obstructing
