false topaz

noun
yellow quartz resembling topaz
Pronunciation: / fɔls ˈtoʊpæz / (AmE), / fɔːls ˈtəʊpaz / (BrE)
a yellow variety of quartz that resembles topaz but is not a true topaz

Examples

Yellow false topaz
Collectors should be careful not to confuse false topaz with genuine topaz.
He inherited a pendant made of false topaz from his grandmother.
The ring was set with a false topaz that gleamed in the sunlight.

Related Terms

  • synonym: citrine - a pale yellow to brownish quartz used as a gemstone
  • antonym: topaz - a hard, typically yellow gemstone, an aluminum silicate fluorine mineral
