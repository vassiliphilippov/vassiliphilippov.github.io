survivable

adjective
able to continue
Pronunciation: / sərˈvaɪvəbəl / (AmE), / səˈvaɪvəbəl / (BrE)
able to continue to exist, remain effective, or be endured after a particular event or condition.

Examples

Survivable contract terms
The contract contains survivable clauses that remain in effect even after termination.
Thanks to new safety measures, the accident was considered survivable for most passengers.
The company ensured that its key policies were survivable during major organizational changes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: enduring - continuing or lasting over time
  • antonym: perishable - likely to decay or not last
