St. John the Apostle

proper noun
disciple of Jesus; New Testament author
8.2
Pronunciation: / seɪnt dʒɑn ði əˈpɑsəl / (AmE), / seɪnt dʒɒn ði əˈpɒsl̩ / (BrE)
one of the twelve original disciples of Jesus, traditionally believed to be the author of the Gospel of John, three epistles, and the Book of Revelation in the New Testament

Examples

St. John the Apostle disciple
In Christian tradition, St. John the Apostle is referred to as 'the beloved disciple.'
Many scholars debate whether the Gospel truly reflects the writings of St. John the Apostle.
The church was named after St. John the Apostle, who is said to have authored several books of the New Testament.
