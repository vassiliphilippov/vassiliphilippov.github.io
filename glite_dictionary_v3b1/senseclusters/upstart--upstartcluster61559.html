upstart

noun
arrogant person risen to power
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpˌstɑrt / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌstɑːt / (BrE)
a person who has risen suddenly to wealth or power, often seen as arrogant or lacking social graces

Examples

A young upstart
Despite being called an upstart, he quickly gained the admiration of his peers.
Many viewed the young entrepreneur as an upstart who didn't respect tradition.
The established politicians dismissed her as an upstart with no real experience.
Her critics dismissed her as an upstart, but she proved her worth through hard work.
Many saw him as an upstart who did not understand the traditions of the company.
The upstart billionaire quickly made enemies among the city's old-money families.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nobody - a person of no importance or authority
  • synonym: parvenu - a person who has recently risen to a higher social or economic class, often regarded as lacking refinement
