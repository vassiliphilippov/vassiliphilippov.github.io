urodele

noun
tailed amphibian, e.g., salamander
Pronunciation: / ˈjʊrəˌdil / (AmE), / ˈjʊərəˌdiːl / (BrE)
A member of an order of amphibians that have a tail throughout life and typically resemble lizards, such as newts and salamanders.

Examples

A urodele amphibian
A biologist observed several rare urodeles in the mountain stream.
The fire salamander is a well-known urodele found in European forests.
Unlike frogs, every urodele retains its tail throughout life.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anuran - an order of amphibians lacking tails (frogs and toads)
  • synonym: salamander - a tailed amphibian related to newts, often included in Urodela
