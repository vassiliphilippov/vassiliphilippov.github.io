twelve-tone music

noun
systematic use of all twelve chromatic tones
7.3 6.8
Pronunciation: /ˈtwelv.toʊn ˈmjuː.zɪk/ (AmE), /ˈtwelv.təʊn ˈmjuː.zɪk/ (BrE)
A modern system of musical composition in which all twelve notes of the chromatic scale are used in a fixed, recurring order, as introduced by Arnold Schoenberg.

Examples

Twelve-tone music system
Many twentieth-century composers experimented with twelve-tone music after learning about Schoenberg's techniques.
She wrote her dissertation on the development of twelve-tone music in the early twentieth century.
The piece is a classic example of twelve-tone music, systematically using every note in the chromatic scale.

Related Terms

  • synonym: atonal music - music without a key center or tonal focus
  • synonym: serialism - method of composition involving ordered series of musical elements
