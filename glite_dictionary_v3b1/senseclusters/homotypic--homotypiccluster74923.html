homotypic

adjective
same type specimen
Pronunciation: / ˌhoʊməˈtɪpɪk / (AmE), / ˌhəʊməˈtɪpɪk / (BrE)
in biological classification, describing names that are based on the same type specimen as another name

Examples

Homotypic species names
In taxonomy, two species names are considered homotypic if they share the same type specimen.
The researcher clarified that the two genera were not homotypic but actually based on different specimens.
Understanding whether names are homotypic helps avoid confusion in scientific classification.
