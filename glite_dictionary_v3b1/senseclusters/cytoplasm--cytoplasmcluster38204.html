cytoplasm

noun
cell material excluding the nucleus
1.0 4.2
Pronunciation: /ˈsaɪ.tə.plæz.əm/ /; / /ˈsaɪ.toʊ.plæz.əm/ (AmE), /ˈsaɪ.tə.plæz.əm/ /; / /ˈsaɪ.təʊ.plæz.əm/ (BrE)
The material within a living cell, excluding the nucleus, containing the organelles and various dissolved substances.

Examples

The cytoplasm inside cells
Certain viruses use the cytoplasm as a site to replicate their genetic material.
The cytoplasm contains many structures called organelles that perform essential functions for the cell.
When viewed under a microscope, the cytoplasm appears as a jelly-like substance filling the space outside the nucleus.

Related Terms

  • synonym: protoplasm - the living substance of a cell or organism
