Agonidae

noun
a family of marine fishes (poachers)
Pronunciation: / ˌæɡəˈnaɪdiː / (AmE), / ˌæɡəˈnɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of marine fishes, commonly known as poachers, characterized by bony armor plates and found in cold northern waters

Examples

Agonidae fish family
A specimen from the Agonidae family was found along the Pacific coastline.
Marine biologists often study Agonidae to understand adaptations in cold water environments.
The Agonidae are easily recognized by their rows of bony plates along the body.
