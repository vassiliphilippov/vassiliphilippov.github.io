resegregate

verb
to separate again
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈsɛɡrəˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈsɛɡrɪˌɡeɪt / (BrE)
to cause parts of a mixture or system to separate again, often by size, density, or composition

Examples

To resegregate the mixture
When the solution cooled, the components began to resegregate into distinct layers.
Engineers observed that the particles would resegregate after being mixed by vibration.
If left undisturbed, the grains in the mixture will eventually resegregate according to their density.
