clog

verb
impede movement, often with weight
Pronunciation: / klɑɡ /; / klɑg / (AmE), / klɒɡ /; / klɒg / (BrE)
to block or become blocked so that movement, progress, or flow is slowed or stopped

Examples

To clog a drain
The heavy chains clogged the prisoners, making escape impossible.
The horse was clogged to prevent it from bolting.
Thick mud clogged the wheels, stopping the carriage from moving further.
If too many particles clog together, the filter will stop working properly.
In cold weather, grease can clog inside the pipes and prevent draining.
The medicine is designed to prevent arteries from clogging with fatty deposits.
An excess of paperwork can clog up the approval process.
The drain quickly became clogged after the heavy rainstorm.
Traffic can clog the streets during rush hour.

Related Terms

  • synonym: block - to obstruct or prevent movement through something
  • antonym: clear - to remove an obstruction, allowing movement or progress
  • synonym: clot - to form into lumps or a thickened mass, especially of blood
  • synonym: hinder - to make it difficult for something to happen
  • synonym: obstruct - to block or prevent movement or progress
  • antonym: unclog - to remove an obstruction from something
