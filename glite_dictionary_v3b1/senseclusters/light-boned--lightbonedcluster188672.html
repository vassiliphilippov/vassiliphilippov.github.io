light-boned

adjective
having a light or delicate bone structure
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪt ˌboʊnd / (AmE), / ˈlaɪt ˌbəʊnd / (BrE)
having a bone structure that is lighter or more delicate in proportion to the surrounding flesh

Examples

Light-boned body
Greyhounds are known for their light-boned bodies and speed.
His light-boned hands contrasted with his broad shoulders.
The dancer's light-boned frame made her movements look effortless.

Related Terms

  • antonym: heavy-boned - having a bone structure that is heavy
  • synonym: slender - having a thin or graceful form
