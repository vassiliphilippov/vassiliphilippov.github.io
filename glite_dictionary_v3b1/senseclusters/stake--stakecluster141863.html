stake

verb
risk or wager something of value
Pronunciation: / steɪk / (AmE), / steɪk / (BrE)
to risk or wager something of value (such as money, reputation, or cryptocurrency) on the outcome of an event or situation

Examples

To stake money
He decided to stake his life savings on the outcome of the game.
She was willing to stake her career on the success of the new project.
They staked everything they had in the hope of starting a successful business.
He decided to stake $100 on the underdog to win the match.
Many fans stake large sums on their favorite teams during the playoffs.
She staked everything she had on the outcome of the race.
Many investors choose to stake their cryptocurrencies to earn passive income from network rewards.
She staked her ETH on a validator and began receiving small daily interest payments.
Before participating, read the white paper to understand how you will stake tokens safely.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bet - to stake money or other valuables on the outcome of an event
  • synonym: delegate - to assign or entrust tokens to a validator or staking pool in some proof-of-stake systems.
  • synonym: lock (up) - to make assets temporarily unavailable for withdrawal, for instance to participate in a protocol.
  • synonym: risk - to expose to the possibility of loss or harm
  • synonym: wager - to risk money or something of value on the outcome of an event
