bilge keel

noun
lengthwise fin on a ship to reduce rolling
9.9 8.2
Pronunciation: /bɪldʒ kiːl/ (AmE), /bɪldʒ kiːl/ (BrE)
either of two long, narrow fins fitted lengthwise to the outside of a ship's hull to reduce rolling movement

Examples

The bilge keels on ship
During dry dock, the engineers inspected both bilge keels for signs of damage.
Many cargo ships are equipped with a bilge keel to reduce unwanted rolling motion.
The addition of a bilge keel helped stabilize the vessel in rough seas.
