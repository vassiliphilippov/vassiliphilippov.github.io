fust

verb
to become musty
9.9
Pronunciation: / fʌst / (AmE), / fʌst / (BrE)
to become or cause something to become musty, stale, or moldy, often developing an unpleasant smell

Examples

Bread fusts quickly
If you leave bread in a damp place, it will soon fust and become inedible.
The old cellar began to fust after years without proper ventilation.
Wine stored in poorly sealed barrels may fust and lose its quality.

Related Terms

  • antonym: freshen - to make something smell or feel fresh again
  • synonym: molder - to decay or rot slowly, especially with a musty smell
