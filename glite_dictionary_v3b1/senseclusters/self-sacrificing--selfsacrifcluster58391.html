Willing to give up one's own interests, needs, or wishes for the sake of others.

Examples

A self-sacrificing person
His self-sacrificing nature made him a beloved leader among his peers.
She is self-sacrificing, always putting her family's needs before her own.
The nurse's self-sacrificing attitude inspired everyone in the hospital.

Synonyms

  • self-denying
    7.0 0.03/m
    self-denying accepts personal hardship and avoids comforts for others, while self-sacrificing is willing to give up interests or needs for others.
  • self-abnegating
    9.9 0.06/m
    self-abnegating often denies personal wants and follows strict self-control, while self-sacrificing focuses on giving up interests for others.
  • self-giving
    7.3 0.32/m
    self-giving gives time and care to others freely and warmly, while self-sacrificing highlights giving up your own needs.
  • selfless
    3.2 3.01/m
    selfless cares deeply about other people's needs and puts them first, while self-sacrificing stresses giving up one's own interests.
  • unselfish
    2.0 0.91/m
    unselfish will put others' needs or interests before their own in everyday choices, while self-sacrificing often means larger personal loss.
  • altruistic
    6.7 2.16/m
    altruistic shows concern for others' well-being as a motive, while self-sacrificing describes actual acts of giving up personal interests.
