tobacco mosaic virus

noun
plant virus causing leaf mottling in tobacco
Pronunciation: / təˈbækoʊ moʊˈzeɪɪk ˈvaɪrəs / (AmE), / təˈbækəʊ məʊˈzeɪɪk ˈvaɪərəs / (BrE)
a plant virus that infects tobacco and other plants, causing mosaic-like mottling of the leaves; the first virus to be discovered (in 1892) and extensively studied in virology

Examples

tobacco mosaic virus infection
Infected leaves showed symptoms of mottling, a classic sign of tobacco mosaic virus infection.
Many laboratory experiments use tobacco mosaic virus to study how plant viruses spread and infect their hosts.
The tobacco mosaic virus was the first virus ever discovered by scientists in the late nineteenth century.

  • synonym: virus - infectious agent that replicates only inside living cells
