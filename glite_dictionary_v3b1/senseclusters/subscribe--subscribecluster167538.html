subscribe

verb
arrange to receive something regularly
1.8 2.8
Pronunciation: / səbˈskraɪb / (AmE), / səbˈskraɪb / (BrE)
to arrange to receive something (such as a publication, service, or online updates) regularly, often by signing up or paying in advance

Examples

To subscribe to a magazine
I subscribe to several science journals to keep up with the latest research.
They decided to subscribe to a meal delivery service to save time.
You can subscribe to the newsletter for weekly updates on events.
Every month, I subscribe to a local magazine to support community journalism.
She decided to subscribe $20 a month to a children's hospital charity.
They subscribe annually to several online educational platforms.
I decided to subscribe to that science channel to get weekly video updates and notifications.
She subscribed to several independent creators so she would see their new posts in her feed.
They are subscribing to the channel now, because it posts tutorials on programming every Monday.

Related Terms

  • synonym: donate - to give money or goods for a cause
  • synonym: follow - to choose to receive someone’s posts or updates on a social or content platform
  • antonym: unsubscribe - to cancel a subscription to a publication or service
