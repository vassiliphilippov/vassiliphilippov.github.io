coronary

noun
heart attack caused by artery blockage
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔːrəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˈkɒr.ə.nər.i / (BrE)
A blockage of blood flow in a coronary artery, often leading to a heart attack.

Examples

A coronary event
After his coronary, he made significant changes to his lifestyle.
Family history of coronaries can increase a person's risk for heart disease.
She was rushed to the hospital when the doctors suspected a coronary.

Related Terms

  • synonym: heart attack - a sudden and sometimes fatal occurrence of coronary thrombosis
