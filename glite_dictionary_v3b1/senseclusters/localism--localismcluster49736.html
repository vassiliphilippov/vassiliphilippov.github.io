localism

noun
preference for a particular locality
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊkəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈləʊkəlɪzəm / (BrE)
preference for or loyalty to a particular locality, often resulting in emphasis on local customs, interests, or control

Examples

Localism in politics
Intense localism can sometimes hinder cooperation between neighboring towns.
Journalists noted the rise of localism as citizens prioritized nearby issues over national debates.
The politician's localism appealed to voters who valued community traditions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cosmopolitanism - the idea of being free from local or national limitations; worldliness
  • synonym: regionalism - strong identification with a specific geographic region
