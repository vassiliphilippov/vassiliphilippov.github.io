dynamism

noun
theory explaining universe through force or energy
7.1 9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪnəˌmɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈdɑːnəmɪzəm / (BrE)
any of various theories or philosophical systems that explain the universe as the result of an immanent force or energy, rather than matter or static principles

Examples

Philosophical dynamism
Historically, dynamism has been influential in debates over the relationship between matter and energy.
In some branches of philosophy, dynamism offers an alternative to materialism in explaining the nature of reality.
The philosopher argued that dynamism better accounts for the apparent vitality of the universe.
