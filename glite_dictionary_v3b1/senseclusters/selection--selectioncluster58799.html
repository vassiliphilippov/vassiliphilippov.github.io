selection

noun
biological process of evolutionary adaptation
2.3 3.1
Pronunciation: /sə.ˈlek.ʃən/ (AmE), /sə.ˈlek.ʃən/ (BrE)
(in biology) the process by which organisms better adapted to their environment tend to survive and reproduce, leading to evolutionary change.

Examples

Natural selection
Darwin's theory of selection transformed our understanding of how species evolve over time.
Environmental pressures play a key role in selection by favoring certain traits.
The rapid adaptation of bacteria to antibiotics is a result of natural selection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: evolution - the process by which different kinds of living organisms are thought to have developed from earlier forms
