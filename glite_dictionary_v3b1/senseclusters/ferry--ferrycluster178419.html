ferry

verb
carry people or goods between places
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛri / (AmE), / ˈfɛri / (BrE)
to carry people or goods from one place to another, especially across water, usually regularly and by boat or other vehicle

Examples

To ferry people
Each morning, the buses ferry hundreds of workers into the city.
Helicopters were used to ferry supplies to the stranded villagers.
The school district ferries students from rural areas to the central campus.
The company ferries over a thousand passengers each day between the islands.
They ferried supplies to the mainland during the storm.
We will ferry across the river when the boat arrives.
Helicopters were used to ferry the injured workers to safety.
The boatman will ferry tourists across the river every hour.
They ferried supplies to the island during the storm.
