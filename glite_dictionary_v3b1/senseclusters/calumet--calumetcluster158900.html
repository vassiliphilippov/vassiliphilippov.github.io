calumet

noun
ceremonial Native American pipe
Pronunciation: / ˈkæl.juˌmɛt / (AmE), / ˈkæl.juː.mɛt / (BrE)
A highly decorated ceremonial pipe used by some Native American peoples, especially in ritual contexts or as a symbol of peace.

Examples

A calumet pipe
During the treaty ceremony, both parties exchanged gifts and shared a calumet.
The museum exhibit displayed an ornate calumet from the 18th century.
The tribal elders gathered to smoke the calumet and discuss important matters.

Related Terms

  • synonym: peace pipe - a ceremonial smoking pipe used as a symbol of peace among some Native American peoples
