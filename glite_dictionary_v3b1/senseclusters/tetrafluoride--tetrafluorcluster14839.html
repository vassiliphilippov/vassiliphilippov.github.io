tetrafluoride

noun
compound with four fluorines
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛtrəˈflʊraɪd / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrəˈflʊəraɪd / (BrE)
a chemical compound in which four fluorine atoms are bonded to another element

Examples

Uranium tetrafluoride
Uranium tetrafluoride is used in the process of uranium enrichment.
Scientists studied the properties of sulfur tetrafluoride in the laboratory.
The production of tetrafluoride compounds requires careful handling due to their reactivity.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: difluoride - a compound with two fluorine atoms bonded to another element
  • synonym: fluoride - a compound containing fluorine
  • degree higher: hexafluoride - a compound with six fluorine atoms bonded to another element
