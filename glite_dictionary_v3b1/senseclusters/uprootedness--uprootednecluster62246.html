uprootedness

noun
state of being uprooted
Pronunciation: / ʌpˈruːtɪdnəs / (AmE), / ʌpˈruːtɪdnəs / (BrE)
the state or condition of being removed from one's original place or roots, especially referring to plants or trees.

Examples

Tree uprootedness
The uprootedness of the ancient trees changed the landscape after the hurricane.
Many people feel a sense of uprootedness when they move to a new country.
The uprootedness caused by construction left the garden bare and empty.

  • antonym: rootedness - the state of being firmly established in a place
