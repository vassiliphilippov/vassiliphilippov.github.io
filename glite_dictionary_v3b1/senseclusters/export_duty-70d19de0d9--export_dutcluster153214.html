export duty

noun
tax imposed on exported goods
4.5 0.06/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…õksp…îrt ÀåduÀêti / (AmE), / Àà…õksp…îÀêt ÀådjuÀêti / (BrE)
A tax or duty imposed by a government on goods being transported out of a country.

Examples

New export duty
A new export duty was implemented to encourage local industry by making raw materials more expensive to ship abroad.
Many countries use export duty as a means to regulate the outflow of critical natural resources.
The government announced an increase in the export duty on raw cotton this year.

Synonyms

  • customs duty
    3.5 0.16/m
    customs duty is charged by border officials on goods entering or leaving a country, while export duty refers only to goods leaving.
  • tariff
    5.6 14/m
    tariff is a government rate for taxes on imports or exports, while export duty names the tax specifically on goods sent out.
  • impost
    9.6 0.04/m
    impost is a formal, old-fashioned word for a tax on trade, while export duty is the modern term for tax on goods leaving.

Related Terms

  • antonym: import duty - a tax imposed on goods brought into a country
