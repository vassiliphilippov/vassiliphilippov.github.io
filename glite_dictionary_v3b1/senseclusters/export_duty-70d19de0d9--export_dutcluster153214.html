export duty

noun
tax imposed on exported goods
4.3 3.7
Pronunciation: /ˈek.spɔːrt ˈduː.tiː/ /; / /ˈek.spɔːrt ˈdjuː.tiː/ (AmE), /ˈek.spɔːt ˈdjuː.tiː/ /; / /ˈɪk.spɔːt ˈdjuː.tiː/ /; / /ˈek.spɔːt ˈdʒuː.tiː/ (BrE)
A tax or duty imposed by a government on goods being transported out of a country.

Examples

New export duty
A new export duty was implemented to encourage local industry by making raw materials more expensive to ship abroad.
Many countries use export duty as a means to regulate the outflow of critical natural resources.
The government announced an increase in the export duty on raw cotton this year.
