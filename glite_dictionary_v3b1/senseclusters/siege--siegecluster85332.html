siege

verb
to surround and attack
7.4
Pronunciation: /siːdʒ/ (AmE), /siːdʒ/ (BrE)
to surround and attack a place in order to capture it, often by cutting off supplies

Examples

To siege a fortress
The army sieged the fortress for months until the defenders surrendered.
During the war, enemy forces sieged the capital, cutting off all supplies.
The rebels sieged the town, hoping to force a quick surrender.
The army began to siege the fortress at dawn, hoping to force a surrender.
For months, the rebels sieged the city, cutting off all food and water supplies.
The general decided to siege the castle rather than attack it directly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: besiege - to surround a place with armed forces to capture it
  • antonym: defend - to protect from attack
