cuttable

adjective
able to be cut
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌtəbəl / (AmE), / ˈkʌtəbəl / (BrE)
Able to be cut; capable of being divided or sliced by cutting.

Examples

A cuttable material
Metals must be sufficiently cuttable for machining processes.
The stone is too hard to be cuttable by ordinary tools.
This cheese is so soft that it's barely cuttable with a knife.

Related Terms

  • synonym: incisable - able to be cut or incised
  • antonym: uncuttable - not able to be cut
