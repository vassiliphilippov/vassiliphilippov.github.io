rope-a-dope

noun
boxing tactic of feigning vulnerability on the ropes
Pronunciation: / ˌroʊp ə ˈdoʊp /; / ˈroʊp ə ˌdoʊp / (AmE), / ˌrəʊp ə ˈdəʊp /; / ˈrəʊp ə ˌdəʊp / (BrE)
a boxing tactic where a fighter pretends to be trapped or weak to lure an opponent into wasting energy; also used figuratively to mean outsmarting someone by appearing passive

Examples

The rope-a-dope tactic
Muhammad Ali famously used the rope-a-dope to tire out George Foreman in the Rumble in the Jungle.
Politicians sometimes use a verbal rope-a-dope, letting opponents exhaust themselves with criticism.
The fighter relied on his rope-a-dope technique to outlast his stronger rival.
During the championship fight, the boxer employed a rope-a-dope to tire his opponent.
She used a rope-a-dope in negotiations, allowing the other side to reveal their strategy first.
The politician's rope-a-dope tactic left his rivals exhausted and off balance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: counterpunch - a punch made in reaction to an opponent's attack
  • synonym: feint - a deceptive or pretended blow, thrust, or other movement
