root rot

noun
plant disease causing root decay
4.1 4.1
Pronunciation: /ruːt rɑːt/ /; / /rʊt rɑːt/ (AmE), /ruːt rɒt/ (BrE)
A disease of plants characterized by the decay of roots, typically caused by soil-borne fungi.

Examples

Root rot disease
Brown, mushy roots are a classic sign of root rot in houseplants.
Excessive watering can make plants vulnerable to root rot.
Gardeners often lose seedlings to root rot during wet seasons.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blight - a plant disease, typically one caused by fungi
  • synonym: wilt - a plant disease causing drooping and loss of firmness in plants
