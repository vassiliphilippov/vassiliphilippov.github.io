dirt

noun
soil; earth's surface layer
1.0 29/m
Pronunciation: / d…ùt / (AmE), / d…úÀêt / (BrE)
the top layer of the earth's surface, made up of soil, humus, and disintegrated rock

Examples

Dirt on the ground
His shoes were covered in dirt after the hike.
Plants need good dirt to grow healthy and strong.
The children spent the afternoon playing in the dirt behind the house.

Synonyms

    2.8 2.00/m
    topsoil is the upper rich layer where most plants grow, while dirt names loose earth at the surface.
  • surface soil
    3.4 0.12/m
    surface soil is the upper layer rich in organic matter and nutrients, while dirt is the general loose surface earth.
  • A horizon
    8.4 0.00/m
    A horizon is the technical name for the top mineral layer with humus, while dirt is the common name for surface earth.
  • soil
    1.0 48/m
    soil is the mixture of minerals and organic matter that plants use, while dirt refers to loose surface earth or grime.
