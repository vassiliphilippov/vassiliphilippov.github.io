method

noun
technique of acting using personal emotions
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛθ.əd / (AmE), / ˈmeθ.əd / (BrE)
a technique of acting in which the actor draws on personal emotions and experiences to create a believable performance, especially as developed by Stanislavsky and popularized in American theatre and film

Examples

Method acting technique
Many famous actors credit the method for their most powerful performances.
She studied method acting to better connect with her characters on stage.
The director encouraged the cast to use the method to bring authenticity to their roles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: method acting - a style of acting in which the actor uses personal experience and emotion to portray a role
