aneroid

noun
barometer without liquid
Pronunciation: / ˈænəˌrɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈænərɔɪd / (BrE)
a barometer that measures atmospheric pressure without the use of liquid

Examples

An aneroid barometer
An aneroid is preferred over a mercury barometer for portable use.
My grandfather kept an old aneroid on his study wall.
The ship's captain checked the aneroid before deciding to sail.
