mealy

adjective
containing or made of meal
Pronunciation: / ˈmiːli / (AmE), / ˈmiːli / (BrE)
containing meal (ground grain) or having a dry, powdery, or grainy texture like meal

Examples

A mealy texture
The baker used a mealy mixture to prepare the traditional bread.
The food had a mealy texture, indicating it was made with ground maize.
This recipe calls for mealy flour as a key ingredient.
She avoided eating the mealy apple because of its dry texture.
The potatoes turned a bit mealy after being cooked for too long.
The surface of the old painting felt mealy to the touch.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grainy - having a texture made up of small particles or grains
  • synonym: mealy-mouthed - hesitant or unwilling to state something plainly
  • antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface; free from perceptible projections, lumps, or indentations
