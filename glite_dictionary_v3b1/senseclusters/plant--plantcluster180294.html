plant

verb
firmly establish an idea in the mind
7.1 5.2
Pronunciation: / plænt / (AmE), / plɑːnt / (BrE)
to introduce an idea, feeling, or memory into someone's mind in a way that makes it become firmly established

Examples

To plant an idea
Negative rumors were planted by his competitors to damage his reputation.
She tried to plant the idea of traveling abroad in her parents' minds.
The teacher hoped to plant a love of reading in her students.

Related Terms

  • antonym: eradicate - to remove completely from the mind
  • synonym: instill - to gradually but firmly establish in someone's mind
