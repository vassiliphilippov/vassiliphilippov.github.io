encomienda

noun
colonial labor grant
9.9
Pronunciation: /en.kə.ˈmjen.də/ /; / /en.kə.ˈmiːn.də/ (AmE), /en.kə.ˈmjen.də/ /; / /en.kə.ˈmiːn.də/ (BrE)
a system in colonial Spanish America where the Spanish crown gave colonists the right to demand labor and tribute from indigenous people

Examples

The encomienda system
The Spanish colonist was granted an encomienda that included several indigenous villages.
Under the encomienda system, native people were forced to provide labor and tribute.
Many historians debate the impact of the encomienda on indigenous societies in the Americas.
The Spanish crown granted an encomienda to each settler in the new colony.
Many indigenous people suffered under the encomienda system during the colonial era.
Historians debate the long-term effects of the encomienda on native populations in Latin America.
The Spanish crown established the encomienda to organize labor in its American colonies.
Many indigenous communities suffered under the harsh conditions of the encomienda system.
Historians debate the long-term effects of the encomienda on native populations in Latin America.
