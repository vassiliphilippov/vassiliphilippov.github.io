superstring

noun
elementary particle in theoretical physics
5.1 9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈsuː.pər.strɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈsjuː.pə.strɪŋ/ /; / /ˈsuː.pə.strɪŋ/ (BrE)
a hypothetical one-dimensional elementary particle, in some theories of physics, that is considered a fundamental component of space and matter

Examples

A superstring theory
If superstrings exist, they are thought to vibrate at different frequencies, producing various particles.
Many physicists study the properties of the superstring to understand the fundamental structure of the universe.
The concept of a superstring emerged from efforts to unify all fundamental forces under one theory.

Related Terms

  • synonym: string theory - the theoretical framework in which point-like particles are replaced by one-dimensional objects called strings
