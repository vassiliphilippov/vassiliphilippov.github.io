tautologically

adverb
in a self-evident way
9.0
Pronunciation: / tɔtəˈlɑdʒɪkli /; / tɔːtəˈlɑːdʒɪkli / (AmE), / tɔːtəˈlɒdʒɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that repeats the same idea or is true by definition, often unnecessarily or without adding new information

Examples

A tautologically true statement
The statement was tautologically true, offering no new information to the discussion.
He explained the rule tautologically, simply restating the definition in different words.
Saying 'a circle is round' is tautologically obvious to everyone.
The politician answered tautologically, repeating the same point without addressing the question.
If you argue tautologically, your reasoning will not convince anyone who wants real evidence.
The explanation was given tautologically, simply restating the original statement in different words.

Related Terms

  • synonym: circularly - in a way that uses circular reasoning
  • antonym: logically - in a way that uses clear and sound reasoning
  • synonym: redundantly - in a way that is unnecessarily repetitive
