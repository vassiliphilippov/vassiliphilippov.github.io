literary pirate

noun
a person who plagiarizes literary works
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪtərɛri ˈpaɪrət / (AmE), / ˈlɪtərəri ˈpaɪrət / (BrE)
A person who copies another person's literary works, ideas, or words and presents them as their own; a plagiarist.

Examples

A literary pirate steals poems
He was accused of being a literary pirate after publishing another writer's poems under his own name.
Many authors have taken legal action against literary pirates to protect their intellectual property.
The novel was shamelessly copied by a literary pirate and sold overseas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plagiarist - a person who plagiarizes the work of others
