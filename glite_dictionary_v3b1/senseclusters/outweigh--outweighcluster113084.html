to be more important, valuable, or significant than something else; to have greater weight or influence

Examples

Benefits outweigh risks
Her desire for adventure outweighed her fear of the unknown.
In this case, the risks clearly outweigh the rewards.
The benefits of the new policy outweigh the potential drawbacks.

Synonyms

  • outbalance
    7.1 0.02/m
    outbalance suggests one factor tips the scale by being more important, while outweigh states that something is more significant overall.
  • overbalance
    6.7 0.03/m
    overbalance describes a stronger effect or influence that tips a situation, while outweigh names what is more important in a decision.
  • preponderate
    9.4 0.01/m
    preponderate is used when numbers or influence are larger, while outweigh focuses on being more important or having greater weight.
  • outstrip
    6.8 0.79/m
    outstrip often means to go past in speed, size, or success, while outweigh talks about being more important or heavier in value.
