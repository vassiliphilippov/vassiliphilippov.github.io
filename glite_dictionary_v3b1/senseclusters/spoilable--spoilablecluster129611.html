spoilable

adjective
liable to spoil or decay
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈspɔɪ.lə.bəl / (AmE), / ˈspɔɪ.lə.bəl / (BrE)
liable to decay, spoil, or become unfit for use or consumption

Examples

A spoilable food

Most fresh vegetables are highly spoilable and need refrigeration.
They used non-spoilable materials to avoid any risk of decay during shipping.
