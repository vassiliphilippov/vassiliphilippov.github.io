positive stimulus

noun
stimulus causing a desirable response
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑzətɪv ˈstɪmjələs / (AmE), / ˈpɒzətɪv ˈstɪmjʊləs / (BrE)
a stimulus that produces a desirable or rewarding response, especially in psychology or behavioral studies

Examples

A positive stimulus effect
A positive stimulus often increases the likelihood of a desired behavior being repeated.
Receiving praise can be a positive stimulus that encourages better performance.
The introduction of a reward system acted as a positive stimulus in the classroom.

Related Terms

  • antonym: negative stimulus - a stimulus with undesirable or aversive consequences
