leitmotiv

noun
recurrent musical theme linked to person or idea
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪtˌmoʊˌtiːf / (AmE), / ˈlaɪtməʊtiːf / (BrE)
A recurrent musical phrase associated with a particular person, idea, or situation, especially in an opera.

Examples

A leitmotiv phrase
Each character in the opera has a distinctive leitmotiv that helps tell the story.
The villain's appearances were always accompanied by a sinister leitmotiv in the orchestra.
Wagner popularized the use of the leitmotiv to unify musical and dramatic elements.

Related Terms

  • synonym: motif - a short musical idea or pattern
