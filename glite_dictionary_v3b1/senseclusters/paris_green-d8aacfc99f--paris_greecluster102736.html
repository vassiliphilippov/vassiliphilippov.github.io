Paris green

noun
a toxic green chemical pigment/insecticide
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛrɪs ˈɡriːn / (AmE), / ˌparɪs ˈɡriːn / (BrE)
a highly toxic, bright green chemical compound, formerly used as a pigment and insecticide, consisting of copper(II) acetoarsenite

Examples

Paris green insecticide
Paris green was once a popular insecticide before safer alternatives became available.
The artists of the nineteenth century often used Paris green in their paintings, despite its toxicity.
The vibrant shade of Paris green made it a favorite, though it was hazardous to human health.
