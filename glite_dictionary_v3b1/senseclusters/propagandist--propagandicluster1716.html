propagandist

noun
person who spreads propaganda
Pronunciation: / ˌprɑː.pəˈɡæn.dɪst / (AmE), / ˌprɒ.pəˈɡæn.dɪst / (BrE)
a person who spreads information, especially biased or misleading information, to promote a particular political cause or point of view

Examples

A political propagandist
As a skilled propagandist, he could sway crowds with persuasive rhetoric.
She was accused of being a propagandist for the regime after publishing that article.
The government hired a propagandist to influence public opinion during the campaign.

Related Terms

  • synonym: advocate - a person who publicly supports or recommends a particular cause or policy
  • antonym: opponent - a person who disagrees with or resists a cause or government
