Aristarchus of Samothrace

proper noun
ancient Greek grammarian and literary scholar
9.9
Pronunciation: /ær.ɪ.ˈstɑːr.kəs əv sæ.ˈmoʊ.θreɪs/ (AmE), /ær.ɪ.ˈstɑː.kəs əv sæ.ˈməʊ.θreɪs/ (BrE)
an ancient Greek grammarian (c. 217–145 BC) known for his commentary on the Iliad and Odyssey, and for establishing critical principles in textual scholarship.

Examples

Aristarchus of Samothrace the grammarian
Aristarchus of Samothrace is often credited with refining ancient literary criticism.
Scholars still reference the methods developed by Aristarchus of Samothrace in their textual analyses.
The commentary of Aristarchus of Samothrace on Homer’s poems influenced generations of classicists.
