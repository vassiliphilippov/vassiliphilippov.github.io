respiratory distress syndrome of the newborn

noun
lung disorder in newborn babies
Pronunciation: / rɛˈspɪrəˌtɔri dɪˈstrɛs sɪnˌdroʊm ʌv ðə ˈnuˌbɔrn / (AmE), / rɪˈspɪrətəri dɪˈstrɛs ˈsɪndrəʊm ɒv ðə ˈnjuːbɔːn / (BrE)
a serious medical condition affecting newborn babies, especially those born prematurely, in which the lungs cannot expand properly due to a lack of surfactant, leading to breathing difficulties

Examples

Newborn respiratory distress syndrome of the newborn
Doctors closely monitor premature infants for signs of respiratory distress syndrome of the newborn.
The risk of respiratory distress syndrome of the newborn increases with lower gestational age.
Treatment for respiratory distress syndrome of the newborn may include supplemental oxygen and surfactant therapy.
