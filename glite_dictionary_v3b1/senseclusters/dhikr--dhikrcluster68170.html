dhikr

noun
Islamic devotional recitation
Pronunciation: / ˈzɪkər / (AmE), / ˈzɪkə / (BrE)
a devotional act in Islam involving the repeated recitation of God's names or sacred phrases, often as a form of remembrance, either individually or in a group

Examples

Many Muslims perform dhikr quietly after their daily prayers.

Every morning, he begins his day with a quiet dhikr to find peace and focus.
The group gathered in the mosque to perform dhikr together after evening prayers.
She whispered a short dhikr taught by her teacher to calm her mind before sleep.

She found peace and focus by participating in dhikr with her friends.
