pour down the drain

verb
waste something irretrievably
Pronunciation: / pɔr daʊn ðə dreɪn / (AmE), / pɔː daʊn ðə dreɪn / (BrE)
to waste something valuable, such as time, effort, or money, so that it is lost and cannot be recovered

Examples

To pour down the drain money
All the money we invested in the project just poured down the drain when it was canceled.
If you aren't careful with electricity, a lot of energy will pour down the drain needlessly.
She hated seeing her efforts pour down the drain after months of hard work.
