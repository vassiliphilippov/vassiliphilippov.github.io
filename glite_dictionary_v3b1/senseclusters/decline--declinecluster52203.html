decline

noun
a gradual decrease; reduction
Pronunciation: / dɪˈklaɪn / (AmE), / dɪˈklaɪn / (BrE)
a gradual decrease or loss in amount, quality, value, or health; a period of deterioration

Examples

A decline in sales
Many scientists warn about the decline of biodiversity worldwide.
The company reported a sharp decline in sales last quarter.
There has been a gradual decline in public trust in the institution.
His health went into a rapid decline after the accident.
The decline of the Roman Empire has been the subject of many historical studies.
There has been a noticeable decline in the quality of customer service lately.
The decline in battery life was noticeable after several months of use.
The scientists observed a rapid decline in voltage during the experiment.
There has been a steady decline in the number of students enrolling in the course.
