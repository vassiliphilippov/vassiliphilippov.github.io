head for the hills

verb
run away to escape danger or trouble
3.6
Pronunciation: / hɛd fəɹ ðə hɪlz /; / hɛd fər ðə hɪlz / (AmE), / hɛd fə ðə hɪlz / (BrE)
to leave or run away quickly to escape danger or trouble

Examples

To head for the hills quickly
After the scandal broke, the politician headed for the hills to escape the media.
As soon as the alarm sounded, everyone headed for the hills to avoid being caught.
When they saw the approaching storm, people living near the coast headed for the hills.
If the business fails, investors may head for the hills to protect themselves.
When the scandal broke, most of the executives tried to head for the hills.
When they heard the alarm, everyone decided to head for the hills immediately.
