kiosk

noun
small open-fronted booth or stall
3.3 2/m
Pronunciation: / kiÀà…ësk / (AmE), / ÀàkiÀê…ísk / (BrE)
A small, open-fronted booth or structure used for selling goods, providing information, or offering services, typically found in public places.

Examples

An information kiosk
An information kiosk near the entrance helped tourists find their way around the museum.
I bought a magazine from the kiosk at the train station.
The shopping mall has several small kiosks selling snacks and phone accessories.

Synonyms

  • booth
    1.4 15/m
    a booth is often partly enclosed and used for selling, voting, or information, while a kiosk is open-fronted and serves the public.
  • stall
    1.0 4/m
    a stall is a market stand that shows and sells goods, while a kiosk is a small public booth for sales, services, or information.
  • sales booth
    2.4 0.01/m
    a sales booth is a temporary stall used at fairs or exhibitions for selling, while a kiosk is often permanent and gives services or information.
