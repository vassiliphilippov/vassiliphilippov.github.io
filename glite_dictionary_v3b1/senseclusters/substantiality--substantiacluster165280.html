substantiality

noun
quality of being substantial or important
Pronunciation: / səbˌstæn.ʃiˈæl.ə.ti / (AmE), / səbˌstæn.ʃɪˈæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality or state of being substantial; the condition of having real existence, significance, or importance.

Examples

The substantiality of facts
Despite its beautiful appearance, the proposal lacked substantiality and was ultimately rejected.
The substantiality of the evidence convinced the jury of the defendant's guilt.
They questioned the substantiality of his claims, demanding more concrete proof.

Related Terms

  • antonym: insubstantiality - the quality of lacking substance or importance
  • synonym: substance - that which has material or real existence; physical matter or the essence of something
