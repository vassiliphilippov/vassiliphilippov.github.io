gault

noun
Cretaceous clay formation
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɡɔlt / (AmE), / ɡɔːlt / (BrE)
a type of heavy, blue-grey clay soil, sometimes used for making bricks or pottery and sometimes known for containing fossils

Examples

The gault clay
The gault in this region is famous for its well-preserved ammonite fossils.
Farmers often find the gault difficult to plow because of its heavy, sticky texture.
A layer of gault separates the chalk hills from the sandy soils above.
The bricks were made from local gault found near the riverbank.
Farmers avoided planting crops in fields with too much gault because of poor drainage.
The potter shaped the vase from fine gault.

Related Terms

  • antonym: chalk - a soft, white, porous sedimentary rock, another common geological formation
  • synonym: clay - a natural, fine-grained earth material that is plastic when wet
  • antonym: loam - a fertile soil mixture of sand, silt, and clay
