barbed

adjective
having sharp points or barbs
4.4 0.26/m
Pronunciation: / b…ërbd / (AmE), / b…ëÀêbd / (BrE)
having or covered with sharp points or barbs, such as those on wire, feathers, or certain animals and plants.

Examples

Barbed wire fence
Some fish have barbed fins to deter attackers.
The farmer installed barbed wire around his property for security.
The hedgehog's barbed spines protect it from predators.

Synonyms

    9.9 0.00/m
    barbellate is a technical term for having tiny bristle-like barbs, while barbed is the common adjective for objects with sharp points.
  • spiny
    3.9 0.32/m
    spiny is covered with spines or sharp points on plants or animals, while barbed means having small barbs or hooks.
  • thorny
    4.2 0.69/m
    thorny has sharp thorns mainly on plants, while barbed describes items with barbs that can be metal, feather, or plant points.
  • prickly
    3.3 2.15/m
    prickly feels rough and has many small sharp points on plants or skin, while barbed points are usually hooked or pointed.
  • burred
    9.1 0.01/m
    burred is used when surfaces are covered with burs or seed hooks on plants, while barbed means having pointed barbs in general.
  • burry
    8.8 0.00/m
    burry describes something with burs or prickly seed pods sticking to fur or clothes, while barbed is a broader sharp-pointed description.
  • bristled
    4.5 0.01/m
    bristled has stiff hair-like points or short spines on an object or animal, while barbed focuses on hooked sharp points.
  • briery
    9.2 0.01/m
    briery means covered in briars or tangled thorny plants, while barbed simply means having sharp points or barbs on any surface.
