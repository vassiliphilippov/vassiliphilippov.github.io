red heat

noun
state of glowing red-hot from heat
3.0
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛd ˌhiːt / (AmE), / ˌred ˈhiːt / (BrE)
A temperature at which an object, especially metal, becomes visibly red due to being heated.

Examples

Red heat metal
The metal glowed at red heat before the blacksmith shaped it.
When iron reaches red heat, it becomes malleable and easier to work with.
You must heat the rod to red heat for proper forging.

Related Terms

  • synonym: incandescence - the emission of visible light by a body due to its high temperature
  • degree higher: white heat - state of being heated to the point of glowing white
