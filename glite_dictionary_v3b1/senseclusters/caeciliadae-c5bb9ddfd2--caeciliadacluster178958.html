Caeciliadae

noun
family of legless burrowing amphibians
Pronunciation: / siːˌsɪliəˈdiː / (AmE), / siːˌsɪliəˈdiː / (BrE)
A family of legless, burrowing amphibians, commonly known as caecilians, belonging to the order Gymnophiona.

Examples

The Caeciliadae family
Scientists studying rainforest habitats often encounter the Caeciliadae beneath logs and moist soil.
The Caeciliadae are adapted for a subterranean lifestyle, making them difficult to observe in the wild.
Unlike other amphibians, members of the Caeciliadae lack limbs and have elongated, segmented bodies.
