warrant

verb
guarantee or assure quality or truth
3.0
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔrənt / (AmE), / ˈwɒrənt / (BrE)
to formally guarantee the quality, accuracy, or condition of something; to provide assurance that something is true or will happen

Examples

Warrants the quality
He warrants that all the products meet the highest standards of quality.
I cannot warrant the accuracy of these statistics without double-checking them.
The company will warrant your new phone against defects for two years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assure - to tell someone something positively or confidently to dispel doubts
  • synonym: guarantee - to provide a formal assurance or promise, especially regarding quality or reliability
