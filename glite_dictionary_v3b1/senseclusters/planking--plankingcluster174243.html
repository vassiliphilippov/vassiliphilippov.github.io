planking

noun
planks forming a ship's surface or flooring
5.1 3.3
Pronunciation: /ˈplæŋ.kɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈplæŋ.kɪŋ/ (BrE)
planks used as material, the structure or surface made from planks, or the process of covering something with planks

Examples

Wooden planking
The old ship's planking had rotted and needed to be replaced.
Water seeped through the gaps in the planking after the storm.
Workers spent weeks repairing the planking along the hull.
After the renovation began, piles of planking were stacked beside the house.
The old ship's deck was made entirely of planking.
We need more planking for the new floor in the hallway.
Proper planking ensures the floor remains stable and even.
Restoring the old house required extensive planking of the damaged floorboards.
The carpenters finished the planking of the ship's hull last week.
