tymbal

noun
insect sound organ
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtɪmbəl / (AmE), / ˈtɪmbəl / (BrE)
a sound-producing organ in certain insects, especially cicadas, that vibrates to create noise

Examples

Insect tymbal organ
The cicada's loud song is produced by a specialized tymbal on its abdomen.
Scientists studied the tymbal to understand how insects generate such powerful sounds.
Male cicadas use their tymbals to attract mates during the summer months.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stridulation - the act of producing sound by rubbing body parts together, as in some insects
