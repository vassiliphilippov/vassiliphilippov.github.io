disruptive

adjective
causing disorder or disturbance
5.8 7.17/m

Causing or tending to cause unrest, disorder, or disturbance; characterized by insubordination or interruption to normal activity.

Examples

Disruptive behavior in class
His disruptive behavior in class made it hard for others to concentrate.
Security was called after a disruptive incident broke out in the audience.
The protest turned disruptive, forcing the police to intervene and restore order.

Synonyms

  • disorderly
    4.4 1.28/m
    disorderly describes unruly, undisciplined behavior in public, while disruptive causes interruptions or unrest that stop normal activity.
  • unruly
    4.3 3.20/m
    unruly is hard to control and often noisy in groups, while disruptive actively interrupts routines and can cause unrest.
  • troublemaking
    3.9 0.05/m
    troublemaking means causing problems on purpose to annoy others, while disruptive may break routines and disturb people without intent.
  • interruptive
    6.5 0.04/m
    interruptive breaks the flow or continuity of events, whereas disruptive also brings disorder or unrest beyond simple interruption.

Related Terms

  • antonym: orderly - well-arranged, peaceful, or behaving in a controlled way
  • synonym: turbulent - characterized by conflict, disorder, or confusion
  • synonym: unruly - difficult to control; disorderly or disruptive
