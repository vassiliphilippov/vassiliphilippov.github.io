Yalta

proper noun
resort city in Crimea; site of a 1945 Allied conference
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈjɑl.tə / (AmE), / ˈjɑːl.tə / (BrE)
A resort city on the Black Sea in Crimea, noted as the location of the Allied conference between Roosevelt, Stalin, and Churchill in February 1945.

Examples

Yalta conference
Many historians consider the decisions made at Yalta to have shaped postwar Europe.
She visited Yalta to see the Livadia Palace, where the famous 1945 conference was held.
The agreements reached at Yalta were subjects of debate for decades after the war.
