diplotene

noun
stage of prophase in meiosis
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪpləˌtin / (AmE), / ˈdɪplətiːn / (BrE)
the fourth stage of prophase in meiosis, during which homologous chromosomes begin to separate but remain attached at chiasmata

Examples

The diplotene stage
During diplotene, homologous chromosomes begin to separate but remain connected at chiasmata.
Errors occurring during diplotene may affect genetic variation in gametes.
The diplotene stage can be observed with a microscope in laboratory studies of cell division.

Related Terms

  • synonym: meiosis - type of cell division reducing chromosome number
  • synonym: prophase - first stage of cell division in mitosis or meiosis
