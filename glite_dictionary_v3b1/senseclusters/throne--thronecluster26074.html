throne

verb
sit on a throne as a ruler
Pronunciation: / θroʊn / (AmE), / θrəʊn / (BrE)
to sit on a throne as a ruler, or to install someone as a monarch

Examples

To throne a king
In legends, only the true king could be throned in the sacred hall.
The ceremony will throne the new monarch next month.
The young prince was throned after his father's abdication.
It was the custom to throne each heir in the ancient hall of kings.
The archbishop will throne the new king during the coronation ceremony.
They throned her with a crown and royal robes in the grand palace.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dethrone - remove from a throne or position of power
  • synonym: enthrone - to install on a throne or in a position of authority
