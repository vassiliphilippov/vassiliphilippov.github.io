walk-in

adjective
large enough to enter (of a room or space)
3.8 3.1
Pronunciation: /ˈwɔːk.ɪn/ /; / /ˈwɑːk.ɪn/ (AmE), /ˈwɔːk.ɪn/ (BrE)
(of a room or space) large enough for a person to enter and move around inside, especially used of closets, refrigerators, or similar enclosures.

Examples

Walk-in refrigerator
She keeps all her clothes organized in a spacious walk-in closet.
The restaurant installed a new walk-in refrigerator to store fresh produce.
They converted a small room into a walk-in pantry for extra food storage.
