cave

verb
give in or surrender under pressure
Pronunciation: / keɪv / (AmE), / keɪv / (BrE)
to give in or yield to pressure, demands, or temptation after resisting (informal)

Examples

He caved under pressure
After hours of negotiation, the opposing party caved and accepted the terms.
She stood her ground for weeks but eventually had to cave.
The company refused to cave to public pressure, despite ongoing protests.
After hours of negotiation, she finally caved and agreed to their terms.
He promised not to eat dessert, but he caved when he saw the chocolate cake.
The team was determined not to cave under the intense pressure from their opponents.

Related Terms

  • antonym: resist - to withstand or oppose the effect of something
  • synonym: submit - to accept or yield to a superior force or authority
  • synonym: yield - to give way under pressure; to surrender or submit
