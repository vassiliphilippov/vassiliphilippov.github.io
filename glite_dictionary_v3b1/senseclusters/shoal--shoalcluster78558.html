shoal

verb
become or make shallow (water)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ʃoʊl / (AmE), / ʃəʊl / (BrE)
(of water) to become shallow or less deep

Examples

The river shoals quickly
Heavy rains can suddenly shoal certain areas of the stream during the wet season.
The river began to shoal as it approached the sandbar.
Years of sediment deposits have shoaled the harbor, making it difficult for large ships to enter.
After the drought, the river began to shoal near the banks.
If this section of the channel continues to shoal, it may become impassable for larger boats.
Over many years, the coastal waters shoaled due to sediment accumulation.
