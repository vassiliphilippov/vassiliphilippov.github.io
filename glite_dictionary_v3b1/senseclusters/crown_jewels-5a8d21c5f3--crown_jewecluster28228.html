crown jewels

noun
most valuable assets (metaphorical)
5.0
Pronunciation: /kraʊn ˈdʒuː.əlz/ /; / /kraʊn dʒuːlz/ (AmE), /kraʊn ˈdʒuː.əlz/ /; / /kraʊn ˈdʒʊ.əlz/ (BrE)
(figurative) the most valuable or important assets, products, or components of an organisation, project, or collection, especially those that should be protected.

Examples

Company crown jewels
The startup's crown jewels are its proprietary algorithm and the customer database that drives growth.
During the acquisition, the board insisted the crown jewels be excluded from the sale.
Protecting the company's crown jewels became the top priority after the cyberattack was discovered.
