dehumanization

noun
the act of making people seem less human
1.0 7.8
Pronunciation: / diˌhjuːmənəˈzeɪʃən /; / diˌhjuːmənɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌhjuːmənʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of depriving people of human qualities, dignity, or individuality, often by treating them in a less human or degrading way

Examples

The dehumanization of workers
Industrialization has sometimes led to the dehumanization of workers.
Many activists protest against the dehumanization of refugees in political rhetoric.
The film explores the dehumanization of prisoners in the justice system.
Many critics argue that technology can contribute to the dehumanisation of personal relationships.
The dehumanisation of prisoners during wartime has been widely condemned by human rights organizations.
The film powerfully explores the dehumanisation experienced by marginalized communities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dehumanize - to deprive someone of human qualities
  • synonym: depersonalization - the act of making someone feel or appear less like a person
  • antonym: humanisation - the process of making something more humane or civilized
  • antonym: humanization - the process of making something more humane or giving it human qualities
