collectivity

noun
group acting as one
6.6
Pronunciation: /kə.lek.ˈtɪv.ə.tɪ/ (AmE), /kə.lek.ˈtɪv.ə.tɪ/ (BrE)
a group of people considered as a single unit, especially when acting together for a shared purpose or identity

In some cultures, collectivity is valued more highly than individual achievement.

In some cultures, the needs of the collectivity are valued above those of the individual.
The project succeeded because the collectivity worked toward a common goal.
