Cabala

noun
Jewish mystical tradition
Pronunciation: / kəˈbɑːlə / (AmE), / kəˈbɑːlə / (BrE)
A mystical Jewish tradition based on interpretations of the Hebrew scriptures, developed chiefly from the 7th to the 18th centuries.

Examples

Cabala teachings
She devoted years to studying the Cabala and its mystical teachings.
Some Rabbis believe that the secrets of the Cabala can deepen one's spiritual understanding.
The Cabala has influenced many writers and philosophers over the centuries.
