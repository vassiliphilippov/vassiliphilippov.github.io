somatesthesia

noun
faculty for bodily sensation
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsoʊməθɛsˈθiːʒə / (AmE), / ˌsəʊməθesˈθiːzɪə /; / ˌsəʊməθɛsˈθiːziə / (BrE)
the ability or process of perceiving bodily sensations, such as touch, body position, and internal organ feelings

Examples

Somatesthesia perception
After the accident, she experienced reduced somatesthesia on her left side.
Research into somatesthesia helps us understand how our bodies process physical sensations.
The loss of somatesthesia can lead to difficulties in movement and balance.
He noticed a change in his somatesthesia after the injury, especially in his fingertips.
Physical therapists often assess somatesthesia when evaluating a patient's sensory function.
Some neurological disorders can impair somatesthesia, making it difficult to sense touch or limb position.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kinaesthesia - awareness of position and movement of the parts of the body
  • synonym: proprioception - sense of the relative position of one's own body parts
