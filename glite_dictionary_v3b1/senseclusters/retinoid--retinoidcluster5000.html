retinoid

noun
vitamin A compound
6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈre.tɪ.nɔɪd/ (AmE), /ˈre.tɪ.nɔɪd/ (BrE)
a chemical compound related to vitamin A, often used in skin treatments for acne or aging

Examples

A retinoid treatment
The dermatologist recommended a retinoid to help reduce the appearance of fine lines.
Many over-the-counter creams contain a mild retinoid for daily skincare.
Some people experience dryness when they first start using a retinoid treatment.

Some people experience skin irritation when they first use retinoids in their skincare routine.
Prescription retinoids are stronger than those found in over-the-counter creams.
