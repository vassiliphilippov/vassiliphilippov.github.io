doornail

noun
large-headed nail for doors (historical)
Pronunciation: / ˈdɔrˌneɪl / (AmE), / ˈdɔːneɪl / (BrE)
A large-headed nail, once commonly used to decorate or fasten doors, especially in older buildings.

Examples

A large doornail
Craftsmen would often use a doornail to strengthen and embellish the entryway of a home.
She examined the old doornail embedded in the medieval church door, marveling at its weathered surface.
The ancient wooden door was studded with heavy iron doornails that had rusted over time.
