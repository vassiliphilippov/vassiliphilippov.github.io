pyrophoric

adjective
spontaneously flammable
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpaɪrəˈfɔrɪk / (AmE), / ˌpaɪrəˈfɒrɪk / (BrE)
able to ignite spontaneously when exposed to air at or below room temperature

Examples

Pyrophoric metal powder
Laboratory staff must store pyrophoric chemicals in special containers to prevent accidents.
The metal powder is highly pyrophoric and should never be exposed to air.
Because it is pyrophoric, this substance requires careful handling and storage procedures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flammable - easily set on fire
  • antonym: nonflammable - not able to catch fire easily
