magistrate

noun
minor judge or civil law official
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmædʒ.ə.streɪt / (AmE), / ˈmædʒ.ɪ.streɪt / (BrE)
an official who acts as a minor judge, especially for cases involving minor offenses or civil matters

Examples

A magistrate court
As a magistrate, she often dealt with cases involving petty crimes and disputes.
Before proceeding to a higher court, the issue was first reviewed by a local magistrate.
The magistrate ruled that the defendant must pay a fine for the minor traffic violation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: judge - an official who presides over court proceedings
