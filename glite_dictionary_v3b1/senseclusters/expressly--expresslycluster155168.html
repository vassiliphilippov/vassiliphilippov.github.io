expressly

adverb
specifically, deliberately
3.4 3.6
Pronunciation: /ek.ˈspres.liː/ /; / /ɪk.ˈspres.liː/ (AmE), /ɪk.ˈspres.liː/ /; / /ek.ˈspres.liː/ (BrE)
clearly, specifically, or for a particular purpose

Examples

Set aside expressly
The funds were set aside expressly to support local artists in the community.
The law was expressly designed to protect endangered species from harm.
This room is expressly reserved for staff meetings and official events.
The instructions expressly state that you must not use water near the device.
He expressly forbade anyone from entering his office after hours.
The contract expressly mentions that late payments will result in a penalty.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clearly - in a way that is easy to see or understand
  • synonym: explicitly - in a clear and direct way
  • antonym: implicitly - in a way that is not directly stated
  • synonym: specifically - clearly defined or identified; for a special purpose
