heart-lung machine

noun
device for circulating and oxygenating blood during surgery
6.5 4.1
Pronunciation: /hɑːrt lʌŋ mə.ˈʃiːn/ (AmE), /hɑːt lʌŋ mə.ˈʃiːn/ (BrE)
a device used during heart surgery to take over the functions of the heart and lungs by oxygenating and circulating blood throughout the body

Examples

The heart-lung machine device
Advancements in heart-lung machine technology have greatly improved patient survival rates during complex procedures.
During open-heart surgery, the patient was connected to the heart-lung machine to maintain blood circulation.
The heart-lung machine allows surgeons to operate on a still heart by taking over its function temporarily.
