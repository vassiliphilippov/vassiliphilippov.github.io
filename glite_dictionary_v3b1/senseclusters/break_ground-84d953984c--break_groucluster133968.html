break ground

verb
to begin a construction project or task
7.6
Pronunciation: / breɪk ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / breɪk ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
to begin work on a new construction project or to start an important undertaking

Examples

To break ground on buildings
Construction companies often hold ceremonies when they break ground for major buildings.
Innovative scientists regularly break ground in the field of medical research.
The city will break ground on the new library later this year.
