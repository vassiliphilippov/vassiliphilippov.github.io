unchaperoned

adjective
without chaperone assistance
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnˈʃæpəˌroʊnd / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈʃæpərəʊnd / (BrE)
not assisted or accompanied by a chaperone, especially referring to a protein folding without molecular chaperone help in biology.

Examples

Unchaperoned protein folding
The newly synthesized enzyme remained unchaperoned in the cytosol and failed to fold correctly.
Researchers studied how unchaperoned proteins behave differently from those with chaperone assistance.
Without proper guidance, some molecules remain unchaperoned and do not reach their functional shape.

Related Terms

  • antonym: assisted - helped or supported by someone or something
  • antonym: chaperoned - accompanied or supervised by a chaperone
