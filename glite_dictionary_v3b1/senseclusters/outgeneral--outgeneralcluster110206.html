outgeneral

verb
defeat by superior strategy
4.9
Pronunciation: / aʊtˈdʒɛnərəl / (AmE), / aʊtˈdʒɛnərəl / (BrE)
to surpass or defeat an opponent by superior strategy or tactics, especially in military or competitive situations

Examples

To outgeneral an opponent
Despite having fewer resources, she outgeneraled her competitors with a bold plan.
The young commander managed to outgeneral his more experienced rival in the decisive battle.
They tried to predict his moves, but he always seemed to outgeneral them.

Related Terms

  • synonym: outmaneuver - to defeat by skillful movement or clever strategy
  • synonym: outwit - to defeat by being more clever
