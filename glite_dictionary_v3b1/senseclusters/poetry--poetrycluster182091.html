poetry

noun
expression of beauty or emotion
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊətri / (AmE), / ˈpəʊɪtri / (BrE)
language or expression that has great beauty or evokes deep feeling, resembling the qualities of traditional poetry.

Examples

Poetry in motion
His speech contained a poetry that moved the entire audience.
The movement of the dancer had the poetry of a gentle breeze.
There is a certain poetry in the way she describes the night sky.

Related Terms

  • antonym: prose - ordinary written or spoken language without metrical structure; the opposite of poetry
