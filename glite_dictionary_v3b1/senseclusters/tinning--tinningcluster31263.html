tinning

noun
application of solder to wire ends
Pronunciation: / ˈtɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of coating or covering a surface with a thin layer of tin or solder, usually to protect it or prepare it for joining

Examples

Good tinning process
Good tinning ensures a strong electrical connection in electronic assemblies.
He spent several minutes tinning the leads before attaching them to the circuit board.
Proper tinning of wires makes the soldering process cleaner and easier.
Food cans often undergo tinning to prevent rust and extend shelf life.
Poor tinning can lead to rapid corrosion in industrial equipment.
The plumber recommended tinning the copper pipe ends before soldering.

Related Terms

  • synonym: galvanizing - coating iron or steel with a protective layer of zinc
  • synonym: soldering - joining metal items by melting and flowing a filler metal (solder)
