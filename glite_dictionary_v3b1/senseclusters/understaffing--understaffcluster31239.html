understaffing

noun
not enough staff
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈstæfɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈstɑːfɪŋ / (BrE)
the situation in which there are not enough employees to do the necessary work

Examples

Chronic understaffing
Chronic understaffing in the restaurant led to long wait times for customers.
The school faced serious understaffing issues after several teachers resigned.
Many companies struggle with understaffing during the busy holiday season.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overstaffing - the situation of having more employees than needed
  • synonym: staff shortage - a lack of enough employees to meet work demands
