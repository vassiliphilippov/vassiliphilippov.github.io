Actaea rubra

noun
a perennial herb with poisonous red berries
9.9
Pronunciation: / ækˈtiː.ə ˈruː.brə / (AmE), / ækˈtiː.ə ˈruː.brə / (BrE)
A North American perennial herb with compound leaves and small white flowers that produce bright red poisonous berries, commonly known as red baneberry.

Examples

Actaea rubra red berries
Children should be cautioned not to eat the berries of Actaea rubra, as they are highly poisonous.
In shaded woodland gardens, Actaea rubra adds a splash of red among the green foliage.
The toxic berries of Actaea rubra should be avoided by both humans and pets.
