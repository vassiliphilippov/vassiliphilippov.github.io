coat

verb
cover with a layer of something
1.0 2.4
Pronunciation: / koʊt / (AmE), / kəʊt / (BrE)
to cover something with a layer of a particular substance

Examples

To coat a surface
Before painting, make sure to coat the surface with primer.
Dust the cake with sugar, then coat it with a layer of chocolate.
She coated the chicken in breadcrumbs before frying it.
After you finish the doughnuts, coat them with powdered sugar.
Make sure to coat the pan evenly with oil before cooking.
The fence was coated with a fresh layer of paint to protect it.
A thin layer of dust coated the old books on the shelf.
Lightly coat the baking tray with oil before placing the dough on it.
The candies are coated in sugar to give them extra sweetness.
