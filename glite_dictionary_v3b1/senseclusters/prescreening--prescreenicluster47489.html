prescreening

noun
advance media showing
3.4
Pronunciation: /priː.ˈskriː.nɪŋ/ /; / /prɪ.ˈskriː.nɪŋ/ (AmE), /priː.ˈskriː.nɪŋ/ /; / /prɪ.ˈskriː.nɪŋ/ (BrE)
an advance showing of a film, television program, or other media to a limited audience before its general release

Examples

Movie prescreening
The director invited a select group of fans to the prescreening of the new movie.
Journalists attended the prescreening to write early reviews before the official release.
Tickets for the prescreening sold out within minutes due to high demand.
