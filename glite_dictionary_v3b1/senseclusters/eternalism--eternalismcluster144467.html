eternalism

noun
philosophy of time
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈtɜːr.nə.lɪ.zəm/ /; / /ə.ˈtɜːr.nə.lɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈtɜː.nə.lɪ.zəm/ /; / /ə.ˈtɜː.nə.lɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the philosophical belief that all points in time—past, present, and future—are equally real and exist simultaneously

Examples

Philosophical eternalism
In the philosophy of time, eternalism suggests that the future is just as real as the past and present.
Many physicists discuss eternalism when exploring the nature of time in the universe.
According to eternalism, every moment in history continues to exist, not just the present.

Related Terms

  • synonym: block universe - the idea that the universe is a four-dimensional block where all times exist equally
  • antonym: presentism - the belief that only the present moment is real
