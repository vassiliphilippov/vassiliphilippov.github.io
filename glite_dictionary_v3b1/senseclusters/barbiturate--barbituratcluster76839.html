barbiturate

noun
sedative-hypnotic drug; barbituric acid derivative
Pronunciation: / bɑːrˈbɪtʃʊˌreɪt / (AmE), / bɑːˈbɪtjʊˌreɪt / (BrE)
a drug derived from barbituric acid, used as a sedative and hypnotic; an overdose can be fatal

Examples

A barbiturate drug
Abuse of barbiturate drugs has declined due to the availability of safer alternatives.
Accidental overdoses of barbiturates can lead to respiratory failure and death.
The patient was prescribed a barbiturate to help with severe insomnia.

  • antonym: stimulant - a substance that raises levels of physiological or nervous activity
