Simon Peter

proper noun
disciple of Jesus, first Pope

Pronunciation: /ˈsaɪ.mən ˈpiː.tər/ /; / /ˈsaɪ.mən ˈpɪ.tər/ (AmE), /ˈsaɪ.mən ˈpiː.tə/ (BrE)
One of the twelve apostles of Jesus Christ, regarded as the leader of the apostles and, according to Catholic tradition, the first Pope.

Examples

Simon Peter apostle leader
According to the New Testament, Simon Peter denied Jesus three times before the rooster crowed.
Catholic tradition holds that Simon Peter was the first Pope of the church.
Many churches around the world are named after Simon Peter in honor of his legacy.
