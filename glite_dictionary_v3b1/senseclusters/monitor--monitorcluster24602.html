monitor

noun
electronic device for checking transmissions
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑː.nɪ.tɚ /; / ˈmɑːnɪtər / (AmE), / ˈmɒn.ɪ.tə /; / ˈmɒnɪtə / (BrE)
an electronic device used to observe, check, or keep track of the quality or content of audio, video, data, or systems

Examples

Audio monitor
Before broadcasting live, they checked the monitor for any audio issues.
The radio station uses a monitor to ensure its signal remains clear and uninterrupted.
The technician installed a new monitor to check the video feed quality.
A sudden temperature rise triggered the monitor to sound an alert in the server room.
Technicians rely on a monitor to detect faults in the engine's performance.
The hospital installed a new monitor to keep track of the patient's vital signs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alarm - a device that gives a warning of danger or malfunction
  • synonym: display - an electronic device that visually represents information
