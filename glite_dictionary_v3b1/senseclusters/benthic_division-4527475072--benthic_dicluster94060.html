benthic division

noun
region including sea bottom and shoreline zones
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛnθɪk dɪˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / ˈbɛnθɪk dɪˈvɪʒən / (BrE)
a region of a body of water that includes the sea floor and the littoral (shoreline) zones

Examples

The benthic division zone
Changes in temperature and salinity often affect the biodiversity within the benthic division.
Deep-sea researchers collected samples from the benthic division to study life forms inhabiting the ocean floor.
The benthic division is crucial for understanding nutrient cycles and marine ecosystems.
