testamentary trust

noun
trust created by a will
7.9 7.6
Pronunciation: /te.stə.ˈmen.tər.iː trʌst/ /; / /te.stə.ˈmen.triː trʌst/ (AmE), /te.stə.ˈmen.tə.riː trʌst/ /; / /te.stə.ˈmen.triː trʌst/ (BrE)
A legal arrangement in which a trust is created under a will and becomes effective upon the death of the person who made the will.

Examples

A testamentary trust under will
Funds from the testamentary trust will be distributed annually to the named beneficiaries.
She set up a testamentary trust in her will to provide for her grandchildren's education expenses.
The lawyer explained how a testamentary trust could help manage assets for minor children after the client's death.

Related Terms

  • antonym: living trust - a trust created and operating during the grantor's lifetime
