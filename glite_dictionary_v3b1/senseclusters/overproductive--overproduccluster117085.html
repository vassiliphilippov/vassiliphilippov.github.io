overproductive

adjective
excessively productive
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərprəˈdʌktɪv / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəprəˈdʌktɪv / (BrE)
producing more than is needed, wanted, or healthy (can refer to farms, factories, or natural environments)

Examples

Overproductive farm land
The lake became overproductive after years of fertilizer runoff from nearby farms.
Scientists warned that overproductive algae could threaten the fish population in the river.
An overproductive ecosystem can sometimes cause more harm than good to local wildlife.
The overproductive farm had to find new markets for its extra crops.
An overproductive gland can cause health problems if not treated.
The team became overproductive and created more reports than anyone could read.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: productive - producing a lot or achieving good results
  • synonym: prolific - producing a large amount of something
  • antonym: underproductive - producing less than is needed or expected
  • antonym: unproductive - not producing much or any result
