natural immunity

noun
inherent immunity present from birth
3.7 3.0
Pronunciation: / ˈnætʃrəl ɪˈmjunəti / (AmE), / ˈnatʃ(ə)rəl ɪˈmjuːnəti / (BrE)
immunity to a disease that is inherent to an individual and not acquired through vaccination or prior infection.

Examples

Natural immunity from mother
Babies receive natural immunity from their mothers that helps protect them from certain illnesses early in life.
Not everyone develops strong natural immunity after being exposed to a virus.
Researchers are studying how natural immunity differs from immunity gained through vaccination.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acquired immunity - immunity gained after exposure to an antigen or through vaccination
