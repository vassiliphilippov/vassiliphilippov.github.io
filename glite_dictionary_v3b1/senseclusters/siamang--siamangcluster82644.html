siamang

noun
large black gibbon from Southeast Asia
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsiː.ə.mæŋ / (AmE), / ˈsiː.ə.mæŋ / (BrE)
a large black gibbon native to Sumatra and the Malay Peninsula, distinguished by webbed toes and a loud call

Examples

A siamang gibbon
At the zoo, we watched a siamang swinging effortlessly between the trees.
The siamang is the largest of all gibbons and is known for its impressive vocalizations.
Unlike other gibbons, the siamang has a webbing between its second and third toes.
