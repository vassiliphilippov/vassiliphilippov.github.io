steamtightness

noun
being sealed against steam leakage
5.3
Pronunciation: / stiːmˈtaɪtnəs / (AmE), / stiːmˈtaɪtnəs / (BrE)
The quality or condition of being sealed so that steam cannot escape.

Examples

The steamtightness quality
Engineers check the steamtightness of the system before starting the turbines.
Poor steamtightness can lead to energy loss and reduced efficiency in power plants.
The old pipes had to be replaced to improve the steamtightness of the heating system.
