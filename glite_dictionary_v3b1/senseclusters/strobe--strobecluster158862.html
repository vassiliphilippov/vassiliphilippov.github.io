strobe

verb
to flash rapidly
5.4
Pronunciation: /stroʊb/ /; / /strəʊb/ (AmE), /strəʊb/ /; / /stroʊb/ (BrE)
to flash on and off rapidly, especially referring to lights or visual effects

Examples

Lights strobe quickly
The club lights began to strobe as the music reached its peak.
During the thunderstorm, the sky seemed to strobe with each lightning strike.
Some people find it uncomfortable when the screen starts to strobe rapidly.
The stage lights strobed in time with the music, creating a dramatic effect.
During the thunderstorm, lightning strobed across the sky every few seconds.
The emergency vehicle's lights strobed as it sped down the street.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flash - to shine or give off light suddenly and briefly
  • synonym: flicker - to shine with a light that is sometimes bright and sometimes weak
