interoceptor

noun
receptor detecting internal body stimuli
Pronunciation: / ɪn.t̬əˈrɑː.sɛp.tɚ / (AmE), / ɪn.təˈrɒ.sɛp.tə / (BrE)
a sensory receptor that responds to stimuli originating within the body

Examples

Internal interoceptors
Pain or discomfort often begins when interoceptors detect abnormal conditions in internal organs.
Scientists study interoceptors to understand how the body senses hunger, thirst, and other internal needs.
Some interoceptors help regulate blood pressure by monitoring changes within blood vessels.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exteroceptor - a receptor that responds to stimuli from outside the body
  • synonym: proprioceptor - a receptor that responds to position or movement of the body
