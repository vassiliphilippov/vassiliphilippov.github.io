Masora

noun
Jewish textual tradition on the Hebrew Scriptures
7.3 9.9
Pronunciation: /mə.ˈsoʊ.rə/ /; / /mə.ˈsɔː.rə/ (AmE), /mə.ˈsɔː.rə/ /; / /mə.ˈsəʊ.rə/ (BrE)
the body of notes, textual commentary, and traditions concerning the Jewish Scriptures, compiled by Jewish scribes between AD 600 and 900, often appearing in the margins or at the end of biblical texts

Examples

The Masora notes
Scholars rely on the Masora to understand ancient textual decisions in the Hebrew Bible.
The marginal notes of the Masora often clarify the meaning of obscure Hebrew words.
Without the Masora, much of the Jewish tradition of scriptural transmission might have been lost.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Masoretic Text - authoritative Hebrew text of the Jewish Bible, shaped by the Masora
