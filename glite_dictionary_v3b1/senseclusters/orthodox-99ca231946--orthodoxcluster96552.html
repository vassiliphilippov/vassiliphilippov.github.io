Orthodox

adjective
of the strictly observant branch of Judaism
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrθəˌdɑks / (AmE), / ˈɔːθədɒks / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the branch of Judaism that strictly observes traditional beliefs and practices

Examples

Orthodox Jewish family
Many Orthodox Jews strictly observe the rules of the Sabbath.
She was raised in an Orthodox Jewish family and attended religious school.
The neighborhood has several Orthodox synagogues and kosher markets.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Conservative - relating to a branch of Judaism that seeks a middle path between Orthodox and Reform
  • antonym: Reform - relating to a branch of Judaism that adopts a more liberal interpretation of Jewish law and tradition
