intel

noun
secret information
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌtɛl / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌtɛl / (BrE)
information or knowledge, especially secret or useful details, often related to military or strategic matters

Examples

Secret intel
The agent provided valuable intel about the enemy's next move.
We need more intel before we can make a final decision.
She always seems to have the latest intel on company developments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: data - facts or information collected for analysis
  • antonym: gossip - casual or unconstrained conversation about others, often unverified
  • synonym: intelligence - secret information collected about an enemy or competitor
