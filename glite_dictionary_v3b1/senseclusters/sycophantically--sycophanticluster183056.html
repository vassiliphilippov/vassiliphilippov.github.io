sycophantically

adverb
excessively flattering
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪkəˈfæntɪkli / (AmE), / ˌsɪkəˈfæntɪkli / (BrE)
in a manner that shows excessive flattery or obedience to someone in authority, often to gain favor

Examples

To sycophantically praise
She laughed sycophantically at all of the manager's jokes, hoping to be noticed.
The students nodded sycophantically as the principal explained the new rules.
He responded sycophantically to every comment made by the celebrity guest.

Related Terms

  • antonym: defiantly - in a way that shows resistance or bold disobedience
  • synonym: obsequiously - in an overly eager or servile way
  • degree lower: respectfully - in a way that shows respect
