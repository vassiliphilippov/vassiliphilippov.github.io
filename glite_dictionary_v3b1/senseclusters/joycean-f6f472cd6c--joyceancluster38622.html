Joycean

adjective
of James Joyce's style
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɔɪsiən / (AmE), / ˈdʒɔɪsiən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of the works, style, or techniques of James Joyce

Examples

Joycean writing style
The author's Joycean narrative style includes long, complex sentences and stream of consciousness.
Critics praised the Joycean elements in her latest novel, especially the inventive use of language.
Some readers find Joycean writing difficult because of its experimental structure and dense allusions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: modernist - relating to modernism in literature or art
