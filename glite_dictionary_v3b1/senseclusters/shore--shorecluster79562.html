shore

noun
land at the edge of water
1.0 33/m
Pronunciation: /  É…îr / (AmE), /  É…îÀê / (BrE)
the land along the edge of a sea, lake, or other large body of water

Examples

Sandy shore
Children were playing on the shore while their parents watched nearby.
The boat drifted slowly toward the shore after the storm.
We walked for hours along the shore, collecting seashells as we went.

Synonyms

  • waterside
    3.2 0.22/m
    waterside is land beside a river or lake often used for walks, while shore means land by seas or large bodies of water.
  • shorefront
    3.6 0.09/m
    shorefront is the area directly along the water often with buildings and shops, while shore is the general land by water.
  • shoreland
    3.4 0.19/m
    shoreland is land that borders lakes or rivers and often appears in maps or law, while shore is everyday land by water.
  • strand
    8.4 0.42/m
    strand is a poetic or old word for a beach or stretch of coast, while shore is modern everyday land by water.
  • shoreline
    2.7 7.11/m
    shoreline is the visible line where land meets water and it changes with tides, while shore is the land by a water body.
