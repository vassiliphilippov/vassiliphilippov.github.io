drowsing

adjective
half asleep; lightly sleeping
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdraʊzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdraʊzɪŋ / (BrE)
being half asleep or in a light sleep, especially in a relaxed state.

Examples

Drowsing in chair
After dinner, I found my grandfather drowsing in his favorite chair.
She sat drowsing in the warm afternoon sun, barely awake.
The lecture left several students drowsing at their desks.

Related Terms

  • antonym: awake - fully alert and not sleeping
  • synonym: dozing - being half asleep; lightly sleeping
