Malinowski

proper noun
British anthropologist, pioneer of participant observation
Pronunciation: / mæ.lɪˈnɒf.ski / (AmE), / mal.ɪˈnɒf.ski / (BrE)
Bronisław Malinowski (1884–1942), a British anthropologist born in Poland, known for pioneering the method of participant observation in fieldwork.

Examples

Malinowski anthropologist
Malinowski's work in the Trobriand Islands revolutionized modern anthropology.
Many students study the methods developed by Malinowski in their ethnography courses.
The contributions of Malinowski to cultural anthropology remain influential today.
