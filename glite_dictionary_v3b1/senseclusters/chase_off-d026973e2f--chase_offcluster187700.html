chase off

verb
drive away; scare away
5.3
Pronunciation: / tʃeɪs ɑf / (AmE), / tʃeɪs ɒf / (BrE)
to force someone or something to leave a place by chasing, scaring, or threatening them

Examples

To chase off a fox
The farmer shouted to chase off the fox threatening his chickens.
The security guard quickly chased off anyone trying to loiter near the entrance.
We had to chase off some teenagers who were making noise in the park late at night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drive away - to make someone or something leave a place
  • synonym: scare off - to make frightened so as to go away
