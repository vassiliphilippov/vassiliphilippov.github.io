receivership

noun
the office or position of a receiver
5.4
Pronunciation: / rɪˈsiːvərˌʃɪp / (AmE), / rɪˈsiːvəʃɪp / (BrE)
a legal situation in which a court appoints a person (a receiver) to manage a company or its assets, usually because of financial problems

Examples

The receivership order
The receivership was challenged by several creditors who were dissatisfied with the management.
They entered receivership after months of financial difficulties and court proceedings.
Upon his appointment to the receivership, he was responsible for overseeing the company’s remaining assets.
After months of financial troubles, the company was placed into receivership to protect its assets.
Several businesses entered receivership during the industry downturn, allowing creditors to recover some debts.
The court ordered a receivership to ensure that the disputed property would not be sold during litigation.
After months of mounting debt, the company was finally placed in receivership.
Many jobs were lost when the factory went into receivership due to financial troubles.
The firm avoided receivership by successfully negotiating with its creditors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bankruptcy - legal status of a person or organization that cannot repay debts to creditors
  • synonym: liquidation - the process of bringing a business to an end and distributing its assets to claimants
  • synonym: receiver - a person appointed to manage assets on behalf of others, especially in bankruptcy or legal matters
