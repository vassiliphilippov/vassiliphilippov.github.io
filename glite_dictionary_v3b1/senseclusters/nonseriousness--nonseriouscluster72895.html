nonseriousness

noun
lack of severity
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈsɪriəsnəs / (AmE), / nɒnˈsɪəriəsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of not being severe, grave, or dangerous, especially regarding an illness, injury, or situation

Examples

Nonseriousness of the illness
The doctor assured her of the nonseriousness of the injury after a careful examination.
His calm reaction reflected the nonseriousness of the situation.
The nonseriousness of the illness meant she could return to work quickly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mildness - the quality of being gentle or not severe
  • antonym: seriousness - the quality of being severe, grave, or dangerous
