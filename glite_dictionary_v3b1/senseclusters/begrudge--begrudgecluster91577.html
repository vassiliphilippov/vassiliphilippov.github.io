begrudge

verb
envy or resent giving something
Pronunciation: / bɪˈɡrʌdʒ / (AmE), / bɪˈɡrʌdʒ / (BrE)
to feel unhappy or resentful that someone has something, or to give or allow something unwillingly

Examples

To begrudge success
He didn’t begrudge his sister her success, even though he worked just as hard.
I don’t begrudge you a moment of your happiness.
She begrudges spending money on herself, preferring to save for her children.
He may begrudge every penny he spends on repairs.
I don't begrudge you the opportunity to travel, but I do wish I could join you.
She didn't begrudge him his success, even though she worked just as hard.
She did not begrudge her friend the award, even though she had worked hard for it herself.
He begrudged every minute he had to spend on chores instead of relaxing.
It is important not to begrudge others their happiness or success.

Related Terms

  • antonym: allow - to permit, let happen
  • synonym: envy - to desire what someone else has
  • antonym: forgive - to stop feeling angry or resentful toward someone
  • antonym: grant - to give or allow something willingly
  • synonym: resent - to feel bitter or indignant about something
