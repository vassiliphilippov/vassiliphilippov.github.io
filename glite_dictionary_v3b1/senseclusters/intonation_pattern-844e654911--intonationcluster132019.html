intonation pattern

noun
pitch movement in speech for meaning
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.toʊˈneɪ.ʃən ˈpæt.ɚn / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈneɪ.ʃən ˈpæt.ən / (BrE)
the characteristic rise and fall in pitch of the voice when speaking, which distinguishes questions, statements, requests, and other types of sentences

Examples

Different intonation patterns
Different languages use different intonation patterns to express emotions or intentions.
Learning to use the correct intonation pattern can help you sound more natural when speaking English.
The rising intonation pattern at the end of a sentence often signals a question.
