interiorization

noun
making internal
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌtɪriərɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɪəriəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something internal, especially in one's thoughts, feelings, or behavior

Examples

Interiorization of values
The interiorization of cultural values often happens during childhood.
Therapists study the interiorization of emotions to understand their patients' behavior.
Education can lead to the interiorization of important social norms.

Related Terms

  • antonym: externalization - the process of making something external or visible
  • synonym: internalization - the process of incorporating ideas or values into one's own thinking
