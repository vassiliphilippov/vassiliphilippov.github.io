bulgur

noun
cracked, parboiled wheat, used as food
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊlɡər / (AmE), / ˈbʊlɡə / (BrE)
a cereal food made from parboiled, dried, and crushed wheat kernels, commonly used in Middle Eastern and Mediterranean cooking

Examples

Cooked bulgur wheat
Many traditional dishes use bulgur as a healthy substitute for rice or couscous.
She prepared a fresh salad with bulgur, tomatoes, and parsley for lunch.
The cook soaked the bulgur in hot water until it became soft and ready to use.
