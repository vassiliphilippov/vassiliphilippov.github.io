trisodium phosphate

noun
inorganic cleaning compound; detergent builder
Pronunciation: / traɪˈsoʊdiəm ˈfoʊsˌfeɪt / (AmE), / traɪˈsəʊdiəm ˈfɒsˌfeɪt / (BrE)
a white, water-soluble inorganic compound used chiefly as a cleaning agent and builder in soaps and detergents

Examples

Trisodium phosphate cleaning agent
Chemists often use trisodium phosphate as a buffering agent in laboratory preparations.
Many industrial cleaners contain trisodium phosphate to improve their effectiveness against grease.
The label warns that trisodium phosphate should be handled with care due to its strong cleaning properties.
