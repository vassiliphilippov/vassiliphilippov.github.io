wave train

noun
series of regularly spaced waves
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˈweɪv treɪn / (AmE), / ˈweɪv treɪn / (BrE)
a series of waves of regular interval and similar form, especially in physics or engineering contexts

Examples

A wave train of waves
A wave train reached the shore after an underwater earthquake.
Meteorologists study the behavior of a wave train in the atmosphere to predict weather patterns.
The experiment generated a wave train of uniform amplitude in the tank.
