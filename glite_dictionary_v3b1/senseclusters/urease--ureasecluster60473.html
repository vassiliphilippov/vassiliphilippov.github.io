urease

noun
enzyme breaking down urea
Pronunciation: / ˈjʊr.i.eɪs / (AmE), / ˈjʊə.ri.eɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme that catalyzes the breakdown of urea into carbon dioxide and ammonia

Examples

Urease enzyme
Certain soil bacteria produce urease, which helps convert urea into usable nitrogen.
Scientists used a urease test to detect the presence of the enzyme in the sample.
The ability to hydrolyze urea with urease is a distinguishing feature of some microorganisms.
