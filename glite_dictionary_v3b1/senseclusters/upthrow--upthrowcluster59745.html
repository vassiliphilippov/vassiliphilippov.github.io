(geology) An uplift of land to a higher elevation, typically as a result of geological forces such as fault movement or mountain building.

Examples

A upthrow fault
Geologists discovered a significant upthrow along the fault line after the earthquake.
Mapping the upthrow helped researchers better understand the area's tectonic history.
The presence of an upthrow can complicate drilling operations in mountainous regions.

Synonyms

  • uplift
    7.0 0.41/m
    uplift is a general rise of land used by geologists, while upthrow names a specific uplift caused by fault movement.
  • upthrust
    8.6 0.02/m
    upthrust is a sudden tectonic rise of land, while upthrow refers to an uplift often linked to fault movement or mountain building.
  • upheaval
    7.3 0.22/m
    upheaval is a large, often violent lifting and disturbance of the crust, while upthrow names the geological uplift to higher ground.
