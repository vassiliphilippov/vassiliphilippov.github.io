flatten

verb
make nested data flat (computing)
4.7
Pronunciation: /ˈflæt.ən/ (AmE), /ˈflæt.ən/ (BrE)
(in computing) to convert a nested or hierarchical data structure into a single-level sequence or structure by collapsing inner levels.

Examples

To flatten a list
The data processing step will flatten the nested arrays into a single list.
Yesterday she flattened the JSON file, removing nested objects for easier analysis.
When flattening large datasets, ensure you preserve keys to avoid losing hierarchical context.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nest - to arrange data within nested or hierarchical structures; opposite of flattening in this context
  • synonym: unnest - to remove nesting from a data structure so elements appear at the same level
