indexation

noun
automatic adjustment based on a price index
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnˌdɛkˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.dɛkˈseɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the adjustment of wages, interest rates, or other payments by linking them to a price index, especially to counteract the effects of inflation

Examples

Indexation of wages
By using indexation, loan repayments automatically adjust according to the consumer price index.
Many employment contracts include indexation clauses to protect workers from inflation.
The government introduced indexation to ensure that pensions kept pace with rising prices.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inflation - the general increase in prices and fall in the purchasing value of money
