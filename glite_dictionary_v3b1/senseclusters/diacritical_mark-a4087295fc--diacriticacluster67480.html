diacritical mark

noun
mark indicating special pronunciation or meaning
7.9 6.4
Pronunciation: / ˌdaɪəˈkrɪtɪkəl mɑrk / (AmE), / ˌdaɪəˈkrɪtɪkəl mɑːk / (BrE)
A sign added to a letter to indicate a difference in pronunciation, stress, or meaning.

Examples

A diacritical mark added
Adding a diacritical mark can change the pronunciation of a word.
Many languages use a diacritical mark to distinguish similar letters.
The word 'naïve' contains a diacritical mark over the 'i'.
