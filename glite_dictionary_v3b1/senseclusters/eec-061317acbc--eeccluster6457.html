EEC

noun
European Economic Community (historical EU organization)
5.2 0.6/m
Pronunciation: / iÀê iÀê siÀê / (AmE), / iÀê iÀê siÀê / (BrE)
abbreviation for European Economic Community, an international organization of European countries, formed after World War II to promote economic integration through reduced trade barriers and increased cooperation among its members; a forerunner to the European Union.

Examples

EEC countries
France played a leading role in the formation of the EEC in the 1950s.
Many policies created by the EEC continue to influence European economic relations today.
The United Kingdom was a member of the EEC before joining the European Union.

Synonyms

  • European Economic Community
    4.3 0.3/m
    European Economic Community is the formal name used in treaties and history, while EEC is the short abbreviation people use in writing.
  • European Community
    3.7 5/m
    European Community is the later, broader name for some EU institutions, while EEC refers to the earlier economic organization.
  • Common Market
    3.7 0.5/m
    Common Market is an informal name that highlights free trade among members, while EEC is the official organization name.
  • EC
    5.8 8/m
    EC is the short abbreviation for European Community, while EEC is the abbreviation for the earlier economic organization.
