EEC

noun
European Economic Community (historical EU organization)
5.2
Pronunciation: /ˈiː.iː.siː/ (AmE), /ˈiː.iː.siː/ (BrE)
abbreviation for European Economic Community, an international organization of European countries, formed after World War II to promote economic integration through reduced trade barriers and increased cooperation among its members; a forerunner to the European Union.

Examples

EEC countries
France played a leading role in the formation of the EEC in the 1950s.
Many policies created by the EEC continue to influence European economic relations today.
The United Kingdom was a member of the EEC before joining the European Union.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: European Union - a political and economic union of European countries that succeeded the EEC
