haymaking

noun
taking advantage of an opportunity
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈheɪˌmeɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈheɪˌmeɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of taking full advantage of a favorable opportunity while it lasts

Examples

Business haymaking
Many companies are haymaking as consumer demand for their products temporarily spikes.
The festival only comes to town once a year, so vendors are busy haymaking during the busy weekend.
With the stock market booming, investors are haymaking while conditions are right.

Related Terms

  • synonym: opportunism - the practice of taking advantage of opportunities regardless of morals
