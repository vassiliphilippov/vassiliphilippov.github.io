catfish

verb
deceive with a fake online identity (esp. romantically)
Pronunciation: / ˈkætˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkat.fɪʃ /; / ˈkætˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
to deceive someone by pretending to be another person online, usually by using a fake identity

Examples

To catfish someone online
He discovered he had been catfished when the person he was chatting with online refused to video call.
Many people have been catfished on dating apps by someone using fake photos.
She warned her friends about the dangers of catfishing after her own experience online.
She discovered she had been catfished by someone pretending to be a famous actor online.
Police warned people about the dangers of catfishing on social media platforms.
He admitted to catfishing several people to get money from them.

Related Terms

  • antonym: honest - free of deceit; truthful and sincere
  • synonym: impersonate - to pretend to be another person
  • synonym: imposter - a person who pretends to be someone else to deceive others
  • synonym: scam - a dishonest scheme or fraud, especially on the internet
