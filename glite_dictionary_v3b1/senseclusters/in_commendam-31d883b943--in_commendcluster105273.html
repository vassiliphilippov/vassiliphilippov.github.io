in commendam

noun
temporary church office
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪn kə.ˈmen.dəm/ /; / /ən kə.ˈmen.dəm/ (AmE), /ɪn kə.ˈmen.dəm/ /; / /ən kə.ˈmen.dəm/ (BrE)
a church benefice or office temporarily entrusted to someone who holds it and receives its income until a permanent appointment is made

Examples

A post in commendam
The bishop held the parish in commendam until a new priest was appointed.
During the transition, the abbey was managed in commendam by a trusted official.
He received the revenues from the church in commendam for several months.
