backwash

verb
to flow backward
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈbækwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈbækwɒʃ / (BrE)
to flow or move backward, especially of water, or to cause a backward flow that may have indirect effects

Examples

Waves backwash against rocks
After the ferry passed, waves began backwashing against the moored boats and shaking them.
The filter system needs regular cleaning to prevent water from backwashing into the pool.
Heavy rain can cause sewage to backwash into basements if drains are blocked.

Related Terms

  • antonym: advance - to move forward
  • synonym: recede - to move back or away from a previous position
