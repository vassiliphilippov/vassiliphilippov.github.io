bloodwit

noun

an old English legal term for a fine paid as punishment for shedding blood or causing bodily harm

Examples

A bloodwit fine
In medieval England, a bloodwit was imposed on those who injured others in a fight.
The court demanded a bloodwit after the altercation left one man wounded.
