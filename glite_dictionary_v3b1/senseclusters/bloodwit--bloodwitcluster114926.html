bloodwit

noun
fine for violence
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈblʌdˌwɪt / (AmE), / ˈblʌdˌwɪt / (BrE)
an old English legal term for a fine paid as punishment for shedding blood or causing bodily harm

Examples

A bloodwit fine
In medieval England, a bloodwit was imposed on those who injured others in a fight.
The court demanded a bloodwit after the altercation left one man wounded.
Historical records mention the payment of bloodwit for acts of violence within the community.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compensation - something, typically money, awarded to someone as a recompense for loss or injury
  • synonym: wergild - a value placed on a person's life, to be paid as compensation
