siderochrestic anemia

noun
anemia with sideroblasts in bone marrow
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪdərəˈkrɛstɪk əˈniːmiə /; / saɪdɚ.oʊˈkrɛstɪk əˈniː.mi.ə / (AmE), / ˌsɪdərəˈkrɛstɪk əˈniːmiə /; / saɪdə.rəʊˈkrɛstɪk əˈniː.mɪ.ə / (BrE)
A chronic refractory type of anemia marked by the presence of sideroblasts (abnormal red blood cell precursors containing iron) in the bone marrow.

Examples

Siderochrestic anemia diagnosis
Some forms of siderochrestic anemia are resistant to standard therapies and require specialized management.
The patient's diagnosis of siderochrestic anemia was confirmed after doctors found abnormal sideroblasts in his bone marrow.
Treatment for siderochrestic anemia may include vitamin supplements or other therapies, depending on the underlying cause.
Doctors found that his chronic fatigue was caused by siderochrestic anaemia, which did not respond to standard iron therapy.
In cases of siderochrestic anaemia, iron accumulates in red blood cell precursors instead of being properly incorporated into hemoglobin.
The patient was diagnosed with siderochrestic anaemia after bone marrow tests revealed abnormal sideroblasts.
