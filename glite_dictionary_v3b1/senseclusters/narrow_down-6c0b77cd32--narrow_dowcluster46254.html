narrow down

verb
limit possibilities or focus
Pronunciation: / ˈnæroʊ daʊn / (AmE), / ˈnærəʊ daʊn / (BrE)
to reduce the number of possibilities or choices, making something more specific or clearly defined

Examples

To narrow down choices
After considering several options, we narrowed down the choices to just three.
I had to narrow down my list of universities before applying.
Please narrow down your topic so that your research will be more manageable.
After hours of discussion, the committee finally narrowed down the list of candidates to three.
The detective used clues to narrow down the possible suspects.
We need to narrow down our options before making a decision.

Related Terms

  • antonym: broaden - to make more general or less specific
  • synonym: specialize - to focus on a specific area or field
  • synonym: specify - to state explicitly or in detail
