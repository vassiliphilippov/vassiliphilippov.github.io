amplitude distortion

noun
nonlinear signal distortion
Pronunciation: / ˈæmplɪˌtuːd dɪˈstɔrʃən / (AmE), / ˈamplɪˌtjuːd dɪˈstɔːʃən / (BrE)
A distortion occurring in an electronic system when the output signal does not change in proportion to the input signal.

Examples

Amplitude distortion in signals
If the amplifier introduces amplitude distortion, the sound quality will be noticeably affected.
Professional audio equipment is designed to minimize amplitude distortion for clearer output.
The engineer measured the system to ensure amplitude distortion was within acceptable limits.

Related Terms

  • synonym: frequency distortion - distortion due to unequal amplification of different frequencies
