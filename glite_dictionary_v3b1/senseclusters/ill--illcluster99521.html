ill

adjective
harmful; causing suffering or adversity
Pronunciation: / ɪl / (AmE), / ɪl / (BrE)
harmful, bad, or likely to cause trouble or suffering

Examples

Ill effects
Environmental pollution can have severe ill effects on wildlife.
It is an ill wind that blows nobody any good.
The new medication had several ill effects that concerned the doctors.
Rumors began to spread following his ill prediction about the future of the company.
The silence in the courtroom felt ill, as if something bad was about to happen.
The sudden darkening of the sky was seen by many as an ill omen.
His ill manners offended everyone at the dinner party.
Superstitions warned sailors about the ill omens before a voyage.
The company suffered from ill repute after the scandal.
