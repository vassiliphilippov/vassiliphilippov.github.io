quadriphonic system

noun
four-channel sound system
Pronunciation: / kwɑːdrɪˈfɑnɪk ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / kwɒd.rɪˈfɒn.ɪk ˈsɪs.təm / (BrE)
A sound recording or reproducing system that uses four separate channels to create surround sound.

Examples

A quadriphonic system setup
Many vintage audio enthusiasts collect equipment for playing old quadriphonic system records.
The quadriphonic system surrounds the listener with sound from all sides, unlike standard stereo setups.
The band released their new album on a quadriphonic system to enhance the listening experience.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: stereophonic - using two channels for sound reproduction
