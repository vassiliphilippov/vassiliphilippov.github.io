clawless

adjective
without a stalk-like base
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈklɔləs / (AmE), / ˈklɔːləs / (BrE)
lacking a narrow stalk-like base (claw), so the base widens directly, especially in petals or sepals

Examples

Clawless petals
The botanist noted the clawless petals on the new species of flower.
Unlike many lilies, this variety has clawless sepals that broaden at the base.
The diagram showed both clawed and clawless forms of the plant's petals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clawed - having a narrow stalk-like base (claw)
