photophone

noun
sound-on-film system
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈfoʊ.tə.foʊn/ (AmE), /ˈfəʊ.tə.fəʊn/ (BrE)
a system for recording or reproducing sound on motion-picture film by converting audio into variations of light on the film's soundtrack

Early filmmakers used the photophone to synchronize dialogue with moving images.
