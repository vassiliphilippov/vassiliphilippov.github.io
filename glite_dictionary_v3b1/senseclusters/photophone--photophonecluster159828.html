photophone

noun
sound-on-film system
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊtəˌfoʊn / (AmE), / ˈfəʊtəˌfəʊn / (BrE)
a system for recording or reproducing sound on motion-picture film by converting audio into variations of light on the film's soundtrack

Examples

Photophone sound system
The invention of the photophone revolutionized the way sound was added to movies.
Early filmmakers used the photophone to synchronize dialogue with moving images.
The museum displayed an original photophone system from the early days of cinema.

Related Terms

  • antonym: phonograph - a device for recording and reproducing sound
  • synonym: sound-on-film - a method of recording sound directly onto film
