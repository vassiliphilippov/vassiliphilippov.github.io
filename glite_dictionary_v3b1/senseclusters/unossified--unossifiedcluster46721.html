unossified

adjective
not turned to bone
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈɑsɪˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈɒsɪfaɪd / (BrE)
not converted into bone or not hardened like bone

Examples

Unossified bones
In newborns, many bones are still unossified and remain soft.
The doctor explained that the injury occurred in an unossified area of the child's skeleton.
Some fish have unossified cartilage instead of hard bones.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ossified - turned into bone or hardened like bone
