act out

verb
express emotions through inappropriate behavior
2.6
Pronunciation: / ækˈt aʊt / (AmE), / ækt ˈaʊt / (BrE)
to express emotions, especially negative ones, through behavior that is often inappropriate or disruptive, rather than talking about them

Examples

Children act out anger
During times of stress, some people act out in unexpected ways.
He tends to act out his anger instead of talking about it.
When children feel neglected, they may act out to gain attention from adults.

Related Terms

  • antonym: internalize - to keep feelings or emotions inside rather than expressing them
  • synonym: misbehave - to behave badly or improperly
