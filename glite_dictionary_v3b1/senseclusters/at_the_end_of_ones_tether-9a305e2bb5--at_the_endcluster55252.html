at the end of one's tether

x
at the limit of patience or endurance
Pronunciation: / æt ði ɛnd əv wʌnz ˈtɛðɚ / (AmE), / æt ði ɛnd ɒv wʌnz ˈtɛðə / (BrE)
having reached the limit of one's patience, endurance, or resources; unable to deal with a situation any longer

Examples

At the end of her tether
After weeks of sleepless nights with her newborn, she was at the end of her tether.
I'm at the end of my tether with this broken computer—it simply won't work.
The customer service agent sounded at the end of his tether after dealing with complaints all day.
