recoil

verb
draw back quickly in fear or pain
8.1 4.0
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈkɔɪl/ /; / /riː.ˈkɔɪl/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈkɔɪl/ /; / /riː.ˈkɔɪl/ (BrE)
to quickly move back or away, either because of fear, pain, disgust, or after being hit or affected by force

Examples

To recoil in fear
He recoiled in horror when he heard the news of the accident.
Many people recoil from the idea of eating insects, despite their nutritional value.
She couldn't help but recoil at the sight of the snake in the garden.
She instinctively recoiled as the snake struck toward her boot.
The dog recoiled from the loud bang, startled by the noise.
The powerful rifle recoiled sharply against his shoulder.
The ball hit the wall and began to recoil toward the center of the room.
The rifle will recoil sharply when you fire it.
When struck, the rubber band will quickly recoil to its original shape.

Related Terms

  • antonym: absorb - to take in or soak up; opposite effect of springing back
  • synonym: bounce - to spring back after hitting a surface
  • synonym: flinch - to make a sudden small movement because of pain or fear
  • synonym: rebound - to bounce back after hitting something
  • synonym: shrink - to move back or away suddenly, especially because of fear
