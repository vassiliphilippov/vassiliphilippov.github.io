to quickly move back or away, either because of fear, pain, disgust, or after being hit or affected by force

Examples

To recoil in fear
He recoiled in horror when he heard the news of the accident.
Many people recoil from the idea of eating insects, despite their nutritional value.
She couldn't help but recoil at the sight of the snake in the garden.
She instinctively recoiled as the snake struck toward her boot.
The dog recoiled from the loud bang, startled by the noise.
The powerful rifle recoiled sharply against his shoulder.
The ball hit the wall and began to recoil toward the center of the room.
The rifle will recoil sharply when you fire it.
When struck, the rubber band will quickly recoil to its original shape.

Synonyms

  • flinch
    4.1 5.65/m
    flinch is a quick, automatic small movement away from pain or surprise, while recoil is a stronger backward motion from force or shock.
  • shrink
    2.3 2.36/m
    shrink moves back suddenly in fear or pain, while recoil often means a stronger backward movement after a forceful impact.
  • wince
    4.9 6.44/m
    wince is a sudden facial expression or small movement from pain or dislike, while recoil is a larger backward physical movement.
  • cringe
    5.1 0.43/m
    cringe means pulling in or showing shame and fear, while recoil usually means a quick backward physical movement from force.
  • kick
    6.8 11/m
    kick is a sudden backward spring of a gun or machine when it fires, while recoil also covers emotional or wider reactions.
  • kick back
    6.1 0.07/m
    kick back is when a machine suddenly springs backward from an impact, while recoil can mean moving back from force or fear.
