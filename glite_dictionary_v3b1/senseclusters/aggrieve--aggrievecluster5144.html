aggrieve

verb
to treat unjustly or injure
5.1 7.6
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈgriːv/ (AmE), /ə.ˈgriːv/ (BrE)
to treat unjustly by infringing upon someone's rights; to cause legal or personal harm or injury

Examples

To aggrieve a worker
Employees who feel aggrieved by unfair treatment can file a complaint.
His actions were found to aggrieve the complainant, resulting in compensation.
The new law seemed to aggrieve the rights of minority groups.

Related Terms
