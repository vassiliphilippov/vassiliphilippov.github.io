disjoin

verb
to separate or disconnect
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈdʒɔɪn / (AmE), / dɪsˈdʒɔɪn / (BrE)
to separate or disconnect things that are joined together

Examples

To disjoin parts
After years of collaboration, the companies decided to disjoin their operations and pursue separate paths.
It is sometimes necessary to disjoin elements of a complex system for maintenance purposes.
The committee voted to disjoin the two proposals and review them individually.
A line was drawn to disjoin the two territories on the map.
Extreme tension can disjoin the parts of the framework over time.
The pieces of the puzzle began to disjoin after repeated use.

Related Terms

  • antonym: connect - to join together; to link
  • synonym: disconnect - to break the connection of or between
  • antonym: join - to bring or come together into a single unit or place
  • synonym: separate - to cause to move or be apart
