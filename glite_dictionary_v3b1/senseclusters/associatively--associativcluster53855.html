associatively

adverb
in an associative way
7.7
Pronunciation: / əˈsoʊsiətɪvli / (AmE), / əˈsəʊsiətɪvli / (BrE)
in a manner that follows the associative property, so that the grouping of operations does not affect the result

Examples

Associatively combine numbers
In mathematics, addition is performed associatively, so the grouping of numbers does not change the sum.
The function combines elements associatively, making it easier to process long sequences.
When operations are done associatively, you can rearrange parentheses without affecting the outcome.

Related Terms

  • synonym: commutatively - in a way that follows the commutative property, where order does not affect the result
