neurodegenerative

adjective
causing nerve cell loss
Pronunciation: / ˌnʊroʊdɪˈdʒɛnərətɪv / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəʊdɪˈdʒɛnərətɪv / (BrE)
causing the gradual loss of function or structure of nerve cells in the brain or nervous system

Examples

Neurodegenerative disease
Alzheimer's is a well-known neurodegenerative disorder that affects millions of people worldwide.
Scientists are searching for treatments to slow the progress of neurodegenerative diseases like Parkinson's.
Early diagnosis is important in managing neurodegenerative conditions and improving quality of life.

Related Terms

  • synonym: degenerative - causing gradual decline or deterioration
  • antonym: regenerative - able to regrow or repair tissue
