oik

noun
rude young person
Pronunciation: / ɔɪk / (BrE)
a derogatory British slang term for a rude, uncouth, or ill-mannered young person

Examples

A rude oik
He called the noisy teenagers a bunch of oiks after they disrupted the meeting.
Some people think wearing a hoodie makes you look like an oik in certain neighborhoods.
The teacher was frustrated by the oik who kept interrupting the lesson with rude comments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gentleman - a polite and well-mannered man
  • synonym: lout - a rude or aggressive young man
  • synonym: yob - a young man who behaves in a noisy or violent way in public
