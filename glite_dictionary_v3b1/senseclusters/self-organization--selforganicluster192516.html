self-organization

noun

(in science and complex systems) the spontaneous emergence of ordered structure, patterns, or behaviour from local interactions among components, without central control.

Examples

Biological self-organization
The flock demonstrated clear self-organization as individual birds adjusted to neighbors' movements.
Researchers modeled chemical reaction-diffusion systems to study self-organization and pattern formation.
Traffic engineers aim to encourage self-organization so vehicles flow smoothly without centralized control.

Related Terms
