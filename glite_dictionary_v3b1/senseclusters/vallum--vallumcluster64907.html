vallum

proper noun
Roman earthwork
Pronunciation: / ˈvæləm / (AmE), / ˈvæləm / (BrE)
the large Roman earthwork consisting of a ditch and bank running south of Hadrian's Wall in northern England

Examples

The Vallum in Britain
The vallum was an important part of the Roman frontier system in Britain.
Archaeologists uncovered new sections of the vallum during their summer excavation.
Visitors to Hadrian's Wall can still see traces of the ancient vallum nearby.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Hadrian's Wall - a defensive fortification in northern England built by the Romans
  • synonym: rampart - a defensive wall or bank of earth
