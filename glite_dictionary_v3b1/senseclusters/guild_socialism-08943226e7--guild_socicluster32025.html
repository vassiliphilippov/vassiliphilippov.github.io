guild socialism

noun
socialist theory advocating worker-managed guilds
5.7 7.8
Pronunciation: /gɪld ˈsoʊ.ʃə.lɪz.əm/ (AmE), /gɪld ˈsəʊ.ʃə.lɪz.əm/ (BrE)
a form of socialism advocating state ownership of industry but management by guilds of workers, especially as developed in early 20th-century Britain

Examples

Guild socialism movement
His writings on guild socialism argued for industries to be managed democratically by workers' guilds.
Some intellectuals promoted guild socialism as an alternative to both capitalism and state-controlled socialism.
The rise of guild socialism in Britain influenced labor policies during the early twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • synonym: syndicalism - a similar movement advocating direct worker control through trade unions
