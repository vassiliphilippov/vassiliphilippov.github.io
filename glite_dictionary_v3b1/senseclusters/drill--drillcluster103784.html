drill

verb
to undergo military training
2.9 2.97/m
Pronunciation: / drÉªl / (AmE), / drÉªl / (BrE)
to train or teach by repeating an activity or exercise many times (including military or educational contexts)

Examples

To drill the team
New recruits must drill every morning before breakfast.
The soldiers drilled in perfect formation across the parade ground.
They spent hours drilling to prepare for the ceremony.
After drilling the dance routine for an hour, the team performed flawlessly.
The teacher drilled the students on their multiplication tables until they knew them by heart.
We drilled French vocabulary every day in class to improve our memory.
New cadets are drilled intensively before their first deployment.
The officer drilled the soldiers in proper combat formation.
The sergeant drilled the recruits in marching and rifle handling every morning.

Synonyms

  • practice
    1.0 41/m
    practice is repeating an activity to improve a skill, while drill is a stricter, often teacher-led repetition for exact training.
  • exercise
    1.4 2.82/m
    exercise is doing an activity repeatedly to learn or improve, while drill is more formal, controlled repetition used in teaching or the military.
