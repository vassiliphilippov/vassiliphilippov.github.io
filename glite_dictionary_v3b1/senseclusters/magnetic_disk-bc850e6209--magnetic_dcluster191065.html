magnetic disk

noun
data storage device with magnetic coating
Pronunciation: / mæɡˈnɛtɪk dɪsk / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɛtɪk dɪsk / (BrE)
(computing) a storage device consisting of a flat disk coated with magnetic material, used to store data electronically.

Examples

A magnetic disk drive
Early portable music players often used a miniature magnetic disk for song storage.
The old computer relies on a magnetic disk for storing all its files.
Unlike optical storage, a magnetic disk uses a magnetic coating to save information.
Early computers used magnetic discs to store operating systems and application files.
Engineers examined the magnetic disc for signs of physical damage after the system crashed.
The data on the magnetic disc can be retrieved even after the power is turned off.

Related Terms

  • synonym: floppy disk - a type of magnetic disk used for data storage
  • synonym: hard disk - a type of magnetic disk used as the primary storage device in computers
  • antonym: optical disc - a data storage device that uses lasers to read and write data
  • antonym: optical disk - a data storage disk using laser technology rather than magnetic storage
