sheave

noun
bundle of grain stalks
9.9
Pronunciation: / ʃiːv / (AmE), / ʃiːv / (BrE)
an old or dialect word for a bundle of cut stalks of grain, also called a sheaf

Examples

A sheave of wheat
The farmer tied each sheave with twine before carrying them to the barn.
After the harvest, workers stacked every sheave in neat rows to dry.
In old poems, a golden sheave often symbolizes abundance and hard work.

  • synonym: sheaf - a bundle of cut stalks of grain
