paleontology

noun
science of fossils and ancient life
9.0 6.0
Pronunciation: /peɪ.li.ən.ˈtɑː.lə.dʒiː/ (AmE), /peɪ.li.ən.ˈtɒl.ə.dʒiː/ /; / /pæ.li.ən.ˈtɒl.ə.dʒiː/ (BrE)
The scientific study of fossils and ancient forms of life preserved in rocks.

Examples

Study of paleontology
Advances in paleontology have greatly expanded our understanding of extinct species.
After studying paleontology, she joined a dig searching for dinosaur bones in Montana.
His passion for paleontology began after he visited a museum and saw a giant fossil.
Major discoveries in palaeontology have shaped our understanding of the Earth's history.
She decided to pursue a career in palaeontology after her fascination with fossils grew.
The museum held a special exhibit on the advances in palaeontology over the past century.
