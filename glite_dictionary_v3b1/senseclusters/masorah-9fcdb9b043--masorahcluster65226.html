Masorah

noun
Jewish traditional notes on Hebrew Scriptures
9.9
Pronunciation: / məˈsɔrə / (AmE), / məˈsɔːrə / (BrE)
the body of Jewish traditional notes on the Hebrew Scriptures, including textual criticism, variant readings, pronunciation, and scribal comments, compiled mainly between AD 600 and 900.

Examples

The Masorah notes
Many medieval manuscripts feature the Masorah written in the margins as a guide to readers.
Scholars study the Masorah to understand the evolution of the Hebrew Scriptures.
The Masorah contains invaluable information about traditional pronunciation and scribal practices.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Masoretic Text - the authoritative Hebrew text of the Jewish Bible, shaped by the Masorah
