stewy

adjective
hot and humid
Pronunciation: / ˈstuːi / (AmE), / ˈstjuːi / (BrE)
characterized by oppressive heat and humidity, making the air feel close and stuffy

Examples

Stewy summer air
The air in the gym became stewy after hours of exercise and no ventilation.
We spent a stewy afternoon in the crowded subway station waiting for the train.
It was so stewy in the classroom that everyone struggled to concentrate.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cool - moderately cold; not warm or hot
  • synonym: stuffy - lacking fresh air and unpleasantly warm
  • synonym: sultry - hot and humid, often uncomfortably so
