heteronymous

adjective
having different names
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛtəˈrɑːnɪməs / (AmE), / ˌhɛtəˈrɒnɪməs / (BrE)
having different names or forms, especially referring to things that are not identical in name or structure

Examples

Heteronymous word forms
In some languages, masculine and feminine forms of a word are heteronymous.
The terms 'teacher' and 'student' are heteronymous because they refer to different roles.
In linguistics, 'I' and 'me' are considered heteronymous pronouns.

Related Terms

  • synonym: heteronym - a word spelled the same as another but with a different meaning and pronunciation
  • antonym: homonymous - having the same name or form
