house of cards

noun
structure made from playing cards
Pronunciation: / ˈhaʊs əv kɑrdz / (AmE), / ˈhaʊs əv kɑːdz / (BrE)
A structure built by stacking playing cards in interlocking formations, typically precarious and easy to collapse.

Examples

Build a house of cards
He proudly photographed the four-tier house of cards before it finally collapsed.
The children spent the afternoon building a house of cards on the kitchen table.
With a gentle breeze, their delicate house of cards toppled instantly.
