microfiber

noun
fine synthetic fabric
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪkroʊˌfaɪbər / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊˌfaɪbə / (BrE)
a very fine synthetic fiber or fabric, often used for cleaning or drying surfaces

Examples

A microfiber towel
She wiped the kitchen counter with a microfiber cloth to remove dust and crumbs.
Using a microfiber towel helps prevent scratches on delicate surfaces like eyeglasses.
Many people prefer microfiber for cleaning because it absorbs water quickly and dries fast.
She used a microfiber towel to quickly dry her hair after swimming.
The car was polished with a soft microfiber cloth to avoid scratches.
Many athletic clothes are made from microfiber because it is lightweight and breathable.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cotton - a natural fiber used in fabrics
  • synonym: polyester - a common synthetic fiber used in fabrics
