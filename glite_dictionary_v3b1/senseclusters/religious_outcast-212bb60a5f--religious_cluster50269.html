religious outcast

noun
person excluded for religious beliefs
4.2
Pronunciation: / rɪˈlɪdʒəs ˈaʊtkæst / (AmE), / rɪˈlɪdʒəs ˈaʊtkɑːst / (BrE)
a person who is excluded from a religious community, especially because their beliefs conflict with the established dogma

Examples

A religious outcast person
After questioning church teachings, he became a religious outcast in his community.
Many religious outcasts found refuge in more tolerant cities during the Middle Ages.
She described herself as a religious outcast after being excommunicated for her beliefs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: apostate - a person who renounces a religious or political belief or principle
  • synonym: heretic - a person who holds religious beliefs contrary to the orthodox doctrine
