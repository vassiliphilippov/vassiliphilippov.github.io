non vult

noun
legal plea of 'no contest'
9.9 9.6
Pronunciation: /nɑːn vʌlt/ /; / /nɑn vʌlt/ (AmE), /nɒn vʌlt/ (BrE)
(Law) A defendant's plea of 'no contest,' not admitting guilt but accepting conviction.

Examples

A non vult plea
A non vult plea can sometimes result in a lighter sentence than a guilty plea.
After much deliberation, her lawyer advised her to plead non vult to the charges.
The defendant entered a non vult plea to avoid a lengthy trial.

Related Terms

  • antonym: guilty plea - when a defendant formally admits guilt in court
  • synonym: nolo contendere - a plea by which a defendant accepts conviction but does not admit guilt
