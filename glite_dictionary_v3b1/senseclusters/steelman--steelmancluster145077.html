steelman

verb
present an argument in its strongest form
Pronunciation: / c8stid0lccm6n / (AmE), / c8stid0lccman / (BrE)
to restate or reformulate an argument in its strongest possible form, typically to test it or allow fair criticism.

Examples

To steelman an argument
Before criticizing, he would steelman his opponent's position to ensure a fair debate.
During workshops, participants practiced steelmanning each other's ideas to discover hidden strengths.
She steelmanned the proposal, making it much stronger before presenting to the board.

Related Terms

  • synonym: charitable interpretation - to interpret another's argument in the strongest or most reasonable way
  • antonym: straw-man (argument) - to misrepresent an opponent's argument to make it easier to attack
