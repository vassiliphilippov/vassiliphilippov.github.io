Sophist

noun
Greek philosopher and teacher (5th century BC)
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑː.fɪst / (AmE), / ˈsɒ.fɪst / (BrE)
a member of a group of ancient Greek philosophers and teachers of the 5th century BC known for their rhetorical skill and speculations on diverse subjects

Examples

Sophists in ancient Greece
Many Sophists were skilled in the art of persuasive speaking.
Plato often criticized the teachings of the Sophists in his dialogues.
The Sophist Protagoras argued that truth is relative to each individual.

Related Terms

  • synonym: philosopher - a person engaged or learned in philosophy
