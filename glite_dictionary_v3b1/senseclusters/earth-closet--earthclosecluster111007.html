earth-closet

noun
a historical dry toilet using earth to cover waste
Pronunciation: / ˈɜrθ ˌklɑzət / (AmE), / ˈɜːθ ˌklɒzɪt / (BrE)
A type of toilet, typically in a small outbuilding, consisting of a bench with holes through which users defecate, where waste is covered with earth instead of being flushed away.

The old farmhouse still had an earth-closet at the back, rather than a modern bathroom.
