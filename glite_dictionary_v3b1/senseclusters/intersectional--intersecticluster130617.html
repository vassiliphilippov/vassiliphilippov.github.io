intersectional

adjective
overlapping identities
7.4
Pronunciation: /ɪn.tər.ˈsek.ʃə.nəl/ (AmE), /ɪn.tə.ˈsek.ʃə.nəl/ (BrE)
relating to how different aspects of a person's identity, such as race, gender, and class, combine to shape their experiences

Examples

An intersectional approach
The study took an intersectional approach to understanding discrimination in the workplace.
Activists argue that intersectional policies are needed to address complex social issues.
Her research highlights the intersectional barriers faced by people with multiple marginalized identities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: individual - relating to a single person, especially as distinct from a group
  • synonym: multifaceted - having many different aspects or features
