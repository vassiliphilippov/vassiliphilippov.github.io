dyslipidemia

noun
abnormal blood lipids
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪslɪpɪˈdiːmiə / (AmE), / ˌdɪslɪpɪˈdiːmiə / (BrE)
a medical condition in which there are abnormal levels of lipids, such as cholesterol or triglycerides, in the blood

Examples

High dyslipidemia levels
The doctor diagnosed him with dyslipidemia after reviewing his blood test results.
Lifestyle changes and medication can help manage dyslipidemia and reduce the risk of heart disease.
People with dyslipidemia are often advised to follow a healthy diet and exercise regularly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hyperlipidemia - high levels of lipids in the blood
  • antonym: hypolipidemia - low levels of lipids in the blood
