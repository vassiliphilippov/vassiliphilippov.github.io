deontology

noun
ethics based on duty
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːɑnˈtɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌdiːɒnˈtɒlədʒi / (BrE)
an ethical theory that judges whether actions are right or wrong based on rules or duties, not on their consequences

Examples

Moral deontology theory
Kant is well known for his contributions to deontology in moral philosophy.
Some people prefer deontology because it emphasizes following moral rules.
The debate between deontology and consequentialism is central in ethics classes.
Many philosophers debate whether deontology or utilitarianism provides a better guide for moral decisions.
Medical professionals often follow deontology to ensure they act according to strict ethical codes.
The lawyer explained how deontology requires following rules even when outcomes are uncertain.

Related Terms

  • antonym: consequentialism - the ethical theory that judges actions by their outcomes
  • synonym: ethics - the study of moral principles that govern behavior
  • antonym: utilitarianism - an ethical theory that judges actions by their consequences
