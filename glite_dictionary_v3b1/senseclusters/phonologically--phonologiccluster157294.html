phonologically

adverb
relating to language sounds
Pronunciation: / fəˌnɑːləˈdʒɪkli / (AmE), / fəˌnɒləˈdʒɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to the sounds and sound patterns of a language

Examples

Different phonologically distinct dialects
The two dialects are phonologically distinct, even though they share much vocabulary.
Children often make phonologically predictable errors when learning to speak.
Some languages are phonologically complex, with many different sounds and patterns.

Related Terms

  • synonym: phonetically - in a way that relates to the physical sounds of speech
