amide

noun
organic compound with –CONH₂ group
4.0 6.9
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈmaɪd/ /; / /ˈæ.maɪd/ /; / /ˈæ.mɪd/ (AmE), /ˈæ.maɪd/ /; / /ˈeɪ.maɪd/ /; / /ə.ˈmaɪd/ (BrE)
a chemical compound containing the group –CONH₂, formed by replacing the hydroxyl group of a carboxylic acid or a hydrogen atom of ammonia with an amino or acyl group

Examples

A simple amide
Formamide is a simple amide used in many chemical reactions.
Many pharmaceuticals contain an amide functional group in their molecular structure.
Urea is technically an amide formed from carbon dioxide and ammonia.
The chemist analyzed the structure of the amide to determine its properties.
Many drugs contain an amide group as part of their molecular structure.
Researchers discovered a new amide that could be useful in treating infections.

Related Terms

  • antonym: amine - a compound derived from ammonia by replacement of one or more hydrogen atoms by organic groups
  • antonym: carboxylic acid - an organic acid containing a carboxyl group (–COOH)
  • synonym: peptide - a compound consisting of two or more amino acids linked in a chain
