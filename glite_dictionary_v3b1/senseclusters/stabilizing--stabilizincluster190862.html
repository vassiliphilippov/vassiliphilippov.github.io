stabilizing

adjective
making something stable
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈsteɪbəˌlaɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsteɪbɪlaɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
causing something to become stable or less likely to change suddenly

Examples

A stabilizing force
A good leader can be a stabilizing force during times of crisis.
Adding sandbags along the riverbank had a stabilizing influence on the eroding shoreline.
The government introduced new policies with a stabilizing effect on the economy.
A strong support system can be a stabilising force during difficult times.
The new policies had a stabilising effect on the country's economy.
Vitamin C acts as a stabilising agent in some pharmaceutical compounds.

Related Terms

  • antonym: destabilising - causing something to become less stable
  • antonym: destabilizing - causing something to become unstable
  • synonym: stabilizing - causing to become stable (American spelling)
