multiplane

noun
animation depth technique
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌltɪˌpleɪn / (AmE), / ˈmʌltɪˌpleɪn / (BrE)
a camera or animation technique that uses several transparent layers moved independently to create depth in two-dimensional animation

Examples

Multiplane camera
The animator used a multiplane to create a realistic sense of distance in the scene.
Classic cartoons often relied on the multiplane technique to make backgrounds appear more dynamic.
By adjusting each layer on the multiplane, the artists achieved a unique visual effect.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cel animation - a traditional animation technique using transparent sheets
  • antonym: stop motion - an animation technique using photographed objects moved in small steps
