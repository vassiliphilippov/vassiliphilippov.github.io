fore-and-aft topsail

noun
triangular sail with foot on gaff, luff on topmast
9.9
Pronunciation: / fɔr ənd æft ˈtɑpˌseɪl / (AmE), / fɔːr ənd ɑːft ˈtɒpseɪl / (BrE)
A triangular fore-and-aft sail set with its foot along the gaff and its luff attached to the topmast.

Examples

The fore-and-aft topsail
Sailors adjusted the fore-and-aft topsail as the wind shifted to the north.
The schooner set its fore-and-aft topsail to catch more wind during the afternoon crossing.
With a strong breeze, the crew hoisted the fore-and-aft topsail to increase the vessel's speed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gaff topsail - a topsail set above a gaff-rigged sail
