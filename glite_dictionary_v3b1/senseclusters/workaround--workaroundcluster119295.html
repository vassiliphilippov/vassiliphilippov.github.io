workaround

noun
temporary solution
3.9
Pronunciation: /ˈwɜːr.kə.raʊnd/ (AmE), /ˈwɜː.kə.raʊnd/ (BrE)
a method or solution used to overcome a problem or limitation, especially when the usual way is blocked

Examples

A clever workaround
The engineers developed a clever workaround to bypass the software bug until it could be fixed.
We used a simple workaround to print the document when the main printer was down.
Sometimes a workaround is necessary when there is no immediate solution to a problem.
The team developed a clever workaround to keep the project on schedule despite the technical issue.
Until the official update is released, users can apply this workaround to avoid the error.
She found a simple workaround that allowed her to print the document even though the printer was offline.

  • antonym: problem - something that is difficult to deal with or needs to be solved
