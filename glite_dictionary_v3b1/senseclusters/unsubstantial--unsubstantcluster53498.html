unsubstantial

adjective
lacking substance; unreal
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnsəbˈstænʃəl / (AmE), / ˌʌnsəbˈstænʃəl / (BrE)
having little or no material form or substance; insubstantial or unreal

Examples

An unsubstantial rumor
His accusations were based on unsubstantial rumors and hearsay.
The ghostly figure appeared so unsubstantial that no one was sure it was real.
The promise of wealth proved unsubstantial in the end, leaving them disappointed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: insubstantial - lacking substance or solidity
  • antonym: substantial - having substance or being considerable in amount
