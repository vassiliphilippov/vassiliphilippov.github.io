previsit

noun
preliminary meeting
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːˌvɪzɪt / (AmE), / ˈpriːˌvɪzɪt / (BrE)
a meeting, call, or check that takes place before an official visit or appointment

Examples

A previsit call
The nurse called for a previsit to confirm the patient's medical history.
A previsit helps ensure all necessary documents are ready before the main appointment.
Parents attended a previsit at the school to meet the teachers before the term started.

Related Terms

  • antonym: follow-up - a meeting or action that happens after an event
  • synonym: preliminary - something that comes before the main event
