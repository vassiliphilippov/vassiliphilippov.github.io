electric clock

noun
clock powered by electricity
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɛktrɪk klɑk / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪk klɒk / (BrE)
A clock that is powered by electricity, typically using a small electric motor to move the hands or display the time.

Examples

An electric clock on wall
She set the electric clock so she wouldn't oversleep for her morning meeting.
The old kitchen still had an electric clock mounted above the stove.
Unlike a traditional wind-up clock, an electric clock keeps ticking as long as it has power.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mechanical clock - a clock powered by mechanical means, such as springs or weights
