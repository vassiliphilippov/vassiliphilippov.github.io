break-off

noun
detached fragment
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈbreɪkˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈbreɪkˌɒf / (BrE)
a piece or fragment that has become detached from a larger object, often by breaking away suddenly.

Examples

A break-off from the rock
After the earthquake, a massive break-off from the mountain blocked the road.
The glacier's break-off created floating icebergs in the bay.
Workers cleared the break-off from the construction site to ensure safety.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chip - a small piece broken off from something
  • synonym: fragment - a small part broken off or separated from something
  • antonym: whole - something complete, not broken or divided
