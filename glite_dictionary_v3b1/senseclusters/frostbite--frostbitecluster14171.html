frostbite

noun
tissue damage from freezing cold
1.4 3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈfrɔːst.baɪt/ /; / /ˈfrɑːst.baɪt/ (AmE), /ˈfrɒst.baɪt/ (BrE)
A medical condition in which skin and other tissues are damaged due to freezing, typically caused by prolonged exposure to extremely cold temperatures.

Examples

Frostbite on fingers
If you don't wear gloves in freezing weather, you could develop frostbite on your fingers.
It's important to spot the early signs of frostbite, such as numbness or tingling in the skin.
The climbers were treated for severe frostbite after being stranded overnight on the mountain.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: chilblain - a painful skin swelling from cold exposure, less severe than frostbite
  • degree higher: gangrene - death of body tissue due to lack of blood flow or infection, can result from severe frostbite
