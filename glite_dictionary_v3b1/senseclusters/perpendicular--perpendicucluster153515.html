perpendicular

adjective
nearly vertical; very steep
6.7 0.16/m
Pronunciation: / Àåp…ö.p…ônÀàd…™k.j…ô.l…ö / (AmE), / Àåp…úÀê.p…ônÀàd…™k.j ä.l…ô / (BrE)
(of a slope or surface) so steep as to be nearly vertical.

Examples

Perpendicular cliff face
A perpendicular drop separated the mountain trail from the valley below.
The perpendicular cliffs made access by boat nearly impossible.
The hikers were daunted by the perpendicular wall of rock before them.

Synonyms

  • sheer
    4.1 2.44/m
    sheer describes a very steep, almost vertical surface, while perpendicular means a slope that is exactly or nearly vertical.
  • precipitous
    7.7 0.91/m
    precipitous describes an extremely steep, often dangerous drop, while perpendicular means a slope that is simply near vertical.
  • steep-sided
    4.3 0.10/m
    steep-sided describes something with very steep or near-vertical sides, while perpendicular means the surface itself is almost vertical.
  • steep
    2.5 15/m
    steep means rising or falling sharply at many angles, while perpendicular means almost vertical and very close to straight up and down.
