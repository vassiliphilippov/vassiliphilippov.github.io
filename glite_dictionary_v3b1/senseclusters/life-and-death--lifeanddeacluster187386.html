life-and-death

adjective
vitally important; critical, often to survival
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪf ən ˈdɛθ / (AmE), / ˈlaɪf ənd ˈdɛθ / (BrE)
involving matters of vital importance, especially those concerning survival or serious consequences

Examples

Life-and-death choices

She faced a life-and-death decision during the emergency.
The negotiations were a life-and-death matter for the company.
