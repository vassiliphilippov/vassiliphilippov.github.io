bar

noun
a narrow band of different color or texture
2.0 2.23/m
Pronunciation: / b…ër / (AmE), / b…ëÀê / (BrE)
a narrow band or stripe of a different color or texture from the background, often found on animals, objects, or designs

Examples

Black bars on wings
The butterfly's wings have distinct yellow bars along the edges.
The tiger's fur is covered in black bars that help it blend into the forest.
You can recognize that flag by the red and white bars across its surface.

Synonyms

  • stripe
    1.0 5.89/m
    a stripe is a long, even band of color often repeated on cloth or animals, while a bar is a narrow contrasting band.
  • band
    1.3 3.76/m
    a band is a wider strip used for decoration or pattern on objects or clothing, while a bar is narrower and more defined.
  • streak
    2.6 5.12/m
    a streak is a thin, often irregular line or flash of color that looks accidental, while a bar is a regular narrow band.
