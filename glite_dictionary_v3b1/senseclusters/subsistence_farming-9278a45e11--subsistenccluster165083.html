subsistence farming

noun
farming for personal or family needs only
2.8 4.5
Pronunciation: / səbˈsɪstəns ˈfɑrmɪŋ / (AmE), / səbˈsɪstəns ˈfɑːmɪŋ / (BrE)
a type of farming in which crops and livestock are produced mainly to meet the farmer's own needs, with little or no surplus for sale or trade

Examples

Subsistence farming for food
A shift from subsistence farming to commercial agriculture can transform the local economy.
Due to poor soil quality, she could barely make a living through subsistence farming on her small plot of land.
In many developing countries, subsistence farming remains the primary source of food for rural families.

Related Terms

  • antonym: commercial farming - farming intended to produce crops or livestock for sale
