complement fixation

noun
immune response using complement proteins
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːm.plə.ˌmɛnt fɪkˈseɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈkɒm.plɪ.mənt fɪkˈseɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
an immune response in which an antigen-antibody complex consumes complement proteins, preventing them from reacting with other antigens

Examples

Positive complement fixation test
A positive complement fixation result confirmed exposure to the virus.
In immunology, complement fixation is a classic method for detecting specific antibodies.
The doctor ordered a complement fixation test to help diagnose the infection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: complement system - a group of proteins in blood plasma aiding immune responses
