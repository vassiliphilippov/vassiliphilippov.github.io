Wheatstone bridge

noun
electrical circuit for resistance measurement
Pronunciation: / ˈwiːtstoʊn brɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈwiːtstən brɪdʒ / (BrE)
an electrical circuit used to precisely measure unknown resistances by balancing two legs of a bridge circuit

Examples

A Wheatstone bridge circuit
A Wheatstone bridge can detect even slight faults in electrical circuits due to its sensitivity.
Engineers calibrated the instrument using a Wheatstone bridge to ensure accurate readings.
The Wheatstone bridge is commonly used in laboratories to measure small changes in electrical resistance.
