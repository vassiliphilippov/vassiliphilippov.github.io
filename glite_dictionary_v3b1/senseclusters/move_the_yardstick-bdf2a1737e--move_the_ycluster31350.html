move the yardstick

verb
change the standards or criteria
Pronunciation: / muv ðə ˈjɑrdˌstɪk / (AmE), / muːv ðə ˈjɑːdˌstɪk / (BrE)
to change the standards or criteria used to judge success, often making achievement more difficult or different than before

Examples

To move the yardstick unfairly
It's frustrating when they move the yardstick after we've already started the evaluation process.
The school board decided to move the yardstick by raising the academic standards this year.
Whenever the team met their targets, management would move the yardstick to keep them striving for more.
