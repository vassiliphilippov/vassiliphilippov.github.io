fork out

verb
pay money reluctantly
Pronunciation: / fɔrk aʊt / (AmE), / fɔːk aʊt / (BrE)
(informal) to pay or hand over money, especially unwillingly or with reluctance.

Examples

To fork out money
After months of saving, she finally had to fork out for a new laptop.
He wasn't happy about having to fork out so much money for repairs.
They expect us to fork out extra charges every time we use the service.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cough up - to reluctantly give or pay money
  • synonym: shell out - to pay money for something, usually reluctantly
