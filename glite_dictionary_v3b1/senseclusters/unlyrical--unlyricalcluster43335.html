unlyrical

adjective
not expressive
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈlɪrɪkəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈlɪrɪkəl / (BrE)
not having a poetic, musical, or expressive quality; lacking lyricism or emotion

Examples

An unlyrical style
His writing style is quite unlyrical, focusing more on facts than on feelings.
The film's soundtrack was intentionally unlyrical to match the somber mood of the story.
Despite the moving subject, her performance remained unlyrical and subdued.
The critic described the novel's style as unlyrical and straightforward.
Her speech was clear but rather unlyrical, lacking any poetic imagery.
Some readers prefer an unlyrical approach that focuses on facts rather than emotions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: emotional - showing strong feelings or emotion
  • antonym: lyrical - expressing deep personal emotion or feelings
  • synonym: prosaic - lacking poetic beauty; straightforward or dull
  • synonym: restrained - showing control and not expressing strong emotion
