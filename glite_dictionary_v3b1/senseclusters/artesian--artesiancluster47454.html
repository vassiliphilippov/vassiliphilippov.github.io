artesian

adjective
rising naturally under pressure (water)
Pronunciation: / ɑrˈtiʒən / (AmE), / ɑːˈtiːʒən / (BrE)
(of water) rising to the surface naturally under internal hydrostatic pressure, as in an artesian well.

Examples

Artesian well water
Farmers benefit from artesian pressure, which brings groundwater to the surface without pumping.
Much of the area's drinking water is supplied by artesian sources deep underground.
The engineers drilled an artesian well to provide fresh water for the village.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: subartesian - of water not quite reaching the surface under its own pressure
