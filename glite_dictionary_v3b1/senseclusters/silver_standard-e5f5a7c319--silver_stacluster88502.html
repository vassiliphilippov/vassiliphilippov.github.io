silver standard

noun
monetary system based on silver
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪlvər ˌstændərd / (AmE), / ˈsɪlvə ˌstændəd / (BrE)
A monetary system in which the standard unit of currency is defined by a specified amount of silver.

Examples

Silver standard currency system
After abandoning the silver standard, the nation experienced significant currency fluctuations.
Debate over the silver standard versus the gold standard played a major role in economic policy.
The country adopted the silver standard in the late nineteenth century.
