mathematical induction

noun
method of proof in mathematics
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌmæθəˈmætɪkəl ɪnˈdʌkʃən / (AmE), / ˌmæθəˈmatɪkəl ɪnˈdʌkʃən / (BrE)
a method of proving that a statement holds for all natural numbers by showing it is true for the first case and that, if it holds for an arbitrary case, it holds for the next one

Examples

Mathematical induction proof
By using mathematical induction, she demonstrated the formula holds for all integer values of n.
Students often find it challenging to construct a valid mathematical induction argument.
The proof that every natural number greater than one is a product of primes relies on mathematical induction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: proof by contradiction - proving a statement by assuming the opposite and reaching a contradiction
