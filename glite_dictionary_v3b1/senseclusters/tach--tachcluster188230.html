tach

noun
instrument for measuring rotational speed
Pronunciation: / tætʃ / (AmE), / tætʃ /; / tæk / (BrE)
an instrument (informal for tachometer) that measures and shows the speed of rotation of an engine or shaft, especially in vehicles

Examples

A tach meter
Many race cars have a digital tach mounted prominently on the dashboard.
My motorcycle's tach showed the RPMs spiking as I accelerated.
The mechanic checked the tach to ensure the engine wasn't over-revving.
Always watch the tach when you're accelerating quickly.
He glanced at the tach to see if he was pushing the engine too hard.
The tach isn't working, so I have to guess my RPMs.
