backcast

noun
analysis of past data
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈbækˌkɑːst / (BrE)
an estimate or analysis of past events or data using current models or information

Examples

A climate backcast
The research team used a backcast to reconstruct the region's climate over the last century.

A backcast can help scientists understand how climate has changed over time.
