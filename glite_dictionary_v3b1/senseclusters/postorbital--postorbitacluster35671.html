postorbital

noun
bone behind eye socket
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊstˈɔːrbɪtəl / (AmE), / ˌpəʊstˈɔːbɪtəl / (BrE)
a bone or bony projection located behind the eye socket in certain vertebrates, especially reptiles and some extinct animals

Examples

Postorbital bone
The dinosaur's postorbital was unusually thick, suggesting it had a powerful bite.
Scientists identified the fossil by examining the shape of its postorbital bone.
In some reptiles, the postorbital helps form the side of the skull behind the eye.

Related Terms

  • synonym: orbit - the bony cavity in the skull that contains the eye
  • antonym: preorbital - a bone or area located in front of the eye socket
