spectroscopist

noun
scientist studying light
Pronunciation: / spɛkˈtrɑːskəpɪst / (AmE), / spɛkˈtrɒskəpɪst / (BrE)
a scientist who studies how light interacts with materials or matter using spectroscopy

Examples

The spectroscopist studies light
The spectroscopist carefully prepared the sample before beginning the analysis.
As a spectroscopist, she often works with advanced optical instruments in the laboratory.
Many discoveries in astronomy have been made by a skilled spectroscopist interpreting starlight.
The spectroscopist analyzed the starlight to determine the star's chemical composition.
As a skilled spectroscopist, she contributed to important discoveries in astrophysics.
Every spectroscopist needs precise instruments to measure the wavelengths of light accurately.
