frostweed

noun
a tall perennial herb with white flowers
3.2
Pronunciation: / ˈfrɑstˌwid / (AmE), / ˈfrɒstˌwiːd / (BrE)
A tall perennial herb native to the eastern United States, with clusters of white flowers, commonly found in fields and open woodlands; also known as white crownbeard.

Examples

Wild frostweed flowers
Gardeners appreciate frostweed for attracting butterflies and providing late-season nectar.
In winter, ice formations around the base of frostweed create a beautiful, crystalline landscape.
The woods behind our house are filled with frostweed blooming in early autumn.
