blur

verb
to lose clear vision
3.7 2/m
Pronunciation: / bl…ùÀê / (AmE), / bl…úÀê / (BrE)
(of eyes or vision) to become unfocused or glassy; to lose clarity of sight.

Examples

Eyes blur suddenly
Fatigue made her vision blur, making it difficult to read the page.
If you stare at the screen for too long, your eyesight can blur temporarily.
When he started to cry, the tears caused his eyes to blur.

Synonyms

  • film over
    7.3 0.05/m
    film over forms a thin coating that makes the eyes dim or cloudy, while blur means vision loses clear detail.
  • unfocus
    7.4 0.02/m
