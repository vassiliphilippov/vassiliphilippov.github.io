privet

noun
shrub often used for hedges
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪvət / (AmE), / ˈprɪvɪt / (BrE)
a shrub of the genus Ligustrum, with smooth leaves and clusters of small white flowers followed by black berries, often grown as a hedge

Examples

A privet hedge
After flowering, the privet produces small black berries that attract birds.
Gardeners often choose privet for its dense foliage and ability to form neat boundaries.
The privet hedge in front of the house needed trimming before summer arrived.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hedge - a fence or boundary formed by closely growing bushes or shrubs
