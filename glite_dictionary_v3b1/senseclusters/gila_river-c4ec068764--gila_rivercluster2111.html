Gila River

proper noun
river in the southwestern United States
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈhiːlə ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈhiːlə ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in the southwestern United States that flows from western New Mexico westward through southern Arizona, joining the Colorado River

Examples

The Gila River flows
After heavy rainfall, the Gila River can rise significantly and cause local flooding.
Early settlers used the Gila River for irrigation and transportation in Arizona.
The Gila River supports a wide variety of wildlife along its banks.
