Gleditsia aquatica

noun
tree of southern U.S. swamps, water locust
Pronunciation: / ɡlɛˈdɪtsiə əˈkwɑtɪkə / (AmE), / ɡlɪˈdɪtsiə ɔˈkwætɪkə / (BrE)
A tree native to swamps and bottomlands of the southern United States, known for its short oval pods and dark, heavy wood; also called water locust.
