rhizomatous begonia

noun
begonia with creeping stems or rhizomes
7.3
Pronunciation: / raɪˈzoʊmətəs bəˈɡoʊniə / (AmE), / raɪˈzəʊmətəs bɪˈɡəʊniə / (BrE)
A begonia species or cultivar characterized by prominent creeping stems or rhizomes, often grown for ornamental foliage.

Examples

The rhizomatous begonia plant
Many gardeners prize the rhizomatous begonia for its unique foliage patterns.
The rhizomatous begonia thrives in shaded areas and spreads through its creeping stems.
Unlike tuberous varieties, the rhizomatous begonia propagates easily from its rhizomes.
