reprovision

verb
to supply again
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˌriːprəˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / ˌriːprəˈvɪʒən / (BrE)
to supply or equip again with necessary resources or services

Examples

To reprovision computers
The IT department will reprovision the computers to ensure they have the latest software.
After the storm, the team had to reprovision the shelter with food and water.
If the system fails, we may need to reprovision the network devices remotely.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deprive - to take something away from someone
  • synonym: resupply - to provide with new supplies
