cold comfort

noun
slight or inadequate consolation
5.0 5.7
Pronunciation: /koʊld ˈkʌm.fərt/ /; / /kəʊld ˈkʌm.fərt/ (AmE), /kəʊld ˈkʌm.fət/ (BrE)
a slight or inadequate consolation; something that offers little relief or reassurance in a difficult situation

Examples

Cold comfort after loss
Knowing he did his best was cold comfort after the team's defeat.
The apology was cold comfort to the victims who had lost everything.
When I lost my job, being told I had learned valuable experience was cold comfort.
