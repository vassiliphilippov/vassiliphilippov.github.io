compact

adjective
concise; using few words or space
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpækt / (AmE), / kəmˈpækt / (BrE)
expressed in a clear, brief, and concise way, using few words or space

Examples

Compact writing
His writing style is remarkably compact, delivering complex ideas in just a few sentences.
She summarized the report in a compact paragraph that covered all the main points.
The professor preferred compact answers that demonstrated understanding rather than lengthy explanations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: concise - giving much information clearly and in a few words
  • synonym: succinct - briefly and clearly expressed
  • antonym: verbose - using more words than needed
