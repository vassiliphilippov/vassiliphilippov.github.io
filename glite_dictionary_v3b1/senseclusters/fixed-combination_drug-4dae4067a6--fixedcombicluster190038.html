fixed-combination drug

noun
a drug with fixed amounts of multiple ingredients
Pronunciation: / fɪkst kəmˈbɪnəʃən drʌɡ / (AmE), / fɪkst kəmˈbɪnəʃən drʌɡ / (BrE)
a drug that contains set amounts of two or more active ingredients in a single dosage form

Examples

A fixed-combination drug
Many modern treatments for high blood pressure involve a fixed-combination drug to improve compliance.
Research shows that a fixed-combination drug can enhance therapeutic outcomes in some chronic diseases.
The doctor prescribed a fixed-combination drug to simplify the patient’s medication regimen.
