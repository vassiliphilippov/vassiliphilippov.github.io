pivot

verb
make a major strategic change
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪvət / (AmE), / ˈpɪvət / (BrE)
to make a significant change in strategy, direction, or approach, especially in business or career contexts.

Examples

To pivot the business
After initial failure, the startup decided to pivot to a subscription-based model.
She chose to pivot from publishing to a career in technology.
When the market shifted, the company had to pivot its business strategy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adapt - to adjust or modify for a new purpose or situation
  • synonym: shift - move or change from one position or direction to another
