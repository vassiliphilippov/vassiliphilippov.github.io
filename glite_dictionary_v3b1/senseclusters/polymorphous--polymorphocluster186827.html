polymorphous

adjective
having many forms (biology, science)
9.2 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / Àåp…ëÀê.liÀàm…îÀêr.f…ôs / (AmE), / Àåp…í.l…™Ààm…îÀê.f…ôs / (BrE)
(of a species or population) existing in or having several different forms or types, not limited by sex differences.

Examples

Polymorphous species
Researchers studied a polymorphous species of lizard that included multiple color variations within the same population.
Some butterflies are polymorphous, with individuals showing distinctly different wing patterns.
The polymorphous nature of the fungus allows it to adapt to a wide range of environments.
