optical glass

noun
glass for making optical lenses
2.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɑptɪkəl ɡlæs / (AmE), / ˈɒptɪkəl ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
a clear, homogeneous glass with a known refractive index, used for making lenses and other optical components

Examples

Precision optical glass
Astronomers rely on telescopes built with precision optical glass to observe distant stars.
Special grades of optical glass are often custom-developed for scientific instruments.
The camera manufacturer uses only high-quality optical glass for its professional lenses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lens - a curved piece of glass or other transparent material used to converge or diverge transmitted light and form images
