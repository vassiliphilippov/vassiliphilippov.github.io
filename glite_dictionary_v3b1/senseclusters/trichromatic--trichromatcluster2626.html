trichromatic

adjective
having or involving three colors
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌtraɪkroʊˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌtrɪkrəʊˈmatɪk / (BrE)
having or involving three colors, especially referring to the ability to see or reproduce colors using three distinct primary colors

Examples

Trichromatic vision
Many primates, including humans, have trichromatic vision that allows them to perceive a wide range of colors.
Scientists used a trichromatic staining technique to differentiate various cell types under the microscope.
The trichromatic printing process mixes three inks to produce full-color images in magazines.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: dichromatic - having or involving two colors
  • antonym: monochromatic - having or consisting of only one color
  • degree higher: polychromatic - having or involving many colors
