Achillea ptarmica

noun
Eurasian herb with white button-shaped flowers
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈkɪliə tɑrˈmaɪkə / (AmE), / əˈkɪlɪə tɑːˈmɪkə / (BrE)
A Eurasian perennial herb with loose clusters of small, button-shaped white flowers and long grey-green leaves that can cause sneezing when powdered.

Examples

Achillea ptarmica plant
Achillea ptarmica thrives in moist soils and provides summer interest in cottage gardens.
In traditional remedies, powdered leaves of Achillea ptarmica were once used to induce sneezing.
The gardener planted Achillea ptarmica along the border for its attractive white blooms.
