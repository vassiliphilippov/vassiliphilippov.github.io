nonglare

adjective
reducing glare
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˈɡlɛr / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈɡleə / (BrE)
designed to reduce or prevent glare from light, making surfaces easier to see

Examples

A nonglare screen
The nonglare coating on the screen made it easier to read in sunlight.
She bought nonglare glasses to reduce eye strain while working at her computer.
Many car dashboards are made with nonglare materials to improve visibility while driving.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reflective - producing a reflection or glare
