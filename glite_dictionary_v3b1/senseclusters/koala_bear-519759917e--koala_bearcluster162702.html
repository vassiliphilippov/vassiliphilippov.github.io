koala bear

noun
Australian tree-dwelling marsupial
1.0 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / ko äÀà…ëÀêl…ô b…õr / (AmE), / k…ô äÀà…ëÀêl…ô b…ôÀê / (BrE)
A sluggish, tailless Australian arboreal marsupial with grey fur and large ears, feeding mainly on eucalyptus leaves and bark; also called a koala.

Examples

A koala bear
After spotting a koala bear in the wild, the tourists snapped several photos.
Children at the zoo were excited to watch the koala bear munching on eucalyptus leaves.
Many people mistakenly refer to the koala bear as a type of bear, but it is actually a marsupial.
