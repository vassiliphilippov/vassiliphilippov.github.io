break

verb
exchange large bill for small change
1.9 1.0
Pronunciation: /breɪk/ (AmE), /breɪk/ (BrE)
to separate, damage, or destroy something by force so that it is in pieces or no longer works; to fracture or crack (including bones, objects, surfaces, sets, etc.)

Examples

To break a window
Before paying the taxi driver, he had to break a fifty at the convenience store.
Could you break this twenty-dollar bill for me?
She went to the cashier to break a large note into smaller bills.
Huge waves often break just before reaching the shore, creating a line of white surf.
Surfers wait offshore for the perfect wave to break so they can ride it in.
We watched as the swell began to break against the rocks at low tide.
If the washing machine breaks, we will have to call a repair technician.
My old laptop finally broke and wouldn't turn on anymore.
The elevator broke yesterday and was out of service all afternoon.

  • synonym: pierce - to make a hole into or through something
  • synonym: rupture - to break or burst suddenly, especially relating to tissue or structures
  • synonym: shatter - to break suddenly into many pieces
  • synonym: snap - to break suddenly with a sharp sound, often into two parts
  • antonym: subside - (of waves) to become calmer and less violent
