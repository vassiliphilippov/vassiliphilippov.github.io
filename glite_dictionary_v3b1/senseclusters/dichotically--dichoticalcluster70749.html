dichotically

adverb
in two contrasting ways
9.9
Pronunciation: / daɪˈkɑːtɪkli /; / daɪˈkɑtɪkli / (AmE), / daɪˈkɒtɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that involves presenting two different or contrasting things (especially sounds) to each ear at the same time

Examples

Sounds dichotically presented
The experiment presented sounds dichotically to each ear to test auditory processing.
The issue was discussed dichotically, leaving no room for a middle ground.
In psychology, information can be delivered dichotically to study how the brain processes competing signals.
The researchers played music dichotically to study how the brain processes competing sounds.
When the words were presented dichotically, participants found it harder to focus on one message.
Listening to two stories dichotically can be challenging for most people.

Related Terms

  • antonym: binaurally - involving both ears, often with the same sound
  • synonym: dichotomously - in a manner that divides into two distinct parts
