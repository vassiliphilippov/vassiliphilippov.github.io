eep

verb
to squeak in surprise
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / iÀêp / (AmE), / iÀêp / (BrE)
to make a short, high-pitched sound, usually from surprise or fear

Examples

To eep in fear
She accidentally eeped when the spider crawled across her hand.
He could not help but eep when the balloon popped behind him.
The mouse eeps whenever it is startled by a sudden noise.
