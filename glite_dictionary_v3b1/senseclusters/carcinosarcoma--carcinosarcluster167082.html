carcinosarcoma

noun
malignant tumor of carcinoma and sarcoma
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːrsɪnoʊsɑːrˈkoʊmə / (AmE), / ˌkɑːsɪnəʊsɑːˈkəʊmə / (BrE)
A malignant tumor composed of both carcinoma and sarcoma cells, extensively intermixed.

Examples

A carcinosarcoma tumor
Histological analysis revealed the presence of carcinosarcoma in the uterine lining.
The patient was diagnosed with a rare carcinosarcoma involving both epithelial and mesenchymal tissues.
Treatment plans for carcinosarcoma may include surgery, chemotherapy, and radiation therapy.
