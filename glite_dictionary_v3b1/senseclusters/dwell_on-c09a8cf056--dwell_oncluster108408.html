dwell on

verb
speak or think at length about
Pronunciation: / ˈdwɛl ɑn / (AmE), / ˈdwɛl ɒn / (BrE)
to think, speak, or write at length about something, especially something unpleasant or troubling

Examples

To dwell on mistakes
He tends to dwell on his mistakes long after they've happened.
They dwelled on the failures instead of celebrating their successes.
Try not to dwell on negative thoughts before bed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: harp on - to repeatedly talk or complain about a subject
  • antonym: move on - to stop thinking or talking about something
