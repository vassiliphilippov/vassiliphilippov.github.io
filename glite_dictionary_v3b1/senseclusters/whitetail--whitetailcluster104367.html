whitetail

noun
North American deer with a white tail
3.7 2.24/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆwaÉªtËŒteÉªl / (AmE), / ËˆwaÉªtËŒteÉªl / (BrE)
A common North American deer (Odocoileus virginianus) known for the white underside of its tail, which it flashes when alarmed.

Examples

A whitetail deer
A whitetail darted across the road just as the sun was rising.
Hunters often look for the flash of a whitetail to spot deer in the dense forest.
The park ranger explained the habits of the local whitetail population to the visitors.
