interrater

noun
agreement measure
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈreɪtər / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈreɪtə / (BrE)
a statistic or measure that shows how much agreement exists between different raters or observers

Examples

Interrater agreement score
The interrater was calculated to assess the consistency between the two judges' scores.
A high interrater indicates that different observers are making similar evaluations.
Researchers reported the interrater to demonstrate the reliability of their coding process.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agreement - the state of having the same opinion or judgment
  • synonym: reliability - the consistency of a measure or test
