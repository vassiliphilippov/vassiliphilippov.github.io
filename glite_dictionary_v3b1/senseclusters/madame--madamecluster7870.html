madame

noun
French title for a married woman
6.1 3.8
Pronunciation: /mə.ˈdæm/ /; / /ˈmæ.dəm/ (AmE), /mə.ˈdɑːm/ /; / /ˈmæ.dəm/ (BrE)
a polite title or form of address for a woman, especially in French-speaking contexts (equivalent to 'Mrs.' or 'Ms.')

Examples

To call madame politely
At the French restaurant, the waiter politely greeted her, saying, 'Madame, your table is ready.'
The concierge addressed her as madame when she entered the hotel lobby.
The letter was neatly written and began with 'Chère madame', following French custom.
The host greeted the audience with 'madame et messieurs' at the start of the ceremony.
When traveling in France, it is polite to address a woman as madame.
The letter was addressed to madame Dupont, the director of the school.
