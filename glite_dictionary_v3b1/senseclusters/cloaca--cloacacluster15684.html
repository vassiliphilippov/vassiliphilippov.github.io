cloaca

noun
common exit cavity in many animals
4.2 8.6
Pronunciation: / kloʊˈeɪkə / (AmE), / kləʊˈeɪkə / (BrE)
(in zoology) a cavity at the end of the digestive tract in birds, reptiles, amphibians, most fish, and monotremes, into which the intestinal, genital, and urinary tracts open.

Examples

Bird cloaca
In birds, waste and eggs both leave the body through the cloaca.
The anatomy lab included a demonstration of a frog's cloaca for the students.
Unlike other mammals, monotremes possess a cloaca instead of separate openings.
