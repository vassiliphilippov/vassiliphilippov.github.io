virosome

noun
synthetic virus particle
Pronunciation: / ˈvaɪrəˌsoʊm / (AmE), / ˈvɪərəˌsəʊm / (BrE)
a synthetic particle that mimics a virus, used to deliver drugs or vaccines into cells

Examples

A virosome particle
Scientists developed a virosome to improve the delivery of the new vaccine.
The virosome technology allows drugs to enter cells more efficiently.
Researchers are testing how virosomes can be used in cancer treatment.

  • synonym: virus - a microscopic infectious agent that replicates inside living cells
