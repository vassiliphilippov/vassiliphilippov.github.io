pitcher's mound

noun
raised area where pitcher stands (baseball)
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪtʃɚz ˌmaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈpɪtʃəz ˌmaʊnd / (BrE)
The slightly raised area on a baseball field where the pitcher stands to throw the ball to the batter.

Examples

The pitcher's mound
Rain had made the pitcher's mound slippery, making it difficult for the game to continue.
The pitcher stepped onto the pitcher's mound and prepared for the first pitch.
Young players practiced throwing from the pitcher's mound during their team’s training session.
