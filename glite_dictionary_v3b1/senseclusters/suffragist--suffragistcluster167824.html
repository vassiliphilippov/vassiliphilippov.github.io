suffragist

noun
supporter of extending voting rights
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌfrədʒɪst / (AmE), / ˈsʌfrədʒɪst / (BrE)
A person who advocates for the extension of voting rights, especially to women.

Examples

Suffragist voting rights
Many suffragists organized rallies and marches to demand voting reforms.
She became a famous suffragist after years of campaigning for women's rights.
The efforts of male and female suffragists helped secure the passage of new voting laws.

Related Terms

  • synonym: suffragette - A woman seeking the right to vote through organized protest, especially in early 20th-century Britain.
