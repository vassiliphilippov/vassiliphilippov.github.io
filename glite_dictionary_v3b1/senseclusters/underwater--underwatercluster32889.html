underwater

adjective
having negative equity
7.0
Pronunciation: /ˈʌn.dər.wɔː.tər/ (AmE), /ˈʌn.də.wɔː.tə/ (BrE)
Having negative equity; owing more on a loan or mortgage than the asset (for example, a house or car) is currently worth.

Examples

Underwater mortgage
After the housing crash, many homeowners found themselves underwater on their mortgages for years.
Because the car depreciated quickly, she was underwater on the loan and couldn't sell it.
Lenders may restructure payments when a borrower is underwater to avoid foreclosure and losses.

Related Terms

  • antonym: above water - having sufficient value or funds so that liabilities do not exceed assets; opposite of underwater
  • synonym: in debt - owing money to lenders or creditors
  • synonym: negative equity - a situation in which an asset's market value is less than the outstanding loan secured on it
