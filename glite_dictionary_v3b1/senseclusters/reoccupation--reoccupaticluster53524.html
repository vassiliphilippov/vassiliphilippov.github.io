reoccupation

noun
taking back possession
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌɑːkjəˈpeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌɒkjʊˈpeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of occupying or returning to a place, job, or position that was previously held

Examples

The reoccupation of town
The army celebrated the reoccupation of the border town after months of conflict.
Following the peace agreement, the reoccupation of the region proceeded without resistance.
The reoccupation of the old headquarters marked a new beginning for the company.
The army's reoccupation of the city was met with mixed reactions from the residents.
After a long absence, his reoccupation of the CEO position surprised many employees.
Negotiations led to the peaceful reoccupation of the disputed territory.

Related Terms

  • antonym: evacuation - the act of leaving a place, especially for safety
  • synonym: resumption - the act of starting something again after a pause
  • synonym: retaking - the act of taking something back
  • antonym: vacation - the act of leaving a position or place
