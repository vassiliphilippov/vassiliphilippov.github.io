outshout

verb
shout more loudly than another
4.3
Pronunciation: / aʊtˈʃaʊt / (AmE), / aʊtˈʃaʊt / (BrE)
to shout more loudly than someone else

Examples

To outshout an opponent
No matter how hard he tried, he could not outshout his angry opponent.
She managed to outshout everyone in the heated debate.
The crowd tried to outshout the protesters but failed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: outcry - a loud protest or commotion
  • antonym: whisper - to speak very softly or quietly
