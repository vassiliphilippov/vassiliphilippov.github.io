parasitization

noun
process of being parasitized
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəˌsaɪtəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpærəˌsaɪtɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which an organism lives as a parasite in or on another organism, often causing harm

Examples

Parasitization of crops
The parasitization of crops by certain insects can lead to significant agricultural losses.
Scientists study parasitization to understand how parasites affect their hosts over time.
Some birds avoid nests that show signs of parasitization by other species.

Related Terms

  • synonym: infection - the invasion of the body by harmful organisms
  • antonym: mutualism - a relationship where both organisms benefit
