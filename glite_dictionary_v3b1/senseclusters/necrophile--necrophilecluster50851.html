necrophile

noun
person fascinated by death
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛkrəˌfaɪl / (AmE), / ˈnɛkrəˌfaɪl / (BrE)
a person who has a strong fascination with death, dead bodies, or funerary customs

Examples

A necrophile writer
The author was called a necrophile because of his detailed writings about ancient burial rituals.
Some people mistakenly believe that anyone interested in cemeteries is a necrophile.
Her collection of antique mourning jewelry led friends to joke that she was a necrophile.

Related Terms

  • synonym: taphophile - a person interested in cemeteries and funerary practices
