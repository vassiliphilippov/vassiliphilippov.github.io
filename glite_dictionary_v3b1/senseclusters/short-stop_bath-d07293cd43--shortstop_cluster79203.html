short-stop bath

noun
acidic solution halting photographic development
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɔrt stɑp bæθ / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːt stɒp bɑːθ / (BrE)
an acidic solution used in photographic processing to halt the development of photographic film or paper before fixing.

Examples

The short-stop bath solution
After developing the film, she immediately placed it in the short-stop bath to halt the chemical reaction.
It is important to prepare the short-stop bath using the correct concentration of acetic acid.
The technician replaced the exhausted short-stop bath to ensure consistent photographic results.
