reassort

verb
to sort again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːəˈsɔrt / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈsɔːt / (BrE)
to sort or arrange things again, especially by placing them into new groups or categories

Examples

To reassort the items
After the move, we had to reassort all the books on the shelves.
The manager decided to reassort the teams to improve productivity.
At the end of the season, the store will reassort its products for the new collection.

  • synonym: sort - to arrange things into groups or categories
