misfocus

verb
to focus incorrectly
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪsˈfoʊkəs / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈfəʊkəs / (BrE)
to direct attention or emphasis to the wrong person, issue, or aspect

Examples

To misfocus your efforts
If you misfocus your energy on small problems, you may miss the bigger issues.
The team misfocused on irrelevant details during the meeting and failed to solve the main challenge.
It is easy to misfocus when there are too many distractions in the workplace.

Related Terms

  • antonym: focus - to direct attention or effort to a particular subject
  • synonym: misdirect - to send attention or effort in the wrong direction
