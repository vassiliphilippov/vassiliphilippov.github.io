-ed

adjective
adjective-forming suffix
Pronunciation: / ɪd / (AmE), / ɪd / (BrE)
a suffix used to form adjectives from nouns, meaning 'having' or 'provided with' the thing mentioned

Examples

-ed for 'having' something
The word 'talented' uses the -ed suffix to mean someone who has talent.
Adding -ed to 'wing' creates 'winged', describing something that has wings.
In 'bearded', the -ed ending shows the person has a beard.

Related Terms

  • synonym: -ful - a suffix meaning 'full of' or 'having the qualities of'
