hyperphagia

noun
excessive eating
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˈfeɪdʒiə / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəˈfeɪdʒiə /; / ˌhaɪpəˈfeɪdʒɪə / (BrE)
an abnormally increased appetite or excessive eating, either as a medical condition or as a natural behavior in animals

Examples

Excessive hyperphagia behavior
The patient developed hyperphagia after starting a new medication.
Doctors monitored his hyperphagia to prevent further health complications.
Some neurological disorders can cause hyperphagia in affected individuals.
During autumn, bears experience hyperphagia to prepare for the long winter ahead.
Some medical conditions can cause hyperphagia and lead to rapid weight gain.
Migratory birds may show signs of hyperphagia before beginning their long journeys.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anorexia - lack or loss of appetite for food
  • synonym: overeating - eating more food than is needed or healthy
  • synonym: polyphagia - excessive or abnormal desire for food
