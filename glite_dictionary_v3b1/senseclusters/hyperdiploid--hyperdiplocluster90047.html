hyperdiploid

noun
cell with extra chromosomes
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˈdɪplɔɪd / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəˈdɪplɔɪd / (BrE)
a cell or organism that has more chromosomes than the normal diploid number

Examples

A hyperdiploid cell
The doctor explained that a hyperdiploid is often found in certain types of leukemia.
Researchers identified a hyperdiploid in the patient's bone marrow sample.
A hyperdiploid can indicate a specific genetic abnormality in cancer cells.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aneuploid - a cell with an abnormal number of chromosomes
  • antonym: hypodiploid - a cell with fewer chromosomes than the normal diploid number
