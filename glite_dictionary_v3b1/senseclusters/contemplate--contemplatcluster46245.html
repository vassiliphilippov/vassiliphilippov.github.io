contemplate

verb
look at thoughtfully; observe in deep thought
3.2 3.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑːn.təm.pleɪt/ (AmE), /ˈkɒn.təm.pleɪt/ (BrE)
to think about something deeply and carefully, often for a long time

Examples

To contemplate the future
As the years passed, I began to contemplate my future more seriously.
He would often contemplate her portrait for hours, lost in memories.
She sat on the porch to contemplate the sunset in silence.
Before making a final decision, they all contemplated the possible outcomes.
He paused to contemplate whether moving to a new city was the right choice.
She began to contemplate a career change after ten years in the same job.
After the ceremony, he sat alone to contemplate his next steps.
She likes to contemplate the meaning of life during quiet moments.
Some monks spend hours each day contemplating in silence.

  • synonym: meditate - to deeply focus one's mind for reflection or spiritual purposes
