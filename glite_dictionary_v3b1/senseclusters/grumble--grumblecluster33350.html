grumble

verb
to complain in a low, dissatisfied voice
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrʌmbəl / (AmE), / ˈɡrʌmbl /; / ˈɡrʌmbəl / (BrE)
to complain quietly or in a low voice, often repeatedly or in a dissatisfied way

Examples

To grumble about work
Instead of taking action, he just kept grumbling under his breath.
She tends to grumble about the cold weather every winter.
The workers grumbled when they heard about the new policy changes.
My grandfather often grumbles about the traffic in the city.
She grumbled quietly as she washed the dishes again.
Whenever the meeting runs late, several colleagues grumble under their breath.
He grumbled a few words under his breath and left the room.
The old engine would grumble to life on cold mornings.
Thunder began to grumble in the distance as the storm approached.

Related Terms

  • synonym: complain - to express dissatisfaction or annoyance about something
  • synonym: murmur - to make a low, continuous sound expressing dissatisfaction
  • antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration
  • degree higher: roar - emit a loud, deep sound, as of thunder or a large animal
