Gosplan

noun
Soviet planning agency
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈgɔːs.plæn/ /; / /ˈgɑːs.plæn/ (AmE), /ˈgɒs.plæn/ (BrE)
the central government agency in the former Soviet Union responsible for economic planning and setting production targets

Examples

Soviet Gosplan agency
The Gosplan played a crucial role in shaping the Soviet Union's industrial growth.
Every factory had to meet the targets set by the Gosplan each year.
Historians often debate the effectiveness of the Gosplan in managing the Soviet economy.
The Gosplan set ambitious production targets for factories across the Soviet Union.
Under Gosplan, economic decisions were made by government officials rather than by market forces.
Historians study the impact of Gosplan on Soviet industrial growth and shortages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: central planning - an economic system where decisions are made by a central authority
  • synonym: five-year plan - a government plan for economic development over five years
  • antonym: market economy - an economic system based on supply and demand with little government control
