husky

adjective
muscular and heavily built (person)
3.3 0.6/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆhÊŒski / (AmE), / ËˆhÊŒski / (BrE)
(of a person, especially a man or boy) muscular and heavily built.

Examples

Husky man
As a teenager, he was already quite husky and strong.
The coach looked for husky players to join the team.
The store has a special section for husky boys' clothing.

Synonyms

  • beefy
    4.0 2/m
    beefy is an informal word for a solid, muscular build with rounded bulk, while husky describes a heavily built, broad-shouldered person.
  • burly
    4.6 3/m
    burly is a common word for a large, strong person with a heavy build, while husky stresses muscular, heavily built form often in men.
  • strapping
    4.4 0.02/m
    strapping is a word for a tall, strong, and sturdy young person, while husky means muscular and heavily built without implying tallness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: burly - large and strong; heavily built
  • antonym: slight - (of a person) small and slender
  • synonym: strapping - (especially of a young person) tall and strong
