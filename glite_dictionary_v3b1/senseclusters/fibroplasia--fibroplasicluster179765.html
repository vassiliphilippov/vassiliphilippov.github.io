fibroplasia

noun
excessive tissue growth
Pronunciation: / ˌfaɪbroʊˈpleɪziə / (AmE), / ˌfɪbrəʊˈpleɪzɪə / (BrE)
the abnormal or excessive formation of fibrous tissue, often leading to scarring or impaired organ function

Examples

Excessive fibroplasia
The doctor explained that fibroplasia can sometimes cause complications after surgery.
Excessive fibroplasia in the lungs may interfere with normal breathing.
Researchers are studying ways to prevent abnormal fibroplasia during wound healing.
