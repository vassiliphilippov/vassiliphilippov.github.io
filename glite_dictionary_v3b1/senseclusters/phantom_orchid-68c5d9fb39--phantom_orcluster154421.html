phantom orchid

noun
rare white leafless plant of western North America
Pronunciation: / ˈfæn.təm ˈɔr.kɪd / (AmE), / ˈfæn.təm ˈɔː.kɪd / (BrE)
A waxy, nearly leafless white plant with clustered stems and racemes of white flowers, found in the northwestern United States to northern California and east to Idaho.

Examples

A phantom orchid plant
Botanists were thrilled to find a small cluster of phantom orchids in the remote canyon.
The phantom orchid is so pale it almost glows in the dense forest undergrowth.
The phantom orchid thrives in shaded areas, relying on fungi instead of photosynthesis.
