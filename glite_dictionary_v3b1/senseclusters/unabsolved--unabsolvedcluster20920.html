unabsolved

adjective
not released from duty
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnəbˈzɑlvd / (AmE), / ˌʌnəbˈzɒlvd / (BrE)
not freed or released from a duty, responsibility, or obligation.

Examples

Unabsolved promise
He felt unabsolved of his promise even after many years had passed.
The committee remained unabsolved of its responsibilities until the final report was submitted.
She could not rest, feeling unabsolved of her obligations to the team.
