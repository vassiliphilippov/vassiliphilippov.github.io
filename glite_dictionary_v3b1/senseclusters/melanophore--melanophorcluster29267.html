melanophore

noun
pigment cell in animals
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈmel.ə.nə.fɑːr/ /; / /ˈmel.ə.nə.fɔːr/ (AmE), /ˈmel.ə.nə.fɔː/ (BrE)
a cell in the skin of animals that contains dark pigment and is responsible for coloration or color changes

Examples

A melanophore cell
The color of the fish changes when its melanophores expand or contract.
Scientists studied the melanophore to understand how reptiles adapt their skin color.
A sudden change in temperature can trigger the melanophore to release more pigment.
The chameleon changes color by expanding and contracting its melanophores in the skin.
Scientists studied the melanophores in fish to understand how they adapt to different backgrounds.
When exposed to light, the frog's melanophores became less visible, making its skin appear lighter.
