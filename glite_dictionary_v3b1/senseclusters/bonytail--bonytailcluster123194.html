bonytail

noun
North American fish
Pronunciation: / ˈboʊniˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈbəʊniˌteɪl / (BrE)
a species of freshwater fish native to North America, known for its slender tail and also called the bonytail chub

Examples

The bonytail fish
The bonytail is considered endangered in the Colorado River.
Scientists are working to restore the population of the bonytail in its native habitat.
A bonytail can be identified by its long, slender tail and silvery body.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bonytail chub - another name for the bonytail fish
  • synonym: chub - a type of freshwater fish in the minnow family
