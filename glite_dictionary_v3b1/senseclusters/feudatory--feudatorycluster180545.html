feudatory

noun
a feudal vassal or landholder
5.7 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfjuːdəˌtɔːri / (AmE), / ˈfjuːdətri / (BrE)
a person who holds a fief and owes allegiance and service to a feudal lord

Examples

A feudatory lord
Many medieval kingdoms consisted of numerous feudatory states owing allegiance to the monarch.
The feudatory was required to provide military service to his liege lord.
The rights and duties of a feudatory were defined by the feudal system.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lord - a feudal superior who grants land in exchange for service
  • synonym: vassal - a person in a subordinate position to a feudal lord; a holder of land by feudal tenure
