feudatory

noun
a feudal vassal or landholder
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàfjuÀêd…ôÀåt…îÀêri / (AmE), / ÀàfjuÀêd…ôtri / (BrE)
a person who holds a fief and owes allegiance and service to a feudal lord

Examples

A feudatory lord
Many medieval kingdoms consisted of numerous feudatory states owing allegiance to the monarch.
The feudatory was required to provide military service to his liege lord.
The rights and duties of a feudatory were defined by the feudal system.

Synonyms

  • vassal
    9.9 0.56/m
    vassal is a medieval landholder who owes service and loyalty to a lord, while feudatory names that person in formal contexts.
  • liege subject
    9.9 0.00/m
    liege subject is someone who owes loyalty and service for land under feudal rule, while feudatory is the formal legal term.
  • liege
    9.9 0.01/m
    liege is used for a person holding land and owing service, but feudatory is a more formal, legal word for the same role.
  • liegeman
    9.9 0.00/m
    liegeman is an old word for a man who owes service to a lord, whereas feudatory is neutral and more formal.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lord - a feudal superior who grants land in exchange for service
  • synonym: vassal - a person in a subordinate position to a feudal lord; a holder of land by feudal tenure
