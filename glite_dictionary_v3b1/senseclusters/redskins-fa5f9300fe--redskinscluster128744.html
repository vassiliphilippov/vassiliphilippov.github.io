Redskins

proper noun
offensive team nickname
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛdˌskɪnz / (AmE), / ˈrɛdˌskɪnz / (BrE)
an offensive former name for sports teams in the United States, especially the Washington, D.C. football team, considered a racial slur against Native Americans

Examples

Redskins football team
The football team changed its name from the Redskins after years of public criticism.
Many Native American groups protested the use of Redskins as a mascot.
The term Redskins is now recognized as offensive and inappropriate in modern usage.
Many schools have changed their team name from Redskins due to concerns about cultural sensitivity.
The football team known as the Redskins faced criticism for its use of a Native American term.
Some alumni still refer to their high school teams as the Redskins, despite the official name change.
