hierarchization

noun
establishing a hierarchy
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪəˌrɑrkɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌhaɪ(ə)rɑrkəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌhaɪəˌrɑːkɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌhaɪ(ə)ˈrɑːkɪˌzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the process of arranging things or people in a hierarchy or the result of establishing such an order

Examples

Hierarchization of groups
Academic disciplines often undergo hierarchization as new fields emerge and older ones lose prominence.
Recent changes in policy reflect the hierarchization of priorities in government funding.
The hierarchization of roles within the company created clear lines of authority.
A new software tool was introduced to assist in the hierarchisation of complex datasets.
Academic disciplines often debate the hierarchisation of knowledge and its implications.
The company underwent a complete hierarchisation of its management structure last year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hierarchy - a system in which people or things are arranged according to importance
  • antonym: homogenization - the process of making things uniform or similar
