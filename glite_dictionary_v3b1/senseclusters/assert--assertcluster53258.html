assert

verb
to insist on one's opinions or rights
Pronunciation: / əˈsɝt /; / əˈsɜrt /; / əˈsɝːt / (AmE), / əˈsɜːt / (BrE)
to state or declare something firmly and confidently, or to insist on your rights or opinions

Examples

To assert your rights
If you don't assert your needs, they may go unnoticed.
It's important to assert your boundaries respectfully in any relationship.
She learned how to assert herself during meetings at work.
During the interview, he asserted his innocence clearly and confidently.
She constantly asserts that her approach is better than anyone else's.
The president chose to assert his authority during the meeting.
He calmly asserted that he had never broken the rules.
She asserted her innocence repeatedly throughout the trial.
The company asserts that its products are safe and effective.

Related Terms

  • synonym: affirm - to state something as true in a confident way
  • synonym: claim - to state that something is true, typically without proof
  • synonym: declare - to say something officially or emphatically
  • antonym: deny - to state that something is not true or refuse a request
  • synonym: maintain - to state something strongly as if it were true
