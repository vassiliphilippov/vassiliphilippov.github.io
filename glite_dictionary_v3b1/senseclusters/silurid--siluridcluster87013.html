silurid

noun
Old World freshwater catfish
7.7 9.2
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪl.ə.rɪd/ (AmE), /ˈsɪl.ə.rɪd/ (BrE)
A member of a family of Old World freshwater catfishes, characterized by naked skin and a long analytic fin often merging with the caudal fin.

Examples

A silurid fish
A scientist examined the unusual anal fin structure of the silurid specimen.
The river is home to several species of silurid found only in Asia.
Unlike other catfish, the silurid lacks scales on its skin.
