coffle

noun
group chained together
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːfəl / (AmE), / ˈkɒfəl / (BrE)
a group or line of animals or people, especially prisoners or slaves, chained or tied together for transport

Examples

A coffle of prisoners
The traders moved the coffle of camels slowly across the desert.
A coffle of prisoners was marched through the streets under heavy guard.
Historians studied records describing a coffle of enslaved people transported in the 1800s.
The traders forced the captives to march in a coffle for hundreds of miles.
Historical records describe a coffle of enslaved people being moved to the coast.
A coffle of donkeys carried supplies through the desert.

Related Terms

  • synonym: caravan - a group of people or animals traveling together, often across a desert
  • synonym: chain gang - a group of prisoners chained together to perform physical labor
