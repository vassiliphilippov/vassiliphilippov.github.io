outcook

verb
to cook better

to cook better than someone else, especially in a competition

Examples

To outcook other chefs
In the final round of the competition, she managed to outcook all the other contestants.
He was determined to outcook his rival by preparing a more creative dish.
Despite her nerves, Maria hoped she could outcook the experienced chefs on the show.
During the competition, she managed to outcook all the other chefs with her creative dishes.
He tried to outcook his brother at the family barbecue, but ran out of time.
No one could outcook Grandma when it came to holiday feasts.
