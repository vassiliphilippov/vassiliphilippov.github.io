to oppose, resist, or not follow something such as a rule, law, expectation, or popular opinion

Examples

To go against rules
It takes courage to go against popular opinion in your community.
She decided to go against her parents' wishes and study art instead of law.
Sometimes employees go against company policy to stand up for what they believe is right.
Breaking your promise would go against everything you said you stood for.
He knew that to go against the company's policy could have serious consequences.
She refused to go against her conscience, even under pressure from others.
His actions went against company policy and resulted in disciplinary measures.
The new proposal goes against everything we have agreed on in previous meetings.
Wearing such bright colors goes against the school's dress code.

Synonyms

  • oppose
    2.9 91/m
    oppose is to be against or try to stop something actively, while go against means not to follow or accept it.
  • resist
    2.2 19/m
    resist pushes back or refuses pressure or change, while go against describes refusing or not following rules or opinion.
  • defy
    3.7 8.02/m
    defy openly challenges or refuses to obey an authority or rule, while go against means opposing or not following it.
  • fly in the face of
    7.6 1.06/m
    fly in the face of directly contradicts beliefs, rules, or common sense, while go against means oppose or not follow them.
  • stand up against
    3.7 1.13/m
    stand up against resists someone or something, often to protect rights, while go against simply means oppose or not follow it.
  • buck
    6.7 2.27/m
    buck resists a general direction or authority in casual speech, while go against is a neutral phrase for opposing or not following it.
  • buck the trend
    6.9 0.17/m
    buck the trend means doing something different from most people, while go against means opposing rules, opinion, or expectations more broadly.
  • violate
    4.4 36/m
    violate breaks a law, rule, or agreement, often causing legal consequences, while go against means oppose or not follow without implying crime.
  • breach
    4.5 1.92/m
    breach breaks a contract, agreement, or rule in a formal way, while go against means oppose or not follow more generally.
  • transgress
    7.8 1.28/m
    transgress means cross a moral or legal boundary and do something forbidden, while go against is a more general word for opposing it.
