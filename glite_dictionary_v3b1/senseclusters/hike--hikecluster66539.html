hike

noun
sudden or large increase (especially in prices)
4.1
Pronunciation: / haɪk / (AmE), / haɪk / (BrE)
a sudden or significant increase in the cost, amount, or level of something, especially prices, rates, or wages

Examples

A price hike
A sudden hike in tuition fees led to student protests across the country.
The government announced a 5% hike in fuel prices last month.
There was an unexpected hike in electricity bills this winter.
Due to rising inflation, the company announced a hike in its product prices.
She was hoping for a 5% hike in her wages this year.
The employees were delighted to receive a substantial hike in their annual salaries.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cut - a reduction or decrease, the opposite of an increase
  • synonym: increase - an instance of becoming greater in size, amount, or degree
  • synonym: raise - an increase in salary or wages
