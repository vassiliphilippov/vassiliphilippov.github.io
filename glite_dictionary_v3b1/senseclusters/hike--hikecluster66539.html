hike

noun
sudden or large increase (especially in prices)
3.6 4.19/m
Pronunciation: / haÉªk / (AmE), / haÉªk / (BrE)
a sudden or significant increase in the cost, amount, or level of something, especially prices, rates, or wages

Examples

A price hike
A sudden hike in tuition fees led to student protests across the country.
The government announced a 5% hike in fuel prices last month.
There was an unexpected hike in electricity bills this winter.
Due to rising inflation, the company announced a hike in its product prices.
She was hoping for a 5% hike in her wages this year.
The employees were delighted to receive a substantial hike in their annual salaries.

  • upsurge
    5.0 1.14/m

  • jump
    3.3 3.21/m
    a jump is a sudden large rise in value or amount, while a hike is often a specific increase in prices or wages.
  • run-up
    5.9 1.23/m
    a run-up is a rapid rise that happens before an event or market change, while a hike is a direct rise in price or wage.
  • step-up
    3.6 0.63/m
    a step-up is a planned or staged increase often set by rules, while a hike is any sudden or notable rise in costs or wages.
  • rise
    1.9 43/m
    a rise is a general increase that can be gradual, while a hike is usually a clear, sudden increase in price or wage.
