oka

noun
old Turkish liquid unit (approx. 1.3 pints)
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊkə / (AmE), / ˈəʊkə / (BrE)
a former Turkish unit of liquid measure, approximately equal to 1.3 pints (0.57 liters)

Examples

A oka of oil
During the Ottoman era, merchants would measure olive oil in okas.
Historical trade records often list commodities in okas rather than in kilograms.
The recipe calls for half an oka of rosewater, following traditional Turkish methods.
