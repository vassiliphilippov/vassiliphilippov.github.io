overcapitalization

noun
raising more capital than warranted
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvɚˌkæpɪtələˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌoʊ.vɚˌkæp.ɪ.təl.aɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəkæpɪtələˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌəʊ.vəˌkæp.ɪ.tələ.aɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
(in business) the situation in which a company issues more shares or raises more capital than its actual value or earning power justifies.

Examples

Company overcapitalization problem
Economists warn that overcapitalization can lead to financial instability and loss of shareholder value.
Reducing overcapitalization often requires restructuring the firm's equity and debt.
The company's overcapitalization left it with too much stock relative to its earnings.
A history of overcapitalisation made the firm vulnerable during the economic downturn.
Analysts warned that rapid expansion could cause overcapitalisation and financial instability.
The company suffered from overcapitalisation, leading to poor returns for its investors.
