reinfusion

noun
returning fluid to system
7.9
Pronunciation: /riː.ɪn.ˈfjuː.ʒən/ /; / /rɪn.ˈfjuː.ʒən/ (AmE), /riː.ɪn.ˈfjuː.ʒən/ /; / /rɪn.ˈfjuː.ʒən/ (BrE)
the process of putting a fluid back into a system, such as returning treated water or coolant to its source

Examples

Reinfusion of coolant
The reinfusion of coolant is essential to keep the machinery from overheating during operation.
After treatment, the reinfusion of water into the aquifer helps maintain groundwater levels.
Engineers monitored the reinfusion process to ensure the fluid returned safely to the system.
