tunica conjunctiva palpebrarum

noun
inner eyelid conjunctiva
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈtuː.nɪ.kə kɑːn.dʒəŋk.ˈtaɪ.və ˈpæl.pə.brə.rəm/ /; / /ˈtuː.nɪ.kə kən.dʒʌŋk.ˈtɪ.və ˈpæl.pə.brə.rəm/ (AmE), /ˈtjuː.nɪ.kə kɒn.dʒʌŋk.ˈtɪ.və ˈpæl.pə.brə.rəm/ /; / /ˈtjuː.nɪ.kə kɒn.dʒʌŋk.ˈtaɪ.və ˈpæl.pə.brə.rəm/ (BrE)
the part of the conjunctiva that lines the inner surface of the eyelids and is continuous with the bulbar conjunctiva

Examples

Inflammation of the tunica conjunctiva palpebrarum may cause irritation and redness of the eyelids.
The tunica conjunctiva palpebrarum protects the inner surface of the eyelids and helps keep the eye moist.
