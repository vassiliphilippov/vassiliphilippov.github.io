taking hold

noun
the act of gripping firmly
Pronunciation: / ˈteɪkɪŋ hoʊld / (AmE), / ˈteɪkɪŋ həʊld / (BrE)
the act of gripping something firmly with the hands or similar appendages

Examples

Strong taking hold of the rope
He lost his taking hold on the rope and slipped back down.
The octopus's taking hold of its prey was swift and efficient.
With a sudden taking hold of the branch, she pulled herself up onto the rock.

  • antonym: release - the act of letting go or freeing something held
