ribosome

noun
cell organelle for protein synthesis
3.7 6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈraɪ.bə.soʊm/ /; / /ˈraɪ.bə.zoʊm/ (AmE), /ˈraɪ.bə.səʊm/ /; / /ˈraɪ.bə.zəʊm/ (BrE)
a small organelle found in the cytoplasm of cells, where proteins are synthesized from amino acids according to the instructions of messenger RNA

Examples

A ribosome in cells
Each ribosome reads messenger RNA to help produce chains of amino acids.
In the cytoplasm, the ribosome assembles proteins needed for various cellular functions.
Scientists use high-powered microscopes to study the structure of the ribosome.
