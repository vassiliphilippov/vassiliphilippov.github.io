hedge in

verb
enclose or restrict closely
Pronunciation: / hɛdʒ ɪn / (AmE), / hɛdʒ ɪn / (BrE)
to enclose or restrict, especially by or as if by a hedge; to surround closely

Examples

To hedge in a yard
Developers often hedge in their properties to mark boundaries and keep out animals.
He felt totally hedged in by the strict rules at boarding school.
Tall trees hedge in the garden on every side, creating a sense of privacy.
