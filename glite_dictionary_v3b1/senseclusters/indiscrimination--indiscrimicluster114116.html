indiscrimination

noun
lack of distinction
7.9
Pronunciation: /ɪn.dɪ.skrɪ.mɪˈneɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ɪn.dɪ.skrɪ.məˈneɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɪn.dɪ.skrɪ.mɪˈneɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ɪn.dɪ.skrɪ.məˈneɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the lack of careful distinction or the act of treating all things or people the same without considering differences

Examples

Indiscrimination in treatment
The indiscrimination shown in the policy led to many innocent people being affected.
Critics argued that indiscrimination in the hiring process resulted in unfair outcomes.
The report highlighted the dangers of indiscrimination during military operations.
The indiscrimination shown in the selection process resulted in a lack of qualified candidates.
Policies based on indiscrimination can sometimes overlook important individual needs.
Her indiscrimination between right and wrong made her decisions unpredictable.

  • synonym: impartiality - fairness and lack of bias or favoritism
  • synonym: indiscriminateness - the quality of not making distinctions
