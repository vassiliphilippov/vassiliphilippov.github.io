parhelic circle

noun
halo-like atmospheric optical phenomenon
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / pɑrˈhɛlɪk ˈsɜrkəl / (AmE), / pɑːˈhiːlɪk ˈsɜːkl̩ / (BrE)
a luminous halo appearing as a white or faintly colored circle parallel to the horizon at the height of the sun, caused by sunlight interacting with ice crystals in the atmosphere

Examples

A parhelic circle in sky
A parhelic circle was visible for hours, stretching across the sky in the cold morning light.
Photographers often wait years to catch a clear parhelic circle above the snowy landscape.
The phenomenon known as the parhelic circle forms when sunlight passes through plate-shaped ice crystals.
