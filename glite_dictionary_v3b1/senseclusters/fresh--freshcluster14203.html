fresh

adjective
feeling renewed energy or vigor
Pronunciation: / frɛʃ / (AmE), / frɛʃ / (BrE)
feeling or giving a sense of renewed energy or vitality; full of energy after rest or a break

Examples

Feel fresh after sleep
A shower in the morning always makes me feel fresh and alert.
After a good night's sleep, I woke up feeling fresh and ready for the day.
She returned from her vacation looking fresh and energized.
After a fresh shower, he felt ready to start the day.
The fresh mountain breeze made her feel alive and energetic.
There's nothing like a fresh burst of air to wake you up in the morning.
After the interruption, they resumed the discussion with fresh enthusiasm.
Our team needs some fresh ideas to solve this problem.
She moved to a new city to make a fresh start after her divorce.

Related Terms

  • synonym: energetic - having or displaying a lot of energy
  • synonym: invigorating - making one feel strong, healthy, and full of energy
  • synonym: new - recently created or having started to exist
  • synonym: original - existing from the beginning; not a copy; innovative
  • synonym: rested - having had enough rest; not tired
  • antonym: stale - (of air) no longer fresh and unpleasant or unhealthy
  • antonym: tired - in need of rest or sleep
