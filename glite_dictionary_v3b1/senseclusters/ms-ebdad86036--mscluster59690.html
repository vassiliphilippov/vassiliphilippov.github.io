MS

noun
master's degree in science
Pronunciation: / ˌɛm ˈɛs / (AmE), / ˌɛm ˈɛs / (BrE)
A master's degree in science, typically awarded after completing postgraduate study in scientific or technical subjects.

Examples

An MS degree
After completing his MS, he pursued a PhD in chemistry.
Many research positions require at least an MS in a relevant scientific field.
She earned her MS in biology from a prestigious university.

Related Terms

  • antonym: MA - master's degree in arts or humanities
  • synonym: MSc - Master of Science, primarily used in British English
