to make something more specific, limited, or described (such as by adding details, conditions, or modifiers)

Examples

To qualify a statement
Historians often qualify events to give them greater context and meaning.
She attempted to qualify the artwork as revolutionary within its genre.
The committee tried to qualify his achievements in their official report.
Grammar teachers often ask students to qualify nouns with descriptive adjectives.
In the sentence 'the tall man', the adjective 'tall' qualifies the noun 'man'.
You can qualify the statement by adding an adverb to limit its meaning.
He needed to qualify his statement with additional evidence before the audience would accept it.
The spokesman qualified his earlier remarks, saying they only applied under certain conditions.
When asked about her comments, she chose to qualify them by explaining her intentions.
