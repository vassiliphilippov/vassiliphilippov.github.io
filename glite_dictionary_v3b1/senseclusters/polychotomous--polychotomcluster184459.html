polychotomous

adjective
having multiple categories
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːliˈkɑːtəməs / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈkɒtəməs / (BrE)
divided or classified into more than two categories or parts

Examples

Polychotomous classification
The researchers used a polychotomous classification to group the different types of responses.
A polychotomous variable allows for more than two possible outcomes in a survey.
The test results were analyzed using a polychotomous scale rather than a simple yes or no.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dichotomous - divided into exactly two parts or categories
  • synonym: multicategorical - involving multiple categories
