venae interlobulares renis

noun
interlobular veins of the kidney
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌviːnaɪ ˌɪntərˌlɒbjəˈlɛəriz ˈriːnɪs / (AmE), / ˌviːni ˌɪntəˌlɒbjʊˈlɑːrɛs ˈriːnɪs / (BrE)
the interlobular veins of the kidney that receive blood from the arcuate vein and empty into the renal veins

Examples

The venae interlobulares renis veins
Anatomical diagrams often highlight the venae interlobulares renis to illustrate renal blood flow.
Damage to the venae interlobulares renis can impair the kidney's filtration process.
The venae interlobulares renis play a crucial role in draining blood from the cortex of the kidney.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: arcuate vein - vein in the kidney that gives rise to interlobular veins
  • degree higher: renal vein - the vein in the kidney that receives blood from interlobular veins
