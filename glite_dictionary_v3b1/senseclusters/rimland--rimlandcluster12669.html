rimland

noun
edge habitat zone
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪmlænd / (AmE), / ˈrɪmlænd / (BrE)
the edge or transitional area between two different ecosystems, often supporting a variety of plant and animal life

Examples

Forest rimland
Many rare species thrive in the rimland where the forest meets the open plain.
Researchers collected soil samples from the rimland to study changes in plant diversity.
The rimland between the wetland and the forest is crucial for migrating birds.
