high-sudsing

adjective
producing many suds
Pronunciation: / haɪ ˈsʌdzɪŋ / (AmE), / haɪ ˈsʌdzɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a detergent or similar product) producing a large amount of suds or foam when mixed with water.

Examples

High-sudsing detergent
Avoid using a high-sudsing soap in automatic washing machines, as it can cause overflowing.
She prefers high-sudsing products for cleaning carpets because they lift dirt effectively.
This high-sudsing detergent is ideal for washing clothes by hand.
