unbusy

adjective
not busy
5.0
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈbɪzi / (AmE), / ʌnˈbɪzi / (BrE)
not busy; calm, unhurried, or having little or nothing to do

Examples

An unbusy office
The office was unusually unbusy for a Monday morning, with few calls and little noise.
She enjoyed an unbusy afternoon, reading quietly by the window.
During the holidays, the usually crowded café felt pleasantly unbusy and peaceful.
Since I was unbusy this afternoon, I decided to organize my desk.
He found himself unbusy after finishing all his assignments early.
If you are unbusy tomorrow, would you like to join us for lunch?

Related Terms

  • antonym: busy - having a lot to do; occupied
  • synonym: free - not occupied or engaged
  • synonym: idle - not working or active
  • synonym: relaxed - calm and free from stress
