peculiarly

adverb
in a unique or characteristic manner
4.3
Pronunciation: / pɪˈkjuljɚli / (AmE), / pɪˈkjuːliəli / (BrE)
in a way that is unique or characteristic of a particular person, place, or thing

Examples

Peculiarly British custom
She reacted in a peculiarly calm manner, considering the circumstances.
The festival has a peculiarly local charm that attracts visitors every year.
The painting was executed in a peculiarly modern style that set it apart from earlier works.
