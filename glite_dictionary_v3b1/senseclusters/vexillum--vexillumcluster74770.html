vexillum

noun
Roman standard; flower petal
Pronunciation: / vɛkˈsɪləm / (AmE), / vɛkˈsɪləm / (BrE)
a type of flag or banner, especially used as a standard in ancient Rome, or the upper petal of certain flowers in botany

Examples

Roman vexillum
The Roman legion marched behind its vexillum, a symbol of unity and pride.
In botany, the vexillum is the large upper petal of a pea flower.
The museum displayed a well-preserved vexillum from the ancient battlefield.

Related Terms

  • synonym: banner - a long strip of cloth bearing a slogan or design
  • synonym: standard - a flag or emblem used as a symbol or rallying point
