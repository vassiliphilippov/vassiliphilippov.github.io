smokeproof

verb
to prevent smoke entry
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈsmoʊkˌpruːf / (AmE), / ˈsməʊkˌpruːf / (BrE)
to make a building, room, or structure resistant to the entry or effects of smoke, usually for fire safety

Examples

To smokeproof a building
They plan to smokeproof the stairwell before winter to improve fire safety.
The company was hired to smokeproof all emergency exits in the building.
After the renovation, the hotel decided to smokeproof its guest rooms for added protection.
