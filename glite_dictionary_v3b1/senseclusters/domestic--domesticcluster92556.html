domestic

adjective
adapted for human use
Pronunciation: / dəˈmɛstɪk / (AmE), / dəˈmɛstɪk / (BrE)
(of animals or plants) converted or adapted to live and be used in or around the home, or under human control.

Examples

Domestic animals
Corn is a domestic plant that has been cultivated for thousands of years.
Most domestic animals depend on humans for food and shelter.
The cat is now considered a domestic species, unlike its wild ancestors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: domesticated - tame and kept by humans; not wild
  • antonym: wild - living or growing in a natural environment; not domesticated
