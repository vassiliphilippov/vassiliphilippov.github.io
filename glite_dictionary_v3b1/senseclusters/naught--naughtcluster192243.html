naught

noun
nothing; zero; nothing of significance
8.3 1.72/m
Pronunciation: / n…ît / (AmE), / n…îÀêt / (BrE)
nothing; zero; the state of having no value, result, or importance

Examples

After hours of work, their efforts came to naught when the machine broke down.

Synonyms

  • nothing
    1.0 637/m
    nothing is the common everyday word for no value or amount, while naught sounds old-fashioned and often means worthlessness.
  • nil
    5.1 1.23/m
    nil is used in sports and formal language to show zero, while naught is older and often used in poetic or negative phrases.
  • zero
    1.0 0.00/m
    zero names the number or amount of none, while naught feels older and often means nothing or no value.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aught - anything; something (archaic/poetic)
  • synonym: nothing - not anything; no single thing
  • antonym: something - a thing that exists or is present
  • synonym: zero - the number 0; nothing at all
