brusquely

adverb
in a blunt or curt manner
7.4 0.65/m
Pronunciation: / Ààbr åskli / (AmE), / ÀàbruÀêskli / (BrE)
In a blunt, abrupt, or curt way; without tact or politeness.

Examples

Answered brusquely
He answered brusquely, making it clear he did not want to continue the conversation.
She brusquely dismissed the question, leaving the audience surprised by her tone.
The manager brusquely ordered everyone back to work without further explanation.

Synonyms

  • snappily
    5.9 0.01/m
    snappily sounds sharp and irritated in speech, while brusquely shows abrupt rudeness without tact or politeness.
  • curtly
    6.0 0.91/m
    curtly uses very short, often rude words in speech, while brusquely means abrupt, blunt behavior lacking politeness.
  • bluntly
    4.6 4.01/m
    bluntly speaks plainly and directly, often hurting feelings, while brusquely adds sudden abruptness and a lack of politeness.
  • tersely
    6.4 0.47/m
    tersely gives few words and sounds concise, while brusquely often sounds abrupt and also lacks tact or politeness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bluntly - in a direct and uncompromising manner
  • antonym: politely - in a courteous or refined manner
