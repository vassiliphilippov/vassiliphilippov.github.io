command module

noun
section of a spacecraft for crew operations
9.8
Pronunciation: / kəˈmænd ˌmɑdʒul / (AmE), / kəˈmɑːnd ˌmɒdjuːl / (BrE)
the section of a spacecraft in which astronauts live, control the spacecraft, and communicate with Earth, especially in missions such as Apollo

Examples

The command module spacecraft
After re-entry, the command module was retrieved from the ocean by the recovery team.
The Apollo missions relied on the command module for both navigation and life support.
The astronauts spent most of their time in the command module while in space.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lunar module - the part of a spacecraft used for landing on the Moon
