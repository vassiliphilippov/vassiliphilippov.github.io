disturbed

adjective
afflicted by anxiety or emotional trouble
1.0
Pronunciation: / dɪˈstɝːbd / (AmE), / dɪˈstɜːbd / (BrE)
feeling or showing emotional or mental trouble, such as anxiety, uneasiness, or mental instability

Examples

Disturbed thoughts
His disturbed mind kept him awake for hours every night.
She looked disturbed after hearing the bad news.
The disturbed expression on her face worried her friends.
His family became concerned when he began acting disturbed and withdrawn.
Many characters in classic literature have been portrayed as deeply disturbed individuals.
The patient was so disturbed that he required constant supervision in the hospital.
After the traumatic event, he became increasingly disturbed and withdrawn from his friends and family.
The counselor worked extensively with several disturbed adolescents who struggled to form healthy relationships.
The film offers an insightful portrayal of a disturbed character trying to reconnect with society.
