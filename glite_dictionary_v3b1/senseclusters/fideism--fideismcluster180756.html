fideism

noun
faith over reason
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈfaɪ.dɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈfaɪ.deɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈfaɪ.dɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈfaɪ.deɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the belief that faith is more important than reason, especially in matters of religion

Examples

Fideism in religion
His fideism led him to accept claims without demanding logical proof.
Some critics argue that fideism can hinder scientific progress by discouraging questioning.
The debate highlighted the difference between fideism and evidence-based reasoning.
Some theologians defend fideism as a way to preserve the mystery of religious belief.
The debate between fideism and rationalism has shaped much of modern philosophy of religion.
He rejected fideism, insisting that faith and reason must work together.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dogmatism - the tendency to lay down principles as undeniably true, without consideration of evidence
  • synonym: faith - strong belief or trust, especially without logical proof
  • antonym: rationalism - the belief that reason is the main source of knowledge
