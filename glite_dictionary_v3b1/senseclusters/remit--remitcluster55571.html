remit

noun
referral of a case to another court (legal)
Pronunciation: / rɪˈmɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈmɪt / (BrE)
(Law) The act of referring a legal case or matter back to another court or authority for reconsideration or further action.

Examples

Court remit
After reviewing the evidence, the Supreme Court decided to remit the case to the original tribunal.
On remit, the judge reconsidered the points of law raised by the appeal.
The appellate court issued a remit to the lower court for further proceedings.
