vouch

verb
give supporting evidence; confirm
9.4 4.1
Pronunciation: /vaʊtʃ/ (AmE), /vaʊtʃ/ (BrE)
to confirm or guarantee the truth, reliability, or good character of someone or something

Examples

To vouch for someone
Before lending her money, he asked if anyone could vouch for her character.
I can vouch for his honesty; he has always been trustworthy.
Several witnesses vouched that they saw the accident happen.
Before hiring a candidate, the company asks employees to vouch for their references.
I can vouch for her honesty because I've known her for years.
The witness vouched for the suspect's whereabouts on the night of the crime.
I can vouch for his honesty; he has always been trustworthy.
She was asked to vouch for her friend's whereabouts on the night of the incident.
The manager refused to vouch for the new supplier without further investigation.
