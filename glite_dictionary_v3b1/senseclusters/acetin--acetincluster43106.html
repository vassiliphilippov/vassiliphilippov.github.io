acetin

noun
a type of chemical acetate compound
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈæsətɪn / (AmE), / ˈæsɪtɪn / (BrE)
Any of three liquid acetates formed by heating acetic acid with glycerin, used in various industrial applications.

Examples

Acetin plasticizer use
Acetin is used as a plasticizer in the production of cellophane and other plastics.
Industrial processes often require pure acetin to ensure product quality.
The laboratory synthesized acetin by combining glycerin and acetic acid under heat.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glycerin - a sweet, colorless, viscous liquid used in food and medicine
