paraparetic

noun
person with paraparesis
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəpəˈrɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌpærəpəˈrɛtɪk / (BrE)
a person who has partial paralysis or weakness affecting both lower limbs (paraparesis)

Examples

A paraparetic patient
The hospital provided special support for each paraparetic patient during rehabilitation.
As a paraparetic, he learned to use adaptive equipment to improve his mobility.
Physical therapy sessions are essential for a paraparetic to maintain muscle strength.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hemiparetic - a person with partial paralysis or weakness on one side of the body
  • degree higher: paraplegic - a person with complete paralysis of both lower limbs
