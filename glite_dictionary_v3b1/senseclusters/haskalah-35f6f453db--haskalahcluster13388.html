Haskalah

noun
Jewish Enlightenment movement
Pronunciation: / hɑːˈskɑːlə / (AmE), / hɑːˈskɑːlə / (BrE)
the Jewish Enlightenment movement in 18th and 19th century Europe that promoted education, secular knowledge, and integration into wider society

Examples

The Haskalah movement
The Haskalah encouraged Jewish communities to embrace modern education and participate in European culture.
Many writers and thinkers were influenced by the ideals of the Haskalah during the 19th century.
The Haskalah played a key role in shaping modern Jewish identity in Europe.
The Haskalah encouraged Jewish communities to embrace modern science and secular studies.
Many writers of the Haskalah advocated for the revival of the Hebrew language.
The influence of the Haskalah led to significant social changes in 19th-century Jewish life.
