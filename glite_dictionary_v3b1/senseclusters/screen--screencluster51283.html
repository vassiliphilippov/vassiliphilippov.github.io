screen

noun
protective mesh covering in a frame
6.9 2.0
Pronunciation: /skriːn/ (AmE), /skriːn/ (BrE)
a physical covering, mesh, partition, or barrier used to protect, provide privacy, or separate spaces or materials

Examples

A window screen
She installed a fine metal screen on the door to let air in but keep bugs out.
The room was separated from the patio by a wire screen fitted within a wooden frame.
We replaced the torn screen on the kitchen window to keep out mosquitoes.
A wire mesh screen is essential in removing lumps from flour.
Large particles are retained by the screen during the mixing process.
The mechanic used a screen to separate fine sand from the gravel.
A mesh screen on the window keeps the insects out during summer.
The old house has storm screens to protect the windows from debris.
They installed a privacy screen around the patio for more seclusion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barrier - something that blocks or protects by standing in the way
  • synonym: blind - a covering for a window to block light or provide privacy
  • synonym: cover - something that covers or conceals another object
  • synonym: curtain - a piece of cloth or other material used to cover a window or doorway
  • synonym: divider - an object that separates areas within a room
  • antonym: exposure - the state of being exposed or visible, not concealed
  • synonym: filter - device for removing impurities or particles from a fluid or air
  • synonym: mesh - material made of a network of wire or thread
  • synonym: partition - a structure dividing a space into parts
  • synonym: sieve - a utensil with a wire or plastic mesh used to strain solids from liquids or separate particles
