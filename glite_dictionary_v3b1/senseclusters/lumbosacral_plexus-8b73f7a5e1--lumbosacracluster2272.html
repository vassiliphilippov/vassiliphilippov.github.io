lumbosacral plexus

noun
nerve network in lower spine
8.1
Pronunciation: /lʌm.boʊ.ˈseɪ.krəl ˈplek.səs/ (AmE), /lʌm.bəʊ.ˈseɪ.krəl ˈplek.səs/ (BrE)
a network of nerves formed by the lower spinal nerves, supplying the lower limbs, perineum, and coccygeal region

Examples

lumbosacral plexus nerves
Injury to the lumbosacral plexus can cause weakness or numbness in the lower limbs.
The lumbosacral plexus is crucial for movement and sensation in the hips and legs.
The surgeon carefully avoided damaging the lumbosacral plexus during the procedure.
