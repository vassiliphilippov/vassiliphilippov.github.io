offstage

adjective, adverb
not in public; in private
1.0 3.4
Pronunciation: /ˈɔːf.steɪdʒ/ /; / /ˈɑːf.steɪdʒ/ (AmE), /ˈɒf.steɪdʒ/ (BrE)
in private or out of public view; not openly or publicly

Examples

Offstage talks
Although the negotiations continued offstage, their outcome was soon announced to the public.

Much of politics happens offstage and never reaches the public.
The company's offstage strategies often surprised competitors.
There were many offstage negotiations that determined the outcome.
