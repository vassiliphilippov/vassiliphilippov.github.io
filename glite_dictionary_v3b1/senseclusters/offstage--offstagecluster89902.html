offstage

adjective, adverb
not in public; in private
Pronunciation: / ˌɔfˈsteɪdʒ /; / ˈɔfˌsteɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌɒfˈsteɪdʒ /; / ˈɒfˌsteɪdʒ / (BrE)
in private or out of public view; not openly or publicly

Examples

Offstage talks
Although the negotiations continued offstage, their outcome was soon announced to the public.
Many important decisions are made offstage before they are presented as policies.
The politicians acted friendly in public, but offstage they often disagreed.
Much of politics happens offstage and never reaches the public.
The company's offstage strategies often surprised competitors.
There were many offstage negotiations that determined the outcome.

Related Terms

  • antonym: public - open to all people; not private
  • antonym: publicly - in public; openly
  • synonym: secret - not known or seen or not meant to be known or seen by others
