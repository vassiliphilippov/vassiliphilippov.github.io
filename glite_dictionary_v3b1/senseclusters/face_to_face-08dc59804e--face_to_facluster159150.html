face to face

adverb
directly facing each other
Pronunciation: / ˌfeɪs tə ˈfeɪs / (AmE), / ˌfeɪs tə ˈfeɪs / (BrE)
in person; with people present together and looking directly at each other, as in a direct meeting or conversation

Examples

Face to face meeting
At the negotiation table, the leaders sat face to face to discuss the agreement.
The two chairs were placed face to face for the interview.
Their offices were face to face across the hallway, making it easy to talk.
After months of online meetings, the team finally met face to face in the office.
Standing face to face with her childhood hero, she could hardly believe it.
The teacher prefers to resolve conflicts face to face rather than through email.
I prefer having face to face conversations instead of texting.
The negotiations were held face to face rather than over the phone.
We finally met face to face after months of speaking online.

Related Terms

  • synonym: in person - in the presence of each other, as opposed to remotely
  • antonym: remotely - from a distance, without direct physical contact
  • antonym: virtually - using computers or the internet, not physically present
