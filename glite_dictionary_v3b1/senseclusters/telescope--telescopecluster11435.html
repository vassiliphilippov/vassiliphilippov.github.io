telescope

verb
compress or condense something
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛləˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪˌskəʊp / (BrE)
to make something shorter by compressing or condensing it, especially in length, duration, or content

Examples

To telescope a story
Historical events were telescoped to fit the hour-long documentary.
Our company plans to telescope the project timeline by eliminating unnecessary steps.
The director decided to telescope the two acts into a single performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: condense - to make something denser or more concise; shorten text or speech
  • antonym: expand - to make something larger or longer; the opposite of shorten or compress
