glyceride

noun
an ester of glycerol and fatty acids
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlɪsəˌraɪd / (AmE), / ˈɡlɪsəraɪd / (BrE)
an ester formed from glycerol and fatty acids, occurring naturally in fats and oils

Examples

Fatty acid glycerides
During digestion, enzymes break down glycerides into fatty acids and glycerol.
Most natural fats are composed largely of glycerides of fatty acids.
The laboratory synthesized a new glyceride for research purposes.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: triglyceride - an ester derived from glycerol and three fatty acids
