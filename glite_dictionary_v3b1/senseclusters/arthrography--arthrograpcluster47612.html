arthrography

noun
medical imaging of a joint with contrast
Pronunciation: / ɑːrˈθrɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / ɑːˈθrɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
A radiological examination of a joint after injecting it with a contrast medium, used to produce detailed images of the joint.

Examples

Arthrography of a joint
Arthrography can help detect small tears in the ligaments or cartilage of a joint.
Modern imaging techniques have largely replaced arthrography for certain diagnostic purposes.
The doctor recommended arthrography to better understand the cause of my shoulder pain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arthrogram - an image produced by arthrography
