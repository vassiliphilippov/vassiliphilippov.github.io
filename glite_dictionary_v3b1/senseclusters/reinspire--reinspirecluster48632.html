reinspire

verb
to give new inspiration
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪnˈspaɪər / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈspaɪə / (BrE)
to fill someone with new or renewed inspiration, enthusiasm, or motivation

Examples

To reinspire new energy
The teacher hoped to reinspire her students after a difficult semester.
A moving speech can reinspire a community facing tough times.
Traveling to new places helped him reinspire his creativity.
The artist traveled to the mountains to reinspire her creativity after a long period of doubt.
A good leader knows how to reinspire a team when morale is low.
Reading about others' achievements can reinspire you to pursue your own goals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: discourage - to cause someone to lose confidence or enthusiasm
  • synonym: encourage - to give someone support or confidence
  • synonym: inspire - to fill someone with the urge or ability to do something
  • synonym: motivate - to provide someone with a reason or incentive to act
