priestless

adjective
without priests
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːstləs / (AmE), / ˈpriːstləs / (BrE)
without a priest or priestly influence; not having a priest or not controlled by priests

Examples

priestless community
The community chose a priestless approach to spirituality, focusing on personal beliefs.
For centuries, the islanders maintained a priestless tradition, relying on elders for guidance.
The movement advocated for a priestless society, free from religious hierarchy.
The remote community remained priestless after the last clergyman moved away.
For decades, the old chapel stood priestless and unused at the edge of the village.
During the war, many churches became priestless due to the shortage of clergy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clerical - relating to priests or the clergy
  • antonym: priestly - relating to or characteristic of a priest
  • synonym: secular - not connected with religious or spiritual matters
