mechanical yielding prop

noun
friction-controlled steel support used in tunnels/mines
Pronunciation: / məˈkænɪkəl ˈjildɪŋ prɑp / (AmE), / məˈkænɪkəl ˈjiːldɪŋ prɒp / (BrE)
a steel support used in mining or engineering in which controlled yield is achieved through friction between sliding surfaces or telescopic tubes

Examples

A mechanical yielding prop support
A mechanical yielding prop absorbs pressure by allowing controlled movement between its telescopic tubes.
Proper placement of each mechanical yielding prop is essential for safety in underground construction.
The miners installed a mechanical yielding prop to support the roof of the tunnel.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hydraulic prop - a type of prop that uses hydraulic pressure for support
