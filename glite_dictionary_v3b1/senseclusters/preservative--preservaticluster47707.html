preservative

adjective
having the ability to preserve
Pronunciation: / prəˈzɝː.və.tɪv / (AmE), / prɪˈzɜː.və.tɪv / (BrE)
having the ability or tendency to preserve or protect against decay or change

Examples

Preservative effect
The museum uses preservative measures to protect ancient artifacts.
They added a preservative coating to the metal to prevent rust.
This paint has preservative properties that help wood last longer.
