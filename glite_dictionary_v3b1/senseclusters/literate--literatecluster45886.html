literate

adjective
knowledgeable or competent in a field
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪt̬.ɚ.ət / (AmE), / ˈlɪt.ər.ət / (BrE)
having knowledge or competence in a particular subject or field, especially beyond basic reading and writing abilities

Examples

Computer literate staff
Employers increasingly expect workers to be computer literate in today's digital world.
Many elderly people are not yet internet literate, which can limit their access to information.
She is literate in both environmental science and economics.

Related Terms

  • antonym: illiterate - lacking knowledge, education, or competence in a subject
