unweight

verb
to remove weight
7.9
Pronunciation: /ʌn.ˈweɪt/ /; / /ən.ˈweɪt/ (AmE), /ʌn.ˈweɪt/ /; / /ən.ˈweɪt/ (BrE)
to make something lighter by removing weight, load, or mass from it

Examples

To unweight a box
Before lifting the box, he tried to unweight it by taking out some books.
Cyclists often unweight their bikes before a steep climb to make pedaling easier.
The movers needed to unweight the truck so it could cross the old bridge safely.
