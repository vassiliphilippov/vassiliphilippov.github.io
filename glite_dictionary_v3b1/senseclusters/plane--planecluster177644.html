plane

verb
move fast across water's surface
Pronunciation: / pleɪn / (AmE), / pleɪn / (BrE)
to move fast enough on water so that a boat or board rises and skims over the surface

Examples

To plane on water
The speedboat began to plane as it picked up speed across the lake.
We watched the surfers' boards planing smoothly over the waves.
When a boat planes, it glides over the water with little resistance.
The speedboat started planing as it picked up speed across the lake.
When the wind was strong enough, the windsurfer enjoyed planing over the waves.
As soon as the boat reached a certain speed, it began planing and left a trail of spray behind.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glide - to move smoothly and continuously along a surface
  • synonym: skid - to slide without rotating (esp. a vehicle or boat)
