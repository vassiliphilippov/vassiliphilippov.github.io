ill-humored

adjective
unfriendly or bad-tempered
Pronunciation: / ˌɪlˈhjumɚd /; / ˌɪlˈhjuːmɚd / (AmE), / ˌɪlˈhjuːməd / (BrE)
often showing irritation or surliness; unfriendly or bad-tempered in manner.

Examples

An ill-humored person
After a long flight, he was too ill-humored to enjoy the family reunion.
Despite his accomplishments, the professor remained ill-humored during most of the interviews.
Her ill-humored comments made everyone else at the meeting uncomfortable.
Despite his ill-humoured remarks, his colleagues knew he meant no real harm.
Her ill-humoured tone made it clear she didn't want to continue the conversation.
The ill-humoured waiter provided little warmth or welcome during our meal.

  • antonym: good-humoured - cheerful and friendly in temperament
  • synonym: gruff - abrupt or rough in manner, often unfriendly
  • synonym: surly - bad-tempered and unfriendly
