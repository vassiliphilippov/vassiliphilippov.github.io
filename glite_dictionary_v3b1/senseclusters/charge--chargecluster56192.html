charge

verb
put electrical energy into a battery
1.0
Pronunciation: / tʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / tʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
to fill or supply with electricity or energy; to make something electrically charged

Examples

To charge a battery
It usually takes a few hours to charge this laptop battery completely.
Please remember to charge your phone overnight before the trip.
You should charge the device fully before first use for optimal performance.
He quickly charged the camera before leaving for his trip.
Make sure to charge the power tool fully to avoid interruptions during use.
The technician charged the extinguisher and checked that it was ready for an emergency.
A glass rod will become positively charged when rubbed with silk.
Scientists used static electricity to charge the metal sphere.
You need to charge the capacitor before the circuit will work.

Related Terms

  • antonym: discharge - to remove the electrical energy from a battery
  • synonym: load - to put something into a device or container so it is ready to be used
