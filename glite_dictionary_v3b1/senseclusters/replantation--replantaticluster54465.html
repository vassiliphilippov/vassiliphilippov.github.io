replantation

noun
planting again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːplænˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːplænˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of planting plants again or restoring vegetation, especially after damage or removal

Examples

Forest replantation
The replantation of native species helped restore the ecosystem after the construction project.
Farmers began a large-scale replantation to recover the fields damaged by flooding.
Community volunteers participated in the replantation of trees along the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deforestation - the removal of trees from an area
  • synonym: reforestation - the process of planting trees to restore a forest
