due to

preposition
because of; as a result of
1.0 106/m
Pronunciation: / du tu / (AmE), / djuÀê tjuÀê / (BrE)
because of; resulting from a particular cause or reason

Examples

Cancelled due to illness
Many events were cancelled due to the pandemic.
She missed the meeting due to a family emergency.
The flight was delayed due to bad weather.

Synonyms

  • because of
    1.0 261/m
    because of is the common spoken phrase for giving a reason, while due to appears more often in formal writing.
  • on account of
    1.7 3.66/m
    on account of sounds more formal or slightly old-fashioned and suits polite speech or writing, while due to is more neutral.
  • by virtue of
    4.5 2.31/m
    by virtue of suggests a formal or legal reason, often implying authority, while due to simply states the cause.
