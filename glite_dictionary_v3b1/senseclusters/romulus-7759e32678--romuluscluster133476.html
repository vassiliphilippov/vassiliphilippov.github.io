Romulus

proper noun
legendary founder of Rome in Roman mythology
9.0
Pronunciation: /ˈrɑː.mjə.ləs/ /; / /ˈrɑː.mjʊ.ləs/ (AmE), /ˈrɒm.jʊ.ləs/ /; / /ˈrɒm.jə.ləs/ (BrE)
(in Roman mythology) the legendary founder of Rome, who was said to have been raised with his twin brother Remus by a she-wolf.

Examples

Romulus and Remus
Romulus is often depicted as the founder of Rome alongside his brother Remus.
According to legend, a she-wolf cared for Romulus and Remus after they were abandoned.
Many ancient Roman coins bear the image of Romulus and the wolf.
