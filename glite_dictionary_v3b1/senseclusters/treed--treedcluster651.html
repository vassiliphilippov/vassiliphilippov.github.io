treed

adjective
forced into a place with no escape
Pronunciation: / trid / (AmE), / triːd / (BrE)
(of an animal or person) forced into a position where escape is impossible, often by being chased up a tree or trapped by a pursuer.

Examples

Feeling treed by argument
Feeling treed by the argument, he had no choice but to respond.
The fox was treed by the hunting dogs and could not escape.
The fugitives found themselves treed on a rooftop, with the police closing in from all sides.

Related Terms

  • synonym: trapped - Unable to escape from a difficult situation
