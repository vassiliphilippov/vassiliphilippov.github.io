glycyrrhizin

noun
licorice compound
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɡlaɪˈsɪrəˌzɪn / (AmE), / ɡlɪˈsɪrɪzɪn / (BrE)
a sweet-tasting compound found in licorice root, used as a flavoring and in medicine

Some candies use glycyrrhizin as a natural sweetener instead of sugar.
