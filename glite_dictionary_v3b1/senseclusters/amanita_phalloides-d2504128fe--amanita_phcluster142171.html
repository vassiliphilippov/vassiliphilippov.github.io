Amanita phalloides

noun
a deadly poisonous mushroom species
9.9
Pronunciation: /ə.mə.ˈnaɪ.tə/ /; / /æ.mə.ˈnaɪ.tə/ (AmE), /ə.mə.ˈnaɪ.tə/ /; / /æ.mə.ˈnaɪ.tə/ (BrE)
a highly poisonous species of fungus, usually pale or white, recognized by its cup-shaped base and white gills; commonly known as the death cap

Examples

The Amanita phalloides mushroom
Consuming even a small amount of Amanita phalloides can lead to serious, often fatal, liver damage.
Foragers must be very careful to distinguish Amanita phalloides from edible mushrooms like Agaricus.
The notorious Amanita phalloides mushroom is responsible for most fatal poisonings due to mistaken identity with edible varieties.
