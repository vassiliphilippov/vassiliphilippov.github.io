transgender

noun
a person whose gender identity differs from birth sex
Pronunciation: / trænzˈdʒɛndə / (AmE), / trænzˈdʒɛndə / (BrE)
a person whose gender identity differs from the sex they were assigned at birth; used to refer to such people (often in phrases like 'transgender people').

Examples

Transgender people
The organization offers support groups for transgender people who are facing workplace discrimination.
Several transgender people shared powerful stories about family acceptance at the conference.
The study surveyed 500 transgender people to learn about their employment experiences.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cisgender - a person whose gender identity matches the sex they were assigned at birth
  • synonym: trans - informal short form of 'transgender', used to refer to transgender people
