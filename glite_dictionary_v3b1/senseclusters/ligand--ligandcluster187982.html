ligand

noun
molecule or ion that binds to a central atom
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪɡænd / (AmE), / ˈlɪɡənd / (BrE)
A molecule, ion, or atom that binds to a central atom to form a coordination complex, especially in chemistry or biochemistry.

Examples

A ligand molecule
Researchers studied the interaction between the protein and its ligand using spectroscopy.
The iron atom in hemoglobin binds to an oxygen ligand during respiration.
This metal complex contains several types of ligand that influence its properties.
