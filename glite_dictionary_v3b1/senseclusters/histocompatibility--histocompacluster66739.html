histocompatibility

noun
compatibility of cells or tissues for transplantation
5.2 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhɪstoʊkəmˌpætəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌhɪstəʊkəmˌpætəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the property by which the cells or tissues of one individual are compatible with those of another, allowing transplantation without immune rejection

Examples

High histocompatibility level
A high degree of histocompatibility is crucial for a kidney transplant to succeed.
Advances in histocompatibility research have improved survival rates for bone marrow transplants.
Scientists test for histocompatibility before selecting organ donors for patients.

Related Terms

  • antonym: incompatibility - the condition in which tissues cannot be successfully transplanted due to immune rejection
