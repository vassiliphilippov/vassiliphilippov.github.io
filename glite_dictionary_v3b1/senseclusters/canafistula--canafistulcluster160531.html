canafistula

noun
tropical tree with medicinal pods
Pronunciation: / ˌkæn.əˈfɪs.tʃə / (AmE), / ˌkan.əˈfɪs.tjʊ.lə / (BrE)
A tropical deciduous or semi-evergreen tree (Cassia fistula) with fragrant yellow flowers and long pods whose pulp is used medicinally, native to Asia, Central and South America, and Australia.

Examples

A large canafistula tree
A large canafistula stood at the edge of the village, its yellow flowers hanging in clusters.
In Central America, the canafistula is often planted in gardens for its decorative blooms.
The ripe pods of the canafistula are collected for traditional medicines.
