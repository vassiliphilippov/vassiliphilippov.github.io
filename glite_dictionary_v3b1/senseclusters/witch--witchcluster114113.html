witch

verb
cast a spell or hex
8.4 6.4
Pronunciation: /wɪtʃ/ (AmE), /wɪtʃ/ (BrE)
To cast a spell or put a hex on (someone or something); to bewitch.

Examples

To witch someone
He claimed that someone had witched his cows, causing them to fall ill overnight.
Legends say the old well is witched and should be avoided at night.
The villagers believed she had witched their crops after a poor harvest.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bless - to confer or invoke divine favor upon someone or something
