stellar parallax

noun
apparent shift of a star's position due to Earth's orbit
7.1 7.6
Pronunciation: /ˈstel.ər ˈpær.ə.læks/ (AmE), /ˈstel.ə ˈpær.ə.læks/ (BrE)
the apparent shift in the position of a star when viewed from different points in Earth's orbit, used to measure its distance from Earth

Examples

Stellar parallax measurement
Astronomers calculate a star's distance from Earth by observing its stellar parallax over the course of a year.
Because stellar parallax is so tiny for distant stars, powerful telescopes are needed to detect it.
The first measurement of stellar parallax provided crucial evidence for the heliocentric model of the solar system.

  • synonym: parallax - apparent displacement of an object due to a change in the observer's point of view
