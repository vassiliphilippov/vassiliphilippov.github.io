calf's-foot jelly

noun
savory jelly made from calves' feet
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkæfs ˌfʊt ˈdʒɛli / (AmE), / ˈkɑːfs ˌfʊt ˈdʒɛli / (BrE)
A savory jelly prepared using gelatin extracted from boiled calves' feet, often served as a traditional dish or used in aspics.

Examples

Traditional calf's-foot jelly
My grandmother used to prepare calf's-foot jelly for special family occasions.
Some old recipes for calf's-foot jelly also include lemon peel and spices.
They served calf's-foot jelly as part of a traditional English supper.
