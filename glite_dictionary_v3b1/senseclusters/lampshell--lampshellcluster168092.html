lampshell

noun
marine animal with bivalve shell (brachiopod)
Pronunciation: / ˈlæmpˌʃɛl / (AmE), / ˈlampˌʃɛl / (BrE)
a marine animal with a bivalve shell and a pair of arms bearing tentacles for capturing food, commonly found worldwide; also known as a brachiopod

Examples

A lampshell animal
Fossilized lampshells can be found in many layers of sedimentary rock.
Scientists studied the feeding mechanisms of the lampshell in its natural habitat.
The biology exhibit included a live specimen of a lampshell in a saltwater aquarium.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brachiopod - a marine animal with two shells and a lophophore; another term for lampshell
