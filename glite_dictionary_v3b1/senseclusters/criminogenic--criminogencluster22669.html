criminogenic

adjective
causing crime
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkrɪmɪnəˈdʒɛnɪk / (AmE), / ˌkrɪmɪnəˈdʒɛnɪk / (BrE)
causing or likely to cause criminal behavior or crime

Examples

Criminogenic effect
Experts warn that overcrowded prisons can have a criminogenic effect on inmates.
Some neighborhoods are described as criminogenic due to high unemployment and lack of resources.
The study examined whether certain policies were actually criminogenic in their impact on youth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: criminal - relating to crime or criminals
  • antonym: innocuous - not harmful or offensive
