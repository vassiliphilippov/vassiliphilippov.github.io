Alzheimer's disease

noun
progressive brain disorder causing memory loss
Pronunciation: / ˈɑlzˌhaɪmɚz dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈalzhʌɪməz dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A progressive brain disorder that causes memory loss, impaired thinking, and eventual loss of the ability to carry out daily activities, typically affecting older adults.

Examples

Alzheimer's disease symptoms
Her grandmother was diagnosed with Alzheimer's disease at the age of sixty-five.
Memory loss and confusion are early symptoms of Alzheimer's disease.
Researchers are working hard to find a cure for Alzheimer's disease.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dementia - loss of mental abilities severe enough to interfere with daily life
