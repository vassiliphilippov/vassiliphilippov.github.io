Cotinus americanus

noun
North American shrubby tree, 'smoke tree'
Pronunciation: / koʊˈtaɪnəs əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnəs / (AmE), / kəˈtʌɪnəs əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnəs / (BrE)
A shrubby tree native to the southern United States, known for its large, feathery, smoke-like flower plumes.

Examples

Cotinus americanus tree
Many gardeners appreciate Cotinus americanus for its autumn foliage colors as well as its unique blossoms.
The Cotinus americanus is often planted for its decorative, smoky-looking flowers in southern gardens.
Wildlife is attracted to the Cotinus americanus when it is in full bloom.
