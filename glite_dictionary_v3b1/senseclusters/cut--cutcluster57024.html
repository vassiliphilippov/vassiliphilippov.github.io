cut

noun
trench made by erosion or excavation
9.2 6.1
Pronunciation: / kʌt / (AmE), / kʌt / (BrE)
a trench, channel, or man-made/natural passage, especially one made by excavation or erosion

Examples

A deep cut in the ground
Rainwater can create a deep cut in the hillside over time.
The construction crew worked to stabilize the cut along the railway.
The highway passes through a steep cut in the mountain.
Engineers constructed a cut to connect the two rivers.
Heavy rains turned the old cut into a fast-flowing stream.
The boat traveled through the narrow cut between fields.
