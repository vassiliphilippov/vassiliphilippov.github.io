ejecta

noun
expelled material
Pronunciation: / iˈdʒɛktə / (AmE), / iˈdʒɛktə / (BrE)
material that is thrown out from a volcano, explosion, or impact event

Examples

Volcanic ejecta
The ground was covered with ejecta after the volcanic eruption.
Scientists studied the ejecta to learn more about the meteor impact.
Layers of ejecta can reveal the history of past explosions in the area.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ash - fine particles left after something is burned, often from a volcano
  • synonym: debris - scattered fragments, typically of something wrecked or destroyed
