giggle

verb
to laugh in a light or nervous way
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /ˈgɪ.gəl/ (AmE), /ˈgɪ.gəl/ (BrE)
to laugh in a silly or nervous way, with repeated short, high sounds

Examples

To giggle nervously
She would often giggle nervously when asked a difficult question.
The children began to giggle at the funny faces their teacher made.
The group couldn't help but giggle during the awkward silence.
He often giggles nervously when he's asked a difficult question.
She tried not to giggle when the teacher mispronounced the word.
The children couldn't stop giggle during the puppet show.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chuckle - to laugh quietly or inwardly
  • synonym: snicker - to laugh in a partly suppressed, disrespectful manner
  • antonym: sob - to cry noisily with short, sudden breaths
