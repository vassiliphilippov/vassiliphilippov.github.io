involution

noun
process of folding inward
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.vəˈluː.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪn.vəˈljuː.ʃən / (BrE)
the process or action of folding or curling inward; the state of being enfolded or entangled

Examples

Involution of tissue
During embryonic development, the involution of tissues forms complex internal structures.
Some marine organisms protect themselves by involution of their bodies into shells.
The involution of the petals caused the flower to take on a closed appearance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: enfolding - the action of folding or wrapping something in
  • antonym: evolution - the gradual process of development, often contrasted in scientific use
