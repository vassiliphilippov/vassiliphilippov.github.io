noun

a person, organization, or authority that gives or issues official permission or a license to someone else, often to use something they own (such as a product, brand, technology, trademark, or property) or to carry out regulated activities (such as publications or other licensed activities).

Examples

The licensor grants permission
The licensor reviewed the agreement before allowing the company to use its trademark.
Each licensor sets their own terms for how their intellectual property can be used.
The software licensor requires annual fees for continued access to updates.
The licensor approved the use of their trademark for the new product.
Before using the software, you must get permission from the licensor.
The licensor can revoke the license if the terms are not followed.
The licensor reviewed all applications before granting permission to operate the new business.
A licensor can revoke a license if the terms are violated.
The broadcasting company acted as the licensor for several regional stations.
