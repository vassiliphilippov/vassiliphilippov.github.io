retcon

verb
to alter story history
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛt.kɑn /; / ˈrɛtˌkɑn / (AmE), / ˈrɛt.kɒn /; / ˈrɛtˌkɒn / (BrE)
to revise or reinterpret established facts or events in a fictional work, often to fit new storylines or resolve inconsistencies

Examples

To retcon a character
The author decided to retcon the villain's origin in the latest novel.
Fans were surprised when the show retconned a major character's death in the new season.
Sometimes writers retcon details to fix plot holes or update the story.
The producers decided to retcon the villain's origin to make the story more interesting.
Fans were surprised when the latest episode retconned a major event from the previous season.
Sometimes writers retcon details to fix plot holes or update characters for new audiences.

Related Terms

  • synonym: revise - to change or alter something already written or decided
  • synonym: rewrite - to write something again in a different way
