beet sugar

noun
sugar made from sugar beets
8.0
Pronunciation: / bit ˈʃuɡɚ /; / ˈbit ˌʃʌɡər / (AmE), / biːt ˈʃuːɡə /; / ˈbiːt ˌʃʊɡə / (BrE)
sugar made from sugar beets, used as a sweetener in foods and drinks

Examples

Beet sugar production
Many European countries produce beet sugar as an alternative to cane sugar.
She prefers using beet sugar in her baking recipes.
The factory converted locally grown beets into refined beet sugar.
Most of the beet sugar produced in Europe comes from locally grown sugar beets.
Some brands prominently display whether their sweetener contains beet sugar or cane sugar.
The company switched to using beet sugar due to fluctuations in cane sugar prices.
