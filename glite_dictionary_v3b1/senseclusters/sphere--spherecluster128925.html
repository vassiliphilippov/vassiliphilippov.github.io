sphere

noun
imaginary surface for celestial bodies
3.7 5.5
Pronunciation: /sfɪr/ /; / /sfɪər/ (AmE), /sfɪə/ (BrE)
the imaginary surface around the earth on which celestial bodies appear to be projected

Examples

Celestial sphere
Medieval astronomers believed the stars were fixed on a crystal sphere surrounding the Earth.
The concept of the celestial sphere helps astronomers describe the positions of stars and planets.
To the naked eye, the planets seem to move across the sphere of the night sky.
