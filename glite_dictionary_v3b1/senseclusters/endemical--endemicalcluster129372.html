endemical

adjective
constantly present (disease) in a region
4.5 7.1
Pronunciation: / ɛnˈdɛmɪkəl / (AmE), / ɛnˈdɛmɪkəl / (BrE)
(of a disease or condition) regularly found and consistently present in a particular place or among a particular group.

Examples

Endemical disease
Malaria is endemical in many tropical regions due to the prevalence of mosquitoes.
Some plant species are endemical to the island and found nowhere else on earth.
The poverty that remains endemical to certain rural areas is a major challenge for development.

Related Terms

  • synonym: endemic - regularly found in a particular area (disease)
  • antonym: epidemic - widespread occurrence of a disease at a particular time
  • antonym: pandemic - an epidemic occurring over a wide geographic area
