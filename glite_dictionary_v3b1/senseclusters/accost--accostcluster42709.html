accost

verb
approach and speak to someone boldly
4.8
Pronunciation: / əˈkɑst / (AmE), / əˈkɒst / (BrE)
to approach and speak to someone in a bold or aggressive way, sometimes with a proposition or offer

Examples

To accost a stranger
As she walked through the park, a stranger suddenly accosted her and asked for directions.
He felt uncomfortable when a group of salespeople accosted him in the shopping mall.
The journalist was accosted by several angry protesters outside the building.
Prostitutes would often accost people walking alone on that street.
She felt uncomfortable when a stranger attempted to accost her with unwanted comments.
The police arrested the man after he tried to accost several passersby late at night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: confront - to face someone, often in a challenging way
  • antonym: ignore - to deliberately not pay attention to someone
  • synonym: solicit - to approach someone with a request or offer, especially of a sexual nature
