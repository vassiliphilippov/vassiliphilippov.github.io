superior court

noun
a court above lower courts in the judicial system
6.7
Pronunciation: / səˈpɪriɚ kɔrt / (AmE), / suːˈpɪəriə kɔːt / (BrE)
A court that has authority to hear cases appealed from lower courts or that has general jurisdiction over certain types of cases.

Examples

The superior court
Many serious criminal matters are tried in the superior court.
The superior court judge ruled in favor of the defendant.
The case has been appealed to the superior court for further review.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inferior court - a court of limited jurisdiction below a superior court
