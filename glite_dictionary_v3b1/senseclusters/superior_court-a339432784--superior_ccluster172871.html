superior court

noun
a court above lower courts in the judicial system
5.3 2.49/m
Pronunciation: / s…ôÀàp…™ri…ö k…îrt / (AmE), / suÀêÀàp…™…ôri…ô k…îÀêt / (BrE)
A court that has authority to hear cases appealed from lower courts or that has general jurisdiction over certain types of cases.

Examples

The superior court
Many serious criminal matters are tried in the superior court.
The superior court judge ruled in favor of the defendant.
The case has been appealed to the superior court for further review.

Synonyms

  • appellate court
    7.4 1.35/m
    an appellate court reviews lower-court decisions and corrects errors, while a superior court may also handle trials and general cases.
  • court of appeals
    5.0 1.90/m
    a court of appeals reviews and may overturn lower decisions, while a superior court can also serve as a trial court with broad power.
  • appeals court
    4.4 2.49/m
    an appeals court focuses on reviewing lower-court rulings and legal errors, while a superior court may also try cases and manage general matters.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inferior court - a court of limited jurisdiction below a superior court
