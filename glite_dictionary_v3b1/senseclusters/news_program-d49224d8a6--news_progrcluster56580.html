news program

noun
broadcast covering current events
Pronunciation: / ˈnuz ˌproʊɡræm / (AmE), / ˈnjuːz ˌprəʊɡram / (BrE)
a television or radio broadcast devoted to reporting current events, often including interviews and commentary

Examples

Local news program
Many viewers rely on their local news program for updates on traffic and weather.
She appeared as a guest expert on a popular morning news program.
The evening news program provided in-depth analysis of the election results.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: talk show - a broadcast featuring interviews and discussions
