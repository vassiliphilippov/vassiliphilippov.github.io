hepatopancreas

noun
digestive gland in animals
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛpətoʊˈpæŋkriəs / (AmE), / ˌhɛpətəʊˈpaŋkrɪəs / (BrE)
a digestive gland in some invertebrates, such as crustaceans and mollusks, that performs the functions of both a liver and a pancreas

Examples

Shrimp hepatopancreas
The hepatopancreas of the shrimp is essential for breaking down its food.
Scientists studied the hepatopancreas to understand how crabs process nutrients.
In some mollusks, the hepatopancreas stores energy and helps with digestion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: liver - an organ that processes nutrients and detoxifies substances
  • synonym: pancreas - an organ that produces digestive enzymes and hormones
