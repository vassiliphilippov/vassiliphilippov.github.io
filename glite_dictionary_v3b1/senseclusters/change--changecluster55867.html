change

verb
make different; cause a transformation
Pronunciation: / tʃeɪndʒ / (AmE), / tʃeɪndʒ / (BrE)
to become different or to make someone or something different; to transform or alter

Examples

To change the rules
Our company plans to change its logo next year.
The experience changed her perspective on life forever.
You can change the settings at any time.
Over the years, her personality changed dramatically.
The seasons change from warm to cold as the year goes on.
Their friendship changed after they moved to different cities.
Her mood tends to change when the weather is gloomy.
Over time, your tastes and preferences may change.
The color of the sky can change dramatically throughout the day.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alter - to make a small change or modification to something
  • antonym: preserve - to keep something in its original state
  • antonym: remain - to continue unchanged, to stay the same
  • synonym: transform - to make a thorough or dramatic change in form, appearance, or character
  • synonym: vary - to become different; to change within limits
