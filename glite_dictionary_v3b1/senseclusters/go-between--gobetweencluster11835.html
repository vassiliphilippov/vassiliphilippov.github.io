go-between

noun
person who mediates between others
4.9 0.63/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…°o ä.b…™Àåtwin / (AmE), / Àà…°…ô ä.b…™ÀåtwiÀên / (BrE)
a person who acts as an intermediary or negotiator between two people or groups

Examples

A go-between person
As a go-between, he tried to resolve the conflict without taking sides.
During the peace talks, a trusted go-between conveyed messages between the rival leaders.
She acted as a go-between for the two companies negotiating the deal.

Synonyms

  • intermediary
    6.8 4.14/m
    an intermediary often works formally for organizations to connect parties, while a go-between is a more general person who links two groups.
  • mediator
    4.0 4.83/m
    a mediator helps people reach an agreement in disputes, while a go-between may just pass messages or requests between parties.
  • middleman
    3.9 0.87/m
    a middleman arranges deals and often earns money from trades, while a go-between simply connects people without that business role.
  • liaison
    6.8 5.12/m
    a liaison acts as a communication link between teams or agencies, while a go-between often works personally between two people or groups.
  • intercessor
    8.8 0.32/m
    an intercessor speaks or acts on someone else's behalf in personal or religious settings, while a go-between negotiates directly between parties.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mediator - a person who attempts to make people involved in a conflict come to an agreement
