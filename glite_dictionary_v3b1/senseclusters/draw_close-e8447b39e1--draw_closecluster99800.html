draw close

verb
pull someone/something toward oneself
Pronunciation: / drɔ kloʊs / (AmE), / drɔː kləʊs / (BrE)
to pull someone or something toward oneself, often in a gesture of comfort or protection

Examples

Draw close a coat
As the wind picked up, he drew close his coat around him for warmth.
He drew close his friend for a reassuring hug.
She gently drew close the frightened puppy to calm it down.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pull in - to move something inward toward oneself
  • antonym: push away - to move something away from oneself
