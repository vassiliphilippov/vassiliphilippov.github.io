telegraph line

noun
wire for carrying telegraph signals
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛləˌɡræf laɪn / (AmE), / ˈtɛlɪɡrɑːf laɪn / (BrE)
a wire or set of wires used to carry telegraph and telephone signals over distances

Examples

The telegraph line wire
During the storm, the telegraph line was damaged, cutting off all communication with the next town.
Engineers inspected every mile of the telegraph line for faults after the unexpected outage.
The old telegraph line still runs alongside the railway track, though it is no longer in use.
