quelea

noun
African weaver bird
Pronunciation: / kwəˈliːə / (AmE), / kwɪˈliːə / (BrE)
a small African weaver bird that forms very large flocks and is known for its impact on crops

Examples

A quelea flock
A huge flock of quelea swept across the savanna, searching for seeds.
Farmers worry when quelea arrive, as the birds can damage their crops.
The quelea is often called the most numerous wild bird in the world.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sparrow - a small, common bird often found in flocks
  • synonym: weaverbird - a small bird known for weaving elaborate nests
