Labrador-Ungava Peninsula

noun
peninsula in eastern Canada
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˌlæbrəˌdɔr ʌnˈɡɑːvə pəˈnɪnsjələ / (AmE), / ˌlæbrədɔː ʌnˈɡɑːvə pəˈnɪnsjələ / (BrE)
a large peninsular region in eastern Canada, situated between Hudson Bay and the Labrador Sea, comprising most of Quebec and the mainland part of Newfoundland and Labrador

Examples

The Labrador-Ungava Peninsula region
Many indigenous communities have lived on the Labrador-Ungava Peninsula for thousands of years.

The vast Labrador-Ungava Peninsula is known for its forests, tundra, and numerous lakes.
