trauma

noun
emotional or psychological injury
4.8 15/m
Pronunciation: / Ààtr…ëÀêm…ô, Ààtra äm…ô / (AmE), / Ààtr…îÀêm…ô, Ààtra äm…ô / (BrE)
A deeply distressing or disturbing experience that causes emotional or psychological injury and may have lasting effects.

Examples

Childhood trauma
Childhood trauma can affect a person's mental health for decades.
She spoke about the trauma of losing her home in the fire.
The accident left him with lasting trauma that required years of therapy.

Synonyms

  • psychic trauma
    7.9 0.04/m
    psychic trauma is a clinical term for deep lasting psychological harm, while trauma is the general word for such distress.
  • emotional wound
    6.3 0.15/m
    emotional wound describes a deep personal pain from upsetting events, while trauma refers to a broader psychological injury that may last.
  • emotional scar
    5.0 0.17/m
    emotional scar shows the lasting mark left by hard experiences, while trauma means the original deeply distressing event or injury.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shock - a sudden upsetting experience or disturbance
