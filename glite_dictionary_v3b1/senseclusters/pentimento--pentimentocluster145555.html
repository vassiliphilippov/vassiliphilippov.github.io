pentimento

noun
underlying image in painting showing through
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛn.tɪˈmɛn.toʊ / (AmE), / ˌpɛntɪˈmɛntəʊ / (BrE)
A visible trace in a painting of an underlying image or drawing that has been painted over, often becoming apparent as the upper layer becomes transparent with age.

Examples

A pentimento in painting
Art historians identified a pentimento in the portrait, revealing an earlier version of the subject's face.
The presence of a pentimento suggests that the artist changed the composition during the creative process.
Under infrared light, a previously hidden pentimento became visible beneath the painting's surface.
