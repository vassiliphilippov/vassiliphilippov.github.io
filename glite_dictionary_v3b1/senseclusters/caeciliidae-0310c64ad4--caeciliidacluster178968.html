Caeciliidae

noun
family of legless burrowing amphibians
9.9
Pronunciation: / siːˌsɪliˈaɪdiː / (AmE), / siːˌsɪliˈaɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, legless, burrowing amphibians within the order Gymnophiona, distinguished by their elongated bodies and reduced or absent limbs.

Examples

Caeciliidae family amphibians
Members of the Caeciliidae family are found primarily in tropical regions.
Researchers studied the Caeciliidae to understand the evolution of limbless amphibians.
The Caeciliidae are known for their elongated, worm-like appearance and subterranean lifestyle.
