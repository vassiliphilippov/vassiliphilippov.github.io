remigrate

verb
to migrate again
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈmaɪɡreɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈmaɪɡreɪt / (BrE)
to migrate again, either by returning to a place you left before or by moving to a new place after having already migrated

Examples

To remigrate home
After several years abroad, the family decided to remigrate to their homeland.
Many birds remigrate to the same nesting grounds each year.
The policy encouraged refugees to remigrate once their villages were rebuilt.
After living in Canada for several years, she chose to remigrate to Australia for work.
Many families remigrate when economic conditions change in their new country.
He decided to remigrate after finding better opportunities abroad.
Every autumn, the geese remigrate to the southern wetlands for the winter.
After several years abroad, she decided to remigrate to her hometown.
Some fish species remigrate upstream to spawn where they were born.

Related Terms

  • antonym: emigrate - to leave one's country or region to live elsewhere
  • synonym: migrate - to move from one country or region to another
  • synonym: return - to go back to a place where you were before
  • synonym: settle - to move to a new place and make it your home
