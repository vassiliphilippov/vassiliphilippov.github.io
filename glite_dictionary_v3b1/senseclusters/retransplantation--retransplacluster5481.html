retransplantation

noun
plant moved again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːtrænˌsplænˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːtrænˌsplɑːnˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of transplanting a plant or seedling again into a new pot or location to improve growth or spacing

Examples

Plant retransplantation
The gardener recommended retransplantation to give the young trees more space to grow.
After the first attempt failed, retransplantation was necessary to save the delicate seedlings.
Proper retransplantation can help plants recover from overcrowding and thrive in their new environment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: transplantation - the act of moving a plant to a new place
