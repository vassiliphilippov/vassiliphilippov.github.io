slave state

noun
U.S. state where slavery was legal before the Civil War
3.2
Pronunciation: / sleɪv steɪt / (AmE), / sleɪv steɪt / (BrE)
any of the southern states of the United States in which slavery was legal before the American Civil War

Examples

A slave state before war
Missouri was admitted to the Union as a slave state under the Missouri Compromise.
Tensions between slave states and free states helped lead to the Civil War.
The status of new territories as either a slave state or a free state was hotly debated in Congress.
