Bosnia-Herzegovina

proper noun
country in southeastern Europe
Pronunciation: / ˌbɑːzniə hɜrtsəgoʊˈviːnə / (AmE), / ˌbɒzniə hɜːtsəgəˈviːnə / (BrE)
A country in southeastern Europe, known officially as Bosnia and Herzegovina, situated on the Balkan Peninsula; formerly part of Yugoslavia, it became independent in 1992.

Examples

Bosnia-Herzegovina country
Several athletes from Bosnia-Herzegovina competed in the Olympic Games this year.

The war in Bosnia-Herzegovina in the 1990s had a profound impact on the region.
