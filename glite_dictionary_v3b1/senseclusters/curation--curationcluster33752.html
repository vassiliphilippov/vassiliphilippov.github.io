curation

noun
selecting and organizing
Pronunciation: / kjʊˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / kjʊəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of carefully selecting, organizing, and managing items or content for a collection or presentation

Examples

The curation of art
The curation of the art gallery's new collection attracted many visitors.
Digital curation is important for organizing information on websites and social media.
She was responsible for the curation of rare books in the university library.
The curation of rare manuscripts requires both expertise and attention to detail.
Digital curation has become important as more museums share their collections online.
She is responsible for the curation of the new art exhibit opening next month.
Effective curation of digital archives helps preserve important historical documents for future generations.
The museum's curation team carefully selects and displays new artworks each season.
Data curation is essential to ensure that research findings remain accurate and accessible over time.

  • synonym: curatorship - the position or duties of a curator
  • synonym: management - the act of controlling or organizing something
  • antonym: neglect - the failure to care for something properly
  • synonym: organization - the act of arranging or structuring things
  • synonym: selection - the process of choosing items from a group
