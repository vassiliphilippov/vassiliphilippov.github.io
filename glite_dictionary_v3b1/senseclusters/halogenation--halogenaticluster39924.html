halogenation

noun
adding halogen element
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˌhælədʒəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌhælədʒɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of adding a halogen element, such as chlorine or bromine, to a chemical compound

Examples

The halogenation reaction
The halogenation of organic compounds is an important reaction in industrial chemistry.
Scientists studied the halogenation process to improve the properties of the material.
Bromine is often used in the halogenation of certain pharmaceuticals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chlorination - the process of adding chlorine to a substance
  • antonym: hydrogenation - the process of adding hydrogen to a compound
