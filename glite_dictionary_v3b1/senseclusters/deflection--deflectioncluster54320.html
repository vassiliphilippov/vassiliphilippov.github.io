deflection

noun
aberration or deviation in behavior or judgment
7.2
Pronunciation: / dɪˈflɛk.ʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈflɛk.ʃən / (BrE)
a deviation or abnormal change, especially a perverse or distorted way of judging or acting

Examples

Critics argued that the judge's deflection of logic was influenced by personal bias.
Her comments revealed a subtle deflection from the accepted standards of fairness.
The psychologist noted a persistent deflection in the patient's moral reasoning.
