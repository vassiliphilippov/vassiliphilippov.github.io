privateer

noun
person authorized to attack enemy ships
Pronunciation: / ˌpraɪvəˈtɪr / (AmE), / ˌpraɪvəˈtɪə / (BrE)
a person, especially a sailor or officer, authorized by a government to attack foreign shipping during wartime; an officer or crew member of a privateer ship

Examples

A famous privateer
Many sailors found fortune and fame as successful privateers during the war.
She read tales of the daring privateer who once raided Spanish galleons.
The notorious privateer sailed under the sanction of the crown.

Related Terms

  • antonym: pirate - a person who attacks and robs ships at sea
