indirect lighting

noun
concealed, diffused lighting method
2.4 3.6
Pronunciation: /ɪn.də.ˈrekt ˈlaɪ.tɪŋ/ /; / /ɪn.dɪ.ˈrekt ˈlaɪ.tɪŋ/ (AmE), /ɪn.dɪ.ˈrekt ˈlaɪ.tɪŋ/ /; / /ɪn.də.ˈrekt ˈlaɪ.tɪŋ/ (BrE)
a method of lighting in which the light source is hidden or shielded so that light is reflected off ceilings or walls, creating a soft, diffused effect

Examples

Indirect lighting effect
Architects often recommend indirect lighting to avoid harsh glare in workspaces.
She installed new indirect lighting fixtures behind the panels to modernize the office.
The living room uses indirect lighting to create a soft and welcoming atmosphere.
