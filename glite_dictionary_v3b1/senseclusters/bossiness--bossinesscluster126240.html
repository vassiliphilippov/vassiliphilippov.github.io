bossiness

noun
controlling behavior
4.3 0.14/m
Pronunciation: / Ààb…ëÀêsin…ôs / (AmE), / Ààb…ísin…ôs / (BrE)
the quality of always telling people what to do in a controlling or domineering way

Examples

Her bossiness behavior
Her bossiness made it difficult for the team to work together smoothly.
He apologized for his bossiness after realizing he was being too controlling.
Some people appreciate a little bossiness when clear direction is needed.

Synonyms

  • domineeringness
    9.2 0.00/m
    domineeringness pushes people around with forceful orders and control, while bossiness is a common, often informal habit of telling people what to do.
  • overbearingness
    8.3 0.00/m
    overbearingness treats others in an intrusive, controlling way, while bossiness is a simpler habit of telling people what to do.
  • imperiousness
    9.3 0.04/m
    imperiousness speaks and acts like a proud ruler, giving commands, while bossiness is more everyday telling people what to do.
  • high-handedness
    8.4 0.06/m
    high-handedness uses power in a rude or unfair way toward others, while bossiness is simply telling people what to do.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authoritarianism - enforcing strict obedience to authority
  • synonym: domineering - asserting control over others in an overbearing way
  • antonym: meekness - the quality of being quiet, gentle, and unwilling to argue or express opinions
