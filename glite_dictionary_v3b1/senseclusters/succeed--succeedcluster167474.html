succeed

verb
take over after someone (e.g., in office, title)
4.4 10/m
Pronunciation: / s…ôkÀàsiÀêd / (AmE), / s…ôkÀàsiÀêd / (BrE)
to take over a position, office, or title after someone else, especially by legal right or order of occurrence

Examples

To succeed a leader
After the CEO's retirement, his son will succeed him as head of the company.
Prince William is expected to succeed his father as king one day.
When the mayor resigned, her deputy was chosen to succeed her.

Synonyms

  • come after
    3.6 1.23/m
    come after means to follow in position or order, while succeed implies officially taking over someone's role by right.
  • accede
    8.4 0.07/m
    accede is a formal word for assuming a throne or office, while succeed is a general term for taking over a position.
  • take office
    3.6 5.99/m
    take office means to begin serving in a post after election or appointment, while succeed focuses on becoming the rightful successor.
  • follow
    1.0 266/m
    follow simply means to come after in time or order, while succeed specifically means to take over someone's official role.

Related Terms

  • antonym: precede - to come before in position or time
