take-away

adjective
food to be eaten off the premises
Pronunciation: / ˈteɪkəˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈteɪkəˌweɪ / (BrE)
(of food) prepared to be eaten off the premises or taken out for consumption elsewhere.

Examples

Take-away food order
She placed an order at the take-away counter before heading home.
The café offers both dine-in and take-away options for customers.
We decided to get some take-away fish and chips for dinner.

Related Terms

  • synonym: takeout - food prepared to be eaten off the premises (chiefly American)
