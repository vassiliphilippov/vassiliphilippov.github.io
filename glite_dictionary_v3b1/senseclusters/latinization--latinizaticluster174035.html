latinization

noun
making Latin in form
8.5
Pronunciation: /læt.ɪ.nə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /lə.tɪ.nə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /læt.ɪ.nə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /lə.tɪ.nə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of changing a name or word into a Latin form or spelling

Examples

Latinization of a name
The latinization of scientific names helps standardize species identification worldwide.
During the Renaissance, scholars often used the latinization of their surnames in published works.
The latinization of place names was common in medieval European maps.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anglicization - the process of making something English in form or character
  • synonym: romanization - the process of writing a language using the Roman alphabet
