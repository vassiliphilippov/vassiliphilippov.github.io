logorrhea

noun
excessive, often incoherent talking
Pronunciation: / ˌlɑːɡəˈriːə / (AmE), / ˌlɒɡəˈriːə / (BrE)
Excessive and uncontrollable flow of words; extreme talkativeness, often with incoherent or meaningless speech.

Examples

Severe logorrhea
During his manic episode, his logorrhea made it difficult for anyone to follow his train of thought.
Her logorrhea was so pronounced that conversations with her felt overwhelming.
The professor's logorrhea caused the lecture to run well over time, exhausting the students.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reticence - the state of being reserved or reluctant to speak
  • synonym: verbosity - the quality of using more words than needed
