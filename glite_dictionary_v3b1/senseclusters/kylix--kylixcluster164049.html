kylix

noun
ancient Greek two-handled drinking cup
Pronunciation: / ˈkaɪlɪks / (AmE), / ˈkɪlɪks / (BrE)
A shallow, two-handled drinking cup used in ancient Greece, often for wine.

Examples

An ancient kylix cup
During the symposium, guests drank wine from a kylix as part of the ritual.
The broken kylix was carefully restored by experts in ancient ceramics.
The museum displayed a beautifully decorated kylix from the fifth century BCE.
