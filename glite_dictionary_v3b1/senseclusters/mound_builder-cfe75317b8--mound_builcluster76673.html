Mound Builder

noun
prehistoric Native American mound-building peoples
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmaʊnd ˌbɪldər / (AmE), / ˈmaʊnd ˌbɪldə / (BrE)
a member of a group of prehistoric Native American peoples known for constructing large earthen mounds for religious, ceremonial, or burial purposes in North America.

Examples

Mound Builder peoples
Archaeologists have uncovered several Mound Builder sites along the Mississippi River.
Artifacts from a Mound Builder burial mound reveal intricate craftsmanship and spiritual beliefs.
The Mound Builder cultures flourished in North America long before European contact.
