forced sale

noun
court-ordered sale to settle debts
6.1
Pronunciation: / fɔrst seɪl / (AmE), / fɔːst seɪl / (BrE)
a sale of property, often ordered by a court, to satisfy an unpaid debt or obligation

Examples

A forced sale of property
After defaulting on the loan, the judge authorized a forced sale of the debtor's property.
He hoped to avoid a forced sale by negotiating a payment plan with his creditors.
The bank initiated a forced sale to recover the outstanding mortgage balance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: foreclosure - the legal process of taking possession of mortgaged property when the mortgagor defaults
