biliteral

adjective
involving two letters
9.9
Pronunciation: / baɪˈlɪtərəl / (AmE), / baɪˈlɪtərəl / (BrE)
consisting of or involving exactly two letters, especially in reference to roots in certain languages

Examples

Biliteral root
In some ancient scripts, a biliteral sign represents a sound made by two letters.
The scholar explained that a biliteral root is common in certain Semitic languages.
Linguists often compare biliteral and triliteral roots when studying word formation.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: triliteral - consisting of three letters, especially in language roots
  • degree lower: uniliteral - consisting of one letter
