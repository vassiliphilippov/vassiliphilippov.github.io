purism

noun
strict insistence on purity or correctness
5.6 7.3
Pronunciation: /ˈpjʊr.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈpjuːr.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈpjʊə.rɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈpjuː.rɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the strict or excessive insistence on maintaining purity, correctness, or traditional forms, especially in language or style

Examples

Linguistic purism
Linguistic purism can sometimes hinder the natural evolution of language.
Many critics accused the artist of excessive purism in his refusal to experiment with new styles.
The committee's purism led them to reject any neologisms in official documents.

Related Terms

  • antonym: liberalism - the belief in more flexible standards or open attitudes, often contrasted with purism
  • synonym: prescriptivism - the belief that norms and prescriptions should govern language use
