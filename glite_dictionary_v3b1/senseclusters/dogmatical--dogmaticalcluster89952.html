dogmatical

adjective
asserting unproven principles as absolute truth
6.7
Pronunciation: / dɔɡˈmætɪkəl / (AmE), / dɒɡˈmætɪkəl / (BrE)
Asserting opinions or principles as unquestionably true, even if not proven or provable.

Examples

A dogmatical opinion
His dogmatical approach to the subject left no room for open discussion.
She was criticized for her dogmatical insistence on following traditional methods.
The speaker's dogmatical tone discouraged questions from the audience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dogmatic - inclined to lay down principles as incontrovertibly true
