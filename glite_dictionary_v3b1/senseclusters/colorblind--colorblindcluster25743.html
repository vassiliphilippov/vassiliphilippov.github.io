colorblind

adjective
ignoring racial differences
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌlərˌblaɪnd / (AmE), / ˈkʌləˌblaɪnd / (BrE)
not considering or giving importance to differences of race or ethnicity; treating people equally regardless of race

Examples

Colorblind policy
The company adopted a colorblind policy to ensure fairness in all hiring decisions.
Many people believe that being colorblind is the best way to promote equality in society.
Her colorblind attitude meant she treated everyone with the same respect, regardless of their background.

Related Terms

  • antonym: biased - showing unfair preference for or against certain people or groups
  • synonym: impartial - treating all people and groups equally
