stratum

noun
layer of material or tissue
Pronunciation: / ˈstreɪtəm / (AmE), / ˈstrɑːtəm / (BrE)
A single layer of material or substance, especially one of several layers lying one on top of another, such as in rock, soil, or biological tissue.

Examples

Rock stratum
Each stratum of the atmosphere has unique properties and functions.
In the heart, a stratum of muscle fibers contracts to pump blood efficiently.
The fossils were found in a stratum of limestone dating back millions of years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: layer - a sheet, quantity, or thickness of material, typically one of several, covering a surface or body
  • synonym: strata - plural form meaning layers
