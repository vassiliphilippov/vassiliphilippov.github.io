law of effect

noun
principle that behavior is shaped by consequences
6.0
Pronunciation: / lɔ əv ɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / lɔː əv ɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
(psychology) the principle that behaviors followed by satisfying outcomes are more likely to be repeated, while those followed by unpleasant outcomes are less likely to be repeated.

Examples

The law of effect principle
Teachers often use the law of effect when reinforcing good study habits in students.
The psychologist explained the law of effect to demonstrate how rewards can shape behavior.
Understanding the law of effect helps animal trainers encourage desired behaviors in their subjects.

Related Terms

  • antonym: punishment - process of discouraging a pattern of behavior
  • synonym: reinforcement - process of encouraging a pattern of behavior
