beardless iris

noun
type of iris without hairs on sepals
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪrləss ˈaɪrɪs / (AmE), / ˈbɪələs ˈaɪərɪs / (BrE)
Any of various species or varieties of iris plants that have no hairs on the drooping sepals (the falls).

Examples

Wild beardless iris
Many wild species of beardless iris grow naturally along riverbanks and wetlands.
The beardless iris is prized in gardens for its elegant, smooth petals.
Unlike the bearded variety, the beardless iris does not have fuzzy hairs on its falls.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bearded iris - iris with soft hairs on the drooping sepals
