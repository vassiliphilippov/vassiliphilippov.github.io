information system

noun
organizational network for managing information
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.fərˈmeɪ.ʃən ˈsɪs.təm / (AmE), / ˌɪn.fəˈmeɪ.ʃən ˈsɪs.təm / (BrE)
A system comprising the network of communication channels and resources used for collecting, storing, and distributing information within an organization.

Examples

An information system network
An effective information system can greatly enhance decision-making in an organization.
Most hospitals now use advanced information systems to manage patient records and appointments.
The company invested heavily in a new information system to improve communication between departments.
