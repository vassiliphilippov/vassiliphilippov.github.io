teaberry

noun
spicy red berry; source of wintergreen oil
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtiˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈtiːˌbɛri / (BrE)
a low-growing North American shrub with aromatic leaves and spicy red berries, or the fruit itself, often used as a source of wintergreen oil

Examples

Teaberry plant
Teaberry oil is often used to add a wintergreen scent to candies and gum.
In the cool woods, the carpet of teaberry plants produced both flowers and berries.
The children picked teaberries in the forest and tasted their spicy flavor.
She chewed a teaberry for its distinctive, minty flavor while hiking.
The oil extracted from teaberry leaves is used to make wintergreen flavoring.
The woods were carpeted with teaberry plants and their glossy green leaves.
