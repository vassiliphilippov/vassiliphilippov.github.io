nitrophilous

adjective
preferring nitrogen-rich soil
9.9
Pronunciation: / naɪˈtrɑːfɪləs / (AmE), / naɪˈtrɒfɪləs / (BrE)
thriving in or preferring soils or environments rich in nitrogen

Examples

nitrophilous plants
Many weeds are nitrophilous and grow well in fertilized fields.
The nitrophilous plants quickly colonized the area after the application of nitrogen fertilizer.
Gardeners often notice that nitrophilous species dominate compost-rich garden beds.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calcifuge - a plant that avoids or does not thrive in calcium-rich soils
  • synonym: nitrophilic - thriving in nitrogen-rich environments
