bluefin tuna

noun
flesh of a large tuna used as food
Pronunciation: / ˈbluːfɪn ˈtuːnə / (AmE), / ˈbluːfɪn ˈtjuːnə / (BrE)
the flesh of a very large species of tuna, prized as food, especially in sushi and sashimi

Examples

Bluefin tuna sushi
Due to overfishing, bluefin tuna is considered a delicacy and commands high prices.
She ordered a salad topped with seared bluefin tuna and a tangy vinaigrette.
The restaurant is famous for serving the freshest bluefin tuna in its sushi rolls.
