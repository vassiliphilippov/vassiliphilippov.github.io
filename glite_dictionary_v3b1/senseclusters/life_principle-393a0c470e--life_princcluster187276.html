life principle

noun
supposed vital force in living things
Pronunciation: / laɪf ˈprɪnsəpəl / (AmE), / laɪf ˈprɪnsɪpl / (BrE)
a supposed vital force or agency credited with causing the distinctive functions and qualities of living things

Examples

The life principle force
Many ancient cultures described the soul as a life principle animating the body.
Modern science generally rejects the notion of a distinct life principle in explaining biological processes.
Some philosophers once believed that a life principle separated living beings from inanimate objects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: vital force - a force that is thought to be responsible for the functions of living organisms
