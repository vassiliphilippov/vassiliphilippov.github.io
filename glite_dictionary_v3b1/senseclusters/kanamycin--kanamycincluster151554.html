kanamycin

noun
antibiotic for severe bacterial infections
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˌkæn.əˈmaɪ.sɪn / (AmE), / ˌkæn.əˈmaɪ.sɪn / (BrE)
an antibiotic used to treat severe bacterial infections, especially when other antibiotics are ineffective

Examples

Kanamycin antibiotic treatment
Prolonged use of kanamycin can lead to kidney damage, so monitoring is necessary.
Some bacteria have developed resistance to kanamycin, making treatment more challenging.
The patient was prescribed kanamycin to combat a resistant bacterial infection.
