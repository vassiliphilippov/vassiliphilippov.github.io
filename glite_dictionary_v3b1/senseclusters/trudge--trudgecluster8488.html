trudge

verb
walk slowly and heavily, usually when tired
6.5 4.4
Pronunciation: / trʌdʒ / (AmE), / trʌdʒ / (BrE)
to walk slowly and with heavy steps, typically because of tiredness or harsh conditions

Examples

To trudge through mud
After hours of hiking, we had to trudge through thick, wet mud to reach the campsite.
Exhausted, she trudged home in the pouring rain after her late shift.
They were trudging along the snow-covered path, barely able to lift their tired feet.
