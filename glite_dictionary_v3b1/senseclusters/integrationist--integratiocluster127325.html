integrationist

noun
proponent of Integrationism
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntəˈɡreɪʃənɪst / (AmE), / ˌɪntɪˈɡreɪʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports or follows Integrationism, the view that language arises from social interaction and context

Examples

Integrationist theory
The integrationist approach challenges traditional views of language structure.
As an integrationist, she believes that meaning is shaped by social context.
Many modern linguists identify as integrationists in their research.

Related Terms

  • synonym: integrationism - a linguistic theory emphasizing language as social interaction
  • antonym: structuralist - a person who supports structuralism in linguistics
