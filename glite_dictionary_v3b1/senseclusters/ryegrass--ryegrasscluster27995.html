ryegrass

noun
type of grass; Lolium genus
8.6 4.0
Pronunciation: /ˈraɪ.græs/ (AmE), /ˈraɪ.grɑːs/ /; / /ˈraɪ.græs/ (BrE)
A grass of any of several species of the genus Lolium, native to Eurasia and widely cultivated for use as pasture, hay, or lawns.

Examples

Green ryegrass field
Farmers prefer planting ryegrass because it grows quickly and provides excellent grazing.
Golf courses often use ryegrass for its lush, green appearance.
The field was sown with ryegrass to improve the quality of the pasture.
