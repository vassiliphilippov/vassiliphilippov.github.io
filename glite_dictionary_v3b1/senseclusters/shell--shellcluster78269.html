shell

noun
artillery ammunition with explosive charge
4.4
Pronunciation: / ʃɛl / (AmE), / ʃɛl / (BrE)
A cylindrical metal container filled with an explosive charge and a projectile, fired from a large gun such as a cannon or tank.

Examples

A tank shell
A single shell can cause extensive damage to buildings and vehicles.
The tank fired a shell that exploded near the bridge.
Workers were clearing unexploded shells from the battlefield.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cartridge - ammunition consisting of a casing, primer, powder, and bullet, used in small arms
