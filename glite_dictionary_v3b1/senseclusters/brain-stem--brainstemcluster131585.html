brain-stem

noun
central brain part linking to spinal cord
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈbreɪn ˌstɛm / (AmE), / ˈbreɪn ˌstɛm / (BrE)
the central part of the brain connecting to the spinal cord, comprising the medulla oblongata, pons, midbrain, and adjoining structures

Examples

The brain-stem area
After the accident, doctors found damage to his brain-stem.
Injuries to the brain-stem can have life-threatening consequences.
The brain-stem controls essential functions like breathing and heart rate.
