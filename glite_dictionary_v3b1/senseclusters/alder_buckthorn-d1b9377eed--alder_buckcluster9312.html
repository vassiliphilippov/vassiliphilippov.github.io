alder buckthorn

noun
small European tree with black berries
Pronunciation: / ˈɔldɚ ˈbʌkˌθɑn / (AmE), / ˈɔːldə ˈbʌkˌθɔn / (BrE)
a small European tree (Frangula alnus) with glossy leaves, small greenish flowers, and black berries, often found in wet places

Examples

The alder buckthorn tree
Berries from the alder buckthorn are sometimes used for dyeing fabrics.
Conservationists planted several alder buckthorn trees to attract butterflies to the area.
The alder buckthorn grows well in damp woodlands and along riverbanks.
