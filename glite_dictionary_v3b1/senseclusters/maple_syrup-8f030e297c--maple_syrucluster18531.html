maple syrup

noun
sweet syrup from sugar maple sap
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪpəl ˌsɪrəp /; / ˈmeɪpəl ˈsɪrəp / (AmE), / ˈmeɪpəl ˌsɪrəp /; / ˈmeɪpəl ˈsɪrəp / (BrE)
a sweet, thick syrup made from the sap of maple trees, often used as a topping for pancakes, waffles, and other foods

Examples

Pure maple syrup
Authentic maple syrup is produced in Canada and parts of the United States.
She poured maple syrup over her pancakes for breakfast.
The recipe calls for two tablespoons of maple syrup to sweeten the glaze.
Canada exports large quantities of maple syrup every year.
She prefers maple syrup to honey for sweetening her oatmeal.
The pancakes were delicious with a generous pour of maple syrup on top.

  • synonym: molasses - a thick, dark syrup produced from sugar cane or sugar beet
