doldrum

noun
calm equatorial sea
9.2
Pronunciation: /ˈdoʊl.drəm/ (AmE), /ˈdɒl.drəm/ /; / /ˈdəʊl.drəm/ (BrE)
a calm area of the ocean near the equator where winds are very light or changeable, often causing sailing ships to stop moving

Examples

The doldrum at sea
The ship was stuck in the doldrum for days, unable to make any progress.
Sailors dreaded entering the doldrum because it could delay their journey for weeks.
After crossing the doldrum, the crew finally felt a steady breeze again.

Related Terms

  • synonym: calm - a period or area with little or no wind at sea
