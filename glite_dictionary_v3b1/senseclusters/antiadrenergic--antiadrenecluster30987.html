antiadrenergic

adjective
blocking adrenergic effects (medical)
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.ti.æˈdrɛn.ər.dʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌænti.əˈdrɛn.ər.dʒɪk / (BrE)
(of a drug or mechanism) blocking or reducing the effects of adrenaline or noradrenaline in the body, especially within the nervous system or cardiovascular system.

Examples

Antiadrenergic drug effects
Researchers are studying how antiadrenergic drugs affect anxiety disorders.
Some antihypertensive medicines have antiadrenergic properties that reduce heart rate.
The doctor prescribed an antiadrenergic medication to help lower his blood pressure.

Related Terms

  • antonym: adrenergic - relating to adrenaline or noradrenaline effects in the body
