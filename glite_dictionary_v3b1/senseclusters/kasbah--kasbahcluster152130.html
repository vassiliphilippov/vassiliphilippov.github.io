kasbah

noun
old quarter or citadel in North African cities
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑz.bɑ / (AmE), / ˈkæz.bɑː / (BrE)
the old, fortified quarter or citadel in many North African cities, traditionally serving as a place of defense and administration

Examples

The kasbah quarter
Many tourists enjoyed wandering the narrow alleys of the kasbah in Tangier, admiring its traditional architecture.
The ancient kasbah stood at the highest point of the city, overlooking the bustling marketplace below.
The movie was filmed on location in a Moroccan kasbah, capturing its unique atmosphere and historic walls.
