impression

noun
mark made by pressing
1.0 3.3
Pronunciation: /ɪm.ˈpreʃ.ən/ (AmE), /ɪm.ˈpreʃ.ən/ (BrE)
a mark, imprint, or indentation made by pressing something into a surface (including in printing or dentistry)

Examples

Impression in clay
He examined the impression of the ancient coin in the clay.
The horse's hoof left a deep impression in the muddy ground.
You could still see the impression of her hand on the soft sand.
Each letter is printed with a precise impression on the page.
The coin bore the impression of a famous ruler's profile.
The official seal left a clear impression on the certificate.
Accurate impressions are essential for creating well-fitting dental appliances.
Once the impression had set, the technician used it to produce a custom mouthguard.
The dentist made an impression of her teeth before fitting the crown.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dent - a slight hollow in a surface made by pressure or a blow
  • synonym: imprint - a mark or impression made by pressure
  • synonym: print - a mark or character made by pressing or printing
  • synonym: stamp - an instrument for impressing a mark or design
