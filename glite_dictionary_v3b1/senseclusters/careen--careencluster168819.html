careen

verb
move swiftly and unsteadily
7.7 1.89/m
Pronunciation: / k…ôÀàrin / (AmE), / k…ôÀàriÀên / (BrE)
to move quickly and in an uncontrolled or unsteady way, often from side to side

Examples

To careen wildly
He tried to steady himself, but ended up careening into the crowd at the busy station.
The car began to careen dangerously down the icy road after hitting a patch of black ice.
The toddler let go of the railing and careened across the living room, giggling as she went.
The bicycle careened across the road after hitting a pothole.
The children cheered as the roller coaster careened around the sharp curve.
The truck began to careen down the icy slope, narrowly missing the trees.

Synonyms

  • lurch
    5.6 4.74/m
    lurch is a sudden, unsteady movement with abrupt jerks, while careen is a fast, uncontrolled tilt or sway from side to side.
  • reel
    4.6 1.67/m
    reel is to move unsteadily as if dizzy or about to fall, while careen is a faster uncontrolled sideways motion.
  • stagger
    4.0 6.84/m
    stagger is to walk unsteadily often from tiredness or imbalance, while careen is a quick uncontrolled sway, usually sideways.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lurch - to make an abrupt, unsteady, uncontrolled movement
  • synonym: stagger - to walk or move unsteadily, as if about to fall
  • synonym: swerve - to change direction abruptly
  • synonym: veer - to change direction suddenly or turn aside
