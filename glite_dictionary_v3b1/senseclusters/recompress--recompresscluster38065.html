recompress

verb
to increase pressure again
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkəmˈprɛs / (AmE), / ˌriːkəmˈprɛs / (BrE)
to subject a person or animal to increased pressure again, especially in a hyperbaric chamber as medical treatment for decompression sickness

Examples

To recompress a patient
The doctor decided to recompress the patient after signs of decompression sickness appeared.
Divers may need to be recompressed in a chamber if they surface too quickly.
Immediate recompression is often necessary to treat severe cases of the bends.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decompress - to reduce pressure, especially in a medical or diving context
