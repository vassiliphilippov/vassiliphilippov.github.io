coir

noun
fiber from coconut husk
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔɪr / (AmE), / ˈkɔɪə / (BrE)
a stiff, coarse fiber obtained from the outer husk of a coconut, used for making rope, matting, and brushes

Examples

Natural coir fiber
Gardeners often use coir as a natural alternative to peat moss for seed starting.
Ropes made from coir are strong and resistant to salt water, making them ideal for use on boats.
The doormat was made of woven coir, giving it a rough texture.

  • synonym: fiber - a thread or filament from which a vegetable tissue, mineral substance, or textile is formed
