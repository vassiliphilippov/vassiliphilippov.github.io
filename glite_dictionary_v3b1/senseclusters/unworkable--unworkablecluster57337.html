unworkable

adjective
not able to be put into practice
5.1 1.12/m
Pronunciation: /  ånÀàw…úrk…ôb…ôl / (AmE), /  ånÀàw…úÀêk…ôb…ôl / (BrE)
not able to be put into effect or carried out successfully

Examples

An unworkable plan
His plan sounded good in theory but turned out to be unworkable in practice.
Many critics argue that the new guidelines are simply unworkable in a real-world classroom.
The committee rejected the proposal as unworkable given the current budget constraints.

Synonyms

  • impracticable
    7.3 0.41/m
    impracticable is a formal word meaning impossible or very hard to carry out, while unworkable sounds more everyday and practical.
  • infeasible
    7.2 0.25/m
    infeasible describes something not possible to do, often in technical or formal settings, while unworkable feels more practical and everyday.
  • unfeasible
    6.8 0.29/m
    unfeasible means not possible to do or achieve and is slightly more formal, while unworkable suggests a plan fails in practice.

Related Terms

  • antonym: feasible - possible and practical to do
  • synonym: impracticable - not able to be carried out in practice
