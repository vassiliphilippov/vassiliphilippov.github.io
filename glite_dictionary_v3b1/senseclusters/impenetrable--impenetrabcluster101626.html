impenetrable

adjective
impossible to pass through or enter
3.7
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɛnɪtrəbəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɛnɪtrəbl / (BrE)
impossible to pass through or enter; not allowing access, passage, or penetration.

Examples

Impenetrable wall
Security forces guarded the facility, making it virtually impenetrable to intruders.
The ancient castle sits atop a hill, surrounded by impenetrable walls.
The thick fog this morning was so impenetrable that I could barely see a few feet ahead.

Related Terms

  • antonym: penetrable - able to be entered or passed through
