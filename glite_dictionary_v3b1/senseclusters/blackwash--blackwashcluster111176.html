blackwash

noun
old medical treatment for syphilitic sores
Pronunciation: / ˈblækˌwɑʃ / (AmE), / ˈblækˌwɒʃ / (BrE)
a mixture of calomel and limewater formerly used to treat syphilitic sores

Examples

Medical blackwash
Historical records mention the use of blackwash as a topical remedy for certain infections.
In 19th-century medicine, blackwash was applied to skin lesions caused by syphilis.
The physician prescribed blackwash in an attempt to soothe the patient's sores.
