SAR

noun
synthetic-aperture radar
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˌɛs.eɪˈɑːr / (AmE), / ˌɛs.eɪˈɑː / (BrE)
a type of radar called synthetic-aperture radar that uses the movement of the antenna to create detailed images, often from satellites or aircraft

Examples

SAR satellite images
The satellite's SAR system provided clear images of the Earth's surface even through clouds.
Researchers analyzed SAR data to monitor changes in the glacier over time.
Emergency teams used SAR images to assess the extent of the flood damage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: LIDAR - a system that uses laser light to measure distances and create images
  • synonym: radar - a system that uses radio waves to detect objects and measure their distance and speed
