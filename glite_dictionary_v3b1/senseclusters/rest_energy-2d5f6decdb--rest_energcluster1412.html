rest energy

noun
energy related to a particle's rest mass
7.4 6.5
Pronunciation: /rest ˈen.ər.dʒiː/ (AmE), /rest ˈen.ə.dʒiː/ (BrE)
the energy equivalent to the mass of a particle at rest, calculated as rest mass times the square of the speed of light

Examples

Rest energy of particle
According to Einstein's equation, the rest energy of a particle equals its mass times the speed of light squared.
Nuclear reactions can release a significant portion of the rest energy of atoms as usable energy.
The positron and electron both have the same rest energy, which is converted into gamma rays upon annihilation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mass–energy equivalence - the concept that mass and energy are interchangeable (E=mc²)
  • degree lower: rest mass - the mass of a body as measured when the body is at rest
