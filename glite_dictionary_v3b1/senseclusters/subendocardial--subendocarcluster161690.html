subendocardial

adjective
beneath heart lining
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌbˌɛndoʊˈkɑːrdiəl / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˌɛndəʊˈkɑːdiəl / (BrE)
located or occurring just beneath the inner lining of the heart

Examples

Subendocardial infarction
The doctor explained that a subendocardial infarction affects the area just beneath the heart's inner lining.
Damage to the subendocardial tissue can be detected using specialized imaging techniques.
A subendocardial injury may not show the same symptoms as a full-thickness heart attack.
