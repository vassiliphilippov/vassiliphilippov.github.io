liquefaction

noun
ground turning liquid
Pronunciation: / ˌlɪkwəˈfækʃən / (AmE), / ˌlɪkwɪˈfækʃən / (BrE)
the process by which solid ground or material becomes liquid, especially during an earthquake

Examples

Earthquake liquefaction
During the earthquake, liquefaction caused the ground to lose its strength and behave like a liquid.
Engineers study liquefaction to design safer buildings in earthquake-prone areas.
The sudden liquefaction of the soil led to severe damage in the city center.

Related Terms

  • synonym: melting - the process of changing from solid to liquid by heat
  • antonym: solidification - the process of becoming solid
