Lord willing and the creek don't rise

x
if everything goes as planned (idiom)
Pronunciation: / lɔrd ˈwɪlɪŋ ənd ðə krik doʊnt raɪz / (AmE), / lɔːd ˈwɪlɪŋ ənd ðə kriːk dəʊnt raɪz / (BrE)
(informal, mainly US) Used to express hope for the success of future plans, meaning 'if nothing goes wrong or intervenes'.

Examples

Lord willing and the creek don't rise
He hopes to finish the house this year, Lord willing and the creek don't rise.
I'll see you at the reunion, Lord willing and the creek don't rise.
We’ll meet again next month, Lord willing and the creek don't rise.

Related Terms

  • synonym: God willing - used to indicate hope for future plans if circumstances allow
