interleukin

noun
protein regulating immune responses
9.9 6.6
Pronunciation: /ɪn.tər.ˈluː.kɪn/ (AmE), /ɪn.tə.ˈluː.kɪn/ (BrE)
any of a group of proteins released by white blood cells that regulate immune responses and communication between cells

Examples

Scientists studied how interleukin levels changed in response to infection.
