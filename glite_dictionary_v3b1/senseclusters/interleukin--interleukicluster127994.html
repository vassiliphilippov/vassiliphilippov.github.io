interleukin

noun
protein regulating immune responses
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.tɚˈluː.kɪn / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈluː.kɪn / (BrE)
any of a group of proteins released by white blood cells that regulate immune responses and communication between cells

Examples

The interleukin protein
Certain types of interleukin can help activate T cells to fight viruses.
Researchers discovered a new interleukin involved in the inflammatory process.
Scientists studied how interleukin levels changed in response to infection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cytokine - a broad category of signaling proteins in the immune system
