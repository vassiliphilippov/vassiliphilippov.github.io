gaudy

adjective
excessively bright or showy, tasteless
5.5 2.14/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…°…îdi / (AmE), / Àà…°…îÀêdi / (BrE)
excessively bright or showy, especially in a way that is tasteless or lacks good taste

Examples

Gaudy clothes
He regretted buying such a gaudy tie when he saw how it clashed with his suit.
She wore a gaudy dress covered in sequins that turned every head at the party.
The decorations were so gaudy that they overwhelmed the small living room.
I prefer simple designs to the gaudy patterns on those shirts.
She wore a gaudy necklace that clashed with her elegant dress.
The hotel's lobby was decorated with gaudy chandeliers and brightly colored carpets.

Synonyms

  • garish
    6.7 1.49/m
    garish is harshly bright and over-decorated, while gaudy specifically suggests showy brightness that lacks good taste.
  • loud
    1.8 3.13/m
    loud is very bright or garish in color and pattern, while gaudy means showy brightness that seems tasteless.
  • flashy
    4.1 3.72/m
    flashy is bright and designed to attract attention, while gaudy focuses on showiness that lacks subtle good taste.
  • showy
    3.2 0.67/m
    showy is meant to draw attention by style or size, while gaudy adds a sense of tasteless, excessive brightness.
  • tacky
    4.3 2.40/m
    tacky is cheap and in poor taste, often from low quality, while gaudy is about loud, excessive brightness.
  • tawdry
    7.8 1.16/m
    tawdry is showy in a cheap, poor-quality way, while gaudy focuses more on bright, tasteless display.
  • kitschy
    8.2 0.40/m
    kitschy is cheap, sentimental, or gimmicky in bad taste, while gaudy is mostly about bright, showy ugliness.
  • glitzy
    4.8 0.82/m
    glitzy is attractively shiny and glamorous in a superficial way, while gaudy is more clearly tasteless and excessive.
  • jazzy
    6.3 0.12/m
    jazzy is lively, bright, and stylish, often with positive energy, while gaudy feels tasteless and overly showy.
