burst out

verb
suddenly begin speaking or expressing emotion
1.7
Pronunciation: / bɝst aʊt / (AmE), / bɜːst aʊt / (BrE)
to suddenly say something or express an emotion, especially by speaking, laughing, or making a noise

Examples

Burst out laughing
"That's not fair!" the boy suddenly burst out.
Everyone burst out cheering when the final goal was scored.
She couldn't help it and burst out laughing in the middle of the meeting.
She couldn't hold back any longer and burst out laughing.
Violence burst out unexpectedly after the tense negotiations broke down.
When the announcement was made, cheers burst out across the stadium.

Related Terms

  • synonym: erupt - to break out suddenly and violently
  • synonym: exclaim - to say something suddenly and loudly
  • synonym: flare up - to become suddenly intense or violent
  • antonym: hold back - to restrain from expressing an emotion or words
