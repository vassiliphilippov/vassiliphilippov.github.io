burst out

verb
suddenly begin speaking or expressing emotion
3.3 1.41/m
Pronunciation: / b…ùst a ät / (AmE), / b…úÀêst a ät / (BrE)
to suddenly say something or express an emotion, especially by speaking, laughing, or making a noise

Examples

Burst out laughing
"That's not fair!" the boy suddenly burst out.
Everyone burst out cheering when the final goal was scored.
She couldn't help it and burst out laughing in the middle of the meeting.
She couldn't hold back any longer and burst out laughing.
Violence burst out unexpectedly after the tense negotiations broke down.
When the announcement was made, cheers burst out across the stadium.

Synonyms

  • blurt out
    3.9 2.60/m
    blurt out means saying something suddenly and without thinking, while burst out expresses sudden speech or emotion like laughing.
  • break into
    4.0 1.87/m
    break into means suddenly beginning to sing, laugh, or cry, while burst out covers sudden speech or sounds often with strong feeling.
  • exclaim
    3.4 11/m
    exclaim means to say something loudly from surprise or strong feeling, while burst out can show sudden speech or laughter.
  • cry out
    1.8 9.29/m
    cry out means to make a loud sound from pain, fear, or surprise, while burst out can include sudden laughter or speech.
  • erupt
    4.2 9.06/m
    erupt means to break out forcefully, often of events or strong emotion, while burst out is mainly sudden speech or laughter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flare up - to become suddenly intense or violent
  • antonym: hold back - to restrain from expressing an emotion or words
