petulance

noun
irritable or sulky mood
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛtʃələns / (AmE), / ˈpɛtjʊləns / (BrE)
the quality of being easily irritated or childishly sulky or bad-tempered

Examples

His petulance showed
His petulance became apparent when things did not go his way.
She answered the question with unexpected petulance, surprising her colleagues.
You could sense a trace of petulance in his tone after receiving the criticism.

Related Terms

  • antonym: good humor - cheerful and pleasant mood
  • synonym: irritability - tendency to become easily annoyed or angry
