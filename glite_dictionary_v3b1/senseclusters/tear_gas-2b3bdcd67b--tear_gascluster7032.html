tear gas

noun
a gas that causes eye irritation and tears
3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈtɪr ˌɡæs / (AmE), / ˈtɪə ˌɡæs / (BrE)
A substance in the form of a gas that causes severe irritation to the eyes, producing tears but not usually causing permanent damage; commonly used by police to disperse crowds.

Examples

tear gas spray
Exposure to tear gas caused severe discomfort, but no lasting harm.
Police used tear gas to break up the demonstration when it became violent.
Protesters covered their faces to protect themselves from tear gas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pepper spray - chemical spray that irritates eyes and skin, used for self-defense or crowd control
