uniserial

adjective
single row arrangement
Pronunciation: / ˌjuːnɪˈsɪriəl / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪˈsɪəriəl / (BrE)
arranged in a single row, line, or sequence; used in biology and mathematics to describe things ordered in a single series

Examples

A uniserial row
The seeds are uniserial, forming a single line inside the pod.

The microscope revealed a uniserial arrangement of cells in the tissue sample.
The professor described a uniserial module where every submodule is contained in another.
In algebra, a uniserial ring has its ideals arranged in a single chain.
Understanding uniserial structures can simplify the classification of certain algebraic objects.
