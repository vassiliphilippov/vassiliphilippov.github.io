vein in the rock

noun
mineral-rich layer in rock
4.2
Pronunciation: / veɪn ɪn ðə rɑk / (AmE), / veɪn ɪn ðə rɒk / (BrE)
a long, narrow layer of mineral that fills a crack or fissure in surrounding rock, often containing valuable minerals or ores

Examples

A vein in the rock
A vein in the rock can often be seen as a contrasting line or band crossing the surface.
Geologists study the formation of a vein in the rock to understand mineral deposits.
The miners discovered a thick vein in the rock that was rich in gold.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lode - a rich source of a mineral within rock
