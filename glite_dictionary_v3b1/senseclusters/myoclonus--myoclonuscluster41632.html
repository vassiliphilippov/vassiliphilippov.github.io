myoclonus

noun
sudden involuntary muscle jerk
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪ.əˈkloʊ.nəs / (AmE), / ˌmʌɪ.əˈkləʊ.nəs / (BrE)
A sudden, involuntary muscle jerk, spasm, or twitch, often of a muscle or group of muscles.

Examples

Sudden myoclonus jerk
During the examination, the patient exhibited myoclonus in his left leg.
Some neurological disorders are characterized by frequent episodes of myoclonus.
The doctor explained that myoclonus can occur as a side effect of certain medications.
