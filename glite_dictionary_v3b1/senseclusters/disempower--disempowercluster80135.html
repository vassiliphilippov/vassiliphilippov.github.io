disempower

verb
to take away power
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsɪmˈpaʊər / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪmˈpaʊə / (BrE)
to take away or reduce someone's power, confidence, or ability to control their own life or situation

Examples

To disempower people
Unfair laws can disempower entire communities and limit their opportunities.
The new manager's strict policies disempowered the staff and lowered morale.
Some people feel disempowered when they are not included in important decisions.
The new policy could disempower employees by limiting their decision-making abilities.
Activists argued that the changes would disempower local communities and reduce their influence.
Some leaders fear that reforms may disempower traditional authorities in the region.

Related Terms

  • antonym: empower - to give someone more control or confidence
  • synonym: weaken - to make someone or something less strong or powerful
