bug-out

verb
to leave quickly
8.2
Pronunciation: /ˈbʌg.aʊt/ (AmE), /ˈbʌg.aʊt/ (BrE)
to leave a place quickly, especially to escape danger or in an emergency situation

Examples

To bug-out fast
When the alarm went off, everyone had to bug-out as quickly as possible.
The hikers decided to bug-out when they saw the storm approaching.
If there is a fire drill, we are supposed to bug-out and gather outside.

Related Terms

  • synonym: evacuate - to leave a place because it is unsafe
  • synonym: flee - to run away from danger or trouble
  • antonym: remain - to stay in the same place
