approach

noun
golf shot aiming for the green
4.9
Pronunciation: / əˈproʊtʃ / (AmE), / əˈprəʊtʃ / (BrE)
the act, instance, or route of moving closer to someone or something in space (including flight paths, golf shots, or roads)

Examples

An approach road
After a perfect drive, he made a strong approach to set up an easy putt.
He lost the hole when his approach rolled over the green.
Her approach shot landed just a few feet from the pin.
Because of poor visibility, the plane had to make a second approach before landing safely.
During the approach, the control tower guided the aircraft through turbulent weather.
The pilot carefully adjusted the approach to ensure a smooth landing on the runway.
As the storm made its approach, the sky grew dark and the wind picked up.
The approach of the train could be heard long before it arrived at the station.
We watched the approach of the comet through our telescope.
