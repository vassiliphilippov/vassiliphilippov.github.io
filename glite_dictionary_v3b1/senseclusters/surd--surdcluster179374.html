surd

noun
voiceless consonant
Pronunciation: / sɝd / (AmE), / sɜːd / (BrE)
A voiceless consonant, produced without vibration of the vocal cords.

Examples

Surd consonant sound
In English, the sound of s in 'sip' is classified as a surd.
Linguists categorize 'f', 'k', and 't' as examples of surd consonants.
Unlike 'z', which is voiced, 's' is a surd since it lacks vocal cord vibration.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sonant - a voiced consonant produced with vibration of the vocal cords
  • synonym: voiceless consonant - a consonant produced without vocal cord vibration
