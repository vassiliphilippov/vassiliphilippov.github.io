catchiness

noun
ease of spreading
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkætʃinəs / (AmE), / ˈkætʃinəs / (BrE)
the quality of being easily spread from one person to another, especially referring to diseases or behaviors

Examples

Catchiness of a virus
The catchiness of the virus surprised doctors, spreading through the community within days.
Public health officials were concerned about the catchiness of the new strain of flu.
The catchiness of certain behaviors can lead to trends spreading quickly among teenagers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: infectiousness - the quality of causing infection or being easily transmitted
  • antonym: resistance - the ability to prevent the spread of disease or behavior
