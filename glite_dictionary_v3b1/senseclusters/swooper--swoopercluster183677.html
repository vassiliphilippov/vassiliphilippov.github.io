swooper

noun
sudden opportunist
8.1
Pronunciation: /ˈswuː.pər/ (AmE), /ˈswuː.pə/ (BrE)
an informal term for a person or group that arrives or acts suddenly to take advantage of a situation, often by acquiring or seizing something quickly

Examples

A business swooper
The company was labeled a swooper after it bought several struggling businesses overnight.
Some investors act as swoopers, waiting for the perfect moment to buy undervalued stocks.
When the sale started, a group of swoopers rushed in to grab the best deals.

  • synonym: raider - someone who attacks or seizes something suddenly
