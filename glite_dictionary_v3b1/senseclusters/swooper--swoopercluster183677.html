swooper

noun
sudden opportunist
8.1 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàswuÀêp…ôr / (AmE), / ÀàswuÀêp…ô / (BrE)
an informal term for a person or group that arrives or acts suddenly to take advantage of a situation, often by acquiring or seizing something quickly

Examples

A business swooper
The company was labeled a swooper after it bought several struggling businesses overnight.
Some investors act as swoopers, waiting for the perfect moment to buy undervalued stocks.
When the sale started, a group of swoopers rushed in to grab the best deals.

Synonyms

  • pouncer
    7.9 0.00/m
    pouncer acts quickly on mistakes or sudden chances to get an advantage, while swooper arrives suddenly to seize or grab things.
  • snagger
    9.3 0.00/m
    snagger quickly secures desirable items or chances without delay, while swooper suggests arriving fast to take or grab something suddenly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: opportunist - a person who takes advantage of situations for personal gain
  • synonym: raider - someone who attacks or seizes something suddenly
