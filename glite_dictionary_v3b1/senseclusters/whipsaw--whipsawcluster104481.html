whipsaw

verb
to saw with a whipsaw
3.0
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪpˌsɔ / (AmE), / ˈwɪpˌsɔː / (BrE)
to cut or saw (wood) with a whipsaw, typically by two people pulling the saw alternately

Examples

To whipsaw a log
In the early days, lumberjacks would whipsaw logs into planks by hand.
They spent the afternoon whipsawing large tree trunks for building materials.
Traditionally, teams whipsawed timber in saw pits before the advent of powered sawmills.
