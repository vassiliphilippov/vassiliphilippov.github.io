unbloodied

adjective
not wounded by blood
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈblʌdid / (AmE), / ʌnˈblʌdid / (BrE)
not wounded or stained with blood; not having shed or been marked by blood

Examples

Unbloodied soldier
The soldier returned from the battle unbloodied and relieved.
Despite the fierce competition, she emerged unbloodied from the debate.
The team left the field unbloodied after a clean and fair match.

Related Terms

  • synonym: unharmed - not injured or damaged
  • antonym: wounded - injured, especially by being cut or pierced
