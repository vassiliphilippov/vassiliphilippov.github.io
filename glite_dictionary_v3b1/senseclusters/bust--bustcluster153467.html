bust

verb
break, go to pieces (informal)
4.0 12/m

to break, damage, or ruin something, especially in an informal way

Examples

To bust a vase
If you drop that vase, it will definitely bust.
My old phone finally busted after years of use.
The engine busted halfway through our road trip.
He accidentally busted his favorite mug while washing the dishes.

Synonyms

  • break
    1.0 131/m
    break is a general verb for damaging something by force, while bust is more informal and fits casual speech.
  • bang up
    7.6 0.16/m
    bang up means to damage something badly in an informal way, while bust is also informal but more general.
  • wreck
    2.9 8.67/m
    wreck means to seriously destroy something, often beyond repair, while bust implies informal damage or breaking that may be less complete.
  • smash
    2.1 14/m
    smash means to break or shatter something with great force, while bust is informal and can mean damage without shattering.

Related Terms

  • synonym: break - to separate into pieces as a result of a blow, shock, or strain
  • synonym: burst - to break open or apart suddenly, often with force
  • synonym: crack - to break or cause to break without a complete separation of the parts
  • synonym: destroy - to damage something so badly that it no longer exists or cannot be repaired
  • antonym: fix - to repair or restore to good condition
  • synonym: snap - to break suddenly with a sharp noise
