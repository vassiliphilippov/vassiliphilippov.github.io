tillering

noun
plant side shoot growth
Pronunciation: / ˈtɪlərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɪlərɪŋ / (BrE)
the process by which a plant, especially a grass, produces new side shoots from its base

Examples

Wheat tillering stage
Farmers monitor tillering in wheat to predict the potential yield of their crops.
Adequate sunlight and nutrients can encourage strong tillering in grass species.
The stage of tillering is crucial for the healthy development of cereal plants.

Related Terms

  • synonym: branching - the process of producing branches or divisions in plants
