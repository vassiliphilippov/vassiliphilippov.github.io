photoelectric

adjective
relating to the emission of electrons by light
3.5 5.9
Pronunciation: /foʊ.toʊ.ɪ.ˈlek.trɪk/ /; / /foʊ.tə.ɪ.ˈlek.trɪk/ (AmE), /fəʊ.təʊ.ɪ.ˈlek.trɪk/ (BrE)
relating to or involving the emission of electrons from a material by the action of light (photoelectricity)

Examples

Photoelectric effect
Albert Einstein won the Nobel Prize for his explanation of the photoelectric phenomenon.
Some solar panels work by using photoelectric cells to convert sunlight into electricity.
The discovery of the photoelectric effect played a key role in the development of quantum theory.

Related Terms

  • synonym: photoelectric effect - the emission of electrons when light falls on a material
