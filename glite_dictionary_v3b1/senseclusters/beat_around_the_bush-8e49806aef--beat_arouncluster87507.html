beat around the bush

verb
avoid speaking directly
1.5
Pronunciation: / biːt əˈraʊnd ðə bʊʃ / (AmE), / biːt əˈraʊnd ðə bʊʃ / (BrE)
to avoid talking directly about the main topic; to not say something clearly or directly

Examples

To beat around the bush often
Instead of beating around the bush, she finally admitted her mistake.
Stop beating around the bush and answer my question honestly.
Whenever I ask about his plans, he just beats around the bush instead of telling me directly.
Please don't beat around the bush; just tell me the truth.
She kept beating around the bush instead of answering the question.
Whenever he's nervous, he tends to beat around the bush during meetings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: get to the point - to address the main topic directly
