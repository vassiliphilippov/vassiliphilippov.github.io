feel blue

verb
to feel sad or depressed
6.5 0.27/m
Pronunciation: / fil bluÀê / (AmE), / fiÀêl bluÀê / (BrE)
to feel sad or depressed; to experience a period of unhappiness

Examples

To feel blue today
After hearing the bad news, she couldn't help but feel blue all afternoon.
It's normal to feel blue from time to time, especially during the winter months.
Whenever I feel blue, listening to music usually cheers me up.

Synonyms

  • feel down
    3.0 0.51/m
    feel down is an informal way to say you are temporarily unhappy, while feel blue suggests a gentle, slightly gloomy sadness.
  • have the blues
    7.0 0.07/m
    have the blues describes a longer or named period of sadness people mention plainly, while feel blue feels more casual and short-lived.
  • sad
    1.0 40/m
    sad is a basic adjective for unhappiness, while feel blue is an informal phrase describing the act of feeling that sadness.
  • blue
    2.1 8.19/m
    blue is a one-word adjective often used in casual speech to mean gloomy, while feel blue is the full phrase for experiencing that gloom.
