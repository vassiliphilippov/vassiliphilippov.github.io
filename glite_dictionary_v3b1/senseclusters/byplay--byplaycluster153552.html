byplay

noun
incidental dramatic action by an actor
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪˌpleɪ / (AmE), / ˈbaɪˌpleɪ / (BrE)
incidental or secondary actions performed by an actor, typically to enhance the main action without drawing full attention; often used for dramatic or comic effect

Examples

Her byplay during the dialogue helped to establish her character's nervous energy.
Subtle byplay between the characters can bring depth to a theatrical performance.
The actor's byplay with the umbrella added humor to the otherwise tense scene.
