Examples

Speaks stagily
He always speaks stagily when telling stories, as if acting on a stage.
He raised his eyebrows stagily during the performance to amuse the audience.
She sighed stagily, drawing everyone's attention to her supposed distress.

Synonyms

  • theatrically
    6.2 0.56/m
    theatrically is used to describe acting or an obvious show of drama, while stagily suggests an overly stage-like, artificial performance.
  • histrionically
    8.9 0.06/m
    histrionically is a more formal word for very dramatic, while stagily feels more negative and means overly like a staged show.
  • melodramatically
    7.2 0.13/m
    melodramatically means showing strong, excessive emotion in a scene, while stagily focuses on an artificial, stage-like way of acting.
