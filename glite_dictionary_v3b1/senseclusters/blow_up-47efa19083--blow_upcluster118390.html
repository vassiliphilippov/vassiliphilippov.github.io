blow up

verb
exaggerate; make bigger in importance or size
Pronunciation: / bloʊ ʌp / (AmE), / bləʊ ʌp / (BrE)
to exaggerate the importance, size, or seriousness of something beyond what is true or reasonable

Examples

To blow up a story
Don't blow up the story; just tell me what really happened.
He always blows up his achievements when speaking to new people.
The media tends to blow up trivial issues to attract viewers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exaggerate - to represent something as being larger, greater, or more important than it really is
  • antonym: minimize - to reduce something to the smallest possible amount or degree, opposite of exaggerate
