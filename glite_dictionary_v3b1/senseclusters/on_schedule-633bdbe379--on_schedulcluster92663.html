on schedule

adverb
at the planned time; not late
2.0 2.68/m
Pronunciation: / …ën Ààsk…õd íuÀêl / (AmE), / …ín Àà É…õdjuÀêl / (BrE)
proceeding or arriving at the time or date planned; not late or behind the intended timetable

Examples

On schedule for opening
Construction is progressing on schedule, so we expect to open the new library next month.
Despite the bad weather, the plane landed on schedule.
The team finished the report on schedule, impressing their supervisor.

Synonyms

  • on time
    1.0 12/m
    on time means arriving at the expected moment for an event, while on schedule focuses on the whole plan staying on track.
  • punctually
    3.5 0.14/m
    punctually means arriving exactly at the appointed moment, while on schedule describes a plan or timetable that is not behind.
  • on the dot
    2.8 0.29/m
    on the dot means at the exact minute someone plans, while on schedule speaks of a whole process proceeding at the planned times.

Related Terms

  • antonym: behind schedule - later than the planned time or timetable
  • synonym: on time - arriving or happening at the correct or planned moment
