cosmic microwave background

noun
the uniform microwave radiation from the early universe
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑz.mɪk ˈmaɪ.kroʊˌweɪv ˈbækˌgraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈkɒz.mɪk ˈmaɪ.krəʊˌweɪv ˈbækˌɡraʊnd / (BrE)
(in cosmology) the faint radiation left over from the early universe, observed as a uniform background of microwaves permeating all of space.

Examples

The cosmic microwave background radiation
Scientists study tiny fluctuations in the cosmic microwave background to learn about the early universe.
The cosmic microwave background provides crucial evidence for the Big Bang theory.
The discovery of the cosmic microwave background revolutionized the field of cosmology.

Related Terms

  • synonym: background radiation - the constant low-level radiation present in the universe
