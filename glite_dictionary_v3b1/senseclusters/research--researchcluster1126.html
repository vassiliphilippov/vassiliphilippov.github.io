research

verb
to study or investigate thoroughly
Pronunciation: / rɪˈsɜrtʃ / (AmE), / rɪˈsɜːtʃ / (BrE)
to study or investigate a subject in detail to discover facts or new information

Examples

To research a topic
As part of her thesis, she had to research the effects of global warming on agriculture.
Before publishing the article, the journalist researched all the relevant facts thoroughly.
He spent weeks researching different companies before choosing where to apply.
Before publishing the article, the journalist thoroughly researched the facts.
She researched the effects of climate change for her final thesis.
We are researching new methods to improve efficiency in our laboratory.

  • synonym: investigate - to carry out a systematic or formal inquiry
