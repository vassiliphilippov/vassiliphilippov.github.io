vena laryngea

noun
vein draining the larynx
Pronunciation: / ˈveɪnə lɛrɪnˈdʒiːə / (AmE), / ˈveɪnə larɪŋˈdʒiːə / (BrE)
One of the two veins that drain blood from the larynx.

Examples

The vena laryngea vein
An obstruction in the vena laryngea can lead to swelling of the larynx.
During surgery, the vena laryngea must be identified to prevent excessive bleeding.
The doctor traced the course of the vena laryngea during the neck dissection.
