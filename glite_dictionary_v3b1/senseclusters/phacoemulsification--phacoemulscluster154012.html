phacoemulsification

noun
ultrasound-assisted cataract surgery
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌfeɪkoʊɪˌmʌlsəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfakəʊɪˌmʌlsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a surgical procedure for removing cataracts in which the lens of the eye is broken up using ultrasound and the fragments are suctioned out

Examples

Phacoemulsification surgery
After his cataract diagnosis, the doctor recommended phacoemulsification to restore his vision.
Modern ophthalmologists often prefer phacoemulsification because it speeds recovery time for patients.
The success rate for phacoemulsification is very high compared to older cataract removal methods.
