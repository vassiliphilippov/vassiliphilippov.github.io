disharmonic

adjective
not harmonious
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪshɑːrˈmɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˌdɪshɑːˈmɒnɪk / (BrE)
not in agreement or harmony; incompatible or inconsistent in appearance, feeling, or ideas

Examples

Disharmonic colors
The colors in the painting were so disharmonic that they clashed rather than blended.
Her disharmonic ideas often led to heated debates in the group.
The disharmonic sounds from the orchestra made the performance difficult to enjoy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: discordant - lacking harmony or agreement
  • antonym: harmonious - forming a pleasing or consistent whole
