interaction

noun
energy exchange between particles or fields (physics)
1.5 5.4
Pronunciation: /ɪn.tə.ˈræk.ʃən/ /; / /ɪn.ə.ˈræk.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɪn.tə.ˈræk.ʃən/ /; / /ɪn.ə.ˈræk.ʃən/ (BrE)
(physics) The process by which energy is transferred between elementary particles, or between a particle and a field, or between fields, typically mediated by gauge bosons.

Examples

Weak interaction
Physicists study the weak interaction to understand processes like beta decay.
The discovery of the Higgs boson improved our understanding of field interaction in particle physics.
The electromagnetic interaction plays a crucial role in the behavior of atoms and molecules.
