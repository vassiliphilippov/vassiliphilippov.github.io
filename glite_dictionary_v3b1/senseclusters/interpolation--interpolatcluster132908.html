interpolation

noun
mathematical estimation between known values
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌtɜrpəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˌtɜːpəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
(mathematics) The process of estimating the value of a function between two known values.

Examples

Mathematical interpolation
In statistics, interpolation helps fill in missing data points within a dataset.
The engineer used interpolation to estimate the temperature at a point between two measurements.
Through linear interpolation, we can determine the value of y when x is 4.5.

Related Terms

  • antonym: extrapolation - estimating beyond known values in mathematics
