catenarian

noun
catenary curve
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkætəˈnɛriən / (AmE), / ˌkætɪˈnɛəriən / (BrE)
a curve formed by a hanging flexible chain or cable under its own weight, known as a catenary.

Examples

Catenarian curve
The bridge's suspension cables followed a perfect catenarian shape between the towers.
Engineers calculated the catenarian to ensure the cable would support the required load.
Artists sometimes use the catenarian as inspiration for elegant, natural curves in their work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: catenary - the curve formed by a hanging chain or cable under its own weight
  • antonym: parabola - a symmetrical open plane curve formed by a moving point
