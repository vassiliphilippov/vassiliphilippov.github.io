feel for

verb
to sympathize or empathize with someone's suffering
3.0 2.49/m
Pronunciation: / fiÀêl f…îr /; / fiÀêl f…ôr / (AmE), / fiÀêl f…ô / (BrE)
to feel sympathy or compassion for someone who is suffering or in difficulty

Examples

To feel for someone
I really feel for you after hearing about your loss.
She could see how upset he was and genuinely felt for him.
We all feel for the victims of the disaster.
I really feel for you and hope things get better soon.
Many people feel for those affected by the natural disaster.
She felt for her colleague, who was going through a difficult divorce.

Synonyms

  • sympathize with
    3.4 1.97/m
    sympathize with often shows understanding and shared feelings in speech, while feel for means feeling simple compassion for someone.
  • sympathize
    3.5 5.67/m
    sympathize means you share or understand another person's feelings, while feel for suggests feeling pity or compassion without joining their pain.
  • feel bad for
    2.8 3.12/m
    feel bad for is a casual phrase showing pity or guilt in speech, while feel for is a more neutral sympathy or compassion.
  • commiserate
    7.0 0.76/m
    commiserate means to express shared sorrow or talk about sad events, while feel for is to quietly feel sympathy without long talk.
  • pity
    1.0 4.18/m
    pity is often a distant, sometimes harsh feeling of sorry for someone, while feel for is warm, caring sympathy.
  • feel someone's pain
    5.5 0.02/m
    feel someone's pain means to understand and share another's suffering deeply, while feel for means to feel sympathy from a distance.
  • feel your pain
    6.8 0.75/m
    feel your pain means to truly share a person's suffering, while feel for means to feel general sympathy without sharing it.
