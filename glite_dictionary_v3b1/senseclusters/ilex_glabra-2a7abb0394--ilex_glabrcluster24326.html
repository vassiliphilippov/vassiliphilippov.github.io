Ilex glabra

noun
a holly species native to eastern North America
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪlɛks ˈɡleɪbrə / (AmE), / ˈaɪlɛks ˈɡleɪbrə / (BrE)
An evergreen holly native to eastern North America, with oblong leathery leaves and small black berries, commonly known as inkberry.

Examples

Ilex glabra plant
Gardeners value Ilex glabra for its resistance to pests and its attractive black berries.
The low, dense foliage of Ilex glabra makes it a popular choice for hedges in landscaping.
Wildlife such as songbirds often feed on the berries produced by Ilex glabra in the winter.
