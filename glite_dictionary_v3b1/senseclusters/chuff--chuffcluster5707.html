chuff

verb
to blow or puff loudly
8.5 7.0
Pronunciation: /tʃʌf/ (AmE), /tʃʌf/ (BrE)
to make a loud, puffing or blowing noise, especially like a steam engine or when breathing heavily

Examples

To chuff loudly
As he reached the top of the stairs, he chuffed and tried to catch his breath.
The old train chuffed into the station, steam billowing from its engine.
The tiger chuffed softly as it greeted its cubs at the zoo.
The old locomotive kept chuffing as it climbed the steep mountain track.
We could hear the steam engine chuff in the distance as it approached the station.
The little train chuffed along the tracks, leaving clouds of steam behind.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hiss - to make a long, sharp sibilant sound
  • synonym: huff - to breathe out loudly, especially in anger or exertion
  • synonym: puff - to breathe or blow out in short bursts
