catchweight

noun
actual item weight
8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈkætʃ.weɪt/ (AmE), /ˈkætʃ.weɪt/ (BrE)
the actual weight of an individual item, especially when items do not have a standard weight and are priced or recorded by their specific weight

Examples

Catchweight steak price
Each steak was priced according to its catchweight, so the total cost varied from package to package.
The deli labels every cheese block with its catchweight for accurate billing.
Because the catchweight of produce can differ, customers are charged based on the exact amount they buy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: net weight - the weight of a product without packaging
  • antonym: standard weight - a fixed or typical weight used for comparison
