Addison's syndrome

noun
adrenal gland disorder marked by fatigue and skin darkening
6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈæd.ɪ.sənz ˈsɪn.droʊm/ (AmE), /ˈæd.ɪ.sənz ˈsɪn.drəʊm/ (BrE)
A disease caused by inadequate function of the adrenal cortex, resulting in symptoms such as anemia, fatigue, and darkening of the skin.

Examples

Addison's syndrome symptoms
After months of fatigue, the doctor finally diagnosed her with Addison's syndrome.
Because Addison's syndrome affects hormone production, it can cause muscle weakness and weight loss.
Patients with Addison's syndrome often require lifelong hormone replacement therapy.
