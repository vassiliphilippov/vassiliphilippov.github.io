crawl space

noun
low space under a building's floor
4.7
Pronunciation: / krɔl speɪs / (AmE), / krɔːl speɪs / (BrE)
a low, narrow unfinished space beneath the first floor of a building, usually providing access to wiring or plumbing

Examples

A crawl space under house
Many houses in this region are built with a crawl space instead of a basement.
Rodents sometimes get into the crawl space and cause damage to insulation.
The plumber had to enter the crawl space to repair a leaking pipe.
