quartzite

noun
hard metamorphic rock formed from quartz sandstone
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɔrt.saɪt / (AmE), / ˈkwɔːt.saɪt / (BrE)
a hard, metamorphic rock consisting mainly of interlocking quartz crystals, formed from sandstone under heat and pressure

Examples

Hard quartzite rock
Builders often use quartzite for countertops due to its durability and attractive look.
Over millions of years, the sandstone transformed into quartzite through heat and pressure.
The mountain range is primarily composed of quartzite, giving it a rugged appearance.
