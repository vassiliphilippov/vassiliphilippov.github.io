purlieus

noun
forest boundary areas
Pronunciation: / ˈpɜrliˌuːz / (AmE), / ˈpɜːlɪjuːz / (BrE)
the areas on the edge of a royal forest that were no longer subject to forest law, especially in historical English law

Examples

Forest purlieus
The ancient purlieus of the forest were marked by old stone posts.
Villagers often gathered firewood in the purlieus where royal restrictions did not apply.
Historical records describe disputes over the exact location of the purlieus.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boundary - the edge or limit of an area
  • antonym: interior - the inner part of something, away from the edge
