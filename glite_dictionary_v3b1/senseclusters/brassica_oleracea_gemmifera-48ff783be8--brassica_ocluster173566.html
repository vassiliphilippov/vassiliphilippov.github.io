Brassica oleracea gemmifera

noun
plant species; Brussels sprout
Pronunciation: / brəˈsɪkə ˌoʊləˈreɪsiə ˌdʒɛmɪˈfɪrə / (AmE), / brəˈsɪkə ˌɒlɪˈreɪsiə ˌdʒɛmɪˈfɪərə / (BrE)
A plant species cultivated for its stout stalks bearing edible small green heads resembling diminutive cabbages; commonly known as the Brussels sprout.

Examples

The Brassica oleracea gemmifera plant
Botanists classify Brussels sprouts under the species Brassica oleracea gemmifera.
Farmers planted rows of Brassica oleracea gemmifera to harvest in the autumn months.
The Brassica oleracea gemmifera is widely grown in Europe for its nutritious sprouts.
