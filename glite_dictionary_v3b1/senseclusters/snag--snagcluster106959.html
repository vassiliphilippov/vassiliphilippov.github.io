snag

noun
standing dead tree in a forest
Pronunciation: / snæɡ / (AmE), / snæɡ / (BrE)
A dead tree that remains standing, typically in an undisturbed forest, providing habitat for wildlife.

Examples

Forest snag
Many rare insects were discovered living under the bark of the old snag near the river.
The wildlife biologist pointed out a snag that served as a lookout for hawks.
Woodpeckers often make their nests in a snag, taking advantage of the decaying wood.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deadwood - fallen or standing dead trees and woody debris in a forest
