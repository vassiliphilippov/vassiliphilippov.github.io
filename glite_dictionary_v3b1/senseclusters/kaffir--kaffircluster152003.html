kaffir

noun

A variety of sorghum grown for its grain, used as food for humans and livestock; noted for its resemblance to Indian corn but with sawtooth-edged leaves.

Examples

Kaffir grain crop
Farmers in dry regions often choose kaffir because it is drought-resistant and yields a good harvest.
This field is planted with kaffir, which provides a valuable food source for the cattle.
Traditionally, kaffir grain is ground into flour and used for making porridge.

Synonyms

  • kaffir corn
    9.7 0.00/m

  • Sorghum bicolor
    5.8 0.04/m
    Sorghum bicolor is the scientific species name for many sorghum types grown for grain, while kaffir names one specific traditional variety.
