indifferent

adjective
showing no interest or concern
1.9 4.4
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdɪf.ər.ənt /; / ɪnˈdɪf.ɚ.ənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɪf.ərənt /; / ɪnˈdɪf.ər.ənt / (BrE)
having no particular interest, concern, or feeling; not caring or emotionally unmoved

Examples

Indifferent to criticism
He remained indifferent to the criticism directed at him.
Many people are indifferent to politics and rarely vote.
She gave an indifferent shrug when asked about the result.
Despite her classmates' excitement, Julia felt completely indifferent about the upcoming field trip.
He greeted the good news with an indifferent shrug, as if it barely mattered to him.
The manager remained indifferent to the team's complaints, barely acknowledging their concerns.
He remained indifferent about which side to support in the debate.
She was indifferent to what movie we watched, as long as we went together.
They seemed indifferent to the weather, walking outside whether it rained or shined.

Related Terms

  • synonym: apathetic - showing or feeling no interest or concern
  • antonym: biased - showing an unreasonable preference or dislike for something
  • antonym: caring - showing concern or interest
  • antonym: concerned - showing interest, care, or worry
  • synonym: uninterested - not interested; not concerned
