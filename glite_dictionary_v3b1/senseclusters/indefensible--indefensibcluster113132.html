Examples

Indefensible argument
He realized his theory was indefensible once new evidence was presented.

They considered her actions legally and morally indefensible.

Synonyms

  • untenable
    8.2 2.10/m
    untenable describes a position that cannot stand up to criticism, while indefensible means it cannot be defended at all.
  • unsupportable
    6.8 0.27/m
    unsupportable cannot be backed by evidence or argument, while indefensible highlights it cannot be protected from attack or criticism.
  • insupportable
    7.8 0.19/m
    insupportable sounds formal and means impossible to defend or accept, while indefensible is more common and bluntly unjustifiable.
  • unjustifiable
    6.8 0.54/m
    unjustifiable means you cannot show something is reasonable or right, while indefensible stresses it cannot be defended against criticism.
  • unwarrantable
    9.2 0.03/m
    unwarrantable is formal and rare, meaning lacking justification or authority, while indefensible focuses on inability to be defended.

Related Terms

  • synonym: untenable - (of arguments) not able to be maintained or defended
  • synonym: vulnerable - exposed to the possibility of attack or harm
