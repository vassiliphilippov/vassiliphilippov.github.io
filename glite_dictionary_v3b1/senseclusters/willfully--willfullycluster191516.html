willfully

adverb
deliberately; intentionally

In a deliberate, intentional, and often stubborn or determined manner; on purpose, sometimes with the implication of disobedience or defiance.

Examples

To willfully ignore rules
He willfully ignored the warnings posted on the door.
She willfully misunderstood his intentions to make her argument stronger.
The company was fined for willfully violating environmental regulations.
He wilfully withheld information from the investigators.
She wilfully ignored the warning signs posted outside the building.
The child wilfully broke the vase after being told not to touch it.

Synonyms

    3.9 3.25/m
    purposefully is done with a clear aim and steady effort, while willfully often adds stubborn or defiant intention.
  • obstinately
    7.2 0.34/m
    obstinately describes refusing to change or accept advice, while willfully means acting deliberately, often openly in defiance.
  • knowingly
    3.9 5.03/m
    knowingly shows full awareness or understanding of facts, while willfully highlights deliberate choice often with stubborn intent.
