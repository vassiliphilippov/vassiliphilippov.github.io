Robinia pseudoacacia

noun
species of tree, also called black locust
Pronunciation: / roʊˈbɪniə ˌsuːdoʊəˈkeɪʃə / (AmE), / rəˈbɪniə ˌsjuːdəʊəˈkeɪsiə / (BrE)
A large, thorny tree native to eastern and central North America, known for its pinnate leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers; widely cultivated and naturalized in temperate regions.

Examples

The Robinia pseudoacacia tree
Farmers often plant Robinia pseudoacacia to prevent soil erosion and provide windbreaks.
In the park, a towering Robinia pseudoacacia cast a pleasant shade over the benches.
Many bees are attracted to the fragrant flowers of Robinia pseudoacacia in early summer.
