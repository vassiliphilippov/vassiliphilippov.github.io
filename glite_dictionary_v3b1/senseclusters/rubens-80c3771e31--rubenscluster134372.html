Rubens

proper noun
Flemish Baroque painter (1577–1640)
Pronunciation: / ˈruːbənz / (AmE), / ˈruːbənz / (BrE)
Peter Paul Rubens (1577–1640), a prolific Flemish Baroque painter known for his vibrant, dramatic, and sensuous works, and knighted by King Charles I of England.

Examples

Rubens paintings
Many art historians regard Rubens as the leading exponent of the Flemish Baroque style.
She wrote her thesis on the religious symbolism in the paintings of Rubens.
The exhibition features several masterpieces by Rubens and his contemporaries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Rembrandt - Dutch Baroque painter renowned for his portraits and use of light
  • synonym: Rubenesque - adjective describing a plump, rounded feminine form in art, inspired by Rubens's paintings
