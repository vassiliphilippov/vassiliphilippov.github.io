maladjustment

noun
failure to adapt to environment
9.0 8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌmæl.əˈdʒʌst.mənt / (AmE), / ˌmal.əˈdʒʌst.mənt / (BrE)
a state of not being able to adapt successfully to one's environment, often resulting in emotional or behavioral problems

Examples

Social maladjustment
Her social maladjustment led her to feel isolated from her peers.
Many experts believe that parental conflict can contribute to adolescent maladjustment.
The child's maladjustment became evident when he struggled to make friends at school.
