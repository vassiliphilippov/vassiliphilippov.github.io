novel coronavirus pneumonia

noun
viral respiratory disease (COVID-19)
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑvəl kəˌroʊnəˌvaɪrəs nuˈmoʊniə / (AmE), / ˈnɒvəl kəˈrəʊnəˌvaɪrəs njuːˈməʊniə / (BrE)
a viral respiratory disease caused by SARS-CoV-2, first identified in 2019, which led to a global pandemic, also known as COVID-19

Examples

Novel coronavirus pneumonia symptoms
Doctors reported that patients with novel coronavirus pneumonia often experienced fever, cough, and difficulty breathing.
Many countries enforced lockdowns to slow the spread of novel coronavirus pneumonia in 2020.
The World Health Organization declared novel coronavirus pneumonia a global pandemic due to its rapid transmission worldwide.

Related Terms

  • synonym: COVID-19 - the illness caused by the novel coronavirus SARS-CoV-2
  • degree lower: SARS - severe acute respiratory syndrome, another coronavirus disease
  • degree lower: pneumonia - lung infection causing inflammation
