insertional

adjective
relating to insertion
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈsɜrʃənəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɜːʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to or caused by the act of inserting, especially in a biological or medical context

Examples

Insertional injury risk
The doctor explained the insertional risks associated with the new medical device.
Researchers observed insertional mutations after introducing the gene into the cells.
The team studied the insertional effects of the implant on surrounding tissue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inserted - placed or put into something
  • antonym: removal - the act of taking something out
