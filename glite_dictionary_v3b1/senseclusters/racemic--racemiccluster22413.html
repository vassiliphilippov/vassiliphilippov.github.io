racemic

adjective
equal enantiomer mixture
Pronunciation: / rəˈsiːmɪk / (AmE), / rəˈsiːmɪk / (BrE)
containing equal amounts of left- and right-handed enantiomers of a compound

Examples

Racemic mixture
The chemist analyzed the racemic mixture to determine its properties.
A racemic drug may have different effects compared to its pure enantiomer.
They separated the racemic compound into its individual enantiomers for further study.

Related Terms

  • antonym: chiral - having a structure that is not superimposable on its mirror image
  • synonym: enantiomeric - relating to one of a pair of molecules that are mirror images of each other
