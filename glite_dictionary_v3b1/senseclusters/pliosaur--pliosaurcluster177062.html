pliosaur

noun
extinct marine reptile
Pronunciation: / ˈplaɪ.əˌsɔr / (AmE), / ˈplaɪ.əˌsɔː / (BrE)
a large, extinct marine reptile with a short neck and powerful jaws that lived during the Mesozoic era

Examples

A pliosaur fossil
The paleontologist carefully reconstructed the skull of a pliosaur found in the cliffs.
A pliosaur could grow to be over ten meters long and was a top predator in ancient seas.
Children were fascinated by the life-sized model of a pliosaur at the natural history museum.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dinosaur - a group of extinct reptiles that lived during the Mesozoic era
  • synonym: plesiosaur - an extinct marine reptile with a long neck and small head
