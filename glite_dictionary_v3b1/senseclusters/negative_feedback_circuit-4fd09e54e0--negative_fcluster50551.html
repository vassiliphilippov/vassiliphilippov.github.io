negative feedback circuit

noun
an electronic circuit with output fed back negatively
4.6
Pronunciation: /ˈneɡ.ə.tɪv ˈfiːd.bæk ˈsɜːr.kɪt/ (AmE), /ˈneɡ.ə.tɪv ˈfiːd.bæk ˈsɜː.kɪt/ (BrE)
An electronic circuit in which a portion of the output is returned to the input in such a way as to subtract from the input, stabilizing or controlling the signal.

Examples

A negative feedback circuit design
A negative feedback circuit helps maintain consistent output levels in sensitive electronic devices.
Many audio systems employ a negative feedback circuit to reduce distortion and noise.
The engineer designed a negative feedback circuit to improve the amplifier's stability.

Related Terms
