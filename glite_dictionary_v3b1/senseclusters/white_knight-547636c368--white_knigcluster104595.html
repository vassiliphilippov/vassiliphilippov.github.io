white knight

noun
friendly acquirer in hostile takeover
Pronunciation: / waɪt naɪt / (AmE), / waɪt naɪt / (BrE)
a person or company who rescues or defends someone in difficulty, especially by saving a company from a hostile takeover

Examples

A white knight stepped in at the last moment, saving the company from an aggressive takeover.

In the face of a hostile takeover, the board searched desperately for a white knight to protect the firm.
She acted as a white knight, offering support to the local charity when it was running out of funds.
