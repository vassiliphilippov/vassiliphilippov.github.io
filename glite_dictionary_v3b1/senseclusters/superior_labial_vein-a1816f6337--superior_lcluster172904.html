superior labial vein

noun
vein draining the upper lip
6.3
Pronunciation: / suˈpɪriər ˈleɪbiəl veɪn / (AmE), / suˈpɪəriə ˈleɪbɪəl veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that is a branch of the facial vein and drains blood from the upper lip

Examples

The superior labial vein
A clot in the superior labial vein can lead to noticeable swelling of the upper lip.
During facial surgery, care must be taken not to damage the superior labial vein.
The superior labial vein is responsible for draining blood from the upper lip area.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inferior labial vein - vein draining the lower lip
