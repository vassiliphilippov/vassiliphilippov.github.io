demersal

adjective
bottom-dwelling aquatic
9.6
Pronunciation: / dɪˈmɜrsəl / (AmE), / dɪˈmɜːsəl / (BrE)
living near or on the bottom of seas or lakes

Examples

Demersal fish species
Many commercial fisheries target demersal species such as cod and haddock.
The scientist studied the behavior of demersal fish in the deep lake.
Unlike pelagic fish, demersal fish spend most of their lives near the seabed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: benthic - relating to the bottom of a sea or lake
  • antonym: pelagic - living in the open water away from the bottom
