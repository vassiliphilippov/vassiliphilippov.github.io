pseudo-Freudianism

noun
superficial use of Freudian ideas
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːdoʊ ˈfrɔɪdiəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌsjuːdəʊ ˈfrɔɪdiːəˌnɪzəm / (BrE)
A misleading or superficial interpretation of Freudian psychology, often lacking in genuine understanding of Freud's ideas.

Examples

Pseudo-Freudianism ideas
Academic circles often dismiss pseudo-Freudianism as lacking true scholarly merit.
Many pop-psychology books are filled with pseudo-Freudianism that oversimplifies childhood trauma.
The critic accused the film of relying on pseudo-Freudianism instead of real psychological insight.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Freudianism - The system of psychological theory founded by Sigmund Freud
