scatter

verb
separate and spread over an area
6.9 2.3
Pronunciation: /ˈskæ.tər/ (AmE), /ˈskæ.tə/ (BrE)
to cause people, animals, or things to move apart quickly in different directions; to disperse

Examples

To scatter a crowd
After the parade, police officers tried to scatter the crowds gathered in the square.
She scattered the puzzle pieces on the table to start sorting them.
The wind will scatter the seeds across the entire field.
The birds scattered as soon as they heard the loud noise.
The children quickly scattered across the playground when recess began.
When the police arrived, the crowd began to scatter in all directions.
As soon as the rain started, people scattered for cover.
Police arrived and scattered the protesters gathered outside the building.
When the alarm rang, the birds scattered from the trees in all directions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disperse - to spread or distribute over a wide area
  • antonym: gather - to bring together in one place
