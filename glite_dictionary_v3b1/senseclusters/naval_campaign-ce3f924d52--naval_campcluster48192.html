naval campaign

noun
military operation by naval forces
Pronunciation: / ˈneɪvəl kæmˈpeɪn / (AmE), / ˈneɪvəl kæmˈpeɪn / (BrE)
A military operation conducted mainly by naval forces to gain, extend, or maintain control over the sea.

Examples

A naval campaign operation
The Mediterranean naval campaign changed the course of the war.
The admiral planned a naval campaign to block enemy supply lines.
Throughout history, control of key waterways has often depended on the success of a naval campaign.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: battle - A single engagement between armed forces
  • antonym: land campaign - Military operations conducted on land
