talkiness

noun
excessive wordiness
6.8 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààt…îki.n…ôs / (AmE), / Ààt…îÀêki.n…ôs / (BrE)
the quality of speaking or writing with more words than necessary, often making communication less clear

Examples

Talkiness in lectures
The professor's talkiness made his lectures difficult to follow for many students.
She tried to reduce the talkiness in her essay to make her arguments clearer.
Some podcasts suffer from too much talkiness and not enough useful information.

Synonyms

  • wordiness
    4.8 0.09/m
    wordiness is using more words than needed in writing, while talkiness is informal excess speech that makes meaning unclear.
  • verbosity
    7.3 0.11/m
    verbosity often means long, formal expression, while talkiness is more casual excess talking that makes ideas less clear.
  • long-windedness
    7.5 0.02/m
    long-windedness describes slow, boring speech that takes too long, while talkiness is general word excess that clouds the meaning.
  • prolixity
    9.9 0.04/m
    prolixity is a formal, old-fashioned word for tedious long writing, while talkiness is casual overuse of words that confuses listeners.

  • synonym: wordiness - the quality of using more words than needed
