Taricha granulosa

noun
a species of poisonous coastal newt
Pronunciation: / təˈriːkə ɡræˈnjuːləsə / (AmE), / təˈriːkə ɡræˈnjuːlɒsə / (BrE)
A species of newt found along the humid coastal regions from Alaska to southern California, known for its potent toxin.

Examples

Taricha granulosa newt
Local guides warned hikers not to handle the Taricha granulosa due to its poisonous skin.
Researchers studied the distribution of Taricha granulosa along the West Coast of North America.
The Taricha granulosa secretes a powerful neurotoxin as a defense mechanism against predators.
