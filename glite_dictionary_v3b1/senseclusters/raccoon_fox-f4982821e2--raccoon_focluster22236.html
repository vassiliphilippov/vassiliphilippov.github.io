raccoon fox

noun
small raccoon-like mammal of North America
1.0
Pronunciation: / rəˈkun ˌfɑks / (AmE), / rəˈkuːn ˌfɒks / (BrE)
A raccoon-like omnivorous mammal native to Mexico and the southwestern United States, featuring a long bushy tail with black and white rings.

Examples

The raccoon fox tail
Due to its distinctive ringed tail, the raccoon fox is sometimes mistaken for an actual raccoon.
Several tourists were delighted to spot a raccoon fox in Big Bend National Park.
The raccoon fox is often seen darting between rocks in desert canyons at dusk.
