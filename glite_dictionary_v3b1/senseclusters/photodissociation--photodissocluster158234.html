photodissociation

noun
breaking by light
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊtoʊdɪˌsoʊsiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊdɪˌsəʊsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process in which a chemical compound is broken apart by the action of light

Examples

Photodissociation of molecules
Ultraviolet radiation can cause photodissociation of water molecules in the atmosphere.
Scientists study photodissociation to understand how sunlight affects air pollution.
The process of photodissociation plays a key role in the chemistry of the ozone layer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: photolysis - the decomposition of molecules by the action of light
  • antonym: synthesis - the combination of components to form a whole
