delicense

verb
to revoke a license
7.9
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈlaɪ.səns/ /; / /də.ˈlaɪ.səns/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈlaɪ.səns/ /; / /də.ˈlaɪ.səns/ (BrE)
to take away or revoke a license from someone or something, usually as a penalty or for noncompliance

Examples

To delicense a company
The government threatened to delicense the broadcaster for violating regulations.
After several safety violations, the board decided to delicense the restaurant.
If a doctor breaks the law, the medical council may delicense them.
