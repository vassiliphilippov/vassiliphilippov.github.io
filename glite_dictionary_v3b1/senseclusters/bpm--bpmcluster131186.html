bpm

noun
beats per minute in music
3.6 0.3/m
Pronunciation: / bi pi …õm / (AmE), / biÀê piÀê …õm / (BrE)
an abbreviation for 'beats per minute', used to indicate the tempo of a piece of music by specifying the number of beats that occur in one minute

Examples

Song bpm
A standard waltz is typically played at around 90 bpm.
DJs often adjust the bpm to match tracks during a set.
The song has a fast pace at 140 bpm.

Synonyms

  • beats per minute
    3.1 0.04/m
    beats per minute is the full phrase for tempo measured by counting beats each minute, while bpm is its common abbreviation.
  • metronome marking
    6.6 0.00/m
    metronome marking is a written tempo instruction showing beats per minute, while bpm is the short numeric label used in scores.
  • M.M.
    8.2 0.00/m
    M.M. is the historical score abbreviation from Maelzel's metronome that shows tempo in beats per minute, while bpm is the modern abbreviation.
