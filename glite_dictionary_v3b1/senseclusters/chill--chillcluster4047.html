chill

noun
a sudden feeling of fear or excitement
5.6
Pronunciation: / tʃɪl / (AmE), / tʃɪl / (BrE)
a sudden feeling of fear, excitement, or dread, often felt as a shiver

Examples

A chill down my spine
A chill ran down her spine as she entered the dark house.
He felt a chill of anticipation before the curtain lifted.
The ghost story sent a chill through everyone in the room.
A chill ran down her spine as she walked through the abandoned house.
Hearing the eerie howl gave me an unexpected chill.
There was a noticeable chill in the air when he mentioned the accident.

Related Terms

  • synonym: frisson - a sudden strong feeling of excitement or fear; a thrill
  • synonym: shudder - a sudden involuntary shiver or tremble often caused by fear or excitement
  • antonym: thrill - a sudden feeling of excitement or heightened emotion
