energy-absorbing

adjective
absorbing energy (adj.), esp. in science
Pronunciation: / ˈɛnɚdʒi əbˈzɔrbɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɛnədʒi əbˈzɔːbɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a process or material, especially in physics or engineering) absorbing or taking in energy, rather than releasing it.

Examples

Energy-absorbing bumpers
Engineers are designing cars with energy-absorbing bumpers to increase safety in crashes.
The new helmet is made from energy-absorbing materials to protect the head during impact.
This reaction is energy-absorbing, requiring heat to proceed rather than producing it.
