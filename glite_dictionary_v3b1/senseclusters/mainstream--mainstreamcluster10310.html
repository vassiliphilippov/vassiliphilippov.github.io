mainstream

noun
the dominant trend or current of thought
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪnˌstriːm / (AmE), / ˈmeɪnstriːm / (BrE)
The ideas, attitudes, or activities that are regarded as normal or conventional; the dominant trend in opinion, fashion, or the arts.

Examples

The mainstream media
Environmental concerns have now moved into the political mainstream.
Her views were considered radical and outside the mainstream of society.
The artist struggled for years before her work finally entered the mainstream.

Related Terms

  • antonym: counterculture - a way of life and set of attitudes opposed to or at variance with the prevailing social norm
  • synonym: orthodoxy - authorized or generally accepted theory, doctrine, or practice
