thallose

adjective
undifferentiated plant body
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈθæloʊs / (AmE), / ˈθaləʊs / (BrE)
having a plant body that is not divided into stem and leaves, typically flat and undifferentiated

Examples

A thallose plant
Many liverworts are thallose, lacking true stems and leaves.
The thallose structure of the plant allows it to absorb water across its entire surface.
Some algae display a thallose form, making them difficult to distinguish from mosses.

Related Terms

  • antonym: leafy - having many leaves or resembling leaves
  • synonym: thalloid - resembling or relating to a thallus
