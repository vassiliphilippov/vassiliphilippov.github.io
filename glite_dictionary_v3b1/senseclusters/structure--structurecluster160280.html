structure

noun
organization of people in a society
Pronunciation: / ˈstrʌktʃər /; / ˈstrʌktʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈstrʌktʃə / (BrE)
the way in which the parts of something are organized or arranged, such as in a system, society, text, or living thing

Examples

Social structure
Class and class relations are important components of the social structure.
Recent changes have significantly altered the structure of society in many countries.
Sociologists analyze the structure of families to understand social change.
Good structure is essential when delivering a persuasive argument.
His novel lacks structure, making it difficult to follow the plot.
The professor praised the structure of her research paper for its clear and logical flow.
The architect explained the structure of the new building in great detail.
The essay had a clear structure with an introduction, body, and conclusion.
Understanding the structure of DNA has been crucial to advances in genetics.
