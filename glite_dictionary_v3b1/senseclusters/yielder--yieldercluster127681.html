yielder

noun
someone who gives in or surrenders
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈjiːldər / (AmE), / ˈjiːldə / (BrE)
a person who yields, gives in, or surrenders to others or to circumstances

Examples

A yielder under pressure
Despite the pressure, the yielder agreed to the terms and signed the contract.
He was known as a yielder in negotiations, often giving up more than he gained.
It takes courage not to become a yielder when facing overwhelming odds.

Related Terms

  • antonym: resister - a person who resists or opposes yielding
  • synonym: surrenderer - a person who gives up or submits to authority
