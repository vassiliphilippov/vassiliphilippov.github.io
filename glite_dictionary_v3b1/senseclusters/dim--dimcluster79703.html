dim

verb
become less bright/lustrous
Pronunciation: / dɪm / (AmE), / dɪm / (BrE)
to become or make less bright; to reduce the brightness of something

Examples

To dim the lights
As the sun set, the room began to dim around us.
His enthusiasm didn't dim even after facing several setbacks.
The headlights would dim automatically when they detected oncoming traffic.
Polishing can restore the shine that years of neglect have dimmed.
She tried to dim the lights before the movie began.
The old photographs had gradually dimmed with age and sunlight.
The fog began to dim the headlights as we drove through the valley.
The newcomer’s achievements seemed to dim the earlier records set by veterans.
Time and distance can dim even the strongest of friendships.
