eyry

noun
lofty nest of a bird of prey
Pronunciation: / ˈɛr.i /; / ˈɪri / (AmE), / ˈɪəri / (BrE)
a high nest or dwelling of a bird of prey, such as an eagle or hawk

Examples

A eyry of eagles
From her vantage point, she could just make out the hawk's eyry among the pines.
Legends say the king built his fortress where the falcon made its eyry on the mountain peak.
The eagle returned to its eyry perched high on the rocky cliff.
Golden eagles built their eyry on a ledge halfway up the mountain face.
On his travels, he encountered a hermit's eyry perched high above the valley.
The old castle stood atop the cliff like an eyry, unreachable and remote.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aerie - a large nest of a bird of prey, especially an eagle, built high in a tree or on a cliff
  • degree lower: nest - a structure built by birds to lay eggs and rear young
