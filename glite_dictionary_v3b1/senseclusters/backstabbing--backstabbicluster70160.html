backstabbing

adjective
treacherous; betraying
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌstæbɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbækˌstæbɪŋ / (BrE)
behaving in a treacherous or deceitful way by betraying others, especially secretly or unexpectedly

Examples

Backstabbing coworkers
He avoided the backstabbing colleagues who spread rumors behind his back.
The office environment became toxic due to backstabbing behavior among team members.
She was tired of the backstabbing politics that made it hard to trust anyone at work.

Related Terms

  • antonym: loyal - showing firm and constant support or allegiance
  • synonym: treacherous - betraying trust or acting disloyally
