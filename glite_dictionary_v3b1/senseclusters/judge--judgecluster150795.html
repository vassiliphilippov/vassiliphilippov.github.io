judge

noun
person who decides merit or quality
3.4 14/m
Pronunciation: / dÊ’ÊŒdÊ’ / (AmE), / dÊ’ÊŒdÊ’ / (BrE)
A person with expert knowledge or authority to form an opinion or decide the merits of something, especially in a competition or contest.

Examples

Competition judge
She was invited to be a judge at the science fair because of her expertise.
The panel of judges reviewed each painting before awarding the prize.
To be a good judge of character, you need both intuition and experience.
