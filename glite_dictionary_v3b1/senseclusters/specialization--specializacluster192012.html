specialization

noun
specific field of work or study
Pronunciation: / ˌspɛʃələˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌspɛʃəlaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌspɛʃəlɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌspɛʃəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a particular area or field that someone focuses on or becomes expert in, especially as a career or subject of study, or the process of becoming an expert in a particular subject or making something suitable for a specific purpose

Examples

Medical specialization
After finishing medical school, she chose oncology as her specialization.
His specialization in environmental law made him a sought-after consultant.
The university offers a wide range of specialization options for graduate students.
Her specialization in cardiology helped her provide expert care to her patients.
Technological advances often lead to increased specialization in the workforce.
The company underwent specialization to focus exclusively on renewable energy sources.
After medical school, he chose cardiology as his specialisation.
Her specialisation is in environmental engineering, where she has built a reputation for innovative research.
Language acquisition is now my main specialisation at the university.

Related Terms

  • antonym: generalisation - the process of making something less specific; the opposite of specialisation
  • antonym: generalization - the process of making something less specialized or more common; the opposite process
  • synonym: specialization - focus or area of expertise in a field (American spelling)
  • synonym: specialty - a person's field of specialization or expertise
