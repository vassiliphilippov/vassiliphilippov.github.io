retighten

verb
to make tight again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈtaɪtən / (AmE), / ˌriːˈtaɪtən / (BrE)
to make something tight again or tighter than it was before, especially after it has become loose (e.g., bolts, nuts, screws)

Examples

To retighten the bolts
After a few hours of driving, you should retighten the wheel nuts to ensure safety.
The plumber had to retighten the pipe connections after noticing a small leak.
If the lid feels loose, simply retighten it before storing the jar.
After checking the bolts, he decided to retighten them for extra safety.
You should retighten the lid if you notice it is not sealed properly.
The mechanic had to retighten the screws after the initial test drive.
