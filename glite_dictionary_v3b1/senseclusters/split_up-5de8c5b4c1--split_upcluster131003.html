split up

noun
increase in a corporation's shares (finance)
Pronunciation: / splɪt ʌp / (AmE), / splɪt ʌp / (BrE)
an increase in the number of outstanding shares of a corporation by dividing existing shares, without altering the total value of shareholders' equity

Examples

A company split up
Analysts predicted that the split up would improve liquidity and trading activity.
Following the split up, each shareholder received twice as many shares as before.
The company announced a two-for-one split up to attract more investors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stock split - division of a company's existing stock into multiple shares
