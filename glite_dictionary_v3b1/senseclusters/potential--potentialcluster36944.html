potential

adjective
possible or likely to happen in future
Pronunciation: / pəˈtɛnʃəl / (AmE), / pəˈtɛnʃəl / (BrE)
having the ability or possibility to develop or become something in the future; possible but not yet real

Examples

Potential danger
Many scientists are studying the potential impact of climate change on agriculture.
The company is looking for potential partners to expand its market reach.
We interviewed several potential candidates for the new position.
The new treatment offers potential benefits for cancer patients.
There is a potential risk of flooding after heavy rains.
We are searching for students with potential to succeed in the sciences.

Related Terms

  • antonym: actual - existing in fact; real; contrasted with potential
  • synonym: possible - able to be done or occur
