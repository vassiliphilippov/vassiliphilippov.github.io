sawpit

noun
pit used for hand sawing logs
6.3 8.4
Pronunciation: /ˈsɑː.pɪt/ /; / /ˈsɔː.pɪt/ (AmE), /ˈsɔː.pɪt/ (BrE)
A pit over which logs are placed to be sawn by two people using a long two-handed saw, formerly used in timber processing.

Examples

A sawpit for logs
Before the advent of sawmills, a sawpit was essential for processing logs into planks.
The old lumber yard still has a preserved sawpit where workers used to cut huge timbers.
Two men would operate a long saw over the sawpit, one above and one inside the pit.
