go to pieces

verb
lose emotional or mental control
6.1 0.25/m
Pronunciation: / …°o ä t…ô ÀàpiÀês…™z / (AmE), / …°…ô ä t…ô ÀàpiÀês…™z / (BrE)
to lose emotional or mental control, often as a result of shock or distress.

Examples

To go to pieces suddenly
After hearing the bad news, she went to pieces in front of everyone.
He goes to pieces whenever he has to speak in public.
It was clear he was going to pieces after the failed exam results.

Synonyms

  • break down
    3.9 5.35/m
    break down often means losing control and crying or collapsing, while go to pieces means a sudden emotional collapse.
  • fall apart
    4.2 4.84/m
    fall apart describes someone losing emotional control from stress or grief, while go to pieces suggests a sudden, shocked collapse.
  • come undone
    5.3 0.35/m
    come undone can mean a person loses emotional control or things fall apart, while go to pieces is about strong panic.
  • break up
    5.0 1.02/m
    break up often describes a longer mental or emotional breakdown that stops daily life, while go to pieces is a quick loss of control.
  • come apart at the seams
    7.2 0.22/m
    come apart at the seams suggests things begin to fail after stability or strain, while go to pieces is sudden emotional collapse.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fall apart - to lose emotional control or become unable to function due to distress
