codlin moth

noun
moth species; larvae infest apples and walnuts
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑd.lɪn mɑːθ / (AmE), / ˈkɒd.lɪn mɒθ / (BrE)
a small grey moth (Cydia pomonella) whose larvae feed inside apples and English walnuts, causing damage to fruit crops

Examples

The codlin moth larvae
Farmers use traps and sprays to control the population of the codlin moth each year.
The larvae of the codlin moth bore into the fruit, making it unsuitable for sale.
The orchard suffered heavy losses after a codlin moth infestation destroyed many apples.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coddling moth - another spelling of 'codlin moth', same species
