wheel and axle

noun
simple machine with wheel and drum for lifting
5.1
Pronunciation: / wil ənd ˈæksəl / (AmE), / wiːl ənd ˈæksl̩ / (BrE)
A simple machine consisting of a wheel and a cylindrical drum or shaft (axle) arranged so that a rope unwinding from the wheel is wound onto the axle, used for lifting or moving objects.

Examples

A wheel and axle machine
Early wells often used a wheel and axle to draw water from deep underground.
Raising the heavy flag was made easy with the help of a wheel and axle mechanism.
The wheel and axle is one of the basic machines studied in physics classes.
