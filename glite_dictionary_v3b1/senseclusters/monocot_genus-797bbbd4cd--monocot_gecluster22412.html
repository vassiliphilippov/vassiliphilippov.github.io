monocot genus

noun
genus of plants with a single cotyledon
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːnəˌkɑt ˈdʒiːnəs / (AmE), / ˈmɒnəkɒt ˈdʒiːnəs / (BrE)
A genus of flowering plants whose members have a single cotyledon (embryonic leaf) in the seed.

Examples

A monocot genus plant
Botanists often identify plants by determining whether they belong to a monocot genus or a dicot genus.
Many grasses are classified under a monocot genus, distinguishing them from dicots.
The lily belongs to a monocot genus characterized by a single seed leaf.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dicot genus - genus of plants with two cotyledons
