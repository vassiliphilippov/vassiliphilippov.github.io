intuitive

adjective
obtained through intuition
6.7 4.5
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈtuː.ə.tɪv/ /; / /ɪn.ˈtjuː.ɪ.tɪv/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈtjuː.ɪ.tɪv/ /; / /ɪn.ˈtʃuː.ɪ.tɪv/ (BrE)
(of knowledge, ideas, feelings, or reactions) known or felt instinctively, without needing to think or reason

Examples

Intuitive feeling
He made an intuitive decision without needing to analyze all the data.
Her intuitive understanding of people's feelings made her a great counselor.
Some animals seem to have an intuitive sense of danger that humans lack.
He had an intuitive sense that something was wrong before anyone said a word.
Her intuitive decision proved to be the correct one, even though she couldn't explain her reasoning.
Many people have an intuitive understanding of fairness from a young age.

Related Terms

  • synonym: instinctive - arising from natural tendency rather than reasoning or observation
  • antonym: logical - based on reasoning or logic rather than intuition
  • antonym: reasoned - based on or guided by conscious reasoning
