Priscoan

noun
the earliest eon in Earth's history
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪskoʊən / (AmE), / ˈprɪskəʊən / (BrE)
the earliest eon in Earth's history, lasting from planetary formation about 4,600 million years ago until the appearance of the oldest known rocks, about 3,800 million years ago, with no evidence of life

Examples

The Priscoan eon
During the Priscoan, conditions on Earth were too harsh for life to develop.
No rocks have been found from the Priscoan eon because Earth's crust was still forming.
Some scientists debate whether the Priscoan eon truly existed as a distinct period.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Hadean - the eon following the Priscoan, from about 4,000 to 4,600 million years ago
