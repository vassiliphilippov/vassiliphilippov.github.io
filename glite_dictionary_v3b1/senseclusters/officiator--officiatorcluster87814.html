officiator

noun
ceremony official
Pronunciation: / əˈfɪʃiˌeɪtər / (AmE), / əˈfɪʃieɪtə / (BrE)
a person who performs a formal ceremony or acts as an official at an event

Examples

The officiator at ceremony
The officiator made sure the graduation ceremony ran smoothly and on time.
At the championship game, the officiator enforced the rules fairly for both teams.
During the award presentation, the officiator announced each winner with enthusiasm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: officiant - a person who leads a religious or formal ceremony
  • antonym: participant - a person who takes part in an event
