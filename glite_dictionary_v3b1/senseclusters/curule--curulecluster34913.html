curule

noun
Roman magistrate's stool
Pronunciation: / ˈkjʊrul /; / ˈkjʊruːl / (AmE), / ˈkjʊəruːl / (BrE)
a special low, folding chair with curved legs used by high-ranking magistrates in ancient Rome, often as a symbol of authority
