civil suit

noun
lawsuit under civil, not criminal, law
3.9 1.36/m
Pronunciation: / Ààs…™v…ôl suÀêt / (AmE), / Ààs…™v.…ôl suÀêt / (BrE)
A lawsuit brought to resolve a dispute involving alleged violations of civil law, rather than criminal law.

Examples

A civil suit case
After the accident, the family initiated a civil suit to seek compensation for damages.
She filed a civil suit against her landlord for breach of contract.
The company faced a civil suit after allegedly violating environmental regulations.

Synonyms

  • civil action
    5.3 0.67/m
    civil action is a formal legal case to protect private rights, while civil suit is the general term for such a civil lawsuit.
  • lawsuit
    4.8 30/m
    lawsuit is any claim brought to court for a legal remedy, while civil suit focuses on disputes under civil, not criminal, law.
  • suit
    5.6 21/m
    suit is the shorter, everyday word for a legal action in court, while civil suit means a case about civil law disputes.
