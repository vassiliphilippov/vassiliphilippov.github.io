Carnegiea gigantea

noun
species of giant cactus (saguaro)
9.8
Pronunciation: / kɑrˈneɪɡiə dʒaɪˈɡæntiə / (AmE), / kɑːˈniːɡiə dʒaɪˈɡantiə / (BrE)
A very large, tree-like cactus native to the deserts of the southwestern United States, known for its thick columnar trunk, sparse branches, white flowers, and edible red fruit; commonly known as the saguaro cactus.
