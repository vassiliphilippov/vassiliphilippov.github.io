sucker

verb
to grow new shoots
7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈsʌ.kər/ (AmE), /ˈsʌ.kə/ (BrE)
to produce new shoots, called suckers, from the base or roots of a plant.

Examples

Roses sucker after pruning
The rose bush began suckering after being cut back in the spring.
Gardeners often remove suckering shoots to keep fruit trees healthy.
If you do not control suckering, the plant may become too dense and crowded.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sprout - to begin to grow new shoots or leaves
  • antonym: wither - to dry up and stop growing
