be on to

verb
aware of someone's intentions or secret
Pronunciation: / bi ˈɑn tu / (AmE), / bi ˈɒn tʊ / (BrE)
to be aware of or have discovered someone's true intentions or secret plans, often with the implication of being close to exposure or capture.

Examples

To be on to someone
I think the police are on to him, so he should be careful.
If you keep making mistakes, your boss will be on to you soon.
The journalist was convinced she was on to a major political scandal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: see through - to realize that someone is trying to deceive you
