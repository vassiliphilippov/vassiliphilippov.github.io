lateralis

noun
species epithet (side)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌlætəˈrælɪs / (AmE), / ˌlætəˈrɑːlɪs / (BrE)
a Latin term used in scientific names to indicate a species with a feature related to the side.

Examples

Species lateralis
The species name lateralis refers to the distinctive stripe along the animal's side.
In scientific classification, lateralis is often used to describe side-related features.
The beetle was named lateralis because of the markings on its lateral surface.
