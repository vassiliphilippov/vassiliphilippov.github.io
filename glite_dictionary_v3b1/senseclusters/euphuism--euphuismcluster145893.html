euphuism

noun
elaborate Elizabethan prose style
Pronunciation: / ˈjuː.fju.ˌɪz.əm /; / ˈjuːfjuˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈjuː.fjʊ.ɪ.zəm /; / ˈjuːfjʊˌɪzəm / (BrE)
a highly ornate and artificial style of writing, especially marked by balance, antithesis, alliteration, and elaborate similes

Examples

The euphuism style
In Elizabethan England, euphuism was admired by some and mocked by others for its affected elegance.
Many critics found the euphuism of his prose excessive and artificial.
The writer adopted a style of euphuism, delighting in ornate language and elaborate metaphors.
Critics accused the poet of using unnecessary euphuism in his latest collection.
The author's reliance on euphuism made the story ornate but difficult to read.
While some admire euphuism for its artistry, others find it tedious and artificial.

Related Terms

  • antonym: plainness - the quality of being simple or unadorned in style
