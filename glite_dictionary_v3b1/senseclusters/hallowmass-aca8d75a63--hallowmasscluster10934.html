Hallowmass

noun
Feast day honoring all saints
Pronunciation: / ˈhæloʊˌmæs / (AmE), / ˈhaləʊˌmæs / (BrE)
A Christian feast day honoring all the saints, traditionally celebrated on November 1st.

Examples

The Hallowmass feast
Families used to gather and light candles on Hallowmass to honor the departed.
Many churches hold special services on Hallowmass to remember the saints.
The tradition of Hallowmass dates back to the 9th century.
