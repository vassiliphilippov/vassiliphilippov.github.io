unconscientiousness

noun
lack of carefulness or dutifulness
8.7 8.1
Pronunciation: /ʌn.kən.ʃɪ.ˈen.ʃəs.nəs/ (AmE), /ʌn.kɒn.ʃɪ.ˈen.ʃəs.nəs/ (BrE)
the quality of not being careful, responsible, or concerned about what is right or expected

Examples

Unconscientiousness at work
His repeated mistakes at work showed a worrying unconscientiousness that alarmed his supervisors.
Teachers are concerned about the growing unconscientiousness among students when it comes to studying.
The project failed partly due to the unconscientiousness of several team members.
His unconscientiousness in handling confidential files put the entire project at risk.
She was frustrated by the unconscientiousness of some team members, who ignored important safety protocols.
The company's culture of unconscientiousness led to repeated breaches of ethical standards.
