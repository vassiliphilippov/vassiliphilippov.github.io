Ashkenazic

adjective
of Central/Eastern European Jews
Pronunciation: / ˌæʃkəˈnɑzɪk / (AmE), / ˌæʃkəˈnɑːzɪk / (BrE)
relating to Jews of Central and Eastern European origin or to their traditions, culture, or language

Examples

Ashkenazic traditions
The community observes Ashkenazic customs during religious holidays.
She learned to read Hebrew using the Ashkenazic pronunciation.
Many Ashkenazic recipes feature dishes like kugel and gefilte fish.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Sephardic - relating to Jews of Spanish, Portuguese, or North African origin
