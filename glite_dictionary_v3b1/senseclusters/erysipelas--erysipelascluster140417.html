erysipelas

noun
acute bacterial skin infection
Pronunciation: / ˌɛrɪˈsɪpələs / (AmE), / ˌɛrɪˈsɪpɪləs / (BrE)
an acute bacterial infection causing deep-red inflammation of the skin, often accompanied by fever and swelling

Examples

Erysipelas skin infection
After surgery, the patient developed erysipelas on his leg, which required antibiotics.
Prompt medical treatment is essential to manage erysipelas and prevent complications.
The bright red, swollen rash on her face was diagnosed as erysipelas by the dermatologist.
