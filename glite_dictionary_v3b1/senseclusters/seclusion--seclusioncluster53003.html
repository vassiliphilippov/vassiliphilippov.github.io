seclusion

noun
isolation from others; privacy
Pronunciation: / sɪˈkluʒən / (AmE), / sɪˈkluːʒən / (BrE)
the state of being private and away from other people; isolation or being hidden from view or contact with others

Examples

In seclusion at home
After months of seclusion, the writer finally emerged with a new novel.
Many monks seek seclusion to focus on meditation and spiritual growth.
The retreat promises a week of peace and seclusion in the mountains.
After the scandal, the actor sought seclusion in the countryside.
She valued her seclusion and rarely invited guests to her home.
The writer preferred seclusion to focus on his new novel without distractions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exposure - the state of being in open view or contact with others
  • synonym: isolation - the state of being separated from others; solitude
  • synonym: privacy - the state of being free from being observed by others
