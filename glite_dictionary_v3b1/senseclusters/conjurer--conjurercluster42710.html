conjurer

noun
person performing magic tricks
8.2 0.19/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëÀên.d í…ô.r…ö / (AmE), / Ààk…ín.d í…ô.r…ô / (BrE)
A person who entertains an audience by performing magic tricks or illusions.

Examples

Stage conjurer
After the show, the conjurer revealed that there was no real magic involved, only skill and practice.
Children gathered around the conjurer at the birthday party, eager to see his next illusion.
The talented conjurer amazed everyone with his card tricks.

Synonyms

  • conjuror
    7.2 0.04/m
    conjuror is an alternative spelling sometimes used in writing, while conjurer names the entertainer who performs magic tricks for audiences.
  • magician
    3.6 7.68/m
    magician is the common word for someone who performs magic for audiences or is believed to have powers, while conjurer is more theatrical.
  • illusionist
    6.9 0.41/m
    illusionist makes large visual tricks and stage illusions to amaze spectators, while conjurer names a performer who uses general magic tricks.
  • prestidigitator
    9.9 0.07/m
    prestidigitator is a formal, rare word that highlights skillful sleight-of-hand, while conjurer is the everyday term for a stage magician.
