Ledum

noun
a genus of evergreen shrubs
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈliː.dəm/ /; / /ˈle.dəm/ (AmE), /ˈliː.dəm/ /; / /ˈle.dəm/ (BrE)
A genus of evergreen shrubs native to northern temperate regions, formerly recognized in the heath family (Ericaceae), now often included in the genus Rhododendron.

Examples

Ledum shrub
Gardeners value Ledum for its aromatic foliage and delicate white flowers.
The botanist collected specimens of Ledum during her expedition in Alaska.
Wild Ledum grows in abundance throughout the boggy areas of northern Canada.
