druidry

noun
druid beliefs and practices
Pronunciation: / ˈdruːɪdri / (AmE), / ˈdruːɪdri / (BrE)
the practices, beliefs, or religion associated with druids, especially as revived or practiced in modern times

Examples

Modern druidry practices
He became interested in druidry after reading about ancient Celtic traditions.
Modern druidry often focuses on a deep respect for nature and seasonal festivals.
Many people practice druidry as a spiritual path inspired by ancient beliefs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: druidism - the religion or philosophy of druids
  • synonym: paganism - religious practices outside the main world religions, often nature-based
