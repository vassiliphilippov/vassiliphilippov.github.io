minnow

noun
small European freshwater fish
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪnoʊ / (AmE), / ˈmɪnəʊ / (BrE)
a very small freshwater fish of the carp family, commonly found in European gravel-bottomed streams and ponds.

Examples

Small minnow fish
Fishermen often use minnows as bait for larger fish.
The children caught a tiny minnow in the shallow stream.
The pond was teeming with small silver minnows darting between the rocks.
