posterior pituitary gland

noun
rear lobe of the pituitary gland
5.4
Pronunciation: / poʊˈstɪriɚ pɪˈtuːɪˌtɛri ɡlænd / (AmE), / pɒˈstɪəri pɪˈtjuːɪˌtəri ɡlænd / (BrE)
the rear part of the pituitary gland, responsible for releasing hormones such as oxytocin and vasopressin into the bloodstream

Examples

Posterior pituitary gland hormones
Hormones released by the posterior pituitary gland play a crucial role in water balance and childbirth.
Injury to the posterior pituitary gland can lead to disorders like diabetes insipidus.
The posterior pituitary gland stores and releases oxytocin and vasopressin produced by the hypothalamus.
