lose face

verb
suffer a loss of respect or dignity
3.1
Pronunciation: / luːz feɪs / (AmE), / luːz feɪs / (BrE)
to suffer a loss of respect or dignity; to be embarrassed or humiliated in the eyes of others

Examples

To lose face publicly
He apologized quickly so he wouldn't lose face in front of his team.
If you admit the mistake, you might lose face with your clients.
The company tried to hide the error to avoid losing face.

Related Terms

  • antonym: save face - to avoid embarrassment or preserve dignity
