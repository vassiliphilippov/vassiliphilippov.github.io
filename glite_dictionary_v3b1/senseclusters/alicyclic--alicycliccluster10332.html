alicyclic

adjective
non-aromatic ringed
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌæləˈsaɪklɪk / (AmE), / ˌælɪˈsɪklɪk / (BrE)
describing organic compounds that have ring structures but are not aromatic

Examples

Alicyclic compound
Cyclohexane is a classic example of an alicyclic compound found in organic chemistry.
Unlike benzene, alicyclic compounds do not have delocalized electrons in their rings.
Many natural products contain alicyclic structures that are important for their biological activity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aromatic - describing compounds with stable ring structures and delocalized electrons
  • synonym: cyclic - having a ring structure in chemistry
