plexus myentericus

noun
nerve network in digestive tract wall
Pronunciation: / ˌplɛksəs ˌmaɪənˈtɛrɪkəs / (AmE), / ˌplɛksəs ˌmaɪənˈtɛrɪkəs / (BrE)
A network of unmyelinated nerve fibers and postganglionic autonomic cell bodies found in the muscular layer of the esophagus, stomach, and intestines, regulating gastrointestinal motility.

Examples

The plexus myentericus network
A biopsy revealed inflammation around the plexus myentericus in the patient's intestinal tissue.
Damage to the plexus myentericus can lead to serious digestive motility disorders.
The plexus myentericus plays a crucial role in coordinating smooth muscle contractions throughout the gut.
