preselection

noun
automatic call routing
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːsəˈlɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːsɪˈlɛkʃən / (BrE)
the process of automatically routing telephone calls through a chosen carrier or service provider, arranged in advance by the user or regulator

Examples

Call preselection
With preselection, all outgoing calls are automatically routed through the preferred service provider.
The regulator introduced preselection to give consumers more control over their phone bills.
After enabling preselection, she no longer had to dial extra codes to use her chosen carrier.

Related Terms

  • synonym: call forwarding - a service that redirects incoming calls to another number
  • antonym: manual selection - choosing a carrier or provider for each call individually
