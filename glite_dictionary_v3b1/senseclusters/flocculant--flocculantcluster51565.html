flocculant

adjective
causing clumping
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈflɑːkjələnt / (AmE), / ˈflɒkjʊlənt / (BrE)
causing or tending to make small particles clump together into larger masses or flocs.

Examples

Flocculant effect
The addition of a flocculant agent helped clear the water by binding the suspended particles.
Engineers selected a highly flocculant chemical to improve the efficiency of the treatment process.
A flocculant solution was used to make the fine sediments settle more quickly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coagulating - causing particles or substances to come together and form a mass
  • antonym: dispersing - causing particles to separate and spread out
