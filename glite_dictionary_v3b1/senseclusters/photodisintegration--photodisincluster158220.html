photodisintegration

noun
nuclear photon breakup
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊtoʊˌdɪsɪnˌtɪɡrəˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊˌdɪsɪnˌtɪɡrəˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a process in which an atomic nucleus absorbs a photon and splits into smaller parts

Examples

Photodisintegration of nuclei
High-energy gamma rays can cause photodisintegration of atomic nuclei in stars.
Scientists study photodisintegration to understand how elements are formed in supernovae.
The process of photodisintegration plays a key role in stellar evolution.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fusion - joining of two atomic nuclei to form a heavier nucleus
  • synonym: photofission - splitting of a nucleus into two parts by photon absorption
