daydream

verb
to indulge in pleasant fantasies while awake
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪˌdriːm / (AmE), / ˈdeɪˌdriːm / (BrE)
to have pleasant, wishful, or distracting thoughts or fantasies while awake

Examples

To daydream in class
He would often daydream about traveling the world instead of listening in class.
She caught herself daydreaming during the boring meeting.
Whenever he is bored, he just daydreams about being a famous musician.
He would often daydream during long meetings at work.
She sat beneath the tree, daydreaming about her summer vacation.
Try not to daydream when you have important tasks to finish.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concentrate - to focus one's attention or mental effort entirely on a task
  • synonym: fantasize - to imagine things that are impossible or improbable
