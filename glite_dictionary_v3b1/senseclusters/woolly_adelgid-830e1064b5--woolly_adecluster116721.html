woolly adelgid

noun
insect pest of hemlock trees
Pronunciation: / ˈwʊli əˈdɛldʒɪd / (AmE), / ˈwʊli əˈdɛldʒɪd / (BrE)
a small sap-sucking insect that feeds on hemlocks, noted for its white, wool-like egg sacs that resemble tufts of cotton

Examples

The woolly adelgid insect

You can spot the presence of woolly adelgids by the white cottony masses on hemlock branches.
