porosis

noun
degree of porosity
Pronunciation: / pəˈroʊsɪs / (AmE), / pəˈrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
the quality or condition of being porous, especially the proportion of open spaces or voids in a material

Examples

Material porosis
The porosis of the ceramic filter allows water to pass through while trapping impurities.
Geologists measured the porosis of the rock to determine its ability to store oil.
High porosis in soil can improve drainage and root growth for plants.
