shudder

verb
to shake or tremble suddenly
3.7 11/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆÊƒÊŒdÉš /; / ËˆÊƒÊŒdÉ™r / (AmE), / ËˆÊƒÊŒdÉ™ / (BrE)
to shake or tremble suddenly and uncontrollably, usually because of fear, cold, or strong emotion

Examples

To shudder with fear
He couldn't help but shudder at the thought of entering the cold water.
She began to shudder as the icy wind blew through the open window.
The old house made me shudder with its eerie silence.
He couldn't help but shudder when he heard nails scraping on a chalkboard.
She felt a chill run down her spine and began to shudder with fear.
The audience shuddered at the terrifying scene in the movie.

Synonyms

  • shiver
    1.2 13/m
    shiver is a quick, small shaking from cold or fear, while shudder is a stronger, sudden tremor from shock or horror.
  • tremble
    2.5 18/m
    tremble is a slight, often long shaking from anxiety or weakness, while shudder is a sudden, stronger shake from fear or cold.

Related Terms

  • antonym: freeze - to become suddenly motionless, often from fear (opposite reaction)
  • synonym: quake - to shake or vibrate, often from shock or fear
  • synonym: shiver - to tremble typically from cold or fear
  • synonym: tremble - to shake involuntarily, especially as a result of anxiety or excitement
