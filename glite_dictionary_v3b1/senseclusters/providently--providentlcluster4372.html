providently

adverb
with foresight; planning for the future
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑː.vɪ.dənt.li / (AmE), / ˈprɒ.vɪ.dənt.li / (BrE)
in a way that shows careful foresight or preparation for the future, especially by saving or planning ahead

Examples

To providently save money
By providently setting aside supplies, the community was prepared for the harsh winter.
He providently avoided unnecessary expenses, so he could support his family during difficult times.
She invested providently for her retirement, ensuring financial security in old age.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carefully - with caution or attention
  • antonym: rashly - without careful consideration, recklessly
