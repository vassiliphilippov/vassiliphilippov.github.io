hooded

adjective
with hood-like marking
3.7
Pronunciation: /ˈhʊd.ɪd/ (AmE), /ˈhʊd.ɪd/ (BrE)
(of an animal) having a marking, fold of skin, or other feature on the head or neck that looks like a hood

Examples

Hooded snake
The hooded snake raised its head, displaying the wide flap of skin around its neck.
A hooded crow landed on the fence, its black head contrasting with grey feathers.
She admired the hooded orchid, which had a petal shaped like a tiny hood.
The hooded cobra spread its neck to warn the intruder.
Some birds have a hooded appearance due to special feathers on their heads.
The lizard displayed its hooded neck to appear larger to predators.

Related Terms

  • synonym: capped - having a top or head that is covered or marked
  • synonym: crested - having a raised or prominent crest or tuft
