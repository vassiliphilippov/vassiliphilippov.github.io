cyano group

noun
the −CN functional group in chemistry
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪˌænoʊ ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈsaɪ.ə.nəʊ ɡruːp / (BrE)
a univalent chemical group consisting of a carbon atom triple-bonded to a nitrogen atom, represented as −CN; commonly found in organic compounds

Examples

A cyano group in chemistry
Many pharmaceuticals incorporate a cyano group to modify their biological activity.
The infrared spectrum shows a strong absorption due to the cyano group.
The presence of a cyano group in the molecule increases its reactivity.
