hemorrhagic septicemia

noun
acute infectious disease causing pneumonia and blood infection
8.9
Pronunciation: /hem.ə.ˈrædʒ.ɪk sep.tɪ.ˈsiː.mi.ə/ (AmE), /hem.ə.ˈrædʒ.ɪk sep.tɪ.ˈsiː.mi.ə/ (BrE)
an acute infectious disease in animals, especially cattle and buffalo, characterized by pneumonia and blood infection, often resulting in sudden death

Examples

Hemorrhagic septicemia in cattle
Outbreaks of hemorrhagic septicemia can cause significant losses in livestock populations.
The sudden death of several buffalo was attributed to hemorrhagic septicemia.
Veterinarians vaccinated the herd to prevent hemorrhagic septicemia during the rainy season.
