drown

verb
kill by submerging in water
4.8
Pronunciation: / draʊn / (AmE), / draʊn / (BrE)
to cause a person or animal to die by holding them under water or another liquid so they cannot breathe

Examples

To drown a kitten
It is illegal to drown unwanted animals in most countries today.
She was horrified to learn someone had drowned the kittens in the river.
The farmer drowned the sick puppies, unable to find homes for them.
The suspect admitted to drowning the injured animal in a nearby canal.
She was accused of drowning her sorrows in alcohol after the breakup.
The villain in the story tried drowning his enemies to get rid of them.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rescue - to save from a dangerous or distressing situation, especially from water
  • synonym: suffocate - to die or kill by preventing breathing
