esophagoscope

noun
instrument for examining the esophagus
Pronunciation: / ɪˈsɑːfəɡəˌskoʊp /; / iːˈsɑːfəɡəˌskoʊp / (AmE), / ɪˈsɒfəɡəˌskəʊp /; / ɪˌsɒfəˈɡəʊskəʊp / (BrE)
a medical instrument used to examine the inside of the esophagus

Examples

An esophagoscope device
After the accident, an esophagoscope was inserted to inspect the extent of internal injuries.
Modern esophagoscopes provide a clear, detailed view of the esophageal lining.
The doctor used an esophagoscope to identify the source of the patient's swallowing difficulties.
A skilled doctor used an oesophagoscope to inspect the patient's esophagus for signs of disease.
Modern oesophagoscopes allow for both diagnostic and minor surgical procedures within the esophagus.
The oesophagoscope was carefully inserted to avoid causing discomfort to the patient.
