conjunction

noun
simultaneous occurrence of events
2.7 6.1
Pronunciation: / kənˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / kənˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
the occurrence of two or more events at the same time; coincidence in time or simultaneous happening, especially in technical contexts.

Examples

Conjunction of events
In particle physics, a conjunction of two signals triggers the detection system.
The conjunction of the celebrations and the public holiday made the city unusually lively.
The conjunction of the two eclipses was a rare astronomical event.
