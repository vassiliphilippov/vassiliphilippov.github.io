Upanishad

noun
ancient Hindu sacred mystical/philosophical text
Pronunciation: / uˈpɑːnɪˌʃɑːd / (AmE), / uˈpænɪʃad / (BrE)
any of a group of ancient Hindu sacred texts of a mystical and philosophical nature, exploring metaphysical questions; a key scripture underlying Vedanta philosophy

Examples

The Upanishads texts
Many scholars consider the Upanishads to be the culmination of Vedic thought.
She devoted years to the study of a particular Upanishad and its metaphysical ideas.
The teachings of the Upanishads have influenced many modern philosophers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Veda - ancient holy texts forming the foundation of Hinduism
  • degree higher: Vedanta - Hindu philosophy derived from the teachings of the Upanishads
