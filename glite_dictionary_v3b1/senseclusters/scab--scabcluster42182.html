scab

verb
work while others are on strike
6.7
Pronunciation: / skæb / (AmE), / skæb / (BrE)
to work or be employed as a replacement for striking workers

Examples

To scab at work
Anyone who decides to scab during the strike will face strong criticism from the union.
Several workers crossed the picket line to scab for higher pay.
She refused to scab even though she needed the money desperately.
During the strike, several local workers scabbed and went back to work for the company.
He refused to scab even though he needed the money.
Some employees secretly scabbed while their colleagues protested outside.

Related Terms

  • synonym: strikebreak - to work during a strike, breaking the strike
  • synonym: strikebreaker - a person who works or is employed in place of others who are on strike
  • antonym: support - to help or stand with a group, such as striking workers
