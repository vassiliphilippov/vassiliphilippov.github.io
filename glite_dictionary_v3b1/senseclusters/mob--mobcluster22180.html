mob

verb
press together in a crowd
5.5 0.08/m
Pronunciation: / mÉ‘b / (AmE), / mÉ’b / (BrE)
to crowd around or overwhelm someone or something as a group

Examples

Fans mob a star
During the sale, customers mobbed the store, hoping to find bargains.

The reporters mobbed the politician with questions after the press conference.
Security was called when people began mobbing the store during the big sale.
