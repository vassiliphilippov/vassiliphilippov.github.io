eclipse

verb
to cause an eclipse (of a celestial body)
Pronunciation: / ɪˈklɪps / (AmE), / ɪˈklɪps / (BrE)
to cause an eclipse of (a celestial body), by having one celestial body move into the shadow of another

Examples

To eclipse the sun
As the planet passed in front, it momentarily eclipsed the distant star.
In rare cases, one moon can eclipse another as seen from Earth.
The Moon will eclipse the Sun during tomorrow's solar eclipse.
