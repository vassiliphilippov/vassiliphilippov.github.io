Madame de Pompadour

proper noun
chief mistress of King Louis XV of France
Pronunciation: / mæˈdæm də ˈpɒmpədʊr / (AmE), / ˈmadəm də ˈpɒmpədʊə / (BrE)
(1721–1764) The title and name of Jeanne Antoinette Poisson, a French noblewoman who was the chief mistress of King Louis XV and an influential figure in his court.

Examples

Madame de Pompadour court influence
In the 18th century, Madame de Pompadour was considered one of the most powerful women in Europe.
Many art historians credit Madame de Pompadour with shaping the Rococo style at the French court.
The influence of Madame de Pompadour extended beyond personal relationships to political and artistic spheres.
