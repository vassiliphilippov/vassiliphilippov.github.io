pond bald cypress

noun
wood from the pond bald cypress tree
Pronunciation: / pɑnd bɔld ˈsaɪprəs / (AmE), / pɒnd bɔːld ˈsaɪprəs / (BrE)
the wood of a cypress tree (Taxodium ascendens) native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in swamps and river bottoms, notable for its expanded trunk base

Examples

Pond bald cypress wood
Old cabins in the South were sometimes constructed using durable pond bald cypress planks.
The lumber mill specializes in high-quality pond bald cypress used for building projects in humid areas.
Water-resistant pond bald cypress is valued for boat building and dock construction.
