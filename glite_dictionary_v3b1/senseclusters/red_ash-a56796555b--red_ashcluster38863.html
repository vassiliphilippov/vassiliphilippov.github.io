red ash

noun
a North American ash tree species
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛd ˈæʃ / (AmE), / ˌrɛd ˈaʃ / (BrE)
a North American ash tree (Fraxinus pennsylvanica), with velvety young branches and lower leaf surfaces, often found in moist areas

Examples

A red ash tree
Botanists studied the leaves of a red ash to understand its adaptation to wet soils.
Many birds nest in the branches of the red ash each spring.
The park is home to several red ash trees with their distinctive velvety twigs.
