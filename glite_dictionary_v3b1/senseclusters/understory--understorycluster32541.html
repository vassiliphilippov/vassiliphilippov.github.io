understory

noun
forest layer below canopy
5.1 0.72/m
Pronunciation: / Àà ånd…öÀåst…îri / (AmE), / Àà ånd…ôÀåst…îÀêri / (BrE)
the layer of plants and small trees growing beneath the main canopy of a forest

Examples

Forest understory
Many birds and insects make their homes in the understory of the rainforest.
The understory receives less sunlight than the canopy, so its plants are often shade-tolerant.
Researchers studied the diversity of plants growing in the understory after the fire.

Synonyms

  • undergrowth
    3.9 1.20/m
    undergrowth is a thick, tangled mass of low shrubs and plants, while understory names the whole lower plant layer under the canopy.
  • underbrush
    5.6 1.32/m
    underbrush is the tangle of small bushes and saplings you see on the forest floor, while understory means the entire lower plant layer.
  • underwood
    8.0 0.83/m
    underwood refers to low-growing trees and bushes found beneath taller trees, while understory names the full lower plant layer.

Related Terms

  • antonym: canopy - the uppermost layer of branches and leaves in a forest
  • degree lower: forest floor - the bottom layer of a forest, consisting of soil and decomposing leaves
