composite school

noun
secondary school for all abilities
6.0
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpɑzɪt skul / (AmE), / kəmˈpɒzɪt skuːl / (BrE)
a large secondary school in Britain or Canada that admits children of all abilities, without academic selection

Examples

A composite school in Britain
After moving to Canada, Helen attended a composite school that welcomed students of all academic backgrounds.
The local composite school offers a wide range of courses to cater to its diverse student population.
Unlike grammar schools, a composite school does not select students based on their exam results.
