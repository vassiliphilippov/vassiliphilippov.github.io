expediently

adverb
in a practical or convenient manner
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈspiːdiəntli / (AmE), / ɪkˈspiːdiəntli / (BrE)
in a practical or effective way, often without considering if it is right or fair

Examples

To act expediently
Although not entirely honest, she completed the task expediently to meet the deadline.
He made the decision expediently, without considering the long-term consequences.
The company acted expediently to resolve the issue and avoid public embarrassment.
The manager resolved the issue expediently to keep the project on schedule.
They acted expediently to prevent the problem from getting worse.
The company expediently addressed customer complaints to maintain its reputation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conveniently - in a way that is easy and suitable for a particular purpose
  • synonym: efficiently - in a way that achieves maximum productivity with minimum wasted effort
  • antonym: improperly - in a way that is not proper
  • antonym: inefficiently - in a way that wastes time or resources
  • synonym: practically - in a practical manner
