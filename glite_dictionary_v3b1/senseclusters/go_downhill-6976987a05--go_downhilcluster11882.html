go downhill

verb
to get worse in quality or condition
4.5
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ daʊnˈhɪl / (AmE), / ɡəʊ daʊnˈhɪl / (BrE)
to gradually become worse in quality, condition, or performance

Examples

To go downhill fast
After retiring, his fitness began to go downhill rapidly.
The service at the restaurant started to go downhill after the new management took over.
When her partner moved away, her mood seemed to go downhill.
Ever since the new management took over, the service has gone downhill.
The once-popular show has gone downhill in recent seasons.
Their friendship really went downhill after the argument.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decline - to decrease in quality or strength
  • synonym: deteriorate - to become progressively worse
  • antonym: improve - to become better
