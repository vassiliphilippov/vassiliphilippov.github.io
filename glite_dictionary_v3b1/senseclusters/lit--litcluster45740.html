lit

adjective
illuminated with artificial light
1.0 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / lÉªt / (AmE), / lÉªt / (BrE)
provided with light, illuminated, or burning

Examples

Lit room
At night, the bridge is beautifully lit up with colorful lights.
She prefers reading in a well-lit room to avoid straining her eyes.
The hallway was lit by a single small lamp in the corner.
A lit fireplace made the cabin warm and cozy.
She carefully picked up the lit lantern to light her way through the garden.
The room was filled with lit candles for the ceremony.

Synonyms

  • illuminated
    4.2 0.02/m
    illuminated means made bright by artificial light in places like streets or rooms, while lit is a more general term.
  • well-lighted
    2.6 0.54/m
    well-lighted means a place has plenty of artificial lamps and looks bright at night, while lit can mean any kind of light.
  • alight
    6.1 0.46/m
    alight often describes something burning or touched by flame, whereas lit can mean simply provided with light without fire.
  • aglow
    5.5 0.56/m
    aglow means shining with a soft, gentle light like candles or embers, while lit is broader and can mean any light.
  • radiant
    4.1 4.39/m
    radiant means sending out strong warm light or joy like a smiling face in sun, whereas lit simply names having light.
  • bright
    1.0 45/m
    bright means giving or reflecting a lot of light or color and often feels strong, while lit just means supplied with light.
  • ablaze
    5.5 1.16/m
    ablaze describes something shining fiercely or on fire with strong flames, while lit is more neutral and less intense.
  • blazing
    3.5 0.04/m
    blazing means shining or burning with very strong light and heat, while lit can mean being lit by any weaker source.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dark - not illuminated; lacking light
  • synonym: illuminated - lit up by light, especially artificial
  • synonym: lighted - set alight or burning
  • antonym: unlit - not set alight; not burning
