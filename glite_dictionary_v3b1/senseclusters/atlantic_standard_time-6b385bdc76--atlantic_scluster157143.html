Atlantic Standard Time

noun
time zone: UTC−4 in parts of the Atlantic region
Pronunciation: / əˈtlæn.tɪk ˈstæn.dɚd taɪm / (AmE), / ətˈlæn.tɪk ˈstæn.dəd tʌɪm / (BrE)
the standard time in the Atlantic Time Zone, which is four hours behind Coordinated Universal Time, used in Puerto Rico, the Virgin Islands, Bermuda, and the Canadian Maritime Provinces

Examples

Atlantic Standard Time zone
Puerto Rico observes Atlantic Standard Time throughout the entire year.
Travelers to the Canadian Maritimes should set their clocks to Atlantic Standard Time upon arrival.
When planning calls with Bermuda, remember they are on Atlantic Standard Time.
