capital ship

noun
large, principal warship in a navy
Pronunciation: / ˈkæpɪtl ʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈkapɪtəl ʃɪp / (BrE)
a large warship of the most important class in a navy, typically heavily armed and armored, such as a battleship or aircraft carrier

Examples

A large capital ship
Aircraft carriers are considered modern capital ships due to their size and strategic importance.
During World War II, the loss of a single capital ship could alter the balance of power.
The fleet's flagship was a capital ship equipped with advanced weaponry and radar.

Related Terms

  • synonym: battleship - a heavily armored warship with large-caliber guns
  • degree lower: cruiser - a class of warship below the size of a battleship
