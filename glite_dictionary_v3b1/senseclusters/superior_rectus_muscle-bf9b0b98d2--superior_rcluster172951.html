superior rectus muscle

noun
eye muscle moving eyeball upward and inward
Pronunciation: / səˈpɪriɚ ˈrɛktəs ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / sjuːˈpɪəriə ˈrɛktəs ˈmʌsl̩ / (BrE)
the muscle in the eye responsible for moving the eyeball upward and medially

Examples

Superior rectus muscle movement
Damage to the superior rectus muscle can prevent the eye from moving upward properly.
During the eye examination, the doctor tested the function of the superior rectus muscle.
The superior rectus muscle works together with other ocular muscles to direct gaze.
