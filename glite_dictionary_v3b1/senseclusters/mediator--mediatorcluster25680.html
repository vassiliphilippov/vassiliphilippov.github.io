mediator

noun
person who helps resolve disputes
4.0 4.83/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàmiÀêdiÀåe…™t…ôr / (AmE), / ÀàmiÀêdiÀêe…™t…ô / (BrE)
a person who helps two or more people or groups reach an agreement by working with them to settle their differences

Examples

A mediator helps people
A skilled mediator can turn a heated argument into a constructive conversation.
During the intense negotiations, the mediator maintained a neutral position at all times.
The mediator helped the two companies reach a mutually beneficial agreement.

Synonyms

  • intermediator
    7.7 0.00/m
    an intermediator negotiates between sides to build a deal and keep talks moving, while mediator aims to settle differences through discussion.
  • go-between
    4.9 0.63/m
    a go-between passes messages and makes introductions in informal disputes, while mediator leads formal talks to resolve serious differences.
  • conciliator
    7.0 0.13/m
    a conciliator offers calm suggestions and reduces anger between sides in formal talks, while mediator helps both sides reach an agreement.
  • reconciler
    6.3 0.03/m
    a reconciler works to restore friendly relations and trust after a fight, while mediator focuses on making an agreement between parties.
  • peacemaker
    3.7 1.09/m
    a peacemaker tries to stop fights and create lasting peace in communities or nations, while mediator helps groups settle specific disputes.
  • intercessor
    8.8 0.32/m

  • relator
    7.4 0.01/m

  • harmonizer
    5.2 0.01/m
