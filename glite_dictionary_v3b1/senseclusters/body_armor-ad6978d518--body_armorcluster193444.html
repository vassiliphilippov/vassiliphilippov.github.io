body armor

noun
protective clothing for the body
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑdi ˌɑrmər /; / ˈbɑːdi ˌɑːrmər / (AmE), / ˈbɒdi ˌɑːmə / (BrE)
protective clothing made from strong materials, designed to absorb or deflect physical attacks and safeguard the wearer's body, especially in combat or hazardous situations.

Examples

Modern body armor
Modern body armor is lightweight yet strong enough to stop most pistol bullets.
Police officers wore body armor during the dangerous raid to protect themselves from gunfire.
The soldier credited his body armor with saving his life on the battlefield.
Modern soldiers are often issued lightweight body armour for protection in the field.
The museum displayed a medieval suit of body armour among its ancient weapons.
The police officer wore body armour during the dangerous operation.
