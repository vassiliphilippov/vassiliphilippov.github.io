nucleosome

noun
DNA-protein unit
Pronunciation: / ˈnuːkliəˌsoʊm / (AmE), / ˈnjuːkliəˌsəʊm / (BrE)
a structural unit in the cell nucleus where a segment of DNA is wrapped around proteins (histones), helping to package DNA

Examples

A nucleosome unit
A nucleosome helps package long strands of DNA into the small space of the cell nucleus.
Scientists study how nucleosomes affect gene expression and DNA accessibility.
Each nucleosome is made of DNA wrapped around a core of histone proteins.
A nucleosome helps package long strands of DNA into the cell nucleus.
Scientists study how nucleosomes affect gene expression and DNA accessibility.
Each nucleosome consists of DNA wrapped around a core of histone proteins.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chromatin - the material in cell nuclei consisting of DNA and proteins
  • synonym: histone - a type of protein around which DNA is wrapped in nucleosomes
