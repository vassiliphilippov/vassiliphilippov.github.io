slack

noun
soft wet area of low land
1.0
Pronunciation: / slæk / (AmE), / slæk / (BrE)
a soft, wet area of low-lying land that sinks underfoot, often found in marshes or dunes

Examples

A muddy slack
Hikers avoided the slack after heavy rain, knowing how muddy and treacherous it could become.

Wildflowers grew in abundance around the edges of the slack near the coast.
