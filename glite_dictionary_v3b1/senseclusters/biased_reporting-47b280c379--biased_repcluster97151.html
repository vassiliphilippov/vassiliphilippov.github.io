biased reporting

noun
news coverage influenced by personal opinions
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪəst rɪˈpɔrtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbaɪəst rɪˈpɔːtɪŋ / (BrE)
news coverage in which information is presented with a particular tendency or personal opinion, rather than impartially

Examples

Biased reporting in news
Concerns have grown over the biased reporting of certain television channels during election season.
Many critics accused the newspaper of biased reporting in its coverage of the protests.
The ethics class discussed the dangers of biased reporting to public trust in journalism.

Related Terms

  • antonym: objective reporting - news coverage aiming to be factual and unbiased
