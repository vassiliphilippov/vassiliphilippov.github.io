underfunding

noun
insufficient funding
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈfʌndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈfʌndɪŋ / (BrE)
the situation or condition of not having enough money to support a project, service, or organization

Examples

Chronic underfunding
The school faced years of underfunding, resulting in outdated textbooks and equipment.
Many public services struggle because of chronic underfunding from the government.
The museum's underfunding led to staff layoffs and reduced opening hours.
Many schools struggle because of chronic underfunding from local and national governments.
The museum's underfunding has led to staff reductions and fewer exhibitions.
Healthcare professionals protested against the underfunding of essential medical services.
The school struggled with underfunding and could not afford new textbooks for its students.
Many public services suffer from chronic underfunding, affecting their ability to help the community.
Due to underfunding, the research project was delayed for several months.

Related Terms

  • antonym: adequate funding - having enough money to operate properly
  • synonym: deficit - the amount by which something is too small, especially money
  • antonym: funding - the act of providing money for a particular purpose
  • antonym: overfunding - the act of providing more money than is needed
  • synonym: shortfall - a lack of something needed or expected
  • antonym: surplus - an amount of something left over when requirements have been met
  • synonym: underinvestment - the act of investing less money than is needed
