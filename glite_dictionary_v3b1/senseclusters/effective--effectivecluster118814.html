(of a law, rule, etc.) in operation or in force; coming into use or producing a definite result.

Examples

Effective from Monday
Once the contract is signed, it will be effective for one year.
The new policy becomes effective from next Monday.
The smoking ban is effective immediately, according to the official statement.

Synonyms

  • in force
    3.9 1.71/m
    in force is used of laws that are currently operative and valid, while effective means working or producing a clear result.
  • in effect
    3.7 4.49/m
    in effect often describes a law or rule that applies now, while effective means something actually produces a clear result in practice.
  • effectual
    7.5 0.03/m
    effectual means producing the intended legal result, while effective is a more common word for working or having force.
