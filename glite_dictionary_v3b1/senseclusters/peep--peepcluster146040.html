peep

verb
look quickly and secretly
3.2 0.95/m
Pronunciation: / pip / (AmE), / piÀêp / (BrE)
to look quickly and secretly, or to cause something to appear briefly or partially

Examples

To peep through curtains
He couldn't resist peeping at his birthday presents early.
She tried to peep through the keyhole without being noticed.
The children peeped behind the curtain to watch the magician set up.
He peeped his head around the door to check if the room was empty.
She peeped her hand through the curtains to wave at him.
The puppy peeped its nose out from under the blanket.

Synonyms

  • peek
    1.1 7.36/m
    peek is a quick, secret look into a place, while peep often also means something appears briefly or partly.
  • glance
    1.5 47/m
    glance is a brief, open look at something, while peep is a secret or partial view hidden from others.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glance - to take a brief look
  • synonym: peek - to look quickly or furtively
  • synonym: poke - to push or thrust (something) quickly or partly through an opening
