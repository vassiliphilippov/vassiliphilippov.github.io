giant star

noun
large, bright, low-density star
2.8 6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒaɪənt stɑr / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪənt stɑː / (BrE)
a very bright star, much larger and less dense than the Sun, typically at a late stage in stellar evolution

Examples

After exhausting its hydrogen, a star may expand into a giant star phase.
Astronomers study the life cycle of a giant star to learn about stellar evolution.
Betelgeuse is a well-known example of a giant star in our galaxy.
