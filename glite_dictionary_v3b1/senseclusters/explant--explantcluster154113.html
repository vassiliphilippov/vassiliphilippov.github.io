explant

verb
to remove tissue
8.5
Pronunciation: /ɪk.ˈsplænt/ (AmE), /ɪk.ˈsplɑːnt/ (BrE)
to remove a device, organ, or tissue from a body or organism for study, research, or transplantation

Examples

To explant an organ
The surgeon will explant the damaged heart valve for further examination.
Researchers explant tissue samples to study cell growth in controlled environments.
After the device failed, doctors decided to explant it from the patient.
The surgeon needed to explant the device after signs of infection appeared.
Doctors may explant a prosthesis if it is no longer functioning properly.
After several complications, the team decided to explant the artificial valve.
Scientists explant tissue samples from animals to study cell growth in the laboratory.
The surgeon needed to explant the organ carefully to avoid damaging surrounding structures.
After the procedure, researchers will explant cells for further genetic analysis.

  • antonym: implant - to insert or fix something, especially tissue, into a living body
  • synonym: remove - to take something away from a place or position
