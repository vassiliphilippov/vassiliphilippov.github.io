off-day

noun
a day when things go poorly
Pronunciation: / ˈɔf ˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˈɒf ˌdeɪ / (BrE)
A day on which a person is not performing at their usual level or when things go poorly.

Examples

An off-day in sports
Despite preparing well, she had an off-day and missed several easy shots.
Everyone has an off-day sometimes, so try not to be too hard on yourself.
It felt like an off-day for the whole team, as nothing seemed to go right.
