thixotropic

adjective
becomes less thick when stirred
Pronunciation: / ˌθɪksəˈtroʊpɪk / (AmE), / ˌθɪksəˈtrɒpɪk / (BrE)
describing a substance that becomes less viscous when shaken, stirred, or otherwise disturbed, and thickens again when left to stand

Examples

A thixotropic gel
The gel is thixotropic, so it spreads easily when you stir it.
Some paints are thixotropic, making them ideal for smooth application on walls.
The thixotropic properties of the fluid help it flow under pressure but remain thick at rest.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rheopectic - becoming more viscous when shaken or stirred
  • synonym: shear-thinning - becoming less viscous when subjected to force
