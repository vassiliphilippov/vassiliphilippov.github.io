Eulerian

adjective
graph with special trail
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɔɪˈlɛriən / (AmE), / ɔɪˈlɪəriən / (BrE)
describing a graph that contains a trail or circuit visiting every edge exactly once

Examples

Eulerian circuit
A graph is called Eulerian if it has a trail that uses every edge exactly once.
The students learned how to determine whether a network is Eulerian in their mathematics class.
Finding an Eulerian circuit is a classic problem in graph theory.
