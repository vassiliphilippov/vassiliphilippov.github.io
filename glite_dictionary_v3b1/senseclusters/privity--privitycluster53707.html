privity

noun
legal relationship
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪvəti / (AmE), / ˈprɪvɪti / (BrE)
a legal relationship or connection between parties that is necessary for certain rights or obligations to exist

Examples

Privity of contract
The court found that there was no privity between the two companies involved in the dispute.
Without privity, a third party cannot sue for breach of contract.
Law students often study cases that explain the concept of privity in contract law.
Only those with privity to the contract could enforce its terms in court.
The lawsuit was dismissed because the plaintiff lacked privity with the defendant.
Legal privity is required for one party to sue another over a breach of contract.

Related Terms

  • synonym: confidentiality - the state of keeping information secret or private
  • synonym: contractual relationship - a legal connection between parties based on a contract
  • synonym: liability - legal responsibility for something
  • antonym: publicity - the state of being public or open to all
