anomie

noun
state of isolation due to lack of social norms
Pronunciation: / ˈænəˌmi / (AmE), / ˈænəmi /; / ˈanəmi / (BrE)
a feeling or state of isolation and anxiety caused by the breakdown or absence of social norms and values, either in a person or in society

Examples

Social anomie feeling
After losing his job and community ties, he experienced deep anomie and struggled to find meaning.
Periods of rapid social change can lead to widespread feelings of anomie in society.
Sociologists study anomie to understand the effects of weakened social bonds.
After the collapse of the government, widespread anomie led to lawlessness and unrest.
Durkheim introduced the concept of anomie to explain high suicide rates during periods of social upheaval.
Sociologists have studied the rise of anomie in rapidly changing urban environments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alienation - the state of being isolated from a group or activity
  • synonym: normlessness - lack of normative social standards
