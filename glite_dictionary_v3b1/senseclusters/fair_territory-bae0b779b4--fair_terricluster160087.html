(in baseball) the part of the playing field between the first and third base lines, where a batted ball is considered in play.

Examples

Fair territory in baseball
Any ball hit into fair territory is considered playable by the rules.
The fly ball landed in fair territory just beyond third base.
The umpire waved his arms to indicate the ball was inside fair territory.
