bristlecone pine

noun
very long-lived pine tree of the western US
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɪsəlˌkoʊn paɪn / (AmE), / ˈbrɪsəlˌkəʊn paɪn / (BrE)
a small, slow-growing pine tree native to the western United States, known for its great longevity and dense branches with thorn-tipped cone scales

Examples

Old bristlecone pines
Scientists have dated some bristlecone pines to be more than 4,500 years old.
The bristlecone pine is considered one of the oldest living tree species on Earth.
The unique appearance of the bristlecone pine attracts many nature enthusiasts to the Rocky Mountains.
