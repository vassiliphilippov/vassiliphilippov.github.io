unserved

adjective
without service
4.7
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈsɜrvd / (AmE), / ʌnˈsɜːvd / (BrE)
not provided with a particular public or commercial service, such as utilities, transport, or internet coverage

Examples

Unserved areas
Many rural areas remain unserved by public transportation, making travel difficult for residents.
The new policy aims to bring electricity to previously unserved communities.
Some remote villages are still unserved by reliable internet connections.

Related Terms

  • antonym: served - provided with a particular service
  • synonym: underserved - not receiving enough service or resources
