priggish

adjective
self-righteously proper or moralistic
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪɡɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈprɪɡɪʃ / (BrE)
Behaving in a self-righteously moralistic and superior way; showing exaggerated conformity to propriety or conventional norms.

Examples

A priggish person
Don't be so priggish—let people enjoy themselves.
His priggish attitude annoyed everyone at the party.
She made a priggish comment about our manners.

Related Terms

  • synonym: prim - formally precise and proper
  • synonym: straitlaced - very strict in moral conduct
