pentavalent

adjective
having a valence of five (chemistry)
9.9 8.0
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛntəˈveɪlənt / (AmE), / ˌpɛntəˈveɪlənt / (BrE)
(of a chemical element or compound) having a valence of five; capable of combining with or replacing five atoms of hydrogen or other monovalent element.

Examples

Phosphorus is commonly found in both trivalent and pentavalent states.
