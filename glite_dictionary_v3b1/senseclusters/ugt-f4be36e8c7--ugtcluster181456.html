UGT

noun
drug-metabolizing enzyme
Pronunciation: / ˌjuː dʒiː ˈtiː / (AmE), / ˌjuː dʒiː ˈtiː / (BrE)
an enzyme from the UDP-glucuronosyltransferase family that helps process and remove drugs and other substances from the body

Examples

UGT enzyme activity
A genetic variation in UGT can affect how quickly a person metabolizes certain medications.
Researchers study UGT activity to understand differences in drug responses among patients.
The doctor ordered a test to check the function of the patient's UGT enzymes.
