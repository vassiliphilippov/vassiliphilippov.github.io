hydrated lime

noun
white powder used in building and agriculture
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪdreɪtɪd laɪm / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdreɪtɪd laɪm / (BrE)
a white, powdery or granular substance made by adding water to quicklime; used in building, agriculture, and water treatment

Examples

Hydrated lime powder
Farmers often use hydrated lime to reduce soil acidity and improve crop yields.
Proper handling of hydrated lime requires protective gear, as it can be caustic.
The builders added hydrated lime to the mortar mix for better workability.

Related Terms

  • synonym: quicklime - calcium oxide, the substance from which hydrated lime is made
