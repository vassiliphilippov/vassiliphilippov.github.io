savings

noun
money set aside as a reserve
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪvɪŋz / (AmE), / ˈseɪvɪŋz / (BrE)
money that has been put aside, especially in a bank, usually to be used later or as a reserve for emergencies or specific purposes.

Examples

His savings account
After years of careful budgeting, his savings finally amounted to a substantial sum.
She used her savings to pay for the down payment on the house.
They dipped into their savings to cover unexpected medical expenses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deposit - money placed in a bank for safekeeping
  • antonym: expenditure - an amount of money spent; the act of spending
