sensational

adjective
exceptionally impressive; attention-grabbing
3.1
Pronunciation: / sɛnˈseɪʃənl / (AmE), / sɛnˈseɪʃənəl / (BrE)
extremely impressive, exciting, or causing great public interest

Examples

A sensational story
Her new novel received sensational reviews from critics and readers alike.
The orchestra gave a sensational performance that brought the audience to its feet.
We had a sensational view of the city from our hotel room.
Her performance at the concert was absolutely sensational and received a standing ovation.
That movie was so sensational that people couldn't stop talking about it for weeks.
The newspaper published a sensational story that quickly went viral.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ordinary - not remarkable or special; usual
  • synonym: spectacular - impressive or dramatic in appearance or effect
  • synonym: stunning - extremely impressive or attractive
  • antonym: unremarkable - not particularly impressive or attention-grabbing
