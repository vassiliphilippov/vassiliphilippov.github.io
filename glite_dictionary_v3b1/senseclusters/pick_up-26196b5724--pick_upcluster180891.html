pick up

verb
learn or notice by chance
Pronunciation: / pɪk ʌp / (AmE), / pɪk ʌp / (BrE)
to learn, notice, or detect something, especially by chance, with your senses, or using equipment

Examples

To pick up a language
I didn't study much, but I picked up enough information to pass the test.
She managed to pick up a few words of Italian while traveling in Rome.
You quickly pick up new skills when you work with experienced people.
I could barely pick up the faint sound of music in the distance.
She quickly picked up the subtle hint in his remark.
The dog managed to pick up the scent of the missing child.
My phone couldn't pick up the wireless signal in the basement.
Some animals can pick up sounds that humans cannot hear.
The radar can pick up distant aircraft flying overhead.

Related Terms

  • synonym: detect - to discover or identify the presence of something
  • synonym: learn - to gain knowledge or skill by studying, experience, or being taught
  • antonym: miss - to fail to notice or perceive something
  • synonym: notice - to become aware of something or someone
