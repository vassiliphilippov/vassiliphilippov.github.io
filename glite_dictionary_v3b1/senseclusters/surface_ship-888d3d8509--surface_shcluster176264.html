surface ship

noun
warship operating on water surface
Pronunciation: / ˈsɜːrfɪs ʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈsɜːfɪs ʃɪp / (BrE)
a warship designed to operate on the surface of the water, as opposed to submarines or submersibles

Examples

A surface ship warship
A surface ship can carry helicopters and missiles for various military missions.
The navy deployed a surface ship to patrol the international waters near the coast.
Unlike a submarine, a surface ship remains visible while performing its operations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: submarine - a warship designed to operate underwater
