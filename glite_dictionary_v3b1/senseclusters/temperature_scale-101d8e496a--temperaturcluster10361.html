temperature scale

noun
system for measuring temperature
2.6 3.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛmpɚˌtʃɚ skeɪl / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərətʃə skeɪl / (BrE)
a system for measuring temperature, often using fixed reference points and units such as Celsius or Fahrenheit

Examples

Kelvin temperature scale
Meteorologists often use the Kelvin temperature scale for scientific work.
The Celsius temperature scale sets the freezing point of water at 0 degrees and the boiling point at 100 degrees.
When traveling abroad, it's useful to understand the local temperature scale.
