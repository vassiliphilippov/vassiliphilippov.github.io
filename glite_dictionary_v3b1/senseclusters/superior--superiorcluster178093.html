superior

noun
character printed above another
Pronunciation: / suˈpɪriər / (AmE), / suːˈpɪəriə / (BrE)
A character or symbol set or printed above and immediately to one side of another character, commonly used in typographic notation.

Examples

A superior in math
In mathematical texts, a superior is used to represent exponents.
The chemical formula includes a superior number to indicate the charge.
When writing footnotes, a superior numeral is often placed at the reference point in the text.
