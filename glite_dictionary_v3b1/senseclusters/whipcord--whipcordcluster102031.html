whipcord

noun
strong fabric with a diagonal rib
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪp.kɔrd / (AmE), / ˈwɪp.kɔːd / (BrE)
a strong, closely woven fabric, usually of worsted or cotton, with a characteristic diagonal rib, used especially for making uniforms, trousers, or outerwear

Examples

Whipcord trousers
He chose a jacket of navy blue whipcord for hiking because of its toughness.
Many military uniforms are traditionally made from whipcord fabric.
The trousers were made of durable whipcord that resists wear and tear.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: gabardine - a tightly woven fabric with a diagonal rib on one side
  • synonym: twill - a fabric with a diagonal weave pattern
