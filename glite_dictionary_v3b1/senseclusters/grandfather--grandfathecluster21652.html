grandfather

verb
to exempt from new rules
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrændˌfɑðɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡrɑːnˌfɑːðə / (BrE)
to allow someone or something to continue under old rules when new rules are introduced

Examples

To grandfather old rules
The new policy will not affect current employees because the company will grandfather them in.
Some older buildings were grandfathered and do not have to meet the latest safety codes.
They decided to grandfather existing users so they could keep their original benefits.
Employees hired before the policy change were grandfathered and kept their original benefits.
The old software license was grandfathered so users did not have to pay the new fees.
Because her house was built before the new law, it was grandfathered and did not require renovations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: enforce - to make sure a rule or law is followed by everyone
  • synonym: exempt - to free someone from a rule or requirement
