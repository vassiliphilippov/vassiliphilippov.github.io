Western Church

noun
Roman Catholic branch of Christianity
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛstərn tʃɝːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
the branch of Christianity based in the Vatican, led by the pope and governed by a hierarchy of bishops, commonly known as the Roman Catholic Church

Examples

The Western Church leader
He studied the liturgical practices unique to the Western Church while visiting Rome.
Many of Europe's oldest cathedrals were built by the Western Church during the Middle Ages.
The Western Church and Eastern Church split formally in the Great Schism of 1054.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Eastern Church - the Christian Churches of Eastern Europe, Asia, and Africa, especially the Orthodox Churches
  • synonym: Roman Catholic Church - the main branch of the Western Church headed by the pope in Rome
