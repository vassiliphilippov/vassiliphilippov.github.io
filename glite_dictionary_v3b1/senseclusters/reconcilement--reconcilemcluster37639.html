reconcilement

noun
restoring friendship
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛkənˈsaɪlmənt / (AmE), / ˌrɛkənˈsaɪlmənt / (BrE)
the act of making people or groups friendly again after a disagreement or conflict, or achieving forgiveness

Examples

Family reconcilement
The reconcilement of the two families brought peace to the village.
After months of tension, a formal reconcilement was finally achieved between the teams.
The community celebrated the reconcilement that ended years of rivalry.
After years of conflict, the community sought reconcilement through shared prayer and reflection.
The priest spoke about the importance of reconcilement with oneself and with others.
Many came to the chapel seeking reconcilement and comfort after months of personal struggle.

Related Terms

  • antonym: alienation - the state of being isolated or estranged
  • antonym: conflict - a serious disagreement or argument
  • synonym: reconciliation - the process of becoming friendly again after a disagreement
