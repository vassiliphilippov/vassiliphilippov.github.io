large-leaved magnolia

noun
a southeastern US tree with large leaves and creamy, purple-tinged flowers
7.4
Pronunciation: / lɑrdʒ livd mægˈnoʊliə / (AmE), / lɑːdʒ liːvd mægˈnəʊliə / (BrE)
a large deciduous shrub or tree of the southeastern United States, known for its huge leaves arranged in dense false whorls and large creamy flowers tinged with purple at the base

Examples

The large-leaved magnolia tree
The large-leaved magnolia is admired for its massive foliage and showy blossoms in southern gardens.
The creamy, purple-tinged flowers of the large-leaved magnolia have a subtle, pleasant fragrance.
We planted a large-leaved magnolia near the patio to provide shade and beauty.
