re-sentencing

noun
reducing or altering a legal sentence
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈsɛntənsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈsɛntənsɪŋ / (BrE)
(law) The act of assigning a new, often reduced, punishment to someone who has already been sentenced.

Examples

Re-sentencing hearing
Advocates argued that re-sentencing offered hope for those serving excessively long terms.
Following the Supreme Court's ruling, dozens of prisoners became eligible for re-sentencing.
The judge granted a re-sentencing after new evidence emerged in the case.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sentencing - the act of assigning a punishment for a crime
