manila maguey

noun
fiber from Philippine agave used for twine
Pronunciation: / məˈnɪlə məˈɡeɪ / (AmE), / məˈnɪlə məˈɡeɪ / (BrE)
a hard fiber obtained from Philippine agave plants, used in making coarse twine and rope

Examples

Strong manila maguey fiber
Experts distinguish manila maguey from similar fibers like sisal based on texture and durability.
The company imports manila maguey to manufacture durable packing twine.
Traditional rope makers still value manila maguey for its strength and resistance to seawater.

Related Terms

  • synonym: manila hemp - a similar fiber obtained from abacá, used for rope and paper
