precipitant

noun
direct trigger or cause for an event
2.8
Pronunciation: / prɪˈsɪp.ɪtənt / (AmE), / prɪˈsɪp.ɪtənt / (BrE)
A factor or agent that directly causes or triggers a particular event, especially a chemical reaction or a sudden change.

Examples

A sudden precipitant
In economic crises, a sudden policy change can serve as a precipitant for unexpected market turmoil.
The argument acted as the precipitant for months of tension finally erupting among the team members.
The scientist identified the precipitant of the chemical reaction as an unexpected contaminant in the solution.

Related Terms

  • synonym: catalyst - a substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction without being consumed
  • synonym: trigger - an event or thing that causes another event to happen
