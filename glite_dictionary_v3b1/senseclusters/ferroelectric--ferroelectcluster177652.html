ferroelectric

adjective
retaining electric polarization
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˌfɛroʊɪˈlɛktrɪk / (AmE), / ˌfɛrəʊɪˈlɛktrɪk / (BrE)
describing a material that can retain electric polarization after an external electric field is removed

Examples

Ferroelectric material
The new sensor uses a ferroelectric material to detect changes in pressure.
Scientists are studying ferroelectric crystals for use in advanced memory devices.
A ferroelectric capacitor can store information even when the power is off.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nonpolar - not having permanent electric polarization
  • synonym: piezoelectric - describing a material that generates electric charge under mechanical stress
