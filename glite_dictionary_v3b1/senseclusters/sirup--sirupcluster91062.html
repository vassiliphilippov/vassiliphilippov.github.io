sirup

noun
variant spelling of syrup (thick sweet liquid)
9.9 1.8
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪ.rəp/ /; / /ˈsɪ.rʌp/ (AmE), /ˈsɪ.rəp/ /; / /ˈsɪ.rʌp/ (BrE)
A thick, sweet, sticky liquid made from sugar and water, often used as a flavoring or sweetener in food and drinks; alternative spelling of 'syrup.'

Examples

Maple sirup
She poured maple sirup over her pancakes for breakfast.
Some medicines are easier to take when made into a sirup.
The recipe calls for one cup of sirup to sweeten the cake batter.
