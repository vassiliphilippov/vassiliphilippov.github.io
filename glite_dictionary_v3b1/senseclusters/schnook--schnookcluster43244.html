schnook

noun
a gullible, simple-minded person
8.4
Pronunciation: / ʃnʊk / (AmE), / ʃnʊk / (BrE)
a person who is easily deceived or taken advantage of; a gullible or simple-minded person, often regarded with pity rather than contempt

Examples

A schnook fooled
He felt like a schnook after falling for such an obvious scam.
She didn’t want to be the schnook who volunteered for every tedious task.
They called him a schnook for believing every sales pitch he heard.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dupe - a person who is easily tricked or deceived
  • synonym: sap - a foolish or gullible person
  • antonym: shrewd - having or showing sharp judgment (opposite quality)
