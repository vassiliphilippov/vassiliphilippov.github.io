reignition

noun
renewed enthusiasm
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪɡˈnɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːɪɡˈnɪʃən / (BrE)
a renewed start or increase of interest, enthusiasm, or activity after a period of decline or inactivity

Examples

Reignition of interest
The documentary led to a reignition of interest in the artist's early work.
There was a sudden reignition of enthusiasm for the project after the new manager arrived.
The team's recent success brought about a reignition of community support.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decline - a gradual decrease or loss of strength or activity
  • synonym: resurgence - a rising again into life or activity
  • synonym: revival - a process in which something becomes active or popular again
