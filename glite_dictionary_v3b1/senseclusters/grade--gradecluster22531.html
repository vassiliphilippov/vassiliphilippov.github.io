degree of ablaut in linguistics

Pronunciation: /greɪd/ (AmE), /greɪd/ (BrE)
(Linguistics) a particular stage or degree of ablaut (systematic vowel change in related forms of a word).

Examples

Zero grade vowel
In some languages, the root vowel alternates between the full and zero grade forms.
The Proto-Indo-European verb shows changes in its vowel depending on the grade of ablaut.
This pattern results from shifting the grade of the stem vowel.
