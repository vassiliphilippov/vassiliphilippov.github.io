eccentricity

noun
deviation from circularity (geometry/physics)
6.4 7.2
Pronunciation: /ek.sən.ˈtrɪs.ə.tiː/ /; / /ek.sen.ˈtrɪs.ə.tiː/ (AmE), /ek.sen.ˈtrɪs.ə.tiː/ /; / /ek.sən.ˈtrɪs.ə.tiː/ (BrE)
(mathematics, physics) a measure of how much a curve or orbit is not perfectly circular

Examples

Orbit eccentricity
Earth's orbit around the sun has a small eccentricity, making it nearly circular.
In engineering, the eccentricity of a rotating shaft can lead to vibrations and noise.
The oval's eccentricity determines how far its shape deviates from a perfect circle.
By calculating the eccentricity, students can compare the shapes of different orbits in astronomy.
In mathematics, a circle has an eccentricity of zero while an ellipse has an eccentricity less than one.
The eccentricity of a conic section determines whether it is a circle, ellipse, parabola, or hyperbola.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: circle - a perfectly round shape with all points equidistant from the center; zero eccentricity
  • synonym: ellipse - a regular oval shape, a type of conic section
  • degree higher: hyperbola - an open curve formed by a plane that cuts the base of a right circular cone
  • degree higher: oval - a shape resembling a flattened or elongated circle
  • degree higher: parabola - a symmetrical open plane curve formed by the intersection of a cone with a plane parallel to its side
