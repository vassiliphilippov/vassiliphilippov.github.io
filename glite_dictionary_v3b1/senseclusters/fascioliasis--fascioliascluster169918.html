fascioliasis

noun
disease caused by liver fluke infestation
9.3
Pronunciation: / fəˌsiːəˈlaɪəsɪs / (AmE), / fæˌsɪəˈlaɪəsɪs / (BrE)
A disease caused by infestation of the liver with the liver fluke Fasciola hepatica, potentially resulting in liver damage.

Examples

Fascioliasis in cattle
Effective control of fascioliasis is essential to prevent economic losses in cattle farming.
Humans can contract fascioliasis by eating contaminated water plants.
The veterinarian diagnosed the sheep with fascioliasis after noticing signs of liver damage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: liver fluke - parasitic flatworm causing disease in the liver
  • degree lower: schistosomiasis - disease caused by infestation with schistosome parasites
