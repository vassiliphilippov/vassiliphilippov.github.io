unconducive

adjective
not suitable or allowing
7.7
Pronunciation: /ʌn.kənˈduː.sɪv/ /; / /ʌn.kənˈdjuː.sɪv/ (AmE), /ʌn.kənˈdjuː.sɪv/ /; / /ʌn.kənˈdʒuː.sɪv/ (BrE)
not suitable or helpful for something to happen or develop

Examples

An unconducive place
A noisy environment is unconducive to studying for important exams.
The thick rubber coating is unconducive to the flow of electricity, making it a good insulator.
Poor lighting can be unconducive to productive work in an office.
The dim lighting was unconducive to reading for long periods.
A tense atmosphere is unconducive to open and honest discussion.
These conditions are unconducive to the growth of healthy plants.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conducive - making a certain situation or outcome likely or possible
  • synonym: insulating - preventing the passage of heat, electricity, or sound
  • synonym: unfavorable - not helpful or likely to produce a good result
