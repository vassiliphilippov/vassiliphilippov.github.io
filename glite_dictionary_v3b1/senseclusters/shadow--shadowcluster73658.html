shadow

verb
follow secretly; monitor someone
Pronunciation: / ˈʃædoʊ / (AmE), / ˈʃædəʊ / (BrE)
to follow someone secretly, especially in order to watch or monitor their actions

Examples

To shadow a suspect
Security agents shadowed the foreign diplomat during his visit.
She felt uneasy, as if someone was shadowing her everywhere she went.
The detectives decided to shadow the suspect for several days.

Related Terms

  • synonym: follow - move or travel behind someone
  • synonym: trail - to follow someone, often secretly
