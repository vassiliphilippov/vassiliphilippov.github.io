methane

noun
a flammable gas; main part of natural gas
Pronunciation: / ˈmeθˌeɪn / (AmE), / ˈmiːθeɪn / (BrE)
a colorless, odorless flammable gas that is the main constituent of natural gas, used as a fuel.

Examples

The methane gas
Cows produce methane as part of their digestive process, contributing to greenhouse gas emissions.
Scientists measured levels of methane in the air to study climate change impacts.
The pipeline leaked methane into the atmosphere, raising environmental concerns.
