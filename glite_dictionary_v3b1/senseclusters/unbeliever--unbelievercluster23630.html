unbeliever

noun
person who does not believe
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnbɪˈliːvər / (AmE), / ˌʌnbɪˈliːvə / (BrE)
a person who does not accept or believe in a particular idea, religion, or set of beliefs

Examples

An unbeliever in religion
As an unbeliever, she often questioned the traditions followed by her family.
The group welcomed both unbelievers and those who were searching for answers.
He was labeled an unbeliever because he refused to accept the popular theory.
The philosopher debated with several unbelievers about the existence of a higher power.
In some communities, unbelievers may feel isolated because of their different views.
The book was written to address the questions of unbelievers and explain the faith.
