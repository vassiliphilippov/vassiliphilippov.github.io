undecidability

noun
cannot be algorithmically solved
9.9
Pronunciation: /ʌn.dɪ.saɪ.də.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /ə.dɪ.saɪ.də.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /ʌn.dɪ.saɪ.də.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /ə.dɪ.saɪ.də.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
the quality of a problem or statement that cannot be determined as true or false by any algorithm or within a formal system (especially in mathematics, logic, or computer science)

Examples

Undecidability in logic
The undecidability of certain mathematical problems has important implications for computer science.
Gödel's incompleteness theorems are closely related to the concept of undecidability.
Researchers often encounter undecidability when analyzing complex logical systems.
The concept of undecidability is central to understanding the limits of mathematical logic.
Some problems in computer science are known for their undecidability and cannot be solved by any algorithm.
Gödel's incompleteness theorems highlight the undecidability present in certain formal mathematical systems.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decidability - the quality of a problem that can be resolved by an algorithm
  • synonym: indecidability - the state of not being able to decide or determine
  • synonym: unsolvability - the state of being impossible to solve
