pseudopodium

noun
temporary projection used by certain microorganisms
8.8 9.6
Pronunciation: / suːˌdoʊˈpoʊdiəm / (AmE), / sjuːˌdəʊˈpəʊdiəm / (BrE)
a temporary projection of the cytoplasm of some single-celled organisms, used for movement or feeding

Examples

A pseudopodium extends
Some protozoa engulf prey by forming a pseudopodium around them.
The amoeba extends a pseudopodium to move toward its food source.
The microscope revealed the rapid retraction of a pseudopodium after contact with a barrier.
