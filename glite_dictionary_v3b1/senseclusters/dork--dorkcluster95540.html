dork

noun
informal, socially awkward or dull person
Pronunciation: / dɔrk / (AmE), / dɔːk / (BrE)
an informal term for a dull, awkward, or socially inept person, often used as a mild insult (sometimes with obsessive interests)

Examples

A dork at school
He used to be called a dork in high school, but now he owns his own company.
I felt like such a dork when I tripped in front of everyone.
Stop being a dork and just ask her out already.
Even as an adult, he still feels like a total dork at parties.
Growing up, he was a dork who preferred books to sports.
She called me a dork for wearing mismatched socks, but I didn't mind.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cool - a fashionable, popular person
  • synonym: geek - a person with excessive enthusiasm for a specialized subject
  • synonym: nerd - a person seen as overly intellectual or socially awkward
