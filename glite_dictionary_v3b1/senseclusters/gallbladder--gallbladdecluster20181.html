gallbladder

noun
organ storing bile for digestion
6.0 3.4
Pronunciation: /ˈgɔːl.blæd.ər/ (AmE), /ˈgɔːl.blæd.ə/ (BrE)
A small muscular sac beneath the liver that stores bile produced by the liver until it is needed for digesting food.

Examples

The gallbladder stores bile
A healthy diet can contribute to better gallbladder function and prevent digestive problems.
After his gallbladder was taken out, he had to adjust his eating habits.
The surgeon removed her gallbladder because it was causing painful gallstones.
