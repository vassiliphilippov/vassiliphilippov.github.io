outpace

verb
surpass in speed or progress
2.0
Pronunciation: / aʊtˈpeɪs / (AmE), / aʊtˈpeɪs / (BrE)
To move, develop, or progress faster than someone or something else; to surpass in speed or rate.

Examples

To outpace rivals
Despite their best efforts, our competitors couldn't outpace us in launching the new product.
Rising housing costs continue to outpace wages in many major cities.
Technological innovation tends to outpace government regulation, creating new challenges for lawmakers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lag behind - to move or develop more slowly than others
  • synonym: outstrip - to move faster than and overtake; surpass
