cavalier hat

noun
17th-century wide-brimmed felt hat
Pronunciation: / ˌkævəˈlɪr hæt / (AmE), / ˌkævəˈlɪə hæt / (BrE)
a soft felt hat with a wide, flexible brim, popular in the 17th century, often decorated with a plume

Examples

A cavalier hat with plume
During the parade, several participants dressed in period costumes complete with cavalier hats.
The actor wore a splendid cavalier hat with a long, white feather for his role in the play.
The museum's exhibit on the English Civil War included an ornate cavalier hat from the 1600s.

Related Terms

  • synonym: musketeer hat - a similar wide-brimmed historical hat, often with feathers
