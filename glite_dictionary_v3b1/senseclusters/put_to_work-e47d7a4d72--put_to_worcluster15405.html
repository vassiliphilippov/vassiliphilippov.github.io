put to work

verb
cause to start working
3.2 3.54/m
Pronunciation: / p ät t…ô w…úrk / (AmE), / p ät t…ô w…úÀêk / (BrE)
to cause someone or something to start working or to assign tasks or duties

Examples

To put to work quickly
After moving to the farm, they immediately put to work repairing the old barn.
He found a stray dog and put it to work guarding the property.
The manager put to work every available employee to meet the deadline.
After the meeting, the interns were put to work filing documents and answering phones.
New equipment was put to work to speed up production at the factory.
She hopes to be put to work on meaningful research during her summer internship.

Synonyms

  • set to work
    3.2 0.90/m
    set to work means to begin a task quickly and with energy, while put to work means to cause or assign someone to work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assign - to give someone a particular job or duty
  • synonym: employ - give someone a job to do; make use of
  • antonym: idle - not working or not active
