damper

noun
adjustable plate regulating airflow in a stove or chimney
3.9 5.7
Pronunciation: /ˈdæm.pər/ (AmE), /ˈdæm.pə/ (BrE)
a movable metal plate in a stove, furnace, or chimney used to control the flow of air or smoke

Examples

Chimney damper
If the damper is left open, cold air can come down the flue.
She adjusted the damper so the fire would burn more evenly.
You should close the damper to keep warm air from escaping up the chimney.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flue - a duct for smoke and waste gases produced by a fire, a gas heater, or other fuel-burning installation
