base

noun
the root form of a word without affixes
2.9
Pronunciation: / beɪs / (AmE), / beɪs / (BrE)
(linguistics) The basic form of a word after all affixes are removed, from which inflected or derived forms are built.

Examples

Base form
In morphology, the base is what remains after all prefixes and suffixes are removed from a word.
Linguists analyze the base to understand how words change form.
The plural form of a noun is often made by adding -s to its base.

Related Terms

  • synonym: root - the most basic form of a word without prefixes or suffixes
  • synonym: stem - the base part of a word to which inflections are added
