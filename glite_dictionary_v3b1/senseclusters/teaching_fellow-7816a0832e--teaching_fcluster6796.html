teaching fellow

noun
graduate student with teaching responsibilities
6.2 3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈtiː.tʃɪŋ ˈfe.loʊ/ (AmE), /ˈtiː.tʃɪŋ ˈfe.ləʊ/ (BrE)
A graduate student at a university who has teaching duties, often assisting professors or leading classes.

Examples

As a teaching fellow, he graded assignments and sometimes substituted for the main professor.
She worked as a teaching fellow while completing her doctoral studies at the university.
The teaching fellow led the discussion section and answered students' questions after lectures.
