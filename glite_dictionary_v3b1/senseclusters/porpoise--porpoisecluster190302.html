porpoise

verb
to leap in water
8.1
Pronunciation: /ˈpɔːr.pəs/ /; / /ˈpɔːr.pɪs/ (AmE), /ˈpɔː.pɪs/ /; / /ˈpɔː.pəs/ (BrE)
to move forward by repeatedly leaping out of and diving back into the water, like a porpoise.

Examples

To porpoise through waves
The dolphins were porpoising along the bow as the ferry cut through the waves.
Seals can often be seen porpoising near the shore when they are hunting for fish.
The speedboat caused the small fish to start porpoising across the surface of the lake.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dolphin - to move in a similar leaping motion through water
  • antonym: glide - to move smoothly and continuously without leaping
