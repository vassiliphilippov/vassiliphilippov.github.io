tuberous vetch

noun
European herb with edible tubers
Pronunciation: / ˈtuːbərəs vɛtʃ / (AmE), / ˈtjuːbərəs vɛtʃ / (BrE)
A European herb (Lathyrus tuberosus) that produces small edible tubers, sometimes used for food and in Scotland to flavor whiskey.

Examples

Tuberous vetch plant
Early farmers collected tuberous vetch as a nutritious supplement to their diets.
Some distillers in Scotland use tuberous vetch to impart a unique flavor to their whiskey.
The fields were dotted with tuberous vetch, prized for its edible roots.
