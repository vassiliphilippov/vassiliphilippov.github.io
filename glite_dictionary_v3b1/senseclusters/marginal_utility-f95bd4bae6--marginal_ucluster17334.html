(in economics) The additional satisfaction or benefit gained from consuming one more unit of a good or service.

Examples

The marginal utility of goods
As you eat more slices of pizza, the marginal utility of each additional slice typically decreases.
Economists analyze marginal utility to determine consumer demand for different products.
The concept of marginal utility helps explain why the value of goods changes as consumption increases.
