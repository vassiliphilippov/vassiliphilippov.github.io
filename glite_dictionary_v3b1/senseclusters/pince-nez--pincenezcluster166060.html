pince-nez

noun
spectacles clipped to the nose
Pronunciation: / pænsˈneɪ, ˈpɪns neɪ / (AmE), / pæ̃sˈneɪ / (BrE)
A style of eyeglasses without earpieces that clip onto the bridge of the nose by means of a spring.

Examples

A pince-nez glasses
He fumbled in his pocket for his pince-nez to read the small print.
The distinguished professor wore a pince-nez perched delicately on his nose during the lecture.
Victorian photographs often show gentlemen with a pince-nez rather than modern eyeglasses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glasses - lenses set in a frame for correcting or assisting vision
  • degree lower: monocle - a single eyeglass, kept in position by the muscles around the eye
  • synonym: spectacles - eyeglasses used for correcting vision
