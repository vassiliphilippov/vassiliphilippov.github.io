spoofer

noun
market manipulator
Pronunciation: / ˈspuːfər / (AmE), / ˈspuːfə / (BrE)
a trader who places fake orders in financial markets to mislead others and manipulate prices

Examples

Market spoofer
The authorities charged the spoofer with market manipulation after a lengthy investigation.
A skilled spoofer can create the illusion of high demand to influence stock prices.
Regulators are increasing efforts to catch every spoofer who tries to deceive the market.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fraudster - a person who commits fraud
  • antonym: investor - a person who puts money into financial schemes for profit
  • synonym: manipulator - a person who controls or influences others or situations unfairly
