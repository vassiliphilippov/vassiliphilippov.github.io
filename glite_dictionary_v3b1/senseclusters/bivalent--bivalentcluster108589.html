bivalent

noun
paired chromosomes
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪˌveɪlənt / (AmE), / ˈbaɪˌveɪlənt / (BrE)
a pair of homologous chromosomes joined together during the first division of meiosis

Examples

A bivalent forms
During meiosis I, each bivalent lines up along the center of the cell.
Scientists observed the formation of a bivalent under the microscope during the experiment.
Errors in bivalent formation can lead to genetic disorders in offspring.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chromosome pair - two chromosomes that are similar in shape and size and pair during meiosis
  • antonym: univalent - a single chromosome that does not pair during meiosis
