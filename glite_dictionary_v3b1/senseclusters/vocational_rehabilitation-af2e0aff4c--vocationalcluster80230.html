vocational rehabilitation

noun
training and support to gain employment after disability or disadvantage
Pronunciation: / voʊˈkeɪʃənl ˌriˌhæbəˌlɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / vəʊˈkeɪʃənəl ˌriːˌhæbɪlɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of helping people with disabilities or disadvantages develop skills to gain or return to employment, often through specialized training or support programs

Examples

Vocational rehabilitation program
After his injury, Mark entered a vocational rehabilitation program to learn new job skills.
She credits vocational rehabilitation with helping her transition into a new career after her accident.
The government funds vocational rehabilitation services for people who have experienced long-term unemployment.
