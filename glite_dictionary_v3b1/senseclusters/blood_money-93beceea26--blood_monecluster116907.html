blood money

noun
reward for information about a murderer
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈblʌd ˌmʌni / (AmE), / ˈblʌd ˌmʌni / (BrE)
money paid as a reward for information leading to the capture or conviction of a murderer.

Examples

Blood money reward
He refused to accept blood money, feeling it was wrong to profit from another's death.
Rumors spread that someone had claimed the blood money after turning in the fugitive.
The police offered blood money to anyone with information about the suspect's whereabouts.
