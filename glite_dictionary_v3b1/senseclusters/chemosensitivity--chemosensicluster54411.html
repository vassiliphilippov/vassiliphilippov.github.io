chemosensitivity

noun
sensitivity to chemicals
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌkiːmoʊˌsɛnsəˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌkiːməʊˌsɛnsɪˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
the susceptibility of cells, tissues, or organisms to the effects of chemicals, especially drugs used in treatment

Examples

Cancer cell chemosensitivity
The chemosensitivity of cancer cells can influence the effectiveness of chemotherapy.
Researchers are studying how genetic factors affect chemosensitivity in different patients.
Doctors tested the tumor's chemosensitivity before deciding on a treatment plan.

Related Terms

  • antonym: chemoresistance - the ability of cells or organisms to resist the effects of chemicals or drugs
