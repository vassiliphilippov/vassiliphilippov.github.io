thoracentesis

noun
removal of chest fluid by needle
Pronunciation: / ˌθɔːr.ə.sɛnˈtiː.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌθɔː.rə.senˈtiː.sɪs / (BrE)
a medical procedure in which fluid is removed from the chest cavity with a needle for diagnosis or treatment

Examples

Thoracentesis procedure
A sample obtained by thoracentesis helped diagnose the underlying lung infection.
Complications from thoracentesis are rare when the procedure is done correctly.
The doctor performed a thoracentesis to relieve the patient's difficulty in breathing.
