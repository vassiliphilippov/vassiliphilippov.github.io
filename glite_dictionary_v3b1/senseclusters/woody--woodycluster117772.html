woody

adjective
made of or like wood
3.9 1.06/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆwÊŠdi / (AmE), / ËˆwÊŠdi / (BrE)
made of wood or having the qualities or structure of wood

Examples

Woody stem
Over time, the shrub developed a thick, woody stem at its base.
The botanist explained that oaks and pines are examples of woody plants.
The wine had a slightly woody flavor that reminded me of oak barrels.
Certain shrubs remain herbaceous, while others become woody as they mature.
The plant develops a woody stem after several years of growth.
With age, the root system turns woody and difficult to cut through.

Synonyms

  • wooden
    1.0 34/m
    wooden is made of or contains wood and often refers to physical objects, while woody means having wood-like structure or qualities.
  • woodlike
    4.5 0.01/m
    woodlike looks or feels similar to wood in appearance or texture, while woody can mean made of wood or wood-like in structure.
