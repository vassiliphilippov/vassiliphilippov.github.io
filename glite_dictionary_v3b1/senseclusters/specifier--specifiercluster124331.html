specifier

noun
defining element
Pronunciation: / ˈspɛsɪˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈspɛsɪˌfaɪə / (BrE)
a word or phrase in grammar that makes the meaning of another word more specific or precise

Examples

Grammar specifier
In the sentence 'the red car', 'the' is a specifier that makes the noun more specific.
A specifier in programming languages can determine the type or scope of a variable.
Linguists often analyze the role of a specifier in sentence structure.
In the sentence 'these books are new', 'these' acts as a specifier for 'books'.
A specifier can help clarify which object or person is being referred to.
In grammar, a specifier often appears before the noun it describes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: identifier - something that establishes the identity of something
  • synonym: modifier - a word or phrase that changes or adds to the meaning of another word
