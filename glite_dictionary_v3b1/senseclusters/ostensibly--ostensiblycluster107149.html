ostensibly

adverb

as something appears or is claimed to be, but possibly not actually so

Examples

He ostensibly agreed
He ostensibly agreed with the plan, though he opposed it in private.
The company was ostensibly founded to improve education, but its real purpose was profit.
The trip was ostensibly for research, but they spent most of their time sightseeing.

Synonyms

  • purportedly
    7.2 2.08/m
    purportedly is used when someone states something as a claim, while ostensibly covers how something appears or is claimed.
  • supposedly
    3.4 24/m
    supposedly is what people say or believe without proof, while ostensibly focuses on appearance or an official claim.
  • apparently
    2.8 99/m
    apparently is used when things look true from evidence or sight, while ostensibly also suggests a claimed reason.
  • seemingly
    3.7 27/m
    seemingly describes how something looks from the outside, while ostensibly adds the idea that it may be only a claim.
  • on the face of it
    5.5 0.86/m
    on the face of it means judging by first appearance, while ostensibly suggests a public claim that may hide the truth.
  • allegedly
    5.4 16/m
    allegedly reports an accusation or claim often in legal context, while ostensibly is more neutral about appearance or stated reason.
