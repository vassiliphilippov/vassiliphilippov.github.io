(of a plant) requiring an abundance of moisture to grow; adapted to very wet environments.

Examples

Hygrophytic plants
Some mosses are highly hygrophytic, needing near-constant moisture to maintain their green coloration.
The botanist identified the plant as hygrophytic because it flourished only in marshy soils.
Tropical rainforests are home to many hygrophytic species that thrive in consistently wet conditions.

Synonyms

  • hydrophytic
    9.9 0.02/m
    hydrophytic describes plants that grow in or on water, while hygrophytic means plants needing very wet, but not fully submerged, ground.
  • water-loving
    4.1 0.04/m
    water-loving describes plants that thrive with lots of water in everyday speech, while hygrophytic is the technical botanical term.
