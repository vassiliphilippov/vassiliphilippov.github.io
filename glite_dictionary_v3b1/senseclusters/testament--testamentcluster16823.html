testament

noun
legal document stating wishes about property after death
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛstəmənt / (AmE), / ˈtɛstəmənt / (BrE)
a legal document in which a person states what should happen to their property after they die; a will

Examples

A last testament
He left everything to his daughter in his testament.
The lawyer read the late woman's testament to the assembled family.
Writing a testament ensures your property is distributed as you wish.

Related Terms

  • synonym: will - a legal document declaring how a person's property will be distributed after death
