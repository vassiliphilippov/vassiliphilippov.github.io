circulate

verb
make information widely known
5.2 3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsɝːkjəˌleɪt /; / ˈsɝː.kjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkjʊleɪt /; / ˈsɜː.kjə.leɪt / (BrE)
to spread or pass information, news, or documents among people or places

Examples

Rumors circulate online
False stories can circulate rapidly on social media.
Rumors began to circulate after the announcement was made.
The company quickly circulated the memo to all employees.
Rumors began to circulate throughout the office after the announcement.
She loves to circulate at parties and talk to everyone.
The petition was circulated among the residents for signatures.
False stories can circulate online before anyone has a chance to check the facts.
Rumors about the company's merger plans began to circulate among employees last week.
The news of their engagement circulated quickly through the small town.
