fro

preposition
archaic 'from'
Pronunciation: / froʊ / (AmE), / frəʊ / (BrE)
an archaic or dialect preposition meaning 'from', used to indicate the point of origin.

Examples

Fro the castle
The messenger came fro the castle with urgent news for the villagers.
In old letters, you may find the word fro used instead of 'from'.
He traveled fro the northern hills to the bustling city below.

Related Terms

  • synonym: from - indicating the starting point or origin
  • antonym: to - indicating the destination or end point
