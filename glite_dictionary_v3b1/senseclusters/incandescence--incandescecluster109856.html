incandescence

noun
light emission due to heat
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.kænˈdɛs.əns / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kænˈdɛs.əns / (BrE)
the emission of visible light from something because it is very hot

Examples

The incandescence of lava
The incandescence of molten lava lit up the night sky.
The stove’s element glowed with a brilliant incandescence after being switched on.
Traditional light bulbs produce incandescence by passing electricity through a filament.
Old-fashioned light bulbs work by producing incandescence when electricity heats the filament.
The incandescence of the metal could be seen from across the room as it glowed red-hot.
The potter admired the incandescence of the kiln just before opening it.

  • antonym: luminescence - light emission not resulting from heat
