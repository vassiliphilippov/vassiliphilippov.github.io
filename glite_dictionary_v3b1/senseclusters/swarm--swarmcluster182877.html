swarm

noun
large group of moving insects or things
1.9
Pronunciation: / swɔrm / (AmE), / swɔːm / (BrE)
a large group of insects, people, or things moving together in a busy or uncontrolled way

Examples

A swarm of bees
A swarm of bees gathered around the hive.
A swarm of tourists filled the small market square.
We watched a swarm of locusts descend upon the field.
A swarm of reporters gathered outside the courthouse waiting for a statement.
A swarm of tourists filled the museum lobby in the morning.
Security struggled to control the swarm rushing toward the stage after the concert.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crowd - a large number of people gathered together
  • synonym: flock - a group of birds, sheep, or other animals kept or moving together
  • antonym: solitary - being alone or without companions
  • synonym: throng - a large, densely packed crowd of people or animals
