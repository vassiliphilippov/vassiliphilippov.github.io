tortfeasor

noun
person who commits a wrongful act
9.9 0.12/m
Pronunciation: / Ààt…îrtÀåfiÀês…ôr / (AmE), / Ààt…îÀêtÀåfiÀês…ô / (BrE)
A person or party who commits a wrongful act (tort) that causes harm to another and can be held legally liable.

Examples

The tortfeasor caused harm
A lawsuit was filed against the alleged tortfeasor for causing financial loss.
Insurance companies may seek reimbursement from the tortfeasor after compensating the injured party.
The court found the tortfeasor liable for damages caused by his negligence.
