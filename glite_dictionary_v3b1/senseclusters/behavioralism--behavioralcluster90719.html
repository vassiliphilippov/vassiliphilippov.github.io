behavioralism

noun
study of observable behavior
8.1
Pronunciation: / bɪˈheɪvjərəlɪzəm / (AmE), / bɪˈheɪvjərəlɪzəm / (BrE)
a scientific approach that focuses on studying observable behavior rather than internal thoughts or feelings, especially in social sciences

Examples

Behavioralism in science
Behavioralism became popular in political science during the mid-twentieth century.
Researchers using behavioralism focus on actions that can be measured and observed.
The rise of behavioralism shifted attention away from traditional theories based on ideas and beliefs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: behaviorism - the theory that behavior can be explained by conditioning without appeal to thoughts or feelings
  • antonym: cognitivism - the theory that mental processes influence behavior
