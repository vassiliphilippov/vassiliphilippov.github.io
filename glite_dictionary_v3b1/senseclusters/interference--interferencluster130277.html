interference

noun
disturbance to signals in communication
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈfɪrəns / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈfɪərəns / (BrE)
undesired electrical or acoustic activity that disrupts or impairs the transmission or reception of signals in communication systems

Examples

Signal interference
Engineers work hard to minimize interference in wireless networks.
Mobile phone conversations often suffer from interference in densely populated areas.
The radio signal was lost due to heavy interference from nearby electrical equipment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: noise - random or unwanted disturbances that interfere with signal transmission
