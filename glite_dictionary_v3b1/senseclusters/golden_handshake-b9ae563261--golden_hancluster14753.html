golden handshake

noun
generous retirement or severance payment
Pronunciation: / ˈɡoʊldən ˈhændʃeɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldən ˈhændʃeɪk / (BrE)
a large sum of money given to an employee when they leave a job, especially as an incentive to retire early or as part of a severance agreement

Examples

A golden handshake payment
Rumors circulated that senior managers would be offered a golden handshake to encourage early retirement.
She was reluctant to leave until the company promised her a substantial golden handshake.
The CEO received a golden handshake worth several million dollars when he retired.
After thirty years of service, the company offered him a golden handshake to retire early.
Many executives hope for a golden handshake when they step down from their positions.
The board approved a golden handshake as part of the outgoing director's compensation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: redundancy payment - compensation given to an employee who is dismissed, especially in the UK
  • synonym: severance package - an arrangement of pay and benefits offered to an employee after leaving a company
