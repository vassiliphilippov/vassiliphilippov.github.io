et al.

adverb
and others (esp. in references)
5.6
Pronunciation: /et æl/ /; / /et ɑːl/ (AmE), /et æl/ /; / /et ɑːl/ (BrE)
used after a name to mean 'and others', especially in academic or formal writing when referring to additional people not named

Examples

Johnson et al.
Several authors, such as Johnson et al., have discussed this issue.
The article referenced Williams et al. for further reading.
The study by Smith et al. has been widely cited in the field.
Research by Martinez et al. supports these findings.
The book was written by Johnson et al. and published last year.
This method was first described by Chan et al. in 2010.
For more information, see the work by Wang et al. on environmental sustainability.
The study by Smith et al. demonstrated a significant correlation between diet and heart health.
This phenomenon is discussed in Johnson et al. (2018) in greater depth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: etc. - and so forth (used to indicate continuation of a list of things)
