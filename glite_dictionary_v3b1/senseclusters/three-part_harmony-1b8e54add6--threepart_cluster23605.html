three-part harmony

noun
musical harmony with three melodic parts
Pronunciation: / ˌθriː pɑrt ˈhɑːrməni / (AmE), / ˌθriː pɑːt ˈhɑːməni / (BrE)
a musical arrangement in which each chord consists of three notes, creating three distinct melodic lines sung or played together

Examples

Three-part harmony singing
Learning to sing in three-part harmony requires careful listening and blending of voices.
Many traditional folk songs are arranged in three-part harmony for small vocal groups.
The choir beautifully performed the piece in three-part harmony.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: four-part harmony - a harmony in which each chord has four notes, creating four melodic lines
