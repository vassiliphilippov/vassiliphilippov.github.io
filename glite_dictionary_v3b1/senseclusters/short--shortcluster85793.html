short

verb
to sell expecting decline
8.6
Pronunciation: /ʃɔːrt/ /; / /ʃoʊrt/ (AmE), /ʃɔːt/ (BrE)
to sell a financial asset you do not own, expecting to buy it back later at a lower price for profit

Examples

To short a stock
He decided to short the company's stock after reading the negative earnings report.
Some traders short currencies when they believe the value will drop soon.
During the market crash, many investors shorted several major tech stocks.
Many investors try to short stocks when they expect the market to decline.
He decided to short the company after reading negative reports about its future.
Some traders lost money when they attempted to short shares that unexpectedly rose in value.

Related Terms

  • antonym: buy - to obtain something by paying money for it
  • synonym: sell - to give something in exchange for money
