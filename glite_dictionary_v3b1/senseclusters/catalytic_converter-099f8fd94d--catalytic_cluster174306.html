catalytic converter

noun
vehicle exhaust pollution-control device
7.5 3.0
Pronunciation: /kæt.ə.ˈlɪt.ɪk kən.ˈvɜːr.tər/ (AmE), /kæt.ə.ˈlɪt.ɪk kən.ˈvɜː.tə/ (BrE)
a device in a vehicle's exhaust system that uses a catalyst to convert harmful gases such as carbon monoxide and pollutants into less harmful substances like carbon dioxide and water

Examples

A catalytic converter device
Many modern cars are equipped with a catalytic converter to reduce exhaust pollution.
Replacing a faulty catalytic converter can be expensive but is necessary for passing emissions tests.
Thieves sometimes steal catalytic converters because they contain precious metals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emission control - devices or methods for reducing harmful substances released into the environment
