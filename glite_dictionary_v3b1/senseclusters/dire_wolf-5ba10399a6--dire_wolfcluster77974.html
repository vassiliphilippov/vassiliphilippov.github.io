dire wolf

noun
extinct large wolf
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪər wʊlf / (AmE), / ˈdaɪə wʊlf / (BrE)
a large extinct species of wolf that lived in North America during the late Pleistocene epoch

Examples

Extinct dire wolf
The dire wolf was one of the largest predators in Ice Age North America.
Scientists have studied dire wolf fossils to learn about prehistoric ecosystems.
Unlike the modern gray wolf, the dire wolf had a more robust build and stronger jaws.
