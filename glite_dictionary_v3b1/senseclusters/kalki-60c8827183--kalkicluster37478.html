Kalki

proper noun
Hinduism's tenth incarnation of Vishnu
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑːl.ki/ /; / /ˈkæl.ki/ (AmE), /ˈkɑːl.ki/ /; / /ˈkæl.ki/ (BrE)
in Hinduism, the prophesied tenth and final incarnation of the god Vishnu, who is expected to appear at the end of the current age to destroy evil and restore righteousness

Examples

Kalki riding horse
Ancient texts describe Kalki riding a white horse and wielding a blazing sword.
In Hindu mythology, Kalki is foretold to appear at the end of the Kali Yuga.
Many believe that the advent of Kalki will restore order to the world.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Vishnu - a principal Hindu deity, preserver in the Hindu trinity
