overbanked

adjective
beyond vertical angle
9.6
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ˈbæŋkt/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ˈbæŋkt/ (BrE)
having a banking angle or tilt that is excessively steep or greater than vertical, especially in vehicles, turns, or structures

Examples

Overbanked turn
The roller coaster features an overbanked turn that tilts riders far past the vertical.
Drivers must be cautious when approaching the overbanked curve on the racetrack.
The new ride is famous for its overbanked sections that create a thrilling sideways sensation.
The pilot noticed the plane was overbanked and quickly adjusted the controls to regain stability.
During the sharp turn, the car became overbanked, making it difficult for the driver to steer safely.
If a turn is overbanked, vehicles may lose traction and risk skidding off the road.
