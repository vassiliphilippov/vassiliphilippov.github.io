poison parsley

noun
highly poisonous biennial herb
Pronunciation: / ˈpɔɪzən ˈpɑrsli / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪzən ˈpɑːsli / (BrE)
a tall, branching biennial plant with fernlike leaves and white flowers, highly toxic in all parts, usually found in damp areas

Examples

Tall poison parsley
Farmers removed poison parsley from their fields to protect their livestock.
Mistaking poison parsley for an edible herb can have fatal consequences.
The children were warned to stay away from the poison parsley growing along the riverbank.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hemlock - a poisonous plant, Conium maculatum, similar to poison parsley
  • antonym: parsley - an edible herb, Petroselinum crispum, often used in cooking
