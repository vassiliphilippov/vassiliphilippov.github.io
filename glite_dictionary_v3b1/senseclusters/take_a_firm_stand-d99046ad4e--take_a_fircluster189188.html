take a firm stand

verb
state a strong, unchanging position
Pronunciation: / teɪk ə fɜrm stænd / (AmE), / teɪk ə fɜːm stænd / (BrE)
to state one's opinion or decision strongly and refuse to change it, especially in the face of opposition

Examples

To take a firm stand against discrimination
It is important to take a firm stand against discrimination in the workplace.
She knew she had to take a firm stand on this issue, even if it wasn't popular.
When the company tried to cut back benefits, the union took a firm stand.

Related Terms

  • antonym: back down - to withdraw a claim or position under pressure
  • synonym: stand one's ground - to refuse to change your decision or position when challenged
