chatter

verb
cut unevenly due to tool vibration
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃætər / (AmE), / ˈtʃætə / (BrE)
(of a cutting tool) to cut a surface unevenly, typically due to rapid vibration or instability in the tool.

Examples

Tool chatters on metal
If the tool is not set properly, it may chatter and leave visible marks on the metal.
Improper clamping can cause the drill to chatter as it moves through the material.
The machinist adjusted the speed to prevent the lathe from chattering during the process.
