special verdict

noun
legal verdict stating jury's findings on specific facts
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈspɛʃəl ˈvɝːdɪkt / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəl ˈvɜːdɪkt / (BrE)
a verdict in a court case in which the jury makes findings on specific factual issues set by the judge, but does not decide in favor of either party

Examples

A special verdict in court
In complex cases, courts often request a special verdict to clarify the jury's findings on critical points.
The judge explained the difference between a general and a special verdict to the jurors.
The jury was instructed to deliver a special verdict based on the facts presented during the trial.

Related Terms

  • antonym: general verdict - a verdict in which the jury finds for one party, deciding the outcome
