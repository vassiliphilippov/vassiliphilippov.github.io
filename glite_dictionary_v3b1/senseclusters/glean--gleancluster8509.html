glean

verb
to gather bit by bit
4.2
Pronunciation: / ɡliːn / (AmE), / ɡliːn / (BrE)
to collect information, facts, or items gradually and with effort, often from various sources

Examples

To glean information
She managed to glean important facts from several different reports.
The journalist gleaned details about the event from eyewitnesses.
Over time, he gleans useful tips from his colleagues at work.

Over the years, he gleaned valuable insights from many different experts.
It can be difficult to glean the main ideas from such a complex article.

He tried to glean as much information as possible from the old documents.
