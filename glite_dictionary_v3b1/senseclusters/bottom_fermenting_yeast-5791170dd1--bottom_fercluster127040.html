bottom fermenting yeast

noun
yeast used in lager brewing
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑt̬əm fəˌmɛntɪŋ jist / (AmE), / ˈbɒtəm fəˌmɛntɪŋ jiːst / (BrE)
a type of brewer's yeast that sinks to the bottom of the fermentation vessel and is used to make lager beers

Examples

Bottom fermenting yeast for lager
Lager is traditionally brewed using bottom fermenting yeast, which works best at lower temperatures.
Modern breweries often select specific strains of bottom fermenting yeast to achieve consistent flavor in their lagers.
The distinction between ales and lagers lies mainly in the use of bottom fermenting yeast versus top-fermenting strains.
