Eustachian tube

noun
tube connecting middle ear to throat
9.3 5.5
Pronunciation: /juː.ˈsteɪ.ʃən tuːb/ /; / /juː.ˈsteɪ.ki.ən tuːb/ /; / /juː.ˈsteɪ.ʃən tjuːb/ (AmE), /juː.ˈsteɪ.ʃən tjuːb/ /; / /juː.ˈsteɪ.ki.ən tjuːb/ /; / /juː.ˈsteɪ.ʃən tuːb/ (BrE)
either of the pair of narrow tubes connecting the middle ear to the upper throat, which help equalize air pressure on both sides of the eardrum

Examples

Blocked Eustachian tube
A blocked Eustachian tube can cause discomfort and hearing problems.
Air travel can make your Eustachian tube work harder to balance ear pressure.
Children are more prone to Eustachian tube dysfunction than adults.
