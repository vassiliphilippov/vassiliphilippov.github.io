backland

noun
land behind buildings
Pronunciation: / ˈbæklænd / (AmE), / ˈbæklænd / (BrE)
land situated behind existing buildings, often considered for development or access purposes

Examples

Backland behind houses
The city council approved the construction of apartments on the backland behind the old factory.
Developers are interested in purchasing the backland for a new community park.
Access to the backland is provided by a narrow lane between two houses.

Related Terms

  • antonym: frontage - the part of land or property facing the street
  • synonym: rear land - land located at the back of a property or building
