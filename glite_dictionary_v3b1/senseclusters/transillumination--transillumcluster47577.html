transillumination

noun
light-based examination
Pronunciation: / ˌtrænzɪˌluːməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtrænzɪˌljuːmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of shining light through a body part or tissue to help with medical examination

Examples

Medical transillumination
The doctor used transillumination to detect abnormalities in the patient's sinus cavity.
Dentists sometimes rely on transillumination to find cracks in teeth that are hard to see.
During the examination, transillumination revealed the presence of fluid behind the eardrum.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inspection - the act of examining something closely
  • antonym: palpation - the use of touch in medical examination
