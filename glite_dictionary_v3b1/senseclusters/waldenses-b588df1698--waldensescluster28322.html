Waldenses

noun
members of a medieval Christian sect
Pronunciation: / wɔlˈdɛnziz / (AmE), / wɔːlˈdɛnziz / (BrE)
members of a Christian sect that originated in southern France in the late 12th century and later adopted Calvinist doctrines in the 16th century.

Examples

Medieval Waldenses sect
Historians have studied the unique practices of the Waldenses in religious history.
Many Waldenses eventually embraced Reformation ideas during the 16th century.
The Waldenses faced persecution for their beliefs throughout medieval Europe.
