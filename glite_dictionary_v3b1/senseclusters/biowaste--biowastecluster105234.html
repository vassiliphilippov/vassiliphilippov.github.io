biowaste

noun
organic waste material
3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈbaɪ.oʊ.weɪst/ (AmE), /ˈbaɪ.əʊ.weɪst/ (BrE)
organic waste from plants or animals, such as food scraps or plant matter, often collected for composting, recycling, or producing energy

Examples

Kitchen biowaste
Please separate biowaste from other trash for composting.
The city collects biowaste weekly to turn it into natural fertilizer.
You can put fruit peels and vegetable scraps in the biowaste bin.
The city encourages residents to separate biowaste from other trash for composting.
Farmers collect biowaste to generate biogas and reduce landfill use.
New regulations require restaurants to dispose of their biowaste in special containers.

  • synonym: compost - decayed organic material used as fertilizer
  • antonym: garbage - waste material, especially household waste
  • degree higher: recyclable - material that can be processed and used again
  • synonym: waste - unwanted or unusable material
