musculus adductor longus

noun
long adductor muscle of the thigh
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌs.kjə.ləs ædˈdʌk.tər ˈlɒŋ.ɡəs / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.kjʊ.ləs ædˈdʌk.tɔː ˈlɒŋ.ɡəs / (BrE)
a long muscle in the inner thigh that acts to adduct (draw inward) the thigh

Examples

The musculus adductor longus muscle
During the anatomy lecture, the professor pointed out the musculus adductor longus in the dissected specimen.
Injuries to the musculus adductor longus can cause groin pain and reduced mobility.
The musculus adductor longus is often strained in athletes who play soccer or run sprints.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adductor - a muscle that moves a part toward the body's midline
