microworld

noun
miniature environment
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪkroʊˌwɜrld / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊˌwɜːld / (BrE)
a very small or miniature world, especially one that can only be seen with a microscope

Examples

The microworld of cells
Scientists use electron microscopes to explore the fascinating microworld of cells and bacteria.
In the microworld, tiny organisms interact in ways invisible to the naked eye.
The documentary revealed the complexity of the microworld found in a single drop of pond water.

Related Terms

  • antonym: macroworld - the world of large-scale objects and phenomena
  • synonym: microcosm - a small, representative system having analogies to a larger system
