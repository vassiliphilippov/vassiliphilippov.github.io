resonate

verb
be understood or have an effect
4.8 4.9
Pronunciation: /ˈre.zə.neɪt/ (AmE), /ˈre.zə.neɪt/ (BrE)
(of an idea, feeling, etc.) to be received or understood in a meaningful or sympathetic way by someone; to have a lasting or powerful effect.

Examples

Resonate with people
Her speech really resonated with the audience, inspiring many to take action.
His experiences resonated deeply, reminding me of my own childhood.
The themes in the novel resonate with readers from different backgrounds.

  • synonym: strike a chord - to produce a strong feeling or response
