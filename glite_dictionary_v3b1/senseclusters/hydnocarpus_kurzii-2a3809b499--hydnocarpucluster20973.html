Hydnocarpus kurzii

noun
East Indian medicinal tree
Pronunciation: / haɪdˈnɑːkɑːrpəs ˈkɜːrzi.aɪ / (AmE), / hɪdˈnɒkɑːpəs ˈkɜːtsi.aɪ / (BrE)
An East Indian tree whose oily seeds produce chaulmoogra oil, formerly used in the treatment of leprosy.

Examples

Hydnocarpus kurzii tree
During the early 20th century, Hydnocarpus kurzii was an important source of chaulmoogra oil for leprosy treatment.
Researchers studied the effectiveness of oil extracted from Hydnocarpus kurzii seeds in treating skin conditions.
The medicinal properties of Hydnocarpus kurzii were especially valued in traditional Indian medicine.
