Federalist Party

proper noun
early U.S. political party favoring strong central government
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛdərəlɪst ˈpɑːrti / (AmE), / ˈfedərəlɪst ˈpɑːti / (BrE)
A major political party in the early United States, founded by Alexander Hamilton, which supported a strong central government.

Examples

Federalist Party leaders
Alexander Hamilton was one of the founders of the Federalist Party.
The Federalist Party eventually declined as the Democratic-Republicans gained prominence.
The Federalist Party played a key role in the early years of American government.
