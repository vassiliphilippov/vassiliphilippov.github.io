snatch

verb
abduct by force, often for ransom
5.6
Pronunciation: / snætʃ / (AmE), / snætʃ / (BrE)
to take someone away by force and often illegally, especially to demand a ransom

Examples

To snatch a child
Authorities believe the suspects snatched the child while she was playing in the yard.
The criminals planned to snatch the wealthy businessman as he walked to his car.
They tried to snatch the diplomat, but their plot was foiled by security.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kidnap - to abduct someone and hold them captive, usually for ransom
