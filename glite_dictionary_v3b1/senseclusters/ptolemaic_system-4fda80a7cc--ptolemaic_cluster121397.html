Ptolemaic system

noun
Earth-centered ancient astronomical model
5.4
Pronunciation: / toʊ.ləˈmeɪ.ɪk ˈsɪs.təm / (AmE), / tɒl.ɪˈmeɪ.ɪk ˈsɪs.təm / (BrE)
an astronomical model, proposed by Ptolemy, that places the Earth at the center of the universe, with the sun and planets revolving around it

Examples

The Ptolemaic system model
In the Ptolemaic system, the Earth remains stationary while all other celestial bodies revolve around it.
Medieval scholars used the Ptolemaic system to explain the movements of the planets.
The Ptolemaic system dominated Western astronomy until the Copernican revolution.

Related Terms

  • synonym: geocentric model - astronomical model with Earth at the center
  • antonym: heliocentric system - astronomical model with the sun at the center
