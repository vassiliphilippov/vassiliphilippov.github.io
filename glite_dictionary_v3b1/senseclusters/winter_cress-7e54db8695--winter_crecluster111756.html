winter cress

noun
a plant used in winter salads
9.9 5.6
Pronunciation: /ˈwɪn.tər kres/ (AmE), /ˈwɪn.tə kres/ (BrE)
a yellow-flowered plant of the cress family (genus Barbarea), often grown for use in winter salads

Examples

Winter cress plant
Winter cress is a hardy vegetable that can be harvested throughout the colder months.
A fresh salad made with winter cress tastes especially good in early spring.
Gardeners often plant winter cress for its nutritious leaves during the off-season.
In early spring, winter cress provides a welcome splash of yellow flowers in the garden.
Some foragers seek out winter cress for its tender leaves, which add a peppery flavor to salads.
The fields along the river are filled with blooming winter cress each April.
