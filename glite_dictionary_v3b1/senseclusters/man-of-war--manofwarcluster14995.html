man-of-war

noun
warship (historical)
Pronunciation: / ˌmæn əv ˈwɔr / (AmE), / ˌmæn əv ˈwɔː / (BrE)
a powerful warship, especially one of the type used from the 16th to the 19th centuries, equipped for combat

Examples

A man-of-war ship
During the 18th century, a man-of-war was the pride of any major fleet.
The British navy launched a man-of-war to patrol the coastal waters.
The museum displays a detailed model of a famous man-of-war.

Related Terms

  • synonym: warship - a ship equipped and armed for fighting
