Sphaeriaceae

noun
family of parasitic fungi (obsolete)
9.9
Pronunciation: / sfɪə.riˈeɪ.si.i / (AmE), / sfɪə.riˈeɪ.si.i / (BrE)
A family of parasitic fungi characterized by globose and sometimes necked or beaked perithecia, formerly recognized in mycology.

Examples

The Sphaeriaceae family
Recent molecular studies have reclassified many species formerly included in the Sphaeriaceae.
Textbooks from the early 20th century frequently reference the Sphaeriaceae in discussions of fungal diversity.
The Sphaeriaceae were once considered a major group of parasitic fungi in older classification systems.
