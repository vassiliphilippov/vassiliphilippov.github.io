take a bite

verb
to eat, try, or reduce something
1.0
Pronunciation: / teɪk ə baɪt / (AmE), / teɪk ə baɪt / (BrE)
to eat, try, or taste something by putting a small piece in the mouth; also, to reduce or diminish something, especially by a noticeable amount

Examples

To take a bite of food
After hours of work, they decided to take a bite out of their sandwiches.
Inflation continues to take a bite out of people's savings.
She encouraged him to take a bite of the homemade apple pie.

Related Terms

  • synonym: take a bite out of - to reduce or diminish by a noticeable amount
