paperbound

adjective
with paper cover
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪpərˌbaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈpeɪpəbˌaʊnd / (BrE)
having a cover made of paper instead of a hard material

Examples

Paperbound book
The library offered both paperbound and hardcover versions of the textbook.
She prefers paperbound books because they are lighter to carry.
A paperbound edition is usually less expensive than a hardcover one.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hardcover - a book with a rigid protective cover
  • synonym: paperback - a book with a flexible paper cover
