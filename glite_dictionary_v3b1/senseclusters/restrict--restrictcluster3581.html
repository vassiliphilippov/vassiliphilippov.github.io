restrict

verb
place a limit on; control amount or range
2.9
Pronunciation: / rɪˈstrɪkt / (AmE), / rɪˈstrɪkt / (BrE)
to limit or control the amount, use, access, or sharing of something

Examples

To restrict access
Due to safety concerns, the school decided to restrict access to the laboratory after hours.
Some diets restrict the number of calories you can consume each day.
The government may restrict the sale of certain medications to protect public health.
Access to the laboratory is restricted to authorized personnel.
The government decided to restrict the use of certain pesticides for safety reasons.
The park authority restricts vehicle entry during nesting season to protect wildlife.
Doctors may restrict your diet if you have certain health conditions.
The school has decided to restrict access to certain websites during class hours.
To improve safety, the company will restrict the number of visitors allowed each day.

Related Terms

  • antonym: allow - to permit information to be shared or disclosed
  • synonym: limit - to set boundaries on something; similar meaning to 'restrict'
  • antonym: permit - to allow something, opposite of restrict
  • antonym: prohibit - to formally forbid by law or authority
