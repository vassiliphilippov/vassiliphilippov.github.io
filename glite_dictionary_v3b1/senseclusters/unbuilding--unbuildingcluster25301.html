unbuilding

verb
to dismantle structure
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈbɪldɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈbɪldɪŋ / (BrE)
to take apart, dismantle, or demolish something that has been built

Examples

To unbuild a stadium
The team spent weeks unbuilding the old stadium to make way for a new park.
Careful unbuilding of the historic house allowed many materials to be reused.
After the exhibition ended, workers began unbuilding the temporary structures in the gallery.

Related Terms

  • antonym: construct - to build or put together something
  • synonym: demolish - to completely destroy a building or structure
  • synonym: dismantle - to take something apart into its separate pieces
