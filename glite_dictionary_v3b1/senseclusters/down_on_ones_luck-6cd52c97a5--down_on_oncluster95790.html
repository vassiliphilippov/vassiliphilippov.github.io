down on one's luck

adjective
experiencing a run of bad luck

experiencing a period of misfortune or bad luck, especially financially or in one's personal life

Examples

Down on his luck man
After losing his job and apartment, he was really down on his luck for months.
She tried to stay positive even though she was down on her luck and struggling to pay the bills.
When he saw a friend down on his luck, he offered him a place to stay.

Synonyms

  • unlucky
    1.0 3.22/m
    unlucky describes someone who often experiences bad fortune, while down on one's luck describes a period of hardship, often with money problems.
  • out of luck
    4.1 1.37/m
    out of luck means you fail to get what you need in one situation, while down on one's luck means a longer period of hardship, often financial.
  • luckless
    5.3 0.23/m
    luckless sounds formal or old-fashioned and means generally unfortunate, while down on one's luck is more conversational and suggests a current practical hardship.
  • hapless
    7.2 2.35/m
    hapless describes someone pitifully unlucky often in a literary or comic way, while down on one's luck focuses on real-life hardship, often financial.
