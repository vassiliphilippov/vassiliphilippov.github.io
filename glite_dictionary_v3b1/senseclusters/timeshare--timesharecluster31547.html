timeshare

noun
shared vacation property
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪmˌʃɛr / (AmE), / ˈtaɪmˌʃeə / (BrE)
a property arrangement where several people share the right to use a place, such as a vacation home, for set periods each year

Examples

A timeshare apartment
They bought a timeshare in Florida so they could visit every summer.
Owning a timeshare allows families to vacation in the same place each year.
The couple decided to sell their timeshare because they wanted to travel to new destinations.
They purchased a timeshare in Spain so they could visit every summer.
Many people attend presentations to learn about timeshares before deciding to buy.
Owning a timeshare allows you to vacation in the same place each year.
