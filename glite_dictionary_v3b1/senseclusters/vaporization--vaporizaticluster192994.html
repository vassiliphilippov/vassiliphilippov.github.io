vaporization

noun
process of becoming a vapor
1.0 4.5
Pronunciation: /veɪ.pə.rə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /veɪ.pə.rɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /veɪ.pə.rə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /veɪ.pə.rɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process by which a substance changes from a liquid or solid state to a vapor

Examples

Water vaporization
A sudden vaporization of liquid nitrogen creates a cloud of mist in the laboratory.
The vaporization of water requires a considerable amount of heat energy.
The vaporization process is essential in distillation techniques.
During the experiment, the vaporisation of the chemical was carefully measured.
Efficient vaporisation is crucial in the operation of steam engines.
The vaporisation of water begins to occur rapidly at 100°C.

Related Terms

  • antonym: condensation - the process of a substance changing from vapor to liquid
  • synonym: evaporation - the process by which liquid turns into vapor, especially at temperatures below boiling point
