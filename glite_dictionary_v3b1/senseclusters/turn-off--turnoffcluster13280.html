turn-off

noun
something that causes disinterest
2.2
Pronunciation: / ˈtɝn ˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈtɜːn ˌɒf / (BrE)
a person, thing, or situation that causes someone to lose interest or feel aversion

Examples

A turn-off moment
Cluttered workspaces are a turn-off for some employers during job interviews.
Smoking is a big turn-off for many people when choosing a partner.
The rude service was a total turn-off and they never returned to that restaurant.

Related Terms

  • antonym: attraction - something that draws interest or liking
  • antonym: turn-on - something that causes excitement or attraction
