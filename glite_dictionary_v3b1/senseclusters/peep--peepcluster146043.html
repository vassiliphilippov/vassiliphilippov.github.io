peep

verb
to speak in a timid or high-pitched voice
5.5
Pronunciation: / pip / (AmE), / piːp / (BrE)
to speak in a weak, hesitant, or high-pitched tone of voice, often softly or timidly

Examples

To peep a request
A child peeped shyly from behind his mother's skirt, asking for a drink.
She barely dared to peep a word during the tense meeting.
The kitten peeped a timid meow as it explored the new room.

Related Terms

  • synonym: murmur - to speak in a low, soft, and indistinct voice
  • synonym: whisper - to speak very softly using one's breath rather than one's throat
