etheric

adjective
non-physical; airy
Pronunciation: / ɪˈθɪrɪk /; / iːˈθɛrɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈθɪərɪk /; / iːˈθɛrɪk / (BrE)
relating to something light, airy, or belonging to a non-physical or spiritual realm

Examples

Etheric body
Some people believe in an etheric body that exists alongside the physical body.
The painting had an etheric quality, as if it captured something beyond the visible world.
She described a sense of etheric energy in the room during meditation.
The artist tried to capture the aetheric beauty of the dawn sky in her painting.
Some philosophers believed in an aetheric substance filling the universe beyond the visible world.
The music had an aetheric quality that made listeners feel as if they were floating.

Related Terms

  • antonym: earthly - relating to the physical world rather than the spiritual
  • synonym: ethereal - extremely delicate and light in a way that seems not of this world
  • antonym: physical - relating to the material or bodily world
