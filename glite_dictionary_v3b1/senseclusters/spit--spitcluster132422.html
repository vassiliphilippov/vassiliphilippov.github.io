spit

verb
speak angrily or contemptuously
Pronunciation: / spɪt / (AmE), / spɪt / (BrE)
to say something or act in an angry or contemptuous way

Examples

To spit words angrily
"I never want to see you again!" she spat through clenched teeth.
He spit out a bitter reply before storming out of the room.
She spat the insult at him, her voice trembling with fury.
He refuses to spit on the memory of his mentors by abandoning their methods.
I would never spit on your hard work after all you've done for the company.
Some critics spit on the new policy, calling it a step backwards.

  • antonym: respect - to show esteem or regard for someone or something
  • synonym: scorn - to show disdain or contempt
  • synonym: snap - say something sharply or abruptly
