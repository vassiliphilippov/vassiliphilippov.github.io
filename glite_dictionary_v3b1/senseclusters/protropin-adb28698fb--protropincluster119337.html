Protropin

noun
trade name for synthetic human growth hormone
9.9
Pronunciation: / proʊˈtroʊpɪn / (AmE), / prəˈtrəʊpɪn / (BrE)
A trade name for a synthetic form of human growth hormone used to treat children with hormone deficiencies; its use is prohibited in athletes and weightlifters.

Examples

Protropin hormone treatment
Some athletes have illegally used Protropin to enhance their physical performance.
The ban on Protropin in competitive sports is strictly enforced.
The doctor prescribed Protropin to help stimulate the child's growth.
