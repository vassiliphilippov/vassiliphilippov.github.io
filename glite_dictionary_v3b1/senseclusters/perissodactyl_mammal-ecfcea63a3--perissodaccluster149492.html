perissodactyl mammal

noun
hoofed mammal with odd number of toes
Pronunciation: / pəˌrɪs.əˈdæk.tɪl ˈmæm.əl / (AmE), / pəˌrɪs.əˈdæk.tɪl ˈmam.əl / (BrE)
A placental mammal with hooves and an odd number of toes on each foot, including horses, rhinoceroses, and tapirs.

Examples

Perissodactyl mammals with hooves
Researchers are studying the evolutionary history of perissodactyl mammals to learn more about their ancient relatives.
Rhinos and tapirs are less familiar perissodactyl mammals that also share this unique foot structure.
The horse is a classic example of a perissodactyl mammal distinguished by its single hoof on each foot.

Related Terms

  • antonym: artiodactyl mammal - placental mammals with an even number of toes on each foot
