display adapter

noun
device that outputs video signals in computers
5.3 3.6
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈspleɪ ə.ˈdæp.tər/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈspleɪ ə.ˈdæp.tə/ (BrE)
a device in a computer that converts data in memory into signals for a display screen

Examples

Computer display adapter
If your display adapter fails, your screen may go blank or show errors.
The display adapter determines what resolutions your monitor can use.
Upgrading the display adapter can significantly improve gaming performance.
If your monitor isn't working, the display adaptor may be faulty.
Modern laptops often have an integrated display adaptor on the motherboard.
The computer's display adaptor was upgraded to improve graphics performance.
