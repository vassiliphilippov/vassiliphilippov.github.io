denuclearize

verb
to remove nuclear weapons
7.5
Pronunciation: /diː.ˈnjuː.kɪ.ə.raɪz/ (AmE), /diː.ˈnjuː.kɪ.ə.raɪz/ (BrE)
to remove or ban nuclear weapons (and sometimes nuclear materials or facilities) from a place, country, or region

Examples

To denuclearize a country
The international community urged both sides to denuclearize the region for lasting peace.
Talks to denuclearize the peninsula have continued for several years without a final agreement.
Leaders signed a treaty to denuclearize all military bases in the area.
The international community urged both countries to denuclearize their border region for lasting peace.
After years of negotiations, the government agreed to denuclearize its military facilities.
The treaty requires all member states to denuclearize by dismantling their nuclear arsenals.
The agreement was designed to denuclearize the peninsula and promote peace.
International inspectors were sent to verify that the country had denuclearized its military bases.
Talks continue as both sides discuss how to denuclearize the border region.
