allegorist

noun
writer of allegories
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈæləɡərɪst / (AmE), / ˈælɪɡərɪst / (BrE)
a person who creates or interprets stories that have symbolic meanings beyond their literal sense

Examples

The allegorist tells stories
The famous allegorist used animals to represent human virtues and vices in his stories.
As an allegorist, she often layered her novels with hidden meanings and moral lessons.
Many readers admire the skill of a true allegorist in weaving complex symbolism into simple tales.
