satiable

adjective
able to be satisfied or fulfilled
2.7
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪ.ʃə.bəl /; / ˈseɪʃəbəl / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.ti.ə.bəl /; / ˈseɪʃəbəl / (BrE)
able to be satisfied or fulfilled (especially of a need, desire, or appetite)

Examples

A satiable thirst
After drinking two glasses of water, his satiable thirst was finally quenched.
Her satiable curiosity led her to explore many topics until she found answers.
The manager looked for someone with a satiable appetite for learning rather than endless demands.
Even after hours in the desert, his thirst remained satiable when offered enough water.
She has a satiable curiosity, always content when her questions are answered.
The dog seemed quite satiable and stopped eating once it was full.
