stagehand

noun
theater worker who handles backstage tasks
4.7 3.7
Pronunciation: / ˈsteɪdʒˌhænd / (AmE), / ˈsteɪdʒˌhænd / (BrE)
a person employed in a theater to move scenery, props, or operate technical equipment during a performance

Examples

A stagehand moves props
After the show, a stagehand helped dismantle the set and pack away the props.
She got her first job in theater as a stagehand, working behind the curtain to change scenery.
The stagehand quickly adjusted the lights between scenes without being seen by the audience.
