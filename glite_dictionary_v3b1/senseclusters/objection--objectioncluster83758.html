objection

noun
challenge in court to evidence or procedure
6.4 3.36/m
Pronunciation: / É™bËˆdÊ’É›k.ÊƒÉ™n / (AmE), / É™bËˆdÊ’É›k.ÊƒÉ™n / (BrE)
(law) A statement made by a party in a court case to challenge the propriety or admissibility of a question, witness, or piece of evidence, requesting the judge to rule on its acceptance.

Examples

Courtroom objection
After several objections, the judge finally overruled and allowed the witness to answer.
Her lawyer stood and made an objection during the cross-examination to challenge the evidence presented.
The defense attorney raised an objection to the prosecutor's question, citing irrelevance.

Synonyms

  • challenge
    3.5 8.60/m
    challenge is a general act of questioning or disputing used in many settings, while objection is a formal legal protest in court.
  • exception
    3.7 9.67/m
    exception names a reason to disapprove or exclude something, while objection is a formal court statement asking a judge to rule.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acceptance - willingness to agree or approve, especially in contrast to raising objections
  • synonym: protest - an expression or declaration of objection or disapproval
