balance the books

verb
check and correct financial records
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈbæl.əns ðə bʊks / (AmE), / ˈbæl.əns ðə bʊks / (BrE)
to check and adjust financial accounts so that the recorded amounts are accurate and consistent

Examples

To balance the books regularly
It's important for small business owners to balance the books regularly to avoid mistakes.
Mistakes can easily slip through if you don't balance the books at the end of each month.
The accountant stayed late to balance the books before the quarterly review.
