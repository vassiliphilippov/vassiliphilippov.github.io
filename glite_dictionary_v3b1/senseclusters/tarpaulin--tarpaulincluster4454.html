tarpaulin

noun
waterproof protective cloth
5.9
Pronunciation: / tɑrˈpɔlɪn / (AmE), / tɑːˈpɔːlɪn / (BrE)
A heavy-duty waterproof cloth, usually made of canvas or plastic, used to protect things from moisture or weather.

Examples

A tarpaulin cover
A blue tarpaulin flapped noisily in the wind, protecting the construction site.
Fishermen used a large tarpaulin to shelter their gear from the rain.
They covered the firewood with a tarpaulin to keep it dry during the storm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tarp - short form of tarpaulin; waterproof protective cover
