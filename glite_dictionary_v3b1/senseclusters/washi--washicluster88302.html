washi

noun
Japanese handmade paper
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑːʃi / (AmE), / ˈwɑːʃi / (BrE)
traditional Japanese paper made from the fibers of plants such as mulberry, mitsumata, or gampi

Examples

Traditional washi paper
The artist chose washi for its strength and beautiful texture in her calligraphy work.
He bought colorful sheets of washi to wrap gifts in a traditional Japanese style.
Museum displays often feature ancient documents written on washi because of its durability.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rice paper - thin paper made from plant fibers, often used in Asian art
