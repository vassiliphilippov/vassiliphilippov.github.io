demonetization

noun
removal of legal tender status from currency
Pronunciation: / diˌmʌnɪtəˈzeɪʃən /; / diːˌmɑːnɪtəˈzeɪʃn̩ / (AmE), / diˌmɒnɪtaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / diːˌmɒnɪtaɪˈzeɪʃn̩ / (BrE)
The act of officially removing the status of legal tender from a form of currency, such as coins or notes, so it is no longer recognized for payment in a country.

Examples

National demonetization policy
Economists debated the long-term impacts of demonetization on the country's cash-dependent economy.
Following the demonetization, people had to exchange old currency notes for new ones at banks.
The government announced the demonetization of all large denomination notes to combat corruption.
After the demonetisation of silver, only gold coins remained in official use.
The government announced the demonetisation of all 500 and 1000 rupee notes.
The sudden demonetisation led to long lines at banks as people exchanged old currency.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monetisation - the process of establishing something as legal tender or converting into money
  • antonym: remonetization - restoring legal tender status to a currency
