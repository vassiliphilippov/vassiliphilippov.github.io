circuit breaker

noun
software pattern that blocks failing service calls

(in software design) a pattern that detects repeated failures in a remote service and temporarily blocks or short-circuits calls to that service to prevent cascading failures and allow recovery.

Examples

Software circuit breaker
The system uses a circuit breaker to prevent cascading failures when the payment API times out.
After repeated errors, the team triggered the circuit breaker to stop requests and protect other services.
Implementing a circuit breaker pattern helped the startup keep the site responsive during outages.
