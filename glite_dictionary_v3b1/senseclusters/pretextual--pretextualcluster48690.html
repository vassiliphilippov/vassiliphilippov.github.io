pretextual

adjective
relating to a false reason
9.0
Pronunciation: /priː.ˈteks.tʃʊ.əl/ /; / /prɪ.ˈteks.tʃʊ.əl/ (AmE), /prɪ.ˈteks.tʃʊ.əl/ /; / /priː.ˈteks.tʃʊ.əl/ (BrE)
based on or relating to a false reason given to hide the true purpose or motive

Examples

A pretextual reason
The lawyer argued that the traffic stop was pretextual and used to search the car without cause.
Many believed the company's explanation for the layoffs was merely pretextual and hid the real financial issues.
The judge dismissed the evidence, stating it was obtained through a pretextual investigation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deceptive - intended to mislead or give a false impression
  • antonym: genuine - truly what something is said to be; authentic
