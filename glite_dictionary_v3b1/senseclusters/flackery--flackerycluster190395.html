flackery

noun
excessive promotion
Pronunciation: / ˈflækəri / (AmE), / ˈflækəri / (BrE)
the work or behavior of a publicist, especially when involving excessive or aggressive promotion

Examples

Publicist flackery
The new product launch was accompanied by a wave of flackery from the company's marketing team.
Some critics complained that the event was more about flackery than genuine achievement.
Tired of all the flackery, she decided to focus on the facts instead of the promotion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hype - exaggerated or intensive publicity or promotion
  • synonym: publicity - attention given to someone or something by the media
  • antonym: understatement - the presentation of something as being smaller or less important than it actually is
