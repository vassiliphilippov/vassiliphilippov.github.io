de-emphasis

noun
making less important
Pronunciation: / diːˈɛmfəsɪs / (AmE), / diːˈɛm.fə.sɪs / (BrE)
the act of making something less important or less prominent

Examples

De-emphasis of tradition
There has been a de-emphasis on traditional grammar in modern teaching.
The new policy led to a de-emphasis on standardized testing in schools.
Some critics argue that the de-emphasis of arts education is a mistake.

Related Terms

  • synonym: downplay - to make something seem less important
  • antonym: emphasis - special importance or attention given to something
