economic

adjective
profitable, financially rewarding
Pronunciation: / ˌiːkəˈnɑːmɪk / (AmE), / ˌiːkəˈnɒmɪk / (BrE)
profitable or cost-effective; producing a financial benefit or reward

Examples

Economic choice
Raising fares made the bus route economic for the company to operate again.
The new project became economic only after they reduced costs significantly.
The plant was closed because it was no longer economic to run it at that scale.

Related Terms

  • synonym: profitable - yielding financial benefit
  • antonym: uneconomic - not profitable; not yielding financial benefit
