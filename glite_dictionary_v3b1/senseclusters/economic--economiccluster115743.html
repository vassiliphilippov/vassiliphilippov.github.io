economic

adjective
profitable, financially rewarding
3.4 5.96/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåiÀêk…ôÀàn…ëÀêm…™k / (AmE), / ÀåiÀêk…ôÀàn…ím…™k / (BrE)
profitable or cost-effective; producing a financial benefit or reward

Examples

Economic choice
Raising fares made the bus route economic for the company to operate again.
The new project became economic only after they reduced costs significantly.
The plant was closed because it was no longer economic to run it at that scale.

Synonyms

    3.8 15/m

  • lucrative
    5.6 7.31/m

  • gainful
    4.9 0.41/m
    gainful describes work or activity that earns money, while economic is broader and means profitable or cost-effective.
  • moneymaking
    3.6 0.00/m
    moneymaking is informal and shows intent to earn money, while economic is neutral and means profitable or cost-effective.
  • cost-effective
    3.9 6.44/m

  • cost-efficient
    3.8 0.66/m
    cost-efficient focuses on saving money while keeping performance, while economic covers being profitable or cost-effective in general.
