fluorapatite

noun
form of apatite mineral with fluorine
4.1
Pronunciation: / flʊrˈæp.əˌtaɪt / (AmE), / flʊəˈrap.ə.taɪt / (BrE)
A mineral form of apatite in which fluorine predominates over chlorine, commonly found in igneous rocks and as a source of phosphorus.

Examples

Fluorapatite mineral form
Fluorapatite is an important source of phosphorus for chemical fertilizers.
Geologists identified fluorapatite crystals within the igneous rock sample.
Researchers studied the structure of fluorapatite to understand its resistance to acid.
