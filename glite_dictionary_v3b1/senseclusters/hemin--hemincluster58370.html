hemin

noun
iron-containing growth factor for bacteria
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈhiː.mɪn/ /; / /ˈhiː.mən/ (AmE), /ˈhiː.mɪn/ /; / /ˈhiː.mən/ (BrE)
(in microbiology) an iron-containing porphyrin compound used as a growth factor (the X factor) to culture certain fastidious bacteria, especially Haemophilus species.

Examples

Hemim for bacteria growth
The Haemophilus influenzae strain requires hemin to grow in culture.
Laboratory technicians add hemin to agar plates to support growth of fastidious bacteria.
The microbiology lab uses chocolate agar enriched with hemin when isolating Haemophilus from throat swabs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: X factor - a growth factor (hemin) required by some Haemophilus species for culture
  • synonym: heme - the iron-containing component of hemoglobin; chemically related to hemin
