nuclear fission

noun
splitting of atomic nucleus releasing energy
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˈnuː.kli.ɚ ˈfɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.klɪ.ə ˈfɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
a nuclear reaction in which a heavy atomic nucleus splits into two or more smaller nuclei, releasing energy

Examples

Nuclear fission reaction
Electricity from nuclear fission is considered a low-carbon energy source.
Scientists studied nuclear fission to understand how atomic bombs work.
The process of nuclear fission generates huge amounts of energy in power plants.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nuclear fusion - a nuclear reaction in which atomic nuclei combine to form a heavier nucleus
