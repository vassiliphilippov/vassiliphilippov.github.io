apostatize

verb
abandon one's beliefs or faith
Pronunciation: / əˈpɑːstəˌtaɪz /; / əˈpɑːstəˌsaɪz / (AmE), / əˈpɒstətaɪz / (BrE)
to formally renounce or abandon one's religious faith or previously held beliefs, especially in favor of another system

Examples

To apostatize from faith
After much inner turmoil, he finally decided to apostatize from the faith he was raised in.
Some people apostatize when faced with persecution, while others hold to their convictions.
Throughout history, many have been ostracized for daring to apostatize from mainstream beliefs.
After years of questioning, he finally chose to apostatise from the religion of his childhood.
She struggled with the choice to apostatise her long-held beliefs.
Some faced persecution simply because they refused to apostatise under pressure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: apostatize - abandon one's religious faith or allegiances (American spelling)
  • antonym: convert - to change from one belief or religion to another (opposite direction)
  • synonym: renounce - to formally abandon a belief or claim
