antagonize

verb
provoke hostility or anger
4.8 6.1
Pronunciation: /æn.ˈtæɡ.ə.naɪz/ (AmE), /æn.ˈtæɡ.ə.naɪz/ (BrE)
to make someone angry or unfriendly; to provoke or annoy someone so they react negatively

Examples

To antagonize a friend
His sarcastic comments only served to antagonize the other members of the team.
She warned him not to antagonize the neighbors with loud music late at night.
Trying to antagonize your boss could hurt your career prospects.
His harsh criticism only served to antagonize his colleagues further.
If you keep interrupting her, you'll only antagonize her and make things worse.
The company did not want to antagonize its customers with higher prices.
His comments did nothing but antagonise the crowd, making the situation worse.
Repeated criticism will only antagonise your colleagues and damage relationships.
She tried not to antagonise her boss by raising controversial issues at the meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: appease - to pacify or placate someone by acceding to their demands
  • antonym: conciliate - to stop someone from being angry; to pacify
  • antonym: placate - to make someone less angry or hostile
  • synonym: provoke - to stimulate a reaction, often anger or annoyance
