exclusiveness

noun
the quality of being exclusive; selectiveness
5.6
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈskluːsɪvnɪs / (AmE), / ɪkˈskluːsɪvnɪs / (BrE)
the quality or state of limiting involvement or association to a select group; tendency to be restricted to a particular circle.

Examples

Sense of exclusiveness
Her sense of exclusiveness kept her from making friends outside her usual circle.
Many criticize the exclusiveness of elite schools for perpetuating social divides.
The club's exclusiveness was evident in its strict membership criteria.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inclusiveness - the quality of including all people, not excluding anyone
