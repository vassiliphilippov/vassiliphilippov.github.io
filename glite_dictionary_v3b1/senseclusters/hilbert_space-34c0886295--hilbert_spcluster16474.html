Hilbert space

noun
complete infinite-dimensional linear metric space
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhɪlbɚt speɪs / (AmE), / ˈhɪlbət speɪs / (BrE)
A complete linear metric space, usually infinite-dimensional, in which the concepts of distance and orthogonality can be generalized from Euclidean space.

Examples

A Hilbert space example
Every finite-dimensional Euclidean space is an example of a Hilbert space.
Quantum mechanics is often formulated using Hilbert space as the mathematical framework.
The concept of orthogonality can be extended naturally in a Hilbert space.
