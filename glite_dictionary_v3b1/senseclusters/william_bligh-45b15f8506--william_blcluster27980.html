William Bligh

proper noun
British admiral, captain during Bounty mutiny
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪljəm blaɪ / (AmE), / ˈwɪljəm blaɪ / (BrE)
(1754–1817) British admiral and naval officer, best known as the captain of the H.M.S. Bounty during the famous mutiny of 1789.

Examples

William Bligh captain
After the mutiny, William Bligh navigated a small boat over 4,000 miles to safety.
Many historians have debated whether William Bligh was a harsh captain or a misunderstood leader.
The story of William Bligh and the mutiny on the Bounty is legendary in maritime history.
