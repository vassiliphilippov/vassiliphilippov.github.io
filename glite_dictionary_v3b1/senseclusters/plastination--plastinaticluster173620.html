plastination

noun
preservation of tissues with polymers
Pronunciation: / ˌplæstəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌplæstɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a technique for preserving biological tissues by replacing water and fat with plastic polymers, which are then hardened

Examples

Plastination technique
Advances in plastination have revolutionized anatomical research.
He is studying the plastination of organs to prepare better teaching models.
The museum features human bodies preserved through plastination for educational purposes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embalming - the process of preserving a dead body by treating it with chemicals
