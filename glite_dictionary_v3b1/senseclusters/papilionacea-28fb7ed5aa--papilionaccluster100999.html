Papilionacea

noun
group of leguminous plants with butterfly-shaped flowers
9.9
Pronunciation: / pəˌpɪliəˈneɪsiə / (AmE), / pəˌpɪlɪəˈneɪsiː / (BrE)
a group of leguminous plants whose flowers have butterfly-shaped corollas, formerly regarded as a separate family but now usually included within Fabaceae or Leguminosae

Examples

Papilionacea flower group
Botanists often classify peas and beans within the Papilionacea due to their unique floral structure.
Many species in the Papilionacea are cultivated for their edible seeds and flowers.
The characteristic butterfly-shaped flower of the Papilionacea distinguishes it from other plant families.
