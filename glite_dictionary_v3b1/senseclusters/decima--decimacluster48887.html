decima

noun
ten-line stanza
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛsɪmə / (AmE), / ˈdɛsɪmə / (BrE)
a ten-line stanza of poetry, especially used in Spanish or Latin American literature

Examples

A decima poem
The poet wrote a beautiful decima to honor his hometown.
In her latest collection, several poems are structured as a decima.
Learning to compose a decima is a tradition in some Latin American schools.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sonnet - a poem of fourteen lines with a fixed rhyme scheme
  • synonym: stanza - a grouped set of lines in a poem
