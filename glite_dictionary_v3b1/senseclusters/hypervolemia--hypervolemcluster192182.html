hypervolemia

noun
increased blood volume (medical)
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpɚvoʊˈlimiə /; / ˌhaɪpərvəˈlimiə / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəvəʊˈliːmɪə /; / ˌhʌɪpəːvɒˈliːmɪə / (BrE)
an abnormally increased volume of circulating blood in the body

Examples

Severe hypervolemia condition
Fluid overload can cause hypervolemia and lead to complications such as heart failure.
Patients with kidney disorders are at higher risk of developing hypervolemia.
The doctor diagnosed hypervolemia after observing significant swelling and elevated blood pressure.
Surgical patients are closely monitored to prevent the development of hypervolaemia during recovery.
The patient was diagnosed with hypervolaemia following symptoms of swelling and high blood pressure.
Treatment for hypervolaemia often includes diuretics to help reduce fluid levels.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hypervolemia - a blood disorder consisting of an increase in the volume of circulating blood (US spelling)
  • antonym: hypovolaemia - a decrease in the volume of circulating blood
  • antonym: hypovolemia - an abnormally decreased volume of circulating blood
