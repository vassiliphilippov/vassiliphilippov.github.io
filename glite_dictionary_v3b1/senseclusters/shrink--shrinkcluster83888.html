to become or make something smaller in size, especially for physical objects (often due to heat, moisture, or drying)

Examples

Clothes shrink in the wash
His jeans have shrunk after several washes.
If you wash it in hot water, the sweater may shrink considerably.
The company needs to shrink this image before uploading it to the website.
If you leave these flowers out of water, they'll quickly shrink and wither.

Synonyms

  • draw
    8.4 1.24/m
    draw means fabric pulls in and gets smaller from washing or heat, while shrink means physical things become smaller.
  • contract
    3.0 5.41/m
    contract is a more formal, broader verb for becoming smaller or tighter in many contexts, while shrink often means physical objects reduce in size.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shrivel - to become wrinkled and reduced in size, especially as a result of drying out
  • synonym: wither - to lose freshness and become dry and shrunken
