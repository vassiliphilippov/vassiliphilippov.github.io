stay afloat

verb
remain functional or successful
2.7
Pronunciation: / steɪ əˈfloʊt / (AmE), / steɪ əˈfləʊt / (BrE)
to remain functional or successful despite difficulties, especially financial or operational challenges

Examples

To stay afloat financially
She worked two jobs just to stay afloat and pay her bills.
The company cut expenses to stay afloat in a competitive market.
The small business struggled to stay afloat during the economic downturn.

Related Terms

  • antonym: go under - to fail, especially financially; to go bankrupt
  • synonym: survive - to continue to exist or function in spite of difficulty
