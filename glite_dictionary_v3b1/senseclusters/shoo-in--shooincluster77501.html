shoo-in

noun
a guaranteed or very likely winner
Pronunciation: / ˈʃuˌɪn / (AmE), / ˈʃuːˌɪn / (BrE)
A person or thing that is certain to win or succeed easily.

Examples

A shoo-in winner
Everyone thought their team was a shoo-in, but they surprisingly lost in the final round.
The film is considered a shoo-in to win Best Picture at the awards ceremony.
With his vast experience, he's a shoo-in for the job.

Related Terms

  • synonym: favorite - person or thing expected to win
  • antonym: underdog - person or team expected to lose
