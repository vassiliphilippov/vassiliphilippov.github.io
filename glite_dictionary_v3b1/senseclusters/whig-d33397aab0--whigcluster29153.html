Whig

noun
member of historic British political party
9.9
Pronunciation: / wɪɡ / (AmE), / wɪɡ / (BrE)
A member of a British political party active from the late 17th to the early 19th centuries, which advocated for social reform and constitutional monarchism, and was opposed to the Tories.

Examples

British Whig leader
Charles Fox was a prominent Whig who championed civil liberties.
In the early 1800s, Whig leaders pushed for parliamentary reforms.
The Whig party played an important role during the reign of George III.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Tory - member of the rival British political party to the Whigs
