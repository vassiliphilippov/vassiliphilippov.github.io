hemoprotein

noun
protein containing heme group
8.8 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåhiÀêmo äÀàpro ätiÀên / (AmE), / ÀåhiÀêm…ô äÀàpr…ô ätiÀên / (BrE)
A protein that contains a heme group (a compound of iron and porphyrin) as its prosthetic group, found in proteins such as hemoglobin and myoglobin and essential in various biological processes such as oxygen transport and cellular respiration.

Examples

A hemoprotein molecule
Cytochromes, a large family of hemoproteins, play a key role in cellular respiration.
Hemoglobin is a well-known hemoprotein responsible for carrying oxygen in the blood.
Researchers studied the structure of the bacterial hemoprotein to better understand its function.
Certain enzymes that act as haemoproteins play a key role in metabolic reactions.
Hemoglobin is a well-known haemoprotein responsible for oxygen transport in the blood.
Scientists study the structure of haemoproteins to better understand cellular respiration.
