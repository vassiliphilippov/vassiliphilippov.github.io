possibilism

noun
philosophical theory of possibility
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːsəbɪˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈpɒsəbɪˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
the philosophical view that possible things or states of affairs have some form of real existence, not just as ideas

Examples

Philosophical possibilism
In his lecture, the professor explained possibilism as an alternative to actualism in metaphysics.
Some philosophers argue that possibilism helps us understand the nature of potential events.
The debate between possibilism and actualism is central to discussions about the existence of possible worlds.

Related Terms

  • antonym: actualism - the view that only actual things exist
  • synonym: modal realism - the theory that possible worlds are as real as the actual world
