Onobrychis viciifolia

noun
Eurasian herb used as forage crop
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊnəˈbraɪkɪs ˌvɪsiɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˌɒnəˈbrɪkɪs ˌvɪsiɪˈfəʊliə / (BrE)
A Eurasian perennial herb with pale pink flowers and curved pods, cultivated as a forage crop and source of honey, especially in Britain.

Examples

Onobrychis viciifolia forage crop
Onobrychis viciifolia is prized by farmers for its high nutritional value as a forage crop.
Beekeepers favor fields of Onobrychis viciifolia for producing quality honey.
In calcareous grasslands, Onobrychis viciifolia provides essential food for livestock.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sainfoin - another name for Onobrychis viciifolia, a forage crop
