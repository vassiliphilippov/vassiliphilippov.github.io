mRNA

noun
biotech platform using messenger RNA
4.2
Pronunciation: /em.ɑːr.en.ˈeɪ/ /; / /ˈmes.ən.dʒər em.ɑːr.en.ˈeɪ/ (AmE), /em.ɑː.en.ˈeɪ/ /; / /ˈmes.ən.dʒə em.ɑː.en.ˈeɪ/ (BrE)
(in biotechnology and medicine) a platform or technology that uses synthetic messenger RNA to instruct cells to produce specific proteins, used for vaccines and therapies.

Examples

mRNA vaccine technology
Researchers are exploring an mRNA platform to develop rapid vaccines and targeted therapies.
Companies invested millions to scale production of mRNA platforms for global vaccine distribution.
The trial tested an mRNA therapeutic designed to cause patients' cells to produce the missing protein.

Related Terms

  • synonym: messenger RNA - the molecular component (messenger RNA) used by this technology to encode proteins
