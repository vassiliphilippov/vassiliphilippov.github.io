amidate

noun
amide-derived ion
Pronunciation: / ˈæmɪˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈæmɪˌdeɪt / (BrE)
a negatively charged ion formed by removing a proton from an amide, often acting as a ligand in coordination chemistry

Examples

Amidate ion
The amidate ligand played a crucial role in stabilizing the metal complex.
Researchers studied the binding properties of the amidate in various chemical reactions.
A strong amidate can enhance the reactivity of certain coordination compounds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amide - a compound derived from ammonia by replacing a hydrogen atom with an acyl group
  • synonym: anion - a negatively charged ion
