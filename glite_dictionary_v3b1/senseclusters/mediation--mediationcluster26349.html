mediation

noun
intervention to help parties reach agreement
4.2 4.82/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåmiÀêdiÀàe…™ É…ôn / (AmE), / ÀåmiÀêdiÀàe…™ É…ôn / (BrE)
the process in which an independent or impartial person helps two or more parties reach an agreement or settle a dispute

Examples

Family mediation
Family mediation can help parents agree on arrangements for their children after a separation.
International mediation played a crucial role in ending the prolonged conflict.
The company entered mediation to resolve the labor dispute without going to court.
During the labor dispute, both sides consented to mediation before escalating to a strike.
Many businesses use mediation to resolve conflicts without going to court.
The couple agreed to try mediation in hopes of settling their divorce amicably.

Synonyms

  • intermediation
    8.1 0.18/m
    intermediation is the act of a neutral person intervening to help parties agree, while mediation is the formal process led by such a third party.
  • conciliation
    6.7 0.89/m
    conciliation seeks to restore peace and friendly relations between groups, while mediation is a structured process to reach a formal agreement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arbitration - the formal process of resolving a dispute by an impartial third party who imposes a decision
  • synonym: conciliation - the action of stopping someone from being angry; the action of mediating between two disputing people or groups
  • synonym: negotiation - discussion aimed at reaching an agreement
