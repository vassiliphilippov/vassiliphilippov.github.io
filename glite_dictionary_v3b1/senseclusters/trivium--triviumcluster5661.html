trivium

noun
introductory curriculum of medieval universities
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɪviəm / (AmE), / ˈtrɪvɪəm / (BrE)
(historical) The introductory curriculum at a medieval university, consisting of grammar, logic, and rhetoric, regarded as a foundation for further study.

Examples

The trivium curriculum
During the Middle Ages, students began their studies with the trivium before advancing to more complex subjects.
Many educational reforms looked back at the trivium as a model for foundational learning.
The trivium helped scholars develop essential skills in argument and expression.
