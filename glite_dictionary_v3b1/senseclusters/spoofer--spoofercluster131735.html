spoofer

noun
parody performer
Pronunciation: / ˈspuːfər / (AmE), / ˈspuːfə / (BrE)
a person who creates or performs humorous imitations or parodies to mock someone or something

Examples

A spoofer on TV
The comedian became famous as a spoofer of popular movies and TV shows.
As a skilled spoofer, she could mimic celebrities with hilarious accuracy.
The online spoofer released a parody video that quickly went viral.

Related Terms

  • synonym: impersonator - a person who imitates others, often for entertainment
  • synonym: satirist - a person who uses humor or irony to criticize or mock
  • antonym: target - a person or thing that is the subject of a spoof or parody
