discrepant

adjective
differing; not in agreement
6.4 6.6
Pronunciation: / dɪˈskrɛpənt / (AmE), / dɪˈskrɛpənt / (BrE)
not in agreement; differing or inconsistent, especially with facts, details, or circumstances

Examples

A discrepant report
A discrepant report may indicate errors in data collection or analysis.
His testimony was discrepant with the evidence presented by the prosecution.
The results of the two experiments are discrepant and require further investigation.
If financial statements are discrepant across departments, an audit may be necessary.
The witness gave a discrepant account of the events compared to the official report.
Their results were discrepant with previous studies and required further investigation.
If the figures are discrepant, we need to check for calculation errors.
The scientist was puzzled by the discrepant results from two similar experiments.
There were several discrepant accounts of what happened during the meeting.
