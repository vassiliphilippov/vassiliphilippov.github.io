soul brother

noun
Black man showing solidarity with others
1.0
Pronunciation: / soʊl ˈbrʌðər / (AmE), / səʊl ˈbrʌðə / (BrE)
a Black man regarded as sharing a sense of solidarity and identity with others, especially within the African American community

Examples

Soul brother in community
During the civil rights movement, the term soul brother gained prominence as a symbol of unity.
Even though they had just met, they felt an instant soul brother connection.
He called out to his soul brother across the room, greeting him with a big smile.

  • synonym: brother - a male sibling or fellow man
