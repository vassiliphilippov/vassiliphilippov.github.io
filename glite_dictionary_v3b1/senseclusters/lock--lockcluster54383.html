lock

noun
mechanical restraint in vehicle ignition
5.9
Pronunciation: / lɑk / (AmE), / lɒk / (BrE)
a mechanical device used to fasten or secure something (such as a door, vehicle, or gun), usually operated by a key or code

Examples

Door lock
He couldn't start the engine because the lock had been tampered with.
Many vehicles use an electronic lock to improve security.
The car was protected by an advanced lock in the ignition system.
A malfunction in the lock could prevent the gun from firing properly.
Many old firearms, such as flintlocks and matchlocks, are named after their type of lock.
The soldier examined the lock of his antique musket before loading it.
He turned the key in the lock and opened the door slowly.
The desk drawer won't open because the lock is jammed.
You need a special code to open the electronic lock on the safe.

  • synonym: latch - a device for keeping a door, gate, or the like closed, operated by a lever or spring
