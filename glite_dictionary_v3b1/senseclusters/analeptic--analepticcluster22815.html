analeptic

noun
central nervous system stimulant (medication)
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˌænəˈlɛptɪk / (AmE), / ˌanəˈlɛptɪk / (BrE)
A drug or medication that stimulates the central nervous system, often used to counteract depression or unconsciousness.

Examples

An analeptic drug
Research into new analeptic drugs is ongoing in the pharmaceutical industry.
Some analeptics are used in emergency medicine to treat respiratory failure.
