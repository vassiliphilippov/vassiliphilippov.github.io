vaccine

noun
substance for inducing immunity to disease
2.5 2.8
Pronunciation: / vækˈsiːn / (AmE), / vækˈsiːn / (BrE)
A substance containing weakened or killed microorganisms, toxins, or parts of them, given to stimulate the body's immune response and provide immunity against specific diseases.

Examples

A flu vaccine
A new vaccine has been developed to prevent the spread of the virus.
Many countries aim to make the COVID-19 vaccine accessible to all citizens.
The flu vaccine is recommended each year to protect against influenza.

Related Terms

  • antonym: antidote - a substance that counteracts a poison or disease
  • synonym: immunization - the process of making a person immune to infection
