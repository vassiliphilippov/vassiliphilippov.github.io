vandalize

verb
deliberately damage or destroy property
Pronunciation: / ˈvændəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvændəlaɪz / (BrE)
to deliberately damage or destroy public or private property, especially public property, by acts of vandalism, often in a wanton or senseless way

Examples

To vandalize public property
It's a serious crime to vandalize public property, and offenders may face heavy fines.
Someone tried to vandalize the mural last night, but was caught by the security cameras.
The community is raising money to repair the playground after it was vandalized last weekend.
It's illegal to vandalise historical monuments, and offenders can face heavy fines.
Police are searching for the people who vandalised several cars in the parking lot.
The bus shelter was vandalised overnight, with graffiti covering the windows.
