minor scale

noun
musical scale with a somber quality
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪnɚ skeɪl / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnə skeɪl / (BrE)
A diatonic musical scale with a specific pattern of intervals that gives it a characteristically somber or sad sound, typically having a lowered third, sixth, and seventh degree compared to a major scale.

Examples

The minor scale pattern
Many classical pieces are composed in the minor scale to evoke a sense of melancholy.
She practiced the C minor scale on the piano to improve her finger technique.
Understanding the difference between a major and a minor scale is essential for music theory students.

Related Terms

  • antonym: major scale - a diatonic scale with a pattern of intervals differing from the minor scale, producing a brighter sound
