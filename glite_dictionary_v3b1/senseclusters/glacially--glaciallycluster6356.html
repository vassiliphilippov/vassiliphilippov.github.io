glacially

adverb
very slowly
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈɡleɪʃəli / (AmE), / ˈɡleɪsɪəli / (BrE)
in a very slow or extremely gradual manner, like the movement of a glacier

Examples

Glacially slow progress
Progress on the construction site advanced glacially because of repeated setbacks.
The negotiations moved glacially, frustrating everyone involved in the process.
Change in the organization happened glacially, despite calls for urgent reform.

Related Terms

  • antonym: quickly - at a fast speed; rapidly
  • synonym: slowly - at a slow speed; not quickly
