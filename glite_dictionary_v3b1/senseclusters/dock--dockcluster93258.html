dock

noun
platform for access to ships and boats
3.2 1.4
Pronunciation: /dɑːk/ (AmE), /dɒk/ (BrE)
a platform or structure in a harbor or on the shore where ships or boats are loaded, unloaded, or tied up

Examples

Fishing dock
Fishermen unloaded their catch onto the weathered wooden dock each morning.
The boat was tied securely to the dock as passengers prepared to board.
We strolled along the dock, watching the sun set over the water.
A new dock was constructed to accommodate larger cruise liners.
The dock was crowded with workers unloading cargo from the morning ships.
They pulled the boat into the dock for repairs after the storm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pier - a structure built on posts extending from land out over water, used as a landing place for ships
  • synonym: quay - a stone or metal platform lying alongside or projecting into water for loading and unloading ships
  • synonym: wharf - a structure on the shore of a harbor where ships may dock to load and unload cargo or passengers
