Sicarii

proper noun
ancient Jewish zealots
Pronunciation: / sɪˈkɑːriaɪ / (AmE), / sɪˈkɑːriaɪ / (BrE)
a group of Jewish zealots in ancient Judea who used violence against Roman authorities and collaborators

Examples

Sicarii rebel group
The Sicarii were feared for their secret attacks against Roman officials in Jerusalem.
Historians debate the impact of the Sicarii on the outcome of the Jewish revolt.
The Sicarii became known for carrying small daggers hidden in their clothing.
