nectar-rich

adjective
having an abundance of nectar (of plants)
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛktɚ rɪtʃ / (AmE), / ˈnɛktə rɪtʃ / (BrE)
(of a plant) having an abundance of nectar, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Examples

nectar-rich plants
Bees are especially drawn to nectar-rich plants during the blooming season.
Planting nectar-rich species can help support local pollinator populations.
The garden was filled with nectar-rich flowers, attracting dozens of butterflies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nectarless - containing no nectar
