glazing

noun
transparent paint layer
5.4
Pronunciation: /ˈgleɪ.zɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈgleɪ.zɪŋ/ (BrE)
a thin, transparent layer of paint applied over another layer to alter the color, tone, or finish in painting

Examples

Painting glazing
The artist achieved a luminous effect by applying several layers of glazing over the base colors.
Careful glazing can create subtle shifts in tone and depth in a painting.
She learned how to use glazing to enhance the vibrancy of her watercolor artwork.

Related Terms

  • antonym: impasto - a painting technique using thick layers of paint
  • synonym: wash - a thin, diluted layer of paint applied to a surface
