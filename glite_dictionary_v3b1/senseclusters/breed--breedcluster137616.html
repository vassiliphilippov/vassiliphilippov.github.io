breed

verb
(of animals) reproduce; have young
4.1 1.6
Pronunciation: /briːd/ (AmE), /briːd/ (BrE)
(of animals) to produce offspring, or to cause animals to produce offspring, usually by mating or selective breeding

Examples

To breed horses
Horses breed once a year, usually in the spring.
Pandas rarely breed in captivity.
Some fish breed only in freshwater rivers despite living in the ocean.
Many farmers breed cattle to improve milk production.
She has been breeding rare birds for several years.
They breed horses on their ranch for racing and work.
Horse owners often carefully select which animals to breed in order to improve bloodlines.
It is common practice to breed thoroughbreds for racing.
The stallion was brought in to breed with the prize mare.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mate - to copulate or pair for breeding
  • synonym: rear - to raise and take care of young animals or children
  • synonym: reproduce - to produce offspring
  • antonym: sterilize - to make an animal unable to produce offspring
