oxytocic

noun
drug that induces labor
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˌɑksɪˈtoʊsɪk / (AmE), / ˌɒksɪˈtəʊsɪk / (BrE)
a drug that induces labor by stimulating contractions of the muscles of the uterus

Examples

An oxytocic drug
Misuse of an oxytocic can lead to complications, so it must be carefully monitored.
Some births require the use of an oxytocic when labor does not start naturally.
The doctor administered an oxytocic to help induce labor in the patient.

Related Terms

  • synonym: uterotonic - an agent that increases the tone or contractions of the uterus
