multifocal

adjective
in several locations
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌltiˈfoʊkəl / (AmE), / ˌmʌltɪˈfəʊkəl / (BrE)
occurring in or originating from several separate locations or centers, especially in a medical context

Examples

Multifocal disease
The doctor explained that the infection was multifocal, affecting several areas of the lung.
A multifocal approach is often needed when treating complex diseases with multiple sites.
Imaging revealed multifocal lesions scattered throughout the patient's liver.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unifocal - occurring in a single location or center
