risk arbitrage

noun
investment strategy involving merger and acquisition deals
9.9 0.03/m

a form of arbitrage in which an investor buys stock in a company being targeted for acquisition and simultaneously sells stock in the acquiring company, profiting if the deal goes through; carries significant risk if the takeover fails

Examples

Many hedge funds specialize in risk arbitrage to profit from merger announcements.
The failure of the merger caused significant losses for those engaged in risk arbitrage.
