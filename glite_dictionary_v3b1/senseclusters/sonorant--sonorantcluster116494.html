sonorant

noun
continuous speech sound
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑːnəˌrænt / (AmE), / ˈsɒnərənt / (BrE)
a speech sound produced with continuous, non-turbulent airflow, such as a vowel, nasal, or liquid

Examples

A sonorant consonant
The English sound 'l' is classified as a sonorant because of its smooth airflow.
Linguists study how sonorants differ from other types of consonants in various languages.
In the word 'man', the 'm' is a sonorant that is easy to sustain.
In English, the sound represented by 'l' is a sonorant and is voiced.
Linguists classify vowels and nasals as types of sonorants because of their smooth airflow.
The difference between a sonorant and an obstruent is important in phonetics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nasal - a speech sound produced by allowing air to escape through the nose
  • antonym: obstruent - a speech sound produced with significant airflow obstruction
  • synonym: vowel - a speech sound made without significant constriction of airflow
