restraint

noun
self-control in behavior
6.6
Pronunciation: / rɪˈstreɪnt / (AmE), / rɪˈstreɪnt / (BrE)
the ability to control one's emotions or impulses, especially in social or personal conduct

Examples

Show restraint in anger
Displaying restraint is often seen as a sign of maturity.
He showed remarkable restraint during the heated argument.
She kept her restraint even when provoked by criticism.

Related Terms

  • antonym: impulsiveness - the tendency to act without careful thought
  • synonym: self-control - the ability to control oneself, in particular one's emotions and desires
