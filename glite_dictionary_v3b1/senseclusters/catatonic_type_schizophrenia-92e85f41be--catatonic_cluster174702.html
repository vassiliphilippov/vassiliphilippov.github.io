catatonic type schizophrenia

noun
a form of schizophrenia marked by immobility and agitation
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˌkætəˈtɑnɪk taɪp skɪtsəˈfriːniə / (AmE), / ˌkatəˈtɒnɪk tʌɪp skɪtsəˈfriːnɪə / (BrE)
A form of schizophrenia characterized by extended periods of immobility or stupor, occasionally interrupted by brief episodes of agitation or excitement.

Examples

Catatonic type schizophrenia symptoms
Periods of extreme excitement sometimes interrupted the otherwise silent stupor characteristic of catatonic type schizophrenia.
The patient with catatonic type schizophrenia remained motionless for hours, barely responding to external stimuli.
Treatment for catatonic type schizophrenia requires careful management of both its immobile and agitated phases.

Related Terms

  • synonym: catatonia - a state of psycho-motor immobility and behavioral abnormality
  • synonym: schizophrenia - a chronic mental disorder involving distortions in thinking, perception, emotions, language, sense of self, and behavior
