mean deviation from the mean

noun
statistical average of absolute deviations from the mean
Pronunciation: / miːn ˌdiviˈeɪʃən frəm ðə miːn / (AmE), / miːn ˌdiːvɪˈeɪʃən frəm ðə miːn / (BrE)
the average of the absolute differences between each value in a data set and the mean of the set

Examples

The mean deviation from the mean value
The mean deviation from the mean provides a simple measure of statistical dispersion.
To summarize the spread of exam scores, the teacher calculated the mean deviation from the mean.
When analyzing data variability, some researchers prefer the mean deviation from the mean over the standard deviation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mean absolute deviation - the mean of the absolute deviations from a central value, often the mean or median
  • degree higher: standard deviation - a measure of the amount of variation or dispersion of a set of values
