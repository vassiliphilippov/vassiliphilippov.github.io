legitimist

adjective
supporting royal succession
Pronunciation: / lɪˈdʒɪtəmɪst / (AmE), / lɪˈdʒɪtɪmɪst / (BrE)
supporting the principle of legitimate succession to a throne or relating to the Legitimist movement.

Examples

Legitimist claim to the throne
The legitimist faction refused to recognize the new government after the king was deposed.
Her legitimist beliefs were rooted in a deep respect for royal tradition.
During the debate, he defended his legitimist stance on the succession crisis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: republican - supporting a government without a monarchy
  • synonym: royalist - supporting a monarchy or royal family
