bulghur

noun
crushed, parboiled wheat used in cooking
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊlɡər / (AmE), / ˈbʊl.ɡər / (BrE)
a cereal food made from wheat that has been parboiled, dried, and crushed, commonly used in Middle Eastern dishes

Examples

Cooked bulghur wheat
Many Mediterranean dishes include bulghur as a nutritious alternative to rice or pasta.
Soak the bulghur in hot water for fifteen minutes before adding it to your recipe.
The tabbouleh salad was made with fresh bulghur, parsley, tomatoes, and lemon juice.

Related Terms

  • synonym: couscous - granulated durum wheat semolina, a staple in North African cuisine
  • degree lower: rice - edible starchy cereal grain often used similarly to bulghur
