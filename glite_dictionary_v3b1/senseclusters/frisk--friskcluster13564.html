frisk

verb
search a person for concealed items by patting clothing
8.6 5.3
Pronunciation: /frɪsk/ (AmE), /frɪsk/ (BrE)
(of a person, especially a police officer) to search someone for concealed items, especially weapons or drugs, by quickly running the hands over their clothing and through their pockets.

Examples

To frisk a suspect
If you refuse to be frisked, you may not be allowed to board the plane.
She felt nervous as the officer began to frisk her at the airport checkpoint.
The security guard frisked each visitor before letting them enter the building.

Related Terms

  • synonym: search - to look carefully for something or through something
