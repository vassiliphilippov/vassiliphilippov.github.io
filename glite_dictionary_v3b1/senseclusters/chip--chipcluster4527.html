chip

verb
break off a small piece
Pronunciation: / tʃɪp / (AmE), / tʃɪp / (BrE)
to break off a small piece from something, or to cut or shape something by breaking small pieces off

Examples

To chip the paint
Over the years, the paint on the fence has chipped in several places.
She accidentally chipped her favorite mug while washing it.
The sculptor chipped away at the stone to form a delicate nose.
Be careful not to chip the cup when you wash it.
He chipped his tooth on a hard piece of candy.
The paint started to chip off the old windowsill.
He carefully chipped his initials into the wooden bench.
The sculptor chipped away at the marble to reveal the statue inside.
Workers chip the ice off the sidewalks every winter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: break off - to become separated from a whole
  • synonym: carve - to cut into a surface, especially to produce an object or design
  • synonym: chip away - to remove small pieces from something by hitting or cutting
  • degree lower: crack - to break without separating into pieces
  • synonym: nick - to make a small cut or notch
  • degree higher: shatter - to break into many pieces
