Müllerian

adjective
about mimicry in nature
9.9
Pronunciation: / mʊˈlɛriən / (AmE), / mjuːˈlɛːriən / (BrE)
relating to Müllerian mimicry, where two or more harmful species evolve similar warning signals to deter predators

Examples

Müllerian mimicry pattern
The bright colors of these frogs are a Müllerian adaptation to warn predators of their toxicity.
Scientists studied how Müllerian mimicry benefits both butterfly species in the rainforest.
Several insects display Müllerian patterns, making it easier for predators to learn to avoid them.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Batesian - relating to Batesian mimicry, where a harmless species mimics a harmful one
