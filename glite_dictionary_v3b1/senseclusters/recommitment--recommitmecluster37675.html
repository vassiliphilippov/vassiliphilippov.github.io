recommitment

noun
renewed promise or support
4.8
Pronunciation: /riː.kə.ˈmɪt.mənt/ /; / /rɪ.kə.ˈmɪt.mənt/ (AmE), /riː.kə.ˈmɪt.mənt/ /; / /rɪ.kə.ˈmɪt.mənt/ (BrE)
a renewed promise or decision to support, fund, or dedicate oneself to something (such as a goal, cause, or responsibility)

Examples

Recommitment to a goal
The company announced a recommitment to its sustainability goals after reviewing its progress.
A recommitment of funds from investors helped the startup overcome its financial difficulties.
After negotiations, the board made a recommitment to supporting community outreach programs.
After a difficult year, the organization announced its recommitment to supporting local communities.
The athlete's recommitment to training led to improved performance in the next season.
A public ceremony marked the city's recommitment to environmental sustainability.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abandonment - the act of giving up or leaving something behind
  • synonym: commitment - a promise or firm decision to do something or support something
  • synonym: dedication - the quality of being devoted to a task or purpose
  • antonym: withdrawal - the act of removing support, money, or resources
