court favor

verb
seek approval by flattery
6.3
Pronunciation: / kɔrt ˈfeɪvɚ /; / kɔrt ˈfeɪvər / (AmE), / kɔːt ˈfeɪvə / (BrE)
to try to gain someone's approval or goodwill by flattery or obsequious behavior

Examples

To court favor with boss
It was clear that he was courting favor with his supervisor to get promoted.
Politicians often court favor with powerful donors during election campaigns.
She tried to court favor with her new boss by complimenting his every decision.
He tried to court favour with the manager by complimenting her ideas at every meeting.
Instead of working hard, she spent her time courting favour with influential colleagues.
Politicians often court favour with powerful interest groups before elections.

Related Terms

  • synonym: curry favor - to seek someone's approval by excessive flattery
  • synonym: curry favour - to seek to gain favor by flattery or obsequious behavior
  • synonym: flatter - to praise excessively or insincerely
