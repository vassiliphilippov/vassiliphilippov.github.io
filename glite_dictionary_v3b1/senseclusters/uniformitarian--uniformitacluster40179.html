uniformitarian

adjective
gradual and consistent
Pronunciation: / ˌjuːnɪˌfɔːrmɪˈtɛriən / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪˌfɔːmɪˈteəriən / (BrE)
relating to the idea that changes happen gradually and consistently over long periods of time

Examples

Uniformitarian principle
The uniformitarian principle is important in understanding how landscapes develop over millions of years.
Geologists often use a uniformitarian approach to explain the slow formation of mountains.
The scientist argued for a uniformitarian explanation rather than a sudden, dramatic event.

Related Terms

  • antonym: catastrophic - involving sudden and major change
  • synonym: gradual - happening slowly over time
