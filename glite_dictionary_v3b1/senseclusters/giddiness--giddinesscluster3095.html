giddiness

noun
sensation of dizziness; feeling faint or unsteady
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɪdi.nəs /; / ˈɡɪdinəs / (AmE), / ˈɡɪd.i.nəs /; / ˈɡɪdinəs / (BrE)
a feeling of dizziness or light-headedness, sometimes with excitement or happiness

Examples

Sudden giddiness
After standing up too quickly, she was overcome by a sudden giddiness and had to sit down.
Intense heat and dehydration can often lead to a feeling of giddiness.
The mountain climber battled waves of giddiness as he peered over the edge of the cliff.
She felt a wave of giddiness after spinning around in circles on the playground.
His giddiness was obvious when he learned he had won the competition.
The news brought such giddiness that she could not stop smiling all day.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calmness - a state of being relaxed and not excited
  • synonym: dizziness - a sensation of spinning or losing one's balance
  • synonym: euphoria - a feeling of intense happiness or excitement
  • synonym: vertigo - a sensation of spinning dizziness, often caused by inner ear problems
