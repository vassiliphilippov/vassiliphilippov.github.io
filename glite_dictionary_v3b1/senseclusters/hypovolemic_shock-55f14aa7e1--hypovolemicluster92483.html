hypovolemic shock

noun
shock from severe blood or fluid loss
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpoʊvəˌliːmɪk ˈʃɑk / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəʊvəˌliːmɪk ˈʃɒk / (BrE)
A serious medical condition caused by severe blood or fluid loss, resulting in inadequate blood flow to the organs.

Examples

Severe hypovolemic shock
Doctors recognized the symptoms of hypovolemic shock and immediately started emergency treatment.
Quickly replacing body fluids is crucial for someone experiencing hypovolemic shock.
The patient went into hypovolemic shock after losing a large amount of blood in the accident.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shock - A sudden, critical medical condition with poor blood circulation
