sonority

noun
quality of being resonant or deep in sound
Pronunciation: / səˈnɔrəti / (AmE), / səˈnɒrəti / (BrE)
the quality of being resonant, loud, or deep in sound

Examples

High sonority sounds
In linguistics, vowels are generally higher in sonority than consonants.
The sonority of his voice captivated everyone in the audience.
The sonority of the church organ filled the vast hall with music.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dullness - lack of resonance or richness (of sound)
  • synonym: resonance - the quality of being deep, full, and reverberating (of sound)
