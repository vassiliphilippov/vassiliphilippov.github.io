melancholia

noun
severe depression; persistent sadness
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛlənˈkoʊliə / (AmE), / ˌmɛlənˈkəʊliə / (BrE)
a deep, lasting feeling of sadness or depression, often without a clear reason

Examples

Deep melancholia
His poetry was colored by a profound sense of melancholia and longing.
She fell into a deep melancholia after her husband's death.
The doctor diagnosed her with melancholia rather than ordinary sadness.
A sense of melancholia filled the quiet room after everyone left.
The film beautifully captures the melancholia that can come with lost dreams.
He often wrote poetry inspired by his feelings of melancholia during the winter months.

Related Terms

  • synonym: depression - a mental health disorder involving persistent sadness and loss of interest
  • antonym: joy - a feeling of great happiness
