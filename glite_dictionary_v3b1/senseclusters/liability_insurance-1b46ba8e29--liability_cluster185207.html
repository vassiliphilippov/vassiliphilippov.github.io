liability insurance

noun
insurance covering liability for damage or injury
Pronunciation: / laɪəˈbɪləti ɪnˈʃʊrəns / (AmE), / laɪəˈbɪlɪti ɪnˈʃʊərəns / (BrE)
insurance that covers the insured against claims for injury or damage caused to other people or their property

Examples

Liability insurance policy
Accidents happen, so liability insurance gives you peace of mind in case of unexpected events.
Every business owner should have liability insurance to protect against potential lawsuits.
The company increased its liability insurance limits after several claims were filed.
