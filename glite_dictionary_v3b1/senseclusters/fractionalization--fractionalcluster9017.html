fractionalization

noun
breaking into parts
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˌfrækʃənələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfrækʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of breaking something into smaller parts, often leading to division or competition among groups

Examples

Political fractionalization
Political fractionalization can make it difficult to form stable governments.
The fractionalization of the company led to several independent businesses competing in the same market.
Social fractionalization sometimes results in groups with conflicting interests and goals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fragmentation - the act of breaking into separate or smaller parts
  • antonym: unification - the process of joining together into a single unit
