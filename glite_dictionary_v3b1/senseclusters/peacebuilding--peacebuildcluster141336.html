peacebuilding

noun
post-conflict recovery
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈpisˌbɪldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpiːsˌbɪldɪŋ / (BrE)
the process or study of creating and maintaining peace after conflict, including reconciliation, rebuilding, and cooperation

Examples

United Nations peacebuilding
The United Nations sent a peacebuilding team to help restore stability in the region.
Effective peacebuilding requires cooperation between local communities and international organizations.
After years of conflict, the country began a peacebuilding process to rebuild trust and institutions.
International organizations often support peacebuilding in countries recovering from civil war.
Effective peacebuilding requires cooperation between local leaders and outside experts.
Education and dialogue are important tools in the process of peacebuilding.
The organization is dedicated to peacebuilding in regions affected by long-term conflict.
She studied peacebuilding to help communities recover after war.
Effective peacebuilding requires understanding the root causes of violence and promoting dialogue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conflict resolution - the process of resolving a dispute or conflict
  • synonym: peacemaking - the process of bringing about peace, especially by diplomatic means
  • synonym: reconciliation - the process of making people or groups friendly again after conflict
  • antonym: warfare - engagement in or the activities involved in war or conflict
