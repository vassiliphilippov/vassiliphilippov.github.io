catch

verb
strike or hit in a particular spot
7.8
Pronunciation: / kætʃ / (AmE), / kætʃ / (BrE)
(of a moving object or blow) to strike or hit someone or something in a particular spot, often unexpectedly.

Examples

Catch a blow
One of the arrows caught her arm as she ran for cover.
The flying stone caught him just above the eye, leaving a bruise.
The soccer ball caught me right in the stomach before I could react.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hit - to strike someone or something with force
  • antonym: miss - to fail to hit, reach, or contact something
