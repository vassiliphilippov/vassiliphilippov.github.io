analogize

verb
draw an analogy; compare for explanation
Pronunciation: / əˈnæl.əˌdʒaɪz /; / əˈnæl.ə.ˌdʒaɪz / (AmE), / əˈnæl.ə.dʒaɪz /; / əˈnæl.ə.ˌdʒaɪz / (BrE)
to draw or make an analogy; to compare one thing with another, typically to clarify or explain.

Examples

To analogize ideas
In discussing climate change, scientists may analogize it to slow-moving natural disasters.
Philosophers often analogize the mind to a computer to explain its functions.
She tried to analogize her situation with that of famous inventors who faced early setbacks.
He analogised the economic system to a living organism in his speech.
It is tempting to analogise the brain to a computer, but the comparison is imperfect.
Scientists often analogise complex concepts to everyday experiences for easier understanding.

Related Terms

  • synonym: analogize - to make an analogy (American spelling)
  • synonym: compare - to examine the similarities or differences between things
  • antonym: contrast - to compare in order to show differences
