bossism

noun
domination by a political boss
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑː.sɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈbɒs.ɪz.əm / (BrE)
Domination of a political organization or institution by a single powerful leader or 'boss', often involving manipulation and control for personal gain.

Examples

Bossism in politics
Bossism undermines democratic processes and often leads to corruption.
Many reformers campaigned against bossism in the early twentieth century.
The city suffered from decades of bossism before new laws were enacted.
