arteria ulnaris

noun
major artery of the forearm and hand
7.8
Pronunciation: / ɑːrˌtɪriə ʌlˈnɛrɪs / (AmE), / ɑːˌtɪərɪə ʌlˈnɛərɪs / (BrE)
a large artery branching from the brachial artery that supplies blood to the muscles of the forearm, wrist, and hand

Examples

The arteria ulnaris artery
During surgery, care must be taken to avoid damaging the arteria ulnaris.
Injuries to the arteria ulnaris can significantly affect hand and wrist function.
The arteria ulnaris plays a critical role in supplying blood to the medial aspect of the forearm.
