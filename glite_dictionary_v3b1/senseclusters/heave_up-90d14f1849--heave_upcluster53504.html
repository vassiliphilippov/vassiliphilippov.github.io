heave up

verb
lift or raise with effort
4.5 0.27/m
Pronunciation: / hiv  åp / (AmE), / hiÀêv  åp / (BrE)
to lift or raise something, often with effort or force

Examples

To heave up a box
They managed to heave up the heavy box onto the shelf.
With a grunt, she heaved up the anchor from the riverbed.
Workers heaved up the steel beam into position using a crane.

Synonyms

  • heave
    3.9 3.44/m
    heave means to lift, pull, or throw something heavy with great effort, while heave up means to raise something.
  • lift up
    1.1 4.06/m
    lift up means to raise something to a higher place in a simple way, while heave up implies strong physical effort.
  • hoist
    4.7 5.61/m
    hoist means to raise something often with ropes or machines on work sites, while heave up suggests doing it by force.
  • pick up
    1.0 49/m
    pick up means to lift something small or light by hand in casual situations, while heave up means lifting with effort.
