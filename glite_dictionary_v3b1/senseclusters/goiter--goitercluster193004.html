goiter

noun
enlarged thyroid gland
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɔɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɡɔɪtə / (BrE)
An abnormally enlarged thyroid gland, often visible as a swelling in the neck, caused by hormonal imbalances or iodine deficiency.

Examples

A goiter swelling
In some regions, a lack of iodine in the diet can cause goiter.
She underwent surgery to remove a large goiter from her neck.
The doctor explained that a goiter develops when the thyroid gland becomes enlarged.
A deficiency of iodine in the diet is a common cause of goitre.
She was diagnosed with a goitre after noticing a swelling in her neck.
The surgeon recommended removing the goitre to ease her breathing difficulties.
