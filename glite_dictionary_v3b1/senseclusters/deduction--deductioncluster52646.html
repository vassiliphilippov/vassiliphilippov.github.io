deduction

noun
reasoning from general to specific
2.4 3.4
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈdʌk.ʃən/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈdʌk.ʃən/ /; / /dɪ.ˈdʊk.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of using reasoning or evidence to reach a conclusion

Examples

Logical deduction
Her deduction that the weather would worsen was based on the darkening clouds.
Mathematics often relies on deduction to prove theorems from established axioms.
Through careful deduction, the detective determined who the real culprit was.
After analyzing the data, we came to the deduction that the experiment had failed.
Her deduction that the meeting was canceled proved to be correct when no one arrived.
The lawyer presented his final deduction to the jury, based on the evidence.
