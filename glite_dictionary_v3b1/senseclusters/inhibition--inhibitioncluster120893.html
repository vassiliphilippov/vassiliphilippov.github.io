the act of prohibiting or preventing something, especially by law, rule, or authority; an instance of forbidding

Examples

Legal inhibition
Medical inhibition of certain substances is sometimes necessary to protect patient health.
The new law imposed an inhibition on smoking in public places.
There was an inhibition of imports during the trade embargo.

Synonyms

  • prohibition
    4.4 8.48/m
    prohibition is an official legal ban used in laws and rules, while inhibition refers to the act of forbidding or preventing something.
  • ban
    1.4 27/m
    ban is a common, direct rule that stops something from happening, while inhibition is a general act of forbidding, often by authority.
  • forbiddance
    8.3 0.00/m
    forbiddance is the state of being officially banned, often old-fashioned in speech, while inhibition names the act of preventing or forbidding.
  • interdiction
    8.9 1.29/m
    interdiction is a formal legal order that blocks activity, while inhibition is any act of forbidding or preventing, not only court orders.
  • disallowance
    7.2 0.02/m
    disallowance is an official refusal to accept or permit something in a formal process, while inhibition is a broader act of forbidding.
  • suppression
    6.1 5.54/m
    suppression is forcing something down or keeping it hidden, often by power or force, while inhibition means forbidding or preventing.

Related Terms

  • antonym: allowance - the act of allowing or permitting something
  • synonym: prohibition - the act of formally forbidding something
