front crawl

noun
swimming stroke with alternate arm movements
7.7
Pronunciation: / frʌnt krɔl / (AmE), / frʌnt krɔːl / (BrE)
A swimming stroke in which the arms are alternately moved overhead while the legs perform a flutter kick.

Examples

The front crawl stroke
He finished the last lap using the front crawl to gain an advantage.
Learning the front crawl is often a priority for beginner swimmers.
She improved her front crawl technique so she could swim faster in competitions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: freestyle - A swimming race in which any stroke is allowed, commonly the front crawl
