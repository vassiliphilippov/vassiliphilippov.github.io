gene-splicing

noun
biotech process of joining DNA fragments
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒin ˌsplaɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːn ˌsplaɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
The process of cutting and joining DNA from different organisms to create recombinant DNA.

Examples

Gene-splicing technology
Concerns about the ethics of gene-splicing continue to influence biotechnology research.
Modern agriculture often relies on gene-splicing to create crops resistant to pests.
The breakthrough in gene-splicing allowed scientists to insert beneficial traits into plants.

  • synonym: genetic engineering - the deliberate modification of the characteristics of an organism by manipulating its genetic material
