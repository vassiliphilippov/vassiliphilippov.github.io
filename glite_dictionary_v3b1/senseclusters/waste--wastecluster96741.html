waste

verb
use or spend carelessly
1.7 1.0
Pronunciation: /weɪst/ (AmE), /weɪst/ (BrE)
to use or spend something carelessly, inefficiently, or for no good reason

Examples

To waste time
He tends to waste his time playing video games instead of studying.
They wasted valuable resources on unnecessary projects.
Try not to waste all your money on things you don't need.
She didn't want to waste her time arguing with someone who wouldn't listen.
The company tries not to waste resources on unnecessary projects.
You shouldn't waste water by leaving the tap running while brushing your teeth.
If you leave the lights on all day, you waste electricity.
She regretted how much time she had wasted on that pointless project.
Try not to waste your money on things you don't need.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conserve - to protect from loss or waste; to use carefully
  • antonym: save - to keep and not waste; to use efficiently
  • synonym: squander - to waste something, especially money or time, in a reckless manner
