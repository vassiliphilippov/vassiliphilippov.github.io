pseudoclassical

adjective
approximating quantum phenomena
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːdoʊˈklæsɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌsjuːdəʊˈklæsɪkəl / (BrE)
using classical concepts or equations to approximate or represent phenomena that are actually quantum or nonclassical in nature

Examples

Pseudoclassical model in physics
The pseudoclassical approach simplifies calculations by ignoring certain quantum effects.
Physicists sometimes use pseudoclassical models to gain insight into complex quantum systems.
Although the system is quantum, a pseudoclassical approximation can still provide useful predictions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: quantum - relating to quantum theory or mechanics
  • synonym: semiclassical - combining classical and quantum approaches
