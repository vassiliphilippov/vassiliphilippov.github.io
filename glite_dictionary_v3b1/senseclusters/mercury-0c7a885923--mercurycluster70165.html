Mercury

proper noun
Roman god; messenger of Jupiter; god of commerce
7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈmɜːr.kjə.riː/ /; / /ˈmɜːr.kə.riː/ (AmE), /ˈmɜː.kjʊ.riː/ /; / /ˈmɜː.kə.riː/ (BrE)
In Roman mythology, the messenger of Jupiter and the god of commerce, communication, and travelers; equivalent to the Greek god Hermes.

Examples

Mercury the messenger god
In ancient Roman religion, Mercury was worshipped as the cleverest of the gods.
Many myths describe Mercury acting as a guide for travelers and delivering messages from Jupiter.
The statue in the plaza depicts Mercury holding his characteristic winged staff and helmet.
