rigor

noun
strictness; harshness; severity
5.4
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪɡər / (AmE), / ˈrɪɡə / (BrE)
the quality of being extremely strict, severe, or harsh, especially in the application of rules or discipline

Examples

The rigor of discipline
During the military academy, cadets were pushed to endure the rigor of constant drills and inspections.
Many critics questioned the rigor of the new workplace regulations.
The rigor of her teaching methods left little room for creativity.
He was admired for his fairness, but some felt his rigour bordered on cruelty.
Parents sometimes criticized the school's rigour in enforcing its policies.
The army training program is known for the rigour of its discipline and routines.

Related Terms

  • antonym: leniency - the quality of being more merciful or tolerant
  • synonym: severity - the quality of being severe or strict
