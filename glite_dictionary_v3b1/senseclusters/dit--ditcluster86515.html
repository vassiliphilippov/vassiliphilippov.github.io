dit

noun
logarithmic unit of information (base 10)
9.9
Pronunciation: / dɪt / (AmE), / dɪt / (BrE)
A logarithmic unit of information or entropy, based on base 10 logarithms, equal to one decimal digit.

Examples

A dit of data
A dit is similar to a bit, but it uses base 10 instead of base 2.
The entropy of the message was calculated as 4 dits.
The storage system can hold up to a million dits of information.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bit - a binary digit; unit of information (base 2)
  • synonym: nat - unit of information based on natural logarithm (base e)
