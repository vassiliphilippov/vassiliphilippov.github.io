no choice

noun
absence of options or alternatives
3.1
Pronunciation: / noʊ tʃɔɪs / (AmE), / nəʊ tʃɔɪs / (BrE)
used to indicate that there are no other possible options or alternatives available; must do something because nothing else is possible

Examples

Have no choice
Sometimes life presents situations where you simply have no choice.
The teacher gave them no choice; they had to complete the assignment today.
We had no choice but to leave early due to the storm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: no alternative - absence of other possibilities
