whitelist

verb
to approve for access
5.4
Pronunciation: /ˈwaɪt.lɪst/ /; / /ˈwaɪt.ləst/ (AmE), /ˈwaɪt.lɪst/ /; / /ˈwaɪt.ləst/ (BrE)
to add a person, program, or item to a list of approved entries so it is allowed access or exempted from restrictions

Examples

To whitelist a user
The IT department will whitelist your email address so you can receive all company messages.
You need to whitelist this application to allow it to run on your computer.
After several complaints, the website decided to whitelist trusted users for faster access.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allow - to permit or give permission
  • antonym: blacklist - to add to a list of banned or unapproved entries
  • antonym: block - to prevent access or stop something
