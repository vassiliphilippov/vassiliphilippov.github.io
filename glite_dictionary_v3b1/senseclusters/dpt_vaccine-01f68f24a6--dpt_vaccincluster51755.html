DPT vaccine

noun
combination vaccine for diphtheria, pertussis, and tetanus
3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈdiː.piː.tiː ˈvæk.siːn/ /; / /ˈdiː.piː.tiː ˈvæk.sɪn/ (AmE), /ˈdiː.piː.tiː ˈvæk.siːn/ /; / /ˈdiː.piː.tiː ˈvæk.sɪn/ (BrE)
a combination vaccine that protects against diphtheria, pertussis (whooping cough), and tetanus, usually administered to children in a series of injections

Examples

The DPT vaccine dose
Children typically receive their first dose of the DPT vaccine at two months of age.
Immunization with the DPT vaccine has greatly reduced the incidence of whooping cough.
The doctor explained the possible side effects of the DPT vaccine to the parents.
