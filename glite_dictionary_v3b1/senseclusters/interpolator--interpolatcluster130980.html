interpolator

noun
person who adds text
9.1
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈtɜːr.pə.leɪ.tər/ /; / /ə.ˈtɜːr.pə.leɪ.tər/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈtɜː.pə.leɪ.tə/ /; / /ə.ˈtɜː.pə.leɪ.tə/ (BrE)
a person who inserts additional words, passages, or material into a text, often altering its original meaning

Examples

The historian suspected that an interpolator had added several lines to the ancient manuscript.
Scholars debated whether the passage was written by the original author or by an interpolator centuries later.
The presence of modern language suggested the work had been altered by an interpolator.
