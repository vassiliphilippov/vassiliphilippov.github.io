weak side

noun
less protected side
3.7
Pronunciation: /wiːk saɪd/ (AmE), /wiːk saɪd/ (BrE)
the side of a team, area, or person's body that is less strong, less protected, or less skilled, especially in sports

Examples

The weak side attack
The coach instructed the players to attack the opponent's weak side for a better chance to score.
Defenders quickly shifted to cover the weak side after noticing the offensive play.
In basketball, passing to the weak side can create open shots for teammates.
She practiced dribbling with her weak side to improve her basketball skills.
Defenders often force attackers to use their weak side during a match.
He scored a goal even though he kicked the ball with his weak side.
