Munsee

proper noun
Native American group
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌnsi / (AmE), / ˈmʌnsi / (BrE)
a Native American people originally from the northeastern United States, and the language they speak

Examples

The Munsee people
The Munsee once lived in what is now New York and New Jersey.
She is studying the Munsee language to help preserve it for future generations.
A group of Munsee elders gathered to share traditional stories and songs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Algonquian - a family of Native American languages including Munsee
  • synonym: Lenape - a Native American people of the northeastern United States, also known as the Delaware
