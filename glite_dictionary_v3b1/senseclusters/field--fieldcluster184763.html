field

noun
airport or airfield for aircraft
3.6 4.78/m
Pronunciation: / fiÀêld / (AmE), / fiÀêld / (BrE)
a place, usually with flat open land, where planes take off and land; an airfield or landing strip

Examples

Air field
During the war, many temporary fields were constructed for military aircraft.
The pilot landed the small plane safely on the field at sunset.
The vintage biplane took off from a grassy field just outside the city.

Synonyms

    3.8 3.16/m
    an airfield is land made for aircraft takeoff and landing, often with runways or simple facilities, while field is a general landing place.
  • aerodrome
    8.4 0.15/m
    an aerodrome has control towers, hangars and passenger facilities, while a field is a more general or simple landing area.
  • airstrip
    3.5 1.69/m
    an airstrip is a short runway with few services, while a field can mean any landing place, large or small.
  • landing strip
    3.5 0.34/m
    a landing strip is a simple runway often without services, while a field can be a general airfield or open landing area.
  • landing field
    3.7 0.14/m
    a landing field is a small prepared area for takeoffs and landings, while a field is a general word for any such place.
  • flying field
    3.7 0.00/m
    a flying field is land prepared for aircraft to use, usually without many buildings, while a field can also mean any landing ground.
  • strip
    7.4 3.11/m
    a strip is a very simple landing area with minimal facilities, often temporary, while a field may be a more permanent airfield.
  • flight strip
    7.2 0.00/m
    a flight strip is a short emergency or military runway with little support, while a field can be any airfield used for regular flights.
