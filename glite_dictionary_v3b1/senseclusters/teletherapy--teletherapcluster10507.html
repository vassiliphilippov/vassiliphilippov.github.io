teletherapy

noun
radiation treatment
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛləˈθɛrəpi / (AmE), / ˌtɛlɪˈθɛrəpi / (BrE)
a medical treatment in which radiation is delivered to a patient from a distance, usually to treat cancer

Examples

Teletherapy for cancer
The doctor recommended teletherapy to target the tumor without surgery.
Advances in technology have made teletherapy more precise and effective for cancer patients.
After several sessions of teletherapy, the patient's symptoms began to improve.

Related Terms

  • antonym: brachytherapy - radiation therapy where the source is placed inside or next to the treatment area
  • synonym: radiotherapy - the use of radiation to treat disease, especially cancer
