barrier island

noun
long, narrow coastal island parallel to shore
6.2 3.7
Pronunciation: /ˈbæ.ri.ər ˈaɪ.lənd/ /; / /ˈbe.ri.ər ˈaɪ.lənd/ (AmE), /ˈbæ.ri.ə ˈaɪ.lənd/ /; / /ˈbe.ri.ə ˈaɪ.lənd/ (BrE)
a long, narrow sandy island running parallel to the mainland coast, separated from it by a lagoon, bay, or marsh, which serves to protect the shore from erosion and storms

Examples

A barrier island protects
After the hurricane, the barrier island absorbed much of the storm's impact, protecting the mainland.
Many barrier islands provide important habitats for birds and marine life.
The Outer Banks are a famous barrier island chain off the coast of North Carolina.
