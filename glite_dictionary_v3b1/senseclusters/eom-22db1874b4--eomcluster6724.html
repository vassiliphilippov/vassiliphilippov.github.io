EOM

noun
electro-optic modulator
Pronunciation: / i oʊ ɛm / (AmE), / iː əʊ ɛm / (BrE)
a device that uses an electric signal to change the properties of a light beam, such as its phase or amplitude

Examples

Laser EOM device
The laboratory uses an EOM to control the intensity of the laser beam.
By adjusting the EOM, the researchers could change the phase of the light signal.
An EOM is essential for experiments that require precise modulation of optical signals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: demodulator - a device that extracts information from a modulated signal
  • synonym: modulator - a device that modifies a signal or wave
