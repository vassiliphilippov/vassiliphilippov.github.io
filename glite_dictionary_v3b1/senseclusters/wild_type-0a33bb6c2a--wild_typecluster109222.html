wild type

adjective
typical natural form
5.5
Pronunciation: / waɪld taɪp / (AmE), / waɪld taɪp / (BrE)
having the typical, non-mutated form of a gene, organism, or trait as found in nature

Examples

Wild type gene
The wild type gene produces a functional protein in most individuals.
Scientists compared the wild type plants to those with a genetic mutation.
This medication has no effect on the wild type strain of the virus.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mutant - an organism or gene that has undergone mutation
  • synonym: normal - conforming to the standard or typical form
