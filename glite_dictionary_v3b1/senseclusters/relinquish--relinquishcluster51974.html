Synonyms

  • give up
    2.3 11/m
    give up is a common, informal phrase for stopping or handing over something, while relinquish is more formal.
  • release
    2.6 3/m
    release means to set free or let go of control or ownership, while relinquish stresses giving up rights or claims.
  • renounce
    6.3 5/m
    renounce means to formally reject or give up a right or belief, while relinquish covers many voluntary handovers.
  • resign
    3.7 1/m
    resign means to formally leave a job or office, while relinquish can mean giving up many kinds of rights or roles.
  • part with
    4.1 2/m
    part with means to give away something you value, while relinquish is neutral and covers rights, positions, or possessions.
  • waive
    6.6 6/m
    waive means to choose not to use a right or claim, while relinquish means actively giving up or handing it over.
  • cede
    8.2 4/m
    cede means to formally hand control or territory to another state, while relinquish covers general voluntary giving up.
  • let go
    1.4 20/m
    let go means to release something from your physical hold, while relinquish usually means giving up rights, claims, or positions.
