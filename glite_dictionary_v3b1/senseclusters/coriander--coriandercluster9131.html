coriander

noun
aromatic herb used in cooking
1.0 2.8
Pronunciation: / ˌkɔriˈændər /; / ˌkɔriˈændɚ / (AmE), / ˌkɒriˈændə / (BrE)
an aromatic plant (also called an herb), whose fresh leaves are used in cooking as a seasoning or garnish

Examples

Fresh coriander leaves
I bought a bunch of coriander for the salsa recipe.
She sprinkled fresh coriander over the curry before serving.
Some people find the taste of coriander leaves to be quite soapy.
Fresh coriander leaves add a bright, citrusy note to many Indian dishes.
She sprinkled chopped coriander over the salad for extra flavor.
The recipe calls for both ground coriander and cumin.
