acron

noun
front part of invertebrate
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈækrɒn / (AmE), / ˈækrɒn / (BrE)
the frontmost part of the body in certain invertebrate animals, especially worms, often containing sensory organs

Examples

Worm acron
The acron of the worm contains its main sensory organs for detecting food.
Scientists studied the structure of the acron to understand how the animal senses its environment.
