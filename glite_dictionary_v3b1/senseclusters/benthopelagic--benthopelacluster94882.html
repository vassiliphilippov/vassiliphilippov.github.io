benthopelagic

noun
bottom and open-water animal
9.9
Pronunciation: /ben.θoʊ.pə.ˈlædʒ.ɪk/ (AmE), /ben.θəʊ.pə.ˈlædʒ.ɪk/ (BrE)
a marine or freshwater animal that lives and feeds both near the bottom and in the open water above it

Examples

A benthopelagic fish
The research team studied a benthopelagic fish that moved between the seabed and open water.
Many benthopelagic species play a key role in connecting deep and surface ecosystems.
Scientists observed the feeding habits of a benthopelagic organism in the lake.

Related Terms

  • synonym: benthic - an animal living on or near the bottom of a body of water
  • synonym: pelagic - an animal living in the open water away from the bottom
