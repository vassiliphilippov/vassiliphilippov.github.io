transpire

verb
release water vapor (plants)
2.2 6.3
Pronunciation: /træn.ˈspaɪr/ (AmE), /træn.ˈspaɪ.ə/ (BrE)
for a plant or animal to give off water vapor through the surface (such as leaves or skin)

Examples

Leaves transpire water
During hot weather, leaves transpire more to keep the plant cool.
If soil is dry, some plants may transpire less to conserve moisture.
Wheat plants transpire large amounts of water throughout the day.
After exercise, the skin may transpire more to help cool the body.
Many leaves transpire significant amounts of moisture during hot summer days.
Plants transpire water through tiny openings in their leaves.

Related Terms

  • antonym: absorb - to take in or soak up (opposite process, context-dependent)
  • synonym: evaporate - to turn from liquid into vapor
