upwards

adverb
from a certain time onward
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpwərdz / (AmE), / ˈʌpwədz / (BrE)
At or towards a later time; starting at a particular point in time and continuing onward.

Examples

Ages five upwards
Children aged five upwards are welcome to join the class.
From the 19th century upwards, the town experienced rapid growth.
He has followed tennis from childhood upwards, never missing a tournament.

Related Terms

  • synonym: onward - continuing forward in time or progress
