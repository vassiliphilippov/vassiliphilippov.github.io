condition

verb
specify a requirement in a contract
Pronunciation: / kənˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
to specify something as a requirement or term in a contract or agreement; to make something contingent upon a stated condition

Examples

To condition an agreement
Her employment is conditioned upon a satisfactory background check.
The company conditioned its approval of the deal on certain regulatory changes.
The contract clearly conditions the bonus on achieving sales targets.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stipulate - to demand or specify a requirement as part of an agreement
