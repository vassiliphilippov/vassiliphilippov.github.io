bivalence

noun
two possible values
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪvəˌlɛns / (AmE), / ˈbaɪvələns / (BrE)
the property of having exactly two possible values, such as true or false in logic

Examples

Law of bivalence
In classical logic, the law of bivalence states that every statement is either true or false.
Some philosophical systems reject bivalence and allow for more than two truth values.
The concept of bivalence is fundamental to digital computing, where signals are either on or off.

Related Terms

  • synonym: binary - having two parts or values
  • antonym: multivalence - the property of having more than two possible values
