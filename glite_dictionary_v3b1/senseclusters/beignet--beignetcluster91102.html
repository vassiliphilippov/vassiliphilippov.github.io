beignet

noun
deep-fried, sugar-dusted pastry
9.9
Pronunciation: / bɛɪˈnjeɪ / (AmE), / beɪˈnjeɪ / (BrE)
A deep-fried pastry made from yeast dough and covered with confectioners' sugar, popular especially in French and French-influenced cuisines.

Examples

Fresh beignets with sugar
New Orleans is famous for its delicious beignets served with coffee.
She tried making beignets at home after visiting Louisiana.
We enjoyed a plate of warm beignets sprinkled with powdered sugar at the café.

Related Terms

  • synonym: doughnut - a small fried cake of sweetened dough, typically ring-shaped
