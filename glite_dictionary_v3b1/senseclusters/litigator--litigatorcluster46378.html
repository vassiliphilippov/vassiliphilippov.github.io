litigator

noun
courtroom lawyer
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪtɪˌɡeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈlɪtɪɡeɪtə / (BrE)
a lawyer who specializes in representing clients in court cases and legal disputes

Examples

The experienced litigator presented a strong case to the jury.
As a litigator, she spends much of her time preparing for trials.
The firm is looking to hire a new litigator to handle complex lawsuits.

As a skilled litigator, she was known for her persuasive courtroom presence.
Many companies hire a litigator when facing complex legal disputes.
The experienced litigator prepared a strong case for her client.
As a skilled litigator, he was known for his persuasive arguments in court.
The company hired a team of litigators to handle the complex lawsuit.

Related Terms

  • antonym: arbitrator - a person appointed to settle a dispute outside of court
  • synonym: attorney - a person legally appointed to act for another in legal matters
  • synonym: barrister - a lawyer entitled to represent clients in higher courts, especially in the UK
  • antonym: defendant - a person or group being sued or accused in a court of law
  • synonym: plaintiff - a person who brings a case against another in a court of law
