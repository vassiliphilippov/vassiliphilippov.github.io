Nasturtium officinale

noun
species of watercress plant
Pronunciation: / næˈstɝːʃəm ɒˌfɪsɪˈnɑːleɪ / (AmE), / næˈstɜːʃəm əˌfɪsɪˈnɑːliː / (BrE)
A perennial Eurasian cress, commonly known as watercress, that grows mainly in springs or running water; its fleshy, pungent leaves are eaten in salads or used as a garnish, and it has been introduced to North America and other regions.

Examples

Nasturtium officinale watercress plant
Nasturtium officinale thrives in clean, running streams and is harvested throughout the summer.
Fresh Nasturtium officinale is often used in salads for its peppery taste.
The chef garnished the soup with chopped Nasturtium officinale from his garden.
