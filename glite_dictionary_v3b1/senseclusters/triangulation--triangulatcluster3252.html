triangulation

noun
division of a surface into triangles (mesh)
7.1
Pronunciation: /traɪ.æŋ.gjuː.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /traɪ.æŋ.gjə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /traɪ.æŋ.gjʊ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /traɪ.æŋ.gjə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
(in computational geometry and computer graphics) the process of subdividing a polygon or surface into triangles to create a triangular mesh used for rendering, simulation, or analysis.

Examples

Mesh triangulation
The software uses triangulation to generate a mesh for finite element analysis.
During modeling class, she learned how triangulation improves rendering speed and geometric accuracy.
Automatic triangulation converted the complex surface into millions of flat triangles for simulation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mesh generation - the process of creating a polygonal mesh (often triangular) for computational simulation or graphics
  • synonym: tessellation - division of a plane or surface into shapes (often triangles) without gaps or overlaps; related to mesh construction
