mutuality

noun
the state of being mutual or shared
Pronunciation: / ˌmjuːtʃuˈæləti /; / ˌmjuː.tʃuˈæl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌmjuːtʃuˈæləti /; / ˌmjuː.tʃuˈæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being mutual, shared, or reciprocal between two or more people or groups

Examples

A mutuality of respect
A sense of mutuality is essential for successful teamwork.
Lasting partnerships are built on trust and mutuality.

Legal agreements often require mutuality in obligations to ensure fairness to both parties.
Successful relationships are built on trust and mutuality rather than selfishness.
There was a strong sense of mutuality between the partners, allowing them to work efficiently together.

  • antonym: unilateralism - the process of acting, reaching a decision, or espousing a principle unilaterally
