Niger-Congo

noun
language family of sub-Saharan Africa
Pronunciation: / ˌnaɪdʒər ˈkɒŋgoʊ / (AmE), / ˌniːʒə ˈkɒŋgəʊ / (BrE)
a large family of languages spoken in West, Central, and Southern Africa, including most of the languages of sub-Saharan Africa.

Examples

Niger-Congo language family
Linguists have traced many similarities among languages within the Niger-Congo group.
Swahili and Yoruba are both part of the Niger-Congo language family.
The Niger-Congo family constitutes the largest group of African languages.
