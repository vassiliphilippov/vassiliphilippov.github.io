mire

noun
difficult situation, hard to escape
5.2
Pronunciation: / maɪr / (AmE), / ˈmaɪə / (BrE)
a difficult or troublesome situation from which it is hard to escape

Examples

A mire of problems
After the scandal, the politician found himself in a mire of accusations and controversy.
Many families struggle to escape the mire of financial hardship.
The negotiations became stuck in a bureaucratic mire that delayed any progress.

Related Terms

  • antonym: escape - to get free from a difficult situation
  • synonym: predicament - a difficult or unpleasant situation
  • synonym: quagmire - a complex or hazardous situation
