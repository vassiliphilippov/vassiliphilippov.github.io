schoolmaster

noun
male head teacher of a school (historical)
2.9
Pronunciation: / ˈskuːlˌmæstər / (AmE), / ˈskuːlˌmɑːstə / (BrE)
a male teacher, especially the head teacher or principal of a school (often used historically)

Examples

Strict schoolmaster
During the Victorian era, the schoolmaster was both respected and feared by his pupils.

The old schoolmaster guided students with a firm but gentle hand.
As a young schoolmaster, he inspired many students with his passion for literature.
In nineteenth-century England, the schoolmaster often lived in a cottage beside the school.
The new schoolmaster introduced strict rules to the classroom.
