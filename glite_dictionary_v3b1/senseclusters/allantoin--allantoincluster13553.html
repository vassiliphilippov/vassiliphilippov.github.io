allantoin

noun
purine metabolism product
8.9
Pronunciation: / əˈlæntoʊɪn / (AmE), / əˈlæntəʊɪn / (BrE)
a chemical compound produced during purine metabolism, found in many animals and plants, and often present in urine and tissues

Examples

Allantoin in urine
Researchers found elevated levels of allantoin in the urine samples after the high-protein diet.
Some skin creams contain allantoin because of its soothing and healing properties.
The presence of allantoin in plant tissues can indicate active purine metabolism.
