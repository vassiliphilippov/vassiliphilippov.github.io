hypofunction

noun
reduced organ activity
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpoʊˈfʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəˈfʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
reduced or below normal activity or function of an organ or body part

Examples

Hypofunction of glands
The doctor explained that hypofunction of the adrenal glands can cause fatigue and weakness.
Blood tests revealed hypofunction in the patient's thyroid, leading to a diagnosis of hypothyroidism.
Treatment for hypofunction often involves hormone replacement therapy to restore normal activity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hyperfunction - excessive or above normal activity of an organ or body part
  • synonym: insufficiency - the state of not functioning adequately
