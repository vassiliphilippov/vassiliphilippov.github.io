currajong

noun
Australian tree yielding fiber and wood
7.9 9.7
Pronunciation: /ˈkʌr.ə.dʒɑːŋ/ /; / /ˈkʌr.ə.dʒɔːŋ/ (AmE), /ˈkʌr.ə.dʒɒŋ/ (BrE)
A tree of eastern Australia (Brachychiton populneus) known for its tough fiber and lightweight, attractively grained wood; its leaves are sometimes used as emergency cattle fodder.

Examples

The currajong tree
Furniture makers value the currajong for its lightweight, attractive wood grain.
Many rural properties in Australia are shaded by beautiful currajong trees.
The farmer used branches of the currajong to feed his cattle during the drought.
