Fordist

noun
supporter of Fordism
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrdɪst / (AmE), / ˈfɔːdɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports or works within the system of mass production and standardized industrial organization known as Fordism

Examples

A dedicated Fordist
As a dedicated Fordist, she believed in the benefits of mass production for economic growth.
The factory manager was a well-known Fordist who implemented strict assembly-line procedures.
Many early twentieth-century industrial leaders were proud to call themselves Fordists.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anti-Fordist - a person who opposes the principles of Fordism
  • synonym: industrialist - a person involved in the ownership or management of industry
