atomic clock

noun
a highly accurate clock based on atomic vibrations
8.3
Pronunciation: / əˈtɑmɪk klɑk / (AmE), / əˈtɒmɪk klɒk / (BrE)
a highly precise timepiece that measures time using the natural oscillations of atoms or molecules, often used as a standard for international timekeeping

Examples

An atomic clock device
Modern GPS satellites rely on an onboard atomic clock for precise time measurement.
Scientists synchronized their experiments using a atomic clock to ensure accuracy.
The atomic clock at the observatory keeps time to within a billionth of a second.
