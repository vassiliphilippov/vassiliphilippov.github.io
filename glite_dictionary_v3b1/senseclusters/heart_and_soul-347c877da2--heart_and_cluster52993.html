heart and soul

adverb
completely and wholeheartedly
1.0
Pronunciation: / hɑrt ənd soʊl / (AmE), / hɑːt ənd səʊl / (BrE)
with complete enthusiasm, commitment, or dedication; completely and wholeheartedly

Examples

To believe heart and soul
He believed heart and soul in his friend's innocence throughout the trial.
She supported the project heart and soul, never doubting its success for a moment.
They threw themselves heart and soul into the charity work, helping wherever they could.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reluctantly - in an unwilling or hesitant way
  • synonym: wholeheartedly - with complete sincerity and commitment
