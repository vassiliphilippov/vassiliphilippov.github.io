narrowhead morel

noun
an edible morel mushroom with a cap attached closely to the stalk
Pronunciation: / ˈnæroʊˌhɛd məˈrɛl / (AmE), / ˈnærəʊˌhɛd məˈrɛl / (BrE)
A morel mushroom whose pitted cap is joined to the stalk with little or no free margin, turning from grey when young to black with age.

Examples

The narrowhead morel mushroom
As it ages, the narrowhead morel changes color from grey to almost black.
Finding a narrowhead morel in the forest is a delight for many mushroom foragers.
The chef used narrowhead morel mushrooms to give the risotto a unique, earthy flavor.
