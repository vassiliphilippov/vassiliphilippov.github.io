scrambly

adjective
steep and uneven
Pronunciation: / ˈskræmbli / (AmE), / ˈskræmbli / (BrE)
steep, uneven, and covered with loose stones or vegetation, making it necessary to use hands when climbing

Examples

Scrambly trail
The trail became scrambly near the summit, forcing us to use our hands for balance.
We avoided the most scrambly section of the path because it looked too dangerous.
Climbers should be prepared for scrambly terrain with loose rocks and steep slopes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rocky - covered with rocks or stones
  • antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface
