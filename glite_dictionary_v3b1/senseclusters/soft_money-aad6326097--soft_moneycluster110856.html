soft money

noun
unregulated political contributions
3.9
Pronunciation: / sɔft ˈmʌni / (AmE), / sɒft ˈmʌni / (BrE)
political contributions made in a way that avoids federal regulations, often by giving to political parties or committees rather than directly to candidates.

Examples

Soft money donations
Changes to the law limited the influence of soft money in national elections.
Many reformers argue that restrictions on soft money are essential for fair elections.
The campaign was accused of receiving large amounts of soft money from interest groups.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hard money - political donations regulated by law directly to candidates or campaigns
