blacklist

verb
to ban or boycott by listing
Pronunciation: / ˈblækˌlɪst / (AmE), / ˈblæk.lɪst /; / ˈblækˌlɪst / (BrE)
to put a person or thing on a list of people or things that are banned, disapproved of, or to be avoided

Examples

To blacklist a worker
Several artists were blacklisted for their political beliefs in the 1950s.
The government threatened to blacklist any company that broke the new regulations.
The publisher was blacklisted by major retailers after the controversy.
The company decided to blacklist suppliers who failed to meet safety standards.
After repeated violations, the website was blacklisted by major search engines.
Some countries blacklist certain movies for political reasons.

Related Terms

  • antonym: allow - to permit or let happen
  • synonym: ban - to officially or legally prohibit
  • synonym: boycott - to refuse to deal with as a form of protest
