reseeding

noun
planting seeds again
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈsiːdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈsiːdɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of planting seeds again in an area, either by people or naturally, to encourage new plant growth

Examples

Field reseeding
After a harsh winter, reseeding the field was necessary to restore the grass.
The park's maintenance team began reseeding areas where the grass had worn away.
Farmers often rely on reseeding to improve crop yields in depleted soil.
After the wildfire, natural reseeding helped restore the forest over several years.
Farmers often use reseeding to improve the quality of their pastures.
The park's recovery relied on both manual planting and natural reseeding of native grasses.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deforestation - the removal of a forest or stand of trees from land
  • synonym: regeneration - the process of renewal or restoration of a habitat or ecosystem
  • synonym: replanting - the act of planting again in the same area
  • synonym: sowing - the act of planting seeds in soil
