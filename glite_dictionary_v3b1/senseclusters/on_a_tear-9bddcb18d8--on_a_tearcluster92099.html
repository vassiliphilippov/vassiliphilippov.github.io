on a tear

adverb
having a streak of activity or success
Pronunciation: / ɑn ə tɛr / (AmE), / ɒn ə tɛə / (BrE)
experiencing a period of intense activity or repeated success, especially in quick succession

Examples

On a tear streak
After struggling all year, the musician is suddenly on a tear with three hit singles.
She has been on a tear at work, closing deals left and right.
The basketball team went on a tear and won ten games in a row.

Related Terms

  • synonym: on a roll - experiencing a continuous period of success
