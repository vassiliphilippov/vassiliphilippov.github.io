stand on the shoulders of giants

verb
make progress using earlier achievements
Pronunciation: / stænd ɑn ðə ˈʃoʊldɚz əv ˈʤaɪənts / (AmE), / stænd ɒn ðə ˈʃəʊldəz əv ˈdʒaɪənts / (BrE)
to make progress or achieve something by using the ideas, discoveries, or work of people who came before you

Examples

To stand on the shoulders of giants
Modern scientists often stand on the shoulders of giants to advance their research.
New technologies are often developed by standing on the shoulders of giants from previous generations.
She acknowledged that her breakthroughs wouldn't be possible without standing on the shoulders of giants.
