relativist

noun
believer in relative truth
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛlətɪvɪst / (AmE), / ˈrɛlətɪvɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that truth or morality is not absolute but depends on context or perspective

Examples

A moral relativist
As a relativist, she argued that moral values change between cultures.
Many modern philosophers are considered relativists because they question universal truths.
The debate between the relativist and the absolutist lasted for hours.
The relativist argued that what is considered right in one culture may be wrong in another.
As a relativist, she questioned the idea of universal moral standards.
Some philosophers debate whether a relativist approach can lead to tolerance or confusion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: absolutist - a person who believes in absolute, unchanging truths or principles
  • synonym: subjectivist - a person who believes that knowledge or values are based on personal perspectives
