host

noun
recipient of transplant (medicine)
Pronunciation: / hoʊst / (AmE), / həʊst / (BrE)
an animal, plant, or person that supports a parasite or receives a transplanted organ

Examples

A host animal
After the surgery, the host was closely monitored for any signs of organ rejection.
Compatible blood types are essential when finding a suitable host for an organ transplant.
The immune system of the host may attack the transplanted heart as a foreign object.
A tapeworm can live inside its host for years without being detected.
Different kinds of parasites require a specific host to complete their life cycle.
The mosquito is a host for several types of parasites.

  • antonym: parasite - an organism that lives in or on another and benefits by deriving nutrients at the other's expense
