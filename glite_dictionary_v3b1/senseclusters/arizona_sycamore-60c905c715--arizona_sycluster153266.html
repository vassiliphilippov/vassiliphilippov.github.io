Arizona sycamore

noun
tree native to Arizona and adjacent areas
Pronunciation: / əˈrɪzoʊnə ˈsɪkəmɔːr / (AmE), / əˈrɪzənə ˈsɪkəmɔː / (BrE)
a medium-sized tree native to Arizona and nearby areas, with deeply lobed leaves and fruits in clusters of three to five

Examples

The Arizona sycamore tree
Scientists studied the leaves of the Arizona sycamore to learn more about the species.
The broad canopy of the Arizona sycamore provides much-needed shade in the desert.
We saw an Arizona sycamore growing near the riverbank during our hike.
