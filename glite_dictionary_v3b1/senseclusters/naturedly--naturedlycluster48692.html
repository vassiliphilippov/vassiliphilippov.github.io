naturedly

adverb
inherent manner
Pronunciation: / ˈneɪtʃərdli / (AmE), / ˈneɪtʃədli / (BrE)
in a way that reflects a person's natural character or temperament, often kindly or good-naturedly

Examples

Kindly naturedly offered
She naturedly offered help to anyone in need, showing her kind personality.
He responded naturedly to criticism, remaining calm and friendly throughout the discussion.
The child naturedly explored the garden, curious about every plant and insect.
She naturedly welcomed the guests with a warm smile.
The teacher naturedly explained the answer to the confused student.
He naturedly accepted the criticism and promised to improve.

Related Terms

  • antonym: artificially - in a way that is not natural; by human effort or design
  • antonym: gruffly - in a rough or unfriendly manner
  • synonym: innately - in a way that exists from birth; naturally
  • synonym: kindly - in a kind or gentle manner
