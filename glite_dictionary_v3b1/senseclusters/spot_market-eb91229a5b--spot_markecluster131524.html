spot market

noun
market for immediate delivery of goods or securities
6.6 4.2
Pronunciation: /spɑːt ˈmɑːr.kɪt/ /; / /spɑːt ˈmɑːr.kət/ (AmE), /spɒt ˈmɑː.kɪt/ /; / /spɒt ˈmɑː.kət/ (BrE)
A market in which commodities or securities are bought and sold for immediate delivery or delivery in the very near future.

Examples

The spot market price
Currency rates in the spot market often differ from those in futures contracts.
Many investors use the spot market to purchase oil for immediate delivery.
Wheat prices fluctuated sharply today in the spot market.
