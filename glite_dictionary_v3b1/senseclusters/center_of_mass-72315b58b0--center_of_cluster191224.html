center of mass

noun
point representing mean position of matter in a body
3.9 4.6
Pronunciation: /ˈsen.tər əv mæs/ /; / /ˈsen.tər ə mæs/ (AmE), /ˈsen.tə əv mæs/ /; / /ˈsen.tə ə mæs/ (BrE)
the point in a body or system at which the entire mass can be considered to be concentrated for certain calculations

Examples

The center of mass point
Astronauts in space must consider the center of mass when moving equipment to prevent spinning.
The center of mass of the Earth is slightly offset because of its uneven distribution of material.
When you balance a ruler on your finger, your finger is at its center of mass.
If you support the board at its centre of mass, it will stay level.
In physics problems, we often calculate the trajectory of the centre of mass rather than tracking every particle.
The centre of mass of the object was located using a simple balancing experiment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: center of gravity - the point in a body where the force of gravity can be considered to act
  • synonym: centroid - the geometric center of a plane figure or object
