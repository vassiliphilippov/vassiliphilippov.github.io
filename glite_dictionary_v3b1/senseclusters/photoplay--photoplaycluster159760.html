photoplay

noun
early term for movie
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊtoʊˌpleɪ / (AmE), / ˈfəʊtəʊˌpleɪ / (BrE)
an old-fashioned term for a motion picture or movie, especially in the early days of cinema

Examples

Silent photoplay
In the 1920s, people eagerly awaited the next silent photoplay to be shown at the local theater.
The museum displayed posters from famous photoplays of the early twentieth century.
She studied the history of the photoplay as part of her film course.
