proof

noun
test photographic print
Pronunciation: / pruːf / (AmE), / pruːf / (BrE)
a test print or preliminary version made to check for errors before final production (in printing or photography)

Examples

A photo proof
Making a proof is a standard part of the photographic printing process.
She asked to see a proof before ordering the full set of prints.
The photographer reviewed the proofs to select the best shots for enlargement.
A proof allows designers to spot mistakes before the full run of books is printed.
The editor carefully examined the proof for any typographical errors before approving the final print.
We received the proof yesterday and noticed several misprints that need correction.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: final proof - the last proof taken before printing the final edition
  • synonym: galley proof - a trial print of a typeset page used for proofreading
  • synonym: print - a photographic print; a reproduction of a photograph
