mortify

verb
restrain one's desires or body
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔːrtɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɔː.tɪ.faɪ /; / ˈmɔːtɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to control or subdue one's desires or body, especially by self-denial for moral or religious reasons

Examples

To mortify the body
He tried to mortify his desire for sweets during Lent.
Some monks mortify their bodies by fasting for extended periods.
The tradition required her to mortify her senses as an act of discipline.
Certain monks mortify the flesh to reach spiritual enlightenment.
His strict routine was designed to mortify physical desires and focus the mind.
She would mortify her appetite by fasting for days at a time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: discipline - to train or control by enforcing rules or self-restraint
  • synonym: subdue - to bring under control, especially by force
  • synonym: suppress - to put an end to by force; to keep under control
