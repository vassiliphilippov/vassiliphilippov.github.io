hero

noun
mythical being of great strength and courage
Pronunciation: / ˈhɪroʊ / (AmE), / ˈhɪərəʊ / (BrE)
(in classical mythology) a being of great strength and courage, often celebrated for daring exploits and typically the child of a mortal and a god.

Examples

Greek hero
Achilles was a legendary hero whose courage and strength became the subject of epic poetry.
In Greek mythology, Hercules is regarded as a celebrated hero known for his extraordinary feats.
Many classical stories describe the adventures and labors of a hero born from both human and divine parents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: demigod - a being with partial divine status, often the offspring of a god and a mortal
