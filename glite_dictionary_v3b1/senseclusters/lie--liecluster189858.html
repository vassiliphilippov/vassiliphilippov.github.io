lie

verb
remain in a particular state or condition
4.9
Pronunciation: / laɪ / (AmE), / laɪ / (BrE)
to exist, be found, or have its origin in a particular state, condition, or person (often abstract)

Examples

The answer lies hidden
Her true feelings would lie hidden beneath a calm exterior.
The lake may lie frozen through the entire winter.
The seeds can lie dormant in the soil for years before sprouting.
Responsibility for the accident lies with the careless driver.
Success often lies in the ability to adapt to new situations.
The final decision lies with the committee, not the CEO.
Much of the confusion lies in the lack of clear communication.
The beauty of this city lies in its rich cultural history.
The responsibility for the failure lies in poor planning at the beginning.
