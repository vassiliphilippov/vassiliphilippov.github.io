Day of Atonement

noun
Jewish holy day of fasting and repentance
Pronunciation: / ˌdeɪ əv əˈtoʊnmənt / (AmE), / ˌdeɪ əv əˈtəʊnmənt / (BrE)
(in Judaism) the holiest day of the Jewish year, marked by fasting, repentance, and prayer, observed on the tenth day of Tishri.

Examples

The Day of Atonement fast
Fasting from sunset to sunset is an important part of the Day of Atonement observance.
Many Jewish families gather at the synagogue for the Day of Atonement each year.
The Day of Atonement encourages reflection and seeking forgiveness for past actions.
