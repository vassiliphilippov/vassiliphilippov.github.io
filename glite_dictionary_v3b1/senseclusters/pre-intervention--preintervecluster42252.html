pre-intervention

adjective
before intervention
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˌɪntərˈvɛnʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːˌɪntəˈvɛnʃən / (BrE)
describing the period, condition, or data existing before an intervention or treatment takes place

Examples

Pre-intervention data
Researchers compared pre-intervention test scores with those collected after the program was introduced.
The pre-intervention assessment helped identify areas needing improvement before any changes were made.
All pre-intervention data must be recorded to measure the true impact of the treatment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: post-intervention - relating to the period after an intervention or treatment
