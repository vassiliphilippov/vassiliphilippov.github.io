insist

verb
state or demand firmly, refuse to yield
2.8 73/m
Pronunciation: / ÉªnËˆsÉªst / (AmE), / ÉªnËˆsÉªst / (BrE)
to say or demand something firmly and refuse to change your mind, especially when others disagree or are reluctant

Examples

To insist on help
If you insist on coming with us, you must be ready to leave early.
No matter how much we argued, he insisted on his original plan.
She insisted that the mistake be corrected before publication.
He insisted tirelessly that the board reconsider its decision.
If you insist on coming with us, you must be ready for a long day.
She insisted that the team follow her directions, repeating her request until everyone agreed.
Despite his apologies, they insisted he take responsibility for the mistake.
She insisted that she was right, even when everyone else disagreed.
The manager insisted on seeing proof before approving the expense.

Synonyms

  • take a firm stand
    4.4 0.08/m
    take a firm stand means stating a position strongly and refusing to change it, while insist means demanding it firmly despite others' objections.
  • assert
    3.9 28/m
    assert is saying something confidently and clearly, while insist is refusing to change that view and pressing it more strongly.
  • maintain
    4.4 18/m
    maintain is to keep stating a belief calmly over time, while insist is to demand it firmly and refuse to be changed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assert - to state something clearly and firmly
  • antonym: concede - to admit something is true or valid after first denying or resisting it
  • synonym: demand - to ask for something forcefully
  • degree higher: implore - to beg someone desperately to do something
  • synonym: persist - to continue firmly in an opinion or course of action
  • degree lower: request - to politely or formally ask for something
  • antonym: yield - to give way to arguments, demands, or pressure
