reframe

verb
to rebuild structure
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈfreɪm / (AmE), / ˌriːˈfreɪm / (BrE)
to replace, rebuild, or alter the structural frame of a building or other physical structure

Examples

To reframe a house
After the hurricane, the workers had to reframe the entire roof to make it safe again.
The old barn was so damaged that they decided to reframe its walls before restoring the rest.
To convert the garage into a living space, the builders needed to reframe the doorway.

Related Terms

  • antonym: demolish - to completely destroy a building or structure
  • synonym: reconstruct - to build something again after it has been damaged or destroyed
  • synonym: repair - to fix or mend something that is broken or damaged
