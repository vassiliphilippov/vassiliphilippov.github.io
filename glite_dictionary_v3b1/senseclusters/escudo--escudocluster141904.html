escudo

noun
former Portuguese currency unit
9.4
Pronunciation: / ɛˈskuːdoʊ / (AmE), / ɛˈskuːdəʊ / (BrE)
the former basic monetary unit of Portugal, equal to 100 centavos, used until the adoption of the euro in 2002

Examples

Portuguese escudo coin
He found a collection of old escudo coins in his grandfather's drawer.
Portugal replaced the escudo with the euro in 2002.
The price was listed in both escudos and euros during the transition period.

Related Terms

  • antonym: euro - currency that replaced the escudo in Portugal
