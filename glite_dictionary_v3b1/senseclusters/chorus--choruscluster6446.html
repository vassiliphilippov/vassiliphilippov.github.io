chorus

noun
group of actors in Greek drama
2.6 4.18/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…îÀêr…ôs /; / Ààk…îr…ôs / (AmE), / Ààk…îÀêr…ôs / (BrE)
a group of people who sing or perform together, especially in a play, musical, or concert

Examples

A chorus of singers
In many ancient Greek plays, the chorus sang odes between the acts.
The chorus entered the stage to narrate the upcoming events in the tragedy.
The chorus provided insights and moral commentary throughout the performance.
A full chorus joined the orchestra for the final movement of the symphony.
She auditioned to sing in the city chorus and was excited to be selected.
The school chorus practiced every afternoon for the spring concert.
A Greek tragedy often featured a chorus that commented on the action.
She joined the theater chorus in her first year at university.
The school chorus performed an energetic medley to open the show.
