do justice

verb
show or represent fairly or fully
1.0
Pronunciation: / du ˈdʒʌstɪs / (AmE), / duː ˈdʒʌstɪs / (BrE)
to show, treat, or appreciate someone or something in a way that fully recognizes their true value or quality

Examples

To do justice to beauty
No photograph can do justice to the beauty of the Grand Canyon.
She described the event with such detail that her words truly did justice to the experience.
The movie adaptation doesn't do justice to the complexity of the novel.
His thoughtful review really does justice to the complexity of the novel.
She worked hard to do justice to her grandmother's secret recipe.
The camera just doesn't do justice to the stunning colors of the sunset.

Related Terms

  • synonym: do honor to - to show respect or bring credit to something or someone
  • antonym: do injustice - to fail to represent accurately or fairly; to be unfair to
