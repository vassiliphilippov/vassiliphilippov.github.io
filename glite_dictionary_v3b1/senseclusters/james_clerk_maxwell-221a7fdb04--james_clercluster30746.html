James Clerk Maxwell

proper noun
Scottish physicist; unified electricity and magnetism
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒeɪmz ˈklɜrk ˈmækswɛl / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪmz ˈklɑːk ˈmækswəl / (BrE)
(1831–1879) A Scottish physicist renowned for unifying electricity and magnetism through his equations, and for recognizing the electromagnetic nature of light.

Examples

James Clerk Maxwell equations
James Clerk Maxwell revolutionized physics with his unified theory of electromagnetism.
