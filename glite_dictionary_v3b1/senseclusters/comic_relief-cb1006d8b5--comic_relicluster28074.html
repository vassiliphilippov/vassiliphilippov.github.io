comic relief

noun
humorous break in a serious work
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːmɪk rɪˈlif / (AmE), / ˈkɒmɪk rɪˈliːf / (BrE)
humorous content or character in a serious work of art or literature, intended to lighten the mood or provide a break from tension

Examples

A comic relief character
During the tense meeting, his witty comment provided some much-needed comic relief.
The play's intense scenes were balanced with moments of comic relief to keep the audience engaged.
The quirky sidekick served as comic relief throughout the otherwise dramatic film.

Related Terms

  • synonym: levity - lightness of mind or humor, especially in a serious situation
