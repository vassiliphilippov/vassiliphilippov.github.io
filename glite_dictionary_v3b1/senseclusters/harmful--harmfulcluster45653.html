harmful

adjective
causing damage or injury
2.6 13/m
Pronunciation: / Ààh…ërmf…ôl / (AmE), / Ààh…ëÀêmf äl / (BrE)
Causing or likely to cause damage, injury, or negative effects.

Examples

Harmful effects
Doctors warn that a poor diet may have harmful consequences for your health.
Excessive noise can be harmful to your hearing over time.
The chemicals in this cleaning product are potentially harmful if inhaled.

Synonyms

  • detrimental
    6.3 5.73/m
    detrimental is usually a formal word for causing harm to something, while harmful is more general everyday word.
  • damaging
    3.1 5.45/m
    damaging causes clear damage or loss to things or reputation, while harmful covers any kind of negative effect.
  • injurious
    7.0 0.86/m
    injurious often appears in law or medicine about physical harm, while harmful is more general and everyday.
  • deleterious
    8.5 1.64/m
    deleterious is used in science and medicine about harm to living things, while harmful is more common in everyday speech.
  • adverse
    6.0 11/m
    adverse is a formal word for unfavorable effects or conditions, while harmful simply means causing damage or injury.
  • dangerous
    1.5 101/m
    dangerous creates a sense of immediate threat or risk, while harmful focuses on causing damage or negative effects.
  • hazardous
    5.2 9.01/m
    hazardous describes things that involve risk or danger, often in work or chemicals, while harmful shows actual damage.
  • unsafe
    1.1 5.23/m
    unsafe means a lack of safety in a place or action, while harmful means it causes damage or negative effects.
  • destructive
    4.2 17/m
    destructive causes very large damage or ruin, often leaving nothing, while harmful can mean less severe negative effects.
  • hurtful
    2.9 0.13/m
    hurtful causes emotional pain or offense to people, while harmful can mean wider physical or general damage.

  • antonym: innocuous - not harmful or offensive
