caper spurge

noun
poisonous plant; seeds yield purgative oil
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪpər spɜrdʒ / (AmE), / ˈkeɪpə spɜːdʒ / (BrE)
a poisonous plant of the spurge family (Euphorbia lathyris), native to the Old World and introduced to America, whose seeds yield a purgative oil

Examples

The caper spurge plant
Gardeners should handle caper spurge carefully since its seeds are highly poisonous.
The milky sap of the caper spurge can cause skin irritation upon contact.
The oil extracted from caper spurge seeds was once used in traditional medicine as a purgative.
