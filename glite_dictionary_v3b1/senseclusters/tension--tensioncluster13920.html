tension

noun
latent hostility or strained relations
1.0 2.7
Pronunciation: /ˈten.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈten.ʃən/ (BrE)
a feeling of nervousness, strain, or hostility between people or within oneself

Examples

High tension in the room
A sense of tension filled the room as the rivals avoided direct eye contact.
Negotiators worked hard to ease the tension after the recent conflict.
There was a noticeable tension between the two colleagues during the meeting.
After weeks of constant deadlines, her tension finally turned into exhaustion.
Meditation is one way to relieve mental tension and promote relaxation.
You could feel the tension in the room before the results were announced.
