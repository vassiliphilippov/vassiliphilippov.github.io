Markov process

noun
stochastic process dependent only on present state
8.1 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààm…ëÀêrk…îv Ààpro äs…õs / (AmE), / Ààm…ëÀêk…íf Ààpr…ô äs…õs / (BrE)
a stochastic process in which the probability of each future state depends only on the current state and not on the sequence of events that preceded it

Examples

A Markov process model
A weather model that uses a Markov process assumes tomorrow's conditions depend only on today's weather.
Stock price movements are often modeled as a Markov process in financial mathematics.
The Markov process framework simplifies the prediction of complex systems by ignoring their history.

Synonyms

  • Markoff process
    9.9 0.00/m
    Markoff process is an older spelling sometimes seen in literature, while Markov process is the standard modern mathematical term.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Markov chain - a discrete-time Markov process with a countable number of states
