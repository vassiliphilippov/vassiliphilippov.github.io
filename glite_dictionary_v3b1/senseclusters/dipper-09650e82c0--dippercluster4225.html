Dipper

noun
group of seven stars in Ursa Minor
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪpər / (AmE), / ˈdɪpə / (BrE)
a group of seven stars forming a shape in the sky, known as the Big Dipper or Little Dipper

Examples

The Big Dipper
Children learned to trace the shape of the Dipper among the stars.
Many people use the Dipper to find the North Star, Polaris.
The Dipper is easy to spot in the northern night sky during summer.
As the sky darkened, the Dipper became visible above the northern horizon.
She pointed out the Dipper to her friends and explained its importance in navigation.
The seven stars of the Dipper are among the most recognizable patterns in the night sky.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Big Dipper - a group of seven bright stars in Ursa Major, forming another dipper-shaped asterism
  • synonym: Little Dipper - the asterism formed by seven stars in Ursa Minor, including Polaris
  • synonym: Plough - British name for the Big Dipper asterism
  • synonym: Ursa Major - the constellation containing the Big Dipper
