reserve

verb
set aside or allocate for a purpose/person
2.6 2.6
Pronunciation: / rɪˈzɜrv /; / rɪˈzɝv / (AmE), / rɪˈzɜːv / (BrE)
to set something aside or hold it back for a particular person, purpose, or future use

Examples

To reserve time
She always reserves Sundays for family time.
We've decided to reserve a portion of our profits for charity this year.
You should reserve a meeting room in advance if you plan to work late.
She reserved her judgment until all the facts were known.
We reserve the right to make changes at any time.
You should reserve some money for emergencies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allocate - to distribute or assign a resource for a particular purpose
  • synonym: retain - to keep possession of; continue to have
  • synonym: set aside - to save something for a special purpose or person
  • synonym: withhold - to hold back; to refuse to give
