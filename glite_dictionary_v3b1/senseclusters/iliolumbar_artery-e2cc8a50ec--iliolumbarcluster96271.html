iliolumbar artery

noun
artery supplying pelvic muscles and bones
Pronunciation: / ɪlioʊˌlʌmbɑr ˈɑrtəri / (AmE), / ɪlɪəʊˌlʌmbɑː ˈɑːtəri / (BrE)
an artery that arises from the internal iliac artery and supplies blood to the pelvic muscles and bones

Examples

iliolumbar artery supplies muscles
An injury to the iliolumbar artery can cause significant bleeding within the pelvis.
During the dissection, the surgeon identified the iliolumbar artery branching from the internal iliac artery.
The iliolumbar artery provides blood to the iliacus and psoas muscles.
