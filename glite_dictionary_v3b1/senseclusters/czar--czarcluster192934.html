czar

noun
a powerful official; policy chief
8.5 8.0
Pronunciation: / zɑr / (AmE), / zɑː / (BrE)
a person invested with great power or authority, especially one appointed to direct a particular area of policy or activity

Examples

Drug czar

Critics argued that giving so much authority to a single czar could undermine existing agencies.
The president appointed a czar to coordinate the country's response to the crisis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kingpin - the most important person in an organization or activity
  • antonym: underling - a person lower in status or rank, subordinate
