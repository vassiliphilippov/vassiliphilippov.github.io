clientelism

noun
patron–client system
9.5
Pronunciation: / klaɪˈɛntəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / klaɪˈɛntɪlɪzəm /; / klaɪˈɛntəlɪzəm / (BrE)
a political or social system where powerful people give benefits or favors to supporters in exchange for their loyalty or support

Examples

Clientelism system
The politician was accused of clientelism after giving government contracts to his supporters.
In some countries, clientelism shapes the way local leaders distribute resources and favors.
Efforts to reduce clientelism often focus on increasing transparency and fair competition.

  • synonym: nepotism - favoritism shown to relatives, especially in jobs
