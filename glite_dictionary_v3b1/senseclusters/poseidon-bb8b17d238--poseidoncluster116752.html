Poseidon

proper noun
Greek god of the sea and earthquakes
6.3
Pronunciation: / poʊˈsaɪdən / (AmE), / pəˈsaɪdən / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the god of the sea, earthquakes, and horses; brother of Zeus and Hades, and counterpart to the Roman god Neptune.

Examples

Poseidon god of sea
A magnificent statue of Poseidon stands in the center of the fountain.
In the ancient epic, Poseidon stirs the sea to challenge Odysseus on his journey home.
Many sailors prayed to Poseidon for safe passage across the Mediterranean.
