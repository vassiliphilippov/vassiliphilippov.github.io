untriable

adjective
not able to be tried
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈtraɪəbəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈtraɪəbəl / (BrE)
not able to be brought to trial or judged in a court of law

Examples

Untriable case
The judge ruled that the evidence was insufficient, making the case untriable.
Due to a legal technicality, the defendant's actions were considered untriable in this jurisdiction.
Some crimes may be untriable if the statute of limitations has expired.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ineligible - not qualified or permitted to do something
  • antonym: triable - able to be brought to trial or judged in court
