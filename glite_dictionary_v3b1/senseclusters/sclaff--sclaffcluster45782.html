sclaff

noun
golf stroke where club hits ground first
9.9
Pronunciation: / sklæf / (AmE), / sklɑːf / (BrE)
In golf, a poor stroke where the club head strikes the ground before making contact with the ball.

Examples

A bad sclaff
A sclaff like that can add several strokes to your round.
He ruined his approach shot with a sclaff just short of the green.
She blamed the wet grass for her unexpected sclaff on the last hole.
