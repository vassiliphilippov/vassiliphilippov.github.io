Purkinje

proper noun
visual sensitivity shift
Pronunciation: / pərˈkɪndʒi / (AmE), / pəˈkɪndʒi / (BrE)
the visual phenomenon where, as light dims, the eye becomes more sensitive to blue light than to red light

Examples

Purkinje phenomenon
The Purkinje phenomenon explains why blue flowers seem brighter than red ones at dusk.
As night falls, the Purkinje effect makes it harder to see red traffic signs clearly.
Scientists study the Purkinje shift to understand how our eyes adapt to darkness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Purkinje effect - the shift in peak visual sensitivity toward blue as light dims
  • synonym: scotopic vision - vision under low light conditions
