drivel

verb
let saliva flow from the mouth
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈdrɪvəl / (AmE), / ˈdrɪvəl / (BrE)
(of saliva) to flow from the mouth; to let saliva run from the mouth, often unintentionally.

Examples

Babies drivel saliva
Infants often drivel when they are teething.
She wiped the puppy's mouth, which continued to drivel onto her shirt.
The elderly man's lips parted as he began to drivel in his sleep.
