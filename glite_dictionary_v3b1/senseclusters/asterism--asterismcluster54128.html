asterism

noun
group of stars forming a pattern
Pronunciation: / ˈæstərˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈastərɪzəm / (BrE)
a group or recognizable pattern of stars in the night sky, often smaller than a constellation

Examples

Night sky asterism
Many amateur astronomers enjoy finding different asterisms with their telescopes.
The Big Dipper is an asterism that forms part of the constellation Ursa Major.
The Summer Triangle is a well-known asterism visible in the northern hemisphere's summer sky.
