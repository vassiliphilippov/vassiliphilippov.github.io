handfast

verb
to pledge in marriage
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhændˌfæst / (AmE), / ˈhændˌfɑːst / (BrE)
to promise to marry or to pledge in marriage, especially in a formal or ceremonial way (archaic)

Examples

To handfast a couple
In ancient times, couples would handfast before a priest as a sign of their engagement.
The tradition to handfast was once common in rural communities before official marriage ceremonies.
They chose to handfast in a private ceremony, exchanging vows and tokens.

Related Terms

  • synonym: betroth - to formally promise to marry
  • degree higher: marry - to become the husband or wife of someone
