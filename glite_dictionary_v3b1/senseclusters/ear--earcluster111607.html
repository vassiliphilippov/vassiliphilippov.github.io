ear

noun
ability to perceive or appreciate sounds
Pronunciation: / ɪr / (AmE), / ɪə / (BrE)
the ability to perceive or appreciate sounds, especially in music, speech, or language

Examples

A musical ear
A musician needs a good ear to distinguish subtle changes in notes.
Over time, his ear for jazz improved and he could identify complex rhythms.
She has an ear for languages and picks up accents easily.

Related Terms

  • synonym: musicality - sensitivity to, knowledge of, or talent for music
  • synonym: talent - a natural ability to do something well
  • antonym: tone-deafness - inability to perceive differences in musical pitch
