collateral

noun
security for a loan
6.5 6.0
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈlæt.ə.rəl/ (AmE), /kə.ˈlæt.ə.rəl/ (BrE)
property or other asset pledged as security for the repayment of a loan, to be forfeited in the event of a default.

Examples

Bank collateral
She used her company shares as collateral to secure additional funding.
The bank required him to put up his house as collateral for the loan.
Without sufficient collateral, the lender will not approve your application.
