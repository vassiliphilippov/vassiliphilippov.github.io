concomitant

noun
something occurring together with another
8.9 0.27/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàk…ëÀêm.…ô.t…ônt / (AmE), / k…ônÀàk…ím.…™.t…ônt / (BrE)
an event or situation that happens at the same time as, or in connection with, another.

Examples

A concomitant problem
Economic growth frequently brings concomitant challenges such as rising inequality.
High blood pressure is often a concomitant of obesity.
The festival and its concomitant celebrations attract thousands of tourists each year.

Synonyms

  • accompaniment
    5.2 0.30/m
    accompaniment is something that happens alongside another event, while concomitant refers to an event linked to and occurring with it.
  • co-occurrence
    7.1 0.00/m
    co-occurrence is a technical term for two things happening together, while concomitant often implies a related event connected in context.
  • attendant
    6.1 1.26/m
    attendant is often an adjective for something that comes with another condition, while concomitant is a noun for that related event.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accompaniment - something that goes with or complements another
