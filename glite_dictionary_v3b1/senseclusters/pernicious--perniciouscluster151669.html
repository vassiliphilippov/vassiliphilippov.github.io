pernicious

adjective
causing harm in a subtle or gradual way
3.7
Pronunciation: / pərˈnɪʃəs / (AmE), / pəˈnɪʃəs / (BrE)
having a very harmful effect, especially in a gradual or subtle way

Examples

A pernicious effect
Long-term exposure to the chemical can have pernicious effects on human health.
Rumors can be pernicious, spreading quickly and causing lasting damage to reputations.
The company's deceptive marketing practices have a pernicious influence on consumers.
Economic inequality can have a pernicious impact on social stability.
He warned of the pernicious effects of smoking on health.
The spread of pernicious misinformation online can damage public trust in science.

Related Terms

  • antonym: beneficial - resulting in good; favorable or advantageous
  • antonym: benign - not harmful or kind in effect
  • synonym: harmful - causing or likely to cause harm
  • synonym: insidious - proceeding in a gradual, subtle way, but with harmful effects
