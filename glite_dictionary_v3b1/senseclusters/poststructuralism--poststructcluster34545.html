poststructuralism

noun
theory challenging fixed meanings
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊstˈstrʌktʃərəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌpəʊstˈstrʌktʃərəlɪzəm / (BrE)
a theory in philosophy and literary criticism that challenges the idea of fixed meanings and structures in language and culture

Examples

Poststructuralism theory
The rise of poststructuralism changed the way many academics approach texts and cultural analysis.
Her research explores how poststructuralism influences modern theories of identity.
Many university courses introduce students to poststructuralism alongside other critical theories.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deconstruction - a method of analysis that questions traditional assumptions about certainty and meaning
  • antonym: structuralism - a theory that analyzes culture and language as systems of interrelated structures
