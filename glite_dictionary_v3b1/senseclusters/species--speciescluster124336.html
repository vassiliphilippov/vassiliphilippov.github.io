species

noun
biological group of interbreeding organisms
2.6
Pronunciation: / ˈspiːʃiːz / (AmE), / ˈspiːʃiːz / (BrE)
(biology) A group of living organisms consisting of similar individuals capable of exchanging genes or interbreeding.

Examples

A species of bird
Scientists discovered a new species of frog in the remote mountains of Vietnam.
The panda is an endangered species due to habitat loss and low birth rates.
There are thousands of bird species found throughout the Amazon rainforest.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: genus - Taxonomic group above species, including similar species
  • degree lower: subspecies - Taxonomic group below species, identifiable variant within a species
