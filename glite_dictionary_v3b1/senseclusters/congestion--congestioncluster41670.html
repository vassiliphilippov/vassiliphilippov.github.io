the excessive accumulation of blood or other fluid in a part of the body, often causing swelling or pressure

Examples

Nasal congestion
Nasal congestion made it difficult for her to breathe normally during her cold.
Swelling and congestion often accompany allergic reactions in sensitive individuals.
The doctor explained that chest congestion could be a sign of a respiratory infection.

Synonyms

  • intumescency
    9.9 0.00/m
    intumescency is a swelling caused by blood or fluid build-up, while congestion is the excess accumulation causing pressure in a body part.
  • engorgement
    9.1 0.09/m
    engorgement is being abnormally filled, often with blood, while congestion describes a dangerous excess of fluid causing local swelling and pressure.
  • tumefaction
    9.9 0.01/m
    tumefaction is the process of tissue swelling from fluid build-up, while congestion names the abnormal accumulation that creates that pressure.
  • edema
    8.0 1.23/m
    edema is excess watery fluid in tissues causing visible swelling, while congestion often involves blood pooling or other types of fluid causing pressure.

  • synonym: inflammation - localized physical condition with redness and swelling
