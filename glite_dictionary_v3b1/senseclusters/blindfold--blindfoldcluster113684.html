blindfold

verb
cover someone's eyes to prevent sight
1.6
Pronunciation: / ˈblaɪndˌfoʊld / (AmE), / ˈblaɪndˌfəʊld / (BrE)
to cover someone's eyes with a cloth or similar covering to prevent them from seeing

Examples

To blindfold a player
Before the game, each participant was blindfolded and spun around.
She blindfolded her little brother so he couldn't see his birthday surprise.
The kidnappers blindfolded the victim before taking him into the building.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unblindfold - to remove a blindfold from someone
