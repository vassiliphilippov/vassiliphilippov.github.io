lection

noun
variant manuscript reading
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˈlɛkʃən / (BrE)
a particular version or reading of a word or passage found in different manuscripts or editions, especially in textual criticism

Examples

A rare lection
The scholar noted a unique lection in the ancient Greek manuscript.
Comparing each lection helps editors reconstruct the original text.
A single altered lection can change the meaning of a historical document.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reading - a particular interpretation or version of a text
  • synonym: variant - a different form or version of something
