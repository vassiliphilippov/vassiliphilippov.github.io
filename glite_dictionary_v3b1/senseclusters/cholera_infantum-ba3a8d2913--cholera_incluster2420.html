An often fatal form of gastroenteritis in young children, especially infants, characterized by severe diarrhea and vomiting, and historically mistaken for cholera.

Examples

Cholera infantum outbreak
During the summer months, outbreaks of cholera infantum were common in overcrowded cities.
In the 19th century, cholera infantum was a leading cause of death among urban infants.
Proper sanitation greatly reduced the incidence of cholera infantum in developed countries.
