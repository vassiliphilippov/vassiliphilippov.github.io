boardinghouse

noun
private house with rooms and meals for paying guests
Pronunciation: / ˈbɔrdɪŋˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈbɔːdɪŋˌhaʊs / (BrE)
A private house where people pay to live and are provided with meals.

Examples

A boardinghouse with meals
After losing his job, Martin was forced to move into a boardinghouse downtown.
She cooked dinner every night for the students staying at her boardinghouse.
The boardinghouse offered both single and shared rooms to its long-term residents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lodging house - house providing accommodations, usually without meals
