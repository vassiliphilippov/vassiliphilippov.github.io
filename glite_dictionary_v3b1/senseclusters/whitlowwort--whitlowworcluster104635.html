whitlowwort

noun
a plant of the genus Paronychia
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪtˌloʊˌwɜrt / (AmE), / ˈwɪtləʊˌwɜːt / (BrE)
a low-growing tufted plant of the genus Paronychia, with tiny greenish flowers and usually whorled leaves, formerly believed to cure whitlows.

Examples

The whitlowwort plant
Historically, whitlowwort was applied as a remedy for infections around the fingernail.
Several species of whitlowwort thrive in warm, sandy soils throughout North America.
The herbalist recommended an ointment containing whitlowwort for skin irritations.
