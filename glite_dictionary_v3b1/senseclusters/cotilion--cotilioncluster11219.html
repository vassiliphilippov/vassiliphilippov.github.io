cotilion

noun
18th-century lively French dance
5.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / koʊˈtɪliən / (AmE), / kəˈtɪliən / (BrE)
A lively social dance, originally from France and popular in the 18th and 19th centuries, characterized by patterned steps and often performed by couples.

Examples

A cotilion dance
At the historical ball, everyone wore period dress and participated in a cotilion.
Learning the steps of the cotilion was considered essential for young ladies in society.
The guests formed sets and danced a cotilion late into the night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: quadrille - a similar style of square dance popular in the same era
