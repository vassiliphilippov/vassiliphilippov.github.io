ragdoll

verb
to collapse limply
Pronunciation: / ˈræɡˌdɑl / (AmE), / ˈræɡˌdɒl / (BrE)
to cause a person or object to fall or move in a limp, uncontrolled way, especially using simulated physics in video games

Examples

To ragdoll a character
When the car hit the barrier, the driver was ragdolled across the track in the simulation.
In the game, defeated enemies ragdoll realistically when struck by powerful attacks.
The character ragdolled down the stairs after losing all health points.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collapse - to fall down or give way suddenly
  • antonym: stiffen - to become rigid or firm
