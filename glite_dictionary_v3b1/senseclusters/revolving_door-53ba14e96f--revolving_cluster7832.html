revolving door

noun
organization with frequent staff turnover
6.8 3.7
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈvɑːl.vɪŋ dɔːr/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈvɒl.vɪŋ dɔː/ (BrE)
a situation or organization where people or things are repeatedly replaced or changed, especially because of frequent staff turnover

Examples

Revolving door of staff
After a year, the department was known as a revolving door because so many staff members had left.
The company has become a revolving door, with employees constantly coming and going.
When management changed, the hospital turned into a revolving door for nurses.
High staff turnover created a revolving door environment in the department.
She was tired of being caught in a revolving door of failed relationships.
The company had a revolving door of managers, making it hard to implement long-term changes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attrition - gradual reduction in staff, especially by not replacing those who leave
  • synonym: turnover - rate at which employees leave and are replaced
