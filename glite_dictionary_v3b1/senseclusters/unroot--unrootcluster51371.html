unroot

verb
to displace people
8.2 0.00/m
Pronunciation: /  ånÀàruÀêt / (AmE), /  ånÀàruÀêt / (BrE)
to force someone or a group to leave their home or familiar environment, often causing emotional or social displacement

Examples

To unroot a community
The war threatened to unroot thousands of families from their ancestral villages.
Rapid urban development can unroot entire communities, leaving people feeling lost.
She felt unrooted after moving to a new country far from her friends and family.
