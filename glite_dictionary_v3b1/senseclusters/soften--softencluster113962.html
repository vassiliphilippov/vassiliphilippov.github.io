soften

verb
make less severe or harsh
1.0 2.6
Pronunciation: /ˈsɑːf.ən/ /; / /ˈsɔːf.ən/ (AmE), /ˈsɒf.ən/ (BrE)
to make something less severe, harsh, intense, or forceful; to make something gentler or less hard (emotionally, physically, or in appearance/sound)

Examples

To soften a tone
His angry expression softened when he heard the explanation.
The company decided to soften its policy after receiving feedback.
The teacher tried to soften her criticism to avoid discouraging the student.
A special lens can soften the image for a more romantic effect.
The audio engineer used a filter to soften the background noise in the recording.
You can soften the light by placing a sheer curtain over the window.
She tried to soften his disappointment with kind words.
The government offered financial aid to soften the blow of the new regulations.
Waiting a few days may soften the impact of the bad news.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cushion - to protect from impact by absorbing shock
  • antonym: harden - make or become more severe or harsh
  • antonym: intensify - make more forceful, severe, or extreme
  • synonym: mitigate - make less severe, serious, or painful
  • synonym: moderate - make less extreme, severe, or violent
  • synonym: mollify - to make someone less angry; to calm
