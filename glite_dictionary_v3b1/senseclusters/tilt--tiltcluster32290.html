tilt

noun
slight preference or bias
Pronunciation: / tɪlt / (AmE), / tɪlt / (BrE)
a slight but noticeable preference or bias toward one side or point of view

Examples

Tilt in reporting
Her reporting showed a clear tilt in favor of progressive policies.
The panel's tilt toward environmental issues was evident in its decisions.
There is a slight tilt to the left in the magazine's editorial stance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bias - a tendency to prefer one person or thing to another, often unfairly
  • antonym: neutrality - the state of not supporting either side in a conflict or disagreement
