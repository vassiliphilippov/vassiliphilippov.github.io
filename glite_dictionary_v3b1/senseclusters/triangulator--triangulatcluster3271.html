triangulator

noun
mesh-generating algorithm
Pronunciation: / traɪˈæŋɡjəˌleɪtər / (AmE), / traɪˈæŋɡjʊleɪtə / (BrE)
a computer program or algorithm that divides a set of points or a surface into triangles for graphics, modeling, or simulation

Examples

3D model triangulator
The engineer used a triangulator to generate a mesh for the 3D model.
Before running the simulation, the software's triangulator processed the surface data.
A good triangulator can handle complex shapes and produce efficient meshes for rendering.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mesher - a program that creates a mesh from geometric data
  • synonym: subdivider - a tool that divides a surface into smaller parts
