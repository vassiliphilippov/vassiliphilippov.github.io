fall asleep

verb
begin to sleep
1.0
Pronunciation: / fɔl əˈslip / (AmE), / fɔːl əˈsliːp / (BrE)
to begin to sleep; to start sleeping after being awake

Examples

To fall asleep in bed
I usually fall asleep within minutes of going to bed.
She was so tired that she fell asleep during the movie.
You might fall asleep if you keep reading in that comfortable chair.
He tends to fall asleep as soon as his head hits the pillow.
I sometimes fall asleep on the train ride home after work.
Try not to fall asleep during the professor's long lecture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: doze off - to start sleeping, especially unintentionally or briefly
  • synonym: nod off - to fall asleep, especially briefly or unintentionally
  • antonym: wake up - to stop sleeping and become conscious
