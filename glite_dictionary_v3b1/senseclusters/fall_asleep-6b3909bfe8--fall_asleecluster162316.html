fall asleep

verb
begin to sleep
1.0 18/m
Pronunciation: / f…îl …ôÀàslip / (AmE), / f…îÀêl …ôÀàsliÀêp / (BrE)
to begin to sleep; to start sleeping after being awake

Examples

To fall asleep in bed
I usually fall asleep within minutes of going to bed.
She was so tired that she fell asleep during the movie.
You might fall asleep if you keep reading in that comfortable chair.
He tends to fall asleep as soon as his head hits the pillow.
I sometimes fall asleep on the train ride home after work.
Try not to fall asleep during the professor's long lecture.

Synonyms

  • go to sleep
    1.0 11/m
    go to sleep means preparing and lying down to begin sleeping, while fall asleep simply means to start sleeping.
  • drift off
    3.8 1.85/m
    drift off describes a slow, gradual move into sleep without intending to, while fall asleep names starting to sleep.
  • drowse off
    5.5 0.02/m
    drowse off means gradually slipping into light sleep while feeling sleepy, while fall asleep is the general act of starting sleep.
  • doze off
    2.7 1.64/m
    doze off means fall into a short, light sleep without meaning to, while fall asleep covers starting sleep in any situation.
  • nod off
    4.1 0.54/m
    nod off means briefly sleep for a short time, often in a chair or during the day, while fall asleep is more general.
  • drop off
    3.0 0.41/m
    drop off means unintentionally start sleeping for a short period, often while relaxed, while fall asleep is neutral and general.
  • hit the pillow
    3.7 0.14/m
    hit the pillow means go to bed and sleep quickly from tiredness, while fall asleep does not imply speed or bed.
  • nod out
    7.9 0.04/m
    nod out means suddenly sleep from extreme tiredness or exhaustion, while fall asleep simply means to begin sleeping.

Related Terms

  • synonym: doze off - to start sleeping, especially unintentionally or briefly
  • synonym: nod off - to fall asleep, especially briefly or unintentionally
  • antonym: wake up - to stop sleeping and become conscious
