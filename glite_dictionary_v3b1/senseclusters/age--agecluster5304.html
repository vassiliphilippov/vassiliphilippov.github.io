age

verb
to become or appear older
Pronunciation: / eɪdʒ / (AmE), / eɪdʒ / (BrE)
to become or make older, or to show signs of getting older

Examples

To age naturally
As we age, our skin naturally becomes less elastic.
She seemed to age overnight after hearing the news.
Stress can cause a person to age faster than normal.
As we age, our perspectives on life often change.
Her face began to age noticeably in her late thirties.
Some wines age better than others, improving in flavor over time.
Exposure to the harsh sun can age your skin faster than usual.
Heavy stress can age a person prematurely.
The loss of her husband seemed to age her overnight.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mature - to develop fully, become adult
  • antonym: rejuvenate - to make or become young again
  • synonym: senesce - to grow old; show the effects of increasing age
