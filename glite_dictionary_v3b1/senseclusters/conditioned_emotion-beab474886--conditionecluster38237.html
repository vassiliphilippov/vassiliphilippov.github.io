conditioned emotion

noun
emotion learned by conditioning
9.1 6.2
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈdɪʃ.ənd ɪ.ˈmoʊ.ʃən/ (AmE), /kən.ˈdɪʃ.ənd ɪ.ˈməʊ.ʃən/ (BrE)
an emotional reaction acquired through conditioning, especially as a result of repeated association of a neutral stimulus with an emotional response

Examples

Conditioned emotion response
Over time, a conditioned emotion like anxiety can be learned in response to certain sounds.
The sight of the laboratory rat triggered a conditioned emotion of fear in the child.
Therapists work to help patients unlearn harmful conditioned emotions through exposure therapy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: learned response - a response that is acquired through experience or conditioning
  • antonym: unconditioned emotion - an inborn or instinctive emotional response, not learned through conditioning
