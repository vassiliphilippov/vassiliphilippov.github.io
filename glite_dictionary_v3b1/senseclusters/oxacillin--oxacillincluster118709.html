oxacillin

noun
penicillinase-resistant antibiotic
6.3
Pronunciation: /ɑːk.sə.ˈsɪl.ɪn/ /; / /ɑːk.ə.ˈsɪl.ɪn/ (AmE), /ɒk.sə.ˈsɪl.ɪn/ /; / /ɒk.ə.ˈsɪl.ɪn/ (BrE)
A semisynthetic form of penicillin that is resistant to penicillinase and used to treat infections caused by penicillin-resistant staphylococci.

Examples

Oxacillin antibiotic
Allergic reactions to oxacillin are rare but can be serious in some patients.
Certain strains of bacteria are unaffected by oxacillin therapy.
The doctor prescribed oxacillin to treat the resistant staph infection.
