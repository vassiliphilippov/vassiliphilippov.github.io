gregarious

adjective
(plants) growing in closely grouped clusters
6.5
Pronunciation: / ɡrɪˈɡɛriəs / (AmE), / ɡrɪˈɡɛːrɪəs / (BrE)
(of plants) growing in groups or clusters that are close together.

Examples

Gregarious plants together
The orchid is a gregarious species, often forming large groups in open meadows.
These gregarious reeds thrive along the banks of the river in dense stands.
Unlike solitary cacti, some gregarious varieties flourish only when growing close together.
