magnanimity

noun
generosity or nobility of spirit
7.4 6.8
Pronunciation: /mæg.nə.ˈnɪm.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /mæg.nə.ˈnɪm.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
the quality of being very generous, forgiving, or noble in mind and spirit, especially toward a rival or someone less powerful

Examples

Great magnanimity
Her magnanimity was evident when she forgave her opponent after the heated debate.
History remembers the magnanimity of leaders who showed kindness to their enemies.
In an act of true magnanimity, he offered to pay for the repairs out of his own pocket.

Related Terms

  • synonym: generosity - the quality of being generous
  • antonym: meanness - lack of generosity or kindness
