tribally

adverb
with group loyalty
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈtraɪbəli / (AmE), / ˈtraɪbəli / (BrE)
in a manner that shows strong loyalty to one's group and opposition to others, often marked by partisanship or factionalism

Examples

Tribally divided debate
The debate was conducted tribally, with each side refusing to listen to the other.
Political discussions often become tribally charged, making compromise difficult.
Social media can encourage people to think tribally rather than consider different perspectives.

Related Terms

  • antonym: objectively - in a way that is not influenced by personal feelings or group loyalty
  • synonym: partisanly - in a way that strongly supports one group or party
