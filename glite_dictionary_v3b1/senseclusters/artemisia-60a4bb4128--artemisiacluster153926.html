Artemisia

noun
genus of aromatic shrubs and herbs
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːrtəˈmiːziə / (AmE), / ˌɑːtɪˈmiːziə / (BrE)
A genus of aromatic shrubs and herbs, including wormwood, sagebrush, mugwort, and tarragon, native to northern temperate regions, South Africa, and western South America.

Examples

Artemisia plants
Gardeners often plant Artemisia for its silvery foliage and drought resistance.
Many species within the genus Artemisia are valued for their medicinal properties.
The bitter flavor of absinthe comes from Artemisia absinthium, also known as wormwood.
