bay laurel

noun
Mediterranean evergreen tree with aromatic leaves
Pronunciation: / beɪ ˈlɔrəl / (AmE), / beɪ ˈlɒrəl / (BrE)
A small Mediterranean evergreen tree (Laurus nobilis) with aromatic glossy leaves used in cooking and, in ancient times, for making ceremonial crowns.

Examples

A bay laurel wreath
A bay laurel wreath was placed on the winner's head in the ancient Olympic Games.
She added a fresh bay laurel leaf to the simmering stew for extra flavor.
The garden features several aromatic plants, including bay laurel and rosemary.
