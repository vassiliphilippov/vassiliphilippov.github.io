Mahayana Buddhism

noun

Pronunciation: /mɑː.hə.ˈjɑː.nə ˈbuː.dɪ.zəm/ /; / /mʌ.hə.ˈjɑː.nə ˈbuː.dɪ.zəm/ /; / /mə.hə.ˈjɑː.nə ˈbʊd.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /mɑː.hə.ˈjɑː.nə ˈbʊd.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /mʌ.hə.ˈjɑː.nə ˈbʊd.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /mə.hə.ˈjɑː.nə ˈbʊd.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
A major branch of Buddhism that emphasizes the path to universal salvation, especially through faith and compassion, and is predominant in China, Tibet, and Japan.

Examples

Mahayana Buddhism teachings
Mahayana Buddhism teaches that enlightenment is accessible to all beings, not just monks.
Many monks in Tibet practice Mahayana Buddhism as part of their spiritual journey.
The temples in Japan often reflect the traditions of Mahayana Buddhism.

Related Terms
