eosinophil

noun
a white blood cell involved in immune response
Pronunciation: / ˌiːəˈsɪnəfɪl / (AmE), / ˌiːəˈsɪnəfɪl / (BrE)
a type of white blood cell that stains readily with eosin and plays a role in allergic reactions and immunity against parasites

Examples

A eosinophil cell
A high count of eosinophils can indicate an allergic reaction or parasitic infection.
In microscopic slides, eosinophils appear with a characteristic pinkish-red staining.
The doctor ordered a blood test to check the levels of eosinophil in the patient's body.
