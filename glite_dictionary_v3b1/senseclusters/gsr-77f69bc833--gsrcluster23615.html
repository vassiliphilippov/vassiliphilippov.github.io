GSR

noun
change in skin's electrical properties (stress measurement)
8.7
Pronunciation: / dʒi ɛs ɑːr / (AmE), / dʒiː ɛs ɑː / (BrE)
a change in the electrical properties of the skin, typically measured to assess physiological responses to stress or anxiety

Examples

GSR measurement
Her GSR spiked as she answered difficult questions.
Scientists use GSR sensors to monitor subjects' physiological responses to stimuli.
The GSR readings indicated elevated stress levels during the test.

Related Terms

  • synonym: galvanic skin response - change in electrical conductance of the skin due to stress
