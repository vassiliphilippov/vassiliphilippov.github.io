deficit

noun
the shortfall when liabilities exceed assets
1.0 3.5
Pronunciation: /ˈde.fə.sɪt/ /; / /ˈde.fɪ.sɪt/ (AmE), /ˈde.fɪ.sɪt/ (BrE)
an amount by which something is less than what is needed or expected, especially money, resources, or time

Examples

Budget deficit
A growing budget deficit can lead to increased national debt.
After several years of running a deficit, the organization finally broke even.
The company reported a deficit of $10 million for the last fiscal year.
A sleep deficit can impact your ability to concentrate and remember information.
The country is addressing its budget deficit by reducing spending and raising taxes.
The organization struggled to overcome its large deficit after a year of reduced donations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: surplus - the amount by which assets or income exceed liabilities
