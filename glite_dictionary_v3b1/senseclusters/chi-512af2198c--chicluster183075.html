ch'i

noun
circulating life energy in Chinese philosophy

Pronunciation: /kaɪ/ /; / /tʃiː/ /; / /tʃaɪ/ /; / /ʃaɪ/ /; / /tʃə/ (AmE), /tʃiː/ /; / /tʃaɪ/ /; / /kaɪ/ /; / /ʃaɪ/ /; / /tʃə/ (BrE)
In Chinese philosophy and traditional medicine, the circulating life energy believed to be inherent in all things and essential for health.

Examples

Flow of ch'i
According to traditional Chinese medicine, illness is caused by a disruption in the flow of ch'i within the body.
Many practitioners of tai chi believe that controlling your ch'i can improve both physical and mental health.
The concept of ch'i is central to understanding acupuncture and other Eastern healing practices.
