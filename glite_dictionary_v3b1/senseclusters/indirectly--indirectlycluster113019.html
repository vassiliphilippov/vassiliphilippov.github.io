indirectly

adverb
not straightforwardly; not directly
3.7 3.4
Pronunciation: /ɪn.də.ˈrekt.liː/ /; / /ɪn.dɪ.ˈrekt.liː/ (AmE), /ɪn.də.ˈrekt.liː/ /; / /ɪn.dɪ.ˈrekt.liː/ (BrE)
in a way that is not straightforward or forthright; without directly expressing or dealing with something

Examples

To speak indirectly
He addressed the problem indirectly, avoiding any mention of names.
She hinted indirectly that she might be interested in the job.
The message was conveyed indirectly through a mutual friend.
