pleurocarp

noun
a moss with reproductive organs on side branches
7.0 9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈplʊə.rə.kɑːrp/ /; / /ˈplʊr.ə.kɑːrp/ (AmE), /ˈplʊə.rə.kɑːp/ /; / /ˈplʊr.ə.kɑːp/ (BrE)
a moss in which the reproductive structures (archegonium or antheridium) are borne on short side branches rather than on the main stem

Examples

A pleurocarp moss
Identifying whether a moss is an pleurocarp or acrocarp helps in classifying the species.
Most mosses growing horizontally on logs are classified as pleurocarp types.
The delicate branches of a pleurocarp can spread widely across damp ground.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acrocarp - a moss whose reproductive organs are borne at the tips of the main stem
