Stalinism

noun
authoritarian rule typified by Stalin
9.4 7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈstɑːlɪˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈstælɪˌnɪzəm / (BrE)
A political system characterized by centralized authoritarian control, absolute power vested in a single leader, and suppression of opposition, as practiced by Joseph Stalin in the Soviet Union.

Examples

Stalinism political system
Many historians have written extensively about the oppressive nature of Stalinism in the Soviet Union.
Some political theorists argue that Stalinism was distinct from other forms of communist rule.
The film depicts how Stalinism affected the daily lives of ordinary citizens.

Related Terms

  • antonym: democracy - a system of government by the whole population, typically through elected representatives
  • synonym: dictatorship - a form of government where a single person has absolute power
  • synonym: totalitarianism - a political system in which the state holds total authority over society
