power law

noun
relationship where one quantity varies as a power of another
8.2 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààpa ä…ôr l…î / (AmE), / Ààpa ä…ô l…îÀê / (BrE)
a mathematical relationship where the magnitude of a subjective sensation increases as a constant power of the stimulus intensity, commonly used in psychophysics and other scientific fields

Examples

The power law in science
According to the power law in psychophysics, our perception of a stimulus grows nonlinearly with its intensity.
Many phenomena in nature can be described by a power law rather than a simple linear rule.
The distribution of city sizes often follows a power law, with a small number of very large cities and many smaller ones.

Synonyms

  • Stevens' power law
    9.9 0.00/m
    Stevens' power law describes perceived stimulus growth in psychophysics experiments, while power law is a broad mathematical relation across fields.
