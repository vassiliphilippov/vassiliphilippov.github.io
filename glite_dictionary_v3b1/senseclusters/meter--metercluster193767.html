meter

noun
unit of length (SI)
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmiːtɚ /; / ˈmiːtər / (AmE), / ˈmiːtə / (BrE)
the basic unit of length in the International System of Units, equal to approximately 39.37 inches or 1.094 yards

Examples

A meter long
He measured the room and found it was exactly five meters in length.
The 100-meter race is one of the highlights of the Olympic Games.
The table is two meters long and one meter wide.
He ran a distance of one hundred metres in record time.
The Olympic pool must be exactly 50 metres long.
The building stands over thirty metres tall.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: centimeter - a unit of length equal to one hundredth of a meter
  • degree higher: kilometer - a unit of length equal to one thousand meters
  • synonym: meter - alternative spelling, chiefly American English, for the unit of length
