epidural anesthesia

noun
regional anesthesia via epidural injection
4.5 5.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˈdʊrəl ænəsˈθiʒə / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈdjʊərəl anaesˈθiːziə /; / ˌɛpɪˈdjʊərəl ˌanəsˈθiːziə / (BrE)
a type of regional anesthesia produced by injecting an anesthetic into the epidural space of the spinal cord, causing loss of sensation in the lower body, often used during childbirth and certain surgeries

Examples

Epidural anesthesia during childbirth
After receiving epidural anesthesia, the patient felt numbness in her lower body.
Many women choose to have an epidural anesthesia during labor to manage pain.
The doctor explained the risks and benefits of epidural anesthesia before the procedure.
She remained awake and comfortable throughout the procedure thanks to the epidural anaesthesia.
Some women prefer epidural anaesthesia for childbirth to minimize discomfort.
The doctor recommended an epidural anaesthesia to help manage her pain during labor.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: general anaesthesia - anaesthesia causing total unconsciousness
  • antonym: general anesthesia - anesthesia that affects the whole body and induces unconsciousness
  • degree lower: local anaesthesia - anaesthesia affecting a small area of the body
  • synonym: spinal anaesthesia - regional anaesthesia injected into the spinal fluid
  • synonym: spinal anesthesia - anesthesia produced by injection into the spinal canal
