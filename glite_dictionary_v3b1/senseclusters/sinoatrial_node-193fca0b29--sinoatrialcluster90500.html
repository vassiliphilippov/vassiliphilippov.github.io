sinoatrial node

noun
heart's natural pacemaker

a specialized group of cells in the right atrium of the heart that acts as the natural pacemaker, initiating and regulating the heartbeat.

Examples

The sinoatrial node controls heartbeat
An irregular heartbeat can occur if the sinoatrial node is not functioning properly.
Doctors may implant an artificial pacemaker if the sinoatrial node fails.
The sinoatrial node is often called the heart's natural pacemaker.

Synonyms

  • SA node
    5.5 0.02/m
    SA node is the common short name used by doctors and textbooks, while sinoatrial node is the full anatomical term.
  • cardiac pacemaker
    4.4 0.06/m
    cardiac pacemaker is a heart tissue region that sets the rhythm in medical descriptions, while sinoatrial node names that exact spot.
  • pacemaker
    4.2 0.12/m
    pacemaker can mean the cells that start the heartbeat or an implanted device, while sinoatrial node is the natural tissue source.
