waterproof

adjective
impervious to water
1.6 3.48/m
Pronunciation: / Ààw…ît…ôrÀåpruf / (AmE), / Ààw…îÀêt…ôÀåpruÀêf / (BrE)
not allowing water to pass through; impervious to water.

Examples

Waterproof jacket
Make sure your tent is waterproof before camping in the rain.
She bought a waterproof jacket for hiking in wet weather.
The watch is waterproof up to a depth of fifty meters.

Synonyms

  • watertight
    2.6 0.60/m
    watertight seals gaps to stop water entering or leaving a container, while waterproof describes materials that do not let water pass through.
  • impermeable
    6.6 0.59/m
    impermeable blocks liquids or gases from passing through surfaces, while waterproof specifically refers to keeping out water only.
  • impervious
    7.5 2.22/m
    impervious resists liquids, gases, and even influence in a figurative sense, while waterproof only stops water passage.
