periodization

noun
training phases planning
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˌpɪriədaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɪəriədaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the systematic division of a training program into distinct phases to optimize performance and recovery

Examples

Training periodization
Coaches often use periodization to help athletes reach their peak performance at the right time.
Proper periodization can reduce the risk of injury and improve long-term results.
The team's success was partly due to a well-designed periodization plan throughout the season.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overtraining - excessive training without adequate rest
  • synonym: training cycle - a planned sequence of training periods
