pictography

noun
writing with pictures
Pronunciation: / pɪkˈtɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / pɪkˈtɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
a way of communicating or recording information using pictures or symbols instead of words

Examples

Pictography with symbols
Early Native American tribes used pictography to communicate important stories and messages.
The museum displayed ancient tablets covered in pictography from early civilizations.
Researchers study pictography to understand how early humans recorded their history.
Ancient civilizations often used pictography to record important events before written language developed.
The museum exhibit explained how pictography helped early people communicate across language barriers.
Children sometimes use pictography in their stories by drawing pictures instead of writing words.
Archaeologists discovered ancient pictography on the walls of the remote cave.
The pictography found in the desert tells stories of early human life.
Researchers study pictography to learn about the beliefs of prehistoric cultures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: alphabet - a set of letters or symbols representing sounds in a language
  • synonym: hieroglyph - a symbol used in a system of writing, especially in ancient Egypt
  • synonym: hieroglyphics - a writing system using pictorial symbols, especially ancient Egyptian
  • synonym: ideogram - a written character symbolizing an idea or object
  • synonym: petroglyph - a rock carving made by prehistoric people
  • synonym: pictogram - a symbol or drawing representing a word or idea
  • antonym: writing - the activity of marking coherent words on paper or another surface
