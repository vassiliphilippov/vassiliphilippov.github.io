bicentric

adjective
having two centers
Pronunciation: / baɪˈsɛntrɪk / (AmE), / baɪˈsɛntrɪk / (BrE)
Having two centers; characterized by or relating to two points or axes of centrality.

Examples

A bicentric quadrilateral
In geometry, a bicentric quadrilateral can be inscribed in a circle and circumscribed about another circle.
The scientist described the molecule as bicentric, with two distinct nuclei at its core.
Their study proposed that the region's economy is bicentric, revolving around two urban centers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monocentric - having a single center
  • antonym: polycentric - having many centers
