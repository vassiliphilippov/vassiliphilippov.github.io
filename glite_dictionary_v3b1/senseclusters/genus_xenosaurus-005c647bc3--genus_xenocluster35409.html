genus Xenosaurus

noun
genus of Mexican lizards (Xenosauridae)
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌzɛn.əˈsɔːr.əs / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəs ˌzɛn.əˈsɔː.rəs / (BrE)
A genus of slender-bodied Mexican lizards, the only genus in the family Xenosauridae, characterized by having the upper surface covered with tiny granules and tubercles.

The genus Xenosaurus lizards
Herpetologists are conducting new research on the unique scalation of the genus Xenosaurus.
Members of the genus Xenosaurus are known for their secretive habits and distinctive skin texture.
The genus Xenosaurus includes lizards that are endemic to the rocky regions of Mexico.
