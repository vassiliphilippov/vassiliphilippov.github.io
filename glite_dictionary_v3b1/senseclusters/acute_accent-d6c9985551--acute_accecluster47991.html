acute accent

noun
diacritical mark (´) for pronunciation
1.8 6.0
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈkjuːt ˈæk.sənt/ (AmE), /ə.ˈkjuːt ˈæk.sənt/ (BrE)
a diacritical mark (´) placed above a vowel to indicate a particular pronunciation, such as stress or a specific vowel quality

Examples

An acute accent mark
French words like 'résumé' contain two acute accents to indicate the correct vowel sound.
In Spanish, the word 'café' uses an acute accent on the final 'e'.
The acute accent is commonly seen in borrowed words from other European languages.
