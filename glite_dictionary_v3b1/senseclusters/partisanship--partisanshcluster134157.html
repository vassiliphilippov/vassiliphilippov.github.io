partisanship

noun
strong support for a group or cause
6.4 7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːr.tɪ.zənˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.tɪ.zən.ʃɪp / (BrE)
an inclination to strongly support a particular group, cause, or viewpoint, often leading to bias or lack of neutrality

Examples

Strong partisanship shown
His partisanship was clear when he defended his party despite obvious faults.
Media partisanship has increased, making it difficult for viewers to get balanced news.
Researchers warn that excessive partisanship can undermine trust in democratic institutions.
