Virginia waterleaf

noun
a North American perennial herb with edible leaves
Pronunciation: / vərˈdʒɪn.i ˈwɔ.tɚˌlif / (AmE), / vəˈdʒɪn.i ˈwɔː.təˌliːf / (BrE)
A showy perennial herb (Hydrophyllum virginianum) native to eastern North America, with white or pale purple flowers and leaves sometimes used as edible greens.

Examples

Virginia waterleaf plant
In spring, Virginia waterleaf produces clusters of delicate white and purple flowers.
