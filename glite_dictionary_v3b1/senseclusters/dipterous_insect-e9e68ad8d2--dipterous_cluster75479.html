dipterous insect

noun
insect with one pair of wings (Diptera)
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪptərəs ˈɪnˌsɛkt / (AmE), / ˈdɪptərəs ˈɪnsekt / (BrE)
An insect of the order Diptera, typically having a single pair of functional wings and reduced hind wings for balance, with mouthparts adapted for sucking, lapping, or piercing.

Examples

Dipterous insect mosquito
Mosquitoes, as dipterous insects, are notorious for spreading diseases among humans.
Scientists collected several species of dipterous insects during their field expedition.
The housefly is a common dipterous insect found in homes worldwide.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fly - a common example of a dipterous insect
  • synonym: mosquito - another example of a dipterous insect
