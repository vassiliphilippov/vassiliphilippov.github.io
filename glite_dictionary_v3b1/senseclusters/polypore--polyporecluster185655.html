polypore

noun
a woody fungus with pores or tubes
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑː.liˌpɔr / (AmE), / ˈpɒl.ɪˌpɔː / (BrE)
A type of woody fungus whose spore-bearing surface is within tubes or pores, typically having persistent, mature fruiting bodies; especially any fungus of the family Polyporaceae or Boletaceae.

Examples

Woodland polypores
A skilled forager can identify edible species among the many woodland polypores.
Researchers study polypore fungi to better understand their role in decomposing wood.
The fallen log was covered in shelf-like polypores thriving in the moist forest environment.
