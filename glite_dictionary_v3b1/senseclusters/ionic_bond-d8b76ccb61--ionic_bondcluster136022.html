ionic bond

noun
chemical bond formed by electron transfer
7.5
Pronunciation: / aɪˈɑnɪk bɑnd / (AmE), / aɪˈɒnɪk bɒnd / (BrE)
A chemical bond formed when one atom transfers an electron to another atom, resulting in oppositely charged ions that attract each other.

Examples

An ionic bond in salt
In table salt, the sodium and chlorine atoms are held together by an ionic bond.
The strength of an ionic bond depends on the charges of the involved ions.
When an ionic bond forms, one atom donates an electron while another accepts it.

Related Terms

  • antonym: covalent bond - a chemical bond formed by sharing electron pairs between atoms
