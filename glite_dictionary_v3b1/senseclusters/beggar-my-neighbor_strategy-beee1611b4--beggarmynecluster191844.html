beggar-my-neighbor strategy

noun
policy benefiting self at others' expense
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛɡər maɪ ˈneɪbɚ ˈstrætədʒi /; / ˈbɛɡɚ maɪ ˈneɪbɚ ˈstrætədʒi / (AmE), / ˈbɛɡə maɪ ˈneɪbə ˈstrætədʒi / (BrE)
an economic or political policy that benefits one's own country or group at the expense of others, often by making competitive moves that disadvantage others

Examples

A beggar-my-neighbor strategy policy
After the crisis, each country adopted its own beggar-my-neighbor strategy to protect domestic jobs.
Many economists warn that a beggar-my-neighbor strategy can lead to escalating trade wars.
The government's tariffs have been criticized as a classic beggar-my-neighbor strategy.
Many economists warn against the long-term effects of a beggar-my-neighbour strategy.
The government adopted a beggar-my-neighbour strategy during the global financial crisis.
Trade wars are often viewed as a classic example of a beggar-my-neighbour strategy.
