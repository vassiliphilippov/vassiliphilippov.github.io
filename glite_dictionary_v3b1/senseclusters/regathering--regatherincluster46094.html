regathering

noun
regaining composure
4.7
Pronunciation: / riˈɡæðərɪŋ / (AmE), / riˈɡæðərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of regaining composure, focus, or concentration after being disturbed or distracted

Examples

Mental regathering
After the sudden interruption, she needed a moment of regathering before continuing her presentation.
The team took a short break for regathering to refocus their strategy.
He practiced deep breathing as a form of regathering before answering the difficult question.

Related Terms

  • antonym: distraction - a state of being unable to concentrate
  • synonym: recovery - the process of returning to a normal state after a setback
