hyperspace

noun
fictional faster-than-light space
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪpərˌspeɪs / (AmE), / ˈhaɪpəˌspeɪs / (BrE)
an imagined space or dimension in science fiction that allows travel faster than the speed of light

Examples

Spaceship enters hyperspace
In many science fiction stories, spaceships enter hyperspace to travel between distant stars.
The crew prepared the engines to jump into hyperspace and escape the black hole.
Scientists debate whether hyperspace could ever exist outside of fiction.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: space - the physical universe beyond Earth's atmosphere
  • synonym: warp drive - a fictional technology for faster-than-light travel
