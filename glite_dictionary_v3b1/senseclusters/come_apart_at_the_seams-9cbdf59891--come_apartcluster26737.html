come apart at the seams

verb
to break down or fail completely
Pronunciation: / kʌm əˈpɑrt ət ðə simz / (AmE), / kʌm əˈpɑːt ət ðə siːmz / (BrE)
to begin to fail, disintegrate, or break down completely, often after a period of stability

Examples

To come apart at the seams suddenly
After months of pressure, the company began to come apart at the seams.
His old backpack is literally coming apart at the seams and needs to be replaced.
Their marriage seemed strong, but it started to come apart at the seams last year.
