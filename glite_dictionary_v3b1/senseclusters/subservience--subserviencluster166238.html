subservience

noun
usefulness or submissiveness in serving a goal
7.2 0.22/m
Pronunciation: / s…ôbÀàs…ùÀê.vi.…ôns / (AmE), / s…ôbÀàs…úÀê.vi.…ôns / (BrE)
The state or quality of being useful or subordinate to achieving an end or carrying out a plan; submissive or subordinate behavior.

Examples

Subservience to a goal
All his actions were in subservience to the general plan.
She resented the expectation of complete subservience to her manager's wishes.
The committee valued creativity over blind subservience to old procedures.

Synonyms

  • submissiveness
    6.0 0.29/m
    submissiveness is a calm willingness to accept another person's wishes, while subservience is acting to serve someone else's plan or aim.
  • servility
    8.7 0.24/m
    servility is an excessive, cringing desire to please others, while subservience is a submissive stance often used to help reach another's goal.
  • subordination
    7.0 2.27/m
    subordination is the state of being lower in rank or control, while subservience is behavior that submits to serve a plan or person.

Related Terms

  • antonym: independence - the state of being self-reliant, not subject to control by others
  • synonym: subordination - the act of placing in a lower rank or position; subjection
