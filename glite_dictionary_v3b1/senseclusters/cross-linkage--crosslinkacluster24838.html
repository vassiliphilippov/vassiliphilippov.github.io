cross-linkage

noun
a bond linking adjacent molecular chains
Pronunciation: / krɒs ˈlɪŋkɪdʒ / (AmE), / krɒs ˈlɪŋkɪdʒ / (BrE)
a chemical bond that connects two adjacent chains of atoms within a complex molecule, often increasing strength or stability

Examples

Protein cross-linkage
Proteins often rely on cross-linkage for their structural stability.
Scientists studied how cross-linkage affects the flexibility of synthetic polymers.
The strength of rubber can be increased through the process of cross-linkage.
