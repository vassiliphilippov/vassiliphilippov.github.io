Aramaic script

noun
ancient writing system for Aramaic
9.8
Pronunciation: / əˈreɪ.mi.ɪk skrɪpt / (AmE), / ˌær.əˈmeɪ.ɪk skrɪpt / (BrE)
an ancient writing system used since the 9th century BC to write the Aramaic language, from which many other scripts developed

Examples

The Aramaic script alphabet
Many modern scripts, such as Hebrew and Arabic, can trace their origins to the Aramaic script.
Scholars have discovered ancient texts written in Aramaic script in archaeological sites across the region.
The Aramaic script was influential in the development of several Middle Eastern alphabets.
