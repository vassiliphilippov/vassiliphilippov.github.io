Allegheny

proper noun
river in Pennsylvania and New York, USA
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌælɪˈɡeɪni / (AmE), / ˌælɪˈɡeɪni / (BrE)
A river in the northeastern United States that flows from north-central Pennsylvania into New York and then south through Pennsylvania, joining the Monongahela River at Pittsburgh to form the Ohio River.

Examples

The Allegheny River
Early settlers used the Allegheny as a major transportation route.
Several cities along the Allegheny depend on it for their water supply.
The Allegheny River joins the Monongahela River at Pittsburgh to form the Ohio River.
