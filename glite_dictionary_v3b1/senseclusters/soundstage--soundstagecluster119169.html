soundstage

noun
audio spatial effect
7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈsaʊnd.steɪdʒ/ (AmE), /ˈsaʊnd.steɪdʒ/ (BrE)
the perceived spatial arrangement and depth of sounds in audio playback, indicating where instruments and voices seem to be positioned in a listening space

Examples

Wide soundstage
The new speakers create a wide soundstage, making the orchestra feel spread across my living room.
Audiophiles often look for headphones that offer a realistic soundstage for immersive listening.
A well-mixed album can give the impression of a three-dimensional soundstage with clear instrument placement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mono - audio with no spatial separation of sounds
  • synonym: stereo image - the sense of width and placement of sounds in stereo audio
