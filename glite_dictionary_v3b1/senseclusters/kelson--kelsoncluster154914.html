kelson

noun
ship's structural beam
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkel.sən/ (AmE), /ˈkel.sən/ (BrE)
a main longitudinal beam in a ship's hull, running above and parallel to the keel to provide strength

Examples

Ship's kelson
The kelson supports the ship's structure and helps distribute weight evenly along the hull.
During repairs, the workers carefully examined the kelson for any signs of weakness.
A strong kelson is essential for the stability and safety of large wooden vessels.

Related Terms
