eat up

verb
enclose or consume completely
2.8 0.21/m
Pronunciation: / it  åp / (AmE), / iÀêt  åp / (BrE)
To enclose or envelop completely, as if swallowing; to consume entirely in a figurative or literal sense.

Examples

Darkness eats up the light
The darkness seemed to eat up the fading light as night fell.
The fast-growing city eventually ate up all the surrounding farmland.
The thick fog ate up the coastline until nothing was visible.

Synonyms

  • swallow up
    4.6 1.53/m
    swallow up is used to show something is completely hidden or absorbed, while eat up means to consume entirely.
  • swallow
    4.8 8.38/m
    swallow often means to engulf or take something into the body, while eat up stresses fully consuming it.
  • devour
    3.9 3.08/m
    devour suggests eating quickly and with great hunger, while eat up can be neutral and simply means consuming completely.
  • immerse
    4.9 0.39/m
    immerse means to put or cover something completely, often in liquid, while eat up means to consume or use entirely.
