abasia

noun
inability to walk (medical)
9.8
Pronunciation: / əˈbeɪʒə / (AmE), / əˈbeɪzɪə / (BrE)
the inability to walk due to impairment of motor coordination or muscle power

Examples

Abasia due to paralysis
Her abasia was attributed to damage in the spinal cord.
Sometimes, abasia can occur temporarily after traumatic experiences.
The patient was diagnosed with abasia following his neurological examination.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ambulation - the ability to walk from place to place
  • synonym: ataxia - lack of muscle coordination affecting voluntary movements
