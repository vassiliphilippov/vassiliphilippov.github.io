avant-garde

noun
innovative artistic or intellectual group
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɑː.vɑːnˈɡɑrd / (AmE), / ˌæv.ɒ̃ˈɡɑːd / (BrE)
A group of people who develop new and experimental ideas and methods in art, music, or literature, often seen as innovative or ahead of their time.

Examples

The avant-garde in art
A new avant-garde emerged in the city, blending technology with performance art.
Many critics dismissed the avant-garde as too radical, but their influence changed the course of modern music.
The avant-garde challenged traditional forms of painting with their bold, experimental works.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mainstream - the ideas, attitudes, or activities that are regarded as normal or conventional
  • synonym: vanguard - the foremost part of an advancing army or movement; forefront of an action or movement
