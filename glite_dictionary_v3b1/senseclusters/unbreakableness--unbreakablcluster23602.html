unbreakableness

noun
quality of being unbreakable
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈbreɪkəblnəs / (AmE), / ʌnˈbreɪkəblnəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being unbreakable; resistance to breaking under pressure.

Examples

The unbreakableness of glass
Her faith in her friends had a certain unbreakableness about it.
The unbreakableness of the new material impressed the engineers.
The product was marketed for its remarkable unbreakableness and long-lasting reliability.

Related Terms

  • synonym: durability - the ability to withstand wear, pressure, or damage
  • antonym: fragility - the quality of being easily broken or damaged
