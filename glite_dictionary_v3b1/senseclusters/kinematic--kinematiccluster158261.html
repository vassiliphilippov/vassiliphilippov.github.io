kinematic

adjective
motion without forces
Pronunciation: / ˌkɪnəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌkɪnəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
relating to the motion of objects without considering the forces that cause the motion

Examples

Kinematic equations
The kinematic equations help predict the position of a moving car over time.
A kinematic study can describe how a ball rolls down a slope without mentioning gravity.
Scientists used kinematic data to analyze the athlete's running technique.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dynamic - relating to forces and motion
  • synonym: motion - the act or process of moving
