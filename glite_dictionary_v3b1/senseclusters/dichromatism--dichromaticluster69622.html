dichromatism

noun
deficiency of color vision using two wavelengths
Pronunciation: / daɪˈkroʊməˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / daɪˈkrəʊmətɪzəm / (BrE)
a deficiency of color vision in which a person can match any color by mixing only two wavelengths of light, rather than the three required by people with normal vision

Examples

dichromatism color vision
Individuals with dichromatism often have difficulty distinguishing between reds and greens.
Researchers study dichromatism to better understand color perception in humans and animals.
The optometrist diagnosed him with dichromatism after a series of vision tests.

Related Terms

  • synonym: color blindness - inability to distinguish certain colors
  • antonym: trichromatism - normal color vision using three wavelengths
