to write, engrave, or mark words or symbols on a surface, especially in a permanent or lasting way (such as in a book, on a monument, or as a dedication)

Examples

To inscribe a name
He would often inscribe short verses inside the covers of novels he gifted to friends.
She asked him to inscribe the poetry collection to her daughter, adding a few kind words.
The author agreed to inscribe a personal message on the title page of my book.
He will inscribe a personal message inside each book he signs.
She asked the artist to inscribe her name on the painting.
The author agreed to inscribe my copy of his novel.
Before burial, they inscribed the tombstone with a loving epitaph.
The artist inscribed a personal message on the painting's frame.
Visitors often inscribe their names in the guestbook at the museum.

Synonyms

  • engrave
    5.2 3.16/m
    engrave means cutting words or designs into a hard surface for lasting effect, while inscribe means writing or marking words permanently.
  • grave
    7.7 0.04/m
    grave is a less common verb for cutting letters into a hard surface, while inscribe is a general word for lasting written marks.
  • etch
    5.5 1.75/m
    etch means cutting or using acid to make fine lines on metal or glass, while inscribe means marking words permanently.
  • letter
    7.7 0.84/m
    letter as a verb means to mark an object with letters by hand or tool, while inscribe means add words in a lasting way.
  • carve
    2.1 19/m
    carve means cutting or shaping material to make or decorate objects, while inscribe focuses on writing or marking words permanently.
  • scratch
    1.1 7.86/m
    scratch means to scrape or cut a surface in a rough, often shallow way, while inscribe means write or engrave words permanently.
