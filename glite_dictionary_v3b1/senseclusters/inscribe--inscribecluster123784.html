inscribe

verb
write a brief note in a book
6.3 4.3
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈskraɪb/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈskraɪb/ (BrE)
to write, engrave, or mark words or symbols on a surface, especially in a permanent or lasting way (such as in a book, on a monument, or as a dedication)

Examples

To inscribe a name
He would often inscribe short verses inside the covers of novels he gifted to friends.
She asked him to inscribe the poetry collection to her daughter, adding a few kind words.
The author agreed to inscribe a personal message on the title page of my book.
He will inscribe a personal message inside each book he signs.
She asked the artist to inscribe her name on the painting.
The author agreed to inscribe my copy of his novel.
Before burial, they inscribed the tombstone with a loving epitaph.
The artist inscribed a personal message on the painting's frame.
Visitors often inscribe their names in the guestbook at the museum.

Related Terms

  • synonym: autograph - to write one's signature, especially for a fan or in a book
  • degree higher: dedicate - to address or inscribe a work formally to someone
  • synonym: engrave - to cut or carve text or designs into a surface
  • antonym: erase - to remove or rub out written or engraved words
  • synonym: etch - to cut or carve a design or words into a surface
  • synonym: sign - to write one's signature on something
