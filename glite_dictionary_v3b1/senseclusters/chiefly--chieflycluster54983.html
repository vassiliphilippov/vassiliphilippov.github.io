chiefly

adverb
mainly; for the most part
3.0 4.37/m
Pronunciation: / Ààt Éifli / (AmE), / Ààt ÉiÀêfli / (BrE)
mainly; for the most part; primarily.

Examples

Is chiefly about
His interests are chiefly in literature and history.
The festival is chiefly celebrated in rural areas.
Their research focuses chiefly on environmental changes.

Synonyms

  • mainly
    1.0 35/m
    mainly is used to say the main thing or reason in plain speech, while chiefly sounds slightly more formal.
  • primarily
    2.4 51/m
    primarily appears in formal or written contexts to show the main point, while chiefly is more neutral.
  • principally
    4.0 4.88/m
    principally sounds formal and fits academic or official writing, while chiefly is more common in everyday speech.
  • predominantly
    3.7 11/m
    predominantly suggests a large degree or majority, often in statistics or reports, while chiefly just marks main importance.
  • mostly
    1.0 106/m
    mostly is casual and used for general situations or habits, while chiefly has a slightly stronger sense of main cause.
  • largely
    2.4 65/m
    largely focuses on extent or amount rather than reason, while chiefly points to the main reason or part.
  • for the most part
    3.2 20/m
    for the most part is a common phrase meaning usually or mainly in many cases, while chiefly stresses the primary cause.
  • in the main
    3.7 1.11/m
    in the main is slightly old-fashioned or formal and means generally, while chiefly gives a clearer idea of the main part.
  • by and large
    3.4 5.55/m
    by and large is a casual idiom meaning generally or on the whole, while chiefly pinpoints the primary reason or part.
