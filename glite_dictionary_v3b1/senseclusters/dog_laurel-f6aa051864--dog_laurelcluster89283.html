dog laurel

noun
evergreen shrub of southeastern U.S.
Pronunciation: / dɔɡ ˈlɔrəl / (AmE), / dɒɡ ˈlɒrəl / (BrE)
a fast-growing evergreen shrub of the southeastern United States, with arching, interlaced branches and clusters of white flowers.

Examples

The dog laurel shrub
Gardeners in the southeastern states often use dog laurel as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.
In early spring, the dog laurel blooms with delicate white flowers along its branches.
The dense growth of dog laurel made it difficult to walk through the forest path.
