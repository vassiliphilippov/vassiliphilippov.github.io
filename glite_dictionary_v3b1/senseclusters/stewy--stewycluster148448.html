stewy

adjective
thick and moist
Pronunciation: / ˈstuːi / (AmE), / ˈstjuːi / (BrE)
having a thick, soft, or moist texture like that of a stew

Examples

Stewy vegetables
After hours of slow cooking, the vegetables turned soft and stewy.
The chef preferred a stewy sauce to coat the pasta evenly.
The rice pudding was too stewy for my taste, almost like a thick soup.

Related Terms

  • antonym: soupy - having a thin, liquid consistency like soup
  • synonym: thick - having a dense or heavy consistency
