stewy

adjective
thick and moist
5.1 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàstuÀêi / (AmE), / ÀàstjuÀêi / (BrE)
having a thick, soft, or moist texture like that of a stew

Examples

Stewy vegetables
After hours of slow cooking, the vegetables turned soft and stewy.
The chef preferred a stewy sauce to coat the pasta evenly.
The rice pudding was too stewy for my taste, almost like a thick soup.

Synonyms

  • mushy
    2.0 0.98/m
    mushy is soft and pulpy, often from overcooking, while stewy means thick and moist like a hearty stew.
  • soupy
    3.9 0.26/m
    soupy is more liquid and runny like thin soup, while stewy is thicker and more chunky.
  • pappy
    7.6 0.00/m
    pappy is porridge-like and loose, often lacking firmness, while stewy is thick, moist, and has larger pieces.
  • squishy
    3.0 1.48/m
    squishy is soft and easily squeezed like a sponge, while stewy refers to a thick, moist food texture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: thick - having a dense or heavy consistency
