short-grass

noun
grass species tolerant of drought, stays short
6.2
Pronunciation: / ʃɔrt ɡræs / (AmE), / ʃɔːt ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a type of grass that remains short and is able to withstand drought, commonly found on dry upland plains, especially east of the Rocky Mountains

Examples

Native short-grass
Many prairie ecosystems rely on native short-grass to prevent soil erosion.
The cattle grazed on the region's abundant short-grass during the dry summer months.
Unlike tall prairie plants, short-grass thrives in areas with little rainfall.
