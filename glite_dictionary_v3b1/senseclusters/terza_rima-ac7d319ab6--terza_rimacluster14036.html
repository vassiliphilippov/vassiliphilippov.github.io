terza rima

noun
verse form with interlocking rhyme
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛrtsə ˈrimə / (AmE), / ˌtɜːtsə ˈriːmə / (BrE)
a verse form consisting of tercets with an interlocking rhyme scheme: aba bcb cdc, and so on.

Examples

Terza rima rhyme scheme
Dante's Divine Comedy is famous for its use of terza rima throughout the epic.
The poet experimented with terza rima to give his elegy a flowing rhythm.
Writing in terza rima requires skillful handling of both rhyme and structure.
