fianchetto

noun
chess bishop development
9.9
Pronunciation: /fɪ.ən.ˈke.toʊ/ /; / /fɪ.ən.ˈtʃe.toʊ/ (AmE), /fɪ.ən.ˈke.təʊ/ /; / /fɪ.ən.ˈtʃe.təʊ/ (BrE)
a chess opening in which a bishop is developed to the second rank of the knight's file to control the long diagonal

Examples

A fianchetto setup
He used the fianchetto to strengthen his king's defense early in the match.
Many grandmasters prefer a fianchetto setup to control the long diagonal.
The fianchetto is a common strategy in hypermodern chess openings.
