nondenial

noun
failure to deny
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑn.dɪˈnaɪ.əl / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.dɪˈnaɪ.əl / (BrE)
a statement or response that does not explicitly deny something, often interpreted as an implicit admission

Examples

A nondenial statement
The CEO's nondenial during the interview led many to believe the rumors were true.
Reporters interpreted his nondenial as a subtle confirmation of the allegations.
Her nondenial in response to the question raised even more suspicion among the audience.
The politician's nondenial made the public wonder if the rumors were true.
Her nondenial of the accusation only increased suspicion among her colleagues.
Reporters noticed the CEO's nondenial when asked about the company's future plans.

Related Terms

  • synonym: admission - an act of acknowledging something as true
  • antonym: denial - a statement that something is not true
  • synonym: evasion - an act of avoiding giving a direct answer
