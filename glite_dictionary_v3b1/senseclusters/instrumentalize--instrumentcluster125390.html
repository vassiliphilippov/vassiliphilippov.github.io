instrumentalize

verb
to use as a tool
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnstrəˈmɛntəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌɪnstrəˈmɛntəlaɪz / (BrE)
to treat something or someone as a tool or means to achieve a particular goal, often disregarding their intrinsic value

Examples

To instrumentalize public opinion
Some politicians try to instrumentalize public fears to gain more support for their policies.
The documentary explores how art can be instrumentalized for political purposes.
Activists warned against instrumentalizing the tragedy to push unrelated agendas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exploit - to use someone or something unfairly for your own advantage
  • antonym: respect - to treat someone or something with honor and consideration
  • synonym: utilize - to make practical use of something
