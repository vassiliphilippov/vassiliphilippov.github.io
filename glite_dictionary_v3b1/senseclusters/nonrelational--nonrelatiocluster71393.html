nonrelational

adjective
not involving relationships
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑn.rɪˈleɪʃənəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒn.rɪˈleɪʃənəl / (BrE)
not based on or involving relationships between entities, especially in the context of databases

Examples

A nonrelational database
A nonrelational database stores information without organizing it into related tables.
Many modern applications use nonrelational systems to handle large amounts of unstructured data.
Choosing a nonrelational approach can offer more flexibility for certain types of data storage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: relational - based on or involving relationships between entities, especially in databases
