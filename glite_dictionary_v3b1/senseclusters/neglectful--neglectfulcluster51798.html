neglectful

adjective
failing to give proper care or attention
3.7
Pronunciation: / nɪˈɡlɛktfəl /; / nəˈɡlɛktfəl / (AmE), / nɪˈɡlɛktfəl / (BrE)
not giving enough care or attention to someone or something; failing to do what you should

Examples

Neglectful parent
He became neglectful of his health after starting his new job.
It is neglectful to leave your dog outside in extreme weather.
The teacher was criticized for being neglectful of her students’ emotional needs.
If you are neglectful of your health, minor problems can quickly become serious.
Poor conditions in the shelter were blamed on a neglectful management team.
The teacher noticed that John was being neglectful of his homework assignments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: careless - not giving sufficient attention or thought to avoiding harm or errors
  • synonym: remiss - lacking care or attention to duty
