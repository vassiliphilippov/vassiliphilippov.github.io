frivolous

adjective
lacking seriousness; superficial
Pronunciation: / ˈfrɪvələs / (AmE), / ˈfrɪvələs / (BrE)
Not having any serious purpose or value; lacking seriousness in attitude or behavior.

Examples

A frivolous comment
His frivolous comments disrupted the serious discussion in the meeting.
It would be frivolous to file a lawsuit over such a minor issue.
She was criticized for her frivolous spending habits during tough economic times.

Related Terms

  • antonym: serious - characterized by deep thought or earnestness
  • synonym: trivial - of little value or importance
