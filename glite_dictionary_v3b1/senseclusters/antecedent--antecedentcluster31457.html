antecedent

noun
word or phrase referred to by an anaphor
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.tɪˈsiː.dənt / (AmE), / ˌæn.tɪˈsiː.dənt / (BrE)
A word, phrase, or clause to which an anaphoric pronoun refers in a sentence.

Examples

Pronoun antecedent
In grammar, it is important to identify the antecedent of each pronoun clearly.
In the phrase 'the dog that barked', 'the dog' is the antecedent of 'that'.
The antecedent of 'she' in the sentence 'Sarah arrived because she was late' is 'Sarah'.

Related Terms

  • antonym: consequent - the part of a sentence that follows 'then' in a conditional statement; opposite of antecedent in logic
  • synonym: referent - the thing that a word or phrase denotes or stands for
