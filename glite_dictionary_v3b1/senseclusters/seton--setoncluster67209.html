seton

noun
surgical drainage thread
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːtən / (AmE), / ˈsiːtən / (BrE)
a piece of thread or similar material passed under the skin to promote drainage, especially in surgical procedures

Examples

A seton thread
The doctor placed a seton to help drain the infected area.
After the surgery, the patient needed a seton to prevent further abscesses.
A seton is often used in the treatment of anal fistulas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drain - a tube or device used to remove fluid from a wound or body cavity
