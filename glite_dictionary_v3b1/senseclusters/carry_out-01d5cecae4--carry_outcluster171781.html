carry out

verb
to perform or complete (a task or action)
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkæri aʊt /; / ˌkæri ˈaʊt / (AmE), / ˈkæri aʊt /; / ˌkæri ˈaʊt / (BrE)
to do, perform, or complete a task, instruction, duty, order, or plan

Examples

To carry out instructions
Please carry out these instructions exactly as written.
Scientists carried out experiments to test the new drug's effectiveness.
The company will carry out a full investigation into the incident.
He was asked to carry out an important mission for the company.
Please carry out the instructions carefully to ensure safety.
The team will carry out the research over the next three months.
He failed to carry out his promise to help with the move.
She was chosen to carry out the experiment in the laboratory.
The team will carry out the project according to the manager's instructions.
