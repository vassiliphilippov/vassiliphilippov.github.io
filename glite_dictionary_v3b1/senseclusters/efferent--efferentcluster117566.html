efferent

noun
a nerve carrying impulses away from the CNS
Pronunciation: / ˈefəˌrənt /; / ˈɛfərənt / (AmE), / ˈefər(ə)nt /; / ˈɛfərənt / (BrE)
a nerve or blood vessel that carries signals or substances away from the central nervous system or a central organ

Examples

The doctor explained that the efferent nerves transmit signals from the brain to the muscles.
Unlike afferent nerves, efferent fibers carry information away from the spinal cord.
The doctor explained that efferents transmit signals from the spinal cord to the muscles.
Damage to the efferents can result in loss of motor function in the affected area.
Researchers studied how efferents influence the movement of substances away from the kidney.
