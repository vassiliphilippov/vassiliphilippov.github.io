upset

verb
cause to overturn (verb)
4.6 3.0
Pronunciation: / ʌpˈsɛt / (AmE), / ʌpˈsɛt / (BrE)
to disturb the normal state, balance, or order of something; to overturn or cause disorder

Examples

To upset the balance
High winds can easily upset a small sailboat if you're not careful.
Please don't upset the glass of water on the table.
She accidentally upset the basket, spilling fruit everywhere.
A careless movement can easily upset the boat, so sit still.
His mistake upset the team's carefully planned strategy.
The sudden announcement upset the balance of power in the company.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disturb - interrupt the normal function or arrangement of something
  • synonym: overturn - to turn something over so that it is upside down; to capsize
  • antonym: stabilize - make or become stable or steady
  • synonym: unsettle - make unstable or cause uncertainty
