upshift

verb
to change to higher gear
4.6
Pronunciation: /ʌp.ˈʃɪft/ /; / /əp.ˈʃɪft/ (AmE), /ʌp.ˈʃɪft/ /; / /əp.ˈʃɪft/ (BrE)
to change to a higher gear in a vehicle or machine to increase speed or efficiency

Examples

To upshift the car
When the car reached 3,000 RPM, she decided to upshift for better fuel efficiency.
Drivers should upshift smoothly to avoid damaging the transmission.
He learned to upshift quickly while driving on the highway.
When the engine reached higher RPMs, she decided to upshift for better fuel efficiency.
Drivers should upshift smoothly to avoid jerking the car during acceleration.
He practiced how to upshift quickly while merging onto the highway.
As the car accelerated onto the highway, the driver decided to upshift for smoother performance.
You should upshift when the engine reaches a higher RPM to avoid overworking it.
Learning when to upshift is an important skill for driving a manual transmission vehicle.
