trisodium

noun
compound with three sodium atoms
Pronunciation: / traɪˈsoʊdiəm / (AmE), / traɪˈsəʊdiəm / (BrE)
a chemical compound or salt that contains three sodium atoms or ions in its structure

Examples

Trisodium compound
The cleaning agent contains trisodium phosphate, which is effective for removing grease.
Scientists studied the properties of trisodium citrate in food preservation.
After the reaction, a white trisodium salt was left as a residue in the beaker.
