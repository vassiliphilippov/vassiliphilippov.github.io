malrotation

noun
abnormal organ rotation
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˌmælroʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmalrəʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
an abnormal rotation or twisting of a bone, limb, or organ, often leading to improper alignment or function

Examples

Intestinal malrotation
The surgeon identified malrotation of the intestine during the operation.
Early diagnosis of malrotation in infants can prevent serious complications.
Doctors use imaging tests to detect malrotation in patients with abdominal pain.
The surgeon corrected the malrotation of the patient's leg to restore normal walking.
Congenital malrotation of the intestine can cause serious digestive problems in infants.
X-rays revealed a malrotation of the forearm bone after the accident.
