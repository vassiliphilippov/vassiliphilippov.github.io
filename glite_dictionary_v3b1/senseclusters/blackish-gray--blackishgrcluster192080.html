blackish-gray

adjective
a dark shade of gray
Pronunciation: / ˈblækɪʃ ˌɡreɪ /; / ˈblækɪʃ ˈɡreɪ / (AmE), / ˈblækɪʃ ˌɡreɪ /; / ˈblakɪʃ ˈɡreɪ / (BrE)
Dark gray in color, resembling the shade between black and gray.

Examples

A blackish-gray coat
She wore a blackish-gray coat that matched the stormy sky.
The artist mixed paints to achieve the perfect blackish-gray shade for the shadows.
The old building's blackish-gray stones gave it a somber appearance.
He wore a blackish-grey suit to the formal event, blending classic and modern styles.
The old photograph had faded to a blackish-grey, losing much of its original color.
The storm clouds turned a blackish-grey as the sun disappeared behind them.
