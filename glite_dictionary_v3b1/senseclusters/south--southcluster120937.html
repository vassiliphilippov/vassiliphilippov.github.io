south

noun
the Global South (developing countries)
Pronunciation: / sa8ab8 / (AmE), / sa8ab8 / (BrE)
(in global politics and development) the countries and regions, mainly in Africa, Latin America, and parts of Asia and Oceania, that are commonly described as less economically developed and that are often contrasted with the Global North.

Examples

The Global South
Scholars argue that trade policies often disadvantage the economies of the South in international negotiations.
Many development programs aim to reduce poverty across countries in the South by improving education and health.
Diplomats from the South met to coordinate a joint position on climate finance at the conference.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Global North - the group of more economically developed countries often contrasted with the Global South
  • synonym: developing country - a country with lower levels of industrialization and per-capita income; often included within the Global South
