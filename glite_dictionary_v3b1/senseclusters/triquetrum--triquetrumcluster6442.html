triquetrum

noun
wrist bone
Pronunciation: / traɪˈkwiːtrəm / (AmE), / trɪˈkwiːtrəm / (BrE)
a small, three-sided bone in the wrist, also known as the triangular bone

Examples

Wrist triquetrum bone
The X-ray showed a fracture in the triquetrum after the patient fell on his hand.
In anatomy class, we learned that the triquetrum is one of the eight carpal bones.
Pain on the outside of the wrist can sometimes be caused by an injury to the triquetrum.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lunate - a crescent-shaped bone in the wrist
  • synonym: scaphoid - a boat-shaped bone in the wrist
