suffer fools gladly

verb
to tolerate fools or incompetence
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌfər fulz ˈɡædli / (AmE), / ˈsʌfə fuːlz ˈɡædli / (BrE)
to have patience or tolerance for people regarded as foolish or incompetent

Examples

To suffer fools gladly
If you can't suffer fools gladly, this job may test your patience.
She is known to suffer fools gladly, always remaining calm with difficult clients.
The new manager does not suffer fools gladly and expects competence from everyone.

Related Terms

  • synonym: endure - put up with something or someone difficult
  • antonym: lose patience - stop being patient or tolerant
  • synonym: tolerate - accept or endure someone or something unpleasant
