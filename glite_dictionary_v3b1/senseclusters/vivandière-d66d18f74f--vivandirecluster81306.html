vivandière

noun
female army supplier
9.9
Pronunciation: / viˌvɑnˈdjɛr /; / ˌviːvɑnˈdjɛr / (AmE), / viˌvɒnˈdjɛə /; / ˌviːvɒnˈdjɛə / (BrE)
a woman who accompanied soldiers (especially in the 19th century) to provide food, drink, and supplies during military campaigns

Examples

Army vivandière
During the war, the vivandière became a familiar and welcome figure among the soldiers.
A vivandière often marched with the regiment, carrying baskets of food and water.

During the campaign, the vivandière ensured the soldiers always had fresh water and bread.
She was affectionately called the team's vivandière for always bringing snacks to meetings.
In historical paintings, a vivandière is often shown marching alongside the troops.
