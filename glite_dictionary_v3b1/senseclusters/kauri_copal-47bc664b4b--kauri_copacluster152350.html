kauri copal

noun
fossil resin from New Zealand kauri trees
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊri ˈkoʊpəl / (AmE), / ˈkaʊri ˈkəʊpəl / (BrE)
a fossil resin obtained from kauri trees of New Zealand, used in varnishes and linoleum

Examples

kauri copal resin
Artisans value kauri copal for its excellent properties in making varnish.
Much of the world's kauri copal comes from ancient deposits in New Zealand.
The museum exhibited a large piece of kauri copal dating back thousands of years.
