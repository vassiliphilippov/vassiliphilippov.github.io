Plimsoll line

noun
maximum safe loading mark on a ship's hull
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈplɪmsəl laɪn / (AmE), / ˈplɪmsəl laɪn / (BrE)
a mark on the hull of a ship showing the maximum safe waterline when loaded

Examples

The Plimsoll line mark
If the water rises above the Plimsoll line, the ship is overloaded and in danger.
Inspectors checked the Plimsoll line before the vessel left port.
The Plimsoll line on the ship indicated that it could carry more cargo safely.
