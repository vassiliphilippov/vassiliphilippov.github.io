Melchizedek

noun
biblical priest-king
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈmel.kɪ.zə.dek/ (AmE), /ˈmel.kɪ.zə.dek/ (BrE)
a biblical figure described as a king and priest, often associated with an eternal or non-hereditary priesthood in Christian theology

Examples

King Melchizedek blesses Abraham
The story of Melchizedek appears in the Book of Genesis as he blesses Abraham.
Many theologians discuss the significance of Melchizedek in relation to Jesus's priesthood.
In the letter to the Hebrews, Jesus is compared to Melchizedek as a priest forever.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Levitical priest - a priest from the tribe of Levi in the Hebrew Bible
  • synonym: priest - a person authorized to perform religious ceremonies
