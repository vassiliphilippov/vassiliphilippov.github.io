flatwork

noun
laundry items ironed mechanically
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈflætˌwɜrk / (AmE), / ˈflætˌwɜːk / (BrE)
Laundry, such as sheets, tablecloths, and towels, that can be ironed or processed mechanically while flat.

Examples

Clean flatwork laundry
All the flatwork, including sheets and pillowcases, must be finished before the guests arrive.
Hotel laundries use large machines to iron their flatwork efficiently.
The commercial laundry separates flatwork from personal clothing to process them differently.
