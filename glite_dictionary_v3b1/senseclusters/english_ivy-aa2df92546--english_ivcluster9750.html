English ivy

noun
climbing evergreen vine with lobed leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈaɪvi / (AmE), / ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ ˈaɪvi / (BrE)
a climbing evergreen vine with lobed leaves and black berry-like fruits, native to Europe and western Asia, often grown as an ornamental plant

Examples

English ivy plant
Gardeners often plant English ivy as ground cover because it spreads quickly.
She trimmed the English ivy to keep it from taking over the fence.
The old stone building was covered in thick English ivy from top to bottom.
