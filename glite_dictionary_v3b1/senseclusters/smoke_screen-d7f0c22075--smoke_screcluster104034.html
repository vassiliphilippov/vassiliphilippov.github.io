smoke screen

noun
action used to conceal or confuse
2.8 4.2
Pronunciation: /smoʊk skriːn/ /; / /smoʊk skrɪn/ (AmE), /sməʊk skriːn/ /; / /sməʊk skrɪn/ (BrE)
An action or statement used to conceal the truth, confuse others, or obscure the real purpose or situation.

Examples

A clever smoke screen
Her apology turned out to be a smoke screen for her actual intentions.
Politicians often use a smoke screen of complicated language to avoid answering difficult questions.
The elaborate investigation was just a smoke screen to distract the media from the real issue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cover - something that hides or protects the real thing
  • synonym: disguise - to give a different appearance in order to conceal identity
  • synonym: pretext - a false reason given to hide the real one
