stretch

noun
continuous period of time
1.0
Pronunciation: / strɛtʃ / (AmE), / strɛtʃ / (BrE)
an uninterrupted period of time spent doing something, especially something difficult or undesirable

Examples

A long stretch at work
After a long stretch at his desk, he decided to take a walk.
He served a two-year stretch in prison before being released early.
There was a particularly boring stretch in the middle of the play.
