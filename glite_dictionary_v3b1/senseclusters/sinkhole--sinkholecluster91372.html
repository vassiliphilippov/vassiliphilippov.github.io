sinkhole

noun
a natural depression formed by ground collapse
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪŋkˌhoʊl / (AmE), / ˈsɪŋkˌhəʊl / (BrE)
a depression or hole in the ground caused by the collapse of a surface layer, often connecting to an underground passage, especially in regions of limestone

Examples

A giant sinkhole
After the heavy rain, a massive sinkhole appeared in the middle of the road, swallowing a parked car.
Florida is known for its frequent sinkholes due to the area's limestone bedrock.
Scientists studied the newly formed sinkhole to understand the collapse of the underground cavern.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: cave - a large underground chamber, often formed by natural processes
  • synonym: doline - a similar term for a natural depression in karst regions
