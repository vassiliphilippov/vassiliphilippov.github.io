cricothyroid

noun
laryngeal muscle
9.9
Pronunciation: /krɪ.kə.ˈθaɪ.rɔɪd/ (AmE), /krɪ.kə.ˈθaɪ.rɔɪd/ (BrE)
a muscle located between the cricoid and thyroid cartilages that stretches and tenses the vocal cords to change voice pitch

Examples

Cricothyroid muscle tension
The surgeon carefully identified the cricothyroid before performing the procedure on the patient's neck.
Damage to the cricothyroid can affect a person's ability to change the pitch of their voice.
During singing lessons, exercises may help strengthen the cricothyroid for better vocal control.

Related Terms

  • synonym: larynx - the organ in the throat involved in breathing, producing sound, and protecting the trachea
  • synonym: vocal cord - the folds of tissue in the larynx that produce sound
