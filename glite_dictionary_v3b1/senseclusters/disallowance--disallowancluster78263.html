disallowance

noun
rejected claim or expense
7.2
Pronunciation: /dɪ.sə.ˈlaʊ.əns/ (AmE), /dɪ.sə.ˈlaʊ.əns/ (BrE)
the act of officially refusing to allow or accept something, especially in a legal, governmental, or financial context

Examples

A disallowance of expenses
The tax authority issued a disallowance for several expenses claimed by the company.
After reviewing the application, the auditor made a disallowance of certain travel costs.
A disallowance can result in a higher tax bill if deductions are not accepted.
The disallowance of the expense report meant the company would not reimburse him.
After a lengthy review, the court announced the disallowance of the evidence.
The team's protest ended in the disallowance of the goal by the referee.

After a thorough review, the minister announced the disallowance of the controversial bill.
A disallowance can prevent a local law from taking effect if it conflicts with national policy.
