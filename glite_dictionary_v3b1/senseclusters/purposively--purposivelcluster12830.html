purposively

adverb
with intention
6.5 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…úÀêrp…ôs…™vli / (AmE), / Ààp…úÀêp…ôs…™vli / (BrE)
in a way that is done with intention or for a specific purpose

Examples

To purposively choose
The researchers purposively chose participants who met specific criteria for the experiment.
She purposively avoided mentioning the topic during the meeting.
The decorations were purposively arranged to create a welcoming atmosphere.

Synonyms

  • purposely
    3.5 4.57/m
    purposely describes doing something on purpose in everyday speech, while purposively sounds rarer and stresses a specific intended aim.
  • intentionally
    3.1 11/m
    intentionally means doing something on purpose to cause a result, while purposively focuses on acting for a particular planned purpose.
  • purposefully
    3.9 3.25/m
    purposefully often shows firm determination or clear aim in action, while purposively emphasizes acting with a specific intended purpose.
  • deliberately
    3.7 22/m
    deliberately suggests careful, planned action done slowly or with thought, while purposively simply means acting with a clear intended purpose.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accidentally - in a way that is not planned or intended
  • synonym: deliberately - in a careful and planned manner
  • synonym: intentionally - in a deliberate or planned way
