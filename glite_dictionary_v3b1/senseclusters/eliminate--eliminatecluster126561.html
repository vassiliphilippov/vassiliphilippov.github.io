eliminate

verb
remove from a contest or competition
3.5
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɪməˌneɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪmɪneɪt / (BrE)
to remove someone or something from a competition, contest, or consideration so they are no longer involved

Examples

To eliminate a team
France eliminated Brazil in the quarter-finals last year.
If you lose this round, you will be eliminated from the competition.
Two teams will be eliminated from the tournament after tonight's matches.
After examining all the evidence, the detective decided to eliminate two suspects from the list.
If you answer incorrectly, you will be eliminated from the competition.
We can eliminate this option since it doesn't fit the requirements.

Related Terms

  • antonym: advance - move on to the next round (the opposite in a competitive context)
  • synonym: disqualify - officially remove from a competition on rule-based grounds
  • synonym: exclude - to deliberately not include something
  • antonym: include - to make someone or something part of a group or list
