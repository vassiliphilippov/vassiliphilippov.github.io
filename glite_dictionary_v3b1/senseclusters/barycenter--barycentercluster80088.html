noun

(in mathematics and statistics) A generalization of the center of mass to probability distributions; a barycenter is an average or representative distribution (or point) that minimizes total distance to a given set of distributions.

Examples

Statistical barycenter
Researchers computed the barycenter of several distributions to summarize the data.
In the lecture, she explained how the Wasserstein barycenter averages different probability measures.
To find a representative model, the team estimated the barycenter across all sampled distributions.
