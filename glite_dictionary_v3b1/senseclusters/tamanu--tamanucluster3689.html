tamanu

noun
wood from tamanu tree
Pronunciation: / təˈmɑːnuː / (AmE), / təˈmɑːnuː / (BrE)
the hard, durable wood of the tamanu tree, used for carving, construction, or as fuel

Examples

Tamanu wood carving
The craftsman chose tamanu for the statue because of its strength and smooth texture.
Builders in the region often use tamanu for making sturdy beams and posts.
A traditional canoe was carved from a single piece of tamanu wood.

Related Terms

  • antonym: balsa - a lightweight wood often used for carving and model making
  • synonym: mahogany - a hard, reddish-brown wood used for furniture and construction
