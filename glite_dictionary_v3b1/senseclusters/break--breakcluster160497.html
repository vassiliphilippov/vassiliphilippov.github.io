break

verb
emerge from water (of animals)
Pronunciation: / breɪk / (AmE), / breɪk / (BrE)
to move away or escape suddenly, especially from confinement or control; to change direction suddenly; to separate or scatter (of clouds, a crowd, etc.); to lose cohesion (of a group or formation)

Examples

To break free
A pod of dolphins suddenly broke near the boat, thrilling the passengers.
The seal breaks frequently for air before diving again.
We watched as a large fish broke the surface, sending ripples through the calm water.
At the last second, the car broke right to avoid the obstacle.
The horse suddenly broke from the pack, catching everyone by surprise.
The runner managed to break to the left, dodging the defender easily.
After several hours of rain, the clouds finally broke and the sun appeared.
As dawn approached, the mist broke, revealing the mountains in the distance.
The crowd broke and people went their separate ways after the announcement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disperse - to break up and scatter in different directions
  • synonym: escape - to get away from confinement or control
  • synonym: flee - to run away from a place or situation of danger
  • antonym: gather - to come together; to assemble
  • antonym: hold - to maintain position or formation, especially in a military sense
  • synonym: swerve - to turn sharply aside from a straight course
