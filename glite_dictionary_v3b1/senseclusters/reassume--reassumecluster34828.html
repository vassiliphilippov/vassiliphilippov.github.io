reassume

verb
to resume again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːəˈsuːm / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈsjuːm / (BrE)
to take back or start again a role, position, responsibility, or activity that you had before

Examples

The committee will reassume discussions on the proposal after the break.

After his recovery, he was able to reassume his role as team leader.
The president will reassume office after the temporary absence.
She plans to reassume her responsibilities once the project restarts.
After the investigation ended, she reassumed her position as team leader.
The president will reassume office once the medical leave is over.
He reassumed his responsibilities at work after returning from vacation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abandon - to stop doing something before it is finished
  • antonym: relinquish - to give up or let go of a position or responsibility
  • synonym: resume - to start something again after a pause
