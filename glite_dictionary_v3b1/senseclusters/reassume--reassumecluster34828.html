reassume

verb
to resume again
6.6 0.1/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀê…ôÀàsuÀêm / (AmE), / ÀåriÀê…ôÀàsjuÀêm / (BrE)
to take back or start again a role, position, responsibility, or activity that you had before

Examples

After the interruption, she reassumed her duties as team leader without hesitation.
The committee will reassume discussions on the proposal after the break.
He reassumed his position at the company following a year-long sabbatical.
After his recovery, he was able to reassume his role as team leader.
The president will reassume office after the temporary absence.
She plans to reassume her responsibilities once the project restarts.

Synonyms

  • reinstate
    5.8 6/m
    reinstate is used when an authority restores someone to a previous post, while reassume means the person takes it back themselves.
  • reoccupy
    5.6 0.2/m
    reoccupy often means returning to a place or job physically, while reassume focuses on starting a previous role or responsibility.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abandon - to stop doing something before it is finished
  • antonym: relinquish - to give up or let go of a position or responsibility
  • synonym: resume - to start something again after a pause
