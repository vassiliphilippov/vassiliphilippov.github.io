overdetermination

noun
multiple causes
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərdɪˌtɜrmɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvədɪˌtɜːmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a situation in which an effect or event is caused by more than one sufficient factor or explanation

Examples

Overdetermination of causes
In some scientific theories, overdetermination explains why an outcome can have several independent causes.
The historian argued that the revolution was a result of overdetermination rather than a single event.
Psychoanalytic theory often discusses overdetermination in relation to the causes of dreams.

Related Terms

  • antonym: determinism - the doctrine that all events are determined by causes external to the will
  • synonym: redundancy - the state of being not strictly necessary due to duplication
