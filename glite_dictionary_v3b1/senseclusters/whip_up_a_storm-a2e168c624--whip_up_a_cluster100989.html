whip up a storm

verb
prepare food quickly or cause excitement/commotion
Pronunciation: / wɪp ʌp ə stɔrm / (AmE), / wɪp ʌp ə stɔːm / (BrE)
to prepare something, especially food, quickly and skillfully, or to cause excitement, commotion, or strong feeling in a situation

Examples

To whip up a storm
He likes to whip up a storm at parties by starting lively debates.
The politician managed to whip up a storm with his controversial comments at the rally.
Whenever unexpected guests arrive, she can whip up a storm and have dinner ready in no time.
