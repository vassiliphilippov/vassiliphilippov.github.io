canescent

adjective
greyish white or whitish-gray
Pronunciation: / kəˈnɛsənt / (AmE), / kəˈnɛsənt / (BrE)
having a greyish white color or being covered with fine, whitish or grayish hairs (especially of plants, animals, or surfaces)

Examples

A canescent beard
His beard had become decidedly canescent over the years.
Some plants have canescent leaves that look silvery in sunlight.
The canescent moon illuminated the misty night.
Several desert shrubs display canescent stems to reflect sunlight and reduce water loss.
The leaves of the sage plant are canescent, giving them a soft, silvery appearance.
Under magnification, the insect's back appeared canescent due to its fine, pale hairs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hoary - grayish white, especially with age
