anvil

noun
metalworking block for shaping metals
Pronunciation: / ˈæn.vəl / (AmE), / ˈæn.vəl / (BrE)
a heavy block of iron or steel with a flat top, used by blacksmiths and metalworkers for shaping hot metals by hammering

Examples

Blacksmith's anvil
During the demonstration, sparks flew from the anvil with each strike of the hammer.
Modern artists sometimes use an anvil to craft metal sculptures by hand.
The blacksmith placed the heated horseshoe on the anvil and began to hammer it into shape.
