rearm

verb
to arm again, especially with weapons
Pronunciation: / riˈɑrm / (AmE), / riˈɑːm / (BrE)
to equip with weapons or armaments again after they have been removed or reduced

Examples

To rearm the army
After the treaty was dissolved, the country began to rearm itself.
The government decided to rearm its military after a decade of peace.
They must rearm quickly to defend against any further threats.
After the treaty expired, several nations began to rearm discreetly.
If tensions rise, we may be forced to rearm sooner than planned.
The country chose to rearm its military forces after years of disarmament.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disarm - to take weapons away from
