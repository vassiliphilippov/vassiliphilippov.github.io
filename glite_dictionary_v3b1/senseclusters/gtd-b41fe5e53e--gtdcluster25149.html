GTD

noun
order valid until date
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒiː tiː ˈdiː / (AmE), / ˌdʒiː tiː ˈdiː / (BrE)
a type of financial order that remains active until a specified expiry date unless it is filled or cancelled

Examples

GTD order
The investor placed a GTD to ensure her order would remain open until the end of the month.
If you want your trade to expire on a specific date, use a GTD instruction.
He set a GTD so the order would automatically cancel if not filled by Friday.

Related Terms

  • synonym: GTC - a financial order that remains active until it is filled or cancelled
  • antonym: day order - a financial order that expires if not executed by the end of the trading day
