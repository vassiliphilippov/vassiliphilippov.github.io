heritable

adjective
able to be inherited

(of a characteristic, property, or legal right) able to be inherited by succeeding generations or heirs.

Examples

A heritable disease
Certain diseases are highly heritable and often run in families.
Eye color is a heritable trait determined by multiple genes.
The estate is heritable and will pass to the oldest child upon the owner's death.

Synonyms

  • inheritable
    5.8 0.29/m
    inheritable means something can be passed to later generations by law or genes, while heritable stresses legal succession to heirs.
  • hereditary
    5.4 1.30/m
    hereditary describes traits or titles passed from parents, often by blood or custom, while heritable highlights legal inheritance and rights.
  • descendable
    7.4 0.00/m
    descendable applies to property or titles that can be passed down to descendants, while heritable focuses on broader inheritance of rights.
