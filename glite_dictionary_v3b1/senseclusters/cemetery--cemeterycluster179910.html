An area of land, usually outside a town or city, set aside for the burial of the dead.

Examples

A cemetery area
A new cemetery was established on the outskirts of the growing city.
The old cemetery was surrounded by large oak trees and ancient headstones.
They visited the cemetery every year to place flowers on their grandfather's grave.

Synonyms

  • burial ground
    3.9 0.80/m
    burial ground is land used for many graves in communities, while cemetery is an area set aside for burying the dead.
  • burying ground
    6.1 0.04/m
    burying ground is an older term for land used for graves, while cemetery is the common modern word for such burial areas.
  • graveyard
    1.8 5.46/m
    graveyard is a burial area often next to a church, while cemetery usually refers to a larger, planned burial ground.
  • burial site
    3.7 0.59/m
    burial site is any specific place used for a grave or tomb, while cemetery means a whole area set aside for burials.
  • resting place
    3.9 1.06/m
    resting place is a gentle word for where someone is buried or remembered, while cemetery names the actual burial area of land.
