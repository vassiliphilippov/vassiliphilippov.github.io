part-singing

noun
singing in multiple vocal parts
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑrtˌsɪŋɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɑːtˌsɪŋɪŋ / (BrE)
singing in which three or more independent vocal parts are sung together, often forming harmonies

Examples

Part-singing in choirs
Part-singing is essential in traditional choral music, adding richness and depth to the sound.
Many folk songs are arranged specifically for part-singing to encourage group participation.
The choir excelled at part-singing, performing complex pieces with four distinct voices.

Related Terms

  • synonym: harmony - the combination of simultaneously sounded musical notes to produce chords
  • antonym: unison - music performed with all parts singing the same notes or melody
