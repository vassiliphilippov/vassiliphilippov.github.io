enslaver

noun
one who dominates
8.1
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈsleɪvər / (AmE), / ɪnˈsleɪvə / (BrE)
a person, thing, or force that dominates, controls, or severely restricts people's freedom, often in a figurative sense.

Examples

Addiction as an enslaver
For many, addiction became an enslaver that was difficult to escape.
Some view technology as an enslaver when it takes over daily life.
Throughout history, debt has often acted as an enslaver of entire communities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: liberator - someone who frees others from control or oppression
  • synonym: oppressor - someone who treats people in a cruel or unfair way
