Al-Fatiha

noun
first chapter of the Quran, central Islamic prayer
Pronunciation: / æl fəˈtiːhə / (AmE), / æl fəˈtiːhə / (BrE)
the first chapter of the Quran, recited as a central prayer in Islam during the five daily prayers and on special occasions

Examples

Al-Fatiha chapter
Many children memorize Al-Fatiha as their first chapter of the Quran.
Muslims recite Al-Fatiha at the beginning of every prayer.
The imam began the service with a recitation of Al-Fatiha.
