bucktail

noun
New York political faction
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌkˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈbʌkˌteɪl / (BrE)
a member or supporter of the Bucktails, a 19th-century political faction in New York State

Examples

A bucktail politician
In the early 1800s, he became a prominent bucktail advocating for political reform in New York.
The bucktail movement played a significant role in shaping state politics during that era.
Many historians study the influence of the bucktail faction on American democracy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Democrat - a member of the Democratic Party
  • antonym: Federalist - a member of the Federalist Party, a rival political group
