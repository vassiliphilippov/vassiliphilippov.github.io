fanega

noun
Spanish dry measure
9.9
Pronunciation: / fəˈneɪɡə / (AmE), / fəˈneɪɡə / (BrE)
a Spanish unit of dry measure, especially for grain, with a value that varies by region

Examples

A fanega of grain
The farmer measured his harvest in fanegas before taking it to the market.
In some regions, a fanega can hold more than fifty kilograms of wheat.
The price of corn was set per fanega in the old trading system.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bushel - a unit of dry measure used in the US and UK, mainly for grain
