lateral epicondyle

noun
bony projection on the outer end of a bone
Pronunciation: / ˈlæt̬.ər.əl ˌɛp.ɪˈkɒn.daɪl / (AmE), / ˈlæt.ər.əl ˌɛp.ɪˈkɒn.daɪl / (BrE)
a bony protrusion on the outer side of the end of a long bone, especially the femur or humerus, serving as an attachment point for muscles and ligaments

Examples

The lateral epicondyle bone
Athletes sometimes experience soreness near the lateral epicondyle after intensive arm workouts.
Pain on the outside of the elbow often indicates inflammation of the lateral epicondyle.
The surgeon identified the lateral epicondyle before attaching the ligament graft.

Related Terms

  • antonym: medial epicondyle - bony protuberance on the inner side of the end of the bone
