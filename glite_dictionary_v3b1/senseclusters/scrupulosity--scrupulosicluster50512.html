scrupulosity

noun
moral carefulness
Pronunciation: / ˌskruːpjəˈlɑːsəti / (AmE), / ˌskruːpjʊˈlɒsɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being extremely careful to do what is morally right and to avoid wrongdoing or mistakes

Examples

Financial scrupulosity
Her scrupulosity meant she double-checked every figure before submitting the financial report.
The judge's scrupulosity in following the law earned him great respect.
He approached every task with scrupulosity, ensuring nothing was overlooked.

Related Terms

  • antonym: carelessness - lack of attention or thought to avoiding harm or mistakes
  • synonym: conscientiousness - the quality of wishing to do one's work or duty well and thoroughly
