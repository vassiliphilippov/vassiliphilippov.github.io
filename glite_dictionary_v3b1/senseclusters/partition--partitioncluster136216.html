partition

verb
divide into sections
2.4
Pronunciation: / pɑrˈtɪʃən / (AmE), / pɑːˈtɪʃən / (BrE)
to divide or separate something into parts or sections

Examples

To partition a room
A curtain was used to partition the room for privacy.
The engineer decided to partition the open office into smaller work areas.
They partitioned the land equally among the heirs after the will was read.
After the war, the country was partitioned into several independent states.
She used a curtain to partition the studio apartment for some privacy.
The company decided to partition the large office space into smaller rooms.

  • antonym: unite - to join together or make into a whole
