control board

noun
panel with controls for operating electrical devices
2.8 3.3
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈtroʊl bɔːrd/ (AmE), /kən.ˈtrəʊl bɔːd/ (BrE)
a flat, insulated electrical panel fitted with switches, dials, and meters for operating and monitoring other electrical devices

Examples

The control board panel
A warning light on the control board alerted the engineer to a potential problem.
All the system's functions can be adjusted from the control board near the entrance.
The technician inspected the control board to find the source of the electrical fault.
