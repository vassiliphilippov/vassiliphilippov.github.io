avera

noun
feudal labor service
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɑːv.ˈe.rə/ (AmE), /ɑːv.ˈe.ə/ (BrE)
an old English term for labor or service that a tenant was required to perform for a lord

Examples

Medieval avera service
In medieval England, peasants were often required to perform avera for their local lord.
The manor records listed the amount of avera each tenant owed during the harvest season.
Failure to complete one's avera could result in fines or other penalties from the lord.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corvée - unpaid labor required by a government or lord
  • synonym: serfdom - the status of peasants under feudalism, often involving forced labor
