whale

noun
very large asset holder (finance/crypto)
8.2
Pronunciation: /weɪl/ /; / /hweɪl/ (AmE), /weɪl/ (BrE)
(in finance and cryptocurrency) an individual or organization that owns a very large amount of a tradable asset and can influence market prices by buying or selling.

Examples

A Bitcoin whale
The Bitcoin whale moved the market by placing a huge sell order overnight.
Traders watched nervously as several whales accumulated the token before the price spike.
If you identify a whale buying, you might see liquidity quickly disappear from the order book.

Related Terms

  • synonym: big player - an individual or firm with large influence or market share
  • antonym: retail investor - an individual investor who buys and sells securities or tokens for personal accounts, typically much smaller than a whale
