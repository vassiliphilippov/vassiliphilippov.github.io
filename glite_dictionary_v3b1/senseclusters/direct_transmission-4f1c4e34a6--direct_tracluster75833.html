direct transmission

noun
spread of infection by direct contact
Pronunciation: / dəˈrɛkt trænzˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛkt trænzˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
the spread of an infectious agent from one person to another by direct physical contact or transfer of infectious droplets

Examples

Direct transmission of disease
Healthcare workers use gloves to prevent direct transmission of infectious diseases.
Many viruses are spread through direct transmission, such as by coughing or sneezing on someone nearby.
The risk of direct transmission increases when people share drinks or utensils.

Related Terms

  • antonym: indirect transmission - the spread of infection through an intermediate object or vector
