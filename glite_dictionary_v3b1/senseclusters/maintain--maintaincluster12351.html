maintain

verb
to have and exercise power or authority
2.2 2.6
Pronunciation: /meɪn.ˈteɪn/ /; / /mə.ˈteɪn/ (AmE), /meɪn.ˈteɪn/ /; / /mə.ˈteɪn/ (BrE)
to cause something to continue or to keep to a state, activity, or schedule (such as authority, focus, or a diet)

Examples

To maintain control
Despite criticism, he managed to maintain authority within the organization.
She struggled to maintain her influence after the leadership change.
The president continued to maintain control over the country's foreign policy.
He tried to maintain focus during the long and complicated meeting.
It is important to maintain a steady rhythm when running long distances.
We must maintain accurate records for all our transactions.
It is important to maintain a healthy diet throughout your life.
She works hard to maintain her reputation for honesty.
The company struggled to maintain its position as a market leader.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abandon - to give up completely or stop maintaining
  • synonym: keep - to continue doing something or to stick to a schedule or pattern
  • antonym: lose track - to stop keeping count or awareness of something
  • synonym: preserve - to keep something in its original state or in good condition
  • synonym: sustain - to keep something going over time
  • synonym: wield - to have and exercise (power, authority, etc.)
