Synonyms

  • keep
    1.0 452/m
    keep is used to continue or hold something in the same state in everyday speech, while maintain sounds slightly more formal.
  • sustain
    4.0 11/m
    sustain makes something last over a long time, while maintain can mean keeping a state or following a plan.
  • stick to
    3.0 16/m
    stick to means keep doing a plan or rule without change, while maintain can be more formal and general.
  • continue
    1.0 42/m
    continue means to keep happening or stay in the same state, while maintain often implies active care or control.
  • hold
    1.4 45/m
    hold often means keep a position or object, sometimes physically, while maintain focuses on causing something to continue.
  • retain
    3.4 26/m
    retain means keep possession or a quality, often passively, while maintain means actively cause something to continue.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abandon - to give up completely or stop maintaining
  • synonym: keep - to continue doing something or to stick to a schedule or pattern
  • antonym: lose track - to stop keeping count or awareness of something
  • synonym: preserve - to keep something in its original state or in good condition
  • synonym: sustain - to keep something going over time
  • synonym: wield - to have and exercise (power, authority, etc.)
