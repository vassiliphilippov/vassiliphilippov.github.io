die back

verb
when plant parts die from the tips inward
8.4
Pronunciation: / daɪ bæk / (AmE), / daɪ bæk / (BrE)
(of a plant) to suffer damage or disease that causes the shoots or leaves to die starting at the tips and progressing inward, often seasonally or due to adverse conditions.

Examples

Plants die back after frost
After the first frost, the leaves and stems of the plant die back to the ground.
Gardeners often cut perennials back when they start to die back in late autumn.
If conditions are too dry, some of the shoots might die back during the summer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blight - to cause to wither or decay, especially in plants
  • synonym: wither - to become dry and shriveled, especially as a result of loss of moisture
