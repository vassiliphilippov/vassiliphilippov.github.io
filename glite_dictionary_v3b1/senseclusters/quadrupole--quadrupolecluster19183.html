quadrupole

noun
ion-filtering device
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɑːdrəˌpoʊl / (AmE), / ˈkwɒdrʊˌpəʊl / (BrE)
a device with four parallel rods or electrodes used in scientific instruments, especially to filter ions by mass-to-charge ratio

Examples

Quadrupole mass filter
The scientist adjusted the quadrupole to improve the accuracy of the mass spectrometry results.
A quadrupole is essential for separating ions by their mass-to-charge ratio in advanced laboratories.
Technicians replaced the old quadrupole to ensure more precise ion filtering in the experiment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: filter - a device that removes unwanted parts from something
  • synonym: mass spectrometer - an instrument used to measure the masses of particles
