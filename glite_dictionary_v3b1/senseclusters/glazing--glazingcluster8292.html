glazing

noun
ceramic glass coating
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɡleɪzɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡleɪzɪŋ / (BrE)
a glass-like coating fused onto pottery or ceramics by firing, used to decorate or protect the surface

Examples

Ceramic glazing
The ancient vase was covered in a blue glazing that shimmered under the lights.
Artists often experiment with different types of glazing to achieve unique finishes on their pottery.
The glazing not only adds color but also protects the ceramic from moisture and stains.

Related Terms

  • synonym: enamel - a hard, glossy coating applied to metal, glass, or ceramics
  • antonym: unglazed - not having a glass-like coating on ceramics
