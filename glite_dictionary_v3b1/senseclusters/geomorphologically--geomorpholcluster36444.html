geomorphologically

adverb
relating to landforms
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒiːoʊˌmɔːrfəˈlɑːdʒɪkli / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːəʊˌmɔːfəˈlɒdʒɪkli / (BrE)
in a manner that relates to the study of landforms and the processes that shape them

Examples

The land is geomorphologically shaped
The landscape is geomorphologically complex, featuring plateaus, valleys, and river systems.
Scientists analyzed the area geomorphologically to understand how the mountains were formed.
The island differs geomorphologically from the mainland due to its volcanic origin.
