Danian

adjective
earliest Paleocene age
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈdeɪ.nɪ.ən/ /; / /ˈdæ.nɪ.ən/ (AmE), /ˈdeɪ.nɪ.ən/ /; / /ˈdæ.nɪ.ən/ (BrE)
relating to the earliest age of the Paleocene epoch in geology, following the Cretaceous period

Examples

Danian strata
The Danian strata contain some of the earliest mammal fossils after the dinosaur extinction.
Scientists studied the Danian rocks to understand climate changes at the start of the Paleocene.
The boundary between the Cretaceous and Danian layers marks a major shift in Earth's history.
