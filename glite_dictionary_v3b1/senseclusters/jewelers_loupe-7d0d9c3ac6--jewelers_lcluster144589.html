jeweler's loupe

noun
magnifying device for jewelers and watchmakers
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒuːələrz luːp / (AmE), / ˈdʒuːələz luːp / (BrE)
a small magnifying glass, usually mounted in an eyepiece, used by jewelers and watchmakers for close inspection of gemstones and intricate parts

Examples

A jeweler's loupe glass
He always brings his jeweler's loupe to antique fairs to examine gems and watches closely.
The jeweler's loupe revealed the tiny flaw in the diamond that was invisible to the naked eye.
Using her jeweler's loupe, she inspected the intricate mechanisms inside the vintage pocket watch.

Related Terms
