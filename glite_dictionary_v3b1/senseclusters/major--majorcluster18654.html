major

adjective
very serious or dangerous
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪdʒɚ /; / ˈmeɪ.dʒɚ / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdʒə /; / ˈmeɪ.dʒə / (BrE)
very important, serious, large, or significant in size, amount, or effect

Examples

Major problem
Losing power for days is a major inconvenience for many families.
She suffered a major injury during the game, requiring immediate surgery.
The city prepared for a major disaster after the sudden warning.
His first major project at the company was a huge success.
She was given a major award for her contributions to science.
Traffic was delayed due to a major accident on the highway.
Her research made a major contribution to the field of genetics.
The company announced a major expansion of its services this year.
There was a major delay due to unexpected weather conditions.
