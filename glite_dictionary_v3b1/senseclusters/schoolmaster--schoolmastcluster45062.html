schoolmaster

verb
to teach strictly
Pronunciation: / ˈskuːlˌmæstər / (AmE), / ˈskuːlˌmɑːstə / (BrE)
to teach, instruct, or discipline someone in a strict or authoritative manner, like a traditional schoolmaster

Examples

To schoolmaster a child
He tried to schoolmaster his younger brother by setting strict study hours every day.
The coach would often schoolmaster the team, insisting on perfect discipline during practice.
She did not appreciate being schoolmastered by her colleague over minor mistakes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: discipline - to train or control by enforcing rules or order
  • antonym: encourage - to give support or confidence to someone
  • synonym: instruct - to teach or give directions to someone
