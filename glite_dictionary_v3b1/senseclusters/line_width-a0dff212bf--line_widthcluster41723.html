line width

noun
text line measurement
Pronunciation: / laɪn wɪdθ / (AmE), / laɪn wɪdθ / (BrE)
the horizontal measurement of a line of text, determining how many words fit on one line

Examples

Line width of text
The line width was adjusted to make the text easier to read on the screen.
If the line width is too wide, readers may lose their place while reading.
Designers often experiment with line width to improve the appearance of printed materials.

Related Terms

  • synonym: column width - the horizontal measurement of a column of text
  • antonym: margin - the blank space around the text on a page
