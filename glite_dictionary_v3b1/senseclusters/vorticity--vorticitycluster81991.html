vorticity

noun
fluid rotation measure
Pronunciation: / vɔrˈtɪsəti / (AmE), / vɔːˈtɪsəti / (BrE)
a measure of the rotation or swirling motion of a fluid, such as air or water, at a specific point

Examples

Vorticity in fluid
Meteorologists analyze vorticity patterns to predict the development of storms and cyclones.
High vorticity in the atmosphere can indicate areas where severe weather may form.
The scientist measured the vorticity of the water to study how eddies are created.

Related Terms

  • synonym: circulation - the movement of fluid in a circular path
  • synonym: turbulence - irregular or chaotic fluid motion
