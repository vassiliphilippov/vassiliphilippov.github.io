asleep

adjective
numb; lacking sensation in a limb
2.9 0.85/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàslip / (AmE), / …ôÀàsliÀêp / (BrE)
(of a limb or body part) numb or lacking sensation, usually as a result of temporary interference with blood flow or nerve signals.

Examples

Hand asleep
After sitting awkwardly, my hand fell asleep and I couldn't feel my fingers.
If your foot is asleep, wait a few moments for the tingling to go away.
She shook her leg because it had gone asleep during the long meeting.

Synonyms

  • numb
    3.1 3.40/m
    numb means lacking physical feeling often from cold or injury, while asleep describes a limb temporarily without sensation from pressure.
  • benumbed
    8.0 0.00/m
    benumbed describes being without feeling from cold, shock, or strong emotion, while asleep describes a limb temporarily without sensation.
  • deadened
    6.0 0.00/m
    deadened often means made less sensitive or without feeling, while asleep refers to a limb temporarily without sensation.
