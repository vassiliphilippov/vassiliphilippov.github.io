badlands

noun
eroded, barren rocky landform
6.0 8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈbædˌlændz / (AmE), / ˈbæd.lændz / (BrE)
areas of barren land with heavily eroded soil and little or no vegetation, typically characterized by steep slopes and intricate patterns of ridges and gullies.

Examples

The badlands area
Farming is nearly impossible in the badlands because of the poor soil and severe erosion.
Many tourists visit the Badlands National Park in South Dakota to experience these unique geological formations.
The rugged badlands stretched as far as the eye could see, with no sign of human habitation.
