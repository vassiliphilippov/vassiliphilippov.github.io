chain reactor

noun
nuclear reactor using a chain reaction
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃeɪn riˈæktər / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪn riˈæktə / (BrE)
a nuclear reactor in which a controlled chain reaction produces energy, typically by nuclear fission

Examples

A chain reactor design
A properly designed chain reactor can generate electricity for thousands of homes.
Scientists work to ensure the safety of every chain reactor in operation.
The Chernobyl disaster was caused by a failure in a chain reactor system.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nuclear reactor - a device used to initiate and control a sustained nuclear chain reaction
