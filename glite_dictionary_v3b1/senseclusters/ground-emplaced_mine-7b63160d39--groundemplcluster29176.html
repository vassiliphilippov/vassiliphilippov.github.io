ground-emplaced mine

noun
explosive device hidden underground
5.6
Pronunciation: / ɡraʊnd ɪmˈpleɪst maɪn / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd ɪmˈpleɪst maɪn / (BrE)
an explosive device placed underground that detonates when pressure is applied, such as when stepped on or driven over

Examples

A ground-emplaced mine device
Before advancing, the engineers scanned for ground-emplaced mines to ensure the path was clear.
Farmers are still at risk from ground-emplaced mines left over from the war decades ago.
Many casualties during the conflict were caused by ground-emplaced mines hidden along major roads.

Related Terms

  • synonym: land mine - an explosive device placed on or just under the ground, designed to explode when triggered by pressure or proximity
