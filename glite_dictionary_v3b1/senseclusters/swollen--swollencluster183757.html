swollen

adjective
abnormally enlarged or distended
3.2 7.26/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆswoÊŠlÉ™n / (AmE), / ËˆswÉ™ÊŠlÉ™n / (BrE)
(of a part of the body or object) abnormally enlarged or distended, especially as a result of an accumulation of fluid or gas.

Examples

Swollen ankle
He touched his swollen eyelid gingerly, worried it might be infected.
Her ankle was swollen after she twisted it playing basketball.
The river became swollen from the heavy rains, threatening to overflow its banks.

Synonyms

    distended means abnormally stretched or expanded, often by fluid or gas, while swollen describes general abnormal enlargement.
  • edematous
    9.9 0.07/m
    edematous is used in medicine for tissue filled with excess fluid, while swollen is the general, everyday term.
  • tumescent
    9.8 0.01/m
    tumescent describes body tissue that grows larger from fluid or gas, while swollen is the general word for abnormal enlargement.
  • intumescent
    9.9 0.00/m
    intumescent means tissues puff up and become enlarged due to fluid, while swollen is the general, nontechnical description.
  • tumid
    9.9 0.01/m
    tumid describes an abnormally enlarged body part in formal or medical language, while swollen is common everyday speech.
  • turgid
    8.6 0.10/m
    turgid often means puffed and firm and appears in formal or literary use, while swollen is common everyday language.
  • bloated
    5.0 3.89/m
    bloated describes a rounded, uncomfortable swelling often from gas or eating, while swollen is a neutral medical description.
  • bulging
    3.6 0.13/m
    bulging means pushing outward in a rounded shape from pressure, while swollen covers general enlargement from fluid or gas.
  • puffy
    2.2 2.65/m
    puffy looks soft, rounded, and often temporary, while swollen implies a more marked abnormal enlargement from fluid or gas.
