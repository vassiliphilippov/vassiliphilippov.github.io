generativity

noun
guiding next generation
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒɛnərəˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛnərəˈtɪvəti / (BrE)
a concern for guiding and supporting the next generation, often shown through parenting, mentoring, or community involvement

Examples

Generativity in parenting
Many adults express generativity by volunteering in their communities and helping young people succeed.
Parenting is often seen as a key way to demonstrate generativity during middle adulthood.
Through generativity, individuals find purpose by contributing to the well-being of future generations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mentorship - the act of advising or training someone, especially a younger person
  • antonym: stagnation - a lack of development, growth, or progress, especially in midlife
