white-tailed jackrabbit

noun
large North American hare with a white tail
Pronunciation: / ˌwaɪt ˌteɪld ˈdʒækˌræbɪt / (AmE), / ˌwaɪt ˌteɪld ˈdʒækˌræbɪt / (BrE)
a large hare of the northern plains and western mountains of the United States, with brownish-grey fur in summer and pale grey fur in winter, and a tail that is almost always entirely white

Examples

The white-tailed jackrabbit in summer
During summer, the white-tailed jackrabbit is more brownish-grey than in winter.
The winter coat of the white-tailed jackrabbit helps it blend into snowy landscapes.
We spotted a white-tailed jackrabbit darting through the fields at sunset.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hare - a fast-running mammal similar to a rabbit but larger
