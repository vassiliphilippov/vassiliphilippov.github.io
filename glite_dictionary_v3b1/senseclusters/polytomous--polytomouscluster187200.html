polytomous

adjective
with multiple branches
Pronunciation: / pəˈlɪtəməs / (AmE), / pəˈlɪtəməs / (BrE)
having more than two immediate descendant lineages at a branching point, especially in biology or phylogenetics

Examples

Polytomous tree diagram
A polytomous tree can indicate uncertainty about the order of evolutionary events.
The diagram showed a polytomous split where several species emerged from a single ancestor.
Researchers debated whether the node was truly polytomous or if more data could resolve the relationships.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dichotomous - having exactly two branches at each division
