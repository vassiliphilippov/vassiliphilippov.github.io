reinflate

verb
to raise prices again
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪnˈfleɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈfleɪt / (BrE)
to cause prices, asset values, or the general level of prices to rise again, especially after a period of decline or deflation

Examples

To reinflate the economy
The government introduced new policies to reinflate the economy after a long recession.
Central banks sometimes reinflate markets by lowering interest rates and increasing liquidity.
Efforts to reinflate asset values can help restore confidence among investors.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deflate - to reduce prices or values, often after a period of inflation
  • synonym: inflate - to increase prices or values, often rapidly
