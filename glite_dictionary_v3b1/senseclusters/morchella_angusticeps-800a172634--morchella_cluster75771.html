Morchella angusticeps

noun
species of morel mushroom
Pronunciation: / mɔrˈkɛl.ə ænˈɡʌs.təˌsɛps / (AmE), / mɔːˈkɛl.ə ænˈɡʌs.tɪˌsɛps / (BrE)
A species of morel mushroom whose pitted cap is closely attached to the stalk, with the cap being gray when young and turning black with age.

Examples

Morchella angusticeps mushroom
As it matures, the cap of Morchella angusticeps darkens from gray to nearly black.
Foragers prize the Morchella angusticeps for its rich flavor and unique appearance.
The elusive Morchella angusticeps can be found in hardwood forests in the spring.

Related Terms
