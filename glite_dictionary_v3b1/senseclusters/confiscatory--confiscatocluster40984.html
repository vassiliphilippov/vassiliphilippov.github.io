confiscatory

adjective
excessively high charges
Pronunciation: / kənˈfɪskəˌtɔri / (AmE), / kənˈfɪskətəri / (BrE)
relating to taking away property, money, or assets by authority, especially through law or very high taxes or fees

Examples

Confiscatory taxes
The government introduced confiscatory taxes that left many small businesses struggling to survive.
Many citizens protested the confiscatory property fees imposed by the new law.
Some experts argue that confiscatory regulations discourage investment and economic growth.
The government introduced a confiscatory policy to address illegal land ownership.
Many citizens protested against the confiscatory measures that targeted their savings.
A confiscatory tax rate can discourage investment and economic growth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: forfeiture - the loss or giving up of something as a penalty
  • antonym: moderate - not excessive or extreme in amount
  • antonym: protective - intended to keep something safe from harm or loss
  • synonym: punitive - intended as a punishment or extremely severe
  • synonym: seizure - the act of taking possession of something by force or legal right
