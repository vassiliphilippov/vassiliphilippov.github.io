European spider crab

noun
large spider crab native to Europe
Pronunciation: / jʊˈroʊpiən ˈspaɪdər kræb / (AmE), / jʊˈrəʊpiən ˈspaɪdə kræb / (BrE)
a large species of spider crab (Maja squinado) native to the coastal waters of Europe, especially the northeast Atlantic and Mediterranean Sea

Examples

The European spider crab
Fishermen often find the European spider crab in the rocky shallows along the Atlantic coast.
Several aquariums display the European spider crab to educate visitors about marine life in European waters.
The European spider crab is distinguished by its long legs and spiny shell.
