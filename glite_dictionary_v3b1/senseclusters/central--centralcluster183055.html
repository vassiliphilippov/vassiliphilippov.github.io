Synonyms

    cardinal is a formal word for something of highest importance in rules or belief, while central is a more general everyday term of importance.
  • key
    2.0 60/m
    key is a common, practical word for something crucial to success, while central is a neutral term meaning important or at the core.
  • essential
    1.9 30/m
    essential means absolutely necessary for something to work, while central means important or at the core without that strict necessity.
  • fundamental
    3.5 20/m
    fundamental points to the basic parts that form something, while central focuses on being important or located at the core.
  • primary
    2.5 50/m
    primary means first in order or main in importance, while central means important at the core but not always the first or main cause.
  • paramount
    6.7 2/m
    paramount means more important than anything else in rank or value, while central means important or at the centre of attention.
  • pivotal

  • critical

  • main

    main is the chief or most used part of something, while central emphasizes being at the core or of key importance.
  • chief
    3.2 60/m

  • capital
