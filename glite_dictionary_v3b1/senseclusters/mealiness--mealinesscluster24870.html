mealiness

noun
dry, crumbly texture
Pronunciation: / ˈmiːli.nəs / (AmE), / ˈmiːlɪnɪs / (BrE)
the quality of being dry, crumbly, and resembling meal or powder, especially in food texture

Examples

Mealiness of potatoes
The mealiness of the baked potato made it perfect for mashing with butter.
Some people dislike the mealiness found in overripe apples.
The chef checked the mealiness of the bread to ensure it was not too dry.

Related Terms

  • synonym: graininess - the quality of having a texture like small grains
  • antonym: smoothness - the quality of having an even, soft texture
