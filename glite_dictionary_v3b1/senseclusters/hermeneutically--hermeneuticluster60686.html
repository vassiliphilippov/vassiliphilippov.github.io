hermeneutically

adverb
in an interpretive way
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhɜːrməˈnuːtɪkli /; / ˌhɜrməˈnuːtɪkli / (AmE), / ˌhɜːməˈnjuːtɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that involves interpreting texts or meaning, especially using hermeneutic methods

Examples

To hermeneutically interpret texts
The scholar examined the ancient manuscript hermeneutically to understand its symbolic meanings.
Biblical passages are often discussed hermeneutically in theology classes.
She approached the poem hermeneutically, seeking to uncover layers of interpretation.
The philosopher analyzed the ancient text hermeneutically to uncover its deeper significance.
When reading poetry, she often thinks hermeneutically about the author's intentions.
The court interpreted the law hermeneutically, considering its historical and cultural context.
