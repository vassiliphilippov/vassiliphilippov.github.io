wind sock

noun
conical cloth tube for showing wind direction
9.5
Pronunciation: / wɪnd sɑk / (AmE), / wɪnd sɒk / (BrE)
A conical tube of cloth, usually mounted on a pole, that indicates wind direction and relative speed, commonly found at airports.

Examples

A wind sock on pole
A brightly colored wind sock made it easy for everyone to see the wind direction from afar.
Pilots check the wind sock before landing to determine the best approach direction.
The airport's wind sock was pointing straight east, indicating a strong westerly wind.

Related Terms

  • synonym: weather vane - rotating pointer that shows wind direction
  • synonym: windsock - alternative spelling for wind sock
