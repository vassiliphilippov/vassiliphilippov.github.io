preemptive bid

noun
early aggressive offer in auctions or M&A
Pronunciation: / pric85bmpt6av b6ad / (AmE), / pric85bmpt6av b6ad / (BrE)
an early, aggressive offer made in an auction, sale, or corporate acquisition intended to discourage or block competing bidders.

Examples

A preemptive bid offer
The company made a bold preemptive bid to acquire the smaller firm before any rival could react.
Investors criticized the preemptive bid as an attempt to block competition in the upcoming auction.
Faced with a rival offer, they launched preemptive bids to increase the cost and deter other suitors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blocking bid - an offer intended to prevent other bidders from succeeding
  • synonym: hostile bid - an offer made directly to shareholders or the market, often opposed by the target's management
