slide

noun
musical gliding between notes
4.1
Pronunciation: / slaɪd / (AmE), / slaɪd / (BrE)
(music) A rapid movement from one note to another, sliding up or down the musical scale.

Examples

Guitar slide
The guitarist used a smooth slide to connect the chords seamlessly.
The violinist added an expressive slide between the notes in the melody.
You can hear a distinct slide in her singing as she moves between pitches.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glissando - a continuous slide upward or downward between two notes
  • synonym: portamento - a smooth, uninterrupted glide from one note to another
