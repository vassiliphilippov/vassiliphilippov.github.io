bobble

verb
to bounce lightly
3.7
Pronunciation: /ˈbɔː.bəl/ /; / /ˈbɑː.bəl/ (AmE), /ˈbɒb.əl/ (BrE)
to move or bounce up and down quickly and lightly, often in a repeated way

Examples

A head bobble
The toy's head will bobble every time you touch it.
As the bus drove over bumps, the passengers' heads began to bobble.
She laughed as the decorations on her hat bobble with each step.
The toy's head would bobble every time the table was bumped.
She watched the balloon bobble in the gentle breeze above the crowd.
If you drive too fast over potholes, your coffee cup will bobble in the holder.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bounce - to move quickly up, back, or away from a surface after hitting it
  • synonym: jiggle - to move with quick, small movements from side to side or up and down
  • antonym: remain still - to stay without moving
  • antonym: steady - to make or become still and not move up and down
