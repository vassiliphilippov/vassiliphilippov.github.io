renal colic

noun
pain from kidney stone passage
9.2 5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈriːnəl ˈkɑlɪk / (AmE), / ˈriːnəl ˈkɒlɪk / (BrE)
Severe, cramping pain in the lower back or side, often radiating to the groin, caused by the passage of a kidney stone through the ureter.

Examples

Severe renal colic pain
Drinking more water may help to prevent episodes of renal colic.
She described the pain as classic renal colic, radiating from her back to her groin.
The patient was admitted with intense renal colic after complaining of pain on his left side.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kidney stone - a hard mass formed in the kidneys from minerals in the urine
