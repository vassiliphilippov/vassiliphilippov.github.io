pick

verb
remove unwanted parts (like feathers or pits)
1.0
Pronunciation: / pɪk / (AmE), / pɪk / (BrE)
to remove unwanted substances or parts from something, often using the fingers or a tool

Examples

To pick seeds out
He carefully picked the pits out of the cherries before baking the pie.
It took her an hour to pick all the feathers from the chicken.
You need to pick the grapes before making jam to remove any stems or leaves.
He picked the seeds out of the watermelon one by one.
She carefully picked the meat from the fish's bones with her fork.
The child picked the chocolate chips from her cookie before eating the rest.
