joint fir

noun
desert shrub with jointed stems (Ephedra)
Pronunciation: / dʒɔɪnt fɝː / (AmE), / dʒɔɪnt fɜː / (BrE)
A desert shrub with jointed stems, almost leafless, having reduced scalelike leaves and reddish fleshy seeds; belonging to the genus Ephedra.

Examples

Joint fir seeds
Researchers studied the medicinal properties of joint fir seeds.
The joint fir thrives in arid environments where few other plants can survive.
Unlike most shrubs, the joint fir has stems that perform photosynthesis instead of leaves.
