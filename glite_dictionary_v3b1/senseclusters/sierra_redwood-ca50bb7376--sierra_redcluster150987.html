Sierra redwood

noun
giant evergreen tree of the Sierra Nevada
Pronunciation: / siˈɛrə ˈrɛdˌwʊd / (AmE), / siˈɛərə ˈrɛdˌwʊd / (BrE)
an extremely tall evergreen tree (Sequoiadendron giganteum) native to the southern part of the western Sierra Nevada in California, noted as the largest living organism by volume.

Examples

A Sierra redwood tree
A single Sierra redwood can live for thousands of years, making it a natural wonder.
Many tourists visit California each year just to marvel at the ancient Sierra redwood trees.
The Sierra redwood is renowned for reaching heights of over 250 feet.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: coast redwood - tallest tree species, closely related but different from Sierra redwood
  • synonym: giant sequoia - another name for Sierra redwood, referring to the same species
