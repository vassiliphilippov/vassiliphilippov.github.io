extirpation

noun
surgical removal of tissue or organ
Pronunciation: / ˌɛkstərˈpeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛkstəˈpeɪʃən / (BrE)
the surgical removal of a body part or tissue

Examples

Surgical extirpation
Advances in surgery have made the extirpation of diseased organs much safer.
Following extirpation of the infected tissue, the patient's recovery was rapid.
The complete extirpation of the tumor was necessary to prevent recurrence.
