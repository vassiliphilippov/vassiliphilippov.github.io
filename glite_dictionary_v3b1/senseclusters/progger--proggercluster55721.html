progger

noun
progressive supporter (informal)
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑːɡər / (AmE), / ˈprɒɡə / (BrE)
an informal, sometimes derogatory term for a person who supports progressive politics or social policies

Examples

A progger supports change
He was labeled a progger by critics who disagreed with his views on climate change.
Some people use the word progger to mock those who support progressive policies.
Despite being called a progger, she continued to advocate for social justice reforms.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conservative - a person who favors traditional values and is cautious about change
  • synonym: progressive - a person who advocates for social reform or new ideas
