whiteside

noun
white or light surface
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪtsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈwaɪtsaɪd / (BrE)
the side or surface of an object that is white or lighter in color than the rest

Examples

The whiteside of wing
The whiteside of the butterfly's wing was visible in the sunlight.
Please place the bread with the whiteside facing up on the plate.
The artist painted only the whiteside of the sculpture to create a striking contrast.

Related Terms

  • antonym: darkside - the side or surface of an object that is dark or darker in color
