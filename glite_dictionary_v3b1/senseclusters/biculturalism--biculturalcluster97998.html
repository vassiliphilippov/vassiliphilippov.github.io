biculturalism

noun
two cultures together
Pronunciation: / baɪˈkʌltʃərəlɪzəm / (AmE), / baɪˈkʌltʃərəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the presence, coexistence, or promotion of two distinct cultures within a society or an individual

Examples

Biculturalism in society
Many immigrants experience biculturalism as they adapt to a new country while keeping their original traditions.
Schools that encourage biculturalism help students appreciate both their heritage and the wider society.
The festival was a celebration of biculturalism, featuring music and food from two different cultures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monoculturalism - the presence or promotion of only one culture in a society
  • degree higher: multiculturalism - the presence of several different cultures in a society
