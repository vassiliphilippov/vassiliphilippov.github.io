head-to-head

adverb
in direct, close competition
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛd tu ˈhɛd / (AmE), / ˌhɛd tə ˈhɛd / (BrE)
used to describe a situation in which two people or teams are competing against each other directly, especially in a very close contest

Examples

Go head-to-head in competition
She enjoyed going head-to-head with her rival in debates.
The election results were so close that the candidates were head-to-head until the very end.
The two teams are going head-to-head for the championship title this year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: neck and neck - very close in a competition; level with each other
  • synonym: toe-to-toe - in direct confrontation or competition
