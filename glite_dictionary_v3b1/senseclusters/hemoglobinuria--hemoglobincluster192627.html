hemoglobinuria

noun
hemoglobin in urine (medical)
Pronunciation: / ˌhiːmoʊˌɡloʊbəˈnjʊriə /; / ˌhiːməˌɡloʊbəˈnjʊriə / (AmE), / ˌhiːməʊˌɡləʊbɪˈnjʊəriə /; / ˌhiːməˌɡləʊbɪˈnjʊəriə / (BrE)
The presence of hemoglobin in the urine, usually due to the breakdown of red blood cells.

Examples

Persistent hemoglobinuria
Persistent hemoglobinuria may indicate underlying hemolytic anemia or kidney problems.
Routine tests revealed traces of hemoglobinuria in the athlete following intense physical activity.
The doctor suspected hemoglobinuria after noticing the patient's dark-colored urine.
Acute haemoglobinuria can occur following severe malaria or incompatible blood transfusion.
Dark-colored urine can be a sign of haemoglobinuria in patients with certain blood disorders.
The doctor ordered tests to confirm whether haemoglobinuria was present after the patient reported red-tinted urine.
