cervical root syndrome

noun
compression of spinal nerve roots in the neck
8.0 5.9
Pronunciation: /ˈsɜːr.vɪ.kəl ruːt ˈsɪn.droʊm/ /; / /ˈsɜːr.və.kəl rʊt ˈsɪn.droʊm/ (AmE), /ˈsɜː.vɪ.kəl ruːt ˈsɪn.drəʊm/ /; / /ˈsɜː.və.kəl ruːt ˈsɪn.drəʊm/ (BrE)
A medical condition caused by compression of the spinal nerve roots in the neck, leading to neck pain, muscular weakness, and abnormal sensations.

Examples

cervical root syndrome pain
Early treatment of cervical root syndrome may prevent further neurological complications.
Physical therapy can help manage the symptoms of cervical root syndrome in many patients.
The doctor diagnosed her with cervical root syndrome after she complained of persistent neck pain and numbness in her arm.
