reletter

verb
to assign new labels
7.3
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈlet.ər/ /; / /rɪ.ˈlet.ər/ /; / /rə.ˈlet.ər/ (AmE), /riː.ˈlet.ə/ /; / /rɪ.ˈlet.ə/ /; / /rə.ˈlet.ə/ (BrE)
to assign new letters as labels to parts of a text, list, diagram, or document, usually to maintain order after changes

Examples

To reletter a diagram
The teacher asked the students to reletter the diagram after adding a new part.
When the list was updated, the secretary had to reletter each item to keep the order clear.
After revising the contract, the lawyer needed to reletter the paragraphs for consistency.
