Sarmatian

noun
Miocene geological stage
9.9
Pronunciation: / sɑːrˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / sɑːˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
a stage or age in the middle Miocene epoch used in the regional geology of Eastern Europe

Examples

Sarmatian stage
The fossils were assigned to the Sarmatian stage, indicating deposition about twelve million years ago.
Geologists studied the sediment layers from the Sarmatian to understand the region's ancient environment.
Many important mammal fossils have been discovered in Sarmatian deposits across Eastern Europe.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: Badenian - a stage preceding the Sarmatian in the Miocene of Central Europe
  • synonym: Miocene - an epoch of the Neogene Period, about 23 to 5 million years ago
  • degree higher: Pannonian - a stage following the Sarmatian in the Miocene of Central Europe
