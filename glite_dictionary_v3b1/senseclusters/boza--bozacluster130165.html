boza

noun
fermented grain drink
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈboʊzə / (AmE), / ˈbəʊzə / (BrE)
a thick, slightly sour, fermented drink made from grains such as millet or wheat, popular in Eastern Europe and the Middle East

Examples

A cup of boza
On a cold evening, we enjoyed a warm cup of boza at the local market.
Many people in the Balkans drink boza as a traditional winter treat.
The café served homemade boza alongside sweet pastries.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ayran - a cold yogurt-based beverage popular in Turkey and nearby regions
  • synonym: kvass - a traditional fermented Slavic beverage made from rye bread
