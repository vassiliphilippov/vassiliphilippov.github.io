bipolarity

noun
having two poles
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪpoʊˈlærəti / (AmE), / ˌbaɪpəʊˈlærəti / (BrE)
the property of having two opposite poles, such as positive and negative electrical or magnetic charges

Examples

Magnet bipolarity
The bipolarity of the magnet allows it to attract and repel different objects.
Scientists studied the bipolarity of the molecule to understand its chemical behavior.
The device relies on the bipolarity of its components to function correctly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: polarity - the state of having distinct and opposite charges or characteristics
  • antonym: unipolarity - the state of having only one pole or type of charge
