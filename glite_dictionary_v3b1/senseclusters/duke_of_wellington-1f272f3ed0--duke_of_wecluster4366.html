Duke of Wellington

proper noun
British general and Prime Minister (1769–1852)
Pronunciation: / duːk əv ˈwɛlɪŋtən / (AmE), / djuːk əv ˈwɛlɪŋtən / (BrE)
A British general and statesman (1769–1852) who defeated Napoleon at Waterloo and later served as Prime Minister of the United Kingdom.

Examples

The Duke of Wellington defeated Napoleon
As Prime Minister, the Duke of Wellington played a significant role in British politics during the 19th century.
Historians often cite the Duke of Wellington as one of Britain's greatest military commanders.
The Duke of Wellington is best remembered for his victory over Napoleon at the Battle of Waterloo.

  • antonym: Napoleon Bonaparte - French military leader defeated by the Duke of Wellington at Waterloo
