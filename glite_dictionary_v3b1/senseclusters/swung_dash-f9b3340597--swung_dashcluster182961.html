swung dash

noun
punctuation mark indicating omission
9.2
Pronunciation: / swʌŋ dæʃ / (AmE), / swʌŋ daʃ / (BrE)
a punctuation mark (⁓) used in writing to indicate the omission of a word or part of a word

Examples

A swung dash mark
In early dictionaries, a swung dash was used to show that a word had been omitted.
The swung dash appears frequently in older reference books.
The editor replaced the repeated word with a swung dash.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tilde - a similar wavy punctuation mark (~) used in writing and mathematics
