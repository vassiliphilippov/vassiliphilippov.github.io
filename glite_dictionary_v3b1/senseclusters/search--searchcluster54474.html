Synonyms

  • look for
    1.0 202/m
    look for is a common, everyday way to try to find something, while search is neutral and slightly more formal.
  • seek out
    3.0 10/m
    seek out is to actively find a specific person or thing with effort, while search covers general looking or investigating.
  • hunt
    1.2 9.15/m
    hunt often means trying to find a person or thing with persistence or tracking, while search is more general looking.
  • scour
    5.1 3.18/m
    scour means searching an area very thoroughly and intensely, while search can mean looking in a less complete way.
  • seek
    1.0 90/m
    seek is often used for finding abstract things like help or advice in formal contexts, while search often means looking physically.
  • look
    1.0 501/m
    look can mean simply turn your eyes or glance casually, while search implies a careful or thorough attempt to find.
  • comb
    3.3 2.94/m
    comb means to search a place bit by bit, especially an area, while search can be wider or for information.
