high-principled

adjective
having strong moral principles
5.2
Pronunciation: / haɪˈprɪnsəpəld / (AmE), / haɪˈprɪnsɪpəld / (BrE)
having strong and high moral or ethical principles; acting according to high standards of honesty or integrity.

Examples

A high-principled person
His high-principled approach earned him the trust and respect of his peers.
She is known as a high-principled leader who always puts ethics before profit.
The committee wanted a high-principled person to chair their organization.

Related Terms

  • synonym: principled - acting according to a set of moral principles
  • antonym: unprincipled - not acting according to moral principles
