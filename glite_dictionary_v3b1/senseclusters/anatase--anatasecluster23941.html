anatase

noun
titanium dioxide mineral
Pronunciation: / ˈænəˌteɪs / (AmE), / ˈænətɑːz / (BrE)
a mineral form of titanium dioxide (TiO2), found as crystals in rocks and sometimes used as a pigment or in industry

Examples

Anatase mineral crystals
The geologist discovered several anatase crystals embedded in the rock sample.
Under the microscope, anatase can be distinguished from other titanium minerals by its crystal shape.
Collectors value anatase for its unique brown and black crystal formations.
The white pigment in the paint was made from anatase for its brightness.
Scientists studied the photocatalytic properties of anatase in solar energy applications.
Natural anatase crystals are sometimes found in metamorphic rocks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rutile - a mineral form of titanium dioxide with a different crystal structure
