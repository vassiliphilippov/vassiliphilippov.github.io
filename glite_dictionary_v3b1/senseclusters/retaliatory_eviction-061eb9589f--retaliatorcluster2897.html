retaliatory eviction

noun
eviction as reprisal for tenant complaints
7.6 7.1
Pronunciation: / rɪˈtæliəˌtɔri ɪˈvɪkʃən / (AmE), / rɪˈtaljətəri ɪˈvɪkʃən / (BrE)
an eviction carried out by a landlord in response to a tenant's legitimate complaints or actions, often considered illegal in many jurisdictions

Examples

Retaliatory eviction by landlord
After filing several complaints about faulty heating, Maria faced a retaliatory eviction from her landlord.
Laws in many states specifically prohibit retaliatory eviction to protect tenants' rights.
The court ruled that the retaliatory eviction was unlawful and ordered the landlord to reinstate the tenant.
