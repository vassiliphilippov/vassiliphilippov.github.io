acylglycerol

noun
ester of glycerol and fatty acids
9.9 8.6
Pronunciation: /ə.sɪl.ˈglɪs.ər.əl/ /; / /eɪ.sɪl.ˈglɪs.ər.əl/ (AmE), /ə.sɪl.ˈglɪs.ə.rəl/ /; / /eɪ.sɪl.ˈglɪs.ə.rəl/ (BrE)
A compound that is an ester formed from glycerol and one or more fatty acids, occurring naturally as fats and oils.

Examples

The acylglycerol content
Scientists measured the acylglycerol content in various vegetable oils to assess their purity.
The presence of free fatty acids and acylglycerol in the sample indicated it was derived from natural fats.
Triglycerides, a type of acylglycerol, are a major component of body fat in humans and animals.
