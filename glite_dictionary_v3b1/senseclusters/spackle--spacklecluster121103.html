spackle

verb
to fill surface holes
6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈspæk.əl/ (AmE), /ˈspæk.əl/ (BrE)
to fill holes or cracks in a wall or ceiling with a special paste before painting or finishing

Examples

To spackle a crack
Before painting the living room, she carefully spackled all the small cracks in the wall.
He spackled the holes left by the old picture frames to make the wall smooth again.
After removing the shelves, they needed to spackle the damaged spots before redecorating.
Before painting the living room, she carefully spackled all the nail holes in the wall.
You should spackle any cracks in the ceiling to get a smooth finish.
He spackled the damaged area and waited for it to dry before sanding.
