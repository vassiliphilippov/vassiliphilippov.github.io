establishmentarian

noun
supporter of institutions
9.4
Pronunciation: / ɪˌstæblɪʃmənˈtɛriən / (AmE), / ɪˌstæblɪʃmənˈteəriən / (BrE)
a person who supports or defends established institutions, the status quo, or traditional authority

Examples

An establishmentarian leader
As an outspoken establishmentarian, she resisted any changes to the traditional system.
The debate featured an establishmentarian arguing for the preservation of the current government structure.
Many establishmentarians believe that stability comes from maintaining established institutions.
As an establishmentarian, he opposed the radical reforms proposed by the new party.
The debate featured an establishmentarian defending the current system against calls for change.
Many establishmentarians believe that stability is more important than rapid transformation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conservative - a person who is resistant to change and prefers traditional values
  • antonym: progressive - a person who supports social or political change
  • antonym: reformer - a person who seeks to change or improve institutions
  • synonym: traditionalist - a person who supports tradition and established practices
