bitterbrush

noun
western North American shrub
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪtərˌbrʌʃ / (AmE), / ˈbɪtəˌbrʌʃ / (BrE)
a hardy shrub native to western North America, commonly found in dry areas and eaten by wildlife such as deer

Examples

The bitterbrush shrub
Deer often feed on bitterbrush during the winter months.
The dry hillsides were covered with bitterbrush and other native plants.
Ranchers value bitterbrush as an important food source for livestock and wildlife.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sagebrush - a common shrub of dry western North America
