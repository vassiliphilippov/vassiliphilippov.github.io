scraggle

verb
to grow unevenly
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈskræɡəl / (AmE), / ˈskræɡəl / (BrE)
to grow or spread in a thin, uneven, or untidy way

Examples

Bushes scraggle along
Wild bushes began to scraggle along the abandoned path after the rain.
The boy's hair would always scraggle out from under his hat on windy days.
Vines scraggled up the side of the old brick wall, giving it a neglected look.
