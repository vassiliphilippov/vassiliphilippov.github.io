lagerphone

noun
Australian percussion instrument with bottle caps
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪɡərˌfoʊn / (AmE), / ˈlɑːɡəfəʊn / (BrE)
an Australian percussion instrument made from a long stick with bottle caps loosely nailed to it, played by striking or banging it on the ground, commonly used in bush music

Examples

An Australian lagerphone
Children crafted their own lagerphones for the school’s Australian culture week.
The musician played the lagerphone with rhythmic energy during the folk festival.
You could hear the jangling sound of the lagerphone above all the other instruments in the bush band.
