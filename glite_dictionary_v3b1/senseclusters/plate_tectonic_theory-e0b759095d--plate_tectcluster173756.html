plate tectonic theory

noun
theory explaining Earth's shifting plates
5.5 3.9
Pronunciation: /pleɪt tek.ˈtɑː.nɪk ˈθiː.ə.riː/ /; / /pleɪt tek.ˈtɑː.nɪk ˈθɪə.ri/ (AmE), /pleɪt tek.ˈtɒ.nɪk ˈθɪə.ri/ /; / /pleɪt tek.ˈtɒ.nɪk ˈθiː.ə.ri/ (BrE)
the scientific theory explaining the movement of the Earth's lithospheric plates and the associated phenomena such as earthquakes, volcanism, and mountain building

Examples

Plate tectonic theory explains earthquakes
Many geologists accept the plate tectonic theory to explain the distribution of earthquakes and volcanoes around the world.
Since its acceptance, plate tectonic theory has revolutionized our understanding of Earth's geological history.
The rise of the Himalayas is often attributed to the collision of two plates, as described in plate tectonic theory.
