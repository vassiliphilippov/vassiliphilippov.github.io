precip

noun
solid from reaction
Pronunciation: / prɪˈsɪp / (AmE), / prɪˈsɪp / (BrE)
a solid substance that forms and separates from a liquid during a chemical reaction; short for precipitate

Examples

A white precip forms
After adding the reagent, a white precip formed at the bottom of the tube.
The lab technician filtered the precip from the solution before continuing the experiment.
If the solution is cooled, a blue precip may appear, indicating a successful reaction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: precipitate - a solid that forms and separates from a solution during a chemical reaction
