haggis

noun
traditional Scottish dish of minced organs
6.2 9.0
Pronunciation: / ˈhæɡɪs / (AmE), / ˈhæɡɪs / (BrE)
A Scottish dish made from sheep's or calf's viscera minced with oatmeal, suet, and onions, boiled in the animal's stomach.

Examples

Traditional haggis dish
Burns Night is often celebrated with a traditional haggis as the centerpiece of the meal.
Many tourists are curious to try haggis when they visit Scotland for the first time.
The chef prepared a modern version of haggis using vegetarian ingredients.
