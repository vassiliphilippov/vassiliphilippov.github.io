underhanded

adjective
thrown or struck from below shoulder level
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈhændɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈhændɪd / (BrE)
(of a throw or stroke) made with the hand moving forward and upward from below shoulder level.

Examples

Underhanded throw
His underhanded technique gave him more control in table tennis.
She prefers an underhanded serve in tennis because it feels more natural.
The pitcher threw an underhanded ball during the demonstration.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overhanded - (of a throw) made with the hand above shoulder level
