phosphatidylserine

noun
cell membrane phospholipid
Pronunciation: / ˌfɑːsfəˌtaɪdɪlˈsɪriːn / (AmE), / ˌfɒsfətɪdɪlˈsɪəriːn / (BrE)
a phospholipid found in cell membranes that signals for cell removal when exposed on the cell surface during apoptosis

Examples

Phosphatidylserine exposure signal
Researchers use phosphatidylserine as a marker to identify cells undergoing apoptosis in laboratory studies.
The presence of phosphatidylserine on the cell surface signals the immune system to remove dying cells.
During programmed cell death, phosphatidylserine moves from the inner to the outer layer of the cell membrane.

Related Terms

  • synonym: apoptosis - programmed cell death in living organisms
  • synonym: phospholipid - a type of lipid forming cell membranes
