Baniwa

proper noun
Amazonian indigenous group
9.9
Pronunciation: / bəˈniːwə / (AmE), / bəˈniːwə / (BrE)
an indigenous people of the Amazon region in South America, and the language they speak

Examples

The Baniwa people
The Baniwa live along the Rio Negro in the Amazon region.
Researchers have documented the traditional stories of the Baniwa people.
The Baniwa language is still spoken by many in their community.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Arawak - a group of indigenous peoples of South America and the Caribbean
  • synonym: Yanomami - an indigenous people of the Amazon rainforest in Brazil and Venezuela
