septum

noun
a dividing wall or partition in anatomy or biology
8.4 6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛptəm / (AmE), / ˈsɛptəm / (BrE)
a thin wall or partition that divides two spaces or cavities, especially in the body or in biology

Examples

Heart septum
Each chamber of the heart is separated by a muscular septum.
In some plants, the ovary is divided by a thin septum into multiple compartments.
The nasal septum can sometimes deviate, causing breathing difficulties.
A thick septum divides the two chambers of the heart.
Damage to the cartilage in your septum can make breathing difficult.
The nasal septum separates the left and right airways in the nose.

  • synonym: partition - a structure dividing a space into parts
  • synonym: wall - a vertical structure that divides or encloses an area
