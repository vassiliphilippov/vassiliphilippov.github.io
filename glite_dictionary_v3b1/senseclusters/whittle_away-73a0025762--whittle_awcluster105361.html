whittle away

verb
gradually reduce or cut away
9.9 5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪt̬l əˈweɪ / (AmE), / ˈwɪt.əl əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to gradually reduce something by cutting away small pieces; to diminish bit by bit.

Examples

Whittle away savings
Government spending is slowly whittling away the country’s savings.
Over the years, constant rain had whittled away the stone steps.
They whittled away the block of wood until only a thin sliver remained.
