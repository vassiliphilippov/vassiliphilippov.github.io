rickettsialpox

noun
infectious disease caused by Rickettsia
Pronunciation: / rɪˌkɛtsiəlˈpɑks / (AmE), / rɪˌkɛtsiəlˈpɒks / (BrE)
A mild infectious disease caused by a bacterium of the genus Rickettsia, transmitted to humans by mite bites, and characterized by fever, headache, and skin lesions similar to chickenpox.

Examples

Rickettsialpox disease
Children living in infested apartment buildings have occasionally contracted rickettsialpox through mite bites.
Symptoms of rickettsialpox usually include fever, headache, and a rash that resembles chickenpox.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with rickettsialpox after noting the distinctive skin lesions and recent mite exposure.
