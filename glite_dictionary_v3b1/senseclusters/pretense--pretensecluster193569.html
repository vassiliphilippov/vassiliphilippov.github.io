pretense

noun
false claim or appearance
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːˌtɛns, prɪˈtɛns /; / ˈpriːˌtɛns /; / ˈpriː.tɛns /; / prɪˈtɛns /; / ˈpriːtns / (AmE), / ˈpriːtɛns, prɪˈtɛns /; / ˈpriː.tɛns /; / ˈpriː.təns /; / ˈpriːtɛns /; / prɪˈtɛns / (BrE)
an attempt to make something that is not true appear true; a false claim or appearance used to deceive or hide the real situation

Examples

He made no pretense of friendship.
The invitation was only a pretense for her to visit.

His cheerful attitude was just a pretense to hide his true feelings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deception - the act of deceiving or lying; making someone believe something that is not true
  • synonym: guise - an external form or appearance used to conceal the true nature of something
  • antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and straightforward
  • synonym: pretense - American spelling of 'pretence', meaning a false claim or display
  • synonym: pretext - a reason given in justification of a course of action that is not the real reason
  • antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist
  • antonym: truth - the quality or state of being true, the opposite of falsehood or pretense
  • antonym: truthfulness - the quality of being truthful or honest
