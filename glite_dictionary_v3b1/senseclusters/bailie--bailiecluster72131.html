bailie

noun
Scottish town magistrate
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈbeɪ.liː/ (AmE), /ˈbeɪ.liː/ (BrE)
a Scottish municipal officer or magistrate, especially one serving on a town council (historically or in modern times)

Examples

A bailie in Scotland
The bailie presided over the town council meeting in Edinburgh.
As a respected bailie, she was responsible for maintaining order in the community.

In medieval Scotland, the bailie was responsible for maintaining order in the town.
The council appointed a new bailie to oversee local trade regulations.
During the festival, the bailie led the procession through the city streets.
