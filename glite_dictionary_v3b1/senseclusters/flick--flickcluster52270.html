flick

noun
a quick, sudden stroke or movement
Pronunciation: / flɪk / (AmE), / flɪk / (BrE)
a quick, light, or sudden movement, especially made with a part of the body or a tool

Examples

A quick flick
He removed the insect from his sleeve with a quick flick.
The magician produced a coin with a flick of his hand.
With a swift flick of her wrist, she turned off the light.
A sudden flick of her wrist sent the marble rolling across the table.
He gave the horse a gentle flick with the reins to make it move.
With a quick flick, she turned the page of the magazine.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flip - a sudden, quick movement, often turning something over
  • synonym: snap - a sudden, sharp movement or sound
  • synonym: tap - a light touch or hit
