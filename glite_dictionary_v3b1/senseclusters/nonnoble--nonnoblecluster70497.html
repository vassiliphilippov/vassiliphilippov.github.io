nonnoble

noun
person without noble rank
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈnoʊbəl / (AmE), / nɒnˈnəʊbəl / (BrE)
a person who is not of noble birth or rank; a commoner

Examples

A nonnoble in society
In medieval times, a nonnoble had limited rights compared to those of noble birth.
The king invited both nobles and nonnobles to the grand celebration.
As a proud nonnoble, she worked hard to earn respect in the royal court.

Related Terms

  • synonym: commoner - a person who is not a member of the nobility
  • antonym: noble - a person of high birth or rank
