issue

verb
to come out or flow from a source
Pronunciation: / ˈɪʃu / (AmE), / ˈɪsjuː / (BrE)
(formal) To come out, flow, or emit from a source; to originate or emerge.

Examples

Music issues from the window
A faint melody issued from the open window, catching everyone's attention.
Cries of delight issued from the playground as the children played.
Smoke issued from the chimney after the fire was lit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emerge - to come into view or existence
  • synonym: originate - to begin, arise, or derive from a source
