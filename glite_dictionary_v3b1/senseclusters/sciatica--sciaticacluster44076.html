sciatica

noun
pain along the sciatic nerve
6.0
Pronunciation: / saɪˈætɪkə / (AmE), / saɪˈatɪkə / (BrE)
Pain affecting the back, hip, and outer side of the leg, caused by compression of a spinal nerve root in the lower back, often due to degeneration of an intervertebral disc.

Examples

Sciatica pain
After lifting heavy boxes, he developed severe sciatica that made walking difficult.
Her doctor diagnosed her with sciatica after she reported sharp pain radiating down her leg.
Physical therapy can help manage the symptoms of sciatica and reduce pain over time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: neuralgia - intense, typically intermittent pain along a nerve
