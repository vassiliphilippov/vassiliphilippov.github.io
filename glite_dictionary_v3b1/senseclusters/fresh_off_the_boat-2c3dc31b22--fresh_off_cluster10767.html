fresh off the boat

adjective
newly arrived and inexperienced
4.5
Pronunciation: / frɛʃ ɔf ðə boʊt / (AmE), / frɛʃ ɒf ðə bəʊt / (BrE)
newly arrived in a place or country and often inexperienced or unfamiliar with local customs; often used to describe immigrants or newcomers, sometimes with connotations of naivety.

Examples

Fresh off the boat immigrant
It's tough being fresh off the boat in a completely new culture.
Many fresh off the boat students struggle to adapt to the local school system.
When she arrived in Canada, she still felt fresh off the boat and nervous about making friends.

Related Terms

  • synonym: greenhorn - a person who is new to or inexperienced at something
  • synonym: newcomer - a person who has recently arrived in a place
