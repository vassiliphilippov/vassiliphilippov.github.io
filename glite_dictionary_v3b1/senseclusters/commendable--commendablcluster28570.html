Synonyms

  • praiseworthy
    4.5 0.2/m
    praiseworthy is clearly deserving warm public praise for good actions, while commendable is a general term for worthy behaviour.
  • laudable
    7.2 0.5/m
    laudable is used in formal praise or reports for good actions, while commendable is more common and slightly less formal.
  • meritorious
    7.5 0.4/m
    meritorious is often used for deeds that earn reward or honor, while commendable is broader praise for being worthy or good.
  • admirable
    3.3 4/m
    admirable is used when something inspires respect or wonder, while commendable is simply deserving praise or approval.
  • applaudable
    6.1 0.00/m
    applaudable is shown as worthy of applause or public praise, while commendable is a general word for something worthy of approval.
  • creditable
    6.2 0.10/m
