post-mortem examination

noun
examination of a body after death
3.2 3.9
Pronunciation: /poʊst ˈmɔːr.təm ɪg.zæ.mə.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /pəʊst ˈmɔː.təm ɪg.zæ.mɪ.ˈneɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
an examination and dissection of a dead body carried out to determine the cause of death or the extent of disease

Examples

Medical post-mortem examination
A post-mortem examination was ordered to establish the cause of his unexpected death.
Doctors performed a post-mortem examination to investigate the effects of the disease.
The post-mortem examination revealed no evidence of foul play.
