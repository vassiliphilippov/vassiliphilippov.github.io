recrudesce

verb
break out or recur again (undesirable events)
7.3 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌriː.kruːˈdɛs /; / ˌriːkruːˈdɛs / (AmE), / ˌriː.kruːˈdɛs /; / ˌriːkruːˈdɛs / (BrE)
to break out or happen again after a period of being inactive, especially something undesirable like a problem or disease

Examples

Old problems recrudesce
Old resentments can recrudesce in times of crisis.
Tensions in the region may recrudesce if diplomatic efforts fail.
The disease seemed to disappear, only to recrudesce a few months later.
Her eczema might recrudesce in response to stress or allergens.
If the infection recrudesces, stronger antibiotics may be necessary.
Symptoms of malaria can recrudesce even after initial treatment appears successful.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abate - to become less intense or widespread
  • synonym: recur - happen again; occur repeatedly
