hypostasis

noun
genetic suppression by another gene
7.6 9.0
Pronunciation: /haɪ.pə.ˈsteɪ.sɪs/ (AmE), /haɪ.pə.ˈsteɪ.sɪs/ (BrE)
(Genetics) The suppression of the effect of one gene by the presence of an unrelated gene.

Examples

Gene hypostasis
In some plants, hypostasis can mask the expression of color genes, leading to unexpected flower colors.
Research into hypostasis has helped clarify the complex interactions between different genes.
The geneticist explained how hypostasis influences the inheritance of certain traits in mice.
