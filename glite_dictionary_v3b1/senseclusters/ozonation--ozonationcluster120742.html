ozonation

noun
reaction with ozone
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊzoʊˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌəʊzəʊˈneɪʃən /; / ˌəʊzəˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of treating or purifying something (such as water or air) by exposing it to ozone gas

Examples

Ozonation of water
The ozonation of drinking water helps remove harmful bacteria and organic pollutants.
Researchers studied the ozonation process to improve the breakdown of industrial waste chemicals.
Proper ozonation can enhance the safety and taste of municipal water supplies.
Many water treatment plants use ozonation to remove harmful microorganisms from drinking water.
The ozonation process can help reduce unpleasant odors in wastewater.
Some swimming pools rely on ozonation instead of chlorine for water purification.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disinfection - the process of cleaning something to destroy bacteria or viruses
  • synonym: oxidation - a chemical reaction involving the addition of oxygen or removal of hydrogen
  • antonym: reduction - a chemical reaction involving the gain of electrons or removal of oxygen
