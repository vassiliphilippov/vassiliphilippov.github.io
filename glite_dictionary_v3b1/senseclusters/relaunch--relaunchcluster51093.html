relaunch

noun
new start after changes
4.2 0.30/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀêÀàl…înt É / (AmE), / ÀåriÀêÀàl…îÀênt É / (BrE)
an act or event of starting or introducing something again, especially after making changes or improvements

Examples

A product relaunch
The company planned a major relaunch of its smartphone with new features and a fresh design.
After a period of low sales, the relaunch of the product attracted much attention from the media.
The festival's relaunch included new events to appeal to a wider audience.

Synonyms

  • reinitiation
    7.0 0.01/m
    reinitiation means starting something again after a pause or end, while relaunch is restarting publicly after changes or improvements.
  • recommencement
    8.0 0.00/m
    recommencement is a formal beginning again after a stop, while relaunch often refers to a public restart after improvements.
  • resumption
    5.6 1.06/m
    resumption is continuing an activity after an interruption, while relaunch is a planned restart, often public and with changes.
  • re-introduction
    4.1 0.00/m
    re-introduction brings something back after rejection or end, while relaunch is a restart that shows new improvements or a new launch.
  • reactivation
    4.2 0.09/m
    reactivation makes a system or service active again, while relaunch is a public restart of a product or project after changes.
