crystalloid

noun
crystal-like particle
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪstəˌlɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstəˌlɔɪd / (BrE)
a substance or particle that resembles a crystal, especially a small crystal-like deposit found in cells or tissues

Examples

Crystalloid particle
The scientist observed several crystalloids under the microscope in the tissue sample.
Doctors noted the presence of crystalloid deposits in the patient's kidney cells.
A crystalloid can sometimes indicate abnormal metabolic processes in the body.

Related Terms

  • antonym: amorphous - lacking a clear shape or structure
  • synonym: crystal - a solid material with a regular, repeating structure
