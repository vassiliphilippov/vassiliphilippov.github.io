Cuban Revolution

proper noun
Fidel Castro's revolution in Cuba, 1956–1959
Pronunciation: / ˈkjuː.bən ˌrɛv.əˈluː.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈkjuː.bən ˌrɛv.əˈluː.ʃən / (BrE)
the revolution led by Fidel Castro and his supporters from 1956 to 1959, resulting in the overthrow of Cuba's government and the rise of a socialist state

Examples

The Cuban Revolution
After the Cuban Revolution, thousands of Cubans emigrated to the United States seeking new opportunities.
Many historians have studied the causes and effects of the Cuban Revolution on Latin America.
The Cuban Revolution transformed the political and social structure of the country in the late 1950s.
