narrow-minded

adjective
not tolerant or open-minded
2.7 4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈnæroʊ ˌmaɪndɪd /; / ˌnæroʊˈmaɪndɪd / (AmE), / ˈnærəʊ ˌmaɪndɪd /; / ˌnærəʊˈmaɪndɪd / (BrE)
not willing to accept or consider ideas, beliefs, or behaviors that are different from one's own; lacking tolerance or flexibility

Examples

A narrow-minded person
Her parents are quite narrow-minded about what careers are acceptable for their children.
It is difficult to have a productive debate with someone so narrow-minded.
The committee was criticized for its narrow-minded approach to the problem.
His narrow-minded approach prevented any real progress on the project.
It is narrow-minded to judge people solely on their appearance.
She found her parents' narrow-minded views on education frustrating.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bigoted - having strong, unreasonable beliefs and disliking others with different views
  • antonym: open-minded - willing to consider new ideas; receptive and tolerant
  • synonym: prejudiced - having or showing a dislike or distrust based on preconceived ideas
  • antonym: tolerant - showing willingness to allow opinions or behavior that you do not necessarily agree with
