Cucumis melo inodorus

noun
group of muskmelon varieties with non-musky aroma
9.9
Pronunciation: / kjuːˈkjuːmɪs ˈmiːloʊ ɪˈnɑːdərəs / (AmE), / kjuːˈkjuːmɪs ˈmiːləʊ ɪˈnɒdərəs / (BrE)
A group of muskmelon varieties having smooth, white rinds and white or greenish flesh that lacks the typical musky aroma.

Examples

A Cucumis melo inodorus melon
Farmers prefer cultivating Cucumis melo inodorus varieties for their long shelf life and mild flavor.
The Cucumis melo inodorus group includes popular fruits like honeydew and casaba melons.
Unlike cantaloupe, Cucumis melo inodorus melons do not emit a musky aroma when ripe.
