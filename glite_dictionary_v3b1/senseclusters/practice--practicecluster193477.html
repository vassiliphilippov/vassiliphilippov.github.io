practice

noun
customary behavior or method
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈpræktɪs /; / ˈpræk.tɪs / (AmE), / ˈpræktɪs /; / ˈpræk.tɪs / (BrE)
a habitual or customary way of doing something; a usual method or procedure

Examples

Company practice
It is the company's practice to review employee performance every year.
The chef has a strict practice of using only fresh ingredients.
Wearing uniforms is a common practice in many schools.
Following local practice, they took off their shoes before entering the house.
It is common practice in the office to greet each other every morning.
The practice of shaking hands dates back centuries.

  • synonym: habit - a settled or regular tendency or practice
  • antonym: innovation - a new method, idea, or thing
