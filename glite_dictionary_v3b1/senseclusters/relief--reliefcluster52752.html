relief

noun
sculpture with raised shapes on a surface
7.3 3.88/m
Pronunciation: / r…™Ààlif / (AmE), / r…™ÀàliÀêf / (BrE)
a sculptural technique in which figures or designs are carved on a flat surface to project outward from the background

Examples

Relief sculpture
Artists often use relief to add depth and interest to flat surfaces.
Many coins feature a relief of a famous person's profile.
The ancient temple walls were decorated with elaborate relief depicting historic battles.

Synonyms

  • sculptural relief
    7.9 0.01/m
    sculptural relief is a common art technique carving figures that project from a flat surface, while relief is the general term.
  • relievo
    9.9 0.00/m
    relievo is an older or borrowed art term used in specialist texts for relief sculpture, while relief is the common English name.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: bas-relief - a sculpture in which figures are only slightly raised from the background
  • degree higher: high relief - a sculptural form with figures deeply carved to stand out far from the background
  • antonym: intaglio - a design engraved into a surface below the main surface level (opposite of relief)
