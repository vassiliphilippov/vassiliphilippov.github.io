undertaxed

adjective
paying too little tax
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈtækst / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈtækst / (BrE)
subject to less tax than is usual, expected, or appropriate

Examples

Large undertaxed companies
Critics argue that large corporations are often undertaxed compared to small businesses.
Some economists believe that luxury items remain undertaxed in many regions.
The report found that certain imported goods were significantly undertaxed for years.
