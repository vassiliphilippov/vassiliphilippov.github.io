antibaryon

noun
antiparticle of a baryon
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˌæntiˈbɛriˌɑn / (AmE), / ˌæntiˈbæriˌɒn / (BrE)
a subatomic particle that is the antiparticle counterpart of a baryon, having a baryon number of -1

Examples

An antibaryon particle
An antibaryon will annihilate with a baryon, releasing energy.
Each antibaryon has the opposite baryon number to its baryon counterpart.
Physicists observed the creation of an antibaryon in the particle accelerator experiment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antiproton - the antiparticle of the proton
  • antonym: baryon - a class of subatomic particles, including protons and neutrons
