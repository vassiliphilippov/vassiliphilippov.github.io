consonant

noun
letter representing a non-vowel sound
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːn.sə.nənt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.sə.nənt / (BrE)
A letter of the alphabet that represents a speech sound that is not a vowel.

Examples

A consonant letter
She practiced pronouncing each consonant clearly when learning English.
Sometimes 'y' functions as a vowel, other times as a consonant.
The letter 'b' is a consonant in the English alphabet.

Related Terms

  • antonym: vowel - a letter representing a speech sound made without any closure in the vocal tract
