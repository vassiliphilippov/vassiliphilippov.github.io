Biblical Magi

noun
the wise men who visited infant Jesus
8.5
Pronunciation: / bɪˈblɪkəl ˈmeɪdʒaɪ / (AmE), / bɪˈblɪkəl ˈmædʒaɪ / (BrE)
the wise men from the East who, according to the Gospel of Matthew, visited the infant Jesus in Bethlehem, guided by a star and bringing gifts of gold, frankincense, and myrrh

Examples

The Biblical Magi visit
According to the Gospel of Matthew, the Biblical Magi followed a star to find the newborn Jesus.
Every Christmas, churches re-enact the journey of the Biblical Magi to Bethlehem.
The gifts brought by the Biblical Magi have become symbolic in Christian tradition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: wise men - the traditional name for the Magi; the wise visitors to Jesus
