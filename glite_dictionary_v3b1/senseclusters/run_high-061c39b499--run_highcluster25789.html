run high

verb
become very intense or strong (about feelings, etc.)
1.8
Pronunciation: / rʌn haɪ / (AmE), / rʌn haɪ / (BrE)
(of emotions, expectations, or tensions) to be very intense or strong.

Examples

Tensions run high
At the end of the season, tensions run high among the players and coaches.
Emotions often run high during heated political debates.
Hopes ran high before the launch of the new product.

Related Terms

  • antonym: run low - to be at a low level or become less intense (about resources, feelings, etc.)
