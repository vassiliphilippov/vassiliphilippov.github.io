bathyal district

noun
steep seabed zone between shelf and abyssal depths
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbæθiəl dɪˈstrɪkt / (AmE), / ˈbaθiəl dɪˈstrɪkt / (BrE)
the steep area of the seabed descending from the edge of the continental shelf down to the abyssal zone, typically found at depths of 200 to 2000 meters

Examples

The bathyal district zone
Many deep-sea creatures are uniquely adapted to life in the bathyal district.
Scientists deployed sensors to monitor changes in the bathyal district off the coast of South America.
The submarine canyon cuts through the bathyal district as it descends toward the ocean's depths.
