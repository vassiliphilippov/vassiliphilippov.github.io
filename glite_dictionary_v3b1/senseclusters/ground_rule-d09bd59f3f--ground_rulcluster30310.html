ground rule

noun
special rule for specific situation, esp. in baseball
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɡraʊnd ruːl / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd ruːl / (BrE)
a basic or fundamental rule established at the beginning of an activity or situation to guide behavior or ensure fairness

Examples

Basic ground rules
Before we start, let's review the basic ground rules for this discussion.
Each ballpark may have its own ground rule that affects how certain plays are judged.
The stadium has a unique ground rule for balls that hit the scoreboard.
Before we begin the project, let's agree on a ground rule for communication.
One important ground rule of this classroom is to respect everyone's ideas.
The committee established a ground rule that all members must attend at least half the meetings.
