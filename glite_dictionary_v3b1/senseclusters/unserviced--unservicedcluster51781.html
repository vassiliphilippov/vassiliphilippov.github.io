unserviced

adjective
not managed or maintained
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈsɜːrvɪst / (AmE), / ʌnˈsɜːvɪst / (BrE)
not provided with necessary services, maintenance, or support

Examples

Unserviced loans
The company struggled to recover funds from several unserviced loans in its portfolio.
An unserviced mortgage can quickly lead to financial problems for both the lender and the borrower.
Banks often sell unserviced debts to collection agencies to minimize their losses.
The apartment building remained unserviced for months after the company went bankrupt.
Many rural roads are unserviced during the winter, making travel difficult.
He bought a used laptop that had been unserviced since it was manufactured.
