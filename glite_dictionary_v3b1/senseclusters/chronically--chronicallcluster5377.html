chronically

adverb
in a persistent or long-lasting way
4.8 2.93/m
Pronunciation: / Ààkr…ëÀê.n…™.kli / (AmE), / Ààkr…í.n…™.kli / (BrE)
in a way that continues for a long time or keeps happening, especially about illness or problems

Examples

To be chronically ill
Many patients remain chronically ill despite long-term treatment.
Some regions are chronically short of clean drinking water.
The building has been chronically understaffed for years, causing constant delays.
Many patients with asthma cough chronically, especially in the winter.
She has suffered chronically from migraines for most of her adult life.
Some people are chronically late despite their best efforts.

Synonyms

  • persistently
    3.8 2.27/m
    persistently means continuing despite problems or resistance, while chronically means happening over a long time, often as an illness.
  • constantly
    1.0 50/m
    constantly happens very often with little break, while chronically refers to problems that last for a long period.
  • all the time
    1.0 77/m
    all the time is informal and means always or very often, while chronically means long-lasting or recurring, often about illness.
  • continuously
    2.3 9.89/m
    continuously means without any break or stop, while chronically means something happens over a long period, not necessarily nonstop.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acutely - in a way that is severe but short in duration
