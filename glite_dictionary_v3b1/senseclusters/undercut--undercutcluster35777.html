undercut

noun
a cut made underneath to remove material
2.2
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndərˌkʌt / (AmE), / ˈʌndəkʌt / (BrE)
a cut made underneath an object or surface to remove material, often creating an overhanging effect

Examples

A precise undercut
An undercut was necessary to fit the two parts together precisely.
The engineer inspected the undercut to ensure it would not weaken the structure.
The welder made an undercut to separate the metal piece from the base.
