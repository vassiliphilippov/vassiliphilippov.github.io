so to speak

adverb
used when speaking figuratively
5.6 4.97/m
Pronunciation: / so ä t…ô spiÀêk / (AmE), / s…ô ä t…ô spiÀêk / (BrE)
used to show that what you just said is not exactly true, but is a way of describing something in a figurative or non-literal way

Examples

Used so to speak
He was, so to speak, the backbone of the organization.
She was, so to speak, glued to her phone all day.
The town was, so to speak, waking up after years of neglect.
He's the backbone of the team, so to speak.
The meeting was a wake-up call, so to speak, for everyone involved.
We had to start from scratch, so to speak, after the data was lost.

Synonyms

  • as it were
    6.9 4.23/m
    as it were is a slightly formal phrase that softens what you say, while so to speak is more casual and conversational.
  • as we say
    5.6 0.74/m
    as we say points to a common phrase people use for style, while so to speak signals a figurative or not literal remark.

Related Terms

  • synonym: as it were - used to indicate something is not to be taken literally; figuratively speaking
