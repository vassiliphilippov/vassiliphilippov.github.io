preassign

verb
to assign in advance
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːəˈsaɪn / (AmE), / ˌpriːəˈsaɪn / (BrE)
to assign or allocate something in advance, before it is needed or used

Examples

To preassign tasks
The manager will preassign tasks to each team member before the project starts.
Seats were preassigned to avoid confusion during the event.
It is helpful to preassign roles in group activities to save time.

  • antonym: reassign - to assign again or to a different person or purpose
