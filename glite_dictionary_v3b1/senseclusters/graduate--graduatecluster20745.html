graduate

verb
confer a degree upon
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrædʒuˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡrædʒʊeɪt /; / ˈɡrædʒueɪt / (BrE)
to complete a course of study and receive a degree or diploma (or for a school to give a degree to a student)

Examples

To graduate from school
Each spring, the university graduates hundreds of new teachers into the workforce.
The college graduated its largest class in history last year.
This medical school graduates more doctors than any other institution in the region.
After four years of hard work, she finally graduated.
He plans to graduate from college next year with a degree in biology.
Once you graduate, you can start applying for jobs in your field.

Related Terms

  • antonym: drop out - to leave school or university before finishing a course
  • synonym: finish - to bring to an end; complete
  • synonym: matriculate - to enroll or be enrolled in a college or university
