conk

verb
to faint; lose consciousness suddenly
Pronunciation: / kɑŋk / (AmE), / kɒŋk / (BrE)
(informal) to suddenly lose consciousness, faint, or die

Examples

To conk out suddenly
After standing in the sun too long, she suddenly conked out at the festival.
He was so exhausted after the marathon that he conked out on the sofa.
If I don't get some water soon, I might just conk out.
He nearly conked during the surgery but pulled through in the end.
The old car finally conked on the highway after years of faithful service.
They joked that Grandpa would never conk because he was so stubborn.

Related Terms

  • synonym: die - to stop living, to cease to be alive
  • synonym: faint - to suddenly lose consciousness for a short time
  • antonym: survive - to continue to live, especially after a dangerous event
