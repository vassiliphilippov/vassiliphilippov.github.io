Le Duc Tho

proper noun
Vietnamese diplomat, negotiated end to Vietnam War
Pronunciation: / leɪ ˌdʊk ˈtoʊ / (AmE), / leɪ ˌdʊk ˈtəʊ / (BrE)
(1911–1990) Vietnamese diplomat known for negotiating an end to the Vietnam War alongside Henry Kissinger.

Examples

Le Duc Tho diplomat
Le Duc Tho was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1973, but he declined to accept it.
In the peace talks with the United States, Le Duc Tho played a crucial negotiating role.
Many historians credit Le Duc Tho with shaping the outcome of the Paris Peace Accords.
