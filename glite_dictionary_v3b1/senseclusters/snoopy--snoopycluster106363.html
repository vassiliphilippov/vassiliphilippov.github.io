snoopy

adjective
offensively inquisitive; prying
8.4 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàsnuÀêpi / (AmE), / ÀàsnuÀêpi / (BrE)
unduly interested in other people's affairs; offensively inquisitive or prying

Examples

A snoopy neighbor
I wish my neighbors weren't so snoopy about everything that happens on our street.
My snoopy coworker is always asking questions about my personal life.
That snoopy reporter kept digging for gossip about the players.

Synonyms

  • prying
    7.0 0.00/m
    prying shows active attempts to uncover private facts, while snoopy denotes being unduly interested and offensively inquisitive.
  • nosy
    3.4 1.86/m
    nosy is a common informal word for someone who asks about others' private matters, while snoopy means offensively prying.
  • intrusive
    6.1 3.67/m
    intrusive describes entering someone's space or disturbing their peace, while snoopy focuses on offensive curiosity about private affairs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: discreet - careful and not likely to be noticed; showing respect for privacy
  • synonym: nosy - annoyingly or intrusively curious
  • synonym: prying - excessively curious about someone's private affairs
