farm out

verb
contract out work or lease for a fee
9.2 4.0
Pronunciation: /fɑːrm aʊt/ (AmE), /fɑːm aʊt/ (BrE)
to arrange for someone else to do work or provide a service, especially by paying them or contracting the work out

Examples

To farm out work
Our team has been farmed out to various departments during the busy season.
The company decided to farm out customer support to an agency overseas.
They often farm out specialized tasks to freelancers.
Instead of handling the entire process in-house, they farmed out parts of the job to freelancers.
The company decided to farm out its customer service operations to an overseas agency.
We often farm out major projects to specialized contractors for efficiency.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hire out - to allow someone to use something, or to be employed, for a fee
  • synonym: outsource - to obtain goods or services from an outside supplier; synonym for farm out
  • synonym: rent out - to allow someone to use something in return for payment
