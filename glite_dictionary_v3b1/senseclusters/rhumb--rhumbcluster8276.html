rhumb

noun
line crossing all meridians at the same angle
9.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / rÊŒm / (AmE), / rÊŒm / (BrE)
A line on the surface of a sphere, especially the earth, that cuts all meridians at the same angle; a path followed by maintaining a constant compass direction.

Examples

A rhumb line
On a map projection, a straight line represents a rhumb rather than a great circle.
Pilots sometimes choose a rhumb to simplify navigation, even if it's not the shortest route.
The ship followed a rhumb across the Atlantic to maintain a steady heading.

Synonyms
