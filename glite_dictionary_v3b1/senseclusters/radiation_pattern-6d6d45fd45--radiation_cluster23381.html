radiation pattern

noun
graphical depiction of antenna radiation by direction
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən ˈpætɚn / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən ˈpætn̩ / (BrE)
a graphical or mathematical depiction of the variation in strength of radiation emitted by an antenna, as a function of direction

Examples

Antenna radiation pattern
A polar graph is often used to display the radiation pattern of a broadcasting station.
Each antenna type produces a unique radiation pattern that determines its directional coverage.
The engineer analyzed the radiation pattern to optimize the antenna's performance.
