diffusiveness

noun
degree of spread
9.1
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfjuːsɪvnəs /; / dɪˈfjusɪvnəs / (AmE), / dɪˈfjuːsɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being widely spread out or dispersed over a large area (such as information, data, or a substance)

Examples

Diffusiveness of information
The diffusiveness of the data made it difficult to identify any clear trends.
A high level of diffusiveness in the prior means the model is less influenced by initial assumptions.
Researchers noted the diffusiveness of the distribution when analyzing the experimental results.
The diffusiveness of the perfume filled the entire room within minutes.
Scientists studied the diffusiveness of pollutants in the atmosphere.
The diffusiveness of her writing style made the main point hard to find.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concentration - the quality of being closely gathered or focused
  • synonym: dispersion - the process of spreading things over a wide area
  • synonym: spread - the extent to which something is distributed over an area
