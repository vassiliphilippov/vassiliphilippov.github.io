epideictic oratory

noun
formal speech of praise or blame
7.8 9.2
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˈdaɪktɪk ɔˈrætəri / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈdaɪktɪk ɒˈrətəri / (BrE)
a formal type of speaking intended to praise or blame a person, group, or institution, often used in ceremonies or commemorations

Examples

Epideictic oratory in ceremonies
Graduation ceremonies frequently feature epideictic oratory to honor the achievements of students.
In ancient Athens, epideictic oratory was used both to praise heroes and to condemn traitors.
Pericles' renowned funeral speech is often cited as a classic example of epideictic oratory.

Related Terms

  • synonym: eulogy - a speech or written tribute praising someone, especially after death
  • antonym: invective - vehement or violent denunciation, censure, or reproach
