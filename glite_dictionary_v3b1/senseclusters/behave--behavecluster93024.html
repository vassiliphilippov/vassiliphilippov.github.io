Synonyms

  • comport
    8.0 1.11/m
    comport means behaving according to expected standards in formal contexts, while behave is the common word for acting properly.
  • conduct
    3.7 4.68/m
    conduct describes how someone behaves, often in formal settings or reports, while behave is the everyday verb for acting.
  • act
    1.0 68/m
    act means to behave sometimes as a role or to pretend, while behave usually means how someone truly conducts themselves.
  • do
    1.0 222/m
    do is a very general verb used for many actions, while behave focuses on how someone acts, often with manners or rules.
  • bear
    6.7 3.10/m
    bear often describes one's manner or calm attitude, while behave covers broader actions and polite conduct.
  • carry
    3.0 2.70/m
    carry shows posture or manner the body gives others, while behave is about actions and polite conduct.
  • deport
    6.1 0.09/m
    deport in this sense is formal and rare, meaning to conduct oneself in line with rules, while behave is common everyday usage.
