interline

verb
to add between lines
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈlaɪn / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈlaɪn / (BrE)
to insert written or printed words between the lines of existing text

Examples

To interline corrections
The teacher asked students to interline their corrections above the original sentences.
Editors often interline suggestions to help clarify the author's intent.
You should interline any missing words so the document is complete.

Related Terms

  • synonym: annotate - to add notes or comments to a text
  • antonym: delete - to remove written or printed words
