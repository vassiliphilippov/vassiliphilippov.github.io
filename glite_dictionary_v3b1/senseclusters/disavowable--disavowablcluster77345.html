disavowable

adjective
able to be disavowed or denied
Pronunciation: / dɪsəˈvaʊəbəl / (AmE), / dɪsəˈvaʊəbl / (BrE)
Capable of being denied or rejected; able to be disavowed.

Examples

A disavowable action
He ensured his actions were always disavowable to avoid personal responsibility.
Some statements are simply not disavowable once they have been made public.
The contract included a disavowable clause in case of unforeseen events.

Related Terms

  • antonym: indisputable - impossible to disavow or deny
