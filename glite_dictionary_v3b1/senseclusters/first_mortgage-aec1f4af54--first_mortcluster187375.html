first mortgage

noun
primary mortgage with top priority
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈfɝst ˈmɔrɡɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈfɜːst ˈmɔːɡɪdʒ / (BrE)
A mortgage that has priority over all other mortgages and liens except for those mandated by law.

Examples

The first mortgage lender
If you default, the lender holding the first mortgage can foreclose before other creditors.
She took out a first mortgage to buy the house, ensuring her loan had top priority.
The bank's first mortgage must be paid before any junior mortgages in case of default.
