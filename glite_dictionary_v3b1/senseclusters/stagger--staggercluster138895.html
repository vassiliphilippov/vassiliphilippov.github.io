An unsteady or uneven way of walking, as if about to fall.

Examples

A drunken stagger
After drinking too much, his walk turned into a clumsy stagger across the room.
He broke into a stagger after twisting his ankle on the uneven pavement.
The patient's stagger was a sign of dizziness from the illness.

Synonyms

  • stumble
    2.2 0.26/m
    stumble is a quick loss of balance from tripping or catching the foot, while stagger is a slow, wobbling walk as if about to fall.
  • lurch
    4.7 0.72/m
    lurch is a sudden uncontrolled jump or sway, while stagger describes a continuing, unsteady walk that looks like falling.

Related Terms
