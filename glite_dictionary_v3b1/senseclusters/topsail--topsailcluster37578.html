topsail

noun
sail above the lowest sail on a mast
7.7 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑpˌseɪl, ˈtɑpsəl / (AmE), / ˈtɒpseɪl, ˈtɒpsəl / (BrE)
a sail set immediately above the lowermost sail on a mast, supported by the topmast, or either of a pair of such sails on a square-rigged ship

Examples

The topsail on mast
He climbed the rigging to secure the topsail before the storm hit.
The crew quickly raised the topsail to catch the shifting wind.
With the topsails unfurled, the ship picked up speed across the open sea.
