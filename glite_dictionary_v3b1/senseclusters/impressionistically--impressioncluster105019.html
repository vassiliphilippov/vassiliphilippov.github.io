impressionistically

adverb
based on impressions
8.9
Pronunciation: /ɪm.prə.ʃə.ˈnɪs.tɪ.kəl.iː/ (AmE), /ɪm.prə.ʃə.ˈnɪs.tɪ.kəl.iː/ (BrE)
in a way that is based on general feelings or impressions rather than precise facts or details

Examples

Impressionistically describe memories
The author wrote impressionistically about her childhood, focusing on emotions rather than specific events.
He painted the landscape impressionistically, capturing the mood instead of every detail.
During the interview, she spoke impressionistically about her experiences, giving a sense of the atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • synonym: subjectively - in a way influenced by personal feelings or opinions
