obsessive-compulsive personality

noun
personality marked by compulsive orderliness and repetition
Pronunciation: / əbˌsɛsɪv kəmˈpʌlsɪv ˌpɜrsəˈnæləti / (AmE), / əbˌsɛsɪv kəmˈpʌlsɪv ˌpɜːsəˈnalɪti / (BrE)
a personality type characterized by a strong need to repeat certain acts or rituals, often with excessive attention to order, perfection, and control

Examples

Obsessive-compulsive personality traits
A strong obsessive-compulsive personality can make it hard to adapt to change in daily routines.
Her obsessive-compulsive personality often leads her to repeat tasks until they feel 'just right.'
People with an obsessive-compulsive personality may feel compelled to keep everything in perfect order.

Related Terms

  • synonym: obsessive-compulsive disorder - an anxiety disorder characterized by recurrent, unwanted thoughts (obsessions) and repetitive behaviors (compulsions)
