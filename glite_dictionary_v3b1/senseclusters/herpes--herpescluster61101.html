herpes

noun
viral disease causing skin or mucous eruptions
4.6 3.4
Pronunciation: /ˈhɜːr.piːz/ (AmE), /ˈhɜː.piːz/ (BrE)
A group of viral diseases marked by painful skin or mucous membrane eruptions, especially those caused by herpes simplex or herpes zoster viruses.

Examples

Painful herpes outbreak
He worried about the possibility of contracting herpes after learning about its symptoms.
Oral herpes commonly causes cold sores around the mouth and lips.
The doctor diagnosed the skin rash as a form of herpes and prescribed antiviral medication.
