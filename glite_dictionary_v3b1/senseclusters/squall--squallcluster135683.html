squall

verb
blow with sudden, violent wind
Pronunciation: / skwɑl / (AmE), / skwɔːl / (BrE)
(of wind) to blow suddenly and violently, typically with a sudden increase in intensity.

Examples

Winds squall suddenly
If the weather threatens to squall, experienced sailors quickly adjust their sails.
Storm clouds gathered overhead, and it started to squall unexpectedly, forcing the sailors to take cover.
The wind began to squall just as our boat reached the open water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gust - a sudden strong rush of wind
