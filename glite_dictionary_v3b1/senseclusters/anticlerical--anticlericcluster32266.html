anticlerical

adjective
opposed to clergy power
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌæntiˈklɛrɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌæntiˈklɛrɪkəl / (BrE)
opposed to the influence or power of religious leaders, especially in political or public affairs

Examples

Anticlerical laws
The new laws reflected an anticlerical attitude among the government officials.
Many writers in the nineteenth century expressed anticlerical views in their works.
The party's anticlerical policies led to conflicts with religious institutions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clerical - relating to religious leaders or the clergy
  • synonym: secular - not connected with religious or spiritual matters
