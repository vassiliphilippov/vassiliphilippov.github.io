engrave

verb
cut a design or letters into a surface
2.5 4.2
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈgreɪv/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈgreɪv/ (BrE)
to cut or carve words, designs, or pictures into the surface of something (such as metal, wood, or glass), sometimes to make prints

Examples

To engrave a name
Ancient craftsmen would engrave detailed scenes onto the surface of bronze vases.

Old books sometimes feature illustrations engraved on metal plates.
She used special tools to engrave the image for the printing process.

  • antonym: erase - to remove or rub out something written or engraved
  • synonym: etch - to cut a design into a surface using acid or a sharp tool
  • synonym: inscribe - to write or carve words or symbols on something
