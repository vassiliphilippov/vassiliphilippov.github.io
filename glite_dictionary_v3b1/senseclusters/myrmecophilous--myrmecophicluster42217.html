myrmecophilous

adjective
living symbiotically with ants
Pronunciation: / mɜrˌmɛkəˈfɪləs / (AmE), / mɜːˌmɛkəˈfɪləs / (BrE)
(of an organism) living in close association with ants, especially in a mutually beneficial relationship.

Examples

Myrmecophilous organisms
A number of fungi have developed myrmecophilous traits to take advantage of ant colonies.
Some beetles are myrmecophilous, living inside ant nests and benefiting from the protection the colony provides.
The myrmecophilous butterfly larvae secrete substances that attract and reward ants for tending them.
