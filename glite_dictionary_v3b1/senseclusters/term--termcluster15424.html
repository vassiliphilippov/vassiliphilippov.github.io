term

noun
a fixed or limited period of time
4.4
Pronunciation: / tɝm /; / tɝːm / (AmE), / tɜːm / (BrE)
a fixed or limited period of time during which something happens, such as a school session, political office, prison sentence, or pregnancy

Examples

A school term
Sarah completed her first term at university with excellent grades.
The judge gave him a five-year term in prison for the crime.
The president’s term of office will end next year.
Babies born at term generally have better health outcomes than those born prematurely.
She carried all three of her children to term without any complications.
The doctor explained that the pregnancy had reached term and labor could begin any day.

Related Terms

  • synonym: duration - the length of time that something lasts
  • synonym: full-term - having completed the normal period of gestation
  • degree higher: overdue - past the expected time of birth
  • synonym: period - an amount of time with a beginning and an end
  • antonym: preterm - born before the end of gestation; premature
