prefabricate

verb
manufacture building sections in advance
4.5 4.8
Pronunciation: /priːf.ˈæ.brɪ.keɪt/ (AmE), /priːf.ˈæ.brɪ.keɪt/ (BrE)
to manufacture or produce parts of something in advance, usually in a factory, so they can be quickly assembled later (especially in construction)

Examples

To prefabricate building parts
Modern builders often prefabricate major components to save time and reduce costs.
The company will prefabricate the wall panels before transporting them to the construction site.
They chose to prefabricate the frames in a factory environment for greater precision.
The company prefabricated the window frames in the factory to speed up on-site installation.
Many modern homes are prefabricated to reduce construction time and costs.
They decided to prefabricate the walls so the building could be assembled in just a few days.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assemble - put together parts or components to form a complete unit
  • synonym: construct - to build something by putting parts together
