bluefin

noun
a large tuna species
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˈbluːˌfɪn / (AmE), / ˈbluːˌfɪn / (BrE)
a large species of tuna, found in mainly temperate seas, known for its size and commercial value

Examples

A giant bluefin
A single bluefin can weigh more than 600 kilograms at full maturity.
Overfishing has drastically reduced the bluefin population in the Atlantic Ocean.
The chef prepared sushi using fresh bluefin caught off the coast of Japan.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bluefin tuna - the full term for the species, a large tuna with blue coloring
