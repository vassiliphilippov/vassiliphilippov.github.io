Fragaria chiloensis

noun
wild strawberry species; strawberry ancestor
Pronunciation: / frəˈɡɛriə kaɪloʊˈɛnsɪs / (AmE), / frəˈɡɛːrɪə kʌɪləʊˈɛnsɪs / (BrE)
A species of wild strawberry native to the western United States and South America, known as a source of many cultivated strawberry varieties.

Examples

Fragaria chiloensis plant
Researchers study Fragaria chiloensis to improve disease resistance in commercial strawberries.
The cultivated strawberry is a hybrid descended from Fragaria chiloensis and other species.
Wild populations of Fragaria chiloensis grow along the Pacific coast.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Fragaria virginiana - another wild strawberry species native to North America
  • synonym: strawberry - the common garden fruit of the Fragaria genus
