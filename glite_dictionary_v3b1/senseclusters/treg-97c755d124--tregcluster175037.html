Treg

noun
immune regulatory cell
Pronunciation: / ti rɛg / (AmE), / ti rɛg / (BrE)
a type of immune cell, called a regulatory T cell, that helps control and suppress the body's immune response

Examples

Tregs immune cells
Scientists are studying how Tregs can be used to treat autoimmune diseases.
A decrease in Treg numbers may lead to an overactive immune response.
Therapies that boost Treg activity are being developed for allergies and inflammation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lymphocyte - a type of white blood cell involved in the immune response
