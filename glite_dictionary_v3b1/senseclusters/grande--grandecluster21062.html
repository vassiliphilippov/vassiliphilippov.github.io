grande

noun
Spanish nobleman
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrɑːndeɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡrændeɪ / (BrE)
a member of the highest rank of Spanish or Portuguese nobility, historically a powerful nobleman with special privileges

Examples

Spanish grande
The grande attended the royal court wearing his ancestral robes and chain of office.
As a grande, she was entitled to special honors and privileges at official ceremonies.
Many historical palaces in Spain were once owned by a powerful grande and his family.

Related Terms

  • antonym: commoner - a person who is not a member of the nobility
  • synonym: grandee - a person of high rank or eminence, especially in Spain or Portugal
  • synonym: noble - a person of high social rank, especially from a family with a title
