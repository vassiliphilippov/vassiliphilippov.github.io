Seder

noun
Passover ceremonial dinner in Judaism
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪ.dər / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.də / (BrE)
A ceremonial dinner held on the first night (or both nights) of Passover to commemorate the Jewish Exodus from Egypt, featuring rituals and readings.

Examples

The Seder dinner
During the Seder, participants read from the Haggadah and eat symbolic foods.
Many families invite guests to their Seder to share in the celebration and rituals.
We joined our friends for their family Seder last year and learned about Passover traditions.
