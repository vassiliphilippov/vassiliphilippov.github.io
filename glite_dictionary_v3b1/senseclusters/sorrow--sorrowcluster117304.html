sorrow

verb
to feel deep sadness or grief
5.2 0.31/m
Pronunciation: / Ààs…ëÀêro ä / (AmE), / Ààs…ír…ô ä / (BrE)
to feel deep sadness or grief, especially for a loss or misfortune

Examples

To sorrow for a loss
He sorrowed in silence, not wanting to burden others with his pain.
It is natural to sorrow after facing such a devastating tragedy.
She continued to sorrow over the loss of her childhood friend, even years later.

Synonyms

  • grieve
    3.8 5.97/m
    grieve means feeling deep pain and loss inside, while sorrow names the strong sadness felt after losing someone or something.
  • mourn
    3.0 9.02/m
    mourn means show sadness and follow funeral customs for someone who died, while sorrow is the deep grief felt privately.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mourn - to show or feel deep sorrow or regret for something or someone who has died
  • antonym: rejoice - to feel or show great joy or delight
