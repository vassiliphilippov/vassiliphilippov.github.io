backstab

verb
to betray secretly
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌstæb / (AmE), / ˈbækˌstæb / (BrE)
to betray someone secretly, especially while pretending to be their friend or ally

Examples

To backstab a friend
He was shocked to learn that his closest friend had backstabbed him during the meeting.
Some colleagues will backstab others to get ahead in a competitive workplace.
After being backstabbed by her teammate, she found it hard to trust anyone at work.
She was shocked to learn that her friend would backstab her over a promotion.
In the world of politics, it is not uncommon for allies to backstab each other.
He warned me not to backstab my teammates if I wanted to keep their trust.

Related Terms

  • synonym: betray - to be disloyal or unfaithful to someone
  • antonym: support - to help or stand by someone
