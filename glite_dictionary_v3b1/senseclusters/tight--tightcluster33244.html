tight

adjective
strictly enforced; rigorous (of rules or procedures)
1.0 3.3
Pronunciation: /taɪt/ (AmE), /taɪt/ (BrE)
(of regulations, security, or procedures) strictly enforced; allowing little or no relaxation from rules or controls.

Examples

Tight security
Tight security measures were put in place ahead of the summit to ensure safety.
Admission is highly restricted, with tight controls on who enters and leaves the facility.
The company enforces tight dress code rules in the office at all times.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stringent - (of regulations or conditions) strict, precise, and exacting
