neurofibroma

noun
tumor of a nerve's fibrous covering
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˌnʊroʊfaɪˈbroʊmə / (AmE), / ˌnjʊərəʊfaɪˈbrəʊmə / (BrE)
a tumor consisting of fibrous tissue that develops from the sheath of a peripheral nerve

Examples

Multiple neurofibromas
Multiple neurofibromas are often associated with a genetic condition called neurofibromatosis.
Surgical removal of a neurofibroma may be necessary if it causes pain or nerve damage.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with a neurofibroma near his spinal cord.
