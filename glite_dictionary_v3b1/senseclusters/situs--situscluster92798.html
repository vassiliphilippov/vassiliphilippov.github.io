situs

noun
organ arrangement
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪtəs / (AmE), / ˈsaɪtəs / (BrE)
the position or arrangement of the internal organs in the body, especially when referring to congenital patterns

Examples

Situs of organs
The doctor explained that the patient's unusual situs could affect the surgery.
Medical students studied diagrams showing normal and abnormal situs of the organs.
A rare condition called situs inversus can complicate diagnosis and treatment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anatomy - the study of the structure of living things
  • synonym: situs inversus - a congenital condition in which the major organs are reversed or mirrored from their normal positions
