checkmate

noun
final chess move trapping king
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɛkˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛkˌmeɪt / (BrE)
a situation in chess where a player's king is in a position to be captured and there is no legal move to escape the threat, ending the game

Examples

Chess checkmate
After ten moves, his opponent had him in checkmate.
She realized it was checkmate and resigned from the game.
The grandmaster delivered a stunning checkmate in only twenty moves.

Related Terms

  • antonym: stalemate - a chess position where no legal move is possible but the king is not in check
