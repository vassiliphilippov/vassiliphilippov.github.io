deserted

adjective
empty and abandoned (of a place)
3.0 0.11/m
Pronunciation: / dÉªËˆzÉËtÉªd / (AmE), / dÉªËˆzÉœËtÉªd / (BrE)
(of a place) having been left by the people who lived or used to be there; empty and abandoned.

Examples

A deserted house
A deserted house stood at the end of the road, its windows boarded up.
The deserted village felt eerie and silent in the fading light.
We wandered through the deserted streets after the festival ended.

Synonyms

  • abandoned
    2.8 0.22/m
    abandoned is left empty by previous owners or users, while deserted often describes a place people have recently left.
  • uninhabited
    4.0 1.03/m
    uninhabited has no people living there, while deserted suggests that people once lived there but have left.
  • unpopulated
    4.8 0.30/m
    unpopulated has no people living there and may never have been settled, while deserted implies people left.
  • desolate
    6.8 2.73/m
    desolate is empty and bleak, often feeling harsh and lonely, while deserted simply means people have left.
  • unoccupied
    3.6 0.50/m
    unoccupied is not taken or controlled by any group or force, while deserted means people have left and the place is empty.

  • antonym: populated - filled with people; inhabited
