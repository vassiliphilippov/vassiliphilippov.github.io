agglutinate

verb
to clump or stick together
6.4 7.7
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈgluː.tɪ.neɪt/ (AmE), /ə.ˈgluː.tɪ.neɪt/ (BrE)
(of cells or particles, especially in biology or medicine) to stick together and form clumps.

Examples

To agglutinate blood
Incompatibility between blood types can cause donated blood to agglutinate dangerously.
The bacteria began to agglutinate when exposed to the immune serum.
When certain antibodies are present, the red blood cells may agglutinate rapidly in the test tube.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coagulate - to change to a solid or semi-solid state, often referring to blood
