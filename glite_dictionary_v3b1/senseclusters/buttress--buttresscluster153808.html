buttress

verb
support or reinforce with a buttress
7.3 0.20/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆbÊŒtrÉ™s / (AmE), / ËˆbÊŒtrÉ™s / (BrE)
to support or reinforce a structure with a projecting support built against its wall

Examples

To buttress a building
Engineers decided to buttress the bridge to prevent collapse during storms.
Massive stone supports were added to buttress the cathedral walls.
They had to buttress the old library to ensure its structural safety.

Synonyms

  • shore
    7.1 2.76/m
    shore props up a wall temporarily with solid supports placed against it, while buttress is a built projecting support fixed to the wall.
  • brace up
    5.5 0.00/m
    brace up supports something from below with extra timber or struts, while buttress is attached outward to strengthen a wall.
  • bolster
    4.8 0.62/m
    bolster adds soft or extra support to make something stronger or more secure, while buttress is a rigid external support built onto a wall.
