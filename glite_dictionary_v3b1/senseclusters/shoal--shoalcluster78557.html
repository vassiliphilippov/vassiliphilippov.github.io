shoal

noun
sandbank visible at low tide
9.9 6.3
Pronunciation: /ʃoʊl/ (AmE), /ʃəʊl/ (BrE)
a shallow area in a body of water, such as a sandbank, often dangerous for boats

Examples

A sandy shoal
At low tide, the shoal was teeming with birds searching for food.
The ship ran aground on a shoal just outside the harbor.
We avoided the shoal as we navigated the river in our boat.
A shoal formed at the mouth of the river, making navigation difficult for large vessels.
Fishermen often find plentiful fish around the edges of a shoal.
The boat ran aground on a hidden shoal near the coast.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deep - an area of deep water
  • synonym: reef - a ridge of rock, coral, or sand in a body of water, just above or below the surface
  • synonym: sandbank - a ridge of sand built up by currents in a river or sea
