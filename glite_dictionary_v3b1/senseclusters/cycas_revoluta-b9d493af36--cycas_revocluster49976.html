Cycas revoluta

noun
dwarf palmlike cycad of Japan
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪkəs rɛvəˈluːtə / (AmE), / ˈsʌɪkəs rɛvəˈluːtə / (BrE)
a small, palm-like cycad native to Japan that yields sago starch

Examples

The Cycas revoluta plant
Although called the sago palm, the Cycas revoluta is actually a cycad, not a true palm.
Farmers in Japan harvest Cycas revoluta for the starchy sago extracted from its trunk.
The Cycas revoluta is often grown as an ornamental plant in gardens worldwide.
