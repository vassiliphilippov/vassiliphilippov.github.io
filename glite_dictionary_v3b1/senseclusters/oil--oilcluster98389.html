oil

noun
a slippery liquid not miscible with water
1.1
Pronunciation: / ɔɪl / (AmE), / ɔɪl / (BrE)
a slippery or thick liquid that does not mix with water, used for lubrication, fuel, or cooking

Examples

Engine oil
Be careful not to spill oil on the kitchen floor, as it can be very slippery.
The mechanic checked the car’s oil and recommended changing it regularly.
Vegetable oil is commonly used for frying foods and making salad dressings.
Engineers regularly change the oil in cars to ensure smooth operation.
Many countries depend on oil exports to support their economies.
Spilled oil from the tanker polluted the coastline for miles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grease - a thick, oily substance used for lubrication
  • synonym: lubricant - A substance, such as oil or grease, used to reduce friction between surfaces
  • synonym: petroleum - A naturally occurring liquid found beneath the earth's surface, used to produce fuel and other products
  • antonym: water - the clear, colorless liquid (H2O) essential for most plant and animal life
