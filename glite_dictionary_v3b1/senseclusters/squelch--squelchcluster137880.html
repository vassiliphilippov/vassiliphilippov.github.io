squelch

verb
crush or compress forcefully
Pronunciation: / skwɛltʃ / (AmE), / skwɛltʃ / (BrE)
to crush or compress something forcefully, often so that it changes shape or condition

Examples

To squelch a bug
He squelched the mud in his hands, shaping it into a ball.
She stepped on the ripe tomato, causing it to squelch beneath her foot.
The engineer had to squelch the pipe to fit it into the narrow space.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crush - to press something so hard it is damaged or destroyed
  • synonym: squeeze - to firmly press something, often changing its shape
