magnetic head

noun
device for writing/reading magnetic signals
4.9 4.9
Pronunciation: /mæg.ˈnet.ɪk hed/ (AmE), /mæg.ˈnet.ɪk hed/ (BrE)
a device, typically an electromagnet, that converts electrical signals into magnetic patterns for recording or retrieving information on magnetic media such as tape or disk.

Examples

The magnetic head device
The magnetic head of the tape recorder needed to be cleaned to improve sound quality.
When repairing the cassette player, he replaced the worn-out magnetic head.
Without a functioning magnetic head, the device cannot read or write data on the tape.
