verticalism

noun
vertical integration policy
8.9
Pronunciation: /ˈvɜːr.tɪ.kəl.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈvɜː.tɪ.kəl.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the practice or policy of organizing a company so that it controls multiple stages of production and distribution within its industry

Examples

Industrial verticalism
The company's strategy of verticalism allowed it to control both manufacturing and distribution.
Critics argued that verticalism reduced competition in the market.
Through verticalism, the firm managed to oversee every step from raw materials to final sales.

Related Terms

  • antonym: horizontal integration - a company's expansion by acquiring similar companies at the same stage of production
  • synonym: vertical integration - a company's control over several stages of production or distribution
