sheet

verb
to adjust a sail
9.1
Pronunciation: / ʃiːt / (AmE), / ʃiːt / (BrE)
to fasten, trim, or adjust a sail using its sheet (rope) so the sail is set at the correct angle to the wind

Examples

To sheet the sail
The sailors sheeted the jib to catch more wind as they changed direction.
Before the storm hit, the captain quickly sheeted the sails to keep the boat steady.
It is important to sheet the mainsail properly for maximum speed and control.

Related Terms

  • antonym: loosen - to make a sail less tight or less controlled by the sheet
  • synonym: trim - to adjust the position of a sail for best performance
