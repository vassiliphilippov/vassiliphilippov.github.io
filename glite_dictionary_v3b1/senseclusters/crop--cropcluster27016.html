crop

verb
cut back plant growth
7.8 2.0
Pronunciation: /krɑp/ (AmE), /krɒp/ (BrE)
to produce or harvest crops; to prepare land or cut back plants for better growth

Examples

To crop the field
It is best to crop your roses in early spring for healthier blooms.
She carefully cropped the hedges to keep the garden tidy.
The gardener spent hours cropping the overgrown shrubs along the path.

The fertile valley crops abundantly each summer.
This kind of soil crops well even in dry years.

Properly cropping the land is essential for a good harvest.
The community worked together to crop the abandoned lot for their vegetable project.
