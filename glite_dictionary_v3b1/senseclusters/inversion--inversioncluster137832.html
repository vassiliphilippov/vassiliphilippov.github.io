inversion

noun
variation in music where intervals are reversed
5.8
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈvɜːrʒən / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
In music, a variation of a melody or part in which ascending intervals are replaced by descending intervals and vice versa.

Examples

Melodic inversion
Baroque music often features melodic inversion as a device in counterpoint.
The composer used inversion to add variety to the recurring theme.
When applying inversion, an upward leap in the melody becomes a downward leap of equal size.

Related Terms

  • synonym: retrograde - musical term where a melody is played backwards
