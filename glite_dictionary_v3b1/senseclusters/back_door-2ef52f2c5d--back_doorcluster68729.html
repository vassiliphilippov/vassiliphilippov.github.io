back door

noun
unauthorized computer system access method
2.5 4.8
Pronunciation: /bæk dɔːr/ /; / /bæk doʊr/ (AmE), /bæk dɔː/ (BrE)
a secret or unofficial way of getting access to a place, system, or opportunity, often by avoiding normal rules or security

Examples

A back door into systems
Security experts warned that the new software update might contain a hidden back door.
The company issued a patch to close a back door discovered in its operating system.
The hackers gained entry to the network through a back door left by a former employee.
He managed to get the software by finding a back door on the company's website.
The club's membership process is strict, so most people try to find a back door in.
There are concerns about politicians using the back door to gain influence over regulatory bodies.

  • synonym: loophole - a means of evading a rule without breaking it
  • synonym: trapdoor - a hidden software mechanism allowing secret access, mainly in computing
