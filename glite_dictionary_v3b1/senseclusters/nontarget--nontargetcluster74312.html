nontarget

adjective
not intended recipient
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˈtɑrɡɪt / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈtɑːɡɪt / (BrE)
not intended as the main focus or object of an action, effect, or process; not limited to specific targets

Examples

Nontarget effects
The chemical was carefully tested to minimize effects on nontarget organisms in the environment.
Researchers monitor nontarget areas to ensure the treatment does not spread unintentionally.
Farmers are concerned about the impact of pesticides on nontarget species such as bees and butterflies.
The laboratory used a nontarget approach to identify unexpected chemicals in the water sample.
Researchers prefer nontarget analysis when they want to discover substances they did not anticipate.
A nontarget screening can reveal hidden contaminants that targeted tests might miss.
