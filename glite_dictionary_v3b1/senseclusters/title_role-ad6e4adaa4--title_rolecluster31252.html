title role

noun
role named in the title of a work
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪtəl roʊl / (AmE), / ˈtaɪtəl rəʊl / (BrE)
the role of the character after whom a play, film, or other work is named

Examples

The title role character
Daniel Day-Lewis played the title role in "Lincoln".
Many actors dream of landing a title role early in their careers.
She was cast in the title role of "Carmen".

Related Terms

  • synonym: lead role - the most important role in a play or film
