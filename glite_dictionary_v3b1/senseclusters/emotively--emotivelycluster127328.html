emotively

adverb
with strong emotion
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmoʊtɪvli / (AmE), / ɪˈməʊtɪvli / (BrE)
in a way that shows or arouses strong emotions

Examples

To speak emotively
The speaker addressed the audience emotively, hoping to inspire action through her words.
He wrote emotively about his childhood experiences, moving many readers to tears.
During the debate, she responded emotively to criticism, showing how much the issue mattered to her.
The actor delivered his lines emotively, moving the audience to tears.
During the debate, she argued emotively rather than relying on facts alone.
He wrote emotively about the loss of his childhood home.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calmly - in a way that shows little or no emotion
  • synonym: passionately - in a way that shows strong feelings or emotions
