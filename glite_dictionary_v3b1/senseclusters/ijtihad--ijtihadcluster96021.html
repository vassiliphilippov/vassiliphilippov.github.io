ijtihad

noun
independent legal reasoning in Islamic law
9.9 0.06/m
Pronunciation: / iÀêd í.tiÀàh…ëÀêd / (AmE), / iÀêd í.t…™Ààh…ëÀêd / (BrE)
the process by which an Islamic scholar interprets Islamic law independently by reasoning, especially by deriving new legal rulings from the Koran and Hadith, rather than by relying solely on established authorities

Examples

ijtihad in Islamic law
Debates on whether to revive ijtihad have shaped Islamic legal reform in recent years.
He wrote extensively on ijtihad, emphasizing its importance in interpreting religious texts.
Modern scholars argue that ijtihad is necessary to address contemporary issues facing Muslim societies.

  • antonym: taqlid - acceptance of legal decisions from established authorities in Islamic law without independent reasoning
