granulate

verb
form granulating tissue (medical)
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡræn.jəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡran.jʊ.leɪt / (BrE)
(of a wound or ulcer) to form new tissue made up of tiny granules during healing.

Examples

Wounds granulate slowly
If the edges of the wound granulate well, recovery is usually faster.
Proper wound care ensures that tissues granulate and heal more effectively.
The patient's ulcer began to granulate within a week of proper treatment.
