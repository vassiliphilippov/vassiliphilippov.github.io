enjambment

noun
continuation of meaning across poetic lines
9.9 8.3
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈdʒæm.bənt/ /; / /ɪn.ˈdʒæm.mənt/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈdʒæm.bənt/ /; / /ɪn.ˈdʒæm.mənt/ (BrE)
the continuation of a sentence or phrase from one line of poetry to the next, without a pause at the end of the line

Examples

Poetry enjambment
Modern poetry often employs enjambment rather than traditional end-stopped lines.
Notice how the enjambment allows the thought to flow into the next line.
The poet uses enjambment to create a sense of urgency in the verse.
