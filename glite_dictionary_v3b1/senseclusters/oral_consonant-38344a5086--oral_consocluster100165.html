oral consonant

noun
a consonant made by air escaping through the mouth
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrəl ˈkɑnsənənt / (AmE), / ˈɔːrəl ˈkɒnsənənt / (BrE)
a consonant sound produced by releasing air only through the mouth, rather than the nose

Examples

oral consonant sound
English has many oral consonant sounds, such as 't', 'p', and 'k'.
The distinction between a nasal and an oral consonant can be subtle for language learners.
When you say the word 'cat', the 't' is an example of an oral consonant.

Related Terms
