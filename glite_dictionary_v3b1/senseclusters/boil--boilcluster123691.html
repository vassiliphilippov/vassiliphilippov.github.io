boil

verb
be agitated or turbulent (liquid/surface)
1.0 4.4
Pronunciation: /bɔɪl/ /; / /ˈbɔɪ.əl/ (AmE), /bɔɪl/ /; / /ˈbɔɪ.əl/ (BrE)
to be or become very agitated, angry, or excited (literally or figuratively)

Examples

To boil with anger
Emotions seemed to boil beneath the surface of the heated debate.
The river began to boil after the heavy rains, making crossing dangerous.
The water in the pot will boil if you leave it on high heat for too long.
His face turned red as he tried not to boil in front of his boss.
She began to boil with anger when she heard the unfair decision.
You could tell he was about to boil over after the repeated insults.

  • synonym: fume - to be visibly angry or agitated
  • synonym: seethe - to be filled with intense but unexpressed anger
