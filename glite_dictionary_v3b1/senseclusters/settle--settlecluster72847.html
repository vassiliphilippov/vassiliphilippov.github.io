settle

verb
take up residence and become established
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛt̬.əl /; / ˈsɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈsɛt.əl /; / ˈsɛtl̩ / (BrE)
to take up residence or establish a home in a place, often permanently or as a group

Examples

To settle in a town
After years of traveling, they settled in a small town by the sea.
Many families settled along the river during the gold rush.
The refugees hope to settle in a peaceful community.
After years of traveling, she decided to settle in Australia permanently.
Many immigrants hoped to settle the western territories and start new lives.
The region was settled by pioneers in the eighteenth century.
After years of moving, she finally settled in a quiet town.
He hopes to settle near his family once he finds a steady job.
Many young people travel after school before they settle into a career.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abandon - to leave a place, typically forever
  • synonym: colonize - to establish control over and inhabit a new place
  • synonym: inhabit - to live in or occupy a place
  • antonym: migrate - to move from one region or habitat to another
  • antonym: unsettle - to make someone anxious or not stable in their situation
