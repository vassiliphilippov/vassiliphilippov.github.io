Chinook

noun
member of a Native American people
Pronunciation: / tʃɪˈnʊk, ʃɪ- / (AmE), / tʃɪˈnʊk, ʃɪ- / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people of the Pacific Northwest, historically inhabiting the region around the mouth of the Columbia River.

Examples

Chinook tribe member
Archaeologists have uncovered ancient tools used by the Chinook in the region.
She is a descendant of the Chinook and proud of her heritage.
The Chinook were skilled traders and established settlements along the Columbia River.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Chinookan - the language historically spoken by the Chinook people
