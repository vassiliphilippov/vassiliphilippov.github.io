heliotropic

adjective
sun-loving
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˌhiːliəˈtroʊpɪk / (AmE), / ˌhiːliəˈtrəʊpɪk / (BrE)
(of plants or organisms) turning or growing toward sunlight

Examples

heliotropic plants
Many desert plants are heliotropic, growing best where sunlight is strongest.
The scientist studied the heliotropic behavior of sunflowers as they turned toward the sun.
Our garden is perfect for heliotropic species that need full sun all day.
Sunflowers are famous for their heliotropic behavior, following the sun across the sky.
The heliotropic movement of the plant helps it absorb more sunlight for photosynthesis.
Some gardeners choose heliotropic flowers to ensure their gardens look bright all day.
