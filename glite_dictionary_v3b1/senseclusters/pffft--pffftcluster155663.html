pffft

verb
to express disbelief
Pronunciation: / pfət / (AmE), / pfət / (BrE)
to make a short, dismissive sound to express disbelief, derision, or lack of interest

Examples

To pffft a story
When he heard the wild story, he just pffft and shook his head in disbelief.
She pffft at the idea that anyone could finish the project in one day.
If someone claims they never make mistakes, most people would simply pffft in response.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scoff - to speak or react with mockery or disbelief
  • synonym: snort - to make a sudden sound through the nose to show amusement or disbelief
