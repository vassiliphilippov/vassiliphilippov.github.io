roll around

verb
occur again at a regular time
Pronunciation: / roʊl əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / rəʊl əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
(of a time or event) to arrive or happen again, especially as part of a regular cycle

Examples

When summer rolls around
By the time summer rolled around, the whole family was ready for a vacation.
Tax season rolls around faster than you expect every year.
When her birthday rolls around, she always throws a big party.
It seems like my birthday rolls around faster every year.
Tax season rolls around in April, and everyone scrambles to get their paperwork done.
When the holidays roll around, we always gather together.
