boom

verb
make a deep hollow sound
3.6 2.9
Pronunciation: /buːm/ /; / /boʊm/ (AmE), /buːm/ (BrE)
to make a deep, loud, and resonant sound, or to hit something with great force producing such a sound (like thunder, a cannon, or a loud voice)

Examples

A voice booms loudly
A deep voice boomed from the back of the auditorium, silencing the crowd.
The church bells boom across the valley every Sunday morning.
Thunder boomed in the distance as the storm approached.
His deep voice boomed through the auditorium, catching everyone's attention.
The cannons boomed across the valley, shaking the ground beneath us.
Thunder boomed overhead as the storm rolled in.
She boomed her fist on the table, startling everyone in the room.
The batter boomed the ball over the left-field fence for a home run.
The drummer boomed the cymbals during the song's climax.

Related Terms

  • synonym: roar - emit a loud, deep, prolonged sound
  • synonym: rumble - to make a continuous low sound
  • synonym: smash - to hit something violently or with great force
  • degree lower: tap - to hit something lightly
  • synonym: thunder - to make a loud, deep, resonant sound like thunder
