noise level

noun
magnitude of random variation in a signal/data

(in electronics, data science, and statistics) the magnitude of random variation, interference, or unwanted fluctuations in a measured signal or dataset that reduces accuracy or clarity.

Examples

Low noise level in data
The engineer reduced the sensor's noise level to improve measurement accuracy during testing.
In preprocessing, they filtered the data to lower the dataset's noise level before modelling.
A higher room temperature increased the amplifier's thermal noise level, degrading the audio signal quality noticeably.
