figurative

adjective
not literal; using figures of speech
2.0 5.2
Pronunciation: /ˈfɪɡ.jə.rə.tɪv/ (AmE), /ˈfɪɡ.jə.rə.tɪv/ (BrE)
(of language) not literal; using words or expressions with a meaning that is different from the usual interpretation.

Examples

Figurative language
Her poem is full of figurative expressions describing the changing seasons.
In a figurative sense, he was chained to his desk.
Writers often use figurative language to create vivid images in the reader’s mind.

Related Terms

  • antonym: literal - (of words or meanings) taking words in their usual or most basic sense without metaphor or exaggeration
