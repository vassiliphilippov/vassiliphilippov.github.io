air horn

noun
carburetor air intake opening
Pronunciation: / ˈɛr hɔrn / (AmE), / ˈeə hɔːn / (BrE)
the air intake opening or passage of a carburetor, through which air enters before mixing with fuel

Examples

Carburetor air horn
A blocked air horn can cause poor engine performance and reduced fuel efficiency.
Inspection of the air horn revealed a buildup of dust restricting airflow to the carburetor.
Technicians recommend cleaning the air horn regularly to maintain optimal engine operation.
