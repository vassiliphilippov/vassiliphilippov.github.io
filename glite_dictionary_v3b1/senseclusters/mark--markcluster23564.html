mark

noun
gullible or easily deceived person
Pronunciation: / mɑːrk / (AmE), / mɑːk / (BrE)
a person who is easily deceived, especially in order to be cheated or taken advantage of

Examples

A mark in a scam
After losing money in the card game, he realized he had been the mark all along.
Swindlers often target elderly people as a potential mark for their schemes.
The hustler singled out the tourist as an easy mark and approached him with a scam.

Related Terms

  • antonym: con artist - a person who deceives others for personal gain
  • synonym: dupe - a person who is easily tricked
  • synonym: victim - a person harmed or tricked by someone else
