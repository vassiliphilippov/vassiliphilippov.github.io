capful

noun
the quantity a cap can hold
Pronunciation: / ˈkæp.fʊl / (AmE), / ˈkap.fʊl / (BrE)
The amount that a cap can hold, used especially for measuring small quantities of liquid.

Examples

One capful detergent
Add one capful of detergent to the washing machine for a small load.
He poured a capful of mouthwash and swished it around his mouth.
The recipe requires a capful of vanilla extract for extra flavor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: handful - the amount that fills the hand
  • synonym: spoonful - the amount that fills a spoon
