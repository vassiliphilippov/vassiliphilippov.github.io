garget

noun
a tall American herb with poisonous berries
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑrɡɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡɑːɡɪt / (BrE)
A tall, coarse perennial American herb with small white flowers and blackish-red berries on long drooping clusters; the young stems are edible, but the berries and root are poisonous.

Examples

Young garget shoots
Some people use young garget shoots as a cooked vegetable in spring.
The berries of the garget are highly toxic if ingested.
The garden was overrun by wild garget plants.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pokeweed - another name for the same plant, Phytolacca americana
