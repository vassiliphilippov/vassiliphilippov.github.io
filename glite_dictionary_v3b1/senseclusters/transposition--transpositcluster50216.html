transposition

noun
reversing the order or position
Pronunciation: / ˌtrænspəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtrænspəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of changing the order, position, or replacing things with each other

Examples

Transposition of letters
In linguistics, transposition of sounds can cause surprising shifts in pronunciation.
The transposition of the letters changed the word's entire meaning.
The puzzle required the transposition of the last two numbers.
A transposition of roles occurred when the manager became the assistant.
In genetics, transposition refers to the replacement of one DNA segment by another.
The transposition of two numbers in the code led to an error.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reversal - the act of turning something the opposite way
  • synonym: substitution - the act of replacing one thing with another
