weasel

verb
to avoid dishonestly
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈwiːzəl / (AmE), / ˈwiːzəl / (BrE)
to avoid doing something or escape responsibility in a sneaky or dishonest way

Examples

To weasel out
He tried to weasel out of helping with the project at the last minute.
Don't let her weasel her way out of taking responsibility for the mistake.
Some people will always find a way to weasel out of their promises.
He weaseled out of taking the blame for the broken window.
Whenever she is asked about her mistakes, she tends to weasel her way out of the conversation.
They tried to weasel out of their responsibilities at work, but their manager noticed.

  • synonym: dodge - to avoid something by moving quickly or cleverly
