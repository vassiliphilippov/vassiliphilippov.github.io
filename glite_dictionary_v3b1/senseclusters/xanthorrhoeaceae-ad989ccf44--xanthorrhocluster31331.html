Xanthorrhoeaceae

noun
a family of flowering plants in botany
Pronunciation: / zænˌθɔroʊˈisiː /; / zænˌθɔroʊˈiːəsiː / (AmE), / zænˌθɒrəʊˈiːsiː /; / zanˌθɒrəʊˈiːəsiː / (BrE)
a family of flowering plants that includes genera such as Asphodelus, Hemerocallis, and Xanthorrhoea, sometimes considered a separate family or included in Liliaceae in plant classification

Examples

The Xanthorrhoeaceae family
Botanists sometimes debate whether Xanthorrhoeaceae is a distinct family or part of Liliaceae.
Some taxonomists classify certain daylilies within the Xanthorrhoeaceae family.
Xanthorrhoeaceae are known for species such as the Australian grass tree.
Recent taxonomic revisions have changed the circumscription of Xanthorrhoeaceae in plant classification.
Some botanists no longer recognize Xanthorrhoeaceae as a separate family, preferring to divide it into smaller subfamilies.
The genus Xanthorrhoea is part of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family, known for its grass trees native to Australia.
