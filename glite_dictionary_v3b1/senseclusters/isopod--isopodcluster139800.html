isopod

noun
small crustacean with seven pairs of legs
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪsəˌpɑd / (AmE), / ˈʌɪsəpɒd / (BrE)
any of various small crustaceans, often with a flattened body and seven pairs of legs, found in terrestrial, freshwater, and marine environments

Examples

A small isopod
Marine scientists discovered a rare isopod beneath the rocks near the beach.
Some isopods can be found deep in the ocean, surviving under extreme pressure.
The pill bug, a familiar garden creature, is actually a type of isopod.
