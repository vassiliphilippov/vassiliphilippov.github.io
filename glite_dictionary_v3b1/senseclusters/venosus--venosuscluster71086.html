venosus

adjective
veined (scientific)
9.9
Pronunciation: /və.ˈnoʊ.səs/ /; / /vɪ.ˈnoʊ.səs/ (AmE), /və.ˈnəʊ.səs/ /; / /vɪ.ˈnəʊ.səs/ (BrE)
used in scientific names to mean 'veined' or 'having prominent veins', especially in reference to plants or animals.

Examples

Venosus leaf
The orchid was named venosus because of its strikingly veined petals.
In the species name Rana venosus, venosus describes the frog's distinctive skin pattern.
Botanists often use venosus to indicate leaves with prominent veins in Latin names.
