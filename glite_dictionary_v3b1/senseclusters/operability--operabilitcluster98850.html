operability

noun
suitability for surgery
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːpərəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌɒpərəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the suitability of a patient, organ, or tumor for surgical operation, or the likelihood that surgery can be performed successfully

Examples

Tumor operability test
The doctor evaluated the operability of the tumor before making a treatment plan.
Advances in technology have improved the operability of previously untreatable conditions.
Low operability may mean that surgery is too risky for the patient.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inoperability - the state of not being suitable for surgery
