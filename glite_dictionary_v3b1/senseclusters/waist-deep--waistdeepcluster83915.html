waist-deep

adverb
up to the waist in depth
Pronunciation: / weɪst ˈdip / (AmE), / weɪst ˈdiːp / (BrE)
reaching as high as a person's waist; up to the waist in depth.

Examples

Waist-deep in snow
She lost her balance while walking waist-deep in the snowdrift.
The children stood waist-deep in the lake, splashing one another.
We waded through the swamp until we were waist-deep in muddy water.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: knee-deep - up to the knees in depth
  • degree higher: neck-deep - up to the neck in depth
