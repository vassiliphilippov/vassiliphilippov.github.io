wiki

noun
collaborative website
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪki / (AmE), / ˈwɪki / (BrE)
a website or online platform that allows users to collaboratively add, edit, and organize content

Examples

A wiki website
Anyone can contribute to the wiki by adding new information or editing existing pages.
The company created an internal wiki to help employees share knowledge and resources.
She spent hours reading articles on her favorite wiki about world history.
Students created a wiki to share research and resources for their science project.
Anyone can edit the wiki to help keep the information up to date.
The company uses a private wiki for employees to document procedures and best practices.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blog - a website where an individual or group regularly posts content
  • synonym: encyclopedia - a reference work containing articles on various topics
