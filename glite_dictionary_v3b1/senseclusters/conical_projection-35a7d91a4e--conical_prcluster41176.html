conical projection

noun
type of map projection onto a cone
8.5 7.1
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑː.nɪ.kəl prə.ˈdʒek.ʃən/ /; / /ˈkoʊ.nɪ.kəl prə.ˈdʒek.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈkɒn.ɪ.kəl prə.ˈdʒek.ʃən/ /; / /ˈkəʊ.nɪ.kəl prə.ˈdʒek.ʃən/ (BrE)
a type of map projection in which the surface of a globe is projected onto a cone placed over part of the globe, often with its point above one of the Earth's poles

Examples

A conical projection map
Cartographers chose a conical projection to minimize distortion near the standard parallels.
In his geography class, Tom learned how a conical projection could depict the Earth's surface on a flat map.
The conical projection is often used for mapping regions with a large east-west extent, such as the United States.

Related Terms

  • synonym: map projection - any method of representing the surface of a sphere on a flat surface
