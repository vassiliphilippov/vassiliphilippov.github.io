scuff

verb
walk dragging the feet
5.2 3.0
Pronunciation: /skʌf/ (AmE), /skʌf/ (BrE)
to walk by dragging or scraping the feet along the ground, often making a rubbing sound

Examples

To scuff while walking
He tends to scuff when he is tired, making a soft shuffling noise across the carpet.
She scuffed through the fallen leaves, enjoying the crisp sound as she dragged her feet.
The children scuffed along the sidewalk, not eager to get to school.
He scuffed down the hallway, clearly not wanting to go to class.
After a long day, she scuffed her way into the living room and collapsed on the sofa.
The tired child scuffed along the sidewalk, dragging his feet behind him.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shuffle - to walk by dragging the feet along the ground without lifting them fully
  • antonym: stride - to walk with long, decisive steps
