dead load

noun
permanent load of a structure's own weight
6.4
Pronunciation: / dɛd loʊd / (AmE), / dɛd ləʊd / (BrE)
the permanent, static weight of a structure itself, not including any temporary or moving loads

Examples

The dead load weight
Steel beams are chosen based on the expected dead load they need to bear.
The building's foundation must support both the dead load and the anticipated live loads.
The engineer calculated the dead load of the bridge before considering the traffic it would carry.

Related Terms

  • antonym: live load - variable load on a structure from occupancy, movement, or external forces
