uterine contraction

noun
muscle tightening of the uterus during labor
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈjuːt̬ərɪn kənˈtrækʃən / (AmE), / ˈjuːtəriːn kənˈtrækʃən / (BrE)
a rhythmic tightening of the muscles of the uterus, especially during labor, that helps move the fetus toward the birth canal for delivery

Examples

Strong uterine contractions
Many women experience strong uterine contractions as their labor progresses.
Medications can sometimes be given to regulate uterine contractions during childbirth.
The doctor monitored the frequency of uterine contractions to assess the stage of labor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: labor contraction - a contraction of the uterine muscles during childbirth
  • antonym: relaxation - the state of being less tense, opposite of contracting
