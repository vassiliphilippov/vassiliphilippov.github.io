work-shy

adjective
unwilling to work or exert effort
Pronunciation: / ˈwɝkˌʃaɪ / (AmE), / ˈwɜːkˌʃaɪ / (BrE)
unwilling to work; avoiding work or effort

Examples

work-shy person
His teachers complained that he was work-shy and rarely completed assignments.
Some people unfairly label the unemployed as work-shy without considering their circumstances.
The company wanted to get rid of its work-shy staff who constantly avoided their duties.

Related Terms

  • antonym: industrious - diligent and hard-working
  • synonym: lazy - unwilling to work or use energy
