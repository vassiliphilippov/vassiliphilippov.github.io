disenchantment

noun
loss of false belief; disillusionment
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsɛnˈtʃæntmənt / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈtʃɑːntmənt / (BrE)
the feeling or process of becoming disappointed or disillusioned, especially after discovering that something is not as good or ideal as one believed

Examples

Deep disenchantment with politics
After years of believing in the system, she felt a deep disenchantment with politics.
His disenchantment with the corporate world led him to seek a new career.
The book captures the disenchantment that comes with growing up and facing reality.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disillusionment - the disappointment from discovering something is not as good as believed
  • antonym: enchantment - a feeling of great pleasure; being under a spell
