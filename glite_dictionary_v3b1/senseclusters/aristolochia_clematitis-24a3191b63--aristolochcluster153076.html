Aristolochia clematitis

noun
A European perennial plant with curved flowers
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌærɪstəˈloʊkiə klɛməˈtaɪtɪs / (AmE), / ˌærɪstəlˈəʊkɪə klɛməˈtɪtɪs / (BrE)
A perennial creeping plant, native to Europe, with curved yellow-green flowers; naturalized in parts of Britain and eastern North America.

Examples

Aristolochia clematitis plant
Aristolochia clematitis can be found growing wild along riverbanks in parts of eastern North America.
The curious flower shape of Aristolochia clematitis is said to resemble a small fetus.
The leaves of Aristolochia clematitis were once used in traditional herbal remedies.
