null

verb
to cancel out
7.8
Pronunciation: / nʌl / (AmE), / nʌl / (BrE)
to make something have no value, effect, or force; to cancel out or neutralize

Examples

To null a signal
The technician tried to null the signal to reduce interference.
The court decided to null the previous agreement due to new evidence.
Engineers worked to null the effects of background noise in the experiment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: invalidate - to make something not valid or legally acceptable
  • synonym: neutralize - to make something ineffective or harmless
  • antonym: reinforce - to make something stronger or more effective
