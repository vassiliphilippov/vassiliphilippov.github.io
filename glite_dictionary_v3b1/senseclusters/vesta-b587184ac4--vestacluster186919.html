Vesta

proper noun
Roman goddess of the hearth
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛstə / (AmE), / ˈvɛstə / (BrE)
(in Roman mythology) the goddess of the hearth and its sacred fire, whose flame was tended by Vestal Virgins; counterpart of the Greek goddess Hestia.

Examples

Roman goddess Vesta
As a counterpart to Hestia in Greek mythology, Vesta was revered throughout Rome.
The ancient Romans prayed to Vesta to protect their homes and families.
The sacred flame of Vesta was never allowed to go out in her temple.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Hestia - Greek goddess of the hearth and home
