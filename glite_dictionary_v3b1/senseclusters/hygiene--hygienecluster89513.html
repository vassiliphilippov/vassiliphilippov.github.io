hygiene

noun
data and cyber cleanliness
4.1
Pronunciation: /ˈhaɪ.dʒiːn/ (AmE), /ˈhaɪ.dʒiːn/ (BrE)
(in computing and data management) practices, processes, and standards for keeping data and digital systems accurate, consistent, secure, and free from errors or unwanted artefacts.

Examples

Data hygiene
Good hygiene for customer data prevents duplicates and improves reporting accuracy across teams.
The IT manager emphasized hygiene like patching and strong passwords to reduce security breaches.
Regular hygiene checks of the dataset removed outdated records before the analytics run.

Related Terms

  • synonym: data integrity - the accuracy and consistency of stored data over its lifecycle
  • synonym: data quality - the degree to which data is accurate, complete, consistent, and reliable
