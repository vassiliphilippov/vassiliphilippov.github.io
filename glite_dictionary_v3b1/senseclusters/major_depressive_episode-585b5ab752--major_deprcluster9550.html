major depressive episode

noun
period of severe depression; psychiatric term
3.4 4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪdʒər dɪˈprɛsɪv ˌɛpəˈsoʊd / (AmE), / ˈmeɪdʒə dɪˈprɛsɪv ˌɛpɪˈsəʊd / (BrE)
an episode of severe depression marked by symptoms such as lack of pleasure, lethargy, sleep disturbances, feelings of worthlessness, and morbid thoughts, occurring without a known physical cause

Examples

A major depressive episode
After her father passed away, she experienced a major depressive episode that lasted several months.
Early intervention is critical when a major depressive episode is identified in a patient.
The doctor diagnosed him with a major depressive episode following his sudden withdrawal from work and social activities.

  • synonym: clinical depression - a more persistent and general state of depression; synonym for major depressive disorder
