equilin

noun
steroid from mares
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɛkwɪlɪn / (AmE), / ˈɛkwɪlɪn / (BrE)
a steroid hormone found in the urine of pregnant mares, used in some hormone replacement medications

Examples

Equilin hormone
Doctors may prescribe equilin as part of hormone replacement therapy for menopausal women.
Researchers analyzed the effects of equilin on bone density in clinical trials.
The medication contains equilin derived from the urine of pregnant horses.

Related Terms

  • antonym: progesterone - a steroid hormone involved in the menstrual cycle and pregnancy
