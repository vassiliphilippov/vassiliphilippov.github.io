Gilbertian

adjective
absurdly comic or satirical (like Gilbert and Sullivan)
8.2 8.8
Pronunciation: /gɪl.ˈbɜːr.ʃən/ /; / /gɪl.ˈbɜːr.tɪ.ən/ (AmE), /gɪl.ˈbɜː.ʃən/ /; / /gɪl.ˈbɜː.tɪ.ən/ (BrE)
having the comic, satirical, witty, and sometimes absurd style of W. S. Gilbert, especially as seen in the Gilbert and Sullivan operas

Examples

Gilbertian comic style
Critics described the film’s plot as delightfully Gilbertian, with an array of eccentric characters and wild coincidences.
Her Gilbertian humor made even the most bizarre situations seem perfectly natural.
The novel unfolds in a Gilbertian manner, full of improbable twists and witty dialogue.
Her Gilbertian approach to satire made the operetta both clever and farcical.
Some critics describe his writing style as distinctly Gilbertian, full of witty rhymes and topsy-turvy logic.
The play's Gilbertian humor delighted both critics and audiences.
