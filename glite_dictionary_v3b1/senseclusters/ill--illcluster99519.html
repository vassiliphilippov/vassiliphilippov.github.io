ill

adverb
with difficulty; scarcely or hardly
1.3 3.4
Pronunciation: /ɪl/ (AmE), /ɪl/ (BrE)
in a bad, difficult, or unfavorable way; with difficulty or bad intent

Examples

Ill afford mistakes
Given our tight budget, we can ill afford any additional expenses this month.
She could ill conceal her disappointment when the results were announced.
The project was ill conceived and met with many obstacles from the start.
He refused to speak ill of his opponents, even after the heated debate.
It is considered disrespectful to think ill of the deceased.
She was hurt when she heard her friends had talked ill of her behind her back.
It ill becomes you to judge others so harshly.
She was ill prepared for the interview and struggled to answer the questions.
The proposal was ill conceived and quickly rejected by the board.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hardly - scarcely; only with great difficulty
  • antonym: well - in a good or satisfactory way (opposite of 'ill' in this sense)
