miombo

noun
African dry woodland
Pronunciation: / miˈɒmboʊ / (AmE), / miˈɒmbəʊ / (BrE)
a type of dry woodland ecosystem found in central and southern Africa, dominated by Brachystegia trees

Examples

A miombo woodland
Many species of birds and mammals live in the miombo woodlands of Mozambique.
Local communities depend on the miombo for firewood and building materials.
Conservationists are working to protect the unique biodiversity of the miombo ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rainforest - a dense, tropical forest with high rainfall
  • synonym: savanna - a grassy plain in tropical and subtropical regions, with few trees
