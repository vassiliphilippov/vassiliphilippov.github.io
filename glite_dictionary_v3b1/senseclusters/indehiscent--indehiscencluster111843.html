indehiscent

adjective
not opening to release seeds (botany)
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.dɪˈhɪs.ənt / (AmE), / ˌɪn.dɪˈhɪs.ənt / (BrE)
(of fruits or seed pods) not opening spontaneously at maturity to release their seeds.

Examples

Indehiscent fruit pods
Peanuts are an example of an indehiscent fruit, meaning they do not split open at maturity.
Some plants produce indehiscent pods to protect their seeds until they are eaten or decay.
The evolutionary significance of indehiscent fruits can be seen in their role in animal seed dispersal.
