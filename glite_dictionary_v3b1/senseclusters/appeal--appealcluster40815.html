appeal

verb
request earnestly for help or support
2.7 6.05/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàpil / (AmE), / …ôÀàpiÀêl / (BrE)
to make a serious or urgent request for help, support, or sympathy

Examples

To appeal for help
He appealed to his friends for support during the crisis.
She tried to appeal to the manager's sense of fairness.
The charity will appeal to the public for more donations next month.

Synonyms

  • plead
    3.9 13/m
    plead is an urgent, emotional request often spoken in desperate moments, while appeal is a more general serious request for help or support.
  • entreat
    8.1 0.73/m
    entreat is a formal and earnest plea used in serious or polite situations, while appeal is common for seeking help, support, or sympathy.
  • beseech
    8.2 1.62/m
    beseech is a strong, old-fashioned plea showing great feeling or need, while appeal is a neutral urgent request for help.
  • supplicate
    9.6 0.14/m
    supplicate is a humble, often religious plea to authority or God, while appeal is a wider, nonreligious urgent request for help.
  • seek help
    2.0 2.34/m
    seek help is to try to obtain practical assistance or advice, while appeal is a direct, serious request for help or sympathy.
