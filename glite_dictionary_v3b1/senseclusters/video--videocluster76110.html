video

noun
recording or transmission of visual images
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪdioʊ / (AmE), / ˈvɪdiəʊ / (BrE)
a recording or broadcast of moving visual images, especially for entertainment or information

Examples

A video clip
He prefers to watch documentaries on video rather than live television.
She appeared in a video that demonstrated the new product features.
The conference was broadcast live via video to viewers around the world.
Poor video quality can make it difficult to follow what’s happening on screen.
The video feed was interrupted during the storm, but we could still hear the audio clearly.
They adjusted the video settings to fix the brightness on the television.
Can you send me the video of the birthday celebration?
I watched a video of the concert last night.
She posted a funny video on social media this morning.

Related Terms

  • antonym: audio - the part of a broadcast that consists of sound
  • synonym: film - a movie; a story or event recorded by a camera
  • synonym: television - transmission of moving images via electronic signals
