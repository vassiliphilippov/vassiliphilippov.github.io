narcoleptic

noun
person with narcolepsy
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑr.kəˈlɛp.tɪk / (AmE), / ˌnɑː.kəˈlep.tɪk / (BrE)
a person who has narcolepsy, a condition marked by frequent and uncontrollable episodes of falling asleep during the day

Examples

A narcoleptic person
As a narcoleptic, he sometimes struggles to stay awake during meetings at work.
The doctor has seen many narcoleptic patients over the years, each with unique sleep challenges.
The support group welcomed a new narcoleptic who wanted to learn strategies for managing the disorder.

Related Terms

  • synonym: narcolepsy - chronic sleep disorder causing uncontrollable sleep attacks
