gangrene

noun
death of body tissue due to lack of blood or infection
8.3 6.9
Pronunciation: /ˈgæŋ.griːn/ (AmE), /ˈgæŋ.griːn/ (BrE)
the death and decay of body tissue, usually caused by infection or loss of blood supply

Examples

Severe gangrene
Doctors tried to save the limb, but gangrene had already set in.
If untreated, gangrene can spread quickly and become life-threatening.
Poor circulation due to diabetes increases the risk of gangrene.
Early treatment is crucial to prevent gangrene from spreading in the affected area.
If gangrene develops, amputation may be necessary to save the patient’s life.
The surgeon examined the foot and confirmed that gangrene had set in.

Related Terms

  • synonym: necrosis - the death of most or all of the cells in an organ or tissue
