condemn

verb
demonstrate someone's guilt
Pronunciation: / kənˈdɛm / (AmE), / kənˈdɛm / (BrE)
to demonstrate or prove the guilt of someone, often by their actions or behavior

Examples

Her actions condemned her
Her strange behavior finally condemned her in the eyes of everyone.
His absence from the scene condemned him more than any words could have.
The evidence condemned him beyond all reasonable doubt.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acquit - to declare someone not guilty
  • synonym: convict - to declare someone guilty, especially in a court of law
