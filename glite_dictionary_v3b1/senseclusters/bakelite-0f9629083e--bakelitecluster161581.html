Bakelite

noun
early synthetic plastic material
4.3 7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪkəˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈbeɪkəlaɪt / (BrE)
A synthetic thermosetting plastic, used especially in the past for electrical insulators, kitchenware, and telephones.

Examples

Bakelite plastic
Collectors prize vintage radios made of Bakelite for their unique colors and durability.
Early telephone receivers were often made from Bakelite because of its excellent insulating properties.
The first commercial plastics included Bakelite, which revolutionized the manufacturing of household items.
