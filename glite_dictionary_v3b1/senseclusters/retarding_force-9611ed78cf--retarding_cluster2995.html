retarding force

noun
force resisting motion, esp. in fluids
8.6
Pronunciation: / rɪˈtɑrdɪŋ fɔrs / (AmE), / rɪˈtɑːdɪŋ fɔːs / (BrE)
A force that opposes and slows down motion, especially through a fluid such as air or water.

Examples

Retarding force of air
Engineers must calculate the retarding force caused by air resistance when designing high-speed trains.
The parachute increased the retarding force acting on the skydiver, allowing a safer descent.
When a car moves through water, the retarding force significantly reduces its speed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drag - resistance by friction from air or water moving over a surface
  • synonym: friction - the resistance that one surface or object encounters when moving over another
