receive

verb
be the object of an action or treatment
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈsiːv/ /; / /rə.ˈsiːv/ /; / /riː.ˈsiːv/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈsiːv/ (BrE)
to experience or be the object of a particular action, treatment, or effect (such as praise, punishment, or injury)

Examples

To receive praise
Her performance received widespread acclaim from critics.
The project received harsh criticism from local officials.
The proposal received very little attention from the media.
Her latest novel was received with great enthusiasm by the critics.
His speech received a mixed reaction from the audience.
The proposal was not well received by the board members.
He received several minor injuries during the accident.
Many patients receive significant relief from the treatment.
She received little comfort after hearing the bad news.
