curfew

noun
official restriction on going outdoors after a set time
Pronunciation: / ˈkɜr.fju /; / ˈkɝː.fjuː / (AmE), / ˈkɜː.fjuː / (BrE)
an official rule or law that requires people to stay indoors after a certain time in the evening, or the time when this rule starts (sometimes marked by a signal like a bell)

Examples

A strict curfew
After the riot, the police enforced a strict curfew throughout the downtown area.
Teens often complain about their parents setting an early curfew on weekends.
The city imposed a curfew starting at 8 p.m. to ensure public safety.
At nine o'clock, the town's curfew rang loud across the rooftops.
Long ago, everyone in the city listened for the evening curfew.
The church bell served as the curfew that told villagers to return home.
As the curfew approached, the usually busy square became quiet and deserted.
The city declared that the curfew would begin at 10 p.m. each night.
We hurried home so we wouldn’t be caught outside after the curfew.
