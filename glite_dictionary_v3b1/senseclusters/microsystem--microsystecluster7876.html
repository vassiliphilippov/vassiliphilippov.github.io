microsystem

noun
small self-contained system
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪkroʊˌsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊˌsɪstəm / (BrE)
a small, self-contained system that operates as part of a larger system

Examples

Department microsystem
Each department functions as a microsystem within the larger organization.
In biology, a cell can be considered a microsystem with its own processes.
The school created several microsystems to manage different student activities efficiently.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ecosystem - a system formed by the interaction of a community of organisms with their environment
  • synonym: subsystem - a secondary or subordinate system within a larger system
