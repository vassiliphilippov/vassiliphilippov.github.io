unreasonably

adverb
to an excessive or unfair degree
4.1 1/m
Pronunciation: /  ånÀàriÀêz…ôn…ôbli / (AmE), /  ånÀàriÀêz…ôn…ôbli / (BrE)
in a way that is not fair, sensible, or reasonable; to an excessive or unjust degree

Examples

Act unreasonably harsh
She felt the referee was unreasonably harsh with his decisions.
The landlord raised the rent unreasonably despite the poor condition of the apartment.
The manager was unreasonably strict about minor mistakes.
It's unreasonably difficult to get a response from their customer service team.
She became unreasonably angry at the slightest criticism.
The landlord unreasonably raised the rent without any explanation.

Synonyms

  • unduly
    6.9 2/m
    unduly means to an excessive, unnecessary degree in formal contexts, while unreasonably covers unfairness or lack of sense more broadly.
  • unjustifiably
    7.0 0.2/m
    unjustifiably describes actions done without good reason or excuse, while unreasonably also includes being unfair or excessive.
  • unwarrantably
    9.9 0.00/m
    unwarrantably means lacking proper grounds or defence in formal speech, while unreasonably is broader and can mean unfair or excessive.
  • unfairly
    1.8 5/m
    unfairly means acting in a way that is not fair to others, while unreasonably can also mean not sensible or overly extreme.
  • unjustly
    5.2 2/m
    unjustly means in a wrongful or unfair way often used in serious contexts, while unreasonably can also imply lack of sense.
  • unconscionably
    8.9 0.1/m
    unconscionably means extremely unfair or morally wrong in a strong way, while unreasonably is less strong and more general.
  • immoderately
    7.6 0.07/m
    immoderately means doing something to an excessive, extreme degree often about quantity, while unreasonably can also mean not sensible.
