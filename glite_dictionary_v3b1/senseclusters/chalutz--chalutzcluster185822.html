chalutz

noun
Jewish pioneer in Palestine
Pronunciation: / xɑːˈluːts / (AmE), / xɑːˈluːts / (BrE)
a Jewish pioneer, especially one who settled in Palestine before the establishment of Israel

Examples

A chalutz in Palestine
The chalutz worked hard to cultivate the land in the early 20th century.
Many young people dreamed of becoming a chalutz and helping to build a new society.
As a chalutz, she faced many challenges but remained dedicated to her ideals.
