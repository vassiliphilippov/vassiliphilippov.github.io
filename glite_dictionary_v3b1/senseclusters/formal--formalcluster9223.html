formal

adjective
expressed in symbolic or simplified form
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrməl /; / ˈfɔr.məl / (AmE), / ˈfɔːməl /; / ˈfɔː.məl / (BrE)
relating to strict rules, logic, or symbolic representation, especially in mathematics or science

Examples

Formal logic rules
Computer science often uses formal languages to define the structure of programming syntax.
In mathematics, a formal proof is constructed using precise symbolic notation and logical rules.
The philosopher presented a formal argument using logical symbols and strict definitions.
A formal proof must follow a precise sequence of logical steps.
His argument was both rigorous and formal, relying solely on deductive reasoning.
The study of formal systems is fundamental to mathematical logic.

  • antonym: informal - not formal; lacking symbolic or predefined structure
  • antonym: intuitive - based on feelings or instinct rather than strict logic
  • synonym: symbolic - using symbols to represent concepts or ideas
