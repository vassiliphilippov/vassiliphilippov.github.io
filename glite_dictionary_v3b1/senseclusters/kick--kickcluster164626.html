kick

noun
rhythmic leg movement in swimming or exercise
Pronunciation: / kɪk / (AmE), / kɪk / (BrE)
a rhythmic or forceful movement of the legs or feet, or the act of striking with the foot (including in sports, exercise, or swimming)

Examples

A high kick

Proper coordination of the arm stroke and the kick is essential in the breaststroke.
The swimmer's kick helps propel her quickly through the water.
He gave the door a hard kick out of frustration.
She scored a goal with a perfectly timed kick.
The referee awarded a free kick to the home team.
