to be present or occur at the same time as something, or to exist together with something else

Examples

Feelings accompany news
A sense of excitement accompanied the announcement of the new project.
Fatigue frequently accompanies a high fever in many illnesses.
Loud thunder often accompanies lightning during a storm.

Synonyms

  • go with
    2.2 6.10/m
    go with is used when something appears or matches another thing, while accompany is a general verb for existing or happening together.
  • come with
    1.3 13/m
    come with normally means an item is included or supplied with another, while accompany means simply to be present or occur together.
