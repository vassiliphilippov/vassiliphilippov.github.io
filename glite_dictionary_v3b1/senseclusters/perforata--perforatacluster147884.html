perforata

noun
group with perforations
Pronunciation: / ˌpɜrfəˈreɪtə / (AmE), / ˌpɜːfəˈreɪtə / (BrE)
a group or species, especially in taxonomy, characterized by having perforations or porous structures

Examples

Perforata coral species
The coral family perforata is distinguished by its porous skeleton.
Scientists classified the new species under perforata due to its unique perforated structure.
Members of perforata are often found in marine environments with abundant calcium deposits.
