pathological process

noun
body process caused by disease
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˌpæθəˈlɑdʒɪkəl ˈprɑsɛs / (AmE), / ˌpæθəˈlɒdʒɪkəl ˈprəʊsɛs / (BrE)
an organic process in the body that occurs as a result of disease

Examples

A pathological process in body
Cancer is considered a serious pathological process involving uncontrolled cell growth.
Doctors examined the tissue to determine if a pathological process was present.
Inflammation is a pathological process that can result from infection or injury.

Related Terms

  • antonym: physiological process - a normal function or activity in a living organism
