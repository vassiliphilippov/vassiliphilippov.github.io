indicatively

adverb
fact-stating manner
6.1
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈdɪk.ə.tɪv.liː/ /; / /ɪn.ˈdɪk.tɪv.liː/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈdɪk.ə.tɪv.liː/ /; / /ɪn.ˈdɪk.tɪv.liː/ (BrE)
in a manner that expresses facts or beliefs, rather than doubts, wishes, or commands.

Examples

Indicatively states facts
She answered the question indicatively, providing only the facts she knew for certain.
The report was written indicatively, focusing on what had actually happened.
He spoke indicatively about his achievements, avoiding speculation or exaggeration.

Related Terms

  • synonym: declaratively - in a way that makes a statement or declaration
  • antonym: imperatively - in a way that gives a command or order
