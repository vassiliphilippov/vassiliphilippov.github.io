reflect

verb
show or express (a quality, attribute, etc.)
1.1
Pronunciation: / rɪˈflɛkt / (AmE), / rɪˈflɛkt / (BrE)
to show, express, or indicate a quality, feeling, or condition

Examples

Reflects his mood
His writing reflected his life experiences and personal journey.
Poor attendance often reflects a lack of interest in the event.
The way she treats others reflects her compassionate nature.
A company's products reflect its commitment to quality.
His dedication to the team reflects his strong work ethic.
The way children behave often reflects the values taught at home.
Her kindness reflects well on her upbringing.
The poor condition of the equipment reflects badly on the school's management.
Your behavior reflects the values you were taught as a child.

Related Terms

  • synonym: betray - to reveal or show unintentionally
  • antonym: conceal - to hide or keep something secret
  • synonym: demonstrate - show clearly by giving proof or evidence
  • synonym: indicate - to point out or show something
  • synonym: suggest - to imply or express indirectly
