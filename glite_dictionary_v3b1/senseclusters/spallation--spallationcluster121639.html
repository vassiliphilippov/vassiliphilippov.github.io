spallation

noun
fragment ejection process
Pronunciation: / spəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / spəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process in which fragments are ejected from a material due to impact, stress, or high-energy particles

Examples

Rock spallation fragments
Scientists observed spallation when the rock was struck by a powerful laser.
The spallation of the metal surface created tiny fragments that were collected for analysis.
In nuclear physics, spallation can occur when high-energy particles hit atomic nuclei.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fragmentation - the process of breaking into pieces
