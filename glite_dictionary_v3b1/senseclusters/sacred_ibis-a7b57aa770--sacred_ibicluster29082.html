sacred ibis

noun
African wading bird venerated by Egyptians
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪ.krəd ˈaɪ.bɪs / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.krɪd ˈaɪ.bɪs / (BrE)
a large African wading bird (Threskiornis aethiopicus) with white plumage and a bare black head and neck, venerated by the ancient Egyptians

Examples

A sacred ibis bird
A flock of sacred ibises waded along the banks of the Nile at sunset.
The sacred ibis was commonly depicted in ancient Egyptian art and hieroglyphics.
The museum’s ornithology section features a well-preserved sacred ibis specimen.
