emanationist

adjective
relating to emanation doctrine
9.9
Pronunciation: /e.mə.ˈneɪ.ʃən.ɪst/ (AmE), /e.mə.ˈneɪ.ʃən.ɪst/ (BrE)
relating to or supporting the doctrine that all things originate from a primary source by a process of emanation.

Examples

Emanationist theory
The philosopher's emanationist views shaped his understanding of the universe's origin.
Some ancient religions are based on emanationist principles, seeing all existence as flowing from a single source.
Her essay compared emanationist theories with other explanations for the creation of the world.

Related Terms

  • antonym: creationist - relating to the belief that the universe and life originate from specific acts of creation
  • synonym: emanative - relating to emanation or the act of flowing out from a source
