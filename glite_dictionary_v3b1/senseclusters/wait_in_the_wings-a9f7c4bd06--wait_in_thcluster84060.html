wait in the wings

verb
be ready for an opportunity
8.9
Pronunciation: / weɪt ɪn ðə wɪŋz / (AmE), / weɪt ɪn ðə wɪŋz / (BrE)
to be ready and prepared to take action or assume a role when the opportunity arises, often while staying out of the spotlight

Examples

To wait in the wings patiently
Although he was not the main team leader, Tom continued to wait in the wings for his moment to shine.
Several ambitious employees wait in the wings hoping to step into the manager's position.
She had been waiting in the wings for years before finally taking over as CEO.
