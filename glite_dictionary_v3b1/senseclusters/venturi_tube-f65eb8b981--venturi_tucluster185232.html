Venturi tube

noun
flow-measuring tube with constriction
Pronunciation: / vɛnˈtʊri tuːb / (AmE), / vɛnˈtjʊəri tjuːb / (BrE)
A short tube with a constricted throat inserted in a pipeline, used to measure the flow rate of a liquid through a decrease in pressure.

Examples

A Venturi tube device
A Venturi tube works by creating a pressure difference that correlates to the liquid's flow rate.
By installing a Venturi tube in the pipeline, engineers accurately determined the water volume passing through.
The Venturi tube is commonly used in industrial flow measurement systems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flowmeter - an instrument for measuring the flow of liquids or gases
