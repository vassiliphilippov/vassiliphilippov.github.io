uphill

noun
an upward slope of a hill or road
2.4 0.42/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆÊŒpËŒhÉªl / (AmE), / ËˆÊŒp.hÉªl / (BrE)
An upward slope or incline, especially of a hill or road.

Examples

A steep uphill
After a long uphill, we finally reached the top of the trail.
That section of the race includes a steep uphill that challenges even experienced runners.
The cyclists struggled as they rode up the uphill toward the summit.

Synonyms

  • ascent
    4.2 0.84/m
    An ascent is a long, steady upward path often used for walking or driving, while uphill refers to the slope of a hill or road.
  • climb
    1.4 3.75/m
    A climb is a steep section you go up on foot or by bike, while uphill describes the slope itself.
  • rise
    2.4 11/m
    A rise is any upward change or small height in land or water, while uphill means a physical upward slope.
  • slope
    1.4 14/m
    A slope is any slanting piece of land up or down, while uphill specifically means the upward side of that slope.
