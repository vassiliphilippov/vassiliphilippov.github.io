break

noun
a short pause from activity
1.5
Pronunciation: / breɪk / (AmE), / breɪk / (BrE)
a short period of rest or pause from work or activity; an interruption in continuity

Examples

A lunch break
After working for hours, he needed a short break to rest his eyes.
Let's take a break and grab some coffee before we continue.
Students have a fifteen-minute break between classes every morning.
Let's take a break after this meeting to grab some coffee.
She enjoyed a quick break in the garden during her busy day.
The students eagerly waited for their lunch break to start.
He asked for a break in the meeting to gather his thoughts.
Let's take a break before we start the next session.
The news was followed by a short commercial break.
