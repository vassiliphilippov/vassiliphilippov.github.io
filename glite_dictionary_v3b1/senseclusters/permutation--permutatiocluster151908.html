permutation

noun

each of the possible ways in which a set of things can be arranged or ordered; the act or result of arranging elements in a group

Examples

A permutation of numbers
The code uses a complex permutation of letters and numbers to ensure security.
There are 120 possible permutations of the five books on the shelf.
Trying every permutation of the puzzle pieces took him all afternoon.
Each new permutation of the list created a different result in the experiment.
Mathematicians study the permutation of objects to understand probability.
The number of possible permutations of these letters is 120.

Synonyms

  • rearrangement
    3.4 0.81/m
    rearrangement is the act of changing the order of items for a new layout, while permutation is each possible ordering of those items.
  • reordering
    3.2 0.41/m
    reordering is the process of putting things in a different sequence when needed, while permutation names each possible sequence.
  • arrangement
    2.7 13/m
    arrangement is how things are placed or organized in a set for a purpose, while permutation is a specific ordering among many.
  • transposition
    7.3 0.11/m
    transposition is the act of swapping positions of elements, while permutation is any complete ordering of those elements.
