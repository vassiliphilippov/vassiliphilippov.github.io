genus Cinchona

noun
genus of South American medicinal trees
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒiː.nəs sɪnˈkoʊ.nə / (AmE), / ˈdʒiː.nəs sɪŋˈkəʊ.nə / (BrE)
A genus of trees native to the Andean region of South America whose bark is a source of medicinal compounds, including quinine.

Examples

Genus Cinchona trees
Botanists continue to study the genus Cinchona to understand its potential health benefits.
Many medicines were historically derived from trees belonging to the genus Cinchona.
The genus Cinchona is renowned for its role in the treatment of malaria.
