March equinox

noun
the equinox around March 21 each year
6.4 4.3
Pronunciation: /mɑːrtʃ ˈiː.kwə.nɑːks/ /; / /mɑːrtʃ ˈe.kwɪ.nɑːks/ (AmE), /mɑːtʃ ˈe.kwɪ.nɒks/ /; / /mɑːtʃ ˈe.kwə.nɒks/ (BrE)
the equinox that occurs around March 20 or 21 each year, when day and night are of approximately equal length, marking the beginning of spring in the Northern Hemisphere and autumn in the Southern Hemisphere

Examples

The March equinox day
During the March equinox, the length of day and night are nearly equal.
Many ancient cultures celebrated the March equinox with festivals and rituals.
The March equinox marks the first day of spring in many parts of the world.
