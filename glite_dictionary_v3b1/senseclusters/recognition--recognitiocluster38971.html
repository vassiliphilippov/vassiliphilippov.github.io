recognition

noun
biological interaction between complementary molecules
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛkəɡˈnɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛkəɡˈnɪʃən / (BrE)
(biology) the process by which a molecule identifies and binds to another molecule with a complementary structure, enabling specific biological interactions.

Examples

Molecule recognition
Hormone-receptor recognition triggers various cellular responses in living organisms.
Molecular recognition is essential for the immune system to distinguish between self and non-self molecules.
The enzyme's active site exhibits high specificity due to its precise recognition of substrate molecules.

Related Terms

  • synonym: binding - attachment of one molecule to another
  • synonym: interaction - process of acting upon each other, especially at the molecular level
