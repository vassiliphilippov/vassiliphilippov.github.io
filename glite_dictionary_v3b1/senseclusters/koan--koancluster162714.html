koan

noun
Zen Buddhist riddle or paradox
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊ.ɑːn / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.æn / (BrE)
a paradoxical question, story, or statement used in Zen Buddhism to demonstrate the limits of logical reasoning and provoke enlightenment

Examples

A koan story
Books on Buddhism often include collections of classic koans for contemplation.
She meditated for years on a single koan before finding insight.
The Zen master asked his students a koan to challenge their understanding.
