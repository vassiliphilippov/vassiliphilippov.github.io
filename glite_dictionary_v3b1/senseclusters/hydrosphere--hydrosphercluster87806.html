hydrosphere

noun
all water on or near Earth's surface
4.5
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪ.drəˌsfɪr / (AmE), / ˈhʌɪ.drəˌsfɪə / (BrE)
the total amount of water on or near the surface of the Earth, including oceans, rivers, lakes, and water vapor in the atmosphere

Examples

The hydrosphere of Earth
Climate change affects every part of the hydrosphere, including oceans, rivers, and glaciers.
Scientists study the hydrosphere to understand how water cycles through the planet.
The melting of polar ice adds fresh water to the Earth's hydrosphere.

Related Terms

  • synonym: atmosphere - the envelope of gases surrounding the Earth or another planet
  • synonym: biosphere - the regions of the surface and atmosphere of the Earth occupied by living organisms
