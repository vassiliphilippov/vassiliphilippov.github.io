Indra

proper noun
chief Hindu god of rain and thunder
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈɪn.drə / (AmE), / ˈɪn.drə / (BrE)
the chief deity in early Hinduism, regarded as the god of rain, thunder, and war in the Rig-Veda

Examples

Indra god of rain
During the festival, devotees prayed to Indra for a good monsoon and abundant crops.
Legends often describe Indra wielding his thunderbolt to protect the heavens.
Many ancient hymns praise Indra as the god who brings rain and defeats demons.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Thor - Norse god of thunder
  • synonym: Zeus - chief god in ancient Greek mythology, god of sky and thunder
