cup

noun
US liquid measure (8 fl oz)
Pronunciation: / kʌp / (AmE), / kʌp / (BrE)
a unit of measurement for liquids or other substances, equal to about 8 fluid ounces (237 milliliters)

Examples

A cup of flour
A standard cup in the US holds 8 fluid ounces.
She measured out two cups of water for the soup.
The recipe calls for one cup of milk.
He poured himself a cup of tea before sitting down.
She measured out precisely one cup of flour for the recipe.
The recipe calls for half a cup of milk.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: fluid ounce - a US or British unit of liquid volume (approximately 29.6 ml US, 28.4 ml UK)
  • degree higher: pint - a US liquid unit equal to 16 fluid ounces
  • degree lower: tablespoon - a smaller unit of liquid measurement, three teaspoons or 1/16 cup
