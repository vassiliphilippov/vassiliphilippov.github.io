acetate disk

noun
audio disk coated with cellulose acetate
Pronunciation: / ˈæsəˌteɪt dɪsk / (AmE), / ˈasɪteɪt dɪsk / (BrE)
A phonograph or audio recording disk coated with cellulose acetate, used especially for making temporary recordings.

Examples

An acetate disk recording
Many early radio broadcasts were preserved on acetate disks before magnetic tape became popular.
Sound engineers often used an acetate disk to make instant recordings in the studio.
The acetate disk began to deteriorate due to improper storage conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lacquer disc - phonograph disc coated with lacquer, similar purpose
