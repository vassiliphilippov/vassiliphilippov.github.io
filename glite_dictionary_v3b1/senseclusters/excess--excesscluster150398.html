an amount of something that is more than necessary, permitted, or desirable; too much of something

Examples

Excess of food
Eating in excess can lead to health problems over time.
The company had to discard the excess inventory at the end of the season.
There was an excess of enthusiasm in the crowd at the concert.
Drinking anything in excess can be harmful to your health.
Stores try to sell their excess inventory during clearance sales.
There was an excess of food after the event, so much of it went to waste.

Synonyms

  • surplus
    3.1 14/m
    surplus is an amount left after needs are met and often used in finance or trade, while excess is any unwanted extra.
  • superfluity
    8.7 0.12/m
    superfluity is a formal word for something unnecessary or extra, while excess is the common everyday term for too much.
  • overabundance
    5.7 0.45/m
    overabundance is a large, often harmful amount that causes problems, while excess simply means more than needed.
  • superabundance
    7.9 0.16/m
    superabundance is an extreme, very large amount beyond need, while excess can be any amount more than appropriate.
  • surfeit
    8.8 0.63/m
    surfeit is an overwhelming extra amount that often causes discomfort or sickness, while excess is a general extra amount.
  • plethora
    7.7 3.30/m
    plethora is a word often used in speech or writing to stress too much, while excess is a plain word for any extra amount.
  • abundance
    3.3 12/m
    abundance is a large, often welcome quantity of something, while excess focuses on too much and can be unwanted.
