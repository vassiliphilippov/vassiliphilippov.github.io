serfdom

noun
the state or system of being a serf
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈsɝfdəm / (AmE), / ˈsɜːfdəm / (BrE)
the status, condition, or system of being a serf, especially in a feudal society, where a person is bound to work on a lord's land and has limited rights or freedom.

Examples

Under serfdom system
For centuries, peasants lived under serfdom, tied to the land and subject to their lords.
Many historians study the impact of serfdom on medieval economies and social structures.
The abolition of serfdom transformed rural society across Europe in the nineteenth century.

Related Terms

  • antonym: freedom - the state of being free, not under restraint
  • synonym: servitude - the condition of being a servant or slave
