Chelicerata

noun
group of arthropods: spiders, scorpions, horseshoe crabs
Pronunciation: / kəˌlɪsəˈreɪtə / (AmE), / kɪˌlɪsəˈrɑːtə /; / kɛˌlɪsəˈrɑːtə / (BrE)
a large group of arthropods (including spiders, scorpions, ticks/mites, and horseshoe crabs) that have chelicerae (claw-like mouthparts)

Examples

Chelicerata group
Horseshoe crabs are one of the oldest living representatives of the Chelicerata.
Scientists classify spiders and scorpions as members of the Chelicerata group.
The distinguishing feature of Chelicerata is the presence of specialized mouthparts called chelicerae.
Horseshoe crabs, although not true crabs, are classified within the Chelicerata.
Many arachnologists study Chelicerata to understand the evolution of spiders and their relatives.
The Chelicerata are distinguished by their unique mouthparts called chelicerae.
