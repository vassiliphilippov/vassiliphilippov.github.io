Ryukyu

proper noun
historical island kingdom
9.7
Pronunciation: / riˈuːkjuː / (AmE), / riˈuːkjuː / (BrE)
a historical kingdom that ruled the Ryukyu Islands from the 15th to the 19th centuries, known for its monarchy and international trade

Examples

Ryukyu Kingdom
The Ryukyu Kingdom was famous for its unique culture and diplomatic relations with China and Japan.
Artifacts from Ryukyu reveal a rich history of trade with neighboring Asian countries.
Many traditional dances and music styles in Okinawa originated in the old Ryukyu Kingdom.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Japan - the country that annexed the Ryukyu Kingdom in the 19th century
  • synonym: Okinawa - the largest island of the Ryukyu Islands and former center of the Ryukyu Kingdom
