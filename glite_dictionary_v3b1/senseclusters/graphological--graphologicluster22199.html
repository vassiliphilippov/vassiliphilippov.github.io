graphological

adjective
written language features
Pronunciation: / ˌɡræfəˈlɑdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌɡræfəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the visual features of written language, such as letters, spelling, punctuation, or layout

Examples

Graphological features of text
The teacher highlighted the graphological differences between handwritten and printed texts.
In literary analysis, graphological choices like unusual spacing can create special effects.
Editors pay attention to graphological details such as font and punctuation in published works.

Related Terms

  • synonym: orthographic - relating to spelling or the conventional writing system of a language
  • antonym: phonological - relating to the sound system of a language
