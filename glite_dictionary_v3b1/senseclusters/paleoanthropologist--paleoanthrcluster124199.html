paleoanthropologist

noun
ancient human scientist
9.9
Pronunciation: /peɪ.lɪ.oʊ.æn.ˈθrɑːp.ə.lə.dʒɪst/ (AmE), /peɪ.lɪ.əʊ.æn.ˈθrɒp.ə.lə.dʒɪst/ (BrE)
a scientist who studies the origins and development of early humans through fossils and ancient remains

Examples

The paleoanthropologist studies fossils
The paleoanthropologist presented new evidence about early human migration at the conference.
As a paleoanthropologist, she spends months excavating ancient sites in Africa.
A team led by a renowned paleoanthropologist uncovered tools dating back two million years.
The paleoanthropologist presented new findings about early human migration at the conference.
As a paleoanthropologist, she spends months in remote areas searching for fossils.
Many paleoanthropologists work closely with geologists to date ancient remains.
