visualizer

noun
software that displays data or audio visually
5.2
Pronunciation: /ˈvɪ.ʒʊə.laɪ.zər/ (AmE), /ˈvɪ.ʒʊə.laɪ.zə/ (BrE)
a software tool or component that creates graphical, often real‑time, representations of audio, data, or system processes to help users see patterns or activity.

Examples

Music visualizer software
The music player now includes a visualizer that responds to the song's beats and colors the screen.
During the presentation, she used a visualizer to show real-time graphs of the sales figures.
Audio engineers ran the track through a visualizer to inspect the frequency peaks and identify noise.
The teacher connected a visualiser so everyone could see the student's sketch on the screen.
Using a visualiser, the presenter displayed live graphs from the dataset during the meeting.
Many museums now use interactive visualisers to let visitors examine fragile artifacts in detail.

Related Terms

  • synonym: document camera - A camera used to project physical documents or objects onto a screen for viewing.
  • synonym: renderer - a program or component that generates a visual display from data or models
  • synonym: visualization - the process or result of representing information or sound in visual form
  • synonym: visualizer - Alternate (chiefly American) spelling of 'visualiser'.
