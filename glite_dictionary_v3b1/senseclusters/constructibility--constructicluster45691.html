constructibility

noun
ability to be constructed
8.1
Pronunciation: / kənˌstrʌktəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / kənˌstrʌktəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being able to be constructed, especially in mathematics or engineering

Examples

Constructibility of a bridge
The constructibility of the bridge was evaluated by a team of engineers.
Mathematicians debated the constructibility of certain geometric figures using only a compass and straightedge.
Before starting the project, the architect assessed the constructibility of the proposed design.

Related Terms

  • synonym: feasibility - the possibility of being done or achieved
  • antonym: impossibility - the state of not being possible
