responsivity

noun
sensor output measure
8.9
Pronunciation: /rɪ.spɑːn.ˈsɪv.ɪ.tɪ/ /; / /rə.spɑːn.ˈsɪv.ɪ.tɪ/ (AmE), /rɪ.spɒn.ˈsɪv.ɪ.tɪ/ /; / /rə.spɒn.ˈsɪv.ɪ.tɪ/ (BrE)
the measure of a sensor's electrical output in response to a given amount of physical input, such as light or radiation

Examples

Sensor responsivity
The responsivity of the sensor determines how accurately it can detect low levels of light.
Engineers compared the responsivity of different photodiodes to select the best one for the project.
A high responsivity is important for devices used in scientific measurements and medical imaging.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sensitivity - the ability of a device to detect small changes or signals
