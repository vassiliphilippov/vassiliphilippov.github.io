as you do

adverb
used to show shared preference or behavior
3.9
Pronunciation: / æz ju du / (AmE), / æz juː duː / (BrE)
used to acknowledge that someone shares a preference or behavior previously mentioned, typically in casual conversation.

Examples

As you do in life
He likes to stay up late, as you do, when he’s working on a project.
She ordered dessert before the meal, as you do if you have a sweet tooth.
They went for a walk after dinner, as you do when the weather is pleasant.

Related Terms

  • synonym: so do I - phrase expressing agreement in having the same preference or experience
