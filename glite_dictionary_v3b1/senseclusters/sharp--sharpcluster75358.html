sharp

adjective
clever; astute; shrewd
Pronunciation: / ʃɑrp / (AmE), / ʃɑːp / (BrE)
having or showing quick intelligence or the ability to notice fine details; astute or perceptive

Examples

Sharp mind
His sharp instincts helped the company avoid a major financial loss.
Maria is a sharp negotiator who rarely misses a detail in contracts.
You need to be sharp if you want to succeed in this industry.
He gave a sharp analysis of the political situation.
Her sharp comments made everyone rethink their assumptions.
Lisa has a sharp mind and always spots errors that others miss.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acute - having or showing a perceptive or insightful mind
  • synonym: astute - having the ability to assess situations cleverly and turn this to one's advantage
  • antonym: dull - lacking sharpness or quickness of intellect
  • synonym: incisive - able to analyze or express ideas clearly and effectively
  • antonym: naive - lacking experience, wisdom, or judgment
  • synonym: shrewd - having or showing sharp powers of judgment; astute
