regeneracy

noun
ability to regrow
9.1
Pronunciation: / rɪˈdʒɛnərəsi / (AmE), / rɪˈdʒɛnərəsi / (BrE)
the ability or capacity to regrow or restore lost parts or functions, especially in living organisms

Examples

Lizard regeneracy
Some lizards show remarkable regeneracy, quickly growing back lost tails after predators attack.
Scientists study regeneracy in starfish to understand how they can regrow their arms.
The concept of regeneracy is important in medical research on tissue repair.

Related Terms

  • antonym: degeneration - the process of declining or losing function or structure
  • synonym: regeneration - the process of regrowing or restoring lost or damaged parts
