neurinoma

noun
benign nerve sheath tumor
Pronunciation: / ˌnʊr.ɪˈnoʊ.mə / (AmE), / ˌnjʊər.ɪˈnəʊ.mə / (BrE)
A tumor, usually benign, that develops from the sheath surrounding a nerve.

Examples

A neurinoma tumor
Her hearing loss was caused by a benign neurinoma pressing on the auditory nerve.
MRI scans revealed a neurinoma in the facial nerve region.
The surgeon removed a neurinoma from the patient's spinal cord.

Related Terms

  • synonym: schwannoma - a type of neurinoma arising from Schwann cells
