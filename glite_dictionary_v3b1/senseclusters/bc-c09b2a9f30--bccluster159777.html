B.C.

adverb
before the Christian era (used after dates)
3.4
Pronunciation: / ˌbiː ˈsiː / (AmE), / ˌbiː ˈsiː / (BrE)
used after a year to show that it is before the traditional date of the birth of Christ; before the Christian era.

Examples

Year B.C.
Aristotle was born in 384 B.C. in ancient Greece.
The city was founded in 753 B.C. according to legend.
This pottery dates back to 2000 B.C..

Related Terms
