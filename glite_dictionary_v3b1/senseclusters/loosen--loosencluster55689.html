loosen

verb
make less compact or dense
Pronunciation: / ˈluːsən / (AmE), / ˈluːsən / (BrE)
make or become less compact or dense; to separate or break up, especially to improve condition or usability, as with soil.

Examples

To loosen hard soil
Before planting, it's important to loosen the soil to help the roots grow.
Gardeners often use a rake to loosen compacted earth in flower beds.
Heavy rains can loosen dirt, making it easier for seeds to sprout.

Related Terms

  • synonym: loosen - to make less tight or firm
  • antonym: tighten - to make more compact or dense
