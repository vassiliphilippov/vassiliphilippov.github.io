Galbraithian

adjective
like Galbraith's style
Pronunciation: / ɡælˈbreɪθiən / (AmE), / ɡælˈbreɪθiən / (BrE)
relating to the ideas, style, or theories of John Kenneth Galbraith, especially in economics or social commentary

Examples

Galbraithian economics
The report offered a Galbraithian critique of modern consumer culture and its effects on society.
Her analysis was praised for its Galbraithian wit and insight into economic inequality.
Many economists disagreed with his Galbraithian approach to government intervention in markets.
The new tax policy has a distinctly Galbraithian emphasis on government regulation.
Many economists debate whether a Galbraithian approach is effective in today's global market.
Her analysis of public spending reflects a Galbraithian perspective on economic management.

Related Terms
