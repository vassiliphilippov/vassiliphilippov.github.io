dark matter

noun
invisible matter in the universe (cosmology)
7.5 4.02/m

A hypothetical form of matter that is invisible, does not emit or absorb light, and is believed to make up most of the universe's mass, exerting a gravitational force despite not interacting with atomic particles.

Examples

Dark matter in space
Astronomers believe that dark matter makes up about 27 percent of the universe.
Scientists are searching for evidence of dark matter to explain the missing mass in galaxies.
The existence of dark matter helps account for the way galaxies rotate.
