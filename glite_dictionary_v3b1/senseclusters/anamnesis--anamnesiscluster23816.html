anamnesis

noun
patient's recalled medical history
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˌænəmˈnisɪs / (AmE), / ˌanəmˈniːsɪs / (BrE)
A medical history of a patient as recounted by the patient, typically used in clinical assessment.

Examples

Patient anamnesis
Accurate anamnesis is crucial for diagnosing conditions that may not show obvious symptoms.
During the initial consultation, the doctor carefully recorded the patient's anamnesis to identify potential risk factors.
The nurse asked for a detailed anamnesis to better understand the patient's current complaints.
