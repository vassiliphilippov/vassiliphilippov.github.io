ionic charge

noun
net electric charge on an ion
1.0 4.6
Pronunciation: /aɪ.ˈɑː.nɪk tʃɑːrdʒ/ (AmE), /aɪ.ˈɒn.ɪk tʃɑːdʒ/ (BrE)
the electric charge possessed by an ion, determined by the difference in the number of protons and electrons.

Examples

Ionic charge value
Chloride ions have an ionic charge of negative one.
Scientists use the ionic charge to predict how atoms will bond in a reaction.
The ionic charge of sodium is positive because it loses one electron.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ion - an atom or molecule with a net electric charge
