chaotic evil

adjective
fictional alignment: disorderly and malicious
5.7
Pronunciation: / keɪˈɑtɪk ˈivəl / (AmE), / keɪˈɒtɪk ˈiːvəl / (BrE)
(in some role-playing games) a fictional alignment characterized by actions that are both disorderly and malicious, disregarding laws or any moral code.

Examples

Chaotic evil character
Many stories feature chaotic evil antagonists who revel in destruction and disorder.
She chose a chaotic evil alignment for her new character in the campaign.
The villain's chaotic evil behavior made him unpredictable and extremely dangerous.

Related Terms

  • antonym: chaotic good - fictional alignment; disorderly but benevolent
  • antonym: lawful evil - fictional alignment; obeys order but is malevolent
  • synonym: neutral evil - fictional alignment; evil without preference for law or chaos
