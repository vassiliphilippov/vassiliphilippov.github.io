shame

noun
state of dishonor or disgrace
1.0
Pronunciation: / ʃeɪm / (AmE), / ʃeɪm / (BrE)
a painful feeling of guilt, disgrace, or humiliation caused by wrong or foolish behavior

The scandal brought deep shame upon the entire organization.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embarrassment - a feeling of self-consciousness or awkwardness
  • synonym: guilt - a feeling of responsibility or remorse for an offense
  • antonym: honor - high respect or great esteem; the opposite state of respect or dignity
  • antonym: pride - a feeling of deep pleasure or satisfaction derived from one's achievements
