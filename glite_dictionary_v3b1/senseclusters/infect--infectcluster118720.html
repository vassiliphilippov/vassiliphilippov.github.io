infect

verb
to transmit disease to; to cause infection
1.0 2.2
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈfekt/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈfekt/ (BrE)
to cause someone or something to become ill by spreading germs, bacteria, or viruses

Examples

To infect a person
A bad cough can easily infect others if you don’t cover your mouth.
Doctors warned that untreated water may infect people with cholera.
Her laughter was so genuine it seemed to infect everyone in the room with happiness.
Doctors took precautions to ensure the wound did not infect other parts of the body.
Food prepared in unsanitary conditions can infect large numbers of people.
It is possible for a single mosquito bite to infect a person with malaria.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contaminate - to make something impure or harmful, especially by contact or mixture
  • antonym: cure - to heal or rid of disease
