biofilm

noun
microbial surface layer
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪoʊˌfɪlm / (AmE), / ˈbaɪəʊˌfɪlm / (BrE)
a thin layer of microorganisms that stick together and to a surface, often forming a protective or slimy coating

Examples

A biofilm layer
A biofilm can form on medical devices, making infections harder to treat.
Scientists study how biofilm develops on rocks in rivers and streams.
Regular brushing helps prevent biofilm from building up on your teeth.
A biofilm can form on pipes and medical equipment if not cleaned.
Dentists warn that biofilm on teeth can lead to cavities and gum disease.
Scientists study how biofilms protect bacteria from antibiotics in hospitals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: microorganism - a microscopic living organism, such as bacteria or fungi
  • synonym: plaque - a soft, sticky film of bacteria on teeth
