amnesty

noun
official pardon and exemption from punishment
Pronunciation: / ˈæm.nə.sti / (AmE), / ˈæm.nə.sti / (BrE)
an official pardon given by a government to a group of people, usually for political offenses or crimes, which exempts them from punishment

Examples

A government amnesty
A tax amnesty was granted to encourage citizens to disclose previously undeclared income.
During the amnesty, thousands of weapons were handed in to the police without penalty.
The government declared an amnesty for political prisoners, allowing many to return home.
He was released from jail after receiving an amnesty.
The government declared an amnesty for all political prisoners.
The rebels surrendered under the promise of amnesty for past crimes.
A general amnesty was announced for those who had evaded military service.
After years in exile, the activists were allowed to return following the presidential amnesty.
The new government declared an amnesty for all political prisoners.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clemency - mercy or lenience shown in punishing someone
  • synonym: pardon - the action of forgiving or being forgiven for an error or offense
  • synonym: reprieve - a cancellation or postponement of punishment
