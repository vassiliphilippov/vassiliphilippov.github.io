caveat

noun
a warning or caution
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪ.vi.ˌæt / (AmE), / ˈkæ.vi.ˌæt / (BrE)
a warning or caution about something, especially to prevent misunderstanding or discourage certain actions

Examples

Important caveat
Before signing the contract, her lawyer added a crucial caveat regarding payment terms.
He approved the idea, but with the caveat that it must not cost more than the budget allows.
The report comes with the caveat that the data may be incomplete.

Related Terms

  • synonym: warning - a statement or event that warns of something or gives notice
