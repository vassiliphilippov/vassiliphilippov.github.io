climb

noun
an upward slope (esp. on a road)
1.4 3.75/m
Pronunciation: / klaÉªm / (AmE), / klaÉªm / (BrE)
an upward slope or incline, especially on a road or path

Examples

A steep climb

Cyclists dread the long climb near the end of the race.
The hardest part of the route is the steep climb after the second bend.

Synonyms

    4.2 0.84/m

  • acclivity
    9.9 0.01/m
    an acclivity is a formal word for an upward slope on land, while climb is a common, everyday name for such a rise.
  • slope
    1.4 14/m
    a slope is any slanted piece of land or hillside, while climb refers specifically to an upward slope on a road or path.
  • incline
    4.2 2.08/m
    an incline is a sloping surface or ramp that connects levels, while climb focuses on a natural road or path slope.
  • rise
    2.4 11/m
    a rise is a small increase or higher spot in the land, while climb names a specific upward slope on a road or path.
  • grade
    4.6 2.07/m
    a grade is the steepness or angle of a road or slope, while climb is the actual upward slope you travel on.
