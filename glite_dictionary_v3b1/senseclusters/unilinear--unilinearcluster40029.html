unilinear

adjective
single line or sequence
Pronunciation: / ˌjuːnɪˈlɪniər / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪˈlɪniə / (BrE)
involving or following a single line, direction, or sequence (for example, a path or a system of inheritance through one family line)

Examples

A unilinear path
The diagram shows a unilinear path from start to finish.
Some theories of evolution are based on a unilinear model of progress.
The artist created a unilinear design using a single continuous stroke.
In anthropology, a unilinear system traces family heritage through only one parent.
Some cultures have unilinear inheritance, passing property down a single family line.
The tribe's unilinear descent rules determine membership and leadership succession.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bilinear - relating to descent or inheritance traced through both the father's and mother's lines
  • synonym: linear - arranged in or extending along a straight line
  • synonym: matrilineal - tracing descent or inheritance through the mother's line
  • antonym: multilinear - involving or consisting of several lines or sequences
  • synonym: patrilineal - tracing descent or inheritance through the father's line
