desolate

verb
to abandon someone helplessly
5.5 6.6
Pronunciation: /ˈdes.ə.leɪt/ /; / /ˈdez.ə.leɪt/ (AmE), /ˈdes.ə.leɪt/ (BrE)
to leave someone who needs or depends on you; to abandon or forsake, especially leaving them alone and helpless

Examples

To desolate a family
He could never forgive his father, who had desolated the family when times grew hard.
She feared her friends would desolate her just when she needed support the most.
The villagers felt betrayed when their leader desolated them during the crisis.
