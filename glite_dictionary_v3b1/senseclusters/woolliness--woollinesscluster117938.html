woolliness

noun
vagueness or lack of precision
7.0
Pronunciation: / c8w8al.i.n59s / (AmE), / c8w8al.i.n6as / (BrE)
The quality of being vague, imprecise, or unclear in expression, argument, or thought.

Examples

Political woolliness
The policy's woolliness makes it hard to assess its real impact.
Scholars criticized the paper for the woolliness of its central argument and unclear evidence.
We left the meeting frustrated by the woolliness surrounding the project's goals and timeline.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ambiguity - the quality of having more than one possible meaning or interpretation
  • antonym: clarity - the quality of being clear and easy to understand; opposite of woolliness
  • synonym: vagueness - the state of being unclear or not specific
