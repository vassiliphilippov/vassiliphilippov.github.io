hydrokinetics

noun
power generation from moving water
7.9
Pronunciation: /haɪ.droʊ.kɪ.ˈne.tɪks/ (AmE), /haɪ.drəʊ.kɪ.ˈne.tɪks/ (BrE)
(in engineering and renewable energy) the practical field and technologies for generating electricity from the kinetic energy of moving water, such as rivers, tides, and ocean currents.

Examples

Hydrokinetics power
Coastal engineers are expanding hydrokinetics projects to generate clean power from tidal currents.
The startup developed a new turbine that improved hydrokinetics efficiency in fast-flowing river channels.
Researchers measured environmental impacts before deploying commercial-scale hydrokinetics arrays near the shipping lane.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hydropower - generation of power from moving or falling water; broadly related but often includes dam-based systems.
  • synonym: tidal energy - energy generated from the movement of tides; a form of hydrokinetic power.
