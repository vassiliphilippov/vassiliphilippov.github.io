Aegypiidae

noun
family of Old World vultures
Pronunciation: / iːˈdʒɪpiˌɪdiː /; / iːˌdʒɪˈpɪɪdiː / (AmE), / iːˌdʒɪpɪˈaɪdiː /; / iːˌdʒɪˈpɪɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of large Old World vultures (birds of prey), sometimes considered a separate family or included in Accipitridae

Examples

Aegypiidae vultures
Some ornithologists still classify Old World vultures under the family Aegypiidae.
The Aegypiidae are distinguished from New World vultures by their lack of a keen sense of smell.
Unlike their American relatives, members of the Aegypiidae have strong feet adapted for walking.
Many species in the Aegypiidae are crucial for cleaning up carrion in their habitats.
Recent research debates whether to classify Old World vultures under Aegypiidae or Accipitridae.
The Aegypiidae are distinguished by their featherless heads and large size.
