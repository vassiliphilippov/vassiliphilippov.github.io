cost-plus

adjective
payment including actual cost plus added profit
6.6 0.14/m
Pronunciation: / kÉ‘st ËˆplÊŒs / (AmE), / kÉ’st ËˆplÊŒs / (BrE)
relating to a method of pricing or payment in which the buyer pays the actual cost of production plus an additional agreed-upon fee or percentage as profit.

Examples

Cost-plus pricing method
Some government projects are managed under cost-plus arrangements, ensuring contractors are paid for actual expenses and a profit.
The company was awarded a cost-plus contract to build the new bridge.
Unlike a fixed-price agreement, a cost-plus model reimburses the contractor for real costs incurred.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fixed-price - relating to a contract or price that does not change regardless of actual costs
