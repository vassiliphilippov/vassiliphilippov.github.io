counterposition

noun
opposite placement
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkaʊntərpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntəpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of placing something directly opposite another thing, or the state of being so placed

Examples

Counterposition of objects
The counterposition of the mirrors allowed the light to reflect perfectly across the room.
Careful counterposition of the speakers improved the sound quality in the auditorium.
In chess, the counterposition of the pieces can create interesting strategic challenges.

Related Terms

  • antonym: alignment - the arrangement in a straight line or in correct relative positions
  • synonym: opposition - the state of being against or opposite to something
