semistate

noun
partially autonomous entity
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛmiˌsteɪt / (AmE), / ˈsɛmiˌsteɪt / (BrE)
a political entity that has some self-governing powers but is not fully independent as a sovereign state

Examples

A semistate government
After the agreement, the territory became a semistate with its own local government.
The island functioned as a semistate before gaining full independence decades later.
Many regions under colonial rule were organized as a semistate with limited autonomy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: colony - a territory governed by a foreign power
  • synonym: protectorate - a region controlled and protected by a more powerful state
  • antonym: sovereign state - an independent and fully self-governing country
