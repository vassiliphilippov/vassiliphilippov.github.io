limb

noun
main branch of a tree
1.6
Pronunciation: / lɪm / (AmE), / lɪm / (BrE)
a large branch or projection from a tree or something that sticks out like an arm or branch

Examples

Tree limb
A heavy limb blocked the path after the windstorm.
Squirrels scampered along a limb to reach the bird feeder.
The storm snapped a thick limb off the old oak tree.
The limb of the tree nearly touched the window.
The peninsula acts as a limb of the mainland, jutting into the sea.
The robot's mechanical limb extended to reach the object on the shelf.

Related Terms

  • synonym: branch - a part of a tree that grows out from the trunk or from a bough
  • degree lower: twig - a small, thin branch from a tree or bush
