outflow

noun
natural movement of water flowing outward
1.8
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtˌfloʊ / (AmE), / ˈaʊt.fləʊ / (BrE)
the movement or process of something (such as water, gas, or money) flowing out from a place, system, or container

Examples

The outflow of water
After heavy rains, the outflow from the cave increased dramatically.
Scientists measured the outflow from the underground spring over several months.
The outflow of groundwater from the hillside created a small stream.
A sudden outflow of funds worried investors.
The outflow of air from the vents cooled the entire room.
The heavy rain caused a rapid outflow of water from the reservoir.
An outflow of water was detected from the reservoir after the heavy rains.
Engineers monitored the outflow of gas from the pipeline to prevent any hazards.
The rapid outflow of air sounded an alarm in the laboratory.
