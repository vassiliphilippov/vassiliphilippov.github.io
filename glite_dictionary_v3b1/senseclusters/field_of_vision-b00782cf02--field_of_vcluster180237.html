field of vision

noun
entire area visible to the eye at once
3.7 0.82/m
Pronunciation: / fiÀêld …ôv Ààv…™ í…ôn / (AmE), / fiÀêld …ôv Ààv…™ í…ôn / (BrE)
the entire area that can be seen at one time by one eye without moving it

Examples

A wide field of vision
A narrow field of vision can make driving more difficult at night.
Peripheral objects may fall outside the field of vision if you stare straight ahead.
Wearing glasses can slightly alter your field of vision.

Synonyms

  • visual field
    4.8 0.57/m
    visual field is the full area the eye sees at once, often used in medicine, while field of vision is everyday wording.
  • field of view
    4.2 2.06/m
    field of view is the area seen at a moment and often used for instruments like cameras, while field of vision refers to the eye.
  • field of regard
    8.7 0.00/m
    field of regard means the whole physical scene visible from one position, while field of vision describes what a single eye sees without moving.

Related Terms
