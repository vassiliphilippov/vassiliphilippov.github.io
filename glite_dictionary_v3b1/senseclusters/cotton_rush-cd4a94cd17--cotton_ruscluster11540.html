cotton rush

noun
bog sedge with cotton-like tufts
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑtən ɹʌʃ / (AmE), / ˈkɒtən rʌʃ / (BrE)
a sedge of the genus Eriophorum, found in bogs of northern temperate regions, with tufted, cotton-like flower spikes

Examples

The cotton rush plant
Botanists studied the cotton rush to understand how it adapts to marshy soil conditions.
Each spring, the cotton rush produces fluffy heads that carpet the bog in white.
In the wetland reserve, the cotton rush swayed in the breeze, its white tufts gleaming in the sunlight.
