segregant

adjective
causing separation
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛɡrɪɡənt / (AmE), / ˈsɛɡrɪɡənt / (BrE)
tending to cause separation or keep groups, elements, or people apart from each other.

Examples

Segregant policy
The new policy was criticized as segregant because it divided students by ability.
Some argued that the club's segregant rules discouraged diversity among its members.
Urban planners warned that segregant zoning could harm community cohesion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: integrative - bringing together or combining different groups
  • synonym: separatist - favoring or causing separation from a group
