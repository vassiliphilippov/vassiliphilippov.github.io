runout

noun
mechanical deviation; depletion
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌnˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈrʌnˌaʊt / (BrE)
a measured deviation from true rotation in a mechanical part, or a situation where something is used up or finished

Examples

Shaft runout
The engineer checked the runout of the rotating shaft to ensure it was within tolerance.
After the runout of supplies, the team had to wait for a new delivery.
A high runout can cause vibrations and reduce the lifespan of machinery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: depletion - the reduction or exhaustion of a resource
  • synonym: exhaustion - the state of being used up or finished
  • antonym: surplus - an amount that is more than needed
