bioprospecting

noun
search for biological resources
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪoʊˈprɑːspɛktɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌbaɪəʊˈprɒspɛktɪŋ / (BrE)
the search for and collection of biological materials or resources, especially for developing new medicines or commercial products

Examples

Bioprospecting for medicine
The pharmaceutical company invested heavily in bioprospecting to find new compounds for medicine.
Some countries have strict laws to regulate bioprospecting and protect native species.
Debates about bioprospecting often focus on the rights of indigenous communities.
Pharmaceutical companies invest heavily in bioprospecting to find new compounds for drug development.
Some indigenous communities are concerned about the impact of bioprospecting on their traditional knowledge.
Bioprospecting in rainforests has led to the discovery of plants with unique medicinal properties.

Related Terms

  • antonym: biopiracy - the unethical or illegal use of biological resources without permission
  • synonym: exploration - the act of searching or traveling for the purpose of discovery
