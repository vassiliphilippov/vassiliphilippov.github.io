flash

verb
emit a brief burst of light
5.9
Pronunciation: / flæʃ / (AmE), / flæʃ / (BrE)
to emit or shine with a sudden, brief light

Examples

To flash brightly
Lightning flashed across the night sky, illuminating the landscape for a moment.
The camera flashed as the photographer took the picture.
Warning lights flashed on the dashboard, signaling a problem with the engine.
A warning sign flashed on the dashboard, indicating a problem with the engine.
Lightning flashed across the sky during the storm.
The police car's lights flashed brightly as it sped down the road.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blink - to shine with an unsteady or intermittent light
  • degree higher: flare - burn or shine with a sudden intensity
  • synonym: flicker - to shine with a wavering or unsteady light
  • degree lower: glimmer - shine with a faint or wavering light
