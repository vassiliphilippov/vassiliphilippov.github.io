pilotfish

noun
small fish that accompanies sharks or ships
5.1 8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪlətˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈpaɪlətˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a small marine fish (Naucrates ductor) known for often swimming alongside sharks, mantas, or ships.

Examples

A pilotfish swims
A pilotfish is often seen swimming just ahead of a shark, feeding on scraps left behind.
Fishermen sometimes spot a pilotfish following their boats for long distances.
Scientists observed a pilotfish gliding beside a large manta ray in the open sea.
