bacca

noun
type of fleshy fruit in botany
8.9 9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈbækə / (AmE), / ˈbakə / (BrE)
A type of fleshy fruit, such as a grape or tomato, that does not split open at maturity and develops from a single ovary.

Examples

A bacca fruit
In botany, a cranberry is an example of a bacca.
Some fruits commonly called berries, like the grape, are actually a bacca.
The tomato is classified as a bacca in botanical terms.
