pep pill

noun
stimulant drug in pill form
6.9
Pronunciation: / pɛp pɪl / (AmE), / pɛp pɪl / (BrE)
a pill containing a stimulant that increases alertness and reduces fatigue, sometimes used medically to treat narcolepsy or depression

Examples

A pep pill
Because he was tired before the exam, he took a pep pill to stay alert.
Doctors warn that using a pep pill without medical supervision can be dangerous.
She relied on a pep pill to help her get through her night shifts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stimulant - a substance that raises levels of physiological or nervous activity in the body
