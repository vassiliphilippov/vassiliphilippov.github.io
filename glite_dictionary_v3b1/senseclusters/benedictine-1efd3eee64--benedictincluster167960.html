Benedictine

noun
member of Saint Benedict's monastic order
Pronunciation: / ˌbɛnəˈdɪktin / (AmE), / ˌbɛnɪˈdɪktiːn / (BrE)
a monk or nun who belongs to the monastic order founded by Saint Benedict

Examples

A Benedictine monk
He met a Benedictine while traveling through Italy who shared stories about monastic life.
She spent several years as a Benedictine in a hilltop abbey.
The Benedictine monks are known for their contributions to education and music.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nun - a woman who has taken religious vows and lives in a convent or monastery
