possibilism

noun
theory of human adaptation
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːsəbɪˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈpɒsəbɪˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
the theory that the environment sets limits but people have the ability to adapt and make choices within those limits

Examples

Environmental possibilism
Possibilism argues that people can overcome environmental challenges through innovation and adaptation.
In geography, possibilism contrasts with the idea that nature alone shapes societies.
The concept of possibilism highlights human creativity in responding to environmental limits.

Related Terms

  • antonym: environmental determinism - the theory that the environment completely determines human actions
