reorganization

noun
major structural change in an organization
1.0 3.1
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌɔːrɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌriːˌɔrɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌɔːɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making major changes to the structure or organization of something, especially a company or government

Examples

Company reorganization
After the new law was passed, a complete reorganization of the agency took place.
The reorganization resulted in several new departments being created.
The company announced a major reorganization to streamline its management structure.
After the reorganization of the department, some employees were given new roles.
The company announced a major reorganization to improve efficiency and cut costs.
The school board approved the reorganization of several academic programs.
After the merger, the university underwent a complete reorganisation of its academic departments.
The company announced a major reorganisation to improve efficiency and adapt to market changes.
The government's reorganisation of the health service caused widespread debate among professionals.
