reorganization

noun
major structural change in an organization

Examples

Company reorganization
After the new law was passed, a complete reorganization of the agency took place.
The reorganization resulted in several new departments being created.
The company announced a major reorganization to streamline its management structure.

Synonyms

  • restructuring
    4.2 6.48/m
    restructuring is the act of changing a company's setup to fix problems or cut costs, while reorganization means major structural change.
  • shakeup
    5.0 0.65/m
    a shakeup is a sudden major change, often moving people or roles, while a reorganization is a planned structural overhaul.
  • overhaul
    4.6 2.37/m
    an overhaul is a thorough update to systems or processes, often technical, while a reorganization focuses on changing structure and roles.
  • reshuffle
    5.3 0.12/m
    a reshuffle is changing people or posts inside an organization, but a reorganization changes overall structure and how units work.
  • reconfiguration
    5.6 0.66/m
    reconfiguration is arranging parts or settings in a new way, while reorganization usually means major structural change across an organization.
  • realignment
    4.6 0.43/m
    realignment is a change in position or focus to match goals, while reorganization is a deeper change in the whole structure.
