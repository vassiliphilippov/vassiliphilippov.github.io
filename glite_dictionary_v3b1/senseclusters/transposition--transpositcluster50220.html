transposition

noun
chromosomal segment movement (genetics)
Pronunciation: / ˌtrænspəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌtrænspəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
(Genetics) a mutation in which a segment of a chromosome is moved to a different position on the same or a different chromosome.

Examples

Chromosome transposition
Certain diseases are linked to the transposition of chromosomal material between different chromosomes.
Researchers study transposition to understand how mutations can drive evolution in organisms.
The geneticist explained that transposition can affect gene expression by relocating segments of DNA.
