titanosaur

noun
large herbivorous dinosaur of the Cretaceous
5.1
Pronunciation: / taɪˈtænəˌsɔr / (AmE), / tɪˈtænəsɔː / (BrE)
A large herbivorous dinosaur of the Cretaceous period, known for its massive size, long thin neck, and whiplike tail, found mainly in the Southern Hemisphere.

Examples

The titanosaur dinosaur
Scientists believe the titanosaur could weigh as much as seventy tons.
The fossilized bones of a titanosaur were discovered in Argentina.
The museum's exhibit features a life-sized model of a titanosaur.

Related Terms
