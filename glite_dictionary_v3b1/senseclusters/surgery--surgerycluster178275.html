surgery

noun
medical operation
2.7 37/m
Pronunciation: / Ààs…ùÀêd í…ôri / (AmE), / Ààs…úÀêd í…ôri / (BrE)
a medical operation in which a doctor cuts into the body to treat or repair it

Examples

Have surgery
After the surgery, the patient needed several weeks to recover fully.
The doctor explained the risks and benefits of the surgery before proceeding.
Modern surgery often uses advanced technology to improve patient outcomes.

Synonyms

  • surgical operation
    3.0 0.19/m
    a surgical operation is the formal medical act of cutting to fix or remove tissue, while surgery names that medical treatment.
  • surgical procedure
    4.3 1.29/m
    a surgical procedure uses precise steps and instruments to treat a problem, while surgery is the general medical operation.
  • operation
    3.7 11/m
    an operation is the specific medical cutting event done to repair damage or stop disease, while surgery is the broader field and act.
  • surgical process
    4.6 0.00/m
    a surgical process describes the series of steps before, during, and after an operation, while surgery names the actual operation.
