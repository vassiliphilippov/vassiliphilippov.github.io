aldehyde-alcohol

noun
a colorless liquid with both alcohol and aldehyde groups
3.3
Pronunciation: / ˈæl.dɪˌhaɪd ˈæl.kəˌhɔl / (AmE), / ˈaldɪˌhaɪd ˈalkəhɒl / (BrE)
an oily, colorless liquid obtained by the condensation of two acetaldehyde molecules, containing both an alcohol group and an aldehyde group

Examples

The aldehyde-alcohol liquid
aldehyde-alcohol is used in organic synthesis to create more complex compounds.
Because of its reactive groups, aldehyde-alcohol must be stored with care.
The laboratory synthesized aldehyde-alcohol for experimental purposes.
