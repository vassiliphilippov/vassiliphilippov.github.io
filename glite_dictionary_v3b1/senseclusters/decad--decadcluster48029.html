decad

noun
ten-day period
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛkæd / (AmE), / ˈdɛkæd / (BrE)
a period of ten days, especially as used in the French Revolutionary calendar

Examples

French Revolutionary decad
During the French Revolution, each decad ended with a day of rest and celebration.
The new calendar divided the month into three decads instead of four weeks.
Farmers planned their work according to the decad rather than the traditional week.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decade - a period of ten years
  • antonym: week - a period of seven days
