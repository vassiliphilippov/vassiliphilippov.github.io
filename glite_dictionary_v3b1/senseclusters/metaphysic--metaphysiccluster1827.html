metaphysic

noun
system of beliefs
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˈfɪzɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈfɪzɪk / (BrE)
a particular system or set of beliefs about the fundamental nature of reality and existence

Examples

A religious metaphysic
Each religion has its own metaphysic that explains the origins and purpose of the universe.
The philosopher developed a new metaphysic to address questions about consciousness and reality.
Her political metaphysic emphasized individual freedom over state control in public policy debates.

Related Terms

  • antonym: materialism - the belief that only physical things exist
  • synonym: philosophy - a set of ideas about knowledge, truth, and the meaning of life
  • synonym: worldview - a particular philosophy or view of life
