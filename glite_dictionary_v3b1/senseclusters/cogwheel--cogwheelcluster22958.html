cogwheel

noun
toothed wheel in machinery
6.2 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑː.gwiːl/ /; / /ˈkɔː.gwiːl/ (AmE), /ˈkɒ.gwiːl/ (BrE)
A wheel with a series of teeth along its edge that mesh with those of another similar wheel to transmit motion in a machine.

Examples

Metal cogwheel
Several cogwheels turned simultaneously inside the intricate machine.
The engineer explained how a cogwheel transmits force to other parts of the mechanism.
The old clock was repaired by replacing a broken cogwheel.
