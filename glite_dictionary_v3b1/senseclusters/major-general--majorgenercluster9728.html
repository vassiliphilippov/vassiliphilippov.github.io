major-general

noun
senior military rank above brigadier general
Pronunciation: / ˌmeɪdʒər ˈdʒɛnərəl / (AmE), / ˌmeɪdʒə ˈdʒɛnərəl / (BrE)
a high-ranking military officer, ranking above a brigadier general and below a lieutenant general

Examples

The major-general rank
After twenty years of service, she was promoted to major-general.
Several major-generals attended the international defense summit.
The major-general inspected the troops before the parade.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: brigadier general - a military officer one rank below major-general
  • degree higher: lieutenant general - a military officer one rank above major-general
