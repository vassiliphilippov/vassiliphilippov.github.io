tukutuku

noun
Maori lattice panel
Pronunciation: / ˌtukuˈtuku / (AmE), / ˌtukuˈtuku / (BrE)
a decorative Maori lattice panel with woven patterns, used to adorn the walls of traditional meeting houses; also refers to the traditional craft of making these panels

Examples

Maori tukutuku panels
The intricate tukutuku panels added cultural significance to the meeting house.
Visitors admired the colorful patterns woven into each tukutuku on the walls.
Learning to create a tukutuku is an important tradition in Maori culture.
The walls of the wharenui were decorated with beautiful tukutuku panels representing tribal stories.
Each tukutuku pattern in the meeting house has a unique meaning passed down through generations.
Visitors admired the intricate tukutuku designs woven by skilled Māori artists.
The intricate tukutuku panels in the meeting house tell stories of the tribe's ancestors.
She learned the art of tukutuku from her grandmother, who was a respected weaver.
Visitors admired the colorful patterns woven into the tukutuku on the marae walls.
