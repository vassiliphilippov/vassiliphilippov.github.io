Ottoman Empire

proper noun
historical Turkish empire (1299–1922)
5.2
Pronunciation: /ˈɑː.tə.mən ˈem.paɪ.ər/ (AmE), /ˈɒt.ə.mən ˈem.paɪ.ə/ (BrE)
A large Islamic empire founded by Turkish tribes in the late 13th century that eventually covered parts of southeastern Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa, lasting until the end of World War I.

Examples

The Ottoman Empire rule
Many architectural wonders in Istanbul were built during the Ottoman Empire's rule.
The Ottoman Empire at its height spanned three continents and included diverse cultures.
The fall of the Ottoman Empire after World War I reshaped the map of the Middle East.

  • synonym: Turkish Empire - another name for the Ottoman Empire
