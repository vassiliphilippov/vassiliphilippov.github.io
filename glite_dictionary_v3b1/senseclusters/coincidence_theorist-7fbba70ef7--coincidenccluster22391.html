coincidence theorist

noun
someone who sees patterns in random events
Pronunciation: / koʊˈɪnsɪdəns ˈθɪəɹɪst / (AmE), / kəʊˈɪnsɪdəns ˈθɪərɪst / (BrE)
a person who attributes significance or patterns to events that are likely random or coincidental

Examples

coincidence theorist online
In the online forum, a coincidence theorist argued that all accidents follow hidden rules.
Some dismiss her ideas as those of a coincidence theorist, but others are intrigued by her analysis.
The professor called him a coincidence theorist after he tried to link the data points.
