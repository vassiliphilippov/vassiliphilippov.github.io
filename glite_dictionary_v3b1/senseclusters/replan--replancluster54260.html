replan

noun
revised plan
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈplæn / (AmE), / ˌriːˈplæn / (BrE)
a new or revised plan created to replace or update a previous one

Examples

A project replan
After the unexpected delay, the project manager presented a new replan to the team.
The replan addressed all the issues found in the original schedule.
We had to create a replan when the client changed their requirements.

Related Terms

  • antonym: original - the first or initial version of something
  • synonym: plan - a detailed proposal for achieving something
  • synonym: revision - a change or set of changes to something
