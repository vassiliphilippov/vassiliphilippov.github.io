division Protista

noun
group of single-celled eukaryotic organisms
Pronunciation: / dɪˈvɪʒən proʊˈtɪstə / (AmE), / dɪˈvɪʒən prəʊˈtɪstə / (BrE)
A group of eukaryotic, mostly single-celled organisms that are distinct from plants, animals, and fungi, including protozoa, slime molds, and certain algae.

Examples

Division Protista group
Biologists once classified a wide variety of microbes under division Protista, though this system has since evolved.
Slime molds, formerly grouped in division Protista, exhibit characteristics of both fungi and amoebae.
Some eukaryotic algae belong to division Protista rather than the plant kingdom.
