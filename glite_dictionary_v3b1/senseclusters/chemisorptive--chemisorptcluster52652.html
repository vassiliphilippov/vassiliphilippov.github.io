chemisorptive

adjective
capable of chemical adsorption
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkɛmɪˈsɔrptɪv / (AmE), / ˌkɛmɪˈsɔːptɪv / (BrE)
having the capacity to adsorb substances by forming chemical bonds, as opposed to physical adsorption

Examples

Chemisorptive behavior
The catalyst exhibits a chemisorptive behavior when exposed to certain gases.
The study discusses the chemisorptive interactions between the surface and the hydrogen atoms.
Unlike physisorptive materials, chemisorptive surfaces form strong chemical bonds with adsorbed molecules.

Related Terms

  • antonym: physisorptive - adsorbing by physical rather than chemical forces
