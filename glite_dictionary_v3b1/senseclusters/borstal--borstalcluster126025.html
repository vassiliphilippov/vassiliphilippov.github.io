borstal

noun
former British reform school for young offenders
Pronunciation: / ˈbɔr.stəl / (AmE), / ˈbɔː.stəl / (BrE)
a former British institution for young offenders, especially those considered too young for prison

Examples

A borstal institution
After being caught stealing, he was sent to a borstal instead of an adult prison.
Many former borstal inmates later spoke about the need for better rehabilitation programs.
The novel describes the harsh discipline of life inside a borstal in the 1950s.
