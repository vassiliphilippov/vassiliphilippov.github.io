arthrotomy

noun
surgical joint opening
Pronunciation: / ɑrˈθrɑtəmi / (AmE), / ɑːˈθrɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which a joint is cut open to access its interior

Examples

An arthrotomy surgery
The doctor recommended an arthrotomy to remove the damaged tissue from the patient's elbow.
After the arthrotomy, the patient needed several weeks of physical therapy to regain movement.
An arthrotomy may be necessary when less invasive treatments for joint infection are not effective.
The surgeon performed an arthrotomy to remove infected tissue from the patient's knee.
After the arthrotomy, the joint was carefully irrigated to prevent further complications.
Recovery from an arthrotomy may require physical therapy to restore joint movement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arthroplasty - surgical reconstruction or replacement of a joint
  • antonym: arthroscopy - a minimally invasive procedure to examine or treat a joint
