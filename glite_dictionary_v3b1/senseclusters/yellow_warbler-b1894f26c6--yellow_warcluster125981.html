yellow warbler

noun
small American yellow songbird
Pronunciation: / ˈjɛloʊ ˈwɔːrblər / (AmE), / ˈjɛləʊ ˈwɔːblə / (BrE)
A small American songbird of the warbler family, notable for its bright yellow plumage and distinctive song.

Examples

A yellow warbler bird
A yellow warbler perched among the willow branches, singing its melodious song.
During migration season, birdwatchers hope to catch a glimpse of the bright yellow warbler.
The yellow warbler is often found near streams or marshy areas where insects are abundant.
