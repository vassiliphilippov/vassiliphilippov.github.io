caulker

noun
person who seals joints
8.1
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑː.kər/ /; / /ˈkɔː.kər/ (AmE), /ˈkɔː.kə/ (BrE)
a person whose job is to seal joints, seams, or gaps (especially in ships, boats, or buildings) to make them watertight

Examples

The caulker fills gaps
The caulker carefully filled the gaps between the planks to prevent leaks.
A skilled caulker is essential for maintaining old wooden boats.
After repairs, the caulker inspected the hull to ensure it was watertight.
The caulker carefully filled every crack around the new window to keep out the rain.
A skilled caulker can make a house much more energy efficient by sealing air leaks.
They hired a caulker to repair the seams in the old wooden boat before launching it.
The experienced caulker inspected every seam to ensure the boat would not leak.
A team of caulkers worked together to prepare the ship for its maiden voyage.
Before painting the hull, the caulker made sure all gaps were properly sealed.
