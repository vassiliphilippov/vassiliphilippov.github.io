huaca

noun
Andean sacred tomb
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑːkə / (AmE), / ˈwɑːkə / (BrE)
a sacred burial mound or tomb from pre-Columbian Andean cultures, often regarded as a holy site

Examples

Ancient huaca site
Archaeologists carefully excavated the ancient huaca to learn more about Andean burial practices.
Local communities still visit the huaca to leave offerings and honor their ancestors.
The huaca stood on a hill, overlooking the valley as a reminder of the region's rich history.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mausoleum - a large, stately tomb
  • synonym: tumulus - an ancient burial mound
