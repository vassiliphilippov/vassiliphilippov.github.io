section

noun
group of similar instruments in an orchestra
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˈsɛkʃən /; / ˈsekʃən / (BrE)
a group or unit within a larger organization, such as a team in the military, police, company, orchestra, or a class group

Examples

The brass section
Each section in the orchestra brings a unique texture to the overall sound.
The conductor asked the brass section to play more softly during the second movement.
The violin section rehearsed separately before joining the full orchestra.
Each section of the unit was responsible for a different area of the operation.
The radio operator stayed close to his section during the mission.
The sergeant led his section through a field training exercise early in the morning.
Many employees work in the research and development section of the company.
She was transferred to the section responsible for international affairs.
The finance section will review the budget proposal tomorrow.

Related Terms

  • synonym: department - a division of an organization with a specific function
  • antonym: lecture - an educational talk to a large group, as opposed to a small section
  • degree higher: platoon - a larger military unit composed of several sections
  • synonym: seminar - small class at a college or university for discussion
  • degree lower: squad - a smaller military unit or team, sometimes used interchangeably
