Hydrophidae

noun
family of sea snakes
9.9
Pronunciation: / haɪˈdrɑːfɪˌdiː / (AmE), / haɪˈdrɒfɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of venomous snakes, commonly known as sea snakes, inhabiting marine environments, especially in the Indo-Pacific region.

Examples

Hydrophidae sea snakes
Most members of the Hydrophidae have paddle-like tails for efficient swimming underwater.
Scientists study Hydrophidae to better understand how reptiles adapted to marine environments.
The Hydrophidae are widely distributed throughout the warm coastal waters of the Indian and Pacific Oceans.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sea snake - any venomous marine snake; member of Hydrophiinae or Hydrophidae
