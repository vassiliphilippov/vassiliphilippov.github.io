sugar-bush

noun
evergreen shrub with reddish-yellow flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈʃuɡɚ bʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈʃʊɡə bʊʃ / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States, having spikes of reddish-yellow flowers and glandular, hairy fruits

Examples

The sugar-bush shrub
Botanists studied the fruiting patterns of the sugar-bush in its native habitat.
She planted a sugar-bush along the southern fence for its attractive foliage and blossoms.
The sugar-bush in our garden bloomed with vibrant reddish-yellow flowers this spring.
