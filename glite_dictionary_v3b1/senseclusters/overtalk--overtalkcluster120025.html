overtalk

noun
overlapping speech
7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈoʊ.vər.tɔːk/ /; / /ˈoʊ.vər.tɑːk/ (AmE), /ˈəʊ.və.tɔːk/ (BrE)
speech that overlaps or interrupts others, making it difficult to hear or follow the conversation

Examples

Loud overtalk
The meeting was hard to follow because of constant overtalk among the participants.
Teachers often remind students to avoid overtalk so everyone can be heard.
During the debate, overtalk made it difficult for the audience to understand the arguments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interrupt - to stop someone from speaking by speaking yourself
  • antonym: silence - a complete absence of speech or sound
