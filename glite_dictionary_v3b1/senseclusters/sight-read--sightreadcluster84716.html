sight-read

verb
perform music without prior rehearsal
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪtˌrid / (AmE), / ˈsaɪtˌriːd / (BrE)
to perform music by reading the score for the first time without having practiced it beforehand

Examples

To sight-read music
Although he plays well, he can't sight-read new music on the spot.
Many orchestra musicians must be able to sight-read complex pieces during auditions.
She learned to sight-read at an early age, impressing her teachers.

Related Terms
