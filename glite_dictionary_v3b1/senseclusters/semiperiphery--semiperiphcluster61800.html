semiperiphery

noun
intermediate position
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmiˈpɛrəˌfɪri / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈpɛrɪfəri / (BrE)
a group, region, or country that is between the most developed (core) and least developed (periphery) areas in a system, often used in world systems theory

Examples

The semiperiphery countries
Several countries in the semiperiphery play a key role in connecting global markets.
The city has moved from the periphery to the semiperiphery due to recent economic growth.
Organizations in the semiperiphery often mediate between powerful and less developed regions.
Countries in the semiperiphery often experience both industrial growth and economic challenges.
The semiperiphery plays a crucial role in connecting the global core and periphery regions.
Many emerging economies are classified as part of the semiperiphery in world systems theory.
