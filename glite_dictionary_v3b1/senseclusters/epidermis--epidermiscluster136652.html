epidermis

noun
outer skin layer of vertebrates
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˈdɜrmɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈdɜːmɪs / (BrE)
the outer layer of cells covering the skin of vertebrates; the outermost layer of the skin

Examples

The epidermis layer
During a sunburn, damage is mainly limited to the epidermis.
The epidermis acts as a protective barrier against bacteria and other environmental hazards.
The human epidermis regenerates regularly to replace dead skin cells.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: dermis - the thick layer of living tissue below the epidermis
