savanna

noun
flat tropical/subtropical grassland
1.0 4.2
Pronunciation: / səˈvænə / (AmE), / səˈvɑːnə / (BrE)
A large, flat grassy plain in tropical or subtropical regions, often with scattered trees.

Examples

The savanna grasses
During the dry season, the savanna grasses turn golden under the intense sun.
Lions and elephants roam the African savanna in search of food and water.
Many documentaries feature scenes of wildebeests migrating across the savanna.

Related Terms

  • synonym: prairie - a large open area of grassland, especially in North America
  • synonym: steppe - a large area of flat unforested grassland in southeastern Europe or Siberia
