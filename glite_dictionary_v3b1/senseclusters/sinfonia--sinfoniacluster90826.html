sinfonia

noun
orchestral composition
Pronunciation: / sɪnˈfoʊniə / (AmE), / sɪnˈfəʊniə / (BrE)
a long orchestral composition in several movements, similar to a symphony, often used in classical music contexts

Examples

A grand sinfonia
The orchestra performed a beautiful sinfonia composed in the early 18th century.
He studied the structure of a sinfonia before writing his own orchestral piece.
The concert opened with a lively sinfonia that set the tone for the evening.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concerto - a musical work for solo instrument and orchestra
  • synonym: symphony - a long musical composition for orchestra, usually in several movements
