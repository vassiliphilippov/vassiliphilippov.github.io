offset

noun
resulting consequence of development
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈɑfˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈɒf.sɛt / (BrE)
a consequence or result arising naturally from the development of something.

Examples

An offset of growth
Increased pollution may be an unavoidable offset of rapid urban development.
The creation of new industries is an offset of technological progress.
Urban sprawl is often an offset of economic growth in large cities.
