kelpwort

noun
plant of marshes/beaches, source of soda ash
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛlpˌwɜrt / (AmE), / ˈkɛlpˌwɜːt / (BrE)
A bushy plant found in salt marshes and sea beaches of the Old World, with prickly leaves and formerly burned to produce crude soda ash.

Examples

Bushy kelpwort plant
Historically, kelpwort was burned to produce soda ash for glassmaking.
Sharp, prickly leaves distinguish the kelpwort from other beach plants.
The kelpwort thrives in salty marshes along the coastline.
