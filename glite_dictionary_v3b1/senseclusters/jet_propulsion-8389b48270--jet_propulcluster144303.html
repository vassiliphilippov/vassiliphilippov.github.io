jet propulsion

noun
propulsion by expelling fluid backward
Pronunciation: / dʒɛt prəˈploʊʒən / (AmE), / dʒɛt prəˈpləʊʒən / (BrE)
the propulsion of an object by expelling a jet of fluid backward, typically used in aircraft and rockets

Examples

Jet propulsion system
Early fighter planes relied on propellers, but modern jets use jet propulsion for much greater speed.
Some aquatic animals, like squid, move quickly underwater using a form of jet propulsion.
The concept of jet propulsion revolutionized both aviation and space travel.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jet engine - an engine that generates thrust by expelling a jet of fluid
  • synonym: rocket propulsion - propulsion using the expulsion of gases from a rocket engine
