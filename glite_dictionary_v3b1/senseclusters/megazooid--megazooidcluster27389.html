megazooid

noun
large specialized zooid
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛɡəˈzuːɔɪd / (AmE), / ˌmiːɡəˈzuːɔɪd / (BrE)
a large individual zooid within a colonial organism, often having a specialized function such as defense

Examples

Large megazooid cell
The megazooid is responsible for protecting the colony from predators.
Scientists observed that the megazooid had a thicker outer layer than other zooids.
In some species, the megazooid develops specialized structures for capturing food.
