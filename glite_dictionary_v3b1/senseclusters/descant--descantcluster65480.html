descant

verb
talk at length about a subject
4.6 7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈde.skænt/ /; / /ˈde.skənt/ (AmE), /ˈde.skænt/ /; / /ˈde.skənt/ (BrE)
to talk or write at length about a particular subject, especially in a detailed or elaborate way

Examples

To descant on history
He descanted on the history of the architecture for over an hour.
She often descants on her favorite novels during book club meetings.
The professor descanted upon the importance of ethical conduct in research.

Related Terms

  • synonym: expatiate - to speak or write in detail about a subject
  • synonym: ramble - to talk or write at length in a confused or inconsequential way
