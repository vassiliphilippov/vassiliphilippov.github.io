daisy chain

noun
a series of connected things or people
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪzi tʃeɪn / (AmE), / ˈdeɪzi tʃeɪn / (BrE)
A series of connected people, things, or events, each linked to the next in succession, often forming a loop or chain.

Examples

A daisy chain of events
A complex daisy chain of transactions was used to move the money across various accounts.
The project turned into a daisy chain of meetings, each leading to the next without resolution.
Their plan depended on a daisy chain of favors from people within different departments.
