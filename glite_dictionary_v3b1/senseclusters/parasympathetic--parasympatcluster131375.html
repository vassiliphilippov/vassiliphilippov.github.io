parasympathetic

noun
part of the autonomic nervous system controlling rest and digestion
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛrəˌsɪmpəˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌpærəsɪmpəˈθɛtɪk / (BrE)
the branch of the autonomic nervous system that originates in the brain stem and lower spinal cord, and is responsible for functions such as stimulating digestive secretions, slowing the heart rate, constricting pupils, and dilating blood vessels, generally opposing the action of the sympathetic nervous system

Examples

Parasympathetic nervous system
Activation of the parasympathetic branch promotes a sense of calm and rest in the body.
Damage to the parasympathetic nerves can affect digestion and heart rate.
The parasympathetic system helps your body relax after periods of stress.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sympathetic - the branch of the autonomic nervous system that prepares the body for activity or stress
