muon

noun
a heavy elementary particle similar to the electron
9.9 8.5
Pronunciation: /ˈmjuː.ɑːn/ /; / /ˈmjuː.ən/ (AmE), /ˈmjuː.ɒn/ /; / /ˈmjuː.ən/ (BrE)
an elementary subatomic particle with a negative electric charge and a half-life of about 2 microseconds, similar to the electron but with much greater mass

Examples

A muon particle
Cosmic rays interacting with Earth's atmosphere can produce muons that reach ground level.
Scientists detected a muon as it passed through the cloud chamber.
The muon is similar to the electron but is much heavier.

Related Terms

  • synonym: electron - a stable subatomic particle with a negative electric charge
  • degree higher: tau - an elementary particle heavier than the muon
