stern chaser

noun
naval gun for firing astern
Pronunciation: / stɝn ˈtʃeɪsər / (AmE), / stɜːn ˈtʃeɪsə / (BrE)
A naval gun mounted at the stern of a ship, used to fire at a pursuing enemy vessel.

Examples

The stern chaser gun
During the sea battle, the captain ordered the crew to man the stern chaser as the enemy closed in from behind.
Old naval vessels were equipped with both bow and stern chasers to defend against attackers.
The ship's stern chaser was ready to fire at the approaching frigate.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bow chaser - a gun at the bow for firing at a ship ahead
