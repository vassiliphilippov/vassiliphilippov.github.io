rue

noun
strong-scented European herb with bitter leaves
9.8 7.6
Pronunciation: / ruː / (AmE), / ruː / (BrE)
a strong-scented herb (Ruta graveolens) with bitter leaves, sometimes used in cooking or medicine, but can cause skin irritation

Examples

Fresh rue leaves
Contact with fresh rue can cause a rash similar to poison ivy.
She grows rue in her herb garden for its distinctive aroma.
Some people use rue leaves in traditional remedies despite their bitterness.
Historically, rue was believed to protect against evil spirits and was grown in many herb gardens.
She used a tiny amount of rue to flavor the stew, warning that too much could be harmful.
The gardener wore gloves when handling rue because its leaves can irritate the skin.
