subduable

adjective
able to be subdued or overcome
Pronunciation: / səbˈduː.ə.bəl / (AmE), / səbˈdjuː.ə.bəl / (BrE)
capable of being subdued or brought under control; susceptible to being overcome.

Examples

A subduable spirit
Her stubborn spirit proved barely subduable even after months of persistent effort.
Some wild animals are only subduable through patient and gentle training.
The rebel forces believed the government was subduable with enough determination and support.

Related Terms

  • antonym: invincible - impossible to defeat or subdue
  • antonym: irresistible - impossible to resist or withstand
