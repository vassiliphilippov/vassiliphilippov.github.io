subduable

adjective
able to be subdued or overcome
8.2 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / s…ôbÀàduÀê.…ô.b…ôl / (AmE), / s…ôbÀàdjuÀê.…ô.b…ôl / (BrE)
capable of being subdued or brought under control; susceptible to being overcome.

Examples

A subduable spirit
Her stubborn spirit proved barely subduable even after months of persistent effort.
Some wild animals are only subduable through patient and gentle training.
The rebel forces believed the government was subduable with enough determination and support.

Synonyms

    9.9 0.00/m
    subjugable describes being brought under domination by others, while subduable focuses on being physically or socially brought under control.
  • conquerable
    5.6 0.01/m
    conquerable refers to territory or people that can be taken by force, while subduable emphasizes being brought under control.
  • vanquishable
    8.6 0.00/m

  • beatable
    3.7 0.19/m

  • vincible

  • superable
    9.9 0.00/m
    superable applies to problems or obstacles that can be overcome, while subduable refers to beings or forces brought under control.
