aversive

adjective
tending to repel or discourage
8.4 1.57/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàv…úrs…™v / (AmE), / …ôÀàv…úÀês…™v / (BrE)
causing avoidance or discouragement; tending to repel or dissuade, especially by being unpleasant or undesirable

Examples

An aversive stimulus
Aversive conditioning uses unpleasant stimuli to prevent unwanted behaviors.
Many people find the taste of grapefruit juice aversive and avoid it.
The loud, unexpected noise had an aversive effect on the animals, causing them to flee.

Synonyms

  • dissuasive
    8.7 0.02/m
    dissuasive aims to discourage a person from acting, while aversive refers to something unpleasant that makes people avoid it.
  • rebarbative
    9.9 0.02/m
    rebarbative sounds formal and means unattractive or offensive, while aversive simply describes something that causes avoidance.
  • repellent
    6.1 0.36/m
    repellent causes strong dislike or disgust, while aversive covers anything unpleasant that makes people avoid or reject it.
  • repulsive
    5.5 2.69/m
    repulsive is extremely unpleasant and often causes disgust, while aversive is less strong and simply makes people avoid it.
  • disgustful
    6.7 0.00/m
    disgustful causes a strong feeling of revulsion in people, while aversive can be unpleasant without causing deep disgust.
