dissent

noun
difference of opinion; disagreement
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsɛnt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛnt / (BrE)
the act of disagreeing or expressing opposition, especially in a formal or public way

Examples

Public dissent
Honest dissent is essential in a healthy democracy.
The judge wrote a powerful dissent against the majority decision.
There was little room for dissent during the heated debate.
His article sparked a wave of dissent throughout the university community.
Public dissent grew after the controversial law was passed.
The activists staged a dissent against the government's new policy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agreement - harmony or accord in opinion or feeling
  • synonym: disagreement - a lack of consensus or approval
  • synonym: protest - an organized public demonstration expressing strong objection
