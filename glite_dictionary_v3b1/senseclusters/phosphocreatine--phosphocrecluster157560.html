phosphocreatine

noun
energy-storing muscle compound
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˌfɑsfoʊˈkriːəˌtin / (AmE), / ˌfɒsfəʊˈkriːətiːn / (BrE)
a compound formed from creatine and phosphoric acid, found in muscle tissue and involved in the storage and release of energy for muscle contraction

Examples

Phosphocreatine in muscles
During intense exercise, phosphocreatine provides a rapid source of energy for muscle contractions.
Patients with certain muscle disorders may have low levels of phosphocreatine in their tissues.
The breakdown of phosphocreatine helps regenerate ATP during short bursts of activity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: creatine - a compound in muscle cells used for energy production
