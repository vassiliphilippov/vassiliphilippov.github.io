animacy

noun
quality of being alive
Pronunciation: / ˈænɪməsi / (AmE), / ˈænɪməsi / (BrE)
the quality of being alive, life-like, or having life (sometimes used as a grammatical distinction in language)

Examples

The animacy distinction
Some languages use animacy to determine how nouns are declined or used in sentences.
The concept of animacy helps explain why certain objects are treated like people in stories.
In grammar, animacy can affect verb agreement and pronoun choice.
The animacy of the characters in the film made them feel real to the audience.
Linguists study how different languages mark animacy in their grammar.
Her sculptures are admired for the sense of animacy they convey despite being made of stone.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inanimacy - the quality of not being alive
  • synonym: life - the state of being alive
  • antonym: lifelessness - the state of lacking life or energy
  • synonym: vitality - the state of being strong and full of energy
