non-cash expense

noun
accounting expense not paid in cash
Pronunciation: / nɑn kæʃ ɪkˈspɛns / (AmE), / nɒn kæʃ ɪkˈspɛns / (BrE)
An expense recorded in accounting that does not involve an actual cash payment, such as depreciation or amortization.

Examples

A non-cash expense item
Depreciation is a common non-cash expense that reduces taxable income.
The financial statement lists several non-cash expenses for the year.
When calculating net income, it's important to account for every non-cash expense.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amortization - gradual write-off of an asset's cost
  • synonym: depreciation - reduction in value of an asset over time
