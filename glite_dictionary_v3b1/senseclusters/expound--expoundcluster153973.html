expound

verb
to explain in detail or at length
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈspaʊnd / (AmE), / ɪkˈspaʊnd / (BrE)
to explain or present something in detail, often thoroughly or clearly

Examples

To expound a theory
During the lecture, the professor expounded the theory in great detail.
In his latest book, the author expounds the importance of ethical leadership.
She was invited to expound on her views during the conference.
During the meeting, she expounded her views on the company's new policy.
He expounded the main principles of his argument in clear, simple language.
The professor expounded on the philosophy of science for over an hour.

Related Terms

  • synonym: elaborate - to add more detail or information to something
  • synonym: explain - make something clear by describing it in detail
  • synonym: outline - give a summary of the main points
  • antonym: summarize - to give a brief statement of the main points
