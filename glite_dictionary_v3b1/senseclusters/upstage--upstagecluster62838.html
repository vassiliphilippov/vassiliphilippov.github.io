upstage

adjective
at or toward the back of a stage
3.3
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpˌsteɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈʌpsteɪdʒ / (BrE)
(in theatre) situated at or toward the back part of the stage, away from the audience.

Examples

Upstage position
He took an upstage position during the scene to create a sense of distance.
Props that are placed upstage are less visible to the audience.
The actor quickly moved upstage to avoid drawing attention during the main character’s speech.

Related Terms

  • antonym: downstage - at or toward the front of the stage, nearest the audience
