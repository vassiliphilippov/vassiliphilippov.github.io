rated voltage

noun
maximum safe operating voltage for a device
2.6 4.4
Pronunciation: /ˈreɪ.tɪd ˈvoʊl.tɪdʒ/ /; / /ˈreɪ.təd ˈvoʊl.tədʒ/ (AmE), /ˈreɪ.tɪd ˈvəʊl.tɪdʒ/ /; / /ˈreɪ.təd ˈvəʊl.tədʒ/ (BrE)
The maximum voltage at which a device, such as a circuit-breaker, can operate safely without risk of damage or excessive arcing.

Examples

The rated voltage limit
Make sure the circuit operates below the rated voltage of the components to avoid damage.
Never exceed the rated voltage, as it could result in hazardous conditions or equipment failure.
The rated voltage is clearly indicated on the nameplate of the electrical device.
