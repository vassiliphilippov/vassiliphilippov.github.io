carvel-built

adjective
shipbuilding method using smooth, flush hull planks
6.3 8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːrvəl bɪlt / (AmE), / ˈkɑːvəl bɪlt / (BrE)
(of ships) having a hull in which the planks are laid edge to edge, making a smooth surface, rather than overlapping.

Examples

Carvel-built ship
During the 15th century, Portuguese explorers favored carvel-built ships for ocean voyages.
Modern replicas of old ships often use carvel-built construction to ensure historical accuracy.
The yacht was carvel-built, giving it a sleek and watertight hull.
