epanorthosis

noun
rhetorical rephrasing for emphasis or correction
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpəˌnɔrˈθoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌɛpænɔːˈθəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A rhetorical device involving the immediate rephrasing of a statement to intensify or correct its meaning.

Examples

Epanorthosis in speech
Her use of epanorthosis made her arguments sound more passionate and urgent.
Literary critics often discuss epanorthosis in the analysis of dramatic monologues.
The speaker used epanorthosis to sharpen his point, correcting his words mid-sentence for greater effect.
