to make a general statement or conclusion from specific cases, or to apply general patterns to new situations

Examples

To generalize from facts
It is not always accurate to generalize from a small sample of data.
Scientists must avoid generalizing too quickly based on limited evidence.
She tends to generalize about people after only one bad experience.
It is easy to generalize about cultures without understanding their complexities.
Please don't generalize; every situation is different and deserves individual attention.
When people generalize from one bad experience, they often make unfair assumptions.
The model must generalize from training data to unseen examples to perform well.
After reducing overfitting, the neural network could generalize across different image datasets without extra tuning.
Researchers measure a model's ability to generalize using held-out validation sets during experiments.

Synonyms

  • extrapolate
    8.5 3.78/m
    extrapolate projects known data beyond observed points to predict new cases, while generalize draws broad rules from specific examples.
  • infer
    5.5 7.59/m
    infer uses evidence or reasoning to reach a specific conclusion, while generalize makes broader rules from several examples.

Related Terms

  • synonym: extrapolate - to infer or estimate values or outcomes beyond the observed data; similar to applying learned patterns to new cases.
  • synonym: generalize - US spelling of 'generalise'
  • antonym: overfit - to fit a model too closely to the training data so it performs poorly on new data; opposite of generalize in ML.
  • antonym: specify - to state or identify clearly and definitely; to mention explicitly
