hot war

noun
war involving active combat
8.5
Pronunciation: / hɑt wɔr / (AmE), / hɒt wɔː / (BrE)
A war involving actual fighting and direct military engagement between conflicting parties, as opposed to political or economic conflict.

Examples

A hot war breaks
During the twentieth century, several hot wars erupted despite diplomatic efforts.
Leaders were desperate to prevent the Cold War from turning into a hot war.
The threat of a hot war in the region has caused international concern.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cold war - A state of political hostility without open warfare between major powers
