acute pyelonephritis

noun
sudden severe kidney infection
5.8
Pronunciation: / əˈkjut ˌpaɪəloʊnəˈfraɪtɪs / (AmE), / əˈkjuːt ˌpaɪələʊnɪˈfraɪtɪs / (BrE)
A sudden and severe kidney infection, usually caused by bacteria spreading from the bladder.

Examples

Severe acute pyelonephritis infection
Antibiotics are the primary treatment for acute pyelonephritis to clear the bacterial infection.
If left untreated, acute pyelonephritis can lead to serious kidney damage.
The patient was diagnosed with acute pyelonephritis after presenting with high fever and flank pain.
