slagging

noun
build-up of slag
8.3
Pronunciation: /ˈslæ.gɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈslæ.gɪŋ/ (BrE)
the accumulation or formation of slag on the surfaces of a furnace, boiler, or kiln, often causing blockages or reduced efficiency

Examples

Furnace slagging
Heavy slagging in the furnace reduced the plant's efficiency and required immediate cleaning.
Engineers monitor slagging to prevent blockages in the boiler tubes during operation.
Regular maintenance helps control slagging and keeps the kiln running smoothly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clogging - the process of becoming blocked or obstructed
  • synonym: fouling - the accumulation of unwanted material on solid surfaces
