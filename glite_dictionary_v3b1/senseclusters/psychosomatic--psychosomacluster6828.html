psychosomatic

adjective
relating to mind-induced physical illness
8.5 6.2
Pronunciation: / saɪˌkoʊsəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / saɪˌkəʊsəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
(of a physical illness or symptom) caused or aggravated by mental factors such as stress or emotional conflict rather than by direct physical causes.

Examples

Psychosomatic illness
He suffered from chronic headaches that were later diagnosed as psychosomatic.
Some patients experience psychosomatic disorders triggered by anxiety or emotional trauma.
The doctor explained that her psychosomatic symptoms were likely caused by extreme stress.

Related Terms

  • antonym: somatic - relating to the body, especially as distinct from the mind
