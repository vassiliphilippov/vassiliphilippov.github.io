interject

verb
to interrupt with a remark
7.9 5.3
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.tɚˈdʒɛkt / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈdʒɛkt / (BrE)
to say or add something suddenly, especially as an interruption or between other things being said

Examples

To interject a comment
"Excuse me," she interjected during their heated debate.
He tried to interject a question, but the speaker ignored him.
Whenever the discussion gets lively, Tom can't help interjecting his own opinions.
