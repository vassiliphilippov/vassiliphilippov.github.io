succory

noun
perennial plant (chicory), edible roots and leaves
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆsÊŒkÉ™ri / (AmE), / ËˆsÊŒkÉ™ri / (BrE)
A perennial plant of the daisy family with blue flowers, cultivated for its edible roots and leaves used in salads; also known as chicory.

Examples

Succory plant leaves
Farmers often grow succory for its roots, which are roasted and ground as a coffee substitute.
Fresh succory leaves add a pleasant bitterness to mixed green salads.
Wild succory can be found blooming along roadsides in the summer.

Synonyms

  • Cichorium intybus
    8.6 0.00/m
    Cichorium intybus is the scientific name used by botanists in books and labels, while succory is the common food or garden name.
  • chicory
    6.5 0.16/m
    chicory is the everyday name for the blue-flowered plant used in salads and coffee, while succory is a less common variant name.
  • blue succory
    9.9 0.00/m
