lubber's hole

noun
nautical: hole in a mast platform for climbing
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlʌbɚz hoʊl / (AmE), / ˈlʌbəz həʊl / (BrE)
A hole in the platform at the top of a ship's mast, allowing a sailor to climb through without leaving the inside of the rigging.

Examples

The lubber's hole on mast
He was mocked for choosing the lubber's hole rather than facing the rigging.
Old ships often had a lubber's hole for less experienced crew members.
The sailor climbed through the lubber's hole instead of venturing onto the shrouds.
