prevarication

noun
the act of evading the truth
5.4
Pronunciation: / prɪˌværɪˈkeɪʃən /; / prɪˌvɛrɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / prɪˌvarɪˈkeɪʃən /; / prɪˌværɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of avoiding telling the truth by being vague, ambiguous, or deliberately misleading

Examples

Constant prevarication
Her constant prevarication during the interview frustrated the investigators looking for clear answers.
The politician's prevarication on the policy issue was obvious to everyone watching the debate.
When asked about the missing funds, his prevarication only raised more suspicions among the auditors.
His prevarication during the interview made everyone doubt his honesty.
Instead of a direct reply, she resorted to prevarication and avoided the issue.
The manager's prevarication frustrated employees who wanted clear answers.
In court, any sign of prevarication can cast doubt on a defendant’s credibility.
Politicians are often accused of prevarication when answering difficult questions.
The witness’s prevarication made it difficult to determine what really happened.

Related Terms

  • antonym: directness - the quality of being straightforward or honest
  • synonym: evasiveness - the quality of avoiding commitment or self-revelation
  • antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful and sincere
  • synonym: lie - a false statement with the intent to deceive
  • antonym: truthfulness - the quality of being honest and telling the truth
