inadequacy

noun
the state of being inadequate
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈædɪkwəsi / (AmE), / ɪnˈadɪkwəsi / (BrE)
the state of not being enough or not having the necessary quality, ability, or amount to meet a need or standard

Examples

A feeling of inadequacy
Many students feel a sense of inadequacy when faced with challenging tasks.
The inadequacy of funds led to the project's cancellation.
The report highlighted the inadequacy of current safety procedures at the plant.
His feeling of inadequacy kept him from applying for the promotion.
The team's inadequacy became apparent during the final game.
There is a clear inadequacy in their training for such complex tasks.
Many critics pointed out the inadequacy of the government's response to the disaster.
The report highlighted the inadequacy of resources available for the new healthcare program.
There is a clear inadequacy of funding for public schools in this area.

Related Terms

  • antonym: adequacy - the state of being sufficient or satisfactory
  • antonym: competence - the ability to do something successfully or efficiently
  • synonym: insufficiency - the state of not being enough or adequate
