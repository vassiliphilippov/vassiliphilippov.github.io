ultrafiltration

noun
fine filtration process
Pronunciation: / ˌʌltrəfɪlˈtreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌʌltrəfɪlˈtreɪʃən / (BrE)
a process that removes very small particles from a liquid by passing it through a fine filter or membrane

Examples

Water ultrafiltration system
Modern water treatment plants use ultrafiltration to remove bacteria and viruses from drinking water.
The laboratory relies on ultrafiltration to separate proteins from other molecules in the solution.
During kidney dialysis, ultrafiltration helps remove excess fluid from the blood.

Related Terms

  • synonym: filtration - the process of removing particles from a liquid or gas by passing it through a filter
  • synonym: purification - the process of making something clean by removing unwanted substances
