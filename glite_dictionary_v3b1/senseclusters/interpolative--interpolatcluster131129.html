interpolative

adjective
inserted into text
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɜːrpəˌleɪtɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːpələtɪv / (BrE)
describing something that has been inserted into a text, record, or speech, often by a later author or editor

Examples

Interpolative passage in a book
The historian argued that the chapter was clearly interpolative and not part of the original manuscript.
Some critics consider the new dialogue in the play to be interpolative and unnecessary.
Scholars debate whether the poem's ending is authentic or interpolative in nature.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inserted - placed or added into something else
  • antonym: original - existing from the beginning; not added or changed
