infirm

adjective
physically weak, especially from age or illness
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfɝm / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɜːm / (BrE)
lacking bodily or muscular strength, especially because of age or illness; weak or frail.

Examples

An infirm patient
After the fall, the infirm gentleman needed help with everyday tasks.
The nursing home provides special care for infirm elderly residents.
Years of illness left her feeling too infirm to go outside.

Related Terms

  • synonym: feeble - lacking physical strength, especially due to age or illness
  • antonym: robust - strong and healthy; vigorous
