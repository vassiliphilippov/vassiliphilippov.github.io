flightless bird

noun
bird unable to fly
3.3 3.1
Pronunciation: /ˈflaɪt.ləs bɜːrd/ (AmE), /ˈflaɪt.ləs bɜːd/ (BrE)
a bird that is unable to fly, usually because of its body structure or evolution (for example, an ostrich, emu, or kiwi)

Examples

A flightless bird species
Several species of flightless birds inhabit New Zealand, including the famous kiwi.

Many islands are home to unique species of flightless birds that evolved in the absence of predators.
The dodo was a famous flightless bird that became extinct in the 17th century.
