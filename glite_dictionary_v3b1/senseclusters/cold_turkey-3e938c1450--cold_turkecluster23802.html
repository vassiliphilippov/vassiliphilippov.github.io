cold turkey

noun
sudden, complete withdrawal from a dependency
Pronunciation: / koʊld ˈtɝki /; / koʊld ˈtɝr.ki / (AmE), / kəʊld ˈtɜːki /; / kəʊld ˈtɜː.ki / (BrE)
the act of suddenly and completely stopping an addictive substance or habit, without gradually reducing it

Examples

Quit cold turkey
After years of drinking coffee, she decided to quit cold turkey.
He stopped taking painkillers cold turkey and faced severe withdrawal symptoms.
Some people find it easier to quit smoking gradually rather than going cold turkey.
After years of smoking, she decided to quit cold turkey and never looked back.
Going cold turkey can cause withdrawal symptoms for people with serious addictions.
He gave up caffeine cold turkey, which made the first week very difficult.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gradually - to do something slowly over time (antonym for abrupt quitting)
  • synonym: withdrawal - the process of ceasing to use an addictive substance
