unmortified

adjective
not spiritually disciplined
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈmɔrtɪˌfaɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈmɔːtɪfaɪd / (BrE)
not having been spiritually or physically disciplined; not having one's desires, passions, or body subdued, especially in religious contexts

Examples

Unmortified desires
He lived an unmortified life, indulging in every comfort despite his religious vows.
Her unmortified desires often conflicted with the strict teachings of her faith.
The monk warned against remaining unmortified in the pursuit of spiritual growth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mortified - having subdued one's desires or body, especially for religious reasons
  • synonym: undisciplined - lacking self-control or restraint
