cover slip

noun
thin glass for microscope specimens
4.4 4.3
Pronunciation: /ˈkʌv.ər slɪp/ (AmE), /ˈkʌv.ə slɪp/ (BrE)
a small, thin piece of glass placed over a specimen on a microscope slide to protect it and aid viewing under a microscope

Examples

A cover slip for microscope
Always use a clean cover slip when examining new specimens under the microscope.
Place the cover slip gently over the specimen to avoid air bubbles.
The student accidentally broke the cover slip while preparing the slide.
