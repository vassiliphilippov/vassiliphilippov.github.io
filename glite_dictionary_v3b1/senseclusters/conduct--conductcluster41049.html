conduct

noun
way of behaving
3.7 26/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëÀên.d…ôkt /; / k…ônÀàd åkt / (AmE), / Ààk…ín.d åkt /; / k…ônÀàd åkt / (BrE)
the way a person behaves or acts, especially in a particular situation

Examples

Professional conduct
All employees are expected to maintain proper conduct in the workplace.
Her conduct at the meeting reflected her professionalism and respect for others.
The principal praised the student's exemplary conduct during the ceremony.
Proper conduct is expected from all students in the classroom.
Questions were raised about the manager's conduct at the company party.
The teacher praised her exemplary conduct during the field trip.

Synonyms

  • behavior
    1.8 146/m
    behavior shows how a person acts in situations or toward others, while conduct focuses on actions in a particular situation.
  • manner
    1.0 15/m
    manner is the specific style someone uses to act or speak, while conduct means their overall actions in a situation.
  • demeanor
    4.9 5.57/m
    demeanor shows a person's outward attitude and manner to others, while conduct covers their actions in a given situation.
  • deportment
    7.6 0.45/m
    deportment is formal behavior shown in social settings or schools, while conduct covers a person's actions in particular situations.
