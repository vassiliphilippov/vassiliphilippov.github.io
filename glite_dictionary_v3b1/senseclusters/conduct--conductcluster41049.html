conduct

noun
way of behaving
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːn.dəkt /; / kənˈdʌkt / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.dʌkt /; / kənˈdʌkt / (BrE)
the way a person behaves or acts, especially in a particular situation

Examples

Professional conduct
All employees are expected to maintain proper conduct in the workplace.
Her conduct at the meeting reflected her professionalism and respect for others.
The principal praised the student's exemplary conduct during the ceremony.
Proper conduct is expected from all students in the classroom.
Questions were raised about the manager's conduct at the company party.
The teacher praised her exemplary conduct during the field trip.
