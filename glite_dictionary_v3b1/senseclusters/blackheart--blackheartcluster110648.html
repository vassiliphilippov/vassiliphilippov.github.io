blackheart

noun
plant disease causing blackened center
7.1 8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈblækˌhɑrt / (AmE), / ˈblækˌhɑːt / (BrE)
A disease affecting plants or crops in which the central tissues turn black, often caused by environmental factors or infection.

Examples

Blackheart disease
Farmers inspect their crops regularly to prevent the spread of blackheart.
Poor ventilation in storage facilities can lead to blackheart in apples.
Potatoes affected by blackheart often show a darkened core upon cutting.
