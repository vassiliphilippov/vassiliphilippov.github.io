cold

noun
sensation of low temperature
Pronunciation: / koʊld / (AmE), / kəʊld / (BrE)
the sensation or state of low temperature; the absence of heat

Examples

The winter cold
After standing outside for hours, the cold seeped into her bones.
He wrapped his coat tighter to protect himself from the cold.
The cold made it difficult for them to stay outdoors for long.
In the cold of winter, the lakes often freeze solid.
Prolonged exposure to extreme cold can lead to frostbite.
Take your coat, or you'll feel the cold as soon as you step outside.
Exposure to extreme cold can be dangerous without proper clothing.
He hurried inside to escape the cold of the winter morning.
The cold was so intense that our breath became visible in the air.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: chill - a feeling or quality of coldness, usually slightly less intense than cold
  • degree lower: coolness - moderately low temperature; less intense than cold
  • degree higher: freeze - an extremely cold condition where liquids harden into solid due to loss of heat
  • degree higher: freezing - the process or state of becoming frozen; extremely low temperature
  • antonym: heat - the sensation produced by high temperatures
  • antonym: warmth - the quality or state of being warm; moderate heat
