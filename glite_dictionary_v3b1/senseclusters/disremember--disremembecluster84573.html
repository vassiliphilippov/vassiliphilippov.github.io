disremember

verb
to refuse to recall
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsrɪˈmɛmbər / (AmE), / ˌdɪsrɪˈmɛmbə / (BrE)
to deliberately refuse to remember or try to erase something from memory

Examples

To disremember the past
After the argument, she chose to disremember her former friend's name.
The community seemed to disremember his contributions after the controversy.
He tried to disremember the painful events of his childhood.
The old man claimed he could not disremember the events of his youth, no matter how hard he tried.
In her letter, she begged him to disremember her past mistakes and forgive her.
Historians sometimes disremember uncomfortable facts when writing about national heroes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: remember - to recall or keep in mind
