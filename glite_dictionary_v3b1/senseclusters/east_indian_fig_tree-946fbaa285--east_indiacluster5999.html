East Indian fig tree

noun
a tropical tree producing aerial roots, also banyan tree
7.6 6.0
Pronunciation: /iːst ˈɪn.diː.ən fɪg triː/ /; / /iːst ˈɪn.dɪ.ən fɪg triː/ /; / /iːst ˈɪn.diː.ən fɪg tʃriː/ (AmE), /iːst ˈɪn.dɪ.ən fɪg triː/ /; / /iːst ˈɪn.dɪ.ən fɪg tʃriː/ (BrE)
an East Indian tree (Ficus benghalensis) that sends out aerial shoots which grow down into the soil and form additional trunks; also known as the banyan tree.

Examples

The East Indian fig tree
Locals gathered for shade under the massive East Indian fig tree on the village green.
The East Indian fig tree can develop an entire grove from a single original trunk by sending out aerial roots.
The roots of the East Indian fig tree grew down from its branches and anchored themselves in the earth.
