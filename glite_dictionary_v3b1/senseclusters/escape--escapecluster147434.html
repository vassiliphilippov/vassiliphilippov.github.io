escape

verb
break free, run away (verb)
Pronunciation: / ɪˈskeɪp / (AmE), / ɪˈskeɪp / (BrE)
to get away from a place, danger, harm, or responsibility

Examples

To escape prison
After months of planning, they escaped the fortress undetected.
Several prisoners managed to escape during the confusion.
The bird escaped from its cage as soon as the door opened.
He tried to escape but was quickly caught by the guards.
Several animals escaped from the zoo during the storm last night.
The prisoner managed to escape through a hole in the fence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: capture - to catch and keep someone who is trying to escape
  • synonym: flee - run away from a place or situation of danger
  • antonym: surrender - give in or submit to control, stop resisting
