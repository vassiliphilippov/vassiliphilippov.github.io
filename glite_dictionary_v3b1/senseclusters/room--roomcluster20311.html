room

verb
live as a paying guest or lodger
Pronunciation: / ruːm / (AmE), / ruːm / (BrE)
to live in a place, especially as a lodger or paying guest, often sharing accommodation with others

Examples

To room with friends
She rooms in a house near the university to save on rent.
They are rooming together for the summer internship in the city.
While studying at college, I roomed with three other students in a small apartment.
