Joseph Haydn

proper noun
Austrian classical composer, 1732–1809
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˌjoʊzəf ˈhaɪdn̩ / (AmE), / ˌjəʊzəf ˈhaɪdn / (BrE)
(1732–1809) An Austrian composer who was a leading figure in the development of classical music, especially the symphony and the string quartet.

Examples

Joseph Haydn composer
Joseph Haydn is often called the 'Father of the Symphony' for his pivotal role in the genre's development.
The music of Joseph Haydn had a profound influence on later composers such as Mozart and Beethoven.
We studied several compositions by Joseph Haydn in our history of classical music course.

  • synonym: Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart - Austrian composer and contemporary of Haydn
