scratch that

verb
used to retract a previous statement
4.3
Pronunciation: / skrætʃ ðæt / (AmE), / skrætʃ ðat / (BrE)
used to indicate that something just said should be ignored or disregarded; to cancel a previous statement or plan.

Examples

To scratch that idea
He said he'd join us, but scratch that, he's busy with work.
I was going to recommend the Italian restaurant, but scratch that; it's closed today.
Wait, scratch that—let's meet at noon instead of eleven.

Related Terms

  • synonym: never mind - an expression used to tell someone to disregard a previous statement
