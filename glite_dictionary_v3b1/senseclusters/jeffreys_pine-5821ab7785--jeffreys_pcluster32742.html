Jeffrey's pine

noun
species of pine tree native to western North America
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛfriz paɪn / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛfriz paɪn / (BrE)
a tall pine tree of western North America with long blue-green needles in groups of three, elongated cones, and spreading, somewhat drooping branches

Examples

Jeffrey's pine tree
Forests in the Sierra Nevada are dominated by Jeffrey's pine and ponderosa pine.
Many hikers enjoy the scent of the resin produced by Jeffrey's pine trees.
The Jeffrey's pine can be identified by its distinctive long needles and large cones.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ponderosa pine - a related species of pine often confused with Jeffrey's pine
