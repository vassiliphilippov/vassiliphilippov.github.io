Pandora's box

noun
mythical box releasing evils; source of trouble
Pronunciation: / pænˈdɔrəz ˈbɑks / (AmE), / pænˈdɔːrəz ˈbɒks / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a box given to Pandora by Zeus, which, when opened, released all the evils and miseries into the world. By extension, a source of unexpected and widespread trouble or problems.

Examples

Open Pandora's box
He knew that investigating the old case would open a Pandora's box of problems.
Social media can be a Pandora's box, bringing both opportunities and new kinds of trouble.
The new law turned out to be a Pandora's box of unintended consequences.

Related Terms

  • synonym: can of worms - a situation that, once started, is likely to become problematic or complicated
