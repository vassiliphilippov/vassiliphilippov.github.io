hoisin sauce

noun
Chinese sweet and savory sauce
Pronunciation: / ˈhɔɪsɪn sɔs / (AmE), / ˈhɔɪsɪn sɔːs / (BrE)
A thick, sweet, and tangy Chinese sauce made from soybeans, garlic, vinegar, and various spices, used especially as a condiment or marinade.

Examples

A spoonful of hoisin sauce
He added a spoonful of hoisin sauce to the stir-fry for extra flavor.
The duck pancakes were served with cucumber, scallions, and hoisin sauce.
You can find bottles of hoisin sauce in most Asian grocery stores.
