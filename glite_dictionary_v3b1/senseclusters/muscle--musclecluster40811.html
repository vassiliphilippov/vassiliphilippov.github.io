muscle

noun
power or influence (figurative)
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / ˈmʌs.əl / (BrE)
power, influence, or authority, especially when applied forcefully or coercively to achieve a goal.

Examples

Political muscle
The company showed political muscle by persuading lawmakers to change the regulations.
The union used its muscle to negotiate a better contract for its members.
They don’t have enough muscle in the organization to push that policy through.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authority - the power or right to give orders or make decisions
  • synonym: clout - informal term for influence or power
  • synonym: influence - the capacity to affect the behavior of others
  • antonym: weakness - lack of strength or power
