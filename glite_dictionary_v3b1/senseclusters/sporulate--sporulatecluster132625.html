sporulate

verb
produce or form spores
Pronunciation: / ˈspɔr.ju.leɪt /; / ˈspɔːr.jə.leɪt / (AmE), / ˈspɔː.rjʊ.leɪt /; / ˈspɔːr.jʊ.leɪt / (BrE)
to produce or release spores (as a fungus, bacterium, plant, or similar organism)

Examples

To sporulate bacteria
Certain bacteria sporulate when conditions become unfavorable for growth.
If the yeast fails to sporulate in the laboratory, the experiment may have been set up incorrectly.
Mushrooms sporulate under moist, warm conditions, releasing spores into the air.
After infection, the bacteria quickly sporulate and spread throughout the tissue.
Many ferns sporulate in late summer as part of their reproductive cycle.
Some fungi sporulate when environmental conditions become unfavorable.
