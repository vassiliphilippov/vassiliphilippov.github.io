degranulation

noun
release of cell granules
9.3
Pronunciation: / diˌɡræn.jəˈleɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / diˌɡræn.jʊˈleɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the process by which cells release granules containing chemicals, especially during immune or allergic responses

Examples

Degranulation of mast cells
The allergic reaction was triggered by the degranulation of mast cells.
Scientists observed degranulation under the microscope during the immune response experiment.
Certain medications can inhibit degranulation to reduce inflammation in allergic patients.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exocytosis - the process by which cells expel substances to the outside
