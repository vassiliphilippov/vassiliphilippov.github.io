wetter

noun
chemical that reduces surface tension
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛtər / (AmE), / ˈwɛtə / (BrE)
a chemical agent that reduces the surface tension of a liquid in which it is dissolved, making it spread more easily

Examples

Paint wetter
Adding a wetter helps the paint to spread evenly on the surface.
In agriculture, a wetter is sometimes mixed with pesticides to increase their effectiveness.
The laboratory tested several wetters to improve the efficiency of the solution.

Related Terms

  • synonym: surfactant - a substance that reduces the surface tension of a liquid; synonym in scientific context
