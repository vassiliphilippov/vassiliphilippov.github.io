fund

noun
financial institution for pooled investment
Pronunciation: / fʌnd / (AmE), / fʌnd / (BrE)
a financial institution that pools money from investors to buy securities such as stocks or bonds, and sells shares to individuals.

Examples

A pension fund
Many people rely on a retirement fund to secure their financial future.
She invested in an international fund to diversify her portfolio.
The fund offers shares to individual investors and manages assets worth billions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mutual fund - an investment fund managed for a group of investors
  • synonym: pension fund - a fund set up to pay retirement benefits
