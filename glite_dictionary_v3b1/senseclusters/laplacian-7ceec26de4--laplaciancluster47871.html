Laplacian

noun
graph theory matrix
9.9
Pronunciation: /lə.ˈpleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /læp.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /lə.ˈpleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /læp.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a matrix in graph theory that represents a discrete version of the Laplace operator, used to analyze properties of graphs and networks

Examples

Laplacian matrix
The Laplacian of a graph is essential for studying its connectivity and clustering properties.
Researchers used the Laplacian to identify important nodes in the network.
Calculating the eigenvalues of the Laplacian helps reveal the structure of complex graphs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: incidence matrix - a matrix showing relationships between edges and vertices in a graph
