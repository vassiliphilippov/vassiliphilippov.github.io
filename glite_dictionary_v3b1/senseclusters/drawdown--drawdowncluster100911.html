drawdown

noun
reduction in value
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdrɔˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈdrɔːdaʊn / (BrE)
a reduction in the value, amount, or level of something, especially in investments or resources

Examples

Investment drawdown
The investment fund suffered a major drawdown after the stock market crash.
Water levels in the reservoir showed a significant drawdown during the dry season.
Investors monitor the maximum drawdown to assess the risk of a portfolio.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decline - a decrease in amount or quality
  • antonym: increase - a rise in amount or value
