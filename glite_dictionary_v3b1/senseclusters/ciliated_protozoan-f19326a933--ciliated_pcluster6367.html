ciliated protozoan

noun
protozoan with hairlike cilia
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪlieɪtɪd proʊˈtoʊzoʊən / (AmE), / ˈsɪlieɪtɪd prəʊˈtəʊzəʊən / (BrE)
a protozoan characterized by the presence of hairlike cilia on its surface, used for movement and feeding

Examples

The ciliated protozoan swims
Scientists observed the ciliated protozoan under a microscope to study its swimming behavior.
Some ciliated protozoans play a crucial role in aquatic ecosystems by consuming bacteria.
The ciliated protozoan uses its tiny hairlike structures to move through water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: protozoan - a single-celled microscopic organism, a former phylum of the kingdom Protista
