branchial

adjective
relating to gills or gill-derived structures
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈbræn.ki.əl / (AmE), / ˈbraŋ.kɪ.əl / (BrE)
relating to gills or to parts of the body derived from embryonic gills.

Examples

branchial clefts
Amphibians have branchial clefts in their early stages of development.
The branchial region in embryos eventually forms parts of the neck and throat.
The fish's branchial arches support its gills for respiration.
