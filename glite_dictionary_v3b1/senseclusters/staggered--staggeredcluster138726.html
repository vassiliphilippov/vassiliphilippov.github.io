staggered

adjective
not in line or time
Pronunciation: / ˈstæɡərd / (AmE), / ˈstæɡəd / (BrE)
arranged so that things are not in a straight line or do not happen at the same time

Examples

Staggered schedule
The meeting times were staggered to avoid crowding in the hallway.
The construction workers placed the bricks in a staggered pattern for extra strength.
School holidays are staggered across regions to reduce travel congestion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aligned - arranged in a straight line or in proper position
  • synonym: alternating - happening or arranged in turns or in a repeated pattern
