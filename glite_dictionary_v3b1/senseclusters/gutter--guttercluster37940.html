gutter

verb
burn or flicker unsteadily (of flame)
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡʌtɚ /; / ˈɡʌtər / (AmE), / ˈɡʌtə / (BrE)
(of a flame or light) to burn or shine with a flickering, unsteady light, often about to go out

Examples

A guttering candle
As the oil lamp guttered, the room was plunged into darkness.
The candle began to gutter as the wind crept in through the open window.
The dying embers guttered in the hearth until only a faint glow remained.
The candle guttered in the breeze, its flame shrinking and almost going out.
As the oil lamp guttered, shadows danced across the old wooden walls.
The fire guttered low in the hearth, barely giving off any light.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flicker - to burn or shine with a wavering light
  • antonym: glow - to shine with a steady light
