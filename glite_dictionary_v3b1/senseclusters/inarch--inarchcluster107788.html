inarch

verb
to graft by joining stems
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌɑrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌɑːtʃ / (BrE)
to join living parts of two plants by bending and uniting their stems or roots so they grow together

Examples

To inarch young trees
Gardeners often inarch young trees to repair damage caused by pests.
The botanist demonstrated how to inarch two saplings to encourage healthy growth.
After the storm, experts decided to inarch the damaged roots to save the old tree.
