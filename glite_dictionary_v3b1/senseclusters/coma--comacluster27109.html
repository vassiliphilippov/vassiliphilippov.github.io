coma

noun
state of deep unconsciousness
1.0 5.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊ.mə / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.mə / (BrE)
a state of deep unconsciousness, usually caused by illness or injury, from which a person cannot be awakened

Examples

In a coma
A severe infection can sometimes lead to a prolonged coma if it affects the brain.
After the accident, he was in a coma for six weeks before regaining consciousness.
Doctors are closely monitoring her coma to determine if there are any signs of improvement.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: catatonia - a state of psychomotor immobility and behavioral abnormality
  • degree lower: unconsciousness - the state of not being conscious, insensible
