disk pack

noun
removable set of magnetic disks for storage
4.9 5.5
Pronunciation: /dɪsk pæk/ (AmE), /dɪsk pæk/ (BrE)
A removable set of magnetic disks, stacked together and used as a unit in a disk storage device.

Examples

A disk pack unit
Each disk pack can store several megabytes of data, which was significant for its time.
He labeled the disk pack to avoid confusion with others in the computer lab.
The technician carefully loaded the disk pack into the mainframe's storage unit.
If the disc pack becomes damaged, all information stored on its magnetic disks can be lost.
Older computers often required users to swap out one disc pack for another to access more data.
The technician carefully inserted the disc pack into the storage unit for the nightly backup.

  • synonym: hard disk - non-volatile storage device containing magnetic disks
