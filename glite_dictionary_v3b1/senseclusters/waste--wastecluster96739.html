waste

noun
careless or inefficient use of resources
Pronunciation: / weɪst / (AmE), / weɪst / (BrE)
the act or habit of using something carelessly, inefficiently, or without purpose

Examples

A waste of money
He was criticized for the waste he showed in managing his affairs.
Her habits were marked by excessive waste of both money and time.
The company's waste of natural resources led to public criticism.
Leaving the lights on when no one is home is just a waste of electricity.
Spending hours arguing about trivial things is a complete waste of time.
The new machine helps reduce waste in the production process.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conservation - the prevention of wasteful use of resources
  • synonym: extravagance - excessive or unnecessary spending or use
  • synonym: misuse - improper or incorrect use
  • antonym: thrift - the quality of using money and resources carefully
  • synonym: wastefulness - the quality of being wasteful; lack of thrift
