deconstruction

noun
breaking down analysis
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːkənˈstrʌkʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːkənˈstrʌkʃən / (BrE)
the act of analyzing something by breaking it down into its parts to understand its meaning or structure

Examples

Text deconstruction
The professor used deconstruction to examine the novel's themes.
In art, deconstruction can reveal hidden assumptions behind traditional styles.
Her essay offered a thoughtful deconstruction of the politician's speech.

Related Terms

  • synonym: analysis - the process of examining something in detail
  • antonym: synthesis - the combination of parts to form a whole
