accommodationist

adjective
favoring religious inclusion
Pronunciation: / əˌkɑːməˈdeɪʃənɪst / (AmE), / əˌkɒməˈdeɪʃənɪst / (BrE)
favoring or supporting policies that allow religious practices within government or public settings rather than enforcing strict separation

Examples

Accommodationist policy on religion
The judge's accommodationist approach allowed religious symbols in public schools.
Some lawmakers hold accommodationist views on the relationship between church and state.
Debates over public holiday displays often reveal accommodationist and separationist perspectives.

Related Terms

  • antonym: separationist - favoring strict separation of religion and government
