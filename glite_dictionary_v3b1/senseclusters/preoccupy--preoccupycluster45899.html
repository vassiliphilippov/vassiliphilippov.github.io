preoccupy

verb
to take possession beforehand
1.0 5.7
Pronunciation: /priː.ˈɑː.kjə.paɪ/ /; / /prɪ.ˈɑː.kjə.paɪ/ (AmE), /prɪ.ˈɒk.jə.paɪ/ /; / /priː.ˈɒk.jə.paɪ/ (BrE)
(formal) To take possession of, occupy, or appropriate (a place or thing) before someone else; to acquire for use in advance.

Examples

To preoccupy land
Several explorers attempted to preoccupy new territories during the age of discovery.
The military forces preoccupied the border regions before the enemy could arrive.
The rebels hoped to preoccupy strategic locations ahead of the advancing army.
