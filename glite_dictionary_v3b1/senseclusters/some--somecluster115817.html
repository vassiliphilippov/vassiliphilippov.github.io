some

adverb
imprecise quantity; approximately
1.0 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / sÊŒm / (AmE), / sÊŒm / (BrE)
used before a number or quantity to mean 'approximately' or 'about', when the exact amount is not known or exact value is not important

Examples

Some fifty people
His collection numbers some three hundred rare books.
The trip lasted some forty-five minutes, give or take a few.
There were some thirty students present at the meeting.
