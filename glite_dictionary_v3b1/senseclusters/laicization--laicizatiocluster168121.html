laicization

noun
removal of religious control
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌleɪəsəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌleɪɪsaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making an institution, organization, or society secular by removing religious authority or influence

Examples

Laicization of schools
The laicization of public schools led to the removal of religious symbols from classrooms.
After years of debate, the government began the laicization of several national institutions.
Many historians study the laicization of European societies during the nineteenth century.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clericalization - the process of placing under religious or clerical control
  • synonym: secularization - the process of making something non-religious or secular
