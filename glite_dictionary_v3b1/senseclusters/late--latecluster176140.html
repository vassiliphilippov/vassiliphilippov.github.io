late

adverb
after the expected time
1.0 141/m
Pronunciation: / leÉªt / (AmE), / leÉªt / (BrE)
after the expected, usual, or planned time; not on time; or continuing until an advanced time

Examples

Stay up late
He always stays up late working on his assignments.
She apologized for arriving late to the dinner.
The meeting started late because the speaker was delayed.
He worked late into the night to meet the deadline.
The train arrived late because of heavy snowfall.
They stayed out late celebrating their victory.

  • tardily
