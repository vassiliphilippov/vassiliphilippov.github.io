leave

noun
official permission or authorized absence
Pronunciation: / liv / (AmE), / liːv / (BrE)
official permission or a period of time to be absent from work or duty

Examples

Paid leave
She took leave of absence from work to care for her father.
The judge granted leave to appeal the decision.
The lawyer requested leave to submit new evidence during the trial.
After the birth of her child, she took maternity leave from her job.
He requested a week's leave to recover from his illness.
Soldiers are often granted leave so they can visit their families.

Related Terms

  • synonym: furlough - a temporary leave of absence from work, especially one granted to a member of the armed forces
  • synonym: permission - the action of officially allowing someone to do something
  • synonym: vacation - an extended period of leisure and recreation, especially away from work
