corridor

noun
enclosed passageway in a building
2.5 23/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…îÀêr.…ô.d…ö / (AmE), / Ààk…ír.…™.d…îÀê / (BrE)
a long, narrow enclosed passage in a building, with doors leading to rooms on either side or at the ends

Examples

Long corridor in building
She walked down the corridor, glancing into each classroom as she passed.
The hotel corridor was lined with paintings and elegant lights.
Voices echoed in the empty corridor late at night.

Synonyms

  • hallway
    1.0 27/m
    a hallway is the everyday long passage that rooms open onto, while a corridor feels slightly more formal.
  • passageway
    3.2 3.54/m
    a passageway connects places inside or outside buildings, while a corridor is an indoor passage with doors to rooms.
  • hall
    1.0 32/m
    a hall is often a large open room or entrance space in a building, while a corridor is a narrow passage lined with doors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hallway - an interior passage in a building that leads to rooms
  • synonym: passage - a narrow way through something, especially inside a building
