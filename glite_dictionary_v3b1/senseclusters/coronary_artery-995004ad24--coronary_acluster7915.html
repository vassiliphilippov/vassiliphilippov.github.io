coronary artery

noun
an artery supplying blood to the heart
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrəˌnɛri ˈɑːrˌtɛri / (AmE), / ˈkɒr.əˌnəri ˈɑː.təri / (BrE)
an artery that branches from the aorta and supplies blood to the heart muscle

Examples

The coronary artery blockage
A blockage in the coronary artery can lead to a heart attack.
Doctors performed surgery to clear the patient's coronary artery.
The left coronary artery is crucial for providing oxygen-rich blood to the heart muscle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: artery - a blood vessel that carries blood away from the heart
