fogey

noun
old-fashioned, conservative person
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊɡi / (AmE), / ˈfəʊɡi / (BrE)
an old-fashioned person with out-of-date ideas or style, especially one who is unwilling to change

Examples

An old-fashioned fogey
Don't be such a fogey—embrace change once in a while!
He dismissed the critics as fogeys who refused to embrace new ideas.
My grandfather can be a bit of a fogey when it comes to technology.
People called her a fogy because she still wore clothes from the 1970s.
The club was full of fogy members who resisted any change.
Tom is a bit of a fogy when it comes to modern technology.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fogey - an old-fashioned person; usually spelled 'fogey' in modern usage
  • synonym: fossil - a person with outdated views or style
  • antonym: modernist - a person who supports modern ideas or styles
