overwind

verb
to make breathless
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈwaɪnd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈwaɪnd / (BrE)
to make someone out of breath or breathless due to physical effort

Examples

To overwind a runner
Climbing the steep trail will overwind even experienced hikers in a matter of minutes.
The coach warned not to overwind yourself during the first half of the race.
Running up the stairs too quickly can easily overwind a person who is not used to exercise.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exhaust - to make someone very tired
  • antonym: invigorate - to give energy or strength to someone
