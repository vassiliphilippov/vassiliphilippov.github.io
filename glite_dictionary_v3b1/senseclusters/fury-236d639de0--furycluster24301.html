Fury

noun
mythological avenging goddess
Pronunciation: / ˈfjʊri / (AmE), / ˈfjʊəri / (BrE)
(in classical mythology) one of the three hideous goddesses with snakes for hair, who pursue and torment those who have committed serious crimes and escaped punishment.

Examples

A Fury with snakes
In Greek tragedies, a Fury would often appear to punish those guilty of murder.
Legends say the Fury never rested until justice was served.
The vengeful Fury haunted the dreams of the tormented king.
