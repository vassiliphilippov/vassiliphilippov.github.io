off-track

adjective
not on rails
Pronunciation: / ˌɔfˈtræk / (AmE), / ˌɒfˈtræk / (BrE)
having moved away from the railway tracks or no longer on the rails, especially of a train or rail vehicle

Examples

Train off-track
After the accident, the train was completely off-track and blocking the road.
The maintenance crew worked quickly to move the off-track carriage back onto the rails.
Heavy rain caused several wagons to go off-track near the station.

Related Terms

  • synonym: derailed - having come off the railway tracks
  • antonym: on-track - remaining on the railway tracks
