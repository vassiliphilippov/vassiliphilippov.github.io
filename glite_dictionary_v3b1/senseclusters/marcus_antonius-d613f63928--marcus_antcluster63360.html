Marcus Antonius

proper noun
Roman general; lover of Cleopatra
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑrkəs ænˈtoʊniəs / (AmE), / ˈmɑːkəs ænˈtəʊniəs / (BrE)
(c. 83–30 BC) A Roman general and politician, ally of Julius Caesar, and lover of Cleopatra, defeated by Octavian at the Battle of Actium.

Examples

Marcus Antonius Roman general
Marcus Antonius played a crucial role in the transformation of the Roman Republic into the Roman Empire.
Shakespeare's play 'Antony and Cleopatra' dramatizes the tragic love affair between Cleopatra and Marcus Antonius.
The defeat of Marcus Antonius and Cleopatra at the Battle of Actium marked the end of the Roman Republic.
