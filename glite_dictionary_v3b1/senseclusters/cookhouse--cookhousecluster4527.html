cookhouse

noun
kitchen area on a ship or camp
6.0 0.14/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆkÊŠkËŒhaÊŠs / (AmE), / ËˆkÊŠk.haÊŠs /; / ËˆkÊŠkËŒhaÊŠs / (BrE)
a building or area, separate from the main structure, where food is prepared and cooked for a group of people, especially in camps, worksites, or on ships

Examples

A cookhouse building
Due to the storm, everyone was forced to eat in the cookhouse instead of on the upper deck.
Each morning, the crew gathered in the cookhouse for breakfast before starting their work.
The smell of fresh bread drifted from the cookhouse across the ship's deck.
During the summer camp, meals were prepared in an old wooden cookhouse.
The army set up a temporary cookhouse near the field for the soldiers.
The workers gathered in the cookhouse for their midday meal.
