osmoregulatory

adjective
water-salt balance
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˌɑzməˌrɛɡjəˈleɪtɔri / (AmE), / ˌɒzməˌrɛɡjʊˈleɪtəri / (BrE)
relating to the regulation of water and salt balance in living organisms

Examples

Osmoregulatory function
Certain fish have osmoregulatory adaptations that allow them to live in both fresh and salt water.
The kidney plays a key osmoregulatory role in mammals by controlling water and salt levels.
Scientists study osmoregulatory processes to understand how animals survive in extreme environments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: homeostatic - relating to maintaining internal stability in an organism
