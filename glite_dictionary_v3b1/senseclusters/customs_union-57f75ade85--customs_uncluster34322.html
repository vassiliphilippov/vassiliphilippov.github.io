customs union

noun
trade bloc with a common external tariff
4.6 4.1
Pronunciation: /ˈkʌs.təmz ˈjuː.njən/ /; / /ˈkʌs.təmz ˈjuː.ni.ən/ (AmE), /ˈkʌs.təmz ˈjuː.njən/ /; / /ˈkʌs.təmz ˈjuː.ni.ən/ (BrE)
an association of countries that agree to eliminate tariffs between themselves and adopt a common external tariff on imports from non-members

Examples

A customs union agreement
A customs union makes it easier for businesses to trade within member countries.
Building a customs union can strengthen economic ties between neighboring states.
The European Union started as a customs union before evolving into a single market.
