subtidal

noun
permanently submerged zone
7.9
Pronunciation: /sʌb.ˈtaɪ.dəl/ /; / /səb.ˈtaɪ.dəl/ (AmE), /sʌb.ˈtaɪ.dəl/ /; / /səb.ˈtaɪ.dəl/ (BrE)
the area of the seafloor that is always underwater, lying below the low-tide mark and never exposed to air

Examples

The subtidal area
Marine biologists collected samples from the subtidal to study underwater plant life.
Many fish species make their homes in the subtidal because it is always covered by water.
The subtidal supports a rich diversity of seaweeds and invertebrates.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intertidal - the area of the shore between high and low tide marks
  • synonym: submarine - existing or occurring under the sea
