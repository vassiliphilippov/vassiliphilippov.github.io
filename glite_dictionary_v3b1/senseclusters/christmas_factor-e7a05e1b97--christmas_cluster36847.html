Christmas factor

noun
blood clotting factor, deficiency causes hemophilia B
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪs·məs ˌfæk·tɚ / (AmE), / ˈkrɪs·məs ˌfæk·tə / (BrE)
a blood coagulation factor (Factor IX) whose deficiency causes hemophilia B

Examples

Christmas factor deficiency
A low level of Christmas factor in the blood increases the risk of prolonged bleeding.
Researchers developed a synthetic version of the Christmas factor to treat patients more effectively.
The absence of the Christmas factor is the cause of hemophilia B.
