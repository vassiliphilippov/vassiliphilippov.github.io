Granth

noun
Sikhism's sacred scripture
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɡrɑːnθ / (AmE), / ɡrɑːnθ / (BrE)
the principal sacred text of Sikhism, containing hymns, poetry, and teachings, especially those of the first five gurus

Examples

The Granth Sahib
He spent the afternoon reading passages from the Granth with his family.
Sikhs treat the Granth with great reverence in their places of worship.
The Granth contains spiritual hymns composed by several Sikh gurus.
