mosaic

noun
genetic condition of mixed cell lines
8.3
Pronunciation: /moʊ.ˈzeɪ.ɪk/ /; / /moʊ.ˈzeɪk/ (AmE), /məʊ.ˈzeɪ.ɪk/ /; / /məʊ.ˈzeɪk/ (BrE)
(in genetics/medicine) an organism, tissue, or sample that contains two or more genetically distinct cell lines originating after fertilization; the condition is called mosaicism.

Examples

Mosaic genetic sample
Genetic testing showed the tumor was a mosaic, containing cells with different chromosomal patterns.
Doctors explained that the child's skin colour pattern suggested he might be a mosaic.
Researchers identified a blood mosaic where some cells carried the mutation and others did not.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mosaicism - the condition in which an individual has two or more genetically different cell lines
