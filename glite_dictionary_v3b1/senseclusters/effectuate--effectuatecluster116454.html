to cause something to happen; to bring about or produce a result

Examples

To effectuate an agreement
It took several meetings to effectuate an agreement between the two companies.
Only the board of directors has the power to effectuate major corporate decisions.
The new policy is designed to effectuate significant changes in the educational system.

Synonyms

  • effect
    6.1 5.93/m
    effect means to bring something about in a simple, direct way, while effectuate sounds more formal and technical.
  • bring about
    3.0 17/m
    bring about causes something to happen in everyday speech, while effectuate is a more formal word used in writing or legal texts.
  • cause
    1.0 262/m
    cause makes something happen often without planning, while effectuate implies actively carrying out a plan to bring it about.
  • induce
    5.3 17/m
    induce makes a process or feeling start often by influence, while effectuate means to carry out and accomplish a plan.
  • actuate
    7.0 0.09/m
    actuate sets a person or device into motion, often by a motive force, while effectuate means to bring a planned result into being.
