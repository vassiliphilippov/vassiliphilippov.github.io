loot

noun
money (informal)
4.4 1.81/m
Pronunciation: / luÀêt / (AmE), / luÀêt / (BrE)
money or goods that have been stolen, especially during a war, riot, or by thieves; also used informally for money gained or spent casually or illegally

Examples

Stolen loot
After a night out, my wallet was empty of loot.
He managed to make a lot of loot from the new business venture.
She was saving her loot for a trip to Europe.
During the riot, shops were broken into and loot was carried away in broad daylight.
Police recovered all of the stolen loot hidden in an abandoned house.
The thieves divided the loot among themselves after the robbery.
After the heist, the thieves divided the loot among themselves.
The museum displayed ancient loot seized from shipwrecks.
The police recovered the stolen loot hidden in an abandoned warehouse.

Synonyms

  • booty
    7.7 4.78/m
    booty is valuable goods taken by force in wars or raids, while loot covers stolen items in many settings.
  • swag
    9.4 0.09/m
    swag is thieves' slang for stolen goods, often casual and modern, while loot is more general and common.
  • plunder
    5.5 0.95/m
    plunder means goods taken by force during war or robbery, often in large amounts, while loot is broader and casual.
  • spoil
    7.8 2.14/m
    spoil refers to items taken in war and shown off by victors, while loot also covers theft in towns.
  • pillage
    7.9 0.02/m
    pillage is the violent act of stealing and destroying in war, while loot names the goods or theft more generally.
  • prize
    9.1 1.88/m
    prize is a legal or formal word for captured property in war or at sea, while loot is everyday speech.
