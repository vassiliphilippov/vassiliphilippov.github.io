coelenteron

noun
central body cavity of a coelenterate
4.8
Pronunciation: / siˈlɛntəˌrɑn / (AmE), / siːˈlɛntərɒn / (BrE)
the central, saclike body cavity of a coelenterate, serving the functions of both digestion and circulation

Examples

The coelenteron cavity
Food particles are distributed throughout the coelenteron by muscular contractions.
In sea anemones, the coelenteron connects to the outside through a single opening.
The coelenteron of a jellyfish is used for both digestion and circulation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gastrovascular cavity - the primary digestive cavity in cnidarians and flatworms
