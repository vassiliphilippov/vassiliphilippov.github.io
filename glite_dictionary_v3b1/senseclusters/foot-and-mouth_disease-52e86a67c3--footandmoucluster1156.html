foot-and-mouth disease

noun
contagious livestock disease with mouth and hoof lesions
3.6 3.8
Pronunciation: / ˌfʊt ən ˈmaʊθ dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˌfʊt ən ˈmaʊθ dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
An acute, highly contagious viral disease of cloven-hoofed animals, characterized by fever and ulcers in the mouth and lesions around the hoofs.

Examples

Foot-and-mouth disease outbreak
An outbreak of foot-and-mouth disease caused severe economic losses to British farmers in 2001.
Many countries ban the import of livestock from regions affected by foot-and-mouth disease.
Veterinarians implemented strict measures to prevent the spread of foot-and-mouth disease among cattle.
