spin

noun
a rapid turning or whirling movement
6.7 1.1
Pronunciation: /spɪn/ (AmE), /spɪn/ (BrE)
the act or result of turning around quickly or spinning (used for objects, people, or aircraft)

Examples

A fast spin
He could tell from the spin that the arrow would hit its target.
The baseball had a tight spin as it flew towards the batter.
The scientist analyzed the spin of the particle in the experiment.
Entering a spin can be disorienting and requires immediate corrective action.
Flight training includes exercises on how to exit a spin safely.
The pilot managed to recover the plane from a dangerous spin just in time.
After a sharp spin, the dancer landed gracefully on the stage.
She gave the basketball a quick spin on her finger during the show.
The washing machine ended its cycle with a fast spin to dry the clothes.

  • antonym: stillness - the absence of movement or rotation
