caprice

noun
sudden desire or mood change
7.4
Pronunciation: / kəˈpris / (AmE), / kəˈpriːs / (BrE)
A sudden and unaccountable change of mood or behavior; a sudden desire or impulse.

Examples

A caprice of mood
Buying the expensive painting was a caprice he later regretted.
He quit his job on a caprice and went backpacking in South America.
Her decision to travel to Paris was just a caprice.

Related Terms

  • synonym: impulse - a sudden strong urge or desire to act
  • synonym: whim - a sudden wish or idea, especially one that cannot be reasonably explained
