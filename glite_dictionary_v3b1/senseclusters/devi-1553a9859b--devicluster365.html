Devi

proper noun
Hindu mother goddess; supreme female power
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪ.vi / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ.vi / (BrE)
A Hindu mother goddess regarded as the supreme power in the universe, embodying both beneficent and malevolent aspects; considered the wife of Shiva or the personification of his female energy.

Examples

Devi goddess
In Hindu mythology, Devi takes many forms, both gentle and fierce.
Many devotees offer prayers to Devi during the festival of Navaratri.
The temple is dedicated to Devi, revered as the mother of the universe.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Shiva - A principal Hindu god, known as the destroyer and transformer in the Hindu trinity
