crossbanding

noun
wood veneer backing
8.9
Pronunciation: /krɑːs.ˈbæn.dɪŋ/ /; / /krɔːs.ˈbæn.dɪŋ/ (AmE), /krɒs.ˈbæn.dɪŋ/ /; / /krɔːs.ˈbæn.dɪŋ/ (BrE)
a layer of wood veneer placed with its grain at right angles to the main surface to stabilize and prevent warping in panels

Examples

Stabilizing crossbanding layer
The cabinetmaker used crossbanding to ensure the tabletop would not warp over time.
High-quality plywood often includes a layer of crossbanding for added strength and stability.
Without proper crossbanding, the wooden panel might bend or crack in humid conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: laminate - a material made by bonding layers together
  • synonym: veneer - a thin decorative layer of fine wood applied to a surface
