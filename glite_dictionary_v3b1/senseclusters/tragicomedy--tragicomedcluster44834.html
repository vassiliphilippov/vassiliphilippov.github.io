tragicomedy

noun
real-life mix of tragic and comic elements
Pronunciation: / ˈtrædʒɪˈkɑːmədi / (AmE), / ˈtrædʒɪˈkɒmədi / (BrE)
An event, situation, or series of happenings in real life in which serious, tragic elements coexist with comic or absurd aspects.

Examples

A real-life tragicomedy
The protest turned into a tragicomedy of slogans and sudden downpours that made national headlines.
Watching the committee collapse under petty arguments felt like a real-life tragicomedy for everyone involved.
Their marriage, full of tender moments and ridiculous misunderstandings, became a private tragicomedy.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: comedy - a play or situation intended to amuse or provoke laughter; the lighter end of the tragic–comic spectrum
  • degree higher: tragedy - a situation or work with serious, sorrowful themes; the heavier end of the tragic–comic spectrum
