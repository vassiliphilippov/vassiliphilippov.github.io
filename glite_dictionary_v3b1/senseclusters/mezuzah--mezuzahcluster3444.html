mezuzah

noun
parchment with biblical text on Jewish doorposts
9.9
Pronunciation: / məˈzuːzə / (AmE), / məˈzuːzə / (BrE)
A piece of parchment inscribed with specific Hebrew verses from Deuteronomy, placed in a case and affixed to the doorpost of Jewish homes as a religious practice.

Examples

A mezuzah on doorpost
Every Jewish home traditionally places a mezuzah on its doorpost.
She touched the mezuzah as she entered the house, a gesture of faith.
The rabbi explained the significance of the mezuzah during the ceremony.
