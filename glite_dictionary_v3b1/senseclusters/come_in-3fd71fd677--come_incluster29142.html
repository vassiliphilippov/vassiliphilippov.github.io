come in

verb
to be received (news, messages, etc.)
Pronunciation: / kʌm ɪn / (AmE), / kʌm ɪn / (BrE)
to arrive, be received, or become available (such as news, information, or something starting to be relevant)

Examples

News comes in
As soon as the results come in, we will announce the winner.
Reports came in throughout the night about the storm's progress.
The first donations came in within minutes of launching the fundraiser.
After months of planning, this is where the new policy comes in and starts to affect everyone.
As the tide comes in, the beach gets much smaller and the water rises.
The news finally came in just after midnight, ending hours of speculation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arrive - to reach a place or be delivered
  • antonym: depart - to leave, especially in order to start a journey
