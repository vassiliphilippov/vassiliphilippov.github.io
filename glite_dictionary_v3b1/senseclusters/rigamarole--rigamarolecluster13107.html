rigamarole

noun
complicated, tedious procedure
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪɡəməˌroʊl / (AmE), / ˈrɪɡəmərəʊl / (BrE)
a long, complicated, and confusing process or procedure, often seen as unnecessary or pointless

Examples

The rigamarole of paperwork
It took hours to get through the rigamarole of applying for a simple permit.
She was frustrated by the endless rigamarole required to file a complaint.
We had to endure the usual bureaucratic rigamarole before we could see the doctor.
Dealing with the insurance company involved a lot of rigamarole that left me frustrated.
He grew tired of the endless rigamarole required just to submit a simple request.
Let's skip all the rigamarole and get straight to the point.
