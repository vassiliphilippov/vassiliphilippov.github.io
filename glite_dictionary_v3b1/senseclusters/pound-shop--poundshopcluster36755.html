pound-shop

noun
British shop selling low-priced goods, usually for one pound
7.6
Pronunciation: / paʊnd ʃɑp / (AmE), / paʊnd ʃɒp / (BrE)
A shop selling a wide range of items at low prices, typically for one pound each.

Examples

A pound-shop store
I picked up cleaning supplies for just £1 each at the pound-shop.
Many students rely on the pound-shop for affordable household items.
The new pound-shop in town attracts a lot of bargain hunters.
