squatty

adjective
short and thick in appearance
5.9 0.08/m
Pronunciation: / Ààskw…ëÀêti / (AmE), / Ààskw…íti / (BrE)
short and thick or broad in appearance, often with reference to body shape or objects; having a low, sturdy, and sometimes compact build.

Examples

A squatty body
That breed of dog is known for its squatty legs and long body.
The cottage had a squatty chimney and moss growing on its roof.
The old man was a squatty figure, compact and sturdy as a tree stump.

Synonyms

  • squat
    4.6 0.11/m
    squat is very low and broad in shape, while squatty describes something short and sturdy with a slightly informal tone.
  • stocky
    2.8 1.96/m
    stocky is broad and solid in build, while squatty is lower and more compact in overall shape.
  • chunky
    3.4 0.80/m
    chunky looks thick and blocky, often for objects or people, while squatty means low and stout in build.
  • stumpy
    4.7 0.35/m
    stumpy suggests something cut short or with short thick parts, while squatty means a low, sturdy overall shape.
  • dumpy
    5.0 0.23/m
    dumpy looks short and thick but often sounds negative and unattractive, while squatty simply means low and sturdy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chunky - thick, solid, and short in build or form
  • antonym: slender - gracefully thin or slim
  • synonym: stumpy - short and thick; stubby
