fractionalization

noun
splitting of particle properties
Pronunciation: / ˌfrækʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfrækʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a physical phenomenon where a property of a particle, such as charge or spin, appears divided into smaller, fractional parts among new excitations

Examples

Fractionalization of charge
Researchers discovered fractionalization of electric charge in certain quantum materials.
The phenomenon of fractionalization challenges traditional ideas about how particles behave.
In some experiments, fractionalization leads to the appearance of particles with unusual properties.

Related Terms

  • antonym: quantization - the process of restricting a property to discrete values
