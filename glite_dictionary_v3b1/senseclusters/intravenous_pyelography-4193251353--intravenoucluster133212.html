intravenous pyelography

noun
X-ray imaging of urinary tract with injected dye
5.1
Pronunciation: /ɪn.trə.ˈviː.nəs paɪ.ə.ˈlɑː.grə.fiː/ (AmE), /ɪn.trə.ˈviː.nəs paɪ.ə.ˈlɒ.grə.fiː/ (BrE)
a medical imaging procedure in which a contrast medium is injected into a vein to produce X-ray images of the urinary tract

Examples

An intravenous pyelography test
An intravenous pyelography can help detect blockages or abnormalities in the urinary tract.
Before the intravenous pyelography, the patient was advised to avoid food and drink for several hours.
The doctor recommended an intravenous pyelography to assess the function of the patient's kidneys.
