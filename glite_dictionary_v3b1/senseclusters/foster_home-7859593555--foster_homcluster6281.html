foster home

noun
temporary home for children in care
2.9 2.2
Pronunciation: /ˈfɑː.stər hoʊm/ /; / /ˈfɔː.stər hoʊm/ (AmE), /ˈfɒ.stə həʊm/ (BrE)
A home in which a child who is unable to live with their biological parents is cared for, usually arranged by a social-service agency.

Examples

A foster home
Becoming a foster home requires approval from social services.
She spent most of her childhood moving from one foster home to another.
The twins were placed in a foster home after their parents passed away.

Related Terms

  • synonym: orphanage - institution for children without parents
