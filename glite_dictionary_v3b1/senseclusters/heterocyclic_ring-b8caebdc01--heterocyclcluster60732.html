heterocyclic ring

noun
ring structure with different types of atoms
8.7 6.5
Pronunciation: /he.tə.rə.ˈsaɪ.kəl rɪŋ/ (AmE), /he.tə.rə.ˈsaɪ.kəl rɪŋ/ (BrE)
a ring of atoms in a molecule in which at least one atom is not carbon, often found in organic chemistry

Examples

A heterocyclic ring molecule
A heterocyclic ring differs from a carbocycle because it includes atoms other than carbon.
Many pharmaceutical drugs contain a heterocyclic ring as part of their molecular structure.
Pyridine is a classic example of a heterocyclic ring containing one nitrogen atom.

Related Terms

  • antonym: homocyclic ring - a ring structure where all the atoms in the ring are of the same kind
