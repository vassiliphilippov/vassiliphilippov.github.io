reclusive

adjective
preferring privacy or seclusion
4.0 5.9
Pronunciation: / rɪˈkluːsɪv / (AmE), / rɪˈkluːsɪv / (BrE)
preferring to be alone and avoiding the company of others; withdrawn from society

Examples

A reclusive life
After her sudden fame, she lived a reclusive life in the countryside, rarely seen in public.
He built his home in a reclusive spot, surrounded by trees and far from the main road.
The artist became increasingly reclusive, shunning interviews and social gatherings.
Despite his reclusive nature, his work was admired by many.
She led a reclusive existence, rarely speaking to her neighbors.
The author became increasingly reclusive after achieving fame, seldom leaving his mountain cabin.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antisocial - not wanting the company of others; avoiding social interaction
  • antonym: gregarious - enjoying the company of others; sociable
  • synonym: secluded - quiet and private, away from others
  • antonym: sociable - enjoying being with others
  • synonym: withdrawn - not wanting to communicate with others
