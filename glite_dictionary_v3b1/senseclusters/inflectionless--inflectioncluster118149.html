inflectionless

adjective
without grammatical changes
8.0
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈflek.ʃən.ləs/ /; / /ə.ˈflek.ʃən.ləs/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈflek.ʃən.ləs/ /; / /ə.ˈflek.ʃən.ləs/ (BrE)
not having changes in form to express grammatical features such as tense, number, or case

Examples

Inflectionless nouns
In English, the word 'deer' is inflectionless because it does not change between singular and plural.
Some languages have many inflectionless words that keep the same form in all contexts.
The noun 'sheep' is inflectionless, appearing the same in both singular and plural forms.
