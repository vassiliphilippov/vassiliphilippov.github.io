monographic

adjective
about a single study
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːnəˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈɡræfɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a detailed written study on a single subject

Examples

A monographic study
The museum released a monographic catalog focusing on the painter's landscapes.
Her research is highly monographic, concentrating on one historical event.
A monographic approach allows for a deep understanding of a specific topic.

Related Terms

  • antonym: general - covering many subjects or topics
  • synonym: monograph - a detailed written study on a single subject
