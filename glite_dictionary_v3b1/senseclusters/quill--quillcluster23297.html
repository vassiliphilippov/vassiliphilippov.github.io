quill

noun
hollow shaft of a feather
Pronunciation: / kwɪl / (AmE), / kwɪl / (BrE)
the hollow central shaft of a feather, or a large stiff feather from a bird's wing or tail

Examples

Feather quill
A bird's quill can be used in traditional handicrafts.
He dipped the quill in ink before writing the letter.
The porcupine's quill can easily detach and stick into predators.
She found a beautiful quill on the forest path and kept it as a souvenir.
The eagle lost a quill from its left wing as it soared through the sky.
Writers in the past often used a quill from a goose to pen letters.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: feather - one of the light structures covering a bird's body
  • synonym: shaft - the long central part of a feather
