late-flowering

adjective
developing talents late in life
6.8
Pronunciation: /ˈleɪt.flaʊ.ər.ɪŋ/ /; / /ˈleɪt.flaʊ.rɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈleɪt.flaʊ.ər.ɪŋ/ /; / /ˈleɪt.flaʊ.ə.rɪŋ/ (BrE)
(figurative) developing important abilities, success, or recognition later than usual in life; showing talent or achievement later in life.

Examples

Late-flowering talent
She became a late-flowering novelist who gained global acclaim in her sixties.
After decades in obscurity, he emerged as a late-flowering painter celebrated for his vision.
Many schools now support late-flowering students who discover their talents after high school.

Related Terms

  • synonym: late-blooming - showing abilities or success later than is typical; similar figurative expression.
