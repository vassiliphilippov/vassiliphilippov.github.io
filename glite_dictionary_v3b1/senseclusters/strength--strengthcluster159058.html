strength

noun
ability to resist force or stress
Pronunciation: / strɛŋkθ / (AmE), / strɛŋθ / (BrE)
the quality of being able to withstand force, stress, or pressure without breaking or being damaged

Examples

Strength of the material
Engineers tested the strength of the material before using it in construction projects.
The bridge's strength allowed it to withstand powerful winds and heavy traffic for decades.
These climbing ropes are valued for their strength and reliability.

Related Terms

  • synonym: durability - the ability to withstand wear, pressure, or damage
  • antonym: weakness - lack of the power to resist force or stress
