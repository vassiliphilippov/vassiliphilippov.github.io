uncircumcision

noun
spiritual impurity
9.9
Pronunciation: /ʌn.sɜːr.kəm.ˈkɪʒ.ən/ /; / /ən.sɜːr.kəm.ˈkɪʒ.ən/ (AmE), /ʌn.sɜː.kəm.ˈkɪʒ.ən/ /; / /ən.sɜː.kəm.ˈkɪʒ.ən/ (BrE)
an archaic or figurative state of moral or spiritual impurity or insensitivity, especially in religious contexts

Examples

Uncircumcision of heart
The prophet spoke of the people's uncircumcision of heart, urging them to seek forgiveness.
In ancient texts, uncircumcision often symbolized a lack of spiritual commitment.
The preacher warned that uncircumcision of the heart could lead to moral decline.

Related Terms

  • synonym: impurity - the state of being morally or spiritually unclean
