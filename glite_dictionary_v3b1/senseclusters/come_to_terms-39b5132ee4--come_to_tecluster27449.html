come to terms

verb
accept or reach understanding
4.1 5.23/m
Pronunciation: / k åm t…ô t…úrmz / (AmE), / k åm t…ô t…úÀêmz / (BrE)
To accept or reconcile oneself to a difficult or unpleasant situation; to reach an agreement or understanding, especially after a period of struggle or reflection.

Examples

To come to terms with loss
After the accident, it took her months to come to terms with her new reality.
He has finally come to terms with the fact that he needs help.
They are slowly coming to terms with their financial loss.

Synonyms

  • reconcile
    5.8 1.98/m
    reconcile describes accepting a difficult fact inside yourself after reflection, while come to terms can also mean making an agreement.
  • reach an agreement
    3.0 1.71/m
    reach an agreement is when both sides calmly decide on terms after discussion and compromise, while come to terms can also mean personal acceptance.
  • settle a disagreement
    3.1 0.05/m
    settle a disagreement means ending a conflict by agreeing on details between people, while come to terms can also mean accepting a hard situation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accept - to receive or agree to something, often reluctantly
  • antonym: resist - to oppose something, especially emotionally or mentally
