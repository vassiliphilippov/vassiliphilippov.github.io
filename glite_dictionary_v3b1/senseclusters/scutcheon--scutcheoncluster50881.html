scutcheon

noun
heraldic shield displaying a coat of arms
Pronunciation: / ˈskʌtʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˈskʌtʃ.ən / (BrE)
a shield, especially one bearing a coat of arms, used in heraldry

Examples

A knight's scutcheon
In medieval times, knights often painted their scutcheon with intricate symbols representing their lineage.
The family's ancient scutcheon was proudly displayed above the fireplace.
The museum's exhibit featured a collection of scutcheons from various noble houses.
