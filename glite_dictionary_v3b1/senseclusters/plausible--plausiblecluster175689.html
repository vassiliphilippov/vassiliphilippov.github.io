plausible

adjective
seeming reasonable or believable
Pronunciation: / ˈplɔːzəbəl / (AmE), / ˈplɔːzəbl /; / ˈplɔːzəbəl / (BrE)
seeming reasonable or believable, even if it might not be true

Examples

A plausible explanation
Although his story sounded plausible, the detective remained suspicious.
She gave a plausible explanation for why she was late to the meeting.
The alibi was certainly plausible, but further investigation was needed.
His plausible explanation cleverly disguised the true facts of the case.
She was a plausible liar who could charm anyone into believing her stories.
The con artist's plausible manner made people trust him too easily.

Related Terms

  • synonym: credible - able to be believed; convincing
  • antonym: improbable - not likely to be true or to happen
  • synonym: specious - superficially plausible, but actually wrong or misleading
