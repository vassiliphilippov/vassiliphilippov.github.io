ham

verb
to act in an exaggerated or theatrical way
5.7
Pronunciation: / hæm / (AmE), / hæm / (BrE)
(of an actor) to perform with exaggerated expressions or gestures, often to an excessive or theatrical degree.

Examples

To ham it up
He loves to ham whenever he's on stage, drawing all attention to himself.
The comedian hammed his role to make the audience laugh even harder.
Whenever there's a camera around, she can't help but ham for it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overact - to act in an exaggerated manner
  • antonym: underact - to act with too little emotion or restraint
