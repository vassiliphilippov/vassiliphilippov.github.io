trough

verb
to form a depression
Pronunciation: / trɔf / (AmE), / trɒf / (BrE)
to form or become shaped like a long, narrow depression or container, especially resembling a trough

Examples

The ground troughed
After the storm, the field troughed in several places where water had collected.
The metal sheet troughed under the weight of the heavy snow.
Over time, the old road troughed from years of constant use and erosion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dip - to sink or become lower in level
  • antonym: peak - to reach a highest point or form a crest
