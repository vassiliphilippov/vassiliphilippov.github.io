Czar Nicholas I

proper noun
Russian emperor (1825–1855), led during Crimean War
Pronunciation: / zɑr ˌnɪkəˈlɑs ðə fɜrst / (AmE), / zɑː ˌnɪkəˈlɒs ðə fɜːst / (BrE)
the emperor of Russia from 1825 to 1855, known for leading Russia into the Crimean War and for his autocratic rule

Examples

Czar Nicholas I of Russia
Czar Nicholas I ruled Russia with a strong autocratic hand during the mid-nineteenth century.
During the Crimean War, Czar Nicholas I faced a coalition of European powers.
The policies of Czar Nicholas I had lasting effects on Russian society and government.
