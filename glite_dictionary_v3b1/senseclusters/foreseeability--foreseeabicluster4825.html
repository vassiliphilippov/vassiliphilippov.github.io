foreseeability

noun
ability to predict
8.2
Pronunciation: /fɑːr.siː.ə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.ti/ /; / /fɔːr.siː.ə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.ti/ (AmE), /fɔː.siː.ə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.ti/ (BrE)
the quality of being able to be predicted or anticipated, especially about possible outcomes or consequences (often used in law)

Examples

The foreseeability of harm
The judge evaluated the foreseeability of the accident when determining liability.
Engineers must consider the foreseeability of potential failures during the design process.
The foreseeability of economic downturns is important for financial planning.
The court considered the foreseeability of the accident when deciding the company's liability.
Lawyers debated whether the harm was within the foreseeability of the defendant's actions.
In negligence cases, foreseeability often determines if a duty of care was owed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: predictability - the quality of being able to be predicted
  • antonym: uncertainty - the state of being unknown or unpredictable
  • antonym: unpredictability - the quality of being impossible to predict
