Apocynum androsaemifolium

noun
a North American perennial plant with pink flowers
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈpɑsɪˌnʌm ˌændrəˌsiːmɪˈfoʊliəm / (AmE), / əˈpɒsɪnəm ˌændrəˌsiːmɪˈfəʊliəm / (BrE)
A North American perennial plant with pinkish flowers in loose cymes, used in folk medicine for joint pain and inflammation.

Examples

Apocynum androsaemifolium pink flowers
Apocynum androsaemifolium is often found growing at the edges of forests across North America.
The delicate pink flowers of Apocynum androsaemifolium bloom in late spring and early summer.
Traditional healers have used Apocynum androsaemifolium to relieve joint soreness and inflammation.
