go over

verb
to review or inspect troops formally
2.5 2.2
Pronunciation: /goʊ ˈəʊ.vər/ /; / /goʊ ˈoʊ.vər/ (AmE), /gəʊ ˈəʊ.və/ (BrE)
to review, inspect, or examine something carefully (such as troops, documents, or work)

Examples

To go over the report
Every year, the president would go over the assembled forces to boost morale.
The commanding officer went over the soldiers, inspecting their uniforms and weapons.
The general will go over the troops before the parade begins.
Before submitting your essay, make sure you go over it for any spelling mistakes.
The mechanic will go over your car to ensure everything is working properly.
We need to go over the details of the contract before signing.
I went over my essay several times to check for mistakes.
Let's go over the list once more to make sure we didn't forget anything.
She always goes over her presentation before a big meeting.
