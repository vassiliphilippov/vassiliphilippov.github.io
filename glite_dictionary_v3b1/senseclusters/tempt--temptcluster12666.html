tempt

verb
make someone want to do/have something bad
3.3 11/m
Pronunciation: / tÉ›mpt / (AmE), / tÉ›mpt / (BrE)
to try to persuade or attract someone to do something, especially something wrong or unwise, by making it seem attractive or inviting (sometimes with the intention of seduction)

Examples

To tempt someone
She tried not to tempt fate by leaving late, but the train was still delayed.
The appealing aroma of fresh bread tempted him into the bakery.
The sale signs in the shop window tempt customers to spend more than they intended.
He tried to tempt her with the promise of a better job.
She refused to let the money tempt her into making a bad decision.
The aroma of freshly baked bread can easily tempt anyone on a diet.
Don't let the offer tempt you if it doesn't feel right.
She was tempted to check her phone during the meeting, but resisted.
The rich dessert tempted me, but I chose to skip it for my diet.

Synonyms

  • entice
    4.9 4.74/m
    entice draws someone in with appealing offers or promises, while tempt often pushes a person toward something wrong or unwise.
  • lure
    3.7 12/m
    lure uses a promise or bait to get someone to act, while tempt suggests attracting someone toward unwise behavior.
  • seduce
    5.9 2.36/m
    seduce leads someone to abandon duty or morals, often with sexual charm, while tempt can mean general attraction to unwise acts.
  • allure
    6.5 0.44/m
    allure attracts by beauty or charm in a quiet, attractive way, while tempt highlights active persuasion toward something unwise.
  • beguile
    8.4 0.26/m
    beguile charms or enchants someone, often to deceive, while tempt focuses on trying to persuade someone into a wrong or unwise choice.
  • cajole
    7.7 1.20/m
    cajole gently persuades with flattery or repeated requests, while tempt usually means attracting someone toward something risky or morally wrong.
  • induce
    4.9 6.33/m
    induce formally causes or brings about an action, often by reason, while tempt implies drawing someone by appeal toward unwise acts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deter - to discourage someone from doing something
  • antonym: dissuade - to persuade someone not to do something
  • synonym: encourage - to give support or confidence to someone to do something
  • synonym: entice - to lure someone by offering hope or reward
  • synonym: persuade - induce someone to do something through reasoning or argument
  • synonym: seduce - to persuade someone to have sex or to do something they would not usually do
