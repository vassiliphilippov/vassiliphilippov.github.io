postural hypotension

noun
a drop in blood pressure upon standing
5.8 6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈpɑːs.tʃər.əl haɪ.pə.ˈten.ʃən/ /; / /ˈpoʊs.tʃər.əl haɪ.poʊ.ˈten.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈpɒs.tʃə.rəl haɪ.pəʊ.ˈten.ʃən/ /; / /ˈpəʊs.tʃə.rəl haɪ.pəʊ.ˈten.ʃən/ (BrE)
a condition in which blood pressure drops abnormally when a person stands up, leading to dizziness or fainting

Examples

Postural hypotension symptoms
Elderly patients are especially prone to postural hypotension after changing positions quickly.
Proper hydration can sometimes reduce episodes of postural hypotension in at-risk individuals.
Symptoms of postural hypotension include dizziness and lightheadedness when rising from a chair.
