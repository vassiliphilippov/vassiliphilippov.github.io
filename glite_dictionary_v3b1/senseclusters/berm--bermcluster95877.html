berm

noun
narrow ledge or shelf at a slope
8.8
Pronunciation: / bɝm / (AmE), / bɜːm / (BrE)
a narrow, raised strip or ledge of land, often used to control water, provide support, or as a shoulder along a road

Examples

A berm along riverbank
A berm was built along the riverbank to prevent flooding during heavy rains.
Hikers often stop to rest on the berm at the top of the trail.
The construction crew added a small berm to direct runoff away from the road.
Cyclists often rest on the grassy berm at the side of the country road.
The driver pulled over onto the berm to check his tires.
Wildflowers were growing all along the berm beside the highway.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shoulder - the strip of land bordering a roadway, for emergencies or breakdowns
