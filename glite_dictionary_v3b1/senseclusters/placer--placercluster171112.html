placer

noun
alluvial deposit with valuable minerals
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈpleɪsər / (AmE), / ˈpleɪsə / (BrE)
An alluvial deposit containing particles of valuable minerals, typically gold, washed down and accumulated in riverbeds or alluvial fans.

Examples

A placer deposit
Many gold prospectors spent years searching for a rich placer in the river valleys.
Placer mining was the earliest method used to extract gold from placer deposits.
The miners found several nuggets in the placer along the creek.
