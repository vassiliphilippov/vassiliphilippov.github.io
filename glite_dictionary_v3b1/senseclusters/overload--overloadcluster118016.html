overload

noun
excessive electrical load
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvərˌloʊd / (AmE), / ˈəʊvələʊd / (BrE)
An electrical load that exceeds the available amount of electrical power, causing a risk of damage or system failure.

Examples

Electrical overload
An overload can trip the circuit breaker to prevent electrical fires.
If you plug too many devices into one outlet, you risk causing an overload.
The technician traced the power failure back to an overload in the main supply line.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overcurrent - an excessive current in an electrical circuit
