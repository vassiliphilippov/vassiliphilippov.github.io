intermesh

verb
to tangle together
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈmɛʃ / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈmɛʃ / (BrE)
to become or cause to become closely connected or tangled together, forming a network or mass that is hard to separate

Examples

Tree roots intermesh
The roots of the old trees intermesh beneath the surface, making it difficult to dig.
As the gears turn, their teeth intermesh perfectly to keep the machine running smoothly.
The branches of the two bushes intermesh, creating a dense barrier along the fence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intertwine - to twist or weave together closely
  • antonym: separate - to move or set apart
