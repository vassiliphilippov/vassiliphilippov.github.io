stumble

noun
an unsteady movement or trip
2.2 0.26/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆstÊŒmbÉ™l / (AmE), / ËˆstÊŒmbÉ™l / (BrE)
An act or instance of walking or moving unsteadily, often because of losing balance temporarily.

Examples

A tired stumble
After a long run, she finished with a tired stumble over the finish line.
His sudden stumble almost made him fall down the stairs.
The actor recovered gracefully from his stumble during the performance.

Synonyms

  • slip
    1.0 0.62/m
    slip involves sliding on a surface and losing footing, while stumble is a brief unsteady trip from catching a foot.
  • stagger
    5.0 0.22/m
    stagger is walking in a long unsteady way as if about to fall, while stumble is a short loss of balance.
  • lurch
    4.7 0.72/m
    lurch is a sudden uncontrolled movement, often forward or sideways, while stumble is a quick, small loss of balance.
