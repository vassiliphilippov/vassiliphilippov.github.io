Britishism

noun
word, phrase, or custom peculiar to Britain
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɪ.t̬ɪʃ.ɪ.zəm /; / ˈbrɪt.ɪ.ʃɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈbrɪ.tɪʃ.ɪ.zəm /; / ˈbrɪt.ɪ.ʃɪz.əm / (BrE)
a word, phrase, or usage that is typical of British English or the United Kingdom, especially as distinct from American English

Examples

Everyday Britishisms
He wrote an article about everyday Britishisms that confuse foreigners.
Many Americans enjoy adopting the occasional Britishism in their speech, such as 'cheers' or 'mate'.
The word 'lorry' is a classic Britishism not widely used in American English.
Her novel is full of Britishisms, which add authenticity to the dialogue.
The word 'lorry' is a classic Britishism unfamiliar to most Americans.
When writing for an American audience, he tries to avoid using too many Britishisms.
