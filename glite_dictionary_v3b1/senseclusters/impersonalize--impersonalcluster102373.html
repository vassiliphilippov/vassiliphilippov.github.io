impersonalize

verb
to make impersonal
7.9
Pronunciation: /ɪm.ˈpɜːr.sən.ə.laɪz/ (AmE), /ɪm.ˈpɜː.sən.ə.laɪz/ (BrE)
to make a verb, clause, or sentence impersonal by removing or avoiding a specific personal subject, often using constructions like 'it' or the passive voice

Examples

To impersonalize a sentence
Writers sometimes impersonalize their language to sound more objective in academic papers.
The teacher asked the students to impersonalize the sentence by using the passive voice.
In scientific writing, it is common to impersonalize statements to avoid using first-person pronouns.

Related Terms

  • synonym: depersonalize - to make something less personal or individual
  • antonym: personalize - to make something personal or specific to an individual
