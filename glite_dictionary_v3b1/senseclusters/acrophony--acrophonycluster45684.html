acrophony

noun
naming a letter using a word with the same initial sound
Pronunciation: / əˈkrɑːfəˌni / (AmE), / əˈkrɒfəni / (BrE)
The practice of naming a letter of the alphabet by using a word whose initial sound is the same as the letter's sound.

Examples

Acrophony in alphabets
Scholars discuss how acrophony influenced the naming of some letters in the Greek alphabet.
The concept of acrophony helps explain why the letter 'B' is called 'beta' in Greek.
The use of acrophony was common in ancient Semitic alphabets.
