windrow

verb
to arrange crops in rows
8.7
Pronunciation: /ˈwɪn.droʊ/ (AmE), /ˈwɪn.drəʊ/ (BrE)
to arrange or gather cut crops, grass, or other material into rows or piles for drying or later collection

Examples

To windrow grass
Farmers windrow the wheat to let it dry before collecting it.
After mowing the field, they windrowed the grass for easier pickup.
The machine can windrow large amounts of hay in a single pass.
After cutting the wheat, the workers windrowed the stalks to dry in the sun.
The machine windrows the grass clippings to make collection easier.
Farmers often windrow hay before it is baled and stored for winter.
The workers used a machine to windrow the cut grass along the edge of the field.
After the storm, crews windrowed the fallen leaves to make cleanup easier.
Farmers often windrow hay before it is collected and baled.
