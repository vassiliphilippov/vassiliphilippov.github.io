expectorate

verb
cough up and spit out phlegm
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈspɛktəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛktəreɪt / (BrE)
to cough up and expel phlegm, mucus, or other material from the lungs or throat

Examples

To expectorate thick mucus
During a chest infection, some people may frequently expectorate thick mucus.
She managed to expectorate the phlegm that had been bothering her all morning.
The doctor instructed the patient to expectorate into a sterile cup for testing.
Certain medications help patients expectorate phlegm from their lungs more easily.
He found it difficult to expectorate due to a severe sore throat.
The doctor advised him to expectorate any mucus instead of swallowing it.
