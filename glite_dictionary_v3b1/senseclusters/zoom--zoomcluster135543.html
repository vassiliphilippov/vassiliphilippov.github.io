zoom

verb
move quickly with a humming sound
1.3 2.47/m
Pronunciation: / zuÀêm / (AmE), / zuÀêm / (BrE)
to move or travel very quickly, often with a humming or buzzing sound

Examples

Cars zoom past
A dragonfly suddenly zoomed over the pond as we walked by.
Children zoom down the hill on their sleds every winter.
The motorcycle zoomed past us on the highway, leaving only a trail of sound behind.
Children zoom down the slide at the playground every afternoon.
The ambulance zoomed through the busy streets to reach the hospital.
The red car zoomed past us on the highway.

Synonyms

  • whizz along
    5.1 0.05/m

  • whiz
    4.5 0.12/m
    whiz moves very fast and often makes a humming or hissing noise as it passes, while zoom suggests fast travel with buzzing.
  • zing
    5.1 0.16/m
    zing moves quickly with a sharp, lively sound, while zoom describes fast travel that often makes a steady buzz.
  • woosh
    7.9 0.01/m
    woosh is a rushing, sibilant sound of something moving very fast, while zoom often suggests fast travel with a buzzing noise.
  • swoosh
    5.6 0.31/m
    swoosh is a sudden rushing or whistling movement often causing a clear sound, while zoom is more about fast travel buzzing.
