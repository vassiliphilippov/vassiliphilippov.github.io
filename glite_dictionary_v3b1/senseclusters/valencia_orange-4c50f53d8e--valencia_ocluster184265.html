Valencia orange

noun
a sweet orange variety used for juice
Pronunciation: / vəˈlɛnsiə ˈɔrɪndʒ / (AmE), / vəˈlɛnsɪə ˈɒrɪndʒ / (BrE)
a variety of sweet orange originally from Spain, widely cultivated for its juicy fruit, especially in Florida and California

Examples

Fresh Valencia oranges
Each summer, California farmers harvest millions of Valencia oranges for markets across the country.
The Valencia orange is prized for its sweet flavor and juiciness, making it ideal for orange juice production.
Unlike the navel orange, the Valencia orange contains seeds but offers a richer flavor.
