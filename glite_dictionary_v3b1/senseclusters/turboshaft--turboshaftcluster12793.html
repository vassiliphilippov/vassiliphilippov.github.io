turboshaft

noun
gas turbine engine
Pronunciation: / ˈtɜːrboʊˌʃæft / (AmE), / ˈtɜːbəʊˌʃɑːft / (BrE)
a type of gas turbine engine that produces power to drive a shaft, commonly used in helicopters and some vehicles

Examples

Turboshaft engine
The helicopter uses a turboshaft to power its main rotor and provide lift.
Modern tanks are sometimes equipped with a turboshaft for greater efficiency and power.
Engineers inspected the turboshaft after the test flight to ensure it was functioning properly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jet engine - an engine that generates thrust by expelling jet gases
  • synonym: turboprop - a gas turbine engine that drives a propeller
