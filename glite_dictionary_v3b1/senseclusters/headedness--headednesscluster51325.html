headedness

noun
having a distinct head
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛdɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈhɛdɪdnəs / (BrE)
the condition of having a distinct head or head region, especially in animals as a result of evolutionary development

Examples

Animal headedness
The headedness of vertebrates allows for centralized control of sensory organs and movement.
Scientists study the evolution of headedness to understand how complex nervous systems developed.
Invertebrates often lack the clear headedness seen in mammals and birds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cephalization - the evolutionary development of a head in animals
