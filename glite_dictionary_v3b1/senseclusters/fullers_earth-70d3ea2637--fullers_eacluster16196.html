fuller's earth

noun
absorbent clay used in cleaning and textile processing
Pronunciation: / ˈfʊlərz ɜrθ / (AmE), / ˈfʊləz ɜːθ / (BrE)
a type of fine, absorbent clay used in cleaning, especially to remove grease or oil, and in processes such as fulling woolen cloth

Examples

Fuller's earth clay
Cosmetic companies sometimes include fuller's earth in facial masks for its oil-absorbing properties.
Historically, fuller's earth was essential in the process of cleaning and thickening woolen fabrics.
The mechanic used fuller's earth to soak up the oil spill on the workshop floor.
