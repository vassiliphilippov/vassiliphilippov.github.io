sternward

adverb
toward the rear
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈstɜrnwɚd /; / ˈstɜrnwərd / (AmE), / ˈstɜːnwəd / (BrE)
toward the rear or stern of a ship, boat, or vehicle

Examples

Move sternward
The crew moved sternward to balance the weight of the boat.
As the train slowed, passengers walked sternward to exit from the rear car.
Equipment was shifted sternward to make space at the front for new supplies.
The crew hurried sternward when they heard the alarm coming from the back of the ship.
As the storm intensified, the passengers were instructed to move sternward for safety.
Cargo was shifted sternward to balance the weight of the vessel during the voyage.
