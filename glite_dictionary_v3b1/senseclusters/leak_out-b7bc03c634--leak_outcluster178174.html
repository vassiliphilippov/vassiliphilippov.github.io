leak out

verb
become known (of secret information)
3.7 1.24/m
Pronunciation: / lik a ät / (AmE), / liÀêk a ät / (BrE)
(of secret or confidential information) to become known to the public, usually unintentionally or without approval

Examples

Secrets leak out
Despite tight security, the confidential information somehow leaked out.
Rumors about the new project leaked out before the official announcement.
The details of the agreement eventually leaked out to the press.
If we aren't careful, the results might leak out ahead of time.
Information tends to leak out before official announcements.
Rumors about the merger started to leak out late last week.

Synonyms

  • get out
    3.5 11/m
    get out often happens when news spreads by mistake through people or media, while leak out means secrets become known without approval.
  • come out
    2.9 34/m
    come out is used when facts are revealed publicly, often deliberately or slowly, while leak out suggests secret information becomes known without permission.

Related Terms
