speckle

verb
mark with small spots
4.4 3.2
Pronunciation: /ˈspek.əl/ (AmE), /ˈspek.əl/ (BrE)
to cover or mark something with many small spots or patches of a different color or texture

Examples

To speckle a surface
Morning dew speckled the grass, sparkling in the early sunlight.
Small flecks of chocolate speckle the top of the dessert, enhancing its visual appeal.
The artist speckled the canvas with bright yellow paint to create a lively effect.
Golden light speckles the forest floor through gaps in the leafy canopy.
Raindrops speckled the window, distorting the view outside.
Tiny flecks of paint speckled her apron after she finished the mural.

Related Terms

  • synonym: daub - to coat or smear carelessly with a thick or sticky substance
  • synonym: spatter - to scatter small drops or spots of a substance
  • synonym: spot - to mark with small round dots
  • synonym: stain - to mark or discolor with patches of different color
  • synonym: stipple - to mark with numerous small dots or specks
