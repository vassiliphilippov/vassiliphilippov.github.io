brachycranial

adjective
having a short, broad head
Pronunciation: / ˌbrækiˈkreɪniəl / (AmE), / ˌbrækiˈkreɪniəl / (BrE)
(of a person or animal) having a short, broad head with a cephalic index of over 80.

Examples

A brachycranial skull
Researchers classified the fossil remains as brachycranial due to their rounded shape.
Some breeds of dogs, such as bulldogs, are considered brachycranial.
The ancient population was predominantly brachycranial, as revealed by skull measurements.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dolichocephalic - having a long, narrow head (cephalic index under 75)
