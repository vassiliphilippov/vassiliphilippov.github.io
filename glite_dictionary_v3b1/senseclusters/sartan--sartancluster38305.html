sartan

noun
blood pressure medication
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑːrtæn / (AmE), / ˈsɑːtæn / (BrE)
a drug used to treat high blood pressure by relaxing blood vessels, belonging to the class of angiotensin II receptor blockers

Examples

Sartan medication
Her doctor recommended a sartan to help manage her high blood pressure.
Many patients tolerate sartans well and experience fewer side effects than with other medications.
The pharmacist explained how a sartan works to relax blood vessels and lower blood pressure.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ACE inhibitor - a drug that lowers blood pressure by inhibiting angiotensin-converting enzyme
  • synonym: angiotensin receptor blocker - a drug that blocks angiotensin II receptors to lower blood pressure
