handfast

noun
old betrothal ceremony
Pronunciation: / ˈhændˌfæst / (AmE), / ˈhændˌfɑːst / (BrE)
an old term for a betrothal or informal marriage ceremony, especially one marked by the joining of hands

Examples

A medieval handfast
In medieval times, a handfast was often used to formalize an engagement before a church wedding.
The couple chose a traditional handfast to celebrate their commitment to each other.
During the handfast, their hands were tied together with a ribbon as a symbol of unity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: betrothal - an agreement to marry; engagement
  • degree higher: wedding - a marriage ceremony and celebration
