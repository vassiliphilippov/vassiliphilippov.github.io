breadroot

noun
North American plant with edible tuberous roots
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɛdˌruːt / (AmE), / ˈbrɛdˌruːt / (BrE)
a perennial plant of central North America with densely hairy stems and edible tuberous roots, formerly used as a food source

Examples

The breadroot plant
Botanists classify breadroot as an important species in the Great Plains ecosystem.
Native American tribes commonly harvested breadroot for its nutritious tubers.
The early settlers dug up breadroot to supplement their diets during harsh winters.
