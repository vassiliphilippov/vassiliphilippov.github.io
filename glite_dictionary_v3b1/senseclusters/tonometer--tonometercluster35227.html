tonometer

noun
instrument for measuring pressure, esp. in the eye
6.9 8.6
Pronunciation: / təˈnɑːmətər / (AmE), / təˈnɒmɪtə / (BrE)
an instrument used to measure tension or pressure, especially the pressure inside the eye

Examples

Eye tonometer
After applying anesthetic drops, the nurse gently placed the tonometer on the surface of the cornea.
Calibration of the tonometer is essential to ensure accurate intraocular pressure measurements.
The ophthalmologist used a tonometer to check for signs of glaucoma in the patient's eyes.
