gloss

verb
deceptively explain or excuse (verb)
8.4 6.4
Pronunciation: /glɔːs/ /; / /glɑːs/ (AmE), /glɒs/ (BrE)
to give a deceptively favorable or misleading explanation or excuse for something, often to cover up faults or mistakes

Examples

To gloss mistakes
He attempted to gloss his absence by blaming a non-existent traffic jam.
Politicians often gloss uncomfortable facts to protect their public image.
The spokesperson tried to gloss over the company's mistakes during the press conference.
