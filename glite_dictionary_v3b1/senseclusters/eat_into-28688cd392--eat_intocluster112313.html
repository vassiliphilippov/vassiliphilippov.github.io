eat into

verb
cause ongoing resentment or anger
Pronunciation: / it ˈɪntu / (AmE), / iːt ˈɪntuː / (BrE)
(of a feeling) to make someone increasingly resentful, angry, or disturbed over time; to gnaw at emotionally.

Examples

Guilt eats into peace
Guilt can eat into your peace of mind if you let it.
His constant criticism began to eat into her self-confidence.
The memory of her betrayal continued to eat into him for years.
