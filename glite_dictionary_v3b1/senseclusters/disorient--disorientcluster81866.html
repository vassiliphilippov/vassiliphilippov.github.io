disorient

verb
cause to lose sense of direction or purpose
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈɔriˌɛnt / (AmE), / dɪsˈɔːriənt / (BrE)
to make someone confused about where they are or what they should do; to make someone lose their sense of direction

Examples

To disorient a person
A severe migraine may disorient a person, making it difficult to focus.
Loud noises sometimes disorient the animals in the shelter.
The sudden darkness can disorient hikers unfamiliar with the area.
