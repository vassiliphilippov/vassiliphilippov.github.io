Dilantin

noun
anticonvulsant drug for epilepsy
6.3
Pronunciation: / dɪˈlæntɪn / (AmE), / dɪˈlæntɪn / (BrE)
A trademarked anticonvulsant drug (phenytoin) used to treat epilepsy and prevent seizures; not a sedative.

Examples

Dilantin medication
He has been taking Dilantin since he was first diagnosed with epilepsy.
Some patients experience side effects when using Dilantin for long-term seizure management.
The doctor prescribed Dilantin to help control her seizures.
