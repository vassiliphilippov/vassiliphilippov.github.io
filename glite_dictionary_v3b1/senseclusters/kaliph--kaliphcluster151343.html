kaliph

noun
Muslim religious and civil leader
Pronunciation: / ˈkælɪf / (AmE), / ˈkælɪf / (BrE)
The civil and religious leader of a Muslim state, regarded as the successor to Muhammad and representative of Allah on earth.

Examples

The kaliph leader
He claimed legitimacy as the kaliph to unite the Muslim world.
The appointment of a new kaliph sparked significant debate within the community.
Throughout history, the role of the kaliph has been both political and spiritual.
