endure

verb
face and withstand hardship or difficulty
1.6
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdʊr / (AmE), / ɪnˈdjʊə / (BrE)
to experience and deal with something difficult or unpleasant, often for a long time, without giving up

Examples

To endure pain
He quietly endured the criticism from his colleagues, never letting it disturb his focus.
Many people endure hardships in pursuit of a better life.
She had to endure months of grueling training to finish the marathon.
She endured criticism and hardship during her rise to success.
The villagers had to endure months of drought before help arrived.
They continue to endure harsh working conditions in the factory.
During the journey, they endured extreme heat and lack of sleep.
He cannot endure the sound of construction outside his window.
She had to endure years of constant criticism at work before she finally quit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bear - to accept, tolerate, or endure something unpleasant

  • antonym: succumb - to give in or yield to something
  • synonym: suffer - to undergo pain, distress, or hardship
  • synonym: survive - to continue to live or exist, especially after something dangerous
  • synonym: tolerate - to allow something to exist or happen even if you do not like it
  • synonym: undergo - to experience or be subjected to (something, typically something unpleasant or arduous)
  • synonym: withstand - to resist or stand up to something successfully
