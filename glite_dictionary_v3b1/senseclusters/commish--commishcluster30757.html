commish

noun
informal commission fee
8.8
Pronunciation: / kəˈmɪʃ / (AmE), / kəˈmɪʃ / (BrE)
an informal term for a commission or fee paid to an agent, salesperson, or middleman

Examples

A ten percent commish
The agent kept a ten percent commish from every sale he arranged for artists.
She asked how much commish she would earn if she closed the deal.
He was happy with the job because the commish was higher than at his last company.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: bonus - an extra payment given as a reward for good work
  • synonym: commission - a fee paid to an agent or salesperson for their services
  • antonym: salary - a fixed regular payment for employment
