circumvent

verb
avoid by cleverness or trickery
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˌsɝː.kəmˈvɛnt /; / ˌsɜrkəmˈvɛnt / (AmE), / ˌsɜː.kəmˈvɛnt /; / ˌsɜːkəmˈvɛnt / (BrE)
to find a way around a rule, obstacle, or responsibility, often by using cleverness or avoidance

Examples

To circumvent the rules
Many students attempt to circumvent the deadline by requesting extensions for vague reasons.
She tried to circumvent the company's strict policies by consulting a loophole in the rules.
They managed to circumvent the security system with an ingenious plan.
Many people attempt to circumvent their responsibilities rather than confront them directly.
She cleverly circumvented the question by changing the subject.
The company tried to circumvent the new regulations by using a loophole.

Related Terms

  • synonym: avoid - to keep away from or prevent something from happening
  • antonym: confront - to face up to and deal with (a problem or difficult situation)
  • synonym: dodge - to avoid (something) by a sudden, clever movement or by cleverness
  • synonym: evade - to escape or get around something, especially by cleverness or trickery
