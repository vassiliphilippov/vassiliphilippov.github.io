channel

noun
navigable stretch of water
2.8
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃæn.əl /; / ˈtʃænəl / (AmE), / ˈtʃæn.əl /; / ˈtʃænəl / (BrE)
a deep and narrow stretch of water, or a passage (natural or artificial) through which water flows

Examples

Shipping channel
A dredger was brought in to deepen the channel so larger vessels could pass through.
Fishermen anchored their boats in the channel during high tide.
The ship carefully navigated the channel to avoid running aground.
A narrow channel carved by centuries of water flow divided the two hills.
Stormwater drains into underground channels beneath the city streets.
The irrigation system uses a network of channels to distribute water to the fields.

Related Terms

  • synonym: canal - an artificial waterway for navigation or irrigation
  • synonym: gutter - a shallow trough fixed beneath the edge of a roof for carrying off rainwater
  • synonym: strait - a narrow passage of water connecting two seas or large areas of water
