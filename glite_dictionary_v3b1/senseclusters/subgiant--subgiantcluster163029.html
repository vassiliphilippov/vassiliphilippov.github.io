subgiant

noun
intermediate star type
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌdʒaɪənt / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌdʒaɪənt / (BrE)
a star that is larger and brighter than a main-sequence star but not yet a giant star

Examples

A subgiant star
The astronomer identified the bright object as a subgiant in the constellation.
As a star ages, it may become a subgiant before turning into a giant.
Scientists study subgiants to understand the evolution of stars.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: giant - a very large and luminous star
  • degree lower: main-sequence star - a star in the main, stable phase of its life
