sebaceous gland

noun
gland secreting oil for skin and hair
Pronunciation: / sɪˈbeɪʃəs ɡlænd / (AmE), / sɪˈbeɪʃəs ɡlænd / (BrE)
A small gland in the skin that produces sebum, an oily substance that lubricates hair and skin, usually opening into a hair follicle.

Examples

A sebaceous gland in skin
Acne can develop when a sebaceous gland becomes blocked or inflamed.
Every hair follicle is typically associated with at least one sebaceous gland.
The sebaceous gland produces oil that keeps the skin moisturized.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sweat gland - a skin gland that secretes sweat
