drown one's sorrows

verb
try to forget unhappiness by eating or drinking
Pronunciation: / draʊn wʌnz ˈsɑroʊz / (AmE), / draʊn wʌnz ˈsɒrəʊz / (BrE)
to try to forget one’s unhappiness or disappointment by eating or drinking, especially drinking alcohol

Examples

To drown one's sorrows in drink
After the breakup, he decided to drown his sorrows at the local bar.
She went out with friends to drown her sorrows after failing the exam.
Some people prefer to drown their sorrows with chocolate and cake instead of alcohol.
