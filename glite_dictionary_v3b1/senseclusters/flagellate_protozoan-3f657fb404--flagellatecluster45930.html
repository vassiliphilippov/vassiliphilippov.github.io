flagellate protozoan

noun
single-celled organism with flagella
Pronunciation: / fləˈdʒɛlət proʊˈtoʊzoʊən / (AmE), / fləˈdʒɛlɪət prəˈtəʊziəʊən / (BrE)
A single-celled organism, typically free-living and nonphotosynthetic, that moves using one or more whiplike appendages called flagella; some can cause disease in humans or animals.

Examples

The flagellate protozoan moves
Scientists observed the rapid movement of the flagellate protozoan under the microscope.
Some flagellate protozoans are responsible for diseases such as giardiasis and sleeping sickness.
The pond water sample contained several flagellate protozoans swimming among the algae.

  • synonym: protozoan - a single-celled microscopic animal
