intellectualization

noun
defense mechanism using reasoning to avoid emotion
7.1
Pronunciation: / ɪn.t̬əlˌɛk.tʃu.əl.əˈzeɪ.ʃən /; / ˌɪntəˌlɛktʃuəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪn.təˌlɛk.tʃu.ə.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən /; / ˌɪntɪˌlɛktjuəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(psychiatry, psychology) The use of reasoning or logic as a defense mechanism to avoid confronting emotional stress or internal conflict.

Examples

Intellectualization defense mechanism
By relying on intellectualization, he could discuss his trauma without ever addressing how it affected him.
During therapy, she recognized her habit of intellectualization as a way to avoid dealing with painful feelings.
The professor’s tendency toward intellectualization made it difficult for him to connect emotionally with his students.
His reliance on intellectualisation prevented him from addressing his true feelings about the loss.
In therapy, she tended to use intellectualisation to discuss traumatic events without emotional involvement.
Some people resort to intellectualisation as a way of coping with personal conflict.
