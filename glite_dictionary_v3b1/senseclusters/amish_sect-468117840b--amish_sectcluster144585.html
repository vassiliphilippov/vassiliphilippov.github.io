Amish sect

noun
religious group of conservative Anabaptists
7.8 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…ëÀê.m…™ É s…õkt / (AmE), / Àà…ëÀê.m…™ É sekt / (BrE)
a strict Anabaptist religious group that broke from the Mennonites in the late 17th century and is known for simple living, plain dress, and reluctance to adopt many conveniences of modern technology; chiefly found in southeastern Pennsylvania and other parts of the U.S.

Examples

The Amish sect community
In southeastern Pennsylvania, members of the Amish sect are known for their handcrafted furniture and homemade foods.
The Amish sect maintains traditions that go back centuries, including horse-drawn transportation and simple living.
Unlike other religious communities, the Amish sect generally avoids modern technology in daily life.
