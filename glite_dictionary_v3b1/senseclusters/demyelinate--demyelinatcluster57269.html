demyelinate

verb
remove the myelin sheath from a nerve
3.1 9.6
Pronunciation: / diˈmaɪə.ləˌneɪt / (AmE), / diˈmaɪ.ə.lɪ.neɪt / (BrE)
to destroy or remove the myelin sheath from a nerve fiber

Examples

To demyelinate nerve fibers
Multiple sclerosis can demyelinate the nerves, leading to muscle weakness and numbness.
Researchers study drugs that might prevent diseases from demyelinating nerve cells.
Some viruses may demyelinate the optic nerve, causing vision problems.
