schematism

noun
diagrammatic representation
Pronunciation: / ˈskiːməˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈskiːmətɪzəm / (BrE)
the practice or result of representing things in a simplified or schematic way, such as using diagrams or basic shapes

Examples

Schematism in diagrams
The architect used schematism to present the building's layout to the clients.
In science textbooks, schematism helps students understand complex systems more easily.
The teacher drew a simple schematism on the board to explain the water cycle.
The artist's schematism reduced complex forms to basic shapes.
Modern architecture often uses schematism to emphasize function over decoration.
Critics argued that the film's schematism made its characters seem unrealistic.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abstraction - the process of considering something independently of its associations
  • synonym: diagram - a drawing that explains or shows the parts of something
  • antonym: realism - the practice of representing things as they actually are
  • synonym: schematic - a simplified drawing showing the structure of something
