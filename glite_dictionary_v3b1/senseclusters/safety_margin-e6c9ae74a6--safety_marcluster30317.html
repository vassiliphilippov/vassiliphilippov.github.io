safety margin

noun
extra allowance for safety
3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪfti ˌmɑrdʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈseɪfti ˌmɑːdʒɪn / (BrE)
an extra amount included to allow for uncertainty or error, ensuring that safety is not compromised

Examples

A safety margin in finance
Good investors always want a safety margin before taking financial risks.
It is important to have a safety margin when calculating medication dosages.
The engineers included a safety margin to account for unexpected loads on the bridge.

Related Terms

  • synonym: margin of safety - the limit or buffer added for safety, especially in engineering or finance
