exorbitant

adjective
excessive, unreasonably high (esp. price)
Pronunciation: / ɪgˈzɔrbɪtənt / (AmE), / ɪgˈzɔːbɪtənt / (BrE)
(of a price, amount, or demand) much greater than is reasonable or expected; excessive.

Examples

An exorbitant price
Critics say the tax is exorbitant and unfair to the city's residents.
Some hotels demand exorbitant prices during major holidays.
The landlord was charging an exorbitant rent for such a small apartment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: extortionate - unreasonably high, especially of prices or demands
  • antonym: moderate - kept within reasonable limits, not extreme
  • synonym: outrageous - shockingly excessive or extravagant
  • antonym: reasonable - fair, moderate in amount, not excessive
