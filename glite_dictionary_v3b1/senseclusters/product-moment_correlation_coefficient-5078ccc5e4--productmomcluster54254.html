product-moment correlation coefficient

noun
statistical measure of linear correlation
Pronunciation: / ˌprɑːdʌkt ˈmoʊmənt kəˌrɛl.ˈeɪ.ʃən kəʊ.ˈɪfə.ʃənt / (AmE), / ˌprɒdʌkt ˈməʊmənt kəˌrɛl.ˈeɪ.ʃən kəʊ.ˈɪfə.ʃənt / (BrE)
a statistical measure of the strength and direction of a linear relationship between two variables

Examples

The product-moment correlation coefficient value
A high product-moment correlation coefficient indicates a strong linear relationship between two variables.
The product-moment correlation coefficient is often denoted by the symbol r in statistics.
To measure the association between height and weight, researchers used the product-moment correlation coefficient.
