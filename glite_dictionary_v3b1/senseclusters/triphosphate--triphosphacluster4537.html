triphosphate

noun
compound with three phosphates
7.0
Pronunciation: / traɪˈfɑsˌfeɪt / (AmE), / traɪˈfɒsfeɪt / (BrE)
a molecule or chemical compound containing three phosphate groups linked together

Examples

Adenosine triphosphate
Adenosine triphosphate is essential for providing energy to many cellular processes.
The scientist explained how a triphosphate releases energy when one phosphate group is removed.
Many enzymes interact with triphosphate molecules during metabolic reactions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ATP - adenosine triphosphate, a molecule that stores energy in cells
  • degree lower: diphosphate - a compound containing two phosphate groups
  • degree lower: monophosphate - a compound containing one phosphate group
