Hassidim

noun
Jewish religious sect members
9.9
Pronunciation: /hə.ˈsiː.dɪm/ /; / /hə.ˈsɪ.dɪm/ (AmE), /hə.ˈsiː.dɪm/ /; / /hə.ˈsɪ.dɪm/ (BrE)
Members of a Jewish religious sect, originating in eastern Europe in the 18th century, known for strict observance of the Mosaic law and distinctive traditions.

Examples

The Hassidim community
Many Hassidim gather each year to celebrate Jewish holidays in their traditional way.
The Hassidim maintain unique customs and dress that distinguish them from other Jewish communities.
The teachings of the Hassidim emphasize joy, mysticism, and devotion in worship.
