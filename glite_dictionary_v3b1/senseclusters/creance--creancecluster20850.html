creance

noun
claim or debt owed
Pronunciation: / ˈkriːəns / (AmE), / ˈkriːəns / (BrE)
an amount of money or claim owed to a creditor, especially in legal or financial contexts (now rare or obsolete)

Examples

A creance owed
The merchant listed the creance among his financial records for the year.
In old legal documents, a creance often referred to a sum owed by a debtor.
She filed a creance with the court to recover the unpaid loan from the firm.

  • synonym: debt - an amount of money owed by one person or entity to another
