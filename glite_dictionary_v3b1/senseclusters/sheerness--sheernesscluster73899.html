sheerness

noun
steepness quality
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɪrnəs / (AmE), / ˈʃɪənəs / (BrE)
the quality of being very steep or almost vertical, especially in reference to a slope or surface

Examples

Sheerness of the cliff
The sheerness of the mountain made the climb especially challenging for the hikers.
Photographers were amazed by the sheerness of the cliffs rising above the sea.
The sheerness of the canyon walls left visitors in awe of the natural landscape.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gentleness - the quality of being gradual or not steep
  • synonym: steepness - the degree to which something rises sharply
