break the story

verb
report news first
Pronunciation: / breɪk ðə ˈstɔri / (AmE), / breɪk ðə ˈstɔːri / (BrE)
to be the first to report or publish news about an event

Examples

To break the story first
Several media outlets tried to break the story as soon as credible details emerged.
She stayed up all night so she could break the story in the morning edition.
The newspaper was proud to break the story ahead of all its competitors.
