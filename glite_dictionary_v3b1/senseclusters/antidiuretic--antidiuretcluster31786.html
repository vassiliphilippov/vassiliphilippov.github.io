antidiuretic

noun
drug reducing urine production
Pronunciation: / ˌæntiˌdaɪjʊˈrɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌæntiˌdaɪjʊəˈrɛtɪk / (BrE)
A drug or substance that reduces the production of urine by the kidneys.

Examples

An antidiuretic drug
In cases of excessive urination, an antidiuretic may be recommended to manage the condition.
Some hormones act as natural antidiuretic agents in the body.
The doctor prescribed an antidiuretic to help regulate his fluid balance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diuretic - a drug that increases the production of urine
