engraftment

noun
graft taking root
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈɡræftmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈɡrɑːftmənt / (BrE)
the process by which a grafted piece of plant tissue becomes attached to and grows with its host plant

Examples

Tree engraftment
Successful engraftment is essential for the new branch to thrive on the host tree.
The gardener monitored the engraftment of the rose cutting over several weeks.
Without proper care, engraftment may fail and the grafted tissue will not survive.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grafting - the act of joining plant tissues together
  • antonym: rejection - failure of a graft to unite with the host
