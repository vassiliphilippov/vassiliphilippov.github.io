simple microscope

noun
microscope with single convex lens
2.2
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪmpəl ˌmaɪ.kroʊ.skoʊp / (AmE), / ˈsɪmpəl ˌmaɪ.krə.skəʊp / (BrE)
a light microscope that uses a single convex lens to produce an enlarged image of an object

Examples

A simple microscope lens
A simple microscope is often used by jewelers to inspect the details of gemstones.
Before the invention of the compound microscope, scientists relied on the simple microscope to study tiny organisms.
The simple microscope paved the way for more advanced optical instruments in scientific research.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: compound microscope - light microscope with two sets of lenses for greater magnification
  • synonym: magnifying glass - hand-held lens that magnifies objects, simplest form of simple microscope
