stimulability

noun
ability to imitate
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˌstɪmjələˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌstɪmjʊləˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the ability to produce a specific sound or behavior when prompted or given a model to imitate

Examples

Speech stimulability test
The speech therapist tested the child's stimulability for producing the 's' sound correctly.
High stimulability often means a child will make faster progress in speech therapy.
The assessment showed low stimulability for certain speech sounds, indicating more practice was needed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: imitability - the quality of being able to be imitated
  • antonym: inability - lack of ability to do something
