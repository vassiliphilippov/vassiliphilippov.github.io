MKS

sym
unit system in physics
Pronunciation: / ɛm keɪ ɛs / (AmE), / ɛm keɪ ɛs / (BrE)
a system of physical units based on the meter, kilogram, and second as fundamental units

Examples

The MKS system
Many scientific calculations use the MKS system for consistency and clarity.
The MKS system defines the meter, kilogram, and second as its base units.
Engineers often convert measurements to the MKS system for international projects.

Related Terms

  • antonym: CGS - a system of units based on the centimeter, gram, and second
  • synonym: SI - the International System of Units, an extension of the MKS system
