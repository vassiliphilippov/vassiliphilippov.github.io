lantern pinion

noun
gear wheel with bars instead of teeth
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlæntɚn ˈpɪniən / (AmE), / ˈlæntən ˈpɪniən / (BrE)
a small gear wheel with cylindrical bars instead of teeth, used mainly in inexpensive clocks.

Examples

A lantern pinion gear
A lantern pinion consists of cylindrical rods instead of the usual gear teeth.
Repairing antique clocks often involves fabricating a new lantern pinion.
The old clock’s lantern pinion was badly worn and needed to be replaced.
