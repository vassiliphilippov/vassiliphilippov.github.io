philosophical doctrine

noun
belief or principle in philosophy
5.3
Pronunciation: / fɪˌlɑːsəˈfɪkəl ˈdɑːktrɪn / (AmE), / fɪˌlɒsəˈfɪkəl ˈdɒktrɪn / (BrE)
a principle or set of beliefs that forms part of a particular philosophy

Examples

A philosophical doctrine of determinism
Her essay critiques the philosophical doctrine of determinism.
Many students struggle to grasp each philosophical doctrine discussed in the course.
The concept of free will is a central philosophical doctrine in many schools of thought.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dogma - a principle or set of principles laid down by an authority as incontrovertibly true
  • synonym: philosophy - the study of the fundamental nature of knowledge, reality, and existence
