abdominal aortic aneurysm

noun
enlargement of the abdominal aorta
5.8
Pronunciation: /æb.ˈdɑː.mə.nəl eɪ.ˈɔːr.tɪk ˈæn.jʊr.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /əb.ˈdɑː.mə.nəl eɪ.ˈɔːr.tɪk ˈæn.jʊr.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /æb.ˈdɒ.mə.nəl eɪ.ˈɔː.tɪk ˈæn.jʊə.rɪ.zəm/ /; / /əb.ˈdɒ.mə.nəl eɪ.ˈɔː.tɪk ˈæn.jʊə.rɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
an enlargement or bulging in the wall of the abdominal aorta, often associated with older age and high blood pressure, which can be life-threatening if it ruptures

Examples

An abdominal aortic aneurysm rupture
An abdominal aortic aneurysm can be life-threatening if it ruptures suddenly.
Screening programs have helped detect abdominal aortic aneurysms in people over the age of 65.
The patient was diagnosed with an abdominal aortic aneurysm during a routine ultrasound scan.
