isospin

noun
quantum symmetry property
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪsoʊˌspɪn / (AmE), / ˈaɪsəʊˌspɪn / (BrE)
a quantum number in nuclear physics that describes the symmetry between protons and neutrons

Examples

Isospin symmetry
The theory of isospin helps physicists understand the relationship between protons and neutrons.
In particle physics, isospin is used to classify particles with similar properties.
The conservation of isospin plays a key role in certain nuclear reactions.
