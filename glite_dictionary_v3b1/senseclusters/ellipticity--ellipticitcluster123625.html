ellipticity

noun
degree of being elliptical (flattened at poles)
8.0 7.2
Pronunciation: /ɪ.lɪp.ˈtɪ.sɪ.tiː/ /; / /ə.lɪp.ˈtɪ.sɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /ə.lɪp.ˈtɪ.sɪ.tiː/ /; / /ɪ.lɪp.ˈtɪ.sɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
the quality or state of being shaped like an ellipse, especially the degree to which a spherical object is flattened at the poles

Examples

The ellipticity of galaxies
Astronomers measure the ellipticity of galaxies to understand their shape and formation history.
Due to its fast rotation, Jupiter has a noticeable ellipticity compared to slower-spinning planets.
The ellipticity of Earth causes the equatorial diameter to be larger than the polar diameter.
