dorsomedial

adjective
toward back and middle
Pronunciation: / ˌdɔrsəˈmiːdiəl / (AmE), / ˌdɔːsəˈmiːdiəl / (BrE)
located toward both the back (dorsal) and the middle (medial) of an organism or structure

Examples

The dorsomedial region
The dorsomedial region of the spinal cord is responsible for certain sensory functions.
Researchers observed changes in the dorsomedial prefrontal cortex during the experiment.
Damage to the dorsomedial thalamic nucleus can affect memory and emotional processing.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ventrolateral - located toward the front and side of an organism or structure
