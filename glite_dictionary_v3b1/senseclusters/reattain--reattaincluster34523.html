reattain

verb
to achieve again
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˌriːəˈteɪn / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈteɪn / (BrE)
to achieve or reach something again after having lost it

Examples

To reattain confidence
After months of hard work, she managed to reattain her confidence.
The athlete is determined to reattain his championship title next season.
It can be difficult to reattain trust once it has been broken.
After months of training, he finally reattained his previous level of fitness.
The company reattained its market share after launching a new product line.
She worked hard to reattain the trust of her colleagues.
