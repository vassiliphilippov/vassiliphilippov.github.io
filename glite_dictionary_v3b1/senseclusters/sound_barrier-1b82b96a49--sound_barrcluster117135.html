sound barrier

noun
aerodynamic effect near speed of sound
Pronunciation: / ˈsaʊnd ˌbæriɚ / (AmE), / ˈsaʊnd ˌbærɪə / (BrE)
The sudden increase in aerodynamic drag and other effects that occur as an aircraft nears the speed of sound.

Examples

Breaking the sound barrier
As the jet approached the sound barrier, the airframe began to vibrate violently.
Improvements in design have made crossing the sound barrier much safer for modern aircraft.
The test pilot attempted to break the sound barrier during his record-setting flight.

  • degree higher: supersonic - faster than the speed of sound
