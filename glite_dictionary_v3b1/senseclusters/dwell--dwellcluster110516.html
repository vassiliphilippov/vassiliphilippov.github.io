dwell

verb
exist or reside within (esp. the mind or heart)
Pronunciation: / dwɛl / (AmE), / dwɛl / (BrE)
to exist or be present in a particular place, especially in the mind, heart, or a situation

Examples

Feelings dwell inside
A deep sadness seemed to dwell within his heart.
Doubts continued to dwell in her mind long after the event.
Resentment can dwell inside us for years if left unaddressed.
A sense of unease dwells in the community after the incident.
For many, hope dwells in the belief that change is possible.
The real issue dwelled in the lack of communication between departments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inhabit - to live in or occupy a place
  • synonym: reside - to live or be situated in a particular place
