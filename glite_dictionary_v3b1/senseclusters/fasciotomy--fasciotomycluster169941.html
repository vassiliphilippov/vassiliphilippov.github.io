fasciotomy

noun
surgical cutting of fascia
9.9
Pronunciation: /fæ.ʃi.ˈɑː.tə.miː/ (AmE), /fæ.ʃi.ˈɒ.tə.miː/ (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which the fascia is cut to relieve tension or pressure in a muscle compartment

Examples

A fasciotomy surgery
The surgeon performed a fasciotomy to relieve the patient's severe leg pain.
After the accident, a fasciotomy was necessary to prevent permanent muscle damage.
Recovery from a fasciotomy can take several weeks, depending on the severity of the injury.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decompression - the act of relieving pressure, especially in a medical context
  • synonym: incision - a surgical cut made in skin or flesh
