craterous

adjective
full of craters
Pronunciation: / ˈkreɪtərəs / (AmE), / ˈkreɪtərəs / (BrE)
covered with or resembling craters, especially having many round depressions or holes like those on the moon's surface

Examples

craterous surface
The old battlefield had a craterous landscape after years of heavy shelling.
Scientists studied the craterous terrain of the asteroid for signs of past impacts.
The volcanic eruption left the ground craterous and uneven for miles around.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pitted - marked with small holes or depressions
  • antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface
