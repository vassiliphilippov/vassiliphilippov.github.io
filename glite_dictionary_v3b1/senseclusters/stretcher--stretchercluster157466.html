stretcher

noun
stone or brick laid lengthwise
Pronunciation: / ˈstrɛtʃər / (AmE), / ˈstrɛtʃə / (BrE)
a stone or brick laid with its long side exposed, especially one forming the top of a wall or building

Examples

Brick stretcher
Historic buildings often feature decorative stretchers along their upper edges.
The architect specified that only limestone stretchers should be used for the façade.
The mason carefully placed a stretcher on top of the wall to finish the row.
