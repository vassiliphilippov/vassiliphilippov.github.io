hard-of-hearing

adjective
partially unable to hear
4.1
Pronunciation: / hɑrd əv ˈhɪrɪŋ / (AmE), / hɑːd əv ˈhɪə.rɪŋ / (BrE)
having reduced hearing ability; partially unable to hear sounds.

Examples

Hard-of-hearing person
Her grandfather is hard-of-hearing and wears a special device to help him understand conversations.
Please speak up because some members of the audience are hard-of-hearing.
The conference provided sign language interpreters for the hard-of-hearing attendees.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: deaf - completely lacking the sense of hearing
  • degree lower: hearing - ability to perceive sound
