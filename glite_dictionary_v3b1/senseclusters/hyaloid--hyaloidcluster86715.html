hyaloid

noun
transparent membrane in the eye
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪəˌlɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈhaɪəlɔɪd / (BrE)
The transparent membrane that surrounds the vitreous humor of the eye and separates it from the retina.

Examples

Hyaloid membrane of the eye
Damage to the hyaloid can complicate certain eye surgeries and may impact vision.
During embryonic development, the hyaloid gradually regresses as the eye matures.
The surgeon carefully removed the hyaloid to gain access to the patient's retina.
