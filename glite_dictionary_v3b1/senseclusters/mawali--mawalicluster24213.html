mawali

noun
client of Arab tribe
9.9
Pronunciation: / maˈwɑːli / (AmE), / maˈwɑːli / (BrE)
a non-Arab client or ally attached to an Arab tribe through a patronage relationship, especially in early Islamic society

Examples

Mawali of a tribe
In early Islamic history, many mawali played important roles in the administration of the caliphate.
The mawali often adopted the customs and language of their Arab patrons.
After gaining his freedom, he became one of the mawali and was integrated into the tribe.

Related Terms

  • synonym: client - a person under the protection of another in a patronage system
  • antonym: patron - a person who gives support or protection to another
