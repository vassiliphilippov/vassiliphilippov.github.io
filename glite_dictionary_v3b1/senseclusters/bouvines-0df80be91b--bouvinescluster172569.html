Bouvines

proper noun
site of 1214 battle in northern France
Pronunciation: / buːˈviːn / (AmE), / buːˈviːn / (BrE)
A village in northern France, famous as the site of the Battle of Bouvines in 1214, where French forces under Philip Augustus defeated a coalition of English, Flemish, and German troops.

Examples

Bouvines village in France
Historians consider the Battle of Bouvines a turning point in medieval European history.
Many French schoolchildren learn about Bouvines and its significance at an early age.
The coalition forces suffered a decisive defeat at Bouvines in 1214.
