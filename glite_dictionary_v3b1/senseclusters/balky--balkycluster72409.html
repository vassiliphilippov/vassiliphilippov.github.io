balky

adjective
uncooperative or stubborn
8.8 0.35/m
Pronunciation: / Ààb…îki / (AmE), / Ààb…îÀêki / (BrE)
unwilling to proceed, act, or cooperate; stubborn or uncooperative, especially when expected to do so

Examples

A balky engine
His balky attitude made the negotiations slow and difficult.
She tried to lead the balky horse, but it would not budge.
The old truck was balky and refused to start on cold mornings.

Synonyms

    4.5 1.31/m
    uncooperative is unwilling to help or work with others, while balky refuses to cooperate especially when action is expected.
  • stubborn
    2.9 8.86/m
    stubborn means unwilling to change your mind even when pressed, while balky often refuses to act or cooperate.
  • recalcitrant
    8.1 1.30/m
    recalcitrant stubbornly resists authority or control, while balky more generally refuses to act or cooperate when expected.
  • stiff-necked
    8.1 0.15/m
    stiff-necked is stubborn and hard to persuade in opinion or behavior, while balky often simply refuses to cooperate.
  • noncompliant
    7.6 0.52/m
    noncompliant refuses to obey rules, orders, or requests, while balky usually refuses to act or cooperate in general situations.
  • reluctant
    3.5 19/m
    reluctant feels hesitant or unwilling to do something, while balky is actively stubborn or refuses to cooperate when expected.
