hand-waving

adjective
vague or unsubstantiated
8.3
Pronunciation: /hænd ˈweɪ.vɪŋ/ (AmE), /hænd ˈweɪ.vɪŋ/ (BrE)
not rigorous or detailed, relying on vague arguments or unsupported statements instead of solid evidence

Examples

Hand-waving claims
The professor criticized the student's hand-waving explanation for lacking clear evidence.
Many political debates are filled with hand-waving claims that are never properly supported.
Instead of a hand-waving answer, the committee requested detailed data and analysis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: specious - seeming correct or plausible but actually wrong or misleading
