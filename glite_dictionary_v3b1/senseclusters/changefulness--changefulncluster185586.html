changefulness

noun
tendency to change; variability
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃeɪndʒ.fəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈtʃeɪndʒ.fəl.nəs / (BrE)
the quality of being likely to change; variability or tendency to alter

Examples

The changefulness of style
She admired the changefulness in his art, as each painting revealed a new style.
The changefulness of the weather in this region makes it difficult to plan outdoor events.
With such changefulness in the economy, investors must be cautious.

Related Terms

  • synonym: changeability - the quality of being able to change easily
  • antonym: consistency - the quality of staying the same over time
