ingrown

adjective
growing inward into the flesh
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌɡroʊn / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌɡrəʊn / (BrE)
(of a hair, nail, etc.) growing abnormally into the skin or flesh instead of outward.

Examples

An ingrown toenail
He suffered from an ingrown toenail that required minor surgery to remove.
Sometimes shaving too closely can cause an ingrown hair.
The doctor explained how to prevent ingrown nails from developing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embedded - fixed firmly and deeply in a surrounding mass
