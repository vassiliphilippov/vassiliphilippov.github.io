let alone

adverb
used to emphasize an unlikely or extreme comparison
4.9 2.9
Pronunciation: /let ə.ˈloʊn/ /; / /lət ə.ˈloʊn/ (AmE), /let ə.ˈləʊn/ /; / /lət ə.ˈləʊn/ (BrE)
used to emphasize that what has just been mentioned is even more unlikely or impossible than another, more obvious thing; much less.

Examples

Coffee, let alone lunch
He didn’t have enough money for a coffee, let alone a full meal.
Most six-year-olds can’t solve algebra problems, let alone calculus equations.
She has never been to Paris, let alone London or Rome.

Related Terms

  • synonym: much less - used to emphasize a greater degree of improbability
  • synonym: still less - even less likely or possible
