disbar

verb
expel a lawyer from legal practice
2.1 6.6
Pronunciation: /dɪs.ˈbɑːr/ (AmE), /dɪs.ˈbɑː/ (BrE)
to officially take away a lawyer's right to work because of illegal or unethical conduct

Examples

To disbar a lawyer
If a lawyer is caught embezzling client funds, the state may disbar them permanently.
Many believe that any lawyer who commits perjury should be quickly disbarred.
The disciplinary committee decided to disbar the attorney after his conviction for fraud.
