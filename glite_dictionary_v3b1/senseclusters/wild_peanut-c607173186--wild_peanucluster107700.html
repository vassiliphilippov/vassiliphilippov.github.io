wild peanut

noun
North American vine with peanut-like pods
4.5
Pronunciation: / waɪld ˈpiː.nʌt / (AmE), / waɪld ˈpiː.nʌt / (BrE)
a vine native to eastern North America that produces clusters of purple to maroon flowers and edible, usually subterranean, pods similar to peanuts

Examples

Wild peanut vine
During the hike, we spotted a patch of wild peanut flowering beside the creek.
Native Americans once harvested the wild peanut for its nutritious, peanut-like pods.
The wild peanut spreads quickly in warm, sandy soils throughout the eastern United States.
