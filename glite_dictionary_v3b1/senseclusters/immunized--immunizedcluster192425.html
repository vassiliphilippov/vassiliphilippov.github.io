immunized

adjective
protected against a disease (usually by vaccination)
1.0 3.2
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈmjuː.naɪzd/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈmjuː.naɪzd/ (BrE)
Having been made immune to a particular disease, typically by vaccination.

Examples

To be immunized against disease
After getting her second shot, she was finally immunized and could travel safely.
All healthcare workers must be immunized against hepatitis B before starting their jobs.
Children are immunized at an early age to protect them from common childhood diseases.
After traveling abroad, she was immunised for several tropical diseases.
All schoolchildren must be immunised against measles before starting classes.
The immunised population had a much lower risk of infection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: immunized - Having been rendered immune to a disease (American spelling)
  • antonym: susceptible - at risk of being affected by a disease
  • synonym: vaccinated - having received a vaccine to provide immunity
