horse armor

noun
video-game cosmetic for mounts
6.1
Pronunciation: /hɔːrs ˈɑːr.mər/ (AmE), /hɔːs ˈɑː.mə/ (BrE)
(in video games) a virtual, cosmetic item for a player's mount that changes the mount's appearance but does not affect protection, stats, or gameplay.

Examples

Game horse armor
The game's new DLC included elaborate horse armor designs sold separately in the store.
Players paid real money for shiny horse armor even though it offered no gameplay benefits.
Community forums debated whether the expensive horse armor was worth buying as a vanity item.

Related Terms

  • synonym: vanity item - an in-game object purchased or equipped solely for appearance
