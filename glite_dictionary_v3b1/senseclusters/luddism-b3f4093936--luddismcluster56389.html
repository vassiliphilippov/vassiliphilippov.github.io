Luddism

noun
anti-industrial movement
Pronunciation: / ˈlʌdɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈlʌdɪzəm / (BrE)
the social movement in early 19th-century England in which textile workers protested industrialization by destroying machinery

Examples

19th-century Luddism protests
The rise of Luddism reflected workers' fears about losing their jobs to machines.
Many books discuss how Luddism shaped attitudes toward technology in Britain.
Some people today use Luddism to describe resistance to new technologies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: industrialization - the process of developing industries in a country or region
  • synonym: machine breaking - the act of destroying industrial machinery as protest
