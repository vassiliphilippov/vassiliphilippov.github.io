anxiety reaction

noun
persistent excessive anxiety and related symptoms
5.5 5.3
Pronunciation: /æŋ.ˈzaɪ.ə.ti/ /riː.ˈæk.ʃən/ /; / /æŋɡ.ˈzaɪ.ə.ti/ /riː.ˈæk.ʃən/ (AmE), /æŋ.ˈzaɪ.ə.ti/ /ri.ˈæk.ʃən/ /; / /æŋɡ.ˈzaɪ.ə.ti/ /ri.ˈæk.ʃən/ (BrE)
a psychological condition marked by persistent, excessive anxiety and symptoms such as tension, sweating, trembling, or irritability, lasting for an extended period

Examples

An anxiety reaction symptom
After his accident, Tom began to show signs of an anxiety reaction that affected his daily life.
Her anxiety reaction became noticeable when she started to avoid social gatherings and appeared constantly tense.
Patients experiencing an anxiety reaction may report chronic worry, restlessness, or problems sleeping.
