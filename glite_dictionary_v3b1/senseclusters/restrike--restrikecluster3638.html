restrike

noun
new copy from original
Pronunciation: / ˈriːˌstraɪk / (AmE), / ˈriːˌstraɪk / (BrE)
a new impression or copy made from an original mold, plate, or die, especially of coins or prints

Examples

A restrike print
The museum displayed a restrike of a famous 19th-century print alongside the original.
Some coin collectors prefer a restrike when the original is too rare or expensive.
The artist authorized a limited restrike of her popular etching for new buyers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: original - the first or earliest version from which copies are made
  • synonym: reproduction - a copy of an original work, especially in art or collectibles
