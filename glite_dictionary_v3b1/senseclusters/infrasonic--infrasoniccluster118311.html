infrasonic

adjective
having frequencies below audible sound
3.7 7.1
Pronunciation: /ɪn.frə.ˈsɑː.nɪk/ (AmE), /ɪn.frə.ˈsɒn.ɪk/ (BrE)
(of sound waves) having frequencies below the threshold of human hearing (generally below 20 hertz).

Examples

Infrasonic sound waves
Researchers are studying the effect of infrasonic noise on human health.
Some animals can communicate using infrasonic sounds that humans cannot hear.
The volcano emitted infrasonic waves that were detected by specialized sensors.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ultrasonic - having frequencies above those audible to humans
