take in

verb
absorb or draw in
1.0
Pronunciation: / teɪk ɪn / (AmE), / teɪk ɪn / (BrE)
to absorb or draw in something physically, such as a liquid, gas, or other substance

Examples

Plants take in air
Plants take in carbon dioxide during photosynthesis.
The sponge quickly took in all the spilled water on the floor.
Your lungs take in oxygen every time you breathe.
Cotton cloth can take in more moisture than synthetic fibers.
His mind was quick to take in new ideas and concepts.
The plant roots take in nutrients from the soil.
