Asiatic flu

noun
1957 Asian influenza pandemic
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪʒiˈætɪk flu / (AmE), / ˌeɪzɪˈatɪk flu / (BrE)
an influenza pandemic caused by the Asian influenza virus, first identified in 1957

Examples

The Asiatic flu pandemic
Doctors studied the Asiatic flu to develop more effective vaccines.
Many public health policies were revised in response to the Asiatic flu outbreak.
The Asiatic flu pandemic of 1957 spread rapidly across the globe.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: Spanish flu - 1918–1919 influenza pandemic
  • synonym: influenza - an infectious viral disease commonly known as the flu
