proprioception

noun
machine or robot self-sensing
9.5
Pronunciation: /proʊ.prɪ.oʊ.ˈsep.ʃən/ /; / /proʊ.prɪ.ə.ˈsep.ʃən/ (AmE), /prəʊ.prɪ.ə.ˈsep.ʃən/ /; / /prəʊ.priː.ə.ˈsep.ʃən/ (BrE)
(in robotics and computing) the internal sensing capability of a machine or robot to perceive its own position, joint angles, orientation, and movement, typically using encoders and internal motion sensors.

Examples

Robot proprioception
The humanoid robot relied on its precise proprioception to place the cup on the shelf without looking.
Engineers improved the drone's proprioception by adding joint encoders and internal motion sensors.
During testing, we measured the arm's proprioception accuracy while it performed delicate assembly tasks.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exteroception - sensing of the external environment (opposite of internal self-sensing)
  • synonym: proprioceptive sensor - a sensor (for example, an encoder or IMU) that provides a machine with information about its own position or movement
