Rusin

noun
East Slavic language
Pronunciation: / ˈruːsɪn / (AmE), / ˈruːsɪn / (BrE)
the East Slavic language spoken by the Rusin (Rusyn) people in parts of Eastern Europe

Examples

Rusin language
He learned to speak Rusin with help from his grandmother over the summer.
Many villages in the Carpathian region still use Rusin in daily conversation.
The documentary explored the history and culture of the Rusin language.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Rusyn - the East Slavic language of the Rusyn people
  • antonym: Slovak - a West Slavic language spoken in Slovakia
  • synonym: Ukrainian - the official language of Ukraine, closely related to Rusin
