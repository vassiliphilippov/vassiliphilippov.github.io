whack

noun
a sharp, loud sound from a strike
Pronunciation: / wæk / (AmE), / wæk / (BrE)
a sharp, forceful blow or the loud sound it makes

Examples

A loud whack
I heard a sudden whack from the next room and rushed to see what happened.
The stick made a loud whack as it hit the table.
With a single whack, he split the log in two.
He gave the old TV a good whack to get it working again.
She received a painful whack on the knee during the game.
With a loud whack, the tennis ball flew over the net.

Related Terms

  • synonym: smack - a sharp, loud sound produced by a slap or hit
  • degree higher: smash - a very forceful blow
  • synonym: strike - to hit forcibly
  • degree lower: tap - a light touch or gentle hit
  • degree lower: thud - a dull sound made by a heavy object hitting something
