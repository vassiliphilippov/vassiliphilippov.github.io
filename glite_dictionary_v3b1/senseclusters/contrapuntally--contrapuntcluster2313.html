contrapuntally

adverb
with interwoven contrasts
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːntrəˈpʌntəli / (AmE), / ˌkɒntrəˈpʌntəli / (BrE)
in a way that combines or contrasts two or more independent elements, especially to create an interwoven effect

Examples

Contrapuntally weaving stories
The novel unfolds contrapuntally, weaving together the lives of two families across generations.
The composer structured the piece contrapuntally, allowing each instrument to play an independent melody.
In the exhibition, the artworks are arranged contrapuntally to highlight their differences and connections.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alternately - in turns, one after another
  • synonym: simultaneously - at the same time
