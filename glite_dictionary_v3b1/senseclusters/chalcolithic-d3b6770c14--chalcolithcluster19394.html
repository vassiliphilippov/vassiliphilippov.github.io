Chalcolithic

adjective
early copper age
9.9
Pronunciation: /kæl.kə.ˈlɪθ.ɪk/ /; / /kæl.koʊ.ˈlɪθ.ɪk/ (AmE), /kæl.kə.ˈlɪθ.ɪk/ /; / /kæl.kəʊ.ˈlɪθ.ɪk/ (BrE)
relating to the period when both stone and copper tools were used before the Bronze Age

Examples

Chalcolithic period
The Chalcolithic period marked a major technological transition in human history.
Archaeologists found Chalcolithic tools made from both stone and copper at the site.
Many Chalcolithic settlements were located near sources of copper ore.
