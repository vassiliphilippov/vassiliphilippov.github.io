to make someone legally or morally bound or compelled to do something

Examples

To obligate a person
By signing the agreement, you obligate yourself to follow its terms.
Parents are legally obligated to provide for their children.
The contract will obligate the company to complete the work within six months.
Parents are legally obligated to care for their children.
The contract obligates the company to pay compensation in case of delays.
You should never obligate people to help if they are unwilling.

Synonyms

  • oblige
    4.5 7.37/m
    oblige makes someone act because of duty, law, or circumstance, while obligate stresses a formal legal or moral binding.
  • compel
    4.8 18/m
    compel forces or pressures someone to do something, while obligate creates a legal or moral duty.
  • bind
    4.4 15/m
    bind makes someone legally or morally required because of an agreement, contract, or promise, while obligate refers to being compelled more broadly.
