superpose

verb
to place or lay over another, exactly aligning
8.5 0.18/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåsuÀêp…ôrÀàpo äz / (AmE), / ÀåsuÀêp…ôÀàp…ô äz / (BrE)
to place or lay one thing over another, often so that both are visible or aligned

Examples

To superpose images
By carefully superposing these transparent images, subtle differences become immediately visible.
In geometry class, we were asked to superpose the two triangles to check if they were congruent.
The technician will superpose the blueprints to identify any discrepancies between the drafts.
In physics, wave patterns can superpose to produce interference.
The architect decided to superpose a modern extension over the historical building.
When you superpose two transparent maps, you can see how the cities align.
