fortepiano

noun
early piano instrument
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˌfɔːrtəˈpjɑːnoʊ / (AmE), / ˌfɔːtɪˈpjɑːnəʊ / (BrE)
an early type of piano, popular from the 18th to early 19th centuries, with a lighter sound than the modern piano

Examples

A fortepiano instrument
The composer preferred the fortepiano for its delicate and expressive sound.
A beautifully restored fortepiano was displayed in the museum's music section.
She learned to play classical pieces on a replica fortepiano from the 18th century.

Related Terms

  • antonym: harpsichord - a keyboard instrument, predecessor to the piano
  • degree higher: piano - a modern keyboard instrument with a wide dynamic range
  • synonym: pianoforte - another term for the early piano
