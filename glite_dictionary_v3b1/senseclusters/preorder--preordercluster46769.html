preorder

noun
reflexive, transitive relation
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːˌɔrdər / (AmE), / ˈpriːˌɔːdə / (BrE)
a binary relation on a set that is reflexive and transitive, also known as a quasiorder

Examples

Mathematical preorder
In mathematics, a preorder is a relation that is both reflexive and transitive.
The concept of a preorder is fundamental in order theory and computer science.
A preorder does not require antisymmetry, unlike a partial order.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: partial order - a binary relation that is reflexive, antisymmetric, and transitive
