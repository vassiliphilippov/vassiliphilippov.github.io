entrainment

noun
synchronization of rhythms
8.5
Pronunciation: /ən.ˈtreɪn.mənt/ /; / /ɪn.ˈtreɪn.mənt/ /; / /en.ˈtreɪn.mənt/ (AmE), /ən.ˈtreɪn.mənt/ /; / /ɪn.ˈtreɪn.mənt/ /; / /en.ˈtreɪn.mənt/ (BrE)
the process by which one thing synchronizes its rhythm or timing with another

Examples

Musical entrainment
The entrainment of the dancers' movements to the music was impressive.
Circadian rhythms in humans rely on entrainment to the natural light-dark cycle.
Scientists study entrainment in animals to understand how they adapt to seasonal changes.

Related Terms
