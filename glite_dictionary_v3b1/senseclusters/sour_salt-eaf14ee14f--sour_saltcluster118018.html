sour salt

noun
citric acid seasoning
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈsaʊɚ sælt / (AmE), / ˈsaʊə sɒlt / (BrE)
crystals of citric acid used as a seasoning to add a sour flavor to food

Examples

Sour salt crystals
In Eastern European cooking, sour salt is often added to borscht or preserves.
The recipe called for a pinch of sour salt to give the soup a tangy flavor.
You can use sour salt instead of lemon juice to provide acidity in baked goods.
