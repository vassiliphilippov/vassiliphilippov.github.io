menat

noun
Egyptian ceremonial necklace
Pronunciation: / ˈmiːnæt / (AmE), / ˈmiːnæt / (BrE)
an ancient Egyptian necklace or amulet used in religious ceremonies, often associated with the goddess Hathor

Examples

An ancient menat necklace
The ancient priestess wore a menat during the temple ceremony to honor the goddess.
Archaeologists discovered a beautifully decorated menat in the tomb of a high-ranking official.
A replica of a menat was featured in the museum's exhibition on Egyptian religion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amulet - an object worn for protection or luck
  • synonym: talisman - an object believed to have magical powers
