hydraulic motor

noun
device converting hydraulic power to rotation
3.9
Pronunciation: / haɪˈdrɑːlɪk ˈmoʊtər / (AmE), / haɪˈdrɒlɪk ˈməʊtə / (BrE)
A mechanical device that converts hydraulic pressure and flow into torque and rotational motion.

Examples

A hydraulic motor device
A sudden drop in pressure can cause the hydraulic motor to lose efficiency and slow down.
Modern excavators use a hydraulic motor to power the rotation of their attachments.
The engineer replaced the faulty hydraulic motor to restore movement to the lift system.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hydraulic pump - a device that converts mechanical energy into hydraulic energy
