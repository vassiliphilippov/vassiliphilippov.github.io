overextension

noun
excessive broadening
7.2
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ɪk.ˈsten.ʃən/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ɪk.ˈsten.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act of applying a word, concept, or rule too broadly beyond its correct or intended use

Examples

Word overextension
Children often show overextension when they use the word 'dog' for all four-legged animals.
Linguists study overextension to understand how language develops in early childhood.
The overextension of a rule can lead to misunderstandings in communication.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overgeneralization - the process of applying a rule or concept too widely
  • antonym: restriction - the act of limiting or narrowing the use of something
