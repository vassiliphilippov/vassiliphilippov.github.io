elliptic

adjective
using few words; deliberately obscure
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɪptɪk / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪptɪk / (BrE)
(of speech or writing) characterized by the use of few words, often so as to be difficult to understand because of omission of elements or explicit details.

Examples

Elliptic remarks
His elliptic comments often puzzled his colleagues, who wished he would be more straightforward.
The author's elliptic prose appealed to readers who enjoyed piecing together subtle hints.
The critic described the novel's style as elliptic, leaving much to the reader's imagination.

Related Terms

  • synonym: concise - using few words; brief and to the point
  • synonym: cryptic - having a meaning that is mysterious or obscure
  • antonym: explicit - stating something clearly and in detail, leaving no room for confusion
