understaff

verb
to have too few staff
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈstæf / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈstɑːf / (BrE)
to provide with fewer workers than are needed for effective operation

Examples

To understaff a restaurant
If you understaff the restaurant, service will be slow.
Hospitals that are understaffed may struggle to provide adequate care to patients.
The company decided not to understaff during the busy holiday season.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overstaff - to provide with more workers than are needed
  • synonym: underemploy - to employ less than the full number of workers needed or to use workers in jobs that do not use their skills
