rhodanthe

noun
Australian annual with pink, nodding flowers
9.9
Pronunciation: / roʊˈdænθi / (AmE), / rəʊˈdænθi / (BrE)
An Australian annual plant of the daisy family, known for its light pink, nodding, everlasting flower heads; sometimes placed in the genus Helipterum.

Examples

The rhodanthe flower
Rhodanthe is prized by florists for its long-lasting color even after picking.
She dried the rhodanthe flowers to use in a decorative bouquet.
The garden was filled with blooming rhodanthe during the spring festival.
