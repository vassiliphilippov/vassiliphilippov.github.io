dewater

verb
to remove water
Pronunciation: / diˈwɔtər / (AmE), / diˈwɔːtə / (BrE)
to remove water from something, especially by draining, pumping, or as part of a process

Examples

To dewater a basement
Engineers worked to dewater the flooded basement after the storm.
The city uses pumps to dewater construction sites before building begins.
After the river receded, the fields slowly began to dewater and dry out.
The construction crew must dewater the site before they can pour the concrete foundation.
Engineers are dewatering the flooded basement using industrial pumps.
After heavy rain, it took several hours to dewater the excavation area.
Engineers worked overnight to dewater the tunnel after the heavy rainfall.
The treatment plant uses special equipment to dewater the sludge before disposal.
They had to dewater the pond to repair the damaged lining.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drain - to remove liquid from something
  • antonym: flood - to cover or fill with water
  • antonym: hydrate - to add water to something
