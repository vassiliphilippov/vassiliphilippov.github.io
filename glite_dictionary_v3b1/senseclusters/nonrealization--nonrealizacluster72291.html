nonrealization

noun
omission in speech
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˌriːələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌrɪəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the omission or failure to produce an expected sound, phoneme, or grammatical form in speech, especially in linguistics

Examples

Nonrealization of sounds
In some English dialects, the nonrealization of final 'r' is a common feature.
Linguists study nonrealization to understand how pronunciation varies across regions.
The nonrealization of certain grammatical endings can affect the meaning of spoken sentences.

Related Terms

  • synonym: elision - the omission of a sound or syllable when speaking
  • antonym: realization - the actual production of a sound or form in speech
