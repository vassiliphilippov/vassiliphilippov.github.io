counterstatement

noun
formal written reply
7.3
Pronunciation: /ˈkaʊn.tər.steɪt.mənt/ (AmE), /ˈkaʊn.tə.steɪt.mənt/ (BrE)
a statement made to oppose, refute, or respond to another statement (often in a formal or legal context)

Examples

A counterstatement in court
The lawyer submitted a counterstatement to address the accusations made in the original complaint.
Each party was required to file a counterstatement before the hearing could proceed.
Her counterstatement provided evidence that challenged the claims in the plaintiff's document.
The spokesperson released a counterstatement to address the allegations made in the press.
After the initial announcement, the company issued a counterstatement clarifying its position.
During the debate, each side prepared a counterstatement to challenge the other's arguments.

  • synonym: rebuttal - a statement that refutes or contradicts another statement
  • antonym: statement - a formal account or declaration
