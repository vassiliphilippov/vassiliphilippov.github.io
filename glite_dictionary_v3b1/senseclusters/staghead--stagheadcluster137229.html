staghead

noun
abnormal tufted tree growth
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈstæɡˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈstæɡˌhɛd / (BrE)
an abnormal tufted growth of small branches on a tree or shrub, typically caused by fungi, insects, or physiological disturbance

Examples

A staghead growth
A staghead can severely weaken the upper branches of a tree and reduce its lifespan.
Foresters monitor trees for signs of staghead as an indicator of environmental stress.
The ancient oak developed a pronounced staghead due to repeated insect attacks.
