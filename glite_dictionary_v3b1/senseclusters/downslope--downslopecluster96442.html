downslope

noun
downward slope or incline
5.7 3.1
Pronunciation: /ˈdaʊn.sloʊp/ (AmE), /ˈdaʊn.sləʊp/ (BrE)
A downward slope or incline; the part of a hill, surface, or terrain that drops to a lower level.

Examples

A downslope hill
Heavy rainfall turned the downslope into a muddy stream.
The avalanche started on the steep downslope of the mountain.
The hikers carefully made their way down the downslope after reaching the summit.
