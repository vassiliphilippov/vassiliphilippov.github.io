permanganic acid

noun
unstable acid; source of permanganates
9.9
Pronunciation: /pər.mæŋ.ˈgæn.ɪk ˈæs.əd/ (AmE), /pə.mæŋ.ˈgæn.ɪk ˈæs.ɪd/ (BrE)
an unstable, deep purple acid (HMnO₄) known only in solution, from which permanganate salts are derived

Examples

Strong permanganic acid
Because permanganic acid is highly unstable, it cannot be isolated in pure form.
Permanganate salts are typically formed by neutralizing permanganic acid with a base.
