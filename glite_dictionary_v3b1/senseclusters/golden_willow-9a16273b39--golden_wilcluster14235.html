golden willow

noun
European willow with yellow-orange twigs
Pronunciation: / ˈɡoʊldən ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldən ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
A type of European willow with greyish leaves and yellow-orange twigs, often used in basketry.

Examples

A golden willow tree
A row of golden willow trees bordered the river, creating a striking scene in autumn.
Gardeners often select the golden willow for ornamental planting due to its vibrant color.
The golden willow is prized for its beautiful yellow twigs used in weaving baskets.
