briefly

adverb
for a short time
Pronunciation: / ˈbɹif.li /; / ˈbriːfli / (AmE), / ˈbriːf.li /; / ˈbriːfli / (BrE)
for a short time or in a concise way; not taking long or using many words

Examples

To briefly stop
He paused briefly to catch his breath before continuing up the stairs.
She worked briefly as a journalist before changing careers.
The power outage briefly interrupted the meeting, but everyone soon returned to work.
Let me briefly explain the situation before we move on to the next topic.
She paused only briefly before continuing her explanation.
The president spoke briefly about the new policy changes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: concisely - in a way that is clear and succinct; using few words
  • antonym: lengthily - in a very long or detailed manner; using many words
  • antonym: permanently - in a way that lasts forever or for a very long time
  • synonym: temporarily - for a limited period of time
