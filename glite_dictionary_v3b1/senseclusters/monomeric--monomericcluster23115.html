monomeric

adjective
single-molecule form
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑnəˈmɛrɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈmɛrɪk / (BrE)
consisting of single molecules that are not joined to others in a polymer

Examples

A monomeric molecule
The enzyme remains monomeric at low concentrations, preventing it from forming larger complexes.
Researchers studied the monomeric structure of the protein to understand its function.
Under certain conditions, the molecule is no longer monomeric and begins to aggregate.

Related Terms

  • antonym: polymeric - consisting of many repeated molecular units
