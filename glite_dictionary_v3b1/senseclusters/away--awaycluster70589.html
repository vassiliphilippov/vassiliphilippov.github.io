away

adverb
kept in reserve
1.0
Pronunciation: / əˈweɪ / (AmE), / əˈweɪ / (BrE)
to put something in a suitable place for storage or safekeeping; kept in reserve

Examples

Put away food
He keeps a little food away just in case unexpected guests arrive.
She always has some money away for emergencies.
They put supplies away in case of a long winter.
After reading the documents, please put them away in the file cabinet.
She packed the dishes away carefully after dinner.
The children always put their toys away before bedtime.

  • antonym: out - from inside to outside, or in a position not inside
