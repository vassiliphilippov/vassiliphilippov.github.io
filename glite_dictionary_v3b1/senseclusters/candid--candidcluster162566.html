candid

noun
unposed photograph
Pronunciation: / ˈkændɪd / (AmE), / ˈkændɪd / (BrE)
a photograph taken without the subject's knowledge, capturing them in a natural and unposed moment

Examples

A candid at a party
She prefers taking candids at events rather than posed pictures.
The photographer captured several candids of the children playing in the park.
I love looking through old candids because they show real emotions and moments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: portrait - a photograph of a person, usually posed
  • synonym: snapshot - a quick, informal photograph
