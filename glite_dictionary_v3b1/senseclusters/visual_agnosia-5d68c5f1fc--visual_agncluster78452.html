visual agnosia

noun
inability to visually recognize objects
9.9 7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈvɪ.ʒ.wəl æɡ.ˈnoʊ.zə/ /; / /ˈvɪ.ʒu.əl æɡ.ˈnoʊ.ʒə/ (AmE), /ˈvɪz.juː.əl æɡ.ˈnəʊ.zə/ /; / /ˈvɪ.ʒuː.əl æɡ.ˈnəʊ.zə/ (BrE)
a condition in which a person is unable to recognize or interpret objects in the visual field despite having normal vision

Examples

Visual agnosia condition
After her stroke, she was diagnosed with visual agnosia and could see but not identify faces or items.
Researchers are studying visual agnosia to better understand how the brain processes visual information.
The patient’s visual agnosia made it difficult for him to recognize everyday objects.
