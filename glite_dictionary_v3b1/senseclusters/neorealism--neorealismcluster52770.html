neorealism

noun
international relations theory
Pronunciation: / ˌnioʊˈriːəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌniːəʊˈrɪəˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
a theory in international relations that explains state behavior mainly by the structure and distribution of power in the international system

Examples

Neorealism in politics
Many scholars debate whether neorealism or liberalism better explains international conflicts.
The professor introduced neorealism as a key framework for understanding global power dynamics.
Critics argue that neorealism overlooks the influence of international organizations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: liberalism - a theory in international relations emphasizing cooperation and institutions
  • synonym: realism - a theory in international relations focusing on power and national interest
