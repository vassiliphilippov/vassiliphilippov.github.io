home economics

noun
household management study
3.1 0.54/m
Pronunciation: / ho äm Àå…õk…ôÀàn…ëm…™ks / (AmE), / h…ô äm ÀåiÀêk…ôÀàn…ím…™ks / (BrE)
the study or theory and practice of managing household and family affairs, including cooking, sewing, budgeting, and child care

Examples

Home economics class
In high school, everyone was required to take at least one home economics class.
She decided to major in home economics because she was interested in nutrition and family management.
The skills learned in home economics are useful for daily life, from cooking to budgeting.

Synonyms

  • domestic science
    4.8 0.04/m
    domestic science is taught as a school subject about cooking and household management, while home economics covers study and practical family care.
  • homemaking
    2.5 0.32/m
    homemaking means managing and organizing daily household life, while home economics includes that work plus formal study and budgeting.
  • household arts
    3.7 0.00/m
    household arts focus on hands-on skills like cooking, sewing, and cleaning, while home economics also teaches theory and family budgeting.
  • housecraft
    7.4 0.00/m
    housecraft means practical skill in home tasks and chores, while home economics adds classroom study and wider family management.
