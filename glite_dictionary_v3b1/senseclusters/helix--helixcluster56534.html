helix

noun
spiral-shaped curve
6.0 0.94/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàhiÀêl…™ks / (AmE), / ÀàhiÀêl…™ks / (BrE)
a spiral or coiled shape, like a curve winding around a cylinder or cone (as in DNA or a spring)

Examples

Double helix structure
DNA is famous for its double helix structure.
He traced the helix drawn on the paper with his finger.
The staircase was designed in the shape of a helix winding upward.
The DNA molecule is structured as a double helix with two strands winding around each other.
The craftsman bent the wire into a helix to form a decorative spring.
We admired the elegant helix of the spiral staircase in the old mansion.

Synonyms

  • coil
    3.7 2.82/m
