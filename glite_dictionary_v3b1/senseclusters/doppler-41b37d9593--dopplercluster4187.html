Doppler

proper noun
wave frequency effect
6.9 0.99/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆdÉ‘plÉš / (AmE), / ËˆdÉ’plÉ™ / (BrE)
the name given to the effect where the frequency or wavelength of a wave changes due to the movement of the source or observer

Examples

Doppler effect
The Doppler principle is used in weather radar to track storms.
Scientists refer to the Doppler phenomenon when explaining changes in sound as objects move.
Medical imaging often uses Doppler technology to measure blood flow.

Synonyms

  • Doppler effect
    6.7 0.10/m
    Doppler effect names the physical phenomenon of changing wave frequency with motion, while Doppler is a shorter, informal name for it.
  • Doppler shift
    6.5 0.30/m
    Doppler shift refers to the actual change in wave frequency or wavelength, while Doppler names the general effect causing that change.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Doppler effect - the change in frequency or wavelength of a wave due to relative motion
