sweet gum

noun
wood from the sweet gum tree
Pronunciation: / swit ɡʌm / (AmE), / swiːt ɡʌm / (BrE)
The reddish-brown wood obtained from the heartwood of the sweet gum tree, used in making furniture and interior finishes.

Examples

Sweet gum wood
Tables made from sweet gum are prized for their attractive reddish-brown color.
The carpenter selected sweet gum for the elegant cabinetry because of its appealing grain.
They ordered several planks of sweet gum to restore the antique furniture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sweet gum tree - a deciduous tree (Liquidambar styraciflua) native to North America, valued for its wood and resin
