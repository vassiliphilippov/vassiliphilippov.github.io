foremilk

noun
milk produced early in breastfeeding
5.7 6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrˌmɪlk / (AmE), / ˈfɔː.mɪlk / (BrE)
the milk produced at the beginning of a breastfeeding session or during the first days after childbirth, which is thinner and higher in lactose than later milk

Examples

The foremilk at start
Foremilk can appear thinner and more watery compared to the creamier hindmilk.
Mothers are advised to allow the baby to nurse long enough to receive both foremilk and hindmilk.
The nurse explained that foremilk is rich in lactose and helps quench the baby's thirst.

Related Terms

  • synonym: colostrum - the first form of milk produced immediately following delivery, rich in antibodies
