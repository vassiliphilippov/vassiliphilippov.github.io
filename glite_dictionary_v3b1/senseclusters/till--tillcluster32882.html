till

verb
prepare land for crops
5.3 1.21/m
Pronunciation: / tÉªl / (AmE), / tÉªl / (BrE)
to prepare land for crops by ploughing, harrowing, and fertilizing it

Examples

To till the land
After the flood, the community worked together to till their damaged soil.
Early settlers had to till the land by hand before they could plant crops.
The farmer uses modern machinery to till the fields every spring.

Synonyms

  • cultivate
    4.0 6.42/m
    cultivate is to prepare and work land and care for plants over time, while till focuses on early soil preparation like ploughing and fertilizing.
  • plow
    1.5 3.42/m
    plow means to turn and break the soil with a plow, while till means to prepare the land more broadly for crops.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cultivate - to prepare and use land for growing crops
