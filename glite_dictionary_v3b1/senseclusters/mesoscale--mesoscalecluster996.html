mesoscale

adjective
intermediate in size
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛsoʊˌskeɪl / (AmE), / ˈmiːsəʊˌskeɪl / (BrE)
describing phenomena of intermediate size, especially in meteorology, between small-scale and large-scale events

Examples

Mesoscale weather events
Meteorologists study mesoscale weather systems to better predict thunderstorms and tornadoes.
A mesoscale convective complex can bring heavy rain to a specific region.
The mesoscale features of the storm made forecasting its path more challenging.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: macroscale - describing very large-scale phenomena
  • degree lower: microscale - describing very small-scale phenomena
