downsizing

noun
reducing home size or possessions (lifestyle)
3.9
Pronunciation: /ˈdaʊn.saɪ.zɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈdaʊn.saɪ.zɪŋ/ (BrE)
the decision by an individual or household to move to a smaller home or reduce possessions, usually to lower costs or simplify life.

Examples

Home downsizing
After retirement they embraced downsizing, moving to a smaller home to simplify life.
Many young professionals choose downsizing, keeping fewer possessions to reduce expenses in big cities.
The couple considered downsizing when their children left, selling furniture and finding a cozier apartment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decluttering - the act of removing unnecessary items from a space to create a neater, simpler environment
  • synonym: minimalism - a lifestyle that emphasizes owning fewer possessions and simplifying life
  • antonym: upsizing - increasing the size of living space or possessions; opposite of downsizing in a household context
