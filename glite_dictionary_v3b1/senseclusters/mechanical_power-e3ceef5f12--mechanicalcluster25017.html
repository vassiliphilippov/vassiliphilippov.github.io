mechanical power

noun
rate at which mechanical work is performed
Pronunciation: / məˈkænɪkəl ˈpaʊər / (AmE), / mɪˈkænɪkəl ˈpaʊə / (BrE)
the rate at which work is performed by a mechanical system; the time derivative of work, typically measured in watts.

Examples

The mechanical power output
He calculated the mechanical power required to lift the load in ten seconds.
Increasing mechanical power can improve the efficiency of industrial machines.
The engine's mechanical power is transmitted to the wheels via the drivetrain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: power - rate at which work is done or energy is transferred
