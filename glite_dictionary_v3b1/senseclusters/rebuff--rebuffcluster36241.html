rebuff

verb
to drive back or repel
Pronunciation: / rɪˈbʌf / (AmE), / rɪˈbʌf / (BrE)
to force or drive back, especially an attack or attacker; to repel

Examples

To rebuff an attack
Despite the initial charge, the castle guards successfully rebuffed the enemy forces.
The city defenders managed to rebuff the advancing army at the gate.
The proposal was rebuffed by community leaders who opposed the project.

Related Terms

  • synonym: repel - to force (something or someone) back or away; to drive off
  • synonym: repulse - to drive back or reject, especially an attack or attacker
