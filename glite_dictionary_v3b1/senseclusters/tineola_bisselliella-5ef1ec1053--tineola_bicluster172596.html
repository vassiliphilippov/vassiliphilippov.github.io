Tineola bisselliella

noun
a moth species known as the common clothes moth
Pronunciation: / taɪˈnoʊlə bɪˌsɛliˈɛlə / (AmE), / tɪˈnəʊlə bɪˌsɛliˈɛlə / (BrE)
A species of tiny moth, commonly known as the common clothes moth, whose larvae feed on and can damage fabrics such as wool and fur.

Examples

Tineola bisselliella larvae
Tineola bisselliella larvae are notorious for damaging woolen clothing stored in closets.
A sudden infestation of Tineola bisselliella led to holes in several historic garments.
The museum took precautions against Tineola bisselliella when setting up the textile exhibition.
