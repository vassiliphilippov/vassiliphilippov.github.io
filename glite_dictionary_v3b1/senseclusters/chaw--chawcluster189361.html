chaw

verb
chew without swallowing (esp. tobacco)
6.0
Pronunciation: / tʃɔ / (AmE), / tʃɔː / (BrE)
to chew something, especially tobacco or in a slow, noisy, or deliberate way

Examples

To chaw tobacco
He would often chaw a piece of tobacco while working in the fields.
The cowboy chawed on a stalk of grass as he watched the cattle.
You shouldn't chaw gum all day; it isn't good for your jaw.
He would chaw on a piece of straw while working in the field.
The old man sat on the porch, chawing tobacco and watching the sunset.
She absentmindedly chawed her pencil during the long meeting.
