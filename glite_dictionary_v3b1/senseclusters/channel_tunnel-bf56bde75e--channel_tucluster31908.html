Channel Tunnel

noun
rail tunnel beneath the English Channel
3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃæn.əl ˈtʌn.əl / (AmE), / ˈtʃæn.əl ˈtʌn.əl / (BrE)
A rail tunnel beneath the English Channel, connecting France and England.

Examples

The Channel Tunnel
The Channel Tunnel allows high-speed trains to pass under the sea between England and France.
The construction of the Channel Tunnel was a remarkable engineering achievement of the 20th century.
We traveled from London to Paris via the Channel Tunnel in just over two hours.
