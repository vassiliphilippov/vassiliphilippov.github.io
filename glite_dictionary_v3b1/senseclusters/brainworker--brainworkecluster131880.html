brainworker

noun
person who does mental work
Pronunciation: / ˈbreɪnwɝːkɚ / (AmE), / ˈbreɪnwɜːkə / (BrE)
a person whose job involves primarily mental rather than physical work

Examples

A brainworker at office
Many brainworkers prefer quiet offices to concentrate on complex tasks.
The company recruited talented brainworkers to develop innovative solutions.
Unlike a manual laborer, a brainworker solves problems using intellectual skills.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intellectual - a person who engages in critical thinking and mental labor
  • antonym: manual worker - a person whose job involves physical labor rather than mental work
