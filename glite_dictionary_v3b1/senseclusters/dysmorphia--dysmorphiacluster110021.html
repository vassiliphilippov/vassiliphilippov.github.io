dysmorphia

noun
distorted body image
9.9
Pronunciation: /dɪs.ˈmɑːr.fiː.ə/ /; / /dɪs.ˈmɔːr.fiː.ə/ (AmE), /dɪs.ˈmɔː.fɪ.ə/ /; / /dɪs.ˈmɔː.fiː.ə/ (BrE)
a mental condition in which a person has a distorted or unrealistic perception of their body or appearance

Examples

Body dysmorphia
People with dysmorphia may spend hours worrying about minor or imagined flaws.
The therapist specializes in treating patients who struggle with dysmorphia and self-image issues.
Social media can sometimes worsen feelings of dysmorphia among teenagers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: body dysmorphic disorder - a mental disorder involving obsessive focus on perceived flaws in appearance
  • antonym: euphoria - a feeling or state of intense happiness and well-being
