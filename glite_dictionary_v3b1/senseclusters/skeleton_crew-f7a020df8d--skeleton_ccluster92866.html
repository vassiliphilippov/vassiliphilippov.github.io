skeleton crew

noun
minimum staff needed to operate
Pronunciation: / ˈskɛlətən kru / (AmE), / ˈskɛlɪtən kruː / (BrE)
The minimum number of people needed to keep a business, service, or operation functioning.

Examples

A skeleton crew
Due to budget cuts, only a skeleton crew was left to manage the late shift.
The hospital operated on a skeleton crew during the holiday weekend.
When the power went out, a skeleton crew stayed behind to monitor the systems.

Related Terms

  • antonym: full staff - all employees present for regular operations
