northern snakehead

noun
an air-breathing, invasive Asian freshwater fish
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈnɔrðərn ˈsneɪkhɛd / (AmE), / ˈnɔːðən ˈsneɪkhɛd / (BrE)
a voracious freshwater fish native to East Asia, known for its ability to breathe air and survive out of water, considered invasive in North America

Examples

The northern snakehead fish
Because the northern snakehead can survive out of water, it's difficult to control its population.
Fishermen are concerned about the spread of the northern snakehead in local rivers.
The northern snakehead is considered a major threat to native fish species in North America.
