countertrend

noun
opposing movement
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntərˌtrɛnd / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌtrɛnd / (BrE)
a movement or tendency that goes in the opposite direction to the main trend, especially in financial markets

Examples

A countertrend movement
Investors watched as a sudden countertrend disrupted the steady rise in stock prices.
Analysts warned that the current countertrend might only be temporary before the main trend resumes.
During volatile periods, a countertrend can confuse traders about the market's true direction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reversal - a change to an opposite direction, position, or course of action
  • antonym: trend - the general direction in which something is developing or changing
