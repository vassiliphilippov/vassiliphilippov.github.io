apocatastasis

noun
restoration of all things
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌæpəˌkætəˈsteɪsɪs / (AmE), / ˌapəˌkatəˈsteɪsɪs / (BrE)
the belief that all things or souls will eventually be restored to a perfect or original state

Examples

Apocatastasis of all souls
The concept of apocatastasis suggests that even the wicked will eventually be reconciled with the divine.
Some early church fathers supported apocatastasis as a hopeful doctrine for humanity.
Debates about apocatastasis have influenced theological discussions for centuries.

Related Terms

  • antonym: damnation - the state of being condemned to eternal punishment
  • synonym: universalism - the belief that all souls will ultimately be saved
