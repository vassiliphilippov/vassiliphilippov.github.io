regular

adjective
symmetrically arranged, uniform in form
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛɡjələr / (AmE), / ˈrɛɡjʊlə /; / ˈrɛɡjələ / (BrE)
having parts or features that are symmetrical, evenly spaced, or conforming to a standard or expected arrangement

Examples

Regular shape
A hexagon is an example of a regular polygon with sides of equal length and angles.
People often admire faces with regular symmetry and balanced proportions.
The artist's sculpture had regular features that gave it a harmonious appearance.
All travelers must go through the regular security checks before boarding their flights.
This restaurant keeps regular business hours, opening at 9 a.m. every day.
You should use a regular detergent instead of a specialized one for this type of fabric.
A cube is a regular solid because all its sides are equal and it fits standard volume formulas.
Scientists often use regular objects, like cylinders and spheres, to demonstrate how to find volume.
The volume of a regular pyramid can be calculated precisely using its base and height.

Related Terms

  • antonym: irregular - not having symmetry or uniform spacing
  • synonym: normal - usual or typical; conforming to a standard
