withering

adjective
scornful; intended to humiliate
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪðərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪðərɪŋ / (BrE)
intended to make someone feel humiliated, small, or foolish; scornful or contemptuous.

Examples

Withering look
His withering remarks silenced the entire room.
She gave him a withering look that made him regret his comment.
The teacher's withering criticism made the students rethink their approach.

Related Terms

  • antonym: complimentary - expressing admiration or praise
  • synonym: scathing - sharply critical and hurtful
