gallium

noun
a rare metallic chemical element (Ga)
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæliəm / (AmE), / ˈɡalɪəm / (BrE)
a rare, soft, silvery metallic element that is brittle at low temperatures but liquid just above room temperature; found in trace amounts in bauxite and zinc ores, with symbol Ga and atomic number 31.

Examples

Gallium metal
Gallium melts in your hand because it becomes liquid just above room temperature.
Researchers used gallium to create new types of semiconductors.
Trace amounts of gallium are found in bauxite and zinc ores.
