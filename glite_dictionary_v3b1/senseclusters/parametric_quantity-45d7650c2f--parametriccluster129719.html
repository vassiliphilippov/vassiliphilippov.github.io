parametric quantity

noun
adjustable constant in a curve's equation
Pronunciation: / ˌpær.əˈmɛtrɪk ˈkwɑn.t̬ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌpar.əˈmɛtrɪk ˈkwɒn.tɪ.ti / (BrE)
A constant in the equation of a curve that can be varied to produce a family of related curves.

Examples

The parametric quantity changes
Changing the parametric quantity in the function shifts the entire family of curves.
Each ellipse can be described by a different parametric quantity in its equation.
The solution depends on adjusting a parametric quantity to fit the observed data.
