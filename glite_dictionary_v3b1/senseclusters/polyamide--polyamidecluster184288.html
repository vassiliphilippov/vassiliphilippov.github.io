polyamide

noun
a polymer with repeating amide groups
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːliˈæmaɪd / (AmE), / ˌpɒliˈæmaɪd / (BrE)
a polymer made up of repeating amide groups, used especially to make synthetic fibers and plastics

Examples

Synthetic polyamide fibers
Many sportswear fabrics are made from synthetic polyamide fibers.
Nylon is a well-known polyamide used in the textile industry.
The strength and durability of polyamide materials make them suitable for engineering applications.
