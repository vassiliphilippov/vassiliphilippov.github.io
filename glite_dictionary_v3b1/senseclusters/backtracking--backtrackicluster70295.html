backtracking

noun
problem-solving method
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈbæktrækɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbæktrækɪŋ / (BrE)
a method of solving problems by trying different possibilities and reversing steps when a solution path fails

Examples

Backtracking algorithm
The puzzle was solved using backtracking to explore all possible moves.
Many computer algorithms rely on backtracking to efficiently search for solutions.
Without backtracking, the program would get stuck when it reaches a dead end.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brute force - a problem-solving method that tries all possible options
  • synonym: trial and error - a way of solving problems by trying different methods until one works
