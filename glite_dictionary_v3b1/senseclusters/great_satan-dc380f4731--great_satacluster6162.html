Great Satan

noun
principal enemy; evil force (idiom)
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪt ˈseɪtn̩ / (AmE), / ɡreɪt ˈseɪtn̩ / (BrE)
A derogatory term used to refer to a country, organization, or individual perceived as a principal enemy or evil force, especially in political or ideological contexts.

Examples

The Great Satan enemy
In political cartoons, the corporation is often depicted as the Great Satan.
Many activists denounced the foreign power as the Great Satan during the protest.
The leader's speech referred to Western influences as the Great Satan.
