microwave bomb

noun
bomb that disables electronics with electromagnetic energy
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪkˌroʊv bɑm / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊv bɒm / (BrE)
a weapon that detonates in midair, releasing a powerful burst of electromagnetic energy intended to disable electronic devices without causing physical harm to people or structures

Examples

A powerful microwave bomb
A microwave bomb can incapacitate electronic equipment without causing casualties or destroying infrastructure.
Experts warn that a microwave bomb could bring down entire power grids in seconds.
Military researchers are developing a microwave bomb to disable enemy communication systems.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conventional bomb - bomb that causes destruction through explosion and blast
  • synonym: electromagnetic pulse bomb - bomb that uses an electromagnetic pulse to disrupt electronics
