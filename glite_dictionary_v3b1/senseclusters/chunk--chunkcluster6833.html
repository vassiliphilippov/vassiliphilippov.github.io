chunk

noun
thick or solid piece or lump
Pronunciation: / tʃʌŋk / (AmE), / tʃʌŋk / (BrE)
a thick piece or a large amount of something, either physical or abstract

Examples

A chunk of bread
A large chunk of bread was enough to satisfy his hunger.
He broke off a chunk of chocolate and handed it to me.
She dropped a chunk of ice into her glass.
A significant chunk of the population supports the policy.
He inherited quite a chunk of land from his grandfather.
She invested a chunk of her savings in the new company.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: bit - a small amount or piece of something
  • synonym: lump - a piece or mass of solid material without a definite shape
  • synonym: portion - a part or share of something
  • antonym: sliver - a thin, small, narrow piece cut or split off
