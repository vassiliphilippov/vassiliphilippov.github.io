mouther

noun
person who mouths words
Pronunciation: / ˈmaʊðər / (AmE), / ˈmaʊðə / (BrE)
a person who mouths words or speaks in an exaggerated or affected way

Examples

A mouther of words
The actor was a skilled mouther of dramatic speeches on stage.
Some critics called the politician a mere mouther of empty promises.
During the rehearsal, she was accused of being a mouther rather than truly feeling the lines.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mumbler - a person who speaks unclearly or indistinctly
  • synonym: orator - a person who gives speeches, especially in a formal or dramatic way
