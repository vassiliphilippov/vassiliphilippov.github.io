Pseudomonas

noun
a genus of bacteria, some pathogenic
9.2
Pronunciation: / suːdəˈmoʊnəs / (AmE), / sjuːdəˈməʊnəs / (BrE)
a genus of rod-shaped, Gram-negative bacteria, some species of which can cause disease in plants and animals

Examples

Pseudomonas aeruginosa bacteria
Pseudomonas aeruginosa is commonly associated with hospital-acquired infections.
Researchers are studying how Pseudomonas bacteria develop resistance to antibiotics.
Some species of Pseudomonas are known to promote plant growth and suppress soil diseases.
