intentionalism

noun
meaning by creator's intent
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɛnʃənəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɛnʃənəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief that the meaning of a work is determined by the creator's intention

Examples

Intentionalism in art
In art criticism, intentionalism argues that the artist's purpose is key to understanding a painting.
Some scholars reject intentionalism and focus instead on how readers interpret a text.
The debate between intentionalism and other theories is central in literary studies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anti-intentionalism - the belief that meaning is independent of the creator's intention
  • synonym: authorial intent - the creator's intended meaning in a work
