overtaxation

noun
excessive taxation
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərtækˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvətækˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of imposing taxes that are considered excessively high or burdensome

Examples

Overtaxation of income
The government faced criticism for its policy of overtaxation on small businesses.
Many economists warn that overtaxation can slow economic growth and reduce investment.
Citizens protested against what they saw as overtaxation by the local authorities.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: taxation - the process of collecting taxes from individuals or businesses
  • antonym: undertaxation - the act of imposing taxes that are too low
