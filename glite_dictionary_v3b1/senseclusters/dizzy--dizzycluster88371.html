dizzy

verb
to make feel unsteady or confused
2.8 0.8/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆdÉªzi / (AmE), / ËˆdÉªzi / (BrE)
to make someone feel unsteady or confused, as if they might lose their balance

Examples

To dizzy someone
He found the sheer amount of information dizzying at first.
The constant spinning of the merry-go-round dizzies most children.
The flashing lights dizzied her until she had to sit down.

Synonyms

  • disorient
    5.2 1/m
    disorient makes someone lose their sense of direction in space, while dizzy makes someone feel physically unsteady and lightheaded.
  • throw off balance
    5.4 0.3/m
    throw off balance causes a person to feel unsettled by a sudden change, while dizzy makes someone physically off-balance.
  • daze
    4.3 0.2/m
    daze leaves someone stunned and unable to think clearly after shock or a blow, while dizzy makes them feel unsteady.
  • discombobulate
    9.9 0.3/m
    discombobulate confuses and bewilders someone mentally, often in a joking way, while dizzy makes them lose balance.
  • make your head spin
    7.0 0.1/m
    make your head spin describes being overwhelmed by rapid facts or choices, while dizzy describes physical unsteadiness and vertigo.
