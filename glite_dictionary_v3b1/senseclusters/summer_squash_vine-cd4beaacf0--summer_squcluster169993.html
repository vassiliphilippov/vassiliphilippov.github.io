summer squash vine

noun
bushy plant producing edible immature squash
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌmɚ skwɑʃ vaɪn / (AmE), / ˈsʌmə skwɒʃ vaɪn / (BrE)
a plant of the squash family that typically grows in a bushy form and produces edible fruit consumed while still immature, before the rind or seeds harden

Examples

The summer squash vine
Farmers recommend picking fruit from the summer squash vine while the skin is still soft and shiny.
She planted several summer squash vines to enjoy fresh zucchini and yellow squash all summer long.
The summer squash vine sprawled across the garden, producing tender vegetables in early July.

Related Terms

  • antonym: winter squash - squash varieties harvested when fully mature with hard rind
  • synonym: zucchini - a type of summer squash with green skin
