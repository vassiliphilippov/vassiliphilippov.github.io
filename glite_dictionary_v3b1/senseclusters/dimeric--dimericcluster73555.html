dimeric

adjective
having two joined parts
8.1
Pronunciation: /daɪ.ˈmer.ɪk/ /; / /dɪ.ˈmer.ɪk/ (AmE), /daɪ.ˈmeə.rɪk/ /; / /dɪ.ˈmeə.rɪk/ (BrE)
consisting of or relating to two similar or identical parts joined together, especially in chemistry or biology

Examples

Dimeric protein structure
The enzyme is active only in its dimeric state, where two subunits are connected.
Scientists observed a dimeric structure in the newly discovered protein.
Some antibodies are dimeric, meaning they are made of two identical molecules joined together.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monomeric - consisting of a single part or unit
  • degree higher: trimeric - consisting of three similar or identical parts joined together
