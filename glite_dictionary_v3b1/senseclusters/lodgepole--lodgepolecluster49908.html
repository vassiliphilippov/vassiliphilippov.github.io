lodgepole

noun
Northwestern US pine tree species
9.9 0.45/m
Pronunciation: / Ààl…ëÀêd íÀåpo äl / (AmE), / Ààl…íd íÀåp…ô äl / (BrE)
a type of pine tree (Pinus contorta) found in the coastal northwestern United States, characterized by two needles per cluster and bark fissured into small squares; also known as lodgepole pine.

Examples

Lodgepole pine tree
Native Americans traditionally used lodgepole trunks to build their tipi frames.
The forest was dominated by lodgepole and ponderosa pines, stretching as far as the eye could see.
Wildfires play a natural role in the regeneration of lodgepole across the region.

Synonyms

  • lodgepole pine
    7.6 0.26/m
    lodgepole pine is the standard common name for the two-needled pine, while lodgepole is a shorter, informal name.
  • Pinus contorta
    8.8 0.00/m
    Pinus contorta is the scientific name used by botanists in papers, while lodgepole is the common everyday name people use.
  • shore pine
    8.4 0.01/m
    shore pine is a shrubby coastal form of this species, while lodgepole describes the taller, often inland pine.
