choke out

verb
say something with difficulty due to emotion
3.3 5.9
Pronunciation: /tʃoʊk aʊt/ /; / /tʃəʊk aʊt/ (AmE), /tʃəʊk aʊt/ (BrE)
to say something with great difficulty, especially because of emotion or nervousness

Examples

To choke out words
It took him a moment to choke out the truth to his friends.
She could barely choke out his name between sobs.
Through the tears, he managed to choke out a weak apology.
