shoestring catch

noun
baseball catch near the ground
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˈʃuˌstrɪŋ kætʃ / (AmE), / ˈʃuːstrɪŋ katʃ / (BrE)
(Baseball) a running catch made very close to the ground, typically by an outfielder.

Examples

Shoestring catch in baseball
Fans cheered when the player dove for a shoestring catch just before the ball hit the grass.
Her quick reflexes allowed her to execute a perfect shoestring catch on the run.
The center fielder made a spectacular shoestring catch to end the inning.
