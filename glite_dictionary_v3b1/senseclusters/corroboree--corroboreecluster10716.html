corroboree

noun
Aboriginal ceremony
Pronunciation: / kəˈrɑːbəri / (AmE), / kəˈrɒbəri / (BrE)
an Australian Aboriginal ceremony featuring music, dance, and storytelling, often held for social, spiritual, or educational purposes

Examples

Traditional corroboree ceremony
The elders led a corroboree to teach the children about their ancestors' traditions.
Tourists were invited to watch a corroboree performed by local Aboriginal dancers.
During the corroboree, stories of the Dreamtime were shared through song and movement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: powwow - a Native American gathering involving dance, music, and ceremony
