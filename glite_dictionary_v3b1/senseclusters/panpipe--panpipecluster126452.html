panpipe

noun
simple wind instrument of bound tubes
Pronunciation: / ˈpænˌpaɪp / (AmE), / ˈpænˌpaɪp / (BrE)
A simple wind instrument made of several tubes of different lengths bound together and played by blowing across their open ends.

Examples

A panpipe instrument
Artifacts found at the site included a handmade panpipe dating back centuries.
She admired the skill required to produce different notes on a panpipe.
The musician played a haunting melody on his panpipe during the festival.

Related Terms

  • synonym: syrinx - another name for panpipe, especially in classical context
