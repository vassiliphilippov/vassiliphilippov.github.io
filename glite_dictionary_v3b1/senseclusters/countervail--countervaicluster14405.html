countervail

verb
offset or neutralize the effect of
4.9 7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈkaʊn.tər.veɪl/ (AmE), /ˈkaʊn.tə.veɪl/ (BrE)
to act against something in order to balance, reduce, or neutralize its effect

Examples

To countervail dangers
Strict regulations can sometimes countervail the dangers posed by industrial expansion.
The government's new tax policy will countervail the effects of inflation.
The union's efforts were intended to countervail the influence of large corporations.
The effects of inflation were largely countervailed by rising wages.
The new taxes will countervail the benefits of the subsidy to some extent.
They introduced policies to countervail the potential economic risks.
The new regulations are designed to countervail the negative effects of pollution.
International aid can help countervail the impact of economic sanctions on a country.
The company introduced new products to countervail declining sales in its main market.

Related Terms

  • synonym: counteract - to act against something in order to reduce its force or neutralize it
  • synonym: neutralize - to make something ineffective by applying an opposite force
  • synonym: offset - to counterbalance or compensate for something
  • antonym: reinforce - to strengthen or support something
