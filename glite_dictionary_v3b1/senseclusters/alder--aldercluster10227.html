alder

noun
deciduous tree/shrub with cone-like fruit
Pronunciation: / ˈɔl.dɚ / (AmE), / ˈɔːl.də / (BrE)
A deciduous tree or shrub of the birch family, found in north temperate regions, with toothed leaves and small, cone-like fruit; noted for its rot-resistant wood and use in tanning and dyeing.

Examples

An alder tree
Native Americans used alder bark to create natural dyes and medicines.
Several species of alder grow along the riverbanks, providing shade and shelter for wildlife.
The wood from an alder tree is prized for its resistance to decay in wet conditions.
