impliedly

adverb
indirectly suggested
7.9
Pronunciation: /ɪm.ˈplaɪd.li/ /; / /ɪm.ˈplaɪd.ə.li/ (AmE), /ɪm.ˈplaɪd.li/ /; / /ɪm.ˈplaɪd.ə.li/ (BrE)
in a way that is suggested or understood without being stated directly

Examples

To speak impliedly
The manager impliedly approved the plan by not raising any objections during the meeting.
By leaving the door open, she impliedly invited us to enter the room.
The law impliedly protects certain rights even if they are not mentioned directly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: implicitly - in a way that is not directly expressed
