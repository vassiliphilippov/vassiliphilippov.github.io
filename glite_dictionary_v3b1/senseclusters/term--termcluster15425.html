term

noun
condition of an agreement or contract
3.3 154/m
Pronunciation: / t…ùm / (AmE), / t…úÀêm / (BrE)
(usually plural) a condition or stipulation specified as part of an agreement, contract, or negotiation.

Examples

Terms of a contract
The terms of the agreement must be read carefully before signing.
The terms outlined in the policy are binding for all parties involved.
They negotiated the terms of the contract over several meetings.

Synonyms

  • stipulation
    6.6 1.57/m
    stipulation is a specific requirement written into an agreement, while term often refers to the set of all such conditions.
  • provision
    5.6 43/m
    provision is a requirement stated in a legal document, while term usually names conditions within an agreement.
  • condition
    3.2 63/m
    condition is a requirement that must be met for something to work, while term usually means conditions in an agreement.
  • clause
    5.3 13/m
    clause is a numbered section in a contract that states a rule, while term means the actual condition or requirement.
  • proviso
    8.4 1.56/m
    proviso is a specific exception or restriction added to a clause, while term refers more broadly to agreement conditions.

  • synonym: provision - a specific condition in a formal document
