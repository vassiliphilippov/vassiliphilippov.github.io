dextran

noun
bacterial polysaccharide
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛkstræn / (AmE), / ˈdɛkstræn / (BrE)
a complex carbohydrate (polysaccharide) made of glucose units, produced by bacteria and used in medicine to increase blood volume

Examples

Dextran solution for blood
Doctors sometimes use dextran as a blood plasma substitute during surgery.
The laboratory grew bacteria to produce large amounts of dextran for research.
Industrial processes use dextran to improve the texture of certain foods.
The doctor administered dextran to help stabilize the patient's blood pressure after severe blood loss.
In emergency situations, dextran is often used to quickly restore blood volume.
Allergic reactions to dextran are rare but can occur in sensitive individuals.
The nurse added dextran to the IV solution to help treat the patient's low blood volume.
Researchers used dextran as a marker to track fluid movement in the experiment.
After surgery, the doctor prescribed dextran to support the patient's recovery.
