ensue

verb
to occur as a result or consequence
5.8 11/m
Pronunciation: / …™nÀàsu / (AmE), / …™nÀàsjuÀê / (BrE)
to occur as a result or consequence; to follow or result from something

Examples

Chaos ensued
An argument broke out, and chaos ensued in the hall.
If these rules are not followed, serious problems may ensue.
When the meeting ran late, confusion soon ensued among the participants.

Synonyms

  • result
    1.6 133/m
    result is a common verb meaning to happen because of something, while ensue is more often used for things that follow immediately.
  • eventuate
    9.1 0.22/m
    eventuate is a formal verb for something that happens in the end after development, while ensue describes things that follow quickly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: follow - to come after as a result or consequence
  • antonym: precede - to come before in time or order
