improv

verb
to perform spontaneously
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈɪmprɑv / (AmE), / ˈɪmprɒv / (BrE)
to perform or create something spontaneously, without preparation or a script, especially in acting or music

Examples

To improv a scene
When the microphone failed, the comedian had to improv his entire routine.
She loves to improv scenes with her friends during drama class.
The band decided to improv a song when the setlist was cut short.

Related Terms

  • synonym: improvise - to create or perform without preparation
  • antonym: rehearse - to practice or prepare in advance
