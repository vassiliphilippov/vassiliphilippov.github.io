decompose

verb
to decay or break down (especially by bacteria)
4.4 2/m
Pronunciation: / ÀådiÀê.k…ômÀàpo äz / (AmE), / ÀådiÀê.k…ômÀàp…ô äz / (BrE)
(of organic matter) to decay or break down into simpler substances, especially through the action of bacteria or fungi.

Examples

To decompose leaves
After several weeks in the compost bin, the food scraps began to decompose.
Heat and moisture cause dead leaves to decompose quickly in the rainforest.
Scientists studied how different bacteria help substances to decompose in the soil.

Synonyms

  • decay
    3.4 2/m
    decay is the natural breakdown of organic matter by bacteria or fungi, while decompose describes that same biological decay process.
  • rot
    1.4 6/m
    rot is the process where organic tissue is consumed by fungi or bacteria, while decompose refers to the general biological breaking down.
  • biodegrade
    4.4 0.2/m
    biodegrade means a substance is broken down by living organisms into harmless parts, while decompose focuses on organic matter decay.
  • rot away
    4.2 0.5/m
    rot away describes slow gradual destruction over time, often from neglect, while decompose stresses biological decay by microbes.
  • break down
    3.7 4/m
    break down often means separating a substance by chemical or physical processes, while decompose implies natural biological decay of organic matter.
  • molder
    7.1 0.3/m
    molder is to crumble slowly from moisture, age, or neglect, often without naming microbes, while decompose means biological breakdown by organisms.
