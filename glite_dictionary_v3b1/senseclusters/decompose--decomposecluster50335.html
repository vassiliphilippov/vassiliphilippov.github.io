decompose

verb
to decay or break down (especially by bacteria)
Pronunciation: / ˌdiː.kəmˈpoʊz / (AmE), / ˌdiː.kəmˈpəʊz / (BrE)
(of organic matter) to decay or break down into simpler substances, especially through the action of bacteria or fungi.

Examples

To decompose leaves
After several weeks in the compost bin, the food scraps began to decompose.
Heat and moisture cause dead leaves to decompose quickly in the rainforest.
Scientists studied how different bacteria help substances to decompose in the soil.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decay - to rot or undergo decomposition
  • antonym: preserve - to keep something from decaying or decomposing
  • synonym: rot - to become bad or decomposed through natural processes
