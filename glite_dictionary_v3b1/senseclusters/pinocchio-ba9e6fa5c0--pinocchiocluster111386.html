Pinocchio

proper noun
wooden boy in fiction
3.7
Pronunciation: / pɪˈnoʊkiˌoʊ / (AmE), / pɪˈnəʊkiəʊ / (BrE)
a fictional wooden boy whose nose grows longer whenever he tells a lie, from the classic children's story

Examples

Pinocchio story
The story of Pinocchio teaches children the importance of honesty.
Whenever he lied, his friends joked that he was turning into Pinocchio.
The puppet Pinocchio dreamed of becoming a real boy one day.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Geppetto - the woodcarver who created Pinocchio
  • antonym: liar - a person who does not tell the truth
