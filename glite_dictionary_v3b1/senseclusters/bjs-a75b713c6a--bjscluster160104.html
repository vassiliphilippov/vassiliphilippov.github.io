BJS

noun
U.S. crime statistics agency (Bureau of Justice Statistics)
8.5
Pronunciation: / biː dʒeɪ ɛs / (AmE), / biː dʒeɪ ɛs / (BrE)
(in the US) the Bureau of Justice Statistics, the federal agency responsible for collecting and analyzing crime and justice statistics.

Examples

BJS crime data
According to the BJS, violent crime rates have decreased over the past decade.
Researchers often rely on data published by the BJS for national crime trends.
The BJS provides annual reports that inform policy makers about the state of criminal justice in the U.S.
