macadam

noun
broken stone used for road surfaces
Pronunciation: / məˈkædəm / (AmE), / məˈkadəm /; / məˈkædəm / (BrE)
a type of road surface made from layers of compacted broken stone, or the broken stone used for this purpose

Examples

Macadam road surface
Early highways were often constructed using layers of macadam.
The rural road was covered in macadam to create a smoother driving surface.
Workers spread new macadam over the old, worn-out road to repair it.
After decades of use, the old macadam began to crumble and needed repair.
The country road was surfaced with macadam to allow for smoother travel.
They replaced the dirt paths with macadam to improve access to the village.

Related Terms

  • synonym: asphalt - a mixture of bitumen with gravel, used for road surfacing
