rearticulate

verb
to join again
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɑːrˈtɪkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːɑːˈtɪkjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to join or fit together again, especially to restore a joint between bones or mechanical parts

Examples

To rearticulate a joint
The surgeon will rearticulate the patient's shoulder after the injury.
Engineers had to rearticulate the parts of the machine to restore its function.
After the accident, doctors managed to rearticulate the broken bones successfully.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dislocate - to separate a joint or connection
  • synonym: reconnect - to join or attach together again
