cognitive operation

noun
mental process or activity
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːɡnəˌtɪv ˌɑːpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈkɒɡnətɪv ˌɒpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a mental process that alters or manipulates information, such as thinking, remembering, or judging

Examples

Basic cognitive operation
Attention is a basic cognitive operation that influences perception and memory.
Learning a new language involves several complex cognitive operations.
The experiment was designed to test the cognitive operation of problem-solving.

Related Terms

  • synonym: thought - the act of thinking or considering
