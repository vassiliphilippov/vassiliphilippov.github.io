dyer's-broom

noun
shrub used for yellow dye, ornamental plant
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪərz ˌbruːm / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəz ˌbruːm / (BrE)
a small Eurasian shrub (Genista tinctoria) with clusters of yellow flowers, used historically for producing dyes and sometimes grown as an ornamental plant

Examples

Dyer's-broom yellow flowers
Dyer's-broom spreads quickly and can easily become a common weed in some areas.
Early textiles were often colored yellow using dyer's-broom.
The garden featured a dyer's-broom among other traditional dye plants.
