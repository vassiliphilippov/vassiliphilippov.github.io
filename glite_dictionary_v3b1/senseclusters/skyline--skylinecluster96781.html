skyline

noun
outline where sky meets land or buildings
Pronunciation: / ˈskaɪˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈskaɪˌlaɪn / (BrE)
the outline or shape where the sky meets the land, buildings, or other objects, especially when seen from a distance

Examples

City skyline
From the top of the hill, we could see the entire skyline of the valley.
Photographers often visit this spot for its breathtaking view of the skyline.
The city's skyline was illuminated by the setting sun.
After the storm, the mountains stood out sharply against the evening skyline.
The New York City skyline is instantly recognizable with its skyscrapers and bridges.
The city plans to limit building heights to preserve the historic skyline.

Related Terms

  • synonym: horizon - the apparent line where the earth's surface and the sky appear to meet
