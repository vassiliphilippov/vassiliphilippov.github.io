impose

verb
to order and collect (tax, fine etc.)
4.2 31/m
Pronunciation: / ÉªmËˆpoÊŠz / (AmE), / ÉªmËˆpÉ™ÊŠz / (BrE)
to officially order that a rule, tax, fine, or punishment must be paid or obeyed, and to collect it

Examples

To impose a fine
Local authorities have the power to impose penalties on businesses that violate health regulations.
The government decided to impose a new tax on luxury goods starting next year.
The university can impose fines on students who damage library property.

Synonyms

  • levy
    5.4 1.12/m
    levy is an official action to collect taxes or charges, while impose means to order rules, fines, or penalties and make people obey.
  • decree
    5.3 3.14/m
    decree is to officially order or declare a law or decision, while impose focuses on forcing rules, taxes, or penalties to be obeyed.
  • mandate
    4.9 16/m
    mandate is to require someone or give them authority to act, while impose means to place rules, taxes, or penalties on people.
