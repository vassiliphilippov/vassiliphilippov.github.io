Samkhya

noun
Indian philosophical system
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈsæm.kjə/ /; / /ˈsʌm.kjə/ /; / /ˈsəm.kjə/ /; / /ˈsɑːm.kjə/ (AmE), /ˈsæm.kjə/ /; / /ˈsəm.kjə/ /; / /ˈsɑːm.kjə/ (BrE)
an ancient Indian philosophical system that explains the universe through enumeration and the dualism of consciousness and matter

Examples

Samkhya philosophy
The Samkhya system describes the world as a combination of consciousness and matter.
Many Indian philosophies, including Yoga, are influenced by Samkhya thought.
Students of philosophy often study Samkhya to understand ancient Indian views on reality.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Vedanta - a major school of Indian philosophy focusing on the end of the Vedas
  • synonym: dualism - the belief that reality consists of two fundamental elements
