Austro-Asiatic language

noun
language family in southern and southeastern Asia
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌɔstroʊ eɪˈʒætɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌɒstrəʊ æsɪˈatɪk ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
any of a family of languages spoken in southern and southeastern Asia, including Vietnamese, Khmer, and Mon

Examples

Austro-Asiatic language family
Khmer, the official language of Cambodia, is the most prominent Austro-Asiatic language in that country.
Researchers have traced the origins of the Austro-Asiatic language family to ancient southern China.
Vietnamese is an example of an Austro-Asiatic language widely spoken in Southeast Asia.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Austronesian language - a related but distinct language family spoken in the Pacific and parts of Southeast Asia
  • antonym: Indo-European language - another major language family including most of Europe and parts of Asia
