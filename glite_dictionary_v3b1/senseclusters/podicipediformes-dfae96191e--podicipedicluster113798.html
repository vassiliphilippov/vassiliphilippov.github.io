Podicipediformes

noun
an order of birds: the grebes
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑdɪˌsɪpɪˈdɪfɔrmiːz / (AmE), / ˌpɒdɪˌsɪpɪˈdɪfɔːmiːz / (BrE)
an order of aquatic birds comprising the grebes, known for their lobed toes and diving abilities

Examples

Podicipediformes aquatic birds
Bird watchers spotted several species within the Podicipediformes order on their trip to the wetlands.
Many field guides include the Podicipediformes alongside other waterfowl such as ducks and loons.
The Podicipediformes are renowned for their unique method of diving underwater to catch fish.
