adhere

verb
to follow a rule or plan strictly
3.3 3.8
Pronunciation: /əd.ˈhɪr/ /; / /æd.ˈhɪr/ (AmE), /əd.ˈhɪə/ /; / /æd.ˈhɪə/ (BrE)
to continue to follow, support, or be loyal to a rule, belief, or person

Examples

Adhere to the rules
Despite several setbacks, the committee continued to adhere to its original timeline.
It is important that all members adhere to the agreed-upon rules during the competition.
She promised to adhere to her training schedule no matter what happened.
He continues to adhere to his family's traditional values despite living abroad.
Many members of the organization adhere to strict ethical codes.
Some politicians adhere closely to their party's platform, while others do not.
Although many doubted him, she continued to adhere to his side throughout the investigation.
Even in difficult moments, true friends will adhere to one another.
They vowed to adhere to their principles no matter the consequences.

  • synonym: follow - to go after or accept as a guide or leader
  • antonym: oppose - to act against or refuse to accept
