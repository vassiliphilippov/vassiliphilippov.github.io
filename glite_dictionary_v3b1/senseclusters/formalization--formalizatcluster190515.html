formalization

noun
the act of making something formal
Pronunciation: / ˌfɔrmələˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌfɔːrmələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɔːmələˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌfɔːməlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something formal, especially by defining official rules, structures, or procedures

Examples

The formalization of rules
In mathematics, the formalization of theories helps ensure clarity and precision.
The formalization of the agreement required both parties to sign a written contract.
The organization began the formalization of its procedures to standardize its operations.
Mathematics often requires the formalisation of intuitive concepts into precise definitions and axioms.
The formalisation of the company's code of conduct helped clarify expectations for all employees.
The committee recommended the formalisation of procedures to ensure consistency in decision-making.
