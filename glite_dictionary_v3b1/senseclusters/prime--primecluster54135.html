prime

verb
to fill a device with priming liquid
Pronunciation: / praɪm / (AmE), / praɪm / (BrE)
to prepare something for use by getting it ready (such as filling with liquid, covering with primer, preparing a weapon, or conditioning a system)

Examples

To prime a pump
Don't forget to prime the water pump after emptying the tank.
She carefully primed the carburetor to ensure a smooth ignition.
The mechanic had to prime the engine before starting the old truck.
She spent the morning priming the metal fence to protect it from rust.
The carpenter primed the wood to ensure the finish would last longer.
You should always prime the walls before applying a coat of paint.
After cleaning the rifle, he began to prime it for the next round.
Engineers must prime the explosive charges prior to demolition.
The soldiers carefully primed the cannon before battle.
