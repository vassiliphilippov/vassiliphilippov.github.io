strip

verb
take away something from someone
Pronunciation: / strɪp / (AmE), / strɪp / (BrE)
to take something away from someone, especially possessions, rights, or honours, often in a forceful or official way

Examples

To strip someone of rights
New laws have stripped citizens of several basic rights.
The company was stripped of its license after repeated violations.
The prisoners were stripped of their personal belongings upon arrival.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deprive - to take something away from someone or prevent them from having it
  • synonym: rob - to take property unlawfully from a person or place by force or threat
