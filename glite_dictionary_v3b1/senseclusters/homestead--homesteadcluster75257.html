homestead

noun
land acquired under US homestead law
4.7 4.5
Pronunciation: /ˈhoʊm.sted/ (AmE), /ˈhəʊm.sted/ (BrE)
A piece of land, especially in the United States, acquired from public lands by filing a record and living on and cultivating it as required by the homestead law.

Examples

A homestead claim
After years of cultivation, their homestead finally became productive farmland.
The settlers claimed a homestead in the Midwest, hoping to build a new life.
Under the Homestead Act, millions of acres were distributed as homestead land.

Related Terms

  • synonym: homesteader - a person who acquires and lives on a homestead under the homestead law
