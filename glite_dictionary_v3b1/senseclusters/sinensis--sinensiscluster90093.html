sinensis

noun
from China (Latin)
Pronunciation: / saɪˈnɛnsɪs / (AmE), / sɪˈnɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a Latin term used in scientific names to indicate a species is from China

Examples

Camellia sinensis
The tea plant is scientifically known as Camellia sinensis.
Many plant species with the name sinensis originate from China.
The botanist explained that sinensis in a species name means it comes from China.

Related Terms
