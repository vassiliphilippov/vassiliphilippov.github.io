monic polynomial

noun
polynomial with leading coefficient 1
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːnɪk pəˈlinoʊmiəl / (AmE), / ˈmɒnɪk pəˈlɪnəʊmiəl / (BrE)
A polynomial in which the leading coefficient (the coefficient of the highest power) is 1.

Examples

A monic polynomial equation
A monic polynomial has a leading coefficient of exactly one, making it unique among polynomials.
Every irreducible monic polynomial plays a key role in field theory.
When solving equations, mathematicians often prefer to work with a monic polynomial for simplicity.

  • synonym: polynomial - an expression consisting of variables and coefficients
