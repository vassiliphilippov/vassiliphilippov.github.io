minstrel

noun
medieval singer or musician
5.6 7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈmɪn.strəl/ (AmE), /ˈmɪn.strəl/ (BrE)
a singer or musician, especially one of medieval times, who sang or recited poetry and played music, often traveling from place to place

Examples

A medieval minstrel
In the castle courtyard, a wandering minstrel performed stories of heroes and legends.
The minstrel entertained the crowded hall with soulful ballads.
The minstrels of old played a vital role in medieval storytelling and music.
