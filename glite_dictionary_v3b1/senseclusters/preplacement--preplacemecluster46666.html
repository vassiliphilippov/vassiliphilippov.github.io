preplacement

noun
early job assignment
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈpleɪsmənt / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈpleɪsmənt / (BrE)
the process or status of being assigned a position or opportunity before the main placement event

Examples

Preplacement workshop
The university organized a preplacement workshop to help students prepare for job interviews.
Many companies conduct preplacement interviews to select candidates before the main recruitment drive.
She received a preplacement offer after impressing the recruiters during her internship.

Related Terms

  • synonym: internship - a temporary position for gaining work experience
  • degree higher: placement - the act of assigning someone to a position or job
