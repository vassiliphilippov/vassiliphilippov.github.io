co-principal

noun
joint school leader
Pronunciation: / ˌkoʊˈprɪnsəpəl / (AmE), / ˌkəʊˈprɪnsəpəl / (BrE)
a person who shares the position and responsibilities of principal with one or more others

Examples

School co-principal
The district decided to appoint two co-principals to manage the growing high school.
As a co-principal, she shares leadership duties and decision-making with her colleague.
The new co-principal system encourages collaboration at the top level of the school.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: assistant principal - a person who helps the principal in managing a school
  • synonym: principal - the head of a school or organization
