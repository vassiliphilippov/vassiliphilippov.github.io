mongolianism

noun
historical term for Down syndrome (offensive)
Pronunciation: / mɒŋˈgoʊliəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / mɒŋˈɡəʊlɪəˌnɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
(offensive, historical) A congenital disorder caused by an extra 21st chromosome, characterized by intellectual disability, short stature, and distinctive facial features; now called Down syndrome.

Examples

The term mongolianism
Early 20th-century medical texts sometimes referred to Down syndrome as mongolianism.
She researched the history of how mongolianism was once described in clinical literature.
The term mongolianism is now considered offensive and is no longer used in medical contexts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Down syndrome - a genetic disorder caused by trisomy of chromosome 21
