raise

verb
increase bid on partner's suit (cards)
Pronunciation: / reɪz / (AmE), / reɪz / (BrE)
to increase a bet or bid in gambling or card games

Examples

To raise a bet
He decided against raising in order to avoid overbidding.
If you want to support your partner, you should raise their suit.
In the last round, she chose to raise her partner's bid to three hearts.
Each time he raised, the other players hesitated before making their decisions.
If you want to stay in the game, you must either raise or call the current bet.
She decided to raise the stakes, putting twenty more dollars into the pot.

Related Terms

  • antonym: call - to match the previous bet in poker and similar games
  • antonym: fold - to withdraw from a hand in card games rather than match a bet
