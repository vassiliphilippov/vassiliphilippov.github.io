unselect

verb
to remove from selection
4.9
Pronunciation: /ʌn.sɪ.ˈlekt/ /; / /ən.sə.ˈlekt/ (AmE), /ʌn.sɪ.ˈlekt/ /; / /ən.sɪ.ˈlekt/ (BrE)
to remove someone or something from a list of chosen people or items, especially in teams or competitions

Examples

To unselect a player
The coach decided to unselect three players from the national squad before the tournament.
If you change your mind, you can unselect any candidate before submitting your final list.
After reviewing the applications, the committee had to unselect several participants due to limited space.
