writer's block

noun
inability to write due to psychological block
Pronunciation: / ˈraɪtərz blɑk /; / ˈraɪtərz ˌblɑk / (AmE), / ˈraɪtəz blɒk /; / ˈraɪtəz ˌblɒk / (BrE)
a temporary period when a writer cannot produce new work, usually because of a lack of ideas or creative inspiration

Examples

Writer's block period
After finishing her first novel, she suffered from writer's block for months.
He feared that his recent writer's block would delay his new book.
Many authors struggle with writer's block at some point in their careers.
After months of writer's block, he finally finished his manuscript.
Many novelists experience writer's block at some point in their careers.
She tried several techniques to overcome her writer's block, but nothing seemed to help.
