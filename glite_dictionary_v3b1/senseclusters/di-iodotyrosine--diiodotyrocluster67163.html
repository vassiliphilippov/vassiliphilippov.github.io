di-iodotyrosine

noun
iodinated tyrosine derivative
Pronunciation: / daɪˌaɪ.ə.doʊ.taɪˈroʊ.sin / (AmE), / daɪˌaɪ.ə.dəʊ.tɪˈrəʊ.siːn / (BrE)
A derivative of tyrosine in which two iodine atoms are attached to the aromatic ring, involved in the biosynthesis of thyroid hormones.

Examples

Di-iodotyrosine molecule
Both monoiodotyrosine and di-iodotyrosine are essential for the production of thyroxine.
Scientists measured the concentration of di-iodotyrosine during the hormone synthesis pathway.
The accumulation of di-iodotyrosine can provide insight into thyroid function disorders.
