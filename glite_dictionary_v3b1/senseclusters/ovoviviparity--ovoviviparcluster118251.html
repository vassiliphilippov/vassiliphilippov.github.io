ovoviviparity

noun
live birth from eggs
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvoʊˌvɪvəˈpærəti / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəʊˌvɪvɪˈpærɪti / (BrE)
a mode of reproduction in which eggs develop inside the parent's body and hatch there or immediately after being laid, resulting in live birth

Examples

Ovoviviparity in snakes
Some species of snakes use ovoviviparity to protect their young during development.
The biologist explained that ovoviviparity is common among certain types of fish and reptiles.
Unlike mammals, animals with ovoviviparity produce eggs that hatch inside the mother's body.

Related Terms

  • antonym: oviparity - reproduction by laying eggs that develop and hatch outside the body
  • antonym: viviparity - reproduction in which the embryo develops inside the body and is born live, without eggs
