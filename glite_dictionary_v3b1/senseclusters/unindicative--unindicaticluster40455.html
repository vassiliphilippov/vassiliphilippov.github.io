unindicative

adjective
not indicative mood
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnɪnˈdɪkətɪv / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪnˈdɪkətɪv / (BrE)
not in the indicative mood; expressed in a grammatical mood other than the indicative, such as the subjunctive or imperative

Examples

Unindicative verb form
The sentence was clearly unindicative, suggesting a wish rather than a statement of fact.
In some languages, unindicative forms are used to express commands or requests.
Her response was unindicative of any certainty, leaving the outcome unclear.

Related Terms

  • antonym: indicative - expressed in the indicative mood, stating facts
  • synonym: subjunctive - expressed in the subjunctive mood, showing doubt or possibility
