monastic order

noun
community of monks or nuns under a rule
Pronunciation: / məˈnæstɪk ˈɔrdər / (AmE), / məˈnastɪk ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
A community of monks or nuns living together under a religious rule, often following a particular spiritual discipline or tradition.

Examples

A monastic order community
Many monastic orders have played a crucial role in preserving ancient manuscripts.
She decided to join a monastic order and dedicate her life to religious service.
The Benedictines are one of the oldest monastic orders in Europe.
