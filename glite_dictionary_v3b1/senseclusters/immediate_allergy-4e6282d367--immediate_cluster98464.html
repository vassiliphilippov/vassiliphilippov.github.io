immediate allergy

noun
allergy with rapid onset
4.0 4.2
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmiːdiət əˈlɚdʒi / (AmE), / ɪˈmiːdiət əˈlədʒi / (BrE)
an allergic reaction that occurs within minutes after exposure in a sensitized person

Examples

An immediate allergy reaction
An immediate allergy develops rapidly, often causing symptoms such as hives or swelling within minutes.
Doctors performed tests to distinguish between a delayed and an immediate allergy.
People with an immediate allergy to peanuts can experience anaphylaxis shortly after ingestion.
