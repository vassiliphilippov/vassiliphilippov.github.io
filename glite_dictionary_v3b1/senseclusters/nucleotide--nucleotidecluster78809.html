nucleotide

noun
basic unit of DNA or RNA
4.2 5.3
Pronunciation: /ˈnuː.kliː.ə.taɪd/ /; / /ˈnjuː.kliː.ə.taɪd/ (AmE), /ˈnjuː.kliː.ə.taɪd/ /; / /ˈnuː.kliː.ə.taɪd/ (BrE)
a compound consisting of a nucleoside bound to a phosphate group, which forms the basic structural unit of nucleic acids such as DNA and RNA

Examples

A nucleotide unit
A mutation occurs when a single nucleotide in the genetic code is changed.
DNA replication relies on the precise pairing of nucleotides to create a new strand.
The structure of RNA is also composed of a chain of nucleotides.
