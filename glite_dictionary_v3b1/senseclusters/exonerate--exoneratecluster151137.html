exonerate

verb
declare someone not guilty; clear from blame
3.1 6.5
Pronunciation: / ɪgˈzɑnəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ɪgˈzɒnəreɪt / (BrE)
to officially state that someone is not guilty of wrongdoing or criminal charges; to clear from blame or responsibility

Examples

To exonerate the defendant
After reviewing the new evidence, the judge decided to exonerate the defendant.
Despite years of accusations, he was finally exonerated and his name was cleared.
The DNA test results helped exonerate her from all suspicion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absolve - to free someone from guilt, blame, or responsibility
  • antonym: convict - to declare someone guilty of a crime
