anthem

noun
song of loyalty or devotion
Pronunciation: / ˈæn.θəm / (AmE), / ˈæn.θəm / (BrE)
a song expressing devotion, loyalty, or praise, especially for a country, group, or in religious ceremonies

Examples

National anthem

She proudly sang the school anthem at graduation.
The national anthem is played before major sporting events.
Many churches open the service with an uplifting anthem sung by the choir.
That song became an unofficial anthem for the civil rights movement.
The crowd stood in silence as the national anthem was played before the match.
