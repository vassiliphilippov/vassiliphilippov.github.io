mink

noun
fur-bearing semiaquatic mammal
7.2
Pronunciation: / mɪŋk / (AmE), / mɪŋk / (BrE)
A small, slender-bodied semiaquatic mammal with partially webbed feet, valued for its soft, dense fur.

Examples

Wild mink
Her coat was made of luxurious mink fur, which attracted many compliments.
The mink swam gracefully along the riverbank in search of food.
Wild minks are difficult to spot due to their elusive nature.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ermine - a small mustelid mammal valued for its white winter fur
  • synonym: otter - a semiaquatic mammal with dense fur and webbed feet
