generator

noun
device that produces an electrical signal or voltage
Pronunciation: / ˈd͡ʒɛnəˌreɪtɚ / (AmE), / ˈd͡ʒɛnəˌreɪtə / (BrE)
an electronic device that produces an electrical signal or voltage, often for testing or powering circuits

Examples

Signal generator
A signal generator can simulate different frequencies for testing audio equipment.
Engineers use a generator in the lab to produce precise voltage waveforms.
The technician adjusted the generator to provide a steady test voltage for the experiment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oscillator - an electronic device for generating a repetitive electronic signal
