geoengineering

noun
environmental engineering work
7.0
Pronunciation: /dʒiː.oʊ.en.dʒɪ.ˈnɪr.ɪŋ/ /; / /dʒiː.oʊ.ən.dʒɪ.ˈnɪr.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /dʒiː.əʊ.en.dʒɪ.ˈnɪə.rɪŋ/ /; / /dʒiː.əʊ.ən.dʒɪ.ˈnɪə.rɪŋ/ (BrE)
the use of engineering techniques to modify, protect, or stabilize the physical environment, especially the ground, soil, or coasts.

Examples

Geoengineering for coastal protection
The government funded several geoengineering projects to prevent coastal erosion.
Advances in geoengineering have made it possible to stabilize landslide-prone areas.
Experts debated the long-term effects of large-scale geoengineering on the environment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: civil engineering - the design and construction of public works like roads, bridges, and dams
  • synonym: environmental engineering - the branch of engineering focused on protecting and improving the environment
