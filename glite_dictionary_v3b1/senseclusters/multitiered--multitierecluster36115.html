multitiered

adjective
having multiple levels
Pronunciation: / ˌmʌltiˈtɪrd / (AmE), / ˌmʌltiˈtɪəd / (BrE)
having several levels, layers, or stages arranged one above another

Examples

A multitiered system
The company introduced a multitiered pricing system to serve different types of customers.
A multitiered approach was used to address the complex problem.
The stadium featured multitiered seating to accommodate more spectators.

Related Terms

  • antonym: single-tiered - having only one level or layer
