mirror-image relation

noun
relation between reflected molecular structures
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪrɚ ˈɪmɪdʒ rɪˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈmɪrə ˈɪmɪdʒ rɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a relationship between crystals or molecules that are exact reflections of each other, typically with respect to chirality or structure

Examples

Mirror-image relation in crystals
In organic chemistry, amino acids often exhibit a mirror-image relation to one another, which determines their biological activity.
Some minerals can form crystals that display a mirror-image relation, making them optical isomers.
The concept of a mirror-image relation is crucial in understanding enantiomers in stereochemistry.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chirality - property of an object being non-superimposable on its mirror image
