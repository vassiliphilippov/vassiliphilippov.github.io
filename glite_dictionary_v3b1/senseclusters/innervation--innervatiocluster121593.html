innervation

noun
supply of nerves to an organ or region
8.3 8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnɚˈveɪʃən /; / ˌɪn.ərˈveɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnəˈveɪʃən /; / ˌɪn.əˈveɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the supply or distribution of nerves to a specific part of the body (such as an organ, muscle, or region)

Examples

Innervation of muscles
Damage to the spinal cord can affect the innervation of the lower limbs.
Proper innervation is essential for muscle control and sensory perception.
The innervation of the hand is mainly provided by the median, ulnar, and radial nerves.
Damage to the spinal cord can affect the innervation of muscles in the legs.
Proper innervation of the digestive tract is necessary for normal gastrointestinal function.
The innervation of the heart is essential for coordinating its rhythmic contractions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: enervation - loss or absence of nerve supply to an organ or body region
  • antonym: paralysis - loss of the ability to move or feel parts of the body
  • synonym: stimulation - the act of encouraging activity or arousal in something
