gley

verb
to waterlog soil
Pronunciation: / ɡleɪ / (AmE), / ɡleɪ / (BrE)
to become, or to cause soil to become, waterlogged and develop a grey or blue-grey color due to lack of oxygen.

Examples

To gley the ground
Heavy rainfall can gley the fields, making the soil difficult to cultivate.
If the drainage is poor, the soil may gley and turn a bluish-grey color.
Farmers try to prevent their land from becoming gleyed by improving water flow.

Related Terms

  • antonym: drain - to remove liquid from something, especially soil
  • synonym: waterlog - to saturate with water so that it becomes soggy or flooded
