nonlegato

noun
detached musical articulation
9.0
Pronunciation: /nɑːn.lə.ˈɡɑː.toʊ/ /; / /nɑːn.le.ˈɡɑː.toʊ/ (AmE), /nɒn.lə.ˈɡɑː.təʊ/ /; / /nɒn.le.ˈɡɑː.təʊ/ (BrE)
a musical direction indicating that notes should be played in a light, detached manner, not smoothly connected

Examples

Marking as nonlegato
The pianist played the passage in nonlegato to create a lighter, more detached sound.
When the score is marked nonlegato, each note should be slightly separated from the next.
The conductor requested a nonlegato style for the opening bars of the piece.

Related Terms

  • antonym: legato - a musical direction to play notes smoothly connected
  • synonym: staccato - a musical direction to play notes sharply separated
