Cytherea

proper noun
Greek goddess of love; another name for Aphrodite
Pronunciation: / sɪˈθɪəriə / (AmE), / sɪˈθɪəriə / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, another name for Aphrodite, the goddess of love and beauty, especially as worshipped in Cythera.

Examples

Cytherea in mythology
In ancient times, worshippers made offerings to Cytherea at her temple on the island of Cythera.
Many poets praised Cytherea in their verses as the embodiment of sensual beauty.
The painting depicts Cytherea emerging from the sea, evoking the famous myth of Aphrodite's birth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Venus - Roman goddess of love and beauty, equivalent to Aphrodite
