disenchanted

adjective
no longer enchanted or captivated
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪs.ɪnˈtʃæn.tɪd / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.ɪnˈtʃæn.tɪd / (BrE)
No longer under a spell or illusion; freed from enchantment or false beliefs.

Examples

Feeling disenchanted with life
After learning the truth, she felt disenchanted with the glamorous world of show business.
He grew disenchanted with politics and decided to step away from public life.
The villagers were finally disenchanted when the magician's tricks were revealed to be fake.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disillusioned - having lost faith or naïve belief
  • antonym: enchanted - placed under a spell or captivated by magical powers
