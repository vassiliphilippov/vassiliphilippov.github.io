waffle

verb
to be indecisive or vacillate
9.4 8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑːfəl / (AmE), / ˈwɒf(ə)l / (BrE)
to be indecisive or avoid committing oneself, especially when expressing an opinion or making a decision

Examples

To waffle on decisions
She tends to waffle whenever someone asks her an important question.
The chairman waffled for several minutes before finally announcing the decision.
When asked about policy changes, the minister began to waffle rather than give a clear answer.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decide - to choose or make a determination
  • synonym: hesitate - to pause before saying or doing something, often due to uncertainty
  • synonym: vacillate - to waver between different opinions or actions
