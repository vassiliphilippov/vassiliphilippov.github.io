passivate

verb
to make inactive
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈpæsɪˌveɪt / (AmE), / ˈpæsɪveɪt / (BrE)
to make someone or something inactive, less effective, or less likely to take action, especially in a figurative sense

Examples

To passivate a group
Strict supervision tended to passivate the students, making them less likely to participate in class discussions.
The company's rigid policies seemed to passivate employee initiative and creativity.
Some leaders worry that too many rules can passivate a community's willingness to solve its own problems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deactivate - to make something inactive or no longer effective
  • antonym: stimulate - to encourage activity or increased action
