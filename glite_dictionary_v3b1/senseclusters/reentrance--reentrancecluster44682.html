reentrance

noun
coastal inlet
8.9
Pronunciation: / riˈɛntrəns / (AmE), / riˈɛntrəns / (BrE)
a small bay, inlet, or recess in a coastline or shoreline where the land curves inward

Examples

Coastal reentrance
The fishermen anchored their boat in a sheltered reentrance along the rocky coast.
Seabirds nested in the cliffs above the narrow reentrance where the waves were calm.
During the storm, the sailors searched for a reentrance to protect their vessel from the wind.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cove - a small sheltered bay on a coast
  • antonym: promontory - a high point of land that juts out into water
