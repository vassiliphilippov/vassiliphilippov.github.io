Vaisya

noun
member of the third Hindu caste (merchant/professional)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈvaɪʃjə / (AmE), / ˈvaɪsjə / (BrE)
A member of the third of the four main Hindu castes, traditionally comprising merchants, traders, and professional groups in Hindu society.

Examples

A Vaisya merchant
As a Vaisya, he was expected to engage in trade and business.
She learned about the traditional duties of a Vaisya in her cultural studies class.
The Vaisya community played an important role in the local economy.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Brahmin - a member of the highest Hindu caste, traditionally priests or scholars
  • degree higher: Kshatriya - a member of the second Hindu caste, traditionally warriors or rulers
  • degree lower: Shudra - a member of the lowest Hindu caste, traditionally laborers or servants
