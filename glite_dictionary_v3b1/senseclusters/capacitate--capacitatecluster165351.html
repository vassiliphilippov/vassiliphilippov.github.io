capacitate

verb
make legally capable or qualified
7.7 8.1
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈpæs.ɪ.teɪt/ (AmE), /kə.ˈpæs.ɪ.teɪt/ (BrE)
to make legally capable or qualified; to grant the legal power or authority to act.

Examples

To capacitate a person legally
After years of training, she was finally capacitated to serve as a legal guardian.
The law intends to capacitate nonprofit organizations to enter into such contracts.
The new statute seeks to capacitate individuals with disabilities to manage their own legal affairs.
