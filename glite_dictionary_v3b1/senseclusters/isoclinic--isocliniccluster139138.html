isoclinic

adjective
having equal magnetic inclination
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪ.səˈklɪn.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌaɪ.səʊˈklɪn.ɪk / (BrE)
having equal magnetic inclination; used especially in describing lines or points on the Earth's surface where the magnetic dip is the same.

Examples

Isoclinic magnetic lines
Isoclinic points are important for understanding variations in the Earth's magnetism.
Geophysicists often chart isoclinic lines to map Earth's magnetic field.
The survey revealed an isoclinic area where the magnetic inclination remained constant across several kilometers.
