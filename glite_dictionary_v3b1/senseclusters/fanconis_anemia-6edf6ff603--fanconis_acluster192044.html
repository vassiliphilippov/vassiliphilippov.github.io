Fanconi's anemia

noun
rare inherited disorder with bone marrow failure
Pronunciation: / fænˈkoʊniːz əˈniːmiə /; / fænˈkoʊniz əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / fanˈkəʊniz əˈniːmiə / (BrE)
A rare inherited disorder characterized by pancytopenia and underdevelopment of the bone marrow, often leading to increased risk of cancer and physical abnormalities.

Examples

Research into treatments for Fanconi's anemia has led to important advances in bone marrow transplantation.

Fanconi's anaemia is often detected in early childhood during routine blood tests.
Following the diagnosis of Fanconi's anaemia, the child underwent bone marrow testing.
Researchers are working to find new treatments for Fanconi's anaemia.
