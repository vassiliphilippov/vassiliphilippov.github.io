overemphasize

verb
place too much emphasis
4.8 4.5
Pronunciation: /oʊ.və.ˈrem.fə.saɪz/ /; / /oʊ.vər.ˈem.fə.saɪz/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ˈrem.fə.saɪz/ /; / /əʊ.və.ˈem.fə.saɪz/ (BrE)
to give something more importance or attention than is necessary or appropriate

Examples

To overemphasize small problems
In his speech, the manager tended to overemphasize minor issues and ignore the bigger picture.
She sometimes overemphasizes her achievements, which can be off-putting to others.
You shouldn't overemphasize the impact of a single test score when assessing a student.
It is easy to overemphasise minor issues while overlooking bigger problems.
Teachers sometimes overemphasise the importance of test results.
The report tends to overemphasise the risks involved in the project.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overemphasize - place special or excessive emphasis on (American spelling)
  • antonym: underemphasise - give too little emphasis to
