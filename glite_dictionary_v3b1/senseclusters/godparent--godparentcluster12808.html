godparent

noun
sponsor at baptism; spiritual guardian
3.0 2.5
Pronunciation: /ˈgɑːd.per.ənt/ /; / /ˈgɑːd.pær.ənt/ (AmE), /ˈgɒd.peə.rənt/ (BrE)
a person who sponsors a child at their baptism, taking responsibility for their religious upbringing or spiritual welfare.

Examples

A godparent at baptism
Many families have a special celebration when choosing a godparent for a child.
She asked her sister to be the godparent of her newborn son.
The role of a godparent often extends beyond religious guidance to providing lifelong support.
