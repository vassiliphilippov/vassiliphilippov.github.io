ague

noun
fever with shivering fits
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈeɪ.gjuː/ (AmE), /ˈeɪ.gjuː/ (BrE)
an illness or attack of fever with chills and sweating, especially one that comes and goes (historically linked to malaria)

Examples

An ague attack
He was seized by an ague that left him shivering uncontrollably.
Her sudden ague was followed by a persistent fever.
In the old days, many suffered from ague during the winter months.
During the 19th century, many in the region were plagued by ague and other fevers.
His strength was sapped by recurring bouts of ague each spring.
The patient suffered a violent ague with shaking chills every evening.
