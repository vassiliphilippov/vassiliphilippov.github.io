augmentation

noun
musical theme stated in longer notes
Pronunciation: / ˌɔɡ.mənˈteɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːɡ.menˈteɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
In music, the statement of a theme in notes of greater duration than the original, typically making each note twice as long.

Examples

Augmentation of a theme
In the fugue, the subject appears in augmentation in the bass voice.
The composer used augmentation to give the melody a slower, more solemn character.
Through augmentation, the familiar theme becomes more dramatic and expansive.
