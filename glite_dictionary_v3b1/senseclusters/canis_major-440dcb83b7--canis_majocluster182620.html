Canis Major

proper noun
constellation with Sirius, southeast of Orion
8.1
Pronunciation: /ˈkeɪ.nɪs ˈmeɪ.dʒər/ (AmE), /ˈkeɪ.nɪs ˈmeɪ.dʒə/ (BrE)
a constellation in the southern sky, located southeast of Orion and containing the bright star Sirius

Examples

Canis Major constellation
Astronomers often observe Canis Major to locate Sirius, the brightest star in the night sky.
In mythology, Canis Major is known as the Great Dog, faithfully following Orion across the heavens.
The constellation Canis Major is best seen from the southern hemisphere during the months of February and March.
