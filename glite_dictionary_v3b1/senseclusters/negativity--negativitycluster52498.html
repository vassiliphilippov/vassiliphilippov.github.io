negativity

noun
measure of quantum entanglement

(in quantum information theory) a numerical measure of entanglement for a bipartite quantum state, defined using the partial transpose of the state's density matrix.

Examples

Quantum negativity
Researchers showed that the negativity of the state indicated entanglement between qubits.
They calculated the negativity to quantify how strongly the two photons were entangled.
Low negativity values suggest the subsystems are nearly separable, not entangled, in practice.

Related Terms

  • synonym: entanglement - a quantum property in which the states of two or more particles are correlated; negativity quantifies this property.
  • synonym: logarithmic negativity - a related entanglement measure derived from negativity, often used to quantify quantum correlations.
