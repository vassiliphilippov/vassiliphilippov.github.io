discountenance

verb
express disapproval; discourage
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪskaʊnˈtɛnəns /; / dɪsˈkaʊntənəns / (AmE), / ˌdɪskaʊnˈtɛnəns /; / dɪsˈkaʊntɪnəns / (BrE)
to express disapproval of something or to discourage it

Examples

To discountenance bad behavior
Historically, certain fashions were discountenanced by leaders who preferred more modest attire.
The company will openly discountenance any attempts at discrimination in the workplace.
The school principal discountenanced the students' proposal for a longer lunch break.
His proposal was discountenanced by senior management.
Many teachers discountenance the use of mobile phones in class.
The organization publicly discountenanced any form of corruption.

Related Terms

  • antonym: approve - to consider something good or acceptable
  • synonym: disapprove - to have or express an unfavorable opinion
  • antonym: encourage - to give support or confidence; to urge on
