Pitot head

noun
pressure-measuring device for airspeed in aircraft
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈpiːtoʊ hɛd / (AmE), / ˈpiːtəʊ hɛd / (BrE)
A device composed of a combined Pitot tube and static tube used to measure both total and static pressure, especially for determining airspeed in aircraft.

Examples

A Pitot head device
Engineers designed a new Pitot head to improve the aircraft's performance in adverse weather.
Ice can sometimes block the Pitot head, causing incorrect airspeed readings.
The Pitot head must be checked for obstructions before every flight.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Pitot tube - a tube for measuring fluid flow velocity by pressure difference
  • synonym: static tube - tube for measuring static pressure in a fluid
