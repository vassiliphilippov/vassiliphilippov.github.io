commination

noun
threat of divine punishment
Pronunciation: / kɑməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / kɒmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
a solemn threat or declaration of divine punishment or vengeance, especially in a religious context

Examples

A commination of sinners
In medieval times, commination was often used to warn parishioners about sin.
Reading the ancient text, we encountered a fierce commination directed at wrongdoers.
The priest delivered a commination against those who broke their vows.
