any digit in a number that contributes to its precision, starting with the first non-zero digit and ending with the last significant figure, which may include a zero if it indicates precision.

Examples

Three significant digits
The measurement was reported to three significant digits, indicating its precision.
When rounding to two significant digits, 0.04562 becomes 0.046.
Zeros at the end of a number may be considered significant digits if they indicate precision.
