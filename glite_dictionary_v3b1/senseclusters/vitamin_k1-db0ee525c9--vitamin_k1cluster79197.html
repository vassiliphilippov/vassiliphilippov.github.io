vitamin K1

noun
a form of vitamin K important for blood clotting
Pronunciation: / ˌvaɪtəmɪn ˌkeɪ ˈwʌn / (AmE), / ˌvɪtəmɪn ˌkeɪ ˈwʌn / (BrE)
a fat-soluble vitamin found in green leafy vegetables, essential for blood clotting and bone health; a specific form of vitamin K known as phylloquinone

Examples

Vitamin K1 in spinach
Lack of vitamin K1 can lead to problems with blood clotting.
Many green vegetables are rich sources of vitamin K1.
Patients on blood thinners should monitor their vitamin K1 intake.

Related Terms

  • synonym: phylloquinone - another name for vitamin K1
  • degree higher: vitamin K2 - a different form of vitamin K, involved in bone and cardiovascular health
