dead-end street

noun
street closed at one end
2.1 0.21/m
Pronunciation: / Ààd…õdÀå…õnd Àåstrit / (AmE), / Ààd…õdÀå…õnd ÀåstriÀêt / (BrE)
a street that is closed at one end and does not allow traffic to pass through

Examples

A dead-end street
Our office moved to a building at the end of a dead-end street, which has less traffic.
The children enjoy playing safely on the dead-end street near their home.
The house is located on a quiet dead-end street away from the main road.

Synonyms

  • dead end
    2.7 1.19/m
    dead end is the common short name for a road closed at one end, while dead-end street is the fuller, more formal phrase.
  • cul-de-sac
    4.7 1.17/m
    cul-de-sac is a slightly formal borrowed word for a short road closed at one end, while dead-end street is more everyday English.
  • blind alley
    4.1 0.01/m
    blind alley is a traditional term for a passage closed at one end, while dead-end street is the modern, widely used name for that road.
  • impasse
    8.1 0.03/m
    impasse is often used for a blocked situation or a closed road in formal speech, while dead-end street names the actual closed road.
