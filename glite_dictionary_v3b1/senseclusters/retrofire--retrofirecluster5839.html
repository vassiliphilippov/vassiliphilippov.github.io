retrofire

noun
reverse rocket firing
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛtroʊˌfaɪər / (AmE), / ˈrɛtrəʊˌfaɪə / (BrE)
the act of firing a rocket engine in the opposite direction to slow down, stop, or change a spacecraft's path

Examples

A retrofire maneuver
The spacecraft performed a retrofire to slow down before entering the planet's atmosphere.
Engineers monitored the retrofire sequence to ensure the capsule would land safely.
A successful retrofire is critical for returning astronauts to Earth from orbit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ignition - the act of setting something on fire or starting to burn
  • synonym: retrograde burn - a rocket engine burn in the opposite direction of travel
