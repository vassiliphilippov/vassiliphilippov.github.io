copper-bottomed deal

noun
guaranteed secure or reliable agreement
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːpər ˈbɑːtəmd diːl / (AmE), / ˌkɒpə ˈbɒtəmd diːl / (BrE)
an agreement that is guaranteed to be secure, reliable, or without risk of failure

Examples

A copper-bottomed deal
She wanted nothing less than a copper-bottomed deal before agreeing to the partnership.
The company offered a copper-bottomed deal to attract new investors.
With such strong guarantees, the contract was considered a copper-bottomed deal by everyone involved.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ironclad agreement - agreement that is extremely strong or unbreakable
  • antonym: risky agreement - agreement that is uncertain and potentially unsafe
