suppressible

adjective
able to be neutralized
Pronunciation: / səˈprɛsəbəl / (AmE), / səˈprɛsəbəl / (BrE)
capable of being neutralized or compensated for, especially by a second mutation in genetics

Examples

Suppressible mutation
The genetic disorder was found to be suppressible by introducing a compensatory mutation.
Some mutations are suppressible, allowing normal function to be restored in the organism.
Researchers are studying which types of errors in DNA are suppressible by other genetic changes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: irreversible - impossible to undo or reverse
  • synonym: reversible - able to be undone or returned to a previous state
