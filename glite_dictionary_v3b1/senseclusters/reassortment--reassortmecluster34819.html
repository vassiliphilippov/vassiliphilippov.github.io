reassortment

noun
genetic mixing process
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˌriːəˈsɔːrtmənt /; / ˌriːəˈsɔrtmənt / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈsɔːtmənt / (BrE)
the process by which genetic material from different sources is mixed or rearranged, especially in viruses, to create new genetic combinations

Examples

Genetic reassortment
The rapid evolution of some viruses is due to reassortment of their genetic material.
Scientists study reassortment to understand how new strains of influenza emerge.
Through reassortment, viruses can acquire genes from different sources, increasing their diversity.
Scientists study reassortment to understand how new strains of influenza viruses emerge.
The rapid reassortment of genetic material can lead to unexpected changes in viral behavior.
Public health officials monitor reassortment events to predict possible outbreaks.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mutation - a change in the genetic sequence of an organism
  • synonym: recombination - the exchange of genetic material between different organisms
