ravel

verb
to tangle or complicate
Pronunciation: / ˈrævəl / (AmE), / ˈrævəl / (BrE)
to tangle or complicate something; to make something more confusing or complex

Examples

To ravel a problem
Bureaucratic procedures often ravel even the simplest tasks.
Her explanation ravelled the issue instead of clarifying it.
The unwelcome new evidence only ravelled the mystery further.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tangle - to twist together into a confused mass
  • antonym: unravel - to make or become untangled or clear
