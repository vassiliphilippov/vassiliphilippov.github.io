paternalist

adjective
restricting for own good
8.4
Pronunciation: /pə.ˈtɜːr.nəl.ɪst/ (AmE), /pə.ˈtɜː.nəl.ɪst/ (BrE)
showing or based on the belief that authorities should restrict people's freedom for their own good, as in paternalism

Examples

Paternalist government policy
The new policy was criticized as paternalist because it limited people's choices for their supposed benefit.
Some leaders take a paternalist approach, believing they know what is best for everyone.
Critics argue that paternalist laws can undermine personal responsibility and freedom.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authoritarian - favoring strict obedience to authority
  • antonym: libertarian - favoring maximum individual freedom
