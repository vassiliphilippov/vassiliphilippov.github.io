spirits

noun
distilled alcoholic drinks
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈspɪr.ɪts / (AmE), / ˈspɪr.ɪts / (BrE)
Strong alcoholic drinks made by distilling fermented substances, such as whiskey, vodka, or gin.

Examples

Bottles of spirits
Duty-free shops at airports often sell bottles of spirits at lower prices.
He prefers drinking spirits to beer because of the stronger taste.
The bar offers a wide selection of spirits, including whiskey, gin, and rum.

Related Terms

  • antonym: beer - alcoholic drink made by fermenting grain, not distilled
  • synonym: liquor - strong distilled alcoholic drink
  • antonym: wine - alcoholic drink made from fermented grapes, not distilled
