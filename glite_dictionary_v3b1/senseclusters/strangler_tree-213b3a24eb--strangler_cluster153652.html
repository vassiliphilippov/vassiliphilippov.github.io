strangler tree

noun
tree that kills its host by growing around it
7.8 7.6
Pronunciation: /ˈstræŋ.glər triː/ /; / /ˈstræŋ.glər tʃriː/ (AmE), /ˈstræŋ.glə triː/ /; / /ˈstræŋ.glə tʃriː/ (BrE)
A tree or vine that grows on another tree and extends aerial roots down the host's trunk, eventually surrounding and killing it.

Examples

A strangler tree vine
Ecologists studied how the strangler tree impacts biodiversity in tropical forests.
Many rainforests are home to the strangler tree, which starts life high in the canopy and slowly chokes its support tree.
The ancient fig, known as a strangler tree, had grown around its host and completely enveloped it.
