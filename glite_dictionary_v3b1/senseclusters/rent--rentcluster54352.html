rent

verb
pay to use something temporarily
1.8 16/m
Pronunciation: / rÉ›nt / (AmE), / rÉ›nt / (BrE)
to pay a sum of money for the temporary use of something such as property, a vehicle, or equipment under a contract

Examples

To rent a car
Many tourists rent bicycles to explore the city.
She rented a small office downtown for her new business.
We decided to rent a cabin by the lake for our summer holiday.
The company rents office space in the city center.
We decided to rent a car for our trip across the country.
You can rent movies online instead of buying them.

Synonyms

  • lease
    3.3 1.84/m
    lease is a formal contract often for longer use of property, while rent means paying money for temporary use.
  • hire
    1.2 14/m
    hire means paying to use something or someone's services for a short time, while rent means paying for temporary use.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hire - to pay money to use something or employ someone temporarily
  • synonym: lease - obtain or grant the use of something for a period in return for payment
  • synonym: let - allow someone to use property in return for payment (chiefly British)
