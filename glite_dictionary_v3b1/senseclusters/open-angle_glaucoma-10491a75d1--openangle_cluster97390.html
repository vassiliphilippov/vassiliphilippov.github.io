open-angle glaucoma

noun
common type of glaucoma with gradual vision loss
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊpən ˈæŋɡəl ɡloʊˈkoʊmə / (AmE), / ˈəʊpən ˈæŋɡl ɡlɔːˈkəʊmə / (BrE)
A common form of glaucoma in which fluid drainage through the canal of Schlemm is blocked, causing gradual loss of peripheral vision.

Examples

Open-angle glaucoma symptoms
Because open-angle glaucoma develops slowly, many people do not notice symptoms until significant damage has occurred.
Early detection of open-angle glaucoma is crucial to prevent irreversible vision loss.
Her doctor recommended regular eye exams to monitor for open-angle glaucoma due to her family history.
