radical

noun
linguistic root form of a word
4.3 5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈrædɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈrædɪkəl / (BrE)
(Linguistics) The basic form of a word after all affixes (prefixes and suffixes) have been removed; also called the root or base.

Examples

A word radical
In the word 'unhappiness', the radical is 'happy'.
Inflectional endings are added to the radical to create different word forms.
The linguist identified the radical before analyzing the word's structure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: root - the base part of a word after removing affixes
  • synonym: stem - the main part of a word to which affixes are added
