soak through

verb
become completely wet with liquid
3.3 2.6
Pronunciation: /soʊk θruː/ /; / /soʊk θrʌf/ (AmE), /səʊk θruː/ /; / /səʊk θrʌf/ (BrE)
to become completely wet or saturated by a liquid; to allow liquid to pass through and make something wet inside or all the way through

Examples

Shoes soaked through
Her shoes soaked through after she walked through the wet grass.
If you spill water on a book, the pages will soak through quickly.
The rain was so heavy that it made my coat soak through in minutes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dry out - to lose all moisture; to become dry
  • synonym: soak - to become very wet by absorbing liquid
