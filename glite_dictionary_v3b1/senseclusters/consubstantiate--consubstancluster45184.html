consubstantiate

verb
unite into one common substance
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːnsəbˈstænʃiˌeɪt / (AmE), / ˌkɒnsəbˈstænʃieɪt / (BrE)
to unite or combine two or more things into one substance or essence, especially in philosophy or theology

Examples

To consubstantiate two things
In his writings, he proposed that spirit and body consubstantiate in human existence.
Some philosophers argue that mind and matter can consubstantiate to form conscious experience.
The doctrine claims that the divine nature will consubstantiate with the elements during the ritual.
In some philosophical discussions, the mind and world are said to consubstantiate through perception.
Over centuries, doctrines about the divine nature have claimed that Christ's human and divine natures consubstantiate.
The two theories consubstantiate into a single coherent framework when examined together.

Related Terms

  • antonym: separate - to move or be apart; to divide into different parts
  • synonym: unite - to come or bring together to form a single entity
