unpick

verb
remove stitches or undo by separating threads
7.6
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈpɪk / (AmE), / ʌnˈpɪk / (BrE)
to remove stitches from fabric or undo sewing by taking out the thread

Examples

To unpick the stitches
After making an error, he painstakingly unpicked the hem.
Carefully unpick the seam so you can add the new panel of fabric.
She had to unpick the stitches to correct her mistake.
He patiently unpicked the seams to alter the fit of his shirt.
She had to unpick the hem to shorten the dress.
While unpicking the stitching, she found the fabric was torn underneath.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sew - to join or repair something by making stitches with a needle and thread
  • synonym: unravel - to undo twisted, knitted, or woven threads
  • synonym: unstitch - to take out stitches from something that has been sewn
