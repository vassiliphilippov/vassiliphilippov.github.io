katun

noun
seasonal shepherd settlement
Pronunciation: / kɑˈtun / (AmE), / kəˈtuːn / (BrE)
a temporary or seasonal mountain settlement used by shepherds for grazing animals, especially in the Balkans and Eastern Europe

Examples

Mountain katun camp
The shepherds built a simple katun each spring high in the mountains.
During the summer, families would live in the katun while tending their sheep.
Old photographs show a cluster of wooden huts forming a traditional katun in the Balkans.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alpage - a mountain pasture used for summer grazing, especially in the Alps
  • synonym: pasture - land covered with grass for grazing animals
