The formal ending or elimination of a system, practice, or institution, especially by law; for example, the abolition of slavery.

Examples

The abolition of slavery
After years of campaigning, the government finally announced the abolition of the outdated tax.
The abolition of slavery transformed societies across the world.
The abolition of the death penalty was a major milestone for human rights in the country.

Synonyms

  • abolishment
    5.5 0.32/m
    abolishment is the act of formally ending something, while abolition often names large system changes like slavery ended by law.
  • abrogation
    8.9 0.37/m
    abrogation is a formal legal canceling of laws or agreements, while abolition covers ending entire systems or institutions by law.
  • repeal
    5.5 4.31/m
    repeal is the official removal of a specific law, while abolition means ending a whole system or institution, often by law.
  • annulment
    6.5 0.79/m
    annulment treats a contract or marriage as if it never existed, while abolition ends a system or practice by law.
