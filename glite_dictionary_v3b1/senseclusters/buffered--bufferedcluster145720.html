buffered

adjective
temporarily stored
4.4
Pronunciation: /ˈbʌf.ərd/ (AmE), /ˈbʌf.əd/ (BrE)
temporarily stored in a buffer to manage data flow or prevent interruptions, especially in computing or electronics

Examples

Buffered video stream
The audio was buffered to prevent any interruptions during playback.
Data packets are often buffered before being sent over the network.
The buffered video loaded quickly, allowing viewers to watch without delays.
