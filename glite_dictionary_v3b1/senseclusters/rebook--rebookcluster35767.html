rebook

verb
to charge again
6.5
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈbʊk/ /; / /rɪ.ˈbʊk/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈbʊk/ /; / /riː.ˈbʊk/ (BrE)
to officially record someone as arrested or charged again, especially by filing new or different criminal charges

Examples

Police rebook a suspect
The police had to rebook the suspect after discovering new evidence.
If additional charges are found, authorities may rebook the individual.
After the case was reviewed, the prosecutor decided to rebook him on different charges.
