sop

verb
give a gift or bribe to placate
9.0
Pronunciation: / sɑp / (AmE), / sɒp / (BrE)
to placate someone by giving a gift or bribe, usually to quiet discontent or gain favor

Examples

To sop angry people
He attempted to sop his critics by offering them generous travel allowances.
She knew a small gift would not sop the angry crowd for long.
The company tried to sop the union with minor concessions during the negotiations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bribe - to dishonestly persuade someone to act in one's favor by a gift of money or other inducement
  • synonym: placate - to make someone less angry or hostile
