ideography

noun
system using ideograms in writing
1.6
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪdiˈɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / ˌaɪdiˈɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the use of ideograms in writing; a system of writing in which ideas are represented by symbols rather than words or sounds

Examples

Ideography system
Before the development of the alphabet, many cultures used ideography to communicate complex ideas.
Chinese characters are an example of ideography in practice.
The study of ancient scripts often focuses on the principles of ideography.
