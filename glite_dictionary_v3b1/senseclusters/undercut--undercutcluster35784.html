undercut

verb
sell at a lower price than competitors
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndɚˈkʌt / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈkʌt / (BrE)
to sell goods or services at a lower price than a competitor

Examples

To undercut prices
Online retailers often undercut local shops by selling items for less.
The new store tried to undercut all of its rivals by offering huge discounts.
To stay competitive, the company has been undercutting its main competitors on price.
