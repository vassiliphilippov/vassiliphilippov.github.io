premorbid

adjective
before illness
Pronunciation: / priˈmɔrbɪd / (AmE), / priˈmɔːbɪd / (BrE)
describing the state or condition of a person before the onset of a disease or disorder

Examples

Premorbid condition
The doctor assessed the patient's premorbid level of functioning to plan the best treatment.
Researchers studied the premorbid cognitive abilities of individuals before the onset of dementia.
Family members described his premorbid personality as outgoing and cheerful.

Related Terms

  • antonym: postmorbid - relating to the period after the onset of a disease
