brain death

noun
irreversible loss of all brain function
3.8 4.7
Pronunciation: /breɪn deθ/ (AmE), /breɪn deθ/ (BrE)
A condition in which all brain activity has permanently ceased, resulting in the irreversible loss of consciousness and the body's ability to function independently, even if the heart continues to beat.

Examples

Brain death diagnosis
Doctors declared brain death after extensive tests showed no brain activity.
Organ donation is only possible in some cases after brain death has been confirmed.
The distinction between a coma and brain death is crucial in critical care medicine.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clinical death - cessation of blood circulation and breathing, marking the end of life
