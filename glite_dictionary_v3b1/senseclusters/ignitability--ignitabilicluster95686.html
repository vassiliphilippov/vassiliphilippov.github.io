ignitability

noun
ability to catch fire
8.1
Pronunciation: / ɪɡˌnaɪtəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ɪɡˌnaɪtəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the ability of a substance to catch fire or ignite easily

Examples

The ignitability of fabrics
The ignitability of certain fabrics makes them dangerous to use near open flames.
Scientists tested the ignitability of the new fuel to ensure it met safety standards.
Proper storage is required because the chemical's high ignitability poses a fire risk.

Related Terms

  • synonym: combustibility - the ability to burn or explode
  • synonym: flammability - the quality of being easily set on fire
  • synonym: inflammability - the ability to catch fire easily
  • antonym: nonflammability - the inability to catch fire easily
