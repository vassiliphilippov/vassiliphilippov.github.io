gall

noun
abnormal plant tissue swelling
8.3 8.8
Pronunciation: / ɡɔl / (AmE), / ɡɔːl / (BrE)
An abnormal growth or swelling of plant tissue, typically caused by insects, fungi, bacteria, or injury.

Examples

A plant gall
Scientists studied the formation of galls to understand plant-insect interactions.
Some galls can be mistaken for fruit, but they are actually abnormal tissue growths.
The oak tree was covered in galls caused by tiny wasps.
