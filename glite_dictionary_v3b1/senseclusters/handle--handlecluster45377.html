handle

verb
show or train an animal for competition
Pronunciation: / ˈhændl̩ / (AmE), / ˈhændl / (BrE)
to train and present (an animal, especially at a show or competition), often by managing its behavior and appearance

Examples

To handle a dog
He learned how to handle show dogs from a renowned breeder.
Mrs. Prescott skillfully handled her poodle during the prestigious dog show.
Only the most experienced trainers are allowed to handle the circus animals in public performances.

Related Terms

  • synonym: train - to teach a person or animal a particular skill or type of behavior through practice
