antituberculous

adjective
treating tuberculosis
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˌæntiˌtuːˈbɜːrkjʊləs / (AmE), / ˌæntiˌtjuːˈbɜːkjʊləs / (BrE)
used to prevent or treat tuberculosis

Examples

Antituberculous medicine
The patient was started on an antituberculous medication to fight the infection.
Doctors recommend antituberculous drugs for people diagnosed with active tuberculosis.
Long-term use of antituberculous therapy is often necessary to ensure the disease is cured.

Related Terms
