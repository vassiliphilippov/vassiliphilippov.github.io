fantasize

verb
imagine unreal or unlikely things
Pronunciation: / ˈfæn.təˌsaɪz /; / ˈfæntəˌsaɪz /; / ˈfæn.tə.saɪz / (AmE), / ˈfæn.tə.saɪz /; / ˈfæntəsaɪz / (BrE)
to imagine or think about things that are not real or very unlikely, often as a form of wishful thinking

Examples

He fantasizes that one day he will win the lottery.
It's normal to fantasize about success when starting a new job.

Many people fantasize about winning the lottery, even if they know it's unlikely.

As a child, she would often fantasise about becoming a famous singer.
People may fantasise about winning the lottery, even if they rarely buy tickets.
Sometimes I fantasise about moving to a tropical island and starting over.
