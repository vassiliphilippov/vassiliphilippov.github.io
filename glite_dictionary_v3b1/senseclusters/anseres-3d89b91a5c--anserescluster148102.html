Anseres

noun
former bird order; ducks, geese, swans
Pronunciation: / ˈænsəˌriːz / (AmE), / ˈænsəriːz / (BrE)
An order of birds, primarily comprising ducks, geese, and swans, used in some older biological classifications equivalent to the family Anatidae.

Examples

Order Anseres birds
In historical texts, waterfowl are sometimes grouped under the order Anseres.
Some ornithologists still use Anseres as a term for ducks, geese, and swans.
The classification of Anseres has changed over time as scientific understanding of birds evolved.
