inertial guidance

noun
navigation by measuring internal motion
7.9
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈɝːʃəl ˈɡaɪdəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈɜːʃəl ˈɡaɪdəns / (BrE)
a navigation method that controls the movement of an aircraft, missile, or spacecraft using instruments that measure acceleration and rotation without external references

Examples

Inertial guidance system
Advances in inertial guidance have enabled more accurate space missions.
Modern submarines often use inertial guidance systems when GPS signals are unavailable.
The missile relies on inertial guidance to maintain its trajectory after launch.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inertial navigation - navigation method using accelerometers and gyroscopes to calculate position
  • antonym: radio guidance - guidance system using radio signals to control movement
