cyanotype

noun
blue photographic print
9.1
Pronunciation: /ˈsaɪ.ə.noʊ.taɪp/ /; / /ˈsaɪ.æn.ə.taɪp/ (AmE), /ˈsaɪ.ə.nəʊ.taɪp/ /; / /ˈsaɪ.æn.əʊ.taɪp/ (BrE)
a photographic printing process that produces a blue image by using iron salts and exposure to light

Examples

Cyanotype process
The museum displayed a rare cyanotype of a 19th-century botanical specimen.
She learned how to make a cyanotype by placing objects on treated paper and exposing it to sunlight.
Many artists appreciate the unique blue tones produced by the cyanotype process.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blueprint - a detailed technical drawing, originally made using a cyanotype process
  • synonym: photograph - an image created by capturing light on a photosensitive surface
