Areopagus

noun
hill and council site in ancient Athens
Pronunciation: / ˌæriˈɑːpəɡəs / (AmE), / ˌærɪˈɒpəɡəs / (BrE)
a hill in Athens, west of the Acropolis, that was the meeting place of the highest governmental council and later a judicial court in ancient Athens

Examples

Areopagus hill
Philosophers like Socrates once defended themselves before the court of the Areopagus.
The ancient council met on the Areopagus to decide matters of justice and governance.
Visitors to Athens can still see the remains of the Areopagus today.
