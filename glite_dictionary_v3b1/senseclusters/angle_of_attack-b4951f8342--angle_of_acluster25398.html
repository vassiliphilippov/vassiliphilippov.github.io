angle of attack

noun
aeronautical: angle between airflow and wing chord
8.6 7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈæŋɡəl əv əˈtæk / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡl əv əˈtæk / (BrE)
the angle between the direction of the airflow and the chord line of an airfoil, commonly used in aeronautics to describe how air approaches a wing or blade.

Examples

High angle of attack
A high angle of attack can cause the wing to stall if exceeded.
Increasing the angle of attack increases lift, but only up to a certain point.
The pilot adjusted the angle of attack to maintain lift during takeoff.

Related Terms

  • synonym: angle of incidence - the angle between a reference line on a body and another line or axis, often used with airfoils
