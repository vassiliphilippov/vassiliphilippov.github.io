co-teach

verb
to teach jointly
3.8
Pronunciation: /koʊ.ˈtiːtʃ/ /; / /kə.ˈtiːtʃ/ (AmE), /kəʊ.ˈtiːtʃ/ /; / /kə.ˈtiːtʃ/ (BrE)
to teach a class together with another teacher, sharing responsibility for planning, instruction, or assessment

Examples

To co-teach a class
The two science teachers will co-teach the new environmental studies course this year.
In some classrooms, a general educator and a special educator co-teach to support all students.
They have been co-teaching for several semesters and have developed a strong partnership.

Related Terms
