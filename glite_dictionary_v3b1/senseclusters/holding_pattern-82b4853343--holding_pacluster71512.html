holding pattern

noun
state of inaction or waiting
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈhoʊldɪŋ ˌpætərn / (AmE), / ˈhəʊldɪŋ ˌpætn / (BrE)
A state or situation in which no progress is made and no decisions are taken, often while waiting for something to happen.

Examples

Company holding pattern
After the merger talks stalled, the company entered a holding pattern for several months.
Negotiations are in a holding pattern until both sides are ready to compromise.
We've been in a holding pattern waiting for the permits to be approved.

Related Terms

  • synonym: impasse - a situation in which no progress is possible
  • synonym: stalemate - a situation with no progress possible
