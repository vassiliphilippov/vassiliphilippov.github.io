heave

noun
an act of lifting with effort
5.5 0.24/m
Pronunciation: / hiv / (AmE), / hiÀêv / (BrE)
an act of lifting, pulling, or throwing something heavy with effort

Examples

A strong heave
It took several heaves to get the boat into the water.
She managed one final heave before the piano was in place.
With a mighty heave, the team lifted the heavy crate onto the truck.
He managed a slight heave of his shoulders to lift the box off the ground.
The workers gave the crate a final heave and slid it onto the truck.
With a mighty heave, they pushed the boat into the water.
After one final heave, the stone rolled over the edge.
He gave the rope a mighty heave and pulled the boat closer.
With a single heave, she lifted the box onto the truck.

Synonyms

  • lift
    1.0 1.08/m
    lift often means raising something upward, often smoothly or by machine, while heave implies strong effort, heavy pulling or throwing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hurl - to throw something with great force
  • synonym: lift - an act of lifting or raising something up
  • degree lower: toss - an act of throwing lightly or carelessly
