contig

adjective
connected without gaps
Pronunciation: / ˈkɒntɪɡ / (AmE), / ˈkɒntɪɡ / (BrE)
touching or connected without gaps, especially used in computing or genomics to describe sequences or blocks that are continuous

Examples

Contig regions
In genome assembly, a contig sequence is formed by overlapping DNA fragments.
The data must be stored in a contig block to ensure efficient access.
Engineers prefer contig memory regions for faster processing in some applications.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contiguous - sharing a common border or being in direct contact
  • antonym: disjoint - not connected or not touching
