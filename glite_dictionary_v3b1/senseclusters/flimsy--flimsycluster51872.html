flimsy

adjective
lacking strength or significance
4.6 1.05/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆflÉªmzi / (AmE), / ËˆflÉªmzi / (BrE)
weak or unconvincing; not persuasive or credible (of an excuse, argument, etc.)

Examples

A flimsy excuse
His flimsy excuses did not convince the teacher, who demanded a better explanation.
The jury found the prosecution's evidence far too flimsy to convict the defendant.
The story was entertaining, but the plot was rather flimsy and lacked depth.
Her explanation for being late was so flimsy that no one believed her.
The committee rejected the proposal because it was based on flimsy evidence.
The lawyer dismissed the accusation, calling it a flimsy attempt to undermine his client.

Synonyms

  • unconvincing
    5.1 1.33/m
    unconvincing fails to persuade people in argument or evidence, while flimsy suggests an excuse or idea that is weak and easy to doubt.
  • insubstantial
    6.8 1.03/m
    insubstantial lacks real substance or detail, while flimsy points to a weak or unconvincing excuse or argument.
  • tenuous
    7.6 3.58/m
    tenuous describes a very weak or slight connection or reason, while flimsy means an argument or excuse easy to challenge.
  • lame
    6.8 9.96/m
    lame sounds weak and disappointing, often used for excuses in casual talk, while flimsy is a more general word for an unconvincing argument.
  • thin
    5.2 14/m
    thin means lacking depth or substance and can describe weak arguments, while flimsy stresses that an excuse or claim is not convincing.
