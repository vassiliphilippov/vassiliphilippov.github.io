dreadnaught

noun
early 20th-century battleship with uniform large guns
Pronunciation: / ˈdrɛdˌnɑt / (AmE), / ˈdrɛd.nɔːt / (BrE)
a battleship of the early 20th century, characterized by an 'all-big-gun' armament of large-caliber guns of uniform size

Examples

A dreadnaught battleship
During the naval parade, a restored dreadnaught drew enthusiastic crowds.
Few dreadnaughts built before World War I have survived to the present day.
The launch of the dreadnaught changed naval warfare forever.

Related Terms

  • synonym: battleship - a large armored warship with a main battery consisting of heavy caliber guns
