tanged

adjective
with a projecting shank
Pronunciation: / tæŋd / (AmE), / tæŋd / (BrE)
having a projecting shank or tongue, especially designed to fit into a handle

Examples

Tanged blade handle
The craftsman preferred using tanged chisels because they fit securely into wooden handles.
A tanged blade is often stronger and more durable than a blade without a tang.
Many traditional swords are tanged to ensure the handle stays firmly attached.

Related Terms

  • antonym: untanged - lacking a projecting shank or tongue for fitting into a handle
