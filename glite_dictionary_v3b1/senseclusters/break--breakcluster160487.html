break

verb
cause someone to go bankrupt
1.0
Pronunciation: / breɪk / (AmE), / breɪk / (BrE)
to cause someone or something to fail, collapse, or suffer financial or organizational ruin

Examples

To break a company
Her lavish spending nearly break the company.
One bad investment was enough to completely break him financially.
The steep medical bills could break an average family.
Her dishonesty finally broke their friendship beyond repair.
One careless comment could break a promising career.
The scandal broke the politician's reputation overnight.
After the hearing, the committee decided to break him for repeated misconduct.
In some armies, an officer may be broken to a lower rank for insubordination.
The company threatened to break any manager who violated the code of conduct.
