Habakkuk

proper noun
Hebrew prophet; book of the Old Testament
9.9
Pronunciation: / həˈbækək / (AmE), / həˈbækək / (BrE)
A Hebrew prophet in the Old Testament, traditionally regarded as the author of the Book of Habakkuk, which includes prophecies about the Chaldeans.

Examples

Habakkuk the prophet
Habakkuk is considered one of the twelve minor prophets in the Old Testament.
At the seminar, the lecturer discussed the relevance of Habakkuk's message for modern times.
Many scholars study the Book of Habakkuk to understand ancient prophecies about the Chaldeans.
