order Pulmonata

noun
order of lung-breathing gastropods (snails and slugs)
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrdər ˌpʌlməˈneɪtə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌpʌlməˈneɪtə / (BrE)
a large order of gastropods, including most land snails and slugs and many freshwater snails, that usually breathe by means of a lung-like sac

Examples

Order Pulmonata snails
Members of the order Pulmonata are adapted to breathing air through a lung-like chamber.
Scientists have revised the classification of the order Pulmonata as new genetic information has emerged.
The order Pulmonata includes many of the terrestrial snails and slugs commonly found in gardens.
