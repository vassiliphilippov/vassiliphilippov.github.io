exophthalmic goiter

noun
goiter with bulging eyes (hyperthyroidism)
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɛk.soʊˈθæl.mɪk ˈɡɔɪ.tər / (AmE), / ˌɛk.sɒfˈθæl.mɪk ˈɡɔɪ.tə / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by swelling of the thyroid gland (goiter) and protrusion of the eyeballs due to hyperthyroidism.

Examples

Exophthalmic goiter symptoms
Doctors recognized exophthalmic goiter by the noticeable bulging of the eyes and enlarged thyroid gland.
The patient's exophthalmic goiter was treated with antithyroid medication and careful monitoring.
Untreated exophthalmic goiter can cause significant health complications, including vision problems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Graves' disease - an autoimmune disorder causing hyperthyroidism and often exophthalmic goiter
  • antonym: hypothyroidism - a condition with underactive thyroid gland
