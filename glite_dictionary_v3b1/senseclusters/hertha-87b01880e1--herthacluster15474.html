Hertha

proper noun
Teutonic goddess of fertility
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈhɜːr.θə/ (AmE), /ˈhɜː.θə/ (BrE)
In Teutonic mythology, the goddess of fertility, later identified with the Norse god Njord.

Examples

Hertha goddess
Archaeologists found offerings dedicated to Hertha near ancient Germanic settlements.
In some traditions, Hertha is considered equivalent to the Norse deity Njord.
Many ancient texts refer to Hertha as the mother of all life in Teutonic mythologies.

  • synonym: Njord - Norse god of the sea, wind, and fertility, with whom Hertha was later identified
