caudle

verb
to serve restorative drink
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkɔː.dəl/ (AmE), /ˈkɔː.dəl/ (BrE)
to give someone a caudle, a warm restorative drink, especially to comfort the sick or a new mother (archaic)

Examples

To caudle the sick
In olden times, nurses caudled patients with warm, spiced drinks to aid their recovery.
After childbirth, women were often caudled by family members to help restore their strength.
The kind neighbor caudled the sick child with a comforting beverage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: comfort - to make someone feel better or less worried
  • antonym: neglect - to fail to care for someone properly
