objectless

adjective
without direct object
Pronunciation: / ˈɑbdʒɪktləs / (AmE), / ˈɒbdʒɪktləs / (BrE)
not taking a direct object; in grammar, describing a verb, clause, or sentence that is intransitive

Examples

Objectless verb
In the sentence 'Birds fly,' the verb is objectless because it does not require an object.
Many verbs in English can be either transitive or objectless depending on the context.
The teacher explained that 'arrive' is always objectless in English grammar.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intransitive - describing a verb that does not take a direct object
  • antonym: transitive - describing a verb that takes a direct object
