sidedness

noun
property of sides
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪdɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdɪdnəs / (BrE)
the property of having a particular number or type of sides, or being oriented to one side

Examples

Molecule sidedness
The sidedness of a molecule affects its chemical behavior.
Some shapes, like triangles and squares, have different sidedness.
In biology, the sidedness of organs can determine their function in the body.
