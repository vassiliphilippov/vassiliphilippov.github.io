step to the fore

verb
come forward or take action
3.0 5.6
Pronunciation: / stɛp tə ðə fɔr / (AmE), / stɛp tə ðə fɔː / (BrE)
to make oneself prominent, take action, or become visible, especially by volunteering or assuming responsibility

Examples

To step to the fore quickly
During the crisis, several employees stepped to the fore to organize relief efforts.
It takes courage to step to the fore when others are hesitant to act.
When the manager resigned abruptly, Jane decided to step to the fore and take charge of the team.

Related Terms

  • antonym: stand back - to remain uninvolved or refrain from taking action
