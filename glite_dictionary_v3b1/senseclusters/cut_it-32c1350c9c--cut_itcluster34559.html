cut it

verb
stop or end something (informal)
7.2 4.15/m
Pronunciation: / kÊŒt Éªt / (AmE), / kÊŒt Éªt / (BrE)
(informal) To stop doing something or bring something to an end, especially when told to do so; to cease an activity.

Examples

Please cut it now
I wish you would cut it and listen to what I'm saying.
If you don't cut it, we're going to have to leave.
The teacher told the class to cut it when they wouldn't stop talking.

Synonyms

  • knock off
    7.4 0.08/m
    knock off tells someone to stop an annoying action now, while cut it is an informal order to stop immediately.
  • lay off
    3.4 2.58/m
    lay off asks someone to stop bothering or criticizing another person, while cut it is a firmer, direct command to stop.
  • leave off
    4.1 3.92/m
    leave off means pause or stop at a certain point, while cut it is an informal command to stop an action now.
  • call it quits
    3.9 1.09/m
    call it quits means agree to stop after trying enough or when tired, while cut it is a quick order to stop.
  • wind up
    3.7 0.62/m
    wind up means finish or bring an event to a planned end, while cut it is an informal command to stop someone immediately.
