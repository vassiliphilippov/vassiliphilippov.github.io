saleability

noun
ease of selling
5.5
Pronunciation: /seɪ.lə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tɪ/ /; / /seɪ.lə.ˈbɪl.ə.tɪ/ (AmE), /seɪ.lə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tɪ/ /; / /seɪ.lə.ˈbɪl.ə.tɪ/ (BrE)
the quality of being easy to sell or likely to be bought

Examples

Saleability of a house
The saleability of a property often depends on its location and condition.
High saleability makes these products attractive to investors looking for quick returns.
Before launching a new product, companies assess its saleability in different markets.
The saleability of the house increased after the renovations were completed.
Designers consider saleability when creating products for a competitive market.
Advertising campaigns can boost the saleability of new technology gadgets.

  • antonym: illiquidity - the difficulty of selling an asset quickly without loss of value
  • synonym: liquidity - the ability to quickly convert an asset into cash
  • synonym: marketability - the quality of being easy to sell in a market
  • antonym: unmarketability - the quality of being difficult or impossible to sell
