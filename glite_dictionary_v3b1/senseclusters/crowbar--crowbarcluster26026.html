crowbar

noun
heavy lever tool for prying
2.9 1.58/m
Pronunciation: / Ààkro äÀåb…ër / (AmE), / Ààkr…ô äÀåb…ëÀê / (BrE)
a heavy iron or steel bar with a curved end that is used as a lever for prying things apart or pulling out nails

Examples

A crowbar tool
He used a crowbar to pry open the old wooden door.
The burglar broke in by forcing the window with a crowbar.
Workers often keep a crowbar in the truck for demolition jobs.

Synonyms

  • pry bar
    4.8 0.18/m
    a pry bar has a wedge-shaped end used to force things apart, while a crowbar usually has a curved end for prying.
  • ripping bar
    8.3 0.01/m
    a ripping bar has a chisel end and a curved claw for heavy prying or splitting, while a crowbar is simpler.
  • wrecking bar
    6.5 0.02/m
    a wrecking bar is heavier with a flattened or forked end for demolition work, while a crowbar is for general prying and nail pulling.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lever - a rigid bar used to exert force
