anti-Semite

noun
person hostile to Jews
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.tiˈsɛm.aɪt /; / ˌæn.tiˈsɛ.maɪt / (AmE), / ˌæn.tiˈsɛm.aɪt /; / ˌæntiˈsɛmaɪt / (BrE)
a person who holds hostile or prejudiced views against Jewish people

Examples

A known anti-Semite
History remembers many leaders who were notorious anti-Semites.
She spoke out against an anti-Semite who was spreading conspiracy theories.
The newspaper condemned the anti-Semite for his hateful rhetoric.
He was labeled an anti-Semite after making several offensive statements about Jewish communities.
The government condemned any anti-Semite actions or rhetoric in the strongest terms.
Throughout history, being called an anti-Semite has carried significant social and political consequences.

  • synonym: antisemitism - hostility or prejudice against Jews
  • antonym: philo-Semite - a person who admires or supports Jewish people
