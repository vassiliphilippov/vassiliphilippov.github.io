reabsorb

verb
be absorbed again, especially biologically
Pronunciation: / riˈæb.zɔrb / (AmE), / riːˈab.zɔːb / (BrE)
(of a substance or fluid) to be absorbed again after having been previously released or lost, especially in a biological or physiological context.

Examples

To reabsorb body fluid
After a bone fracture, some bone tissue may reabsorb as part of the healing process.
During dehydration, the kidneys reabsorb more water to maintain fluid balance.
Plants can reabsorb nutrients from leaves before they are shed in autumn.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absorb - to take in a substance or energy
