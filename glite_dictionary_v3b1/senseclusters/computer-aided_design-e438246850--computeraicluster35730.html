computer-aided design

noun
use of computers for design work
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpjuːtər ˌeɪdɪd dɪˈzaɪn / (AmE), / kəmˈpjuːtə ˌeɪdɪd dɪˈzaɪn / (BrE)
the use of computer software to assist in creating, modifying, analyzing, or optimizing a design, especially in engineering, architecture, and manufacturing

Examples

Computer-aided design software
Architects now rely heavily on computer-aided design to create precise building plans.
Many manufacturing companies invest in computer-aided design software to speed up product development.
Thanks to computer-aided design, errors in blueprints can often be corrected before construction begins.
