pre-teach

verb
to teach in advance
3.8
Pronunciation: /priː.ˈtiːtʃ/ /; / /prə.ˈtiːtʃ/ (AmE), /priː.ˈtiːtʃ/ /; / /prə.ˈtiːtʃ/ (BrE)
to teach or introduce material to learners before the main lesson or activity begins

Examples

To pre-teach vocabulary
The teacher will pre-teach key vocabulary before starting the reading activity.
It is helpful to pre-teach difficult concepts so students are not confused during the lesson.
Some tutors pre-teach grammar rules to prepare learners for upcoming topics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: preview - to show or introduce something before it is fully presented
  • antonym: review - to go over material again after it has been taught
