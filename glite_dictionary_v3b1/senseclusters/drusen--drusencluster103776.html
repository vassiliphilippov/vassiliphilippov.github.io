drusen

noun
retinal deposits in the eye
Pronunciation: / ˈdruːzən / (AmE), / ˈdruːzən / (BrE)
Small yellow or white accumulations of extracellular material under the retina, often associated with aging or certain eye diseases.

Examples

Yellow drusen spots
A buildup of drusen can affect vision and may require regular monitoring.
Age-related macular degeneration is often associated with the presence of drusen.
The doctor noticed several drusen during the eye examination.
