posturing

noun
behaving to impress; not sincere
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑstʃərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɒstʃərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of behaving in a way that is intended to impress or attract attention rather than being sincere

Examples

His posturing during meeting
His posturing during the meeting made it clear he wanted to stand out.
Instead of posturing, she decided to speak honestly about her experience.
The politician's constant posturing annoyed the public and distracted from real issues.

Related Terms

  • synonym: posing - adopting a particular position, often to impress others
  • antonym: sincerity - the absence of pretense or deceit; genuine behavior
