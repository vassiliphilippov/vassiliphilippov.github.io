Babbitt metal

noun
an alloy used for lining bearings
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈbæbɪt ˌmɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ˈbæbɪt ˌmɛtl̩ / (BrE)
an alloy of tin with copper and antimony, used chiefly as a lining material for bearings to reduce friction

Examples

Babbitt metal alloy
Engineers often choose Babbitt metal for its low friction and durability in heavy machinery.
Industrial repair specialists replaced the worn lining with fresh Babbitt metal.
The machine's bearings were lined with Babbitt metal to ensure smooth operation.
