preexperimental

adjective
before testing
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˌɛkspəˌrɪmɛnˈtæl / (AmE), / ˌpriːˌɛkspəˌrɪmɛnˈtæl / (BrE)
existing or happening before an experiment is conducted, such as ideas, data, or conditions

Examples

Preexperimental ideas
The scientist's preexperimental ideas guided the design of the first study.
Any preexperimental assumptions must be clearly stated before beginning the research.
Her model was still preexperimental and had not yet been tested in the lab.
Researchers collected preexperimental data to establish a baseline for their study.
The preexperimental conditions were carefully controlled to ensure accurate results.
All participants completed a survey during the preexperimental phase.

Related Terms

  • antonym: experimental - based on or relating to scientific testing or experiments
  • synonym: hypothetical - based on a suggested idea or theory, not yet proven
  • antonym: postexperimental - relating to the period after an experiment is conducted
  • synonym: preliminary - coming before the main event or activity
