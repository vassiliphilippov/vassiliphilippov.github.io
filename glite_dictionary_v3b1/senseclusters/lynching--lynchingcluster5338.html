lynching

noun
figurative public shaming or cancellation
9.1 0.5/m
Pronunciation: / \u02c8l\u026ant\u0283\u026a\u014b / (AmE), / \u02c8l\u026ant\u0283\u026a\u014b / (BrE)
a figurative use meaning a very public, often aggressive condemnation, shaming, or 'cancellation' of a person by a group (especially online), carried out without fair process.

Examples

Online lynching
The online lynching of the celebrity prompted a public apology and platform bans.
After one angry tweet, the politician faced a virtual lynching that destroyed his reputation overnight.
Editors debated whether the harsh column amounted to a journalistic lynching rather than fair criticism.

Synonyms

    8.4 0.5/m

  • denouncement

Related Terms

  • synonym: public shaming - the act of criticizing or humiliating someone publicly, especially online
