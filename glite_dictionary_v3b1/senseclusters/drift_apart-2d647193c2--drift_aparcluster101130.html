drift apart

verb
gradually lose closeness or contact
4.7 3.1
Pronunciation: / drɪft əˈpɑrt / (AmE), / drɪft əˈpɑːt / (BrE)
(of people) to gradually become less close or lose contact with each other over time

Examples

Friends drift apart
After college, the two friends drifted apart and rarely saw each other again.
Despite living in the same city, they found themselves drifting apart as their interests changed.
Over the years, we have slowly drifted apart due to our different lifestyles.
After they went to different universities, they began to drift apart and lost touch.
Many old friends drift apart when their lives take different directions.
The siblings realized they had drifted apart over the years, seeing each other only at holidays.
