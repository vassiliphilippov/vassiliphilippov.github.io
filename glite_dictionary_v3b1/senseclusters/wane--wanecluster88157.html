wane

verb
to decrease or diminish
Pronunciation: / weɪn / (AmE), / weɪn / (BrE)
to decrease or become weaker in size, amount, strength, or intensity

Examples

Energy wanes
During the winter, daylight hours wane and darkness sets in earlier each evening.
His enthusiasm for the project waned after several setbacks.
Support for the proposed law has begun to wane sharply in recent weeks.
As the night progressed, her energy gradually waned.
His influence over the company is starting to wane.
Public enthusiasm for the campaign began to wane after several months.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diminish - to become or make smaller or less
  • antonym: increase - to become or make greater in size, amount, or degree
