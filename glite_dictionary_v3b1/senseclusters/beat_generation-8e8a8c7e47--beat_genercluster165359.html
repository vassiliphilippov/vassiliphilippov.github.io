Beat Generation

noun
American literary and cultural movement of the 1950s
8.0
Pronunciation: /biːt dʒen.ə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /biːt dʒen.ə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
A group of American writers and artists in the 1950s and early 1960s, and the youth subculture associated with them, known for rejecting conventional societal values and advocating spontaneous creativity, communal living, and experimentation with drugs and new forms of expression.

Examples

The Beat Generation writers
Jack Kerouac and Allen Ginsberg were prominent figures in the Beat Generation.
Members of the Beat Generation sought spiritual liberation and artistic creativity above all else.
The influence of the Beat Generation can still be felt in literature and pop culture today.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conformist - person who accepts and follows traditional standards or rules
  • degree higher: hippie - member of the 1960s counterculture, also rejecting mainstream values
