underbridge

noun
bridge beneath another
5.4
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndərˌbrɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌbrɪdʒ / (BrE)
a bridge that passes underneath another road, railway, or structure, allowing traffic to cross below

Examples

An underbridge below road
The old stone underbridge allowed the river to flow beneath the busy highway.
Engineers inspected the underbridge for signs of wear after the heavy rains.
A new underbridge was constructed to let pedestrians safely cross under the railway tracks.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overpass - a bridge that carries a road or railway over another
  • synonym: viaduct - a long bridge carrying a road or railway over a valley or other roads
