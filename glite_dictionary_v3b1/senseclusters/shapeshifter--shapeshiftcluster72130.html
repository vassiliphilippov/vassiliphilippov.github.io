shapeshifter

noun
person who adapts identity
Pronunciation: / ˈʃeɪpˌʃɪftər / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪpˌʃɪftə / (BrE)
a person who frequently changes their opinions, loyalties, or public identity to fit different situations

Examples

A political shapeshifter
The senator was called a shapeshifter after changing his stance on several key issues.
In the business world, being a shapeshifter can help you survive constant change.
Her reputation as a political shapeshifter made it hard for voters to trust her promises.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chameleon - a person who changes their behavior or opinions to suit different situations
  • synonym: opportunist - a person who takes advantage of situations for personal gain
  • antonym: steadfast - firm and unwavering in beliefs or loyalty
