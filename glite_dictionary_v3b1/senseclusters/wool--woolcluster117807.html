wool

verb
to shear sheep
Pronunciation: / wʊl / (AmE), / wʊl / (BrE)
to remove wool from sheep or other animals by shearing, or to gather wool.

Examples

To wool a sheep
Every spring, the farmers wooled their sheep to collect fresh fleece.
The workers wooled dozens of animals before the market opened.
After they wooled the flock, the barn was filled with soft, white fibers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: harvest - to gather a crop or resource
  • synonym: shear - to cut the wool off a sheep
