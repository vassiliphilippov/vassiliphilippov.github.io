adapid

noun
extinct group of early primates
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈæ.də.pɪd/ (AmE), /ˈæ.də.pɪd/ (BrE)
an extinct group of small, mostly diurnal primates that lived in North America and Europe 30 to 50 million years ago, believed to be ancestors or relatives of lemurs and possibly early anthropoids

Examples

Fossil adapids
Fossil evidence of adapids has helped scientists understand primate evolution.
Some researchers think adapids are related to modern lemurs.
The diet of adapids likely included fruit and leaves.
