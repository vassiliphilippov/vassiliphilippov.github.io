self-restraint

noun
control of one's own impulses or desires

The ability to control one's own emotions, impulses, or desires; restraint imposed on oneself.

Examples

Strong self-restraint
Her self-restraint in refusing the tempting offer was admirable.
It took a great deal of self-restraint for him not to shout back during the argument.
Maintaining self-restraint is essential when dealing with difficult situations.

Synonyms

  • self-control
    3.3 4.82/m
    self-control is an immediate inner ability to resist urges, while self-restraint names the general habit of holding back feelings.
  • restraint
    4.8 7.09/m
    restraint is a general act of holding back in behavior or speech, while self-restraint is that holding back done by oneself.
  • self-discipline
    4.4 1.53/m
    self-discipline is steady training to control habits and feelings, while self-restraint is the immediate act of stopping an impulse.
  • self-command
    7.0 0.02/m
    self-command is a firm, sometimes formal control shown with strong will, while self-restraint is more everyday holding back.
  • temperance
    7.1 0.89/m
    temperance is about avoiding excess, often with food or drink, while self-restraint covers control of many emotions or impulses.
  • temperateness
    7.5 0.00/m
    temperateness is a formal quality of moderation in general life, while self-restraint is a personal skill of holding back impulses.

Related Terms

  • antonym: impulsiveness - tendency to act on impulse rather than restraint
  • synonym: self-control - control of one's own emotions and actions
