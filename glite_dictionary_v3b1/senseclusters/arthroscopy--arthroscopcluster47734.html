arthroscopy

noun
minimally invasive joint surgery
8.8
Pronunciation: / ɑːrˈθrɑːskəpi / (AmE), / ɑːˈθrɒskəpi / (BrE)
a minimally invasive surgical procedure to examine and treat the interior of a joint using an arthroscope inserted through a small incision

Examples

An arthroscopy procedure
After undergoing arthroscopy, she recovered much faster than she expected.
Shoulder arthroscopy often results in shorter hospital stays and less scarring.
The doctor recommended arthroscopy to diagnose the cause of my knee pain.
