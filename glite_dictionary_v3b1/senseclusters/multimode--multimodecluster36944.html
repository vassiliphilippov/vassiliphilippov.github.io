multimode

noun
optical fiber type
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌltiˌmoʊd / (AmE), / ˈmʌltiˌməʊd / (BrE)
a type of optical fiber that transmits light in multiple modes, commonly used for short-distance data communication

Examples

Multimode fiber
The technician replaced the old cable with a new multimode to improve the building's network.
For short-distance data transmission, a multimode is often more cost-effective than other fiber types.
The IT team ordered several multimode cables for the new office installation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: singlemode - an optical fiber that transmits light in a single mode
