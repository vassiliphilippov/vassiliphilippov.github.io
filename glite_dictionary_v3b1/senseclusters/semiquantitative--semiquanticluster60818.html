semiquantitative

adjective
partly quantitative
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmiˌkwɑntɪˈteɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˌkwɒntɪˈteɪtɪv / (BrE)
describing a measurement or result that is partly but not fully quantitative, giving approximate rather than exact values

Examples

A semiquantitative method
The laboratory used a semiquantitative method to estimate the concentration of the chemical.
Doctors rely on semiquantitative tests when precise measurements are not necessary.
A semiquantitative analysis can help identify trends without providing exact numbers.
