fallout

noun
adverse consequence or side effect
6.7 4.55/m
Pronunciation: / Ààf…îlÀåa ät / (AmE), / Ààf…îÀêl.a ät / (BrE)
an adverse or unintended consequence of an event, especially one causing difficulties or problems

Examples

Political fallout
Politicians debated how to manage the fallout of the controversial policy change.
The fallout from the company's bankruptcy affected thousands of employees and investors.
There was significant economic fallout after the rapid rise in oil prices.

Synonyms

  • repercussion
    6.7 5.01/m
    repercussion is an indirect consequence that often appears later and causes further problems, while fallout is the harmful result after an event.
  • ramification
    8.1 5.34/m
    ramification is a consequence that spreads into many parts and makes things more complex, while fallout is an adverse result that causes practical problems.
  • side effect
    3.3 14/m
    side effect is an unintended effect often used for drugs or treatments, while fallout is an adverse consequence of an event.

Related Terms

  • antonym: benefit - an advantage or profit gained from something
  • synonym: consequence - a result or effect of an action or condition
  • synonym: repercussion - an unintended consequence, often unwelcome, of an event or action
