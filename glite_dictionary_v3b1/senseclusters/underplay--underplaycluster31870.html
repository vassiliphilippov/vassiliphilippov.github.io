underplay

verb
to downplay importance
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈpleɪ / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈpleɪ / (BrE)
to make something seem less important, serious, or significant than it really is

Examples

To underplay a problem
The spokesperson tried to underplay the impact of the new policy during the press conference.
He tends to underplay his role in the team's success, even though he was crucial.
Critics argued that the report underplayed the seriousness of the environmental damage.
The spokesperson tried to underplay the impact of the new policy on employees.
She tends to underplay her achievements when talking to others.
It is not wise to underplay the risks involved in this project.
He tried to underplay the risks involved in the investment.
The coach did not underplay the team's achievements after the big win.
She tends to underplay her own contributions during group projects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: downplay - to make something appear less important or significant
  • antonym: emphasize - to give special importance or attention to something
  • antonym: exaggerate - to make something seem more important or serious than it is
