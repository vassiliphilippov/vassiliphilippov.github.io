monitor

noun
large carnivorous tropical lizard
2.8 7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑː.nɪ.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈmɒn.ɪ.tə / (BrE)
A large tropical carnivorous lizard of the family Varanidae, native to Africa, Asia, and Australia, some species of which are fabled to warn of crocodiles.

Examples

Monitor lizard
A monitor lizard was spotted sunbathing on the rocks outside the village.
Many villagers believe the monitor gives warnings when crocodiles are near the river.
The monitor can grow up to three meters in length and is highly territorial.

Related Terms

  • synonym: varanid - any lizard of the family Varanidae, to which monitor lizards belong
