moralistically

adverb
from a moral viewpoint
Pronunciation: / ˌmɔːrəlˈɪstɪkli / (AmE), / ˌmɒrəlˈɪstɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that judges or focuses on moral principles or ideas of right and wrong

Examples

To speak moralistically
The policy was criticized for being moralistically rigid and ignoring real-world challenges.
She spoke moralistically about the importance of honesty in business.
Debates about the law are often framed moralistically rather than practically.
She answered the question moralistically, focusing on what she believed was right and wrong.
The politician often speaks moralistically about the need for honesty in government.
Instead of offering practical advice, he responded moralistically to the student's concerns.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ethically - in a way that relates to moral principles
  • antonym: objectively - in a way that is unbiased and not influenced by personal feelings or opinions
  • antonym: pragmatically - in a way that is practical rather than based on ideals
  • synonym: preachily - in a way that is overly moralizing or sermon-like
