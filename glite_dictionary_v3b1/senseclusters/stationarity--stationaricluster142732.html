stationarity

noun
unchanging statistical properties
8.9
Pronunciation: /steɪ.ʃə.ˈnæ.rə.tɪ/ (AmE), /steɪ.ʃə.ˈnæ.rə.tɪ/ (BrE)
the quality of a process or system whose statistical properties remain constant over time

Examples

Stationarity in data
The economist tested the stationarity of the data before building the forecast model.
Understanding stationarity is essential when analyzing time series in statistics.
If a time series lacks stationarity, its mean and variance may change over time.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nonstationarity - the quality of a process whose statistical properties change over time
  • synonym: stability - the state of being steady and not changing
