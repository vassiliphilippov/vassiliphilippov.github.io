gouge

verb
force out or remove with thumb/object

to make a deep groove, hole, or remove something by force, especially with a sharp object or the thumb

Examples

To gouge a hole
He tried to gouge the stone out of the ground with his thumb.
She had to gouge the old gum from under the desk.
The thief attempted to gouge out the painting's eyes in anger.
If you gouge the wall, you'll have to fill the mark before painting.
Someone used a knife to gouge a deep line into the wooden table.
Years of flooding have gouged a channel through the rocky hillside.

Synonyms

  • trench
    6.5 0.01/m
    trench cuts deeply into a surface to make a channel, while gouge removes material forcefully, often leaving a hole.
  • rout
    8.9 0.58/m
    rout cuts a groove or channel using a router or tool, while gouge usually means digging out material by force.
  • groove
    5.1 0.39/m
    groove makes a long narrow channel, often shallow and neat, while gouge means making a deep, rough hole by force.
