vitamin K

noun
fat-soluble vitamin essential for blood clotting
Pronunciation: / ˈvaɪtəmɪn keɪ / (AmE), / ˈvɪtəmɪn keɪ / (BrE)
a fat-soluble vitamin essential for blood clotting and bone health

Examples

Green leafy vitamin K
Green leafy vegetables are a good source of vitamin K in the diet.
Newborns are often given a vitamin K injection to prevent bleeding disorders.
The doctor prescribed a vitamin K supplement to help improve blood clotting.
