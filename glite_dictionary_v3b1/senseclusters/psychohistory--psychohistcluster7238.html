psychohistory

noun
predicting group behavior
9.9
Pronunciation: /saɪ.koʊ.ˈhɪs.tər.iː/ (AmE), /saɪ.kəʊ.ˈhɪs.tə.riː/ (BrE)
a theoretical science that uses mathematics and psychology to predict the future behavior of large groups of people

Examples

Fictional psychohistory predictions
In science fiction, psychohistory is imagined as a way to forecast the rise and fall of empires.
The concept of psychohistory combines psychology and statistics to analyze societal trends.
Many readers are fascinated by the idea of psychohistory as described in Asimov's books.
