wedge

verb
force or squeeze into a tight space
6.7 4.9
Pronunciation: / wɛdʒ / (AmE), / wɛdʒ / (BrE)
to force or fit something or someone tightly into a narrow or tight space

Examples

To wedge a door
He tried to wedge himself between the closing doors of the elevator.
She managed to wedge her suitcase into the crowded overhead compartment.
The cat wedged itself behind the sofa to hide from the noise.
He wedged the door open with a stack of books.
She wedged her suitcase under the seat in front of her.
The mechanic wedged a piece of wood between the tire and the curb.

  • synonym: jam - to push or squeeze tightly into a space
