Cherenkov

noun
blue light from fast particles
9.9
Pronunciation: /tʃə.ˈren.kɑːv/ /; / /tʃə.ˈren.kɑːf/ (AmE), /tʃə.ˈren.kɒv/ /; / /tʃə.ˈren.kɒf/ (BrE)
a faint blue light produced when a charged particle moves through a medium faster than the speed of light in that medium

Examples

Cherenkov radiation glow
The scientists observed a bright Cherenkov glow in the reactor pool.
Special detectors use Cherenkov light to identify high-speed particles.
The blue Cherenkov effect is often seen in nuclear reactors under water.

  • synonym: radiation - energy emitted as waves or particles
