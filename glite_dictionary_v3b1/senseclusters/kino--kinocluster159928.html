kino

noun
tree yielding medicinal resin
4.6 8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈkiːnoʊ / (AmE), / ˈkiːnəʊ / (BrE)
An East Indian tree (such as Pterocarpus marsupium) that yields a reddish resin often used medicinally or in tanning.

Examples

A kino tree
Tanners use kino extract for its astringent properties in leather processing.
The bark of the kino tree is a valuable source of medicinal resin.
Traditional healers sometimes prescribe kino for treating diarrhea and other ailments.
