monetary standard

noun
basis for the value of money
5.2 4.9
Pronunciation: /ˈmɑː.nə.tər.iː ˈstæn.dərd/ /; / /ˈmʌn.ə.tər.iː ˈstæn.dərd/ (AmE), /ˈmʌn.ɪ.tri ˈstændəd/ /; / /ˈmʌn.ɪ.tə.riː ˈstændəd/ (BrE)
A system or principle that determines the value of money in a country's monetary system, such as the gold standard or fiat standard.

Examples

The monetary standard system
Inflation can be influenced by the type of monetary standard a nation adopts.
Many countries abandoned the gold monetary standard in the twentieth century.
The shift to a fiat monetary standard changed how people view paper currency.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fiat money - money without intrinsic value established as legal tender by government decree
  • synonym: gold standard - a monetary system where currency value is backed by gold
