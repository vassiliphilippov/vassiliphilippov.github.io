strip

verb
remove clothes from self or others
Pronunciation: / strɪp / (AmE), / strɪp / (BrE)
to remove all or most of your clothes, or someone else's clothes

Examples

To strip off clothes

She stripped quickly and jumped into the pool.
The children stripped off their muddy clothes after playing outside.
He refused to strip for the medical examination.
Some people feel embarrassed to strip in public changing rooms.
The performer began to strip as the music played.
