voluntarily

adverb
by one's own free will
3.4 9.42/m
Pronunciation: / Àåv…ëÀêl…ônÀàt…õr…ôli / (AmE), / Àåv…íl.…ônÀàte…ô.r…™.li / (BrE)
In a way that is done by one's own free will, without being forced or coerced.

Examples

To voluntarily help others
He voluntarily submitted to the fingerprinting process as part of the investigation.
She voluntarily donated her time to help the community center.
The company CEO voluntarily reduced his salary during the financial crisis.

Synonyms

  • willingly
    1.9 7.01/m
    willingly is done with happy readiness to help, while voluntarily means doing something by free choice, without force.
  • by choice
    3.1 1.64/m
    by choice is used when someone decides intentionally, while voluntarily emphasizes acting from one's own free will without coercion.
  • volitionally
    8.4 0.03/m
    volitionally describes actions done by conscious will in technical or formal contexts, while voluntarily is a general everyday choice without force.
  • freely
    1.0 21/m
    freely means with no limits or without cost, while voluntarily focuses on choosing to act without being forced.

Related Terms

  • antonym: involuntarily - in a manner done without one's will; not by choice
  • synonym: willingly - in a willing manner, without reluctance
