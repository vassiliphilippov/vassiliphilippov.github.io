ipecac

noun
drug formerly used to induce vomiting
Pronunciation: / ˈɪpɪˌkæk / (AmE), / ˈɪpɪˌkæk / (BrE)
a medicinal substance obtained from the roots of certain South American plants, formerly used to induce vomiting, especially in cases of poisoning or overdose.

Examples

The ipecac syrup
After accidentally ingesting poison, the child was given ipecac to induce vomiting.
Old medical kits often contained a bottle of ipecac for use in emergencies.
The doctor recommended that parents no longer keep ipecac syrup at home, as guidelines had changed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emetic - a substance that causes vomiting
