ipecac

noun
drug formerly used to induce vomiting
8.6 8.5
Pronunciation: /ˈɪp.ə.kæk/ /; / /ˈɪp.ɪ.kæk/ (AmE), /ˈɪp.ə.kæk/ /; / /ˈɪp.ɪ.kæk/ (BrE)
a medicinal substance obtained from the roots of certain South American plants, formerly used to induce vomiting, especially in cases of poisoning or overdose.

Examples

The ipecac syrup

Old medical kits often contained a bottle of ipecac for use in emergencies.
