delta ray

noun
electron ejected by ionizing radiation
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛltə reɪ / (AmE), / ˈdɛltə reɪ / (BrE)
an electron that is emitted from an atom or molecule as a result of ionizing radiation, especially in the form of a high-speed secondary electron ejected by a passing charged particle

Examples

High-speed delta rays
Physicists often study delta rays to understand the behavior of ionizing radiation in different materials.
The track of a delta ray can be seen radiating from the path of the alpha particle in the detector.
When high-energy gamma rays interact with matter, they can produce a delta ray.

Related Terms

  • synonym: secondary electron - an electron ejected from an atom due to an incident energetic particle
