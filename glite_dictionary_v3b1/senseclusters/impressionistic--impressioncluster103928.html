impressionistic

adjective
based on impressions, not facts
6.4 6.5
Pronunciation: /ɪm.pre.ʃən.ˈɪs.tɪk/ (AmE), /ɪm.pre.ʃən.ˈɪs.tɪk/ (BrE)
based on subjective impressions rather than on objective facts or detailed analysis; giving a general sense or feeling rather than precise details

Examples

Impressionistic memories
Her impressionistic memories of early childhood are vivid but not always accurate.
The artist's impressionistic style captured the energy of the city rather than its precise architecture.
The critic wrote an impressionistic account of the play, focusing on mood rather than plot details.
