Amaranthus cruentus

noun

Pronunciation: /æ.mə.ˈræn.θəs kruː.ˈen.təs/ /; / /ə.mə.ˈræn.θəs kruː.ˈen.təs/ (AmE), /æ.mə.ˈræn.θəs kruː.ˈen.təs/ /; / /ə.mə.ˈræn.θəs kruː.ˈen.təs/ (BrE)
A tall, ornamental tropical American annual plant with hairy stems and long spikes of usually red flowers above purplish leaves, whose seeds are often used as a cereal.

Examples

Amaranthus cruentus plant
Amaranthus cruentus is often cultivated for its striking red flower spikes and nutritious seeds.
Farmers in several countries grow Amaranthus cruentus both as a grain and as an ornamental plant.
The leaves of Amaranthus cruentus are sometimes eaten as a leafy vegetable in traditional dishes.
