bisphosphonate

noun
bone density drug
Pronunciation: / baɪsˈfɑsfəˌneɪt / (AmE), / bɪsˈfɒsfəneɪt / (BrE)
a type of medicine or drug used to slow bone loss and strengthen bones, often prescribed for osteoporosis

Examples

Bisphosphonate medicine for bones
The doctor prescribed a bisphosphonate to help strengthen her bones.
Patients with osteoporosis often take a bisphosphonate to reduce the risk of fractures.
Long-term use of a bisphosphonate should be monitored by a healthcare professional.
Doctors often prescribe bisphosphonate medications to help prevent fractures in patients with osteoporosis.
A bisphosphonate can reduce the risk of bone loss after menopause.
She takes a bisphosphonate once a week as part of her treatment plan.

Related Terms

  • synonym: calcitonin - a hormone used as a medication to treat osteoporosis
  • synonym: osteoporosis - a medical condition in which bones become weak and brittle
  • synonym: osteoporosis medication - a drug used to treat or prevent osteoporosis
