semiclassical

adjective
classical and quantum
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmɪˈklæsɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌsɛmɪˈklæsɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to an approach that combines elements of classical and quantum theories, especially in physics

Examples

Semiclassical physics method
The semiclassical method is often used to approximate solutions in quantum mechanics.
Researchers developed a semiclassical model to bridge the gap between old and new theories.
In some cases, a semiclassical approach provides accurate predictions without full quantum calculations.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: classical - relating to traditional physics theories before quantum mechanics
  • degree higher: quantum - relating to the theory of quantum mechanics
