textualist

adjective
literal text-focused
8.4
Pronunciation: /ˈteks.tʃʊ.əl.ɪst/ /; / /ˈteks.tjʊ.əl.ɪst/ (AmE), /ˈteks.tʃʊ.əl.ɪst/ /; / /ˈteks.tjʊ.əl.ɪst/ (BrE)
emphasizing the literal meaning of the text itself, rather than considering external context or intent

Examples

Textualist approach
The judge took a textualist approach, focusing strictly on the wording of the law.
Her textualist reading of the novel ignored the author's personal history.
Some critics argue that a textualist interpretation can miss deeper meanings in literature.

Related Terms

  • synonym: literal - taking words in their usual or most basic sense
