sepsis

noun
life-threatening infection in the blood
2.7
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛp.sɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɛp.sɪs / (BrE)
A serious medical condition in which the presence of harmful bacteria or their toxins in the blood or tissues causes widespread inflammation and, if untreated, can be life-threatening.

Examples

Sepsis infection
Doctors worked quickly to identify the source of sepsis in the critically ill infant.
Early recognition of sepsis is crucial to improving survival rates.
The patient developed sepsis after surgery and required immediate treatment.
