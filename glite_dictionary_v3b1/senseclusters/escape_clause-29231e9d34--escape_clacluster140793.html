escape clause

noun
contractual provision for withdrawal or exemption
4.9 4.4
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈskeɪp klɔːz/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈskeɪp klɔːz/ (BrE)
a provision in a contract that allows one or both parties to withdraw from the agreement or avoid certain obligations under specified circumstances

Examples

An escape clause in contract
After the merger talks stalled, the company triggered the escape clause to exit the deal.
She was relieved to find an escape clause that let her cancel the contract without a penalty.
The rental agreement included an escape clause for tenants in case of job relocation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: force majeure - a legal clause excusing parties from obligations due to extraordinary events
