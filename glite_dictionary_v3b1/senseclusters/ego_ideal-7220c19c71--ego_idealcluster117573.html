ego ideal

noun
psychoanalytic standard of personal excellence
4.5
Pronunciation: / ˈiːɡoʊ aɪˈdiːəl / (AmE), / ˈiːɡəʊ aɪˈdɪəl / (BrE)
(psychoanalysis) the part of the ego that represents an internal standard of personal excellence toward which a person aspires.

Examples

The ego ideal in psychology
According to Freud, the ego ideal develops during childhood from parental and societal expectations.
She struggled when her actual achievements did not match her ego ideal.
The ego ideal motivates individuals to strive for their highest personal standards.

Related Terms

  • synonym: superego - the part of a person's mind that acts as a self-critical conscience
