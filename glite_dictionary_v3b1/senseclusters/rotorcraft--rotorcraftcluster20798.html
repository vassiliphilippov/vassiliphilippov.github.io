rotorcraft

noun
aircraft with rotors
7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈroʊ.tər.kræft/ (AmE), /ˈrəʊ.tə.kræft/ (BrE)
an aircraft that is lifted and propelled by one or more rotating blades or rotors

Examples

A rotorcraft aircraft
A rotorcraft can take off and land vertically, making it useful in tight spaces.
The military uses various types of rotorcraft for transport and reconnaissance missions.
Unlike airplanes, a rotorcraft can hover in place for extended periods.

Related Terms

  • antonym: airplane - a fixed-wing aircraft powered by engines
  • synonym: helicopter - an aircraft with one or more horizontal rotors for lift and propulsion
