rimfire

adjective
cartridge ignition type
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪmˌfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈrɪmˌfaɪə / (BrE)
using or relating to cartridges that are ignited by striking the rim of the base.

Examples

Rimfire ammunition
The old rifle uses rimfire ammunition, which is less expensive than centerfire rounds.
Many beginners start with a rimfire pistol because of its low recoil and affordable cartridges.
He collects vintage rimfire firearms from the early twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • antonym: centerfire - using cartridges ignited by striking the center of the base
