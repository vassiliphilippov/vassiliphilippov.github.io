cricket

noun
bat-and-ball team sport
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪk.ət / (AmE), / ˈkrɪk.ɪt / (BrE)
A team game played with a ball and bat by two teams of eleven players, who take turns batting to score runs while the other team fields.

Examples

A cricket match
Many countries, especially in the Commonwealth, are passionate about cricket.
Rain often interrupts matches during the English cricket season.
The rules of cricket can seem complicated to newcomers.
