purge

noun
sudden removal from an organization
7.8 1.93/m
Pronunciation: / p…ùÀêd í / (AmE), / p…úÀêd í / (BrE)
an abrupt or sudden removal of people, especially from an organization or government, often for political reasons

Examples

A political purge
After the failed coup, many military leaders were caught up in the purge.
During the purge, thousands lost their jobs and positions of influence.
The government conducted a purge of corrupt officials last year.

Synonyms

  • lustration
    9.9 0.04/m
    lustration is a formal legal process that clears officials linked to a past regime, while purge is any sudden political removal.
  • deposition
    8.4 0.17/m
    deposition is the act of removing a leader from office, while purge usually names a wider, sudden political ousting.
  • ouster
    7.4 1.36/m
    ouster is removing someone from a position often by force or law, while purge implies sudden, often political mass removals.
  • expulsion
    6.1 2.89/m
    expulsion forces a person out of a place or group, while purge usually means abrupt political removal from power or party.
  • removal
    3.4 8.33/m
    removal is the general act of dismissing someone from a post, while purge suggests sudden, political or mass dismissals.
  • housecleaning
    4.9 0.25/m
    housecleaning means removing unwanted people or practices to reform an organization, while purge often implies harsher, political mass removal.

Related Terms

  • antonym: appointment - the act of assigning a person to a position, often antonymous to removal
  • synonym: expulsion - the act of forcing someone to leave an organization or country
