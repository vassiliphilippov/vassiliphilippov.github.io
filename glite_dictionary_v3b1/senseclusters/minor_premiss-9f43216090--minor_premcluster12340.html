minor premiss

noun
logic: premise with minor term in syllogism
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪnər prɪˈmɪs / (AmE), / ˈmaɪnə prɪˈmɪs / (BrE)
The premise in a syllogism that contains the minor term, which is the subject of the conclusion.

Examples

The minor premiss in logic
Every valid syllogism depends on both the major and minor premiss.
In a classic syllogism, the minor premiss gives an example relating to a broader rule.
The minor premiss of the argument stated that Socrates is a man.
