collinearity

noun
statistical variable correlation
9.5
Pronunciation: /kə.lɪ.ˈnɪə.rɪ.tiː/ /; / /kɑːl.ɪ.ˈnɪə.rɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /kə.lɪ.ˈnɪə.rɪ.tiː/ /; / /kɒl.ɪ.ˈnɪə.rɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
a situation in statistics where two or more predictor variables are highly correlated, making it difficult to separate their individual effects

Examples

High collinearity in data
High collinearity among variables can make statistical models less reliable.
The researcher checked for collinearity before interpreting the regression results.
To avoid collinearity, the analyst removed one of the highly correlated predictors from the model.

Related Terms

  • antonym: independence - a situation where variables are not correlated with each other
  • synonym: multicollinearity - a situation where more than two variables are highly correlated in a statistical model
