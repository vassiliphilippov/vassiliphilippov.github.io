goose step

noun
a style of stiff-legged marching
8.6
Pronunciation: / ɡus stɛp / (AmE), / ɡuːs stɛp / (BrE)
A style of marching in which the legs are kept straight and swung high with each step, used by some military and ceremonial units.

Examples

Ceremonial goose step
The ceremonial guard is famous for its precise goose step during official events.
The soldiers performed the goose step as they paraded past the reviewing stand.
Watching the old footage, you can see entire regiments marching in perfect goose step.
