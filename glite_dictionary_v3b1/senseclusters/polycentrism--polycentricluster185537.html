polycentrism

noun
multiple centers of power
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑliˈsɛntrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈsɛntrɪzəm / (BrE)
the principle or condition of having multiple centers of authority, importance, or power within a system or organization

Examples

Political polycentrism
The new government promoted polycentrism to give more autonomy to regional authorities.
Urban planners discussed the benefits of polycentrism in reducing congestion in city centers.
Some organizations adopt polycentrism to encourage innovation across different departments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: centralization - the concentration of control or authority in a single center
  • synonym: decentralization - the distribution of power away from a central authority
