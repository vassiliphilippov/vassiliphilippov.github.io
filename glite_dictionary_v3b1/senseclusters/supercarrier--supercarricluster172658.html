supercarrier

noun
highly infectious carrier
8.7
Pronunciation: /ˈsuː.pər.kær.ɪ.ər/ (AmE), /ˈsuː.pə.kær.ɪ.ə/ (BrE)
a person or animal that carries and spreads a contagious disease to many others, often due to having a very high amount of the pathogen

Examples

COVID-19 supercarrier
The health department traced the outbreak to a supercarrier who attended several public events.
Scientists are studying how a supercarrier can infect so many people without showing symptoms.
Identifying a supercarrier early can help prevent the rapid spread of disease in a community.
