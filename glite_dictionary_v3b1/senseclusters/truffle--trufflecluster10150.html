truffle

noun
edible subterranean fungus, culinary delicacy
Pronunciation: / ˈtrʌfəl / (AmE), / ˈtrʌf(ə)l / (BrE)
a highly prized edible underground fungus used as a delicacy in cooking

Examples

Black truffle delicacy
Chefs prize the intense aroma of truffle in gourmet dishes.
The restaurant's pasta was topped with fresh truffle shavings for added flavor.
We found several black truffles beneath the roots of oak trees.
Pigs and dogs are often trained to sniff out truffles in the wild.
The chef shaved black truffle over the pasta to add a rich, earthy flavor.
White truffles are considered a rare delicacy and can be very expensive.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mushroom - an edible fungus with a stem, cap, and gills, growing above ground
