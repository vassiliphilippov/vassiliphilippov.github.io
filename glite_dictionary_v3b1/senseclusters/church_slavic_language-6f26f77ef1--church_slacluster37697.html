Church Slavic language

noun
historical Slavic liturgical language
Pronunciation: / tʃɝtʃ ˈslævɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / tʃɜːtʃ ˈslævɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
the liturgical Slavic language used in Orthodox Christian worship, based on the Old Church Slavonic translation of the Bible in the 9th century

Examples

Church Slavic language texts
Many ancient manuscripts are written in Church Slavic language.
Scholars study the Church Slavic language to understand the origins of Slavic textual tradition.
The liturgy was conducted entirely in Church Slavic language.
