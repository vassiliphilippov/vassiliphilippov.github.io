thoracostomy

noun
chest wall opening
9.9
Pronunciation: /θɔːr.ə.ˈkɑːs.tə.miː/ /; / /θə.ˈræk.ə.stə.miː/ (AmE), /θɒ.rə.ˈkɒs.tə.miː/ /; / /θɔː.rə.ˈkɒs.tə.miː/ (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which an opening is made in the chest wall to allow drainage or access

Examples

A thoracostomy procedure
The surgeon performed a thoracostomy to relieve pressure from the patient's collapsed lung.
A thoracostomy tube was inserted to drain excess fluid from the pleural cavity.
After the accident, emergency doctors quickly carried out a thoracostomy to save the patient's life.
The doctor performed a thoracostomy to drain fluid from the patient's chest.
After the accident, an emergency thoracostomy was necessary to relieve pressure in the lungs.
Proper care of the thoracostomy site is important to prevent infection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: thoracotomy - a surgical incision into the chest wall
  • synonym: tracheostomy - a surgical opening in the trachea
