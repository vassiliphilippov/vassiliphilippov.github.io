airframe

noun
the framework of an aircraft or rocket
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˈɛrˌfreɪm / (AmE), / ˈeəˌfreɪm / (BrE)
the structure and outer covering of an aircraft or rocket, excluding the engines and other equipment

Examples

The airframe structure
After the crash, investigators carefully examined the airframe for clues to the cause of the accident.
Modern jetliners are designed with lightweight materials to strengthen the airframe while reducing fuel consumption.
The engineers tested the airframe for any signs of fatigue or structural weakness.
