asleep

adjective
in low-power standby mode (of devices)
3.4
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈsliːp/ (AmE), /ə.ˈsliːp/ (BrE)
(of a device or software) in a low-power or standby state; not fully active, with operations suspended to conserve energy.

Examples

Laptop asleep
The laptop went asleep after five minutes of inactivity to save battery power.
After the update, the server remained asleep until a scheduled task woke it.
Make sure the phone doesn't go asleep during the presentation by changing its power settings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: awake - fully active; not in a low-power or suspended state
  • synonym: hibernate - a deeper power-saving state that preserves more system state to disk before shutting down hardware
  • synonym: sleep mode - a low-power state in electronic devices where most activity is suspended to save energy
