LOC

noun
loss of consciousness
8.9
Pronunciation: / ɛl oʊ si / (AmE), / ɛl əʊ si / (BrE)
an abbreviation for 'loss of consciousness', meaning a temporary state in which a person becomes unconscious and unresponsive

Examples

Sudden LOC after injury
The patient suffered a brief LOC after hitting his head during the fall.
Medical staff recorded the duration of LOC to assess the severity of the injury.
After regaining consciousness, he could not remember the events leading up to his LOC.

Related Terms

  • antonym: alertness - the state of being awake and responsive
  • degree higher: coma - a prolonged state of deep unconsciousness
  • synonym: unconsciousness - the state of not being awake or aware
