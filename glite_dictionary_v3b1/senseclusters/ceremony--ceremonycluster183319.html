ceremony

noun
formal or elaborate act or procedure
1.0 2.2
Pronunciation: /ˈse.rə.moʊ.nɪ/ /; / /ˈse.rɪ.moʊ.nɪ/ (AmE), /ˈse.rɪ.mə.nɪ/ /; / /ˈse.rə.mə.nɪ/ (BrE)
a formal act or event performed on a special occasion, often following traditional rules or customs

Examples

A ceremony of tasting
He made a ceremony of tasting the wine before serving it to the guests.
She signed the document without any ceremony or fuss.
The awards were presented with great ceremony and tradition at the annual banquet.
A traditional tea ceremony involves a series of precise steps and gestures.
He bowed during the award ceremony to show respect.
The graduation ceremony was attended by students, families, and faculty.
A special ceremony was organized to welcome the new citizens to the country.
She wore traditional clothing to the wedding ceremony to honor her family's heritage.
The graduation ceremony was held outdoors this year due to the large number of attendees.

  • antonym: informality - lack of ceremony or formality; casualness
  • synonym: ritual - an established or prescribed procedure for a ceremony
