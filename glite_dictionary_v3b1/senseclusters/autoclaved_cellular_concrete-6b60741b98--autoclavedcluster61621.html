autoclaved cellular concrete

noun
lightweight precast concrete building material
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈɔːtəˌkleɪvd ˈsɛljələr ˈkɑnkrit / (AmE), / ˈɔːtəʊkleɪvd ˈsɛljʊlə ˈkɒŋkriːt / (BrE)
A lightweight, precast concrete building material that provides structure, insulation, and resistance to fire and mold, invented in the mid-1920s.

Examples

Autoclaved cellular concrete blocks
Due to its resistance to fire and mold, autoclaved cellular concrete is popular in climates with high humidity.
Many modern buildings use autoclaved cellular concrete panels for both their strength and insulation properties.
The construction company chose autoclaved cellular concrete because it is lightweight and easy to install.
