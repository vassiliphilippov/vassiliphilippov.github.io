roentgen ray

noun
type of electromagnetic radiation (X-ray)
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛntɡən ˌreɪ / (AmE), / ˈrɛntɡən ˌreɪ / (BrE)
a form of electromagnetic radiation with very short wavelength, produced when high-speed electrons strike a solid target, commonly used in medical imaging

Examples

Roentgen rays in medicine
Early researchers discovered the effects of roentgen rays on human tissue.
The doctor ordered a roentgen ray to examine the patient's broken arm.
Wilhelm Röntgen first observed the mysterious roentgen ray in 1895.

Related Terms

  • synonym: X-ray - electromagnetic radiation of short wavelength used for imaging, also called roentgen ray
