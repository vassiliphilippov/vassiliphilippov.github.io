lean on

verb
rest against for support
1.8 4.10/m
Pronunciation: / liÀên …ën / (AmE), / liÀên …ín / (BrE)
to rest against or on something or someone for physical support

Examples

To lean on a friend
He needed to lean on his friend while walking after the injury.
She let her tired head lean on his shoulder.
You can lean on the railing if you feel unsteady.

Synonyms

  • lean against
    1.4 13/m
    lean against means resting your body lightly against something for balance, while lean on also covers resting on someone for support.
  • rest on
    1.0 11/m
    rest on means placing weight or an object on something for support, while lean on means resting your body against someone or something.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rest on - to be supported by something
