gastroepiploic vein

noun
vein draining the stomach's greater curvature
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌɡæstroʊˌɛpɪˈploʊɪk veɪn / (AmE), / ˌɡæstrəʊˌɛpɪˈpləʊɪk veɪn / (BrE)
either of two veins that drain blood from the greater curvature of the stomach into the portal venous system

Examples

The gastroepiploic vein
During surgery, the gastroepiploic vein must be carefully preserved to prevent excessive bleeding.
Imaging revealed a blockage in the left gastroepiploic vein causing gastric drainage issues.
The right gastroepiploic vein runs along the greater curvature of the stomach.
