porpoising

noun
vehicle bouncing motion
Pronunciation: / ˈpɔːrpəsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpɔːpəsɪŋ / (BrE)
a rapid up-and-down motion of a vehicle, especially at high speed in racing

Examples

Porpoising at high speed
The new race car design reduced porpoising on the straight sections of the track.
Drivers complained that porpoising made it difficult to control the vehicle at high speeds.
Engineers worked overnight to fix the porpoising problem before the next race.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bouncing - moving up and down repeatedly
  • antonym: stability - the state of being steady and not moving up and down
