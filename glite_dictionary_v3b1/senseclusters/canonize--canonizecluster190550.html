canonize

verb
declare someone a saint
Pronunciation: / ˈkænəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkænənaɪz /; / ˈkanənaɪz /; / ˈkanənʌɪz / (BrE)
to officially declare someone a saint (especially in the Roman Catholic Church), or to treat someone as perfect or above criticism

Examples

To canonize a saint
It often takes many years for the Vatican to canonize someone after their death.
Many believed he would eventually be canonized for his extraordinary acts of charity.
The church decided to canonize the nun after a lengthy investigation into her life and miracles.
He tends to canonize his mentors, overlooking their flaws.
Some critics canonize authors whose books profoundly changed literature.
The church canonized Mother Teresa for her charitable work.
He was officially canonised for his lifelong dedication to helping the poor.
Many early Christian martyrs were canonised centuries after their deaths.
The Vatican announced plans to canonise the beloved nun.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: beatify - declare a deceased person to be blessed, a step before sainthood in Catholicism
  • synonym: idolize - to admire or revere excessively
  • degree higher: sainthood - the state or condition of being recognized as a saint
  • synonym: sanctify - make holy or set apart as sacred
  • antonym: vilify - to speak or write about with strong criticism or hatred
