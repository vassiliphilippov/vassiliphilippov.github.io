noun

Synonyms

  • parody
    5.0 7/m
    a parody gently copies a work's style to make fun, while burlesque broadly exaggerates form across literature, drama, or music.
  • travesty
    7.7 2/m
    a travesty makes an absurd, distorted imitation for comic effect, while burlesque also exaggerates style but often stays theatrical.
  • lampoon
    8.0 0.6/m
    a lampoon attacks and ridicules a person or group in writing, while burlesque focuses on exaggerating artistic style for humor.
  • spoof
    5.3 1/m
    a spoof copies films or shows with playful jokes, while burlesque plays with and exaggerates styles in theatre, music, or literature.
  • send-up
    8.4 0.4/m
    a send-up exaggerates a person's style to mock them in performance, while burlesque targets broader artistic forms for comic effect.
  • takeoff
    6.3 0.4/m
    a takeoff imitates someone's manner in a quick joke, while burlesque enlarges whole styles in plays, songs, or writings for satire.
  • pasquinade
    9.9 0.01/m
    a pasquinade posts a sharp public lampoon of an individual, while burlesque is a broader theatrical or literary exaggeration of style.
