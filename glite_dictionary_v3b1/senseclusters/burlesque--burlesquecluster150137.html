burlesque

noun
comic imitation or parody of a style
Pronunciation: / bɝːˈlɛsk / (AmE), / bɜːˈlɛsk / (BrE)
a literary, dramatic, or musical work that imitates and exaggerates the style of someone or something in a humorous or satirical way

Examples

A burlesque story
He wrote a burlesque that pokes fun at the conventions of opera.
The play is a clever burlesque of Victorian melodrama.
Their performance was more burlesque than sincere tribute.

Related Terms

  • antonym: homage - a respectful imitation or tribute
  • synonym: parody - a humorous or satirical imitation of a serious piece of literature or writing
  • synonym: satire - the use of humor, irony, or exaggeration to criticize
