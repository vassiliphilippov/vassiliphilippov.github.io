soap powder

noun
powdered cleaning agent for laundry
Pronunciation: / soʊp ˈpaʊdər / (AmE), / səʊp ˈpaʊdə / (BrE)
A powdery substance composed of soap and alkaline ingredients, used as a cleaning agent, especially for washing clothes.

Examples

A box of soap powder
Many people prefer liquid detergent over soap powder for convenience and effectiveness.
Modern soap powder dissolves quickly and removes tough stains from clothes.
She added two scoops of soap powder to the washing machine before starting the cycle.
