musculus anconeus

noun
muscle that extends the forearm at the elbow
9.6
Pronunciation: / mʌsˈkjuːləs ænˈkoʊni.əs / (AmE), / mʌsˈkjuːləs ænˈkəʊnɪ.əs / (BrE)
A small muscle at the elbow that helps extend the forearm and abduct the ulna during wrist pronation.

Examples

The musculus anconeus muscle
During anatomical studies, the musculus anconeus is often shown adjacent to the triceps brachii.
In some injuries, weakness of the musculus anconeus can affect wrist rotation.
The musculus anconeus assists in the extension of the forearm at the elbow joint.
