coercive

adjective
using or involving force or threats
7.6 3.73/m
Pronunciation: / ko äÀà…ùÀês…™v / (AmE), / k…ô äÀà…úÀês…™v / (BrE)
using force or threats to make someone do something; serving or intended to compel by force.

Examples

A coercive method
His management style was criticized as overly coercive and controlling.
The coercive tactics used during interrogations were widely condemned.
The government passed new coercive laws to suppress dissent.

Synonyms

  • intimidatory
    8.1 0.01/m
    intimidatory aims to frighten or threaten a person directly, while coercive uses force or threats to make someone comply.
  • oppressive
    6.3 4.41/m
    oppressive describes long, harsh control or unfair treatment of people, while coercive uses force or threats to make someone act.
