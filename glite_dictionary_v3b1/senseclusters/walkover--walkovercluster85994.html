walkover

noun
easy task or victory
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔkˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈwɔːkˌəʊvə / (BrE)
an undertaking or contest that is easily won or accomplished, requiring little effort

Examples

An easy walkover
The election was a walkover for the popular candidate, who won by a landslide.
What was expected to be a tough negotiation became a walkover after the other side agreed immediately.
With such inexperienced opponents, the game turned out to be a walkover.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cakewalk - something easily accomplished or won
  • synonym: no-brainer - a decision or task that is very easy to do
  • antonym: struggle - a difficult or strenuous effort
