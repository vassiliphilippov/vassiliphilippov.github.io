complication

noun
secondary disease arising during another illness
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːmplɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒmplɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
a disease or disorder that develops while treating or as a result of another existing disease

Examples

Medical complication
Bed sores are a common complication in cases of paralysis.
Physicians closely monitor patients for any complication that may arise post-surgery.
Pneumonia can be a serious complication of influenza, especially in elderly patients.
