unforgivingness

noun
harsh, unyielding quality
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˌʌn.fɚˈɡɪ.vɪŋ.nəs / (AmE), / ˌʌn.fəˈɡɪ.vɪŋ.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being harsh and unyielding, showing no pity or allowance for weakness, mistakes, or shortcomings.

Examples

The unforgivingness of nature
Many explorers have underestimated the unforgivingness of the mountain's steep and icy terrain.
She spoke about the unforgivingness of corporate culture, where mistakes were rarely tolerated.
The unforgivingness of the desert climate made survival a daily challenge for the researchers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: forgiveness - The act of forgiving or showing mercy
  • synonym: mercilessness - The quality of showing no mercy or compassion
