flimflam

noun
deceptive talk
Pronunciation: / ˈflɪmˌflæm / (AmE), / ˈflɪmˌflæm / (BrE)
deceptive or nonsensical talk intended to trick or mislead people

Examples

Flimflam talk
The politician's speech was full of flimflam and empty promises.
Don't fall for his flimflam; he's just trying to sell you something you don't need.
After listening to all that flimflam, I still don't know what the plan is.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fraud - the crime of deceiving people to gain money or advantage
  • synonym: nonsense - words or ideas that are silly or have no meaning
  • antonym: truth - the quality of being true or accurate
