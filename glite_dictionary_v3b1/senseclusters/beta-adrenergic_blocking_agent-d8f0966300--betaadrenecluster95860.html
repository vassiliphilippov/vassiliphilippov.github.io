beta-adrenergic blocking agent

noun
drug reducing heart activity by beta-receptor blocking
Pronunciation: / ˌbeɪtə əˌdrɛnərˈdʒɪk ˈblɑkɪŋ ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˌbiːtə əˌdrɛnəˈdʒɪk ˈblɒkɪŋ ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
any of a group of drugs that treat hypertension or arrhythmia by reducing the force and rate of heart contractions through blocking beta-adrenergic receptors in the autonomic nervous system

Examples

A beta-adrenergic blocking agent drug
A beta-adrenergic blocking agent can help manage certain types of arrhythmias by slowing the heart rate.
Patients with high blood pressure may be prescribed a beta-adrenergic blocking agent to control their condition.
Some athletes are prohibited from using any beta-adrenergic blocking agent due to its effect on heart function.
