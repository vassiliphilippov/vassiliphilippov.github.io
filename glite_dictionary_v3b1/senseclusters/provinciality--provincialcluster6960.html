provinciality

noun
regional character
Pronunciation: / prəˌvɪnʃiˈæləti / (AmE), / prəˌvɪnʃiˈæləti / (BrE)
the quality or state of having the customs, attitudes, or characteristics typical of a particular province or region

Examples

Provinciality in customs
The provinciality of the town was evident in its unique festivals and local traditions.
Critics argued that the novel's provinciality limited its appeal to a wider audience.
She appreciated the provinciality of the cuisine, which reflected the region's history and culture.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cosmopolitanism - the quality of being open to and familiar with many different cultures
  • synonym: regionalism - loyalty to or emphasis on a particular region
