beta-naphthol

noun
a chemical isomer used in rubber production
Pronunciation: / ˌbeɪtə ˈnæfˌθɑl / (AmE), / ˌbiːtə ˈnæfˌθɒl / (BrE)
A chemical compound, one of the two isomers of naphthol, used especially as an antioxidant in the rubber industry.

Examples

Beta-naphthol antioxidant
Beta-naphthol is often incorporated into the production of tires to improve their durability.
Many antioxidants, including beta-naphthol, help prevent the degradation of rubber in industrial applications.
The chemist ordered a new supply of beta-naphthol for the laboratory experiments.

Related Terms
