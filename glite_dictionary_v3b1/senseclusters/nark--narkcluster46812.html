nark

verb
to annoy
Pronunciation: / nɑːk / (BrE)
to annoy or irritate someone, especially in British English

Examples

To nark someone
It really narks me when people don't return my calls.
She was narked by his constant interruptions during the meeting.
Don't let little things nark you so easily.

Related Terms

  • synonym: annoy - to make someone slightly angry or irritated
  • synonym: irritate - to make someone feel annoyed or impatient
  • antonym: please - to make someone happy or satisfied
