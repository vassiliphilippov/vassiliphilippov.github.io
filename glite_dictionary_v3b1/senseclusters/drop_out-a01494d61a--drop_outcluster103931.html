Synonyms

  • withdraw
    2.8 20/m
    withdraw is a formal word for leaving or stopping participation in a group or activity, while drop out is informal and sudden.
  • pull out
    3.4 10/m
    pull out means to stop taking part or to leave an agreement, while drop out often means leaving before finishing.
  • drop the course
    3.0 0.03/m
    drop the course means to officially leave a class before it ends, while drop out covers leaving school or activities more widely.
  • unenroll
    4.8 0.00/m
    unenroll means to remove enrollment from a program in an official way, while drop out is more general and informal.
  • disenroll
    6.8 0.02/m
    disenroll refers to an official removal from a course or school record, while drop out is leaving without finishing.
  • drop by the wayside
    7.5 0.02/m
    drop by the wayside describes people or projects that stop progressing from difficulty, while drop out is a deliberate act of leaving.
  • fall by the wayside
    6.5 0.5/m
    fall by the wayside shows failing or stopping because of hardship or neglect, while drop out focuses on choosing to leave.
  • bow out
    4.9 0.9/m
    bow out means to leave politely or give up a role by choice, while drop out suggests stopping before finishing, often informally.

Related Terms

  • antonym: continue - to keep doing something without stopping

  • synonym: give up - to stop trying to do something because it is too difficult
  • antonym: graduate - to successfully complete a course of study
  • synonym: withdraw - to leave a course or program by choice
