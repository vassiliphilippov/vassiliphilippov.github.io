crusty

adjective
having a hard outer crust
2.5 1.41/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆkrÊŒsti / (AmE), / ËˆkrÊŒsti / (BrE)
having a hard, crisp outer layer or covering, like a crust

Examples

Crusty bread
After a day in the sun, mud had dried into a crusty layer on his boots.
She loves the crusty top of a freshly baked casserole.
The bakery is famous for its crusty sourdough bread.

Synonyms

  • crustlike
    3.7 0.00/m
    crustlike has an outer layer that looks like a crust, while crusty means the surface is hard and crisp to touch.
  • crusted
    4.2 0.22/m
    crusted is covered with a formed hardened layer, while crusty describes a surface that is hard and crisp in texture.
  • encrusted
    5.8 0.00/m
    encrusted is thickly covered, often with stuck-on deposits, while crusty means a thin hard, crisp outer layer like bread.

Related Terms

  • antonym: soft - not hard or firm to the touch
