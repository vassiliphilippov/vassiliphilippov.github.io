incidental music

noun
music accompanying a drama or performance
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnsɪˈdɛntl ˈmjuːzɪk / (AmE), / ˌɪnsɪˈdentl ˈmjuːzɪk / (BrE)
music composed to accompany the action of a play, film, or other performance, or to fill intervals between scenes

Examples

Incidental music for plays
He wrote several pieces of incidental music for radio dramas in the 1940s.
The play was enhanced by beautiful incidental music composed specifically for the production.
The stage manager cued the incidental music as the actors left for the intermission.
