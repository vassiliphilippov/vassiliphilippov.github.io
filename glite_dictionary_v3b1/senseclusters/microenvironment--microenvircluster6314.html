microenvironment

noun
localized environment
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪkroʊɪnˈvaɪrənmənt / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəʊɪnˈvaɪrənmənt / (BrE)
a small, specific area within a larger environment that has different conditions from its surroundings

Examples

Forest microenvironment
Each plant species adapts to its own microenvironment within the forest.
The tumor's microenvironment can influence how cancer cells grow and spread.
Researchers studied the microenvironment around coral reefs to understand their unique conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: habitat - the natural home or environment of an organism
  • antonym: macroenvironment - the larger, overall environment that surrounds a microenvironment
