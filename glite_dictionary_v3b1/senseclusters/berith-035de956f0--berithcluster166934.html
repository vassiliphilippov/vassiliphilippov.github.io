Berith

noun
Jewish circumcision ceremony
9.9
Pronunciation: / bəˈrɪθ / (AmE), / bəˈrɪθ / (BrE)
the Jewish religious ceremony of circumcising a male child on the eighth day of his life, also known as brit milah

Examples

The Berith ceremony
A rabbi was present to conduct the Berith according to tradition.
The family gathered to celebrate the Berith of their newborn son.
The significance of the Berith is deeply rooted in Jewish culture and religion.
