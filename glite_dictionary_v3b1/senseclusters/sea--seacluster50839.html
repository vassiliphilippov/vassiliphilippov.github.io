sea

noun
turbulent water with large waves
1.0 13/m
Pronunciation: / siÀê / (AmE), / siÀê / (BrE)
a state of the ocean or another large body of water characterized by large, turbulent waves and swells, especially as experienced by ships at sea

Examples

Rough sea conditions
After the storm, the sea finally began to calm, making travel safer.
Fishermen often face rough seas during storms.
The ship struggled through the sea as giant waves crashed against its hull.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - state of water with little or no movement or waves
  • degree lower: surf - waves breaking on the shore, usually with turbulence
