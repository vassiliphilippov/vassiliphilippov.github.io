penny-pinching

adjective
unwilling to spend money
4.6 6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈpen.ɪ ˈpɪn.tʃɪŋ/ /; / /ˈpen.ɪ.pɪn.tʃɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈpen.ɪ ˈpɪn.tʃɪŋ/ /; / /ˈpen.ɪ.pɪn.tʃɪŋ/ (BrE)
unwilling to spend or give much money; very reluctant to part with money

Examples

Penny-pinching uncle
Everyone avoided dining out with his penny-pinching uncle, who never wanted to split the bill fairly.
Her penny-pinching attitude meant she would never buy anything she didn't absolutely need.
The company’s penny-pinching policies frustrated employees who needed better equipment to work efficiently.
