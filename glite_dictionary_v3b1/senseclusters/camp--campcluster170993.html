camp

noun
site for children's summer activities
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /kæmp/ (AmE), /kæmp/ (BrE)
a place where people stay in temporary shelters, such as tents or cabins, for recreation, travel, military, or safety (e.g., summer camp, refugee camp, military camp)

Examples

A summer camp
Many parents look for a reputable camp that offers educational programs for children.
My daughter spent two weeks at a camp in the mountains last summer.
The camp provides swimming lessons, arts and crafts, and outdoor games for kids.
Conditions in the camp were harsh, but it offered safety from violence outside.
The United Nations established a new camp to accommodate refugees escaping political persecution.
Thousands of families lived in the camp after fleeing the conflict in their homeland.
After weeks in the field, returning to camp was a relief for everyone.
Security around the camp was tightened following the recent attacks.
The soldiers assembled in the camp before sunrise each morning.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: base - permanent military station
  • degree lower: bivouac - temporary outdoor camp without tents or cover
  • synonym: campsite - a place where a camp is set up
  • synonym: caravan park - an area where people can park caravans or motorhomes and stay temporarily
  • degree higher: concentration camp - a camp where large numbers of people are detained, often in harsh conditions
  • synonym: encampment - a place with temporary accommodations of huts, tents, or other structures
  • synonym: prison camp - a camp where prisoners are confined, especially during wartime
  • synonym: refugee camp - a camp providing shelter to people forced to flee their country
  • synonym: summer camp - a camp for children during the summer
