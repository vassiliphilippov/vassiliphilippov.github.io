palaver

verb
persuade by gentle urging or flattery
Pronunciation: / pəˈlævər / (AmE), / pəˈlɑːvə / (BrE)
to influence or persuade someone by gentle urging, caressing, or flattering; to cajole or coax

Examples

To palaver someone
He managed to palaver his co-workers into staying late to help him finish the project.
She palavered her parents with compliments until they agreed to let her go to the concert.
They spent hours palavering the stubborn client, hoping he would accept their proposal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cajole - to persuade by flattery or gentle pleading
  • synonym: coax - to persuade gently, often using flattery
