slip

verb
move out of normal position (joint/bone)
Pronunciation: / slɪp / (AmE), / slɪp / (BrE)
(of a joint or bone) to move out of its normal position in the body, often causing pain or injury.

Examples

To slip a joint
After the fall, the child's elbow appeared to slip out of its socket.
If the shoulder joint were to slip, it would cause severe discomfort.
The patient experienced pain when the disc began to slip.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dislocate - to put out of place, especially of a joint
  • synonym: luxate - to dislocate a bone or joint
