word-perfect

adjective
knowing or repeating exactly without error
Pronunciation: / wɝd ˈpɚ.fɪkt / (AmE), / wɜːd ˈpɜː.fɪkt / (BrE)
able to recite or reproduce something exactly as written or spoken, without any errors; knowing the exact words of a text or speech by heart.

Examples

Word-perfect memory
After months of rehearsal, the actor was word-perfect on opening night.
She studied so hard that she was word-perfect for her final exam presentation.
The children were word-perfect in reciting the poem at the assembly.
