ependymal

noun
cell lining brain cavities
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˈpɛndɪməl / (AmE), / ɪˈpɛndɪməl / (BrE)
a cell that is part of the ependyma, the thin membrane lining the brain's ventricles and the spinal canal

Examples

Ependymal cell
The scientist studied the ependymal under a microscope to understand its role in the brain.
Damage to the ependymal can affect the flow of cerebrospinal fluid in the central nervous system.
Researchers discovered that the ependymal helps maintain the environment around brain cells.

Related Terms
