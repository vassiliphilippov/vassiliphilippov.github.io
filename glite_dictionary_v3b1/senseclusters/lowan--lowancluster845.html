lowan

noun
Australian mound-building bird (malleefowl)
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊ.ən / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.ən / (BrE)
An Australian bird (the malleefowl) that incubates its eggs in sandy mounds built from soil and vegetation.

Examples

The lowan bird
Conservationists are working to protect the habitat of the endangered lowan.
Researchers studied the nesting habits of the elusive lowan in Western Australia.
The lowan constructs large mounds to incubate its eggs in the Australian outback.
