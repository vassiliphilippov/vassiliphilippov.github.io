lamination

noun
bonding thin sheets together
Pronunciation: / ˌlæməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌlæmɪˈneɪʃən /; / ˌlamɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of bonding thin layers together, or the result of this process (a layered structure), often to protect or strengthen something

Examples

The lamination process
During manufacturing, the lamination process ensures the circuit boards are reliable.
The lamination of the documents helps protect them from moisture and damage.
This table is made with a wood lamination for extra strength and durability.
The lab examined the lamination to study each individual layer.
The windshield uses a lamination of glass and plastic for safety.
Wooden floors often have a protective lamination to increase durability.
The lamination of important documents helps protect them from water and tearing.
After lamination, the menu was much easier to clean and lasted longer.
He used lamination to preserve his favorite photographs for years to come.

Related Terms

  • synonym: laminate - to bond layers together to make a composite
  • synonym: layer - a single thickness or stratum in a structure
  • synonym: stratification - the formation of layers
