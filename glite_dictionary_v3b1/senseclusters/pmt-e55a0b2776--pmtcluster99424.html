PMT

noun
light-detecting device
Pronunciation: / piː ɛm tiː / (AmE), / piː ɛm tiː / (BrE)
a photomultiplier tube, a device that detects and amplifies very weak light signals in scientific instruments

Examples

PMT sensor
The scientist connected the PMT to the telescope to capture faint starlight.
A PMT is often used in laboratories to detect very low levels of light.
The experiment required a sensitive PMT to measure the weak signal accurately.

Related Terms

  • synonym: photodetector - a device that senses and measures light
  • synonym: sensor - a device that detects changes in physical conditions
