buckminsterfullerene

noun
spherical carbon molecule (C60 fullerene)
Pronunciation: / ˌbʌkmɪnstərˈfʊləriːn / (AmE), / ˌbʌkmɪnstəˈfʊləriːn / (BrE)
a spherical molecule consisting of 60 carbon atoms arranged in a pattern similar to a soccer ball; the first and most well-known example of a fullerene

Examples

A buckminsterfullerene molecule
Researchers were excited to discover buckminsterfullerene in soot collected from their experiments.
Scientists often refer to buckminsterfullerene as C60 due to its sixty carbon atoms.
The structure of buckminsterfullerene resembles a soccer ball with twenty hexagons and twelve pentagons.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fullerene - a class of carbon molecules made entirely of carbon, often in the form of hollow spheres, ellipsoids or tubes
