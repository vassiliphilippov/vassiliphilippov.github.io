nuclear energy

noun
energy from nuclear reactions
Pronunciation: / ˈnuː.kli.ɚ ˈɛn.ɚ.dʒi / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.klɪ.ə ˈɛn.ə.dʒi / (BrE)
energy released during nuclear reactions, especially by fission or fusion, used for producing electricity or in weapons

Examples

Nuclear energy production
Many countries invest heavily in nuclear energy to reduce carbon emissions.
Proper disposal of waste is a major challenge for nuclear energy providers.
The development of nuclear energy plants sparked public debates about safety.
