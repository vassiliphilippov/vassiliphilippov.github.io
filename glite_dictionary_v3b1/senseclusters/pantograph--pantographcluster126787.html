pantograph

noun
mechanical device for scaling drawings
Pronunciation: / ˈpæn.təˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈpæn.tə.ɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a mechanical device with linked arms used to copy a drawing or plan at a different scale, either larger or smaller than the original

Examples

A pantograph device
Before digital tools, a pantograph was essential for architects needing to reproduce plans at various sizes.
The engineer demonstrated the pantograph to show how blueprints could be scaled up or down.
Using a pantograph, the artist was able to precisely enlarge the original sketch.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tracing - the act of copying a drawing or design
