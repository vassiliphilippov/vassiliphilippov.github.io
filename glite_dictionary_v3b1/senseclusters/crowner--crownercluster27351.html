crowner

noun
person who crowns
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkraʊnər / (AmE), / ˈkraʊnə / (BrE)
a person who places a crown on someone, especially during a coronation ceremony, or who makes or fits crowns

Examples

Crowner at a coronation
During the ancient ceremony, the crowner placed the golden crown on the new king's head.
The skilled crowner crafted intricate crowns for the royal family.
In medieval times, the crowner held an important role at every coronation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coronator - a person who performs a coronation
  • antonym: monarch - a king or queen who is crowned
