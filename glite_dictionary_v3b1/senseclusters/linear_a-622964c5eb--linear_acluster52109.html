Linear A

noun
ancient Cretan writing system
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌlɪniər ˈeɪ / (AmE), / ˌlɪniə ˈeɪ / (BrE)
an undeciphered writing system used in ancient Crete in the 17th century BC, preceding Linear B.

Examples

Linear A script
Archaeologists discovered clay tablets bearing Linear A inscriptions during their excavations in Knossos.
Many believe that Linear A was used for administrative purposes in Minoan society.
The meaning of the symbols in Linear A remains a mystery to linguists and historians.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Linear B - a later syllabic script used on Crete and mainland Greece, deciphered as Greek
