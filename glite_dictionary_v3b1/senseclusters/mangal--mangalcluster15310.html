mangal

noun
mangrove wetland
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈmæŋɡəl / (BrE)
a coastal wetland area dominated by mangrove trees, often found in tropical and subtropical regions

Examples

Coastal mangal
The mangal provides a habitat for many fish and bird species.
Local communities rely on the mangal for fishing and protection from coastal erosion.
Scientists are studying how climate change affects the delicate balance of the mangal ecosystem.

Related Terms

  • antonym: desert - a dry, barren area of land with little rainfall
  • synonym: mangrove - a type of tree or shrub that grows in coastal saline or brackish water
  • synonym: swamp - an area of low-lying, uncultivated ground where water collects
