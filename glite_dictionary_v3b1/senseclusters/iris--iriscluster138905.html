iris

verb
to adjust camera aperture
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪrɪs / (AmE), / ˈaɪrɪs / (BrE)
to adjust the iris or aperture of a camera or similar device, especially to control light or create a visual effect

Examples

To iris in a scene
The director asked the camera operator to iris in for a dramatic closing shot.
During the scene, she quickly irised the lens to adjust for the changing light.
To highlight the actor, the technician began irising the camera as the lights dimmed.
The director irised out at the end of the scene to create a classic film effect.
In early cinema, editors often irised in to introduce a new character or location.
She irised the shot to focus the audience's attention on the main subject.

  • synonym: aperture - the opening in a camera lens that controls the amount of light entering
  • antonym: close - to shut or make something less open
  • synonym: dissolve - to transition between scenes by blending one into another
