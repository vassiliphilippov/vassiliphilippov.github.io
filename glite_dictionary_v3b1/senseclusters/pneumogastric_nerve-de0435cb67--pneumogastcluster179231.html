pneumogastric nerve

noun
tenth cranial (vagus) nerve
7.2
Pronunciation: /nuː.moʊ.ˈgæs.trɪk nɜːrv/ /; / /njuː.moʊ.ˈgæs.trɪk nɜːrv/ (AmE), /njuː.məʊ.ˈgæs.trɪk nɜːv/ /; / /nuː.məʊ.ˈgæs.trɪk nɜːv/ (BrE)
the tenth cranial nerve, supplying the pharynx, larynx, lungs, heart, esophagus, stomach, and most of the abdominal organs; also known as the vagus nerve

Examples

The pneumogastric nerve
Damage to the pneumogastric nerve can lead to voice changes and difficulty swallowing.
The pneumogastric nerve plays a critical role in regulating heart rate and digestion.
The surgeon identified the pneumogastric nerve during the procedure to avoid accidental injury.
