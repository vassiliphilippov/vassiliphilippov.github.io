telescopic sight

noun
optical sighting device for firearms
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛləˈskɑpɪk saɪt / (AmE), / ˌtɛlɪˈskɒpɪk saɪt / (BrE)
an optical sighting device containing a telescope, mounted on a firearm to help the user aim accurately at distant targets

Examples

A telescopic sight on rifle
He attached a telescopic sight to his rifle to improve his accuracy at long range.
Modern hunters often rely on a telescopic sight to spot game from afar.
The sniper adjusted his telescopic sight before taking aim at the target.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scope - a short form for telescopic sight, commonly used by shooters
