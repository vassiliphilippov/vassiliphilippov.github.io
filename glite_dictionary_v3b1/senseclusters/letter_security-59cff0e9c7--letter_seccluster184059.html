letter security

noun
unregistered stock or bond
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛtər sɪˈkjʊrəti / (AmE), / ˈlɛtə sɪˈkjʊərəti / (BrE)
A stock or bond that is not registered with the Securities and Exchange Commission and therefore cannot be sold in the public market.

Examples

A letter security bond
Because it was a letter security, the bond could not be traded freely on the open market.
He received a letter security after writing a letter to the issuer promising not to sell it publicly.
The private placement resulted in the issuance of letter securities to accredited investors.

Related Terms

  • antonym: registered security - a security registered with the SEC and able to be publicly sold
