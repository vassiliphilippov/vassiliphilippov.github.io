polyose

noun
complex carbohydrate; polysaccharide
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːlioʊs / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪəʊz / (BrE)
Any of a class of carbohydrates whose molecules are made up of long chains of monosaccharide units; a polysaccharide.

Examples

A polyose chain
Cellulose is a common polyose found in plant cell walls.
Scientists analyzed the polyose content of the new starch sample.
The structure of this polyose makes it resistant to most digestive enzymes.
