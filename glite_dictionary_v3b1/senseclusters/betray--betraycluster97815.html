betray

verb
fail or abandon someone
1.0 3.5
Pronunciation: /bɪ.ˈtreɪ/ /; / /bə.ˈtreɪ/ (AmE), /bɪ.ˈtreɪ/ /; / /bə.ˈtreɪ/ (BrE)
to disappoint or abandon someone, especially when they rely on you; to prove undependable in a crucial moment

Examples

Betray a friend
During the difficult times, his courage betrayed him and he could not act.
She felt that her friends had betrayed her when she needed them most.
When the team betrayed their coach by giving up, everyone was disappointed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: support - to give help or encouragement, especially in a difficult situation
