serotina

noun
species epithet
Pronunciation: / səˈrɑːtɪnə / (AmE), / sɪˈrɒtɪnə / (BrE)
a species name used in scientific Latin, often referring to plants that flower or fruit late in the season

Examples

Prunus serotina tree
The black cherry tree is scientifically known as Prunus serotina.
Botanists identified the specimen as belonging to the species serotina.
Many plants with the epithet serotina bloom later in the year than related species.
