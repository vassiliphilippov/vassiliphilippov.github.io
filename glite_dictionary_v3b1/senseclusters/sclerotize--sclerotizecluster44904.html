sclerotize

verb
to harden tissue
Pronunciation: / ˈsklɛrəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈsklɪərəˌtaɪz / (BrE)
to harden a biological tissue by a chemical process, forming a tough outer layer, especially in arthropods.

Examples

To sclerotize insect shells
Scientists sclerotize insect exoskeletons in the lab to study their durability.
The outer shell of the beetle is sclerotized to protect it from predators.
During development, certain tissues sclerotize to form a rigid structure.
