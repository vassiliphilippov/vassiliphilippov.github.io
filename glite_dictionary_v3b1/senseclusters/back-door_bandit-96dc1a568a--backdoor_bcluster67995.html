back-door bandit

noun
person who illegally accesses computer systems
Pronunciation: / ˈbæk dɔɹ ˈbændɪt / (AmE), / ˈbæk dɔː ˈbændɪt / (BrE)
a person who illegally gains access to computer systems or information, often by exploiting hidden vulnerabilities

Examples

Back-door bandit hacker
After the breach, investigators discovered a back-door bandit had accessed customer accounts for weeks.
The IT manager warned staff about phishing scams used by a typical back-door bandit.
The company improved its cybersecurity to defend against any back-door bandit trying to steal sensitive data.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cracker - someone who breaks into computer systems, typically with malicious intent
  • synonym: hacker - a person who gains unauthorized access to computer systems
