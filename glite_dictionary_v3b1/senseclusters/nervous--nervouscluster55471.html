nervous

adjective
easily agitated or alarmed
Pronunciation: / ˈnɝvəs /; / ˈnɜrvəs /; / ˈnɝːvəs / (AmE), / ˈnɜːvəs / (BrE)
feeling or showing anxiety, worry, or unease; easily excited or agitated, especially due to fear or sensitivity

Examples

Nervous about exams
She spoke in a nervous voice, glancing around the room constantly.
That puppy is particularly nervous around strangers and loud noises.
The nervous horse refused to let anyone approach it.
His nervous laughter revealed how anxious he was about the interview.
She felt nervous before stepping onto the stage for her first performance.
The team's nervous energy was obvious just before the big match.
As the race was about to start, every runner looked nervous but eager.
She felt nervous before her job interview, excited yet unsure of what to expect.
The children grew nervous as the magician prepared his final trick.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anxious - experiencing worry, unease, or nervousness
  • antonym: calm - not showing or feeling nervousness, anger, or other emotions
  • synonym: skittish - easily frightened or excited, especially of animals
