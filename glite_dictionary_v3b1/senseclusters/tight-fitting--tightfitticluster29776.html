tight-fitting

adjective
fitting closely and snugly
Pronunciation: / taɪt ˈfɪtɪŋ /; / ˈtaɪtˌfɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / taɪt ˈfɪtɪŋ /; / ˈtaɪtˌfɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
fitting closely and firmly to the body or object, not loose

Examples

Tight-fitting clothes
Athletes often wear tight-fitting uniforms to reduce wind resistance.
She wore a tight-fitting dress that accentuated her figure.
The plumber squeezed into the tight-fitting crawl space under the house.
For cycling, he prefers tight-fitting clothing to reduce wind resistance.
She wore a tight-fitting dress to the party that accentuated her figure.
The tight-fitting lid kept the contents of the jar fresh for weeks.
