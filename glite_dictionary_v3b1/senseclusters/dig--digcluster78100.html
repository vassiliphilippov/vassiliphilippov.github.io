dig

noun
organized excavation, often archaeological
3.1
Pronunciation: / dɪg / (AmE), / dɪg / (BrE)
an excavation or the site where people dig to find ancient objects, fossils, or buildings

Examples

The university is funding a major dig near the old Roman road.
Her dream was to join an archaeological dig in Egypt one day.
Security at the dig was tight to protect the valuable artifacts found there.
The team spent months working at the dig uncovering pottery and jewelry.
