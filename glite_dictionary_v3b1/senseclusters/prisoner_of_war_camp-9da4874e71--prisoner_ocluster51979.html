prisoner of war camp

noun
camp for captured enemy soldiers
3.0
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪzənər əv wɔr kæmp / (AmE), / ˈprɪzənə əv wɔː kæmp / (BrE)
A place where soldiers captured by the enemy during a war are kept as prisoners.

Examples

A prisoner of war camp
Conditions in the prisoner of war camp were harsh, but the inmates found ways to keep their spirits up.
During World War II, thousands of soldiers were held in prisoner of war camps across Europe.
He wrote letters home from the prisoner of war camp, describing life behind the barbed wire.
