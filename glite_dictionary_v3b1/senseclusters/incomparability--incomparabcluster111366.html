incomparability

noun
state of being impossible to compare
3.0
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌkɑːmpərəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ɪnˌkɒmpərəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
The state or condition of being impossible to compare or equate, often because of lack of a common measure or a significant difference in nature.

Examples

The incomparability of two ideas
Some works of art have a unique incomparability that challenges critics and historians.
The incomparability of the two artists makes it difficult to judge who is better.
The committee struggled with the incomparability of the competing proposals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: incomparableness - the quality of being incomparable; similar meaning
