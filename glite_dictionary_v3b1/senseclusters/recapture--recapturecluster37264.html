recapture

verb
to revive or regain something
1.8
Pronunciation: / riˈkæptʃər /; / riˈkæptʃɚ / (AmE), / riˈkæptʃə / (BrE)
to experience or feel something again, especially a mood, feeling, or situation from the past

Examples

To recapture the feeling
He hoped the new sequel would recapture the magic of the original film.
The artist tried to recapture the feeling of her early works.
The novel beautifully recaptures the atmosphere of 1920s Paris.
She closed her eyes and tried to recapture the feeling of that unforgettable day.
The author managed to recapture the magic of her earlier novels with this sequel.
Years later, he tried to recapture the excitement of his youth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: relive - to live through (an experience or feeling) again
  • synonym: revive - to bring something back to mind, use, or existence
