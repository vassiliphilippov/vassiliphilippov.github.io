Pepcid

noun
trademarked drug for ulcers and reflux
5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈpep.sɪd/ (AmE), /ˈpep.sɪd/ (BrE)
A trademarked histamine blocker (famotidine) used to treat peptic ulcers, gastritis, and esophageal reflux.

Examples

Pepcid medicine
She took Pepcid to relieve her heartburn after a spicy meal.
Some people prefer Pepcid over other antacids because it works quickly.
The doctor recommended Pepcid for his recurrent gastritis.

Related Terms
