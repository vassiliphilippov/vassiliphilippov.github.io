Draco

proper noun
faint northern sky constellation
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˈdreɪkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdreɪkəʊ / (BrE)
a constellation in the northern sky, winding between Ursa Major and Cepheus near the North Celestial Pole

Examples

Draco constellation
Legend says Draco represents the dragon slain by Hercules.
She pointed out Draco in the clear night sky near the North Celestial Pole.
The constellation Draco can be seen winding between Ursa Major and Cepheus.
Draco can be seen throughout the year from most locations in the Northern Hemisphere.
Many astronomers enjoy tracing the long, winding shape of Draco in the night sky.
The star Thuban is part of the Draco constellation and was once the pole star.
