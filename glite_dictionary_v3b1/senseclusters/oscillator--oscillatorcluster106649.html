oscillator

noun
device producing regular oscillations
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːsɪˌleɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɒsɪleɪtə / (BrE)
a device that produces regular oscillations, such as sound waves or alternating electrical current

Examples

Quartz oscillator
A quartz crystal oscillator keeps the watch running precisely.
Engineers adjusted the oscillator to produce a stable sine wave signal.
The radio station uses an oscillator to generate the necessary carrier frequency.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: amplifier - a device that increases the strength of electrical signals
  • synonym: generator - a device that produces something, especially electricity
