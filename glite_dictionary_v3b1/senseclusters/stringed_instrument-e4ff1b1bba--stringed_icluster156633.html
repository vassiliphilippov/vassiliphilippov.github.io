stringed instrument

noun
musical instrument with taut strings
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈstrɪŋd ˈɪnstrəmənt / (AmE), / ˈstrɪŋd ˈɪnstrʊmənt / (BrE)
A musical instrument that produces sound by means of taut strings that are plucked, struck, or bowed.

Examples

A stringed instrument player
Ancient cultures developed their own unique stringed instrument designs for traditional music.
She learned to play several stringed instruments, including the guitar and the harp.
The violin is a type of stringed instrument commonly used in orchestras.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: bowed instrument - stringed instrument played by using a bow
  • antonym: percussion instrument - musical instrument that sounds by being struck or shaken
  • degree lower: plucked instrument - stringed instrument played by plucking the strings
  • antonym: wind instrument - musical instrument in which sound is produced by air movement
