off

adverb
away from a place or position
Pronunciation: / ɑf / (AmE), / ɒf / (BrE)
away from a place or position; removed or separated from something

Examples

Jump off the bus
He jumped off the bus as soon as it stopped.
The train sped off before I could wave goodbye.
They drove off into the sunset, ready for their new adventure.
He took off his shoes at the door.
Please wipe off the table before dinner.
The paint started to peel off after years in the sun.

Related Terms

  • synonym: forth - forward in time or place (archaic/poetic use)
