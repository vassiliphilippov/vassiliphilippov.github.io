inassimilable

adjective
impossible to integrate
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnəˈsɪmɪləbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪnəˈsɪmɪləbəl / (BrE)
impossible to absorb, integrate, or adapt into another group, culture, language, or system

Examples

Inassimilable customs
Some cultural practices are considered inassimilable into mainstream society.
The material proved inassimilable and could not be broken down by the body.
Certain beliefs may remain inassimilable despite repeated efforts at integration.
Some cultural concepts are simply inassimilable into other societies without losing their original meaning.
The unique rhythm of the language made certain expressions inassimilable in translation.
He argued that the traditions were inassimilable within the modern legal framework.

Related Terms

  • antonym: assimilable - able to be absorbed or integrated
  • synonym: unassimilable - not able to be absorbed or integrated
  • synonym: untranslatable - impossible to translate accurately into another language
