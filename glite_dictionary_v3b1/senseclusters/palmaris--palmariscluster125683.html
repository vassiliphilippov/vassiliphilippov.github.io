palmaris

noun
forearm muscle
9.1
Pronunciation: / pælˈmɛrɪs / (AmE), / pɑːlˈmɑːrɪs / (BrE)
a muscle in the forearm that helps flex the hand at the wrist, sometimes absent in some people

Examples

Palmaris muscle
The surgeon checked if the patient had a palmaris before using it for a tendon graft.
Some people are born without a palmaris muscle in one or both arms.
To test for the palmaris, press your thumb and little finger together and flex your wrist.
