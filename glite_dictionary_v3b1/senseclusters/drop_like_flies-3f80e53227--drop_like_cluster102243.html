drop like flies

verb
collapse or die in large numbers
5.7
Pronunciation: / drɑp laɪk flaɪz / (AmE), / drɒp laɪk flaɪz / (BrE)
(of many people or things) to collapse, die, or drop out in large numbers, typically suddenly or rapidly.

Examples

People drop like flies
After the virus outbreak, employees were dropping like flies at the office.
During the marathon, runners began to drop like flies as the heat intensified.
The competitors dropped like flies after the grueling first round of the tournament.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fall like dominoes - fail or collapse one after another in quick succession
