nag

verb
repeatedly complain or criticize
4.5 2.2
Pronunciation: /næg/ (AmE), /næg/ (BrE)
to repeatedly criticize, complain, or urge someone in an annoying way

Examples

To nag a friend
He nagged his friends to join the club until they finally gave in.
If you nag your partner too much, it can damage the relationship.
She tends to nag her children about keeping their rooms clean.
He nagged his friend until he finally agreed to come to the gym.
She would nag her husband every day about quitting smoking.
Stop nagging me about my homework; I will do it later.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bother - to take the trouble to do something; annoy or worry someone
  • antonym: encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope to someone
  • synonym: harass - to subject to aggressive pressure or intimidation
  • synonym: pester - to trouble or annoy someone repeatedly
