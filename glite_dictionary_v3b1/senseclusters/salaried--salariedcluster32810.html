salaried

adjective
receiving a regular salary
5.8 2.8
Pronunciation: /ˈsæl.ə.riːd/ /; / /ˈsæl.ə.rɪd/ (AmE), /ˈsæl.ə.rɪd/ /; / /ˈsæl.ə.riːd/ (BrE)
receiving a regular, fixed salary for work, rather than being paid by the hour or for each task

Examples

Salaried employee
All salaried employees will receive health insurance benefits.
Many salaried workers have set office hours but sometimes work overtime.
The company offered both salaried and hourly positions.
He moved from an hourly wage job to a salaried position last year.
Many salaried employees do not track the exact hours they work each week.
The company offers benefits only to its salaried staff, not to part-time workers.
Many workers prefer salaried employment for the stability it offers.
She accepted a salaried position as a project manager at the company.
Teachers in this district are salaried employees with excellent benefits.

Related Terms

  • synonym: paid - receiving payment for work
  • synonym: stipendiary - receiving a stipend or fixed regular payment
  • antonym: waged - receiving wages, usually paid hourly or per day/week
