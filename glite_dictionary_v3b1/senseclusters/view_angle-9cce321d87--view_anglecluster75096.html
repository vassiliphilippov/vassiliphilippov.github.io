view angle

noun
angle covered by a camera lens
Pronunciation: / vju ˈæŋɡəl / (AmE), / vju ˈæŋɡəl / (BrE)
the angle covered by a photographic lens, measuring how much of a scene can be captured by the lens

Examples

A wide view angle
A wide view angle is ideal for photographing large groups or scenery.
By adjusting the view angle, the photographer was able to include more of the city skyline in the picture.
The lens's view angle determines how much of the landscape you can capture in one shot.
