draft

noun
large, hurried swallow
8.5
Pronunciation: / dræft / (AmE), / drɑːft / (BrE)
a swallow or serving of liquid, especially beer or medicine

Examples

A draft of beer
He emptied his glass with a single draft and wiped his mouth.
She took a deep draft of water after her run.
With each hearty draft, the mug of beer became lighter.
I prefer my beer on draft rather than in a bottle.
The pub offers several local ales on draft.
Would you like a draft or a bottled beer?
He sipped the bitter draft before going to bed.
Only after taking the draft did he finally fall asleep.
The doctor gave her a draft to help with her cough.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bottle - a drink served from a bottle
  • synonym: draft - American English spelling for beer served from a keg or barrel
  • synonym: draught - dose of liquid medicine (chiefly British spelling)
  • synonym: gulp - an act of swallowing quickly or in large amounts
  • synonym: mixture - a medicinal liquid preparation, especially one made up by mixing different ingredients
  • antonym: sip - a small, gentle swallow of liquid
