journal

noun
specialized periodical publication
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɝːnəl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɜːnəl / (BrE)
a magazine or periodical that publishes articles and research on a particular subject or academic field

Examples

Medical journal article
Many doctors keep up to date by reading the latest medical journals.
She submitted her research article to an academic journal for publication.
The library subscribes to several scientific journals each year.

Related Terms

  • antonym: book - a written or printed work consisting of pages bound together, not regularly published like a journal
  • synonym: magazine - a periodical publication containing articles and illustrations, often on a particular subject
  • synonym: newsletter - a report or periodical sent out to a group, typically relating to a particular topic
