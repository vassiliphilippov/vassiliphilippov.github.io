bivariate

adjective
involving two variables
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪˈvɛr.i.ət / (AmE), / ˌbaɪˈveə.ri.ət / (BrE)
involving or depending on two variables, especially in statistics or mathematics.

Examples

Bivariate data analysis
In statistics, a bivariate distribution examines the interaction of two different variables.
Researchers conducted a bivariate study to explore the correlation between income and education levels.
The bivariate analysis helped reveal the relationship between height and weight.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: multivariate - involving more than two variables
  • antonym: univariate - involving only one variable
