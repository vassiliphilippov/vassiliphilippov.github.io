recover

verb
regain financial condition after a loss
3.1 2.5
Pronunciation: / rəˈkʌvər /; / rɪˈkʌvər /; / rɪˈkʌvɚ / (AmE), / rɪˈkʌvə / (BrE)
to return to a normal or previous state, or to get back something lost (such as health, strength, control, or possessions)

Examples

To recover from illness
It took several years for the business to recover from its substantial losses.

After the accident, it took several months to recover his strength.
She worked hard to recover the money she had invested.
The company managed to recover most of its losses after the market crash.
After several weeks, she finally began to recover from the flu.
He needed support from family and friends to recover from the sudden loss.
It can take a long time to recover after a major operation.
