Diplococcus pneumoniae

noun
bacterium causing pneumonia (historical name)
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪpləˈkɑːkəs nuˌmoʊˈniː.aɪ / (AmE), / ˌdɪpləˈkɒkəs njuːˌməʊˈnɪː.aɪ / (BrE)
a species of bacterium, formerly known as Diplococcus pneumoniae, that causes pneumonia in humans and mice; now commonly called Streptococcus pneumoniae

Examples

Diplococcus pneumoniae bacterium
Early studies on Diplococcus pneumoniae provided key insights into the genetic mechanisms of transformation.
Pneumonia caused by Diplococcus pneumoniae was once a major health concern before the introduction of antibiotics.
The discovery of Diplococcus pneumoniae significantly advanced the understanding of bacterial diseases.
