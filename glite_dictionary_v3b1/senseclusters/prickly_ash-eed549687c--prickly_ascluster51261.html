prickly ash

noun
Australian tree with prickly-toothed leaves and white flowers
3.1 7.8
Pronunciation: /ˈprɪk.liː æʃ/ (AmE), /ˈprɪk.liː æʃ/ (BrE)
An Australian tree with alternate simple leaves (pinnate and prickly-toothed when young) and slender spikes of white flowers.

Examples

Australian prickly ash
A prickly ash was planted in the botanical garden for educational purposes.
The bark of the prickly ash is sometimes used in traditional remedies.
Young prickly ash trees have leaves with sharp, toothed margins.
