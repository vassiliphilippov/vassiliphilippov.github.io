Francis turbine

noun
water turbine used in hydroelectric plants
6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈfræn.sɪs ˈtɜːr.baɪn/ /; / /ˈfræn.sɪs ˈtɜːr.bɪn/ (AmE), /ˈfræn.sɪs ˈtɜː.baɪn/ /; / /ˈfræn.sɪs ˈtɜː.bɪn/ (BrE)
A type of water turbine used in hydroelectric power plants, with inward-flow radial design, suitable for wide ranges of water flow and head.

Examples

A Francis turbine design
Engineers examined the Francis turbine to ensure it was operating efficiently.
Modern dam projects often install multiple Francis turbines for maximum energy output.
The Francis turbine is widely used in medium-head hydroelectric power plants around the world.
