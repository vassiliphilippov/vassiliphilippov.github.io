blabbermouthed

adjective
prone to reveal secrets; talks too much
Pronunciation: / ˈblæbɚˌmaʊθt /; / ˈblæbərˌmaʊθt / (AmE), / ˈblæbəˌmaʊθt / (BrE)
(of a person) talking too much and likely to reveal secrets or confidential information; indiscreet

Examples

Blabbermouthed neighbor
Jane regretted sharing her idea with her blabbermouthed neighbor.
Our blabbermouthed friend told everyone about the surprise party.
You can't trust a blabbermouthed coworker with sensitive company plans.
Don't be blabbermouthed about what we discussed in the meeting.
He was so blabbermouthed that nobody trusted him with secret information.
My blabbermouthed cousin revealed the surprise party plans to everyone.

Related Terms

  • antonym: secretive - inclined to conceal feelings and intentions or not to disclose information
  • synonym: talkative - tending to talk a lot
  • antonym: tight-lipped - unwilling to speak or reveal information
