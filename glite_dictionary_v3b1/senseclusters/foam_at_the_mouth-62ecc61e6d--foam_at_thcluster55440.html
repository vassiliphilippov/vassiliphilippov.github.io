foam at the mouth

verb
show extreme anger
Pronunciation: / foʊm æt ðə maʊθ / (AmE), / fəʊm æt ðə maʊθ / (BrE)
to show extreme or uncontrolled anger or excitement in a very visible way

Examples

To foam at the mouth angrily
He was so frustrated by the delay that he began to foam at the mouth during the meeting.
The fans started foaming at the mouth when the referee made a controversial call.
Whenever critics attack her work, she seems to foam at the mouth with indignation.
He was so upset by the accusation that he began to foam at the mouth.
She almost foamed at the mouth during the heated argument.
The fans foam at the mouth whenever their team scores a last-minute goal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fly off the handle - to suddenly become very angry
  • synonym: froth at the mouth - to be extremely angry or agitated; a close synonym
  • synonym: lose one's temper - to fail to control one's anger
