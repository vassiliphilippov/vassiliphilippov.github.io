idler

noun
passive pulley that guides or tensions a belt
7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈaɪ.də.lər/ (AmE), /ˈaɪ.dlə/ /; / /ˈaɪ.də.lə/ (BrE)
a passive wheel or pulley in a belt-drive or conveyor system that guides, supports, or maintains tension on a belt without transmitting drive power.

Examples

Idler pulley
The mechanic replaced the worn idler to stop the belt from slipping under load.
On inspection the engineer found the faulty idler pulley caused misalignment and loud noise.
A properly positioned idler keeps the conveyor belt centered while distributing tension evenly across rollers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pulley - a wheel on an axle used to change direction of force or transmit movement in belt and rope systems.
