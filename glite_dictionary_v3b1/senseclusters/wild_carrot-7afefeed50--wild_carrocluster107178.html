wild carrot

noun
a plant with lacy leaves and white flower clusters
Pronunciation: / waɪld ˈkærət / (AmE), / waɪld ˈkærət / (BrE)
a tall plant with finely divided leaves and umbels of small white or yellowish flowers, native to Eurasia and naturalized widely; also known as Queen Anne's lace

Examples

The wild carrot plant
Early settlers sometimes used wild carrot roots as a food source, though they are much tougher than cultivated carrots.
She picked a bouquet of wild carrot and daisies for the kitchen table.
The wild carrot blooms throughout the summer in meadows and along roadsides.
