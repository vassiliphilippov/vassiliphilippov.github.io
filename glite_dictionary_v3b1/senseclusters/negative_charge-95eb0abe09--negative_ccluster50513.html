negative charge

noun
electrical charge with excess electrons
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛɡətɪv tʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / ˈnɛɡətɪv tʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
an electrical charge resulting from having more electrons than protons, giving an object or particle a lower electric potential

Examples

Negative charge of electrons
Electrons are particles that carry a negative charge in an atom.
Lightning involves the sudden movement of negative charge between clouds and the ground.
The balloon acquired a negative charge after being rubbed against the wool sweater.
