quietus

noun
poetic/literary term for death
Pronunciation: / kwaɪˈitəs / (AmE), / kwaɪˈiːtəs / (BrE)
A poetic or literary term for death or final rest.

Examples

Final quietus
Shakespeare used the word quietus to mean a final escape from life’s troubles.
She met her quietus peacefully in her sleep, surrounded by family.
The poem refers to winter as the earth’s quietus, a gentle rest until spring.

Related Terms

  • synonym: death - the end of life
