ridge

verb
plough alternate strips, raising ridges of soil
7.5
Pronunciation: / rɪdʒ / (AmE), / rɪdʒ / (BrE)
to form or make long, narrow, raised lines or strips, especially in soil or on a surface

Examples

To ridge the soil
In certain areas, it is common practice to ridge the land before planting potatoes.
The farmer decided to ridge his field to improve drainage.
They ridged the soil in the spring, preparing it for the next crop.
After the heavy plowing, the field ridged unevenly across its surface.
The designer chose a pattern that ridges the fabric for added texture.
The mountain range ridges sharply along the northern border.
Farmers often ridge the soil to improve drainage and root growth.
In spring, we spend several days ridging the field to prepare for crops.
The gardener ridged each row before planting the potatoes.
