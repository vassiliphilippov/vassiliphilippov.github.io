span

noun
distance or interval between two points
Pronunciation: / spæn / (AmE), / spæn / (BrE)
the distance, interval, or full period between two points in space or time

Examples

A span of years
The bridge has a central span of 200 meters.
The falcon's wings had an impressive span when fully extended.
Within the short span of an hour, everything changed.
A human life span can vary greatly depending on circumstances.
Over the span of his career, he won several awards.
The project was completed within a span of two weeks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: duration - the period of time something lasts
  • synonym: extent - the area covered by something
  • synonym: interval - a period of time between events
  • degree lower: moment - a very short period of time
  • synonym: stretch - a continuous period or extent of something
