spill

verb
cause a solid to flow or run out
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /spɪl/ (AmE), /spɪl/ (BrE)
to accidentally or carelessly cause a liquid or substance to flow or fall out of a container

Examples

To spill water
Children often spill snacks on the carpet during playtime.
He accidentally spilled the rice onto the kitchen floor.
Please be careful not to spill the sugar when you pour it into the bowl.
Be careful not to spill your coffee on the papers.
Oil spilled from the broken pipe onto the ground.
She accidentally spilled water all over the kitchen floor.
Many songs mention the price paid by those who spill their courage on the battlefield.
She tried not to spill any tears during the farewell.
Soldiers were ordered not to spill blood unless absolutely necessary.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pour - to cause liquid to flow continuously in a stream
  • synonym: scatter - to throw loosely about; distribute at irregular intervals
  • synonym: shed - to allow to flow out, especially in drops; e.g., 'shed tears'
