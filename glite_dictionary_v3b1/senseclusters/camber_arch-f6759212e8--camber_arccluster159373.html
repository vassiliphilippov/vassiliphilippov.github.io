camber arch

noun
arch with straight extrados, arched intrados
5.2 7.1
Pronunciation: /ˈkæm.bər ɑːrtʃ/ /; / /ˈkæm.bər ɑːk/ (AmE), /ˈkæm.bə ɑːtʃ/ /; / /ˈkæm.bə ɑːk/ (BrE)
An arch with a straight horizontal upper surface (extrados) and a slightly curved lower surface (intrados), commonly used in construction.

Examples

A camber arch structure
A camber arch combines the appearance of a flat arch with the strength of a slight curve.
The engineer recommended a camber arch for the doorway to handle the structural load.
Traditional brick bridges often feature the camber arch in their design.
