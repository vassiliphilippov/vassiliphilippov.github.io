withered

adjective
lean and wrinkled, shrunken
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪðərd / (AmE), / ˈwɪðəd / (BrE)
dry, shriveled, and lifeless, often due to age, lack of water, or illness (especially of plants or living things)

Examples

A withered plant
After months without rain, the crops appeared withered and lifeless.
Her withered hands trembled as she poured the tea for her guests.
The withered leaves crunched beneath his feet as he walked through the garden.
After weeks without rain, the once-green fields were now covered in withered grass.
The old bouquet sat in the vase, its withered petals crumbling at a touch.
They could see withered vines clinging to the abandoned garden wall.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dried-up - having lost all moisture, especially through heat or lack of water
  • antonym: fresh - (of plants) not dried or withered, retaining moisture and life
  • synonym: sere - (of plants) dry or withered
  • synonym: shrivelled - lean and wrinkled from shrinkage, especially from dryness or age
  • synonym: wizen - to become dry, shrunken, and wrinkled, often due to aging
