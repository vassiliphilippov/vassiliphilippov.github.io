review

verb
look back on; recall events
2.3 1.61/m
Pronunciation: / r…™Ààvju / (AmE), / r…™ÀàvjuÀê / (BrE)
to look back on or think about something that happened in the past, especially to assess or remember it

Examples

To review the year
After the ceremony, she reviewed her achievements with a sense of pride.
He often reviews the past year at the beginning of each New Year.
Sitting by the fire, they reviewed the events of their long friendship.

Synonyms

  • reflect
    3.9 32/m
    reflect is to think deeply and examine ideas or feelings, while review is to look back and assess events or work.
  • retrace
    4.4 0.68/m
    retrace is to go back over events step by step to rebuild what happened, while review is to look back and assess them.
  • look back
    2.6 16/m
    look back means to think about past times, often with feeling, while review focuses on looking back to judge or assess.
  • cast one's mind back
    6.3 0.05/m
    cast one's mind back is used to ask someone to remember deliberately, while review describes looking back to assess what happened.
  • think back
    1.9 4.52/m
    think back means to try to remember a past detail, often briefly, while review means to examine or assess past events more fully.

Related Terms

  • antonym: forget - to be unable to remember
  • synonym: recall - to bring a memory of something into your mind
