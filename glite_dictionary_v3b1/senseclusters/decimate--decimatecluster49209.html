decimate

verb
kill or destroy in large numbers
2.1
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛsɪˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛsɪmeɪt / (BrE)
to kill or destroy a large proportion of a group or population

Examples

To decimate a population
An invasive disease can decimate crops if not controlled quickly.
The wildfire decimated large areas of forest in just a few hours.
War and famine decimated the country's population over the past decade.

Related Terms

  • synonym: destroy - to damage something so badly it no longer exists or cannot be repaired
  • synonym: devastate - to cause great destruction or damage to something
