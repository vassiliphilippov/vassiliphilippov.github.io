posthypnotic suggestion

noun
suggestion made under hypnosis, acted on after waking
6.7 6.0
Pronunciation: /poʊst.hɪp.ˈnɑː.tɪk sə.ˈdʒes.tʃən/ (AmE), /pəʊst.hɪp.ˈnɒ.tɪk sə.ˈdʒes.tʃən/ (BrE)
a suggestion given to a person under hypnosis, intended to cause a specific action or response after the person has awakened

Examples

Posthypnotic suggestion effect
A common use of posthypnotic suggestion is to help people break bad habits.
He was surprised when he followed a posthypnotic suggestion to cluck like a chicken after the session.
The therapist gave her a posthypnotic suggestion to feel calm when she heard a bell.
