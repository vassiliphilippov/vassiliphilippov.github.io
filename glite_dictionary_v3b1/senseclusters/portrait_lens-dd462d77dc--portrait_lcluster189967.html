portrait lens

noun
camera lens with large aperture for portraits
1.5 4.4
Pronunciation: /ˈpɔːr.trət lenz/ /; / /ˈpɔːr.treɪt lenz/ (AmE), /ˈpɔː.trɪt lenz/ /; / /ˈpɔː.treɪt lenz/ (BrE)
A camera lens with a large maximum aperture, designed to take clear photographs of people, usually with a shallow depth of field.

Examples

A portrait lens for photos
Many photographers prefer using a portrait lens for capturing stunning headshots.
She invested in a new portrait lens to improve the quality of her studio portraits.
The 85mm portrait lens produces beautifully blurred backgrounds in my photos.

Related Terms

  • synonym: telephoto lens - a camera lens with a longer focal length than standard, often used for similar portrait effects
