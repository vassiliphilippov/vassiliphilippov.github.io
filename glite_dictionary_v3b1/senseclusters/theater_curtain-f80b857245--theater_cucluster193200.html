theater curtain

noun
curtain concealing the stage in a theater
2.9 2.3
Pronunciation: /ˈθiː.ə.tər ˈkɜːr.tən/ /; / /ˈθɪ.ə.tər ˈkɜːr.tən/ (AmE), /ˈθiː.ə.tə ˈkɜː.tən/ /; / /ˈθɪ.ə.tə ˈkɜː.tən/ (BrE)
a large hanging cloth in a theater that hides the stage from the audience, raised or parted at the start of a performance and lowered or closed between acts or at the end

Examples

The theater curtain rises
As the theater curtain rose, the audience quieted in anticipation of the opening scene.
She waited backstage, nervously watching the theater curtain for her cue to enter.
The red velvet theater curtain closed slowly, marking the end of the performance.
As the theatre curtain closed, the audience applauded enthusiastically.
She waited nervously behind the theatre curtain before making her entrance.
The theatre curtain slowly rose to reveal the first scene of the play.

Related Terms

  • synonym: curtain - a piece of cloth used to cover or conceal an opening, such as a window or stage
