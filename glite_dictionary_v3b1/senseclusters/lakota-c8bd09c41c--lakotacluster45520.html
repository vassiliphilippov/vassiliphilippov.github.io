Lakota

noun
member of the western Sioux people
8.8
Pronunciation: /lə.ˈkoʊ.tə/ /; / /lɑː.ˈkoʊ.tə/ (AmE), /lə.ˈkəʊ.tə/ (BrE)
A member of the Western branch of the Sioux people, consisting of several groups who traditionally lived on the plains of North America.

Examples

A Lakota elder
A respected Lakota elder spoke at the community gathering about tribal history.
Many modern Lakota people continue to practice their cultural traditions.
The Lakota were known for their skills as horsemen and warriors on the Great Plains.
