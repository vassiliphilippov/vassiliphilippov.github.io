radical

adjective
new and far-reaching in effect
Pronunciation: / ˈrædɪkəl /; / ˈræd.ɪ.kəl / (AmE), / ˈrædɪkəl /; / ˈræd.ɪ.kəl / (BrE)
(of change, ideas, or opinions) very new, different from the usual, or affecting the fundamental nature of something; advocating thorough or complete reform

Examples

Radical change
Her radical proposal challenged every established norm in the industry.
Scientists have made a radical breakthrough in the search for a cure.
The new government implemented a radical set of reforms to overhaul the education system.
She proposed a radical solution that would overhaul the entire organization.
The committee called for radical changes to address the system's deep flaws.
There is a radical difference between minor errors and fundamental structural problems.
His radical views often spark heated debates among his colleagues.
She called for radical changes in the educational system.
The new policy represents a radical departure from past practices.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conservative - holding to traditional attitudes and values and cautious about change or innovation
  • synonym: extreme - very far from what is considered normal, especially in opinions or actions
  • synonym: fundamental - forming a necessary base or core; of central importance
  • degree lower: moderate - not extreme; within reasonable limits
  • synonym: revolutionary - involving or causing a complete or dramatic change
