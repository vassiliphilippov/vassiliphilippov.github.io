bureaucratism

noun
system of government by nonelected officials
5.0 7.1
Pronunciation: /bjʊə.rə.ˈkræt.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /bjuː.rə.ˈkræt.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /bjʊə.rə.ˈkræt.ɪ.zəm/ /; / /bjuː.rə.ˈkræt.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
The system or practice of government by nonelected officials, often characterized by excessive adherence to rules and procedures.

Examples

Excessive bureaucratism rules
Critics argued that increased bureaucratism led to unnecessary delays in processing applications.
Efforts to reduce bureaucratism have been central to government reforms in recent years.
The organization suffered from bureaucratism, making it difficult to adapt to new challenges.
