Indian tobacco

noun
North American plant, Lobelia inflata
Pronunciation: / ˈɪndiən təˈbækoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən təˈbækəʊ / (BrE)
A North American wild plant (Lobelia inflata) with small blue flowers and inflated seed capsules, formerly used in medicine as an antispasmodic.

Examples

Indian tobacco flowers
Indian tobacco is known for its attractive blue flowers and inflated seed pods.
Some herbalists still value Indian tobacco for its purported antispasmodic effects.
The dried leaves of Indian tobacco were once commonly used in traditional medicine.
