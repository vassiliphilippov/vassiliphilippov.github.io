Nazarene

noun
person from Nazareth
Pronunciation: / ˌnæzəˈriːn / (AmE), / ˌnæzəˈriːn / (BrE)
A native or inhabitant of Nazareth, an ancient town in Galilee, especially in historical or biblical contexts.

Examples

A Nazarene resident
He identified himself as a Nazarene in the historical documents.
Many early Christians were referred to as Nazarene because they followed Jesus of Nazareth.
The village once known as Nazareth is the birthplace of the Nazarene.
