expect

verb
regard as reasonable or due
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈspɛkt / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɛkt / (BrE)
to demand, require, or consider something as necessary or appropriate

Examples

To expect good service
Customers expected a higher quality of service from the hotel.
I expect your full cooperation during this project.
The teacher expects her students to submit their assignments on time.
Parents often expect their children to help with chores around the house.
The manager expects everyone to attend the weekly meeting without exception.
We expect our guests to arrive promptly at seven o'clock.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ask - to request someone to do something
  • synonym: demand - to ask for something forcefully, as one's right
  • degree lower: request - to politely or formally ask for
  • synonym: require - to need or expect as a necessary condition
