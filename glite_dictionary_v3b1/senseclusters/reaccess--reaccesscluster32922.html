reaccess

verb
to access again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈæksɛs / (AmE), / ˌriːˈæksɛs / (BrE)
to access something again, or to restore access to something (such as a file, system, or location)

Examples

To reaccess your account
After the system update, you will need to reaccess your account using your new credentials.
If you forget your password, follow the instructions to reset it and then reaccess the website.
The technician had to reaccess the server to complete the maintenance work.
The IT department had to reaccess the files after the system update.
If you forget your password, an administrator can reaccess your account for you.
After the permissions were changed, she was able to reaccess the shared folder.

Related Terms

  • synonym: access - to enter or use something, especially a computer system or file
  • synonym: restore - to bring back to a previous state or condition
  • antonym: revoke - to take away or cancel access or permission
