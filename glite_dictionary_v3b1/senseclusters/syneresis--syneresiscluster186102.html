syneresis

noun
liquid separation from a gel
9.9 9.1
Pronunciation: /sɪ.ˈne.rəs/ /; / /sɪ.ˈne.rɪs/ (AmE), /sɪ.ˈne.rɪs/ /; / /sɪ.ˈne.rəs/ (BrE)
the separation of liquid from a gel caused by contraction, often observed in processes like cheese making or gelatin setting

Examples

Syneresis in yogurt
During yogurt production, syneresis can be seen as liquid whey separates from the gel.
Excessive syneresis can affect the texture and appearance of cheese.
To prevent syneresis, the custard must be cooked gently and stirred constantly.
