headedness

noun
having a central element
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛdɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈhɛdɪdnəs / (BrE)
the quality of having a central or leading element, especially in grammar or organizational structures

Examples

Phrase headedness
In linguistics, headedness determines which word in a phrase is the most important.
The headedness of a team can affect how decisions are made within the group.
Understanding headedness helps explain the structure of complex sentences.

Related Terms

  • synonym: centrality - the state of being central or most important
  • synonym: hierarchy - a system in which elements are ranked or organized by importance
