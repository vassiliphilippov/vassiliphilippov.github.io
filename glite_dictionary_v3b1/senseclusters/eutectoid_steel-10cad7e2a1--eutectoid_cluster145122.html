eutectoid steel

noun
steel with about 0.9% carbon (pure pearlite)
8.1
Pronunciation: /juː.ˈtek.tɔɪd stiːl/ (AmE), /juː.ˈtek.tɔɪd stiːl/ (BrE)
A type of carbon steel containing approximately 0.9% carbon, at which it transforms entirely into pearlite upon cooling.

Examples

Eutectoid steel hardness
Most cutting tools are made from eutectoid steel because of its high hardness.
Pearlite is the microstructure seen in pure eutectoid steel after slow cooling.
When heated and cooled properly, eutectoid steel forms a uniform pearlite structure throughout.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: hypereutectoid steel - steel with more than 0.9% carbon content
  • degree lower: hypoeutectoid steel - steel with less than 0.9% carbon content
