Maranta arundinaceae

noun
plant yielding arrowroot starch
9.9
Pronunciation: / məˈræntə əˌrʌndəˈneɪsiˌi / (AmE), / məˈræntə əˌrʌndɪˈniːsiː / (BrE)
A white-flowered tropical plant of the West Indies whose root produces arrowroot starch, used in cooking and industry.

Examples

Maranta arundinaceae plant
Maranta arundinaceae is cultivated in many tropical countries for its edible starch known as arrowroot.
The delicate white flowers of Maranta arundinaceae make it a unique addition to botanical gardens.
Traditional Caribbean recipes sometimes use Maranta arundinaceae starch as a thickener in desserts.
