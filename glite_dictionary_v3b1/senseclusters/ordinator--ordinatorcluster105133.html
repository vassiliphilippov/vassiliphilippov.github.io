ordinator

noun
person conferring ordination
Pronunciation: / ˈɔːrdəˌneɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɔːdɪneɪtə / (BrE)
a person, especially a priest, who confers holy orders or performs an ordination ceremony

Examples

Priest ordinator at ceremony
The ordinator led the ceremony with great solemnity and care.
Each ordinator must be authorized by the church to perform the rite.
During the service, the ordinator placed his hands on the new priest's head.
