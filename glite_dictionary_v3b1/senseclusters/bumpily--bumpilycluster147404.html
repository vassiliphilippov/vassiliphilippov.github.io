bumpily

adverb
not smoothly
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌmpɪli / (AmE), / ˈbʌmpɪli / (BrE)
in a way that is not smooth, with bumps or uneven movement

Examples

To move bumpily
The car moved bumpily along the old country road full of potholes.
Their relationship progressed bumpily due to frequent misunderstandings and arguments.
The train started bumpily before settling into a smoother ride.
The old bus moved bumpily along the rough country road, making everyone bounce in their seats.
He answered the question bumpily, pausing and stumbling over his words in front of the crowd.
The project started bumpily but became more organized as the team gained experience.
The old bus traveled bumpily over the country roads.
After the storm, the plane landed bumpily on the wet runway.
The bike rolled bumpily across the gravel path, making it hard to steer.
