cleg

noun
a large bloodsucking fly (horsefly)
9.7
Pronunciation: / klɛg / (AmE), / klɛg / (BrE)
A large, swift fly, especially the horsefly, the female of which sucks the blood of various animals.

Examples

A cleg fly
Farmers often complain about clegs bothering their horses and cows.
In the summer, the woods were filled with clegs buzzing around the cattle.
She was bitten by a cleg while hiking through the marshland.

Related Terms

  • synonym: horsefly - a large biting fly that feeds on the blood of mammals
