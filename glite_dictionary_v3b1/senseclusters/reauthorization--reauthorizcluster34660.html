reauthorization

noun
renewal of approval
6.4
Pronunciation: /riː.ɔː.θə.rə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /riː.ɔː.θə.rə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the official act of granting permission or approval again, especially to renew a law, program, or funding

Examples

The reauthorization of funding
The government announced the reauthorization of the education funding program for another five years.
You will need to complete the reauthorization process before your access is restored.
Congress debated the reauthorization of the health care law before voting.
The reauthorization of the education bill was debated in Congress for several months.
Without reauthorization, the program would have lost its funding at the end of the year.
Lawmakers are working on the reauthorization of important environmental protection measures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: expiration - the end of a period of validity or permission
  • synonym: renewal - the process of making something valid again
