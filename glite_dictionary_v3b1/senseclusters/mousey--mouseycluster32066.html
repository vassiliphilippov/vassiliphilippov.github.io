mousey

adjective
quiet, timid, and ineffectual
Pronunciation: / ˈmaʊsi / (AmE), / ˈmaʊsi / (BrE)
(of a person) quiet, timid, and ineffectual in manner or appearance.

Examples

Mousey person
Despite his mousey demeanor, he had strong opinions that he kept to himself.
She has always been considered the mousey one in her extroverted family.
The mousey assistant rarely spoke up during meetings and preferred to stay in the background.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bold - showing willingness to take risks; confident and courageous
  • synonym: timid - showing a lack of courage or confidence; easily frightened
