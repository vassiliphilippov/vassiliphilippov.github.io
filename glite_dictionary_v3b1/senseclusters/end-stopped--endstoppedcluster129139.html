end-stopped

adjective
verse: having a pause at line end
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɛnd stɑpt / (AmE), / ˈɛnd stɒpt / (BrE)
(Of a line of verse) having a pause or a natural stop at the end, usually indicated by punctuation.

Examples

End-stopped line
In many sonnets, each line is end-stopped to create a deliberate, measured rhythm.
The poem's end-stopped lines give a strong sense of finality to each phrase.
Unlike enjambed verses, end-stopped lines often coincide with complete sentences.

Related Terms

  • antonym: enjambed - verse: having no pause at the end of the line, running over to the next
