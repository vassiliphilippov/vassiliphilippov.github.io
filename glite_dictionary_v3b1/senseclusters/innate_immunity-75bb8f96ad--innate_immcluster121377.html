innate immunity

noun
natural immunity present from birth
Pronunciation: / ɪˈneɪt ɪˈmjunɪti / (AmE), / ɪˈneɪt ɪˈmjuːnɪti / (BrE)
immunity to disease that is present from birth as part of an individual's natural biological makeup, without prior exposure to pathogens or immunization.

Examples

Strong innate immunity
A strong innate immunity can help prevent illness even before vaccines or treatments are needed.
Newborns rely heavily on innate immunity to protect them from infections before their immune systems mature.
Physical barriers like skin and mucous membranes are important components of innate immunity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: adaptive immunity - immunity developed after exposure to specific pathogens
