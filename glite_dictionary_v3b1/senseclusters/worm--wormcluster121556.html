worm

noun
self-replicating computer program
7.6
Pronunciation: / wɝːm / (AmE), / wɜːm / (BrE)
a self-replicating software program that spreads across computers or networks, often causing harm or consuming resources

Examples

A computer worm
A worm can spread rapidly, sometimes affecting thousands of machines within minutes.
The company suffered a major security breach after a worm infected all of its servers.
Unlike a virus, a worm does not need to attach itself to existing files in order to propagate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: virus - a malicious computer program that attaches itself to files and spreads to other systems
