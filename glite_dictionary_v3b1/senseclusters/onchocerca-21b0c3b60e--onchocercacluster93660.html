Onchocerca

proper noun
parasitic worm genus
Pronunciation: / ˌɑŋkoʊˈsɜrkə / (AmE), / ˌɒŋkəˈsɜːkə / (BrE)
a genus of parasitic worms that infect humans and animals, sometimes causing diseases such as river blindness

Examples

Onchocerca parasite worm
Onchocerca is responsible for causing river blindness in some regions.
Scientists study Onchocerca to develop treatments for parasitic infections.
The life cycle of Onchocerca involves both humans and blackflies as hosts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: filaria - a type of parasitic worm that can cause disease in humans
