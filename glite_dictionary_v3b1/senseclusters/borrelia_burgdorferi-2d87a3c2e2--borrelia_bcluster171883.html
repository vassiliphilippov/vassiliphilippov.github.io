Borrelia burgdorferi

noun
bacterium causing Lyme disease
Pronunciation: / bəˈriːliə bɜrɡˌdɔːrˈfɛri / (AmE), / bɒˈrɛliə bɜːɡˌdɔːˈfɛəri / (BrE)
a bacterium of the genus Borrelia that causes Lyme disease, transmitted mainly by ticks of the genus Ixodes

Examples

Borrelia burgdorferi bacterium
Antibiotics are commonly prescribed to eliminate Borrelia burgdorferi after a diagnosis of Lyme disease.
Researchers are developing new methods to detect Borrelia burgdorferi infection in humans and animals.
The tick tested positive for Borrelia burgdorferi, confirming a risk of Lyme disease in the area.
