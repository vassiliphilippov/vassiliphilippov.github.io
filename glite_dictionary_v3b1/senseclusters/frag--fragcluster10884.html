frag

verb
to attack fellow soldier
9.9
Pronunciation: / fræɡ / (AmE), / fræɡ / (BrE)
to deliberately kill or attempt to kill a fellow member of the military, especially a superior officer, often using a grenade or explosive.

Examples

To frag a sergeant
The unit was shaken after rumors spread that someone had fragged their sergeant during the night.
During the war, some soldiers feared being fragged by their own men if they were too strict.
He was court-martialed for allegedly fragging a fellow officer with an explosive device.

Related Terms

  • synonym: murder - to unlawfully kill someone intentionally
  • antonym: protect - to keep someone safe from harm
