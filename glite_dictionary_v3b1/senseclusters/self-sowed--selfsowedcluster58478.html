self-sowed

adjective
growing without human-sown seed
Pronunciation: / sɛlf ˈsoʊd / (AmE), / sɛlf ˈsəʊd / (BrE)
(of a plant) growing from seed that has been dispersed and germinated without human intervention.

Examples

A self-sowed plant
Several self-sowed sunflowers appeared in the garden this spring.
The meadow was full of self-sowed poppies from last year’s crop.
Wildflowers often become self-sowed after a season of strong winds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: self-seeded - growing from seed that dispersed and germinated on its own
