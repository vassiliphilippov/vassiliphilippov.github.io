cumulative preferred

noun
preferred stock with accumulating dividends
5.0 6.6
Pronunciation: /ˈkjuː.mjə.lə.tɪv prə.ˈfɜːrd/ /; / /ˈkjuː.mjʊ.leɪ.tɪv prɪ.ˈfɜːrd/ (AmE), /ˈkjuː.mjʊ.lə.tɪv prə.ˈfɜːd/ /; / /ˈkjuː.mjʊ.leɪ.tɪv prɪ.ˈfɜːd/ (BrE)
Preferred stock on which unpaid dividends accumulate and must be paid out before common stockholders receive dividends.

Examples

Cumulative preferred stock
Dividends missed on cumulative preferred stock must be paid before any are issued to common shareholders.
Investors often choose cumulative preferred stock for its guaranteed dividend payments.
The company failed to pay dividends on its cumulative preferred shares for two years in a row.
