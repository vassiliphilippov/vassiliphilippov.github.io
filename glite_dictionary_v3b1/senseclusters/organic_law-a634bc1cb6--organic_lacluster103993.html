organic law

noun
law establishing basic government principles
Pronunciation: / ɔrˈɡænɪk lɔ / (AmE), / ɔːˈɡænɪk lɔː / (BrE)
a law that establishes the fundamental principles or structure of a government

Examples

An organic law document
A constitution is considered an organic law because it sets out the basic framework for governance.
Any changes to the organic law require a supermajority vote in the legislature.
The country's organic law was amended to include new human rights protections.

Related Terms

  • synonym: constitution - the system of fundamental principles or established precedents by which a state is governed
