monolithic

adjective
large and solid in size or form
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑː.nəˈlɪθ.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒ.nəˈlɪθ.ɪk / (BrE)
Imposing in size, bulk, or solidity; massive and uniform in form or structure.

Examples

Monolithic stone wall
A monolithic wall of stone separated the ancient fortress from the rest of the city.
Soviet-era buildings are often criticized for their monolithic appearance and lack of subtlety.
The skyscraper's monolithic façade dominated the city skyline.

Related Terms

  • antonym: delicate - Very fine in texture or structure; fragile
  • synonym: massive - Large and heavy or solid
  • synonym: monumental - Impressively large or enduring
