exponential decay

noun
rapid decrease by exponential function
Pronunciation: / ˌɛkspoʊˈnɛntʃəl dɪˈkeɪ / (AmE), / ˌɛkspəˈnɛnʃəl dɪˈkeɪ / (BrE)
a decrease where the quantity diminishes at a rate proportional to its current value, following an exponential function

Examples

Exponential decay rate
Radioactive substances gradually disappear through exponential decay over time.
Scientists use the law of exponential decay to estimate the age of ancient artifacts.
The population of bacteria showed exponential decay after the introduction of the antibiotic.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exponential growth - increase that follows an exponential function
