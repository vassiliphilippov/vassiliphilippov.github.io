pearlfish

noun
a slender marine fish living inside sea creatures
Pronunciation: / ˈpɜrlˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈpɜːlˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a slender marine fish (family Carapidae) that lives inside sea cucumbers or between the shells of pearl oysters

Examples

A pearlfish in oyster
Researchers discovered a pearlfish living between the shells of a pearl oyster.
Some species of pearlfish have developed unique adaptations for surviving inside their unusual hosts.
The pearlfish can often be found hiding within the body of a sea cucumber.
Divers often encounter the elusive pearlfish while exploring coastal seagrass beds.
Scientists were surprised to find a pearlfish lodged in the oyster's shell.
The pearlfish is known for its unusual habit of living inside sea cucumbers.
