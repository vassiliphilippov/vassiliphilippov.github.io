heterochrony

noun
developmental timing change
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛtəˈrɑkrəni / (AmE), / ˌhɛtəˈrɒkrəni / (BrE)
a change in the timing or rate of developmental events in an organism, often leading to evolutionary differences

Examples

Heterochrony in development
Scientists study heterochrony to understand how changes in development can lead to new species.
The concept of heterochrony helps explain why some animals retain juvenile traits into adulthood.
Evolutionary biologists often discuss heterochrony when comparing the growth patterns of related species.

Related Terms

  • antonym: isomorphy - similarity in form or structure between organisms
  • synonym: paedomorphosis - the retention of juvenile features in the adult form
