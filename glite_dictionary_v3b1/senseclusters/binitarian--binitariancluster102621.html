binitarian

adjective
belief in two in Godhead
9.9
Pronunciation: /bɪ.nɪ.ˈter.iː.ən/ (AmE), /bɪ.nɪ.ˈteə.riː.ən/ (BrE)
relating to the belief that the Godhead consists of two persons or aspects

Examples

Binitarian doctrine
The church adopted a binitarian view, emphasizing the Father and the Son.
Some early Christian groups were known for their binitarian theology.
A binitarian doctrine stands in contrast to the more widespread trinitarian belief.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unitarian - relating to the belief that God is a single person
