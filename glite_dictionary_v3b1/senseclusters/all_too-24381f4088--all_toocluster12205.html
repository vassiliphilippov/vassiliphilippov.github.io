all too

adverb
to a greater (often excessive) degree
4.7 14/m
Pronunciation: / …îl tuÀê / (AmE), / …îÀêl tuÀê / (BrE)
to a much greater degree than is desirable or expected; very or excessively.

Examples

All too aware
He was all too aware of the risks involved in the project.
It became all too obvious that someone had made a mistake.
The children grew up all too quickly for their parents.

Synonyms

  • excessively

  • unduly
