asynclitism

noun
fetal head misalignment during labor
Pronunciation: / əˈsɪnklɪˌtɪzəm / (AmE), / əˈsɪnklɪˌtɪzəm / (BrE)
A condition during labor in which the head of the fetus is presented at an abnormal angle to the pelvis.

Examples

Asynclitism during labor
Asynclitism can sometimes be corrected with changes in the mother's position during labor.
During the difficult delivery, the doctor diagnosed asynclitism as a contributing factor.
Midwives are trained to recognize signs of asynclitism to manage labor more effectively.
