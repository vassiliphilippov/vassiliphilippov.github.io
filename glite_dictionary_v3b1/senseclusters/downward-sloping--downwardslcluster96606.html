downward-sloping

adjective
sloping down steeply
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊnwɚd ˈsloʊpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnwəd ˈsləʊpɪŋ / (BrE)
sloping down in direction, especially at a noticeable or steep angle.

Examples

A downward-sloping path
He stopped to rest on the downward-sloping path that led into the valley.
Housing prices often fall when there is a downward-sloping demand curve.
The road ahead was downward-sloping and covered in loose gravel, making it difficult to walk safely.

Related Terms

  • antonym: upward-sloping - sloping up rather steeply
