bulldoze

verb
flatten or clear with a bulldozer
4.2 1.54/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆbÊŠlËŒdoÊŠz / (AmE), / ËˆbÊŠlËŒdÉ™ÊŠz / (BrE)
to flatten or clear (an area) with a bulldozer or to forcefully remove or destroy something as if using a bulldozer

Examples

To bulldoze a site
Developers threatened to bulldoze the historic site despite protests from local residents.
The construction crew bulldozed a path through the forest for the new road.
They had to bulldoze the old building to make way for the new supermarket.

Synonyms

  • raze
    7.1 1.28/m
    raze levels a site completely to leave nothing standing, while bulldoze means flattening or forceful clearing of an area.
  • demolish
    3.7 3.14/m
    demolish tears a building down or breaks it into pieces, while bulldoze suggests flattening or forceful clearing with heavy machines.
  • tear down
    3.7 3.42/m
    tear down removes or destroys parts of a building, while bulldoze implies heavy flattening or clearing of an entire area.
