fenland

noun
low-lying wet land; marshy area
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛnˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈfɛnˌlænd / (BrE)
Low-lying wet land with grassy vegetation, often found as a transitional area between land and water, typically characterized by marshes or bogs.

Examples

The fenland area
Many rare birds nest in the wet grasses of the fenland.
The East Anglian fenland has been drained over centuries for agriculture.
The village lies on the edge of an ancient fenland region near the coast.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bog - wet spongy ground, similar but often more acidic
  • synonym: marshland - land consisting of marshes; similar wetland area
  • antonym: upland - high or hilly land; opposite of low-lying fenland
