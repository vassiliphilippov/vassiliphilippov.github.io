Solomon's seal

noun
six-pointed star symbolizing Judaism
9.1 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààs…ëÀêl…ôm…ônz siÀêl / (AmE), / Ààs…íl…ôm…ônz siÀêl / (BrE)
a six-pointed star formed by two overlapping equilateral triangles, used especially as a symbol of Judaism; also called the Star of David

Examples

A Solomon's seal necklace
He wore a necklace featuring a gold Solomon's seal as a sign of his faith.
Many synagogues display the Solomon's seal as a prominent emblem above their entrance.
The ancient manuscript was adorned with a Solomon's seal on its cover.

Synonyms

  • Star of David
    3.8 0.27/m
    Star of David is the common English name for the six-pointed Jewish symbol, while Solomon's seal is a less used variant.
  • Magen David
    6.5 0.01/m
    Magen David is the Hebrew name often used in Jewish contexts, while Solomon's seal is an older English name for the same symbol.
  • Shield of David
    6.0 0.00/m
    Shield of David is a literal, older English name sometimes used in history books, while Solomon's seal is a different English name.
