adytum

noun
temple inner sanctuary
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈædɪtəm / (AmE), / ˈædɪtəm / (BrE)
the innermost and most sacred part of an ancient temple, especially in classical antiquity, where only priests could enter

Examples

Temple adytum
Only the high priest was allowed to enter the adytum during the sacred ceremony.
The ancient temple's adytum was hidden from the public and shrouded in mystery.
Archaeologists discovered offerings left in the adytum of the ruined sanctuary.

Related Terms

  • synonym: altar - a table or flat-topped block used for religious rituals
  • synonym: sanctum - a sacred or private place
