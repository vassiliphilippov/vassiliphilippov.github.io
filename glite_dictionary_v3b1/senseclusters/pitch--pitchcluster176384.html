pitch

noun
up-and-down motion of a vessel or vehicle
6.9
Pronunciation: / pɪtʃ / (AmE), / pɪtʃ / (BrE)
the abrupt up-and-down motion of a ship, aircraft, or other vehicle, especially as caused by waves or turbulence

Examples

The pitch of a ship
Experienced sailors are accustomed to the pitch and roll of the sea.
The ship's pitch made walking across the deck quite difficult.
Turbulence caused considerable pitch and discomfort during the flight.

Related Terms

  • synonym: roll - side-to-side motion of a ship or aircraft
