Ull

proper noun
Norse god of archery and skiing
9.9
Pronunciation: / ul / (AmE), / ʊl / (BrE)
In Norse mythology, one of the Aesir gods, known for his beauty and skill with the bow and skis; the son of Sif and stepson of Thor.

Ull in Norse myths
In Norse mythology, Ull was revered as a master archer and skier.
Mythological tales often describe Ull as the son of Sif and the stepson of Thor.
The temple was dedicated to Ull, honoring his legendary skill on snow and ice.
