native

noun
a person originally from a place; indigenous inhabitant
Pronunciation: / ˈneɪ.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈneɪ.tɪv / (BrE)
a person belonging to an indigenous group originating from a particular place, especially as distinguished from later settlers or foreigners

Examples

Island natives
Many natives were displaced when settlers arrived on the island.
She studied the customs and languages of the region's natives during her fieldwork.
The museum has a special exhibit showcasing the traditional crafts of the natives of the Amazon.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aboriginal - a person belonging to the indigenous peoples of a place
  • antonym: settler - a person who moves to a new area to live, especially as part of a group colonizing it
