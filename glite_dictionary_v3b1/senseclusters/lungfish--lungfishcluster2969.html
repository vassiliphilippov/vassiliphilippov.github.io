lungfish

noun
freshwater fish with lungs and gills
Pronunciation: / ˈlʌŋˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈlʌŋ.fɪʃ / (BrE)
a type of freshwater fish that has lungs as well as gills and can breathe air, typically having an elongated body and fleshy paired fins.

Examples

The lungfish swims
Scientists study the lungfish to understand the evolution of air-breathing in vertebrates.
The lungfish can survive out of water for months by burrowing into mud and breathing air.
Unlike most fish, the lungfish has both gills and lungs, enabling it to thrive in harsh environments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coelacanth - ancient lobe-finned fish, related to lungfish
