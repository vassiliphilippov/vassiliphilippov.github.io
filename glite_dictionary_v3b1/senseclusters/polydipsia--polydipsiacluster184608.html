polydipsia

noun
abnormally excessive thirst
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːliˈdɪpsiə / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈdɪpsɪə / (BrE)
a condition characterized by excessive or abnormal thirst, often associated with diabetes or kidney disorders

Examples

Chronic polydipsia
Chronic polydipsia may be a warning sign of underlying kidney dysfunction.
Patients with diabetes often experience polydipsia due to elevated blood sugar levels.
The doctor noted that polydipsia and frequent urination were among the patient's primary symptoms.
