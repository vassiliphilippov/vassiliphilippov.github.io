antihemophilic factor

noun
blood-clotting protein (Factor VIII)
5.0 5.7
Pronunciation: /æn.ti.hiː.mə.ˈfɪl.ɪk ˈfæk.tər/ (AmE), /æn.ti.hiː.məʊ.ˈfɪl.ɪk ˈfæk.tə/ (BrE)
a protein present in blood that is essential for normal blood clotting, whose deficiency causes hemophilia A; also known as Factor VIII

Examples

Antihemophilic factor protein
A deficiency of antihemophilic factor leads to impaired blood clotting and increased bleeding risk.

The patient receives regular infusions of antihemophilic factor to manage his hemophilia A.
Modern therapies include the injection of antihaemophilic factor concentrates to prevent bleeding episodes.
Replacement of the antihaemophilic factor is crucial in treating patients with hemophilia A.
The laboratory tested his blood for antihaemophilic factor levels after he experienced unexplained bleeding.
