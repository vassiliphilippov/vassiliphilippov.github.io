hemolytic anemia

noun
anemia from destruction of red blood cells
Pronunciation: / ˌhiːməˈlɪtɪk əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˌhiːməˈlɪtɪk əˈniːmiə / (BrE)
Anemia caused by the abnormal destruction of red blood cells (erythrocytes).

Examples

Hemolytic anemia disease
Genetic disorders, such as sickle cell disease, can lead to hemolytic anemia.
The patient was diagnosed with hemolytic anemia after tests revealed rapid destruction of red blood cells.
Treatment for hemolytic anemia often involves managing the underlying cause of the red blood cell destruction.
Certain medications can sometimes trigger haemolytic anaemia in susceptible individuals.
People with haemolytic anaemia may experience fatigue, jaundice, and shortness of breath.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with haemolytic anaemia after observing a rapid loss of red blood cells.
