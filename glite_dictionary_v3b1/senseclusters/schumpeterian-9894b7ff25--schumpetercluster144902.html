Schumpeterian

noun
follower of Schumpeter
9.9
Pronunciation: /ʃʊm.ˈpiː.tɜːr.ɪ.ən/ /; / /ʃʊm.piː.ˈtɪə.rɪ.ən/ (AmE), /ʃʊm.ˈpiː.tə.rɪ.ən/ /; / /ʃʊm.piː.ˈtɪə.rɪ.ən/ (BrE)
a person who supports or follows the economic theories of Joseph Schumpeter, especially the idea that entrepreneurs and innovation drive economic change

Examples

A Schumpeterian economist
As a Schumpeterian, she argued that entrepreneurs are the main drivers of long-term economic growth.
Many Schumpeterians believe that innovation is the key to economic development.
The professor described himself as a Schumpeterian during the lecture on economic theory.
