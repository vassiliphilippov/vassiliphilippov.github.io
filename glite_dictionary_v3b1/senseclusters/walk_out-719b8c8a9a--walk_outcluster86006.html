walk out

verb
stop working in protest, as in a strike
4.1 0.41/m
Pronunciation: / w…ëk a ät / (AmE), / w…îÀêk a ät / (BrE)
(of employees) to stop working and leave as a form of protest or to press for demands, especially during a strike.

Examples

Workers walk out
Teachers across the city plan to walk out in support of better classroom funding.
The factory workers decided to walk out after negotiations with management broke down.
When their safety concerns were ignored, the engineers threatened to walk out the next day.

Synonyms

  • strike
    3.2 7.23/m
    strike is a collective refusal to work, often planned to demand better pay or conditions, while walk out is leaving work suddenly in protest.
  • down tools
    8.1 0.02/m
    down tools means workers stop working at once as a protest, while walk out emphasizes leaving the workplace to press demands.

Related Terms

  • synonym: strike - refusal to work as a form of protest by employees
