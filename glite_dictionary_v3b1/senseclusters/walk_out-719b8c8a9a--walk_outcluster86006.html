walk out

verb
stop working in protest, as in a strike
1.0 3.3
Pronunciation: /wɑːk aʊt/ /; / /wɔːk aʊt/ (AmE), /wɔːk aʊt/ (BrE)
(of employees) to stop working and leave as a form of protest or to press for demands, especially during a strike.

Examples

Workers walk out
Teachers across the city plan to walk out in support of better classroom funding.
The factory workers decided to walk out after negotiations with management broke down.
When their safety concerns were ignored, the engineers threatened to walk out the next day.
