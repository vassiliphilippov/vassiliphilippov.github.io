overbrowse

verb
to eat excessive plants
9.2
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ˈbraʊz/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ˈbraʊz/ (BrE)
to eat too much vegetation in an area, especially by animals such as deer, causing environmental damage.

Examples

To overbrowse plants
If deer overbrowse the area, young trees may never grow to maturity.
Park rangers monitor the forest to prevent animals from overbrowsing sensitive habitats.
Unchecked overbrowsing by goats led to the loss of many native plants.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: browse - to feed on leaves, twigs, or other high-growing vegetation
  • synonym: overgraze - to allow animals to eat so much grass or plants that the land is damaged
