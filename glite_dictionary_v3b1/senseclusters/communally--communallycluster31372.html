communally

adverb
collectively; as a community
4.1 0.43/m
Pronunciation: / k…ôÀàmjun…ôli / (AmE), / k…ôÀàmjuÀên…ôli / (BrE)
by a group of people collectively rather than by an individual; in a communal manner.

Examples

Farmed communally
The land is farmed communally by several families.
They made the decision communally after a long discussion.
Water resources are managed communally in the village.

Synonyms

  • collectively
    3.6 10/m
    collectively means acting as a group to reach one result, while communally suggests shared ownership and everyday use by a community.
  • cooperatively
    3.3 1.60/m
    cooperatively describes people working together with shared effort to reach a goal, while communally implies shared ownership and everyday use.
  • jointly
    3.5 5.50/m
    jointly means two or more parties act together in a planned way, while communally focuses on community sharing and common ownership.
  • together
    1.0 216/m
    together means people act in unison or at the same time, while communally suggests actions done for and by a community.
  • en masse
    5.6 2.08/m
    en masse often describes a large group moving or acting all at once, while communally describes shared life or ownership within a community.
