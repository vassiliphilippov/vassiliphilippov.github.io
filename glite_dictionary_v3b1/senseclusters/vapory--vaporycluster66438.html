vapory

adjective
vague or insubstantial
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈveɪpəri / (AmE), / ˈveɪpəri / (BrE)
lacking substance or solidity; airy, vague, or insubstantial, often used figuratively

Examples

Vapory promises
Her vapory promises left the team uncertain about the project's future.
The novel's plot felt vapory, with ideas that never fully developed.
He gave a vapory answer that avoided the main issue entirely.

Related Terms

  • synonym: insubstantial - lacking strength or solidity
  • antonym: solid - firm and stable in form or substance
