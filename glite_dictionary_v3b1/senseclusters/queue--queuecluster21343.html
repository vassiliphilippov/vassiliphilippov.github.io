queue

verb
to form or join a line while waiting
3.4 1.33/m
Pronunciation: / kju / (AmE), / kjuÀê / (BrE)
(of people) to form or join a line while waiting for something.

Examples

To queue for tickets
Dozens of fans started queueing outside the stadium early in the morning.
People queued patiently at the bus stop despite the rain.
We had to queue for over an hour to get tickets for the concert.

Synonyms

    3.1 0.24/m
    queue up is an informal phrasal verb used for forming a line in speech, while queue is the neutral verb for joining a line.
  • stand in line
    1.7 3.35/m
    stand in line is a common spoken phrase for waiting in place, while queue is the verb for forming or joining a line.
  • line up for
    2.1 1.06/m

  • file
    3.7 4.42/m
    file means to move in a neat one-after-another procession, while queue means to form or join a waiting line.
