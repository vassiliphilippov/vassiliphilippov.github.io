E-bomb

noun
bomb emitting disabling electromagnetic energy
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàiÀê b…ëm / (AmE), / ÀàiÀê b…ím / (BrE)
A bomb designed to explode in midair, releasing a powerful burst of electromagnetic energy that disables electronic devices without causing harm to people or buildings.

Examples

An E-bomb explosion

Fears of an E-bomb attack have prompted governments to strengthen their cyber defenses.
The military used an E-bomb to disable enemy radar systems without causing physical destruction.
