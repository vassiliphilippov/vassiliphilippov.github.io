Namtaru

proper noun
Mesopotamian death demon; Ereshkigal’s messenger
9.9
Pronunciation: /næm.ˈtɑː.ruː/ /; / /nɑːm.ˈtɑː.ruː/ (AmE), /næm.ˈtɑː.ruː/ /; / /nɑːm.ˈtɑː.ruː/ (BrE)
In Mesopotamian mythology, a demon who personifies death and serves as the messenger for the underworld goddess Ereshkigal, bringing death to humans.

Examples

Namtaru demon messenger
Archaeologists have discovered cuneiform tablets that reference Namtaru in rituals to ward off death.
In ancient texts, Namtaru is often described as the fearsome envoy who delivers death to mortals.
Legends recount how Namtaru would appear at the behest of Ereshkigal, spreading plague across the land.
