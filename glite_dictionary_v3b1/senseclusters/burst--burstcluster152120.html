burst

verb
break open suddenly and noisily
Pronunciation: / bɜrst /; / bɝst / (AmE), / bɜːst / (BrE)
to break open or apart suddenly and violently, often from internal pressure

Examples

To burst a balloon
She burst the confetti popper to celebrate the announcement.
The dam could burst if the pressure keeps rising.
The water balloon burst when it hit the pavement, splashing everyone nearby.
She laughed so hard she felt as if she might burst.
The pipe burst and flooded the basement within minutes.
When heated, the kernels quickly burst into popcorn.
The plumber warned that freezing temperatures might burst the pipes.
The pressure from the water could burst the dam if not controlled.
Too much air in the balloon will burst it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: explode - to burst violently and noisily as a result of rapid combustion
  • antonym: implode - to burst inward, as opposed to outward
  • synonym: rupture - to break or burst suddenly, especially of something hollow or containing fluid
