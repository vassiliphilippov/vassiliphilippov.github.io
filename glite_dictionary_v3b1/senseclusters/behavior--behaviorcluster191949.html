behavior

noun
way of acting or conducting oneself
1.0 1.6
Pronunciation: /bɪ.ˈheɪ.vjər/ /; / /bɪ.ˈheɪ.vər/ (AmE), /bɪ.ˈheɪ.vjə/ /; / /bɪ.ˈheɪ.və/ (BrE)
the way a person or animal acts or responds, especially toward others or in social situations or in a particular situation

Examples

Polite behavior
Good behavior is expected from all employees at work.
His behavior changed noticeably after the meeting.
The teacher praised the student's behavior during the assembly.
His polite behavior at the party impressed everyone.
She was rewarded for her good behavior during the school trip.
The teacher praised the student's respectful behavior toward her classmates.
Animal behaviour can often be predicted by observing environmental changes.
Psychologists study human behaviour to understand how people react in different situations.
The teacher noticed a sudden change in the student's behaviour during the semester.

  • synonym: conduct - the manner in which a person behaves, especially on a particular occasion or in a particular context
  • synonym: demeanor - outward behavior or bearing
  • antonym: misbehavior - bad or improper behavior
  • antonym: misbehaviour - bad or improper behaviour, especially by children
  • antonym: misconduct - unacceptable or improper behavior
