hip

noun
roof angle where two slopes meet
9.9
Pronunciation: / hɪp / (AmE), / hɪp / (BrE)
(architecture) The external angle formed where a sloping side and a sloping end of a roof meet.

Examples

Hip roof angle
The architect decided to emphasize the hip with decorative tiles.
The carpenter carefully measured the hip to ensure a perfect corner on the roof.
Water tends to run off quickly at the hip of a well-designed building.

Related Terms

  • antonym: valley - internal angle formed by the intersection of two roof slopes
