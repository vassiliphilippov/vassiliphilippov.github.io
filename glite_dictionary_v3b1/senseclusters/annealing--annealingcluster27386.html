annealing

noun
heat treatment process to alter material properties
6.9
Pronunciation: / əˈniːlɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈniːlɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of heating a material, such as metal or glass, and then gradually cooling it to make it less brittle or to alter its physical properties

Examples

Annealing metal process
Engineers recommend annealing after welding to relieve internal stresses.
Proper annealing of the glass prevents it from shattering easily.
The metal parts underwent annealing to improve their durability.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hardening - process of making a material harder
  • synonym: tempering - process of improving the hardness and elasticity of metal by reheating
