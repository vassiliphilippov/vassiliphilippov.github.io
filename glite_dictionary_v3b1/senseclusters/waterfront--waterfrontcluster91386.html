waterfront

noun
land or area next to water, often in a city
3.2 5.80/m
Pronunciation: / Ààw…ît…ôrÀåfr ånt / (AmE), / Ààw…îÀêt…ôrÀåfr ånt / (BrE)
the part of a town or city next to or along a body of water, especially where there are docks, wharves, or piers

Examples

The waterfront area
She enjoys taking evening walks on the waterfront to watch the boats.
The city's waterfront was redeveloped into a lively entertainment district.
There are many restaurants and shops along the waterfront.

Synonyms

  • shorefront
    3.6 0.09/m
    shorefront is land directly along a shore, often near beaches or ports, while waterfront is the city's edge with docks.
  • shoreside
    3.4 0.08/m
    shoreside is land used for buildings or services next to water, while waterfront is the part of a town with docks.
  • waterside
    3.2 0.22/m
    waterside is the land along a river, lake, or sea that can be rural or quiet, while waterfront is urban and built with piers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dockyard - an area with docks where ships are repaired or built
  • synonym: harbor - a place on the coast where vessels may find shelter
