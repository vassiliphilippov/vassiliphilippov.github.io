subordinate

adjective
lower in rank or importance
Pronunciation: / səˈbɔːrdɪnət /; / səˈbɔrdn̩ət / (AmE), / səˈbɔːdɪnət / (BrE)
lower in rank, position, or importance; under the authority or control of someone or something else

Examples

Subordinate role
Her needs became subordinate to those of her children after they were born.
In the company hierarchy, a manager is usually subordinate to a director.
The committee's recommendations were considered subordinate to the main policy.
Her position in the company remained subordinate to that of her older colleagues.
In many companies, managers have subordinate roles to directors.
The tribal leaders formed a subordinate council under the chief's authority.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inferior - lower in status or quality; used as a synonym in some contexts
  • antonym: superior - higher in rank or importance
