replace

verb
to put in the place of another
1.0
Pronunciation: / rɪˈpleɪs / (AmE), / rɪˈpleɪs / (BrE)
to put someone or something in the place of another; to substitute or provide a new thing or person for one that is broken, lost, or no longer effective

Examples

To replace a part
They had to replace the broken window before winter arrived.
When my old phone stopped working, I decided to replace it with a newer model.
You can replace sugar with honey in many recipes for a healthier alternative.
After the manager resigned, her assistant was chosen to replace her.
He will replace the outgoing president at the end of the year.
Many factories have begun to replace workers with machines.
I had to replace my phone after it stopped working last week.
If your card is lost, the bank will replace it free of charge.
They replaced the damaged tiles on the kitchen floor with new ones.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oust - to remove and replace someone from a position or place
  • antonym: remove - to take away something from a place
  • synonym: substitute - to use in place of another person or thing
  • synonym: succeed - take over a job, position, or title from another
  • synonym: supersede - take the place of something or someone previously in authority or use
