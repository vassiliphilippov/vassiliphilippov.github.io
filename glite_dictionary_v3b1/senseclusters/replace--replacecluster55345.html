Synonyms

  • substitute
    2.4 6/m
    substitute stands in for someone or something in use, while replace often means putting a new or repaired item in the old place.
  • supersede
    7.2 0.7/m
    supersede takes the place of something older or less useful, while replace is a more general act of putting a new thing in place.
  • interchange
    4.1 0.1/m
    interchange means two things can swap positions with each other, while replace usually means one thing goes into another's place.
  • exchange
    1.8 13/m
    exchange means trading things back and forth or getting something in return, while replace often means simply putting a new item in place.
