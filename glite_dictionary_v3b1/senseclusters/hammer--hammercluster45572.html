hammer

noun
bone of the middle ear (malleus)
Pronunciation: / ˈhæmər / (AmE), / ˈhamə / (BrE)
the small bone of the middle ear that is attached to the eardrum and transmits sound vibrations to the anvil (incus); also called the malleus

Examples

Ear hammer
A loud noise can sometimes dislodge the hammer in the middle ear, affecting hearing.
Damage to the hammer can result in conductive hearing loss.
The surgeon explained that the hammer transfers vibrations from the eardrum to the inner ear.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: anvil - the middle ear bone (incus) that the hammer connects to
  • synonym: malleus - the small bone in the middle ear, also known as the hammer
