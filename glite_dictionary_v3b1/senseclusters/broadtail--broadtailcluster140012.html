broadtail

noun
breed of sheep valued for lambs' soft black fur
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɔdˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːdteɪl / (BrE)
a hardy, coarse-haired breed of sheep from central Asia, whose young lambs are prized for their soft, curly black fur

Examples

Broadtail sheep breed
Conservationists monitor the population of broadtail sheep due to their economic significance.
Furriers often use pelts from broadtail lambs to make high-end garments.
