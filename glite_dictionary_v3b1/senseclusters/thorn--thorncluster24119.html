thorn

noun
letter (þ) in Old English alphabets
9.9 9.3
Pronunciation: /θɔːrn/ /; / /θɑːrn/ (AmE), /θɔːn/ (BrE)
A letter (þ or Þ) used in Old English and some other early Germanic alphabets to represent the 'th' sound, derived from the runic script.

Examples

The letter thorn

The thorn was gradually replaced by the 'th' digraph in Middle English.
The Old English word 'þæt' begins with the character thorn.
