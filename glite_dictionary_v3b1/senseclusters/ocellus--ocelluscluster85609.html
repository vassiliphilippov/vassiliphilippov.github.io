a simple eye with a single lens, found in many invertebrates such as insects and some jellyfish

Examples

Insect ocellus
Some jellyfish possess a single ocellus to help them sense changes in light.
The honeybee has three small ocelli on top of its head in addition to its compound eyes.
Unlike the compound eye, the ocellus is primarily used to detect light intensity rather than form images.
