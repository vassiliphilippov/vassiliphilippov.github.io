reflux condenser

noun
laboratory device for condensing returning vapors
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈriːˌflʌks kənˈdɛnsər / (AmE), / ˈriːflʌks kənˈdɛnsə / (BrE)
a laboratory device that condenses vapors and returns them to the vessel, preventing the contents from boiling dry during heating

Examples

A reflux condenser device
A reflux condenser is essential for many organic synthesis reactions.
The chemist attached a reflux condenser to the flask before heating the solution.
Without a reflux condenser, the solvent would quickly evaporate and leave the reaction mixture dry.
