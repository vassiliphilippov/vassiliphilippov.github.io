Charadriidae

noun
family of birds including plovers
Pronunciation: / kəˌrædriˈɪdiː / (AmE), / kəˌrædriˈɪdiː / (BrE)
The family of small to medium-sized wading birds known as plovers, which are found worldwide and typically inhabit shorelines and wetlands.

Examples

Charadriidae family birds
Members of Charadriidae are known for their short bills and rounded bodies.
The Charadriidae are well adapted to life along sandy beaches and mudflats.
There are more than 60 species in the Charadriidae family worldwide.

Related Terms
