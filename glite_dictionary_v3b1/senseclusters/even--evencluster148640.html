even

adjective
(of a number) divisible by two
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈivən / (AmE), / ˈiːvən / (BrE)
(of a number) divisible by two without remainder.

Examples

An even number
If you add two even numbers, the result is always even.
The number twelve is an even number because it can be divided by two without a remainder.
Zero is considered an even integer in mathematics.

Related Terms

  • antonym: odd - (of a number) not divisible by two without remainder
