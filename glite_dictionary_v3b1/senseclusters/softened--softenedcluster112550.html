softened

adjective
made less hard or severe
2.4 2.8
Pronunciation: /ˈsɔːf.ənd/ /; / /ˈsɑːf.ənd/ (AmE), /ˈsɒf.ənd/ (BrE)
made less hard, severe, intense, or loud; toned down in strength or impact

Examples

A softened tone
His softened tone helped defuse the tension in the room.
The CEO announced a softened stance on remote work policies after employee feedback.
The paint had a softened appearance after years of sunlight.
Her voice softened as she spoke gently to the child.
The harsh lights were softened by frosted glass covers.
The sound of the rain was softened by the thick curtains.

  • synonym: muffled - (of a sound) not loud because of being obstructed in some way
  • synonym: muted - (of sound) quiet or not bright
  • degree lower: soft - not hard or firm; gentle in effect
