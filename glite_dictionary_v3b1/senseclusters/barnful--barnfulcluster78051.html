barnful

noun
the quantity a barn can hold
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑːrnfʊl / (AmE), / ˈbɑːnfʊl / (BrE)
An amount equal to the quantity that a barn can hold.

Examples

A barnful of grain
Despite the drought, they managed to get almost a barnful of grain.
The farmer stored a barnful of hay for the winter months.
They hauled in a barnful of corn after the harvest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: roomful - an amount that fills a room
