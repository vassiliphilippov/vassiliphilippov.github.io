sleep-in

noun
overnight protest event
5.6 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / sliÀêp …™n / (AmE), / sliÀêp …™n / (BrE)
an organized event in which people stay overnight at a location (often as a form of protest) to draw attention to a cause or demand change.

Examples

A sleep-in protest
Activists organized a sleep-in outside City Hall to protest the proposed development.
Students planned a sleep-in on the university lawn to demand better campus safety.
The union staged a noisy sleep-in that lasted until management agreed to talks.
