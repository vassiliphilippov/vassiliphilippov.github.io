insurgency

noun
armed rebellion against authority
8.4 6.36/m
Pronunciation: / …™nÀàs…úrd í…ônsi / (AmE), / …™nÀàs…úÀêd í…ônsi / (BrE)
An organized attempt by a group of people to overthrow an established government, typically by subversion and armed conflict.

Examples

A violent insurgency
Over the last decade, the region has been plagued by a violent insurgency.
Talks between the government and leaders of the insurgency finally began last month.
The military responded quickly to suppress the insurgency in the northern provinces.

Synonyms

  • insurgence
    8.7 0.07/m
    insurgence is an organized rebellion often involving armed conflict and subversion, while insurgency is a longer, ongoing campaign against a government.
  • insurrection
    9.1 2.28/m
    insurrection is a violent, often sudden attempt to overthrow authority, while insurgency describes a planned, ongoing campaign using subversion or armed force.
  • rebellion
    4.1 7.73/m
    rebellion is organized armed resistance that aims to challenge or overthrow rulers, while insurgency suggests a longer, sustained campaign with subversion.
  • revolt
    4.6 6.71/m
    revolt is an organized attempt to remove rulers that often uses force, while insurgency implies a broader, ongoing campaign with subversion.
  • uprising
    5.1 11/m
    uprising is an act of resistance that may be brief and local, while insurgency usually means a sustained, organized attempt to overthrow government.

Related Terms

  • synonym: insurrection - violent uprising against an authority or government
  • synonym: rebellion - open resistance to authority, often with violence
  • synonym: revolution - forcible overthrow of a government or order
