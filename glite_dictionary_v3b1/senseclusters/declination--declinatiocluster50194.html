(astronomy) The angular distance of a celestial object north or south of the celestial equator, measured in degrees and used with right ascension to specify its position in the sky.

Examples

Star declination
Astronomers use right ascension and declination to locate objects in the night sky.
The planet's declination changed as it moved along its orbit.
The star's declination determines how high it appears above the horizon.

Synonyms

  • celestial latitude
    9.1 0.01/m
    celestial latitude is a full technical phrase used by astronomers for north-south angular position, while declination is the usual term.
  • dec
    9.9 0.05/m
    dec is the abbreviation often used in tables and notes, while declination is the full formal name for the same angle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: right ascension - the angular distance of a point measured eastward along the celestial equator from the vernal equinox
