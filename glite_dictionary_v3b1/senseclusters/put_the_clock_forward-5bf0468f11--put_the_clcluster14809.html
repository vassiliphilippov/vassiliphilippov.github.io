put the clock forward

verb
adjust clock for daylight saving
Pronunciation: / pʊt ðə klɑk ˈfɔrwərd / (AmE), / pʊt ðə klɒk ˈfɔːwəd / (BrE)
to adjust a clock to a later time, especially as part of beginning daylight saving time

Examples

To put the clock forward one hour
Don't forget to put the clock forward when daylight saving starts this Sunday.
Every spring we put the clock forward by one hour and lose some sleep.
My phone automatically puts the clock forward on the correct date.

Related Terms

  • antonym: put the clock back - to adjust a clock to an earlier time, especially at the end of daylight saving time
