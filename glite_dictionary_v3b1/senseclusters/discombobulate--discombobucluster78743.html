discombobulate

verb
to confuse or bewilder
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪskəmˈbɑbjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˌdɪskəmˈbɒbjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to confuse or bewilder someone; to make someone unable to think clearly

Examples

To discombobulate a person
Complicated instructions can easily discombobulate new team members.
Jet lag tends to discombobulate me for a couple of days after I travel overseas.
The sudden change of plans seemed to discombobulate everyone in the office.
Her unexpected question completely discombobulated him during the interview.
The sudden change in schedule seemed to discombobulate everyone in the office.
Traveling to a new country can sometimes discombobulate even the most experienced traveler.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clarify - to make something less confused and more clearly comprehensible
  • synonym: confuse - to cause someone to be unable to think clearly or understand something
  • synonym: perplex - to make someone feel completely baffled
