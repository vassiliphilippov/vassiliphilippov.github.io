dabigatran

noun
anticoagulant drug
Pronunciation: / ˌdæbɪˈɡætræn /; / dəˌbɪɡəˈtræn / (AmE), / ˌdæbɪˈɡætræn /; / dəˌbɪɡəˈtræn / (BrE)
a medication used to prevent blood clots and reduce the risk of stroke, especially in people with certain heart conditions

Examples

Dabigatran medication
The doctor prescribed dabigatran to reduce the risk of stroke in patients with atrial fibrillation.
Patients taking dabigatran should have regular check-ups to monitor for side effects.
Unlike some older anticoagulants, dabigatran does not require frequent blood tests.
The doctor prescribed dabigatran to help prevent blood clots after surgery.
Patients with atrial fibrillation may take dabigatran to reduce their risk of stroke.
Before starting dabigatran, your doctor will check your kidney function.
