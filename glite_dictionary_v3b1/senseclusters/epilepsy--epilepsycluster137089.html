epilepsy

noun
neurological disorder with convulsions
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈɛpɪˌlɛp.si / (AmE), / ˈɛpɪˌlɛp.si / (BrE)
A neurological disorder marked by sudden recurrent episodes of sensory disturbance, loss of consciousness, or convulsions, caused by abnormal electrical activity in the brain.

Examples

Epilepsy disorder
Despite her diagnosis of epilepsy, she manages her condition effectively and leads a full life.
His epilepsy was triggered by flashing lights during the concert.
The doctor explained that epilepsy can be controlled with medication for many patients.

Related Terms

  • synonym: seizure - a sudden attack of illness, especially an epileptic fit
