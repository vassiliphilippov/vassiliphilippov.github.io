foreclose

verb
prevent or make impossible
4.3 6.3
Pronunciation: /fɔːr.ˈkloʊz/ /; / /fɑːr.ˈkloʊz/ (AmE), /fɔː.ˈkləʊz/ (BrE)
(formal) To prevent something from happening or arising; to make impossible.

Examples

To foreclose possibilities
His refusal to compromise foreclosed any hope of a peaceful resolution.
Strict safety regulations foreclose the possibility of such accidents occurring again.
The new evidence does not foreclose the case, but it makes a guilty verdict unlikely.

Related Terms

  • antonym: permit - to allow something to happen
  • synonym: preclude - to prevent something from happening or someone from doing something
  • synonym: prevent - to stop something from happening
