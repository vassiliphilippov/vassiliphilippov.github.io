foreclose

verb
prevent or make impossible
7.9 1.07/m
Pronunciation: / f…îrÀàklo äz / (AmE), / f…îÀêÀàkl…ô äz / (BrE)
(formal) To prevent something from happening or arising; to make impossible.

Examples

To foreclose possibilities
His refusal to compromise foreclosed any hope of a peaceful resolution.
Strict safety regulations foreclose the possibility of such accidents occurring again.
The new evidence does not foreclose the case, but it makes a guilty verdict unlikely.

Synonyms

  • preclude
    7.2 7.79/m
    preclude means stop something from happening or make it impossible, while foreclose sounds formal and often blocks options or opportunities.
  • forestall
    7.1 2.19/m
    forestall means act early to stop a problem developing, while foreclose is more about permanently making something impossible.
  • close out
    6.5 0.04/m
    close out means take action to make an idea or option impossible or excluded, while foreclose is a formal way to block possibilities.
  • prevent
    2.3 130/m
    prevent means stop something from happening in general, while foreclose implies formally making that thing impossible or not allowed.
  • forbid
    2.8 2.82/m
    forbid means refuse or order that someone must not do something, while foreclose is about making events or options impossible.

Related Terms

  • antonym: permit - to allow something to happen
  • synonym: preclude - to prevent something from happening or someone from doing something
  • synonym: prevent - to stop something from happening
