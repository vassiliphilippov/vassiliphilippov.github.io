canvass

verb
examine or discuss in detail
Pronunciation: / ˈkæn.vəs / (AmE), / ˈkæn.vəs / (BrE)
to examine or discuss something in detail, especially in order to discover its essential features or meaning

Examples

To canvass an idea
Literary critics often canvass different interpretations of a challenging poem.
The committee will canvass all the available options before making a recommendation.
We should canvass the pros and cons of each proposal thoroughly before proceeding.

Related Terms

  • synonym: analyze - to examine in detail in order to explain and interpret it
  • synonym: scrutinize - to examine closely and critically
