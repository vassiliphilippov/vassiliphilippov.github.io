Colocasia esculenta

noun
tropical plant grown for edible root and foliage
9.9
Pronunciation: / koʊloʊˈkeɪʒə ˌɛskjəˈlɛntə / (AmE), / kɒləˈkeɪsɪə ˌɛskjʊˈlɛntə / (BrE)
a tropical plant native to the Pacific islands, grown for its starchy edible root (taro) and as an ornamental for its large, glossy leaves

Examples

Colocasia esculenta plant
Colocasia esculenta is widely cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions for its nutritious tubers.
In many Pacific cultures, Colocasia esculenta is a dietary staple and is used in traditional dishes.
The large, heart-shaped leaves of Colocasia esculenta make it a favorite ornamental plant in gardens.
