Tuatha De

noun
group of gods in Irish mythology
Pronunciation: / tuːˈɑːhə ˈdei / (AmE), / tuːˈɑːhə ˈdeɪ / (BrE)
In Irish mythology, a group of Celtic gods or demigods said to have ruled Ireland during the Golden Age.

Examples

Tuatha De gods
Many ancient stories recount the powers and battles of the Tuatha De against other supernatural races.
Some believe that the traditions of the Tuatha De influenced later Irish folklore about fairies.
The myths describe the Tuatha De as skilled in magic and art.
