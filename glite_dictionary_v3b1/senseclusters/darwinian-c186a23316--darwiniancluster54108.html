Darwinian

noun
advocate of Darwinism
Pronunciation: / dɑːrˈwɪniən / (AmE), / dɑːˈwɪniən / (BrE)
a person who supports or advocates the theory of evolution by natural selection as proposed by Charles Darwin

Examples

A true Darwinian
As a proud Darwinian, she often defended the theory of natural selection in heated debates.
Despite criticism, the Darwinian maintained unwavering support for evolutionary ideas.
Many Darwinians contributed to modern evolutionary biology in the twentieth century.
