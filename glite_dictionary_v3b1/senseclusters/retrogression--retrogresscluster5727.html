retrogression

noun
a return to a former, usually worse, state
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛtroʊˈɡrɛʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛtrəˈɡrɛʃən / (BrE)
The process of returning to an earlier, usually worse or less developed state.

Examples

Social retrogression
After years of improvement, there was a sudden retrogression in public health standards.
Scientists warned against the retrogression of environmental protection policies.
The country's economic retrogression alarmed investors worldwide.

Related Terms

  • antonym: progression - the process of developing or moving gradually towards a more advanced state
  • synonym: regression - the act of going back to a previous state, especially a less advanced one
