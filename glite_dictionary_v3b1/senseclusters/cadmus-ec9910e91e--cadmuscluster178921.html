Cadmus

proper noun
legendary founder of Thebes in Greek mythology
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkæd.məs/ (AmE), /ˈkæd.məs/ (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the brother of Europa and the legendary founder of Thebes in Boeotia.

Examples

Cadmus in mythology
According to legend, Cadmus was sent by his father to search for his sister Europa.
Many myths credit Cadmus with introducing the Phoenician alphabet to Greece.
The city of Thebes is said to have been founded by Cadmus after he slew a dragon.
