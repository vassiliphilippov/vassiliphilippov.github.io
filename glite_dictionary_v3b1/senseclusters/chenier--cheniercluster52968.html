chenier

noun
coastal sand ridge
Pronunciation: / ʃəˈnɪər / (AmE), / ʃəˈnɪə / (BrE)
a long, narrow ridge of sand or shell found in coastal marshes, especially along the Gulf Coast of the United States

Examples

A chenier ridge
The hurricane reshaped the chenier along the Louisiana coastline.
Many birds nest on the elevated chenier above the surrounding marsh.
Scientists study how a chenier forms and changes over time in coastal environments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barrier island - a long, narrow island lying parallel to the mainland, protecting the coast
  • synonym: marsh - an area of low, wet land, often found near coasts
