Seleucid

proper noun
ancient dynasty or empire
Pronunciation: / səˈluːsɪd / (AmE), / sɪˈljuːsɪd / (BrE)
a member of the Seleucid dynasty or relating to the ancient Seleucid Empire, which ruled parts of Asia after Alexander the Great

Examples

Historians study Seleucid coins to learn about the empire's economy.
A Seleucid king once ruled over large parts of Persia and Syria.
