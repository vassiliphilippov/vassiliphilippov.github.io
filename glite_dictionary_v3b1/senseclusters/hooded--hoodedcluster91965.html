hooded

adjective
with a head covering
3.2
Pronunciation: /ˈhʊd.ɪd/ (AmE), /ˈhʊd.ɪd/ (BrE)
having a hood or head covering, or wearing something that covers the head (such as clothing or to hide the face)

Examples

Hooded jacket
He pulled up his hooded jacket as the rain began to fall.
The monk wore a long, hooded robe during the ceremony.
She bought a hooded sweatshirt for her morning runs.
The witnesses were hooded to protect their identities during the trial.
In the old photograph, a hooded figure stands alone in the snow.
The guards hooded the prisoners before moving them to another location.

Related Terms

  • synonym: masked - having the face covered to hide identity
  • antonym: uncovered - not covered or protected
