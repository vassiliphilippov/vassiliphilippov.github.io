headlong

adverb
with the head first
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛdˌlɔŋ / (AmE), / ˈhɛdlɒŋ /; / ˈhɛd.lɒŋ / (BrE)
with the head first, especially when moving quickly or suddenly forward

Examples

Headlong dive
She ran headlong down the stairs, eager to greet her friend at the door.
The cyclist fell headlong over the handlebars after hitting a rock.
The diver plunged headlong into the pool, barely making a splash.
She rushed headlong into the icy water without hesitation.
The bicyclist went headlong over the handlebars after hitting the curb.
The children tumbled headlong down the grassy hill.
