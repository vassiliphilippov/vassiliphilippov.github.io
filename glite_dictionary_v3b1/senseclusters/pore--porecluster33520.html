pore

noun
tiny opening in a surface
Pronunciation: / pɔr / (AmE), / pɔː / (BrE)
a very small opening in the skin or surface of an animal, through which liquids or gases can pass

Examples

Facial pore
Oil can build up in the pores of your face and cause acne.
Some fabrics have microscopic pores that allow air but not water to pass through.
Sweat escapes from the skin through tiny pores all over the body.
After washing her face, she could feel her pores tighten.
Sweat is released through tiny pores in the skin.
The skin's pores can become clogged with oil and dirt.

Related Terms

  • synonym: duct - a tube or channel in the body for carrying substances
  • synonym: follicle - small cavity in the skin, often associated with hair roots
  • synonym: opening - an act or place of something being opened
