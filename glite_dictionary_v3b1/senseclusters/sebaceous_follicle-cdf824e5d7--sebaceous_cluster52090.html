sebaceous follicle

noun
gland that secretes sebum in skin
8.7
Pronunciation: / səˈbeɪʃəs ˈfɑlɪkəl / (AmE), / sɪˈbeɪʃəs ˈfɒlɪkəl / (BrE)
A small gland in the skin that secretes sebum, usually into a hair follicle, to lubricate hair and skin.

Examples

The sebaceous follicle gland
Excess activity in the sebaceous follicle can contribute to oily skin and acne.
Sebum is secreted by each sebaceous follicle to protect and moisturize the skin.
The dermatologist explained how a blocked sebaceous follicle leads to the formation of pimples.
