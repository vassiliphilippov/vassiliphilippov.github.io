run off

verb
force to leave; drive away
4.7 1.15/m
Pronunciation: / rÊŒn É”f / (AmE), / rÊŒn É’f / (BrE)
to force someone or something to leave a place; to drive away.

Examples

To run off intruders
Security guards ran off several trespassers late at night.
She tried hard to run off her negative thoughts and focus on the task.
The farmer had to run off a pack of stray dogs from his yard.
