germinate

verb

to (cause a seed or spore to) begin to grow or sprout

Examples

To germinate seeds
Consistent warmth and moisture will germinate the seeds quickly.
Sometimes old seeds require extra care to germinate successfully.
The gardener used a special light to germinate the tomato seeds indoors.
After a week, the beans began to germinate in the window box.
Given the right conditions, some species of cacti germinate very quickly.
The seeds need moist soil to germinate successfully.

Synonyms

  • sprout
    2.0 5.80/m
    sprout means to begin growing and produce small shoots, while germinate describes a seed or spore starting to grow.
  • shoot
    2.8 0.95/m
    shoot means to put out buds or new branches, while germinate usually means a seed or spore begins to grow.

Related Terms
