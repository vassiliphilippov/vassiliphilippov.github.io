mere

adjective
nothing more than; only
3.4 42/m
Pronunciation: / mÉªr / (AmE), / mÉªÉ™ / (BrE)
used to emphasize that something is small, unimportant, or nothing more than what is stated

Examples

A mere detail
He trembled at the mere thought of speaking in public.
Her success was due to the mere fact that she worked harder than anyone else.
The mere mention of his name makes her smile.
A mere handful of people turned up for the meeting.
He was a mere child when he started playing the piano.
It was a mere formality, but it still had to be completed.
