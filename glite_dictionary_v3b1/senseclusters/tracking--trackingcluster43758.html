tracking

noun
following movement
Pronunciation: / ˈtrækɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtrækɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of following or monitoring the movement, progress, or location of someone or something

Examples

Package tracking
The delivery company provides tracking so customers can see where their packages are at any time.
Scientists use satellite tracking to study the migration routes of endangered birds.
With modern technology, tracking lost phones has become much easier than before.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ignoring - the act of paying no attention to something
  • synonym: monitoring - the act of observing or checking something regularly
  • synonym: pursuit - the act of chasing or following someone or something
