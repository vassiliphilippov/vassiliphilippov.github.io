squelch

verb
make a soft, sucking sound
Pronunciation: / skwɛltʃ / (AmE), / skwɛltʃ / (BrE)
to make a soft, sucking sound or to walk noisily through wet, muddy ground

Examples

Boots squelch in mud
After the rain, every step on the trail squelched loudly.
Her boots squelched as she walked across the muddy field.
The wet sponge squelched between his fingers as he cleaned the dish.
Her boots squelched as she made her way through the sodden field after the rain.
The children laughed as they squelched through the wet grass after the storm.
We had to squelch across the muddy path to reach the cabin.

Related Terms

  • synonym: splosh - to move with splashing or squelching sounds, especially in wet conditions
  • synonym: squelch - to suppress or silence (another sense of 'squelch')
