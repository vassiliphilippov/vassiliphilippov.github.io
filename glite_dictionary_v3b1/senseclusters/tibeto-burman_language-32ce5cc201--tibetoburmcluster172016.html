Tibeto-Burman language

noun
branch of the Sino-Tibetan language family
Pronunciation: / təˌbɛdoʊ ˈbɜrmən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / tɪˌbɛtəʊ ˈbɜːmən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a branch of the Sino-Tibetan language family, spoken in regions from Tibet to the Malay Peninsula, including Burmese, Tibetan, and many others

Examples

Tibeto-Burman languages family
Linguists have documented hundreds of Tibeto-Burman languages across Southeast Asia.
Many minority communities in northern India speak a Tibeto-Burman language as their mother tongue.
Scholars debate the classification of several dialects within the Tibeto-Burman language group.
