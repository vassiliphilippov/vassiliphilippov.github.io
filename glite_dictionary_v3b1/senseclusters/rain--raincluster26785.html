rain

noun
water drops falling from clouds
Pronunciation: / reɪn / (AmE), / reɪn / (BrE)
water that falls from clouds in small drops as precipitation (especially as weather)

Examples

Heavy rain
After a long drought, the farmers were grateful for the rain that finally arrived.
Children love to splash in puddles left by the rain after a summer storm.
The steady rain forced us to postpone the picnic until the following day.
After weeks of drought, the rain finally arrived and refreshed the fields.
The rain fell steadily all night, filling the rivers and streams.
We had to cancel the picnic because of the heavy rain.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: downpour - a heavy rain
  • degree lower: drizzle - light rain with very small drops
  • antonym: dryness - the state of being dry; lack of rain
  • synonym: precipitation - water that falls from the atmosphere to the Earth's surface
  • antonym: snow - frozen precipitation in the form of white flakes
