Very cold in temperature; extremely cold.

Examples

Frigid winter night
Despite his warm coat, the mountain air remained frigid all night.
She shivered as she dipped her toes into the frigid water of the lake.
The frigid wind made walking outside nearly impossible.

Synonyms

  • freezing
    1.0 0.00/m
    freezing describes weather at or below the point water turns to ice, while frigid sounds more formal and very cold.
  • freezing cold
    1.3 0.48/m
    freezing cold feels like the air at the freezing point and is informal for strong chill, while frigid is more formal.
  • ice-cold
    1.0 1.28/m
    ice-cold often describes drinks or objects with a sharp chill, while frigid usually describes weather and sounds more formal.
  • icy
    1.0 7.35/m
    icy describes things covered in ice or a very cold feeling and can be metaphorical, while frigid means intensely cold.
  • gelid
    9.9 0.06/m
    gelid is a rare, literary word meaning icy cold and sounds formal, while frigid is common everyday English for very cold.
  • perishingly cold
    7.2 0.00/m
    perishingly cold sounds dramatic and stresses danger from severe chill, while frigid is a straighter, more neutral word for extreme cold.
  • bone-chilling
    7.0 0.22/m
    bone-chilling suggests a deep, painful cold or scary feeling, while frigid is more neutral and focuses on low temperature.
  • glacial
    6.1 0.17/m
    glacial evokes huge ice and a very cold, slow feeling, while frigid means simply very cold without the slow image.
  • arctic
    4.5 1.06/m
    arctic refers to the polar region or weather like that and feels geographic, while frigid simply means very cold.
  • polar
    4.2 8.05/m
    polar describes the poles or climates near them and is geographic or scientific, while frigid simply describes being very cold.
