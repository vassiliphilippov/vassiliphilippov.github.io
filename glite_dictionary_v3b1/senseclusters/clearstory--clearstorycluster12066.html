clearstory

noun
upper wall section with windows for light
Pronunciation: / ˈklɪrˌstɔri / (AmE), / ˈklɪəˌstɔːri / (BrE)
An upper section of a wall in a building, above adjoining roofs or aisles, with windows for admitting light to the interior.

Examples

A clearstory window
Extra windows were installed in the clearstory to improve natural lighting.
The architect designed a high clearstory to create an airy, open feeling in the hall.
The church's clearstory allowed sunlight to stream into the nave.
