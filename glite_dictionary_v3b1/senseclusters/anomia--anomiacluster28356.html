anomia

noun
inability to name objects (medical)
Pronunciation: / əˈnoʊ.mi.ə / (AmE), / əˈnəʊ.mɪ.ə / (BrE)
a medical condition characterized by an inability to name objects or recall the names of objects

Examples

Anomia after stroke
Researchers are studying treatments to help individuals cope with anomia after a stroke.
The patient displayed clear signs of anomia when she struggled to recall common objects during the exam.
While his speech was fluent, anomia made it difficult for him to name even familiar faces.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aphasia - loss or impairment of the ability to use or understand words, often after brain injury
