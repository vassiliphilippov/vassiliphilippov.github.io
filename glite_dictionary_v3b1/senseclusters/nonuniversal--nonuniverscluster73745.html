nonuniversal

adjective
not true in all cases
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˌjunɪˈvɜrsəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌjuːnɪˈvɜːsəl / (BrE)
not applicable or true in all cases or situations

Examples

A nonuniversal rule
This policy is nonuniversal and only applies to certain departments.
The scientist explained that the theory is nonuniversal and has exceptions.
Because the rule is nonuniversal, it cannot be used in every situation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: partial - existing or true in part but not in whole
  • antonym: universal - true or applicable in all cases or situations
