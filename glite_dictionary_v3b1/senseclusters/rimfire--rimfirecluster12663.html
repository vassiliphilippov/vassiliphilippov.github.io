rimfire

noun
type of firearm
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪmˌfaɪɚ / (AmE), / ˈrɪmˌfaɪə / (BrE)
a firearm that uses cartridges ignited by striking the rim of the base rather than the center.

Examples

A rimfire rifle
The antique rimfire was carefully cleaned and placed in the display case.
Many beginners learn to shoot with a .22 caliber rimfire because of its low recoil.
Collectors often seek out rare rimfire models from the nineteenth century.

Related Terms

  • antonym: centerfire - a firearm that uses cartridges ignited by striking the center of the base
