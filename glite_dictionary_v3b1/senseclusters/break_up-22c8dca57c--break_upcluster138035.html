break up

verb
to interrupt or pause
Pronunciation: / breɪk ʌp / (AmE), / breɪk ʌp / (BrE)
to interrupt or bring a pause to something, such as an event or continuous activity

Examples

Storms break up the signal
Storms may break up the satellite broadcast unexpectedly.
The teacher had to break up the heated discussion when it became too loud.
We paused the meeting to break up the session for a short break.

Related Terms

  • antonym: continue - to persist in an activity without interruption
  • synonym: interrupt - to stop the continuous progress of something briefly
