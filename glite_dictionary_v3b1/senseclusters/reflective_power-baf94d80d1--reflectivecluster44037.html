reflective power

noun
the ability to reflect light; ratio of reflected to incident light
7.2 4.8
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈflek.tɪv ˈpaʊ.ər/ /; / /rə.ˈflek.tɪv ˈpaʊ.ər/ /; / /rɪ.ˈflek.tɪv paʊr/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈflek.tɪv ˈpaʊ.ə/ /; / /rə.ˈflek.tɪv ˈpaʊ.ə/ (BrE)
the ability of a surface or material to reflect light, measured as the ratio of reflected to incident light

Examples

Different surfaces have different reflective power depending on their texture and color.

The reflective power of snow makes it highly effective at bouncing sunlight away.
