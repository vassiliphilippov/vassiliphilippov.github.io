link

noun
connection or channel between groups
1.0 1.3
Pronunciation: /lɪŋk/ (AmE), /lɪŋk/ (BrE)
a connection, channel, or relationship between people, things, or places

Examples

A link between people
Intelligence agencies established a secret link to share sensitive information.
She acted as the vital link between management and the staff.
The embassy serves as a link to the local community.
Scientists have established a link between pollution levels and climate change.
The link between smoking and lung cancer is well documented in medical research.
There is a strong link between exercise and improved mental health.
A secure link is essential for transmitting sensitive data between offices.
Engineers repaired the damaged fiber optic link to restore internet service.
The satellite link allows instant communication between continents.
