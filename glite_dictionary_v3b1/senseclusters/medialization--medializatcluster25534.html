medialization

noun
moving toward midline
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmiːdiəlɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmiːdiəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of moving a body part or structure closer to the midline of the body

Examples

Medialization of vocal cord
The doctor recommended medialization of the vocal cord to help the patient speak more clearly.
After the injury, medialization of the shoulder muscle was necessary for proper movement.
Surgical medialization can improve function in patients with certain nerve disorders.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lateralization - the process of moving a body part away from the midline
