poorness

noun
meagerness or insufficiency
Pronunciation: / ˈpʊrnəs /; / ˈpɔrnəs / (AmE), / ˈpɔːnəs /; / ˈpɔːnɪs / (BrE)
the state or quality of being insufficient, meager, or of low quality; inadequacy or inferiority

Examples

Poorness of quality
He complained about the poorness of the fabric used in the cheap jacket.
The poorness of the harvest left the villagers struggling to survive the winter.
The essay was criticized for the poorness of its arguments and lack of supporting evidence.
Critics complained about the poorness of the sound quality in the recording.
The poorness of construction was evident after only a year of use.
The poorness of the repair work became clear when the paint started chipping off.
Critics commented on the poorness of the movie's script and direction.
Her argument was weakened by the poorness of supporting evidence.
The poorness of the soil made farming difficult for many settlers.
