essentialism

noun
core curriculum philosophy
Pronunciation: / ɪˈsɛnʃəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪˈsɛnʃəlɪzəm / (BrE)
an educational philosophy that emphasizes teaching a core set of essential knowledge and skills, often through teacher-centered instruction

Examples

Essentialism in education
Many traditional schools follow essentialism by focusing on reading, writing, and mathematics.
The debate between essentialism and progressivism shapes how teachers design their lessons.
Supporters of essentialism believe that students need a strong foundation in basic subjects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: perennialism - an educational philosophy emphasizing timeless knowledge and universal truths
  • antonym: progressivism - an educational philosophy focused on student-centered learning and experience
