wildness

noun
quality of being wild or untamed
2.6 3.2
Pronunciation: /ˈwaɪld.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈwaɪld.nəs/ (BrE)
the quality or state of being wild, untamed, or lacking restraint (in nature or behavior)

Examples

Wildness of nature
She admired the wildness of the mountains, untouched by human hands.
The wildness of the jungle made travel nearly impossible for the explorers.
There was a certain wildness in his spirit that refused to be tamed.
Her wildness and unpredictability made her the most exciting member of the team.
The wildness of the sea during the hurricane was both terrifying and beautiful.
The landscape's wildness attracted adventurers from around the globe.
Despite her parents' strict rules, an irrepressible wildness often showed in her actions.
He struggled to balance his natural wildness with society's expectations of good behavior.
The wildness in his eyes made everyone nervous during the meeting.
