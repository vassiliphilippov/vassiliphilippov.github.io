conjunct

noun
connecting word or phrase
6.8 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëÀênd í å≈ãkt / (AmE), / Ààk…índ í å≈ãkt / (BrE)
a word or phrase, such as 'and', 'but', or 'however', that connects clauses or sentences in writing or speech

Examples

A conjunct like 'and'
The word 'however' is a common conjunct in English writing.
Writers often use a conjunct to smoothly connect two related ideas.
In the sentence, 'She was tired, but she finished her work,' 'but' acts as a conjunct.

Synonyms

  • conjunction
    1.0 0.68/m
    conjunction is the common term for words like and or but used inside sentences, while conjunct refers especially to connectors between clauses or sentences.
  • connective
    3.6 1.97/m
    connective is a broader, more technical word for items that link parts of text, while conjunct names specific clause-or-sentence connectors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conjunction - a word used to connect clauses or sentences
  • antonym: disjunct - a word or phrase that expresses an attitude or comment on the content of a sentence
