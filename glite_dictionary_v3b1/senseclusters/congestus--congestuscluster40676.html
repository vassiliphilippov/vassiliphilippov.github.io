congestus

noun
towering cumulus cloud
Pronunciation: / kənˈdʒɛstəs / (AmE), / kənˈdʒɛstəs / (BrE)
a type of cumulus cloud with strong vertical growth, often signaling the possibility of rain or storms

Examples

A congestus cloud
A large congestus cloud formed quickly, warning us of the approaching storm.
Pilots watch for congestus clouds because they can indicate turbulent weather ahead.
The afternoon sun illuminated the rising congestus above the mountains.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cumulus - a type of fluffy, white cloud with a flat base
  • degree higher: nimbus - a type of rain cloud
