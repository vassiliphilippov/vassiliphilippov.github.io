disincentive

noun
something that discourages action
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsɪnˈsɛntɪv / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnˈsɛntɪv / (BrE)
A factor or circumstance that discourages a person from taking a particular action.

Examples

A disincentive factor
Lack of career advancement serves as a disincentive for highly skilled workers.
Strict regulations can be a significant disincentive to innovation.
The new tax policy acts as a disincentive for investment in small businesses.

Related Terms

  • antonym: incentive - something that encourages a person to do something
