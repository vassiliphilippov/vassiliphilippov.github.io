whereof

adverb
of what; about which
Pronunciation: / wɛrˈʌv / (AmE), / wɛəˈrɒv / (BrE)
of what or about which; used in formal or old-fashioned language to refer to something just mentioned.

Examples

The evidence whereof
The evidence whereof you speak has not been presented to the court.
He wrote a book, the subject whereof was the history of the city.
The agreement, the details whereof are confidential, was signed yesterday.

Related Terms

  • synonym: wherein - in which; inside of which
