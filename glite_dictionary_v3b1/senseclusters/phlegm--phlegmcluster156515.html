phlegm

noun
thick mucus from respiratory passages
8.3 0.84/m
Pronunciation: / flÉ›m / (AmE), / flÉ›m / (BrE)
The thick, sticky substance secreted by the mucous membranes of the respiratory passages, especially when produced in excess during illness. In ancient and medieval physiology, it was one of the four bodily humors believed to cause sluggishness or apathy.

Examples

Thick phlegm in throat
In ancient medicine, an excess of phlegm was thought to make a person apathetic.
It's important to spit out phlegm rather than swallow it when you have a chest infection.
The cold left him with a persistent cough and thick phlegm in his throat.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mucus - a slippery secretion produced by mucous membranes
  • synonym: sputum - matter expectorated from the respiratory tract
