mucous membrane

noun
membrane lining body cavities, secretes mucus
6.7 3.8
Pronunciation: /ˈmjuː.kəs ˈmem.breɪn/ (AmE), /ˈmjuː.kəs ˈmem.breɪn/ (BrE)
A moist tissue layer lining body cavities and passages that open to the outside, secreting mucus to protect and lubricate surfaces.

Examples

The mucous membrane lining
Doctors inspected the patient's mucous membranes for signs of dehydration.
The lining of your nose is a mucous membrane that helps trap dust and microbes.
When irritated, a mucous membrane may become red and produce excess mucus.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mucosa - The mucous membrane itself, especially in anatomical terminology
  • antonym: serous membrane - A membrane lining closed internal body cavities, not open to the exterior
