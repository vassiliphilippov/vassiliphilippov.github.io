Réaumur scale

noun
historical temperature scale, 0–80 degrees
9.9
Pronunciation: /reɪ.ə.ˈmjʊr skeɪl/ /; / /reɪ.ə.ˈmjuːr skeɪl/ (AmE), /reɪ.ə.ˈmjʊə skeɪl/ /; / /reɪ.ə.ˈmjuːə skeɪl/ (BrE)
a temperature scale in which the freezing point of water is 0 degrees and the boiling point is 80 degrees

Examples

The Reaumur scale temperature
On the Réaumur scale, water boils at 80 degrees and freezes at 0.
Scientists in the 18th century often recorded temperatures using the Réaumur scale.
Some old European thermometers are still marked with the Réaumur scale.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Celsius scale - temperature scale with 0°C as water's freezing point and 100°C as boiling point
  • synonym: Fahrenheit scale - temperature scale with 32°F as water's freezing point and 212°F as boiling point
