hot-work steel

noun
alloy steel for high-heat tool use
7.7 5.3
Pronunciation: /hɑːt wɜːrk stiːl/ (AmE), /hɒt wɜːk stiːl/ (BrE)
an alloy steel that retains its hardness even at high temperatures, used especially for making metal-cutting tools

Examples

Hot-work steel tools
Engineers selected hot-work steel for components that must maintain strength at high temperatures.
High-speed drills are often made from hot-work steel to withstand the intense heat generated during operation.
The durability of hot-work steel makes it essential in the construction of forging dies.

  • synonym: tool steel - a type of steel used to make cutting tools
