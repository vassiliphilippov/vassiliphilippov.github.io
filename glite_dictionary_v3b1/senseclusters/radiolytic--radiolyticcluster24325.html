radiolytic

adjective
caused by radiation
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪdiəˈlɪtɪk / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiəˈlɪtɪk / (BrE)
relating to or caused by the chemical changes that occur when a substance is exposed to radiation

Examples

The radiolytic breakdown
The radiolytic breakdown of water is important in nuclear reactors.
Scientists study radiolytic processes to understand how radiation affects living cells.
The radiolytic products formed during treatment can influence the safety of food irradiation.
