post-holiday slump

noun
drop in consumer spending after holidays
4.2
Pronunciation: /poʊst ˈhɑː.lə.deɪ slʌmp/ /; / /poʊst ˈhɑː.lɪ.deɪ slʌmp/ (AmE), /pəʊst ˈhɒ.lɪ.deɪ slʌmp/ /; / /pəʊst ˈhɒ.lə.deɪ slʌmp/ (BrE)
a temporary decrease in economic activity, especially consumer spending and retail sales, that follows major holiday seasons.

Examples

Post-holiday slump in sales
Economists predicted a sharp post-holiday slump in retail sales across the country in January.
After the gift season, small businesses struggled with a prolonged post-holiday slump in online orders.
To prepare for the annual post-holiday slump, the chain slashed inventory and delayed new product launches.

Related Terms

  • synonym: slump - a sudden or prolonged fall in economic activity, sales, or prices
