rheme

noun
new information in sentence
9.9
Pronunciation: / riːm / (AmE), / riːm / (BrE)
the part of a sentence that gives new information about the topic or subject

Examples

Sentence rheme
In the sentence 'The cat is sleeping on the sofa', 'is sleeping on the sofa' is the rheme.
Linguists study how the rheme changes depending on the structure of the sentence.
Understanding the rheme helps clarify what new information is being communicated.

Related Terms

  • synonym: comment - the part of a sentence that says something about the subject
  • antonym: theme - the part of a sentence that indicates what the sentence is about
