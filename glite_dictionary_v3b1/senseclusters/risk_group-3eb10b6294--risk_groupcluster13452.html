risk group

noun
group with shared likelihood of harm/disease
Pronunciation: / rɪsk ɡruːp / (AmE), / rɪsk ɡruːp / (BrE)
a group of people sharing a higher likelihood of being affected by a particular event, disease, or condition.

Examples

A risk group of children
Children under five are considered a risk group for certain infectious diseases.
Identifying each risk group is crucial for effective prevention strategies.
The vaccination campaign prioritized healthcare workers, who are in a risk group.
