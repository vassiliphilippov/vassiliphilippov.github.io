prescreening

verb
to evaluate in advance
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈskriːnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈskriːnɪŋ / (BrE)
to examine or evaluate someone or something in advance to determine if they meet requirements or should proceed

Examples

To prescreen candidates
The company is prescreening all applicants before inviting them for interviews.
Doctors are prescreening patients to identify those at higher risk for the disease.
We are prescreening submissions to ensure they meet our basic requirements.

Related Terms

  • synonym: review - to look over or assess something
  • synonym: screen - to examine or test to decide suitability
