low-water mark

noun
lowest level reached by water or something else
Pronunciation: / ˌloʊ ˈwɔtər ˌmɑrk / (AmE), / ˌləʊ ˈwɔːtə ˌmɑːk / (BrE)
a line or mark that shows the lowest level reached by water or, figuratively, the lowest point or level attained by something

Examples

Visible low-water mark
After the drought, the rocks showed a distinct low-water mark on their surface.
Each spring, the river rises well above its usual low-water mark.
The scandal represented the low-water mark of his political career.

Related Terms

  • antonym: high-water mark - the highest point that water rises to
