train

verb
to teach and supervise someone
3.8 1.0
Pronunciation: /treɪn/ (AmE), /treɪn/ (BrE)
to teach, instruct, or help someone (or an animal) develop skills or abilities through practice or guidance

Examples

To train a team
He trained the soccer team every afternoon before the big tournament.
She was chosen to train the new employees in customer service skills.
They are training young artists in the basics of sculpture and design.
He moved to London to train for his new job as a chef.
Many athletes train every day to improve their skills.
She decided to train as a nurse after graduating from college.
He trained as a chef in Paris before opening his own restaurant.
The company trains new employees every month to ensure everyone is up to date.
We are training our dog to obey simple commands like 'sit' and 'stay'.

  • synonym: educate - to give someone knowledge or training in a particular subject or activity
  • synonym: instruct - give instructions or direction
  • synonym: practice - to perform an activity repeatedly for skill
  • synonym: schooled - educated or trained in a particular field or skill
  • synonym: study - to apply oneself to learning a subject
  • synonym: teach - impart knowledge or instruct someone
