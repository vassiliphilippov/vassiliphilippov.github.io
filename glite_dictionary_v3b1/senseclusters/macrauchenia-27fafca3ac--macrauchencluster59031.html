Macrauchenia

noun
extinct South American mammal
Pronunciation: / ˌmækrɔːˈkiːniə / (AmE), / ˌmækrɔːˈkiːniə / (BrE)
an extinct genus of large, hoofed mammals that lived in South America during the Cenozoic era

Examples

The Macrauchenia fossil
The Macrauchenia had a long neck and lived in prehistoric South America.
Scientists discovered a nearly complete Macrauchenia skeleton in the Patagonian region.
Unlike modern horses, the Macrauchenia had nostrils positioned high on its head.
