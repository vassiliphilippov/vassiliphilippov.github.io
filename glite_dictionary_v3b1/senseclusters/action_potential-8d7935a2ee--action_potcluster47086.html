action potential

noun
electrical signal in nerve or muscle cells
5.9 4.2
Pronunciation: /ˈæk.ʃən pə.ˈten.ʃəl/ (AmE), /ˈæk.ʃən pə.ˈten.ʃəl/ (BrE)
a rapid, temporary change in electrical potential across a cell membrane, especially in nerve or muscle cells, as a signal is transmitted

Examples

A action potential spike
Scientists study the action potential to better understand how the nervous system processes information.
The generation of an action potential depends on the movement of ions across the cell membrane.
When a neuron fires, an action potential travels along its axon to communicate with other cells.
