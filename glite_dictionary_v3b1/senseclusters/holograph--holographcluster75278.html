holograph

noun
handwritten document or book
2.2
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑːləˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈhɒləˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
A handwritten book or document, especially one written entirely by the person whose signature it bears.

Examples

Holograph will
Legal disputes often arise over the authenticity of a holograph document.
She donated her father's holograph manuscript to the university library.
The will was a holograph that the deceased had written and signed himself.
