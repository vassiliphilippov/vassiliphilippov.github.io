countertraction

noun
opposing medical force
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌkaʊntərˈtrækʃən / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntəˈtrækʃən / (BrE)
a force or device applied in the opposite direction to traction to stabilize or align a body part during medical treatment

Examples

Apply countertraction force
The nurse applied countertraction to keep the patient's leg steady during the procedure.
Proper countertraction is essential to prevent further injury when setting a broken bone.
Doctors rely on countertraction to maintain correct alignment in patients with fractures.
The doctor applied countertraction to keep the patient's leg stable during the procedure.
Proper countertraction is essential to prevent further injury when using a traction device.
Nurses are trained to set up countertraction for patients with certain bone fractures.
