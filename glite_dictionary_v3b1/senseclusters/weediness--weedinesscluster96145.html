weediness

noun
tendency to spread
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈwiːdinəs / (AmE), / ˈwiːdinəs / (BrE)
the quality of a plant species to spread rapidly and compete aggressively, often behaving like a weed

Examples

Weediness of a plant
The weediness of the new grass variety made it difficult to control in the garden.
Researchers are studying the weediness of certain crops to prevent them from overtaking native plants.
Farmers worry about the weediness of some species that can quickly dominate their fields.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: hardiness - the ability of a plant to survive difficult conditions
  • synonym: invasiveness - the ability of a species to spread and dominate an area
