Banat

noun
province ruled by a ban
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑːnɑt / (AmE), / ˈbænæt / (BrE)
a historical province or territory governed by a ban, especially in southeastern Europe during the Middle Ages

Examples

Medieval Banat province
The medieval Banat played a crucial role in defending the kingdom's borders.
Historians study the administration of the Banat to understand regional politics in southeastern Europe.
The territory known as the Banat changed hands several times throughout history.
