factuality

noun
state of being factual or actual
Pronunciation: / ˌfæk.tʃuˈæ.lə.ti / (AmE), / ˌfæk.tʃuˈæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality of being based on facts or reality, as opposed to imagination or speculation.

Examples

The factuality of news
Some news sources have been criticized for lacking factuality in their reporting.
The historian emphasized the importance of factuality in all her research.
We must question the factuality of these statements before accepting them as truth.

  • synonym: actuality - the state of existing in reality
