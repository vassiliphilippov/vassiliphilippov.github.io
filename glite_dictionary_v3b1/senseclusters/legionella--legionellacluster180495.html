legionella

noun
bacterium causing Legionnaires' disease
Pronunciation: / ˌlidʒəˈnɛlə / (AmE), / ˌliːdʒəˈnɛlə / (BrE)
a motile, aerobic, rod-shaped Gram-negative bacterium that thrives in water systems such as central heating and air conditioning and can cause Legionnaires' disease

Examples

Legionella bacteria in water
Engineers tested the cooling towers for traces of legionella to prevent an outbreak.
Proper maintenance can reduce the risk of legionella contaminating air conditioning units.
The hospital was shut down after legionella bacteria were discovered in its water system.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Legionnaires' disease - a severe form of pneumonia caused by Legionella bacteria
