absolutist

noun
believer in absolutes
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈæbsəˌluːtɪst / (AmE), / ˈæbsəluːtɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes in or supports absolute principles, especially in politics or ethics, without accepting compromise

Examples

A strict absolutist
As an absolutist, she insisted that there could be no exceptions to the rule.
The debate featured an absolutist who rejected any form of compromise.
Some critics argue that being an absolutist can make cooperation difficult.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dogmatist - a person who lays down principles as undeniably true
  • antonym: relativist - a person who believes that truth and morality are not absolute but depend on context
