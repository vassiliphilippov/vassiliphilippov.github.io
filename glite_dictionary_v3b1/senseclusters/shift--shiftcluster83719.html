shift

noun
loose, straight dress without a waist
Pronunciation: / ʃɪft / (AmE), / ʃɪft / (BrE)
a simple, loose-fitting dress or undergarment worn by women

Examples

A shift dress
A classic shift is comfortable to wear on warm days.
She wore a light blue shift to the garden party, looking effortlessly elegant.
The fashion designer introduced a new line of shift dresses for summer.
In medieval times, a shift was a common undergarment for women of all classes.
She wore a thin shift under her dress to keep warm.
The actress donned a white shift for her role in the historical play.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sheath dress - a close-fitting dress, often contrasted with a shift dress
