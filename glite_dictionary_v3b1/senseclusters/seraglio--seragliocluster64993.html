seraglio

noun
living quarters for women in a Muslim household (historical)
Pronunciation: / səˈrɑːljoʊ /; / səˈrɑːlioʊ / (AmE), / sɪˈrɑːlɪəʊ /; / səˈrɑːliəʊ / (BrE)
a palace or the part of a Muslim household (especially in the past) where the wives and female relatives lived; a harem, especially in the Ottoman Empire

Examples

The seraglio palace
Intrigue and luxury were common stories about life inside the seraglio.
The sultan's wives lived in the seraglio for much of their lives.
Tourists visited the restored seraglio in the old palace complex.
The sultan's seraglio was famous for its beautiful gardens and intricate architecture.
Visitors were not allowed inside the seraglio, which was reserved for the royal family.
Legends about the treasures hidden in the seraglio have fascinated historians for centuries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: harem - the separate part of a Muslim household reserved for wives and female servants
  • synonym: palace - a large, impressive residence of a ruler or dignitary
