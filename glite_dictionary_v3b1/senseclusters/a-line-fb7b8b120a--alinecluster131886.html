A-line

adjective
flared dress shape
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪ laɪn / (AmE), / ˈeɪ laɪn / (BrE)
describing a skirt or dress that is fitted at the waist and gradually widens towards the hem, resembling the shape of the letter A

Examples

A-line skirt style
She chose an A-line skirt for the interview because it looked both professional and stylish.
The designer's new collection features several A-line dresses in bright summer colors.
Many people find the A-line silhouette flattering for different body types.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fitted - closely following the shape of the body
  • synonym: flared - widening gradually towards the bottom
