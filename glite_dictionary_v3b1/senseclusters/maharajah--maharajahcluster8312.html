maharajah

noun
Indian king or high-ranking prince
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːhəˈrɑːdʒə / (AmE), / ˌmɑːhəˈrɑːdʒə / (BrE)
A king or prince in India, ranking above a raja, especially in the former princely states.

Examples

The maharajah of India
A jeweled turban was part of the traditional attire of a maharajah.
After India's independence, many maharajahs lost their official powers but retained their titles.
The maharajah welcomed European visitors with an extravagant feast at his palace.
