telomeric

adjective
of chromosome ends
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛləˈmɛrɪk / (AmE), / ˌtiːləˈmɪərɪk / (BrE)
relating to or located at the telomeres, the ends of a chromosome that protect genetic material

Examples

Telomeric DNA
Scientists observed changes in telomeric DNA during the process of cell division.
The telomeric regions play a crucial role in protecting chromosomes from deterioration.
Mutations in telomeric sequences can lead to genetic disorders and premature aging.

Related Terms

  • antonym: centromeric - relating to the centromere, the central part of a chromosome
  • synonym: chromosomal - relating to chromosomes
