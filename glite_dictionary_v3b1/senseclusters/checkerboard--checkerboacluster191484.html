checkerboard

verb
to make a square pattern
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɛkərˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛkəbɔːd / (BrE)
to mark or arrange something in a pattern of alternating squares, like a checkerboard.

Examples

To checkerboard a floor
The designer checkerboarded the kitchen floor with red and white tiles for a retro look.
After the snowstorm, the fields were checkerboarded with patches of white and green.
They checkerboard the picnic blankets to create a festive atmosphere at the park.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pattern - to decorate or arrange in a repeated design
  • antonym: randomize - to arrange without a specific pattern
