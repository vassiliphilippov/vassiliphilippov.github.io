perversion

noun
distorting something to a wrong use
6.0 7.9
Pronunciation: / pərˈvɜrʒən / (AmE), / pəˈvɜːʃn /; / pəˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
the act or result of distorting, corrupting, or misusing something from its original or proper state

Examples

Perversion of justice
He regarded censorship as a perversion of freedom of expression.
Many critics saw the film's depiction as a perversion of the original story's message.
The judge called the ruling a perversion of the legal process.
The film examines the perversion of justice in a corrupt legal system.
Some people view certain traditions as a perversion of the original culture.
He was accused of perversion for his unusual and unacceptable behavior.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corruption - the process by which something is changed from its original form to one that is morally wrong
  • synonym: deviance - behavior that differs from accepted standards
  • synonym: distortion - the action of twisting or altering something out of its true, natural, or original state
  • antonym: normality - the state of being normal or typical
  • antonym: rectification - the act of putting something right; correction
