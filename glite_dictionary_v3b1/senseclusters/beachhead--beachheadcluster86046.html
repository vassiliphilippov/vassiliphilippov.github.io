beachhead

noun
secured landing area on enemy shore
Pronunciation: / ˈbitʃˌhɛd / (AmE), / ˈbiːtʃˌhɛd / (BrE)
an area on an enemy shore that an invading force secures and holds as a base for further advance inland, typically established by an amphibious landing

Examples

Military beachhead
Securing the beachhead was critical to the success of the operation.
The marines established a beachhead before advancing inland.
The troops faced fierce resistance as they tried to hold the beachhead against counterattacks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bridgehead - a strong position secured in enemy territory from which further advance can be made
