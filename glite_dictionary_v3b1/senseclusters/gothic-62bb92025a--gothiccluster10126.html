Gothic

noun
medieval European architectural style
2.5 8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɑːθɪk / (AmE), / ˈɡɒθɪk / (BrE)
a style of architecture that originated in northern France in the 12th century and spread throughout Europe, characterized by pointed arches, ribbed vaults, and flying buttresses

Examples

Gothic cathedral
Many European cities feature magnificent Gothic cathedrals built during the Middle Ages.
Notre-Dame Cathedral is a masterpiece of Gothic architecture, renowned for its soaring spires and stained glass windows.
The pointed arches and ribbed vaults are hallmarks of the Gothic style.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Baroque - a later elaborate European architectural style following Gothic
  • degree lower: Romanesque - an earlier style of European architecture preceding Gothic, with rounded arches and heavy construction
