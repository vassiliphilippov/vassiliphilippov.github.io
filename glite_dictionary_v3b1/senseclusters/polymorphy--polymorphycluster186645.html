polymorphy

noun
multiple forms in species
9.5
Pronunciation: /ˈpɑː.lɪ.mɑːr.fiː/ /; / /ˈpɑː.lɪ.mɔːr.fiː/ (AmE), /ˈpɒ.lɪ.mɔː.fiː/ (BrE)
the occurrence of two or more distinct forms or types within a single species, often due to genetic or environmental factors

Examples

Animal polymorphy

Scientists study polymorphy to understand how species adapt to changing environments.
In some plants, polymorphy results in flowers of various shapes and colors within the same species.
