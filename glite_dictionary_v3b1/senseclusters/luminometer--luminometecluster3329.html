luminometer

noun
light-measuring instrument
Pronunciation: / ˌluːməˈnɑːmɪtər / (AmE), / ˌluːmɪˈnɒmɪtə / (BrE)
a scientific instrument used in laboratories to measure the amount or intensity of light, especially from chemical or biological reactions

Examples

A luminometer device
The scientist used a luminometer to measure the amount of light produced in the reaction.
A luminometer can quickly detect bacterial contamination in water samples by measuring luminescence.
After adding the reagent, the technician placed the sample in the luminometer to check for ATP.
The laboratory technician calibrated the luminometer before starting the experiment.
Researchers used a luminometer to detect low levels of light emitted by the chemical reaction.
Accurate readings from the luminometer were essential for the success of the study.
