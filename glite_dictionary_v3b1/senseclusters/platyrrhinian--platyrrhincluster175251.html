platyrrhinian

noun
New World monkey with wide nostrils
Pronunciation: / ˌplætɪˈrɪniən / (AmE), / ˌplætɪˈrɪniən / (BrE)
A monkey of a group native to Central and South America, characterized by widely separated nostrils and usually a prehensile tail; a New World monkey.

Examples

A platyrrhinian monkey
Researchers observed the feeding habits of various platyrrhinians in the rainforest canopy.
Spider monkeys are a well-known type of platyrrhinian found in South America.
The main difference between a platyrrhinian and a catarrhine monkey lies in the shape and position of their nostrils.

  • antonym: catarrhine - Old World monkeys with narrow, downward-facing nostrils
