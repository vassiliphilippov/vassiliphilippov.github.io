rind

noun
outer skin of food
8.5
Pronunciation: / raɪnd / (AmE), / raɪnd / (BrE)
the tough outer skin of certain foods such as fruits, cheese, or bacon, often removed before eating

Examples

Orange rind
Pigs are often fed watermelon rind because it's nutritious and easy to digest.
She carefully cut the rind off the cheese before serving it.
The orange rind can be used to add flavor to desserts and drinks.
