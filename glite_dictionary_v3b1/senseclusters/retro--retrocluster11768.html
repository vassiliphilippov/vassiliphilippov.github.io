retro

adjective
taking effect from a past date
7.2 3.01/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆrÉ›troÊŠ / (AmE), / ËˆrÉ›trÉ™ÊŠ / (BrE)
(of a law, payment, or policy) taking effect from a date in the past; relating to or affecting things that have already happened.

Examples

Retro pay policy
A retro policy ensured that employees would be compensated for prior overtime.
She received a retro pay adjustment for the work she did last year.
The company issued a retro bonus after reviewing performance.

Synonyms

  • retroactive
    7.1 1.27/m
    retroactive applies a law or payment to past dates in formal legal use, while retro is a shorter, less formal adjective.
  • ex post facto
    9.9 0.28/m
    ex post facto refers to laws that apply to past actions in formal legal contexts, while retro is a broader, everyday adjective.

Related Terms

  • antonym: prospective - relating to or effective in the future
  • synonym: retroactive - (of a law or decision) taking effect from a date in the past
