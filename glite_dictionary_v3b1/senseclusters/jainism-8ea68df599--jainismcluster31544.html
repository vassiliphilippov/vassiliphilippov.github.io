Jainism

noun
Indian religion emphasizing nonviolence and asceticism
8.1
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒeɪ.nɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈdʒeɪ.nɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
an ancient Indian religion that teaches nonviolence, asceticism, and the pursuit of liberation from the cycle of rebirth

Examples

Jainism religion
Jainism teaches that all living beings have souls and deserve compassion.
Many followers of Jainism practice strict nonviolence in their daily lives.
The principles of Jainism have influenced the culture and philosophy of India.
Jainism promotes the principle of non-violence towards all living beings as a central tenet.
Many people in western India practice Jainism and follow its strict vegetarian diet.
The teachings of Jainism have influenced other Indian religions and philosophies.
