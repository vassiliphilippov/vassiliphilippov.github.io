analog

noun
something comparable to another thing
6.2 4.8
Pronunciation: /ˈæn.ə.lɔːg/ /; / /ˈæn.əl.ɔːg/ (AmE), /ˈæn.ə.lɒg/ /; / /ˈæn.əl.ɒg/ (BrE)
a person or thing seen as comparable to another; something that is analogous to something else

Examples

Analog to a story
His recent work serves as an analog to developments in modern architecture.
In art, stained glass is often seen as an analog to painting due to its use of color and light.
The wings of a bird and the wings of an airplane are considered analogs in function.
In biology, a bat's wing is an analogue of a bird's wing, serving a similar function.
Some people view the electric car as an analogue to earlier technological breakthroughs.
The exhibition compared ancient maps with their modern analogues to show advancements in cartography.

Related Terms

  • synonym: counterpart - a person or thing that corresponds to another in a different place or situation
  • synonym: equivalent - a person or thing equal to another in value, function, or meaning
  • antonym: opposite - something that is completely different or contrary
