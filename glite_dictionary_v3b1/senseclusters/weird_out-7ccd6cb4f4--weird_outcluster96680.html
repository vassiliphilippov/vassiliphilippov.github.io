weird out

verb
to make uneasy
Pronunciation: / wɪrd aʊt / (AmE), / wɪəd aʊt / (BrE)
to make someone feel strange, uncomfortable, or uneasy

Examples

To weird out someone
The movie's creepy soundtrack really weirded out the audience.
He was weirded out by the strange message he received late at night.
The way she stared at me completely weirded out everyone at the table.
The strange noises in the old house really weirded out the children.
I do not want to weird out my friends by talking about ghosts all night.
That movie was so bizarre it totally weirded out everyone in the theater.

Related Terms

  • antonym: comfort - to make someone feel better or less worried
  • synonym: disturb - to make someone feel worried or upset
  • synonym: freak out - to become or cause someone to become very anxious or upset
