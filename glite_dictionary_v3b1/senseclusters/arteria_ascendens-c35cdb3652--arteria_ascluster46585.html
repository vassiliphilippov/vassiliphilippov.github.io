arteria ascendens

noun
branch of artery supplying the ascending colon
9.1
Pronunciation: / ɑːrˈtɪəriə əˈsɛndɛnz / (AmE), / ɑːˈtɪəriə əˈsɛndɛnz / (BrE)
The branch of the ileocolic artery that supplies blood to the ascending colon.

Examples

The arteria ascendens branch
Damage to the arteria ascendens can compromise blood flow to the colon.
During surgery, the surgeon carefully ligated the arteria ascendens to prevent bleeding.
The arteria ascendens plays an important role in supplying blood to the ascending colon.
