pettifog

verb
argue over trivial matters
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛtiˌfɑɡ /; / ˈpɛtiˌfɑg / (AmE), / ˈpɛtɪfɒɡ /; / ˈpɛtifɒg / (BrE)
to argue or quibble over trivial or petty matters, especially in a petty, dishonest, or legal way

Examples

To pettifog over rules
Instead of making progress, the teams pettifogged about inconsequential rules.
Please don't pettifog when there are more important issues to discuss.
The committee wasted time by pettifogging over minor details in the proposal.
The lawyer tried to pettifog the case by focusing on irrelevant technicalities.
Instead of addressing the main issue, he kept pettifogging over minor details.
The committee grew frustrated as members continued to pettifog rather than make decisions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nitpick - to find fault with small or unimportant details
  • synonym: quibble - to argue or raise objections about a trivial matter
  • antonym: resolve - to settle or find a solution to a problem
