eared

adjective
worn or with turned-down corners (esp. of pages)
8.7 0.14/m

(of an object, especially a book or its pages) worn or shabby from overuse, or having corners turned down.

Examples

A eared book
She pulled a eared envelope from her bag, its corners obviously bent.
The librarian discarded eared and tattered books from the collection.
The old novel had eared pages from being read so many times.

Synonyms

  • dog-eared
    4.4 0.44/m
    dog-eared shows pages with folded-down corners from heavy use, while eared means generally worn or shabby with turned corners.
  • thumbed
    4.8 0.00/m
    thumbed means pages are soiled and worn from frequent handling with thumbs, while eared means worn or shabby, often with turned corners.
  • worn
    2.1 0.52/m
    worn is a general word for something damaged by use, while eared specifically describes books or pages shabby with turned corners.
