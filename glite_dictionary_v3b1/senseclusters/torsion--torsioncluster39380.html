torsion

noun
a twisting force; act of being twisted
Pronunciation: / ˈtɔr.ʒən /; / ˈtɔrʃən / (AmE), / ˈtɔː.ʃən /; / ˈtɔːʃən / (BrE)
the act or state of twisting, or a twisted condition (especially in objects or materials)

Examples

Shaft torsion test
A mechanic measures the torsion in the drive shaft to ensure it functions safely.
The bridge's cables are designed to withstand considerable torsion from wind and vehicles.
When you apply torsion to a rod, it twists around its axis.
The gymnast's routine included moments of remarkable torsion and balance.
The metal rod displayed significant torsion after being bent by the powerful machine.
The sculptor admired the natural torsion in the driftwood he found on the beach.
