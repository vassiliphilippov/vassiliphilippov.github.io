voxel

noun
3D digital cube
Pronunciation: / ˈvɑksəl / (AmE), / ˈvɒksəl / (BrE)
a small cube or unit that represents a value in three-dimensional digital space, especially in computer graphics or imaging

Examples

3D voxels model
The medical scan was converted into a 3D model made up of thousands of voxels.
Game developers use voxels to create detailed and interactive virtual worlds.
Each voxel in the simulation stores information about color and density.
Each voxel in the scan contains information about the tissue at that point.
The video game world was constructed entirely from colorful voxels instead of traditional polygons.
Medical imaging software can display a 3D model by arranging millions of voxels.
Each voxel in the MRI scan contains information about the tissue's properties at that location.
Scientists use millions of voxels to create detailed 3D models of the human brain.
The software allows users to adjust the color of every voxel in the simulation for better visualization.
