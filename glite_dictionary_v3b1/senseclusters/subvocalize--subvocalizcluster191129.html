subvocalize

verb
to articulate words inaudibly
9.9
Pronunciation: / sʌbˈvoʊkəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / sʌbˈvəʊkəlaɪz / (BrE)
to articulate words inaudibly, especially when reading to oneself; to pronounce words internally or without vocal sound

Examples

To subvocalize words silently
He found it helpful to subvocalize the text during difficult reading assignments.
When learning new vocabulary, some students subvocalize the words as they read them silently.
While solving math problems, she would always subvocalize the steps to herself.
If you subvocalise during an exam, the teacher might notice your lips moving.
She tried to subvocalise the speech before delivering it aloud.
Some students subvocalise while reading difficult texts to themselves.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: whisper - to speak very softly, using the breath rather than the vocal cords
