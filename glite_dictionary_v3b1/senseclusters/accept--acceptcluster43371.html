accept

verb
believe/admit as true or valid
1.0 1.3
Pronunciation: / əkˈsɛpt / (AmE), / əkˈsɛpt / (BrE)
to believe, agree with, or officially approve something as true, valid, or satisfactory

Examples

To accept an idea
Many scientists accept the theory of evolution based on the evidence.
She refused to accept his explanation for why he was late.
We must accept that mistakes were made and try to learn from them.
Many people found it difficult to accept the dramatic changes in policy.
Over time, society began to accept new styles of art.
She still can't accept the fact that her theory was proven false.
After months of debate, the council finally accepted the recommendations put forward by the task force.
The board voted unanimously to accept the committee's final report.
The university senate will accept the panel's findings during the next meeting.
