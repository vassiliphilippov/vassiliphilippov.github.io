wild

adjective
involving risk or danger
1.0
Pronunciation: / waɪld / (AmE), / waɪld / (BrE)
(of ideas, plans, or actions) risky, unconventional, unrealistic, or foolish

Examples

Wild idea
His friends thought it was a wild investment idea, but he went ahead anyway.
Launching a new company during a recession is a wild move.
They came up with a wild plan to hike across the desert without a guide.
Don't believe every wild rumor you hear on the internet.
He came up with a wild plan to build a city on the moon.
Some critics dismissed her theory as a wild fantasy with no basis in fact.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fantastic - imaginative or fanciful; remote from reality
  • synonym: hazardous - involving or exposing one to risk (especially of loss or harm)
  • synonym: risky - involving the possibility of danger or loss
  • antonym: safe - not involving risk or harm
  • antonym: sensible - based on or acting on good judgment and practical ideas or understanding
