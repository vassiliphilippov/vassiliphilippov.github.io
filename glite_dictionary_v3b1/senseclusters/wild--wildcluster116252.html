Examples

Synonyms

  • wild-eyed
    8.0 0.04/m
    wild-eyed describes extreme, unrealistic plans that seem driven by strong emotion, while wild covers risky or foolish ideas more broadly.
  • harebrained
    8.5 0.38/m
    harebrained means very foolish and ill-considered plans that ignore risk, while wild also suggests odd or risky actions and ideas.
  • crazy
    1.4 56/m
    crazy feels informal and suggests strange or surprising ideas, while wild can mean risky, unrealistic, or foolish in many contexts.
  • risky
    3.6 15/m
    risky focuses on danger or loss and suits financial or physical danger, while wild includes unconventional or foolish ideas too.
