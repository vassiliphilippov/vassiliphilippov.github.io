reestimate

verb
to estimate again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈɛstəˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈɛstɪmeɪt / (BrE)
to make a new or revised estimate of something, especially after receiving new information

Examples

To reestimate the cost
After discovering new expenses, the accountant had to reestimate the annual budget.
The engineers decided to reestimate the timeline once the project requirements changed.
We should reestimate the number of guests now that more people have responded.

Related Terms

  • synonym: estimate - to make an approximate judgment or calculation
  • synonym: reassess - to evaluate again or differently
