metabolic indican

noun
biochemical compound found in urine
9.9
Pronunciation: / məˌtæbəlɪk ɪnˈdikeɪn / (AmE), / məˌtæbəlɪk ɪnˈdɪkæn / (BrE)
an ester produced in the human body, especially in the liver, with the chemical formula C8H7NO4S, commonly found in urine as a metabolic product

Examples

High metabolic indican level
An elevated amount of metabolic indican can indicate problems with protein digestion.

The test for metabolic indican is often used in clinical laboratories.
