reengineer

verb
to redesign for improvement
5.5
Pronunciation: /riː.en.dʒə.ˈnɪr/ /; / /riː.en.dʒɪ.ˈnɪr/ (AmE), /riː.en.dʒə.ˈnɪə/ /; / /riː.en.dʒɪ.ˈnɪə/ (BrE)
to redesign or change something, especially a process, system, or product, to make it work better or more efficiently

Examples

To reengineer a process
The company decided to reengineer its entire production process to increase efficiency.
After several complaints, the software team had to reengineer the user interface.
Many businesses reengineer their workflows to adapt to new technologies.
The company decided to reengineer its entire supply chain to reduce costs.
After several failures, the team had to reengineer the product to meet safety standards.
Many organizations reengineer their processes to stay competitive in a changing market.
The company decided to reengineer its manufacturing process to reduce costs and increase efficiency.
After receiving customer feedback, the team reengineered the app to make it more user-friendly.
Engineers are working to reengineer the bridge so it can handle heavier traffic.

Related Terms

  • synonym: improve - to make something better
  • antonym: maintain - to keep something in its existing state without change
  • synonym: modify - to make changes to something
  • synonym: overhaul - to completely examine and improve a system or process
  • synonym: redesign - to design something again in a new or improved way
