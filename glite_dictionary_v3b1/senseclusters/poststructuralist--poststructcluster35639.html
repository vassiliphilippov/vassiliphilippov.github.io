poststructuralist

noun
theory critic
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊstˈstrʌktʃərəlɪst / (AmE), / ˌpəʊstˈstrʌktʃərəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who studies or supports theories that question fixed meanings in language, culture, or society

Examples

A poststructuralist thinker
The poststructuralist argued that language cannot have a single, fixed meaning.
Many literary critics identify as poststructuralists when analyzing modern texts.
A poststructuralist approach often challenges traditional views of culture and society.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deconstructionist - a person who analyzes texts by deconstructing their assumed meanings
  • antonym: structuralist - a person who studies or supports structuralism, focusing on underlying structures in culture or language
