Serratula tinctoria

noun
a plant yielding yellow dye
9.9
Pronunciation: / sɛrəˈtjuːlə tɪŋkˈtɔːriə / (AmE), / sɛrəˈtjuːlə tɪŋkˈtɔːriə / (BrE)
A European perennial plant with serrated leaves that produce a yellow dye, also known as dyer's saw-wort.

Examples

Serratula tinctoria leaves
Botanists identified the distinctive serrated leaves of Serratula tinctoria thriving in the meadow.
Historically, Serratula tinctoria was cultivated to provide a yellow dye for textiles.
The fields were covered with Serratula tinctoria, their purple blooms attracting bees and butterflies.
