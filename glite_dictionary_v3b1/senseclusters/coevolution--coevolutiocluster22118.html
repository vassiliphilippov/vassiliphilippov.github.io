coevolution

noun
mutual evolutionary change
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˌkoʊˌɛvəˈluːʃən / (AmE), / ˌkəʊˌiːvəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
the process by which two or more things change or develop together by influencing each other, especially in biology or technology

Examples

The coevolution of species
The coevolution of flowering plants and pollinating insects has shaped many ecosystems.
Technological coevolution can be seen in the way cars and road systems have developed together.
Cultural coevolution between neighboring countries often leads to shared traditions and customs.
The coevolution of predators and prey can lead to remarkable adaptations in both groups.
Scientists study coevolution to understand how plants and insects have shaped each other's development.
The close coevolution between certain birds and flowers results in specialized feeding behaviors.
In artificial intelligence research, coevolution is used to improve the performance of competing algorithms.
The coevolution of predators and prey can be simulated to study adaptive strategies.
Scientists observed coevolution between two machine learning models as they learned from each other.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coevolve - to evolve together with another system or population
  • antonym: competition - interaction where species compete for resources
  • synonym: evolution - the gradual development of something, especially from a simple to a more complex form
  • antonym: independent evolution - evolution or change occurring separately without mutual influence
  • synonym: interaction - a process where two or more things affect each other
  • synonym: mutualism - a relationship where two species benefit from each other
