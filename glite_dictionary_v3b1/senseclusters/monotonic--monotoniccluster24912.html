monotonic

adjective
having an unvarying pitch or tone
8.1 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / ËŒmÉ‘nÉ™ËˆtÉ‘nÉªk / (AmE), / ËŒmÉ’nÉ™ËˆtÉ’nÉªk / (BrE)
(of a sound or voice) having an unvarying pitch or tone; lacking in variation of tone or inflection.

Examples

Monotonic voice
Her singing was criticized as monotonic, lacking any emotional highs or lows.
The automated announcement was delivered in a monotonic tone, making it hard to distinguish important details.
The professor's monotonic voice made it difficult for the students to stay focused during the lecture.

Synonyms

    5.8 0.08/m
    monotone is a single, unvarying voice or sound, while monotonic describes that same lack of pitch change in speech.
  • monotonal
    7.3 0.02/m
    monotonal uses only one musical or sound pitch, while monotonic refers to an unchanging voice tone in general speech.
  • inflectionless
    7.9 0.01/m
    inflectionless is spoken without any change in pitch or emphasis, while monotonic simply means an unvarying tone.
  • monotonous
    5.3 0.49/m
    monotonous sounds dull and often repeats the same pattern, while monotonic focuses only on an unvarying pitch or tone.
  • flat
    4.4 17/m
    flat sounds dull and lacking emotion in many contexts, while monotonic describes only a voice with unchanging pitch.
