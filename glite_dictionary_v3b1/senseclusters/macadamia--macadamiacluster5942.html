macadamia

noun
Australian tree cultivated for edible nuts
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˌmæk.əˈdeɪ.mi.ə / (AmE), / ˌmək.əˈdeɪ.mi.ə / (BrE)
A tropical tree of the genus Macadamia, native to Australia, cultivated for its edible nuts.

Examples

Macadamia tree
A mature macadamia can produce hundreds of nuts each year.
Farmers have started planting more macadamia trees to meet the rising demand for nuts.
The macadamia thrives in the subtropical regions of Australia and Hawaii.
