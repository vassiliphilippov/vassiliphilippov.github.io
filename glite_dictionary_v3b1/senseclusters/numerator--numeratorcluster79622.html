numerator

noun
top number in a fraction
3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈnuː.məˌreɪ.t̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈnjuː.məˌreɪ.tə / (BrE)
the number above the line in a fraction, which shows how many parts are being considered

Examples

The numerator in a fraction
By increasing the numerator, you make the fraction larger.
If the numerator and denominator are the same, the fraction equals one.
The numerator of the fraction 3/4 is 3.

Related Terms

  • antonym: denominator - the bottom number in a fraction
