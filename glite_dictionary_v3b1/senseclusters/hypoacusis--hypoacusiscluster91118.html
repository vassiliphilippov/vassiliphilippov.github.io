hypoacusis

noun
partial hearing loss
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpoʊəˈkuːsɪs / (AmE), / ˌhʌɪpəʊəˈkjuːsɪs / (BrE)
a partial loss of hearing or a reduced ability to hear sounds

Examples

Partial hypoacusis loss
The audiologist explained that hypoacusis can affect people of all ages.
After years of working in a noisy factory, he developed mild hypoacusis.
Early detection of hypoacusis can help prevent further hearing damage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hearing loss - a reduced or total inability to hear
  • antonym: normal hearing - the ability to hear sounds without impairment
