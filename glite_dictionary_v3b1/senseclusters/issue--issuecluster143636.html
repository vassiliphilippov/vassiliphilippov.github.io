issue

noun
officially distributed government supplies
Pronunciation: / ˈɪʃu / (AmE), / ˈɪsjuː / (BrE)
the act of officially giving, supplying, or distributing something (such as supplies, documents, or printed materials)

Examples

Issue of supplies
Each soldier received a weekly issue of rations and clean uniforms.
The issue of ammunition was delayed due to transportation problems.
Winter coats were part of the standard issue for all new recruits.
After the issue of the press release, the story appeared in several newspapers.
The issue of the new magazine was delayed by a week.
We await the issue of the annual company report.
Due to a shortage, the issue of ration cards was delayed.
Each issue of supplies must be recorded accurately in the inventory log.
The issue of new uniforms to all employees will begin next week.
