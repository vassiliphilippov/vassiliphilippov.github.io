emotivism

noun
moral theory of emotion
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmoʊtɪˌvɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪˈməʊtɪˌvɪzəm / (BrE)
the theory that moral judgments are expressions of emotional attitudes rather than statements of fact

Examples

Moral emotivism
Many philosophers debate whether emotivism can fully explain the nature of moral language.
According to emotivism, saying 'stealing is wrong' simply expresses disapproval of stealing.
Some critics argue that emotivism fails to account for the reasoning behind moral arguments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: moral realism - the belief that moral statements are factual claims about objective reality
  • synonym: subjectivism - the doctrine that moral judgments are statements about personal feelings or attitudes
