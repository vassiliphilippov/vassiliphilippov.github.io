come out of one's shell

verb
become more outgoing or sociable
2.6
Pronunciation: / kʌm aʊt əv wʌnz ʃɛl / (AmE), / kʌm aʊt əv wʌnz ʃɛl / (BrE)
to become more confident, outgoing, or willing to interact with others than before

Examples

To come out of her shell

It was wonderful to see Mark come out of his shell during summer camp.
Teachers hope their students will come out of their shell and participate more in class.
