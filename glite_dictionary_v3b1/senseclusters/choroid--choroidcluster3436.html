choroid

noun
vascular membrane in the eye
9.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…îÀêrÀå…î…™d / (AmE), / Ààk…îÀêr…î…™d / (BrE)
the highly vascular layer of the eye between the retina and the sclera, which contains dark pigment to reduce light scattering within the eye

Examples

The choroid layer
A disease affecting the choroid can lead to vision problems or blindness.
During the examination, the doctor noted abnormalities in the patient's choroid.
The choroid supplies oxygen and nutrients to the outer layers of the retina.
