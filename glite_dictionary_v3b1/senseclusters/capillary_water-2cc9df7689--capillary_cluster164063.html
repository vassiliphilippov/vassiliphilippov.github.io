capillary water

noun
water retained in soil by capillary action
5.9 5.0
Pronunciation: /ˈkæp.ɪ.lə.riː ˈwɔː.tər/ /; / /ˈkæp.ɪ.lə.riː ˈwɑː.tər/ (AmE), /ˈkæp.ɪ.lə.riː ˈwɔː.tə/ /; / /kə.ˈpɪl.ə.riː ˈwɔː.tə/ (BrE)
water held in the soil after gravitational water has drained away, remaining as a film around soil particles and moving by capillary action

Examples

Capillary water in soil
Capillary water remains available to plant roots even after excess water has drained from the soil.
Farmers monitor capillary water levels to determine optimal irrigation schedules.
The film of capillary water around soil grains is crucial for sustaining crops during dry periods.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gravitational water - water in the soil that drains under gravity after rainfall or irrigation
  • degree lower: hygroscopic water - water in soil held so tightly by particles that it cannot be removed by air drying
