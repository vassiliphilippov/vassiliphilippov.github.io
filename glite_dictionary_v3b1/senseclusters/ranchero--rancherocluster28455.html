ranchero

noun
ranch worker or owner
6.8
Pronunciation: /ræn.tʃ.ˈer.oʊ/ /; / /rɑːn.tʃ.ˈer.oʊ/ (AmE), /ræn.tʃ.ˈeə.rəʊ/ /; / /rɑːn.tʃ.ˈeə.rəʊ/ (BrE)
a person who owns or works on a ranch, especially in Mexico or the southwestern United States

Examples

A ranchero rides horses
The ranchero rode across the fields to check on the cattle.
Every morning, the ranchero wakes up early to tend to the horses.
The family hired a skilled ranchero to help manage their land in Texas.
The ranchero rode his horse across the open fields at sunrise.
Many rancheros in the region raise cattle and grow crops for a living.
As a young ranchero, he learned to care for animals and repair fences.
