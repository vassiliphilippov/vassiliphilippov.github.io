sheaf

noun
bundle of stalks tied together
Pronunciation: / ʃif / (AmE), / ʃiːf / (BrE)
A bundle of items, usually stalks of grain, tied together for carrying or storing.

Examples

A sheaf of papers
A sheaf of documents was stacked neatly on the lawyer's desk.
He carried a sheaf of wheat under his arm as he walked through the field.
The teacher handed her a sheaf of exams to grade over the weekend.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bundle - A collection of things tied or wrapped together
