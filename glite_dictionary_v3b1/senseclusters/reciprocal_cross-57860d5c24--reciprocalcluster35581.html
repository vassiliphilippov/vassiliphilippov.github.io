reciprocal cross

noun
genetics: breeding pairing with reversed parental roles
7.6
Pronunciation: / rɪˈsɪprəkəl krɔs / (AmE), / rɪˈsɪprəkəl krɒs / (BrE)
a breeding experiment in which two individuals with different genotypes are crossed in two ways, reversing which parent provides each genotype, to observe differences due to parental sex

Examples

A reciprocal cross experiment
Performing a reciprocal cross helps identify whether a gene is located on a sex chromosome.
Scientists used a reciprocal cross to determine the effect of parent sex on inheritance.
The reciprocal cross revealed that the trait was inherited maternally.
