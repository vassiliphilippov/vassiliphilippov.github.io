isostatic

adjective
crust equilibrium
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪsəˈstætɪk / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəˈstætɪk / (BrE)
relating to the equilibrium of Earth's crust as it floats on the mantle, especially after changes in weight such as melting glaciers

Examples

Isostatic adjustment of land
The isostatic adjustment of the land continued for thousands of years after the ice sheets disappeared.
Scientists study isostatic changes to understand how landscapes evolve over time.
The coastline rose due to isostatic rebound following the retreat of ancient glaciers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: isostasy - the state of balance of Earth's crust floating on the mantle
