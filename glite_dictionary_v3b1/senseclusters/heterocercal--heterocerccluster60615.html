heterocercal

adjective
with asymmetrical tail fins (fish)
8.5 9.1
Pronunciation: /het.ə.rə.ˈsɜːr.kəl/ (AmE), /het.ə.rə.ˈsɜː.kəl/ (BrE)
(of a fish's tail) having the upper lobe larger than the lower and the vertebral column extending into the upper lobe.

Examples

Heterocercal tail
Paleontologists studied the structure of the heterocercal tail to understand the fish's movement.
Sharks are known for their heterocercal caudal fins, which aid in powerful swimming.
The prehistoric fish had a distinctly heterocercal tail, unlike most modern species.

Related Terms

  • antonym: homocercal - having a tail fin with symmetrical upper and lower lobes
