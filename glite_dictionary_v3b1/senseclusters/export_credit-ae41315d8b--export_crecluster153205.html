export credit

noun
credit arrangement for financing exports
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɛkˌspɔrt ˈkrɛdɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛkˌspɔːt ˈkrɛdɪt / (BrE)
a loan or line of credit provided to an importer by a bank in the exporter's country, used to finance the purchase of exported goods or services

Examples

Export credit loan
Exporters often rely on export credit to bridge the gap between shipment and payment.
The company secured an export credit to facilitate the sale of machinery to Brazil.
The government introduced new export credit schemes to boost international trade.
