submission

noun
the act or state of yielding to authority
Pronunciation: / səbˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / səbˈmɪʃən /; / səbˈmɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act or state of accepting the control or authority of someone else; obedience or yielding to another's will

Examples

Forced submission
After months of negotiations, the company finally forced the workers into submission.
He regarded submission to tradition as a sign of weakness.
Her submission to his authority surprised everyone in the room.
He showed submission by bowing his head and apologizing.
Her quiet submission to the rules impressed the teachers.
The general demanded complete submission from his troops before they entered battle.
His quiet submission revealed more strength than open defiance ever could.
She bowed her head in submission as the judge delivered his verdict.
The villagers lived in complete submission to their ruler's wishes.
