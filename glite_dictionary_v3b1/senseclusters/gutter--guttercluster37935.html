gutter

noun
rainwater channel on roof
Pronunciation: / ˈɡʌt̬.ɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡʌt.ər / (BrE)
A narrow channel fixed to the edge of a roof or along the eaves to collect and carry away rainwater.

Examples

Roof gutter
After the heavy storm, leaves filled the gutter along the roof.
Clogged gutters can cause water to overflow and damage your house.
The old house needed new gutters to protect its foundation from rainwater.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drainpipe - pipe that carries rainwater from a gutter down a building
  • synonym: eavestrough - Canadian term for a gutter fixed to roof eaves
