metasomatic

adjective
rock altered by fluids
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˈsɑːmɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈsɒmætɪk / (BrE)
relating to the chemical alteration of a rock or mineral by fluids, especially during geological processes

Examples

The metasomatic rock changes
The metasomatic changes in the rock were caused by hot, mineral-rich fluids.
Geologists studied the metasomatic zones to understand the area's mineral deposits.
A metasomatic process can introduce new elements into a rock, altering its composition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: metamorphic - relating to rocks changed by heat and pressure
