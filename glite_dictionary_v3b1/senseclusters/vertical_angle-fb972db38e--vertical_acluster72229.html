vertical angle

noun
angle opposite another at line intersection
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈvɝːtɪkəl ˈæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈvɜːtɪkəl ˈæŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
either of the two angles opposite each other formed when two straight lines intersect; these angles are equal.

Examples

When two lines cross, each pair of vertical angles is always equal to one another.
