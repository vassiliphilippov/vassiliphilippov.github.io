matte

noun
mixture of sulfides from smelting ore
9.9
Pronunciation: /mæt/ (AmE), /mæt/ (BrE)
a mixture of sulfides, especially of metals such as copper and iron, formed as an intermediate product when sulfide metal ores are smelted

Examples

Copper matte
After the first stage of smelting, a layer of matte separates from the rest of the material.
Molten matte collects below the slag during the copper smelting process.
The converter removes iron and sulfur from the matte to obtain pure metal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: slag - the glass-like byproduct left over after the desired metal has been separated from its raw ore
