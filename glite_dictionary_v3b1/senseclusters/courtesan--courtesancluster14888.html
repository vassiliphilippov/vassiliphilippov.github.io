courtesan

noun
a woman who is the companion of wealthy or powerful men
5.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrtɪsən / (AmE), / ˈkɔːtɪzæn / (BrE)
a woman who has sexual relations with influential or wealthy men in exchange for money or privileges, especially as a recognized part of high society in the past

Examples

A courtesan in history
During the Renaissance, a courtesan could wield significant social influence at court.
She read a biography about a famous courtesan who lived in 18th-century Paris.
The king was rumored to have a courtesan who advised him on political matters.
