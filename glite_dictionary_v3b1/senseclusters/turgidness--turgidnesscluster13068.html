turgidness

noun
pompous or ornate quality in language
Pronunciation: / ˈtɚrdʒɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈtɜːdʒɪdnəs / (BrE)
The quality of being pompous or excessively ornate, especially in language or style.

Examples

Turgidness of writing
Critics mocked the turgidness of her latest novel, calling it unnecessarily flowery.
Some readers are put off by the turgidness of academic writing.
The turgidness of his speech made it difficult for the audience to follow.

Related Terms

  • antonym: plainness - simple or unornamented language
  • synonym: pomposity - self-important or pretentious language or style
