trilemma

noun
three-way dilemma
9.6
Pronunciation: / traɪˈlɛmə / (AmE), / traɪˈlɛmə / (BrE)
a situation where you must choose between three difficult or competing options, and you cannot have all three at the same time

Examples

A trilemma situation
The committee faced a trilemma when deciding which of the three projects to fund.
Economists often discuss the policy trilemma involving exchange rates, capital flow, and monetary policy.
Choosing between work, family, and personal health can sometimes feel like a trilemma for many people.
The government faced a trilemma when trying to balance economic growth, environmental protection, and social equality.
In international finance, the policy trilemma means a country cannot have free capital movement, a fixed exchange rate, and independent monetary policy all at once.
The manager explained the trilemma of choosing between speed, quality, and cost in project development.
The economist explained the policy trilemma that forces countries to choose only two out of three goals.
In philosophy, a trilemma often challenges people to reconsider their assumptions.
The committee faced a trilemma when trying to balance cost, quality, and speed.
