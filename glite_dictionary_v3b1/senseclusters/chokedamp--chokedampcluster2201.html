chokedamp

noun
mine atmosphere high in carbon dioxide
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃoʊkˌdæmp / (AmE), / ˈtʃəʊkˌdamp / (BrE)
an atmosphere in a mine, especially after an explosion, that contains a high concentration of carbon dioxide and is incapable of supporting life

Examples

Deadly chokedamp in mine
After the mining accident, rescue workers encountered pockets of chokedamp that made breathing impossible.
Miners are trained to recognize the dangers of chokedamp and use breathing apparatus when necessary.
The presence of chokedamp in the tunnel required immediate evacuation of all personnel.

Related Terms

  • synonym: afterdamp - gas mixture remaining in a mine after an explosion, mainly carbon monoxide, dangerous to life
  • degree lower: firedamp - a mixture of gases, mainly methane, found in coal mines
