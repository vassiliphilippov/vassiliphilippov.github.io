reflectionist

noun
theory of reflection believer
Pronunciation: / rɪˈflɛkʃənɪst / (AmE), / rɪˈflɛkʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes in or practices the theory that art or knowledge reflects reality, especially in philosophy or art

Examples

Reflectionist art theory
The reflectionist argued that art should mirror the real world as closely as possible.
Many philosophers debated with the reflectionist about the true nature of knowledge.
As a reflectionist, she believed literature must represent actual social conditions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: idealist - a person who believes that reality is mentally constructed or immaterial
  • synonym: realist - a person who believes that art or knowledge should represent reality accurately
