Examples

Had theretofore lived
He lived in Paris, having theretofore resided in London for many years.
No evidence had emerged theretofore to prove his innocence.

Synonyms

  • previously
    1.1 59/m
    previously means at an earlier time or before the time mentioned, while theretofore refers to up to that specific earlier time.
  • heretofore
    9.7 0.86/m
    heretofore is used to mean up to this time in formal or legal texts, while theretofore points back to an earlier time.
  • hitherto
    8.2 2.12/m
    hitherto means until now or up to the present, while theretofore refers back to a time earlier than the present.
