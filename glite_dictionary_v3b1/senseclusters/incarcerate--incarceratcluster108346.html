incarcerate

verb
to imprison or confine
8.4 0.68/m
Pronunciation: / …™nÀàk…ërs…ôÀåre…™t / (AmE), / …™nÀàk…ëÀês…ôre…™t / (BrE)
to put or keep someone in prison or a place from which they cannot escape; to confine in jail

Examples

To incarcerate criminals
Activists argue that society should address root causes of crime instead of simply incarcerating offenders.
After being wrongly incarcerated for a decade, she was finally released when new evidence emerged.
The judge decided to incarcerate him for five years due to the seriousness of his crimes.

Synonyms

  • imprison
    4.2 9.33/m
    imprison means to put someone in prison as punishment or by force, while incarcerate is a more formal legal word.
  • jail
    2.1 4.63/m
    jail often means holding someone in a local lockup or for a short time, while incarcerate sounds more formal and long term.

Related Terms
