separatism

noun
the policy or practice of separation from a larger group
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛpəˌrætɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈsepərətɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief in or practice of people separating from a larger group, country, or organization, often for reasons of ethnicity, religion, or politics.

Examples

Regional separatism
Economic disparities fueled separatism among several regions of the country.
Government leaders debated how to manage rising separatism within the border provinces.
Religious separatism sometimes leads to the formation of new communities or schools.

Related Terms

  • antonym: integration - the action of incorporating into a whole, especially in social or political contexts
  • synonym: secession - the action of formally withdrawing from a group or organization
