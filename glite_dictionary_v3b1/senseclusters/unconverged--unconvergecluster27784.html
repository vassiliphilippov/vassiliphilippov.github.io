unconverged

adjective
not stable or final
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnkənˈvɜrdʒd / (AmE), / ˌʌnkənˈvɜːdʒd / (BrE)
not having reached a stable, final, or agreed state, especially in calculations or processes

Examples

Unconverged results
The results remained unconverged even after increasing the number of iterations in the algorithm.
If the process is unconverged, the final outcome cannot be trusted.
Engineers checked why the model was still unconverged after several hours of computation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: converged - having reached a stable or final state
  • synonym: unstable - not steady or fixed; likely to change
