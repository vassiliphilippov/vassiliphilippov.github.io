deaf-mutism

noun
congenital inability to hear and speak
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛf ˌmjuː.tɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈdɛːf ˌmjuː.tɪzəm / (BrE)
A congenital condition in which a person is both deaf and unable to speak, typically due to inability to hear language.

Examples

A deaf-mutism condition
Doctors diagnosed the child with deaf-mutism at an early age.
Early intervention is crucial for those affected by deaf-mutism to develop effective communication skills.
Historically, education for children with deaf-mutism focused on alternative communication methods.

  • synonym: deafness - complete or partial inability to hear
