gag rule

noun
rule prohibiting debate on an issue
4.0 6.9
Pronunciation: /gæɡ ruːl/ (AmE), /gæɡ ruːl/ (BrE)
a rule or regulation that limits or prohibits discussion or debate on a particular topic in a deliberative body

Examples

A gag rule in parliament
A gag rule was imposed, silencing all debate on the subject in parliament.
Activists argued that the gag rule violated freedom of speech within the committee.
The senator strongly opposed the gag rule that prevented discussion on the controversial bill.
