ANDA

noun
FDA generic drug application
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈeɪ.ɛn.diː.eɪ/ /; / /ˈeɪ.ən.diː.eɪ/ (AmE), /ˈeɪ.ɛn.diː.eɪ/ /; / /ˈeɪ.ən.diː.eɪ/ (BrE)
an application submitted to the U.S. Food and Drug Administration to request approval to market a generic drug (Abbreviated New Drug Application)

Examples

An ANDA submission
The pharmaceutical company submitted an ANDA to gain approval for its generic medication.
Before marketing a generic drug in the U.S., an ANDA must be approved by the FDA.
The review process for an ANDA ensures the generic drug is safe and effective.
