dominate

verb
have control or power over
3.7 25/m
Pronunciation: / Ààd…ëÀê.m…ôÀåne…™t / (AmE), / Ààd…ím.…™.ne…™t / (BrE)
to have control, influence, or power over someone or something; to master or defeat completely

Examples

Dominate the game
In the chess game, he managed to dominate his opponent from the very start.
She let fear dominate her decisions, leading to missed opportunities.
The company continued to dominate the market despite strong competition.

Synonyms

  • control
    2.4 93/m
    control means managing or directing people, things, or processes, while dominate suggests having stronger, lasting power or total control.
  • rule over
    4.2 1.44/m
    rule over is to have formal authority over a place or people, while dominate covers any strong control or influence.
  • hold sway
    6.6 1.04/m
    hold sway means to have strong influence over people or events, while dominate often implies complete power or control.
  • subject
    7.7 4.91/m
    subject means to force someone under control or authority, while dominate can mean to control broadly or defeat completely.
  • subjugate
    9.2 1.70/m
    subjugate is to bring people under control, often by force and conquest, while dominate can be broader or less violent.
  • conquer
    3.7 16/m
    conquer means to take control of land or people by force, while dominate can mean control or influence in many situations.
  • overpower
    3.8 1.47/m
    overpower means to defeat someone using greater strength or force, while dominate can include influence without direct force.
  • prevail over
    5.8 0.82/m
    prevail over means to win or prove more powerful in a contest, while dominate suggests ongoing control or wide influence.
  • master
    4.1 15/m
    master means to learn or overcome something by skill, while dominate focuses on holding power or defeating others completely.

Related Terms

  • antonym: submit - to yield to another's authority or control
