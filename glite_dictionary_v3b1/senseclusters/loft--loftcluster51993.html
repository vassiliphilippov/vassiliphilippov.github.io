loft

noun
backward slant of a golf club's face
8.1 0.8/m
Pronunciation: / lÉ‘ft / (AmE), / lÉ’ft / (BrE)
(in golf) the backward slant of the face of a golf club that determines how high the ball will rise when struck.

Examples

Loft of a club
Adjusting the loft of your club can help you control the ball's trajectory.
He prefers a club with more loft for shorter, higher shots onto the green.
The driver has less loft than the wedge, making the ball travel farther but lower.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lie - the angle between the shaft and the ground when the club is in a normal playing position (golf)
