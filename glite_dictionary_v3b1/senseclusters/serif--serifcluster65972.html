serif

noun
small line at end of letter stroke
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛr.ɪf / (AmE), / ˈsɛr.ɪf / (BrE)
a small line or stroke attached to the end of a letter or symbol in a font or typeface

Examples

Serif font style
He prefers books printed in serif fonts over sans-serif ones.
Many traditional newspapers use fonts with serifs for easier reading.
The designer chose a modern, serif typeface for the headings.
I prefer reading books in typefaces that have serifs, as they feel more traditional.
Modern digital screens often display serif fonts less clearly than sans-serif ones.
The designer chose a classic font with elegant serifs for the headline.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sans serif - typeface style without serifs
  • antonym: sans-serif - a typeface without serifs
