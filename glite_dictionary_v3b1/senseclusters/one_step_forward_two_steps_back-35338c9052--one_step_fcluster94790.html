one step forward, two steps back

x
progress negated by greater setbacks
1.2
Pronunciation: / wʌn stɛp ˈfɔrwərd tu stɛps bæk / (AmE), / wʌn stɛp ˈfɔːwəd tuː stɛps bæk / (BrE)
used to describe a situation in which progress is offset or undone by even greater setbacks

Examples

One step forward, two steps back situation
Dealing with bureaucracy often feels like one step forward, two steps back when every solution brings new issues.
His recovery seemed to be one step forward, two steps back, as good days were always followed by setbacks.
Trying to master the new software was one step forward, two steps back; every time I learned something, I ran into more problems.
