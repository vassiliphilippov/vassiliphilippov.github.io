purge

verb
cleanse or rinse with a liquid
Pronunciation: / pɝdʒ /; / pɝːdʒ / (AmE), / pɜːdʒ / (BrE)
to remove or get rid of unwanted substances, people, or things, especially by cleansing or elimination

Examples

To purge old files
Before refilling the aquarium, you should purge it with fresh water to remove any residue.
Doctors will purge a wound to ensure there is no infection left behind.
The technician had to purge the fuel lines before starting the repairs.
Drinking plenty of water can help purge toxins from your system.
She practiced meditation to purge negative thoughts from her mind.
The company had to purge its database of outdated records to improve performance.
After the government changed hands, several officials were purged from their positions for opposing the new regime.
She was purged from the committee due to her outspoken views.
The party regularly purges its ranks of anyone suspected of disloyalty.
