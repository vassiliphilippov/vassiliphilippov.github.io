loose

adjective
lacking restraint or responsibility
5.6 2.10/m
Pronunciation: / lus /; / luÀês / (AmE), / luÀês / (BrE)
careless or lacking restraint, discipline, or morals (especially in behavior, speech, or sexual conduct)

Examples

Loose talk
A loose tongue can quickly lead to misunderstandings or even conflicts.
He got into trouble because of his loose way of talking about confidential matters.
She was criticized for her loose handling of the department's finances.
He warned his son to stay away from loose company that could get him into trouble.
Some people unfairly labeled her as a loose woman because of her outgoing nature.
Tabloid magazines often gossip about celebrities' so-called loose lifestyles.
