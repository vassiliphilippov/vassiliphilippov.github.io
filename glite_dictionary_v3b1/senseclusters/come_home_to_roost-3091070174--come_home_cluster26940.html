come home to roost

verb
to face consequences of one's actions
8.2
Pronunciation: / kʌm hoʊm tu ruːst / (AmE), / kʌm həʊm tuː ruːst / (BrE)
(of one's actions or words) to have unpleasant consequences for the person who performed or said them; to face the results of one's behavior, usually after some time.

Examples

Problems come home to roost
After years of neglecting his health, the effects finally came home to roost in his forties.
All the lies she told at work are about to come home to roost.
His dishonesty came home to roost when no one trusted him anymore.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reap what you sow - to experience the consequences of one's actions
