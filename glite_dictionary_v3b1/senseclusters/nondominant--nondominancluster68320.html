nondominant

adjective
not dominant (genetics)
7.0
Pronunciation: /nɑːn.də.ˈmɪn.ənt/ (AmE), /nɒn.də.ˈmɪn.ənt/ (BrE)
not expressing dominance; describing an allele or trait that does not show its effect when paired with a dominant allele.

Examples

Nondominant gene
A nondominant gene will only show its effect if both copies are the same.
The disorder is caused by a nondominant allele inherited from both parents.
Scientists studied the nondominant traits to understand how they are passed down.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dominant - an allele or trait that expresses itself over another
  • synonym: recessive - an allele or trait that is masked by a dominant one
