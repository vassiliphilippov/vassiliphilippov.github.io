radio compass

noun
navigation instrument using radio signals
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪdioʊ ˈkɑːmpəs / (AmE), / ˈreɪdiəʊ ˈkʌmpəs / (BrE)
an instrument that determines direction by detecting the direction of incoming radio signals, used for navigation

Examples

A radio compass instrument
Engineers demonstrated how a radio compass can quickly locate emergency signals.
Older ships often relied on the radio compass for safe navigation at sea.
The pilot checked the radio compass to confirm the plane's heading during the storm.
