handicap

noun
advantage/disadvantage to equalize competition
Pronunciation: / ˈhæn.diˌkæp / (AmE), / ˈhæn.dɪ.kæp / (BrE)
an advantage or disadvantage given to a competitor in order to equalize the chances of winning

Examples

A golf handicap
Because he was a much better golfer, he played with a handicap to make the match fair.
Chess matches sometimes use a handicap by starting one player with a pawn less.
The tournament organizers imposed a time handicap to give the new team a better chance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: advantage - something that helps one to be more successful than others
  • antonym: disadvantage - an unfavorable circumstance that reduces the chances of success
