Christmas disease

noun
rare bleeding disorder, hemophilia B
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪs·məs dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈkrɪs.məs dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
a rare clotting disorder, also known as hemophilia B, caused by a congenital deficiency of factor IX in the blood

Examples

Christmas disease bleeding
His nephew was diagnosed with Christmas disease, so the family sought specialized treatment.
Patients with Christmas disease require regular infusions of factor IX to prevent bleeding episodes.
The distinction between hemophilia A and Christmas disease is important for proper medical management.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hemophilia - a group of inherited bleeding disorders characterized by difficulty in blood clotting
  • degree lower: hemophilia A - a similar bleeding disorder caused by a deficiency of factor VIII
