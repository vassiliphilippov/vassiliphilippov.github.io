Rottweiler

noun
large German breed of dog
Pronunciation: / ˈrɑtˌwaɪlər / (AmE), / ˈrɒtˌvaɪlə / (BrE)
A large, powerful breed of dog with a short black coat and tan markings, originally from Germany and often used as a guard or cattle dog.

Examples

A Rottweiler dog
A well-trained Rottweiler can make a loyal and protective family pet.
My neighbor adopted a Rottweiler from the animal shelter last month.
The police use Rottweilers because of their strength and intelligence.
