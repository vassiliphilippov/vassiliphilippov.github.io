Synonyms

  • type I diabetes
    3.5 0.09/m
    type I diabetes is a chronic condition where the pancreas makes little or no insulin, while juvenile diabetes names the same early-onset, insulin-dependent illness.
  • juvenile-onset diabetes
    4.1 0.03/m
    juvenile-onset diabetes appears in children or adolescents and needs insulin from early on, while juvenile diabetes is the common term for that condition.
  • insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus
    4.9 0.04/m
    insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus says regular insulin injections are needed, while juvenile diabetes focuses on its early life onset and lifelong insulin use.
  • autoimmune diabetes
    4.5 0.00/m
    autoimmune diabetes is caused by the immune system attacking insulin cells, while juvenile diabetes emphasizes the early age and lifelong insulin treatment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: type 2 diabetes - a type of diabetes that develops mainly in adults, often associated with overweight
