to leave out or not include someone or something, either intentionally or accidentally

Examples

To omit a detail
If you omit any required fields on the form, it will not be processed.
She accidentally omitted his name from the guest list.
The editor decided to omit the final chapter from the published version.
Certain sensitive data was omitted from the official report.
If you omit any details, your application might be rejected.
She decided to omit the final paragraph from her essay.

Synonyms

  • leave out
    2.3 10/m
    leave out means not to include someone or something in a list or group, while omit covers accidental or deliberate leaving out.
  • leave off
    4.4 0.72/m
    leave off means to stop including something or to leave it out in discussion, whereas omit can be accidental or intentional.
  • drop
    3.1 60/m
    drop means to remove or stop mentioning someone or something casually, while omit is more neutral about reason or intent.
  • exclude
    3.0 34/m
    exclude means to keep someone or something out on purpose, while omit can be either a choice or a simple mistake.
  • overlook
    2.9 19/m
    overlook means to miss or fail to notice something, whereas omit includes both failing to include and deliberately leaving out.
  • take out
    1.9 32/m
    take out means to remove something from a place or group physically or abstractly, but omit focuses on not including.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exclude - to deliberately leave something out
  • antonym: include - to contain as part of the whole
