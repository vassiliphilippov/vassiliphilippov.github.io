Chemnitzer concertina

noun
a hand-held European concertina instrument
Pronunciation: / kɛmˈnɪtsər kənˈsɜrtɪnə / (AmE), / kɛmˈnɪtsə kənˈsɜːtɪnə / (BrE)
a type of hand-held, bellows-driven free-reed musical instrument similar to an accordion, originating from Chemnitz, Germany, and characterized by its square shape and distinct button layout.

Examples

A Chemnitzer concertina tune
He played a lively tune on his Chemnitzer concertina at the festival.
She inherited her grandfather's antique Chemnitzer concertina.
The Chemnitzer concertina is popular in traditional polka music.
