Azalea

noun
flowering shrub, subgenus of Rhododendron
3.2
Pronunciation: / əˈzeɪliə / (AmE), / əˈzeɪliə / (BrE)
a subgenus of mostly evergreen or deciduous flowering shrubs, formerly treated as a separate genus, now included in the genus Rhododendron

Examples

Bright azalea bushes
Every spring, the azalea bushes in our garden burst into vibrant blooms.
Some species of azalea thrive better in shady, acidic soil.
They planted a pink azalea near the entrance to add color to the landscape.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Rhododendron - any shrub or small tree of the genus Rhododendron, including azaleas
