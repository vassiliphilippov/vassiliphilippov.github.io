Precambrian eon

noun
early geological eon of Earth
9.9
Pronunciation: /priː.ˈkæm.briː.ən ˈiː.ɑːn/ /; / /prɪ.ˈkæm.brɪ.ən ˈiː.ɑːn/ (AmE), /priː.ˈkæm.brɪ.ən ˈiː.ən/ /; / /prɪ.ˈkæm.brɪ.ən ˈeɪ.ɒn/ (BrE)
the eon in Earth's geological history that follows the Hadean and precedes the Phanerozoic, spanning from about 3,800 million to 544 million years ago

Examples

The Precambrian eon
Fossils from the Precambrian eon are extremely rare due to the simple life forms that existed then.
Many of the oldest rocks on Earth formed during the Precambrian eon.
The Precambrian eon represents nearly 90% of Earth's geological history.
Fossils from the Precambrian aeon are rare due to the simple life forms of that time.
Geologists believe the Earth's crust formed during the Precambrian aeon.
Many of the rocks studied in the Canadian Shield date back to the Precambrian aeon.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: Hadean eon - the eon preceding the Precambrian, when the Earth was forming
  • degree higher: Phanerozoic eon - the eon following the Precambrian, marked by abundant animal and plant life
