teepee

noun
cone-shaped Native American tent
9.4 5.2
Pronunciation: /ˈtiː.piː/ (AmE), /ˈtiː.piː/ (BrE)
A cone-shaped tent traditionally used by some North American Indigenous peoples, especially the Plains tribes, typically made of animal skins or fabric draped over wooden poles.

Examples

A teepee tent
A traditional teepee can be quickly assembled or dismantled, making it ideal for a nomadic lifestyle.
At the museum, children learned how Plains tribes built their teepees from buffalo hides.
They spent the night in a teepee beneath the stars during their camping trip.
A traditional tepee was made from animal hides stretched over wooden poles.
Each family constructed a tepee as their home during the summer months.
The tribe assembled their tepee in a wide circle on the open prairie.
A tipi can be quickly assembled and taken down for easy travel.
Museum visitors admired the decorated tipi on display.
The family spent the night in a traditional tipi by the river.

Related Terms

  • synonym: teepee - alternative spelling for a conical Native American tent
  • synonym: wigwam - a domed Native American dwelling, typically from eastern or southern tribes
