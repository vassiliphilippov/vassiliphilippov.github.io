moot court

noun
mock court for law students
Pronunciation: / muːt kɔrt / (AmE), / muːt kɔːt / (BrE)
a mock court in which law students practice arguing hypothetical cases as part of their legal education

Examples

Moot court session
Participating in moot court helped her develop strong legal argument skills.
Students prepared for weeks before presenting their case in the moot court.
The law school hosts a moot court competition every spring.
