sporogony

noun
parasite spore formation
Pronunciation: / spəˈrɑːɡəni / (AmE), / spəˈrɒɡəni / (BrE)
the stage in the life cycle of certain parasites, such as malaria, during which spores are produced, often inside a host organism

Examples

sporogony stage
The malaria parasite undergoes sporogony inside the mosquito before it can infect humans.
Researchers study sporogony to better understand how parasites reproduce and spread disease.
Interrupting sporogony in mosquitoes could help prevent the transmission of malaria.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sporulation - the process of spore formation in organisms
