flowline

noun
directional path line
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈfloʊˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈfləʊˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a line on a diagram or map that shows the direction and path of movement of a fluid or material

Examples

Map flowline
The diagram used a flowline to indicate how oil moves through the pipeline.
Engineers analyzed each flowline to optimize the water distribution system.
A flowline on the map shows the direction of the river's current.
The flowline on the map shows how the glacier has moved over time.
Engineers studied the flowline to understand the river's changing course.
Each flowline in the diagram represents the direction of water movement in the system.

Related Terms

  • synonym: streamline - a line representing the flow of a fluid at a given instant
  • synonym: trajectory - the path followed by a moving object
