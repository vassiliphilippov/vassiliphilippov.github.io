Having eyes filled with tears, usually because of emotion or irritation.

Examples

A teary-eyed look
After watching the emotional film, he became teary-eyed and tried to hide it from his friends.
She gave her friend a teary-eyed smile as they said goodbye at the airport.
The bride's father looked teary-eyed as he walked her down the aisle.

Synonyms

  • teary
    2.0 1.48/m
    teary is a short, informal adjective for eyes full of tears, while teary-eyed is the fuller phrase used in speech or writing.
  • tearful
    1.8 1.70/m
    tearful shows strong emotion or crying, while teary-eyed focuses on wet eyes and can also signal irritation.
  • watery-eyed
    4.1 0.02/m
    watery-eyed often suggests watery eyes from cold or irritation, while teary-eyed points to actual tears from strong feeling.
  • misty-eyed
    4.8 0.24/m
    misty-eyed often feels gentle or sentimental, while teary-eyed names wet eyes from tears, possibly from sadness or irritation.
