conform to

verb
act according to rules or standards
1.6
Pronunciation: / kənˈfɔːrm tu /; / kənˈfɔrm tu / (AmE), / kənˈfɔːm tuː /; / kənˈfɔːm tə /; / kənˈfɔːm tʊ / (BrE)
to act according to or meet rules, standards, or requirements

Examples

To conform to rules
All employees must conform to the company's code of conduct.
He always tries to conform to his parents' expectations.
New buildings have to conform to strict safety regulations.
All building materials must conform to strict safety standards before being approved.
The software does not conform to the company's privacy policies.
To get the license, your submission must conform to the prescribed guidelines.
All members must conform to the club's regulations.
Students are expected to conform to the school's dress code.
These buildings do not conform to modern safety standards.
