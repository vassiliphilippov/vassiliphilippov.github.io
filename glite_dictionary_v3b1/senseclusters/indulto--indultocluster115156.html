indulto

noun
church exemption
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdultoʊ / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʊltəʊ / (BrE)
a special permission or exemption granted by church authority, especially the Pope, from a church law or regulation

Examples

Papal indulto
The bishop requested an indulto from the Vatican to allow the old rite in the parish.
After years of petitioning, the community finally received an indulto to celebrate Mass in Latin.
Without an indulto, the practice would have remained against church regulations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dispensation - special permission to be exempt from a rule or law
  • synonym: permission - the act of allowing someone to do something
  • antonym: prohibition - the act of forbidding something by law or authority
