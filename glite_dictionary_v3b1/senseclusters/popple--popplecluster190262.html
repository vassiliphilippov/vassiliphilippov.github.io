popple

verb
to move with bubbling motion
9.7
Pronunciation: /ˈpɑːp.əl/ (AmE), /ˈpɒp.əl/ (BrE)
to move or bubble with a light, irregular motion, especially of water or liquid

Examples

Waves popple gently
After the wind picked up, the surface of the lake began to popple gently.
Rain caused the puddles to popple as drops fell steadily from the sky.
The brook would popple over the stones, making a soft, pleasant sound.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ripple - to move in small waves or undulations
  • antonym: still - to become calm or motionless
