aureus

noun
Roman gold coin
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɔːriəs / (AmE), / ˈɔːrɪəs / (BrE)
an ancient Roman gold coin used as currency during the Roman Empire

Examples

Roman aureus coin
The archaeologist discovered an aureus buried alongside other Roman artifacts.
Collectors value the aureus for its historical significance and gold content.
A single aureus could buy a slave in ancient Rome.
