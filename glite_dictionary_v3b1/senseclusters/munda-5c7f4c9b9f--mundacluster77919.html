Munda

noun
language family of central/eastern India
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmʊn.də / (AmE), / ˈmʌn.də / (BrE)
A family of languages spoken by various peoples in central and eastern India, forming a branch of the Austroasiatic language family.

Examples

Munda language family
Linguists have traced the origins of the Munda languages to the Austroasiatic family.
Several tribes in Jharkhand and Orissa speak Munda languages as their mother tongue.
The Munda language family includes languages such as Santali and Mundari.
