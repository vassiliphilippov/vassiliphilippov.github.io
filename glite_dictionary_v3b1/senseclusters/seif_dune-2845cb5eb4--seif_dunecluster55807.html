seif dune

noun
long, narrow sand dune with sharp crest
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪf duːn / (AmE), / ˈseɪf djuːn / (BrE)
A long, narrow sand dune with a sharp crest, usually oriented in the direction of the prevailing wind, commonly found in desert regions such as the Sahara.

Examples

A seif dune ridge
A seif dune can reach several hundred kilometers in length, making it a prominent feature of desert regions.
Scientists studied the formation of seif dunes to understand how wind shapes desert environments.
The Sahara Desert is home to many impressive seif dunes stretching for miles across the landscape.
