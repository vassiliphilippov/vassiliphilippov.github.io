perchance

adverb
(archaic) perhaps; by chance
Pronunciation: / pərˈtʃæns / (AmE), / pəˈtʃɑːns / (BrE)
(archaic or literary) perhaps; possibly; by chance

Examples

Perchance he will come
Perchance he will come to the feast, though I doubt it.
She read the letter aloud, hoping perchance to find a clue within its lines.
Wander through the city streets, perchance you'll discover something wonderful.
Do you think, perchance, we might see her at the ball tonight?
He wandered the gardens, perchance to find inspiration for his latest poem.
I shall wait here, perchance you change your mind.

Related Terms

  • antonym: certainly - definitely; surely
  • synonym: maybe - used to express possibility
  • synonym: perhaps - possibly; maybe
  • synonym: possibly - by chance; perhaps
