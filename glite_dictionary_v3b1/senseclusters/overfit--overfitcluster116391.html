overfit

verb
to fit model too closely
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈfɪt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈfɪt / (BrE)
to fit a statistical or machine-learning model too closely to its training data, so it does not work well on new or unseen data

Examples

To overfit a model
If you overfit your model, it may not perform well on new data.
The algorithm overfits when it memorizes the training data instead of learning general patterns.
Researchers try to avoid overfitting by using techniques like cross-validation.
If you overfit your model, it may not work well on new data.
The researchers realized they had overfitted the algorithm by using too many features.
To avoid overfitting, it is important to validate your model on separate data.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overtrain - to train a model so much that it performs poorly on new data
  • antonym: underfit - to fit a model too loosely, missing important patterns in the data
