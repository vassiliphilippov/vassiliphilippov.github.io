medieval

adjective
characteristic of Middle Ages chivalry and knighthood
6.5 1.17/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåmiÀêd…™Ààiv…ôl / (AmE), / Àåm…õd…™ÀàiÀêv…ôl / (BrE)
Relating to the qualities, customs, or atmosphere of chivalry, knighthood, or feudal society in the Middle Ages.

Examples

Medieval knight
He studied the medieval codes of chivalry that governed the behavior of knights.
The castle's medieval architecture features towers and battlements reminiscent of knighthood.
Their reenactment group wears medieval armor and participates in jousting tournaments.

Synonyms

  • chivalric
    9.9 0.62/m
    chivalric focuses on the knightly code of honor, manners, and heroic deeds, while medieval covers wider Middle Ages life and customs.
  • knightly
    8.0 0.04/m
    knightly describes actions, dress, or bearing of individual knights, while medieval refers to the overall social atmosphere of the Middle Ages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chivalric - relating to chivalry, knighthood, and medieval ideals
  • antonym: modern - relating to the current or recent times (opposite of medieval)
