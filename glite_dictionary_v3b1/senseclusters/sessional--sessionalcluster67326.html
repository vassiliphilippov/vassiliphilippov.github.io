sessional

noun
temporary academic staff
7.2
Pronunciation: /ˈseʃ.ən.əl/ (AmE), /ˈseʃ.ən.əl/ (BrE)
a person, especially a teacher or lecturer, employed temporarily for a single academic session or term

Examples

A sessional teacher
The university hired a sessional to cover the psychology course for one semester.
Many sessionals balance teaching with other professional commitments outside academia.
After working as a sessional, she hoped to secure a permanent teaching position.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adjunct - a part-time or temporary university teacher
  • antonym: permanent staff - a person employed on a continuing basis
