public property

noun
property owned by a government
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌpʌblɪk ˈprɑːpɚti / (AmE), / ˌpʌblɪk ˈprɒpəti / (BrE)
Property owned by a government rather than by private individuals or corporations.

Examples

Government public property
Citizens protested against the sale of public property to private developers.
The new park is public property and open to all residents.
Vandalism of public property is a crime punishable by law.

Related Terms

  • antonym: private property - property owned by individuals or corporations, not by the government
