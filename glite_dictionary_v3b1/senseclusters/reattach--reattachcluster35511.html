reattach

verb
to transfer authority
Pronunciation: / ˌriːəˈtætʃ / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈtætʃ / (BrE)
to return or transfer an organization, department, or office to the authority or administration of another body

Examples

To reattach a department
The university decided to reattach the research center to the main campus administration.
After several years of independence, the department was reattached to its original parent organization.
The government plans to reattach the agency to the ministry for better oversight.

Related Terms

  • antonym: detach - to remove from a position or authority
  • synonym: reassign - to assign again or to a different place or authority
