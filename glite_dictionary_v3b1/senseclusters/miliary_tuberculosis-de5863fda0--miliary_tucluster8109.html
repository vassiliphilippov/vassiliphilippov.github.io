miliary tuberculosis

noun
acute form of tuberculosis with widespread tiny tubercles
8.1
Pronunciation: /ˈmɪl.iː.ɛr.iː/ /tuː.bɜːr.kjə.ˈloʊ.sɪs/ /; / /ˈmɪl.iː.ɛr.iː/ /tjuː.bɜːr.kjə.ˈloʊ.sɪs/ (AmE), /ˈmɪl.ə.riː ˈtjuː.bɜː.kjʊ.ˈləʊ.sɪs/ /; / /ˈmɪl.ə.riː /tjuː.bə.kjʊ.ˈləʊ.sɪs/ (BrE)
A form of acute tuberculosis marked by the presence of numerous tiny tubercles across one or more organs, typically caused by the spread of tubercle bacilli through the bloodstream.

Examples

Miliary tuberculosis infection
A blood test confirmed the suspicion of miliary tuberculosis, prompting immediate medical intervention.
Due to its rapid spread, miliary tuberculosis can be particularly dangerous if not treated early.
The patient was diagnosed with miliary tuberculosis after imaging revealed numerous tiny lesions in the lungs.
