real property

noun
land and buildings; immovable property
5.9 3.2
Pronunciation: / ˈriːəl ˈprɑːpɚti / (AmE), / ˈrɪəl ˈprɒpəti / (BrE)
Land and anything permanently attached to it, such as buildings; immovable property as distinguished from personal property.

Examples

Real property ownership
He inherited a substantial amount of real property after his uncle passed away.
Taxes on real property vary depending on the location and municipal regulations.
The contract covered the sale of real property including the house and the surrounding land.
