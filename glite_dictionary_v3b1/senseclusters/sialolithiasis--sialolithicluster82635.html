sialolithiasis

noun
salivary gland stones
Pronunciation: / saɪˌæloʊlɪˈθaɪəsɪs / (AmE), / saɪˌaləʊlɪˈθaɪəsɪs / (BrE)
a medical condition in which stones form in the salivary glands, often causing pain or swelling

Examples

Sialolithiasis in glands
The dentist suspected sialolithiasis when the patient complained of swelling near the jaw.
Treatment for sialolithiasis may involve removing the stone from the affected gland.
Chronic mouth pain can sometimes be a sign of sialolithiasis in adults.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: nephrolithiasis - the formation of kidney stones
  • synonym: salivary gland stone - a hard deposit that forms in a salivary gland
