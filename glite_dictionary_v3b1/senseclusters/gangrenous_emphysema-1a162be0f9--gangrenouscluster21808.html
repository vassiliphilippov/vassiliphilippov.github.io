gangrenous emphysema

noun
deadly bacterial gangrene with gas production
9.3
Pronunciation: /ˈgæŋ.grə.nəs em.fə.ˈziː.mə/ /; / /ˈgæŋ.grɪ.nəs em.fɪ.ˈsiː.mə/ (AmE), /ˈgæŋ.grə.nəs em.fɪ.ˈsiː.mə/ /; / /ˈgæŋ.grə.nəs em.fə.ˈsiː.mə/ (BrE)
A severe and often fatal form of gangrene characterized by gas-producing infection, usually caused by Clostridium bacteria, leading to rapid tissue death.

Examples

Gangrenous emphysema infection
Doctors warned that without antibiotic treatment, gangrenous emphysema could lead to systemic toxicity and death.
Prompt surgical intervention is often required to treat gangrenous emphysema before it becomes fatal.
The patient was diagnosed with gangrenous emphysema after developing a rapidly spreading infection in his leg.
