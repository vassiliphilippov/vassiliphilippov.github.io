gangrenous emphysema

noun

A severe and often fatal form of gangrene characterized by gas-producing infection, usually caused by Clostridium bacteria, leading to rapid tissue death.

Examples

Gangrenous emphysema infection
Doctors warned that without antibiotic treatment, gangrenous emphysema could lead to systemic toxicity and death.
Prompt surgical intervention is often required to treat gangrenous emphysema before it becomes fatal.
The patient was diagnosed with gangrenous emphysema after developing a rapidly spreading infection in his leg.

Synonyms

  • emphysematous gangrene
    9.9 0.00/m
    emphysematous gangrene is the standard clinical phrase for gas-filled tissue decay, while gangrenous emphysema is a less common term.
  • gas gangrene
    7.7 0.02/m
    gas gangrene is the common name for Clostridium infections that make gas and toxins, while gangrenous emphysema is a formal term.
  • clostridial myonecrosis
    9.9 0.00/m
    clostridial myonecrosis attacks muscle with gas and toxins, while gangrenous emphysema names gas-forming gangrene in any tissue.
  • progressive emphysematous necrosis
    9.9 0.00/m
    progressive emphysematous necrosis describes rapidly advancing tissue death with gas, while gangrenous emphysema is a broader clinical name.
  • emphysematous phlegmon
    9.9 0.00/m
    emphysematous phlegmon is a spreading inflamed area that may form gas, while gangrenous emphysema implies full tissue death and gangrene.
  • gas phlegmon
    9.9 0.00/m
