unmaking

noun
reversal or undoing
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈmeɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ʌnˈmeɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of reversing, undoing, or destroying something that was previously made or established

Examples

The unmaking of law
The sudden unmaking of the law left many people uncertain about their rights.
Historians studied the unmaking of the ancient city after the invasion.
The unmaking of their friendship was slow but inevitable after the argument.

Related Terms

  • antonym: creation - the act of bringing something into existence
  • synonym: destruction - the act of causing something to end or be ruined
