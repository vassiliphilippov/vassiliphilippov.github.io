red alder

noun
tree with reddish wood used for furniture
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛd ˈɔldɚ / (AmE), / ˈrɛd ˈɔːldə / (BrE)
A large tree native to the Pacific coast of North America, known for its hard reddish wood, often used in furniture making.

Examples

A red alder tree
After the storm, several red alder branches had fallen onto the hiking trail.
The red alder is prized for its smooth, reddish wood in cabinetmaking.
Wildlife often finds shelter in stands of red alder along the riverbanks.
