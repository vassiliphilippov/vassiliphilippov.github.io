in a nutshell

adverb
briefly; in summary
7.2
Pronunciation: / ɪn ə ˈnʌtʃˌɛl / (AmE), / ɪn ə ˈnʌtʃɛl / (BrE)
used to introduce a summary or to say something in a very brief and clear way

Examples

In a nutshell summary
So, in a nutshell, we need more time and more resources.
The report, in a nutshell, is a call for urgent action.
To put it in a nutshell, the plan simply didn’t work as intended.
In a nutshell, the project was a complete success.
If you want my opinion in a nutshell, I think we should postpone it.
She described the whole situation in a nutshell at the meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: at length - in great detail; fully
  • synonym: briefly - using few words; short in time or length
  • synonym: in summary - used to state something briefly or to sum up
