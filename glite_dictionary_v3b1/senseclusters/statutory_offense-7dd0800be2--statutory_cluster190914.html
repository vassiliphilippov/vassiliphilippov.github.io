statutory offense

noun
crime defined by statute
3.8 7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈstætʃuˌtɔri əˈfɛns /; / stəˈtʃuː.tɔːr.i əˈfɛns / (AmE), / ˈstatjʊtəri əˈfɛns /; / stəˈtjuː.tər.i əˈfɛns / (BrE)
A crime defined and prohibited by a statute, rather than by common law.

Examples

A statutory offense
Tax evasion is considered a statutory offense under federal law.
The new law created several statutory offenses related to cybercrime.
Unlike theft, which is based in common law, this is a statutory offense.
A statutory offence may carry penalties set out in the legislation itself.
Driving without insurance is considered a statutory offence in many countries.
Unlike theft, which can be a common law crime, copyright infringement is a statutory offence.
