emulate

verb
to try to equal or surpass
Pronunciation: / ˈɛm.jə.leɪt /; / ˈɛm.jəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛm.jʊ.leɪt /; / ˈɛmjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to try to equal or be as good as someone or something, often by imitating them

Examples

To emulate a hero
He aspired to emulate the precision and grace of the masters in his piano performances.
Her company has tried to emulate the achievements of larger, more established firms.
Many young athletes emulate their sports heroes in hopes of reaching similar success.
Many companies try to emulate the innovative culture of leading tech firms.
She hoped to emulate her mentor's success in the scientific community.
Young artists often emulate the styles of their predecessors when developing their own techniques.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: excel - to be exceptionally good at or surpass others
  • synonym: imitate - to copy the behavior or appearance of someone or something
  • degree higher: surpass - to do or be better than
