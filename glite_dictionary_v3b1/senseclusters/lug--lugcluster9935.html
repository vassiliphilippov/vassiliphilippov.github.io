lug

noun
four-cornered sail set obliquely to the mast
6.5 9.9
Pronunciation: / lʌɡ / (AmE), / lʌɡ / (BrE)
A sail with four corners that is hoisted from a yard set obliquely to the mast, commonly used in small boats.

Examples

Lug sail
He repaired the torn lug before setting out on his journey.
The fisherman raised the lug as the wind began to pick up.
Traditional Scottish boats often used a lug for sailing in coastal waters.
