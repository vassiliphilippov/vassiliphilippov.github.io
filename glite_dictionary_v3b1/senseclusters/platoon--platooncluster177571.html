platoon

verb
to alternate players
7.0
Pronunciation: /plə.ˈtuːn/ (AmE), /plə.ˈtuːn/ (BrE)
to use two or more players alternately in the same position, especially to gain an advantage based on matchups in sports

Examples

To platoon players
The coach decided to platoon the two catchers to maximize their strengths against different pitchers.
Many baseball teams platoon players at certain positions to take advantage of favorable matchups.
By platooning his running backs, the manager kept both players fresh throughout the game.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rotate - to change players or positions in a regular sequence
  • synonym: substitute - to replace one player with another during a game
