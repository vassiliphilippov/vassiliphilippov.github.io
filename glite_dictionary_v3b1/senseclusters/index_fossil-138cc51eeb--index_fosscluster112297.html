index fossil

noun
fossil used to date rock layers
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈɪndɛks ˈfɑsəl / (AmE), / ˈɪndɛks ˈfɒsəl / (BrE)
a fossil of an organism that lived during a specific geological period, used to identify and date the rock layer in which it is found

Examples

Important index fossils
Because ammonites have a wide geographic range, they serve as important index fossils for the Mesozoic Era.
The discovery of an index fossil helped the scientists quickly determine the age of the sedimentary rocks.
Trilobites are a classic example of an index fossil used by geologists to date Paleozoic rock layers.

Related Terms
