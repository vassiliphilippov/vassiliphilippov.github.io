inwardness

noun
concern with inner self or values
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnwɚdnəs /; / ˈɪnwərdnəs / (AmE), / ˈɪnwədnɪs /; / ˈɪnwədnəs / (BrE)
a focus on one's own inner thoughts, feelings, or values; introspection or self-reflection

Examples

Personal inwardness
Her poetry expresses a deep sense of inwardness and spiritual searching.
Meditation can promote inwardness, helping people to reflect on their true values.
The philosopher's inwardness led him to question the very nature of existence.
After years of solitude, his inwardness became the defining aspect of his personality.
Many poets explore inwardness as a way to understand their deepest motivations.
The philosopher was known for her intense inwardness and constant self-reflection.

Related Terms

  • antonym: extroversion - the state of being interested in things outside oneself
  • synonym: introspection - the examination of one's own thoughts and feelings
  • antonym: outwardness - concern with external matters rather than inner nature
