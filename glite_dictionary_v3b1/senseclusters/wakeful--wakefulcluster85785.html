wakeful

adjective
alert and not asleep
Pronunciation: / ˈweɪkfəl / (AmE), / ˈweɪkfəl /; / ˈweɪkfʊl / (BrE)
not asleep or sleeping lightly so that you wake up easily; fully conscious and easily awakened

Examples

Wakeful night
After drinking coffee, I felt much more wakeful and ready to study.
He remained wakeful during the long, boring lecture.
The baby was wakeful all night, making it hard for anyone to sleep.
Children often have wakeful sleep when they are anxious or excited.
He spent a wakeful night, tossing and turning in bed.
My wakeful hours increased as the thunderstorm raged outside.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alert - quick to notice and respond; fully awake
  • antonym: asleep - in a state of sleep; not awake
  • antonym: deep - (of sleep) difficult to rouse from; not easily disturbed
  • synonym: restless - unable to rest or relax; constantly moving
