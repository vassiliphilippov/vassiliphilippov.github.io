puddle

noun
waterproof clay-sand mixture for pond lining
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpʌd.əl / (AmE), / ˈpʌd.əl / (BrE)
a mixture of wet clay and sand used to line a pond, which becomes waterproof when dry

Examples

Puddle clay lining
A mixture of clay, sand, and water, known as puddle, was spread across the pond bed.
Before modern liners, builders often relied on puddle to waterproof garden ponds.
The old canal was sealed with a thick layer of puddle to prevent water leakage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: puddle clay - clay used to create a watertight barrier, especially in lining ponds or canals
