natural fiber

noun
fiber from plants or animals
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈnætʃɚəl ˈfaɪbɚ /; / ˈnætʃrəl ˈfaɪbɚ / (AmE), / ˈnætʃᵊrəl ˈfaɪbə /; / ˈnætʃrəl ˈfaɪbə / (BrE)
a fiber obtained from plants or animals, used especially in textiles and manufacturing, as opposed to one produced synthetically.

Examples

A natural fiber fabric
Bamboo is an example of a natural fiber that is both strong and sustainable.
Consumers are increasingly interested in products made from natural fiber due to environmental concerns.
Many clothing manufacturers now use natural fiber fabrics like cotton and wool.
Cotton is a common natural fibre used in clothing around the world.
The company promotes the use of natural fibre products for environmental sustainability.
Wool is a natural fibre that comes from sheep.
