ixodid

noun
a hard-bodied tick of the family Ixodidae
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈaɪ.kə.dɪd/ /; / /ˈɪk.sə.dɪd/ (AmE), /ˈaɪ.kə.dɪd/ /; / /ˈɪk.sə.dɪd/ (BrE)
A tick of the family Ixodidae, characterized by a hard shield on the back and mouthparts that project from the head.

Examples

A ixodid tick
Researchers collected several ixodids from the field to study tick-borne pathogens.
The bite of an ixodid can transmit several diseases to both humans and animals.
Unlike soft ticks, the ixodid has a tough protective shield on its back.
