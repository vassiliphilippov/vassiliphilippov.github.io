Liberty Party

proper noun
former U.S. anti-slavery political party (1839–1848)
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪbɚti ˈpɑːrti / (AmE), / ˈlɪbəti ˈpɑːti / (BrE)
a U.S. political party formed in 1839 to oppose slavery, which later merged with the Free Soil Party in 1848

Examples

The Liberty Party formed
Many leaders of the Liberty Party later joined forces with the Free Soil Party for greater influence.
The Liberty Party only lasted about a decade, but its anti-slavery stance influenced later political movements.
The Liberty Party was one of the first political groups in America to make abolition its main platform.
