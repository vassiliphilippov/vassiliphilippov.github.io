formalist

noun
structure-focused person
7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈfɔːr.mə.lɪst/ (AmE), /ˈfɔː.mə.lɪst/ (BrE)
a person who gives special importance to structure, rules, or form, especially in art, literature, or law

Examples

As a formalist, she focused on the painting's composition rather than its subject.
The critic, a well-known formalist, praised the novel's structure over its emotional content.
Many architects consider themselves formalists because they prioritize design principles and order.

Some writers reject the formalist approach in favor of more experimental styles.

The artist was known as a formalist who valued technique over emotional expression.
Critics argued that being a formalist can sometimes ignore the real-world impact of decisions.
