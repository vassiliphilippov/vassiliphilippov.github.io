roust

verb
to stir to action
8.4
Pronunciation: /raʊst/ (AmE), /raʊst/ (BrE)
to force someone to get up, leave, or become active, often suddenly or forcefully

Examples

To roust the team
The coach managed to roust the team from their lethargy before the final match.
A loud knock on the door rousted him from his deep sleep early in the morning.
The community leader tried to roust support for the new environmental initiative.
The police had to roust the group of teenagers from the abandoned building.
She was rousted out of bed early by a loud knock at the door.
Security guards roust people who try to sleep in the train station overnight.
The police tried to roust the campers from the park early in the morning.
She had to roust her brother out of bed so he would not miss the bus.
Security guards rousted the crowd from the building after closing time.
