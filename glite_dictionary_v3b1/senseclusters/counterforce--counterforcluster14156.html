counterforce

noun
opposing force
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntərˌfɔrs / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌfɔːs / (BrE)
a force that acts in opposition to another force

Examples

Apply a counterforce
The engineer applied a counterforce to keep the structure stable.
A strong counterforce was needed to balance the weight of the bridge.
In martial arts, using a counterforce can help redirect an opponent's attack.

Related Terms

  • antonym: force - a push or pull that can cause an object to move or change
  • synonym: resistance - a force that opposes motion or another force
