bestir oneself

verb
become active; begin to take action
9.9
Pronunciation: / bɪˈstɜr wʌnˈsɛlf / (AmE), / bɪˈstɜː wʌnˈself / (BrE)
to make an effort to become active or start doing something, especially after a period of inactivity

Examples

To bestir oneself quickly
If we do not bestir ourselves soon, we will miss our opportunity.
It was only after much persuasion that she bestirred herself to help organize the event.
Realizing he was late, he finally bestirred himself and got ready for work.

Related Terms

  • antonym: idle - to be inactive; to do nothing
  • synonym: stir oneself - to make a move or effort, to become active
