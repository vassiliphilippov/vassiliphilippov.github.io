outwale

noun
boat edge reinforcement
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtˌweɪl / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌweɪl / (BrE)
a strip of wood attached along the top edge of a boat's side to provide strength and protection

Examples

The outwale of a boat
The old canoe needed a new outwale after years of use on the river.
He carefully sanded the outwale before applying a fresh coat of varnish.
Replacing the damaged outwale improved the boat's strength and appearance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gunwale - the upper edge of the side of a boat
