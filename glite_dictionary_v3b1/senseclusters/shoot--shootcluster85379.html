shoot

verb
move suddenly and quickly
5.9 1.4
Pronunciation: /ʃuːt/ (AmE), /ʃuːt/ (BrE)
to move, send, or happen suddenly and quickly; to make something move or happen quickly

Examples

To shoot forward
As soon as the bell rang, the children shot out of the classroom.
She shot up the stairs to answer the phone.
The cat shot across the road as the car approached.
A sharp pain shot up his leg as he twisted his ankle.
A stabbing pain began to shoot through his arm whenever he moved it.
Sudden aches would shoot down her spine after long hours of sitting.
A rabbit shot across the field, startling the children.
She shot out of the classroom when the bell rang.
The train shot past the station without stopping.
