logically

adverb
in a logical manner
2.4
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑːdʒɪkli / (AmE), / ˈlɒdʒɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that is based on clear, sound reasoning or logic

Examples

Think logically
If you think logically, the answer becomes clear.
She logically explained her decision to change jobs.
The plan was carefully and logically constructed to solve the problem.
Logically, the evidence points to only one possible conclusion.
Logically, we should leave early to avoid the rush hour traffic.
If you think logically about the problem, the solution becomes clearer.

Related Terms

  • antonym: illogically - in an illogical manner; lacking logic or clear reasoning
  • synonym: rationally - in a rational manner; with reason
