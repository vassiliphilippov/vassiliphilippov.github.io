demand-pull inflation

noun
inflation driven by rising demand or money supply
3.7 6.1
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈmænd pʊl ɪn.ˈfleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /də.ˈmænd pʊl ɪn.ˈfleɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈmɑːnd pʊl ɪn.ˈfleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /dɪ.ˈmɑːnd pəl ɪn.ˈfleɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
A rise in the general price level caused by increased demand for goods and services or by an increase in the supply of money.

Examples

Demand-pull inflation rise
Experts distinguish demand-pull inflation from inflation resulting from increased production costs.
Many economists believe that demand-pull inflation occurs when consumer spending increases significantly.
The government tried to control demand-pull inflation by tightening monetary policy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cost-push inflation - inflation caused by increases in the cost of production
