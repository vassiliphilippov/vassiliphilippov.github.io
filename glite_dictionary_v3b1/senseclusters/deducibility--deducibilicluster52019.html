deducibility

noun
ability to be derived
Pronunciation: / dɪˌduːsəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / dɪˌdjuːsəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of something being able to be logically deduced or inferred from facts, evidence, or reasoning

Examples

The deducibility of facts
The deducibility of this conclusion depends on the validity of the initial assumptions.
In logic, deducibility is essential for establishing the truth of mathematical statements.
Researchers debated the deducibility of the hypothesis from the available data.
The deducibility of the theory was questioned by several scientists at the conference.
Mathematical proofs rely on the deducibility of statements from established axioms.
He doubted the deducibility of her conclusions without more supporting evidence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: derivability - the quality of being able to be derived from something
  • antonym: independence - the quality of not being derivable from given facts or axioms
  • antonym: inducibility - the quality of being able to be induced or brought about
  • synonym: inferability - the quality of being able to be inferred
  • synonym: provability - the quality of being able to be proven
