bank holding company

noun
company owning one or more banks
Pronunciation: / bæŋk ˈhoʊldɪŋ ˌkʌmpəni / (AmE), / bæŋk ˈhəʊldɪŋ ˌkʌmpəni / (BrE)
a company that owns or controls one or more banks, typically for investment or management purposes

Examples

A bank holding company
Acquiring several regional banks made the corporation a major bank holding company.
J.P. Morgan Chase is a well-known bank holding company with global influence.
The Federal Reserve regulates any bank holding company with significant U.S. banking operations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: holding company - a company that owns enough voting stock in other companies to control their policies and management
