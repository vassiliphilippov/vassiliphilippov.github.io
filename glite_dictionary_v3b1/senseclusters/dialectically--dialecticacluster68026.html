dialectically

adverb
in a dialectic/logical-argument manner
Pronunciation: / daɪəˈlɛktɪkli / (AmE), / daɪəˈlɛktɪkli / (BrE)
In a manner relating to or using logical argumentation or the dialectic method of reasoning, especially through dialogue involving opposing perspectives.

Examples

To think dialectically
He approached the contradiction dialectically, seeking to reconcile opposing viewpoints.
Social change and economic structures are often dialectically related in historical analysis.
The philosopher argued dialectically to examine both sides of the issue.

  • synonym: logically - in a logical manner; using reason
