antiphospholipid antibody

noun
autoantibody linked to blood clotting disorders
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˌæntiˌfoʊsfoʊˈlɪpɪd ˈæntɪˌbɑːdi / (AmE), / ˌæntɪˌfɒsfəʊˈlɪpɪd ˈæntɪˌbɒdi / (BrE)
an autoantibody found in the blood that targets phospholipids and can increase the risk of abnormal blood clotting

Examples

antiphospholipid antibody syndrome
He was diagnosed with antiphospholipid antibody syndrome, which required careful management of his condition.
Presence of an antiphospholipid antibody is associated with certain autoimmune disorders.
The doctor ordered tests for antiphospholipid antibody after her unexplained blood clots.
