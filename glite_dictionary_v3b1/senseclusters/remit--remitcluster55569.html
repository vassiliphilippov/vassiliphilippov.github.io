remit

verb
cancel or forgive (debts, taxes, etc.)
3.1 6.0
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈmɪt/ /; / /rɪ.ˈmɪt/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈmɪt/ /; / /riː.ˈmɪt/ (BrE)
to cancel, forgive, or refrain from demanding payment of a debt, punishment, or obligation

Examples

To remit a debt
Her penalty fees were remitted after she provided evidence of financial difficulty.
The bank decided to remit the outstanding loan charges due to the customer's hardship.
The charity appealed to the government to remit taxes for disaster victims.
After reviewing the case, the judge decided to remit the fine.
The church promised that God would remit their sins if they truly repented.
The king agreed to remit the prisoner's sentence after hearing his plea.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cancel - to nullify or make void (a debt or obligation)
  • synonym: forgive - to stop requiring repayment of a debt or sin
