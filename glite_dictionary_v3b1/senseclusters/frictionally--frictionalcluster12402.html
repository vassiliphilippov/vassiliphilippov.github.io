frictionally

adverb
with tension or conflict
Pronunciation: / ˈfrɪkʃənəli / (AmE), / ˈfrɪkʃənəli / (BrE)
in a way that causes or involves disagreement, tension, or conflict between people or groups

Examples

Frictionally working together
The departments worked frictionally at first, but eventually found common ground.
Negotiations proceeded frictionally, with both sides unwilling to compromise.
The merger was handled frictionally, leading to ongoing disputes among staff.

Related Terms

  • antonym: amicably - in a friendly and peaceful manner
  • synonym: contentiously - in a way that causes or is likely to cause an argument
