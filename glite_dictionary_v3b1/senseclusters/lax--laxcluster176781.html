lax

adjective
pronounced with relaxed tongue/jaw (phonetics)
8.3
Pronunciation: / læks / (AmE), / læks / (BrE)
(of a vowel) pronounced with muscles of the tongue and jaw relatively relaxed, as in the vowel sound in 'bet'.

Examples

Lax vowel sound
In English, the vowel in "sit" is a lax vowel sound.
Phonologists often compare lax vowels to tense vowels in their analyses.
The distinction between tense and lax vowels is common in Germanic languages.

Related Terms

  • antonym: tense - (of a vowel) pronounced with greater muscular effort; the opposite of lax in phonetics
