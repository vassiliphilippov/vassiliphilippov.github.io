homophony

noun
musical texture with one dominant voice
5.1 9.6
Pronunciation: / həˈmɑːfəni / (AmE), / həˈmɒfəni / (BrE)
Music in which one melody is dominant and all accompanying parts move together harmonically (in a homophonic style).

Examples

Choral homophony
Baroque composers often balanced homophony and polyphony within their choral works.
Many popular songs favor homophony over complex counterpoint.
The choir performed a piece rich in homophony, allowing the soprano line to shine.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monophony - musical texture with a single melodic line without harmony
  • antonym: polyphony - musical texture with two or more independent melodies
