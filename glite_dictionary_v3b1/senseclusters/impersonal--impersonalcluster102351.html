impersonal

adjective
anonymized; not identifying individuals
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpɜrsənəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpɜːsənəl / (BrE)
(of information or data) not identifying or connected to a particular person; anonymized or de-identified.

Examples

Impersonal data
The dataset is impersonal, containing no names, addresses, or direct identifiers of the participants.
To comply with privacy rules, the researchers provided impersonal summaries instead of case reports with patient details.
Web forms should collect only impersonal information, like product preferences, not users' personal contact details.
