revulse

verb
to cause disgust
9.3
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvʌls / (AmE), / rɪˈvʌls / (BrE)
to cause someone to feel a sudden and strong sense of shock, disgust, or horror

Examples

To revulse the audience
The graphic images on the news revulsed many viewers, prompting complaints to the station.
She was revulsed by the cruelty shown towards the animals in the documentary.
The idea of cheating on the exam revulses him, as he values honesty above all.
The cruelty of the experiment revulsed the entire audience.
Many people are revulsed by the idea of animal testing.
The news report revulsed viewers with its graphic details.

Related Terms

  • antonym: attract - to draw someone in or cause interest
  • antonym: delight - to give great pleasure or joy
  • synonym: disgust - to cause a strong feeling of dislike or disapproval
  • synonym: horrify - to shock or frighten someone greatly
  • synonym: repel - to drive away or cause aversion
