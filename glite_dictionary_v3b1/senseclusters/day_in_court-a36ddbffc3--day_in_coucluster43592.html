day in court

noun
opportunity to present a case in court
Pronunciation: / deɪ ɪn kɔrt / (AmE), / deɪ ɪn kɔːt / (BrE)
an occasion during a legal proceeding or trial when someone is given the opportunity to present their case or be heard

Examples

Have a day in court
After years of waiting, he finally had his day in court to prove his innocence.
Many people seek justice but never get their day in court.
The company will have its day in court next month to defend against the charges.
