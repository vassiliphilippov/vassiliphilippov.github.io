interpret away

verb
dismiss or explain away criticism/problems
5.9 6.4
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈtɜːr.prət ə.ˈweɪ/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈtɜː.prɪt ə.ˈweɪ/ (BrE)
to dismiss or explain away criticism, problems, or objections, often by reinterpreting them in a less negative or more favorable way

Examples

To interpret away criticism
He tried to interpret away the harsh comments by saying they were just jokes.
Politicians often interpret away criticism by blaming the media or opposition.
She managed to interpret away her mistakes as learning experiences during the interview.
