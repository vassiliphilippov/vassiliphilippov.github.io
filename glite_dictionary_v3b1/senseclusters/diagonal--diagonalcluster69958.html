a straight line that joins two non-adjacent corners of a shape, or runs across something at a slant (not parallel or perpendicular to the sides), such as in geometry, mathematics, games, or fabric

Examples

A diagonal line
Checkers are often captured by jumping over opponents on the diagonal.
She counted the number of red squares on the diagonal of the board.
The bishop can only move along a diagonal during the game.
In a square, the diagonal divides it into two congruent triangles.
The diagonal of the rectangle measures five centimeters.
To solve the problem, draw a diagonal connecting opposite corners of the hexagon.
In some matrices, only the diagonal contains nonzero values; the rest are zero.
The diagonal of the matrix contains all the elements where the row and column indices are the same.
When calculating the trace of a matrix, you sum the entries on its diagonal.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: main diagonal - the diagonal in a matrix from the upper left to the lower right
  • synonym: oblique - slanting or not parallel or perpendicular
  • degree lower: secondary diagonal - the diagonal in a matrix from the upper right to the lower left
  • antonym: side - a straight edge of a polygon
  • antonym: straight - line without angle, not oblique
