aortic arch

noun
curved section of the aorta
Pronunciation: / eɪˌɔrtɪk ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / eɪˌɔːtɪk ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
the curved section of the aorta between the ascending and descending parts, through which blood is distributed from the heart to the body

Examples

The aortic arch curve
An aneurysm in the aortic arch can be a serious medical condition requiring surgery.
The aortic arch gives rise to major arteries that supply blood to the head, neck, and arms.
The surgeon closely examined the patient's aortic arch for any abnormalities during the procedure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aorta - the main artery carrying blood from the heart to the rest of the body
