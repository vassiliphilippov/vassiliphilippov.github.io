drawdown

noun
reduction in water level
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈdrɔˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈdrɔːdaʊn / (BrE)
a reduction in the level of water in a well, reservoir, or aquifer due to pumping or withdrawal

Examples

Reservoir drawdown
The drawdown of the reservoir was necessary to repair the dam's foundation.
Farmers were concerned about the drawdown in the aquifer after a long period of irrigation.
Engineers monitored the drawdown to ensure the well would not run dry.

Related Terms

  • synonym: depletion - the reduction in the quantity of something
  • antonym: recharge - the process of refilling a well or aquifer with water
