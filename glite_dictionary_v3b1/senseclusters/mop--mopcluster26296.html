mop

verb
make a sad or sulky face
9.9 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / mÉ‘p / (AmE), / mÉ’p / (BrE)
(archaic or literary) To make a sad or sulky face, often by thrusting out one's lower lip.

Examples

To mop sadly
He likes to mop and act aggrieved to get sympathy from his friends.
She would mop in disappointment whenever she was denied a treat.
When scolded, the boy began to mop and whimper softly in the corner.

Synonyms

  • mow
    9.9 0.05/m
    mow is an old word for pulling a pouting lower lip to show displeasure, while mop is the literary, archaic term.
  • pout
    3.4 1.50/m
    pout is a common modern verb for pushing out the lips to show annoyance or hurt, while mop is archaic and literary.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pout - push one's lips or lower lip forward to show annoyance or sadness
