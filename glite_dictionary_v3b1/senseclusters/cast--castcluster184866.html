cast

verb
throw something forcefully
Pronunciation: / kæst / (AmE), / kɑːst / (BrE)
to throw or send something forcefully, or to cause something (like light, a shadow, or a spell) to appear or spread

Examples

To cast a shadow
He cast the letter onto the fire without a second thought.
She cast the stone over the wall with surprising strength.
The fisherman cast his line far into the lake.
His criticism was cast as helpful advice rather than an attack.
She cast her reply in very formal terms.
The introduction was cast in simple, accessible language.
She cast a warm glow with her smile, brightening the room.
The magician cast a spell that filled the hall with shimmering light.
The tree cast a long shadow across the lawn at sunset.
