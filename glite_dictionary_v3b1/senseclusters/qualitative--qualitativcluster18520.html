qualitative

adjective
relating to qualities, not quantities
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɑː.ləˌteɪ.tɪv /; / ˈkwɑː.ləˌteɪ.t̬ɪv / (AmE), / ˈkwɒl.ɪ.tə.tɪv / (BrE)
relating to the quality or qualities of something, not the amount or quantity

Examples

Qualitative research methods
The researchers used qualitative methods to study the participants' experiences.
There was a significant qualitative difference between the two products.
This report focuses on qualitative analysis rather than numerical data.
Researchers used qualitative methods to understand human behavior in depth.
The study provided a qualitative analysis of customer feedback.
There was a qualitative change in the material's properties after heating.

Related Terms

  • antonym: quantitative - relating to measurement by quantity rather than quality
