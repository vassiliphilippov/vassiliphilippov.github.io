nerve fiber

noun
threadlike extension of a nerve cell
6.1 2.9
Pronunciation: /nɜːrv ˈfaɪ.bər/ (AmE), /nɜːv ˈfaɪ.bə/ (BrE)
A long, slender extension of a nerve cell (neuron) that transmits or conducts electrical impulses; typically an axon (which conducts impulses away from the cell body) or a dendrite.

Examples

A nerve fiber transmits signals
Damage to a single nerve fiber can affect sensation in part of the body.
Myelin sheaths insulate each nerve fiber to speed up nerve signal transmission.
The optic nerve is composed of many nerve fibers bundled together.
A single nerve fibre carries signals from your finger to your brain.
Scientists used microscopes to observe the structure of each nerve fibre.
When the nerve fibre is damaged, muscle weakness may result.

Related Terms

  • synonym: axon - long projection of a neuron that conducts impulses away from the cell body
  • synonym: dendrite - branched extension of a neuron that receives impulses
