panmixia

noun
random mating
9.9
Pronunciation: /pæn.ˈmɪk.siː.ə/ /; / /pæn.ˈmɪk.sɪə/ (AmE), /pæn.ˈmɪk.sɪə/ /; / /pæn.ˈmɪk.siː.ə/ (BrE)
the condition in which all individuals in a population are equally likely to mate with each other, without restrictions or preferences

Examples

Population panmixia
In a population exhibiting panmixia, genetic diversity tends to remain high over generations.
The concept of panmixia is important in evolutionary biology and population genetics.
If panmixia is disrupted, certain traits may become more common due to selective mating.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inbreeding - mating between closely related individuals
  • synonym: outbreeding - mating between unrelated individuals
