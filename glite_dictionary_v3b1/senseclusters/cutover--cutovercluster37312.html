cutover

noun
system switch process
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌtˌoʊvər / (AmE), / ˈkʌtˌəʊvə / (BrE)
the process of switching from an old system or method to a new one, especially in technology or business

Examples

Cutover to new system
The cutover to the new payment system will take place over the weekend.
Careful planning is required to ensure a smooth cutover during the software upgrade.
After the cutover, employees will need to use the updated procedures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: migration - the act of moving data or systems from one environment to another
  • antonym: rollback - the process of returning to a previous system or state
  • synonym: transition - the process of changing from one state or condition to another
