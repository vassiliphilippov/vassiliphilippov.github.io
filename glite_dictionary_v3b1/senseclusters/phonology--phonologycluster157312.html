phonology

noun
the study of the sound systems of languages
5.5
Pronunciation: / fəˈnɑlədʒi / (AmE), / fəˈnɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of linguistics concerned with the systematic organization of sounds in languages, including the analysis and classification of phonemes.

Examples

Study of phonology
She specialized in phonology during her graduate studies in linguistics.
The phonology of English differs greatly from that of Mandarin.
Understanding phonology helps teachers instruct students in accurate pronunciation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: phonetics - the study of the physical sounds of human speech
