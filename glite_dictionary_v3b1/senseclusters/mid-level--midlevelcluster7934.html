mid-level

adjective
intermediate rank
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪdˌlɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈmɪdˌlɛvəl / (BrE)
having a position, skill, or status that is intermediate between high and low within an organization or system

Examples

Mid-level manager
She accepted a mid-level position at the technology company after several years as an entry-level employee.
Many mid-level managers are responsible for overseeing teams and reporting to senior staff.
The training program is designed for mid-level professionals looking to advance their careers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intermediate - being between two levels, stages, or degrees
  • degree lower: junior - having a lower rank or position
  • degree higher: senior - having a higher rank or position
