discursive

adjective
moving from topic to topic; rambling
8.3 0.69/m
Pronunciation: / d…™sÀàk…úrs…™v / (AmE), / d…™sÀàk…úÀês…™v / (BrE)
(of speech or writing) moving from topic to topic without strict order or coherence; rambling or digressive.

Examples

Discursive speech
He tended to give discursive answers that made it hard to follow his argument.
Her discursive essay covered everything from art history to quantum mechanics.
The professor's discursive lectures often wandered far from the main topic.

Synonyms

  • digressive
    8.1 0.08/m
    digressive is speech that briefly leaves the main topic, while discursive moves broadly from topic to topic without strict order.
  • rambling
    6.1 0.50/m
    rambling is long, confused speaking that wanders, while discursive shifts between topics without clear order or focus.
  • excursive
    9.7 0.00/m
    excursive describes speech that strays over many subjects, while discursive moves from topic to topic without tight structure.
  • long-winded
    5.8 0.91/m
    long-winded is speech that uses too many words and feels tedious, while discursive focuses on shifting topics rather than just length.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concise - giving a lot of information clearly and in a few words; opposite of discursive
  • synonym: rambling - (of speech or writing) lengthy and confused or inconsequential; similar meaning to discursive
