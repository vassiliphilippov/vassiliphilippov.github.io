discursive

adjective
moving from topic to topic; rambling
6.4 6.6
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈskɜːr.sɪv/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈskɜː.sɪv/ (BrE)
(of speech or writing) moving from topic to topic without strict order or coherence; rambling or digressive.

Examples

Discursive speech
He tended to give discursive answers that made it hard to follow his argument.
Her discursive essay covered everything from art history to quantum mechanics.
The professor's discursive lectures often wandered far from the main topic.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concise - giving a lot of information clearly and in a few words; opposite of discursive
  • synonym: rambling - (of speech or writing) lengthy and confused or inconsequential; similar meaning to discursive
