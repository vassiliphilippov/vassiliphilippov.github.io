agent

noun
instigator of action in a sentence (linguistics)
3.3 5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
(Linguistics) The semantic role of the animate entity that instigates or causes the action expressed by a verb in a clause.

Examples

Agent in a sentence
In the sentence 'The cat chased the mouse,' 'the cat' functions as the agent.
Linguists identify the agent as the performer of the action in a verb phrase.
Sometimes, the agent is omitted in passive constructions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: patient - the entity that undergoes an action in linguistics
