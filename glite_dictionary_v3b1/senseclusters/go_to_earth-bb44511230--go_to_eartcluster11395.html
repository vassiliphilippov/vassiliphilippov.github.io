go to earth

verb
to hide or withdraw from view
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ tə ɜrθ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ tə ɜːθ / (BrE)
to hide or retreat from public notice, often to avoid trouble or attention

Examples

To go to earth quickly
After the scandal broke, the mayor went to earth and refused all interviews.
He often goes to earth whenever he needs to escape the pressures of fame.
When the story became public, she went to earth to avoid reporters.

Related Terms

  • antonym: come out of hiding - to stop hiding and become visible again
  • synonym: lie low - to keep out of sight to avoid trouble
