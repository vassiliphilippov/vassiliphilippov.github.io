topwater

noun
surface water layer
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑpˌwɔtər / (AmE), / ˈtɒpˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
the upper surface layer of a body of water, especially where fish are active or visible

Examples

Fish near the topwater
Anglers often use special lures to attract fish swimming near the topwater at sunrise.
The sunlight reflected off the calm topwater, making it easy to spot the fish below.
Birds skimmed across the topwater searching for insects early in the morning.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deepwater - the deeper part of a body of water, far from the surface
  • synonym: surface - the outside or uppermost layer of something
