go

verb
operate or function (machines)
2.2
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ / (BrE)
to start, operate, or function; to begin or be in action (especially of machines, events, or people)

Examples

Go off suddenly
Does this clock still go after so many years?
My phone won't go if the battery is completely dead.
The elevator didn't go until the repairman fixed it.
Let me know when you're ready to go and we can begin the game.
On your mark, get set, go!
When the race official shouts, 'go!', the runners dash forward.

Related Terms

  • antonym: break down - to stop functioning, especially of machines
  • synonym: run - to function or operate, often referring to engines or devices
  • synonym: start - to begin doing something or to cause something to begin
  • antonym: stop - to cease movement or operation; to come to an end
  • synonym: work - to be in operation or functioning, especially of machines
