misfire

verb
(of a gun) to fail to fire
4.7 5.1
Pronunciation: /mɪs.ˈfaɪr/ (AmE), /mɪs.ˈfaɪə/ (BrE)
(of a gun, engine, or explosive) to fail to discharge or work as intended

Examples

The engine misfires
If your engine misfires frequently, you should take your car to a mechanic.
She pulled the trigger, but the rifle misfired and nothing happened.
The old pistol misfired during the demonstration, startling the crowd.
The car engine started to misfire after driving through the heavy rain.
His attempt at humor completely misfired and left the audience silent.
If the plan misfires, we will need to come up with a new strategy.

  • synonym: malfunction - to fail to function properly
  • antonym: succeed - to achieve the desired result
