convertible security

noun
security exchangeable for another, usually stock
8.3 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàv…ùÀê.t…ô.b…ôl s…ôÀàkj är.…ô.ti / (AmE), / k…ônÀàv…úÀê.t…ô.b…ôl s…™Ààkj ä…ô.r…™.ti / (BrE)
a corporate financial instrument, such as a bond or preferred stock, that can be exchanged for another security, typically common stock, under specified conditions

Examples

A convertible security option
Holding a convertible security gives the option to convert debt into equity under favorable market conditions.
Investors are attracted to a convertible security for its potential upside if the company's stock performs well.
The company issued a convertible security that could be exchanged for common shares within five years.

Synonyms

  • convertible
    7.7 0.41/m
    convertible is a short name for a security that can change into stock, while convertible security is the full formal term used in finance.
  • common stock equivalent
    6.7 0.00/m
    common stock equivalent is a type of security treated like stock when converted, while convertible security names a specific convertible instrument used by companies.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: common stock - a security that represents ownership in a corporation
  • synonym: convertible bond - a bond that can be converted into a predetermined amount of the company's equity
  • degree lower: preferred stock - a class of ownership in a corporation that has a higher claim on assets and earnings than common stock
