somatogenic

adjective
originating from physical (not psychological) causes
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsoʊmətoʊˈdʒɛnɪk / (AmE), / ˌsəʊmətəʊˈdʒɛnɪk / (BrE)
(of a disease or disorder) arising from physiological or bodily causes rather than psychological ones.

Examples

Somatogenic disease causes
Modern treatments often consider whether symptoms are somatogenic rather than purely psychological.
Some researchers believe that schizophrenia may have a somatogenic origin linked to chemical imbalances.
The doctor discussed both psychogenic and somatogenic factors contributing to the patient's condition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: organic - relating to or arising from bodily organs or tissues
  • antonym: psychogenic - originating from psychological causes
