kaleidoscopic

adjective
constantly changing in pattern or color
Pronunciation: / kəˌlaɪdəˈskɑːpɪk / (AmE), / kəˌlaɪdəˈskɒpɪk / (BrE)
continually changing or having a complex pattern of colors and shapes, reminiscent of a kaleidoscope

Examples

A kaleidoscopic pattern
He described the city's nightlife as a kaleidoscopic experience, full of excitement and surprise.
Her memories formed a kaleidoscopic pattern, shifting with every passing thought.
The parade moved through the streets in a kaleidoscopic display of colors and music.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monotonous - lacking in variety, uniform or unchanging
  • synonym: variegated - having different colors or varied appearance
