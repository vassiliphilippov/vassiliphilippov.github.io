follow-through

noun
completion of a stroke or swing in sports
6.7 4.0
Pronunciation: /ˈfɑː.loʊ.θruː/ (AmE), /ˈfɒl.əʊ.θruː/ (BrE)
the forward movement that continues after the main part of a stroke or swing, especially in sports, to complete the action smoothly and effectively

Examples

Tennis follow-through
Good tennis players practice their follow-through to maintain control over the ball.
His follow-through made his golf swing more powerful and accurate.
