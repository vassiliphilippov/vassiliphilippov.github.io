discontinuously

adverb
in separate stages
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪskənˈtɪnjuəsli / (AmE), / ˌdɪskənˈtɪnjuːəsli / (BrE)
in a way that is not continuous, happening in separate parts or at intervals

Examples

To work discontinuously
The experiment was conducted discontinuously due to equipment failures.
Rain fell discontinuously throughout the afternoon, with long dry periods in between.
The service operated discontinuously during the holiday season, causing some confusion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: continuously - in a way that happens without interruption
  • synonym: intermittently - in a way that starts and stops at intervals
