overswing

noun
excessive swing
5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈoʊ.vər.swɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈəʊ.və.swɪŋ/ (BrE)
an act of swinging with too much force or beyond the intended range, especially in sports

Examples

A golf overswing
The golfer's overswing caused the ball to fly far past the green.
A common mistake among beginners is an overswing that leads to loss of control.
The coach analyzed his player's overswing to help improve her batting technique.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overhit - to hit something with too much force
  • antonym: underswing - a swing that does not go far enough
