preaching

noun
moralistic lecturing or rebuke
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːtʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpriːtʃɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of delivering moral advice or criticism in a way that is seen as self-righteous or unwelcome

Examples

Annoying preaching
His preaching fell on deaf ears, as no one wanted to listen to his lectures.
I don't need your preaching; I just want your support.
She was tired of his constant preaching about her lifestyle choices.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lecture - a long or serious speech of warning or reprimand
  • antonym: praise - expression of approval or admiration
  • synonym: sermonizing - speaking in a moralizing manner
