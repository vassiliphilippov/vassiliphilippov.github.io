nudge

verb
push gently, usually to get attention
Pronunciation: / nʌdʒ / (AmE), / nʌdʒ / (BrE)
to push someone or something gently, often with an elbow, to get their attention or prompt an action

Examples

To nudge his arm
During the meeting, Sarah gave Tom a gentle nudge to let him know it was his turn to speak.
He tried to nudge her awake without startling her.
The dog nudged my hand, wanting me to pet him.

Related Terms

  • antonym: shove - to push someone or something roughly
