nudge

verb
push gently, usually to get attention

Pronunciation: / nÊŒdÊ’ / (AmE), / nÊŒdÊ’ / (BrE)
to push someone or something gently, often with an elbow, to get their attention or prompt an action

Examples

To nudge his arm
During the meeting, Sarah gave Tom a gentle nudge to let him know it was his turn to speak.
He tried to nudge her awake without startling her.
The dog nudged my hand, wanting me to pet him.

Synonyms

  • jog
    3.0 0.10/m
    jog is a small push used to remind someone or to attract attention, while nudge is a gentle elbow push.
  • prod
    3.7 0.40/m

  • poke at
    2.5 1.02/m
    poke at is a repeated touch with a finger or stick to examine or annoy, while nudge is a single gentle elbow push.
