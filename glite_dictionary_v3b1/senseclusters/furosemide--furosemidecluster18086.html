furosemide

noun
diuretic medicine for hypertension and edema
Pronunciation: / fjʊˈroʊsəˌmaɪd / (AmE), / fjʊəˈrɒsɪmaɪd / (BrE)
a medicine that increases urine production, used chiefly to treat high blood pressure and fluid retention

Examples

Furosemide medicine
Patients with heart failure may be treated with furosemide to reduce fluid buildup.
The doctor prescribed furosemide to help manage her high blood pressure.
You should take furosemide in the morning to avoid frequent trips to the bathroom at night.
