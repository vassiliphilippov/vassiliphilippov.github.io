windlass

noun
mechanical lifting or hauling device
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪndləs / (AmE), / ˈwɪndləs / (BrE)
A device for lifting or hauling, consisting of a horizontal cylinder turned by a crank, onto which a cable or rope is wound.

Examples

A windlass device
A rope was wound tightly around the windlass as they lifted the heavy load.
Maintenance of the windlass is essential for smooth sailing on any ship.
The crew used a windlass to hoist the anchor from the sea floor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: winch - a mechanical device for pulling or lifting, similar to a windlass
