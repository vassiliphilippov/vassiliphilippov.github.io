board up

verb
cover with boards for protection or security
3.8 1.20/m
Pronunciation: / b…îrd  åp / (AmE), / b…îÀêd  åp / (BrE)
to cover (a window, door, or building) with wooden boards, usually to protect or secure it

Examples

To board up windows
After the shop closed down, the owners had to board up the windows to prevent vandalism.
Residents rushed to board up their homes as the storm approached.
The city decided to board up the abandoned house due to safety concerns.

Synonyms

  • plank over
    6.5 0.00/m
    plank over covers a surface with wooden planks for general protection, while board up seals windows or doors to secure them.
  • plank
    6.0 0.20/m
    plank means to lay or cover with planks, but board up usually means nailing boards to windows or doors to protect them.
