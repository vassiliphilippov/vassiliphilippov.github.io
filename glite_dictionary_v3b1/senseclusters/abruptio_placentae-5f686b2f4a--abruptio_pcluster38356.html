abruptio placentae

noun
premature separation of the placenta during pregnancy
Pronunciation: / əˌbrʌp.tioʊ plæˈsɛn.ti / (AmE), / əˌbrʌp.ti.əʊ plæˈsɛn.tiː / (BrE)
A disorder of pregnancy in which the placenta separates prematurely from the wall of the uterus, which can cause bleeding and endanger the mother and fetus.

Examples

abruptio placentae disorder
Doctors monitor pregnant women with high blood pressure for signs of abruptio placentae.
Emergency treatment was required due to the sudden onset of abruptio placentae during her third trimester.
The patient was diagnosed with abruptio placentae after experiencing severe abdominal pain and bleeding.
