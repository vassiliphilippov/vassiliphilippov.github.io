sympodia

noun
botanical stem segments
Pronunciation: / sɪmˈpoʊdiə / (AmE), / sɪmˈpəʊdiə / (BrE)
segments of a plant stem or axis formed by sympodial growth, where the main axis develops from successive lateral branches.

Examples

Orchid sympodia
The orchid produced several sympodia that each bore a cluster of flowers.
In some plants, sympodia allow for more flexible and adaptive stem growth.
Botanists studied the arrangement of sympodia to understand the plant's branching pattern.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monopodium - a single main stem or axis in plant growth
