spot welding

noun
joining metal surfaces at points by welding
6.6 3.4
Pronunciation: /spɑːt ˈweld.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /spɒt ˈweld.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
A process of joining two or more metal surfaces at small distinct points by applying heat and pressure, typically using an electric current.

Examples

Spot welding process
He learned spot welding in his apprenticeship at the auto factory.
Proper spot welding ensures that the metal sheets remain securely fastened.
The car’s body panels are joined through spot welding to provide greater structural integrity.
