outcross

verb
to breed with different line
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtkrɔs /; / ˈaʊtˌkrɔs / (AmE), / ˈaʊtkrɒs /; / ˈaʊtˌkrɒs / (BrE)
to breed an animal or plant with another from a different genetic line to increase genetic diversity

Examples

To outcross a breed
To prevent genetic disorders, breeders often outcross their dogs with unrelated lines.
The botanist decided to outcross the rare plant to strengthen its resistance to disease.
Farmers sometimes outcross their crops to introduce new traits and improve yields.
