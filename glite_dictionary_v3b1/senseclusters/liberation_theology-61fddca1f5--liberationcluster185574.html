liberation theology

noun
Christian theology focused on social and political liberation
5.2 8.6
Pronunciation: / lɪbəˈreɪʃən θiˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / lɪbəˈreɪʃən θɪˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
A branch of Christian theology that interprets the teachings of Jesus Christ in terms of liberation from social, political, and economic oppression, especially prominent in Latin America.

Examples

Liberation theology in Latin America
Many priests in South America have been deeply influenced by liberation theology.
Opponents of liberation theology argue that it conflates religion with political activism.
The rise of liberation theology challenged traditional interpretations of Christian doctrine.
