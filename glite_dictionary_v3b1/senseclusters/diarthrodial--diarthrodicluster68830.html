diarthrodial

adjective
of movable joints
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˌdaɪɑːrˈθroʊdiəl / (AmE), / ˌdaɪɑːˈθrəʊdiəl / (BrE)
relating to a freely movable joint in the body, such as the knee or elbow

Examples

Diarthrodial joint movement
The shoulder is a diarthrodial joint that allows for a wide range of motion.
Damage to diarthrodial joints can lead to reduced mobility and pain.
Physical therapy often focuses on improving movement in diarthrodial joints after injury.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: amphiarthrodial - relating to a slightly movable joint
  • synonym: synovial - relating to joints that are freely movable and contain synovial fluid
