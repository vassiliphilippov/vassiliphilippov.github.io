licensure

noun
official authorization
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪsənʃər / (AmE), / ˈlaɪsənʃə / (BrE)
the official process of granting legal permission to perform a regulated activity or profession

Examples

Apply for licensure
After completing her training, she applied for licensure as a registered nurse.
State licensure is required before you can practice as a professional engineer.
The new regulations have changed the licensure process for teachers in the region.
State licensure requirements for teachers vary from one region to another.
The hospital must maintain its licensure to continue operating legally.
He completed all the necessary steps for professional licensure in engineering.
After passing the exam, she applied for licensure to become a registered nurse.
Many professions require licensure to ensure practitioners meet certain standards.
Without proper licensure, it is illegal to practice medicine in most countries.

  • synonym: registration - the act of recording or enrolling officially
  • antonym: revocation - the act of officially cancelling a license or right
