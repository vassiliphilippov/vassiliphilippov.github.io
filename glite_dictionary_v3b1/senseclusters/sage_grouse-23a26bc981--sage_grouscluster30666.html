sage grouse

noun
large North American grouse of sagebrush areas
Pronunciation: / seɪdʒ ɡraʊs / (AmE), / seɪdʒ ɡraʊs / (BrE)
a large North American grouse found in sagebrush habitats, noted for its elaborate mating displays

Examples

The sage grouse bird
Conservationists are concerned about declining sage grouse populations due to habitat loss.
The sage grouse relies on vast stretches of unbroken sagebrush for survival.
We spotted a male sage grouse performing its unique courtship dance at dawn.
