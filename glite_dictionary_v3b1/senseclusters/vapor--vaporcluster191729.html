vapor

noun
fine particles suspended in air
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈveɪ.pɚ /; / ˈveɪpər / (AmE), / ˈveɪ.pə /; / ˈveɪpə / (BrE)
a substance in the air in the form of a gas or very small drops, such as mist, steam, or smoke

Examples

Water vapor
Clouds form when water vapor in the atmosphere cools and condenses.
The bathroom mirror fogged up due to vapor from the hot shower.
You could see the vapor rising from the kettle as the water boiled.
Warm air can hold more water vapor than cold air.
The kettle released a cloud of vapor as the water boiled.
You could see your breath as vapor in the cold morning air.
In the early morning, a thin vapour rose from the surface of the lake.
The chemist demonstrated how the substance turned into vapour when heated.
You could see the vapour escaping from the kettle as it boiled.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gas - a state of matter with no fixed shape or volume
  • antonym: liquid - a state of matter with a definite volume but no fixed shape
  • synonym: mist - a thick, visible suspension of tiny water droplets in the air
  • synonym: smoke - visible suspension of particles from burning
  • synonym: steam - water in the form of an invisible gas or as visible mist
  • synonym: vapor - Visible suspension in air (American spelling)
