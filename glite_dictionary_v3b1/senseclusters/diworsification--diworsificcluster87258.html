diworsification

noun
poor diversification of investments
9.4 9.9
Pronunciation: /dɪ.wɜːr.sɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /diː.wɜːr.sɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /dɪ.wɜː.sɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /diː.wɜː.sɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
The process or result of making a portfolio or set of investments worse by diversifying too much or into unsuitable assets, thereby reducing overall returns or increasing risk.

Examples

Portfolio diworsification risk
Analysts warned that further expansion could result in diworsification rather than effective growth.
Excessive acquisition of unrelated companies led to diworsification and a decline in shareholder value.
Instead of improving returns, his portfolio suffered from diworsification after investing in poorly performing sectors.
