wander

verb
move in a winding course
1.3 20/m
Pronunciation: / Ààw…ënd…ö /; / Ààw…ëÀênd…ö /; / Ààw…ëÀênd…ôr / (AmE), / Ààw…índ…ô / (BrE)
to move from place to place without a fixed course, often in a relaxed, aimless, or winding way

Examples

To wander the streets
Our hike followed a trail that wandered through the ancient forest.
The little stream wandered across the meadow, twisting between tufts of grass.
The old stone wall wandered along the hillside, curving around boulders and trees.
During the recession, many workers were forced to wander from town to town looking for jobs.
She wandered the city streets with no particular destination in mind.
The dog likes to wander through the fields on sunny afternoons.
After dinner, we wandered into town to explore the streets.
Rather than following a map, he just wandered around the city all afternoon.
She likes to wander through the forest, enjoying the sights and sounds of nature.

Synonyms

  • rove
    6.4 2/m

  • ramble
    4.4 1/m
    ramble means walking for pleasure with no set route, while wander covers moving aimlessly in many ways, not just walking.
  • walk about
    3.1 0.8/m
    walk about means walking around different places without a plan, while wander often feels looser and more aimless in movement.
  • meander
    5.3 4/m
    meander means moving along a slow winding path with many turns, while wander can mean aimless or relaxed movement more broadly.
  • stroll
    1.8 10/m
    stroll means walking slowly and pleasantly for pleasure, while wander is more aimless and may not be a deliberate stroll.
  • range
    3.3 4/m
    range means moving freely over an area, often covering distance or territory, while wander suggests looser, aimless movement.
  • drift
    2.9 20/m
    drift means being carried slowly or moving without clear direction, often passively, while wander implies active, aimless movement.
  • traipse
    7.6 0.5/m
    traipse means walking about casually, often with little energy or care, while wander is more neutral and can be relaxed.
  • stray
    2.8 4/m
    stray means moving away from the correct place or group, often accidentally, while wander means moving aimlessly without necessarily being lost.
