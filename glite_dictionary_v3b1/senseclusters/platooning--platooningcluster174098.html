platooning

noun
alternating players
Pronunciation: / pləˈtuːnɪŋ / (AmE), / pləˈtuːnɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of alternating players at a position in team sports to take advantage of matchups or manage playing time

Examples

Platooning in baseball
The coach used platooning to keep his players fresh throughout the long baseball season.
Effective platooning allowed the team to exploit the opposing pitcher's weaknesses.
Many modern football teams rely on platooning to manage player fatigue and injuries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rotation - the regular changing of players in a team lineup
  • synonym: substitution - the act of replacing one player with another during a game
