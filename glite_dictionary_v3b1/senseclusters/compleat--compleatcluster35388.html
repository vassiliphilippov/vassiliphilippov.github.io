compleat

proper noun
archaic 'complete'
9.9
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpliːt / (AmE), / kəmˈpliːt / (BrE)
an archaic spelling of 'complete', famously used in the title of the 17th-century book 'The Compleat Angler' by Izaak Walton.

Examples

The Compleat Angler
The famous book 'The Compleat Angler' remains a classic of English literature.
In the 17th century, the word compleat was commonly used instead of 'complete'.
Scholars often reference 'The Compleat Angler' when discussing early nature writing.
