holy thistle

noun
a tall thistle with blotched leaves and purple flowers
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈhoʊli ˌθɪsəl / (AmE), / ˈhəʊli ˌθɪsəl / (BrE)
A tall biennial thistle (Silybum marianum) with large white-blotched leaves and purple flower heads, native to Europe and Asia, and naturalized elsewhere.

Examples

Holy thistle plant
Holy thistle has been used in traditional remedies for centuries.
Many people plant holy thistle in their gardens for its striking appearance.
The fields were scattered with the spiky leaves of holy thistle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: milk thistle - another common name for the holy thistle, used interchangeably
