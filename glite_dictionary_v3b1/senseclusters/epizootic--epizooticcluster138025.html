epizootic

adjective
animal epidemic; affecting many animals in a region
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪzoʊˈɑtɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪzəʊˈɒtɪk / (BrE)
(of animal diseases) occurring as an outbreak affecting many individuals of one species in a particular area; epidemic among animals.

Examples

An epizootic outbreak
A sudden epizootic outbreak devastated the region's cattle population.
The government imposed restrictions to prevent the epizootic from reaching nearby farms.
Veterinarians worked tirelessly to control the epizootic disease spreading among wild deer.

Related Terms

  • antonym: enzootic - (of a disease) regularly affecting animals in a particular region
  • synonym: epidemic - a widespread occurrence of an infectious disease in a community
