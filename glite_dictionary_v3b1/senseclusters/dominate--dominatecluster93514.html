dominate

verb
overlook from a height
3.7 6.79/m
Pronunciation: / Ààd…ëÀê.m…ô.ne…™t / (AmE), / Ààd…ím.…™.ne…™t / (BrE)
to have a commanding position over; to overlook from a position above

Examples

A tower dominates the city
A tall skyscraper dominates the city skyline, making it visible from miles away.
From its hilltop perch, the castle dominates the entire valley below.
Their apartment balcony dominates the busy intersection, giving them a great view of downtown.

Synonyms

  • command
    6.2 1.36/m
    command refers to a position that has a clear view over an area, while dominate stresses holding a commanding position above others.
  • overlook
    3.7 9.42/m
    overlook simply gives a view over a place from above, while dominate suggests control and a stronger commanding position.
  • overtop
    6.5 0.03/m
    overtop rises above or looks down on something, while dominate emphasizes holding power or control from a higher position.
  • tower over
    4.8 1.55/m
    tower over is to be much taller and make something seem small, while dominate focuses on control from a higher place.
  • tower
    4.6 7.97/m
    tower is to stand much taller than nearby things, while dominate stresses holding a commanding position over those things.
  • predominate
    7.0 0.31/m
    predominate appears as the most common or visible feature in a place, while dominate implies active control from above.
