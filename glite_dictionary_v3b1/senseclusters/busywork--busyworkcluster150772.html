busywork

noun
work of little real value
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪziˌwɝːk / (AmE), / ˈbɪziˌwɜːk / (BrE)
work or activity that keeps someone busy but has little real value or importance

Examples

busywork tasks
He spent most of the afternoon doing busywork that didn’t help his project at all.
Instead of focusing on important goals, she filled her schedule with busywork.
The teacher assigned extra busywork to keep the students occupied during the last period.

Related Terms

  • synonym: make-work - work created primarily to keep someone busy, not necessarily productive
  • antonym: productive work - work that generates real value or useful results
