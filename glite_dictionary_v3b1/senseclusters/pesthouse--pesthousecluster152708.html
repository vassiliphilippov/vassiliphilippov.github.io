pesthouse

noun
hospital for infectious disease isolation (historical)
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛstˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈpɛstˌhaʊs / (BrE)
A hospital or building where people with infectious diseases, especially historically, were isolated and cared for.

Examples

A pesthouse hospital
During the plague, the city built a pesthouse on the outskirts to contain the sick.
Many old maps mark the location of the former pesthouse by the river.
The novel describes a grim pesthouse where patients were kept away from the healthy population.
