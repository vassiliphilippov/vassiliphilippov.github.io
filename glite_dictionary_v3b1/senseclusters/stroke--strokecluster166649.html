stroke

noun
movement in swimming or rowing
Pronunciation: / stroʊk / (AmE), / strəʊk / (BrE)
a particular movement of the arms and legs used in swimming or rowing to propel the body through water

Examples

Butterfly stroke
He switched to the butterfly stroke during the final lap of the race.
She perfected her freestyle stroke and became a faster swimmer.
The rower matched his stroke to the rhythm of the coxswain's call.

Related Terms
