fox

noun
shifty or deceptive person
Pronunciation: / fɑks / (AmE), / fɒks / (BrE)
a person who is shifty, clever, and deceptive

Examples

A sly fox
His reputation as a fox made colleagues wary of his proposals.
No one trusted the new manager, as he was known to be a fox in business dealings.
She played the part of a fox, always scheming to get ahead at others' expense.

Related Terms

  • antonym: simpleton - a person lacking in subtlety and cleverness
  • synonym: wolf - a fierce or cruel person (metaphorical use)
