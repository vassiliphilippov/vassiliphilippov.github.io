propylene

noun
flammable hydrocarbon gas used in industry
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈproʊpəliːn / (AmE), / ˈprɒpɪliːn / (BrE)
a flammable gaseous hydrocarbon obtained by cracking petroleum, used in organic synthesis and as a fuel

Examples

Propylene gas
Industrial plants use propylene as a raw material for making plastics and chemicals.
Most of the propylene produced worldwide comes from the petrochemical cracking process.
The scientist measured the flammability of propylene in the laboratory.
