progressive culture

noun
forward-thinking societal values
1.8
Pronunciation: / prəˈɡrɛsɪv ˈkʌltʃər / (AmE), / prəˈɡrɛsɪv ˈkʌltʃə / (BrE)
a set of societal values or attitudes characterized by openness to change, innovation, and support for social reform

Examples

Progressive culture in companies
Many tech companies are known for their progressive culture and innovative working environments.
The city prides itself on its progressive culture, embracing diversity and inclusivity.
Universities often foster a progressive culture that encourages new ideas and social change.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conservative culture - societal values resistant to change or innovation
