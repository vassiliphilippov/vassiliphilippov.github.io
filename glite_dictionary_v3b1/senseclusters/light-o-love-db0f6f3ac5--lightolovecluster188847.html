light-o'-love

noun
a woman inconstant in love (archaic)
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪt əˈlʌv / (AmE), / ˈlaɪt əˈlʌv / (BrE)
a woman considered to be fickle or inconstant in love; often used in old literature.

Examples

A light-o'-love woman
He warned his son to steer clear of a light-o'-love, for they bring only heartache.
In the ballad, the heroine was unjustly labeled a light-o'-love after refusing her suitor.
The villagers gossiped about the so-called light-o'-love who charmed and left many hearts broken.
