pauper

noun
very poor person
Pronunciation: / ˈpɔpər / (AmE), / ˈpɔːpə / (BrE)
a person who is extremely poor, especially one who relies on public charity

Examples

A pauper in poverty
After losing his fortune, he died a pauper in a small rented room.
During the nineteenth century, many pauper children were sent to workhouses.
The charity was founded to help paupers living on the streets of the city.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beggar - a person who lives by asking for money or food
  • antonym: millionaire - a person whose wealth amounts to at least a million units of currency
