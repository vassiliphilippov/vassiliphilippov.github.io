greater burdock

noun
large Eurasian species of burdock plant
Pronunciation: / ˈɡreɪtər ˈbɜːrdɑk / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪtə ˈbɜːdɒk / (BrE)
A large species of burdock (Arctium lappa) with heart-shaped leaves, native to Europe and Asia, often found in woodland, hedgerows, and rough grassland, and sometimes cultivated for medicinal or culinary purposes.

Examples

The greater burdock plant
Greater burdock is often grown in gardens for its edible root and medicinal properties.
Herbal remedies sometimes include extracts from greater burdock for their purported health benefits.
The heart-shaped leaves of greater burdock can be seen along the edges of woodlands in early summer.
