banneret

noun
a knight entitled to a square banner
Pronunciation: / ˌbænəˈrɛt / (AmE), / ˌbanəˈrɛt / (BrE)
a knight of rank, especially one who was entitled to lead troops under his own square banner, typically honored for valor in battle

Examples

A banneret knight
As a banneret, he commanded respect both on the battlefield and in court.
In medieval England, a banneret led his followers into battle beneath a distinctive square banner.
The king rewarded him for his valor by making him a banneret.
