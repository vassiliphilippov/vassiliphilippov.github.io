leave behind

verb
depart without taking something or someone
1.0
Pronunciation: / liv bɪˈhaɪnd / (AmE), / liːv bɪˈhaɪnd / (BrE)
to go away from a place without taking someone or something with you; to abandon someone or something

Examples

To leave behind old things
He decided to leave behind his old clothes when moving out.
Sometimes we have to leave behind painful memories to move forward.
They left behind a note for their friends explaining their absence.
He decided to leave behind all his belongings and start a new life.
They quickly packed up, worried they would leave behind something important.
When you move to college, you may have to leave behind some childhood friends.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abandon - to leave someone or something completely and for good
