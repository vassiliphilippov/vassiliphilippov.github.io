sweep

verb
to extend or cover an area or period
2.3 10/m
Pronunciation: / swip / (AmE), / swiÀêp / (BrE)
to extend or cover a wide area, space, or period of time

Examples

Sweep of history
A feeling of melancholy sweeps through the novel, spanning decades of history.
Golden fields sweep across the countryside, glowing in the afternoon light.
The forest seemed to sweep for miles without interruption.
As the spotlight swept the stage, the actors froze in their positions.
Her eyes swept the audience, searching for a familiar face.
The new law sweeps across all industries, affecting every company in the country.

Synonyms

  • span
    3.4 10/m
    span stretches across a distance or time in a steady way, while sweep suggests moving broadly and covering a wide area.
  • cover
    3.3 11/m
    cover means reaching or lying over an area directly, while sweep implies moving across broadly and often quickly.
  • extend
    3.2 57/m
    extend means to make something longer in time or size, while sweep suggests stretching across space or a long period.
  • spread
    1.6 48/m
    spread means to move out and cover an area in parts, while sweep suggests a single broad movement across space or time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scan - to look at all parts of something carefully
  • synonym: skim - to move quickly or lightly over a surface or area
  • synonym: span - to extend across or over a space or time
