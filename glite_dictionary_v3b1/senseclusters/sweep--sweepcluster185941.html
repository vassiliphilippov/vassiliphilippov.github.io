sweep

verb
to extend or cover an area or period
6.5
Pronunciation: / swip / (AmE), / swiːp / (BrE)
to extend or cover a wide area, space, or period of time

Examples

Sweep of history
A feeling of melancholy sweeps through the novel, spanning decades of history.
Golden fields sweep across the countryside, glowing in the afternoon light.
The forest seemed to sweep for miles without interruption.
As the spotlight swept the stage, the actors froze in their positions.
Her eyes swept the audience, searching for a familiar face.
The new law sweeps across all industries, affecting every company in the country.
