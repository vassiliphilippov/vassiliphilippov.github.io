delay line

noun
circuit delaying a signal
5.9 5.4
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈleɪ laɪn/ /; / /də.ˈleɪ laɪn/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈleɪ laɪn/ (BrE)
an electronic circuit that intentionally delays the transmission of a signal by a precise amount of time

Examples

An electronic delay line
A delay line can be implemented using coils and capacitors to achieve the desired effect.
Older types of radar systems relied on a delay line for proper signal timing.
The engineer used a delay line to synchronize the audio and video signals in the system.
