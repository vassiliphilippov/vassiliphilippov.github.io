gauntly

adverb
in a bleak way
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɔntli / (AmE), / ˈɡɔːntli / (BrE)
in a bleak, severe, or grim manner, often suggesting starkness or desolation.

Examples

Stood gauntly alone
The old trees stood gauntly along the deserted road, their branches bare against the winter sky.
He stared gauntly at the empty plate, his face shadowed by hunger and fatigue.
The mountain rose gauntly above the valley, its rocky slopes untouched by vegetation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cheerfully - in a happy or optimistic manner
  • synonym: grimly - in a stern or forbidding manner
