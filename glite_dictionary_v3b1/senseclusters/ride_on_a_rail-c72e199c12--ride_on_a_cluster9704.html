ride on a rail

verb
to be publicly punished or ridiculed
Pronunciation: / raɪd ɑn ə reɪl / (AmE), / raɪd ɒn ə reɪl / (BrE)
to be publicly punished or ridiculed, especially in a humiliating or ceremonial manner

Examples

To ride on a rail publicly
After the scandal broke, the mayor felt he might ride on a rail out of town.
He joked that if he forgot their anniversary again, he would probably ride on a rail at home.
If the employees found out about the decision, the boss could ride on a rail at the company meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tar and feather - to punish or humiliate publicly by covering with tar and feathers
