allay

verb
reduce or soothe feelings
Pronunciation: / əˈleɪ / (AmE), / əˈleɪ / (BrE)
to make a feeling such as fear, anger, or worry less strong or to relieve it

Examples

To allay fears
Regular communication can allay employees' concerns during organizational changes.
She tried to allay his fears by explaining the situation clearly.
The medicine helped allay the child's pain after the injury.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aggravate - make worse or more severe
  • synonym: alleviate - make (a problem or feeling) less severe
