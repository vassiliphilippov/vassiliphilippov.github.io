interactionism

noun
theory of combined influences
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈækʃəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɪntərˈækʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
the theory that behavior and development result from the interaction between biological and environmental factors

Examples

Personality interactionism
Many psychologists support interactionism when studying how personality develops over time.
The theory of interactionism suggests that both genes and environment shape human behavior.
Modern research often relies on interactionism to explain complex mental health issues.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biopsychosocial - an approach considering biological, psychological, and social factors
  • antonym: nature versus nurture - the debate over whether genetics or environment shapes behavior
