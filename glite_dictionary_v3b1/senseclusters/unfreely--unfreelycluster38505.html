unfreely

adverb
in a stingy way
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈfriːli / (AmE), / ʌnˈfriːli / (BrE)
in a way that is not free, open, or generous; with reluctance, constraint, or sparingness

Examples

To speak unfreely
He shared his knowledge unfreely, offering only brief and vague answers to our questions.
The supplies were distributed unfreely among the villagers, leaving many without enough.
She gave her approval unfreely, making it clear she was not fully supportive.
He answered the questions unfreely, clearly worried about saying the wrong thing.
The artist felt she was painting unfreely under the strict guidelines of her employer.
During the interview, she spoke unfreely because she feared possible repercussions.
He spoke unfreely about his past, avoiding direct answers to the questions.
The child unfreely handed over the toy, clearly not wanting to share it.
She agreed unfreely to the proposal, her hesitation obvious to everyone in the room.

Related Terms

  • antonym: freely - without restriction or constraint
  • antonym: generously - in a giving or liberal way
  • synonym: reluctantly - in a way that shows unwillingness or hesitation
  • synonym: sparingly - in a restricted or limited way
  • antonym: willingly - in a way that shows readiness or eagerness
