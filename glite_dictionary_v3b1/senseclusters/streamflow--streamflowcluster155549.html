streamflow

noun
river water movement
4.4
Pronunciation: /ˈstriːm.floʊ/ (AmE), /ˈstriːm.fləʊ/ (BrE)
the flow or amount of water moving in a river or stream, often measured to predict flooding or water supply

Examples

Streamflow measurement
Scientists monitor streamflow to predict flooding risks.
Low streamflow during the summer can affect fish and other wildlife in the river.
Heavy rainfall caused the streamflow to increase rapidly, raising concerns about possible flooding.
Scientists monitor streamflow to predict possible flooding after heavy rainfall.
Low streamflow during the summer can affect water supplies for nearby towns.
Accurate streamflow data helps manage irrigation for local farms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: discharge - the volume of water flowing through a river or stream
  • synonym: runoff - water from rain or snow that flows over the ground into streams
