mawkishness

noun
excessive or insincere sentimental emotion
9.9 7.6
Pronunciation: /ˈmɑː.kɪʃ.nəs/ /; / /ˈmɔː.kɪʃ.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈmɔː.kɪʃ.nəs/ (BrE)
excessive or insincere sentimentality or emotion, especially in a way that seems false or overly sweet

Examples

Excessive mawkishness
Critics accused the novel of mawkishness for its overly sentimental depiction of family reunions.
Her speech was criticized for its mawkishness and lack of genuine emotion.
The film's ending was full of mawkishness, making it feel insincere rather than touching.
He spoke of his childhood with such mawkishness that some people felt uncomfortable.
Many viewers were put off by the film's mawkishness and artificial sentiment.
The novel's mawkishness made it difficult for critics to take its message seriously.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sentimentality - excessive tenderness, sadness, or nostalgia
  • antonym: sincerity - the quality of being free from pretense or deceit
