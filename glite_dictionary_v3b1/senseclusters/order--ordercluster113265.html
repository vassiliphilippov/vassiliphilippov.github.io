Synonyms

  • enjoin
    8.6 2/m
    enjoin is a formal legal order given by someone in power, while order is a general authoritative command to someone.
  • prescribe
    4.2 9/m
    prescribe states an official rule or action to follow, while order means giving a direct command or instruction with authority.
  • dictate
    5.0 13/m
    dictate gives strict commands and often controls how things are done, while order simply means to give an authoritative instruction.
  • instruct
    2.8 14/m
    instruct gives detailed directions on how to do something, while order focuses on telling someone authoritatively to do it.
  • direct
    1.6 20/m
    direct gives guidance or shows the way in practical terms, while order is an authoritative command to be obeyed.
  • tell
    1.0 600/m
    tell asks or informs someone and can be casual, while order is a stronger, authoritative instruction from someone in charge.
