videocast

verb
to broadcast video online
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪdioʊˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈvɪdiəʊˌkɑːst / (BrE)
to broadcast video content over the internet, either live or as a recorded program

Examples

To videocast an event
The university will videocast the graduation ceremony so families can watch from home.
They plan to videocast their weekly meetings for remote team members.
Many musicians now videocast their concerts to reach a global audience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: broadcast - to transmit a program or information by radio or television
  • synonym: stream - to transmit audio or video content over the internet in real time
