skewness

noun
asymmetry or slant; statistical measure of asymmetry
7.7 0.32/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàskjuÀên…ôs / (AmE), / ÀàskjuÀên…™s / (BrE)
the quality or state of being asymmetrical or slanted, especially in terms of a lack of balance or evenness; in statistics, a measure of the asymmetry of a probability distribution.

Examples

The skewness of data
In architecture, skewness in a structure can indicate foundational problems.
Researchers measured the skewness of the income distribution to assess inequality.
The skewness of the data suggests that most students scored above the average.

Synonyms

  • asymmetry
    6.5 2.64/m
    asymmetry is the general condition of parts not matching in shape or size, while skewness focuses on directional slant, often in statistics.
  • dissymmetry
    9.2 0.02/m
    dissymmetry is a formal term for lack of balance among parts, while skewness often describes a directional imbalance in data.
  • lopsidedness
    6.1 0.03/m
    lopsidedness is a casual, physical idea of one side being larger or lower, while skewness is a measured asymmetry, often in statistics.
