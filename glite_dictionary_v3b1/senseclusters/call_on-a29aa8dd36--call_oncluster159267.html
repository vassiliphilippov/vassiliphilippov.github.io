call on

verb
challenge someone to justify a statement
4.7 3.56/m
Pronunciation: / k…îl …ën / (AmE), / k…îÀêl …ín / (BrE)
to challenge someone to justify a statement or take responsibility for an action, especially by pointing out an inconsistency or offense

Examples

To call on his words
He was called on his inappropriate comment by several members of the audience.
The journalist called on the politician about the inconsistency in his story.
When he lied during the meeting, she called on him to provide evidence for his claim.

Synonyms

  • call to account
    6.0 0.15/m
    call to account is a formal demand that someone explain or accept responsibility, while call on points out an inconsistency and forces a reply.
  • call (someone) out
    5.8 0.01/m
    call (someone) out publicly criticizes or names a wrong action, while call on challenges someone to explain or accept responsibility.

Related Terms

  • synonym: confront - to face someone with a challenge or criticism
