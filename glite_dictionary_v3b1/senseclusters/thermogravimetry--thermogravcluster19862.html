thermogravimetry

noun
technique measuring weight change with temperature
9.9 9.0
Pronunciation: /θɜːr.moʊ.ˈgræv.ɪ.mə.triː/ /; / /θɜːr.mə.ˈgræv.ɪ.mə.triː/ (AmE), /θɜː.məʊ.ˈgræv.ɪ.mə.triː/ /; / /θɜː.mə.ˈgræv.ɪ.mə.triː/ (BrE)
the measurement and analysis of changes in a substance's weight as its temperature changes, used to assess chemical properties or composition

Examples

Thermogravimetry analysis method
Thermogravimetry is essential in studying polymers that change weight with temperature.
Researchers used thermogravimetry to analyze how the material decomposed when heated.
The laboratory's thermogravimetry results revealed small impurities in the metal sample.
