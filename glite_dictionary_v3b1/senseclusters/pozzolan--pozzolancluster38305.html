pozzolan

noun
concrete additive
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːtsəlæn / (AmE), / ˈpɒtsəlæn / (BrE)
a natural or artificial material that reacts with lime to improve the strength and durability of concrete

Examples

Natural pozzolan material
The engineers mixed pozzolan with cement to create a more durable concrete structure.
Ancient Romans used volcanic ash as a pozzolan in their famous buildings.
Modern construction often relies on pozzolan to enhance the performance of concrete.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aggregate - a material such as sand or gravel used in construction
  • synonym: cement - a powdery substance used to bind building materials together
