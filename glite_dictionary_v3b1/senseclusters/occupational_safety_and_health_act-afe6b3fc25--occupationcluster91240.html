Occupational Safety and Health Act

noun
US law for workplace safety standards
4.5
Pronunciation: / ˌɑː.kjəˈpeɪ.ʃən.əl ˈseɪf.ti ənd hɛlθ ækt / (AmE), / ˌɒk.jʊˈpeɪ.ʃən.əl ˈseɪf.ti ənd hɛlθ ækt / (BrE)
A United States law enacted in 1970 designed to ensure safe and healthy working conditions for employees by establishing standards and enforcement through the Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA).

Examples

The Occupational Safety and Health Act law
Every employer in the US must comply with the standards set forth by the Occupational Safety and Health Act.
Since the passage of the Occupational Safety and Health Act, workplace injuries have significantly decreased.
The Occupational Safety and Health Act established strict guidelines for workplace safety in the United States.
