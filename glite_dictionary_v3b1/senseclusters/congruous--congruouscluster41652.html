congruous

adjective
in agreement; harmonious; matching
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑŋɡruəs /; / ˈkɑːŋɡruəs / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋɡruːəs / (BrE)
in agreement, harmony, or suitable together; matching or consistent

Examples

A congruous match
His actions were congruous with his stated beliefs and values.
It is important that your words are congruous with your intentions.
The new design is congruous with the building's traditional style.
Her values are congruous with her actions, creating a sense of integrity.
His explanation was not congruous with the facts of the case.
The modern art was surprisingly congruous with the historical architecture of the building.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compatible - able to exist or work together without conflict
  • antonym: incongruous - not in harmony; out of place
