deductibility

noun
tax-deductible status
Pronunciation: / dɪˌdʌktəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / dɪˌdʌktəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of an expense or item that allows it to be subtracted from a total, especially from taxable income according to tax laws

Charities often highlight the deductibility of donations to encourage more contributions.
The deductibility of certain expenses can reduce your overall tax bill.
Before making a purchase, check the deductibility of the item for your business.
Changes in law may affect the deductibility of charitable donations.

  • antonym: taxable - subject to being taxed
