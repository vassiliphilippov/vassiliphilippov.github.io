subjectification

noun
formation of identity
Pronunciation: / səbˌdʒɛktɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / səbˌdʒɛktɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which a person develops a sense of self or identity, often shaped by social institutions and practices

Examples

Subjectification of identity
The subjectification of students occurs as they internalize the values taught in school.
Media plays a significant role in the subjectification of young people by shaping their beliefs and behaviors.
Researchers discussed how subjectification influences the way individuals see themselves in society.

Related Terms

  • antonym: objectification - the process of treating a person as an object rather than as an individual
  • synonym: socialization - the process of learning and adopting the norms and values of a society
