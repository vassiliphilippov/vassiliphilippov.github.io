undercut

noun
material removed by a cut underneath
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndɚˌkʌt / (AmE), / ˈʌndəkʌt / (BrE)
the material that is removed by making a cut from underneath, especially in carpentry, mining, or metalworking.

Examples

Mining undercut
In mining, controlling the undercut is crucial for preventing collapses.
The carpenter explained that too much undercut could weaken the support beam.
The operator carefully measured the undercut before proceeding to the next phase of the metalworking process.
