variably

adverb
in a variable manner
3.2
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛriəbli / (AmE), / ˈveəriəbli / (BrE)
In a way that changes or varies; not consistently or uniformly.

Examples

To change variably
Her moods shift variably depending on the circumstances.
Temperatures in the region can change variably from day to day.
The results were variably successful across different trials.

Related Terms

  • antonym: constantly - in a continuous, unchanging way
  • synonym: variable - likely to change, not constant (adjective)
