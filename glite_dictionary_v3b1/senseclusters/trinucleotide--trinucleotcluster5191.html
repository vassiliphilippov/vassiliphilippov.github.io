trinucleotide

noun
three joined nucleotides
Pronunciation: / traɪˌnuːkliəˌtaɪd /; / traɪˈnuːkliəˌtaɪd / (AmE), / traɪˌnjuːkliəˌtaɪd /; / traɪˈnjuːkliəˌtaɪd / (BrE)
a group or sequence of three nucleotides in DNA or RNA, often acting as a unit that can code for a specific amino acid

Examples

The mutation involved a change in a single trinucleotide within the DNA strand.
A mutation in a trinucleotide can lead to genetic disorders such as Huntington's disease.
During translation, each trinucleotide in the mRNA specifies a particular amino acid.
Scientists study trinucleotide repeats to understand certain inherited conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: codon - a sequence of three nucleotides that codes for an amino acid
  • degree lower: dinucleotide - a molecule made of two joined nucleotides
  • degree higher: oligonucleotide - a short chain of nucleotides, usually more than three
