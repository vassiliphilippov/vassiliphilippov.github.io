artiodactyl mammal

noun
even-toed hoofed mammal group
3.4 7.6
Pronunciation: /ɑːr.tiː.əʊ.ˈdæk.təl ˈmæm.əl/ (AmE), /ɑː.tɪ.əʊ.ˈdæk.tɪl ˈmæm.əl/ (BrE)
A placental mammal with hooves and an even number of functional toes on each foot, such as deer, pigs, and camels.

Examples

An artiodactyl mammal species
Pigs, as artiodactyl mammals, have two main weight-bearing toes on each hoof.
The giraffe is an example of an artiodactyl mammal native to Africa.
The study focused on the evolutionary history of artiodactyl mammals and their adaptation to various habitats.

Related Terms

  • antonym: odd-toed ungulate - placental mammal with an odd number of toes (e.g., horse, rhinoceros)
