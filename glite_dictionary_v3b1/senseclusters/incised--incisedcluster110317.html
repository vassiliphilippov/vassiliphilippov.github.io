incised

adjective
cut or carved into a surface
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈsaɪzd / (AmE), / ɪnˈsaɪzd / (BrE)
cut or carved into a surface with a sharp instrument, leaving lines, patterns, or marks

Examples

Incised patterns on stone
A series of incised lines decorated the temple wall.
The ancient vase featured an incised pattern around its rim.
The artist preferred to create incised designs rather than painting.
Each stone was carefully incised with a unique symbol.
The artist created an incised design on the metal plate for printing.
The surgeon made an incised opening to access the damaged tissue.

Related Terms

  • antonym: embossed - raised in relief on a surface
  • synonym: engraved - carved or etched into a surface
  • antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface, not cut or marked
