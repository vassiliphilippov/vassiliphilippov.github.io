unipolar depression

noun
major depression without mania
6.2 4.7
Pronunciation: /juː.nɪ.ˈpoʊ.lər dɪ.ˈpreʃ.ən/ /; / /juː.nə.ˈpoʊ.lər də.ˈpreʃ.ən/ (AmE), /juː.nɪ.ˈpəʊ.lə dɪ.ˈpreʃ.ən/ /; / /juː.nə.ˈpəʊ.lə də.ˈpreʃ.ən/ (BrE)
A type of major depressive disorder characterized by depressive episodes without the manic or hypomanic phases typical of bipolar disorder.

Examples

Unipolar depression diagnosis
Her doctors diagnosed her with unipolar depression after ruling out any history of manic episodes.
Many patients with unipolar depression experience persistent sadness and a lack of energy.
Treatment for unipolar depression often includes a combination of psychotherapy and medication.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bipolar disorder - a mental disorder involving episodes of mania and depression
  • synonym: major depressive disorder - a severe mood disorder characterized by persistent feelings of sadness and loss of interest
