Matthew

proper noun
one of Jesus's apostles; Gospel author
Pronunciation: / ˈmæθ.ju / (AmE), / ˈmæθ.juː / (BrE)
(in the New Testament) one of Jesus's twelve apostles, traditionally regarded as the author of the first Gospel.

Examples

Apostle Matthew
Matthew is often depicted with a winged man or angel as his symbol.
According to tradition, Matthew was a former tax collector before becoming an apostle.
The Gospel according to Matthew is the first book of the New Testament.
