Metroxylon

proper noun
genus of Malayan palm trees (sago palms)
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtroʊˈzaɪlɒn / (AmE), / ˌmɛtrɒkˈsaɪlɒn / (BrE)
A genus of Malayan pinnate-leaved palm trees that flower and fruit once before dying, including the sago palm.

Examples

Metroxylon palm tree
After flowering, a Metroxylon palm completes its life cycle and dies.
Many villages rely on Metroxylon plants to harvest edible sago starch.
The Metroxylon genus is best known for the sago palm, a staple food source in some Asian regions.
