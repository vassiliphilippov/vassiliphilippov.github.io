parlement

noun
French high court
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːrləˈmɑ̃ / (AmE), / ˌpɑːləˈmɑ̃ / (BrE)
a high court of justice in pre-revolutionary France, especially one of the main provincial appellate courts such as the Parlement of Paris

Examples

Parlement of Paris
The parlement of Paris played a significant role in the political life of pre-revolutionary France.
Records from the parlement provide valuable insights into the legal history of the era.
Many important decisions were made by the parlement before the French Revolution.

Related Terms

  • synonym: court - a place where legal cases are heard and decided
  • synonym: tribunal - a body established to settle certain types of disputes
