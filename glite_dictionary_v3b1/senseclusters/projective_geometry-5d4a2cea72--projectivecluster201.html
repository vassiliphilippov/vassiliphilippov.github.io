projective geometry

noun
branch of geometry on projection invariants
Pronunciation: / prəˈdʒɛktɪv dʒiˈɑmɪtri / (AmE), / prəˈdʒɛktɪv dʒiˈɒmɪtri / (BrE)
a branch of geometry that studies properties which remain unchanged under projection, such as the alignment of points and the intersection of lines
