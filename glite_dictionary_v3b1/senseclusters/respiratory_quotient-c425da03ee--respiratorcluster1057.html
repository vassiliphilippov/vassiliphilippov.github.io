respiratory quotient

noun
ratio of CO₂ produced to O₂ consumed
Pronunciation: / rɪˈspɪrəˌtɔri ˈkwoʊʃənt / (AmE), / rɪˈspɪrəˌtɔːri ˈkwəʊʃənt / (BrE)
the ratio of the volume of carbon dioxide produced to the volume of oxygen consumed by an organism or cell over a given period

Examples

The respiratory quotient value
A low respiratory quotient suggests fat is being used as the main energy source.
Researchers measured the respiratory quotient of athletes during intense exercise.
The respiratory quotient can help determine metabolic changes in patients.
