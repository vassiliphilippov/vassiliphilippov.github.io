at first blush

adverb
on first impression
Pronunciation: / ət ˈfɝst blʌʃ / (AmE), / ət ˈfɜːst blʌʃ / (BrE)
used to say how something appears or seems when you first see or think about it, before looking more closely

Examples

At first blush it seems
At first blush, her explanation seemed perfectly reasonable, but later I noticed some inconsistencies.
He dismissed the idea at first blush, not realizing its potential benefits upon further review.
The proposal looked promising at first blush, but the details raised several concerns.
At first blush, the data seemed to support our hypothesis, but later analysis revealed flaws.
At first blush, the painting appeared simple, yet it held many hidden details.
His explanation sounded reasonable at first blush, but the facts told a different story.

Related Terms

  • synonym: at first glance - as a first impression or upon first looking
