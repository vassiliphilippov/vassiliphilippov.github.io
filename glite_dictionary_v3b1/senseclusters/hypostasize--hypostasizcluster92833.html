hypostasize

verb
to treat as a real entity

to treat or regard something abstract as a real, distinct, or concrete entity (especially in philosophy or theology)

Examples

To hypostasize an idea
Theologians often hypostasize the Holy Spirit as a distinct person within the Trinity.
Some philosophers hypostasize concepts like justice or truth as if they exist independently.
In his writings, he refused to hypostasize abstract ideas, preferring to discuss them as human constructs.
Some theories hypostasize the mind as a separate entity from the body.
It is easy to hypostasize ideas like love or justice in philosophical discussions.
The author warned not to hypostasize social constructs as if they were natural laws.
