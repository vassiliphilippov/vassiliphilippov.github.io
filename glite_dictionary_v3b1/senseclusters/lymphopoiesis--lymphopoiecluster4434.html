lymphopoiesis

noun
formation of lymphocytes
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˌlɪm.foʊ.pɔɪˈiː.sɪs / (AmE), / ˌlɪm.fəʊ.pɔɪˈiː.sɪs / (BrE)
the formation of lymphocytes in the bone marrow, lymph nodes, thymus, and spleen

Examples

lymphopoiesis in bone marrow
Certain diseases can disrupt lymphopoiesis, leading to immune deficiencies.
Research into lymphopoiesis has helped scientists understand autoimmune disorders.
The process of lymphopoiesis ensures the body can produce enough lymphocytes to fight infection.
