skiagraph

noun
radiographic image (esp. X-ray)
Pronunciation: / ˈskaɪəˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈskɪəɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a photographic image produced on a radiosensitive surface by radiation other than visible light, especially by X-rays or gamma rays

Examples

A skiagraph image
A high-quality skiagraph can reveal details invisible to the naked eye.
Many early diagnoses relied on interpreting a skiagraph rather than modern computerized images.
The doctor examined the skiagraph to check for any signs of a fracture.
