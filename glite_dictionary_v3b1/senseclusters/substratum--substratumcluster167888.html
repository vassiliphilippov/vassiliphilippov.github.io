substratum

noun
surface or layer for organism growth
Pronunciation: / səbˈstreɪtəm / (AmE), / sʌbˈstrɑːtəm / (BrE)
a surface or layer on which an organism grows or to which it is attached, especially in biology.

Examples

Coral substratum
Corals secrete calcium carbonate to build a solid substratum for their colonies.
Researchers studied the effects of different soil substrata on plant root development.
The lichen anchored itself firmly to the rocky substratum by its root-like structures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: substrate - a surface on which an organism grows or is attached; sometimes used interchangeably with substratum
