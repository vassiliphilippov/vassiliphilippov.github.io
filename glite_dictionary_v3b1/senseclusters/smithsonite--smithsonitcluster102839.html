smithsonite

noun
zinc carbonate mineral; ore of zinc
Pronunciation: / ˈsmɪθ.səˌnaɪt / (AmE), / ˈsmɪθ.sə.naɪt / (BrE)
a carbonate mineral of zinc, often occurring in botryoidal masses and used as an ore of zinc

Examples

Smithsonite mineral sample
Collectors prize smithsonite for its wide range of colors and crystal forms.
Refined smithsonite was historically an important source of zinc.
The geologist discovered a beautiful specimen of smithsonite in the ancient quarry.
