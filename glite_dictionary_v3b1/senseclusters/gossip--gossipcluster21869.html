gossip

verb
talk about others' private matters
1.0 1.2
Pronunciation: /ˈgɑː.sɪp/ /; / /ˈgɑː.səp/ (AmE), /ˈgɒs.ɪp/ /; / /ˈgɒs.əp/ (BrE)
to talk about other people's private lives, often spreading rumors or personal information

Examples

To gossip about friends
It's easy to gossip without realizing the damage it can do to someone's reputation.
People in the office love to gossip about their coworkers during lunch breaks.
She refused to gossip about her friend's personal problems.
Even though she promised not to gossip, she couldn't help telling him the latest news.
The neighbors love to gossip about each other's families.
They were gossiping in the kitchen when I arrived.

  • antonym: keep quiet - to refrain from talking about something, especially secrets
  • synonym: rumor - to spread unverified information about others
