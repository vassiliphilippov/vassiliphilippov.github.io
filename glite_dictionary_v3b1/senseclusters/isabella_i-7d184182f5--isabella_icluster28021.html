Isabella I

proper noun
Queen of Castile, united Spain, sponsored Columbus
9.0
Pronunciation: /ɪ.zə.ˈbe.lə ðə fɜːrst/ (AmE), /ɪ.zə.ˈbe.lə ðə fɜːst/ (BrE)
(1451–1504) Queen of Castile whose marriage to Ferdinand of Aragon united Spain; she established the Spanish Inquisition and sponsored Columbus's 1492 voyage.

Examples

Isabella I of Castile
Isabella I played a crucial role in the unification of Spain through her marriage to Ferdinand of Aragon.
During the Spanish Inquisition, Isabella I and Ferdinand II sought to establish religious uniformity throughout Spain.
Many historians credit Isabella I with sponsoring Christopher Columbus’s famous 1492 voyage to the New World.
