Korean velvet grass

noun
a drought-resistant Asiatic lawn grass
7.3
Pronunciation: / kəˈriːən ˈvɛlvɪt ɡræs / (AmE), / kəˈriːən ˈvɛlvɪt ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
A creeping perennial grass native to Asia, introduced to the southern United States for use as a drought-resistant lawn grass.

Examples

Korean velvet grass spreads quickly
Korean velvet grass spreads quickly and provides a dense, green cover even in hot climates.
Many homeowners in Texas have replaced their lawns with Korean velvet grass to conserve water.
The landscaper recommended Korean velvet grass for its ability to thrive during dry summers.
