zonally

adverb
east–west direction
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈzoʊnəli / (AmE), / ˈzəʊnəli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to zones or bands, especially in an east–west direction (often used in science for movement or variation across a region)

Examples

Winds blow zonally
The winds blew zonally across the continent, affecting weather patterns over a wide area.
Ocean currents often flow zonally near the equator, moving water from west to east.
Meteorologists observed that the air masses were moving zonally rather than in a north–south direction.
The temperature varies zonally as you move from the coast to the mountains.
Winds often flow zonally along lines of latitude in the atmosphere.
Rainfall is distributed zonally in some climate regions, creating distinct bands of vegetation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: locally - in a way that relates to a specific place or area
  • antonym: meridionally - in a north–south direction or manner
  • synonym: regionally - in a way that relates to regions
