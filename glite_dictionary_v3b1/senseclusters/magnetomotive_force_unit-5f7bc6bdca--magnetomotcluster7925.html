magnetomotive force unit

noun
unit of magnetomotive force
Pronunciation: / ˌmæɡnətoʊˈmoʊtɪv fɔrs ˈjunɪt / (AmE), / ˌmæɡnətəʊˈməʊtɪv fɔːs ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a unit used to measure magnetomotive force, which drives magnetic flux through a circuit

Examples

One magnetomotive force unit
One magnetomotive force unit is required to drive the specified magnetic flux through the circuit.
The ampere-turn is a common magnetomotive force unit used in electrical engineering.
To calculate the total MMF, express the result in a standard magnetomotive force unit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ampere-turn - unit of magnetomotive force equal to the MMF produced by one ampere flowing in a single turn of wire
