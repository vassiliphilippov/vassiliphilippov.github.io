fenny

noun
marshy land
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈfe.nɪ/ (AmE), /ˈfe.nɪ/ (BrE)
an archaic or dialect word for a fen or marsh; a stretch of marshy or boggy land

Examples

A wide fenny
The old map showed a large fenny stretching across the valley.
Farmers avoided planting crops near the fenny because the ground was too wet.
In spring, the fenny would fill with water and attract many birds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fen - a low and marshy or frequently flooded area of land
  • antonym: hill - a naturally raised area of land, not marshy
  • synonym: marsh - an area of low-lying, wet, and soft ground
