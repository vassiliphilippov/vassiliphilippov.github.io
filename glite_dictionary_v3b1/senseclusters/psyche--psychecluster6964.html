psyche

verb
to mentally prepare
6.8
Pronunciation: / saɪk / (AmE), / saɪk / (BrE)
to prepare oneself or someone mentally for a challenge or difficult situation, often to increase confidence or readiness

Examples

To psyche up
Before the big game, the coach psyched the team up with an inspiring speech.
She psyched herself before the interview to stay calm and focused.
He psyched himself up to give the presentation in front of the large audience.

Related Terms

  • antonym: discourage - to make someone less confident or enthusiastic
  • synonym: motivate - to encourage or inspire someone to take action
