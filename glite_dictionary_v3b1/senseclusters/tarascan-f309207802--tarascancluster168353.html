Tarascan

proper noun
Mexican indigenous group
Pronunciation: / təˈræskən / (AmE), / təˈræskən / (BrE)
a member of an indigenous people of western Mexico or the language spoken by this group

Examples

A Tarascan person
The Tarascan civilization built impressive stone structures in western Mexico.
Many people in Michoacán still speak the Tarascan language today.
Researchers are studying the traditions of the Tarascan people to learn more about their history.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Purépecha - the self-designation of the Tarascan people and their language
