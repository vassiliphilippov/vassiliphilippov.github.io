noninstrumental

adjective
not influential
7.7
Pronunciation: /nɑːn.ɪn.strʌ.ˈmen.təl/ /; / /nən.ɪn.strʌ.ˈmen.təl/ (AmE), /nɒn.ɪn.strʊ.ˈmen.təl/ /; / /nɒn.ɪn.strə.ˈmen.təl/ (BrE)
not serving as a means to achieve something or not playing an important or decisive role in an outcome

Examples

A noninstrumental role
Her comments were considered noninstrumental in the final decision-making process.

Her interest in philosophy was purely noninstrumental, driven by curiosity rather than career goals.
