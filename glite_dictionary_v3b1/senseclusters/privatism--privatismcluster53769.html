privatism

noun
belief in privatization
9.0
Pronunciation: /ˈpraɪ.və.tɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈprɪ.və.tɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈprɪ.və.tɪ.zəm/ /; / /ˈpraɪ.və.tɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
a political or economic belief that public services, industries, or assets should be transferred to private ownership or control

Examples

Privatism in government
The rise of privatism has led to debates about the role of government in public services.
Critics argue that privatism can reduce access to essential utilities for low-income families.
Supporters of privatism believe it increases efficiency and innovation in formerly state-run industries.

Related Terms

  • antonym: collectivism - the practice or principle of giving a group priority over each individual
  • synonym: privatization - the process of transferring public assets to private ownership
