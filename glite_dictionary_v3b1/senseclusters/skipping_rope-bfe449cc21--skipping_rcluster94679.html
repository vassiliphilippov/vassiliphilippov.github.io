skipping rope

noun
rope used for jumping exercise or play
Pronunciation: / ˈskɪpɪŋ roʊp / (AmE), / ˈskɪpɪŋ rəʊp / (BrE)
A length of rope, typically with handles at each end, that is swung over and under the body while a person jumps to avoid it as part of exercise or play.

Examples

A skipping rope game
Children often play with a skipping rope during recess at school.
She bought a new skipping rope to use during her morning workouts.
The athlete used a skipping rope to warm up before the race.
