gross estate

noun
total value of all assets at death
Pronunciation: / ɡroʊs ɪˈsteɪt / (AmE), / ɡrəʊs ɪˈsteɪt / (BrE)
the total value of all assets owned by an individual at the time of their death before deductions or liabilities are subtracted

Examples

The gross estate value
Executors must appraise all assets to establish the exact value of the gross estate.
Her gross estate included several properties, stocks, and valuable artwork.
The calculation of the gross estate is essential for determining estate tax liability.
