Enuki

noun
Babylonian earth spirits or genii
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɛˈnuːki / (AmE), / ɛˈnuːki / (BrE)
Any of a group of powerful earth spirits or genii in Babylonian mythology, believed to be servitors of the gods.

Examples

The Enuki spirits
Ancient texts describe the Enuki as intermediaries between mortals and the gods.
Legends say the Enuki held immense power over the fertility of the land.
The priests would invoke the Enuki during certain religious ceremonies in Babylon.
