co-sponsor

verb
to jointly support
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkoʊˈspɑːnsər / (AmE), / ˌkəʊˈspɒnsə / (BrE)
to support or organize an event, project, or activity together with one or more people or groups

Examples

To co-sponsor an event
Three organizations agreed to co-sponsor the charity marathon to raise more funds.
The two senators decided to co-sponsor the new education bill in parliament.
Local businesses often co-sponsor community events to increase their visibility and support good causes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: oppose - to act against or not support something
  • synonym: sponsor - to support or organize something by providing resources or endorsement
