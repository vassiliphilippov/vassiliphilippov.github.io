matzah meal

noun
coarse flour from ground matzos
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑtsə ˌmiːl / (AmE), / ˈmætsə ˌmiːl / (BrE)
a coarse flour made from ground matzos, used especially in Jewish cooking during Passover

Examples

Matzah meal flour
During the holiday, matzah meal replaces regular flour in many traditional recipes.
For the dumplings, add two cups of matzah meal to the mixture and stir well.
She used matzah meal instead of breadcrumbs to make the meatballs suitable for Passover.

Related Terms

  • synonym: matzo - unleavened bread traditionally eaten during Passover
