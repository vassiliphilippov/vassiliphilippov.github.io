Helen

proper noun
mythical Greek beauty; cause of Trojan War
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛlən / (AmE), / ˈhɛlən / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the beautiful daughter of Zeus and Leda whose abduction by Paris led to the Trojan War.

Examples

Mythical Helen of Troy
According to the myth, Helen was taken to Troy by Paris, sparking a legendary conflict.
Many poets have described Helen as the most beautiful woman in the world.
The story of Helen and the Trojan War has inspired countless works of literature and art.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Helen of Troy - another name for Helen, emphasizing her association with Troy
