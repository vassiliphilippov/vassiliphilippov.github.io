orphanage

noun
public institution for orphans
Pronunciation: / ˈɔɹ.fən.ɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈɔː.fən.ɪdʒ / (BrE)
a public institution where children whose parents are deceased or unable to care for them are cared for and housed

Examples

A city orphanage
After losing his parents, Tom was sent to an orphanage in the city.
She volunteered at an orphanage during the summer, helping care for the children.
The charity raises funds to support the local orphanage and its children.

Related Terms

  • synonym: children's home - residential institution for children without parental care
  • antonym: foster home - a private home offering care for children unable to live with their parents
