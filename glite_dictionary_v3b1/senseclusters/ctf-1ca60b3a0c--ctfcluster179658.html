CTF

noun
UK children's savings account
Pronunciation: / si ti ɛf / (AmE), / siː tiː ɛf / (BrE)
a UK government savings account for children, called a Child Trust Fund, used to save money until adulthood

Examples

A CTF account
Her parents opened a CTF when she was born to help her save for the future.
You can only access the money in your CTF when you turn eighteen.
Many families use a CTF to encourage children to learn about saving money.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ISA - a UK tax-free savings account (Individual Savings Account)
  • synonym: savings account - a bank account for saving money
