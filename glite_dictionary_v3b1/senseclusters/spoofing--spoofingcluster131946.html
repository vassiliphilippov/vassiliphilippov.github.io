spoofing

noun
market manipulation tactic
9.6
Pronunciation: /ˈspuː.fɪŋ/ /; / /ˈspʊ.fɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈspuː.fɪŋ/ /; / /ˈspʊ.fɪŋ/ (BrE)
the illegal practice of placing and quickly canceling large buy or sell orders to create a false impression of demand or supply in financial markets

Examples

Market spoofing scheme
The trader was banned for engaging in spoofing to manipulate stock prices.
Financial regulators are increasing efforts to detect spoofing in electronic trading platforms.
Authorities charged the firm with spoofing after uncovering a series of fake orders.

Related Terms

  • synonym: front running - trading based on advance knowledge of pending orders
  • synonym: market manipulation - actions intended to deceive or mislead in financial markets
