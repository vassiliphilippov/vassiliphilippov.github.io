Athabaskan

noun
Indigenous group of North America
Pronunciation: / ˌæθəˈbæskən / (AmE), / ˌaθəˈbaskən / (BrE)
A member of any of the Indigenous North American peoples who speak an Athabaskan language, primarily inhabiting subarctic regions of western Canada and central Alaska.

Examples

An Athabaskan person
Linguists study the origins of Athabaskan languages to trace the migration patterns of these groups.
Many Athabaskan communities continue to practice traditional hunting and fishing in Alaska.
The Athabaskan peoples have a rich oral history conveyed through storytelling.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Athabaskan language - a family of related Indigenous languages spoken in North America
