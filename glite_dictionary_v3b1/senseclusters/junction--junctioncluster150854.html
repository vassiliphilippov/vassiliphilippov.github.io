junction

noun
point or manner of joining
3.9 2.5
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒʌŋk.ʃən/ (AmE), /ˈdʒʌŋk.ʃən/ (BrE)
a place or point where two or more things are joined or connected (such as roads, rivers, or railway lines)

Examples

Road junction
A junction between the two rivers creates a wide delta.
The railway junction was vital for transporting goods between cities.
We stopped at the junction of Main Street and Oak Avenue.
At the junction of the river and the stream, the water flows faster.
The company built a metal junction to link the two pipes together.
The new highway includes a major junction connecting three different routes.
The new railway junction improved transportation between the two towns.
The traffic was backed up at the junction where the two highways meet.
There is a junction ahead; take the road on the left for the city center.

Related Terms

  • synonym: connection - a link or relationship between things
  • synonym: intersection - the point where two things, such as roads, cross each other
  • antonym: separation - the action or state of moving or being moved apart
