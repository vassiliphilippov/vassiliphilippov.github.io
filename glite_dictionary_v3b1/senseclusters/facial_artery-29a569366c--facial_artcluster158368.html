facial artery

noun
artery supplying blood to the face and neck
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈfeɪʃəl ˈɑːr.t̬ə.ri / (AmE), / ˈfeɪʃəl ˈɑː.tə.ri / (BrE)
an artery that arises from the external carotid artery and supplies blood to the structures of the face and neck

Examples

The facial artery supplies blood
An injury near the jawline can sometimes impact the facial artery and cause heavy bleeding.
During surgery, care must be taken not to damage the facial artery.
The facial artery supplies oxygenated blood to many parts of the face.
