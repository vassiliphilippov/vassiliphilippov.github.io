overpower

verb
defeat or control with superior force
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvɚˈpaʊɚ /; / ˌoʊvərˈpaʊər / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈpaʊə / (BrE)
to defeat or gain control over someone or something by using greater strength, force, or influence

Examples

To overpower an enemy
She was quickly overpowered by sleep after the long journey.
The security guards managed to overpower the intruder before he could escape.
The soldiers tried to overpower their opponents with sheer numbers.
The guards quickly overpowered the intruder before he could escape.
She was overpowered by the strong smell of chemicals in the laboratory.
The team overpowered their opponents with superior tactics and strength.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overwhelm - to defeat completely; to have a strong emotional effect
  • synonym: subdue - to bring under control, especially by force
  • antonym: succumb - to fail to resist; to be defeated by
  • antonym: surrender - to give up or yield to another
