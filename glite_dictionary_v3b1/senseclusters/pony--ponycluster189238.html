pony

verb
to pay reluctantly
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊni / (AmE), / ˈpəʊni / (BrE)
to pay or contribute money, especially when it is required or expected, often reluctantly

Examples

To pony up money
After much hesitation, he finally ponied up the money for his share of the bill.
You will have to pony up if you want to join the group trip this summer.
She refused to pony up the extra fee for express shipping.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contribute - to give money or resources for a common purpose
  • synonym: pay - to give money in exchange for something
  • antonym: withhold - to refuse to give something that is due
