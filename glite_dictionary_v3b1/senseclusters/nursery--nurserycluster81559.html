nursery

noun
child care center
1.0 2.96/m
Pronunciation: / Ààn…úrs…ôri / (AmE), / Ààn…úÀês…ôri / (BrE)
a place where young children are cared for and taught before they start school (such as a childcare center or preschool)

Examples

Children's nursery
She enrolled her son in a local nursery so he could play with other children.
The nursery provides a safe and fun environment for toddlers during the day.
Many parents visit the nursery before deciding where to send their child.
Many parents drop their children off at the nursery before going to work.
The nursery sells a wide variety of young trees and flowers for your garden.
She decorated the baby's nursery with colorful animals and soft toys.

Synonyms

  • preschool
    1.3 12/m
    preschool offers structured early education for ages three to five, while nursery cares for and teaches children before school.
  • nursery school
    1.6 1.12/m
    nursery school prepares very young children for primary classes with planned lessons, while nursery more broadly cares and teaches before school.
  • preprimary
    3.9 0.01/m
    preprimary is a formal program focused on early learning before primary school, while nursery can be less formal and also offer care.
  • day nursery
    2.2 0.01/m
    day nursery cares for children during the day while parents work, whereas nursery also emphasizes early teaching before school.
  • daycare
    1.0 4.32/m
    daycare gives daytime supervision and care mainly for working parents, while nursery adds early learning to prepare children for school.
  • creche
    5.7 0.10/m
    creche looks after babies and very young children during the day, while nursery suggests wider care and early education before school.

Related Terms

  • synonym: daycare - a place where children are cared for during the day while parents are at work
  • synonym: greenhouse - a building for growing plants in controlled conditions
  • synonym: kindergarten - a school or class for young children, usually before they start primary school
