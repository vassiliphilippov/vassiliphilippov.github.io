Examples

An unreasonable request
She found his requests completely unreasonable and refused to comply.
The rent increase was unreasonable given the state of the property.
They were charged an unreasonable amount for basic services.
He made an unreasonable demand for immediate payment.
It would be unreasonable to expect her to finish all that work in one day.
Some landlords set unreasonable rents that tenants cannot afford.

Synonyms

  • excessive
    3.6 20/m
    excessive means too much in amount or degree, while unreasonable often implies a choice or demand that lacks good sense or fairness.
  • undue
    6.0 4.32/m
    undue is a formal word for more than is proper or deserved, while unreasonable is more general and may feel unfair.
  • inordinate
    7.7 1.93/m
    inordinate means far more than is normal or needed, while unreasonable covers any action or demand that lacks good sense.
  • unwarranted
    7.0 2.62/m
    unwarranted means not justified or needed, while unreasonable can mean unfair or not based on good sense.
