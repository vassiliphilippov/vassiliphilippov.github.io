tantalizing

adjective
arousing unattainable desire or curiosity
8.3 5.1
Pronunciation: /ˈtæn.tə.laɪ.zɪŋ/ /; / /ˈtæn.ə.laɪ.zɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈtæn.tə.laɪ.zɪŋ/ /; / /ˈtæn.ə.laɪ.zɪŋ/ (BrE)
arousing curiosity or desire by being very attractive or tempting, especially when something is hard to get

Examples

A tantalizing offer
She caught a tantalizing glimpse of the ocean through the trees.
The aroma of freshly baked bread was tantalizing to the hungry children.
The job offer was tantalizing, but ultimately too good to be true.
She caught a tantalizing glimpse of the painting through the open door.
The tantalizing smell of freshly baked bread filled the kitchen.
The chef's new dish looked tantalizing on the menu, making it hard to resist.
Despite his tantalising promises, the deal never actually materialized.
She felt a tantalising sense of adventure after reading his mysterious letter.
The aroma of fresh bread was tantalising to those waiting outside the bakery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alluring - powerfully and mysteriously attractive or fascinating
  • synonym: teasing - provoking or tormenting by keeping something desired just out of reach
  • synonym: tempting - attractive or appealing (tempting the senses or desires)
  • antonym: unappealing - not attractive or pleasing
  • antonym: unattainable - impossible to achieve or obtain
