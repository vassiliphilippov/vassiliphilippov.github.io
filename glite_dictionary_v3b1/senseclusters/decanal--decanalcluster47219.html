decanal

noun
fragrance aldehyde
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛkəˌnæl / (AmE), / ˈdɛkənæl / (BrE)
a colorless liquid aldehyde with a strong citrus-like odor, used in perfumes and flavorings

Examples

Fresh decanal scent
The chemist added decanal to the mixture to create a fresh citrus aroma.
Many luxury perfumes contain decanal for its distinctive scent.
Food scientists use decanal to enhance the flavor of certain citrus products.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aldehyde - an organic compound containing a formyl group
  • degree lower: nonanal - an aldehyde with nine carbon atoms, used in fragrances
  • degree higher: undecanal - an aldehyde with eleven carbon atoms, used in perfumes
