hormone-replacement therapy

noun
medical treatment for menopause symptoms
3.7
Pronunciation: / ˈhɔːr.moʊn rɪˈpleɪsmənt ˈθɛr.ə.pi / (AmE), / ˈhɔː.məʊn rɪˈpleɪsmənt ˈθer.ə.pi / (BrE)
a treatment in which hormones such as estrogen and progestin are administered to postmenopausal women, often to reduce symptoms of menopause or lower the risk of certain conditions like osteoporosis

Examples

Hormone-replacement therapy for menopause
Doctors sometimes prescribe hormone-replacement therapy to reduce the risk of osteoporosis in postmenopausal women.
Many women consider hormone-replacement therapy to manage hot flashes during menopause.
She discussed the potential side effects of hormone-replacement therapy with her physician before starting treatment.
