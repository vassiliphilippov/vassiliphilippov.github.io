immobility

noun
state of not moving
Pronunciation: / ˌɪmoʊˈbɪlɪti /; / ɪˌmoʊˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɪməʊˈbɪlɪti /; / ɪˌməʊˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the state of not moving or being unable to move; lack of motion

Examples

Body immobility
Immobility during long flights can increase the risk of blood clots.
The patient's immobility led to muscle weakness over time.
The statue's sense of immobility gave it a quiet, solemn presence.
His immobility during meditation helped him concentrate deeply.
Snow and ice caused the complete immobility of vehicles on the mountain roads.
The doctor was concerned about the patient's prolonged immobility after surgery.
