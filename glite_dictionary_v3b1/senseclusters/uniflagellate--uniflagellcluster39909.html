uniflagellate

noun
single-flagellum organism
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌjuːnɪˈflædʒəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪˈflædʒɪlət / (BrE)
a cell or organism that possesses a single flagellum for movement or sensory functions

Examples

A uniflagellate organism
The scientist observed a uniflagellate swimming quickly across the slide.
Each uniflagellate uses its single flagellum to propel itself through the liquid.
Some algae are classified as uniflagellate because they have only one flagellum.

Related Terms

  • antonym: biflagellate - a cell or organism with two flagella
  • degree higher: flagellate - a cell or organism with one or more flagella
