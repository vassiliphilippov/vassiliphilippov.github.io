woody-stemmed

adjective
having a stem made of woody tissue
Pronunciation: / ˈwʊdi stɛmd / (AmE), / ˈwʊdi stɛmd / (BrE)
(of a plant) having a stem consisting chiefly of wood or woody tissue; not herbaceous.

Examples

Woody-stemmed plants
Most trees and shrubs are woody-stemmed and can survive harsh winters.
Roses are woody-stemmed perennials that grow best in well-drained soil.
The botanist explained that woody-stemmed plants typically live longer than herbaceous ones.

Related Terms

  • antonym: herbaceous - (of a plant) having non-woody stems
