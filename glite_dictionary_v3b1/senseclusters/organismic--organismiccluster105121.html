organismic

adjective
relating to an organism as a whole
Pronunciation: / ˌɔrɡəˈnɪzmɪk /; / ɔːrˈɡænɪzmɪk / (AmE), / ˌɔːɡəˈnɪzmɪk /; / ɔːˈɡænɪzmɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of an organism or living being considered as a whole, rather than its individual parts

Examples

Organismic perspective
His theory takes an organismic perspective, looking at society as a unified entity.
Many biologists argue for an organismic view of ecological communities, treating them as integrated wholes.
The organismic approach in psychology emphasizes studying the whole person rather than isolated parts.
The organismic approach to psychology considers the whole person rather than just individual parts.
Researchers studied the organismic responses of plants to environmental stress.
The organismic structure of the ecosystem supports its resilience.
