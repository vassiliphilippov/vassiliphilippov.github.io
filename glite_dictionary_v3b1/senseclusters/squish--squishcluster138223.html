squish

verb
to press or crush
3.2
Pronunciation: /skwɪʃ/ (AmE), /skwɪʃ/ (BrE)
to press, squeeze, or crush something so it becomes soft, flat, or out of shape, often making a soft or wet sound

Examples

To squish a bug
She loved to squish the clay between her fingers while making sculptures.
If you squish the sandwich too hard, all the filling will come out.
The grapes squished under his feet as he walked through the vineyard.
She likes to squish the clay between her fingers when making pottery.
If you squish the sandwich too hard, the filling will spill out.
The children laughed as they squished the mud with their boots in the garden.
She accidentally squished the sandwich in her backpack, making it flat and messy.
If you squish the clay too much, it will lose its shape.
The child laughed as he squished the mud between his fingers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crush - to press something so hard that it breaks or loses its shape
  • synonym: flatten - to make something flat or flatter
  • antonym: inflate - to fill something with air or gas so that it becomes larger
  • synonym: squeeze - to press something firmly together
