assimilation

noun
sound change to match nearby sound (linguistics)
Pronunciation: / əˌsɪməˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / əˌsɪmɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
in linguistics, the process by which a sound becomes more like a neighboring sound in pronunciation

Examples

Sound assimilation
In rapid speech, assimilation often causes 'handbag' to be pronounced as 'hambag'.
Many languages exhibit assimilation when certain consonants are placed together.
The assimilation of the 'n' sound in 'input' makes it sound like 'imput'.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dissimilation - the process by which two similar sounds in a word become less similar
