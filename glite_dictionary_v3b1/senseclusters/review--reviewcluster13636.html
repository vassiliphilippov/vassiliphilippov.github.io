review

verb
critically assess or evaluate
2.9 31/m
Pronunciation: / r…™Ààvju / (AmE), / r…™ÀàvjuÀê / (BrE)
to examine or assess something critically, especially in order to give an opinion or evaluation about its quality, value, or significance

Examples

To review a movie
Before publication, experts review scientific papers for accuracy.
He will review my performance and provide constructive feedback.
She was asked to review the latest novel for the magazine.

Synonyms

  • evaluate
    3.2 56/m
    evaluate means to judge value or quality carefully, while review involves examining and giving an overall opinion or evaluation.
  • assess
    3.0 67/m
    assess means to estimate value, quality, or ability, while review is a more formal or public critical evaluation that gives an opinion.
  • critique
    6.7 3.77/m
    critique gives a detailed, often critical analysis of merits and faults, while review gives a broader opinion on quality.
  • appraise
    4.8 1.02/m
    appraise means to judge monetary or market value, while review judges quality, meaning, or significance and may appear in reports or articles.
  • scrutinize
    6.2 6.45/m
    scrutinize means to examine something very closely to find errors or small details, while review aims to assess and give an opinion.
  • examine
    2.5 120/m
    examine means to look at or study something carefully to find information, while review includes judging and giving an opinion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: critique - to evaluate or analyze critically
  • antonym: praise - to express approval of (the opposite of criticizing in a review)
