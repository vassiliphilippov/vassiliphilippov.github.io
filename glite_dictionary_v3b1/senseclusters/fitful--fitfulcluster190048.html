fitful

adjective
occurring in irregular bursts
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪtfəl / (AmE), / ˈfɪtfʊl / (BrE)
happening in irregular bursts, starting and stopping rather than being continuous

Examples

A fitful breeze
A fitful breeze stirred the leaves, never blowing for long.
After a fitful night's sleep, she woke feeling more tired than before.
The campaign progressed in fitful bursts, with periods of intense activity followed by lulls.
His fitful efforts at studying meant he rarely finished his assignments on time.
She endured a fitful sleep, waking up every hour throughout the night.
The fitful wind made sailing difficult for the small boat.

Related Terms

  • antonym: continuous - without interruption or break
  • synonym: intermittent - occurring at intervals; not continuous
  • synonym: spasmodic - occurring in sudden, brief bursts; irregular
  • antonym: steady - regular and continuous; not fitful
