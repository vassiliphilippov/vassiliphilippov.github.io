striated muscle cell

noun
cell in striated muscle tissue
Pronunciation: / straɪˌeɪtɪd ˈmʌsəl sɛl / (AmE), / straɪˌeɪtɪd ˈmʌsl sɛl / (BrE)
an elongated cell found in striated muscle tissue, capable of contracting and responsible for voluntary movement

Examples

A striated muscle cell contracts
A striated muscle cell can contract rapidly to generate movement in the body.
Each striated muscle cell contains multiple nuclei located along its length.
Under the microscope, the distinctive bands of a striated muscle cell are clearly visible.

  • synonym: skeletal muscle fiber - muscle cell in skeletal muscle tissue
