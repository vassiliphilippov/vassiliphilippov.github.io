Benin franc

noun
former currency of Benin
Pronunciation: / bəˈniːn ˈfræŋk / (AmE), / bəˈniːn ˈfræŋk / (BrE)
the former basic unit of currency in Benin, used before adoption of the West African CFA franc

Examples

The Benin franc currency
After independence, Benin stopped using the Benin franc and adopted the CFA franc.
Before 1945, the Benin franc was the country's official currency.
Collectors sometimes seek old Benin francs for their numismatic value.

Related Terms

  • synonym: franc - the basic monetary unit of several countries, originally France
