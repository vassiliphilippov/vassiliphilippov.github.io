insider information

noun
confidential corporate information not released to the public
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌsaɪdɚ ˌɪn.fɚˈmeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈɪn.saɪ.də ˌɪn.fəˈmeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
confidential information about a company's plans or condition that is not publicly available, often used illegally for personal advantage

Examples

Insider information about stocks
He was accused of using insider information to buy stocks before a major company merger.
Possession of insider information gives certain individuals an unfair advantage in the stock market.
The regulator is investigating allegations of insider information being shared with external investors.
