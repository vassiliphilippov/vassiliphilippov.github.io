instrumentalize

verb
to make instrumental
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnstrəˈmɛntəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌɪnstrəˈmɛntəlaɪz / (BrE)
to arrange a song or piece of music so that it is performed without vocals, as an instrumental version

Examples

To instrumentalize a song
The band decided to instrumentalize their latest single for the upcoming film soundtrack.
After removing the lyrics, the producer instrumentalized the song for a relaxing background track.
Many artists choose to instrumentalize popular songs to showcase their musical skills.

Related Terms

  • antonym: vocalize - to add vocals to a piece of music
