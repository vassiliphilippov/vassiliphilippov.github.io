viperfish

noun
deep-sea predatory fish
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈvaɪpərˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈvaɪpəˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a deep-sea fish with long, sharp teeth and a light-producing organ used to attract prey

Examples

The viperfish with teeth
The viperfish is famous for its needle-like teeth and ability to live in the ocean's depths.
Scientists study the viperfish to learn how it uses bioluminescence to hunt in darkness.
A viperfish can open its mouth wide to catch prey much larger than itself.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anglerfish - a deep-sea fish with a bioluminescent lure to attract prey
  • synonym: lanternfish - a small deep-sea fish known for its light-producing organs
