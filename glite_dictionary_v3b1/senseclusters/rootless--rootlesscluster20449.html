rootless

adjective
not requiring root privileges (computing)
7.7 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààrutl…ôs / (AmE), / ÀàruÀêtl…ôs / (BrE)
(in computing) running without, or not requiring, root (superuser) privileges; of software, containers, or processes designed to operate without administrative rights.

Examples

Rootless process
Many modern containers are run rootless to avoid giving apps root access.
The security team recommended deploying rootless containers to reduce the blast radius of a breach.
Developers tested the application in a rootless namespace to ensure compatibility without elevated privileges.

Related Terms

  • antonym: privileged - having administrative or elevated permissions, often including root access.
  • synonym: unprivileged - not granted administrative or superuser privileges; lacking elevated permissions.
