grisaille

noun
painting or glasswork in grey shades
Pronunciation: / ɡrɪˈzaɪ / (AmE), / ɡrɪˈzaɪ / (BrE)
A style of painting, or stained glass work, executed entirely in shades of grey or another neutral color, often to imitate the appearance of relief sculpture.

Examples

Grisaille painting style
Many Renaissance altarpieces feature elaborate grisaille in their predella sections.
She admired the stained glass window, noting its subtle grisaille technique.
The artist completed the central panel in delicate grisaille to emphasize its sculptural quality.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chiaroscuro - the use of light and shadow to achieve a sense of volume in painting
