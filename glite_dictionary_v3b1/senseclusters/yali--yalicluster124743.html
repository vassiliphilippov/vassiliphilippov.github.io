yali

noun
mythical temple guardian
Pronunciation: / ˈjɑːli / (AmE), / ˈjɑːli / (BrE)
a mythical creature, often lion-like or composite, used as a guardian figure in South Indian temple architecture

Examples

Temple yali statue
The ancient temple was guarded by a stone yali at each entrance.
Artists spent years carving the fierce face of the yali into the temple pillars.
Visitors admired the detailed sculptures of the yali along the temple walls.
