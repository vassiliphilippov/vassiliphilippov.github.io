wicking

noun
drawing liquid by capillarity
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of drawing liquid through a material by capillary action, often to keep surfaces dry

Examples

Wicking process
Wicking fabric helps keep athletes dry during exercise.
The wicking of water through the soil can help plants get moisture from deeper layers.
Modern sportswear uses wicking technology to move sweat away from the skin.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absorption - the process of taking in a liquid
  • antonym: repellency - the ability to resist liquid penetration
