melanogenesis

noun
production of melanin
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛlənoʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌmiːlənəʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (BrE)
the biological process by which melanin pigment is produced in living organisms

Examples

Melanogenesis in skin
Ultraviolet light can increase melanogenesis in the skin, leading to tanning.
Certain genetic conditions can affect the normal process of melanogenesis.
Researchers study melanogenesis to better understand skin disorders like vitiligo.

Related Terms

  • antonym: depigmentation - loss or reduction of pigment in tissues
  • synonym: pigmentation - the coloring of tissues by pigment
