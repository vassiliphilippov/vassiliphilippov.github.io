antiqua

noun
serif typeface style
9.9
Pronunciation: / ænˈtiːkwə / (AmE), / ænˈtiːkwə / (BrE)
a style of serif typeface used in printing, especially as opposed to blackletter typefaces

Examples

Antiqua typeface style
The designer chose antiqua for the headings to give the magazine a classic look.
Early printed books in Europe often used antiqua instead of the traditional blackletter style.
Modern publishers sometimes prefer antiqua fonts for their readability and elegance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blackletter - a style of script with dense, angular letters used in early printing
  • synonym: serif - a typeface with small lines at the ends of characters
