thresher

noun
a shark with a long tail
Pronunciation: / ˈθrɛʃər / (AmE), / ˈθrɛʃə / (BrE)
a large shark of warm seas, known for its long, whiplike tail used to herd and stun small fish

Examples

Thresher shark
A thresher can use its powerful tail to stun schools of fish.
Divers were excited to spot a thresher near the coral reef.
Many fisheries try to protect the endangered thresher from overfishing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: thresher shark - another term for the same large shark with a long tail
