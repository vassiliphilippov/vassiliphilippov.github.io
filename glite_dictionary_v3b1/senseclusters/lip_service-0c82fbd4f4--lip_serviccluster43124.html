lip service

noun
insincere support or agreement
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪp ˌsɜrvɪs /; / lɪp ˈsɜrvɪs / (AmE), / ˈlɪp ˌsɜːvɪs /; / lɪp ˈsɜːvɪs / (BrE)
an insincere expression of support or agreement that is not backed by real actions

Examples

Lip service to causes
He paid lip service to environmental issues but never supported real change.
Politicians sometimes give lip service to reform without taking meaningful action.
The company's commitment to diversity turned out to be just lip service.
Politicians often give lip service to healthcare reform without passing meaningful laws.
The company paid lip service to environmental issues while continuing to pollute the river.
We need more than just lip service; we need real change in this organization.

Related Terms

  • antonym: action - the process of doing something to achieve a result
  • synonym: empty promise - a promise made without intention of keeping it
  • synonym: empty words - insincere or meaningless talk
  • synonym: tokenism - the practice of making only a perfunctory or symbolic effort
