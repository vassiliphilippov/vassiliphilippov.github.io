slow motion

noun
technique where action appears slower than real time
1.4 3.2
Pronunciation: / sloʊ ˈmoʊʃən / (AmE), / sləʊ ˈməʊʃən / (BrE)
a filming or playback technique in which action appears to happen more slowly than in real life, achieved by recording at a faster frame rate and playing at normal speed

Examples

Slow motion video
In slow motion, every detail of the athlete's movement was clearly visible.
Many action movies use slow motion to create dramatic effect during fight scenes.
The car crash was replayed in slow motion to analyze what happened.
