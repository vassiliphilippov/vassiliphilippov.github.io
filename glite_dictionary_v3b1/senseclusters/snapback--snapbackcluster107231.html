snapback

noun
automatic reinstatement
8.2
Pronunciation: /ˈsnæp.bæk/ (AmE), /ˈsnæp.bæk/ (BrE)
a provision or mechanism that automatically reinstates a previous rule or measure, such as sanctions, when certain conditions are met

Examples

Snapback clause
The treaty included a snapback clause to quickly restore sanctions if the agreement was violated.
Lawmakers debated whether a snapback mechanism would be effective in enforcing compliance.
If the country fails to meet its obligations, the snapback provision will automatically reimpose restrictions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reinstatement - the act of restoring something to its previous state
  • antonym: removal - the act of taking something away or ending it
