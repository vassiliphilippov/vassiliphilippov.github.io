to make a problem, situation, or negative feeling worse or more severe

Examples

To exacerbate problems
Cutting funding to education will only exacerbate the state's problems.
Stress can exacerbate symptoms of certain medical conditions.
The new law could exacerbate the existing housing crisis in the city.
Attempts to fix the old machine sometimes exacerbate the issues rather than solve them.
Her harsh words seemed to exacerbate his anxiety instead of calming him.
Raising taxes now would only exacerbate the country's economic problems.

Synonyms

  • worsen
    3.9 5.43/m
    worsen is a common, simple verb for making a bad situation worse, while exacerbate sounds more formal and clinical.
  • aggravate
    4.6 4.54/m
    aggravate means make a problem more severe in speech and writing, while exacerbate is slightly more formal and stronger.
  • compound
    6.9 5.97/m
    compound often suggests several problems add together and become worse, while exacerbate focuses on making one problem more severe.
  • escalate
    5.5 12/m
    escalate often shows a situation rising step by step into greater danger, while exacerbate stresses worsening of a problem.
  • deepen
    3.6 6.61/m
    deepen means feelings or problems grow stronger or more intense, while exacerbate means they become worse in a harmful way.
  • add to
    1.8 61/m
    add to describes increasing size or amount of something, while exacerbate specifically means making a problem or pain worse.
