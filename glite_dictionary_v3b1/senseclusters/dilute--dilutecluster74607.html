dilute

verb
make a liquid less concentrated
Pronunciation: / daɪˈluːt / (AmE), / dɪˈluːt / (BrE)
to make a liquid or solution weaker or less concentrated by adding water or another solvent

Examples

To dilute paint
If the paint is too thick, you can dilute it to get the desired consistency.
She decided to dilute the juice with some water for the children.
They had to dilute the solution before using it in the experiment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concentrate - to make a liquid or substance stronger by removing water or another diluting agent
  • synonym: weaken - to reduce in strength, force, or intensity
