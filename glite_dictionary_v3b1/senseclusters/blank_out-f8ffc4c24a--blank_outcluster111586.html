blank out

verb
temporarily forget something
1.9
Pronunciation: / blæŋk aʊt / (AmE), / blæŋk aʊt / (BrE)
to be suddenly unable to remember or think of something; to experience a mental block

Examples

To blank out a memory
He blanked out his address when asked by the police.
I completely blanked out during the test and forgot everything I had studied.
Whenever she feels nervous, she tends to blank out on important facts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: forget - to be unable to recall or remember
