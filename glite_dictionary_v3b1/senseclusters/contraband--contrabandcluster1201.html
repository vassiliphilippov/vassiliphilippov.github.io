contraband

adjective
illegally distributed or sold
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːn.trəˌbænd / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.trə.bænd / (BrE)
(of goods) distributed, imported, or sold illegally; prohibited by law from being traded.

Examples

Contraband cigarettes sold
Shops selling contraband electronics often operate in secret.
Some contraband cigarettes were found hidden in the shipment.
The police seized several boxes of contraband alcohol during the raid.

Related Terms

  • synonym: illegal - forbidden by law
  • antonym: licit - permitted by law
