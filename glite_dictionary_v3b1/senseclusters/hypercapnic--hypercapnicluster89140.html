hypercapnic

adjective
high blood carbon dioxide
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˈkæpnɪk / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəˈkæpnɪk / (BrE)
having or relating to an abnormally high level of carbon dioxide in the blood

Examples

A hypercapnic patient
The doctor noted that the patient was hypercapnic after reviewing the blood gas results.
People with chronic lung disease can become hypercapnic if their breathing worsens.
Emergency treatment was needed when the child became hypercapnic during the asthma attack.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hypocapnic - having an abnormally low level of carbon dioxide in the blood
