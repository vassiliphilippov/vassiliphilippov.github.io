chelate compound

noun
compound with metal ion bonded in a ring structure
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈkiːleɪt kəmˌpaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈkiːleɪt kəmˌpaʊnd / (BrE)
A chemical compound in which a metal ion is bonded to a ring structure formed by at least two coordinate bonds from other atoms (usually nonmetals) in the molecule.

Examples

A chelate compound structure
Researchers synthesized a new chelate compound that can remove toxic metals from wastewater.
The antibiotic EDTA is a well-known chelate compound used to bind metal ions in the body.
The stability of a chelate compound depends on the strength of the bonds formed between the ligand and the metal ion.
