handwringing

noun
excessive worry
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈhændˌrɪŋɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈhændˌrɪŋɪŋ / (BrE)
an excessive or nervous display of worry or anxiety, often shown by gestures or anxious talk

Examples

Investor handwringing
There was a lot of handwringing among investors after the sudden drop in stock prices.
Instead of handwringing, the team decided to focus on finding practical solutions to the problem.
The mayor urged citizens to avoid unnecessary handwringing and remain calm during the crisis.
Her constant handwringing during the interview showed how nervous she was.
The news of the accident led to much handwringing among the family members.
He tried to hide his handwringing when he heard the unexpected results.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anxiety - a feeling of worry or unease
  • antonym: calm - a state of being relaxed and not worried
  • antonym: calmness - a state of being relaxed and free from anxiety
  • synonym: fidgeting - making small movements due to nervousness or impatience
