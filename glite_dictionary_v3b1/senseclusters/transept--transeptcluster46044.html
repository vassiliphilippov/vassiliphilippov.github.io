transept

noun
side arm of a cruciform church
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtræn.sɛpt / (AmE), / ˈtræn.sɛpt / (BrE)
A part of a church building that crosses the main body at right angles, forming the arms of a cross-shaped (cruciform) layout.

Examples

The transept of church
The transept creates a cross-shaped floor plan, common in Gothic cathedrals.
The choir gathered in the south transept to rehearse before the service.
Tourists admired the stained glass windows in the church's west transept.
