hatch

noun
shading with lines in drawing or engraving
3.6 6.1
Pronunciation: /hætʃ/ (AmE), /hætʃ/ (BrE)
A method of shading in drawing or engraving, consisting of multiple parallel or crossing lines to create tone or texture.

Examples

Fine hatch in art
His sketches were covered with intricate hatch to indicate light and form.
In wood engraving, a careful pattern of hatch can suggest subtle areas of shadow.
The artist used fine hatch to give depth and texture to the portrait.

Related Terms
