parsimoniously

adverb
economically; simply
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːrsəˈmoʊniəsli / (AmE), / ˌpɑːsɪˈməʊniəsli / (BrE)
in a way that uses the fewest resources, assumptions, or elements necessary; simply and economically.

Examples

Explain parsimoniously
The theory was developed parsimoniously, relying on only a few basic principles.
She decorated her apartment parsimoniously, choosing only essential furniture and simple designs.
The report was written parsimoniously, avoiding unnecessary details and focusing on the main findings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: economically - in a way that avoids waste or excess
  • antonym: extravagantly - in a way that uses more resources than necessary
  • synonym: simply - in a straightforward or uncomplicated way
