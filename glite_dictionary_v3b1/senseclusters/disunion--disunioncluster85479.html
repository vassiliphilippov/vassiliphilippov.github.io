disunion

noun
the ending of unity or agreement
5.1
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈjunjən / (AmE), / dɪsˈjuːnjən / (BrE)
The ending or breakdown of unity or agreement; the state of being separated or divided.

Examples

The disunion of groups
Political differences led to the eventual disunion of the board members.
The civil war threatened the disunion of the country.
The novel explores the personal effects of family disunion on the protagonist.

Related Terms

  • synonym: division - the act of separating into parts or groups; separation
  • antonym: unity - the state of being united or joined as a whole
