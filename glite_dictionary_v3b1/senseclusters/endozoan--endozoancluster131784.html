endozoan

noun
internal animal parasite
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˌɛndoʊˈzoʊ.ən / (AmE), / ˌɛndəʊˈzəʊ.ən / (BrE)
a parasite that lives inside the internal organs of an animal, especially intestinal worms

Examples

An endozoan worm
Intestinal roundworms are a common type of endozoan that affect both humans and animals.
Scientists discovered a new endozoan species residing in the digestive tract of fish.
Treatment must target the endozoan without harming the host animal.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ectozoan - a parasite that lives on the surface of an animal
