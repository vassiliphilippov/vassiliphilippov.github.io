sweat gland

noun
skin organ that produces sweat
4.7 2.3
Pronunciation: /swet glænd/ (AmE), /swet glænd/ (BrE)
a small organ in the skin that produces and releases sweat (perspiration) to help regulate body temperature

Examples

The sweat gland duct
Each sweat gland is connected to the surface of the skin by a tiny duct.
Excessive activity of the sweat gland can cause problems like hyperhidrosis.
The human body contains millions of sweat glands to help it cool down.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sebaceous gland - gland in the skin that secretes oily matter (sebum)
