laser-assisted subepithelial keratomileusis

noun
laser eye surgery for vision correction
8.4
Pronunciation: /ˈleɪ.zər ə.ˈsɪs.tɪd sʌb.ep.ɪ.ˈθiː.li.əl ke.rə.toʊ.maɪ.ˈljuː.sɪs/ /; / /ˈleɪ.zər ə.ˈsɪs.təd sʌb.ep.ɪ.ˈθiː.li.əl ke.rə.toʊ.maɪ.ˈljuː.sɪs/ (AmE), /ˈleɪ.zə ə.ˈsɪs.tɪd sʌb.ep.ɪ.ˈθiː.li.əl ke.rə.təʊ.maɪ.ˈljuː.sɪs/ /; / /ˈleɪ.sə ə.ˈsɪs.tɪd sʌb.ep.ɪ.ˈθiː.li.əl ke.rə.təʊ.maɪ.ˈljuː.sɪs/ (BrE)
a refractive surgical procedure in which a laser reshapes the cornea after creating a hinged flap in the eye's epithelial layer

Examples

Laser-assisted subepithelial keratomileusis surgery
After years of wearing glasses, she decided to have laser-assisted subepithelial keratomileusis to correct her vision.
Doctors often recommend laser-assisted subepithelial keratomileusis as an alternative to LASIK for certain patients.
The recovery time after laser-assisted subepithelial keratomileusis can be slightly longer than with other laser eye surgeries.
