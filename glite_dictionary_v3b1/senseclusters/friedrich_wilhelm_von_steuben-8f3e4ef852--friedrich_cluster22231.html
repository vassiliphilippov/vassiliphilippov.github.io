Friedrich Wilhelm von Steuben

proper noun
Prussian officer who trained American troops in the Revolutionary War
Pronunciation: / ˈfraɪdrɪk ˈvɪlhɛlm fʌn ˈʃtɔɪbən / (AmE), / ˈfriːdrɪx ˈvɪlhɛlm fɔn ˈʃtɔɪbən / (BrE)
(1730–1794) Prussian-born military officer who served as inspector general and major general of the Continental Army during the American Revolutionary War, helping to train and discipline American troops under George Washington.

Examples

Friedrich Wilhelm von Steuben military leader
Friedrich Wilhelm von Steuben played a crucial role in transforming the Continental Army at Valley Forge.
Many historians credit Friedrich Wilhelm von Steuben with introducing effective drills and discipline to American troops.
The military manual written by Friedrich Wilhelm von Steuben became the standard for the U.S. Army for decades.
