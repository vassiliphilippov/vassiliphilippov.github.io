subpixel

noun
pixel component
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌpɪksəl / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌpɪksəl / (BrE)
a single colored component, such as red, green, or blue, that combines with others to form a full pixel in a digital display

Examples

A red subpixel
Each subpixel emits a different color to help create the full range of hues on the screen.
The arrangement of subpixels can influence the sharpness and clarity of a digital display.
Engineers analyzed the subpixel structure to improve the monitor's color accuracy.
