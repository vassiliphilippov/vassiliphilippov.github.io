chronic obstructive pulmonary disease

noun
serious lung disease causing breathing difficulty
2.5 3.6
Pronunciation: /ˈkrɑːn.ɪk əb.ˈstrʌk.tɪv ˈpʌl.mə.nər.iː dɪ.ˈziːz/ (AmE), /ˈkrɒn.ɪk əb.ˈstrʌk.tɪv ˈpʌl.mə.nər.iː dɪ.ˈziːz/ (BrE)
a serious, progressive lung disease, usually caused by smoking, that makes it hard to breathe and is a combination of emphysema and chronic bronchitis.

Examples

Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease symptoms
People with chronic obstructive pulmonary disease often experience shortness of breath during daily activities.
Smoking is the leading cause of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease worldwide.
There are treatments available to help manage the symptoms of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chronic bronchitis - a long-term inflammation of the bronchi in the lungs
  • synonym: emphysema - a lung condition involving damage to the air sacs
