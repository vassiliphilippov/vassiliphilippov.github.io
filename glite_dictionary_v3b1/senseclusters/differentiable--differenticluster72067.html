differentiable

adjective
able to be distinguished from something else
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃɪəbl̩ / (BrE)
able to be distinguished or recognized as different from something else; capable of being differentiated.

Examples

Differentiable species
Species with similar markings may not be differentiable without genetic testing.
The two samples are easily differentiable under a microscope.
Their results showed that the two populations were not differentiable based on appearance alone.

Related Terms

  • synonym: distinct - recognizably different in an obvious way
  • antonym: indistinguishable - not able to be distinguished or perceived as different
