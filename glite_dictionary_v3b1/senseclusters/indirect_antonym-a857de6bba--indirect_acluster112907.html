indirect antonym

noun
an antonym related through an intermediate word
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.daɪˈrɛkt ˈæn.tə.nɪm / (AmE), / ˌɪn.dɪˈrɛkt ˈæn.tə.nɪm / (BrE)
an antonym whose opposition to another word is mediated by an intermediate relationship, rather than being directly opposed

Examples

Indirect antonym relationship
In his essay, he discussed how 'happy' and 'miserable' could be considered indirect antonyms through their links to 'unhappy'.
Teachers sometimes introduce indirect antonyms to show complex word relationships beyond straightforward opposites.
The word 'parched' is an indirect antonym of 'wet' because its opposition is mediated by the word 'dry'.
