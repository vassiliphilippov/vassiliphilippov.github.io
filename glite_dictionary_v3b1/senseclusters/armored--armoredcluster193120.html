armored

adjective
having protective natural covering (for animals)
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːrmɚd /; / ˈɑːr.mɚd / (AmE), / ˈɑːməd /; / ˈɑː.məd / (BrE)
(of animals) having a natural protective covering such as scales, plates, or shells, serving as armor.

Examples

Armored dinosaur
Many dinosaurs, such as the Ankylosaurus, were heavily armored to defend against predators.
The armored armadillo rolls into a ball when threatened.
The pangolin is an armored mammal covered in hard, overlapping scales.
Many dinosaurs, such as the ankylosaurus, were armoured for protection.
The armoured armadillo rolls into a ball to defend itself from predators.
The armoured scales of the pangolin make it difficult for most animals to harm it.
