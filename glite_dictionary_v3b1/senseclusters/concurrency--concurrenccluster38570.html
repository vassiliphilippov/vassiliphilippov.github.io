concurrency

noun
simultaneous occurrence of events or actions
Pronunciation: / kənˈkɝːənsi / (AmE), / kənˈkʌrənsi / (BrE)
the state of two or more actions, events, or circumstances occurring or acting together at the same time

Examples

Concurrency of events
His research focuses on the concurrency of economic and social changes in the region.
The unexpected concurrency of several emergencies placed the hospital staff under great pressure.
There was a remarkable concurrency between technological innovation and legal reform at that time.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sequence - a following of one action after another
  • synonym: simultaneity - the state of happening at the same time
