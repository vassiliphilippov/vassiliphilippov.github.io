bend sinister

noun
heraldic diagonal band (top right to bottom left)
Pronunciation: / bɛnd ˈsɪnɪstər / (AmE), / bɛnd ˈsɪnɪstə / (BrE)
a diagonal band running from the upper right to the lower left of a shield in heraldry, sometimes used to indicate illegitimacy or as decoration

Examples

A bend sinister on shield
In heraldry, a bend sinister runs from the shield's upper right to its lower left.
The family crest features a bend sinister, indicating a historical lineage of illegitimacy.
The knight's shield bore a prominent bend sinister across a field of blue.
In heraldic traditions, a bend sinister may also simply be a decorative device without further meaning.
The family coat of arms featured a bend sinister as a central element.
The presence of a bend sinister in the shield was interpreted by some as a sign of illegitimacy.
