hyperconfidence

noun
excessive self-confidence
8.7
Pronunciation: /haɪ.pər.ˈkɑːn.fɪ.dəns/ /; / /ˈhaɪ.pər.kən.fɪ.dəns/ (AmE), /haɪ.pə.ˈkɒn.fɪ.dəns/ /; / /ˈhaɪ.pə.kən.fɪ.dəns/ (BrE)
a state of having much more confidence than is reasonable or justified; being overly sure of oneself

Examples

Extreme hyperconfidence
The manager's hyperconfidence led the team to take unnecessary risks during the project.
Despite his lack of experience, his hyperconfidence made him believe he could not fail.
Investors were wary of the entrepreneur's hyperconfidence in his untested business model.
His hyperconfidence led him to underestimate the risks involved.
The team's hyperconfidence after their last victory made them careless in the next match.
She approached the interview with such hyperconfidence that she ignored important preparation.
