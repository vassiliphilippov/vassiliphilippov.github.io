sublimate

verb
change from solid to vapor directly
2.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbləˌmeɪt /; / ˈsʌb.ləˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsʌblɪmeɪt /; / ˈsʌb.lɪ.meɪt / (BrE)
to change directly from a solid to a vapor (or cause this), or to purify a substance by this process (chemistry/physics)

Examples

To sublimate dry ice
In dry air, solid carbon dioxide will sublimate rather than melt.
Some chemists use special chambers to sublimate purer forms of certain compounds.
When heated, iodine crystals can sublimate and form a purple vapor.
During freeze-drying, the water in the food sublimates from ice directly to vapor.
The chemist sublimated the compound to purify it, collecting the crystals after condensation.
When heated, iodine can sublimate and then deposit again as crystals on a cool surface.
Some salts can easily sublimate under reduced pressure to separate impurities.
The laboratory technician needed to sublimate the compound before analysis.
To create a pure sample, the chemist chose to sublimate the solid mixture using heat.

Related Terms

  • synonym: purify - to remove impurities; to make pure
  • synonym: sublimation - the process of changing directly from a solid to a vapor
