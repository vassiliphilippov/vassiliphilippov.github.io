water table

noun
underground level of saturated soil or rock
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtɚ ˌteɪbəl / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌteɪbəl / (BrE)
The level below the ground where the soil or rocks are fully saturated with water.

Examples

The water table level
After several weeks of heavy rain, the water table rose significantly in the region.
Drilling a new well requires careful measurement of the water table depth.
Farmers monitor the water table to ensure there is enough moisture for crops during dry seasons.

Related Terms

  • synonym: groundwater - water held underground in the soil or in pores and crevices in rock
