Hosea

proper noun
Hebrew prophet; Old Testament book
9.9
Pronunciation: /hoʊ.ˈziː.ə/ /; / /hoʊ.ˈzeɪ.ə/ /; / /hoʊs.ˈiː.ə/ (AmE), /həʊ.ˈziː.ə/ /; / /həʊ.ˈzeɪ.ə/ /; / /həʊs.ˈiː.ə/ (BrE)
A Hebrew prophet of the 8th century BC, who called for repentance among the people of Israel and condemned idolatry; also, the title of a book in the Old Testament named after him.

Examples

Prophet Hosea
Hosea warned the Israelites about the consequences of idolatry.
In Sunday school, we learned about the prophet Hosea and his messages of repentance.
Many scholars study the book of Hosea to understand the history of ancient Israel.
