Allegheny spurge

noun
semi-evergreen perennial herb, ground cover plant
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌæləˈɡeni spɜrdʒ / (AmE), / ˌæləˈɡɛni ˈspɜːdʒ / (BrE)
a low, semi-evergreen perennial herb (Pachysandra procumbens) with small spikes of white or pinkish flowers, native to the southern United States and commonly grown as ground cover elsewhere

Examples

Allegheny spurge plant
Gardeners often choose Allegheny spurge for its attractive foliage and low maintenance needs.
The shady garden bed was filled with Allegheny spurge, creating a lush green carpet.
Unlike many ground covers, Allegheny spurge thrives in dry, wooded areas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Japanese spurge - a related ground cover plant (Pachysandra terminalis), common in gardens
