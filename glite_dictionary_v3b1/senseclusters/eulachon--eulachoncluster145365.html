eulachon

noun
Pacific coast fish
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈjuːləkɒn /; / ˈjuːləkən / (AmE), / ˈjuːləkɒn /; / ˈjuːləkən / (BrE)
a small, oily fish from the Pacific coast of North America, valued for its oil and as food, especially by Indigenous peoples

Examples

The eulachon fish
The eulachon was an important food source for many Indigenous communities along the Pacific coast.
Traders valued eulachon oil for its use in cooking and as a source of light.
During the spring run, families would gather to catch and process eulachon for the coming year.
The annual migration of the eulachon is an important event for many coastal communities.
Traditionally, people rendered oil from the eulachon for cooking and trade.
Scientists are studying the declining numbers of eulachon in several river systems.
