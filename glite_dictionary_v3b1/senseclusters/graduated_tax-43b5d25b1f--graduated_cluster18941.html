A tax system in which the tax rate increases as the amount subject to taxation increases.

Examples

A graduated tax system
Many economists argue that a graduated tax system is fairer to lower-income individuals.
The country implemented a graduated tax to address income inequality.
Under a graduated tax, higher earners pay a higher percentage of their income.
