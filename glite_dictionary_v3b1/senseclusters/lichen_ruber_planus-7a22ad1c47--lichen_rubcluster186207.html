lichen ruber planus

noun
skin disease with purplish itchy bumps
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪkən ˈruːbər ˈpleɪnəs / (AmE), / ˈlɪkən ˈruːbə ˈpleɪnəs / (BrE)
A skin disease characterized by shiny, flat-topped, purplish, and usually itchy bumps, commonly affecting the wrists, forearms, and thighs.

Examples

lichen ruber planus skin disease
Patients with lichen ruber planus often experience persistent itching and discomfort.
The dermatologist diagnosed her rash as lichen ruber planus after a thorough examination.
Treatment for lichen ruber planus may include topical corticosteroids to relieve symptoms.
