Carpaccio

proper noun
Italian Renaissance painter
Pronunciation: / kɑrˈpɑːtʃioʊ / (AmE), / kɑːˈpætʃiəʊ / (BrE)
Vittore Carpaccio (c. 1465–1525) was an Italian Renaissance painter known for his detailed narrative cycles and religious scenes.

Examples

Carpaccio paintings
The museum's exhibit included several panels by Carpaccio, illustrating Venetian life in rich detail.
Carpaccio is celebrated for his vivid storytelling and use of color in religious paintings.
Art historians often study Carpaccio to understand the culture of Renaissance Venice.
