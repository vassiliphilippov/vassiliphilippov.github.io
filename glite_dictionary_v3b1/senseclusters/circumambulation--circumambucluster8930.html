circumambulation

noun
indirect approach
9.9
Pronunciation: /sɜːr.kəm.æm.bjʊ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /sɜː.kəm.æm.bjʊ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
an indirect or roundabout way of speaking or dealing with a subject, often to avoid the main point

Examples

Political circumambulation
The politician's circumambulation frustrated reporters who wanted a clear answer.
Instead of addressing the issue directly, she resorted to circumambulation in her explanation.
His circumambulation of the topic made it difficult to understand his true opinion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: circumlocution - the use of many words to avoid stating something directly
  • antonym: directness - the quality of being straightforward and clear
