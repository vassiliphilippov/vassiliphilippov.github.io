Sphinx

noun
mythical winged creature with woman's head and lion's body
9.1
Pronunciation: / sfɪŋks / (AmE), / sfɪŋks / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a winged creature with a woman's head and breast on a lion's body, famous for posing riddles to travelers.

Examples

The Sphinx in mythology
According to legend, the Sphinx asked travelers a riddle and devoured those who could not answer it.
Artists throughout history have depicted the Sphinx in statues, paintings, and literature.
The Sphinx is a symbol of mystery and enigma in many cultures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chimera - a fire-breathing monster from Greek mythology with lion, goat, and serpent features
