cephalopod mollusk

noun
marine mollusk with tentacles, e.g., octopus
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛfələˌpɑd məˈlʌsk / (AmE), / ˌsɛfələˌpɒd məˈlʌsk / (BrE)
A marine mollusk distinguished by a well-developed head, prominent eyes, and arms or tentacles with suckers, including creatures such as octopuses, squids, and cuttlefish.

Examples

A cephalopod mollusk animal
Many cephalopod mollusks change color rapidly to communicate or blend into their surroundings.
Scientists discovered a new species of cephalopod mollusk in the deep ocean trench.
The octopus is a remarkable cephalopod mollusk known for its intelligence and ability to camouflage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: octopus - a soft-bodied, eight-limbed cephalopod mollusk
  • synonym: squid - a fast-swimming cephalopod with eight arms and two tentacles
