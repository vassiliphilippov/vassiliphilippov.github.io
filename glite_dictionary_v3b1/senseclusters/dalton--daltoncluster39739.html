dalton

noun
atomic mass unit
Pronunciation: / ˈdɔltən / (AmE), / ˈdɔːltən / (BrE)
a unit of mass used to express atomic and molecular weights, equal to one atomic mass unit

Examples

One dalton unit
The molecular weight of water is about 18 daltons.
Scientists measured the protein's mass as 64,000 daltons using mass spectrometry.
A single hydrogen atom has a mass close to one dalton.

Related Terms

  • synonym: atomic mass unit - a standard unit of mass for atoms and molecules
  • antonym: gram - a metric unit of mass equal to one thousandth of a kilogram
