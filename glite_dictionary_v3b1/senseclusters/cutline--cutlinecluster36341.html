cutline

noun
cleared land strip
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌtˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkʌtˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a strip of land cleared of vegetation to provide access, act as a firebreak, or allow surveying and exploration.

Examples

Forest cutline
The surveyors marked a cutline through the dense forest to map the new property boundary.
Firefighters quickly created a cutline to help stop the wildfire from spreading.
A narrow cutline allowed the team to transport equipment deep into the wilderness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: firebreak - a gap in vegetation that stops the spread of fire
  • synonym: trail - a path or track through a forest or countryside
