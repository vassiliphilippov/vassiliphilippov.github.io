limber

adjective
able to move or bend easily
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪmbɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɪmbə / (BrE)
able to move, bend, or be bent easily; flexible (used for people, bodies, or objects)

Examples

Limber body
As a gymnast, she needs to keep her body limber to perform difficult routines.
Even after years of dancing, he remains remarkably limber for his age.
Yoga practice helps me stay limber and reduce muscle stiffness.
She preferred limber plastic tubing for her art project because it was so easy to bend.
The limber metal wire could be twisted easily into different shapes.
Unlike the old, brittle pipe, the new one was much more limber and pliant.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flexible - able to bend easily without breaking; adaptable
  • antonym: rigid - unable to bend or be forced out of shape; not flexible
  • antonym: stiff - unable to move easily; not supple
