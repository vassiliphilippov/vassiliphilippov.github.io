dropsical

adjective
swollen from fluid accumulation
9.8 9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈdrɑːp.sɪ.kəl/ /; / /ˈdrɔːp.sɪ.kəl/ (AmE), /ˈdrɒp.sɪ.kəl/ (BrE)
swollen with an excessive accumulation of fluid, especially as a result of disease (historically, related to dropsy); resembling dropsy.

Examples

A dropsical abdomen
A dropsical abdomen is a classic sign of advanced liver disease.
Historical novels often described characters with dropsical features due to poor health.
The patient's legs appeared dropsical, indicating a buildup of fluid.

Related Terms
