reflooding

noun
flooding again
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈflʌdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈflʌdɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or process of flooding an area again, especially after it has previously dried out or been drained

Examples

The reflooding of wetlands
The reflooding of the wetlands helped restore local wildlife habitats.
After years of drought, the government initiated the reflooding of the river basin.
Environmentalists supported the reflooding project to bring back native plant species.

Related Terms

  • antonym: drainage - the process of removing water from an area
  • synonym: flooding - the covering of land with water, especially from a river or rain
