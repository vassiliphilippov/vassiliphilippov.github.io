SDS

noun
laboratory detergent
Pronunciation: / ˌɛs diː ˈɛs / (AmE), / ˌɛs diː ˈɛs / (BrE)
a chemical compound, sodium dodecyl sulfate, used as a detergent and to break up proteins in laboratory and industrial settings

Examples

SDS solution
The researchers added SDS to the solution to help dissolve the cell membranes.
Before running the gel, the technician mixed the protein sample with SDS to denature it.
Industrial cleaners often contain SDS because of its strong ability to remove oils and fats.

Related Terms

  • synonym: detergent - a cleaning agent that removes dirt and oil
  • synonym: surfactant - a substance that reduces surface tension between liquids or a liquid and a solid
