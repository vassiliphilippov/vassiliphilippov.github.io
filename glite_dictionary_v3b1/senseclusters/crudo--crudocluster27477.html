crudo

adjective
raw, uncooked (food)
Pronunciation: / ˈkruːdoʊ / (AmE), / ˈkruːdəʊ / (BrE)
served raw and uncooked, especially referring to fish or meat prepared with simple seasoning or dressing.

Examples

Crudo fish appetizer
The chef recommended the tuna crudo as a light appetizer before the main course.
At the new restaurant, I tried a delicious beef crudo with capers and lemon zest.
She prefers her seafood crudo, simply dressed with olive oil and herbs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cooked - prepared by heating or cooking
  • synonym: raw - not cooked or processed
