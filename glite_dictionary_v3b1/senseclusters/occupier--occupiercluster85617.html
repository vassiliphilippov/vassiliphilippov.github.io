occupier

noun
person living in or using a place
4.1 0.26/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…ëÀêkj…ôÀåpa…™…ôr / (AmE), / Àà…íkj äÀåpa…™…ô / (BrE)
a person who lives in or uses a particular place, especially for a long time or as a resident

Examples

The occupier of the house
As the former occupier moved out, the apartment became available for rent.
Please write the name of the occupier on the tenancy agreement.
The current occupier of the house has lived there for over twenty years.

Synonyms

  • occupant
    3.3 6.27/m
    an occupant lives in or uses a space for a period, while occupier implies longer term or resident use.
  • resident
    1.8 78/m
    a resident lives in a place for a long time and feels settled, while occupier can also mean someone who uses it.
  • inhabitant
    2.8 14/m
    an inhabitant lives in a region or town as part of its population, while occupier focuses on using or holding a specific place.
  • dweller
    3.2 5.31/m
    a dweller lives in or at a place and often appears in phrases like 'city dweller', while occupier is more general or legal user.

Related Terms

  • synonym: resident - a person who lives somewhere permanently or on a long-term basis
  • synonym: tenant - a person who occupies land or property rented from a landlord
  • antonym: visitor - a person visiting a place, usually for a short time
