unpaired

adjective
having no matching partner; missing its pair
1.0
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈpɛrd / (AmE), / ʌnˈpeəd / (BrE)
(of something) being the remaining member of a pair, with its partner missing.

Examples

An unpaired shoe
After the storm, we were left with an unpaired shoe lying in the yard.
I found only one unpaired sock in the drawer after doing laundry.
She always seems to have an unpaired glove in her coat pockets.

Related Terms

  • synonym: odd - having no matching pair (as in an odd sock)
