chordotonal

adjective
insect sensory organs
Pronunciation: / ˌkɔːrdəˈtoʊnəl / (AmE), / ˌkɔːdəˈtəʊnəl / (BrE)
relating to specialized sensory organs in insects that detect vibrations or sound

Examples

Chordotonal sensory organs
Scientists study chordotonal organs to understand how insects sense vibrations in their environment.
The mosquito's chordotonal system allows it to detect the wingbeats of potential mates.
Damage to chordotonal organs can affect an insect's ability to respond to sound.
