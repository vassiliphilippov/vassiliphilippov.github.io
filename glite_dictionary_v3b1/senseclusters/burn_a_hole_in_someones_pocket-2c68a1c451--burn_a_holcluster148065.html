burn a hole in someone's pocket

verb
money creating urge to spend quickly
Pronunciation: / bɝn ə hoʊl ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈpɑkɪt / (AmE), / bɜːn ə həʊl ɪn ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈpɒkɪt / (BrE)
(of money) to make someone feel a strong urge to spend it quickly, often leading to imprudent spending.

Examples

Money burns a hole in her pocket
Money always burns a hole in her pocket whenever she goes shopping.
My allowance is burning a hole in my pocket and I can't wait to buy something new.
The bonus he received seemed to burn a hole in his pocket, so he spent it within days.

Related Terms

  • antonym: save one’s money - to avoid spending money unnecessarily
  • synonym: spendthrift - a person who spends money recklessly
