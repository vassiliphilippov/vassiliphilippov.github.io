cold weather

noun
a period of unusually cold weather
1.0
Pronunciation: / koʊld ˈwɛðɚ / (AmE), / kəʊld ˈwɛðə / (BrE)
A period during which the weather is unusually or uncomfortably cold.

Examples

A cold weather period
Farmers were concerned that the cold weather would damage the crops.
The city is not well prepared for prolonged cold weather events.
We stayed indoors during the cold weather to avoid catching a cold.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cold snap - a sudden brief spell of cold weather
  • antonym: hot weather - a period of unusually hot weather
