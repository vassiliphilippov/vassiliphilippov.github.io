feel

noun
tactile quality perceived by touch
1.8 5/m
Pronunciation: / fil / (AmE), / fiÀêl / (BrE)
the quality of something as perceived by touch; the tactile sensation it gives when handled or contacted

Examples

The feel of fabric
Each fabric has a different feel, some soft and some rough.
He liked the feel of the leather seats in the new car.
The feel of silk against your skin is smooth and luxurious.

Synonyms

  • tactile sensation
    4.9 0.02/m
    tactile sensation is the physical feeling from skin contact, while feel is the general quality of an object's touch.
  • touch sensation
    3.9 0.00/m
    touch sensation is the skin's immediate response when something contacts it, while feel describes how an object seems to touch.
  • tactual sensation
    7.4 0.00/m
    tactual sensation is a formal term for touch perceptions like pressure or heat, while feel is everyday description of touch quality.
  • texture
    3.4 12/m
