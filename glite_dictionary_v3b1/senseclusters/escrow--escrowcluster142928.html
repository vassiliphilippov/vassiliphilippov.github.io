escrow

verb
to hold in trust
6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈes.kroʊ/ (AmE), /ˈes.krəʊ/ (BrE)
to place money, documents, or property in the care of a third party until certain conditions are fulfilled

Examples

To escrow funds
The payment will be escrowed until all the terms of the agreement are met.
They escrowed the documents with a lawyer to ensure a fair transaction.
Funds are often escrowed during the sale of a house to protect both parties.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deposit - to place money in a bank or with a trusted party
  • antonym: release - to set free or hand over something held
