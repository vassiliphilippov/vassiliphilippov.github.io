piriform lobe

noun
pear-shaped brain region in olfactory system
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪrɪˌfɔrm loʊb / (AmE), / ˈpɪrɪfɔːm ləʊb / (BrE)
a pear-shaped area of the brain, part of the rhinencephalon, located on each side of the brain, involved in olfaction

Examples

The piriform lobe area
Damage to the piriform lobe can affect a person's sense of smell.
Researchers mapped neural pathways connecting the piriform lobe to other parts of the brain.
The piriform lobe is crucial in processing olfactory information in mammals.
