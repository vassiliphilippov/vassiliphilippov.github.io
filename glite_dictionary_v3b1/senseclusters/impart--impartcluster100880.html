impart

verb
communicate or transmit knowledge or skills
4.3 2.66/m
Pronunciation: / …™mÀàp…ërt / (AmE), / …™mÀàp…ëÀêt / (BrE)
to communicate or pass on knowledge, information, or skills to others

Examples

To impart knowledge
She took every opportunity to impart wisdom she had gained over the years.
Teachers have a duty to impart knowledge to their students.
The master chef was eager to impart his cooking skills to young apprentices.

Synonyms

  • pass on
    2.7 6.49/m
    pass on is often used for passing stories or knowledge between people, while impart sounds more formal and one-way.
  • convey
    3.4 23/m
    convey is a formal way to communicate ideas or feelings clearly, while impart focuses on giving knowledge or skills.
  • teach
    1.0 283/m
    teach gives direct instruction to help someone learn, while impart means to communicate knowledge without full teaching.
  • instruct
    2.6 7.55/m
    instruct tells someone exactly how to do something in a formal way, while impart covers passing on knowledge more generally.
  • educate
    2.2 53/m
    educate gives someone knowledge or skills over time through training, while impart is a quicker act of sharing knowledge.
  • transmit
    3.7 12/m
    transmit sends information or signals between places or devices, while impart is usually a human act of teaching or explaining.
  • pass along
    2.5 3.06/m
    pass along means to relay information from one person to another, while impart often implies teaching or careful explanation.
  • share
    1.0 43/m
    share is to tell or show something with others in a friendly way, while impart feels more formal and instructive.
  • give
    1.0 783/m
    give is a general word for handing or offering objects or help, while impart specifically means passing on knowledge or skills.

Related Terms

  • synonym: communicate - to share or exchange information, news, or ideas
  • synonym: convey - to make ideas, feelings, or information known to someone
  • antonym: withhold - to refuse to give something that is due or desired by another
