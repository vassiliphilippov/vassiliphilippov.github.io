overlight

verb
to make too bright
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈlaɪt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈlaɪt / (BrE)
to provide with too much light or illumination, making something excessively bright

Examples

To overlight a room
The photographer accidentally overlighted the scene, making the image too bright.
If you overlight your living room, it can feel harsh and uncomfortable.
The stage was overlighted, causing the actors to squint during the performance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: darken - to make less bright or more dim
  • synonym: illuminate - to light up or make brighter
  • synonym: overexpose - to expose to too much light, especially in photography
