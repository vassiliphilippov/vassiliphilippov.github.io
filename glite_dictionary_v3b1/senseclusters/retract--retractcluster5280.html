retract

verb
formally withdraw a statement or belief
1.6 4.9
Pronunciation: / rɪˈtrækt / (AmE), / rɪˈtrakt / (BrE)
to formally withdraw or disavow a statement, belief, or commitment, especially under pressure or official demand

Examples

To retract a statement
After facing public criticism, the politician decided to retract his earlier comments.
He was pressured by his superiors to retract his statement about the company's practices.
When confronted with new evidence, she had no choice but to retract her accusation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abjure - solemnly renounce a belief, cause, or claim
  • antonym: affirm - to state something as true firmly and publicly
  • synonym: recant - formally withdraw a previous statement or belief
