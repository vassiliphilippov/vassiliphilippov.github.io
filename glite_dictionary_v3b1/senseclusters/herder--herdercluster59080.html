herder

noun
person who drives a herd of animals
3.7 1.51/m
Pronunciation: / Ààh…úrd…ôr / (AmE), / Ààh…úÀêd…ô / (BrE)
A person whose job is to take care of and drive a herd of animals, such as cattle or sheep.

Examples

A herder of sheep
As a young herder, he learned to care for sheep in the mountains.
Many herders spend months away from home during grazing season.
The herder guided the cattle across the open plain.

Synonyms

  • herdsman
    5.0 0.25/m
    herdsman is a male worker who looks after and drives farm animals, while herder is a neutral job term for anyone.
  • drover
    9.4 0.27/m
    drover drives animals long distances between places, while herder cares for and watches over a herd at home or nearby.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cowboy - person who herds cattle, especially in the US
  • synonym: shepherd - person who herds sheep
