sideslip

noun
an unexpected sideways slide
8.3 6.9
Pronunciation: /ˈsaɪd.slɪp/ (AmE), /ˈsaɪd.slɪp/ (BrE)
an unexpected sideways slide, especially of a vehicle or aircraft, often caused by loss of traction or control

Examples

A car sideslip
A sudden gust of wind caused the cyclist to have a dangerous sideslip on the wet pavement.
During the storm, the airplane experienced a brief sideslip before landing safely.
The car went into a sideslip on the icy road, but the driver regained control quickly.

  • synonym: skid - a sudden uncontrolled slide, especially of a vehicle
