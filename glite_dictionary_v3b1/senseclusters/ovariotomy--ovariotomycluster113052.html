ovariotomy

noun
ovarian surgery
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvəriˈɑːtəmi / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəriˈɒtəmi / (BrE)
a surgical operation to remove part or all of an ovary, or to remove a tumor or cyst from an ovary

Examples

An ovariotomy operation
The doctor explained that an ovariotomy could remove the cyst while keeping the ovary intact.
After her ovariotomy, she recovered quickly and returned to her normal activities.
A minimally invasive ovariotomy was performed to treat the patient's ovarian tumor.
The surgeon performed an ovariotomy to treat the patient's ovarian tumor.
After the ovariotomy, the patient needed time to recover in the hospital.
Advances in medical technology have made ovariotomy a safer procedure than in the past.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hysterectomy - surgical removal of the uterus
  • synonym: oophorectomy - surgical removal of one or both ovaries
