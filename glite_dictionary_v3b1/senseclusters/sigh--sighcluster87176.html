sigh

verb
utter with a sigh
2.9 1.7
Pronunciation: /saɪ/ (AmE), /saɪ/ (BrE)
to say or express something by letting out a long, audible breath, often to show sadness, relief, or tiredness

Examples

To sigh a word
"It's over," Andy sighed, finally letting go of the tension he'd held all day.
He sighed his answer, barely audible over the hum of the air conditioner.
“I guess we’ll have to try again,” she sighed quietly, disappointment clear in her voice.

Related Terms

  • antonym: shout - to say something loudly
  • synonym: whisper - to speak very softly
