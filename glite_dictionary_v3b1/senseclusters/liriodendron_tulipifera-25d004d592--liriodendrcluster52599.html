Liriodendron tulipifera

noun
North American tulip tree species
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌlɪrioʊˈdɛndrən ˌtjuːlɪˈpɪfərə / (AmE), / ˌlɪrɪəˈdɛndrən ˌtjuːlɪˈpɪfərə / (BrE)
A tall North American deciduous tree with large tulip-shaped greenish-yellow flowers and conelike fruit, valued for its soft white wood used especially in cabinet making.

Examples

Tall Liriodendron tulipifera tree
A mature Liriodendron tulipifera can reach impressive heights, dominating the eastern North American forests.
Carpenters prize the wood of Liriodendron tulipifera for its softness and ease of use in furniture making.
The Liriodendron tulipifera is often admired for its striking, tulip-shaped flowers in spring.
