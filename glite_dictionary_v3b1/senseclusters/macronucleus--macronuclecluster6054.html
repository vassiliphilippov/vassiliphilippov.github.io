macronucleus

noun
large cell nucleus
Pronunciation: / ˌmækroʊˈnuːkliəs / (AmE), / ˌmækrəʊˈnjuːkliəs / (BrE)
the larger type of nucleus in certain single-celled organisms, responsible for controlling everyday cell functions

Examples

The macronucleus controls cells
The macronucleus in a paramecium regulates its everyday activities.
Scientists observed that the macronucleus divides differently from the micronucleus during cell reproduction.
Without a functioning macronucleus, the cell cannot perform its normal metabolic processes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: micronucleus - the smaller type of nucleus in some single-celled organisms, involved in reproduction
