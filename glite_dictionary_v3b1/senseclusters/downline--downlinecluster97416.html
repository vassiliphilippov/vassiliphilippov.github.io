downline

noun
descent guide rope
7.9
Pronunciation: /ˈdaʊn.laɪn/ (AmE), /ˈdaʊn.laɪn/ (BrE)
a rope, cable, or line used to lower or guide people or equipment down a steep or vertical descent, especially in diving or climbing

Examples

Diver holds the downline
The divers held onto the downline as they descended into the deep water.
Before lowering the equipment, the team secured it to the downline for safety.
Climbers often use a downline to control their descent on steep cliffs.

  • synonym: lifeline - a rope or line used for safety or support in climbing or diving
