detractive

adjective
reducing importance or value
Pronunciation: / dɪˈtræktɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈtræktɪv / (BrE)
causing a reduction in importance, value, or quality; diminishing.

Examples

Several detractive aspects
Critics pointed to several detractive aspects of the new policy.
The ongoing scandal had a detractive effect on the company's reputation.
Unstable economic factors can be detractive to long-term investment growth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: damaging - causing harm or injury
  • antonym: enhancing - increasing or improving in value or quality
