supernaturalism

noun
belief in supernatural forces
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərˈnætʃrəˌlɪzəm /; / suː.pɚˈnætʃ.rə.lɪ.zəm / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˈnætʃrəlɪzəm /; / ˌsuː.pəˈnætʃ.rə.lɪ.zəm / (BrE)
the belief that events or things can be caused by supernatural forces or beings, beyond what science or nature can explain

Examples

Belief in supernaturalism
Many religions are based on supernaturalism, attributing unexplained phenomena to higher powers.
She rejected supernaturalism in favor of scientific explanations for unusual events.
The debate between naturalism and supernaturalism has shaped philosophical thought for centuries.
His arguments were criticized for relying too heavily on supernaturalism instead of scientific explanation.
Some philosophies reject supernaturalism and rely solely on observation and reason.
The novel is filled with elements of supernaturalism, giving it an eerie atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • antonym: naturalism - belief that everything arises from natural properties and causes
  • synonym: supernatural - (adj.) attributed to some force beyond scientific understanding or the laws of nature
