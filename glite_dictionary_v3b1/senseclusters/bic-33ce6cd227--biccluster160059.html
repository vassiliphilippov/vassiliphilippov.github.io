BIC

noun
statistical model criterion
Pronunciation: / biː aɪ siː / (AmE), / biː aɪ siː / (BrE)
an abbreviation for the Bayesian Information Criterion, a statistical measure used to compare models by balancing fit and simplicity

Examples

The BIC value
The model with the lowest BIC was chosen as the best fit for the data.
Researchers often report both the AIC and BIC when comparing statistical models.
A lower BIC value suggests a better balance between model complexity and accuracy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: AIC - Akaike Information Criterion, a similar statistic for model comparison
