huk

noun
Jewish law commandment
9.9
Pronunciation: / huːk / (AmE), / huːk / (BrE)
a commandment in Jewish law whose reason is not explained or understood

Examples

A huk in Judaism
The prohibition against mixing wool and linen is considered a huk in Jewish tradition.
Many scholars debate the significance of each huk found in the Torah.
Some people follow every huk even if they do not understand its purpose.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mishpat - a commandment in Jewish law with an explained reason
  • synonym: mitzvah - a commandment or good deed in Jewish law
