candidiasis

noun
yeast infection caused by Candida
Pronunciation: / ˌkændɪˈaɪəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌkandɪˈaɪəsɪs / (BrE)
A fungal infection caused by yeasts of the genus Candida, especially Candida albicans, often affecting the mouth, skin, or genitals.

Examples

Candidiasis infection
Antibiotic use can sometimes increase the risk of developing candidiasis.
The doctor diagnosed her with oral candidiasis after noticing white patches in her mouth.
Treatment for candidiasis often involves antifungal medications.

Related Terms

  • synonym: thrush - oral form of candidiasis, especially in infants or immunocompromised persons
