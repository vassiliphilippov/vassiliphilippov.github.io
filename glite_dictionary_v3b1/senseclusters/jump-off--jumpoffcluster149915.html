jump-off

noun
starting point
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʌmp ˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌmp ˌɒf / (BrE)
the starting point or beginning of an activity, event, or competition

Examples

Marathon jump-off
The marathon's jump-off was crowded with excited runners and cheering fans.
We met at the jump-off before the hiking trip began early in the morning.
The coach gave a final pep talk at the jump-off of the tournament.

Related Terms

  • antonym: finish line - the line or place where a race or event ends
  • synonym: starting line - the line or place where a race or event begins
