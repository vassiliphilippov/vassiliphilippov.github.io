defeasance

noun
debt release arrangement
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfiːzəns / (AmE), / dɪˈfiːzəns / (BrE)
a financial arrangement in which assets are set aside to pay off or replace debt, removing legal claims or obligations on property

Examples

Defeasance of a loan
The company used defeasance to remove the mortgage lien from its property.
After arranging a defeasance, the borrower was no longer liable for the original loan.
Banks often recommend defeasance to clients seeking to clear property titles before selling.

Related Terms

  • antonym: foreclosure - the legal process of taking possession of a mortgaged property when the borrower fails to keep up payments
  • synonym: redemption - the act of repaying or buying back a debt or security
