Pelobatidae

noun
family of spadefoot toads
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛləˈbætɪdi / (AmE), / ˌpɛləˈbætɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of burrowing toads, commonly known as spadefoot toads, characterized by their spade-like projections on the hind feet used for digging.

Examples

Pelobatidae family toads
Researchers found several species in the Pelobatidae family while conducting fieldwork near the river.
The family Pelobatidae is distributed across parts of Europe, North Africa, and Western Asia.
Unlike most frogs, members of the Pelobatidae dig burrows using the spade-like structure on their feet.
