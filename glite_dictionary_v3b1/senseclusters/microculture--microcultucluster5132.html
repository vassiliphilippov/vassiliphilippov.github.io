microculture

noun
small distinct group
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪkroʊˌkʌltʃər / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊˌkʌltʃə / (BrE)
a small group within a larger culture that has its own distinct values, beliefs, or practices

Examples

Workplace microculture
The research team formed a unique microculture based on collaboration and open communication.
Each classroom can develop its own microculture that influences how students interact.
Social media platforms often give rise to a microculture with specific language and customs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mainstream - the dominant or widely accepted culture in a society
  • synonym: subculture - a cultural group within a larger culture, often with different beliefs or interests
