double-crossing

noun
an act of betrayal
Pronunciation: / ˌdʌbəlˈkrɔsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌdʌbəlˈkrɒsɪŋ / (BrE)
an act of betraying someone, especially after pretending to cooperate or be loyal

Examples

A double-crossing act
His double-crossing of his partner shocked everyone in the company.
She discovered the double-crossing before the plan could go any further.
Stories of double-crossing have always fascinated readers of crime novels.

Related Terms

  • antonym: loyalty - the quality of being loyal
