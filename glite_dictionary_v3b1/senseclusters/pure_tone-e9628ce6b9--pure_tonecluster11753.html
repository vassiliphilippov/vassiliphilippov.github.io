pure tone

noun
sound with only one frequency
Pronunciation: / pjʊr toʊn / (AmE), / pjʊə təʊn / (BrE)
a sound of a single, constant frequency without overtones or harmonics

Examples

A pure tone sound
A pure tone is produced by a tuning fork vibrating at a single frequency.
Audiologists often use a pure tone to test for hearing loss in patients.
The laboratory measured the response of the ear to a pure tone at 1000 Hz.

Related Terms

  • antonym: complex tone - a sound composed of a fundamental tone and its overtones
