Italian Renaissance

noun
period of cultural flourishing in Italy (14th–17th cent.)
4.7
Pronunciation: / ɪˈtæliən ˌrɛnəˈsɑns / (AmE), / ɪˈtæl.i.ən ˌrɛn.əˈsɑːns / (BrE)
the period of great cultural and artistic achievement in Italy that began in the 14th century and lasted into the 17th century, marking the early phase of the Renaissance movement in Europe

Examples

The Italian Renaissance period
During the Italian Renaissance, Florence became renowned for its art and architecture.
Many masterpieces of painting and sculpture were created in the Italian Renaissance.
The influence of the Italian Renaissance spread throughout Europe, inspiring artists and thinkers.
