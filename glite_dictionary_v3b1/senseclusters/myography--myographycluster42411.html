myography

noun
study of muscle activity
Pronunciation: / maɪˈɑːɡrəfi / (AmE), / maɪˈɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
the scientific study or recording of muscle activity, especially by using instruments to measure muscle contractions

Examples

Instrument myography recording
Researchers use myography to analyze how muscles respond to different types of exercise.
Advances in myography have improved our understanding of muscle diseases.
The laboratory specializes in myography to study muscle function in animals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: electromyography - the recording of electrical activity in muscles
  • synonym: neurology - the scientific study of the nervous system
