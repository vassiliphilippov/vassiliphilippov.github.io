spike suppressor

noun
device that protects against electrical surges
9.4
Pronunciation: / spaɪk səˈprɛsɚ / (AmE), / spaɪk səˈprɛsə / (BrE)
an electrical device installed in a power line to protect equipment from sudden fluctuations or surges in electrical current

Examples

A spike suppressor device
Heavy storms can cause fluctuations in current, so a spike suppressor is essential for sensitive equipment.
The technician installed a spike suppressor to safeguard the computer from power surges.
Without a spike suppressor, sudden voltage spikes might damage expensive electronics.
