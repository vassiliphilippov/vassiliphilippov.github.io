chloracne

noun
chemical-induced skin disease
9.9
Pronunciation: / klɔːrˈækni / (AmE), / klɔːˈrækni / (BrE)
a skin disease resembling severe acne, caused by exposure to certain toxic chlorinated chemicals

Examples

Severe chloracne rash
Prolonged exposure to industrial chemicals can lead to chloracne in workers.
Doctors diagnosed several patients with chloracne after a chemical spill at the factory.
Protective equipment is essential to prevent chloracne among employees handling toxic substances.

  • synonym: dermatitis - inflammation of the skin
