desperate

adjective
reckless or violent from urgency or despair
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛspɚɪt /; / ˈdɛsprət /; / ˈdɛspɚət / (AmE), / ˈdes.p(ə).rət /; / ˈdɛspərət / (BrE)
involving or showing risky, bold, or dangerous actions or situations because of extreme need, urgency, or lack of options

Examples

Desperate action
Faced with bankruptcy, he made a desperate attempt to rob a store.
The desperate fugitive risked everything to escape the police.
The desperate men threatened violence unless their demands were met.
He took a desperate risk, knowing it was his only chance of success.
In a desperate attempt to save the project, she worked through the night.
The team made a desperate effort to score before the final whistle blew.
In a desperate effort, the firefighters broke through the wall to rescue the trapped family.
The team made a desperate bid for victory in the final seconds of the game.
Their desperate courage during the crisis inspired the entire community.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cautious - being careful to avoid danger or risks
  • synonym: determined - having made a firm decision and being resolved not to change it
  • synonym: dire - extremely serious or urgent
  • synonym: heroic - showing great courage or bravery, especially in dangerous situations
  • synonym: hopeless - having no hope; despairing
  • synonym: reckless - showing a lack of care about danger or consequences
  • antonym: timid - lacking courage or confidence; easily frightened
