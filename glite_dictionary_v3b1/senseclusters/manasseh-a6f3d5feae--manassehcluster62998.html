Manasseh

proper noun
biblical king of Judah
9.4
Pronunciation: / məˈnæsə / (AmE), / məˈnæsə / (BrE)
a king of Judah in the Hebrew Bible, known for his long reign and for promoting idolatry

Examples

King Manasseh
The reign of Manasseh is described in detail in the Book of Kings.
Manasseh was criticized by prophets for encouraging idol worship in Judah.
According to tradition, Manasseh ruled longer than any other king of Judah.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Hezekiah - the father of Manasseh and a king of Judah in the Hebrew Bible
  • synonym: Josiah - the son of Manasseh and a king of Judah known for religious reforms
