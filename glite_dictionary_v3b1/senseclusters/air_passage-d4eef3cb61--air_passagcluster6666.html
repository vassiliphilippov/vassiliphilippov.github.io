air passage

noun
duct for ventilation, e.g. in mines
2.9 3.0
Pronunciation: /er ˈpæs.ɪdʒ/ /; / /er ˈpæs.ədʒ/ (AmE), /eə ˈpæs.ɪdʒ/ /; / /eə ˈpæs.ədʒ/ (BrE)
a duct or channel designed to allow ventilation, especially for supplying or removing air in places such as mines or buildings.

Examples

The air passage system
A network of air passages is essential for keeping mines safe and properly ventilated.
Dust often accumulates inside the air passage, reducing the efficiency of the ventilation system.
The engineer inspected the air passage for any blockages that might restrict airflow.
