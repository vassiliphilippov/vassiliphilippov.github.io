paper money

noun
government-issued currency in paper form
1.6 0.83/m

Money in the form of printed paper notes issued by a government or central bank, used as a medium of exchange instead of coins.

Examples

Paper money notes
Many countries switched from gold coins to paper money in the 20th century.
She always carries some paper money in case credit cards are not accepted.
When traveling abroad, you may need to exchange your paper money for local currency.

Synonyms

  • banknote
    3.0 1.01/m
    a banknote is the formal name for printed legal tender issued by a central bank, while paper money is the common general term.
  • paper currency
    2.5 0.23/m
    paper currency is a formal phrase for printed bank notes used as legal money, while paper money is the everyday name.
  • bill
    1.5 15/m
    a bill is a common word for a single paper note people carry and spend, while paper money is the general category.
  • folding money
    3.2 0.03/m
    folding money is an informal phrase for paper notes you fold and carry, while paper money is the neutral term.
  • government note
    3.7 0.01/m
    a government note is a formal name for paper money issued by a state's authority, while paper money is the common everyday term.
