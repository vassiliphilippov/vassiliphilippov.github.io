L-beam

noun
beam with an L-shaped cross-section
5.7
Pronunciation: /ˈel.biːm/ (AmE), /ˈel.biːm/ (BrE)
A structural beam with an L-shaped cross-section, used in construction and engineering.

Examples

An L-beam structure
Because of its shape, an L-beam can reinforce edges and frames effectively.
The engineer specified an L-beam for added support in the corner of the structure.
They used steel L-beams to assemble the framework of the warehouse.
