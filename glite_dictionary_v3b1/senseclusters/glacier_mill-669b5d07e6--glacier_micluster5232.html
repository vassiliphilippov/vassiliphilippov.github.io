glacier mill

noun
vertical shaft in a glacier formed by flowing water
6.6 8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈgleɪ.ʃər mɪl/ /; / /ˈglæ.ʃər mɪl/ (AmE), /ˈgleɪ.sjə mɪl/ /; / /ˈglæ.sjə mɪl/ (BrE)
a vertical or nearly vertical shaft in a glacier, formed by melting and erosion, through which surface water enters the glacier and may reach bedrock or the depth of crevasse formation

Examples

A glacier mill shaft
A sudden roar signaled water plunging into a hidden glacier mill deep within the glacier.
Scientists used ropes to descend into the glacier mill to study how meltwater travels beneath the ice.
The team mapped the network of glacier mills to better understand glacial melt and movement.
