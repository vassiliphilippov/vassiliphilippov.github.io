electrical skin response

noun
change in skin's electrical properties due to stress
4.5
Pronunciation: / əˈlɛktrɪkəl skɪn rɪˈspɑns / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪkəl skɪn rɪˈspɒns / (BrE)
a change in the electrical properties of the skin, typically caused by stress or anxiety, measured by skin resistance or small currents generated by the body

Examples

The electrical skin response test
An increase in electrical skin response can indicate heightened anxiety or excitement.
Researchers use electrical skin response technology to study emotional reactions in participants.
The polygraph measures the electrical skin response to determine if someone is stressed or anxious.

Related Terms

  • synonym: galvanic skin response - a related term for changes in skin resistance caused by psychological or physiological arousal
