foundling hospital

noun
institution caring for abandoned infants
Pronunciation: / ˈfaʊndlɪŋ ˌhɑːspɪtəl / (AmE), / ˈfaʊndlɪŋ ˌhɒspɪtəl / (BrE)
a hospital or institution where foundlings—infant children of unknown parents—are taken in and cared for.

Examples

A foundling hospital
During the Victorian era, many children were raised in a foundling hospital after being left by their parents.
The city's renowned foundling hospital provided shelter for countless abandoned infants each year.
The history of the foundling hospital reflects changing attitudes towards child welfare.
