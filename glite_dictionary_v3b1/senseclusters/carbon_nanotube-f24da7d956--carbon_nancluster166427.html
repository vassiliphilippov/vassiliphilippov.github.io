carbon nanotube

noun
cylindrical molecule made of carbon atoms
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑrbən ˈnænoʊtuːb / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbən ˈnanəʊtjuːb / (BrE)
a molecule composed of carbon atoms arranged in a long, hollow cylinder, known for its extraordinary strength and electrical properties

Examples

A carbon nanotube molecule
A single carbon nanotube can be stronger than steel yet much lighter in weight.
Researchers are exploring ways to use carbon nanotube fibers in flexible electronic devices.
Scientists discovered that carbon nanotubes have remarkable electrical conductivity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fullerene - a molecule made entirely of carbon, taking the form of a hollow sphere, ellipsoid, or tube
