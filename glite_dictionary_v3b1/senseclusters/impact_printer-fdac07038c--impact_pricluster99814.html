impact printer

noun
printer using print head striking method
Pronunciation: / ˈɪmpækt ˈprɪntər / (AmE), / ˈɪmpækt ˈprɪntə / (BrE)
a printer that produces text and images by striking an inked ribbon with a print head, leaving an impression on paper.

Examples

An impact printer machine
Many early ATMs were equipped with an impact printer to produce transaction receipts.
The office still uses an impact printer for printing multi-part forms.
Unlike inkjets, an impact printer is loud and can print through carbonless copies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dot matrix printer - a type of impact printer that forms characters using a matrix of dots
  • antonym: non-impact printer - a printer that does not use mechanical impact to print, such as inkjet or laser
