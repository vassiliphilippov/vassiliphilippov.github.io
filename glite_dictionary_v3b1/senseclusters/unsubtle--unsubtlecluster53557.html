unsubtle

adjective
not subtle; obvious
9.9
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈsʌtəl / (AmE), / ʌnˈsʌtəl / (BrE)
not subtle; obvious or lacking in delicacy or refinement.

Examples

An unsubtle reaction
His reaction to the surprise was quite unsubtle, laughing loudly and clapping his hands.
Some critics found the film's message too unsubtle to be truly effective.
The politician's unsubtle attempt to win votes was noticed by everyone in the room.

Related Terms

  • antonym: subtle - not obvious, delicate or understated
