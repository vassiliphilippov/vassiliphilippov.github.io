latent period

noun
time between stimulus and response
3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪtənt ˈpɪriəd /; / ˈleɪtnt ˌpɪriəd / (AmE), / ˈleɪt(ə)nt ˈpɪərɪəd /; / ˈleɪtnt ˌpɪəriəd / (BrE)
the period of time between when something happens (like a stimulus or exposure to a disease) and when the effects or symptoms appear, especially in science or medicine

Examples

The latent period in medicine
A short latent period is often seen in nerve response experiments.
During the latent period of the disease, no symptoms are apparent.
The latent period between the injection and the muscle contraction was measured accurately.
After exposure to the virus, the latent period can last several days before any symptoms are noticeable.
Researchers measured the latent period to determine how quickly the disease progresses after infection.
Understanding the latent period is crucial for controlling the spread of infectious diseases.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: incubation period - the time between exposure to an infection and the appearance of first symptoms
