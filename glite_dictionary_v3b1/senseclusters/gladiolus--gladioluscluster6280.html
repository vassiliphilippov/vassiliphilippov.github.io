gladiolus

noun
central part of the breastbone
Pronunciation: / ɡlædˈioʊləs / (AmE), / ɡlædɪˈəʊləs / (BrE)
the broad, central part of the breastbone (sternum), located between the manubrium above and the xiphoid process below

Examples

The gladiolus bone
A fracture of the gladiolus is rare but may occur with severe chest trauma.
The sternum is divided into the manubrium, the gladiolus, and the xiphoid process.
The surgeon made an incision just below the gladiolus to access the heart.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sternum - the breastbone, the long flat bone in the center of the chest
