weak spot

noun
a place of special vulnerability
2.7 1.06/m
Pronunciation: / wik sp…ët / (AmE), / wiÀêk sp…ít / (BrE)
a place or aspect of someone or something that is especially vulnerable or easy to defeat; a point of weakness

Examples

A weak spot in security
Hackers often search for a weak spot in a company's security system.
Her inability to delegate tasks proved to be the biggest weak spot in her leadership.
The team's defense has a clear weak spot that their opponents are trying to exploit.

Synonyms

  • weak point
    3.0 0.61/m
    weak point is a direct, common term for a vulnerable feature, while weak spot is the everyday phrase for such weakness.
  • weak part
    4.2 0.08/m
    weak part names a specific vulnerable section and sounds technical, while weak spot is more natural in spoken English.
  • soft spot
    4.4 0.26/m
    soft spot is often used to show emotional or physical vulnerability, while weak spot is a neutral term for any weakness.
  • chink in the armor
    7.4 0.10/m
    chink in the armor is an idiom for a small, hidden weakness, while weak spot is plain, general, and widely used.
  • soft underbelly
    6.7 0.09/m
    soft underbelly names a vulnerable part of an organization or plan that can be attacked, while weak spot is broader.
  • underbelly
    6.6 0.24/m
    underbelly describes the weakest, often hidden area of a system or place, while weak spot is a simple label for any weakness.
  • black spot
    5.3 0.02/m
    black spot refers to a trouble area or blemish on a place or record, while weak spot focuses on vulnerability.
