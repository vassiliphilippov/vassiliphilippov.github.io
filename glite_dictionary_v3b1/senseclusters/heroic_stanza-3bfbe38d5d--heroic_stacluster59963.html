heroic stanza

noun
four-line stanza in iambic pentameter, abab rhyme
6.6 7.8
Pronunciation: /hɪ.ˈroʊ.ɪk ˈstæn.zə/ (AmE), /hɪ.ˈrəʊ.ɪk ˈstæn.zə/ (BrE)
a stanza of four lines (quatrain) written in iambic pentameter with an abab rhyme scheme, often used in elevated or epic poetry

Examples

A heroic stanza poem
Her assignment was to write a poem in the form of a heroic stanza with an abab rhyme scheme.
Many eighteenth-century English poems employ the heroic stanza for their narrative passages.
The poet composed the opening lines in a heroic stanza to set an epic tone.
