mandate

verb
require officially by authority
Pronunciation: / ˈmæn.deɪt /; / ˈmænˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈmæn.deɪt / (BrE)
to officially require something to be done or to give someone official authority to do something

Examples

To mandate new rules
The company is mandating new security protocols for all employees.
The government mandated the use of seat belts in all vehicles.
The school board mandates annual health screenings for students.
The board mandated a thorough review of all safety procedures.
The constitution mandates that elections be held every four years.
The new policy mandates regular training for all employees.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authorize - to give official permission or approval
  • antonym: forbid - to order not to do something
  • antonym: permit - to allow or make something possible
  • synonym: require - to demand something as necessary
