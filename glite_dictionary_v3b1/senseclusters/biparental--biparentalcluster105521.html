biparental

adjective
from both parents
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪpəˈrɛntəl / (AmE), / ˌbaɪpəˈrɛntəl / (BrE)
involving or relating to both parents, especially in genetics, reproduction, or care of offspring

Examples

Biparental inheritance
In most animals, genetic traits are inherited in a biparental manner from both the mother and the father.
The study focused on biparental care, where both parents contribute to raising the offspring.
Scientists observed a rare case of biparental inheritance of mitochondrial DNA in the experiment.
In some species, biparental care increases the survival rate of the offspring.
The research compared biparental and uniparental family structures in mammals.
Birds that exhibit biparental feeding tend to have healthier chicks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: parental - relating to a parent or parents
  • antonym: uniparental - involving only one parent
