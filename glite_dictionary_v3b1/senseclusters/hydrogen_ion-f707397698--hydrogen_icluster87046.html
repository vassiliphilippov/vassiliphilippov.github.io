hydrogen ion

noun
positively charged hydrogen atom (proton)
2.3 4.0
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪdrədʒən aɪˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdrədʒən ˌaɪ.ɒn / (BrE)
A positively charged ion consisting of a single proton, formed when a hydrogen atom loses its electron.

Examples

Concentration of hydrogen ions
In acid solutions, the concentration of hydrogen ions increases significantly.
Pure water contains very few hydrogen ions compared to strong acids.
The movement of hydrogen ions across membranes is essential for cellular energy production.

  • synonym: proton - a subatomic particle with a positive charge
