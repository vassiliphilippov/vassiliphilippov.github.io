renal insufficiency

noun
impaired kidney function; inadequate waste removal
5.7 5.1
Pronunciation: /ˈriː.nəl ɪn.sə.ˈfɪʃ.ən.siː/ (AmE), /ˈriː.nəl ɪn.sə.ˈfɪʃ.ən.siː/ (BrE)
a medical condition in which the kidneys are unable to adequately remove waste products from the blood

Examples

Renal insufficiency diagnosis
Early detection of renal insufficiency can help prevent progression to more severe kidney disease.
People with renal insufficiency often require medication to support their kidney function.
The doctor explained that renal insufficiency could lead to a buildup of toxins in the body.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chronic kidney disease - long-term, progressive loss of kidney function
  • degree higher: kidney failure - complete loss of kidney function
