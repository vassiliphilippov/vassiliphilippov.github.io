labyrinthian

adjective
like a labyrinth; complex or intricate
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˌlæbəˈrɪnθiən / (AmE), / ˌlæbəˈrɪnθɪən / (BrE)
Resembling a labyrinth, especially in being intricate or complex.

Examples

A labyrinthian maze
City streets became labyrinthian as the suburbs sprawled in every direction.
Her novel's plot is so labyrinthian that readers often have to double back to keep track of the clues.
The old castle had a labyrinthian series of corridors that left visitors bewildered.
