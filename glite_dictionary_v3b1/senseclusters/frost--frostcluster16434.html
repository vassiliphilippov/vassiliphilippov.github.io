frost

verb
to cover with a layer of ice crystals
2.2 0.77/m
Pronunciation: / frÉ‘st / (AmE), / frÉ’st / (BrE)
to cover or become covered with a thin layer of ice crystals, or to damage something by freezing

Examples

Fields frost overnight
A thick layer of icy dew frosted the fields overnight.
The bakery frosted the cupcakes before placing them in the display window.
This morning, a cold wind frosted the windows with beautiful patterns.
Be sure to cover your flowers, or the cold may frost them overnight.
Many crops can be frosted if they are left unprotected during an early spring freeze.
The cold snap last night frosted the tomato plants, killing most of them.

Related Terms

  • antonym: defrost - to remove frost; become free of frost
  • synonym: freeze - to become or cause to become ice due to cold
