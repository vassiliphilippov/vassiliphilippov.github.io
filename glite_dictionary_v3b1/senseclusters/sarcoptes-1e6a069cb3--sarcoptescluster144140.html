Sarcoptes

noun
genus of itch-causing mites
Pronunciation: / sɑrˈkɒp.tiz /; / sɑrˈkɑp.tiːz / (AmE), / sɑːˈkɒp.tiːz / (BrE)
a genus of mites that cause scabies and itchy skin conditions in humans and animals

Examples

Sarcoptes mite
Sarcoptes is responsible for causing scabies in humans and mange in animals.
Under a microscope, Sarcoptes can be identified by its round body and short legs.
Veterinarians often treat animals infested with Sarcoptes mites.
Sarcoptes is a medically important genus due to its role in skin diseases.
Scabies is caused by the Sarcoptes mite burrowing into the skin.
Veterinarians often test animals for Sarcoptes infestation when itching is severe.
