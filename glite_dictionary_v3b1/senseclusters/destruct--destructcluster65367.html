destruct

verb
deliberately destroy or cause destruction
3.4
Pronunciation: / dɪˈstrʌkt / (AmE), / dɪˈstrʌkt / (BrE)
to deliberately destroy something, especially by an internal or controlled process (often used in technical or military contexts)

Examples

To destruct a device
Some files are programmed to destruct automatically after being read.
The engineers decided to destruct the old bridge using explosives.
When the missile failed mid-flight, it was remotely destructed for safety reasons.
After the malfunction, engineers had to destruct the rocket to prevent it from crashing into a populated area.
If an unauthorized course is detected, protocols require the operator to destruct the launch vehicle.
Mission control decided to destruct the missile remotely due to a loss of guidance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: destroy - to put an end to the existence of something by damaging it or attacking it
