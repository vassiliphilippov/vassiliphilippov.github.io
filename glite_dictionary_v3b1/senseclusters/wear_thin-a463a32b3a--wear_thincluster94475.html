wear thin

verb
deteriorate through use or stress
6.9
Pronunciation: / wɛr θɪn / (AmE), / wɛə θɪn / (BrE)
Gradually become weakened, less effective, or deteriorated due to continuous use, friction, or stress.

Examples

Carpet wears thin
After years of walking on the old carpet, it has started to wear thin in several places.
My patience is beginning to wear thin after hearing the same excuse repeatedly.
The paint on the benches will wear thin if not maintained properly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: wear out - to use something until it is no longer usable
