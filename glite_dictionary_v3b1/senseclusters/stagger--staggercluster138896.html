stagger

verb
to shock or astonish greatly
Pronunciation: / ˈstæɡɚ /; / ˈstæɡər / (AmE), / ˈstæɡə / (BrE)
to shock or greatly surprise someone

Examples

The news staggered him
I was completely staggered by the amount of money he had spent.
It will stagger you to learn how easy the solution really is.
The news of her sudden resignation staggered everyone in the office.
The news of his sudden resignation staggered everyone in the office.
She was staggered by the amount of support she received from her friends.
The final results staggered the judges, who had not expected such a close competition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amaze - to fill with great wonder or surprise
  • synonym: astonish - to surprise greatly
  • antonym: calm - to make someone feel relaxed and not surprised
  • synonym: overwhelm - to overpower or deeply affect emotionally
