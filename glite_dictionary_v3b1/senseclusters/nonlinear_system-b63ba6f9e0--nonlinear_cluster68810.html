nonlinear system

noun
system not governed by linear equations
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˈlɪniɚ ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈlɪniə ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
a system whose behavior or output cannot be accurately described by linear equations, often exhibiting complex or unpredictable relationships between inputs and outputs

Examples

A nonlinear system behavior
Controlling a nonlinear system can be much more challenging than managing a linear one.
Engineers often use computer models to study the behavior of a nonlinear system.
The weather is influenced by many factors that make it a classic example of a nonlinear system.

Related Terms

  • antonym: linear system - a system whose output is directly proportional to its input and follows linear equations
