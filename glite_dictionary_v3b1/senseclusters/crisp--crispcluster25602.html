crisp

adjective
pleasantly firm and fresh (esp. food)
Pronunciation: / krɪsp / (AmE), / krɪsp / (BrE)
(of food) firm, fresh, and easily broken; not soft or wilted

Examples

Crisp bacon
Make sure the bacon is crisp before adding it to the sandwich.
She bit into a crisp apple, enjoying the satisfying texture.
The salad tasted so much better with crisp lettuce straight from the garden.
She enjoys crisp apple slices in her salad.
The toast was perfectly crisp after a few minutes in the oven.
These crisp potato chips are my favorite snack.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crunchy - making a loud sound when chewed; noticeably crisp
  • antonym: soft - not firm or crisp to the touch
