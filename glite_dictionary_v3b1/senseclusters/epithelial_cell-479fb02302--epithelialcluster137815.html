epithelial cell

noun
cell forming the epithelium
3.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˈθiːliəl sɛl / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈθiːlɪəl sɛl / (BrE)
a cell that is closely packed with others to form the epithelium, the tissue lining the surfaces and cavities of the body

Examples

A epithelial cell layer
Damage to a single epithelial cell can compromise the barrier function of the tissue.
Researchers observed that the epithelial cell samples were regenerating rapidly in culture.
The outermost layer of the skin is composed of epithelial cells that protect against infection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: epithelium - the thin tissue forming the outer layer of a body's surface
