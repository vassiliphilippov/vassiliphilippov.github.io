English system

noun
system of non-metric weights and measures
4.2 3.5
Pronunciation: /ˈɪŋ.glɪʃ ˈsɪs.təm/ /; / /ˈɪŋ.lɪʃ ˈsɪs.təm/ (AmE), /ˈɪŋ.glɪʃ ˈsɪs.təm/ /; / /ˈɪŋ.lɪʃ ˈsɪs.təm/ (BrE)
a system of weights and measures based on the units of the foot, pound, second, and pint, primarily used in the United States and formerly in the United Kingdom

Examples

Many construction projects in the United States use the English system for measurements.

Students often find the English system confusing compared to the simplicity of the metric system.
