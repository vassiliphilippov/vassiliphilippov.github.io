holystone

verb
scrub a ship's deck with a holystone
Pronunciation: / ˈhoʊliˌstaɪn / (AmE), / ˈhəʊliˌstaɪn / (BrE)
to scrub the deck of a ship with a block of soft sandstone called a holystone, traditionally as a method of cleaning wooden decks

Examples

To holystone the deck
After a long voyage, the crew carefully holystoned the wooden planks to remove all traces of salt and dirt.
It was tradition for young seamen to spend hours holystoning the deck as part of their daily duties.
The sailors would holystone the deck every morning to keep it spotless.

Related Terms

  • synonym: polish - to make something smooth and shiny by rubbing
  • synonym: scrub - clean something by rubbing hard, especially with a brush
