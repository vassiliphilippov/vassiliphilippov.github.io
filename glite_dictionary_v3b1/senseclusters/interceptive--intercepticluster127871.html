interceptive

adjective
able to stop
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈsɛptɪv / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈsɛptɪv / (BrE)
relating to the act of intercepting or capable of stopping or preventing something from continuing

Examples

Interceptive action
The security system has several interceptive features to block unauthorized access.
Her interceptive actions prevented the package from being delivered to the wrong address.
The team used an interceptive strategy to stop their opponents from scoring.

Related Terms

  • antonym: permissive - allowing or permitting something to happen
  • synonym: preventive - intended to stop something from happening
