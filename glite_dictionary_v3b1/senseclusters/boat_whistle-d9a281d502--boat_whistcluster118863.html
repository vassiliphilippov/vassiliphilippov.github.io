boat whistle

noun
a whistle on a boat for signals or warnings
3.8
Pronunciation: / boʊt ˈwɪsəl / (AmE), / bəʊt ˈwɪsəl / (BrE)
A whistle fitted on a boat, used to sound warnings or signals.

Examples

Boat whistle sound
Every ship is required to have a functioning boat whistle for emergencies.
The captain sounded the boat whistle as they approached the foggy harbor.
Tourists jumped in surprise when the loud boat whistle echoed across the lake.

Related Terms

  • synonym: siren - a device that makes a loud warning sound, especially on ships or emergency vehicles
