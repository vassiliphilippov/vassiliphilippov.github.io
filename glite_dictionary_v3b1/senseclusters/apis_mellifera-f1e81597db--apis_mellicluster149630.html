Apis mellifera

noun
the western honey bee (species name)
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪ.pɪs məˈlɪf.ə.rə / (AmE), / ˌeɪ.pɪs mɛˈlɪf.ərə / (BrE)
The scientific name for the western honey bee, a social bee species commonly domesticated for its honey production.

Examples

Apis mellifera honey bee
Many beekeepers keep colonies of Apis mellifera to collect honey and beeswax.
Modern agriculture often relies on Apis mellifera for effective crop pollination.
The decline of Apis mellifera populations has raised global concerns about food security.
