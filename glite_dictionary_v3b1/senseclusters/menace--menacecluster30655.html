Synonyms

  • threaten
    3.0 58/m
    threaten says someone or something is likely to cause harm, while menace often means to pose a dangerous or frightening presence.
  • endanger
    3.7 5.98/m
    endanger puts someone at risk, often by an action or choice, while menace means actively threaten or pose danger to them.
  • imperil
    7.8 1.01/m
    imperil sounds formal and legal and means put in danger, while menace is a more common word for threatening or harmful presence.
  • jeopardize
    6.3 8.37/m
    jeopardize puts something like a plan, job, or health at risk, while menace focuses on a direct threat or danger.
  • peril
    8.2 0.01/m
    peril puts into serious danger, often used in formal or literary contexts, while menace is commonly used for a threatening person or thing.
