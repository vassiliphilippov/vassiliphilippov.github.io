Watergate

noun
generic term for a political scandal
6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈwɔː.tər.geɪt/ /; / /ˈwɑː.tər.geɪt/ (AmE), /ˈwɔː.tə.geɪt/ (BrE)
a major political scandal or cover-up; used metonymically to refer to any scandal similar in seriousness to the original Watergate affair.

Examples

A new Watergate
The whistleblower's revelations quickly turned into a national Watergate, dominating headlines for weeks.
Opposition leaders called the incident a modern Watergate and demanded immediate investigations.
After the leaked documents, commentators warned the situation could become another Watergate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scandal - an action or event regarded as morally or legally wrong and causing public outrage
