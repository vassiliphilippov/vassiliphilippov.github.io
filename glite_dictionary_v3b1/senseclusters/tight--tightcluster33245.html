tight

adjective
hard to get out of (situation)
2.7
Pronunciation: / taɪt / (AmE), / taɪt / (BrE)
(of a situation) difficult to handle or get out of; hard to deal with or circumvent.

Examples

Tight situation
It's always stressful to be caught in a tight situation with no clear solution.
Negotiations became tight when neither side was willing to compromise.
The team found themselves in a tight position after losing two key players.

Related Terms

  • synonym: difficult - not easy; requiring effort or skill
  • antonym: easy - not difficult; simple to do or accomplish
