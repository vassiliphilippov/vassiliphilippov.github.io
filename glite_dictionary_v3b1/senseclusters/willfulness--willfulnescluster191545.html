willfulness

noun
tendency to disobey or act without discipline
2.3
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪl.fəl.nəs /; / ˈwɪlfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈwɪl.f(ə)l.nəs /; / ˈwɪlfʊlnɪs / (BrE)
a strong tendency to do as one pleases, often resulting in disobedience or lack of discipline

Examples

Her willfulness caused trouble
Her willfulness caused tension in the team, as she often ignored the coach's instructions.
His willfulness made it difficult for him to accept authority at work.
Parents sometimes struggle with the willfulness of young children who insist on doing things their own way.
Despite warnings, her wilfulness led her to ignore the rules.
His wilfulness often got him into trouble at school.
The manager grew frustrated with the wilfulness of the new recruits.

Related Terms

  • antonym: obedience - the act or practice of following orders or rules
  • synonym: obstinacy - the quality of being stubborn or refusing to change one's opinion
  • synonym: stubbornness - the quality of being stubborn or hard to move
