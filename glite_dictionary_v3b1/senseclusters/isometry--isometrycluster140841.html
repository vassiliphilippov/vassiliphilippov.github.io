isometry

noun
distance-preserving mapping (math)
9.9 8.7
Pronunciation: /aɪ.ˈsɑː.mə.triː/ (AmE), /aɪ.ˈsɒm.ə.triː/ (BrE)
a mapping between metric spaces that preserves the distance between every pair of points

Examples

Plane isometry
Every rotation of a regular polygon is an isometry of the plane.
In geometry, reflections and translations are classic examples of an isometry.
The isometry ensured that the measurements were preserved during the mapping.

Related Terms

  • synonym: congruence - equality in shape and size, especially in geometry
  • degree higher: transformation - a general mathematical operation altering a space, but not always isometric
