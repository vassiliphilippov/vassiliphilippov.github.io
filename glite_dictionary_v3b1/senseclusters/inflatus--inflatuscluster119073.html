inflatus

noun
Latin for swollen
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfleɪtəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈfleɪtəs / (BrE)
a Latin term used in scientific names to indicate that a species has an inflated or swollen part.

Examples

Species inflatus
The species name inflatus was chosen because the plant's fruit appears puffed up.
In zoology, inflatus often describes animals with noticeably expanded body parts.
The Latin word inflatus is commonly used in taxonomy to indicate a swollen feature.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deflated - having lost air or fullness; not swollen
  • synonym: swollen - enlarged or puffed up, often due to fluid
