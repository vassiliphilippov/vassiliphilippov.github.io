metabisulphite

noun
industrial chemical salt
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəbaɪˈsʌlfaɪt / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəbaɪˈsʌlfaɪt / (BrE)
a salt containing the metabisulphite anion, used as a bleaching agent, disinfectant, or reducing agent in industrial and laboratory processes

Examples

Metabisulphite solution
The laboratory used metabisulphite to remove chlorine from the water before conducting experiments.
Food manufacturers sometimes add metabisulphite as a preservative to prevent spoilage.
Always handle metabisulphite with care, as it can cause irritation if inhaled or touched.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bisulfite - a salt containing the bisulfite anion, used as a reducing agent and preservative
  • synonym: sulfite - a salt containing the sulfite anion, used as a preservative and antioxidant
