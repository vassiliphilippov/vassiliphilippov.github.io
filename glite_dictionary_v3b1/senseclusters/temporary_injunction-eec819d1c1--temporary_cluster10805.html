temporary injunction

noun
court order to preserve status during trial
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛmpəˌrɛri ɪnˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərəri ɪnˈdʒʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
a court order that maintains the status quo or protects the subject matter of a lawsuit until the case is resolved

Examples

A temporary injunction order
A temporary injunction was granted, stopping construction until the lawsuit's outcome was decided.
The judge issued a temporary injunction to prevent the company from releasing the disputed product.
Without a temporary injunction, the property could have been irreparably damaged before the case ended.

Related Terms

  • antonym: permanent injunction - a final court order to maintain or prohibit an action indefinitely
  • synonym: preliminary injunction - an injunction issued before or during a trial that lasts until a final decision is made
