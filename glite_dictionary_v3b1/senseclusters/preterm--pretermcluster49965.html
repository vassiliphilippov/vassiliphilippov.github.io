preterm

adjective
born before full term
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːtɜrm / (AmE), / ˈpriːtɜːm / (BrE)
born or occurring before the usual or expected time, especially before 37 weeks of pregnancy

Examples

Preterm birth
The hospital has a special unit for preterm babies who need extra care.
A preterm birth can sometimes lead to health complications for the infant.
Doctors monitor preterm infants closely to ensure they develop properly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: full-term - born at the normal or expected time of pregnancy
  • synonym: premature - born or happening before the expected time
