nonsolid color

noun
color made from patterned dots
5.3 6.0
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˈsɑlɪd ˈkʌlər / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈsɒlɪd ˈkʌlə / (BrE)
A color created by a pattern of differently colored dots that together simulate a single color, often used in printing or graphic design.

Examples

A nonsolid color pattern
The designer specified a nonsolid color pattern for the background to add visual texture.
The magazine cover used a nonsolid color to achieve a more vibrant effect.
You can see the difference between a nonsolid color and a solid color when you look closely at the print.
If you zoom in, you'll see the nonsolid colour is actually made up of tiny blue and yellow dots.
In offset printing, designers often use nonsolid colour to achieve gradients without using extra ink.
The poster's background appeared green, but it was a nonsolid colour created from overlapping circles.

Related Terms

  • antonym: solid colour - a color that is uniform and not made up of patterns or dots
