volcano

noun
mountain through which lava erupts
1.0 1.8
Pronunciation: / voʊlˈkeɪnoʊ / (AmE), / vɒlˈkeɪnəʊ / (BrE)
a mountain or hill with an opening in the earth's crust through which lava, ash, and gases can erupt

Examples

A volcano mountain
Mount Fuji is a famous volcano in Japan.
Scientists monitor the volcano for signs of increased volcanic activity.
The eruption of the volcano forced nearby residents to evacuate their homes.
Lava poured down the sides of the volcano during the eruption.
Mount Fuji is an active volcano that last erupted in the 18th century.
The island was created by an underwater volcano millions of years ago.

  • synonym: mountain - large natural elevation of the earth's surface
