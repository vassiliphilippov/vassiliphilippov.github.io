bluff

adjective
very steep (of cliff or headland)
9.9
Pronunciation: / blʌf / (AmE), / blʌf / (BrE)
(of a cliff or headland) very steep, with a prominent and almost vertical front.

Examples

Bluff cliff
A bluff escarpment towered over the valley below.
The hikers reached the edge of the bluff cliffs overlooking the sea.
They built their home atop a bluff promontory for its stunning views.

Related Terms

  • synonym: precipitous - very steep, like a precipice
  • synonym: sheer - very steep or almost vertical (of cliff, etc.)
