carbon arc

noun
electric arc between carbon electrodes
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑrbən ɑrk / (AmE), / ˈkɑːbən ɑːk / (BrE)
An electric arc struck between carbon electrodes, formerly used for lighting, welding, and heating.

Examples

A carbon arc lamp
A carbon arc generates intense heat and bright light, making it useful for welding.
Many early projectors used a carbon arc to create a beam of light for film projection.
The carbon arc was a popular light source before the incandescent bulb was invented.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arc lamp - a lamp in which an electric arc produces light
