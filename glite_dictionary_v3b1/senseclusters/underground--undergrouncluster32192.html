underground

adjective, adverb
beneath the ground's surface
1.9
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈɡraʊnd / (BrE)
located, happening, or moving beneath the surface of the ground

Examples

Underground tunnel system
London has an extensive network of underground tunnels.
The workers spent hours repairing the underground pipes.
They stored the vegetables in an underground cellar to keep them cool.
Plant roots extend deep underground in search of water.
Some animals build nests underground to protect themselves from predators.
The river disappears underground before reappearing a mile away.
