starboard

verb
to turn a ship or rudder to the right
Pronunciation: / ˈstɑːrˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈstɑːbəd / (BrE)
(of a ship's helm or rudder) to turn or steer to the right (the starboard side).

Examples

To starboard the rudder
He starboarded the rudder at the signal from the bridge.
The captain ordered the helmsman to starboard as the ship approached the harbor entrance.
You must starboard quickly to avoid that floating debris.
