pervious

adjective
permeable; allowing passage through
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈpɝː.vi.əs / (AmE), / ˈpɜː.vi.əs / (BrE)
Allowing water, light, air, or other substances to pass through; permeable.

Examples

Pervious fabric
Certain fabrics are naturally pervious to air, making them more comfortable in hot weather.
The new driveway was made of pervious concrete to allow rainwater to seep into the soil.
These filters are pervious to most gases but block larger particles.
