warren

noun
colony of rabbits; network of burrows
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔr.ən / (AmE), / ˈwɒr.ən / (BrE)
a network of interconnected underground tunnels or burrows where a colony of rabbits lives

Examples

Rabbit warren
After the rain, several young rabbits were seen playing near the entrance to the warren.
Farmers often keep an eye out for signs of a warren in their fields.
The warren was well hidden beneath a thicket and difficult for predators to find.
A complex warren stretched beneath the field, housing dozens of rabbits.
Foxes sometimes try to dig into a warren to catch their prey.
The rabbits disappeared into their warren as soon as they sensed danger.

Related Terms

  • synonym: burrow - a hole or tunnel dug by a small animal
