somatic chromosome

noun
a non-sex chromosome in cells
6.6
Pronunciation: / səˈmæt̬ɪk kɹoʊˈmoʊsoʊm / (AmE), / səˈmatɪk krəʊˈməʊsəʊm / (BrE)
any chromosome that is not a sex chromosome; typically found in pairs in body cells and singly in gametes.

Examples

A somatic chromosome pair
During fertilization, each gamete contributes a single set of somatic chromosomes to the offspring.
Genetic mutations in a somatic chromosome can lead to various inherited disorders.
Humans have 22 pairs of somatic chromosomes and one pair of sex chromosomes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: autosome - any chromosome that is not a sex chromosome
  • antonym: sex chromosome - chromosome involved in determining sex
