get the hang

verb
become skilled at something
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt ðə hæŋ / (AmE), / ɡɛt ðə hæŋ / (BrE)
to become proficient or skilled at something, especially after some practice or experience

Examples

To get the hang of driving
After a few lessons, I finally got the hang of driving a manual car.
Don't worry if you make mistakes at first; you'll get the hang of it soon enough.
It took her a week to get the hang of using the new software at work.
