bellicose

adjective
inclined to fight or argue; aggressive
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛlɪˌkoʊs / (AmE), / ˈbɛlɪkəʊs / (BrE)
having or showing a tendency to argue or fight; aggressive or combative in manner.

Examples

A bellicose attitude
Despite his bellicose reputation, Mark often avoided physical confrontations.
Some politicians use bellicose language to appear strong to their supporters.
The general's bellicose attitude worried his advisors during the tense negotiations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aggressive - having or showing aggression or readiness to attack
  • antonym: peaceful - not involving violence, conflict, or fighting
