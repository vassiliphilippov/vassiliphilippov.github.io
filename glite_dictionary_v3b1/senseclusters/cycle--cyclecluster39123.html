cycle

noun
a regularly recurring sequence of events
2.0 2.4
Pronunciation: /ˈsaɪ.kəl/ /; / /saɪkl/ (AmE), /ˈsaɪ.kəl/ /; / /saɪkl/ (BrE)
a series or period of events that repeat in the same order, or a single repetition of such a process (including in science and time)

Examples

A cycle of seasons
A lunar cycle lasts about 29.5 days from new moon to new moon.
Each cycle of the washing machine takes approximately 40 minutes.
The water cycle describes how water moves through the environment again and again.
Many plants follow a natural cycle of growth and decay each year.
Some conflicts fall into a cycle of violence and revenge that is hard to end.
The business cycle often includes periods of growth followed by recession.
Every spring marks the start of a new cycle in the agricultural calendar.
The cycle of day and night governs the routines of many animals.
The economy often goes through a cycle of growth, recession, and recovery.

Related Terms

  • antonym: break - an interruption or pause in an activity or sequence
  • synonym: hertz - the SI unit of frequency, equal to one cycle per second
  • synonym: pattern - a repeated or regular way in which something happens or is done
  • synonym: sequence - a set of related events or things that follow each other in a particular order
  • synonym: series - a number of things, events, or people of a similar kind or related nature coming one after another
