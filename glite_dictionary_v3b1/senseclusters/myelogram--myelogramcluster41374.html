myelogram

noun
X-ray of spinal cord using contrast dye
7.9 9.0
Pronunciation: /ˈmaɪ.ə.loʊ.græm/ /; / /ˈmaɪ.lə.græm/ (AmE), /ˈmaɪ.ə.lə.græm/ /; / /ˈmaɪ.lə.græm/ (BrE)
an X-ray image of the spinal cord, spinal nerve roots, and subarachnoid space, usually taken after injecting a contrast dye into the spinal canal

Examples

A myelogram image
A myelogram can help detect problems such as spinal cord compression or herniated discs.
After reviewing the myelogram, the neurologist recommended surgery to relieve the pressure on the nerve roots.
The doctor ordered a myelogram to investigate the cause of the patient's persistent back pain.
