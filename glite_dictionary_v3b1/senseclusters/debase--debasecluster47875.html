debase

verb
lower the value of coins by adding base metal
6.9 5.5
Pronunciation: /də.ˈbeɪs/ /; / /dɪ.ˈbeɪs/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈbeɪs/ (BrE)
to reduce the value, quality, or status of something, especially by adding something inferior

Examples

To debase the currency
Collectors value coins made before the government began to debase their metal content.
In ancient times, rulers would often debase coins to finance wars.
The government decided to debase the currency to increase its silver reserves.
Historically, governments have debased their currency by reducing the amount of precious metal in coins.
Some unscrupulous dealers debase precious metals by mixing them with less valuable metals.
The manufacturer was fined for trying to debase the product with cheaper ingredients.
Spreading false rumors can debase someone's reputation in the community.
The company refused to debase its products by using cheaper materials.
He felt that the movie's humor only served to debase an important topic.
