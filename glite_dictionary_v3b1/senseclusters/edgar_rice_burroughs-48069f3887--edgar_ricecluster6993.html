Edgar Rice Burroughs

proper noun
American novelist, creator of Tarzan
Pronunciation: / ˈɛdɡəɹ raɪs ˈbɝroʊz / (AmE), / ˈɛdɡə raɪs ˈbɜːrəʊz / (BrE)
(1875–1950) An American novelist best known as the creator of the Tarzan series and other adventure stories.

Examples

Edgar Rice Burroughs novels
Edgar Rice Burroughs wrote the first Tarzan novel in 1912, captivating readers with tales of the jungle.
Many science fiction fans consider Edgar Rice Burroughs a pioneer of the genre alongside H.G. Wells and Jules Verne.
The works of Edgar Rice Burroughs have been adapted into numerous films and comic strips over the decades.
