inclusion body encephalitis

noun
rare chronic brain inflammation by measles virus
8.5
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkluːʒən ˌbɑdi ɛnˌsɛfəˈlaɪtəs / (AmE), / ɪnˈkluːʒən ˌbɒdi ɛnˌkɛfəˈlaɪtɪs / (BrE)
A rare chronic brain inflammation caused by the measles virus, mainly affecting children and young adults, often leading to death within three years.

Examples

Inclusion body encephalitis in children
Inclusion body encephalitis is most often seen in children infected with the measles virus before they turn two.
Doctors diagnosed the patient with inclusion body encephalitis after observing progressive neurological symptoms.
The progression of inclusion body encephalitis is fatal, typically within a few years of onset.

  • synonym: subacute sclerosing panencephalitis - a chronic, progressive brain inflammation related to measles infection
