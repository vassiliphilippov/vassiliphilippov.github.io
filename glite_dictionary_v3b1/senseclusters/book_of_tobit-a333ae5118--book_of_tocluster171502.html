Book of Tobit

noun
Apocryphal biblical book about Tobit
9.9
Pronunciation: / bʊk əv ˈtoʊbɪt / (AmE), / bʊk əv ˈtəʊbɪt / (BrE)
An Apocryphal book, included in some versions of the Bible, that tells the story of Tobit and his family.

Examples

The Book of Tobit
Many art historians reference the Book of Tobit when discussing depictions of the angel Raphael.
The Book of Tobit is often read for its themes of faith and family devotion.
The story in the Book of Tobit has inspired both literature and religious traditions over the centuries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Book of Judith - another Apocryphal book featuring a legendary heroine
  • synonym: Book of Ruth - biblical book about a Moabite woman who becomes an ancestor of David
