in the main

adverb
generally; for the most part
Pronunciation: / ɪn ðə meɪn / (AmE), / ɪn ðə meɪn / (BrE)
for the most part; generally; usually

Examples

In the main generally
In the main, the conference was a great success despite a few minor problems.
He has, in the main, supported the committee's recommendations.
The reports are, in the main, accurate and well-researched.
In the main, the students performed well on the exam, though there were a few exceptions.
The changes have, in the main, improved the company's performance.
Their views are, in the main, supportive of the new proposal.
