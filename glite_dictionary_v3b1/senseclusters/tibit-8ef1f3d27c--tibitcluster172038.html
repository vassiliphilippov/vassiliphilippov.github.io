Tibit

noun
unit of digital information (2^40 bits)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪ.bɪt / (AmE), / ˈtɪb.ɪt / (BrE)
A unit of digital information equal to 1,024 gibibits, or 2^40 (1,099,511,627,776) bits.

Examples

One Tibit unit
A single Tibit represents over a trillion bits, making it useful for describing massive datasets.
Network administrators often measure extremely large data transfers in Tibits to avoid confusion with decimal-based units.
The storage array has a capacity of 4 Tibits, which is sufficient for the entire project.
