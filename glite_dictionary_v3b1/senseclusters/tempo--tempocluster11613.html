tempo

noun
speed or rate of an event, esp. in music
2.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛm.poʊ /; / ˈtɛmpoʊ / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpəʊ / (BrE)
the speed at which something happens, especially the speed of music or a piece of music

Examples

Fast tempo
The conductor increased the tempo, making the orchestra play faster.
This song has a slow tempo, giving it a relaxed feeling.
We tried to keep a steady tempo during our morning run.
Changing the tempo of a song can completely alter its mood.
The conductor asked the musicians to slow down the tempo during the second movement.
The orchestra performed the symphony at a brisk tempo to energize the audience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pace - the speed or rate of movement or progress
  • synonym: rhythm - a regular repeated pattern of sounds or movements
  • synonym: speed - the rate at which someone or something moves or operates
