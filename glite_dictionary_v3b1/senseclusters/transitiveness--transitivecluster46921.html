transitiveness

noun
property of a verb taking a direct object
7.7 7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈtræn.zɪ.tɪv.nəs/ /; / /ˈtræn.zə.tɪv.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈtræn.zɪ.tɪv.nəs/ /; / /ˈtræn.zə.tɪv.nəs/ (BrE)
the grammatical property of a verb that enables it to take a direct object (i.e., being transitive)

Examples

The transitiveness of verbs
In English, the transitiveness of a verb affects whether it requires an object.
Linguists debate the importance of transitiveness in the classification of verbs.
Some verbs change their transitiveness depending on the context in which they are used.
