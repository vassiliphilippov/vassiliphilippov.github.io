letrozole

noun
breast cancer medication
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛtrəˌzoʊl / (AmE), / ˈlɛtrəzəʊl / (BrE)
a medication used to treat certain types of breast cancer, especially in postmenopausal women, by reducing estrogen production

Examples

Letrozole for breast cancer
Her oncologist recommended letrozole after surgery to reduce the risk of cancer returning.
Some patients experience mild side effects while taking letrozole for breast cancer treatment.
The pharmacist explained how to take letrozole and what to expect during therapy.
The doctor prescribed letrozole to help lower estrogen levels in her body.
Many postmenopausal women with breast cancer are treated with letrozole.
Side effects of letrozole can include hot flashes and joint pain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tamoxifen - a medication used to treat and prevent breast cancer
