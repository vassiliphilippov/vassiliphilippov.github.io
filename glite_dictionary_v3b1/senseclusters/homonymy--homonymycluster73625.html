homonymy

noun
words sharing form but differing in meaning
6.0 7.8
Pronunciation: / həˈmɑː.nə.mi / (AmE), / hɒˈmɪn.ɪ.mi / (BrE)
the state or condition of two words having the same spelling or pronunciation but different meanings

Examples

Homonymy in language
English learners often struggle with homonymy when the same word appears with unrelated meanings.
The homonymy between 'lead' (to guide) and 'lead' (the metal) can be confusing.
The homonymy of 'bear' complicates its meaning in different contexts.
