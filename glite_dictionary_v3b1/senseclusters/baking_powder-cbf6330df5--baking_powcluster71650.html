baking powder

noun
powdered leavening agent for baking
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪkɪŋ ˌpaʊdər / (AmE), / ˈbeɪkɪŋ ˌpaʊdə / (BrE)
a white, powdery mixture used in baking to make dough or batter rise, typically containing a carbonate or bicarbonate and a weak acid

Examples

Baking powder for cakes
Don't forget to add baking powder to the cake batter, or it won't rise properly.
She realized she was out of baking powder halfway through making biscuits.
Some recipes call for both baking powder and baking soda for the best texture.
