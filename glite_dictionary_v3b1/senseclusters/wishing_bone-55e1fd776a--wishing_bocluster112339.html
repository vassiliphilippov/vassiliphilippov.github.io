wishing bone

noun
forked bone of a bird, used in luck tradition
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪʃɪŋ ˌboʊn / (AmE), / ˈwɪʃɪŋ ˌbəʊn / (BrE)
the forked clavicular bone (furcula) of a bird, especially a chicken or turkey, traditionally pulled apart in a game for good luck

Examples

The wishing bone game
After dinner, the children argued over who would get to pull the wishing bone.
She carefully cleaned the wishing bone and set it aside to dry.
Traditionally, two people make a wish and snap the wishing bone to see whose wish comes true.
