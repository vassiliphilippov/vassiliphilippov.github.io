wander

verb
to stray from the main topic or focus
3.3 3.92/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆwÉ‘ndÉ™r / (AmE), / ËˆwÉ’ndÉ™ / (BrE)
(of a person's attention, thoughts, or speech) to lose focus or go off the main subject; to digress or stray in direction or clarity.

Examples

Let your mind wander
Her mind tends to wander during long meetings, making it hard to keep up.
His attention would wander whenever the discussion became too technical.
Try not to let your speech wander too much, or the audience may get confused.

Synonyms

  • digress
    6.8 1.37/m
    digress means leaving the main subject in speech or writing, while wander covers losing focus or straying in speech or direction.
  • stray
    4.0 1.98/m
    stray means moving away from the main topic in speech or writing, while wander also describes losing focus or direction more broadly.
  • ramble on
    6.2 0.90/m
    ramble on means talking at length in an aimless or confused way, while wander is losing focus or drifting from the main subject.
  • drift
    2.9 25/m
    drift means move slowly or aimlessly, often without clear purpose, while wander highlights losing focus or straying in speech or thought.
  • stray
    2.8 4.06/m
    stray means move away from the correct place or path physically, while wander can mean losing attention or moving without clear direction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: digress - to leave the main subject temporarily in speech or writing
  • antonym: focus - to pay particular attention to one subject
  • synonym: ramble - to talk or write at length in a confused or inconsequential way
