wretched

adjective
of poor quality or bad condition
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛtʃɪd / (AmE), / ˈrɛtʃɪd / (BrE)
of extremely poor quality or in a very bad or unpleasant condition

Examples

Wretched living conditions
She was appalled by the wretched state of the abandoned house.
The company's wretched service led to numerous customer complaints.
The refugees lived in wretched conditions during the harsh winter.
After the storm, they spent a wretched night without electricity or heat.
He looked so wretched after being out in the cold rain all day.
The refugees endured wretched conditions in the overcrowded camp.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deplorable - deserving strong condemnation; shockingly bad in quality
  • antonym: excellent - extremely good; of the highest quality
  • synonym: miserable - feeling or causing unhappiness or discomfort
  • synonym: woeful - full of woe; very bad or miserable
