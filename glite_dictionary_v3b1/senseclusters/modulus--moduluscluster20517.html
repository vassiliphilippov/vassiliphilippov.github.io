modulus

noun
absolute value of a complex number
9.6 7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈmɑː.dʒə.ləs/ /; / /ˈmɑː.də.ləs/ (AmE), /ˈmɒd.jʊ.ləs/ /; / /ˈmɒd.jə.ləs/ (BrE)
the absolute value of a complex number, equal to the square root of the sum of the squares of its real and imaginary parts

Examples

Modulus of a complex number
Engineers often use the modulus when analyzing alternating current circuits.
The modulus of 3 + 4i is 5.
To find the modulus of a complex number, you calculate the square root of the sum of the squares of its parts.
