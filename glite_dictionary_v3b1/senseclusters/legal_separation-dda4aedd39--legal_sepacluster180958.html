legal separation

noun
court-ordered arrangement for married couples living apart
Pronunciation: / ˈliːgəl ˌsɛpəˈreɪʃən /; / ˈliɡəl ˌsɛpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈliːɡəl ˌsɛpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
an official arrangement, decided by a court or agreement, where a married couple lives apart but remains legally married

Examples

A legal separation agreement
A legal separation allows couples to resolve property and support issues while remaining married.
After years of conflict, they decided on a legal separation rather than divorce.
The court issued a legal separation to clarify custody and financial responsibilities.
A legal separation allows couples to live apart while remaining technically married.
After years of arguing, they decided a legal separation would be best for everyone involved.
Many people opt for a legal separation before considering divorce.
