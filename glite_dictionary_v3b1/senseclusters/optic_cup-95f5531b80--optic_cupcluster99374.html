optic cup

noun
embryonic eye structure forming the retina
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈɑptɪk ˌkʌp / (AmE), / ˈɒptɪk ˌkʌp / (BrE)
(Embryology) A two-layered cuplike structure in the developing embryo that gives rise to the pigmented and sensory layers of the retina.

Examples

The optic cup structure
Damage to the optic cup during embryogenesis can result in vision problems.
During eye development, the optic cup forms as the neural ectoderm invaginates.
Scientists studied the formation of the optic cup to understand retinal disorders.
