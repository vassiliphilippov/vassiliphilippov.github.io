atonalism

noun
absence of key in music
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈtoʊ.nəˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / əˈtəʊ.nə.lɪz.əm / (BrE)
the principle or practice of composing music without a key or tonal center, as an alternative to traditional diatonic systems

Examples

Atonalism in music
Schoenberg is often associated with the development of atonalism in Western classical music.
Some listeners find atonalism unsettling because it lacks a familiar tonal framework.
The rise of atonalism in the early twentieth century challenged traditional notions of harmony.
