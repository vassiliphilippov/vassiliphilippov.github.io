prefocused

adjective
set in focus beforehand
Pronunciation: / priːˈfoʊkəst / (AmE), / priːˈfəʊkəst / (BrE)
adjusted to be in focus in advance, before use or before an event occurs

Examples

Prefocused camera lens
The security camera was prefocused on the entrance to ensure nothing was missed.
He used a prefocused lens to take quick photos at the sports event.
The microscope came prefocused for easy use by students in the lab.

Related Terms

  • synonym: focused - adjusted so that something is in clear view
  • antonym: unfocused - not adjusted to be in clear view
