anglophile

noun
admirer of English culture or England
Pronunciation: / ˈæŋɡləˌfaɪl / (AmE), / ˈaŋɡləʊfaɪl / (BrE)
A person who admires England, its people, or its culture.

Examples

An anglophile person
As an anglophile, she collects British novels and drinks tea every afternoon.
Despite being American, he developed a reputation as an anglophile at the university.
Many Anglophiles dream of visiting London to experience its history and culture firsthand.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anglophobe - someone who dislikes or fears England or English things
  • synonym: francophile - an admirer of France and French things
