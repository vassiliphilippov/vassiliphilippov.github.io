state bank

noun
bank regulated by a state government
Pronunciation: / steɪt bæŋk / (AmE), / steɪt baŋk / (BrE)
a bank that is chartered and regulated by a state government rather than the federal government

Examples

A state bank account
She opened her first savings account at a local state bank when she was a teenager.
The new regulations affected every state bank across the country.
Unlike a national bank, a state bank operates under state laws and oversight.

Related Terms

  • antonym: national bank - a bank chartered and regulated by the federal government
