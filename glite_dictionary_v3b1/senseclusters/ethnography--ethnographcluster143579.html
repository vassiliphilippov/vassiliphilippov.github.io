ethnography

noun
scientific description of human societies
7.2 6.8
Pronunciation: /eθ.ˈnɑː.grə.fiː/ (AmE), /eθ.ˈnɒ.grə.fiː/ (BrE)
The scientific study and systematic description of individual human societies, especially through direct observation and fieldwork.

Examples

An ethnography study
During fieldwork, he took detailed notes that later became part of his ethnography.
Her ethnography of the local community provided new insights into their traditions and rituals.
The professor published an influential ethnography about rural life in South America.
