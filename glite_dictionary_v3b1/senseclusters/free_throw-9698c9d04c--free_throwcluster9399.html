free throw

noun
unopposed basketball shot from foul line
Pronunciation: / fri ˈθroʊ / (AmE), / friː ˈθrəʊ / (BrE)
A basketball shot taken from the free-throw line without interference, usually awarded after a foul by the opposing team.

Examples

A free throw shot
After the foul, he went to the line for a free throw.
Missing that crucial free throw cost them the victory.
She calmly made both free throws to tie the game.
