snail-flower

noun
flowering tropical American vine with coiled flowers
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈsneɪlˌflaʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈsneɪlˌflaʊə / (BrE)
A perennial tropical American vine cultivated for its showy yellow and purple flowers, whose corolla keel is coiled like a snail shell; sometimes placed in the genus Phaseolus.

Examples

The snail-flower plant
Botanists often admire the coiled corolla of the snail-flower as a fascinating adaptation.
She planted a snail-flower vine along her backyard fence for its striking colors.
The unique blossoms of the snail-flower attract both gardeners and pollinators alike.

Related Terms

  • synonym: centrosema - a genus of tropical American vines with similar coiled flowers
