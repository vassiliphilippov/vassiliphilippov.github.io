sclerotized

adjective
hardened outer layer
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsklɛrəˌtaɪzd / (AmE), / ˈsklɪərəˌtaɪzd / (BrE)
hardened by the development of a tough outer layer, especially in the exoskeletons of insects and arthropods

Examples

A sclerotized shell
The beetle's sclerotized shell protects it from predators.
Many insect legs are sclerotized to provide extra strength and support.
Scientists examined the sclerotized plates on the spider's body under a microscope.
