fixed-point notation

noun
number representation with unchanging decimal point
7.2
Pronunciation: /fɪkst pɔɪnt noʊ.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /fɪkst pɔɪnt nəʊ.ˈteɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
A method of representing numbers in which the position of the decimal (or radix) point is fixed by convention, not varying with the number.

Examples

Fixed-point notation method
Many embedded systems use fixed-point notation because it requires less computational power than floating-point notation.
The programmer chose fixed-point notation to ensure precise and consistent handling of large data sets.
When working with currency, fixed-point notation helps to avoid rounding errors common with floating-point calculations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: floating-point notation - number representation where the decimal point's position can change
