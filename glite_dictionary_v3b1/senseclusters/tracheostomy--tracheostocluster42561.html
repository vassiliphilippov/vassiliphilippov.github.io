tracheostomy

noun
surgical opening in the trachea for breathing
Pronunciation: / treɪkiˈɑstəmi / (AmE), / trəˈkiːɒstəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which an opening is made in the trachea to allow breathing, typically by inserting a tube through the neck

Examples

A tracheostomy tube
After her surgery, she breathed through a tracheostomy tube for several weeks.
Doctors performed an emergency tracheostomy to save his life.
The patient required a tracheostomy after experiencing a severe airway obstruction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tracheotomy - a surgical incision into the trachea to open the airway
