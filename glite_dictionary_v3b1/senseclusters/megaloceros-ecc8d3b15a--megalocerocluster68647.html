Megaloceros

proper noun
extinct giant deer
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛɡəˈloʊsərəs /; / ˌmɛɡəˈlɑsərəs / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡəˈlɒsərəs / (BrE)
an extinct genus of giant deer, famous for its enormous antlers, that lived across Eurasia during the Ice Age (often called the Irish elk)

Examples

The Megaloceros skeleton

Paintings of Megaloceros have been found in prehistoric caves in France.
The Megaloceros is well known for its massive antlers, which could span over three meters.
Scientists believe that Megaloceros roamed grasslands and open woodlands during the last Ice Age.
Fossilized remains of Megaloceros have been discovered in many parts of Europe and Asia.

  • synonym: deer - hoofed grazing animals with antlers, related to Megaloceros
