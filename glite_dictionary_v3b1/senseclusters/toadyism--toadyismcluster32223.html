toadyism

noun
excessive flattery
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtoʊdiˌɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈtəʊdiˌɪzəm / (BrE)
the behavior of flattering or being excessively obedient to someone important in order to gain favor

Examples

Toadyism toward manager
His obvious toadyism toward the manager made his colleagues uncomfortable.
The politician's toadyism was criticized by the media as insincere and self-serving.
She refused to engage in toadyism, preferring to speak honestly even to her superiors.

Related Terms

  • antonym: independence - the quality of not relying on or flattering others for personal gain
  • synonym: obsequiousness - excessive eagerness to please or obey someone
  • synonym: sycophancy - the practice of praising powerful people too much to gain advantage
