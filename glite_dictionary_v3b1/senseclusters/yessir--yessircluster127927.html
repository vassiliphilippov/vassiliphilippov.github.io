yessir

interjection
informal agreement
Pronunciation: / ˈjɛsɚ / (AmE), / ˈjɛsə / (BrE)
an informal way to say yes, especially to show respect, agreement, or readiness to follow an order (often to someone in authority)

Examples

Yessir, coach
When the coach asked if he was ready, he replied, "yessir, I am!"
The soldier stood at attention and said, "yessir, understood."
"Yessir, I'll finish the report by this afternoon," she promised her manager.
When the coach asked if he was ready, he replied with a loud yessir.
The waiter answered, 'yessir, coming right up,' when the customer placed his order.
In the army, soldiers often respond with yessir to show respect to their officers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: no - used to express disagreement or refusal
  • synonym: yes - used to express agreement or affirmation
