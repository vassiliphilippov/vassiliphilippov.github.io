prehistorian

noun
expert on prehistory
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːhɪˈstɔːriən / (AmE), / ˌpriːhɪˈstɔːriən / (BrE)
a person who studies human history and cultures from times before written records existed

Examples

A prehistorian studies history
The prehistorian discovered tools that were over ten thousand years old.
As a prehistorian, she often travels to remote sites to study ancient remains.
A team led by a renowned prehistorian uncovered evidence of early human settlements.

Related Terms

  • synonym: archaeologist - a person who studies human history through excavation and analysis of artifacts
  • antonym: historian - a person who studies and writes about the past, especially using written records
