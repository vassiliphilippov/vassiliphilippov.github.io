Jacobian

noun
matrix of derivatives
9.9
Pronunciation: /dʒə.ˈkoʊ.biː.ən/ /; / /dʒeɪ.ˈkoʊ.biː.ən/ (AmE), /dʒə.ˈkəʊ.biː.ən/ /; / /jɑː.ˈkəʊ.biː.ən/ (BrE)
a matrix containing all the first-order partial derivatives of a vector-valued function, used in calculus and differential equations

Examples

A Jacobian matrix
The Jacobian helps determine whether a function is invertible at a given point.
In multivariable calculus, the Jacobian is used to change variables in integrals.
Engineers often compute the Jacobian when analyzing robotic arm movements.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Hessian - a square matrix of second-order partial derivatives
  • synonym: derivative - a measure of how a function changes as its input changes
