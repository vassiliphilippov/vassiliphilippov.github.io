sixpenny

adjective
of little worth; trivial
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪkspəni /; / ˈsɪksˌpɛni / (AmE), / ˈsɪkspəni / (BrE)
cheap or of little value; trivial or insignificant

Examples

A sixpenny item
He filled the report with sixpenny observations that added little substance.
She dismissed his concerns as sixpenny complaints not worth her attention.
The play was criticized for its sixpenny plot and predictable ending.
He bought a sixpenny novel from the market.
The shop was filled with sixpenny trinkets and souvenirs.
She dismissed his sixpenny advice as unhelpful and unimportant.
