Darwinist

noun
supporter of Darwin's theories
6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈdɑːr.wɪ.nɪst/ (AmE), /ˈdɑː.wɪ.nɪst/ (BrE)
a person who supports or believes in the theory of evolution by natural selection, as proposed by Charles Darwin

Examples

A Darwinist scientist
The scientist was called a Darwinist because of his strong support for the theory of natural selection.
Some critics accused the policy maker of being a Darwinist for favoring competition over social welfare.
In debates about evolution, a Darwinist often argues against creationist viewpoints.
As a dedicated Darwinist, he often debates the evidence for evolution in public forums.
The museum's new exhibit was curated by a well-known Darwinist and evolutionary biologist.
Some critics argue with the views held by a Darwinist on the origins of species.
As a proud Darwinist, she often discussed the evidence for evolution in her biology classes.
Many Darwinists argue that natural selection explains the diversity of life on Earth.
The debate between Darwinists and creationists has continued for over a century.

Related Terms

  • antonym: creationist - a person who believes that the universe and life originated from specific acts of divine creation
  • synonym: evolutionist - a person who believes in or supports the theory of evolution
