tympanostomy

noun
eardrum incision procedure
Pronunciation: / ˌtɪmpəˈnɑstəmi / (AmE), / ˌtɪmpəˈnɒstəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which a small opening is made in the eardrum to allow fluid drainage or pressure equalization

Examples

After the tympanostomy, the patient noticed a significant improvement in hearing and comfort.
A tympanostomy is often performed to prevent fluid buildup behind the eardrum.
The doctor recommended a tympanostomy to help the child with recurring ear infections.
