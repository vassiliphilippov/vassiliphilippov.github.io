wide

adjective, adverb
away from the intended target
1.0 4.4
Pronunciation: / waɪd / (AmE), / waɪd / (BrE)
not on target; missing the intended point or mark (used for shots, statements, etc.)

Examples

Shot goes wide
He fired, but the shot went wide and hit the wall instead.
Her last dart landed wide, costing her the match.
The soccer ball flew wide of the goalpost, disappointing the crowd.
Her comment was wide and did not address the main issue.
His final shot was wide and missed the goal by several feet.
That prediction was wide of the mark and surprised everyone.
