nationalize

verb
bring under state ownership
1.0 5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈnæʃənəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnaʃ(ə)nəlaɪz /; / ˈnæʃənəlaɪz / (BrE)
(of a government) to bring an industry, company, or asset under state control or ownership.

Examples

To nationalize a company
Several industries were nationalized after the revolution to ensure public welfare.
The government decided to nationalize the failing railway system to improve its management.
There was a heated debate about whether to nationalize major energy companies.
Rising unemployment led the new administration to nationalise key energy companies.
Several industries were nationalised in the 20th century to protect public interests.
The government decided to nationalise the railway system after years of private mismanagement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: expropriate - to take property from its owner for public use
  • antonym: privatise - to transfer from public to private ownership
  • antonym: privatize - to transfer ownership from the public sector to private individuals or organizations
