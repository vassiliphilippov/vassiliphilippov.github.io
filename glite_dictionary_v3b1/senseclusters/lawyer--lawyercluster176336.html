lawyer

noun
a person qualified to practice law
1.0 152/m
Pronunciation: / Ààl…îÀê.j…ö / (AmE), / Ààl…îÀê.j…ô / (BrE)
A person who is professionally qualified to practice law, represent clients in legal matters, and provide legal advice.

Examples

A lawyer in court
After graduating from law school, she became a successful lawyer in a large firm.
If you have any legal questions, it's best to consult a lawyer.
Their lawyer advised them against signing the contract without further discussion.

Synonyms

  • attorney
    4.6 88/m
    an attorney is formally authorized to represent clients in court and sign legal papers, while a lawyer practices law broadly.
  • counselor
    4.1 5.14/m
    a counselor gives clients legal advice and may represent them in court, while a lawyer covers all professional law work.
  • counsel
    4.3 25/m
    counsel advises and speaks for a client in court in formal settings, while a lawyer is the general trained legal professional.
  • advocate
    5.8 8.81/m
    an advocate pleads a client's case in court and argues for them, while a lawyer may do many legal jobs beyond pleading.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attorney - a person appointed to act for another in legal matters
  • antonym: client - a person who engages a lawyer's services
  • synonym: solicitor - a type of lawyer in the UK qualified to advise clients and represent them in lower courts
