inoculate against

verb
protect, especially by vaccination or preparation
6.3 5.5
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈnɑː.kjə.leɪt ə.ˈɡenst/ /; / /ɪ.ˈnɑː.kjə.leɪt ə.ˈɡeɪnst/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈnɒk.jʊ.leɪt ə.ˈɡeɪnst/ /; / /ɪ.ˈnɒk.jʊ.leɪt ə.ˈɡenst/ (BrE)
to protect a person or thing from something harmful, especially by introducing a vaccine or by preparing them to resist or reject negative influence, ideas, etc.

Examples

To inoculate against diseases
Parents often inoculate against diseases such as measles and mumps during childhood.
Scientists continue developing ways to inoculate against emerging viruses.
Teachers try to inoculate against the spread of misinformation by encouraging critical thinking.

Related Terms

  • antonym: expose - to make susceptible to or unprotected against something harmful (opposite meaning in this context)
  • synonym: vaccinate - to treat with a vaccine to produce immunity against a disease
