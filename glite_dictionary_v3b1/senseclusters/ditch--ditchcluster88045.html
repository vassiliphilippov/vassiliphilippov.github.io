ditch

verb
throw away; discard
Pronunciation: / dɪtʃ / (AmE), / dɪtʃ / (BrE)
to get rid of, abandon, or leave something or someone suddenly or irresponsibly (including objects, plans, or relationships)

Examples

To ditch a friend
After the update, many users ditched the old app in favor of the new one.
I decided to ditch my old shoes instead of repairing them again.
She quickly ditched all the extra paperwork she didn't need anymore.
After years of working together, he decided to ditch his business partner without warning.
If you get cold feet before the ceremony, don't just ditch your fiancé at the altar.
She felt hurt when her friends ditched her at the party to hang out with someone else.
After dating for two years, she decided to ditch him just before the holidays.
Are you seriously going to ditch our plans just because something better came up?
He felt terrible when his friends ditched him at the party without saying a word.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abandon - to leave someone or something behind permanently or for a long time
  • synonym: desert - to leave someone behind in a difficult situation
  • synonym: discard - to get rid of something as no longer useful
  • synonym: dump - to end a relationship with someone abruptly or unceremoniously
  • antonym: keep - to retain possession of
