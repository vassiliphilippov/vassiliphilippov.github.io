irredeemable

adjective
unable to be redeemed for coin (of money)
2.5
Pronunciation: / ˌɪrɪˈdiməbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪrɪˈdiːməbəl / (BrE)
(of paper money) not able to be exchanged for coin or precious metal at the holder's request.

Examples

Irredeemable banknotes
Before 1933, U.S. banknotes were backed by gold, but modern currency is often irredeemable.
During periods of financial crisis, governments sometimes declare banknotes irredeemable to stabilize the economy.
The irredeemable notes caused concern among those who preferred assets backed by precious metals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: redeemable - able to be exchanged for coin or precious metal
