suffocative

adjective
causing trouble breathing (from heat, airlessness)
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌfəˌkeɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈsʌfəkətɪv / (BrE)
causing difficulty in breathing, especially due to lack of fresh air or heat

Examples

A suffocative atmosphere
She opened the window to escape the suffocative atmosphere in the crowded room.
The suffocative heat made it difficult to sleep during the summer blackout.
The humidity in the tent was almost suffocative after a night of heavy rain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: suffocating - causing someone to feel as though they cannot breathe
