overlong

adjective
excessively long
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
longer than necessary, expected, or desirable

Examples

An overlong film
The director was criticized for making the film overlong and difficult to watch in one sitting.
Her speech was so overlong that many people lost interest before she finished.
The report became overlong because it included too many unnecessary details.

Related Terms

  • antonym: brief - lasting only a short time
  • synonym: lengthy - taking a long time or having great length
