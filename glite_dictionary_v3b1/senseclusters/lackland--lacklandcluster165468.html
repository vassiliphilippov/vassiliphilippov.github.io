lackland

noun
landless person
Pronunciation: / ˈlækˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈlækˌlænd / (BrE)
an old term for a person who owns no land or property

Examples

A lackland person
In medieval society, a lackland often worked for those who owned large estates.
The story follows a young lackland seeking fortune and status.
Being a lackland meant having few rights and little influence in the community.

Related Terms

  • synonym: landless - without land or property
  • antonym: landowner - a person who owns land
