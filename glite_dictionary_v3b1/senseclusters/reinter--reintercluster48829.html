reinter

verb
to bury again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪnˈtɜr / (AmE), / ˌriːɪnˈtɜː / (BrE)
to bury a dead body again, especially in a different grave or place

Examples

To reinter a body
After the cemetery was relocated, the family decided to reinter their ancestors in a new plot.
The remains were carefully reinterred following the archaeological excavation.
Authorities had to reinter the body due to flooding in the original burial site.
After the cemetery was relocated, the remains were reinterred in a new grave.
The family decided to reinter their ancestor in the ancestral village.
Historical artifacts were discovered when workers reinterred the old bones.

  • antonym: exhume - to dig out a buried body from the ground
