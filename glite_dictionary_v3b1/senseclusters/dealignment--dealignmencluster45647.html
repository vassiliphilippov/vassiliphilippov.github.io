dealignment

noun
decline in party loyalty; political nonalignment
3.4
Pronunciation: / diˈlaɪnmənt / (AmE), / diˈlaɪnmənt / (BrE)
the process by which voters become less aligned with political parties, leading to weaker party loyalty and structure

Examples

Voter dealignment process
During recent elections, dealignment became evident as more citizens identified as nonpartisan.
Many analysts believe that voter dealignment has led to the rise of independent candidates.
The phenomenon of dealignment is reshaping traditional party politics in several democracies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: realignment - a shift of voters toward stronger party affiliation or new coalitions
