futurist

noun
someone who predicts the future
6.6 2.97/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàfjuÀêt É…ôr…™st / (AmE), / ÀàfjuÀêt É…ôr…™st / (BrE)
a person who studies and predicts what may happen in the future, especially based on current trends

Examples

A futurist predicts trends
As a leading futurist, she often speaks about how technology will shape our daily lives.
Many companies hire a futurist to help anticipate market changes and new opportunities.
The conference featured a futurist discussing innovations that could change transportation in the next decade.

Synonyms

  • futurologist
    8.5 0.05/m
    a futurologist studies long-term trends and future scenarios in depth, while a futurist focuses on practical predictions from current trends.
  • forecaster
    3.7 2.28/m
    a forecaster uses data and models to predict specific events or trends, while a futurist studies wider social and technological change.
  • predictor
    4.4 0.18/m
    a predictor gives estimates based on data or models, while a futurist studies broader trends and possible long-term futures.
  • prognosticator
    9.5 0.32/m
    a prognosticator claims insight and gives bold forecasts, often sounding formal or old-fashioned, while a futurist studies trends and predicts likely changes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: forecaster - a person who predicts future events, especially weather or financial trends
  • synonym: prophet - a person regarded as an inspired teacher or proclaimer of the will of God, sometimes predicting the future
