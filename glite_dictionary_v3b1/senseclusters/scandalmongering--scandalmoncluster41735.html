scandalmongering

noun
spreading malicious gossip
Pronunciation: / ˈskæn.dlˌmʌŋ.ɡər.ɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskæn.dlˌmʌŋ.ɡər.ɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of spreading scandalous or malicious gossip, especially with the intention of causing harm or excitement

Examples

Office scandalmongering
He lost many friends due to his involvement in scandalmongering at the office.
Social media can sometimes become a platform for widespread scandalmongering.
The tabloid was regularly accused of scandalmongering and sensationalism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gossip - casual or unconstrained conversation about others, often involving details that are not confirmed as true
