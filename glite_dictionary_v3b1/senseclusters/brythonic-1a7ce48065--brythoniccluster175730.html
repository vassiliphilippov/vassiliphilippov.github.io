Brythonic

noun
southern Celtic language group
9.6
Pronunciation: / brɪˈθɑnɪk / (AmE), / brɪˈθɒnɪk / (BrE)
the southern branch of Celtic languages, including Welsh, Cornish, and Breton

Examples

Brythonic languages
Cornish is closely related to other Brythonic languages such as Breton and Welsh.
Some ancient inscriptions in Britain are written in a form of Brythonic.
Welsh is a modern representative of the Brythonic language group.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Goidelic - the northern branch of Celtic languages, including Irish and Scottish Gaelic
