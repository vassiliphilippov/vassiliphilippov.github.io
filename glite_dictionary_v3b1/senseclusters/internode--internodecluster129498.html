internode

noun
segment of a stem between two nodes
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈɪntɚˌnoʊd / (AmE), / ˈɪntəˌnəʊd / (BrE)
a segment of a plant stem between two nodes, where leaves and branches may emerge

Examples

Plant internode segment
Researchers measured each internode to study how the plant grew under different conditions.
The internode length determines the overall height of the plant.
The stem broke at the last internode just below the flowering branch.

Related Terms

  • antonym: node - the part of a plant stem from which leaves or branches grow
