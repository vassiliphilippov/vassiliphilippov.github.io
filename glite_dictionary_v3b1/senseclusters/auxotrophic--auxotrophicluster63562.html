auxotrophic

adjective
requires external nutrients
Pronunciation: / ˌɔksəˈtroʊfɪk / (AmE), / ˌɔːksəˈtrɒfɪk / (BrE)
unable to synthesize a specific organic compound required for growth and must obtain it from the environment

Examples

Auxotrophic strain
The scientist studied an auxotrophic strain of yeast that could not produce its own vitamins.
Because the bacteria are auxotrophic, they need to be supplied with certain amino acids in the lab.
Researchers use auxotrophic mutants to investigate metabolic pathways in microorganisms.

Related Terms

  • antonym: prototrophic - able to synthesize all compounds needed for growth
