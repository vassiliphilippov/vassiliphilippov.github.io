midbody

noun
central section of structure
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪdˌbɑdi / (AmE), / ˈmɪdˌbɒdi / (BrE)
the central section of a vehicle, vessel, or structure, especially in engineering or design contexts

Examples

Rocket midbody
The engineers inspected the midbody of the rocket for any signs of stress after the test flight.
Damage to the ship's midbody required immediate repairs before it could continue its journey.
Designers reinforced the midbody of the aircraft to improve its structural integrity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: afterbody - the rear section of a vehicle or structure
  • antonym: forebody - the front section of a vehicle or structure
  • synonym: midsection - the middle part of something, especially a structure or body
