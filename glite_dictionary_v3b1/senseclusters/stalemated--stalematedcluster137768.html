stalemated

adjective
at a standstill due to opposition
Pronunciation: / ˈsteɪlˌmeɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈsteɪlˌmeɪtɪd / (BrE)
unable to proceed or make progress because of equally strong opposing forces; at a complete standstill.

Examples

A stalemated chess game
After several moves, the chessboard was stalemated, and neither player could win.
Efforts to pass new legislation are stalemated by disagreement between the two main parties.
The negotiations remained stalemated after hours of discussion, with neither side willing to compromise.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deadlocked - unable to reach agreement or progress due to strong opposing forces
