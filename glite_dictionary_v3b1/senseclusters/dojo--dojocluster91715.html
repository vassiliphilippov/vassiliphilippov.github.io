dojo

noun
training hall
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈdoʊdʒoʊ / (AmE), / ˈdəʊdʒəʊ / (BrE)
a place where people practice or learn martial arts, especially Japanese styles like judo or karate (sometimes used for other types of intensive training or learning)

Examples

A dojo for karate
The students entered the dojo quietly before their meditation session began.
She spent years training in a traditional dojo to master her martial art.
The dojo was decorated with calligraphy and symbols of peace.
The karate dojo was filled with students practicing their moves in unison.
Our company holds a weekly coding dojo to help developers improve their skills together.
She joined a yoga dojo to deepen her understanding of meditation and movement.
Every Saturday, children gather at the dojo to learn karate from their instructor.
The new dojo in town offers classes in both judo and aikido.
She decorated the dojo with traditional banners and calligraphy.
