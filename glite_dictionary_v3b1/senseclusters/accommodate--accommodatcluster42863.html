accommodate

verb
adapt for a new purpose
5.0 5.5
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈkɑː.mə.deɪt/ (AmE), /ə.ˈkɒ.mə.deɪt/ (BrE)
to adapt or adjust something to make it suitable for a new use, purpose, or situation

Examples

Accommodate new needs
The old school building was accommodated to serve as a community center.
The recipe was accommodated to fit the available ingredients in the region.
We had to accommodate our plans after the weather changed unexpectedly.
The proposal was revised to accommodate the concerns raised by the committee.
The software was updated to accommodate the needs of a wider range of users.
We must accommodate our schedule with the changes in the project timeline.
