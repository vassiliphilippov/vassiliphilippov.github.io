swallow

noun
migratory songbird with long wings
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈswɑloʊ / (AmE), / ˈswɒləʊ / (BrE)
a small songbird with long, pointed wings and a forked tail, known for its swift, graceful flight and regular migrations

Examples

A swallow flying
A swallow darted through the evening sky, catching insects on the wing.
Every spring, the swallow returns to nest in the old barn.
The sight of a swallow is often a sign that summer is approaching.

Related Terms

  • synonym: martin - type of songbird closely related to the swallow, also known for aerial feeding
