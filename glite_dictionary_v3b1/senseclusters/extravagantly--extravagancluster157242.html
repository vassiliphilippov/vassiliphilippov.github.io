Synonyms

  • prodigally
    9.1 0.02/m
    prodigally means spending money wastefully and freely, while extravagantly means using more than is necessary in a showy way.
  • profligately
    9.9 0.02/m
    profligately describes reckless, immoral waste of money or resources, while extravagantly means using more than is necessary, often lavishly.
  • excessively
    3.0 3.81/m
    excessively means to a degree that is too much, while extravagantly adds a sense of lavish or wasteful display.
  • overly
    3.5 14/m
    overly means more than is needed or wanted, while extravagantly suggests wasteful or very showy use of money or things.
  • exorbitantly
    7.3 0.16/m
    exorbitantly often describes prices or costs that are unreasonably high, while extravagantly focuses on wasteful, lavish behavior or spending.
  • inordinately
    7.5 0.75/m
    inordinately means to an unusually large degree, often formal, while extravagantly points to wasteful or lavish action in everyday use.
