nativism

noun
policy supporting native cultures
6.0 7.6
Pronunciation: /ˈneɪ.tɪ.vɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈneɪ.tɪ.vɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the belief, policy, or practice of favoring native inhabitants or their culture over those of outsiders or immigrants

Examples

Anti-immigrant nativism
Critics argue that excessive nativism can lead to social isolation and prejudice against newcomers.
Many activists supported nativism to protect indigenous languages and traditions from disappearing.
The government's policy of nativism faced criticism for resisting multicultural influences.
Nativism became a powerful force in politics during periods of high immigration.
Some groups promoted nativism to restrict the rights of newcomers.
Debates about nativism often arise during discussions of national identity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acculturation - the process of adopting the cultural traits or social patterns of another group
  • antonym: multiculturalism - the support or presence of several distinct cultural groups in a society
  • synonym: xenophobia - fear or dislike of people from other countries
