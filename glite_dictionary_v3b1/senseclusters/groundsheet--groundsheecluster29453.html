groundsheet

noun
waterproof sheet placed on the ground
5.2 3.8
Pronunciation: /ˈgraʊnd.ʃiːt/ (AmE), /ˈgraʊnd.ʃiːt/ (BrE)
a waterproof sheet of cloth or material that is spread on the ground, especially under a tent, to protect against moisture.

Examples

A groundsheet under tent
A torn groundsheet can let water into your sleeping area and ruin your night.
The campers rolled up the groundsheet after packing away their gear.
We put a groundsheet under the tent to prevent any dampness from seeping in.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tarpaulin - heavy-duty waterproof cloth, often used as a covering
