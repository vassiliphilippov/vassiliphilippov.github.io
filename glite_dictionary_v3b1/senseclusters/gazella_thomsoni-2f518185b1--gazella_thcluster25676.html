Gazella thomsoni

noun
East African gazelle species
Pronunciation: / ɡəˈzɛlə θɑmˈsoʊni / (AmE), / ɡəˈzɛlə tɒmˈsəʊni / (BrE)
A species of gazelle native to East Africa, known for being the smallest gazelle.

Examples

The Gazella thomsoni species
The Gazella thomsoni is known for its remarkable speed and agility on the African plains.
Unlike other antelopes, the Gazella thomsoni is easily recognized by its distinctive black side stripe.
Wildlife photographers often travel to Kenya to capture images of the Gazella thomsoni in its natural habitat.
