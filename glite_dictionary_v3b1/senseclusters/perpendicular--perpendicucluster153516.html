perpendicular

noun
a line or plane at right angles
Pronunciation: / ˌpɜr.pənˈdɪk.jə.lɚ / (AmE), / ˌpɜː.pənˈdɪk.jʊ.lə / (BrE)
A straight line or plane that is at a right angle to another line or surface.

Examples

A perpendicular to line
If two lines meet to form a right angle, one is a perpendicular to the other.
The architect drew a perpendicular to mark the exact midpoint of the wall.
We dropped a perpendicular from the point to the base of the triangle.

Related Terms

  • antonym: parallel - A line or surface always the same distance from another; not meeting at any point
