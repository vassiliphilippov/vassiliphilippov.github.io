unthread

verb
to remove from threading
5.1
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈθrɛd / (AmE), / ʌnˈθrɛd / (BrE)
to remove something (such as thread or similar) from where it has been threaded, such as from a needle, fabric, or a hole

Examples

To unthread a needle
She had to unthread the needle to change the color.
After finishing the sewing, he carefully unthreaded the machine.
If the string gets stuck, you may need to unthread it and start again.
She used a small pair of scissors to unthread the seam on her dress.
Before repairing the tear, he had to unthread the old, frayed stitches.
The tailor patiently unthreaded the hem to adjust the length of the trousers.
After sewing the button, she carefully unthreaded the needle.
He had to unthread the rope from the pulley before replacing it.
The technician unthreaded the wire from the machine to check for damage.
