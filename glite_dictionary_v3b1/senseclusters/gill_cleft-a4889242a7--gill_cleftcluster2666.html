gill cleft

noun
slit opening in fish/amphibian pharynx for water passage
9.0 7.5
Pronunciation: /gɪl kleft/ /; / /dʒɪl kleft/ (AmE), /gɪl kleft/ /; / /dʒɪl kleft/ (BrE)
one of a series of slit-like openings in the pharynx of fishes and aquatic amphibians through which water passes for respiration

Examples

Fish gill clefts
In amphibian larvae, gill clefts are a vital pathway for respiration.
The young shark has several gill clefts along each side of its head.
Water passes through the fish's mouth and out through the gill clefts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gill slit - an opening between the gill arches of a fish or amphibian
