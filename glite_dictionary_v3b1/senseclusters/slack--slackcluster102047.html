slack

noun
calm or motionless water area
8.1
Pronunciation: / slæk / (AmE), / slæk / (BrE)
a stretch of water where the current is slow or motionless

Examples

A slack in the river
After hours of battling the rapids, the boat drifted peacefully into a slack.
Fishermen often seek out a slack where the water is still and easier to navigate.
The river widened and slowed, forming a quiet slack between the bends.

Related Terms

  • antonym: current - a body of water moving in a definite direction, especially through a surrounding body of water
  • synonym: eddy - a circular movement of water causing a small whirlpool
