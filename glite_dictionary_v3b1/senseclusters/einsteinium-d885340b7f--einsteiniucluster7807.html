Einsteinium

noun
a radioactive chemical element (Es, atomic number 99)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪnˈstaɪniəm / (AmE), / ˌaɪnˈstiːniəm / (BrE)
a radioactive transuranic element (symbol Es) produced by bombarding plutonium with neutrons; atomic number 99

Examples

Einsteinium element
Due to its radioactivity, einsteinium has no significant commercial applications outside scientific research.
Research teams synthesized einsteinium by bombarding plutonium with neutrons in a nuclear reactor.
Scientists discovered einsteinium in the debris of the first hydrogen bomb explosion.
