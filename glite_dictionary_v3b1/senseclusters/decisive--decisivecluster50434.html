decisive

adjective
crucial; marking a turning point
6.0
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsaɪsɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈsaɪsɪv / (BrE)
having a strong effect on deciding the result of something; marking a turning point

Examples

Decisive victory
Her quick action proved to be decisive in saving the company.
The outcome hinged on a decisive moment during the negotiations.
The team scored a decisive goal in the last minute of the match.
Her decisive action during the crisis prevented a disaster.
The team's victory was sealed with a decisive goal in the final minutes.
Weather conditions were a decisive factor in the outcome of the match.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conclusive - serving to settle or decide a question; decisive
  • synonym: critical - of decisive importance; forming or having the nature of a turning point or crisis
  • synonym: crucial - decisively important or critical
  • antonym: indecisive - not having or showing the ability to make decisions quickly and effectively; not producing a definite result
