dowry

noun
property or money given at marriage
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊri / (AmE), / ˈdaʊəri / (BrE)
money or property that a woman brings to her husband at marriage

Examples

A dowry payment
Her family gave a substantial dowry when she married.
In some cultures, the dowry tradition still plays an important role.
The dispute arose over the amount of dowry to be paid.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bride price - money or property paid by the groom or his family to the bride's family
