gene linkage

noun
inheritance of linked genes
4.1
Pronunciation: / dʒin ˈlɪŋkɪdʒ / (AmE), / dʒiːn ˈlɪŋkɪdʒ / (BrE)
in genetics, the tendency of certain genes located close together on a chromosome to be inherited together

Examples

Gene linkage in genetics
Researchers discovered that gene linkage could explain why certain traits are inherited together.
The concept of gene linkage challenged traditional Mendelian genetics by showing that some genes do not assort independently.
When studying fruit flies, scientists observed patterns of gene linkage on the same chromosome.
