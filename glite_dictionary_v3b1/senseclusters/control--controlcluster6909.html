control

noun
regulation of a bodily function or action
1.0
Pronunciation: / kənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / kənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
the power or ability to direct, manage, or have authority over people, things, or situations; skill or discipline in managing oneself or actions

Examples

Control of emotions
Despite the injury, his control of fine motor skills remained excellent.
Loss of bladder control is a common issue in older adults.
Physical therapy helped her regain control over her leg muscles.
He was placed in control of the budget for the entire project.
Strict control over the manufacturing process ensures high quality.
The control of the mob by the police was admirable.
After the new manager took control of the team, productivity increased significantly.
Rival groups are constantly competing for control of the region.
She fought hard to maintain control over her own life despite outside pressures.
