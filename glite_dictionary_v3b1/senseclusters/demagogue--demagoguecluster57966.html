demagogue

noun
political leader exploiting popular desires/prejudices
9.6 7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈde.mə.ɡɑːg/ /; / /ˈde.mə.ɡɔːg/ (AmE), /ˈde.mə.ɡɒɡ/ (BrE)
a political leader who seeks support by appealing to popular desires, emotions, and prejudices rather than by using rational argument

Examples

A demagogue rallies crowds
Critics warned that his speeches had the tone of a demagogue rather than a statesman.
History has shown that a demagogue can gain power quickly by stirring up public emotions.
The politician was often accused of being a demagogue who exploited people's fears for votes.

Related Terms
