leaf miner

noun
larva living inside and feeding on leaves
Pronunciation: / lif ˈmaɪnər / (AmE), / liːf ˈmaɪnə / (BrE)
an insect larva, especially of certain moths or flies, that lives and feeds inside leaf tissue, creating visible tunnels or blotches

Examples

A leaf miner insect
A leaf miner creates winding, pale trails on the surface of infected leaves.
Gardeners often struggle to control leaf miners in vegetable crops.
The leaves were damaged by a leaf miner infestation in early spring.
