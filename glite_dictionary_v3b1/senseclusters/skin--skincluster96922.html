skin

noun
outer body covering
1.3 1.0
Pronunciation: /skɪn/ (AmE), /skɪn/ (BrE)
the natural outer layer covering the body of a person or animal

Examples

Human skin
After spending too much time in the sun, his skin became red and irritated.
Babies have very soft and delicate skin that needs gentle care.
The doctor examined the rash that had appeared on her skin.
Her skin was soft and unblemished after the treatment.
Some snakes shed their skin several times a year.
The skin of a giraffe is covered in distinctive patches.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flesh - the soft substance consisting of muscle and fat that is found between the skin and bones of an animal or a human
  • synonym: hide - the thick skin of an animal, especially when tanned or dressed
