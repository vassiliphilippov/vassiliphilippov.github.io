snow job

noun
elaborate deception or misrepresentation
9.2
Pronunciation: / snoʊ dʒɑb / (AmE), / snəʊ dʒɒb / (BrE)
an attempt to deceive or mislead someone, especially by using exaggerated or insincere talk

Examples

A snow job story
I can't believe you fell for that snow job about the company's financial health.
She gave me a real snow job when I asked about her missing the deadline.
The politician's explanation was nothing but a snow job to distract the public.
After listening to the sales pitch, I realized it was just another snow job.
The politician's speech sounded like a snow job to win public support.
Whenever he gets caught, he tries to cover it up with a snow job.

Related Terms

  • synonym: con job - an act of cheating or tricking someone, especially for personal gain
  • synonym: flattery - excessive and insincere praise
  • antonym: honesty - the quality of being truthful or sincere
  • synonym: scam - a dishonest scheme or fraud
  • antonym: truth - what is true or factual, opposite of deception
