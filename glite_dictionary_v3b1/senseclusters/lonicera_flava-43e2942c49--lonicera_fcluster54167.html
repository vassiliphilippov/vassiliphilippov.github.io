Lonicera flava

noun
a yellow-flowered honeysuckle of the southeastern U.S.
Pronunciation: / loʊˈnɪsərə ˈflɑːvə / (AmE), / ləˈnɪsərə ˈflɑːvə / (BrE)
A climbing deciduous shrub, native to the southeastern United States, with fragrant yellow flowers that later turn orange, arranged in terminal whorls; also known as yellow honeysuckle.

Examples

Lonicera flava shrub
Gardeners in the southeastern U.S. often choose Lonicera flava for its bright blooms and climbing habit.
The fragrant flowers of Lonicera flava attract many species of butterflies and hummingbirds.
Unlike some invasive honeysuckles, Lonicera flava is native and supports local wildlife.
