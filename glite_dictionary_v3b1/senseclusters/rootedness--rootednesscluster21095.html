rootedness

noun
strong connection
Pronunciation: / ˈruːtɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˈruːtɪdnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being firmly established or strongly connected to a place, community, or set of values

Examples

Rootedness in the community
Her rootedness in the local culture helped her adapt quickly to changes in the community.
Many people seek a sense of rootedness when they move to a new country.
The rootedness of traditions can provide comfort during uncertain times.

Related Terms

  • antonym: alienation - a feeling of being isolated or disconnected
  • synonym: attachment - a strong emotional connection to someone or something
  • synonym: belonging - a feeling of being accepted and included
