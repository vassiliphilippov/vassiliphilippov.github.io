unlawfully

adverb
illegally; not in conformity with the law
4.3 0.73/m
Pronunciation: /  ånÀàl…îfli / (AmE), /  ånÀàl…îÀêfli / (BrE)
In a manner that does not conform to the law; illegally.

Examples

To unlawfully enter
He was accused of unlawfully entering the building without permission.
The company was fined for unlawfully dumping waste in the river.
They discovered the documents had been unlawfully obtained.

Synonyms

  • illegally
    2.1 9.33/m
    illegally means acting in a way the law does not allow, while unlawfully is a formal word for the same action.
  • illicitly
    6.8 0.18/m
    illicitly describes secret or improper acts that break law or custom, while unlawfully focuses simply on actions against the law.
  • illegitimately
    6.8 0.13/m
    illegitimately often means lacking proper legal or social acceptance, while unlawfully stresses that an action breaks the law.
  • criminally
    6.5 1.31/m
    criminally refers to actions that amount to crimes or show blame, while unlawfully covers any act that is against the law.
  • lawlessly
    7.2 0.02/m
    lawlessly suggests living or acting without regard for rules in a wild or chaotic way, while unlawfully simply means against the law.

Related Terms

  • synonym: illegally - in a way that is against the law
  • antonym: lawfully - in a way that is allowed by law
