cook up

verb
devise or invent, often dishonestly
5.6 1.29/m
Pronunciation: / kÊŠk ÊŒp / (AmE), / kÊŠk ÊŒp / (BrE)
to invent or make up something, especially a story, excuse, or plan, often dishonestly or imaginatively

Examples

To cook up a story
She managed to cook up an elaborate story to explain her absence.
The company tried to cook up a reason for the sudden price increase.
The suspect had clearly cooked up a fake alibi.
She claimed her brother had cooked up the story to avoid getting into trouble.
The politician tried to cook up a convincing explanation for his absence.
They managed to cook up a clever scheme to surprise their friend.

Synonyms

  • make up
    3.2 30/m
    make up is the everyday phrasal verb for inventing stories or excuses, while cook up often adds a sly or playful tone.
  • invent
    3.2 9.64/m
    invent is a neutral word for creating ideas or excuses, while cook up often suggests making them dishonestly or playfully.
  • concoct
    6.0 2.66/m
    concoct suggests cleverly mixing ideas or plans, while cook up often means inventing something quickly and sometimes to deceive.
  • think up
    3.2 2.47/m
    think up focuses on the mental act of creating an idea, while cook up adds a sense of trick or quick invention.
  • dream up
    3.9 2.41/m
    dream up suggests imagining something unusual or playful, while cook up can be practical or dishonest in purpose.
  • fabricate
    7.3 2.68/m
    fabricate means to make false facts to deceive, while cook up is more casual and can be creative or slightly dishonest.
  • manufacture
    6.3 3.55/m
    manufacture sounds formal and often means producing false evidence, while cook up feels informal and covers excuses or plans.
  • spin a story
    4.8 0.02/m
    spin a story means to tell a tale with imaginative or exaggerated details, while cook up focuses on creating or inventing it.
