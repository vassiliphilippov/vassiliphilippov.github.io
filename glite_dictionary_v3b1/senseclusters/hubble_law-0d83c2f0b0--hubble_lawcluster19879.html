Hubble law

noun
astronomical law about galaxy recession speed and distance
7.0 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / Ààh åb…ôl l…î / (AmE), / Ààh åb…ôl l…îÀê / (BrE)
the principle that the speed at which a distant galaxy recedes from an observer is proportional to its distance, used as evidence for the expansion of the universe

Examples

The Hubble law principle
According to the Hubble law, the farther away a galaxy is, the faster it appears to be moving away from us.
Astronomers measure the rate of expansion using the Hubble law and observations of distant galaxies.
The Hubble law provides strong evidence for the theory that the universe is expanding.

Synonyms

  • Hubble's law
    6.3 0.01/m
    Hubble's law is the common possessive name used by scientists and textbooks, while Hubble law is the simple noun form.
