scrub oak

noun
small shrubby North American oak
Pronunciation: / skrʌb oʊk / (AmE), / skrʌb əʊk / (BrE)
any of various small shrubby oaks, especially those in North America, that often grow in dry, thin soils and form dense thickets

Examples

Dense scrub oak thickets
Firefighters struggled to move through the tangled growth of scrub oak during the wildfire.
Many birds nest in stands of scrub oak due to the thick cover they offer.
The hillside was covered in dense scrub oak that provided shelter for wildlife.
