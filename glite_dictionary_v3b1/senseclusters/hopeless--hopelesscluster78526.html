hopeless

adjective
unable or incompetent at something
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈhoʊpləs / (AmE), / ˈhəʊpləs / (BrE)
(of a person) unable to do something well; lacking skill or ability at a particular activity.

Examples

Hopeless at math
Despite taking lessons, she remained hopeless at driving.
He always felt hopeless at sports compared to his classmates.
I'm absolutely hopeless at remembering names.

Related Terms

  • antonym: competent - having the necessary ability or skills to do something successfully
  • antonym: skilful - having or showing skill at a particular activity
