embrittlement

noun
becoming brittle
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈbrɪtlmənt / (AmE), / ɪmˈbrɪtlmənt / (BrE)
the process or state in which a material becomes brittle and loses its flexibility or toughness, often due to environmental factors or stress

Examples

Embrittlement of materials
Repeated heating and cooling can lead to embrittlement of certain plastics over time.
Engineers must consider embrittlement when designing structures exposed to extreme cold.
The embrittlement of metal pipes increases the risk of sudden failure in old buildings.
Repeated exposure to cold temperatures can cause embrittlement in certain types of plastic.
Engineers tested the metal for signs of embrittlement after years of heavy use.
The chemical process led to rapid embrittlement of the pipeline, increasing the risk of leaks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brittleness - the quality of being easily broken or cracked
  • antonym: toughening - the process of making a material stronger or less likely to break
  • antonym: toughness - the ability of a material to absorb energy and resist breaking
