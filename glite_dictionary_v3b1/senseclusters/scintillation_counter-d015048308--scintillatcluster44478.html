scintillation counter

noun
device for detecting ionizing radiation
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪn.təˈleɪ.ʃən ˈkaʊn.tɚ / (AmE), / ˌsɪn.tɪˈleɪ.ʃən ˈkaʊn.tə / (BrE)
a device that detects and measures ionizing radiation by using flashes of light produced in a scintillating material

Examples

A scintillation counter device
A scintillation counter measures radiation by converting flashes of light into electrical signals.
Modern hospitals often use a scintillation counter for monitoring radiation levels during procedures.
The scientist used a scintillation counter to detect radioactive particles in the laboratory.
