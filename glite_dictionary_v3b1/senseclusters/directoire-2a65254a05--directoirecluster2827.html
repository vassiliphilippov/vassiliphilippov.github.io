Directoire

proper noun
French government 1795–1799
Pronunciation: / diˌrɛkˈtwɑr / (AmE), / diˌrɛkˈtwɑː / (BrE)
the executive government of France from 1795 to 1799, consisting of five directors known as the Directory

Examples

The Directoire government
The Directoire struggled to maintain stability in France after the Revolution.
Many historians debate the effectiveness of the Directoire as a form of government.
Napoleon overthrew the Directoire and established the Consulate in 1799.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Consulate - the French government that succeeded the Directoire in 1799
  • synonym: Directory - the English term for the Directoire government in France
