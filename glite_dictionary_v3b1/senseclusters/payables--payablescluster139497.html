payables

noun
money a company owes, pending payment
6.3 3.8
Pronunciation: /ˈpeɪ.ə.bəlz/ (AmE), /ˈpeɪ.ə.bəlz/ (BrE)
amounts of money a company owes to its suppliers or creditors, expected to be paid in the near future

Examples

Accounts payables
Finance must ensure that all payables are settled before the end of the month.
Rising payables can indicate that a business is delaying payments to improve cash flow.
The company's payables increased significantly this quarter due to new inventory purchases.
