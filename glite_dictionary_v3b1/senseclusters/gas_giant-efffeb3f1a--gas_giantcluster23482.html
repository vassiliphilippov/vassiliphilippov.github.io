gas giant

noun
large gaseous planet (e.g. Jupiter)
4.7 3.7
Pronunciation: /gæs ˈdʒaɪ.ənt/ /; / /gæs dʒaɪnt/ (AmE), /gæs ˈdʒaɪ.ənt/ /; / /gæs dʒaɪnt/ (BrE)
A large planet composed chiefly of hydrogen and helium, lacking a well-defined solid surface, such as Jupiter or Saturn.

Examples

A gas giant planet
Jupiter is the largest gas giant in our solar system and has dozens of moons.
Scientists believe that many exoplanets discovered are similar in size to a gas giant.
Unlike the rocky planets, a gas giant lacks a solid surface.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Jovian planet - another term for gas giant, especially in reference to Jupiter-like planets
  • antonym: terrestrial planet - a planet composed primarily of silicate rocks or metals, like Earth or Mars
