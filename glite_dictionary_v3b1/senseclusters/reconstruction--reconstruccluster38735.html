reconstruction

noun
interpretation assembled from evidence
Pronunciation: / ˌriː.kənˈstrʌk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌriː.kɒnˈstrʌk.ʃən / (BrE)
an interpretation or account created by assembling fragments of evidence, facts, or remembered details

Examples

Historical reconstruction
His reconstruction of the events relied mainly on eyewitness testimony and a few preserved letters.
Historians debated the accuracy of her reconstruction of medieval court life.
The archaeologists gave a detailed reconstruction of the ancient city's layout based on their findings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: recreation - a re-creation or imitation of something, especially a past event
