moot

adjective
open to argument or debate
7.4 1/m
Pronunciation: / muÀêt / (AmE), / muÀêt / (BrE)
subject to debate, dispute, or uncertainty; open to argument or discussion.

Examples

A moot point
It's a moot point whether their actions really made a difference.
The effectiveness of the new policy remains moot among experts.
Whether the project will be completed on time is still a moot question.

Video Examples

Synonyms

  • debatable
    5.0 3/m
    debatable is commonly used to say something is open to discussion in everyday speech, while moot feels more formal.
  • arguable
    6.0 1.0/m
    arguable suggests someone can make a reasonable case for an idea, while moot here means it is open to wider dispute.
  • disputable
    6.6 0.2/m
    disputable sounds more formal and often appears in legal or strict writing, while moot is a broader term for debate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: debatable - open to discussion or argument; not certain
  • antonym: indisputable - impossible to doubt or question; certain
