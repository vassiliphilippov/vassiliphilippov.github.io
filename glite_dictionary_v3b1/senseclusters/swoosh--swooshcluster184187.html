swoosh

verb
move quickly with a whistling sound
5.6 0.31/m
Pronunciation: / swu É / (AmE), / swuÀê É / (BrE)
to move quickly with or cause to move with a rushing or whistling sound

Examples

To swoosh past
She swooshed the mop across the floor, leaving a trail of suds behind.
The train doors swooshed open as passengers waited on the platform.
The wind swooshed through the trees as the storm began to intensify.

Synonyms

  • whoosh
    4.5 0.76/m
    whoosh means moving very fast with a loud rushing sound, while swoosh often describes a softer, cleaner rushing noise.
  • swish
    4.1 1.63/m
    swish moves or makes a smooth, sweeping hissing sound in air, while swoosh implies a louder, quicker rushing sound.
  • whish
    8.2 0.01/m
    whish makes a soft, sweeping rushing sound like cloth or wind, while swoosh usually suggests a stronger, more forceful rush.
  • whiz
    4.5 0.12/m
    whiz moves very fast and often makes a high humming or buzzing sound, while swoosh focuses on a sweeping rushing noise.
  • whistle
    3.3 2.21/m
    whistle makes a clear high tone like wind or lips, while swoosh means a broader rushing or whooshing movement sound.
