joust

verb
compete verbally; debate
7.5
Pronunciation: / d\u0292a\u028ast /; / dʒaʊst / (AmE), / d\u0292a\u028ast /; / dʒaʊst / (BrE)
to compete or argue with someone in a direct, often public or formal way, especially in a debate or contest

Examples

To joust in debate
In the annual debate, the candidates joust for support from swing voters.
Two columnists regularly joust in print over the government's economic policies.
Opposing lawyers jousted in court with sharp questions and confident claims.
The two lawyers spent hours jousting over the details of the contract in court.
During the meeting, the managers were jousting for control of the new project.
She enjoys jousting with her colleagues during lively discussions about politics.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agree - to have the same opinion as someone else
  • synonym: compete - to try to win against others
  • synonym: debate - to discuss a subject formally, especially in public, with opposing arguments.
  • synonym: spar - to engage in a short, usually verbal, argument or exchange of ideas.
