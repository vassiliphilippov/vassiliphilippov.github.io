Synonyms

  • parsimony
    9.9 0.50/m
    parsimony means careful use of money or resources in a formal way, while miserliness implies mean refusal to spend for others.
  • tightfistedness
    8.6 0.00/m
    tightfistedness is a colloquial word for refusing to spend money, while miserliness suggests a lasting, greedy habit of holding money back.
  • penny-pinching
    7.5 0.04/m

  • stinginess
    6.8 0.15/m
    stinginess is a general lack of generosity with money or gifts, while miserliness describes a stronger, more extreme refusal to spend.
