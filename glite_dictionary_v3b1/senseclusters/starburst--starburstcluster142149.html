starburst

noun
intense star formation
8.1
Pronunciation: /ˈstɑːr.bɜːrst/ (AmE), /ˈstɑː.bɜːst/ (BrE)
a period in which a galaxy experiences an exceptionally high rate of new star formation

Examples

A galaxy starburst
The galaxy is currently experiencing a starburst, producing hundreds of new stars each year.
Astronomers study starbursts to understand how galaxies evolve over time.
A sudden starburst can dramatically change the appearance of a galaxy.
