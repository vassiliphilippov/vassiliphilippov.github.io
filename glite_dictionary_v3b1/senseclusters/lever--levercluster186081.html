lever

noun
rigid bar used to lift or move objects
1.2 6.32/m
Pronunciation: / Ààl…õv…ôr /; / ÀàliÀêv…ôr, Ààl…õv…ôr / (AmE), / ÀàliÀêv…ô, Ààl…õv…ô / (BrE)
a rigid bar that pivots on a fixed point (fulcrum) to move or lift something by applying force

Examples

A lever lifts weight
A simple lever can multiply the force you apply, making it easier to move objects.
By placing the rock under the lever, she was able to shift it with minimal effort.
The engineer used a lever to lift the heavy stone into place.
Archimedes famously said that with a long enough lever and a fulcrum, he could move the world.
In physics class, we learned how a lever can multiply applied force.
The child used a lever to lift the heavy stone off the ground.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bar - a long rigid piece used as a lever, support, etc.
  • synonym: fulcrum - the support or point on which a lever pivots
  • synonym: pulley - a wheel on an axle used to change the direction of force
