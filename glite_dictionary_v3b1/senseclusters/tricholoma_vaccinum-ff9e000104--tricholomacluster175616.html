Tricholoma vaccinum

noun
a species of reddish-capped mushroom
Pronunciation: / trɪkəˈloʊmə vækˈsiːnəm / (AmE), / trɪkəˈləʊmə vakˈsiːnəm / (BrE)
A species of agaric mushroom with a cap densely covered in reddish fibrils, and pale gills and stalk, found in coniferous forests.

Examples

Tricholoma vaccinum mushroom
Tricholoma vaccinum is commonly found growing under pine and spruce trees in northern forests.
Some mushroom hunters caution against eating Tricholoma vaccinum due to its questionable edibility.
The cap of Tricholoma vaccinum is easily recognized by its reddish, hairy surface.
