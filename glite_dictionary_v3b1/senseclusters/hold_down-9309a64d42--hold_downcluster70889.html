hold down

verb
manage to keep a job or position
1.0 3.2
Pronunciation: /hoʊld daʊn/ (AmE), /həʊld daʊn/ (BrE)
to manage to keep or maintain a job or position, especially in difficult circumstances or over a period of time

Examples

To hold down a job
After his illness, he struggled to hold down steady work.
It's difficult to hold down a stressful job and study at the same time.
She managed to hold down the manager position for five years.
He managed to hold down three jobs to support his family while attending college.
It's impressive that she can hold down three jobs without missing a beat.
Many people hold down multiple jobs to make ends meet these days.
