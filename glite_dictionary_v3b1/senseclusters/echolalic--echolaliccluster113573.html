echolalic

adjective
repeating others' words
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɛkoʊˈleɪlɪk / (AmE), / ˌɛkəˈleɪlɪk / (BrE)
relating to or characterized by echolalia, the involuntary repetition of another person's words

Examples

Echolalic speech
The therapist noted the child's echolalic responses during the language assessment.
Some individuals with autism may display echolalic speech patterns in conversation.
Her echolalic repetition of questions made communication challenging for her teachers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: repetitive - involving repetition
  • antonym: spontaneous - arising naturally without repetition
