heat of dissociation

noun
amount of heat needed to break substance into simpler parts
4.5
Pronunciation: / hiːt əv dɪˌsɔsiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / hiːt əv dɪˌsɒsiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
The amount of heat required to break a substance into simpler components, such as molecules into atoms or ions, in a fluid state.

Examples

The heat of dissociation energy
Scientists measured the heat of dissociation during the experiment with hydrogen gas.
The heat of dissociation for water molecules must be considered in thermodynamic calculations.
Understanding the heat of dissociation is essential for analyzing high-temperature plasma reactions.
