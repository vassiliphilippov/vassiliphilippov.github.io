read

verb
to have a particular wording or phrasing
2.9 48/m
Pronunciation: / riÀêd / (AmE), / riÀêd / (BrE)
(of a text, document, or inscription) to have specific wording or to be worded in a particular way.

Examples

The letter reads clearly
Her memo reads differently from what we discussed in the meeting.
The contract reads that tenants must provide thirty days' notice before moving out.
The sign on the door reads 'No Admittance'.

Synonyms

  • say
    1.0 463/m
    say shows information on a sign or when someone speaks, while read refers to the exact words written in a text or inscription.

Related Terms

  • synonym: say - to convey or express in written or spoken words
  • synonym: state - to say or specify in written or spoken form
