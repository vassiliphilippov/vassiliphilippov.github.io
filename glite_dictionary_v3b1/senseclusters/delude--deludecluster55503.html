delude

verb
mislead or deceive
6.3 2.87/m
Pronunciation: / d…™Ààlud / (AmE), / d…™ÀàluÀêd / (BrE)
to make someone believe something that is not true; to mislead or deceive someone

Examples

To delude investors
He tried to delude his investors into thinking the company was profitable.
Politicians often delude the public with empty promises.
She refused to delude herself about the reality of the situation.

Synonyms

  • deceive
    3.9 12/m
    deceive means making someone believe something false to gain advantage, while delude often implies causing false hope or belief.
  • mislead
    3.9 4.51/m
    mislead gives false or unclear information that causes error, while delude often means making someone firmly believe something untrue.
  • dupe
    6.4 2.79/m
    dupe fools someone into believing something false, often by tricking them quickly, while delude suggests a deeper or longer false belief.
  • trick
    3.5 9.77/m
    trick uses clever or dishonest actions to fool someone for advantage, while delude focuses on causing belief in something untrue.
  • fool
    2.1 20/m
    fool makes someone look or feel silly by tricking them in informal ways, while delude specifically means causing false belief.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deceive - to cause someone to believe something that is not true
  • antonym: disillusion - to cause someone to realize their belief is false
  • synonym: mislead - to give someone the wrong idea or impression
