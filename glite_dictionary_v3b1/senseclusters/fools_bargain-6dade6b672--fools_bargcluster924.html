fool's bargain

noun
seemingly good but actually bad deal
5.7 6.5
Pronunciation: /fuːlz ˈbɑːr.gən/ /; / /fuːlz ˈbɑːr.gɪn/ (AmE), /fuːlz ˈbɑː.ɡən/ /; / /fuːlz ˈbɑː.ɡɪn/ (BrE)
an agreement or deal that seems beneficial at first but ultimately proves disadvantageous or unwise

Examples

A fool's bargain
He thought he was getting a great price, but it was actually a fool's bargain.
Many investors fell for the scheme, only to realize it was a fool's bargain.
Signing the contract without reading the fine print turned out to be a fool's bargain.
