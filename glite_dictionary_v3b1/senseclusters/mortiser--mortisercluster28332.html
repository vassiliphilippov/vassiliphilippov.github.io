mortiser

noun
woodworking machine
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔrtɪsər / (AmE), / ˈmɔːtɪsə / (BrE)
a machine or tool used to cut square or rectangular holes in wood for mortise and tenon joints

Examples

Woodworking mortiser machine
The carpenter used a mortiser to create precise joints for the new table.
A mortiser makes it much easier to cut square holes in thick wooden beams.
Before buying a mortiser, she researched different models to find the best one for her workshop.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chisel - a hand tool with a sharp edge for cutting or shaping wood, stone, or metal
  • synonym: drill press - a machine for drilling holes in wood, metal, or other materials
