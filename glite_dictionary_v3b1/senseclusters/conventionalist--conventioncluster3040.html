conventionalist

noun
follower of tradition
Pronunciation: / kənˈvɛnʃənəlɪst / (AmE), / kənˈvɛnʃənəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who follows traditional practices, customs, or accepted standards rather than adopting new ideas

Examples

A conventionalist family
As a proud conventionalist, she always celebrated holidays in the same way as her parents.
The committee was dominated by conventionalists who resisted any proposals for change.
He was labeled a conventionalist because he preferred classic literature over modern works.

Related Terms

  • antonym: iconoclast - a person who challenges or rejects established beliefs or traditions
  • synonym: traditionalist - a person who supports tradition and resists change
