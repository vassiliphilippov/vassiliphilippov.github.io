up to someone's ears in

adjective
very busy or deeply involved
8.9
Pronunciation: / ʌp tə ˈsʌmwʌnz ɪrz ɪn / (AmE), / ʌp tə ˈsʌmwənz ɪəz ɪn / (BrE)
Extremely busy with or deeply involved in a particular activity, often to the point of being overwhelmed.

Examples

Up to her ears in bills
After the holidays, she found herself up to her ears in bills.
I'm up to my ears in paperwork and won't be able to leave the office early.
We're up to our ears in orders this month, so deliveries might be delayed.
