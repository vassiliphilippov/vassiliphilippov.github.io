supplementary benefit

noun
British welfare payment to support minimum income
2.1 4.8
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌpləˈmɛntəri ˈbɛnəfɪt / (AmE), / ˌsʌplɪˈmentəri ˈbɛnɪfɪt / (BrE)
(in the UK) a government payment made to raise an individual's income to a legally set minimum level.

Examples

Apply for supplementary benefit
After he lost his job, he applied for supplementary benefit to help cover his living expenses.
Many families relied on supplementary benefit before the government reformed the welfare system.
She qualified for supplementary benefit because her income was below the legal minimum.
