mason's level

noun
long spirit level used by masons
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪsənz ˈlɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈmeɪsənz ˈlɛvəl / (BrE)
a spirit level that is longer than a carpenter's level, used by masons to check the horizontality of surfaces such as walls and floors

Examples

A mason's level tool
A mason's level ensures that long stretches of stonework are perfectly horizontal.
She bought a new mason's level for her masonry class to help build straighter walls.
The bricklayer checked the alignment of the wall with a mason's level before laying the next row of bricks.
