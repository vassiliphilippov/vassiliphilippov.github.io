technical

adjective
to do with strict legal or rule-based interpretation
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛknɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈtɛknɪkəl / (BrE)
conforming to the precise requirements or rules of a law or regulation, rather than to its general meaning or intent.

Examples

Technical violation
Although the contract was violated in a technical sense, no harm was done.
His argument follows a technical reading of the law, not its spirit.
The court dismissed the case on a technical point rather than the merits.

Related Terms

  • synonym: literal - taking words in their usual or most basic sense
  • antonym: substantive - relating to the essential legal principles, as opposed to technicalities
