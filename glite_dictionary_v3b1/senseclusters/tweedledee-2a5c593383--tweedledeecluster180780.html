Tweedledee

proper noun
fictional twin character
Pronunciation: / ˌtwiːdəlˈdiː / (AmE), / ˌtwiːdəlˈdiː / (BrE)
a fictional character from Lewis Carroll's works, often paired with Tweedledum, representing one of two people who are nearly identical

Examples

Tweedledee and Tweedledum
For the play, he dressed up as Tweedledee while his friend played Tweedledum.
The two politicians were compared to Tweedledee and Tweedledum because of their similar views.
In the story, Tweedledee and his brother are always together and rarely disagree.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Tweedledum - the fictional twin of Tweedledee, also from Lewis Carroll's works
