dullard

noun
an unintelligent or slow-witted person
9.9 6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌl.ərd / (AmE), / ˈdʌl.ɑːd /; / ˈdʌl.əd / (BrE)
a person who is not very intelligent or is slow to understand things; a dull or stupid person

Examples

A dullard person
Don't be such a dullard—think before you act.
His boss treated him like a dullard for not understanding the report.
The character was portrayed as a lovable dullard who always got into trouble.
At the party, Karen tried to avoid the dullard who was discussing accounting for hours.
Being labeled a dullard in class motivated him to work harder and prove everyone wrong.
He's a complete dullard who never has anything interesting to say in meetings.

  • antonym: genius - a person of extraordinary intellectual power
  • synonym: idiot - a very stupid person
  • synonym: simpleton - a foolish or gullible person
