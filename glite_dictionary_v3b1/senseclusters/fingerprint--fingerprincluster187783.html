fingerprint

noun
mark/impression of finger ridges
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪŋɡɚˌprɪnt /; / ˈfɪŋɡərˌprɪnt / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡəˌprɪnt / (BrE)
a mark or impression made by a finger, often used for identification or left as a smudge

Examples

A unique fingerprint
Each person has a unique fingerprint that can be used for identification.
The detective carefully lifted a fingerprint from the broken glass at the crime scene.
The new phone unlocks automatically when it recognizes your fingerprint.
She wiped away the fingerprint from the glass before serving the drink.
The detective noticed a greasy fingerprint on the doorknob.
There was a fingerprint on the clean window that I had just washed.

Related Terms
