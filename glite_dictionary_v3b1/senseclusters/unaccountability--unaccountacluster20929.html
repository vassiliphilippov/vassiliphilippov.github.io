unaccountability

noun
lack of responsibility
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnəˌkaʊntəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌʌnəˌkaʊntəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the state of not being responsible or answerable for actions or decisions

Examples

The government faced criticism for its culture of unaccountability after the scandal.

The manager warned that unaccountability would not be tolerated on the team.
The lack of oversight led to widespread unaccountability within the organization.
Citizens protested against the government's unaccountability in handling public funds.
Without clear rules, unaccountability can become a serious problem in any institution.
