buffer zone

noun
neutral area separating rivals
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌfər zoʊn /; / ˈbʌfɚ ˌzoʊn / (AmE), / ˈbʌfə zəʊn / (BrE)
an area or region that separates two groups, powers, or activities to reduce conflict or negative effects

Examples

A buffer zone of trees
A buffer zone of trees was planted to protect the wetland from agricultural runoff.
After the peace agreement, both sides withdrew troops to create a buffer zone along the border.
The United Nations established a buffer zone between the two countries to prevent further clashes.
A row of trees was planted as a buffer zone between the houses and the main road.
The company set up a buffer zone to limit interactions between separate departments.
Wildlife reserves often act as a buffer zone to protect endangered species from human activity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barrier - Something that prevents movement or access between two places
  • synonym: demilitarized zone - area where military forces are not allowed, especially as a buffer between countries
  • antonym: frontline - a military line directly engaged with the enemy
  • synonym: no-man's-land - disputed or unoccupied land between two opposing positions
