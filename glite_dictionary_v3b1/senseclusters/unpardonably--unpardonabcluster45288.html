unpardonably

adverb
in an unforgivable manner
8.0 0.01/m
Pronunciation: /  ånÀàp…ërd…ôn…ôbli / (AmE), /  ånÀàp…ëÀêd…ôn…ôbli / (BrE)
In a way that cannot be forgiven; to an unforgivable extent.

Examples

Unpardonably rude behavior
His actions were unpardonably rude to everyone at the dinner table.
She found his carelessness unpardonably reckless considering the risks involved.
The manager behaved unpardonably towards the staff during the meeting.

Synonyms

  • unforgivably
    7.1 0.11/m
    unforgivably says an action cannot be forgiven by others, while unpardonably feels more formal and old-fashioned in tone.
  • inexcusably
    6.9 0.11/m
    inexcusably describes behavior that has no excuse or justification, while unpardonably focuses on being unable to be forgiven.

Related Terms

  • antonym: forgivably - in a way that can be forgiven
  • synonym: inexcusably - in a way that cannot be excused
