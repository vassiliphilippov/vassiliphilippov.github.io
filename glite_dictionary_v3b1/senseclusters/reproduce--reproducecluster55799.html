reproduce

verb
produce offspring; procreate
3.7 6.03/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀêpr…ôÀàduÀês / (AmE), / ÀåriÀêpr…ôÀàdjuÀês / (BrE)
(of an animal, plant, or microorganism) to produce offspring or new individuals of the same kind.

Examples

To reproduce offspring
Endangered animals may struggle to reproduce in captivity.
Many fish can reproduce several times throughout their lives.
Under the right conditions, yeast cells reproduce rapidly.

Synonyms

  • propagate
    6.3 1.39/m
    propagate is used to cause plants or animals to produce new individuals or pass on traits, while reproduce means biological production of offspring.
  • procreate
    6.5 1.22/m
    procreate means to produce offspring, often used for humans or animals in natural mating, while reproduce covers all living organisms.
  • breed
    1.8 9.58/m
    breed refers to animals producing offspring, often by selective mating under human control, while reproduce is a general biological process.
  • multiply
    3.2 7.17/m
    multiply means to increase greatly in number, often by fast growth or copying, while reproduce focuses on making new offspring.

Related Terms

  • synonym: procreate - to produce offspring, especially by sexual reproduction
