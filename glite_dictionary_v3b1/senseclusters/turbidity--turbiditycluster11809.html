turbidity

noun
cloudiness caused by suspended particles
4.9 9.0
Pronunciation: / tɚˈbɪdəti / (AmE), / tɜːˈbɪdəti / (BrE)
The quality or state of a liquid being cloudy or opaque due to suspended particles or sediment.

Examples

High turbidity water
High turbidity in the river made it difficult to see the fish below the surface.
The lake’s turbidity increased after the heavy rains stirred up sediment.
Water treatment plants regularly measure turbidity to ensure clean drinking water.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clarity - clearness of a liquid; opposite of turbidity
  • synonym: cloudiness - the state of being cloudy; similar to turbidity
