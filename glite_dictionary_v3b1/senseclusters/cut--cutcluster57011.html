cut

noun
film: immediate shot-to-shot transition
2.4
Pronunciation: / kʌt / (AmE), / kʌt / (BrE)
an omission, deletion, or reduction made to something, especially in text, film, or spending

Examples

A cut in spending
After the chase, there was a sudden cut to a peaceful countryside.
Editors debated whether a slow fade or a sharp cut would work better at the end of the sequence.
The director used a rapid cut to heighten the sense of urgency in the scene.
Budget cuts have resulted in fewer public services.
The company announced a cut in production to save costs.
The school faced a significant cut in its funding this year.
Several cuts were necessary to meet the magazine's word limit.
That scene was not in the final version because of a cut during editing.
The author was unhappy with the cut made by the editor to her introduction.

Related Terms

  • antonym: addition - an insertion of new material in editing
  • synonym: deletion - removal of text or material
  • antonym: dissolve - a gradual transition between shots in film editing
  • antonym: fade - a gradual transition from one image to another in film
  • antonym: increase - an instance of growing or making greater in size, amount, or degree
  • synonym: reduction - the act or process of making something smaller or less
