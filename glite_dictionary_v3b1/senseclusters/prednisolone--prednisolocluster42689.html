prednisolone

noun
synthetic glucocorticoid drug
6.9
Pronunciation: / prɛdˈnɪsəˌloʊn / (AmE), / prɛdˈnɪsələʊn / (BrE)
a synthetic glucocorticoid drug used to treat a variety of inflammatory and autoimmune conditions.

Examples

To take prednisolone medicine
Children with severe asthma are sometimes treated with prednisolone for short periods.
High doses of prednisolone may cause side effects such as weight gain or mood changes.
The doctor prescribed prednisolone to help reduce the patient's inflammation.

Related Terms
