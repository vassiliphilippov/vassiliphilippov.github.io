dissipate

verb
convert usable energy to heat
6.7 0.80/m
Pronunciation: / \u02c8d\u026as\u0259\u02ccpe\u026at / (AmE), / \u02c8d\u026as\u026ape\u026at / (BrE)
(in physics and engineering) to convert usable energy (for example electrical or kinetic energy) into heat or other forms so that it is no longer available for useful work.

Examples

To dissipate energy
In this circuit, the resistor will dissipate energy as heat, lowering efficiency.
Friction quickly dissipated the skateboarder's kinetic energy and slowed his descent down the ramp.
Engineers added a damping system so vibrations would dissipate harmlessly instead of transferring to the frame.

Related Terms

  • synonym: convert (energy) - to change energy from one form to another, often into heat
  • synonym: waste - to lose or use something (such as energy) inefficiently or uselessly
