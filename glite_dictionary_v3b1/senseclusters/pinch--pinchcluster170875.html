pinch

verb
squeeze tightly between the fingers
2.2 1.6
Pronunciation: /pɪntʃ/ (AmE), /pɪntʃ/ (BrE)
to squeeze or press something tightly between the fingers, often causing discomfort or pain; or to cause a sharp, irritating feeling

Examples

To pinch your skin
If you pinch the dough, it will seal the edges better.
She pinched his arm to get his attention during the movie.
The child cried out when his sister pinched him on the cheek.
A mild allergy can pinch your skin, making it feel irritated.
As I stood too close to the fire, the rising heat began to pinch my ankles.
The cold wind seemed to pinch his cheeks until they stung.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nip - to pinch or bite sharply
  • synonym: prick - to cause a small, sharp pain
  • antonym: soothe - to gently calm or reduce pain
  • synonym: squeeze - to press something firmly together with your fingers or hand
