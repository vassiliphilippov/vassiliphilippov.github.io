desaturate

verb
to make unsaturated
Pronunciation: / diˈsætʃəˌreɪt / (AmE), / diˈsætʃəreɪt / (BrE)
to remove hydrogen atoms from a molecule, creating double bonds and making the molecule unsaturated, especially in chemistry or biochemistry

Examples

To desaturate fatty acids
The enzyme helps desaturate fatty acids during the process of metabolism.
Scientists can desaturate certain compounds to study their chemical properties.
In biochemistry, some reactions desaturate molecules to create double bonds.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hydrogenate - to add hydrogen atoms to a molecule, making it more saturated
  • antonym: saturate - to fill or combine with the maximum amount of another substance
