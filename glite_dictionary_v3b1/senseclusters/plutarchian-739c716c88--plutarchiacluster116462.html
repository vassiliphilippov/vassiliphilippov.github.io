Plutarchian

noun
follower of Plutarch
Pronunciation: / pluːˈtɑːrkiən / (AmE), / pluːˈtɑːkɪən / (BrE)
a follower, admirer, or scholar of Plutarch, especially one who adopts his moral, biographical, or literary approach

Examples

A Plutarchian scholar
As a dedicated Plutarchian, he often referenced the moral lessons found in Plutarch's Lives.
The professor was known as a leading Plutarchian in the field of classical studies.
Her essays reflect a distinctly Plutarchian approach to biography and ethics.
