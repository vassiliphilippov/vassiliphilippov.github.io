trickster

noun
mischievous supernatural figure in folklore
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɪkstɚ / (AmE), / ˈtrɪkstə / (BrE)
a mischievous supernatural figure, often appearing in folklore, who uses cunning or deception to achieve aims, sometimes marked by exaggerated features or appetites

Examples

A mythical trickster
In Native American tradition, the coyote is often portrayed as a trickster.
The trickster in many myths uses cleverness to outwit gods or humans.
The story features a trickster who causes mischief among the villagers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deceiver - someone who deceives or tricks others
  • antonym: hero - the principal character in a story, usually admired for courage or noble qualities
