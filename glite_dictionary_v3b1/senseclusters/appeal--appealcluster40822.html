appeal

verb
cite a law or authority for support
1.8 4.1
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈpiːl/ /; / /ə.ˈpɪl/ (AmE), /ə.ˈpiːl/ /; / /ə.ˈpɪl/ (BrE)
to mention or refer to a law, principle, or authority in support of an argument or action

Examples

To appeal to authority
During negotiations, he appealed to the company's code of ethics.
In her defense, the lawyer appealed to the recent Supreme Court ruling.
The speaker appealed to the audience's sense of justice to strengthen her argument.

Related Terms

  • synonym: invoke - to cite or call on an authority in support of an argument
  • synonym: refer - to mention for information or support
