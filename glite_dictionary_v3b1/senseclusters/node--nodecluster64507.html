node

noun
bulge or swelling of a body part
3.8
Pronunciation: / noʊd / (AmE), / nəʊd / (BrE)
a swelling or small mass in the body, especially in anatomy or medicine (such as a lymph node)

Examples

Lymph node
Surgeons removed a benign node that had developed on his vocal cord.
Swollen lymph nodes can indicate an underlying infection or immune response.
The doctor detected a small node on the patient's thyroid gland during the examination.
An inflamed node can be a sign of an underlying illness.
Lymphocytes are produced in each node and play a crucial role in fighting infection.
The doctor felt a swollen node in my neck during the examination.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lymph node - a small bean-shaped structure that is part of the body's immune system
