bonyness

noun
extreme leanness; prominence of bones
Pronunciation: / ˈboʊni nəs / (AmE), / ˈbəʊni nəs / (BrE)
the condition of being extremely thin, so that bones are prominent, often due to starvation or illness

Examples

Extreme bonyness
After the long illness, her bonyness became a cause for concern.
The bonyness of his arms was obvious beneath his torn shirt.
Veterinarians checked the dog's bonyness before recommending a new diet.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emaciation - extreme thinness due to starvation or illness
