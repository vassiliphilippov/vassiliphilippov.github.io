reciprocal pronoun

noun
pronoun expressing mutual action or relationship
Pronunciation: / rɪˈsɪprəkəl proʊˈnaʊn / (AmE), / rɪˈsɪprəkəl prəˈnaʊn / (BrE)
a pronoun used to indicate that two or more people perform an action mutually or have a mutual relationship, as in 'each other' or 'one another'

Examples

Reciprocal pronoun each other
'Each other' is a common example of a reciprocal pronoun in English.
In the phrase 'They love one another,' 'one another' is a reciprocal pronoun.
The teacher asked the students to use a reciprocal pronoun in their sentences.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reflexive pronoun - a pronoun referring back to the subject of the clause
