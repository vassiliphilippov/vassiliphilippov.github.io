tetravalent

adjective
having a valence of four (chemistry)
6.5 7.7
Pronunciation: /te.trə.ˈveɪ.lənt/ (AmE), /te.trə.ˈveɪ.lənt/ (BrE)
Having a valence of four; capable of forming four chemical bonds.

Examples

Tetravalent carbon atom
Carbon is tetravalent, meaning it can form four single covalent bonds.
In silicon dioxide, the silicon atom acts as a tetravalent element.
Research focuses on the behavior of tetravalent metals in complex molecules.
