ratchet

verb
move incrementally in one direction
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈrætʃɪt / (AmE), / ˈrætʃɪt / (BrE)
to move or cause to move by increments in one direction only, especially using a mechanism that prevents backward movement

Examples

To ratchet up prices
Costs may ratchet higher if the repairs are delayed until next year.
The tension on the cable can ratchet up with each turn of the lever.
With each adjustment, the mechanism will ratchet to a tighter position.

Related Terms

  • synonym: advance - to move forward; to progress
