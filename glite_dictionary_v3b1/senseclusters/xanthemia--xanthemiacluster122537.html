xanthemia

noun
excess carotene in the blood
Pronunciation: / zænˈθiːmiə / (AmE), / zanˈθiːmɪə / (BrE)
a condition marked by excess carotene in the bloodstream, causing a pale yellow or reddish discoloration of the skin

Examples

Xanthemia skin discoloration
A rare case of xanthemia was documented in the medical journal after the patient's skin turned orange-yellow.
High intake of carrots and sweet potatoes can sometimes lead to xanthemia in children.
The doctor explained that xanthemia is usually harmless and resolves once dietary carotene is reduced.
