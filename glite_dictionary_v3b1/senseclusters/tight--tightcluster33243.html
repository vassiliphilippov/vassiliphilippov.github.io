Synonyms

  • tight-fitting
    3.4 0.6/m
    tight-fitting is made to fit closely and firmly to a body or object, while tight means not loose or slack.
  • close-fitting
    3.7 0.2/m
    close-fitting fits closely to the body or object and usually feels less harsh, while tight means firmly fixed and not loose.
  • skin-tight
    3.5 0.3/m
    skin-tight clings very closely to the skin and looks extreme, while tight means fitting closely but not necessarily against the skin.
  • snug
    3.5 0.3/m
    snug fits closely and feels comfortable and warm, while tight means firmly fixed and can be uncomfortable or restrictive.
  • taut
    4.8 4/m
    taut is pulled tight and shows clear tension, while tight covers many senses from firm fitting to closely packed.
  • close
    2.6 18/m
    close sits near items or fits tightly in space, while tight stresses being firmly fixed or not loose in many contexts.
  • close-packed
    4.9 0.04/m
    close-packed describes items pressed together with little space between them, while tight covers fitting closely or being firmly held.
  • packed
    1.3 1/m
    packed means things are pressed together into a small space, while tight can mean closely fitted or firmly fixed around objects.
  • compressed
    3.7 0.02/m
    compressed is made smaller or tighter by pressure, while tight means not loose or slack and can describe fit or tension.
  • dense
    3.2 1/m
    dense is tightly packed matter or crowded space, while tight more often describes being firmly held, stretched, or fitting closely.
