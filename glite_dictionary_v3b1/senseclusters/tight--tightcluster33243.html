tight

adjective
pulled firmly; taut
Pronunciation: / taɪt / (AmE), / taɪt / (BrE)
firmly fixed, stretched, or held; not loose or slack; closely packed or fitted together

Examples

Tight lid
Make sure the lid is tight so the jar doesn't leak.
She tied the rope tight around the package to keep it secure.
The strings on the guitar must be tight to produce the correct notes.
A tight security cordon was established around the embassy.
The city was closed off by a tight blockade, making escape impossible.
The soldiers advanced in a tight formation to minimize exposure to enemy fire.
A tight cloth is preferred for making windproof jackets.
The sheets have a tight weave that gives them a smooth texture.
This fabric is woven so tight that water barely passes through it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compressed - pressed together into less space
  • antonym: leaky - allowing liquid or gas to pass through
  • antonym: loose - not tight; not firmly held or taut
  • synonym: snug - comfortably tight or well-fitted
  • synonym: taut - stretched or pulled tight; not slack
