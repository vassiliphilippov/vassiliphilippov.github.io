huddle together

verb
crowd closely together
3.1 2.6
Pronunciation: /ˈhʌd.əl tə.ˈgeð.ər/ /; / /ˈhʌd.əl tʊ.ˈgeð.ər/ (AmE), /ˈhʌd.əl tə.ˈgeð.ə/ /; / /ˈhʌd.əl tʊ.ˈgeð.ə/ (BrE)
to gather or crowd closely together, usually for warmth, comfort, or in response to fear or uncertainty.

Examples

To huddle together closely
In fear, the children huddled together when the thunder roared outside.
Spectators huddled together under the shelter to avoid the sudden downpour.
The campers decided to huddle together in the tent to keep warm during the storm.
