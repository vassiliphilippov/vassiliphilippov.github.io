eccentric

adjective
not sharing the same center (geometry)
6.5
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈsɛntrɪk / (AmE), / ɪkˈsɛntrɪk / (BrE)
(of two or more objects, especially circles or spheres) not having the same center; not concentric.

Examples

Eccentric circles
An eccentric pulley can cause irregular motion in machinery.
The diagram shows two eccentric circles that overlap slightly.
The earth's orbit is slightly eccentric rather than perfectly circular.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concentric - having a common center (geometry)
