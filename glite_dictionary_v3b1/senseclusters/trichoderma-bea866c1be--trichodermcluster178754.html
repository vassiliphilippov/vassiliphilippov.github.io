Trichoderma

proper noun
fungus genus
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌtraɪkoʊˈdɜːrmə / (AmE), / ˌtrɪkəˈdɜːmə / (BrE)
a genus of fungi commonly used in agriculture and biotechnology for controlling plant diseases and promoting growth

Examples

Trichoderma in research
Researchers are studying Trichoderma for its ability to protect crops from harmful soil fungi.
Many organic farmers add Trichoderma to the soil to improve plant health and yield.
Biotechnology companies use Trichoderma in the production of enzymes for industrial applications.

Related Terms
