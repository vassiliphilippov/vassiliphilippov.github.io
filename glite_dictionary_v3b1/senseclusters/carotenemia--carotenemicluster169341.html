carotenemia

noun
excess carotene in blood causing yellow or red skin
9.9 8.5
Pronunciation: / ˌkæɹ.ə.təˈni.mi.ə / (AmE), / ˌkær.ə.təˈniː.mi.ə / (BrE)
a condition in which excess carotene in the bloodstream causes the skin to turn a yellow or reddish color

Examples

Carotenemia skin color
A diet extremely high in carrots and sweet potatoes can sometimes lead to carotenemia.
Although harmless, carotenemia can alarm patients who mistake it for a more serious condition.
The doctor diagnosed the child's yellowish skin as carotenemia rather than jaundice.
