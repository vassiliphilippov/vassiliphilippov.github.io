Huygenian

adjective
of Huygens's theories
Pronunciation: / haɪˈɡeɪniən / (AmE), / haɪˈɡeɪniən / (BrE)
relating to the theories or principles of Christiaan Huygens, especially in physics such as wave theory

Examples

Huygenian wave theory
The Huygenian explanation of light as a wave was revolutionary in the 17th century.
Many modern optics concepts are based on Huygenian principles.
The debate between Huygenian and Newtonian theories shaped the history of physics.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Newtonian - relating to the theories of Isaac Newton
