canny

adjective
shrewd, clever, and cautious
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkæni / (AmE), / ˈkani / (BrE)
Having or showing shrewdness and good judgment, especially in money or business matters; clever and cautious, especially in dealing with people.

Examples

A canny investor
He's a canny investor who always thinks carefully before taking risks.
Her canny negotiating skills helped the company secure a much better deal.
The detective was canny enough to spot the inconsistencies in the suspect's story.

Related Terms

  • antonym: naive - showing a lack of experience, wisdom, or judgment
  • synonym: shrewd - having or showing sharp powers of judgment; astute
