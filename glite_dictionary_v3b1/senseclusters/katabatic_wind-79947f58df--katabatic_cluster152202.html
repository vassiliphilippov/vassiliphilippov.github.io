katabatic wind

noun
wind from descending cold air
Pronunciation: / ˌkætəˈbætɪk wɪnd / (AmE), / ˌkatəˈbatɪk wɪnd / (BrE)
A wind formed by the downward movement of cold, dense air, typically descending a slope or mountainside.

Examples

A katabatic wind down slope
A katabatic wind swept down the glacier, making the evening much colder than expected.
Scientists studied the effects of the katabatic wind in Antarctica on local weather patterns.
The villagers were accustomed to the fierce katabatic wind that raced down from the mountains each night.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anabatic wind - wind caused by the upward movement of warm air up a slope
