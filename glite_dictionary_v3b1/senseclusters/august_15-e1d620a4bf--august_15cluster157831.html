August 15

proper noun
Catholic feast of the Assumption, August 15
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈɔɡəst fɪfˈtiːn / (AmE), / ˈɔːɡəst fɪfˈtiːn / (BrE)
A festival in the Roman Catholic Church commemorating the Virgin Mary's assumption into heaven at the end of her earthly life; also known as the Feast of the Assumption.

Examples

August 15 feast day
In some countries, August 15 is a public holiday marking a major religious event.
Many Catholics attend Mass on August 15 to celebrate the Assumption of Mary.
The Feast of the Assumption, observed on August 15, is an important date in the liturgical calendar.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Assumption - the Virgin Mary's being taken into heaven (Roman Catholic feast)
  • synonym: Dormition - the belief in Mary's peaceful death and assumption, especially in Eastern Orthodoxy
