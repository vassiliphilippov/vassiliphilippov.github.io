Blessed Virgin

proper noun
Mary, mother of Jesus in Christianity
Pronunciation: / ˈblɛsɪd ˈvɜrdʒɪn / (AmE), / ˈblɛsɪd ˈvɜːdʒɪn / (BrE)
A title for Mary, the mother of Jesus, especially honored in Christian traditions, particularly by Roman Catholics.

Examples

Blessed Virgin Mary
Many cathedrals are dedicated to the Blessed Virgin throughout Europe.
She prayed for guidance from the Blessed Virgin every morning.
The feast of the Blessed Virgin is celebrated annually by many Christians.
