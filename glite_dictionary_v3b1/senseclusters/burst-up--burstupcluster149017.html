burst-up

noun
sudden argument or quarrel
Pronunciation: / ˈbɝːst ʌp / (AmE), / ˈbɜːst ʌp / (BrE)
a sudden argument, disagreement, or quarrel, often resulting in a dramatic end to a relationship or situation

Examples

A burst-up argument
The team's burst-up affected their performance in the tournament.
Their friendship never recovered after a bitter burst-up last autumn.
There was a major burst-up between the manager and his assistant during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blow-up - a sudden argument or angry confrontation
  • synonym: falling-out - a disagreement or quarrel leading to separation
  • antonym: reconciliation - the restoration of friendly relations after a disagreement
