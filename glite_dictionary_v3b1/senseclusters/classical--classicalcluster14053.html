classical

adjective
relating to a civilization's key early period
Pronunciation: / ˈklæsɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈklæsɪkəl / (BrE)
well-known, long-established, or serving as a standard or model; relating to the first significant or authoritative period or style

Examples

Classical style
Many works of art from the classical period of Greece are still admired today.
She specializes in classical Arabic literature in her research.
The university offers courses in classical psychology as it was first developed.
Many designers return to classical silhouettes for inspiration in their collections.
She prefers a classical style of painting that emphasizes harmony and proportion.
The theater was built in a classical architectural style reminiscent of ancient Greece.
