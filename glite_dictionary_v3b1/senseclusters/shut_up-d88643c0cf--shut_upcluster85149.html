shut up

verb
confine someone or something securely
Pronunciation: / ʃʌt ʌp / (AmE), / ʃʌt ʌp / (BrE)
to confine someone or something in a place so that escape or removal is not possible

Examples

To shut up animals
During the quarantine, the animals were shut up in their stalls to prevent disease spread.
The rebels were shut up in the old fortress for weeks.
They shut up the valuables in the safe before leaving for vacation.
