bidder

noun
person who makes a bid (esp. at auction)
1.0 2.5
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪdər / (AmE), / ˈbɪdə / (BrE)
a person or organization that offers to pay a particular amount of money for something, especially at an auction.

Examples

Auction bidder
Each bidder must register before participating in the online auction.
Only one bidder showed up for the government contract.
The highest bidder won the antique vase after a fierce competition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: offeror - a person who makes an offer, especially in legal contexts
  • antonym: seller - person who sells something, the opposite role to bidder at an auction
