centermost

adjective
most central
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛntərˌmoʊst / (AmE), / ˈsɛntəməʊst / (BrE)
being at the exact center or most central point of something (physically or in importance)

Examples

The centermost seat
The centermost seat in the theater offers the best view of the stage.
At the centermost point of the city stands a historic monument.
Her family is the centermost part of her life and happiness.
The centermost table in the restaurant is reserved for special guests.
She placed the vase on the centermost spot of the shelf for balance.
The treasure was hidden in the centermost chamber of the ancient pyramid.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: central - located in the center or of main importance
  • antonym: outermost - farthest from the center
  • antonym: peripheral - on the edge or outer part; not central
