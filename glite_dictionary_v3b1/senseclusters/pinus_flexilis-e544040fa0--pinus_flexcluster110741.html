Pinus flexilis

noun
a North American pine tree species
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪnəs flɛkˈsaɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˈpaɪnəs flɛkˈsɪlɪs / (BrE)
A species of pine tree native to western North America, known for its long needles, very flexible branches, and dark-grey furrowed bark.

Examples

Pinus flexilis tree
Researchers studied the Pinus flexilis to learn about its adaptation to mountainous regions.
The Pinus flexilis can survive harsh winds thanks to its flexible branches.
We identified a Pinus flexilis by its long needles and distinctive bark.

Related Terms

  • synonym: limber pine - another common name for Pinus flexilis
