circle

verb
travel around something
1.0 1.6
Pronunciation: /ˈsɜːr.kəl/ (AmE), /ˈsɜː.kəl/ (BrE)
to move all the way around something or to travel in a circular path above or around something

Examples

Birds circle the sky
Migratory birds often circle the area before choosing a place to rest.
The spacecraft will circle the planet before landing.
We plan to circle the island by boat during our trip.
A hawk circled above the open field, searching for prey.
The helicopter circled the building several times before landing.
Vultures were circling over the carcass in the desert sun.
