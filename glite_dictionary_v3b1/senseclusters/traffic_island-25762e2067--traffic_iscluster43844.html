traffic island

noun
raised area dividing lanes of traffic
6.0 2.5
Pronunciation: / ˈtræfɪk ˌaɪlænd / (AmE), / ˈtræfɪk ˌaɪlənd / (BrE)
a raised or curbed area in the middle of a road, from which vehicles are excluded, providing a safe space for pedestrians or controlling traffic flow

Examples

A traffic island in road
A row of flowers was planted on the traffic island to beautify the street.
Pedestrians waited on the traffic island before crossing the next part of the busy intersection.
The traffic island helped to separate the opposing lanes and improve safety.
