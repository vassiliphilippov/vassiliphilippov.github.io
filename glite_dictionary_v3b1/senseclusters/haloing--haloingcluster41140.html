haloing

noun
ring artifact in images
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈheɪloʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈheɪləʊɪŋ / (BrE)
the appearance of a bright or colored ring or glow around an object in an image or photograph, often due to editing, processing, or optical effects

Examples

Haloing in photos
Improper editing can cause haloing around the edges of a photo.
The photographer noticed haloing in the sky after increasing the image contrast.
Using the wrong sharpening filter may result in visible haloing around objects.
The camera lens caused haloing around the streetlights in the photograph.
Some people with eye conditions may notice haloing when looking at bright lights at night.
Foggy weather often results in haloing around car headlights, making driving more difficult.

Related Terms

  • synonym: artifact - an unwanted visual effect in a digital image
  • synonym: ghosting - a faint duplicate image or visual distortion
  • synonym: glare - uncomfortably bright or dazzling light
  • synonym: halo - a ring of light appearing to surround an object
