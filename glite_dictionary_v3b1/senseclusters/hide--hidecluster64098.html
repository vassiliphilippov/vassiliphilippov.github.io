hide

verb
make difficult to see or find
Pronunciation: / haɪd / (AmE), / haɪd / (BrE)
to put or keep someone or something out of sight; to conceal or make difficult to see, find, or understand

Examples

To hide the truth
He used complicated language to hide the real meaning of his report.
She tried to hide her disappointment behind a smile.
The document was hidden from public view for years.
A blanket of mist hid the lake at dawn.
The thick fog hid the mountains from view.
Uncertainty and speculation hide the true origins of the painting.
After hearing the intruders, she quickly hid under the bed to avoid being seen.
He was hiding in the basement until the storm passed.
The children hid behind the bushes during their game of hide-and-seek.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conceal - to prevent something from being seen or known about
  • antonym: reveal - to make something known or visible
