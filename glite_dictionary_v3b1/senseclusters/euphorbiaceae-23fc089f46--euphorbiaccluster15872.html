Euphorbiaceae

noun
plant family (spurge)
Pronunciation: / juˌfɔr.biˈeɪ.siˌaɪ /; / juːˌfɔːrbiˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / juːˌfɔː.bɪˈeɪ.sɪˌiː /; / juːˌfɔːbɪˈeɪsiː / (BrE)
a large family of flowering plants, known as the spurge family, which includes many herbs, shrubs, and trees such as poinsettia, rubber tree, and cassava

Examples

The Euphorbiaceae family
Many members of the Euphorbiaceae family produce a milky sap that can be toxic if ingested.
Some ornamental plants, such as the poinsettia, are well-known representatives of the Euphorbiaceae.
The rubber tree, an important source of natural rubber, belongs to the Euphorbiaceae.
Botanists have identified more than 6,000 species within the Euphorbiaceae.
Cassava, a staple food in many countries, belongs to the Euphorbiaceae.
The Euphorbiaceae family contains many species with milky sap that can be toxic.
