ordinary

noun
judge in a probate court (legal term)
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrdəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˈɔːdnəri / (BrE)
(legal) A judge with authority to administer probate matters, especially in a probate court.

Examples

Ordinary of the court
He appealed the decision made by the ordinary in the probate matter.
The ordinary was responsible for granting probate in the county.
Under English law, an ordinary has power over wills and estates.
