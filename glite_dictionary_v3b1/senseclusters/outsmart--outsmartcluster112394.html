outsmart

verb
defeat by being more clever
4.1 1.51/m
Pronunciation: / a ätÀàsm…ërt / (AmE), / a ätÀàsm…ëÀêt / (BrE)
to defeat or gain an advantage over someone by being more clever, intelligent, or skillful

Examples

To outsmart an opponent
No matter how tricky the puzzle was, he always found a way to outsmart it.
She managed to outsmart her opponents in the debate with a clever argument.
The thief tried to escape, but the police outsmarted him by anticipating his moves.
No one could outsmart the clever detective during the investigation.
She managed to outsmart her competitors in the final round of the contest.
The fox tried to outsmart the hunters by doubling back on its tracks.
