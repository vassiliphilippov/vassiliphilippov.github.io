trench warfare

noun
prolonged, deadlocked struggle or contest
3.3
Pronunciation: / trɛntʃ ˈwɔrˌfɛr / (AmE), / trɛntʃ ˈwɔːfɛə / (BrE)
A situation or struggle, especially a prolonged one, in which neither side can defeat the other or make much progress, often used figuratively for difficult and deadlocked competition.

Examples

Corporate trench warfare
After months of trench warfare between the two companies, neither could claim victory in the market.
Political debates often descend into trench warfare, with both parties refusing to compromise.
The negotiations became a form of trench warfare, with each side stubbornly defending its position.

Related Terms

  • antonym: breakthrough - an important and dramatic advance; an end to a prolonged stalemate
  • synonym: deadlock - a situation in which no progress can be made
  • synonym: stalemate - a situation in which further action is blocked; deadlock
