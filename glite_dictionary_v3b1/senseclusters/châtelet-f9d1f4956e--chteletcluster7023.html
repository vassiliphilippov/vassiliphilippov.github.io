châtelet

noun
small fortified gatehouse
9.9
Pronunciation: / ʃætəˈleɪ / (AmE), / ʃæˈtɛleɪ / (BrE)
a small castle or fortified gatehouse, especially one guarding an entrance in medieval architecture

Examples

Medieval châtelet gate
The ancient châtelet stood at the entrance to the walled city, protecting it from invaders.
Visitors admired the stonework of the restored châtelet at the castle's main gate.
A narrow bridge led directly to the châtelet, which once controlled access to the fortress.
