strapline

noun
secondary heading above a main title
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈstræpˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈstræpˌlaɪn / (BrE)
a secondary heading, especially one printed above a main headline or title, used to add emphasis or information

Examples

A strapline above headline
The new advertising campaign featured a bold strapline to attract attention.
The newspaper’s strapline teased a major story inside.
They designed a catchy strapline to run above the headline on the front page.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: headline - the main title of a newspaper story or article
  • degree lower: subheading - a heading given to a subsection of a piece of writing
