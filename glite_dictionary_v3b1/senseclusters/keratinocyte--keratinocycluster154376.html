keratinocyte

noun
skin cell type
9.4
Pronunciation: / kəˈrætənoʊˌsaɪt / (AmE), / kəˈrætɪnəˌsaɪt / (BrE)
a type of cell in the skin that produces keratin and forms the outer layer of the skin

Examples

Skin keratinocytes
The outermost layer of your skin is made up of millions of keratinocytes.
A keratinocyte produces keratin, which helps protect the skin from damage.
Scientists study keratinocytes to better understand skin diseases and wound healing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: epidermis - the outermost layer of the skin
  • antonym: melanocyte - a skin cell that produces melanin pigment
