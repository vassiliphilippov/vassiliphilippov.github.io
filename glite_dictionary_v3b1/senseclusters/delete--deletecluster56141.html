delete

verb
remove or erase information
1.0
Pronunciation: / dɪˈlit /; / dəˈlit / (AmE), / dɪˈliːt / (BrE)
to remove or erase information so that it no longer exists or is visible (such as text, files, or data)

Examples

To delete a file
He accidentally deleted an important file from his computer.
Please delete my name from your list if I no longer qualify.
You can delete old messages to free up space on your phone.
After you delete a message, it cannot be recovered from your inbox.
I accidentally deleted all my photos from the computer yesterday.
Please make sure to delete any sensitive information before sending the file.
He asked the editor to delete the final paragraph from the article.
She accidentally deleted an important file from her computer.
You can delete individual items from your shopping cart by clicking the 'remove' button.

Related Terms

  • synonym: erase - to remove written or recorded information completely
  • antonym: insert - to put something into something else, especially text or data
