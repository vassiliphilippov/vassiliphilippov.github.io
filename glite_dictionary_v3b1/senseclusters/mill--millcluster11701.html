mill

verb
move aimlessly or in confusion (as a group)
Pronunciation: / mɪl / (AmE), / mɪl / (BrE)
(of people or animals) to move around in a confused or aimless manner, often in a group.

Examples

People mill around
Cattle milled in the pen, confused by the sudden noise.
People began to mill about in the hallway after the fire alarm sounded.
While they waited for the bus, the students milled around the stop, chatting and laughing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scatter - move or spread in different directions disorderly
  • synonym: wander - move about with no clear direction or purpose
