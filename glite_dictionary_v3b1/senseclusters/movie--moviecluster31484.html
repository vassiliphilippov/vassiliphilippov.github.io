movie

noun
motion picture; film for entertainment
1.0 274/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàmuÀêvi / (AmE), / ÀàmuÀêvi / (BrE)
A film; a story or event recorded by a camera as a set of moving images, usually shown in a theater or on television.

Examples

A movie theater
She dreams of directing her own movie one day.
The movie received excellent reviews from critics.
We watched a movie together at the cinema last night.

Synonyms

  • film
    1.0 193/m
    film is the standard term critics use in arts and reviews, while movie is common everyday speech for popular entertainment.
  • motion picture
    3.0 3.45/m
    motion picture is a formal phrase often used in law or archives, while movie is the casual word people say every day.
  • picture show
    2.5 0.45/m
    picture show refers to films shown in a cinema long ago, while movie is the general modern word for filmed stories.
  • flick
    4.1 3.83/m
    flick is an informal, lively word for a short, fun film, while movie is the broader everyday term for any filmed story.
  • pic
    8.1 0.64/m
    pic is a very informal short form people use in speech, while movie is the full common word for a filmed story.
