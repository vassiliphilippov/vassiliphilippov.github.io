unscrupulously

adverb
in a morally unprincipled way
8.0 0.04/m
Pronunciation: /  ånÀàskruÀêpj…ôl…ôsli / (AmE), /  ånÀàskruÀêpj äl…ôsli / (BrE)
In a way that shows no moral principles or scruples; without regard for what is right or wrong.

Examples

To unscrupulously manipulate data
He unscrupulously manipulated the data to support his false claims.
She unscrupulously took credit for her colleague's achievements.
The company unscrupulously exploited its workers without concern for their well-being.

Synonyms

  • unethically
    7.0 0.26/m
    unethically describes actions that break moral rules in work or law, while unscrupulously describes acting without moral principles for personal gain.
  • immorally
    7.2 0.13/m
    immorally means acting against common moral beliefs or values, while unscrupulously means acting without concern for right or wrong.
  • corruptly
    7.1 0.14/m
    corruptly describes behaviour done for personal gain, often by officials, while unscrupulously means acting without moral principles in any context.
  • dishonestly
    3.5 0.29/m
    dishonestly means trying to deceive or cheat others, while unscrupulously means acting without moral principles in a broader, ruthless way.
