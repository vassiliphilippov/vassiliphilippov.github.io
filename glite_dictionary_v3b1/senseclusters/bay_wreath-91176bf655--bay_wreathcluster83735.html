bay wreath

noun
laurel wreath symbolizing victory
5.0
Pronunciation: / beɪ riθ / (AmE), / beɪ riːθ / (BrE)
A wreath made of laurel leaves, typically worn on the head as a symbol of victory or honor in ancient Greece and Rome.

Examples

A bay wreath crown
In ancient Greece, the champion athlete was crowned with a bay wreath.
The poet received a golden bay wreath as a mark of distinction.
The sculpture depicted Caesar adorned with a bay wreath upon his brow.
