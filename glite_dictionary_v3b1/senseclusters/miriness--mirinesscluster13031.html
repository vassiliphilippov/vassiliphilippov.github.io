miriness

noun
state of being swampy or boggy
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪrinəs / (AmE), / ˈmʌɪrɪnəs / (BrE)
The state or quality of being swampy, boggy, or muddy.

Examples

The miriness of land
Despite its miriness, the marsh supports a variety of unique plant species.
The miriness of the field made walking difficult after the heavy rain.
Travelers complained about the miriness that slowed their carriages along the old country roads.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dryness - the state of being dry, opposite of wet or boggy
  • synonym: miry - swampy or muddy in texture or appearance
