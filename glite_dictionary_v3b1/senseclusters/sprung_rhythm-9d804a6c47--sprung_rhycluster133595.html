sprung rhythm

noun
poetic rhythm imitating speech patterns
Pronunciation: / sprʌŋ ˈrɪðəm / (AmE), / sprʌŋ ˈrɪðəm / (BrE)
a poetic rhythm designed to replicate the natural patterns of speech, characterized by varying numbers of unstressed syllables between stressed ones

Examples

Sprung rhythm in poetry
Gerard Manley Hopkins is known for popularizing sprung rhythm in English poetry.
Many readers find sprung rhythm challenging but rewarding to analyze in literary studies.
The poet experimented with sprung rhythm to bring a more natural cadence to her verses.
