hue and cry

noun
loud public outcry or protest
8.6 6.1
Pronunciation: /hjuː ənd kraɪ/ /; / /juː ənd kraɪ/ /; / /hjuː ænd kraɪ/ (AmE), /hjuː ənd kraɪ/ /; / /juː ənd kraɪ/ /; / /hjuː ænd kraɪ/ (BrE)
a loud and persistent outcry from many people, often expressing protest, alarm, or demand for action

Examples

The hue and cry rises
Despite the hue and cry raised by the public, the policy was implemented anyway.
The government's decision was met with a hue and cry from citizens across the country.
When the scandal broke, there was a hue and cry demanding accountability from the officials.

  • synonym: uproar - a state of noisy confusion, especially among a crowd
