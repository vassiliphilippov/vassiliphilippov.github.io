dredging bucket

noun
bucket used for dredging riverbeds
Pronunciation: / ˈdrɛdʒɪŋ ˈbʌkɪt / (AmE), / ˈdrɛdʒɪŋ ˈbʌkɪt / (BrE)
a bucket designed for lifting and removing material from the bottom of a channel or riverbed, typically used in dredging operations

Examples

A dredging bucket tool
A large dredging bucket can clear debris from the harbor floor efficiently.

The crew lowered the dredging bucket into the river to remove the built-up silt.
