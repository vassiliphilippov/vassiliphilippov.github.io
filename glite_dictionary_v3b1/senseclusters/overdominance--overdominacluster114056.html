overdominance

noun
heterozygote advantage
9.9
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ˈdɑː.mɪ.nəns/ /; / /oʊ.vər.ˈdɑː.mə.nəns/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ˈdɒm.ɪ.nəns/ /; / /əʊ.və.ˈdɒ.mə.nəns/ (BrE)
a genetic situation in which a heterozygote has greater fitness than either of its homozygous forms

Examples

Overdominance in genetics
The phenomenon of overdominance can help explain why certain genetic diseases persist in populations.
Researchers observed overdominance in plants that showed greater resistance to disease when heterozygous.
Understanding overdominance is important for studying the evolution of genetic diversity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: heterosis - increased vigor or other superior qualities arising from the crossbreeding of genetically different plants or animals
  • antonym: underdominance - a genetic situation where the heterozygote has lower fitness than either homozygote
