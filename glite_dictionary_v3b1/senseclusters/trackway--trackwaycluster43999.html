trackway

noun
timber walkway in marsh
8.4
Pronunciation: /ˈtræk.weɪ/ (AmE), /ˈtræk.weɪ/ (BrE)
a path or walkway, often constructed of timber, built across wet or marshy ground, especially as found in archaeological sites

Examples

Wooden trackway
The ancient trackway was discovered beneath layers of peat in the bog.
Workers carefully preserved the wooden trackway found during the construction project.
Tourists can walk along a reconstructed trackway to experience how people crossed marshes centuries ago.
