crackable

adjective
prone to cracking
5.1
Pronunciation: /ˈkræk.ə.bəl/ (AmE), /ˈkræk.ə.bəl/ (BrE)
able to develop cracks or fissures, especially when exposed to stress, temperature changes, or aging.

Examples

Crackable ceramic vase
The old ceramic vase was so crackable that it needed to be handled with extreme care.
After years in the sun, the plastic pipes became crackable and started to leak.
If the paint is crackable, it may peel off the wall during the winter months.

  • antonym: unbreakable - not able to be broken or cracked
