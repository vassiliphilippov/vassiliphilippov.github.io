defensive

adjective
attempting to justify oneself against criticism
1.2 3.3
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfɛnsɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛnsɪv / (BrE)
Behaving in a way that shows you feel people are criticizing or attacking you and you want to justify your actions, words, or opinions.

Examples

Defensive attitude
She sounded defensive during the interview when asked about the company's mistakes.
There's no need to get defensive; we're just looking for honest feedback.
Whenever someone questions his decisions, he becomes quite defensive and tries to explain himself quickly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aggressive - ready or likely to attack or confront
  • synonym: protective - aiming to protect or shield from harm or injury
