microphthalmia

noun
abnormally small eyes
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪkroʊˌθælˈmiːə / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəʊˌθælˈmiːə / (BrE)
a congenital condition in which one or both eyes are abnormally small

Examples

Congenital microphthalmia condition
Infants diagnosed with microphthalmia often require specialized medical care and support.
Genetic counseling is recommended for families with a history of microphthalmia.
Some children with microphthalmia may benefit from prosthetic eyes for cosmetic reasons.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: anophthalmia - absence of one or both eyes at birth
  • antonym: macrophthalmia - abnormally large eyes
