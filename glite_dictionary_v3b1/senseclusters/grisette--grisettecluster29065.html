grisette

noun
light gray fabric
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɡrɪˈzɛt / (AmE), / ɡrɪˈzɛt / (BrE)
a lightweight, inexpensive gray or mixed-fibre fabric used historically for working clothes

Examples

Grisette fabric apron
The tailor used soft grisette to make aprons for the villagers.
In the 19th century, many workers wore clothes made from grisette because it was affordable.
She admired the simple dress sewn from grisette displayed in the museum.

Related Terms

  • antonym: denim - a sturdy cotton twill fabric, often blue
  • synonym: muslin - a lightweight cotton fabric
