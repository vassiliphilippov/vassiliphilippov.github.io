barrater

noun
one guilty of barratry (legal mischief)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbærətər / (AmE), / ˈbærətə / (BrE)
A person who is guilty of barratry, especially someone who instigates frequent lawsuits or quarrels.

Examples

A barrater in court
Accused of being a barrater, he was barred from initiating further legal action.
Historically, a barrater could be punished for inciting unnecessary litigation.
The court declared him a barrater for repeatedly filing baseless lawsuits.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barratry - persistent incitement of lawsuits or quarrels, esp. for profit or harassment (law)
