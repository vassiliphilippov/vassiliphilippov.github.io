trans-

x
prefix for opposite sides
Pronunciation: / træns / (AmE), / tranz / (BrE)
a prefix used in chemistry and biology to indicate that two similar parts of a molecule are on opposite sides of a double bond, ring, or axis

Examples

Trans- isomer
In organic chemistry, a trans- isomer has groups on opposite sides of a double bond.
The trans- configuration affects the physical properties of the molecule.
Scientists distinguish between trans- and cis- forms when analyzing molecular structures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cis- - a prefix indicating similar parts are on the same side of a double bond or ring
