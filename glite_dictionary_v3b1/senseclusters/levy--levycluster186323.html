levy

verb
impose and collect officially (e.g., taxes, fines)
5.4 1.12/m

(of an authority) to impose and collect a tax, fee, fine, or other charge officially.

Examples

To levy a tax
If you fail to pay, the agency may levy the amount directly from your account.
The court has the authority to levy fines against violators of the law.
The government decided to levy a new tax on luxury goods.

Synonyms

  • tax
    1.9 12/m
    tax means charging money on income, goods, or activities, while levy refers to officially imposing and collecting a tax or other charge.
  • impose
    4.2 31/m
    impose means officially order or force a rule, tax, or punishment, while levy refers specifically to charging and collecting a tax or fee.
