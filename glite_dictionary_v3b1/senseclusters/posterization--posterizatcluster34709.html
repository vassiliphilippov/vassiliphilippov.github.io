posterization

noun
image color reduction
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊstərəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpəʊstəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
a process that reduces the number of tones or colors in an image, resulting in abrupt changes between color areas

Examples

Posterization of a photo
The posterization of the image gave it a bold, graphic look with only a few colors.
Graphic designers sometimes use posterization to create striking visual effects in advertisements.
After applying posterization, the photograph lost its smooth color transitions and appeared more abstract.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gradation - a smooth transition between colors or tones
  • synonym: solarization - a photographic effect causing reversed tones or colors
