infrared spectrum

noun
spectrum of infrared radiation
8.2 4.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.frəˈrɛd ˈspɛk.trəm / (AmE), / ˌɪn.frəˈrɛd ˈspɛk.trəm / (BrE)
The range of wavelengths of electromagnetic radiation that are longer than visible light but shorter than microwaves, typically perceived as heat.

Examples

The infrared spectrum range
Astronomers used the infrared spectrum to detect heat signatures from distant planets.
Certain molecules absorb specific wavelengths in the infrared spectrum, revealing their chemical structure.
Proteins can be analyzed by studying their infrared spectrum in the laboratory.
