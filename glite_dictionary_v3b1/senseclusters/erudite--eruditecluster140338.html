erudite

adjective
having great scholarly knowledge
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈɛr.jəˌdaɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛr.juː.daɪt / (BrE)
having or showing great scholarly knowledge; deeply learned or educated

Examples

An erudite scholar
His erudite remarks impressed everyone at the academic conference.
Many considered her one of the most erudite scholars in her field.
The erudite lecturer captivated the audience with her insightful analysis of classical literature.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness
  • synonym: learned - having or showing much knowledge, scholarly
