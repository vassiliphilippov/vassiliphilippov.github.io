allogeneic

adjective
of genetically different cells/tissues (same species)
Pronunciation: / ˌæləˈdʒiːniɡ / (AmE), / ˌalə(ʊ)dʒɪˈniːɪk / (BrE)
relating to cells or tissues that are genetically different although within the same species, especially those used in transplantation.

Examples

An allogeneic transplant
An allogeneic bone marrow transplant involves a donor whose genetic makeup differs from the recipient's.
Doctors monitor closely for immune reactions after allogeneic stem cell transplants.
The risk of rejection is higher with allogeneic tissue than with autologous tissue.

Related Terms

  • antonym: autologous - cells or tissues derived from the same individual
  • antonym: syngeneic - cells or tissues from genetically identical individuals (e.g., identical twins)
