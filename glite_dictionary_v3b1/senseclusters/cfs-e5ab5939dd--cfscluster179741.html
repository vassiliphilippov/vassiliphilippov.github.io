CFS

noun
cold-formed steel
Pronunciation: / si ɛf ɛs / (AmE), / si ɛf ɛs / (BrE)
cold-formed steel: steel shaped at room temperature into structural sections, commonly used for light framing in construction

Examples

CFS wall studs
The engineer specified CFS for the interior wall studs due to its strength and light weight.
Many modern buildings use CFS because it is easy to transport and assemble on site.
Contractors prefer CFS for framing in areas where wood is not suitable.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hot-rolled steel - steel shaped at high temperatures, often used for heavier structural elements
  • synonym: structural steel - steel used in construction for making structural frameworks
