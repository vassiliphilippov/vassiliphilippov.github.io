incoherent

adjective
unable to speak clearly or fluently
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnkoʊˈhɪrənt / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəʊˈhɪərənt / (BrE)
unable to express yourself clearly or fluently, especially due to emotion, confusion, or illness.

Examples

Incoherent with fear
After hearing the shocking news, she became incoherent with shock and couldn't say a word.
Grief can make a person incoherent, struggling to find the right words.
His incoherent explanation only added to the confusion in the meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: articulate - able to express thoughts and feelings clearly and easily
  • synonym: tongue-tied - unable to speak easily because of shyness or embarrassment
