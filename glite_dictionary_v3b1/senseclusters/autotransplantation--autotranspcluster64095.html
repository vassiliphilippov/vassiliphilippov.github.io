autotransplantation

noun
tissue transfer (self)
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˌɔːtoʊˌtrænsplænˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəʊˌtrænsplænˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a medical procedure in which a person's own tissue or cells are moved or transplanted to another part of their body

Examples

Autotransplantation of tissue
The surgeon performed autotransplantation of a kidney to restore blood flow in the patient.
Dental autotransplantation can be used to replace a lost tooth using another tooth from the same person.
After the accident, autotransplantation of skin helped the burn victim recover more quickly.
The patient underwent autotransplantation of bone marrow after intensive chemotherapy.
Doctors recommended autotransplantation to restore kidney function using the patient's own tissue.
Advances in autotransplantation have improved recovery times for many surgical procedures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: allotransplantation - transplantation of tissue between two individuals of the same species
  • synonym: autograft - a tissue graft from one part of a person's body to another part of the same body
  • synonym: transplantation - the process of moving tissue or an organ from one body to another
