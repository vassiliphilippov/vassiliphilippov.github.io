Angoumois moth

noun
grain-infesting moth
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.ɡuːˈmwɑː mɑːθ / (AmE), / ˌæn.ɡuːˈmwɑː mɒθ / (BrE)
a small moth (Sitotroga cerealella) whose larvae feed on the kernels of stored grains, often causing damage to crops

Examples

The Angoumois moth larvae
Farmers took precautions to prevent the Angoumois moth from damaging their stored wheat.
Infestation by the Angoumois moth can lead to significant losses in cereal grain quality.
The larvae of the Angoumois moth bore into kernels, making them unsuitable for consumption.
