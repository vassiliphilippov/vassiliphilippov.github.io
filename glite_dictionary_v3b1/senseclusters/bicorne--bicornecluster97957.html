bicorne

noun
two-cornered historical hat
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪˌkɔrn / (AmE), / ˈbaɪˌkɔːn / (BrE)
a hat with a wide brim turned up on two sides to form two points, worn especially in the late 18th and early 19th centuries.

Examples

A bicorne hat
Collectors prize the bicorne for its association with 19th-century military history.
Napoleon Bonaparte is famously depicted wearing a bicorne on the battlefield.
The officer wore a perfectly preserved bicorne as part of his ceremonial uniform.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: tricorn - a hat with three points, popular in the 18th century
