discharge

noun
electrical conduction through a gas
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪsˌtʃɑrdʒ /; / ˈdɪs.tʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / ˈdɪs.tʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
an electrical event where charge moves or is released, often producing light or other effects

Examples

An electric discharge
A strong discharge occurred between the metal plates, producing a bright flash of light.
Neon signs glow because of the electrical discharge through the gas inside the tubes.
Scientists studied the discharge in the atmosphere to better understand lightning.
Lightning is caused by the discharge of static electricity in the atmosphere.
The battery's discharge can be dangerous if not handled properly.
There was a bright flash as the capacitor produced a sudden discharge of electricity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: absorption - the process of taking in energy, opposite of giving off
  • synonym: arc - a visible electrical discharge between two electrodes
  • synonym: emission - the act of giving off something such as energy or gas
  • synonym: release - the act of allowing something to escape or be set free
  • synonym: spark - a brief and sudden electrical discharge
