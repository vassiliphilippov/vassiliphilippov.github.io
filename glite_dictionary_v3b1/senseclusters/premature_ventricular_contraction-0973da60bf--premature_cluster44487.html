premature ventricular contraction

noun
early heartbeat from the ventricles
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːməˈtʃʊr vɛnˈtrɪkjələr kənˈtrækʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːməˈtʃɔː vɛnˈtrɪkjʊlə kənˈtrækʃən / (BrE)
an irregular heartbeat caused by early contraction of the ventricles, often perceived as a skipped heartbeat and possibly a precursor to more severe arrhythmias

Examples

Frequent premature ventricular contractions
Frequent premature ventricular contractions can sometimes indicate underlying heart disease.
He wore a heart monitor to track episodes of premature ventricular contraction during his daily activities.
The doctor explained that a premature ventricular contraction is often felt as a skipped beat in the chest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arrhythmia - an abnormal heart rhythm
  • degree higher: ventricular fibrillation - rapid, irregular heart rhythm originating in the ventricles
