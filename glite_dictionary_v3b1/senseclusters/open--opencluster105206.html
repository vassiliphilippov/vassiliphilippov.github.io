(of an interval in mathematics) not including either of its endpoints.

Examples

Open interval
In mathematics, an open interval does not include its endpoints.
The set (0, 1) is an open interval because it contains all points between 0 and 1, but not 0 or 1.
When solving inequalities, solutions are often represented as open intervals if the endpoints are not included.
