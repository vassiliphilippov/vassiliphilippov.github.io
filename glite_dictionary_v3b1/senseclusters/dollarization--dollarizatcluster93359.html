dollarization

noun
use of foreign currency
Pronunciation: / ˌdɑːlərɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɒləraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the use of the US dollar or another foreign currency in place of the local currency, either officially or unofficially

Examples

Dollarization of economy
The government considered dollarization to stabilize the economy after years of inflation.
Many businesses in the region have adopted dollarization to attract international customers.
Critics argue that dollarization reduces a country's control over its own monetary policy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: de-dollarization - the process of moving away from using the US dollar as a country's currency
  • synonym: euroization - the use of the euro instead of a local currency
  • antonym: localization - the use of local currency or adaptation to local conditions
