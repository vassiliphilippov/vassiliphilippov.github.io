plausibly

adverb
in a way that seems reasonable or likely
6.3 1.41/m
Pronunciation: / Ààpl…îÀêz…ôbli / (AmE), / Ààpl…îÀêz…ôbli / (BrE)
In a way that seems reasonable or probable, or is likely to be true based on available evidence.

Examples

Explain plausibly
He explained the mistake very plausibly, so everyone believed him.
Her theory was plausibly supported by the evidence found at the scene.
The witness answered the lawyer's questions plausibly, but some facts didn't add up.

Synonyms

  • credibly
    4.4 0.95/m
    credibly suggests something is supported by evidence and experts, while plausibly means it appears reasonable though not fully proven.
  • believably
    3.2 0.17/m
    believably feels true to an audience because listeners accept it easily, while plausibly means it seems likely from the available evidence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: probably - likely to happen or be the case
