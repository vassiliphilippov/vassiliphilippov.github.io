resensitization

noun
restoring light sensitivity
8.6
Pronunciation: /riː.sen.sɪ.tə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /rɪ.sen.sɪ.tə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /rɪ.sen.sɪ.tə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /riː.sen.sɪ.tə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of restoring sensitivity to light in photographic film, paper, or electronic imaging sensors after it has been reduced or lost

Examples

Resensitization of film
The technician performed resensitization on the old film to make it usable again.
Advances in resensitization techniques have improved the quality of digital imaging sensors.
After exposure to bright light, resensitization was necessary to restore the paper's sensitivity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: desensitization - the process of making something less sensitive to light
  • synonym: sensitization - the process of making something sensitive to light
