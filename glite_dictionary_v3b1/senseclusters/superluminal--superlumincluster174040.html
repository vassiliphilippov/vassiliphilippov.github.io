superluminal

noun
faster-than-light object
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərˈluːmɪnəl / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˈluːmɪnəl / (BrE)
a particle, signal, or motion that moves or appears to move faster than the speed of light, especially in astrophysics

Examples

A superluminal particle
Scientists debated whether the observed jet was truly superluminal or just an optical illusion.
The concept of a superluminal signal challenges our understanding of physics.
Some theories predict the existence of superluminal particles, though none have been confirmed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: faster-than-light - moving or appearing to move faster than the speed of light
  • antonym: subluminal - moving at a speed less than that of light
