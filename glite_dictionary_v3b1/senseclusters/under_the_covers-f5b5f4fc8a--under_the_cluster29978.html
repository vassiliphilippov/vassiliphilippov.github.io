under the covers

adverb
hidden; behind the scenes
6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈʌn.dər ðə ˈkʌv.ərz/ /; / /ˈʌn.dər ðiː ˈkʌv.ərz/ (AmE), /ˈʌn.də ðə ˈkʌv.əz/ /; / /ˈʌn.də ðiː ˈkʌv.əz/ (BrE)
(figurative) hidden from view or operating behind the scenes, especially referring to technical processes or decisions that are not visible to users or the public.

Examples

Runs under the covers
The app runs under the covers, processing data without user intervention.
Journalists discovered decisions were being made under the covers, away from public scrutiny.
The update fixes several bugs that previously ran under the covers on older devices.

Related Terms

  • synonym: behind the scenes - out of public view; work or activity that happens privately rather than openly
  • synonym: under the hood - (computing) the internal parts or workings of a system, often not seen by users
