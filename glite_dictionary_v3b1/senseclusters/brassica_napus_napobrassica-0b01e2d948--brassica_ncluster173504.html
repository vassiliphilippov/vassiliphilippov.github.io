A cruciferous plant with a thick, bulbous, edible yellow root, commonly known as rutabaga or swede.

Examples

Brassica napus napobrassica root
Brassica napus napobrassica is widely cultivated for its nutritious root, known as rutabaga in North America.
Diced and roasted, the root of Brassica napus napobrassica adds a mild, sweet flavor to stews and soups.
Farmers often rotate crops by planting Brassica napus napobrassica to enrich the soil and provide a winter vegetable.
