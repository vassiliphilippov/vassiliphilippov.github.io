Synonyms

  • go up
    1.5 21/m
    go up is an informal phrasal verb for increasing in amount or value, while rise is neutral and wider in use.
  • climb
    2.2 10/m
    climb shows an upward movement often for prices or rank, while rise is more general for any increase.
  • gain
    1.1 32/m
    gain suggests getting more by effort or change, often with an object, while rise simply means to increase.
  • mount
    3.6 7.25/m
    mount describes a gradual, steady increase often in pressure or costs, while rise covers both quick and slow increases.

Related Terms

  • synonym: climb - to go up, especially gradually
  • antonym: decrease - to become less in amount, intensity, or degree
  • antonym: fall - to become lower in mood or spirit; to feel dejected
  • synonym: increase - to become greater in size, amount, or degree
  • synonym: meet the challenge - to deal successfully with something difficult
