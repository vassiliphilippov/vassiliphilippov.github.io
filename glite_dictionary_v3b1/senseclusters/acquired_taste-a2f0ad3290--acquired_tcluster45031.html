acquired taste

noun
preference developed over time
3.4
Pronunciation: / əˈkwaɪɚd teɪst / (AmE), / əˈkwaɪəd teɪst / (BrE)
a liking for something that is gained only after repeated exposure or experience

Examples

An acquired taste
Blue cheese is an acquired taste—not everyone likes it at first.
For many, black coffee is an acquired taste that grows with time.
Opera remains an acquired taste for those unfamiliar with classical music.

Related Terms

  • synonym: taste - the sense of what is pleasant or unpleasant in food or drink
