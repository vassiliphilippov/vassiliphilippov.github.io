immobilize

verb
(finance) convert assets to fixed capital
3.3 6.1
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmoʊbəˌlaɪz /; / ɪˈmoʊ.bəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ɪˈməʊbəlaɪz /; / ɪˈməʊ.bɪ.laɪz /; / ɪˈməʊbɪˌlaɪz /; / ɪˈməʊbɪlaɪz / (BrE)
(finance) to make assets or capital unavailable, fixed, or inactive

Examples

To immobilize funds
Banks often immobilize certain assets to support long-term projects.
Financial regulations can influence how and when firms choose to immobilize their resources.
The company decided to immobilize its cash reserves by investing in new manufacturing plants.
By choosing to immobilize their funds, investors miss potential short-term profit opportunities.
Due to economic uncertainty, the government decided to immobilize a portion of its foreign currency reserves.
Some firms immobilize capital in long-term investments rather than keeping it in liquid assets.
Banks have the power to immobilize funds if they suspect illegal activity.
International sanctions allowed authorities to immobilize financial resources linked to the regime.
The government decided to immobilize the assets of companies suspected of fraud.

Related Terms

  • synonym: freeze - to prevent something, especially assets or capital, from being used or moved
  • antonym: liquidate - to convert assets into cash
  • antonym: release - to make available for use again, opposite of immobilise in this sense
