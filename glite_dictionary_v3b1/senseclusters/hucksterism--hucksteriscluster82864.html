hucksterism

noun
aggressive selling
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌkstərɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈhʌkstəɪzəm / (BrE)
the use of aggressive, pushy, or deceptive promotion or selling, especially to persuade people or attract attention

Examples

The politician's speech was criticized for its blatant hucksterism and empty promises.

Some people see modern advertising as little more than clever hucksterism.
The politician's campaign was criticized for its blatant hucksterism and lack of substance.
Many viewers were turned off by the hucksterism in the late-night infomercial.
Some media outlets rely on hucksterism to boost ratings rather than providing real news.
