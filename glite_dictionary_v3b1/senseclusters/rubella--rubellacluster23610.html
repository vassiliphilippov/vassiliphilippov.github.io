rubella

noun
contagious mild viral disease; German measles
6.3 0.39/m
Pronunciation: / ruÀàb…õl…ô / (AmE), / ruÀêÀàb…õl…ô / (BrE)
a contagious viral disease, also known as German measles, characterized by a mild rash and fever, which can cause serious harm to a developing fetus if contracted during early pregnancy

Examples

Rubella vaccination
Rubella vaccinations are often given to children to prevent outbreaks.
She was diagnosed with rubella after developing a mild rash and fever.
The doctor explained that rubella is particularly dangerous for pregnant women.

Synonyms

  • German measles
    3.0 0.05/m
    German measles is the common everyday name used in speech, while rubella is the medical term used by doctors.
  • three-day measles
    4.2 0.00/m
    three-day measles stresses the short, three- or four-day illness, while rubella is the clinical name used by doctors.
  • epidemic roseola
    9.0 0.00/m
    epidemic roseola is described as a milder measles-like illness, while rubella specifically names the viral disease also called German measles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chickenpox - a contagious disease causing skin rash and blisters, common in children
  • degree higher: measles - a highly contagious viral disease marked by fever and rash
