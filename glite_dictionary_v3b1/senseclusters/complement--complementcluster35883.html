complement

noun
word or phrase completing a grammatical construction
2.5 5.2
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑːm.plə.mənt/ /; / /ˈkɑːm.plɪ.mənt/ (AmE), /ˈkɒm.plɪ.mənt/ /; / /ˈkɒm.plə.mənt/ (BrE)
(Linguistics) A word or phrase that is necessary to complete the meaning of a grammatical construction.

Examples

Verb complement in grammar
A verb like 'be' often requires a complement to complete its meaning.
In the sentence 'She called him a genius,' the phrase 'a genius' functions as the complement.
The grammar teacher explained how to identify the subject and complement in a sentence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: adjunct - a word or phrase that adds information but is not necessary to complete the construction
  • synonym: object - a noun, noun phrase, or pronoun governed by a verb or preposition
