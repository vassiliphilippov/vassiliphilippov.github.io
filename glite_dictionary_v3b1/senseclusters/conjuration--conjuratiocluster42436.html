conjuration

noun
summoning a spirit or devil
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑndʒəˈreɪʃən /; / ˌkɑːndʒəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒndʒəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of summoning spirits or supernatural powers, especially by using magic words or rituals

Examples

Ancient conjuration ritual
Legends tell of wizards who practiced conjuration in secret chambers lit by candlelight.
She warned that attempting a conjuration could have dangerous and unforeseen consequences.
The ancient text describes a conjuration performed to summon a protective spirit.
Stories of medieval conjurations often involve summoning spirits or demons for knowledge or power.
The old book described a conjuration to protect one’s home from evil influences.
The wizard began his conjuration, chanting ancient words in the flickering candlelight.

Related Terms

  • antonym: banishment - the act of driving away a spirit or supernatural entity
  • synonym: enchantment - the use of magic or spells to cast a charm
  • synonym: evocation - the act of calling forth a spirit or supernatural being
  • antonym: exorcism - the expulsion of an evil spirit by ritual
  • synonym: incantation - a series of words said as a magic spell or charm
