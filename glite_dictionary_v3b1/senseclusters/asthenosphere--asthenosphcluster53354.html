asthenosphere

noun
semi-fluid layer beneath Earth's lithosphere
Pronunciation: / əˈsθɛnəˌsfɪr / (AmE), / æsˈθiːn.ə.sfɪə / (BrE)
A semi-fluid layer of the Earth's upper mantle below the lithosphere, which allows for the movement of tectonic plates.

Examples

The asthenosphere layer
Geologists study the asthenosphere to understand earthquakes and continental drift.
Magma from the asthenosphere can rise to form new crust at mid-ocean ridges.
The movement of tectonic plates is largely enabled by the properties of the asthenosphere.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: lithosphere - the rigid outer layer of the Earth above the asthenosphere
  • synonym: mantle - the region of the Earth's interior below the crust and above the core
