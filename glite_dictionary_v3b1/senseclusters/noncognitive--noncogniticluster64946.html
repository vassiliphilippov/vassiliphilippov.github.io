noncognitive

adjective
not involving reasoning
Pronunciation: / nɑnˈkɑɡnətɪv / (AmE), / nɒnˈkɒɡnətɪv / (BrE)
relating to skills, traits, or abilities that do not involve thinking, reasoning, or intellectual activity

Examples

Noncognitive skills
Teachers often emphasize noncognitive skills like perseverance and teamwork in the classroom.
Success in the workplace can depend on noncognitive traits such as motivation and self-control.
Many educational programs now focus on developing noncognitive abilities alongside academic knowledge.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cognitive - relating to mental processes of thinking and reasoning
