States' Rights Democratic Party

noun
segregationist U.S. political party (1948)
Pronunciation: / steɪts raɪts ˌdɛməˈkrætɪk ˈpɑːrti / (AmE), / steɪts raɪts ˌdɛməˈkrætɪk ˈpɑːti / (BrE)
a former political party in the United States, formed in 1948 by Southern Democrats to oppose the candidacy of Harry S. Truman, advocating states' rights and segregationist policies

Examples

The States' Rights Democratic Party formed
Historians debate the long-term impact of the States' Rights Democratic Party on American politics.
The States' Rights Democratic Party nominated Strom Thurmond for president in the 1948 election.
The platform of the States' Rights Democratic Party was centered around opposition to federal civil rights legislation.
