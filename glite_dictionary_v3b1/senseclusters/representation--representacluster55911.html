representation

noun
visual or tangible depiction
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛprɪˌzɛnˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛprɪzɛnˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a visual, symbolic, or tangible depiction or presentation of something, or something that stands for or substitutes for something else

Examples

Visual representation
The film's historical representation sparked much debate among critics.
The gallery features a representation of the artist's childhood home.
This sculpture is a striking representation of the human form.
Her novel provides a powerful representation of grief and healing.
Language allows for the representation of abstract concepts in the human mind.
The artist's painting was a vivid representation of his childhood memories.
A map is a spatial representation of the physical world.
In the experiment, the model served as a representation of the larger system.
This painting is a striking representation of 19th-century life.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abstraction - something not intended as a realistic representation
  • synonym: depiction - the act of showing something in a picture, painting, or other image
  • synonym: idea - a mental impression or thought
  • synonym: image - a picture or visual representation in the mind
  • synonym: substitute - a person or thing acting or serving in place of another
