seed fern

noun
extinct, seed-producing fern-like plant
Pronunciation: / sid fɝn / (AmE), / siːd fɜːn / (BrE)
an extinct group of seed-producing, fern-like plants belonging to the order Cycadofilicales (Pteridospermae), prominent in the Paleozoic era

Examples

Fossil seed ferns
Fossils of seed ferns provide insights into the evolution of seed plants.
Scientists believe that seed ferns dominated many prehistoric forests.
The extinction of seed ferns marked a major shift in plant diversity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cycad - a seed plant with a stout trunk and large compound leaves, similar to ancient seed ferns
  • synonym: fern - a non-flowering plant with feathery leaves, reproducing by spores
