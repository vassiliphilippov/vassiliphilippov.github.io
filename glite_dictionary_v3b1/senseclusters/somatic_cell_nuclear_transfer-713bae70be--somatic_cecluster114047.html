somatic cell nuclear transfer

noun
technique of transferring a somatic cell nucleus to an egg
9.9
Pronunciation: / soʊˈmætɪk sɛl ˈnuːkliɚ ˈtrænsfɚ / (AmE), / səˈmatɪk sɛl ˈnjuːklɪə ˈtrɑːnsfə / (BrE)
a laboratory technique in which the nucleus of a somatic cell is transferred into an egg cell from which the nucleus has been removed, commonly used in cloning and genetic research

Examples

Somatic cell nuclear transfer technique
Somatic cell nuclear transfer has played a crucial role in advances in cloning technology.
Researchers used somatic cell nuclear transfer to create genetically identical sheep in the laboratory.
The ethical implications of somatic cell nuclear transfer are widely debated in scientific communities.
