peremptory

adjective
insisting on immediate obedience; not allowing refusal
3.2 7.4
Pronunciation: /pər.ˈempt.ər.iː/ /; / /pə.ˈremp.tər.ɪ/ (AmE), /pə.ˈremp.tə.rɪ/ /; / /pə.ˈremp.tər.ɪ/ (BrE)
insisting on immediate obedience or action, often in a bossy or domineering way, leaving no opportunity for refusal

Examples

A peremptory order
His peremptory tone silenced the entire room.
She closed the discussion with a peremptory gesture, leaving no room for further debate.
The manager gave a peremptory order, expecting everyone to follow without question.
A peremptory decision was made to end the negotiations without delay.
His peremptory tone left no room for argument or discussion.
The judge issued a peremptory order requiring them to vacate the premises immediately.
Her peremptory tone made it clear she expected everyone to obey immediately.
His peremptory gesture silenced the room at once.
The manager issued a peremptory order, leaving no room for discussion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authoritative - commanding and self-confident; likely to be respected and obeyed
  • synonym: bossy - fond of giving people orders; domineering
  • synonym: decisive - having the power to decide; conclusive
  • synonym: imperious - arrogantly domineering or overbearing
  • antonym: submissive - ready to conform to the authority or will of others
