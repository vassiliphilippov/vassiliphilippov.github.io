shaft

noun
long, straight part of an arrow
5.3 3.4
Pronunciation: /ʃæft/ (AmE), /ʃɑːft/ /; / /ʃæft/ (BrE)
the long, straight, central part of something, such as an arrow, bone, feather, rod, tool handle, weapon, machine part, or column

Examples

Arrow shaft
He carefully sanded the shaft to ensure a smooth finish for the arrow.
If the shaft of your arrow is bent, it will not fly accurately.

Osteomyelitis can affect both the ends and the shaft of long bones.
The shaft of the femur is often fractured in car accidents.
The surgeon inserted a rod along the shaft to stabilize the broken leg.
As she examined the bird's wing, she noticed a crack in the shaft of one feather.
The shaft of the feather was strong and helped it maintain its shape.
The artist painted each detail, including the delicate shaft running through the center of the plume.
