heterochromatin

noun
condensed inactive DNA
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛt̬əroʊˈkroʊmətɪn / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəʊˈkrəʊmətɪn / (BrE)
a tightly packed form of DNA in a cell nucleus that is usually inactive in gene expression and helps organize and protect genetic material

Examples

Tightly packed heterochromatin
Scientists study heterochromatin to understand how certain genes are turned off in cells.
The inactive X chromosome in female mammals is mostly made up of heterochromatin.
Changes in heterochromatin structure can affect how genes are expressed during development.
Centromeres are rich in heterochromatin, which remains condensed throughout the cell cycle.
Scientists study heterochromatin to understand how certain genes are kept inactive in cells.
The presence of heterochromatin at telomeres helps protect chromosome ends from damage.
Scientists study heterochromatin to understand how cells control gene expression.
The presence of heterochromatin helps protect important genetic information from damage.
Changes in heterochromatin structure can affect how genes are turned on or off.
