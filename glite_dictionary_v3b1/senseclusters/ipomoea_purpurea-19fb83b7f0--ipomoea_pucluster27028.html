Ipomoea purpurea

noun
a flowering climbing plant; common morning glory
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪpoʊˈmiːə pɜːrˈpjʊəriə / (AmE), / ˌɪpəˈməʊ.iə pɜːˈpjʊəriə / (BrE)
A tropical and subtropical climbing annual plant with funnel-shaped flowers that are blue, purple, pink, or white, commonly known as the common morning glory.

Examples

Ipomoea purpurea flowers
Gardeners often plant Ipomoea purpurea for its fast-growing vines and colorful blossoms.
Seeds of the Ipomoea purpurea should be soaked before planting to improve germination.
The vibrant flowers of the Ipomoea purpurea open in the early morning sunlight.
