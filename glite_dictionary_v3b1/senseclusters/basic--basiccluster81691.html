basic

noun
essential item or commodity (usually plural)
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪsɪk / (AmE), / ˈbeɪsɪk / (BrE)
A necessary item or commodity considered essential for daily life, especially food, clothing, or shelter; usually used in the plural.

Examples

Everyday basics
Access to the basics is considered a fundamental right in many countries.
During difficult times, many families can barely afford the basics like food and housing.
Supermarkets are stockpiling basics such as bread, milk, and eggs ahead of the holidays.

Related Terms

  • antonym: luxury - something nonessential and expensive, opposite of a basic
  • synonym: necessity - something necessary or indispensable
