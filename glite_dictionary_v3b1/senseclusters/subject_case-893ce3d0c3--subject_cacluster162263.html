subject case

noun
noun/pronoun case for grammatical subject
3.5 5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbdʒɪkt keɪs / (AmE), / ˈsʌbdʒɪkt keɪs / (BrE)
the grammatical case used for a noun or pronoun when it functions as the subject of a verb

Examples

The subject case form
In English, the pronoun 'he' is in the subject case when used as in 'He runs.'
Many languages mark the subject case differently from the object case.
This grammar lesson explains when to use the subject case for pronouns.
