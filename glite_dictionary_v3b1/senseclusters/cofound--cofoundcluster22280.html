cofound

verb
to start together
Pronunciation: / koʊˈfaʊnd / (AmE), / kəʊˈfaʊnd / (BrE)
to start an organization, company, or group together with one or more other people

Examples

To cofound a company
She cofounded a tech startup with her college friend after graduation.
Several artists cofounded the gallery to promote local talent in the city.
He is proud to have cofounded a charity that helps children in need.
They decided to cofound a nonprofit organization to support local artists.
After years of planning, she cofounded a successful software company with her friend.
He is proud to be a cofounder of the environmental initiative.
She helped cofound a successful tech startup with her college friends.
Two scientists decided to cofound a research institute focused on renewable energy.
He plans to cofound a charity to support local education initiatives.
