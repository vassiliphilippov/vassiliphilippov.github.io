circumvallate

verb
surround with a fortification
Pronunciation: / sɜrkəmˈvæleɪt / (AmE), / səːkəmˈvæleɪt / (BrE)
to surround (a place) with or as if with a rampart or fortification, especially in a military context

Examples

To circumvallate a city
During the siege, the army moved to circumvallate the enemy city, cutting off all escape routes.
Roman engineers were ordered to circumvallate the fortress before launching their assault.
The general planned to circumvallate the rebel stronghold to pressure their surrender.
