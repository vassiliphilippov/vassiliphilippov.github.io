underuse

verb
to use too little
3.6
Pronunciation: /ʌn.dər.ˈjuːz/ (AmE), /ʌn.də.ˈjuːz/ (BrE)
to use something less than it could or should be used; to make insufficient use of something

Examples

To underuse skills
Many talented employees feel frustrated when companies underuse their skills and experience.
If you underuse your gym membership, you might consider canceling it to save money.
Some medicines are underused because doctors are not aware of their full benefits.

Related Terms

  • synonym: neglect - to fail to care for or use something properly
  • antonym: overuse - to use something too much or too often
  • antonym: utilize - to make practical and effective use of something
