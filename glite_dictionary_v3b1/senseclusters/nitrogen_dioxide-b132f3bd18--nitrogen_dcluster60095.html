nitrogen dioxide

noun
a poisonous brown gas (NO₂)
3.6 4.4
Pronunciation: /ˈnaɪ.trə.dʒən daɪ.ˈɑːk.saɪd/ (AmE), /ˈnaɪ.trə.dʒən daɪ.ˈɒk.saɪd/ (BrE)
a poisonous brown gas (NO₂) formed by the oxidation of nitric oxide, commonly found as an air pollutant

Examples

Brown nitrogen dioxide gas
High concentrations of nitrogen dioxide in the air can cause respiratory problems, especially in vulnerable populations.
Strict environmental regulations aim to reduce emissions of nitrogen dioxide from vehicles and industrial sources.
The reaction between oxygen and nitric oxide produces nitrogen dioxide as a byproduct.
