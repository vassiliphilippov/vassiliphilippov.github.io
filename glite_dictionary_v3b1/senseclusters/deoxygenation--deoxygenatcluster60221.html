deoxygenation

noun
removal of oxygen
Pronunciation: / diˌɑksɪdʒəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌɒksɪdʒəˈneɪʃən /; / diˌɒksɪdʒɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of removing oxygen from a substance or environment

Examples

Water deoxygenation
The deoxygenation of water in lakes can harm fish and other aquatic life.
Industrial processes often require deoxygenation to prevent unwanted chemical reactions.
Scientists studied the deoxygenation of organic compounds during the experiment.
The deoxygenation of molten steel is essential to prevent the formation of bubbles and weak spots.
Modern refineries use advanced techniques for deoxygenation to ensure high-quality metal products.
Improper deoxygenation can lead to defects that compromise the strength of the finished metal.
The deoxygenation of rivers can lead to fish deaths during hot weather.
Industrial processes sometimes cause deoxygenation in nearby lakes, affecting water quality.
Scientists monitor deoxygenation levels to protect marine ecosystems from damage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: degassing - the removal of gases from a liquid or solid
  • antonym: oxidation - a chemical reaction that involves the addition of oxygen or loss of electrons
  • antonym: oxygenation - the process of adding oxygen to a substance
  • synonym: reduction - a chemical reaction that involves the gain of electrons or removal of oxygen
