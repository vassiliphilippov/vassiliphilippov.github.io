sampling

noun
measuring signals at intervals for digital conversion
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈsæmplɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsɑːmplɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of measuring the amplitude of a continuously varying signal at regular intervals, typically to convert it into digital form

Examples

Digital sampling
In signal processing, sampling allows continuous waveforms to be represented digitally.
Modern audio systems use sampling to convert analog music into digital files.
The quality of the digital recording depends largely on the sampling rate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: digitization - the process of converting information into a digital format
