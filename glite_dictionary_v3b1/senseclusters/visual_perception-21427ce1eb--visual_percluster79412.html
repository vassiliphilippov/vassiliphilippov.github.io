visual perception

noun
perception by means of the eyes
1.4
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪʒuəl pərˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒu.əl pəˈsɛp.ʃən / (BrE)
the ability to interpret information and surroundings from visible light reaching the eyes; perception by means of sight

Examples

Child visual perception
Children develop visual perception as they learn to recognize shapes and colors.
Damage to the occipital lobe can impair visual perception and lead to difficulties in recognizing objects.
Good lighting is essential for accurate visual perception in the workplace.
