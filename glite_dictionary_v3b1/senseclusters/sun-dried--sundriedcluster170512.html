sun-dried

adjective
dried by exposure to the sun
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌnˌdraɪd / (AmE), / ˈsʌnˌdraɪd / (BrE)
dried by direct exposure to sunlight, typically to preserve.

Examples

Sun-dried apricots
She packed a bag of sun-dried apricots as a healthy snack for the hike.
The chef recommended using sun-dried tomatoes for a richer flavor in the pasta.
The village still follows the tradition of making sun-dried bricks for their homes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: air-dried - dried by exposure to air rather than heat or sunlight
  • degree higher: sunny - bright with sunlight
