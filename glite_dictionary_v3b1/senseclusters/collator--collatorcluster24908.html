collator

noun
locale-aware string comparator
7.5
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈleɪ.tər/ (AmE), /kə.ˈleɪ.tə/ (BrE)
(in computing) a software component or library that performs locale-aware comparison of text strings to determine correct sort order.

Examples

Text collator software
The library's collator compares strings correctly for sorting names in Spanish.
Developers updated the collator to follow new Unicode collation rules across locales.
When indexing documents, the search engine used a collator to ensure consistent order.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collation - the process or rules for ordering text strings according to language and locale conventions
  • synonym: sort - to arrange items or data in a specific order
