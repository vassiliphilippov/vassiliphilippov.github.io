organometallic

noun
compound with metal-carbon bond
Pronunciation: / ˌɔːrɡənoʊməˈtælɪk / (AmE), / ˌɔːɡənəʊməˈtælɪk / (BrE)
a compound that contains at least one bond between a carbon atom of an organic group and a metal atom

Examples

An organometallic catalyst
The chemist synthesized a new organometallic to test its catalytic properties.
Many organometallic compounds are sensitive to air and must be handled carefully.
Researchers study organometallic substances for their potential use in medicine and industry.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inorganic compound - a compound that does not contain carbon-metal bonds
  • synonym: organometallic compound - a compound with a direct bond between a metal and a carbon atom
