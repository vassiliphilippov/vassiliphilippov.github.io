Ares

proper noun
Greek god of war in mythology
8.2
Pronunciation: /ˈer.iːz/ /; / /ɑːrz/ (AmE), /ˈeə.riːz/ (BrE)
The Greek god of war, son of Zeus and Hera, identified with the Roman god Mars in mythology.

Examples

Ares Greek god
In classical mythology, Ares was known for his fierce and aggressive nature in battle.
The ancient Greeks often depicted Ares in art as a helmeted warrior carrying a spear.
Unlike Athena, Ares represented the brutal and chaotic aspects of war.
