spike

noun
a brief surge in voltage or current
Pronunciation: / spaɪk / (AmE), / spaɪk / (BrE)
a sudden sharp increase in something, such as amount, intensity, or voltage

Examples

Spike in prices
A sudden spike in voltage damaged the computer's power supply.
Engineers noticed a sharp spike in the current when the device was switched on.
The equipment is designed to protect against power spikes caused by lightning.
After the initial spike, COVID-19 cases dropped significantly in the area.
The chart showed a sudden spike in internet traffic during the event.
We saw a spike in temperature before it fell back to normal levels.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dip - a brief downward movement or drop in a value
  • synonym: peak - the highest point of something, especially in a graph or chart
  • synonym: surge - a sudden powerful forward or upward movement, especially by a crowd or by a natural force such as the tide
