preplacement

noun
temporary positioning
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈpleɪsmənt / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈpleɪsmənt / (BrE)
the act of temporarily positioning an object or component in preparation for its final placement or assembly

Examples

Preplacement of parts
The engineer checked the preplacement of all parts before starting the final assembly.
Proper preplacement of cables ensures a smoother installation process later on.
During training, students practiced the preplacement of surgical instruments on the tray.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arrangement - the action of organizing or positioning items
  • degree higher: placement - the act of putting something in its final position
