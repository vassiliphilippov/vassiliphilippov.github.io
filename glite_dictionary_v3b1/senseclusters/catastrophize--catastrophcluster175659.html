catastrophize

verb
to exaggerate danger
8.4
Pronunciation: / kəˈtæstrəˌfaɪz / (AmE), / kəˈtæstrəfaɪz / (BrE)
to think about a situation as much worse than it really is, or to imagine the worst possible outcome

Examples

To catastrophize a problem
Try not to catastrophize every small mistake you make at work.
When facing challenges, some people tend to catastrophize instead of staying calm.
The news report seemed to catastrophize the situation, making it sound much worse than it was.
When facing challenges, it is easy to catastrophize and believe everything will go wrong.
She tends to catastrophize minor problems, turning small issues into major crises in her mind.
Instead of catastrophizing about the exam, try to focus on what you can control.

Related Terms

  • antonym: downplay - to make something seem less important or serious than it is
  • synonym: exaggerate - to make something seem larger, better, or worse than it really is
  • antonym: minimize - to make something seem less important or serious than it is
