down tools

verb
stop working, often in protest
8.1 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / da än tuÀêlz / (AmE), / da än tuÀêlz / (BrE)
(of workers) to stop working, especially as a form of protest or industrial action.

Examples

To down tools in protest
After the announcement of pay cuts, the workers decided to down tools until management addressed their concerns.
At noon, the foreman told everyone to down tools and take their lunch break.
During negotiations, union leaders threatened to down tools if their demands were not met.

Synonyms

  • go on strike
    3.2 1/m
    go on strike is the normal phrase workers use to stop work as protest, while down tools sounds more informal and visual.
  • strike
    3.2 8/m
    strike is the standard verb or noun for stopping work to protest, while down tools is a more informal action phrase.
  • strike work
    5.7 0.00/m
    strike work is a formal or literal phrase meaning to stop work in protest, while down tools is idiomatic and more visual.
  • walk out
    4.1 0.6/m
    walk out is to leave the workplace during a protest, while down tools means simply to stop working, not necessarily to leave.
