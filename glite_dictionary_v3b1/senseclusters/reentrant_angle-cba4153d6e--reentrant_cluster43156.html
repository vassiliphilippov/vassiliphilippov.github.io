reentrant angle

noun
an interior angle over 180° in a polygon
9.9
Pronunciation: / riˈɛntrənt ˈæŋɡəl / (AmE), / riˈɛntrənt ˈaŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
an interior angle of a polygon that is greater than 180 degrees, causing the shape to turn inward at that point

Examples

A reentrant angle in polygon
A star-shaped figure is characterized by its multiple reentrant angles at the narrow points.
The concave polygon had a single reentrant angle that pointed inward.
When drawing the floor plan, the architect identified a reentrant angle in the alcove corner.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reflex angle - an angle greater than 180 degrees but less than 360 degrees
