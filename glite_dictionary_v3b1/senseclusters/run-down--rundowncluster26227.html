run-down

adjective
in poor or neglected condition
4.5 1.04/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆrÊŒnËŒdaÊŠn / (AmE), / ËˆrÊŒnËŒdaÊŠn / (BrE)
(of a building, area, or object) in poor condition due to neglect or long use; dilapidated or worn out.

Examples

A run-down building
After years of hard use, the barn looked completely run-down.
Many residents complained about the run-down playground and requested repairs.
The run-down house at the end of the street was finally put up for sale.

Synonyms

  • dilapidated
    6.2 1.62/m
    dilapidated describes a building in serious repair needs, while run-down means generally in poor condition from neglect or age.
  • decrepit
    6.6 0.99/m
    decrepit describes something weak and ruined by long age, while run-down means in poor condition from neglect or use.
  • tumbledown
    5.6 0.00/m
    tumbledown paints a picture of a building about to fall, while run-down simply means in poor, neglected condition.
  • derelict
    7.5 0.78/m
    derelict is usually empty and abandoned by people, while run-down describes a place in poor condition whether or not empty.
  • broken-down
    4.4 1.08/m
    broken-down mainly refers to vehicles or machines that stop working, while run-down often refers to buildings or areas in poor repair.
  • worn-out
    3.1 1.46/m
    worn-out feels like an object too used to work well any more, while run-down describes buildings or areas left to decay.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decrepit - worn out or ruined because of age or neglect
  • synonym: dilapidated - in a state of disrepair or ruin due to age or neglect
  • synonym: neglected - not receiving proper care; poorly maintained
