run-down

adjective
in poor or neglected condition
5.8 3.7
Pronunciation: /ˈrʌn.daʊn/ (AmE), /ˈrʌn.daʊn/ (BrE)
(of a building, area, or object) in poor condition due to neglect or long use; dilapidated or worn out.

Examples

A run-down building
After years of hard use, the barn looked completely run-down.
Many residents complained about the run-down playground and requested repairs.
The run-down house at the end of the street was finally put up for sale.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dilapidated - in a state of disrepair or ruin due to age or neglect
  • synonym: neglected - not receiving proper care; poorly maintained
