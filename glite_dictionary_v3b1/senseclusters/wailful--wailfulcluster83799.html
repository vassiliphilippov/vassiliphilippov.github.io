wailful

adjective
expressing grief or sorrow, mournful
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈweɪlfəl / (AmE), / ˈweɪlfʊl / (BrE)
full of or expressing grief or sorrow, especially in a way that is loud or mournful.

Examples

Wailful lament
Her wailful lament echoed through the empty church.
The wailful cries of the wind made the night even more unsettling.
The wailful tones of the bagpipes moved everyone to tears at the memorial service.

Related Terms

  • antonym: joyful - feeling, expressing, or causing great pleasure and happiness
  • synonym: mournful - feeling, expressing, or inducing sadness, regret, or grief
