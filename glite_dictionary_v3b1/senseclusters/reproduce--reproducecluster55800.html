reproduce

verb
make a copy or duplicate
4.0 6.08/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀêpr…ôÀàduÀês / (AmE), / ÀåriÀêpr…ôÀàdjuÀês / (BrE)
to make a copy or duplicate of something; to produce something that closely resembles the original

Examples

To reproduce a document
It is illegal to reproduce copyrighted music without permission.
The artist managed to reproduce the painting with remarkable accuracy.
This printer can reproduce images in high resolution.

Synonyms

  • copy
    1.0 17/m
    copy is a common verb meaning to make an exact or close reproduction, while reproduce often sounds more formal and technical.
  • duplicate
    3.5 6.29/m
    duplicate means to make an exact match or repeat something exactly, while reproduce can mean making a close but not perfect copy.
  • replicate
    4.9 6.03/m
    replicate is used for exact copying in science or tech settings, while reproduce is more general and used in many contexts.
  • recreate
    4.5 5.86/m
    recreate means to make something again or to imitate roughly, while reproduce focuses on making a close or faithful copy.
