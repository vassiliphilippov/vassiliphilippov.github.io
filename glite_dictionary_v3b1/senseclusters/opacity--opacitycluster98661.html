opacity

noun
impenetrability to radiation or light
Pronunciation: / oʊˈpæs.ə.ti /; / oʊˈpæsəti / (AmE), / əʊˈpæs.ɪ.ti /; / əʊˈpæsɪti / (BrE)
the quality or degree of not allowing light (or other electromagnetic radiation) to pass through; how opaque something is

Examples

Glass opacity
In astrophysics, opacity plays an important role in understanding the behavior of stars.
Scientists measured the opacity of the atmosphere to determine how much sunlight reaches the surface.
The opacity of the material prevents X-rays from passing through.
Medical imaging uses dye to increase the opacity of blood vessels on an X-ray.
The opacity of the frosted glass prevented us from seeing into the room.
The artist adjusted the opacity of each layer in the digital painting.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: translucency - the property of allowing some light to pass through but diffusing it
  • antonym: transparency - the property of allowing light or radiation to pass through
