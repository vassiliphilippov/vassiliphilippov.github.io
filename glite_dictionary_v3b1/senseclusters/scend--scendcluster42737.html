scend

noun
upward wave motion
Pronunciation: / sɛnd / (AmE), / sɛnd / (BrE)
the upward movement or rise of water, especially a wave, in nautical contexts

Examples

The scend of waves
The ship rose and fell with the scend of the waves during the storm.
Fishermen waited for the right scend to launch their boats safely from the shore.
The sudden scend lifted the small vessel higher than expected.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ebb - the movement of the tide out to sea; a receding motion
  • synonym: surge - a sudden powerful forward or upward movement, especially of water
