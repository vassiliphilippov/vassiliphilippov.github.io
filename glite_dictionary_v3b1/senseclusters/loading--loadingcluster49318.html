loading

noun
aeronautics: ratio of aircraft weight to lift factor
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈləʊdɪŋ / (BrE)
The ratio of the gross weight of an airplane to a factor that determines its lift, used in aeronautics to assess aircraft performance.

Examples

Aircraft loading ratio
Engineers examined the loading to ensure the new design could support higher weights.
Improper loading can negatively affect the lift and performance of an airplane.
The aircraft's loading must be carefully calculated before every flight.
