whizbang shell

noun
small high-velocity artillery shell
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪzˌbæŋ ʃɛl / (AmE), / ˈwɪzˌbæŋ ʃɛl / (BrE)
A small, high-velocity artillery shell that makes a whizzing sound followed by a bang when it explodes.

Examples

A whizbang shell
During World War I, the terrifying noise of a whizbang shell became all too familiar to those at the front.
Soldiers quickly learned to recognize the telltale sound of a whizbang shell approaching.
The trenches shook as another whizbang shell exploded overhead.
