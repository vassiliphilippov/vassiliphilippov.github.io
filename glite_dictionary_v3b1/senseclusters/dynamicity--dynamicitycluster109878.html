dynamicity

noun
ability to change
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˌdaɪnəˈmɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌdaɪnəˈmɪsəti / (BrE)
the quality of being dynamic, able to change, or characterized by constant activity or progress

Examples

Dynamicity of the system
The dynamicity of the software allows developers to update features without restarting the application.
Modern programming languages often emphasize dynamicity to support rapid development and customization.
The system's high dynamicity made it suitable for environments where requirements change frequently.
The dynamicity of the market makes long-term predictions difficult.
Her leadership brought a new level of dynamicity to the organization.
Modern technology adds dynamicity to the way we communicate and work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dynamism - the quality of being energetic and full of new ideas
  • synonym: flexibility - the ability to adapt or change easily
  • antonym: stability - the quality of being steady and unchanging
  • antonym: staticness - the quality of being unchanging or fixed
