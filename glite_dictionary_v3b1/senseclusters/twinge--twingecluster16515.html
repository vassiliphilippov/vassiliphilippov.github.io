twinge

verb
feel a sudden sharp pain
6.5
Pronunciation: / twɪndʒ / (AmE), / twɪndʒ / (BrE)
to feel a sudden, sharp pain or emotion

Examples

To twinge with pain
He suddenly twinged in his lower back as he bent to pick up the box.
Occasionally, I twinge in my knee after a long run.
She twinged with anxiety every time she heard the phone ring.
She twinged with regret after missing the important meeting.
His conscience twinged when he remembered the promise he had broken.
I twinged with jealousy when I saw my friend win the award.
She twinged with regret after making the wrong decision.
His ankle twinged suddenly as he stepped off the curb.
Whenever he thought of the past, he twinged with sadness.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: ache - to feel a continuous or prolonged pain
  • synonym: sting - to feel a sharp, sudden pain
