intermediate

noun
temporary substance in a chemical reaction
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.tɚˈmiː.di.ət / (AmE), / ˌɪn.təˈmiː.di.ət / (BrE)
a substance formed during a chemical reaction that exists temporarily before converting into the final product

Examples

A chemical intermediate
During the synthesis of aspirin, an intermediate is produced before the final product is formed.
Scientists identified a new intermediate that helps explain the reaction's unusual speed.
The presence of a stable intermediate can sometimes be detected by spectroscopic methods.
