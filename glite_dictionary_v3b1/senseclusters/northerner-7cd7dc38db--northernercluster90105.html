Northerner

noun
person from the northern region
3.9 3.0
Pronunciation: /ˈnɔːr.ðər.nər/ (AmE), /ˈnɔː.ðə.nə/ (BrE)
a person who comes from or lives in the northern part of a country, especially the north of England or the northern United States

Examples

A Northerner from England
As a Northerner, he was not used to the hot, humid climate of the South.
Many Northerners have strong regional accents that are easy to recognize.

As a proud Northerner, he often spoke about his family's history in New York.
During the Civil War, a Northerner might have held very different views from a Southerner.
Many Northerners moved west after the end of the war.
