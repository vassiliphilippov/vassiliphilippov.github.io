participial

noun
non-finite verb form used as adjective or tense
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑrtɪˈsɪpiəl / (AmE), / ˌpɑːtɪˈsɪpɪəl / (BrE)
A non-finite verb form, such as the present or past participle, used mainly as an adjective or in forming compound verb tenses.

Examples

A participial phrase
In the sentence, 'Baked fresh daily,' the word 'baked' is a participial.
Some English learners struggle to identify the correct participial in complex sentences.
The phrase 'running late' contains a participial form of the verb 'run'.
