lushly

adverb
richly or fully
Pronunciation: / ˈlʌʃli / (AmE), / ˈlʌʃli / (BrE)
in a rich, full, and pleasing way, especially when describing sound, music, or texture

Examples

Lushly played music
The orchestra played lushly, creating a warm and enveloping atmosphere in the concert hall.
The garden was lushly planted with a variety of colorful flowers and thick green shrubs.
Her voice flowed lushly through the melody, captivating everyone in the audience.

Related Terms

  • antonym: plainly - in a simple or unadorned way
  • synonym: richly - in a way that is full, deep, or impressive
