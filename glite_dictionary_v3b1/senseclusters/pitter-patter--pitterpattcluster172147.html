pitter-patter

verb
make gentle, rapid sounds (rain or footsteps)
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪt̬ɚ ˌpæt̬ɚ /; / ˈpɪtərˌpætər / (AmE), / ˈpɪtə ˌpætə /; / ˈpɪtəˌpætə / (BrE)
to make a series of light, rapid, repeated sounds, like raindrops or small footsteps

Examples

Raindrops pitter-patter on glass
Raindrops began to pitter-patter against the window as the storm approached.
She lay in bed listening to the rain pitter-patter quietly on the roof.
Suddenly, I heard water pitter-patter down the drainpipe outside my door.
I could hear her tiny feet pitter-pattering across the wooden floor upstairs.
Raindrops began to pitter-patter softly against the windowpane as evening fell.
The squirrel’s paws pitter-pattered through the leaves as it searched for acorns.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drizzle - to rain lightly in very small drops
  • synonym: pat - make a light repeated tapping sound
