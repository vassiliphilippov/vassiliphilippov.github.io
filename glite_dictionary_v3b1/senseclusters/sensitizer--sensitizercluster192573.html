sensitizer

noun
substance that enables a catalytic reaction
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛn.səˌtaɪ.zɚ /; / ˈsɛnsɪˌtaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.sɪˌtaɪ.zə /; / ˈsɛnsɪˌtaɪzə / (BrE)
In chemistry, a substance that, though not a catalyst itself, enables or accelerates the beginning of a catalytic reaction.

Examples

A chemical sensitizer
Researchers are studying how this sensitizer influences the speed of chemical reactions.
The sensitizer is essential in many photochemical processes.
The addition of a sensitizer allowed the reaction to proceed at a lower temperature.
Researchers added a sensitiser to lower the activation energy of the process.
Some industrial processes require a sensitiser to initiate specific chemical transformations.
The presence of a sensitiser in the mixture made the reaction proceed more rapidly.
