indrawing

noun
inward body movement
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌdrɔɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌdrɔːɪŋ / (BrE)
the inward movement of a part of the body, especially the chest, often observed during breathing

Examples

Chest indrawing
The nurse observed indrawing of the child's chest during each breath.
Severe indrawing can be a sign of respiratory distress in infants.
Doctors look for indrawing when assessing patients with breathing difficulties.

Related Terms

  • antonym: expansion - the act of increasing in size or volume, especially outward movement
  • synonym: retraction - the act of drawing back or pulling in, especially of a body part
