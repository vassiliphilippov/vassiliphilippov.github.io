reaffirm

verb
state again with emphasis; confirm again
4.4 5.97/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀê.…ôÀàf…úrm / (AmE), / ÀåriÀê.…ôÀàf…úÀêm / (BrE)
To state something again with emphasis, especially to show that it remains true or important.

Examples

To reaffirm a promise
At each meeting, the leader would reaffirm the organization's core values.
She called him to reaffirm her support after the disagreement.
The government sought to reaffirm its commitment to reducing carbon emissions.
