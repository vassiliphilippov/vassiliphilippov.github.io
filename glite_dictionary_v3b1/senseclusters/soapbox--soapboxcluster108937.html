soapbox

noun
raised platform for public speaking
6.6 0.78/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆsoÊŠpËŒbÉ‘ks / (AmE), / ËˆsÉ™ÊŠpËŒbÉ’ks / (BrE)
a raised platform, often improvised, on which someone stands to address an audience and attract attention, especially to express opinions publicly

Examples

Stand on a soapbox
She never misses an opportunity to climb onto her soapbox and advocate for animal rights.
Street speakers would use a soapbox to make sure everyone could see and hear them.
The politician stood on a soapbox in the park to address the crowd.

Synonyms

  • stump
    7.5 1.72/m
    a stump is a raised platform used for political campaigning to address voters, while a soapbox is often improvised for public opinion.
  • rostrum
    7.1 0.20/m
    a rostrum is a formal raised platform that shows a speaker to an audience, while a soapbox is more informal and improvised.
  • podium
    3.8 6.72/m
    a podium is a small raised stand used for speeches or awards, while a soapbox is a rough, public platform for outspoken opinions.
  • dais
    7.6 1.36/m
    a dais is a formal front platform for honored speakers to sit or stand on, while a soapbox is informal and used to attract attention.
