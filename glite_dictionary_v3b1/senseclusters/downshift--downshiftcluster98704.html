downshift

verb
to change to lower gear
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊnˌʃɪft /; / ˈdaʊnʃɪft / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnʃɪft / (BrE)
to change a vehicle's transmission to a lower gear, usually to reduce speed or increase engine power

Examples

To downshift the car
She was downshifting as she approached the intersection to slow the car smoothly.
When driving downhill, it is safer to downshift to maintain better control of the vehicle.
He quickly downshifted to gain more power while climbing the steep hill.
She downshifted before turning onto the narrow road.
When traffic slowed suddenly, he quickly downshifted to maintain control of the car.
After years of working long hours, he decided to downshift and focus on a simpler lifestyle.
The driver had to downshift before climbing the steep hill.
You should downshift when approaching a sharp turn to maintain control of the car.
He quickly downshifted to avoid stalling the engine in heavy traffic.
