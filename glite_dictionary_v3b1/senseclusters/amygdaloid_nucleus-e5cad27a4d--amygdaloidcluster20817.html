amygdaloid nucleus

noun
almond-shaped brain structure in the limbic system
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈmɪɡdəˌlɔɪd ˈnuː.kli.əs / (AmE), / əˈmɪɡ.də.lɔɪd ˈnjuː.kli.əs / (BrE)
an almond-shaped group of neurons located in the anterior part of the temporal lobe, part of the limbic system and important in motivation and emotional behavior

Examples

The amygdaloid nucleus group
Damage to the amygdaloid nucleus can significantly affect emotional processing in patients.
Researchers studied the amygdaloid nucleus to understand its role in fear and anxiety.
The amygdaloid nucleus interacts closely with the hippocampus to influence memory and mood.
