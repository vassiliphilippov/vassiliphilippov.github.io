rechartering

noun
granting new charter
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈtʃɑːrtərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈtʃɑːtərɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of granting a new charter to an organization or institution, allowing it to continue or renew its operations

Examples

Official rechartering ceremony

Without rechartering, the organization would have lost its legal status to operate.
The process of rechartering requires approval from the national headquarters every five years.
