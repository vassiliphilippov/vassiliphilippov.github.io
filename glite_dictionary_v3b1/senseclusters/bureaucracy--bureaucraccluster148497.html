bureaucracy

noun
organization hindered by red tape
6.3 13/m
Pronunciation: / bj äÀàr…ëÀêkr…ôsi / (AmE), / bj ä…ôÀàr…íkr…ôsi / (BrE)
a system or organization run by officials (not elected), often with too many rules and complicated procedures that make things slow or difficult

Examples

The bureaucracy system
Many citizens complain that the city bureaucracy makes even simple requests take months to process.
She was frustrated by the bureaucracy that demanded endless paperwork for every minor change.
The new policy is meant to cut through the bureaucracy and make services more accessible.
He worked for the military bureaucracy for over twenty years.
Many citizens are frustrated by the bureaucracy that slows down government processes.
The new health plan was delayed due to excessive bureaucracy within the department.
Many citizens feel frustrated by the slow decision-making process within the government bureaucracy.
Some critics argue that too much bureaucracy stifles innovation and efficiency.
The country is governed by a complex bureaucracy rather than elected representatives.

Synonyms

  • bureaucratism
    8.9 0.01/m
    bureaucratism is the practice of government run by unelected officials who follow strict rules, while bureaucracy is the system of rules that slows things down.

  • synonym: civil service - the permanent professional branches of a government's administration
  • antonym: democracy - a system of government by the whole population, typically through elected representatives
  • antonym: efficiency - the quality of being efficient or productive
  • synonym: red tape - excessive bureaucracy or adherence to rules and formalities
