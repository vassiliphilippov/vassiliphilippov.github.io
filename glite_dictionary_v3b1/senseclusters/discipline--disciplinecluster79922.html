discipline

verb
to punish for control or obedience
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪsəˌplɪn / (AmE), / ˈdɪs.ə.plɪn /; / ˈdɪs.ɪ.plɪn / (BrE)
to train or punish someone in order to make them obey rules or behave properly

Examples

To discipline a child
Parents may choose different ways to discipline their children based on their beliefs.
The manager warned he would discipline any employee caught violating company policy.
The principal had to discipline several students for breaking school rules.
It can be difficult to discipline a stubborn puppy without patience and consistency.
She disciplined herself to resist distractions while studying for her exams.
Teachers should aim to discipline students by setting clear expectations and positive reinforcement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: punish - to subject someone to a penalty for an offense
  • synonym: train - to teach a person or animal a particular skill or type of behavior through practice and instruction
