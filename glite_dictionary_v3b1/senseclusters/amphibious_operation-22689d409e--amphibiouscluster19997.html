amphibious operation

noun
military action with land and sea forces
Pronunciation: / æmˈfɪbiəs ˌɑpəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / amˈfɪbɪəs ˌɒpəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a military operation that involves both land and sea forces, especially one where troops are landed from the sea onto shore

Examples

Large amphibious operations
During World War II, several major battles began with large-scale amphibious operations.
Successful amphibious operations require careful coordination between naval and ground forces.
The marines prepared for an amphibious operation on the enemy-held coastline.
