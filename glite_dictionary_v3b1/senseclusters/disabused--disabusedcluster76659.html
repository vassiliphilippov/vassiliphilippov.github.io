disabused

adjective
freed from a mistaken belief
7.3 6.1
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsəˈbjuːzd / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈbjuːzd / (BrE)
No longer holding a mistaken or misguided belief; having been freed from an error or misconception.

Examples

To be disabused of ideas
After reading the report, she was disabused of her assumption that the process was simple.
He is now disabused of the belief that money alone brings happiness.
Many students left the seminar disabused of their previous misconceptions about climate change.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disabuse - to persuade someone that an idea or belief is mistaken
  • antonym: misled - having been led into error or false belief
