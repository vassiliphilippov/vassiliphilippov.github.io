doomful

adjective
full of foreboding
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈduːmfəl / (AmE), / ˈduːmfʊl / (BrE)
giving a strong sense of disaster, danger, or something bad about to happen

Examples

Doomful warning
The sky was covered in doomful clouds before the storm began.
His doomful predictions made everyone in the room uneasy.
A doomful silence filled the hall after the announcement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hopeful - giving a sense of hope or optimism
  • synonym: ominous - suggesting that something bad will happen
