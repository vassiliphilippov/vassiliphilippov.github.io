backflip

noun
sudden policy reversal
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌflɪp / (AmE), / ˈbækˌflɪp / (BrE)
an abrupt reversal of a previous decision, opinion, or policy, especially in politics or public statements

Examples

A political backflip
The minister's backflip on the tax proposal angered many voters.
After public pressure, the company performed a backflip and reinstated the canceled service.
Critics accused the leader of a political backflip after he changed his stance overnight.

Related Terms

  • antonym: commitment - a firm decision or promise to do something
  • synonym: reversal - a change to an opposite direction, position, or course of action
  • synonym: u-turn - a complete change of opinion or policy
