preindication

noun
sign of things to come; omen
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːɪn.dɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːɪn.dɪˈkeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
an event or sign that is regarded as indicating important things to come; a forewarning or omen

Examples

A preindication of trouble
Her sudden silence struck him as a troubling preindication of future difficulties.
Some investors look for any preindication of market shifts to adjust their strategies.
The dark clouds were seen as a preindication of the approaching storm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: augury - a sign of what will happen in the future; an omen
  • synonym: omen - an event seen as a sign of future good or bad luck
