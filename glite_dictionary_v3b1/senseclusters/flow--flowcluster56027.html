flow

verb
cause something to move like a stream
5.4 1.0
Pronunciation: /floʊ/ (AmE), /fləʊ/ (BrE)
to move steadily and continuously in a stream or current, like a liquid or gas

Examples

To flow smoothly
She flowed a thin layer of glaze across the surface of the pottery.
The artist carefully flowed the ink onto the canvas to create smooth gradations.
The technician flowed coolant into the system to prevent overheating.
After the heavy rain, water began to flow down the hillside, flooding the road below.
Rivers flow to the sea, carrying water from mountains across the land.
She watched the syrup flow slowly out of the bottle onto her pancakes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: freeze - become solid and cease to flow, as a liquid
  • synonym: pour - to cause a liquid to move in a stream, especially from a container
  • synonym: stream - move in a continuous flow, like a liquid
  • degree lower: trickle - flow slowly in a thin stream
