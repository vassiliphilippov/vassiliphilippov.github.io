cellulase

noun
cellulose-degrading enzyme
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛljəˌleɪs / (AmE), / ˈsɛljʊleɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme (or enzyme mixture) that breaks down cellulose into simpler sugars

Examples

Cellulase enzyme
Scientists use cellulase to convert plant material into biofuels.
The addition of cellulase helps break down paper waste in recycling plants.
Some laundry detergents contain cellulase to remove stains from cotton fabrics.
Certain fungi produce cellulase to help decompose plant material.
Scientists use cellulase in laboratories to break down plant fibers for research.
The presence of cellulase in the soil helps recycle nutrients from dead plants.
