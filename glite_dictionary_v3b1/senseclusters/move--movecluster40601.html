move

verb
change position or perform a motion
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /muːv/ (AmE), /muːv/ (BrE)
to change position or place, or to be in motion (physically or metaphorically)

Examples

To move your body
He moves his fingers gently when playing the piano.
She moved her arm to wave at her friend.
The dog moved its tail in excitement.
Could you move the car out of the driveway?
She decided to move her savings to a different account for better interest rates.
The manager moved several tasks to the new team member.
As the parade moves through the city, crowds gather to cheer.
News of the merger moved quickly throughout the company.
The train will move out of the station at 7 o'clock sharp.

Related Terms

  • synonym: advance - to move forward, especially in a purposeful way
  • antonym: be still - to not move; to remain motionless
  • synonym: proceed - to go forward or continue
  • antonym: remain - to stay in the same place; not move
  • synonym: shift - to change position, especially slightly
  • antonym: stay - to remain in the same place or state
  • antonym: still - to make or become motionless
  • synonym: travel - to go from one place to another, typically over a distance
