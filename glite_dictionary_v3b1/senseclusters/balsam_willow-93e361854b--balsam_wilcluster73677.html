balsam willow

noun
tree with balsam-scented leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑlsəm ˈwɪloʊ / (AmE), / ˈbælsəm ˈwɪləʊ / (BrE)
A small shrubby tree native to eastern North America, whose leaves emit a balsam-like scent when crushed.

Examples

The balsam willow leaves
Her field guide helped her identify the balsam willow by its distinctive odor and glossy leaves.
The balsam willow is often found along riverbanks in eastern Canada and the northern United States.
When crushed, the leaves of the balsam willow release a fragrant, resinous scent.
