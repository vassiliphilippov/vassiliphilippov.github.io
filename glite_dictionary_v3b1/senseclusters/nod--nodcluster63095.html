nod

noun
a downward movement of the head
Pronunciation: / nɑd / (AmE), / nɒd / (BrE)
a quick downward movement of the head, often as a signal or gesture of agreement, greeting, or command

Examples

A quick nod
She gave a friendly nod as she entered the room.
The teacher acknowledged the answer with a nod of approval.
With a quick nod, he signaled that he understood the instructions.
A simple nod from the coach signaled the start of the game.
She gave a quick nod to show her approval.
With a polite nod, he greeted the new guest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beckon - make a gesture with hand or head to signal someone
  • antonym: shake - the act of shaking the head, often to indicate 'no' or disagreement
