media

noun
mass communication channels
2.1
Pronunciation: / ˈmiːdiə / (AmE), / ˈmiːdiə / (BrE)
the main ways of communicating information to large numbers of people, such as television, radio, newspapers, and the internet

Examples

News media
The media reported extensively on the election results throughout the night.
Many people rely on social media to stay informed about current events.
Concerns about bias in the media have led to debates about journalistic standards.
Many people rely on the media to stay informed about current events around the world.
The influence of the media on public opinion has grown significantly in recent years.
Social media platforms have changed the way people share news and information.

Related Terms

  • antonym: audience - the people who watch, read, or listen to media
  • synonym: journalism - the activity of reporting, writing, and editing news stories
  • synonym: press - newspapers and journalists collectively
