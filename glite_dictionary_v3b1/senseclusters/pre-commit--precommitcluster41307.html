pre-commit

verb
to commit in advance
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːkəˈmɪt / (AmE), / ˌpriːkəˈmɪt / (BrE)
to commit oneself, resources, or plans in advance of a future event or decision

Examples

To pre-commit resources
The company decided to pre-commit resources to the new initiative before the official launch.
By asking members to pre-commit to the event, organizers ensured a higher attendance rate.
Some investors prefer to pre-commit their funds to secure early access to promising projects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pledge - to make a serious promise or commitment
  • synonym: promise - to state that you will definitely do or not do something
  • antonym: withdraw - to take back or remove a commitment or offer
