fecundity

noun
intellectual productivity or creativity
5.8
Pronunciation: / fəˈkʌn.də.ti / (AmE), / fɪˈkʌn.dɪ.ti / (BrE)
the ability to produce many new and original ideas; intellectual productivity, especially of a creative imagination

Examples

Creative fecundity
Einstein's remarkable fecundity of ideas led to groundbreaking developments in physics.
The fecundity of her mind allowed her to write novels that captivated readers for decades.
The painter was celebrated for the fecundity of his imagination, which was evident in his unique artwork.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fertility - ability to produce offspring or fruit; also used metaphorically for productivity
