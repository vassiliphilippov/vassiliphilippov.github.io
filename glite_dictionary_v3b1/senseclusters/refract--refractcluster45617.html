refract

verb
measure optical refraction
Pronunciation: / rɪˈfrækt / (AmE), / rɪˈfrɑːkt / (BrE)
to measure the refracting power of a lens or other optical element.

Examples

To refract a lens
In the lab, scientists routinely refract different types of glass to study their optical properties.
The optician will refract each lens to ensure it meets specifications.
The technician refracted the new lens before installing it in the microscope.
