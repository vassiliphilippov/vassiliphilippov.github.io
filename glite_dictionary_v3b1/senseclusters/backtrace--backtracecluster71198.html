backtrace

verb
to trace backward
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌtreɪs / (AmE), / ˈbækˌtreɪs / (BrE)
to follow steps backward in a process to find the origin of a problem or event

Examples

To backtrace a problem
The technician had to backtrace the network issue to its original cause.
After the system crashed, the developer began to backtrace the sequence of events in the code.
To solve the puzzle, you need to backtrace each step taken so far.
