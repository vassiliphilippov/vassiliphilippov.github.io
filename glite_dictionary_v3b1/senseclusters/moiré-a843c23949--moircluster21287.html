moiré

adjective
wavy patterned
Pronunciation: / mwɑːˈreɪ / (AmE), / mwɑːˈreɪ / (BrE)
having a wavy, rippled, or watered pattern, often seen in fabric or visual effects

Examples

Moire pattern fabric
The artist used a moiré effect to create a sense of movement in the painting.
She wore a dress with a subtle moiré pattern that shimmered in the light.
Photographers sometimes notice unwanted moiré patterns when shooting fine fabrics or screens.

Related Terms

  • antonym: plain - having no pattern or decoration
  • synonym: watered - having a surface with a wavy or rippled pattern
