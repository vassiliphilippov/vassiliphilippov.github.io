reef point

noun
short line in a sail for reefing
7.1 9.0
Pronunciation: / ɹiːf pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ɹiːf pɔɪnt / (BrE)
(nautical) one of several short lengths of line sewn into a sail, used for tying part of the sail down to reduce its area in strong winds.

Examples

Reef points on sail
After lowering the mainsail a little, they gathered the excess fabric using the reef points.
The sailor secured each reef point carefully before the storm hit.
You must tie the reef points tightly to prevent the sail from flapping.
