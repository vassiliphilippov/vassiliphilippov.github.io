faultfinding

adjective
inclined to criticize others
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔltˌfaɪndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɔːltˌfaɪndɪŋ / (BrE)
always criticizing or pointing out faults in others or things

Examples

Faultfinding people
A good leader avoids being faultfinding and instead encourages positive change.
His faultfinding attitude made it difficult for others to enjoy working with him.
She tried to ignore her colleague's faultfinding remarks during the meeting.
Despite his success, he had to endure the faultfinding remarks of his critics.
Her faultfinding comments often discouraged those around her from sharing their ideas.
The manager's faultfinding attitude made it difficult for the team to stay motivated.

Related Terms

  • synonym: critical - inclined to find fault or express disapproval
  • antonym: forgiving - ready to overlook faults and not criticize
  • antonym: tolerant - showing willingness to allow the existence of opinions or behavior that one does not necessarily agree with
