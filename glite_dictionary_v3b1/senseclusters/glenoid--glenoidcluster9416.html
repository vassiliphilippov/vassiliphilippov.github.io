glenoid

noun
shoulder socket
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlɛnɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈɡliːnɔɪd / (BrE)
the shallow socket on the scapula that receives the head of the humerus to form the shoulder joint

Examples

The glenoid socket
The surgeon examined the glenoid for signs of damage before proceeding with the shoulder replacement.
Arthritis can cause the glenoid to wear down, leading to joint pain and stiffness.
A fracture of the glenoid may require surgery to restore proper shoulder function.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acetabulum - the socket in the hip bone that receives the head of the femur
