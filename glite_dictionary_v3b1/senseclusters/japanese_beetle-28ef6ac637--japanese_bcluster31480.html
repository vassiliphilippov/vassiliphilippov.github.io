Japanese beetle

noun
metallic green and brown plant pest beetle
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈpænɪs ˈbiːtl̩ / (AmE), / dʒəˈpænɪs ˈbiːtl / (BrE)
a small metallic green and brown beetle native to eastern Asia, now a serious plant pest in North America

Examples

The Japanese beetle pest
Gardeners use traps to monitor and control Japanese beetle infestations.
Large numbers of Japanese beetles were found feeding on the corn leaves.
The Japanese beetle has caused widespread damage to rose bushes in my garden this summer.
