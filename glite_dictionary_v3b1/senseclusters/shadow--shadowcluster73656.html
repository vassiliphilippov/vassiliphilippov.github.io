shadow

verb
cast a shadow over
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈʃædoʊ / (AmE), / ˈʃædəʊ / (BrE)
To cast a shadow over (someone or something); to make (an area, object, etc.) dark by blocking light.

Examples

To shadow a face
She stood so the umbrella would shadow her face from the harsh sunlight.
Tall trees shadow the garden in the late afternoon, making it pleasantly cool.
The mountain shadowed the village for most of the day, keeping the houses in shade.

Related Terms

  • synonym: obscure - to make less clear or harder to see
