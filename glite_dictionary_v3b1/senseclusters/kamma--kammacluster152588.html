kamma

noun
Buddhist moral action
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑː.mə/ /; / /ˈkæ.mə/ (AmE), /ˈkɑː.mə/ /; / /ˈkæ.mə/ (BrE)
the principle in Buddhist and Hindu philosophy that a person's actions (especially moral ones) have consequences that affect their future experiences

Examples

The kamma principle
In Buddhist teachings, every kamma has consequences that shape future experiences.
She tried to perform good kamma to bring about positive results in her life.
Monks often discuss the importance of mindful kamma during their sermons.
Many Buddhists believe that kamma shapes their future lives through their actions.
He tried to do good deeds every day to improve his kamma.
The concept of kamma encourages people to act kindly and responsibly.
Many Buddhists believe that good kamma leads to happiness in future lives.
He tried to improve his kamma by helping others and being honest.
Some people see misfortune as the result of bad kamma from past actions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fate - the idea that events are predetermined and inevitable
  • synonym: karma - the sum of a person's actions, influencing future fate
