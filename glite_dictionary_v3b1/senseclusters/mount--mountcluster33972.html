mount

verb
fix or attach to a support
3.8 17/m
Pronunciation: / maÊŠnt / (AmE), / maÊŠnt / (BrE)
to fix or attach something to a support, backing, or setting

Examples

To mount a camera
Please mount the projector securely on the ceiling before use.
She is mounting the gemstones in silver settings for the new collection.
The technician carefully mounted the photograph in a sleek black frame.
After assembling the shelves, she mounted them on the living room wall.
The technician will mount the new camera above the entrance for better security.
You need to mount the mirror securely to prevent it from falling.

Synonyms

  • fix
    1.0 17/m
    fix secures something firmly so it does not move, while mount often means placing it onto a support or backing.
  • fasten
    1.6 6.55/m
    fasten means secure something firmly in place, while mount usually adds the idea of fixing it to a backing or display surface.
  • attach
    2.5 39/m
    attach means join or connect two things together, while mount often means attaching an object to a support for display.
  • affix
    5.0 2.32/m
    affix is a formal or adhesive way to stick something to a surface, while mount implies placing it on a support.
