Synonyms

  • make a decision
    1.0 13/m
    make a decision is the common phrase for choosing after thought, while decide names the act in one simple verb.
  • choose
    1.0 200/m
    choose means picking one option from several, while decide emphasizes thinking and reaching a judgment about those options.
  • make up one's mind
    3.2 5/m
    make up one's mind is an informal way to settle on something, while decide is the general verb for making that choice.
  • come to a decision
    2.7 0.8/m
    come to a decision describes reaching a choice after thinking, while decide names the action of choosing in a single verb.
  • arrive at a decision
    2.9 0.03/m
    arrive at a decision sounds formal and measured after review, while decide is the neutral verb for choosing.
  • conclude
    3.1 20/m
    conclude often means decide after considering evidence or facts, while decide covers both quick choices and careful judgments.
  • come to a conclusion
    3.0 0.6/m
    come to a conclusion often follows reasoning and shows a judgment, while decide can mean making a choice with or without full proof.
  • settle upon
    3.9 0.03/m
    settle upon implies choosing after some discussion or agreement, while decide is the broader verb for making that choice alone.
  • resolve
    3.4 4/m
    resolve means make a firm, determined decision often after effort, while decide is a neutral word for choosing or judging.
  • opt
    3.2 10/m
    opt is a short, often formal verb for choosing one option, while decide stresses the process of thinking before choosing.
  • determine
    3.2 20/m
    determine often means decide officially or find a final answer in formal settings, while decide is used in general everyday choice.
