licorice

noun
a chewy black candy
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪkərɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈlɪkərɪs /; / ˈlɪk.ər.ɪs / (BrE)
A chewy black candy or sweet made with extract from the root of the licorice plant, often with a distinct, slightly bitter flavor.

Examples

A licorice candy
He offered her a piece of licorice on the train ride.
Many people dislike the distinctive flavor of black licorice.
My grandmother always kept a bag of licorice in her kitchen for a sweet treat.
Many people find the taste of liquorice either delightful or unpleasant.
She bought a bag of liquorice at the sweet shop for her grandfather.
Traditional liquorice is often softer and less sweet than the modern version.

Related Terms

  • synonym: candy - a sweet food made mainly of sugar
  • synonym: licorice - a black candy flavored with the dried root of the licorice plant (American spelling)
