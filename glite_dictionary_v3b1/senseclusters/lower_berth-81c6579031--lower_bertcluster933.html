lower berth

noun
the bottom bed of two stacked berths
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊ.ər bɜrθ / (AmE), / ˈləʊ.ə bɜːθ / (BrE)
the bottom bed or sleeping place of two stacked one above the other, especially in a train or ship compartment

Examples

The lower berth
I prefer the lower berth to avoid using the ladder at night.
She requested the lower berth because it was easier for her to climb into.
The train compartment had an upper and a lower berth for overnight passengers.
