toxicity

noun
harmful or hostile behaviour in a group
5.9
Pronunciation: /tɑːk.ˈsɪs.ə.tiː/ /; / /tɑːk.ˈsɪs.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /tɒk.ˈsɪs.ə.tiː/ /; / /tɒk.ˈsɪs.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
The quality or level of harmful, hostile, or negative behaviour and attitudes within a group, community, or environment (for example, an online forum, workplace, or team).

Examples

Workplace toxicity
The toxicity of the online forum discouraged new members from asking even simple questions.
After the meeting, she said the department's toxicity made honest collaboration impossible.
Fans blamed the team's toxicity for low morale and several recent player departures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hostility - aggressive, unfriendly, or antagonistic behaviour within a group
  • antonym: supportiveness - behaviour that encourages, helps, and supports members of a group
