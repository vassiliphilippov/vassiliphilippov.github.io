economism

noun
focus on economics
9.4
Pronunciation: / ɪˈkɑːnəˌmɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪˈkɒnəmɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief that economic factors are the most important, often ignoring social or cultural aspects

Examples

Political economism
Some politicians are criticized for their economism, ignoring the cultural needs of communities.
The debate highlighted the dangers of economism in shaping public policy.
Her approach to education was shaped by economism, focusing mainly on job outcomes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: holism - the idea that systems should be viewed as wholes, not just as a collection of parts
  • synonym: materialism - the belief that material or economic things are most important
