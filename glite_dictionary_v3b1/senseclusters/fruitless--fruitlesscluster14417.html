fruitless

adjective
unsuccessful; unproductive
4.8 2.18/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàfruÀêtl…ôs / (AmE), / ÀàfruÀêtl…™s / (BrE)
failing to achieve the desired results; unproductive or unsuccessful.

Examples

A fruitless search
After a fruitless search for his keys, he finally gave up and called a locksmith.
She made one last fruitless attempt to persuade him to stay.
Their efforts to improve the system proved fruitless despite months of discussion.

Synonyms

  • unproductive
    3.8 2.50/m
    unproductive describes work or effort that gives no useful result, while fruitless focuses on failure to reach a goal.
  • futile
    6.0 6.54/m
    futile feels pointless and gives little hope of success, while fruitless simply says an action fails to produce results.
  • unsuccessful
    2.9 6.96/m
    unsuccessful means not achieving a planned goal, while fruitless often stresses that effort or work produced no results.
  • unavailing
    8.2 0.10/m
    unavailing sounds formal and means efforts fail to help, while fruitless is more common to say attempts gave no result.
  • unfruitful
    5.9 0.24/m
    unfruitful often implies lack of growth or benefit, while fruitless highlights failing to reach the wanted result.
  • ineffective
    3.4 9.06/m
    ineffective focuses on lacking the needed effect or power, while fruitless stresses that the effort produced no success.
  • abortive
    7.7 0.77/m
    abortive often means a plan or attempt stops or fails early, while fruitless simply says it did not succeed.
  • vain
    3.9 4.77/m
    vain describes efforts that do not work despite effort and may sound literary, while fruitless is plain and widely used.
  • nonproductive
    4.5 0.34/m
    nonproductive sounds technical and means not making output, while fruitless is more general for attempts that fail to achieve results.

Related Terms

  • synonym: futile - incapable of producing any useful result; pointless
  • antonym: successful - achieving the desired aim or result
  • synonym: vain - producing no result; useless
