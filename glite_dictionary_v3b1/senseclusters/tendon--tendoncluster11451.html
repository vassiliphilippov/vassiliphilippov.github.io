tendon

noun
cord of tissue connecting muscle to bone
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛn.dən / (AmE), / ˈtɛn.dən / (BrE)
A cord or band of tough, inelastic tissue that connects a muscle to a bone.

Examples

Achilles tendon
Achilles’ tendon injury is common among runners.
He suffered a torn tendon in his shoulder during the game.
The doctor explained that a tendon connects muscle to bone, unlike a ligament.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ligament - a band of tissue connecting bones at a joint
