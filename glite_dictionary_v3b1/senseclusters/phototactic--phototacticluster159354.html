phototactic

adjective
responding to light
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊtəˈtæktɪk / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəˈtæktɪk / (BrE)
describing an organism that moves toward or away from light

Examples

Phototactic organisms move
Moths are often described as phototactic because they are attracted to light sources at night.
The phototactic behavior of algae helps them move toward sunlight for photosynthesis.
Some aquatic organisms display phototactic movement, swimming toward or away from light depending on the time of day.

Related Terms

  • antonym: chemotactic - moving in response to a chemical stimulus
  • synonym: phototropic - growing or turning toward light
