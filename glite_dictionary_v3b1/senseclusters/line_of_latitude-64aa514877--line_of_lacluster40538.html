line of latitude

noun
imaginary Earth line parallel to the equator
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪn əv ˈlætɪˌtud / (AmE), / ˈlaɪn əv ˈlætɪˌtjuːd / (BrE)
An imaginary line encircling the Earth, parallel to the equator, used to indicate position north or south.

Examples

The line of latitude
Every line of latitude represents a fixed distance from the equator.
Maps often display each line of latitude to help users determine global position.
The Tropic of Cancer is a line of latitude located north of the equator.

Related Terms

  • synonym: latitude - Angular distance north or south of the equator
