out of the side of one's mouth

adverb
quietly or discreetly (idiom)
6.3
Pronunciation: / aʊt əv ðə saɪd əv wʌnz maʊθ / (AmE), / aʊt əv ðə saɪd əv wʌnz maʊθ / (BrE)
in a quiet, discreet, or indirect way, often to avoid being overheard or noticed

Examples

Speak out of the side of one's mouth
He whispered advice out of the side of his mouth during the meeting to avoid drawing attention.
She quietly shared the news out of the side of her mouth so nobody else could hear.
Trying not to be noticed, Lisa mumbled a warning out of the side of her mouth.
