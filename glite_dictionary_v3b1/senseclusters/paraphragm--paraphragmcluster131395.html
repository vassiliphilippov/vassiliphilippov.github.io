paraphragm

noun
camera light opening
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpærəˌfræm / (AmE), / ˈpærəˌfræm / (BrE)
an adjustable opening in a camera or lens that controls the amount of light passing through, also known as an aperture

Examples

Camera paraphragm
She adjusted the paraphragm to reduce the amount of light entering the lens during the shoot.
A wider paraphragm lets in more light, which is useful in low-light photography.
The photographer explained how the paraphragm affects the depth of field in an image.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aperture - an opening in a camera lens that controls the amount of light entering
  • synonym: diaphragm - a device in a camera or optical instrument that controls the size of the opening for light
