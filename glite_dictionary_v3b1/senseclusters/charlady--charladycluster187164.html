charlady

noun
female cleaner (old-fashioned)
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɑrˌleɪdi / (AmE), / ˈtʃɑːˌleɪdi / (BrE)
a woman whose job is to clean houses or offices, especially in the past

Examples

The charlady cleans
The charlady arrived early to tidy the office before work began.
In old novels, a charlady often appears as a hardworking woman cleaning wealthy homes.
My grandmother once worked as a charlady in London during the 1950s.
