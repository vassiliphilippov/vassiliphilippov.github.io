allomerism

noun
variability of composition with same crystal form (chemistry)
Pronunciation: / əˈlɑː.məˌrɪz.əm / (AmE), / əˈlɒ.mə.rɪ.zəm / (BrE)
(Chemistry) The phenomenon in which substances have different chemical compositions but share the same crystalline form.

Examples

Allomerism in crystals
Despite their different chemical compositions, the minerals showed allomerism in their crystal structure.
Researchers were intrigued by the allomerism observed in various inorganic salts.
The concept of allomerism helps crystallographers understand anomalies in solid-state chemistry.
