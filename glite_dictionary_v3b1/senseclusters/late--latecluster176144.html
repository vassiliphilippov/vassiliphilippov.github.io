late

adjective, adverb
at an advanced age or stage
2.4
Pronunciation: / leɪt / (AmE), / leɪt / (BrE)
occurring near or toward the end of a period, stage, or process; or relating to a later stage in history or development

Examples

Late stage
He started painting late and still became a renowned artist.
Many people choose to change careers late, seeking new challenges.
She decided to get her degree late in life, after raising her children.
In the late nineteenth century, technology advanced rapidly.
Symptoms usually appear in the late phase of the illness.
The late stage of the project required more resources and careful management.
Scholars often distinguish between Early and Late Egyptian in their research.
She is an expert in Late Latin vocabulary and its evolution.
The museum's exhibit focuses on ceramics from the Late Bronze Age.
