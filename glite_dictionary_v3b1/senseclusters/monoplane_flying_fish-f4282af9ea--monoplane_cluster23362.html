monoplane flying fish

noun
flying fish with only pectoral fins enlarged
9.6
Pronunciation: /ˈmɑː.nə.pleɪ ˈflaɪ.ɪŋ fɪʃ/ (AmE), /ˈmɒ.nə.pleɪ ˈflaɪ.ɪŋ fɪʃ/ (BrE)
A type of flying fish distinguished by having only the pectoral fins enlarged, enabling it to glide above water.

Examples

The monoplane flying fish glides
Marine biologists are fascinated by the gliding abilities of the monoplane flying fish.
The monoplane flying fish uses its enlarged pectoral fins to glide long distances above the waves.
Unlike its relatives, the monoplane flying fish lacks enlarged pelvic fins.
