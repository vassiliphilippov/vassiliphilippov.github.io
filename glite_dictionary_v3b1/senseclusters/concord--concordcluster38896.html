concord

noun
agreement of grammatical features between words
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːn.kɔrd / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.kɔːd / (BrE)
(Linguistics) Agreement between words in gender, number, case, or person, often shown by inflectional endings or forms.

Examples

Grammatical concord
Errors in concord are common when learners translate word-for-word from their native language.
In French, noun-adjective concord requires adjectives to match the gender and number of the noun.
Teachers explained subject-verb concord to help students write grammatically correct sentences.
