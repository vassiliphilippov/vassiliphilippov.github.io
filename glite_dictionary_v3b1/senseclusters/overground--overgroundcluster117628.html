overground

noun
mainstream scene
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvərˌɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌɡraʊnd / (BrE)
the mainstream, public, or legally accepted part of a scene or activity, as opposed to the underground.

Examples

The overground of culture
After years of playing in small clubs, the band finally broke into the overground with a hit album.
Many artists prefer to stay in the overground to reach a wider audience and gain recognition.
The festival celebrates music from both the overground and the underground scenes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mainstream - the ideas, attitudes, or activities regarded as normal or conventional
  • antonym: underground - a group or movement that operates outside the mainstream or in secret
