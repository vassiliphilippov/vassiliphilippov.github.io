broad-headed

adjective
having a wide or broad head

Having a relatively wide or broad head in proportion to its length; brachycephalic.

Examples

Broad-headed people
Some ancient populations are classified as broad-headed in anthropological studies.
The dog was noted for its unique broad-headed appearance in the show.
This species of lizard is broad-headed, which helps it hunt larger prey.

Synonyms

  • short-headed
    7.9 0.00/m
    short-headed is used for a head that is both short and wide in everyday speech, while broad-headed is a general descriptive term.
  • brachycephalic
    9.9 0.00/m
    brachycephalic is the medical term for a short, broad skull used in science, while broad-headed is more general and informal.
  • roundheaded
    8.1 0.00/m
    roundheaded describes a short, rounded skull shape often seen in animals, while broad-headed only stresses width visually.
  • brachycranial
    9.9 0.00/m
    brachycranial names a skull type measured by a high cephalic index in anthropology, while broad-headed is a plain descriptive adjective.
  • brachycranic
    9.9 0.00/m
    brachycranic describes a short, broad skull usually by a specific measurement, while broad-headed is a non-technical visual description.
