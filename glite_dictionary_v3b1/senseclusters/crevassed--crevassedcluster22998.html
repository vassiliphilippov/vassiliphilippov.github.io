crevassed

adjective
full of deep cracks
Pronunciation: / krəˈvæst / (AmE), / krɪˈvæst / (BrE)
having deep cracks or fissures, especially in ice, earth, or rock

Examples

Crevassed glacier surface
The mountaineers carefully crossed the crevassed glacier, watching for hidden dangers beneath the snow.
After the earthquake, the once flat field became dangerously crevassed and difficult to walk across.
Scientists studied the crevassed surface of the polar ice sheet to understand how it was changing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fissured - having long, narrow openings or cracks
  • antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface; free from cracks
