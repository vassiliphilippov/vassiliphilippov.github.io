alms tray

noun
tray for collecting church offerings
Pronunciation: / ɑlmz treɪ / (AmE), / ɑːlmz treɪ / (BrE)
A tray used to collect offerings or donations from members of a congregation during a religious service.

Examples

The alms tray passed
During the Sunday service, the usher passed the alms tray along each pew.
The silver alms tray was engraved with the church's name and founding date.
Volunteers cleaned the alms tray before returning it to the sacristy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collection plate - a plate passed around to collect offerings in church
