sodium pyrophosphate

noun
sodium salt used in soaps and detergents
2.4
Pronunciation: / ˌsoʊdiəm paɪˌroʊˈfɑsˌfeɪt / (AmE), / ˌsəʊdiəm paɪˌrəʊˈfɒs.feɪt / (BrE)
a sodium salt of pyrophosphoric acid, commonly used as a builder in soaps and detergents

Examples

Sodium pyrophosphate in detergents
Some detergents contain sodium pyrophosphate to enhance their cleaning effectiveness.
The food industry sometimes uses sodium pyrophosphate as an emulsifier and texturizer in processed foods.
You should check if your dishwashing powder includes sodium pyrophosphate among its ingredients.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tetrasodium pyrophosphate - a specific form of sodium pyrophosphate often found in food additives and cleaning agents
