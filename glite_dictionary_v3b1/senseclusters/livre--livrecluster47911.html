livre

noun
old French weight unit
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈliː.vər/ /; / /ˈliːv.rə/ (AmE), /ˈliːv.rə/ /; / /ˈliː.və/ (BrE)
a former French unit of weight, roughly equivalent to a pound, used before the metric system and varying regionally

Examples

Livre of cheese
Before the metric system, French merchants weighed goods in livres instead of kilograms.
The recipe called for two livres of flour, which confused modern bakers.
Historians often convert livre measurements to grams for clarity in research.
