quantum chromodynamics

noun
theory of strong interactions in particle physics
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɑntəm ˌkroʊmoʊdaɪˈnæmɪks / (AmE), / ˈkwɒntəm ˌkrəʊməʊdaɪˈnæmɪks / (BrE)
the theory in particle physics that describes the strong interaction binding quarks together within protons, neutrons, and other hadrons, mediated by gluons

Examples

Quantum chromodynamics theory
According to quantum chromodynamics, gluons are the particles responsible for binding quarks together.
Students studied quantum chromodynamics in their advanced particle physics course.
The development of quantum chromodynamics revolutionized our understanding of the forces inside the atomic nucleus.
