depletion

noun
significant reduction of something
4.3 4.3
Pronunciation: / dɪˈpliʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈpliːʃən / (BrE)
the reduction or decrease in the amount or number of something, especially when it becomes low or used up

Examples

Fish depletion
Overfishing can lead to rapid depletion of fish stocks in the ocean.
The depletion of natural resources is a serious global concern.
The team monitored the depletion of funds in the account over the course of the year.
Frequent use of the medicine can cause depletion of essential vitamins in the body.
Overfishing has led to a significant depletion of the region's fish population.
The depletion of natural resources is a growing concern for environmentalists.

  • antonym: increase - the act of becoming greater in size or amount
  • synonym: reduction - the act of making something smaller or less
  • antonym: replenishment - the act of making something full again
