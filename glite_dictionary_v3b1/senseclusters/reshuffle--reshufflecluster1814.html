reshuffle

verb
shuffle or reorganize again
1.0 4.8
Pronunciation: / ˌriˈʃʌfəl /; / riˈʃʌfəl / (AmE), / ˌriːˈʃʌf(ə)l /; / riˈʃʌfəl / (BrE)
to change the order or arrangement of something, especially by reorganizing people or positions

Examples

To reshuffle the team
After the committee meeting, they had to reshuffle the schedule to fit everyone's availability.
The magician quickly reshuffled the cards to impress the audience.
To make the game fair, the dealer decided to reshuffle the deck.
After a period of poor performance, management reshuffled key roles within the department.
The Prime Minister decided to reshuffle the cabinet to bring in new talent.
The company often reshuffles staff to improve efficiency and morale.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reorganize - to arrange something again or in a different way
  • synonym: restructure - to organize something differently, often with major changes
  • synonym: shuffle - to mix up the order of (typically cards)
