limb

noun
part of a bow from handle to tip
9.9
Pronunciation: / lɪm / (AmE), / lɪm / (BrE)
a part of a tool or instrument that extends outward, such as the arm of a bow or the arc of a measuring device

Examples

A crack in the lower limb could weaken the bow significantly.
Custom bows can be fitted with different limbs to suit the shooter's strength.

Always inspect the limb of your instrument to ensure accurate readings during fieldwork.
Old astronomical telescopes featured a brass limb engraved with degree marks for measurement.
The technician carefully aligned the reading on the limb of the theodolite.
