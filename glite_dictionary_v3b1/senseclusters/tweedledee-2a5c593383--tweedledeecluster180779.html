Tweedledee

noun
foolish or trivial person
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌtwiːdəlˈdiː / (AmE), / ˌtwiːdəlˈdiː / (BrE)
a foolish, trivial, or insignificant person, often used contemptuously to suggest lack of importance or intelligence

Examples

A Tweedledee person
They treated him like a Tweedledee, ignoring his suggestions during the meeting.
The article described the politician as a mere Tweedledee with no real influence.
She refused to be seen as a Tweedledee and spoke up for herself.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fool - a person who acts unwisely or imprudently
  • antonym: genius - a person with exceptional intellectual ability
  • synonym: simpleton - a person lacking in common sense or intelligence
