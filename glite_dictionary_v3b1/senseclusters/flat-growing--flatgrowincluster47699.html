flat-growing

adjective
growing close and low to the ground
Pronunciation: / ˈflæt ˌɡroʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈflæt ˌɡrəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a plant) growing close and low to the ground, spreading out rather than rising upright.

Examples

Flat-growing plants
Many weeds have a flat-growing habit, making them hard to remove.
She prefers flat-growing plants along the edge of the pathway.
The flat-growing juniper is perfect for ground cover in gardens.

Related Terms

  • antonym: upright - (of a plant) growing vertically or erect
