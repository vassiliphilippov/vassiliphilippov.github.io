foot-pound

noun
unit of work or energy (force x distance)
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈfʊt ˌpaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈfʊt ˌpaʊnd / (BrE)
A unit of work or energy equal to the amount done by a force of one pound acting through a distance of one foot.

Examples

Fifty foot-pounds
He calculated the work done as fifty foot-pounds in total.
One foot-pound is a common unit used in physics for measuring energy.
The engine is capable of producing 400 foot-pounds of torque.

Related Terms

  • synonym: joule - SI unit of work or energy equal to the work done when one newton moves an object one meter
