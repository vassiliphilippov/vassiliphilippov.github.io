salience

noun
quality of being prominent or noticeable
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪ.li.əns / (AmE), / ˈseɪ.li.əns / (BrE)
The quality of being particularly noticeable, important, or prominent.

Examples

The salience of issues
Media attention often increases the salience of certain issues in the public mind.
The salience of climate change in recent elections has greatly influenced voters’ decisions.
The salience of her comments made everyone stop and listen carefully.

Related Terms

  • antonym: insignificance - lack of importance or noticeability; not prominent
  • synonym: prominence - the state of being important or famous; noticeable
