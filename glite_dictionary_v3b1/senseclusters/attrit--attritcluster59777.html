attrit

verb
to erode by rubbing
Pronunciation: / əˈtrɪt / (AmE), / əˈtrɪt / (BrE)
to wear away or gradually erode a surface by friction or rubbing

Examples

To attrit a surface
Over time, the constant movement of sand can attrit the surface of rocks along the shore.
The repeated use of the tool began to attrit its handle, making it smooth and thin.
If you do not protect the statue, wind and rain will slowly attrit its features.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abrade - to wear down or rub away by scraping
  • synonym: erode - to gradually wear away or destroy by natural forces
  • antonym: preserve - to keep something in its original state
