sandworm

noun
worm living in sand
Pronunciation: / ˈsændˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈsændˌwɜːm / (BrE)
a worm that lives in sand, often used as fishing bait, or a large fictional worm in science fiction stories

Examples

A sandworm for bait
Fishermen often dig for sandworms early in the morning to use as bait.
In the famous science fiction novel, giant sandworms roam the desert planet.
The biology class studied the habitat of sandworms found along the coast.
Fishermen often use sandworm as bait for catching fish.
The novel describes a giant sandworm that lives beneath the desert surface.
Children found a small sandworm while digging on the beach.

Related Terms

  • synonym: earthworm - a common worm that lives in soil
  • synonym: lugworm - a worm found in sandy beaches, used as fishing bait
