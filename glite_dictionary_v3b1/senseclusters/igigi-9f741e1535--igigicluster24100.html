Igigi

noun
group of Mesopotamian heavenly deities
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈgiː.giː/ /; / /iː.ˈgiː.giː/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈgɪ.gɪ/ /; / /ɪ.ˈgiː.giː/ (BrE)
In ancient Mesopotamian mythology, any of a group of celestial deities or spirits who served under the god Anu.

Examples

The Igigi gods
According to myth, the Igigi once rebelled against their labor imposed by the god Enlil.
Archaeologists study references to the Igigi to understand Sumerian religious beliefs.
The ancient texts often depict the Igigi as intermediaries between mortals and the higher gods.
