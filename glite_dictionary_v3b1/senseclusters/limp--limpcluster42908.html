limp

noun
uneven walk, usually from injury
3.7
Pronunciation: / lɪmp / (AmE), / lɪmp / (BrE)
an uneven or abnormal way of walking, typically caused by injury or weakness in a leg or foot

Examples

A noticeable limp
After twisting his ankle, he developed a noticeable limp for several days.
Her limp became more pronounced each time she stood up from her chair.
The doctor observed the patient's limp while she walked across the room.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hobble - to walk in an awkward way, usually because of pain or injury
