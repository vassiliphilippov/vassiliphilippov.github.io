Synonyms

  • foreordination

  • preordination
    9.9 0.01/m
    preordination states God decides events in advance for all time, while predestination is the broad doctrine that outcomes are fixed by divine will.
  • predetermination
    9.7 0.09/m
    predetermination names the theological idea that God sets outcomes beforehand, while predestination is the belief that people's lives are already decided.
  • predestinarianism
    9.9 0.04/m
    predestinarianism refers to the doctrine or movement holding all is divinely decided, while predestination is the general theological belief about fate.
  • predestiny
    9.9 0.01/m
    predestiny describes a state where events are fixed by fate or a higher power, while predestination is a formal theological doctrine about God's choice.
