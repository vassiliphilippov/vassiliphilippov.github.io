predestination

noun
doctrine that events are predetermined, esp. by God
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˌdɛstəˈneɪʃən /; / ˌprɛdˌɛstəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːˌdɛstɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the belief or doctrine that all events, especially what happens to people, are already decided by God or fate and cannot be changed

Examples

The predestination belief
Calvin's teachings on predestination became a central tenet of Reformed theology.
Some people find comfort in the idea of predestination, believing their lives follow a divine plan.
The debate over predestination has divided theologians for centuries.
He questioned whether his life was governed by chance or by predestination.
In theology class, we studied arguments for and against the doctrine of predestination.
Many religious traditions discuss the concept of predestination in relation to human actions and divine will.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fate - the development of events beyond a person's control, regarded as predetermined
  • antonym: free will - the ability to act at one's own discretion without being constrained by fate or divine will
