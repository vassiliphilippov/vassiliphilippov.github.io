dowdy

adjective
unfashionable; lacking style
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊdi / (AmE), / ˈdaʊdi / (BrE)
unfashionable and dull in appearance or dress; not stylish or attractive

Examples

A dowdy dress
Despite her talent, her dowdy appearance often went unnoticed.
She felt self-conscious in her dowdy dress at the elegant party.
The furniture looked dowdy after many years of neglect.
Despite her brilliance, many colleagues overlooked her due to her dowdy appearance.
Her dowdy clothes made her look much older than she actually was.
The living room was furnished in a dowdy style that hadn't changed in decades.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drab - lacking brightness or interest; drearily dull
  • synonym: frumpy - unfashionable and old-fashioned, especially in dress
  • synonym: shabby - in poor condition through long use or lack of care
  • antonym: stylish - fashionably elegant and sophisticated
