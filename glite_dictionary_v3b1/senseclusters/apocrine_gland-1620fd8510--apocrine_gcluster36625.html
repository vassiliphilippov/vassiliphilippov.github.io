apocrine gland

noun
type of sweat gland in hairy regions
7.8 5.9
Pronunciation: /ˈæp.ə.krɪn glænd/ /; / /ˈæp.ə.kraɪn glænd/ (AmE), /ə.ˈpɒ.krɪn glænd/ /; / /ˈæp.ə.krɪn glænd/ (BrE)
A type of large sweat gland found in certain hairy areas of the body that produces both sweat and secretions associated with scent.

Examples

The apocrine gland sweat
Excessive sweating in some people can be linked to overactive apocrine glands.
The apocrine gland is most active during puberty, especially in the armpits and groin.
Unlike the eccrine gland, the apocrine gland releases a thicker, milky fluid.
