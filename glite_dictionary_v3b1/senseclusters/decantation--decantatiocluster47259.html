decantation

noun
the process of separating liquid from sediment
Pronunciation: / dɪˌkænˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˌkænˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of slowly pouring a liquid from one container to another without disturbing any sediment that may have settled at the bottom

Examples

Liquid decantation process
Lab technicians often use decantation to separate liquids from precipitates.
Proper decantation is essential when dealing with delicate vintage wines.
The wine was poured by decantation to avoid disturbing the sediment at the bottom of the bottle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: filtration - the process of separating solids from liquids by passing through a filter
