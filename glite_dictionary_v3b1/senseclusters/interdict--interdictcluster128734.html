interdict

noun
court order forbidding an action
5.4 7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɪntərˌdɪkt / (AmE), / ˈɪntədɪkt / (BrE)
A legal order issued by a court that prohibits a person or group from performing a specific act.

Examples

Court interdict order
A temporary interdict was granted, halting construction until the hearing.
The company sought an interdict to prevent the publication of confidential documents.
Without an interdict, the protestors continued their demonstration outside the factory.

Related Terms

  • synonym: injunction - a court order requiring a party to do or refrain from doing something
