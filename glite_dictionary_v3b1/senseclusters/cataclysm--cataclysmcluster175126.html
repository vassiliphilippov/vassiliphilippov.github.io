cataclysm

noun
disastrous event; great upheaval
8.4 7.3
Pronunciation: /ˈkæt.ə.klɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈkæt.ə.klɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
a sudden and violent event that causes great destruction or change, especially in nature or society

Examples

Economic cataclysm
After the economic cataclysm, many businesses never reopened their doors.
Historians argue that the fall of the empire was a cultural cataclysm that changed Europe forever.
The sudden collapse of the dam proved to be a cataclysm for the valley residents.
A sudden cataclysm destroyed the city and changed the course of the river.
Scientists study ancient cataclysms to understand mass extinctions in Earth's history.
The volcanic eruption was a cataclysm that reshaped the entire island.

  • synonym: catastrophe - a sudden event causing great damage or suffering
  • synonym: disaster - an event that causes serious damage, destruction, or death
  • synonym: tragedy - a very sad event or situation, especially one involving death or suffering
