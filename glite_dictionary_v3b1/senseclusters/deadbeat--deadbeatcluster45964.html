deadbeat

noun
someone who does not pay debts
2.4 7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛdˌbit /; / ˈdɛdˌbiːt / (AmE), / ˈdɛdˌbiːt / (BrE)
a person who does not pay debts or avoids responsibilities, especially financial ones

Examples

A deadbeat debtor
Banks have strict policies to avoid lending money to deadbeats.
She accused him of being a deadbeat for never settling his credit card bills.
The landlord was frustrated with yet another deadbeat tenant who refused to pay rent.
The landlord was frustrated with his deadbeat tenant who never paid rent on time.
She refused to date a deadbeat who had no job or ambition.
Many people criticize deadbeat parents for not supporting their children financially.

Related Terms

  • antonym: paymaster - someone responsible for paying others or who regularly pays debts
  • antonym: responsible person - someone who fulfills their duties and obligations
  • synonym: shirker - someone who avoids doing their duty or work
  • synonym: slacker - a person who avoids work or effort
