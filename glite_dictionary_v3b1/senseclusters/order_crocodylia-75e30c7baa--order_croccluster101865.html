order Crocodylia

noun
taxonomic order of crocodilians
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrdər ˌkrɑkəˈdɪliə / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə ˌkrɒkəˈdɪliə / (BrE)
A taxonomic order of large, predatory, semi-aquatic reptiles that includes crocodiles, alligators, caimans, and gharials.

Examples

The order Crocodylia
Adaptations in the sensory systems of the order Crocodylia help these reptiles thrive in aquatic environments.
Fossil records indicate that the order Crocodylia has existed for over 80 million years.
Many scientists study the evolution of the order Crocodylia to understand the differences between crocodiles and alligators.
