ecumenically

adverb
promoting church unity
8.1
Pronunciation: /ɪ.kjuː.ˈmen.ɪ.kə.liː/ /; / /iː.kjuː.ˈmen.ɪ.kə.liː/ (AmE), /ɪ.kjuː.ˈmen.ɪ.kə.liː/ /; / /iː.kjuː.ˈmen.ɪ.kə.liː/ (BrE)
in a way that promotes unity, cooperation, or acceptance among different Christian churches or denominations

Examples

Ecumenically unite churches
The churches worked ecumenically to provide aid to the local community.
The event was planned ecumenically to include members from various Christian traditions.
Leaders from different denominations met ecumenically to discuss shared concerns.
The new policy was welcomed ecumenically by leaders from various Christian denominations.
The festival was celebrated ecumenically, bringing together churches from across the city.
The statement was endorsed ecumenically, showing unity among different Christian groups.
