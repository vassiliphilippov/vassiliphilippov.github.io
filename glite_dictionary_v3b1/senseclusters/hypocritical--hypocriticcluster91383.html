hypocritical

adjective
pretending to have moral standards
Pronunciation: / ˌhɪpəˈkrɪtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəˈkrɪtɪkəl / (BrE)
pretending to have moral standards or beliefs that one's own behavior does not conform to; insincere or deceitful

Examples

Hypocritical behavior
Her apology sounded hypocritical because she showed no real remorse.
It's hypocritical to criticize others for lying when you often lie yourself.
They considered his complaints hypocritical since he broke the same rules he condemned.

Related Terms

  • synonym: insincere - not expressing genuine feelings; hypocritical
  • antonym: sincere - honest and genuine in expression; not hypocritical
