realistic

adjective
sensible and practical; true to reality
Pronunciation: / ˌriəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌrɪəˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
accepting or representing things as they really are; having or showing a sensible and practical idea of what can be achieved or expected

Examples

Realistic expectations
It's important to set realistic goals that you can actually achieve.
She offered a realistic assessment of the project's timeline based on current resources.
The movie provides a realistic depiction of life during the war.

Related Terms

  • antonym: idealistic - characterized by idealism; unrealistically aiming for perfection
  • synonym: practical - sensible and realistic in approach to situations
