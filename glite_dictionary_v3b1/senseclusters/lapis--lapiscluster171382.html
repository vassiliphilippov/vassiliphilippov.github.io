lapis

noun
blue pigment from stone
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈlæpɪs / (AmE), / ˈlæpɪs / (BrE)
a deep blue pigment made from ground lapis lazuli, historically used in painting and art

Examples

Lapis pigment
The artist mixed lapis with oil to achieve a brilliant blue on the canvas.
During the Middle Ages, lapis was more valuable than gold for painters.
She admired the rich color of lapis in the ancient manuscript's illustrations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ochre - an earthy pigment ranging from yellow to deep orange or brown
  • synonym: ultramarine - a vivid blue pigment originally made from lapis lazuli
