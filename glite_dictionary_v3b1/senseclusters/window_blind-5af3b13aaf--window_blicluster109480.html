window blind

noun
covering for a window for privacy or light control
6.9 1.9
Pronunciation: /ˈwɪn.doʊ blaɪnd/ (AmE), /ˈwɪn.dəʊ blaɪnd/ (BrE)
A covering for a window, typically made of slats or fabric, used to block or filter light and provide privacy.

Examples

Pull down window blind
For privacy at night, they always pull down the window blind before turning on the lights.
She lowered the window blind to block out the bright afternoon sun.
The window blind in the conference room was broken and wouldn't roll up.

Related Terms
