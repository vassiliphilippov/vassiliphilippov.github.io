foot rot

noun
disease causing rot at a plant's base
6.2 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆfÊŠt ËŒrÉ‘t / (AmE), / ËˆfÊŠt ËŒrÉ’t / (BrE)
A disease in plants in which the base of the stem or trunk decays or rots, often caused by fungi or bacteria.

Examples

Foot rot in plants
Gardeners should check for signs of foot rot at the base of their plants after heavy rains.
Preventive treatments can reduce the risk of foot rot in susceptible crops.
The vineyard suffered significant losses due to foot rot affecting many young vines.
