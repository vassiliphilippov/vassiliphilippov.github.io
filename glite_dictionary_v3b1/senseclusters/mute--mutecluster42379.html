mute

noun
a person unable to speak
9.1 0.66/m
Pronunciation: / mjut / (AmE), / mjuÀêt / (BrE)
a person who is unable to speak, especially as a result of congenital deafness; now considered outdated and potentially offensive

Examples

Mute in society
Before the advent of sign language, a mute often found communication extremely challenging.
Old novels sometimes use the word mute to describe a character who cannot speak.
The teacher made special arrangements so that the mute student could participate in class discussions.

Synonyms

  • deaf-mute
    9.9 0.23/m
    deaf-mute is an old term for a person who cannot hear and speak, while mute names inability to speak and is now seen as offensive.
  • deaf-and-dumb
    9.9 0.01/m
    deaf-and-dumb is an offensive, dated phrase that links hearing loss with lack of speech, while mute describes inability to speak but sounds less insulting today.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deaf-mute - a person who is both deaf and unable to speak
