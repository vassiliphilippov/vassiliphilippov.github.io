Freudianism

noun
Freud's psychological theories
8.4
Pronunciation: / frɔɪˈdiːənɪzəm /; / ˈfrɔɪdiəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / frɔɪˈdiːənɪzəm /; / ˈfrɔɪdiənɪzəm / (BrE)
the psychological theories and methods developed by Sigmund Freud, especially focusing on the unconscious mind and interpreting behavior

Related Terms

  • antonym: behaviorism - a theory focusing on observable behaviors rather than the unconscious mind
  • synonym: psychoanalysis - a method of treating mental disorders by exploring unconscious desires and conflicts
