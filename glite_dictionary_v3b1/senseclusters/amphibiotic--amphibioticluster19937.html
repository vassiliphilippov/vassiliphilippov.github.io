amphibiotic

adjective
having aquatic larvae and terrestrial adults
4.7 9.1
Pronunciation: /æm.fɪ.ˈbaɪ.ɑː.tɪk/ /; / /əm.fɪ.ˈbaɪ.ɑː.tɪk/ (AmE), /æm.fɪ.ˈbaɪ.ɒ.tɪk/ /; / /əm.fɪ.ˈbaɪ.ɒ.tɪk/ (BrE)
(of an organism) having an aquatic early or larval stage and a terrestrial adult stage.

Examples

Amphibiotic insect life
Many insects are amphibiotic, spending their larval stage in water before becoming land-dwelling adults.
Researchers studied the amphibiotic characteristics of dragonflies in the wetland area.
The life cycle of frogs is a classic example of an amphibiotic organism.
