Plagianthus betulinus

noun
New Zealand tree producing 'New Zealand cotton' fiber
Pronunciation: / pleɪˈdʒiːænθəs bəˈtjuːlɪnəs / (AmE), / plaɪˈɡiːænθəs bɛtjʊˈliːnəs / (BrE)
A deciduous tree native to New Zealand, whose inner bark produces a strong fiber resembling flax, known as New Zealand cotton.

Examples

Plagianthus betulinus tree
Conservationists are working to protect Plagianthus betulinus in its natural habitats across New Zealand.
The inner bark of Plagianthus betulinus was traditionally used by Māori for making ropes and textiles.
The strong, flax-like fiber from Plagianthus betulinus is sometimes referred to as New Zealand cotton.
