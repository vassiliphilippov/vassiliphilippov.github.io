medulla oblongata

noun
part of brainstem controlling vital functions
Pronunciation: / məˌdʌlə ˌɑːblɒŋˈɡɑːtə / (AmE), / mɪˌdʌlə ɒˌblɒŋˈɡɑːtə / (BrE)
the lower part of the brainstem that connects to the spinal cord, controlling vital functions such as breathing, heart rate, and blood pressure

Examples

The medulla oblongata controls breathing
Damage to the medulla oblongata can interfere with breathing and heart function.
Medical students must learn about the medulla oblongata and its crucial roles.
The medulla oblongata acts as a relay between the brain and spinal cord.
