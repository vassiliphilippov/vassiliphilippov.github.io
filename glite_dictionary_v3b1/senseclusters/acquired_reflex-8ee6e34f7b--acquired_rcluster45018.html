acquired reflex

noun
a learned physiological response to a stimulus
3.4 5.3
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈkwaɪ.ərd ˈre.fl.eks/ /; / /ə.ˈkwaɪrd ˈre.fl.eks/ (AmE), /ə.ˈkwaɪ.əd ˈre.fl.eks/ /; / /ək.ˈwaɪ.əd ˈre.fl.eks/ (BrE)
A response learned through experience or conditioning, triggered by a specific stimulus rather than being innate.

Examples

An acquired reflex response
Blinking at the flash of a camera is not an acquired reflex, but salivating at the sound of a bell can be.
The sound of the bell became associated with food, creating an acquired reflex in the dog.
Through repeated training, the pilot developed an acquired reflex to respond instantly to emergency signals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conditioned reflex - a learned response to a stimulus, also known as conditioned response
  • antonym: innate reflex - a natural, unlearned response to a stimulus
