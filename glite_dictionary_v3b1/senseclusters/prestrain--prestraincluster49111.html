prestrain

verb
to apply strain in advance
8.9
Pronunciation: /priː.ˈstreɪn/ /; / /prɪ.ˈstreɪn/ (AmE), /priː.ˈstreɪn/ /; / /prɪ.ˈstreɪn/ (BrE)
to apply a strain to a material or component before it is put into use or subjected to its normal load

Examples

To prestrain a cable
Engineers must prestrain the steel rods to ensure the bridge remains stable under heavy loads.
Before installation, workers prestrained the cables to minimize future stretching.
It is important to prestrain certain materials to improve their performance in demanding conditions.
