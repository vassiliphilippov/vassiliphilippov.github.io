lactifuge

noun
agent that suppresses milk secretion
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈlæk.tɪˌfjudʒ / (AmE), / ˈlæk.tɪˌfjuːdʒ / (BrE)
A substance or agent that decreases or suppresses the secretion of milk, especially in women who are not breastfeeding.

Examples

A lactifuge substance
Certain herbal remedies act as a lactifuge for women experiencing unwanted lactation.
The doctor prescribed a lactifuge to help cease her milk production.
The nurse discussed the potential side effects of taking a lactifuge after childbirth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: galactagogue - an agent that increases milk secretion
