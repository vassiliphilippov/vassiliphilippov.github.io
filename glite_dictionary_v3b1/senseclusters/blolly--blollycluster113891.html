blolly

noun
evergreen climbing shrub of southern Florida and the West Indies
Pronunciation: / ˈblɑli / (AmE), / ˈblɒli / (BrE)
An evergreen climbing shrub native to southern Florida and the West Indies, known for its clusters of fragrant white to creamy flowers followed by round, white, succulent berries.

Examples

The blolly shrub
Hummingbirds were attracted to the fragrant blossoms of the blolly along the fence.
In the West Indies, farmers often plant blolly as a decorative hedge around their homes.
The garden's rarest plant was a blolly with its clusters of creamy white flowers.
