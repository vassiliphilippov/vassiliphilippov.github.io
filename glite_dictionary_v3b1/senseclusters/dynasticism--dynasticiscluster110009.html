dynasticism

noun
rule by one family
9.3
Pronunciation: /daɪ.ˈnæs.tɪ.sɪ.zəm/ /; / /dɪ.ˈnæs.tɪ.sɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /daɪ.ˈnæs.tɪ.sɪ.zəm/ /; / /dɪ.ˈnæs.tɪ.sɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the practice or system where the same family holds power or control over several generations

Examples

Dynasticism in families
The country's long history of dynasticism shaped its political structure.
Many ancient empires practiced dynasticism to maintain power within a single lineage.
Critics argue that dynasticism can limit opportunities for capable leaders outside the ruling family.
The country's political landscape was shaped by dynasticism, with power passing from parent to child.
Critics argued that dynasticism in the company prevented talented outsiders from rising to leadership roles.
Some believe that dynasticism in the arts limits opportunities for new voices and ideas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hereditary monarchy - a system where the throne is passed down within a family
  • antonym: meritocracy - a system where advancement is based on ability or achievement
  • synonym: nepotism - favoritism shown to relatives, especially in jobs
  • antonym: republicanism - the principle of government by elected representatives, not by a royal family
