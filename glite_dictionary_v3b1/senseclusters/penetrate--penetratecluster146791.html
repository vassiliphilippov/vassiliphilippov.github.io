penetrate

verb
come to understand deeply
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛn.əˌtreɪt /; / ˈpɛnəˌtreɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɛn.ɪˌtreɪt /; / ˈpɛnɪtreɪt / (BrE)
to understand or grasp the true nature or meaning of something; to be deeply affected by an idea or feeling

Examples

To penetrate the truth
Despite their efforts, they could not penetrate the real motives behind his actions.
Her keen intellect allows her to penetrate complex philosophical arguments quickly.
It took years for scientists to penetrate the mysteries of black holes.
A sudden realization penetrated his mind as he listened to the news.
He was so overwhelmed that grief penetrated every part of his being.
The teacher's words slowly penetrated her consciousness, and she began to understand the concept.

Related Terms

  • synonym: affect - to emotionally influence or move deeply
  • synonym: comprehend - to grasp mentally; to understand
  • synonym: fathom - to understand something deeply
  • synonym: pervade - to be present and apparent throughout
