Fast of the Firstborn

noun
Jewish minor fast day before Passover
Pronunciation: / fæst əv ðə ˈfɜrstˌbɔrn / (AmE), / fɑːst əv ðə ˈfɜːstˌbɔːn / (BrE)
(in Judaism) a minor fast day observed by firstborn males on Nissan 14, the day before Passover, commemorating the salvation of the Israelite firstborns in Egypt.

Examples

The Fast of the Firstborn
Many synagogues hold a special service to mark the Fast of the Firstborn.
Since he was a firstborn son, he participated in the Fast of the Firstborn each year.
The Fast of the Firstborn is traditionally observed on the day before Passover.
