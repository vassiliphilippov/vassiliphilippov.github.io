Al-Hasan ibn al-Haytham

proper noun
Arab mathematician and physicist, optics pioneer
Pronunciation: / æl ˈhɑːsən ɪbn æl ˈhaɪθəm / (AmE), / æl ˈhɑːsən ɪbn æl ˈhaɪθəm / (BrE)
(c. 965–1040) An Arab mathematician, physicist, and astronomer, known for his pioneering work in optics and the scientific method, whose research influenced science into the 17th century.

Examples

Al-Hasan ibn al-Haytham optics work
Al-Hasan ibn al-Haytham is renowned for his influential work on the principles of optics and vision.
Many historians consider Al-Hasan ibn al-Haytham the 'father of modern optics' due to his groundbreaking studies.
The scientific method as practiced today owes much to the approaches established by Al-Hasan ibn al-Haytham.
