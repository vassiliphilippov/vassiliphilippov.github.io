senility

noun
mental and physical decline with age
5.8
Pronunciation: / sɪˈnɪlɪti / (AmE), / sɪˈnɪlɪti / (BrE)
the condition of becoming mentally or physically weak in old age, especially with memory loss or confusion

Examples

Senility in old age
Her grandfather's senility progressed rapidly after he turned ninety.
Many associate senility with forgetfulness, but it can also include changes in personality.
The doctor explained that senility can cause confusion and memory loss in older adults.
His once sharp mind had been dulled by the onset of senility.
Medical professionals caution against confusing normal aging with senility.
The family worried that their grandmother’s forgetfulness might be an early sign of senility.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dementia - loss of cognitive function, especially in old age
  • synonym: senile - showing the weaknesses of old age, especially mental decline
  • antonym: youth - the period of being young and typically mentally and physically healthy
  • antonym: youthfulness - the quality or state of being young
