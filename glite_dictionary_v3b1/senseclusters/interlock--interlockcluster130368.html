interlock

noun
vehicle safety device preventing engine start
2.2 5.5
Pronunciation: /ɪn.tər.ˈlɑːk/ /; / /ɪn.tər.ˈlɔːk/ (AmE), /ɪn.tə.ˈlɒk/ (BrE)
a safety device or mechanism that prevents unsafe operation by making sure certain conditions are met

Examples

Ignition interlock
Authorities required him to install an interlock after his conviction for drunk driving.
Many modern cars are equipped with an interlock that requires the brake pedal to be pressed before starting.
The mechanic explained how the faulty interlock was preventing the engine from turning over.
The machine will not start unless the interlock is properly engaged.
A safety interlock prevents the oven door from opening while it is operating.
Technicians checked the interlock to ensure the equipment was safe to use.
