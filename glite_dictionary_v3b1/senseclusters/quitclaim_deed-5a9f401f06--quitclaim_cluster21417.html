quitclaim deed

noun
property transfer legal document
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkwɪtˌkleɪm did / (AmE), / ˈkwɪtˌkleɪm diːd / (BrE)
a legal document by which a person transfers their interest, title, or claim in a property to another without guaranteeing the validity of the title

Examples

A quitclaim deed transfer
A quitclaim deed is often used to clear up title disputes between family members.
She signed a quitclaim deed relinquishing any interest she had in the house after the divorce.
The seller provided a quitclaim deed to transfer ownership of the land without any title guarantees.

Related Terms

  • antonym: warranty deed - legal document transferring property with guarantees of title
