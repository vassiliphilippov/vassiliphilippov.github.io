modulate

verb
adjust or regulate (degree, amount, etc.)
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːdʒəˌleɪt /; / ˈmɑdʒ.ə.leɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɒdjʊleɪt /; / ˈmɒd.jʊ.leɪt / (BrE)
to adjust or change the pitch, tone, or volume of sound, especially your voice or music

Examples

To modulate your voice
It is important to modulate your voice when speaking to a large audience.
The technician modulated the temperature to maintain optimal conditions in the lab.
You can modulate the volume using the control on the remote.
Actors often modulate their speech to convey different emotions or characters.
She modulates her tone to sound more friendly and approachable during customer calls.
To keep his audience engaged, the speaker modulated his voice to emphasize key points.
He tried to modulate the volume so the music wouldn't be too loud.
Singers must modulate their tone for different styles of music.
The teacher skillfully modulated her voice to keep the students' attention.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adjust - to change something slightly to achieve the desired result
  • degree higher: intensify - to increase in degree or strength
  • antonym: monotone - speech delivered without inflection or variation in pitch
  • synonym: regulate - to control or maintain the rate or speed of a process
  • degree lower: soften - to make softer in sound or intensity
