doldrums

noun
state of inactivity or stagnation
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈdoʊl.drəmz / (AmE), / ˈdɒl.drəmz / (BrE)
a period or state of inactivity, stagnation, or slump, especially in business or artistic activity

Examples

In the doldrums at work
After the holidays, sales often fall into the doldrums until spring arrives.
The artist was stuck in the doldrums and unable to come up with new ideas.
The local economy remained in the doldrums for several years after the factory closed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: boom - a period of rapid economic growth or activity
  • synonym: stagnation - a state of not flowing or not changing, especially in economic growth
