ground-hugging

adjective
growing close to the ground
Pronunciation: / ˈɡraʊnd ˌhʌɡɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊnd ˌhʌɡɪŋ / (BrE)
(of plants) growing very close to the ground; low-growing.

Examples

Ground-hugging plants
Many succulents have a ground-hugging habit, spreading out rather than growing tall.
The garden was filled with ground-hugging plants that formed a dense green carpet.
This ground-hugging shrub is ideal for covering open spaces in a landscape.
