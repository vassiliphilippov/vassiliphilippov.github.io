chargee

noun
holder of security
Pronunciation: / tʃɑːrˈdʒiː / (AmE), / tʃɑːˈdʒiː / (BrE)
a person or organization to whom property or rights are given as security for a debt, especially in legal contexts

Examples

The chargee holds property
The chargee may take possession of the property if the loan is not repaid.
A chargee has certain rights under the terms of the security agreement.
If the debt remains unpaid, the chargee can enforce the charge through legal action.

Related Terms

  • antonym: chargor - a person who gives property as security for a debt
  • synonym: mortgagee - a person or institution to whom property is mortgaged
