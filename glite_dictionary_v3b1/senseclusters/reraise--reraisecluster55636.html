reraise

verb
to raise again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈreɪz / (AmE), / ˌriːˈreɪz / (BrE)
to increase a bet again after another player has already raised in a betting game, especially poker

Examples

To reraise a bet
After his opponent raised, John decided to reraise to put more pressure on the table.
She chose to reraise rather than just call, hoping to win a bigger pot.
If you reraise too often, other players may catch on to your strategy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fold - to withdraw from a betting round and forfeit the chance to win
  • degree lower: raise - to increase the amount of a bet in a game
