adjoin

verb
be next to or in contact with
6.7 4.2
Pronunciation: / əˈdʒɔɪn / (AmE), / əˈdʒɔɪn / (BrE)
to be next to, in direct contact with, or share a boundary with something; to border

Examples

Adjoin the main office
The conference room adjoins the main office, making it easy to organize meetings.
The two countries adjoin each other along a peaceful border.
Their house adjoins the local park, so it's only a short walk to the playground.
The conference rooms adjoin each other on the second floor of the building.
The kitchen adjoins the dining area, making it easy to serve meals.
Their property adjoins a large national park, making it very desirable for nature lovers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abut - to be adjacent or border on; to adjoin
  • synonym: neighbor - to be situated next to or near (of places or buildings)
  • antonym: separate - to set or keep apart; to not be adjoining
