k-meson

noun
an unstable subatomic particle (kaon)
9.9
Pronunciation: /keɪ ˈmiː.zən/ /; / /keɪ ˈme.zən/ (AmE), /keɪ ˈmiː.zɒn/ /; / /keɪ ˈme.zɒn/ (BrE)
A type of unstable meson, also known as a kaon, produced during high-energy particle collisions and decaying rapidly into other particles.

Examples

A k-meson particle
Many experiments at the accelerator focus on producing and detecting k-mesons.
Physicists observed the decay of a k-meson in the laboratory after a particle collision.
The discovery of the k-meson provided new insights into the behavior of subatomic particles.
