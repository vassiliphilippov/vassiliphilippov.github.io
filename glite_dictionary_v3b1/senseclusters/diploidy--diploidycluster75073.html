diploidy

noun
the state of having two chromosome sets
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪp.lɔɪ.di / (AmE), / ˈdɪp.lɔɪ.di / (BrE)
the condition in which a cell or organism has two complete sets of chromosomes, one from each parent

Examples

Normal diploidy condition
Errors during cell division can sometimes disrupt normal diploidy, leading to genetic disorders.
In many plants, diploidy allows for greater genetic variation compared to haploid organisms.
The vast majority of human cells exhibit diploidy as they contain pairs of homologous chromosomes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: haploidy - the condition of having a single set of chromosomes
  • degree higher: polyploidy - the condition of having more than two complete sets of chromosomes
