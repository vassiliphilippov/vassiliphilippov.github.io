peel

verb
remove skin or outer layer
1.0 16/m
Pronunciation: / pil / (AmE), / piÀêl / (BrE)
to remove the outer covering or skin from a fruit, vegetable, or other object

Examples

To peel a banana
Can you peel the potatoes for dinner?
He was peeling the apple with a small knife.
She peeled the orange and offered me a slice.

Video Examples

Synonyms

  • peel off
    3.4 1/m
    peel off is used when you pull away an outer layer in strips, while peel is the general action of removing a skin.
  • pare
    4.8 0.5/m
    pare means cutting away a thin outer layer with a knife, while peel can mean taking off a skin by hand or knife.
  • skin
    2.7 0.3/m
    skin often means removing an animal's or fruit's skin completely, while peel covers removing an outer covering from many objects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pare - to trim (something) by cutting away its outer edges or skin
