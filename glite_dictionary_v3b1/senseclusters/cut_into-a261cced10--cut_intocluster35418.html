cut into

verb
turn or loosen earth (gardening/agriculture)
3.2 3.7
Pronunciation: /kʌt ˈɪn.tuː/ /; / /kʌt ˈɪn.tə/ /; / /kʌt ɪn.ˈtuː/ (AmE), /kʌt ˈɪn.tuː/ /; / /kʌt ˈɪn.tə/ /; / /kʌt ɪn.ˈtuː/ (BrE)
to turn up, loosen, or remove earth, especially with a tool such as a spade or plow

Examples

To cut into the soil
Before planting the seeds, gardeners need to cut into the soil to improve its aeration.
She grabbed her spade and started cutting into the garden bed to loosen the dirt.
The tractor cut into the field, preparing the earth for the new crop.
