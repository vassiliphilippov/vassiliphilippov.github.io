auteurism

noun
director-centered film theory
8.9
Pronunciation: / oʊˈtɜːrɪzəm / (AmE), / əʊˈtɜːrɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief or practice that a film reflects the director's personal creative vision and style

Examples

Auteurism in film
Film students often debate the merits of auteurism versus more collaborative approaches to filmmaking.
The director's unique style is a key example of auteurism in modern cinema.
Many critics discuss auteurism when analyzing classic films by famous directors.
The director's strong sense of auteurism is evident in the unique themes of all his movies.
Film critics often debate whether auteurism limits or enhances the collaborative nature of cinema.
Her approach to auteurism set her films apart from mainstream Hollywood productions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: auteur - a film director with a distinctive personal style
  • synonym: auteur theory - the idea that the director is the main creative force in a film
  • antonym: collaborative filmmaking - the process where many people contribute equally to a film's creation
