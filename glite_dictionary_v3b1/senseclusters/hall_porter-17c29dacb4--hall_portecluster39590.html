hall porter

noun
person attending entrance to a building
Pronunciation: / hɔl ˈpɔrtɚ / (AmE), / hɔːl ˈpɔːtə / (BrE)
a person employed to attend the entrance of a building and assist visitors, especially in hotels or university halls

Examples

Hall porter at hotel
As a hall porter, his duties included answering questions and monitoring visitors coming in and out.
At the old university, the hall porter kept a careful log of all room keys distributed to students.
The hall porter greeted every guest with a polite nod and directed them to the reception desk.

Related Terms

  • synonym: concierge - a person in charge of the entrance or reception area, especially in a hotel
  • synonym: doorman - a person stationed at the door of a building to control the entrance
