hall porter

noun
person attending entrance to a building
6.5 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / h…îl Ààp…îrt…ö / (AmE), / h…îÀêl Ààp…îÀêt…ô / (BrE)
a person employed to attend the entrance of a building and assist visitors, especially in hotels or university halls

Examples

Hall porter at hotel
As a hall porter, his duties included answering questions and monitoring visitors coming in and out.
At the old university, the hall porter kept a careful log of all room keys distributed to students.
The hall porter greeted every guest with a polite nod and directed them to the reception desk.

Synonyms

    4.3 0.69/m
    porter greets and admits guests at an entrance, while a hall porter also helps with luggage and serves visitors in hotels or halls.
  • doorman
    1.2 2.93/m
    doorman stands at a building entrance to control access and open doors, while a hall porter focuses on greeting guests and helping them.
  • commissionaire
    8.2 0.02/m

  • doorkeeper
    2.8 0.09/m
    doorkeeper guards and controls who may enter a place, whereas a hall porter greets guests and helps them, often in hotels.
