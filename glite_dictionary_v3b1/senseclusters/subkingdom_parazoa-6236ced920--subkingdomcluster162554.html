subkingdom Parazoa

noun
taxonomic group for simple multicellular animals (sponges)
9.9
Pronunciation: / sʌbˌkɪŋdəm ˌpærəˈzoʊə / (AmE), / sʌbˌkɪŋdəm ˌpærəˈzəʊə / (BrE)
A group of multicellular organisms with less specialized cells than in the Metazoa, comprising the phylum Porifera (sponges).

Examples

Subkingdom Parazoa sponges
Sponges are classified within the subkingdom Parazoa due to their simple cellular structure.
The subkingdom Parazoa includes organisms that lack true tissues unlike animals in the Metazoa.
Zoologists often compare the cellular organization of subkingdom Parazoa with that of more complex animals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Metazoa - multicellular animals with specialized tissues
  • synonym: Porifera - the phylum of sponges, the only members of Parazoa
