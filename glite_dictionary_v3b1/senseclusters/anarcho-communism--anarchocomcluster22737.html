anarcho-communism

noun
anarchist form of communism
6.9
Pronunciation: / ænˌɑrkoʊˈkɑmjəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ænˌɑːkəʊˈkɒmjʊˌnɪzəm / (BrE)
A political theory that combines anarchism and communism, advocating the abolition of the state, capitalism, wages, and private property, and supporting common ownership, direct democracy, and voluntary associations.

Examples

Anarcho-communism political theory
He wrote extensively about anarcho-communism in his political treatises.
Many activists were inspired by anarcho-communism during the early twentieth century.
The meeting focused on the principles of anarcho-communism and how they could be applied locally.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anarchism - a political philosophy advocating stateless societies
  • synonym: communism - a political theory advocating communal ownership and absence of class
