downturn

noun
decline in business or economic activity
4.4 7/m
Pronunciation: / Ààda änÀåt…ùn / (AmE), / Ààda änÀåt…úÀên / (BrE)
a period when business or economic activity becomes worse or decreases

Examples

Economic downturn
After years of growth, the housing market faced a sudden downturn.
Analysts predict that the downturn will be short-lived if consumer confidence returns.
The company struggled to survive during the recent downturn in the economy.

Synonyms

  • downswing
    6.1 0.09/m
    downswing describes a period when business activity falls as part of a cycle, while downturn names a general economic decline.
  • slump
    4.6 5/m
    slump is a sudden or deep fall in economic activity that hurts jobs and sales, while downturn is a general economic decline.
  • downtrend
    3.9 0.2/m
    downtrend points to a steady fall in prices or values over time, while downturn means a wider economic slowdown.
  • downslide
    5.1 0.04/m
    downslide describes a gradual worsening or slipping situation in casual use, while downturn is the standard term for economic decline.
