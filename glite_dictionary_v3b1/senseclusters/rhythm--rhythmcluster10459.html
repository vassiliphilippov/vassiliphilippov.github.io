rhythm

noun
the basic rhythmic unit in music
9.4 2.8
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪð.əm /; / ˈrɪðəm / (AmE), / ˈrɪð.əm /; / ˈrɪðəm / (BrE)
a regular, repeated pattern of sounds, movements, events, or visual elements, especially in music, dance, or design

Examples

Catch the rhythm
Classical compositions often feature a complex rhythm that guides the performers.
The dancers matched their steps perfectly to the rhythm of the music.
The drummer kept a steady rhythm throughout the entire song.
Farmers adjust their work to the rhythm of the seasons.
His daily routine followed a steady rhythm that brought comfort and stability.
The rhythm of the tides shapes life along the coast.
Architects often use repeated motifs to establish rhythm in a building's exterior.
She admired the rhythm in the arrangement of windows across the structure.
The rhythm of the columns along the façade creates a pleasing sense of order.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cycle - a series of events that are regularly repeated in the same order
  • synonym: meter - the rhythmic structure of a verse or musical piece
