fraktur

noun
folk-art manuscript
9.9
Pronunciation: / frækˈtʊr / (AmE), / frækˈtʊə / (BrE)
a decorated manuscript or document, often made by Pennsylvania Germans, featuring calligraphy and colorful folk-art designs

Examples

A fraktur document
The museum displayed a beautiful fraktur created by a Pennsylvania German artist in the 1800s.
Her family treasures an old fraktur that records the birth of her great-great-grandfather.
Collectors often seek out fraktur for their unique calligraphy and vibrant folk-art motifs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: certificate - an official document attesting a fact, such as birth or baptism
  • synonym: illuminated manuscript - a handwritten document decorated with gold, silver, or colorful designs
