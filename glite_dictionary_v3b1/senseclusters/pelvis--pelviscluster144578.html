pelvis

noun
bony structure supporting lower limbs
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛlvɪs / (AmE), / ˈpɛlvɪs / (BrE)
the large bony structure near the base of the spine to which the lower limbs are attached in humans and corresponding structures in other vertebrates

Examples

Human pelvis bones
During the anatomy lesson, we examined a model of the human pelvis.
Pain in the pelvis can sometimes indicate a serious medical condition.
The doctor explained that her pelvis had been fractured in the accident.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hip bone - the large bone forming the main part of the pelvis
