biparous

adjective
producing two offspring at once
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪˌpær.əs / (AmE), / ˈbʌɪ.pə.rəs / (BrE)
(of an animal) producing two offspring at a time.

Examples

Biparous animals
Certain rodent species are biparous, usually giving birth to two young per pregnancy.
Research on biparous animals has provided valuable insights into reproductive strategies.
The veterinarian confirmed that the rabbit was biparous after observing the delivery of twin kits.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: multiparous - producing more than one offspring at a time
  • antonym: uniparous - producing one offspring at a time
