focalizer

noun
perspective setter
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈfoʊkəˌlaɪzər / (AmE), / ˈfəʊkəlaɪzə / (BrE)
a person or thing that determines the perspective or point of view in a narrative or system

Examples

Story focalizer
In the novel, the main focalizer is the young protagonist.
The author uses a different focalizer in each chapter to show various perspectives.
A shift in the focalizer can change how readers interpret the events of the story.

Related Terms

  • synonym: character - a person in a story, sometimes serving as the viewpoint
  • synonym: narrator - the person telling the story in a narrative
