elocutionary

adjective
overly ornate style of speaking
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɛləˈkjuːʃəˌnɛri / (AmE), / ˌɛl.əˈkjuː.ʃən.ər.i / (BrE)
(of a style of speaking) excessively formal, ornate, or embellished in delivery.

Examples

Elocutionary style criticism
Critics described his presentation as elocutionary, lacking natural warmth.
Her elocutionary manner distracted from the content of her speech.
The actor delivered the monologue in a highly elocutionary fashion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rhetorical - relating to speech that is elaborate or persuasive
