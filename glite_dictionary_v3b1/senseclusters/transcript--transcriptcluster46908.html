transcript

noun
written record of spoken words
Pronunciation: / ˈtrænˌskrɪpt / (AmE), / ˈtrænˌskrɪpt / (BrE)
an official written or typed copy of words, such as a record of speech, conversation, academic achievements, or legal proceedings

Examples

An official transcript
After the meeting, the assistant prepared a transcript for the official records.
She requested a transcript of the interview for her research.
The court provided a transcript of the entire trial to the defense attorney.
After the trial, the lawyer reviewed the transcript of the witness's testimony.
She submitted her high school transcript with her college application.
The university required an official transcript from all previous schools attended.

Related Terms

  • synonym: transcription - the process of making a written copy of speech or data
