Sir Matthew Flinders

proper noun
British explorer who mapped Australia
7.7
Pronunciation: /sɜːr ˈmæθ.juː ˈflɪn.dərz/ (AmE), /sɜː ˈmæθ.juː ˈflɪn.dəz/ (BrE)
(1774–1814) a British explorer and navigator who mapped much of the Australian coastline and proved that Australia was a continent.

Examples

Sir Matthew Flinders explorer
Sir Matthew Flinders is credited with being the first to circumnavigate and map the coastline of Australia.
Many historians regard Sir Matthew Flinders as one of the most important explorers of the early 19th century.
The achievements of Sir Matthew Flinders are commemorated in place names across Australia.
