limewater

noun
solution of calcium hydroxide in water
4.3 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈlaɪm.wɔː.tər/ /; / /ˈlaɪm.wɑː.tər/ (AmE), /ˈlaɪm.wɔː.tə/ (BrE)
a clear, colorless solution of calcium hydroxide in water, used in chemical tests and sometimes as an antacid

Examples

Clear limewater solution
Chemists may use limewater during experiments to indicate the presence of acids or gases.
Some patients take limewater as a mild antacid to relieve heartburn.
To test for carbon dioxide, bubble the gas through limewater and observe if it turns cloudy.
