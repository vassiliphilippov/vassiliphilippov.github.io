stumpage

noun
timber harvesting fee
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈstʌmpɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈstʌmpɪdʒ / (BrE)
the price paid for the right to harvest timber from a specific area of land

Examples

Stumpage price
The government increased the stumpage rates to encourage sustainable forestry practices.
Logging companies must pay stumpage before they can cut down any trees on public land.
Rising stumpage costs have affected the profitability of the timber industry this year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lease - a contract granting use of property for a period
  • synonym: royalty - a payment made to use natural resources
