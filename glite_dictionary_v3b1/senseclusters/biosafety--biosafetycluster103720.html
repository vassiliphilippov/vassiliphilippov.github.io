biosafety

noun
measures against exposure to biological hazards
2.8 5.3
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪ.oʊˈseɪf.ti / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.əʊˈseɪf.ti / (BrE)
measures and practices intended to prevent exposure to harmful biological agents, especially in laboratory or healthcare settings.

Examples

Strict biosafety protocols
Strict biosafety protocols are essential when working with infectious diseases in the laboratory.
The hospital updated its biosafety guidelines after the outbreak of a new virus.
Training in biosafety practices is required for all research staff handling hazardous microorganisms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biosecurity - measures to prevent the spread or introduction of harmful organisms
