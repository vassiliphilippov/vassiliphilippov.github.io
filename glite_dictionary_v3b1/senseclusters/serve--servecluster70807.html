serve

verb
perform duties in the military
3.2 1.5
Pronunciation: / sɝv / (AmE), / sɜːv / (BrE)
to work for, act as a servant, perform duties, hold a position, serve in the military, or devote oneself to someone or something

Examples

To serve in the army
After college, she decided to serve in the army for five years.
He proudly told stories about his time serving overseas.
Many young men were required to serve during the war.
As a volunteer, she was happy to serve the elderly residents at the care home.
For many years, he served the royal family as a trusted butler.
They hired a new assistant to serve guests at the front desk.
After the election, she served on the city council for eight years.
He served as committee chair during a critical time for the organization.
Several veterans have served in local government positions after their military careers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attend - to wait on or serve someone, attending to their needs
  • antonym: betray - to be disloyal to a person, country, or group
  • antonym: demobilize - to be released from military service
  • antonym: employ - to make use of someone in one's service; opposite in role to 'serve'
  • synonym: enlist - to join the armed forces
  • antonym: retire - to leave one's job or office permanently, especially on reaching retirement age
  • synonym: wait on - to serve someone, especially as a waiter in a restaurant
  • synonym: work - to be engaged in physical or mental activity as a job
  • synonym: work for - to be employed by or act on behalf of someone or something
