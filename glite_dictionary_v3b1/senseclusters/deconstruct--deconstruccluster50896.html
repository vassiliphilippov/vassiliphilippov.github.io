deconstruct

verb
analyze a text using deconstruction
5.1 5.7
Pronunciation: /diː.kən.ˈstrʌkt/ /; / /də.kən.ˈstrʌkt/ (AmE), /diː.kən.ˈstrʌkt/ /; / /diː.kən.ˈstrʊkt/ (BrE)
to analyze or break down a text, artwork, or cultural work to reveal its underlying ideas, structure, or assumptions

Examples

To deconstruct a novel
Literary theorists often deconstruct novels to reveal hidden assumptions in the narrative.
She spent years deconstructing the cultural biases present in traditional fairy tales.
The painting was deconstructed by critics to expose its political symbolism.
Many critics attempt to deconstruct popular songs to uncover hidden messages within the lyrics.
She enjoys deconstructing movies to understand how their plots are structured.
The professor encouraged us to deconstruct the novel's themes during class discussion.
