nothingness

noun
state of nonexistence; absence of anything
Pronunciation: / ˈnʌθɪŋ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈnʌθɪŋ.nɪs / (BrE)
the state or condition of being nothing; complete nonexistence or absence of anything.

Examples

Complete nothingness
He stared into the dark void, feeling an overwhelming sense of nothingness.
Some philosophers debate whether nothingness can truly exist in the universe.
The novel explores the existential dread that arises from confronting nothingness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: being - the state or fact of existing
  • synonym: emptiness - the state of containing nothing; void
