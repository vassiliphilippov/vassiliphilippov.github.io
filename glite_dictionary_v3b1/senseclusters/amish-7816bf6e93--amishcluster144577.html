Amish

noun
member of a traditionalist Christian group
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɑː.mɪʃ, ˈæ.mɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈɑː.mɪʃ, ˈæ.mɪʃ / (BrE)
A member of a traditionalist Christian group, primarily living in North America, known for simple living, plain dress, and reluctance to adopt many conveniences of modern technology.

Examples

The Amish community
Many tourists visit Pennsylvania to learn about the Amish way of life.
The Amish are known for traveling in horse-drawn buggies rather than using cars.
The Amish community values simplicity and strong family ties.

Related Terms
