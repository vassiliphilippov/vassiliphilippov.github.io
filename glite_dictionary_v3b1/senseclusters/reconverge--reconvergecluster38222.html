reconverge

verb
to become stable again
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkənˈvɜrdʒ / (AmE), / ˌriːkənˈvɜːdʒ / (BrE)
to begin to approach a stable state or solution again after having diverged.

Examples

To reconverge on a solution
After the disturbance, the algorithm will reconverge to the correct solution within a few iterations.
The two paths may separate briefly but eventually reconverge at the main road.
If the process is stable, it should reconverge even after unexpected changes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: converge - to come together or approach a common point
  • antonym: diverge - to move or extend in different directions from a common point
