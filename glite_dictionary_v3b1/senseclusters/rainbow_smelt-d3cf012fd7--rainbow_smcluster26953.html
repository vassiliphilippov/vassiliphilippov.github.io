rainbow smelt

noun
a small North American fish species
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪnˌboʊ smɛlt / (AmE), / ˈreɪnbəʊ smɛlt / (BrE)
a small silvery North American fish (Osmerus mordax) found in fresh and coastal waters, often eaten as food

Examples

The rainbow smelt fish
During the spring, anglers catch many rainbow smelt as they migrate upstream to spawn.
Fried rainbow smelt is a traditional dish in some northern communities.
The rainbow smelt has a distinctive silvery body with hints of iridescent colors.
Grilled rainbow smelt is a seasonal delicacy served in some northeastern U.S. restaurants.
Many anglers eagerly anticipate the spring run of rainbow smelt in the local rivers.
The rainbow smelt population has declined in some areas due to overfishing and habitat loss.
