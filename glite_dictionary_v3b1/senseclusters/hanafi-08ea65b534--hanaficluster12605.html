Hanafi

noun
Sunni legal school
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑːnəfi / (AmE), / ˈhænəfi / (BrE)
the Sunni school of Islamic law founded by Imam Abu Hanifa, known for its distinctive legal interpretations and traditions

Examples

Hanafi school teachings
Many Muslims in South Asia follow the Hanafi school of Islamic law.
The Hanafi tradition emphasizes reason and analogy in legal rulings.
He studied both Hanafi and Shafi'i jurisprudence at the Islamic university.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Hanbali - a Sunni school of Islamic law based on the teachings of Ahmad ibn Hanbal
  • synonym: Maliki - a Sunni school of Islamic law based on the teachings of Malik ibn Anas
  • synonym: Shafi'i - another major Sunni school of Islamic law
