conceitedness

noun
excessive pride in oneself
Pronunciation: / kənˈsiː.tɪd.nəs / (AmE), / kənˈsiː.tɪd.nɪs / (BrE)
The quality of being excessively proud of oneself or showing an overly high opinion of one's abilities or worth.

Examples

His conceitedness showed
His conceitedness made it hard for him to listen to anyone else's ideas.
People were put off by her conceitedness and constant bragging.
Success never altered his character, and he was free of conceitedness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: humility - the quality of having a modest or low view of one's importance
  • synonym: vanity - excessive pride in or admiration of one's own appearance or achievements
