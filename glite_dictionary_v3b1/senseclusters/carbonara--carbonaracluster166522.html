carbonara

noun
Italian pasta sauce with eggs, bacon, and cheese
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːrbəˈnɑːrə / (AmE), / ˌkɑː.bəˈnɑː.rə / (BrE)
an Italian pasta sauce made with eggs, bacon or ham, and grated cheese, typically served with spaghetti or other pasta

Examples

Spaghetti carbonara
He ordered the spaghetti carbonara at the Italian restaurant.
I learned how to make a creamy carbonara from my grandmother.
The rich flavor of carbonara comes from the combination of eggs, cheese, and bacon.
