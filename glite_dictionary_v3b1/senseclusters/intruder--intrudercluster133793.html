intruder

noun
person who enters without permission
3.9 8.81/m
Pronunciation: / ÉªnËˆtruËdÉ™r / (AmE), / ÉªnËˆtruËdÉ™ / (BrE)
A person who enters a place without permission, especially to commit a crime or disturb others.

Examples

The intruder entered
Security cameras caught the intruder lurking around the property late at night.
The alarm was triggered when an intruder entered the house through the back door.
The family was frightened by the sight of an intruder in their living room.

Synonyms

  • trespasser
    4.9 0.86/m
    a trespasser enters someone's land or property without permission, while an intruder often means someone entering to threaten or commit a crime.
  • infiltrator
    8.2 0.56/m
    an infiltrator secretly enters a place or group to gather information or do harm, while an intruder is any unwelcome person entering without permission.
  • interloper
    8.9 1.05/m
    an interloper becomes involved where they are not wanted socially, while an intruder specifically enters without permission to disturb or threaten others.

Related Terms
