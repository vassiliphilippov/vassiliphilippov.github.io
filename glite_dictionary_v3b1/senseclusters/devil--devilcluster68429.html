devil

noun
chief spirit of evil; tempter in religions
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈdɛvəl /; / ˈdɛv.əl / (BrE)
the chief evil spirit or a supernatural being representing evil, especially in religion; also used as a symbol of evil or temptation

Examples

The devil in Hell
In art and literature, the devil is often depicted as a horned, menacing figure presiding over Hell.
Many religious texts describe the devil as the embodiment of evil and the tempter of humanity.
Some believe that the devil tries to lead people away from the path of righteousness.
In the play, the hero faces a battle with the devil himself.
Many cultures have stories about the devil tempting humans to do wrong.
The preacher warned the congregation about the dangers of the devil.
He fought against the devil of addiction every day.
In many stories, the devil offers tempting deals that lead to trouble.
Sometimes your inner devil tries to convince you to take the easy way out.

  • synonym: demon - an evil spirit or supernatural being
  • synonym: satan - the chief evil spirit; the Devil in Christianity and Islam
