A small, waxy, white or pinkish-white woodland plant (Monotropa uniflora) that lacks chlorophyll, has scalelike leaves and a nodding flower, and turns black with age.

Examples

The Indian pipe plant
As summer progresses, the once white Indian pipe gradually turns black.
Because Indian pipe lacks chlorophyll, it does not rely on sunlight for photosynthesis.
We found a cluster of Indian pipe growing in the shady woods near the creek.
