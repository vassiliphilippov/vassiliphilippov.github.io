family Solanaceae

noun
the nightshade plant family
Pronunciation: / səˌleɪnəˈsiː.iː / (AmE), / səˌleɪnəˈsiː.iː / (BrE)
A large and economically important family of flowering plants, including herbs, shrubs, or trees, often with strong scents and sometimes containing narcotic or poisonous compounds; includes important genera such as Solanum (potatoes, tomatoes), Capsicum (peppers), Nicotiana (tobacco), and Atropa (belladonna).

Examples

The family Solanaceae plants
Many staple crops, such as potatoes and tomatoes, belong to the family Solanaceae.
Researchers study the family Solanaceae for its unique alkaloid compounds, some of which are highly toxic.
The ornamental petunia is a popular garden plant from the family Solanaceae and is prized for its colorful flowers.
