caparison

noun
decorated covering for a horse
9.6
Pronunciation: / kəˈpærɪsən / (AmE), / kəˈpærɪsən / (BrE)
a decorated cloth covering, often richly ornamented, placed over a horse, especially in ceremonial or historical contexts

Examples

A horse's caparison
During the parade, each stallion was adorned with a brightly colored caparison.
Museum exhibits often display the caparison alongside medieval armor and weapons.
The knight's horse wore an elaborate caparison embroidered with the family's crest.
