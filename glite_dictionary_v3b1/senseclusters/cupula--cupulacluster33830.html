cupula

noun
inner ear structure
Pronunciation: / ˈkjuːpjələ / (AmE), / ˈkjuːpjʊlə / (BrE)
a small, dome-shaped structure in the body, such as in the inner ear or at the top of the lung

Examples

Inner ear cupula
The doctor explained that the cupula plays a key role in our sense of balance.
Damage to the cupula can cause dizziness and problems with movement.
Researchers studied how the cupula responds to changes in head position.
The X-ray revealed a shadow near the cupula at the top of the lung.
Inflammation of the cupula can sometimes cause pain in the lower neck.
The surgeon carefully examined the cupula for any signs of abnormal growth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ampulla - the widened part of a semicircular canal in the inner ear containing the cupula
  • synonym: crista ampullaris - the sensory organ in the ampulla of the semicircular canal, associated with the cupula
  • synonym: pleura - the membrane surrounding the lungs and lining the chest cavity
