slops

noun
cheap clothing supplied to sailors
6.9
Pronunciation: / slɑps / (AmE), / slɒps / (BrE)
Cheap, ready-made clothing, especially as formerly supplied to sailors in Britain.

Examples

Sailors' slops
In the 18th century, sailors often wore slops because they were affordable and easy to replace.
Records show that many seamen purchased slops from the ship’s store before setting sail.
The naval quartermaster distributed slops to the new recruits as part of their uniform.
