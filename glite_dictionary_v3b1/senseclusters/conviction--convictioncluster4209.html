conviction

noun
firmly held belief
Pronunciation: / kənˈvɪkʃən / (AmE), / kənˈvɪkʃən / (BrE)
a firmly held belief or opinion that does not require proof or evidence

Examples

Strong conviction
Despite the lack of evidence, his conviction never wavered.
Her conviction in the importance of honesty inspired the whole team.
She spoke with such conviction that everyone in the room was convinced by her argument.

Related Terms

  • synonym: certainty - the quality of being reliably true or strongly believed
  • antonym: doubt - a feeling of uncertainty or lack of conviction
