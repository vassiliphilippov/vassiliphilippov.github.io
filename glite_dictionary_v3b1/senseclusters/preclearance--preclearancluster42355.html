preclearance

noun
advance official permission
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈklɪrəns / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈklɪərəns / (BrE)
advance official permission or approval required from an authority before an action, law, or policy can take effect

Examples

Government preclearance required
All travelers must obtain preclearance before entering the restricted area of the airport.
The company requires preclearance for any financial transactions above a certain amount.
Without preclearance from the supervisor, employees are not allowed to speak to the media.
The new law cannot be implemented without preclearance from the federal government.
Election officials submitted their proposed changes for preclearance before proceeding.
The company needed preclearance to export sensitive technology to another country.

Related Terms

  • synonym: approval - agreement or permission to do something
  • synonym: authorization - official permission or approval for something
  • antonym: prohibition - the act of forbidding something
  • antonym: rejection - the act of refusing to accept or approve something
