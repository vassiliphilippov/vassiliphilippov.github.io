subclimax

noun
intermediate ecological stage
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌklaɪmæks / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌklaɪmæks / (BrE)
a stable stage in ecological succession that is maintained for a long period but is not the final climax community

Examples

Ecological subclimax
Frequent wildfires kept the grassland in a subclimax state for many years.
Grazing animals can cause an area to remain at the subclimax stage instead of reaching a forest.
The ecologist explained how human activity can maintain a subclimax community indefinitely.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: climax - the final, stable community in ecological succession
  • synonym: succession - the process of change in the species structure of an ecological community over time
