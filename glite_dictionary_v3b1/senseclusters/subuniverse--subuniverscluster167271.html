subuniverse

noun
subset closed under operations
9.9
Pronunciation: /səb.ˈjuː.nə.vɜːr/ /; / /sʌb.ˈjuː.nɪ.vɜːr/ (AmE), /səb.ˈjuː.nə.vɜː/ /; / /sʌb.ˈjuː.nɪ.vɜː/ (BrE)
in mathematics, a nonempty subset of an algebra's domain that is closed under all the algebra's operations

Examples

A mathematical subuniverse
In universal algebra, the set of even numbers is a subuniverse of the integers under addition.
A subuniverse must be nonempty and closed under all the operations of the algebra.
Researchers identified a subuniverse within the model that preserved the required properties.

Related Terms

  • synonym: subalgebra - an algebraic structure formed from a subset closed under the operations of the original algebra
  • antonym: universe - the entire set or domain under consideration in mathematics
