irrevocably

adverb
in a way that cannot be changed
Pronunciation: / ɪˈrɛvəkəbli / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛvəkəbli / (BrE)
In a way that cannot be changed, reversed, or undone.

Examples

To irrevocably change
His decision to resign was irrevocably final, with no possibility for reconsideration.
She irrevocably changed the course of her life by moving to another country.
The contract was irrevocably signed, leaving both parties bound to its terms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: permanently - in a way that lasts forever and cannot be changed
  • antonym: reversibly - in a manner that can be reversed or undone
