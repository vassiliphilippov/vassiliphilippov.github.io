totaled

adjective
damaged beyond repair (especially vehicles)
7.7 4.4
Pronunciation: /ˈtoʊ.təld/ (AmE), /ˈtəʊ.təld/ (BrE)
(of an automobile) completely destroyed or damaged beyond repair, especially so that the cost of repairs exceeds the vehicle's value.

Examples

A totaled car
After the accident, the mechanic told me my car was totaled and not worth fixing.
He had to buy a new car after his old one was totaled in the crash.
The insurance company compensated us because the truck was declared totaled.
After the crash, the car was totalled and had to be scrapped.
Her insurance company paid out the value of the totalled SUV.
The mechanic told me the vehicle was totalled and not worth repairing.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intact - not damaged or impaired in any way; complete
  • synonym: wrecked - (of a vehicle) badly damaged, often beyond use
