corrupter

noun
one who introduces errors
7.8
Pronunciation: / kəˈrʌptər / (AmE), / kəˈrʌptə / (BrE)
a person or thing that alters, damages, or introduces errors into texts, records, or documents

Examples

Text corrupter
The historian warned that a single corrupter could change the meaning of ancient texts.
Digital archives must be protected from any corrupter that could damage important records.
He was accused of being a corrupter after unauthorized edits were found in the official document.

Related Terms

  • synonym: falsifier - a person who changes information to make it false
  • antonym: preserver - a person who keeps something in its original state
