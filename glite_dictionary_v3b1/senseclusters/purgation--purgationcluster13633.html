purgation

noun

(in computing and data management) the deletion or secure erasure of digital files, records, or logs, typically to satisfy privacy, retention, or storage requirements.

Examples

Data purgation
The IT policy required secure purgation of obsolete customer records after five years of retention.
During the compliance audit, they found incomplete purgation of backup logs, which triggered a remediation plan.
Automated scripts run monthly to ensure purgation of temporary files and free up storage space.
