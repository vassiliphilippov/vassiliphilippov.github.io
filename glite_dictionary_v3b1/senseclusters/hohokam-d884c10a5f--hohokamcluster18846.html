Hohokam

proper noun
ancient Native American culture
9.9
Pronunciation: / hoʊˈhoʊkəm / (AmE), / həʊˈhəʊkəm / (BrE)
a prehistoric Native American people who lived in the southwestern United States, especially in present-day Arizona, known for their advanced irrigation systems

Examples

The Hohokam people
The Hohokam built extensive canal systems to irrigate their crops in the desert.
Artifacts from the Hohokam culture have been discovered near the Salt River in Arizona.
Many modern irrigation techniques in the region are inspired by the ancient Hohokam methods.
