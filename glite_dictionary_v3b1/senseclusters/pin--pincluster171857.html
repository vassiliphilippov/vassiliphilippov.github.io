pin

noun
wrestling move pinning opponent's shoulders
5.3
Pronunciation: / pɪn / (AmE), / pɪn / (BrE)
In wrestling, an act of holding an opponent's shoulders on the mat for a specified period of time, thereby winning the bout.

Examples

A wrestling pin
His defense prevented his opponent from securing a pin for the entire bout.
She won the match with a quick pin in the second round.
The referee declared a pin as soon as both shoulders touched the mat.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fall - wrestling term for a pin resulting in victory
