terminate

verb

to end the employment of someone; to dismiss from a job or position

Examples

To terminate an employee
Due to downsizing, the company terminated dozens of employees last month.
Her contract was terminated after repeated violations of company policy.
If you continue to underperform, the firm may have to terminate your employment.

Synonyms

    3.3 3.63/m

  • fire
    2.8 45/m
    fire is an informal, strong word for firing someone from a job, while terminate is the neutral employment-ending term.
  • sack
    3.5 1.77/m
    sack is a British informal verb for firing someone suddenly, while terminate is a neutral term used in formal contexts.
  • let go
    3.8 22/m
    let go is a polite, informal phrase employers use to avoid blame, while terminate is more formal and direct.
  • discharge
    4.4 4.46/m
    discharge is a formal release from duty often used for military or service roles, while terminate is general employment ending.
  • release
    4.2 1.49/m
    release is a formal act making someone free to leave a post, while terminate focuses on ending a person's employment.
