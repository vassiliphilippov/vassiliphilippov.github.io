Khrushchev

proper noun
Soviet leader and premier (1894–1971)
7.9
Pronunciation: / kruːˈʃtʃɛf / (AmE), / krʊˈʃtʃɛf / (BrE)
(1894–1971) Soviet statesman and leader; served as premier of the Soviet Union, known for denouncing Stalin and initiating reforms.

Examples

Khrushchev's reforms
Khrushchev's policies marked a significant shift in the Soviet Union after Stalin's death.
During the Cuban Missile Crisis, President Kennedy negotiated directly with Khrushchev.
Nikita Khrushchev delivered his famous secret speech denouncing Stalin in 1956.
