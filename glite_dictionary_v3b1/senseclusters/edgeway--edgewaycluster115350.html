edgeway

noun
path along edge
Pronunciation: / ˈɛdʒˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈɛdʒˌweɪ / (BrE)
an old term for a path or passage running along the edge of something, such as a field or cliff

Examples

The edgeway path
The old edgeway followed the riverbank for several miles.
Travelers used the narrow edgeway to avoid the dense forest.
He discovered an overgrown edgeway that led to the abandoned mill.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pathway - a route or track between two places
  • synonym: trail - a beaten path through countryside or wilderness
