lard

verb
prepare or cook with lard
6.9 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / l…ërd / (AmE), / l…ëÀêd / (BrE)
to add lard or other fat (or ingredients) to food, especially to make it moist or flavorful during cooking

Examples

To lard the meat
Before baking, you should lard the meat to keep it moist during cooking.
The chef decided to lard the roast with strips of bacon for added flavor.
Traditionally, they would lard the pie crust to make it flaky and rich.
The chef decided to lard the roast with garlic and herbs.
Before roasting, she carefully larded the beef to keep it moist and flavorful.
Some traditional recipes call for cooks to lard game birds with strips of bacon.
