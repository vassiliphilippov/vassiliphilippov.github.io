produce

verb
bring out and display something
2.5 27/m
Pronunciation: / pr…ôÀàdus / (AmE), / pr…ôÀàdjuÀês / (BrE)
to bring something out and show or present it, especially as evidence or proof

Examples

To produce evidence
During the meeting, she produced several documents to support her argument.
He quickly produced his identification when requested by the official.
The detective asked him to produce proof of his whereabouts during the incident.

Synonyms

  • demonstrate
    3.0 82/m
    demonstrate shows or proves something with evidence or examples, while produce means to bring out and present it.
  • show
    1.0 329/m
    show can mean simply display or point out something for others to see, while produce means to bring out and present it.
  • exhibit
    3.4 27/m
    exhibit displays something publicly or in a show, while produce means to bring out and present it often as evidence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conceal - to keep something hidden
  • synonym: reveal - to make something visible or known
  • synonym: show - to present something so others can see it
