stolidness

noun
lack of emotional response or sensitivity
9.1 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààst…ëÀêl…™dn…ôs / (AmE), / Ààst…íl…™dn…ôs / (BrE)
the quality of showing little or no emotion or sensitivity to pleasure or pain; impassiveness

Examples

His stolidness in pain
His stolidness in the face of bad news surprised everyone at the meeting.
She admired his stolidness during the crisis, but wished he would show some feeling.
The stolidness of the guards made it impossible to guess what they were thinking.

Synonyms

  • stolidity
    9.9 0.03/m
    stolidity is almost the same quiet lack of feeling, while stolidness names that impassive quality in general speech.
  • unemotionality
    8.3 0.00/m
    unemotionality is a neutral term for showing little feeling, while stolidness often suggests a dull, steady lack of visible emotion.
  • stoicism
    7.8 0.42/m
    stoicism is enduring pain or hardship without complaint, while stolidness focuses simply on showing little or no emotion.

Related Terms
