witness

verb
be present at or see an event happen
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪtnəs / (AmE), / ˈwɪtnəs / (BrE)
to see, observe, or be present at an event as it happens

Examples

To witness an event
Several people witnessed the robbery and provided statements to the police.
Tourists witness the changing of the guard at Buckingham Palace every day.
We witnessed an incredible sunset from the mountain peak.
During her career, she witnessed many important historical events firsthand.
The past decade has witnessed remarkable changes in technology and communication.
Thousands of people witnessed the lunar eclipse from the city center.

Related Terms

  • synonym: experience - to encounter or undergo an event
  • antonym: miss - to fail to see or be present at an event
  • synonym: observe - to watch carefully, especially with attention to details
