to see, observe, or be present at an event as it happens

Examples

To witness an event
Several people witnessed the robbery and provided statements to the police.
Tourists witness the changing of the guard at Buckingham Palace every day.
We witnessed an incredible sunset from the mountain peak.
During her career, she witnessed many important historical events firsthand.
The past decade has witnessed remarkable changes in technology and communication.
Thousands of people witnessed the lunar eclipse from the city center.

Synonyms

  • see
    1.0 407/m
    see is experiencing or being present during something happening, while witness often implies formally noting or testifying about that event.
  • observe
    2.4 76/m
    observe means watching carefully to notice or learn, while witness mainly means being present and may not study details.
  • watch
    1.0 435/m
    watch is looking at someone or something attentively for some time, while witness focuses on being present when something happens.
  • view
    1.0 38/m
    view is to look at or judge something from a distance or angle, while witness means being present during the actual event.
  • look on
    3.4 3.31/m
    look on is watching an event without taking part, while witness usually means being present and may be called to report it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: experience - to encounter or undergo an event
  • antonym: miss - to fail to see or be present at an event
  • synonym: observe - to watch carefully, especially with attention to details
