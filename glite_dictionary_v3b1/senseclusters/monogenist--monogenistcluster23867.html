monogenist

noun
single-gene theorist
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈmɑː.nə.dʒə.nɪst/ (AmE), /ˈmɒn.ə.dʒə.nɪst/ (BrE)
a person who believes that a particular trait or disease is caused by a single gene or origin

Examples

Genetic monogenist
The scientist was a well-known monogenist who argued that the disorder was inherited from a single gene.
As a monogenist, she focused her research on diseases with a single genetic cause.
Debates between monogenists and polygenists shaped early theories in genetics.
