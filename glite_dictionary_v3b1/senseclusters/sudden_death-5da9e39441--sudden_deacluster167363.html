sudden death

noun
overtime ending as soon as one side scores
6.9 2.9
Pronunciation: /ˈsʌd.ən dɛθ/ (AmE), /ˈsʌd.ən dɛθ/ (BrE)
(In sports) an overtime period in which play ends immediately when one side scores, determining the winner instantly.

Examples

Sudden death overtime
Golf tournaments sometimes use sudden death playoffs to break ties between players.
The hockey game was decided by sudden death after a tied score in regulation time.
They entered sudden death overtime, where the first team to score would win the match.

  • synonym: overtime - extra time played to break a tie in sports
