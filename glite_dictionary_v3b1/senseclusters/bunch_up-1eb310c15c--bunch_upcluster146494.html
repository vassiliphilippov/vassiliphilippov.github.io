Synonyms

  • bunch together
    3.1 0.3/m
    bunch together means to form a tight group naturally, while bunch up emphasizes forming a close, often sudden cluster.
  • bunch
    1.2 2/m
    bunch is a shorter verb meaning to gather into a group, while bunch up suggests forming tightly or more suddenly.
  • cluster
    3.5 6/m
    cluster is used for things or people gathered close, often in groups, while bunch up focuses on forming a tight, single cluster.
  • clump
    4.4 0.9/m
    clump forms a small thick group, often of plants or objects, while bunch up refers to people or things grouping tightly.
  • crowd together
    3.0 0.3/m
    crowd together gathers many people close, often in one place, while bunch up can mean any small tight group.
  • pack
    2.3 19/m
    pack means to press or cram things closely, often tightly by force, while bunch up simply means to gather closely.
