bunch up

verb
to form into a tight group
Pronunciation: / bʌntʃ ʌp / (AmE), / bʌntʃ ʌp / (BrE)
to form into a tight group or cluster; to gather closely together

Examples

To bunch up papers
Please don't bunch up your papers; keep them in a neat stack.
The marathon runners tend to bunch up at water stations during the race.
The students started to bunch up near the entrance when it began to rain.
