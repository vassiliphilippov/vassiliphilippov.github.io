copulative

adjective
connecting; linking (linguistics)
8.8 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëÀêpj…ôÀåle…™t…™v / (AmE), / Ààk…ípj äl…ôt…™v / (BrE)
(of a word, especially a conjunction) connecting words, phrases, or clauses in a sentence to indicate connected or additive meaning.

Examples

Copulative conjunction
In grammar lessons, teachers often highlight the copulative function of the word 'and'.
Some linguists classify 'as well as' as a copulative connector.
The word copulative refers to conjunctions that link elements in a sentence, such as 'and'.

Synonyms

  • coordinating
    4.2 0.00/m
    coordinating is used for conjunctions that join equal grammatical parts like words or clauses, while copulative highlights additive 'and' meaning.
  • coordinative
    8.3 0.04/m
    coordinative links two elements of the same type in grammar, while copulative focuses on joining with an additive 'and' sense.
  • conjunctive
    7.1 0.11/m
    conjunctive serves to connect clauses or words in general contexts, while copulative specifically stresses an adding, 'and'-like connection.
