copulative

adjective
connecting; linking (linguistics)
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːpjəˌleɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈkɒpjʊlətɪv / (BrE)
(of a word, especially a conjunction) connecting words, phrases, or clauses in a sentence to indicate connected or additive meaning.

Examples

Copulative conjunction
In grammar lessons, teachers often highlight the copulative function of the word 'and'.
Some linguists classify 'as well as' as a copulative connector.
The word copulative refers to conjunctions that link elements in a sentence, such as 'and'.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coordinating - joining words or groups of equal grammatical rank
  • antonym: disjunctive - (of a conjunction) expressing an alternative or contrast, e.g., 'or'
