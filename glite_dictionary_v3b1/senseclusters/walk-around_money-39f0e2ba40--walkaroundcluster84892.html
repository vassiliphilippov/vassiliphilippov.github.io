walk-around money

noun
money given to influence voters in elections
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔk əˌraʊnd ˌmʌni / (AmE), / ˈwɔːk əˌraʊnd ˌmʌni / (BrE)
money distributed, especially during political campaigns, to influence voters or cover informal expenses, often as a form of bribery

Examples

Walk-around money during elections
Some officials were charged with handing out walk-around money during the city elections.
The candidate distributed walk-around money to local organizers to get out the vote.
There's growing concern about the role of walk-around money in corrupting the democratic process.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bribe - money or favor given to influence someone's actions, especially illegally
