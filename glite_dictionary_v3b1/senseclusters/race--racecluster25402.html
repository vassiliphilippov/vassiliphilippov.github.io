race

verb
cause someone or something to move quickly
1.0
Pronunciation: / reɪs / (AmE), / reɪs / (BrE)
to compete in a contest of speed or to make someone or something compete in such a contest

Examples

To race a car
During the experiment, scientists raced mice through a complex maze to test their memory.
She raced her children to the car as the storm approached.
The coach decided to race the new horse against the others.
If you race against him, you might surprise yourself with how fast you can run.
She raced her brother to see who could reach the finish line first.
The children decided to race to the end of the street after school.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compete - to take part in a contest or competition
  • synonym: hurry - to move or act with speed
  • degree lower: lose - to be defeated in a contest
  • synonym: rush - to move or cause to move with urgent haste
  • degree higher: win - to achieve first place in a competition
