undercurrent of mistrust

noun
hidden feeling of distrust
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndɚˌkɝənt əv mɪsˈtrʌst / (AmE), / ˈʌndəkʌrənt əv mɪsˈtrʌst / (BrE)
a hidden or not immediately obvious feeling of distrust present in a group or situation

Examples

An undercurrent of mistrust
As the meeting continued, the undercurrent of mistrust became harder to ignore.
Even though everyone smiled, an undercurrent of mistrust ran beneath the surface of their conversation.
There was an undercurrent of mistrust throughout the negotiations, making progress difficult.
