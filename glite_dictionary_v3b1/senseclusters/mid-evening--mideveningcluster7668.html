mid-evening

noun
middle of evening
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪdˈivnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌmɪdˈiːvnɪŋ / (BrE)
the period around the middle part of the evening, typically between early and late evening

Examples

Mid-evening dessert
We usually have dessert in the mid-evening after dinner.
The concert started in the mid-evening when most people had arrived.
She likes to take a walk during the mid-evening to relax after a busy day.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: early evening - the beginning part of the evening
  • degree higher: evening - the part of the day between late afternoon and night
  • degree higher: late evening - the later part of the evening, close to night
  • degree higher: night - the period of darkness between sunset and sunrise
