catch

verb
perceive briefly or suddenly with senses
1.0 1.8
Pronunciation: /kætʃ/ (AmE), /kætʃ/ (BrE)
to perceive, notice, or understand something suddenly or briefly, especially with the senses or mind

Examples

To catch a smell
As I entered the kitchen, I caught the aroma of freshly baked bread.
He could just catch the faint sound of music coming from the next room.
She managed to catch a glimpse of the rare bird before it flew away.
Did you catch the mistake in his calculations?
She always tries to catch her colleagues when they slip up.
The editor managed to catch two errors in the final manuscript.
Did you catch the announcement on the loudspeaker?
He spoke so quietly that nobody could catch his name in the meeting.
I'm sorry, I didn't catch what you just said—could you repeat it?
