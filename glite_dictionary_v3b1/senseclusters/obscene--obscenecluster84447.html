obscene

adjective
sexually suggestive or indecent
6.2 2.04/m
Pronunciation: / …ôbÀàsin / (AmE), / …ôbÀàsiÀên / (BrE)
offensive or indecent, especially in a sexual or moral way

Examples

Obscene phone calls
She received a series of obscene phone calls that made her feel uncomfortable.
Some people found his jokes obscene, while others thought they were just harmless fun.
The magazine was banned for publishing obscene images considered offensive by authorities.
Her artwork was labeled as obscene due to its explicit imagery.
Some critics argued that the performance was obscene and not suitable for broadcast.
The committee found the film's content to be obscene and banned it from theaters.

Synonyms

  • indecent
    6.7 1.90/m
    indecent describes something improper or against social rules, while obscene is stronger and often shocks with sexual offensiveness.
  • lewd
    7.4 1.56/m
    lewd is openly sexual and meant to excite, while obscene is a broader legal or moral label for deeply offensive material.
  • salacious
    9.9 1.11/m
    salacious shows an excessive interest in sex and teases the reader, while obscene is judged offensive by law or morals.
  • raunchy
    8.6 1.02/m
    raunchy is rough, sexually explicit, and often funny in a rude way, while obscene is serious and deeply offensive.
  • vulgar
    6.5 1.16/m
    vulgar is tasteless, coarse, or crude in speech or dress, while obscene specifically shocks by sexual or moral indecency.
  • filthy
    4.7 1.61/m
    filthy is very dirty or rude, often with sexual content meant to shock, while obscene is used for morally offensive material.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lewd - crude and offensive in a sexual way
