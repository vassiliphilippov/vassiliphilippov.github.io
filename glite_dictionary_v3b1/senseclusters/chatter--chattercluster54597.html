chatter

noun
noisy or rapid talk
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃæt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʃætə / (BrE)
continuous and sometimes meaningless talk, usually in a lively or noisy way.

Examples

Lively chatter in class
Excited chatter filled the classroom as the students waited for the teacher.
He could hear the cheerful chatter of birds outside his window.
The chatter from the next table made it hard to concentrate on my book.

Related Terms

  • synonym: babble - talk rapidly and continuously in a foolish, excited, or incomprehensible way
  • synonym: prattle - talk at length in a foolish or inconsequential way
