endemically

adverb
in a regular local way
7.7
Pronunciation: / ɛnˈdɛmɪkli / (AmE), / ɛnˈdɛmɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that is regularly found or typical in a particular place or among certain people

Examples

Occur endemically in regions
Certain diseases occur endemically in regions with poor sanitation.
The plant grows endemically in only a few isolated valleys.
Corruption was found endemically throughout the organization, making reform difficult.
Malaria occurs endemically in certain tropical regions where mosquitoes thrive.
Corruption was so endemically entrenched in the organization that reforms made little difference.
Some traditions are endemically present in rural communities, passed down through generations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: locally - in a way that relates to a particular area
  • synonym: pervasively - in a way that spreads widely throughout an area or group
  • antonym: sporadically - in a way that occurs at irregular intervals or only in a few places
  • antonym: universally - in a way that applies everywhere or to everyone
