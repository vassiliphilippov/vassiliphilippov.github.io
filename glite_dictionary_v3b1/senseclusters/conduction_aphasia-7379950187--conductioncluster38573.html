conduction aphasia

noun
difficulty repeating language due to brain lesion
7.8
Pronunciation: / kənˈdʌk.ʃən əˈfeɪ.ʒə / (AmE), / kənˈdʌk.ʃən əˈfeɪ.zi.ə / (BrE)
A form of aphasia characterized by difficulty in repeating words or sentences, typically due to damage in the brain's language association pathways.

Examples

Conduction aphasia symptoms
Patients with conduction aphasia often speak fluently but have trouble repeating phrases accurately.
Research suggests that conduction aphasia is caused by damage to the arcuate fasciculus in the brain.
The neurologist diagnosed her with conduction aphasia after she struggled to repeat simple sentences.

  • synonym: aphasia - loss or impairment of the ability to use or comprehend language
