solitary wave

noun
a single, non-dissipative wave in nonlinear systems (physics)
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑːlɪˌtɛri weɪv / (AmE), / ˈsɒlɪtəri weɪv / (BrE)
(physics) a single wave that propagates unchanged over long distances in certain nonlinear systems, retaining its shape and energy without dissipating or overlapping with others.

Examples

A solitary wave in physics
In certain optical fibers, a solitary wave maintains its shape while transmitting light over long distances.
Physicists study the properties of a solitary wave to improve the design of energy-efficient transmission systems.
The discovery of the solitary wave helped explain how tidal bores travel upriver with minimal distortion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: soliton - a solitary wave that maintains its shape while traveling at constant speed
