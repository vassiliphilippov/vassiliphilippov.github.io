earn

verb
receive or deserve through effort
1.0 58/m
Pronunciation: / …ùÀên / (AmE), / …úÀên / (BrE)
to receive or deserve something as a result of one's own actions, effort, or behavior

Examples

Earn respect
His honest actions earned him the respect of his peers.
If you want trust, you must earn it through your behavior.
She worked hard to earn her promotion within the company.

Synonyms

    1.4 83/m
    gain means obtaining something often by effort or chance, while earn implies you deserve it because of your work.
  • win
    1.0 51/m

  • garner
    6.0 7.83/m
    garner is a formal word for collecting or gathering things or praise, while earn focuses on deserved rewards from one's actions.
