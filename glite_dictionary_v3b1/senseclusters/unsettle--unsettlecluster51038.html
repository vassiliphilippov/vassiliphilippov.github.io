unsettle

verb
disturb composure; make uneasy
2.2
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈsɛt̬l̩ / (AmE), / ʌnˈsɛtl / (BrE)
to disturb the composure or peace of; to make someone feel anxious, uneasy, or insecure

Examples

To unsettle the mind
Some horror films intentionally unsettle their audiences to provoke a strong emotional response.
The sudden change in her tone began to unsettle him during the meeting.
The unexpected news clearly unsettled everyone in the office.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disturb - to interrupt the normal condition or functioning of someone or something
  • antonym: reassure - to restore confidence or composure to someone
