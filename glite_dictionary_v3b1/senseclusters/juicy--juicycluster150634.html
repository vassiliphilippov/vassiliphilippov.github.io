juicy

adjective
full of juice; very moist
1.0 4.15/m
Pronunciation: / Ààd íusi / (AmE), / Ààd íuÀêsi / (BrE)
Full of juice; containing a lot of liquid, especially in a way that makes it pleasant to eat or drink.

Examples

Juicy orange slices
Choose the ripest, most juicy peaches for the best dessert.
She loves eating juicy watermelon slices on hot summer days.
The juicy orange burst with flavor when I bit into it.

Synonyms

  • succulent
    4.2 1.43/m
    succulent is tender and full of juice, while juicy means full of liquid and pleasant to eat.
  • luscious
    4.3 1.42/m
    luscious tastes rich, sweet, and very pleasing to the senses, while juicy focuses on being full of liquid and pleasant to eat.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dry - not containing much liquid or moisture
