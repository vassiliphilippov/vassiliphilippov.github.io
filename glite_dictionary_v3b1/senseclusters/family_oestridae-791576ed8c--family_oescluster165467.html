family Oestridae

noun
family of parasitic flies (warble flies)
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈfæməli oʊˈstrɪdiː / (AmE), / ˈfæməli əʊˈstrɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large flies, commonly known as warble flies, whose larvae are internal parasites of mammals.

Examples

The family Oestridae includes several species whose larvae live under the skin of mammals.
Veterinarians often encounter infestations caused by members of the family Oestridae in livestock.
