from

preposition
starting point or origin
Pronunciation: / frəm / (AmE), / frɒm / (BrE)
used to indicate the starting point, origin, or source of something in space, time, or action

Examples

From the airport
The package was sent from Germany and arrived in just three days.
She walked home from school every afternoon, enjoying the fresh air.
We have been friends from childhood and share many memories together.

Related Terms

  • synonym: since - used to indicate the starting point in time
  • antonym: to - used to indicate the destination or end point
