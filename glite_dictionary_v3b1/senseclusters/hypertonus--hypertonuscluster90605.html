hypertonus

noun
increased muscle tone (medical)
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ha…™Ààp…úr.to ä.n…ôs / (AmE), / ha…™Ààp…úÀê.t…ô.n…ôs / (BrE)
An abnormal increase in muscle tone; the state of being hypertonic, especially in muscular tissue.

Examples

Muscle hypertonus
Neurologists assess hypertonus by evaluating resistance to passive movement in muscles.
Spasticity is a form of hypertonus that can interfere with movement and daily activities.
The patient exhibited pronounced hypertonus in the lower limbs after the stroke.

Synonyms

  • hypertonicity
    9.9 0.00/m
    hypertonicity describes the state of abnormally high muscle tension in tissue, while hypertonus names that same muscular condition.
  • hypertonia
    9.8 0.00/m
    hypertonia is the clinical term for increased muscle tone that causes resistance to movement, while hypertonus names the abnormal muscle state.
