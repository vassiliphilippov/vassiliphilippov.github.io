Salix candida

noun
species of North American shrub willow
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪlɪks kænˈdiːdə / (AmE), / ˈseɪlɪks kanˈdɪdə / (BrE)
A species of North American shrub willow characterized by whitish, woolly leaves, commonly known as sageleaf willow.

Examples

Salix candida shrub
Salix candida is often found in wet meadows and along the shores of lakes in northern North America.
Botanists studied the ecological role of Salix candida in supporting native wildlife populations.
The distinctive whitish leaves of Salix candida make it easy to identify among other willow species.
