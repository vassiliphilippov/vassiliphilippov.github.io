diamond

noun
precious gem; cut and polished diamond
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪmənd /; / ˈdaɪəmənd / (AmE), / ˈdaɪəmənd / (BrE)
a very hard, clear, and valuable stone made of pure carbon, used in jewelry and industry

Examples

Diamond ring
She inherited a beautiful diamond necklace from her grandmother.
The engagement ring featured a sparkling diamond set in white gold.
The jeweler showed us a rare blue diamond valued at millions of dollars.
Industrial drills sometimes use diamond tips to cut through hard materials.
She wore a necklace with a large diamond pendant to the gala.
The jeweler placed the sparkling diamond into a velvet box.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gem - a precious or semi-precious stone
  • antonym: graphite - a soft black form of carbon, a less hard allotrope than diamond
  • synonym: jewel - a precious stone used in jewelry
  • degree lower: ruby - a red precious stone, one of the four precious gemstones
