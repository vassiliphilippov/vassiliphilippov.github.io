externalize

verb
make something external, objective, or visible
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈstɜrnəˌlaɪz /; / ɪkˈstɝː.nə.laɪz /; / ɪkˈstɝrnəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ɪkˈstɜːnəlaɪz /; / ɪkˈstɜː.nə.laɪz / (BrE)
to express or treat an internal thought, feeling, or process as something outside yourself or observable

Examples

To externalize feelings
Children often externalize their fears through drawings and stories.
She tried to externalize her emotions by writing in her journal daily.
The company’s culture begins to externalize in the way employees interact with customers.
By externalizing their anxiety, patients often attribute internal conflicts to external factors.
He tends to externalize his frustrations, blaming others for his problems.
Some theories suggest that people externalize their fears by projecting them onto the world around them.
Children often externalise their feelings by drawing or acting them out.
His ability to externalise complex ideas makes his lectures very engaging.
Therapists encourage patients not to externalise blame for their problems.

Related Terms
