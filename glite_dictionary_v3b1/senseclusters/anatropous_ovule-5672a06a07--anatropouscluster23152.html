anatropous ovule

noun
inverted ovule in botany
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈnætrəpəs ˈoʊvjul / (AmE), / əˈnatrəpəs ˈəʊvjuːl / (BrE)
an ovule that is completely inverted, turned back 180 degrees on its stalk so that the micropyle faces the placenta

Examples

Anatropous ovule inverted
Many flowering plants produce anatropous ovules, in which the micropyle is positioned close to the placenta.
The characteristic feature of an anatropous ovule is its complete inversion during development.
Under the microscope, the stalk of an anatropous ovule can be seen curving to bring the micropyle near the funiculus.

Related Terms

  • antonym: orthotropous ovule - an ovule with a straight or upright orientation in its stalk
