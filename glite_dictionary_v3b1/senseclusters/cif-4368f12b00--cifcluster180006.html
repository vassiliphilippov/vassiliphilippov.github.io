CIF

noun
video resolution standard
9.5
Pronunciation: / siː aɪ ɛf / (AmE), / siː aɪ ɛf / (BrE)
a video resolution standard (usually 352×288 pixels) used in older videoconferencing and surveillance systems

Examples

CIF video format
The security camera recorded footage in CIF to save storage space.
Early video calls often used CIF resolution for smoother streaming over slow connections.
Many older surveillance systems still operate with CIF as their default video format.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: 4CIF - a four-times larger version of CIF video resolution
  • degree lower: QCIF - a quarter-size version of CIF video resolution
