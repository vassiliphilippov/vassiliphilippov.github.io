categorem

noun
term in logic that can stand alone
Pronunciation: / ˈkæt̬ɪɡəˌrɛm / (AmE), / ˈkatɪɡəˌrɛm / (BrE)
A term or expression that can stand alone as the subject or predicate of a logical proposition.

Examples

A categorem term
In classical logic, the word 'man' functions as a categorem in many propositions.
Nouns and pronouns are typical examples of a categorem in logical analysis.
The distinction between a categorem and a syncategorem is important in medieval philosophy.
