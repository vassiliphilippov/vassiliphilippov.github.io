put out feelers

verb
make preliminary inquiries or investigations
9.9 5.0
Pronunciation: /pʊt aʊt ˈfiː.lərz/ /; / /pʌt aʊt ˈfiː.lərz/ (AmE), /pʊt aʊt ˈfiː.ləz/ (BrE)
to make informal or discreet inquiries to find out what people think or if something is possible, usually before taking action

Examples

To put out feelers about plans
Before approaching the board, she decided to put out feelers about the new proposal.
He put out feelers to his contacts to learn more about job opportunities.
The company is putting out feelers to see if any partners are interested in collaboration.
Before committing to the project, she decided to put out feelers to see if anyone was interested.
He quietly put out feelers about switching departments, but didn't make any official moves yet.
We should put out feelers to potential investors before launching the product.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sound out - to try to find out someone's opinion or intentions by talking to them
