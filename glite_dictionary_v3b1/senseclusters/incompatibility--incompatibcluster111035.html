incompatibility

noun
immune rejection of foreign material
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnkəmˌpætəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəmˌpætəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
(Medical) The condition in which the immune system tries to reject foreign material, such as transfused blood or transplanted tissue, because it does not match the body's own tissues.

Examples

Blood incompatibility
Incompatibility between donor and recipient blood can lead to dangerous transfusion reactions.
Doctors tested for tissue incompatibility before performing the organ transplant.
Immunological incompatibility was the main reason for the body's rejection of the graft.

Related Terms

  • antonym: compatibility - the ability of things to exist or work together without conflict
