Korsakov's syndrome

noun
memory disorder caused by alcoholism
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔr səˌkɑvz ˌsɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / ˈkɔː səˌkɒvz ˌsɪn.drəʊm / (BrE)
A chronic memory disorder caused by severe deficiency of thiamine, typically associated with alcoholism, characterized by the loss of short-term memory while long-term memory remains relatively intact.

Examples

Korsakov's syndrome memory loss
Alcohol-induced vitamin deficiency is a leading cause of Korsakov's syndrome.
People suffering from Korsakov's syndrome often cannot remember recent conversations but recall events from their youth.
The patient was diagnosed with Korsakov's syndrome following years of heavy drinking.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dementia - chronic or persistent disorder of mental processes marked by memory disorders
