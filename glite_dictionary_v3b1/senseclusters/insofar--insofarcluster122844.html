insofar

adverb
to the extent or degree that
6.7 5.71/m
Pronunciation: / Àå…™n.so äÀàf…ër / (AmE), / Àå…™n.s…ô äÀàf…ëÀê / (BrE)
to the extent or degree that something happens or is true; as far as.

Examples

Insofar as possible
Insofar as I understand the situation, we have no choice but to comply.
Insofar as possible, we try to use local ingredients in our restaurant.
The new policy is effective insofar as it encourages teamwork among employees.

Synonyms

  • so far as
    3.5 8.96/m
    so far as often introduces a factual limit or qualification in speech, while insofar points to the extent something is true.
  • insomuch
    9.2 0.10/m
    insomuch is a formal or old-fashioned way to show degree or cause, while insofar is more common in modern usage.
  • to that extent
    3.9 0.88/m
    to that extent describes how much something applies in a situation, while insofar expresses the degree that something is true.
  • to that degree
    3.9 0.12/m
    to that degree refers to a level or amount of change, while insofar focuses on the extent something is true or applies.
