in too deep

adverb
overly involved or committed, hard to withdraw
5.3 7.4
Pronunciation: / ɪn tu dip / (AmE), / ɪn tʃuː diːp / (BrE)
In a situation where one is overly involved, committed, or entangled, especially to the point that it is difficult to withdraw or resolve matters easily.

Examples

In too deep trouble
After agreeing to lead the project, Sarah quickly realized she was in too deep.
He found himself in too deep with gambling debts he couldn't pay back.
They tried to fix the car themselves, but soon they were in too deep to finish without help.
