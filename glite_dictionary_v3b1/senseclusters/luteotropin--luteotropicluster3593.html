luteotropin

noun
hormone stimulating milk production
6.0
Pronunciation: / luːˈtiː.əˌtroʊ.pɪn / (AmE), / luːˈtiː.ə.trəʊ.pɪn / (BrE)
a hormone secreted by the anterior pituitary gland that stimulates growth of the mammary glands and milk production after childbirth

Examples

Luteotropin hormone levels
Luteotropin levels rise significantly after childbirth to promote milk production.
Researchers studied the effect of reduced luteotropin on lactation in mammals.
The secretion of luteotropin is regulated by signals from the hypothalamus.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oxytocin - a pituitary hormone involved in labor and milk ejection, related functionally to luteotropin
  • synonym: prolactin - another name for the same hormone produced by the pituitary gland
