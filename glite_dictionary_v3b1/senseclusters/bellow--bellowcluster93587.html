bellow

verb
to make a loud, deep roar
7.8 3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛloʊ / (AmE), / ˈbɛləʊ / (BrE)
to shout or make a loud, deep roar, especially in anger or pain

Examples

To bellow in anger
She bellowed at her children to come inside before it got dark.
The bull bellowed loudly in the field, startling everyone nearby.
The wounded lion bellowed in pain, echoing through the savannah.
He bellowed at his team to hurry up during the last few minutes of the game.
She bellowed with laughter, startling everyone nearby.
The teacher bellowed instructions to the noisy students across the playground.

Related Terms

  • synonym: roar - to make a deep, loud, prolonged sound
  • antonym: whisper - to speak very softly
  • synonym: yell - to shout loudly, especially in anger, fear, or excitement
