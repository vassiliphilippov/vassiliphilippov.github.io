pontifically

adverb
in a formal, ceremonial way
Pronunciation: / pɒnˈtɪfɪkli / (AmE), / pɒnˈtɪfɪkli / (BrE)
in a manner that is formal, ceremonial, or characteristic of a pope or high religious authority

Examples

To speak pontifically at mass
The priest spoke pontifically during the important ceremony, impressing everyone with his solemn tone.
She entered the hall pontifically, as if she were presiding over a grand event.
The official delivered the announcement pontifically, following every traditional protocol.

Related Terms

  • antonym: casually - in a relaxed or informal way
  • synonym: ceremoniously - in a very formal or ritualistic way
