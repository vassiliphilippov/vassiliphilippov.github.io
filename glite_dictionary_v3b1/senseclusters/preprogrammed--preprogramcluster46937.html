preprogrammed

adjective
determined in advance
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈproʊɡræmd / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈprəʊɡræmd / (BrE)
set up or programmed in advance to act or operate automatically, without further input

Examples

Preprogrammed instructions
The robot followed its preprogrammed instructions to clean the entire floor.
Some animals have preprogrammed behaviors that help them survive in the wild.
His answer sounded preprogrammed, as if he had practiced it many times before.
The coffee machine is preprogrammed to start brewing at 7 a.m. every day.
Many modern cars have preprogrammed safety features that activate automatically.
The lights in the building are preprogrammed to turn off at midnight.

Related Terms

  • synonym: automatic - happening without conscious thought or control
  • synonym: instinctive - done naturally, without thinking
  • antonym: manual - operated or done by hand rather than automatically
  • antonym: spontaneous - done freely and without planning
