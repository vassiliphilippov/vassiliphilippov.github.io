bevel gear

noun
gear for angled motion transmission
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛvəl ɡɪr / (AmE), / ˈbɛvəl ɡɪə / (BrE)
a gear designed to mesh with another gear at an angle, typically used to transmit motion between intersecting shafts

Examples

A bevel gear wheel
Engineers replaced the standard gear with a bevel gear for more efficient power transfer.
If the bevel gear wears out, the performance of the transmission will be affected.
The machine uses a bevel gear to change the direction of rotation between the two shafts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: spur gear - gear with straight teeth parallel to the axis
