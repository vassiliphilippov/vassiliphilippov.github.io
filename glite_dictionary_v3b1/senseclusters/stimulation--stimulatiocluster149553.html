stimulation

noun
encouragement of activity or response
1.0 4.4
Pronunciation: / ˌstɪmjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌstɪmjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of encouraging a response or activity in an organism or body part, especially by applying a stimulus (such as to nerves, organs, or tissues)

Examples

Nerve stimulation
Prolonged stimulation of the nerves can cause muscle contractions.
Sensory stimulation is vital for brain development in young children.
The research focused on the effects of electrical stimulation on heart tissue.
Electrical stimulation of the brain can trigger muscle movement.
The doctor explained that stimulation of these nerves reduces pain.
Visual stimulation is important for early childhood development.

Related Terms

  • synonym: encouragement - the act of giving someone support or motivation
  • synonym: excitation - the process of increasing the activity of an organ or tissue
  • antonym: inhibition - the process of restraining or suppressing activity, especially in nerves or organs
  • antonym: suppression - the act of stopping or reducing a response or activity
