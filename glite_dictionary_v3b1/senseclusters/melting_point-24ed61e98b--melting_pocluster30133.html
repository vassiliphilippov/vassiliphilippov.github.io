melting point

noun
temperature where solid becomes liquid
4.8 3.1
Pronunciation: /ˈmel.tɪŋ pɔɪnt/ (AmE), /ˈmel.tɪŋ pɔɪnt/ (BrE)
The temperature at which a solid substance changes to a liquid; also, a critical moment when significant change occurs.

Examples

Crisis melting point
Negotiations reached their melting point before a sudden breakthrough resolved the dispute.
Scientists recorded the melting point of the new compound during the experiment.
The melting point of iron is much higher than that of aluminum.
