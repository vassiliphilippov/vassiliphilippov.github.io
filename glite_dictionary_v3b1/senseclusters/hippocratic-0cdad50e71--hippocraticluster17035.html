Hippocratic

adjective
relating to Hippocrates or his medical school
Pronunciation: / ˌhɪpəˈkrætɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɪpəˈkrætɪk / (BrE)
of or relating to Hippocrates or his school of medicine; especially associated with the ancient principles and practices of medicine established by Hippocrates.

Examples

Hippocratic oath
Some doctors adhere strongly to Hippocratic principles in their practice.
The Hippocratic Oath remains an important symbolic vow in medical ethics.
The Hippocratic writings emphasize observation and systematic study of disease.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Hippocratic Oath - an oath historically taken by physicians swearing to uphold ethical standards
