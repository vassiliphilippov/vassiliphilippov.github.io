Bomarea edulis

noun
tropical vine with edible roots
Pronunciation: / boʊˈmɛəriə ˈɛdʒʊlɪs / (AmE), / bəʊˈmɑːriə ˈɛdjʊlɪs / (BrE)
A tropical vine native to the West Indies and northern South America, known for its pink-and-yellow flowers spotted with purple and edible roots, which are sometimes boiled as a potato substitute.

Examples

Bomarea edulis vine
In some cultures, Bomarea edulis is valued both as an ornamental plant and for its edible tubers.
Many gardeners are attracted to Bomarea edulis for its striking pink-and-yellow flowers.
The roots of Bomarea edulis are sometimes used as a potato substitute in traditional South American dishes.
