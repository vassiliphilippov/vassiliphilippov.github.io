surveillance

noun
close observation by authorities or police
3.2 5.0
Pronunciation: / sɚˈveɪ.ləns / (AmE), / səˈveɪ.ləns / (BrE)
the careful and continuous observation of a person, group, or place, especially by the police or authorities.

Examples

Electronic surveillance
Advances in technology have made electronic surveillance more effective and widespread.
The government increased surveillance of public spaces after the recent attacks.
Under constant surveillance, the suspects were unable to meet in secret.

Related Terms

  • synonym: monitoring - continuous or periodic observation for oversight or safety
  • antonym: neglect - failure to watch or care for someone or something
  • synonym: supervision - the act of overseeing people or activities
