calving

noun
glacier shedding ice (forming icebergs)
7.4
Pronunciation: /ˈkæl.vɪŋ/ /; / /ˈkæv.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈkɑːv.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
the process by which a glacier, ice shelf, or ice sheet breaks off and releases large pieces of ice (icebergs) into the sea or lake.

Examples

Glacier calving event
Scientists recorded increasing rates of calving along the glacier's seaward edge during summer melt.
Massive calving events can cause tsunamis when large icebergs fall into the ocean near shore.
Satellite images help researchers measure seasonal calving patterns and predict future sea-level contributions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: iceberg calving - the breaking away of ice from a glacier or ice shelf to form icebergs
