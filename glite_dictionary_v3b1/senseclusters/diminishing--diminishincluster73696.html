diminishing

adjective
becoming smaller or less
Pronunciation: / dɪˈmɪnɪʃɪŋ / (AmE), / dɪˈmɪnɪʃɪŋ / (BrE)
becoming smaller, fewer, or less; decreasing in size, amount, or importance

Examples

A diminishing number
Her confidence showed no sign of diminishing despite the setbacks.
The company faced diminishing profits as competition intensified.
There are concerns about the diminishing number of native speakers in the region.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decreasing - becoming less in amount, size, or degree
  • antonym: increasing - becoming greater in size, amount, or degree
