dateline

noun
news article heading with date and place
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪtˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈdeɪt.laɪn / (BrE)
A line at the beginning of a news article stating the date and place from which the report was sent.

Examples

Article dateline
The editor asked her to correct the dateline before sending the article to print.
The reporter's story had a dateline reading 'Beijing, March 15'.
Without a dateline, readers might not know where the news originated.

Related Terms

  • synonym: byline - line in a newspaper article naming the writer
