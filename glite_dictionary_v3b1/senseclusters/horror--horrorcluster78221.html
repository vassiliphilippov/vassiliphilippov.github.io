horror

noun
intense fear or aversion
Pronunciation: / ˈhɔrɚ /; / ˈhɔrər / (AmE), / ˈhɒrə / (BrE)
a strong feeling of intense fear, shock, or disgust

Examples

Feel horror
After reading the report, she could not hide her horror at the findings.
She recoiled in horror upon seeing the gruesome scene.
The idea of cruelty to animals filled him with horror.
He recalled with horror the events of that terrible night.
She froze in horror as the shadow moved across the wall.
The film was filled with scenes of unspeakable horror.

Related Terms

  • antonym: delight - great pleasure, opposite of aversion or horror
  • synonym: terror - extreme fear
