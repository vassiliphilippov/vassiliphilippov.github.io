fougasse

noun
decorative French flatbread
Pronunciation: / fuːˈɡæs / (AmE), / fuːˈɡas / (BrE)
a type of flatbread from Provence, France, often shaped decoratively and flavored with herbs or olives

Examples

A fougasse loaf
The bakery displayed a beautiful fougasse decorated with sprigs of rosemary and black olives.
She learned to bake fougasse while traveling in southern France last summer.
A warm fougasse pairs perfectly with a bowl of homemade soup.

Related Terms

  • antonym: baguette - a long, thin loaf of French bread
  • synonym: focaccia - an Italian flatbread often topped with herbs or other ingredients
