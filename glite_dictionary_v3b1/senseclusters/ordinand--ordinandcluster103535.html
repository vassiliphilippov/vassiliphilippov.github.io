ordinand

noun
person to be ordained
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌɔr.dɪˈnænd / (AmE), / ˌɔː.dɪˈnænd / (BrE)
a person who is about to be ordained, especially as a priest or minister.

Examples

New ordinand welcomed
After the service, the new ordinand was welcomed by the congregation.
Each ordinand spent months in training before the ceremony.
The bishop met privately with every ordinand to offer personal guidance.
