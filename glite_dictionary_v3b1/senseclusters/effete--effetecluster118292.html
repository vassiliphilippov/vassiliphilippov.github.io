effete

adjective
affected or overrefined; effeminate
9.9 0.21/m

Examples

Effete style
His speech struck many as effete, marked by a delicacy and elegance uncommon among his peers.
Some saw the aristocrat's mannerisms as excessively effete for their taste.
The critic described the poet's style as effete and lacking substance.

Synonyms

    7.5 0.93/m

  • unmanly
    6.8 0.22/m
    unmanly means not showing strength or courage people expect from men, while effete suggests being too refined and lacking real vigor.
