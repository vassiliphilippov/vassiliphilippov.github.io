subnormal

noun
person of below-normal intelligence (dated, offensive)
Pronunciation: / sʌbˈnɔrməl / (AmE), / sʌbˈnɔːməl / (BrE)
(dated, offensive) A person who has less than normal intellectual ability; a person with intellectual disability.

Examples

A subnormal (person)
He was cruelly labeled a subnormal in a time when understanding of learning disabilities was limited.
Staff were trained to help subnormal children adapt to daily life, though such language is no longer acceptable.
The institution once housed several subnormal individuals, but the term is now recognized as outdated and offensive.

Related Terms

  • antonym: genius - a person of exceptional intellectual or creative power
  • synonym: idiot - a person of very low intelligence (offensive, dated)
