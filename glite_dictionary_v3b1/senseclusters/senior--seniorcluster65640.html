senior

adjective
greater in rank or age
2.9 54/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàsiÀêni…ö / (AmE), / ÀàsiÀêni…ô / (BrE)
having a higher rank, greater age, or longer length of service than others; superior in status or position.

Examples

Senior manager
Despite her young age, she quickly became a senior engineer at the firm.
He was appointed as the senior manager in the department.
The senior members of the team guided the new recruits.

Synonyms

  • superior
    4.1 8.60/m
    superior means a higher status or rank and can imply better quality, while senior often shows age or longer service.
  • high-ranking
    4.2 3.08/m
    high-ranking describes someone with an important position in an organization, while senior can also refer to greater age or service.
  • upper-level
    4.6 0.63/m
    upper-level refers to a higher layer or grade in a system, while senior refers to a person with higher rank, age, or service.

Related Terms

  • antonym: junior - having a lower rank, younger, or with less experience
