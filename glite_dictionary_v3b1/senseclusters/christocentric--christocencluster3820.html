christocentric

adjective
focused on Christ
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkraɪstoʊˈsɛntrɪk / (AmE), / ˌkrɪstəʊˈsɛntrɪk / (BrE)
having Christ as the central focus or principle, especially in theology or religious practice

Examples

A christocentric message
The church's teachings are distinctly christocentric, emphasizing the life and message of Jesus.
Her christocentric worldview shapes how she interprets religious texts and traditions.
Many modern theologians advocate for a more christocentric approach to Christian doctrine.

Related Terms

  • synonym: christological - relating to the study of the nature and work of Christ
  • antonym: theocentric - having God as the central focus
