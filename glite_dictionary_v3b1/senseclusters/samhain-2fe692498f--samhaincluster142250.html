Samhain

noun
neopagan harvest festival
Pronunciation: / ˈsaʊ.ɪn /; / ˈsaʊ.wɪn / (AmE), / ˈsaʊ.ɪn /; / ˈsaʊ.wɪn / (BrE)
a festival (ancient and modern) held around October 31 to mark the end of the harvest, the start of winter, and to honor the dead

Examples

Samhain festival
Many modern pagans celebrate Samhain by lighting candles and remembering loved ones who have passed away.
The traditions of Samhain include feasting, storytelling, and honoring ancestors.
Some people view Samhain as the spiritual new year in their religious calendar.
Many people celebrate Samhain with bonfires and costumes to honor the changing seasons.
During Samhain, it was believed that the boundary between the living and the dead became thinner.
Modern pagans often observe Samhain as a time to remember ancestors and lost loved ones.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Beltane - a Celtic festival marking the beginning of summer
  • synonym: Halloween - a holiday celebrated on October 31 with costumes and traditions
