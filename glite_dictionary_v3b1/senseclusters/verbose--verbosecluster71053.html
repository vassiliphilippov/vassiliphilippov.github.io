verbose

adjective
using too many words
9.9
Pronunciation: / vərˈboʊs / (AmE), / vɜːˈbəʊs / (BrE)
using or expressed in more words than are needed; wordy, especially in speech or writing.

Examples

A verbose report
Her verbose report could have been summarized in just a few paragraphs.
The professor's verbose lecture made it difficult for students to stay engaged.
Try to avoid verbose language and get straight to the point in your writing.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concise - expressing much in few words
  • synonym: wordy - using or expressed in too many words
