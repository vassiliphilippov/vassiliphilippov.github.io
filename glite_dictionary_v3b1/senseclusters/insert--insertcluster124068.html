Synonyms

  • introduce
    3.1 0.9/m
    introduce is often used to present ideas or people formally, while insert covers physical or casual placing.
  • stick in
    4.1 1/m
    stick in is used to add a casual comment into speech or writing, while insert is more neutral and general.
  • slip in
    4.4 0.6/m
    slip in means mention something quietly or subtly during talk, while insert is more plain and can be physical.
  • sneak in
    3.4 0.5/m
    sneak in means bring or put something in without being noticed, while insert does not imply secrecy or stealth.
  • stick in
    3.0 1/m
    stick in often means push something into another by force or fit it tightly, while insert is more general and neutral.
