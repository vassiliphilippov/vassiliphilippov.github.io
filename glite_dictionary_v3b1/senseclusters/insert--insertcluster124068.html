insert

verb
put into something else snugly
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈsɝːt /; / ɪnˈsɝt /; / ɪnˈsɜrt / (AmE), / ɪnˈsɜːt / (BrE)
to put or introduce something into something else, either physically or in a conversation, text, or situation

Examples

She managed to insert a clever joke into the conversation without anyone noticing.
The author would often insert personal anecdotes subtly into her essays.
