fibroblast

noun
cell that forms connective tissue fibers
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪ.brəˌblæst / (AmE), / ˈfʌɪ.brəʊˌblɑːst / (BrE)
a cell found in connective tissue that produces collagen and other fibers essential for tissue structure and repair

Examples

Fibroblast cells produce collagen
Fibroblast cells play a crucial role in wound healing by producing collagen to repair tissue.
Abnormal fibroblast proliferation can lead to excessive tissue fibrosis in certain diseases.
Researchers observed a rapid increase in fibroblast activity during the early stages of scar formation.
