Atlantic white cedar

noun
evergreen tree of the US east coast
Pronunciation: / ətˈlæntɪk waɪt ˈsiːdər / (AmE), / ətˈlæntɪk waɪt ˈsiːdə / (BrE)
A slow-growing, medium-sized evergreen tree native to the eastern coast of the United States, resembling the American arborvitae.

Examples

The Atlantic white cedar tree
Logging of Atlantic white cedars has significantly reduced their populations in some regions.
Scientists are studying the Atlantic white cedar to understand its role in sequestering carbon in coastal ecosystems.
The Atlantic white cedar thrives in wetland habitats along the eastern United States.
