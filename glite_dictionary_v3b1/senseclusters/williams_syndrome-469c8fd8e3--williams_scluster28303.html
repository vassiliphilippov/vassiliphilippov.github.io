Williams syndrome

noun
rare genetic disorder with distinctive features
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪljəmz ˌsɪnˌdroʊm / (AmE), / ˈwɪljəmz ˌsɪn.drəʊm / (BrE)
a rare congenital disorder caused by deletion of genetic material on chromosome 7, characterized by distinctive facial features, intellectual disability, and a highly social personality with strong verbal skills

Examples

Williams syndrome disorder
Children with Williams syndrome often display unusual friendliness and strong verbal abilities.
Researchers are studying the genetic deletions responsible for Williams syndrome to better understand its effects.
The distinctive facial features of Williams syndrome have been described as 'elfin'.
