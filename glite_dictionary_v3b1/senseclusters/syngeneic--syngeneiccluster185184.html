syngeneic

adjective
genetically identical
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪndʒəˈniːɪk / (AmE), / ˌsɪndʒəˈniːɪk / (BrE)
genetically identical or very similar, especially referring to individuals or tissues from the same species

Examples

Syngeneic bone marrow
A syngeneic bone marrow transplant is possible between identical twins.
Researchers used syngeneic mice to study immune responses without genetic differences.
The risk of organ rejection is very low in syngeneic transplants.

Related Terms

  • antonym: allogeneic - genetically different, especially referring to tissue from a donor of the same species
  • antonym: autologous - originating from the same individual, especially referring to tissue or cells
