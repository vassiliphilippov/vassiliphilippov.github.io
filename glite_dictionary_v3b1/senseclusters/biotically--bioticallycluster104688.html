biotically

adverb
by living organisms
7.5
Pronunciation: / baɪˈɑːtɪkli / (AmE), / baɪˈɒtɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that involves or is caused by living organisms or biological processes

Examples

Enriched biotically by plants
The soil was enriched biotically through the decomposition of plant material.
Some minerals are formed biotically by the actions of bacteria and algae.
The river's ecosystem changed biotically after the introduction of new fish species.
The forest is biotically diverse, with many different species living together.
Coral reefs are biotically complex ecosystems that support a wide range of marine life.
The soil was biotically enriched by the presence of earthworms and microorganisms.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abiotically - in a manner not involving living organisms
  • synonym: biologically - in a way that relates to biology or living things
