segmentally

adverb
by speech segments
Pronunciation: / sɛɡˈmɛntəli / (AmE), / sɛɡˈmɛntəli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to individual speech sounds, such as consonants and vowels, rather than to features like intonation or stress

Examples

Segmentally analyze sounds
The linguist examined the word segmentally to identify each individual sound.
Phonological rules can be applied segmentally to analyze how consonants and vowels change.
Instead of looking at stress patterns, the teacher focused segmentally on the pronunciation of each letter.

Related Terms

  • antonym: suprasegmentally - in a way that relates to features like intonation or stress
