Levi

proper noun
New Testament disciple of Jesus
Pronunciation: / ˈliːvaɪ / (AmE), / ˈliːvaɪ / (BrE)
(in the New Testament) a disciple of Jesus, traditionally identified as the author of the first Gospel (the Gospel of Matthew).

Examples

Levi the disciple
Levi was called by Jesus while sitting at the tax collector's booth.
In the Gospel accounts, Levi later became known as Matthew.
Many scholars debate the identification of Levi with the apostle Matthew.
