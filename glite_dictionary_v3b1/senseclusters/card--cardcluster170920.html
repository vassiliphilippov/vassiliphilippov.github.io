card

noun
identification document
Pronunciation: / kɑrd / (AmE), / kɑːd / (BrE)
a small, usually rectangular, piece of paper, cardboard, or plastic used for playing games, sending messages, identification, payment, or as a computer component

Examples

A credit card
He reached into his wallet to show his university card at the library desk.
The security guard asked to see her card before allowing entry to the building.
When you pick up your badge, be sure to bring another form of card with you.
He always leaves a small card to let people know he's visited their office.
She found a card on her desk from a colleague who had stopped by earlier.
The doctor left his card with the receptionist after making a house call.
Many laptops do not have slots for additional cards.
My old computer couldn't run the program until I installed a new graphics card.
The sound card stopped working, so I had to replace it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cardboard - a stiff, thick paper material
  • synonym: credit card - a card that lets the holder borrow money up to an agreed limit to pay for goods and services
  • synonym: expansion card - a printed circuit board inserted into a computer to enhance its capabilities
  • synonym: greeting card - a card bearing a message of greeting, often sent on special occasions
  • synonym: identification card - a document that proves a person's identity
  • degree lower: paper - material manufactured in thin sheets
  • synonym: passport - an official document issued for international travel and identity
  • synonym: playing card - a card used as part of a set in card games
  • degree lower: postcard - a card for sending messages by mail without an envelope
  • synonym: virtual card - a digital payment card created for online or mobile payments and stored in an app or wallet
