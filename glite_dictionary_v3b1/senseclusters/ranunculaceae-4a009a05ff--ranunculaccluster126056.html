Ranunculaceae

noun
family of flowering plants (buttercup family)
Pronunciation: / rəˌnʌŋkjəˈleɪsiˌi / (AmE), / rəˌnʌŋkjʊˈleɪsiːˌiː / (BrE)
a large family of flowering plants, commonly known as the buttercup family, which includes numerous genera such as Ranunculus, Clematis, and Anemone

Examples

The Ranunculaceae family
Biologists study the Ranunculaceae to understand evolutionary relationships among plants.
Some species in the Ranunculaceae are known for their medicinal properties as well as their toxicity.
The Ranunculaceae include many familiar garden and wild flowers, such as buttercups and clematis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: buttercup - any of various species in the genus Ranunculus, members of the Ranunculaceae family
