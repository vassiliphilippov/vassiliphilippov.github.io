makeshift

adjective
temporary substitute using available materials
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪkˌʃɪft / (AmE), / ˈmeɪkʃɪft / (BrE)
made or used as a temporary substitute with whatever is at hand, often because nothing better is available

Examples

Makeshift shelter
After the tent collapsed, we built a makeshift shelter out of branches and blankets.
The doctor used a makeshift splint to stabilize the man's injured leg until help arrived.
We had to eat dinner on a makeshift table made from cardboard boxes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: improvised - created or done without preparation, using what is available
