bawl

verb
cry or shout loudly
4.3
Pronunciation: / bɑl /; / bɔl / (AmE), / bɔːl / (BrE)
to cry or shout very loudly and without restraint

Examples

To bawl loudly
Please don't bawl in the store; let's talk calmly outside.
She couldn't help but bawl at the sad ending of the movie.
The child began to bawl the moment his toy was taken away.
Fans would bawl their support from the stands during every game.
She could hear someone bawling in the hallway late at night.
The toddler began to bawl when his toy was taken away.
She bawled his name across the crowded room so he could hear her.
Stop bawling and listen to what I have to say.
The coach bawled at the players for not following instructions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shout - to say something very loudly
  • synonym: sob - cry noisily, making loud, convulsive gasps
  • synonym: wail - cry with a long, loud sound as of grief or pain
  • antonym: whisper - speak very softly, using breath rather than the vocal cords
  • synonym: yell - to shout loudly
