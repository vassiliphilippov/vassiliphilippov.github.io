photoconduction

noun
increase in conductivity due to light absorption
1.6 8.2
Pronunciation: /foʊ.toʊ.kən.ˈdʌk.ʃən/ (AmE), /fəʊ.təʊ.kən.ˈdʌk.ʃən/ (BrE)
an increase in the electrical conductivity of a material caused by the absorption of electromagnetic radiation, such as light

Examples

Photoconduction effect
Researchers measured the effect of ultraviolet light on the photoconduction of the new material.
The photoconduction of certain crystals is used in light-sensitive electronic devices.
Understanding photoconduction helps in the design of solar cells and photodetectors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: photoconductivity - the electrical conductivity of a substance when exposed to light
