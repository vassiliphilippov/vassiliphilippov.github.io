burning at the stake

noun
execution by being burned alive at a stake
3.3 8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈbɜːr.nɪŋ ət ðə steɪk/ /; / /ˈbɜːr.nɪŋ æt ðiː steɪk/ (AmE), /ˈbɜː.nɪŋ ət ðə steɪk/ /; / /ˈbɜː.nɪŋ æt ðiː steɪk/ (BrE)
a method of execution in which a person is tied to a stake and burned alive, historically used as punishment for crimes such as heresy or witchcraft

Examples

Burning at the stake punishment
In the Middle Ages, many were sentenced to burning at the stake for accusations of heresy.
Joan of Arc was famously executed by burning at the stake in 1431.
The film depicted the terror of burning at the stake during the witch trials.
