pluckily

adverb
in a courageous and determined manner
8.1 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆplÊŒkÉªli / (AmE), / ËˆplÊŒkÉªli / (BrE)
In a way that shows courage and determination, especially in facing difficulties.

Examples

To pluckily face challenges
Despite the odds against her, she pluckily completed the marathon.
He pluckily defended his position against harsh criticism from the audience.
The small dog pluckily stood its ground against the much larger animal.

Synonyms

  • bravely
    1.7 2.74/m
    bravely means acting with courage in dangerous situations, while pluckily adds a small, determined, spirited quality when facing trouble.
  • courageously
    3.8 0.92/m
    courageously shows steady moral or physical courage in hard moments, while pluckily highlights lively determination in small struggles.
  • valiantly
    5.5 0.85/m
    valiantly suggests great, often public or heroic courage, while pluckily implies brave, spirited effort in everyday difficulties.
  • fearlessly
    3.9 0.69/m
    fearlessly means acting without fear and with boldness, while pluckily means brave but often still feeling some fear and determination.
  • dauntlessly
    8.0 0.01/m
    dauntlessly means facing danger without fear and with boldness, while pluckily suggests gritty, determined courage despite difficulties.
