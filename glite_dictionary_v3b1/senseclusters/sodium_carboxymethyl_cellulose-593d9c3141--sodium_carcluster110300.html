sodium carboxymethyl cellulose

noun
thickening/emulsifying chemical agent
7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈsoʊ.diː.əm kɑːr.bɑːk.siː.ˈmeθ.əl ˈse.ljə.loʊs/ (AmE), /ˈsəʊ.dɪ.əm kɑː.bɒk.sɪ.ˈmeθ.əl ˈse.l.jʊ.ləʊz/ (BrE)
a white, gummy powder that is the sodium salt of carboxymethyl cellulose, used chiefly as a thickening or emulsifying agent in food, pharmaceuticals, and other products

Examples

Sodium carboxymethyl cellulose thickening agent
Sodium carboxymethyl cellulose is commonly added to ice cream to improve texture and prevent ice crystal formation.
Many gluten-free recipes include sodium carboxymethyl cellulose to replicate the binding effect of gluten.
The pharmaceutical company uses sodium carboxymethyl cellulose as a tablet binder in many of its products.
