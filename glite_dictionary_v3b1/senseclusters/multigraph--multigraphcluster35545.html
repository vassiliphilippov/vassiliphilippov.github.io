multigraph

noun
letter group for one sound
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌltɪˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈmʌltɪˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
a sequence of two or more letters that together represent a single sound in a language's writing system

Examples

The 'sch' multigraph
In the word 'scholar', the letters 'sch' form a multigraph representing a single sound.
Linguists study how a multigraph can simplify the spelling of certain words.
The German language uses the multigraph 'sch' to represent the 'sh' sound.

Related Terms

  • synonym: digraph - a pair of letters representing a single sound
  • synonym: trigraph - a group of three letters representing a single sound
