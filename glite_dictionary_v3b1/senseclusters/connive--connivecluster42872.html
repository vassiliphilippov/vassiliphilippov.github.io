connive

verb
secretly allow wrongdoing
4.3 7.0
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈnaɪv/ (AmE), /kə.ˈnaɪv/ (BrE)
to secretly allow, support, or take part in something wrong, dishonest, or illegal

Examples

To connive with criminals
By staying silent, they connived with the criminal enterprise.
She refused to connive at her colleague's illegal activities.
The officials were accused of having connived in the fraud that cost taxpayers millions.
He would never connive in such a dishonest scheme, even if it benefited him.
Several officials were discovered to connive with the smugglers in exchange for bribes.
She accused her coworkers of trying to connive against her for the management position.

Related Terms

  • antonym: betray - to be disloyal or unfaithful; act against someone's trust
  • synonym: collude - to cooperate secretly for an illegal or deceitful purpose
  • synonym: condone - to accept or allow morally wrong or offensive behavior
  • antonym: oppose - to act against or resist
  • synonym: plot - to make secret plans to carry out something illegal or harmful
