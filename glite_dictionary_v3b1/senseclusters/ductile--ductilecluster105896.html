ductile

adjective
easily influenced or persuaded
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌktaɪl / (AmE), / ˈdʌktaɪl / (BrE)
(of a person or character) easily influenced or led; yielding readily to influence or persuasion.

Examples

Ductile young mind
Children can be quite ductile when exposed to strong role models.
She was a ductile student, always willing to accept guidance from her mentors.
The politician found the young advisor to be especially ductile in adopting his viewpoints.

Related Terms

  • synonym: malleable - (of a person) easily influenced; pliable
  • synonym: pliant - easily influenced or directed; yielding
  • antonym: stubborn - refusing to change one's mind or to be influenced
