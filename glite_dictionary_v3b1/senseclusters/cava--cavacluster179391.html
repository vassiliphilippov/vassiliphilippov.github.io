cava

noun
major heart vein
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːvə / (AmE), / ˈkɑːvə / (BrE)
either of the two large veins, the superior or inferior vena cava, that carry deoxygenated blood to the heart

Examples

Inferior cava
The surgeon carefully inspected the cava to ensure there were no signs of blockage before closing.
A blood clot in the cava can cause serious health problems if not treated quickly.
Doctors monitored the patient's cava during the heart surgery to check blood flow.

Related Terms

  • antonym: artery - a blood vessel that carries blood away from the heart
  • synonym: vena cava - either of the two main veins returning blood to the heart
