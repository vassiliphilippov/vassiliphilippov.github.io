glycopeptide

noun
peptide-sugar molecule
Pronunciation: / ˌɡlaɪkoʊˈpɛptaɪd / (AmE), / ˌɡlaɪkəʊˈpɛptaɪd / (BrE)
a molecule consisting of a peptide linked to a sugar, often used as an antibiotic in medicine

Examples

A glycopeptide antibiotic
The doctor prescribed a glycopeptide to treat the resistant bacterial infection.
Researchers are developing new glycopeptide antibiotics to combat hospital-acquired infections.
A glycopeptide can be effective when other antibiotics fail to work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antibiotic - a substance that kills or inhibits the growth of bacteria
  • synonym: peptide - a compound consisting of two or more amino acids linked in a chain
