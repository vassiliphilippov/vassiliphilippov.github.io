prickish

adjective
rude or mean
Pronunciation: / ˈprɪkɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈprɪkɪʃ / (BrE)
behaving in a rude, mean, or easily irritated way; quick to take offense

Examples

Prickish behavior
His prickish attitude made it hard for anyone to work with him.
She thought his prickish remarks were completely unnecessary during the meeting.
Don't be so prickish just because things didn't go your way.
He can be quite prickish when people joke about his work.
Her prickish attitude made it hard for others to give her feedback.
Try not to sound prickish if someone offers you advice.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jerkish - acting in an unpleasant or inconsiderate way
  • antonym: kind - friendly and considerate in behavior
  • synonym: sensitive - quick to take offense or be affected by criticism
  • antonym: thick-skinned - not easily offended or upset
  • synonym: touchy - easily upset or offended
