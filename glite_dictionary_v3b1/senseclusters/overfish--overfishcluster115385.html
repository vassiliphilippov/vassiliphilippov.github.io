overfish

verb
to catch too many fish
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈfɪʃ / (BrE)
to catch too many fish in an area, causing harm to the fish population

Examples

To overfish the sea
If we overfish these waters, there may be no seafood left for future generations.
Many coastal regions have been overfished because of a lack of regulations.
Strict laws are needed to prevent companies from overfishing endangered species.
If we overfish these waters, there will be no fish left for future generations.
Many species are endangered because people continue to overfish in their habitats.
Strict regulations were introduced to prevent companies from overfishing the coastal areas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deplete - to use up or reduce something so that there is less left
