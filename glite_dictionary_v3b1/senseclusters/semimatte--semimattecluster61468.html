semimatte

noun
partly matte finish
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmiˈmæt / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈmæt / (BrE)
a finish or surface that is partly matte and partly shiny, offering a balance between gloss and dullness

Examples

Semimatte surface
The artist chose a semimatte varnish to protect the painting without too much shine.
Many photographers prefer semimatte paper for prints because it reduces glare but still shows vibrant colors.
The new smartphone has a semimatte finish that resists fingerprints and looks elegant.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: glossy - a finish that is very shiny and reflective
  • degree lower: matte - a finish that is completely dull and not shiny
