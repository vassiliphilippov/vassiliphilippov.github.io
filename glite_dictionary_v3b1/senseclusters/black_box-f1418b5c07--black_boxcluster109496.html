black box

noun
system with hidden internal workings
3.9
Pronunciation: /blæk bɑːks/ /; / /blæk bɔːks/ (AmE), /blæk bɒks/ (BrE)
a system, model, or process whose internal workings are hidden, unknown, or not examined, so only its inputs and outputs are observed; often used in computing, AI, and engineering.

Examples

AI black box
Many AI models are treated as a black box because their internal reasoning is not transparent.
Regulators demanded explanations instead of relying on the bank's algorithmic black box for loan approvals.
Engineers prefer white-box testing when possible rather than treating the control system as a black box.

Related Terms

  • synonym: opaque system - a system whose internal processes are not visible or understandable
  • antonym: white box - a system or model whose internal structure and processes are visible and can be examined
