Free World

noun
anti-communist countries, esp. Cold War era
6.2
Pronunciation: / fri wɜrld / (AmE), / friː wɜːld / (BrE)
The countries with non-communist governments, especially those opposed to the Soviet Union and its allies during the Cold War.

Examples

The Free World countries
During the Cold War, the United States claimed to lead the Free World against the spread of communism.
Many people hoped the arrival of democracy would bring their country into the Free World.
The leaders of the Free World met to discuss strategies to strengthen alliances.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Communist Bloc - The group of communist countries led by the Soviet Union during the Cold War
