MPC

noun
marginal propensity to consume
8.9
Pronunciation: /em piː siː/ /; / /ˈem.piː.siː/ (AmE), /em piː siː/ /; / /ˈem.piː.siː/ (BrE)
the fraction of additional income that a household is likely to spend rather than save, known as marginal propensity to consume

Examples

Household MPC rate
Economists often calculate the MPC to understand how changes in income affect consumer spending.
A higher MPC means that people are likely to spend more of any extra money they receive.
The government considered the country's average MPC when designing its stimulus package.

Related Terms

  • synonym: APC - average propensity to consume; the proportion of total income spent on consumption
  • antonym: MPS - marginal propensity to save; the fraction of additional income that is saved
