interdict

noun
church censure withdrawing sacraments
Pronunciation: / ˈɪntərˌdɪkt / (AmE), / ˈɪntədɪkt / (BrE)
An ecclesiastical censure by the Roman Catholic Church that withdraws certain sacraments and Christian burial from a person or from all persons in a specific area.

Examples

Church interdict penalty
During the Middle Ages, kings would sometimes use political pressure to have the interdict lifted.
The pope placed the entire kingdom under an interdict after the conflict with the king.
The villagers feared the consequences of the interdict imposed on their parish.

Related Terms

  • synonym: excommunication - official exclusion from participation in the sacraments and services of the Christian Church
