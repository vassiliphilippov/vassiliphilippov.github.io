bucker

noun
one who resists authority
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌkər / (AmE), / ˈbʌkə / (BrE)
a person who openly resists or opposes authority, rules, or accepted ideas

Examples

A rule bucker
As a known bucker, he challenged the committee's rules at every meeting.
The new manager was a bucker who questioned every company policy.
She earned a reputation as a bucker for refusing to follow outdated traditions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conformist - a person who accepts and follows established rules or customs
  • synonym: rebel - a person who resists authority or control
