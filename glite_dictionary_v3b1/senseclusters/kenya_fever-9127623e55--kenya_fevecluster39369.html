Kenya fever

noun
tick-borne rickettsial disease
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈken.jə ˈfiː.vər/ /; / /ˈkiːn.jə ˈfiː.vər/ (AmE), /ˈken.jə ˈfiː.və/ /; / /ˈkiːn.jə ˈfiː.və/ (BrE)
A disease caused by a rickettsia, transmitted to humans by a reddish-brown tick found on dogs and other mammals, mainly in India and the Mediterranean region.

Examples

Kenya fever disease
Kenya fever is most commonly transmitted through the bite of an infected tick.
After traveling with his dog in rural India, he was diagnosed with Kenya fever.
Doctors in the Mediterranean region are trained to recognize the symptoms of Kenya fever.
