low brass

noun
brass alloy with ≤30% zinc
Pronunciation: / loʊ bræs / (AmE), / ləʊ brɑːs / (BrE)
A type of brass alloy that contains 30% or less zinc.

Examples

Low brass alloy
Engineers recommended using low brass for the components exposed to high temperatures.
Musical instruments are rarely made from low brass due to its specific metallurgical properties.
The pipes were manufactured from low brass to increase their resistance to corrosion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: high brass - brass alloy with a high percentage of zinc (often around 35% or more)
