swamp blueberry

noun
shrubs with edible blueberry-like berries
7.5
Pronunciation: / swɑmp ˈbluːˌbɛri / (AmE), / swɒmp ˈbluːˌbɛri / (BrE)
A high-growing deciduous shrub of eastern North America that produces edible bluish to blackish berries; an important source of cultivated blueberries.

Examples

The swamp blueberry plant
Many cultivated varieties of blueberries can be traced back to the swamp blueberry.
The swamp blueberry thrives in moist, acidic soils found throughout eastern North America.
Wildlife often feed on the swamp blueberry during the late summer months.
