hai

interjection
Japanese 'yes' interjection
Pronunciation: / haɪ / (AmE), / haɪ / (BrE)
an interjection borrowed from Japanese used to mean 'yes', 'I agree', or 'I understand', often when quoting or imitating Japanese speech

Examples

She replied hai
When asked if she understood the instructions, she replied with a clear hai.
He raised his hand and said hai to show he agreed with the plan.
In the movie, the character answered the command with a respectful hai.

Related Terms

  • antonym: no - a negative reply or refusal
  • synonym: yes - an affirmative reply or agreement
