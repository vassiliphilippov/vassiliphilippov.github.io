quipu

noun
ancient Inca record-keeping device
Pronunciation: / ˈkiːpuː / (AmE), / ˈkiːpuː / (BrE)
An ancient Inca device for recording information, consisting of a main cord with variously colored knotted strings attached, used for keeping records and performing calculations.

Examples

An ancient quipu device
During the presentation, the archaeologist explained how a quipu was used to document census data.
Researchers study the patterns of knots on the quipu to better understand Incan accounting methods.
The museum displayed a well-preserved quipu that once belonged to an Inca administrator.
