(Philosophy) The theory that reality is fundamentally based on the mind or ideas, and that physical objects do not exist independently of perception.

Examples

Philosophical idealism
German philosophers like Kant were significant contributors to the development of idealism.
In his lectures, the professor explained idealism as the belief that reality is constructed by the mind.
Some critics argue that idealism overlooks the importance of the physical world.

Synonyms

  • mentalism
    9.9 0.02/m

  • immaterialism
    9.9 0.01/m
    immaterialism teaches that only nonmaterial things truly exist, while idealism focuses on ideas and perception as reality's basis.
