flat-footed

adjective
having broad, flat feet
Pronunciation: / flæt ˈfʊtɪd / (AmE), / flæt ˈfʊtɪd / (BrE)
having broad, flat feet that typically turn outward

Examples

Flat-footed children
Children who are flat-footed may need special shoes for support.
Her flat-footed stance made her easy to recognize at dance rehearsals.
The old boxer walked with a distinctive flat-footed gait after years in the ring.

Related Terms

  • antonym: arched - having a high arch in the foot (opposite physical trait)
  • synonym: flatfooted - having broad, flat feet (alternative spelling)
