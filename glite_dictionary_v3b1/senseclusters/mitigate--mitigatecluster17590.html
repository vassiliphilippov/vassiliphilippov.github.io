mitigate

verb
make less severe or serious
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪt̬.ɪˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈmɪt.ɪ.ɡeɪt / (BrE)
to make something less severe, harmful, or painful; to reduce the bad effects of something

Examples

To mitigate the damage
Laws were introduced to mitigate the effects of industrial pollution.
New policies are needed to mitigate the impact of climate change on vulnerable communities.
Taking swift action can help mitigate the risks involved in this process.
Effective communication can help mitigate conflicts in the workplace.
The new policies were designed to mitigate the effects of climate change.
They took several measures to mitigate the risk of infection after the outbreak.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alleviate - make something less severe or burdensome
  • antonym: exacerbate - make a situation worse or more severe
