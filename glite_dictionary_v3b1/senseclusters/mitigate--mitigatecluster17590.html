to make something less severe, harmful, or painful; to reduce the bad effects of something

Examples

To mitigate the damage
Laws were introduced to mitigate the effects of industrial pollution.

Synonyms

  • alleviate
    5.3 8.12/m
    alleviate is used to make pain or problems easier to bear, while mitigate focuses on reducing the bad effects.
  • assuage
    7.7 1.80/m
    assuage eases unpleasant feelings or pain directly, while mitigate refers to reducing harm or severity in a broader situation.
  • lessen
    2.8 4.32/m
    lessen simply makes an amount or effect smaller, while mitigate implies careful action to reduce harm or severity.
  • ameliorate
    8.1 1.96/m
    ameliorate means make a bad situation better overall, while mitigate focuses on lowering specific harm or negative effects.
  • abate
    6.8 2.85/m
    abate means become or make something less intense, while mitigate is used to reduce harm or bad effects.
  • attenuate
    8.2 0.00/m
    attenuate describes something losing strength or being weakened, while mitigate describes actions that reduce harm or severity.
  • palliate
    8.8 0.01/m
    palliate eases symptoms or makes a problem seem less serious without fixing the cause, while mitigate aims to reduce harm.
  • counteract
    4.8 3.98/m
    counteract means act directly against something to cancel or neutralize it, while mitigate means lessen its harmful effects.
  • countervail
    9.3 0.87/m
