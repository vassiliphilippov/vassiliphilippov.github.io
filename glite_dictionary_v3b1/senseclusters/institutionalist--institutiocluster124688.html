institutionalist

noun
supporter of institutions
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.stɪˈtuː.ʃən.əl.ɪst /; / ˌɪnstɪˈtuːʃənəlɪst / (AmE), / ˌɪn.stɪˈtjuː.ʃən.əl.ɪst /; / ˌɪnstɪˈtjuːʃənəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who studies, supports, or emphasizes the importance or role of institutions in society or economics

Examples

An institutionalist view
The institutionalist perspective highlights the role of laws and traditions in shaping economic systems.
As an institutionalist, she believes that social organizations are key to understanding political change.
Many institutionalists argue that education systems influence national development.
As an institutionalist, she believes that laws and customs shape economic outcomes.
The conference brought together many leading institutionalists from around the world.
An institutionalist might focus on how education systems influence social mobility.

Related Terms

  • antonym: individualist - a person who emphasizes individual freedom and self-reliance
  • synonym: structuralist - a person who analyzes society or culture in terms of structures
