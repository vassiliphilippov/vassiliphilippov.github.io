MEDLARS

proper noun
biomedical bibliographic database (US NLM)
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛdˌlɑrz / (AmE), / ˈmɛdˌlɑːz / (BrE)
a computerized bibliographic database developed by the United States National Library of Medicine for storing and retrieving biomedical literature references

Examples

MEDLARS database
Before PubMed, many libraries accessed MEDLARS to obtain comprehensive bibliographic data on biomedical topics.
Researchers relied on MEDLARS to find articles related to emerging medical challenges.
The early success of MEDLARS paved the way for other digital information systems in the health sciences.
