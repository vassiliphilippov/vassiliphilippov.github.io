cave

verb
to hollow out as if making a cave
5.0
Pronunciation: / keɪv / (AmE), / keɪv / (BrE)
(of a force such as water or wind) to hollow out something as if forming a cave or opening.

Examples

Waves cave cliffs
Over centuries, the relentless ocean waves caved the soft limestone cliffs.
Strong winds cave the rock, shaping the dramatic formations found in the desert.
The flowing river gradually began caving the banks, widening its course each year.
