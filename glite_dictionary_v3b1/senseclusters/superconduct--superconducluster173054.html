superconduct

verb
to make superconducting
8.2
Pronunciation: /suː.pər.kən.ˈdʌkt/ (AmE), /suː.pə.kən.ˈdʌkt/ (BrE)
to (cause to) have zero electrical resistance at very low temperatures, so that electric current can flow without loss

Examples

To superconduct metal
Scientists managed to superconduct the metal by cooling it with liquid helium.
The experiment failed because the material would not superconduct at the expected temperature.
Researchers are searching for new ways to superconduct materials at higher temperatures.
Some metals can superconduct only when they are cooled to extremely low temperatures.
Scientists are searching for materials that superconduct at higher temperatures for practical applications.
When cooled below its critical temperature, the material began to superconduct electricity perfectly.

  • antonym: insulate - to prevent the passage of electricity
  • synonym: supercool - to cool a substance below its normal freezing point without it becoming solid
