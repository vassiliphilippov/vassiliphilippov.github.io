beta-interferon

noun
type of interferon used in MS treatment
Pronunciation: / ˌbeɪtə ˌɪntərˈfɪrɑn / (AmE), / ˌbiːtə ˌɪntəˈfɪərɒn / (BrE)
a type of interferon produced by fibroblasts, used to treat multiple sclerosis and with antiviral properties

Examples

Beta-interferon treatment
Recent studies have explored the antiviral effectiveness of beta-interferon against various viral infections.
The doctor prescribed beta-interferon injections to help control the progression of MS symptoms.
Treatment with beta-interferon can reduce the frequency of relapses in multiple sclerosis patients.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interferon - a group of proteins produced in response to viruses
