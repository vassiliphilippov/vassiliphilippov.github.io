brokerage firm

noun
company acting as intermediary for securities trades
4.1 0.86/m
Pronunciation: / Ààbro äk…ôr…™d í f…ùm / (AmE), / Ààbr…ô äk…ôr…™d í f…úÀêm / (BrE)
A company that acts as an intermediary between buyers and sellers of financial securities, typically charging a fee for its services.

Examples

A brokerage firm company
Many people rely on a brokerage firm for advice and investment management.
She opened an account at a well-known brokerage firm to start investing in stocks.
The brokerage firm charges a commission on every trade executed for its clients.

Synonyms

  • brokerage
    5.8 3.12/m
    brokerage can mean a fee or the company that arranges trades for commission, while brokerage firm always means the company.
  • brokerage house
    4.5 0.44/m
    brokerage house is an older or formal name for a firm that buys and sells stocks, while brokerage firm is the common modern business term.
