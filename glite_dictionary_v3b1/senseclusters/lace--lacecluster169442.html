lace

verb
intertwine or twist together
3.9 3.2
Pronunciation: /leɪs/ (AmE), /leɪs/ (BrE)
to intertwine, braid, or make lace by twisting threads or materials together

Examples

To lace threads
He watched as they laced the colored threads together to make a bracelet.
She carefully laced the ribbons through the braids in her daughter's hair.
The vines had laced themselves around the old wooden fence.
It takes skill to lace delicate patterns for bridal veils.
Many artisans still lace fabrics using traditional techniques passed down through generations.
She spent hours lacing the intricate tablecloth by hand.
He learned to lace from his grandmother, who was a renowned craftswoman.
She spent her afternoons lacing delicate patterns onto fine linen.
The women were lacing at the market, selling their handmade goods.
