intermarry

verb
marry within or between groups
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈmæri / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈmæri / (BrE)
To marry within the same ethnic, social, or family group, or to marry between different such groups.

Examples

To intermarry groups
Historically, royal families often intermarried to strengthen political alliances.
In some cultures, families prefer to intermarry to maintain traditions and values.
The two communities began to intermarry after living side by side for decades.

Related Terms

  • antonym: outmarry - to marry outside one’s group
