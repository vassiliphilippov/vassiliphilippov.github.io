stay

verb
to stop or halt (formal)
4.7 4.3
Pronunciation: /steɪ/ (AmE), /steɪ/ (BrE)
to stop, halt, or suspend something, especially by formal or legal order

Examples

To stay an order
Emergency medical teams worked tirelessly to stay the outbreak before it spread further.
The government decided to stay the execution of the new law until further notice.
The judge issued an order to stay the proceedings pending more evidence.
A higher court may stay the execution of a sentence pending appeal.
The court can stay an order if there are compelling reasons to do so.
The judge decided to stay the proceedings due to new evidence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: continue - to persist in an activity or process
  • synonym: halt - to bring or come to an abrupt stop
  • antonym: resume - to begin again or continue after a pause or interruption
  • synonym: suspend - to temporarily prevent from continuing or being in force or effect
