sacrificial lamb

noun
person sacrificed for others' benefit
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˌsækrɪˈfɪʃəl læm / (AmE), / ˌsækrɪˈfɪʃl læm / (BrE)
a person who is sacrificed or made to suffer for the benefit or interests of others, often unfairly or to protect others

Examples

The sacrificial lamb blamed
After the company scandal, he was made the sacrificial lamb to appease the public.
She knew she was just a sacrificial lamb in the negotiations and would take the blame if things went wrong.
The coach acted as the sacrificial lamb, taking responsibility so the team's morale would stay high.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scapegoat - a person who is blamed for the wrongdoings, mistakes, or faults of others
