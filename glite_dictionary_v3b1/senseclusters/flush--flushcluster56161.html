flush

verb
clean or rinse with a liquid
Pronunciation: / flʌʃ / (AmE), / flʌʃ / (BrE)
to clean or rinse something by causing a flow of liquid through or over it

Examples

To flush a wound
After cutting his hand, the doctor told him to flush the wound with sterile water.
She always flushes the pipes before using them for drinking water.
You should flush out the radiator to remove any contamination.
After each experiment, the technician would flush the beakers to remove any residue.
Heavy rains helped flush the chemicals from the soil.
Please flush the pipes with clean water before using them.
After cleaning the aquarium, she flushed the dirty water down the drain.
Please flush the old chemicals out of the pipes before using them again.
You should always flush the system with fresh water after each use.
