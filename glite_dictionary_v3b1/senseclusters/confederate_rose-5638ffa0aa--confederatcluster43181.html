Confederate rose

noun
Chinese shrub with color-changing flowers
9.4
Pronunciation: / kənˈfɛdərət roʊz / (AmE), / kənˈfɛdərət rəʊz / (BrE)
a shrub or small tree (Hibiscus mutabilis) native to China, with large white or pink flowers that become deep red as they age, widely cultivated and naturalized in the southeastern United States

Examples

A Confederate rose flower
A mature Confederate rose can reach up to fifteen feet in height.
Gardeners love the Confederate rose for its dramatic color-changing blossoms.
The Confederate rose blooms in late summer, adding vibrant colors to Southern gardens.
