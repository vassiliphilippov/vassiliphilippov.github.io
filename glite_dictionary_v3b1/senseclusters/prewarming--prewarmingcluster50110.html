prewarming

noun
warming in advance
Pronunciation: / priːˈwɔrmɪŋ / (AmE), / priːˈwɔːmɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of warming something in advance, especially before a procedure or event

Examples

Prewarming before surgery
The nurse recommended prewarming the patient to prevent chills during the operation.
Proper prewarming of equipment can improve the safety of certain laboratory procedures.
In cold weather, prewarming your car engine can help it run more smoothly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cooling - the process of making something colder
  • synonym: preheat - to heat something beforehand, especially an oven
