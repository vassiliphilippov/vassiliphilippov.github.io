arranging

noun
the adaptation of a musical composition
Pronunciation: / əˈreɪndʒɪŋ / (AmE), / əˈreɪndʒɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of adapting and organizing a piece of music, typically for instruments or voices different from the original composition

Examples

Musical arranging work
After several weeks of arranging, the song was ready for the orchestra to perform.
She made a name for herself in the jazz world through her innovative arranging.
The arranging of classic tunes for modern bands requires both creativity and technical skill.
