A medical term for Parkinson's disease, a progressive neurological disorder marked by tremor and impaired muscle coordination.

Examples

Paralysis agitans disease
In older medical texts, paralysis agitans is frequently used instead of Parkinson's disease.
The patient was diagnosed with paralysis agitans after exhibiting persistent tremors and rigidity.
Treatment options for paralysis agitans have evolved significantly since it was first described in the 19th century.

Synonyms

  • Parkinson's disease
    3.1 1.18/m
    Parkinson's disease is the common medical name used in hospitals and books, while paralysis agitans is an older technical term.
  • Parkinson's
    4.3 0.00/m
    Parkinson's is the informal, shorter name used in speech and news, while paralysis agitans is a formal medical term.
  • Parkinson's syndrome
    3.5 0.01/m
    Parkinson's syndrome describes Parkinson-like symptoms from other causes, while paralysis agitans refers specifically to classic Parkinson's disease.
