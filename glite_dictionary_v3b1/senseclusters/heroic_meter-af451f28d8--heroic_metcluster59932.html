heroic meter

noun
metrical verse form for heroic poetry
Pronunciation: / həˈroʊɪk ˈmiːtər / (AmE), / hɪˈrəʊɪk ˈmiːtə / (BrE)
In poetry, a traditional verse form used for writing about heroic or elevated subjects, typically dactylic hexameter in classical poetry or iambic pentameter in English.

Examples

Heroic meter in poetry
Learning about heroic meter helps students appreciate the structure of classical poetry.
Many epic poems in Greek literature are composed in heroic meter.
Shakespeare frequently wrote his plays in heroic meter to convey grandeur and drama.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dactylic hexameter - a metrical pattern of six dactyls per line, often used in epic poetry
  • synonym: iambic pentameter - a metrical pattern of five iambs per line, common in English heroic verse
