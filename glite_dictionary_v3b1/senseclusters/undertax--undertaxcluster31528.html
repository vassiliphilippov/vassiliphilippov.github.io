undertax

verb
to tax too little
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈtæks / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈtæks / (BrE)
to tax at a rate lower than is appropriate, required, or fair

Examples

To undertax wealthy individuals
Critics argue that the new policy will undertax wealthy individuals compared to the middle class.
Some countries are accused of undertaxing multinational corporations to attract business.
The report found that the city had been undertaxing certain properties for years.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overtax - to tax at a rate higher than is reasonable or fair
  • synonym: tax - to impose a financial charge or levy by authority
