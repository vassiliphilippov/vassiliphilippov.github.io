Thea

proper noun
Titaness; mother of Helios, Selene, and Eos in Greek myth
Pronunciation: / ˈθiə / (AmE), / ˈθiːə / (BrE)
(in Greek mythology) a Titaness, mother of Helios (the Sun), Selene (the Moon), and Eos (the Dawn).

Examples

Thea in mythology
According to ancient texts, Thea and Hyperion were the parents of Helios, Selene, and Eos.
In Greek mythology, Thea was revered as the mother of the sun, moon, and dawn.
The temple was dedicated to Thea, honoring her role as a primordial goddess.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Hyperion - a Titan, consort of Thea and father of Helios, Selene, and Eos
  • synonym: Rhea - another Titaness, sister to Thea in Greek mythology
