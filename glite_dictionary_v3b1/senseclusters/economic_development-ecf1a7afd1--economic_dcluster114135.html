economic development

noun
improvement of economic and social well-being
1.1 2.9
Pronunciation: /ek.ə.ˈnɑː.mɪk dɪ.ˈvel.əp.mənt/ /; / /iː.kə.ˈnɑː.mɪk dɪ.ˈvel.əp.mənt/ (AmE), /ek.ə.ˈnɒm.ɪk dɪ.ˈvel.əp.mənt/ /; / /iː.kə.ˈnɒm.ɪk dɪ.ˈvel.əp.mənt/ (BrE)
the process by which a community, region, or country improves the economic, political, and social well-being of its people, often through sustained policy actions and investments

Examples

Economic development projects
International organizations provide funding and support for economic development projects in developing countries.
Successful economic development often requires investment in education and infrastructure.
The government launched new initiatives to encourage economic development in rural areas.
