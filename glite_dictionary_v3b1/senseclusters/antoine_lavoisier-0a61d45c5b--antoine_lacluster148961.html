Antoine Lavoisier

proper noun
pioneering French chemist (1743–1794)
8.3
Pronunciation: /æn.ˈtwɑːn lə.vɔɪ.ˈzjeɪ/ (AmE), /æn.ˈtwɑːn lə.vɔɪ.ˈzjeɪ/ (BrE)
(1743–1794) A French chemist regarded as the father of modern chemistry, known for discovering oxygen and disproving the phlogiston theory.

Examples

Antoine Lavoisier discovered oxygen
In school, we learned how Antoine Lavoisier identified and named oxygen.
Many regard Antoine Lavoisier as the founder of modern chemistry due to his groundbreaking discoveries.
The experiments conducted by Antoine Lavoisier forever changed scientific thought about combustion.
