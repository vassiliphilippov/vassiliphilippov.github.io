Anabaena

proper noun
cyanobacteria genus
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌænəˈbiːnə / (AmE), / ˌænəˈbiːnə / (BrE)
a genus of filamentous cyanobacteria found in water, known for forming colonies, fixing nitrogen, and sometimes living in symbiosis with aquatic plants

Examples

Anabaena colonies in water
Researchers discovered a new species of Anabaena in the freshwater lake.
The ability of Anabaena to fix nitrogen makes it important for aquatic ecosystems.
Under the microscope, the Anabaena filaments appeared as chains of round cells.
Scientists study Anabaena to understand how it helps plants by fixing nitrogen in aquatic environments.
The presence of Anabaena in rice paddies can naturally increase soil fertility.
Farmers use the symbiosis between Azolla and Anabaena to reduce the need for chemical fertilizers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Oscillatoria - a genus of filamentous cyanobacteria
  • synonym: cyanobacteria - photosynthetic bacteria found in water
