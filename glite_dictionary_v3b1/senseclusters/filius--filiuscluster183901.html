filius

noun
Latin 'son of'
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪliəs / (AmE), / ˈfɪliəs / (BrE)
a Latin word meaning 'son', often used in Roman names, inscriptions, or legal/historical contexts

Examples

Roman filius
In ancient Rome, a man's name might include filius to show his father's identity.
The inscription read 'Marcus Tullius filius Quintus', indicating Marcus was the son of Quintus.
Historians often use the abbreviation 'f.' for filius when studying Roman family trees.
In Roman law, a filius had specific rights and responsibilities within the family.
The term filius is often found in historical documents written in Latin.
The phrase 'filius nullius' refers to someone without legal parents.

Related Terms

  • antonym: daughter - a female child in relation to her parents
  • synonym: son - a male child in relation to his parents
