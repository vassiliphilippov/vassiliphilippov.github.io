wind

verb
coil a spring by turning a knob
8.6
Pronunciation: / waɪnd / (AmE), / waɪnd / (BrE)
to coil the spring of a mechanical device, such as a watch, by turning a knob or stem

Examples

To wind a watch
Don't forget to wind your watch every night before you go to bed.
He forgot to wind his alarm clock, so it didn't ring in the morning.
She carefully wound the old clock, listening to its mechanism click into place.
