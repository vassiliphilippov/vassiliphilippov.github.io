parlance

noun
manner or style of speaking
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑrləns / (AmE), / ˈpɑː.ləns / (BrE)
a particular way of using words or expressing oneself, especially one characteristic of a specific group, profession, or context

Examples

In everyday parlance
In everyday parlance, 'kids' is more common than 'children'.
In legal parlance, the term 'plaintiff' refers to the person who brings a case to court.
Modern internet parlance is full of abbreviations and emojis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jargon - special words or expressions used by a particular group
