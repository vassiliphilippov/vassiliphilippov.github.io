turnip-rooted parsley

noun
parsley with an edible, parsnip-like root
Pronunciation: / ˈtɜrnɪp ˌrutɪd ˈpɑrsli / (AmE), / ˈtɜːnɪp ˌruːtɪd ˈpɑːsli / (BrE)
A plant with smooth leaves and an enlarged edible white taproot, resembling a parsnip but belonging to the parsley family.

Examples

Fresh turnip-rooted parsley
At the farmers' market, I found a bunch of fresh turnip-rooted parsley for tonight's dinner.
She used turnip-rooted parsley in the soup for an earthy, mild flavor.
The chef recommended roasting turnip-rooted parsley with olive oil and herbs.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: parsley - a common herb with flat or curly leaves, often used as a garnish or flavoring
  • degree higher: parsnip - a root vegetable with a sweet, nutty flavor, similar in appearance to a white carrot
