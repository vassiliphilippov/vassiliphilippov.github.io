switchback

noun
zigzag railway track
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˈswɪtʃˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈswɪtʃˌbæk / (BrE)
a road, path, railway, or section that zigzags up or down a steep slope with sharp turns

Examples

Mountain switchback road
The old mountain railway uses a switchback to help trains climb the steep hillside.
Engineers designed a switchback to allow the train to safely descend the sharp slope.
Passengers enjoyed the view as the train reversed direction on each switchback up the mountain.
The hikers followed the switchback to reach the top of the steep hill.
Driving up the mountain, we navigated several sharp switchbacks along the way.
The switchback made the climb less tiring by spreading the ascent over a longer distance.
The hiking trail climbed the mountain in a series of switchbacks to make the ascent easier.
Drivers must slow down when approaching a switchback on a steep mountain road.
The cyclist carefully navigated each switchback during the challenging race.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hairpin bend - a very sharp turn in a road, especially on a mountain
  • synonym: hairpin turn - a very sharp bend in a road, resembling a hairpin
  • antonym: straightaway - a straight section of a road or path
  • synonym: zigzag - a pattern or path with sharp turns in alternating directions
