prazosin

noun
antihypertensive medication
Pronunciation: / ˈpræzəˌsɪn / (AmE), / ˈpræzəʊsɪn / (BrE)
a medication used to treat high blood pressure by relaxing and widening blood vessels; also used for other conditions such as benign prostatic hyperplasia.

Examples

prazosin medication
Research shows that prazosin can also reduce nightmares related to PTSD.
She experienced fewer side effects after switching to prazosin from another medication.
The doctor prescribed prazosin to help manage his high blood pressure.
