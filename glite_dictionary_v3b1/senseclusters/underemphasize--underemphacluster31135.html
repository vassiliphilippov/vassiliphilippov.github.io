underemphasize

verb
to downplay importance
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈɛmfəˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈɛmfəsaɪz / (BrE)
to give too little importance or attention to something

Examples

We should not underemphasize the importance of regular exercise.
The report tends to underemphasize the risks involved in the project.
Teachers sometimes underemphasize the value of creative thinking in the classroom.
The teacher warned not to underemphasize the importance of regular practice for improvement.
Some news articles underemphasize the long-term effects of climate change.
If you underemphasize your achievements in the interview, you might miss out on the job.
The report underemphasizes the risks involved in the new investment strategy.
Teachers should not underemphasize the importance of regular practice to their students.
Some articles underemphasize the benefits of a balanced diet for long-term health.
