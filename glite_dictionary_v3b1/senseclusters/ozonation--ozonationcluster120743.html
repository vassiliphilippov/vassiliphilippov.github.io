ozonation

noun
formation of ozone
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊzoʊˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌəʊzəˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of forming or increasing ozone in the atmosphere, often due to chemical reactions or pollution

Examples

Urban ozonation
Industrial emissions can lead to increased ozonation in urban areas, affecting air quality.
Scientists monitor ozonation levels to assess the impact of pollution on the environment.
High temperatures and sunlight accelerate the ozonation of air in large cities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deozonation - the removal or reduction of ozone
  • synonym: oxidation - a chemical reaction involving the addition of oxygen
