upstart

adjective
newly rich; lacking social grace
4.4 5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpˌstɑrt / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌstɑːt / (BrE)
(of a person) having risen suddenly to wealth or position, often regarded as lacking the necessary manners or social skills for the new status.

Examples

Upstart millionaire
His upstart attitude alienated those who valued tradition and respect.
Many saw the new CEO as an upstart who didn't understand the company's culture.
The party was full of upstart millionaires unfamiliar with traditional etiquette.

Related Terms

  • synonym: parvenu - a person who has recently acquired wealth or status but lacks the manners and social skills of the new class
