upstart

adjective
newly rich; lacking social grace
7.2 0.68/m
Pronunciation: / Àà åpÀåst…ërt / (AmE), / Àà åpÀåst…ëÀêt / (BrE)
(of a person) having risen suddenly to wealth or position, often regarded as lacking the necessary manners or social skills for the new status.

Examples

Upstart millionaire
His upstart attitude alienated those who valued tradition and respect.
Many saw the new CEO as an upstart who didn't understand the company's culture.
The party was full of upstart millionaires unfamiliar with traditional etiquette.

Synonyms

  • parvenu
    9.9 0.00/m
    parvenu is newly wealthy and often not accepted by high society, while upstart highlights a sudden rise and rude ambition.
  • parvenue
    9.9 0.00/m
    parvenue is a woman newly risen into wealth who lacks social acceptance, while upstart focuses on the sudden rise and often impolite behavior.
  • nouveau-riche
    9.1 0.01/m
    nouveau-riche is used for people who recently got rich and lack refined taste, while upstart stresses sudden rise and social pushiness.
