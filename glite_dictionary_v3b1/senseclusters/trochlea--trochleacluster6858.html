trochlea

noun
pulley-shaped structure
9.9
Pronunciation: /trɑː.ˈkliː.ə/ (AmE), /trɒ.ˈkliː.ə/ (BrE)
a structure in the body shaped like a pulley, especially a part of a bone or joint that guides movement

Examples

Bone trochlea joint
The trochlea of the femur helps the knee joint move smoothly.
During anatomy class, we studied the trochlea in the human elbow.
Damage to the trochlea can affect the movement of the arm.
