yoke

noun
joining device or connector
Pronunciation: / joʊk / (AmE), / jəʊk / (BrE)
a device or frame used to join or connect things together, such as a wooden bar for animals, a connector for objects, or a frame for carrying loads

Examples

A wooden yoke
A special yoke was used to connect the pipe sections so they wouldn't move apart.
Electrical engineers sometimes use a yoke to ensure components remain aligned during operation.
The two oxen were joined by a heavy yoke, allowing them to pull the plow together.
A wooden yoke joined the two water buffalo as they pulled the heavy cart together.
Craftsmen carved a new yoke to replace the one that had cracked during last season's harvest.
The farmer fitted the oxen with a sturdy yoke before heading out to plow the fields.
She placed the yoke carefully before attaching two pails of milk.
The farmer balanced the buckets of water on a yoke across his shoulders.
The traditional yoke made it easier to carry heavy loads over long distances.
