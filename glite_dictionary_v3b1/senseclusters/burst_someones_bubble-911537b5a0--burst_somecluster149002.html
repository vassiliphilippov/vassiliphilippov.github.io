burst someone's bubble

verb
disappoint or shatter someone's illusions
Pronunciation: / bɝst sʌmwʌnz ˈbʌbəl / (AmE), / bɜːst sʌmwʌnz ˈbʌbəl / (BrE)
to shatter someone's illusions or disappoint them by revealing an unpleasant truth

Examples

To burst your bubble truth
I hate to burst your bubble, but the store is actually closed on Sundays.
Joan didn't want to burst his bubble when he was so hopeful about the outcome.
The news report burst their bubble about winning the lottery.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cheer up - make someone more cheerful; opposite of disappointing
  • synonym: disillusion - cause someone to realize a belief is false
