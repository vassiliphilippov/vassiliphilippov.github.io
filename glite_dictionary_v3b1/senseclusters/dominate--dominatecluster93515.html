dominate

verb
be more significant than
Pronunciation: / ˈdɑːməˌneɪt /; / ˈdɑmɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɒmɪneɪt / (BrE)
to be the most important, noticeable, or significant part of something; to be greater in number, quantity, or status

Examples

Dominate the discussion
Her achievements dominate every conversation during family gatherings.
The debate about climate change tends to dominate discussions at international conferences.
The new skyscraper will dominate the city skyline, making other buildings seem small.
Fear can sometimes dominate our thoughts and prevent us from taking risks.
In the match, one team began to dominate after halftime, controlling the ball completely.
She always tries to dominate conversations at work, rarely letting others speak.
In this region, women dominate the workforce, making up over 65% of employees.
Large corporations dominate the technology sector, shaping innovation and consumer trends worldwide.
Tall pine trees dominate the landscape, blocking sunlight from reaching the forest floor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: control - to exercise power over; to direct
  • synonym: eclipse - to overshadow or surpass in significance
  • synonym: overshadow - appear more important or significant than; outshine
  • synonym: predominate - to be the largest or most important part
  • synonym: reign - to hold supreme power or authority; to be predominant
  • antonym: submit - to yield or surrender to the control or authority of another
  • antonym: subordinate - to treat or regard as of lesser importance than something else
