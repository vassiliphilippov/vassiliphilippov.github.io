trolley

noun
electrically powered rail vehicle for city transport
2.5 1.95/m
Pronunciation: / Ààtr…ëÀêli / (AmE), / Ààtr…íli / (BrE)
A wheeled vehicle that runs on rails and is powered by electricity, used for carrying passengers, especially within cities.

Examples

City trolley ride
She takes the trolley to work every morning instead of driving her car.
The city decided to modernize its public transport system by introducing a new trolley line.
We watched the old trolley make its way down Main Street, sparking on the overhead wires.

Synonyms

  • trolley car
    3.8 0.10/m
    a trolley car is the full name for an electric rail vehicle on city streets, while trolley is the shorter everyday word.
  • tramcar
    3.4 0.03/m
    a tramcar is an electric passenger rail vehicle that runs on city tracks, while trolley is the more familiar, shorter name.
  • streetcar
    3.0 1.60/m
    a streetcar runs along public streets and mixes with road traffic, while trolley names the general electric city rail vehicle.
  • tram
    1.3 1.51/m
    a tram can mean various rail vehicles, even a mine wagon, while trolley refers specifically to an electric city passenger vehicle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tram - a rail vehicle for public transportation, especially in British English
