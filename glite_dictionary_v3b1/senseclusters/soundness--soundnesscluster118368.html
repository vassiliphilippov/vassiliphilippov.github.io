soundness

noun
state of being undamaged or in good condition
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈsaʊnd.nəs / (AmE), / ˈsaʊnd.nəs / (BrE)
The quality or state of being free from damage, decay, or defect; being whole or in good condition.

Examples

Soundness of structure
He doubted the soundness of the old wooden beams in the attic.
Regular maintenance is key to preserving the soundness of your house's foundation.
The soundness of the bridge was confirmed after a thorough inspection.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decay - the process of gradually becoming worse or weaker
  • synonym: integrity - the state of being whole and undivided
