penetration

noun
depth something penetrates, e.g. a projectile
2.4 3.9
Pronunciation: /pen.ə.ˈtreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /pen.ɪ.ˈtreɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /pen.ɪ.ˈtreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /pen.ə.ˈtreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act, depth, or ability of entering into or passing through something (such as a substance or object)

Examples

Bullet penetration of armor
Armor thickness is designed to reduce penetration from high-velocity rounds.
Engineers tested the penetration of different materials under controlled impact.
The bullet's penetration was measured at eighteen inches in ballistic gel.
Modern materials can resist the penetration of water and dust.
The penetration of women into upper management roles has increased significantly over the last decade.
The company was concerned about the penetration of their network security.
Glass with low UV penetration helps protect artwork from fading.
Scientists measured the soil’s penetration by plant roots under different conditions.
The bullet’s penetration of the armor was much greater than expected.
