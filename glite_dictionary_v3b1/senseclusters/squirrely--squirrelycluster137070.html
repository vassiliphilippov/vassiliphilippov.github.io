squirrely

adjective
secretive or odd
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈskwɜrli /; / ˈskwɝːli / (AmE), / ˈskwɪəli / (BrE)
behaving in a strange, nervous, or unpredictable way

Examples

A squirrely person
He started acting squirrely after someone mentioned the missing money.
Her squirrely behavior made everyone wonder what she was hiding.
Whenever the topic comes up, he gets a little squirrely and changes the subject.
He started acting squirrely when the police asked him questions.
Whenever she has too much coffee, she gets a bit squirrely and can't sit still.
The cat became squirrely during the thunderstorm, running around the house.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - not showing nervousness or excitement
  • synonym: shady - behaving in a way that seems dishonest or secretive
  • synonym: skittish - easily frightened or excited; unpredictable
  • synonym: suspicious - acting in a way that makes others think something is wrong or hidden
  • antonym: trustworthy - reliable and deserving of trust
