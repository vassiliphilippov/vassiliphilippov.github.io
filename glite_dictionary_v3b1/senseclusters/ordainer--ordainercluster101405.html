ordainer

noun
a cleric who ordains others
9.2 8.4
Pronunciation: /ɔːr.ˈdeɪ.nər/ (AmE), /ɔː.ˈdeɪ.nə/ (BrE)
a cleric who formally invests someone with holy orders; one who performs the act of ordination in a religious context

Examples

The ordainer of priests
A bishop often serves as the ordainer in many Christian denominations.
The ordainer laid his hands on the candidate during the ceremony.
The role of the ordainer is central in the process of conferring holy orders.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bishop - a senior cleric empowered to ordain others in some Christian traditions
  • antonym: candidate - a person receiving holy orders
  • synonym: officiant - a person who leads a religious ceremony
