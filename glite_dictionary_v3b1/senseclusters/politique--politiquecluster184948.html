politique

noun
French moderate politician
Pronunciation: / pəˈliːtik /; / ˌpɑːləˈtik / (AmE), / pɒlɪˈtiːk /; / ˌpɒlɪˈtiːk / (BrE)
a person who is pragmatic and flexible in political matters, especially one who prioritizes practical solutions over strict ideology (sometimes specifically a member of a moderate group in 16th-century France)

Examples

A skilled politique
During the French Wars of Religion, the politiques advocated for compromise to maintain peace.
Many historians believe the influence of the politiques helped end the cycle of violence in France.
A politique would support policies that unite citizens regardless of their religious beliefs.
During the religious wars, the politique advocated for tolerance to prevent further bloodshed.
Historians often describe Queen Elizabeth I as a skilled politique who balanced competing interests.
The politiques in government worked to find compromise between rival factions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: extremist - a person with radical or extreme political or religious views
  • antonym: ideologue - a person who strongly supports a particular ideology
  • synonym: moderate - a person with centrist or middle-of-the-road political views
  • synonym: pragmatist - a person who deals with things sensibly and realistically
