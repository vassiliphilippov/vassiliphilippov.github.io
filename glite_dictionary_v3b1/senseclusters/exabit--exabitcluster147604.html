exabit

noun
unit of digital information (10^18 bits)
Pronunciation: / ˈɛks.əˌbɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛks.əˌbɪt / (BrE)
a unit of information equal to 1,000 petabits or 10^18 (one quintillion) bits, used in computing and telecommunications

Examples

One exabit unit
Advances in technology have made transferring an exabit of data more feasible than ever before.
Scientists predict that the world's internet traffic will soon exceed several exabits daily.
The total bandwidth of the new undersea cable network can handle up to one exabit per second.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: zettabit - a unit of information equal to 10^21 bits
