Cucurbita foetidissima

noun
perennial vine with hard inedible fruit
Pronunciation: / kuːˈkɝː.bɪ.tə foʊˌɛtɪˈdɪsɪmə / (AmE), / kjuːˈkɜː.bɪ.tə fəʊˌetɪˈdɪsɪmə / (BrE)
A perennial vine native to the central and southwestern United States and Mexico, known for its small, hard, and mottled green inedible fruit.

Examples

Cucurbita foetidissima vine
Cucurbita foetidissima is commonly found growing wild in arid regions of the southwestern United States.
Native American cultures have historically used Cucurbita foetidissima for various purposes, including making soap from its seeds.
The hard fruit of Cucurbita foetidissima is not suitable for human consumption.
