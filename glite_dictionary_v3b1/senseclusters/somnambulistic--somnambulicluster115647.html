somnambulistic

adjective
automatic or dazed
Pronunciation: / sɑmˌnæmbjəˈlɪstɪk / (AmE), / sɒmˌnæmbjʊˈlɪstɪk / (BrE)
acting or appearing as if sleepwalking; in a dazed, automatic, or unthinking way

Examples

Somnambulistic state
He moved through his morning routine in a somnambulistic state, barely noticing what he was doing.
The workers' somnambulistic responses showed they were not truly engaged with the project.
After a sleepless night, she gave a somnambulistic performance at the meeting.
Her somnambulistic expression made it seem as if she was lost in thought.
He wandered through the empty house in a somnambulistic state after his long shift.
The actor's somnambulistic performance gave the character an eerie, dreamlike quality.

Related Terms

  • antonym: alert - fully aware and attentive
  • synonym: automatic - done without conscious thought or intention
  • synonym: mechanical - performed as if by machine, without feeling or thought
  • synonym: sleepwalking - walking or performing activities while asleep
