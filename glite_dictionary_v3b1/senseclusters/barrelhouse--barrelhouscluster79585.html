barrelhouse

noun
energetic blues style
Pronunciation: / ˈbærəlˌhaʊs / (AmE), / ˈbærəlˌhaʊs / (BrE)
a lively, energetic style of blues or jazz music, often played on piano in bars or clubs

Examples

Barrelhouse piano music
The pianist played a lively barrelhouse tune that filled the room with energy.
Many early jazz musicians were influenced by the barrelhouse style of piano playing.
You can still hear barrelhouse rhythms in some modern blues performances.

Related Terms

  • synonym: boogie-woogie - a fast, rhythmic style of blues piano music
  • synonym: ragtime - a style of early jazz music with a syncopated rhythm
