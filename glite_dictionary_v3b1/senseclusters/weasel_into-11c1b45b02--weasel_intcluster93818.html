weasel into

verb
sneakily gain access to something
Pronunciation: / ˈwizəl ˈɪntu / (AmE), / ˈwiːzl ˈɪntuː / (BrE)
to enter or gain access to a place, group, or situation by sly or sneaky means

Examples

To weasel into a group
He managed to weasel into the discussion by pretending to know the topic.
She tried to weasel into the exclusive event without an invitation.
They attempted to weasel into the restricted area but were quickly spotted by security.
