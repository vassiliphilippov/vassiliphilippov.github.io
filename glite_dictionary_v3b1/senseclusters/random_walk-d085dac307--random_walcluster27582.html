random walk

noun
process of random sequential changes
6.6 6.2
Pronunciation: /ˈræn.dəm wɑːk/ /; / /ˈræn.dəm wɔːk/ (AmE), /ˈræn.dəm wɔːk/ (BrE)
a process in which a variable changes in steps, with each step determined randomly, often used to model unpredictable movements or fluctuations.

Examples

A random walk process
In physics, Brownian motion is a classic example of a random walk in particle movement.
Scientists use random walk models to study the spread of molecules in a fluid.
The stock market is often described as a random walk because its future movements are hard to predict.

  • synonym: stochastic process - a process involving a sequence of random variables
