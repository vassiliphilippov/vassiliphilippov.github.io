duty tour

noun
period of military service assignment
5.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàduÀêti t är / (AmE), / ÀàdjuÀêti t ä…ô / (BrE)
a period of time assigned to a person, especially in the military, to perform specific duties or service at a particular post or deployment

Examples

Complete a duty tour
After completing his duty tour overseas, he returned home to his family.
Each duty tour can last anywhere from six months to a year, depending on assignment.
Soldiers often face new challenges with every duty tour they undertake.

Synonyms

  • tour of duty
    6.0 1.07/m
    tour of duty is a set time serving at a post in the military, while duty tour highlights an assigned period at a specific deployment.
  • tour
    4.1 10/m
    tour is a general period of work that can be civilian or shift-based, while duty tour refers to assigned military service at a post.
  • hitch
    6.8 0.21/m
    hitch is an informal short stint in the armed forces, while duty tour is a formal assigned period of service at a post.
