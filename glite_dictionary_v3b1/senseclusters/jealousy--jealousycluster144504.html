jealousy

noun
resentment or envy toward a rival
1.0 1.4
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒel.ə.siː/ /; / /ˈdʒel.ə.sɪ/ (AmE), /ˈdʒel.ə.sɪ/ (BrE)
a feeling of resentment or anger caused by the belief that someone else has something or someone you want, especially a rival in love or success

Examples

Romantic jealousy
His jealousy was obvious whenever he saw his colleague receive praise at work.
She felt overwhelming jealousy when she saw her ex-boyfriend with someone new.
Sibling jealousy can arise when children feel they are competing for their parents' attention.

Related Terms

  • antonym: admiration - respect and warm approval, often contrasted with resentment or envy
  • synonym: envy - a feeling of discontent or covetousness regarding another's advantages, success, or possessions
