stylohyoid

noun
neck muscle for swallowing
9.9
Pronunciation: /staɪ.loʊ.ˈhaɪ.ɔɪd/ (AmE), /staɪ.ləʊ.ˈhaɪ.ɔɪd/ (BrE)
a slender muscle that runs from the styloid process of the temporal bone to the hyoid bone, aiding in swallowing

Examples

Stylohyoid muscle
The stylohyoid helps elevate the hyoid bone during the act of swallowing.
Injury to the stylohyoid can cause difficulty when trying to swallow solid foods.
During anatomy class, we located the stylohyoid between the jaw and the throat.

Related Terms

  • synonym: digastric - a muscle under the jaw involved in swallowing and jaw movement
  • synonym: mylohyoid - a muscle forming the floor of the mouth, aiding in swallowing
