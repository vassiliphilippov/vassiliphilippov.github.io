trachoma

noun
infectious eye disease causing scarring
8.8 9.9
Pronunciation: / trəˈkoʊmə / (AmE), / trəˈkəʊmə / (BrE)
a chronic contagious disease of the eye caused by a bacterium, characterized by inflammation of the conjunctiva and cornea and formation of scar tissue, which can lead to blindness

Examples

Eye trachoma infection
Early diagnosis and treatment of trachoma can help prevent long-term vision loss.
Health workers distributed antibiotics to combat the spread of trachoma in the rural community.
Trachoma remains a leading cause of preventable blindness in certain parts of the world.
