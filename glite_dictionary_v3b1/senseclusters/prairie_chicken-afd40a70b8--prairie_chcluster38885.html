prairie chicken

noun
North American grouse of western prairies
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛri ˈtʃɪkən / (AmE), / ˈpreəri ˈtʃɪkɪn / (BrE)
a brownish, mottled grouse native to western North American prairies, noted for its distinctive booming call during mating displays

Examples

The prairie chicken calls
Conservationists are working to protect the prairie chicken population from further decline.
The prairie chicken's unique courtship display attracts birdwatchers from around the country.
We spotted a prairie chicken dancing in the tall grass at dawn.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grouse - a bird of the family Tetraonidae, to which the prairie chicken belongs
