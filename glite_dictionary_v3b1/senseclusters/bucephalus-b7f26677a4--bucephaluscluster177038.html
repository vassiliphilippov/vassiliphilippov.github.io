Bucephalus

proper noun
Alexander's legendary horse
Pronunciation: / bjuːˈsɛfələs / (AmE), / bjuːˈsɛfələs / (BrE)
the legendary horse of Alexander the Great, famous in ancient history for his loyalty and courage

Examples

Alexander's Bucephalus horse
According to legend, Bucephalus was the only horse Alexander the Great could tame.
Many stories celebrate the bravery of Bucephalus in ancient battles.
Historians often mention Bucephalus as a symbol of loyalty and strength.
