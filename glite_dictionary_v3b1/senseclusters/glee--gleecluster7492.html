glee

noun
malicious or delighted satisfaction
Pronunciation: / ɡliː / (AmE), / ɡliː / (BrE)
great delight, often with a sense of malicious satisfaction at someone else's misfortune or discomfort.

Examples

Glee at failure
She could barely hide her glee at her rival's embarrassing mistake.
The critics watched with glee as the company experienced another setback.
There was a look of glee on his face when he learned of their defeat.

Related Terms

  • antonym: regret - a feeling of sadness or disappointment
  • synonym: schadenfreude - pleasure derived from another person's misfortune
