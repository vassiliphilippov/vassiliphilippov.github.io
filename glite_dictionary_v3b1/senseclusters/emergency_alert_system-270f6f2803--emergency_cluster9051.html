Emergency Alert System

noun
U.S. federal emergency broadcast system
Pronunciation: / ɪˈmɜrdʒənsi əˈlɝt ˈsɪstəm / (AmE), / ɪˈmɜːdʒənsi əˈlɜːt ˈsɪstəm / (BrE)
a federal system in the United States that allows authorities to broadcast emergency warnings to the public via radio and television.

Examples

The Emergency Alert System message
All radio stations simultaneously broadcast the Emergency Alert System message about the natural disaster.
During the test, the Emergency Alert System issued a loud tone followed by a prerecorded announcement.
The Emergency Alert System interrupted programming to warn viewers of the approaching tornado.
