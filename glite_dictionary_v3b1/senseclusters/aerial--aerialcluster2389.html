aerial

adjective
light, insubstantial, or intangible
Pronunciation: / ˈɛriəl / (AmE), / ˈɪəriəl / (BrE)
Having a light, delicate, or insubstantial quality; as intangible or impalpable as air.

Examples

Aerial beauty
A soft, aerial glow surrounded the sculpture, making it seem almost intangible.
Her thoughts drifted into aerial fancies, far removed from reality.
The painter’s aerial brushstrokes gave the landscape a dreamlike quality.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ethereal - extremely delicate and light in a way that seems too perfect for this world
  • antonym: substantial - having substance or being tangible or solid
