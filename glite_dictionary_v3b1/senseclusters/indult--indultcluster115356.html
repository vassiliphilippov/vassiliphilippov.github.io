indult

verb
to grant exemption
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdʌlt / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌlt / (BrE)
to grant an official permission or exemption, especially by church authority.

Examples

To indult a group
The bishop decided to indult the parish to hold the special ceremony.
After much discussion, the council indulted the group to use the chapel for their event.
It is rare for the authorities to indult such requests without strong justification.

Related Terms

  • antonym: forbid - to refuse to allow or prohibit
  • synonym: permit - to officially allow or authorize
