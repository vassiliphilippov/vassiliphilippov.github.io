oral exam

noun
spoken examination
1.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrəl ɪgˈzæm / (AmE), / ˈɔːrəl ɪgˈzæm / (BrE)
An examination in which information is tested through spoken questions and answers rather than by written tests.

Examples

The oral exam session
During the oral exam, students must answer questions clearly and confidently.
She was nervous about the oral exam because she preferred written tests.
The professor scheduled the oral exam for the last week of the semester.

Related Terms

  • antonym: written exam - an examination conducted in writing
