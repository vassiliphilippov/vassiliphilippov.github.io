Chief Executive

noun
official title for the US President
1.0
Pronunciation: / tʃiːf ɪɡˈzɛkjətɪv / (AmE), / tʃiːf ɪɡˈzɛkjʊtɪv / (BrE)
the official title used for the President of the United States as the head of the executive branch of the government

Examples

The Chief Executive officer
As Chief Executive, the President has the authority to issue executive orders.
Every four years, Americans elect a new Chief Executive.
The Chief Executive delivered a national address last night.
