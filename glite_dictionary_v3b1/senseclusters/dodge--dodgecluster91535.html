dodge

noun
an evasive or tricky statement
6.0 6.1
Pronunciation: / dɑdʒ / (AmE), / dɒdʒ / (BrE)
a clever or dishonest trick or statement used to avoid answering a question or responsibility

Examples

A clever dodge
Every time he's confronted, he comes up with a clever dodge instead of giving a straight reply.
She saw through his dodge and pressed him for a real answer.
The politician's response was just another dodge to avoid admitting the truth.
Pretending to be sick was only a dodge to get out of going to school.
The politician's answer was just a dodge to avoid the real issue.
They saw through his clever dodge and insisted on a direct explanation.

  • synonym: ruse - a clever plan intended to deceive
  • antonym: straight answer - an honest and direct answer
  • synonym: stratagem - a plan or scheme, especially one used to outwit an opponent or achieve an end
  • synonym: trick - an action intended to deceive someone
