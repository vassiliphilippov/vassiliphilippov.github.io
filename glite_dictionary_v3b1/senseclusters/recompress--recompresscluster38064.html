recompress

verb
to compress again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkəmˈprɛs / (AmE), / ˌriːkəmˈprɛs / (BrE)
to compress something again, especially after it has been decompressed or released (can refer to digital files or other things)

Examples

To recompress a file
After editing the image, you may need to recompress it to save storage space.
The software allows users to recompress audio files into different formats for easier sharing.
To meet the upload limit, she decided to recompress the video before sending it.
After decompressing the file, you may need to recompress it to save space.
Engineers had to recompress the gas before transporting it through the pipeline.
The diver used special equipment to recompress the air in the tank for the next dive.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compress - to reduce the size of a file or data by encoding it
  • antonym: decompress - to restore compressed data to its original form
