professional

adjective
relating to a profession
Pronunciation: / prəˈfəʃənl̩ /; / prəˈfɛʃənəl /; / prəˈfɛʃənl̩ / (AmE), / prəˈfɛʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to a profession, or showing the skill, standards, or competence expected in a profession

Examples

Professional standards
A professional code of conduct ensures high ethical standards in the workplace.
She joined several professional organizations after obtaining her medical license.
The company sets strict professional standards for its lawyers and accountants.
Many people are attracted to professional careers because of their stability and prestige.
Medicine and law are considered professional fields requiring special training.
Only professional teachers can apply for this position at the university.
She gave the presentation in a very professional manner.
The company offers professional development courses to improve employees' skills.
We hired a professional photographer to take pictures at the event.

Related Terms

  • antonym: amateur - not engaged in for pay or as a profession; not professional
  • synonym: occupational - relating to a particular occupation or work
