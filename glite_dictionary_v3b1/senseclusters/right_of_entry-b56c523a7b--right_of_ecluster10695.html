right of entry

noun
legal right to enter or possess property
Pronunciation: / raɪt əv ˈɛntri / (AmE), / raɪt əv ˈɛntri / (BrE)
the legal right to enter or take possession of property, especially real estate, in a lawful and peaceable manner

Examples

The right of entry law
In some jurisdictions, a right of entry must be explicitly stated in the contract.
The landlord exercised his right of entry to inspect the apartment after giving proper notice.
Under the lease agreement, the tenant has no right of entry beyond the rented premises.
