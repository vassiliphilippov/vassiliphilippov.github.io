bore

noun
hole or passage made by drilling
5.9
Pronunciation: / bɔr / (AmE), / bɔː / (BrE)
a hole made by drilling, or the inside diameter of a tube, pipe, or gun barrel

Examples

A narrow bore
A narrow bore was drilled into the ground for soil sampling.
The engineers inspected the bore to assess the rock layers below the surface.
They capped the old bore to prevent water from seeping in.
Each barrel's bore must be uniform to guarantee accuracy.
Engineers measured the bore of the pipe to ensure a proper fit.
The bore of this shotgun is much larger than that of a standard rifle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: caliber - diameter of a bullet or gun barrel; related measurement
  • synonym: drill hole - a hole made by a drill, typically in rock or earth
