cretonne

noun
heavy, printed fabric for draperies or covers
7.9
Pronunciation: / krəˈtən / (AmE), / krəˈtɒn / (BrE)
A heavy, unglazed fabric, often brightly printed, used especially for making slipcovers and draperies.

Examples

A cretonne fabric
Old-fashioned cottages often feature cretonne slipcovers with lively patterns and hues.
She chose a cheerful cretonne for the living room drapes, adding a splash of color to the windows.
The armchairs were covered in a floral cretonne that brightened the entire room.
