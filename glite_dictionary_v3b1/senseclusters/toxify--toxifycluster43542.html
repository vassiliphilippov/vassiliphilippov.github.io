toxify

verb
to make harmful
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑksɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈtɒksɪˌfaɪ / (BrE)
to make a relationship, environment, or situation harmful, unpleasant, or hostile, especially in a figurative sense

Examples

To toxify a workplace
Negative gossip can easily toxify the atmosphere in a workplace.
If you constantly criticize your friends, you may toxify your relationships with them.
Social media arguments sometimes toxify what could have been a helpful discussion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: poison - to make something harmful or dangerous
  • antonym: purify - to make something clean or free from harmful substances
