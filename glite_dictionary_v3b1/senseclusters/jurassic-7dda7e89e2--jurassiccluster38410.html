Jurassic

noun
geological period dominated by dinosaurs
5.1
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈræsɪk / (AmE), / dʒʊəˈræsɪk / (BrE)
the period of geological time from about 201 million to 145 million years ago, characterized by the dominance of dinosaurs and extensive conifer forests

Examples

The Jurassic period
Dinosaurs flourished during the Jurassic, populating much of the Earth.
Many films depict the Jurassic as an era ruled by giant reptiles.
The famous cliffs at Dorset contain fossils from the Jurassic period.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Cretaceous - the period following the Jurassic in the Mesozoic Era
  • degree lower: Triassic - the period preceding the Jurassic in the Mesozoic Era
