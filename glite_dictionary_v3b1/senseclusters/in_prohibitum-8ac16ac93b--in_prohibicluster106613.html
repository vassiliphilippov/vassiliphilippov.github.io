in prohibitum

noun
legally prohibited offence
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪn prə.hɪ.ˈbɪ.təm/ /; / /ɪn proʊ.hɪ.ˈbɪ.təm/ (AmE), /ɪn prə.hɪ.ˈbɪ.təm/ /; / /ɪn prəʊ.hɪ.ˈbɪ.təm/ (BrE)
an offence that is considered wrongful only because it is prohibited by law, not because it is inherently immoral

Examples

In prohibitum offence
Littering is often classified as an in prohibitum offence rather than a serious crime.
The judge explained that the act was illegal as an in prohibitum, not because it was morally wrong.
Many traffic violations are considered in prohibitum offences under the law.

Related Terms

  • antonym: malum in se - an act that is wrong in itself, inherently immoral
  • synonym: malum prohibitum - an act that is wrong only because it is prohibited by law
