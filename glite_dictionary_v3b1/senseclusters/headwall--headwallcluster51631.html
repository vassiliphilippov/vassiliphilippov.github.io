headwall

noun
retaining wall for culvert
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛdˌwɔl / (AmE), / ˈhɛdˌwɔːl / (BrE)
a retaining wall built at the end of a culvert, drain, or pipe to support soil and control water flow

Examples

Concrete headwall installation

A sturdy headwall helps prevent soil erosion around the culvert opening.
After heavy rain, the headwall kept the embankment stable and protected the road.
