sarcastically typical of white people or white privilege
9.9
Pronunciation: / saʊndz əˈbaʊt waɪt / (AmE), / saʊndz əˈbaʊt waɪt / (BrE)
(especially in US informal contexts) said to sarcastically suggest that something is typical of the behavior, attitudes, or excuses associated with white people or white privilege.

Examples

Sounds about white excuse
He apologized for the mistake but didn't actually change anything, which sounds about white.
Someone called the police on a kid selling lemonade? Sounds about white to me.
When the company ignored the complaints about diversity, she just shrugged and said, sounds about white.
