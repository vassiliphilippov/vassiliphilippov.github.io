polyneuropathy

noun
damage to many nerves
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑliˌnuːˈrɑpəθi / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˌnjuːˈrɒpəθi / (BrE)
a disease or condition that causes damage to many peripheral nerves at the same time

Examples

Polyneuropathy nerve damage
The patient was diagnosed with polyneuropathy after reporting tingling in his hands and feet.
Diabetes is a common cause of polyneuropathy in adults.
Physical therapy can help manage symptoms of polyneuropathy and improve quality of life.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mononeuropathy - damage to a single nerve
  • synonym: neuropathy - damage or disease affecting nerves
