bone char

noun
black material from charred bone; filtering/pigment
Pronunciation: / boʊn tʃɑr / (AmE), / bəʊn tʃɑː / (BrE)
a black material made by charring animal bones, used chiefly as a pigment or for filtering

Examples

Bone char pigment
Historically, bone char was used in sugar refining to decolorize and purify the product.
Modern water treatment plants sometimes use bone char as a filtration medium.
The artist selected bone char as a pigment for its distinctive black hue.
