golden parachute

noun
lucrative severance for executives after takeover
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɡoʊldən ˈpærəˌʃut / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊldən ˈpærəˌʃuːt /; / ˈɡəʊldən ˈpærəʃuːt / (BrE)
an agreement to give a top executive a large financial compensation package if they are dismissed or forced to leave a company, especially after a takeover

Examples

A golden parachute clause
Many executives negotiate a golden parachute clause in case of unexpected mergers.
Shareholders criticized the board for approving extravagant golden parachute deals.
The CEO received a golden parachute worth millions when the company was sold.
After the merger, several executives received golden parachutes to ease their departures.
Many investors criticized the board for approving such a generous golden parachute.
The CEO left the company with a golden parachute worth ten million dollars.
