transcendent

adjective
beyond ordinary experience or understanding
2.8
Pronunciation: / trænˈsɛndənt / (AmE), / trænˈsɛndənt / (BrE)
going beyond normal or ordinary limits, especially in experience, knowledge, quality, or achievement

Examples

A transcendent experience
Her music achieved a transcendent quality that moved listeners deeply.
Some people seek transcendent experiences through meditation and art.
The philosopher wrote about transcendent realities that lie beyond ordinary perception.
He achieved a transcendent level of mastery in his craft.
Her performance was so transcendent that it left the entire audience speechless.
The beauty of the natural landscape was truly transcendent, far beyond anything he had seen before.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exceptional - unusually excellent or outstanding
  • antonym: immanent - existing or operating within; inherent
  • antonym: mundane - lacking interest or excitement; dull, pertaining to the ordinary world
  • synonym: supernatural - not existing in nature or not subject to explanation according to natural laws
