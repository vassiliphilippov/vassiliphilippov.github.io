electromagnetic delay line

noun
device delaying signals using electromagnetic waves
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktroʊmæɡˈnɛtɪk dɪˈleɪ laɪn / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊmæɡˈnɛtɪk dɪˈleɪ laɪn / (BrE)
an electrical device that delays signals by using the time it takes for electromagnetic waves to travel along it

Examples
