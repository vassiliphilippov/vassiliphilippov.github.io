pyrolyze

verb
to decompose by heat
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪrəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈpɪrəˌlaɪz / (BrE)
to decompose a material or substance by heating it in the absence of oxygen, often to produce gases or chemicals

Examples

To pyrolyze waste
Researchers pyrolyze agricultural waste to create biochar for soil improvement.
To recover valuable chemicals, engineers often pyrolyze plastic in specialized reactors.
Some recycling plants pyrolyze old tires to extract useful oils and gases.

The laboratory pyrolyzed the sample to analyze the resulting gases.
Industries often pyrolyze rubber tires to recover valuable materials.

  • antonym: oxidize - to react with oxygen, especially by burning
