Achates

proper noun
ancient Sicilian river
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈkeɪtiːz / (AmE), / əˈkeɪtiːz / (BrE)
an ancient name for a river in Sicily, historically known as the source of agate gemstones

Examples

The Achates river
In ancient times, traders sought agate along the banks of the Achates.
The Achates is now known as the Dirillo River in modern Sicily.
Legends about the Achates inspired many classical writers and poets.
