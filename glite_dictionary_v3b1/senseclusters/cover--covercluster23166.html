cover

verb
to conceal or envelop as by a shroud
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌvɚ /; / ˈkʌvər / (AmE), / ˈkʌvə / (BrE)
to put or spread something over or on top of something else, to protect, hide, or enclose it

Examples

To cover a cake
His past remains covered in secrecy, with very few people knowing the truth.
The entire city was covered in a thick fog, hiding its streets from sight.
The landscape was covered in darkness as the sun disappeared behind the mountains.
After the victory, the team covered themselves with glory in the eyes of their fans.
The artist's new work covered her with praise from critics across the country.
The speech covered him with shame for his past actions.
Please cover the pot while the soup is simmering.
She covered her face with her hands in embarrassment.
The picnic table was covered with a bright red cloth.
