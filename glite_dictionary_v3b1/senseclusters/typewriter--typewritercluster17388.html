typewriter

noun
machine for printing characters on paper
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪpˌraɪtər / (AmE), / ˈtaɪpˌraɪtə / (BrE)
A machine with keys that, when pressed, print characters on paper, one at a time, used for writing documents before computers became common.

Examples

An old typewriter
He loves the clicking sound of a typewriter as he works on his novel.
She found an old typewriter in her grandmother's attic.
Writers used to rely on the typewriter before personal computers became popular.

Related Terms

  • antonym: computer - electronic device for storing and processing data
  • synonym: keyboard - set of keys for typing characters
