windowing

noun
segmenting data
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪndoʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪndəʊɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of dividing data or signals into segments, called windows, for analysis or processing

Examples

Windowing audio data
The audio editing software uses windowing to process sound signals in manageable pieces.
In data analysis, windowing helps to focus on specific time intervals for more detailed study.
Engineers apply windowing techniques to reduce noise in digital signal processing.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aggregation - the process of combining multiple pieces of data into a whole
  • synonym: segmentation - the act of dividing something into parts or sections
