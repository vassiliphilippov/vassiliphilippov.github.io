spark off

verb
provoke or trigger (an event or reaction) suddenly
3.9 0.09/m
Pronunciation: / sp…ërk …îf / (AmE), / sp…ëÀêk …íf / (BrE)
to cause something, especially a process, event, or reaction, to begin suddenly; to trigger.

Examples

To spark off a conflict
A simple misunderstanding can sometimes spark off a major conflict.
His remarks sparked off widespread protests across the city.
The announcement sparked off a heated debate among the committee members.

Synonyms

  • trigger off
    5.1 0.01/m
    trigger off causes events to start suddenly and forcefully, while spark off often starts a quick reaction or debate.
  • trigger
    4.2 30/m
    trigger means cause something to start or happen in general, while spark off suggests starting a sudden reaction or chain.
  • set off
    3.1 5.20/m
    set off means cause something to begin or activate, while spark off usually describes starting a sudden reaction or debate.
  • touch off
    7.4 0.96/m
    touch off causes a sudden event or explosion, while spark off more often starts a reaction or argument between people.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - to cause to become calm or quiet, opposite of incite or spark off
  • synonym: provoke - to cause a reaction, especially an intense one
  • synonym: trigger - to cause something to start; set off
