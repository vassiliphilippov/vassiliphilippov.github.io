hissy

adjective
easily upset
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈhɪsi / (AmE), / ˈhɪsi / (BrE)
easily upset or showing a petulant, overreacting temper, especially in a childish or exaggerated way

Examples

Hissy mood
He got a little hissy when his friends teased him about his new haircut.
Whenever things do not go her way, she tends to become quite hissy.
The coach warned the team not to get hissy over minor mistakes during practice.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - not easily upset or excited
  • synonym: petulant - easily irritated or annoyed in a childish way
