respect

verb
admire or hold in high regard
1.0
Pronunciation: / rɪˈspɛkt / (AmE), / rɪˈspɛkt / (BrE)
to admire or show regard for someone or something

Examples

To respect her decision
I greatly respect her for the way she handled the situation.
It is important to respect other people's opinions, even if you disagree.
Many students respect their teachers for their dedication.
Children are taught to respect their elders and teachers at school.
If you respect nature, you'll help preserve it for future generations.
It's important to respect the wishes of others, even when you disagree.

  • antonym: disrespect - to show a lack of respect; to insult
  • synonym: honor - to regard with great respect; to show esteem
