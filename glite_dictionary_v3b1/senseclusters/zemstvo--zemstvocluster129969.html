zemstvo

noun
Russian local council
Pronunciation: / ˈzɛmstvoʊ / (AmE), / ˈzɛmstvəʊ / (BrE)
a local government council in pre-revolutionary Russia responsible for managing community affairs, administration, and public services such as education and health

Examples

The zemstvo council
The zemstvo played a key role in improving rural education in 19th-century Russia.
Many villages relied on the zemstvo to organize healthcare and build schools.
After the revolution, the zemstvo system was replaced by new forms of local government.
The zemstvo was responsible for building schools and hospitals in rural areas of Russia.
Many reforms in the 19th century aimed to increase the power of the zemstvo in local governance.
Historians study the zemstvo to understand the development of Russian civil society before the revolution.
