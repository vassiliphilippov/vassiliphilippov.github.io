aprioristic

adjective
theoretical, not empirical
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪˌpraɪəˈrɪstɪk / (AmE), / ˌeɪˌprɪəˈrɪstɪk / (BrE)
based on theory, logic, or reasoning rather than on experience or observation

Examples

An aprioristic approach
The philosopher's aprioristic approach relied on logic rather than experimental data.
Critics argued that the theory was too aprioristic and disconnected from real-world evidence.
Her conclusions were drawn from aprioristic reasoning, not from any direct observation.
His argument was criticized for being too aprioristic and not considering real-world data.
Philosophers often debate whether mathematics is an aprioristic or empirical science.
The theory was developed in an aprioristic manner, relying solely on logical deduction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: a priori - relating to knowledge independent of experience
  • antonym: empirical - based on observation or experience rather than theory
  • synonym: theoretical - based on ideas or theory rather than practical experience
