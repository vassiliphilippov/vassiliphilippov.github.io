snark

verb
to make sarcastic remarks
8.4
Pronunciation: / snɑrk / (AmE), / snɑːk / (BrE)
to make sarcastic or mocking remarks, often in a witty or critical way

Examples

To snark about mistakes
She couldn't resist snarking about her coworker's unusual outfit during lunch.
Instead of offering helpful feedback, he just snarked at every suggestion.
People online often snark about celebrities and their latest scandals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mock - to make fun of someone or something in a cruel or sarcastic way
  • antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration
