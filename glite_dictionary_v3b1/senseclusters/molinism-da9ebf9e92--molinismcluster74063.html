Molinism

noun
doctrine on free will
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmoʊlɪˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈmɒlɪˌnɪzəm / (BrE)
a theological doctrine that seeks to reconcile God's foreknowledge with human free will, developed by Luis de Molina

Examples

Molinism doctrine
Many theologians have debated the implications of Molinism for centuries.
The professor explained how Molinism addresses the problem of divine foreknowledge.
Some Christian denominations reject Molinism in favor of other doctrines about free will.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Arminianism - a theological doctrine emphasizing free will in salvation
  • antonym: predestination - the doctrine that all events are willed by God
