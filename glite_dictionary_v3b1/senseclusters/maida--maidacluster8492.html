maida

noun
refined wheat flour
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪdə / (AmE), / ˈmaɪdə / (BrE)
a finely milled, refined wheat flour commonly used in South Asian cooking for breads and sweets

Examples

Soft maida bread
She made soft maida rotis for dinner.
Many Indian sweets are prepared using maida to achieve a light texture.
The recipe calls for two cups of maida and a pinch of salt.

Related Terms

  • synonym: all-purpose flour - a type of wheat flour used for general baking
  • antonym: whole wheat flour - flour made from the entire wheat grain
