vic

noun
informal for vicar
Pronunciation: / vɪk / (AmE), / vɪk / (BrE)
an informal term for a vicar, a priest in charge of a parish, especially in British English

Examples

The local vic
The vic greeted everyone warmly after the Sunday service.
We invited the vic to join us for tea in the church hall.
During the school visit, the vic explained the history of the old chapel.
