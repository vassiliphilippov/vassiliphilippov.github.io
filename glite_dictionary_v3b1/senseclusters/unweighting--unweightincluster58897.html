unweighting

noun
removal of weights
7.9
Pronunciation: /ʌn.ˈweɪ.tɪŋ/ /; / /ən.ˈweɪ.tɪŋ/ (AmE), /ʌn.ˈweɪ.tɪŋ/ /; / /ən.ˈweɪ.tɪŋ/ (BrE)
the process of removing weighting factors from data so that all observations are treated equally

Examples

Data unweighting
The analyst performed unweighting to ensure each response had equal influence on the results.
After unweighting, the data set no longer reflected the original sampling design.
Researchers often use unweighting when they want to analyze raw, unadjusted data.

Related Terms

  • synonym: normalization - the process of adjusting values measured on different scales to a common scale
  • antonym: weighting - the process of assigning different importance to data points
