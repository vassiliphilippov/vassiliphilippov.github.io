drudging

adjective
engaged in tedious or laborious work
Pronunciation: / ˈdrʌdʒɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdrʌdʒɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person or group) engaged in dull, tedious, or physically demanding work.

Examples

A drudging worker
In the story, the drudging servant dreams of a better life.
She was tired of the drudging routine that filled her every day.
The drudging workers continued their tasks despite the long hours and meager pay.

Related Terms

  • antonym: idling - not working or active
  • synonym: laboring - engaged in physical or manual work
  • synonym: toiling - working extremely hard or incessantly
