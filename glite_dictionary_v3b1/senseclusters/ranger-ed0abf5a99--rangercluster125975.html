Ranger

noun
Texas state law officer or frontier lawman
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪndʒər / (AmE), / ˈreɪndʒə / (BrE)
(in the US) a member of the Texas state highway patrol or, formerly, a mounted law officer responsible for maintaining order on the frontier, especially in Texas.

Examples

Texas Ranger officer
He grew up reading about the legendary Ranger exploits in history books.
Modern Rangers patrol highways and assist in law enforcement across Texas.
The Texas Ranger questioned the witnesses at the crime scene.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lawman - an officer responsible for enforcing the law, especially in the Old West
