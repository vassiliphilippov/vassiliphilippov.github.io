none other than

adverb
used to emphasize a notable identity
6.2
Pronunciation: / nʌn ˈʌðɚ ðæn / (AmE), / nʌn ˈʌðə ðan / (BrE)
used to highlight the identity of a person or thing in a way that draws attention to their noteworthiness or surprise

Examples

None other than the chef
Imagine my shock when the chef turned out to be none other than my former teacher.
The keynote speech was delivered by none other than the Nobel Prize winner.
The letter was signed by none other than the company president.
