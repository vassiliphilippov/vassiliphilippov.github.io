shelve

verb
postpone or suspend a plan or idea
7.9 5.0
Pronunciation: / ʃɛlv / (AmE), / ʃɛlv / (BrE)
to decide not to continue with a plan, project, or idea; to postpone or set aside

Examples

To shelve a plan
Due to budget constraints, the committee decided to shelve the new building project.
The proposal was shelved after much debate by the board.
We may have to shelve our travel plans until next year.
Because of the budget cuts, the company had to shelve its expansion plans.
She reluctantly shelved her plans to move abroad when a new opportunity arose at home.
They decided to shelve the proposal until more research could be done.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abandon - to give up on a plan or idea
  • synonym: postpone - to delay something until a later time
