weaker vessel

noun
someone considered weaker, esp. a woman (historical)
Pronunciation: / ˈwikər ˈvɛsəl / (AmE), / ˈwiːkə ˈvɛsl̩ / (BrE)
(historical, often disapproving) Someone, especially a woman, considered to be weaker or more fragile, both physically or morally.

Examples

The weaker vessel
In many old texts, women are described as the weaker vessel compared to men.
She refused to accept the role of a weaker vessel in modern society.
The phrase weaker vessel now carries disapproving or outdated connotations.
