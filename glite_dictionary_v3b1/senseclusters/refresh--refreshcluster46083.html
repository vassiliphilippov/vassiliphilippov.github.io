refresh

verb
make someone feel lively or revived
1.0 2.4
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈfreʃ/ /; / /rə.ˈfreʃ/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈfreʃ/ /; / /rə.ˈfreʃ/ (BrE)
to make someone or something feel or appear new, clean, energetic, or less tired again; to restore strength or freshness

Examples

To refresh yourself
A cool shower can really refresh you after a long run.
She stopped to refresh herself with a glass of lemonade.
The breeze refreshed everyone at the outdoor event.
A cool shower in the morning always helps me refresh before work.
After a long walk in the sun, she went inside to refresh herself.
He paused during the exam to refresh his mind with a glass of water.
A cold drink can quickly refresh you on a hot summer day.
A short walk outside will help refresh your mind after a long meeting.
She pressed the button to refresh the webpage and see the updated results.

Related Terms

  • synonym: invigorate - to fill with energy or strength
  • synonym: reinvigorate - to give new energy or strength to
  • synonym: renew - make new or as if new again
  • synonym: revive - to restore to life or consciousness
  • antonym: tire - to make someone feel tired
