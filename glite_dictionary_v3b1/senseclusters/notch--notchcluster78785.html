notch

verb
cut a V-shaped mark or slit
Pronunciation: / nɑtʃ / (AmE), / nɒtʃ / (BrE)
to make a small cut, indentation, or V-shaped mark on an edge or surface, often for record, measurement, or decoration

Examples

To notch a stick
Before tying the knot, he notched the rope to secure it more firmly.
She carefully notched the wooden panel so it would fit perfectly.
The carpenter used a knife to notch the end of the board.
He notched the stick each day to track the passage of time.
She notched the wall after every successful hunt to keep count.
The carpenter notched the board to mark where to cut next.
