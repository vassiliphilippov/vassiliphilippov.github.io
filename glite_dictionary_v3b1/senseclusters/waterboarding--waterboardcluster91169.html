waterboarding

noun
torture by simulated drowning
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔːtərˌbɔːrdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəbɔːdɪŋ / (BrE)
A form of torture in which water is poured over a cloth covering the face and breathing passages of a captive, causing the sensation of drowning.

Examples

Waterboarding torture
He testified that waterboarding left him traumatized for years after his release.
Many human rights groups have condemned waterboarding as inhumane and illegal.
The captured men reported that they had endured waterboarding during their imprisonment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: torture - the act of inflicting severe pain as punishment or to force information
