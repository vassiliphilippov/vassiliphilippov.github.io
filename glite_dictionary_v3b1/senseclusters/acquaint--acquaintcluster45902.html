acquaint

verb
to make someone aware; to inform
1.0 4.0
Pronunciation: / əˈkweɪnt / (AmE), / əˈkweɪnt / (BrE)
to make someone aware of or familiar with something or someone; to introduce or inform

Examples

To acquaint someone with facts
I would appreciate it if you could acquaint me with the current situation.
Please acquaint your team with the new procedures before next week.
She took a moment to acquaint him with the company policies.
It is important to acquaint new employees with the company's safety procedures.
The guide helped the tourists acquaint themselves with local customs and traditions.
You should acquaint yourself with the basic rules before starting the game.
Allow me to acquaint you with the members of our team.
It took time for the students to acquaint themselves with university life.
She took a moment to acquaint herself with the new software before starting work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: familiarize - to make oneself or someone else familiar with something
  • synonym: inform - to give someone information; to tell
  • synonym: introduce - to bring (someone) into a group; to make known by name or sight
  • synonym: notify - to formally tell someone about something
