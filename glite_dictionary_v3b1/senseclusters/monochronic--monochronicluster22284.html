monochronic

adjective
single-task focused
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːnəˈkrɑːnɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəˈkrɒnɪk / (BrE)
describing a culture or person that prefers to do one thing at a time and values schedules and punctuality

Examples

Monochronic time culture
In a monochronic workplace, meetings start exactly on time and interruptions are discouraged.
People from monochronic cultures often find flexible schedules challenging when traveling abroad.
Her monochronic approach to work means she prefers to finish one task before starting another.

Related Terms

  • antonym: polychronic - describing a culture or person that prefers to do multiple things at once
