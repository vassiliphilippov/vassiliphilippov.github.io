Lithane

noun
trade name for lithium carbonate drug/compound
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪˌθeɪn / (AmE), / ˈlɪˌθeɪn / (BrE)
A proprietary name for lithium carbonate, a white powder used in the manufacture of glass and ceramics and, as a drug, to treat certain forms of depression and manic-depressive disorder.

Examples

Lithane powder
Manufacturers use Lithane in both the glass industry and in psychiatric medicine.
Some patients may experience side effects while taking Lithane for bipolar disorder.
The psychiatrist prescribed Lithane to help stabilize her mood swings.
