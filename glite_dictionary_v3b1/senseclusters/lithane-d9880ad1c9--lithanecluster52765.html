Lithane

noun
trade name for lithium carbonate drug/compound
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪˌθeɪn / (AmE), / ˈlɪˌθeɪn / (BrE)
A proprietary name for lithium carbonate, a white powder used in the manufacture of glass and ceramics and, as a drug, to treat certain forms of depression and manic-depressive disorder.
