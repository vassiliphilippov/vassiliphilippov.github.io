narratio

noun
facts section of speech
9.9
Pronunciation: / nəˈreɪʃioʊ / (AmE), / nəˈreɪtɪəʊ / (BrE)
the section of a classical speech in which the speaker presents the facts of the case

Examples

The speech's narratio
In his opening, the orator's narratio carefully detailed the events leading up to the dispute.
A well-structured narratio helps the audience understand the background of the case.
The narratio was followed by arguments that built on the facts just presented.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exordium - the introduction of a classical speech
  • synonym: narration - the act of telling a story or describing events
