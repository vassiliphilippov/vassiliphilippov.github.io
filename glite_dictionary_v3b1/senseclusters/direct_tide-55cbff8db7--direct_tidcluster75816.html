A high tide occurring on one side of the Earth at the same time as a high tide on the opposite side, caused by the gravitational pull of the moon and sun.

Examples

The direct tide
At the same time as the direct tide faces the moon, a second high tide occurs on the opposite side of the Earth.
Scientists monitor the direct tide to better understand the effects of lunar gravity on the oceans.
The phenomenon of a direct tide explains why there are two high tides each day in most coastal areas.
