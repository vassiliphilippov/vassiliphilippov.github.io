a physical trait that develops during sexual maturity and distinguishes the sexes of a species, but is not directly involved in reproduction, such as facial hair or breast development

Examples

A secondary sex characteristic trait
Breast development in females is an example of a secondary sex characteristic that emerges in adolescence.
Facial hair in males is a common secondary sex characteristic that appears during puberty.
Voice deepening in boys is considered a secondary sex characteristic rather than a primary one.
