electric field

noun
region of force around a charged particle
4.4 3.2
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈlek.trɪk fiːld/ /; / /ə.ˈlek.trɪk fiːld/ /; / /ɪ.ˈlek.trɪk fɪld/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈlek.trɪk fiːld/ /; / /ə.ˈlek.trɪk fiːld/ (BrE)
the region around a charged particle or object within which a force would be exerted on other charged particles or objects

Examples

An electric field force
An electric field can cause charged particles to move and create a current.
In physics class, we used iron filings to visualize the pattern of the electric field around a charged rod.
The strength of the electric field decreases as you move farther from the charge.
