take a look

verb
look at something attentively
1.0
Pronunciation: / teɪk ə lʊk / (AmE), / teɪk ə lʊk / (BrE)
to look at or examine something, either briefly or carefully

Examples

To take a look at a car
Could you take a look at my car? It's making a strange noise.
Let me take a look at your report before you submit it.
You should take a look at the website for more information.
Could you take a look at this report before the meeting?
I’ll take a look at your essay and suggest some improvements.
She stopped to take a look at the latest art display in the lobby.

Related Terms

  • synonym: have a look - to look at something (synonymous phrasing)
  • synonym: look at - to direct one's gaze towards; to inspect or examine
