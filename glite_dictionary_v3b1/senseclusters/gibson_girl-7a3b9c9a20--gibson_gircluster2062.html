Gibson Girl

noun
the idealized young woman of 1890s America
Pronunciation: / ˈgɪbsən ɡɜrl / (AmE), / ˈɡɪbsən ɡɜːl / (BrE)
An idealized American young woman of the 1890s and early 1900s, characterized by beauty, independence, and poise, as depicted in drawings by Charles Dana Gibson.

Examples

The Gibson Girl style
Many fashion trends of the era were influenced by the style of the Gibson Girl.
She dressed as a Gibson Girl for the vintage costume party, complete with high hair and a long skirt.
The Gibson Girl became a symbol of feminine elegance and confidence in the late 19th century.
