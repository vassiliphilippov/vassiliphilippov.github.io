unimproved

adjective
not developed or enhanced
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnɪmˈpruvd / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪmˈpruːvd / (BrE)
not developed, improved, or made more valuable; in its natural or original state, especially referring to land or property

Examples

Unimproved land
Many rural areas still have unimproved dirt roads that can become impassable after heavy rain.
Taxes on unimproved land are generally lower than those on developed property.
The house was sold as an unimproved property, requiring significant investment to be habitable.
Prices for unimproved lots are generally much lower than for those with utilities or buildings.
The estate includes 100 acres of unimproved land, mostly covered by dense forest.
They purchased an unimproved parcel on the outskirts of town to build their dream home.

Related Terms

  • antonym: improved - made better or more valuable
