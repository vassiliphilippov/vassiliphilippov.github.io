roman à clef

noun
novel with disguised real people/events
Pronunciation: / roʊˌmɑn ɑ ˈkleɪ / (AmE), / ˌrəʊmæn a ˈkleɪ / (BrE)
A novel in which real people or events appear with invented names, allowing knowledgeable readers to identify them from their fictional disguises.

Examples

A roman a clef novel
Critics soon realized the book was a roman à clef about the author's former employer.
She wrote a roman à clef that thinly disguised the scandals of her social circle.
The novel became popular as readers tried to match each character in the roman à clef to a real-life figure.
