quartz lamp

noun
lamp with a quartz enclosure
Pronunciation: / kwɔrts læmp / (AmE), / kwɔːts læmp / (BrE)
A mercury-vapor lamp enclosed in a quartz container instead of glass, used for high-intensity lighting and ultraviolet applications.

Examples

A quartz lamp bulb
A quartz lamp produces intense illumination ideal for certain scientific experiments.
Because the quartz lamp can handle higher temperatures, it's preferred over glass lamps in some settings.
The laboratory used a quartz lamp to sterilize the equipment with ultraviolet light.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mercury-vapor lamp - lamp that produces light by an electric arc through vaporized mercury
