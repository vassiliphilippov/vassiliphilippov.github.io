post-alveolar

noun
speech sound near the alveolar ridge
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊst ælˈviələr / (AmE), / ˌpəʊst ælˈviːələ / (BrE)
(phonetics) A speech sound produced with the tongue near or touching the back of the alveolar ridge, just behind the upper front teeth.

Examples

Post-alveolar sound
In phonetics class, we discussed how post-alveolar sounds differ from alveolar and palatal ones.
Many Slavic languages have several post-alveolar consonants in their phonetic inventories.
The English sound /ʃ/ as in 'shoe' is a post-alveolar fricative.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: alveolar - a speech sound produced with the tongue against the alveolar ridge
  • degree higher: palatal - a speech sound produced with the tongue near the hard palate
