disruptiveness

noun
degree of causing change
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈrʌptɪvnəs / (AmE), / dɪsˈrʌptɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of causing disturbance, interruption, or significant change to a situation, system, or environment

Examples

Analysts debated the disruptiveness of the startup's approach to the traditional market.
The disruptiveness of the new policy led to confusion among employees.
Teachers are concerned about the disruptiveness of mobile phones in the classroom.
The disruptiveness of the protest forced the event to be postponed.

  • synonym: disturbance - an interruption of a state of peace or order
  • synonym: innovation - the introduction of new ideas or methods
  • antonym: orderliness - the quality of being neat and well-organized
  • antonym: stability - the state of being steady and unchanging
