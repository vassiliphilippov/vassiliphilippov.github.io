tabulating machine

noun
early data-processing device
Pronunciation: / təˈbjuːleɪtɪŋ məˈʃin / (AmE), / təˈbjuːleɪtɪŋ məˈʃiːn / (BrE)
an early electromechanical device used to process and summarize data, especially by sorting and counting punched cards.

Examples

Early tabulating machine
Early accountants relied on the tabulating machine to quickly count and sort huge volumes of information.
Herman Hollerith's tabulating machine was a key development in the history of computing.
The tabulating machine revolutionized business by automating the processing of census data.

Related Terms

  • synonym: punch card - a card with holes punched in it to represent data for processing by machines
