flickertail

noun
a type of ground squirrel of western North America
8.7 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈflɪkərˌteɪl / (AmE), / ˈflɪkəteɪl / (BrE)
A ground squirrel, especially the Richardson's ground squirrel, found in sagebrush and grassland areas of the western United States and Canada, noted for its characteristic tail movements.

Examples

A flickertail squirrel
Farmers often spot flickertails scurrying across open grasslands early in the morning.
North Dakota is sometimes called the 'Flickertail State' after this unique squirrel species.
The flickertail is commonly seen darting in and out of its burrow on the prairies.
