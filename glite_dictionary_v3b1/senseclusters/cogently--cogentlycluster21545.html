cogently

adverb
clearly and convincingly
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊdʒəntli / (AmE), / ˈkəʊdʒəntli / (BrE)
in a clear, logical, and convincing manner

Examples

To cogently argue
The lawyer argued cogently for her client's innocence in court.
He presented his ideas cogently, making it easy for everyone to understand his point.
During the debate, she responded cogently to every question raised by the panel.

Related Terms

  • synonym: persuasively - in a way that convinces others
  • antonym: vaguely - in a way that is not clear or definite
