for all intents and purposes

adverb
in every practical sense
4.2
Pronunciation: / fər ɔl ɪnˈtɛnts ənd ˈpɜr.pəs.ɪz / (AmE), / fə ɔːl ɪnˈtents ənd ˈpɜː.pəs.ɪz / (BrE)
used to indicate that a situation is true in a practical sense, even if it is not formally or technically the case.

Examples

For all intents and purposes
For all intents and purposes, the renovation project is complete, even though a few minor tasks remain.
The new regulations have, for all intents and purposes, changed how the company operates.
Their marriage was, for all intents and purposes, over long before the divorce was finalized.

Related Terms

  • synonym: effectively - in effect; in practice rather than in theory
  • synonym: practically - in a practical sense; almost
