pelagic

adjective
of or relating to the open sea
7.8
Pronunciation: / pəˈlædʒɪk / (AmE), / pəˈlædʒɪk / (BrE)
relating to or occurring in the open sea, especially away from the coast or sea floor.

Examples

Pelagic fish
Many species of tuna are considered pelagic fish, living far from the shore.
Scientists study pelagic currents to understand how nutrients are distributed in the ocean.
The pelagic zone is home to whales, sharks, and countless smaller marine creatures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: benthic - relating to the bottom of the sea or ocean
