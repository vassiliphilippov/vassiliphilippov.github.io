direct quotation

noun
exact words quoted from a speaker or text
5.8
Pronunciation: / dəˈrɛkt ˌkwoʊˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈrɛkt ˌkwəʊˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
an exact reproduction of someone's spoken or written words, often marked by quotation marks

Examples

A direct quotation from Shakespeare
In his speech, he began with a direct quotation from Shakespeare to make his point.
The journalist used a direct quotation from the interview to support her argument.
When writing academic essays, it is important to include a direct quotation when referencing original sources.

Related Terms

  • antonym: indirect quotation - a report of what someone said using different words (paraphrase) rather than exact wording
