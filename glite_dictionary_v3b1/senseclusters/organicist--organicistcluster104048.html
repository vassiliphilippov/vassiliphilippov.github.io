organicist

noun
theory of living wholes
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɔːr.ˈgæn.ɪst/ /; / /ɑːr.ˈgæn.ɪst/ (AmE), /ɔː.ˈgæn.ɪst/ (BrE)
a person who believes in or studies the theory that living systems function as integrated wholes rather than as collections of parts

Examples

The organicist theory
The organicist emphasized the importance of understanding the body as a complete system.
As an organicist, she rejected the idea that life can be explained only by its parts.
Many scientists debate whether an organicist or reductionist approach is better for studying biology.

Related Terms

  • synonym: holist - a person who believes that systems should be viewed as wholes
  • antonym: reductionist - a person who analyzes complex systems by breaking them down into simpler parts
