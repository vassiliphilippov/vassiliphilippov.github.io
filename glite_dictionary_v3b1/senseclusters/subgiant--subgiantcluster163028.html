subgiant

adjective
star between phases
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌdʒaɪənt / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌdʒaɪənt / (BrE)
relating to a star that is in the evolutionary stage between the main sequence and the giant phase

Examples

Subgiant star phase
Astronomers classified the star as a subgiant based on its temperature and brightness.
The subgiant phase is a brief period in a star's evolution before it becomes a giant.
They studied several subgiant stars to understand how they change over time.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: giant - a star that has evolved off the main sequence and expanded greatly
  • degree lower: main-sequence - a star in the main, stable phase of its life cycle
