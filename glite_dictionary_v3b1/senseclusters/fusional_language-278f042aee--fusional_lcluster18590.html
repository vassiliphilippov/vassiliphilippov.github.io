a language in which single morphemes can express several grammatical meanings (such as tense, number, and case), characteristic of certain synthetic languages.

Examples

A fusional language suffix
In a fusional language, a single suffix might indicate case, number, and gender at the same time.
Many Indo-European languages, like Russian and Latin, are considered fusional languages.
Spanish is an example of a fusional language, where verb endings encode both tense and subject.
