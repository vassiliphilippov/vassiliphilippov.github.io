half step

noun
the smallest interval in Western music
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈhæf ˌstɛp / (AmE), / hɑːf ˌstɛp / (BrE)
the smallest musical interval in Western music, equal to the distance between two adjacent keys on a keyboard instrument or two adjacent notes in a chromatic scale

Examples

A half step in music
If you move up a half step from C, you reach C-sharp.
On the piano, a half step is played by moving to the very next key, whether black or white.
The melody ascends by half steps in the final bars.

Related Terms

  • synonym: semitone - another term for half step in music
  • degree higher: whole step - a musical interval equal to two half steps, or semitones
