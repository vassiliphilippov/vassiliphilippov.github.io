naval brass

noun
a corrosion-resistant brass alloy for marine use
8.0 5.3
Pronunciation: /ˈneɪ.vəl bræs/ (AmE), /ˈneɪ.vəl brɑːs/ /; / /ˈneɪ.vəl bræs/ (BrE)
An alloy of brass containing a small amount of tin, making it resistant to corrosion by seawater; used especially in marine applications.

Examples

Naval brass alloy
Engineers often use naval brass in ship fittings for its excellent durability.
The propeller was made of naval brass to prevent corrosion from seawater.
This pipe is manufactured from naval brass to meet marine industry standards.
