produce

verb
create or manufacture something
2.0 140/m
Pronunciation: / pr…ôÀàdus / (AmE), / pr…ôÀàdjuÀês / (BrE)
to make, create, or cause something to exist or happen

Examples

To produce results
Modern farms produce more food than ever before thanks to new technology.
The factory produces thousands of televisions every month for the international market.
This artist produced a series of sculptures that gained national attention.
Certain medications can produce unwanted skin rashes as a side effect.
The caterpillar will produce beautiful wings during metamorphosis.
With age, some men produce grey hair as a natural change.

Synonyms

  • make
    1.0 900/m
    make means causing or building things in everyday situations, while produce sounds broader and often more formal for outputs.
  • create
    1.0 400/m
    create means bringing something new into existence, while produce can mean both making items and causing events to happen.
  • generate
    3.2 60/m
    generate means producing something as the result of a process or action, while produce is more general everyday making.
  • yield
    3.6 30/m
    yield often means give or result in an output or profit, while produce covers making, creating, or causing many kinds of things.
  • bring forth
    4.1 1/m
    bring forth is a formal or poetic way to bring something into view, while produce is the common, neutral verb for making.
  • form
    1.0 50/m
    form means shape or create something, often an organization or group, while produce is broader and includes making things or causing events.
  • bring about
    3.0 16/m
    bring about means cause something to happen, especially events or change, while produce also means make physical things as well as cause.
  • give rise
    3.5 7/m
    give rise means cause something to happen or exist, often indirectly, while produce is used for actively making or creating things.

Related Terms

  • antonym: consume - to use up something, especially resources or products
  • synonym: create - to bring something into existence
  • antonym: destroy - to put an end to the existence of something, the opposite of producing
  • synonym: develop - to grow or cause to grow and become more mature or advanced
  • synonym: generate - to produce or create something, especially energy or power
  • synonym: grow - to undergo natural development by increasing in size and changing physically
  • synonym: manufacture - to make something on a large scale using machinery
