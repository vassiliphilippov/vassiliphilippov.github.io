bugeye

noun
Chesapeake Bay sailboat
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌɡˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˈbʌɡˌaɪ / (BrE)
a traditional two-masted sailing vessel from Chesapeake Bay, used mainly for oyster dredging and recognized by its broad hull and centerboard

Examples

Chesapeake bugeye boat
The historic bugeye sailed across the bay, its sails full in the morning breeze.
A restored bugeye is now a centerpiece at the maritime museum.
Fishermen once relied on the bugeye for harvesting oysters in Chesapeake Bay.
