radio-frequency spectroscopy

noun
study of transitions using radio frequencies
7.6
Pronunciation: /ˈreɪ.dɪ.oʊ ˈfriː.kwən.siː spek.ˈtrɑːs.kə.piː/ (AmE), /ˈreɪ.dɪ.əʊ ˈfriː.kwən.siː spek.ˈtrɒs.kə.piː/ (BrE)
The branch of spectroscopy that studies the measurement of transitions between atomic or molecular energy levels separated by frequencies in the range of about 10⁵ to 10⁹ hertz, notably lower than those involved in optical spectroscopy.

Examples

Radio-frequency spectroscopy measurement
Radio-frequency spectroscopy provides valuable insights into atomic and molecular structure by measuring transitions in the radio-frequency range.
Advancements in radio-frequency spectroscopy have enabled the development of precise imaging techniques in medicine.
The physicist specialized in radio-frequency spectroscopy to analyze molecular rotations and internal energy levels.
