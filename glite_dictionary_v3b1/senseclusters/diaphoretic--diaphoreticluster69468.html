diaphoretic

noun
medicine inducing sweating
Pronunciation: / ˌdaɪ.əfəˈrɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌdaɪ.əfəˈrɛtɪk / (BrE)
A medicine or substance that promotes or induces sweating.

Examples

A diaphoretic remedy
Many herbal remedies act as mild diaphoretics during a cold or flu.
The doctor prescribed a diaphoretic to help reduce the patient's fever.
The use of a diaphoretic was common in traditional medicine to treat chills.
