pragmatist

noun
supporter of philosophical pragmatism
Pronunciation: / ˈpræɡmətɪst / (AmE), / ˈpræɡmətɪst / (BrE)
a person who advocates or adheres to the philosophical doctrine of pragmatism, which judges ideas by their practical consequences

Examples

A philosophical pragmatist
As a leading pragmatist, he argued that beliefs must be measured by their usefulness.
Many American philosophers in the early twentieth century were pragmatists who focused on practical effects.
The debate was shaped by pragmatist thinkers who prioritized real-world outcomes over abstract principles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pragmatism - the philosophical doctrine that evaluates theories or beliefs by their practical consequences
