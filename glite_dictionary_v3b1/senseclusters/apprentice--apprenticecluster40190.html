apprentice

noun
person learning a trade from an expert
Pronunciation: / əˈprɛn.tɪs / (AmE), / əˈprɛn.tɪs / (BrE)
a person who learns a trade or craft by working for a skilled employer over a fixed period

Examples

An apprentice baker
After finishing school, she became an apprentice at a local bakery.
In medieval times, it was common for young people to work as an apprentice before becoming a master.
The apprentice watched carefully as the carpenter demonstrated the correct technique.

Related Terms

  • synonym: trainee - a person undergoing training for a particular job or profession
