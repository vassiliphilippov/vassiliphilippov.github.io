revel

verb
celebrate noisily; take great pleasure
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈrɛvəl / (BrE)
to celebrate or take great pleasure in a lively and noisy way, especially by drinking and dancing

Examples

To revel all night
Crowds revelled in the city streets after the national team's win.
He loves to revel in the excitement of the festival every year.
The students revelled in their victory by dancing and singing through the night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carouse - to drink and party noisily
  • synonym: celebrate - to observe with festivities or ceremonies
  • antonym: mourn - to feel or express sadness, especially for someone's death
