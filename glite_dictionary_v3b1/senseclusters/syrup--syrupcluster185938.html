syrup

noun
thick sweet liquid, flavoring or sweetener
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪ.rəp/ /; / /ˈsɜːr.əp/ (AmE), /ˈsɪ.rəp/ /; / /ˈsɜː.rəp/ (BrE)
a thick, sweet liquid made by dissolving sugar in water, often used as a flavoring or sweetener

Examples

A spoonful of syrup
A spoonful of syrup made the bitter tea taste much better.
She poured maple syrup over her pancakes for breakfast.
The pharmacist mixed the medicine with a sugary syrup to make it easier for children to swallow.
