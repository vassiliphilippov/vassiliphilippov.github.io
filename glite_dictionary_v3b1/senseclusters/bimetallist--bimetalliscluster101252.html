bimetallist

noun
advocate of a two-metal money standard
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪməˈtælɪst / (AmE), / ˌbaɪmɪˈtælɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports or advocates the use of a monetary system based on two metals, typically gold and silver, as legal tender at a fixed ratio

Examples

A bimetallist supporter
During the late 19th century, many politicians in the United States were outspoken bimetallists.
Historians often debate the influence bimetallists had on economic policy during the 1800s.
The bimetallist argued passionately for the reintroduction of silver into the currency system.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monometallist - an advocate of a monetary system based on one metal
