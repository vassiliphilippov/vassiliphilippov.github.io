photomorphogenesis

noun
plant growth by light
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊtoʊˌmɔːrfəˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊˌmɔːfəˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (BrE)
the process by which plants develop and grow in response to light signals

Examples

Photomorphogenesis in plants
Scientists investigate photomorphogenesis to learn how light influences plant structure and function.
The process of photomorphogenesis helps seedlings adapt to their environment after germination.
Understanding photomorphogenesis is important for improving crop yields in agriculture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: phototropism - the growth of plants in response to the direction of light
  • antonym: skotomorphogenesis - plant development in the absence of light
