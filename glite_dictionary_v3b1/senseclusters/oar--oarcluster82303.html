oar

noun
implement for rowing or steering a boat
5.0
Pronunciation: / ɔr / (AmE), / ɔː / (BrE)
A long pole with a flat blade at one end, used to row or steer a boat through the water.

Examples

A wooden oar
He carved his own wooden oar for his fishing canoe.
She lost her oar while steering the small boat across the lake.
The rowers dipped each oar into the water in perfect rhythm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: paddle - a similar implement, usually shorter, used for propelling a canoe or kayak
