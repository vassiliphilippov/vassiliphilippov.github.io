High Anglicanism

noun
emphasis on Catholic tradition in Anglicanism
9.5
Pronunciation: / haɪ ˈæŋɡlɪˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / haɪ ˈæŋɡlɪnɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A doctrine and practice within the Church of England that emphasizes Catholic tradition, rituals, and theology.

Examples

High Anglicanism tradition
Debates about High Anglicanism shaped the identity of the Church of England in the nineteenth century.
Many churches embracing High Anglicanism emphasize the importance of sacraments and ritual.
The bishop was a strong proponent of High Anglicanism and supported traditional liturgical practices.
