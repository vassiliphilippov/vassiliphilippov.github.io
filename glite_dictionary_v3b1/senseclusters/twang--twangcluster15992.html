twang

verb
make a sharp, vibrating sound
Pronunciation: / twæŋ / (AmE), / twæŋ / (BrE)
to make a sharp, vibrating sound by plucking or striking a string or similar object

Examples

To twang a string
He twanged the rubber band and watched it shoot across the room.
She carefully twanged the banjo string, producing a bright note.
The musician twanged his guitar several times to tune it.
He twanged the strings of his guitar before the performance began.
She twanged the bow to test its tension before shooting the arrow.
The harpist gently twanged each string, filling the hall with music.
As the archer released the arrow, the bowstring twanged sharply in the quiet air.
She accidentally bumped the ruler, causing it to twang loudly on the desk.
The guitar string began to twang as soon as he plucked it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clang - make a loud, resonant metallic sound
  • synonym: pluck - to pull and release (a string), causing it to vibrate
  • synonym: strum - to play a stringed instrument in a sweeping motion
  • synonym: thrum - make a continuous rhythmic humming sound
