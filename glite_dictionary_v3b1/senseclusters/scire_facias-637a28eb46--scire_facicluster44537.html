scire facias

noun
judicial writ requiring cause to be shown
Pronunciation: / ˌskaɪˌriː fəˈsaɪəs / (AmE), / ˌskaɪəri ˈfaːsɪˌæs / (BrE)
a judicial writ requiring a party to show cause why a record should not be enforced or annulled

Examples

A scire facias writ
A scire facias proceeding can be used to revive a dormant judgment.
His lawyer filed a response to the scire facias challenging the enforcement of the record.
The court issued a scire facias to compel the debtor to appear and show cause.
