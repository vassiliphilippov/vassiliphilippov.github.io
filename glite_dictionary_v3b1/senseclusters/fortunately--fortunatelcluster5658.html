fortunately

adverb
by good luck; luckily
2.5 26/m
Pronunciation: / Ààf…îrt É…ôn…ôtli / (AmE), / Ààf…îÀêt É…ôn…ôtli / (BrE)
By good luck; as a result of favorable circumstances or chance.

Examples

Fortunately, the weather
Fortunately, the weather stayed clear during our entire trip.
He missed the last train, but fortunately there was a bus he could catch instead.
The document was lost, but fortunately a backup copy existed.

Synonyms

  • luckily
    1.0 13/m
    luckily is used when good chance helps people or events, while fortunately is a neutral adverb for good outcomes.
  • fortuitously
    7.5 0.36/m
    fortuitously sounds formal and focuses on chance, while fortunately is common and fits everyday writing or speech.
  • serendipitously
    7.7 0.28/m
    serendipitously suggests a happy discovery by chance, while fortunately is a broad comment on any lucky outcome.
  • as luck would have it
    5.4 0.43/m
    as luck would have it is a spoken phrase that notes a lucky event, while fortunately is a single-word adverb used in many styles.
  • happily
    1.0 4.39/m
    happily often shows emotion or pleasure, while fortunately points to a lucky result without strong personal feeling.
