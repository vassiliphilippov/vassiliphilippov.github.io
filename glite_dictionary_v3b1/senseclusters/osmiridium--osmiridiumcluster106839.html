osmiridium

noun
hard, corrosion-resistant osmium-iridium alloy mineral
9.3
Pronunciation: / ɑzˈmɪrɪdiəm / (AmE), / ɒzˈmɪrɪdiəm / (BrE)
A hard, corrosion-resistant mineral consisting of a natural alloy of osmium and iridium, often with traces of rhodium and platinum; used in items such as needles and pen nibs.

Examples

Hard osmiridium mineral
Because of its resistance to wear, osmiridium became essential in producing surgical needles.
Mining companies sought veins rich in osmiridium to supply manufacturers of fine instruments.
The tip of the fountain pen was made from osmiridium, prized for its durability.

  • synonym: osmium - a hard, brittle, bluish-white transition metal element
