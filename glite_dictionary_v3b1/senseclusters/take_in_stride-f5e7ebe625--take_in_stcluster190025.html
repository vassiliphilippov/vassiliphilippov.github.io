take in stride

verb
handle calmly and without difficulty
Pronunciation: / teɪk ɪn straɪd / (AmE), / teɪk ɪn straɪd / (BrE)
to deal with something calmly and without letting it upset or disturb you

Examples

To take in stride problems
Despite the extra workload, she took it in stride and completed everything on time.
He takes criticism in stride, never letting it affect his confidence.
They took the unexpected delay in stride and waited patiently for the train.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cope - to deal effectively with something difficult
  • antonym: falter - to lose strength or confidence, especially in the face of difficulty
