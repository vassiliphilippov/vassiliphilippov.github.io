outplay

verb
perform better than an opponent
Pronunciation: / aʊtˈpleɪ / (AmE), / aʊtˈpleɪ / (BrE)
to perform better than and defeat an opponent in a game or competition

Examples

To outplay the opponent
Despite their injuries, they still outplayed the visiting team and secured the championship.
If you want to win, you'll need to outplay your competitors with smart strategies.
The home team managed to outplay their rivals in every aspect of the game.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defeat - win a victory over someone
  • antonym: lose - be defeated in a competition or contest
  • synonym: outperform - perform better than someone else
