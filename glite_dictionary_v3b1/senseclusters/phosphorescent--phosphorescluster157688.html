phosphorescent

adjective
emitting light without heat
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˌfɑːsfəˈrɛsənt / (AmE), / ˌfɒsfəˈrɛsənt / (BrE)
emitting light without noticeable heat, typically by a slow chemical process such as oxidation of phosphorus or other substances

Examples

Phosphorescent light
Scientists studied the phosphorescent qualities of the mineral under ultraviolet light.
The phosphorescent markings on the clock made it easy to read in the dark.
The algae created a phosphorescent glow in the waves at night.
