carotenoid

noun
a class of yellow to red plant pigments
7.7 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈkær.ə.tən.nɔɪd/ (AmE), /ˈkær.ə.tən.nɔɪd/ (BrE)
any of a class of naturally occurring pigments, typically yellow to red, found in plants and some animals

Examples

Carotenoid pigments
Carrots contain a large amount of carotenoid pigments, which give them their orange color.
Scientists study carotenoids to understand their role in plant photosynthesis.
The flamingo's pink feathers are a result of the carotenoid pigments in its diet.
