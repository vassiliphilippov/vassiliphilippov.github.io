internment

noun
placing property into legal custody
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɝːnmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːnmənt / (BrE)
the act of placing private property in the legal custody of an officer, especially as a preventive or protective measure

Examples

Legal internment of property
Following the investigation, the internment of the car was authorized by the authorities.
Legal internment of goods may occur when ownership is unclear or contested.
The court ordered the internment of the disputed assets until the case was resolved.
