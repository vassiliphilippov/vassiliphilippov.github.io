repass

verb
to approve again
8.8
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈpæs/ (AmE), /riː.ˈpɑːs/ (BrE)
to approve, confirm, or pass again, especially referring to a law, bill, or official measure

Examples

To repass a law
The committee decided to repass the bill after making several important amendments.
If the president vetoes the proposal, Congress may attempt to repass it with a larger majority.
Following public feedback, the council will repass the ordinance to address the concerns raised.
