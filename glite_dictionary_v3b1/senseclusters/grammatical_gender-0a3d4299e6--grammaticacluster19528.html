grammatical gender

noun
linguistic system classifying nouns by gender
Pronunciation: / ɡrəˌmætɪkəl ˈdʒɛndɚ / (AmE), / ɡrəˌmatɪkəl ˈdʒɛndə / (BrE)
A classifying system in certain languages in which nouns, pronouns, and adjectives are grouped into categories (such as masculine, feminine, or neuter), often influencing agreement within a sentence.

Examples

Grammatical gender system
Learning the grammatical gender of every noun is essential for mastering German.
Many Indo-European languages use grammatical gender to categorize nouns as masculine, feminine, or neuter.
Unlike English, French relies heavily on grammatical gender for noun and adjective agreement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gender - the state of being male, female, or neuter in language; grammatical classification
