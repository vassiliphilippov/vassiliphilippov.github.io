replete

adjective
filled or well-supplied with
Pronunciation: / rɪˈplit / (AmE), / rɪˈpliːt / (BrE)
filled or well-supplied with something; having a large amount of something (usually used as 'replete with').

Examples

Replete with ideas
His essay was replete with insightful observations and clever arguments.
The garden was replete with vibrant flowers in every color imaginable.
The museum is replete with rare artifacts from ancient civilizations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: empty - containing nothing; not filled or occupied
  • synonym: full - containing as much as possible; filled to capacity
