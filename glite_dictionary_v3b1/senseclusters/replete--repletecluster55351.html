replete

adjective
filled or well-supplied with
6.6 2.28/m
Pronunciation: / r…™Ààplit / (AmE), / r…™ÀàpliÀêt / (BrE)
filled or well-supplied with something; having a large amount of something (usually used as 'replete with').

Examples

Replete with ideas
His essay was replete with insightful observations and clever arguments.
The garden was replete with vibrant flowers in every color imaginable.
The museum is replete with rare artifacts from ancient civilizations.

Synonyms

  • full of
    1.0 80/m
    full of is commonly used in everyday speech to mean containing a lot, while replete is more formal and literary.
  • filled
    1.0 0.00/m
    filled often describes something physically full or holding as much as possible, while replete suggests being well supplied or abundant.
  • rich with
    3.3 1.43/m
    rich with describes having many good qualities or features, while replete is neutral and means simply well supplied.
  • stocked with
    3.7 1.37/m
    stocked with usually refers to stores or places supplied with goods, while replete is more general and not only for inventory.
  • imbued
    6.3 0.00/m
    imbued means deeply filled or influenced by a quality or idea, while replete refers to being filled with things or abundance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: empty - containing nothing; not filled or occupied
  • synonym: full - containing as much as possible; filled to capacity
