underdrain

noun
subsurface drainage pipe
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndərdreɪn / (AmE), / ˈʌndədreɪn / (BrE)
a pipe or channel installed below ground to carry away excess water from soil or structures

Examples

A field underdrain
The engineers designed an underdrain system to keep the sports field dry after heavy rain.
Without a proper underdrain, water can accumulate and damage the building's foundation.
The workers checked the underdrain for blockages before starting the landscaping project.

Related Terms

  • synonym: culvert - a tunnel or pipe allowing water to flow under a road or railway
  • synonym: drain - a pipe or channel that removes unwanted water
