sifter

noun
kitchen utensil for sifting dry ingredients
6.9 2.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪftɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɪftə / (BrE)
a kitchen utensil with a mesh bottom used to separate and break up clumps in dry ingredients such as flour, or to aerate and mix them.

Examples

A sifter for flour
Every baker keeps a sifter handy for making cakes and breads.
Pour the flour into the sifter before adding it to the batter.
She used a sifter to remove lumps from the powdered sugar.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sieve - a utensil consisting of a wire or plastic mesh in a frame, used for straining, sifting, or separating particles
