alizarin

noun
orange-red dye compound
Pronunciation: / əˈlɪzərɪn / (AmE), / əˈlɪzərɪn / (BrE)
an orange-red crystalline compound used to make red pigments and dyes, especially for textiles

Examples

Alizarin red pigment
Artists prized paints made with alizarin for their brilliant, lasting colors.
Chemical manufacturers developed synthetic alizarin in the nineteenth century to meet growing demand for dyes.
The vivid red of the fabric comes from alizarin extracted from madder roots.
