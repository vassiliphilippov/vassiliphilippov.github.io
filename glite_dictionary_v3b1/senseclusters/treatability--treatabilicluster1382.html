treatability

noun
suitability for treatment
7.0
Pronunciation: /triː.tə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /trɪ.tə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /triː.tə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /trɪ.tə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
the quality of a material or surface that makes it suitable for being treated, finished, or processed, such as by painting or coating

Examples

Treatability of metal
The treatability of the metal determines how well it can be painted or coated.
Engineers assess the treatability of new materials before using them in manufacturing.
Improving the treatability of plastics can lead to better product finishes.
