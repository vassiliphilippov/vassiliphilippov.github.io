full-blood

adjective
of unmixed ancestry
3.8 5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈfʊl.blʌd/ (AmE), /ˈfʊl.blʌd/ (BrE)
Of unmixed ancestry, especially referring to a person or animal whose parents are both members of the same race, breed, or group.

Examples

Full-blood member
He is recognized as a full-blood member of the tribe.
Her grandfather was a full-blood Cherokee.
The farm keeps only full-blood Angus cattle for breeding.
