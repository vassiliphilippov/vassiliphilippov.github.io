Aeneid

noun
epic Latin poem by Virgil about Aeneas
Pronunciation: / əˈniːɪd / (AmE), / ɪˈniːɪd / (BrE)
an epic poem in Latin by Virgil recounting the legendary adventures of Aeneas after the Trojan War, providing a mythic origin for Rome

Examples

The Aeneid poem
Many students read the Aeneid in their classical literature courses.
The Aeneid tells the story of Aeneas's journey from Troy to the founding of Rome.
Virgil's Aeneid is often compared to Homer's Iliad and Odyssey.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Odyssey - ancient Greek epic poem by Homer about Odysseus's journey
