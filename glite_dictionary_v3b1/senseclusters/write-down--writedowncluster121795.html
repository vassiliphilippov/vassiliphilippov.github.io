write-down

noun
reduction in book value of an asset
5.3 5.2
Pronunciation: /ˈraɪt.daʊn/ (AmE), /ˈraɪt.daʊn/ (BrE)
(Accounting) A reduction in the recorded value of an asset to reflect its current, lower market value or impaired condition.

Examples

A write-down of assets
A write-down can negatively affect a firm's reported profits for that period.
After the market crash, the company announced a significant write-down of its real estate assets.
The write-down reflected the actual value of inventory that had become obsolete.
