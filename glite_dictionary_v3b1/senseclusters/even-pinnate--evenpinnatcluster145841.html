(of a leaf) having leaflets arranged in pairs along the stem with a pair of leaflets at the tip, and no terminal leaflet.

Examples

Even-pinnate leaf
The leaves of some acacia species are even-pinnate, with pairs of leaflets arranged symmetrically.
This plant can be easily identified by its even-pinnate leaves, each ending in two small leaflets.
Unlike the odd-pinnate variety, even-pinnate leaves have no single leaflet at the tip.
