chondromalacia

noun
softening of cartilage
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːndroʊməˈleɪʃə / (AmE), / ˌkɒndrəʊməˈleɪsɪə /; / ˌkɒndrəʊməˈleɪʃə / (BrE)
a medical condition where cartilage in a joint, especially under the kneecap, softens or breaks down, often causing pain

Examples

Chondromalacia of knee
The athlete was sidelined due to chondromalacia in her knee joint.
Physical therapy can help manage the symptoms of chondromalacia.
Doctors often diagnose chondromalacia after patients report persistent joint pain.
The doctor diagnosed her knee pain as chondromalacia after a thorough examination.
Athletes are sometimes affected by chondromalacia due to repeated stress on their knees.
Physical therapy can help manage the symptoms of chondromalacia and improve joint function.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arthritis - inflammation of the joints causing pain and stiffness
  • synonym: osteoarthritis - a joint disease involving the breakdown of cartilage and bone
