labyrinthine artery

noun
artery supplying the inner ear
7.8
Pronunciation: /læ.bər.ˈɪn.θiːn ˈɑːr.tə.riː/ /; / /læ.bər.ˈɪn.θaɪn ˈɑːr.tə.riː/ (AmE), /læ.bə.ˈrɪn.θaɪn ˈɑː.tə.ri/ /; / /læ.bə.ˈrɪn.θiːn ˈɑː.tə.ri/ (BrE)
an artery branching from the basilar artery that supplies blood to the inner ear (labyrinth)

Examples

The labyrinthine artery
A blockage in the labyrinthine artery may lead to sudden deafness or vertigo.
Damage to the labyrinthine artery can result in hearing loss and problems with balance.
During the operation, the surgeon carefully avoided the labyrinthine artery to prevent inner ear complications.
