seek time

noun
time for disk head to reach data
8.1 0.08/m
Pronunciation: / siËk taÉªm / (AmE), / siËk taÉªm / (BrE)
(computing) the time required for a read/write head in a disk drive to move to the track where data is stored.

Examples

Low seek time
Manufacturers often advertise drives with low seek time to attract performance-focused buyers.
The hard drive's seek time affects how quickly it can find and retrieve files.
Upgrading to a solid-state drive significantly reduces seek time and improves system performance.
