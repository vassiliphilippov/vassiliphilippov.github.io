middle name

noun
name between first name and surname
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪd.əl neɪm / (AmE), / ˈmɪd.əl neɪm / (BrE)
a name given to a person that comes between their first name and surname, often used for distinction or family tradition

Examples

Use the middle name
He doesn't usually use his middle name except on official documents.
Her middle name is Marie, just like her grandmother.
Please write your middle name on the application form.
Emily only discovered her mother's middle name after finding her birth certificate.
He was named after his uncle, which is why his middle name is David.
On official documents, you are often asked to write your middle name as well as your first and last names.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: surname - the family name, usually the last part of a person's name
