ethnologically

adverb
in a cultural study way
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˌɛθnəˈlɑdʒɪkli / (AmE), / ˌɛθnəˈlɒdʒɪkli / (BrE)
in a manner that relates to the study of different peoples and cultures

Examples

To study ethnologically
The region was ethnologically diverse, with many different groups living side by side.
Researchers analyzed the festival ethnologically to understand its cultural significance.
The museum exhibit was organized ethnologically to highlight the traditions of each community.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anthropologically - in a manner relating to the study of humans and societies
