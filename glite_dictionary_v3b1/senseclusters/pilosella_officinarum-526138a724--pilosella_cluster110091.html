Pilosella officinarum

noun
a European hawkweed with hairy leaves
9.9
Pronunciation: / paɪləˈsɛlə ˌɑfɪsɪˈnɑrəm / (AmE), / pɪlɒˈsɛlə ˌɒfɪsɪˈnɑːrəm / (BrE)
A European hawkweed with soft, hairy leaves, sometimes classified in the genus Hieracium; also known as mouse-ear hawkweed.

Examples

Pilosella officinarum plant
Pilosella officinarum is native to Europe but has spread to other parts of the world.
Gardeners often remove Pilosella officinarum to prevent it from overtaking their lawns.
The soft, hairy leaves are a distinctive feature of Pilosella officinarum.
