unbalance

verb
to throw out of balance
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈbæl.əns / (AmE), / ʌnˈbæl.əns / (BrE)
to throw out of balance or equilibrium; to cause something to become unstable or uneven

Examples

To unbalance a system
Adding so many new features at once could unbalance the entire software system.
Her harsh criticism began to unbalance the team's morale.
High winds can easily unbalance a cyclist on the road.

Related Terms

  • antonym: balance - to keep or put in a steady position so that it does not fall
  • synonym: destabilize - to make unstable or less steady
