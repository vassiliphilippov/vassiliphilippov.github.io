redshift

noun
astronomical phenomenon; spectral shift toward red
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛdˌʃɪft / (AmE), / ˈrɛdʃɪft / (BrE)
(astronomy) a displacement of the spectral lines of light from distant celestial objects toward longer wavelengths, typically interpreted as evidence that the universe is expanding.

Examples

A redshift in light
Astronomers observed a significant redshift in the light coming from distant galaxies.
By measuring the redshift, scientists can estimate how far away a galaxy is.
The discovery of the cosmic redshift supported the theory that the universe is expanding.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blueshift - a shift of spectral lines toward shorter wavelengths (the blue end of the spectrum)
