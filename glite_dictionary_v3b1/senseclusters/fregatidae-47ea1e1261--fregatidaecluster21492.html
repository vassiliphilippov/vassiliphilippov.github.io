Fregatidae

noun
family of seabirds: frigate birds
9.9
Pronunciation: / frɪˈɡætɪˌdiː / (AmE), / frɪˈɡatɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of large tropical seabirds known as frigate birds, characterized by long wings, forked tails, and males with inflatable throat pouches.

Examples

The Fregatidae family
Members of the Fregatidae family are known for their impressive soaring skills over tropical oceans.
Scientists have classified all living Fregatidae within a single genus, Fregata.
The Fregatidae are easily recognized by their large wingspans and aerial agility.
