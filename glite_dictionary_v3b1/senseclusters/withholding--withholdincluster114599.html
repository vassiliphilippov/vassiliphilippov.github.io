withholding

noun
the act of holding back something
2.2 5.6
Pronunciation: /wɪð.ˈhoʊl.dɪŋ/ /; / /wɪθ.ˈhoʊl.dɪŋ/ (AmE), /wɪð.ˈhəʊl.dɪŋ/ /; / /wɪθ.ˈhəʊl.dɪŋ/ (BrE)
the act of holding back or keeping something under one's control, especially by refusing or failing to give it when expected or required

Examples

Withholding information
Repeated withholding of medical records can hinder patient care.
She accused her colleague of withholding important details about the project.
The company's withholding of key financial information drew criticism from regulators.
