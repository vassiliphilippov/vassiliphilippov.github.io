atomicity

noun
indivisibility quality
9.2
Pronunciation: /æ.tə.ˈmɪ.sɪ.tiː/ /; / /ə.tə.ˈmɪ.sɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /æ.tə.ˈmɪ.sɪ.tiː/ /; / /ə.tə.ˈmɪ.sɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
the quality of being indivisible or composed of discrete, indivisible units, especially in computing or science

Examples

Atomicity of a transaction
In database systems, atomicity ensures that a transaction is either fully completed or not done at all.
The concept of atomicity is crucial in chemistry when discussing the smallest units of matter.
Programmers rely on atomicity to prevent errors during simultaneous data updates.
