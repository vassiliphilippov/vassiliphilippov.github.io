supposition

noun
an assumption or hypothesis
1.6 5.8
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌpəˈzɪʃən /; / ˌsʌp.əˈzɪʃ.ən / (AmE), / ˌsʌpəˈzɪʃ(ə)n /; / ˌsʌpəˈzɪʃən /; / ˌsʌp.əˈzɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
an idea or belief that something is true, even if it is not proven (an assumption or hypothesis)

Examples

A supposition of truth
Her entire argument is based on the supposition that the data is accurate.
It is mere supposition to say that he was involved in the crime.
Without evidence, any claim remains a supposition and not a fact.
His argument was based on the supposition that everyone agreed with his views.
The entire theory rests on a supposition that hasn't been proven yet.
Without evidence, her claim remains a mere supposition.
Her theory is based on pure supposition rather than solid evidence.
The detective dismissed his initial supposition after uncovering new facts.
Without proof, any accusation remains a supposition rather than a fact.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assumption - something that is accepted as true without proof
  • antonym: certainty - something that is definitely true or known
  • synonym: conjecture - an opinion or conclusion formed on the basis of incomplete information
  • synonym: hypothesis - a proposed explanation made on the basis of limited evidence
