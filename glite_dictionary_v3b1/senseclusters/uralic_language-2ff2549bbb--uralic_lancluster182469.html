Uralic language

noun
a family of languages including Finnish and Hungarian
7.3
Pronunciation: / jʊˈrælɪk ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / jʊˈrɑːlɪk ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
a member of the family of languages spoken mainly in northern Eurasia, including Finnish, Hungarian, and Estonian

Examples

Uralic language family
Estonian is a Uralic language with its own unique grammar and vocabulary.
Finnish is an example of a Uralic language still widely spoken today.
Hungarian belongs to the Uralic language family rather than the Indo-European group.

Related Terms
