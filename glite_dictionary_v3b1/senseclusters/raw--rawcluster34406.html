raw

adjective
exposed and painful (of skin or flesh)
Pronunciation: / rɔ / (AmE), / rɔː / (BrE)
painful, red, or sore skin or flesh, especially from being abraded or exposed

Examples

Raw skin
After slipping on the gravel, she nursed a raw scrape on her knee.
His hands were raw from hours of working with rough rope.
The dog's constant scratching left a raw patch on its shoulder.
Hiking in wet shoes left his heels painfully raw and blistered.
His skin was raw after hours in the sun without protection.
She winced when the raw wound was touched by the doctor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tender - sensitive or painful to the touch
