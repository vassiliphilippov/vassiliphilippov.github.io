across the country

preposition
from one side of a nation to the other
Pronunciation: / əˈkrɔs ðə ˈkʌntri / (AmE), / əˈkrɒs ðə ˈkʌntri / (BrE)
from one side of a country to the other, usually referring to travel or movement over a long distance within a country

Examples

Travel across the country
He moved across the country for a new job opportunity.
The band embarked on a tour across the country to promote their new album.
Wildfires have spread across the country during the unusually hot summer.
