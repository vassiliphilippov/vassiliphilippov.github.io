chalutzim

noun
Jewish pioneers in Palestine
Pronunciation: / xɑːˌluːˈtsiːm / (AmE), / xəˌluːˈtsiːm / (BrE)
early Jewish pioneers who settled and developed land in Palestine before the establishment of the state of Israel

Examples

Jewish chalutzim in Palestine
The chalutzim faced many hardships as they built new communities in the desert.
Many young people joined the chalutzim to help establish agricultural settlements.
Stories of the chalutzim are still taught in Israeli schools today.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pioneer - a person who is among the first to settle a new area
  • synonym: settler - a person who moves to a new place to live, especially as part of a group
