skip

verb
cause to bounce across a surface (esp. water)
6.1
Pronunciation: / skɪp / (AmE), / skɪp / (BrE)
to cause something, especially a flat stone, to move and bounce lightly across the surface of water

Examples

To skip a stone
As a child, I loved to skip stones across the lake with my friends.
He tried to skip the rock, but it sank instead of bouncing.
She taught her little brother how to skip pebbles over the still water.
