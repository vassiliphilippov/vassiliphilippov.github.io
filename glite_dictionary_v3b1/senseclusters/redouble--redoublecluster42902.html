redouble

verb
to double again; increase greatly
3.4 5.3
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈdʌ.bəl/ /; / /rɪ.ˈdʌ.bəl/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈdʌ.bəl/ /; / /riː.ˈdʌ.bəl/ (BrE)
to make something much greater, stronger, or more intense, especially after it has already increased

Examples

As the storm worsened, the wind seemed to redouble in strength.
She resolved to redouble her efforts after the initial setback.

As the deadline approached, her determination redoubled and she worked through the night.
The soldiers redoubled their efforts after the enemy advanced.

We must redouble our efforts if we are to finish the project on time.
