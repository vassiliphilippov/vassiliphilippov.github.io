evolutionary biology

noun
the study of evolution in biology
4.0
Pronunciation: / ɪˌvoʊluˌʃəˌnɛri baɪˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ɪˌvɒljuːˈʃənəri baɪˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The branch of biology that studies the processes underlying evolution and the development of life over time.

Examples

Evolutionary biology studies evolution
Debates in evolutionary biology often focus on the role of natural selection.
New discoveries in evolutionary biology have shed light on human origins.
She studied evolutionary biology at university to understand how species change over time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: evolution - the process by which different kinds of living organisms are thought to have developed and diversified from earlier forms
