anecdotally

adverb
using personal stories
Pronunciation: / ˌænɪkˈdoʊtəli / (AmE), / ˌænɪkˈdəʊtəli / (BrE)
in a way that uses personal stories or individual cases, rather than scientific evidence

Examples

Explained anecdotally with stories
The doctor explained the treatment anecdotally, sharing stories from her own experience with patients.
He often speaks anecdotally to make his lectures more engaging for students.
The article discussed the issue anecdotally rather than providing scientific evidence.
Anecdotally, some people claim that drinking green tea helps them concentrate better.
The treatment is anecdotally effective, but there is little scientific research to support it.
He anecdotally mentioned that his friends had similar experiences with the new medication.

Related Terms

  • antonym: factually - in a way based on facts rather than stories
  • synonym: narratively - in a way that tells a story or narrative
  • antonym: scientifically - in a way that is based on scientific methods or evidence
