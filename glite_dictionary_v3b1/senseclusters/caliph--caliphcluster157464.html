caliph

noun
Muslim religious and civil leader
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪ.lɪf, ˈkæ.lɪf / (AmE), / ˈkeɪ.lɪf, ˈkæl.ɪf / (BrE)
the chief civil and religious leader of a Muslim state, regarded as the successor to the Prophet Muhammad and representative of Allah on earth

Examples

The caliph of Islam
After the death of Muhammad, Abu Bakr became the first caliph of the Islamic community.
Historians debate the legitimacy of each caliph after the initial four successors.
The caliph ruled over vast territories stretching from Spain to India during the golden age of Islam.

Related Terms

  • synonym: imam - leader in Islamic worship, especially of a mosque
  • synonym: khalif - Alternative spelling for 'caliph'
