bitter pill to swallow

noun
unpleasant truth or situation that must be accepted
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪt̬ɚ pɪl tu səˈwɑːloʊ / (AmE), / ˈbɪtə pɪl tə səˈwɒləʊ / (BrE)
an unpleasant or painful fact, event, or situation that must be accepted even though it is difficult

Examples

A bitter pill to swallow fact
For many fans, hearing their favorite team had cheated was a bitter pill to swallow.
Losing her job after so many years was a bitter pill to swallow.
The decision to move away from his hometown was a bitter pill to swallow.
