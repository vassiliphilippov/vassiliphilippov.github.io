interactionist

noun
mind-body dualist
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈækʃənɪst / (AmE), / ˌɪntərˈækʃənɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that mental and physical events are distinct but can causally influence each other

Examples

A interactionist philosopher
The interactionist explained how thoughts can influence physical actions in the body.
As an interactionist, she argued that the mind and body communicate with each other.
Many philosophers debate the claims made by an interactionist about consciousness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dualist - a person who believes in the separation of mind and body
  • antonym: materialist - a person who believes only physical matter exists
