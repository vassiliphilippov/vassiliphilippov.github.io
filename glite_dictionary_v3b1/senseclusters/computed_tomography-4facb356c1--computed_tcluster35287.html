computed tomography

noun
medical imaging using computer-processed X-rays
3.6 0.48/m
Pronunciation: / k…ômÀàpjuÀêt…™d t…ôÀàm…ëÀê…°r…ôfi / (AmE), / k…ômÀàpjuÀêt…™d t…ôÀàm…í…°r…ôfi / (BrE)
a medical imaging technique in which computer-processed X-ray measurements are used to create cross-sectional images of internal body structures

Examples

Computed tomography scan
Advances in computed tomography have greatly improved the detection of tumors.
Modern hospitals often rely on computed tomography for diagnosing internal injuries.
The doctor recommended a computed tomography scan to get clearer images of the abdomen.

Synonyms

  • computerized tomography
    4.1 0.04/m
    computerized tomography is an alternative term used in reports and texts, while computed tomography is the standard name for the imaging method.
  • computed axial tomography
    4.3 0.02/m
    computed axial tomography is a term that highlights image slices along one axis, while computed tomography refers to cross-sectional images in general.
  • computerized axial tomography
    4.8 0.00/m
    computerized axial tomography names the same slice-by-slice X-ray method in some reports, while computed tomography is the general imaging term.
  • CT
    3.7 1.70/m
    CT is the common abbreviation seen in hospitals and radiology reports, while computed tomography is the full formal name of the scan.
  • CAT
    4.6 7.61/m
    CAT is an older abbreviation often found in older reports or textbooks, while computed tomography is the widely used modern term.
