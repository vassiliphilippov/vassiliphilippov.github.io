gonadotropin

noun
hormone stimulating gonads
Pronunciation: / ˌɡoʊ.nə.dəˈtroʊ.pɪn /; / ɡoʊˌnædəˈtroʊfɪn / (AmE), / ˌɡəʊ.nə.dəˈtrəʊ.pɪn /; / ˌɡəʊnədəˈtrəʊfɪn / (BrE)
a hormone secreted by the anterior pituitary gland or placenta that stimulates the activity of the gonads and regulates reproductive processes

Examples

Gonadotropin hormone injection
Certain fertility treatments involve injections of gonadotropin.
Low gonadotropin can lead to reproductive health issues.
The doctor ordered a blood test to check her gonadotropin levels.
A deficiency in gonadotrophin can result in reproductive disorders.
Levels of gonadotrophin rise during pregnancy to support fetal development.
Treatment for infertility may involve injections of gonadotrophin to stimulate the ovaries.
