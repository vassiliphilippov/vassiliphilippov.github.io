destitute

adjective
extremely poor; lacking necessities
6.5 1.68/m
Pronunciation: / Ààd…õst…ôÀåtut / (AmE), / Ààd…õst…™ÀåtjuÀêt / (BrE)
Extremely poor and lacking the means to provide for oneself; without the basic necessities of life.

Examples

Destitute family
After losing his job and home, he was left completely destitute.
Charities work hard to support families who are destitute and struggling to survive.
Many refugees arrive in the country destitute and without any belongings.

Synonyms

  • indigent
    8.7 1.23/m
    indigent describes people who lack basic needs and need outside help, while destitute stresses having no means at all.
  • poverty-stricken
    5.1 0.80/m
    poverty-stricken often describes whole communities or families in deep need, while destitute refers to lacking basic life necessities.
  • impoverished
    6.2 5.23/m
    impoverished describes people or areas made poor over time, while destitute means having no means to meet basic needs now.
  • penurious
    9.9 0.03/m
    penurious describes extreme lack of money in formal or literary speech, while destitute highlights missing everyday necessities and support.
  • needy
    1.5 3.19/m
    needy refers to people who need help or support, often socially or emotionally, while destitute means lacking basic physical necessities.
  • penniless
    4.3 1.02/m
    penniless means having no money at all, while destitute adds lacking basic food, shelter, or other everyday necessities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: impoverished - lacking sufficient money; very poor
  • antonym: wealthy - having a great deal of money, resources, or assets
