inconvertible

adjective
not able to be converted
4.8 9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnkənˈvɝːtəbəl / (AmE), / ˌɪnkənˈvɜːtəbəl / (BrE)
not able to be changed into something else; incapable of conversion.

Examples

Inconvertible metal
Due to its chemical stability, gold is considered an inconvertible metal.
Some currencies were once inconvertible on international markets.
The contract stated that the offer was inconvertible and could not be exchanged for cash.
