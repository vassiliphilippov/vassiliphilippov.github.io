looseness

noun
state of allowing movement or space between parts
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈluːs.nəs / (AmE), / ˈluːs.nɪs /; / ˈluːsnɪs / (BrE)
the state of being loose; not tight or securely fastened, allowing movement or space between parts

Examples

Looseness in the joint
The mechanic noted some looseness in the steering mechanism.
There was an uncomfortable looseness in the hinge, causing the door to rattle.
This design flaw leads to looseness where the two parts join together.
Careful inspection revealed the looseness of the joint, making it unsafe to use.
He noticed the looseness in the hinges and decided to tighten the screws.
The looseness of the bolt allowed the wheel to wobble while driving.

Related Terms

  • antonym: tightness - the state of being tight or having little or no space for movement
