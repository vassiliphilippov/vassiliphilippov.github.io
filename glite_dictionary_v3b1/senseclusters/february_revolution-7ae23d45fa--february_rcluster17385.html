February Revolution

noun
Russian revolution in March 1917
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛb.ju.ɛri ˌrɛv.əˈlu.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈfɛb.jʊ.ə.ri ˌrɛv.əˈluː.ʃən / (BrE)
the revolution in Russia in March 1917 that led to the abdication of Tsar Nicholas II and the establishment of a provisional government, marking the first phase of the Russian Revolution

Examples

The February Revolution 1917
After the February Revolution, the provisional government struggled to maintain order in Russia.
Many historians argue that the February Revolution was a spontaneous uprising by the people of Petrograd.
The February Revolution led to the collapse of centuries of Russian imperial rule.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Russian Revolution - the overall period of revolutionary events in Russia in 1917
