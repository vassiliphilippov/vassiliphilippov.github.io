blooming

adverb
informal intensifier (esp. British)
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈbluːmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbluːmɪŋ / (BrE)
Used as an intensifier to emphasize annoyance, admiration, or surprise, often in informal British English.

Examples

A blooming mess
He made a blooming mess of the kitchen while cooking.
I'm blooming tired of waiting for the train.
That was a blooming good meal, thank you!

Related Terms

  • synonym: bloody - Used as an intensifier in British English, sometimes considered rude.
  • synonym: flaming - Another informal euphemistic intensifier, chiefly British.
