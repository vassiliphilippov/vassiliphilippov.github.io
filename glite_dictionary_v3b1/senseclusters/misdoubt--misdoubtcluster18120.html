misdoubt

noun
doubt or suspicion
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪsˈdaʊt / (AmE), / ˌmɪsˈdaʊt / (BrE)
a feeling of doubt, suspicion, or lack of trust, especially in an archaic context

Examples

A sudden misdoubt
A sudden misdoubt crept into his mind as he read the letter.
The villagers' misdoubt of strangers was clear in their cautious behavior.
She could not shake the misdoubt that something was being hidden from her.

Related Terms

  • synonym: suspicion - a feeling that something is wrong or someone cannot be trusted
  • antonym: trust - belief that someone or something is reliable or honest
