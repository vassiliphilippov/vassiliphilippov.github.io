isotype

noun
antibody class
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪsəˌtaɪp / (AmE), / ˈaɪsəʊˌtaɪp / (BrE)
a class of antibody distinguished by differences in the constant region of its heavy chain, such as IgG or IgM

Examples

Isotype of antibody
The doctor ordered tests to identify the isotype of antibodies present in the patient's blood.
Each isotype of immunoglobulin has a unique role in the immune response.
Researchers compared the isotype profiles of patients before and after vaccination.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allotype - a genetic variant of an antibody within the same isotype
  • degree lower: subclass - a further division within a class of antibodies
