achromatize

verb
make free from color distortion (optics)
9.7
Pronunciation: / əˈkroʊməˌtaɪz / (AmE), / əˈkrəʊmətaɪz / (BrE)
to remove color from something, especially to make (a lens or optical instrument) free from chromatic aberration; to remove or correct unwanted color effects

Examples

To achromatize a lens
It is necessary to achromatize the objective lens to eliminate color fringes.
Modern microscopes achromatize their lenses to produce clear images.
Optical engineers work to achromatize telescopes for more accurate observations.
It is possible to achromatise glass by combining different materials.
Optical engineers often achromatise lenses to reduce color fringing in images.
The company uses special coatings to achromatise its camera lenses.

Related Terms

  • synonym: achromatize - remove color from (American spelling)
  • antonym: chromatize - to add color or produce color effects
