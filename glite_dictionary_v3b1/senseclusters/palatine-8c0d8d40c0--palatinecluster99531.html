Palatine

proper noun
the central and most famous hill of Rome
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpæl.ə.taɪn / (AmE), / ˈpæl.ə.tiːn / (BrE)
the most important of the Seven Hills of Rome, traditionally regarded as the site of the first settlement and the location of several imperial palaces

Examples

The Palatine Hill
According to legend, Rome was founded on the Palatine by Romulus and Remus.
Excavations on the Palatine have revealed remains of ancient imperial palaces.
Many important Roman emperors had their residences built atop the Palatine.
