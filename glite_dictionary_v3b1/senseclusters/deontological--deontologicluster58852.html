deontological

adjective
ethics of duty
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːˌɑːntəˈlɑːdʒɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌdiːˌɒntəˈlɒdʒɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to ethical theories that judge the morality of actions based on rules or duties rather than consequences

Examples

A deontological approach
Her deontological approach meant she always followed the rules, regardless of the outcome.
The philosopher explained the difference between deontological and consequentialist ethics in his lecture.
A deontological perspective emphasizes the importance of moral duties over results.

Related Terms

  • antonym: consequentialist - relating to ethical theories that judge actions by their outcomes
  • synonym: duty-based - focused on moral obligations or rules
