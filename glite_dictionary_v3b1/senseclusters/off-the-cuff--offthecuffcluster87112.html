off-the-cuff

adjective
without preparation or planning
Pronunciation: / ˌɔf ðə ˈkʌf / (AmE), / ˌɒf ði ˈkʌf / (BrE)
Spoken or done without any previous thought or preparation.

Examples

Off-the-cuff remarks
His off-the-cuff speech at the wedding was heartfelt and genuine.
Reporters appreciated the candidate's ability to answer questions off-the-cuff.
She made an off-the-cuff remark during the meeting that surprised everyone.

Related Terms

  • synonym: extemporaneous - spoken or done without preparation
  • synonym: impromptu - done without being planned, organized, or rehearsed
  • antonym: prepared - made ready in advance
