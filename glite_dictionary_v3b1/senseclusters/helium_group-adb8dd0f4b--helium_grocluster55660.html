helium group

noun
group of inert (noble) gases

the group of chemically inert gases in the periodic table, also known as noble gases, including helium, neon, argon, krypton, xenon, and radon

Examples

The helium group gases
All members of the helium group are colorless, odorless, and tasteless under standard conditions.
Because of their stability, the helium group gases rarely participate in chemical reactions.
The helium group of elements are often used in lighting and signage due to their inertness.
