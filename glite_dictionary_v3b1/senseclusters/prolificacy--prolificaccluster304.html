prolificacy

noun
the quality of producing abundantly
Pronunciation: / prəˈlɪfɪkəsi / (AmE), / prəˈlɪfɪkəsi / (BrE)
the quality or state of producing large amounts of something; abundant productivity or growth

Examples

High prolificacy rate
Scientists studied the prolificacy of the plant species in different environmental conditions.
She was renowned for her prolificacy as a novelist, publishing several books each year.
The prolificacy of the vineyard surprised even the experienced farmers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: barrenness - the quality of not producing or incapable of producing
  • synonym: fertility - the ability to produce abundantly, especially in plants or animals
