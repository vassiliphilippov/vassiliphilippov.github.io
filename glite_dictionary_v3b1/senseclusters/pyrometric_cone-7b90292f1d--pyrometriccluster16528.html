pyrometric cone

noun
ceramic cone for measuring kiln temperature
Pronunciation: / ˌpaɪrəˈmɛtrɪk koʊn / (AmE), / ˌpaɪrəˈmɛtrɪk kəʊn / (BrE)
a small cone made of ceramic material that melts at a specific temperature, used to gauge the heat of a kiln or furnace

Examples

A pyrometric cone melts
After the pyrometric cone bent, the kiln was shut off to prevent over-firing.
Each pyrometric cone in the kiln told the technician how hot the firing had become.
The pottery studio relies on pyrometric cones to ensure proper glaze maturity.
