scattergram

noun
dot graph of variables
5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈskæt.ər.græm/ (AmE), /ˈskæt.ə.græm/ (BrE)
a graph that displays the relationship between two variables by showing dots for each data point

Examples

A scattergram graph
The researcher created a scattergram to visualize the correlation between age and income.
Each dot on the scattergram represents a different participant in the study.
By examining the scattergram, the students could see if there was a trend in the data.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bar chart - a graph that uses bars to show quantities for different categories
  • synonym: scatterplot - a graph showing data points as dots to display relationships between variables
