recreant

adjective
having deserted a cause or principle
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛkriənt / (AmE), / ˈrɛkriənt / (BrE)
cowardly or unfaithful; lacking courage or loyalty

Examples

Recreant soldier

Her recreant response to the crisis disappointed everyone who expected her to take a stand.
The recreant knight dropped his sword and begged for mercy before the battle even began.
The general called his troops recreant when they refused to advance under enemy fire.
