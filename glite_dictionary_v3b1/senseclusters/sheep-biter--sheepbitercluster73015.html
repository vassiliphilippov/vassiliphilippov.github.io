sheep-biter

noun
archaic: thief, especially of sheep
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈʃipˌbaɪtər / (AmE), / ˈʃiːpˌbaɪtə / (BrE)
(archaic) A person who steals livestock, especially sheep; a deceitful or treacherous person.

Examples

A sheep-biter thief
Old stories warned children never to trust a sheep-biter with their livestock.
Once branded a sheep-biter, it was difficult for him to regain the trust of the community.
The villagers soon realized that Tom was the sheep-biter who had been stealing their animals.
