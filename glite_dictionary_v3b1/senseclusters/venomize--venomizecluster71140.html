venomize

verb
to make hostile
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛnəˌmaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvɛnəmaɪz / (BrE)
to make someone or something hostile, bitter, or filled with malice, as if poisoned with venom

Examples

To venomize a speech
The politician's harsh words venomized the debate, making it difficult to reach an agreement.
Social media can sometimes venomize discussions that start out friendly.
Her criticism venomized the atmosphere in the office, causing tension among colleagues.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embitter - to make someone feel bitter or resentful
  • synonym: poison - to make something harmful or toxic
  • antonym: soothe - to make someone calmer or less angry
