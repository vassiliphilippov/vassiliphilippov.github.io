Fox

noun
member of an Algonquian Native American people
8.9
Pronunciation: / fɑks / (AmE), / fɒks / (BrE)
A member of an Algonquian Native American people originally living west of Lake Michigan along the Fox River.

Examples

Fox people
Anthropologists have studied the traditions and language of the Fox extensively.
Descendants of the Fox people now live in Oklahoma as part of the Sac and Fox Nation.
The Fox were known for their strong resistance against French colonization in the 18th century.
