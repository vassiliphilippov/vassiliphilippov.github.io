water arum

noun
wetland plant with white spathe and red berries
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔt̬ɚ ˈɛrəm / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˈærəm / (BrE)
a plant of wetlands and bogs in temperate regions, with small greenish flowers partly enclosed in a white spathe and red berries

Examples

The water arum plant
In late summer, the water arum produces clusters of bright red berries.
Many gardeners plant water arum to add interest to shaded water gardens.
The water arum thrives in shallow ponds and marshy areas.
