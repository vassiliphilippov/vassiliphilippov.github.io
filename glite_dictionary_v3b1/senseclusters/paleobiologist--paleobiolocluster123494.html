paleobiologist

noun
scientist of ancient life
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpeɪliˌoʊbaɪˈɑlədʒɪst / (AmE), / ˌpeɪlɪəʊbaɪˈɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a scientist who studies ancient life forms by examining fossils and other geological evidence

Examples

The paleobiologist studies fossils
The paleobiologist presented her findings about dinosaur ecosystems at the international conference.
As a paleobiologist, he spends months in the field searching for fossil evidence of early life.
A team led by a paleobiologist uncovered new clues about the extinction of ancient marine species.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: biologist - a scientist who studies living organisms
  • synonym: paleontologist - a scientist who studies fossils to learn about ancient life
