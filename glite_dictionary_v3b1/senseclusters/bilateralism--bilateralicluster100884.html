bilateralism

noun
two-country cooperation
7.5
Pronunciation: / baɪˈlæt̬ɚəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / baɪˈlætərəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the policy or practice of two countries working together to make agreements or solve issues directly with each other

Examples

Bilateralism in trade
The new trade agreement is based on bilateralism between the two neighboring countries.
Diplomats discussed bilateralism as a way to resolve the border dispute peacefully.
Some experts argue that bilateralism can be more effective than larger international negotiations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: multilateralism - the practice of multiple countries working together on agreements
  • antonym: unilateralism - the policy of acting independently without cooperation from other countries
