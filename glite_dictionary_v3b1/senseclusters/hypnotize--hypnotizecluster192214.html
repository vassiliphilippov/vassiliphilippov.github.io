hypnotize

verb
induce hypnosis in someone
Pronunciation: / ˈhɪp.nəˌtaɪz /; / ˈhɪpnəˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈhɪp.nə.taɪz /; / ˈhɪpnətaɪz / (BrE)
to put someone into a state of hypnosis, making them highly responsive to suggestion or direction

Examples

To hypnotize a person
During the stage show, the magician attempted to hypnotize several audience members.
Some people believe you can't hypnotize someone against their will.
The therapist was able to hypnotize the patient and help them recall childhood memories.
The therapist was hypnotizing the patient to help reduce his chronic pain during the session.
She learned how to hypnotize people as part of her psychology studies.
During the show, the magician hypnotized several volunteers from the audience.
She was so skilled that she could hypnotise someone in just a few minutes.
Stage performers often hypnotise volunteers from the audience for entertainment.
The therapist managed to hypnotise the patient to help them overcome their fear of flying.

Related Terms

  • antonym: awaken - to cause someone to stop sleeping or to become alert
  • synonym: mesmerize - to capture someone's complete attention or induce a trance-like state
