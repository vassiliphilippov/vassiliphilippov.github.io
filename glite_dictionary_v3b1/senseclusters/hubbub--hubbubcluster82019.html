hubbub

noun
loud, confused noise
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌb.ʌb / (AmE), / ˈhʌb.ʌb / (BrE)
a loud, confused noise caused by a crowd or by many sources occurring at once

Examples

A loud hubbub
After the surprise announcement, a hubbub broke out among the reporters.
She struggled to make her voice heard above the hubbub of the crowd.
The hubbub from the marketplace could be heard all the way down the street.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clamor - a loud and confused noise, especially that of people shouting
  • synonym: din - a loud, unpleasant, and prolonged noise
  • antonym: silence - complete absence of sound
