warrantless

adjective
without legal warrant
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔrəntləs / (AmE), / ˈwɒrəntləs / (BrE)
done without a legal warrant or official authorization; not justified by law or proper authority

Examples

Warrantless search
The judge ruled that the warrantless search violated the defendant's rights.
Police conducted a warrantless entry into the apartment, raising concerns about privacy.
Any warrantless surveillance must be justified by exceptional circumstances under the law.
The judge ruled that the warrantless search of the house was illegal.
Critics argued that the new policy allowed for warrantless surveillance of citizens.
She objected to the warrantless accusations made against her in the meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: authorized - having official permission or approval
  • synonym: illegal - not allowed by law
  • antonym: justified - having a good reason or being supported by evidence
  • synonym: unauthorized - not having official permission or approval
  • synonym: unjustified - not shown to be reasonable or necessary
  • synonym: unsupported - not backed by evidence or proof
