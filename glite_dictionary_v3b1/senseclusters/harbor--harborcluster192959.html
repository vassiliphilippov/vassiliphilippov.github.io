harbor

verb
keep a feeling or idea secretly
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑrbər /; / ˈhɑːrbər / (AmE), / ˈhɑːbə / (BrE)
to keep a thought, feeling, or idea in your mind, especially secretly or for a long time

Examples

To harbor doubts
Do you harbor any hopes of winning the competition?
He secretly harbored resentment toward his colleague for years.
She continued to harbor doubts about the project's success, even after the presentation.
He tried not to harbor any ill will towards his former colleagues.
Many people harbor doubts about the effectiveness of the new policy.
She continued to harbor resentment after the argument was over.
He harboured hopes of reconciliation, despite their long separation.
She has harboured resentment towards her former manager for years.
Try not to harbour negative thoughts about yourself.

Related Terms

  • synonym: entertain - keep thoughts or feelings in the mind
  • antonym: let go - to stop holding onto a feeling or grudge
  • synonym: nurse - to hold onto a feeling, usually negative, for a long time
