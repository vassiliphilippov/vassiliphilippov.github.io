aedile

noun
Roman city magistrate
Pronunciation: / ˈiːdaɪl / (AmE), / ˈiːdaɪl / (BrE)
a magistrate in ancient Rome responsible for public buildings, festivals, and the maintenance of markets and order

Examples

The aedile in Rome
The aedile was responsible for organizing public games and maintaining the city’s infrastructure.
In ancient Rome, an aedile supervised the markets and ensured fair trade.
Julius Caesar once served as an aedile before rising to higher political office.

Related Terms

  • synonym: consul - a high-ranking official in ancient Rome
  • synonym: magistrate - an official who administers the law
