skeg

noun
brace on rear keel supporting rudder
9.9
Pronunciation: / skɛg /; / skɛɡ / (AmE), / skɛg /; / skɛɡ / (BrE)
a projection or fin on the underside or rear of a boat, kayak, or surfboard that provides stability and helps protect the rudder

Examples

Boat skeg fin
A well-designed skeg can improve a vessel's directional stability.
After years in saltwater, the skeg showed signs of corrosion.
The boat's skeg was damaged when it struck a submerged rock.
The skeg on her surfboard helped her maintain balance while riding the wave.
Without a skeg, the kayak kept turning sideways in the wind.
He checked the skeg for damage before launching the boat into the water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: keel - the main structural element running along the bottom of a boat
  • synonym: rudder - a flat piece used to steer a boat or ship
