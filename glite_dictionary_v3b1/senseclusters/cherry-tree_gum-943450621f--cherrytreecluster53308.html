cherry-tree gum

noun
gummy substance from cherry trees
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɛri tri ɡʌm / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛri tri ɡʌm / (BrE)
A gummy exudate from cherry trees (Prunus genus), similar in appearance and properties to gum arabic.

Examples

Cherry-tree gum exudate
Collectors valued cherry-tree gum for its similarity to more expensive plant gums.
Some traditional candies use cherry-tree gum as a natural thickener.
The cherry-tree gum oozed slowly from a crack in the bark.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gum arabic - an edible, water-soluble gum from the acacia tree
