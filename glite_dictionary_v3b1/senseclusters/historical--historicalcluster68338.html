historical

adjective
real, not mythical; actually existed
Pronunciation: / hɪˈstɔrɪkəl / (AmE), / hɪˈstɒrɪkəl / (BrE)
having actually existed, lived, or taken place in the real world, as opposed to being mythical or legendary

Examples

Historical figure
Evidence for a historical Troy was found during archaeological excavations.
Some scholars debate whether King Arthur was a historical figure or purely legendary.
The documentary explores the historical Jesus, separating fact from myth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: legendary - relating to legends, not real or historical
  • antonym: mythical - existing only in myths, not actually historical
