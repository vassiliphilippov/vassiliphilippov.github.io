appeals court

noun
court reviewing lower court decisions
4.4 2.49/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàpilz k…îrt / (AmE), / …ôÀàpiÀêlz k…îÀêt / (BrE)
A court that has the authority to review and decide on appeals from decisions made by lower courts or agencies.

Examples

The appeals court decision
A verdict can sometimes be reversed if the appeals court finds legal errors in the trial.
He decided to take his case to the appeals court after losing in the lower court.
The appeals court upheld the previous ruling after reviewing all the evidence presented.

Synonyms

  • court of appeals
    5.0 1.90/m
    court of appeals is the specific official name used for some courts, while appeals court is a general way to refer to such courts.
  • appellate court
    7.4 1.35/m
    appellate court is the formal legal term used in law and documents, while appeals court is the usual name people use in speech.

Related Terms

  • antonym: trial court - a court of first instance where cases are initially tried
