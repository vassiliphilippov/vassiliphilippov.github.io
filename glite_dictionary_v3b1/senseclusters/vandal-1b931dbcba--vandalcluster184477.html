Vandal

noun
member of a Germanic people who sacked Rome
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈvændl̩ / (AmE), / ˈvændl̩ / (BrE)
a member of an East Germanic people who invaded and settled parts of Europe and North Africa in the 5th century, famously sacking Rome in 455

Examples

The Vandals sacked Rome
In 455, the Vandals infamously entered and plundered the city of Rome.
Many history books detail the migrations of the Vandal people across Europe.
The Vandals established their own kingdom in North Africa after crossing into the region from Spain.
