dramatic mezzo-soprano

noun
operatic mezzo-soprano voice type
9.9
Pronunciation: / drəˈmæt̬ɪk ˌmet.soʊ səˈprɑː.noʊ / (AmE), / drəˈmæt.ɪk ˌmet.səʊ səˈprɑː.nəʊ / (BrE)
A type of mezzo-soprano voice with a strong, rich tone capable of singing over an orchestra, typically ranging from the F below middle C to the G two octaves above.

Examples

A dramatic mezzo-soprano voice
Many Verdi operas feature parts written for a dramatic mezzo-soprano, demanding both strength and subtlety.
She trained for years to develop into a true dramatic mezzo-soprano capable of performing challenging roles.
The role requires a dramatic mezzo-soprano with a powerful voice and commanding stage presence.
