phantom

adjective
illusory; lacking physical reality
8.4 7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈfæn.təm / (AmE), / ˈfæn.təm / (BrE)
existing only in the mind or as an illusion; something that seems real but has no physical presence

Examples

Phantom pain
A phantom itch made him scratch at a spot where his arm used to be.
Scientists studied the phenomenon of phantom sounds reported after severe hearing loss.
She often experienced phantom pains in her missing leg after the accident.

Related Terms

  • synonym: illusory - not real and based on illusion
  • antonym: real - actually existing as a thing or occurring in fact
