Yajur-Veda

noun
collection of sacrificial Hindu prayers
Pronunciation: / ˌjɑːdʒər ˈveɪdə / (AmE), / ˌjɑːdʒʊə ˈveɪdə / (BrE)
one of the four Vedas, consisting of a collection of sacrificial formulas and prayers used in ancient Hindu rituals

Examples

The Yajur-Veda hymns
Many hymns in the Yajur-Veda focus on prayers for prosperity and peace.
Scholars have translated portions of the Yajur-Veda to better understand ancient Indian traditions.
The Yajur-Veda contains instructions for conducting various Hindu rituals and sacrifices.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Atharva-Veda - a Veda consisting of spells and incantations
  • synonym: Rig-Veda - the oldest Veda, consisting mainly of hymns
  • synonym: Sama-Veda - a Veda consisting of chants and melodies
