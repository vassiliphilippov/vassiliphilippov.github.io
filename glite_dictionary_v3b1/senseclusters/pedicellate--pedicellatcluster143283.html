pedicellate

adjective
teeth with separated crown
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛdɪˈsɛlət / (AmE), / ˌpɛdɪˈsɛlət / (BrE)
having a crown and base separated by a narrow, often uncalcified zone, especially in the teeth of many amphibians

Examples

Pedicellate teeth
Many amphibians have pedicellate teeth, which help them catch slippery prey.
The biologist noted the pedicellate structure of the frog's teeth under the microscope.
In some salamanders, the pedicellate teeth are a key feature for species identification.
