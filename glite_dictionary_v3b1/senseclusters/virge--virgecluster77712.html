virge

noun
ceremonial staff
Pronunciation: / vɜrdʒ / (AmE), / vɜːdʒ / (BrE)
an old term for a rod, staff, or wand, especially one used in ceremonies or processions

Examples

Ceremonial virge
The bishop carried a virge during the procession.
In medieval ceremonies, a virge was often held by an official as a symbol of office.
The ancient ritual required the use of a special virge decorated with silver.

Related Terms

  • synonym: staff - a long stick carried in the hand, often as a symbol of authority
  • synonym: wand - a thin stick or rod, often used in ceremonies or magic
