grain

verb
become granular; form grains
5.9
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪn / (AmE), / ɡreɪn / (BrE)
to form or separate into small hard particles or granules

Examples

To grain the metal
During the cooling process, the metal will grain and separate into fine particles.
If the mixture is not stirred constantly, it may grain and lose its smooth texture.
In some photographic processes, the image may grain over time, affecting clarity.
Certain salts grain when exposed to humidity.
During the manufacturing process, the powder will grain into tiny pellets.
The chemist designed a method to grain the substance for pharmaceutical use.
