quadrangular prism

noun
prism with quadrilateral bases
Pronunciation: / kwɑːˈdræŋɡjələr ˈprɪzəm / (AmE), / kwɒˈdraŋɡjʊlə ˈprɪzəm / (BrE)
A solid geometric figure with two parallel, congruent bases that are quadrilaterals, and four other faces that are parallelograms.

Examples

A quadrangular prism shape
A quadrangular prism can be unfolded into a net of six quadrilaterals.
In the geometry class, the teacher explained how to calculate the volume of a quadrangular prism.
The model was shaped like a quadrangular prism with four rectangular sides.
