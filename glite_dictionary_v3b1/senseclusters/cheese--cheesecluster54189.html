cheese

verb
to win by exploiting
Pronunciation: / tʃiːz / (AmE), / tʃiːz / (BrE)
to defeat or overcome a challenge by using a simple exploit or unfair tactic, especially in games or competitions

Examples

To cheese a game
They cheesed the final boss by exploiting a simple glitch to win quickly.
Some players prefer to cheese difficult levels instead of learning the proper strategy.
He cheesed his way through the exam by copying answers from his friend.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cheat - to act dishonestly or unfairly to gain an advantage
  • synonym: exploit - to use a flaw or loophole to gain an advantage
  • antonym: play fair - to compete honestly and without unfair tactics
