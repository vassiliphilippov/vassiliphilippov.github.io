oversell

verb
to exaggerate benefits
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈsɛl / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈsɛl / (BrE)
to exaggerate, praise, or promote something more than it deserves, making it seem better than it really is

Examples

To oversell a product
The advertisement oversells the product by promising results it cannot deliver.
Be careful not to oversell your skills during the job interview.
He oversold the benefits of the new software to his team.
The advertisement oversells the product by promising impossible results.
She oversold the movie, so I was disappointed when I finally watched it.
Be careful not to oversell your skills during the job interview.
The marketing team did not want to oversell the product and risk disappointing customers.
He tends to oversell his achievements during job interviews.
If you oversell the features, people may not trust your recommendations in the future.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exaggerate - to make something seem larger, better, or more important than it is
  • antonym: undersell - to make something seem less important or valuable than it is
  • antonym: understate - to describe something as less important or serious than it really is
