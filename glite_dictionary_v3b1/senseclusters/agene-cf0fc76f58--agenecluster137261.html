Agene

noun
volatile chemical used in flour bleaching
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪdʒiːn / (AmE), / ˈeɪdʒiːn / (BrE)
A yellow, pungent, and volatile chemical substance (trade name: Agene) formerly used to bleach and age flour.

Examples

Agene bleaching flour
Many bakeries once used Agene to improve the appearance of their flour.
Samples of flour treated with Agene showed changes in texture and color.
The use of Agene was discontinued after concerns about its safety.
