Hadean time

noun
the earliest eon in Earth's history
9.9
Pronunciation: / heɪˈdiː.ən taɪm / (AmE), / həˈdiː.ən tʌɪm / (BrE)
the earliest eon in the history of the Earth, spanning from the formation of the planet about 4,600 million years ago to roughly 3,800 million years ago, before the appearance of the oldest known rocks and any evidence of life

Examples

Hadean time period
Geologists study Hadean time to understand the Earth's earliest formation processes.
No rocks have survived from Hadean time, making it a mysterious chapter in our planet's history.
The atmosphere during Hadean time was vastly different from what we experience today.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Archean - the eon following the Hadean, marked by the formation of the Earth's crust and earliest life
