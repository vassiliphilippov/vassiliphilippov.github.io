incommutability

noun
quality of not being interchangeable
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.kəˌmjuː.təˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌɪn.kəˌmjuː.təˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality or state of not being interchangeable or capable of being exchanged for something else.

Examples

The incommutability of art
He argued for the incommutability of art and its resistance to being treated as a commodity.
The incommutability of certain mathematical operators is fundamental to quantum mechanics.
This policy emphasizes the incommutability of moral principles regardless of circumstance.
