devolve

verb
pass or delegate duties/responsibility
5.9
Pronunciation: / dɪˈvɑlv / (AmE), / dɪˈvɒlv / (BrE)
to pass or transfer duties, responsibilities, rights, or property to another person or group, often at a lower level or as an inheritance

Examples

To devolve power
The manager decided to devolve some of his tasks to junior staff members.
The responsibility for maintenance has devolved from the central government to local councils.
With the new policy, more authority will devolve to regional offices over time.
According to the agreement, all liabilities devolve to the heirs of the estate.
After the owner's passing, the property devolved to his niece as specified in the will.
The title will devolve to the eldest son upon the lord's death.

Related Terms

  • synonym: delegate - to assign responsibility or authority to another
  • synonym: inherit - to receive (property or rights) as an heir
  • antonym: retain - to keep or hold onto responsibilities or authority
