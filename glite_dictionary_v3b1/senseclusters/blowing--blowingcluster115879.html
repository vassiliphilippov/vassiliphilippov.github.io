blowing

noun
process of moving/directed gas
2.6
Pronunciation: / ˈbloʊɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbləʊɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of moving or directing gas, typically with force, for industrial or scientific purposes

Examples

Blowing oxygen
In metallurgy, blowing oxygen through molten iron helps reduce impurities.
Proper blowing is required to maintain ideal conditions inside the industrial furnace.
The blowing of hot air is essential during the glassmaking process.

Related Terms

  • antonym: suction - the process of drawing something in, as a contrast to blowing out
  • synonym: ventilation - the process of supplying fresh air and removing stale air
