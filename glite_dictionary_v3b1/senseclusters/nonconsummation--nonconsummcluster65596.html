nonconsummation

noun
failure to complete
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnkɒnsəˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkɒnsjʊˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
the failure to complete or fulfill something, especially the act of not completing a marriage by sexual intercourse

Examples

Marriage nonconsummation
The court granted an annulment on the grounds of nonconsummation of the marriage.
In some cultures, nonconsummation is considered a valid reason to end a marriage.
The couple struggled with nonconsummation and sought professional help to resolve the issue.

Related Terms

  • antonym: consummation - the act of making something complete, especially a marriage by sexual intercourse
  • synonym: incompletion - the state of not being finished or completed
