spite

verb
deliberately hurt someone's feelings
Pronunciation: / spaɪt / (AmE), / spaɪt / (BrE)
to deliberately hurt or offend the feelings of (someone), especially out of malice

Examples

To spite a friend
He only made that comment to spite me.
She refused to invite him, simply out of a desire to spite him.
Sometimes people spite others for no real reason at all.

Related Terms

  • antonym: console - to comfort someone who is upset
  • synonym: insult - to speak or act in a way that is rude or scornful toward someone
  • synonym: offend - to hurt someone's feelings; to insult
