double entendre

noun
expression with two meanings, one usually risqué
9.6 6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈdʌb.əl ɑːn.ˈtɑːn.drə/ (AmE), /ˈdʌb.əl ɒn.ˈtɒn.drə/ (BrE)
a phrase or expression open to two interpretations, one of which is usually risqué or indecent

Examples

A double entendre phrase
Her comment about the banana was a classic double entendre that didn't go unnoticed.

Writers often use double entendre to add layers of humor or suggestiveness to their work.
