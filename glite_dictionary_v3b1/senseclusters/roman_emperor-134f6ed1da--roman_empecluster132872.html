Roman Emperor

noun
sovereign ruler of the Roman Empire
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊmən ɛmˈpɪrər / (AmE), / ˈrəʊmən ɪmˈpɪərə / (BrE)
the sovereign ruler of the ancient Roman Empire, wielding supreme authority from Augustus to the fall of the Western Empire

Examples

The Roman Emperor Augustus
Augustus was the first Roman Emperor, establishing a new era of leadership in Rome.
Constantine, a notable Roman Emperor, played a key role in the spread of Christianity.
The power of the Roman Emperor was nearly absolute, especially during the early Imperial period.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: Roman Empire - the empire of ancient Rome
  • antonym: Roman Republic - the state of ancient Rome before the Empire
  • synonym: emperor - supreme ruler of an empire
