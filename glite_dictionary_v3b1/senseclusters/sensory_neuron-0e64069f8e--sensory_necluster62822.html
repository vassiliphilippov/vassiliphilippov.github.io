sensory neuron

noun
nerve cell transmitting sensory impulses
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛn.sə.ri ˈnʊrˌɑn / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.sər.i ˈnjʊə.rɒn / (BrE)
a nerve cell that carries impulses from sensory receptors toward the brain or spinal cord

Examples

Sensory neuron transmits signals
A sensory neuron transmits signals from your skin to your spinal cord when you touch something hot.
Damage to a sensory neuron can reduce your ability to feel pain or temperature changes.
The experiment measured the response of the sensory neuron to different stimuli.

Related Terms

  • antonym: motor neuron - a neuron carrying impulses away from the brain or spinal cord to muscles or glands
