clientelist

adjective
political exchange system
Pronunciation: / ˈklaɪəntəˌlɪst / (AmE), / ˈklaɪəntɪlɪst / (BrE)
relating to a system in which goods or services are exchanged for political support

Examples

Clientelist politics
The government was accused of using clientelist methods to secure votes in rural areas.
Many analysts believe that clientelist networks hinder the development of true democracy.
A clientelist approach often leads to unequal distribution of resources.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: corrupt - acting dishonestly for personal gain, especially in politics
  • synonym: patronage - the support given by a patron, often in politics
