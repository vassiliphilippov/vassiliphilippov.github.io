wasting disease

noun
disease causing progressive weight and strength loss
Pronunciation: / ˈweɪstɪŋ dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈweɪstɪŋ dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A disease, especially one affecting the lungs, that causes a gradual loss of weight, strength, and vitality.

Examples

A wasting disease of lungs
After months of illness, the patient was diagnosed with a rare wasting disease affecting her lungs.
Doctors warned that malnutrition could lead to a dangerous wasting disease if left untreated.
Tuberculosis was once known as a wasting disease because of its gradual weakening of the patient.
