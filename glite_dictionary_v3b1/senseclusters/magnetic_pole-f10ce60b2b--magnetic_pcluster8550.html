magnetic pole

noun
either point where Earth's magnetic field is vertical
Pronunciation: / mæɡˈnɛtɪk poʊl / (AmE), / mæɡˈnɛtɪk pəʊl / (BrE)
either of the two points on the Earth's surface where the magnetic field is vertical, usually referred to as the North Magnetic Pole and the South Magnetic Pole.

Examples

Earth's magnetic pole
Compasses point toward the magnetic pole, not the geographic North Pole.
Scientists located the exact position of the magnetic pole during their Arctic expedition.
The magnetic pole is constantly shifting due to changes in the Earth's core.

Related Terms

  • antonym: geographic pole - either of the two points where the Earth's axis of rotation meets the surface
