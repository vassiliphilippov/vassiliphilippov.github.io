nickel-and-dime

verb
accumulate gradually in small amounts
9.9 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈnɪk.əl ən daɪm/ /; / /ˈnɪk.əl ænd daɪm/ (AmE), /ˈnɪk.əl ən daɪm/ /; / /ˈnɪk.əl ænd daɪm/ (BrE)
to handle money or resources in a way that involves only small amounts, often by saving, accumulating, or spending very little at a time

Examples

To nickel-and-dime expenses
He had to nickel-and-dime his way through college by working part-time jobs.
She managed to nickel-and-dime enough money together to pay the bills each month.
The organization hopes to nickel-and-dime small donations until they reach their fundraising goal.
He tends to nickel-and-dime every part of his budget, sometimes to a fault.
The company nickel-and-dimes its employees on expense reimbursements.
We can't nickel-and-dime our way to financial success without making bigger changes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accumulate - to gather or acquire over time
  • synonym: scrimp - to save or economize severely
