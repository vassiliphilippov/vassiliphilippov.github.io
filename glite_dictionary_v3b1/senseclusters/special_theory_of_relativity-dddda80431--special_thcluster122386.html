special theory of relativity

noun
Einstein's 1905 theory of relativity and light speed
6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈspeʃ.əl ˈθiː.ə.ri əv re.lə.ˈtɪv.ə.tiː/ /; / /ˈspeʃ.əl ˈθiː.riː əv re.lə.ˈtɪv.ə.tiː/ (AmE), /ˈspeʃ.əl ˈθɪə.ri əv re.lə.ˈtɪv.ə.tiː/ (BrE)
The scientific theory proposed by Albert Einstein in 1905, stating that the laws of physics are the same in all inertial frames of reference and that the speed of light in a vacuum is constant, regardless of the observer's motion.

Examples

Einstein's special theory of relativity
Einstein's special theory of relativity revolutionized our understanding of space and time.
Many scientific experiments have confirmed the predictions made by the special theory of relativity.
The special theory of relativity explains why the speed of light remains constant for all observers.
