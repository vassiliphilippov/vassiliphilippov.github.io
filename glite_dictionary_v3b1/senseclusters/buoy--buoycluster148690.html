buoy

verb
keep afloat; prevent sinking
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈbuː.i /; / ˈbu.i, ˈbɔɪ / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪ / (BrE)
to keep someone or something afloat or to support and encourage emotionally

Examples

To buoy spirits
Hope buoyed the stranded sailors as they awaited rescue.
The floatation device buoyed him in the rough water.
The life jacket buoyed her until help arrived.
Her hopes were buoyed by the good news from her doctor.
The life jacket will buoy you if you fall into the water.
The small boat managed to buoy through the waves after the storm.
The positive news helped to buoy the team's spirits after their recent losses.
Rescue workers used a life jacket to buoy the child in the water until help arrived.
Her encouragement continued to buoy him through the challenges of his new job.

Related Terms

  • antonym: discourage - to make someone less confident or hopeful
  • synonym: encourage - to give someone support or confidence
  • synonym: float - to stay on the surface of a liquid and not sink
  • synonym: lift - to raise or improve someone's mood or situation
