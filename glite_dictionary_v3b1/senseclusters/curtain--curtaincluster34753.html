curtain

verb
to provide with curtains
Pronunciation: / ˈkɝː.tn̩ /; / ˈkɜrtən / (AmE), / ˈkɜː.tən /; / ˈkɜːtən / (BrE)
to cover, enclose, or screen something by hanging a curtain or similar material over it

Examples

To curtain a window
After moving in, they decided to curtain all the front windows for privacy.
She quickly curtained the open shelves to hide the clutter.
The stagehands will curtain the theater before the performance begins.
They were curtaining the windows before the guests arrived to darken the room.
The stage crew quickly curtained the set between scenes to prepare for the next act.
She likes curtaining her bedroom to keep out the morning sunlight.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drape - to cover or decorate with cloth in loose folds
  • antonym: uncover - to remove a cover from something
