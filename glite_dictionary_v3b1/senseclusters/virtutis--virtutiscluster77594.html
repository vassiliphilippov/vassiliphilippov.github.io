virtutis

noun
Latin genitive of virtue
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈvɜːr.tuː.tɪs/ /; / /ˈvɜːr.tjuː.tɪs/ (AmE), /ˈvɜː.tuː.tɪs/ /; / /ˈvɜː.tjuː.tɪs/ (BrE)
the Latin genitive form meaning 'of virtue', used in legal or scholarly contexts to indicate possession or relation to virtue

Examples

Monumentum virtutis
The inscription read 'monumentum virtutis' to honor the hero's character.
In Roman law, the phrase 'praemium virtutis' referred to a reward for bravery.
Scholars often discuss the meaning of 'testamentum virtutis' in ancient texts.
