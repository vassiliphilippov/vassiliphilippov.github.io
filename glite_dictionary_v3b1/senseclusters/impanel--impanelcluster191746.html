impanel

verb
enroll people, especially jurors, onto a panel
8.2
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpænəl /; / ɛmˈpænəl / (AmE), / ɪmˈpæn(ə)l /; / ɪmˈpænəl /; / ɪmˈpanəl / (BrE)
to officially select and enroll people to serve as members of a jury (form a jury or panel for a trial)

Examples

To impanel a jury
After the lawyers finished their questioning, the clerk began to impanel the selected individuals.
It is the judge's responsibility to impanel a fair and impartial group of jurors.
The court will impanel a new jury for the trial next week.
It took almost a day to impanel enough jurors for the high-profile case.
Once the judge decided to impanel a grand jury, the legal process moved forward.
The court will impanel a jury before the start of the trial.
Before the proceedings can start, the judge must empanel the committee.
It took several hours to empanel a panel of impartial jurors.
The court will empanel a new jury for the upcoming trial.

Related Terms

  • synonym: empanel - to select jurors from a list; alternative spelling of 'impanel'
  • synonym: impanel - to select or enroll jurors for a jury (alternative spelling)
