violate

verb
to damage or disrespect something sacred or private
1.8 4.0
Pronunciation: /ˈvaɪ.ə.leɪt/ (AmE), /ˈvaɪ.ə.leɪt/ (BrE)
to damage, disrespect, or treat something sacred, private, or respected in a way that shows a lack of respect; to desecrate

Examples

To violate a sacred place
By entering my office without permission, you violate my personal space and trust.
The construction of the new road would violate the peaceful environment of the old village.
They argued that cutting down the trees would violate a place considered sacred by the local community.
He was accused of having violated the sanctity of the memorial site.
It is deeply offensive to violate sacred traditions during such ceremonies.
The vandals violated the ancient temple, leaving graffiti on its walls.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defile - to spoil or damage the purity or appearance of something sacred
  • synonym: desecrate - to treat a sacred place or thing with violent disrespect
  • synonym: profane - to treat something sacred with irreverence or disrespect
  • antonym: sanctify - to make holy or to treat as sacred
