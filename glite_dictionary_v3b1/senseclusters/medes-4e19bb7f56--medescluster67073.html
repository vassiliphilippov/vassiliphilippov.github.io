Medes

proper noun
ancient Iranian people
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmiːdz / (AmE), / ˈmiːdz / (BrE)
an ancient Iranian people who lived in northwestern Iran and established a powerful kingdom in the first millennium BCE

Examples

The Medes people
The Medes formed an alliance with the Babylonians to defeat the Assyrian Empire.
Archaeologists have discovered artifacts from the ancient Medes in northwestern Iran.
The kingdom of the Medes was eventually absorbed by the Persian Empire.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Assyrians - an ancient Mesopotamian people and empire neighboring the Medes
  • synonym: Persians - an ancient Iranian people who later established the Persian Empire
