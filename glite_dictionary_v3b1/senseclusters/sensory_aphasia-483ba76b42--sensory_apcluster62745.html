sensory aphasia

A medical condition in which a person can speak fluently but their speech is meaningless, and they have great difficulty understanding spoken or written language.

Examples

Sensory aphasia symptoms
Due to his sensory aphasia, reading and writing became extremely challenging tasks.
Individuals with sensory aphasia often struggle to understand what others are saying to them.
The neurologist diagnosed the patient with sensory aphasia after observing fluent but meaningless speech.

Synonyms

  • Wernicke's aphasia
    9.5 0.01/m
    Wernicke's aphasia is the standard clinical name for fluent meaningless speech and poor comprehension, while sensory aphasia is a descriptive label.
  • receptive aphasia
    9.9 0.00/m
    receptive aphasia is a descriptive term that highlights loss of understanding, while sensory aphasia names the same fluent but meaningless speech.
  • fluent aphasia
    8.6 0.01/m
    fluent aphasia describes speech that stays smooth but often nonsensical, while sensory aphasia focuses on the added severe comprehension loss.
  • impressive aphasia
    9.9 0.00/m
    impressive aphasia is an older term used in some texts for fluent meaningless speech, while sensory aphasia is the modern clinical phrase.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Wernicke's aphasia - a specific type of sensory aphasia involving damage to Wernicke's area
  • antonym: motor aphasia - a type of aphasia with impaired speech production but relatively preserved comprehension
