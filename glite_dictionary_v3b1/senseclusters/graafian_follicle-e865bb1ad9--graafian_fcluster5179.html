Graafian follicle

noun
ovarian structure containing a developing egg
Pronunciation: / ɡreɪˈæfiən ˈfɑlɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈɡrɑːfiən ˈfɒlɪkəl / (BrE)
a fluid-filled cavity in the mammalian ovary that surrounds and supports the development of an egg cell

Examples

A Graafian follicle in ovary
A healthy Graafian follicle is essential for normal female fertility.
Researchers examined the structure of the Graafian follicle in laboratory animals.
The Graafian follicle matures and releases an egg during ovulation.
