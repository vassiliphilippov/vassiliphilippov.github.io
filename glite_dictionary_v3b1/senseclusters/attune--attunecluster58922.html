attune

verb
make aware or in harmony with
6.4 1.93/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàtun / (AmE), / …ôÀàtjuÀên / (BrE)
to make someone or something familiar with or able to respond to something; to bring into harmony or accord

Examples

To attune ears
Musicians must attune their ears to subtle differences in pitch.
She tried to attune herself to the rhythms of city life after moving from the countryside.
The therapist's role is to attune to the emotional needs of the client.

Synonyms

  • attune to
    6.5 1.15/m
    attune to means actively adjust or adapt yourself to a particular situation or person, while attune means bring something into general harmony.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adapt - to adjust to new conditions
  • antonym: alienate - to make someone feel estranged or out of harmony
  • synonym: harmonize - to bring into agreement or harmony
