planned

adjective
prearranged; done with intention
3.3
Pronunciation: / plænd / (AmE), / plænd / (BrE)
done intentionally or arranged in advance according to a plan

Examples

A planned event
His speech was clearly planned and practiced in advance.
The company executed a planned expansion into new markets last year.
They took a planned approach to solving the complicated problem.
The company held a meeting to discuss the planned expansion of its operations.
The new neighborhood is a planned community with parks and schools incorporated.
Their planned vacation was postponed due to unforeseen circumstances.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deliberate - done consciously and intentionally
  • synonym: organized - arranged in an orderly way, often with a plan
  • antonym: spontaneous - done without planning or premeditation
  • antonym: unplanned - not arranged or designed in advance
