farfetched

adjective
unlikely or unconvincing idea or story
7.5 0.57/m
Pronunciation: / f…ërÀàf…õt Ét / (AmE), / f…ëÀêÀàf…õt Ét / (BrE)
(of ideas, stories, or explanations) very unlikely, difficult to believe, or unconvincing; not seeming natural or reasonable.

Examples

A farfetched story
Her explanation for missing the meeting sounded a bit farfetched to me.
Some of his conspiracy theories are incredibly farfetched and lack any evidence.
The plot of that movie is so farfetched that it's almost laughable.

Synonyms

  • implausible
    7.0 2.34/m
    implausible describes something that seems unlikely and not reasonable, while farfetched suggests ideas feel exaggerated or forced.
  • unbelievable
    4.9 13/m
    unbelievable often means very surprising or amazing, while farfetched means unlikely and hard to believe in a realistic way.

Related Terms

  • antonym: credible - believable or convincing
  • synonym: implausible - not seeming reasonable or probable
