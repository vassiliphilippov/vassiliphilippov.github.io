thresh out

verb
discuss thoroughly to clarify or solve
5.8
Pronunciation: / θrɛʃ aʊt / (AmE), / θrɛʃ aʊt / (BrE)
to discuss a problem, idea, or details thoroughly in order to reach a clear conclusion or solution

Examples

To thresh out problems
Management and staff must thresh out their differences before the project can proceed.
The committee will thresh out the key issues at tomorrow's meeting.
We stayed up late to thresh out the terms of the agreement.
