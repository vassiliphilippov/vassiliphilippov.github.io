mackinaw

noun
flat-bottomed Great Lakes boat
Pronunciation: / ˈmækɪˌnɔ / (AmE), / ˈmakɪnɔː / (BrE)
a flat-bottomed boat, especially one used on the upper Great Lakes, designed for use in shallow waters

Examples

Mackinaw boat
Fishermen loaded supplies onto the mackinaw before heading out onto Lake Superior.
Local historians restored a 19th-century mackinaw for the maritime museum display.
The old mackinaw was perfectly suited for navigating the shallow waters near the shore.
