coronagraph

noun
telescope light blocker
Pronunciation: / kəˈroʊnəˌɡræf / (AmE), / kəˈrəʊnəˌɡrɑːf / (BrE)
an instrument attached to a telescope that blocks direct light from a star so that faint objects near the star, such as its corona, can be observed

Examples

A coronagraph instrument
The astronomer used a coronagraph to study the faint rings around the distant planet.
A powerful coronagraph can reveal exoplanets that would otherwise be hidden by a star's glare.
Scientists developed a new coronagraph to improve the imaging of circumstellar disks.
The scientists used a coronagraph to capture images of the sun's faint outer atmosphere.
A coronagraph makes it possible to study the solar corona during times other than a total eclipse.
Modern telescopes often include a coronagraph to help astronomers observe exoplanets near bright stars.

Related Terms

  • synonym: spectrograph - an instrument that separates and records light by wavelength
  • synonym: spectroscope - an instrument for observing spectra
  • synonym: telescope - an instrument for viewing distant objects
