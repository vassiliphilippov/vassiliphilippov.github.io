snare

noun
thing that deceives or traps unexpectedly
Pronunciation: / snɛr / (AmE), / snɛə / (BrE)
a situation or thing that tricks or traps someone, often by appearing attractive or harmless at first.

Examples

The snare of fame
He fell into the snare of thinking money would solve all his problems.
The offer seemed generous, but it turned out to be a clever snare.
The path to fame can be full of hidden snares for the unwary.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pitfall - a hidden danger or difficulty that is easily encountered
  • synonym: trap - a device or situation for catching someone or something by surprise
