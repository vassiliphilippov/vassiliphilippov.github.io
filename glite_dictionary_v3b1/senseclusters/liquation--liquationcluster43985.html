liquation

noun
metal separation process
9.9
Pronunciation: /lɪ.ˈkweɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /lɪ.ˈkweɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a process of separating metals by partially melting a mixture so that one metal liquefies and can be removed

Examples

Liquation of metals
The ancient technique of liquation was used to separate silver from copper alloys.
Modern refineries sometimes employ liquation to recover valuable metals from mixed ores.
By using liquation, metallurgists can efficiently isolate metals with different melting points.

Related Terms

  • synonym: refining - purifying a substance, especially metals
  • synonym: smelting - extracting metal from ore by heating and melting
