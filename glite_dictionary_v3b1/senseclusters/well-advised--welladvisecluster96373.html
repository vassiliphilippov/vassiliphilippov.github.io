well-advised

adjective
wise due to advice or consideration
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌwɛl əˈdvaɪzd / (AmE), / ˌwɛl ədˈvaɪzd / (BrE)
Showing good judgment or wisdom, typically as a result of careful consideration or taking advice.

Examples

To be well-advised
It is well-advised to check the weather forecast before planning a picnic.
The committee made a well-advised decision to postpone the project.
You would be well-advised to speak with a lawyer before signing the contract.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ill-advised - done without wise consideration; unwise
