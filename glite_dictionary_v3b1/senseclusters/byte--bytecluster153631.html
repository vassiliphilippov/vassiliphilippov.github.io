byte

noun
unit of digital information (8 bits)
4.0
Pronunciation: / baɪt / (AmE), / baɪt / (BrE)
a unit of digital information consisting of eight bits, used to represent a character such as a letter or number in computing

Examples

A byte of data
Each byte can store a single character in computer memory.
Modern computers usually use 8 bits to form one byte.
The file size was measured in bytes to ensure accuracy.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: bit - the basic unit of information in computing, representing a binary value (0 or 1)
  • degree higher: word - in computing, a group of bytes processed as a single unit by the CPU
