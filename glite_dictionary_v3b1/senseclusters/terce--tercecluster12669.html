terce

noun
third canonical hour (about 9 a.m.)
Pronunciation: / tɜrs / (AmE), / tɜːs / (BrE)
the third of the canonical hours, traditionally observed around 9 a.m.

Examples

The terce prayer
According to the old liturgical schedule, terce occurred after prime and before sext.
In medieval monasteries, terce marked a pause in daily labor for devotion.
The monks gathered in the chapel to pray at terce.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: prime - the first canonical hour, about 6 a.m.
  • degree higher: sext - the sixth canonical hour, about 12 p.m.
