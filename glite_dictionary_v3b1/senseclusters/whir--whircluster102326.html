whir

noun
soft continuous sound of rapid motion
3.7 0.77/m
Pronunciation: / w…ùÀê / (AmE), / w…úÀê / (BrE)
a continuous soft, rapid sound made by something moving quickly or rotating

Examples

A whir of fans
A sudden whir erupted as the old computer started up.
He listened to the whir of insect wings outside the window.
The only sound in the room was the gentle whir of the ceiling fan.

Synonyms

  • whirr
    7.4 0.34/m
    whirr is a constant buzzing or humming from fast-moving parts, while whir is a softer, quicker rotating sound.
  • whirring
    4.4 1.25/m
    whirring describes the ongoing rapid sound of spinning or moving parts, while whir names that short, soft continuous noise.
  • birr
    9.9 0.00/m
    birr is a whirring, humming noise from rapid motion, while whir is a gentle, continuous sound of spinning parts.
  • buzz
    1.0 3.95/m
    buzz is a low, steady hum like a bee or motor, while whir is a higher, faster spin sound.
