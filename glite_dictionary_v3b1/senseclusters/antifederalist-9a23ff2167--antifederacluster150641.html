Antifederalist

adjective
opposed to federalism
9.4
Pronunciation: /æn.tɪ.ˈfed.rəl.ɪst/ /; / /ən.tɪ.ˈfed.rəl.ɪst/ (AmE), /æn.tɪ.ˈfed.rəl.ɪst/ /; / /ən.tɪ.ˈfed.rəl.ɪst/ (BrE)
opposed to federalism or the transfer of power from local or regional governments to a central government

Examples

Antifederalist ideas
The Antifederalist viewpoint was popular among those who feared a strong central government.
Some historians argue that Antifederalist concerns influenced the addition of the Bill of Rights.
The council adopted an Antifederalist stance, insisting on more local control over education.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decentralist - favoring the distribution of power away from a central authority
  • antonym: federalist - supporting a strong central government or federalism
