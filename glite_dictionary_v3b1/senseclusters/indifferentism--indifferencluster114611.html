indifferentism

noun
religious equality doctrine
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdɪfərəntɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪnˈdɪfərəntɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief or doctrine that all religions are equally valid and that no single religion is uniquely true.

Examples

Indifferentism in religion
Some philosophers argue that indifferentism promotes tolerance among people of different faiths.
The church leaders debated whether indifferentism undermines the importance of religious doctrine.
Critics of indifferentism claim it ignores the unique teachings of each religion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exclusivism - the belief that only one religion is true
  • synonym: pluralism - the acceptance of multiple religions or beliefs as valid
