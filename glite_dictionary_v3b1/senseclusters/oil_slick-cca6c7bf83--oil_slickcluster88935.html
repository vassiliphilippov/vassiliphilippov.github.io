oil slick

noun
thin layer of oil on water
Pronunciation: / ɔɪl slɪk / (AmE), / ɔɪl slɪk / (BrE)
a thin layer of oil floating on the surface of water, often caused by an accidental spill of crude oil or fuel

Examples

An oil slick on water
An oil slick spread across the surface of the ocean after the tanker accident.
Officials worked quickly to contain the oil slick before it reached the marina.
The birds were covered in oil from the oil slick near the coastline.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oil spill - an accidental release of oil into the environment
