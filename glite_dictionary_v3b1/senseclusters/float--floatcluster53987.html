float

verb
let a currency's value fluctuate
Pronunciation: / floʊt / (AmE), / fləʊt / (BrE)
to allow the value of a currency to change freely in response to market forces, rather than fixing or controlling it

Examples

To float a currency
After years of government control, the central bank decided to float the national currency.
Many emerging economies have chosen to float their currencies to attract foreign investment.
The decision to float the peso led to significant changes in the exchange rate.

Related Terms

  • antonym: peg - to fix a currency's exchange rate to another currency or basket of currencies
