pure mathematics

noun
mathematics focusing on theory, not applications
Pronunciation: / pjʊr ˌmæθəˈmætɪks / (AmE), / pjʊə ˌmæθəˈmætɪks / (BrE)
The branches of mathematics that focus on the theoretical foundations and structure of mathematics itself, without regard to direct practical application.

Examples

Pure mathematics theory
He published several groundbreaking papers in the field of pure mathematics during his career.
Many students find pure mathematics challenging because it emphasizes theory over direct application.
She has always been fascinated by pure mathematics and enjoys studying abstract algebra.

Related Terms

  • antonym: applied mathematics - branches of mathematics concerned with practical applications
