fracture

noun
crack in the earth's crust (geology)
1.6
Pronunciation: / ˈfræk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˈfræk.tʃə /; / ˈfræk.tʃər / (BrE)
a break or crack in something hard, especially a bone or the earth

Examples

Bone fracture
Geologists surveyed the fracture to better understand the regional fault lines.
The ancient river once flowed along a natural fracture in the Earth's crust.
The earthquake caused a major fracture in the bedrock beneath the city.
A fracture of the ankle can take months to recover fully.
He wore a cast for six weeks to help the fracture heal properly.
The X-ray confirmed a fracture in her left arm.
A loud crack announced the fracture of the ice beneath their feet.
Sudden temperature changes can cause the fracture of glass.
The X-ray revealed a fracture in her arm after she fell.

Related Terms

  • synonym: break - an instance of breaking something, especially a bone
  • antonym: dislocation - an injury in which a bone is forced from its normal position
  • synonym: fault - a break in the earth's crust causing displacement; geological feature
  • antonym: repair - the act of fixing something that is broken
