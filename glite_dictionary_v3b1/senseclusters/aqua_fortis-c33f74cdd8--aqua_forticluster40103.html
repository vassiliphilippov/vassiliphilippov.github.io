aqua fortis

noun
nitric acid (technical/historical)
Pronunciation: / ˌɑkwə ˈfɔrtɪs / (AmE), / ˌakwə ˈfɔːtɪs / (BrE)
Nitric acid, especially when used in the production of fertilizers, explosives, and rocket fuels.

Examples

Aqua fortis acid
Alchemists in the Middle Ages referred to nitric acid as aqua fortis because of its ability to dissolve metals.
The laboratory stored bottles of aqua fortis for various chemical experiments.
When mixed with other chemicals, aqua fortis can be used to create powerful explosives.
