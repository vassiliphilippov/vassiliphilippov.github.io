foreboding

adjective
giving an ominous warning
Pronunciation: / fɔrˈboʊdɪŋ / (AmE), / fɔːˈbəʊdɪŋ / (BrE)
giving a strong sense of something bad or harmful that will happen; ominous or predicting evil

Examples

Foreboding silence
A foreboding silence filled the air before the storm broke.
Dark clouds gathered with a foreboding presence over the city.
The novel opens with a foreboding tone that hints at tragedy to come.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hopeful - giving a sense that good things will happen
  • synonym: ominous - giving the impression that something bad is going to happen
