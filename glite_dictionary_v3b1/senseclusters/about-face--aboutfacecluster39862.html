about-face

verb
to change abruptly to the opposite view
9.6 6.7
Pronunciation: / əˈbaʊt feɪs / (AmE), / əˈbaʊt feɪs / (BrE)
to suddenly change your opinion or position to the exact opposite.

Examples

To about-face on an issue
After hearing the evidence, she did an about-face and supported the new proposal.
His sudden about-face on this issue surprised his colleagues.
The company performed an about-face on its environmental policy when customers complained.

Related Terms

  • synonym: U-turn - a complete change of opinion or plan
  • synonym: flip-flop - to make a sudden and complete change in policy or opinion
  • antonym: stick to one's guns - to refuse to change your opinion or decision
