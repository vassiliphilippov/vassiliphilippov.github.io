Balarama

proper noun
elder brother of Krishna in Hindu mythology
Pronunciation: / ˌbɑːləˈrɑːmə / (AmE), / ˌbæləˈrɑːmə / (BrE)
In Hindu mythology, the elder brother of Krishna and an incarnation of the god Vishnu.

Examples

Balarama in mythology
Many devotees worship Balarama as a symbol of strength and duty in Hindu tradition.
Stories about Balarama and Krishna are popular in Indian mythology.
The festival of Raksha Bandhan is said to celebrate the bond between Balarama and his siblings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Krishna - a major Hindu deity worshipped as the eighth avatar of Vishnu
  • synonym: Vishnu - a principal Hindu deity, the preserver in the Hindu trinity
