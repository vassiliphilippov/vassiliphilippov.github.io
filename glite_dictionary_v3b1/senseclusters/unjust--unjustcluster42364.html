unjust

adjective
not fair or morally right
4.0 5.67/m
Pronunciation: / ÊŒnËˆdÊ’ÊŒst / (AmE), / ÊŒnËˆdÊ’ÊŒst / (BrE)
not fair or not morally right; lacking justice

Examples

An unjust decision
It would be unjust to punish everyone for the mistake of a single person.
Many considered the new law to be unjust and called for its repeal.
She fought against unjust treatment in the workplace.
It would be unjust to punish everyone for the actions of a few.
Many considered the new law to be unjust and protested its implementation.
She felt the accusation was completely unjust and defended herself strongly.
It would be unjust to blame her for something she did not do.
Many people protested against the unjust law that targeted minorities.
They believed the verdict was unjust and planned to appeal the decision.

Synonyms

  • unfair
    1.0 22/m
    unfair describes actions that treat people unequally or with bias, while unjust emphasizes lacking moral rightness or legal justice.
  • inequitable
    8.0 0.84/m
    inequitable refers to unequal or unfair treatment or distribution, while unjust focuses on a lack of moral or legal rightness.
  • wrongful
    4.6 2.19/m
    wrongful often means illegal or against the law, while unjust means morally not right and may not always be illegal.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fair - treating people equally without favoritism or discrimination
  • antonym: just - based on or behaving according to what is morally right and fair
  • synonym: unfair - not based on or behaving according to the principles of equality and justice
