represent

verb
act as legal counsel in court
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛprəˈzɛnt /; / ˌrɛprɪˈzɛnt / (AmE), / ˌrɛprɪˈzɛnt / (BrE)
to act or speak officially for someone or a group, especially in legal, political, or organizational situations

Examples

To represent a client
He has represented dozens of clients in high-profile criminal cases.
Ms. Thompson was hired to represent the accused during the trial.
Only a licensed attorney can represent someone in federal court.
He was chosen to represent the entire team at the meeting.
She was elected to represent her district in Parliament.
The lawyer will represent the victims in court.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defend - to speak or act in support of someone in a court case
  • synonym: delegate - a person acting as a representative for others
  • antonym: misrepresent - to state or present in a false or misleading way
  • synonym: speak for - to state something on behalf of another
