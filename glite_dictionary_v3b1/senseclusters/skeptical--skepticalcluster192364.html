skeptical

adjective
doubting accepted beliefs, especially religious claims
Pronunciation: / ˈskɛptɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈskɛptɪkəl / (BrE)
having or showing doubt; not easily convinced that something is true or real

Examples

A skeptical look
He gave a skeptical look when asked to believe in the existence of ghosts.
Her skeptical attitude made her question every religious story she heard.
Many scientists remain skeptical about claims of supernatural miracles.
Despite his skeptical attitude, he decided to give the proposal a chance.
Many scientists remain skeptical about the effectiveness of the new treatment.
She gave him a skeptical look when he explained his incredible story.
Many historians are sceptical of ancient legends that lack solid evidence.
She remains sceptical about the existence of miracles despite what she has heard.
The committee took a sceptical approach to the extraordinary claims presented.

Related Terms

  • antonym: convinced - completely certain about something
  • antonym: credulous - willing to believe things easily
  • synonym: cynical - believing people are motivated by self-interest; distrustful of sincerity
  • synonym: doubtful - feeling unsure or lacking conviction
  • synonym: skeptical - Alternative spelling, chiefly American English
