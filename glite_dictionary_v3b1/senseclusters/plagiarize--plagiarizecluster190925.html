plagiarize

verb
copy another's work without attribution
3.4 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈpleɪ.dʒə.raɪz/ /; / /ˈpleɪ.dʒɪə.raɪz/ (AmE), /ˈpleɪ.dʒɪə.raɪz/ /; / /ˈpleɪ.dʒə.raɪz/ (BrE)
to take and use someone else's ideas, words, or work and present them as your own without proper acknowledgment

Examples

To plagiarize a paper
If you plagiarize another author's work, you may face serious consequences in academia.
The student was punished after he plagiarized large sections of his essay from an online source.
Writers are expected to research thoroughly but never plagiarize someone else's ideas.
If you plagiarise in your essay, you may risk severe academic penalties.
The student admitted that he had plagiarised large portions of his report.
Universities have strict policies to discourage students from plagiarising in their assignments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cite - to refer to as a source or authority
  • synonym: copy - to reproduce someone else's work or ideas
  • synonym: plagiarize - to copy someone else's work without credit (American spelling)
