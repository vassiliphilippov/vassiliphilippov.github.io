mislead

verb
cause to believe something untrue
Pronunciation: / mɪsˈliːd /; / mɪsˈlid / (AmE), / mɪsˈliːd / (BrE)
to cause someone to believe something that is not true, or to give information that leads someone to make a mistake

Examples

To mislead readers
Her statement might mislead readers into thinking the problem is solved.
The manager was accused of trying to mislead shareholders about the company's financial health.
We must ensure the advertising does not mislead the public.
He did not intend to mislead anyone, but his directions were confusing.
Her explanation was so vague that it misled most of the new employees.
The outdated map misled travelers heading to the park.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deceive - to make someone believe something that is not true
  • antonym: guide - to show someone the right way or give correct directions
  • antonym: inform - to provide someone with facts or information
  • synonym: misguide - to guide someone wrongly
  • synonym: misinform - to give someone false or inaccurate information
