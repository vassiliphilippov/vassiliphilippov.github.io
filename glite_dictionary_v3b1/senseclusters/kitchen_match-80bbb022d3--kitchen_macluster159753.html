kitchen match

noun
wooden match usable on rough surfaces
Pronunciation: / ˈkɪtʃən mætʃ / (AmE), / ˈkɪtʃɪn mætʃ / (BrE)
A wooden friction match that can be struck on any rough surface, typically used for lighting stoves or fires in kitchens.

Examples

A kitchen match box
He reached for a kitchen match to light the old gas stove.
The kitchen match lit easily against the side of the pan.
We keep a box of kitchen matches in the drawer for emergencies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: safety match - a match that only ignites when struck on a specially prepared surface
