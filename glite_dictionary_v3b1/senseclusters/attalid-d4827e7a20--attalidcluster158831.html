Attalid

proper noun
Pergamon dynasty member
Pronunciation: / ˈætəlɪd / (AmE), / ˈætəlɪd / (BrE)
a member of the dynasty that ruled the ancient city of Pergamon in Asia Minor from the 3rd to 2nd centuries BCE

Examples

An Attalid king
The Attalid dynasty was renowned for its impressive library in Pergamon.
Many ancient sculptures were commissioned by Attalid rulers to decorate their city.
Historians study the influence of Attalid kings on Hellenistic culture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Ptolemaic - a member of the Ptolemaic dynasty that ruled Egypt after Alexander the Great
  • synonym: Seleucid - a member of the Seleucid dynasty that ruled parts of Asia after Alexander the Great
