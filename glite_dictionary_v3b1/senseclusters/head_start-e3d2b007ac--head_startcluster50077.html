head start

noun
an early advantage over others
Pronunciation: / hɛd stɑrt / (AmE), / hɛd stɑːt / (BrE)
an advantage gained by beginning something before others, often giving a better chance of success

Examples

A head start in race
She had a head start on her homework and finished it before dinner.
The company got a head start in the market by launching its product early.
Training all summer gave him a head start over the other athletes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: advantage - a condition or circumstance that puts one in a favorable or superior position
  • antonym: disadvantage - an unfavorable circumstance or condition that reduces the chances of success
