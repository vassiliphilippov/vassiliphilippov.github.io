Dipogon lignosus

noun
South African evergreen vine with rosy purple flowers
Pronunciation: / daɪˈpoʊɡɒn lɪɡˈnoʊsəs / (AmE), / dɪˈpəʊɡɒn lɪɡˈnəʊsəs / (BrE)
A South African evergreen climbing plant with rosy purple flowers and edible bean-like pods, sometimes grown as green manure.

Examples

Dipogon lignosus climbing plant
Dipogon lignosus produces colorful clusters of blooms in late spring and early summer.
The edible pods of Dipogon lignosus can be cooked and eaten like snap beans.
The gardener planted Dipogon lignosus along the fence to create a flowering screen.
