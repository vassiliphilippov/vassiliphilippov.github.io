waft through the air

verb
move or float gently in the air
2.6 3.9
Pronunciation: /wɑːft θruː ðiː er/ /; / /wæft θruː ðiː er/ /; / /wɑːft θruː ðə er/ (AmE), /wɒft θruː ðiː eə/ /; / /wɑːft θruː ðiː eə/ /; / /wɒft θruː ðə eə/ (BrE)
to move lightly and smoothly through the air, typically referring to scents, sounds, or light objects

Examples

To waft through the air gently
In spring, the aroma of blooming flowers wafts through the air each morning.
Soft music wafted through the air from the open windows of the house.
The scent of fresh bread wafted through the air as I walked by the bakery.
