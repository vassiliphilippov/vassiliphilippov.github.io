Age of Man

noun
geological epoch of human existence
Pronunciation: / eɪdʒ əv mæn / (AmE), / eɪdʒ əv mæn / (BrE)
the geological period covering the last 2 million years, marked by the presence and evolution of humans; also called the Anthropocene or the age of Homo sapiens.

Examples

The Age of Man period
Fossils from the Age of Man reveal much about the evolution of our species.
Many scientists believe the Age of Man began with the rise of Homo erectus.
The Age of Man has seen rapid technological and cultural development.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Anthropocene - the proposed current geological epoch marked by significant human impact on Earth's geology and ecosystems
