candelilla

noun
Mexican wax-producing shrub
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˌkændəˈliːjə / (AmE), / ˌkændəlˈɪlə /; / ˌkændɪˈliːjə / (BrE)
a small wax-coated shrub (Euphorbia antisyphilitica) native to Mexico and the southwestern United States, whose stems are used to produce candelilla wax

Examples

The candelilla plant
Candelilla wax is harvested from the dried stems of the candelilla plant.
Local farmers collect candelilla for use in cosmetics and food products.
The candelilla shrub thrives in the arid climates of northern Mexico.
Many cosmetics contain candelilla wax as a natural ingredient.
Near the Texas border, fields of candelilla thrive in the arid climate.
The candelilla plant is harvested for its valuable natural wax.
