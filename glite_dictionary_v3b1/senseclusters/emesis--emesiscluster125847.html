emesis

noun
medical term for vomiting
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɛməsɪs / (AmE), / ˈɛməsɪs / (BrE)
the act or process of vomiting; the reflex of ejecting the contents of the stomach through the mouth

Examples

Chronic emesis
Chronic emesis may lead to dehydration if left untreated.
Nausea and emesis are common side effects of chemotherapy.
