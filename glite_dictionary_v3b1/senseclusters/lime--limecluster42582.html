lime

noun
white caustic substance from limestone
4.4 1.83/m
Pronunciation: / laÉªm / (AmE), / laÉªm / (BrE)
a white, alkaline or caustic substance (mainly calcium oxide) used in building materials and agriculture

Examples

Lime powder for soil
Adding lime to farmland can improve soil quality.
Old kilns were used for producing lime for mortar and plaster.
The walls were coated with lime to help preserve them from moisture.
Exposure to lime dust can irritate the eyes and lungs, so proper protection is advised.
The builders mixed lime with sand and water to create mortar for the walls.
When heated, limestone breaks down to form lime and carbon dioxide.

Synonyms

  • quicklime
    8.3 0.11/m
    quicklime is the industrial name for calcium oxide made by heating limestone, while lime is the general building and agricultural term.
  • caustic lime
    6.6 0.00/m
    caustic lime is a slightly formal technical name for caustic calcium oxide, while lime is the common word used in construction and farming.
  • calcined lime
    8.1 0.00/m
    calcined lime is the term for limestone heated to produce calcium oxide, while lime is the everyday name used in building and farming.
  • burnt lime
    7.4 0.00/m

  • unslaked lime
    9.9 0.00/m
    unslaked lime is calcium oxide not yet mixed with water, while lime generally refers to the dry caustic substance used in building and agriculture.
