proceed

verb
begin or continue an action or process
Pronunciation: / proʊˈsiːd /; / prəˈsid / (AmE), / prəˈsiːd / (BrE)
to begin or continue doing something, especially after a pause or according to a plan or direction

Examples

To proceed with plans
After the safety checks, we can proceed with the experiment.
Passengers are asked to proceed to gate 22 for boarding.
The meeting will proceed as planned despite the delay.
Let’s proceed to the next topic on the agenda.
The meeting will proceed as scheduled despite the delay.
They were unsure how the negotiations would proceed after the disagreement.
After the break, the conference will proceed as planned.
The train proceeded toward its final destination despite the weather.
We proceeded down the dark hallway with caution.

Related Terms

  • antonym: pause - to stop talking briefly
  • antonym: stop - to cease moving or acting
