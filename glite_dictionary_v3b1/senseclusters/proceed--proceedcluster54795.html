Synonyms

    1.0 88/m
    continue resumes an action after a pause and stays simple, while proceed often sounds more formal and can follow a plan.
  • go ahead
    2.3 47/m
    go ahead means start or carry on with someone's permission or without delay, while proceed is more formal and neutral.
  • carry on
    2.7 11/m
    carry on means keep doing something, often despite problems or interruption, while proceed is more formal and focused on a plan.
  • go on
    1.0 76/m
    go on means continue an activity or speech for some time, while proceed often implies following a step or plan.
  • move ahead
    2.7 2.88/m
    move ahead means make progress toward a goal or place, often physically or in time, while proceed is more general and formal.
  • go forward
    1.0 8.59/m
    go forward means move ahead in space, time, or progress, often with visible steps, while proceed can mean begin or continue more broadly.
  • go
    1.0 665/m
    go means move, develop, or happen in many ways and contexts, while proceed is more about starting or continuing by plan.
