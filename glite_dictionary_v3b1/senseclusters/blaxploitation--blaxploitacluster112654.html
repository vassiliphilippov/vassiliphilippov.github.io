blaxploitation

noun
the use of Black stereotypes, especially in films
Pronunciation: / blækˌsplɔɪˈteɪʃn̩ /; / ˌblækˌsplɔɪˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / blakˌsplɔɪˈteɪʃən /; / ˌblækˌsplɔɪˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
a style of films from the 1970s featuring Black characters and themes, often using stereotypes and made for commercial gain

Examples

Blaxploitation films
During the 1970s, Hollywood produced a wave of blaxploitation movies targeting urban audiences.
Many critics argue that blaxploitation films reinforced negative stereotypes of African Americans.
While some see blaxploitation as empowering, others view it as problematic and offensive.
The rise of blaxploitation in the 1970s brought new Black heroes to the big screen.
Many critics debate whether blaxploitation films helped or harmed the image of Black communities.
She wrote her thesis on the influence of blaxploitation movies in American cinema history.
