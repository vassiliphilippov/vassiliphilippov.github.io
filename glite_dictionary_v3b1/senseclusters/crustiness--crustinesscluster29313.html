crustiness

noun
hardness or irritability
4.6
Pronunciation: /ˈkrʌ.stɪ.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈkrʌ.stɪ.nəs/ (BrE)
the quality or state of having a hard, dry, or crisp outer layer (as in bread, skin, or surfaces)

Examples

The crustiness of bread
The crustiness of the bread made it perfect for soup.
His crustiness sometimes hides a very caring personality.
I love the crustiness of freshly baked baguettes with butter.
The nurse gently cleaned the child's cheek, removing the stubborn crustiness from the rash.
After the wound healed, only a slight crustiness remained on the skin.
The doctor examined the crustiness around the cut to check for signs of infection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crispness - the quality of being firm and easily broken or snapped
  • antonym: friendliness - the quality of being kind and pleasant
  • synonym: irritability - the quality of being easily annoyed or angered
  • synonym: scabbiness - the condition of being covered with scabs
  • antonym: smoothness - the quality of having an even, unbroken surface
  • antonym: softness - the quality of being smooth or not hard
