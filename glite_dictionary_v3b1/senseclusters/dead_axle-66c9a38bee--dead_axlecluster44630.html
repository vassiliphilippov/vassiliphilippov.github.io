dead axle

noun
axle that does not drive the wheel
7.2 6.1
Pronunciation: /ded ˈæk.səl/ (AmE), /ded ˈæk.səl/ (BrE)
An axle that supports a wheel but does not transmit driving power to it.

Examples

A dead axle supports
A dead axle simply supports the load without contributing to vehicle propulsion.
The front wheels of many trucks are mounted on a dead axle that does not deliver power.
Unlike the drive axle, the dead axle remains stationary relative to the suspension system.

Related Terms

  • antonym: live axle - an axle that transmits power to the wheel as well as supports it
