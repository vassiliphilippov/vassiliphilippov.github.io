unearth

verb
discover through investigation
8.0
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈɜrθ / (AmE), / ʌnˈɜːθ / (BrE)
to find or discover something, especially through careful investigation or search

Examples

To unearth a secret
During the investigation, detectives unearthed several documents that had been hidden for decades.
Journalists worked hard to unearth the truth behind the company’s sudden collapse.
The researchers managed to unearth new evidence after years of searching the archives.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bury - to cover something so as to hide it
  • synonym: discover - to find something for the first time
