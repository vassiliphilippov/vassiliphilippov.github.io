disobedient

refusing to obey authority

refusing or failing to obey rules, orders, or someone in authority

Examples

Disobedient child
His disobedient attitude often got him into trouble at school.
The dog's disobedient behavior made training difficult for its owner.
The teacher found it hard to manage the disobedient children in her class.

Synonyms

  • noncompliant
    7.6 0.52/m
    noncompliant is used in formal situations to mean refusing rules or requests, while disobedient is a general refusal to obey.
  • insubordinate
    8.1 0.30/m
    insubordinate means refusing orders or showing no respect in workplaces like the army, while disobedient is more general.
  • defiant
    5.6 4.36/m
    defiant shows open, bold resistance people see in faces or tone, while disobedient just means not following rules.
  • rebellious
    4.5 3.03/m
    rebellious shows a desire to resist authority or rules, often in youth or politics, while disobedient simply refuses orders.
  • recalcitrant
    8.1 1.30/m
    recalcitrant is stubbornly refusing control or discipline, often sounding formal and strong, while disobedient simply fails to obey.

Related Terms

  • antonym: obedient - willing to comply with orders or requests; submissive to authority
  • synonym: unruly - difficult to control; not following rules or discipline
