rabbet

verb
join using a rabbet joint
6.9 8.1
Pronunciation: /ˈræb.ɪt/ (AmE), /ˈræb.ɪt/ (BrE)
to cut a rectangular groove (rabbet) in wood or other material, or to join pieces using such a groove

Examples

To rabbet a board
He decided to rabbet the edges of the boards to create a stronger joint.
The carpenter rabbeted the frame pieces before attaching the panel.
When you rabbet properly, the shelves fit snugly into the cabinet sides.
He rabbeted the panels so they would join flush with the frame.
The carpenter will rabbet the edge of the board to fit the window frame.
You need a special tool to rabbet wood cleanly and accurately.

  • synonym: notch - to cut a notch as in woodworking
