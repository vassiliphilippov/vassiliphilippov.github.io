merc

noun
hired soldier
9.8
Pronunciation: / mɜrk / (AmE), / mɜːk / (BrE)
an informal term for a mercenary; a person hired to fight or work for money

Examples

A hired merc
The group hired a skilled merc to help with their mission.
In the story, a lone merc is sent behind enemy lines.
Many mercs work for private military companies these days.
The merc accepted the dangerous job without hesitation.
Rumors spread quickly about the new merc joining the team.
He worked as a merc in several conflict zones around the world.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mercenary - a professional soldier hired to serve in a foreign army
  • synonym: soldier - a person who serves in an army
  • antonym: volunteer - a person who offers to do something without pay
