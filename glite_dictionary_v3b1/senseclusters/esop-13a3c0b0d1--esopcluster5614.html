ESOP

noun
employee share ownership program
7.3
Pronunciation: /ˈiː.sɑːp/ /; / /ˈiː.səp/ (AmE), /ˈiː.sɒp/ /; / /ˈiː.səp/ (BrE)
A program in which employees earn shares in the company they work for, potentially allowing them to assume control of the company.

Examples

An ESOP program
After several years with the firm, Maria gained a significant stake through the ESOP.
Many technology companies offer an ESOP to attract and retain talented employees.
The ESOP gave workers a direct interest in the company's success.
