take responsibility

verb
accept accountability for something
Pronunciation: / teɪk rɪˌspɑnsɪˈbɪləti / (AmE), / teɪk rɪˌspɒnsɪˈbɪləti / (BrE)
to accept accountability or blame for something; to acknowledge and accept one's duty or role in a situation

Examples

To take responsibility for mistakes
After the accident, he bravely took responsibility and apologized to everyone involved.
It's important to take responsibility for your mistakes in order to grow.
The manager failed to take responsibility for the team's poor performance.
