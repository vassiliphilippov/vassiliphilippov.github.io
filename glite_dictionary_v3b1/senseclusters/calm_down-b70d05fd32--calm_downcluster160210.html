calm down

verb
become quieter or less agitated
1.4 14/m
Pronunciation: / k…ëm da än / (AmE), / k…ëÀêm da än / (BrE)
to become or make someone become less angry, excited, or upset; to relax after being agitated

Examples

To calm down quickly
After the argument, everyone needed a few minutes to calm down.
She took deep breaths to calm down before giving her speech.
The storm will calm down soon, so we can go outside.
He tried to calm down the children during the thunderstorm.

Please calm down before we discuss this any further.

Please calm down before we talk about what happened.
