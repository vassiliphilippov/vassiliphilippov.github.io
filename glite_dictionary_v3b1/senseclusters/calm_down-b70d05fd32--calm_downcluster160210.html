calm down

verb
become quieter or less agitated
1.0 1.3
Pronunciation: /kɑːm daʊn/ /; / /kɑːlm daʊn/ (AmE), /kɑːm daʊn/ /; / /kɑːlm daʊn/ (BrE)
to become or make someone become less angry, excited, or upset; to relax after being agitated

Examples

To calm down quickly
After the argument, everyone needed a few minutes to calm down.
She took deep breaths to calm down before giving her speech.
The storm will calm down soon, so we can go outside.
He tried to calm down the children during the thunderstorm.
It took her a few minutes to calm down after the argument.
Please calm down before we discuss this any further.
After a few deep breaths, he managed to calm down and explain the situation.
It took her a while to calm down after receiving the upsetting news.
Please calm down before we talk about what happened.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agitate - to make someone nervous or upset
  • synonym: cool off - to become calmer, less angry or excited
  • antonym: flare up - to become suddenly angry or violent again
  • synonym: relax - to make or become less tense or anxious
  • synonym: settle down - to become calm, stable, or less active
  • synonym: soothe - to make someone calmer or less upset
