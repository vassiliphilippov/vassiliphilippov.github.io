bell glass

noun
bell-shaped glass cover for protection or display
Pronunciation: / bɛl ɡlæs / (AmE), / bɛl ɡlɑːs / (BrE)
a bell-shaped glass cover used to protect and display delicate objects, cover scientific apparatus, or contain gases

Examples

A bell glass cover
Antique clocks are often displayed beneath a bell glass to showcase their intricate designs.
Scientists used a bell glass to contain the gas during the experiment.
The delicate sculpture was placed under a bell glass to protect it from dust and damage.
