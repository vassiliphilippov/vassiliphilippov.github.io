depredate

verb
to plunder or prey
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛprəˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛprɪdeɪt / (BrE)
to prey upon or plunder, especially by animals or pests causing damage to crops, livestock, or wildlife

Examples

To depredate crops
Wild boars often depredate farmers' fields, destroying crops overnight.
The invasive insects began to depredate the orchard, leaving many trees bare.
If left unchecked, rats can depredate stored grain supplies in rural areas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plunder - to steal goods, typically using force
  • antonym: protect - to keep safe from harm or damage
  • synonym: ravage - to cause severe and extensive damage
