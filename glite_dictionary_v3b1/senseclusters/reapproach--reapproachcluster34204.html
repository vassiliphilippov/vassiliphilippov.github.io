reapproach

verb
to approach again
5.8
Pronunciation: / ˌriːəˈproʊtʃ / (AmE), / ˌriːəˈprəʊtʃ / (BrE)
to approach again, especially after a previous attempt or contact

Examples

To reapproach the topic
After a short break, she decided to reapproach the subject with her manager.
He waited a few days before he would reapproach his friend about the disagreement.
If the first attempt fails, you can always reapproach the problem from a different angle.
