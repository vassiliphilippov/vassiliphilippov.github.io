Synonyms

  • cut off
    4.4 1.01/m
    cut off is a common phrase for detaching by cutting, while sever is more formal and often means complete separation.
  • excise
    7.2 1.17/m
    excise is a formal term for cutting something out, while sever is neutral and used for general complete separation.
  • cut away
    3.4 0.47/m
    cut away means removing part from the main piece by cutting, while sever stresses full detachment from the whole.
  • cut out
    3.2 5.10/m
    cut out means removing or shaping something by cutting, while sever focuses on cutting completely away from the whole.
  • chop off
    3.4 1.38/m
    chop off describes removing with a heavy quick blow, while sever is more neutral and does not imply violence.
  • lop off
    4.6 0.52/m
    lop off means cutting something away in a single quick swing, while sever is formal and may not suggest a single stroke.
