understructure

noun
lowest supporting part of a structure

the lowest supporting part of a building or structure; a base or foundation that supports the main structure above it

Examples

The understructure of a building
Before constructing the building, workers ensured the understructure was stable and secure.
Heavy rains caused erosion that threatened the understructure of the ancient temple.
The engineers carefully inspected the understructure of the bridge for any signs of damage.

Synonyms

  • substructure
    7.2 0.32/m
    substructure is the supporting construction under a building, while understructure is the lowest part that holds up the main structure above.
  • foundation
    3.6 10/m
    foundation is the deep base often below ground that spreads a building's weight, while understructure supports the parts above.
  • fundament
    9.5 0.01/m
    fundament is a formal, older word for a building's base, while understructure is the common term for the supporting part.
  • base
    1.0 22/m
    base is a general bottom or support for many objects, while understructure refers specifically to the lowest supporting part of a building.
  • foot
    1.0 26/m
    foot is the lowest support or bottom part of an object, while understructure means the full base that supports the whole building.
