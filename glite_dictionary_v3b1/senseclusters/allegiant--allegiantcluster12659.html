allegiant

adjective
loyal and devoted
8.2 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàlid í…ônt / (AmE), / …ôÀàliÀêd í…ônt / (BrE)
loyal and steadfast in devotion, especially to a ruler, government, or cause.

Examples

Allegiant to the king
As a knight, he remained allegiant to his king even in difficult times.
Despite the political turmoil, she stayed allegiant to her beliefs.
Many citizens felt allegiant to their country's traditions and values.

Synonyms

  • loyal
    1.0 20/m
    loyal means steady support in everyday relations, while allegiant emphasizes devoted support for a ruler, government, or cause.
  • steadfast
    5.6 2.17/m
    steadfast is firm and unwilling to change over time, while allegiant focuses on loyal devotion to a ruler or cause.
  • devoted
    3.4 4.32/m
    devoted shows warm, strong personal commitment to someone or something, while allegiant stresses duty toward a ruler or cause.
  • faithful
    1.8 5.96/m
    faithful stays true in promises or relationships, often emotional or marital, while allegiant centers on political or cause loyalty.

Related Terms
