Monothelite

noun
believer in one will of Christ
Pronunciation: / məˈnɑːθəˌlaɪt / (AmE), / məˈnɒθəˌlaɪt / (BrE)
a person who believes that Christ has only one will, according to a doctrine in early Christianity

Examples

A Monothelite believer
The Monothelite controversy was a major issue in the early Christian church.
He was labeled a Monothelite because of his views on the nature of Christ.
The council declared the Monothelite doctrine to be heretical.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Dyothelite - a person who believes Christ has two wills
  • synonym: Monophysite - a person who believes Christ has a single nature
