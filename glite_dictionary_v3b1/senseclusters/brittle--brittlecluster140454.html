brittle

adjective
easily broken or shattered
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɪt̬.əl / (AmE), / ˈbrɪt.əl / (BrE)
hard but easily broken or shattered; not flexible or elastic

Examples

Brittle glass
Her bones grew brittle with age, making her more susceptible to fractures.
Some plastics turn brittle in cold weather and may break unexpectedly.
The dry branches had become so brittle that they snapped with the slightest touch.
Due to improper processing, the metal remains brittle and prone to breaking.
Handle the brittle material with care to avoid accidental fractures.
The brittle glass shattered into tiny pieces upon impact.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ductile - (of metal) able to be drawn out into a thin wire; not brittle
  • synonym: fragile - easily broken or damaged
  • antonym: robust - strong and unlikely to break or fail
