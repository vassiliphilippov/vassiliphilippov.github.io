apoplexy

noun
sudden loss of consciousness from brain blood vessel issue
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈæp.əˌplɛk.si /; / ˈæpəˌplɛksi / (AmE), / ˈap.ə.plek.si /; / ˈapəplɛksi / (BrE)
a sudden loss of consciousness or control, especially due to a stroke (old term), or extreme anger

Examples

Sudden apoplexy attack
Her sudden collapse was first diagnosed as apoplexy by the attending physician.
In the nineteenth century, many sudden deaths were attributed to apoplexy.
The patient suffered apoplexy after the vessel in his brain ruptured.
The news of the scandal sent him into a fit of apoplexy.
After the heated argument, she was left speechless with apoplexy.
In the past, apoplexy was a common term for what we now call a stroke.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - a state of being relaxed and not angry
  • synonym: fury - intense, uncontrolled anger
  • synonym: stroke - sudden interruption of blood supply to the brain
