crisis

noun
crucial turning point or period of difficulty
Pronunciation: / ˈkraɪ.sɪs /; / ˈkraɪsɪs / (AmE), / ˈkraɪ.sɪs /; / ˈkraɪsɪs / (BrE)
a very difficult or dangerous time when important decisions must be made; a turning point

Examples

Financial crisis
After the financial crisis of 2008, many banks changed their policies.
The company faced a severe crisis when sales suddenly dropped last year.
The patient reached a crisis in his illness where immediate action was necessary.
During the financial crisis, many people lost their jobs.
She stayed calm throughout the crisis and helped everyone get to safety.
The company faced a major crisis when its main product failed.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: catastrophe - an event causing great and usually sudden damage or suffering
  • degree lower: difficulty - a state of being hard to deal with; less severe than crisis
  • synonym: disaster - a sudden event causing great damage or suffering
  • synonym: emergency - a serious, unexpected situation requiring immediate action
  • antonym: opportunity - a favorable time or set of circumstances
