tail

noun
rear part of a ship
1.0
Pronunciation: / teɪl / (AmE), / teɪl / (BrE)
the rear part of an animal, object, or structure, or something that sticks out at the end (such as a comet, ship, or aircraft)

Examples

A cat's tail
As we boarded, I noticed some rust at the tail.
The crew gathered near the tail before the vessel left the harbor.
The damage was most severe at the tail of the ship.
Birds use their tail for stability, much like an aircraft does.
Inspectors carefully checked the tail after the turbulent flight.
The damaged tail of the plane made landing difficult.
A comet's tail can be seen stretching across the night sky.
The airplane has a tail at the rear for stability.
The letter 'Q' is easy to recognize by the tail that curves downward.

Related Terms

  • synonym: appendage - a projecting part of a living organism or an object
  • antonym: head - the front part of the body in animals, especially distinguished from the tail
  • antonym: nose - the front part of an aircraft
  • synonym: stern - the rearmost part of a ship or boat
