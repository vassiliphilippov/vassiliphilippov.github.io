walk

noun
slow horse gait with two feet always on the ground
Pronunciation: / wɑk / (AmE), / wɔːk / (BrE)
a slow gait of a horse in which at least two feet are on the ground at all times

Examples

Horse's walk
During the parade, riders kept their horses at a steady walk.
She asked the trainer about the difference between a walk and a trot.
The horse moved at a gentle walk along the trail.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: trot - a faster gait of a horse, between walk and canter
