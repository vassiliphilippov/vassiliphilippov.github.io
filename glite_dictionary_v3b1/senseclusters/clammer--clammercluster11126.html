clammer

verb
to demand loudly
8.4
Pronunciation: /ˈklæm.ər/ (AmE), /ˈklæ.mə/ (BrE)
to make a loud noise or demand something loudly and persistently.

Examples

To clammer for help
Protesters began to clammer for justice outside the courthouse.
The children would clammer for attention whenever their parents entered the room.
Reporters clammered for details after the surprising announcement.
