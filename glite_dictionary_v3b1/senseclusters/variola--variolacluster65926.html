variola

noun
viral disease; smallpox
8.1 8.8
Pronunciation: / vəˈraɪ.ələ / (AmE), / vəˈraɪ.ələ / (BrE)
a highly contagious, often fatal viral disease marked by fever and a distinctive skin eruption with pustules, also known as smallpox.

Examples

Variola virus
Historical records describe devastating outbreaks of variola across continents until the 20th century.
Patients infected with variola often displayed a widespread rash followed by the development of pustules.
The global eradication of variola was declared by the World Health Organization in 1980.
