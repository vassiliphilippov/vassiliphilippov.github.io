air

noun
one of the ancient four elements
1.0 8.6
Pronunciation: /er/ /; / /eər/ (AmE), /eə/ (BrE)
In ancient and medieval science, a substance once believed to be one of the four elements composing the universe, associated with the bodily humour blood.

Examples

The element air
Empedocles proposed that earth, water, fire, and air constituted all matter in the universe.
In ancient philosophy, air was considered one of the fundamental elements of the natural world.
Medieval physicians believed that an excess of air in the body contributed to a sanguine temperament.
