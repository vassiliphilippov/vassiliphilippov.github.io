wind up

verb
to coil a spring by turning a stem
Pronunciation: / waɪnd ʌp / (AmE), / waɪnd ʌp / (BrE)
to coil the spring of a mechanical device by turning a knob or stem, especially in order to make it operate

Examples

To wind up a watch
He forgot to wind up his watch, so it stopped overnight.
I always wind up my old alarm clock before going to bed.
You need to wind up the toy car to make it move.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rewind - to wind again, especially in the opposite direction or to reset to the start
