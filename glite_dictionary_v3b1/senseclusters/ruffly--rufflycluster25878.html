ruffly

adjective
slightly disordered
7.0 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆrÊŒfli / (AmE), / ËˆrÊŒfli / (BrE)
having a rumpled, creased, or slightly disordered appearance, as if ruffled.

Examples

Ruffly collar
After the long trip his collar was ruffly, and he smoothed it before the meeting.
She wore a ruffly dress that looked as if it had just come out of a suitcase.
The bedspread looked ruffly after the children jumped on it all morning.

Synonyms

  • rumpled
    4.2 1.52/m
    rumpled is creased and untidy like a shirt left in a pile, while ruffly suggests soft gathered edges.
  • crumply
    5.2 0.01/m
    crumply looks strongly wrinkled and crushed in texture, while ruffly is softer and shows small decorative folds.
  • crinkled
    3.7 0.03/m
    crinkled has many small, sharp wrinkles on the surface, while ruffly suggests loose, gathered edges or a slightly messy look.
  • tousled
    5.3 0.59/m
    tousled is hair or fabric made messy by touch or wind, while ruffly describes neat decorative gathers or slight rumples.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rumpled - creased or wrinkled in appearance
  • antonym: smooth - having an even, regular surface without wrinkles
