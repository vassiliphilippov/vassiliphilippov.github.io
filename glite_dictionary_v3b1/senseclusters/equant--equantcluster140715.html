equant

noun
equidimensional crystal
Pronunciation: / ˈiːkwənt / (AmE), / ˈiːkwənt / (BrE)
a crystal or grain that is roughly equal in length, width, and height, making it nearly equidimensional

Examples

An equant grain
The mineral sample contained an equant that was almost a perfect cube.
Geologists often describe grains as equant when their dimensions are nearly the same.
Under the microscope, she identified an equant whose faces were almost the same in every direction.

Related Terms

  • antonym: elongate - a crystal or grain that is much longer in one dimension
  • antonym: tabular - a crystal or grain that is flat and plate-like
