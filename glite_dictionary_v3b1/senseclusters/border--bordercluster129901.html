border

verb
share a boundary with
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈbɔrdɚ /; / ˈbɔrdər / (AmE), / ˈbɔːdə / (BrE)
to be next to, share a boundary with, or surround something

Examples

To border a country
Our property bordered a large forest when I was growing up.
The Czech Republic borders Germany, Austria, Slovakia, and Poland.
This park borders the river on the east side of the city.
A tall hedge borders the garden, providing both privacy and shade.
Dense forests bordered the old highway on both sides.
The country borders two major rivers in the region.
A wall of ancient pines bordered the old cemetery, encircling it in green.
The tall hedge borders the entire property, providing privacy from all sides.
Thick fog bordered the village last night, making it feel completely cut off.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adjoin - to be next to or joined with
  • synonym: encircle - to form a circle around; to surround
  • antonym: separate - to set or keep apart
  • synonym: surround - to be all around something
