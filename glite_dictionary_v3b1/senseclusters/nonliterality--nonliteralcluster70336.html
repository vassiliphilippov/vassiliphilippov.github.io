nonliterality

noun
figurative quality
9.5
Pronunciation: /nɑːn.lɪt.ə.ˈræl.ɪ.tɪ/ (AmE), /nɒn.lɪt.ə.ˈræl.ɪ.tɪ/ (BrE)
the quality of not being literal, often involving figurative or symbolic meaning instead of exact wording

Examples

Nonliterality of a phrase
The nonliterality of her speech made it difficult for some listeners to understand her true meaning.
Writers often use nonliterality to add depth and creativity to their stories.
Understanding the nonliterality in idioms is important when learning a new language.

Related Terms

  • synonym: figurativeness - the use of words or expressions with a meaning different from the literal interpretation
  • antonym: literalness - the quality of being literal or exact in meaning
