tumor

noun
abnormal mass of tissue
Pronunciation: / ˈtuːmɚ / (AmE), / ˈtjuːmə / (BrE)
A mass of abnormal tissue that forms when cells grow and divide more than they should or do not die when they should, serving no useful purpose.

Examples

A benign tumor
Benign tumors do not spread to other parts of the body.
Early detection of a tumor can improve the chances of successful treatment.
The doctor discovered a tumor in her lung during a routine checkup.
After surgery, the tumour was sent to the lab for analysis.
Early detection of a tumour can significantly improve a patient's chances of recovery.
The doctor discovered a benign tumour during her routine examination.
