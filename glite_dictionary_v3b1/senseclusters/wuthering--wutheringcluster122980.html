wuthering

adjective
stormy or turbulent
Pronunciation: / ˈwʌðərɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwʌðərɪŋ / (BrE)
describing something wild, stormy, or turbulent, especially in a literary context

Examples

A wuthering wind
The novel describes a wuthering landscape where the wind never seems to rest.
Her wuthering feelings made it hard for her to find peace after the argument.
A wuthering sky loomed over the moors, echoing the characters' inner turmoil.
The wuthering wind rattled the windows and made the old house creak all night.
On the moors, the wuthering gusts swept across the grass with a wild, haunting sound.
She wrapped her coat tightly as the wuthering weather battered the lonely hillside.
