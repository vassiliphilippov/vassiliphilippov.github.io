vasoactive

noun
blood vessel drug
Pronunciation: / ˌveɪzoʊˈæktɪv / (AmE), / ˌveɪsəʊˈæktɪv / (BrE)
a drug or substance that affects the diameter or tone of blood vessels, often used to control blood pressure.

Examples

A vasoactive injection
The doctor administered a vasoactive to help stabilize the patient's blood pressure.
In emergency situations, a vasoactive may be used to quickly improve circulation.
Nurses must monitor patients closely when a vasoactive is being infused.

Related Terms

  • synonym: vasoconstrictor - a drug that narrows blood vessels
  • synonym: vasodilator - a drug that widens blood vessels
