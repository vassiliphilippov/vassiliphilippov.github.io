euphemism

noun
mild substitute for offensive word
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈjuːfəˌmɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈjuːfəmɪzəm / (BrE)
A mild or indirect word or expression used instead of one that may be considered harsh, blunt, or offensive.

Examples

A euphemism for layoffs
Many companies use the euphemism 'downsizing' instead of 'layoffs'.
Politicians often rely on euphemism to make unpleasant truths sound more acceptable.
The phrase 'passed away' is a common euphemism for 'died'.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dysphemism - a derogatory or unpleasant term used instead of a pleasant or neutral one
