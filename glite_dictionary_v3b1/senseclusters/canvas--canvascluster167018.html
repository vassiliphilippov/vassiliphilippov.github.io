canvas

noun
the scope or background for a narrative or work
2.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkæn.vəs / (AmE), / ˈkæn.vəs / (BrE)
a setting or background that serves as the scope or context for a narrative, work of art, or dramatic presentation

Examples

A canvas of life
Her memoir paints a vivid canvas of life during the revolution.
The author uses the city as a canvas for exploring complex human emotions.
The film unfolds on the grand canvas of twentieth-century Europe.

Related Terms

  • synonym: backdrop - a background setting, especially in theater or narrative, against which action takes place
  • synonym: setting - the environment or context in which an event or story takes place
