ruling class

noun
social group in power
Pronunciation: / ˈruːlɪŋ klæs / (AmE), / ˈruːlɪŋ klɑːs / (BrE)
The social group that holds power and authority in a society, especially those controlling government or wealth.

Examples

The ruling class controls power
The ruling class often shapes the laws to suit its own interests.
The revolution aimed to overthrow the ruling class and establish a more equal society.
Throughout history, members of the ruling class have sometimes lost touch with the needs of ordinary citizens.

Related Terms

  • synonym: elite - a select group regarded as the best or most powerful
  • antonym: working class - the social group consisting of people who are employed for wages, especially in manual or industrial work
