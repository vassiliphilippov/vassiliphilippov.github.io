proxy war

noun
conflict caused by outside powers who do not directly fight
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑksi wɔr / (AmE), / ˈprɒksi wɔː / (BrE)
A war instigated by a major power that does not itself become directly involved.

Examples

A proxy war between countries
Analysts warned that the civil war could escalate into a proxy war between global superpowers.
Several regional conflicts in Africa have been described as proxy wars fueled by foreign interests.
The Vietnam conflict is often cited as a classic example of a proxy war during the Cold War era.

Related Terms

  • antonym: direct war - A war in which principal powers participate directly
