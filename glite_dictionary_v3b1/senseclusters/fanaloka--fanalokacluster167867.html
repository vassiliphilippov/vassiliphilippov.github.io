fanaloka

noun
a civet-like mammal of Madagascar
Pronunciation: / fəˈnɑːləˌkɑː / (AmE), / fəˈnæləkə / (BrE)
a small carnivorous mammal (Fossa fossana), also known as the Malagasy civet, found in Madagascar

Examples

The fanaloka animal
Scientists visited Madagascar to study the habits of the fanaloka in its natural habitat.
The fanaloka is mostly nocturnal and elusive, making it hard to spot in the wild.
Unlike larger predators, the fanaloka feeds primarily on small vertebrates and insects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: civet - a small, catlike carnivorous mammal of the family Viverridae
