unmortgage

verb
to remove mortgage
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈmɔːrɡɪdʒ / (AmE), / ʌnˈmɔːɡɪdʒ / (BrE)
to remove a mortgage or legal claim from property so it is no longer used as security for a loan

Examples

To unmortgage a house
After years of saving, they were finally able to unmortgage their home.
The couple celebrated when the bank agreed to unmortgage their property.
It took a decade of payments before he could unmortgage his apartment.
Once the loan was paid off, the bank agreed to unmortgage the house.
You must submit the final documents before the lender will unmortgage your property.
After years of payments, she was finally able to unmortgage her apartment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mortgage - to use property as security for a loan
  • synonym: redeem - to pay off a mortgage and regain full ownership
  • synonym: release - to set free from a legal claim or obligation
