precisionist

noun
modernist art movement
9.6
Pronunciation: / prɪˈsɪʒənɪst / (AmE), / prɪˈsɪʒənɪst / (BrE)
a member of an early 20th-century American art movement characterized by clean lines, precise forms, and industrial subjects

Examples

Precisionist painting style
The museum displayed several works by a famous precisionist from the 1920s.
As a precisionist, she focused on painting factories and cityscapes with sharp, clear lines.
Many art historians consider Charles Demuth a leading precisionist of his time.
