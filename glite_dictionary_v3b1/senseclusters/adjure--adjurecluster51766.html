adjure

verb
to urge or request solemnly
4.3 8.6
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈdʒʊr/ /; / /æ.ˈdʒʊr/ (AmE), /ə.ˈdʒʊə/ /; / /ə.ˈdʒɔː/ (BrE)
to urge or command someone very seriously or formally, often under oath or with strong emotion

Examples

To adjure someone seriously
He adjured them to remain calm in the face of danger.
I adjure you, do not let anger cloud your judgment.
The lawyer adjured the witness to tell the whole truth.
I adjure you, by all you hold dear, to tell the truth in this matter.
The witness was adjured to speak only what he knew to be true.
They adjured their leader to act in the name of justice and mercy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beseech - to ask someone for something earnestly or anxiously
  • antonym: command - to order someone authoritatively to do something
  • synonym: implore - to beg someone earnestly or desperately to do something
