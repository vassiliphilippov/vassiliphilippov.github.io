compression

noun
increase in density by pressing together
5.2
Pronunciation: / kəmˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / kəmˈprɛʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of pressing or squeezing something to make it smaller or denser

Examples

Air compression
Engineers study how the compression of gases affects performance.
Sound waves travel faster in a medium with high compression.
The compression of the material caused it to become much denser.
During the experiment, increasing compression caused the gas to become significantly denser.
File compression reduces the size of digital data for easier storage and transfer.
The doctor explained that the pain was due to nerve compression in her spine.
Doctors used an elastic bandage to provide compression and reduce swelling.
The compression of the gas caused it to heat up rapidly.
The engine works by the compression of air and fuel before ignition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compaction - the process of making something more compact by pressing
  • synonym: contraction - the process of becoming smaller or shorter
  • antonym: decompression - the process of reducing pressure or density
  • antonym: expansion - the process of becoming larger or more extensive
