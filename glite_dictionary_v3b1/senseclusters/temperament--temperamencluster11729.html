temperament

noun
musical adjustment of intervals for tuning
9.5 7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛmpɚəmənt / (AmE), / ˈtɛmpərəmənt / (BrE)
(Music) An adjustment of the regular musical intervals in tuning an instrument, especially a keyboard, so that the scale can be used to play in different keys.

Examples

Musical temperament
Debates about which temperament is best for harpsichord music have gone on for centuries.
Historical organs often use a different temperament compared to modern instruments.
The piano was tuned using a special temperament to allow it to play in any key.

Related Terms

  • synonym: equal temperament - a system of tuning in which the intervals are adjusted so that the octave is divided into equal steps; used in modern Western music
