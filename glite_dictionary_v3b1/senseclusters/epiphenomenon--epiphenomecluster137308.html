epiphenomenon

noun
secondary effect or by-product
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪfəˈnɑːmɪnɑn / (AmE), / ˌiːpɪfəˈnɒmɪnən / (BrE)
A secondary effect or by-product that occurs alongside or as a result of another phenomenon, but is not itself a direct cause.

Examples

An epiphenomenon of illness
Fatigue can be regarded as an epiphenomenon of chronic illness rather than a cause.
Some philosophers argue that consciousness is merely an epiphenomenon of brain processes.
The rise in sea levels was initially thought to be an epiphenomenon, but further study revealed its significance.
