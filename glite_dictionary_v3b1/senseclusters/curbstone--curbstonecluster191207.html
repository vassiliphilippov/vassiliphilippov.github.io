curbstone

noun
paving stone forming a curb
Pronunciation: / ˈkɝːbˌstoʊn / (AmE), / ˈkɜːbˌstəʊn /; / ˈkɜːbstəʊn / (BrE)
A paving stone that forms part of the edge of a sidewalk or street curb.

Examples

A curbstone edge
Each granite curbstone was carefully set in place by the workers.
He tripped on a loose curbstone while crossing the street.
The city replaced every broken curbstone along the main avenue.
She tripped on a loose kerbstone while crossing the road.
The car mounted the kerbstone and came to a stop on the pavement.
Workers replaced the damaged kerbstone along the busy street.
