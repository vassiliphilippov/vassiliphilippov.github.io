scented fern

noun
aromatic Eurasian perennial herb
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛntɪd fɜrn / (AmE), / ˈsɛntɪd fɜːn / (BrE)
A common perennial aromatic herb native to Eurasia, with yellow button-like flower heads and bitter-tasting pinnate leaves, sometimes used medicinally.

Examples

Medicinal scented fern
In traditional remedies, extracts of scented fern were once used to treat digestive issues.
Medicinal tea can be made from scented fern leaves despite their bitter taste.
The garden's border was lined with scented fern to repel insects naturally.
