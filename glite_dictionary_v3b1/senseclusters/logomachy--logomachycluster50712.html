logomachy

noun
argument about words or meanings
9.9
Pronunciation: / loʊˈɡɑːməki / (AmE), / ləʊˈɡɒməki / (BrE)
an argument or dispute about words or the meaning of words

Examples

A logomachy about words
Political discussions online often become a logomachy rather than a meaningful exchange of ideas.
The debate devolved into a pointless logomachy over the precise definition of 'art'.
They tired quickly of the logomachy, realizing they were arguing over semantics rather than substance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: semantics - the study of meaning in language
