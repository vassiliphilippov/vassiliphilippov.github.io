picket fence

noun
fence with upright slats
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪkɪt fɛns / (AmE), / ˈpɪkɪt fɛns / (BrE)
A fence consisting of a row of upright wooden or metal slats, often with spaces between them, typically used to mark boundaries around yards or gardens.

Examples

A picket fence around yard
Children ran along the picket fence chasing butterflies in the garden.
The house was surrounded by a classic picket fence, freshly painted white.
They decided to replace the old hedge with a new picket fence for a more traditional look.
