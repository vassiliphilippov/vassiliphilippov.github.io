neumatic

adjective
chant with few notes per syllable
9.9
Pronunciation: / nuˈmætɪk / (AmE), / njuːˈmætɪk / (BrE)
relating to a style of chant or melody in which each syllable is sung to a small group of notes, usually two to four

Examples

Neumatic chant style
The choir performed a neumatic chant, giving each syllable two or three notes.
In medieval music, neumatic melodies were common in liturgical settings.
Students learned to distinguish between neumatic and melismatic styles in their music history class.

Related Terms

  • antonym: melismatic - a style where a single syllable is sung to many notes
  • antonym: syllabic - a style where each syllable is sung to a single note
