wind

noun
breath; air in the lungs
Pronunciation: / wɪnd / (AmE), / wɪnd / (BrE)
the air that is taken into or expelled from the lungs, especially when referring to one's ability to breathe after exertion or a blow

Examples

Out of wind
After running up the stairs, I was out of wind and had to sit down.
She paused during her speech to catch her wind and continue.
The sudden punch to his stomach knocked the wind out of him.
