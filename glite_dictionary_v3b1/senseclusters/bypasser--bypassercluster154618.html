bypasser

noun
alternative route device
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪˌpæsər / (AmE), / ˈbaɪˌpɑːsə / (BrE)
a device or arrangement that provides an alternative route to divert flow or traffic around an obstacle

Examples

Water bypasser
The engineers installed a bypasser to keep the water flowing during repairs.
A temporary bypasser was used to redirect traffic around the construction site.
Without a bypasser, the system would have been completely shut down for maintenance.
