Meistersinger

noun
German poet-musician
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪtərˌsɪŋər / (AmE), / ˈmaɪtəˌsɪŋə / (BrE)
a member of a German guild of poets and musicians active from the 15th to 17th centuries

Examples

A medieval Meistersinger
The opera tells the story of a famous Meistersinger in medieval Nuremberg.
As a Meistersinger, he followed strict rules for composing and performing songs.
The tradition of the Meistersinger influenced later German music and poetry.

Related Terms

  • synonym: minnesinger - a German lyric poet and singer of the 12th to 14th centuries
