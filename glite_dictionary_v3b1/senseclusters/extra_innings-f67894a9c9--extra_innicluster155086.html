extra innings

noun
overtime play in baseball to break a tie
Pronunciation: / ˈɛkstrə ˈɪnɪŋz / (AmE), / ˈɛkstrə ˈɪnɪŋz / (BrE)
additional innings played to break a tie at the end of the regular nine innings in baseball, continuing until one team leads at the end of an inning.

Examples

Extra innings in baseball
After nine scoreless innings, the game went into extra innings to decide the winner.
Because of a tie, the match lasted much longer due to extra innings.
The fans were thrilled as their team pulled ahead during the tenth extra innings.
