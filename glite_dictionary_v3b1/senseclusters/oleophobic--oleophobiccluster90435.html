oleophobic

adjective
repelling or lacking affinity for oil

(of a material or surface) having little or no affinity for oils; tending to repel oils rather than absorb them.

Examples

Oleophobic surface
Laboratory gloves made of nitrile are often oleophobic, making them suitable for handling oily substances.
Lenses with an oleophobic surface are easier to clean because oil does not easily stick to them.
The new phone has an oleophobic coating that helps resist fingerprints and smudges.

Related Terms
