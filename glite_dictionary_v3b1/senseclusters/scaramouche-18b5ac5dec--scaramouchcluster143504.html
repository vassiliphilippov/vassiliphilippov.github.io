Scaramouche

noun
boastful cowardly commedia dell'arte character
Pronunciation: / ˌskærəˈmuːʃ / (AmE), / ˌskærəˈmuːʃ / (BrE)
a boastful but cowardly character in commedia dell'arte, often depicted as a comic figure who blusters and is easily frightened

Examples

The Scaramouche character
He dressed as Scaramouche for the masquerade ball, complete with a flamboyant costume.
In the play, the Scaramouche relies on bluff to get out of tricky situations.
The Scaramouche character was famous for his comic cowardice on stage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Harlequin - comic servant character in commedia dell'arte known for his colorful costume and agile movements
