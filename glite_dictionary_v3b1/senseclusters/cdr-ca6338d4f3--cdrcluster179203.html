CDR

noun
call record details
Pronunciation: / si di ɑr / (AmE), / siː diː ɑː / (BrE)
a record created by a telephone or mobile network that details information about a call or message, such as time, duration, and numbers involved

Examples

Phone CDR record
The police requested the CDR to track the suspect's phone activity.
Telecom companies store each CDR for billing and security purposes.
By analyzing the CDR, investigators could determine the time and duration of the calls.

Related Terms

  • synonym: billing record - a record used for charging customers for phone usage
  • synonym: call log - a list of incoming and outgoing phone calls
