trillium

noun
a genus of three-leaved, three-petaled flowering plants
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɪliəm / (AmE), / ˈtrɪlɪəm / (BrE)
A perennial plant of the genus Trillium, native to North America and Asia, with a whorl of three leaves at the top of the stem and a single three-petaled flower.

Examples

A trillium flower
Many gardeners prize trilliums for their unique three-petaled blossoms.
The white trillium is the official flower of Ontario, Canada.
We spotted a beautiful trillium blooming in the shade of the forest.
