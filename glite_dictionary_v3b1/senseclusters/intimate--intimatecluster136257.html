intimate

verb
suggest or imply indirectly
Pronunciation: / ˈɪn.tə.meɪt /; / ˈɪn.t̬ə.meɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.tɪ.meɪt / (BrE)
to suggest or imply something indirectly, without stating it directly

Examples

To intimate a plan
During the meeting, he intimated his intention to resign without saying it directly.
Her silence intimated her disagreement with the plan.
The report intimates that further action may be necessary.
He intimated that changes were coming but gave no details.
She intimated her resignation without saying it directly.
The principal intimated to the teachers that budget cuts were expected next year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: imply - to express indirectly; to suggest without stating
  • synonym: insinuate - to suggest or hint something bad in an indirect way
  • synonym: suggest - to mention as a possible cause or solution
