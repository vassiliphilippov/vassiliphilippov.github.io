Steinert's disease

noun
inherited muscular dystrophy affecting various muscles
9.6
Pronunciation: /ˈstaɪn.ərts dɪ.ˈziːz/ /; / /ˈstaɪn.ərts də.ˈziːz/ (AmE), /ˈstaɪn.əts dɪ.ˈziːz/ /; / /ˈstaɪn.əts də.ˈziːz/ (BrE)
a severe, inherited form of muscular dystrophy characterized by progressive muscle weakness and wasting, especially affecting the face, neck, hands, and feet, with slow progression and onset from birth to middle age

Examples

Steinert's disease symptoms
Speech difficulties and facial muscle wasting are common features of Steinert's disease.
Symptoms of Steinert's disease often begin in adolescence or early adulthood but can vary widely in severity.
The patient's progressive muscle weakness and stiff hands led doctors to diagnose him with Steinert's disease.

Related Terms

  • synonym: muscular dystrophy - a group of inherited diseases causing progressive muscle weakness
