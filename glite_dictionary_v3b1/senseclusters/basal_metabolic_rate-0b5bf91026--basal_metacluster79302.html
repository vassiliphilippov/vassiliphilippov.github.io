basal metabolic rate

noun
rate of energy expenditure at rest
9.4 4.0
Pronunciation: /ˈbeɪ.səl me.tə.ˈbɑː.lɪk reɪt/ /; / /ˈbeɪ.zəl me.tə.ˈbɑː.lɪk reɪt/ (AmE), /ˈbeɪ.səl me.tə.ˈbɒl.ɪk reɪt/ /; / /ˈbeɪ.zəl me.tə.ˈbɒl.ɪk reɪt/ (BrE)
the rate at which the body expends energy while at rest to maintain vital functions such as breathing and keeping warm

Examples

The basal metabolic rate
An individual’s basal metabolic rate can be affected by age, genetics, and body composition.
Doctors use the basal metabolic rate to help determine a patient’s daily caloric needs.
Improving muscle mass can increase your basal metabolic rate, helping you burn more calories at rest.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: caloric expenditure - the amount of calories used by the body for all activities
  • synonym: resting metabolic rate - the rate of energy expenditure while at rest, slightly different from BMR
