transparent

adjective
obvious or easy to understand
2.6 2.9
Pronunciation: /træn.ˈspær.ənt/ /; / /træns.ˈpær.ənt/ (AmE), /træn.ˈspær.ənt/ /; / /træns.ˈpær.ənt/ (BrE)
(of an action, statement, or process) easy to perceive or detect; obvious or readily understood, often because of a lack of subtlety or effort to conceal.

Examples

Transparent excuse
He gave a transparent excuse for being late, which nobody believed.
Her motives for volunteering were completely transparent to everyone in the room.
The company's attempts to hide the price increase were anything but transparent.
