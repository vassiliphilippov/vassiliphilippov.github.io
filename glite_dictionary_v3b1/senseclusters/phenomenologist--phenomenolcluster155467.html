phenomenologist

noun
student of phenomenology
Pronunciation: / fəˌnɑːməˈnɑːlədʒɪst / (AmE), / fəˌnɒmɪˈnɒlədʒɪst / (BrE)
a person who studies or specializes in phenomenology, focusing on how people experience and perceive things

Examples

A phenomenologist studies experience
The phenomenologist explored how individuals interpret their daily experiences.
As a phenomenologist, she focused on the subjective nature of perception.
The conference brought together every leading phenomenologist in the field of philosophy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: empiricist - a person who supports the theory that knowledge comes from experience
  • synonym: philosopher - a person engaged in the study of philosophy
