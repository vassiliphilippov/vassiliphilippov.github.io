relay

noun
fresh team of animals to relieve a tired one
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈriː.leɪ /; / ˈriːleɪ /; / ˈriˌleɪ /; / ˈriːˌleɪ / (AmE), / ˈriː.leɪ /; / ˈriːleɪ / (BrE)
a group or team (of people or animals) that takes over from another to continue a task, or the act of passing something along in turns

Examples

Relay team
A fresh relay of oxen pulled the wagon over the mountain pass.
The travelers arranged for a relay of horses at each station along the route.
Without a relay of mules, the journey would have taken much longer.
A new relay of nurses took over the ward at the end of the shift.
The night relay arrived promptly at midnight to continue construction on the bridge.
There was always a fresh relay of workers ready to replace the tired team.
Each swimmer in the relay must wait for their teammate to touch the wall before starting.
Our school's relay team won first place at the state championships.
The 4x100 meter relay is one of the most exciting events in track and field.

Related Terms

  • synonym: race - a competition of speed
  • synonym: transmission - the act of sending something from one person or place to another
  • antonym: withhold - to refuse to give something to someone
