white sheep

noun
a unique or virtuous person in a group
Pronunciation: / waɪt ʃiːp / (AmE), / waɪt ʃiːp / (BrE)
A person who differs from others in a group, especially by being virtuous, law-abiding, or distinctive in a positive way.

Examples

The white sheep of the family
Among his rebellious siblings, Mark was considered the white sheep of the family.
In a corrupt office, she was the white sheep who always did the right thing.
While his friends constantly broke rules, John stood out as the white sheep.

Related Terms

  • antonym: black sheep - a person who is considered a disgrace or outcast in a group, especially a family
