lavender

noun
aromatic shrub with pale purple flowers
1.5 1.4
Pronunciation: /ˈlæ.vən.dər/ (AmE), /ˈlæ.vən.də/ (BrE)
a small aromatic shrub or subshrub of the mint family, native to the Old World, with narrow leaves and pale purple or blue flowers, often grown for its fragrance or ornamental use.

Examples

Dried lavender flowers
Bundles of dried lavender hung from the beams in the old country kitchen.
She planted lavender along the garden path for its lovely scent.
The soothing aroma of lavender is often used in essential oils and perfumes.
