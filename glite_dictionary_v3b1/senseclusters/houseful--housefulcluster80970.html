houseful

noun
as many as a house can hold
Pronunciation: / ˈhaʊsˌfʊl / (AmE), / ˈhaʊsˌfʊl / (BrE)
the maximum number of people that can be accommodated in a house at one time; a full house

Examples

A houseful of guests
After the wedding, we had a houseful of relatives staying with us for the weekend.
The comedian performed in front of a houseful every night.
We cooked enough food to feed a houseful of hungry children.
