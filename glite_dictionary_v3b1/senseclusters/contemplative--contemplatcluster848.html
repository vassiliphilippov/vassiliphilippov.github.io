Synonyms

  • reflective
    4.5 7/m
    reflective shows careful thinking about past events or ideas, while contemplative focuses on calm, deep, often meditative thought.
  • meditative
    5.6 2/m
    meditative is quiet, calm thinking often like sitting for a long time, while contemplative is serious, thoughtful reflection.
  • musing
    6.1 0.00/m
    musing is a soft, wandering thought or short daydream, while contemplative is deeper, more focused serious thinking.
  • ruminative
    7.9 0.1/m
    ruminative repeats thoughts over and over, often worryingly, while contemplative is calm, steady, and not necessarily anxious.
  • introspective
    7.4 2/m
    introspective looks inward at personal feelings and motives, while contemplative is broader quiet thinking about many things.
  • thoughtful
    2.6 7/m
    thoughtful carefully considers others or ideas and can mean kind, while contemplative mainly means quiet, serious inward thought.
  • pensive
    7.0 1/m
    pensive is quiet and sad or serious in mood, while contemplative is calm, reflective thought without the strong sadness.
