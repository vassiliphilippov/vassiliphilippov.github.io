fundamental law

noun
basic law forming government framework
5.6 0.15/m
Pronunciation: / Àåf ånd…ôÀàm…õnt…ôl l…î / (AmE), / Àåf ånd…ôÀàm…õnt…ôl l…îÀê / (BrE)
a law or set of laws that establishes the basic principles and framework of a government; a constitution or constitutional rule

Examples

The fundamental law
Amendments to the fundamental law require a two-thirds majority in parliament.
Judges must interpret all legislation with respect to the fundamental law of the land.
The country's fundamental law guarantees freedom of speech and religion.

Synonyms

  • constitution
    5.1 33/m
    constitution is a written set of basic rules and rights that guides a country, while fundamental law names that constitutional framework.
  • organic law
    7.9 0.03/m
    organic law establishes the basic structure and powers of a government in legal texts, while fundamental law refers to the constitution.
  • law of the land
    4.7 1.71/m
    law of the land is the set of rules in force across a country, whereas fundamental law names the constitutional source of those rules.
