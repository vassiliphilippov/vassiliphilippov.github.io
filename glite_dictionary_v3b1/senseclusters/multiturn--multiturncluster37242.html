multiturn

noun
device with multiple rotations
8.5
Pronunciation: /ˈmʌl.tɪ.tɜːr/ /; / /ˈmʌl.tiː.tɜːr/ (AmE), /ˈmʌl.tɪ.tɜːn/ /; / /ˈmʌl.tiː.tɜːn/ (BrE)
a device, such as a potentiometer or rotary encoder, that requires several full rotations to adjust through its entire range

Examples

A multiturn device
The engineer installed a multiturn to allow for precise voltage adjustments in the circuit.
A multiturn potentiometer is often used when fine control is needed over a wide range.
He replaced the old knob with a multiturn for smoother and more accurate tuning.
