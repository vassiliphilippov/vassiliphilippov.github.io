underconsumption

noun
insufficient intake
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərkənˈsʌmpʃən / (AmE), / ˌʌndəkənˈsʌmpʃən / (BrE)
the act or state of consuming less than is needed, especially of food or essential nutrients, often leading to health problems

Examples

Underconsumption of nutrients
Prolonged underconsumption of protein can lead to serious health issues in children.
The report highlighted underconsumption of fruits and vegetables in the population.
Efforts to address underconsumption include nutrition education and food assistance programs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: malnutrition - a condition caused by not getting enough nutrients
  • antonym: overconsumption - the act of consuming more than is needed
