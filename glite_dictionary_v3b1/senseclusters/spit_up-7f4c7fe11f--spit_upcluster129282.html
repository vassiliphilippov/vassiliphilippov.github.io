spit up

verb
discharge phlegm or saliva from the mouth
2.3 3.4
Pronunciation: /spɪt ʌp/ /; / /spɪt əp/ (AmE), /spɪt ʌp/ /; / /spɪt əp/ (BrE)
to discharge phlegm, sputum, or saliva from the mouth, typically by forcefully expelling it from the lungs or throat

Examples

Babies spit up milk
Babies sometimes spit up milk after feeding, but adults may spit up phlegm when they are ill.
He started to spit up phlegm after a week of having a cold.
The doctor asked him how often he spits up mucus during the day.
