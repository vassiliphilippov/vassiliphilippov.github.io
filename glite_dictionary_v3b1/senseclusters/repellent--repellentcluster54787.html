repellent

adjective
resistant to absorption or mixing
4.7
Pronunciation: / rɪˈpɛl.ənt / (AmE), / rɪˈpel.ənt / (BrE)
Not allowing a particular substance to be absorbed or mixed; resistant to penetration by a liquid, gas, etc.

Examples

Repellent fabric
Her jacket has a repellent surface that resists stains and water.
The tent is made from a repellent material that keeps rain out.
These repellent coatings are especially useful for outdoor gear.

Related Terms

  • antonym: absorbent - able to soak up liquid easily
  • synonym: impermeable - not allowing fluid to pass through
