camelopard

noun
archaic term for giraffe
Pronunciation: / kəˈmɛləˌpɑrd / (AmE), / kəˈmiːlɵˌpɑːd / (BrE)
an archaic term for a giraffe, a tall African mammal with a very long neck, legs, and spotted coat

Examples

The camelopard animal
In antique bestiaries, the camelopard was often described as a mixture of camel and leopard.
The explorer was amazed to see a camelopard grazing peacefully among the acacia trees.
Victorian travelers sometimes called the giraffe a camelopard when writing about their African journeys.
