FFA

noun
shipping financial contract
Pronunciation: / ɛf ɛf eɪ / (AmE), / ɛf ɛf eɪ / (BrE)
an abbreviation for Forward Freight Agreement, a financial contract in shipping used to hedge or speculate on future freight rates

Examples

FFA contract
Many shipping companies use FFA contracts to protect themselves from unpredictable freight rate changes.
Traders speculated on future market trends by buying and selling FFA agreements.
The rise in global demand led to increased activity in the FFA market.

Related Terms

  • synonym: derivative - a financial contract whose value depends on an underlying asset
  • synonym: futures contract - an agreement to buy or sell an asset at a future date for a set price
