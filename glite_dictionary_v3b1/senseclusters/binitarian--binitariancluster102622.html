binitarian

noun
believer in twofold God
9.9
Pronunciation: /bɪ.nɪ.ˈter.iː.ən/ (AmE), /bɪ.nɪ.ˈteə.riː.ən/ (BrE)
a person who believes that God exists as two distinct persons or aspects, rather than three as in the Trinity

Examples

A binitarian believer
The early church debated whether a binitarian view of God was supported by scripture.
As a binitarian, he rejected the traditional doctrine of the Trinity.
Some theologians have written extensively about the differences between binitarian and trinitarian beliefs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: trinitarian - a person who believes in the doctrine of the Trinity
  • antonym: unitarian - a person who believes that God exists as one person
