sweet gale

noun
aromatic bog shrub with bitter leaves
7.3
Pronunciation: / swit ɡeɪl / (AmE), / swiːt ɡeɪl / (BrE)
a shrub of bogs in the north temperate zone, known for its fragrant, bitter-tasting leaves

Examples

Sweet gale leaves
Early settlers sometimes used sweet gale leaves to flavor their ales and repel insects.
She crushed a handful of sweet gale leaves to release their fragrant aroma.
The sweet gale grows abundantly in wet, marshy areas across northern Europe.
