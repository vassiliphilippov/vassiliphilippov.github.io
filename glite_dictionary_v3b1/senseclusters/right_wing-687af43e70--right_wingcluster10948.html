right wing

noun
conservative political group or ideology
1.6
Pronunciation: / ˈraɪt ˌwɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈraɪt ˌwɪŋ / (BrE)
the group of people who support conservative or traditional political, social, or economic policies, often favoring minimal change.

Examples

The right wing party
Tensions grew between the right wing and the left wing over economic reforms.
The new prime minister is a strong supporter of the right wing in parliament.
The party's right wing opposed the proposed changes to immigration policy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: left wing - those who support progressive or liberal political, social, or economic policies
