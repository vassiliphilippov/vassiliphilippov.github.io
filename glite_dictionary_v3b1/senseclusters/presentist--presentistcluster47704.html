presentist

noun
theologian believing Apocalypse prophecies are now fulfilled
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛzəntɪst / (AmE), / ˈprɛzəntɪst / (BrE)
a theologian who believes that the prophecies in the Book of Revelation are being fulfilled in the present time

Examples

A presentist theologian
As a presentist, she interprets world events as direct fulfillments of biblical prophecy.
Some scholars consider Hal Lindsey a notable presentist in modern Christian thought.
The debate between futurists and presentists has long shaped interpretations of Revelation.
