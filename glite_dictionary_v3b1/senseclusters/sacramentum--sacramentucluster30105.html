sacramentum

noun
solemn Roman oath
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsækrəˈmɛntəm / (AmE), / ˌsakrəˈmɛntʊm / (BrE)
a solemn oath or pledge, especially one made in ancient Roman law or military service

Examples

Roman sacramentum
Before joining the legion, each recruit swore a sacramentum to the Roman state.
The sacramentum was considered unbreakable and central to Roman military discipline.
Breaking a sacramentum could result in severe punishment in ancient Rome.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oath - a formal promise to do something or tell the truth
  • synonym: pledge - a serious or formal promise or agreement
