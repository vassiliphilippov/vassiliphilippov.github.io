joblessness

noun
state of unemployment
3.4 0.62/m
Pronunciation: / Ààd í…ëÀêbl…ôsn…ôs / (AmE), / Ààd í…íbl…ôsn…ôs / (BrE)
the state of not having paid employment; unemployment

Examples

High joblessness rate
The government introduced new programs to reduce joblessness in rural areas.
Periods of high joblessness can lead to increased poverty and social problems.
After the factory closed, the town faced widespread joblessness for several years.

Synonyms

  • unemployment
    2.3 44/m
    unemployment is used for people without paid jobs who often seek work, while joblessness names the general state of no employment.
  • worklessness
    6.8 0.00/m
    worklessness means having no work or paid job, often in speech or reports, while joblessness focuses on not holding paid employment.
  • nonemployment
    5.3 0.02/m
    nonemployment is a formal statistical term for not being in paid work, while joblessness is the common word for lacking employment.
