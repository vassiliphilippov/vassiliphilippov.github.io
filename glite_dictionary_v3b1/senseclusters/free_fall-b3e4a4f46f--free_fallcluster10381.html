free fall

noun
sudden sharp decrease (esp. financial)
4.6 0.40/m
Pronunciation: / fri f…îl / (AmE), / friÀê f…îÀêl / (BrE)
A sudden and sharp decrease in the value or amount of something, especially in financial or economic contexts.

Examples

Stock free fall
After the bad earnings report, the company's stock went into free fall and lost 30% in one day.
Oil prices are in free fall due to oversupply and decreased demand.
The athlete's reputation went into free fall following the scandal.

Synonyms

  • nosedive
    4.9 0.42/m
    a nosedive is a sudden, very steep fall in value or amount, while free fall describes a sudden sharp decline in value.
  • crash
    4.8 4.81/m
    a crash is a sudden severe collapse of prices or systems, while free fall is a sharp decrease in value, often in markets.
  • drop
    2.5 15/m
    a drop is a sudden significant fall in amount or level, while free fall stresses a sharp, often rapid decline in value.
  • drop-off
    3.9 0.82/m
    a drop-off is a noticeable decrease in amount, rate, or quality, while free fall implies a sudden sharp fall in value.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plunge - a rapid and steep fall in value or amount
  • antonym: rebound - an increase in value after a decline
