trespass

verb
commit a sin; violate moral or divine law
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɛsˌpæs / (AmE), / ˈtrɛspəs / (BrE)
(formal or literary) To commit a sin or wrongdoing, especially by violating a moral or divine law.

Examples

To trespass against rules
He feared he might trespass against his own deeply held beliefs.
She prayed for forgiveness, knowing she had trespassed in thought and deed.
They asked the congregation not to trespass against the teachings of their faith.

Related Terms

  • antonym: obey - to comply with or follow rules or laws
  • synonym: offend - to violate a law, rule, or code of conduct
  • synonym: sin - an act regarded as a transgression against divine law
