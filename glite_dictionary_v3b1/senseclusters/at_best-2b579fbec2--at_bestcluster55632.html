at best

adverb
in the most favorable scenario
5.5
Pronunciation: / ət ˈbɛst /; / æt ˈbɛst /; / æt bɛst / (AmE), / ət ˈbɛst /; / æt bɛst / (BrE)
used to show that even in the most favorable or optimistic situation, the result is not very good

Examples

At best only break even
At best, the plan will only break even, not turn a profit.
She will, at best, recover partially from the injury.
This solution is, at best, a temporary fix to a much deeper problem.
At best, we can hope for a partial recovery this year.
His explanation was unclear at best, if not completely misleading.
This solution is temporary at best and may not last long.
Her chances of winning the competition are slim at best.
His explanation was unconvincing at best, and completely misleading at worst.
The weather will be uncomfortable at best during the hike this weekend.
