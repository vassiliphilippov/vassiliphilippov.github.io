intellectualist

noun
person valuing intellect
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntəˈlɛktʃuəlɪst / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈlɛktʃuəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes that intellect or reason is more important than emotions, especially in understanding, decision-making, or moral questions

Examples

A logical intellectualist
As an intellectualist, she often relied on logic rather than feelings when making decisions.
The debate featured an intellectualist who argued that reason should always come first.
Some critics say that being an intellectualist can make a person seem distant or unemotional.
The philosopher was known as an intellectualist who valued reason above all in ethical debates.
As an intellectualist, she argued that understanding moral principles leads to virtuous actions.
Many ancient thinkers were considered intellectualists because they believed knowledge determined moral behavior.

Related Terms

  • antonym: emotionalist - a person who values emotions over reason
  • antonym: empiricist - a person who believes that knowledge comes primarily from sensory experience
  • synonym: rationalist - a person who emphasizes reason as the main source of knowledge
