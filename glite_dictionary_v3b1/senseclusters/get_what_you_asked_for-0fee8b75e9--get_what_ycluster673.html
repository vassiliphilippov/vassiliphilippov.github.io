get what you asked for

verb
receive the consequences of one's request
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt wʌt ju æskt fɔr / (AmE), / ɡɛt wɒt juː ɑːskt fə / (BrE)
to receive, often unexpectedly or undesirably, the consequences or results of one's own demands or requests

Examples

To get what you asked for at work
She wanted responsibility at work, and she soon got what she asked for.
Sometimes it's best not to wish for change because you might just get what you asked for.
When you insist on making all the decisions, don't complain when you get what you asked for.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reap what you sow - to experience the consequences of one's actions
