LPM

noun
licensed midwife
7.3
Pronunciation: / ɛl pi ɛm / (AmE), / ɛl piː ɛm / (BrE)
a licensed professional midwife, a person trained and legally authorized to provide midwifery care

Examples

Certified LPM
After passing her exams, she became an LPM and started working in a community clinic.
The hospital hired an LPM to support natural births and provide prenatal care.
Many families in rural areas rely on an LPM for safe and professional childbirth assistance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: midwife - a person trained to assist women in childbirth
  • antonym: obstetrician - a doctor specializing in pregnancy and childbirth
