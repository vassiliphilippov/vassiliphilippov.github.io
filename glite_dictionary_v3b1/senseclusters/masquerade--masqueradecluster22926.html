masquerade

verb
to participate in a masquerade or disguise oneself
Pronunciation: / ˌmæskəˈreɪd / (AmE), / ˌmæskəˈreɪd / (BrE)
to take part in a masquerade or disguise oneself by wearing a mask or costume

Examples

To masquerade in costume
Every year, locals masquerade at the town’s elaborate winter festival.
He loves to masquerade as a famous actor at costume parties.
The criminal masqueraded as a police officer to avoid suspicion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disguise - to give a new appearance to a person or thing to conceal its true form
