transiency

noun
the state of being temporary or short-lived
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈtrænziənsi / (AmE), / ˈtrænziənsi / (BrE)
The state or quality of being temporary or short-lived, often with the implication that ending or dying is inevitable.

Examples

The transiency of youth
Many poets write about the transiency of youth and beauty.
She found a strange comfort in the transiency of grief, knowing it would eventually fade.
The cherry blossoms are a symbol of the transiency of life in Japanese culture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: impermanence - the state of not lasting forever; temporary nature
  • antonym: permanence - the state or quality of lasting forever or remaining unchanged
