draft evader

noun
person who illegally avoids military draft
1.6
Pronunciation: / dræft ɪˈveɪdər / (AmE), / drɑːft ɪˈveɪdə / (BrE)
a person who illegally avoids or refuses compulsory military service when drafted

Examples

Draft evaders avoid army
During the Vietnam War, some Americans became draft evaders to avoid compulsory military service.
He was labeled a draft evader after fleeing to another country during the conflict.
The government imposed penalties on any draft evader caught by authorities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conscientious objector - a person who refuses military service on moral or religious grounds
