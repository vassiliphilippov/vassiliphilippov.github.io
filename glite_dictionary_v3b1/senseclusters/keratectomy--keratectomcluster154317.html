keratectomy

noun
corneal surgery
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˌkɛrəˈtɛktəmi / (AmE), / ˌkɛrəˈtɛktəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which part of the cornea is removed to improve vision or treat disease

Examples

A keratectomy surgery
After the keratectomy, the patient's vision gradually improved over several weeks.
A keratectomy may be recommended for people with certain corneal diseases.
The surgeon explained the risks and benefits of keratectomy before the procedure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: keratoplasty - surgical repair or transplantation of the cornea
