strontium-90

noun
radioactive isotope of strontium (mass number 90)
Pronunciation: / ˌstrɑːnʃiəm ˈnaɪnti / (AmE), / ˌstrɒn.ti.əm ˈnaɪnti / (BrE)
a radioactive isotope of strontium, with mass number 90, found in the fallout from nuclear explosions and assimilated into bones like calcium

Examples

strontium-90 isotope
Exposure to strontium-90 is especially dangerous because the isotope can accumulate in human bones.
Researchers measured the levels of strontium-90 in the soil after the nuclear accident.
The presence of strontium-90 in milk led to widespread health concerns during nuclear testing.
