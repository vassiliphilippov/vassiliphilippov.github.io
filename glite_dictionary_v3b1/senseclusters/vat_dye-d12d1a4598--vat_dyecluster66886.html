vat dye

noun
water-insoluble dye used in textile dyeing
3.6 6.3
Pronunciation: / væt daɪ / (AmE), / væt daɪ / (BrE)
a water-insoluble dye applied to fabric by reducing it to a soluble form in alkaline solution, then regenerating its insoluble state by oxidation, commonly used for dyeing cotton

Examples

A vat dye fabric
Because of its washfastness, a vat dye is often preferred for outdoor fabrics.
Indigo is a classic example of a vat dye used to color denim jeans.
To ensure deep color penetration, the textile was treated with a vat dye in an alkaline solution.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: acid dye - a dye applied using acidic conditions, mainly for wool, silk, or nylon
  • antonym: direct dye - a dye that can be applied directly to fabric without mordanting
