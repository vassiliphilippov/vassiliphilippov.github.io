international law

noun
laws governing relations between nations
1.0 3.3
Pronunciation: /ɪn.tər.ˈnæ.ʃə.nəl lɑː/ /; / /ɪn.tər.ˈnæ.ʃə.nəl lɔː/ (AmE), /ɪn.tə.ˈnæ.ʃə.nəl lɔː/ (BrE)
the system of rules and principles that govern the relations and conduct of nations with each other

Examples

International law rules
All countries are expected to follow international law in their dealings with others.
Many humanitarian issues are addressed only through international law.
The court ruled that the treaty was a violation of international law.
All states are expected to respect international law when engaging in negotiations.
Many disputes between countries are settled under international law rather than through conflict.
The treaty was in violation of international law according to several legal experts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: domestic law - The law that governs the internal affairs of a country
  • degree lower: private international law - Legal rules determining which jurisdiction's law applies in cross-border legal disputes
  • synonym: public international law - The body of law governing relations between states and other international entities
