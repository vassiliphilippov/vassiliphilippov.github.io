camelina

noun
a plant genus of oilseed herbs
Pronunciation: / ˌkæməˈliːnə / (AmE), / ˌkæmɛˈliːnə / (BrE)
A genus of annual or biennial herbs of the mustard family, native to the Mediterranean and central Asia, some species of which are cultivated for their oil-rich seeds.

Examples

Camelina oil seeds
Camelina is considered a promising biofuel crop due to its hardiness and oil content.
Many farmers have started growing camelina for its valuable seed oil.
The flowers of camelina are small and yellow, typical of the mustard family.
