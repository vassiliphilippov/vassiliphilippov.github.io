diabolize

verb
make devilish; turn into a devil
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪəbəˌlaɪz /; / daɪˈɑːbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.ə.bə.laɪz /; / daɪˈɒbəlaɪz / (BrE)
to turn into a devil or make devilish in character; to portray as evil or demon-like

Examples

To diabolize a leader
Centuries of conflict seemed to diabolize their leaders in the history books.
Some critics argue that media coverage can diabolize certain groups unjustly.
The villain's actions began to diabolize his reputation among the townspeople.
She accused the media of diabolising her actions during the trial.
The novel's villain is thoroughly diabolised by the author's vivid descriptions.
War can diabolise even the most compassionate individuals over time.
