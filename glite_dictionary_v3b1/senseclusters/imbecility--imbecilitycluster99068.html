imbecility

noun
state of significantly low intelligence (offensive, outdated)
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪm.bə.ˈsɪl.ɪ.tɪ/ /; / /ɪm.bɪ.ˈsɪl.ɪ.tɪ/ (AmE), /ɪm.bɪ.ˈsɪl.ɪ.tɪ/ /; / /ɪm.bə.ˈsɪl.ɪ.tɪ/ (BrE)
a state or condition of having a significantly below-average intelligence or mental capacity, historically used as a medical classification between 'moron' and 'idiot'; now offensive and no longer used in medicine

Examples

Medical imbecility
His actions displayed a level of imbecility that surprised even his critics.
Historical texts often classify mental disabilities under terms like imbecility, moron, or idiot.
The term imbecility is no longer used in modern psychology due to its offensive nature.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: moron - a person with mild intellectual disability (outdated, offensive)
