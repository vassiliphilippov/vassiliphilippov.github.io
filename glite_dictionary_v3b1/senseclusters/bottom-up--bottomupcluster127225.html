bottom-up

adjective
starting from details and building up
Pronunciation: / ˌbɑːtəmˈʌp / (AmE), / ˌbɒtəmˈʌp / (BrE)
(of a method or approach) starting at the most basic or detailed level and progressing to a more general or abstract level.

Examples

Bottom-up approach
In computer science, a bottom-up parser analyzes the smallest units before constructing the overall structure.
Learning a language can benefit from bottom-up techniques that focus on mastering individual sounds first.
The company's new strategy is a bottom-up approach that values employee input in decision-making.

Related Terms

  • antonym: top-down - (of a method or approach) starting from the highest or most general level and working down to details
