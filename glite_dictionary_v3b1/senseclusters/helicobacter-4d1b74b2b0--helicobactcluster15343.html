Helicobacter

noun
stomach-infecting bacteria
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛlɪkoʊˈbæktər / (AmE), / ˌhɛlɪkəʊˈbæktə / (BrE)
a genus of bacteria, especially H. pylori, that can infect the stomach and cause digestive problems

Examples

Helicobacter infection
The doctor explained that Helicobacter infection can lead to stomach ulcers if left untreated.
Researchers are studying how Helicobacter survives in the acidic environment of the stomach.
A test for Helicobacter is often recommended for patients with chronic digestive discomfort.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bacterium - a single-celled microorganism
  • antonym: virus - a microscopic infectious agent that replicates only inside living cells
