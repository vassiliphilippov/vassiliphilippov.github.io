return to roost

verb
come back to a resting place (birds)
Pronunciation: / rɪˈtɜrn tə ruːst / (AmE), / rɪˈtɜːn tə ruːst / (BrE)
(Of birds) to come back to their resting place to sleep after being away.

Examples

Birds return to roost
As dusk fell, the chickens returned to roost inside their coop.
Dozens of crows return to roost in the same spot near the river each night.
Every evening, the sparrows return to roost in the old oak tree.
