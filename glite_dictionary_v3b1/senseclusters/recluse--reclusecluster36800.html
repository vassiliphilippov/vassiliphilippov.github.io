recluse

noun
person who lives in solitude
7.8 1.07/m
Pronunciation: / r…™ÀàkluÀês / (AmE), / r…™ÀàkluÀês / (BrE)
a person who lives alone and avoids other people; someone who withdraws from society

Examples

A recluse writer
After losing his family, the author became a recluse and rarely spoke to anyone.
She admired the peaceful lifestyle of a recluse, far from the noise of society.
The recluse lived on the edge of town, surrounded by woods and wildlife.

Synonyms

  • hermit
    6.1 2.10/m
    a hermit lives alone, often for religious or quiet reflection, while a recluse withdraws from society to avoid other people.
  • solitary
    7.2 0.03/m
    a solitary person lives alone and tends to avoid company, while a recluse cuts off most social contact and public life.
  • loner
    4.2 2.71/m
    a loner prefers to be alone and may avoid others for comfort, while a recluse withdraws from society more deeply and deliberately.
