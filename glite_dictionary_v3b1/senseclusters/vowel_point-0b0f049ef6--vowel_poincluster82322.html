vowel point

noun
diacritical mark for vowel sounds
Pronunciation: / ˈvaʊəl pɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈvaʊəl pɔɪnt / (BrE)
a mark added to a consonant letter, especially in scripts like Hebrew or Arabic, to indicate a vowel sound

Examples

A vowel point mark
In Hebrew, each vowel point represents a specific vocalization for the consonant.
Linguists study vowel points to understand how ancient texts were pronounced.
The Arabic script uses vowel points to clarify pronunciation, especially for learners.
