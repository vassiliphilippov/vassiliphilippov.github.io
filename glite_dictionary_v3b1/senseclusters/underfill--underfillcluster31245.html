underfill

noun
chip gap filler
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈʌndərˌfɪl / (AmE), / ˈʌndəˌfɪl / (BrE)
a polymer or epoxy material applied beneath an electronic chip to fill gaps and protect solder joints

Examples

Chip underfill material
The engineer selected a new underfill to improve the reliability of the microchip assembly.
Proper application of underfill can prevent damage from thermal expansion in electronic devices.
After placing the chip, the technician injected underfill to secure the solder joints.

Related Terms

  • synonym: encapsulant - a material used to cover and protect electronic components
