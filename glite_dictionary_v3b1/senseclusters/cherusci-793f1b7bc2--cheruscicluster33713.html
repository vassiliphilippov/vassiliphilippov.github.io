Cherusci

proper noun
ancient Germanic tribe
9.9
Pronunciation: / kəˈruːski / (AmE), / kəˈruːski / (BrE)
an ancient Germanic tribe known for resisting Roman expansion in northern Europe

Examples

The Cherusci tribe
The Cherusci were famous for their victory against the Romans in the Teutoburg Forest.
Historians study the Cherusci to understand early Germanic resistance to Roman rule.
Arminius, a leader of the Cherusci, became a symbol of Germanic independence.
