derivatively

adverb
in an unoriginal way
Pronunciation: / dɪˈrɪvətɪvli / (AmE), / dɪˈrɪvətɪvli / (BrE)
in a way that is based on or copied from something else rather than original

Examples

Made derivatively from others
The movie was criticized for being made derivatively from popular blockbusters.
She wrote her essay derivatively, relying heavily on other authors' ideas.
The band's new album sounds derivatively similar to their earlier work.
