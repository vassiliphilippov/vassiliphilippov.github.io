re-uptake

noun
absorption of a substance again, esp. neurotransmitters
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈriːˌʌp.teɪk / (AmE), / ˈriːˌʌp.teɪk / (BrE)
the process by which a substance, especially a neurotransmitter, is absorbed again by the cell or tissue that released it, often reducing its activity in the body

Examples

The re-uptake of serotonin
Many antidepressants work by inhibiting the re-uptake of serotonin in the brain.
Scientists are investigating how re-uptake mechanisms influence mood disorders.
The efficiency of dopamine re-uptake affects overall neural activity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: uptake - the process of taking in or absorbing a substance
