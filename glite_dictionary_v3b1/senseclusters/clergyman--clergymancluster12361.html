clergyman

noun
male member of the Christian clergy
Pronunciation: / ˈklɝːdʒiˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈklɜːdʒiˌmæn / (BrE)
a male member of the clergy, especially in the Christian Church, who performs religious duties and provides spiritual guidance

Examples

A clergyman in church
As a respected clergyman, he was often called upon to counsel parishioners in need.
The clergyman delivered an inspiring sermon to the congregation on Sunday morning.
The village clergyman visited the sick and offered prayers for the community.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clergywoman - female member of the Christian clergy
  • synonym: minister - a member of the clergy, especially a Protestant minister
  • synonym: priest - an ordained minister in the Christian Church
