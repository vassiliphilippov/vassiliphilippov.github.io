decelerate

verb
reduce speed; slow down
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsɛləˌreɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈsɛləreɪt /; / dɪˈsɛləˌreɪt / (BrE)
to reduce speed; to move or make something move more slowly

Examples

To decelerate a vehicle
Drivers should decelerate as they approach the school zone.
Suddenly, the plane started decelerating before landing.
The train began to decelerate after leaving the tunnel.
Pilots must decelerate the aircraft before landing.
The car began to decelerate as it approached the traffic signal.
When cycling downhill, he had to decelerate to avoid going too fast.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accelerate - to increase speed; move more quickly
  • synonym: slow down - to reduce speed
