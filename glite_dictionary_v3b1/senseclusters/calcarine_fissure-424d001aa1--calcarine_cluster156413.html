calcarine fissure

noun
groove on the occipital lobe in the brain
7.9
Pronunciation: / kælˈkɛr.iːn ˈfɪʃ.ər / (AmE), / kælˈkæ.rɪn ˈfɪʃ.ə / (BrE)
a deep groove on the medial surface of the occipital lobe in the brain, marking the location of the primary visual cortex

Examples

The calcarine fissure in brain
Brain scans often reveal the calcarine fissure as a prominent feature of the occipital lobe.
Damage near the calcarine fissure can result in partial loss of vision.
The primary visual cortex is located along the calcarine fissure of the occipital lobe.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gyri - the ridges between the grooves of the brain
  • synonym: sulcus - a groove or furrow, especially one on the surface of the brain
