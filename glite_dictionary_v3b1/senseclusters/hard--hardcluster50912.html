hard

adjective
strict, unsentimental, dispassionate
1.1 362/m
Pronunciation: / h…ërd / (AmE), / h…ëÀêd / (BrE)
needing a lot of effort, skill, or endurance; not easy to do or deal with; harsh or strict in attitude or situation

Examples

Hard work
As a hard negotiator, she rarely let emotions influence her dealings.
He took a hard look at the evidence before making his decision.
The prosecutor was known for her hard approach to cross-examination in court.
It's hard to believe that it's already December.
She found it hard to explain her feelings to her parents.
This math test was much harder than I expected.

Training for the marathon proved to be a hard challenge, both physically and mentally.
