inoculate

verb
instill an idea or attitude into the mind
9.6 5.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˈnɑːkjəˌleɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈnɒkjʊleɪt / (BrE)
to introduce an idea or attitude into someone's mind, especially to prevent the influence of opposing ideas

Examples

To inoculate a mind
Her father tried to inoculate her against prejudice from an early age.
Political leaders often attempt to inoculate the public with their core values.
The professor hoped to inoculate his students with a love of free inquiry.

Related Terms

  • synonym: indoctrinate - to teach a person or group to accept a set of beliefs uncritically
  • synonym: instill - to gradually impart or introduce an idea or feeling into someone's mind
