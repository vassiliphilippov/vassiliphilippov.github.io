cervical smear

noun
test using cervical cell sample
Pronunciation: / ˈsɝː.vɪ.kəl smɪr / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.vɪ.kəl smɪə / (BrE)
a medical test in which a sample of cells is taken from the cervix to check for abnormal or precancerous changes

Examples

A cervical smear test
Guidelines suggest that a cervical smear should be performed every three years for women over 25.
Many women find the cervical smear procedure slightly uncomfortable but vital for early detection.
The doctor recommended a cervical smear as part of her annual health checkup.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Pap smear - a similar screening test for cervical cancer
  • degree higher: biopsy - removal of tissue for diagnostic examination
