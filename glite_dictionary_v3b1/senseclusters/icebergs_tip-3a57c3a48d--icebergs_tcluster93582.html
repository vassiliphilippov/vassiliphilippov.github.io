iceberg's tip

noun
visible part of a larger problem
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪsˌbɝɡz tɪp / (AmE), / ˈaɪsbɜːɡz tɪp / (BrE)
A small, easily observed part of a much larger and hidden problem or situation.

Examples

The iceberg's tip visible
Scientists warn that visible symptoms are the iceberg's tip of the disease's true impact.
The complaints are just the iceberg's tip; many more issues remain unreported.
What we discovered was only the iceberg's tip of corruption within the company.
