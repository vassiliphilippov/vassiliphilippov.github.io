ironclad alibi

noun
unquestionable excuse or evidence
Pronunciation: / ˈaɪərnˌklæd ˈæləˌbaɪ / (AmE), / ˈaɪənˌklad ˈælɪˌbaɪ / (BrE)
an excuse or evidence that is so strong and convincing that it cannot be doubted or disproved

Examples

An ironclad alibi
His ironclad alibi was supported by multiple witnesses and security camera footage.
The detective admitted the suspect's ironclad alibi left no room for doubt.
With an ironclad alibi, she was quickly cleared of all accusations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alibi - an excuse or piece of evidence that proves someone was elsewhere when a crime occurred
