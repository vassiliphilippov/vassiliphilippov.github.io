alicyclic compound

noun
aliphatic ring-containing compound
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˌælɪˈsaɪklɪk kəmˈpaʊnd / (AmE), / ˌælɪˈsɪklɪk kəmˈpaʊnd / (BrE)
a chemical compound that is aliphatic but contains a ring of carbon atoms in its structure

Examples

An alicyclic compound ring
Cyclohexane is a classic example of an alicyclic compound commonly studied in organic chemistry.
The laboratory synthesized several new alicyclic compounds for pharmaceutical testing.
Unlike aromatic compounds, an alicyclic compound does not contain delocalized electrons in its ring.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aromatic compound - a compound with a ring structure and delocalized electrons, like benzene
  • synonym: cyclic compound - any compound that contains a ring of atoms
