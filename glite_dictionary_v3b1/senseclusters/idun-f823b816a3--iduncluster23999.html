Idun

proper noun
Norse goddess of youth and spring
9.9 0.11/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàiÀêÀåduÀên / (AmE), / ÀàiÀêÀåduÀên / (BrE)
In Norse mythology, the goddess of spring and youth, wife of Bragi, who guarded the apples granting eternal youth to the gods.

Examples

Idun goddess of youth
In the old tales, Idun is celebrated as the keeper of the golden apples.
Stories of Idun and her apples highlight the importance of renewal in Norse mythology.
The Norse gods depended on Idun for their eternal youth and vitality.
