Lear

proper noun
tragic Shakespearean king
8.9
Pronunciation: / lɪr / (AmE), / lɪə / (BrE)
the king and central character in William Shakespeare's tragedy 'King Lear,' who is betrayed by two of his daughters.

Examples

Lear the king
Many actors consider portraying Lear to be the ultimate test of their skills.
Scholars often debate Lear's motivations and his descent into madness.
The tragedy of Lear unfolds as he divides his kingdom between his daughters.
