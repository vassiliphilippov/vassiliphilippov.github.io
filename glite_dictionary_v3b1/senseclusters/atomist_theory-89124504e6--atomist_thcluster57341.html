noun

A theory proposing that all matter is made up of tiny, indivisible, and indestructible particles called atoms.

Examples

The atomist theory of matter
Many philosophers in ancient Greece supported the atomist theory to explain the nature of the universe.
Modern science has greatly expanded upon the original atomist theory proposed by early philosophers.
The atomist theory faced opposition from those who believed matter was continuous rather than made of particles.

Synonyms

  • atomistic theory
    8.6 0.00/m
    atomistic theory appears in chemistry and philosophy to say matter is made of tiny indivisible particles, while atomist theory states that idea.
  • atomism
    9.8 0.09/m
    atomism names the doctrine that matter is made of tiny indivisible particles, while atomist theory describes that same idea as a formal theory.
  • atomic theory
    3.6 0.06/m
    atomic theory is a scientific account of matter using atoms, while atomist theory stresses atoms as indivisible and indestructible particles.
