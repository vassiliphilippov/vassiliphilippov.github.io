deputy sheriff

noun
law officer serving as sheriff's deputy
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛpjəti ˈʃɛɹɪf / (AmE), / ˈdɛpjʊti ˈʃɛɹɪf / (BrE)
a law officer who is authorized to act as the sheriff's substitute, especially in emergencies or when the sheriff is absent

Examples

Deputy sheriff on duty
As a deputy sheriff, she often had to fill in when the sheriff was called away.
Several deputy sheriffs assisted in organizing the search for the missing child.
The deputy sheriff arrived quickly to handle the emergency at the courthouse.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: constable - a lower-ranking law enforcement officer
  • degree higher: sheriff - the principal law officer in a county
