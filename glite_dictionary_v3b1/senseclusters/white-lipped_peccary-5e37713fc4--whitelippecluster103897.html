white-lipped peccary

noun
a large peccary with whitish cheeks
Pronunciation: / waɪt lɪpt ˈpɛkəri / (AmE), / waɪt lɪpt ˈpɛkəri / (BrE)
a large wild piglike mammal of Central and South America with blackish fur and whitish cheeks, larger than the collared peccary

Examples

The white-lipped peccary animal
Researchers tracked the migration patterns of the white-lipped peccary throughout the national park.
The rainforest is home to the elusive white-lipped peccary, often seen moving in large herds.
Unlike the collared peccary, the white-lipped peccary has strikingly pale cheeks.
