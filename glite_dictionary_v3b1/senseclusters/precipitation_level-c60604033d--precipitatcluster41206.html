precipitation level

noun
amount of measured rain or snow
1.4
Pronunciation: / prəˌsɪpɪˈteɪʃən ˈlɛvəl / (AmE), / prɪˌsɪpɪˈteɪʃən ˈlɛvəl / (BrE)
the amount of rain, snow, or other forms of precipitation measured over a specific period or in a particular place

Examples

High precipitation level
A sudden drop in precipitation level led to concerns about drought.
Scientists track precipitation levels to monitor changes in climate patterns.
This region experiences high precipitation levels during the winter months.
