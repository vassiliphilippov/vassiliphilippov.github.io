relieve

verb
grant exemption from a rule or requirement
Pronunciation: / rɪˈliv / (AmE), / rɪˈliːv / (BrE)
to free or exempt someone from a duty, responsibility, or obligation

Examples

To relieve someone of duty
He was relieved from jury duty due to a family emergency.
The principal decided to relieve her from gym class because of her injury.
They relieve employees from certain regulations during public holidays.
New staff were brought in to relieve the tired workers after the long shift.
She was finally relieved from her duties at the front desk.
The night nurse will relieve you so you can take a break.
He was relieved from jury duty after providing a doctor's note.
Students may be relieved from certain courses if they have prior experience.
The manager relieved her assistant of weekend responsibilities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: burden - to load with duties or responsibilities
  • synonym: discharge - to officially relieve someone of a duty
  • synonym: excuse - to release from an obligation or duty
  • synonym: exempt - to free someone from an obligation or rule
  • synonym: replace - to take the place of someone in performing a duty
  • antonym: subject - to make someone liable to a rule or requirement
