crosscut handsaw

noun
handsaw for cutting across wood grain
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɔsˌkʌt ˈhændˌsɔ / (AmE), / ˈkrɒskʌt ˈhændsɔː / (BrE)
a handsaw designed to cut wood across the grain, rather than along it

Examples

A crosscut handsaw
A crosscut handsaw is ideal for trimming boards to length.
Before power tools, carpenters relied on the crosscut handsaw for accurate cuts.
He used a crosscut handsaw to make a smooth, clean cut across the plank.

Related Terms

  • antonym: rip saw - handsaw that cuts parallel to the wood grain
