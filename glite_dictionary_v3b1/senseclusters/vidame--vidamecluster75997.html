vidame

noun
medieval church officer
Pronunciation: / viˈdɑːm / (AmE), / viˈdɑːm / (BrE)
a feudal officer or title in medieval France who managed lands and affairs for a church or bishop

Examples

The vidame title
The vidame was responsible for protecting the lands owned by the abbey.
During the Middle Ages, a vidame often acted as a secular guardian for church property.
The title of vidame disappeared as feudal systems declined in France.
In medieval France, the vidame was responsible for overseeing the bishop's estates.
The vidame held significant power in the region, acting on behalf of the church.
Historical records mention a vidame negotiating treaties for the bishop.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bishop - a senior member of the Christian clergy, typically in charge of a diocese
  • synonym: vicar - a representative or deputy of a bishop or church authority
