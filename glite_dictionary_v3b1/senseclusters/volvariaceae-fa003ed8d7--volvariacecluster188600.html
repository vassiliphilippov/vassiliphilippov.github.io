Volvariaceae

noun
family of fungi in Agaricales
Pronunciation: / voʊlˌvɛriˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / vɒlˌvɛərɪˈeɪsɪiː / (BrE)
A family of fungi in the order Agaricales, typically characterized by the presence of a volva or cup-like structure at the base.

Examples

Volvariaceae fungi family
Members of the Volvariaceae can be identified by their distinctive cup-shaped volva.
Researchers studied spore samples from the Volvariaceae collected in temperate forests.
The Volvariaceae family includes several edible and poisonous mushrooms.
