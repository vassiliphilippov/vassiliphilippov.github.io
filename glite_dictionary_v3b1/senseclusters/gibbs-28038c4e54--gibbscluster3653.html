Gibbs

proper noun
American scientist
9.5
Pronunciation: / ɡɪbz / (AmE), / ɡɪbz / (BrE)
Josiah Willard Gibbs (1839–1903), an American scientist known for his foundational work in thermodynamics and statistical mechanics.

Examples

Gibbs free energy
Many concepts in physical chemistry are named after Gibbs due to his groundbreaking research.
The Gibbs free energy equation is essential for understanding chemical reactions.
In lectures, professors often reference Gibbs when discussing entropy and thermodynamics.
