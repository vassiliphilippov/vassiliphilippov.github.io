japanning

noun
decorative lacquer process
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈpænɪŋ / (AmE), / dʒəˈpænɪŋ / (BrE)
a process of applying a hard, glossy, often black finish to metal or wood objects

Examples

Japanning process
The antique box was restored using traditional japanning methods to achieve its glossy black surface.
Many 18th-century European trays feature japanning as a decorative technique.
Collectors admire the detailed designs created through japanning on old metalware.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lacquering - the process of applying lacquer to surfaces for a shiny finish
  • synonym: varnishing - the act of coating a surface with varnish for protection or shine
