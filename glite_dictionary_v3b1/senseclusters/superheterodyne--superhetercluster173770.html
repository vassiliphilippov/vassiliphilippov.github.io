superheterodyne

adjective
radio receiver principle
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərˈhɛtərəˌdaɪn / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˈhɛtərəˌdaɪn / (BrE)
relating to a radio receiver or circuit that uses the superheterodyne principle to convert received signals to a fixed intermediate frequency for easier processing.

Examples

Superheterodyne circuit design
The engineer designed a superheterodyne circuit to improve the radio's selectivity.
Many modern radios use superheterodyne technology for better signal reception.
A superheterodyne receiver can filter out unwanted frequencies more effectively than older designs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: direct-conversion - relating to a radio receiver that converts signals directly to audio frequency
  • synonym: heterodyne - relating to mixing two frequencies to produce new frequencies
