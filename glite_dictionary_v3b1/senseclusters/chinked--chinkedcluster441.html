chinked

adjective
having narrow gaps filled (esp. logs, walls)
9.3 8.4
Pronunciation: / tʃɪŋkt / (AmE), / tʃɪŋkt / (BrE)
(of a structure, especially a wall or log building) having narrow openings or gaps that have been filled, typically with a material such as mortar or clay, to prevent drafts or leaks.

Examples

A chinked wall
After construction, the spaces between logs must be chinked to make the structure weatherproof.
Many old log cabins are carefully chinked to improve insulation and durability.
The walls were chinked with a thick layer of clay to keep out the cold winds.

Related Terms

  • antonym: unfilled - not having gaps filled, left empty
