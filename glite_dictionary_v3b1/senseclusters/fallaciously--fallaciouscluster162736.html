fallaciously

adverb
with faulty reasoning
8.4
Pronunciation: / fəˈleɪʃəsli / (AmE), / fəˈleɪʃəsli / (BrE)
in a way that is wrong, misleading, or based on a mistaken belief or false idea

Examples

To fallaciously assume
The theory was fallaciously assumed to be true without sufficient evidence.
He fallaciously argued that all large dogs are dangerous.
The data was fallaciously interpreted, leading to the wrong conclusion.
The advertisement fallaciously claimed that the product could cure all diseases.
He fallaciously argued that success is guaranteed with no effort.
The report was written fallaciously to mislead the public about the real situation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: correctly - in a way that is true or accurate
  • synonym: deceptively - in a way that gives a false impression
  • synonym: erroneously - in a mistaken or incorrect way
  • antonym: truthfully - in a way that is honest and based on facts
