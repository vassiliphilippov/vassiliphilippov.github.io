harness

verb
to control or keep in check
2.0
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑrnɪs /; / ˈhɑrnəs / (AmE), / ˈhɑːnɪs / (BrE)
to control and make use of something, such as power, energy, or emotions, for a purpose

Examples

To harness energy
Learning to harness your emotions can lead to better decision-making.
She tried to harness her anger before responding to the rude comment.
The country must harness its resources to ensure future prosperity.
Farmers have learned to harness the river's flow for irrigation throughout the region.
Scientists are working to harness solar energy to provide electricity for remote villages.
The campaign hopes to harness the enthusiasm of young volunteers for social good.
By investing in wind farms, the country hopes to harness the power of the wind for energy.
She struggled to harness her emotions during the tense negotiation.
The team was able to harness everyone's enthusiasm and finish the project ahead of schedule.
