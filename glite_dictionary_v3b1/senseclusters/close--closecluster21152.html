close

verb
bring/come together around
Pronunciation: / kloʊz / (AmE), / kləʊz / (BrE)
to move towards or reduce the distance to something or someone; to approach or engage closely

Examples

To close the gap
As the storm raged outside, she closed her arms around her scared child.
He instinctively closed his hands over the fragile bird to protect it.
The dog closed its jaws gently around the stick.
The soldiers prepared to close with the enemy as they advanced into the trench.
Under cover of darkness, the special forces team began to close with their target.
When the signal was given, both armies moved to close, starting the battle at dawn.
As dusk fell, the hikers closed with their campsite, relieved to see the tents.
The aircraft closed rapidly with the target area as the mission continued.
The ships closed with the enemy at dawn, preparing for battle.

Related Terms

  • antonym: open - to move or make something apart or unfastened
  • antonym: withdraw - to move away from a place or person
