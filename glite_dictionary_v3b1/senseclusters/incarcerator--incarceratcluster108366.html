incarcerator

noun
one who imprisons
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkɑːrsəˌreɪtər / (AmE), / ɪnˈkɑːsəˌreɪtə / (BrE)
a person or device that puts someone in prison or confines them

Examples

The incarcerator locks
The incarcerator ensured that all security protocols were followed in the prison.
Some view the judge as an incarcerator rather than a fair arbiter of justice.
The new automated incarcerator system was designed to safely confine dangerous criminals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jailer - a person in charge of a jail or prisoners
  • antonym: liberator - a person who frees others from imprisonment
  • synonym: warden - the person responsible for a prison
