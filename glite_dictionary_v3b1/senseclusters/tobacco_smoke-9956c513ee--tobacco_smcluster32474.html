tobacco smoke

noun
smoke from burning tobacco products
1.0
Pronunciation: / təˈbækoʊ smoʊk / (AmE), / təˈbækəʊ sməʊk / (BrE)
the smoke produced by burning tobacco products such as cigarettes, cigars, or pipes

Examples

Tobacco smoke exposure
Exposure to tobacco smoke can increase the risk of lung disease in non-smokers.
Many public places now ban tobacco smoke to protect people’s health.
The smell of tobacco smoke lingered in the room long after the party ended.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cigarette smoke - smoke produced specifically by burning cigarettes
  • synonym: secondhand smoke - inhaled environmental tobacco smoke from other people's tobacco products
