plow

verb
move forcefully through something
7.8
Pronunciation: / plaʊ / (AmE), / plaʊ / (BrE)
to move forcefully or steadily through a substance, as a plow does through soil

Examples

To plow through snow
She plowed her way through the crowded street, determined to reach her destination.
The large ship plowed through the icy waters, leaving a trail behind.
The runner plowed through the mud during the final stretch of the race.
He ploughed his way through the crowd to reach the exit.
The ferry ploughed through the thick fog, barely visible from the shore.
The runner ploughed on through the mud despite the heavy rain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cut - move through something with force
  • antonym: glide - move smoothly or effortlessly
  • synonym: plow - US spelling of 'plough', same verb sense
