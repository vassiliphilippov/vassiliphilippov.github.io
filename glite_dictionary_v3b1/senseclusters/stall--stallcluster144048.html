stall

noun
sudden loss of lift in aircraft flight
Pronunciation: / stɔl / (AmE), / stɔːl / (BrE)
an aerodynamic condition in which an aircraft loses lift suddenly, resulting in a downward plunge, usually due to excessive angle of attack

Examples

Plane stall
A stall can be dangerous if the pilot does not recognize and correct it quickly.
During the training session, the instructor intentionally induced a stall to teach recovery techniques.
The pilot recovered from the stall just in time to avoid a crash.
