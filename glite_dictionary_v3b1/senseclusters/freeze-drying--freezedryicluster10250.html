freeze-drying

noun
method of drying by freezing and sublimation
Pronunciation: / ˈfriːz ˌdraɪɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfriːz ˌdraɪɪŋ / (BrE)
a method of drying material, such as food or pharmaceuticals, by freezing it and then removing the ice by sublimation in a vacuum, so that its physical structure is preserved

Examples

Freeze-drying food
Freeze-drying is commonly used to preserve fruits, making them lightweight and long-lasting.
Blood plasma can be stored for many years when processed through freeze-drying.
The pharmaceutical company uses freeze-drying to ensure the stability of sensitive medications.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lyophilization - the process of freeze-drying, especially in scientific contexts
