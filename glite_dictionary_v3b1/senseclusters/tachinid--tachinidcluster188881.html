tachinid

adjective
of Tachinidae flies
Pronunciation: / ˈtækɪnɪd / (AmE), / ˈtækɪnɪd / (BrE)
relating to or belonging to the family Tachinidae, a group of flies often known for being parasitoids of other insects.

Examples

Tachinid fly species
The tachinid larvae develop inside caterpillars, eventually killing their hosts.
Farmers often encourage tachinid flies as a natural way to control crop pests.
A tachinid species was introduced to help reduce the population of invasive beetles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: parasitic - living as a parasite on or in another organism
