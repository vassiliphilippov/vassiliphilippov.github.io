Joyce

proper noun
Irish writer (1882–1941), innovator of literary style
Pronunciation: / dʒɔɪs / (AmE), / dʒɔɪs / (BrE)
James Joyce (1882–1941), an influential Irish writer known for his innovative literary techniques, especially stream of consciousness.

Examples

Joyce the writer
Joyce's novel 'Ulysses' is celebrated for its experimental use of language and narrative techniques.
Many literary scholars study Joyce to understand the development of modernist literature.
The influence of Joyce can be seen in writers from across the world.
