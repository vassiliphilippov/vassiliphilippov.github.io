flatwoods

noun
flat, wet forest area
Pronunciation: / ˈflætˌwʊdz / (AmE), / ˈflætˌwʊdz / (BrE)
a type of forested area with flat terrain, often featuring wet, sandy soil and characteristic vegetation

Examples

The flatwoods forest
Many rare plants grow in the flatwoods of the southeastern United States.
The flatwoods provide important habitat for a variety of bird species.
After heavy rain, the flatwoods can become flooded, creating temporary ponds for wildlife.

Related Terms

  • antonym: upland - an area of high or hilly land, often drier than surrounding areas
  • synonym: wetland - land area that is saturated with water, either permanently or seasonally
