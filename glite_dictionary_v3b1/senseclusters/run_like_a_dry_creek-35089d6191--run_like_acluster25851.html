run like a dry creek

verb
(of a flow) to be nearly dry or minimal
9.1 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / r ån la…™k …ô dra…™ krik / (AmE), / r ån la…™k …ô dra…™ kriÀêk / (BrE)
(of a stream, river, etc.) to flow very little or almost not at all, especially due to dryness or drought.

Examples

To run like a dry creek slowly
After the dry season, the once powerful river began to run like a dry creek.
During the drought, the fountain in the park ran like a dry creek, barely producing a trickle.
Without rainfall, even large streams sometimes run like a dry creek for weeks.

Synonyms
