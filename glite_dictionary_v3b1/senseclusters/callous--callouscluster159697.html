callous

adjective
emotionally insensitive or unfeeling
7.3 2.08/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk√¶l…ôs / (AmE), / Ààk√¶l…ôÀês / (BrE)
showing or having an insensitive and cruel disregard for others; emotionally unfeeling.

Examples

Callous comment
Her callous remark about the accident shocked everyone in the room.
It was callous of him to ignore his friend's distress.
The manager's callous attitude made the employees feel unappreciated.

Synonyms

  • heartless
    4.1 2.55/m
    heartless is cruel and shows no kindness to others, while callous describes a cold, unfeeling attitude that ignores people's pain.
  • coldhearted
    6.0 0.18/m
    coldhearted is emotionally very cold and refuses to help others, while callous focuses on cruel disregard for others' feelings.
  • unfeeling
    5.9 0.72/m
    unfeeling shows no visible emotion or sympathy, while callous adds a harsher, often deliberate cruelty toward people's needs.
  • unsympathetic
    4.9 0.39/m
    unsympathetic does not show understanding or concern, while callous treats people coldly and may act without care.
  • insensitive
    4.6 4.69/m
    insensitive can mean unaware of others' feelings and may be accidental, while callous implies a cruel, intentional lack of feeling.

  • synonym: insensitive - lacking sensitivity to others' feelings
