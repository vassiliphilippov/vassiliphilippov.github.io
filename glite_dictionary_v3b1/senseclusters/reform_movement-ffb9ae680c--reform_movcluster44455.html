reform movement

noun
organized campaign for social or humanitarian change
3.7
Pronunciation: / rɪˈfɔrm ˈmuvmənt / (AmE), / rɪˈfɔːm ˈmuːvmənt / (BrE)
an organized effort to bring about social or humanitarian change, often through political, legal, or cultural means

Examples

The reform movement of 1960s
Education policies were heavily influenced by the reform movement of the 1960s.
Many people joined the reform movement to campaign for equal rights.
The reform movement sought to improve conditions for workers in the nineteenth century.

Related Terms

  • synonym: social movement - a large, organized group aiming for social change
