flame

verb
to glow or burn with bright fire
3.0 1.5
Pronunciation: /fleɪm/ (AmE), /fleɪm/ (BrE)
to shine, glow, or burn with a bright or intense light

Examples

Lanterns flame brightly
During the festival, lanterns flame brightly along the riverbank.
The mountain peak flamed with the first light of dawn, casting a golden glow.
The sky seemed to flame in brilliant shades of orange and red at sunset.
Candles flamed suddenly when the wind swept through the room.
His eyes flamed with excitement when he heard the news.
The horizon flamed with brilliant red as the sun set.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flare - to blaze up with a sudden burst of flame or light
  • antonym: smolder - to burn slowly with smoke but no flame
