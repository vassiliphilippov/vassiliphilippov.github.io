heterocyclic compound

noun
chemical compound with a non-carbon ring atom
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛt̬.əˈraɪ.klɪk kəmˈpaʊnd / (AmE), / ˌhɛt.ər.əˈsɪk.lɪk kəmˈpaʊnd / (BrE)
a chemical compound containing a ring structure in which at least one of the atoms in the ring is not carbon, usually found in organic chemistry

Examples

A heterocyclic compound ring
Many pharmaceuticals are based on heterocyclic compounds because of their unique chemical properties.
Pyridine is a common example of a heterocyclic compound used in organic synthesis.
Scientists synthesized a new heterocyclic compound as part of cancer research.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: aromatic compound - compound with conjugated ring structures and delocalized electrons
  • antonym: carbocyclic compound - compound with ring where all atoms are carbon
