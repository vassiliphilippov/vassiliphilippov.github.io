flying start

noun
a start where motion has already begun
Pronunciation: / ˈflaɪɪŋ stɑrt / (AmE), / ˈflaɪɪŋ stɑːt / (BrE)
a very good or fast beginning to something, often giving an advantage

Examples

A flying start to business
Her new business had a flying start, attracting customers from day one.
The sprinters got a flying start in the relay, giving them an early lead.
With a flying start to the project, the team finished ahead of schedule.
The new project got off to a flying start thanks to early support from investors.
The team hopes for a flying start in the championship this season.
With her experience, she made a flying start in her new job.

  • antonym: slow start - a beginning that is not quick or efficient
