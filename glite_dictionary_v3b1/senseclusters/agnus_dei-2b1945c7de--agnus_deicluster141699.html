Agnus Dei

noun
Christian liturgical prayer 'Lamb of God'
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːɡ.nəs ˈdeɪ.i / (AmE), / ˌæɡ.nəs ˈdeɪ.aɪ / (BrE)
A liturgical prayer addressed to Christ as the Lamb of God, usually recited or sung during the Roman Catholic Mass and other Christian services.

Examples

The Agnus Dei prayer
At the end of Mass, the congregation joined silently in the Agnus Dei.
Composers throughout history have set the Agnus Dei to music for choral performances.
The choir sang the Agnus Dei with remarkable clarity during the Easter service.
