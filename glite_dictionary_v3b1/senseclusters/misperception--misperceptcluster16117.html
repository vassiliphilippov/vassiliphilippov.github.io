misperception

noun
incorrect understanding
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪspərˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɪspəˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
a mistaken or incorrect understanding or interpretation of something

Examples

A common misperception
A common misperception is that all snakes are dangerous to humans.
Her misperception of the rules caused her to make a costly mistake.
The article aims to correct the misperception that healthy food is always expensive.
A common misperception is that bats are blind, but they can actually see quite well.
The fog caused a misperception of distance, making the building seem much farther away.
Her misperception of his tone led to an unnecessary argument.
