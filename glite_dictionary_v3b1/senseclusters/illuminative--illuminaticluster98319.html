illuminative

adjective
decorating manuscripts
9.5
Pronunciation: / ɪˈluːmɪnətɪv / (AmE), / ɪˈluːmɪnətɪv / (BrE)
relating to the decoration of manuscripts or books with gold, silver, and bright colors, especially in the art of illumination

Examples

Illuminative manuscript
The library owns several illuminative manuscripts from the medieval period.
Art historians study illuminative techniques used in ancient religious texts.
The exhibition featured an illuminative page adorned with gold and intricate designs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: illuminated - decorated with gold, silver, or bright colors, especially in manuscripts
  • antonym: plain - not decorated or ornamented
