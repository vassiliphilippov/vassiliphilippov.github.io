sawgrass

noun
marsh grass with sharp leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈsɔːˌɡræs / (AmE), / ˈsɔːˌɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a tall grass with sharp-edged leaves that grows in marshy or wetland areas, especially in the southeastern United States

Examples

Tall sawgrass leaves
The Everglades are covered with dense sawgrass that stretches for miles.
Be careful when walking through sawgrass because its leaves can easily cut your skin.
Many birds and animals make their homes among the thick sawgrass in the wetlands.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cattail - a tall, reed-like plant found in wetlands
  • synonym: marsh grass - grass species that grow in wetland or marshy environments
