wind

verb
move in a winding or twisting path
Pronunciation: / waɪnd / (AmE), / waɪnd / (BrE)
to move or cause to move in a curving or twisting path

Examples

To wind through trees
A small stream winds through the ancient forest before emptying into the lake.
The narrow road winds up the mountain, offering beautiful views along the way.
The parade route winds through the city center, attracting large crowds every year.
A small river wound around the foot of the mountain.
The hiking trail winds up the hill, offering beautiful views at every turn.
The narrow street winds through the historic part of the city.

Related Terms

  • synonym: meander - to follow a winding and turning course
  • antonym: straighten - to make or become straight, without bends or curves
  • synonym: twist - to form into a bent, curling, or distorted shape; to follow a winding course
