line

verb
form a row along the edge
3.2 1.9
Pronunciation: /laɪn/ (AmE), /laɪn/ (BrE)
to mark, draw, or cover with lines, or to form a row along the edge or surface of something

Examples

Flowers line the path
Bright flowers lined both sides of the walkway.
Spectators lined the streets to watch the parade go by.
Tall palm trees line the main avenue leading to the hotel.
Age and laughter line his face with gentle, familiar patterns.
The artist lined the surface with faint pencil marks before painting.
Years of worry had lined her forehead with deep creases.
Before painting, he lined the walls to mark where the shelves would go.
She carefully lined the edges of the paper with a pencil.
The children lined their notebooks with colorful markers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: border - to form a boundary along an edge

  • synonym: fringe - to form a border or edging around something
  • synonym: trace - to copy or mark out by drawing lines
  • synonym: wrinkle - to form small lines or folds, especially in skin
