quadrivium

noun
medieval higher curriculum: arithmetic, geometry, music, astronomy
7.1 9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkwɑː.drɪ.vɪ.əm/ (AmE), /ˈkwɒ.drɪ.vɪ.əm/ (BrE)
(in the Middle Ages) The higher division of the seven liberal arts in medieval universities, consisting of arithmetic, geometry, music, and astronomy.

Examples

The quadrivium arts
At medieval universities, the quadrivium was considered essential for advanced scholarly study.
Students who completed the trivium would then move on to the quadrivium.
The quadrivium included both mathematical and musical disciplines.
