rebel

verb
rise in opposition; resist authority
5.0 3.3
Pronunciation: / rɪˈbɛl / (AmE), / rɪˈbɛl / (BrE)
to oppose or resist authority, control, or tradition

Examples

To rebel against rules
If conditions do not improve, the workers may rebel against management.
Teenagers often rebel when they feel misunderstood by adults.
The citizens decided to rebel against the unfair new law.
Artists sometimes rebel against conventional styles to create something entirely new.
She decided to rebel after years of following strict workplace routines.
Teenagers often rebel against their parents by questioning family traditions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conform - to comply with rules, standards, or laws
  • synonym: revolt - to take violent action against authority or government
  • antonym: submit - to accept or yield to a superior force
