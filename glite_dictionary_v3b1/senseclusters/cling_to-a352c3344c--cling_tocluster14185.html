cling to

verb
hold tightly to someone or something
6.4
Pronunciation: / klɪŋ tu / (AmE), / klɪŋ tə / (BrE)
to hold tightly to someone or something, usually with one's hands or arms, often out of fear, need, or affection

Examples

Children cling to parents
Even as the train pulled away, he continued to cling to the door handle.
Some people cling to old traditions, refusing to accept change.
The frightened child began to cling to her mother's leg during the thunderstorm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hold on to - to keep a firm grip on something
  • antonym: let go of - to release one's grip on something
