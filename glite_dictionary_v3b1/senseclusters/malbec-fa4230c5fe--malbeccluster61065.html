Malbec

noun
red wine grape
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈmælˌbɛk / (AmE), / ˈmælˌbɛk / (BrE)
a variety of red wine or the dark-skinned grape used to produce it, originally from France and widely grown in Argentina

Examples

A glass of Malbec
She ordered a glass of Malbec with her steak.
Argentinian Malbec is known for its deep color and rich flavor.
The vineyard specializes in producing high-quality Malbec grapes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Cabernet Sauvignon - a well-known variety of red wine and the grape used to make it
  • synonym: Merlot - a popular variety of red wine and the grape used to make it
  • antonym: white wine - wine made from green or yellowish grapes
