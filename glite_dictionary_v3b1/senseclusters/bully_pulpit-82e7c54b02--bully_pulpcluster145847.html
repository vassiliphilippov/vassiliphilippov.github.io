bully pulpit

noun
position offering prominent opportunity to speak out
9.9 7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊli ˈpʊlpɪt / (AmE), / ˈbʊli ˈpʊl.pɪt / (BrE)
a public office or position of authority that provides its occupant with an outstanding opportunity to speak out and be listened to on any issue

Examples

A bully pulpit position
Holding such a bully pulpit allows leaders to influence national debate.
She regarded her new role as a bully pulpit for advancing educational reforms.
The president used the bully pulpit to rally support for his policy initiatives.
