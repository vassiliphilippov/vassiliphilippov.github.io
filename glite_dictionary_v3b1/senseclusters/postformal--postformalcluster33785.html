postformal

adjective
advanced complex thinking
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊstˈfɔːrməl / (AmE), / ˌpəʊstˈfɔːməl / (BrE)
relating to advanced thinking that goes beyond traditional logical reasoning, often involving complexity and multiple perspectives

Examples

Postformal thinking
Researchers study how postformal thinking develops in adults facing complex life situations.
Her approach to the problem was distinctly postformal, considering emotional and social factors as well as logic.
In psychology, postformal reasoning is seen as a sign of mature cognitive development.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dialectical - involving reasoning that considers multiple viewpoints or contradictions
  • antonym: formal - relating to traditional logical reasoning
