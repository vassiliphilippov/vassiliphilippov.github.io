dichromacy

noun
color vision deficiency involving two color receptors
7.5
Pronunciation: / daɪˈkroʊməsi / (AmE), / dɪˈkrəʊməsi / (BrE)
a deficiency of color vision in which a person needs only two wavelengths of light to match all hues, rather than the normal three

Examples

Dichromacy color vision
Dichromacy is more common in males and often results in difficulty distinguishing reds and greens.
People with dichromacy can match all visible colors using just two primary hues.
The scientist explained the genetic basis for dichromacy during the lecture.
