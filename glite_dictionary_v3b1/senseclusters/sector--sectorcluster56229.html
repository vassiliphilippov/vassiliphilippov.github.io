sector

noun
data storage unit on a disk
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛktɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛktə / (BrE)
a subdivision of a track on a magnetic or optical disc used for storing a fixed amount of data, typically 512 bytes unless otherwise specified

Examples

A disk sector
Each sector on the hard drive can store 512 bytes of information.
Formatting the disk creates a new sector structure for data storage.
If a sector becomes corrupted, the data in that part of the disk may be lost.

Related Terms

  • synonym: block - a contiguous set of bytes forming a single unit of data storage
