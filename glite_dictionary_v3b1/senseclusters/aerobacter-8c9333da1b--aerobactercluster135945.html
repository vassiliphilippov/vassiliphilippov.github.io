Aerobacter

noun
a genus of aerobic bacteria
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɛərəˈbæktɚ / (AmE), / ˌɛərəˈbæktə / (BrE)
A genus of aerobic bacteria widely distributed in nature, formerly used for certain bacteria now mostly classified as Enterobacter.

Examples

Aerobacter species
Aerobacter species were once considered common soil and water bacteria.
Scientists no longer frequently use the term Aerobacter because most of its species have been reclassified.
The classification of Aerobacter has changed over time as bacterial taxonomy evolved.
