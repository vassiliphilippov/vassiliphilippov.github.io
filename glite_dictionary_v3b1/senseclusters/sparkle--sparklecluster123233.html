sparkle

verb
emit or produce sparks
3.4 1.0
Pronunciation: /ˈspɑːr.kəl/ (AmE), /ˈspɑː.kəl/ (BrE)
to shine or glitter with bright flashes of light

Examples

To sparkle in the sun
If you rub certain metals together, they may sparkle in the dark.
The faulty outlet caused the plug to sparkle and smoke.
The wires began to sparkle violently after the tree fell on them.
After the rain, the grass sparkled with tiny droplets of water.
Diamonds sparkle beautifully under bright lights.
Her earrings sparkled as she turned her head.
She wore a necklace that seemed to sparkle with every movement she made.
The lake began to sparkle in the morning sunlight, making the scenery look magical.
The soda started to sparkle as soon as it was poured into the glass.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dull - not bright, shiny, or reflecting light
  • synonym: flash - to emit a sudden burst of light
  • synonym: gleam - to shine with a soft or intermittent light
  • synonym: glitter - to shine with a bright, shimmering, reflected light
  • degree lower: glow - to give out a steady light
