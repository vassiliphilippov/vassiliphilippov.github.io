luteinization

noun
formation of corpus luteum
9.9
Pronunciation: /luː.tɪ.naɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /luː.tiː.naɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /luː.tɪ.naɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /luː.tiː.naɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process by which an ovarian follicle transforms into a corpus luteum after ovulation

Examples

Ovarian luteinization
Hormonal changes trigger luteinization in the ovary after an egg is released.
A lack of luteinization can lead to fertility problems in some women.
Researchers studied the effects of certain hormones on luteinization in laboratory animals.
