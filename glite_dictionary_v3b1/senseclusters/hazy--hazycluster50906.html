hazy

adjective
full of haze, fog, or mist
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈheɪzi / (AmE), / ˈheɪzi / (BrE)
not clear, either because of mist, fog, or because something is hard to see or remember

Examples

A hazy sky
After the storm, the air was still hazy as the fog lingered over the fields.
Driving was difficult on the hazy road, with a fine mist hanging in the air.
The mountains were hazy in the early morning light, barely visible in the distance.
Her memories of childhood were hazy, like a dream half remembered.
On hot summer afternoons, the mountains appear hazy in the distance.
The photograph was so hazy that we couldn't make out anyone's face.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blurry - lacking clear definition; making it difficult to see details
  • antonym: clear - free from haze, mist, or cloudiness; easy to see through
  • synonym: foggy - full of or covered with thick mist or low clouds
