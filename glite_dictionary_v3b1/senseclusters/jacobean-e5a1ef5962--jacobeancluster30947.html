Jacobean

noun
notable figure from the reign of James I
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒækəˈbiən / (AmE), / ˌdʒakəˈbiːən / (BrE)
a distinguished person or figure associated with the reign of James I of England (1603–1625)

Examples

A famous Jacobean
Literature from the period often references the values held by the Jacobeans.
Many Jacobeans were influential in shaping the politics of early seventeenth-century England.
The architecture designed by prominent Jacobeans can still be seen today.
