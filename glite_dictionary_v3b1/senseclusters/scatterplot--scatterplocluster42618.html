scatterplot

verb
to plot data points
4.5
Pronunciation: / ˈskætərˌplɑt / (AmE), / ˈskætəˌplɒt / (BrE)
to display data by plotting individual values as dots on a graph with two variables

Examples

To scatterplot results
The scientist decided to scatterplot the results to identify any correlation between the two variables.
You can scatterplot the data to see if there is a trend or pattern.
After collecting the survey responses, she used software to scatterplot the information for analysis.
