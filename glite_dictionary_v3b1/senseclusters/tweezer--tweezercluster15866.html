tweezer

noun
nanoscale molecular gripping device
9.9 0.08/m
Pronunciation: / c8twid0z59r / (AmE), / c8twid0z59 / (BrE)
(in nanotechnology and molecular biology) a nanoscale molecular device, often built from DNA or synthetic molecules, that opens and closes like a pair of tweezers to grip or manipulate single molecules.

Examples

DNA tweezer
Researchers used a DNA tweezer to hold a single molecule during the nanoscale experiment.
In the paper, the team showed that programmable tweezers can deliver drugs to specific cells.
She demonstrated how the synthetic tweezer grasped the tiny protein for measurement.

Related Terms

  • synonym: DNA tweezer - a specific type of molecular tweezer made from DNA strands that can change shape to hold molecules
  • synonym: molecular tweezer - a molecule or molecular assembly designed to bind or hold a target molecule by noncovalent interactions
