interneuron

noun
connecting nerve cell
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈnʊrɑn / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈnjʊərɒn / (BrE)
a nerve cell in the brain or spinal cord that connects other neurons and passes signals between them

Examples

An interneuron connects neurons
An interneuron transmits signals between sensory and motor neurons in the spinal cord.
Damage to a single interneuron can disrupt communication within a neural circuit.
Researchers studied how each interneuron contributes to learning and memory in the brain.
An interneuron helps relay signals between sensory and motor neurons in the spinal cord.
Damage to a single interneuron can disrupt communication within neural circuits.

Related Terms

  • antonym: motor neuron - a nerve cell that carries signals from the brain or spinal cord to muscles
  • antonym: sensory neuron - a nerve cell that carries signals from sensory organs to the brain or spinal cord
