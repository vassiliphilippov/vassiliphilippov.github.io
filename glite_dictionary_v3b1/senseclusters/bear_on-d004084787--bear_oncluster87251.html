bear on

verb
to press or impel someone to act
Pronunciation: / bɛr ɑn / (AmE), / bɛə ɒn / (BrE)
to press, drive, or impel (someone) to action or completion of an action

Examples

Pressure bears on him
Financial pressures bore on him to accept the job offer quickly.
Parental expectations often bear on students to pursue certain careers.
The strict deadline bore on the team, motivating them to work late into the night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: push - to encourage or force someone to do something
