the act or process of making someone less willing to do something, especially by expressing opposition or disapproval

Examples

Discouragement from parents
Despite the discouragement from critics, she continued with her project.
She spoke with clear discouragement about his plans to quit school.
Their constant discouragement made it difficult for him to pursue his dream.

Synonyms

  • dissuasion
    7.7 0.06/m
    dissuasion aims to persuade someone not to act by reasoned argument or advice, while discouragement makes someone less willing through disapproval.
  • determent
    8.5 0.00/m
    determent warns people of possible bad consequences to stop attempts, while discouragement expresses disapproval to make someone less willing.
  • deterrence
    7.5 6.95/m
    deterrence uses fear of serious punishment to prevent actions, while discouragement often uses disapproval to make someone less willing.
