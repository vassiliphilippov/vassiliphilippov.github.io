goffering iron

noun
iron for pressing pleats and ridges
8.9 9.1
Pronunciation: /ˈgɑː.fər.ɪŋ ˈaɪ.ərn/ (AmE), /ˈgɒf.ə.rɪŋ ˈaɪ.ən/ (BrE)
A heated metal rod used to press and shape pleats or ridges in fabric, especially for ruffles and frills.

Examples

An antique goffering iron
Antique goffering irons are sometimes displayed in museums of textiles and fashion.
She carefully heated the goffering iron before pressing it to the delicate ruffles.
The tailor used a goffering iron to create crisp pleats on the cuffs.
