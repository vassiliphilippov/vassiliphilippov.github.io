dim

adjective
not bright; made less bright
Pronunciation: / dɪm / (AmE), / dɪm / (BrE)
not bright; having little light or brightness

Examples

A dim light
His face was barely visible in the dim hallway.
She turned on the dim lamp by her bedside to read before sleep.
The restaurant creates a romantic atmosphere with dim lighting.
It was hard to see in the dim hallway late at night.
She read her book by the dim glow of a nightlight.
The restaurant had a dim atmosphere perfect for a romantic dinner.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bright - giving out or reflecting a lot of light; vivid
  • degree higher: dark - with little or no light
  • synonym: subdued - (of light) not strong or intense
