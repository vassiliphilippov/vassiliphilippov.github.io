didact

noun
pedantic teacher
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪdækt / (AmE), / ˈdɪdakt / (BrE)
a person who teaches or is inclined to teach, especially in a formal or overly instructive way

Examples

A didact teacher
The new manager was a didact who insisted on explaining every detail to his team.
As a didact, she often gave lengthy lectures that some students found overwhelming.
He was labeled a didact because of his tendency to correct others constantly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pedant - a person who is excessively concerned with minor details or rules
  • antonym: student - a person who is learning, especially in a school
  • synonym: teacher - a person who teaches, especially in a school
