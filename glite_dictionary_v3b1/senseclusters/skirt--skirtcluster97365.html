skirt

verb
avoid addressing or confronting directly
7.6 5.5
Pronunciation: / skɝt / (AmE), / skɜːt / (BrE)
to avoid dealing with or addressing (an issue, question, or duty) directly; to sidestep or evade.

Examples

To skirt a problem
Instead of addressing the mistake, she chose to skirt the issue entirely.
It's frustrating when people skirt their responsibilities at work.
The politician tried to skirt the reporter's pointed questions during the press conference.

Related Terms
