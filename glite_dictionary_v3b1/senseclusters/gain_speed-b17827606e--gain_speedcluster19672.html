gain speed

verb
increase in speed or velocity
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɡeɪn spiːd / (AmE), / ɡeɪn spiːd / (BrE)
to begin moving faster; to increase the rate of movement or velocity

Examples

To gain speed quickly
As the plane taxied down the runway, it started to gain speed.
Cyclists must gain speed quickly to compete in a race.
The car began to gain speed as it went downhill.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accelerate - to begin to move more quickly; to cause to increase in speed
  • antonym: lose speed - to decrease in velocity or move more slowly
