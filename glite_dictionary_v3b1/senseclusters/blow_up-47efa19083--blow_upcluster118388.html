blow up

verb
to suddenly become very angry
Pronunciation: / bloʊ ʌp / (AmE), / bləʊ ʌp / (BrE)
to suddenly become very angry; to lose one's temper and express anger strongly

Examples

He blew up in anger
I didn't mean to blow up, but the repeated mistakes were frustrating.
She tends to blow up if you question her decisions in front of others.
Whenever traffic builds up, he can easily blow up at the slightest provocation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm down - to become less angry or agitated
  • synonym: lose one's temper - to fail to control one's anger
