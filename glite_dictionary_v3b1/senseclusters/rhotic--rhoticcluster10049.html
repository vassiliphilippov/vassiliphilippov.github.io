rhotic

noun
r-like speech sound
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊtɪk / (AmE), / ˈrəʊtɪk / (BrE)
a speech sound, either a consonant or vowel, that has an r-like quality or is pronounced with an r sound

Examples

A rhotic sound
In Scottish English, the rhotic is pronounced clearly at the end of words.
Some American accents are known for their strong rhotic sounds.
The linguist explained how the rhotic varies between different dialects.
