induce

verb
reason or establish by induction
3.0 5.1
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈduːs/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈdjuːs/ /; / /ɪn.ˈduːs/ (BrE)
to derive or establish (a general principle) from specific observations or facts, especially by the method of induction

Examples

To induce a rule
From the pattern in the data, we can induce a general law of behavior.
If we induce that all metals conduct electricity from our experiments, we're applying inductive reasoning.
Scientists often induce theories by observing repeated phenomena and identifying common features.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deduce - to reach a conclusion by reasoning from general principles to specific cases
