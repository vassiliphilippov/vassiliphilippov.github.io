school

verb
train or educate in refined skill or taste
3.0 3.21/m
Pronunciation: / skuÀêl / (AmE), / skuÀêl / (BrE)
to educate or train someone, especially in a formal or systematic way

Examples

To school a student
He was schooled in classical music by a renowned composer.
Her palate has been schooled by years of sampling fine wines.
The chef schooled his apprentices in the art of French cuisine.
He was schooled in classical music from an early age.
Her parents schooled her at home until she was ten.
The recruits were thoroughly schooled in military protocol before their first mission.

Synonyms

  • educate
    2.2 53/m
    educate means giving knowledge or skills over time, while school means teaching or training in a formal, organized way.
  • teach
    1.0 283/m
    teach means giving instruction or help in learning directly, while school means educating or training over longer, planned programs.
  • train
    1.0 70/m
    train means practicing specific skills often for a job, while school means educating or training in a broad, formal program.
