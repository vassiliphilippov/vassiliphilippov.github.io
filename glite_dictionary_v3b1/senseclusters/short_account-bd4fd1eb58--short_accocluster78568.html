short account

noun
brokerage account for short selling
Pronunciation: / ʃɔrt əˈkaʊnt / (AmE), / ʃɔːt əˈkaʊnt / (BrE)
a brokerage account used by an investor to sell securities short, i.e., to sell securities they do not own in anticipation of buying them back later at a lower price

Examples

A short account position
A short account requires strict margin maintenance to avoid a margin call.
He opened a short account to profit from falling stock prices.
Many experienced traders maintain a short account as part of their investment strategy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: margin account - a brokerage account in which the broker lends the customer cash to purchase securities
