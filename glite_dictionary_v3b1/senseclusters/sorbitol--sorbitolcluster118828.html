sorbitol

noun
osmotic laxative
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈsɔːrbɪˌtɔl / (AmE), / ˈsɔːbɪtɒl / (BrE)
a sugar alcohol used as a sweetener and as a medicine to relieve constipation by drawing water into the intestines

Examples

Sorbitol for constipation
The doctor prescribed sorbitol to help relieve the patient's constipation.
Some sugar-free candies contain sorbitol as a sweetener, which can have a laxative effect if consumed in large amounts.
Pharmacists often recommend sorbitol for patients who need a gentle osmotic laxative.

Related Terms

  • antonym: constipation - difficulty in emptying the bowels
  • synonym: lactulose - a synthetic sugar used as an osmotic laxative
