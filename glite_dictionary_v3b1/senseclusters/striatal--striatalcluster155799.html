striatal

adjective
of the striatum
9.1
Pronunciation: / straɪˈeɪtəl / (AmE), / straɪˈeɪtəl / (BrE)
relating to the striatum, a part of the brain involved in movement and reward processing

Examples

Striatal brain activity
Researchers observed increased striatal activity in participants during the reward phase of the experiment.
Damage to striatal regions can affect a person's ability to control movement.
The medication was found to alter striatal dopamine levels in patients with Parkinson's disease.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cortical - relating to the cortex of the brain
  • synonym: striated - having stripes or striations
