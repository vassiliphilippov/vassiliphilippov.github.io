Rh

noun
a blood group antigen important in transfusions
6.0
Pronunciation: / ɑr eɪtʃ / (AmE), / ɑː ɪtʃ / (BrE)
a protein found on the surface of red blood cells in most people, used to determine blood group compatibility, especially in transfusions; also called the Rh factor.

Examples

Rh blood type
All donors are screened for Rh compatibility before giving blood.
Being Rh-negative can complicate pregnancy if the fetus is Rh-positive.
The baby's Rh status was tested immediately after birth.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: ABO blood group - another major blood group system
  • synonym: Rh factor - the same antigen, often referred to as 'Rh factor'
