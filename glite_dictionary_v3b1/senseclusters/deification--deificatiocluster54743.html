deification

noun
the act of making someone into a god
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˌdiəfɪˈkeɪʃən /; / ˌdiːəfəˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making someone or something into a god, or treating them as if they were a god

Examples

The deification of heroes
Some critics argue that the media's deification of celebrities is unhealthy for society.
The deification of the emperor was a common practice in ancient Rome.
The legend tells of the deification of a mortal hero after his great deeds.
Many ancient rulers sought deification to legitimize their authority and inspire loyalty.
Some critics argue that the deification of technology overlooks its potential risks.
The rock star's deification by fans left him feeling isolated from reality.
Many artists have spoken against the deification of fame in modern society.
Some movements warn against the deification of technology and progress.
The deification of leaders has occurred in various cultures throughout history.

Related Terms

  • synonym: apotheosis - the highest point in the development of something; the elevation of someone to divine status
  • antonym: demonization - the act of portraying someone as wicked or evil
  • synonym: idolization - the act of admiring or worshipping someone to an extreme degree
