fool around

verb
act in a playful or silly way
1.6
Pronunciation: / fuːl əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / fuːl əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
to behave in a playful, silly, or aimless way instead of being serious or working

Examples

Kids fool around
During recess, the students were fooling around on the playground.
Stop fooling around and pay attention to your work.
The children began to fool around instead of cleaning their room.
Please don’t fool around in the kitchen with sharp knives.
The children began to fool around instead of doing their homework.
We all fool around a bit when there’s nothing urgent to do.

  • synonym: mess around - to behave in a silly or aimless way
  • antonym: work - to engage in activity for a purpose or to be productive
