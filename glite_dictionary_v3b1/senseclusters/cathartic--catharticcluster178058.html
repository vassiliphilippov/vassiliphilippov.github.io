cathartic

adjective
emotionally cleansing or releasing
8.8
Pronunciation: / kəˈθɑrtɪk /; / kəˈθɑːr.tɪk / (AmE), / kəˈθɑːtɪk /; / kəˈθɑː.tɪk / (BrE)
providing emotional or psychological relief by expressing strong feelings; emotionally purging

Examples

Cathartic experience
His speech had a cathartic effect on the audience, allowing them to confront their feelings.
Many people find watching tragic films to be a cathartic experience.
Writing about her experiences was a truly cathartic process for her.
After the heated argument, he found writing in his journal to be surprisingly cathartic.
Many people find it cathartic to talk about their feelings openly with friends.
Watching the sad movie was a deeply cathartic experience for her, allowing her to finally cry.

Related Terms

  • synonym: therapeutic - having a healing effect; making someone feel better
  • antonym: traumatic - emotionally disturbing or distressing (contrast)
