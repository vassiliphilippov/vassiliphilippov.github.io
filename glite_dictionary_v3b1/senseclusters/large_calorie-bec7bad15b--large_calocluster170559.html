large calorie

noun
unit of food energy (kilocalorie)
1.0 3.1
Pronunciation: /lɑːrdʒ ˈkæl.ə.ri/ /; / /lɑːrdʒ ˈkæl.ə.rɪ/ (AmE), /lɑːdʒ ˈkæl.ə.rɪ/ /; / /lɑːdʒ ˈkæl.ə.ri/ (BrE)
a unit of heat equal to the amount needed to raise the temperature of one kilogram of water by one degree Celsius; used to measure the energy value of food

Examples

A large calorie unit
Dieticians calculate daily energy needs based on the number of large calories consumed.
Nutrition labels usually indicate the energy content in large calories or kilocalories.
One large calorie is the amount of energy needed to heat one kilogram of water by one degree Celsius.
