jet

noun
sudden stream of liquid or gas
Pronunciation: / dʒɛt / (AmE), / dʒɛt / (BrE)
a narrow, forceful stream or spray of liquid or gas

Examples

Jet of water
A powerful jet of water shot out from the hose, soaking the pavement.
She squirted a jet of perfume onto her wrist before leaving.
The steam engine released a jet of vapor as it started up.
She turned on the hose and a strong jet of water sprayed the garden.
Suddenly, a jet of steam burst from the pipe and filled the room.
The fountain shot a powerful jet of water into the air.

Related Terms

  • synonym: spray - liquid sent out in very small drops
  • synonym: spurt - a sudden strong stream or burst, especially of liquid
