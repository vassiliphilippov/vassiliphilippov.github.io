lacuna

noun
a gap or missing part
Pronunciation: / ləˈkunə /; / ləˈkuːnə / (AmE), / ləˈkjuːnə / (BrE)
an empty space, gap, or missing part, especially in a manuscript, text, or body of knowledge

Examples

A lacuna in text
His essay had a lacuna that made the argument hard to follow.
The historian pointed out a significant lacuna in the ancient records.
There is a lacuna on page five where a paragraph is missing.
Her argument is weakened by a lacuna in the supporting evidence.
The historian noted a significant lacuna in the record of that period.
The manuscript contains a lacuna where several pages are missing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absence - the state of being away or missing
  • synonym: gap - an unfilled space or interval
  • synonym: void - a completely empty space
