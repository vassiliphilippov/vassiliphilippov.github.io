b-meson

noun
very short-lived meson with a bottom quark
Pronunciation: / ˈbiˌmɛzɑn / (AmE), / ˈbiːˌmiːzɒn / (BrE)
A very short-lived meson containing a bottom quark, observed in high-energy physics experiments.

Examples

A b-meson particle
Physicists detected the presence of a b-meson during the particle collision experiment.
Research on the b-meson has helped scientists understand quark behavior and fundamental forces.
The decay modes of the b-meson provide valuable insights into the Standard Model of particle physics.
