karst

noun
landscape with caves
9.5
Pronunciation: /kɑːrst/ (AmE), /kɑːst/ (BrE)
a type of landscape formed by the dissolution of soluble rocks, with features like caves, sinkholes, and underground streams

Examples

Karst landscape
The region's unique karst formations attract geologists and tourists from around the world.
Many underground rivers flow through the karst landscape, creating spectacular caves.
Farmers in karst areas often deal with sudden sinkholes appearing in their fields.
The region is famous for its dramatic karst formations and underground rivers.
Scientists study karst landscapes to understand how caves and sinkholes develop over time.
Tourists explored the unique karst terrain, marveling at the natural stone arches and deep caverns.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cave - a natural underground space often found in karst areas
  • synonym: limestone - a sedimentary rock often forming karst landscapes
  • synonym: sinkhole - a depression or hole in the ground caused by collapse, common in karst areas
