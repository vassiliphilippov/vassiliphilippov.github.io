military expedition

noun
a targeted overseas military campaign
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪlɪˌtɛri ˌɛkspəˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈmɪlɪtəri ˌɛkspəˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
a military campaign undertaken to achieve a particular objective in a foreign country

Examples

Ancient military expeditions
Several ancient empires launched military expeditions to expand their territories.
The general led a military expedition into the mountainous region to suppress the uprising.
The government justified the military expedition as necessary for national security.

Related Terms

  • synonym: invasion - an instance of invading a country with an armed force
  • antonym: retreat - the act of withdrawing troops to avoid defeat
