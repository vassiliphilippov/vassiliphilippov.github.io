long legs

noun
lasting attention
6.8
Pronunciation: / lɔŋ lɛɡz / (AmE), / lɒŋ lɛɡz / (BrE)
the quality of continuing to attract attention or remain important for a long time, especially in news or rumors

Examples

Long legs in the news
The story about the celebrity's secret wedding had long legs and was discussed for months.
Some rumors have long legs and keep circulating long after the facts are known.
The political scandal proved to have long legs, dominating headlines for weeks.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fad - something that is popular for a short time and then forgotten
  • synonym: staying power - the ability to last or remain relevant for a long time
