condemnation

noun
official declaration of unfitness or expropriation
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑn.dəmˈneɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.dəmˈneɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
(law) The act of officially declaring something, such as property or a building, unfit for use or subject to seizure for public use.

Examples

Building condemnation
After the city's condemnation of the old theater, it was demolished to make way for a new park.
Government condemnation of land for highway expansion often leads to disputes with property owners.
The condemnation process requires authorities to prove the building is unsafe for residents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: expropriation - the act of taking away property for public use
  • synonym: seizure - the act of taking possession of something by legal right
