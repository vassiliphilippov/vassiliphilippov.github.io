stare fear in the face

verb
confront fears directly
Pronunciation: / stɛr fɪr ɪn ðə feɪs / (AmE), / stɛə fɪə ɪn ðə feɪs / (BrE)
to confront one's fears directly and without avoidance

Examples

To stare fear in the face
After her illness, she learned to stare fear in the face rather than let anxiety control her decisions.
The climber had to stare fear in the face before attempting the dangerous ascent.
When you're afraid of making mistakes, sometimes you have to stare fear in the face and do it anyway.
