caldarium

noun
Roman hot bath room
Pronunciation: / kælˈdɛəriəm / (AmE), / kælˈdɛəriəm / (BrE)
a hot room in an ancient Roman bathhouse used for bathing and sweating

Examples

The caldarium room
The ancient Romans would relax in the caldarium before moving to cooler rooms.
Steam filled the caldarium as bathers enjoyed the intense heat.
Archaeologists discovered the remains of a caldarium in the ruins of the old city.

Related Terms

  • antonym: frigidarium - a cold room in an ancient Roman bathhouse
  • synonym: tepidarium - a warm room in an ancient Roman bathhouse
