third ventricle

noun
cavity in the brain between lateral and fourth ventricles
8.0
Pronunciation: / θɝd ˈvɛn.trɪ.kəl / (AmE), / θɜːd ˈvɛn.trɪ.kəl / (BrE)
a narrow cavity in the brain located along the midline below the corpus callosum that connects to the fourth ventricle via the cerebral aqueduct

Examples

The third ventricle cavity
Fluid filled the third ventricle during the MRI scan, allowing clear visualization of the brain's structures.
Obstruction of the third ventricle can lead to a buildup of cerebrospinal fluid and increased pressure.
The neurosurgeon explained that the tumor was compressing the third ventricle, causing the patient's symptoms.
