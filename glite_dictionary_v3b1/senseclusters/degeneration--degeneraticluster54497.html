degeneration

noun
decline in quality or vitality
1.7 6.0
Pronunciation: /dɪ.dʒen.ə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /dɪ.dʒen.ə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of declining to a lower state, quality, or condition (including moral or mental decline)

Examples

Moral degeneration
He feared the degeneration of moral standards in society.
Medical research focuses on preventing the degeneration of nerve cells in the elderly.
The degeneration of the city's infrastructure was evident after years of neglect.
Many critics saw the novel as a sign of moral degeneration in society.
Some believed that urbanization would lead to cultural degeneration and loss of tradition.
The degeneration of values was lamented by the older generation.

  • antonym: improvement - the process of becoming better
