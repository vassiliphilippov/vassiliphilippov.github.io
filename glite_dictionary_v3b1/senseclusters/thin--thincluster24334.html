thin

adjective
lacking substance or significance
3.8 4.2
Pronunciation: /θɪn/ (AmE), /θɪn/ (BrE)
lacking strength, depth, or substance; weak, insubstantial, or not convincing (used for things like arguments, sounds, or gestures)

Examples

Thin argument
She found the plot of the movie too thin to hold her interest.
The prosecutor's case was built on thin evidence that didn't convince the jury.
Their argument for more funding was thin and poorly supported by facts.
He offered a thin smile during the meeting, clearly not enjoying himself.
Her apology felt like a thin attempt to cover her true feelings.
The speech was met with only thin applause from the audience.
Her voice sounded thin and weak over the phone, almost as if she were whispering.
Music from the broken radio was so thin it barely filled the room.
The old speaker produced a thin, tinny noise that was hard to hear.

Related Terms

  • synonym: faint - (of sound) barely audible or very weak
  • synonym: flimsy - lacking strength or substance; unconvincing
  • antonym: full - (of sound) rich, resonant, and strong
  • antonym: genuine - truly what something is said to be; sincere
  • synonym: insincere - not expressing genuine feelings
  • antonym: strong - having strength or substance; convincing or significant
  • synonym: weak - lacking power or strength, both literally and figuratively
