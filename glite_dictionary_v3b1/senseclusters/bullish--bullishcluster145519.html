bullish

adjective
expecting a rise in prices (finance)
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊlɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbʊlɪʃ / (BrE)
Expecting prices, especially of stocks or commodities, to rise; optimistic about the future movement of financial markets.

Examples

A bullish market
Even during market volatility, her outlook remained bullish.
Investors are bullish about the long-term prospects of renewable energy.
The analyst became bullish on tech stocks after several strong earnings reports.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bearish - expecting a decline in prices (finance)
