catface

verb
to scar fruit
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkætˌfeɪs / (AmE), / ˈkætˌfeɪs / (BrE)
to cause a fruit, especially tomatoes, to develop scars or deformities, often due to frost, pests, or poor pollination.

Examples

To catface tomatoes
A late frost began catfacing many of the young tomatoes on the north-facing plants.
Improper pollination can catface fruit, making it less appealing for sale.
Gardeners try to prevent anything that might catface their crops during the growing season.

  • synonym: scar - to mark or damage the surface of something
