stockpile

verb
keep a large supply for future use
4.6 1.78/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆstÉ‘kËŒpaÉªl / (AmE), / ËˆstÉ’kËŒpaÉªl / (BrE)
to keep a large supply of something for future use or emergencies

Examples

To stockpile supplies
During the winter, many families stockpile extra food and water in case of emergencies.
Pharmacies often stockpile medicines before a flu outbreak to meet increased demand.
The company stockpiled raw materials to avoid shortages during the strike.

Synonyms

  • stock up
    2.9 2.06/m
    stock up means buying many supplies for future use, while stockpile implies keeping a large reserve for emergencies or long term.
  • store away
    2.6 0.21/m
    store away means putting things in a safe place for later use, while stockpile means building a very large supply for emergencies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deplete - to use up the supply of something
  • synonym: hoard - to accumulate and hide a supply of something
