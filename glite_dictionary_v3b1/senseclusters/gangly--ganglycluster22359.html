gangly

adjective
tall, thin, with long limbs
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæŋ.li /; / ˈɡæŋli / (AmE), / ˈɡæŋ.li /; / ˈɡæŋli / (BrE)
tall and thin, often with long, awkward limbs or a loosely built appearance

Examples

A gangly teenager
At thirteen, she grew rapidly and became a gangly teenager, all arms and legs.
Despite his gangly frame, the basketball coach saw great potential in him.
The gangly boy struggled to keep up with his more athletic classmates during the race.
She had a gangly frame that made her look even taller than she was.
The gangly puppy struggled to control its long legs as it ran.
The gangly teenager towered over his classmates at the basketball tryouts.
