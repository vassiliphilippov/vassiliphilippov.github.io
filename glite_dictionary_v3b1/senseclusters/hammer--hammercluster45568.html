hammer

noun
hand tool for striking objects
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /ˈhæm.ər/ (AmE), /ˈhæm.ə/ (BrE)
a tool with a heavy head used for striking things, such as nails or rocks (includes manual and power hammers)

Examples

A hammer and nails
He used a hammer to drive the nail into the wall.
Make sure to hold the hammer firmly to avoid hitting your fingers.
The carpenter picked up his hammer and began working on the frame.
In mining operations, a pneumatic hammer is essential for breaking apart large rocks.
Please wear ear protection when operating the power hammer in the quarry.
The workers used a hammer to bore holes into the granite wall.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jackhammer - a powerful tool used to break up pavement or rock
  • synonym: mallet - tool with a large, usually wooden head for striking
  • degree higher: sledgehammer - large, heavy hammer used for demolition
  • degree lower: tap - a light or gentle striking movement
