Rosa multiflora

noun
a species of wild rose with clustered small flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊzə ˌmʌltiˈflɔrə / (AmE), / ˈrəʊzə ˌmʌltɪˈflɔːrə / (BrE)
a species of vigorously growing rose with clusters of many small flowers, often used for hedges and as grafting stock

Examples

Rosa multiflora rose plant
Gardeners often use Rosa multiflora as a rootstock for hybrid roses.
The hedgerow was dominated by Rosa multiflora, its tiny white blossoms crowding the branches.
The rapid spread of Rosa multiflora makes it both a popular hedge and an invasive species in some regions.
