Huron

proper noun
Native American people
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈhjʊrɑn / (AmE), / ˈhjʊərɒn / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people originally living near the Great Lakes region of North America

Examples

Huron people
The Huron established villages along the shores of Lake Huron and Lake Ontario.
Many Huron traditions and stories have been passed down through generations.
French explorers formed alliances with the Huron during the fur trade era.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Iroquois - a group of Native American peoples historically based in the northeastern United States
  • synonym: Wyandot - a Native American people descended from the Huron
