renourishment

noun
restoring with material
6.3
Pronunciation: / riˈnɝːɪʃmənt / (AmE), / riˈnʌrɪʃmənt / (BrE)
the process of adding material, such as sand, to restore or improve a physical area, especially a beach

Examples

Beach renourishment project
The government funded a renourishment project to protect the coastline from further damage.
After years of erosion, the beach finally underwent renourishment to restore its width.
Environmental groups debated the long-term effects of renourishment on local wildlife.
The city approved a renourishment project to protect the coastline from future storms.
After years of erosion, the beach finally received much-needed renourishment last summer.
Environmental groups debated the impact of renourishment on local wildlife habitats.

Related Terms

  • antonym: erosion - the gradual destruction or wearing away of something, especially land
  • synonym: replenishment - the act of refilling or restoring something
  • synonym: restoration - the act of returning something to its original condition
