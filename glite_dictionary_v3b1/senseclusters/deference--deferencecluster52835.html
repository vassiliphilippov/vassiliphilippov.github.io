deference

noun
respectful or polite regard
4.8 6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛf.ɚ.əns /; / ˈdɛfərəns / (AmE), / ˈdɛf.ər.əns /; / ˈdɛfərəns / (BrE)
polite respect or submission to someone else's wishes, opinions, or feelings

Examples

Out of deference to others
He gave up his seat out of deference to the elderly woman.
In deference to her parents' wishes, she postponed the trip.
The staff spoke to the judge with great deference.
In deference to his teacher's wishes, he changed his research topic.
Out of deference to the elderly guests, everyone waited for them to be seated first.
She spoke with deference to the panel of experts during the interview.
In deference to his wishes, we postponed the discussion.
She showed deference to her elders during the meeting.
The soldiers bowed in deference to their commanding officer.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disobedience - refusal to comply with rules or someone in authority
  • antonym: disregard - lack of consideration or respect
  • antonym: disrespect - lack of respect or courtesy
  • synonym: respect - a feeling of deep admiration or esteem
