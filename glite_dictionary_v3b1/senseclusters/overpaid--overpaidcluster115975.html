overpaid

adjective
receiving too much pay
3.2
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈpeɪd / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈpeɪd / (BrE)
receiving more money than is fair or usual for a job or service

Examples

Top overpaid athletes
Many people think top athletes are overpaid.
Some believe that movie stars are overpaid for their work compared to teachers.
He felt overpaid for the simple tasks he was assigned at the company.

Related Terms

  • antonym: underpaid - receiving less money than is fair or usual for a job
  • degree lower: well-paid - receiving good or high pay for a job
