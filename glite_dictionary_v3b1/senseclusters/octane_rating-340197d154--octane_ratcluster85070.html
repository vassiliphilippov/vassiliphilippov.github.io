octane rating

noun
measure of gasoline's antiknock properties
7.4 3.7
Pronunciation: /ˈɑːk.teɪn ˈreɪ.tɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈɒk.teɪn ˈreɪ.tɪŋ/ (BrE)
A measure of the antiknock properties of gasoline, indicating its effectiveness in preventing engine knocking.

Examples

High octane rating
Choosing a gasoline with a higher octane rating can help prevent engine knocking in high-performance cars.
Most vehicles require fuel with a minimum octane rating to operate efficiently.
The octane rating displayed at the pump tells you how resistant the fuel is to premature combustion.
