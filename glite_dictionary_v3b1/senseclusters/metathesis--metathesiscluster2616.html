metathesis

noun
exchange reaction in chemistry
4.8 8.3
Pronunciation: /me.tə.ˈθiː.sɪs/ /; / /mə.tæ.ˈθe.sɪs/ (AmE), /me.tə.ˈθiː.sɪs/ /; / /mə.tæ.ˈθe.sɪs/ (BrE)
a chemical reaction in which two compounds exchange elements or groups to form two new compounds, such as AB + CD → AD + CB

Examples

Chemical metathesis
A classic example of metathesis is the reaction of silver nitrate with sodium chloride to produce silver chloride and sodium nitrate.
Chemistry students performed a metathesis experiment to observe the formation of a precipitate.
In a metathesis reaction, the ions of two compounds swap partners, yielding two new substances.
