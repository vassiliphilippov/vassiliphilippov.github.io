empiric

noun
practical observer
8.9
Pronunciation: /em.ˈpɪr.ɪk/ /; / /ɪm.ˈpɪr.ɪk/ (AmE), /em.ˈpɪr.ɪk/ /; / /ɪm.ˈpɪr.ɪk/ (BrE)
a person who relies on observation or experience rather than theory, especially in medicine or science

Examples

An empiric doctor
The early physician was considered an empiric because he based his treatments on direct observation.
Unlike the theorists, the empirics trusted only what they could see and test themselves.
In ancient times, empirics often clashed with those who preferred abstract reasoning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pragmatist - a person who deals with things sensibly and realistically
  • antonym: theorist - a person who forms theories rather than relying on practical experience
