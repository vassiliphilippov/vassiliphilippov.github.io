Ulmus americana

noun
large North American elm tree
Pronunciation: / ˈʌlməs əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnə / (AmE), / ˈʌlməs əˌmɛrɪˈkeɪnə / (BrE)
A species of large deciduous tree, commonly known as the American elm, native to eastern North America and noted for its graceful, spreading branches.

Examples

Ulmus americana tree
Many city parks once featured avenues of Ulmus americana before Dutch elm disease struck.
The Ulmus americana can live for several hundred years under favorable conditions.
The Ulmus americana is valued both for its shade and its majestic appearance.
