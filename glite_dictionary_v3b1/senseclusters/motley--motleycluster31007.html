motley

noun
multicolored jester's costume
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːtli /; / ˈmɑtli / (AmE), / ˈmɒtli / (BrE)
a multicolored garment or fabric, especially the costume worn by a jester in history

Examples

A jester's motley
Centuries ago, performers wore motley to entertain the royal household.
Her costume, inspired by a traditional motley, was vivid with contrasting patches.
The court jester appeared in his brightly colored motley and cap with bells.
In medieval times, court entertainers were often dressed in motley.
The jester wore a suit made of motley that brightened the gray stone hall.
The original motley fabric was woven to create a lively, patchwork appearance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monochrome - in one color or shades of one color
  • synonym: particolored - having different colors in patches
