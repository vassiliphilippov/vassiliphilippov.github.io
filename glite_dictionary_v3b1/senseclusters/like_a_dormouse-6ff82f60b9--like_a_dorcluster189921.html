like a dormouse

adverb
very deeply or soundly asleep
8.1
Pronunciation: / laɪk ə ˈdɔrˌmaʊs / (AmE), / laɪk ə ˈdɔːmaʊs / (BrE)
(of a person) sleeping very deeply or soundly, similar to how a dormouse hibernates.

Examples

Sleep like a dormouse
After the long hike, he slept like a dormouse all night.
Babies often sleep like a dormouse after a warm bath.
She curled up on the sofa and was soon sleeping like a dormouse.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sleep like a log - to sleep very deeply or soundly
