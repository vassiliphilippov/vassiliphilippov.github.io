Galenic

adjective
of Galen's medical system
Pronunciation: / ɡəˈlɛnɪk / (AmE), / ɡəˈliːnɪk / (BrE)
relating to the ancient Greek physician Galen or to his theories and system of medicine

Examples

Galenic medicine
The Galenic tradition shaped medical practice in Europe for over a thousand years.
Many medieval doctors followed Galenic theories about the balance of bodily humors.
Modern medicine has moved beyond Galenic ideas, but his influence is still studied today.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Hippocratic - relating to Hippocrates or his medical teachings
