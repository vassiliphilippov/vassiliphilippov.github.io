Indian rhubarb

noun
North American herb with large leaves and pink flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈɪndiən ˈruːbɑːrb / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən ˈruːbɑːb / (BrE)
a perennial herb (Darmera peltata) with large shield-like leaves and clusters of white to bright pink flowers, native to stream banks in the Sierra Nevada, California

Examples

Indian rhubarb flowers bloom
Gardeners prize the Indian rhubarb for its striking pink flower clusters in spring.
The broad leaves of the Indian rhubarb create dramatic shadows along the riverbank.
We spotted Indian rhubarb flourishing beside the streams in the Sierra Nevada.
