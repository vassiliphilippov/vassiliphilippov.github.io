thyristor

noun
electronic switch
8.5
Pronunciation: /ˈθaɪ.rɪs.tər/ (AmE), /ˈθaɪ.rɪ.stə/ (BrE)
an electronic component that acts as a switch, allowing or blocking the flow of electrical current

Examples

A thyristor switch
The engineer replaced the faulty thyristor to restore the machine's power control.
A thyristor is often used in circuits that require precise switching of high voltages.
Modern light dimmers commonly use a thyristor to adjust brightness smoothly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: switch - a device for making and breaking the connection in an electric circuit
  • synonym: transistor - a semiconductor device used to amplify or switch electronic signals
