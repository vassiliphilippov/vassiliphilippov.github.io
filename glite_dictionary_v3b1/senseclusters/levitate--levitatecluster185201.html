levitate

verb
rise or float in the air
6.5 6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈlev.ɪ.teɪt/ (AmE), /ˈlev.ɪ.teɪt/ (BrE)
to rise or cause something to rise and float in the air, apparently defying gravity, often by supernatural or magical means

Examples

To levitate above ground
In the film, the young girl seemed to levitate out of her bed during the haunting.
Some people believe that intense meditation can enable a person to levitate.
The magician appeared to levitate above the stage, stunning the audience.
In the performance, the assistant appeared to levitate above the stage.
Some people claim to have the power to levitate objects with their minds.
The magician managed to levitate a table in front of the amazed audience.
