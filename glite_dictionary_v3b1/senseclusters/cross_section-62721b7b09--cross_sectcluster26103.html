cross section

noun

(physics) A measure of the probability that a specific interaction, such as capture or ionization, will occur between particles, typically quantified in barns.

Examples

Particle cross section
A larger cross section indicates a higher probability of interaction between the particles.
The total cross section for neutron capture was measured in the experiment.
Understanding the cross section is crucial for calculating reaction rates in nuclear physics.
