immortalization

noun
cells dividing indefinitely
Pronunciation: / ɪˌmɔːrtələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌmɔːtəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process in cell biology by which cells are changed or selected to divide indefinitely and avoid aging

Examples

Cell immortalization
The immortalization of certain cells allows scientists to study diseases over many generations.
Researchers are investigating the immortalization process to better understand cancer development.
By introducing specific genes, the immortalization of the cell line was successfully achieved.

Related Terms

  • synonym: immortality - the state of living or lasting forever
  • antonym: senescence - the process of aging in cells
