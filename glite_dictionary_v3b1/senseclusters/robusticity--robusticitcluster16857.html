robusticity

noun
strength of structure
Pronunciation: / roʊˌbʌˈstɪsəti / (AmE), / rəʊˌbʌˈstɪsəti / (BrE)
the quality of being strong, sturdy, or well-built, especially in bones or body structure

Examples

Bone robusticity
The robusticity of the ancient skeleton indicates a life of hard physical labor.
Scientists measured the robusticity of the bones to compare different human populations.
In anthropology, robusticity is often used to describe the strength of skeletal remains.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fragility - the quality of being easily broken or damaged
  • synonym: robustness - the quality of being strong and healthy
