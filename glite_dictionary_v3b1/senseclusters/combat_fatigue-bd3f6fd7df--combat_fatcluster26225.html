combat fatigue

noun
war-related mental disorder
1.5 6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmˌbæt fəˈtiɡ / (AmE), / ˈkɒmbæt fəˈtiːɡ / (BrE)
a psychological disorder affecting soldiers, caused by prolonged exposure to active warfare and severe stress; also known as battle fatigue or shell shock

Examples

Combat fatigue in soldiers
Many soldiers suffered from combat fatigue after months of front-line duty during World War II.
Military doctors were trained to recognize the early signs of combat fatigue in their patients.
Symptoms of combat fatigue include anxiety, nightmares, and difficulty concentrating.

Related Terms

  • synonym: post-traumatic stress disorder - a mental health condition triggered by experiencing or witnessing a terrifying event
  • synonym: shell shock - psychological disturbance caused by prolonged exposure to active warfare, especially bombardment
