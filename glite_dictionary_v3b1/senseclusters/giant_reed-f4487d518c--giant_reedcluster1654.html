giant reed

noun
a tall grass with feathery flowers, found near water
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒaɪənt ˌrid / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌɪənt ˌriːd / (BrE)
a very tall perennial grass with jointed stems and large greyish-white feathery flower clusters, often found by riversides and in ditches

Examples

Tall giant reed
Farmers are exploring the use of giant reed as a bioenergy crop due to its rapid growth.
Gardeners sometimes plant giant reed as an ornamental grass for its striking appearance.
The giant reed flourished along the banks of the river, towering above the other plants.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reed - any of various tall, slender-leaved grasses growing in water or marshy ground
