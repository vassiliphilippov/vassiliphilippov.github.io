verge on

verb
almost reach a state or condition
5.6 0.54/m
Pronunciation: / v…ùd í …ën / (AmE), / v…úÀêd í …ín / (BrE)
to be very close to or nearly at a particular state, quality, or condition, often something extreme or negative.

Examples

To verge on rudeness
Her comments verged on rudeness, making everyone at the table uncomfortable.
His excitement verged on hysteria when he heard the good news.
The movie's plot verged on the ridiculous, but it was still entertaining.

Synonyms

  • border on
    5.2 2.50/m
    border on is used when something is almost an extreme or bad state, while verge on is the common phrase.
  • verge
    5.1 0.70/m
    verge is used to say something almost happens or exists, while verge on specifically means being nearly in a particular state.
