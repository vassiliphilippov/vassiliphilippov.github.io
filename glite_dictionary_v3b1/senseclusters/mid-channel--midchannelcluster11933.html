mid-channel

noun
center of a channel
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪdˌtʃænəl / (AmE), / ˈmɪdˌtʃænəl / (BrE)
the central frequency or part of a communication, broadcast, or electronic channel, such as in radio or audio signals

Examples

Mid-channel frequency
The technician measured the signal strength at the mid-channel to ensure optimal performance.
Audio engineers often adjust the mid-channel to balance the stereo mix.
Interference was detected near the mid-channel, so the frequency was shifted slightly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: center frequency - the central frequency of a channel or signal
  • antonym: sideband - a band of frequencies on either side of the main channel
