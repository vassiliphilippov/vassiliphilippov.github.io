keep

noun
prison cell or stronghold room
Pronunciation: / kip / (AmE), / kiːp / (BrE)
A cell in a jail or prison, especially a strong room in a medieval castle used for prisoners.

Examples

Prison keep
Legends tell of captives who escaped from the castle's keep in the dead of night.
The keep was the most secure part of the fortress, used to detain important prisoners.
The prisoners were locked in the keep until their trial.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cell - a small room in a prison or jail for holding one or more prisoners
  • synonym: dungeon - an underground prison cell, especially in a castle
