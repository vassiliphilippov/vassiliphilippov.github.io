MMF

noun
money market fund
6.9
Pronunciation: / ɛm ɛm ɛf / (AmE), / ɛm ɛm ɛf / (BrE)
an abbreviation for money market fund, a mutual fund that invests in short-term, low-risk financial instruments to provide liquidity and modest returns

Examples

MMF investment
She decided to move her emergency fund into an MMF for better liquidity.
Many investors use an MMF to park cash temporarily between other investments.
The MMF offered a higher yield than her regular checking account.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mutual fund - an investment fund that pools money from many investors to buy securities
  • antonym: savings account - a bank account that earns interest on deposited money
