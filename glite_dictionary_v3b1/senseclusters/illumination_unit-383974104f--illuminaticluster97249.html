illumination unit

noun
unit for measuring illumination
4.0
Pronunciation: / ɪˌluːməˈneɪʃən ˈjuːnɪt / (AmE), / ɪˌljuːmɪˈneɪʃən ˈjuːnɪt / (BrE)
a unit used to measure the amount of illumination or light falling on a surface

Examples

The illumination unit lux
International standards use the lux as the standard illumination unit for lighting design.
The laboratory technician measured the light using an illumination unit called the lux.
To ensure proper visibility in the workspace, a minimum illumination unit is required.

Related Terms

  • synonym: foot-candle - non-SI unit for measuring illuminance
  • synonym: lux - SI unit of illuminance, measuring light per area
