procession

noun
theological concept of the Holy Spirit's origination
4.7 8.8
Pronunciation: /prə.ˈse.ʃən/ /; / /proʊ.ˈse.ʃən/ (AmE), /prə.ˈse.ʃən/ (BrE)
(theology) the origination and emanation of the Holy Spirit, especially as a doctrinal concept in Christian theology regarding the relation between the Holy Spirit, the Father, and the Son.

Examples

Theological procession
Scholars often debate the meaning and implications of the procession from the Father alone or from both the Father and the Son.
The council issued a decree concerning the procession of the Holy Spirit in response to doctrinal disputes.
The doctrine of the procession of the Holy Spirit is central to Western Christian theology.
