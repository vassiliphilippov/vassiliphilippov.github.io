narrow

verb
make more limited or precise
Pronunciation: / ˈnæroʊ /; / ˈnɛroʊ / (AmE), / ˈnærəʊ / (BrE)
to reduce or focus the range, scope, or possibilities; to become more focused or limited

Examples

Narrow the choices
Further investigation helped police narrow the list of suspects.
The committee worked to narrow the topic for the conference.
We need to narrow our options before making a final decision.
As the investigation progressed, detectives began to narrow their search to only a few suspects.
In her later years, the poet chose to narrow her studies to ancient mythologies.
Medical students often narrow their focus to a specific specialty during their training.

Related Terms

  • antonym: broaden - to make wider or more general in scope
  • synonym: focus - to direct attention or effort to a specific area or detail
  • synonym: specialize - to concentrate on a specific activity or subject
