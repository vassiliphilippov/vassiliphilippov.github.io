backtracking

noun
retracing steps
5.3
Pronunciation: /ˈbæk.træk.ɪŋ/ /; / /bæk.ˈtræk.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈbæk.træk.ɪŋ/ /; / /bæk.ˈtræk.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
the act of retracing one’s route or steps, especially to return to a previous point or correct a mistake.

Examples

Backtracking on the trail
After realizing they were lost, the hikers started backtracking to find the correct trail.
The detective’s backtracking of the suspect’s movements helped solve the case.
Sometimes backtracking is necessary when you make a wrong turn on a road trip.
