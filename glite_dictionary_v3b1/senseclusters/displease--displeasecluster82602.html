displease

verb
make someone annoyed or dissatisfied
2.9
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈpliz / (AmE), / dɪsˈpliːz / (BrE)
to make someone feel annoyed or dissatisfied

Examples

To displease someone
It would displease me if you arrived late without calling.
Nothing displeased her more than unfair criticism.
The manager's decision may displease some employees, but it was necessary for the company.

Related Terms

  • synonym: annoy - to make someone slightly angry or irritated
  • antonym: please - to make someone happy or satisfied
