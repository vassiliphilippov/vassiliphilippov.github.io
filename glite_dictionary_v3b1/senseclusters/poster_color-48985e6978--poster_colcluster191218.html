poster color

noun
water-based paint used for posters
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊstər ˌkʌlər / (AmE), / ˈpəʊstə ˌkʌlə / (BrE)
a type of paint consisting of pigment mixed with water-soluble glues or binders, used especially for posters and educational art projects

Examples

Poster color paint
She used poster color to create bright and bold artwork for her project.
The art teacher recommended poster color for the classroom because it dries quickly and is easy to clean up.
The vivid hues of poster color made the school play's scenery stand out.
Children often use poster colour to create vibrant school projects.
The artist prefers poster colour because of its bright and opaque finish.
You should let the poster colour dry completely before applying a second layer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gouache - A method of painting using opaque pigments ground in water and thickened with glue or size
  • synonym: tempera - paint made with pigment and a water-soluble binder like egg yolk
  • degree lower: watercolour - A painting method using pigments suspended in water
