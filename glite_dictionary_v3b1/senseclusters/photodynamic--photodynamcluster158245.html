photodynamic

adjective
light-activated effects
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊtoʊdaɪˈnæmɪk / (AmE), / ˌfəʊtəʊdaɪˈnæmɪk / (BrE)
relating to the effects of light on chemical or biological systems, especially in medical or scientific contexts

Examples

Photodynamic therapy
The photodynamic process is used in certain cancer treatments to destroy abnormal cells.
Researchers studied the photodynamic effects of sunlight on algae growth in the laboratory.
A photodynamic reaction can be triggered by exposing the chemical to a specific wavelength of light.

Related Terms

  • synonym: photosensitive - reacting to or affected by light
