wabble

verb
to move unsteadily
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwæbəl / (AmE), / ˈwæbəl / (BrE)
to move unsteadily from side to side or shake with an irregular motion

Examples

To wabble on the chair
The old chair would wabble every time someone sat on it.
If you don't tighten the screws, the bookshelf will wabble when touched.
The cart began to wabble as it rolled over the uneven ground.

Related Terms

  • antonym: steady - to make or become firm, stable, or regular
  • synonym: wobble - to move with an unsteady or rocking motion
