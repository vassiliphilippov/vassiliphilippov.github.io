defoliator

noun
insect that strips leaves from plants
9.9
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfoʊliˌeɪtər / (AmE), / dɪˈfəʊliˌeɪtə / (BrE)
an insect or agent that removes the leaves from plants, especially by eating them

Examples

Defoliators eat leaves
Farmers struggle to control defoliators that threaten their crops each summer.
Some defoliator outbreaks can devastate entire ecosystems if not managed quickly.
The gypsy moth is a notorious defoliator of oak forests.
