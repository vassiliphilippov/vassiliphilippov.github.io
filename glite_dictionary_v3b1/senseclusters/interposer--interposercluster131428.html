interposer

noun
person who interrupts
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈpoʊzər / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈpəʊzə / (BrE)
a person who interrupts or inserts themselves or their remarks into a discussion or event

Examples

A talkative interposer
The debate was disrupted by an interposer who kept voicing opinions out of turn.
During the ceremony, an interposer suddenly shouted a question from the audience.
No one appreciated the constant comments from the interposer at the meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interrupter - a person who breaks into a conversation or event
  • antonym: participant - a person who takes part in something
