interstitium

noun
small gap or space
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈstɪʃiəm / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈstɪsiəm / (BrE)
a small space or gap between objects or parts, especially in a general or non-medical context

Examples

A narrow interstitium
Dust collected in the narrow interstitium between the floorboards and the baseboard.
Tiny insects made their home in the interstitium of the old stone wall.
Light filtered through the interstitium between the curtains, casting patterns on the floor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interval - a space or gap between two things
  • synonym: void - an empty space
