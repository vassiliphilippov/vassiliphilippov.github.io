transposer

noun
music key changer
Pronunciation: / trænˈspoʊzər /; / trænzˈpoʊzər / (AmE), / trænˈspəʊzə /; / trænzˈpəʊzə / (BrE)
a person or device that changes the key of a piece of music

Examples

Music transposer
The pianist used a transposer to play the song in a higher key.
Many electronic keyboards have a built-in transposer function for easy key changes.
A transposer can help musicians adapt music to suit different vocal ranges.
The transposer adjusted the song so that the soprano could sing it comfortably.
As a skilled transposer, he often helped the band play pieces in different keys.
The choir relied on their transposer to adapt music for each performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arranger - a person who adapts music for particular voices or instruments
  • synonym: capo - a device used on the neck of a guitar to change its key
  • synonym: composer - a person who writes music
  • synonym: transpose - to change the key of a piece of music
