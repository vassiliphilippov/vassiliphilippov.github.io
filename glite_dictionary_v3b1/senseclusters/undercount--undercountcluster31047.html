undercount

verb
to count too few
5.6
Pronunciation: /ˈʌn.dər.kaʊnt/ (AmE), /ˈʌn.də.kaʊnt/ (BrE)
to count fewer people or things than are actually present or involved

Examples

To undercount people
The official report may undercount the number of people affected by the disaster.
If you undercount the votes, the results will not be accurate.
Some populations are often undercounted in national surveys due to lack of access.
If officials undercount the population, some communities may receive less funding.
The survey likely undercounted the number of people affected by the new policy.
Researchers worry that certain groups are often undercounted in national statistics.
If officials undercount the population, some communities may receive less funding.
The survey may undercount certain groups who are hard to reach.
Researchers warned that the data could undercount the true number of cases.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overcount - to count more people or things than are actually present
  • synonym: underestimate - to judge something as less than its actual value or number
