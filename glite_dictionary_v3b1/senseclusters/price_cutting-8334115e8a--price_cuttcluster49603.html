price cutting

noun
reduction of usual or advertised prices
4.6 2.4
Pronunciation: / ˈpraɪs ˌkʌtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈpraɪs ˌkʌtɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of reducing the price of goods or services below the usual or advertised amount, typically to attract customers or compete with rivals

Examples

Excessive price cutting
Excessive price cutting can erode profit margins and affect product quality.
Many small retailers struggle to survive when large chains resort to price cutting to attract more customers.
To stay competitive, the company engaged in aggressive price cutting during the holiday season.
