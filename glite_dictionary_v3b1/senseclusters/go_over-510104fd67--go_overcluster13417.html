go over

verb
be received/perceived in a certain way
3.6
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ ˈoʊvər / (AmE), / ɡəʊ ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
(of an action, event, or idea) to be received or perceived in a particular way by others; to have a particular effect or reaction.

Examples

The idea went over well
Her joke didn't go over well with the audience, who seemed confused.
His suggestion went over surprisingly well given how critical everyone was before.
I hope my proposal will go over better at the next meeting.
