coign

noun
printing wedge for securing type
9.9
Pronunciation: /kɔɪn/ (AmE), /kɔɪn/ (BrE)
An expandable metal or wooden wedge used by printers to lock up a form within a chase.

Examples

Printer's coign
He learned to set type and use a coign as part of his apprenticeship in the print shop.
Several coigns were required to keep the type from shifting during the press run.
The printer adjusted the coign to make sure the form was held tightly in the chase.
