recusation

noun
legal objection to a judge's impartiality
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛkjəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrɛkjuːˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌrɛkjʊˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
(Law) an objection or challenge to a judge or juror because of possible bias or conflict of interest

Examples

A recusation of judge
Her lawyer explained that a formal recusation was necessary due to the judge's family connection to the plaintiff.
The defense filed a recusation against the presiding judge, citing a potential conflict of interest.
The motion for recusation was reviewed and ultimately denied by the court.
Any party may request the recusation of a juror if there is evidence of bias.
The court considered the recusation request before proceeding with the trial.
The defense attorney filed a motion for recusation of the presiding judge due to a potential conflict of interest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: recuse - to disqualify oneself as a judge due to conflict of interest or bias
