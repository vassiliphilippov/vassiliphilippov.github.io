refeeding

noun
diet calorie increase
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈfiːdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈfiːdɪŋ / (BrE)
a planned period of increased calorie intake during a diet, intended to restore metabolism or provide a psychological break

Examples

Refeeding day
Many athletes include refeeding days in their diet plans to support their training and recovery.
After several weeks of strict calorie restriction, a scheduled refeeding can help boost metabolism.
Some nutritionists recommend refeeding to prevent the negative effects of long-term dieting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cheat day - a day when someone temporarily breaks their diet rules
  • antonym: fasting - the practice of abstaining from food for a period of time
