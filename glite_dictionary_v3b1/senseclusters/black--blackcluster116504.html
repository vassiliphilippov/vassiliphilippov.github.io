black

adjective
deliberately misleading (esp. propaganda)
Pronunciation: / blæk / (AmE), / blæk / (BrE)
(of intelligence operations or information) conducted with the intention to deceive; deliberately misleading or false, especially for propaganda purposes.

Examples

Black operation
Historians discovered old files describing a major black campaign during the war.
Much of the information released was later revealed to be black propaganda.
The government admitted to orchestrating black operations to confuse the enemy.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: gray - (of intelligence operations) having ambiguous or partially truthful content, between 'black' and 'white' operations
  • antonym: white - (of intelligence operations) openly attributed and typically truthful, in contrast to 'black' (covert, misleading)
