caesura

noun
pause in a line of verse
8.9 8.2
Pronunciation: /sɪ.ˈzjʊr.ə/ /; / /sə.ˈzjʊr.ə/ /; / /sə.ˈʒʊr.ə/ (AmE), /sɪ.ˈzjʊə.rə/ /; / /sə.ˈzjʊə.rə/ /; / /sə.ˈʒʊə.rə/ (BrE)
a pause or break in a line of poetry, or an interruption or pause in speech or activity

Examples

A caesura in poetry
In classical poetry, a caesura often divides a line into two parts of equal length.
The poet uses a caesura in the middle of the line to create a dramatic pause.
When reading aloud, observe the caesura to capture the poem's intended rhythm.
After a brief caesura, she continued telling her story.
The poet skillfully used a caesura to create emphasis in the middle of the line.
There was a sudden caesura in the conversation as everyone turned to look at the door.
