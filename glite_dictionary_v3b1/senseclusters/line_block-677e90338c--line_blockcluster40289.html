line block

noun
an engraved or etched printing block
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪn ˌblɑk / (AmE), / ˈlaɪn ˌblɒk / (BrE)
an engraved or etched block used for printing images, especially one in which the design consists mainly of lines

Examples

A line block print
A line block can produce sharp, detailed illustrations for books and magazines.
Early comic strips were often printed using a line block to capture the fine ink lines.
The printer made several copies from the original line block created by the artist.
