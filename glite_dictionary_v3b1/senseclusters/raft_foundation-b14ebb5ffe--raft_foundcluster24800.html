raft foundation

noun
type of building foundation slab
Pronunciation: / ræft faʊnˈdeɪʃən / (AmE), / rɑːft faʊnˈdeɪʃən / (BrE)
a large, reinforced concrete slab forming the base of a building, used to spread loads over soft or unstable ground

Examples

A raft foundation slab
A raft foundation helps distribute the weight of the building evenly over a large area.
The engineers decided to use a raft foundation due to the marshy ground on the construction site.
When soil conditions are poor, a raft foundation can prevent uneven settling of the structure.
