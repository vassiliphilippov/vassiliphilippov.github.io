Bromus secalinus

noun
weedy grass species often found in wheat
Pronunciation: / ˈbroʊ.məs sɛˈkæl.ɪ.nəs / (AmE), / ˈbrəʊ.məs sɪˈkæ.lɪ.nəs / (BrE)
A species of weedy annual grass native to Europe, commonly found as a weed in wheat fields and widely distributed in temperate regions.

Examples

Bromus secalinus weed
Bromus secalinus is often an unwanted weed in wheat fields across Europe and North America.
Careful monitoring is required to prevent the spread of Bromus secalinus in cultivated crops.
Farmers sometimes mistake Bromus secalinus for young wheat because of its similar appearance.
