contemporary

adjective
existing at the same time
Pronunciation: / kənˈtɛmpəˌrɛri / (AmE), / kənˈtɛmpərəri / (BrE)
existing or happening in the same period of time as someone or something else

Examples

Contemporary with events
Her poetry is contemporary with the rise of the modernist movement.
Mozart and Haydn were contemporary composers who influenced each other's music.
Several contemporary novels address the issues of the early twentieth century.

Related Terms

  • synonym: simultaneous - existing or happening at the same time
