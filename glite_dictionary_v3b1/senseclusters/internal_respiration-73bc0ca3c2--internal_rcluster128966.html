internal respiration

noun
gas exchange between blood and body cells
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtɜrnəl ˌrɛspəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈtɜːnəl ˌrɛspɪˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
the exchange of gases between the blood and the body's cells, where oxygen is delivered to cells and carbon dioxide is collected for removal

Examples

Internal respiration process
Internal respiration occurs in the body's tissues, where oxygen is delivered to cells and carbon dioxide is collected.
Biologists study internal respiration to understand how organisms generate energy at the cellular level.
During internal respiration, carbon dioxide is produced inside the cells and transported in the blood to the lungs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: external respiration - the exchange of gases between the lungs and the blood
