carry

verb
provide financial support
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkæri / (AmE), / ˈkæri / (BrE)
to support, help, or compensate for someone or something, especially financially or in a team

Examples

To carry a team
A generous grant carried the theater through its first difficult season.
For years, her parents carried her financially while she pursued her art career.
The company could not have survived if the investors had not carried it during the recession.
In the match, he carried the entire squad with his outstanding performance.
It's exhausting to always carry the group through every challenge.
She often has to carry her project team when others slack off.
After the third glass, I realized I couldn't carry as much as I thought I could.
He prides himself on being able to carry more drinks than his friends without getting tipsy.
It's impressive how she can carry her liquor and still stay completely coherent.
