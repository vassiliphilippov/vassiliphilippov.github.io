Pacific sturgeon

noun
large North American sturgeon species
Pronunciation: / pəˈsɪfɪk ˈstɝːdʒən / (AmE), / pəˈsɪfɪk ˈstɜːdʒən / (BrE)
a large food and game fish (Acipenser transmontanus) found in marine and fresh waters of the northwestern coast of North America

Examples

The Pacific sturgeon fish
Anglers from around the world come to the Columbia River hoping to catch a massive Pacific sturgeon.
Overfishing has caused steep declines in Pacific sturgeon populations in recent decades.
The Pacific sturgeon is prized both for its size and for its delicate roe, used to make caviar.
