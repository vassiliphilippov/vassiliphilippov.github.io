plotlessness

noun
absence of storyline
Pronunciation: / ˈplɑtləsnəs / (AmE), / ˈplɒtləsnəs / (BrE)
the quality of lacking a structured or traditional storyline in a literary or artistic work

Examples

Plotlessness in a novel
The plotlessness of the film made it difficult for some viewers to stay engaged.
Many modernist works are famous for their deliberate plotlessness and experimental style.
Critics debated whether the novel's plotlessness was a weakness or a creative choice.

Related Terms

  • antonym: narrativity - the presence of a narrative or story structure
  • synonym: structurelessness - the quality of lacking structure or organization
