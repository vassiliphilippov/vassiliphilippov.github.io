agnathan

noun
jawless vertebrate (e.g., lampreys, hagfish)
9.9
Pronunciation: /æg.ˈnæ.θən/ /; / /æg.ˈnə.θən/ (AmE), /æg.ˈnæ.θən/ /; / /æg.ˈnə.θən/ (BrE)
an eel-shaped jawless vertebrate, lacking paired appendages, including cyclostomes and some extinct forms

Examples

Ancient agnathans
Fossils of ancient agnathans provide clues to the early evolution of vertebrates.
Modern examples of agnathans include lampreys and hagfish.
Unlike most fish, agnathans do not possess jaws or paired fins.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cyclostome - jawless fish belonging to the class Cyclostomata, such as lampreys and hagfish
  • antonym: gnathostome - vertebrate with jaws
