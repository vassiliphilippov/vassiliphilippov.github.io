trochlear nerve

noun
the fourth cranial nerve controlling eye movement
Pronunciation: / ˈtroʊkliər nɜrv / (AmE), / ˈtrɒkliə nəːv / (BrE)
either of the two cranial nerves (the fourth cranial nerves) that control the superior oblique muscles of the eyes, enabling downward and lateral eye movement

Examples

Trochlear nerve controls eyes
A lesion affecting the trochlear nerve may cause the affected eye to drift upward.
Damage to the trochlear nerve can result in double vision and difficulty moving the eye downward.
The ophthalmologist tested the function of the patient's trochlear nerve during the eye exam.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cranial nerve - one of the twelve pairs of nerves that originate in the brain
