inheritance

noun
property, title, or office received after death
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈhɛrɪtəns / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɛrɪtəns / (BrE)
property, money, or a title received from someone after they die, or the act of receiving it

Examples

A large inheritance
After his uncle passed away, James received a large inheritance that included both money and land.
She used her inheritance to start a new business in her hometown.
The dispute over the inheritance caused tension among the siblings for years.
After his aunt passed away, he received a large inheritance that changed his life.
She invested her inheritance wisely to secure her children's future.
The family disputed the division of the inheritance for several years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bequest - a gift of personal property by will
  • antonym: disinheritance - the act of depriving someone of a share in an inheritance
  • synonym: heritage - something inherited from the past, especially traditions or cultural property
  • synonym: legacy - something left to a person by a will
