chance

noun
possibility of success or opportunity
Pronunciation: / tʃæns / (AmE), / tʃɑːns / (BrE)
the possibility or likelihood that something will happen, including opportunities or risks

Examples

A chance to win
If you work hard, you'll have a better chance of getting promoted.
She knew this was her only chance to win the competition.
There is still a chance that he will recover fully from his injury.
I didn't want to miss the chance to see my favorite band live.
There's a good chance it will rain this afternoon, so bring an umbrella.
This job interview is my best chance to start a new career.
The chance of winning the lottery is extremely low.
There is a good chance of rain tomorrow, so don't forget your umbrella.
You increase your chance of success by preparing thoroughly.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: certainty - something that is sure to happen
  • synonym: opportunity - a time or set of circumstances that makes it possible to do something
  • degree lower: possibility - the state or fact of being possible
  • synonym: probability - the extent to which something is likely to happen
  • antonym: risk - the possibility that something bad may happen
  • antonym: safety - freedom from risk or danger
