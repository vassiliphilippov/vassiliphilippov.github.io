American Indian Day

noun
US holiday honoring Native Americans
Pronunciation: / əˌmɛrɪkən ˈɪndiən deɪ / (AmE), / əˌmɛrɪkən ˈɪndiən deɪ / (BrE)
In the United States, a day observed on the fourth Friday in September to honor and recognize the contributions of Native Americans.

Examples

American Indian Day celebration
Many schools host cultural events to celebrate American Indian Day each year.
On American Indian Day, there were traditional music and dance performances at the city center.
State governors often issue proclamations recognizing American Indian Day in September.
