cocoon

noun
silky envelope spun by insect larvae or spiders
4.0 2.6
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈkuːn/ (AmE), /kə.ˈkuːn/ (BrE)
a silky covering spun by the larvae of many insects to protect themselves during the pupal stage, or by spiders to protect their eggs

Examples

A silkworm cocoon
Spiders wrap their eggs in a protective cocoon to shield them from predators.
The silkworm spins a cocoon of fine silk around itself.
We found a cocoon hanging from a branch in the garden.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chrysalis - the pupal stage of butterflies; often inside a cocoon
