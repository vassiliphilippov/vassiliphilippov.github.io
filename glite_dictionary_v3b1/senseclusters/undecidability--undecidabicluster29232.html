undecidability

noun
impossibility to decide
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndɪˌsaɪdəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌʌndɪˌsaɪdəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being impossible to decide or determine due to insufficient or inconclusive evidence

Examples

Undecidability of the case
The undecidability of the problem left the scientists searching for more data.
Legal scholars debated the undecidability of the case due to conflicting evidence.
In philosophy, undecidability often arises when arguments are equally strong on both sides.

Related Terms

  • antonym: certainty - the state of being sure or definite
  • synonym: indeterminacy - the state of not being precisely determined or fixed
