panchromatic

adjective
sensitive to all colors
Pronunciation: / ˌpænkrəˈmætɪk / (AmE), / ˌpænkrəˈmætɪk / (BrE)
sensitive to all visible colors of light, especially in reference to photographic film or sensors

Examples

Panchromatic film
The new camera uses a panchromatic sensor to capture images in true-to-life colors.
Early photographers preferred panchromatic film for its ability to record the full spectrum of visible light.
Satellite images taken with panchromatic technology provide detailed black-and-white photos of the Earth's surface.

Related Terms

  • antonym: monochromatic - sensitive to or showing only one color
  • antonym: orthochromatic - sensitive to all visible light except red, especially in photographic film
