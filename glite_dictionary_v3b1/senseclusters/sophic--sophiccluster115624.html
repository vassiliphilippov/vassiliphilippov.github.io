sophic

adjective
wise or learned
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑːfɪk / (AmE), / ˈsɒfɪk / (BrE)
relating to wisdom or knowledge, especially in an archaic or literary context

Examples

The sophic teachings
The ancient text was filled with sophic teachings that inspired generations.
Her sophic advice guided the community through difficult times.
In his sophic writings, the poet explored the nature of human understanding.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ignorant - lacking knowledge or awareness
  • synonym: wise - having or showing experience, knowledge, and good judgment
