hedge

noun
noncommittal or ambiguous statement
Pronunciation: / hɛdʒ / (AmE), / hɛdʒ / (BrE)
a statement that avoids giving a clear or definite answer, often to avoid commitment

Examples

A hedge answer
His response was so full of hedge that no one knew what he really thought.
Politicians often use a hedge to avoid making promises they can't keep.
She filled her answer with one hedge after another, refusing to give a clear opinion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: commitment - an explicit promise or firm decision
  • synonym: equivocation - use of ambiguous language to conceal the truth
