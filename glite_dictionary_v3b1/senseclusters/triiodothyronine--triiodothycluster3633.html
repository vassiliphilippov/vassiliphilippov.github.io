triiodothyronine

noun
potent thyroid hormone regulating metabolism
9.7
Pronunciation: / traɪˌaɪədoʊˈθaɪrəˌnin / (AmE), / traɪˌaɪədəʊˈθaɪrəˌniːn / (BrE)
A hormone produced by the thyroid gland that regulates metabolism, similar to thyroxine but with three iodine atoms and is more potent and shorter-acting.

Examples

Triiodothyronine hormone
An imbalance in triiodothyronine can lead to symptoms such as weight changes or fatigue.
Doctors routinely measure triiodothyronine levels to assess thyroid function in patients.
Treatment of hypothyroidism may involve replacing missing triiodothyronine.

Related Terms

  • synonym: thyroxine - another major thyroid hormone
