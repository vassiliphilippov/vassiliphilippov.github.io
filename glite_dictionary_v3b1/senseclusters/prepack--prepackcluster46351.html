prepack

noun
insolvency sale arrangement
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːˌpæk / (AmE), / ˈpriːˌpæk / (BrE)
an arrangement in which a company's business or assets are sold immediately after entering administration, usually agreed before insolvency proceedings begin

Examples

A business prepack
The company was saved through a prepack that allowed it to continue trading under new ownership.
A prepack can help protect jobs by ensuring a quick sale of the business.
The administrators recommended a prepack to maximize the value for creditors.

Related Terms

  • antonym: liquidation - the process of closing a business and selling its assets to pay debts
  • synonym: prepackaged administration - a formal process where a sale is arranged before insolvency proceedings
