backstage

adjective, adverb
hidden from public view or attention
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˌbækˈsteɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌbækˈsteɪdʒ / (BrE)
hidden from public view; not known or seen by the general public (used about actions, decisions, etc.)

Examples

Backstage talks
Many important decisions were made backstage during the negotiations.
Some of the most intense drama happened backstage, away from the public eye.
The press wasn't allowed to see the backstage discussions among the leaders.
Much of the negotiation took place backstage, away from media attention.
She worked quietly backstage to ensure the event went smoothly.
The actors waited backstage before making their grand entrance on stage.

  • antonym: onstage - in view of the audience; publicly visible
