self-effacing

adjective
modest; not drawing attention to oneself
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlf əˈfeɪsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf ɪˈfeɪsɪŋ / (BrE)
not claiming attention for oneself; modest and unassuming.

Examples

A self-effacing person
Despite her impressive achievements, she remained self-effacing in interviews and public appearances.
His self-effacing humor endeared him to colleagues and friends alike.
The scientist's self-effacing attitude often led people to underestimate her groundbreaking research.

Related Terms

  • antonym: confident - having self-assurance; showing certainty in one's abilities
  • synonym: modest - having a moderate estimate of one's abilities; not boastful
