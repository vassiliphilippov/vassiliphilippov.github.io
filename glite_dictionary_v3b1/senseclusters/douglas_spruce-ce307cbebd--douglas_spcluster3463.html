Douglas spruce

noun
a type of evergreen tree of western North America
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌɡləs spɹus / (AmE), / ˈdʌɡləs ˈspruːs / (BrE)
A tall evergreen tree native to northwestern North America, with short needles and egg-shaped cones; also known as the Douglas fir.

Examples

A tall Douglas spruce
A mature Douglas spruce can provide habitat for many species of birds and mammals.
The forest was filled with towering Douglas spruce that seemed to touch the sky.
They collected cones from a Douglas spruce for a science project about native trees.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Douglas fir - another common name for the same tree species, Pseudotsuga menziesii
  • degree higher: spruce - any tree of the genus Picea, often with similar appearance
