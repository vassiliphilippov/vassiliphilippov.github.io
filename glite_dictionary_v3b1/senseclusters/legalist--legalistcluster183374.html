legalist

adjective
strict about rules
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈliːɡəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈliːɡəlɪst / (BrE)
emphasizing strict adherence to laws or formal rules rather than considering moral or social factors

Examples

Legalist approach to rules
The committee took a legalist stance, insisting every rule be followed exactly.
His legalist interpretation of the contract left no room for compromise.
A legalist attitude can sometimes overlook the human side of a situation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flexible - willing to change or adapt; not strict
  • synonym: rigid - unwilling to change or adapt; strict
