Cypraea moneta

noun
shell used as currency in Pacific and Africa
Pronunciation: / saɪˈpriːə moʊˈniːtə / (AmE), / sɪˈpriːə məʊˈniːtə / (BrE)
A species of cowrie whose shell was used as money in parts of the southern Pacific and Africa.

Examples

The Cypraea moneta shell
Collectors prize the distinctive glossy appearance of the Cypraea moneta shell.
Historians have recorded the economic impact of Cypraea moneta in ancient Pacific societies.
The shells of Cypraea moneta were once widely traded as currency in West Africa.
