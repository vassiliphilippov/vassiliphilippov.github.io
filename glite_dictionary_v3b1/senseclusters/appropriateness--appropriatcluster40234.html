Synonyms

  • appositeness
    8.6 0.01/m
    appositeness is a formal, literary word for being well suited to a situation, while appropriateness is the common, neutral term.
  • suitability
    3.2 1.55/m
    suitability is the everyday, neutral word for how well something fits a purpose, while appropriateness sounds slightly more formal.
  • suitableness
    5.6 0.02/m
    suitableness is an older or less common form meaning fitness for use, while appropriateness is the modern, more usual choice.
  • aptness
    6.1 0.08/m
    aptness shows that something is especially fitting or timely in a situation, while appropriateness means general suitability.
  • fittingness
    7.3 0.05/m
    fittingness describes how something fits or matches its purpose in a practical way, while appropriateness covers general suitability.
  • properness
    6.5 0.01/m
    properness refers to correctness or social rules about behavior, while appropriateness focuses on fitting a situation or purpose.
