Navy Secretary

noun
US government position: head of the former Navy Department
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈneɪvi ˌsɛkrəˌtɛri / (AmE), / ˈneɪvi ˌsɛkrətri / (BrE)
the head of the former United States Department of the Navy, a government position that existed before being merged into the Secretary of Defense in 1947

Examples

The Navy Secretary position
Historians often refer to correspondence between the President and the Navy Secretary during wartime.
In 1945, the Navy Secretary played a crucial role in the administration of the US naval forces.
The office of Navy Secretary was dissolved when the Department of Defense was created.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Defense Secretary - head of the US Department of Defense; modern, combined version
  • synonym: Secretary of the Navy - official title for head of the US Navy Department; still in use under Department of Defense
  • degree lower: War Secretary - head of the former War Department; similar US role before 1947
