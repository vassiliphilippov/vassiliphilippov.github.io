port-access coronary bypass surgery

noun
minimally invasive coronary bypass surgery
6.0
Pronunciation: /pɔːrt ˈæk.ses ˈkɔːr.ə.nər.iː ˈbaɪ.pæs ˈsɜːr.dʒər.iː (AmE), /pɔːt ˈæk.ses ˈkɒr.ə.nər.iː ˈbaɪ.pɑːs ˈsɜː.dʒər.iː (BrE)
a type of heart surgery in which a coronary artery bypass is performed using small instruments and cameras inserted through small incisions, while the heart is temporarily stopped and blood circulation is maintained by a heart-lung machine

Examples

Port-access coronary bypass surgery procedure
Advancements in technology have made port-access coronary bypass surgery a viable option for many patients.
He underwent port-access coronary bypass surgery to treat his severe coronary artery blockages.
The cardiologist explained that port-access coronary bypass surgery would result in smaller scars and a quicker recovery.
