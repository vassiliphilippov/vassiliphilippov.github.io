Nazarite

noun
biblical vow-taker
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈnæzəˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈnæzəraɪt / (BrE)
a person who takes a special vow of abstinence or religious dedication, especially as described in the Bible

Examples

A Nazarite vow
Samson was a famous Nazarite known for his strength.
According to the Old Testament, a Nazarite avoided wine and cutting their hair.
The tradition of becoming a Nazarite involved making a vow of dedication to God.
After his conversion, he lived as a Nazarite, avoiding all forms of alcohol and tobacco.
The community respected her decision to become a Nazarite and dedicate herself to a life of abstinence.
He explained his Nazarite vow as a personal commitment to spiritual growth and discipline.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ascetic - a person who practices strict self-discipline for religious reasons
  • antonym: hedonist - a person who pursues pleasure as a goal
