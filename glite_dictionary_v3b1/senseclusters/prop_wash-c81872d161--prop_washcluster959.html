prop wash

noun
disturbed air from propeller or rotor blades
7.9
Pronunciation: / prɑp wɑʃ / (AmE), / prɒp wɒʃ / (BrE)
the disturbed flow of air or water driven backward by the rotating blades of an aircraft propeller or helicopter rotor

Examples

The prop wash flow
The helicopter's prop wash made it difficult to stand nearby.
The pilot warned of strong prop wash behind the spinning blades.
You should secure loose items before the prop wash shakes the area.
