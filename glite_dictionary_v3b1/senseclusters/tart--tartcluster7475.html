tart

verb
to decorate showily
6.9
Pronunciation: /tɑːrt/ (AmE), /tɑːt/ (BrE)
to dress or decorate something in a flashy, showy, or overly elaborate way, often to attract attention

Examples

To tart up a room
They tarted up the old building with bright lights and banners for the festival.
She tarted up her outfit with lots of jewelry and glittery makeup.
The restaurant tarts up its menu with fancy descriptions to attract more customers.
They decided to tart up the office with colorful posters and new furniture.
He tried tarting up his old car with shiny rims and decals.
The restaurant was tarted up for the grand reopening, with bright lights and bold decorations.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adorn - to decorate or add beauty to something
  • synonym: embellish - to make something more attractive by adding decorative details
  • antonym: simplify - to make something less complicated or less decorated
