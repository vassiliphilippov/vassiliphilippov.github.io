reimagine

verb
to create anew
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪˈmædʒɪn / (AmE), / ˌriːɪˈmædʒɪn / (BrE)
to think about, create, or present something in a new or different way

Examples

To reimagine a story
The director decided to reimagine the classic story with a modern setting.
Artists often reimagine old songs to give them a fresh sound.
The city plans to reimagine its public spaces to make them more welcoming.
The artist decided to reimagine the city skyline in bright, abstract colors.
Many filmmakers choose to reimagine old stories to appeal to younger audiences.
The chef reimagined traditional dishes by adding unexpected ingredients and flavors.
The artist decided to reimagine the city skyline in her latest painting.
Many companies are trying to reimagine their products for a digital world.
The author reimagined the fairy tale with a modern twist.

Related Terms

  • antonym: preserve - to keep something in its original state
  • synonym: reinterpret - to explain or understand something in a new way
  • synonym: reinvent - to create something again in a new form
  • synonym: rethink - to think about something again, especially to change it
  • synonym: revise - to change or improve something by making corrections or updates
