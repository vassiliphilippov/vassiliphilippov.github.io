modality

noun
category expressing necessity or possibility
Pronunciation: / moʊˈdæl.ə.ti / (AmE), / məʊˈdæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the way in grammar or language to express possibility, necessity, or obligation (such as using modal verbs or verb forms)

Examples

Grammatical modality use
Different languages use various grammatical means to indicate modality in their sentences.
In logic, modality classifies statements as necessary, possible, or impossible.
The sentence 'She might come tomorrow' expresses modality through the use of the modal verb 'might'.
Grammatical modality plays an important role in how speakers convey certainty or doubt.
In English, modal verbs such as 'can', 'must', and 'should' express different types of modality.
The modality of a verb can indicate possibility, necessity, or obligation.
