remineralization

noun
adding minerals to water
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌmɪnərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌmɪnərəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of adding minerals back into purified or demineralized water to improve its quality or taste

Examples

Water remineralization
The bottled water company uses remineralization to enhance the flavor of its products.
After reverse osmosis, remineralization is necessary to restore essential minerals to the water.
Some home filtration systems include a remineralization stage to balance the water's mineral content.

Related Terms

  • antonym: demineralization - the removal of minerals from water or another substance
  • synonym: purification - the process of removing impurities from a substance
