skinhead

noun
person with a shaved head; youth subculture member
7.4 6.9
Pronunciation: /ˈskɪn.hed/ (AmE), /ˈskɪn.hed/ (BrE)
a person, especially a young person, with a shaved or very short haircut who is part of a youth subculture, sometimes associated with distinctive clothing or certain social or political attitudes

Examples

A skinhead youth
He became a skinhead in his youth but left the movement after a few years.
Not all skinheads share the same beliefs or attitudes.
The group of skinheads stood on the corner, their shaved heads gleaming in the sunlight.
He joined a group of skinheads in his teens but later left the movement behind.
Many people wrongly assume that all skinheads hold racist views, although the group has diverse members.
The police arrested several skinheads after the violent demonstration on Saturday night.
During the 1970s, the rise of skinheads brought new styles and attitudes to British youth culture.
Several skinheads were spotted at the concert, dressed in their distinctive boots and suspenders.
The film depicts the lives of young skinheads struggling with identity in an urban environment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: punk - member of a rebellious youth subculture, especially in the late 1970s and 80s
