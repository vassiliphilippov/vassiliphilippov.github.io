Watergate scandal

noun
1970s U.S. political scandal leading to Nixon's resignation
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔːtərˌɡeɪt ˈskændəl / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌɡeɪt ˈskændl̩ / (BrE)
the political scandal in the United States during the early 1970s involving abuse of power, bribery, and obstruction of justice, which led to President Richard Nixon's resignation in 1974

Examples

The Watergate scandal
Many documentaries have explored the events and impact of the Watergate scandal.
The Watergate scandal fundamentally changed the American public's trust in government.
The resignation of President Nixon was a direct result of the Watergate scandal.
