lethargic

adjective
sluggish; lacking energy
Pronunciation: / lɪˈθɑrdʒɪk / (AmE), / lɪˈθɑːdʒɪk / (BrE)
lacking energy or enthusiasm; sluggish and inactive.

Examples

Feeling lethargic all day
After staying up all night, she felt lethargic and could barely keep her eyes open at work.
Patients with the flu often become lethargic and lose interest in their usual activities.
The heat made everyone lethargic during the long, humid afternoon.

  • synonym: sluggish - moving or acting more slowly than normal
