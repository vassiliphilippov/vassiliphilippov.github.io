Common Market

noun
European Economic Community; EU precursor
3.7 0.51/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëm…ôn Ààm…ërk…ôt / (AmE), / Ààk…ím…ôn Ààm…ëÀêk…™t / (BrE)
the original economic association of certain European countries formed to reduce trade barriers and promote economic cooperation; used especially for the European Economic Community (EEC), predecessor of the European Union.

Examples

The Common Market countries
Britain joined the Common Market in 1973, sparking significant debate in the country.
Many regard the Common Market as the first major step towards present-day European integration.
The six founding members created the Common Market to encourage economic growth after World War II.

Synonyms

  • European Economic Community
    4.3 0.32/m
    European Economic Community is the formal organization promoting economic integration, while Common Market is an older informal name for that union.
  • EEC
    5.2 0.64/m
    EEC is the common abbreviation used in official texts, while Common Market is the informal name people often used for the same organization.
  • European Community
    3.7 4.37/m
    European Community is the later, broader political grouping of member states, while Common Market refers to the earlier economic union.
  • EC
    5.8 7.76/m
    EC is the abbreviation for the European Community used in formal writing, while Common Market is a more informal, older name for that entity.
