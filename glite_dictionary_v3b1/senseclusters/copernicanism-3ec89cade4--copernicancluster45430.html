Copernicanism

noun
Kantian philosophy
Pronunciation: / koʊˌpɜrnɪˈkænɪzəm / (AmE), / kəˌpɜːnɪˈkænɪzəm / (BrE)
the philosophical doctrine that knowledge is shaped by the mind's structures rather than by objects conforming to the mind

Examples

Copernicanism in philosophy
Kant's philosophy is often described as a form of Copernicanism in epistemology.
The idea of Copernicanism shifted the focus from objects to the mind's role in shaping experience.
Many modern philosophers discuss Copernicanism when exploring the foundations of knowledge.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Kantianism - the philosophy of Immanuel Kant
  • antonym: empiricism - the theory that knowledge comes primarily from sensory experience
