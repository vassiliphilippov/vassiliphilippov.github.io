nonstaple

adjective
not essential item
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˈsteɪpəl / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈsteɪpəl / (BrE)
not considered a basic or essential item, especially in diet or trade

Examples

Nonstaple food item
In many countries, meat is considered a nonstaple food due to its higher cost.
The store sells both staple and nonstaple items to meet all customer needs.
During emergencies, people often focus on buying staples rather than nonstaple products.

Related Terms

  • synonym: luxury - something expensive or not necessary, often for pleasure
  • antonym: staple - a basic or essential item, especially a main food
