degras

noun
emulsion for leather tanning
Pronunciation: / diˈɡrɑ / (AmE), / ˈdeɪɡrɑː / (BrE)
a semisolid emulsion obtained by treating certain animal skins with oxidized fish oil, used especially in leather tanning

Examples

Leather degras treatment
After processing, the hides were soaked in degras to achieve the desired texture.
The boots are treated with degras to improve their durability and resistance to water.
Traditional tanneries often use degras to soften and waterproof high-quality leather.
