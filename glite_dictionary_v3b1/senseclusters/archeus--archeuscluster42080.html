archeus

noun
vital force in old medicine
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɑːrˈkiːəs / (AmE), / ɑːˈkiːəs / (BrE)
a vital force or spirit once believed to control bodily functions and health

Examples

The archeus force
In ancient medical texts, the archeus was believed to guide the body's natural processes.
Some early physicians attributed healing to the mysterious power of the archeus.
The theory of the archeus is no longer accepted in modern medicine.

Related Terms

  • synonym: spirit - a non-physical force or essence thought to animate living beings
  • synonym: vitalism - the belief that living things are governed by a vital force
