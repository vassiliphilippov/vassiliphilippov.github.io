wise

adjective
having good judgment or knowledge
Pronunciation: / waɪz / (AmE), / waɪz / (BrE)
having or showing good judgment, knowledge, or understanding, especially as a result of experience

Examples

Wise advice
His wise words helped calm everyone during the crisis.
It is wise to start saving money early for retirement.
The committee relied on her wise advice to make their final decision.
Her wise decision helped the company avoid major losses.
It is wise to save some money for emergencies.
The child surprised everyone with his wise advice.

Related Terms

  • antonym: foolish - lacking good sense or judgment
  • synonym: prudent - acting with or showing care and thought for the future
  • synonym: sagacious - having or showing keen mental discernment and good judgment
