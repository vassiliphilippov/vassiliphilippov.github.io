gnaw

verb
to gradually wear away or deteriorate
6.4 4.9
Pronunciation: / nɔ / (AmE), / nɔː / (BrE)
(of something abstract, such as feelings or conditions) to gradually wear away, erode, or deteriorate.

Examples

Worries gnaw at you

Persistent anxiety can gnaw away at your peace of mind.
Years of criticism began to gnaw at his self-esteem.

Related Terms
