gnaw

verb
to gradually wear away or deteriorate
4.9 1.51/m
Pronunciation: / n…î / (AmE), / n…îÀê / (BrE)
(of something abstract, such as feelings or conditions) to gradually wear away, erode, or deteriorate.

Examples

Worries gnaw at you
Doubt continued to gnaw at her mind until she found proof.
Persistent anxiety can gnaw away at your peace of mind.
Years of criticism began to gnaw at his self-esteem.

Synonyms

  • gnaw at
    6.1 0.36/m
    gnaw at is a phrasal verb used for worries that slowly trouble someone, while gnaw means to erode or wear away.
  • erode
    4.2 7.93/m
    erode slowly weakens things over time in a formal way, while gnaw feels more active and personal, wearing away gradually.
  • eat at
    7.1 0.74/m
    eat at slowly eats away at someone's confidence or peace, while gnaw more generally means to wear away or erode.
  • eat away
    4.1 0.67/m
    eat away often describes chemical or physical damage that removes material, while gnaw often suggests slow, inner or emotional wearing.
  • wear away
    3.8 0.41/m
    wear away usually means rubbing or exposure removes a surface over time, while gnaw often means a slow internal erosion or decay.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deteriorate - to become worse or of less value
  • synonym: erode - to gradually destroy or be destroyed by natural forces; to wear away
