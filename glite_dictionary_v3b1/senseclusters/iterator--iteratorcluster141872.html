iterator

noun
one who repeats
Pronunciation: / ˈɪtəˌreɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɪtəˌreɪtə / (BrE)
a person or thing that repeats an action, process, or set of words, especially in a systematic way

Examples

A mechanical iterator
The old tape recorder acted as an iterator, replaying the same phrase over and over during testing.
In the experiment, the iterator was programmed to repeat the sequence until a result was found.
As an iterator of instructions, the robot performed the same task multiple times without error.

Related Terms

  • antonym: originator - a person who creates or begins something
  • synonym: repeater - a person or device that repeats something
