slip

noun
an unexpected sliding movement
Pronunciation: / slɪp / (AmE), / slɪp / (BrE)
an act of sliding or losing balance, often by accident

Examples

A slip on ice
He had a slip on the icy sidewalk but managed to catch himself.
Her slip on the wet floor left her feeling embarrassed.
The gymnast recovered quickly after a small slip during her routine.
Despite their efforts, the thief made a clean slip from custody.
The suspect gave the police the slip by disappearing into the crowd.
With a quick turn, the fox managed a clever slip from the hunters.
Be careful on the wet pavement, or you might have a slip and hurt yourself.
Her slip on the icy stairs startled everyone, but she managed to catch herself.
The sudden slip cost him the lead in the race.

Related Terms

  • antonym: capture - the act of catching or being caught
  • synonym: escape - the act of getting away from imprisonment or danger
  • degree higher: fall - an act of coming down freely, often after losing balance
  • synonym: trip - an accidental stumble or misstep that may result in a fall
