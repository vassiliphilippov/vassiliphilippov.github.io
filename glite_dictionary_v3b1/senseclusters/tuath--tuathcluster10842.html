tuath

noun
Irish petty kingdom
Pronunciation: / tuːə / (AmE), / tuːə / (BrE)
a territory or small kingdom in ancient or medieval Ireland, typically ruled by a local chief or king

Examples

A tuath kingdom
In early medieval Ireland, each tuath had its own laws and customs.
The boundaries of a tuath were often marked by rivers or forests.
A powerful chief could unite several tuatha under his rule.
