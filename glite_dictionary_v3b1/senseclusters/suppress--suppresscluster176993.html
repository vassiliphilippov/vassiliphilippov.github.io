suppress

verb
put down by force or authority
1.6
Pronunciation: / səˈprɛs / (AmE), / səˈprɛs / (BrE)
to stop or put an end to something, especially by force or authority

Examples

To suppress a protest
Student protests were suppressed by the authorities with mass arrests.
The police brutally suppress any demonstration that challenges the government.
The regime tried to suppress all forms of political opposition.
She struggled to suppress her laughter during the solemn ceremony.
The government moved quickly to suppress the rebellion before it could spread.
The new regulations are designed to suppress illegal trading activities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope (opposite in sense)
  • synonym: oppress - to keep someone in subservience and hardship, especially by unjust exercise of authority
  • synonym: quell - to put an end to, typically by force
  • synonym: repress - to subdue someone or something by force
  • synonym: subdue - to bring under control, often by force
