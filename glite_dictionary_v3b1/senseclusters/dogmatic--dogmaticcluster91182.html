dogmatic

adjective
relating to dogma; opinionated
6.2 7.9
Pronunciation: / dɔɡˈmætɪk /; / dɔgˈmætɪk / (AmE), / dɒɡˈmætɪk /; / dɒgˈmætɪk / (BrE)
expressing opinions or beliefs as if they are certainly correct and not considering other people's views or evidence

Examples

A dogmatic opinion
Her dogmatic approach to teaching left little room for discussion or debate.
Some critics argue that the organization's statements are overly dogmatic and dismissive of alternative views.
The philosopher is known for his dogmatic writings about morality.
Although he was knowledgeable, his dogmatic opinions often stifled debate during meetings.
Critics accused the leader of being too dogmatic in enforcing party doctrine.
Her dogmatic approach to teaching left little room for student questions or alternative viewpoints.
His dogmatic statements left little room for discussion or debate.
Many people find dogmatic attitudes frustrating in group projects.
She has a dogmatic approach to teaching and refuses to consider alternative methods.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authoritarian - favoring strict obedience to authority
  • synonym: doctrinaire - imposing strict adherence to doctrine or theory
  • antonym: open-minded - willing to consider new ideas; not dogmatic
  • synonym: rigid - unwilling to change or adapt
