Islamization

noun
conversion to Islam
8.6
Pronunciation: /ɪz.lɑː.mə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɪz.lɑː.mə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of making people, societies, or institutions more Islamic, either by converting them to Islam or by introducing Islamic laws, beliefs, or practices

Examples

The Islamization process
The Islamization of the region occurred gradually over several centuries.
Some historians study the Islamization of local customs and traditions in North Africa.
Debates about Islamization often arise in discussions of cultural and religious change.
The government announced a new policy aimed at the Islamization of the education system.
Debates over Islamization have shaped the country's political landscape for decades.
Some citizens expressed concern about the rapid Islamization of public institutions.
The Islamization of the legal system changed many aspects of daily life in the country.
Historians debate the causes and effects of Islamization in medieval North Africa.
Some schools underwent Islamization by introducing new religious courses and practices.
