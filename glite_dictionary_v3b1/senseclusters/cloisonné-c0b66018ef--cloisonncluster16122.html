cloisonné

noun
decorative enamelware with metal compartments
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌklɔɪ.zəˈneɪ / (AmE), / ˌklwɑː.zɒˈneɪ / (BrE)
A decorative art technique in which colored enamel is separated into compartments by thin metal strips fused to a metal surface.

Examples

Cloisonné art technique
A craftsman demonstrated how cloisonné is made using thin wires and enamel paste.
She collects cloisonné jewelry because of its vibrant colors and intricate designs.
The museum displayed an exquisite cloisonné vase from the Ming dynasty.
