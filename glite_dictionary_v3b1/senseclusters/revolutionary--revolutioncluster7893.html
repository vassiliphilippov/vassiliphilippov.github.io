revolutionary

noun
supporter of revolution
5.8 4.23/m
Pronunciation: / Àår…õv…ôÀàluÀê É…ôÀån…õri / (AmE), / Àår…õv…ôÀàljuÀê É…ôn…ôri / (BrE)
a person who actively supports or participates in a political or social revolution

Examples

A revolutionary leader
Che Guevara is often celebrated as a revolutionary who fought for social change in Latin America.
Many famous revolutionaries have inspired people to challenge injustice throughout history.
The young revolutionary distributed pamphlets calling for an end to the monarchy.

Synonyms

  • revolutionist
    7.0 0.15/m
    revolutionist is a formal or older-sounding term for someone joining a revolution, while revolutionary is common and widely used.
  • revolter
    7.9 0.03/m
    revolter openly rebels in a revolt or local uprising, while revolutionary joins or supports a wider political or social revolution.
  • subverter
    9.9 0.02/m
    subverter works to secretly weaken or overthrow institutions, while revolutionary takes public action to start or join a revolution.
  • subversive
    8.1 0.41/m
    subversive seeks to undermine a system often by secret or gradual ways, while revolutionary acts openly to overthrow it.

Related Terms

  • antonym: counterrevolutionary - a person who opposes a revolution or revolutionary government
  • synonym: insurgent - a person who rises in revolt against authority, especially one not recognized as a lawful combatant
  • degree lower: reformer - a person who advocates gradual changes to improve a system
