lipase

noun
digestive enzyme that breaks down fats
5.8 7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪˌpeɪs / (AmE), / ˈlɪpeɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme in the digestive system that catalyzes the breakdown of fats into fatty acids and glycerol

Examples

Lipase enzyme
Doctors often test for lipase in the blood to diagnose certain digestive disorders.
The pancreas produces lipase to help digest dietary fats.
When lipase levels are abnormally high, it may indicate a problem with the pancreas.
