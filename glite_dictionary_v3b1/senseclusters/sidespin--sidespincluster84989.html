sidespin

noun
spin causing sideways curve
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪdˌspɪn / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdˌspɪn / (BrE)
a type of spin applied to a ball that makes it curve sideways as it moves through the air

Examples

Sidespin on the ball
The table tennis player used sidespin to make the ball curve sharply to the left.
Adding sidespin to your serve can make it much harder for your opponent to return.
Golfers sometimes unintentionally put sidespin on the ball, causing it to slice or hook.

Related Terms

  • antonym: backspin - spin that makes a ball rise or slow down after bouncing
  • antonym: topspin - spin that makes a ball dip downward in flight
