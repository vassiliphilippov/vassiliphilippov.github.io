Synonyms

  • scruple
    8.4 1.02/m
    scruple is a moral doubt before acting, while compunction can mean hesitation or guilt both before and after wrongdoing.
  • guilt
    2.4 21/m
    guilt is a clear feeling of responsibility after wrongdoing, while compunction often adds a moral hesitation that may prevent action.
  • self-reproach
    7.1 0.11/m
    self-reproach is blaming yourself and feeling guilty after an action, while compunction can be a milder moral hesitation.
  • remorse
    4.5 6.18/m
    remorse is deep, painful regret after a serious wrong, while compunction is a milder moral unease that may not be so intense.
  • contriteness
    8.4 0.01/m
    contriteness is deep sorrow and disciplined regret often tied to guilt, while compunction is a milder hesitation or uneasy conscience.
