methotrexate

noun
drug used in cancer and autoimmune disease treatment
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛθ.oʊˈtrɛk.seɪt / (AmE), / ˌmiː.θəʊˈtrɛk.seɪt / (BrE)
a toxic drug that inhibits cell growth and reproduction by interfering with folic acid; used to treat certain cancers, psoriasis, and rheumatoid arthritis

Examples

methotrexate treatment
Chemotherapy regimens for some cancers often include methotrexate as a key component.
Taking methotrexate requires regular blood tests to monitor for potential side effects.
The doctor prescribed methotrexate to control her rheumatoid arthritis symptoms.
