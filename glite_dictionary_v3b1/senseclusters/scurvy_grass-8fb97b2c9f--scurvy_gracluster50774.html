scurvy grass

noun
a yellow-flowered edible cress
Pronunciation: / ˈskɝːvi ɡræs / (AmE), / ˈskɜːvi ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a plant of the genus Barbarea, especially Barbarea verna, with yellow flowers, cultivated as a winter salad and sometimes used for its high vitamin C content

Examples

Scurvy grass salad leaves
Fresh scurvy grass can add a peppery flavor to winter salads.
Many herbalists recommend scurvy grass for its high vitamin C content.
Sailors once ate scurvy grass to ward off vitamin C deficiency on long voyages.
