reaction turbine

noun
type of turbine using pressure drop to generate torque
Pronunciation: / riˈækʃən ˈtɝːbaɪn / (AmE), / riˈækʃən ˈtɜːbaɪn / (BrE)
A turbine in which the blades are arranged to generate torque by utilizing the gradual decrease of steam or fluid pressure from inlet to exhaust.

Examples

A reaction turbine blade
A reaction turbine operates best when the pressure drop is distributed over the entire runner.
Engineers chose a reaction turbine for its efficiency with varying fluid flow.
The reaction turbine is commonly used in modern hydroelectric power plants.

Related Terms

  • antonym: impulse turbine - a turbine in which torque is produced entirely by the impact of fluid
