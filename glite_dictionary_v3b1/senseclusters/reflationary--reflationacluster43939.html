reflationary

adjective
stimulating economic growth
8.6
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈfleɪ.ʃə.rɪ/ /; / /rɪ.ˈfleɪ.ʃə.rɪ/ (AmE), /riː.ˈfleɪ.ʃə.rɪ/ /; / /rɪ.ˈfleɪ.ʃə.rɪ/ (BrE)
describing policies or actions intended to stimulate economic growth and increase prices after a period of deflation

Examples

Reflationary economic policies
The central bank introduced reflationary measures to help the country recover from the recession.
Economists debated whether the government's reflationary policies would lead to sustainable growth.
A reflationary approach was adopted to counteract the effects of falling prices.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deflationary - intended to reduce prices or slow economic growth
  • synonym: expansionary - intended to encourage economic growth
