herd

verb
to gather or drive into a group
5.0
Pronunciation: / hɜrd /; / hɝd / (AmE), / hɜːd / (BrE)
to move or gather people together as a group, especially in a way that is controlled or resembles animals

Examples

To herd people
Security personnel herded the crowd towards the exits after the concert ended.
The teachers herded the students onto the bus for the field trip.
We herded our sheep into the barn before the storm began.
During the emergency, people herded toward the nearest exit.
The sheep herded into the barn as the storm approached.
Tourists began to herd through the museum when it opened.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disperse - to scatter in different directions; to break up
  • synonym: gather - to come together in one place; assemble
  • synonym: round up - to gather together people or animals into one place
