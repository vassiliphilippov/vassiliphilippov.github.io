PCM

noun
digital audio encoding
7.1
Pronunciation: / pi si ɛm / (AmE), / piː siː ɛm / (BrE)
a method of digitally representing analog signals by sampling and quantizing, widely used in audio and telecommunications

Examples

PCM audio data
Most CDs use PCM to store high-quality audio data.
The engineer recommended PCM for its accuracy in digital sound recording.
Telecommunications systems often rely on PCM to transmit voice signals clearly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: analog - a continuous signal that varies over time
  • synonym: digital - using discrete values, especially in computing or electronics
