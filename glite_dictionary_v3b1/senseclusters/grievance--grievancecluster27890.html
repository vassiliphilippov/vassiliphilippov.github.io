grievance

noun
formal complaint about injustice or illegality
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡriː.vəns /; / ˈɡriːvəns / (AmE), / ˈɡriː.vəns /; / ˈɡriːvəns / (BrE)
a complaint or feeling that something is unfair or wrong, especially when it causes resentment or leads to a formal complaint

Examples

A formal grievance
She submitted a formal grievance to the board regarding the denial of her legal rights.
The employees filed a grievance against the company, citing unfair working conditions.
Unresolved grievances often lead to disputes between management and workers.
Addressing every grievance promptly can help improve workplace morale.
She felt she had a legitimate grievance after being unfairly dismissed from her job.
The workers filed a grievance with management over unsafe working conditions.
After years of silence, he finally voiced his grievance to the board.
Many workers harbor a grievance about the lack of proper safety measures at the factory.
She filed a formal grievance against her manager for unfair treatment.
