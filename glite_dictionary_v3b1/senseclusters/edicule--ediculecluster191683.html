edicule

noun
decorative architectural frame
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɛdɪˌkjuːl /; / ˈiːdɪˌkjuːl / (AmE), / ˈiːdɪˌkjuːl / (BrE)
a small decorative or sacred architectural structure or frame, often with columns or found in a church or temple, used to decorate an opening, mark a special place, or house a statue, relic, or tomb

A marble edicule stood at the center of the old cemetery, marking an important grave.
