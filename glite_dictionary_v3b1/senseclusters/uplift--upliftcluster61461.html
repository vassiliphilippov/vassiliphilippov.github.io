uplift

noun
incremental causal effect in marketing
Pronunciation: / ʌpˈlɪft / (AmE), / ʌpˈlɪft / (BrE)
(in marketing and analytics) the increase in a target outcome that is causally attributable to a specific action or intervention, measured relative to a baseline or control group.

Examples

Sales uplift
The campaign's uplift in purchases after the email push surprised the marketing team.
Analysts estimate the ad's uplift by comparing treated and control groups over the month.
We ran an experiment to measure whether the free sample created any measurable uplift.

Related Terms

  • synonym: incremental effect - the additional change in an outcome caused by a specific intervention compared with a baseline.
  • synonym: lift - the measured increase in a metric attributable to an action; used in marketing analytics similarly to 'uplift'.
