Gemara

noun
part of the Talmud; rabbinical commentary on the Mishna
Pronunciation: / gəˈmɑrə / (AmE), / gəˈmɑːrə / (BrE)
the portion of the Talmud composed mainly of rabbinical analysis and commentary on the Mishna

Examples

The Gemara study
Many yeshivas focus their advanced lessons on mastering the Gemara.
Scholars spend years studying the Gemara to understand the Talmudic debates.
The Gemara expands on the laws introduced in the Mishna.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: Mishna - the first part of the Talmud, compiling oral traditions
  • degree higher: Talmud - central text of Rabbinic Judaism consisting of the Mishna and Gemara
