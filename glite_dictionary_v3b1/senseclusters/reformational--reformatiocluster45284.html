reformational

adjective
of a reformation
Pronunciation: / rɪˌfɔːrˈmeɪʃənəl / (AmE), / rɪˌfɔːməˈneɪʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of a reformation, especially a significant religious or social change

Examples

Reformational change
The country experienced reformational movements that changed its political landscape.
Many historians study the reformational period to understand shifts in religious thought.
Her reformational ideas inspired others to seek social justice and equality.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conservative - favoring traditional views and values; resistant to change
  • synonym: reformatory - intended to bring about reform or improvement
