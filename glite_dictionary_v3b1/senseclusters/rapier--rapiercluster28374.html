rapier

noun
thin, sharp-pointed sword
Pronunciation: / ˈreɪ.pi.ər / (AmE), / ˈreɪ.pɪə / (BrE)
a thin, light, sharp-pointed sword with a narrow blade, used especially in the 16th and 17th centuries

Examples

A rapier sword
A rapier is often seen in historical reenactments and stage combat.
He admired the elegant design of the antique rapier displayed in the museum.
The duelist skillfully wielded his rapier, parrying every attack with ease.
