Nagari

noun
syllabic script for Sanskrit and Hindi
Pronunciation: / nəˈɡɑːri / (AmE), / nəˈɡɑːri / (BrE)
A syllabic script used in writing Sanskrit and Hindi, forming the basis of the Devanagari alphabet.

Examples

Nagari script
Hindi is often written using the DevaNagari script.
Linguists study Nagari to understand the evolution of South Asian writing systems.
The ancient Nagari script can be seen in old Sanskrit manuscripts.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Devanagari - A major script derived from Nagari, used for Sanskrit, Hindi, and other languages
