recombinant

noun
cell or organism from genetic recombination
7.8
Pronunciation: / rɪˈkɑːmbənənt / (AmE), / rɪˈkɒmbɪnənt / (BrE)
A cell or organism produced by genetic recombination, containing a new combination of genetic material.

Examples

A recombinant organism
Each recombinant carries a unique set of genes resulting from genetic exchange.
Modern medicines are often produced using recombinant organisms in the laboratory.
The scientist isolated a recombinant from the bacterial culture for further study.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hybrid - an organism produced by combining different breeds, varieties, or species
