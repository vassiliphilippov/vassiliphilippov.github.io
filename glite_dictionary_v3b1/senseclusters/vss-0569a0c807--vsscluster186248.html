VSS

noun
organic solids measurement
Pronunciation: / vi ɛs ɛs / (AmE), / viː ɛs ɛs / (BrE)
a laboratory measurement of the amount of organic matter suspended in wastewater or sludge, known as volatile suspended solids

Examples

VSS level test
The VSS value helps determine the organic load in wastewater treatment plants.
Engineers monitor VSS to assess the efficiency of sludge digestion processes.
A high VSS reading can indicate excessive organic pollution in the sample.

Related Terms

  • synonym: TSS - total suspended solids, a measurement of all suspended particles in water
