odynophagia

noun
painful swallowing (medical)
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: /oʊ.dɪ.nə.ˈfeɪ.dʒə/ (AmE), /əʊ.dɪ.nə.ˈfeɪ.dʒə/ /; / /oʊ.dɪ.nə.ˈfeɪ.dʒə/ (BrE)
Severe pain when swallowing, typically caused by a disorder of the esophagus.

Examples

Severe odynophagia pain
Chronic odynophagia can significantly impact a person's ability to eat and maintain nutrition.
The patient reported experiencing odynophagia after starting a new medication.
Unlike dysphagia, odynophagia is characterized by severe pain during the act of swallowing.
