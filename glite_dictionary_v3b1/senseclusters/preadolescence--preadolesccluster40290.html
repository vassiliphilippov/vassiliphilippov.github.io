preadolescence

noun
stage before puberty
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˌædəˈlɛsəns / (AmE), / ˌpriːˌædəˈlɛsəns / (BrE)
the period in a child's life just before the beginning of puberty, typically between late childhood and early adolescence

Examples

During preadolescence period
During preadolescence, children often become more independent and start to form stronger friendships.
Teachers notice that students in preadolescence may show sudden changes in mood and interests.
Physical growth can accelerate in preadolescence, even before the first signs of puberty appear.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: adolescence - the period following the onset of puberty during which a young person develops into an adult
  • degree lower: childhood - the period of being a child before adolescence
