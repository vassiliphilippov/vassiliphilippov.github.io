B vitamin

noun
group of water-soluble vitamins
7.4 2.7
Pronunciation: /biː ˈvaɪ.tə.mɪn/ /; / /biː ˈvɪt.ə.mɪn/ (AmE), /biː ˈvɪt.ə.mɪn/ /; / /biː ˈvaɪ.tə.mɪn/ (BrE)
any of a group of water-soluble vitamins that are essential for various metabolic processes and are found in many foods, originally thought to be a single vitamin but now known as several distinct vitamins (such as B1, B2, B6, and B12)

Examples

A B vitamin deficiency
Deficiency in a B vitamin can lead to health problems.
Leafy greens are a good source of B vitamin.
Many energy drinks contain added B vitamin for an extra boost.
