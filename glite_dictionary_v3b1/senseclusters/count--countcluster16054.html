count

verb
to consider; to rely on
2.1
Pronunciation: / kaʊnt / (AmE), / kaʊnt / (BrE)
to include or take into account someone or something as part of a group or when making a decision

Examples

To count someone in
The company didn't count on such strong opposition to their plan.
We must count environmental impact among our main concerns.
You can always count on me for help if you need it.
The charity counts over 5,000 members worldwide.
The team counted two Olympic medalists among its ranks.
We count her among our closest friends.
The report didn't count the impact of recent policy changes.
When making the final decision, we must count everyone's opinion.
You should always count personal feelings before acting on important matters.

Related Terms

  • synonym: consider - to think carefully about, especially in making decisions
  • antonym: disregard - to ignore or not consider as relevant
  • antonym: exclude - to leave out; to not include as part of a group
  • antonym: ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to
  • synonym: number - assign or recognize a numerical value or position
  • synonym: rely - to depend on with full trust or confidence
