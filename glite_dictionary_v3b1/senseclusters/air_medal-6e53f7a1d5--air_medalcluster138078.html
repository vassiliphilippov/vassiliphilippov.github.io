Air Medal

noun
US military medal for aerial achievement
Pronunciation: / ˈɛr ˌmɛdəl / (AmE), / ˈeə ˌmɛdəl / (BrE)
A United States military decoration awarded for meritorious achievement while participating in aerial flight.

Examples

The Air Medal award
After earning three Air Medals, she was promoted to captain.
He received the Air Medal for his heroic actions during the mission.
The Air Medal is often awarded to pilots and aircrew members for exceptional performance.
