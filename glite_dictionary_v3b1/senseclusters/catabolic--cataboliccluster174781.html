catabolic

adjective
relating to destructive metabolism
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkætəˈbɑːlɪk /; / ˌkæt̬.əˈbɑː.lɪk / (AmE), / ˌkatəˈbɒlɪk /; / ˌkæt.əˈbɒl.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to the process in living things where complex molecules are broken down into simpler ones, usually releasing energy

Examples

Catabolic process
A catabolic state can lead to muscle wasting and weakness.
Intense exercise may increase catabolic activity in the muscles.
The hormone cortisol has catabolic effects on body tissues.
Athletes need to balance their diets to avoid excessive catabolic activity, which can break down muscle tissue.
Certain hormones, like cortisol, have a catabolic effect on the body's metabolism.
The patient's muscles became weak due to a catabolic response caused by prolonged illness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: anabolic - relating to constructive metabolism or synthesis of complex molecules
