whittle

verb
carve or pare shavings from wood
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪtəl / (AmE), / ˈwɪtəl / (BrE)
to carve small pieces or pare shavings from a material, especially wood, typically using a knife

Examples

To whittle wood carefully
Carefully, he whittled a stick to use as a walking cane.
During the camping trip, she spent hours whittling by the fire.
He likes to whittle small animal figures from blocks of wood.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carve - to cut wood or another material in order to make an object or design
  • synonym: chip - to cut small flakes or pieces from a hard material
