Synonyms

  • by rights
    5.6 0.3/m
    by rights is used to say what is fair or proper in everyday speech, while by right refers to legal entitlement or formal claim.
  • rightfully
    3.7 4/m
    rightfully means having a moral or legal claim to something, while by right often stresses a legal entitlement or official right.
  • legally
    2.8 20/m
    legally means allowed or defined by law, while by right also suggests a just or personal entitlement beyond mere legality.
  • lawfully
    2.5 2/m
    lawfully means done according to the law, while by right often refers to what someone is entitled to by justice or law.
  • legitimately
    3.7 4/m
    legitimately is accepted or proper under rules or law, while by right stresses a legal or moral claim someone holds.
  • justly
    3.4 2/m
    justly means acting in a fair, moral way, while by right focuses on legal entitlement or what someone is owed.
  • rightly
    2.6 9/m
    rightly means correctly or as deserved, while by right points to legal or rightful entitlement, not just being correct.
