rotting

noun
biological decay of organic matter
3.4
Pronunciation: / ˈrɑtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɒtɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of decay and decomposition in organic matter, typically caused by bacteria or fungi

Examples

The rotting of vegetables
Improper storage can lead to the rotting of vegetables in a matter of days.
Scientists studied the rotting of leaves to understand nutrient cycles in the forest.
The rotting of fruit was accelerated by the warm, damp conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decomposition - the process by which organic substances are broken down into simpler matter
  • synonym: putrefaction - the process of decay or rotting in a body or other organic matter
