black greasewood

noun
a spiny shrub of western North America
9.9
Pronunciation: / blæk ˈɡrisˌwʊd / (AmE), / blæk ˈɡriːswʊd / (BrE)
a low, hardy, much-branched spiny shrub native to alkaline soils in western North America

Examples

Black greasewood shrub
Black greasewood is often found thriving in the harsh, alkaline soils of the western United States.
Livestock tend to avoid black greasewood because of its spiny branches and bitter taste.
Wildlife biologists study black greasewood as an indicator plant for certain soil conditions.
