init

noun
system initializer
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪnɪt / (BrE)
a process or command that initializes a computer system or program, especially during startup

Examples

Init process starts
When the server boots up, the init process is the first to run.
The administrator checked the init logs to troubleshoot the startup issue.
Many Unix systems rely on init to manage background services during boot.

Related Terms

  • antonym: shutdown - the process of turning off a computer or program
  • synonym: startup - the process of starting a computer or program
