monohydrate

noun
compound with one water molecule
Pronunciation: / ˌmoʊ.nəˈhaɪ.dreɪt / (AmE), / ˌmɒn.əˈhaɪ.dreɪt / (BrE)
a chemical compound that contains one molecule of water for each molecule of the substance

Examples

Monohydrate compound
Calcium chloride monohydrate is used in laboratories for various chemical reactions.
Creatine monohydrate is a popular supplement among athletes for improving performance.
The label indicates that the drug is supplied as the monohydrate form.
