watchful

adjective
alert and observant
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑtʃ.fəl /; / ˈwɑtʃfəl / (AmE), / ˈwɒtʃ.fəl /; / ˈwɒtʃfəl / (BrE)
paying close attention to possible danger or problems; alert and observant

Examples

Watchful eyes
Parents are often watchful when their children play near the street.
She cast a watchful eye over the proceedings, ready to step in if needed.
The security guard remained watchful throughout his entire shift.
Our teacher is always watchful during exams to catch any signs of cheating.
Security guards remained watchful throughout the event to prevent any trouble.
The mother kept a watchful eye on her children as they played in the park.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alert - quick to notice and respond to potential danger
  • antonym: inattentive - not paying attention
  • antonym: negligent - failing to take proper care or attention
  • synonym: vigilant - keeping careful watch for possible danger or difficulties
