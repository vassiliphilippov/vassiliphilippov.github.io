rein

verb
to slow or stop a horse by pulling the reins
5.8 4.0
Pronunciation: /reɪn/ (AmE), /reɪn/ (BrE)
to control, guide, or restrain someone or something, as one would with reins on a horse

Examples

To rein in spending
He reined in his excitement and waited patiently for his turn.
She quickly reined her horse as they approached the busy road.
The coach signaled the driver to rein in the horses outside the inn.
It's important to rein in your anger when discussing sensitive topics.
She reined in her excitement during the formal meeting.
The rider reined his horse to a stop at the gate.
He skillfully reined his horse before reaching the old bridge.
She tried to rein her impatience during the lengthy meeting.
The government must rein in spending to avoid a budget crisis.
