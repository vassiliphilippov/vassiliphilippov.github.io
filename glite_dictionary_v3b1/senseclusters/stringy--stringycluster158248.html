stringy

adjective
containing tough, fibrous strands (esp. in food)
Pronunciation: / ˈstrɪŋi / (AmE), / ˈstrɪŋi / (BrE)
(of meat or other food) containing tough, fibrous strands; difficult to chew or bite through.

Examples

Stringy meat
Overcooking can make beef become tough and stringy.
She pushed aside the stringy pieces of chicken in her soup.
The roast was so stringy that it was hard to eat.

Related Terms

  • antonym: tender - (of meat) easy to cut or chew
  • synonym: tough - (of food) difficult to cut or chew
