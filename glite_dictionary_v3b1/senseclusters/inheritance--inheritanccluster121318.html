inheritance

noun
immaterial attribute or tradition received from ancestors
3.9 3.1
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈher.ɪ.təns/ /; / /ɪn.ˈher.ə.təns/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈher.ɪ.təns/ /; / /ɪn.ˈher.ə.təns/ (BrE)
a quality, attribute, tradition, or right passed down to a person from their ancestors, especially something non-material such as a blessing or reputation

Examples

Cultural inheritance
His most valuable inheritance was the wisdom his father had shared with him.
Language is an inheritance handed down from generation to generation.
She considered her strong sense of justice as part of her inheritance from her ancestors.
