one-dimensionality

noun
the property of having one dimension
Pronunciation: / ˌwʌn.dɪˌmɛnʃəˈnæl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌwʌn.dɪˌmɛn.ʃəˈnæl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality or state of having only one dimension, as in length, without width or height.

Examples

The one-dimensionality of length
In this experiment, we only consider the one-dimensionality of the movement for simplicity.
Some critics argue that the one-dimensionality of the plot made the story less engaging.
The one-dimensionality of a straight line is a basic concept in geometry.
