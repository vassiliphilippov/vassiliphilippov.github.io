heart block

noun
condition disrupting the heart's electrical signals
5.3 0.12/m
Pronunciation: / h…ërt bl…ëk / (AmE), / h…ëÀêt bl…ík / (BrE)
a condition in which the electrical signals that control the heartbeat are partially or completely blocked, leading to slow or irregular heartbeats and sometimes sudden loss of consciousness

Examples

Heart block condition
Permanent pacemakers are often used to treat patients suffering from heart block.
Symptoms of heart block may include dizziness, fatigue, or episodes of unconsciousness.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with a severe heart block after he fainted unexpectedly.

Synonyms

  • atrioventricular block
    6.8 0.02/m
