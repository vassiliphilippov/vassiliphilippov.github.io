tease

verb
to annoy or provoke persistently
5.6 1.7
Pronunciation: /tiːz/ (AmE), /tiːz/ (BrE)
to mock, annoy, or provoke someone, often in a playful or persistent way

Examples

To tease a friend
If you tease the dog too much, it might get upset.
She didn't like it when her brother teased her about her new glasses.
The older kids would tease him every day on the bus.
Every time I wore my new glasses, my brother would tease me about them.
She loved to tease her best friend with silly jokes during lunch.
The children teased each other in the playground, but it was all in good fun.
Please don't tease your sister while she's trying to do her homework.
She was hurt when her coworkers began to tease her about the mistake.
The older kids would often tease him about his accent.

Related Terms

  • antonym: compliment - to politely congratulate or praise (opposite of tease in the sense of provocation)
  • synonym: harass - to trouble or disturb someone persistently
  • synonym: mock - to tease or laugh at in a scornful way
  • antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration
  • synonym: taunt - to provoke or challenge someone with insulting remarks
