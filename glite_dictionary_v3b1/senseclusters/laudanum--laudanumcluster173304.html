laudanum

noun
narcotic solution of opium
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈlɔdəˌnəm / (AmE), / ˈlɔːdənəm / (BrE)
A narcotic tincture consisting of an alcohol solution of opium, used historically as a painkiller and sedative.

Examples

Bottle of laudanum
He became addicted to laudanum after months of treatment for his injury.
The doctor prescribed laudanum to relieve her severe pain.
Victorian writers often referenced laudanum as a common medicine of their era.

Related Terms

  • synonym: opium - a narcotic drug obtained from the poppy plant
