Old Church Slavic

noun
early Slavic literary language
Pronunciation: / oʊld tʃɝtʃ ˈslɑvɪk /; / oʊld tʃɜrtʃ ˈslævɪk / (AmE), / əʊld tʃɜːtʃ ˈslɑːvɪk /; / əʊld tʃɜːtʃ ˈslævɪk / (BrE)
the earliest recorded Slavic literary language, used in the 9th century for early translations of the Bible and Christian texts

Examples

Old Church Slavic language
Linguists study Old Church Slavic to understand the evolution of Slavic languages.

Scholars often study Old Church Slavic to better understand the origins of Slavic languages.
