population control

noun
algorithmic management of candidate populations
4.3
Pronunciation: /pɑːp.jə.ˈleɪ.ʃən kən.ˈtroʊl/ (AmE), /pɒp.jə.ˈleɪ.ʃən kən.ˈtrəʊl/ /; / /pɒp.jʊ.ˈleɪ.ʃən kən.ˈtrəʊl/ (BrE)
(in evolutionary computation and other population-based algorithms) a method or mechanism for limiting or managing the number or composition of candidate solutions to balance computational resources and promote effective search or convergence.

Examples

Algorithm population control
The evolutionary algorithm implemented a new population control method to limit candidate solutions each generation.
Researchers compared several population control strategies to balance computational cost and solution quality on benchmarks.
To prevent bloating, the team applied aggressive population control after crossover and mutation steps.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pruning - removing less-fit or redundant individuals from a population in an algorithm to reduce size or complexity
  • synonym: selection - the process of choosing individuals from a population based on fitness or other criteria during evolutionary search
