palanquin

noun
covered litter carried by bearers
Pronunciation: / ˌpælənˈkin / (AmE), / ˌpælənˈkiːn / (BrE)
A covered litter, often richly decorated, carried on the shoulders of several bearers, used chiefly in South and East Asia for transporting people.

Examples

A palanquin carriage
Historical dramas often depict royalty traveling in a palanquin through crowded streets.
The bride was carried to the wedding ceremony in an ornate palanquin.
Their ancestors used to ride in a palanquin when visiting neighboring villages.
