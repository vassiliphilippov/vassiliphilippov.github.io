auditory hallucination

noun
hearing sounds with no external source
2.6
Pronunciation: / ˈɔdɪˌtɔri həˌluːsɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈɔːdɪtəri həˌluːsɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
An experience of hearing sounds that are not present, especially voices or noises, without an external source.

Examples

Auditory hallucination of voices
His auditory hallucination included the sound of footsteps following him when nobody was there.
Schizophrenia is commonly associated with auditory hallucinations such as hearing voices.
The patient reported experiencing frequent auditory hallucinations during the night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: visual hallucination - seeing things that are not present
