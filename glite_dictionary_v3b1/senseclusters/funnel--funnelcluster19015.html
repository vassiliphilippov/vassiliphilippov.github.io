funnel

noun
conical tube or shape with wide and narrow ends
Pronunciation: / ˈfʌnəl /; / ˈfʌn.əl / (AmE), / ˈfʌnəl /; / ˈfʌn.əl / (BrE)
a tube or utensil with a wide top and narrow bottom, used for pouring liquids or powders into containers with small openings

Examples

A funnel for oil
She used a funnel to pour the oil into the bottle without spilling.
The chef grabbed a funnel to transfer the flour into a smaller container.
The tornado formed a funnel shape as it touched down on the ground.
A plastic funnel is essential for filling bottles with homemade oil.
Pour the flour through a funnel to avoid spilling it on the counter.
She used a funnel to transfer the juice into a bottle.

  • synonym: spout - a tube or lip that liquid comes out from
