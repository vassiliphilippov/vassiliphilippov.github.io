tree tobacco

noun
evergreen South American shrub, sometimes poisonous to livestock
Pronunciation: / tri təˈbækoʊ / (AmE), / tri təˈbækəʊ / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub native to South America, naturalized in the United States, with yellow tubular flowers and leaves that can be toxic to livestock

Examples

Tree tobacco plant
Tree tobacco has yellow flowers and grows rapidly by roadsides throughout the Southwest.
Although native to South America, tree tobacco has become common in parts of California.
Farmers are careful to fence off pastures where tree tobacco grows, as it can poison their cattle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Nicotiana glauca - scientific name for tree tobacco
  • degree lower: tobacco - plant of genus Nicotiana, leaves processed for smoking or chewing
