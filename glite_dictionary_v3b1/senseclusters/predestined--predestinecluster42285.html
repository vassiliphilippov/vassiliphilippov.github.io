predestined

adjective
determined in advance by fate or destiny
7.7 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ËŒpriËˆdÉ›stÉªnd / (AmE), / ËŒpriËËˆdÉ›stÉªnd / (BrE)
(of an event or outcome) determined in advance by fate or divine will; established or arranged unalterably before it happens.

Examples

A predestined future
He felt his success was predestined, the result of plans made long before he was born.
Some people believe that their lives are predestined and cannot be changed by personal effort.
The two lovers seemed predestined to meet, as if their meeting was written in the stars.

Synonyms

    9.9 0.06/m

  • fated
    5.3 2.12/m
    fated is often used to show something seems caused by fate, while predestined implies a firm divine plan set beforehand.
  • destined
    4.1 0.15/m
    destined feels natural or meant to happen in life, while predestined suggests a fixed outcome set by fate or divine will.
  • predetermined
    5.3 0.00/m
    predetermined describes things decided earlier by people or rules, while predestined adds a sense of fate or divine choice.
  • foregone
    4.6 1.09/m
    foregone often appears as foregone conclusion to mean obviously certain, while predestined implies fate or divine will fixed the outcome.
