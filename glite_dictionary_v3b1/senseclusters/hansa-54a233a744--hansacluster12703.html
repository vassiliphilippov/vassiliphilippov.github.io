Hansa

proper noun
medieval trade league
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhɑːnsə / (AmE), / ˈhan.sə / (BrE)
a medieval association of merchant guilds and towns in northern Europe that promoted trade and economic cooperation

Examples

The Hansa League
The Hansa controlled much of the trade in the Baltic region during the Middle Ages.
Many northern European cities prospered as members of the Hansa.
The influence of the Hansa can still be seen in the architecture of old port towns.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Hanseatic League - a powerful commercial and defensive confederation of merchant guilds and towns in northern Europe
