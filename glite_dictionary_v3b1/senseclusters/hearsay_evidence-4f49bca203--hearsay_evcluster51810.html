hearsay evidence

noun
evidence not from direct knowledge
5.8 6.4
Pronunciation: / ˈhɪrˌseɪ ˈɛvɪdəns / (AmE), / ˈhɪəseɪ ˈɛvɪdəns / (BrE)
evidence introduced in a court that is based on what someone else has said, rather than what the witness personally knows; generally not admissible as proof.

Examples

Hearsay evidence in court
Most courts do not allow hearsay evidence except under certain exceptions.
The case was dismissed due to the lack of anything beyond hearsay evidence.
The objection was sustained because the testimony consisted of hearsay evidence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: direct evidence - evidence directly observed by a witness
