competent

adjective
adequate but not exceptional
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːmpətənt / (AmE), / ˈkɒmpɪtənt / (BrE)
adequate for the intended purpose but not outstanding or exceptional

Examples

Competent performance
His work is competent, but lacks the creativity needed to stand out.
She is a competent pianist, though not considered particularly gifted.
The actor gave a competent performance that met expectations but did not impress the critics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adequate - satisfactory or acceptable in quality or quantity
  • antonym: exceptional - unusually good; outstanding
