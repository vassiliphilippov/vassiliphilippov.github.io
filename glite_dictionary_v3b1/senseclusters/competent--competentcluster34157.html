competent

adjective
adequate but not exceptional
4.2 2.47/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëÀêmp…ôt…ônt / (AmE), / Ààk…ímp…™t…ônt / (BrE)
adequate for the intended purpose but not outstanding or exceptional

Examples

Competent performance
His work is competent, but lacks the creativity needed to stand out.
She is a competent pianist, though not considered particularly gifted.
The actor gave a competent performance that met expectations but did not impress the critics.

Synonyms

  • adequate
    2.7 28/m
    adequate means enough to do a job or meet a need, while competent suggests someone has the skill to perform it.
  • satisfactory
    2.3 7.11/m
    satisfactory is acceptable and meets a requirement or grade, while competent focuses on a person's real ability to do the task.
  • decent
    2.3 22/m
    decent means fairly good or acceptable in general everyday use, while competent stresses having enough skill for a specific task.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adequate - satisfactory or acceptable in quality or quantity
  • antonym: exceptional - unusually good; outstanding
