subiculum

noun
supporting fungal mat
Pronunciation: / səˈbɪkjələm / (AmE), / sjuːˈbɪkjʊləm / (BrE)
a mat or layer of fungal hyphae or lichen tissue that forms on a surface and supports fruiting bodies or the thallus

Examples

Fungal subiculum layer
The scientist identified a thick subiculum beneath the lichen's fruiting bodies on the tree bark.
A white subiculum covered the decaying wood, supporting clusters of tiny mushrooms.
The presence of a subiculum is important for the attachment of certain fungi to their substrate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mycelium - the mass of hyphae forming the vegetative part of a fungus
