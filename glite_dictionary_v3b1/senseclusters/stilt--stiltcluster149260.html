stilt

noun
supporting post for buildings
4.9 0.45/m
Pronunciation: / stÉªlt / (AmE), / stÉªlt / (BrE)
a long, upright post or column, especially one used to support a building above the ground or water

Examples

House on stilts
Many coastal homes in the area stand on stilts above the marshy ground.
The house was built on stilts to protect it from flooding during the rainy season.
Workers drove several stilts into the sand to support the new pier.

Synonyms

  • pile
    6.8 1.14/m
    a pile is a long column driven deep into the ground to support heavy structures, while a stilt raises a building above ground or water.
  • upright
    5.5 0.45/m
    an upright is a vertical support that forms part of a frame, while a stilt is a single post used to lift a building.
  • stanchion
    8.8 0.38/m
    a stanchion is a solid vertical post often used in fences or railings, while a stilt supports and lifts an entire building above ground.
  • post
    1.0 11/m
    a post is a general pole used for fences, markers, or light support, while a stilt specifically lifts and supports buildings above water.
