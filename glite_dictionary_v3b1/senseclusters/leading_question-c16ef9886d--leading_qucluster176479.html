leading question

noun
a question suggesting its own answer
5.4 4.2
Pronunciation: /ˈliː.dɪŋ ˈkwes.tʃən/ /; / /ˈliː.dɪŋ ˈkwes.tjən/ (AmE), /ˈliː.dɪŋ ˈkwes.tʃən/ /; / /ˈliː.dɪŋ ˈkwes.tjən/ (BrE)
a question that is phrased to suggest the answer that is desired, often used to influence or guide the respondent

Examples

A leading question in court
During cross-examination, the lawyer posed a leading question to the witness.
He was criticized for using a leading question that steered the discussion toward his viewpoint.
The teacher avoided asking leading questions so students could think independently.
