involute

adjective
closely coiled (shells, axis obscured)
Pronunciation: / ˈɪn.vəˌlut /; / ˈɪnvəˌluːt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.və.ljuːt /; / ˈɪnvəˌluːt / (BrE)
having a coiled, spiraled, or complex form or structure

Examples

Involute shell shape

The artist created an involute pattern that covered the entire wall.

Related Terms

  • antonym: evolute - (of shells) coiled so that the whorls are not closely coiled and the axis is visible
  • synonym: intricate - very detailed and complicated
  • antonym: simple - easy to understand or not complicated
