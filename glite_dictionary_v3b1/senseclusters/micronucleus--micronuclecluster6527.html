micronucleus

noun
small reproductive nucleus
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪkroʊˈnuːkliəs / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəʊˈnjuːkliəs / (BrE)
a small nucleus in certain single-celled organisms that is involved in reproduction and genetic exchange

Examples

Ciliate micronucleus
The micronucleus contains the genetic material needed for sexual reproduction in ciliates.
During conjugation, the micronucleus undergoes division and exchange between cells.
Scientists observed changes in the micronucleus under the microscope after the experiment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: macronucleus - the larger nucleus in some single-celled organisms, controlling non-reproductive functions
