mollify

verb
make less severe or more acceptable
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑː.ləˌfaɪ /; / ˈmɑːlɪˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈmɒl.ɪ.faɪ /; / ˈmɒlɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to make someone or something less angry, harsh, or severe; to calm or soften

Examples

To mollify anger
Adding a touch of honey can mollify the sharpness of the lemon juice in the recipe.
She tried to mollify her angry colleague by offering an apology.
The CEO’s statement did little to mollify public criticism about the company’s actions.
He brought flowers in an attempt to mollify his girlfriend after their argument.
Her soothing words helped mollify his irritation after the disagreement.
The manager tried to mollify the frustrated employees with promises of change.
A gentle breeze can sometimes mollify the harshness of a cold winter night.
Nothing could mollify his stubborn determination during the negotiations.
The company tried to mollify angry customers with a public apology.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aggravate - to make worse or more severe
  • antonym: anger - a strong feeling of annoyance or hostility
  • synonym: appease - to calm or pacify, usually by giving in to demands
  • antonym: harden - to make or become more rigid or severe
  • synonym: soften - to make or become less hard or severe
