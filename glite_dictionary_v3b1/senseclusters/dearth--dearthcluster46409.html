dearth

noun
a lack or scarcity; insufficiency
Pronunciation: / dɝːθ /; / dɜrθ / (AmE), / dɜːθ / (BrE)
a lack or not enough of something; scarcity

Examples

A dearth of food
A dearth of qualified candidates slowed down the hiring process.

There was a dearth of evidence to support the claim, making prosecution difficult.
A dearth of evidence made it difficult to solve the case.
The company faced a dearth of qualified applicants for the position.
There is a dearth of affordable housing in the city.
