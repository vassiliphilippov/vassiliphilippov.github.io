corner

verb
go round a corner (vehicle/driver)
4.0 0.43/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…îrn…ö / (AmE), / Ààk…îÀên…ô / (BrE)
(of a vehicle or driver) to go round a corner in a particular way, especially in terms of speed or control.

Examples

To corner quickly
Be careful on this road, as the truck doesn't corner easily.
Motorcycles corner differently from cars, so adjust your speed before turning.
The sports car corners smoothly even at high speeds.

Synonyms

  • turn
    1.0 238/m
    turn means changing direction by steering in general, while corner focuses on how a driver goes around a bend with speed control.
  • round
    3.0 5.57/m
    round means moving along a curved path, often without focus on technique, while corner emphasizes how a driver handles the bend.
