inexcusability

noun
impossible to excuse
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnɪkˌskjuːzəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / ˌɪnɪkˌskjuːzəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being impossible to excuse or justify

Examples

The inexcusability of error
The inexcusability of the error led to immediate disciplinary action.
Many argued about the inexcusability of her late arrival to the important meeting.
He tried to apologize, but the inexcusability of his behavior was obvious to all.

Related Terms

  • antonym: excuse - a reason given to justify a fault or offense
  • synonym: unforgivability - the quality of being impossible to forgive
