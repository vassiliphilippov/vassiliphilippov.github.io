Judges

proper noun
Old Testament book about Israel's judges
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʌdʒɪz / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌdʒɪz / (BrE)
A book of the Old Testament that recounts the history of Israel under leaders known as judges.

Examples

Judges book
He referenced the account of Samson found in Judges during his sermon.
Many stories in Judges illustrate the cyclical nature of Israel's faith and disobedience.
The Book of Judges contains some of the most dramatic episodes in biblical history.
