bandpass

noun
frequency filter
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈbændˌpæs / (AmE), / ˈbændˌpɑːs / (BrE)
a filter or circuit that allows signals within a certain frequency range to pass while blocking others

Examples

A bandpass circuit
The engineer adjusted the bandpass to isolate the desired radio signal.
A bandpass is essential in audio equipment to filter out unwanted noise.
They installed a new bandpass to improve the clarity of the transmission.

Related Terms

  • antonym: band-stop - a filter that blocks frequencies within a certain range and allows others to pass
  • antonym: high-pass - a filter that allows only frequencies above a certain point to pass
  • antonym: low-pass - a filter that allows only frequencies below a certain point to pass
