with one voice

adverb
in complete agreement or unity
Pronunciation: / wɪθ wʌn vɔɪs / (AmE), / wɪð wʌn vɔɪs / (BrE)
If people speak or act with one voice, they do so in complete agreement or unity.

Examples

Speak with one voice
After much discussion, the committee responded with one voice to support the proposal.
The family spoke with one voice in defense of their loved one.
The protestors demanded change with one voice, making their message impossible to ignore.

Related Terms

  • antonym: divided - not in agreement; in conflict or disagreement
  • synonym: unanimously - with complete agreement by everyone involved
