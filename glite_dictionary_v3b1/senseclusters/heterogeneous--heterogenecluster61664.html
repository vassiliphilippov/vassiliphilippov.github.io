heterogeneous

adjective
diverse in kind or nature
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛtəroʊˈdʒiniəs /; / ˌhɛt̬əroʊˈdʒiːniəs / (AmE), / ˌhɛtərəˈdʒiːniəs / (BrE)
consisting of parts or elements that are different or diverse in kind or nature

Examples

A heterogeneous mixture
A heterogeneous mixture contains substances that remain physically separate.
Her interests are quite heterogeneous, ranging from classical music to experimental physics.
The committee is heterogeneous, including scientists, artists, and business leaders.
The city is known for its heterogeneous population, with residents from over fifty countries.
The mixture was heterogeneous, containing sand, pebbles, and bits of metal.
Their medical team took a heterogeneous approach, using several different types of treatments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: homogeneous - consisting of parts that are all the same kind or nature
