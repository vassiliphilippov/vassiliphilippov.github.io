inferable

adjective
able to be deduced
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfɜːrəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɜːrəbl / (BrE)
able to be deduced or concluded from evidence or reasoning

Examples

Inferable from evidence
The motive behind the crime was inferable from the suspect's previous actions.
It is inferable from the data that sales will increase next quarter.
Although not directly stated, the answer was inferable from the context of the discussion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deducible - able to be derived or inferred logically
  • degree higher: obvious - easily perceived or understood; clear
  • antonym: unclear - not easy to understand or be certain about
