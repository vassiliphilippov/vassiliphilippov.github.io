cowtown

noun
small town in cattle-raising region (North America)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊˌtaʊn / (AmE), / ˈkaʊˌtaʊn / (BrE)
a small town in a cattle-raising region, especially in western North America, often historically associated with cattle trade or ranching culture

Examples

A cowtown in Texas
After the railroad arrived, the remote village quickly grew into a classic cowtown.

The old cowtown is now a bustling city, but its Wild West roots are still remembered.
