revoice

verb
to give new voice
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈvɔɪs / (AmE), / ˌriːˈvɔɪs / (BrE)
to provide a new voice or sound for something, especially in audio or film production

Examples

To revoice a character
The director decided to revoice the main character to better match the new script.
After the actor left the project, the studio had to revoice all his lines.
For the animated film's French release, they will revoice every character.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dub - to replace the original voice or sound in a film with a new one
  • antonym: mute - to remove or silence the sound or voice
