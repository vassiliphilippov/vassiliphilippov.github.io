monolithic structure

noun
large, massive or inflexible structure
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑː.nəˈlɪθ.ɪk ˈstrʌk.tʃər / (AmE), / ˌmɒ.nəˈlɪθ.ɪk ˈstrʌk.tʃə / (BrE)
A very large and massive structure, often made from a single piece of material; or an organization that is inflexible and cannot easily change.

Examples

A large monolithic structure
Reforming a monolithic structure like the government bureaucracy took years of effort.
The ancient temple was built as a monolithic structure from a single block of granite.
The new city hall is a monolithic structure that dominates the downtown landscape.

Related Terms

  • synonym: monolith - a large single upright block of stone
