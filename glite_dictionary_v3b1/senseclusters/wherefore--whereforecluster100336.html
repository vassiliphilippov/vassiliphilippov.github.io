wherefore

noun
reason or explanation (esp. in 'the whys and wherefores')
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛrˌfɔr / (AmE), / ˈwɛəˌfɔː / (BrE)
the reason or explanation for something, especially as used in the phrase 'the whys and wherefores'

Examples

The wherefore of reasons
She wanted to know the wherefore behind his sudden departure.
The committee never explained the wherefores of their decision.
We discussed the whys and wherefores of the new policy at length.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reason - a cause, explanation, or justification for an action or event
