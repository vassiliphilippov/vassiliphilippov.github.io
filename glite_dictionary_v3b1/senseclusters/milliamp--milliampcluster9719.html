milliamp

noun
unit of electric current
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪliˌæmp / (AmE), / ˈmɪliˌæmp / (BrE)
a unit of electric current equal to one thousandth of an ampere

Examples

A milliamp unit
The device requires only a few milliamps to function efficiently.
A current of 10 milliamps is considered safe for most electronic circuits.
The LED draws less than one milliamp when operating at low brightness.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: ampere - the base unit of electric current in the International System of Units
  • degree lower: microamp - a unit of electric current equal to one millionth of an ampere
