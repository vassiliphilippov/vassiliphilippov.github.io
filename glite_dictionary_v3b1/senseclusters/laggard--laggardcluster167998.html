laggard

noun
someone who falls behind
1.8 6.4
Pronunciation: /ˈlæ.gərd/ (AmE), /ˈlæ.gəd/ (BrE)
a person or thing that makes slower progress than others and falls behind

Examples

A laggard student
As technology advances, laggard countries may struggle to keep up with global trends.
Teachers often give extra support to any laggard in the classroom to help them catch up.
The company can't afford to have a laggard slowing down the entire team.
