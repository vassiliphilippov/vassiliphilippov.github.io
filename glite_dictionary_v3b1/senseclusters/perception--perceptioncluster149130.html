perception

noun
machine interpretation of sensory data
Pronunciation: / pərˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / pəˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
(in computing and robotics) a system's ability to interpret sensory data (for example from cameras, lidar, or microphones) and form a model or understanding of its environment.

Examples

Robot perception
The autonomous drone relies on its perception system to detect obstacles in real time.
Modern self-driving cars use multiple sensors so their perception constantly updates the driving model.
Researchers improved the robot's perception by integrating lidar, cameras, and neural networks for better mapping.

Related Terms

  • synonym: computer vision - techniques that enable machines to interpret and process visual information from images or video
  • synonym: sensor fusion - the process of combining data from multiple sensors to produce more accurate environmental information
