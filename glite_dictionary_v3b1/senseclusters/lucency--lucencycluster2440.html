lucency

noun
transparency or translucence
8.5 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàluÀês…ônsi / (AmE), / ÀàluÀês…ônsi / (BrE)
the quality of allowing light to pass through, making something translucent or transparent

Examples

Glass lucency
The X-ray revealed a region of lucency in the patient's bone.
The artist admired the lucency of the stained glass window as sunlight streamed through.
Modern smartphone screens are designed for maximum lucency and clarity.

Synonyms

  • transparency
    4.6 0.87/m
    transparency lets light pass so objects behind are seen clearly, while lucency refers to allowing light through either clearly or diffusely.
  • pellucidity
    9.9 0.00/m
    pellucidity allows light to pass without diffusion or blur, while lucency covers both clear transparency and more diffuse translucency.
  • limpidity
    8.6 0.00/m
    limpidity is clear and free from cloudiness, while lucency includes that clear quality and also more hazy translucence.
  • diaphaneity
    9.9 0.00/m
    diaphaneity describes how much light a material lets through, while lucency names the general quality of allowing light to pass.
  • translucency
    6.9 0.20/m
    translucency lets light pass but blurs shapes so objects are not clear, while lucency can mean either clear or blurred light passage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: opacity - the quality of not allowing light to pass through
  • synonym: translucency - the quality of allowing some light to pass through but not detailed shapes
  • synonym: transparency - the quality of being able to see through something
