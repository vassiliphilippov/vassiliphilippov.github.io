spoonfeeding

noun
overly simplified, discouraging independence

The practice of teaching or giving information in an overly simple and detailed way that discourages independent thinking or initiative.

Examples

Student spoonfeeding
Many teachers worry that too much spoonfeeding prevents students from developing critical thinking skills.
Some learners prefer spoonfeeding, but others thrive when challenged to find solutions on their own.
The course relied on spoonfeeding instead of encouraging students to explore topics independently.
