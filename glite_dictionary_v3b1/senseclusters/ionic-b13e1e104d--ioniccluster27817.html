Ionic

adjective
of the Ionic order in Greek architecture
Pronunciation: / aɪˈɑnɪk / (AmE), / aɪˈɒnɪk / (BrE)
relating to the Ionic order, one of the classical orders of ancient Greek architecture characterized by scroll-shaped ornaments (volutes) on the capital of columns.

Examples

Ionic column
Architects often study the proportions and decorations of the Ionic order when designing neoclassical buildings.
The Ionic style is easily recognized by the scroll-like volutes on top of its columns.
The temple features beautiful Ionic columns with distinctive capitals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Corinthian - relating to the Corinthian order of classical Greek architecture
  • synonym: Doric - relating to the Doric order of classical Greek architecture
