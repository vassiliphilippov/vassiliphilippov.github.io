bristle up

verb
show sudden anger or indignation
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɪsəl ʌp / (AmE), / ˈbrɪsəl ʌp / (BrE)
to show anger or indignation, often suddenly or visibly

Examples

To bristle up angrily
He could see his colleague bristle up the moment the topic was mentioned.
The manager bristled up at the unfair accusation from the client.
Whenever she's criticized, she tends to bristle up and defend herself fiercely.
