tenuously

adverb
in a weak or insubstantial way
8.5 0.25/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆtÉ›njuÉ™sli / (AmE), / ËˆtÉ›njÊŠÉ™sli / (BrE)
In a way that is very weak, slight, or insubstantial; barely or with little strength or substance.

Examples

To tenuously hold
Her argument was tenuously supported by the available evidence, making it less convincing to the committee.
The theory is tenuously based on a handful of outdated studies.
The two countries remained tenuously connected through a trade agreement despite years of tension.

Synonyms

  • insubstantially
    8.1 0.04/m
    insubstantially means lacking real substance or strength, while tenuously describes a weak, thin, or shaky connection.
  • faintly
    2.7 6.14/m
    faintly is used for something barely seen, heard, or smelled, while tenuously means weak or not strongly held.
  • scarcely
    4.2 11/m
    scarcely means almost not or only just, while tenuously describes a weak or slight link rather than amount or time.
