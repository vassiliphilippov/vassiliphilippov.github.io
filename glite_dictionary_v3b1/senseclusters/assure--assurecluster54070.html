assure

verb
promise or state confidently
2.1
Pronunciation: / əˈʃʊr / (AmE), / əˈʃʊə /; / əˈʃɔː / (BrE)
to tell someone something confidently to remove their doubts or fears

Examples

To assure a friend
I can assure you that everything will be completed on time.
She assured her friend that the exam wasn't as difficult as expected.
They assured us of their full support during the project.
I can assure you that the results will arrive on time.
She assured her parents that she would be careful while traveling.
The manager assured everyone of the project's success during the meeting.
I can assure you that your application is being processed as quickly as possible.
She assured him that everything would be fine after the surgery.
The manager assures all customers that their information is kept confidential.

Related Terms

  • antonym: doubt - to feel uncertain or lack confidence about something
  • synonym: guarantee - to promise or make sure that something will happen or be true
  • synonym: promise - to state you will definitely do something or that something will happen
  • synonym: reassure - to say or do something to remove someone's doubts or fears
