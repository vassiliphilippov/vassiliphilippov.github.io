inasmuch as

conjunction
because; since
6.6
Pronunciation: / ɪn əzˈmʌtʃ æz /; / ˌɪnəzˈmʌtʃ æz / (AmE), / ɪn əzˈmʌtʃ æz /; / ˌɪnəzˈmʌtʃ æz / (BrE)
because; since; used to introduce the reason or extent for something

Examples

Inasmuch as you agree
Inasmuch as you have completed the requirements, you are eligible for the certificate.
The proposal was accepted inasmuch as it met all the necessary criteria.
Inasmuch as the evidence is clear, we must proceed with the decision.
The project will continue, inasmuch as we have enough resources to support it.
He is responsible, inasmuch as he was the last person to leave the office.
You may attend the meeting, inasmuch as you are a member of the committee.
