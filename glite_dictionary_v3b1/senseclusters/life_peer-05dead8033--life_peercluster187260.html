life peer

noun
UK peerage without hereditary title
5.1
Pronunciation: / laɪf pɪɹ / (AmE), / laɪf pɪə / (BrE)
In the UK, a member of the peerage whose title is not hereditary and lapses at death.

Examples

A life peer in UK
After years of public service, she was appointed a life peer by the Queen.
The House of Lords was reformed to include more life peers and fewer hereditary peers.
Unlike a hereditary title, the honor of life peer ceases upon the recipient's death.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hereditary peer - a peer whose title passes to descendants
