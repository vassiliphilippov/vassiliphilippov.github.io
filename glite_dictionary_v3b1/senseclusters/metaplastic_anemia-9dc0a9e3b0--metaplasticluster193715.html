metaplastic anemia

noun
a type of pernicious anemia with blood cell changes
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛtəˈplæstɪk əˈniːmiə / (AmE), / ˌmɛtəˈplɑːstɪk əˈniːmiə /; / ˌmɛtəˈplastɪk əˈniːmɪə / (BrE)
A form of pernicious anemia in which the various formed elements of the blood undergo abnormal changes.

Examples

Metaplastic anemia disease
Doctors are researching new treatments for metaplastic anemia to improve outcomes.
The patient was diagnosed with metaplastic anemia after laboratory tests revealed abnormal blood cell changes.
Unlike typical cases, metaplastic anemia involves alterations in more than one blood cell type.
Patients with metaplastic anaemia often require vitamin B12 injections as part of their treatment.
