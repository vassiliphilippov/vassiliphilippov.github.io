dualist

noun
believer in dualism (philosophy/religion)
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈduːəlɪst, ˈdjuːəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈdjuːəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who believes in or supports the doctrine of dualism, especially in philosophy or religion

Examples

A dualist philosopher
As a committed dualist, she argued that the mind and body are fundamentally different substances.
Descartes is often cited as a prominent dualist in Western philosophy.
The debate between the monist and the dualist philosophers has lasted for centuries.
