dense

adjective
not letting light through; thick or concentrated
3.9 3.76/m
Pronunciation: / dÉ›ns / (AmE), / dÉ›ns / (BrE)
(of a substance) permitting little or no light to pass through because of thickness or concentration; difficult to see through.

Examples

Dense fog
The dense fog made it impossible to see the road ahead.
The forest became so dense that barely any sunlight reached the ground.
Thick, dense smoke billowed out of the burning building.

Synonyms

  • impenetrable
    7.0 0.34/m
    impenetrable is so solid that nothing, including light, passes through, while dense means thick or closely packed.
  • opaque
    4.7 0.03/m
    opaque does not let light pass through and looks solid, while dense focuses on thickness or closely packed material that blocks light.
  • thick
    1.0 61/m
    thick describes a large distance or heavy material, while dense emphasizes close packing or concentration that makes it hard to see through.

Related Terms

  • synonym: opaque - not able to be seen through; not transparent
