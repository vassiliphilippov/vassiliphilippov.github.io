existentially

adverb
relating to existence
Pronunciation: / ˌɛɡzɪˈstɛnʃəli / (AmE), / ˌɛɡzɪˈstɛnʃəli / (BrE)
in a way that relates to existence, being, or survival

Examples

To feel existentially lost
She felt existentially lost after moving to a new country and leaving her old life behind.
The novel explores how people are existentially affected by sudden changes in their environment.
He questioned his choices existentially, wondering if they truly reflected who he was.
The species is existentially threatened by rapid climate change and habitat loss.
After the accident, he began to question his life existentially and sought deeper meaning.
The company was existentially at risk after losing its main investor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fundamentally - in a basic or essential way
  • synonym: philosophically - in a way that relates to philosophy or deep thinking
  • antonym: superficially - in a way that is only on the surface or not deep
