mortgage-backed security

noun
bond backed by a pool of mortgages
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔrɡɪdʒ bækt səˈkjʊrɪti / (AmE), / ˈmɔːɡɪdʒ bækt sɪˈkjʊərɪti / (BrE)
a type of investment security made up of a bundle of mortgages, where investors receive payments from the principal and interest of those mortgages, often with some government guarantee

Examples

A mortgage-backed security investment
Banks package home loans together to create a mortgage-backed security for sale to other investors.
Investors flocked to the new mortgage-backed security offerings, drawn by their attractive yields.
The collapse of the mortgage-backed security market contributed to the financial crisis of 2008.

Related Terms

  • synonym: asset-backed security - a security backed by a pool of assets such as loans or receivables
