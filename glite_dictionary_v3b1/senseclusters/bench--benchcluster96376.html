bench

noun
the judges collectively in a court
2.6
Pronunciation: / bɛntʃ / (AmE), / bɛntʃ / (BrE)
the judge or judges in a court of law, or the seat where they sit

Examples

The lawyer addressed the bench directly during his argument.
After years at the bench, she was respected for her fairness and wisdom.
He presented his argument directly to the bench during the trial.
The judge took his place on the bench and called the court to order.
