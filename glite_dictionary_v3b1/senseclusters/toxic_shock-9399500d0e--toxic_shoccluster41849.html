toxic shock

noun
serious acute bacterial infection syndrome
5.8 6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈtɑːk.sɪk ʃɑːk/ /; / /ˈtɑːk.sɪk ʃɔːk/ (AmE), /ˈtɒk.sɪk ʃɒk/ (BrE)
a rare, life-threatening medical condition caused by certain bacterial infections, characterized by high fever, rash, low blood pressure, and organ dysfunction, often associated with menstruation and tampon use

Examples

Toxic shock syndrome
Doctors warned about the risks of toxic shock linked to improper tampon use.
Early recognition of toxic shock can be crucial to successful treatment.
The hospital treated her for toxic shock after she developed a sudden high fever and rash.
