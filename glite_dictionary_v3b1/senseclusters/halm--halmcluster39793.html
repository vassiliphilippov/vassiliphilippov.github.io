halm

noun
stems of crops used for thatching or bedding
3.9
Pronunciation: / hɑm / (AmE), / hɑːm / (BrE)
The stems of beans, peas, potatoes, and grasses considered collectively, especially as used for thatching or bedding.

Examples

Dry halm stems
Ancient cottages were often insulated using halm from peas and beans.
Farmers stored halm in the barn to use for animal bedding during the winter.
The thatched roof was constructed from halm gathered after the harvest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: straw - dried stalks of cereal plants after grain removed
