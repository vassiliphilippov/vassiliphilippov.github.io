red shift

noun
shift to longer light wavelengths in astronomy
7.2 0.15/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆrÉ›d ÊƒÉªft / (AmE), / ËˆrÉ›d ÊƒÉªft / (BrE)
(astronomy) a change in the light from distant celestial objects toward longer (redder) wavelengths, usually seen as evidence of the expansion of the universe.

Examples

A red shift in light
A pronounced red shift indicates that a star or galaxy is moving away from us at high speed.
By measuring the red shift, scientists can estimate the distance of a galaxy from Earth.
The astronomer explained how the red shift of galaxies supports the theory of an expanding universe.
