sure

adjective
infallible or unfailing; certain
Pronunciation: / ʃʊr /; / ʃʊr, ʃɜr / (AmE), / ʃɔː /; / ʃɔː, ʃʊə / (BrE)
certain to happen, be true, or inevitable; not subject to doubt

Examples

Sure sign
A quick response is a sure indication of good customer service.
Her victory seemed sure after she gained a large lead early in the race.
The dark clouds were a sure sign that a storm was coming.
Be sure that you have all your belongings before exiting the train.
Please be sure to turn off the lights before you leave.
She checked twice to be sure the door was locked.
A sure way to win trust is to always tell the truth.
The results provide sure evidence that the method works.
There was sure proof that the suspect was at the scene of the crime.
