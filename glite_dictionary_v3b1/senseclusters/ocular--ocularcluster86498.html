ocular

noun
eyepiece of an optical instrument
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːkjələr / (AmE), / ˈɒkjʊlə / (BrE)
the lens or combination of lenses at the viewing end of an optical instrument, such as a microscope or telescope, through which the observer looks

Examples

Microscope ocular
He adjusted the ocular to get a clearer view of the specimen.
Make sure the ocular is clean before looking through the microscope.
The technician replaced the damaged ocular with a new one for better magnification.
