meager

adjective
lacking in quantity or quality
Pronunciation: / ˈmiɡər /; / ˈmiːɡər / (AmE), / ˈmiːɡə / (BrE)
lacking in quantity, quality, or extent; insufficient or scanty

Examples

A meager harvest
After the drought, only a meager harvest was gathered.
The portions at the restaurant were surprisingly meager.
They struggled to survive on their meager wages.
His salary was so meagre that he struggled to pay the rent.
The refugees survived on meagre rations for several weeks.
They made the most of their meagre resources during the crisis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abundant - existing or available in large quantities
  • synonym: meager - lacking in quantity, quality, or extent; insufficient (American English spelling)
  • synonym: scant - barely sufficient in amount or quantity
  • synonym: scarce - insufficient for the demand
