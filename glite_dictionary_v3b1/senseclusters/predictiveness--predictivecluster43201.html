predictiveness

noun
ability to forecast
7.5
Pronunciation: / prɪˈdɪktɪvnəs / (AmE), / prɪˈdɪktɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality or ability of something to accurately forecast or indicate future events or outcomes

Examples

The predictiveness of models
Scientists evaluated the predictiveness of the new weather model before using it for forecasts.
The predictiveness of her analysis impressed the entire research team.
We need to improve the predictiveness of our sales projections for next year.
Researchers evaluated the predictiveness of the new test for early disease detection.
The predictiveness of weather models has improved with better technology.
We questioned the predictiveness of the survey results for future consumer trends.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accuracy - the degree to which something is correct or precise
  • synonym: predictability - the degree to which something can be predicted
  • synonym: predictive power - the ability of a measure or test to accurately forecast outcomes
  • antonym: randomness - the lack of pattern or predictability in events
  • antonym: uncertainty - the state of being unsure or not able to be predicted
