grass tree

noun
Tasmanian shrubby evergreen tree with long tapering leaves
Pronunciation: / ɡræs tri / (AmE), / ɡrɑːs triː / (BrE)
A gaunt evergreen shrubby tree native to Tasmania, characterized by slender, tapering leaves that are 3 to 5 feet long.

Examples

Tasmanian grass tree
A grass tree can be identified by its tall, narrow leaves and shrubby appearance.
Botanists studied several mature grass trees in the region to understand their growth patterns.
The grass tree thrives in the wild landscapes of Tasmania.
