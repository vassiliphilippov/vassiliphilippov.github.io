klystron

noun
microwave electron tube for amplification/generation
Pronunciation: / ˈklaɪˌstrɑn / (AmE), / ˈklaɪstrɒn / (BrE)
an electron tube designed to generate or amplify microwave-frequency electromagnetic waves by means of velocity modulation of electrons

Examples

A klystron tube
Engineers adjusted the klystron to improve amplification performance in the laboratory experiment.
Modern satellite communication systems often use a klystron as a microwave amplifier.
The radar system relies on a klystron to generate high-frequency microwave signals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: magnetron - a type of vacuum tube used to generate microwaves
  • degree lower: microwave - electromagnetic wave with wavelengths between infrared and radio waves
