escheat

verb
to revert property to state
Pronunciation: / ɪsˈtʃit / (AmE), / ɪsˈtʃiːt / (BrE)
to revert property to the state or government when there are no legal claimants or heirs

Examples

To escheat land
If a person dies without a will or heirs, their property may escheat to the state.
The abandoned estate escheated after years of legal proceedings.
Under certain laws, unclaimed bank accounts can escheat to the government.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inherit - to receive property from someone after their death
  • synonym: revert - to return to a previous state or owner
