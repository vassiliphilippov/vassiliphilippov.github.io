mandatum

noun
Roman law contract
9.9
Pronunciation: / mænˈdeɪtəm / (AmE), / mænˈdɑːtəm / (BrE)
a contract or commission in Roman law where a person agrees, without payment, to manage another's affairs or perform services

Examples

Roman mandatum contract
In ancient Rome, a mandatum was often used to entrust business matters to a trusted friend.
The lawyer explained the concept of mandatum as a type of unpaid service agreement in Roman law.
He accepted a mandatum to oversee the estate without expecting any compensation.

  • synonym: mandate - an official order or commission to do something
