delocalization

noun
moving jobs abroad
7.7
Pronunciation: /diː.loʊ.kə.laɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /də.loʊ.kə.laɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /diː.ləʊ.kə.laɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /də.ləʊ.kə.laɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of moving production, services, or jobs from one region or country to another, often to lower costs

Examples

Job delocalization
The delocalization of manufacturing led to job losses in the small town.
Many companies consider delocalization to remain competitive in the global market.
Critics argue that delocalization can harm local economies and communities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: localization - the process of making something local or adapting it to a particular place
  • synonym: offshoring - the relocation of business processes to another country
  • synonym: outsourcing - the practice of obtaining goods or services from an outside supplier
