horizontalism

noun
non-hierarchical organization
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˌhɔːrɪˈzɑːntəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌhɒrɪˈzɒntəlɪzəm / (BrE)
a system or belief in organizing groups or societies without hierarchical leadership, emphasizing equal participation and decision-making

Examples

Horizontalism in groups
The activist group practiced horizontalism to make sure every member had a voice in decisions.
Many modern organizations are exploring horizontalism as an alternative to traditional management structures.
By adopting horizontalism, the team avoided having a single leader and shared responsibilities equally.

Related Terms

  • synonym: egalitarianism - the belief in equal rights and opportunities for all people
  • antonym: hierarchy - a system in which people or groups are ranked one above the other
