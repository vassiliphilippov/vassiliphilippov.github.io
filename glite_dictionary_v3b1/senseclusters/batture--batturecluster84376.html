batture

noun
riverbank sandbar
Pronunciation: / ˈbætʃər /; / bæˈtjʊr / (AmE), / ˈbætjʊə / (BrE)
a strip of land or sandbar along a riverbank, often exposed when water is low and formed by river deposits

Examples

The batture strip
Fishermen set up their nets on the batture during the dry season.
After the floodwaters receded, a wide batture appeared along the riverbank.
Birds gathered on the batture to search for food at low tide.
The fisherman set up his camp on the batture beside the Mississippi River.
After the flood, new batture appeared along the city’s waterfront.
Local laws determine who can use the batture for farming or recreation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alluvium - soil or sediment deposited by a river
  • synonym: islet - a small island, especially in a river
  • synonym: riverbank - the land along the edge of a river
  • synonym: sandbar - a long, narrow strip of sand in a river or sea
