kymograph

noun
instrument for recording physiological changes
9.5 9.4
Pronunciation: /ˈkaɪ.mə.græf/ /; / /ˈkɪ.mə.græf/ (AmE), /ˈkaɪ.mə.grɑːf/ /; / /ˈkɪ.mə.grɑːf/ (BrE)
a scientific instrument that records physiological movements or changes, such as breathing or blood pressure, by means of a stylus tracing on a rotating drum of paper

Examples

A kymograph machine
Early experiments in physiology often relied on the kymograph to document muscle contractions.
The scientist used a kymograph to record the patient's breathing patterns.
With the advent of digital technology, the kymograph has become largely obsolete in modern laboratories.
