deliverability

noun
ability to be delivered
Pronunciation: / dɪˌlɪvərəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / dɪˌlɪvərəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the ability of something, such as a product or message, to be successfully delivered to its intended recipient

Examples

Email deliverability
Improving email deliverability is crucial for successful digital marketing campaigns.
The deliverability of the package depends on the accuracy of the address provided.
Companies often test the deliverability of their products before launching them in new markets.

Related Terms

  • synonym: transmissibility - the ability to be transmitted or sent from one place to another
  • antonym: unattainability - the state of being impossible to reach or deliver
