black

adjective
of the darkest color; absorbing all light
1.0
Pronunciation: / blæk / (AmE), / blæk / (BrE)
of the darkest color, like coal or the night; having no light or almost all light absorbed; very dark

Examples

Black night sky
His boots were polished until they looked perfectly black.
She wore a black dress to the ceremony.
The black night sky was filled with stars.
After the storm, the house was black inside because the electricity was out.
He walked into the black tunnel and could see nothing ahead of him.
It was a black night, and not even the stars were visible.
The sky looked black just before the storm began.
Her new shoes are even blacker than her old ones.
This is the blackest paint I have ever seen.
