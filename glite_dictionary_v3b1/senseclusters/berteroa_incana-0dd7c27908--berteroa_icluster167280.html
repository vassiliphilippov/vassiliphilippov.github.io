Berteroa incana

noun
a European annual plant with white flowers
Pronunciation: / bɜːrˈtɛr.oʊ ɪnˈkæn.ə / (AmE), / bɜːˈtɪə.rəʊ ɪnˈkɑː.nə / (BrE)
A tall annual plant native to Europe, with grey-green, downy leaves and dense clusters of small white flowers, followed by hairy seed pods; often found as a weed and naturalized in North America.

Examples

Berteroa incana plant
Berteroa incana often invades disturbed soils along roadsides in North America.
Farmers sometimes struggle to control Berteroa incana in alfalfa fields due to its rapid spread.
The small white flowers of Berteroa incana make it easy to spot among other weeds.
