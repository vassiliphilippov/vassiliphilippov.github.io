vihuela

noun
Spanish stringed instrument
9.9
Pronunciation: / viˈweɪlə / (AmE), / viˈweɪlə / (BrE)
a Spanish stringed instrument from the Renaissance period, similar to a guitar and played by plucking

Examples

Renaissance vihuela
The musician played a haunting melody on the vihuela during the concert.
In Renaissance Spain, the vihuela was a popular instrument among court musicians.
She learned to play the vihuela as part of her study of early music.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lute - a plucked string instrument popular in Renaissance music
