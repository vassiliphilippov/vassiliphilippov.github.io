phospholipid

noun
type of lipid with phosphate group
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌfɑːs.foʊˈlɪp.ɪd / (AmE), / ˌfɒs.fəˈlɪp.ɪd / (BrE)
a lipid molecule containing a phosphate group, fatty acids, and a nitrogenous base, forming a major component of cell membranes

Examples

A phospholipid molecule
A disruption in phospholipid balance can affect cellular health and signaling.
Cell membranes owe their structure and function to phospholipid layers.
The body synthesizes phospholipids as essential components of all living cells.
