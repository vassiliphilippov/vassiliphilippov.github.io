bishop's throne

noun
official ceremonial seat of a bishop
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪʃəps ˈθroʊn / (AmE), / ˈbɪʃəps ˈθrəʊn / (BrE)
the official ceremonial chair or seat occupied by a bishop, especially in a cathedral

Examples

The bishop's throne seat
During the ceremony, the newly appointed bishop sat upon the bishop's throne for the first time.
The cathedral was built to house the bishop's throne, symbolizing his authority over the diocese.
Visitors admired the ornate carvings on the bishop's throne behind the altar.
