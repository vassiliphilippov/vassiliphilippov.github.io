fatigable

adjective
prone to fatigue damage
9.8
Pronunciation: / fəˈtiːɡəbəl / (AmE), / fəˈtiːɡəbəl / (BrE)
liable to develop fatigue damage or fail after repeated stress or use, especially in engineering or materials science

Examples

Fatigable material
Engineers must avoid using fatigable materials in critical structural components.
The repeated loading made the metal beam increasingly fatigable over time.
Designers look for alloys that are less fatigable for use in aircraft wings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: durable - able to withstand wear, pressure, or damage
  • antonym: fatigue-resistant - not easily damaged by repeated stress or use
