hypercoagulability

noun
increased blood clotting
9.9
Pronunciation: /haɪ.pər.koʊ.æɡ.jə.ˈbɪl.ə.tiː/ (AmE), /haɪ.pə.kəʊ.æɡ.jə.ˈbɪl.ə.tiː/ (BrE)
a medical condition in which the blood has an increased tendency to form clots

Examples

Hypercoagulability condition
People with hypercoagulability may require medication to prevent dangerous blood clots.
Doctors monitor patients for signs of hypercoagulability after certain surgeries.
Inherited forms of hypercoagulability can increase the risk of deep vein thrombosis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hypocoagulability - a condition in which the blood has a decreased tendency to clot
  • synonym: thrombophilia - a tendency to develop blood clots due to abnormal clotting
