chuck wagon

noun
wagon for cooking and supplies on cattle drives
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃʌk ˌwæɡən / (AmE), / ˈtʃʌk ˌwæɡən / (BrE)
a wagon equipped with a stove and food supplies, used to provide meals for cowboys or workers on cattle drives

Examples

The chuck wagon camp
During the long cattle drive, the chuck wagon was the heart of the camp each night.
Historians restored an original chuck wagon for the museum's Wild West exhibit.
The cook prepared breakfast at the chuck wagon before sunrise.
