Hokan

noun
member (or language family) of Native American peoples in western North America
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhoʊkən / (AmE), / ˈhəʊkən / (BrE)
A member of any of a group of Native American peoples believed to have spoken related languages in western North America, or the family of languages itself.

Examples

Hokan peoples
Some linguists continue to debate whether the Hokan languages have a common origin.
The museum displayed artifacts from several Hokan cultures that once inhabited the region.
Though the Hokan language family is not widely spoken today, its influence is still present in local place names.
