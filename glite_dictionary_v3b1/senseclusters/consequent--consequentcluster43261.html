Synonyms

  • resultant
    4.9 3/m
    resultant is something that directly follows as an effect, while consequent is a general adjective for something caused by another.
  • ensuing
    4.4 1/m
    ensuing means coming immediately after and often as a result, while consequent simply means caused by something earlier.
  • attendant
    6.2 1/m
    attendant refers to things occurring in connection with an event, while consequent means happening as a result of that event.
  • accompanying
    3.4 5/m
    accompanying describes things that happen at the same time or together, while consequent focuses on being a result.

Related Terms

  • antonym: causal - relating to or acting as a cause; not resultant, but connected by cause-and-effect
  • synonym: ensuing - happening immediately after something as a result
  • synonym: resultant - resulting from something; produced as a result
