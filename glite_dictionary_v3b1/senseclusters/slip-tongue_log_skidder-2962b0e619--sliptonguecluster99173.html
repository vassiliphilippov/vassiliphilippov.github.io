slip-tongue log skidder

noun
historical logging device for moving logs
9.9
Pronunciation: / slɪp tʌŋ lɔɡ ˈskɪdər / (AmE), / slɪp tʌŋ lɒɡ ˈskɪdə / (BrE)
a historical type of skidder used in logging, featuring dangling tongs between rolling wheels to lift one end of a log off the ground for easier transport

Examples

A slip-tongue log skidder
Before modern machinery, a slip-tongue log skidder was essential for transporting heavy logs over rough terrain.
In the museum's collection, a restored slip-tongue log skidder illustrates early logging methods.
The workers hitched the slip-tongue log skidder to a team of horses to move timber out of the forest.
