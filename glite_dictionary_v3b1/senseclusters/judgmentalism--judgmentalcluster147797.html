judgmentalism

noun
tendency to judge harshly
8.0
Pronunciation: /dʒʌdʒ.ˈmen.təl.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /dʒʌdʒ.ˈmen.təl.ɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the tendency to judge people or situations harshly or quickly, often without understanding or empathy

Examples

Constant judgmentalism
His constant judgmentalism made it difficult for his friends to share their problems with him.
The teacher warned against judgmentalism and encouraged students to listen with empathy.
Workplaces that discourage judgmentalism often have a more supportive and positive environment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: open-mindedness - the quality of being willing to consider ideas and opinions that are new or different
  • synonym: prejudice - an unfair and unreasonable opinion or feeling formed without enough thought or knowledge
