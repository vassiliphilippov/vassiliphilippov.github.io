neutralize

verb
make a military target or force useless
Pronunciation: / ˈnuːtrəˌlaɪz /; / ˈnuː.trəˌlaɪz /; / ˈnuːtʃrəlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtrəˌlaɪz /; / ˈnjuː.trəˌlaɪz /; / ˈnjuːtrəlaɪz / (BrE)
to render a military target or threat incapable of action, often by force or elimination

Examples

To neutralize a threat
Special forces were deployed to neutralize the threat posed by the hostile militants.
The airstrike was conducted to neutralize enemy missile sites before the main invasion.
The main objective of the mission was to neutralize enemy communications to prevent coordination.
Authorities confirmed that the leader of the criminal gang had been neutralized in the operation.
The government dispatched a special unit to neutralize the terrorist cell before it could attack.
The spy was instructed to neutralize any enemy agents encountered during the mission.
Intelligence reports suggest that the group plans to neutralise the opposition leadership.
Several high-profile targets were neutralised during the covert operation.
The agent was given orders to neutralise anyone attempting to sabotage the mission.

Related Terms

  • antonym: activate - make active or operative (opposite meaning)
  • synonym: assassinate - murder an important person for political reasons
  • synonym: destroy - put an end to the existence of (something), especially by damaging it so badly that it cannot function
  • synonym: disable - make unable to function; render powerless
  • synonym: disarm - to take weapons away from or render powerless
  • synonym: eliminate - remove or get rid of completely
  • synonym: liquidate - to kill someone, especially for political reasons
  • antonym: protect - keep safe from harm or injury
  • antonym: strengthen - to make stronger, especially in a military sense
