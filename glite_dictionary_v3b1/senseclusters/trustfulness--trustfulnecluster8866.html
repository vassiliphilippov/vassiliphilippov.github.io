trustfulness

noun
willingness to trust others
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈtrʌst.fəl.nəs / (AmE), / ˈtrʌst.fəl.nəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being willing to believe that others are honest or reliable

Examples

Great trustfulness shown
Children often show great trustfulness toward adults they know.
His trustfulness sometimes made him vulnerable to deceitful people.
The negotiator's trustfulness helped build a positive relationship with the other party.

Related Terms

  • antonym: suspicion - a feeling or belief that someone is guilty, dishonest, or dangerous
  • synonym: trust - firm belief in the reliability, truth, or ability of someone or something
