overinvest

verb
to invest excessively
6.1
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ɪn.ˈvest/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ɪn.ˈvest/ (BrE)
to put more money, time, effort, or resources into something than is wise or necessary

Examples

To overinvest in stocks
It is risky to overinvest in a single stock without diversifying your portfolio.
Some parents overinvest their time and energy in their children's activities.
The city decided not to overinvest in infrastructure that may not be needed.
If you overinvest in one area, you might neglect other important opportunities.
The company overinvested in new technology that did not pay off.
She realized she was overinvesting her time in a project with little return.
If you overinvest in one project, you might not have enough resources for others.
The startup realized it had overinvested in marketing and neglected product development.
Some parents overinvest their time and energy in their children's activities.
