streak

verb
move quickly in a straight line
9.0
Pronunciation: / striːk / (AmE), / striːk / (BrE)
to move very quickly in a straight line, sometimes leaving a visible trail

Examples

To streak past
A rabbit suddenly streaked across the road in front of my car.
She streaked down the track to win the race in record time.
The meteor streaked through the night sky, leaving a trail of light behind it.
A shooting star was streaking across the sky, leaving a bright, glittering trail behind.
The sprinter streaked down the track, finishing well ahead of the others.
Lightning streaked across the dark clouds, illuminating the night for a brief moment.
