translucently

adverb
partially revealing
6.5
Pronunciation: /træn.ˈluː.sənt.li/ /; / /træns.ˈluː.sənt.li/ (AmE), /træn.ˈluː.sənt.li/ /; / /træns.ˈluː.sənt.li/ (BrE)
in a way that allows some information or meaning to be seen or understood, but not completely clearly or directly.

Examples

She explains translucently
She spoke translucently about her plans, hinting at changes without giving details.
The artist translucently expressed his feelings through subtle colors and shapes.
The company translucently addressed the rumors, providing some information but not the full story.

Related Terms
