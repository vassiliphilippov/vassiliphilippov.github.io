Unification Church

proper noun
religious movement founded by Sun Myung Moon
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˌjuː.nɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən tʃɜrtʃ / (AmE), / ˌjuː.nɪ.fɪˈkeɪ.ʃən tʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a new religious movement founded in South Korea in 1954 by Sun Myung Moon, characterized by its Christian-based teachings, inclusion of some Buddhist elements, and large-scale communal events such as mass weddings

Examples

Unification Church members
Many members of the Unification Church participate in missionary work around the world.
The Unification Church gained international attention in the 1970s for its large group weddings.
The teachings of the Unification Church emphasize family values and global unity.
