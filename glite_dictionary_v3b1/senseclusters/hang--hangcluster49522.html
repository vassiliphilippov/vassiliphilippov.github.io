hang

noun
way or technique of doing something
Pronunciation: / hæŋ / (AmE), / hæŋ / (BrE)
a way of doing something, especially one that needs practice or skill to learn; the knack or technique of achieving something.

Examples

Get the hang of it
He has the hang of negotiation after years of dealing with difficult clients.
I couldn't quite get the hang of the new software at first.
Once you get the hang of using chopsticks, eating Asian food becomes much easier.

Related Terms

  • synonym: knack - a special skill or talent for doing something
