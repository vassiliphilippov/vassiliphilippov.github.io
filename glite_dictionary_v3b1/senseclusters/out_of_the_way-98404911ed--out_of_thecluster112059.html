out of the way

adjective, adverb
not obstructing; out of the path
5.7
Pronunciation: / aʊt əv ðə weɪ / (AmE), / aʊt əv ðə weɪ / (BrE)
not blocking or obstructing; moved aside so as not to be in the way

Examples

Move the chair out of the way

The workers stored the tools out of the way when they finished the job.
Could you move your bike out of the way before the guests arrive?
Please store your boxes out of the way so people can walk through the hallway.
She pulled the chair out of the way to let the children pass.
