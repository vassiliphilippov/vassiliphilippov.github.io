chromophore

noun
chemical group giving color to a molecule
8.6 8.0
Pronunciation: /ˈkroʊ.mə.fɔːr/ (AmE), /ˈkrəʊ.mə.fɔː/ (BrE)
a chemical group within a molecule responsible for its color by absorbing certain wavelengths of light

Examples

A chromophore group
Certain proteins contain a chromophore that gives them a distinct color under specific light.
Scientists engineered the molecule to include an artificial chromophore for better visualization.
The chromophore in chlorophyll absorbs light necessary for photosynthesis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fluorophore - a chemical group that both absorbs and emits light (fluorescence)
