canary whitewood

noun
North American timber tree; soft white wood
7.3 7.9
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈner.iː ˈwaɪt.wʊd/ /; / /kə.ˈneə.riː ˈwaɪt.wʊd/ (AmE), /kə.ˈneə.riː ˈwaɪt.wʊd/ /; / /kə.ˈner.iː ˈwaɪt.wʊd/ (BrE)
A tall North American deciduous tree, also known as the tulip tree or yellow poplar, with large tulip-shaped greenish yellow flowers and conelike fruit, yielding soft white wood used especially for cabinet work.

Examples

The canary whitewood tree
In the spring, the canary whitewood blooms with distinctive greenish yellow flowers.
Many pieces of antique American furniture are made from canary whitewood due to its light color and durability.
The carpenter chose canary whitewood for the cabinets because of its smooth, workable grain.
