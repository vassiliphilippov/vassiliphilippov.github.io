Hungarian grass

noun
drought-resistant grass used as hay or forage
5.0
Pronunciation: / hʌnˈɡɛriən ɡræs / (AmE), / hʌŋˈɡɛəriən ɡrɑːs / (BrE)
a coarse, drought-resistant annual grass grown for hay, forage, and sometimes grain, especially in Europe, Asia, and the United States

Examples

Hungarian grass crop
In some regions, Hungarian grass is an important crop for both hay and pasture.
Many farmers plant Hungarian grass because it tolerates dry conditions and grows quickly.
The bales of Hungarian grass made excellent feed for the livestock during winter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: foxtail millet - a similar cereal grass also grown for forage and hay
