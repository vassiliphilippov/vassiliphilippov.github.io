soundbox

noun
resonating chamber in a musical instrument
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈsaʊndˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈsaʊndˌbɒks / (BrE)
the hollow chamber in a musical instrument, such as a violin or guitar, that amplifies its sound

Examples

The soundbox of a guitar

Many stringed instruments feature a wooden soundbox to naturally amplify their music.
The violin's soundbox is crucial for producing its rich tone.
