spoonfeed

verb
to teach in an overly simple way
Pronunciation: / ˈspunˌfid / (AmE), / ˈspuːnfiːd / (BrE)
to provide information or instruction in an overly simple, undemanding way that requires little or no effort from the learner

Examples

To spoonfeed information
If you spoonfeed employees, they may never learn to solve problems independently.
The course does not spoonfeed its students, instead it expects active participation and research.
The teacher refused to spoonfeed the class, encouraging them to find answers on their own.

Related Terms

  • antonym: challenge - to test someone's abilities in a demanding way
  • antonym: encourage independent learning - to promote self-guided study and critical thinking
