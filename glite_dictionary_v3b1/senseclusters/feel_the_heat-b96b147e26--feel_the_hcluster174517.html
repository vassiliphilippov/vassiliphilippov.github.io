feel the heat

verb
perceive warmth from a source
Pronunciation: / fil ðə hit / (AmE), / fiːl ðə hiːt / (BrE)
to sense or perceive warmth, especially as given off by a source like fire or sunlight

Examples

To feel the heat of sun
I love to feel the heat of the sun on my face during summer.
She quickly moved back when she began to feel the heat from the burning stove.
Standing so close, you can feel the heat radiating from the oven.
