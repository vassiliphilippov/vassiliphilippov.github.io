aspirate

noun
consonant pronounced with a burst of breath
8.0 6.2
Pronunciation: /ˈæ.spə.reɪt/ /; / /ˈæ.spər.ət/ /; / /ˈæ.spɪr.ət/ /; / /ˈæ.spɪ.reɪt/ (AmE), /ˈæ.spə.reɪt/ /; / /ˈæ.spə.rət/ /; / /ˈæ.spɪr.ət/ (BrE)
a speech sound pronounced with a strong burst of breath, like the 'h' in 'hat' or the 'p' in 'pin'

Examples

An aspirate sound
In English, the 'h' sound is considered an aspirate.
Linguists study how the aspirate is pronounced in different languages.
The initial 'k' in 'kite' is an aspirate in many dialects.
In Hindi, some consonants are distinguished by whether they are aspirate or not.
The word 'hat' begins with an aspirate sound in English.
When teaching English pronunciation, instructors often highlight the difference between aspirate and unaspirated consonants.

Related Terms

  • synonym: consonant - a speech sound produced by obstructing the airflow
  • synonym: plosive - a consonant produced by stopping the airflow, then releasing it
  • antonym: unaspirated - a speech sound produced without an audible breath
