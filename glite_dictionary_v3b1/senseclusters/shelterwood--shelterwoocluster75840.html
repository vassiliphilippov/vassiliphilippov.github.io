shelterwood

noun
protective tree stand
8.1
Pronunciation: /ˈʃel.tər.wʊd/ (AmE), /ˈʃel.tə.wʊd/ (BrE)
a method of forest management where some mature trees are left standing to protect and help young trees grow; also, the group of trees left for this purpose

Examples

A shelterwood stand
The forester explained how a shelterwood helps young trees grow by protecting them from harsh weather.
After the storm, the shelterwood remained standing, providing cover for the new seedlings.
Many forest managers use the shelterwood method to encourage natural regeneration of native species.
The shelterwood technique allows young trees to grow under the protection of older, mature trees.
Many foresters recommend the shelterwood method to promote healthy forest regeneration.
By using a shelterwood system, the forest maintains biodiversity and protects seedlings from harsh weather.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clearcut - an area of forest where all trees have been cut down
  • antonym: clearcutting - a forestry method where all trees in an area are cut down
  • synonym: selection system - a forestry method where individual trees are selectively harvested
  • synonym: windbreak - a row of trees or shrubs planted to protect an area from wind
