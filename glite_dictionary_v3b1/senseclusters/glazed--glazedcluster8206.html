glazed

adjective
coated with a shiny layer (food)
5.2 3.4
Pronunciation: / ɡleɪzd / (AmE), / ɡleɪzd / (BrE)
(of food) covered with a shiny or glossy surface, often produced by applying sugar, egg, or gelatin.

Examples

Glazed doughnuts
She brought a box of glazed doughnuts for the office breakfast.
The chef prepared a beautifully glazed ham for the holiday dinner.
The pastry had a glazed surface that caught the light.

Related Terms

  • antonym: plain - without a shiny or decorative coating (food)
