pressure cooker

noun
sealed pot for fast cooking; stressful situation
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛʃər ˌkʊkər / (AmE), / ˈprɛʃə ˌkʊkə / (BrE)
A sealed pot for cooking food quickly under steam pressure; also used to describe a situation or environment characterized by intense stress or pressure.

Examples

Workplace pressure cooker
After a long day in the pressure cooker, he needed to relax.
She prepared the stew in the pressure cooker to save time.
Working in that office is a real pressure cooker—everyone is stressed out all the time.
