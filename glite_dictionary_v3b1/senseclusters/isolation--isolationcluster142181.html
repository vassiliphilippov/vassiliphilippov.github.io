isolation

noun
psychiatric defense mechanism separating feelings from memory
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪsəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
(Psychiatry) A defense mechanism in which the memory of an unacceptable act or impulse is separated from the emotion originally associated with it.

Examples

Isolation of feelings
Freud described isolation as a way people separate disturbing thoughts from their feelings.
In therapy, understanding isolation can help address unresolved trauma.
The patient exhibited isolation by recalling the incident without any visible emotional reaction.
