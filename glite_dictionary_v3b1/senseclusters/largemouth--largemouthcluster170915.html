largemouth

noun
a species of black bass fish
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑrdʒˌmaʊθ / (AmE), / ˈlɑːdʒˌmaʊθ / (BrE)
A type of black bass (Micropterus salmoides) with a large mouth, where the angle of the jaw extends beyond the eye, commonly found in North American freshwater.

Examples

A largemouth bass
A record-breaking largemouth was caught in the river last spring.
Anglers often compete to catch the biggest largemouth during the tournament.
The lake is known for its abundance of largemouth and crappie.

Related Terms

  • antonym: smallmouth - a species of black bass with a smaller mouth, Micropterus dolomieu
