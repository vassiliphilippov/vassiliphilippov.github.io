disadvantage

verb
to put in an unfavorable position
2.8 3.8
Pronunciation: /dɪs.əd.ˈvæn.tɪdʒ/ /; / /dɪs.əd.ˈæn.ɪdʒ/ (AmE), /dɪs.əd.ˈvɑːn.tɪdʒ/ /; / /dɪs.əd.ˈvæn.tɪdʒ/ (BrE)
to put in an unfavorable position; to hinder or harm someone or something compared to others

Examples

To disadvantage a group
Changing the regulations may disadvantage small businesses compared to larger corporations.
Some policies disadvantage people living in rural areas.
Strict deadlines can disadvantage students who work part-time jobs.
