outlier

noun
person or thing differing from others; extreme value
8.5 5.4
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtˌlaɪər / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌlaɪə / (BrE)
A person or thing that is markedly different from others in a particular group, especially an observation that lies an abnormal distance from other values in a dataset.

Examples

A outlier value
In most years, the weather is mild, but last winter was a clear outlier.
Statisticians pay close attention to outliers when analyzing experimental data.
The scientist discarded the outlier in the data set because it skewed the results.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exception - a person or thing that does not follow a rule or general pattern
