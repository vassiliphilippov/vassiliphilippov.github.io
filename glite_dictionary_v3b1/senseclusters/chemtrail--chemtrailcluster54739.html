chemtrail

noun
aircraft vapor trail
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛmˌtreɪl / (AmE), / ˈkɛmˌtreɪl / (BrE)
a visible trail left in the sky by an aircraft, consisting of water vapor and ice crystals formed at high altitude

Examples

White chemtrail in sky
After the jet passed overhead, a white chemtrail slowly spread across the blue sky.
Some people believe that a chemtrail is more than just condensed water vapor from planes.
On clear mornings, you can often see a chemtrail stretching from horizon to horizon.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contrail - a line-shaped cloud formed by aircraft engine exhaust
