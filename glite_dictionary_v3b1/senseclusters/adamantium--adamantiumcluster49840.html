adamantium

noun
unbreakable quality
Pronunciation: / ˌædəˈmæntiəm / (AmE), / ˌædəˈmæntiəm / (BrE)
a figurative term for an unbreakable quality or extreme hardness, especially of will or determination

Examples

Adamantium will
Her resolve was made of adamantium, never bending under pressure.
The team's unity was their adamantium against all challenges.
He faced criticism with adamantium, never letting it affect his confidence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fragility - the quality of being easily broken or damaged
  • synonym: indestructibility - the quality of being impossible to destroy
