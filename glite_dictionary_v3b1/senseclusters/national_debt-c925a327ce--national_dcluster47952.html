national debt

noun
total government financial liabilities
Pronunciation: / ˈnæʃənəl dɛt /; / ˈnæʃənl dɛt / (AmE), / ˈnæʃənəl dɛt /; / ˈnaʃənəl dɛt / (BrE)
the total amount of money that a country's government owes to creditors

Examples

The national debt amount
Interest payments on the national debt account for a significant part of the federal budget.
Reducing the national debt has become a major political issue.
The country's national debt reached a record high last year.
Debates over how to reduce the national debt are common among politicians.
Rising interest rates have made the national debt more expensive to service each year.
The country's national debt is now twice the size of its annual economic output.

Related Terms

  • antonym: private debt - money owed by individuals or businesses, not by the government
  • synonym: public debt - the total debt incurred by governments, synonymous in some contexts with national debt
