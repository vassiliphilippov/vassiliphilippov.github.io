echolocation

noun
locating objects using reflected sound waves
5.7 5.6
Pronunciation: / ˌɛkoʊloʊˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌiːkəʊləʊˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the use of reflected sound waves to determine the location of objects, used especially by animals such as bats and dolphins

Examples

Echolocation by bats
Bats rely on echolocation to hunt insects in complete darkness.
Dolphins use echolocation to navigate through murky waters.
Scientists study echolocation to develop better sonar technology.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sonar - a system using sound waves to detect objects underwater
