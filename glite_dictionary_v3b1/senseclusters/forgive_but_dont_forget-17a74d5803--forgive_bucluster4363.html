pardon but remember the offense
1.0
Pronunciation: / fərˈɡɪv bət doʊnt fərˈɡɛt / (AmE), / fəˈɡɪv bət dəʊnt fəˈɡɛt / (BrE)
to pardon someone for a wrongdoing, but continue to remember the offense or retain the lesson learned from the experience

Examples

To forgive but don't forget mistakes
His motto was to forgive but don't forget after being deceived by his friends.
Many people forgive but don't forget, using their experiences to become wiser in the future.
You should forgive but don't forget when someone hurts you, to avoid being hurt again.

Related Terms
