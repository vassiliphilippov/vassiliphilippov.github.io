co-defendant

noun
person jointly accused in a court case
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˌkoʊ dɪˈfɛndənt / (AmE), / ˌkəʊ dɪˈfendənt / (BrE)
a person who is jointly accused with one or more others in the same legal case

Examples

The co-defendant in court
Each co-defendant hired a different lawyer to represent them in court.
Police arrested the co-defendant shortly after charging the primary suspect.
The judge heard arguments from both the main defendant and the co-defendant during the trial.
