intervale

noun
river floodplain land
Pronunciation: / ˈɪntərˌveɪl / (AmE), / ˈɪntəˌveɪl / (BrE)
a tract of low-lying, flat land, especially along a river, often used for farming in New England

Examples

The intervale land
The cows grazed in the lush intervale by the river.
Farmers planted corn every spring in the fertile intervale near the village.
After the snow melted, the intervale was covered with wildflowers and new grass.

Related Terms

  • synonym: floodplain - flat land near a river that is sometimes flooded
  • synonym: meadow - a field of grass, often used for hay or grazing
