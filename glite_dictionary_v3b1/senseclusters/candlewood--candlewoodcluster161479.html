candlewood

noun
resinous tree or shrub burned for light
9.3 8.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkæn.dəl.wʊd/ (AmE), /ˈkæn.dəl.wʊd/ (BrE)
Any of several resinous trees or shrubs whose wood can be burned for light, historically used as a source of illumination.

Examples

candlewood tree
Early settlers often used candlewood to light their cabins at night.
Local artisans demonstrated how candlewood burns brightly without the need for candles.
The forest contained several candlewood trees prized for their flammable resin.
