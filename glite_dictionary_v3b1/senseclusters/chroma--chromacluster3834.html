chroma

noun
purity or vividness of a color
8.0 7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkroʊ.mə / (AmE), / ˈkrəʊ.mə / (BrE)
the purity or intensity of a color, measured as its freedom from dilution with white and resulting vividness of hue

Examples

High chroma color
Digital screens can display hues with high chroma for intense visual impact.
In color theory, reducing the chroma results in a less vivid appearance.
The artist adjusted the chroma of the paint to make the colors more vibrant.

Related Terms

  • antonym: paleness - lack of intensity or vividness in color
  • synonym: saturation - the intensity or purity of a color
