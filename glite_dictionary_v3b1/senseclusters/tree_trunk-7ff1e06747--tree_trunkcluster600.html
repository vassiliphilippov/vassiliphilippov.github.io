tree trunk

noun
main stem of a tree
Pronunciation: / triː trʌŋk / (AmE), / triː trʌŋk / (BrE)
the main woody stem of a tree, typically covered with bark, supporting the branches and leaves above the ground

Examples

A large tree trunk
After the storm, several logs and a large tree trunk blocked the hiking path.
Moss grew thickly on the shaded side of the tree trunk.
The old oak's tree trunk was so wide that it took three people to hug it.
