tree trunk

noun
main stem of a tree
1.0 1.72/m
Pronunciation: / triÀê tr å≈ãk / (AmE), / triÀê tr å≈ãk / (BrE)
the main woody stem of a tree, typically covered with bark, supporting the branches and leaves above the ground

Examples

A large tree trunk
After the storm, several logs and a large tree trunk blocked the hiking path.
Moss grew thickly on the shaded side of the tree trunk.
The old oak's tree trunk was so wide that it took three people to hug it.

Synonyms

  • trunk
    1.9 8.17/m
    trunk is the common single word for a tree's main woody stem, while tree trunk is a clearer two-word phrase.
  • bole
    9.1 0.20/m
    bole is the trunk term often used for the timber part of a tree, while tree trunk means the whole main woody stem.
