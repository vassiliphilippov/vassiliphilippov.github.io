sand bar

noun
narrow ridge of sand in water
3.1 0.01/m

A long, narrow deposit of sand formed by currents in a river or sea, often visible above the surface of the water.

Examples

A sand bar in river
A colony of birds nested on the sand bar that formed at low tide.
Boats cannot pass through that channel because a sand bar blocks the way.
Waves crashed against the sand bar just beyond the shoreline.

Synonyms

  • sandbar
    2.8 1.14/m
    sandbar is the common one-word form in modern English, while sand bar is the spaced variant used in some writings.
  • sandbank
    3.2 0.10/m
    sandbank is a larger, often submerged raised area of sand, while sand bar is a thinner, long deposit that often shows above water.
  • batture
    9.9 0.01/m
    batture is a riverbank strip often exposed at low water, while sand bar is a general long, narrow deposit in rivers or seas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sandbank - a raised area of sand in a river or sea, similar to a sand bar
