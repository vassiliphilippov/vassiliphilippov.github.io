United States President

noun
head of state and government of the United States
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˌjuːnəˈtaɪtɪd steɪts ˈprɛzɪdənt / (AmE), / ˌjuːnɪtɪd steɪts ˈprɛzɪdənt / (BrE)
the elected head of state and government of the United States of America, responsible for overseeing the executive branch and serving as the chief representative of the nation

Examples

The United States President
Foreign leaders expressed their congratulations to the new United States President after the election results were announced.
The United States President addressed Congress in a nationally televised speech last night.
The United States President has the authority to sign bills into law or veto them.
