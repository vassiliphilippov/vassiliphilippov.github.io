distance

noun
remote point in time
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪstəns / (AmE), / ˈdɪstəns / (BrE)
an amount of time between two points or events; a period far from the present

Examples

Distance of years
At the distance of twenty years, their friendship had endured all obstacles.
From a distance of several decades, the events of his youth seemed almost unreal.
She hoped to look back, from the distance of years, with pride on her achievements.
He covered the entire journey in the distance of a few days.
In just a short distance of two hours, their lives changed completely.
The distance between sunrise and noon seemed to stretch forever.
