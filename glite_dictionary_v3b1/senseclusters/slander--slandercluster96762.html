slander

verb
make false damaging spoken statements
Pronunciation: / ˈslændɚ / (AmE), / ˈslɑːndə / (BrE)
to make false spoken statements that damage the reputation of someone

Examples

To slander a person
He threatened to sue anyone who tried to slander his character.
It is illegal to slander someone in the media with baseless accusations.
Politicians sometimes slander their opponents during heated campaigns.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defame - to damage someone's good reputation by false statements
  • synonym: libel - to make false written statements damaging to someone's reputation
  • antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration for someone or something
