peopled

adjective
inhabited; occupied by people
4.3 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàpiÀêp…ôld / (AmE), / ÀàpiÀêp…ôld / (BrE)
(of a place) inhabited or occupied by people.

Examples

Peopled islands
Many legends spoke of distant islands peopled by mysterious ancient tribes.
The novel is peopled with vividly drawn characters from all walks of life.
The once-empty valley is now peopled with farmers and their families.

Synonyms

  • inhabited
    2.9 0.00/m
    inhabited describes places where people or animals live regularly, while peopled usually means a place is filled specifically with people.
  • populated
    2.5 0.00/m
    populated means a place is filled with people or other living things, while peopled stresses that people specifically occupy it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: settled - (of a place) occupied by people who live there
  • antonym: uninhabited - (of a place) not lived in by people
