caduceus

noun
symbol of medicine (staff with two snakes)
9.9 8.3
Pronunciation: /kə.ˈdjuː.sɪ.əs/ /; / /kə.ˈduː.sɪ.əs/ (AmE), /kə.ˈdjuː.sɪ.əs/ /; / /kə.ˈduː.sɪ.əs/ (BrE)
a staff with two snakes twined around it and often surmounted by wings, used as a symbol of the medical profession

Examples

The caduceus symbol
Many ambulance logos in the United States display the caduceus as a symbol of emergency medical services.
The caduceus is sometimes confused with the rod of Asclepius, which has only one serpent.
The doctor wore a pin shaped like a caduceus on her white coat.
