to give out or distribute something, such as medicine, food, or other items, to people

Examples

To dispense medicine
Doctors are not allowed to dispense medication directly to patients in some countries.
She was trained to dispense medicines safely and answer patients' questions.
The pharmacist will dispense your prescription once it has been verified.
At the shelter, volunteers dispensed food and water to those in need after the storm.
The pharmacist is responsible for dispensing medication to patients with valid prescriptions.
The vending machine will dispense a can of soda when you insert a coin.

Synonyms

  • distribute
    2.0 8.85/m
    distribute means to give or spread items across people or places, while dispense is about giving items directly to people.
  • administer
    4.8 15/m
    administer means to give medicine or tests in a formal, controlled way, while dispense focuses on handing out items like medicine.
  • give out
    2.3 4.63/m
    give out means to hand or share things to several people, while dispense often refers to supplying items like medicine officially.
  • hand out
    1.8 7.63/m
    hand out means to give something directly to each person by hand, while dispense can mean supplying items more broadly.
  • pass out
    2.7 2.22/m
    pass out means to give items to people often by moving from person to person, while dispense is broader supply to people.
  • pass around
    2.1 1.64/m
    pass around means to move an item among people so each sees or takes it, while dispense is intentional distribution to recipients.
  • dole out
    4.0 2.44/m
    dole out means to give small amounts or share slowly, while dispense means to give or distribute items to people generally.
  • portion out
    3.4 0.08/m
    portion out means to divide something into set shares and give them, while dispense means to hand out items to people.
  • parcel out
    4.8 0.24/m
    parcel out means to divide goods into parts for different groups, while dispense means to give items directly to people.
  • deal out
    3.2 0.17/m
    deal out means to give or deal items usually like cards or blame, while dispense mainly means to distribute useful items to people.
  • mete out
    7.9 0.39/m
    mete out means to give punishment or measured portions in judgement, while dispense usually means supplying goods or medicine to people.
