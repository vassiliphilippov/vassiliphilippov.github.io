impatient

adjective
full of eagerness to start
2.6 2.9
Pronunciation: /ɪm.ˈpeɪ.ʃənt/ (AmE), /ɪm.ˈpeɪ.ʃənt/ (BrE)
(usually followed by 'to') feeling or showing strong eagerness to do something; unable to wait to begin.

Examples

Impatient to start
Everyone was impatient to see the results of the competition.
She was impatient to share her good news with her friends.
The children grew impatient to open their presents as the morning progressed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: eager - keen or enthusiastic to do something
  • antonym: reluctant - unwilling and hesitant; not eager
