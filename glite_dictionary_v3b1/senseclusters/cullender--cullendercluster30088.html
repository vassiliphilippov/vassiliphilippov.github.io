cullender

noun
archaic term for a colander
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌlɪndər / (AmE), / ˈkʌlɪndə / (BrE)
An old term for a bowl-shaped kitchen utensil with perforations, used for washing or draining foods such as vegetables or pasta.

Examples

A cullender bowl
He found a dusty cullender on the top shelf, unused for years.
In the Victorian kitchen, a cullender was as essential as a kettle.
The old recipe instructed her to pour the pasta into a cullender to drain the water.
