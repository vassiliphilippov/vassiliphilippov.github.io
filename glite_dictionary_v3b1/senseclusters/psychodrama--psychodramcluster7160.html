psychodrama

noun
therapy using role-play
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪkoʊˌdrɑːmə / (AmE), / ˈsaɪkəʊˌdrɑːmə / (BrE)
a form of therapy in which people act out real-life situations to explore emotions and solve problems

Examples

Psychodrama therapy session
During psychodrama, participants act out real-life situations to gain insight.
The therapist recommended psychodrama to help the group express their emotions more openly.
Many people find psychodrama helpful for understanding their relationships and personal challenges.

Related Terms

  • synonym: group therapy - therapy involving a group of people discussing and working through problems together
  • synonym: role-play - acting out roles to explore situations or feelings
