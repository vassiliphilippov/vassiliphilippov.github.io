crucify

verb
treat with extreme cruelty or severity
Pronunciation: / ˈkruːsəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈkruːsɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to treat or criticize someone with extreme cruelty or severity, especially in public

Examples

To crucify a performer
Critics tend to crucify the film for its lack of originality.
She felt her classmates would crucify her if she made another mistake.
The media was quick to crucify the politician after the scandal broke.
She felt the public would crucify her if the truth ever came out.
The manager was crucified by his team after the failed project.
The media crucified the actor for his insensitive comments.
