alcoholic

noun
person addicted to alcohol
1.1
Pronunciation: / ˌæl.kəˈhɑ.lɪk / (AmE), / ˌæl.kəˈhɒl.ɪk / (BrE)
a person who habitually drinks excessive amounts of alcohol and is often unable to stop.

Examples

An alcoholic person
His family struggled for years to help their alcoholic relative overcome his addiction.
She realized she had become an alcoholic when drinking interfered with her job and relationships.
The support group was founded to assist alcoholics on their road to recovery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drunkard - person who is habitually drunk
  • antonym: teetotaler - person who abstains completely from alcohol
