glucose

noun
a simple sugar; key energy source
3.4 6.16/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…°luÀêko äs / (AmE), / Àà…°luÀêk…ô äs / (BrE)
A simple sugar (monosaccharide) that is an important source of energy in living organisms and is found naturally in many foods.

Examples

Blood glucose level
After a meal, the level of glucose in the blood rises and provides energy.
Diabetics need to monitor their glucose levels regularly.
Plants produce glucose during photosynthesis, using sunlight as an energy source.

Synonyms

  • dextrose
    6.1 0.18/m
    dextrose is the common sweetener and medical sugar used in food and hospitals, while glucose is the general simple sugar.
  • dextroglucose
    8.8 0.00/m
    dextroglucose is the D-form sugar found in honey and fruits, while glucose is the general simple sugar in many foods.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fructose - a monosaccharide sugar similar to glucose, found especially in fruit
  • synonym: sugar - a sweet-tasting soluble carbohydrate; a category to which glucose belongs
