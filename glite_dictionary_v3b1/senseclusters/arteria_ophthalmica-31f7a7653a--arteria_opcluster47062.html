arteria ophthalmica

noun
artery supplying the eye
9.0
Pronunciation: / ɑːrˌtɪriə ɑfˈθælmɪkə / (AmE), / ɑːˌtɪəriə ɒfˈθælmɪkə / (BrE)
a branch of the internal carotid artery that supplies blood to the eye and neighboring structures

Examples

The arteria ophthalmica branch
Damage to the arteria ophthalmica can result in vision problems or even blindness.
During the procedure, surgeons carefully avoided injuring the arteria ophthalmica.
The arteria ophthalmica plays a crucial role in delivering oxygenated blood to the retina.
