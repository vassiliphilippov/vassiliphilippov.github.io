interpretive dancing

noun
dance expressing emotions or telling a story
3.1 5.8
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈtɜːr.prə.tɪv ˈdæn.sɪŋ/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈtɜː.prə.tɪv ˈdɑːn.sɪŋ/ (BrE)
a style of dance in which movements are used to express emotions or narrate a story, rather than following set choreography

Examples

Interpretive dancing style
Many contemporary art shows include elements of interpretive dancing to enhance the emotional impact.
She chose interpretive dancing to express her feelings after the loss of her friend.
The performance featured interpretive dancing to convey the struggles of the characters.

Related Terms

  • synonym: modern dance - a style of dance that developed as a reaction to classical ballet
