Faustus

proper noun
legendary German alchemist who sold his soul
9.3
Pronunciation: /ˈfɔːs.təs/ (AmE), /ˈfɔːs.təs/ (BrE)
an alchemist in German legend who sold his soul to the devil in exchange for knowledge and power; a central character in works by Christopher Marlowe and Goethe.

Examples

Faustus sells soul
In Marlowe's play, Faustus makes a pact with Mephistopheles for worldly gain.
Many adaptations of the Faustus legend exist in literature, theater, and music.
The moral dilemma faced by Faustus has become a symbol of the dangers of unchecked ambition.
