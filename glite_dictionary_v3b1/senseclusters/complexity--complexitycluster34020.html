complexity

noun
the quality of being intricate or complicated
1.0
Pronunciation: / kəmˈplɛksɪti / (AmE), / kəmˈplɛksɪti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being intricate or made of many interconnected parts, making something difficult to understand or analyze

Examples

Biological complexity
Modern ecosystems are remarkable for their biological complexity.
She admired the complexity of his theories in the book.
The complexity of the machinery made repairs challenging for inexperienced workers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: complication - something that makes a situation more difficult or complex
  • antonym: simplicity - the quality of being easy to understand or not complex
