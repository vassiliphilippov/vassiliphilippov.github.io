Lupinus texensis

noun
species of lupine native to Texas
Pronunciation: / luːˈpaɪnəs tɛkˈsɛnsɪs / (AmE), / luːˈpaɪnəs tɛkˈsɛnsɪs / (BrE)
a species of lupine native to Texas, with blue flowers and known as the Texas bluebonnet

Examples

Lupinus texensis blue flowers
Lupinus texensis, commonly called Texas bluebonnet, is celebrated as the state flower of Texas.
Gardeners in Texas often choose Lupinus texensis for its vibrant flowers and easy maintenance.
The fields near Austin turn blue every spring as Lupinus texensis comes into bloom.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Lupinus subcarnosus - another species of bluebonnet found in Texas
