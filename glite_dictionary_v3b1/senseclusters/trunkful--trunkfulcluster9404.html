trunkful

noun
amount filling a trunk
Pronunciation: / ˈtrʌŋkfʊl / (AmE), / ˈtrʌŋkfʊl / (BrE)
an amount that fills a trunk, such as a suitcase, car trunk, or similar large container

Examples

A trunkful of clothes
They packed a trunkful of clothes for the trip.
After the move, we found a trunkful of old family photos in the attic.
She donated a trunkful of books to the local library.
They brought a trunkful of supplies for the camping trip.
After the garage sale, we donated a trunkful of clothes to charity.
He returned from the orchard with a trunkful of fresh apples.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bagful - an amount that fills a bag
  • synonym: boxful - an amount that fills a box
  • synonym: carload - an amount that fills a car
  • degree lower: handful - an amount that can be held in one hand
  • degree higher: truckload - an amount that fills a truck
