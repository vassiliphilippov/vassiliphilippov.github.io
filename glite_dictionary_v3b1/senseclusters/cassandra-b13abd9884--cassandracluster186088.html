Cassandra

proper noun
prophetess in Greek mythology; her true prophecies were never believed
9.1
Pronunciation: / kəˈsændrə / (AmE), / kəˈsændrə / (BrE)
(In Greek mythology) a daughter of Priam, king of Troy, who was given the gift of prophecy but cursed so that her true predictions were never believed.

Examples

The Cassandra prophecy
He warned the company about the risks, but his colleagues dismissed him as a modern Cassandra.
Many see her as a Cassandra, always predicting disaster but never being heeded.
The story of Cassandra has become a symbol for ignored warnings and overlooked truths.
