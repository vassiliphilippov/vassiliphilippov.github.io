squeeze

verb
pressure someone to act or give information
Pronunciation: / skwiz /; / skwiːz / (AmE), / skwiːz / (BrE)
to use pressure or force to make someone do something or to obtain something from them

Examples

To squeeze money out
Governments sometimes squeeze companies with strict regulations to achieve policy goals.
She squeezed him for details about the surprise party.
The manager tried to squeeze more hours out of his employees before the deadline.
He claimed the officials squeezed a large sum from him with threats of exposure.
The mob tried to squeeze money out of the restaurant owners.
The reporter was accused of squeezing information from reluctant interviewees.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coerce - to force someone to do something by using threats or pressure
  • synonym: extort - to obtain something by force or threats
  • synonym: pressure - to attempt to persuade or coerce someone into doing something
