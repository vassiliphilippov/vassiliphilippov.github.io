warm

adjective
at a comfortably high temperature
Pronunciation: / wɔrm / (AmE), / wɔːm / (BrE)
having or producing a comfortable or moderately high degree of heat, or still showing signs of recent activity (such as being recently used and still warm)

Examples

A warm room
Please put on your coat if you're not feeling warm enough.
She slipped into the warm bath and immediately felt relaxed.
The warm summer breeze made it pleasant to be outside in the evening.
The detectives followed a warm trail left by the suspect.
The footprints in the mud were still warm when we arrived at the scene.
The scent was so warm that the tracking dog picked it up instantly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fresh - recently made or done; not stale or old
  • degree higher: hot - having a high degree of heat or temperature
  • synonym: recent - having happened or started not long ago
