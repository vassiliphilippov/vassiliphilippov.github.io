break-in

noun
unauthorized access to a computer or network
4.5
Pronunciation: / ˈbreɪk ɪn / (AmE), / ˈbreɪk ɪn / (BrE)
An incident of unauthorized access to a computer system or network, often involving hacking or other security breaches.

Examples

A computer break-in
The security team noticed an unusual spike in traffic that suggested a break-in overnight.
After the break-in, engineers isolated the vulnerable server and began their forensic analysis.
The insurer requested a full report before covering losses from the reported break-in.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intrusion - unauthorized penetration of a computer system or network
  • synonym: security breach - an incident in which confidential, protected or sensitive data is accessed or disclosed without authorization
