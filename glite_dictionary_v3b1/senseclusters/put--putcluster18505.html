put

noun
option to sell a security at a set price
Pronunciation: / pʊt / (AmE), / pʊt / (BrE)
an option giving the right to sell a particular security or commodity at a specified price within a set time period

Examples

A put option
A put can be valuable if you anticipate a decline in the underlying asset.
Experienced traders often use puts to protect their portfolios during volatile markets.
She bought a put to hedge against a possible drop in the stock price.
