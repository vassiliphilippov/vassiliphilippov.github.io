macerate

verb
soften or separate by soaking
9.5 6.8
Pronunciation: /ˈmæs.ə.reɪt/ /; / /ˈmæs.ə.rɪt/ (AmE), /ˈmæs.ə.reɪt/ /; / /ˈmæs.ə.rɪt/ (BrE)
to soften or break down something by soaking it in liquid, or to become soft or broken down from being soaked

Examples

To macerate fruit
After soaking overnight, the dried fruit will macerate and become plump and flavorful.
The chef instructed us to macerate the onions in vinegar before adding them to the salad.
To make a perfect salsa, you need to macerate the tomatoes with salt and lime juice.
Long exposure to rain will macerate the cardboard, making it fall apart.
Some recipes require you to macerate dried fruits in rum overnight.
The chef decided to macerate the strawberries in sugar to enhance their flavor.
If paper is left in water for too long, it will macerate and fall apart.
The fruit began to macerate after soaking in syrup overnight.
The old manuscript macerated in the floodwater, making the text almost impossible to read.
