exine

noun
outer pollen layer
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɛksaɪn / (AmE), / ˈɛksaɪn / (BrE)
the tough outer layer of the wall of a pollen grain or spore

Examples

The exine layer
The exine is often covered with unique patterns that help identify different plant species.
Scientists study the exine to understand how pollen grains survive harsh environmental conditions.
Damage to the exine can affect the ability of pollen to fertilize plants.
