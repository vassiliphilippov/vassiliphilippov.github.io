bullwhacker

noun
oxen team driver
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊlˌwækər / (AmE), / ˈbʊlˌwækə / (BrE)
a person who drove teams of oxen, especially in the American West during the 19th century

Examples

The bullwhacker cracked whip
The bullwhacker cracked his whip to urge the oxen forward along the dusty trail.
In the old West, a skilled bullwhacker was essential for transporting goods across long distances.
Stories of the adventurous life of a bullwhacker are common in American frontier history.
