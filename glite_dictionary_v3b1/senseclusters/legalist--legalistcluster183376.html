legalist

noun
follower of Legalism
Pronunciation: / ˈliːɡəlɪst / (AmE), / ˈliːɡəlɪst / (BrE)
a person who supports or follows Legalism, an ancient Chinese philosophy advocating strict laws and strong state control

Examples

Ancient Chinese legalist
The ancient legalist believed that strict laws were necessary to maintain order in society.
As a legalist, he argued for harsh punishments to deter crime and disobedience.
Historians often compare the ideas of Confucian scholars and legalists in ancient China.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Confucian - a follower of Confucianism, a Chinese philosophy emphasizing moral values and social harmony
  • synonym: authoritarian - a person who supports strict obedience to authority at the expense of personal freedom
