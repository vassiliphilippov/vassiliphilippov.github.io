pin

verb
hold or trap in place
7.5 2.1
Pronunciation: / pɪn / (AmE), / pɪn / (BrE)
to hold something or someone firmly in place so that they cannot move or escape, often by pressing or trapping

Examples

To pin someone down
She was pinned beneath the rubble after the earthquake struck.
The strong wind pinned the tent against the hillside, making it hard to move.
The wrestler managed to pin his opponent to the mat for the victory.

Related Terms

  • antonym: release - to set free from confinement or restraint
  • synonym: trap - to catch or hold something so it cannot escape
