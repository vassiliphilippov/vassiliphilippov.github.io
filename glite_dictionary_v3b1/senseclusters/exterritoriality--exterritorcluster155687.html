exterritoriality

noun
legal authority abroad
Pronunciation: / ˌɛkstɛˌtɛrɪˌtɔriˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌɛkstɛˌtɛrɪˌtɒriˈæləti / (BrE)
the legal principle by which a state applies its laws or authority beyond its own territorial borders

Examples

Exterritoriality of laws
The concept of exterritoriality is often discussed in international law classes.
Some countries claim exterritoriality to prosecute crimes committed by their citizens abroad.
Debates about exterritoriality arise when national laws conflict with foreign jurisdictions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: extraterritoriality - the right of a country to apply its laws outside its borders
  • antonym: sovereignty - supreme power or authority within a territory
