pufferfish

noun
a delicacy fish with dangerous toxins
Pronunciation: / ˈpʌfərˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈpʌfəˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
a type of fish that can puff up into a ball and is often poisonous

Examples

A pufferfish fish
After training for years, the chef was finally allowed to prepare pufferfish for customers.
Eating improperly prepared pufferfish can result in fatal poisoning due to its toxins.
Many diners travel to Japan to try pufferfish, despite its potentially deadly reputation.
Divers often spot colorful pufferfish among the coral reefs.
Many pufferfish puff up into a ball when threatened by predators.
The chef prepared the pufferfish with extreme caution due to its potential toxicity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blowfish - another name for pufferfish, especially in culinary contexts
  • synonym: fugu - Japanese term for pufferfish, especially as food
