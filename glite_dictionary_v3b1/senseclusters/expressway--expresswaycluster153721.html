A wide road designed for high-speed traffic, usually with limited access and no intersections at grade.

Examples

A expressway road
Heavy traffic was reported on the expressway after the morning rush hour.
The city recently completed a new expressway to reduce commuting times.
We took the expressway to get to downtown much faster than using local roads.

Synonyms

  • freeway
    2.2 12/m
    a freeway has no intersections or traffic lights and is the common US term, while an expressway is a more general label.
  • motorway
    1.8 0.57/m
    a motorway has multiple lanes and limited access and is the British standard term, while an expressway often refers to similar high-speed roads.
  • superhighway
    3.5 0.36/m
    a superhighway is an especially wide, well-built multi-lane road often called informally, while an expressway is the standard traffic term.
  • throughway
    4.1 0.07/m
    a throughway describes a broad high-speed road and is an older, less common word in use, while an expressway is the modern standard term.

Related Terms

  • antonym: country road - a smaller, typically lower-speed road in a rural area
  • synonym: highway - a main public road, especially one connecting towns and cities
  • synonym: motorway - a major road for fast traffic, commonly used in British English
