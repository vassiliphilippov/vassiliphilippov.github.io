circuit card

noun
computer board for expanding functions
4.4 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààs…úr.k…™t k…ërd / (AmE), / Ààs…úÀê.k…™t k…ëÀêd / (BrE)
a printed circuit board that can be inserted into an expansion slot in a computer to enhance its capabilities

Examples

A circuit card upgrade
If your graphics performance is slow, upgrading the circuit card might help.
Many computers allow users to add a circuit card for wireless connectivity.
The technician installed a new circuit card to give the computer additional USB ports.

Synonyms

  • plug-in
    4.3 0.14/m
    plug-in is a circuit board that slides into an expansion slot to add features, while circuit card is the general printed board name.
  • PC board
    4.2 0.04/m
    PC board is a removable board used in personal computers to add functions, while circuit card is the generic printed board used for expansion.
  • add-in
    5.4 0.09/m
    add-in is any extra hardware or software that extends a computer's features, while circuit card refers specifically to a printed expansion board.
