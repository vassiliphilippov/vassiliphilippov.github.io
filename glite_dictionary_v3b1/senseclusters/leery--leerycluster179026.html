leery

adjective
cautious or openly distrustful
9.3 5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪri / (AmE), / ˈlɪəri / (BrE)
Cautious or wary due to suspicion or lack of trust; openly distrustful.

Examples

To be leery of strangers
He became leery of strangers offering help for no apparent reason.
Investors are leery of putting money into such a risky venture.
She was leery of entering the abandoned building after hearing strange noises.

  • antonym: trusting - showing or tending to have a belief in someone's honesty or reliability
