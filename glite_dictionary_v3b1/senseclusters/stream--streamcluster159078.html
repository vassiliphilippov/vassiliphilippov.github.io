stream

verb
broadcast media online in real time
3.4 5.62/m
Pronunciation: / striÀêm / (AmE), / striÀêm / (BrE)
to transmit or receive (audio or video content) over the Internet in real time, without downloading the entire file beforehand

Examples

To stream a video
Many universities stream their lectures to make them accessible to students who are off campus.
She decided to stream the event for viewers around the world.
We will stream the conference so remote participants can join online.

Synonyms

  • livestream
    3.2 0.06/m
    livestream means broadcasting an event live as it happens over the internet, while stream can mean sending or receiving content in real time.
  • webcast
    3.4 0.07/m
    webcast is a broadcast that may be live or pre-recorded for online viewing, while stream means real-time sending or receiving without downloading first.
