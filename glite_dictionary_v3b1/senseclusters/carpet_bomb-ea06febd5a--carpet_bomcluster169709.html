carpet bomb

verb
bomb a wide area systematically
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑrpət bɑm / (AmE), / ˈkɑːpɪt bɒm / (BrE)
To drop a large number of bombs over a wide area in a systematic and extensive manner, especially to destroy or demoralize.

Examples

To carpet bomb a city
During the war, the air force carpet bombed the entire city, leaving little standing.
Some critics argue that to carpet bomb industrial sites causes excessive civilian casualties.
The general ordered his troops to carpet bomb enemy positions ahead of the ground assault.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bombard - attack with bombs, shells, or missiles
  • antonym: precision bomb - bomb a specific, selected target
