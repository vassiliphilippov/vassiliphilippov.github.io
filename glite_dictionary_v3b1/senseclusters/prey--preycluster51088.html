prey

verb
to exploit or take advantage of
7.8 2.51/m
Pronunciation: / preÉª / (AmE), / preÉª / (BrE)
to take advantage of someone or something in an unfair or exploitative way, often for personal gain

Examples

To prey on fears
He would prey on people's fears to sell his useless products.
Scammers often prey on elderly people by pretending to be bank officials.
Unscrupulous companies prey on vulnerable customers during difficult economic times.

Synonyms
