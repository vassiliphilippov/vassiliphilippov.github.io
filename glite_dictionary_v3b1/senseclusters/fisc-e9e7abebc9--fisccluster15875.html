F.I.S.C.

noun
U.S. secret federal court for surveillance authorization
9.9
Pronunciation: /fɪsk/ (AmE), /fɪsk/ (BrE)
A secret federal court in the United States, established in 1978 by the Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act, responsible for authorizing electronic surveillance and searches regarding suspected spies and terrorists.

Examples

The F.I.S.C. court
Controversy arose when previously classified F.I.S.C. documents were released to the public.
Legal challenges to the F.I.S.C. process sparked a national debate on privacy and surveillance.
The government must obtain approval from the F.I.S.C. before placing wiretaps on suspected foreign agents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Foreign Intelligence Surveillance Act - Law establishing procedures for electronic surveillance and collection of foreign intelligence information.
