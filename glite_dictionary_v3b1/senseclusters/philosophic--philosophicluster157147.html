philosophic

adjective
calm and rational; like a philosopher
Pronunciation: / ˌfɪləˈsɑfɪk / (AmE), / ˌfɪləˈsɒfɪk / (BrE)
calmly rational, especially in the face of trouble or adversity; characteristic of a philosopher's thoughtful or detached attitude

Examples

Philosophic calm
Adopting a philosophic attitude helped him cope with the stresses of his demanding job.
Despite losing the match, she remained philosophic about the result and focused on learning from the experience.
He faced criticism with a philosophic calm, refusing to let emotions cloud his judgment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: philosophical - showing calm, rational thought, especially when facing difficulty
