bark

verb
speak sharply or abruptly
Pronunciation: / bɑrk / (AmE), / bɑːk / (BrE)
to speak sharply or abruptly, often in an unfriendly or commanding tone

Examples

To bark an order
He barked out orders without looking at anyone.
She barked at her assistant for making a mistake.
The teacher barked a command at the noisy classroom.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: murmur - to speak in a low or soft voice
  • degree higher: shout - to speak very loudly
  • synonym: snap - to say something quickly and sharply
