gelatinously

adverb
in a jelly-like way
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈlætənəsli / (AmE), / dʒəˈlætɪnəsli / (BrE)
in a way that is like jelly; thick, wobbly, or slow-moving

Examples

Moves gelatinously
The dessert wobbled gelatinously on the plate as she carried it to the table.
After the rain, the slug moved gelatinously across the wet sidewalk.
The lava lamp's colored blobs floated gelatinously inside the glass.
The pudding sat gelatinously in the bowl, barely moving when touched.
Raindrops clung gelatinously to the window, slowly sliding down the glass.
The sauce thickened gelatinously as it cooled on the stove.

Related Terms

  • antonym: firmly - in a solid or steady way
  • antonym: fluidly - in a smooth and flowing manner
  • synonym: jelly-like - having the qualities of jelly
  • synonym: viscously - in a thick and sticky manner
