crustose

adjective
crust-like lichen form
9.9 8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈkrʌstoʊs / (AmE), / ˈkrʌstəʊs / (BrE)
(of lichens) having a thin, crust-like layer that closely adheres to the surface on which it grows.

Examples

crustose lichen layer
Scientists collected crustose samples to study their adaptation to harsh environments.
The rocks were covered in crustose lichens that formed a thin, tightly adhering layer.
Unlike foliose lichens, crustose species are almost impossible to remove without damaging the substrate.

Related Terms

  • antonym: foliose - (of lichens) having a leaf-like structure that is loosely attached
  • antonym: fruticose - (of lichens) having a bushy, branched, or shrubby form
