major planet

noun
main planets of the Solar System
3.7 3.0
Pronunciation: /ˈmeɪ.dʒər ˈplæn.ɪt/ /; / /ˈmeɪ.dʒər ˈplæn.ət/ (AmE), /ˈmeɪ.dʒə ˈplæn.ɪt/ /; / /ˈmeɪ.dʒə ˈplæn.ət/ (BrE)
any of the principal planets in the Solar System that revolve around the sun and shine by reflected light, traditionally including Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune, and Pluto

Examples

The major planet
Astronomers now distinguish between major planet and other types of celestial bodies like asteroids and dwarf planets.
Early textbooks often listed nine major planets in the Solar System, including Pluto.
Pluto was once considered a major planet before its reclassification as a dwarf planet in 2006.
