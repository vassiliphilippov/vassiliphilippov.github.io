semispinalis

noun
deep back muscles
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmiˌspaɪˈneɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˌspaɪˈneɪlɪs / (BrE)
a group of deep muscles in the back that help extend and rotate the spine

Examples

The semispinalis muscle
The semispinalis plays a key role in extending the neck and upper back.
Injury to the semispinalis can cause pain and limit movement in the spine.
Physical therapists often target the semispinalis during rehabilitation exercises for posture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: erector spinae - a group of muscles that straighten and rotate the back
  • synonym: multifidus - a deep muscle in the back that stabilizes the spine
