Mrs. Gandhi

proper noun
Indian prime minister (1917–1984)
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪsɪz ˈɡændi / (AmE), / ˈmɪsɪz ˈɡandi / (BrE)
(1917–1984) Indian prime minister, daughter of Jawaharlal Nehru, who served as the Prime Minister of India from 1966 to 1977 and again from 1980 to 1984.

Examples

Mrs. Gandhi as prime minister
Mrs. Gandhi implemented significant economic and social reforms during her time in office.
Many remember Mrs. Gandhi for her role during the Emergency period in India.
The assassination of Mrs. Gandhi in 1984 marked a turning point in Indian politics.
