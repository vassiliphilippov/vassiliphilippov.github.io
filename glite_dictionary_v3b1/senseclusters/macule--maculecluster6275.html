macule

noun
flat, discolored skin patch
4.8 7.8
Pronunciation: /ˈmæk.juːl/ /; / /ˈmæk.jəl/ (AmE), /ˈmæk.juːl/ /; / /ˈmæk.jəl/ (BrE)
a flat, distinct, discolored area of skin that is not raised above the surface; often caused by disease or injury

Examples

A red macule
A red macule appeared on her cheek, prompting further medical examination.
Freckles are a common type of macule found in fair-skinned individuals.
The dermatologist noted a small macule on the patient’s forearm that did not blanch when pressed.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: papule - a small, raised, solid pimple or swelling on the skin
  • degree higher: patch - a larger area of discolored, flat skin; larger than a macule
