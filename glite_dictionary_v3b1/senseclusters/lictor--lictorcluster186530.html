lictor

noun
Roman official attendant
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪktər / (AmE), / ˈlɪktə / (BrE)
an official in ancient Rome who accompanied and protected magistrates, carrying the fasces as a symbol of authority

Examples

Ancient lictors carrying fasces
In ancient Rome, each consul was accompanied by several lictors carrying bundles of rods.
The lictor cleared the way for the magistrate through the crowded forum.
A lictor was easily recognized by the fasces he carried as a symbol of authority.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attendant - a person who accompanies or serves another
  • antonym: magistrate - a civil officer or judge who administers the law
