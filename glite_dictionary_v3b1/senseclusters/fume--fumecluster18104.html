fume

verb
emit smoke, vapor, or fine particles
4.6 0.12/m
Pronunciation: / fjuÀêm / (AmE), / fjuÀêm / (BrE)
to emit smoke, vapor, or fine particles, often as a visible cloud

Examples

Fume thick smoke
Old buses used to fume thick black smoke on the streets.
The factory chimney continued to fume long after sunset.
The volcano began to fume, sending a gray cloud into the sky.

Synonyms

  • smoke
    1.5 6.21/m
    smoke means to send out a visible cloud from burning, while fume means to give off thinner vapor or chemical particles.
  • give off
    2.6 4.19/m
    give off is used to describe producing smells, heat, or light, while fume means to emit visible vapor or fine particles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: smoke - emit smoke, as from a fire or engine
  • synonym: vent - emit or release (gas, liquid, etc.)
