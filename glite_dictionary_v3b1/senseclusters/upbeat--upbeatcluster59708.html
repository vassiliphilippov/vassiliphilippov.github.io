upbeat

adjective
cheerfully optimistic
3.8 4.68/m
Pronunciation: / Àà åpÀåbit / (AmE), / Àà åpÀåbiÀêt / (BrE)
cheerful and hopeful, often in a way that may seem unrealistically optimistic

Examples

Upbeat mood
Despite the project's challenges, her upbeat attitude inspired the whole team.
He tried to remain upbeat even when things weren't going well.
The news anchor ended the broadcast on an upbeat note, sharing a story about community kindness.

Synonyms

    5.8 0.64/m
    buoyant feels lively and confident and lifts the mood, while upbeat is cheerful and hopeful, often slightly optimistic.
  • sanguine
    7.4 1.13/m
    sanguine stays calmly positive even in hard times, while upbeat is cheerful and hopeful, maybe a little unrealistically optimistic.
  • optimistic
    3.2 23/m
    optimistic expects good things and looks ahead with confidence, while upbeat is cheerful and hopeful and shows lively mood.
  • cheerful
    1.0 9.01/m
    cheerful looks happy and shows it in mood and face, while upbeat is both cheerful and openly hopeful about future.
  • sunny
    1.0 14/m
    sunny feels bright and pleasant like warm weather, while upbeat is cheerful and hopeful and may seem slightly idealistic.
  • lighthearted
    3.7 1.56/m
    lighthearted is carefree and free from worry, while upbeat adds hope about the future to that cheerful mood.
  • jolly
    1.0 2.41/m
    jolly is lively and merry, often loud and social, while upbeat is a cheerful, hopeful tone without loud celebration.
  • positive
    2.8 55/m
    positive shows confidence and an approving view, while upbeat is more about cheerful feeling plus hope for what comes next.
  • hopeful
    1.0 3.27/m
    hopeful expects success or good outcomes for the future, while upbeat combines that hope with an immediately cheerful mood.
  • rosy
    3.2 1.87/m

  • Pollyannaish
    9.9 0.00/m
