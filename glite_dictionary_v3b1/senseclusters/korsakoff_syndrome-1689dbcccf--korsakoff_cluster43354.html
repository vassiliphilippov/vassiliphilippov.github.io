Korsakoff syndrome

noun
alcohol-related brain disorder
7.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkɑːr.sə.kɑːf ˈsɪn.droʊm/ /; / /ˈkɔːr.sə.kɔːf ˈsɪn.droʊm/ (AmE), /ˈkɔː.sə.kɒf ˈsɪn.drəʊm/ /; / /ˈkɒ.sə.kɒf ˈsɪn.drəʊm/ (BrE)
a brain disorder caused by severe thiamine (vitamin B1) deficiency, often due to alcoholism, marked by severe memory loss, confusion, and difficulty forming new memories

Examples

Korsakoff syndrome memory loss
The doctor diagnosed the patient with Korsakoff syndrome after years of heavy alcohol use.
People with Korsakoff syndrome often struggle to remember recent events or learn new information.
Treatment for Korsakoff syndrome includes thiamine supplements and support for alcohol dependence.
The patient with Korsakoff syndrome could not remember recent events or conversations.
Chronic alcohol abuse is a common cause of Korsakoff syndrome in adults.
Doctors recognized Korsakoff syndrome by the patient's severe memory loss and tendency to confabulate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Wernicke encephalopathy - a related brain disorder caused by thiamine deficiency, often preceding Korsakoff syndrome
  • synonym: Wernicke's encephalopathy - a neurological disorder often preceding Korsakoff syndrome
  • synonym: amnesia - loss of memory, especially after injury or illness
  • synonym: dementia - a general term for loss of memory and other mental abilities
