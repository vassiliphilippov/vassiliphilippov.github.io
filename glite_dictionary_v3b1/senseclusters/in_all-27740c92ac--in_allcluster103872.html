in all

adverb
in total, including everything
7.3
Pronunciation: / ɪn ɔl / (AmE), / ɪn ɔːl / (BrE)
Including everything or everyone; in total.

Examples

Fifteen hours in all
She spent fifteen hours in all preparing the project.
There were over a hundred guests in all at the wedding.
We collected five thousand dollars in all for the charity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: altogether - completely; when everything is considered
  • antonym: in part - only part of the whole
  • synonym: in total - with everything combined
