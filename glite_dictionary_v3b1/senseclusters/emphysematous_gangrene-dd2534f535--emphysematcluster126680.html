emphysematous gangrene

noun
severe gangrene with gas production in tissues
9.9
Pronunciation: /em.fɪ.ziː.ˈmæt.əs ˈgæŋ.griːn/ /; / /em.fɪ.zə.ˈmæt.əs ˈgæŋ.griːn/ /; / /em.fɪ.ziː.ˈmæt.əs ˈgæŋ.grɪn/ (AmE), /em.fɪ.zə.ˈmæt.əs ˈgæŋ.griːn/ /; / /em.fɪ.ziː.ˈmæt.əs ˈgæŋ.griːn/ /; / /em.fɪ.zə.ˈmæt.əs ˈgæŋ.grɪn/ (BrE)
A severe and often fatal form of gangrene characterized by tissue death accompanied by the production of gas within tissues, usually caused by Clostridium bacteria.

Examples

Emphysematous gangrene infection
Early diagnosis is critical in treating emphysematous gangrene to prevent systemic toxicity and death.
The patient was immediately admitted due to the rapid progression of emphysematous gangrene in his foot.
The presence of gas bubbles in tissue is a hallmark of emphysematous gangrene, often visible on an X-ray.
