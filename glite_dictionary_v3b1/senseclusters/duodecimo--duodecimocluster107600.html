duodecimo

noun
small book size
Pronunciation: / ˌduːoʊˈdɛsɪˌmoʊ / (AmE), / ˌdjuːəˈdɛsɪməʊ / (BrE)
a book size made by folding a sheet of paper to form twelve leaves, resulting in a small, compact volume

Examples

A duodecimo volume
The collector prized the rare duodecimo edition for its compact size and elegant binding.
Early novels were often published in duodecimo to make them more affordable and portable.
She found a duodecimo volume of poetry tucked away on the highest shelf.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: octavo - a book size made by folding a sheet into eight leaves
  • degree lower: sextodecimo - a book size made by folding a sheet into sixteen leaves
