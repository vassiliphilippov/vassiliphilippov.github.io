redline

verb
to exceed engine limit
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛdlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈrɛdlaɪn / (BrE)
to operate an engine at or above its maximum safe speed, as indicated by the red zone on a tachometer.

Examples

To redline the engine
If you redline the engine too often, it can cause serious damage over time.
The mechanic warned him not to redline the car during the test drive.
She accidentally redlined the motorcycle while trying to accelerate quickly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: idle - to run an engine at its lowest speed
  • synonym: overrev - to run an engine beyond its recommended speed
