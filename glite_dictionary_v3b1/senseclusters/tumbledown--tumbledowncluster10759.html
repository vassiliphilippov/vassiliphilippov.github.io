tumbledown

adjective
in poor condition
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈtʌmbəlˌdaʊn / (AmE), / ˈtʌmbəldaʊn / (BrE)
in a very bad or broken condition, especially because of age or neglect

Examples

A tumbledown house
The family lived in a tumbledown house at the edge of the village.
We passed a tumbledown barn on our way to the farm.
The old theater had become so tumbledown that it was unsafe to enter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dilapidated - in a state of disrepair or ruin
  • synonym: ruined - severely damaged or destroyed
  • antonym: well-kept - in good, neat, and tidy condition
