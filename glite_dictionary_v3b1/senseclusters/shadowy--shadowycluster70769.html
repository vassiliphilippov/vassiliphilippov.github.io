shadowy

adjective
filled with shade; dimly lit
Pronunciation: / ˈʃædoʊi / (AmE), / ˈʃædəʊi / (BrE)
(of a place or object) filled with shade or not in direct sunlight; partly dark or obscured by shadows.

Examples

Shadowy room
The shadowy corridors of the old house gave it a mysterious atmosphere.
The deer rested in a shadowy glen, hidden from the midday sun.
We found a shadowy corner in the park to escape the afternoon heat.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shady - cast or full of shade; giving shelter from the sun
  • antonym: sunny - bright with sunlight; full of sunlight
