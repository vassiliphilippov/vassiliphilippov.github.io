Phanerozoic

noun
current geologic eon with abundant life
Pronunciation: / ˌfænəroʊˈzoʊɪk / (AmE), / ˌfanərəʊˈzəʊɪk / (BrE)
the current geologic eon, beginning about 540 million years ago and marked by the abundance of animal and plant life

Examples

The Phanerozoic eon
Fossils from the Phanerozoic period provide key evidence for understanding evolutionary history.
The Phanerozoic eon is characterized by the emergence and evolution of complex life forms.
The Earth's crust contains numerous layers dating back to the Phanerozoic eon.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Precambrian - the eon before the Phanerozoic, covering Earth's earliest history
