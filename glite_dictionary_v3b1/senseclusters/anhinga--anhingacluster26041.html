anhinga

noun
fish-eating water bird with a long neck
7.1
Pronunciation: / ænˈhɪŋɡə / (AmE), / ænˈhɪŋɡə / (BrE)
a fish-eating bird of warm inland waters, with a long flexible neck and a slender, sharp-pointed bill, found in the Americas; also called snakebird or darter.

Examples

The anhinga bird
The anhinga plunged underwater to catch a fish in its sharp beak.
Unlike herons, the anhinga swims with only its neck exposed, resembling a snake.
We spotted an anhinga drying its wings by the edge of the lake.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cormorant - a similar, related water bird with a hooked bill
  • synonym: darter - another name for anhinga or similar birds in the family Anhingidae
