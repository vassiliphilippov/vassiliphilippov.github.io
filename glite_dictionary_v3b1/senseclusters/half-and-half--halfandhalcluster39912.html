half-and-half

adjective, adverb
in equal proportions
Pronunciation: / ˌhæf ən ˈhæf /; / ˈhæf ən ˈhæf / (AmE), / ˌhɑːf ən ˈhɑːf /; / ˈhɑːf ən ˈhɑːf / (BrE)
in equal parts; consisting of two things in the same amount

Examples

Half-and-half split
Ownership of the property was shared half-and-half by both partners.
The bill was split half-and-half between the two roommates.
They decided to divide the chores half-and-half to keep things fair.
Pour a half-and-half mix of milk and cream into your coffee for a richer taste.
The paint is a half-and-half blend of blue and green.
They decided on a half-and-half agreement, splitting the profit equally.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fifty-fifty - equal division or share between two parties
  • antonym: unequal - not equal in size, amount, or value
