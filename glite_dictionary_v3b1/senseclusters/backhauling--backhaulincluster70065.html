backhauling

noun
return trip shipment
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌhɔlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbækˌhɔːlɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of carrying a load or shipment on a vehicle or vessel during its return trip to avoid traveling empty

Examples

Backhauling empty trucks
The trucking company increased efficiency by arranging backhauling for its vehicles after deliveries.
By using backhauling, the shipping firm reduced costs and minimized empty return trips.
Logistics managers often plan backhauling to make the most of each journey.

Related Terms

  • antonym: empty run - a return trip made without carrying any cargo
  • synonym: freight - goods transported in bulk by truck, train, ship, or aircraft
