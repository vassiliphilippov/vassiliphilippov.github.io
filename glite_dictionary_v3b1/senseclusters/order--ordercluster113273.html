order

noun
biological classification rank above family
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɔrdɚ / (AmE), / ˈɔːdə / (BrE)
A taxonomic group in biology that ranks above family and below class, containing one or more related families.

Examples

The order Carnivora
Birds belong to the order Passeriformes, which is the largest among the avian groups.
In mammals, primates are an order that includes humans, apes, and monkeys.
The beetle order, Coleoptera, contains more species than any other animal group.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: class - taxonomic group above order, containing orders
  • degree lower: family - taxonomic group below order, containing genera
