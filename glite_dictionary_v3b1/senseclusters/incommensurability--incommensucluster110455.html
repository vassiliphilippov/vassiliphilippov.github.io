incommensurability

noun
no common measure
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnkəˌmɛnsərəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəˌmɛnsjʊrəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the property of two quantities that cannot be exactly measured by the same unit or expressed as a ratio of whole numbers

Examples

Incommensurability of numbers
The incommensurability of the circle's circumference and its diameter led to the discovery of irrational numbers.
Mathematicians often discuss incommensurability when comparing lengths that cannot be measured by the same unit.
The concept of incommensurability was important in ancient Greek geometry.

Related Terms

  • antonym: commensurability - the property of two quantities having a common measure or being expressible as a ratio of integers
  • synonym: irrationality - the quality of being an irrational number, not expressible as a ratio of integers
