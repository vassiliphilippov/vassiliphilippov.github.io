Mendelism

noun
theory of genetic inheritance based on Mendel's laws
7.2 6.9
Pronunciation: /ˈmen.dəl.ɪz.əm/ (AmE), /ˈmen.dəl.ɪz.əm/ (BrE)
The theory of heredity that states that traits are inherited independently according to the laws discovered by Gregor Mendel.

Examples

Mendelism theory
Many exceptions to Mendelism have been discovered, but its basic ideas remain fundamental to biology.
Modern genetics has its foundation in Mendelism, which explains how traits are passed from one generation to the next.
The principles of Mendelism revolutionized the study of heredity in the nineteenth century.
