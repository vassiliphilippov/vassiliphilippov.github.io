externality

noun
indirect economic effect
8.3
Pronunciation: /ɪk.stɜːr.ˈnæl.ə.tiː/ (AmE), /ɪk.stɜː.ˈnæl.ə.tiː/ (BrE)
an indirect effect of an action or activity on others who are not directly involved, especially in economics

Examples

Negative externality from pollution
Air pollution is a classic example of a negative externality caused by factories.
Vaccination can create a positive externality by protecting people who are not vaccinated.
The government introduced a tax to reduce the externality of carbon emissions.
Noise from the construction site is an externality that affects nearby residents.
Government policies often aim to reduce negative externalities like pollution.
Positive externalities can include benefits such as improved public health from vaccination programs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: consequence - a result or effect of an action
  • synonym: spillover - an effect of an activity that spreads to others
