ack

noun
confirmation signal
Pronunciation: / æk / (AmE), / æk / (BrE)
a signal or message sent to confirm that data or a transmission has been received successfully, especially in computing or telecommunications

Examples

Send an ack signal
The device waits for an ack before sending the next packet of information.
If the ack is not received, the system will automatically resend the data.
Each message sent over the network requires an ack to confirm successful delivery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acknowledgment - a message sent to indicate receipt of data
  • antonym: nack - a signal indicating data was not received successfully
