niggle

verb
worry or fuss unnecessarily
6.6 6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈnɪg.əl/ (AmE), /ˈnɪg.əl/ (BrE)
to be bothered, worried, or annoyed by something minor or to cause slight but persistent discomfort or worry

Examples

A worry niggles at you
It started to niggle at him that he might have forgotten to lock the door.
She tends to niggle over every small detail, even when it's not important.
Stop letting minor issues niggle you so much and try to relax.
A minor pain continued to niggle at his knee during the match.
The thought of unfinished work would often niggle at her mind late at night.
Even after the repairs, a small leak continued to niggle him every time it rained.

Related Terms

  • synonym: annoy - to make someone slightly angry or irritated
  • synonym: bother - to cause someone slight trouble or worry
  • synonym: dwell - to linger over or worry about something repeatedly
  • synonym: fuss - to show unnecessary concern or excitement
  • antonym: reassure - to make someone feel less worried
