eumelanin

noun
dark biological pigment
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌjuːˈmɛlənɪn / (AmE), / ˌjuːˈmɛlənɪn / (BrE)
a dark brown or black pigment found in the hair, skin, and eyes of humans and animals

Examples

Dark eumelanin pigment
People with black hair have a high concentration of eumelanin in their hair follicles.
The amount of eumelanin in the skin helps determine how dark a person's complexion is.
Scientists study eumelanin to understand its protective role against UV radiation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: melanin - a group of natural pigments found in most organisms
  • antonym: pheomelanin - a reddish-yellow pigment found in hair and skin
