nihilism

noun
doctrine advocating destruction of systems
Pronunciation: / ˈnaɪ.əˌlɪ.zəm /; / ˈnaɪ.əˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈniː.ɪˌlɪ.zəm /; / ˈnɪɪ.lɪ.zəm /; / ˈnɪ.ɪ.lɪ.zəm / (BrE)
the belief that all established systems, values, and beliefs should be rejected, and that life has no real meaning or purpose

Examples

Deep nihilism belief
His writings reflect a deep nihilism, rejecting every traditional authority and value.
Some accused the radical group of embracing nihilism, since they only wanted to destroy, not to build.
The movement was inspired by nihilism and sought to tear down all existing institutions.
His philosophy of nihilism led him to reject all forms of government and tradition.
Some critics argue that cultural nihilism is prevalent in modern art.
The writer's dark outlook was shaped by an underlying sense of nihilism.
After reading the philosopher's works, she began to question reality and embraced nihilism for a time.
Some critics argue that nihilism leads to despair and a rejection of all moral values.
The movie explored themes of nihilism and the sense that life has no ultimate purpose.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anarchism - belief in abolishing all government and social hierarchy
  • antonym: existentialism - the philosophical theory emphasizing individual existence and freedom
  • synonym: pessimism - the belief that the worst will happen or that all things are bad
  • synonym: skepticism - doubt as to the truth of something
