toxigenic

adjective
producing toxins
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˌtɑksɪˈdʒɛnɪk / (AmE), / ˌtɒksɪˈdʒɛnɪk / (BrE)
able to produce toxins or poisonous substances, especially referring to microorganisms

Examples

Toxigenic bacteria strains
Certain strains of bacteria are highly toxigenic and can cause food poisoning.
The laboratory tests confirmed that the fungus was toxigenic and dangerous to humans.
Researchers study toxigenic organisms to understand how they produce harmful substances.

Related Terms

  • antonym: non-toxic - not poisonous or harmful
  • synonym: toxic - containing or being poisonous
