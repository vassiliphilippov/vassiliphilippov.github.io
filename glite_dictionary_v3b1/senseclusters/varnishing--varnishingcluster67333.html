varnishing

noun
superficial cover-up
Pronunciation: / ˈvɑrnɪʃɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈvɑːnɪʃɪŋ / (BrE)
a superficial improvement or cover that hides faults or problems, especially in a figurative sense

Examples

Varnishing the truth
The report was just a varnishing of the company's real financial troubles.
Politicians often use a thin varnishing to make their actions seem more positive.
Despite the varnishing in the advertisement, the product had many flaws.

Related Terms

  • synonym: camouflage - something that hides or disguises the true nature of something
  • antonym: exposure - the act of revealing something hidden
  • synonym: facade - a deceptive outward appearance
