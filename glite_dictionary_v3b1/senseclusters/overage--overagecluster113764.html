overage

noun
fee for exceeding an allowance
6.6
Pronunciation: /ˈoʊ.və.rɪdʒ/ /; / /ˈoʊ.və.reɪdʒ/ (AmE), /ˈəʊ.və.rɪdʒ/ /; / /ˈəʊ.və.reɪdʒ/ (BrE)
a charge or fee imposed when use exceeds the allowance in a service plan (for example, data, minutes, or storage).

Examples

Overage fee
The phone plan imposes an overage charge when you exceed your data limit.
After streaming all weekend, she received an unexpected bill with a large overage fee.
Many internet providers allow some rollover but still apply an overage cost for extra usage.

Related Terms

  • antonym: allowance - the quantity or amount included in a plan before extra charges apply
  • synonym: surcharge - an additional charge or payment added to the usual cost
