Gettysburg

proper noun
Civil War battle, 1863
Pronunciation: / ˈɡɛtisˌbɜrɡ / (AmE), / ˈɡɛtɪzbɜːɡ / (BrE)
a major battle of the American Civil War fought in 1863, resulting in a significant Union victory over the Confederate forces.

Examples

Gettysburg battle
Abraham Lincoln delivered the Gettysburg Address shortly after the victory at Gettysburg.
Many tourists visit Gettysburg to walk the famous battlefield and learn about its history.
The Battle of Gettysburg marked a turning point in the American Civil War.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Waterloo - site of a decisive battle, often referenced similarly
