retrack

verb
to lay tracks again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈtræk / (AmE), / ˌriːˈtræk / (BrE)
to lay, repair, or adjust tracks again, especially for vehicles or machinery

Examples

To retrack the rails
After the storm damaged the rails, the crew had to retrack the entire section.
Engineers were called in to retrack the machinery for improved performance.
The old tram line was retracked to meet modern safety standards.

  • synonym: repair - to fix or restore something that is damaged
