Sappho

proper noun
ancient Greek lyric poet of Lesbos
Pronunciation: / ˈsæfoʊ / (AmE), / ˈsæfəʊ / (BrE)
(c. 630–570 BC) An ancient Greek lyric poet from the island of Lesbos, renowned for her poetry, of which only fragments survive.

Examples

Sappho the poet
Although only fragments remain, Sappho is still considered one of the greatest poets of antiquity.
Many scholars study Sappho and her surviving poetry to understand ancient Greek lyric traditions.
The emotional intensity in Sappho's verses has inspired generations of poets and artists.
