Synonyms

  • straightforward
    3.7 9.15/m

  • intelligible
    4.4 2.21/m
    intelligible is a more formal word for something you can understand, while clear is common everyday language for the same idea.
  • understandable
    1.6 11/m
    understandable describes something people can grasp or forgive easily, while clear focuses on being not confusing or ambiguous.
  • plain
    1.0 14/m
    plain describes something simple, obvious, and easy to see or hear, while clear is broader and means not confusing.
  • obvious
    1.0 91/m
    obvious looks like it is easy to notice or already seen, while clear means easy to understand or recognize.
  • crystal clear
    2.5 1.75/m
    crystal clear sounds very strong and emphatic about no doubt, while clear is a neutral word for being easy to understand.
  • clear-cut
    4.2 2.45/m
    clear-cut describes a definite, easily divided choice or case, while clear is a general word for not confusing.
  • unambiguous
    5.9 2.49/m
    unambiguous means having only one possible meaning, while clear can also mean easy to understand but may allow some doubt.
  • unequivocal
    6.7 2.30/m
    unequivocal leaves no doubt and sounds formal and strong, while clear is more common and can be less forceful.
