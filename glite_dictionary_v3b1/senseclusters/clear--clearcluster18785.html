clear

adjective
easily perceived; distinct
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /klɪr/ (AmE), /klɪə/ (BrE)
easy to understand, perceive, or recognize; not confusing or ambiguous

Examples

Clear instructions
She gave a clear answer that left no room for doubt.
The footprints in the mud were clear and easy to follow.
The mountain was visible with a clear outline against the sky.
Her instructions were not clear, so everyone was confused.
I'm not clear about how the new policy works.
You need to keep a clear head when making important decisions.
Her handwriting is clear and easy to read, even from a distance.
His meaning was clear from the way he emphasized each word.
The instructions were so clear that anyone could follow them without confusion.

  • antonym: obscure - difficult to understand or see
  • synonym: obvious - easy to perceive or understand; clear, self-evident
  • synonym: perceptive - having or showing sensitive insight
  • antonym: slow-witted - slow to understand or perceive things
  • antonym: unclear - not easy to understand or lacking clarity
  • antonym: vague - not clear or distinct; indefinite
