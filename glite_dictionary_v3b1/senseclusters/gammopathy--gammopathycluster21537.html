gammopathy

noun
abnormal antibody protein increase in blood
Pronunciation: / ɡæˈmɑpəθi / (AmE), / ɡæˈmɒpəθi / (BrE)
a condition in which there is an abnormal increase in the production of immunoglobulins (antibody proteins) in the blood.

Examples

Monoclonal gammopathy
A monoclonal gammopathy is often associated with multiple myeloma.
Certain types of gammopathy can lead to serious complications such as kidney failure.
The doctor diagnosed a rare form of gammopathy after reviewing the patient's blood tests.
