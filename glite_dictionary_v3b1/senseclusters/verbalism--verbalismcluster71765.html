verbalism

noun
excessive use of words; verbosity
7.8 7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈvɜrbəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈvɜːbəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The use of an excessive amount of words without adding substance or meaning; overabundance of words in speech or writing.

Examples

Excessive verbalism
Academic writing should avoid unnecessary verbalism to communicate ideas effectively.
Her letter was filled with verbalism, making it difficult to understand her main point.
The politician's speech was criticized for its verbalism and lack of clear proposals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conciseness - the quality of being brief and to the point
  • synonym: verbosity - the quality of using more words than needed
