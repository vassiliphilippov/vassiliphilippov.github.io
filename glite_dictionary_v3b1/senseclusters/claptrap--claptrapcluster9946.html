claptrap

noun
pretentious or insincere language
9.5 7.3
Pronunciation: /ˈklæp.træp/ (AmE), /ˈklæp.træp/ (BrE)
language or ideas that are insincere, pretentious, or designed to impress but lack real meaning or value.

Examples

Empty claptrap
Don't be fooled by his fancy claptrap; he doesn't actually have a plan.
I wish you'd stop repeating the same old claptrap every time we argue.
The politician's speech was full of the usual claptrap about change and progress.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bombast - high-sounding language with little meaning; used to impress
  • synonym: nonsense - language or ideas that are absurd or meaningless
  • antonym: plain speaking - direct and honest speech without pretension
