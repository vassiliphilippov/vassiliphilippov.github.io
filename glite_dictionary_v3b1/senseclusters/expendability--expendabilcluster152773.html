expendability

noun
ability to be used up
7.7
Pronunciation: / ɪkˌspɛndəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪkˌspɛndəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality of being able to be used up, replaced, or considered not essential

Examples

The expendability of materials
The expendability of certain materials made them ideal for single-use medical equipment.
Managers must weigh the expendability of resources when planning a long-term project.
Some workers felt uneasy about the company's attitude toward their expendability during layoffs.
The company treated its temporary staff with a sense of expendability that hurt morale.
Soldiers often feel the weight of their own expendability during dangerous missions.
He was frustrated by the expendability of employees in the fast-paced tech industry.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disposability - the quality of being designed to be thrown away after use
  • antonym: indispensability - the quality of being absolutely necessary or essential
  • synonym: replaceability - the quality of being able to be replaced
