idolatry

noun
excessive admiration

very strong admiration or love for someone or something, often to an excessive or unreasonable degree

Examples

Idolatry of celebrities
Some fans' idolatry of celebrities can lead them to imitate everything their idols do.
The leader was uncomfortable with the idolatry shown by his most devoted followers.
Her idolatry of the artist inspired her to pursue a career in painting.

Synonyms

  • idolization
    6.8 0.06/m
    idolization is strong admiration or devotion often used in general speech, while idolatry means extreme, unreasonable worship.
  • hero worship
    5.1 0.41/m
    hero worship is intense, often uncritical admiration of a famous person, while idolatry means excessive love that can be almost religious.
  • adulation
    8.4 1.25/m
    adulation is excessive, often insincere praise given to flatter someone, while idolatry is deep, unreasonable love or worship.
