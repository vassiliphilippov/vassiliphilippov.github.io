diltiazem

noun
calcium channel blocker medication
Pronunciation: / dɪlˈtaɪəzɛm / (AmE), / dɪlˈtʌɪəzɛm / (BrE)
a medication that acts as a calcium channel blocker, used to treat high blood pressure, angina, and certain heart conditions

Examples

diltiazem medication
His angina symptoms improved significantly after starting diltiazem therapy.
Patients taking diltiazem should avoid grapefruit juice, as it may interfere with the drug's effects.
The doctor prescribed diltiazem to help control her high blood pressure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nifedipine - another type of calcium channel blocker used for heart and blood pressure
  • synonym: verapamil - a medication similar to diltiazem, used to treat heart conditions
