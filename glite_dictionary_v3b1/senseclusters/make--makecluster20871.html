Synonyms

  • do
    1.0 1183/m
    do is the everyday, simple verb for carrying out actions, while make often implies creating or producing something.
  • perform
    1.3 108/m
    perform is a neutral, slightly formal verb for carrying out tasks, while make is more common and can mean to create things.
  • execute
    3.6 13/m
    execute is a formal verb for carrying out a plan or order, while make is general and less technical.
