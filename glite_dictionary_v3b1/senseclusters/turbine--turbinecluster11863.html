turbine

noun
rotary engine converting fluid energy to motion
1.6
Pronunciation: / ˈtɝː.baɪn / (AmE), / ˈtɜː.baɪn / (BrE)
A rotary engine that converts the kinetic energy of a moving fluid (such as water, steam, or air) into mechanical energy by causing blades mounted on a rotor to turn.

Examples

A turbine engine
A jet engine relies on a powerful turbine to operate efficiently.
Engineers inspected the turbine blades for signs of wear and damage.
The wind farm uses massive turbines to generate electricity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: engine - machine with moving parts that converts power into motion
