bow out

verb
withdraw gracefully; retire politely
3.6 4.0
Pronunciation: /baʊ aʊt/ /; / /boʊ aʊt/ (AmE), /baʊ aʊt/ /; / /bəʊ aʊt/ (BrE)
to leave or withdraw from a job, activity, or commitment in a polite or dignified way

Examples

To bow out politely
After thirty years in politics, she decided to bow out and spend more time with her family.
The defending champion chose to bow out of the tournament citing an injury.
When new management arrived, he thought it best to bow out and let others take over.
After many years in public service, she decided to bow out and enjoy her retirement.
He was expected to speak at the event but chose to bow out at the last moment.
It takes courage to bow out gracefully when the time is right.
After twenty years at the company, he decided to bow out and enjoy his retirement.
It's admirable when a leader knows when to bow out rather than overstay their welcome.
She plans to bow out of the project before the final phase begins.
