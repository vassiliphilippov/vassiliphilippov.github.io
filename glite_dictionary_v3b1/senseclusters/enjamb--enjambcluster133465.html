enjamb

verb
to run lines together
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɛnˈdʒæm / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʒæm / (BrE)
to continue a sentence or phrase from one line of poetry to the next without a pause at the end of the line

Examples

To enjamb a line
The poet chose to enjamb several lines to create a sense of movement in the poem.
When you enjamb a phrase, the meaning often carries over to the next line.
Modern poetry frequently uses enjamb to break traditional rhythmic patterns.

Related Terms

  • antonym: end-stop - to end a line of poetry with a pause or punctuation
