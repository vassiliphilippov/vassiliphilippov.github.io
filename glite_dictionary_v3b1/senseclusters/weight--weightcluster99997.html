weight

noun
system of measurement for heaviness
Pronunciation: / weɪt / (AmE), / weɪt / (BrE)
a measurement of how heavy something is, or an object used to measure or show heaviness

Examples

A weight in kilograms
Different industries may use different weight systems, such as avoirdupois or troy weight.
He needed to convert the weight from kilograms to pounds for the shipment.
The metric weight is commonly used in most countries.
Astronauts experience a loss of weight in space because of the lack of gravity.
The weight of the suitcase made it difficult to carry up the stairs.
The bridge can support a maximum weight of ten tons.
Antique scales often came with a set of brass weights for precise measurements.
She chose a weight labeled '500 grams' to check the scale's accuracy.
The laboratory technician placed a weight on each side of the balance to calibrate it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: counterweight - a weight used as a counterbalance
  • synonym: heaviness - the quality of being heavy
  • synonym: mass - quantity of matter in a body; related physical property
