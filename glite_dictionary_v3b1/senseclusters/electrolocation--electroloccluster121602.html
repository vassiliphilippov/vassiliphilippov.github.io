electrolocation

noun
ability to locate objects via electric fields
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktroʊloʊˈkeɪʃən /; / \u026a\u02ccl\u025bktro\u028alo\u028a\u02c8ke\u026a\u0283\u0259n / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrələʊˈkeɪʃən /; / \u026a\u02ccl\u025bktr\u0259l\u0259\u028a\u02c8ke\u026a\u0283\u0259n / (BrE)
the ability or technique of detecting and locating objects by sensing electric fields, used by some animals and artificial systems

Examples

Fish use electrolocation
Scientists study how certain fish use electrolocation to navigate and find prey in murky waters.
Some aquatic animals rely on electrolocation rather than sight to understand their surroundings.
The ability of sharks to detect hidden animals is due in part to electrolocation.
Engineers fitted the autonomous underwater vehicle with active electrolocation to detect obstacles in turbid coastal waters.
Researchers published a paper demonstrating cheap sensors that enable small robots to perform electrolocation in dark environments.
The lab tested various frequencies to improve the resolution of artificial electrolocation for subsea mapping tasks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: active sensing - a technique where a device emits signals and measures responses to sense its environment
  • synonym: electroreception - the detection of electric fields by living organisms
