Coccinellidae

noun
family of beetles known as ladybugs
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːksəˈnɛləˌdiː / (AmE), / ˌkɒksɪˈnɛlɪdiː / (BrE)
a family of small beetles commonly known as ladybugs or ladybirds, characterized by their rounded bodies and often brightly colored, spotted elytra

Examples

The Coccinellidae beetles
Coccinellidae are often used in agriculture as natural pest control agents.
Scientists are studying the life cycles of various Coccinellidae species to improve crop protection.
The bright colors of Coccinellidae serve as a warning to potential predators.
