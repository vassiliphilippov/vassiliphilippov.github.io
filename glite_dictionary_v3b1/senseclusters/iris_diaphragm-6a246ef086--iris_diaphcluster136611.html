iris diaphragm

noun
adjustable device for controlling light in optics
3.5 6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈaɪ.rəs ˈdaɪ.ə.fræm/ /; / /ˈaɪ.rɪs ˈdaɪ.ə.fræm/ (AmE), /ˈaɪ.rɪs ˈdaɪ.ə.fræm/ /; / /ˈaɪ.rəs ˈdaɪ.ə.fræm/ (BrE)
a device made of thin, overlapping plates that can be adjusted to control the size of a central opening, commonly used to regulate the amount of light passing through cameras, microscopes, or other optical instruments

Examples

The iris diaphragm control
In microscopy, the iris diaphragm allows fine control over the illumination of the specimen.
The photographer adjusted the iris diaphragm to let in more light for the shot.
Turning the ring changes the opening of the iris diaphragm in the camera lens.
