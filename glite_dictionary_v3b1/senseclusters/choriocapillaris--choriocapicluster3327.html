choriocapillaris

noun
eye capillary layer
Pronunciation: / ˌkɔriˌoʊkəˌpɪˈlɛrɪs / (AmE), / ˌkɔːriəʊkəˌpɪˈlærɪs / (BrE)
a thin layer of capillaries in the eye that supplies blood to the retina

Examples

The choriocapillaris layer
The choriocapillaris plays a crucial role in nourishing the outer layers of the retina.
Damage to the choriocapillaris can lead to vision problems and retinal diseases.
Researchers are studying how the choriocapillaris is affected in age-related macular degeneration.

Related Terms

  • synonym: choroid - the vascular layer of the eye containing connective tissue
  • synonym: retina - the light-sensitive layer at the back of the eye
