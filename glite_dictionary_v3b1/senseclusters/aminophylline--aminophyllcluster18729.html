aminophylline

noun
bronchodilator drug for respiratory conditions
7.4
Pronunciation: / əˌmɪnəˈfaɪlɪn / (AmE), / əˌmɪnəʊˈfɪlɪn / (BrE)
a compound used as a bronchodilator in the treatment of asthma, emphysema, and bronchitis

Examples

aminophylline medicine
People with chronic bronchitis may be prescribed aminophylline to improve breathing.
Side effects of aminophylline can include nausea and restlessness.
The doctor administered aminophylline to relieve the patient's asthma attack.
