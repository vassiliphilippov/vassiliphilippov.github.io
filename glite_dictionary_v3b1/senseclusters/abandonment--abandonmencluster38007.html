abandonment

noun
voluntary surrender of property rights
Pronunciation: / əˈbændənmənt / (AmE), / əˈbændənmənt / (BrE)
the voluntary surrender of property or a right to property, without any intention of reclaiming it or transferring it to another

Examples

Legal abandonment of property
Legal abandonment requires a clear act and intent to relinquish all ownership rights.
The company’s abandonment of its unused factory meant it could not reclaim the property later.
Without proper notice, the abandonment of the land was considered intentional and complete by the courts.
