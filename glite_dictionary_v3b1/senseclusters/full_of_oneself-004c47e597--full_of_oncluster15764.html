full of oneself

adjective
arrogant or self-important (idiom)
Pronunciation: / fʊl əv wʌnˌsɛlf / (AmE), / fʊl əv wʌnˌsɛlf / (BrE)
Having an excessively high opinion of one's own abilities or importance; arrogant or self-important.

Examples

Full of oneself attitude
After his big win, he became so full of oneself that nobody wanted to work with him.
She tends to get full of oneself whenever she receives compliments.
Try not to be full of oneself, even when you’re doing well.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arrogant - having or revealing an exaggerated sense of one's own importance
  • antonym: humble - having or showing a modest or low estimate of one's own importance
