pitch-out

noun
baseball defensive pitch
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪtʃ aʊt / (AmE), / ˈpɪtʃ aʊt / (BrE)
a baseball pitch intentionally thrown outside the strike zone to prevent a stolen base or catch a runner off base

Examples

Baseball pitch-out
The coach called for a pitch-out to stop the runner from stealing second base.
A well-timed pitch-out can catch even the fastest base runners by surprise.
After the pitch-out, the catcher quickly threw to first to try for an out.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pickoff - a play in baseball to catch a runner off base
