corner

noun
exterior angle of a building
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrnər /; / ˈkɔrnɚ / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnə / (BrE)
the place or point where two lines, surfaces, or edges meet, such as in a room, on an object, or at a street intersection

Examples

Room corner
Rainwater can sometimes seep into the corner of an old stone church.
The architect added decorative detailing to each corner of the building.
Vines grew up the corner where the two walls met.
A cube has eight corners, one at each point where the sides intersect.
She placed the decoration precisely at the corner where the three walls meet.
The toy block has a sharp corner that scratched my hand.
I’ll meet you at the corner of Main Street and Oak Avenue.
She waited on the corner for the traffic light to change.
There’s a bakery right on the corner next to the post office.
