woadwaxen

noun
yellow-flowered dye plant shrub
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwoʊdˌwæksən / (AmE), / ˈwəʊdˌwæksən / (BrE)
a small Eurasian shrub (Genista tinctoria) with clusters of yellow flowers, formerly used to yield a yellow dye; also grown as an ornamental plant and sometimes a weed

Examples

The woadwaxen shrub
Gardeners sometimes grow woadwaxen for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
In some regions, woadwaxen has become an invasive weed, spreading beyond cultivated gardens.
The bright yellow flowers of woadwaxen once provided a natural source of dye for textiles.
