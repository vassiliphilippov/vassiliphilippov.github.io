short

adjective
lacking memory or recall
Pronunciation: / ʃɔrt / (AmE), / ʃɔːt / (BrE)
deficient in memory or foresight; unable to remember well or not thinking ahead

Examples

Short memory
He has a short memory for names and faces, often forgetting people he's just met.
Her short memory made it difficult for her to recall recent events.
Politicians are sometimes said to have a short memory when it comes to their past mistakes.
His short view of the project failed to anticipate future problems.
The committee's decision was criticized for being too short and reactionary.
We cannot afford to be short in our planning if we want lasting success.

Related Terms

  • antonym: far-sighted - having or showing foresight; planning prudently for the future
  • synonym: forgetful - unable to remember things easily
  • synonym: myopic - lacking imagination, foresight, or intellectual insight
  • synonym: shortsighted - lacking imagination or foresight; narrow-minded
