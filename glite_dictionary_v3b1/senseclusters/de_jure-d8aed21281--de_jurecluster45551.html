de jure

adjective
according to law; by right
Pronunciation: / deɪ ˈdʒʊri / (AmE), / di ˈdʒʊəreɪ / (BrE)
existing or holding a position according to law or by right, as opposed to in practice (de facto).

Examples

De jure government
He is the de jure owner of the property, though others reside there.
Slavery was abolished de jure in the 19th century, but discrimination persisted.
The country had a de jure government, but the military held power in reality.

Related Terms

  • antonym: de facto - existing in fact, whether legally recognized or not
