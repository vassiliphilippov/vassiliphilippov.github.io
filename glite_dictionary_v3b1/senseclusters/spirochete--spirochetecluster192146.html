spirochete

noun
spiral-shaped bacterium, some pathogenic
Pronunciation: / ˈspaɪroʊˌkiːt / (AmE), / ˈspaɪərəʊˌkiːt /; / ˈspaɪərəˌkiːt / (BrE)
a flexible or slender, spiral-shaped bacterium, some species of which cause diseases in humans and animals

Examples

A spirochete bacterium
A spirochete known as Treponema pallidum is the causative agent of syphilis.
Some spirochetes are responsible for diseases such as Lyme disease and relapsing fever.
Under the microscope, the corkscrew motion of the spirochete was clearly visible.
Lyme disease is caused by a spirochaete transmitted through tick bites.
Some spirochaetes are harmless, but others can lead to serious health conditions such as syphilis.
The scientist observed the characteristic spiral movement of the spirochaete under the microscope.
