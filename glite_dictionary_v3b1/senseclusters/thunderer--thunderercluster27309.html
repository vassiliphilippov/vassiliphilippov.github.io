thunderer

noun
loud or forceful speaker
Pronunciation: / ˈθʌndərər / (AmE), / ˈθʌndərə / (BrE)
a person or thing that makes a loud, booming noise, or someone with a powerful or influential presence

Examples

A loud thunderer
The coach was a well-known thunderer whose voice echoed across the field.
During the debate, she became a thunderer, making her points with great force.
The old engine was a real thunderer, rattling the windows every time it started.
The crowd fell silent as the thunderer took the stage and began to speak.
In mythology, Zeus is often called the thunderer because of his control over storms.
The old newspaper earned the nickname thunderer for its powerful and bold headlines.

Related Terms

  • synonym: roarer - someone or something that roars loudly
  • synonym: shouter - a person who shouts loudly
  • antonym: whisperer - a person who speaks very quietly
