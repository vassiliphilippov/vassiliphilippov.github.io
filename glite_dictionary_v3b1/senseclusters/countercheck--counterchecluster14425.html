countercheck

noun
secondary verification; confirmation check
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntərˌtʃɛk / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌtʃɛk / (BrE)
An additional check made to confirm or verify the correctness of a previous check.

Examples

Final countercheck
Always run a countercheck after your initial inspection to catch any missed errors.
The accountant performed a final countercheck to ensure there were no discrepancies in the financial report.
The software included an automatic countercheck for data validation.
