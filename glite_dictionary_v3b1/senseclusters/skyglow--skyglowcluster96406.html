skyglow

noun
urban night brightness
7.3
Pronunciation: /ˈskaɪ.ɡloʊ/ (AmE), /ˈskaɪ.ɡləʊ/ /; / /ˈskaɪ.ɡloʊ/ (BrE)
the brightening of the night sky over populated areas caused by artificial lights, making stars less visible

Examples

City skyglow
Astronomers often travel far from cities to avoid skyglow and observe the stars clearly.
The increase in skyglow has made it difficult for people in urban areas to see the Milky Way.
Efforts to reduce skyglow include using shielded streetlights and lowering outdoor lighting at night.

Related Terms

  • antonym: darkness - the absence of light, especially at night
  • synonym: light pollution - excessive or misdirected artificial light in the environment
