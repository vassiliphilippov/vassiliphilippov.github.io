familism

noun
family-centered ideology
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfæmɪˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief or value that the family is the most important unit in society and its needs should come before those of individuals

Examples

Strong familism values
The country's policies reflect a strong sense of familism, prioritizing family welfare over individual needs.
Debates about social programs often reveal tensions between familism and more individualistic approaches.
Some critics argue that familism can limit personal freedoms in favor of traditional family roles.
In traditional societies, familism often shapes how people make important life choices.
The strong sense of familism in her community meant that family needs always came first.
Some researchers study how familism affects attitudes toward work and education.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collectivism - the practice or principle of giving a group priority over each individual
  • antonym: individualism - the belief in the importance of individual rights and independence
