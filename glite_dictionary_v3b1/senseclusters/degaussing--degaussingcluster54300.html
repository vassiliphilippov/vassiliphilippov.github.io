degaussing

noun
process of making ship's hull nonmagnetic
Pronunciation: / diˈɡaʊsɪŋ / (AmE), / diˈɡaʊsɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of reducing or eliminating the magnetic field of a ship's hull by generating an opposing magnetic field, making it nonmagnetic

Examples

Ship degaussing
After degaussing, the steel hull was much less susceptible to triggering underwater explosives.
Routine degaussing procedures keep the vessel's magnetic signature at a safe level.
The naval engineer explained the importance of degaussing to protect the ship from magnetic mines.

Related Terms

  • synonym: demagnetization - the process of removing magnetic properties from an object
