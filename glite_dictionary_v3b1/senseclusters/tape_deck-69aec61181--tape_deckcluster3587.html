tape deck

noun
device for playing or recording magnetic tapes
5.0
Pronunciation: / teɪp dɛk / (AmE), / teɪp dɛk / (BrE)
an electronic device used for recording or playing back sound on magnetic tapes, typically as part of a stereo system and without built-in amplifiers or speakers

Examples

A vintage tape deck
Many audiophiles appreciate the warm sound quality of a vintage tape deck.
She carefully inserted the cassette tape into the tape deck and pressed play.
The old stereo system still has a functioning tape deck for playing cassettes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cassette player - a device for playing audio cassettes
  • antonym: turntable - a device for playing vinyl records
