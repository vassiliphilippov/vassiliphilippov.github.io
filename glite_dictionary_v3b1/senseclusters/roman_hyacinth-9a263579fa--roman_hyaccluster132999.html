Roman hyacinth

noun
a hyacinth with multiple loose flower spikes
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊ.mən haɪˈænsɪθ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊ.mən hɪˈæn.sɪθ / (BrE)
A type of hyacinth plant with several loosely flowered spikes, typically with multiple stems arising from a single bulb.

Examples

The Roman hyacinth plant
Gardeners appreciate the delicate fragrance of the Roman hyacinth in their borders.
The Roman hyacinth blooms earlier than the common Dutch hyacinth in spring.
Unlike other varieties, the Roman hyacinth often produces several flower spikes from one bulb.
