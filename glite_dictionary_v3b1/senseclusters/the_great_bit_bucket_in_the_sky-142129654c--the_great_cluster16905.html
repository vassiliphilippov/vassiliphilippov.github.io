the great bit bucket in the sky

noun
humorous term for irretrievably lost data
7.1 9.0
Pronunciation: / ðə ɡreɪt bɪt ˈbʌkɪt ɪn ðə skaɪ / (AmE), / ðə ɡreɪt bɪt ˈbʌkɪt ɪn ðə skaɪ / (BrE)
A humorous metaphor for where lost or deleted digital data is believed to go; a joking expression used to refer to data that has been permanently lost and cannot be recovered.

Examples

The great bit bucket in the sky joke
After the crash, all his unsaved progress disappeared into the great bit bucket in the sky.
If you don't back up your documents, they might end up in the great bit bucket in the sky someday.
When the server failed, the team joked that their files had gone to the great bit bucket in the sky.

  • synonym: bit bucket - slang for a fictitious place where lost computer data supposedly goes
