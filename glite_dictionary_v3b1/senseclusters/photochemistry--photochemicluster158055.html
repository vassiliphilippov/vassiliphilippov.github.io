photochemistry

noun
chemical effects of light
1.1 6.2
Pronunciation: /foʊ.tə.ˈkem.ɪ.striː/ /; / /foʊ.toʊ.ˈkem.ɪ.striː/ (AmE), /fəʊ.tə.ˈkem.ɪ.striː/ /; / /fəʊ.təʊ.ˈkem.ɪ.striː/ (BrE)
The branch of chemistry that studies the chemical effects of light and other electromagnetic radiation.

Examples

The photochemistry of light
Chlorophyll plays a crucial role in the photochemistry of photosynthesis.
Many important reactions in photochemistry are initiated by ultraviolet light.
Recent advances in photochemistry have improved solar energy technologies.
