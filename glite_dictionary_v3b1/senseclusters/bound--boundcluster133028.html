certain or destined to do or experience something, especially as if determined by fate (usually followed by 'to')

Examples

Bound to happen
An argument was bound to arise after that controversial decision.
It was bound to rain after such dark clouds gathered above the city.
She is bound to succeed with her determination and talent.

Synonyms

  • destined
    4.1 0.2/m
    destined is used for a future outcome or life path that seems sure, while bound usually expresses immediate certainty about something.
  • fated
    5.3 3/m
    fated is used when events seem controlled by fate or unavoidable, while bound states a general, less dramatic certainty.
  • predestined
    7.7 0.00/m
    predestined describes an outcome fixed in advance, often by divine will, while bound simply means certain to happen.
  • meant to be
    3.7 9/m
    meant to be is a casual phrase about things feeling intended or right, often in love, while bound states firm certainty.
