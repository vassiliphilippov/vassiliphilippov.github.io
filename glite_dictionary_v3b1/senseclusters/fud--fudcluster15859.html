fud

verb
to spread fear or doubt
Pronunciation: / fʌd / (AmE), / fʌd / (BrE)
to spread fear, uncertainty, or doubt about something, often to discourage support or adoption

Examples

To fud new ideas
Some people tried to fud the new technology by exaggerating its risks.
The company accused its rivals of attempting to fud their product launch.
Online forums warned users not to fud without checking the facts first.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reassure - to make someone feel less worried or uncertain
  • synonym: scaremonger - to spread alarming or worrying information
