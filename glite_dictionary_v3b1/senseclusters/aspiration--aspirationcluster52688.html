aspiration

noun
release of breath in speech (phonetics)
2.1 7.2
Pronunciation: / ˌæspəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌaspɪˈreɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
(Phonetics) The audible release of breath that accompanies or follows some spoken sounds, especially certain consonants.

Examples

Aspiration of a sound
In English, the aspiration of the "p" sound distinguishes "pat" from "bat".
Korean consonants typically show a clear difference between aspiration and unaspirated forms.
Linguists often study the effects of aspiration on accent and pronunciation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plosive - a consonant sound characterized by complete closure in the vocal tract followed by a release; often aspirated
  • antonym: unaspirated - pronounced without an audible release of breath
