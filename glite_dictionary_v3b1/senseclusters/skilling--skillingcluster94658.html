skilling

noun
old Scandinavian coin
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈskɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
a small historical coin or monetary unit formerly used in Scandinavia, especially Denmark, Norway, and Sweden

Examples

A silver skilling
The museum displayed a rare skilling from 18th-century Sweden in its coin collection.
He inherited a box of old coins, including a Danish skilling from the 1800s.
Collectors often search for a skilling to complete their set of Scandinavian currency.

Related Terms

  • antonym: krone - the current currency of Denmark and Norway
  • synonym: penny - a small denomination coin in various countries
