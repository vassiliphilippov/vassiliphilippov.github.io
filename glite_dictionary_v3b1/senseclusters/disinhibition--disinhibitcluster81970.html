disinhibition

noun
loss of inhibition
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsɪnhɪˈbɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪsɪnhɪˈbɪʃən / (BrE)
a reduction or loss of normal inhibition in the nervous system, leading to increased activity or involuntary responses

Examples

Neurological disinhibition
Neurologists observed disinhibition in the patient's reflexes after the brain injury.
Certain medications can cause disinhibition, leading to unexpected emotional or physical reactions.
The study focused on the effects of disinhibition in people with frontal lobe damage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: excitation - the process of increasing the activity of neurons or muscles
  • antonym: inhibition - the process of restraining or suppressing neural activity
