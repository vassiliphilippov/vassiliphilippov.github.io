get away with

verb
escape punishment or blame
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt əˈweɪ wɪð / (AmE), / ɡɛt əˈweɪ wɪð / (BrE)
to escape being punished for or caught doing something wrong or dishonest

Examples

To get away with cheating
He tried to get away with cheating on the test, but the teacher noticed.
Many people think they can get away with breaking small rules without consequences.
She was surprised when her little brother managed to get away with taking the last cookie.
