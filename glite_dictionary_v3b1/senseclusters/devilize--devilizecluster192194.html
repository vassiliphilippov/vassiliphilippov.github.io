devilize

verb
make into a devil or render devilish
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛv.əˌlaɪz /; / ˈdɛvɪˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈdɛv.ɪlaɪz /; / ˈdɛvɪlaɪz / (BrE)
to make into a devil or to portray as devilish or evil

Examples

To devilize a character
His actions were misrepresented in ways that seemed to devilize his entire character.
Some historians argue that media coverage tends to devilize political opponents.
The novel depicts a society that attempts to devilize those who question authority.
His heart was devilised by years of bitterness and betrayal.
Literature often devilises its villains to heighten the contrast with its heroes.
The horrors of battle seemed to devilise even the most gentle souls.

Related Terms

  • synonym: demonize - to portray as wicked or demonic
  • antonym: sanctify - to make holy or sacred
