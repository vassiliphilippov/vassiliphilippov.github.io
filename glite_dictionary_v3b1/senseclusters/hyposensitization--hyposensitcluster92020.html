hyposensitization

noun
allergy desensitization
8.7
Pronunciation: /haɪ.pə.sen.sɪ.tɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /haɪ.poʊ.sen.sɪ.tɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /haɪ.pə.sen.sɪ.tɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a medical treatment that reduces a person's sensitivity to an allergen by gradually exposing them to increasing amounts of it

Examples

Hyposensitization treatment
The doctor recommended hyposensitization to help her build tolerance to pollen.
Many allergy sufferers undergo hyposensitization to reduce their reactions over time.
After a year of hyposensitization, his symptoms became much milder during spring.

Related Terms

  • synonym: desensitization - the process of making someone less sensitive to a substance or stimulus
  • synonym: immunotherapy - treatment that uses the body's immune system to fight disease, including allergies
