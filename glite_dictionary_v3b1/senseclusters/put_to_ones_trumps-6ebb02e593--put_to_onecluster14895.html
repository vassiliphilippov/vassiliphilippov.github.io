put to one's trumps

verb
faced with a demanding challenge
Pronunciation: / pʊt tu wʌnz trʌmps / (AmE), / pʊt tʊ wʌnz trʌmps / (BrE)
to be confronted with a challenge that requires all of one's skill, ability, or resources

Examples

To put to their trumps
During the negotiations, the team was put to their trumps to secure a favorable deal.
He felt truly put to his trumps when given a project with a tight deadline.
The surprise audit put the company to its trumps to produce the required documents.
