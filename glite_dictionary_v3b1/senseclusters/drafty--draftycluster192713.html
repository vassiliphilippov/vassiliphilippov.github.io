drafty

adjective
not airtight; lets air in
2.6 2.1
Pronunciation: /ˈdræf.tiː/ /; / /ˈdræf.tɪ/ (AmE), /ˈdrɑːf.tiː/ /; / /ˈdrɑːf.tɪ/ (BrE)
(of a room, building, or window) allowing cold air to enter because it is not completely closed or sealed.

Examples

A drafty room
After fixing the gaps in the door, the hallway was much less drafty.
She noticed a drafty window letting cold air into the living room.
The old house was drafty in winter, so we used extra blankets at night.
Despite the heating, the room felt draughty in the winter.
The old house was so draughty that we had to wear coats indoors.
We fitted new windows to make the cottage less draughty.

Related Terms

  • antonym: airtight - completely sealed so that air cannot get in or out
  • synonym: drafty - having currents of cold air (American spelling)
  • degree higher: windy - exposed to or full of wind
