daughter cell

noun
cell formed by cell division
Pronunciation: / ˈdɔtɚ sɛl / (AmE), / ˈdɔːtə sɛl / (BrE)
a cell produced by the division or budding of another cell, typically identical to the parent cell

Examples

Daughter cells after division
After division, the daughter cells begin to grow and prepare for the next cycle.
During mitosis, a single parent cell divides to form two daughter cells with identical genetic material.
Each daughter cell inherits half of the chromosomes from the parent cell.

Related Terms

  • antonym: parent cell - the original cell that divides to produce daughter cells
