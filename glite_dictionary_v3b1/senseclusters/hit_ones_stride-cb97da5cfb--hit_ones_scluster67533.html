hit one's stride

verb
reach peak performance or comfort
Pronunciation: / hɪt wʌnz straɪd / (AmE), / hɪt wʌnz straɪd / (BrE)
to reach a point at which one is performing at their best or most efficient level, typically after a period of adjustment

Examples

To hit her stride quickly
After months of training, she finally hit her stride and began winning races.
Once the meeting started, the speaker hit his stride and spoke with confidence.
The team hit its stride midway through the season and won every game after that.

Related Terms

  • synonym: get into one's stride - to settle into a comfortable and effective pace of activity
