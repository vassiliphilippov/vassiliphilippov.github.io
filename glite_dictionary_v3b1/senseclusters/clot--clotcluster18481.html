clot

noun
a lump formed from a liquid
5.0 3.63/m
Pronunciation: / klÉ‘t / (AmE), / klÉ’t / (BrE)
a thick, lump-like mass formed when a liquid, especially blood, coagulates or solidifies

Examples

A blood clot
Doctors discovered a clot in his artery during the examination.
Fat droplets can form a clot in soups if not stirred continuously.
The injury led to the formation of a blood clot that needed treatment.

Synonyms

  • blood clot
    3.8 1.59/m
    blood clot is a lump formed in blood that can block vessels, while clot can mean any thick lump in a liquid
  • coagulum
    9.9 0.01/m
    coagulum is a formal medical term for a semi solid mass after coagulation, while clot is the common everyday word

Related Terms

  • synonym: coagulate - to change from a fluid into a thickened mass
  • antonym: fluid - a substance that flows, such as a liquid or gas
