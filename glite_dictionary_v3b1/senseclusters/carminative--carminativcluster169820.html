carminative

noun
drug that relieves or prevents gas in the digestive tract
Pronunciation: / kɑrˈmɪnətɪv / (AmE), / kɑːˈmɪnətɪv / (BrE)
A substance or medicine that prevents the formation of gas in the digestive tract or relieves flatulence by aiding its expulsion.

Examples

A carminative herb
Ginger is sometimes used as a carminative to relieve indigestion and bloating.
Many herbal teas have mild carminative properties, helping soothe the digestive system.
Your doctor may recommend a carminative if you frequently experience uncomfortable gas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antiflatulent - a substance that reduces or prevents flatulence
  • degree higher: laxative - a substance that promotes bowel movements
