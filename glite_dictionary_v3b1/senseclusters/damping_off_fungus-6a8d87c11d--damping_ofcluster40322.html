damping off fungus

noun
fungus causing seedling disease
5.9 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈdæm.pɪŋ ɔːf ˈfʌŋ.gəs/ /; / /ˈdæm.pɪŋ ɑːf ˈfʌŋ.gəs/ (AmE), /ˈdæm.pɪŋ ɒf ˈfʌŋ.gəs/ /; / /ˈdæm.pɪŋ ɔːf ˈfʌŋ.gəs/ (BrE)
a fungus that causes damping off disease in seedlings, leading to their decay or death, often in moist conditions

Examples

A damping off fungus disease
A damping off fungus can destroy seedlings before they develop true leaves.
The greenhouse lost half its young tomato plants to a damping off fungus last spring.
To prevent damping off fungus, growers ensure proper air circulation in the seed trays.
