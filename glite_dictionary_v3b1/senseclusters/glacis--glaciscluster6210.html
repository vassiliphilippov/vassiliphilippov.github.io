glacis

noun
sloped fortification area
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡleɪsɪs / (AmE), / ˈɡleɪsiː / (BrE)
a gentle slope, especially one in front of a fortification designed to expose attackers to defensive fire

Examples

Fortress glacis
The soldiers advanced cautiously across the glacis toward the fortress walls.
The engineer explained how the glacis made it difficult for attackers to approach unseen.
Grass covered the old glacis, blending the fortification into the surrounding landscape.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ditch - a trench or channel, often used as a defensive feature
  • synonym: rampart - a defensive wall or bank around a fort
