phthisis

noun
tuberculosis; wasting lung disease
7.1 9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈθaɪ.sɪs/ /; / /ˈfθaɪ.sɪs/ /; / /ˈtaɪ.sɪs/ (AmE), /ˈθaɪ.sɪs/ /; / /ˈfθaɪ.sɪs/ /; / /ˈtaɪ.sɪs/ (BrE)
A wasting disease of the lungs, especially pulmonary tuberculosis, characterized by progressive emaciation and weakness.

Examples

Phthisis lung disease
Before antibiotics, phthisis was often a death sentence for those afflicted with the disease.
The poet died young from phthisis, as did many of his contemporaries in the nineteenth century.
The sanatorium was built to care for patients suffering from phthisis.
