censitary suffrage

noun
voting system based on wealth or rank
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆsÉ›nsÉªËŒtÉ›ri ËˆsÊŒfrÉªdÊ’ / (AmE), / ËˆsÉ›nsÉªtÉ™ri ËˆsÊŒfrÉªdÊ’ / (BrE)
A voting system in which the weight or number of votes a person may cast depends on their rank or economic status, typically measured by income, property, or taxes paid.

Examples

Censitary suffrage system
During the 19th century, many European countries implemented censitary suffrage to limit political power to the wealthy.
In some colonies, censitary suffrage meant that only landowners could vote in elections.
The reformers demanded an end to censitary suffrage in favor of universal suffrage.

Synonyms

  • census suffrage
    9.9 0.00/m

Related Terms

  • synonym: plural voting - system where certain individuals have multiple votes based on criteria like wealth or education
  • antonym: universal suffrage - right of almost all adults to vote, regardless of wealth or status
