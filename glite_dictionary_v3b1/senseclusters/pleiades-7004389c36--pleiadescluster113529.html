Pleiades

proper noun
star cluster in Taurus constellation
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈplaɪ.əˌdiz / (AmE), / ˈpliː.əˌdiːz /; / ˈpliː.ə.diːz / (BrE)
a group of stars in the constellation Taurus, also known as the Seven Sisters, and in Greek mythology, the seven daughters of Atlas placed among the stars

Examples

The Pleiades stars
From her backyard, she could easily spot the Pleiades, also called the Seven Sisters, near Taurus.
The Pleiades have inspired myths and legends in numerous cultures around the world.
Through his telescope, he admired the sparkling beauty of the Pleiades on a clear winter night.
Astronomers study the Pleiades to learn about the formation of young star clusters.
In mythology, the Pleiades were turned into stars to escape Orion's pursuit.
The constellation Pleiades is easily visible in the winter night sky.
