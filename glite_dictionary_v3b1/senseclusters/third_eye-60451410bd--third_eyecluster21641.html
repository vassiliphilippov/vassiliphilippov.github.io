third eye

noun
light-sensitive organ in reptiles
3.9
Pronunciation: / θɜrd aɪ / (AmE), / θɜːd aɪ / (BrE)
a light-sensitive organ located on the top of the head in some reptiles, capable of detecting changes in light

Examples

The third eye organ
Scientists studied the function of the third eye in tuataras to understand its role in regulating behavior.
Some lizards possess a third eye on their head that helps them sense changes in sunlight.
The third eye of certain reptiles is known as the parietal eye.
