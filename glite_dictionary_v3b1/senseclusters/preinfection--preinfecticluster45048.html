preinfection

noun
measures before infection
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːɪnˈfɛkʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːɪnˈfɛkʃən / (BrE)
the time, state, or actions before an infection occurs (such as preventive measures or the period before infection)

Examples

Preinfection period
Doctors emphasized the importance of preinfection strategies such as vaccination and handwashing.
Travelers were given a list of preinfection recommendations before visiting the region.
Effective preinfection planning can greatly reduce the spread of contagious diseases.
Researchers measured the preinfection levels of antibodies in the study participants.
Understanding the preinfection phase can help prevent the spread of disease.
The vaccine was administered during the preinfection period to maximize its effectiveness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: incubation period - the time between exposure to an infection and the appearance of symptoms
  • antonym: postinfection - occurring after an infection has taken place
  • synonym: prevention - the act of stopping something from happening
  • synonym: prophylaxis - treatment or actions to prevent disease
