orca

noun
large predatory whale; killer whale
7.3 2.9
Pronunciation: /ˈɔːr.kə/ (AmE), /ˈɔː.kə/ (BrE)
A large predatory black-and-white toothed whale (also called a killer whale), found chiefly in cold seas, with a tall dorsal fin.

Examples

A orca whale
During our trip to Alaska, we saw a pod of orcas swimming near the boat.
The orca is known for its intelligence and complex social behavior.
The orca is sometimes called the killer whale due to its powerful hunting skills.
