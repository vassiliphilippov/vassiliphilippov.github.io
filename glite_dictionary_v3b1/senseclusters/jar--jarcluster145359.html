jar

verb
be incompatible or in conflict
3.9 5.8
Pronunciation: /dʒɑːr/ (AmE), /dʒɑː/ (BrE)
to have an unpleasant, disturbing, or conflicting effect; to shock or unsettle

Examples

To jar the nerves
Her loud voice can sometimes jar on people who prefer quiet.
The brightly colored curtains jar with the rest of the room's decor.
The modern design elements jar with the old architecture of the house.
His dismissive comments jarred the listeners, making them feel uneasy.
The abrupt change in music style jarred the mood of the entire evening.
The loud crash jarred everyone in the quiet library.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clash - to be in conflict or not go well together
  • synonym: disturb - to interrupt the normal state or activity; to unsettle
  • antonym: soothe - to make calm or relieve discomfort
