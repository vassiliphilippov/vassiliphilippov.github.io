moocher

noun
person who tries to get things for free
Pronunciation: / ˈmuːtʃər / (AmE), / ˈmuːtʃə / (BrE)
a person who habitually tries to get things, especially food or money, without paying or giving anything in return

Examples

A moocher at dinner
He's known as a moocher who never pays for his share of dinner.
The party was full of food, but the moocher still managed to find something extra for free.
Tired of the moocher always asking for money, Sarah finally confronted him.

Related Terms

  • antonym: benefactor - person who gives help or support to others
  • synonym: freeloader - person who takes advantage of others' generosity without giving anything in return
  • synonym: sponger - person who lives off others by continually taking advantage of their generosity
