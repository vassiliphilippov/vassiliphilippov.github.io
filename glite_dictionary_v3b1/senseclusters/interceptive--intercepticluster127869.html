interceptive

adjective
contraceptive after fertilization
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈsɛptɪv / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈsɛptɪv / (BrE)
relating to contraceptive methods that act after fertilization but before implantation to prevent pregnancy

Examples

Interceptive contraception
Researchers are developing new interceptive drugs that work after fertilization but before implantation.
The doctor explained how interceptive methods differ from traditional contraceptives.
Some ethical debates focus on whether interceptive contraception should be considered distinct from other forms.
