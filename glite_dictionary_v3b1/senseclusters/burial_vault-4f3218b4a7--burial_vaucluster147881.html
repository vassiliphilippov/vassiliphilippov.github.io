burial vault

noun
underground chamber for burial
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛriəl vɔlt / (AmE), / ˈbɛriəl vɔːlt / (BrE)
an underground chamber used for burying a coffin, often designed to protect it from the elements or for family interment

Examples

An old burial vault
During the renovation, an old burial vault was discovered beneath the church floor.
Some cemeteries require a burial vault for all new graves to prevent ground collapse.
The family purchased a burial vault to ensure their loved one would be protected from the elements.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mausoleum - a large building housing tombs
