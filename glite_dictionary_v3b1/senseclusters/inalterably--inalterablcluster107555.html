inalterably

adverb
unchangeably
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈɔltərəbli / (AmE), / ɪnˈɔːltərəbli / (BrE)
in a way that cannot be changed or altered

Examples

Set inalterably by rules
The rules were set inalterably by the founders of the organization.
Her decision was inalterably fixed, no matter what anyone said.
The treaty was inalterably written into international law.

Related Terms

  • synonym: immutably - in a way that cannot be changed
  • synonym: permanently - in a way that lasts forever
  • antonym: temporarily - for a limited time only
