staff

noun
rod symbolizing authority
Pronunciation: / stæf / (AmE), / stɑːf / (BrE)
a long stick or rod, often used for support, as a symbol of authority, or for special purposes

Examples

A walking staff
During the ceremony, the leader raised the staff to silence the crowd.
The bishop carried a jeweled staff symbolizing his position.
The king entered the hall holding his staff as a sign of office.
Hikers often bring a sturdy staff to help them on rough trails.
The king carried a jeweled staff as a symbol of power.
The old shepherd leaned on his staff as he watched the flock.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cane - a stick, especially one used as an aid in walking
  • synonym: rod - a straight, slender stick or bar, especially of wood or metal
  • synonym: scepter - a decorated rod carried by a sovereign as a symbol of authority
