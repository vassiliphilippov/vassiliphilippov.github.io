gamma radiation

noun
high-energy electromagnetic radiation from radioactive decay
2.2 4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæmə ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈɡæmə ˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
High-energy electromagnetic radiation emitted by certain radioactive substances during nuclear decay, characterized by an extremely short wavelength.

Examples

High-energy gamma radiation
Exposure to gamma radiation can cause serious health problems if not properly controlled.
Scientists use gamma radiation to sterilize medical equipment and kill bacteria.
The discovery of gamma radiation helped advance our understanding of radioactive decay.
