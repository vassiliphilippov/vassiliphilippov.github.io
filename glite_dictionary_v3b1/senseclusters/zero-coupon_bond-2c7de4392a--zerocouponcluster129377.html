zero-coupon bond

noun
bond with no periodic interest payments
Pronunciation: / ˌzɪroʊ ˈkuːpɑn bɑnd / (AmE), / ˌzɪərəʊ ˈkuːpɒn bɒnd / (BrE)
A bond sold at a price much lower than its face value and that pays no periodic interest, with the full face value paid at maturity.

Examples

A zero-coupon bond price
A zero-coupon bond, unlike a traditional bond, does not provide periodic interest payments.
Many investors prefer a zero-coupon bond for long-term savings goals, such as college tuition.
The company issued a zero-coupon bond that will mature in ten years.
