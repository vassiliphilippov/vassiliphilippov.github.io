nonintegrable

adjective
not solvable exactly
9.9
Pronunciation: /nɑːn.ɪn.ˈte.grə.bəl/ /; / /nən.ɪn.ˈte.grə.bəl/ (AmE), /nɒn.ɪn.ˈte.grə.bəl/ /; / /nən.ɪn.ˈte.grə.bəl/ (BrE)
not having the necessary independent integrals of motion for a complete solution, often resulting in chaotic or unpredictable behavior in a system.

Examples

Nonintegrable system
The three-body problem is a classic example of a nonintegrable system in physics.
Researchers found that the equations describing the weather are highly nonintegrable and lead to chaotic forecasts.
In dynamical systems, a nonintegrable model often exhibits complex, unpredictable motion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chaotic - showing unpredictable or random behavior
  • antonym: integrable - able to be solved exactly using integrals of motion
