bootlegger

noun
person who illegally sells alcohol
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˈbuːtˌlɛɡər / (AmE), / ˈbuːtˌlɛɡə / (BrE)
a person who makes, distributes, or sells alcohol illegally, especially during Prohibition in the United States

Examples

Alcohol bootlegger
During Prohibition, many bootleggers supplied illegal alcohol to speakeasies across the city.
Her grandfather used to tell stories about outsmarting the authorities as a bootlegger in the 1920s.
The police arrested a notorious bootlegger after months of surveillance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: smuggler - person who illegally moves goods across borders
