murther

noun
archaic for murder
Pronunciation: / ˈmɜːrðər / (AmE), / ˈmɜːθə / (BrE)
an archaic spelling of 'murder', meaning the unlawful killing of a person

Examples

A brutal murther
In old texts, the word murther often appears instead of 'murder'.
The judge read the charge of murther as it was written in the ancient document.
Shakespeare's plays sometimes use murther to describe a killing.
