rehash

verb
present or use again with little change
7.5 5.2
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈhæʃ/ /; / /rɪ.ˈhæʃ/ (AmE), /riː.ˈhæʃ/ /; / /rɪ.ˈhæʃ/ (BrE)
to present, use, or discuss something again with little or no change, especially when it lacks originality

Examples

To rehash an essay
He simply rehashed his last report with only a few minor edits.
Rather than rehashing the same arguments, let's try to find a new approach.
The director was criticized for choosing to rehash old plotlines instead of creating something new.
He simply rehashed the old proposals instead of offering anything novel.
The author tends to rehash the same arguments in every book he writes.
There is no need to rehash yesterday’s meeting; let’s focus on new solutions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: recycle - to process and use again, especially materials or ideas
  • synonym: repeat - to say or do something again
  • synonym: revamp - to revise or improve something significantly
