overexpansion

noun
excessive growth
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərɪkˈspænʃən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərɪkˈspænʃən / (BrE)
the process or result of growing or increasing too much or too quickly, often causing problems (especially in business or the economy)

Examples

Rapid overexpansion
The rapid overexpansion of the company led to financial difficulties within a few years.
Many economists warn that overexpansion in the housing market can result in a bubble.
Government policies aimed to prevent overexpansion of credit and control inflation.
The rapid overexpansion of the company caused it to lose control of its finances.
Many cities face problems when overexpansion leads to overcrowding and strained resources.
The airline's overexpansion into new markets resulted in significant losses.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contraction - the process of becoming smaller or less extensive
  • degree lower: expansion - the process of becoming larger or more extensive
