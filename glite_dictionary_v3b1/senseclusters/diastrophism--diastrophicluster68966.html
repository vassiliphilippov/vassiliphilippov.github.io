diastrophism

noun
deformation process shaping Earth's crust
4.9
Pronunciation: / daɪˈæstrəˌfɪzəm / (AmE), / daɪˈæstrəˌfɪzəm / (BrE)
the process of deformation of the Earth's crust that results in the formation of continents, ocean basins, mountains, and other geological features

Examples

Diastrophism of Earth's crust
Diastrophism is responsible for the uplift of mountain ranges over millions of years.
Many geological features, such as rift valleys, are shaped by diastrophism.
Scientists study diastrophism to understand the history and evolution of continents.
