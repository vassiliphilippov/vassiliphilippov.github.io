yadda

verb
to speak repetitively
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈjædə / (AmE), / ˈjædə / (BrE)
to talk in a dull, repetitive, or trivial way, or to skip details by using a filler phrase instead of full information

Examples

To yadda for hours
He started to yadda about his weekend, repeating the same stories over and over.
During the meeting, she would often yadda instead of getting to the main point.
When asked for details, he just yaddaed and moved on without explaining anything important.

Related Terms

  • synonym: babble - to talk rapidly and continuously in a foolish, excited, or incomprehensible way
  • antonym: elaborate - to explain or describe something in more detail
  • synonym: ramble - to talk or write at length in a confused or inconsequential way
