precontrol

noun
preliminary control device
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːkənˈtroʊl / (AmE), / ˌpriːkənˈtrəʊl / (BrE)
a device, system, or process that monitors or regulates conditions before a main operation begins

Examples

Precontrol system
The engineer checked the precontrol system to ensure all parameters were within safe limits before starting the machine.
A malfunction in the precontrol led to inaccurate readings before the main process began.
Modern factories often use automated precontrol devices to monitor environmental conditions ahead of production.
A thorough precontrol process can help prevent costly mistakes during production.
The team performed a precontrol inspection before starting the experiment.
Implementing precontrol measures is essential for ensuring safety in the laboratory.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: control - the power to direct or regulate something
  • synonym: inspection - the act of examining something carefully
  • antonym: postcontrol - monitoring or regulation after the main operation
  • synonym: precaution - an action taken in advance to prevent harm or ensure safety
