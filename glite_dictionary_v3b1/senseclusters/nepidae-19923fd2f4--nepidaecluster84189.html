Nepidae

noun
family of aquatic insects (water scorpions)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛpɪˌdi / (AmE), / ˈnɛpɪˌdiː / (BrE)
a family of aquatic insects known as water scorpions, recognized for their elongated bodies and grasping forelegs used for catching prey.

Examples

Nepidae water insects
Members of Nepidae are known for their raptorial forelegs, used to capture prey.
Scientists studied the hunting behavior of Nepidae in their natural habitat.
The Nepidae are commonly found in ponds and slow-moving streams.
