neck

noun
narrow strip of land
Pronunciation: / nɛk / (AmE), / nɛk / (BrE)
a narrow stretch or strip of land projecting from a larger area, often connecting two larger land masses or extending into a body of water

Examples

Neck of land
A narrow neck of land connected the island to the mainland.
The town was built on a neck of land jutting into the lake.
They hiked across the neck to reach the other side of the bay.

Related Terms

  • synonym: isthmus - a narrow strip of land connecting two larger land areas
