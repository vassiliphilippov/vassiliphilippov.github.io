vena pulmonalis

noun
pulmonary vein (carries blood from lungs)
9.2 7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈviː.nə pʌl.ˈmoʊ.nə.lɪs/ (AmE), /ˈviː.nə pʌl.ˈməʊ.nə.lɪs/ (BrE)
Any of the four veins that carry oxygenated blood from the lungs to the left atrium of the heart; also called a pulmonary vein.

Examples

The vena pulmonalis vein
Impaired flow in the vena pulmonalis can lead to serious circulatory problems.
Medical students studied the anatomy of the vena pulmonalis during their cardiology module.
The vena pulmonalis transports oxygen-rich blood from the lungs to the heart.
