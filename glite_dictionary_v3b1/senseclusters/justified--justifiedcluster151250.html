justified

adjective
having good reason
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒʌstɪfaɪd / (AmE), / ˈdʒʌstɪfaɪd / (BrE)
having a good reason for something or shown to be right or reasonable

Examples

Justified anger
Her anger was completely justified after hearing the unfair decision.
The company felt justified in raising prices due to increased costs.
He gave a justified explanation for missing the important meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reasonable - based on good sense or logic
  • antonym: unjustified - not having a good reason or excuse
