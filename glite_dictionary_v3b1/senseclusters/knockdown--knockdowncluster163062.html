knockdown

noun
reduction of gene expression
9.3
Pronunciation: / c8n51kccda8an / (AmE), / c8n52kccda8an / (BrE)
(in genetics and molecular biology) a reduction in the expression or activity of a gene or its protein product, typically produced experimentally (for example by RNA interference or antisense methods) to study function.

Examples

Gene knockdown
Researchers achieved a knockdown of the target gene using siRNA to study its function.
A partial knockdown of the protein reduced tumor cell growth in the laboratory experiment.
After treatment, they observed sustained knockdown of gene expression for several days.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gene silencing - the process of preventing a gene from being expressed as RNA or protein
  • degree higher: knockout - a genetic alteration that completely abolishes the function or expression of a gene; typically a more complete loss than a knockdown
