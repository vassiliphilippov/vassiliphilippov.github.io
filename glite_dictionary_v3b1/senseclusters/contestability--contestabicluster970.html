contestability

noun
degree of being open to challenge
Pronunciation: / kənˌtɛstəˈbɪləti /; / kənˌtɛstəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / kənˌtɛstəˈbɪləti /; / kənˌtɛstəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being open to challenge, dispute, or entry by others (such as in markets, ideas, or legal matters)

Examples

The contestability of markets
The contestability of the telecommunications industry has increased with new technology.
High contestability in a market often leads to better services for consumers.
The contestability of his theory was debated by several experts at the conference.
The contestability of the contract terms led to a lengthy court case.
Scholars debated the contestability of the historical evidence presented in the article.
Regulators examined the contestability of the company's market position before approving the merger.

Related Terms

  • antonym: barrier - something that prevents entry or competition
  • synonym: disputability - the quality of being able to be disputed or argued about
  • antonym: indisputability - the quality of not being open to dispute or challenge
  • synonym: openness - the quality of being open to new ideas or competition
