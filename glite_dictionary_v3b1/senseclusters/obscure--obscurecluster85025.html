obscure

adjective
not clearly expressed or understood
1.6 6.6
Pronunciation: /əb.ˈskjʊr/ /; / /əb.ˈskjɜːr/ (AmE), /əb.ˈskjʊə/ /; / /əb.ˈskjɔː/ (BrE)
difficult to understand because it is unclear, complex, or ambiguous

Examples

Obscure instructions
Because the instructions were so obscure, nobody could assemble the furniture.
The manuscript contained several obscure references that only experts could interpret.
The meaning of his statement remained obscure to most of the audience.
Her novel received mixed reviews for its obscure language and complex themes.
I found the instructions obscure, making it hard to assemble the furniture.
