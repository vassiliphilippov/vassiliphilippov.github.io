vena cava

noun
major vein returning blood to heart
4.9 0.10/m
Pronunciation: / ËŒviËnÉ™ ËˆkeÉªvÉ™ / (AmE), / ËŒviËnÉ™ ËˆkÉ‘ËvÉ™ / (BrE)
either of the two large veins (the superior vena cava and the inferior vena cava) that carry oxygen-depleted blood back to the right atrium of the heart

Examples

The vena cava vein
A blood clot in the vena cava can cause serious medical complications.
During the surgery, the doctor carefully avoided damaging the vena cava.
The vena cava carries deoxygenated blood from the body back to the right atrium of the heart.

Synonyms
