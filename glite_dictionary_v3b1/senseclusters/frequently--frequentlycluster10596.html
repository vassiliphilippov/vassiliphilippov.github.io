frequently

adverb
often; many times
1.0 60/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàfriÀêkw…ôntli / (AmE), / ÀàfriÀêkw…ôntli / (BrE)
Often; many times at short intervals.

Examples

To frequently check
He frequently checks his email throughout the day for important updates.
She frequently travels abroad for work and has visited over fifty countries.
The teacher frequently asked questions to keep the students engaged during class.

Synonyms

  • often
    1.0 476/m
    often appears in everyday speech for many occasions, while frequently sounds slightly more formal and more general.
  • oftentimes
    4.9 4.80/m
    oftentimes is used in speech and older texts to mean many times, while frequently is more neutral today.
  • repeatedly
    1.0 29/m
    repeatedly means doing something again and again in everyday life, while frequently focuses on how often it happens.
  • continually
    2.9 16/m
    continually suggests little or no break and long action, while frequently means many separate short times.
  • time and again
    1.8 3.56/m
    time and again is a friendly phrase for repeating often, while frequently is more neutral and not idiomatic.
  • time and time again
    1.8 3.47/m
    time and time again sounds stronger and emphatic for repeated events, while frequently simply notes common occurrence in everyday life.
  • over and over again
    1.0 11/m
    over and over again shows repetition many times, often with frustration, while frequently is neutral and factual.
  • regularly
    1.0 40/m
    regularly means at fixed or usual times, while frequently only says something happens often without set times.
  • on a regular basis
    2.3 6.31/m
    on a regular basis describes events that follow a steady pattern, while frequently only says they happen many times.
