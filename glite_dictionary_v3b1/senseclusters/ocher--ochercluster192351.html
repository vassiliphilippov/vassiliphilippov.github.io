ocher

verb
to color with ochre
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊkər / (AmE), / ˈəʊkə / (BrE)
to coat, stain, or color something with ochre, a yellow-brown natural pigment

Examples

To ocher a canvas
The artist ochred the canvas to give it a warm, earthy tone.
Ancient people often ochred their pottery for decoration and protection.
They ochre the walls every spring to refresh the color in the living room.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bleach - to make something lighter in color
  • synonym: paint - to cover a surface with a colored substance
