Lemna minor

noun
species of small aquatic duckweed plant
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛm.nə ˈmaɪ.nɚ / (AmE), / ˈlɛm.nə ˈmaɪ.nə / (BrE)
A small floating aquatic plant of the duckweed family, found in temperate regions except eastern Asia and Australia; commonly called common duckweed.

Examples

Lemna minor thrives in ponds and slow-moving streams, providing shelter for small aquatic animals.
Farmers use Lemna minor as a natural way to control algae and oxygen levels in small water bodies.
