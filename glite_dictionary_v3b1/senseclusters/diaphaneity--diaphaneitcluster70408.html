diaphaneity

noun
degree of transparency
Pronunciation: / daɪˌæfəˈniːɪti / (AmE), / daɪˌæfəˈniːɪti / (BrE)
the degree to which a material allows light to pass through it; transparency or translucency

Examples

Diaphaneity of glass
The diaphaneity of the crystal made it highly valued by collectors.
Scientists measured the diaphaneity of the fabric to determine its suitability for curtains.
Differences in diaphaneity help geologists identify various minerals in the field.

Related Terms

  • antonym: opacity - the quality of not allowing light to pass through; being opaque
  • synonym: transparency - the quality of allowing light to pass through so that objects behind can be seen clearly
