cabbage-bark tree

noun
tropical tree with toxic bark and medicinal uses
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈkæbɪdʒ bɑrk tri / (AmE), / ˈkæbɪdʒ bɑːk triː / (BrE)
a tropical tree with shaggy, foul-smelling, toxic bark and strong, durable wood; its bark and seeds are used as a purgative, vermifuge, and narcotic

Examples

The cabbage-bark tree seeds
Herbalists warn that the seeds of the cabbage-bark tree must be used with caution due to their toxicity.
Local communities sometimes use the wood of the cabbage-bark tree to build durable furniture.
The bark of the cabbage-bark tree is known for its strong medicinal properties.
