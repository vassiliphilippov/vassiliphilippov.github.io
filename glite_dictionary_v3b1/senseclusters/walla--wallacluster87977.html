walla

verb
to perform background chatter
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑːlə / (AmE), / ˈwɒlə / (BrE)
to create or perform background conversation or indistinct murmuring for a film, radio, or television scene to simulate a crowd.

Examples

To walla in scenes
The director asked the actors to walla quietly in the background to make the party scene more realistic.
During the recording session, the extras were instructed to walla so the restaurant sounded busy.
Sound designers often use people to walla in order to create a natural crowd atmosphere in movies.
