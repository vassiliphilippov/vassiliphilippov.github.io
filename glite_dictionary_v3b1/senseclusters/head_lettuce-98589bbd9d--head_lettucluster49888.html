head lettuce

noun
lettuce forming a tight, compact head
9.9
Pronunciation: / hɛd ˈlɛtɪs / (AmE), / hɛd ˈlɛtɪs / (BrE)
A type of lettuce with leaves tightly packed into a dense, compact ball or head, such as iceberg or butterhead varieties.

Examples

A head lettuce variety
At the market, he compared romaine and head lettuce before making his choice.
Many sandwiches use crisp head lettuce for extra crunch and flavor.
The salad was made with fresh head lettuce from the garden.

Related Terms
