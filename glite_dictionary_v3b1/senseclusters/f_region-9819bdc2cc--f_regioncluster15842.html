F region

noun
the highest part of the ionosphere
8.6
Pronunciation: /ef ˈriː.dʒən/ (AmE), /ef ˈriː.dʒən/ (BrE)
the highest region of the Earth's ionosphere, lying about 90 to 600 miles above the surface, which contains the greatest concentration of free electrons and plays a key role in long-range radio transmission

Examples

The F region layer
During the night, the F region becomes the primary layer supporting shortwave radio transmission.
Scientists monitor ionization levels in the F region to forecast radio signal quality.
The F region plays an essential role in reflecting radio waves for long-distance communication.
