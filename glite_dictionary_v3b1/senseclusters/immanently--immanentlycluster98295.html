immanently

adverb
inherently, within
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈɪmənəntli / (AmE), / ˈɪmənəntli / (BrE)
in a way that exists or operates within something; inherently or intrinsically.

Examples

Value immanently present
The philosopher argued that value is immanently present in every human action.
Some traditions believe the divine exists immanently within the natural world.
Creativity can be found immanently in the process of solving everyday problems.

Related Terms

  • antonym: externally - in a way that exists or happens outside something
  • synonym: inherently - in a way that is a natural part of something
  • synonym: intrinsically - in an essential or natural way
