saddleback roof

noun
roof with two sloping sides and gable ends
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈsædəlˌbæk ˈruːf / (AmE), / ˈsæd(ə)lbæk ˈruːf / (BrE)
A roof with two sloping sides that meet at a ridge, with a gable at each end.

Examples

A saddleback roof design
Many barns in the region feature a classic saddleback roof design.
The architect recommended a saddleback roof for better water drainage in heavy rain.
The old church was restored with its original saddleback roof still intact.
