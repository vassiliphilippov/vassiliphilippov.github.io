fibrinoid

noun
fibrin-like tissue deposit
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪbrɪˌnɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈfɪbrɪˌnɔɪd / (BrE)
a substance found in tissue that resembles fibrin, often seen in certain diseases or pathological conditions

Examples

Layer of fibrinoid
The pathologist identified a layer of fibrinoid in the damaged blood vessel walls.
Accumulation of fibrinoid is a common feature in certain autoimmune diseases.
Under the microscope, the tissue showed extensive fibrinoid deposits indicating severe inflammation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fibrin - an insoluble protein formed during blood clotting
