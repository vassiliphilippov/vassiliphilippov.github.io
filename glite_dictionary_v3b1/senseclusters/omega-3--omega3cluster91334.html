omega-3

noun
type of polyunsaturated fatty acid
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊməɡə ˈθri / (AmE), / ˌəʊməɡə ˈθri / (BrE)
A polyunsaturated fatty acid with its first double bond three carbon atoms from the end of the carbon chain, important for human health.

Examples

Omega-3 fatty acids
Doctors often recommend taking omega-3 supplements for their anti-inflammatory effects.
Including more omega-3 in your diet can improve heart health.
Many fish such as salmon are rich sources of omega-3.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fatty acid - A carboxylic acid with a long hydrocarbon chain
  • degree higher: omega-6 - A polyunsaturated fatty acid with its first double bond six carbons from the end
