doxastic logic

noun
modal logic of belief and disbelief
Pronunciation: / dɑkˈsæstɪk ˈlɑdʒɪk / (AmE), / dɒkˈsæstɪk ˈlɒdʒɪk / (BrE)
A branch of modal logic concerned with the formal study of belief and disbelief.

Examples

doxastic logic study
Many philosophers use doxastic logic to model rational belief in agents.
The development of doxastic logic has helped formalize theories of knowledge in epistemology.
Unlike epistemic logic, doxastic logic focuses specifically on belief rather than knowledge.

Related Terms

  • synonym: modal logic - a type of logic that deals with modes of truth such as necessity and possibility
