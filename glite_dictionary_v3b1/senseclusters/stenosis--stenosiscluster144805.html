stenosis

noun
abnormal narrowing of a bodily passage
5.3 7.6
Pronunciation: / stəˈnoʊ.sɪs / (AmE), / stɪˈnəʊ.sɪs / (BrE)
an abnormal narrowing of a bodily canal or passageway, often caused by disease or injury

Examples

Spinal stenosis
Spinal stenosis can cause pain and numbness in the legs.
The patient was diagnosed with aortic stenosis, which restricted blood flow from the heart.
Treatment depends on the severity and location of the stenosis.
