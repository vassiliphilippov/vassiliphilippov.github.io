kB

noun
unit of digital information (1000 bytes)
Pronunciation: / keɪ biː / (AmE), / keɪ biː / (BrE)
a unit of digital information equal to about 1,000 or 1,024 bytes, used to measure computer memory or data size

Examples

kB of data
A simple text document often takes up less than 10 kB of space.
Email attachments are usually limited to a few thousand kB.
The file size was only 25 kB, so it downloaded quickly.
My old computer had just 64 kB of RAM.
Please ensure the document does not exceed 100 kB when you email it.
The image file size is only 512 kB, so it downloads very quickly.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: MB - a unit of digital information equal to 1024 kilobytes
  • degree lower: byte - a unit of digital information equal to 8 bits
  • synonym: kilobyte - a unit of information equal to 1,000 bytes
