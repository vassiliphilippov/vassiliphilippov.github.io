grounding

noun
connecting electrical equipment to earth
1.8 3.3
Pronunciation: /ˈgraʊn.dɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈgraʊn.dɪŋ/ (BrE)
The act or process of connecting electrical equipment to the earth to ensure safety by preventing unwanted voltages.

Examples

Electrical grounding
Proper grounding is essential to minimize the risk of electric shock in your home.
Serious accidents can occur if grounding procedures are not correctly followed.
The electrician checked the grounding of all new outlets during the renovation.
