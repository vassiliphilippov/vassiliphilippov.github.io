childe

noun
young nobleman (archaic)
9.9
Pronunciation: / tʃaɪld / (AmE), / tʃaɪld / (BrE)
an archaic term for a young nobleman, especially used in poetry or historical contexts

Examples

Noble childe
In the ancient ballad, the brave childe set out to prove his worth.
The poet described the journey of a wandering childe seeking adventure.
Legends often tell of a childe who later became a famous knight.
