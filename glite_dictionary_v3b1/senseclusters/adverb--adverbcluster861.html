adverb

noun
word that modifies verbs, adjectives, adverbs
Pronunciation: / ˈæd.vɝːb /; / ˈædˌvɝb / (AmE), / ˈæd.vɜːb /; / ˈædvɜːb / (BrE)
a word that modifies a verb, adjective, or another adverb to show how, when, where, or to what degree something happens

Examples

An adverb of time
In the sentence 'He quickly ran home,' the word quickly is an adverb.
Not every word ending in '-ly' is an adverb, but many are.
The teacher explained that an adverb can answer questions like how, when, and where.
In the sentence 'She sings beautifully', 'beautifully' functions as an adverb.
Quickly is an example of an adverb modifying the verb 'run'.
The adverb often comes after the verb it modifies.
