mediastinotomy

noun
surgical chest incision
9.9
Pronunciation: /miː.di.ə.stɪ.ˈnɑː.tə.miː/ /; / /miː.di.ə.stɪ.ˈnɔː.tə.miː/ (AmE), /miː.di.ə.stɪ.ˈnɒt.ə.miː/ (BrE)
a surgical procedure in which an incision is made into the mediastinum to access structures in the chest

Examples

A mediastinotomy procedure
The doctor recommended a mediastinotomy to obtain a tissue sample from the chest.
A mediastinotomy can help diagnose certain diseases affecting the mediastinum.
After the mediastinotomy, the patient was closely monitored in the recovery room.
