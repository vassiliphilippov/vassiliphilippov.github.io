sarcoidosis

noun
chronic disease with nodules in organs
Pronunciation: / ˌsɑːrkoʊˈdoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌsɑːkɔɪˈdəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A chronic disease of unknown cause marked by the formation of nodules (granulomas) in various organs, especially the lungs, lymph nodes, and salivary glands.

Examples

Sarcoidosis lung disease
Sarcoidosis can have a wide range of symptoms, making it difficult for doctors to identify early.
After months of testing, she was finally diagnosed with sarcoidosis affecting her lungs.
New treatments are emerging for patients living with sarcoidosis to help manage chronic symptoms.
