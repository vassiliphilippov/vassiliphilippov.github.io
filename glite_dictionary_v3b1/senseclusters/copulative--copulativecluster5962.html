copulative

noun
a verb linking subject and complement
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːpjəˌleɪtɪv / (AmE), / ˈkɒpjʊlətɪv / (BrE)
a verb, such as 'be' or 'become', that links the subject of a sentence with its complement; a linking verb

Examples

A copulative verb
In the sentence 'She is happy', the word is functions as a copulative.
Teachers often explain that a copulative connects the subject to additional information about the subject.
The most common copulative in English is the verb 'to be'.

Related Terms
