intravenous anesthetic

noun
anesthetic injected into a vein
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntrəˈvinəs ænəsˈθɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌɪntrəˈviːnəs ˌanɪsˈθetɪk / (BrE)
an anesthetic drug that induces anesthesia when injected directly into a vein

Examples

An intravenous anesthetic injection
Because of its quick onset, an intravenous anesthetic is preferred for certain outpatient surgeries.
Modern procedures often use an intravenous anesthetic for rapid induction of unconsciousness.
The surgeon administered an intravenous anesthetic to ensure the patient felt no pain during the operation.
