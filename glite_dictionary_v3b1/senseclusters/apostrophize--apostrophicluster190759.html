apostrophize

verb
use an apostrophe in writing
Pronunciation: / əˈpɑstrəˌfaɪz /; / əˈpɑːstrəˌfaɪz / (AmE), / əˈpɒstrəˌfaɪz /; / əˈpɒstrəfaɪz / (BrE)
To mark or indicate with an apostrophe; to use an apostrophe in writing.

Examples

To apostrophize words
Many people mistakenly apostrophize plurals, which is incorrect.
She had to apostrophize the contraction to show the missing letters.
When writing possessives, remember to apostrophize correctly.
If you don't apostrophise correctly, your writing may appear careless.
In British English, writers commonly apostrophise words like 'it's' or 'can't'.
She carefully apostrophised all the contractions in her essay.

Related Terms

  • synonym: apostrophize - to use an apostrophe (American spelling)
  • antonym: omit - to leave out, especially in writing or speech
