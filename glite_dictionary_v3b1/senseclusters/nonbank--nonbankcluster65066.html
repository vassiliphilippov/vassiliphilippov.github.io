nonbank

noun
financial institution
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑnˌbæŋk / (AmE), / ˈnɒnˌbæŋk / (BrE)
a financial institution that provides services similar to a bank but does not have a full banking license

Examples

A nonbank lender
Many people get their mortgages from a nonbank lender.
A nonbank can offer loans and payment services without being a traditional bank.
The government introduced new regulations for nonbank financial institutions to protect consumers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bank - a licensed financial institution that accepts deposits and makes loans
  • synonym: credit union - a member-owned financial cooperative offering banking services
