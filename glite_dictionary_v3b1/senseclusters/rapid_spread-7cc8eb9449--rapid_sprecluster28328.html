rapid spread

noun
very quick increase or extension
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˈræpɪd sprɛd / (AmE), / ˈrapɪd sprɛd / (BrE)
a sudden or very quick increase or extension in range, occurrence, or influence, especially of something undesirable

Examples

The rapid spread of disease
Scientists are worried about the rapid spread of invasive species in the lake.
The rapid spread of the flu forced schools to close for a week.
The company saw a rapid spread of its new technology across the industry.

Related Terms

  • antonym: slow spread - gradual or slow increase in occurrence
