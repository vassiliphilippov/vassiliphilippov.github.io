incomplete fracture

noun
bone fracture not going all the way through
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnkəmˈplit ˈfræk.tʃɚ / (AmE), / ˌɪnkəmˈpliːt ˈfræk.tʃər / (BrE)
a bone fracture in which the crack does not extend across the entire width of the bone

Examples

An incomplete fracture in bone
A child is more likely to sustain an incomplete fracture because their bones are softer and more flexible.
The X-ray revealed an incomplete fracture in the patient's forearm after the accident.
Unlike a complete break, an incomplete fracture may not cause the bone to be misaligned.

Related Terms

  • antonym: complete fracture - a bone fracture in which the bone is broken all the way through
