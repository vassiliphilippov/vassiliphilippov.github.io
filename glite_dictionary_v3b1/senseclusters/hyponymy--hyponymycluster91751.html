hyponymy

noun
semantic relationship of inclusion
9.9 9.0
Pronunciation: / haɪˈpɑːnɪmi / (AmE), / haɪˈpɒnɪmi / (BrE)
The semantic relationship where the meaning of one word is included within the meaning of another, more general word.

Examples

The hyponymy relation
In linguistics, the hyponymy between 'sparrow' and 'bird' means that every sparrow is a bird.
The hyponymy of 'rose' to 'flower' illustrates how language can express specific and general relationships.
Understanding hyponymy helps students organize vocabulary into hierarchies and categories.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hypernymy - the semantic relation of being more general; the opposite of hyponymy
  • synonym: hyponym - a word whose meaning is included within another's (e.g., 'rose' is a hyponym of 'flower')
