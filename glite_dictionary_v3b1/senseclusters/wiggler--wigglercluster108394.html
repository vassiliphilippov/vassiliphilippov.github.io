wiggler

noun
common worm used as fishing bait
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪɡlər / (AmE), / ˈwɪɡlə / (BrE)
A common name for a terrestrial worm, especially one used as bait by anglers and known for burrowing into and aerating soil.

Examples

Fishing wiggler
After the rain, dozens of wigglers appeared on the wet pavement.
Children watched a wiggler crawl through the loose soil in the backyard.
The fisherman dug in the garden to find a wiggler for his bait box.

Related Terms

  • synonym: worm - a limbless, soft-bodied invertebrate animal
