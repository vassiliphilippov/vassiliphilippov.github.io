wash out

verb
clean thoroughly by washing
3.1 1.8
Pronunciation: /wɑːʃ aʊt/ /; / /wɔːʃ aʊt/ (AmE), /wɒʃ aʊt/ /; / /wɔːʃ aʊt/ (BrE)
to clean or remove dirt, stains, or other substances from something by washing it thoroughly

Examples

To wash out a stain
Please wash out the coffee pot before using it again.
She washed out the old paintbrushes in the sink.
The stains should wash out easily if you treat them quickly.
It's best to wash out grass stains from clothing as soon as possible.
She soaked the shirt in cold water to wash out the coffee spill.
The cleaner managed to wash out every trace of paint from the carpet.
If you don't act quickly, the ink stain will wash out and become impossible to trace.
Repeated laundering can wash out the bright colors of the fabric.
The heavy rains threatened to wash out the bridge across the river.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fade - to gradually lose color or brightness
  • synonym: rinse - to wash quickly with clean water to remove soap or residue
  • antonym: stain - to leave a mark that is difficult to remove
  • synonym: wash off - to clean something by removing substances from its surface with water
