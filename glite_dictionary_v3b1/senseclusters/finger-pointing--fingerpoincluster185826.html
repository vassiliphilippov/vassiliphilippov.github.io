finger-pointing

noun
the act of blaming others
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪŋɡɚ ˌpɔɪntɪŋ /; / ˈfɪŋɡər ˌpɔɪntɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈfɪŋɡə ˌpɔɪntɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of blaming others for problems, especially in a public or accusatory way, instead of trying to solve them

Examples

Finger-pointing at others
Instead of finger-pointing, the team should focus on finding solutions.
The committee urged members to avoid finger-pointing and work together.
There was a lot of finger-pointing after the project failed.
Government officials engaged in finger-pointing rather than addressing the underlying issues.
She grew tired of all the finger-pointing and wanted to focus on solutions instead.
There was a lot of finger-pointing after the deadline was missed.
