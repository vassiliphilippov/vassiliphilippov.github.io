ploy

noun
tactic or maneuver to gain an advantage
5.4 4.0
Pronunciation: /plɔɪ/ (AmE), /plɔɪ/ (BrE)
a clever or strategic action or plan used to gain an advantage, especially in conversation, negotiation, or games

Examples

A clever ploy
He began the meeting with a clever ploy to break the ice and gain everyone's trust.
Her flattery was just a ploy to get out of doing her share of the work.
The salesman used an old ploy to make the offer seem more attractive.
His offer to help was just a ploy to gain her trust.
She came up with a clever ploy to distract her competitors during the game.
The company used a marketing ploy to boost their holiday sales.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ruse - an action intended to deceive someone; a trick
  • synonym: stratagem - a plan or scheme, especially one used to outwit an opponent
  • synonym: strategy - a plan of action designed to achieve a long-term or overall aim
  • synonym: tactic - a planned action or strategy intended to achieve a specific end
