neotraditionalism

noun
return to tradition
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˌniːoʊtrəˈdɪʃənəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌniːəʊtrəˈdɪʃənəlɪzəm / (BrE)
a movement or belief in reviving or intentionally using traditional values, styles, or techniques, especially in culture, art, design, or music

Examples

Neotraditionalism in architecture
Neotraditionalism has shaped the architecture of many new homes in the city.
The artist's work reflects a strong influence of neotraditionalism in its use of classic techniques.
Some critics argue that neotraditionalism limits innovation in contemporary music.
The artist's latest exhibition is a celebration of neotraditionalism in contemporary painting.
Many architects embrace neotraditionalism to create buildings that reflect historical styles.
Critics argue that neotraditionalism limits innovation by focusing too much on the past.

  • synonym: traditionalism - the upholding or maintenance of tradition
