etchant

noun
chemical for etching
Pronunciation: / ˈɛtʃənt / (AmE), / ˈɛtʃənt / (BrE)
a chemical substance used to corrode or remove material from a surface, especially in manufacturing or engraving

Examples

A etchant chemical
The engineer carefully applied the etchant to the metal plate to create the desired pattern.
Always wear protective gloves when handling an etchant in the laboratory.
The etchant quickly removed the thin layer of copper from the circuit board.
