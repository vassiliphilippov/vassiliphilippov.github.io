indistinct

adjective
not clear or sharply defined
Pronunciation: / ˌɪn.dɪˈstɪŋkt / (AmE), / ˌɪn.dɪˈstɪŋkt / (BrE)
not clear or sharply defined; difficult to see, hear, or understand clearly

Examples

An indistinct memory
He has only an indistinct recollection of his early childhood.
Her voice was indistinct on the recording, making it hard to understand what she said.
The mountain was just an indistinct outline against the evening sky.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clear - easy to perceive or understand; not ambiguous
  • synonym: unclear - not easy to see, hear, or understand
