viaticus settlement

noun
sale of insurance policy by terminally ill person
9.9
Pronunciation: / vaɪˈætɪkəs ˈsɛtəlmənt / (AmE), / vaɪˈatɪkəs ˈsɛtəlmənt / (BrE)
The sale of a life insurance policy by a terminally ill policyholder to a third party, often at a value less than the death benefit but greater than the cash surrender value.

Examples

A viaticus settlement sale
A viaticus settlement can provide immediate funds that would otherwise be inaccessible until after death.
Some companies specialize in purchasing insurance policies through viaticus settlements from terminally ill policyholders.
The family decided on a viaticus settlement to manage medical expenses in his final months.
