premature

adjective
occurring too soon; too hasty
4.5 6.13/m
Pronunciation: / Àåprim…ôÀàt É är /; / ÀåpriÀêm…ôÀàt É är / (AmE), / ÀåpriÀêm…ôÀàt É ä…ô / (BrE)
happening or done before the usual or expected time; too soon or too hasty

Examples

Premature decision
His premature announcement caused confusion among the team.
It was premature to celebrate victory before the final results were in.
Making such a decision now would be premature.
Her premature departure from the company surprised her coworkers.
Many investors made a premature decision to sell their shares when the market dipped.
The doctor warned that celebrating the results now would be premature.

Synonyms

  • untimely
    5.3 1.72/m
    untimely refers to something happening at a bad or inappropriate time, while premature describes something occurring too early.
  • ill-timed
    5.5 0.19/m
    ill-timed means happening at an inconvenient or unsuitable moment, while premature means it happens earlier than expected.
  • hasty
    3.9 3.71/m
    hasty describes actions done quickly without enough thought or care, while premature stresses that something happens too soon.

Related Terms

  • antonym: late - occurring after the expected or usual time
