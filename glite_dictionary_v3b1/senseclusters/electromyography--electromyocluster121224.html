electromyography

noun
medical test recording muscle electrical activity
Pronunciation: / ɪˌlɛktroʊmaɪˈɑɡrəfi / (AmE), / ɪˌlɛktrəʊmaɪˈɒɡrəfi / (BrE)
a medical diagnostic procedure that records the electrical activity of muscles to detect neuromuscular disorders

Examples

Electromyography test
Electromyography is commonly used to assess nerve and muscle function in patients with unexplained symptoms.
After undergoing electromyography, he was diagnosed with a neuromuscular disorder.
The doctor recommended electromyography to investigate her muscle weakness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: electroencephalography - diagnostic recording of brain electrical activity
  • synonym: electromyogram - a recording made by electromyography
