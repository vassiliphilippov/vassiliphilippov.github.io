humility

noun
modesty; lack of pride or arrogance
2.4 3.2
Pronunciation: /hjuː.ˈmɪl.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /hjuː.ˈmɪl.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
the quality of being modest and not thinking you are more important than others; lack of pride or arrogance

Examples

With humility and grace
He accepted the award with humility, thanking his team for their support.
Her humility made a lasting impression on everyone she met.
Learning from failure requires a certain amount of humility.
His humility was evident in the way he always gave credit to his team.
She accepted the award with remarkable humility, thanking her colleagues rather than focusing on herself.
True humility enables leaders to admit mistakes and learn from others.

Related Terms

  • antonym: arrogance - the quality of having an exaggerated sense of one's own importance
  • synonym: modesty - the quality of not being too proud or boastful
  • antonym: pride - a high or inordinate opinion of one's own dignity, importance, or superiority
