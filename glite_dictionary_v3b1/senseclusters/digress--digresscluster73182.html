digress

verb
deviate from main topic
Pronunciation: / daɪˈɡrɛs / (AmE), / daɪˈɡrɛs / (BrE)
to leave the main subject temporarily in speech or writing

Examples

To digress from topic
Although she often digresses, her conversations are always fascinating.
Let's not digress and instead focus on the issue at hand.
The professor began to digress from the topic, sharing stories from his travels.
During the meeting, she began to digress into stories unrelated to the topic.
Try not to digress too much during your speech, or you may lose the audience.
Whenever he writes essays, he tends to digress from the central argument.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deviate - to depart from an established course
  • synonym: diverge - to move or extend in different directions from a common point
  • antonym: focus - to concentrate attention or effort
  • antonym: stay - to remain on a subject or path
  • synonym: stray - to move away from a place where one should be or from a subject
