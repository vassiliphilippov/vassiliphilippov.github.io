ozone layer

noun
protective atmospheric layer of ozone
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊˌzoʊn ˌleɪər / (AmE), / ˈəʊzəʊn ˌleɪə / (BrE)
A layer of the Earth's stratosphere that contains a high concentration of ozone, protecting the planet by absorbing most of the sun's harmful ultraviolet radiation.

Examples

The ozone layer
Scientists monitor changes in the ozone layer to study the impacts of pollution.
The ozone layer plays a crucial role in shielding life on Earth from harmful radiation.
The depletion of the ozone layer increases the risk of skin cancer caused by ultraviolet rays.
