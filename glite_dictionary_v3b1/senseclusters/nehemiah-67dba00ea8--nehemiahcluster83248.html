Nehemiah

proper noun
book of the Old Testament; Jewish leader
9.0
Pronunciation: / niːəˈmaɪ.ə / (AmE), / niːɪˈmaɪ.ə / (BrE)
A book of the Old Testament recounting how Nehemiah, a Jewish official at the court of Artaxerxes I, led the rebuilding of Jerusalem after the Babylonian Captivity.

Examples

Book of Nehemiah
During Bible study, she read several chapters from Nehemiah.
Many scholars study Nehemiah to understand Jewish life after the exile.
The book of Nehemiah tells the story of the rebuilding of Jerusalem's walls.
