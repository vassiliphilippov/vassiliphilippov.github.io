endocrine

noun
hormone-secreting gland
Pronunciation: / ˈɛndoʊˌkraɪn / (AmE), / ˈɛndəʊˌkraɪn / (BrE)
a gland in the body that releases hormones directly into the bloodstream

Examples

An endocrine gland
The thyroid is an important endocrine in the human body.
Damage to an endocrine can affect hormone levels and overall health.
Doctors study how each endocrine influences different body functions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exocrine - a gland that releases substances through ducts to the outside of the body
  • synonym: gland - an organ that produces and releases substances
