shiver

noun
involuntary trembling or shaking
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɪvər /; / ˈʃɪvɚ / (AmE), / ˈʃɪvə / (BrE)
an involuntary trembling or tingling feeling or movement of the body, usually caused by cold, fear, or excitement

Examples

A cold shiver
A cold shiver passed through the crowd when the announcement was made.
He tried to hide his shiver of excitement as he opened the envelope.
She felt a sudden shiver run down her spine as the door creaked open.
A sudden shiver ran down my spine when I heard the strange noise.
She felt a shiver of excitement as the curtain rose.
The story sent a pleasant shiver through the audience.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - a state free from motion or disturbance
  • synonym: frisson - a sudden strong feeling of excitement or fear; a thrill
  • synonym: tremble - a slight involuntary shaking movement
  • synonym: tremor - an involuntary quivering or shaking movement
