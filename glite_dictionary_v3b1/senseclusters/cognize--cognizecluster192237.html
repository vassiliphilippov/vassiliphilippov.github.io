cognize

verb
recognize or become aware of
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːɡˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈkɒɡ.naɪz /; / ˈkɒɡˌnaɪz / (BrE)
to be aware of or know something; to perceive or recognize mentally

Examples

To cognize the truth
He slowly cognized the truth of the situation after reading the evidence.
It took several minutes for her to cognize the full meaning of his words.
Some philosophers debate whether animals truly cognize their environment or merely react to stimuli.
He gradually cognised the truth about his heritage.
It took me some time to cognise the full implications of the announcement.
Many fail to cognise the complexity of the situation at first glance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ignore - to deliberately pay no attention to something; disregard
  • synonym: know - to have knowledge or information about something
  • synonym: recognize - to identify something as already known or experienced
