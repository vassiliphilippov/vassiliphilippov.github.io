palpus

noun
spider reproductive organ
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈpæl.pəs/ /; / /ˈpæl.pʌs/ (AmE), /ˈpæl.pəs/ /; / /ˈpæl.pʌs/ (BrE)
in arachnids, especially male spiders, a modified appendage used to transfer sperm to the female during reproduction

Examples

Spider palpus
The male spider uses his palpus to transfer sperm to the female during mating.
Scientists observed the structure of the palpus under a microscope to study its function.
Damage to a spider's palpus can prevent successful reproduction.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pedipalp - one of the second pair of appendages in arachnids, often modified for various functions
