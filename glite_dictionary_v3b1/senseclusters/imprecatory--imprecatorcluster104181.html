imprecatory

adjective
wishing harm
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈprɛkəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ɪmˈprɛkətəri / (BrE)
expressing or relating to a wish or prayer for harm, a curse, or divine punishment, especially in religious texts

Examples

Imprecatory prayer
The poem included several imprecatory lines directed at the king's enemies.
He spoke in an imprecatory tone, hoping misfortune would befall those who wronged him.
Some ancient prayers are imprecatory, asking for harm to come to adversaries.
The psalmist wrote several imprecatory verses asking for justice against his enemies.
Some religious leaders debate whether imprecatory prayers are appropriate in modern worship.
The ancient text contains imprecatory passages that call for divine retribution.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blessing - expressing a wish for good or happiness for someone
  • synonym: cursing - using words to wish harm or evil on someone
