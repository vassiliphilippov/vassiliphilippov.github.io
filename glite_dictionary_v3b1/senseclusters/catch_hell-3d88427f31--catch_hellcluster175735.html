catch hell

verb
receive severe criticism or punishment
Pronunciation: / kætʃ hɛl / (AmE), / kætʃ hɛl /; / katʃ hɛl / (BrE)
(informal) to receive strong or severe criticism or punishment for something wrong or a mistake

Examples

To catch hell from Dad
If you forget to lock the door again, you’ll catch hell from Dad.
She knows she’ll catch hell at work if she’s late again.
The whole team caught hell after they lost the championship game.
He caught hell from his boss after missing the deadline.
The students caught hell for not turning in their assignments on time.
We knew we would catch hell if we broke the rules again.
