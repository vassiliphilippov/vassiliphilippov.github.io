Usuli

noun
supporter of a Shiite Muslim movement in Iran
9.9
Pronunciation: /uː.ˈsuː.liː/ (AmE), /uː.ˈsuː.liː/ (BrE)
a member or supporter of a religious movement in 17th-century Iran among Persian Shiite Muslims, which emphasized the use of reason and independent legal judgment and opposed the Akhbari school

Examples

Usuli religious movement
A major divide in Shiite Islam developed between the Usuli and Akhbari factions.
Many Iranian clerics today follow principles established by the Usuli movement.
The Usuli school stressed the importance of rational interpretation in Islamic law.
