gapped scale

noun
musical scale with fewer than seven notes
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / ɡæpt skeɪl / (AmE), / ɡæpt skeɪl / (BrE)
A musical scale that contains fewer than seven notes, often created by omitting one or more notes from a standard scale.

Examples

A gapped scale in music
Many East Asian musical traditions use a gapped scale rather than a full seven-note scale.
Students learned to recognize the sound of a gapped scale in traditional melodies.
The folk song is built around a gapped scale that omits certain notes from the standard major scale.
