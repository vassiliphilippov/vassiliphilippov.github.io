Taft

proper noun
US President and Supreme Court Chief Justice (1857–1930)
Pronunciation: / tæft / (AmE), / tɑːft / (BrE)
(1857–1930) The 27th President of the United States (1909–13) and later the 10th Chief Justice of the U.S. Supreme Court (1921–30), the only person to have held both offices.

Examples

Taft presidency
Taft was both President and Chief Justice of the United States, a unique achievement in American history.
Historians often discuss Taft's approach to the presidency compared to his predecessor, Theodore Roosevelt.
The election of Taft in 1908 marked a significant shift in U.S. politics.
