Tietze's syndrome

noun
painful swelling of rib cartilage
Pronunciation: / ˈtiːtsəz ˌsɪndroʊm / (AmE), / ˈtiːtsəz ˌsɪndrəʊm / (BrE)
A medical condition characterized by swelling and pain in the cartilage of the upper ribs, often causing chest pain.

Examples

Tietze's syndrome chest pain
Doctors often distinguish Tietze's syndrome from other causes of chest pain through physical examination.
Symptoms of Tietze's syndrome can be alarming but are usually benign.
The patient was diagnosed with Tietze's syndrome after complaining of persistent chest pain.
