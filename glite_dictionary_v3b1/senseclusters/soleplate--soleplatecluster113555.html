soleplate

noun
underside of sports shoe
7.9
Pronunciation: /ˈsoʊl.pleɪt/ (AmE), /ˈsəʊl.pleɪt/ (BrE)
the rigid plate on the underside of a shoe, especially a sports boot, to which studs or treads are attached

Examples

Boot soleplate with studs
The new soccer boots feature a lightweight soleplate with molded studs for better traction.
After several games, the soleplate of his football boots showed signs of wear.
Manufacturers often design the soleplate to improve grip and stability on the field.

Related Terms

  • antonym: insole - the inner part of a shoe that touches the foot
  • synonym: outsole - the outermost sole of a shoe that contacts the ground
