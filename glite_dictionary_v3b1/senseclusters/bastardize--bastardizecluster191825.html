bastardize

verb
corrupt or lower the quality of something
9.9 6.5
Pronunciation: /ˈbæs.tər.daɪz/ (AmE), /ˈbæs.tə.daɪz/ (BrE)
to lower the quality or value of something by adding unwanted elements or by changing it in a way that makes it less pure or true to its original form

Examples

To bastardize a recipe
He refused to bastardize his family's recipes just to appeal to modern tastes.

Adding too many modern elements can bastardise a classic recipe.
Some critics argue that mass production has bastardised traditional craftsmanship.
The translation was so loose that it bastardised the original meaning of the poem.
