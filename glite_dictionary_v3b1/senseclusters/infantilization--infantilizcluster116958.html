infantilization

noun
treating adults as children
8.8
Pronunciation: /ɪn.fæn.tɪ.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ɪn.fæn.tə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɪn.fæn.tɪ.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ɪn.fæn.tə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act or process of treating an adult as if they were a child, especially by limiting their independence or responsibility

Examples

Critics argue that strict dress codes in schools are a form of infantilization.

The infantilization of the elderly often results in their voices being ignored in important decisions.

After his illness, his family’s infantilization made him feel powerless and frustrated.
