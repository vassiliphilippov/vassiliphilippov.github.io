aiglet

noun
sheath at end of shoelace or ribbon
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪɡlət / (AmE), / ˈiːɡlɪt /; / ˈeɪɡlɪt / (BrE)
a small metal or plastic tip or sheath at the end of a shoelace, ribbon, or cord, used to prevent fraying or as a decoration

Examples

Plastic aiglets
Modern shoelaces usually have plastic aiglets instead of metal ones.
She replaced the ribbon's frayed aiglet with a new one for a neater appearance.
The aiglet on his shoelace broke off, making it hard to thread the lace.
Historical reenactors often pay close attention to small details, such as period-correct aiglets.
The general’s uniform was distinguished by golden aiglets on his shoulders.
The ornate aiglet completed the ceremonial appearance of the officer’s braid.
