Acheulean

noun
prehistoric tool industry
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈʃuːliən / (AmE), / əˈʃuːliən / (BrE)
a prehistoric stone-tool industry of the Lower and Middle Paleolithic, known for its distinctive bifacial handaxes

Examples

The Acheulean period
The Acheulean is famous for its large, teardrop-shaped handaxes found across Africa and Europe.
Researchers discovered Acheulean tools at the archaeological site, dating back over a million years.
The transition from the Oldowan to the Acheulean marks a major development in early human technology.
