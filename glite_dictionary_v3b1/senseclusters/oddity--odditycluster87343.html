oddity

noun
strange habit or mannerism
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːdəti /; / ˈɑːd.ɪ.ti / (AmE), / ˈɒdəti /; / ˈɒd.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
a strange or unusual habit, quality, or way of thinking

Examples

A personal oddity
Among his many oddities was a habit of collecting bottle caps from every country he visited.
Her tendency to talk to plants was considered an oddity by her neighbors.
No one minded her little oddity of humming while she worked, as it brightened the mood.
Her oddity made her stand out in a crowd, but she embraced her uniqueness.
Living alone in the forest was seen as an oddity by his neighbors.
The artist was well known for his many oddities and unconventional habits.

Related Terms

  • antonym: normality - the state of being normal
  • synonym: quirk - a peculiar behavioral habit
