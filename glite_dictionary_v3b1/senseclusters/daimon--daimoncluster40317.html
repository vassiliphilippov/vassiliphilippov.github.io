daimon

noun
evil supernatural being in Greek myth
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪ.mɒn / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.mɒn / (BrE)
(in ancient Greek belief) a supernatural being, often an evil spirit or lesser deity, influencing human lives.

Examples

Evil daimon
In the play, a malevolent daimon manipulates the fate of the protagonist.
Philosophers once debated whether a daimon was guiding Socrates' actions.
Some mythologies describe a daimon as a force that tempts people into evil deeds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: demon - an evil spirit or devil, especially in Christian and Jewish belief
