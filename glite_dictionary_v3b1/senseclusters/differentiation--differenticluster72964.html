differentiation

noun
the act of distinguishing between things
2.0 4.7
Pronunciation: /dɪ.fə.rɛn.ʃɪ.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /dɪ.frɛn.ʃɪ.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /dɪ.fə.rɛn.ʃɪ.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /dɪ.frɛn.ʃɪ.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act or process of recognizing or showing that two or more things are different and distinct from each other

Examples

Product differentiation
Modern brands use differentiation to distinguish their products from competitors in the market.
Proper differentiation between facts and opinions is essential in academic writing.
The differentiation of twins based on subtle physical traits can be challenging.
