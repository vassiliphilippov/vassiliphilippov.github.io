moneyed

adjective
having much money; wealthy
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌnid / (AmE), / ˈmʌnɪd / (BrE)
having a lot of money; wealthy or rich

Examples

Moneyed family
She came from a moneyed family that owned several businesses across the city.
The moneyed elite often have disproportionate influence over political decisions.
The proposal was backed by several moneyed interests seeking to protect their investments.
Despite his humble beginnings, he soon found himself among the moneyed aristocrats.
Only the moneyed elite could afford to purchase property in that exclusive area.
The reforms were designed to limit the influence of the moneyed classes in politics.
