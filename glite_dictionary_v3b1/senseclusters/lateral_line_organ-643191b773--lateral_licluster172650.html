lateral line organ

noun
sensory organ in fish/amphibians for detecting water pressure
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈlæt̬.ɚ.əl laɪn ˈɔr.ɡən / (AmE), / ˈlæt.ər.əl laɪn ˈɔː.ɡən / (BrE)
a sensory organ in fish and amphibians that detects pressure changes and movements in the surrounding water

Examples

The lateral line organ in fish
Damage to the lateral line organ can affect a fish’s ability to navigate its environment.
Many aquatic creatures rely on their lateral line organ to sense vibrations and avoid predators.
Researchers studied the lateral line organ to better understand how fish detect currents in the water.
