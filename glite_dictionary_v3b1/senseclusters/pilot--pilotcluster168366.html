pilot

noun
metal frame in front of a locomotive
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪlət / (AmE), / ˈpaɪlət / (BrE)
an inclined metal frame attached to the front of a locomotive to clear obstacles from the tracks, also known as a cowcatcher

Examples

Train pilot
Railway workers inspected the pilot to ensure it would safely clear debris ahead of the train.
Some collectors restore antique locomotives with a polished pilot at the front.
The old steam engine's pilot was dented after hitting fallen branches on the tracks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cowcatcher - a metal frame at the front of a locomotive for clearing the track
