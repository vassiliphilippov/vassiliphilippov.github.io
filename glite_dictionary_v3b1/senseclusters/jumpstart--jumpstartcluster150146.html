jumpstart

verb
start or revive something energetically
4.7 0.34/m
Pronunciation: / Ààd í åmpÀåst…ërt / (AmE), / Ààd í åmpÀåst…ëÀêt / (BrE)
to start or restart something energetically or forcefully, especially to quickly make it active or effective again after a period of inactivity or stagnation

Examples

To jumpstart a project
She worked all weekend to jumpstart her stalled project.
The government hopes the tax cuts will jumpstart the sluggish economy.
We need a new strategy to jumpstart sales this quarter.
