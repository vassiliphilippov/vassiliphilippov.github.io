vigilance

noun
close and continuous attention; alert watchfulness
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪdʒ.ə.ləns /; / ˈvɪdʒələns / (AmE), / ˈvɪdʒ.ɪ.ləns /; / ˈvɪdʒɪləns / (BrE)
the state or quality of being watchful and alert to possible danger or problems

Examples

Vigilance at night
Because the safety of the camp depended on his vigilance, he stayed awake all night.
Her vigilance in monitoring the patients ensured that any problems were caught early.
In the face of rising cyber threats, constant vigilance is required to protect sensitive information.
Cybersecurity requires ongoing vigilance against new threats.
Parental vigilance is essential when children are playing near water.
The soldiers maintained constant vigilance throughout the night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alertness - the quality of being quick to notice and respond to danger
  • antonym: inattention - lack of attention or alertness
  • antonym: negligence - failure to take proper care or attention
  • synonym: watchfulness - the state of being alert and ready to notice possible danger or difficulties
