annihilation

noun
complete destruction or obliteration
1.5 6.4
Pronunciation: /ə.naɪ.ə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ə.naɪ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ə.naɪ.ə.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ə.naɪ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the complete destruction or total obliteration of something, so that nothing remains

Examples

Total annihilation
Some scientists fear that climate change could lead to the annihilation of certain species.
The ancient civilization faced near annihilation during the devastating war.
The nuclear bomb threatened the annihilation of the entire city.
Some scientists warn of the possibility of human annihilation through nuclear war.
The bomb caused the annihilation of several buildings in the city center.
The invasion led to the near annihilation of the entire settlement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: creation - the act of bringing something into existence
  • synonym: destruction - the action or process of causing so much damage to something that it no longer exists or cannot be repaired
  • synonym: devastation - great destruction or damage
