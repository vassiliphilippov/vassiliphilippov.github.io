rusticate

verb
give stone a rustic or roughened look
7.4 7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌstɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈrʌstɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to give a building, stone, or surface a rough, rustic appearance, especially in architecture

Examples

To rusticate stone walls
Masons meticulously rusticated the stone blocks to achieve the desired rugged texture for the façade.
Some buildings rusticate their ground floors to evoke the style of Renaissance palazzos.
The architect decided to rusticate the lower level of the mansion to create a dramatic visual contrast.
Many homeowners choose to rusticate their patios to create a warm, inviting space.
The architect plans to rusticate the facade with stone cladding for a country cottage effect.
They decided to rusticate the kitchen by exposing the original wooden beams.
