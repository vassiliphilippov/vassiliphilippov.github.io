shoot

verb
throw or propel something toward a target
1.3 14/m
Pronunciation: /  Éut / (AmE), /  ÉuÀêt / (BrE)
to kick, throw, or propel a ball or object toward a target, especially in sports

Examples

To shoot a ball
Before you shoot the dice, make sure you understand the rules of craps.
During the game, players must shoot the ball toward the opposing team's goal.
He shot the paper ball across the room and into the trash can.
Every time he gets close to the net, he tries to shoot and score.
She aimed carefully and shot the basketball through the hoop.
The forward shot from the edge of the penalty box, but the goalkeeper made the save.
