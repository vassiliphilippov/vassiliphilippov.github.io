prescreen

noun
preliminary screening
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːˌskriːn / (AmE), / ˈpriːˌskriːn / (BrE)
a preliminary screening or test conducted before the main selection, decision, or public release of something

Examples

A prescreen event
The company held a prescreen of the new documentary for invited guests only.
Applicants must pass a prescreen before moving on to the main interview stage.
Critics attended a prescreen of the movie a week before its official release.

Related Terms

  • antonym: final - the last or main event or selection
  • synonym: preview - an advance showing or viewing before the official release
  • synonym: screening - the showing or testing of something, especially a film or candidates
