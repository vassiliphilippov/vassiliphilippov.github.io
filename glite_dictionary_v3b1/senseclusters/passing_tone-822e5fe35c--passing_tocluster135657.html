passing tone

noun
nonharmonic note for melodic transition
Pronunciation: / ˈpæsɪŋ toʊn / (AmE), / ˈpɑːsɪŋ təʊn / (BrE)
A nonharmonic note inserted between two harmonic notes to create a smooth melodic transition.

Examples

A passing tone note
In this measure, the G note acts as a passing tone between the F and A.
Singers often add a passing tone for extra expression in slower pieces.
The composer used a passing tone in the melody to connect two chord tones smoothly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: neighbor tone - a nonharmonic note that moves to a dissonance then returns to the original note
