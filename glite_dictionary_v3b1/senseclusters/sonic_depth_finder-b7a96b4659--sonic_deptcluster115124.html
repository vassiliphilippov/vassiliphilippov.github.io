sonic depth finder

noun
device measuring water depth using ultrasound
Pronunciation: / ˈsɑːnɪk ˈdɛpθ ˌfaɪndər / (AmE), / ˈsɒnɪk ˈdɛpθ ˌfaɪndə / (BrE)
an instrument that uses ultrasound waves to determine the depth of water or a submerged object

Examples

The sonic depth finder device
Technicians calibrated the sonic depth finder before the research vessel began its survey.
The ship's sonic depth finder indicated the ocean floor was 500 meters below.
Using a sonic depth finder, divers located the submerged wreck quickly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: echo sounder - device for determining water depth by measuring the time echo takes to return
