nubby

adjective
rough or knobbly-textured (especially fabric)
7.5 0.23/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆnÊŒbi / (AmE), / ËˆnÊŒbi / (BrE)
(of fabric or yarn) having a rough or knobbly surface; characterized by small lumps or tufts.

Examples

A nubby wool blanket
A nubby wool blanket draped over the chair added a cozy touch to the room.
She wore a nubby scarf that felt warm against her neck.
The designer preferred nubby fabrics for their tactile appeal.

Synonyms

  • nubbly
    8.0 0.04/m
    nubbly has a rough textile surface with small knots or lumps, while nubby often describes yarn with tiny tufts.
  • knobby
    5.8 0.29/m
    knobby has rounded lumps or bumps on a surface, while nubby refers specifically to rough fabric or yarn with small tufts.
  • nobby
    8.4 0.00/m
    nobby has knobs or rounded bumps often on hard surfaces, while nubby describes soft textiles with small tufts or knobbles.
