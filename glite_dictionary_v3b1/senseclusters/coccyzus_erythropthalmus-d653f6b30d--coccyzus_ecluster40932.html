A species of North American cuckoo, known as the black-billed cuckoo, which builds nests and rears its own young.

Examples

Coccyzus erythropthalmus bird
During migration season, birdwatchers often hope to spot the elusive Coccyzus erythropthalmus in wet woodlands.
The nest of Coccyzus erythropthalmus is typically found low in dense shrubs or thickets.
Unlike some cuckoos, Coccyzus erythropthalmus rears its own young rather than laying eggs in other birds' nests.
