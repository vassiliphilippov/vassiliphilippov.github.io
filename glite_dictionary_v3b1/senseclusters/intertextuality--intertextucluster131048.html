intertextuality

noun
relationship between texts
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˌtɛkstʃuˈæləti / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˌtɛkstʃuˈælɪti / (BrE)
the relationship between texts, especially when one text refers to or is influenced by another

Examples

The intertextuality of texts
The intertextuality in the film adds depth by referencing famous scenes from classic movies.
Literary critics often analyze the intertextuality present in modern novels.
Her essay explores the intertextuality between contemporary poetry and ancient myths.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allusion - an indirect reference to another work or text
  • antonym: originality - the quality of being new and not derived from something else
