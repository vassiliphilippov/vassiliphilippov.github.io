hysterotomy

noun
surgical incision into the uterus
Pronunciation: / ˌhɪstərəˈtoʊmi / (AmE), / ˌhɪstərəˈtəʊmi / (BrE)
a surgical incision into the uterus, typically performed during procedures such as a cesarean section

Examples

A hysterotomy surgery
A hysterotomy is sometimes necessary in certain complicated pregnancies.
Complications after a hysterotomy can include infection or uterine bleeding.
The doctor performed a hysterotomy to gain access to the fetus during surgery.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cesarean section - surgical delivery of a baby by cutting through the abdominal wall and uterus
