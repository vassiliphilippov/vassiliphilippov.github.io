Spenserian sonnet

noun
sonnet form by Edmund Spenser
8.8 9.6
Pronunciation: /spen.ˈsɪr.iː.ən ˈsɑːn.ɪt/ (AmE), /spen.ˈsɪə.riː.ən ˈsɒn.ɪt/ (BrE)
A sonnet consisting of three quatrains and a concluding couplet in iambic pentameter, with the rhyme scheme abab bcbc cdcd ee.

Examples

A Spenserian sonnet poem
Her collection includes several Spenserian sonnets exploring themes of love and nature.
Learning to write a Spenserian sonnet required attention to its unique rhyme scheme.
The poet crafted a Spenserian sonnet to pay homage to the Elizabethan era.
