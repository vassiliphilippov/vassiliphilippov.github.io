thermite

noun
high-temperature chemical mixture
8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈθɜːr.maɪt/ (AmE), /ˈθɜː.maɪt/ (BrE)
a chemical mixture, usually of powdered aluminum and iron oxide, that burns at a very high temperature to produce molten metal

Examples

Thermite welding
The workers used thermite to weld the railway tracks together.
A small amount of thermite can generate enough heat to melt steel.
Firefighters must be cautious when dealing with thermite because of its intense heat.
