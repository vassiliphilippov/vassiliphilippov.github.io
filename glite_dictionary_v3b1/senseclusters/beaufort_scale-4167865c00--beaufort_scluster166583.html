Beaufort scale

noun
scale measuring wind force (0–12)
Pronunciation: / ˈboʊ.fərt skeɪl /; / ˈboʊfərt skeɪl / (AmE), / ˈbəʊ.fət skeɪl /; / ˈbəʊfət skeɪl / (BrE)
a scale or system for measuring or estimating wind force or strength, ranging from 0 (calm) to 12 (hurricane)

Examples

Beaufort scale wind force
According to the Beaufort scale, a gentle breeze corresponds to force 3.
Sailors often consult the Beaufort scale to assess sea conditions before departure.
The weather report indicated a wind speed of force 7 on the Beaufort scale.
According to the Beaufort scale, a wind speed of 20 knots is classified as a fresh breeze.
Meteorologists frequently refer to the Beaufort scale when describing wind conditions in weather reports.
The storm reached a level 10 on the Beaufort scale, causing severe damage along the coast.
