antipope

noun
rival pope elected in opposition to the legitimate pope
9.9 9.5
Pronunciation: /ˈæn.tɪ.poʊp/ /; / /ˈæn.taɪ.poʊp/ (AmE), /ˈæn.tɪ.pəʊp/ (BrE)
a person who is elected pope in opposition to the one viewed as the legitimate or canonically elected pope

Examples

An antipope claims power
During the Western Schism, several antipopes claimed the papacy and created deep divisions within the Church.
Historians study the influence of antipopes on the development of church authority.
The election of an antipope often led to political and religious turmoil in medieval Europe.
