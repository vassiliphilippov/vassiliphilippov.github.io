activist judge

noun
judge influenced by personal beliefs
Pronunciation: / ˈæk.tɪ.vɪst dʒʌdʒ / (AmE), / ˈæk.tɪ.vɪst dʒʌdʒ / (BrE)
a judge whose rulings are influenced by personal beliefs or who actively seeks to promote social change through judicial decisions

Examples

Activist judge decisions
Politicians often disagree on whether a decision reflects the work of an activist judge or a strict interpreter of the law.
Some critics accused the court of being led by an activist judge rather than interpreting the law neutrally.
The new laws were struck down by an activist judge seeking to uphold civil rights.

Related Terms

  • synonym: judicial activism - the practice of judges making decisions based on personal or political considerations rather than existing law
