medium

noun
nutrient substance for growing micro-organisms
2.5
Pronunciation: / ˈmiːdiəm / (AmE), / ˈmiːdiəm / (BrE)
a substance or environment in which something exists, grows, is preserved, or takes place (such as for growing cells, preserving specimens, or as a surrounding material)

Examples

Scientists observed the mold culture developing in the nutrient medium.

This medium contains the necessary nutrients for yeast growth.
Artists often work with different mediums depending on their intended effect, but here it means the environment, like air or water.
Certain bacteria can only survive in a specific medium that supplies the nutrients they require.
Coral reefs thrive in a marine medium rich with salt and sunlight.
Air is the medium through which most smells travel.
The bacteria were cultured in a nutrient medium in the laboratory.
Water can act as a medium for the transmission of sound.
