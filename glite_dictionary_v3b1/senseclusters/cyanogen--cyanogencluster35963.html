cyanogen

noun
a toxic, colorless gas used in chemistry
Pronunciation: / saɪˈænədʒən / (AmE), / saɪˈænədʒən / (BrE)
a colorless, toxic gas with a pungent almond-like odor, used in chemical manufacturing and formerly in chemical warfare

Examples

Cyanogen gas
Cyanogen is highly toxic and must be handled with extreme caution in laboratory settings.
During World War I, cyanogen was considered for use as a chemical weapon due to its poisonous properties.
The pungent odor of cyanogen has been described as similar to almonds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cyanide - a highly toxic chemical compound containing the cyanide ion
