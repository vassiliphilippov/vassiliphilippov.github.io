inbreed

verb
to encourage insularity
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌbriːd / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌbriːd / (BrE)
to make a group, institution, or set of ideas more insular or narrow by encouraging close internal relations and excluding outside influence

Examples

To inbreed ideas
The committee tended to inbreed its leadership, rarely considering outside candidates.
If organizations inbreed their ideas, they risk becoming stagnant and resistant to change.
Academic departments that inbreed may struggle to adapt to new research trends.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diversify - to make more varied or different
  • synonym: isolate - to keep something or someone apart from others
