backstabbing

verb
to betray secretly
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌstæbɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbækˌstæbɪŋ / (BrE)
to betray someone secretly or unfairly, especially by acting against them behind their back.

Examples

To backstab a friend
He admitted to backstabbing his friend to get the promotion.
Rumors spread quickly after she was accused of backstabbing her colleagues at work.
No one expected such backstabbing behavior from someone they trusted.

Related Terms

  • synonym: betray - to be disloyal or unfaithful to someone
  • antonym: support - to help or stand by someone
