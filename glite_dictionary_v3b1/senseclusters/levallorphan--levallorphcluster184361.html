levallorphan

noun
drug reversing morphine respiratory depression
9.9
Pronunciation: / lɛˈvælɔˌrfæn / (AmE), / lɛˈvaləˌfæn / (BrE)
A drug related to morphine, used to counteract respiratory depression caused by morphine overdose without affecting its pain-relieving properties.

Examples

Levallorphan drug
Doctors administered levallorphan after the patient developed breathing difficulties from a morphine overdose.
The use of levallorphan is sometimes indicated to reverse opioid-induced respiratory depression.
Unlike pure antagonists, levallorphan preserves the pain relief effects of morphine.
