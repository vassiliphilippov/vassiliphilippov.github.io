show

verb
express a feeling or quality openly
Pronunciation: / ʃoʊ / (AmE), / ʃəʊ / (BrE)
to make something visible, noticeable, or apparent to others; to display or exhibit

Examples

To show your feelings
Although she tried to hide it, her face showed her true feelings.
He showed his gratitude with a warm smile.
Try not to show your nervousness during the interview.
He showed the audience how the device works during the conference.
The artist will show her new paintings at the gallery next month.
They are showing their vintage car collection at the event this weekend.
Her disappointment showed on her face despite her efforts to hide it.
His excitement really shows when he talks about his new job.
The stain on your shirt will show if you don't cover it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: appear - become visible or come into sight
  • antonym: conceal - to hide or keep a feeling or emotion secret
  • synonym: demonstrate - to show how something works or is done
  • antonym: disappear - cease to be visible, go out of sight
  • synonym: display - to put something in a place where people can see it easily
  • synonym: exhibit - to display publicly for others to see
  • synonym: express - to show or communicate a feeling or thought
  • antonym: hide - to put or keep out of sight
