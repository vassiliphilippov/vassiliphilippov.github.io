take charge

verb
assume control or responsibility
3.5 4.91/m
Pronunciation: / te…™k t É…ërd í / (AmE), / te…™k t É…ëÀêd í / (BrE)
to assume control or responsibility for something or someone; to become the leader or person in command

Examples

To take charge quickly
After months of indecision, he finally chose to take charge and solve the problem himself.
During emergencies, it's important for someone to quickly take charge and organize everyone.
When the manager was absent, Sarah decided to take charge of the meeting.
After the manager resigned, someone had to take charge of the project.
If you don't take charge of your finances, you might run into trouble.
Whenever the team faces a crisis, Julia is always ready to take charge.

Synonyms

  • take the lead
    3.2 4.23/m
    take the lead means assuming control or initiative in a situation, while take charge means taking overall responsibility and command.
  • take control
    3.5 8.23/m
    take control means to begin having authority and making decisions, while take charge implies becoming the leader who accepts responsibility.
  • take the helm
    4.4 0.53/m
    take the helm means leading an organization or group in a formal role, while take charge is more general leadership and responsibility.
  • lead
    1.0 137/m
    lead means to guide or direct people in actions or tasks, while take charge stresses assuming responsibility and overall control.
  • take over
    3.1 28/m
    take over means to begin control, often replacing someone else, while take charge can mean starting to lead without replacing them.
  • take up the running
    7.4 0.00/m
    take up the running means to assume management after someone else, while take charge can mean taking control at any time.
  • hold the reins
    4.9 0.14/m
    hold the reins means to control operations or people behind the scenes, while take charge implies openly taking leadership and visible responsibility.
  • take the wheel
    4.7 0.70/m
    take the wheel often means to drive literally but can mean taking immediate control, while take charge covers broader leadership and duty.
  • run the show
    4.1 1.59/m
    run the show means to manage an event or operation in an informal way, while take charge sounds more formal and official.
  • call the shots
    4.2 2.82/m
    call the shots means to make the important decisions, often informally, while take charge means to accept full responsibility and leadership.
