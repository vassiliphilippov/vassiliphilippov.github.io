infilling

noun
material for filling gaps
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌfɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌfɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of filling a space or gap, or the material used to fill it (especially in construction or urban development)

Examples

Infilling material
The workers used sand as infilling to stabilize the foundation of the new building.
Proper infilling is essential to prevent water from collecting in the gaps between paving stones.
The landscaper recommended a gravel infilling for better drainage in the garden path.
Urban planners encourage infilling to make better use of existing city spaces.
The infilling of the old quarry created new land for housing.
Proper infilling can help prevent erosion in construction projects.
