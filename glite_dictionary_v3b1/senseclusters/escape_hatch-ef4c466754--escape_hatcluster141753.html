escape hatch

noun
provision allowing exit from obligations
5.0
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈskeɪp hætʃ/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈskeɪp hætʃ/ (BrE)
a provision, clause, or informal mechanism (in a contract, policy, plan, or system) that allows a party to exit, avoid, or limit obligations or consequences

Examples

Contract escape hatch
The revised contract includes an escape hatch permitting termination if performance falters.
Designers added an escape hatch to the policy so teams could opt out during the pilot phase.
When negotiations stalled, she suggested an escape hatch to avoid a costly long-term commitment.

  • synonym: escape clause - a clause in an agreement that allows withdrawal or limits liability under specified conditions
