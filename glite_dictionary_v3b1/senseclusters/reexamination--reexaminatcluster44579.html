reexamination

noun
questioning a witness again in court
4.5
Pronunciation: / ˌriˌɛɡˌzæmɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːɪɡˌzæmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
(law) The questioning of a witness by the party that called them, after the witness has been cross-examined.

Examples

Witness reexamination
Reexamination provided the prosecution with an opportunity to reinforce their main arguments.
During the reexamination, the lawyer clarified the intention behind the witness's previous statement.
The judge allowed a brief reexamination to address new issues raised in cross-examination.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cross-examination - Questioning of a witness by the opposing party after direct examination
  • antonym: direct examination - Initial questioning of a witness by the party who called them
