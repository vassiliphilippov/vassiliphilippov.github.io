forger

noun
person who operates a forge
7.1 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / Ààf…îrd í…ôr / (AmE), / Ààf…îÀêd í…ô / (BrE)
a person who operates a forge, typically working with metal by heating and shaping it; a blacksmith.

Examples

Village forger
As a young apprentice, he learned from the village forger how to craft tools.
Before the advent of factories, every town relied on a skilled forger for its metalwork.
The forger skillfully shaped the red-hot iron into a new horseshoe.

Synonyms

  • blacksmith
    4.8 3.02/m
    a blacksmith heats and hammers iron at a forge to make tools, while a forger operates a forge shaping metal.
  • metalsmith
    4.2 0.26/m
    a metalsmith shapes and joins many kinds of metal for objects, while a forger mainly works a forge to shape iron.
