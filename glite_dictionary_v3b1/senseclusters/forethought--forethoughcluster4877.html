forethought

noun
careful advance planning
1.8 4.5
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔrˌθɔt / (AmE), / ˈfɔːˌθɔːt / (BrE)
careful consideration or planning for the future; the act of thinking ahead before acting

Examples

Careful forethought
Her forethought saved the company from a costly mistake.
If you had shown more forethought, this accident could have been avoided.
With a little forethought, the project was completed without any problems.
It was her forethought that ensured we had enough supplies for the trip.
Successful projects often depend on careful forethought and planning.
With a little more forethought, we could have avoided this problem entirely.

  • antonym: recklessness - lack of regard for danger or consequences
