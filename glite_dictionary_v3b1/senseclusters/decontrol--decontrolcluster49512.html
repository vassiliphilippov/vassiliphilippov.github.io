decontrol

verb
remove or relax official controls
8.7 6.1
Pronunciation: /diː.kən.ˈtroʊl/ /; / /dɪ.kən.ˈtroʊl/ (AmE), /diː.kən.ˈtrəʊl/ /; / /dɪ.kən.ˈtrəʊl/ (BrE)
to remove or relax official controls, regulations, or restrictions, especially those imposed by the government

Examples

To decontrol prices
After years of strict oversight, the government decided to decontrol the import of foreign goods.
Some policy makers argue that it is time to decontrol certain agricultural prices.
There is an ongoing debate over whether to decontrol the energy markets.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deregulate - to remove government regulations from an industry or sector
  • antonym: regulate - to control or supervise by means of rules and regulations
