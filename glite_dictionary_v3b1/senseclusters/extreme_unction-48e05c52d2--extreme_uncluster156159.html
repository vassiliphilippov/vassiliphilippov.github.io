extreme unction

noun
Catholic sacrament for the dying
6.7 9.7
Pronunciation: /ɪk.ˈstriːm ˈʌŋk.ʃən/ /; / /ɛk.ˈstriːm ˈʌŋk.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɪk.ˈstriːm ˈʌŋk.ʃən/ /; / /ɛk.ˈstriːm ˈʌŋk.ʃən/ (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic Church) the sacrament in which a priest anoints a dying person with oil and prays for their salvation.

Examples

Extreme unction sacrament
Extreme unction is also known as the anointing of the sick in modern terminology.
Many Catholics seek extreme unction as a final sacrament before death.
The priest administered extreme unction to the dying woman, offering prayers for her soul.
