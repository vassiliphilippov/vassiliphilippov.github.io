licentiate

noun
holder of a licentiate degree
8.3
Pronunciation: / laɪˈsɛnʃiət / (AmE), / lɪˈsɛnʃiət / (BrE)
A person who has been granted a licentiate degree, especially by a European university.

Examples

A licentiate degree
As a licentiate, he was eligible to teach in the faculty.
The board only accepts applications from a certified licentiate of a European institution.
Upon completion of the program, she became a licentiate in philosophy at the university.

Related Terms

  • synonym: graduate - a person who has completed a university degree
