ergo

adverb
therefore; consequently
6.3 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…úr…°o ä / (AmE), / Àà…úÀê…°…ô ä / (BrE)
therefore; as a result; consequently (used as a formal sentence connector)

Examples

He ergo failed
He did not study for the test; ergo, he did not pass.
The experiment failed to produce any results; ergo, the hypothesis was incorrect.
The roads were icy, ergo it was unsafe to drive.

Synonyms

  • therefore
    3.2 149/m
    therefore is a common connector used in speech and writing, while ergo is a rarer, more formal literary connector.
  • consequently
    3.4 17/m
    consequently is a formal result word often used in reports and tests, while ergo feels more old-fashioned and rare.
  • hence
    3.2 30/m
    hence often appears in formal or scientific statements to show result, while ergo is more literary and less common.
  • thus
    3.2 142/m
    thus is used both for showing result and for showing how something happens, while ergo only links results.
