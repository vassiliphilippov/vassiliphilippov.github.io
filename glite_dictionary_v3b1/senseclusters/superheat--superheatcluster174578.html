superheat

noun
excess vapor temperature
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈsuːpərˌhiːt / (AmE), / ˈsuːpəhɪːt / (BrE)
the amount by which the temperature of a vapor exceeds its boiling point at a given pressure

Examples

Measure the superheat
The technician measured the superheat to ensure the air conditioner was charged correctly.
A high superheat reading can indicate that the refrigerant level is too low.
Engineers monitor superheat to prevent damage to the compressor in cooling systems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overheat - to become hotter than is safe or normal
  • antonym: saturation temperature - the temperature at which a substance changes from liquid to vapor at a given pressure
