coping saw

noun
handsaw for cutting curves in wood
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊ.pɪŋ sɔ / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.pɪŋ sɔː / (BrE)
a handsaw with a thin, taut blade held in a U-shaped frame, used for cutting small curves or intricate shapes in wood

Examples

A coping saw blade
A coping saw is ideal for cutting intricate patterns in thin pieces of wood.
She replaced the blade on her coping saw before starting the new project.
The woodworker used a coping saw to create delicate curves in the chair's backrest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fret saw - a saw with a very fine blade for intricate cutting, especially in woodworking or metalworking
