Anglo-Saxon

noun
a person of Anglo-Saxon descent or culture
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˌæŋɡloʊˈsæksən / (AmE), / ˌæŋɡləʊˈsæksən / (BrE)
a person of Anglo-Saxon origin, especially someone from England before the Norman Conquest or their descendants

Examples

An Anglo-Saxon person
Her family claims Anglo-Saxon heritage dating back to the Middle Ages.
Historians debate the impact of the Anglo-Saxon legal system on modern British law.
The museum displayed ancient artifacts belonging to the Anglo-Saxon settlers of England.
Archaeologists discovered an Anglo-Saxon burial site in the countryside.
Historians debate the extent of Anglo-Saxon society's organization before the Norman invasion.
The Anglo-Saxon settlers greatly influenced the English language and culture.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Norman - a member of the people who conquered England in 1066
  • synonym: Saxon - member of a Germanic people who migrated to England
  • synonym: WASP - White Anglo-Saxon Protestant, a term for a social group of wealthy, well-connected white Americans of Protestant English descent
