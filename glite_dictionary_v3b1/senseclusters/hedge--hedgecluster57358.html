hedge

verb
protect against loss or risk
6.7 1.16/m
Pronunciation: / hÉ›dÊ’ / (AmE), / hÉ›dÊ’ / (BrE)
to protect oneself against possible loss or risk, especially in finance, by taking compensatory measures

Examples

To hedge against loss
Farmers often hedge against crop failures by planting different varieties.
Many investors hedge their currency exposure to protect against fluctuations in exchange rates.
She decided to hedge her bets by applying to several companies.
Financial advisors often recommend hedging as a strategy to manage risk.
Many investors hedge their portfolios by buying options or futures contracts.
She hedged her investments to avoid heavy losses during the market downturn.

Synonyms

  • hedge in
    7.9 0.00/m

  • hedge one's bets
    6.8 0.40/m
    hedge one's bets means supporting several options as a safe choice, while hedge means taking measures to reduce possible loss.

Related Terms

  • synonym: insure - to arrange for compensation in case of loss or damage
  • antonym: speculate - to engage in risky financial transactions expecting profit
