sound off

verb
to begin playing or making a sound
Pronunciation: / saʊnd ɔf / (AmE), / saʊnd ɒf / (BrE)
(of musicians or signals) to begin to play or make a sound, especially to mark a start.

Examples

Trumpets sound off
As the ceremony began, the trumpets sounded off in perfect unison.
At precisely noon, the band sounded off with a lively march.
When the gates opened, the orchestra sounded off to signal the start of the event.

Related Terms

  • synonym: strike up - to begin playing music; to start a tune
