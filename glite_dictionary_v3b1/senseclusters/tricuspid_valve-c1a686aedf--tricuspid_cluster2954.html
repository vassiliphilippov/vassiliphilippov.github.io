tricuspid valve

noun
valve in the heart between right atrium and ventricle
4.2 3.9
Pronunciation: /traɪ.ˈkʌ.spɪd vælv/ /; / /traɪ.ˈkʌ.spəd vælv/ (AmE), /traɪ.ˈkʌ.spɪd vælv/ (BrE)
a heart valve with three flaps that sits between the right atrium and right ventricle, allowing blood to flow from the atrium to the ventricle and preventing backflow during ventricular contraction

Examples

The tricuspid valve in heart
A faulty tricuspid valve can lead to regurgitation and heart problems.
During surgery, the doctor carefully examined the patient's tricuspid valve for signs of damage.
The tricuspid valve prevents blood from flowing back into the right atrium when the ventricle contracts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mitral valve - the valve between the left atrium and left ventricle of the heart
