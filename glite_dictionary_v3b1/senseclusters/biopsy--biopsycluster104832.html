biopsy

verb
to remove tissue for testing
4.9
Pronunciation: /ˈbaɪ.ɑːp.siː/ (AmE), /ˈbaɪ.ɒp.siː/ (BrE)
to remove a small piece of tissue from the body for examination, usually to diagnose a disease

Examples

To biopsy a lump
The surgeon decided to biopsy the lump to determine if it was cancerous.
After noticing unusual cells, the doctor biopsied a small section of the patient's liver.
It is common to biopsy suspicious moles to check for skin cancer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: excise - to cut out or remove surgically
  • antonym: implant - to insert or place something into the body
