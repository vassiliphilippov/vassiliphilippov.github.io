patten

noun
historical footwear with wooden soles
7.3 9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈpætən / (AmE), / ˈpæt(ə)n / (BrE)
A type of footwear with a thick wooden sole, traditionally worn over ordinary shoes to raise the wearer above mud or water.

Examples

A patten shoe
In medieval times, many people wore pattens to keep their feet dry on muddy streets.
Jane slipped her shoes into pattens before going out into the rainy courtyard.
The museum displayed a pair of pattens alongside other historic garments.
