cuneiform

adjective
of wedge-shaped bones in anatomy
Pronunciation: / kjuˈniəˌfɔrm /; / ˈkjuːniəˌfɔrm / (AmE), / kjuːˈniːɪfɔːm / (BrE)
shaped like a wedge, especially describing certain bones in the body

Examples

Cuneiform bone
Arthritis in the cuneiform region of the foot can make walking painful.
The surgeon examined the cuneiform bones during the operation on the patient's foot.
X-rays revealed a fracture in the medial cuneiform after the accident.
In anatomy, certain wrist bones are described as cuneiform due to their wedge-like shape.
The fossil displayed distinctly cuneiform marks left by ancient toolmakers.
The tablets featured cuneiform impressions characteristic of early writing systems.
