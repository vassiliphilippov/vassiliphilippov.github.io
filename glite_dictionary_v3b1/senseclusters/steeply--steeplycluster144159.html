steeply

adverb
at a sharp angle or slope
3.0 1.62/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàstiÀêpli / (AmE), / ÀàstiÀêpli / (BrE)
in a way that rises or falls at a sharp angle; at a steep slope or gradient

Examples

Rise steeply
Prices have risen steeply in the last few years, making housing less affordable.
The path descends steeply to the river, so watch your footing.
The railway climbs steeply through the mountains before reaching the summit.

Synonyms

  • precipitously
    7.6 1.31/m
    precipitously means very steeply or suddenly, often used for sudden drops or rises, while steeply focuses on a sharp slope.
  • sharply
    2.3 12/m
    sharply means a quick, strong change in direction or amount, while steeply describes movement or land at a steep angle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abruptly - suddenly or steeply (close synonym)
  • antonym: gently - in a gentle or gradual manner (opposite of steeply)
