hashing

noun
data conversion process
4.2
Pronunciation: /ˈhæʃ.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈhæʃ.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
the process of converting data into a fixed-size string or code, often for security or efficient data retrieval

Examples

Hashing passwords
Many websites use hashing to protect users' passwords from being stolen.
The hashing of files allows quick comparison to detect duplicates in large databases.
Modern security systems rely on hashing to verify the integrity of transmitted data.
