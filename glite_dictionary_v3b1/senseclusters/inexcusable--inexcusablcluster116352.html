Examples

Synonyms

  • unforgivable
    5.2 1.59/m
    unforgivable cannot be forgiven easily by people, while inexcusable means an act cannot be excused or justified.
  • unpardonable
    7.6 0.19/m
    unpardonable sounds formal and means too severe to be forgiven, while inexcusable refers to actions that cannot be excused.
  • inexpiable
    9.9 0.01/m
    inexpiable is a formal term for a fault that cannot be atoned for, while inexcusable focuses on being unjustifiable or unforgivable.
  • unjustifiable
    6.8 0.54/m
    unjustifiable cannot be shown as reasonable or right, while inexcusable stresses that the action cannot be excused or forgiven.
  • undefensible
    7.7 0.00/m
    undefensible cannot be defended in argument or practice, while inexcusable stresses being so bad that no excuse fits.
  • unconscionable
    8.9 1.51/m
    unconscionable is shockingly unfair or morally wrong, while inexcusable means something too bad to be excused or justified.
  • unsupportable
    6.8 0.27/m
    unsupportable cannot be backed or maintained with reasons, while inexcusable focuses on moral or social inability to excuse behavior.
