inexcusable

adjective
unjustifiable; not excusable
1.0 4.6
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ɪk.ˈskjuː.zə.bəl/ (AmE), /ɪn.ɪk.ˈskjuː.zə.bəl/ (BrE)
too bad or unacceptable to be justified or forgiven; impossible to excuse

Examples

An inexcusable mistake
Arriving late without notifying anyone is simply inexcusable.
His behavior at the meeting was inexcusable and shocked everyone present.
The error was so obvious that it was considered inexcusable by the reviewers.
Forgetting your child's birthday is simply inexcusable as a parent.
His rude behavior at the dinner table was inexcusable and embarrassed everyone present.
The team's failure to follow basic safety rules was considered inexcusable by the company.
