fraternal

adjective
of twins, from two separate eggs
5.9 0.64/m
Pronunciation: / fr…ôÀàt…ùÀên…ôl / (AmE), / fr…ôÀàt…úÀên…ôl / (BrE)
(of twins) derived from two separate fertilized eggs, and therefore not genetically identical; dizygotic.

Examples

Fraternal twins
Although fraternal twins can look very different, they are just like any other siblings.
The doctor explained that they were fraternal twins, not identical ones.
The likelihood of having fraternal twins increases with certain genetic factors.

Synonyms

  • dizygotic
    9.1 0.09/m
    dizygotic describes twins from two separate eggs fertilized by different sperm in medical terms, while fraternal names nonidentical twins.
  • dizygous
    9.9 0.00/m
    dizygous refers to embryos from two separate eggs fertilized by different sperm, while fraternal describes nonidentical twins.
  • biovular
    9.9 0.01/m
    biovular means born from two separate ova fertilized independently, while fraternal is the common adjective for such nonidentical twins.
