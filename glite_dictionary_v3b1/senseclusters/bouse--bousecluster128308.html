bouse

verb
haul with a tackle (nautical)
Pronunciation: / baʊs / (AmE), / baʊs / (BrE)
(nautical) To pull or haul with a tackle, especially using a rope and pulley for heavy loads.

Examples

To bouse the sail
He boused the sail tight as the wind picked up.
The sailors had to bouse the anchor chain with all their strength.
They are bousing the heavy cargo aboard using block and tackle.
