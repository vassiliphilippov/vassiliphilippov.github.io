thrum

verb
play strings (musical instrument) in a repetitive way
Pronunciation: / θrʌm / (AmE), / θrʌm / (BrE)
to sound the strings of a stringed instrument, especially in a repetitive or gentle manner

Examples

To thrum a guitar
He began to thrum a soft tune on his guitar while waiting for the band.
She would often thrum absentmindedly on the strings as she composed her songs.
The musician thrummed the harp, filling the room with gentle music.

Related Terms

  • synonym: strum - to play (a stringed instrument) by sweeping the fingers across the strings
