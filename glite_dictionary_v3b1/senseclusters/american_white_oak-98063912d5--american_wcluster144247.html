American white oak

noun
deciduous North American tree; source of durable hardwood
5.1
Pronunciation: / əˈmɛrɪkən waɪt oʊk / (AmE), / əˈmɛrɪkən waɪt əʊk / (BrE)
a large, slow-growing deciduous tree native to eastern North America, with stout spreading branches and leaves with rounded lobes, valued for its strong, durable hardwood

Examples

The American white oak tree
Many wildlife species rely on the acorns produced by the American white oak.
The furniture was made from American white oak because of its strength and beauty.
We planted an American white oak in our backyard for shade and habitat.
