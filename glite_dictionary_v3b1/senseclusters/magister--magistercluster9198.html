magister

noun
Roman official title
9.9
Pronunciation: / məˈɡɪstər / (AmE), / məˈɡɪstə / (BrE)
an official title for a high-ranking officer or administrator, especially in ancient Rome or medieval institutions

Examples

Roman magister
In ancient Rome, a magister was responsible for overseeing important civic duties.
The university awarded the title of magister to distinguished scholars in the Middle Ages.
