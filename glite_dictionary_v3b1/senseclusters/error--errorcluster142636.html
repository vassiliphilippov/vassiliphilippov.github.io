error

noun
misplay by a defensive player in baseball
5.0 4.5
Pronunciation: /ˈe.rər/ (AmE), /ˈe.rə/ (BrE)
(Baseball) A misplay by a defensive player that allows a batter or base runner to continue playing when an out could have been made with ordinary effort.

Examples

A fielding error
He set a record for fewest errors in a single season by a first baseman.
That play was ruled an error instead of a hit by the official scorer.
The shortstop committed an error that allowed the runner to reach second base.
