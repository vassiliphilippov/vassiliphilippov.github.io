long-eared bat

noun
bat species with long ears
3.3
Pronunciation: / lɔŋ ˈɪrd bæt / (AmE), / lɒŋ ˈɪəd bæt / (BrE)
any of various bats found in the Old or New World characterized by very long ears, including species in the genera Plecotus and others.

Examples

Long-eared bat species
Researchers found a long-eared bat roosting in the attic of an old farmhouse.
The long-eared bat uses its oversized ears to detect the faintest sounds of insects in total darkness.
Unlike some other species, the long-eared bat is able to hear high-frequency sounds with remarkable accuracy.
