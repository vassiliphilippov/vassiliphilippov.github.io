Aramaic

noun
ancient Semitic language of southwestern Asia
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˌærəˈmeɪɪk / (AmE), / ˌærəˈmeɪɪk / (BrE)
a Semitic language originally spoken by the ancient Arameans, later used by various peoples in southwestern Asia and still spoken by some communities today

Examples

Ancient Aramaic language
Many ancient inscriptions in the region are found in Aramaic.
Some small communities in the Middle East still speak Aramaic today.
The sacred texts were originally written in Aramaic before being translated.
