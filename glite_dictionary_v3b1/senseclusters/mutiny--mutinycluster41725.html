mutiny

noun
open rebellion against authority
Pronunciation: / ˈmjuːtəni / (AmE), / ˈmjuːtəni / (BrE)
an open rebellion against the proper authorities, especially by soldiers or sailors against their officers

Examples

A mutiny on the ship
Harsh discipline sometimes led to mutiny in naval fleets during the 18th century.
The crew staged a mutiny against the ship's captain due to poor conditions.
There was talk of mutiny among the soldiers after several unpopular orders were given.

Related Terms

  • synonym: insurrection - a violent uprising against an authority or government
  • antonym: obedience - compliance with an order, request, or law; submission to another's authority
  • synonym: rebellion - an act of violent or open resistance to an established government or ruler
