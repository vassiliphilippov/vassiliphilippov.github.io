overpay

verb
to pay too much
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈpeɪ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈpeɪ / (BrE)
to pay more than is necessary or appropriate for something or someone

Examples

To overpay the bill
If you overpay for your groceries, you might not have money left for other expenses.
She realized she had overpaid for the tickets after seeing them on sale elsewhere.
Some employees are accidentally overpaid and must return the extra money to the company.
She realized she had overpaid for the concert tickets after seeing the regular price online.
If you overpay your bill, the extra amount will be credited to your next statement.
The company accidentally overpaid several employees due to a payroll error.

Related Terms

  • antonym: underpay - to pay too little
