cadre

noun
core group in a larger movement or organization
5.1 6.3
Pronunciation: /ˈkæ.driː/ /; / /ˈkæ.dreɪ/ (AmE), /ˈkɑː.drə/ /; / /ˈkɑː.də/ (BrE)
a small group of people who form the core or leadership of a larger organization, movement, or military unit

Examples

A cadre of leaders
A dedicated cadre of activists formed the backbone of the political campaign.
She was recruited into a cadre that coordinated the party’s efforts at the local level.
The movement relied on a small cadre to spread its message and organize events.
During mobilization, a small cadre trains and leads the influx of recruits.
Each regiment maintained a cadre to ensure readiness for rapid expansion during wartime.
The new unit was formed with a cadre of experienced soldiers as its foundation.
