wield

verb
have and exercise power, authority, etc.
8.0 3.6
Pronunciation: /wiːld/ (AmE), /wiːld/ (BrE)
to have and use something (such as a tool, weapon, power, or influence) effectively

Examples

To wield power
She wielded power with a firm but fair hand throughout her career.
The prime minister continues to wield significant influence over party decisions.
They wield authority within the company, guiding its major projects.
She wields significant influence in the company despite her young age.
The chef wielded his knife with impressive precision.
The knight expertly wielded his sword in the tournament.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brandish - to wave or swing (something, especially a weapon) as a threat or in anger or excitement
  • synonym: exert - to apply or use (especially power or effort)
  • synonym: manipulate - to handle or control (a tool, mechanism, etc.) skillfully
