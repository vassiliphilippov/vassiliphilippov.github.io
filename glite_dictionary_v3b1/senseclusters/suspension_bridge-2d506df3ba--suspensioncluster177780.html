suspension bridge

noun
bridge with a deck supported by cables
Pronunciation: / səˈspɛnʃən brɪdʒ / (AmE), / səˈspɛnʃ(ə)n brɪdʒ / (BrE)
a type of bridge in which the deck is hung below suspension cables anchored at both ends

Examples

A suspension bridge span
Engineers chose to build a suspension bridge to span the wide river valley.
Many modern suspension bridges feature striking architectural designs.
The Golden Gate is a famous suspension bridge in San Francisco.
