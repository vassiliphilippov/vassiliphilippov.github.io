binomial

noun
polynomial with two terms
4.0
Pronunciation: / baɪˈnoʊmiəl / (AmE), / baɪˈnəʊmiəl / (BrE)
an expression or phrase with two parts, such as an algebraic sum or a fixed pair of words

Examples

A binomial expression
A common example of a binomial is x + y.
The expression binomial is used to describe polynomials with exactly two terms.
You can expand a binomial raised to a power using the binomial theorem.
The phrase 'salt and pepper' is a well-known binomial in English.
In algebra, a binomial like x + y can be expanded using the binomial theorem.
Writers often use binomials such as 'pros and cons' to create rhythm in their sentences.
