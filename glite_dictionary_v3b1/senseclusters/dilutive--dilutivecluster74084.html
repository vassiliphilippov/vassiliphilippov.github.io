dilutive

adjective
reducing share value
Pronunciation: / daɪˈluːtɪv / (AmE), / daɪˈluːtɪv / (BrE)
causing a reduction in the value or ownership percentage of existing shares, especially by issuing new shares

Examples

Dilutive shares issued
The company warned that the new stock offering could be dilutive to existing shareholders.
Analysts debated whether the merger would have a dilutive or accretive effect on earnings per share.
Shareholders were concerned about the dilutive impact of issuing additional equity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accretive - increasing the value or ownership percentage of existing shares
  • synonym: dilution - the process of reducing the value or ownership percentage of shares
