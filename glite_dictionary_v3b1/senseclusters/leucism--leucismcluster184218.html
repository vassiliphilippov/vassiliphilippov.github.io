leucism

noun
partial loss of pigment
Pronunciation: / ˈluːsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈluːsɪzəm / (BrE)
a condition in animals causing partial loss of pigmentation in the skin, hair, or feathers, resulting in pale or patchy coloration

Examples

Leucism in animals
The white peacock's color is due to leucism rather than albinism.
Some lions with leucism have pale fur but normal eye color.
Unlike albinism, leucism does not affect the pigmentation of the eyes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: albinism - a condition causing complete absence of pigment in skin, hair, and eyes
  • antonym: melanism - a condition causing increased dark pigmentation in skin, hair, or feathers
