sideroblast

noun
immature red blood cell with iron granules
9.9 9.2
Pronunciation: /ˈsaɪ.dər.ə.blæst/ /; / /ˈsɪ.dər.ə.blæst/ (AmE), /ˈsaɪ.də.rə.blɑːst/ /; / /ˈsɪ.də.rə.blɑːst/ (BrE)
a type of immature red blood cell (erythroblast) containing granules of ferritin or iron in its cytoplasm, seen especially in certain anemias

Examples

A sideroblast cell
A hematologist may study sideroblast formation to diagnose certain blood disorders.
Doctors examined the patient's blood smear and identified abnormal sideroblasts.
The presence of ringed sideroblasts in the bone marrow can indicate a type of anemia.
