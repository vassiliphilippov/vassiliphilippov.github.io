hold close

verb
hold something or someone firmly
Pronunciation: / hoʊld kloʊs / (AmE), / həʊld kləʊs / (BrE)
to hold something or someone firmly and tightly, usually in one's hands or arms

Examples

To hold close someone
He held close his daughter after the frightening dream.
She held close the old letter, unwilling to let go of the memory.
The child held close her favorite toy throughout the journey.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embrace - to hold someone closely in one's arms, especially as a sign of affection
  • synonym: hug - to squeeze someone tightly in one's arms, typically to express affection
