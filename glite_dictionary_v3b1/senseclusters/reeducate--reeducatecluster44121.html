reeducate

verb
to retrain physical functions
4.2
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈedʒ.ə.keɪt/ /; / /rɪ.ˈedʒ.ə.keɪt/ (AmE), /riː.ˈedʒ.ə.keɪt/ /; / /rɪ.ˈedʒ.ə.keɪt/ (BrE)
to restore or retrain physical functions, muscles, nerves, or reflexes through therapeutic training after injury or illness

Examples

To reeducate injured muscles
After the accident, doctors worked to reeducate his leg muscles so he could walk again.
Physical therapists use special exercises to reeducate nerves affected by a stroke.
The patient needed months of therapy to reeducate her hand movements.
