slaked lime

noun
calcium hydroxide used in mortar and plaster
8.5
Pronunciation: / sleɪkt laɪm / (AmE), / sleɪkt laɪm / (BrE)
a soft, white, caustic powder (calcium hydroxide) obtained by adding water to quicklime, used especially in making mortar and plaster

Examples

Slaked lime powder
Slaked lime is commonly used to reduce soil acidity in gardens and farmlands.
Protective gloves should be worn when handling slaked lime due to its caustic nature.
The construction workers mixed slaked lime with sand to make a smooth plaster.

Related Terms

  • synonym: quicklime - calcium oxide produced by heating limestone
