willful

adjective
deliberate or intentional (esp. of wrongdoing)
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪlfəl / (AmE), / ˈwɪlfʊl / (BrE)
(of a bad or undesirable action) done deliberately or intentionally, especially with the intention of causing harm or defiance.

Examples

Willful damage
Her actions were not accidental, but clearly willful and calculated.
The jury found the defendant guilty of willful neglect of duty.
The manager fired him for willful disobedience of company policies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accidental - happening by chance, not intended
  • synonym: intentional - done on purpose, not by accident
