floor

noun
bottom surface of a body of water
1.0 154/m
Pronunciation: / fl…îr / (AmE), / fl…îÀê / (BrE)
the flat surface at the bottom of a room, building, tent, or other structure, or the bottom surface of a natural or hollow area

Examples

Wooden floor
Sediments gradually settled on the floor of the reservoir over many years.
Strange plants grew thickly across the floor of the shallow bay.
The diver collected samples from the floor of the lake.
Campers slept side by side on the tent's floor during the storm.
Her keys fell to the floor and slid under the couch.
We swept the floor before laying down the new carpet.
Doctors examined the floor of the mouth for any abnormalities.
Moss grew along the floor of the old well.
The sediment covered the entire floor of the cave.

Synonyms

    1.0 63/m
