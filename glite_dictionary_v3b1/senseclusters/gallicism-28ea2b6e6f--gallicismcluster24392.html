Gallicism

noun
French loanword or expression
Pronunciation: / ˈɡælɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈɡælɪsɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
A word or expression borrowed from French and used in another language, particularly English.

Examples

French Gallicisms
Her speech included several Gallicisms that some listeners did not understand.
Many writers deliberately use a Gallicism to give their prose a touch of sophistication.
The English word rendezvous is a well-known Gallicism.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Anglicism - A word or expression borrowed from English
  • synonym: Latinism - A word or expression borrowed from Latin
