conjuror

noun
magician or illusionist
7.2 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëÀên.d í…ö.…ö / (AmE), / Ààk…ín.d í…ô.r…ô / (BrE)
a person who entertains an audience by performing magic tricks or illusions

Examples

Birthday conjuror
She hired a conjuror for her daughter's birthday party.
The conjuror amazed the children by pulling a rabbit out of a hat.
The conjuror performed a series of astonishing illusions on stage.

Synonyms

  • conjurer
    8.2 0.19/m
    conjurer is the common spelling used by performers and writers, while conjuror is an alternative form.
  • magician
    3.6 7.68/m
    magician can mean a performer or a person believed to have real powers, while conjuror specifically entertains with stage tricks.
  • illusionist
    6.9 0.41/m
    illusionist focuses on making audiences see impossible acts and sleight of hand, while conjuror is a general magic performer.
  • prestidigitator
    9.9 0.07/m
    prestidigitator names a skilled sleight-of-hand expert in formal use, while conjuror is the everyday word for a magic performer.
