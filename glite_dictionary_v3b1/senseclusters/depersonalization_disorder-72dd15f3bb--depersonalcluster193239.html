depersonalization disorder

noun
mental disorder with feelings of unreality

A dissociative mental disorder characterized by persistent or recurrent feelings of unreality and detachment from one's own self, as if observing oneself from outside.

Examples

Persistent depersonalization disorder
After months of stress, she was diagnosed with depersonalization disorder due to her persistent feelings of detachment from reality.
People suffering from depersonalization disorder often describe the sensation of watching themselves as if in a movie.
Treatment for depersonalization disorder may involve therapy and medication to help reconnect with one's sense of self.
After experiencing trauma, she was diagnosed with depersonalisation disorder and began therapy.
It is common for those with depersonalisation disorder to report feeling like an outside observer of themselves.
People with depersonalisation disorder often feel disconnected from their thoughts or bodies.
