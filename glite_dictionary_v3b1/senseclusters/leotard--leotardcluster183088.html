leotard

noun
tight-fitting one-piece garment for dancers/gymnasts
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈliːəˌtɑrd / (AmE), / ˈliːətɑːd / (BrE)
a close-fitting one-piece garment, usually made of stretchable fabric, that covers the torso and is worn especially by dancers, gymnasts, or acrobats

Examples

A leotard for dancers
Gymnasts often change into their leotards before practice at the gym.
She bought a new leotard with long sleeves for her ballet class.
The dancer wore a bright red leotard during her performance.
Ballet students are usually required to practice in a leotard and tights.
She packed several colorful leotards for the dance competition.
The gymnast wore a sparkling leotard during her floor routine.

Related Terms

  • synonym: unitard - a close-fitting one-piece garment covering the entire body except for the head, hands, and feet
