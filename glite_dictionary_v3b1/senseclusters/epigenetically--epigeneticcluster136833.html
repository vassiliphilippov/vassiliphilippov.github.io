epigenetically

adverb
by gene regulation
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪdʒəˈnɛtɪkli / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪdʒɪˈnɛtɪkli / (BrE)
in a manner that involves changes in gene activity without altering the DNA sequence

Examples

Changed epigenetically
Some diseases can be influenced epigenetically by environmental factors such as diet or stress.
Researchers are studying how traits are passed on epigenetically rather than through changes in DNA.
The gene was not mutated but was silenced epigenetically in the experiment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: genetically - in a way that relates to genes or heredity
