tuft

verb
to form clumps
Pronunciation: / tʌft / (AmE), / tʌft / (BrE)
to form or arrange into tufts or small clumps, especially of hair, feathers, or plants

Examples

To tuft the grass
In spring the meadow began tufting as new grass shoots formed tight clumps near the path.
The artisan spent hours tufting the cushion to create a decorative pattern.
Birds often tuft their feathers to keep warm in cold weather.

  • antonym: scatter - to spread or separate widely
