shambling

noun
slow, dragging way of walking
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈʃæmblɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈʃæmblɪŋ / (BrE)
The act of walking with slow, dragging steps without lifting the feet properly.

Examples

Slow shambling steps
His shambling was so pronounced that strangers often offered him a seat.
I noticed the shambling of the tired workers as they left the factory.
The detective's shambling betrayed his exhaustion after the long night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shamble - To walk with a slow, awkward, dragging movement
