cruiser

noun
large motorboat with living quarters
5.4 4/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàkruÀêz…ôr / (AmE), / ÀàkruÀêz…ô / (BrE)
a large motorboat equipped with living accommodations such as a cabin and plumbing, designed for people to live on board while traveling or at anchor

Examples

Luxury cruiser boat
He dreams of retiring and sailing a cruiser around the world.
The family bought a new cruiser equipped with two bedrooms and a small kitchen.
They spent the summer living aboard their cruiser as they traveled along the coast.

Synonyms

  • cabin cruiser
    5.3 0.1/m
    a cabin cruiser is a motorboat with a cabin and facilities for living aboard, while a cruiser is the general name.
  • pleasure craft
    5.1 0.08/m
    a pleasure craft is a recreational motorboat fitted for living aboard, while a cruiser emphasizes travel and staying on board.
  • pleasure boat
    3.4 0.2/m
    a pleasure boat is a recreational motorboat with cabin comforts for short trips, while a cruiser is meant for extended living aboard.
  • motor yacht
    3.5 0.07/m
    a motor yacht is a luxury engine-powered boat often larger and fancier, while a cruiser is a more general live-aboard motorboat.
