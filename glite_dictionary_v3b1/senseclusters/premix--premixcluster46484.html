premix

noun
commercially prepared dry ingredient mixture
Pronunciation: / ˈpriˌmɪks / (AmE), / ˈpriː.mɪks / (BrE)
a mixture of dry ingredients that has been prepared and sold commercially in advance for convenience or specific use

Examples

A cake premix
He bought a premix for bread, saving time on measuring each ingredient separately.
Most supermarkets now carry a pancake premix in the baking aisle.
The cake recipe called for a premix to make baking quick and easy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mix - a combination of different substances put together
