purport

verb
to claim or intend (formally)
Pronunciation: / pərˈpɔrt / (AmE), / pəˈpɔːt / (BrE)
to claim or present oneself as being or doing something, often falsely or without proof

Examples

To purport to help
He purports to represent the interests of the local community, but many are skeptical.
In her letter, she purported to resign for personal reasons.
The organization purports to provide free legal advice to those in need.
He purports to have inside information about the company’s finances.
That document purported to be an original, yet experts found it was a forgery.
The plan purports to improve education, but its effectiveness is questionable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: claim - to assert or state as a fact
  • antonym: deny - to state that something is not true
  • synonym: intend - to have as a plan or purpose
  • synonym: profess - to declare openly, often falsely
