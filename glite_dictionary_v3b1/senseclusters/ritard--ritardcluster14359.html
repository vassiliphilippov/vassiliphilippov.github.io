ritard

verb
to slow tempo
9.0
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈtɑːrd/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈtɑːd/ (BrE)
to slow down the tempo gradually in a piece of music

Examples

To ritard the music
The conductor signaled the orchestra to ritard at the end of the movement.
If the music feels too fast, you can ritard slightly to match the mood.
The pianist chose to ritard before the final chord for dramatic effect.
