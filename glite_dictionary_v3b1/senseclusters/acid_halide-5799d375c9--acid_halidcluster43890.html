acid halide

noun
organic compound with a –COX group, X a halogen
6.2 6.8
Pronunciation: /ˈæs.əd ˈhæ.laɪd/ /; / /ˈæs.ɪd ˈheɪ.laɪd/ (AmE), /ˈæs.ɪd ˈheɪ.laɪd/ /; / /ˈæs.ɪd ˈhæ.laɪd/ (BrE)
an organic compound derived from an acid by replacing the hydroxyl group with a halogen, typically containing the group –COX where X is a halogen atom

Examples

An acid halide compound
Acid chlorides are a common type of acid halide used in organic synthesis.
The preparation of an acid halide often involves reacting a carboxylic acid with thionyl chloride.
When an acid halide is hydrolyzed, it produces the corresponding acid and a hydrogen halide.
