hyperfunction

noun
generalized function (math)
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˈfʌŋkʃən / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəˈfʌŋkʃən / (BrE)
a mathematical generalized function defined as the boundary values of holomorphic functions, used in complex analysis and sheaf theory

Examples

Mathematical hyperfunction
Sato introduced the concept of hyperfunction to extend the theory of distributions in mathematics.
The professor explained how a hyperfunction can be represented as the difference of boundary values of holomorphic functions.
Modern complex analysis often uses hyperfunctions to solve advanced problems in mathematical physics.
