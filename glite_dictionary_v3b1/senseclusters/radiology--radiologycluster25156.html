radiology

noun
medical imaging using radiation
1.0 3.9
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪdiˈɑlədʒi /; / ˌreɪdiˈɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌreɪdiˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of medicine that uses X-rays and other forms of radiation to diagnose and treat diseases

Examples

Medical radiology
Advancements in radiology have made non-invasive diagnostics more accurate.
She decided to specialize in radiology after completing her medical degree.
The hospital has invested in new radiology equipment for cancer diagnosis.
Advancements in radiology have greatly improved the detection of internal injuries.
She decided to specialize in radiology during her medical training.
The doctor ordered a radiology scan to check for broken bones.

Related Terms

  • synonym: diagnostics - procedures or tests used to determine the nature or cause of a disease or condition
  • synonym: imaging - the process of producing an image, especially using technology or medical equipment
  • synonym: radiography - the process or technique of producing images by X-rays
  • antonym: ultrasound - medical imaging using sound waves
