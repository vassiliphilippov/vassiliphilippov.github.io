Oedipal

adjective
relating to Oedipus myth
Pronunciation: / ˈɛdɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈiːdɪpəl / (BrE)
relating to the Greek myth of Oedipus, especially themes of tragic fate, patricide, incest, or complex family relationships

Examples

Oedipal tragedy
The novel explores Oedipal themes of fate and family secrets.
His Oedipal struggle with his father shaped much of his adult life.
Many tragedies contain Oedipal elements inspired by ancient myths.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Freudian - relating to the theories of Sigmund Freud, especially about the unconscious mind
  • synonym: Oedipus complex - a psychoanalytic concept involving a child's feelings toward their parents
