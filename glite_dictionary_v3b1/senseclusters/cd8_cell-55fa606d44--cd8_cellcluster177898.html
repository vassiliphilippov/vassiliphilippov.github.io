CD8 cell

noun
immune T cell with CD8 receptor
5.5
Pronunciation: / si di ˈeɪt sɛl / (AmE), / siː diː ˈeɪt sɛl / (BrE)
a type of T cell that has the CD8 receptor and recognizes and kills virus-infected or abnormal cells

Examples

CD8 cells kill viruses
A lack of CD8 cells can leave patients more vulnerable to certain infections.
Researchers measured the activity of CD8 cells following vaccination.
The immune response relies on CD8 cells to eliminate virus-infected cells.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cytotoxic T cell - a T cell that kills infected or cancerous cells
