nanoprobe

noun
tiny detection device
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈnænoʊˌproʊb / (AmE), / ˈnanəʊˌprəʊb / (BrE)
a very small device or agent, at the nanometer scale, used in scientific research to detect, measure, or manipulate things at the nanoscale

Examples

A nanoprobe device
Scientists used a nanoprobe to detect specific proteins in the blood sample.
The nanoprobe emits light when it binds to certain chemicals, making it useful for imaging cells.
Researchers developed a new nanoprobe that can identify cancer cells at a very early stage.
Scientists developed a new nanoprobe to detect viruses in blood samples.
The nanoprobe can manipulate individual atoms on a surface for advanced research.
Using a nanoprobe, engineers measured the electrical properties of the tiny circuit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biosensor - a device that detects biological information
  • degree higher: microscope - an instrument used to see objects too small for the naked eye
  • synonym: sensor - a device that detects or measures a physical property
