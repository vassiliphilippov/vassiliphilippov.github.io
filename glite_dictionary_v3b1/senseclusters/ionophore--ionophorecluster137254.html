ionophore

noun
ion-transport compound
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈaɪ.ə.nə.fɔːr/ (AmE), /ˈaɪ.ə.nə.fɔː/ (BrE)
a chemical compound that helps ions move across a cell membrane by forming a channel or binding to them

Examples

An ionophore compound
The scientist added an ionophore to the solution to increase the movement of potassium ions across the membrane.
Certain antibiotics act as ionophores, disrupting the balance of ions in bacterial cells.
Researchers studied how the ionophore affected calcium transport in nerve cells.
Researchers added an ionophore to the solution to increase the movement of calcium ions into the cell.
The effectiveness of the ionophore was tested in several types of biological membranes.
By using an ionophore, scientists can study how ions cross cell membranes more easily.
The veterinarian recommended adding an ionophore to the cattle feed to reduce the risk of infection.
Researchers studied how the ionophore affected the growth rates of broiler chickens.
Many farms rely on ionophores to help control coccidiosis in their livestock.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antibiotic - a substance that kills or inhibits the growth of microorganisms
  • synonym: carrier - a substance that transports something, especially across a membrane
  • synonym: carrier protein - a protein that transports substances across a cell membrane
  • antonym: channel - a protein that allows ions to pass through a membrane
  • synonym: channel protein - a protein that forms a passageway for ions across a membrane
  • synonym: coccidiostat - a drug used to prevent or control coccidiosis in animals
