Jews' harp

noun
small plucked musical instrument held in the mouth
Pronunciation: / dʒuz hɑrp / (AmE), / dʒuːz hɑːp / (BrE)
A small lyre-shaped musical instrument played by placing it between the teeth and plucking a flexible metal tongue while changing the shape of the mouth cavity.

Examples

Play the Jews' harp
He amazed the audience by playing a tune on his Jews' harp.
The musician carried a Jews' harp in his pocket wherever he went.
Though small and simple, the Jews' harp can produce surprisingly rich sounds.
