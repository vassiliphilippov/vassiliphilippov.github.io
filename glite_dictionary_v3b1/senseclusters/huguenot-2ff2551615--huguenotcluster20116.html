Huguenot

noun
French Protestant (16th/17th centuries)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhjuːɡəˌnɑt / (AmE), / ˈhjuːɡənɒt / (BrE)
A member of a French Protestant group, particularly of the 16th or 17th centuries, who followed the teachings of John Calvin.

Examples

A Huguenot refugee
He traced his ancestry back to a family of Huguenot refugees.
Many Huguenots fled France after the revocation of the Edict of Nantes in 1685.
The Huguenot community made significant contributions to English culture and industry.
