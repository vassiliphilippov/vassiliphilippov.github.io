China tree

noun
deciduous tree with purple flowers, grown for shade
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃaɪnə triː / (AmE), / ˈtʃaɪnə triː / (BrE)
a deciduous tree native to northern India and China, with purple flowers and small yellow inedible fruit, often grown as a shade tree in the southern United States

Examples

Purple-flowered China tree
In the spring, the China tree bursts into clusters of fragrant purple flowers.
Many southern gardens feature the China tree for its attractive foliage and distinctive yellow fruit.
The large China tree in the backyard provides excellent shade during the summer months.
