divert

verb
move money for dishonest purposes
6.8 3.89/m
Pronunciation: / da…™Ààv…ùt / (AmE), / da…™Ààv…úÀêt / (BrE)
to take money away from its intended purpose or account and move it elsewhere, often secretly or with dishonest intent

Examples

To divert funds
Investigators found that the mayor had diverted millions meant for public projects.
She was caught diverting donations from the charity for her own use.
The accountant was accused of trying to divert company funds into his personal account.

Synonyms

  • siphon off
    7.9 0.29/m
    siphon off secretly moves money into a person's own account for private use, while divert moves funds from their intended purpose.
  • defalcate
    9.9 0.00/m
    defalcate unlawfully takes funds entrusted to someone in legal or formal settings, while divert means taking money from its intended purpose.
  • hive off
    9.5 0.00/m
    hive off moves part of funds or assets to another account or unit, often as an internal business transfer, while divert implies secretly taking money for other use.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embezzle - steal or misappropriate money placed in one's trust or belonging to one's employer
