divert

verb
move money for dishonest purposes
Pronunciation: / daɪˈvɝt / (AmE), / daɪˈvɜːt / (BrE)
to take money away from its intended purpose or account and move it elsewhere, often secretly or with dishonest intent

Examples

To divert funds
Investigators found that the mayor had diverted millions meant for public projects.
She was caught diverting donations from the charity for her own use.
The accountant was accused of trying to divert company funds into his personal account.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embezzle - steal or misappropriate money placed in one's trust or belonging to one's employer
