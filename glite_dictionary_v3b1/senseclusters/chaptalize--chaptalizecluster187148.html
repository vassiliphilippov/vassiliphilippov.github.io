chaptalize

verb
to add sugar in winemaking
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈʃæptəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈʃæptəlaɪz / (BrE)
to add sugar (or other fermentable material) to grape juice or a fermenting beverage to increase the alcohol content

Examples

To chaptalize wine
Some winemakers chaptalize their wines to achieve a higher alcohol level.
In cooler climates, producers may need to chaptalize the must to ensure proper fermentation.
European regulations limit how much a winery can chaptalize its products.
Winemakers sometimes chaptalize grape must to achieve a higher alcohol content in the finished wine.
It is illegal to chaptalize wine in some countries due to strict regulations.
Homebrewers may chaptalize their cider if the natural sugar content is too low for proper fermentation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fortify - to strengthen wine by adding alcohol or other substances
