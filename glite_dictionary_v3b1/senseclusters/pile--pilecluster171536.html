pile

noun
early type of electric battery
8.7 0.05/m
Pronunciation: / paÉªl / (AmE), / paÉªl / (BrE)
a battery consisting of voltaic cells arranged in series, especially the earliest electric battery devised by Volta

Examples

A voltaic pile
Scientists used a pile in early experiments to demonstrate the flow of electric current.
The invention of the pile by Volta marked a turning point in the history of electricity.
Volta's original pile consisted of alternating discs of zinc and copper.
