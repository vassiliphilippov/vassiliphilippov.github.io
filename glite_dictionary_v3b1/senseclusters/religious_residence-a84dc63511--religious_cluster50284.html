religious residence

noun
a residence for religious seclusion
Pronunciation: / rɪˈlɪdʒəs ˈrɛzɪdəns / (AmE), / rɪˈlɪdʒəs ˈrɛzɪdəns / (BrE)
a residence where people live in religious seclusion, such as a monastery or convent

Examples

A religious residence building
She decided to spend a year living in a religious residence to focus on spiritual growth.
The monks retreated to a religious residence deep in the mountains, away from the distractions of daily life.
Visitors are not allowed in certain areas of the religious residence to preserve the community’s privacy and tranquility.
