kaleidoscopical

adjective
constantly shifting or changing
8.6
Pronunciation: / kəˌlaɪdəˈskɑpɪkəl / (AmE), / kəˌlaɪdəˈskɒpɪkəl / (BrE)
continually shifting or rapidly changing in appearance or character; having a complex, colorful, and constantly changing pattern

Examples

A kaleidoscopical pattern
His emotions were kaleidoscopical, shifting from joy to sorrow in the space of an hour.
The novelist portrayed society as a kaleidoscopical mixture of cultures and ideas.
Through the window, the city lights formed a kaleidoscopical display that changed with every passing second.
