unprotested

adjective
not officially dishonored
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnˈproʊtɛstɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌnˈprəʊtɛstɪd / (BrE)
not formally declared as dishonored or unpaid, especially referring to a bill, check, or negotiable instrument

Examples

Unprotested check
The bank returned the unprotested check to the customer without any official notice.
Because the bill was unprotested, no legal action was taken against the issuer.
An unprotested note may still be valid, but it lacks formal evidence of dishonor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: protested - formally declared as dishonored or unpaid
