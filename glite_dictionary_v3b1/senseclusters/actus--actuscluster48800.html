actus

noun
Latin for act
Pronunciation: / ˈæktəs / (AmE), / ˈæktəs / (BrE)
a Latin term meaning an act or action, especially used in legal or philosophical contexts

Examples

Actus reus
In criminal law, both actus reus and mens rea must be proven for a conviction.
The philosopher discussed the concept of actus as a fundamental element of human behavior.
Legal scholars often refer to actus when analyzing the requirements for criminal responsibility.

Related Terms

  • synonym: action - the process of doing something
  • antonym: inaction - lack of action or activity
