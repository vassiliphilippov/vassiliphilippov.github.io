scuff

verb
become scratched or marked
9.0 3.0
Pronunciation: /skʌf/ (AmE), /skʌf/ (BrE)
to mark, scratch, or damage the surface of something by scraping or rubbing it against another object

Examples

To scuff the floor
After moving the furniture, the hallway tiles scuffed and lost their shine.
She was upset when her new sneakers scuffed on the pavement.
The old gym floor scuffed easily after years of use.
Be careful not to scuff your new shoes when walking on gravel.
She tried to scuff the mark off the wall, but it wouldn't come off.
The movers accidentally scuffed the wooden floor with their heavy equipment.
He accidentally scuffed the paintwork when he bumped the car door in the garage.
Be careful not to scuff your new shoes on the rough pavement.
The floor was scuffed after the party, so we had to clean it thoroughly.
