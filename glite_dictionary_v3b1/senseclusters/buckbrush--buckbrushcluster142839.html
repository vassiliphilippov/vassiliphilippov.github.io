buckbrush

noun
shrub eaten by deer
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌkˌbrʌʃ / (AmE), / ˈbʌkˌbrʌʃ / (BrE)
a North American shrub with small leaves, commonly eaten by deer and found in forests and grasslands

Examples

The buckbrush shrub
The hillside was covered in buckbrush, providing food for the local deer population.
Hikers often spot wildlife hiding among the dense buckbrush along the trail.
In spring, the buckbrush blooms with small white flowers attracting bees and butterflies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shrub - a small to medium-sized woody plant
