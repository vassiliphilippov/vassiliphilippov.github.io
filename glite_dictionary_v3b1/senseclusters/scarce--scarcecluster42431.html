scarce

adjective
in short supply; not enough
4.8 10/m
Pronunciation: / sk…õrs / (AmE), / sk…ëÀês / (BrE)
not enough or difficult to find because the supply is less than the demand.

Examples

Scarce water supply
After the long drought, water was extremely scarce in the region.
During the winter months, fresh fruit became scarce in the small village.
In times of war, basic supplies can quickly become scarce.

Synonyms

  • scanty
    6.6 0.41/m
    scanty describes a very small, barely enough amount, while scarce means supplies are too low for demand.
  • rare
    4.1 45/m
    rare means not common and happens seldom, while scarce means there is not enough to meet demand.

Related Terms

  • antonym: plentiful - existing in large quantities; more than sufficient
  • synonym: rare - not occurring very often; uncommon
