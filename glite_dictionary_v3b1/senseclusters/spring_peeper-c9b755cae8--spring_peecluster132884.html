spring peeper

noun
North American tree frog with a shrill spring call
8.8
Pronunciation: / sprɪŋ ˈpipɚ / (AmE), / sprɪŋ ˈpiːpə / (BrE)
a small North American tree frog (Pseudacris crucifer) with a distinctive high-pitched call, commonly heard near wetlands in early spring

Examples

The spring peeper frog
Every year, the distinctive call of the spring peeper announces the arrival of warmer weather.
The chorus of spring peepers can be deafening on a mild evening in April.
We spotted several spring peepers hiding among the wet leaves by the pond.
