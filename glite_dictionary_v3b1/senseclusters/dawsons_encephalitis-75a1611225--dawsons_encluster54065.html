Dawson's encephalitis

noun
rare progressive brain inflammation (measles-related)
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈdɔː.sənz en.se.fə.ˈlaɪ.tɪs/ /; / /ˈdɔː.sənz eŋ.ke.fə.ˈlaɪ.tɪs/ (AmE), /ˈdɔː.sənz en.se.fə.ˈlaɪ.tɪs/ /; / /ˈdɔː.sənz eŋ.ke.fə.ˈlaɪ.tɪs/ (BrE)
A rare, chronic, progressive brain inflammation caused by the measles virus, occurring mainly in children and young adults, and usually leading to death within a few years.

Examples

Dawson's encephalitis disease
Children who develop Dawson's encephalitis often have a history of early measles infection.
Researchers are studying possible treatments for Dawson's encephalitis, although the prognosis remains poor.
The diagnosis of Dawson's encephalitis was confirmed after a brain biopsy.
