cephalothorax

noun
fused head and thorax
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛfələˈθɔːræks / (AmE), / ˌsɛfələˈθɔːræks / (BrE)
the fused head and thorax of certain arthropods, such as spiders and crabs

Examples

The cephalothorax of spider
The cephalothorax of a spider contains its eyes, mouthparts, and legs.
In crabs, the cephalothorax is covered by a hard shell for protection.
Scientists examined the cephalothorax to study the arrangement of the animal's limbs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abdomen - the rear part of an arthropod's body
  • synonym: thorax - the middle section of an insect's body
