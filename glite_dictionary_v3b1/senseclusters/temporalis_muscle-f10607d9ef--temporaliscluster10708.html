temporalis muscle

noun
muscle of the side of the head for jaw movement
Pronunciation: / tɛmˈpɔːrəlɪs ˈmʌsəl / (AmE), / tɛmˈpɔːrəlɪs ˈmʌsl / (BrE)
A large, fan-shaped muscle extending from the temporal fossa to the coronoid process of the mandible, involved in raising the lower jaw and closing the mouth.

Examples

The temporalis muscle action
During jaw surgery, the surgeon carefully avoided damaging the temporalis muscle.
Tension headaches can sometimes be caused by strain in the temporalis muscle.
The temporalis muscle plays an important role in chewing and biting.
