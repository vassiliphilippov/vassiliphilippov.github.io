large-headed

adjective
having a large head
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈlɑrdʒ ˌhɛdɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɑːdʒ ˌhɛdɪd / (BrE)
having a head that is significantly larger than usual, either proportionally or absolutely.

Examples

A large-headed statue
She described the sculpture as oddly large-headed compared to its body.
Some turtles are known for their large-headed appearance.
The baby was born large-headed and healthy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: small-headed - having a head that is smaller than usual
