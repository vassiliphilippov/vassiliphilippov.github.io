liquate

verb
to become liquid
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪkweɪt / (AmE), / ˈlɪkweɪt / (BrE)
to become liquid or to melt, especially as a result of heat.

Examples

To liquate in heat
When heated, the metal will liquate and flow into the mold.
The wax started to liquate as the temperature in the room increased.
If left in the sun, the ice cream will quickly liquate and make a mess.

Related Terms

  • synonym: melt - to change from solid to liquid by heat
  • antonym: solidify - to become solid or make something solid
