standing

adjective
executed from a standing position
2.2
Pronunciation: / ˈstændɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstændɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a person, object, or action) in an upright position, not sitting, lying, or attached to something else

Examples

Standing position
He prefers to start races from a standing position rather than a running start.
In the Olympics, there used to be a standing long jump event.
The crowd gave her a standing ovation after the performance.
A standing fan can be moved from room to room as needed.
The exhibition featured a standing sculpture rather than one mounted on the wall.
We installed a standing lamp in the corner of the living room.
Despite being tired, she continued standing during the whole ceremony.
The audience remained standing throughout the entire performance.
We waited for hours in a long standing line at the ticket counter.
