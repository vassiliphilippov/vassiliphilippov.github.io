amphibole group

noun
group of related silicate minerals
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈæm.fəˌboʊl ɡruːp / (AmE), / ˈam.fɪˌbəʊl ɡruːp / (BrE)
a group of minerals with similar crystal structures, composed of silicate chains combined mainly with sodium, calcium, magnesium, iron, and aluminum

Examples

Amphibole group minerals
Amphiboles in the amphibole group are important indicators of rock formation conditions.
Geologists identified hornblende as a member of the amphibole group.
The amphibole group includes minerals commonly found in metamorphic rocks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pyroxene group - group of silicate minerals similar in structure to amphiboles but with different arrangements
