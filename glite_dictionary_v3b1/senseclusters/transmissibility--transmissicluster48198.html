transmissibility

noun
ability to spread
7.2
Pronunciation: /trænz.mɪ.sə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /trænz.mɪ.sɪ.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /trænz.mɪ.sə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /trænz.mɪ.sɪ.ˈbɪl.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
the ability of a disease, virus, or other agent to be passed from one person or organism to another

Examples

Transmissibility of a virus
Scientists are studying the transmissibility of the virus to understand how quickly it can spread.
The high transmissibility of the disease makes it difficult to control outbreaks.
Public health measures aim to reduce the transmissibility of infectious agents in the community.
