lyre-flower

noun
ornamental plant with heart-shaped flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪər ˌflaʊər / (AmE), / ˈlaɪə ˌflaʊə / (BrE)
A garden plant with deep-pink, drooping, heart-shaped flowers, commonly grown as an ornamental.

Examples

A lyre-flower plant
A row of lyre-flowers lined the path, their heart-shaped blossoms nodding in the breeze.
She planted a lyre-flower near the gate to add charm to the walkway.
The lyre-flower brightened the garden with its delicate pink blooms each spring.
