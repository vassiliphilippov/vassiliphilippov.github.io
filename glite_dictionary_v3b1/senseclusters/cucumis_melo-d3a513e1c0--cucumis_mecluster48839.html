Cucumis melo

noun
species of vine producing muskmelons
8.1
Pronunciation: /ˈkjuː.kə.mɪs ˈme.loʊ/ (AmE), /ˈkjuː.kə.mɪs ˈme.ləʊ/ (BrE)
A species of vine that produces edible muskmelons, including varieties such as cantaloupe and honeydew, with netted rind and sweet, aromatic flesh.

Examples

Cucumis melo vine
Botanists classify cantaloupe and honeydew as varieties of Cucumis melo.
Farmers in the region specialize in growing Cucumis melo due to its popularity.
The sweet flesh of the Cucumis melo is prized in summer salads worldwide.

Related Terms

  • antonym: watermelon - a large fruit with watery, sweet flesh
