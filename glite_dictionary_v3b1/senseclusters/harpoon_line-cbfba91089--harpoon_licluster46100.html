harpoon line

noun
rope attached to a harpoon, for securing catch
8.6
Pronunciation: / hɑrˈpun laɪn / (AmE), / hɑːˈpuːn laɪn / (BrE)
a strong rope attached to a harpoon, used to secure the catch to the harpooner's boat

Examples

A strong harpoon line
A strong harpoon line is essential for securing large catches at sea.
The whalers coiled the harpoon line carefully to avoid tangles during the hunt.
When the whale was struck, the taut harpoon line nearly pulled the boat under.

Related Terms

  • synonym: harpoon - a long spear with a barbed point used for hunting large fish or whales
