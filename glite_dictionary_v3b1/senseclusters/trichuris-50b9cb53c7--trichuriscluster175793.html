Trichuris

noun
parasitic roundworm genus
Pronunciation: / trɪˈkjʊrɪs / (AmE), / trɪˈkjʊərɪs / (BrE)
a genus of parasitic roundworms, commonly called whipworms, that infect the intestines of mammals

Examples

The Trichuris worm
The veterinarian diagnosed the dog with a Trichuris infection after examining a stool sample.
Humans can become infected with Trichuris by ingesting contaminated food or water.
Treatment for Trichuris usually involves specific antiparasitic medications prescribed by a doctor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Ascaris - a genus of large parasitic roundworms
  • synonym: whipworm - a common name for worms of the genus Trichuris
