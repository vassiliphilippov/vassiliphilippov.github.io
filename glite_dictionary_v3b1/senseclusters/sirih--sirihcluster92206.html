sirih

noun
betel nut chew
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːri / (AmE), / ˈsɪrɪ / (BrE)
a traditional chew made by wrapping areca nut and other ingredients in a betel leaf, commonly used in Southeast Asia

Examples

To chew sirih after meals
After dinner, the host offered each guest a sirih as a gesture of hospitality.
Chewing sirih is a common tradition at many cultural ceremonies in Indonesia.
The elderly woman prepared a fresh sirih for her afternoon break.
