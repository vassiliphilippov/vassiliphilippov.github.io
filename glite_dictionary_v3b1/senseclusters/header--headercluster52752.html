header

noun
heading at the start of a section or page
3.7 3.0
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛdər / (AmE), / ˈhɛdə / (BrE)
a line or section at the top of a page, document, or website that introduces or identifies the content below

Examples

A page header
Please add a header to your document with your name and the date.
The report contained a clear header at the top of each page.
Without a proper header, readers might not understand what the section is about.
In HTML, the header element typically contains the site logo and primary navigation links.
Accessibility guidelines recommend using a header landmark to help screen readers find page introductions.
When building the blog theme, she styled the header across all pages for a consistent look.

Related Terms

  • synonym: banner - an HTML landmark role or page area representing site-oriented header content; often used interchangeably with page header in web accessibility.
  • antonym: footer - a line of text at the bottom of a page
  • synonym: heading - a title at the head of a page or section
