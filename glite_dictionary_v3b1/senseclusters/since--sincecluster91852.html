since

adverb
from then until now
1.0
Pronunciation: / sɪns / (AmE), / sɪns / (BrE)
from a particular time in the past until now or until a later time mentioned.

Examples

Ever since then
He moved to the city in 2010 and has lived there ever since.
I started learning French last year and have practiced every day since.
We met at a conference in March and have kept in touch since.

Related Terms

  • synonym: afterward - at a later time; subsequently
  • antonym: before - at an earlier time
