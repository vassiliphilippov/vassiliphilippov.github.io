cybernetically

adverb
in a machine-like way
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪbərˈnɛtɪkli / (AmE), / ˌsaɪbəˈnɛtɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that is controlled by or relates to computers, technology, or cybernetics

Examples

Move cybernetically
The robot moved cybernetically through the assembly line, never making a mistake.
She answered the questions cybernetically, with no hint of emotion in her voice.
The dancers performed the routine almost cybernetically, each step perfectly synchronized.
The robot adjusted its movements cybernetically in response to changes in its environment.
Modern prosthetics can be controlled cybernetically through signals from the user's nervous system.
The team studied how information is processed cybernetically in both machines and living organisms.
The factory's production line is now operated cybernetically for greater efficiency.
She controlled her smart home devices cybernetically through a central computer system.
The drone adjusted its flight path cybernetically in response to changing weather conditions.

  • antonym: manually - by hand or without the use of machines
  • synonym: mechanically - in a way that is automatic or without emotion
