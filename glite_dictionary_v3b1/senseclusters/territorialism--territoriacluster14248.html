territorialism

noun
policy of territorial control
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛrɪˈtɔːriəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌtɛrɪˈtɔːriəˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
a political policy or ideology focused on controlling, claiming, or expanding territory and strongly defending territorial sovereignty

Examples

National territorialism
The rise of territorialism in the region led to increased border tensions.
Critics argue that territorialism can hinder peaceful international relations.
The leader's speech promoted territorialism as a way to protect national interests.

Related Terms

  • synonym: expansionism - a policy of increasing a country's territory or influence
  • antonym: internationalism - a policy of cooperation among nations
  • synonym: nationalism - a strong identification with and support for one's nation
