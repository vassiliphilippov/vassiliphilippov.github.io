Giovanni Mastai-Ferretti

proper noun
Italian pope (Pius IX), 1846–1878
9.9
Pronunciation: / dʒoʊˈvɑːni ˌmɑːstaɪ fəˈrɛti / (AmE), / dʒəʊˈvɑːni ˌmæstaɪ fəˈretti / (BrE)
(1792–1878) Italian pope, known as Pope Pius IX, who served from 1846 to 1878 and proclaimed the dogma of the Immaculate Conception.

Examples

Giovanni Mastai-Ferretti pope
Giovanni Mastai-Ferretti was elected pope in 1846 and later adopted the name Pius IX.
As pope, Giovanni Mastai-Ferretti declared the dogma of the Immaculate Conception in 1854.
History books detail Giovanni Mastai-Ferretti's lengthy papacy and influence on the Catholic Church.
