hurl

verb
make a thrusting forward movement
3.5 2.64/m
Pronunciation: / h…úrl / (AmE), / h…úÀêl / (BrE)
to make a forceful or thrusting forward movement, especially suddenly or with great energy

Examples

To hurl himself forward
He hurled himself into the debate, determined to make his point heard.
The soldier hurled himself at the enemy line, surprising everyone with his courage.
With a sudden burst of energy, she hurled forward to block the door before it closed.

Synonyms

  • thrust
    3.9 7.78/m
    thrust pushes something quickly and often into or through, while hurl is a sudden forceful forward movement, sometimes throwing.
  • lunge
    4.1 2.24/m
    lunge is a sudden forward move to reach or attack, while hurl is a strong forward motion that can apply to objects.
  • fling
    4.1 1.53/m
    fling means to throw or move oneself suddenly and often without control, while hurl suggests a more forceful, aimed movement.
  • hurtle
    6.8 2.19/m
    hurtle means to move very fast with violent speed and little control, while hurl is a forceful forward action that can be deliberate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: thrust - to push or drive with force, as in a forward movement
