trot

verb
run at a moderately swift pace
Pronunciation: / trɑt / (AmE), / trɒt / (BrE)
to run at a moderately swift pace, especially referring to animals such as horses, or to move briskly at a pace between a walk and a run

Examples

To trot quickly
As soon as he heard his name, the dog started trotting toward its owner.
She trotted across the field to greet her friends.
The horses began to trot as they left the starting line.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: gallop - to move or run fast, especially of a horse, at its fastest gait
  • degree lower: walk - to move at a regular, relatively slow pace by lifting and setting down each foot in turn
