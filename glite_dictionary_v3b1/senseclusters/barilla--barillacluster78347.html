barilla

noun
plant burned to produce soda ash/calcium carbonate
Pronunciation: / bəˈrɪlə / (AmE), / bəˈrɪlə / (BrE)
a plant (especially of the genus Salsola) that was burned in the past to produce soda ash for making glass and soap

Examples

The barilla plant
Historically, barilla was harvested and burned to obtain valuable soda ash.
In 19th-century Algeria, fields were cultivated specifically for growing barilla.
The process of extracting calcium carbonate from barilla was vital to ancient glassmaking.
Dried barilla plants were burned to extract chemicals for glassmaking.
In the eighteenth century, barilla was an important source of soda ash in Europe.
The villagers collected barilla from the salt marshes to use in soap production.
