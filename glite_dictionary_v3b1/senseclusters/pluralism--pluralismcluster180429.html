pluralism

noun
acceptance of multiple groups in society
Pronunciation: / ˈplʊr.əˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈplʊə.rə.lɪz.əm / (BrE)
the condition or system in which different racial, religious, ethnic, or cultural groups are accepted and maintained within a society

Examples

Cultural pluralism
Modern democracies are often characterized by pluralism, allowing many different communities to flourish.
Some critics argue that pluralism can lead to social fragmentation if not balanced with shared values.
The university prides itself on its commitment to cultural pluralism and open dialogue.

Related Terms

  • synonym: multiculturalism - the presence and co-existence of diverse cultures within a society
