Champlain

proper noun
lake in northeastern US and southern Canada
Pronunciation: / ʃæmˈpleɪn / (AmE), / ʃæmˈpleɪn / (BrE)
a lake in northeastern New York, northwestern Vermont, and southern Quebec, known for its historical significance during major wars.

Examples

Champlain Lake
Many tourists enjoy boating and fishing on Champlain each summer.
The Battle of Champlain was a key event in the War of 1812.
Wildlife conservation efforts around Lake Champlain have increased significantly in recent years.
