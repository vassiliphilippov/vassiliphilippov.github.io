acidulant

noun
substance increasing acidity
7.5
Pronunciation: / əˈsɪdʒələnt / (AmE), / əˈsɪdjʊlənt / (BrE)
a substance added to food or drink to make it more acidic, either to give a sour taste or to help with preservation or processing

Examples

An acidulant in food
The food scientist added an acidulant to the sauce to help preserve its freshness.
Cosmetic products often contain an acidulant to maintain the correct pH for skin safety.
An acidulant is sometimes used in industrial processes to control the acidity of solutions.
Citric acid is a common acidulant in soft drinks.
Manufacturers often use an acidulant to balance the sweetness in candies.
The recipe calls for an acidulant to enhance the flavor of the sauce.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acidifier - a substance that increases acidity
  • antonym: alkalizer - a substance that increases alkalinity
  • antonym: sweetener - a substance used to make food or drink taste sweet
