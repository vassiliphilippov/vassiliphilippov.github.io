coefficient of absorption

noun
measure of absorption of radiation by a material
6.0 7.5
Pronunciation: / koʊˌɪfɪʃənt əv əbˈsɔːrpʃən / (AmE), / kəʊˌɪfɪʃənt əv əbˈsɔːpʃən / (BrE)
A quantity that measures how much electromagnetic radiation (such as light) is absorbed by a substance per unit mass or thickness as the radiation passes through it.

Examples

coefficient of absorption value
Glass has a low coefficient of absorption for visible light but a higher one for ultraviolet radiation.
Researchers measured the coefficient of absorption to optimize the protective material's effectiveness.
The coefficient of absorption for each material determines how much light is lost as it passes through.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absorptivity - the property of a material that determines how much radiation is absorbed
  • degree lower: coefficient of reflection - measure of how much radiation is reflected by a surface
  • antonym: transmittance - the fraction of radiation that passes through a substance
