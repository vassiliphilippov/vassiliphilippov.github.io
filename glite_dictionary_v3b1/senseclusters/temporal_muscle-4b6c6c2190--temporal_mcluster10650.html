temporal muscle

noun
broad jaw-closing muscle at the side of the head
5.8 5.1
Pronunciation: /ˈtem.pə.rəl ˈmʌs.əl/ (AmE), /ˈtem.pə.rəl ˈmʌs.əl/ (BrE)
a broad, fan-shaped muscle extending from the temporal fossa to the coronoid process of the mandible, responsible for raising the lower jaw and closing the mouth

Examples

The temporal muscle contracts
During chewing, the temporal muscle contracts to help raise the lower jaw.
Surgeons must avoid injuring the temporal muscle during certain cranial procedures.
The temporal muscle can sometimes become sore after excessive clenching of the teeth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: masseter - a facial muscle important in chewing, located at the jaw
