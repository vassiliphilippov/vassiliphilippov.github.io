giant garlic

noun
a large, leek-like European vegetable
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒaɪənt ˈɡɑrlɪk / (AmE), / ˈdʒaɪənt ˈɡɑːlɪk / (BrE)
A European variety of leek (Allium ampeloprasum) cultivated and used similarly to leeks, often noted for its large size and mild flavour.

Examples

Fresh giant garlic
Many chefs prefer giant garlic for its mild flavour and impressive size when preparing soups.
The recipe recommends slicing giant garlic and sautéing it as you would with regular leeks.
We planted giant garlic in our garden next to the traditional leeks and onions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: leek - a long, white-stemmed vegetable related to onions and garlic
