mannerism

noun
deliberate or exaggerated behavior
Pronunciation: / ˈmænəˌrɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈmænərɪzəm / (BrE)
a deliberate or exaggerated way of behaving, speaking, or performing, especially one intended to impress or conceal one's true feelings

Examples

A theatrical mannerism
He adopted a theatrical mannerism whenever he spoke at public events.
Her constant use of grand gestures was merely a mannerism to impress her audience.
The actor's exaggerated mannerism in the role made the character seem less believable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: affectation - an artificial or pretentious behavior or speech
