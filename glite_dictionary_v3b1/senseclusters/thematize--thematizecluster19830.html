thematize

verb
to identify themes
8.2
Pronunciation: /ˈθem.ə.taɪz/ /; / /ˈθiː.mə.taɪz/ (AmE), /ˈθem.ə.taɪz/ /; / /ˈθiː.mə.taɪz/ (BrE)
to make something the main theme or to organize materials by identifying and focusing on themes

Examples

To thematize data
Researchers often thematize interview data to find patterns in participants' responses.
The teacher asked students to thematize the novel by identifying its main topics.
In her report, she thematized the survey results to highlight recurring issues.
The professor chose to thematize migration in her course on modern literature.
Many documentaries thematize the impact of technology on society.
The artist's latest exhibition thematizes the relationship between humans and nature.

  • antonym: ignore - to pay no attention to something
