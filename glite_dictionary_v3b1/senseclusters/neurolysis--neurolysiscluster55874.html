neurolysis

noun
nerve-release surgery
9.9
Pronunciation: /nʊr.ə.ˈlɪ.sɪs/ /; / /nuː.rə.ˈlɪ.sɪs/ (AmE), /njʊə.rə.ˈlɪ.sɪs/ /; / /njuː.rə.ˈlɪ.sɪs/ (BrE)
a surgical procedure to free a nerve from surrounding scar tissue, adhesions, or compressing structures without damaging the nerve

Examples

Surgical neurolysis procedure
The surgeon performed neurolysis to relieve the patient's chronic nerve pain.
After the accident, neurolysis was necessary to restore movement in her hand.
Successful neurolysis can help patients regain sensation and function in affected areas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nerve decompression - surgical relief of pressure on a nerve
  • antonym: neurectomy - surgical removal of part of a nerve
