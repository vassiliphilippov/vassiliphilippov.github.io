Baeda

proper noun
English monk, scholar, and historian (672–735)
Pronunciation: / ˈbeɪdə / (AmE), / ˈbiːdə / (BrE)
An English monk, historian, and scholar (672–735), also known as Bede or the Venerable Bede, noted for his influential writings, especially on the history of England.

Examples

Baeda the monk
Baeda wrote his famous Ecclesiastical History, which remains an important source for early English history.
Historians consider Baeda one of the greatest scholars of Anglo-Saxon England.
Many medieval scholars referenced the works of Baeda in their own writings.
