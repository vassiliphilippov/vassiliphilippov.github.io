Clark cell

noun
historical voltaic cell standard
Pronunciation: / klɑrk sɛl / (AmE), / klɑːk sɛl / (BrE)
a type of voltaic cell formerly used as a standard of electromotive force in scientific measurements

Examples

The Clark cell standard
Although replaced by more modern standards, the Clark cell marked a significant advance in electrical measurement.
Calibration of instruments often relied on the stable output of a Clark cell.
The Clark cell was once widely used to provide a reference voltage for laboratories.

Related Terms

  • synonym: voltaic cell - an electrochemical cell that generates electricity from a chemical reaction
