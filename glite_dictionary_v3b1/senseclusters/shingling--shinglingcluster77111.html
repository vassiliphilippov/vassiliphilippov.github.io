shingling

noun
creating overlapping text k-grams
9.3
Pronunciation: /ˈʃɪŋ.ɡlɪŋ/ /; / /ˈʃɪŋ.ɡəl.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈʃɪŋ.ɡlɪŋ/ /; / /ˈʃɪŋ.ɡəl.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
(in information retrieval and computer science) the process of producing overlapping substrings (called shingles or k-grams) from a text or document to measure similarity or detect duplicates.

Examples

Text shingling
The algorithm uses shingling to compare documents by counting shared shingles.
By applying shingling, the plagiarism detector found matching passages across student essays.
Researchers evaluated different shingling strategies to speed up large-scale web crawling.

Related Terms

  • synonym: k-gram - a substring of length k from a larger string, used in text processing and comparison
  • synonym: shingle - a contiguous substring (k-gram) extracted from text, used in similarity algorithms
