lowlight

noun
dim lighting
4.6
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈləʊˌlaɪt / (BrE)
a condition or situation where there is very little light or dim illumination

Examples

Lowlight conditions
The photographer struggled to get a clear shot in the lowlight of the old theater.
Many animals have adapted to see better in lowlight environments such as forests at dusk.
Reading in lowlight for long periods can strain your eyes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: brightness - the quality of being full of light
  • degree higher: darkness - the absence of light
  • synonym: dimness - the state of being not bright or clear
