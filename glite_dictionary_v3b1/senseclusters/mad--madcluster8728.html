Synonyms

  • angry
    1.0 81/m
    angry is direct and general for strong displeasure, while mad is common informal talk for similar strong upset.
  • furious
    3.0 13/m
    furious means very strong, violent anger, while mad is a more general word for being angry or upset.
  • livid
    5.5 0.90/m
    livid shows an extreme, pale-face anger people picture, while mad describes general anger without that dramatic image.
  • infuriated
    4.5 0.03/m
    infuriated means someone is made extremely angry by something, while mad covers normal angry feelings without that force.
  • enraged
    4.1 0.01/m
    enraged means filled with violent rage, while mad is more everyday and can be less intense than that full rage.
  • irate
    5.3 1.47/m
    irate is a formal way to say very angry, while mad is informal and common in everyday speech.
  • incensed
    6.2 0.00/m
    incensed shows anger at unfair or wrong acts, while mad can mean anger but not always this moral shock.
  • outraged
    4.1 0.99/m
    outraged is angry and shocked at obvious injustice, while mad simply means angry without the strong moral shock.
  • riled
    7.1 0.03/m
    riled means annoyed or stirred up, usually by someone, while mad covers broader anger from many causes.
  • ticked off
    7.9 0.19/m
    ticked off is an informal way to say annoyed or angry, while mad is more neutral and fits many situations.
  • annoyed
    1.0 4.77/m
    annoyed is mildly angry or irritated, while mad can be stronger and mean deeper or louder anger.
  • peeved
    6.7 0.49/m
    peeved means small, petty irritation over a minor thing, while mad covers stronger or more serious anger.
  • miffed
    7.1 0.40/m
    miffed means slightly upset or offended, often small and personal, while mad covers larger or clearer anger.
  • sore
    3.9 3.06/m
    sore means hurt and upset, sometimes quietly angry, while mad usually means clear anger that others can notice.
  • huffy
    5.9 0.15/m
    huffy means easily offended and quick to show annoyance, while mad is a general state of feeling angry.
