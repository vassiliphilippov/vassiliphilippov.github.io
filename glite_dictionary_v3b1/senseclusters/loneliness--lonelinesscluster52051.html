loneliness

noun
disposition toward being alone
2.6
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊnli.nəs / (AmE), / ˈləʊnli.nəs / (BrE)
a tendency to seek solitude or prefer being alone

Examples

Quiet loneliness
Despite his loneliness, he felt comfortable and at peace by himself.
His loneliness was not sadness, but rather a strong preference for solitude.
She always showed a natural loneliness, preferring quiet corners to crowded rooms.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gregariousness - a tendency to seek the company of others
  • synonym: solitude - the state of being alone, often by choice
