paraffin

noun
waxy solid from petroleum used for candles, coating
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛrəˌfɪn /; / ˈpæɹ.ə.fɪn / (AmE), / ˈpærəfɪn /; / ˈpær.ə.fɪn / (BrE)
a white or colorless, waxy solid made from petroleum, used in candles, waterproofing, and as a preservative

Examples

Paraffin wax candle
Paraffin is commonly used in making candles because it burns cleanly.
She coated the cheese in paraffin to preserve it for several months.
The old lantern was fueled with paraffin to light the room.
Candles are often made from paraffin wax due to its stability and affordability.
He spilled some paraffin on the bench while fueling the lamp.
The laboratory used paraffin as a solvent in several experiments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: wax - a general term for various substances, including paraffin, used for candles and coatings
