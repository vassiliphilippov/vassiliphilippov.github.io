morganatic

adjective
of a royal or noble marriage to a partner of lower rank, with no rights for spouse or children
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: /mɑːr.ɡə.ˈnæ.tɪk/ /; / /mɔːr.ɡə.ˈnæ.tɪk/ (AmE), /mɔː.ɡə.ˈnæ.tɪk/ (BrE)
(of a marriage) relating to or designating a marriage between a person of royal or noble birth and a partner of lower rank, in which the spouse of lower status and their children do not receive the titles or property rights of the higher-ranking partner.

Examples

Morganatic marriage
In some European countries, morganatic unions were once a way for royals to marry commoners without altering the line of succession.
The offspring of a morganatic marriage typically had no claim to noble titles or inheritance.
The prince's morganatic marriage meant his wife would never become queen.
