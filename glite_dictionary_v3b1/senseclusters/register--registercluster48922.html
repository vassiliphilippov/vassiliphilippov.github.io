register

verb
officially record with an authority
1.0 2.4
Pronunciation: /ˈre.dʒɪ.stər/ /; / /ˈre.dʒə.stər/ (AmE), /ˈre.dʒɪ.stə/ /; / /ˈre.dʒə.stə/ (BrE)
to record or enter information officially, such as in a list, with an authority, or to enroll (including for voting)

Examples

To register a company
All companies are required to register their business names before operating legally.
The birth of their child was registered at the city hall.
You must register your car with the local authorities within seven days of purchase.
Many citizens registered to vote for the first time this year.
The government encourages young people to register as soon as they turn eighteen.
You must register before you are eligible to vote in the election.
All guests must register at the front desk upon arrival.
Doctors are required to register each birth in the national records.
She registered the new company with the local authorities last week.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deregister - to remove (something) from an official list or register
  • synonym: enroll - to officially sign up or register for something
  • antonym: erase - to remove written or recorded information
  • synonym: record - to set down in writing or some other permanent form for later reference
