maori hen

noun
flightless New Zealand bird, also known as weka
Pronunciation: / ˈmaʊri hɛn / (AmE), / ˈmaʊəri hɛn / (BrE)
a large, flightless bird of New Zealand, known for its short wings with a spur and noted for its aggressive and thievish behavior; also called the weka

Examples

The maori hen bird
Early explorers encountered the maori hen in dense New Zealand forests, noting its fearless scavenging.
The maori hen is famous for its curiosity and tendency to steal shiny objects from campsites.
Unlike most birds, the maori hen uses the spur on its wing to defend itself from predators.

Related Terms
