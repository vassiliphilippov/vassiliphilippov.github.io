fictionalization

noun
work based on real events but written as fiction
Pronunciation: / ˌfɪkʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌfɪkʃənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process, act, or result of creating a fictional version of real events or people; also a story, book, film, or other work based on real events or facts but presented as fiction.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nonfiction - writing that is factual rather than imaginary or fictional
