hecatomb

noun
large ancient sacrifice; mass destruction
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛkəˌtoʊm / (AmE), / ˈhɛkətəʊm / (BrE)
A great public sacrifice, especially the ancient Greek or Roman ritual slaughter of 100 oxen; also, a large-scale destruction or loss of life.

Examples

A hecatomb sacrifice
Poets have used hecatomb as a metaphor for sweeping destruction on the battlefield.
The ancient Greeks would sometimes offer a hecatomb to appease the gods during times of crisis.
The memorial recalls the hecatomb of lives lost in the conflict.

Related Terms

  • synonym: massacre - the killing of many people
  • synonym: offering - something offered in sacrifice
