lasting effect

noun
an enduring consequence or result
3.2
Pronunciation: / ˈlæstɪŋ ɪˈfɛkt / (AmE), / ˈlɑːstɪŋ ɪˈfɛkt / (BrE)
a consequence or result that endures for a long time after an event or action

Examples

A lasting effect
Getting enough sleep as a child can have a lasting effect on cognitive development.
The company’s decision had a lasting effect on its reputation.
The new law is expected to have a lasting effect on education reform.

Related Terms

  • antonym: immediate effect - a consequence occurring right after an event or action
  • synonym: long-term effect - a consequence lasting over an extended period of time
