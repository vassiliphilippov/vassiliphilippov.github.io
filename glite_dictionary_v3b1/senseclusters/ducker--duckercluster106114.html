ducker

noun
audio volume controller
9.4
Pronunciation: /ˈdʌk.ər/ (AmE), /ˈdʌk.ə/ (BrE)
a device or audio processor that automatically lowers the volume of one sound when another sound is present, especially in broadcasting or audio mixing

Examples

Audio ducker device
The sound engineer used a ducker to automatically lower the background music when someone spoke.
Modern radio stations often rely on a ducker to keep the announcer's voice clear over music.
By adjusting the ducker, the technician ensured the interview was always easy to hear.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compressor - an audio device that reduces the dynamic range of a sound
  • synonym: limiter - an audio processor that prevents a signal from exceeding a set level
