polychaete

noun
bristle-bearing marine worm
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑliˌkit / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪˌkiːt / (BrE)
A segmented marine worm of the class Polychaeta, having paired appendages with bristles on each segment and typically possessing both sexes.

Examples

A polychaete worm
A polychaete can regenerate lost segments, making it a fascinating subject for research.
Scientists study polychaete populations to monitor the health of marine ecosystems.
The ocean floor was teeming with polychaete worms of various colors and sizes.
