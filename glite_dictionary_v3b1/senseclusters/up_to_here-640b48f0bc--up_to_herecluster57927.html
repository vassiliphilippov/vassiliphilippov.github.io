up to here

adverb
at the limit of patience or tolerance
Pronunciation: / ʌp tə hɪr / (AmE), / ʌp tə hɪə / (BrE)
having reached the limit of one's patience, tolerance, or endurance, especially due to repeated annoyance or difficulty

Examples

I am up to here
I am up to here with all these extra assignments they keep giving me at work.
She's up to here with the constant arguing in her household and just wants some peace.
We were all up to here with delays by the time the meeting finally began.

Related Terms

  • synonym: at the end of one's tether - having no patience left to deal with a problem
  • synonym: fed up - tired of something to the point of annoyance
