farm

verb
to collect fees or profits (historical)
Pronunciation: / fɑrm / (AmE), / fɑːm / (BrE)
(historical) To collect fees or profits, especially by undertaking the right to do so from an authority in exchange for a fixed payment.

Examples

To farm taxes
He secured the right to farm customs duties at several ports.
In the 18th century, wealthy merchants would farm the taxes for the government.
The king allowed certain nobles to farm revenues from the royal estates.

Related Terms

  • synonym: tax farming - the practice of collecting taxes on behalf of an authority, often at a profit
