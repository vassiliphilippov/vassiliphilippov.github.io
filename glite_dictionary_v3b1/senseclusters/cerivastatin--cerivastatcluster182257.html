cerivastatin

noun
cholesterol-lowering drug (Baycol)
Pronunciation: / səˌrɪvəˈstætn̩ / (AmE), / səˌrɪvəˈstætn̩ / (BrE)
a synthetic drug used orally to lower blood cholesterol levels; formerly marketed under the trade name Baycol

Examples

Cerivastatin drug
After concerns about side effects, cerivastatin was withdrawn from the market.

The doctor prescribed cerivastatin to help manage his high cholesterol.
