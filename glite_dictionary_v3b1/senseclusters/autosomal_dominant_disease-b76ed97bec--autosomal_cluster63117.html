autosomal dominant disease

noun
disease caused by a dominant autosomal gene mutation
9.9 5.2
Pronunciation: /ɑː.tə.ˈsoʊ.məl ˈdɑː.mə.nənt dɪ.ˈziːz/ /; / /ɔː.tə.ˈsoʊ.məl ˈdɑː.mə.nənt dɪ.ˈziːz/ (AmE), /ɔː.tə.ˈsəʊ.məl ˈdɒ.mɪ.nənt dɪ.ˈziːz/ (BrE)
A genetic disorder caused by a dominant mutation on a non-sex chromosome (autosome), where only one copy of the altered gene is necessary to cause the disease.

Examples

An autosomal dominant disease
Huntington's chorea is an example of an autosomal dominant disease.
If one parent has an autosomal dominant disease, each child has a 50% risk of inheriting it.
Many autosomal dominant diseases can be detected through genetic testing before symptoms appear.
