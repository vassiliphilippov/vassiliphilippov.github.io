acylation

noun
addition of an acyl group to a molecule
6.6 8.3
Pronunciation: /ə.saɪ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ə.saɪ.ˈleɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of introducing an acyl group into a molecule, often as part of a chemical reaction

Examples

Acylation reaction
During acylation, an acyl group is transferred to a specific position on the molecule.
Enzyme-catalyzed acylation reactions are widely used in pharmaceutical chemistry.
The acylation of aniline is an important step in the synthesis of dyes.
