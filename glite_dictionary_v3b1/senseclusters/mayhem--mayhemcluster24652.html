mayhem

noun
unlawful mutilation or crippling (legal term)
9.1
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪ.hɛm / (AmE), / ˈmeɪ.hɛm / (BrE)
the crime of willfully and unlawfully crippling or mutilating another person, especially by depriving them of a body part

Examples

Criminal mayhem
Historic cases of mayhem often involved the intentional disabling of an opponent to prevent them from fighting.
In some jurisdictions, mayhem is considered a felony offense, separate from assault or battery.
The defendant was charged with mayhem after the violent altercation left the victim permanently disfigured.

Related Terms

  • synonym: maiming - the act of injuring or wounding seriously and permanently
