coax

noun
type of cable for high-frequency signals
Pronunciation: / koʊ.æks / (AmE), / kəʊ.æks / (BrE)
A type of electrical cable with a central conductor, used to transmit high-frequency signals, especially in telecommunications and broadcasting.

Examples

A coax cable
Before installing the new router, check if the coax is properly connected to the wall outlet.
High-speed internet is often delivered to homes using coax cables.
The technician replaced the old coax connecting the television to the antenna.

Related Terms

  • synonym: coaxial cable - an electrical cable with an inner conductor and a tubular insulating layer, used to transmit signals
