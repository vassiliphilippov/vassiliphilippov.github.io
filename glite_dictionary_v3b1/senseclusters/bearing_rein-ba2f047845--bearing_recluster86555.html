bearing rein

noun
rein for a horse's head position
3.8 8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛrɪŋ reɪn / (AmE), / ˈbeərɪŋ reɪn / (BrE)
A rein used to keep a horse's head in a particular position, typically raised, while driving or riding.

Examples

A bearing rein used
Animal welfare advocates criticized the use of the bearing rein as cruel to horses.
The coachman adjusted the bearing rein to keep the horse's head held high.
The historical film depicted horses fitted with a bearing rein while pulling carriages through the city.
