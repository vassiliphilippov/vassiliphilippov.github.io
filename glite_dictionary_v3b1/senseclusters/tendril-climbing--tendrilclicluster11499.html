tendril-climbing

adjective
climbing using tendrils (botanical)
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛndrəl ˌklaɪmɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtɛndrɪl ˌklaɪmɪŋ / (BrE)
(of a plant) climbing by means of tendrils that coil around a support.

Examples

tendril-climbing plant species
Botanists observed that the tendril-climbing species adapted well to the trellis system.
Many peas and sweet peas are tendril-climbing plants with slender, curling structures.
The tendril-climbing habit allows these vines to grow vertically without strong stems.

Related Terms

  • antonym: twining - (of plants) climbing by spiraling around a support
