ground

verb
to prevent from flying (aviation)
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ɡraʊnd / (BrE)
to prevent (an aircraft or pilot) from flying, usually for safety or disciplinary reasons

Examples

To ground a plane
Following the safety inspection, the airline grounded all its Boeing 737 aircraft.
Severe weather conditions grounded flights at the airport for several hours.
The young pilot was grounded for failing to follow proper landing procedures.
