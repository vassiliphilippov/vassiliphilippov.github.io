ruddy

adjective
having a healthy reddish color
9.9 4.3
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌdi / (AmE), / ˈrʌdi / (BrE)
having a healthy red color, especially in the face or cheeks

Examples

His ruddy cheeks
His ruddy cheeks glowed in the cold winter air.
She admired the ruddy apples piled high in the basket.
The farmer’s face was ruddy from working outdoors all year.
After a day of hiking, her ruddy cheeks glowed with good health.
Santa Claus is often depicted with a jolly, ruddy face.
The farmer's ruddy complexion showed his years spent working outdoors.

Related Terms

  • antonym: pale - lacking color in the face, often from poor health or fear
  • synonym: red - of the color of blood, fire, or rubies
  • synonym: rosy - having a pink or red color, especially in the cheeks
