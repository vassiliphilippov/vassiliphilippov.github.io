wash hands

verb
to clean hands for hygiene
Pronunciation: / wɑʃ hændz / (AmE), / wɒʃ handz / (BrE)
to clean one's hands, typically using soap and water, in order to maintain hygiene and prevent the spread of illness

Examples

To wash hands thoroughly
Doctors always wash hands before examining their patients to reduce infection risk.
It's important to wash hands after returning home from public places.
The sign in the restroom reminds employees to wash hands thoroughly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: contaminate - to make something dirty or impure by contact with something undesirable
  • synonym: sanitize - to make clean and free from bacteria, especially with chemicals
