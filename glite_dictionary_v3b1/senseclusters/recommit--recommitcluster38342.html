recommit

verb
to commit again
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkəˈmɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːkəˈmɪt / (BrE)
to decide again to dedicate oneself to a goal, promise, or course of action

Examples

To recommit to goals
After reflecting on his goals, he decided to recommit to his studies for the new semester.
The organization will recommit its resources to helping the local community this year.
She needed to recommit herself to healthy habits after a period of neglect.
After reflecting on his values, he decided to recommit to his volunteer work.
The team gathered to recommit themselves to their shared mission for the new year.
She promised to recommit to her fitness goals after recovering from her injury.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abandon - to give up or stop supporting something
  • synonym: renew - to make something valid or effective again
