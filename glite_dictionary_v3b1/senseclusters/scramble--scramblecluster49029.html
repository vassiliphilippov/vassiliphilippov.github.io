scramble

verb
move/climb awkwardly using hands and feet
5.9 2.7
Pronunciation: /ˈskræm.bəl/ (AmE), /ˈskræm.bəl/ (BrE)
to climb or move quickly and awkwardly, often using your hands as well as your feet

Examples

To scramble up rocks
She had to scramble over the rocks to reach the other side of the stream.
The children tried to scramble up the steep hillside before sunset.
We watched the goats scrambling along the narrow mountain path.
As soon as the gates opened, people began to scramble for the best seats.
She scrambled to collect her papers as the bell rang.
The hikers had to scramble over the rocks to reach the summit.

Related Terms

  • antonym: amble - to walk slowly or leisurely
  • synonym: clamber - to climb with effort or difficulty, using hands and feet
  • synonym: dash - to run or move quickly or suddenly
  • synonym: scurry - to move quickly with short, hurried movements
