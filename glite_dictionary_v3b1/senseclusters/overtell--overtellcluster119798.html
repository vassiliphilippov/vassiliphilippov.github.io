overtell

verb
to exaggerate story
7.1
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ˈtel/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ˈtel/ (BrE)
to exaggerate or embellish a story or event, making it seem more important or dramatic than it really is

Examples

To overtell an adventure
He tends to overtell his travel stories, making every trip sound like an epic adventure.
When recounting the event, she would overtell the details to impress her friends.
The author was criticized for overtelling minor incidents in his autobiography.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embellish - to add details to a story to make it more interesting or exciting
  • synonym: exaggerate - to make something seem larger, better, or worse than it really is
  • antonym: understate - to describe something as less important or serious than it really is
