semiclassical

adjective
partly classical style
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmiˈklæsɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈklæsɪkəl / (BrE)
partly classical in style, combining elements of classical tradition with modern or popular forms, especially in music, dance, or architecture

Examples

Semiclassical music style
The orchestra performed a semiclassical piece that mixed traditional melodies with jazz rhythms.
Her new building design is considered semiclassical because it blends ancient columns with modern materials.
They enjoyed a semiclassical concert that appealed to both classical and pop music fans.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: classical - relating to the traditional style of ancient Greece or Rome
  • degree lower: modern - relating to the present or recent times, not classical
