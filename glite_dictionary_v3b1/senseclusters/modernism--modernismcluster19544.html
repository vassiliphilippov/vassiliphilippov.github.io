modernism

noun
art/literature movement breaking from tradition
1.6 5.9
Pronunciation: /ˈmɑː.dər.nɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈmɒd.ən.ɪz.əm/ (BrE)
A style or movement in art, literature, and architecture that makes a deliberate break with traditional forms and conventions, especially those prevailing in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.

Examples

Modernism in art
Many artists in the early 20th century embraced modernism to challenge traditional artistic values.
The gallery features works from the key figures of modernism in painting and sculpture.
Virginia Woolf and James Joyce are often cited as leaders of literary modernism.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: postmodernism - artistic movement emerging as a reaction to and development from modernism
