orographic

adjective
mountain-related weather
9.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɔrəˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˌɒrəˈɡræfɪk / (BrE)
relating to the effects or influence of mountains, especially on weather or climate

Examples

Orographic rainfall
The orographic effect causes increased rainfall on the windward side of the mountains.
Scientists study orographic patterns to predict local climate changes in hilly regions.
The valley's unique weather is largely due to orographic influences from the surrounding peaks.

Related Terms

  • antonym: coastal - relating to or near a coast
  • synonym: mountainous - having many mountains or relating to mountains
