sodium hydride

noun
a flammable grey compound (NaH) used in chemistry
Pronunciation: / ˈsoʊdiəm haɪˌdraɪd / (AmE), / ˈsəʊdiəm hʌɪˌdrʌɪd / (BrE)
a flammable, grey, crystalline compound (NaH) consisting of sodium and hydrogen, used as a strong base in organic synthesis

Examples

Sodium hydride compound
Sodium hydride is commonly used as a powerful base in organic chemistry reactions.
Scientists handle sodium hydride carefully due to its reactivity with water and its flammability.
We stored the sodium hydride in a tightly sealed container to prevent any dangerous reactions.
