ignite

verb
start or keep a fire burning
Pronunciation: / ɪɡˈnaɪt / (AmE), / ɪɡˈnaɪt / (BrE)
to start or cause something to catch fire; to set on fire

Examples

To ignite a fire
It only took a small spark to ignite the pile of dry wood in the fireplace.
Make sure to ignite the stove carefully to avoid any accidents.
The caretaker will ignite the old furnace every morning during winter.
A single spark can ignite dry grass in seconds.
She forgot to turn off the stove, causing the oil to ignite.
The fireworks are engineered to ignite precisely at midnight.
A single match can easily ignite a pile of dry leaves.
Placing paper near the heater could ignite it accidentally.
The firework failed to ignite, disappointing the crowd.
