callused

verb
to develop thick skin
4.5
Pronunciation: / ˈkæl.əst / (AmE), / ˈkæl.juːst / (BrE)
to develop a hard, thickened area of skin as a result of repeated friction or pressure; to become emotionally hardened.

Examples

To callused over time
After weeks of rowing, his palms had callused and no longer hurt.
Years of playing guitar had callused her fingertips, making it easier to play for hours.
The farmworker's hands callused quickly from handling rough tools every day.
