versus

preposition
in opposition to
2.3
Pronunciation: / ˈvɜrsəs / (AmE), / ˈvɜːsəs / (BrE)
used to indicate opposition, contrast, or competition between two people, teams, or things

Examples

Team versus team
Tonight's basketball game is the Wildcats versus the Tigers.
The debate was about tradition versus innovation in education.
In court, it was Smith versus Johnson over the property dispute.
