solitary confinement

noun
isolation of a prisoner as punishment
Pronunciation: / ˌsɑːləˌteri kənˈfaɪnmənt / (AmE), / ˌsɒlɪˌteəri kənˈfaɪnmənt / (BrE)
the isolation of a prisoner from all other inmates, typically as a form of punishment or protective custody

Examples

Solitary confinement cell
After the riot, he was placed in solitary confinement for two weeks.
Many human rights groups campaign against the use of solitary confinement.
The prisoner described the psychological impact of solitary confinement during his testimony.

  • synonym: isolation - state of being separated from others
