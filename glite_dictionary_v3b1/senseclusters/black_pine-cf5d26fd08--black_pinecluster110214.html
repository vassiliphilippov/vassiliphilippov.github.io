black pine

noun
a large European timber pine tree
Pronunciation: / blæk paɪn / (AmE), / blæk paɪn / (BrE)
a large pine tree of southeastern Europe, with dark bark and two needles per cluster, valued for its timber

Examples

European black pine
Carpenters often prefer black pine for its strong, straight timber.
During our hike in Greece, we saw many ancient black pine trees towering overhead.
The dense forest of black pine created deep shade along the trail.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Austrian pine - another common name for the black pine (Pinus nigra)
