assail

verb
attack someone physically or emotionally
Pronunciation: / əˈseɪl / (AmE), / əˈseɪl / (BrE)
to attack someone or something forcefully, either physically, emotionally, or with words

Examples

To assail with fear
Anxiety can assail people unexpectedly in stressful situations.
The fortress was assailed by enemy troops at dawn.
The journalist was assailed by critics after publishing her article.
Several opinion pieces assail the company's environmental record.
She assailed her critics in a passionate speech.
The article assailed the government's latest economic policy.
She was assailed by critics after her controversial statement.
The castle has been assailed many times throughout its long history.
The soldiers prepared to assail the heavily fortified enemy position at dawn.
