Jack

noun
sailor; seaman (archaic usage)
4.1
Pronunciation: / dʒæk / (AmE), / dʒæk / (BrE)
a man who serves as a sailor, especially an ordinary seaman

Examples

A Jack sailor
In the bustling port, every Jack had a story to tell of the sea.
Old tales spoke of a brave Jack who saved his shipmates during a fierce storm.
The captain barked orders, and not a single Jack hesitated to obey.

Related Terms

  • antonym: landsman - a person unfamiliar with the sea or seafaring; not a sailor
  • synonym: sailor - a person who works as a member of the crew of a ship
