queue up

verb
form a line; wait in a queue
3.1 0.24/m
Pronunciation: / kjuÀê  åp / (AmE), / kjuÀê  åp / (BrE)
to form a line or wait in a queue, often for something or to enter a place

Examples

People queue up outside
At the cinema, customers queue up to buy popcorn and drinks.
People began to queue up outside the bakery before it opened.
We had to queue up for over an hour to get tickets to the concert.

Synonyms

  • queue
    3.4 1.33/m
    queue means people form or join a waiting line in general, while queue up focuses on actively getting into that line.
  • stand in line
    1.7 3.35/m
    stand in line means waiting in a queue for your turn, while queue up names the act of getting into that waiting line.
  • line up for
    2.1 1.06/m
    line up for means waiting in a queue to receive or do something, while queue up can mean joining any waiting line.
  • line up
    2.6 5.83/m
    line up often means arrange people or things in a row, while queue up specifically means forming or waiting in a queue.
