queue up

verb
form a line; wait in a queue
3.3
Pronunciation: / kjuː ʌp / (AmE), / kjuː ʌp / (BrE)
to form a line or wait in a queue, often for something or to enter a place

Examples

People queue up outside
At the cinema, customers queue up to buy popcorn and drinks.
People began to queue up outside the bakery before it opened.
We had to queue up for over an hour to get tickets to the concert.
