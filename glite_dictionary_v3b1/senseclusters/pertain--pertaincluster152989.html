to be related or relevant to something; to concern or apply to something

Examples

Information pertains to topic
His research pertains to climate change and its effects on agriculture.
Much of the legislation pertains only to businesses in the city.
The documents pertain to last year's financial records.
Her comments did not pertain to the issue under discussion.
The documents pertaining to the case were reviewed by the lawyer.
The new regulations pertain only to commercial properties, not residential homes.

Synonyms

  • appertain
    9.1 0.14/m
    appertain sounds formal and shows rightful belonging to something, while pertain simply means to be related or relevant.
  • concern
    2.1 45/m
    concern often means affect or involve people or things directly, while pertain means to be related or relevant in general.
  • have to do with
    2.7 29/m
    have to do with is an informal phrase for being connected with something, while pertain is more formal and used in writing.
  • bear on
    5.1 3.65/m
    bear on is a phrasal verb meaning have relevance or effect on a matter, while pertain simply means to be related.
  • refer
    3.0 14/m
    refer usually means point to or be about a topic directly, while pertain means to be related or relevant more generally.
  • relate
    2.2 33/m
    relate often means show or have a connection and can mean tell a story, while pertain focuses on being relevant.

Related Terms

  • synonym: belong - to be rightly placed in a specified position or environment
  • antonym: irrelevant - not connected with or relevant to something
  • synonym: refer - to mention or allude to something; to direct attention to
  • synonym: relate - to have a connection to something
