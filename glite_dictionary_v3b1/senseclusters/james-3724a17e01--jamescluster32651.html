James

proper noun
King of England, Scotland, and Ireland (1566–1625)
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒeɪmz / (AmE), / ˈdʒeɪmz / (BrE)
(1566–1625) The first Stuart king of England and Ireland (as James I, 1603–1625) and of Scotland (as James VI, 1567–1625); the son of Mary, Queen of Scots, known for commissioning the King James Bible and asserting the divine right of kings.

Examples

King James I
James united the crowns of England and Scotland in 1603, marking the start of the Stuart dynasty.
Parliament often clashed with James over his views on royal authority and the divine right of kings.
The King James Bible, commissioned by James I, remains one of the most influential English translations.

  • degree lower: Elizabeth I - the predecessor Queen of England whom James succeeded
