reflexiveness

noun
property of reflexive pronouns relating to their subject
9.9
Pronunciation: / rɪˈflɛksɪvnəs / (AmE), / rɪˈflɛksɪvnɪs / (BrE)
the grammatical property or relation in which a reflexive pronoun refers back to its own subject or antecedent

Examples

Grammatical reflexiveness
Accurate use of reflexiveness is essential when teaching English grammar.
Linguists study reflexiveness to understand how pronouns relate to their antecedents in different languages.
The reflexiveness of the pronoun 'herself' links it to the subject of the sentence.
