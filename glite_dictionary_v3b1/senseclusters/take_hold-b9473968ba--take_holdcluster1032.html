take hold

verb
grasp or seize something deliberately
Pronunciation: / teɪk hoʊld / (AmE), / teɪk həʊld / (BrE)
to grasp or seize something deliberately with your hands

Examples

Take hold of the rope
He reached out to take hold of the rope before he slipped.
She quickly took hold of her bag when the phone rang.
The nurse gently took hold of the patient's arm to examine it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grab - to take or grasp something suddenly
  • antonym: release - to let go of something held or seized
  • synonym: seize - to take hold of suddenly and forcibly
