reconstitution

noun
forming again
7.4
Pronunciation: /riː.kən.stɪ.ˈtuː.ʃən/ /; / /riː.kɑːn.stɪ.ˈtuː.ʃən/ (AmE), /riː.kɒn.stɪ.ˈtjuː.ʃən/ /; / /riː.kən.stɪ.ˈtjuː.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act of forming, organizing, or establishing something again, especially after it has been changed or dissolved

Examples

Reconstitution of the committee
The reconstitution of the board brought in several new members with fresh ideas.
After the war, the country focused on the reconstitution of its government and institutions.
The reconstitution of the committee was necessary after many members resigned.

  • synonym: restoration - the act of bringing something back to its original state
