rattling

noun
series of short, loud sounds
Pronunciation: / ˈræt̬.lɪŋ /; / ˈræt̬lɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈræt.lɪŋ / (BrE)
a rapid series of short, sharp, or loud noises

Examples

A rattling noise
A faint rattling could be heard through the stethoscope as she breathed.
The doctor detected a rattling in the patient's lungs during the examination.
The nurse listened carefully for any sign of rattling when monitoring his recovery.
The rattling of dishes in the kitchen woke me up early this morning.
We could hear the rattling of the loose window during the storm.
The old car made a constant rattling as it drove down the gravel road.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rattle - a rapid succession of short, sharp noises
  • antonym: silence - the absence of sound or noise
