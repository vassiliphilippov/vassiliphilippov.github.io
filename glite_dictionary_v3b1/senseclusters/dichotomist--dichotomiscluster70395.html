dichotomist

noun
person who splits into two
9.5
Pronunciation: / daɪˈkɑːtəmɪst / (AmE), / daɪˈkɒtəmɪst / (BrE)
a person who divides things into two distinct and often opposing groups or categories

Examples

A strict dichotomist
As a dichotomist, she often saw issues as either right or wrong with no middle ground.
The dichotomist approach can oversimplify complex social problems.
He was labeled a dichotomist because he divided people into friends and enemies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: categorizer - a person who classifies things into categories
  • antonym: pluralist - a person who recognizes or accepts many different groups or categories
