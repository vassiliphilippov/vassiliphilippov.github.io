involuntary

adjective
done without conscious control
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnˈvɑːlənˌtɛri /; / ɪnˈvɑːlənˌtɛri / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɒl.ən.tər.i /; / ɪnˈvɒləntri / (BrE)
done without conscious control or against one's will

Examples

Involuntary muscle twitch
His leg gave an involuntary twitch as he fell asleep.
Sneezing is often an involuntary reaction to dust or allergens.
The doctor explained the difference between involuntary and voluntary muscles.
Her involuntary gasp surprised everyone in the silent auditorium.
His involuntary laughter broke the tension in the room.
Muscle spasms are often involuntary and cannot be easily prevented.

Related Terms

  • antonym: voluntary - done by one's own free will or with conscious control
