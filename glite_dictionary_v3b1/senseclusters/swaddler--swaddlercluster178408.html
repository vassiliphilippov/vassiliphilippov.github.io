swaddler

noun
person or device for wrapping babies
Pronunciation: / ˈswɑːdlər / (AmE), / ˈswɒdlə / (BrE)
a person or device that wraps a baby tightly in cloths or blankets to restrict movement and provide comfort

Examples

A swaddler for babies
The nurse acted as a swaddler for the newborns in the hospital.
Many parents use a swaddler to help their babies sleep more soundly at night.
A modern swaddler can make wrapping a baby quick and easy for new parents.
