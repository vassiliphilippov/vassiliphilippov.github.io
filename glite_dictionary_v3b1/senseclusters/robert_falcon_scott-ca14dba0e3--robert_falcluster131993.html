Robert Falcon Scott

proper noun
English Antarctic explorer (1868–1912)
Pronunciation: / ˈrɑbərt ˈfælkən skɑt / (AmE), / ˈɹɒbət ˈfɔːlkən skɒt / (BrE)
(1868–1912) English explorer who led expeditions to Antarctica, reaching the South Pole just after Amundsen, and died with his team on the return journey.

Examples

Robert Falcon Scott expedition
Robert Falcon Scott is remembered for his tragic expedition to the South Pole.
Many books have been written about the Antarctic adventures of Robert Falcon Scott.
The bravery of Robert Falcon Scott and his crew continues to inspire explorers today.
