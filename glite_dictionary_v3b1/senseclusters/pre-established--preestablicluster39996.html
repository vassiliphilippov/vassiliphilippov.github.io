pre-established

adjective
decided in advance
Pronunciation: / ˌpriː ɪˈstæblɪʃt / (AmE), / ˌpriː ɪˈstæblɪʃt / (BrE)
decided, arranged, or set up before a particular time or event

Examples

A pre-established plan
The team worked according to a pre-established set of guidelines for the project.
Access to the building is only allowed at pre-established times during the week.
The contract includes several pre-established conditions that both parties must follow.

Related Terms

  • synonym: predetermined - decided or established in advance
  • antonym: spontaneous - done without planning or prearrangement
