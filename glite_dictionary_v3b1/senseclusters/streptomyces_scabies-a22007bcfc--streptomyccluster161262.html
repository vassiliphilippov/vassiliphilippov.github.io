Streptomyces scabies

noun
bacterium causing potato scab
Pronunciation: / stɹɛpˈtoʊməs ˈskeɪbiːz / (AmE), / strɛpˈtɒmɪiːz ˈskeɪbiːz / (BrE)
a species of bacterium that causes common scab disease in potatoes, resulting in brownish, corky tissue on tubers

Examples

Streptomyces scabies bacterium
Streptomyces scabies is the primary cause of common scab in potatoes worldwide.
Researchers are developing new methods to detect and control Streptomyces scabies in agricultural soils.
The presence of Streptomyces scabies can significantly reduce the quality of potato crops.
