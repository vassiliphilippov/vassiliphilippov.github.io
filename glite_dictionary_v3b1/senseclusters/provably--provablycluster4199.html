provably

adverb
in a way that can be proven
6.8 0.23/m
Pronunciation: / Ààpro äv…ôbli / (AmE), / ÀàpruÀêv…ôbli / (BrE)
In a manner that can be proven to be true; so as to be demonstrable by evidence or argument.

Examples

To be provably true
It is provably false that the earth is flat, given all the scientific evidence.
She made a provably accurate statement about the company's profits.
Their theory is not provably correct, since there is currently no supporting data.

Synonyms

  • demonstrably
    5.5 1.59/m
    demonstrably is used when something is clearly shown with evidence or facts, while provably is used when it is shown by formal proof or argument.
  • verifiably
    5.2 0.12/m
    verifiably is used when something can be checked or tested by evidence or measurement, while provably is used when it is shown by formal proof.

Related Terms

  • synonym: demonstrably - in a way that can be demonstrated or shown to be true
