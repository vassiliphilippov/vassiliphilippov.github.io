physical pendulum

noun
rigid body oscillating about a horizontal axis
8.4 6.2
Pronunciation: /ˈfɪz.ɪ.kəl ˈpen.dʒə.ləm/ /; / /ˈfɪz.ɪ.kəl ˈpɪn.dʒə.ləm/ (AmE), /ˈfɪz.ɪ.kəl ˈpen.dʒə.ləm/ /; / /ˈfɪz.ɪ.kəl ˈpɪn.dʒə.ləm/ (BrE)
a pendulum consisting of a rigid body free to rotate about a horizontal axis not passing through its center of mass

Examples

A physical pendulum swings
Engineers often use a physical pendulum to measure the distribution of mass in irregular objects.
The motion of a physical pendulum can be analyzed to determine its moment of inertia.
Unlike a simple point mass, a physical pendulum swings with a period affected by its shape.

Related Terms

  • antonym: simple pendulum - an ideal pendulum consisting of a point mass suspended from a string or rod
