ideomotion

noun
unconscious movement
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪdiəˈmoʊʃən / (AmE), / ˌaɪdiəˈməʊʃən / (BrE)
an unconscious movement of the body produced by mental activity or suggestion rather than by conscious intention

Examples

Ideomotion effect
The magician explained that the pendulum's swing was due to ideomotion rather than supernatural forces.
During the experiment, subtle ideomotion caused the subject's hand to move without conscious effort.
Scientists study ideomotion to understand how thoughts can influence physical actions unconsciously.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reflex - an automatic, involuntary movement in response to a stimulus
  • antonym: voluntary movement - a movement made by conscious intention
