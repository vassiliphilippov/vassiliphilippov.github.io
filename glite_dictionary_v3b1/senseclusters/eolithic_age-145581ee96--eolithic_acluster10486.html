Eolithic Age

noun
early phase of the Stone Age
Pronunciation: / ˌiː.əˈlɪθ.ɪk eɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌiː.əˈlɪθ.ɪk eɪdʒ / (BrE)
the earliest part of the Stone Age, characterized by the first traces of human tools and culture

Examples

The Eolithic Age
Debates over the Eolithic Age illustrate the challenges in interpreting ancient evidence.
Many archaeologists no longer recognize the Eolithic Age as a distinct phase of prehistory.
Supposed stone tools from the Eolithic Age were later shown to be naturally formed rocks.
