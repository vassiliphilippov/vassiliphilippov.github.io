microplankton

noun
tiny aquatic plankton
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪkroʊˈplæŋktən / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəʊˈplæŋktən / (BrE)
very small plankton, typically between 20 and 200 micrometers in size, found in aquatic environments

Examples

Small microplankton species
Researchers observed a variety of microplankton species under the microscope.
The abundance of microplankton in the ocean can indicate water quality and nutrient levels.
Fish larvae often feed on microplankton during their early stages of development.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: macroplankton - plankton larger than microplankton, usually 200–2000 micrometers in size
  • degree lower: nanoplankton - plankton smaller than microplankton, usually 2–20 micrometers in size
