paraquat poisoning

noun
poisoning from paraquat ingestion or exposure
8.7
Pronunciation: /ˈpær.ə.kwɑːt ˈpɔɪ.zən.ɪŋ/ /; / /ˈpær.ə.kwɔːt ˈpɔɪ.zən.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈpær.ə.kwɒt ˈpɔɪ.zən.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
Poisoning caused by ingestion or exposure to paraquat, characterized by progressive damage to the esophagus, liver, and kidneys.

Examples

Paraquat poisoning symptoms
A single swallow of paraquat poisoning can cause fatal organ failure if left untreated.
Early symptoms of paraquat poisoning include pain and swelling in the mouth and throat.
The farmer was hospitalized after suffering from paraquat poisoning due to accidental ingestion.
