allowance account

noun
reserve fund in company accounts
2.0 6.8
Pronunciation: / əˈlaʊəns əˌkaʊnt / (AmE), / əˈlaʊəns əˌkaʊnt / (BrE)
an account in which a company sets aside funds as a reserve to cover potential future changes in the value of its assets, typically recorded as a charge against profits

Examples

An allowance account reserve
A well-maintained allowance account helps a business prepare for unexpected asset losses.
Auditors reviewed the allowance account to ensure accuracy in the firm's financial statements.
The company increased its allowance account to cover a potential decline in inventory value.
