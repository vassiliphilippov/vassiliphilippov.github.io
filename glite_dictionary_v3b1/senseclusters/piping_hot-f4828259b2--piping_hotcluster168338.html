piping hot

adjective
extremely hot (food or drink)
Pronunciation: / ˈpaɪpɪŋ hɑt / (AmE), / ˈpaɪpɪŋ hɒt / (BrE)
(of food or drink) extremely hot, often so hot that it is steaming or just cooked; freshly made and very warm.

Examples

Piping hot soup
Make sure the soup is piping hot before serving it to the guests.
She prefers her oatmeal piping hot, with a little honey on top.
The waiter brought out the piping hot pizza straight from the oven.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lukewarm - (of liquid or food) moderately warm
  • synonym: scalding - extremely hot, often enough to burn
