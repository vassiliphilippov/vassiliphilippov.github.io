effeteness

noun
weakness or overrefinement
Pronunciation: / ɪˈfiːtnəs / (AmE), / ɪˈfiːtnəs / (BrE)
the quality or state of being weak, exhausted, or no longer strong or effective

Examples

The effeteness of aristocracy
The effeteness of the aristocracy was often criticized by social reformers.
Some believed that cultural effeteness led to the decline of the empire.
His writing style was marked by a certain effeteness that divided critics.
The effeteness of the old empire was evident in its inability to defend its borders.
Years of overuse led to the effeteness of the farmland, making it unfit for crops.
Critics argued that the institution's effeteness was the reason for its decline in influence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sterility - the state of being unable to produce or reproduce
  • antonym: vigor - physical or mental energy and strength
  • antonym: vitality - the state of being strong and full of energy
  • synonym: weakness - the state or quality of lacking strength
