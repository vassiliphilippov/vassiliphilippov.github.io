speak up

verb
express one's opinion openly
3.1 6.54/m
Pronunciation: / spiÀêk  åp / (AmE), / spiÀêk  åp / (BrE)
to express your opinion openly and assertively, especially when it might be difficult

Examples

To speak up for yourself
If you disagree with the plan, you should speak up during the discussion.
She finally spoke up about the unfair treatment at work.
We encourage all employees to speak up if they notice any problems.
If you disagree with the plan, you should speak up at the meeting.
In class, students are encouraged to speak up and share their ideas.
She finally found the courage to speak up about the unfair treatment.

Synonyms

  • speak out
    3.4 11/m
    speak out is to express opinions openly and without fear in public, while speak up adds an assertive tone when something is wrong.
  • speak one's mind
    3.5 0.89/m
    speak one's mind means saying your honest thoughts directly, while speak up emphasizes standing up for a view in hard situations.
  • assert
    3.9 28/m
    assert means stating something firmly and confidently, often formally, while speak up focuses on speaking openly and assertively in conversation.
  • voice
    3.2 14/m
    voice is to express an opinion publicly or in writing, while speak up stresses saying it aloud and more forcefully.
  • speak openly
    3.4 0.45/m
    speak openly means talking in a candid, honest way, while speak up implies doing so assertively and when it may be hard.
