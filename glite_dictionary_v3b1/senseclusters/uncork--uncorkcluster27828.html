uncork

verb
release or let out (figurative)
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈkɔrk / (AmE), / ʌnˈkɔːk / (BrE)
to release or let out, especially something previously restrained, such as feelings or emotions

Examples

To uncork anger
He tried not to uncork his frustration during the meeting, but eventually he spoke up.
Sometimes, a good laugh uncorks all the tension you've been holding inside.
The coach uncorked a passionate speech that inspired the whole team.

Related Terms

  • synonym: release - to set free or allow to escape or move freely
