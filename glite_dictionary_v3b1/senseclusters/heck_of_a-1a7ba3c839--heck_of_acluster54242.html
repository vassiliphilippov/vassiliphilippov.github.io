heck of a

adjective
very impressive
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛk əv ə / (AmE), / ˈhɛk əv ə / (BrE)
used informally to emphasize that something is very impressive, extreme, or remarkable

Examples

A heck of a concert
That was a heck of a concert; the band played for three hours straight.
She did a heck of a job organizing the event despite the short notice.
Climbing that mountain was a heck of a challenge, but we made it to the top.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hell of a - used informally to strongly emphasize how great or extreme something is
  • antonym: ordinary - not special or impressive
