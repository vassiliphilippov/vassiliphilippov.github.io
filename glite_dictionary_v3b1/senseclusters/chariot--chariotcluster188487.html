chariot

noun
ancient horse-drawn ceremonial carriage
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɛriət /; / ˈtʃæriət / (AmE), / ˈtʃæriət / (BrE)
a light, horse-drawn vehicle with two or four wheels, used in ancient times for war, racing, or ceremonies

Examples

Ancient chariot race
He drove his chariot skillfully during the ancient festival parade.
The museum displayed a restored chariot once used in Roman processions.
The victorious general entered the city in a golden chariot.
In ancient Egypt, warriors fought from fast-moving chariots during battles.
Museum exhibits include an authentic Greek chariot from the fifth century BC.
The victorious general rode his chariot through the cheering crowds in Rome.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carriage - a four-wheeled horse-drawn passenger vehicle
  • synonym: cart - a simple vehicle with two or four wheels pulled by animals or people
