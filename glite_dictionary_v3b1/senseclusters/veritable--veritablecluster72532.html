veritable

adjective
real or genuine; used for emphasis
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛrɪtəbl /; / ˈvɛrɪtəbəl / (AmE), / ˈvɛrɪtəbl /; / ˈvɛrɪtəbl̩ / (BrE)
used to emphasize that something is truly or really what it is said to be

Examples

A veritable sea
During the festival, the streets became a veritable sea of colors and music.
He was a veritable expert in the field, with decades of experience.
The garden was a veritable paradise, filled with exotic flowers.
After the rain, the garden became a veritable jungle of weeds overnight.
She is a veritable genius when it comes to solving complex problems.
The museum is a veritable treasure trove of ancient artifacts.
