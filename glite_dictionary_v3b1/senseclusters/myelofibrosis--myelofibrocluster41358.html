myelofibrosis

noun
bone marrow fibrosis disorder
2.0
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪəloʊfaɪˈbroʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌmaɪələʊfaɪˈbrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A serious disorder in which the bone marrow becomes scarred, leading to a decrease in its ability to produce blood cells.

Examples

Myelofibrosis disease
As myelofibrosis progresses, patients often develop severe anemia and an enlarged spleen.
The patient was diagnosed with myelofibrosis after a bone marrow biopsy showed extensive scarring.
Treatment options for myelofibrosis may include medications to manage symptoms or a stem cell transplant.
