wild potato vine

noun
tropical plant with large starchy root
6.3
Pronunciation: / waɪld pəˈteɪtoʊ vaɪn / (AmE), / waɪld pəˈteɪtəʊ vaɪn / (BrE)
A tropical American trailing or climbing perennial plant, known for its large, starchy root; sometimes considered an ancestor of the sweet potato.

Botanists studied the wild potato vine to learn more about the origins of the sweet potato.
