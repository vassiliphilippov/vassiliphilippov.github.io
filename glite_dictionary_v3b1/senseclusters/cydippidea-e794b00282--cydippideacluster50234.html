Cydippidea

noun
order of ctenophores with long tentacles
Pronunciation: / sɪˌdɪpɪˈdiːə / (AmE), / sɪˌdɪpɪˈdiːə / (BrE)
An order of ctenophores characterized by having two long, pinnate tentacles.

Examples

Order Cydippidea species
Researchers discovered several new species belonging to the order Cydippidea in the deep ocean.
The Cydippidea are notable for their transparent, globular bodies and two long, branching tentacles.
Unlike other ctenophores, Cydippidea typically use their tentacles to capture prey in the water column.
