retrospective

adjective
relating to the past
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛtroʊˈspɛktɪv / (AmE), / ˌrɛtrəˈspɛktɪv / (BrE)
looking back on, or dealing with past events or situations

Examples

Retrospective analysis
Her explanation for quitting seemed like retrospective justification rather than her true motivation at the time.
Medical studies often use retrospective data to identify patterns and outcomes from previous cases.
The report included a retrospective analysis of the company's performance over the past decade.

Related Terms

  • antonym: prospective - relating to or likely to happen in the future
