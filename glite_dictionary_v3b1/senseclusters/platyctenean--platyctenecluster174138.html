platyctenean

noun
a type of ribbon-like ctenophore
Pronunciation: / ˌplætɪkˈtiniən / (AmE), / ˌplætɪkˈtiːniən / (BrE)
A member of an order (Platyctenea) of ctenophores characterized by long tentacles and a flattened, often ribbon-like body.

Examples

A platyctenean species
A marine biologist discovered a new species of platyctenean during the deep-sea expedition.
Many platycteneans inhabit shallow coastal waters, where their long tentacles help capture prey.
The platyctenean glided silently through the water, its flattened body undulating with the current.
