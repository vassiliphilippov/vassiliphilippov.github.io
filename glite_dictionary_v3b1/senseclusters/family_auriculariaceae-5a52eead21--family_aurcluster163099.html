family Auriculariaceae

noun
family of fungi with gelatinous bodies
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfæməli ɔːˌrɪkjəˌlɛriˈeɪsiː / (AmE), / ˈfæmɪli ɔːˌrɪkjʊləˈrɪəsaɪ / (BrE)
A family of fungi characterized by gelatinous fruiting bodies, belonging to the order Auriculariales.

Examples

Family Auriculariaceae fungi
Fungi in the family Auriculariaceae are valued for their gelatinous texture in Asian cuisine.
Mycologists study the family Auriculariaceae to understand the diversity of jelly fungi.
The family Auriculariaceae includes mushrooms commonly known as wood ear fungi.
