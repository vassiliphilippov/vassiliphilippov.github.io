subjectification

noun
making subordinate
9.9
Pronunciation: / səbˌdʒɛktɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / səbˌdʒɛktɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making a person or group subject to the authority or control of another

Examples

Subjectification of people
The subjectification of local communities led to the loss of their traditional rights.
Historians debate the long-term effects of subjectification during colonial rule.
The new policy was criticized for encouraging the subjectification of minority groups.

Related Terms

  • antonym: liberation - the act of setting someone free from control or oppression
  • synonym: subjugation - the act of bringing someone or something under control
