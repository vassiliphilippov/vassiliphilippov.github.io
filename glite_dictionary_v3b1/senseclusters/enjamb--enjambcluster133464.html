enjamb

verb
to step over
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈdʒæm/ /; / /en.ˈdʒæm/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈdʒæm/ /; / /en.ˈdʒæm/ (BrE)
(archaic) to step or stride over something, especially by crossing with a long step

Examples

To enjamb a barrier
The knight would enjamb the fallen log as he hurried through the forest.
In old tales, heroes often enjamb streams and ditches on their journeys.
She tried to enjamb the narrow gap between the rocks with a single leap.
