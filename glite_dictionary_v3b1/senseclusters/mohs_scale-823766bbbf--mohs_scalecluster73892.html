Mohs scale

noun
scale for mineral hardness, 1–10
6.0
Pronunciation: / moʊz skeɪl / (AmE), / məʊz skeɪl / (BrE)
a scale for classifying minerals based on their ability to scratch one another, ranging from talc (1, the softest) to diamond (10, the hardest)

Examples

The Mohs scale hardness
Diamond registers as a 10 on the Mohs scale, making it the hardest naturally occurring mineral.
Talc, which is rated 1 on the Mohs scale, can be easily scratched with a fingernail.
The Mohs scale is commonly used to compare the hardness of different minerals in geology.
