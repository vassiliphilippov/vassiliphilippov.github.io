mentor

verb
act as a trusted adviser or guide
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛn.tɔɹ / (AmE), / ˈmɛn.tɔː / (BrE)
to act as a trusted adviser or guide, especially in a professional or educational context

Examples

To mentor a student
After joining the company, she was mentored by a senior engineer during her first year.
He has been mentoring young entrepreneurs to help them launch their own businesses.
The university offers programs where alumni mentor current students in their chosen fields.

Related Terms

  • synonym: advise - to offer suggestions about the best course of action
  • synonym: coach - to train or give instruction to someone in a particular area
  • antonym: follow - to accept the guidance or leadership of
