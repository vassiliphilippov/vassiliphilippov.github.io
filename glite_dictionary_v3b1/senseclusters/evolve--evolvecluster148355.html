evolve

verb
to develop gradually
5.8 3.3
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈvɑːlv/ /; / /iː.ˈvɑːlv/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈvɒlv/ (BrE)
to develop or change gradually over time, often becoming more advanced or complex

Examples

To evolve over time
Her ideas on education evolved as she gained more experience in teaching.
Over centuries, the language evolved into its modern form.
Scientists hope the species will evolve new traits to adapt to climate change.
Over the years, she evolved a deep sense of empathy for others.
The company evolved new strategies to adapt to changing markets.
Through constant practice, he evolved impressive musical talents.
Her ideas about education have continued to evolve over the years.
Species can evolve new characteristics to help them survive in changing environments.
Technology tends to evolve rapidly, bringing constant innovation to our daily lives.
