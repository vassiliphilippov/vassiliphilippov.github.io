mantid

noun
predatory insect (praying mantis)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmæn.tɪd / (AmE), / ˈmæn.tɪd / (BrE)
A predatory insect of warm regions with a long body and large eyes, known for holding up its forelimbs as if in prayer.

Examples

A mantid insect
A praying mantid can rotate its head to scan for both predators and potential meals.
Researchers studied the hunting strategies of a common mantid species found in tropical forests.
The mantid remained perfectly still, blending with the leaves as it waited for its prey.
