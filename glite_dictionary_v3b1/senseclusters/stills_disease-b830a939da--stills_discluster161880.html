Still's disease

noun
juvenile rheumatoid arthritis affecting children
7.8
Pronunciation: /stɪlz dɪ.ˈziːz/ /; / /stɪlz də.ˈziːz/ (AmE), /stɪlz dɪ.ˈziːz/ /; / /stɪlz də.ˈziːz/ (BrE)
A rare form of rheumatoid arthritis that primarily affects children, causing inflammation of large joints and potentially slowed bone growth.

Examples

Childhood Still's disease
Because Still's disease is rare, it is sometimes difficult for doctors to recognize immediately.
Early treatment can help manage the symptoms of Still's disease and improve quality of life.
The young patient was diagnosed with Still's disease after weeks of joint pain and fever.

Related Terms

  • synonym: juvenile idiopathic arthritis - a broader term for various forms of arthritis affecting children
  • synonym: rheumatoid arthritis - a chronic inflammatory disorder affecting joints, often in adults
