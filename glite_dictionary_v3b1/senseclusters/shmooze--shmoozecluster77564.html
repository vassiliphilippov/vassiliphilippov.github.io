shmooze

verb
talk casually in a friendly way
Pronunciation: / ʃmuːz / (AmE), / ʃmuːz / (BrE)
to engage in casual or friendly conversation, often to gain an advantage or make a social connection

Examples

To shmooze with guests
He spent the entire evening shmoozing with local politicians at the fundraiser.
It's not enough to do good work—you also have to shmooze a bit to get noticed.
She loves to shmooze with her colleagues at conferences to build new contacts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chat - to talk in a friendly and informal way
  • degree higher: gossip - to talk about other people in a casual way, often spreading rumors
