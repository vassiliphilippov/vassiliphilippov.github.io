stuntman

noun
performer of dangerous acts
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈstʌntˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈstʌntˌmæn /; / ˈstʌntˌman / (BrE)
a person who performs dangerous or difficult actions, especially in movies, shows, or to attract attention

Examples

The stuntman jumps
The stuntman amazed the audience by jumping through a ring of fire.
During the parade, a stuntman balanced on a moving car to entertain the crowd.
It takes years of training to become a professional stuntman in live performances.
The stuntman climbed the building to protest the new law and attract media coverage.
As a well-known stuntman, he often organizes dramatic events to highlight social issues.
The mayor criticized the stuntman for risking public safety just to get attention.
The stuntman trained for months before attempting the dangerous car chase scene.
During the explosion, a skilled stuntman took the place of the lead actor.
She admired the bravery of the stuntman who leaped from the burning building.
