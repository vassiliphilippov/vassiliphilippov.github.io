nonprobability

noun
nonrandom sample
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˌprɑbəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌprɒbəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
a sample or sampling method in which participants are not chosen randomly, often leading to potential bias

Examples

A nonprobability sample
Researchers used a nonprobability sample to quickly gather opinions from people at the event.
A nonprobability approach can introduce bias because not everyone has an equal chance of being selected.
The study's findings were limited by its reliance on nonprobability sampling methods.

Related Terms

  • antonym: probability - a sample or method where participants are chosen randomly
  • synonym: quota sampling - a nonrandom sampling method where quotas are set for subgroups
