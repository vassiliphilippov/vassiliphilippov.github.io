lead

noun
thin metal strip for spacing lines in printing
Pronunciation: / lɛd / (AmE), / lɛd / (BrE)
A thin strip of metal used to separate lines of type in traditional printing.

Examples

A lead strip
Before digital typesetting, leads were essential for spacing out printed text.
He adjusted the lead to create a larger gap between the paragraphs.
The printer inserted a lead between the lines to improve readability.

Related Terms

  • synonym: slug - piece of metal used in typesetting for spacing
