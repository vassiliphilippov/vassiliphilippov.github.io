dissolve

verb
end (of relationship or arrangement)
Pronunciation: / dɪˈzɑlv / (AmE), / dɪˈzɒlv / (BrE)
to officially or formally end or break up an organization, agreement, or relationship

Examples

To dissolve a company
After years of conflict, their partnership finally dissolved.
The committee dissolved after completing its final report.
With trust broken, the alliance slowly dissolved over time.
If they violate the agreement, either party can dissolve the contract.
New management chose to dissolve the partnership and restructure the business.
The government decided to dissolve the committee after months of controversy.
After years of declining membership, the association decided to dissolve in 2020.
Amidst constant disagreements, the committee eventually dissolved and ceased all activity.
The company may dissolve its research division if funding does not improve.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abolish - to formally put an end to a system or institution
  • synonym: break up - to come to an end or terminate, especially a relationship or group
  • synonym: disband - (usually of an organization) to break up or cease to exist
  • synonym: dissolution - the formal closing down or dismissal of an assembly, partnership, or official body
  • antonym: establish - to set up or found formally (opposite action)
  • antonym: form - (of a group or organization) to come together or be established
