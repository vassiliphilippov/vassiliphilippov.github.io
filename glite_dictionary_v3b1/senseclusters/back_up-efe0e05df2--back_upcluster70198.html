(of a system, pipe, or flow) to become blocked or obstructed, causing accumulation or stoppage; to cause such a blockage.

Examples

Pipes back up
Paper started to back up in the printer when it jammed.
The sink will back up if you put grease down the drain.
Traffic tends to back up during rush hour on this highway.

Synonyms

    3.7 0.45/m
    clog up makes a pipe or drain blocked so water cannot pass, while back up means the flow stops and collects behind the blockage.
  • clog
    3.2 1.62/m
    clog often names a simple block that slows movement in pipes or machines, while back up describes the resulting stoppage and build up.
  • congest
    5.3 0.04/m
    congest usually describes crowded roads or noses that block movement, while back up refers to systems or pipes stopping and piling up.
  • choke off
    6.4 0.36/m
    choke off cuts or stops a supply or flow deliberately, while back up describes a physical blockage that causes flow to pile up.
  • choke
    3.7 9.13/m
