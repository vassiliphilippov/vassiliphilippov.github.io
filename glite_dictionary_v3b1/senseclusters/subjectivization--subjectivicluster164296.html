subjectivization

noun
making subjective
9.8
Pronunciation: /səb.dʒek.tɪ.və.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /səb.dʒek.tɪ.vɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /səb.dʒek.tɪ.və.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /səb.dʒek.tɪ.vɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of making something subjective or interpreting it from a personal or individual perspective

Examples

Subjectivization of criticism
The subjectivization of art criticism means that personal taste plays a larger role in evaluations.
Modern philosophy often discusses the subjectivization of knowledge and experience.
Some argue that the subjectivization of news reporting can lead to biased perspectives.
