warp

noun
lengthwise threads on a loom
8.1 1.23/m
Pronunciation: / w…îrp / (AmE), / w…îÀêp / (BrE)
the set of threads running lengthwise in a woven fabric, held in tension on a loom and crossed by the weft (woof) during weaving

Examples

Warp threads
If the warp threads are not tight enough, the fabric may lose its shape.
The artisan carefully arranged the warp on the loom before starting to weave.
The silk warp contrasted beautifully with the colored wool weft.

Related Terms

  • antonym: weft - the threads that are woven across the warp in a fabric
