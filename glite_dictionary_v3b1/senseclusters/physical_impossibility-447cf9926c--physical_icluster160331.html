physical impossibility

noun
something that can't happen due to physical laws
Pronunciation: / ˌfɪzɪkəl ɪmˌpɑsəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌfɪzɪkəl ɪmˌpɒsɪˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
A situation or event that cannot occur because it contradicts the laws of physics or nature.

Examples

A physical impossibility
Building a perpetual motion machine is a physical impossibility according to current physics.
Some science fiction ideas remain physical impossibilities, regardless of technological advances.
Traveling faster than the speed of light is considered a physical impossibility.
