extra dividend

noun
additional dividend beyond regular payment
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈɛkstrə dɪˈvɪdɛnd / (AmE), / ˈɛkstrə dɪˈvɪdənd / (BrE)
A dividend paid by a company in addition to its regular, scheduled dividend, often due to unexpected profits or surplus.

Examples

An extra dividend payment
An extra dividend is sometimes issued to mark a particularly successful financial year.
Shareholders were delighted to receive an extra dividend on top of their usual payment.
The company announced an extra dividend after reporting higher-than-expected earnings this quarter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dividend - a sum of money paid regularly by a company to its shareholders
