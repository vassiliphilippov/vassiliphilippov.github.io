bearer

noun
person holding a negotiable financial document
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛrər / (AmE), / ˈbeərə / (BrE)
a person in possession of a check, note, bond, or document of title that is payable to whoever holds it or is endorsed to them.

Examples

Bearer of a check
He lost a bond marked ‘to bearer’ and now anyone holding it can claim its value.
The check is payable to bearer, so anyone presenting it can cash it.
‘Pay to bearer’ is a common phrase on negotiable instruments.

Related Terms

  • antonym: payee - the person to whom money is paid or is to be paid, especially by a check or note
