roller-coasterish

adjective
having dramatic ups and downs
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊlərˌkoʊstərɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈrəʊləˌkəʊstərɪʃ / (BrE)
(of a story or experience) characterized by dramatic changes, especially alternating periods of excitement and calm, or success and failure.

Examples

A roller-coasterish story
Critics praised the novel for its roller-coasterish twists and emotional highs and lows.
Her life took a roller-coasterish turn after moving to a new city.
The film's roller-coasterish plot kept audiences on the edge of their seats.
