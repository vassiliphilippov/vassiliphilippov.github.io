Artemisia cana

noun
a silvery-leaved sagebrush of western US
Pronunciation: / ɑːrtəˈmɪziə ˈkænə / (AmE), / ɑːtɪˈmɪzɪə ˈkɑːnə / (BrE)
A species of sagebrush native to the western United States, characterized by silvery leaves and widely used as browse and shelter for wildlife.

Examples

Artemisia cana plant
Artemisia cana provides important winter food for deer and antelope in the region.
Many conservationists recommend planting Artemisia cana to restore native habitats.
The silvery appearance of Artemisia cana makes it easy to identify among other shrubs.
