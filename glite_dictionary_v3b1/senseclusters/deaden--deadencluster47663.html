deaden

verb
make less intense or lively; dull
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛdən / (AmE), / ˈdɛdən / (BrE)
to make something less strong, intense, lively, or active; to dull, weaken, or reduce the force, effect, or feeling of something

Examples

To deaden the pain
Painkillers can deaden the sensation in your mouth during dental treatments.
Repeated disappointment can deaden a person's enthusiasm over time.
The thick curtains helped to deaden the noise from the street.
Adding a drag chute helped deaden the aircraft's landing speed.
Special barriers were installed to deaden the train before it reached the buffer stop.
They dropped anchor to deaden the ship's headway as they approached the dock.
She took medicine to deaden the pain in her back.
The bad news seemed to deaden his enthusiasm for the project.
The thick curtains help to deaden the sound of traffic from outside.
