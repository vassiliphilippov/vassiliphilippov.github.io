depopulated

adjective
having lost inhabitants
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːˈpɑpjəˌleɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌdiːˈpɒpjʊˌleɪtɪd / (BrE)
(of a place) having lost most or all of its inhabitants, typically due to war, disease, or other disasters.

Examples

A depopulated city
Following the civil war, the once-thriving city was almost entirely depopulated.
Many villages were depopulated after the severe drought that struck the region.
The forest grew wild over the depopulated town, hiding any sign of past life.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abandoned - left without inhabitants or occupants
  • antonym: populated - having people living there; inhabited
