chersonese

proper noun
historical peninsula name
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkɜːr.sə.niːs/ (AmE), /ˈkɜː.sə.niːs/ (BrE)
a historical name for a peninsula, especially used in ancient Greek and Roman geography to refer to specific regions such as the Tauric or Malay Chersonese

Examples

The Tauric Chersonese
The ancient Greeks referred to the Crimean Peninsula as the Tauric Chersonese in their writings.
Early maps labeled the Malay Peninsula as the Golden Chersonese due to its rich resources.
Historians study the role of the Chersonese in trade between Europe and Asia.
