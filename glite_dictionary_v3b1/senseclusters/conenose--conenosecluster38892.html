conenose

noun
large bloodsucking bug
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊnˌnoʊz / (AmE), / ˈkəʊnˌnəʊz / (BrE)
A large bloodsucking bug, especially one belonging to the subfamily Triatominae, some species of which are known to transmit Chagas disease.

Examples

A conenose bug
A conenose can transmit diseases to humans and animals.
Scientists studied the conenose population in rural communities.
The bite of a conenose often goes unnoticed but may have serious effects.

Related Terms

  • synonym: kissing bug - a common name for bloodsucking bugs of the genus Triatoma
