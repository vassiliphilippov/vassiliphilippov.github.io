bulwark

noun
defensive wall or embankment
6.5 6.9
Pronunciation: /ˈbʊl.wərk/ (AmE), /ˈbʊl.wək/ (BrE)
a strong wall or barrier built to protect a place, coastline, or ship from attack or danger

Examples

Stone bulwark
Engineers reinforced the crumbling bulwark to prevent it from collapsing.
Soldiers took cover behind the stone bulwark during the siege.
The ancient city was surrounded by a massive bulwark to keep invaders out.
A stone bulwark was built to shield the village from the relentless tides.
Construction of a concrete bulwark has helped preserve the coastline for decades.
The city reinforced the old bulwark to prevent further beach erosion during storms.
Crew members leaned against the bulwark as they looked out at the horizon.
Salt from the sea collected on the wooden bulwark over time.
The ship's bulwark offered protection from the crashing waves during the storm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: barricade - a barrier erected for defense or obstruction
  • synonym: breakwater - a barrier built out into a body of water to protect a coast or harbor from the force of waves
  • synonym: parapet - a low protective wall along the edge of a roof, bridge, or balcony
  • synonym: rampart - a defensive wall of a castle or walled city
