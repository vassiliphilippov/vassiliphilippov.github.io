intoxicant

noun
substance causing intoxication (esp. alcohol)
1.0 6.0
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈtɑːk.sɪ.kənt/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈtɒk.sɪ.kənt/ (BrE)
a substance, especially alcohol or a drug, that makes someone drunk or intoxicated

Examples

An intoxicant like alcohol
He claimed that he had never consumed any intoxicant in his life.
The law prohibits driving under the influence of intoxicants.
The sale of intoxicants is strictly regulated in many countries.
Alcohol is the most commonly used intoxicant in many societies.
Certain herbs were once used as a mild intoxicant in traditional rituals.
The sale of any intoxicant to minors is strictly prohibited by law.

Related Terms

  • antonym: stimulant - a substance that raises levels of physiological or nervous activity
