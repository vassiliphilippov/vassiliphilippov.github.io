day shift

noun
workers on the daytime work period
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪ ʃɪft / (AmE), / ˈdeɪ ʃɪft / (BrE)
the period during the day when people work, or the group of workers who work at that time (usually from morning to afternoon)

Examples

The day shift workers
Many hospitals require nurses to rotate between the day shift and night shift.
She prefers working the day shift because it fits her family's schedule.
The day shift clocks in at 8 a.m. and leaves at 4 p.m. every weekday.
After finishing her day shift, Maria went grocery shopping before heading home.
He usually works the day shift at the hospital and spends evenings with his family.
The factory hires extra workers for the day shift during busy seasons.
