rewarm

verb
to revive an idea
4.8
Pronunciation: /riː.ˈwɔːrm/ /; / /riː.ˈwɑːrm/ /; / /rə.ˈwɔːrm/ (AmE), /riː.ˈwɔːm/ /; / /rɪ.ˈwɔːm/ /; / /rə.ˈwɔːm/ (BrE)
to present or use something again, especially an idea or argument, in a way that revives it

Examples

To rewarm an idea
The author decided to rewarm an old theory in her latest article.
During the meeting, he tried to rewarm arguments that had already been dismissed.
The politician attempted to rewarm promises from previous campaigns to gain support.
