midbody

noun
central body section
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪdˌbɑdi / (AmE), / ˈmɪdˌbɒdi / (BrE)
the central part of an organism's body, often used in scientific or anatomical contexts

Examples

Snake midbody
The scientist measured the width of the snake's midbody for her research.
Distinct patterns appear along the midbody of some fish species.
In some insects, the midbody is clearly separated from the head and tail regions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: extremity - a limb or appendage of the body, such as an arm or leg
  • synonym: torso - the main part of the body excluding the head and limbs
