take in

verb
make clothes smaller by sewing
3.7
Pronunciation: / teɪk ɪn / (AmE), / teɪk ɪn / (BrE)
to make a piece of clothing or fabric narrower or smaller, especially by sewing or folding

Examples

To take in a dress
After losing weight, he had to take in most of his trousers.
Could you please take in this dress around the waist?
The tailor offered to take in my jacket so it would fit better.
As the wind died down, the crew needed to take in some of the canvas.
Before the storm hit, the sailors rushed to take in the sails.
Could you take in this dress at the waist?
