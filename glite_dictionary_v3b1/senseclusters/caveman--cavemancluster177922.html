caveman

noun
prehistoric man living in caves
Pronunciation: / ˈkeɪvˌmæn / (AmE), / ˈkeɪvˌman / (BrE)
A prehistoric man who lived in caves, typically referring to early humans of the Paleolithic period.

Examples

A caveman in history
Some scientists study caveman remains to learn more about early human life.
Stories about the caveman often depict him hunting with primitive tools.
The museum's exhibit featured life-sized models of a caveman and his family.

Related Terms

  • synonym: troglodyte - a prehistoric cave-dweller or someone living in a cave
