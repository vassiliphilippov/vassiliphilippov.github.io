relocate

verb
move to a new location
Pronunciation: / ˌriˈloʊ.keɪt /; / ˌriːˈloʊ.keɪt / (AmE), / ˌriːˈləʊ.keɪt / (BrE)
to move yourself, someone, or something to a new place and become established there

Examples

To relocate to another city
After the flood, many families were forced to relocate to safer areas.

The company decided to relocate its headquarters to a larger city.
After graduating, she decided to relocate to New York City for her new job.
Many companies relocate their headquarters to save on operating costs.
The family relocated from Chicago to Denver last year.
