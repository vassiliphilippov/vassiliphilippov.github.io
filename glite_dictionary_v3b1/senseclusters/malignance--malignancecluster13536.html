malignance

noun
malignant state (medical)
Pronunciation: / məˈlɪɡnəns / (AmE), / məˈlɪɡnəns / (BrE)
(medicine) The condition or quality of being malignant, especially a disease that is invasive, progressive, and resistant to treatment, often causing death.

Examples

Cancer malignance
Doctors feared the malignance would spread to other tissues rapidly.
Research is ongoing to find new therapies that can address the malignance of certain cancers.
The malignance of the tumor made it difficult for doctors to treat successfully.

Related Terms

  • synonym: malignancy - the state of being malignant (in medicine, a cancerous growth or its tendency)
