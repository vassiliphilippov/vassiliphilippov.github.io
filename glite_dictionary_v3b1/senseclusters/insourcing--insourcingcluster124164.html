insourcing

noun
using internal staff
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌsɔrsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌsɔːsɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of using a company's own employees to do work instead of hiring outside contractors, including bringing work back from external providers

Examples

Insourcing company work
After years of outsourcing, the company switched to insourcing to improve control over its operations.
Many businesses believe that insourcing can lead to higher quality and better communication.
The IT department expanded as a result of the company's new insourcing strategy.
The company decided that insourcing its customer support would improve service quality.
After years of outsourcing, the firm began insourcing its IT operations to reduce costs.
Many organizations consider insourcing when they want more control over critical business functions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: outsourcing - the practice of hiring external companies or people to do work
