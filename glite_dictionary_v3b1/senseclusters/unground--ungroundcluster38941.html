unground

adjective
not electrically grounded
8.7
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈɡraʊnd / (AmE), / ʌnˈɡraʊnd / (BrE)
not connected to an electrical ground or earth, lacking a grounding connection for safety

Examples

Unground wire
Using an unground appliance can increase the risk of electric shock in your home.
The electrician warned that the outlet was unground and needed to be fixed immediately.
Many older buildings still have unground wiring, which may not meet modern safety standards.

Related Terms

  • antonym: earthed - connected to the earth for electrical safety
  • antonym: grounded - connected to an electrical ground for safety
