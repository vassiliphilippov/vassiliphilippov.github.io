throw a monkey wrench into the works

verb
cause unexpected disruption or problems
Pronunciation: / θɹoʊ ə ˈmʌŋki ɹɛntʃ ˈɪntu ðə wɜɹks / (AmE), / θɹəʊ ə ˈmʌŋki rɛntʃ ˈɪntuː ðə wɜːks / (BrE)
to deliberately cause difficulties or problems that prevent something from happening smoothly or as planned

Examples

To throw a monkey wrench into the works suddenly
Her sudden resignation threw a monkey wrench into the works for the team project.
If you forget to submit the forms, it could throw a monkey wrench into the works of the whole process.
The last-minute changes to the schedule threw a monkey wrench into the works of our event planning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sabotage - deliberately destroy or damage something to prevent success
  • antonym: smooth sailing - an activity or process going easily without problems
