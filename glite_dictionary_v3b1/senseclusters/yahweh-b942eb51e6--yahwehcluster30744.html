Yahweh

proper noun
the God of the Old Testament (Hebrew name)
Pronunciation: / ˈjɑːˌweɪ / (AmE), / ˈjɑːweɪ / (BrE)
A form of the name of God used in the Old Testament, derived from the Hebrew consonants YHVH.

Examples

Yahweh in Bible
Ancient Hebrews believed that Yahweh should not be spoken aloud.
Many scholars use Yahweh when referring to the God of ancient Israel.
The name Yahweh appears in various translations of the Hebrew Bible.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Jehovah - a later form of the name of God based on YHVH
