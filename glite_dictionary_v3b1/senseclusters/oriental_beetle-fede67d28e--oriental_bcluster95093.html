Oriental beetle

noun
a beetle species harmful to grasses and crops
Pronunciation: / ɔːˈriːəntəl ˈbiːtəl / (AmE), / ɒˈriːəntəl ˈbiːtl̩ / (BrE)
a species of beetle (Anomala orientalis), native to Asia and introduced to North America, whose larvae feed on the roots of sugarcane and other grasses

Examples

The Oriental beetle larvae
Farmers are concerned about Oriental beetle infestations affecting their sugarcane fields.
Researchers are studying how to control the spread of the Oriental beetle in North America.
The Oriental beetle has become a pest in American lawns, damaging turfgrass roots.
