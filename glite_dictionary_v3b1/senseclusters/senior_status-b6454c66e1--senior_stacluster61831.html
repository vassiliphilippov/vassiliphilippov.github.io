senior status

noun
higher rank due to longer service
2.7
Pronunciation: / ˈsiː.njɚ ˈsteɪ.təs / (AmE), / ˈsiː.ni.ə ˈsteɪ.təs / (BrE)
a position of higher rank or privilege granted due to longer service or greater experience, often used in professional, organizational, or judicial contexts

Examples

Senior status in company
After 25 years of service, she finally attained senior status in the company.
His senior status affords him more privileges and responsibilities than his colleagues.
Judges may assume senior status after meeting certain age and service requirements.

Related Terms

  • antonym: junior status - lower rank, especially due to less experience or shorter service
