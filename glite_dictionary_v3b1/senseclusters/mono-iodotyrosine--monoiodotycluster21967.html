mono-iodotyrosine

noun
iodinated form of tyrosine
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌmoʊnoʊˌaɪədoʊtaɪˈroʊsiːn / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəʊˌaɪədəʊtaɪˈrəʊsiːn / (BrE)
A compound formed when a single iodine atom is added to tyrosine, serving as an intermediate in the production of thyroid hormones.

Examples

The mono-iodotyrosine compound
A deficiency of mono-iodotyrosine can affect proper hormone synthesis.
Researchers measured the levels of mono-iodotyrosine in the blood samples.
The thyroid gland synthesizes mono-iodotyrosine as a step in the production of thyroid hormones.
