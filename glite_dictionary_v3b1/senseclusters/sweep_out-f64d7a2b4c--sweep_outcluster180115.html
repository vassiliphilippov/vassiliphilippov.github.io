sweep out

verb
remove or clear away by sweeping
Pronunciation: / swip aʊt / (AmE), / swiːp aʊt / (BrE)
to remove or clear away something, especially by sweeping

Examples

To sweep out dust
Before we moved in, we had to sweep out the garage to get rid of the dust and cobwebs.
Every spring, the school janitors sweep out the classrooms to prepare for the new term.
She carefully swept out the leaves that had blown into the hallway during the storm.
