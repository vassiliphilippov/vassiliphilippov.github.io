parieto-occipital sulcus

noun
groove separating parietal and occipital lobes
Pronunciation: / pəˌraɪətoʊ əkˌsɪpɪtl ˈsʌlkəs / (AmE), / pəˌraɪətəʊ ɒkˌsɪpɪtl ˈsʌlkəs / (BrE)
A deep groove near the back of each cerebral hemisphere, separating the parietal lobe from the occipital lobe.

Examples

The parieto-occipital sulcus groove
Damage near the parieto-occipital sulcus may affect visual and spatial processing.
Neurosurgeons use the parieto-occipital sulcus as a landmark during certain brain surgeries.
The parieto-occipital sulcus can be seen clearly in this MRI scan of the brain.

Related Terms

  • synonym: central sulcus - major groove separating the frontal and parietal lobes
  • synonym: lateral sulcus - groove separating temporal and frontal/parietal lobes
