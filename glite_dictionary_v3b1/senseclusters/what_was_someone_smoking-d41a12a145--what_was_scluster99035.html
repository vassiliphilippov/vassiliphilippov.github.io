what was someone smoking

x
express disbelief at someone's irrational idea or action
5.2
Pronunciation: / wʌt wəz ˈsʌmwʌn ˈsmoʊkɪŋ / (AmE), / wɒt wəz ˈsʌmwən ˈsməʊkɪŋ / (BrE)
used to express disbelief or incredulity at someone's idea or action, implying that it is so irrational it could only result from intoxication or impaired judgment

Examples

What was he smoking
He thought we could finish the project in one day—what was he smoking?
The mayor proposed building a rollercoaster downtown; what was she smoking?
You paid that much for an old car? What were you smoking?

Related Terms

  • synonym: are you out of your mind - expression used to question someone's sanity or judgment
  • synonym: what were you thinking - expression used to question someone's reasoning or decision
