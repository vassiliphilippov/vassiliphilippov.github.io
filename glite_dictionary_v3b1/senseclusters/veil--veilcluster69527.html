veil

verb
to obscure or conceal
6.2 4.1
Pronunciation: /veɪl/ (AmE), /veɪl/ (BrE)
to cover, obscure, or conceal something, either literally or figuratively

Examples

To veil the truth
His true intentions were carefully veiled behind a polite smile.
The details of the contract were veiled in complex legal language.
The meaning of her words was veiled, leaving everyone in confusion.
She tried to veil her disappointment with a polite smile.
The artist chose to veil the true meaning of his work behind abstract symbols.
Thick fog veiled the entire landscape, making it hard to see anything clearly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conceal - to hide or keep something secret
  • synonym: mask - to cover or disguise as with a mask
  • synonym: obscure - to make unclear or hard to see
  • antonym: reveal - to make something known or visible
