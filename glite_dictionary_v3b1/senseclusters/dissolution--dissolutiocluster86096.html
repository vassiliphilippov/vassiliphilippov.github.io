dissolution

noun
ending or breaking up of a relationship or union
2.6
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪsəˈluːʃən / (AmE), / ˌdɪsəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of ending, breaking up, or formally dismissing a relationship, organization, or official group

Examples

Dissolution of parliament
After years of disagreement, the dissolution of the alliance seemed inevitable.
The dissolution of their partnership was announced in the press today.
The king's decree led to the dissolution of parliament.
After decades, the company faced dissolution as its departments split off one by one.
Scientists studied the dissolution of salt into its ions when placed in solution.
The dissolution of the tablet in water was observed under the microscope.
Following the dissolution of the assembly, the members left the hall.
The dissolution of the annual board meeting was announced at 5 p.m.
The chairman declared the dissolution of the session after all items were addressed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: separation - the action or state of moving or being moved apart; ending of association
  • antonym: union - the action or fact of joining or being joined together
