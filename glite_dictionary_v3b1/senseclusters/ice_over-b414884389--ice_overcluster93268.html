ice over

verb
become covered with ice
Pronunciation: / aɪs ˈoʊvɚ / (AmE), / aɪs ˈəʊvə / (BrE)
to become covered with a layer of ice, especially referring to surfaces such as lakes, roads, or windows

Examples

Lakes ice over
Careful when you drive in the morning—bridges tend to ice over before the roads do.
The pond will ice over if the temperature stays below freezing for another night.
We watched the windshield slowly ice over during the snowstorm.
