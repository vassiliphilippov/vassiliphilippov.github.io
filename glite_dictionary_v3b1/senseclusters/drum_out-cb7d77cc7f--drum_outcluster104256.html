drum out

verb
force someone to leave a position or group
7.4 0.17/m
Pronunciation: / drÊŒm aÊŠt / (AmE), / drÊŒm aÊŠt / (BrE)
to force someone to leave a group, organization, or position, often publicly or with disgrace

Examples

To drum out a member
After the scandal, he was drummed out of the company with no ceremony.
She feared being drummed out of the organization if her secret was discovered.
The officer was drummed out of the army for misconduct.
After the incident, the team decided to drum out the player responsible for the breach.
He was drummed out of the army for his misconduct.
The organization may drum out members who fail to follow its code of ethics.
