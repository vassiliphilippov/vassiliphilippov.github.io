corrosive

adjective
able to destroy by chemical action
1.4 5.3
Pronunciation: / kəˈroʊsɪv / (AmE), / kəˈrəʊsɪv / (BrE)
(of a substance) able to destroy or eat away materials by means of a chemical reaction, especially causing damage or deterioration.

Examples

Corrosive chemicals
Acids like hydrochloric acid are highly corrosive and must be stored safely.
Always wear protective gloves when handling corrosive chemicals in the laboratory.
The corrosive effect of saltwater can weaken metal structures over time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: caustic - able to burn or corrode organic tissue; highly corrosive
