bitter pecan

noun
hickory tree with bitter nuts
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌbɪt̬ɚ pɪˈkæn / (AmE), / ˌbɪtə pɪˈkæn / (BrE)
A hickory tree native to the southern United States, known for its many narrow leaflets and bitter-tasting nuts.

Examples

A bitter pecan tree
Botanists identified the tree as a bitter pecan based on its leaf structure and location.
The wood of the bitter pecan is sometimes used for furniture and flooring.
Unlike the sweet pecan, the bitter pecan produces nuts that are unpleasant to eat raw.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hickory - a type of nut-bearing tree, including pecan and other species
