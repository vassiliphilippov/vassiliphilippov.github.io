counterproductively

adverb
opposite of intended effect
Pronunciation: / ˌkaʊntərprəˈdʌktɪvli / (AmE), / ˌkaʊntəprəˈdʌktɪvli / (BrE)
in a way that has the opposite effect to what was intended or desired

Examples

Acting counterproductively
He counterproductively interrupted everyone, making it harder to reach an agreement.
The new policy was applied counterproductively, causing more confusion than clarity.
By criticizing her team counterproductively, she lowered their motivation instead of improving performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ineffectively - in a way that does not produce the desired result
  • antonym: productively - in a way that achieves positive results
