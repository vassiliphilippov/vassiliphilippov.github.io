call out

verb
challenge to a duel (historical)
1.0
Pronunciation: / kɔl aʊt / (AmE), / kɔːl aʊt / (BrE)
(historical) To challenge someone to a duel, especially in response to an insult or perceived wrongdoing.

Examples

To call out a rival
Aaron Burr famously called out Alexander Hamilton, leading to their notorious duel.
It was not uncommon in the 18th century for men to call out one another over perceived insults.
The offended gentleman decided to call out his rival on the field of honor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: challenge - to invite someone to compete or take part, especially in a contest
