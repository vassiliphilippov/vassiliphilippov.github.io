shariah

noun
Islamic legal code based on Quran and Muhammad
Pronunciation: / ʃəˈriːə / (AmE), / ʃəˈrɪə / (BrE)
The system of Islamic law based on the Quran and the teachings and example of Muhammad.

Examples

Shariah law
Debates over the application of shariah often arise in discussions about religious freedom and law.
He studied how shariah influences contemporary banking practices in some Muslim-majority countries.
Many countries incorporate elements of shariah into their legal systems.
