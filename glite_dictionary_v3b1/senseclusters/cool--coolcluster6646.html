cool

verb
to make less warm
1.0 20/m
Pronunciation: / kuÀêl /; / kul / (AmE), / kuÀêl / (BrE)
to make something less warm or to become less hot; to lower the temperature

Examples

To cool the soup
Freshly brewed tea should be left to cool before serving over ice.
Let the cake cool before frosting it.
They used fans to cool the overheated engine.
Even the hottest days cool down as night falls.
Let the soup cool before you serve it to the guests.
She opened the window to cool the room after baking.

Synonyms

  • cool down
    1.1 1/m
    cool down is used to lower temperature quickly or after heating, while cool is the general verb for reducing heat.
  • chill
    1.0 12/m
    chill means lower something to a cold temperature often by refrigeration, while cool means simply make something less warm.
  • cool off
    2.7 1/m
    cool off can mean calm down emotionally or become less hot, while cool only refers to lowering temperature.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chill - to make (something) colder
  • antonym: heat - to make hot or hotter
