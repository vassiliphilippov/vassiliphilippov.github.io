conserve

verb
protect from harm or loss
Pronunciation: / kənˈsɝv / (AmE), / kənˈsɜːv / (BrE)
to protect something from harm, loss, or waste by careful use or management

Examples

To conserve water
Efforts are being made to conserve rare species in the national park.
It is crucial to conserve water during periods of drought.
Scientists work hard to conserve valuable natural resources for future generations.
She tries to conserve her strength for the final round of the competition.
Turning off the lights when you leave the room helps to conserve energy.
We need to conserve water during the summer months to avoid shortages.

Related Terms

  • antonym: destroy - to ruin completely or put an end to
  • synonym: preserve - to keep something in its original state or in good condition
  • synonym: protect - to keep safe from harm or injury
  • synonym: save - to keep and store up for future use
  • antonym: waste - to use carelessly, extravagantly, or to no purpose
