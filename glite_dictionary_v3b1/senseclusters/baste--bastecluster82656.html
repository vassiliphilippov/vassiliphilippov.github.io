baste

verb
moisten food with liquid while cooking
9.5
Pronunciation: / beɪst / (AmE), / beɪst / (BrE)
To pour liquid, such as melted fat or juices, over food while it is cooking to keep it moist and add flavor.

Examples

To baste a chicken
During the last hour in the oven, she basted the chicken with melted butter.
If you don’t baste the meat while it’s roasting, it could dry out.
The chef remembered to baste the turkey every thirty minutes for a juicy result.

Related Terms

  • synonym: marinate - to soak food in a seasoned liquid before cooking
  • synonym: roast - to cook food, especially meat, by dry heat in an oven or over a fire
