octant

noun
historic instrument for angle measurement
Pronunciation: / ˈɑk.tənt / (AmE), / ˈɒk.tənt / (BrE)
a historical measuring instrument for determining angles to celestial bodies, similar to a sextant but calibrated up to 45 degrees

Examples

The navigator used an octant to determine his ship's latitude at sea.
