muddy

adjective
dull or dirty color; not clear or bright
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌdi / (AmE), / ˈmʌdi / (BrE)
(of a color) dull, unclear, or discolored by impurities; lacking brightness or clarity.

Examples

Muddy colors
After mixing too many colors, her painting started to look muddy and lifeless.
The living room walls were covered in a muddy shade of green that made the space feel dim.
The paint dried to a muddy brown instead of the vibrant red I expected.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bright - (of color) vivid and clear; the opposite of dull or muddy
  • synonym: dingy - (of color) drab; not bright or clear
  • antonym: vivid - (of color) intensely deep or bright; much purer than muddy
