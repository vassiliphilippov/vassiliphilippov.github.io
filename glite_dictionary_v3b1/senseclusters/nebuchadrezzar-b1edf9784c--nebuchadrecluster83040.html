Nebuchadrezzar

proper noun
Babylonian king who destroyed Jerusalem
9.9
Pronunciation: /nə.bə.kə.ˈdrez.ər/ /; / /nɪ.bə.kə.ˈdrez.ər/ (AmE), /nə.bə.kə.ˈdrez.ə/ /; / /nɪ.bə.kə.ˈdrez.ə/ (BrE)
(in the Old Testament) King of Chaldea who captured and destroyed Jerusalem and exiled the Israelites to Babylonia (c. 630–562 BC).

Examples

Nebuchadrezzar king of Babylon
Nebuchadrezzar is often mentioned in biblical texts as the king who destroyed Jerusalem.
According to history, Nebuchadrezzar ruled Babylon at its height and expanded its empire.
The exile of the Israelites to Babylonia was ordered by Nebuchadrezzar after the conquest of Jerusalem.
