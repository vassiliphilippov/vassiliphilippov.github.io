Jacques Offenbach

proper noun
French composer of operettas and opera
8.1
Pronunciation: / ʒɑk ˈɔːfənˌbɑk / (AmE), / ʒak ˈɒfənbæk / (BrE)
(1819–1880) a French composer known for his operettas and for the opera 'The Tales of Hoffmann'.

Examples

Jacques Offenbach operetta composer
Jacques Offenbach is best known for his operetta 'Orpheus in the Underworld'.
During the 19th century, Jacques Offenbach was a leading figure in Parisian musical theatre.
The works of Jacques Offenbach greatly influenced the development of light opera.
