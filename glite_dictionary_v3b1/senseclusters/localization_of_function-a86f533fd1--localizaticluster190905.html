localization of function

noun
physiological principle linking function to body regions
Pronunciation: / ˌloʊkəlaɪˈzeɪʃən əv ˈfʌŋkʃən /; / ˌloʊ.kə.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən əv ˈfʌŋk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌləʊkəlaɪˈzeɪʃən əv ˈfʌŋkʃən /; / ˌləʊ.kə.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən əv ˈfʌŋk.ʃən / (BrE)
The principle in physiology that specific functions are associated with particular regions or organs in the body.
