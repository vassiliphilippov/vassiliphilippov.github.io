potshot

noun
an easy or casual criticism or attack
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑtˌʃɑt / (AmE), / ˈpɒtʃɒt /; / ˈpɒtˌʃɒt / (BrE)
an attack, criticism, or shot that is easily made and not carefully considered, often aimed at an easy target (can be literal or figurative)

Examples

To take potshots
Instead of offering solutions, he just took potshots at his opponents.
She grew tired of the constant potshots directed at her work.
The article was full of potshots at the company's leadership.
His review included a potshot at the previous government's environmental policies.
She couldn't resist taking a potshot at her rival during the debate.
The hunters took a potshot at the rabbit as it darted out of the bushes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attack - an aggressive criticism or action against someone or something
  • antonym: compliment - a polite expression of praise or admiration
  • synonym: crack - an attempt to do something, often to succeed or achieve something
