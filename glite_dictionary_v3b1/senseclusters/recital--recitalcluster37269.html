recital

noun
detailed spoken or written account
Pronunciation: / rɪˈsaɪtəl /; / rɪˈsaɪt̬əl / (AmE), / rɪˈsaɪtəl / (BrE)
a detailed spoken or written account or list of facts or events

Examples

A recital of facts
His letter included a lengthy recital of every obstacle he had faced during the project.
She grew weary of his constant recital of grievances and complaints.
The committee listened patiently to her recital of recent events within the department.
His report was more of a chronological recital than an analytical account.
The contract began with a recital of the history of the agreement.
The witness gave a detailed recital of the events leading up to the accident.

Related Terms

  • synonym: enumeration - the action of mentioning a number of things one by one
  • synonym: narration - the act of narrating or telling a story
  • antonym: summary - a brief statement or account of the main points
