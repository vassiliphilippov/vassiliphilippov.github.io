inert

adjective
chemically inactive; unreactive
7.3
Pronunciation: / ɪˈnɝt / (AmE), / ɪˈnɜːt / (BrE)
(of a substance) having little or no ability to react chemically; not active in chemical processes.

Examples

Inert gas
Gold remains inert under most conditions, making it ideal for jewelry.
Helium is an inert gas, so it does not readily form compounds.
The tank was filled with an inert substance to prevent unwanted chemical reactions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inactive - not active; without action, also used of substances
  • antonym: reactive - tending to react chemically; opposite of inert
