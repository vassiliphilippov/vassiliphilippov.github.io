standing army

noun
permanent professional army
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈstændɪŋ ˈɑːrmi / (AmE), / ˈstændɪŋ ˈɑːmi / (BrE)
A permanent, professional army maintained by a nation, composed of paid soldiers who are kept ready for action at any time.

Examples

A standing army
Many countries maintain a standing army to ensure national security.
The framers of the Constitution debated the dangers of a permanent standing army.
Unlike medieval levies, a standing army is kept active even during peacetime.

Related Terms

  • antonym: militia - a military force raised from the civil population, not a permanent army
