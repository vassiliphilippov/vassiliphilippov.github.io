lie low

verb
avoid attracting attention; remain inconspicuous
6.0
Pronunciation: / laɪ ˈloʊ /; / laɪ loʊ / (AmE), / laɪ ˈləʊ /; / laɪ ləʊ / (BrE)
to avoid attracting attention or to keep out of sight, especially to avoid trouble or being detected

Examples

To lie low quietly
After the scandal broke, the politician decided to lie low for a while.
She was advised to lie low and not draw any more attention to herself.
We need to lie low until things calm down before contacting anyone.
After the robbery, they decided to lie low until things calmed down.
She had to lie low to avoid being recognized by the press.
The suspect is believed to be lying low in another city to evade capture.
After the incident, he decided to lie low until things settled down.
It's wise to lie low if you want to avoid any more trouble from them.
She chose to lie low rather than speak publicly about the controversy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: keep a low profile - to avoid attracting attention
  • antonym: stand out - to attract attention, be conspicuous
  • antonym: surface - to come out into the open after hiding
