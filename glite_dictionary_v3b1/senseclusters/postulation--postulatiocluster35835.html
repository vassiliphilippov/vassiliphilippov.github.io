postulation

noun
an assumed basis for argument
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊstʃəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɒstjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
An assumption or claim accepted without proof as a basis for reasoning or argument, especially in logic or philosophy.

Examples

Logical postulation
Before proceeding, let us agree on the postulation that everyone in the group is rational.
Her philosophical argument depends heavily on the postulation of free will.
The scientist began his theory with the postulation that all objects fall at the same rate in a vacuum.

Related Terms

  • synonym: axiom - a statement taken to be true as a premise for reasoning
  • synonym: hypothesis - a supposition made as a starting point for further investigation
