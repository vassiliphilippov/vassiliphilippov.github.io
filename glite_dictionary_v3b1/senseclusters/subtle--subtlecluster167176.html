subtle

adjective
not obvious; difficult to detect or analyze
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌt̬l /; / ˈsʌt̬əl / (AmE), / ˈsʌtl /; / ˈsʌt.əl / (BrE)
not obvious or easy to notice; difficult to detect or understand

Examples

Subtle change
Sometimes, the most subtle flavors make a dish memorable.
The artist used subtle shading to give depth to the painting.
There was a subtle shift in her tone that few people noticed.
Corruption can have a subtle influence on an organization, slowly undermining its integrity.
Some viruses are extremely subtle, making them hard to detect before symptoms appear.
The effects of the subtle toxin were not noticed until weeks later.

  • antonym: obvious - easily perceived or understood; clear
