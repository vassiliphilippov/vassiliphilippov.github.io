Uranometria

proper noun
historic star atlas
Pronunciation: / ˌjʊrənoʊˈmiːtriə / (AmE), / ˌjʊərənəʊˈmiːtrɪə / (BrE)
the title of a 1603 star atlas by Johann Bayer, known for introducing the Bayer designation for stars

Examples

The Uranometria atlas
The Uranometria was groundbreaking for its detailed maps of the night sky.
Astronomy historians often reference the Uranometria when discussing early star catalogues.
Bayer's Uranometria introduced a new system for naming stars that is still used today.
