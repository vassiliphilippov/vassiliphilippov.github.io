red salmon

noun
small Pacific salmon with red flesh
Pronunciation: / rɛd ˈsæmən / (AmE), / rɛd ˈsæmən / (BrE)
A species of small Pacific salmon with reddish flesh, highly valued as food, whose adults die after spawning.

Examples

Wild red salmon
After spawning in the river, adult red salmon die, providing nutrients to the ecosystem.
Grilled red salmon is a popular dish during the summer in Alaska.
Many people prefer red salmon because of its rich flavor and firm texture.

Related Terms

  • antonym: chum salmon - a different species of Pacific salmon
  • synonym: sockeye salmon - another common name for the red salmon (Oncorhynchus nerka)
