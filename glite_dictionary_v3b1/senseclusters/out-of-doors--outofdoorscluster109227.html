out-of-doors

noun
the open air or outside environment
3.2 0.03/m
Pronunciation: / Àåa ät …ôv Ààd…îÀêrz / (AmE), / Àåa ät …ôv Ààd…îÀêz / (BrE)
in or into the open air; the outside environment, especially away from buildings or shelter

Examples

To be out-of-doors
After a long winter, everyone was eager to be out-of-doors enjoying the fresh air.
Children benefit from playing out-of-doors rather than staying inside all day.
She loves spending her weekends out-of-doors, hiking in the mountains.

Synonyms

  • outdoors

  • outside
    1.0 109/m
    outside means in or to a place not indoors and fits many uses, while out-of-doors feels more formal or old-fashioned.
  • open air
    1.0 0.79/m
    open air is the outdoor space without a roof or walls, while out-of-doors is the older phrase for being outside.
  • open
    1.0 1.36/m
    open is used for wide, unobstructed land or space, while out-of-doors focuses on being in the open air away from buildings.
