paratroop

noun
parachuting military unit
Pronunciation: / ˈpærəˌtruːp / (AmE), / ˈpærəˌtruːp / (BrE)
a soldier or group of soldiers trained to parachute into combat, or a military operation involving such soldiers

Examples

A paratroop was deployed to secure the airfield before reinforcements arrived.
Training for the paratroop included both parachuting and ground combat exercises.
The army launched a paratroop behind enemy lines to surprise the opposition.
During the war, a daring paratroop secured the bridge before dawn.
A successful paratroop can quickly change the course of a battle.
The paratroop unit was deployed behind enemy lines during the night.
He trained for months to become a paratroop in the army.
During the operation, a paratroop landed safely despite strong winds.
