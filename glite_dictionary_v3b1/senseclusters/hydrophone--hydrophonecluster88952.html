hydrophone

noun
underwater sound detector
7.9
Pronunciation: /ˈhaɪ.drə.foʊn/ /; / /ˈhaɪ.droʊ.foʊn/ (AmE), /ˈhaɪ.drə.fəʊn/ (BrE)
a device used to detect or record sound underwater, especially in oceans or lakes

Examples

Underwater hydrophone device
The research team used a hydrophone to monitor the sounds of dolphins in the bay.
Engineers placed a hydrophone on the lakebed to study underwater noise pollution.
A hydrophone can pick up the faint sounds of distant ships passing through the ocean.
The scientists used a hydrophone to record the sounds of dolphins communicating underwater.
A hydrophone can pick up even faint noises made by marine animals deep in the sea.
During the experiment, a hydrophone was placed near the coral reef to monitor fish activity.
