Opuntia

noun
genus of cacti (prickly pears)
8.5
Pronunciation: / oʊˈpʌnʃə / (AmE), / əˈpʌntɪə / (BrE)
A large genus of cacti native to the Americas, commonly known as prickly pears, characterized by flat, jointed stems and often edible fruit.

Examples

Opuntia cactus plant

The Opuntia thrives in arid climates across North and South America.
You can often see wild Opuntia along desert hiking trails.
