genius loci

noun
guardian spirit of a place
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒiːniəs ˈloʊ.saɪ / (AmE), / ˌdʒiːniəs ˈləʊ.saɪ / (BrE)
the guardian spirit of a particular place, regarded as having a special influence or character

Examples

Ancient genius loci
Architects often consider the genius loci when designing new buildings in historic cities.
The ancient Romans believed every home and field had its own genius loci to protect it.
The festival celebrated the genius loci of the forest, honoring its unique spirit.
