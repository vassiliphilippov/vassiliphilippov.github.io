antisense

adjective
complementary genetic strand
Pronunciation: / ˈæntiˌsɛns / (AmE), / ˈæntiˌsɛns / (BrE)
describing a strand of DNA or RNA that is complementary to a coding sequence and can bind to it

Examples

Antisense DNA strand
The antisense strand of DNA pairs with the sense strand during transcription.
Scientists use antisense technology to block the expression of certain genes.
An antisense oligonucleotide can be designed to target specific messenger RNA.

Related Terms

  • antonym: sense - describing a DNA or RNA strand with the same sequence as the coding sequence
