whereat

conjunction
introducing consequence
Pronunciation: / wɛrˈæt / (AmE), / wɛərˈæt / (BrE)
at or upon which; used to introduce a reaction or immediate consequence, especially in formal or archaic contexts

Examples

Whereat they laughed
He made a bold statement, whereat the audience fell silent in surprise.
The door creaked open, whereat everyone in the room turned to look.
She finished her speech, whereat the crowd erupted in applause.

Related Terms

  • synonym: whereupon - immediately after which; introducing a result or reaction
