West Germany

proper noun
former republic in western Germany (1949–1990)
3.7
Pronunciation: / wɛst ˈdʒɜrməni / (AmE), / west ˈdʒɜːməni / (BrE)
the former republic in north central Europe, established in 1949 from the British, French, and American occupation zones of Germany, which existed until reunification with East Germany in 1990

Examples

West Germany after World War II
After World War II, West Germany rapidly rebuilt its economy and became a leading industrial nation.
Athletes from West Germany competed separately from those in East Germany until 1990.
The fall of the Berlin Wall paved the way for reunification between West Germany and East Germany.
