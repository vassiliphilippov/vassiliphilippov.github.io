hypertonic

adjective
having higher osmotic pressure than another solution
7.8 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / Àåha…™.p…öÀàt…ëÀê.n…™k / (AmE), / Àåha…™.p…ôÀàt…ín.…™k / (BrE)
(of a solution) having a higher osmotic pressure than another solution, resulting in the movement of water out of cells placed in it.

Examples

Hypertonic saline solution
Doctors may prescribe a hypertonic saline solution to treat certain medical conditions.
The plant cells lost water and became flaccid in the hypertonic environment.
When red blood cells are placed in a hypertonic solution, they shrink as water leaves the cells.

Related Terms
