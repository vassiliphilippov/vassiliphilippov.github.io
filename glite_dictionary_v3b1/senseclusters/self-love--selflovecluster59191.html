self-love

noun
regard for one's own happiness or interests
2.2
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlfˈlʌv /; / ˈsɛlf lʌv / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈlʌv /; / ˈsɛlf lʌv / (BrE)
regard and care for your own well-being and happiness

Examples

Self-love practice
Practicing self-love helped her develop more confidence and resilience.
Some critics see his attitude as bordering on self-love rather than healthy self-esteem.
Without a sense of self-love, people may struggle to set boundaries or care for themselves properly.
Learning self-love helped him recover from years of insecurity.
Many therapists encourage self-love as a foundation for healthy relationships.
She wrote a book on the importance of self-love in overcoming adversity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: narcissism - excessive interest in oneself and one's physical appearance
  • synonym: self-esteem - confidence in one's own worth or abilities
  • antonym: self-hatred - intense dislike or hatred of oneself
  • antonym: selflessness - concern for the needs and wishes of others
