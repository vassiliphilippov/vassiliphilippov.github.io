tying arrangement

noun
sales agreement linking two products
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪ.ɪŋ əˌreɪndʒ.mənt / (AmE), / ˈtaɪ.ɪŋ əˌreɪndʒ.mənt / (BrE)
an agreement in which a seller supplies a product or service to a buyer only on the condition that the buyer also purchases a different, often unrelated, product or service.

Examples

A tying arrangement agreement
Many consumers objected to the tying arrangement that required them to buy unnecessary accessories.
Regulators investigate tying arrangements to ensure competitive practices in the marketplace.
The court ruled that the software company's tying arrangement violated antitrust laws.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: bundling - the practice of selling two or more products or services together as a combined offer
  • synonym: tying - the practice of making the sale of one product contingent on the purchase of another
