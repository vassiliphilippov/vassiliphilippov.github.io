knap

verb
break a small piece off by striking
9.4 9.3
Pronunciation: /næp/ (AmE), /næp/ (BrE)
to shape or break stone (especially flint) by striking it, usually to make tools or weapons

Examples

To knap flint stones
Archaeologists often knap flint to reproduce ancient tools.
He used a hammerstone to knap the stone into a sharp blade.
If you knap the edge too hard, it might break the arrowhead.
He took the stone and knapped it sharply to make a tool.
She knapped the brittle biscuit, breaking it cleanly in half.
The craftsman knapped the flint to shape it into an arrowhead.
The archaeologist demonstrated how to knap flint into sharp blades using a hammerstone.
Ancient people would knap obsidian to create arrowheads for hunting.
Learning to knap stone tools requires patience and careful technique.
