constitute

verb
form or make up; compose
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːn.stəˌtut /; / ˈkɑn.stəˌtut / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.stɪ.tjuːt / (BrE)
to be the parts or elements that make up or form something; to compose or amount to

Examples

Constitute a large part
Her contributions constitute a large part of the project's success.
These five houses constitute the entire village on the island.
Twelve months constitute a year in the Gregorian calendar.
The committee's decisions constitute the official policy of the organization.
These five villages constitute the majority of the region's population.
Women constitute nearly half of the workforce in this field.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compose - to be the parts that combine to form something
  • synonym: comprise - to consist of; be made up of
  • synonym: form - to make up or compose; constitute
  • synonym: represent - to serve or stand for; to amount to
