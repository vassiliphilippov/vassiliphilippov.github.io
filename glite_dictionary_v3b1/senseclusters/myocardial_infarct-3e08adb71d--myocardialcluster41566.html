myocardial infarct

noun
destruction of heart muscle due to blocked blood supply
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪ.oʊˈkɑːr.di.əl ɪnˈfɑrkt / (AmE), / ˌmaɪ.əʊˈkɑː.di.əl ɪnˈfɑːkt / (BrE)
The destruction of heart muscle tissue caused by an obstruction of its blood supply, commonly referred to as a heart attack.

Examples

A myocardial infarct attack
Prompt treatment is essential to minimize tissue loss following a myocardial infarct.
The doctor explained that a myocardial infarct can cause permanent damage to the heart muscle.
The patient suffered a myocardial infarct during the procedure and was rushed to the intensive care unit.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: angina - chest pain due to reduced blood flow to the heart
  • synonym: heart attack - a common term for myocardial infarction
