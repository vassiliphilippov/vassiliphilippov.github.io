musculus

noun
Latin for muscle
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌskjələs / (AmE), / ˈmʌskjʊləs / (BrE)
a Latin term meaning muscle, used in scientific or medical names for muscles

Examples

Musculus biceps
The term musculus is often found in anatomical names such as musculus biceps brachii.
In medical textbooks, musculus is used to refer to specific muscles in Latin.
The doctor explained that the musculus gluteus maximus is the largest muscle in the body.

Related Terms

  • synonym: muscle - a tissue in the body that contracts to produce movement
