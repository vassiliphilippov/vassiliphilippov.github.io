Examples

Excludability of goods
Excludability is important when determining whether a product can be sold in a market.
The excludability of a service affects how companies set their pricing strategies.
Public parks typically lack excludability, making them accessible to everyone.
The excludability of the service means only paying members can use it.

Increasing excludability can make certain resources less accessible to the general public.
