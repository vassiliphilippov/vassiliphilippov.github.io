excludability

noun
ability to restrict use
Pronunciation: / ɪkˌskluːdəˈbɪlɪti /; / ɪkˌskluːdəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪkˌskluːdəˈbɪlɪti /; / ɪkˌskluːdəˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being able to prevent others from accessing or using something

Examples

Excludability of goods
Excludability is important when determining whether a product can be sold in a market.
The excludability of a service affects how companies set their pricing strategies.
Public parks typically lack excludability, making them accessible to everyone.
The excludability of the service means only paying members can use it.
Public goods are often defined by their lack of excludability.
Increasing excludability can make certain resources less accessible to the general public.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exclusion - the act of preventing someone from entering or participating
  • antonym: inclusivity - the quality of including all people and groups
  • antonym: nonexcludability - the inability to prevent others from using a good or service
  • synonym: rivalry - the property of a good where one person's use reduces its availability to others
