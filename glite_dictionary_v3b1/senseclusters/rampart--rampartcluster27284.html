rampart

noun
defensive embankment or wall
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈræmˌpɑrt / (AmE), / ˈræmpɑːt / (BrE)
A broad embankment or wall, often with a parapet on top, built around a fort, castle, or city for defensive purposes.

Examples

The ramparts of a castle
Soldiers lined the ramparts, ready to defend the city against attack.
The castle's rampart offered a commanding view of the surrounding countryside.
Tourists can still walk along the ancient rampart that once protected the town.

Related Terms

  • synonym: parapet - a low protective wall along the edge of a roof, bridge, or balcony, or on top of a rampart
  • synonym: wall - a structure made for defense or to enclose an area
