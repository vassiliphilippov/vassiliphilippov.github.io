rank

adjective
conspicuously bad; flagrant
Pronunciation: / ræŋk / (AmE), / ræŋk / (BrE)
(of something bad or undesirable) complete and utter; extreme or flagrant, used for emphasis

Examples

Rank disrespect
His behavior at the meeting was nothing short of rank disrespect.
It was a rank betrayal, obvious to everyone involved.
The judge condemned the rank injustice that occurred during the trial.
It was a rank injustice to blame her for the error.
The proposal was dismissed as rank nonsense by the committee.
The room was in rank disorder after the wild party.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absolute - complete; total and without limit
  • synonym: egregious - outstandingly bad; shocking
  • synonym: flagrant - conspicuously or obviously offensive
  • synonym: heinous - utterly odious or wicked
  • antonym: partial - incomplete; not total
  • synonym: utter - complete; total (often used for emphasis)
