bronchoscopy

noun
airway examination procedure
Pronunciation: / brɑŋˈkɑskəpi / (AmE), / brɒŋˈkɒskəpi / (BrE)
a medical procedure in which a thin tube is inserted into the airways to examine the lungs and breathing passages

Examples

A bronchoscopy procedure
The doctor scheduled a bronchoscopy to investigate the cause of the patient's persistent cough.
During the bronchoscopy, the specialist carefully examined the inside of the airways for any blockages.
A bronchoscopy can help diagnose infections, tumors, or other lung problems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biopsy - the removal of tissue for examination
  • synonym: endoscopy - a procedure using a flexible tube to look inside the body
