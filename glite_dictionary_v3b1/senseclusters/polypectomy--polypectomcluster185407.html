polypectomy

noun
surgical removal of a polyp
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑːliˈpɛktəmi / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈpɛktəmi / (BrE)
the surgical removal of a polyp from an organ or tissue, especially in the digestive tract

Examples

A polypectomy surgery
A polypectomy can often be performed on an outpatient basis with minimal recovery time.
Complications from a polypectomy are rare but may include bleeding or infection.
The doctor recommended a polypectomy after a polyp was found during the colonoscopy.
