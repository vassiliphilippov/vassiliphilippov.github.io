remunerator

noun
person who pays compensation
Pronunciation: / rɪˈmjunəˌreɪtər / (AmE), / rɪˈmjuːnəreɪtə / (BrE)
a person who pays money as compensation for work, services, or loss

Examples

A remunerator pays money
A fair remunerator values the contributions of employees and compensates them accordingly.
In the contract, the remunerator is responsible for covering travel expenses.
The company, acting as the remunerator, ensured every contractor received prompt payment.
