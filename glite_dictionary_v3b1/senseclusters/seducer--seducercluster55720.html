seducer

noun
person who entices others into wrongdoing
Pronunciation: / sɪˈdusər / (AmE), / sɪˈdjuːsə / (BrE)
a person who entices others into wrongdoing or immoral behavior, often by means of persuasion or temptation

Examples

A seducer of friends
He was branded a seducer after convincing his friends to take part in the illegal scheme.
Many tales warn against the charms of a seducer who can lead honest people astray.
The cult leader proved to be a skillful seducer of the vulnerable members of the community.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corrupter - a person who causes others to become morally depraved or corrupt
  • synonym: tempter - a person who tempts others to do something wrong
