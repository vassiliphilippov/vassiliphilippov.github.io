antinomy

noun
contradiction between reasonable principles
7.2 9.9
Pronunciation: /æn.ˈtɪ.nə.miː/ /; / /ˈæn.tɪ.nə.miː/ (AmE), /æn.ˈtɪ.nə.miː/ /; / /ˈæn.tɪ.nə.miː/ (BrE)
A contradiction between two principles or statements that both seem logically reasonable or valid.

Examples

A antinomy in logic
Kant identified an important antinomy in our understanding of the universe.
The legal scholar explored an antinomy that exists between justice and mercy.
The philosopher discussed the antinomy between free will and determinism at great length.
