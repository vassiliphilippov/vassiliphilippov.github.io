brake lining

noun
material on brake shoe in contact with drum
4.9 3.5
Pronunciation: /breɪk ˈlaɪ.nɪŋ/ (AmE), /breɪk ˈlaɪ.nɪŋ/ (BrE)
a layer of material attached to the brake shoe that makes contact with the brake drum to slow or stop a vehicle

Examples

The brake lining material
Dust from the brake lining can accumulate inside the brake drum over time.
If your brake lining is thin, it's crucial to have it checked and possibly replaced.
The mechanic replaced the worn brake lining to ensure the car stopped safely.
