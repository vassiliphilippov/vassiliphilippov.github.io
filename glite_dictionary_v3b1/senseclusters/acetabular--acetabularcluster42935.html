acetabular

adjective
relating to the hip socket
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌæsəˈtæbjələr / (AmE), / ˌæsɪˈtæbjʊlə / (BrE)
relating to the acetabulum, the cup-shaped socket in the pelvis that receives the head of the thigh bone

Examples

Acetabular fracture
A fracture of the acetabular region can complicate hip replacement surgery.
Developmental acetabular dysplasia is a common cause of hip instability in infants.
The surgeon examined the acetabular rim for any signs of damage.
