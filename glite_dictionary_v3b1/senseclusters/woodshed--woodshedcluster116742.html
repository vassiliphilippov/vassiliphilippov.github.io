woodshed

verb
to practice intensely
Pronunciation: / ˈwʊdˌʃɛd / (AmE), / ˈwʊdʃɛd / (BrE)
to practice something, especially music, intensely and privately in order to improve skill

Examples

To woodshed a song
The jazz pianist woodshed for hours each night to master the new piece.
Before the audition, she woodshed every difficult passage until she felt confident.
He woodshed in his garage, determined to improve his guitar solos.

Related Terms

  • antonym: perform - to present or carry out a task, especially in front of an audience
  • synonym: rehearse - to practice something repeatedly in preparation for a performance
