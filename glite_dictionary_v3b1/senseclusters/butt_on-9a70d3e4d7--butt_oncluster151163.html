butt on

verb
lie directly against; adjoin (esp. of boundaries)
9.9 7.2
Pronunciation: /bʌt ɑːn/ /; / /bʌt ɔːn/ /; / /bʌt ən/ (AmE), /bʌt ɒn/ /; / /bʌt ən/ (BrE)
(of land or property) to touch or lie directly against something, especially at a boundary; to adjoin.

Examples

Butt on garden fence
The two gardens butt on at the rear, separated only by a low fence.
The warehouse butts on to a busy railway line, making transport convenient.
Their new property will butt on to the national park, giving them easy access to hiking trails.
