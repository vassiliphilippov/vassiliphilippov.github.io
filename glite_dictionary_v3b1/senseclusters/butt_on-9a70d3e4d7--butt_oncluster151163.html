butt on

verb
lie directly against; adjoin (esp. of boundaries)
9.1 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / bÊŒt É‘n / (AmE), / bÊŒt É’n / (BrE)
(of land or property) to touch or lie directly against something, especially at a boundary; to adjoin.

Examples

Butt on garden fence
The two gardens butt on at the rear, separated only by a low fence.
The warehouse butts on to a busy railway line, making transport convenient.
Their new property will butt on to the national park, giving them easy access to hiking trails.

Synonyms

  • abut
    6.9 0.69/m
    abut is the formal term for land touching directly along a boundary, while butt on sounds less formal and is common in property talk.
  • adjoin
    5.2 3.56/m
    adjoin is a neutral, slightly formal word used in maps and plans, while butt on feels more casual and specific to property.
  • butt
    7.3 0.09/m
    butt is the older verb for land touching another parcel, while butt on is a phrasal variant used in boundary descriptions.
  • butt against
    5.6 0.04/m
    butt against is used when one thing leans or presses on another, while butt on describes pure boundary contact for land.
  • border
    2.3 4.77/m
    border often refers to country or large-area limits on maps, while butt on talks about immediate property edges touching directly.
  • edge
    3.2 0.36/m
    edge is used for the outer line of any area or object, while butt on specifically means land or property touching along a boundary.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abut - to touch along an edge or boundary
  • synonym: adjoin - to be next to or joined with something
