self-fertilized

adjective
fertilized by own pollen or sperm
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlfˈfɜrtəˌlaɪzd /; / ˌsɛlfˈfɝːtəˌlaɪzd / (AmE), / ˌsɛlfˈfəːtɪˌlaɪzd /; / ˌsɛlfˈfɜːtɪlaɪzd / (BrE)
(of a plant or animal) fertilized by its own pollen or sperm, rather than from another individual.

Examples

Self-fertilized plants
Certain flowers are capable of becoming self-fertilized without the help of insects.
The pea plant produced seeds because it was self-fertilized.
These self-fertilized plants maintain genetic uniformity across generations.
Researchers observed that the self-fertilised plants showed less genetic diversity compared to cross-fertilised ones.
Some species of flowers are naturally self-fertilised, which helps them reproduce in the absence of pollinators.
The pea plants in Mendel's experiments were initially self-fertilised for several generations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cross-fertilised - fertilized by pollen from another individual; the opposite process
  • antonym: cross-fertilized - fertilized by pollen or sperm from another individual
