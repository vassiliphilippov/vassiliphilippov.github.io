relax

verb
become less strict or severe
4.2
Pronunciation: / rɪˈlæks / (AmE), / rɪˈlæks / (BrE)
to make rules, restrictions, or demands less strict or severe; to allow more freedom or flexibility

Examples

To relax the rules
After the new policy was introduced, the company decided to relax the dress code for employees.
Safety regulations have begun to relax as the pandemic situation improves worldwide.
When the crisis ended, local authorities chose to relax some of the strict traffic laws.
During emergencies, some safety protocols may be relaxed to speed up operations.
The committee decided to relax the entry requirements to attract more candidates.
The company chose to relax its dress code, allowing employees to wear jeans.
As the negotiations progressed, both sides relaxed their demands.
Don't relax your focus just because the job is almost done.
You can relax the rules a little once everyone understands the process.
