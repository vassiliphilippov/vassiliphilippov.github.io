operate

verb
function; work as intended
2.8 38/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…ëÀê.p…ôÀåre…™t / (AmE), / Àà…íp.…ô.re…™t /; / Àà…íp.…ôÀåre…™t / (BrE)
to make a machine, device, or system work, or to work as intended

Examples

To operate a machine
After years of use, his old laptop still operates smoothly.
If the generator doesn't operate during storms, the hospital could lose power.
The new coffee maker will not operate unless it is plugged in properly.
Never operate unfamiliar appliances without reading the instructions first.
Trained technicians operate the complex equipment in the laboratory.
You must have a license to operate heavy machinery on this site.

Synonyms

  • run
    2.2 135/m
    run means a machine or process is working or being kept working, while operate focuses on making or controlling it work.
  • work
    1.0 393/m
    work means to function or be effective in general, while operate often means to make a machine or system work directly.
  • function
    2.6 35/m
    function means to do a specific role or work in a system, while operate means to make a machine or device run as intended.
  • go
    1.0 274/m
    go can mean a machine starts or moves into action in casual speech, while operate focuses on making it work correctly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: malfunction - fail to work properly
  • synonym: run - to make something function or work, especially machinery
  • synonym: work - perform as expected or intended (machines, devices)
