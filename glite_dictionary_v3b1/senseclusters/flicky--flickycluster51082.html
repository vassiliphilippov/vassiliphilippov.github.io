flicky

adjective
flickering or flashing
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈflɪki / (AmE), / ˈflɪki / (BrE)
tending to flicker, flash, or turn on and off repeatedly, especially describing lights or electronic displays

Examples

Flicky lights
The television screen became flicky during the thunderstorm, making it hard to watch the movie.
She replaced the flicky lamp in her bedroom because it kept turning on and off by itself.
The old computer monitor was annoyingly flicky, so he decided to buy a new one.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flickering - shining or burning with an unsteady or wavering light
  • antonym: steady - not changing or moving; constant and regular
