leave

verb
to go away from or stop being involved with
1.0 507/m
Pronunciation: / liv / (AmE), / liÀêv / (BrE)
to go away from a place, person, or organization; to depart or stop being involved

Examples

To leave a place
After five years with the company, Maria decided to leave and pursue a new opportunity.
Nathan will leave the committee next month after completing his term as chair.
When tensions grew at home, he chose to leave and find his own place to live.
The last bus leaves the city center at midnight.
We had to leave the party early because our child was sick.
What time does your train leave tomorrow morning?
Once the meeting ended, everyone left the conference room quickly.
She was sad to leave her friends at the end of the summer.
We should leave the house by seven o'clock to avoid traffic.

Synonyms

  • exit
    1.0 8.88/m
    exit is to go out of a place or situation, while leave is the general verb for going away or stopping involvement.
  • go away
    1.0 17/m
    go away means to move to another place or person, while leave covers going away or stopping involvement in general.
  • depart
    1.3 13/m
    depart is a more formal word often used for journeys, while leave is used in many everyday or formal situations.
  • withdraw
    2.8 26/m
    withdraw means to remove yourself or stop taking part in a group or activity, while leave is more general.
  • go out
    1.0 46/m
    go out often means leaving to do a specific activity like shopping or socializing, while leave is more general.
  • get out
    1.0 80/m
    get out is an informal or urgent phrase to tell someone to go away quickly, while leave is a neutral general verb.

Related Terms

  • antonym: arrive - to reach a place; to come to a place
  • synonym: depart - to go away, especially in order to start a journey
  • antonym: join - to become a member or part of a group or organization
