pressboard

noun
compressed cellulose board
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛsˌbɔrd / (AmE), / ˈprɛsˌbɔːd / (BrE)
a stiff, dense board made by compressing paper, wood fibers, or cellulose, used in construction, furniture, or as insulation

Examples

A pressboard panel
The technician cut a piece of pressboard to fit inside the electrical panel.
Old transformers often contain layers of pressboard for insulation between components.
They used pressboard sheets to separate the wires and prevent short circuits.
The shelves were made from sturdy pressboard to support the weight of the books.
He replaced the damaged panel with a new piece of pressboard for a smooth finish.
Many inexpensive desks use pressboard instead of solid wood to reduce costs.
The back of the bookshelf was made from pressboard.
She used a sheet of pressboard to reinforce the bottom of the drawer.
Many inexpensive furniture items are constructed with pressboard instead of solid wood.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chipboard - a type of paperboard made from reclaimed paper stock
  • synonym: fiberboard - a board made from compressed wood fibers
  • synonym: fibreboard - a board made from wood or plant fibers pressed together
  • synonym: hardboard - a dense, rigid board made from compressed wood fibers
  • synonym: plywood - a board made from thin layers of wood glued together
