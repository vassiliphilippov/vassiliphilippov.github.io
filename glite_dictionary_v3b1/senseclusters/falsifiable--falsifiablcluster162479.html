falsifiable

adjective
able to be proved false by testing
Pronunciation: / ˌfɔlˈsaɪ.ə.bəl / (AmE), / ˌfɔːlˈsaɪ.ə.bəl / (BrE)
capable of being proven false by experiment or observation; able to be shown to be incorrect under certain conditions.

Examples

A falsifiable hypothesis
A falsifiable hypothesis can be tested through experiments and observations.
Some philosophical claims are not falsifiable, as they cannot be disproved by any evidence.
The theory is considered scientific only if it is falsifiable.
