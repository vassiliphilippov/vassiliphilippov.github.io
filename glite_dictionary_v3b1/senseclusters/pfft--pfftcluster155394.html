pfft

verb
to express disbelief
9.9
Pronunciation: / pfːt / (AmE), / pfːt / (BrE)
to make a short sound by blowing air through the lips, often to show disbelief, dismissal, or mild amusement

Examples

To pfft at ideas
When he heard the unbelievable story, he just pfft and shook his head.
She pfft at the idea that anyone could finish the project in one day.
If you suggest that to him, he'll probably just pfft and ignore it.

Related Terms

  • synonym: scoff - to speak or react with mockery or disbelief
  • synonym: snort - to make a sudden sound through the nose to show amusement or disbelief
