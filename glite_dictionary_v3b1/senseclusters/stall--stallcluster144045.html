stall

noun
compartment for an animal in a stable or barn
Pronunciation: / stɔl /; / stɑl / (AmE), / stɔːl / (BrE)
a small, separated area in a building, such as a compartment for an animal or a private space for study

Examples

A cow stall
Each cow has its own stall in the barn, lined with fresh straw.
He spent the morning cleaning out the stalls before feeding the animals.
The horse was resting comfortably in its stall after a long day of riding.
Each stall was cleaned and filled with fresh hay daily.
She brushed the cow in its stall while the farmer prepared the feed.
The horses were led back to their stall after an afternoon in the field.
Each library stall is equipped with a lamp and power outlet.
Many students reserve a stall during exam season for privacy.
She preferred to study in a stall where she wouldn't be disturbed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: booth - a small temporary stall or stand for selling goods or providing information
  • synonym: cubicle - a small partitioned-off area of a room, such as in an office or library
  • synonym: stable - building where horses are kept
