eat someone's lunch

verb
outperform or outcompete someone
9.4 8.6
Pronunciation: / it sʌmˈwʌnz lʌntʃ / (AmE), / iːt ˈsʌmwʌnz lʌntʃ / (BrE)
(informal) To outperform or outcompete someone, especially by taking away their opportunities or success in business or competition.

Examples

To eat someone's lunch in business
If we don't update our technology, our competitors will eat our lunch next year.
The new startup is so innovative, it might eat our lunch if we aren't careful.
They're worried that cheaper imports could eat their lunch in the domestic market.
