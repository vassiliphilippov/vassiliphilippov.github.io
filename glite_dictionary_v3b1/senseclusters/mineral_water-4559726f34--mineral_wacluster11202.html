mineral water

noun
water with dissolved minerals, often carbonated
2.1
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪnərəl ˌwɔtər / (AmE), / ˈmɪnərəl ˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
Water that contains dissolved mineral salts or gases, either naturally or added artificially, often carbonated and sometimes used therapeutically.

Examples

Mineral water bottle
Many people believe that drinking mineral water is healthier than regular tap water.
She ordered a bottle of mineral water with her lunch.
The spa offers baths filled with natural mineral water from underground springs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sparkling water - carbonated water, often used as a synonym for mineral water
  • antonym: tap water - water supplied through pipes in a building, typically without added minerals
