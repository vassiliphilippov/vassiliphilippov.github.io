nidus

noun
central point of infection or disease
Pronunciation: / ˈnaɪ.dəs / (AmE), / ˈnaɪ.dəs / (BrE)
A central point or focus where infection or disease develops in an organism.

Examples

Nidus of infection
A foreign object under the skin may form a nidus for recurrent abscesses.
Dental plaque can serve as a nidus for bacterial growth in the mouth.
The surgeon located the nidus of the infection and removed the affected tissue.
