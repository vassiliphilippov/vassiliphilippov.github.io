Appaloosa

noun
a breed of horse with a spotted coat
Pronunciation: / ˌæpəˈluːsə / (AmE), / ˌæpəˈluːsə / (BrE)
A hardy breed of saddle horse developed in western North America, known for its distinctive spotted coat, especially on the rump.

Examples

Spotted Appaloosa horse
Many riders appreciate the gentle temperament of their Appaloosa horses.
She owns an Appaloosa with a strikingly spotted coat.
The Appaloosa is admired for its stamina and unique appearance.
