burnout

noun
extreme exhaustion
Pronunciation: / ˈbɜrnaʊt / (AmE), / ˈbɜːnaʊt / (BrE)
a state of extreme physical or mental exhaustion caused by prolonged stress or overwork

Examples

Job burnout
After working twelve-hour days for months, he experienced severe burnout and needed a long break.
Many teachers report feeling burnout by the end of the school year.
Companies are trying to prevent burnout by encouraging employees to take regular vacations.
After months of working late every night, he suffered from burnout and needed a long vacation.
Many teachers report feeling burnout by the end of the school year.
Taking regular breaks can help prevent burnout in high-pressure jobs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fatigue - a feeling of great tiredness
  • antonym: recovery - a return to a normal state of health or strength
