burnout

noun
extreme exhaustion
4.1 14/m
Pronunciation: / Ààb…úrna ät / (AmE), / Ààb…úÀêna ät / (BrE)
a state of extreme physical or mental exhaustion caused by prolonged stress or overwork

Examples

Job burnout
After working twelve-hour days for months, he experienced severe burnout and needed a long break.
Many teachers report feeling burnout by the end of the school year.
Companies are trying to prevent burnout by encouraging employees to take regular vacations.
After months of working late every night, he suffered from burnout and needed a long vacation.
Many teachers report feeling burnout by the end of the school year.
Taking regular breaks can help prevent burnout in high-pressure jobs.

Synonyms

  • mental exhaustion
    6.0 0.05/m
    mental exhaustion is severe tiredness of the mind from long stress, while burnout is a broader, work-related collapse.
  • exhaustion
    3.6 7.22/m
    exhaustion is extreme physical or mental tiredness, while burnout is exhaustion from long stress that often lowers work motivation.
  • mental fatigue
    4.4 0.05/m
    mental fatigue is tiredness after long mental effort and feels temporary, while burnout is deeper and follows prolonged stress or overwork.
  • frazzle
    7.4 0.09/m
    frazzle is an informal word for being worn out and scattered, while burnout is a chronic, work-related collapse from long stress.
