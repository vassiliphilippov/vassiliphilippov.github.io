contemptibly

adverb
in a contemptible manner
Pronunciation: / kənˈtɛmptəbli / (AmE), / kənˈtɛmptəbli / (BrE)
In a manner deserving contempt; in a way that arouses disgust or strong disapproval.

Examples

Act contemptibly rude
He dismissed their concerns contemptibly, without any consideration.
His behavior at the meeting was contemptibly rude and selfish.
She acted contemptibly by spreading false rumors about her colleagues.

Related Terms

  • antonym: admirably - in a manner deserving admiration or praise
  • synonym: despicably - in a manner deserving hatred or contempt
