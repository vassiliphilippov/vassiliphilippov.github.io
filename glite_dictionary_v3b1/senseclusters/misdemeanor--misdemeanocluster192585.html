misdemeanor

noun
minor crime; less serious than a felony
Pronunciation: / ˌmɪsdɪˈmiːnɚ /; / ˌmɪsdɪˈmiːnər / (AmE), / ˌmɪsdɪˈmiːnə / (BrE)
A minor wrongdoing or criminal offense that is less serious than a felony.

Examples

A misdemeanor crime
He was charged with a misdemeanor for trespassing on private property.
Shoplifting is often classified as a misdemeanor rather than a felony.
The court usually imposes a fine rather than jail time for a misdemeanor.
He was charged with a misdemeanour after the incident at the store.
In many cases, traffic violations are classified as a misdemeanour rather than a serious crime.
The judge explained that a misdemeanour carries a lighter penalty than a felony.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: felony - a serious crime typically punishable by imprisonment for more than one year or by death
  • degree lower: infraction - a minor violation of a rule or law, less serious than a misdemeanor
  • synonym: misdemeanor - the American English spelling of 'misdemeanour'
