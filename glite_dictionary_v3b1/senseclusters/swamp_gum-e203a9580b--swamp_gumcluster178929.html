swamp gum

noun
Australian eucalyptus tree
Pronunciation: / swɑmp ɡʌm / (AmE), / swɒmp ɡʌm / (BrE)
A medium-sized eucalyptus tree native to southern Australia, typically found in damp or swampy habitats.

Examples

A swamp gum tree
Many birds make their nests in the branches of a swamp gum.
Researchers studied the growth patterns of swamp gums in Tasmanian wetlands.
The old swamp gum in the paddock provided shade for the cattle.
