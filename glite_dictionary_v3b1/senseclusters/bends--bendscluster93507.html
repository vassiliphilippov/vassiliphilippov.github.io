bends

noun
painful condition from rapid pressure changes (diving)
7.6
Pronunciation: / bɛndz / (AmE), / bɛndz / (BrE)
A painful and potentially dangerous condition caused by rapid changes in air pressure, especially affecting divers, resulting in pain and other symptoms due to nitrogen bubbles forming in the body.

Examples

The bends condition
If not treated promptly, the bends can cause lasting neurological damage.
The diver surfaced too quickly and developed a severe case of the bends.
To avoid the bends, divers must ascend slowly and follow proper decompression procedures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decompression sickness - a medical condition caused by rapid decrease in pressure
