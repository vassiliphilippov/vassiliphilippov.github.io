succession

noun
the act of following in order
9.1
Pronunciation: / səkˈsɛʃən / (AmE), / səkˈsɛʃən / (BrE)
a number of people or things coming one after another in a sequence or order

Examples

A succession of events
A succession of cars passed by the house during the parade.
Day follows night in an endless succession.
The runners finished the race in quick succession.
She experienced a succession of setbacks before finally achieving success.
The king was followed by a succession of weak rulers.
There was a succession of loud knocks at the door late that night.
A succession of vehicles passed by our house in the morning.
He faced a succession of challenges after starting his new job.
The band played a succession of popular songs during the concert.

Related Terms

  • synonym: series - a number of things, events, or people of a similar kind or related nature coming one after another
