stevedore

noun
a port laborer loading ships
9.9 0.27/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàstiÀêv…ôÀåd…îr / (AmE), / ÀàstiÀêv…ôd…îÀê / (BrE)
a person employed at a port to load and unload ships' cargo

Examples

A stevedore at the docks
After twenty years as a stevedore, he knew every inch of the docks.
At sunrise, a team of stevedores began unloading the cargo from the freighter.
The stevedore guided the crane operator as heavy containers were lifted onto the ship.

Synonyms

  • longshoreman
    8.1 0.28/m
    longshoreman is used in American English for people who load ships, while stevedore is the general or older term for the same job.
  • dockworker
    4.2 0.24/m
    dockworker works at the dock loading or unloading ships and other cargo, while stevedore specifically names a ship cargo worker.
  • docker
    4.3 0.07/m
    docker loads and unloads ships at a dock and is a common informal term, while stevedore sounds more formal or traditional.
  • wharfman
    8.1 0.00/m
    wharfman works on a wharf handling cargo and equipment, while stevedore refers broadly to ship cargo workers.
  • dockhand
    6.7 0.10/m
    dockhand does general manual tasks on the dock and often helps crews, while stevedore names the skilled ship cargo worker.
  • loader
    3.2 0.18/m
    loader lifts and moves goods in many places like warehouses or trucks, while stevedore usually works at a ship's cargo.
  • lumper
    9.2 0.00/m
    lumper is a casual laborer who loads trucks or ships for short jobs, while stevedore is a regular port cargo worker.
