lucubrate

verb
write or discourse in a scholarly way
Pronunciation: / ˈluːkjəˌbreɪt / (AmE), / ˈluːkjʊˌbreɪt / (BrE)
To express learned ideas in writing, especially in a detailed, scholarly, or elaborate manner.

Examples

To lucubrate on ideas
Critics admired the way she would lucubrate on complex philosophical concepts in her essays.
He was known to lucubrate for hours, filling page after page with thorough commentary.
The professor would often lucubrate late into the night, preparing detailed lectures for his students.

Related Terms

  • synonym: elaborate - add details to; expand on a subject
