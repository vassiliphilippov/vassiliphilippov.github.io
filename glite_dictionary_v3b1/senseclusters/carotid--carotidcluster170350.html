carotid

noun
neck artery
4.4
Pronunciation: / kəˈrɑːtɪd / (AmE), / kəˈrɒtɪd / (BrE)
either of the two main arteries in the neck that supply blood to the brain

Examples

Check the carotid pulse
The nurse gently pressed the patient's carotid to check for a pulse.
A blockage in the carotid can lead to serious health problems like a stroke.
Doctors often examine the carotid arteries during a physical checkup.

Related Terms

  • synonym: artery - a blood vessel that carries blood away from the heart
  • antonym: vein - a blood vessel that carries blood toward the heart
