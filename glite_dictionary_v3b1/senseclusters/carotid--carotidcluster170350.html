carotid

noun
neck artery
4.4 0.66/m
Pronunciation: / k…ôÀàr…ëÀêt…™d / (AmE), / k…ôÀàr…ít…™d / (BrE)
either of the two main arteries in the neck that supply blood to the brain

Examples

Check the carotid pulse
The nurse gently pressed the patient's carotid to check for a pulse.
A blockage in the carotid can lead to serious health problems like a stroke.
Doctors often examine the carotid arteries during a physical checkup.

Synonyms

  • carotid artery
    3.8 0.67/m
    carotid artery names the main neck vessel supplying blood to the head and neck, while carotid is the shorter term used for those arteries.
  • arteria carotis
    6.0 0.00/m
    arteria carotis is the Latin medical name used in anatomy and reports, while carotid is the common English term for those neck arteries.
