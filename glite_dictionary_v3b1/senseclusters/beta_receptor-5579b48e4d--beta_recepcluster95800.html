beta receptor

noun
type of sympathetic nervous system receptor
5.1 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààbe…™t…ô r…™Àås…õpt…ôr / (AmE), / ÀàbiÀêt…ô r…™Àås…õpt…ô / (BrE)
a receptor on nerve cell membranes in the sympathetic nervous system that responds to specific agents, influencing functions such as vasodilation and increased heart rate

Examples

The beta receptor site
Many asthma medications work by activating the beta receptor to relax airway muscles.
Stimulation of a beta receptor can cause an increase in heart rate.
The doctor explained how blocking the beta receptor helps to reduce high blood pressure.

Synonyms

  • beta-adrenoceptor
    8.1 0.00/m
    beta-adrenoceptor is the technical name used for the sympathetic receptor that responds to beta agents, while beta receptor is a more general, everyday term.
  • beta-adrenergic receptor
    6.5 0.01/m
    beta-adrenergic receptor sits on heart and blood vessel cells and responds to adrenaline, while beta receptor names the sympathetic nerve-membrane receptor.

Related Terms
