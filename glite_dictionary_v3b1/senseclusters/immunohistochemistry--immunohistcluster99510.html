immunohistochemistry

noun
antigen detection in tissue via antibody markers
8.5 9.0
Pronunciation: / ɪˌmjunəˌhɪstoʊkəˈmɪstri / (AmE), / ɪˌmjuːnəˌhɪstəʊkəˈmɪstri / (BrE)
a laboratory technique that detects specific antigens in tissue samples using antibodies tagged with markers such as fluorescent dyes or enzymes

Examples

Immunohistochemistry technique
Advances in immunohistochemistry have improved the accuracy of disease diagnosis.
Researchers rely on immunohistochemistry to study the distribution of proteins in tissues.
The laboratory used immunohistochemistry to identify cancer cells in the biopsy specimen.
