nolo contendere

noun
legal plea: no contest, no admission of guilt
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌnoʊloʊ kənˈtɛndəˌri / (AmE), / ˌnəʊləʊ kənˈtɛndəri / (BrE)
(Law) A plea by which a defendant in a criminal prosecution accepts conviction but does not admit guilt; also called a plea of 'no contest'.

Examples

A nolo contendere plea
A plea of nolo contendere has the same immediate legal effect as pleading guilty.
By entering a nolo contendere plea, the defendant accepted the sentence without admitting fault.
He chose nolo contendere in court to avoid a lengthy trial and further publicity.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: guilty plea - a plea in which the defendant admits guilt to the charge(s)
  • degree lower: not guilty plea - a plea in which the defendant denies the charge(s) or claims innocence
