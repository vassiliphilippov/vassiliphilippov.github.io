aerogel

noun
synthetic ultralight porous material
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɛr.oʊˌdʒɛl / (AmE), / ˈeə.rəʊ.dʒɛl / (BrE)
A synthetic, porous, ultralight material made by replacing the liquid part of a gel with a gas.

Examples

A piece of aerogel
He marveled at how light the piece of aerogel felt in his hand.
Scientists use aerogel to capture space dust particles in scientific experiments.
The insulation properties of aerogel make it ideal for use in extreme environments.
