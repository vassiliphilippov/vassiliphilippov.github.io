back-channel

adjective
conducted secretly or unofficially
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌtʃænəl / (AmE), / ˈbækˌtʃanəl / (BrE)
(of communication, negotiations, etc.) conducted secretly or unofficially, especially to bypass normal procedures or channels.

Examples

A back-channel agreement
A back-channel agreement helped resolve the dispute discreetly.
Important progress was made through back-channel negotiations, without the media's knowledge.
The leaders maintained back-channel communication to avoid public scrutiny.

Related Terms

  • antonym: official - authorized or following established processes
