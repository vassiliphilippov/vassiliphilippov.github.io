communications intelligence

noun
intelligence from foreign communications
1.0
Pronunciation: / kəˌmjunɪˈkeɪʃənz ɪnˈtɛlədʒəns / (AmE), / kəˌmjuːnɪˈkeɪʃənz ɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəns / (BrE)
Technical and intelligence information obtained from the interception and analysis of foreign communications by unintended recipients, especially for military or governmental purposes.

Examples

Communications intelligence analysis
Advancements in technology are changing the field of communications intelligence worldwide.
Military agencies heavily rely on communications intelligence to monitor potential threats.
The discovery was made through classified communications intelligence gathered by government operatives.
