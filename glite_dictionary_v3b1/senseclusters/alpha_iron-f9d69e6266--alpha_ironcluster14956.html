alpha iron

noun
magnetic allotrope of iron
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈælfə ˈaɪɚn / (AmE), / ˈalfə ˈaɪən / (BrE)
the magnetic form of iron that is stable below 906°C

Examples

Alpha iron magnetic form
At room temperature, pure iron exists as alpha iron with a body-centered cubic structure.
The magnetic properties of alpha iron make it important in the production of electrical steels.
The transformation from alpha iron to gamma iron occurs at 906°C.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gamma iron - a non-magnetic allotrope of iron stable at higher temperatures
