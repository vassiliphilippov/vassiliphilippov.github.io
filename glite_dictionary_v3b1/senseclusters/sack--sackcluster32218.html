sack

noun
amount that fills a sack
Pronunciation: / sæk / (AmE), / sæk / (BrE)
a large bag made of paper, plastic, or cloth, used for carrying or storing things; also, the amount that fills such a bag

Examples

A sack of flour
He ordered a sack of flour for the bakery.
The farmer delivered a sack of potatoes to the market.
They bought a sack of rice to last throughout the month.
Please put the vegetables in a separate sack, if you don't mind.
She carried her groceries in a brown paper sack from the store.
The cashier handed him a plastic sack with his items inside.
