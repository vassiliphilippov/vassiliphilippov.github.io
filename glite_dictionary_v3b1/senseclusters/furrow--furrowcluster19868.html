furrow

noun
a slight fold or groove in a surface
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈfɜːroʊ / (AmE), / ˈfʌrəʊ / (BrE)
a slight depression or fold in a smooth surface, especially one caused by movement or pressure

Examples

A furrow in fabric
Ironing the shirt removed every furrow and left the fabric perfectly smooth.
Rainwater collected in the shallow furrows that had formed over time on the old statue.
She noticed a deep furrow between his brows as he concentrated on the puzzle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crease - a line or ridge produced on paper, cloth, etc. by folding, pressing, or crushing
  • synonym: wrinkle - a small line or fold in something, especially fabric or skin
