overleverage

verb
to take on too much debt
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈlɛvərɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈlɛvərɪdʒ / (BrE)
to cause a person, company, or institution to take on more debt than can be managed, making them excessively dependent on borrowed funds

Examples

To overleverage a company
The company became overleveraged after taking out several large loans to fund its expansion.
If you overleverage your investments, you risk losing everything if the market drops.
Many banks failed during the crisis because they were overleveraged and could not repay their debts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overextend - to commit more resources or effort than can be managed
  • antonym: underleverage - to use less debt than is possible or optimal
