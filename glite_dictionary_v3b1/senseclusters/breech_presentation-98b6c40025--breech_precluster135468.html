breech presentation

noun
fetal position where buttocks or feet come first
9.1
Pronunciation: / britʃ ˌprɛzənˈteɪʃən / (AmE), / briːtʃ ˌprɛzənˈteɪʃən / (BrE)
the position of a fetus in which the feet or buttocks are positioned to be delivered first rather than the head

Examples

breech presentation at birth
A breech presentation can complicate natural childbirth and may require a cesarean section.
Doctors often monitor fetal position late in pregnancy to detect any breech presentation.
The midwife recognized a breech presentation during the routine ultrasound examination.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cephalic presentation - fetal position where the head appears first
