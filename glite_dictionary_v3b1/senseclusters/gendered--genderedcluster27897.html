gendered

adjective
associated with gender
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛndərd / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛndəd / (BrE)
having qualities, roles, or characteristics associated with a particular gender, or showing influence or bias based on gender

Examples

Gendered roles
The toys in the store were clearly gendered, with separate sections for boys and girls.
Many languages use gendered nouns to classify objects as masculine or feminine.
Critics argued that the film reinforced gendered stereotypes about careers and family roles.
The language in the advertisement was clearly gendered and favored men over women.
Many traditional job titles are gendered, such as 'fireman' or 'stewardess'.
She noticed that the toys in the store were heavily gendered by color and theme.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gender-neutral - not specific to any gender
  • synonym: sexist - showing prejudice or discrimination based on sex or gender
  • antonym: unbiased - not influenced by personal feelings or prejudice
