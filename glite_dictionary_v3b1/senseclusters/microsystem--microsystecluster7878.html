microsystem

noun
immediate social environment
7.2
Pronunciation: /ˈmaɪ.kroʊ.sɪs.təm/ (AmE), /ˈmaɪ.krəʊ.sɪs.təm/ (BrE)
the immediate social environment, such as family, school, or peers, that directly affects an individual's development and daily life

Examples

Family microsystem
A child's microsystem includes their family, school, and close friends who shape their daily experiences.
Teachers play a significant role in a student's microsystem by influencing learning and social skills.
Changes in the microsystem, such as moving to a new school, can impact a child's development.

Related Terms

  • antonym: macrosystem - the broader cultural or societal context influencing an individual
