echinoderm

noun
marine animal (starfish, sea urchin, etc.) with radial symmetry
6.2 8.0
Pronunciation: / ɪˈkaɪnəˌdɜrm / (AmE), / ɪˈkɪnədɜːm / (BrE)
a marine invertebrate animal of the phylum Echinodermata, such as a starfish or sea urchin, characterized by tube feet and five-part radial symmetry

Examples

A echinoderm animal
A sea urchin, another type of echinoderm, moves slowly along the ocean floor.
Scientists study echinoderms to learn more about marine ecosystems and evolution.
The starfish is an example of an echinoderm found in many ocean habitats.
