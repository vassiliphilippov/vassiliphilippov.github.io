growth regulator

noun
substance controlling plant growth
Pronunciation: / ɡroʊθ ˈrɛɡjəˌleɪtər / (AmE), / ɡrəʊθ ˈrɛɡjʊleɪtə / (BrE)
A natural or synthetic substance in plants that regulates growth and development, typically acting like a hormone.

Examples

A growth regulator hormone
Farmers sometimes apply a growth regulator to modify the size of fruit.
Scientists study how each growth regulator influences plant development under stress.
The growth regulator helped stimulate flowering in the experimental crops.
