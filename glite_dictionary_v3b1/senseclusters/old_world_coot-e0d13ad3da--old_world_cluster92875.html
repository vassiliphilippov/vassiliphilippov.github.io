Old World coot

noun
Eurasian species of coot (bird)
8.0
Pronunciation: / oʊld wɜrld kuːt / (AmE), / əʊld wɜːld kuːt / (BrE)
a species of coot native to Eurasia, known for its white bill and frontal shield

Examples

The Old World coot bird

During migration, flocks of Old World coots gather in large freshwater wetlands.
The Old World coot can often be seen swimming in lakes across Europe and Asia.
