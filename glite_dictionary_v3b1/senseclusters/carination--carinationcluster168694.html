carination

noun
keel-like ridge
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkɛrəˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkærɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the formation or presence of a keel-like ridge on a structure, especially in biology or anatomy

Examples

The carination of leaf

Scientists noted the pronounced carination along the edge of the fossilized shell.
In some birds, the carination of the breastbone is important for flight muscle attachment.
