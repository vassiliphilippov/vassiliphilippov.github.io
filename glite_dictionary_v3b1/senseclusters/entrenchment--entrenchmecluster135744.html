entrenchment

noun
firm establishment
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈtrɛntʃmənt / (AmE), / ɪnˈtrɛntʃmənt / (BrE)
the state of being firmly established and difficult to change, especially in attitudes or practices

Examples

Entrenchment of ideas
The entrenchment of traditional values made reform difficult in the community.
Political entrenchment can prevent new ideas from being accepted.
The entrenchment of outdated policies slowed the company's progress.

Related Terms

  • synonym: entrench - to establish something very firmly so that it is difficult to change
  • antonym: instability - the state of being likely to change or not firmly established
