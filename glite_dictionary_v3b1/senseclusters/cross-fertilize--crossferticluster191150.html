cross-fertilize

verb
cause to undergo cross-fertilization
4.3 5.7
Pronunciation: /krɔːs.ˈfɜːr.tə.laɪz/ (AmE), /krɒs.ˈfɜː.tɪ.laɪz/ (BrE)
to cause or undergo cross-fertilization by mixing genetic material from different individuals or sources

The scientists decided to cross-fertilize the two plant species to observe the characteristics of the hybrids.

Certain insects help flowers to cross-fertilise, leading to increased genetic diversity.
The biologist observed the species as they began to cross-fertilise in the controlled environment.
When two different plant varieties are grown together, they may cross-fertilise naturally in the wild.
