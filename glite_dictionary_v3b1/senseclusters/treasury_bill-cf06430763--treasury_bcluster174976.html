Treasury bill

noun
short-term government security
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɛʒəri bɪl / (AmE), / ˈtrɛʒəri bɪl / (BrE)
A short-term government security sold at a discount and redeemable at full face value on maturity; it does not bear interest and typically matures within a year.

Examples

A Treasury bill maturity
At maturity, you receive the face value of the Treasury bill, which is higher than the purchase price.
He invested in Treasury bills because they are considered low-risk and easily converted to cash.
The government auctioned a new series of Treasury bills to raise short-term funding.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: bond - a long-term debt security issued by governments or corporations
  • synonym: certificate of deposit - a savings certificate with a fixed maturity and interest rate
