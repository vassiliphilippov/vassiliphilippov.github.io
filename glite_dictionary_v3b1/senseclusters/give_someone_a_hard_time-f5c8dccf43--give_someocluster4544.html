give someone a hard time

verb
make things difficult or unpleasant for someone
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɡɪv ˈsʌmwʌn ə hɑrd taɪm / (AmE), / ɡɪv ˈsʌmwən ə hɑːd taɪm / (BrE)
to deliberately make things difficult, unpleasant, or challenging for someone, often by criticizing, nagging, or teasing them

Examples

To give someone a hard time
Don't give your brother a hard time just because he made a mistake.
My boss keeps giving me a hard time about being late to work.
She was giving him a hard time for not returning her call.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hassle - to annoy or harass someone repeatedly
  • antonym: praise - to express approval or admiration
  • synonym: tease - to make fun of or provoke someone
