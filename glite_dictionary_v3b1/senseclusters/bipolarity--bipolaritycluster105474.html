bipolarity

noun
two-power dominance
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪpoʊˈlærəti / (AmE), / ˌbaɪpəʊˈlærəti / (BrE)
a situation in which two major states or alliances dominate the political and military power in the international system

Examples

Global bipolarity
During the Cold War, the world was characterized by bipolarity between the United States and the Soviet Union.
Many political scientists study the effects of bipolarity on global stability and conflict.
Some argue that bipolarity creates a more predictable international environment than multipolarity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: multipolarity - a system where power is distributed among several states or alliances
  • antonym: unipolarity - a system dominated by a single state or alliance
