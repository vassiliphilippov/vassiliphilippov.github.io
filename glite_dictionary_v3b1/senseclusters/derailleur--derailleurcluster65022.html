derailleur

noun
bicycle gear device
7.2
Pronunciation: /də.ˈreɪ.lər/ /; / /dɪ.ˈreɪ.lər/ /; / /də.ˈreɪ.jər/ (AmE), /də.ˈreɪ.lə/ /; / /dɪ.ˈreɪ.lə/ /; / /də.ˈreɪ.jə/ (BrE)
a device on a bicycle that moves the chain between gears to change speed or resistance

Examples

A derailleur device
The cyclist adjusted the derailleur to make shifting between gears smoother.
A damaged derailleur can make it difficult to ride up steep hills.
He learned how to clean and maintain his bike's derailleur after every long ride.
The cyclist replaced the old derailleur to ensure smoother gear changes during the race.
If your derailleur is not aligned properly, shifting gears can become difficult.
Modern mountain bikes often have a rear derailleur for a wide range of gears.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gear shifter - a device for changing gears on a bicycle
  • antonym: hub gear - an internal gear system for bicycles
