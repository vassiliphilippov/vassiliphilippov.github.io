graham

noun
coarse whole wheat flour
Pronunciation: / ˈɡreɪ.əm /; / ˈɡræm / (AmE), / ˈɡreɪ.əm / (BrE)
a type of whole-wheat flour, or a sweet cracker made from it, often used in desserts

Examples

Graham cracker
He prefers graham over white flour for its richer flavor and texture.
The recipe calls for one cup of graham to make the cookies healthier.
Whole wheat and graham are often recommended for high-fiber diets.
She made a cheesecake crust using crushed graham crackers and melted butter.
The recipe calls for one cup of graham flour to give the bread a hearty texture.
Children enjoyed spreading peanut butter on graham crackers for a quick snack.

  • synonym: whole-wheat - made from the entire wheat grain
  • synonym: wholemeal flour - British term for flour made from whole wheat grains
