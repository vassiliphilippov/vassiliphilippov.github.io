nesting

noun
pregnancy home preparation
Pronunciation: / ˈnɛstɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈnɛstɪŋ / (BrE)
a strong urge, especially during late pregnancy, to prepare the home for a new baby by cleaning, organizing, or decorating

Examples

Nesting instinct
Many expectant mothers experience nesting and spend hours organizing the baby's room.
Her sudden burst of nesting led her to clean the entire house before the baby arrived.
During the last month of pregnancy, nesting often becomes a daily activity for some women.

Related Terms

  • synonym: preparing - making something ready for use or for an event
