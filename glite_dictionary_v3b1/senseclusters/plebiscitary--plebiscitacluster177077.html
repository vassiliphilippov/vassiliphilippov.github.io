plebiscitary

adjective
direct popular approval
Pronunciation: / ˌplɛbɪˈsɪtəri / (AmE), / ˌplɛbɪˈsɪtəri / (BrE)
relating to or involving direct approval or voting by the people, especially through referendums or mass votes

Examples

plebiscitary vote
The leader adopted a plebiscitary approach, calling for frequent public votes on major decisions.
Critics argued that the plebiscitary system undermined the role of elected representatives.
A plebiscitary style of government often relies on mass appeals to the population.
The new constitution was approved through a plebiscitary process involving all eligible voters.
Some leaders prefer a plebiscitary approach to major policy decisions to ensure public support.
The reforms were enacted after a plebiscitary vote showed overwhelming approval.

Related Terms

  • synonym: referendum - a direct vote by the people on a specific issue
  • antonym: representative - relating to a system where people elect officials to make decisions for them
