vociferation

noun
loud protest or outcry
Pronunciation: / voʊˌsɪfəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / vəˌsɪfəˈreɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
A loud and forceful utterance, especially one made in protest or opposition.

Examples

A loud vociferation
Her peaceful speech was interrupted by a vociferation from the back of the room.
The politician's comments were met with immediate vociferation from the opposition benches.
The sudden vociferation outside caught everyone's attention.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clamor - a loud and confused noise, especially from a crowd
  • synonym: outcry - a loud shout, often of protest
  • antonym: silence - absence of sound or noise
