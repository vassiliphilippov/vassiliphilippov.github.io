present participle

noun
verb form ending in '-ing'
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈprɛznt ˈpɑrtɪsɪpəl / (AmE), / ˈprɛznt ˈpɑːtɪsɪpl̩ / (BrE)
a verb form expressing ongoing action or state, in English formed by adding '-ing' to the base verb (e.g., 'walking', 'running')

Examples

The present participle form
In the sentence 'She is singing', 'singing' is a present participle.
Some adjectives, like 'interesting', originate from the present participle form of verbs.
You form the present participle by adding '-ing' to the base verb.

Related Terms

  • antonym: past participle - a participle expressing completed action (e.g., 'written', 'broken')
