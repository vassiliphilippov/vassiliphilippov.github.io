excimer

noun
unstable excited molecule
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɛksɪmər / (AmE), / ˈɛksɪmə / (BrE)
a short-lived molecule formed from two atoms or molecules, one of which is in an excited electronic state

Examples

Excimer molecule
The excimer laser is widely used in eye surgery to reshape the cornea.
Scientists studied the properties of the excimer to improve ultraviolet light sources.
An excimer forms only when one atom is in an excited state, making it highly unstable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dimer - a molecule formed from two identical smaller molecules
