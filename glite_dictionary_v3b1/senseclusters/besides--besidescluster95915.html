besides

adverb
in addition; anyway
Pronunciation: / bɪˈsaɪdz / (AmE), / bɪˈsaɪdz / (BrE)
used to add another reason, point, or piece of information; in addition; anyway

Examples

And besides that
He didn't want to join us, and besides, he was too busy with work.
I'm not interested in going for a run; besides, it's raining outside.
The concert was expensive, and besides, it was sold out.
Nobody wanted to leave early; besides, it was still raining outside.
She enjoys hiking and, besides, she volunteers at the local shelter.
The project is challenging and, besides, it pays well.

Related Terms

  • synonym: also - in addition; too
  • synonym: anyway - used to add a point or return to a topic
  • antonym: however - in contrast; on the other hand
  • synonym: moreover - as a further matter; besides
