Thecodontia

noun
extinct reptiles with teeth in sockets
Pronunciation: / θiːˈkoʊdɑːntiə / (AmE), / θiːˈkəʊdɒntiə / (BrE)
an extinct group of prehistoric reptiles, characterized by having teeth set in sockets, that lived from the late Permian to Triassic periods

Examples

Fossil Thecodontia
Fossils of Thecodontia have provided important clues about reptile evolution.
Scientists note that Thecodontia had teeth set in sockets, a trait shared with later archosaurs.
The Thecodontia were dominant terrestrial predators before the rise of dinosaurs.
