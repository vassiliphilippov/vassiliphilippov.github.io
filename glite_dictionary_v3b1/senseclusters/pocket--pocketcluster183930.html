pocket

noun
small hollow or cavity
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑkɪt / (AmE), / ˈpɒkɪt / (BrE)
a small hollow, cavity, or enclosed area within something (such as in rock, air, or an animal's body)

Examples

An air pocket
A pocket of air was detected beneath the ice sheet.
Erosion formed a deep pocket in the sandstone cliff.
The geologist discovered a pocket of gold within the rock formation.
Small pockets of oil were discovered between layers of rock.
The rescuers drilled into a pocket of air to help the trapped miners survive.
There is often a pocket of resistance in remote areas during times of conflict.
A pelican's throat pocket helps it store fish before swallowing.
Some rodents have a cheek pocket where they can keep food for later.
The kangaroo carries its joey in a special pocket on its belly.
