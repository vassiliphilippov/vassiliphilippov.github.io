subliterary

adjective
not intended as literature
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌblɪˈtɛrəri / (AmE), / ˌsʌblɪˈtɛrəri / (BrE)
(of a work or text) not written as or intended to be literature; lacking recognized literary quality.

Examples

Subliterary work
His journal was considered a subliterary piece, valued more for its historical than artistic merit.
Many scholars study subliterary documents to better understand everyday life in the past.
The collection included both subliterary texts like family letters and acclaimed literary works.

Related Terms

  • antonym: literary - related to literature or books
