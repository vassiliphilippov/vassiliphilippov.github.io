indeterminate

adjective
having growth that continues at the apex
6.8 7.6
Pronunciation: / ˌɪndəˈtɝːmənət / (AmE), / ˌɪndɪˈtɜːmɪnət / (BrE)
(of a plant or plant part) having the capacity to continue growing at the apex or tip.

Examples

Indeterminate tomato plant
Some beans have indeterminate vines that keep climbing and flowering for months.
The botanist explained that the indeterminate stems would never stop extending unless pruned.
Tomatoes with indeterminate growth continue producing fruit throughout the season.
