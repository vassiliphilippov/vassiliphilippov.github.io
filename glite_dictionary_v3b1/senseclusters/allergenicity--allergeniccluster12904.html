allergenicity

noun
ability to cause allergy
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˌælɚdʒəˈnɪsəti / (AmE), / ˌælədʒəˈnɪsəti / (BrE)
the ability of a substance to cause an allergic reaction in some people

Examples

The allergenicity of foods
Scientists are studying the allergenicity of genetically modified crops to ensure they are safe for consumption.
The allergenicity of this medication is low, making it suitable for most patients.
Labeling laws require manufacturers to disclose the allergenicity of ingredients in packaged foods.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allergy - a reaction by the immune system to a substance
  • degree higher: immunogenicity - the ability of a substance to provoke an immune response
  • antonym: tolerance - the ability to not react to a substance
