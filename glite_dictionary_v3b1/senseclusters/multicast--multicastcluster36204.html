multicast

verb
to send data to many
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌltɪˌkæst /; / ˈmʌltiˌkæst / (AmE), / ˈmʌltɪˌkɑːst / (BrE)
to send or transmit data to multiple recipients at the same time over a network

Examples

To multicast a video
The company will multicast the training session to all its branch offices simultaneously.
To reduce network traffic, the application can multicast updates to multiple users at once.
Engineers set up the system to multicast video feeds to every classroom in the building.
The server can multicast video streams to several users at once, saving bandwidth.
During the online seminar, the system multicasts the presentation to all registered participants.
To update all devices efficiently, the administrator decided to multicast the software patch.

Related Terms

  • synonym: broadcast - to send data or a message to all recipients on a network
  • antonym: unicast - to send data or a message to a single recipient on a network
