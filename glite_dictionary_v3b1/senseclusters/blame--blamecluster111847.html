blame

noun
responsibility for a fault or wrong
1.0
Pronunciation: / bleɪm / (AmE), / bleɪm / (BrE)
responsibility or censure for a fault, wrong, or bad situation

Examples

Take the blame
After the accident, the driver faced much public blame.
No one wanted to accept blame for the mistake that cost them the contract.
The manager placed the blame for the failure on the entire team.
He accepted the blame for the mistake to protect his colleague.
She refused to take the blame for something she didn't do.
There was much blame assigned after the project failed.
