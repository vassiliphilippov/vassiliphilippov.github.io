hypopyon

noun
pus in eye chamber
Pronunciation: / haɪˈpɑːpiˌɑn / (AmE), / haɪˈpɒpɪən / (BrE)
a collection of pus in the lower part of the front chamber of the eye, usually due to infection or inflammation

Examples

Hypopyon in eye
The ophthalmologist noticed a hypopyon forming in the patient's right eye during the examination.
Treatment for a hypopyon often involves antibiotics to address the underlying infection.
A hypopyon can cause blurred vision and discomfort if not treated promptly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abscess - a swollen area within body tissue containing pus
  • synonym: inflammation - a localized physical condition with redness, swelling, and pain
