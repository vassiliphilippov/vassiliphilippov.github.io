deep

adjective
mysterious or obscure in meaning
1.0
Pronunciation: / dip / (AmE), / diːp / (BrE)
difficult to understand, mysterious, or requiring great intellectual effort

Examples

Deep mystery
He kept a deep secret that had never been revealed to anyone.
The scientist's explanation was so deep that few people understood what he meant.
They shared a deep and mysterious connection that nobody else could explain.
Her explanation of quantum mechanics became so deep that most students could not follow.
His theories are considered deep, requiring significant background knowledge to fully grasp.
The book explores deep philosophical questions that challenge even experienced scholars.
His latest novel is filled with deep philosophical themes.
She was known for her deep understanding of human nature.
The professor encouraged deep reflection on the ethical issues involved.

Related Terms

  • synonym: abstruse - difficult to understand; obscure
  • antonym: clear - easy to perceive, understand, or interpret
  • synonym: inscrutable - impossible to understand or interpret
  • synonym: mysterious - difficult or impossible to understand, explain, or identify
  • synonym: profound - (of thought or knowledge) showing great depth or insight
  • synonym: recondite - (of a subject or knowledge) little known; abstruse
  • antonym: simple - easily understood or done; presenting no difficulty
  • antonym: superficial - lacking depth of thought or feeling; shallow
