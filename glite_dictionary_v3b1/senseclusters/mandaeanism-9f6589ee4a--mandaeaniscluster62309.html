Mandaeanism

noun
Gnostic religion venerating John the Baptist
9.9
Pronunciation: / mænˈdiː.əˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / mænˈdiː.ə.nɪ.zəm / (BrE)
A Gnostic religion that originated in the 2nd and 3rd centuries CE, venerating John the Baptist as the Messiah and combining Jewish and Christian elements within a dualistic belief system.

Examples

Mandaeanism religion
Many refugees who practiced Mandaeanism fled persecution in their homeland.
Scholars have studied the rituals and texts of Mandaeanism to understand its unique dualistic worldview.
The central figure in Mandaeanism is John the Baptist, regarded as the true Messiah.
