blue-green algae

noun
photosynthetic microorganisms, also called cyanobacteria
Pronunciation: / bluːˌɡrin ˈæl.dʒi, ˈæl.ɡi / (AmE), / bluːˌɡriːn ˈæl.dʒiː / (BrE)
A group of mainly freshwater, photosynthetic microorganisms with blue and green pigments, also known as cyanobacteria.

Examples

Blue-green algae blooms
Scientists study blue-green algae for their potential to produce renewable biofuels.
Some types of blue-green algae can produce toxins that are harmful to humans and animals.
The pond turned green because of a bloom of blue-green algae during the hot summer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cyanobacteria - photosynthetic bacteria known as blue-green algae
