half-cock

noun
state of confusion from poor preparation
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhæf.kɑk / (AmE), / ˈhɑːf.kɒk / (BrE)
A state of confusion or disorder caused by insufficient preparation.

Examples

At half-cock start
He always starts projects at half-cock and often has to fix mistakes later.
The meeting was held at half-cock, with no one sure of the agenda.
Their plan went off at half-cock because they hadn't finished the research.

Related Terms

  • antonym: preparedness - the state of being ready for something
