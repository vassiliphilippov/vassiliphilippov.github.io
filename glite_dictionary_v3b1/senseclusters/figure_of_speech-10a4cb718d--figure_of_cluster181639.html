figure of speech

noun
nonliteral or imaginative expression
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪɡjɚ əv spiːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈfɪɡə əv spiːtʃ / (BrE)
an expression in which words are used in a nonliteral or imaginative way to create a particular effect or meaning

Examples

A figure of speech
A common figure of speech is calling someone a 'night owl' to mean they stay up late.
He tends to use more than one figure of speech in every paragraph he writes.
The phrase 'it's raining cats and dogs' is a figure of speech and not meant to be taken literally.

Related Terms

  • synonym: metaphor - a figure of speech comparing two unlike things without using 'like' or 'as'
  • synonym: simile - a figure of speech that compares two things using 'like' or 'as'
