pretraining

noun
initial training phase
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˈtreɪnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌpriːˈtreɪnɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of training a model or person on basic tasks before more advanced learning or tasks

Examples

Pretraining a model
Pretraining is often used in machine learning to help models learn general patterns before specific tasks.
The pretraining phase helps new employees become familiar with company procedures.
Effective pretraining can make advanced learning much easier and more successful.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fine-tuning - the process of making small adjustments after initial training
  • synonym: training - the process of teaching or learning skills or knowledge
