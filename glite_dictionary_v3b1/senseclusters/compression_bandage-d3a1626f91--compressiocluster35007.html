compression bandage

noun
bandage applying pressure to stop bleeding
5.6
Pronunciation: / kəmˈprɛʃən ˈbændɪdʒ / (AmE), / kəmˈprɛʃ(ə)n ˈbændɪdʒ / (BrE)
a bandage designed to stop or control bleeding by applying pressure to a wound, especially over an artery

Examples

A compression bandage stops bleeding
After the accident, a compression bandage was used to stop blood loss until help arrived.
Learning how to apply a compression bandage is an important first aid skill.
The nurse quickly wrapped a compression bandage around his leg to control the bleeding.
