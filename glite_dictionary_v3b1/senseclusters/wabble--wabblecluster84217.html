wabble

verb
to be indecisive
Pronunciation: / ˈwæbəl / (AmE), / ˈwæbəl / (BrE)
to be undecided or to change repeatedly between different opinions or choices

Examples

To wabble on choices
He tends to wabble whenever he faces a difficult decision.
The committee continued to wabble between two possible locations for the event.
If you wabble too long, you might miss the opportunity entirely.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decide - to make a clear choice or judgment
  • synonym: hesitate - to pause before deciding or acting
  • synonym: waver - to be uncertain or change between different choices
