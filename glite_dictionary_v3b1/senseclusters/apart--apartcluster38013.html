apart

adverb
excluding from consideration
1.0 17/m
Pronunciation: / …ôÀàp…ërt / (AmE), / …ôÀàp…ëÀêt / (BrE)
Used to show that something is excluded from consideration; except for or aside from.

Examples

Apart from school
Apart from the weather, we had a wonderful holiday.
All financial difficulties apart, the project was a huge success.
All jokes apart, you really should see a doctor.

Synonyms

  • other than
    1.0 17/m
    other than is a common phrase for exceptions in speech and writing, while apart often appears as 'apart from' in use.
  • except
    4.1 0.15/m
    except is the usual word to leave something out in lists or rules, while apart often marks an exception in everyday speech.
  • aside
    4.1 26/m
    aside is used after a clause to exclude something from view, while apart usually appears as 'apart from' to mean except.
  • beside
    1.0 2.29/m
    beside can mean physically next to or, in some uses, 'apart from', while apart mainly signals exclusion or exception.
