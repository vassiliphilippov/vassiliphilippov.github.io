Aspergillus

noun
genus of common molds
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˌæspɚˈdʒɪləs /; / ˌæspərˈdʒɪləs / (AmE), / ˌaspəˈdʒɪləs / (BrE)
a genus of common molds, some species of which can spoil food or cause disease in plants and animals

Examples

Aspergillus mold species
Aspergillus species are often found growing on damp walls and decaying food.
Some strains of Aspergillus can produce dangerous mycotoxins in stored grain.
The infection was caused by inhaling spores of Aspergillus.
Exposure to Aspergillus spores can cause respiratory problems in immunocompromised patients.
Some species of Aspergillus are used in the production of soy sauce and sake.
The laboratory confirmed the presence of Aspergillus in the contaminated peanut butter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Penicillium - another genus of common molds with medical and industrial importance
