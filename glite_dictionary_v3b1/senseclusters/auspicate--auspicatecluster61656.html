auspicate

verb
initiate with the intent to bring good luck
Pronunciation: / ˈɔːspɪˌkeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɔːspɪkeɪt / (BrE)
to begin or inaugurate (an activity or event) in a way believed to bring good luck

Examples

To auspicate a ceremony
Some ancient societies would auspicate military campaigns with elaborate rituals.
The couple decided to auspicate their marriage by planting a tree in their garden.
They auspicated the opening of the new store by breaking a bottle of wine at the entrance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inaugurate - to begin or introduce (a system, policy, or period)
