crape fern

noun
New Zealand fern with woolly stalks
7.1
Pronunciation: / kreɪp fɝn / (AmE), / kreɪp fɜːn / (BrE)
A fern native to New Zealand with pinnate leaves and densely woolly stalks, sometimes classified under the genus Todea.

Examples

The crape fern plant
Botanists often study the crape fern for its unique woolly stalks.
The crape fern thrives in the moist forests of New Zealand.
We saw several large crape ferns near the edge of the river.
