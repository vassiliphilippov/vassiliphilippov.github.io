lanthanoid

noun
rare earth element, lanthanide series (elements 57–71)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlænθəˌnɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈlanθənɔɪd / (BrE)
any of the fifteen rare earth elements from lanthanum to lutetium with atomic numbers 57 to 71, typically found together in ores.

Examples

Rare lanthanoids
Industrial processes often extract lanthanoids from mineral ores found in China.
Neodymium, a lanthanoid, is used in high-performance speakers and headphones.
The lanthanoid elements are crucial in the manufacture of strong permanent magnets.
