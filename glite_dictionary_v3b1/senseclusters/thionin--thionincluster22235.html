thionin

noun
plant defense protein
Pronunciation: / ˈθaɪ.ə.nɪn / (AmE), / ˈθaɪ.ə.nɪn / (BrE)
a small, cysteine-rich protein found in plants that is toxic to microbes and insects and helps defend the plant

Examples

Plant thionin protein
Scientists discovered that thionin helps protect plants from harmful bacteria and fungi.
The wheat variety produced more thionin, making it more resistant to insect attacks.
Researchers are studying how thionin can be used to develop natural pesticides.
