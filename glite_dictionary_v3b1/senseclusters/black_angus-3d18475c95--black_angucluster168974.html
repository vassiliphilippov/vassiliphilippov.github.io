Black Angus

noun
hornless black cattle breed from Scotland
Pronunciation: / blæk ˈæŋɡəs / (AmE), / blak ˈaŋɡəs / (BrE)
A breed of black, hornless cattle originating from Scotland, valued for its high-quality beef.

Examples

The Black Angus cattle
At the livestock auction, the prized Black Angus fetched the highest bid of the day.
Ranchers prefer raising Black Angus cattle because of their gentle nature and excellent beef quality.
The steakhouse is famous for its juicy Black Angus steaks, cooked to perfection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Angus - a breed of cattle from Scotland, including both black and red varieties
