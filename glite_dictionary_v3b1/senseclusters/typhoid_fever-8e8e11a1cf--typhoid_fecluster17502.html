typhoid fever

noun
infectious disease caused by Salmonella typhi
2.7
Pronunciation: / ˈtaɪˌfɔɪd ˈfiːvɚ / (AmE), / ˈtaɪ.fɔɪd ˈfiː.və / (BrE)
a serious infectious disease causing intestinal inflammation and ulceration, usually spread by contaminated food or water, caused by Salmonella typhi

Examples

Typhoid fever infection
After weeks of high fever and stomach pain, the doctor diagnosed her with typhoid fever.
The outbreak of typhoid fever was traced back to contaminated drinking water in the village.
Vaccination is recommended for travelers to areas where typhoid fever is common.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cholera - bacterial infection causing severe diarrhea and dehydration
  • degree lower: paratyphoid fever - similar infection caused by a related bacterium, usually less severe
