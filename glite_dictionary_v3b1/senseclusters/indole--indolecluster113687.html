indole

noun
a heterocyclic organic compound used in perfume and medicine
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌdoʊl / (AmE), / ˈɪndəʊl / (BrE)
a white or yellowish crystalline organic compound with a distinctive odor, found in coal tar and produced by some bacteria, used in perfumery, medicine, and as a flavoring agent

Examples

Indole compound
Indole is a key ingredient in some high-end perfumes due to its complex scent.
Several bacteria in the human gut produce indole as a metabolic byproduct.
The laboratory synthesized pure indole for use in pharmaceutical research.
