Nicotiana rustica

noun
South American/Mexican tobacco plant species
9.9
Pronunciation: / nɪˌkoʊʃiˈænə ˈrʌstɪkə / (AmE), / nɪˌkəʊtɪˈɑːnə ˈrʌstɪkə / (BrE)
a species of tobacco plant native to South America and Mexico, often used for its high nicotine content

Examples

Nicotiana rustica plant
Indigenous peoples traditionally grew Nicotiana rustica for shamanic rituals and healing ceremonies.

The leaves of Nicotiana rustica contain much higher nicotine levels than ordinary tobacco.
