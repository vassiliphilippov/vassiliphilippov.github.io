gainst

preposition
archaic 'against'
Pronunciation: / ɡeɪnst / (AmE), / ɡeɪnst / (BrE)
an old or poetic form of 'against', meaning in opposition to or in contact with

Examples

Stand gainst the wind
He stood gainst the wind, refusing to move.
The knight fought bravely gainst his enemies on the battlefield.
She leaned gainst the old oak tree, lost in thought.
