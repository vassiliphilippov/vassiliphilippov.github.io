split infinitive

noun
infinitive with a word between 'to' and the verb
Pronunciation: / splɪt ɪnˈfɪnətɪv / (AmE), / splɪt ɪnˈfɪnɪtɪv / (BrE)
A construction in which an adverb or other word is placed between ‘to’ and the verb of an infinitive, as in ‘to boldly go’.

Examples

A split infinitive example
Many traditional grammar guides advise against using a split infinitive.
The famous phrase 'to boldly go' is an example of a split infinitive.
Writers sometimes use a split infinitive for emphasis or clarity in a sentence.
