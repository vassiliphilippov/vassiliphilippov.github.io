philosophy

noun
personal belief or guiding principle
2.7 3.6
Pronunciation: /fə.ˈlɑː.sə.fiː/ /; / /fɪ.ˈlɑː.sə.fiː/ (AmE), /fə.ˈlɒs.ə.fiː/ /; / /fɪ.ˈlɒs.ə.fiː/ (BrE)
a set of beliefs or guiding principles about how to live or act, held by a person or group

Examples

Personal philosophy
Her philosophy is to treat everyone with respect, no matter their background.
Steve's parenting philosophy emphasizes independence and responsibility.
The company adopted a new philosophy of putting customers first in all decisions.
His personal philosophy emphasizes honesty, hard work, and respect for others.
Many schools have their own unique educational philosophy that shapes their teaching methods.
The company has a strict philosophy of putting the customer first in all situations.
