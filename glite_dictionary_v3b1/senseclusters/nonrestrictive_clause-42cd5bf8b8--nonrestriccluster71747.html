nonrestrictive clause

noun
extra-information clause in a sentence
6.0 5.5
Pronunciation: /nɑːn.rɪ.ˈstrɪk.tɪv klɔːz/ (AmE), /nɒn.rɪ.ˈstrɪk.tɪv klɔːz/ (BrE)
A clause that adds extra information to a sentence but does not limit or define the noun it modifies.

Examples

Nonrestrictive clause with commas
Nonrestrictive clauses are usually set off by commas.
A nonrestrictive clause can add details to a sentence without changing the fundamental meaning.
The phrase 'which was painted red' in the sentence 'The car, which was painted red, belongs to my brother' is a nonrestrictive clause.

Related Terms

  • antonym: restrictive clause - clause that limits the meaning of the noun it modifies
