advantage

verb
put in a favorable position (verb)
Pronunciation: / ədˈvæn.t̬ɪdʒ /; / ədˈvæntɪdʒd / (AmE), / ədˈvɑːn.tɪdʒ /; / ədˈvɑːntɪdʒd / (BrE)
to give someone or something a more favorable position or benefit

Examples

To advantage a team
Some policies may advantage certain groups over others.
The new schedule is designed to advantage early risers.
This tax system clearly advantages high-income earners.
The new tax policy advantaged large corporations over small businesses.
Scholarship programs are designed to advantage students from underrepresented backgrounds.
The changes in the rules advantaged experienced players in the competition.
