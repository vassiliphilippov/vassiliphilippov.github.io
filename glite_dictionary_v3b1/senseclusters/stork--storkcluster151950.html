stork

noun
large wading bird with long legs and bill
9.9
Pronunciation: / stɔrk / (AmE), / stɔːk / (BrE)
A large wading bird, chiefly of the Old World, typically with long legs, a long bill, and white-and-black plumage.

Examples

A stork bird
Several storks migrated south as autumn approached.
The legend says a stork delivers newborn babies to their families.
We were lucky enough to spot a stork building its nest near the riverbank.
