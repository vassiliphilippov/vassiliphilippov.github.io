scarify

verb
make shallow cuts in a surface
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈskær.əˌfaɪ / (AmE), / ˈskar.ɪ.faɪ / (BrE)
to scratch or make shallow cuts in the surface of something, especially to improve growth or absorption

Examples

To scarify seeds
Before planting, it is often necessary to scarify the seeds to promote germination.
Gardeners recommend that you scarify your lawn every spring to allow air and water to reach the roots.
The technician scarified the surface of the soil to help the new grass take root.

  • synonym: scratch - to make a mark or cut with something sharp
