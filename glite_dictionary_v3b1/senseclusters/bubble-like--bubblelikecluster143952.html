bubble-like

adjective
unsustainable rapid growth
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌbəl laɪk / (AmE), / ˈbʌbəl laɪk / (BrE)
showing rapid, unsustainable growth and risk of sudden collapse, similar to a speculative economic bubble

Examples

Bubble-like growth
The sudden surge in cryptocurrency prices seemed bubble-like to many financial experts.
Investors grew cautious as the market's bubble-like behavior reminded them of past crashes.
Some analysts described the tech sector's rapid expansion as dangerously bubble-like.

Related Terms

  • synonym: speculative - involving high risk and potential for sudden loss
  • antonym: stable - not likely to change or fail suddenly
