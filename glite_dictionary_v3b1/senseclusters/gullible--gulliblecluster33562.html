gullible

adjective
easily deceived or tricked
5.7 4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈɡʌləbəl / (AmE), / ˈɡʌlɪbəl / (BrE)
easily persuaded to believe something; easily deceived or tricked (because of being too trusting)

Examples

A gullible person
Because she is gullible, her friends often play harmless pranks on her.
He was so gullible that he believed every email scam he encountered.
Tourists can be gullible targets for street vendors selling fake goods.
Don’t be so gullible—always check the facts before believing a rumor.
Many gullible people lost money in the internet scam.
The magician amazed the gullible crowd with simple tricks.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cynical - distrustful of human sincerity or integrity; skeptical
  • synonym: naive - lacking experience, wisdom, or judgment; easily trusting
  • antonym: suspicious - having or showing distrust of someone or something
