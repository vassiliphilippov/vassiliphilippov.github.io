absolute

adjective
perfect or complete; pure
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈæbsəˌluːt /; / ˈæbsəˌlut / (AmE), / ˈabsəluːt /; / ˈæbsəluːt / (BrE)
complete, total, or perfect; used to emphasize the highest degree of something

Examples

Absolute certainty
Her absolute loyalty to her friends is admired by everyone.
This wine is made from absolute alcohol of the highest quality.
We need absolute silence during the performance.
Calling it an absolute disaster would be an understatement.
She has absolute trust in her closest friends.
The judge granted him absolute freedom to make decisions.
