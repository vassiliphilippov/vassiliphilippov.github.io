far-fetched

adjective
improbable, hard to believe
5.5 4.8
Pronunciation: /ˈfɑːr.fetʃt/ (AmE), /ˈfɑː.fetʃt/ (BrE)
(of an idea, story, or explanation) very unlikely and difficult to believe; exaggerated or unrealistic

Examples

A far-fetched idea
She dismissed his explanation as too far-fetched to be true.
Some of his theories sound far-fetched, but they are supported by evidence.
The plot of the movie was so far-fetched that I couldn't take it seriously.
Although the movie's plot was entertaining, some scenes were simply too far-fetched to be believable.
Her story about being late sounded a bit far-fetched to everyone in the office.
The scientist dismissed the hypothesis as too far-fetched to be taken seriously.
Her excuse for being late sounded rather far-fetched to the teacher.
Some of the movie's plot twists were just too far-fetched to take seriously.
The story about aliens stealing his homework is clearly far-fetched.
