bile salt

noun
compound involved in lipid emulsification
7.4 3.9
Pronunciation: /baɪl sɔːlt/ /; / /baɪl sɑːlt/ (AmE), /baɪl sɒlt/ /; / /baɪl sɔːlt/ (BrE)
A salt formed from a bile acid and a base, which acts as an emulsifier of lipids and fatty acids in the digestive system.

Examples

Bile salts in digestion
A deficiency in bile salts can lead to poor lipid absorption and digestive problems.
Doctors sometimes supplement patients with bile salts after gallbladder removal surgery.
The liver produces bile salts that aid in the digestion of fats.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bile acid - any of a group of steroid acids found in bile
  • synonym: emulsifier - a substance that helps mix two substances such as oil and water
