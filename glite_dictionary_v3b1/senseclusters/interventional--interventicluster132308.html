interventional

adjective
clinical study type
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈvɛnʃənəl / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈvɛnʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to actions, procedures, or treatments that actively try to change or improve a situation, especially in medicine

Examples

Interventional study
The hospital conducted an interventional study to evaluate the new drug's safety.
Researchers compared the results of interventional and observational trials in their report.
An interventional approach was chosen to directly test the effectiveness of the therapy.
The hospital introduced new interventional procedures to improve patient outcomes.
Many experts support interventional policies to address climate change more effectively.
The city council debated whether interventional strategies would help reduce homelessness.
The hospital specializes in interventional cardiology to treat heart conditions without open surgery.
Advances in interventional radiology have made many procedures less invasive and safer for patients.
Her doctor recommended an interventional approach to manage the chronic pain more effectively.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diagnostic - relating to identifying a disease or condition
  • synonym: intervening - taking action to become involved in a situation
  • antonym: observational - relating to studies where researchers only observe without intervening
  • antonym: passive - not actively taking action or influencing events
  • synonym: preventive - intended to stop something from happening
  • synonym: therapeutic - relating to the treatment of disease or disorders
