pluck

verb
remove quickly by pulling
3.4 3.5
Pronunciation: / plʌk / (AmE), / plʌk / (BrE)
to pull or remove something quickly and sharply, especially with the fingers (such as a flower, fruit, or splinter)

Examples

To pluck a flower
A nurse had to pluck a splinter from his finger.
He tried to pluck the weed out of the garden bed.
She reached out to pluck a ripe apple from the tree.
Gently, she plucked a rose from the garden.
He plucked a hair from his sweater before stepping outside.
The musician plucked the guitar strings, filling the room with soft melodies.
Children eagerly plucked ripe strawberries from the plants in the garden.
He leaned down to pluck a mushroom from under the tree.
She stopped by the roadside to pluck some wildflowers for her kitchen table.

  • synonym: pull - to exert force in order to move something toward oneself
