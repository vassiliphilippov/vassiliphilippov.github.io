uranium

noun
radioactive chemical element (U)
4.8
Pronunciation: / jʊˈreɪniəm / (AmE), / jʊəˈreɪniəm / (BrE)
a heavy radioactive silvery-white metallic element (symbol U), used as a fuel in nuclear reactors and in the manufacture of nuclear weapons.

Examples

Enriched uranium fuel
Many nuclear power plants rely on enriched uranium as a fuel source.
Scientists carefully handle uranium due to its radioactive properties.
The discovery of uranium revolutionized the development of nuclear energy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: plutonium - a radioactive metallic element used in nuclear reactors and weapons
  • synonym: thorium - a radioactive metallic element similar to uranium
