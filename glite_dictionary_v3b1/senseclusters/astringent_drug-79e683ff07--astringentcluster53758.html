astringent drug

noun
drug causing tissue contraction
7.7
Pronunciation: / əˈstrɪndʒənt drʌɡ / (AmE), / əˈstrɪndʒənt drʌɡ / (BrE)
a drug that causes the contraction of body tissues and canals, used to reduce bleeding or discharge

Examples

An astringent drug treatment
An astringent drug can be useful in treating certain types of skin irritation.
Some over-the-counter creams contain an astringent drug to help reduce inflammation.
The doctor applied an astringent drug to stop the minor bleeding.
