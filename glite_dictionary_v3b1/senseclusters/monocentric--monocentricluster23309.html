monocentric

adjective
single-centered
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːnoʊˈsɛntrɪk / (AmE), / ˌmɒnəʊˈsɛntrɪk / (BrE)
having a single center or central point where things are concentrated (such as in a city, lens, or geometric object)

Examples

Monocentric city layout
The city is monocentric, with most businesses and offices located downtown.
Urban planners debated whether a monocentric structure was best for future growth.
A monocentric region often faces traffic congestion near its central hub.
The scientist explained that a monocentric lens reduces distortion in optical instruments.
In geometry, a monocentric sphere has all its surfaces centered at the same point.
The design called for a monocentric structure to ensure perfect symmetry.
