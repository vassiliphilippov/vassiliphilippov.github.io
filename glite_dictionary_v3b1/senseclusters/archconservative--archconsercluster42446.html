archconservative

noun
extreme traditionalist
Pronunciation: / ˌɑːrtʃkənˈsɜːrvətɪv / (AmE), / ˌɑːtʃkənˈsɜːvətɪv / (BrE)
a person who holds extremely traditional or conservative views, especially in politics or social issues

Examples

An archconservative leader
The new law was strongly opposed by every archconservative in the legislature.
As an archconservative, he resisted any changes to the traditional school curriculum.
Many archconservatives believe that society should return to older values and customs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: progressive - a person who supports social or political change and reform
  • synonym: reactionary - a person who opposes political or social progress or reform
