aliphatic compound

noun
organic compound with open chains
Pronunciation: / ˌælɪˈfætɪk kəmˈpaʊnd / (AmE), / ˌælɪˈfatɪk kəmˈpaʊnd / (BrE)
an organic compound whose carbon atoms form open chains (not aromatic rings), such as alkanes, alkenes, and alkynes

Examples

An aliphatic compound chain
Hexane is an example of an aliphatic compound with a straight-chain structure.
Many fuels and solvents are made from aliphatic compounds found in petroleum.
Unlike aromatic compounds, aliphatic compounds do not contain benzene rings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: aromatic compound - organic compound containing a benzene ring or similar ring structure
