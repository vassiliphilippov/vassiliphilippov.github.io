Russification

noun
making more Russian
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˌrʌsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌrʌsɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making people, places, or things more Russian in language, culture, or identity, often through official policies

Examples

Russification policy
The government implemented a policy of Russification in schools to promote the Russian language.
Many local traditions disappeared as a result of Russification during the 19th century.
Some communities resisted Russification to preserve their own cultural identity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Americanization - the process of making something more American in character or culture
  • synonym: assimilation - the process of becoming similar to another group, especially culturally
  • antonym: diversification - the process of making something more diverse or varied
