An abbreviation for 'Before the Common Era,' used to mark years before the start of the Common Era, often preferred for its secular tone.

Examples

Year B.C.E.
Alexander the Great died in 323 B.C.E., marking the end of an era.
Historians believe the artifact dates back to 500 B.C.E..
The temple was built in the third century B.C.E. and still stands today.

Synonyms
