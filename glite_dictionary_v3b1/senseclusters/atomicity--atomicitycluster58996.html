atomicity

noun
number of atoms
Pronunciation: / əˌtɑːˈmɪsəti / (AmE), / əˌtɒˈmɪsəti / (BrE)
the number of atoms present in a molecule of a chemical substance

Examples

Atomicity of a molecule
The atomicity of oxygen gas is two, as each molecule contains two oxygen atoms.
Chemists use atomicity to describe how many atoms are in a single molecule of a substance.
Hydrogen has an atomicity of two because its molecules are made up of two hydrogen atoms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: molecule - a group of atoms bonded together
  • synonym: valency - the combining power of an element, especially as measured by the number of hydrogen atoms it can displace or combine with
