hardness

noun
rigidity; resistance to pressure or scratching
4.8 0.85/m
Pronunciation: / Ààh…ërd.n…ôs / (AmE), / Ààh…ëÀêd.n…™s / (BrE)
the quality or state of being firm or rigid and resistant to pressure, especially the degree to which a material is difficult to scratch or dent, as measured by the Mohs scale.

Examples

Mohs hardness scale
Geologists used the Mohs scale to determine the hardness of the mineral sample.
The hardness of diamond makes it ideal for cutting other materials.
Water with high hardness can cause scale buildup in pipes and appliances.

Related Terms

  • antonym: softness - the quality of being easily penetrated, bent, or scratched; opposite of hardness
