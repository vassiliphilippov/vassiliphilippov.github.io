contrastiveness

noun
degree of difference
7.5
Pronunciation: / kənˈtræstɪvnəs / (AmE), / kənˈtrɑːstɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality or degree of being different or distinct when compared to something else

Examples

Contrastiveness of colors
The contrastiveness of the two paintings made them stand out in the gallery.
Designers often use contrastiveness in colors to draw attention to important information.
The contrastiveness between her calm voice and his loud tone was striking during the debate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: distinctiveness - the quality of being clearly different from others
  • antonym: similarity - the state of being alike or having common features
