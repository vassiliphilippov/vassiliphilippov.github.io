dissilience

noun
the bursting open of seed pods
9.9
Pronunciation: / dɪˈsɪliəns / (AmE), / dɪˈsɪliəns / (BrE)
(in botany) the bursting open of seed pods to release seeds when they are ripe.

Examples

Seed pod dissilience
A sudden change in humidity can trigger the dissilience of certain legumes.
Some plants rely on dissilience to efficiently disperse their seeds in the wild.
The dissilience of the poppy pods released thousands of seeds onto the ground.
