biparentally

adverb
from both parents
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪpəˈrɛntəli / (AmE), / ˌbaɪpəˈrɛntəli / (BrE)
in a manner involving or derived from both parents

Examples

Biparentally inherited genes
In humans, most genetic traits are inherited biparentally from both the mother and the father.
The study found that the disease is transmitted biparentally rather than from a single parent.
Some plants reproduce biparentally, combining genetic material from two different individuals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: uniparentally - in a manner involving or derived from only one parent
