inculturation

noun
religious adaptation to culture
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪn.kʌl.tʃə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ɪn.kəl.tʃə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɪn.kʌl.tʃə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ /; / /ɪn.kəl.tʃə.ˈreɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process by which a religion adapts its practices and expressions to fit a particular culture, especially in Christianity

Examples

Religious inculturation
The church promoted inculturation by including local music and art in its ceremonies.
Successful inculturation can help religious practices feel more meaningful to local communities.
Debates about inculturation often arise when new customs are introduced into traditional worship.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acculturation - the process of adopting the cultural traits of another group
  • synonym: assimilation - the process of becoming similar to another culture
