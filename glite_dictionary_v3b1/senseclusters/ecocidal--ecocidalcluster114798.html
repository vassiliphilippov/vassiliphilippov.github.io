ecocidal

adjective
environmentally destructive
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˌiːkoʊˈsaɪdəl / (AmE), / ˌiːkəʊˈsaɪdəl / (BrE)
causing or relating to severe, large-scale destruction of the natural environment

Examples

Ecocidal damage
The court found the company's actions to be ecocidal due to the irreversible harm caused to the river ecosystem.
Many activists warn that large-scale deforestation is an ecocidal practice threatening global biodiversity.
The new law aims to hold corporations accountable for any ecocidal activities that damage the environment.
The company's ecocidal practices led to widespread deforestation in the region.
Many activists condemned the dam project as ecocidal due to its impact on wildlife.
New laws aim to prevent ecocidal actions that threaten the planet's future.
