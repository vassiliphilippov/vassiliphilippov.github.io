anthropomorphous

adjective
having human form or traits
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˌænθrəpəˈmɔrfəs / (AmE), / ˌanθrəpəˈmɔːfəs / (BrE)
Having the form or characteristics of a human being, especially as applied to animals or objects.

Examples

Anthropomorphous animal shape
He described the cloud formation as anthropomorphous, seeing faces and gestures within it.
Some cartoons feature anthropomorphous animals who speak and behave like people.
The ancient statue had an anthropomorphous shape, resembling a human figure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: anthropomorphic - attributing human characteristics to non-human things
