pickaback plant

noun
herb with plantlets growing on leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪk.əˌbæk plænt / (AmE), / ˈpɪk.ə.bæk plɑːnt / (BrE)
a perennial herb (Tolmiea menziesii) native to western North America, with erect racemes of flowers and a unique trait where new plantlets develop where the leaf blade meets the leafstalk

Examples

The pickaback plant leaflets
Botanists admire the pickaback plant for its ability to self-propagate through leaf plantlets.
She added a flourishing pickaback plant to her indoor collection for its unusual growth habit.
The pickaback plant produces tiny plantlets at the base of its leaves, making it easy to propagate.
