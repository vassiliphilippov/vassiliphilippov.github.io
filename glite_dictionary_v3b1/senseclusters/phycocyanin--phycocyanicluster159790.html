phycocyanin

noun
blue pigment in algae
4.3
Pronunciation: / ˌfaɪkoʊˈsaɪənɪn / (AmE), / ˌfʌɪkəʊˈsʌɪənɪn / (BrE)
a blue pigment-protein complex found in certain algae, especially cyanobacteria, that is involved in photosynthesis

Examples

Blue phycocyanin pigment
Researchers are studying phycocyanin for its potential antioxidant properties.
Scientists isolated phycocyanin from the blue-green algae for laboratory analysis.
The deep blue color in some lakes is due to the presence of phycocyanin produced by cyanobacteria.
