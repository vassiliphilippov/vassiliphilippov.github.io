emissary vein

noun
vein connecting intracranial and extracranial veins
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɛmɪˌsɛri veɪn / (AmE), / ˈɛmɪsəri veɪn / (BrE)
a vein in the scalp and head that connects the venous sinuses inside the skull with veins outside the skull, allowing blood to drain between these vessels

Examples

The emissary vein in scalp
During surgery, the surgeon carefully avoided damaging the emissary vein behind the ear.
Infections can spread from the scalp to the brain through an emissary vein.
The anatomy textbook included a diagram of a typical emissary vein connecting cranial sinuses to external veins.
