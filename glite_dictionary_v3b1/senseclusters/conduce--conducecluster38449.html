conduce

verb
to contribute to a result or situation
9.0 0.15/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàdus / (AmE), / k…ônÀàdjuÀês / (BrE)
to help to bring about a particular result or situation; to contribute to something

Examples

To conduce to success
A quiet environment can conduce to better concentration during study sessions.
Regular exercise conduces to good health and longevity.
Their efforts conduced greatly to the success of the project.

Synonyms

  • make for
    3.7 8.78/m
    make for helps cause a particular result in everyday speech, while conduce is more formal and means to contribute.
  • contribute
    2.7 102/m
    contribute means giving something or effort to help a result, whereas conduce focuses on helping bring that result about.
  • lead
    3.2 200/m
    lead often shows a direct cause and clear outcome, while conduce suggests a helping role that supports a result.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contribute - to help to cause or bring about something
  • antonym: hinder - to prevent or make more difficult
