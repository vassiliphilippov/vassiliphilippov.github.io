prepose

verb
to place before
9.5
Pronunciation: /priː.ˈpoʊz/ /; / /prɪ.ˈpoʊz/ (AmE), /priː.ˈpəʊz/ /; / /prɪ.ˈpəʊz/ (BrE)
to put or place something before another in order, position, or time

Examples

To prepose a chapter
The editor had preposed the appendix to the front of the book to emphasize its importance.
In some languages, adjectives are preposed before the nouns they modify.
The teacher preposed the test to an earlier date due to scheduling conflicts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: postpose - to place after something else
  • synonym: precede - to come before in order or position
