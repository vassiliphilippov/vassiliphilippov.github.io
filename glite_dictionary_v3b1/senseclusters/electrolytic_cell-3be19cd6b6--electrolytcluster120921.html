electrolytic cell

noun
device for driving chemical reactions with electricity
6.9 5.8
Pronunciation: /ɪ.lek.trə.ˈlɪ.tɪk sel/ /; / /ə.lek.trə.ˈlɪ.tɪk sel/ (AmE), /ɪ.lek.trə.ˈlɪ.tɪk sel/ /; / /ə.lek.trə.ˈlɪ.tɪk sel/ (BrE)
a device containing an electrolyte in which an external voltage causes a non-spontaneous chemical reaction to occur, such as the decomposition of water into hydrogen and oxygen

Examples

An electrolytic cell device
In chemistry class, we built an electrolytic cell to split water into hydrogen and oxygen gases.
The process of metal plating often involves the use of an electrolytic cell.
When a current is applied to an electrolytic cell, it forces a chemical reaction that would not occur on its own.

Related Terms

  • antonym: galvanic cell - electrochemical cell that generates electricity from a spontaneous chemical reaction
