commendatory

adjective
granted in commendam
9.9
Pronunciation: / kəˈmɛndəˌtɔri / (AmE), / kəˈmɛndətəri / (BrE)
historical, of a benefice or its holder: granted in commendam, receiving revenues or title without performing the usual duties

Examples

Commendatory abbot
He was appointed as a commendatory abbot, receiving the income but not living in the monastery.
The bishop held several commendatory benefices during his career in the church.
In medieval times, a commendatory title often meant authority without responsibility for daily duties.

Related Terms

  • synonym: titular - holding a title without the associated duties
