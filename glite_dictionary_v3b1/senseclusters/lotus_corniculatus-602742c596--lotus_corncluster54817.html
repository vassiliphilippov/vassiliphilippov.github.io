Lotus corniculatus

noun
a European forage plant; bird's-foot trefoil
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈloʊtəs kɔrˌnɪkjəˈleɪtəs / (AmE), / ˈləʊtəs kɔːˌnɪkjʊˈleɪtəs / (BrE)
A perennial European forage plant with yellow flowers and claw-shaped pods, commonly known as bird's-foot trefoil, often grown for pasture and ground cover.

Examples

Lotus corniculatus plant
Lotus corniculatus is often planted to improve soil fertility due to its nitrogen-fixing properties.
Farmers appreciate Lotus corniculatus for its palatability and as a nutritious forage for livestock.
The fields were covered in bright yellow blooms of Lotus corniculatus during the late spring.
