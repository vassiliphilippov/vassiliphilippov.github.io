indisposed

adjective
unwilling or reluctant
5.9 5.5
Pronunciation: /ɪn.dɪ.ˈspoʊzd/ /; / /ɪn.də.ˈspoʊzd/ (AmE), /ɪn.dɪ.ˈspəʊzd/ /; / /ɪn.də.ˈspəʊzd/ (BrE)
(usually followed by 'to') unwilling or reluctant to do something; disinclined.

Examples

Indisposed to help
He was clearly indisposed to accept any compromise on the matter.
She seemed indisposed to discuss her personal life with strangers.
The chairman was indisposed to approve the proposal without further consideration.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antipathetic - feeling or showing a strong aversion
  • synonym: averse - having a strong feeling of opposition or dislike
  • antonym: disposed - willing or prepared to do something
  • antonym: inclined - willing or favorable towards something
