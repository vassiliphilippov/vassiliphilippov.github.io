confession

noun
admitting sins to a priest (religious)
Pronunciation: / kənˈfɛʃən / (AmE), / kənˈfɛʃən / (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic Church) the act of admitting one's sins to a priest as part of the sacrament of penance, seeking forgiveness.

Examples

Catholic confession
After making his confession, he felt a great sense of relief and peace.
Every Saturday afternoon, parishioners line up for confession at the cathedral.
She encouraged her son to go to confession before Easter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: admission - the act of admitting something
  • antonym: denial - refusal to admit the truth or reality
