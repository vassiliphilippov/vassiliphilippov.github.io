fact mood

noun
grammatical mood for stating facts
Pronunciation: / fækt muːd / (AmE), / fækt muːd / (BrE)
(Linguistics) The grammatical mood that expresses statements of fact or objective reality, typically unmarked and used for straightforward assertions.

Examples

The fact mood
In English, most sentences are in the fact mood when describing reality.
Questions, commands, and wishes do not use the fact mood.
The teacher explained that the fact mood is often called the indicative.
