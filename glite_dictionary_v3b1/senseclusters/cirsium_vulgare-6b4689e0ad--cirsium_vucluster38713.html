Cirsium vulgare

noun
a European thistle species with prickly leaves
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsɜrsiəm vʌlˈɡɛr.i / (AmE), / ˈsɜː.sɪ.əm vʌlˈɡɪə.ri / (BrE)
A European species of thistle with large flower heads and prickly leaves, often naturalized as a weed in North America.

Examples

Cirsium vulgare plant
Cirsium vulgare is considered an invasive weed in many parts of the United States.
Gardeners often struggle to remove Cirsium vulgare from their plots due to its resilience.
The field was covered with the prickly growth of Cirsium vulgare in late summer.
