hydrogen azide

noun
a volatile, explosive chemical compound
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪdrədʒən ˈæzaɪd / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdrədʒən ˈazɪd / (BrE)
a colorless, volatile, and poisonous liquid with an unpleasant odor, used mainly as a chemical reagent and known for being highly explosive

Examples

hydrogen azide liquid
Because hydrogen azide is both toxic and foul-smelling, it should only be used in a fume hood.
Laboratory safety protocols must be followed strictly when handling hydrogen azide due to its explosive nature.
The researchers detected traces of hydrogen azide in the reaction vessel after the experiment.
