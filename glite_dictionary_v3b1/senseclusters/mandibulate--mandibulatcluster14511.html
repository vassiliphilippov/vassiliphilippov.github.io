mandibulate

adjective
having mandibles
Pronunciation: / mænˈdɪbjələt / (AmE), / mænˈdɪbjʊlət / (BrE)
(of an animal) having mandibles; possessing jaw-like mouthparts.

Examples

Mandibulate insects
Most insects are classified as mandibulate because they have jaw-like mouthparts for biting or chewing.
Scientists compared the feeding habits of mandibulate and non-mandibulate arthropods.
The mandibulate structure of beetles allows them to consume a wide variety of foods.

Related Terms

  • antonym: edentate - lacking teeth (or, in some uses, mandibles); toothless
