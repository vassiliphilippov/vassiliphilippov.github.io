exteriorization

noun
making something outwardly visible
7.2
Pronunciation: / ɪkˌstɪriərɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ɪkˌstɪriəɹɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˌstɪəriəraɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ɛkˌstɪəriəraɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or process of making something outwardly visible or giving it an external form

Examples

The exteriorization of feelings
His anxiety found exteriorization in constant fidgeting and nervous habits.
The artist's work is an exteriorization of her inner emotions.
Through ritual, there is an exteriorization of spiritual beliefs.
Meditation can be seen as both the interiorisation and exteriorisation of consciousness in different traditions.
The architect aimed for a direct exteriorisation of function in the building's bold design.
The artist's paintings are a clear exteriorisation of her inner thoughts and feelings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embodiment - the tangible or visible form of an idea or feeling
  • antonym: interiorisation - the process of making something internal or inward
