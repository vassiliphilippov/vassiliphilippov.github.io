distortion

noun
misrepresentation of meaning
Pronunciation: / dɪˈstɔr.ʃən / (AmE), / dɪˈstɔː.ʃən / (BrE)
the act of twisting, misrepresenting, or giving a false account of the truth or facts

Examples

Distortion of facts
Her statement was a clear distortion of the truth.
Political debates are often filled with distortion and selective facts.
The media was accused of distortion in its reporting of the events.
Political debates often involve the distortion of statistics to support a point.
She accused the newspaper of deliberate distortion of her statements.
The article contained a serious distortion of the facts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exaggeration - making something seem greater or more important than it is
  • degree higher: fabrication - the action of inventing false information
  • synonym: misrepresentation - the act of giving a false or misleading account of something
  • antonym: truth - the quality of being true or accurate
