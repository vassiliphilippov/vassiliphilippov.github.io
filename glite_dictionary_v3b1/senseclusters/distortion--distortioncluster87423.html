Examples

Distortion of facts
Her statement was a clear distortion of the truth.
Political debates are often filled with distortion and selective facts.

Synonyms

  • misrepresentation
    7.5 2.56/m
    misrepresentation gives a false or misleading account to others, while distortion describes twisting or changing the facts more broadly.
  • twisting
    6.3 0.30/m
    twisting changes words or facts to make a different meaning, while distortion means any act of bending or misrepresenting truth.
  • falsification
    7.7 0.36/m
    falsification involves deliberately altering documents or data to make them false, while distortion can be accidental or wider misrepresentation.
  • fabrication
    6.5 1.65/m
    fabrication invents new false details or stories to deceive, while distortion often includes changing real facts or their meaning.
  • straining
    7.7 0.02/m
    straining stretches an idea to suggest a new meaning, while distortion covers any twisting or false account of facts.
  • lie
    2.7 56/m
    lie is one deliberate false statement a person makes, while distortion is a broader act of twisting or misrepresenting facts.
