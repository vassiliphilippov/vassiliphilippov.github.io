kangaroo court

noun
unofficial or sham court
Pronunciation: / ˌkæŋɡəˈru kɔrt /; / ˌkæŋɡəˈru ˌkɔrt / (AmE), / ˌkæŋɡəˈruː kɔːt /; / ˌkæŋɡəˈruː ˌkɔːt / (BrE)
an unofficial or sham court or tribunal that ignores legal standards and delivers a biased or predetermined verdict

Examples

A kangaroo court decision
Accusations were decided by a kangaroo court rather than through legitimate legal proceedings.
Many saw the disciplinary hearing as a kangaroo court lacking fairness and due process.
The accused was convicted in a kangaroo court and denied the right to proper defense.
After the abrupt verdict, many believed they had witnessed a kangaroo court rather than a proper trial.
His case was decided so swiftly and unfairly that it resembled a kangaroo court.
The protestors claimed that the disciplinary hearing was a kangaroo court with a foregone conclusion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: court of law - a court that follows the legal system and official procedures
  • synonym: show trial - a public trial where the verdict is predetermined
