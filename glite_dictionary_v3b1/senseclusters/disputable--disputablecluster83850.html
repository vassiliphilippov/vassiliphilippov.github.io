disputable

adjective
open to argument or debate

open to argument, debate, or doubt; not certain or universally accepted

Examples

A disputable claim
His claim is disputable, as it lacks enough supporting evidence.
The results of the study are highly disputable and have sparked intense academic discussion.
Whether this method is effective remains a disputable issue among experts.
Her conclusions are interesting but disputable, as other experts disagree with her analysis.
The evidence in the case was highly disputable, leading to much debate among the lawyers.
Whether the painting is an original is disputable among art historians.

Synonyms

  • controvertible
    8.3 0.01/m
    controvertible is able to be argued in formal contexts, while disputable is a general word for things open to argument.
  • debatable
    5.0 2.49/m
    debatable is commonly used in speech and writing to show uncertainty, while disputable names things open to argument.
  • arguable
    6.0 1.02/m
    arguable invites an argument or claim in everyday use, while disputable says something can be questioned or argued.
  • moot
    7.4 0.84/m
    moot often appears in legal or academic talk as a hypothetical issue, while disputable means open to real argument.
  • dubitable
    9.9 0.00/m
    dubitable suggests something seems doubtful or suspect, while disputable simply means open to argument or doubt.
  • problematical
    7.7 0.18/m
    problematical sounds formal and suggests causing difficulty or doubt, while disputable focuses on being open to argument.
