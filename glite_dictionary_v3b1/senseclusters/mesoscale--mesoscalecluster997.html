mesoscale

noun
intermediate scale
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛsoʊˌskeɪl / (AmE), / ˈmiːsəʊˌskeɪl / (BrE)
the intermediate spatial or temporal scale of phenomena, especially in meteorology and oceanography, between small and large scales

Examples

The mesoscale level
Scientists study weather systems at the mesoscale to better predict thunderstorms and tornadoes.
Ocean currents on the mesoscale can influence regional climate patterns significantly.
The mesoscale is important for understanding how local and global weather interact.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: macroscale - the largest scale of observation, focusing on very large phenomena
  • degree lower: microscale - the smallest scale of observation, focusing on very small phenomena
