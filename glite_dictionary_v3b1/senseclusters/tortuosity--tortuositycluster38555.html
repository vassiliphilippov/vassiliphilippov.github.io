tortuosity

noun
twisted or winding shape or quality
9.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Àåt…îÀêr.t ÉuÀà…ëÀê.s…ô.ti / (AmE), / Àåt…îÀê.tjuÀêÀà…ís.…™.ti / (BrE)
the state or quality of being full of twists and turns; a winding or twisted form or path

Examples

The tortuosity of paths
Researchers studied the tortuosity of the maze to measure how winding its paths were.
The tortuosity of the mountain road made driving slow and difficult.
The roots of the tree grew with remarkable tortuosity, wrapping around rocks and obstacles.

Synonyms

  • tortuousness
    9.9 0.00/m
    tortuousness means having many twists and often being hard to follow, while tortuosity names the winding quality itself.
  • sinuousness
    8.0 0.02/m
    sinuousness describes smooth curves and winding shapes often seen in rivers or roads, while tortuosity implies twisting and turns.
  • sinuosity
    8.7 0.03/m
    sinuosity names the measured degree of winding in a river or road, while tortuosity refers to a general twisting form.

Related Terms
