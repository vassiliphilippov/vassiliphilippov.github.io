microglial

adjective
of brain immune cells
Pronunciation: / ˌmaɪkroʊˈɡliːəl / (AmE), / ˌmaɪkrəʊˈɡliːəl / (BrE)
relating to the small immune cells, called microglia, found in the brain and spinal cord

Examples

Microglial cells
The scientists observed increased microglial activity after the brain injury occurred.
Certain diseases can cause abnormal microglial responses in the central nervous system.
Researchers are studying how microglial cells contribute to neurodegenerative disorders.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glial - relating to the supportive cells in the nervous system
  • antonym: neuronal - relating to nerve cells (neurons)
