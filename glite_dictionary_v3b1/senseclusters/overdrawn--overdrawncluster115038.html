overdrawn

adjective
bank account negative
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈdrɔn / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈdrɔːn / (BrE)
having spent more money than is available in a bank account, resulting in a negative balance

Examples

Overdrawn bank account
After several unexpected expenses, his account became overdrawn and he had to pay extra fees.
The bank notified her that she was overdrawn and needed to deposit funds immediately.
If you write a check without enough money in your account, it may become overdrawn.

Related Terms

  • synonym: in debt - owing money to a person or institution
  • antonym: solvent - having enough money to pay all debts
