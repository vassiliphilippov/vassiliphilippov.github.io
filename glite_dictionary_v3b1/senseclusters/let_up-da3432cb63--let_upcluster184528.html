let up

verb
become less intense or severe
Pronunciation: / lɛt ʌp / (AmE), / lɛt ʌp / (BrE)
to become less strong, severe, or intense (especially of something unpleasant like pain, pressure, or bad weather)

Examples

The rain let up
Her headache didn't let up all day, making it difficult to concentrate.
The rain finally began to let up after several hours.
We waited until the traffic would let up before leaving the city.
If the pain doesn't let up soon, you should see a doctor.
The rain finally began to let up after several hours of pouring without stopping.
The traffic jam didn't let up until late in the evening.
