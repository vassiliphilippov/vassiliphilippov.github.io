vena poplitea

noun
vein behind the knee
8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈviː.nə pɑːp.lɪ.ˈtiː.ə/ (AmE), /ˈviː.nə pɒp.lɪ.ˈtiː.ə/ (BrE)
a large vein located behind the knee, which carries blood from the lower leg and becomes the femoral vein

Examples

The vena poplitea vein
A blood clot in the vena poplitea can cause swelling and pain in the calf.
After passing through the knee, the vena poplitea continues upward as the femoral vein.
The vena poplitea is often assessed during vascular examinations of the lower limb.
