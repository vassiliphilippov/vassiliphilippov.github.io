rhabdomyolysis

noun
muscle breakdown condition
Pronunciation: / ræbˌdoʊmaɪˈɑləsɪs / (AmE), / ræbˌdəʊmaɪˈɒlɪsɪs / (BrE)
a serious medical condition in which damaged muscle tissue breaks down and releases substances into the bloodstream, potentially causing kidney damage

Examples

Rhabdomyolysis muscle damage
After running a marathon in the heat, she developed rhabdomyolysis and needed immediate medical attention.
Doctors warned that certain medications could increase the risk of rhabdomyolysis in some patients.
Early symptoms of rhabdomyolysis include muscle pain, weakness, and dark-colored urine.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: muscle strain - an injury to a muscle or tendon
  • synonym: myopathy - a disease of muscle tissue
