phytohaemagglutinin

noun
toxic bean protein
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌfaɪtoʊˌhiːməˈɡlʌtənɪn / (AmE), / ˌfaɪtəʊˌhiːməˈɡlʌtɪnɪn / (BrE)
a toxic protein found in certain raw or undercooked beans, especially red kidney beans, that can cause severe gastrointestinal illness

Examples

Phytohaemagglutinin in beans
Consuming raw red kidney beans can expose you to phytohaemagglutinin, which may cause nausea and vomiting.
Scientists study phytohaemagglutinin to understand its effects on the digestive system.
Properly cooking beans destroys phytohaemagglutinin and makes them safe to eat.

  • synonym: toxin - a poisonous substance produced by living organisms
