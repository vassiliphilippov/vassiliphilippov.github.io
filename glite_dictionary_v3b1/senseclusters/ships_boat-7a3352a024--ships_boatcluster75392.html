ship's boat

noun
boat used to travel between ship and shore
3.6
Pronunciation: / ʃɪps boʊt / (AmE), / ʃɪps bəʊt / (BrE)
a small boat carried by a ship and used for transportation between the ship and the shore

Examples

The ship's boat
In an emergency, the ship's boat was used to bring supplies from the harbor.
The captain ordered the ship's boat to be lowered so the crew could go ashore.
Visitors were ferried from the shore to the yacht by the ship's boat.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dinghy - a small boat often used as a tender from ship to shore
