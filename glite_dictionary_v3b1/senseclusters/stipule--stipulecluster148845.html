stipule

noun
small leaf-like outgrowth at leaf base (botany)
4.6 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪpjul / (AmE), / ˈstɪ.pjuːl / (BrE)
a small, usually paired, leaf-like appendage at the base of a leaf stalk in some plants, often shed early

Examples

Small stipules on leaves
Roses often have small stipules at the base of each leaf stalk.
Some species lack stipule structures entirely, making them harder to distinguish botanically.
The stipules of this plant fall off early and can be hard to spot.
