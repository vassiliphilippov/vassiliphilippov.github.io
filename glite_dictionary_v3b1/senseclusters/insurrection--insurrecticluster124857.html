An organized attempt by a group of people to overthrow their government or the authority in power, often involving violence.

Examples

The insurrection began
During the insurrection, several key buildings were seized by the rebels.
Historians study the causes behind each insurrection to understand political change.

Synonyms

  • insurgence
    8.7 0.07/m
    insurgence is a term for an organized armed rebellion with subversion, while insurrection usually names a sudden violent overthrow attempt.
  • insurgency
    8.4 6.36/m
    insurgency describes a long, armed campaign by rebels, while insurrection usually refers to a sudden, short attempt to seize power.
  • rebellion
    4.1 7.73/m
    rebellion is broad organized resistance sometimes lasting long, while insurrection often means a brief, forceful attempt to overthrow authority.
  • revolt
    4.6 6.71/m
    revolt is a direct, often sudden act to remove rulers, while insurrection implies a planned attempt to overthrow authority, often violently.
  • uprising
    5.1 11/m
    uprising is a local or popular rising of people against rulers, while insurrection suggests a focused attempt to seize state power.
  • rising
    6.8 0.55/m
    rising is a general or sometimes sudden popular movement against authority, while insurrection names a specific violent overthrow attempt.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rebellion - organized, open resistance to authority, often less violent than insurrection
  • synonym: revolt - an attempt to overthrow authority, usually on a local scale
  • degree higher: revolution - a forcible overthrow of government or social order, typically broader than insurrection
