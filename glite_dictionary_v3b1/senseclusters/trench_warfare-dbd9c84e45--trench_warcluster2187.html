trench warfare

noun
combat fought from opposing trenches
Pronunciation: / trɛntʃ ˈwɔrˌfɛr / (AmE), / trɛntʃ ˈwɔː.feə / (BrE)
A method of fighting in which opposing armies attack, counterattack, and defend from relatively permanent systems of trenches dug into the ground facing each other.

Examples

Bloody trench warfare
After months of trench warfare, both sides began to suffer from low morale and heavy losses.
The movie accurately depicts the harsh realities of trench warfare.
World War I is often remembered for the brutal trench warfare that left millions dead.

Related Terms

  • antonym: guerrilla warfare - a form of irregular warfare using small, mobile groups and surprise attacks
