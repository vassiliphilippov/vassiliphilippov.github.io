stock dividend

noun
dividend paid as additional shares
5.1
Pronunciation: / stɑk ˈdɪvɪˌdɛnd / (AmE), / stɒk ˈdɪvɪˌdɛnd / (BrE)
A dividend paid to shareholders in the form of additional shares, rather than cash.

Examples

A stock dividend payment
Issuing a stock dividend can increase the total number of outstanding shares in the market.
Shareholders received a 5% stock dividend instead of the usual annual cash payout.
The company announced a stock dividend to reward its shareholders without reducing its cash reserves.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cash dividend - a dividend paid in cash to shareholders
