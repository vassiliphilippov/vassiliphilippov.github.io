fair

adjective
moderate; neither excessive nor extreme
5.9
Pronunciation: / fɛr / (AmE), / feə / (BrE)
moderate or average in amount, quality, or ability; not especially good or bad

Examples

Fair amount
She received a fair amount of praise for her hard work.
The team made a fair effort, but ultimately they fell short of victory.
We paid a fair price for the house, considering its location.
Although the food was fair, I wouldn't recommend the restaurant for a special occasion.
Her performance in the competition was fair, but she didn't stand out.
The movie was just fair; it didn't really impress me.
