hyperbaton

noun
reversal of normal word order
6.2
Pronunciation: / haɪˈpɜrbətɒn / (AmE), / haɪˈpɜːbətɒn / (BrE)
A rhetorical device in which normal word order is reversed or rearranged for emphasis or effect.

Examples

Hyperbaton in poetry
In the poem, the use of hyperbaton gives the line a dramatic, unusual rhythm.
Shakespeare often employed hyperbaton to emphasize certain words or ideas in his plays.
The phrase 'sweet is the night air' is an example of hyperbaton in English literature.

Related Terms

  • synonym: inversion - the reversal of the normal word order in a sentence
