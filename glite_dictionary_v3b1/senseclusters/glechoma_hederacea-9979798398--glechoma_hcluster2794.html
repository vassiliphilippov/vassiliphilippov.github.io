Glechoma hederacea

noun
aromatic trailing plant of the mint family
9.9
Pronunciation: /gle.ˈkoʊ.mə he.də.ˈreɪ.siː/ (AmE), /gle.ˈkəʊ.mə he.də.ˈreɪ.siː/ (BrE)
A trailing European aromatic plant of the mint family, with rounded leaves and small purplish flowers, often grown as an ornamental and naturalized in North America.

Examples

Glechoma hederacea is often used in hanging baskets for its decorative foliage.
Glechoma hederacea spreads rapidly and can cover large areas in suitable climates.
