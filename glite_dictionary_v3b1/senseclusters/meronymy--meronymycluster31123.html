meronymy

noun
part-whole semantic relationship
9.9 8.9
Pronunciation: / məˈroʊnəmi / (AmE), / məˈrɒnəmi / (BrE)
The semantic relationship between a part and the whole to which it belongs.

Examples

Meronymy part whole
"Wheel" and "car" illustrate meronymy, since a wheel is part of a car.
In semantics, meronymy is contrasted with hyponymy, which relates to class membership.
Linguists study meronymy to understand how words relate as parts and wholes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: holonymy - the semantic relation holding between a whole and its parts
