sporophyll

noun
leaf that bears sporangia in plants
9.9 9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈspɔːrəˌfɪl /; / ˈspɔrəˌfɪl / (AmE), / ˈspɒrəfɪl /; / ˈspɒrəˌfɪl / (BrE)
a leaf or leaf-like organ that bears sporangia (spore-producing structures), especially in ferns, mosses, and some seed plants

Examples

A sporophyll leaf
In ferns, the sporophyll is often distinct from the sterile leaves.
Some club mosses produce cones composed of closely packed sporophylls.
The arrangement of sporophylls can help botanists identify different plant species.
Botanists examined the structure of the sporophyll to better understand the plant's reproductive cycle.
In some mosses, the sporophyll is modified to maximize spore dispersal.
The fern produces sporophylls that carry clusters of sporangia on their undersides.
