sea-poose

noun
seaward undercurrent after waves break
Pronunciation: / ˈsiː ˌpuːs / (AmE), / ˈsiː ˌpuːs / (BrE)
The seaward undercurrent created after waves have broken on the shore.

Examples

The sea-poose current
Local fishermen warned that the sea-poose could pull small boats back out to sea.
Swimmers should be cautious of the sea-poose that forms after large waves crash ashore.
The child felt the tug of the sea-poose around his ankles as the waves receded.

Related Terms

  • synonym: undertow - a current below the surface of the sea that moves offshore when waves are approaching the shore
