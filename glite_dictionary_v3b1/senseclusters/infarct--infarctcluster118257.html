infarct

verb
to cause tissue death
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfɑrkt / (AmE), / ɪnˈfɑːkt / (BrE)
to cause or undergo tissue death due to a lack of blood supply.

Examples

Tissue infarcts
If the artery is blocked for too long, the tissue may infarct and lose its function.
Doctors acted quickly to restore blood flow before the heart muscle could infarct.
Brain cells can infarct rapidly during a severe stroke if not treated immediately.

Related Terms

  • synonym: necrose - to undergo or cause tissue death
  • antonym: revive - to bring back to life or consciousness
