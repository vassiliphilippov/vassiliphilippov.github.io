magnum

noun
large wine/liquor bottle (1.5 liters)
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˈmæɡ.nəm / (AmE), / ˈmaɡ.nəm / (BrE)
A wine or liquor bottle that holds twice the standard amount, typically 1.5 liters.

Examples

A magnum bottle
Collectors often seek out rare wines in magnum bottles.
He brought a magnum of red wine to the dinner party.
The restaurant offered a magnum of champagne for the celebration.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: jeroboam - a very large wine bottle (equivalent to four magnums)
