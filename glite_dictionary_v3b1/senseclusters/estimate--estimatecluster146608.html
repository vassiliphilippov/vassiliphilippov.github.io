estimate

noun
judgment or opinion about qualities
Pronunciation: / ˈɛstəˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈɛstɪmət / (BrE)
a judgment or opinion about the qualities, value, or nature of something or someone; (rare) the respect in which a person is held

Examples

Her estimate of him
Her estimate of his honesty was very high.
In my estimate, this project has a lot of potential.
The public’s estimate of the politician changed after the debate.
Her estimate among colleagues grew after the successful project.
In the eyes of the community, his estimate declined due to the scandal.
The scientist was held in the highest estimate by his peers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assessment - an evaluation or appraisal of something
  • antonym: calculation - the process of using mathematics to find out an amount or number (different sense of estimate)
  • antonym: contempt - lack of respect or regard for someone
  • synonym: estimation - the process of forming a judgment or opinion; high regard
