drive

noun
hard, straight return shot in sports
Pronunciation: / draɪv / (AmE), / draɪv / (BrE)
a hard, forceful shot or stroke in sports, especially in golf or tennis

Examples

A powerful drive
His drive was too fast for his opponent to reach in time.
She practiced hitting a low drive against the side wall during her squash lesson.
The player returned the serve with a powerful drive down the line.
After a poor drive, he had to take a penalty stroke.
He watched his drive sail straight down the fairway.
Her powerful drive landed just short of the green.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lob - a high, arcing shot in sports like tennis, the opposite of a drive
  • synonym: smash - a forceful overhead shot in sports like tennis and badminton
  • synonym: tee shot - the first shot played from the teeing area, especially in golf
