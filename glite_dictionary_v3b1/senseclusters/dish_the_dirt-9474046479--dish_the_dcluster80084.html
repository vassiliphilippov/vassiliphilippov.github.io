dish the dirt

verb
gossip or reveal secrets
Pronunciation: / dɪʃ ðə dɚt / (AmE), / dɪʃ ðə dɜːt / (BrE)
to spread gossip or reveal personal secrets about others

Examples

To dish the dirt on friends
At the reunion, some old friends were eager to dish the dirt on former classmates.
Reporters try to dish the dirt on celebrities to sell more magazines.
She promised not to dish the dirt about what happened at the party.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gossip - to talk about the personal lives of others
  • antonym: keep quiet - to not reveal information, to remain silent about secrets
