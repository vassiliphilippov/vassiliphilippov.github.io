mint condition

noun
perfect, like new condition
Pronunciation: / mɪnt kənˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / mɪnt kənˈdɪʃən / (BrE)
in perfect, pristine condition as though newly made or never used

Examples

Mint condition item
Collectors will pay more for toys that are kept in original boxes and in mint condition.
She sold her old smartphone because it was in almost mint condition.
The vintage guitar he bought was still in mint condition after all these years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brand new - completely new; not previously used
  • synonym: pristine - in its original condition; unspoiled
  • antonym: worn - damaged or deteriorated through use
