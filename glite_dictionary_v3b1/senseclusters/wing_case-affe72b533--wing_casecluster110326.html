wing case

noun
protective front wing of a beetle or some insects
Pronunciation: / wɪŋ keɪs / (AmE), / wɪŋ keɪs / (BrE)
either of the hard, protective front wings in beetles and some other insects that cover and protect the hind wings

Examples

Beetle wing case
Scientists examined the texture of the wing case to identify different species of beetles.
The beetle's wing case is usually bright and shiny, making it easy to spot in the grass.
When threatened, the insect quickly closes its wing case to protect its delicate hind wings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: elytron - the hardened forewing of a beetle
