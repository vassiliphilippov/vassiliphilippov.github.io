booley

noun
seasonal cattle pasture
Pronunciation: / ˈbuːli / (AmE), / ˈbuːli / (BrE)
a temporary summer pasture or hut used for grazing cattle, especially in Ireland or Scotland

Examples

A booley pasture
The farmers led their cattle up to the booley when the weather grew warmer.
Each summer, the family stayed in a small booley high in the hills.
Traditionally, herders would spend months living in the booley with their livestock.

Related Terms

  • synonym: shieling - a hut or pasture used during summer for grazing livestock, especially in Scotland
