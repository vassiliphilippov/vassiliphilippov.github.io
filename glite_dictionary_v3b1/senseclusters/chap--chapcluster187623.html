chap

verb
to crack, especially of skin or lips
4.1 5.4
Pronunciation: /tʃæp/ (AmE), /tʃæp/ (BrE)
(of the skin or lips) to crack, roughen, or become sore, especially as a result of exposure to cold or dry weather.

Examples

Lips chap in winter
If you don’t use lip balm, your lips might chap during the dry season.
My hands chap quickly in the cold winter air.
Wind and sun can easily chap unprotected skin.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crack - to break partially without dividing into separate parts; to develop a narrow opening
  • antonym: heal - to become healthy or well again; to repair a wound or damage
