crowder

noun
macromolecule additive
9.5
Pronunciation: /ˈkraʊ.dər/ (AmE), /ˈkraʊ.də/ (BrE)
a substance added to a solution to increase the concentration of macromolecules, often used in biochemical experiments

Examples

Biology crowder

Researchers used a molecular crowder to study protein folding in vitro.
The presence of a crowder can significantly alter the outcome of biochemical reactions.
