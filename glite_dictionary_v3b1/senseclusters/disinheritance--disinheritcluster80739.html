disinheritance

noun
the act of depriving someone of inheritance
4.7
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪs.ɪnˈhɛr.ɪ.təns / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.ɪnˈhɛr.ɪ.təns / (BrE)
The act of depriving someone of the right to inherit property or money from a will or estate.

Examples

The disinheritance of children
He spoke bitterly about his disinheritance after the revised will was revealed.
The billionaire's sudden disinheritance of her son shocked the entire family.
The threat of disinheritance persuaded her to change her behavior.
