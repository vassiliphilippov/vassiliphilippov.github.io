scrutinization

noun
close examination
Pronunciation: / ˌskruːtənɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌskruːtənaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of examining something very closely or carefully

Examples

Close scrutinization
The new policy is under intense scrutinization by the media and the public.
Every financial report goes through careful scrutinization before being published.
After much scrutinization, the committee approved the research proposal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: examination - a detailed look at or study of something
  • synonym: inspection - the act of looking at something carefully to check its condition
  • antonym: neglect - the act of not paying attention to something
