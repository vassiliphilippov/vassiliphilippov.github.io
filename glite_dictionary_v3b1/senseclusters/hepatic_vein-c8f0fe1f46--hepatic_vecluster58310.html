hepatic vein

noun
vein carrying blood from the liver
Pronunciation: / hɪˈpætɪk veɪn / (AmE), / hɪˈpætɪk veɪn / (BrE)
a vein that carries blood away from the liver and empties into the inferior vena cava

Examples

The hepatic vein blood
An obstruction in the hepatic vein can lead to serious liver complications.
Blood from the liver is carried to the heart via the hepatic vein.
The surgeon carefully avoided damaging the hepatic vein during the liver operation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: portal vein - vein carrying blood to the liver from the digestive organs
