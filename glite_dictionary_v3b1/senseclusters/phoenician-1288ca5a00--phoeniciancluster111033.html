Phoenician

proper noun
ancient seafaring people
8.7
Pronunciation: / fəˈnɪʃən / (AmE), / fəˈniːʃən / (BrE)
a member of an ancient seafaring people from the region of modern Lebanon, known for trade and the alphabet they developed

Examples

The Phoenician civilization
The Phoenician civilization was renowned for its maritime trade across the Mediterranean.
Historians believe the Phoenician alphabet influenced many modern writing systems.
Archaeologists discovered ancient Phoenician artifacts along the coast of Lebanon.
