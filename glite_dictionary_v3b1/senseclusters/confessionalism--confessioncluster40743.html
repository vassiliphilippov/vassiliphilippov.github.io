confessionalism

noun
religious group system
9.9
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈfeʃ.ən.əl.ɪz.əm/ (AmE), /kən.ˈfeʃ.ən.əl.ɪz.əm/ (BrE)
a system in which different religious groups are officially recognized and given representation in society or government

Examples

Confessionalism in government
The country's constitution is based on confessionalism to ensure fair representation of all religious groups.
Critics argue that confessionalism can deepen divisions between communities in a diverse society.
Lebanon is often cited as an example of a nation governed by confessionalism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sectarianism - strong support for the interests of one's own religious group
  • antonym: secularism - the principle of separating religion from government
