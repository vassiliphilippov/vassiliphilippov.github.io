cornetfish

noun
slender tropical fish with tubular snout
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrnɪtˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈkɔːnɪtˌfɪʃ / (BrE)
A slender tropical marine fish with a long tubular snout and bony plates in place of scales, belonging to the family Fistulariidae.

Examples

A cornetfish in ocean
Diving in tropical waters, we spotted several brightly colored cornetfishes near the shipwreck.
Researchers observed how a cornetfish uses its elongated snout to catch prey.
The cornetfish glided silently through the coral reefs, barely visible among the sea fans.
