Tuscarora

noun
a member of a Native American people
Pronunciation: / ˌtʌskəˈrɔrə / (AmE), / ˌtʌskəˈrɔːrə / (BrE)
a member of a Native American people originally from North Carolina, later part of the Iroquois Confederacy in New York State

Examples

A Tuscarora chief
After migrating north, many Tuscarora settled in upstate New York.
James traces his ancestry back to the Tuscarora people.
The Tuscarora played a significant role in the history of the Iroquois Confederacy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Iroquois - a group of historically powerful Native American peoples of northeastern North America
