slide action

noun
firearm mechanism using sliding lever
Pronunciation: / slaɪd ˈæk.ʃən / (AmE), / slaɪd ˈæk.ʃən / (BrE)
a type of firearm mechanism in which a sliding lever is moved back and forth to eject the spent cartridge, cock the firearm, and load a new round

Examples

The slide action mechanism
Inspectors checked the slide action to ensure the firearm operated smoothly.
Many modern shotguns use a slide action to cycle shells quickly for successive shots.
The slide action mechanism is popular with hunters for its reliability.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pump action - firearm mechanism operated by sliding the forearm back and forth
