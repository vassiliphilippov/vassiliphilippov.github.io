plasticize

verb
make flexible or moldable, esp. by adding plasticizer
3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈplæs.tɪ.saɪz /; / ˈplæs.tɪˌsaɪz /; / ˈplæstəˌsaɪz /; / ˈplæstɪˌsaɪz / (AmE), / ˈplæs.tɪ.saɪz /; / ˈplɑːstɪsaɪz /; / ˈplɑːstɪsʌɪz / (BrE)
to make a material flexible or moldable by adding a plasticizer

Examples

To plasticize rubber
Certain additives can plasticize rubber, making it suitable for various industrial applications.
The manufacturer will plasticize the material to improve its flexibility for packaging uses.
When heated and treated with a solvent, the film will gradually plasticize and become easier to shape.
If you plasticize the material too much, it may lose its structural integrity.
Manufacturers often plasticize PVC to enhance its flexibility for use in cables and hoses.
The company developed a new method to plasticize natural rubber for industrial applications.
Manufacturers often plasticise PVC to create flexible tubing for medical use.
The addition of certain chemicals can plasticise otherwise rigid polymers.
You need to plasticise the material before it can be molded into shape.
