iteroparity

noun
repeated reproduction
Pronunciation: / ˌɪtəroʊˈpærəti / (AmE), / ˌɪtəˈrɒpərɪti / (BrE)
the biological characteristic of being able to reproduce multiple times during an organism's lifetime

Examples

Iteroparity in mammals
Many mammals display iteroparity, giving birth to several litters over their lives.
The study compared the survival rates of iteroparity and semelparity in different fish species.
Birds that practice iteroparity can raise multiple broods in a single breeding season.

Related Terms

  • antonym: semelparity - the condition of reproducing only once in a lifetime
