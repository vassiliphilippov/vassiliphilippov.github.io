lungworm

noun
parasitic lung worm
Pronunciation: / ˈlʌŋˌwɜrm / (AmE), / ˈlʌŋˌwɜːm / (BrE)
a parasitic worm that lives in the lungs of animals or humans and can cause respiratory disease

Examples

A lungworm infection
The veterinarian diagnosed the coughing sheep with a lungworm infection.
Farmers often treat their cattle to prevent lungworm outbreaks during the wet season.
Some species of lungworm can also infect domestic pets like dogs and cats.

Farmers are advised to monitor livestock for signs of lungworm to prevent outbreaks.
