floating exchange rate

noun

An exchange rate system in which the value of a currency is determined by supply and demand in the foreign exchange market, rather than being fixed by a government.

Examples

A floating exchange rate
Many countries switched to a floating exchange rate after the collapse of the Bretton Woods system.
The central bank decided not to intervene in the floating exchange rate system.
Under a floating exchange rate, currency values fluctuate based on market supply and demand.

Synonyms

  • fluctuating exchange rate
