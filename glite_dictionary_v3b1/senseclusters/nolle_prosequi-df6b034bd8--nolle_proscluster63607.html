nolle prosequi

noun
legal notice to drop prosecution
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑli ˈprɑsɪˌkwaɪ /; / ˌnɒli proʊˈsiːkwaɪ / (AmE), / ˌnɒli ˈprɒsɪˌkwaɪ /; / ˌnɒlɛ ˈprɒsɪkwʌɪ / (BrE)
a formal legal notice or order stating that prosecution of a case will not continue

Examples

A nolle prosequi order
After much consideration, the judge allowed a nolle prosequi in the case.
The prosecutor entered a nolle prosequi when new evidence came to light.
With the filing of a nolle prosequi, all charges against the defendant were dismissed.
After months of deliberation, the court received a nolle prosequi and the charges were dropped.
In legal proceedings, granting a nolle prosequi effectively ends the case against the defendant.
The prosecutor filed a nolle prosequi after determining there was insufficient evidence for conviction.
