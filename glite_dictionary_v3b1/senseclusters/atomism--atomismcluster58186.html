atomism

noun
theory that matter consists of atoms
4.3 7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈæt.ə.mɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /ˈæt.ə.mɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the theory that all matter is composed of tiny, indivisible, and indestructible particles called atoms

Examples

Atomism in physics
Atomism was first proposed by ancient Greek philosophers to explain the nature of matter.
Modern science has built upon atomism to develop our understanding of chemical reactions.
Some philosophical debates still consider the implications of atomism versus other models of reality.
