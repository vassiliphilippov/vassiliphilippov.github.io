land

noun
extensive country property owned by someone
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: / lænd / (AmE), / lænd / (BrE)
the solid part of the earth's surface; an area of ground, country, or territory (including for building, agriculture, or as a nation)

Examples

A piece of land
She manages several hundred acres of land in rural Scotland.
The old family land has remained untouched for generations.
They inherited a vast land in the countryside from their ancestors.
The farmer sold a portion of his land to the city for a new park.
They purchased the land next to the lake to build their new home.
This area of land is zoned for commercial development.
After years of drought, the land became dry and barren.
Farmers depend on fertile land to grow their crops successfully.
The company bought several acres of land for agricultural development.
