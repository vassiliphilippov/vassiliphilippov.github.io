palmer

noun
medieval pilgrim
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑlmər / (AmE), / ˈpɑːlmə / (BrE)
an old term for a pilgrim, especially one who has returned from the Holy Land, often carrying a palm branch as a sign

Examples

Medieval palmer pilgrim
In medieval Europe, a palmer was recognized by the palm branch he carried from Jerusalem.
The old legend tells of a palmer who brought stories of distant lands to his village.
A palmer was often welcomed with honor after completing the long pilgrimage to the Holy Land.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pilgrim - a person who travels to a holy place for religious reasons
