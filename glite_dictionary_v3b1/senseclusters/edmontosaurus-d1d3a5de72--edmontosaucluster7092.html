Edmontosaurus

noun
duck-billed dinosaur genus
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɛdˌmɑntəˈsɔrəs / (AmE), / ɛdˌmɒntəˈsɔːrəs / (BrE)
A genus of large, herbivorous duck-billed dinosaurs that lived in what is now Canada and the United States during the late Cretaceous period.

Examples

The Edmontosaurus dinosaur
Paleontologists discovered a nearly complete Edmontosaurus skeleton in Alberta.
The enormous Edmontosaurus grazed in herds across ancient floodplains.
Unlike some other hadrosaurs, Edmontosaurus had a broad, flat beak.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Tyrannosaurus - large genus of predatory dinosaurs from the Cretaceous
  • synonym: hadrosaur - duck-billed dinosaur of the family Hadrosauridae
