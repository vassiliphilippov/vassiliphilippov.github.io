groundwood

noun
mechanically pulped paper
Pronunciation: / ˈɡraʊndˌwʊd / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊndˌwʊd / (BrE)
paper made from wood pulp produced by mechanically grinding wood, typically used for inexpensive printing

Examples

Groundwood paper
Most newspapers are printed on groundwood because it is affordable and easy to produce.
The magazine switched from glossy paper to groundwood to reduce production costs.
Books with short lifespans are often made using groundwood due to its lower quality.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bond paper - high-quality, durable writing paper
  • synonym: newsprint - cheap, low-quality paper used for printing newspapers
