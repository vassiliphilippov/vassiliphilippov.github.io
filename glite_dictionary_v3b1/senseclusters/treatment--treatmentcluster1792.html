treatment

noun
detailed discussion of a subject
4.4 13/m
Pronunciation: / Ààtrit.m…ônt / (AmE), / ÀàtriÀêt.m…ônt / (BrE)
a thorough or particular way of discussing, examining, or presenting a subject in writing, speech, or art

Examples

A detailed treatment
Her latest article offers a fresh treatment of climate change policy.
The book's detailed treatment of economic theory made it an essential read for students.
The professor's treatment of ancient history in his lectures was both comprehensive and engaging.
Her treatment of light in the painting is inspired by the Impressionists.
The architect's treatment of the building's facade received critical acclaim.
The director's treatment of the story gave the film a unique atmosphere.

Synonyms

  • exposition
    5.5 2/m
    exposition gives a clear, detailed explanation of an idea, while treatment shows the chosen approach or style for presenting that idea.
  • discourse
    6.4 20/m
    discourse is formal spoken or written discussion, while treatment focuses on the particular way a subject is presented or examined.

Related Terms

  • synonym: approach - a particular way of doing or thinking about something, especially in the arts
  • synonym: discussion - an act or process of talking about something in order to reach a decision or to exchange ideas
  • synonym: handling - the way in which something is dealt with, especially in creative or artistic contexts
