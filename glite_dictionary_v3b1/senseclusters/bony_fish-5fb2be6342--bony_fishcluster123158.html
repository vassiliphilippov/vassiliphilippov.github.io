bony fish

noun
fish with a bony skeleton
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈboʊni fɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈbəʊni fɪʃ / (BrE)
any fish belonging to the class Osteichthyes, characterized by a skeleton made mainly of bone rather than cartilage.

Examples

Bony fish species
Many aquarium enthusiasts keep bony fish such as goldfish and guppies.
Salmon are a type of bony fish found in cold, fresh waters.
Unlike sharks, bony fish have skeletons made primarily of bone.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cartilaginous fish - fish with a skeleton made mainly of cartilage, such as sharks and rays
