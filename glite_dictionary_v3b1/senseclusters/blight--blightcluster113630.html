blight

noun
state of deterioration or ruin
9.9 5.9
Pronunciation: /blaɪt/ (AmE), /blaɪt/ (BrE)
A state or condition of being damaged, deteriorated, or ruined, especially by neglect or adverse circumstances.

Examples

Urban blight
Economic decline led to widespread blight throughout the once-prosperous city.
Efforts to revitalize the downtown area aimed to remove the urban blight.
The abandoned buildings contributed to the sense of blight in the neighborhood.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decay - gradual decline in strength or soundness
  • antonym: prosperity - the state of being successful or thriving
  • synonym: ruin - serious damage or destruction to something
