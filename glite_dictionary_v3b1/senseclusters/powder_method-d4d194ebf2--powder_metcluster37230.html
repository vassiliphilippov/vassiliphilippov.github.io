powder method

noun
crystallography technique for identifying substances
Pronunciation: / ˈpaʊdɚ ˈmɛθəd / (AmE), / ˈpaʊdə ˈmɛθəd / (BrE)
a technique in crystallography for identifying minerals or crystals by analyzing the X-ray diffraction patterns produced from a powdered sample

Examples

The powder method technique
Researchers confirmed the identity of the crystal using the powder method.
The powder method is commonly used to analyze complex mineral mixtures.
To teach students about X-ray crystallography, we performed the powder method in the lab.

Related Terms

  • synonym: X-ray diffraction - the scattering of X-rays by the regular arrangement of atoms in a crystal, revealing structure
