flip-flop

verb
change opinion or policy abruptly
Pronunciation: / ˈflɪpˌflɑp /; / ˈflɪp flɑp / (AmE), / ˈflɪp.flɒp /; / ˈflɪp flɒp / (BrE)
to suddenly or frequently change one's opinion, policy, or decision

Examples

To flip-flop on issues
After much debate, the committee flip-flopped on their decision regarding the new policy.
Politicians sometimes flip-flop on issues when public opinion changes.
She tends to flip-flop between supporting different candidates during election season.
It's frustrating when leaders flip-flop instead of taking a clear stance.
She used to support the proposal, but now she's flip-flopped again.
The politician was criticized for his tendency to flip-flop on important issues.

Related Terms

  • synonym: vacillate - to waver between different opinions or actions
  • synonym: waffle - to be indecisive or change one's mind repeatedly
