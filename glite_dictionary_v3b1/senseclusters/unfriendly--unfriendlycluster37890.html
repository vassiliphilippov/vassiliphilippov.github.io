unfriendly

adjective
harsh or inhospitable (environment, conditions)

(of an environment or conditions) not suitable for life, growth, or development; harsh or inhospitable.

Examples

Unfriendly environment
A sudden drop in temperature created unfriendly conditions for the seedlings.
Scientists study how certain plants can survive in unfriendly climates.
The Sahara Desert is an unfriendly environment for most forms of life.

Synonyms

    7.0 1.48/m

  • hostile
    5.2 4.32/m
    hostile means strongly harmful or dangerous to life and growth, while unfriendly means harsh and not welcoming.
  • inimical
    8.9 0.89/m
    inimical sounds formal and means harmful or against something, while unfriendly means harsh and not suitable for life.
  • unconducive
    7.7 0.02/m

  • unfavorable
    4.5 3.88/m
    unfavorable means not likely to bring success or approval, while unfriendly focuses on harsh, inhospitable conditions for life.
  • uncongenial
    8.0 0.09/m
    uncongenial means unpleasant or not a good fit for people, while unfriendly describes harsh environments that harm life.
