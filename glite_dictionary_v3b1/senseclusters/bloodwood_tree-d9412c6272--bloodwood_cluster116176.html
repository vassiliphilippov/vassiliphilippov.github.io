bloodwood tree

noun
tree with hard reddish wood for dye
Pronunciation: / ˈblʌdˌwʊd tri / (AmE), / ˈblʌdˌwʊd triː / (BrE)
A spiny shrub or small tree native to Central America and the West Indies, with bipinnate leaves, small bright yellow flowers, and hard brown or reddish heartwood used to make black dye.

Examples

Central American bloodwood tree
Bright yellow flowers appear in spring on the bloodwood tree, attracting many pollinators.
Local artisans often use wood from the bloodwood tree to craft durable tools and instruments.
The bloodwood tree produces a heartwood prized for its use in making black dye.
