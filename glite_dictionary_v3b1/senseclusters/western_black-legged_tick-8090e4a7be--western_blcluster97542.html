western black-legged tick

noun
a species of tick found in western US, Lyme disease vector
7.0
Pronunciation: /ˈwe.stərn blæk legd tɪk/ (AmE), /ˈwe.stən blæk legd tɪk/ (BrE)
a species of tick (Ixodes pacificus) found in the western United States, known to bite humans and transmit Lyme disease, especially in northern California.

Examples

The western black-legged tick
Hikers are advised to check for western black-legged ticks after walking through wooded areas in the West.
Researchers are studying the habitat of the western black-legged tick to understand how to control its population.
The western black-legged tick is responsible for spreading Lyme disease in northern California.
