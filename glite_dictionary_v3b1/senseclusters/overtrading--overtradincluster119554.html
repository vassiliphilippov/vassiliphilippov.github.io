overtrading

noun
excessive trading activity
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈtreɪdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈtreɪdɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of buying and selling financial assets too frequently, often resulting in high costs or poor returns

Examples

Investor overtrading
Many new investors fall into the trap of overtrading and end up losing money on fees.
The financial advisor warned her client about the dangers of overtrading in volatile markets.
His habit of overtrading led to poor performance in his investment portfolio.

Related Terms

  • antonym: investing - the act of putting money into financial assets for long-term growth
  • synonym: speculation - the act of trading financial assets in the hope of making quick profits
