grandfamily

noun
family with caregiving grandparents
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrændˌfæməli / (AmE), / ˈɡrændˌfæmɪli / (BrE)
a family in which grandparents are the primary caregivers for their grandchildren, often in the absence of the children's parents

Examples

A grandfamily household
Many children in a grandfamily are raised by their grandparents due to various family circumstances.
Support organizations offer resources to help grandfamilies with legal and financial challenges.
The school created a special program to assist students from grandfamilies in their community.

Related Terms

  • synonym: extended family - a family that includes relatives beyond the nuclear family
  • antonym: nuclear family - a family consisting of two parents and their children
