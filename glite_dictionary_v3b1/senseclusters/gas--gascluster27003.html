gas

noun
liquid fuel for engines (gasoline)
Pronunciation: / ɡæs / (AmE), / ɡæs / (BrE)
A volatile and flammable liquid fuel derived from petroleum, used mainly in internal-combustion engines; known as gasoline in American English.

Examples

Gas station
I stopped at the station to fill the tank with gas before heading out of town.
She realized she was almost out of gas and needed to find a fuel station soon.
The price of gas has increased significantly in recent months.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gasoline - a volatile petroleum-derived fuel for engines
  • synonym: petrol - British English for gasoline, a liquid fuel for engines
