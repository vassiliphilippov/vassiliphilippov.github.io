sublimate

verb
redirect impulses into acceptable activities
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌblɪˌmeɪt / (AmE), / ˈsʌblɪmeɪt / (BrE)
to redirect strong or unacceptable emotions or impulses into socially acceptable or creative activities (psychology)

Examples

To sublimate anger
He sublimated his frustration into writing and soon published his first novel.
Many people sublimate their aggressive impulses through competitive sports.
She tried to sublimate her anger by channeling it into her artwork.
Athletes often sublimate their aggressive impulses into competitive sports.
She learned to sublimate her anxiety into art, producing some of her best paintings during stressful times.
Therapists may encourage patients to sublimate negative emotions through creative hobbies or volunteering.

Related Terms

  • synonym: channel - to direct feelings or energy into a particular activity or purpose
  • antonym: repress - to hold back or suppress impulses or emotions
