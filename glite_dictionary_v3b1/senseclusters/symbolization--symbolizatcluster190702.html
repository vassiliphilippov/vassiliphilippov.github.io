symbolization

noun
use of symbols to represent meaning
2.3 6.0
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪmbələˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌsɪmbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪmbəlaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / ˌsɪmbəlaɪˈzeɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the act or process of representing ideas, qualities, or meanings using symbols, or of using symbols or symbolic images to represent ideas, qualities, or objects

Examples

Symbolization of ideas
Modern art often relies heavily on symbolization to convey deeper meanings.
Religious rituals are rich with the symbolization of complex beliefs.
The symbolization of love as a heart shape is common in many cultures.
He studied the symbolization of dreams in psychoanalytic theory.
Modern art often relies on symbolization rather than realistic depiction.
The symbolization of peace by a white dove is widely recognized.
In religious ceremonies, water can serve as a symbolization of purification and renewal.
The dove is often used as a symbolization of peace in art and literature.
The red ribbon is a worldwide symbolization of support for people living with HIV/AIDS.

Related Terms

  • antonym: literalization - the act of interpreting or representing something in a literal sense
  • synonym: representation - the act of representing or depicting something
  • synonym: symbol - a thing that represents or stands for something else
  • synonym: symbolism - the practice of using symbols to represent ideas or qualities
  • synonym: symbolization - alternative spelling, especially in American English
  • synonym: symbolize - to represent something by means of a symbol
