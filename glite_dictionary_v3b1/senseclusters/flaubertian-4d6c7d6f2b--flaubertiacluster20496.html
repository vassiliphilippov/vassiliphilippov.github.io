Flaubertian

adjective
of Flaubert's style
9.9
Pronunciation: / floʊˈbɛrtiən / (AmE), / fləʊˈbɛːtɪən / (BrE)
relating to or characteristic of Gustave Flaubert or his literary style, especially in precision and realism

Examples

Flaubertian style
The novel's Flaubertian attention to detail impressed literary critics around the world.
Her prose is often described as Flaubertian because of its clarity and careful structure.
Many writers admire the Flaubertian pursuit of perfection in every sentence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: realistic - showing things as they really are
