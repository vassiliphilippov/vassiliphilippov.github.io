ennobling

adjective
inspiring moral or social elevation
3.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˈnoʊblɪŋ /; / ɪnˈnoʊblɪŋ / (AmE), / ɪˈnəʊblɪŋ /; / ɪnˈnəʊblɪŋ / (BrE)
making someone or something better, more dignified, or more noble in character or purpose

Examples

An ennobling experience
He found the work challenging but deeply ennobling in purpose.
Her selfless acts had an ennobling influence on everyone around her.
Reading about historic heroes can have an ennobling effect on young minds.
His speech had an ennobling effect on everyone who heard it.
She found the experience of helping others deeply ennobling.
The ennobling influence of art can inspire people to achieve greatness.

Related Terms

  • antonym: degrading - causing a loss of respect or standing; lowering in dignity
  • synonym: exalting - raising in rank, power, or character; elevating
  • synonym: uplifting - making someone feel better or more hopeful
