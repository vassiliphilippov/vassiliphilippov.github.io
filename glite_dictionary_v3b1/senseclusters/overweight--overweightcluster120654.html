overweight

adjective
weighted more than benchmark (finance)
7.0
Pronunciation: /ˈoʊ.vər.weɪt/ (AmE), /ˈəʊ.və.weɪt/ (BrE)
(finance) having a larger weight or allocation in a portfolio or model than the benchmark or index; indicating a recommendation to hold more of an asset or sector relative to the benchmark.

Examples

Overweight stocks
Many portfolios remain overweight in technology shares after the strong rally this quarter.
The fund manager advised clients to stay overweight on renewable energy stocks for long-term growth.
Analysts turned overweight on the company after it reported higher-than-expected quarterly earnings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: underweight - having a smaller weight or allocation than the benchmark; opposite of overweight in finance.
