trunking

noun
bulk goods transport
6.8
Pronunciation: /ˈtrʌŋ.kɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈtrʌŋ.kɪŋ/ (BrE)
the transport of goods in large quantities along main routes between distribution centers or hubs, rather than by local delivery.

Examples

Trunking shipments
The company saves money by trunking shipments between regional warehouses on a weekly schedule.
Efficient trunking reduces the number of vehicles needed for long-distance transport.
Many logistics firms use trunking to move goods quickly between major distribution centers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: distribution - the process of delivering goods to various locations
  • synonym: freight - goods transported in bulk by truck, train, ship, or aircraft
  • antonym: local delivery - the transport of goods over short distances to final destinations
