sympatholytic

adjective
inhibiting sympathetic nerves
9.9
Pronunciation: /sɪm.pə.θə.ˈlɪ.tɪk/ /; / /sɪm.pə.θoʊ.ˈlɪ.tɪk/ (AmE), /sɪm.pə.θə.ˈlɪ.tɪk/ /; / /sɪm.pə.θəʊ.ˈlɪ.tɪk/ (BrE)
relating to drugs or agents that inhibit the actions of the sympathetic nervous system

Examples

Sympatholytic drug
The doctor prescribed a sympatholytic medication to help control the patient's high blood pressure.
Beta blockers are a common type of sympatholytic drug used in cardiology.
Some sympatholytic agents can cause side effects such as fatigue or dizziness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adrenergic blocker - a drug that blocks the effects of adrenaline on the body
  • antonym: sympathomimetic - a drug that stimulates the sympathetic nervous system
