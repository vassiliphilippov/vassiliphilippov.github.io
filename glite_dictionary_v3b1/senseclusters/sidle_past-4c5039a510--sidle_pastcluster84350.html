sidle past

verb
move sideways to pass stealthily
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪdəl pæst / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdəl pɑːst / (BrE)
to move sideways past something or someone in a cautious or stealthy manner, often to avoid drawing attention

Examples

To sidle past quietly
He tried to sidle past the group without being noticed.
She managed to sidle past the sleeping dog without waking it.
The student attempted to sidle past the teacher, hoping not to be stopped for a question.

Related Terms
