inequivalent

adjective
not equivalent
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnɪˈkwɪvələnt / (AmE), / ˌɪnɪˈkwɪvələnt / (BrE)
not equal in value, function, or meaning; not equivalent

Examples

Inequivalent values
The two proposals are inequivalent and cannot be compared directly.
In mathematics, these sets are considered inequivalent because they have different properties.
The roles assigned to the team members were inequivalent in terms of responsibility.

Related Terms

  • antonym: equivalent - equal in value, function, or meaning
  • synonym: unequal - not equal in amount, size, or value
