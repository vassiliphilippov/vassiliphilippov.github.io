Indian agent

noun
government official managing American Indian affairs
3.2
Pronunciation: / ˈɪndiən ˌeɪdʒənt / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən ˌeɪdʒənt / (BrE)
A representative of the United States federal government assigned to liaise with and oversee the affairs of American Indian tribes, especially on reservations.

Examples

The Indian agent role
Historical records often mention disputes between the tribe and the Indian agent.
She is researching how Indian agents affected tribal governance in the 19th century.
The Indian agent was responsible for distributing supplies to the tribe on the reservation.
