High Holiday

noun
major Jewish holy day (Rosh Hashanah or Yom Kippur)
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪ ˈhoʊləˌdeɪ / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ ˈhɒlɪˌdeɪ / (BrE)
Either of the two most important Jewish holy days, Rosh Hashanah and Yom Kippur, observed with particular solemnity.

Examples

The High Holidays season
Businesses often close or reduce hours in areas with large Jewish communities for the High Holidays.
Many Jewish families attend synagogue during the High Holidays.
The rabbi delivered a special sermon for the congregation on the first High Holiday of the year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Rosh Hashanah - Jewish New Year festival, one of the High Holidays
  • synonym: Yom Kippur - Jewish Day of Atonement, one of the High Holidays
