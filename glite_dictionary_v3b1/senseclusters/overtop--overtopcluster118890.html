overtop

verb
rise above and look down on
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈtɑp / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈtɒp / (BrE)
to rise above or look down on; to be higher than something else, typically so as to overlook it

Examples

Tall trees overtop houses
A tall fence overtops the garden, ensuring privacy for the residents.
New skyscrapers now overtop many historic buildings in the city center.
The cliffs overtop the old harbor, casting long shadows at sunset.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dominate - to have a commanding position over; to overlook
  • synonym: tower over - to be much taller than; to be much higher than someone or something else
