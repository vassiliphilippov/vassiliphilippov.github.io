consociational

adjective
relating to alliances
Pronunciation: / kənˌsoʊsiˈeɪʃənəl / (AmE), / kənˌsəʊsiˈeɪʃənəl / (BrE)
relating to a formal association or cooperative arrangement, especially a political system where power is shared among different groups

Examples

A consociational government
The new government adopted a consociational approach to ensure all major groups were represented.
A consociational agreement was signed to promote cooperation between the two rival organizations.
The committee's consociational structure helped balance the interests of different departments.
The country adopted a consociational model to ensure fair representation for all ethnic groups.
A consociational approach can help prevent conflict in societies with deep divisions.
Many scholars study consociational democracies to understand how power-sharing works in practice.

Related Terms

  • antonym: adversarial - involving conflict or opposition between groups
  • synonym: collaborative - involving cooperation between groups or organizations
  • antonym: majoritarian - relating to a system where the majority holds power
  • synonym: power-sharing - a system where different groups share political power
