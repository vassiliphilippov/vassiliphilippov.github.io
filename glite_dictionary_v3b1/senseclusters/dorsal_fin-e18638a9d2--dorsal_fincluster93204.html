dorsal fin

noun
fin on the back of fishes and aquatic animals
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈdɔr.səl fɪn / (AmE), / ˈdɔː.səl fɪn / (BrE)
an unpaired fin located on the back of fishes and some other aquatic animals, which helps to maintain balance while swimming

Examples

The dorsal fin shape
Marine biologists studied the shape of the dorsal fin to help identify different dolphin species.
Some fish have a single dorsal fin, while others may have two or more along their backs.
The shark's distinctive dorsal fin broke the surface of the water as it approached the boat.

Related Terms

  • synonym: caudal fin - fin at the tail of a fish, used for propulsion
  • synonym: pectoral fin - fin located on the sides of fishes, behind the head
