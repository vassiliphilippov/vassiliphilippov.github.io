brisk

adjective
quick and energetic
Pronunciation: / brɪsk / (AmE), / brɪsk / (BrE)
quick, energetic, and active in movement or action

Examples

A brisk walk
Let's take a brisk walk before dinner to energize ourselves.
She gave a brisk nod and continued her work efficiently.
The air was cool and the wind grew brisk as the sun set.
He walked at a brisk pace to keep warm in the cold wind.
The café enjoyed a brisk trade during the morning rush hour.
There was a brisk exchange of ideas at the meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lively - full of life and energy
  • antonym: slow - not quick or energetic; lacking speed
  • antonym: sluggish - slow and lacking energy
