perjuriously

adverb
in a faithless way
Pronunciation: / pərˈdʒʊriəsli / (AmE), / pəˈdʒʊəriəsli / (BrE)
in a way that involves lying under oath or breaking a solemn promise

Examples

To act perjuriously
He acted perjuriously when he broke his sworn promise to his friends.
The witness spoke perjuriously, betraying the trust of the court.
She was accused of behaving perjuriously after abandoning her allies in their time of need.
The witness spoke perjuriously in court, risking criminal charges.
He was accused of answering perjuriously during the official investigation.
Giving perjuriously sworn statements can lead to severe penalties.

Related Terms

  • antonym: faithfully - in a loyal and trustworthy manner
  • synonym: falsely - in a way that is untrue or incorrect
  • synonym: treacherously - in a way that betrays trust or is disloyal
  • antonym: truthfully - in a way that is honest and true
