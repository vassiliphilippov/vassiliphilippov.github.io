buoy up

verb
to keep afloat; support on a liquid
Pronunciation: / ˈbɔɪ ʌp / (AmE), / ˈbɔɪ ʌp / (BrE)
to keep something or someone afloat on the surface of a liquid; to support so that it does not sink

Examples

To buoy up a boat
Special foam inserts buoy up the boat when it's heavily loaded.
The life vest buoyed up the child in the water.
Waves buoyed up the debris for several days before it washed ashore.

Related Terms

  • synonym: float - to rest or move on the surface of a liquid without sinking
  • antonym: sink - to go down below the surface of a liquid
