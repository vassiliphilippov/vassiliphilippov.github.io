bowse

verb
haul using a tackle (nautical)
Pronunciation: / baʊz / (AmE), / baʊz / (BrE)
to haul or pull with the aid of a tackle, especially in nautical contexts

Examples

To bowse a line
He bowsed the line taut before securing it to the post.
The sailors bowsed the heavy anchor aboard using a sturdy block and tackle.
They quickly bowsed the boom into position as the wind picked up.
