subordinative

adjective
serving to connect subordinate clauses
4.9 7.1
Pronunciation: /sə.ˈbɔːr.dɪ.nə.tɪv/ /; / /ˈsʌb.ɔːr.dɪ.nə.tɪv/ (AmE), /sə.ˈbɔː.dɪ.nə.tɪv/ /; / /ˈsʌb.ɔː.dɪ.nə.tɪv/ (BrE)
(of a word, especially a conjunction) serving to connect a subordinate clause to a main clause.

Examples

Subordinative conjunction
Grammarians often classify 'although' as a subordinative conjunction.
In the sentence 'She stayed home since it was raining,' 'since' acts as a subordinative word.
The word 'because' is a subordinative conjunction that introduces a dependent clause.

Related Terms

  • antonym: coordinative - (of a conjunction) joining elements of equal grammatical rank
  • synonym: subordinating - (of a conjunction) joining a subordinate clause to a main clause
