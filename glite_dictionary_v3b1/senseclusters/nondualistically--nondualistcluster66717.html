nondualistically

adverb
in a unified way
9.9
Pronunciation: /nɑːn.duː.ə.ˈlɪs.tɪ.kliː/ /; / /nɑːn.djuː.ə.ˈlɪs.tɪ.kliː/ /; / /nən.duː.ə.ˈlɪs.tɪ.kliː/ (AmE), /nɒn.djuː.ə.ˈlɪs.tɪ.kliː/ /; / /nən.djuː.ə.ˈlɪs.tɪ.kliː/ (BrE)
in a way that does not recognize a division between two opposing concepts, emphasizing unity or oneness

Examples

To think nondualistically
The philosopher spoke nondualistically about the relationship between mind and body.
Many spiritual traditions encourage thinking nondualistically to overcome feelings of separation.
She approached the conflict nondualistically, seeking common ground rather than taking sides.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dually - in a way that involves two parts or aspects
  • synonym: holistically - in a way that considers the whole rather than separate parts
