bifocals

noun
eyeglasses with two focal lengths
Pronunciation: / baɪˈfoʊkəlz / (AmE), / baɪˈfəʊkəlz / (BrE)
Eyeglasses with lenses having two distinct optical powers, one for near vision and one for distance vision.

Examples

Bifocals glasses
After turning forty, Jane started wearing bifocals to see both her book and the television clearly.
He frequently lost his bifocals, making it difficult to read and recognize faces at a distance.
The optometrist recommended bifocals because I was struggling with reading and driving.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: single vision glasses - eyeglasses with lenses of only one focal length
  • degree higher: trifocals - eyeglasses with lenses having three focal lengths
