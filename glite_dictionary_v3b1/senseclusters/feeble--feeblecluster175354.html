feeble

adjective
weak; lacking strength or effectiveness
4.8 3.2
Pronunciation: /ˈfiː.bəl/ (AmE), /ˈfiː.bəl/ (BrE)
physically or mentally weak; lacking strength, energy, or vitality, often due to age, illness, or exhaustion

Examples

Feeble old man
After running for hours, her legs felt feeble and shaky.
His feeble attempt to lift the box only made his friends chuckle.
The patient spoke in a feeble whisper, barely audible to the nurse.
After a long illness, he was too feeble to walk unaided.
Her voice sounded feeble after shouting for help all night.
The feeble light from the flashlight barely illuminated the path.
After her long illness, she felt too feeble to get out of bed.
His voice was feeble, barely more than a whisper.
The feeble old man needed help walking up the stairs.
