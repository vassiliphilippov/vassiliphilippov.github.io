glacial landscape

noun
land shaped by glaciers
Pronunciation: / ˈɡleɪʃəl ˈlændˌskeɪp / (AmE), / ˈɡleɪsɪəl ˈlændskeɪp / (BrE)
an area of land whose features have been shaped or formed by the action of glaciers

Examples

A glacial landscape area
Much of northern Europe is covered by features characteristic of a glacial landscape.
Scientists study a glacial landscape to understand Earth's climatic history.
The hikers marveled at the glacial landscape stretching out before them.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glacier - a large mass of ice that moves slowly over land
