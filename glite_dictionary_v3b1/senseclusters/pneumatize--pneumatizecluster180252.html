pneumatize

verb
to fill with air
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈnuːməˌtaɪz / (AmE), / ˈnjuːmətaɪz / (BrE)
to fill with air or gas, especially referring to biological structures becoming air-filled

Examples

To pneumatize bones
Some dinosaur bones would pneumatize to reduce their weight and make movement easier.
In certain birds, the skull can pneumatize as part of their adaptation for flight.
Researchers observed that the sinus cavities began to pneumatize during early development.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deflate - to release air or gas from something
  • synonym: inflate - to fill something with air or gas
