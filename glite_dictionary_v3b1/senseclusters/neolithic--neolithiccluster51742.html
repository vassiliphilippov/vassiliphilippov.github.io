neolithic

adjective
later Stone Age period
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˌniː.oʊˈlɪθ.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌniː.əˈlɪθ.ɪk / (BrE)
relating to the later part of the Stone Age, when ground or polished stone weapons and tools were used

Examples

Neolithic stone tools
Archaeologists discovered neolithic pottery fragments at the site.
Some neolithic structures are still visible in northern Europe.
The neolithic revolution marked a shift from hunting to farming.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mesolithic - relating to the middle part of the Stone Age, between the Paleolithic and Neolithic periods
  • antonym: paleolithic - relating to the earlier Stone Age, before the Neolithic period
