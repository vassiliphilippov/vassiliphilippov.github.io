cerebellar artery

noun
artery supplying the cerebellum
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛrəˈbɛlər ˈɑːr.tɛr.i / (AmE), / ˌsɛrəˈbɛlə ˈɑː.tər.i / (BrE)
an artery that supplies blood to the cerebellum, a part of the brain responsible for motor control

Examples

The cerebellar artery supplies blood
A blockage in the cerebellar artery can lead to problems with coordination and balance.
Blood supply from the cerebellar artery is essential for normal cerebellar function.
The neurosurgeon repaired the damaged cerebellar artery following the stroke.
