mobilize

verb
prepare people or resources for action
1.3
Pronunciation: / ˈmoʊbəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈməʊbəlaɪz /; / ˈməʊbɪlaɪz / (BrE)
to organize and prepare people or resources for active use or action, especially in order to achieve a particular goal

Examples

To mobilize support
Environmental activists are working to mobilize public support for climate action.
The government decided to mobilize additional resources to deal with the crisis.
We need to mobilize all available staff to meet the tight deadline.
Community leaders are working to mobilise volunteers for the clean-up campaign.
She managed to mobilise enough support to launch the new initiative.
The government had to mobilise emergency services after the earthquake struck.

Related Terms

  • antonym: demobilise - to disband or release from a state of readiness or active service
  • synonym: deploy - to move into position for action
  • synonym: marshal - to arrange or assemble people or things in order
  • synonym: mobilize - alternative (American) spelling: to make ready for action or use
