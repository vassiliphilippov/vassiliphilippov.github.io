family Avicenniaceae

noun
a plant family with only Avicennia genus
Pronunciation: / ˌævɪˌsɛniˈeɪsiˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˌævɪˌsɛniˈeɪsɪˌiː / (BrE)
A family of tropical coastal plants, considered by some classifications to consist solely of the genus Avicennia.

Examples

The family Avicenniaceae
Some taxonomists recognize the family Avicenniaceae separately from Verbenaceae and Acanthaceae.
The family Avicenniaceae is sometimes considered synonymous with the genus Avicennia in botanical classifications.
The mangroves of the family Avicenniaceae are found in coastal regions of tropical and subtropical areas.
