nonassociative

adjective
not having the associative property
Pronunciation: / nɑn əˈsoʊsiətɪv / (AmE), / nɒn əˈsəʊsɪətɪv / (BrE)
(of an operation) Not having the associative property; in which changing the grouping of elements changes the result.

Examples

Nonassociative operation
In computer science, certain functions are deliberately designed to be nonassociative.
Multiplication is associative, but subtraction is nonassociative.
Some algebraic structures are defined by their nonassociative operations.

Related Terms

  • antonym: associative - having the associative property (grouping does not affect the result)
