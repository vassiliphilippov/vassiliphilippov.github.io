hold firm

verb
refuse to abandon one's opinion or belief
Pronunciation: / hoʊld fɝːm / (AmE), / həʊld fɜːm / (BrE)
to refuse to change your opinion, decision, or belief despite pressure to do so

Examples

To hold firm in beliefs
Despite the criticism, she decided to hold firm in her beliefs.
Even under pressure from his peers, he held firm and did not change his decision.
The committee needs to hold firm on this policy if they want it to succeed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: give in - to surrender or yield to pressure or persuasion
  • synonym: stand firm - to refuse to change your opinion or position
