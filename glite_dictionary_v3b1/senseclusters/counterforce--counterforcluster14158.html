counterforce

verb
to oppose a force
Pronunciation: / ˈkaʊntərˌfɔrs / (AmE), / ˈkaʊntəˌfɔːs / (BrE)
to apply a force that opposes or resists another force in order to counteract its effect

Examples

To counterforce a movement
Engineers had to counterforce the lever's movement to keep the mechanism aligned during testing.
To stabilize the bridge, workers needed to counterforce the strong winds pushing against it.
The team designed a system to automatically counterforce any sudden shifts in pressure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: counteract - to act against something to reduce its effect
  • antonym: reinforce - to strengthen or support with additional force
  • synonym: resist - to withstand the force or effect of something
