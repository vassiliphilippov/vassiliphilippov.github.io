visual hallucination

noun
seeing things that are not present
Pronunciation: / ˈvɪʒuəl həˌluːsɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˈvɪʒuəl həˌluːsɪˈneɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
an experience of seeing something that is not actually present, caused by a disorder of perception

Examples

Experience a visual hallucination
He described seeing faces in the shadows, which were later diagnosed as visual hallucinations.
People with Parkinson's disease may experience visual hallucinations as a side effect of medication.
The doctor determined that the bright lights she saw were a visual hallucination caused by sleep deprivation.
