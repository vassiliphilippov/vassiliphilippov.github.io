justiciable

adjective
can be tried in court
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈstɪʃiəbl / (AmE), / dʒʌˈstɪʃəbl / (BrE)
able to be decided or settled by a court of law

Examples

Justiciable legal issue
The court ruled that the issue was justiciable and could proceed to trial.
Some political questions are not considered justiciable under the law.
The lawyer argued that the claim was clearly justiciable and deserved a hearing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: actionable - giving legal grounds for a lawsuit
  • antonym: nonjusticiable - not able to be decided by a court of law
