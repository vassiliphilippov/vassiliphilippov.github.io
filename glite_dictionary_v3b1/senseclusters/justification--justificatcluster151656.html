justification

noun
reason showing something is reasonable
2.0 3.3
Pronunciation: /dʒʌ.stə.fə.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /dʒʌ.stɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /dʒʌ.stɪ.fɪ.ˈkeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a reason or explanation that shows something is right, reasonable, or necessary, or the act of giving such a reason

Examples

A justification for action
She needed a strong justification for canceling the event at the last minute.
The financial report provided little justification for further spending.
There is no justification for such rude behavior in the workplace.
His excuse sounded more like a weak justification than a valid reason.
She offered a detailed justification for her decision to leave the company.
The report failed to provide adequate justification for the proposed changes.
She offered a lengthy justification for her decision to resign from the committee.
The company failed to provide any justification for the price increase.
There was little justification for his sudden outburst during the meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accusation - a charge or claim that someone has done something wrong
  • antonym: condemnation - expression of very strong disapproval
  • synonym: defense - an argument or explanation offered to justify something
  • synonym: excuse - a reason given to explain an action, often to avoid blame
  • synonym: pretext - a reason that is given to hide the real reason for doing something
  • synonym: rationalization - the act of attempting to explain or justify behavior with logical reasons, even if not appropriate
