live-and-die

noun
sensitive-leaved shrub of tropical America and Australia
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪv ən ˈdaɪ / (AmE), / ˈlɪv ən ˈdaɪ / (BrE)
a prostrate or semi-erect shrub of tropical America and Australia, noted for its sensitive leaflets that fold and droop when touched or at night, typically armed with recurved thorns.

The live-and-die is often planted in gardens for its fascinating leaf movement.
