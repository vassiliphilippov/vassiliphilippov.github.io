bushwhack

verb
force a path through bush or woods
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊʃˌwæk / (AmE), / ˈbʊʃˌwæk / (BrE)
to make one's way through dense woods or undergrowth by cutting or pushing through it

Examples

To bushwhack a trail
After bushwhacking through thick vines and brambles, they finally found an open clearing.
The explorers spent hours bushwhacking along the riverbank, creating a new path.
We had to bushwhack our way through the overgrown forest to reach the lake.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hack - to cut with heavy blows in a rough way
