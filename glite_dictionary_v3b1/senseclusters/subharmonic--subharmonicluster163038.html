subharmonic

noun
fractional frequency
8.4
Pronunciation: /sʌb.hɑːr.ˈmɑːn.ɪk/ /; / /səb.hɑːr.ˈmɑːn.ɪk/ (AmE), /sʌb.hɑː.ˈmɒn.ɪk/ /; / /səb.hɑː.ˈmɒn.ɪk/ (BrE)
a frequency that is an exact fraction, such as one-half or one-third, of a fundamental frequency

Examples

A subharmonic peak
The engineer noticed a strong subharmonic in the sound spectrum of the engine.
Musicians sometimes use a subharmonic to create a richer, deeper tone in their music.
The scientist measured a subharmonic at one-third the frequency of the main signal.
