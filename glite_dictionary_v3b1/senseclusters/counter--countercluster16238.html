counter

noun
a person who counts items or people
3.6 2.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkaʊn.tər/ /; / /ˈkaʊn.ər/ (AmE), /ˈkaʊn.tə/ (BrE)
a person or device that counts things, or a register in a computer that stores a count

Examples

A people counter
At the event, a counter stood near the entrance, keeping track of the number of visitors.
She worked as a counter in the inventory department, making sure every product was tallied.
The counter carefully recorded each ballot during the election.
A 16-bit counter can represent integers from 0 to 65535.
A digital counter is essential for keeping track of events in an embedded system.
The counter increases its value by one each clock cycle.
Every time you enter the website, the counter at the bottom increases by one.
She installed a step counter on her phone to track her daily activity.
The scientist used a Geiger counter to record radiation levels in the lab.
