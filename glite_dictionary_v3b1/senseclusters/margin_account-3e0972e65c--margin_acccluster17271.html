margin account

noun
brokerage account with borrowed funds
6.2 7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑrdʒɪn əˌkaʊnt / (AmE), / ˈmɑːdʒɪn əˌkaʊnt / (BrE)
an account at a brokerage in which the broker lends money to the investor to buy securities, using the securities as collateral

Examples

Open a margin account
He opened a margin account to increase his buying power in the stock market.
The risks of trading with a margin account include potential losses greater than your initial investment.
With a margin account, investors can borrow money from their broker to purchase additional shares.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cash account - a brokerage account in which purchases must be paid for in full
