vortex

noun
whirling mass forming a spiral shape
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈvɔr.tɛks /; / ˈvɔːr.tɛks / (AmE), / ˈvɔː.tɛks / (BrE)
a mass of air or water that spins rapidly in a spiral or circular shape and pulls things toward its center

Examples

A vortex of wind
Leaves were caught in the swirling vortex of wind at the corner of the building.
The artist painted a mesmerizing vortex to represent the storm’s energy.
The tornado formed a powerful vortex as it touched down in the open field.
A documentary showed how storms could generate a massive vortex in the open ocean.
He watched leaves spinning in a miniature vortex in the swiftly moving stream.
Swimmers were warned to avoid the vortex near the pier because of its dangerous pull.

Related Terms

  • synonym: eddy - a circular movement of water or air
  • synonym: whirlpool - a powerful circular current of water
