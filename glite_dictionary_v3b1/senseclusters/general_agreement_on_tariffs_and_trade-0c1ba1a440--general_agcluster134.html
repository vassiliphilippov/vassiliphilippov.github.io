General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade

proper noun
international trade treaty and predecessor to WTO
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛnəɹəl əˈɡriːmənt ɑn ˈtærɪfs ənd treɪd / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛnərəl əˈɡriːmənt ɒn ˈtærɪfs ənd treɪd / (BrE)
an international agreement created in 1947 to promote global trade by reducing tariffs and other trade barriers; it later became the foundation for the World Trade Organization (WTO)

Examples

General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade rules
Many developing countries benefited from reduced trade barriers under the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade.
The General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade played a key role in shaping postwar international commerce.
The General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade was replaced by the World Trade Organization in 1995.
