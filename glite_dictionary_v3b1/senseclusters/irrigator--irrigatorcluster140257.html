irrigator

noun
watering device
3.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɪrɪˌɡeɪtər / (AmE), / ˈɪrɪˌɡeɪtə / (BrE)
a machine or device used to supply water to land or crops for agricultural purposes

Examples

Field irrigator
The farmer used a new irrigator to ensure all the crops received enough water.
After the storm damaged the old irrigator, the field remained dry for several days.
Modern irrigators can be programmed to water fields automatically at set times.
The irrigator ensured that every part of the field received enough water during the dry season.
Modern farms often use an automatic irrigator to save time and labor.
As an experienced irrigator, she knew exactly when the crops needed more water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drip system - an irrigation method that delivers water directly to plant roots
  • synonym: irrigation - the process of supplying water to land or crops
  • synonym: sprinkler - a device that sprays water over an area
