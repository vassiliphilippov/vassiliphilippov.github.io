cotton on

verb
begin to understand; realize
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑtən ɑn / (AmE), / ˈkɒtən ɒn / (BrE)
to begin to understand or realize something, often after some time or confusion

Examples

To cotton on quickly

It took me a while to cotton on to what they were talking about.
She finally cottoned on to the reason why everyone was acting so strangely.

He finally cottoned on to what they were really talking about.
Once you cotton on to the pattern, the puzzle becomes much easier.
