barranca

noun
steep ravine
Pronunciation: / bəˈræŋkə / (AmE), / bəˈræŋkə / (BrE)
a steep-sided ravine or gully, especially found in Spanish-speaking regions

Examples

A deep barranca
The river carved a deep barranca through the landscape over thousands of years.
Farmers built terraces along the edge of the barranca to prevent soil erosion.
Wildflowers grew in abundance on the slopes of the barranca after the rainy season.
