CT scan

noun
medical imaging test
Pronunciation: / si ti skæn / (AmE), / siː tiː skæn / (BrE)
a medical imaging test that uses X-rays and computer processing to create detailed pictures of the inside of the body

Examples

A CT scan of the chest
The doctor recommended a CT scan to get a clearer view of the patient's lungs.
After the accident, she underwent a CT scan to check for any internal injuries.
A CT scan can help detect tumors or other abnormalities inside the body.

Related Terms

  • antonym: MRI scan - a medical imaging test using magnetic fields and radio waves
  • synonym: X-ray - a medical imaging test using X-rays to view inside the body
