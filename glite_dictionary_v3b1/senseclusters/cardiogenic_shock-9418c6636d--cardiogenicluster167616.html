cardiogenic shock

noun
heart-induced circulatory failure
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑrdioʊˈdʒɛnɪk ʃɑk / (AmE), / ˌkɑːdiəʊˈdʒɛnɪk ʃɒk / (BrE)
a serious medical condition in which the heart suddenly cannot pump enough blood to meet the body's needs, usually caused by severe heart problems such as a heart attack

Examples

Cardiogenic shock after heart attack
Cardiogenic shock is a life-threatening complication often resulting from a severe heart attack.
Prompt intervention can improve survival rates in cases of cardiogenic shock.
The emergency team worked quickly to treat the patient's cardiogenic shock following his cardiac arrest.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: shock - a sudden and severe drop in blood flow through the body
