bullbrier

noun
prickly woody vine of eastern U.S.
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈbʊlˌbraɪər / (AmE), / ˈbʊlˌbraɪə / (BrE)
a very prickly woody vine native to the eastern United States, with tough round stems, shiny leathery leaves, small greenish flowers, and clusters of inedible shiny black berries

Examples

The bullbrier vine
Clusters of shiny black berries appeared on the old bullbrier behind the barn.
The gardener warned us about the tough stems of the bullbrier near the property line.
We got scratched by bullbrier while hiking through the dense forest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: greenbrier - another common name for bullbrier, a prickly vine of the Smilax genus
