Examples

Synonyms

    2.5 0.1/m
    plentifully is used to say that something exists in large amounts, while plenteously feels slightly old-fashioned and poetic.
  • abundantly
    3.5 3/m
    abundantly describes more than enough supply in many contexts, while plenteously sounds more literary or rarer in everyday speech.
  • aplenty
    4.2 0.00/m
    aplenty means there is a lot, often after nouns, while plenteously is a standard adverb used more in formal writing.
  • bountifully
    4.6 0.09/m
    bountifully paints a generous, warm scene often linked to gifts, while plenteously is more neutral about amount.
  • copiously
    6.1 0.4/m
    copiously suggests very large, often measurable amounts like notes or rain, while plenteously simply means abundant.
  • bounteously
    8.1 0.01/m
    bounteously highlights generous giving or gifts, while plenteously focuses mainly on plentiful quantity without kindness.
  • profusely
    4.8 2/m
    profusely often means so much it seems excessive, for example sweating, while plenteously stays neutral about plenty.
  • aboundingly
    6.3 0.00/m
