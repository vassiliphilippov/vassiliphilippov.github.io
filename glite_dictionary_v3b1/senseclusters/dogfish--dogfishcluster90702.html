dogfish

noun
primitive North American freshwater fish
Pronunciation: / ˈdɔɡˌfɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈdɒɡ.fɪʃ / (BrE)
a primitive, long-bodied carnivorous freshwater fish with a very long dorsal fin, found in sluggish waters of North America

Examples

North American dogfish
Anglers sometimes mistake the dogfish for other similar freshwater species.
Scientists are studying the unique hunting behaviors of the dogfish in North America.
The dogfish is often found lurking near the muddy bottoms of slow-moving rivers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bowfin - a North American freshwater fish sometimes called dogfish
  • synonym: burbot - a related type of freshwater fish, sometimes called dogfish
