see the glass half-full

verb
focus on the positive side of things
Pronunciation: / si ðə ɡlæs hæf ˈfʊl / (AmE), / siː ðə ɡlɑːs hɑːf ˈfʊl / (BrE)
to look at a situation in a positive way; to focus on the good aspects rather than the bad

Examples

To see the glass half-full attitude
Even after losing her job, she tried to see the glass half-full and found new opportunities.
He always encourages his friends to see the glass half-full when they're feeling down.
If you learn to see the glass half-full, challenges become chances to grow.

Related Terms

  • antonym: see the glass half-empty - to look at a situation in a pessimistic way; to focus on what is missing
