explode

verb
suddenly express strong emotion
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈsploʊd / (AmE), / ɪkˈspləʊd / (BrE)
to suddenly and forcefully express a strong emotion, such as anger or laughter

Examples

To explode with anger
If you keep pushing him, he'll eventually explode.
She exploded in anger when she heard the news.
The coach exploded with fury after the referee's decision.
Her laughter exploded unexpectedly, startling everyone nearby.
The tension in the meeting finally exploded when the two managers began shouting.
When he heard the news, he exploded with rage and stormed out of the room.

Related Terms

  • synonym: burst - to break open or out suddenly and violently
  • synonym: erupt - to burst out suddenly, especially with emotion
  • synonym: lose one's temper - to become suddenly angry
  • antonym: restrain oneself - to control or suppress one's emotions
