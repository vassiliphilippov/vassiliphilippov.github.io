Synonyms

  • burst out
    3.3 1/m
    burst out describes suddenly laughing, crying, or shouting, while explode describes forcefully showing a very strong emotion.
  • erupt
    4.2 10/m
    erupt often describes sudden violent events or crowds showing emotion, while explode focuses on one person's sudden, forceful emotional outburst.
  • burst
    1.8 3/m
    burst can mean letting out something kept inside, like tears or laughter, while explode means loudly showing a very strong emotion.
  • burst forth
    5.4 0.4/m
    burst forth describes something appearing suddenly and visibly, like flowers or a crowd, while explode describes a person showing strong emotion loudly.
