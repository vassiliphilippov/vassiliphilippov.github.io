Mebaral

noun
barbiturate drug used for epilepsy and sedation
Pronunciation: / ˈmiːbəˌræl / (AmE), / ˈmiːbəˌral / (BrE)
A long-acting barbiturate drug used as a sedative and anticonvulsant, especially in the treatment of epilepsy.

Examples

Mebaral medication
Due to its sedative effects, Mebaral must be taken exactly as directed by a physician.
Patients should not abruptly stop taking Mebaral without consulting their healthcare provider.
The doctor prescribed Mebaral to help control his epileptic seizures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mephobarbital - the generic barbiturate compound; Mebaral's active ingredient
