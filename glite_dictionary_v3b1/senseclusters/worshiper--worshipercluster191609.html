worshiper

noun
person who takes part in worship
3.2 1.37/m
Pronunciation: / Ààw…úr É…™p…ôr / (AmE), / Ààw…úÀê É…™p…ô / (BrE)
a person who shows reverence and adoration for a deity or religious figure, especially in a formal religious setting

Examples

Church worshiper
As a devoted worshiper, he attended church every Sunday morning.
Hundreds of worshipers gathered at the temple to celebrate the festival.
In the quiet mosque, the worshipers knelt in silent prayer.
As a lifelong worshipper, she attends services and observes all important ceremonies.
Many worshippers gather here every Sunday to pray together.
The temple was filled with worshippers from early morning until late at night.

Synonyms

  • votary
    9.9 0.03/m
    a votary is formally devoted and often bound by vows in religious life, while a worshiper shows reverence and adoration in services.
  • venerator
    9.9 0.00/m
    a venerator shows deep respectful honor, often in formal religious contexts, while a worshiper actively adores and praises a deity in services.
  • devotee
    6.0 2.96/m
    a devotee is strongly devoted and may follow people or causes beyond religion, while a worshiper focuses on reverence to a deity in services.
