eucaryotic

adjective
having cells with membrane-bound nuclei
4.2 6.4
Pronunciation: /juː.kə.ˈrɑː.tɪk/ /; / /juː.kær.i.ˈɑː.tɪk/ (AmE), /juː.kə.ˈrɒ.tɪk/ /; / /juː.kær.i.ˈrɒ.tɪk/ (BrE)
(of a cell or organism) having cells with a distinct membrane-bound nucleus and specialized organelles.

Examples

Eucaryotic cell structure
All plants and animals are eucaryotic organisms with complex cellular structures.
Fungi are classified as eucaryotic because their cells contain membrane-bound organelles.
The presence of a true nucleus distinguishes eucaryotic cells from prokaryotic ones.
