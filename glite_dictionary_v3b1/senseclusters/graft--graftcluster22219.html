graft

verb
insert donor organ/tissue by surgery
6.5
Pronunciation: / ɡræft / (AmE), / ɡrɑːft / (BrE)
to insert an organ or tissue from a donor into the body of a recipient, especially as a medical operation

Examples

To graft an organ
Advances in medicine now allow physicians to graft organs with a higher success rate.
Doctors graft a healthy kidney into the patient during the transplant surgery.
Surgeons were able to graft skin from his leg onto his burned arm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: transplant - to move an organ or tissue from one body to another
