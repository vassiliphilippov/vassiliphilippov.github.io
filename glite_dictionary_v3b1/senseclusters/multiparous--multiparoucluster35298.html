multiparous

adjective
producing multiple offspring at once
9.9
Pronunciation: / mʌlˈtɪpərəs / (AmE), / mʌlˈtɪpərəs / (BrE)
(of an animal or person) producing more than one offspring at a time or having borne more than one child.

Examples

Multiparous female animals
In veterinary practice, cows are often classified as primiparous or multiparous based on their calving history.
The multiparous mother rabbit gave birth to a large litter every spring.
Women who are multiparous may have higher risks of certain complications during pregnancy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: uniparous - producing a single offspring at a birth
