muddy

verb
make cloudy or turbid
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌdi / (AmE), / ˈmʌdi / (BrE)
to make something dirty, wet, or unclear with mud or sediment

Examples

To muddy the water
Heavy rains can muddy the river, making it unsafe for swimming.
Please don't muddy the water with unnecessary details.
Political debates sometimes muddy the issues instead of clarifying them.
After the rainstorm, the children muddied their shoes playing in the yard.
Be careful not to muddy the floor with your boots.
The heavy truck muddied the road as it drove through the construction site.
Heavy rain will muddy the dirt roads, making travel difficult.
His explanation only served to muddy the facts further.
The workers tried not to muddy the clean floor with their boots.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clarify - to make (a liquid) clear
  • antonym: clean - to make something clean; opposite of dirty
  • synonym: dirty - to make something unclean
  • synonym: obscure - to make unclear or difficult to see or understand
