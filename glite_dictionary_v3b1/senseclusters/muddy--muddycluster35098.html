muddy

verb
make cloudy or turbid
1.9 0.45/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆmÊŒdi / (AmE), / ËˆmÊŒdi / (BrE)
to make something dirty, wet, or unclear with mud or sediment

Examples

To muddy the water
Heavy rains can muddy the river, making it unsafe for swimming.
Please don't muddy the water with unnecessary details.
Political debates sometimes muddy the issues instead of clarifying them.
After the rainstorm, the children muddied their shoes playing in the yard.
Be careful not to muddy the floor with your boots.
The heavy truck muddied the road as it drove through the construction site.
Heavy rain will muddy the dirt roads, making travel difficult.
His explanation only served to muddy the facts further.
The workers tried not to muddy the clean floor with their boots.

Synonyms

  • muck
    3.5 0.13/m
    muck makes things dirty with mud or filth, while muddy often means to cover something with mud or make it unclear.
  • mire
    7.9 0.12/m
    mire covers or soils things with wet sticky mud, while muddy is more general and can mean dirty or unclear.
  • mud
    5.1 0.01/m
    mud covers or smears objects with mud, often for building or decoration, while muddy means simply to make things dirty or unclear.
  • muck up
    6.7 0.01/m
    muck up makes a thing dirty, often in spoken casual English, while muddy is neutral and used in many registers.
  • bemire
    9.9 0.00/m
    bemire covers or stains someone with mud in a formal or literary way, while muddy is common everyday language for dirtying.
  • bedraggle
    9.9 0.09/m
    bedraggle makes clothes wet and dirty by dragging through mud or water, while muddy is more general and not always wet.
  • draggle
    9.4 0.03/m
    draggle drags and makes things wet and dirty from mud or water, while muddy can simply mean dirty without the dragging action.
