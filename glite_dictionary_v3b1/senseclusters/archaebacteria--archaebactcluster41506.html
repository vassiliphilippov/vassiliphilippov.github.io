archaebacteria

noun
ancient group of microorganisms
Pronunciation: / ˌɑrkioʊbækˈtɪəriə / (AmE), / ˌɑːkiːbækˈtɪəriə / (BrE)
A group of microorganisms regarded as one of the earliest forms of life, distinct from true bacteria and blue-green algae, with unique genetic and biochemical characteristics.

Examples

Ancient archaebacteria
Scientists study archaebacteria to learn about the earliest life on Earth.
Some archaebacteria can survive in extreme environments such as hot springs and salt lakes.
Unlike bacteria, archaebacteria have unique cell membrane structures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bacteria - a large group of unicellular microorganisms which have cell walls but lack organelles and an organized nucleus
