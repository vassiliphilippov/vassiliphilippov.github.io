corbel arch

noun
arch formed by overlapping masonry
7.5 7.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkɔːr.bəl ɑːrtʃ/ /; / /ˈkɔːr.bəl ɑrk/ (AmE), /ˈkɔː.bəl ɑːtʃ/ /; / /ˈkɔː.bəl ɑːk/ (BrE)
An arch constructed by overlapping layers of stone or brick (corbels) that gradually advance inward until they meet at the top.

Examples

A corbel arch structure
Builders employed a corbel arch to span the narrow passageway.
Early temples in India often featured the corbel arch technique in their design.
The ancient structure was supported by a corbel arch above the entrance.
