vena lingualis

noun
vein draining the tongue
Pronunciation: / ˌvinə lɪŋˈɡweɪlɪs / (AmE), / ˌviːnə lɪŋˈɡweɪlɪs / (BrE)
a vein that drains blood from the tongue and the floor of the mouth, emptying into the internal jugular or facial vein.

Examples

Vena lingualis vein
During surgery, the surgeon carefully avoided damaging the vena lingualis.
Inflammation in the mouth can sometimes affect the flow through the vena lingualis.
The vena lingualis plays a crucial role in draining blood from the tongue to larger veins.
