license

noun
excessive freedom; lack of restraint
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪ.səns /; / ˈlaɪsəns / (AmE), / ˈlaɪ.səns /; / ˈlaɪsəns / (BrE)
freedom to act without following usual rules or conventions, especially in behavior or art

Examples

Artistic license
He warned the students that academic freedom should not be confused with license to disrupt the university.
The new law was criticized for giving the authorities too much license in conducting surveillance.
Without discipline, artistic expression can devolve into mere license rather than meaningful creativity.
He took the license to improvise several lines in the play.
Some artists use license when representing historical events to make the story more engaging.
The director exercised license in adapting the novel for the screen.
Certain comedians are given more licence in their jokes than others.
The director took considerable artistic licence with the story's historical events.
The poet's use of grammar shows a deliberate licence to create a unique style.

Related Terms

  • synonym: liberty - the state of being free within society from oppressive restrictions
  • synonym: license - freedom to deviate from rules; US spelling variant
  • synonym: poetic license - the freedom of a writer or artist to disregard rules of grammar, syntax, or fact for effect
  • antonym: restraint - moderation or self-control; opposite of excessive freedom
