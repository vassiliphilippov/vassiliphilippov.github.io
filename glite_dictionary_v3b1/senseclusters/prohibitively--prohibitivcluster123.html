prohibitively

adverb
to an extent that prevents something
4.5 5.7
Pronunciation: /prə.ˈhɪb.ɪ.tɪv.liː/ /; / /proʊ.ˈhɪb.ɪ.tɪv.liː/ (AmE), /prə.ˈhɪb.ɪ.tɪv.liː/ /; / /prəʊ.ˈhɪb.ɪ.tɪv.liː/ (BrE)
To such a degree that it prevents something from happening, especially with reference to cost or difficulty.

Examples

A prohibitively high price
Some medications remain prohibitively expensive without proper insurance coverage.
The rent in that part of the city is prohibitively high for most students.
Travel to remote islands is often prohibitively complicated due to limited transportation options.

Related Terms

  • antonym: affordably - at a price or cost that one can afford; not expensive
  • synonym: excessively - to a greater degree than is reasonable or acceptable
