burrow

verb
move or dig through (like an animal)
3.8 3.3
Pronunciation: /ˈbɜːr.oʊ/ /; / /ˈbʌr.oʊ/ (AmE), /ˈbʌ.rəʊ/ (BrE)
to move through something by digging or making a tunnel, typically like an animal in soil or undergrowth

Examples

To burrow into the ground
During winter, some animals burrow through the snow to find shelter.
The children tried to burrow through the pile of autumn leaves.
The rabbit managed to burrow beneath the fence and escape into the garden.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dig - to break up and move earth with a tool, hands, or animal's snout
