contraction

noun
shortened word form (e.g., can't)
Pronunciation: / kənˈtrækʃən / (AmE), / kənˈtrækʃən / (BrE)
a shortened form of a word or group of words, made by omitting or combining certain sounds or letters

Examples

A contraction like 'don't'
In informal writing, you’ll often see contractions to make sentences sound more conversational.
The teacher explained that ‘she’s’ is a contraction for ‘she is’.
Words like can't and won't are common contractions used in speech and writing.
