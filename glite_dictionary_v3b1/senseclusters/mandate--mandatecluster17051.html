mandate

noun
authority granted by electoral victory
Pronunciation: / ˈmænˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈmæn.deɪt / (BrE)
the authority given to a government and its policies as the result of an electoral victory

Examples

Electoral mandate
A narrow win may make it difficult for a government to claim a mandate for radical change.
After the election, the new prime minister claimed a strong mandate to enact reforms.
The president interpreted his victory as a clear mandate from the voters.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authority - the power or right to give orders or make decisions
  • degree lower: permission - the action of officially allowing someone to do a particular thing
