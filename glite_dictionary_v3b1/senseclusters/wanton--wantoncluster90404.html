wanton

adjective
occurring without cause or provocation
8.2 0.60/m
Pronunciation: / Ààw…ëÀên.t…ôn / (AmE), / Ààw…ín.t…ôn / (BrE)
(of an action) occurring without cause, reason, or provocation; deliberate and unrestrained.

Examples

Wanton destruction
He was accused of a wanton act of violence against his neighbor.
The wanton waste of resources during the festival was widely criticized.
The city was shocked by the wanton destruction of the historic building.

Synonyms

  • unprovoked
    6.5 0.94/m
    unprovoked is done without any cause or trigger, while wanton describes deliberate, unrestrained acts often showing cruelty or excess.
  • motiveless
    8.3 0.04/m
    motiveless refers to actions that lack any clear reason, while wanton suggests deliberate, unrestrained behavior often with harmful intent.

Related Terms

  • synonym: motiveless - without apparent motivation or cause
  • synonym: unprovoked - without cause or provocation
