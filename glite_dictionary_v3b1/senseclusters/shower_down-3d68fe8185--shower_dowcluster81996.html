shower down

verb
fall or pour abundantly from above
Pronunciation: / ˈʃaʊɚ daʊn /; / ˈʃaʊ.ər daʊn / (AmE), / ˈʃaʊə daʊn /; / ˈʃaʊ.ə daʊn / (BrE)
to fall or pour down from above in large amounts or numbers, like rain, ash, or sparks

Examples

To shower down ash
Ash began to shower down from the erupting volcano, covering the city in gray dust.
Confetti showered down on the newlyweds as they left the church.
Stars seemed to shower down from the sky during the meteor shower.
As sparks showered down from the welding torch, we kept a safe distance.
Hundreds of confetti pieces began to shower down from the ceiling during the celebration.
Leaves shower down onto the ground every autumn, covering the pathways in gold.
