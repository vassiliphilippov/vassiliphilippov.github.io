spread-eagle

verb
defeat overwhelmingly
Pronunciation: / ˈsprɛd ˌiːɡəl / (AmE), / ˈsprɛd ˌiːɡl̩ / (BrE)
(of a person or team) to defeat disastrously, especially by a large margin or in a dominant fashion.

Examples

To spread-eagle the competition
If they keep playing this well, they’ll spread-eagle the rest of the league by mid-season.
The champion boxer spread-eagled his inexperienced opponent in less than three rounds.
The home team managed to spread-eagle their rivals with a remarkable final score of 42–7.

  • synonym: trounce - to defeat heavily in a contest
