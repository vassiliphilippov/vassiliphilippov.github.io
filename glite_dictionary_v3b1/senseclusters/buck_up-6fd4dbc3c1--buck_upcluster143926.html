buck up

verb
gain or show more courage or cheerfulness
6.5
Pronunciation: / bʌk ʌp / (AmE), / bʌk ʌp / (BrE)
to become or make someone more cheerful, confident, or brave; to encourage oneself or others

Examples

To buck up quickly
After hearing some good news, she finally managed to buck up.
Come on, buck up—things aren't as bad as they seem.
You need to buck up and face the challenge ahead.
She tried to buck up her friend after he failed the exam.
The coach told the team to buck up before the second half.
You need to buck up if you want to get through this tough time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cheer up - to become happier or more optimistic
  • antonym: lose heart - to become discouraged or lose confidence
