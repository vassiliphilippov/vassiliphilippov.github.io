gadfly

noun
persistently annoying or provoking person
Pronunciation: / ˈɡædˌflaɪ / (AmE), / ˈɡæd.flʌɪ / (BrE)
a person who persistently annoys or provokes others, often by criticizing or challenging them

Examples

Political gadfly
His gadfly behavior sometimes frustrated his colleagues, but it spurred important changes.
She played the role of gadfly in the meeting, never letting any issue go unchallenged.
The senator was known as a political gadfly, constantly questioning established policies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nuisance - someone or something causing inconvenience or annoyance
  • synonym: provocateur - someone who provokes others, especially to action
