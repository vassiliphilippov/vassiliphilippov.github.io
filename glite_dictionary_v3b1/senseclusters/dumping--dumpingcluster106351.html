dumping

noun
selling goods abroad below domestic prices
Pronunciation: / ˈdʌmpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈdʌmpɪŋ / (BrE)
the practice of selling goods in a foreign market at a price lower than in the domestic market, often to gain market share or eliminate competition

Examples

Unfair dumping practices
International trade laws are designed to prevent unfair dumping practices.
Many companies have faced penalties for dumping goods below cost in overseas markets.
The government accused the foreign company of dumping steel products on the domestic market.

Related Terms

  • synonym: import quota - a limit on the amount of a product that can be imported
  • synonym: protectionism - the policy of shielding a country's domestic industries from foreign competition
  • synonym: tariff - a tax on imports or exports
