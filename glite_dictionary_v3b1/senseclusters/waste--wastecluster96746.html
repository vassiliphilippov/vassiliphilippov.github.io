waste

noun
legal harm to property or estate value
7.9
Pronunciation: / weɪst / (AmE), / weɪst / (BrE)
(Law) Harm or reduction in the value of property or an estate caused by intentional or negligent act of a tenant or other person in possession.

Examples

[law] Tenant waste
A tenant may be liable for waste if they allow a building to fall into disrepair.
Cutting down the orchard without permission was considered legal waste by the court.
The landlord sued the tenant for committing waste on the property during his lease.
