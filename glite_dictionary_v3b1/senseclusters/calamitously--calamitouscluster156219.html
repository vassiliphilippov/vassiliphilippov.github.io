calamitously

adverb
disastrously
Pronunciation: / kəˈlæmɪtəsli / (AmE), / kəˈlæmɪtəsli / (BrE)
in a way that causes or results in great harm, disaster, or serious misfortune

Examples

Ends calamitously
The expedition ended calamitously when a sudden storm destroyed their equipment.
If the safety measures fail, the consequences could be calamitously severe.
The company invested heavily but calamitously underestimated the risks involved.

Related Terms

  • synonym: catastrophically - in a way that involves great damage or suffering
  • synonym: disastrously - in a way that leads to disaster or great failure
  • antonym: successfully - in a way that achieves the desired result
