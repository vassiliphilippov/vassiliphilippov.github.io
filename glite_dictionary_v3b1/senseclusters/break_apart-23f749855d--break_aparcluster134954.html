break apart

verb
separate into pieces; disassemble
Pronunciation: / breɪk əˈpɑrt / (AmE), / breɪk əˈpɑːt / (BrE)
to separate into pieces, either physically or metaphorically (such as a group or object coming apart)

Examples

To break apart pieces
Rising tension threatened to break apart the group.
She tried to break apart the puzzle to see how it worked.
The old machine finally broke apart after years of heavy use.
If you twist it too hard, the toy will break apart.
The glass vase broke apart when it hit the floor.
The old bridge broke apart during the storm.
After years of tension, the community began to break apart.
If you bend the stick too far, it will break apart.
The glass vase hit the floor and broke apart into tiny shards.

Related Terms

  • antonym: assemble - to put together into a single unit
  • synonym: break up - to separate into smaller pieces or end a relationship
  • synonym: disassemble - to take apart into pieces
  • synonym: disintegrate - to break into small parts or lose cohesion
  • synonym: fall apart - to break into pieces, usually gradually
  • synonym: shatter - to break suddenly into many pieces
