break apart

verb
separate into pieces; disassemble
3.0 1.62/m

to separate into pieces, either physically or metaphorically (such as a group or object coming apart)

Examples

To break apart pieces
Rising tension threatened to break apart the group.
She tried to break apart the puzzle to see how it worked.
The old machine finally broke apart after years of heavy use.
If you twist it too hard, the toy will break apart.
The glass vase broke apart when it hit the floor.
The old bridge broke apart during the storm.

Synonyms

  • break up
    2.7 3.75/m
    break up is used for ending groups or dividing things into smaller parts, while break apart highlights coming into separate pieces.
  • split up
    2.6 0.81/m
    split up is used for dividing groups or things into parts, while break apart covers physical or emotional separation into pieces.
  • split
    1.3 25/m
    split means to cut or divide into parts, while break apart focuses on pieces or groups losing their unity or shape.
  • come apart
    3.1 1.90/m
    come apart falls into pieces by itself, while break apart can describe separation that is caused or happens suddenly.
  • fall apart
    2.8 6.48/m
    fall apart means stop working or collapse emotionally, while break apart simply means separating into pieces or parts.
  • take apart
    3.0 1.51/m
    take apart means to dismantle something piece by piece by action, while break apart can be accidental or metaphorical separation.
  • break
    1.0 131/m
    break often means to damage or make something not work, while break apart stresses separation into many distinct pieces.
  • disintegrate
    5.8 3.13/m
    disintegrate means to fall into very small parts over time, while break apart can be sudden or caused by force.
