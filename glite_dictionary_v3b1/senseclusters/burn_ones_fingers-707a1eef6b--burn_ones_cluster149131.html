burn one's fingers

verb
suffer consequences of a mistake
3.5
Pronunciation: / bɝːn wʌnz ˈfɪŋɡɚz /; / bɝn wʌnz ˈfɪŋɡɚz / (AmE), / bɜːn wʌnz ˈfɪŋɡəz / (BrE)
to suffer negative consequences or harm because of your own mistake or risky action

Examples

To burn one's fingers again
After her last business failed, she decided not to burn her fingers again.
He burned his fingers trying to fix the computer without proper knowledge.
Many investors have burned their fingers by blindly following market trends.
After burning his fingers in the stock market, he became much more risk-averse.
He burned his fingers trusting unreliable partners and now double-checks every detail.
She won't invest in start-ups anymore because she burned her fingers last year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: get one's fingers burned - to suffer from a negative experience, usually as a result of taking risks
  • synonym: suffer the consequences - to experience negative results as a result of one's actions
