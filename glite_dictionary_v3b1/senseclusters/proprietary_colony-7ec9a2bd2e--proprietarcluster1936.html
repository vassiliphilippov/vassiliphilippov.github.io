proprietary colony

noun
colony governed by proprietors (historical)
9.9
Pronunciation: / prəˈpraɪəˌtɛri ˈkɑːləni / (AmE), / prəˈprʌɪət(ə)ri ˈkɒləni / (BrE)
a colony in which the British crown granted land and governing rights to an individual or group, who acted as the colony's proprietor, especially in 17th-century America.

Examples

A proprietary colony
Maryland was originally established as a proprietary colony granted to Lord Baltimore.
Several American colonies, including Pennsylvania, began as proprietary colonies before becoming royal colonies.
Unlike a royal colony, a proprietary colony was governed by individuals with authority from the crown.

Related Terms

  • antonym: royal colony - colony administered directly by the British crown
