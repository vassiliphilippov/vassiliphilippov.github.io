theatricalization

noun
making dramatic
8.9
Pronunciation: /θiː.ə.trɪ.kə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /θɪə.trɪ.kə.lə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the act of making something more dramatic, showy, or suitable for performance, as if for the stage

Examples

The theatricalization of scenes
The theatricalization of the novel brought its characters to life on stage.
Critics praised the theatricalization of the historical event for its creativity.
Through theatricalization, the director turned a simple story into a captivating performance.
The theatricalization of the trial made it seem more like a show than a legal proceeding.
Critics argued that the theatricalization of politics distracts from real issues.
The theatricalization of historical events can sometimes distort the truth for dramatic effect.
The theatricalization of the speech made it seem less genuine to the audience.
Critics noted the theatricalization of everyday events in the director's latest film.
Some people dislike the theatricalization of reality TV shows because it feels artificial.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dramatization - the process of making something into a drama or more dramatic
  • synonym: sensationalism - the use of exciting or shocking stories to attract attention
  • antonym: understatement - the presentation of something as less important or dramatic than it really is
