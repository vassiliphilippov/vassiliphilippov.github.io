unplowed

adjective
not plowed (of fields or land)
6.2 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / ÊŒnËˆplaÊŠd / (AmE), / ÊŒnËˆplaÊŠd / (BrE)
(of land or fields) not having been plowed or turned over with a plow.

Examples

Unplowed field
Heavy snowfall made many rural roads unplowed and difficult to drive on.
The unplowed fields stretched for miles beyond the village.
They decided to leave a portion of the land unplowed for wildlife habitats.
After a harsh winter, parts of the field remained unploughed until spring.
Local wildlife found shelter among the unploughed hedgerows bordering the property.
The farmer left several acres of unploughed land for wildflowers to grow naturally.

Synonyms

  • untilled
    6.6 0.07/m
    untilled is land left uncultivated and rough, while unplowed means it has not been turned over by a plow.
  • unbroken
    5.7 0.26/m
    unbroken is land kept in its natural, continuous state, while unplowed means the soil is not turned over for farming.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ploughed - of land, turned over with a plough
  • antonym: plowed - (of land) turned over or cultivated with a plow
