lap-strake

adjective
overlapping planks (boat hull)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈlæpˌstreɪk / (AmE), / ˈlæpˌstreɪk / (BrE)
(of a boat hull) constructed with planks that overlap along their edges.

Examples

A lap-strake hull
A lap-strake hull is preferred by some sailors for its classic appearance and structural strength.
Boatbuilders demonstrated how lap-strake construction keeps water out more effectively than other designs.
The old fisherman rebuilt his lap-strake skiff using traditional tools and techniques.
