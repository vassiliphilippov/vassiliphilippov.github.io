jam

noun
deliberate electromagnetic interference
Pronunciation: / dʒæm / (AmE), / dʒam / (BrE)
the deliberate radiation or reflection of electromagnetic energy to disrupt the use of electronic devices or systems, especially for military purposes

Examples

Military jam of radar
It is illegal for civilians to jam radio communications in most countries.
Stealth aircraft are equipped with equipment designed to jam hostile missile guidance signals.
The military used a powerful device to jam enemy radar systems during the operation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: jamming - the act or process of interfering with communication signals
