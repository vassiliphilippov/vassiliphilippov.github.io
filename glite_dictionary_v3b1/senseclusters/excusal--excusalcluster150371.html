excusal

noun
official exemption
6.3
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈskjuːzəl / (AmE), / ɪkˈskjuːzəl / (BrE)
the act of officially allowing someone to avoid a duty or obligation

Examples

Medical excusal
The teacher granted an excusal for students who had a valid reason for missing class.
He requested an excusal from work due to a family emergency.
Jury duty excusal is possible if you provide appropriate documentation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exemption - the act of freeing someone from an obligation
  • antonym: obligation - something a person is required to do
