Capulet

proper noun
Shakespearean family name
Pronunciation: / ˈkæpjʊˌlɛt / (AmE), / ˈkæpjʊlɪt / (BrE)
a family name, most famously used for a noble family in Shakespeare's play 'Romeo and Juliet'

Examples

The Capulet family
Juliet was born into the Capulet family, one of the two feuding houses in Verona.
The Capulet ball was the setting for Romeo and Juliet's first meeting.
Many students learn about the Capulet family when studying Shakespeare in school.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Montague - the rival family to the Capulets in 'Romeo and Juliet'
