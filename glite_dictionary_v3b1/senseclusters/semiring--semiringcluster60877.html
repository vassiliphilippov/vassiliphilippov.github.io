semiring

noun
algebraic structure
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈse.mɪ.rɪŋ/ /; / /ˈse.mə.rɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈse.mɪ.rɪŋ/ /; / /ˈse.mə.rɪŋ/ (BrE)
a mathematical structure with two operations, like addition and multiplication, but not requiring negatives or subtraction

Examples

A semiring in math
The set of natural numbers forms a semiring under addition and multiplication.
In computer science, a semiring can be used to model certain types of automata.
Researchers studied the properties of a new semiring to solve optimization problems.
