toughness

noun
material's ability to absorb energy before cracking
1.1 3.8
Pronunciation: /ˈtʌf.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈtʌf.nəs/ (BrE)
The ability of a material, especially a metal, to absorb energy and resist fracturing, combining both elasticity and hardness.

Examples

Metal toughness test
Engineers test the toughness of metals to ensure they can withstand high impacts.
Improving the alloy increased the material's toughness and made it less likely to crack under stress.
The toughness of steel allows it to be used in the construction of skyscrapers and bridges.

Related Terms

  • antonym: brittleness - the tendency of a material to crack or break without significant deformation
  • synonym: hardness - the resistance of a material to deformation or scratching
