haloing

noun
zone around colonies
8.8
Pronunciation: /ˈheɪ.loʊ.ɪŋ/ (AmE), /ˈheɪ.ləʊ.ɪŋ/ (BrE)
the formation of a clear or colored zone around microbial colonies on a growth medium, indicating chemical or enzymatic activity

Examples

Bacterial haloing
The scientist observed haloing around the bacterial colonies, which suggested enzyme activity.
Agar plates often display haloing when bacteria break down certain nutrients in the medium.
The presence of haloing can help researchers identify specific microbial processes in the lab.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clearing - the removal of color or opacity in a medium around colonies
