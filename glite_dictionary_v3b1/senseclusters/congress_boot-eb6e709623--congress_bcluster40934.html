congress boot

noun
ankle-high boot with elastic sides
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑŋɡrəs buːt / (AmE), / ˈkɒŋɡrəs buːt / (BrE)
an ankle-high boot with elastic gussets on the sides, typically worn in the 19th century.

Examples

A pair of congress boots
He wore a polished pair of congress boots to the formal dinner.
The congress boot was a popular style in the nineteenth century.
Victorian gentlemen often chose the congress boot for comfort and elegance.
