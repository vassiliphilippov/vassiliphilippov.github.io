turn up one's nose

verb
show disdain or contempt
Pronunciation: / tɝn ʌp wʌnz noʊz / (AmE), / tɜːn ʌp wʌnz nəʊz / (BrE)
to show disdain or contempt for something; to treat something as unworthy or not good enough

Examples

He turned up his nose at food
He turned up his nose at the idea of eating leftovers.
She tends to turn up her nose at anything that isn't designer brand.
The committee turned up its nose at the proposal, calling it unoriginal.
He turned up his nose when offered a job he thought was beneath him.
She turned up her nose at the cheap wine, clearly unimpressed by the selection.
Some children turn up their noses at vegetables, even before trying them.

Related Terms

  • antonym: admire - to regard with respect or warm approval
  • synonym: despise - to regard with contempt or distaste
  • synonym: look down on - to regard with a sense of superiority or disdain
  • synonym: scorn - to feel or show contempt or disdain for someone or something
  • synonym: snub - to reject or ignore disdainfully
