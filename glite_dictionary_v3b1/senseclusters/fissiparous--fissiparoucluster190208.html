fissiparous

adjective
tending to promote division or separation
Pronunciation: / fɪˈsɪpərəs / (AmE), / fɪˈsɪpərəs / (BrE)
Tending to break up or promote division into separate parts, groups, or entities, especially in relation to political or social organization.

Examples

Fissiparous groups
Critics accused the leader of fostering a fissiparous atmosphere within the organization.
Some feared that the fissiparous nature of the coalition would lead to its collapse.
The party's fissiparous tendencies became clear during the heated debate over policy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: divisive - tending to cause disagreement or hostility between people
  • antonym: unifying - causing people or things to be joined or brought together
