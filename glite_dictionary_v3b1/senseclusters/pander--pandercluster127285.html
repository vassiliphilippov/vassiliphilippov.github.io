pander

verb
to indulge or gratify an improper desire
Pronunciation: / ˈpændər /; / ˈpændɚ / (AmE), / ˈpandə /; / ˈpændə / (BrE)
to do or say what someone wants, especially when it is immoral or bad, in order to please them

Examples

To pander to desires
Some politicians are accused of pandering to voters just to win support.
The tabloids often pander to people’s basest instincts for sensational news.
You shouldn’t pander to every unreasonable demand your child makes.
He was notorious for having pandered to powerful men, arranging secret meetings.
In past centuries, an unscrupulous servant might pander to the desires of wealthy clients.
The law strictly punishes anyone who attempts to pander for profit.

  • antonym: resist - to withstand the action or effect of something; to refuse to yield
