flatline

verb
to stop functioning
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈflætˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈflætˌlaɪn / (BrE)
to show no activity or to stop functioning, especially as indicated by a flat line on a monitor (often meaning to die or stop working)

Examples

The patient flatlined
After several minutes without a heartbeat, the patient finally flatlined.
Sales began to flatline after the product lost popularity.
If the machine flatlines, it means there is no electrical activity being detected.
After several minutes without a pulse, the patient finally flatlined in the emergency room.
If the economy continues to flatline, many businesses may struggle to survive.
The heart monitor began to flatline, signaling a medical emergency.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cease - to stop happening or existing
  • antonym: continue - to keep happening or existing without stopping
