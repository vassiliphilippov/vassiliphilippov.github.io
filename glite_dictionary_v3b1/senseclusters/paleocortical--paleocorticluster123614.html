paleocortical

adjective
relating to the olfactory cortex
Pronunciation: / ˌpeɪlioʊˈkɔrtɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌpæliəʊˈkɔːtɪkəl / (BrE)
relating to the oldest part of the cerebral cortex, especially the olfactory cortex involved in the sense of smell

Examples

Paleocortical brain area
Damage to the paleocortical areas can affect the sense of smell in mammals.
Researchers study the paleocortical structures to understand evolutionary changes in brain function.
The paleocortical regions are primarily responsible for processing olfactory information in many vertebrates.

Related Terms

  • antonym: neocortical - relating to the neocortex, the most recent evolutionary part of the cerebral cortex
