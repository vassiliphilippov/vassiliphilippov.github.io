infrared emission

noun
radiation with wavelengths longer than visible light
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnfrəˈrɛd ɪˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪnfrəˈrɛd ɪˈmɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
electromagnetic radiation with wavelengths longer than visible light but shorter than radio waves, typically emitted by warm objects

Examples

Infrared emission from stars
Astronomers study the infrared emission of distant galaxies to learn about star formation.
Every object above absolute zero produces some amount of infrared emission.
The sensor is designed to detect infrared emission from living organisms.
