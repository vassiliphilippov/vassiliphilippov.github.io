doze off

verb
fall asleep unintentionally
Pronunciation: / doʊz ɔf / (AmE), / dəʊz ɒf / (BrE)
to fall asleep, usually unintentionally and for a short time

Examples

To doze off quietly
Despite all her efforts, she began to doze off during the long meeting.
He was embarrassed when he dozed off on the train and missed his stop.
I often doze off while reading in bed at night.
He kept dozing off during the long meeting despite his best efforts to stay awake.
I nearly dozed off while reading on the train.
She often finds herself dozing off in front of the television in the evening.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nod off - fall asleep, often briefly and unintentionally
  • antonym: wake up - stop sleeping and become awake
