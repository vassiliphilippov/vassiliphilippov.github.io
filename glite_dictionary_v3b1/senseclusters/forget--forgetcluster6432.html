forget

verb
stop remembering or dismiss from the mind
Pronunciation: / fərˈɡɛt / (AmE), / fəˈɡɛt / (BrE)
To deliberately put something out of one's mind or stop thinking about it.

Examples

Forget bad memories
He wished he could simply forget everything that had happened.
She tries to forget the pain of her childhood by keeping busy.
Sometimes it's healthier to forget old arguments and move on.

Related Terms

  • antonym: remember - to recall or keep in mind
  • synonym: repress - to subdue or push out of conscious memory
