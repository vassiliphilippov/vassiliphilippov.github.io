load line

noun
mark on ship for safe loading limit
Pronunciation: / loʊd laɪn / (AmE), / ləʊd laɪn / (BrE)
a line marked on a ship's hull showing the maximum depth to which the vessel may be safely loaded

Examples

The load line mark
After a heavy rainfall, water reached just below the load line on the freighter's hull.
Regulations require every commercial vessel to have a clearly visible load line.
The inspector checked the load line to ensure the ship was not overloaded before departure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Plimsoll line - the load line, especially as established by regulation
