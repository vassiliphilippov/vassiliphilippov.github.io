respray

verb
to repaint surface
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈspreɪ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈspreɪ / (BrE)
to spray something again with a liquid, especially to apply a new layer of paint or treatment

Examples

To respray a car
After the accident, they decided to respray the car to hide the damage.
The old bicycle looked brand new after he resprayed it with a fresh coat of blue paint.
Many people choose to respray their kitchen cabinets instead of replacing them.
After noticing missed spots, the workers had to respray the fence with protective coating.
The gardener decided to respray the plants to ensure all pests were eliminated.
If the color fades, you may need to respray your car to restore its shine.

Related Terms

  • synonym: spray - to apply a liquid to a surface in tiny drops
  • antonym: strip - to remove paint or covering from a surface
