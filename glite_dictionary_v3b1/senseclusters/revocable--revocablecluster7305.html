revocable

adjective
able to be cancelled or withdrawn
2.9 6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈre.və.kə.bəl/ (AmE), /ˈre.və.kə.bəl/ (BrE)
able to be withdrawn, cancelled, or annulled; not permanent

Examples

A revocable trust
A revocable trust can be changed or cancelled by its creator at any time.
The contract was signed under revocable terms, allowing either party to withdraw with notice.
They issued a revocable permit for the new construction project.
A revocable trust allows the settlor to make changes or terminate the trust as needed.
His license was revocable if he failed to meet certain conditions.
The contract is revocable at any time by either party with written notice.
