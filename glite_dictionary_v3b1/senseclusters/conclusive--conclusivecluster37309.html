conclusive

adjective
decisive; ending doubt or uncertainty
4.8 3.71/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàkluÀês…™v / (AmE), / k…ônÀàkluÀês…™v / (BrE)
(of evidence or argument) serving to prove a case, decisive, or ending doubt or uncertainty.

Examples

A conclusive argument
Her argument was so conclusive that no one dared to disagree.
The detective presented conclusive evidence that proved the suspect's guilt.
The medical test results were conclusive and confirmed the doctor's diagnosis.

Synonyms

  • decisive
    3.2 0.99/m
    decisive is clear and settles a choice or result quickly, while conclusive focuses on ending doubt about evidence.
  • definitive
    5.6 6.22/m
    definitive gives a final, authoritative decision or answer, while conclusive is evidence that ends doubt in a case.
  • unequivocal
    6.7 2.30/m
    unequivocal is completely clear with no ambiguity, while conclusive is evidence or argument that proves a case.
  • undeniable
    4.2 4.17/m
    undeniable seems impossible to deny or argue against, while conclusive means the evidence actually proves the matter.
  • proven
    3.2 0.03/m
    proven is shown to be true by tests or facts, while conclusive describes evidence that ends doubt and settles a case.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decisive - having the power to decide; conclusive
  • antonym: inconclusive - not leading to a definite result or ending doubt
