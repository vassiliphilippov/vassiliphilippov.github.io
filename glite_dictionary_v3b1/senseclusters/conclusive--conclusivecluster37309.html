conclusive

adjective
decisive; ending doubt or uncertainty
2.6 3.9
Pronunciation: / kənˈkluːsɪv / (AmE), / kənˈkluːsɪv / (BrE)
(of evidence or argument) serving to prove a case, decisive, or ending doubt or uncertainty.

Examples

A conclusive argument
Her argument was so conclusive that no one dared to disagree.
The detective presented conclusive evidence that proved the suspect's guilt.
The medical test results were conclusive and confirmed the doctor's diagnosis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decisive - having the power to decide; conclusive
  • antonym: inconclusive - not leading to a definite result or ending doubt
