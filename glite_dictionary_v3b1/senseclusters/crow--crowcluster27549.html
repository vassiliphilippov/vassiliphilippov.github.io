crow

noun
large black bird with a harsh call
7.4
Pronunciation: / kroʊ / (AmE), / krəʊ / (BrE)
a large, black bird with a harsh, loud call, belonging to the genus Corvus

Examples

A black crow
A crow perched noisily on the telephone wire near the house.
A flock of crows gathered in the field at dusk.
In folklore, seeing a crow is sometimes considered an omen.

Related Terms

  • synonym: raven - a larger member of the genus Corvus with a heavier bill and shaggier throat feathers
  • synonym: rook - a Eurasian member of the crow family distinguished by its bare, greyish face
