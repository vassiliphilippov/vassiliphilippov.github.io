dance around the maypole

verb
take part in a traditional May Day dance
3.7
Pronunciation: / dæns əˈraʊnd ðə ˈmeɪˌpoʊl / (AmE), / dɑːns əˈraʊnd ðə ˈmeɪˌpəʊl / (BrE)
to take part in a traditional dance in which people circle a tall decorated pole, often as part of May Day festivities

Examples

To dance around the maypole
Every spring, children dance around the maypole to celebrate May Day in the village.
Tourists gathered to watch locals dance around the maypole at the cultural festival.
We all joined hands and began to dance around the maypole in the sunny park.
