trail

noun
a rough path through wild country
1.6 42/m
Pronunciation: / treÉªl / (AmE), / treÉªl / (BrE)
a rough path or track made through wild or hilly country, often for walking, hiking, or riding

Examples

A hiking trail
She enjoys running on the local trail every morning before work.
The mountain trail is steep in some places but offers breathtaking views.
We followed the trail through the forest until we reached the lake.

Synonyms

  • path
    1.0 74/m
    a path is a general route for walking, cycling, or travel, while a trail is a rough outdoor track for hiking or riding.
  • footpath
    1.9 0.92/m
    a footpath is a narrow walking route through the countryside, while a trail is often rough and used for hiking or riding.
  • pathway
    2.7 6.04/m
    a pathway is a beaten route used by people and animals and often appears in formal speech, while a trail is rough and outdoors.
  • track
    2.2 55/m
    a track is a mark or route made by people, animals, or vehicles and is broader, while a trail is a rough path for walking or riding.
