Americanize

verb
make American in character or style
9.6 5.3
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈmer.ɪ.kə.naɪz/ (AmE), /ə.ˈmer.ɪ.kə.naɪz/ (BrE)
to make something or someone more American in character, style, or culture; to adopt American customs or ways

Examples

To Americanize culture
Living in California for a decade has thoroughly Americanized her tastes and habits.
Many companies try to Americanize their products to appeal to U.S. consumers.
Some critics argue that globalization is helping to Americanize cultures around the world.
After moving to New York, he gradually began to Americanize his accent and habits.
As immigrants spent more time in the country, their traditions started to Americanize.
Several companies chose to Americanize their brands to appeal to the U.S. market.
Living in New York for five years really Americanised her tastes and habits.
Many European cities have become increasingly Americanised in recent decades.
The restaurant chain plans to Americanise its menu to appeal to tourists.
