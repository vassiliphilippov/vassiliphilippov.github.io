waterlog

verb
to saturate with water
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtərˌlɔɡ / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəlɒɡ / (BrE)
to soak or fill something with water so much that it becomes saturated and cannot absorb more

Examples

To waterlog the soil
Heavy rains can waterlog the fields, making it difficult for crops to grow.
If you waterlog your houseplants, their roots may begin to rot.
The tent became waterlogged after the storm, making it unusable for the night.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dry - to remove moisture from something
  • synonym: soak - to make something very wet by immersing it in liquid
