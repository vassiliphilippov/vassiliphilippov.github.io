light-headed

adjective
feeling dizzy or faint
2.3 3.5
Pronunciation: /laɪt ˈhɛd.ɪd/ /; / /laɪt ˈhɛd.əd/ (AmE), /laɪt ˈhɛd.ɪd/ /; / /laɪt ˈhɛd.əd/ (BrE)
feeling dizzy, weak, or as if you might faint

Examples

Feel light-headed
After skipping breakfast, she began to feel light-headed and had to sit down.
He became light-headed after spending hours working in the sun.
Standing up too quickly can sometimes make you light-headed.
Climbing the stairs too quickly can leave some people light-headed for a moment.
She felt light-headed after donating blood at the clinic.
Skipping lunch made me feel light-headed during the afternoon meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clear-headed - able to think in a calm and sensible way
  • synonym: dizzy - feeling as if you might lose your balance or fall
  • synonym: faint - to feel weak and as if you are about to lose consciousness
