stressor

noun
tool for overloading servers
Pronunciation: / ˈstrɛsər / (AmE), / ˈstrɛsə / (BrE)
a computer program or online service that overloads a website or server with traffic, often used in denial-of-service attacks

Examples

A stressor attack
The hacker used a stresser to bring down the company's website for several hours.
Law enforcement agencies are cracking down on illegal stresser services sold online.
Many stresser tools are advertised as testing services but are often used for attacks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: booter - an online service used to launch denial-of-service attacks
  • synonym: denial-of-service attack - an attack that makes a computer or network unavailable by overwhelming it with traffic
