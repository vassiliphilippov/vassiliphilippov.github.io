sonata

noun
classical musical composition of several movements
Pronunciation: / səˈnɑtə / (AmE), / səˈnɑːtə / (BrE)
a musical composition, typically for one or two instruments, consisting of three or four movements with contrasting forms and tempos

Examples

A sonata movement
Beethoven's "Moonlight Sonata" is one of his most celebrated works.
Many composers wrote at least one sonata for violin and piano.
The pianist performed a difficult sonata at the concert last night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: suite - a set of instrumental compositions, typically in several movements
  • degree higher: symphony - a large-scale musical composition for orchestra, usually with multiple movements
