cryptosporidium

noun
microscopic parasite
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌkrɪptoʊspəˈrɪdiəm / (AmE), / ˌkrɪptəʊspəˈrɪdiəm / (BrE)
a microscopic parasite that can cause stomach or intestinal illness, often spread through contaminated water

Examples

Cryptosporidium parasite
Drinking untreated water can expose you to cryptosporidium and cause severe stomach problems.
The laboratory confirmed the presence of cryptosporidium in the water sample.
After swimming in the lake, several people became ill with cryptosporidium infection.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with cryptosporidium after several days of severe diarrhea.
Drinking contaminated water can expose you to cryptosporidium and other harmful parasites.
Proper handwashing is important to prevent the spread of cryptosporidium in childcare settings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: giardia - a microscopic parasite causing intestinal infection
