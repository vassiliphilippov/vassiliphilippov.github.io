fasces

noun
ancient Roman symbol of authority
Pronunciation: / ˈfæsiːz / (AmE), / ˈfæsiːz / (BrE)
A bundle of rods with an axe blade protruding, carried in ancient Rome as a symbol of a magistrate's power, and later used as a symbol of authority or fascism.

Examples

The fasces symbol
On some government buildings, you can see carvings of fasces to represent law and power.
The fasces became a prominent emblem of the Fascist Party in 20th-century Italy.
The Roman consul was preceded by lictors bearing the fasces as a symbol of his authority.
