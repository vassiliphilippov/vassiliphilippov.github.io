indictable

adjective
liable to be charged by indictment
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdaɪtəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈdaɪtəbəl / (BrE)
(of an offense) liable to be charged or prosecuted by indictment; able to be formally accused of a crime in a court of law.

Examples

Indictable offense
Fraud is considered an indictable offense in many jurisdictions.
He was arrested on several indictable charges related to embezzlement.
Only serious crimes, such as burglary, are usually indictable in the court system.
