flare

noun
sudden burst of flame or light
Pronunciation: / flɛr / (AmE), / fleə / (BrE)
a sudden burst or brief period of bright light or flame

Examples

A sudden flare of light
A sudden flare lit up the night sky after the explosion.
As the wind blew harder, a flare shot up from the campfire.
The chemical reaction produced a brilliant flare visible for miles.
A single red flare lit up the night sky above the stranded hikers.
Emergency vehicles may use a flare to signal their position during accidents.
The sailors fired a flare to attract the attention of the rescuers.
A flare from the Sun can disrupt satellite communications on Earth.
Astronomers observed a massive flare linked to a group of sunspots last night.
The intense radiation from the solar flare caused temporary radio interference.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beacon - a fire or light set up in a high position as a warning or signal
  • synonym: flash - a sudden burst of light
  • synonym: signal - an action or device that conveys information or instructions
  • synonym: solar flare - an eruption of electromagnetic radiation from the Sun's atmosphere
