absinth

noun
a green liqueur with wormwood and anise
Pronunciation: / ˈæb.sɪnθ / (AmE), / ˈæb.sɪnθ / (BrE)
a strong green alcoholic liqueur flavored with wormwood and anise, traditionally made in Europe

Examples

A glass of absinth
Drinking too much absinth was rumored to cause hallucinations.
Many artists in 19th-century Paris drank absinth for inspiration.
The bartender served a glass of green absinth with a sugar cube on a spoon.
