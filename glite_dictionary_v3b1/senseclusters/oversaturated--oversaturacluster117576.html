oversaturated

adjective
too much solute
6.8
Pronunciation: /oʊ.vər.ˈsætʃ.ə.reɪ.tɪd/ /; / /oʊ.vər.ˈsæt.ə.reɪ.tɪd/ (AmE), /əʊ.və.ˈsætʃ.ə.reɪ.tɪd/ /; / /əʊ.və.ˈsæt.ə.reɪ.tɪd/ (BrE)
containing more dissolved solute than can normally be held at a given temperature, especially in a solution

Examples

Oversaturated solution
The chemist noticed that the oversaturated solution began to form crystals when cooled.
If a solution is oversaturated, even a small disturbance can cause the excess solute to precipitate.
The experiment failed because the mixture became oversaturated and could not dissolve any more salt.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: saturated - containing the maximum amount of dissolved solute possible at a given temperature
  • synonym: supersaturated - containing more dissolved material than could be dissolved by the solvent under normal circumstances
  • antonym: unsaturated - containing less than the maximum amount of dissolved solute possible
