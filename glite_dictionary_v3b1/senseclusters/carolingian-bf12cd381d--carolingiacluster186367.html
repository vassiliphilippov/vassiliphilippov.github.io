Carolingian

noun
member of the Carolingian dynasty
Pronunciation: / ˌkærəˈlɪndʒiən / (AmE), / ˌkærəˈlɪndʒiən / (BrE)
a member of the Carolingian dynasty, a Frankish noble family that ruled parts of Western Europe from the 8th to 10th centuries

Examples

A Carolingian ruler
Some historians argue that no Carolingian matched Charlemagne’s influence.
The lands ruled by each Carolingian often shifted due to inheritance customs.
The museum features artifacts once owned by a prominent Carolingian.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Merovingian - a member of the dynasty preceding the Carolingians in France
