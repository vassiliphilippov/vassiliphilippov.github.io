overshielding

noun
excessive protection or shielding
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈʃiːldɪŋ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈʃiːldɪŋ / (BrE)
The act or result of providing more protection or shielding than is necessary or desirable.

Examples

Avoid overshielding in design
Engineers must avoid overshielding in circuit design, as it can cause unwanted side effects.
Parents sometimes risk overshielding their children from everyday challenges.
The laboratory found that overshielding the device interfered with its intended function.
