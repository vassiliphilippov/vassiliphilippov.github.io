flash bulb

noun
momentary photographic light source
2.3
Pronunciation: / flæʃ bʌlb / (AmE), / flæʃ bʌlb / (BrE)
a small electric lamp that produces a brief, intense burst of light for taking photographs

Examples

A flash bulb for photos
In the old days, cameras needed a flash bulb to take pictures indoors.
Suddenly, a flash bulb popped as the reporter snapped a quick photo.
The photographer replaced the spent flash bulb before taking another shot.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flash - a sudden brief burst of bright light
