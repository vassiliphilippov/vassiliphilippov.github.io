lame duck

noun
elected official near end of term
6.7 5.9
Pronunciation: /leɪm dʌk/ (AmE), /leɪm dʌk/ (BrE)
an elected official or leader who is still in office but has lost power or influence, usually after losing an election or nearing the end of their term

Examples

A lame duck president
After losing the election, the president became a lame duck during his final months in office.
Congress passed the bill while the lame duck session was still underway.
The outgoing mayor, now a lame duck, struggled to push through new policies.
After the election loss, the senator became a lame duck with little political influence.
As his final term drew to a close, the governor felt like a lame duck in office.
The CEO was considered a lame duck during the transition to new management.

Related Terms

  • synonym: caretaker - a person who temporarily carries out a leader's duties; acting replacement
  • synonym: incumbent - a person currently holding office
  • synonym: outgoing - leaving a position of power or responsibility, especially in government
