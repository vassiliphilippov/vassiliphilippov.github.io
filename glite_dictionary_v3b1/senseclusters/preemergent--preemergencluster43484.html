preemergent

adjective
before emergence
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːɪˈmɜrdʒənt / (AmE), / ˌpriːɪˈmɜːdʒənt / (BrE)
applied or occurring before the appearance or emergence of something, especially plants or weeds

Examples

Preemergent herbicide treatment
Gardeners often apply preemergent herbicides in early spring to prevent weed growth.
The preemergent treatment must be used before the seeds begin to sprout.
Using a preemergent product can help keep your lawn free of unwanted plants.

Related Terms

  • antonym: postemergent - applied or occurring after the appearance or emergence of something
