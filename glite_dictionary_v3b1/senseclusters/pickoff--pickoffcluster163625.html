pickoff

noun
distant shooting incident
Pronunciation: / ˈpɪkˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈpɪkɒf / (BrE)
an instance of shooting and killing or wounding a target from a distance, especially by a sniper or marksman

Examples

Sniper pickoff
The team's morale dropped after the sudden pickoff of their best sniper during the mission.
A well-timed pickoff by the marksman changed the outcome of the battle.
The soldiers moved cautiously to avoid becoming the next pickoff in the open field.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ambush - a surprise attack from a hidden position
  • synonym: sniping - shooting at someone from a hidden position at a distance
