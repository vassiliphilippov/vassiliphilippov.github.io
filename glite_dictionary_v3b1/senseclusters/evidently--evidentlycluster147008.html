evidently

adverb
clearly; obviously, based on evidence
Pronunciation: / ˈɛv.ə.dənt.li / (AmE), / ˈɛv.ɪ.dənt.li / (BrE)
Clearly or obviously; in a way that is easily seen or understood from the evidence or facts.

Examples

He evidently forgot
He had evidently forgotten about the meeting, as he did not attend.
She was evidently upset by the news, judging by the expression on her face.
The room was evidently empty, with no signs of recent activity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: apparently - As far as one knows or can see; seemingly.
  • synonym: obviously - In a way that is easily perceived or understood; clearly.
  • antonym: secretly - In a way that is not meant to be known by others; covertly.
