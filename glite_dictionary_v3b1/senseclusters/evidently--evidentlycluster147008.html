evidently

adverb
clearly; obviously, based on evidence
3.5 13/m

Clearly or obviously; in a way that is easily seen or understood from the evidence or facts.

Examples

He evidently forgot
He had evidently forgotten about the meeting, as he did not attend.
She was evidently upset by the news, judging by the expression on her face.
The room was evidently empty, with no signs of recent activity.

Synonyms

  • clearly
    1.0 102/m
    clearly is used to say something is easy to see or understand by anyone, while evidently suggests this follows from facts.
  • obviously
    1.0 143/m
    obviously states a fact that most people notice quickly, while evidently suggests the conclusion comes from shown evidence.
  • demonstrably
    5.5 1.59/m
    demonstrably is proved or shown by facts or tests, while evidently means something appears clear from available evidence.
  • plainly
    1.8 5.83/m
    plainly says something in simple, direct language that is easy to grasp, while evidently points to proof or signs.
  • unmistakably
    4.1 2.05/m
    unmistakably is so clear that it cannot be mixed up with anything else, while evidently suggests likely truth from facts.
  • distinctly
    3.2 7.47/m
    distinctly is noticed clearly by sight, sound, or thought, while evidently implies a clear result from the evidence.
  • patently
    5.6 2.34/m
    patently is often used to stress a clear or obvious error, while evidently more often suggests something follows from the facts.
  • unequivocally
    6.9 2.86/m
    unequivocally leaves no doubt and sounds formal and strong, while evidently is more neutral and shows something from evidence.
  • manifestly
    5.6 1.39/m
    manifestly is a formal word for showing clear truth in a public or official way, while evidently points to everyday evidence.
