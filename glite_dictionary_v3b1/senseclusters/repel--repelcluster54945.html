Synonyms

  • beat back
    6.0 0.96/m

  • force back
    4.9 0.67/m

  • push back
    3.9 4.05/m

  • fend off
    3.9 2.89/m
    fend off describes defending yourself to stop harm or trouble, while repel describes actively driving someone or something away.
  • fight off
    3.7 2.42/m
    fight off shows success in resisting an attack or illness, while repel is the act of forcing attackers or things away.
  • rebuff
    7.9 0.30/m
    rebuff often means a sharp rejection or refusal, while repel mainly refers to forcing people or things away physically or by influence.
  • ward off
    4.4 3.50/m
    ward off suggests preventing danger from coming near, often by action or charm, while repel means driving it away.
  • keep away
