precip

verb
to form a solid
8.3
Pronunciation: / prɪˈsɪp / (AmE), / prɪˈsɪp / (BrE)
to cause a substance to separate from a solution as a solid, especially in laboratory contexts; informal for 'precipitate'.

Examples

To precip a solid
The chemist decided to precip the salt by adding silver nitrate to the solution.
You need to precip the proteins before running the next test.
After mixing the reagents, they waited for the compound to precip out of the solution.
