Sacred Trinity

noun
the Christian doctrine of three persons in one God
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌseɪkrɪd ˈtrɪnɪti / (AmE), / ˌseɪkrɪd ˈtrɪnɪti / (BrE)
(in Christian theology) the union of the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit as three persons in one God.

Examples

The Sacred Trinity belief
Belief in the Sacred Trinity distinguishes mainstream Christian theology from other monotheistic religions.
During the service, the pastor spoke about the mystery of the Sacred Trinity.
The doctrine of the Sacred Trinity is central to most Christian faiths.
