farcical

adjective
absurd or like a farce
8.5 0.66/m
Pronunciation: / Ààf…ërs…™k…ôl / (AmE), / Ààf…ëÀês…™k…ôl / (BrE)
absurd or ridiculous, resembling a farce; extremely silly or improbable, often in a humorous way

Examples

A farcical situation
It was a farcical situation when the committee forgot to invite its own chairperson to the meeting.
The play turned into a farcical series of misunderstandings that had the audience in stitches.
Their attempt to escape was so badly planned it became almost farcical.

Synonyms

  • ridiculous
    1.6 42/m
    ridiculous describes something extremely silly or unreasonable in many situations, while farcical suggests a silly situation like a staged comedy.
  • ludicrous
    6.0 5.46/m
    ludicrous feels more extreme and harsh, while farcical focuses on silly, staged or comic situations people laugh at.
  • absurd
    4.4 17/m
    absurd means wildly unreasonable or illogical in idea or action, while farcical suggests a funny, staged or dramatic silliness.
  • laughable
    4.1 3.89/m
    laughable is something deserving ridicule or scorn because it fails, while farcical is silly and like a comic play.
  • comical
    3.1 3.27/m
    comical causes laughter by being unusual or amusing, while farcical is more like an absurd staged scene in a play.
  • risible
    8.0 0.18/m
    risible is a formal word meaning laughable or able to cause laughter, while farcical is more plain and tied to comic scenes.
