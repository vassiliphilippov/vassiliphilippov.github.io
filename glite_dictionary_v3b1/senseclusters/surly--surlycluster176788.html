surly

adjective
bad-tempered and unfriendly
7.2 1.59/m
Pronunciation: / Ààs…ùÀêli / (AmE), / Ààs…úÀêli / (BrE)
bad-tempered and unfriendly; inclined to anger or irritation, often with a threatening manner

Examples

A surly person
After a long, stressful day, Emma became noticeably surly with her coworkers.
His surly reply ended the conversation abruptly.
The usually surly doorman surprised us by smiling warmly today.

Synonyms

    5.7 0.26/m
    ill-tempered is easily annoyed and often grumpy, while surly suggests unfriendly mood and a sometimes threatening manner.
  • curmudgeonly
    9.5 0.29/m
    curmudgeonly is cranky and bad-tempered, often in older people, while surly is unfriendly and can seem threatening.
  • cantankerous
    8.6 0.62/m
    cantankerous is argumentative and hard to deal with, while surly focuses more on an unfriendly, hostile manner.
  • waspish
    8.1 0.04/m
    waspish is quick to speak sharply and sting others, while surly is openly bad-tempered and often physically threatening.
  • ornery
    8.5 0.81/m
    ornery is stubborn and easily annoyed in informal talk, while surly is more openly unfriendly and menacing in tone.
  • splenetic
    9.9 0.10/m
    splenetic is short-tempered and rare in modern speech, while surly is common and clearly unfriendly or threatening.
