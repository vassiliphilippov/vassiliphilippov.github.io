informational RNA

noun
type of RNA carrying genetic code for protein synthesis
4.7
Pronunciation: /ɪn.fər.ˈmeɪ.ʃən.əl ɑːr.en.ˈeɪ/ /; / /ɪn.fɔːr.ˈmeɪ.ʃən.əl ɑːr.en.ˈeɪ/ (AmE), /ɪn.fə.ˈmeɪ.ʃən.əl ɑː.en.ˈeɪ/ /; / /ɪn.fɔː.ˈmeɪ.ʃən.əl ɑː.en.ˈeɪ/ (BrE)
RNA that carries genetic information from DNA in the nucleus to the ribosome, serving as a template for protein synthesis; also called messenger RNA.

Examples

Informational RNA carries code
During protein synthesis, informational RNA delivers the genetic code from DNA to the ribosome.
Scientists often refer to informational RNA as messenger RNA or mRNA in their research papers.
Without informational RNA, the instructions for building proteins could not leave the cell nucleus.
