referentiality

noun
quality of making references
9.6
Pronunciation: /re.fə.ˈren.ʃəl.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /rə.fə.ˈren.ʃəl.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /re.fə.ˈren.ʃəl.ɪ.tiː/ /; / /rə.fə.ˈren.ʃəl.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
the quality of a text, artwork, or other work that contains or makes explicit references to other texts, works, events, or sources

Examples

Referentiality in literature
The novel's referentiality to famous myths adds depth for readers familiar with the stories.
Some critics praised the film's referentiality to earlier works in the genre.
The artist is known for the strong referentiality in her paintings, often quoting classic masterpieces.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allusion - an indirect reference to something
  • synonym: intertextuality - the relationship between different texts through references or influences
