exsanguinate

verb
to drain vitality
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪk.ˈsæŋ.gwɪ.neɪt/ /; / /ɪk.ˈsæŋ.gwə.neɪt/ (AmE), /ɛk.ˈsæŋ.gwɪ.neɪt/ /; / /ɪk.ˈsæŋ.gwɪ.neɪt/ (BrE)
to deprive something of vitality, strength, or essential resources, making it weak or ineffective (figurative use)

Examples

To exsanguinate a system
Years of budget cuts began to exsanguinate the public health system, leaving clinics understaffed and underfunded.
The constant demands of the project threatened to exsanguinate the team's motivation and energy.
If we continue to ignore maintenance, these policies will exsanguinate our infrastructure over time.

  • antonym: invigorate - to give strength or energy to
  • synonym: weaken - to make less strong or effective
