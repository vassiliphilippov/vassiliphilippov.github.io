derepression

noun
release of repression
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːrɪˈprɛʃən / (AmE), / ˌdiːrɪˈprɛʃən / (BrE)
the process of reducing or removing psychological repression, allowing previously suppressed thoughts or feelings to become conscious.

Examples

Derepression of memories
Through therapy, the patient experienced derepression of childhood memories that had been hidden for years.
The psychologist explained that derepression can sometimes lead to emotional breakthroughs.
After several sessions, a gradual derepression of long-suppressed feelings became evident.

Related Terms

  • synonym: catharsis - the process of releasing strong or repressed emotions
  • antonym: repression - the act of keeping distressing thoughts or feelings unconscious
