nasal canthus

noun
inner corner of the eye
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈneɪzəl ˈkænθəs / (AmE), / ˈneɪzəl ˈkanθəs / (BrE)
the inner corner of the eye, where the upper and lower eyelids meet near the nose

Examples

The nasal canthus of eye
During the examination, the doctor inspected both the lateral and nasal canthus for signs of infection.
She noticed redness and swelling in her right nasal canthus.
Tears typically collect in the nasal canthus before draining through the tear ducts.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lateral canthus - the outer corner of the eye, where the eyelids meet away from the nose
