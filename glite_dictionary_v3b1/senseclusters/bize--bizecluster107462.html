bize

noun
dry cold north wind in southeastern France
Pronunciation: / ˈbiːz / (AmE), / ˈbiːz / (BrE)
a dry, cold north wind that blows in southeastern France, especially in the Rhone valley

Examples

The bize wind
During winter, the persistent bize can last for days, bringing clear skies and a biting chill.
Farmers worry when a strong bize descends, as it can damage delicate crops in the fields.
The bize swept through the village, making the air feel much colder than the temperature suggested.
