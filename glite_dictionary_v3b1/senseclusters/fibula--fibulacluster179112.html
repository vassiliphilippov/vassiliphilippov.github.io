fibula

noun
outer lower leg bone
2.7 5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfɪb.jʊ.lə / (AmE), / ˈfɪb.jʊ.lə / (BrE)
the outer and thinner of the two long bones in the human lower leg, running parallel to the tibia between the knee and the ankle

Examples

The fibula bone
A break in the fibula can occur during severe sprains or trauma to the leg.
The X-ray showed that her fibula was fractured just above the ankle.
Unlike the tibia, the fibula does not bear much weight.

Related Terms

  • antonym: tibia - the inner and larger bone of the lower human leg
