nominal damages

noun
token sum awarded for legal injury
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑː.mə.nəl ˈdæ.mɪdʒɪz / (AmE), / ˈnɒ.mɪ.nəl ˈdæm.ɪdʒɪz / (BrE)
A small sum of money awarded by a court to recognize that a legal right has been violated, even if no substantial harm was done.

Examples

Award of nominal damages
Although the plaintiff won the case, only nominal damages were awarded because no real loss was proven.
The court may grant nominal damages when a legal right is violated without resulting in significant harm.
The jury awarded nominal damages of one dollar to acknowledge the breach of contract.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: compensatory damages - damages awarded to compensate for actual loss or injury
  • degree higher: punitive damages - damages awarded to punish and deter wrongdoing
