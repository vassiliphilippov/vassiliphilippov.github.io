studied

adjective
deliberate; planned with intention
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈstʌd.iɪd / (AmE), / ˈstʌd.id / (BrE)
deliberate and carefully planned, rather than spontaneous or natural; done with intention or premeditation

Examples

Studied indifference
He greeted us with a studied indifference, pretending not to care.
Her studied response impressed the panel with its thoughtful detail.
The studied elegance of the décor gave the restaurant a sophisticated atmosphere.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deliberate - done consciously and intentionally
  • antonym: spontaneous - performed or occurring as a result of a sudden impulse or inclination; not planned
