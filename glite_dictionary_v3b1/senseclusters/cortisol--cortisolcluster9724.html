cortisol

noun
a steroid hormone regulating metabolism and stress
3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrtəˌsɑl / (AmE), / ˈkɔːtɪsɒl / (BrE)
a steroid hormone produced by the adrenal cortex, important for regulating metabolism, immune response, and stress

Examples

cortisol hormone level
Chronic lack of sleep can disrupt your cortisol balance and affect your health.
Doctors may test cortisol levels to diagnose certain adrenal disorders.
When under stress, the body produces higher levels of cortisol to help respond to the situation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hydrocortisone - another name for cortisol, especially as a medication
