envisage

verb
to imagine or visualize something
6.8 2.66/m
Pronunciation: / ÉªnËˆvÉªzÉªdÊ’ / (AmE), / ÉªnËˆvÉªzÉªdÊ’ / (BrE)
to form a mental picture of something not present or to imagine as a possibility

Examples

To envisage a future
It is difficult to envisage a world without electricity.
She could easily envisage herself living in the countryside.
The architects envisage a new city park where the old factory stands.

Synonyms

  • envision
    4.1 15/m
    envision is imagining a future event clearly and in detail, while envisage often means picturing something possible or likely.
  • visualize
    3.8 5.14/m
    visualize means forming a clear mental image of how something looks, while envisage also includes imagining it as a real possibility.
  • picture
    2.9 17/m
    picture is to form a simple mental image or scene, while envisage often adds the sense of a likely or planned possibility.
  • imagine
    1.3 133/m
    imagine is to think of something unreal or creative, while envisage focuses on seeing it as a possible real situation.
  • conceive of
    5.3 3.59/m
    conceive of often means forming an abstract idea or plan in the mind, while envisage stresses picturing it as possible.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conceive - to form an idea or plan in the mind
  • synonym: imagine - to form a mental image or concept of something
