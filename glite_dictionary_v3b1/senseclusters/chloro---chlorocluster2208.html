chemical prefix for chlorine
6.1
Pronunciation: /ˈklɔːr.oʊ/ (AmE), /ˈklɔː.rəʊ/ (BrE)
a prefix used in scientific and technical words to mean 'green' or to show the presence of chlorine

Examples

Chloro- prefix in chemistry
The chemist used the chloro- prefix to indicate the presence of chlorine in the compound.
Adding the chloro- prefix tells chemists that chlorine is part of the molecule.
In organic chemistry, chloro- is used to indicate chlorine substitution in hydrocarbons.
The prefix chloro- is found in words like chlorophyll and chlorine.
Scientists use chloro- in naming compounds that contain chlorine.
In biology, chloro- often refers to the green color in plant cells.
