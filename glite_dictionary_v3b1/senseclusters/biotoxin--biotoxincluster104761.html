biotoxin

noun
poison from organisms
Pronunciation: / ˈbaɪoʊˌtɑksɪn / (AmE), / ˈbaɪəʊˌtɒksɪn / (BrE)
a poisonous substance produced by a living organism such as a plant, animal, or microorganism

Examples

A biotoxin from mushrooms
Some mushrooms contain a dangerous biotoxin that can cause severe illness if eaten.
Scientists are studying the biotoxin produced by jellyfish to develop new medicines.
The laboratory tests confirmed the presence of a biotoxin in the contaminated water.

Related Terms

  • antonym: antidote - a substance that counteracts a poison
  • antonym: antitoxin - a substance that counteracts a toxin
  • synonym: poison - a substance that can cause harm or death if taken into the body
  • synonym: toxin - a poisonous substance, especially one produced by living cells
