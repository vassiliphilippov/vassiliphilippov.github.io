tardy

adjective
late; delayed
3.9 0.85/m
Pronunciation: / Ààt…ëÀêrdi / (AmE), / Ààt…ëÀêdi / (BrE)
Arriving or happening after the expected or usual time; late or delayed.

Examples

Tardy arrival
His apology was tardy, but it was still appreciated by his friends.
Please try not to be tardy for your interview tomorrow, as punctuality is important.
The students received a detention for being tardy to class repeatedly.

Synonyms

  • late
    1.0 96/m
    late is the common everyday word for arriving after the expected time, while tardy is more formal and often used in schools.
  • unpunctual
    6.2 0.01/m
    unpunctual describes someone who does not arrive at agreed times, while tardy is a common word for being late.
  • belated
    4.4 1.31/m
    belated is used for things like greetings or apologies that come after the expected time, while tardy describes people arriving late.
  • delayed
    1.3 0.12/m
    delayed often shows something happened later because of a cause like traffic or a problem, while tardy stresses lateness in arrival.
  • behindhand
    8.9 0.02/m
    behindhand often means late in doing tasks or paying money, while tardy usually refers to arriving late or being delayed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: late - happening or arriving after the scheduled time
  • antonym: punctual - happening or arriving at the scheduled or correct time
