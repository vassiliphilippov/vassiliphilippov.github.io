poor

adjective
having little money or possessions
1.0 76/m
Pronunciation: / p…îr / (AmE), / p…îÀê / (BrE)
having little money or few possessions; not wealthy

Examples

Poor family income
He grew up in a poor neighborhood, but worked hard to succeed.
Many poor families struggle to afford basic necessities.
The organization helps poor children access education and healthcare.
Many families in the poor neighborhood struggle to afford basic necessities.
She donated money to organizations that support poor communities around the world.
The poor district lacked adequate schools and hospitals compared to wealthier areas.

Synonyms

  • impoverished
    6.2 5.23/m
    impoverished often describes severe, long-term lack of money and resources, while poor is a more general lack of wealth.
  • needy
    1.5 3.19/m
    needy lacks basic needs and requires help from others, while poor simply means having little money or possessions.
  • deprived
    3.9 1.23/m
    deprived lacks basic services or a healthy home and faces worse living conditions, while poor focuses on little money or possessions.
  • broke
    1.8 7.20/m
    broke has no money and sounds informal, often temporary, while poor is a general situation of low wealth.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: destitute - without the basic necessities of life; extremely poor
  • synonym: impoverished - reduced to poverty; very poor
  • degree lower: needy - lacking the necessities of life; requiring help
  • antonym: rich - having a lot of money or possessions; wealthy
