controllability

noun
degree of control
7.0
Pronunciation: / kənˌtroʊləˈbɪləti / (AmE), / kənˌtrəʊləˈbɪləti / (BrE)
the degree to which something (such as a machine, system, or process) can be directed, managed, or kept under control

Examples

Car controllability
Engineers tested the controllability of the new car model under different weather conditions.
The controllability of the software allows users to customize many features easily.
Pilots value the controllability of an aircraft, especially during emergency situations.
The controllability of the new robot was tested in various challenging environments.
Researchers analyzed the controllability of the network to improve its performance.
Understanding controllability is essential for designing safe and reliable automated systems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: manageability - the quality of being easy to control or deal with
  • synonym: observability - the ability to infer the internal state of a system from its outputs
  • synonym: stability - the tendency of a system to return to equilibrium after a disturbance
  • antonym: uncontrollability - the state of being impossible to control
