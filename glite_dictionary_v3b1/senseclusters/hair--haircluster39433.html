hair

noun
threadlike strand growing from skin
Pronunciation: / hɛr / (AmE), / heə / (BrE)
the fine threadlike strands that grow from the skin of humans and animals, especially on the head

Examples

Long hair
A single hair can sometimes reveal important information in forensic science.
She found a hair on her shirt and brushed it away.
The soup had a hair in it, so she sent it back.
After the shower, her hair was still damp and tangled.
The stylist suggested a new hair color to brighten her appearance.
You should cover your hair while cooking so it doesn’t fall into the food.
Some plants are covered in tiny hairs that help protect them from insects.
The hair on a cat's tail fluffed up when it was startled.
Under a microscope, you can see the fine hairs on the surface of a leaf.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bald - lacking hair on the scalp
  • synonym: fur - the soft, thick hair covering the bodies of some animals
