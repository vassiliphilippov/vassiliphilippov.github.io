reconstitute

verb
form again; give new structure to
7.0 1.93/m
Pronunciation: / riÀàk…ën.st…ôÀåtut / (AmE), / riÀàk…ín.st…™.tjuÀêt / (BrE)
to form or create something again or with a new structure after it has been changed or destroyed

Examples

To reconstitute a group
After the breakup, he worked hard to reconstitute his life and career.
Engineers plan to reconstitute the damaged bridge using stronger materials.
The government decided to reconstitute the advisory board after several members resigned.

Synonyms

    3.9 2.61/m
    reconstruct means build or restore a damaged thing to its former shape, while reconstitute means form it again, possibly with a new structure.
  • rebuild
    2.8 24/m
    rebuild means build something again after damage, often keeping the same form, while reconstitute can mean forming it anew or with a changed structure.
  • reestablish
    4.3 3.16/m
    reestablish means bring something back into use or position after it is lost, while reconstitute means form or create it again, sometimes with changes.
  • refound
    6.2 0.04/m
    refound means start an organization again after it ends, while reconstitute means form it again, often changing its structure rather than just founding it.
  • restructure
    4.4 4.92/m
    restructure means change how something is organized to improve it, while reconstitute means form or create it again after change or damage.
