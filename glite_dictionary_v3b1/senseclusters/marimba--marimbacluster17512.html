marimba

noun
percussion instrument with wooden bars and resonators
7.5
Pronunciation: / məˈrɪmbə / (AmE), / məˈrɪmbə / (BrE)
a large percussion instrument consisting of wooden bars arranged like a piano keyboard and played with mallets, each bar having a resonator to amplify its sound

Examples

A marimba instrument
African folk music often features the marimba for its warm, resonant tones.
She learned to play the marimba in her high school percussion ensemble.
The musician skillfully played the marimba during the concert, captivating the audience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: xylophone - a similar percussion instrument with wooden bars but typically without resonators
