harmful stereotype

noun
negative, oversimplified group belief
2.1 3.8
Pronunciation: /ˈhɑːrm.fəl ˈster.ɪə.taɪp/ /; / /ˈhɑːrm.fəl ˈstɪr.ɪə.taɪp/ (AmE), /ˈhɑːm.fəl ˈster.ɪə.taɪp/ /; / /ˈhɑːm.fəl ˈstɪr.ɪə.taɪp/ (BrE)
A widely held but overly simplistic and negative belief or idea about a particular group of people, often leading to prejudice or discrimination.

Examples

Harmful stereotype about groups
Media representation can either combat or reinforce harmful stereotypes.
Teachers should challenge harmful stereotypes in the classroom to create an inclusive environment.
The movie has been criticized for promoting harmful stereotypes about immigrants.
