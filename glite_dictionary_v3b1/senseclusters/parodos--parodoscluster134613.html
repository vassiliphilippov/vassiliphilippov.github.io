parodos

noun
choral entrance song
Pronunciation: / ˈpærəˌdɒs / (AmE), / ˈpærədɒs / (BrE)
the first choral song in ancient Greek drama, sung by the chorus as it enters the stage

Examples

Chorus parodos song
The parodos introduced the chorus and set the mood for the entire play.
In ancient Greek theater, the parodos was often accompanied by music and dance.
Scholars study the parodos to understand the themes presented at the start of Greek tragedies.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: exodos - the final scene or departure song in Greek tragedy
  • degree higher: stasimon - a choral ode in Greek tragedy sung after the parodos
