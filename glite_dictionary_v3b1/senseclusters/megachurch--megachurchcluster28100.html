megachurch

noun
large modern church
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛɡətʃɝːtʃ / (AmE), / ˈmɛɡətʃɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a very large church, usually with thousands of members, offering multiple services and programs

Examples

A megachurch building
The new megachurch in town attracts thousands of people every Sunday.
Many families enjoy the variety of programs offered by their local megachurch.
Some critics argue that a megachurch can feel less personal than a smaller congregation.
The new megachurch was built to accommodate over five thousand worshippers at each service.
Many families travel from neighboring towns to attend the Sunday programs at the megachurch.
The megachurch offers community events, youth groups, and support services throughout the week.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cathedral - a large and important Christian church, often the seat of a bishop
  • antonym: chapel - a small place of Christian worship
