setoff

noun
abrupt narrowing in a wall or building
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛtˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈsɛtɒf / (BrE)
an abrupt step or narrowing in the thickness of a wall or building, usually marking a change in thickness

Examples

A setoff in wall
A visible setoff in the building's façade reveals where the upper floors are thinner than the ground floor.
During restoration, the original setoff was preserved to maintain the historical integrity of the structure.
The architect included a setoff in the wall to accommodate the new doorway.
