schizotypal personality

noun
eccentric personality disorder resembling mild schizophrenia
5.9 8.7
Pronunciation: /skɪt.soʊ.ˈtaɪ.pəl pɜːr.sə.ˈnæl.ə.tiː/ /; / /skɪz.oʊ.ˈtaɪ.pəl pɜːr.sə.ˈnæl.ə.tiː/ (AmE), /skɪt.səʊ.ˈtaɪ.pəl pɜː.sə.ˈnæl.ə.tɪ/ /; / /skɪz.əʊ.ˈtaɪ.pəl pɜː.sə.ˈnæl.ə.tɪ/ (BrE)
A personality disorder characterized by eccentric behavior and difficulty forming close relationships, with symptoms similar to but less severe than schizophrenia.

Examples

Schizotypal personality disorder
People with schizotypal personality may experience odd beliefs and social anxiety.
The psychiatrist diagnosed her with schizotypal personality after a comprehensive assessment.
Therapists often distinguish schizotypal personality from more severe psychiatric disorders like schizophrenia.
