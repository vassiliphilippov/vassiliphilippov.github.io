patronize

verb
to treat condescendingly
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈpeɪtrəˌnaɪz /; / ˈpætɹəˌnaɪz / (AmE), / ˈpætrənaɪz / (BrE)
to treat someone in a way that shows you think you are more intelligent or important than they are

Examples

To patronize a person
Please do not patronize me; I can handle the task myself.
She hates it when people patronize her because of her age.
Stop patronizing your colleagues and try listening to their ideas.
He tends to patronise his coworkers by offering unnecessary advice.
Please don't patronise me—I'm perfectly capable of making my own decisions.
She felt insulted when her teacher started to patronise her during the discussion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: condescend - to behave as if one is superior to others
  • antonym: respect - to treat with consideration and esteem
