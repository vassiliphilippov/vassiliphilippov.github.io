homeobox

noun
DNA sequence in developmental genes
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhoʊmi.oʊˌbɑks / (AmE), / ˈhəʊmi.ə(ʊ)ˌbɒks / (BrE)
a DNA sequence found within genes involved in regulating development, especially relating to the segmentation of an embryo's body plan

Examples

A homeobox gene
Many animals share a similar homeobox sequence that governs body segmentation.
Mutations in a homeobox can lead to significant developmental abnormalities.
Scientists study homeobox genes to understand evolutionary changes in animal body structures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: homeotic gene - a gene that controls the developmental fate of groups of cells
