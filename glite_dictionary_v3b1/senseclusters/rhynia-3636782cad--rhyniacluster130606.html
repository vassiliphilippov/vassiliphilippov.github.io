Rhynia

noun
genus of extinct Devonian plants
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈraɪ.nɪ.ə/ (AmE), /ˈraɪ.nɪ.ə/ (BrE)
A genus of small, leafless, extinct plants with dichotomously branching stems and terminal sporangia, found as fossils from the early Devonian period.

Examples

Rhynia fossil plant
Rhynia grew in a swampy environment about 400 million years ago.
Scientists studied the structure of Rhynia to understand primitive vascular plants.
The discovery of Rhynia fossils shed light on early land plant evolution.
