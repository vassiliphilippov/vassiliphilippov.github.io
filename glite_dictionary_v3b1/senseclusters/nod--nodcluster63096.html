nod

verb
be about to fall asleep
3.0 1.51/m
Pronunciation: / nÉ‘d / (AmE), / nÉ’d / (BrE)
to be about to fall asleep, especially when your head drops forward from tiredness

Examples

To nod off in class
During the lecture, he began to nod in his chair.
Grandpa would often nod by the window after lunch.
She tried to stay awake, but kept nodding off at her desk.
After a long day, he began to nod over his book.
She tried to listen to the lecture but kept nodding in her seat.
The student struggled to stay awake and almost nodded off in class.

Synonyms

  • nod off
    4.1 0.54/m
    nod off means falling asleep briefly and usually by accident, while nod means being about to fall asleep with head dropping.
  • nod out
    7.9 0.04/m
    nod out means falling asleep, often from exhaustion and not planned, while nod means being about to sleep with a tired head drop.
  • drift off
    3.8 1.85/m
    drift off means slowly falling asleep without meaning to, while nod means you are about to fall asleep and your head may drop.
  • drowse off
    5.5 0.02/m
    drowse off means dozing lightly and slipping into sleep slowly, while nod means being on the point of sleep with a forward head drop.
