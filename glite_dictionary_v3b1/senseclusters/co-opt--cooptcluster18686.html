co-opt

verb
adopt for one's own use
6.1 7.2
Pronunciation: / koʊˈɑpt / (AmE), / kəʊˈɒpt / (BrE)
to take or adopt (an idea, method, etc.) for one's own use, often without permission or acknowledgment

Examples

To co-opt an idea
Many political movements have tried to co-opt the language of their opponents to gain broader appeal.
She managed to co-opt the original idea and present it as her own.
The company sought to co-opt innovative startups to expand its portfolio.

Related Terms

  • synonym: usurp - take (a position of power or importance) illegally or by force
