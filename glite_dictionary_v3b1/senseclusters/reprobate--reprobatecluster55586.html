reprobate

verb
condemn to eternal damnation
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛprəˌbeɪt / (AmE), / ˈrɛprəʊˌbeɪt / (BrE)
(formal) to condemn someone to eternal damnation; to reject as unworthy of salvation.

Examples

To reprobate a sinner
In the sermon, the priest warned his congregation against actions that might cause the Lord to reprobate them.
Many preachers warned that sinful behavior could cause God to reprobate an entire community.
Some theological doctrines taught that the wicked would be reprobated forever.

Related Terms

  • synonym: condemn - to pronounce as guilty; to sentence, especially in a legal or religious sense
