turbomachinery

noun
energy-transferring machines
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˌtɜːrbəˈməʃɪnəri / (AmE), / ˌtɜːbəˈməʃɪnəri / (BrE)
machines such as turbines, compressors, and fans that transfer energy between a rotor and a fluid, or the engineering field that studies these machines

Examples

Turbomachinery design
Engineers working in turbomachinery design efficient turbines for power generation and aviation.
The university offers a specialized course in turbomachinery for mechanical engineering students.
Advances in turbomachinery have led to more reliable and powerful jet engines.
Modern jet engines rely on advanced turbomachinery to achieve high efficiency and power.
The course covers the design and operation of various types of turbomachinery used in industry.
Engineers analyze turbomachinery to improve the performance of pumps and turbines in power plants.

Related Terms

  • synonym: turbine - a machine that produces power by spinning from the movement of fluid
