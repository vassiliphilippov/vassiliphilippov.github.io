exuberant

adjective
growing in great abundance; luxuriant
5.8 0.62/m
Pronunciation: / …™gÀàzuÀêb…ôr…ônt / (AmE), / …™gÀàzjuÀêb…ôr…ônt / (BrE)
produced or growing in great abundance; luxuriant

Examples

Exuberant plant growth
Spring brought an exuberant burst of flowers in the park, filling the air with color and scent.
The exuberant foliage covered the entire garden wall, making it look wild and lush.
They admired the exuberant growth of vines that thrived in the warm, wet climate.

Synonyms

  • luxuriant
    6.0 0.39/m
    luxuriant grows thick and full in plants and hair, while exuberant describes strong, abundant growth often with lively energy.
  • lush
    3.7 6.12/m
    lush is rich, green, and full of life in gardens or food, while exuberant stresses great abundance and growth.
  • riotous
    7.5 0.15/m
    riotous shows wild, overflowing growth full of color and movement, while exuberant focuses on abundant, luxuriant production.
  • profuse
    6.5 0.38/m
    profuse means plentiful or excessive, often for things offered like apologies or rain, while exuberant describes rich plant growth.
