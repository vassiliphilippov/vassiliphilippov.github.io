Portuguese heath

noun
a dense flowering shrub native to Iberia
Pronunciation: / ˌpɔrtʃəˌgiːz ˈhiːθ / (AmE), / ˌpɔːtʃʊˌgiːz ˈhiːθ / (BrE)
an evergreen shrub native to the western Iberian peninsula, with dense foliage and profuse white or pink flowers, also naturalized in parts of southwestern England.

Examples

Portuguese heath plant
Gardeners in England often plant Portuguese heath for its attractive evergreen foliage.
In its native habitat, Portuguese heath thrives on rocky hillsides and poor soils.
The Portuguese heath produces clusters of bell-shaped pink flowers in the spring.
