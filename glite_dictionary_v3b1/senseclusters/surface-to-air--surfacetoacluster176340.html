surface-to-air

adjective
launched from ground at airborne target
7.2 5.3
Pronunciation: / ˌsɜrfɪs tə ˈɛr / (AmE), / ˌsɜːfɪs tə ˈeə / (BrE)
(of a weapon or missile) designed to be launched from the ground at a target in the air.

Examples

Surface-to-air missile
During the exercise, the team practiced using surface-to-air defenses to protect the base from air attacks.
The army tested a new surface-to-air missile system capable of intercepting enemy aircraft.
They detected an incoming aircraft and fired a surface-to-air weapon to neutralize the threat.

Related Terms

  • antonym: air-to-air - (of a weapon or missile) designed to be launched from one airborne vehicle towards another in the air
  • antonym: surface-to-surface - (of a weapon or missile) designed to be launched from the ground or a platform at a ground or surface target
