monobasic acid

noun
acid with one replaceable hydrogen atom
Pronunciation: / ˌmoʊnəˈbeɪsɪk ˈæsɪd / (AmE), / ˌməʊnəˈbeɪsɪk ˈæsɪd / (BrE)
an acid containing only one replaceable hydrogen atom per molecule, capable of donating one proton in a reaction

Examples

A monobasic acid molecule
Hydrochloric acid is a classic example of a monobasic acid in chemistry.
Scientists classified the unknown substance as a monobasic acid after analyzing its chemical behavior.
When dissolved in water, a monobasic acid releases only one hydrogen ion per molecule.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: dibasic acid - an acid with two replaceable hydrogen atoms per molecule
