urokinase

noun
kidney enzyme involved in dissolving blood clots
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌjʊroʊˈkaɪneɪs / (AmE), / ˌjʊərəʊˈkaɪneɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme produced in the kidneys that converts plasminogen to plasmin, thereby initiating the breakdown of blood clots (fibrinolysis)

Examples

Urokinase enzyme activity
A deficiency in urokinase can increase the risk of thrombosis.
Scientists are studying how urokinase can be used in different therapies for stroke victims.
The patient was treated with urokinase to help dissolve the blood clot.
