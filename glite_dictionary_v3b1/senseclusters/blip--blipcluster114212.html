blip

verb
to appear briefly
4.9
Pronunciation: /blɪp/ (AmE), /blɪp/ (BrE)
to appear or disappear quickly and briefly, often as a spot of light or a short sound on a screen or device

Examples

A light blips on
A warning symbol blipped on the dashboard before disappearing a second later.
The satellite briefly blipped across the technician's monitor during the test.
Her username blipped onto the chat list and then was gone.
The submarine's location blipped on the sonar screen for just a second.
Every time a car passed by, the security camera light blipped on and off.
A message blipped onto his phone and then quickly disappeared.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disappear - to stop being visible or present
  • synonym: flash - to appear suddenly and briefly
  • antonym: linger - to stay in a place longer than expected
