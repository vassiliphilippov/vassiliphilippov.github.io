triple down

verb
increase commitment or effort
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˈtrɪp.əl daʊn / (AmE), / ˈtrɪp.əl daʊn / (BrE)
to increase one's commitment or level of effort, especially after a setback or during a risky situation

Examples

To triple down effort

Instead of backing off, he decided to triple down and work even harder than before.
Realizing the importance of education, she chose to triple down on her studies this semester.
