unphilosophical

adjective
not calm or patient
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnˌfɪləˈsɑfɪkəl / (AmE), / ˌʌnˌfɪləˈsɒfɪkəl / (BrE)
not calm, patient, or accepting when facing problems or disappointment; lacking a philosophical attitude.

Examples

Unphilosophical reaction
He became unphilosophical when his travel plans were suddenly canceled.
It is easy to be unphilosophical when things do not go your way.
Her unphilosophical reaction surprised everyone during the stressful meeting.

Related Terms

  • synonym: impatient - not willing to wait calmly
  • antonym: philosophical - calm and accepting in difficult situations
