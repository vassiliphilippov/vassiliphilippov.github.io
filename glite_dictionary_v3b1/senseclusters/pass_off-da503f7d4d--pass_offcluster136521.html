pass off

verb
to emit gases or smells
Pronunciation: / pæs ɑf / (AmE), / pɑːs ɒf / (BrE)
to emit or release (gases or odors), often in a discreet or unnoticed manner

Examples

To pass off gas
After eating beans, he tried to quietly pass off the resulting gas without anyone noticing.
The chemical factory was fined when it continued to pass off harmful fumes into the atmosphere.
The sewage treatment plant is designed not to pass off any unpleasant odors into the neighborhood.
