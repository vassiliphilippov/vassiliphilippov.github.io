high-tension

adjective
designed to handle high voltage
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪˈtɛn.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌhaɪˈtɛn.ʃən / (BrE)
designed for or able to operate at a relatively high voltage, especially in reference to electrical wires or equipment

Examples

High-tension power line
A fault in a high-tension line can cause widespread power outages.
Electricians must take special precautions when working near high-tension wires.
The high-tension cables carry electricity from the power plant across long distances.
