spiritualism

noun
theological doctrine of God's independent existence
1.0 6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈspɪrɪtʃuəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈspɪrɪtʃuəlɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
(Theology) The doctrine that asserts the separate existence of God, independent of matter or the physical universe.

Examples

Spiritualism vs materialism
Debates between spiritualism and materialism have shaped modern theology.
He wrote extensively about spiritualism and its implications for the existence of God.
Some branches of Christianity adopt spiritualism as a core concept in their teachings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: materialism - the doctrine that physical matter is the only or fundamental reality
  • synonym: theism - the belief in the existence of a god or gods, especially a creator who intervenes in the universe
