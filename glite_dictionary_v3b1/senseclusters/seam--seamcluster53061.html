seam

noun
pixel path used in content-aware resizing
8.2
Pronunciation: /siːm/ (AmE), /siːm/ (BrE)
(in digital image processing) a connected path of pixels, usually of low energy, identified by seam-carving algorithms and removed or duplicated to change an image's dimensions while preserving important content.

Examples

Image seam removal
The editor used a content-aware tool to remove a distracting seam across the horizon.
During resizing, the algorithm computes the lowest-energy seam and removes it to preserve details.
Photographers sometimes duplicate a vertical seam to widen an image without stretching important features.

Related Terms

  • synonym: content-aware resizing - a set of techniques for changing image dimensions by considering the importance of different pixels or regions
  • synonym: seam carving - an image retargeting algorithm that removes or inserts seams to resize images while preserving important content
