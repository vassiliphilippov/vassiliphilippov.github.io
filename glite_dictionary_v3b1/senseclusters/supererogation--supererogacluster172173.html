supererogation

noun
action beyond duty or obligation
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərˌɛrəˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsuːpərəˌrɒˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
The act of doing more than duty requires, especially as a morally good but not obligatory action.

Examples

An act of supererogation
Her charity work was an act of supererogation rather than something she was required to do.
The principle of supererogation is often discussed in moral philosophy.
Volunteering extra hours for the project was an example of supererogation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: duty - something that one is required or expected to do
  • antonym: obligation - a requirement to do something; something one must do
