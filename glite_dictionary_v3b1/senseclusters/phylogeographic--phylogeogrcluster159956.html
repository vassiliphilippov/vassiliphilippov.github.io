phylogeographic

adjective
genetic distribution by region
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌfaɪloʊˌdʒiːəˈɡræfɪk / (AmE), / ˌfʌɪləʊˌdʒiːəˈɡrafɪk / (BrE)
relating to the geographic distribution of genetic lineages or populations within a species

Examples

Phylogeographic study
A phylogeographic study can reveal how animal populations migrated after the last ice age.
Researchers used phylogeographic methods to trace the origins of the plant species across islands.
The phylogeographic patterns suggest multiple colonization events in the region.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biogeographic - relating to the distribution of species and ecosystems in geographic space
  • synonym: genetic - relating to genes or heredity
