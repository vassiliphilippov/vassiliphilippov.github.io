banian

noun
East Indian fig tree with aerial roots
Pronunciation: / ˈbæniən / (AmE), / ˈbanɪən / (BrE)
An East Indian fig tree (Ficus benghalensis) with aerial roots that descend to the ground and develop into additional trunks, forming a large canopy.

Examples

A giant banian tree
A banian can cover a surprisingly large area due to its multiple trunks.
Many people gathered in the shade of the sprawling banian on hot afternoons.
The ancient banian in the village stretched its branches over the entire square.
