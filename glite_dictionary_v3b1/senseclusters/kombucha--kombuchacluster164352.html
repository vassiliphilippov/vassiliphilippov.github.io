kombucha

noun
fermentation culture
Pronunciation: / kəmˈbuːtʃə / (AmE), / kɒmˈbuːtʃə / (BrE)
a gelatinous mass of bacteria and yeast used to ferment sweetened tea to make a probiotic drink

Examples

Kombucha culture
The kombucha floated on top of the tea as it began to ferment.
She carefully transferred the kombucha to a new jar to start another batch.
After a week, the kombucha had grown thicker and the tea tasted slightly sour.

Related Terms

  • synonym: SCOBY - a symbiotic culture of bacteria and yeast used in fermentation
  • synonym: fermentation - the process of converting sugars into alcohol or acids using microorganisms
