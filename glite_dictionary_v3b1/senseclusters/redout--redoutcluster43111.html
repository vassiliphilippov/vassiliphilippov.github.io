redout

verb
to suffer red vision
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛdˌaʊt / (AmE), / ˈrɛdˌaʊt / (BrE)
to experience a temporary reddening or dimming of vision caused by excessive blood flow to the head, especially during high acceleration

Examples

To redout in flight
Pilots may redout if they experience extreme upward acceleration during flight maneuvers.
After the rapid ascent, she started to redout and saw everything through a reddish haze.
Astronauts are trained to recognize the signs when they begin to redout in a simulator.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blackout - to lose consciousness or vision, often due to lack of blood flow to the brain
