tarmac

noun
paved surface with tar and crushed stone
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtɑːrˌmæk /; / ˈtɑːrmæk / (AmE), / ˈtɑːmæk / (BrE)
a hard, smooth surface made from a mixture of tar and crushed stone, used for roads, playgrounds, and airport areas (also the material itself)

Examples

Airport tarmac
Children rode their bikes across the smooth tarmac in the school playground.
The plane waited on the tarmac for an hour before taking off.
Workers repaired the cracks in the tarmac after the storm.
Fresh tarmac was laid on the high street overnight, causing a pleasant, new smell in the morning.
Planes lined up on the tarmac waiting for takeoff.
The long driveway was resurfaced with tarmac to make it smoother.
The passengers waited on the tarmac as the crew prepared the plane for departure.
After landing, the aircraft taxied slowly across the tarmac to the terminal.
Workers repaired a section of the tarmac damaged by heavy rain last week.

Related Terms

  • synonym: apron - the area at an airport where aircraft are parked and serviced
  • synonym: asphalt - a similar dark bituminous substance used for paving
  • synonym: runway - a long, flat surface where airplanes take off and land
