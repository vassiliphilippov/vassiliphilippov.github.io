block

noun
temporary mental inability to recall or think
4.4 5.7
Pronunciation: /blɑːk/ /; / /blɔːk/ (AmE), /blɒk/ (BrE)
a temporary inability to remember or think of something you normally can do, often caused by emotional tension

Examples

A mental block
Despite years of practice, she still gets a mental block during stressful exams.
He often experiences a block when trying to recall names under pressure.
The actor suffered a sudden block during his performance and forgot his lines.
