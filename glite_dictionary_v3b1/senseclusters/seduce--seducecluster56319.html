seduce

verb
entice away from duty or principles
5.9 2.36/m
Pronunciation: / s…™Ààdus / (AmE), / s…™ÀàdjuÀês / (BrE)
to persuade someone to abandon duty, principles, or proper behavior, often by offering something attractive or tempting

Examples

To seduce someone from duty
He was seduced by the promise of instant success, neglecting his responsibilities.
She feared that fame might seduce her into compromising her values.
The offer of a higher salary was enough to seduce him away from his long-standing job.
