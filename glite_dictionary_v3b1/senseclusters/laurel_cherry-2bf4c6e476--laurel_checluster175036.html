laurel cherry

noun
small evergreen tree of the southern US
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈlɔrəl ˈtʃɛri / (AmE), / ˈlɒrəl ˈtʃɛri / (BrE)
a small evergreen tree of the southern United States, valued for its glossy leaves and often used in landscaping

Examples

Southern laurel cherry
Gardeners like to plant laurel cherry for its glossy green foliage and dense form.
The laurel cherry is a popular choice for ornamental hedges in the southern states.
The berries of the laurel cherry closely resemble those of true cherries, but are not edible.
