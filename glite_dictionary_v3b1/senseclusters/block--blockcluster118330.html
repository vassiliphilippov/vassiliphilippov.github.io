block

noun
grooved wheel for rope in pulleys
Pronunciation: / blɑk / (AmE), / blɒk / (BrE)
A device consisting of a wheel with a groove that a rope runs through, used to change the direction or point of application of force; a component of a pulley system.

Examples

A pulley block
A worn groove in the block can cause the rope to fray and break.
The mechanic inspected the block to ensure the pulley system operated smoothly.
To lift heavy loads, the workers used a block and tackle mechanism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pulley - a wheel with a grooved rim in which a rope can run, used to lift or move loads
