retrospective

noun
team review meeting after a sprint
6.1
Pronunciation: / \u02ccr\u025btro\u028a\u02c8sp\u025bkt\u026av / (AmE), / \u02ccr\u025btr\u0259\u02c8sp\u025bkt\u026av / (BrE)
(in project management, especially Agile software development) a meeting held at the end of an iteration or sprint in which a team reflects on their work and identifies improvements for future cycles.

Examples

Team retrospective
After the sprint, the team scheduled a retrospective to discuss what went well and what to change.
Many teams run short weekly retrospectives to fix problems before they affect future work.
The scrum master prepared notes to lead a focused retrospective about deployment failures and process improvements.

Related Terms

  • synonym: post-mortem - an analysis or meeting held after a project or incident to determine causes and lessons
  • synonym: review - a formal assessment or discussion of work that has been done
