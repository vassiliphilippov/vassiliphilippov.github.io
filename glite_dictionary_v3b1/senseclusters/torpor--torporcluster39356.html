torpor

noun
state of inactivity; mental or physical
8.9 0.38/m
Pronunciation: / Ààt…îrp…ö /; / Ààt…îrp…ôr / (AmE), / Ààt…îÀêp…ô / (BrE)
a state of physical or mental inactivity, with little energy or enthusiasm

Examples

Deep torpor
After days of fever, he sank into a torpor from which it was difficult to rouse him.
Economic torpor gripped the region following the financial crisis.
The torpor of the hot summer afternoon made it hard to work.
A sense of torpor settled over the office during the hot afternoon.
After weeks of torpor, the city finally buzzed with activity when spring arrived.
The patient's torpor worried the doctors, as he showed no interest in his surroundings.

Synonyms

  • listlessness
    6.8 0.19/m
    listlessness is a state of low interest and no energy, while torpor is a deeper physical or mental inactivity with little enthusiasm.
  • lethargy
    6.3 0.98/m
    lethargy is strong tiredness and slow movement, while torpor is broader mental or physical inactivity with low energy or interest.
  • enervation
    9.1 0.01/m
    enervation is extreme physical weakness and fatigue, while torpor is inactivity that also includes low energy and little enthusiasm.
  • inactivity
    3.5 1.57/m
    inactivity is simply not moving or acting, while torpor involves lack of energy plus reduced mental interest or response.
  • idleness
    4.5 1.01/m
    idleness is choosing to be inactive or lazy, while torpor describes a passive physical or mental lack of energy or enthusiasm.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lethargy - a lack of energy and enthusiasm
  • antonym: vigor - active bodily or mental strength; energy
