Toxoplasma

noun
parasitic infection
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˌtɑksəˈplæzmə / (AmE), / ˌtɒksəˈplazmə / (BrE)
an infection caused by the parasite Toxoplasma gondii, often referred to informally as toxoplasmosis

Examples

Toxoplasma infection
After the prenatal screening, she discovered she had Toxoplasma and started a course of antibiotics.
Doctors recommend avoiding undercooked meat to reduce the risk of Toxoplasma infection.
The patient was diagnosed with Toxoplasma after showing flu-like symptoms and swollen lymph nodes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: toxoplasmosis - infection caused by Toxoplasma gondii
