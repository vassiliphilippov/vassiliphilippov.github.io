Synonyms

  • obey
    1.0 19/m
    obey means doing what an authority tells you directly, while follow orders stresses carrying out specific commands without question.
  • take orders
    3.2 4/m
    take orders means receiving commands and being expected to obey a superior, while follow orders highlights actually doing those commands.
  • comply
    3.8 15/m
    comply means acting in line with a request, rule, or command, while follow orders implies obeying a person's commands without question.
  • follow
    1.0 50/m
    follow means accepting or acting on rules, advice, or ideas more generally, while follow orders specifically means obeying a person's commands.
