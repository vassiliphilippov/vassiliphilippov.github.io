follow orders

verb
obey instructions without question
2.9 1.79/m
Pronunciation: / Ààf…ëÀêlo ä Àà…îrd…ôrz / (AmE), / Ààf…íl…ô ä Àà…îÀêd…ôz / (BrE)
to do what someone in authority tells you to do, especially without questioning it

Examples

To follow orders exactly
It's important in some jobs to follow orders exactly as given.
She didn't agree with the company's decision, but she continued to follow orders.
Soldiers are trained to follow orders without hesitation, even in dangerous situations.

Synonyms

  • obey
    1.0 20/m
    obey means doing what an authority tells you directly, while follow orders stresses carrying out specific commands without question.
  • take orders
    3.2 1.85/m
    take orders means receiving commands and being expected to obey a superior, while follow orders highlights actually doing those commands.
  • comply
    3.8 35/m
    comply means acting in line with a request, rule, or command, while follow orders implies obeying a person's commands without question.
  • follow
    1.0 56/m
    follow means accepting or acting on rules, advice, or ideas more generally, while follow orders specifically means obeying a person's commands.

Related Terms
