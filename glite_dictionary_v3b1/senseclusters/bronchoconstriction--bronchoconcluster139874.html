bronchoconstriction

noun
narrowing of airways
Pronunciation: / ˌbrɑːŋkoʊkənˈstrɪkʃən / (AmE), / ˌbrɒŋkəʊkənˈstrɪkʃən / (BrE)
the narrowing of the airways in the lungs caused by tightening of the surrounding muscles

Examples

Bronchoconstriction during asthma
During an asthma attack, bronchoconstriction can make it very hard to breathe.
Certain medications are used to prevent bronchoconstriction in people with respiratory conditions.
Allergens in the air may trigger bronchoconstriction in sensitive individuals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: asthma - a condition causing difficulty in breathing due to airway narrowing
  • antonym: bronchodilation - the widening of the airways in the lungs
