reel

verb
walk or move unsteadily
4.6 1.67/m
Pronunciation: / ril / (AmE), / riÀêl / (BrE)
to walk or move in an unsteady or uncontrolled way, often as if about to fall

Examples

To reel across the room
After spinning in circles, the child began to reel across the playground.
Shocked by the news, she seemed to reel as she left the room.
The injured player tried to stand but could only reel a few steps before sitting down again.

Synonyms

  • stagger
    4.0 6.84/m
    stagger means to walk with heavy, uneven steps from weakness or drink, while reel means to sway as if about to fall.
  • totter
    4.8 1.35/m
    totter is used for small, shaky steps as with a child or old person, while reel is a larger dizzy sway.
  • stumble
    1.6 12/m
    stumble means to trip or nearly fall on a step, while reel means to sway and stagger from loss of balance.
  • falter
    5.6 0.39/m
    falter means to move hesitantly or lose strength in steps, while reel means to sway wildly as if about to fall.
  • lurch
    5.6 4.74/m
    lurch means to make a sudden, jerky movement forward or sideways, while reel means to sway slowly and lose balance.
  • careen
    7.7 1.89/m
    careen means to move fast and out of control, often side to side, while reel means to sway weakly and almost fall.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lurch - to make an abrupt, uncontrolled movement
  • synonym: stagger - to walk or move unsteadily, as if about to fall
