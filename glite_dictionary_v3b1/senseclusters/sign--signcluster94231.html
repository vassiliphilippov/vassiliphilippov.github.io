(Linguistics) A fundamental unit that links a signifier (form) to that which is signified (meaning or concept).

Examples

A linguistic sign
In linguistic theory, the sign connects both sound and concept in the process of communication.
Saussure argued that a sign is composed of both a material form and an abstract meaning.
The relationship between the signifier and the signified in a sign is central to structuralism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: symbol - something that stands for or represents something else, especially a material object representing something abstract
