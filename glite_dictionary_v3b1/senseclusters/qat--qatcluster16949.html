qat

noun
leaves chewed as a stimulant
Pronunciation: / kɑt / (AmE), / kæt / (BrE)
the leaves of the shrub Catha edulis, chewed or used to make tea as a stimulant, especially in East Africa and the Arabian Peninsula

Examples

Fresh qat leaves
He bought a bundle of fresh qat at the market to chew with friends in the afternoon.
Some people brew qat leaves into a tea for their stimulating properties.
The use of qat is especially prevalent in Yemen, where its effects are socially significant.

Related Terms

  • synonym: khat - alternative spelling of qat, same plant and use
