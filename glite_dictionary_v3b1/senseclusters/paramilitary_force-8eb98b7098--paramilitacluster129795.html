paramilitary force

noun
civilian group organized like military unit
2.2 6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛrəˈmɪlɪˌtɛri fɔrs / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈmɪlɪtəri fɔːs / (BrE)
A group of civilians organized in a military manner, often to operate in place of or to assist regular army troops.

Examples

A paramilitary force group
A notorious paramilitary force was implicated in the attacks on villages along the border.
The government deployed a paramilitary force to assist the police during the unrest.
The new administration sought to disband all paramilitary forces operating outside official control.
