common purslane

noun
edible mat-forming herb with yellow flowers
6.8 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëm.…ôn Ààp…úr.sle…™n / (AmE), / Ààk…ím.…ôn Ààp…úÀê.sle…™n / (BrE)
A low-growing, mat-forming herb with succulent leaves and small yellow flowers, often cultivated or found as a weed; its mildly sour leaves are edible and used in salads and cooked dishes, especially in Indian, Greek, and Middle Eastern cuisines.

Examples

Fresh common purslane
Fresh common purslane is a popular addition to salads in Mediterranean cuisine.
Many gardeners remove common purslane as a weed, not knowing its leaves are edible.
The tangy flavor of common purslane makes it a favorite ingredient in Greek dishes.
