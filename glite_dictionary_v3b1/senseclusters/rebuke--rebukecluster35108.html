rebuke

noun
expression of criticism or disapproval
6.8 1.79/m
Pronunciation: / r…™Ààbjuk / (AmE), / r…™ÀàbjuÀêk / (BrE)
an expression of sharp criticism or disapproval

Examples

A harsh rebuke
His careless comments earned him a sharp rebuke from the judge.
Instead of getting angry, he accepted the rebuke gracefully and promised to improve.
She received a stern rebuke from her manager for being late to the meeting.
