phenylbutazone

noun

9.9
Pronunciation: /fen.əl.bjuː.ˈteɪ.zoʊn/ /; / /fen.əl.bjuː.ˈteɪ.zən/ (AmE), /fen.əl.bjuː.ˈteɪ.zəʊn/ /; / /fen.əl.bjuː.ˈteɪ.zən/ (BrE)
A synthetic anti-inflammatory drug used to relieve pain and inflammation, especially in the treatment of arthritis in animals and, formerly, in humans.

Examples

Long-term use of phenylbutazone can result in serious health complications.
