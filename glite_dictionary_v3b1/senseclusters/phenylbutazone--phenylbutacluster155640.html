phenylbutazone

noun
anti-inflammatory drug
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌfɛnəlˈbjutəˌzoʊn / (AmE), / ˌfiːnɪlˈbjuːtəzəʊn / (BrE)
A synthetic anti-inflammatory drug used to relieve pain and inflammation, especially in the treatment of arthritis in animals and, formerly, in humans.

Examples

Phenylbutazone drug
Due to its side effects, phenylbutazone is now rarely used in human medicine.
Long-term use of phenylbutazone can result in serious health complications.
The veterinarian prescribed phenylbutazone to reduce the horse's inflammation after the injury.
