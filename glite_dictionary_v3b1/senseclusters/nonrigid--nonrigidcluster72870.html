nonrigid

adjective
not having a rigid internal framework (airships)
5.3 6.6
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˈrɪdʒɪd / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈrɪdʒɪd / (BrE)
relating to an airship whose shape is maintained only by gas pressure, without an internal supporting structure.

Examples

Nonrigid airship
During World War I, nonrigid airships were often used for naval patrols and observation.
The Zeppelin museum displayed a famous nonrigid airship from the early twentieth century.
Unlike rigid airships, nonrigid blimps rely entirely on internal gas pressure to hold their shape.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blimp - a nonrigid airship (no internal frame)
  • antonym: rigid - having a fixed, inflexible structure (contrast with nonrigid in airships)
