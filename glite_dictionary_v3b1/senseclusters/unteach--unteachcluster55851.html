unteach

verb
cause to unlearn something
6.9 6.7
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈtitʃ / (AmE), / ʌnˈtiːtʃ / (BrE)
to cause someone to unlearn, forget, or discard something they have previously learned, such as a habit, belief, or knowledge

Examples

To unteach a bad habit
It is difficult to unteach bad habits once they are deeply ingrained.
She tried to unteach her dog the tricks it had learned incorrectly.
The coach had to unteach the players their old, ineffective methods before introducing new techniques.
It is often harder to unteach incorrect information than to teach something new.
It took years to unteach the old ways from the staff.
The new manager hopes to unteach employees their bad habits.

Related Terms

  • antonym: teach - to impart knowledge or skill
  • synonym: unlearn - to discard knowledge or a habit from memory
