nonrevolutionary

adjective
not radical change
6.9
Pronunciation: /nɑːn.rev.əˈluː.ʃə.rɪ/ (AmE), /nɒn.rev.əˈluː.ʃən.rɪ/ (BrE)
not involving, supporting, or characterized by revolution or major change; preferring the existing order or gradual change

Examples

Nonrevolutionary policies
The government introduced nonrevolutionary policies to avoid sudden disruption.
Her approach to education was nonrevolutionary, focusing on small improvements.

Many citizens supported nonrevolutionary reforms rather than drastic changes to the system.
