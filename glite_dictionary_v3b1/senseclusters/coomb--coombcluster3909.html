coomb

noun
small deep valley
Pronunciation: / kuːm / (AmE), / kuːm / (BrE)
a small, deep valley, often found in southern England, typically with steep sides

Examples

A coomb valley
The hikers rested in a shaded coomb after climbing the steep hill.
Wildflowers covered the slopes of the quiet coomb near the old farmhouse.
Mist lingered in the coomb long after the sun had risen over the fields.

Related Terms

  • synonym: glen - a narrow valley, especially in Scotland or Ireland
  • antonym: hill - a naturally raised area of land, not as high as a mountain
  • synonym: ravine - a deep, narrow gorge with steep sides
