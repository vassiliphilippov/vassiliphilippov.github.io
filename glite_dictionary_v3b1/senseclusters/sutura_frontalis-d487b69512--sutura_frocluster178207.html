sutura frontalis

noun
suture in the frontal bone of the skull
9.9
Pronunciation: / suːˈtʊrə frʌnˈteɪlɪs / (AmE), / suːˈtjʊərə frʌnˈteɪlɪs / (BrE)
the suture between the two halves of the frontal bone in the skull, usually disappearing by age six

Examples

The sutura frontalis line
In some adults, the sutura frontalis can still be seen on X-rays.
Surgeons must account for the sutura frontalis when performing cranial procedures on infants.
The presence of a persistent sutura frontalis is rare after early childhood.

Related Terms

  • synonym: metopic suture - another term for the same suture in the frontal bone
