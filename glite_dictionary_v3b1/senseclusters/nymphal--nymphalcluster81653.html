nymphal

adjective
of insect nymph stage
Pronunciation: / ˈnɪmfəl / (AmE), / ˈnɪmfəl / (BrE)
relating to the nymph stage of an insect, which is the immature form between larva and adult

Examples

Nymphal stage
The nymphal period is crucial for the insect's growth and development.
Scientists observed the nymphal forms feeding on plant leaves in the laboratory.
During the nymphal stage, the insect gradually develops wings and adult features.

Related Terms

  • antonym: adult - fully developed and mature stage of an insect
  • antonym: larval - relating to the larva stage of an insect
