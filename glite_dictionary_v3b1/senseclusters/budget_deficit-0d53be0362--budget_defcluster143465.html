budget deficit

noun
amount by which spending exceeds income
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌdʒɪt ˈdɛfəsɪt / (AmE), / ˈbʌdʒɪt ˈdɛfɪsɪt / (BrE)
A financial situation where expenditures exceed revenues, typically within a government, organization, or individual budget over a specific period.

Examples

A budget deficit problem
Many economists warn that a large budget deficit can lead to increased national debt over time.
The company reduced its budget deficit by cutting unnecessary expenses and increasing revenue.
The government's budget deficit reached a new high last year, sparking concerns about fiscal responsibility.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deficit - the amount by which something, especially money, is too small
  • antonym: surplus - an amount by which income exceeds expenditures
