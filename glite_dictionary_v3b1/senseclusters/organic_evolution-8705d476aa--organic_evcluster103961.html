organic evolution

noun
biological process of species evolution
1.9
Pronunciation: / ɔrˈɡænɪk ˌɛvəˈluʃən / (AmE), / ɔːˈɡanɪk ˌiːvəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
the process of gradual change and development in living organisms, leading to the formation of new species over time

Examples

Organic evolution process
Paleontologists study fossils to uncover the history of organic evolution.
Some early scientists were skeptical of the concept of organic evolution in explaining species differences.
The theory of organic evolution is central to our understanding of biodiversity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: evolution - gradual development of organisms over time
  • synonym: natural selection - process by which organisms adapt and evolve
