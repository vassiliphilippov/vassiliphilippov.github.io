ride on someone's coattail

verb
gain success by benefiting from another's achievements
9.9
Pronunciation: / raɪd ɑn ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈkoʊt.teɪl / (AmE), / raɪd ɒn ˈsʌmwʌnz ˈkəʊt.teɪl / (BrE)
to achieve success or gain advantages by benefiting from the success or influence of another person, rather than through one's own efforts

Examples

To ride on someone's coattail to success
Many people believed the politician had simply ridden on his predecessor's coattails to win the election.
Rather than working hard himself, he tried to ride on his teammate's coattails to get a promotion.
The band enjoyed fame for years by riding on the coattails of their popular lead singer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: piggyback - to use something that someone else has done in order to get an advantage
  • antonym: stand on one's own two feet - to be independent and not rely on others for success
