tupelo tree

noun
North American swamp tree (gum tree)
Pronunciation: / ˈtuːpəˌloʊ triː / (AmE), / ˈtjuːpɪləʊ triː / (BrE)
A North American tree of swampy areas, valued for its hard wood and often called a gum tree.

Examples

Tupelo trees in swamps
Bees often gather nectar from the blossoms of tupelo trees for a light, fragrant honey.
He pointed out a rare water-loving tupelo tree during our nature hike.
The wetland was filled with towering tupelo trees and cypress knees.

Related Terms

  • synonym: gum tree - a tree that exudes gum, especially the eucalyptus in Australia or tupelo in North America
