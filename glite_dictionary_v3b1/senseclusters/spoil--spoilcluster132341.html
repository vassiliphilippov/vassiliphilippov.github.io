spoil

verb
treat with excessive indulgence
Pronunciation: / spɔɪl / (AmE), / spɔɪl / (BrE)
to treat someone, especially a child, with excessive indulgence, often resulting in bad behavior or unrealistic expectations

Examples

To spoil a child
Constant praise and rewards can spoil a student instead of motivating them.
Her grandmother loves to spoil her with sweets and gifts every time she visits.
They didn't want to spoil their children by giving them everything they asked for.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mollycoddle - to treat someone in an overprotective or indulgent way
  • synonym: pamper - to treat with excessive care or attention
