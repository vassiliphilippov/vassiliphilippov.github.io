Tintoretto

proper noun
Italian Renaissance painter
Pronunciation: / ˌtɪntəˈrɛtoʊ / (AmE), / ˌtɪntəˈrɛtəʊ / (BrE)
(1518–1594) A renowned Italian painter of the Venetian school, known for his dramatic use of perspective and vibrant color.

Examples

Tintoretto paintings
Tintoretto was famous for his energetic brushwork and innovative use of light in his paintings.
Art historians often compare the works of Tintoretto to those of his contemporary, Veronese.
The museum’s new exhibition features several masterpieces by Tintoretto.
