anaphora

noun
repetition at clause beginnings
9.1
Pronunciation: / əˈnæfərə / (AmE), / əˈnafərə / (BrE)
the repetition of a word or phrase at the beginning of successive clauses or sentences, used for emphasis or effect

Examples

Anaphora in poetry
In poetry, anaphora can enhance the emotional impact of repeated ideas.
The famous speech used anaphora with the phrase "I have a dream" repeated at the start of each sentence.
Writers often employ anaphora to create rhythm and emphasis in their works.

Related Terms

  • antonym: epiphora - repetition of a word or phrase at the end of successive clauses
