Examples

To glower at people

She sat at the table and continued to glower at her brother after their argument.

Synonyms

  • lower
    7.9 4.55/m
    lower is to look angry or sullen quietly, while glower is to stare with a dark, openly angry expression.
  • lour
    9.9 0.00/m
    lour is an old literary verb meaning to look sullen and frown, while glower is more common and shows a hard angry stare.
  • scowl
    4.1 4.44/m
    scowl is to frown in anger, showing a tight brow, while glower often implies a long, sullen stare.
  • glare
    3.2 7.66/m
    glare is to stare fiercely and openly in anger, while glower is darker, more sullen and quieter in expression.
  • frown
    1.0 20/m
    frown is to wrinkle the brow for anger or concentration, while glower is a full angry or sullen stare at someone.
