uncountably

adverb
as a mass noun
8.1
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈkaʊntəbli / (AmE), / ʌnˈkaʊntəbli / (BrE)
in a way that refers to something as a mass or uncountable noun, not as separate, countable items

Examples

Uncountably used nouns
In English, the word 'information' is used uncountably and does not have a plural form.
Some nouns, like 'furniture', are treated uncountably in grammar and do not take an 's' for plural.
The teacher explained that 'rice' is almost always used uncountably in English sentences.

Related Terms

  • antonym: countably - in a way that refers to separate, countable items
