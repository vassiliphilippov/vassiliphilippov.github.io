genetic screening

noun
testing for genetic susceptibility to disease
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈnɛtɪk ˈskriːnɪŋ / (AmE), / dʒəˈnɛtɪk ˈskriːnɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of testing a group of people to identify individuals who are at risk of a particular genetic disorder or disease

Examples

Genetic screening programs
Many countries have introduced genetic screening programs for newborns to detect treatable diseases.
Routine genetic screening can help identify inherited health risks early in life.
The ethical implications of genetic screening are widely debated among medical professionals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: genetic testing - testing for specific genetic mutations or disorders, usually in individuals
