pontoon bridge

noun
temporary floating bridge
6.8 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / p…ënÀàtun br…™d í / (AmE), / p…ínÀàtuÀên br…™d í / (BrE)
A temporary bridge supported by floating pontoons, often used by military or engineering teams to cross rivers or other bodies of water.

Examples

A pontoon bridge over river
After the flood washed away the old bridge, a pontoon bridge was set up as a temporary solution.
During the training exercise, soldiers practiced building a pontoon bridge in just under two hours.
The army engineers quickly constructed a pontoon bridge across the river to transport vehicles.

Synonyms

  • floating bridge
    4.2 0.07/m
    a floating bridge sits on pontoons or floating platforms for crossings in many settings, while a pontoon bridge uses linked pontoons for military or emergency crossings.
  • bateau bridge
    9.9 0.00/m
    a bateau bridge is built on flat-bottom boats for quick military or emergency crossings, while a pontoon bridge uses any floating pontoons for temporary crossings.
