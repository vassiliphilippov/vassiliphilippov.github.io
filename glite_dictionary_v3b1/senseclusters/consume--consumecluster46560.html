consume

verb
eat or drink completely
2.4 14/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàsuÀêm / (AmE), / k…ônÀàsjuÀêm / (BrE)
to eat or drink something; to take food or drink into the body

Examples

To consume a meal
After the long hike, she consumed her dinner with remarkable speed.
The children managed to consume an entire cake within a few minutes.
The group consumed all the snacks before the movie even started.
Children should not consume too many sugary snacks throughout the week.
Doctors recommend that adults consume at least five servings of fruits and vegetables every day.
Some people consume caffeine regularly in the form of coffee or tea.

Synonyms

  • eat
    1.0 309/m
    eat is putting food into your mouth, chewing, and swallowing, while consume is a more formal, general word for taking food or drink.
  • ingest
    3.6 4.11/m
    ingest is used in medical or scientific contexts for swallowing substances, while consume is a general word for eating or drinking.
  • have
    1.0 167/m
    have is commonly used when you eat or drink a meal in everyday speech, while consume is more formal and general.
  • take in
    2.5 0.62/m
    take in often means eating or drinking in a particular or regular way, while consume simply names taking food or drink into the body.
  • partake
    6.5 0.70/m
    partake means joining others to eat or drink in a shared or formal setting, while consume simply means taking food or drink.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abstain - to deliberately avoid consuming or partaking in something
  • synonym: devour - to eat something quickly and eagerly
  • synonym: ingest - to take food or drink into the body by swallowing or absorbing it
