ironic

adjective
humorously sarcastic or mocking
4.8 2.74/m
Pronunciation: / a…™Ààr…ëÀên…™k / (AmE), / a…™Ààr…ín…™k / (BrE)
Expressing an idea, remark, or situation in a way that is deliberately contrary to what is expected, often to be humorously sarcastic or mocking.

Examples

Ironic comment
He gave her an ironic smile after she missed the obvious answer.
Her ironic comments during the debate made the audience laugh.
The ironic tone of his letter made it clear he was not truly angry.

Synonyms

  • ironical
    6.4 0.06/m
    ironical is a less common adjective used in writing to sound formal or old-fashioned, while ironic is more common.
  • wry
    7.5 2.84/m
    wry is dry, slightly sarcastic humor often with a gentle smile, while ironic is broader and can be harsher.
  • tongue-in-cheek
    7.4 0.81/m
    tongue-in-cheek is a playful, joking way to speak that is not serious, while ironic can be more general and subtle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sarcastic - using irony to mock or convey contempt
  • antonym: sincere - genuine; not ironic or mocking
