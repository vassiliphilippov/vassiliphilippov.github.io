concealed

adjective
hidden; kept out of sight
3.7 0.27/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàsild / (AmE), / k…ônÀàsiÀêld / (BrE)
kept hidden or out of sight; not visible

Examples

A concealed camera
She carried a concealed camera in her purse to document the meeting.
The building had a concealed staircase for emergencies.
The detective found a concealed door behind the bookshelf.
She carried a concealed weapon for personal protection.
The entrance to the cave was concealed by thick undergrowth.
The treasure was concealed behind a false wall in the basement.

Synonyms

  • hidden
    1.1 7.26/m
    hidden is the everyday word for something kept out of sight, while concealed sounds more formal and careful.
  • out of sight
    3.4 0.94/m
    out of sight describes something not visible from view, while concealed suggests it is purposely kept hidden or covered.
  • covert
    6.8 6.39/m
    covert describes secret actions or plans kept from view, while concealed mainly means something physically hidden from sight.
  • secret
    2.0 41/m
    secret refers to information or actions kept from others, while concealed focuses on being physically kept out of sight.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hidden - kept out of sight; concealed
