Mennonite

noun
member of an Anabaptist Christian group
6.9
Pronunciation: /ˈmen.ə.naɪt/ (AmE), /ˈmen.ə.naɪt/ (BrE)
A member of a Christian Anabaptist group, originating in Holland, known for simple living and pacifism.

Examples

A Mennonite church
Historically, Mennonites faced persecution for their religious beliefs and commitment to nonviolence.
Many Mennonites continue to live in rural communities and practice traditional farming methods.
The Mennonite church emphasizes peace, community service, and simplicity.
