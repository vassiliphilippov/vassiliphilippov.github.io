brand-name drug

noun
patented drug sold under a trade name
6.7 3.5
Pronunciation: /brænd neɪm drʌg/ (AmE), /brænd neɪm drʌg/ (BrE)
A drug sold under a proprietary, trademarked name and protected by a patent, produced and sold exclusively by the company owning the patent.

Examples

A brand-name drug patent
After the patent expires, a brand-name drug can often be produced as a generic.
Insurance may not always cover the cost of a brand-name drug if a cheaper alternative exists.
The doctor prescribed a brand-name drug because no generic equivalent was available.

Related Terms

  • antonym: generic drug - a drug not protected by a patent, usually sold under its chemical name
