preoccupation

noun
prior occupancy; taking possession first
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːˌɑːkjəˈpeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌpriːˌɒkjʊˈpeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of taking possession or occupancy of something before someone else does

Examples

Preoccupation of land
Historical disputes often began with the preoccupation of valuable territory.
The preoccupation of the land by settlers prevented others from claiming it.
The court ruled that his preoccupation of the property was legitimate and binding.
