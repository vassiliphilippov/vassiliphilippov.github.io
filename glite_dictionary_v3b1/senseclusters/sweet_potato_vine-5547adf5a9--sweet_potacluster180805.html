sweet potato vine

noun
plant grown for edible sweet tubers
1.8 2.9
Pronunciation: /swiːt pə.ˈteɪ.toʊ vaɪn/ (AmE), /swiːt pə.ˈteɪ.təʊ vaɪn/ (BrE)
A tropical to subtropical trailing plant cultivated for its large, sweet, orange-fleshed tuberous roots, commonly known as sweet potatoes.

Examples

Many gardeners use the sweet potato vine as an ornamental plant because of its vibrant foliage.
The sweet potato vine spread quickly across the garden bed, producing beautiful purple flowers.
