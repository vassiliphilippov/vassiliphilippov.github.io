brucellosis

noun
infectious disease from animals, causes fever
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˌbruːsəˈloʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌbruːsɪˈləʊsɪs /; / ˌbruːsəˈləʊsɪs / (BrE)
an infectious bacterial disease that affects animals (such as cattle and sheep) and can be transmitted to humans, causing fever and other symptoms

Examples

Brucellosis infection in animals
Public health officials warned that unpasteurized milk could transmit brucellosis to consumers.
The veterinarian suspected brucellosis after the farmer's repeated fevers and muscle pain.
Travelers to rural areas are at risk of brucellosis if they come into contact with livestock.
Farmers are advised to vaccinate their cattle to prevent the spread of brucellosis.
Humans can contract brucellosis through unpasteurized dairy products from infected animals.
The veterinarian diagnosed the cow with brucellosis after observing several symptoms.
