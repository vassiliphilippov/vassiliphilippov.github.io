chronicon

proper noun
medieval chronicle title
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɑːnɪˌkɑn / (AmE), / ˈkrɒnɪˌkɒn / (BrE)
the title of several medieval chronicles or historical works, often used for Latin historical records.

Examples

The Chronicon of Thietmar
The Chronicon of Thietmar of Merseburg provides valuable insight into early medieval Germany.
Scholars often reference the Chronicon to study the events of the 12th century.
Matthew Paris's Chronicon is an important source for English medieval history.
