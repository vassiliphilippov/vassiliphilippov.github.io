photomultiplier

noun
light-detecting device
9.4
Pronunciation: /foʊ.toʊ.ˈmʌl.tɪ.plaɪ.ər/ (AmE), /fəʊ.təʊ.ˈmʌl.tɪ.plaɪ.ə/ (BrE)
an electronic device that detects very weak light and amplifies it into a measurable signal

Examples

A photomultiplier tube
The photomultiplier is essential for experiments that require detecting single photons.
Astronomers use a photomultiplier to observe faint stars in distant galaxies.
A photomultiplier can amplify even the weakest light signals for scientific analysis.
