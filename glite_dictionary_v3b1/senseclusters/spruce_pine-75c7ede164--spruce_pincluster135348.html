spruce pine

noun
pine tree of southeastern US
6.1 6.3
Pronunciation: /spruːs paɪn/ (AmE), /spruːs paɪn/ (BrE)
a pine tree (Pinus glabra) native to the southeastern and eastern United States and Canada, with two needles per cluster and light, soft wood, valued especially for pulpwood

Examples

Tall spruce pine trees
The forest was dominated by tall spruce pines reaching toward the sky.
The light wood of spruce pine is often used in construction.
We identified several spruce pine specimens during our hike in Georgia.
He identified the tree as a spruce pine by its slender needles and smooth bark.
Many paper mills use spruce pine due to its suitability for making pulp.
The forests in Georgia are home to the spruce pine, which thrives in moist, lowland soils.
