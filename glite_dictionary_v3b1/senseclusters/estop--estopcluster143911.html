estop

verb
to prevent a claim
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈstɑːp/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈstɒp/ (BrE)
to prevent someone from asserting a claim or fact that is inconsistent with their previous actions or statements, especially in a legal context

Examples

To estop a claim
The court may estop a company from denying responsibility after it has accepted liability.
If you sign the contract, you could be estopped from later claiming you did not agree.
The judge decided to estop the witness from contradicting her earlier testimony.

Related Terms

  • antonym: allow - to permit or let someone do something
  • synonym: bar - to officially prevent someone from doing something
