peach blight

noun
disease of peach trees
Pronunciation: / pitʃ blaɪt / (AmE), / piːtʃ blaɪt / (BrE)
A disease affecting peach trees and related species, typically causing damage to leaves, fruit, or branches.

Examples

Peach blight disease
Effective management strategies can reduce the risk of peach blight destroying an entire harvest.
Farmers are urged to inspect their crops regularly for signs of peach blight.
The orchard suffered significant losses when peach blight spread rapidly through the trees.
