enculturate

verb
to adopt cultural norms
9.0
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkʌltʃəˌreɪt / (AmE), / ɪnˈkʌltʃəreɪt / (BrE)
to learn or be taught the customs, values, and behaviors of a particular culture or group

Examples

Children enculturate quickly
Children quickly enculturate by observing the traditions and language of their community.
New immigrants often enculturate through daily interactions with local residents.
Schools play a key role in helping students enculturate into the wider society.
Parents play a key role in helping children enculturate into their community's traditions.
New employees must enculturate quickly to fit in with the company's values and practices.
Traveling abroad can help people enculturate by exposing them to different customs and beliefs.
Children are often enculturated through family traditions and community events.
New employees are enculturated into the company’s values during orientation.
Immigrants may enculturate by learning the language and customs of their new country.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acculturate - to adopt the cultural traits of another group
  • synonym: assimilate - to become part of a group or culture by adopting its ways
  • synonym: socialize - to learn to behave in a way that is acceptable in society
