adaptationist

adjective
evolutionary adaptation view
8.4
Pronunciation: /ə.dæp.ˈteɪ.ʃən.ɪst/ /; / /æ.dæp.ˈteɪ.ʃən.ɪst/ (AmE), /ə.dæp.ˈteɪ.ʃən.ɪst/ /; / /æ.dæp.ˈteɪ.ʃən.ɪst/ (BrE)
relating to the belief that traits or behaviors evolved mainly as adaptations for survival or reproduction

Examples

Adaptationist theory
The adaptationist perspective suggests that most animal behaviors have evolved for specific purposes.
Some scientists criticize the adaptationist approach for overlooking other evolutionary factors.
Her research takes an adaptationist stance when explaining the development of human language.

Related Terms

  • synonym: evolutionary - relating to the process of evolution
  • antonym: non-adaptationist - not supporting the view that traits evolved mainly as adaptations
