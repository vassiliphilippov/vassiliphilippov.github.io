autophyte

noun
plant making its own food
6.0 8.7
Pronunciation: /ˈɑː.tə.faɪt/ /; / /ˈɔː.tə.faɪt/ (AmE), /ˈɔː.tə.faɪt/ (BrE)
A plant that is capable of synthesizing its own food from inorganic substances, typically through photosynthesis.

Examples

An autophyte plant
Algae are often cited as classic examples of autophytes in aquatic ecosystems.
The majority of green plants are considered autophytes due to their ability to perform photosynthesis.
Unlike parasites, autophytes do not depend on other organisms for their nutrition.

Related Terms

  • synonym: autotroph - organism that produces its own food from inorganic substances
  • antonym: heterophyte - plant that obtains nourishment from other organisms
