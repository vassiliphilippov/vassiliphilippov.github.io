erode

verb
to gradually weaken or deteriorate
6.5 3.4
Pronunciation: / ɪˈroʊd / (AmE), / ɪˈrəʊd / (BrE)
(of something abstract) to gradually diminish or be destroyed over time; to weaken or deteriorate slowly.

Examples

Erode trust
Her trust in the company has slowly eroded over time after repeated disappointments.
The constant pressure can erode even the strongest self-confidence.
Years of criticism gradually eroded his enthusiasm for the project.
