pyridoxamine

noun
a form of vitamin B6
Pronunciation: / pɪˌrɪdəksəˈmin / (AmE), / pɪˌrɪdəksəˈmiːn / (BrE)
a form of vitamin B6 that is essential for the metabolism of amino acids and starch in the body

Examples

Form of pyridoxamine vitamin
A deficiency in pyridoxamine can lead to problems with metabolism and overall health.
Some enzymes require pyridoxamine to help process amino acids in the liver.
Vitamin B6 is found in several forms, including pyridoxamine, pyridoxal, and pyridoxine.
