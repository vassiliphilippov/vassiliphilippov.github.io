chronology

noun
record of events by order of occurrence
5.3 3.28/m
Pronunciation: / kr…ôÀàn…ël…ôd íi /; / kr…ôÀàn…ëÀêl…ôd íi / (AmE), / kr…ôÀàn…íl…ôd íi / (BrE)
the order or arrangement of events in the order they happened, or a record of this order

Examples

Synonyms

  • chronological succession
    6.8 0.01/m
    chronological succession shows events in time in a simple linear sequence, while chronology often means the record or study of that order.
  • temporal order
    5.9 0.07/m
    temporal order arranges events by when they occur in time, while chronology often refers to the record of those events.
  • temporal arrangement
    7.0 0.00/m
    temporal arrangement organizes events by time for planning or display, while chronology is the factual order or record of occurrence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: history - the study of past events, particularly in human affairs
  • synonym: timeline - a graphical representation of a period of time, typically showing events in chronological order
