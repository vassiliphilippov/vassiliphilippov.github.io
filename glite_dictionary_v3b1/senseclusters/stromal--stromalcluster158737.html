stromal

adjective
of supporting tissue
Pronunciation: / ˈstroʊməl / (AmE), / ˈstrəʊməl / (BrE)
relating to the stroma, the supportive or connective tissue or matrix in an organ, cell, or structure

Examples

Stromal tissue support
The stromal cells provide structural support to the organ's functional tissue.
Researchers studied the stromal components of the tumor to understand its growth.
In fungi, the stromal tissue often contains the reproductive structures necessary for spore production.
The stromal cells provide essential support for the organ's structure and function.
Researchers are investigating how stromal tissue influences the progression of certain cancers.
Damage to the stromal layer can affect the healing process in many organs.
The stromal cells provide structural support within the bone marrow.
Scientists studied the stromal tissue to understand its role in tumor growth.
Chloroplast stromal enzymes are essential for the process of photosynthesis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: connective - relating to tissue that supports or binds other tissues
  • antonym: parenchymal - relating to the functional tissue of an organ
  • synonym: stroma - the supportive framework of a biological cell, tissue, or organ
