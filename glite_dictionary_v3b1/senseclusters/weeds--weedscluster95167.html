weeds

noun
mourning clothes (archaic)
9.9
Pronunciation: / widz / (AmE), / wiːdz / (BrE)
(archaic) Black clothes, especially a dress, worn by a widow as a sign of mourning.

Examples

Wearing weeds of mourning
In the old portrait, the woman is shown in somber weeds, a symbol of her loss.
She dressed in her finest weeds for the funeral, her sorrow plain to all.
Victorian widows were often expected to wear weeds for an extended period after a husband's death.
