asepsis

noun
freedom from harmful microorganisms
Pronunciation: / əˈsɛp.sɪs / (AmE), / əˈsɛp.sɪs / (BrE)
the state or process of being free from harmful microorganisms, especially to prevent infection in medical or laboratory settings

Examples

Good asepsis practice
Good asepsis is essential in any laboratory handling biological samples.
Strict asepsis must be maintained during surgical procedures to prevent infection.
The instruments were sterilized to achieve complete asepsis before the operation began.
Modern hospitals rely on asepsis to keep operating rooms free from harmful microorganisms.
Strict asepsis must be maintained during surgery to reduce the risk of infection.
The introduction of asepsis transformed medical procedures in the late 19th century.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antisepsis - the practice of using antiseptics to eliminate microorganisms
  • antonym: sepsis - the presence of harmful bacteria and their toxins in tissues
  • synonym: sterility - the condition of being sterile, not supporting the growth of microorganisms
