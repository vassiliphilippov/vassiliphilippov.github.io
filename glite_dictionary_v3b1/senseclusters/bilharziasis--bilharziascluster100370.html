bilharziasis

noun
disease caused by Schistosoma parasites
Pronunciation: / bɪlˌhɑːrˈzaɪəsɪs / (AmE), / bɪlˌhɑːˈzʌɪəsɪs / (BrE)
A disease caused by infection with parasitic worms of the genus Schistosoma, especially common in tropical and subtropical regions, leading to a range of symptoms depending on the organs affected.

Examples

Bilharziasis infection
Many rural communities in Africa face health challenges due to high rates of bilharziasis.
Preventing bilharziasis requires improving water sanitation and reducing contact with infested water sources.
The doctor diagnosed the patient with bilharziasis after detecting Schistosoma eggs in a blood sample.
