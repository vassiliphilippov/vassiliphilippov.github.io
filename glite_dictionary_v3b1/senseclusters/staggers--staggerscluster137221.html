staggers

noun
disease causing staggering in livestock
8.1 8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈstæɡərz / (AmE), / ˈstæɡəz / (BrE)
A disease of the central nervous system in animals, especially horses and cattle, marked by a staggering, swaying gait and frequent falling.

Examples

Animal staggers disease
Farmers are warned to watch for signs of staggers during the wet season.
Sheep suffering from staggers often collapse suddenly in their pens.
The veterinarian diagnosed the horse with staggers after observing its unsteady gait.
