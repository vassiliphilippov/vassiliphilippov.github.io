benchland

noun
level land on slope
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛntʃˌlænd / (AmE), / ˈbɛntʃˌlænd / (BrE)
a strip of level or gently sloping land on the side of a hill or valley, often used for farming

Examples

The benchland strip
The farmer planted wheat on the benchland above the river valley.
Vineyards thrive on the benchland where the soil is rich and the slope is gentle.
Erosion is less severe on benchland compared to steeper hillsides.

Related Terms

  • antonym: plain - a large area of flat land
  • synonym: terrace - a flat area created on a slope for farming
