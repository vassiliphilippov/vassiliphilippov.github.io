cut

verb
fell trees or timber
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: / kʌt / (AmE), / kʌt / (BrE)
to divide, separate, or make an opening in something using a sharp tool; to make something shorter or into pieces by slicing or trimming

Examples

To cut a branch
After the storm, they had to cut many fallen branches to clear the road.
The company plans to cut timber in the northern forest next spring.
Workers used chainsaws to cut dozens of pine trees in the clearing.
Farmers usually cut the hay in late summer.
Modern machines help cut large fields of grain much faster than before.
The workers began to cut the wheat early in the morning.
Doctors may need to cut the skin to perform the operation.
Please cut the paper along the dotted line as instructed.
She accidentally cut her finger while chopping vegetables in the kitchen.
