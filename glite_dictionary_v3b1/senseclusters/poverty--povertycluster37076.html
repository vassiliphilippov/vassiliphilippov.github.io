poverty

noun
state of being extremely poor
2.0 52/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…ëÀêv…öti / (AmE), / Ààp…ív…ôti / (BrE)
The state of being extremely poor, having very little money or material possessions.

Examples

Extreme poverty
His childhood was marked by poverty and hardship, but he never lost hope.
The organization works to reduce poverty in urban and rural communities.
Thousands of families are still living in poverty despite recent economic growth.

Synonyms

  • destitution
    7.9 0.62/m
    destitution means having no money, possessions, or support at all, while poverty is lack of money or basic possessions.
  • penury
    9.7 0.29/m
    penury is extreme, almost total lack of money and goods, while poverty can refer to general lack of resources.
  • indigence
    9.9 0.12/m
    indigence denotes severe lack of basic needs and shelter, while poverty can be less extreme or include low income.
  • pennilessness
    6.8 0.01/m
    pennilessness means having no money at all, while poverty covers both low money and few material possessions.
  • neediness
    4.6 0.02/m
    neediness shows lack of necessities like food or shelter, while poverty is the wider state of being very poor.
  • pauperism
    9.9 0.07/m
    pauperism names long-term life as a pauper in old usage, while poverty is the general modern term for being poor.
  • poorness
    2.5 0.01/m
    poorness means low quality or lack of wealth in plain speech, while poverty is the clearer term for extreme lack of money.

Related Terms

  • synonym: destitution - a state of extreme poverty, lacking basic necessities
  • antonym: wealth - an abundance of valuable possessions or money
