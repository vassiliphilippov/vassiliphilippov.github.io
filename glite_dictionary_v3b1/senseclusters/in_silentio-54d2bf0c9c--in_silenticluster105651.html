a Latin legal term meaning 'in silence' or 'without explicit statement', used when something is done or decided without being openly stated

Examples

In silentio ruling
The judge ruled in silentio, offering no written explanation for the verdict.
Some legal principles are established in silentio when courts do not address them directly.
The contract was renewed in silentio, as neither party objected or commented.

Related Terms

  • antonym: explicit - stated clearly and in detail
  • synonym: tacit - understood or implied without being stated
