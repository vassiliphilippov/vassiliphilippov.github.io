bronchoconstrictor

noun
agent narrowing airways
9.1
Pronunciation: /brɑːŋ.koʊ.kən.ˈstrɪk.tər/ /; / /brɑːŋ.kə.kən.ˈstrɪk.tər/ (AmE), /brɒŋ.kəʊ.kən.ˈstrɪk.tə/ /; / /brɒŋ.kə.kən.ˈstrɪk.tə/ (BrE)
a substance or agent that causes the narrowing of the bronchial tubes in the lungs

Examples

Bronchoconstrictor drug
Certain medications can act as a bronchoconstrictor and make breathing more difficult for asthma patients.
Allergic reactions may trigger the release of a bronchoconstrictor in the body.
Doctors advise avoiding known bronchoconstrictors if you have respiratory conditions.
