drudgery

noun
tedious or monotonous work
Pronunciation: / ˈdrʌdʒəri / (AmE), / ˈdrʌdʒəri / (BrE)
hard, monotonous, and often dull work; tedious and repetitive labor.

Examples

Endless drudgery work
Most of the drudgery in the office involves endless paperwork and data entry.
She longed for adventure instead of the daily drudgery of washing dishes and laundry.
Technology has helped reduce the drudgery of many household tasks.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ease - lack of difficulty or effort; comfort
  • synonym: labor - physical or mental work, especially of a hard or fatiguing kind
  • synonym: toil - hard and continuous work
