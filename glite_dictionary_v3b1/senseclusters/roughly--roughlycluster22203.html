roughly

adverb
in a rough or violent way
6.1
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌf.li / (AmE), / ˈrʌf.li / (BrE)
In a rough or violent manner; without gentleness or care.

Examples

Pushed roughly aside
He was pushed roughly aside by the impatient crowd.
She woke up suddenly after being shaken roughly by her brother.
The guards handled the prisoners roughly, showing little concern for their welfare.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gently - in a mild, kind, or tender manner
