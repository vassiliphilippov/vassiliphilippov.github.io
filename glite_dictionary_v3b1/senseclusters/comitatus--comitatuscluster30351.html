comitatus

noun
county under a count
Pronunciation: / ˌkoʊmɪˈteɪtəs / (AmE), / ˌkɒmɪˈtɑːtəs / (BrE)
a county or administrative district governed by a count, especially in historical contexts

Examples

A medieval comitatus
In medieval Europe, a comitatus was often governed by a noble appointed by the king.
The boundaries of the comitatus changed frequently due to wars and political alliances.
Historical documents list the village as part of the local comitatus in the twelfth century.

Related Terms

  • synonym: county - an administrative division of a country or state
  • synonym: duchy - a territory ruled by a duke or duchess
