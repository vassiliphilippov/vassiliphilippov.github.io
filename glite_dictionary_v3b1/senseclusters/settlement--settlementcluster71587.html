settlement

noun
ending a business by paying off liabilities
1.8 2.9
Pronunciation: /ˈse.təl.mənt/ (AmE), /ˈse.təl.mənt/ (BrE)
the finalization of a business or financial operation, such as paying off debts or completing a trade or payment

Examples

Financial settlement
Efficient settlement of failed enterprises is essential for a healthy business environment.
Following the bankruptcy, the court oversaw the company's settlement to ensure all creditors were paid.
The settlement of the partnership involved selling its remaining assets and distributing the proceeds.
The platform completes the settlement of trades on the blockchain, automatically transferring funds.
Banks use central systems to confirm each settlement before releasing securities to the buyer's account.
After the smart contract executed, the settlement was recorded on the ledger and cannot be reversed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clearing - the process that matches and prepares financial transactions so they can be settled
  • synonym: liquidation - the process of dissolving a business by selling assets and paying debts
  • synonym: settle - to complete a payment or transfer so that the recipient receives funds or ownership
