out-of-the-way

adjective
remote; not easily reached
8.5 4.9
Pronunciation: /aʊt.əv.ðə.ˈweɪ/ (AmE), /aʊt.əv.ðə.ˈweɪ/ (BrE)
(of a place) remote from centers of population or much-traveled areas; not easily reached or frequently visited.

Examples

Out-of-the-way village
She enjoyed vacationing in out-of-the-way villages that few tourists ever visit.
The hikers preferred to explore out-of-the-way trails away from the crowds.
We discovered an out-of-the-way café while wandering through the old quarter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: remote - (of a place) far from the main centers of population
  • synonym: secluded - (of a place) not seen or visited by many people
