brain fever

noun
historical term for bacterial meningitis
6.9 8.6
Pronunciation: /breɪn ˈfiː.vər/ (AmE), /breɪn ˈfiː.və/ (BrE)
An old term for severe inflammation of the brain or its membranes, particularly bacterial meningitis.

Examples

Brain fever illness
Before advances in medicine, brain fever was commonly believed to be a mysterious and deadly illness.
In Victorian novels, characters often succumb to brain fever following emotional distress or trauma.
The doctor feared the patient was suffering from brain fever after observing the severe symptoms.
