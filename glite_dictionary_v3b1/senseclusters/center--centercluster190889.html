center

noun
central filling of a candy
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛn.tɚ / (AmE), / ˈsɛn.tə / (BrE)
the sweet central part of a piece of candy, usually enclosed by chocolate or another coating

Examples

Candy center
Each piece in the box had a different kind of center, from nougat to mint.
She prefers truffles with a fruit-flavored center.
The caramel center oozed out as I bit into the chocolate.
If you cut the bonbon in half, you'll see the fruit-flavored centre inside.
My favorite candies are the ones with a creamy caramel centre.
She carefully bit through the chocolate shell to taste the soft centre of the truffle.
