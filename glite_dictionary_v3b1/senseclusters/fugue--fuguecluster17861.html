fugue

noun
dissociative disorder involving loss of identity and travel
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / fjuːɡ /; / ˈfjuːɡ / (AmE), / fjuːɡ /; / ˈfjuːɡ / (BrE)
a state of lost memory and identity, often involving wandering or acting without awareness

Examples

Fugue state
After weeks missing, the man was found living under a different name, suffering a fugue state.
Cases of fugue are rare but well-documented in psychiatric literature.
He remembered nothing about the time spent away during his fugue.
During her fugue, she traveled several hundred miles without any memory of doing so.
Episodes of fugue are rare but can profoundly affect a person's sense of self.
The patient entered a fugue after the traumatic event, unable to recall his own name for days.
