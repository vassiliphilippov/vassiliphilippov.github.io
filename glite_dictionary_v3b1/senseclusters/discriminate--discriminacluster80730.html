discriminate

verb
treat unfairly based on group identity
3.4
Pronunciation: / dɪˈskrɪməˌneɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈskrɪmɪneɪt / (BrE)
to treat a person or group differently, especially in a worse way, because of their sex, race, or other characteristic

Examples

To discriminate against people
It is illegal to discriminate against someone based on their race or gender.
Many companies have strict policies not to discriminate in hiring practices.
She felt that the club discriminated against women by refusing them membership.

Related Terms

  • antonym: favor - to treat someone better than others, often unfairly
  • synonym: segregate - to separate groups of people based on race or other differences
