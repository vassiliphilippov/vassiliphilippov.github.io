go along to get along

verb
avoid conflict by conforming
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊ əˈlɔŋ tu ɡɛt əˈlɔŋ / (AmE), / ɡəʊ əˈlɒŋ tə ɡɛt əˈlɒŋ / (BrE)
to act in a way that conforms or agrees with others in order to avoid conflict or maintain harmony

Examples

To go along to get along
If you always go along to get along, you may never achieve what you truly want.
In meetings, she often chooses to go along to get along rather than voice her objections.
Some people prefer to go along to get along in order to keep the peace at family gatherings.
