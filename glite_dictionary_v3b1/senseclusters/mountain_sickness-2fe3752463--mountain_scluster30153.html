mountain sickness

noun
altitude-related illness with nausea and breathlessness
2.0 3.8
Pronunciation: /ˈmaʊn.tən ˈsɪk.nəs/ /; / /ˈmaʊn.tən ˈsɪk.nɪs/ (AmE), /ˈmaʊn.tɪn ˈsɪk.nəs/ /; / /ˈmaʊn.tɪn ˈsɪk.nɪs/ (BrE)
A condition characterized by nausea, shortness of breath, and other symptoms experienced at high altitudes, typically above 10,000 feet.

Examples

Mountain sickness symptoms
Climbers should ascend slowly to avoid mountain sickness at high altitudes.
He suffered from severe mountain sickness after reaching the summit of the mountain.
Proper acclimatization can help prevent mountain sickness during expeditions in the Himalayas.

Related Terms

  • synonym: altitude sickness - illness caused by reduced oxygen at high elevations
