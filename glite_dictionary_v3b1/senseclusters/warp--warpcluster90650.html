warp

verb
to bend or twist out of shape
4.9 1.65/m
Pronunciation: / w…îrp / (AmE), / w…îÀêp / (BrE)
to bend or twist out of shape, especially due to heat, pressure, damp, or stress

Examples

To warp wood
Don't leave your vinyl records in the sun or they might warp.
Repeated exposure to heat can warp plastic containers.
The wooden floor began to warp after the pipe burst and flooded the basement.

Synonyms

  • deform
    4.8 3.79/m
    deform is a general change that makes objects bent or twisted, while warp is bending caused by heat, damp, or stress.
  • misshape
    6.9 0.07/m
    misshape gives an object an incorrect or ugly form, while warp usually refers to bending from heat, pressure, or damp.
  • buckle
    4.1 2.68/m
    buckle bends or collapses under heavy pressure or heat, while warp often means a slow twist or bend from damp or stress.
  • heave
    5.9 0.16/m
    heave bulges or lifts a surface under pressure or heat, while warp usually means a twist or bend over time.
  • contort
    7.5 1.95/m
    contort twists or bends something in a violent, strange way, while warp describes gradual bending from heat, damp, or stress.
  • distort
    4.3 6.67/m
    distort changes shape or meaning so it is wrong or unclear, while warp focuses on physical bending from heat, damp, or stress.
