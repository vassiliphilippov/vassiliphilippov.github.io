cycle per second

noun
a unit of frequency; one hertz
2.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪkəl pɚ ˈsɛkənd / (AmE), / ˈsaɪkəl pə ˈsɛkənd / (BrE)
a unit of frequency equal to one oscillation or cycle occurring every second; equivalent to one hertz

Examples

One cycle per second
Before the adoption of the hertz, scientists commonly used cycles per second as the unit for frequency.
The frequency of the alternating current is measured in cycles per second.
This radio signal has a frequency of one million cycles per second.
