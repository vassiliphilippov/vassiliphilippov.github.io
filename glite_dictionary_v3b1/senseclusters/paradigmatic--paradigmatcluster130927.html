paradigmatic

adjective
relating to substitutable members of a class (linguistics)
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˌpær.ə.dɪgˈmæt.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌpar.ə.dɪgˈmat.ɪk / (BrE)
(Linguistics) Relating to or denoting the relationship of elements that can substitute for each other in a particular context, as members of a class.

Examples

Paradigmatic relation
In linguistics, a paradigmatic relation exists when words can fill the same grammatical slot.
The teacher explained paradigmatic word associations using examples from the vocabulary list.
Verb forms in different tenses are paradigmatic variations within the same grammatical structure.

Related Terms

  • antonym: syntagmatic - relating to the combination of linguistic units in sequence
