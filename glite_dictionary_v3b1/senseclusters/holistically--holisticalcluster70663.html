holistically

adverb
considering the whole
5.4
Pronunciation: / hoʊˈlɪstɪkli / (AmE), / həˈlɪstɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that considers the whole or all parts together, rather than focusing on individual elements

Examples

To look holistically
The doctor examined the patient holistically, taking into account both physical and mental health.
To address climate change holistically, we must consider economic, social, and environmental factors together.
The teacher encouraged students to think holistically about the project, not just focus on their individual parts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: comprehensively - in a thorough and complete way
  • antonym: partially - in a way that only considers part of something
