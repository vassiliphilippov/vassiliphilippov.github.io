breach

noun
unauthorized access to data
5.8 3/m
Pronunciation: / briÀêt É / (AmE), / briÀêt É / (BrE)
An incident in which protected, sensitive, or confidential digital information is accessed, disclosed, or taken without authorization, often as the result of hacking, security failures, or inadequate safeguards.

Examples

A data breach
The company announced a breach of customers' personal data after the attack.
Regulators require firms to report any breach that exposes consumer information within seventy-two hours.
After a phishing email, the team discovered a breach and began incident response immediately.

Synonyms

  • compromise
    6.5 0.9/m
    compromise is a security incident where systems or accounts are secretly accessed or changed, while breach covers any unauthorized access or data exposure.
  • data leak
    4.6 0.01/m
    data leak is when confidential information is exposed to outsiders, often by accident, while breach can include access, theft, or exposure.
  • leakage
    4.6 0.2/m
    leakage is an accidental or unauthorized release of private digital information, while breach often implies a wider security failure or hack.
  • leak
    4.8 6/m
    leak is the act of making secret information public, often by someone inside, while breach refers to a security failure that allows access.
  • break-in
    4.5 0.06/m
    break-in is an unauthorized entry into a computer system, usually focused on access, while breach can also mean data theft or wider exposure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: data leak - the accidental or intentional release of private or confidential data to unauthorized recipients
  • synonym: security breach - an incident in which security is compromised, allowing unauthorized access to systems or data
