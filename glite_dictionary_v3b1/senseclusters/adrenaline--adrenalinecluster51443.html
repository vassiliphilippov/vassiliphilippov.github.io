adrenaline

noun
hormone produced in response to stress
1.8 3.0
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈdren.ə.lɪn/ /; / /ə.ˈdren.ə.lən/ (AmE), /ə.ˈdren.ə.lɪn/ /; / /ə.ˈdren.ə.lən/ (BrE)
a hormone secreted by the adrenal glands in response to stress, increasing heart rate and energy; also used as a drug to treat severe allergic reactions

Examples

Adrenaline rush
Doctors sometimes inject adrenaline to treat severe allergic reactions.
The thrilling roller coaster ride gave everyone an intense adrenaline rush.
When faced with danger, your body releases adrenaline to help you react quickly.
