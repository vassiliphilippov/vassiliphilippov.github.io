saeta

noun
Spanish religious song
Pronunciation: / saˈeɪtə / (AmE), / saˈeɪtə / (BrE)
a traditional Spanish religious song, often sung during Holy Week processions, expressing deep emotion and devotion

Examples

A saeta song
During the Holy Week procession, a singer performed a moving saeta from a balcony.
The crowd fell silent as the haunting notes of the saeta filled the night air.
Many visitors to Seville are deeply touched by the tradition of the saeta during Easter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cante jondo - a style of Andalusian flamenco song with deep emotion
  • synonym: hymn - a religious song of praise
