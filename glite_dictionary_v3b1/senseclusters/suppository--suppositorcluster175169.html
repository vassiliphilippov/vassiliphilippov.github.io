suppository

noun
medication plug for rectal or vaginal insertion
5.7
Pronunciation: / səˈpɑːzəˌtɔri / (AmE), / səˈpɒzətəri / (BrE)
a small, solid dose of medication made to be inserted into the rectum or vagina, where it melts and is absorbed by the body

Examples

A suppository dose
If you have trouble swallowing pills, your medication may be available as a suppository.
Store the suppository in a cool place so it does not melt before use.
The doctor recommended using a suppository to relieve the child's fever.
