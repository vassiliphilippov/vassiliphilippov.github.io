semivolatile

adjective
partly evaporating
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛmiˈvɑlət̬aɪl / (AmE), / ˌsɛmiˈvɒlətaɪl / (BrE)
partly able to evaporate at normal temperatures, especially referring to chemicals that are not fully volatile

Examples

A semivolatile compound
A semivolatile compound can be found in both air and soil samples.
Researchers measured the levels of semivolatile organic chemicals in the river water.
Proper storage is important to prevent loss of semivolatile substances during analysis.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: nonvolatile - not evaporating at normal temperatures
  • degree higher: volatile - easily evaporating at normal temperatures
