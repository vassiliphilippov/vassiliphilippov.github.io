spot

noun
scheduled segment in entertainment or broadcast
3.9 3.1
Pronunciation: /spɑːt/ (AmE), /spɒt/ (BrE)
a short segment or slot in entertainment, broadcasting, or advertising

Examples

A TV spot
Each performer will have a ten-minute spot during the broadcast.
She was thrilled to get a spot in the comedy show lineup.
The band played an energetic set during their spot on the festival stage.
A clever spot can make an advertisement memorable to viewers.
The station aired a thirty-second spot during halftime.
They bought a full-page spot in the magazine to promote their new product.

Related Terms

  • synonym: slot - an allotted time or position, especially in a schedule
