malapportionment

noun
unequal representation
Pronunciation: / ˌmæl.əˈpɔːr.ʃən.mənt /; / ˌmæl.ə.pɔːrʃən.mənt / (AmE), / ˌmal.əˈpɔː.ʃən.mənt /; / ˌmal.əˈpɔːʃən.mənt / (BrE)
an unfair or unequal distribution of representatives, seats, or resources among electoral districts or groups

Examples

Unfair malapportionment in voting
The court found that malapportionment in the state legislature violated the constitution.
Efforts to reform malapportionment aim to ensure fairer representation for all citizens.
Many countries have laws to prevent malapportionment in their electoral systems.
The court ruled that the state's voting districts suffered from malapportionment and needed to be redrawn.
Critics argued that malapportionment of educational funding disadvantaged rural schools.
Efforts to correct malapportionment aim to ensure fair representation for all communities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: apportionment - the act of dividing and assigning representatives fairly
  • antonym: equitable distribution - the fair and equal allocation of resources or responsibilities
  • synonym: gerrymandering - manipulating electoral boundaries for political advantage
