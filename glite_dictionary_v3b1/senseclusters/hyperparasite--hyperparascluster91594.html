hyperparasite

noun
exploiter of exploiters
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərˈpærəsaɪt / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəˈpærəsaɪt / (BrE)
a person or organization that takes advantage of or profits from others who are themselves exploitative

Examples

A hyperparasite profits twice
The media called the lobbyist a hyperparasite for profiting from already corrupt officials.
Some critics argue that certain consultants act as hyperparasites in the financial industry.
During the investigation, it became clear that a hyperparasite was exploiting the fraudulent companies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: benefactor - a person who helps others, especially by giving money
  • synonym: parasite - a person or organism that lives off or exploits others
