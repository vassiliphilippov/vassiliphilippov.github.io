cousinship

noun
close relationship
7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈkʌz.ən.ʃɪp/ (AmE), /ˈkʌz.ən.ʃɪp/ (BrE)
a close relationship, resemblance, or shared origin between people, groups, or things, often used figuratively

Examples

Cousinship between languages
There is a certain cousinship between Spanish and Italian, as both languages evolved from Latin.
The two artists shared a creative cousinship that was evident in their similar styles.
Some scientists argue for a cousinship between whales and hippos based on genetic evidence.
