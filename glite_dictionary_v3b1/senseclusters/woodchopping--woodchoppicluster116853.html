woodchopping

noun
cutting wood with axe
Pronunciation: / ˈwʊdˌtʃɑpɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwʊdˌtʃɒpɪŋ / (BrE)
the act or sport of cutting wood into pieces, usually with an axe

Examples

A woodchopping contest
He spent the afternoon woodchopping to prepare firewood for winter.
The annual festival features a woodchopping competition that attracts skilled participants from across the region.
After hours of woodchopping, they had enough logs to last through the cold season.

Related Terms

  • synonym: logging - the activity of cutting down trees for timber
  • synonym: lumberjacking - the work of cutting down trees and preparing timber
