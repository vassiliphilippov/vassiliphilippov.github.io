pour

verb
supply or invest in large amounts
3.2 9.69/m
Pronunciation: / p…îr / (AmE), / p…îÀê / (BrE)
to supply or invest something, especially money or resources, in large quantities

Examples

To pour money in
After the hurricane, charitable organizations poured food and supplies into the affected region.
Investors poured resources into the startup, hoping for rapid growth.
The government poured billions of dollars into the new infrastructure project.

Synonyms

  • flood
    2.1 7.42/m
    flood fills something with an excessive, often unwanted amount that overwhelms it, while pour means to supply resources in large quantities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: invest - to put money, effort, or time into something to make a profit or achieve a result
  • antonym: withdraw - to remove or take away resources or money from a place or activity
