Fatimid

proper noun
Islamic dynasty
Pronunciation: / ˈfætɪmɪd / (AmE), / ˈfætɪmɪd / (BrE)
a member of or relating to a medieval Islamic dynasty that ruled parts of North Africa and the Middle East from the 10th to 12th centuries

Examples

The Fatimid dynasty
The Fatimid caliphate was known for its cultural and scientific achievements.
Cairo was founded as the capital of the Fatimid dynasty in the 10th century.
Many architectural landmarks in Egypt date back to the Fatimid period.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Abbasid - a member of another major medieval Islamic dynasty
  • synonym: Umayyad - a member of an earlier Islamic dynasty
