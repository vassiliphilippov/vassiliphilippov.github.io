hegemonic

adjective
dominant or controlling
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛdʒəˈmɑnɪk / (AmE), / ˌhɛɡɪˈmɒnɪk / (BrE)
having dominant control or influence over others, especially in social, political, or cultural contexts

Examples

Hegemonic power
The country exerted a hegemonic influence over its neighbors for many years.
Critics argue that the media's hegemonic role shapes public opinion in subtle ways.
The organization challenged the hegemonic structures within the industry to promote fairness.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dominant - having power and influence over others
  • antonym: subordinate - lower in rank or position; under the authority of another
