rebound

verb
recover or return to former condition
1.0
Pronunciation: / riˈbaʊnd /; / ˈriːˌbaʊnd / (AmE), / rɪˈbaʊnd /; / ˈriːˌbaʊnd / (BrE)
to return to a previous good condition or recover quickly after experiencing difficulties or decline

Examples

Companies rebound after loss
After several months of hardship, the company finally began to rebound and regain its market share.
He struggled with sadness after the breakup but managed to rebound quickly and felt hopeful again.
Many economies rebound after recessions, although the pace of recovery can vary.
After losing the first set, she managed to rebound and win the match.
The basketball hit the rim and began to rebound across the court.
He was able to rebound from his disappointment and try again with renewed energy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bounce - to spring back after hitting something
  • antonym: collapse - to fall down or fail suddenly
  • synonym: recover - to return to a normal state after trouble, illness, or setback
  • antonym: relapse - to fall back into a previous state, often worse than before; opposite of rebound in the recovery context
