costly

adjective
expensive; high-priced
1.7 6.29/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëÀêstli / (AmE), / Ààk…ístli / (BrE)
Having a high price; expensive.

Examples

A costly mistake
He bought a costly watch as a gift for his wife.
Making a mistake in this business can become extremely costly.
The repairs proved to be quite costly for the homeowners.

Synonyms

  • expensive
    1.0 83/m
    expensive simply means costing a lot of money, while costly often sounds more formal or serious in tone.
  • high-priced
    3.5 1.79/m
    high-priced describes items that are set at high cost for sale, while costly focuses on their expensive nature.
  • pricey
    4.4 4.55/m
    pricey is informal and suits everyday talk about cost, while costly feels more formal or weighty.
  • dear
    7.0 2.13/m
    dear is mainly British or old-fashioned when speaking about cost, while costly is more neutral and modern.
  • big-ticket
    5.1 0.89/m
    big-ticket describes very expensive items that cost a lot at once, while costly just means generally expensive.
