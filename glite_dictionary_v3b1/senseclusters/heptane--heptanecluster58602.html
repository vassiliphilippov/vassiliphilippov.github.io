heptane

noun
a volatile liquid hydrocarbon
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛpˌteɪn / (AmE), / ˈhɛp.teɪn / (BrE)
a colorless, volatile, highly flammable liquid hydrocarbon obtained from petroleum, used as a solvent and in determining octane ratings

Examples

Pure heptane liquid
Because heptane is highly flammable, it should be handled with care in industrial settings.
Pure heptane is often used in laboratories as a non-polar solvent.
The heptane content of gasoline helps determine its octane rating.
