imperfectly

adverb
in a faulty or incomplete way
3.2 3.1
Pronunciation: /ɪm.ˈpɜːr.fɪkt.liː/ (AmE), /ɪm.ˈpɜː.fɪkt.liː/ (BrE)
In a way that is not perfect; in a manner that has faults or is incomplete.

Examples

Instructions for assembling the furniture were imperfectly translated, leading to some confusion.
She imperfectly remembered the details of her dream upon waking.
The artist's early sketches were imperfectly executed, lacking detail and symmetry.
