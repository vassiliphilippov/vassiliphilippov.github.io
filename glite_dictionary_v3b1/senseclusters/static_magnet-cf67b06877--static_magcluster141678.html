static magnet

noun
magnet that keeps magnetism when field is removed
3.3 4.9
Pronunciation: /ˈstæt.ɪk ˈmæɡ.nət/ /; / /ˈstæt.ɪk ˈmæɡ.nɪt/ (AmE), /ˈstæt.ɪk ˈmæɡ.nət/ (BrE)
a magnet that retains its magnetic properties even after the external magnetic field is removed

Examples

A static magnet needle
The compass needle is a small static magnet that aligns with the Earth's magnetic field.
Unlike an electromagnet, a static magnet does not require electricity to function.
You can use a static magnet to hold notes on your refrigerator door.

Related Terms

  • antonym: electromagnet - a magnet created by electric current and only magnetic while current flows
  • synonym: permanent magnet - a magnet retaining its magnetism without an external field
