rollback

noun
reversion of system or database state
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊlbæk / (AmE), / ˈrəʊlbæk / (BrE)
(in computing) the process of restoring a system, database, or software environment to a previous state, typically after a failed update or error.

Examples

System rollback
After the failed update, the engineers performed a rollback to the previous stable release.
The database automatically started a rollback when the transaction detected inconsistent records during the import.
Their incident response plan requires an immediate rollback if the new configuration causes service outages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: restore - to bring back a system, data, or file to a former or original state
  • synonym: revert - to return something to a previous state or condition
