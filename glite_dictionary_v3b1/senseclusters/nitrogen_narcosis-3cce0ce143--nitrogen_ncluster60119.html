nitrogen narcosis

noun
state of confusion caused by nitrogen under pressure
8.0 6.9
Pronunciation: /ˈnaɪ.trə.dʒən nɑːr.ˈkoʊ.sɪs/ (AmE), /ˈnaɪ.trə.dʒən nɑː.ˈkəʊ.sɪs/ (BrE)
A confused or stuporous state experienced by divers caused by high levels of dissolved nitrogen in the blood when breathing air under high pressure.

Examples

Nitrogen narcosis during dives
Many experienced divers have learned techniques to avoid nitrogen narcosis during deep dives.
Safety guidelines are in place to help reduce the risk of nitrogen narcosis among professional divers.
The effects of nitrogen narcosis can be compared to feeling drunk underwater.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decompression sickness - a condition caused by dissolved gases forming bubbles inside the body, often in divers
  • synonym: rapture of the deep - another term for nitrogen narcosis experienced by divers
