childish

adjective
showing a lack of maturity

(of an adult or their behavior) showing a lack of maturity; behaving in a way more typical of a child than an adult.

Examples

Childish behavior
Despite being nearly thirty, he sometimes displays childish jealousy over small things.
His childish outburst embarrassed everyone in the meeting.
She finds it frustrating when her colleagues resort to childish name-calling during arguments.

Synonyms

  • immature
    3.5 4.45/m
    immature describes someone who lacks emotional or mental growth, while childish shows behavior like a child in actions.
  • infantile
    6.5 1.07/m
    infantile describes very childish behavior like a small child, while childish means acting in a childlike, immature way.
  • puerile
    9.1 0.58/m
    puerile means silly or trivial in a formal tone, while childish simply means behaving like a child and can be less formal.
  • juvenile
    4.2 3.40/m
    juvenile often refers to youth or immature acts in formal use, while childish focuses on an adult's childlike behavior.
  • babyish
    3.0 0.11/m
    babyish describes silly, baby-like actions such as crying or whining, while childish means behaving like a child in general.

Related Terms

  • antonym: adult - showing the qualities of a responsible grown person
  • synonym: immature - not fully developed or grown; lacking maturity
