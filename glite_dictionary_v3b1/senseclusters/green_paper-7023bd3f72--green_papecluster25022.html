green paper

noun
government's preliminary policy report for discussion
Pronunciation: / ˈɡriːn ˌpeɪpɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡriːn ˌpeɪpə / (BrE)
a preliminary report published by a government proposing ideas for discussion before a policy is formally decided

Examples

A green paper report
After reviewing responses to the green paper, the minister plans to publish a more detailed proposal.
The government released a green paper outlining possible reforms to the education system.
This green paper sets out several options for public consultation before any policy is decided.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: white paper - an authoritative report or proposal issued by a government after consultation
