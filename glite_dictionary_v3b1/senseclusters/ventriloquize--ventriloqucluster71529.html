ventriloquize

verb
to project voice elsewhere
Pronunciation: / vɛnˈtrɪləˌkwaɪz / (AmE), / vɛnˈtrɪləˌkwaɪz / (BrE)
to speak in a way that makes it seem as if the voice is coming from somewhere else, like a ventriloquist

Examples

To ventriloquize a puppet
She learned to ventriloquize for her puppet show and amazed the audience.
It takes years of practice to ventriloquize convincingly without moving your lips.
He could ventriloquize so well that people thought the voice came from the other side of the room.
She practiced for months to ventriloquize convincingly during her stage performance.
The comedian could ventriloquize so well that the audience believed the puppet was talking.
He amazed his friends by learning how to ventriloquize at the talent show.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ventriloquism - the art of making one's voice appear to come from another source
