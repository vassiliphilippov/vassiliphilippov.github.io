catacomb

noun
underground burial tunnel or chamber
Pronunciation: / ˈkæt̬.ə.koʊm / (AmE), / ˈkæt.ə.kəʊm / (BrE)
an underground cemetery consisting of tunnels and chambers with recesses for tombs, especially one used in ancient times

Examples

Ancient catacomb tunnels
Bones and urns were found preserved in the catacomb walls.
Early Christians often held their worship services in the catacombs beneath Rome.
The tour took us deep into a network of ancient catacombs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crypt - an underground room or vault beneath a church, used as a chapel or burial place
