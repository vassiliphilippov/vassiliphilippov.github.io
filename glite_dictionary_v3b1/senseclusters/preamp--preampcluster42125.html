preamp

noun
signal amplifier device
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːˌæmp / (AmE), / ˈpriːˌæmp / (BrE)
an electronic device that amplifies weak electrical signals before they are processed by other equipment or a main amplifier

Examples

Guitar preamp
The guitarist connected his electric guitar to a preamp before plugging into the main amplifier.
A high-quality preamp can make a big difference in recording clear vocals.
Many audio systems include a built-in preamp to boost weak input signals.
The guitarist plugged his instrument into a preamp to boost the signal before it reached the main amplifier.
A high-quality preamp can make a big difference in recording studio sound.
She adjusted the settings on the preamp to reduce background noise during the podcast.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: amplifier - a device that increases the power of electrical signals
  • synonym: preamplifier - an electronic amplifier that prepares a small signal for further amplification
