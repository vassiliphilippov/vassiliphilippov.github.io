throw

verb
to direct a look or gesture
1.0 2.4
Pronunciation: / θroʊ / (AmE), / θrəʊ / (BrE)
to direct or express something (such as a look, smile, or accusation) toward someone

Examples

To throw a look
He threw a smile in my direction as he left the room.
She threw a quick glance at her watch before answering.
She threw a worried look at her friend when she heard the news.
During the argument, she began to throw accusations at her colleague.
He didn't mean to throw blame at you, but he was frustrated.
It's unkind to throw insults at someone when you're upset.
