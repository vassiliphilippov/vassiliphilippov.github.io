alloy iron

noun
cast iron with added alloying elements
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈælɔɪ ˈaɪɚn / (AmE), / ˈæləʊ ˈaɪən / (BrE)
cast iron to which other elements (such as nickel, chromium, copper, or molybdenum) have been added to increase strength or improve properties such as heat resistance.

Examples

Special alloy iron
By adding nickel, the foundry produced a special alloy iron for high-temperature applications.
Modern plumbing systems often feature pipes made from alloy iron due to their corrosion resistance.
The manufacturer used alloy iron to make the engine parts more durable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cast iron - a hard, brittle form of iron used for casting
