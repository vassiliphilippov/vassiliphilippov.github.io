public intellectual

noun
intellectual contributing to public debate
Pronunciation: / ˈpʌblɪk ˌɪntəˈlɛktʃuəl / (AmE), / ˈpʌblɪk ˌɪntəˈlɛktʃuəl / (BrE)
an intellectual, usually a scholar or writer, who takes an active role in contributing ideas and critiques to public discourse or debate outside academia

Examples

A public intellectual speaks
As a public intellectual, she bridges the gap between academia and society.
Many public intellectuals use social media to share their ideas widely.
The public intellectual delivered a thought-provoking lecture on democracy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: intellectual - a person possessing a highly developed intellect
