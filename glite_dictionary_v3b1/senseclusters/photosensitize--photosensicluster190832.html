photosensitize

verb
make sensitive to light or radiation
Pronunciation: / ˌfoʊ.t̬oʊˈsɛn.sɪ.taɪz /; / ˌfoʊtoʊˈsɛnsɪtaɪz / (AmE), / ˌfəʊ.təʊˈsɛn.sɪ.taɪz /; / ˌfəʊtəʊˈsɛnsɪtaɪz / (BrE)
to make (an organism or substance) sensitive to the influence of radiant energy, especially light

Examples

To photosensitize the skin
Certain chemicals can photosensitize the skin, increasing the risk of sunburn.
Many researchers photosensitize cells to study their reactions to different wavelengths of light.
The drug was used to photosensitize the tumor, making it more responsive to laser treatment.
Certain medications can photosensitise the skin, causing a greater reaction to sunlight.
Specially treated dyes are known to photosensitise fabrics used in solar energy applications.
The researchers photosensitised the compound to enhance its effectiveness under light exposure.
