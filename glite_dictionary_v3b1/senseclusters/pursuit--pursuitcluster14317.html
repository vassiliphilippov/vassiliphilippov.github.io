pursuit

noun
the act of striving for something
3.7 14/m
Pronunciation: / p…ôrÀàsuÀêt / (AmE), / p…ôÀàsjuÀêt / (BrE)
the act of striving for or seeking to attain something, such as an ideal, goal, or object

Examples

Pursuit of success
His pursuit of happiness led him to travel the world.
In the pursuit of success, one must be willing to take risks.
She devoted years to the pursuit of scientific knowledge.

Synonyms

  • seeking
    2.9 0.16/m
    seeking is actively looking for something now, while pursuit often means a longer, ongoing effort to reach a goal.
  • striving
    3.5 1.31/m
    striving means making a strong, ongoing effort to improve, while pursuit covers both seeking and chasing a specific goal.
  • endeavor
    4.1 16/m
    endeavor is a planned attempt or effort that people try hard at, while pursuit focuses on the ongoing seeking or striving for something.
  • pursuance
    8.1 0.18/m
    pursuance is a formal word for carrying out or pushing something forward, while pursuit is more common and general.
  • quest
    4.7 19/m
    quest is a long, often adventurous search with challenges, while pursuit can be any act of striving or seeking a goal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: quest - a long search for something difficult to find or achieve
  • synonym: search - the act of looking for something carefully
