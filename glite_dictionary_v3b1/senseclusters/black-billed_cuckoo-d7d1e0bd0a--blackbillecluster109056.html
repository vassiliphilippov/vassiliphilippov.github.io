black-billed cuckoo

noun
North American cuckoo species
6.6
Pronunciation: / blæk bɪld ˈkʊku / (AmE), / blæk bɪld ˈkʊkuː / (BrE)
A North American cuckoo (Coccyzus erythropthalmus) with a slender black bill, known for building its own nest and rearing its young.

Examples

The black-billed cuckoo bird
The soft, repetitive song of the black-billed cuckoo is a familiar sound in eastern North American woodlands.
Unlike many cuckoos, the black-billed cuckoo raises its own chicks rather than parasitizing other birds' nests.
We spotted a black-billed cuckoo perched quietly in the dense willow thicket.
