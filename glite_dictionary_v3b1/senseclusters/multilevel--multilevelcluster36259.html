multilevel

adjective
having multiple hierarchical levels (organizations, marketing)
Pronunciation: / ccm8cltic8l5bv59l / (AmE), / ccm8cltic8l5bv59l / (BrE)
Having or involving several hierarchical levels; especially used for organizations or systems (for example, multilevel marketing) in which people or units are arranged in layers and may receive commissions or authority across those layers.

Examples

Multilevel marketing system
Several firms use multilevel marketing, so salespeople earn commissions from recruits and their recruits.
She joined a multilevel company that promised training and bonuses for recruiting new members.
The charity adopted a multilevel approach to fundraising, involving local, regional, and national teams.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flat - having few or no levels of management or hierarchy; opposite of hierarchical
  • synonym: hierarchical - arranged in levels of rank or authority
