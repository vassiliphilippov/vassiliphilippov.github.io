Church Father

noun
early influential Christian theologian
2.3 8.5
Pronunciation: /tʃɜːrtʃ ˈfɑː.ðər/ (AmE), /tʃɜːtʃ ˈfɑː.ðə/ (BrE)
an early Christian theologian, especially one from the 2nd to 7th centuries, whose writings helped establish and define church doctrine

Examples

The Church Fathers writings
Augustine is considered one of the greatest Church Fathers in the Western tradition.
Many of the Church Fathers wrote extensively on the nature of the Trinity.
The writings of the Church Fathers continue to influence Christian theology today.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Doctor of the Church - a title given by the Catholic Church to saints recognized for significant contributions to theology or doctrine
  • synonym: theologian - a person who studies religious faith, practice, and experience, especially the study of God and of God's relation to the world
