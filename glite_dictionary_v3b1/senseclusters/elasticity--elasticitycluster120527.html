(in computing, especially cloud computing) the ability of a system or platform to automatically increase or decrease allocated resources (such as CPU, memory, or instances) in response to changing demand.

Examples

Cloud elasticity
The platform's elasticity enables seamless automatic scaling of resources during traffic spikes.
Cloud providers advertise elasticity so customers only pay for capacity they actually use.
System architects design elasticity into services to handle sudden increases or decreases in load.

Related Terms

  • synonym: autoscaling - automatic adjustment of computing resources based on predefined rules or metrics
  • synonym: scalability - the capacity of a system to handle increased load by adding resources
