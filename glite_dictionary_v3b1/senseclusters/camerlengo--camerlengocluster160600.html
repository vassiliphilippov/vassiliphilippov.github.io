camerlengo

noun
historical household treasurer
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkæmərˈleɪŋɡoʊ / (AmE), / ˌkæməlˈleŋɡəʊ / (BrE)
a chamberlain or treasurer responsible for managing the domestic affairs or finances of a royal or noble household, especially in historical contexts

Examples

Royal camerlengo
The camerlengo was trusted with the keys to the treasury and all household accounts.
In medieval courts, the camerlengo played a vital role in organizing banquets and ceremonies.
The duke appointed a new camerlengo to oversee the estate's finances after the previous one retired.
