imitative electronic deception

noun
emission of signals imitating enemy emissions
Pronunciation: / ˌɪməˌteɪtɪv ɪˌlɛkˈtrɑnɪk dɪˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪmɪˌteɪtɪv ɪˌlɛkˈtrɒnɪk dɪˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
the deliberate emission of electromagnetic energy to mimic an enemy's signals, in order to confuse or mislead their electronic systems

Examples

Effective imitative electronic deception
Effective imitative electronic deception can lead to confusion and misdirection among opposing forces.
The military unit used imitative electronic deception to make the enemy think their radar system was operating normally.
Through imitative electronic deception, false signals appeared identical to genuine enemy transmissions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: electronic countermeasure - the use of electronic devices to disrupt enemy electronics
  • synonym: electronic jamming - deliberate transmission of signals to interfere with enemy communications
