Stations of the Cross

noun
Christian devotion representing Christ's path to crucifixion
7.5 8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈsteɪʃənz əv ðə ˈkrɔs / (AmE), / ˈsteɪʃənz əv ðə ˈkrɒs / (BrE)
(in the Roman Catholic Church) a devotion consisting of fourteen prayers and meditations, each representing an event of Jesus' journey from Pilate's house to his crucifixion, typically depicted by a series of images or carvings.

Examples

Stations of the Cross devotion
Every Friday, the congregation gathers to walk the Stations of the Cross together.
Many Christians participate in the Stations of the Cross during Lent to remember Jesus' suffering.
The church set up beautiful paintings for the Stations of the Cross along the nave.
