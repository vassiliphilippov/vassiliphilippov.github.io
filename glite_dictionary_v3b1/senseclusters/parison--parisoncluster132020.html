parison

noun
preformed molten mass
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpærɪsən / (AmE), / ˈpærɪsən / (BrE)
a mass of molten glass or plastic shaped into a preliminary form for further processing, such as blowing or molding

Examples

A parison of glass
The glassblower carefully shaped the parison before creating a vase.
In plastic bottle manufacturing, the heated parison is expanded inside a mold.
A uniform parison is essential for producing high-quality glass containers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blank - a roughly shaped piece of material for further processing
  • synonym: preform - an initial shape made before final processing
