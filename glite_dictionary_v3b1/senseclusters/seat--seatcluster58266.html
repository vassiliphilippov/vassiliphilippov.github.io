seat

noun
a space reserved for sitting
Pronunciation: / siːt / (AmE), / siːt / (BrE)
a place or space reserved for one person to sit, especially in a theater, train, airplane, etc.

Examples

A seat on the train
He reserved their seats in the front row for the concert.
Please take your seat before the train departs.
There were no empty seats left on the airplane.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bench - a long seat for multiple people
  • synonym: chair - a piece of furniture for one person to sit on, usually with a back
  • antonym: stand - to be in an upright position on the feet, opposite of sitting
