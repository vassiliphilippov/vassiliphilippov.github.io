continue

verb
do something repeatedly without stopping
1.0 327/m
Pronunciation: / k…ônÀàt…™n.ju /; / k…ônÀàt…™nju / (AmE), / k…ônÀàt…™n.juÀê /; / k…ônÀàt…™njuÀê / (BrE)
to keep doing something or to persist in an activity, state, or movement without stopping

Examples

To continue working
Despite the criticism, she continued giving her speeches with confidence.
If you continue asking the same question, you'll eventually get an answer.
The rain continued for days, causing widespread flooding in the region.
As the debate continued, the audience grew increasingly restless.
If these problems continue, we may need to reconsider our approach.
The rain continued throughout the entire weekend, ruining our plans for a picnic.
It is important to continue these environmental protections to preserve natural habitats.
The committee voted to continue the annual scholarship program for another year.
We must continue the fight for equal rights, just as our predecessors did.

Synonyms

    1.6 11/m
    keep going is informal and stresses not stopping an action, while continue is the neutral verb for carrying on activities.
  • keep on
    1.4 12/m
    keep on is casual and means doing something without stopping, while continue is a general, neutral verb.
  • go on
    1.0 76/m
    go on means to carry on an action or story, often in speech, while continue suits formal and general use.
  • carry on
    2.7 11/m
    carry on is common in British English for going on after problems, while continue is neutral and widely used.
  • carry on with
    2.6 0.98/m
    carry on with means return to or keep working on a specific task after a pause, while continue is more general for any activity.
  • get on with
    2.3 5.04/m
    get on with means resume work or make steady progress after an interruption, while continue is broader and not task-specific.
  • proceed
    3.0 43/m
    proceed is formal and often starts or moves forward after a pause or plan, while continue is neutral and fits many situations.
  • persist in
    4.1 3.17/m
    persist in means stay doing something in a determined way despite opposition, while continue is more neutral and less forceful.
  • press on
    3.9 1.33/m
    press on means keep going with effort despite obstacles, while continue is neutral and may not show struggle.
  • push on
    3.7 1.55/m
    push on means force yourself to move forward with effort and will, while continue is a general verb without that effort sense.
  • hang on
    3.3 1.23/m
    hang on means keep going or endure in hard times, often said informally, while continue is neutral and widely used.
  • hold on
    3.3 7.43/m
    hold on means keep going or wait firmly in difficult moments, often informal, while continue is neutral and widely used.
  • keep
    1.0 452/m
    keep can mean retain possession or maintain a state over time, while continue specifically means to carry on an action without stopping.
