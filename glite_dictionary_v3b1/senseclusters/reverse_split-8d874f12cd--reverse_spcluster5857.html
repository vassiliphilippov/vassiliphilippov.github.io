reverse split

noun
reduction in outstanding shares (finance)
5.6
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvɝs splɪt / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːs splɪt / (BrE)
a decrease in the number of a company's outstanding shares without altering the total value of shareholders' equity

Examples

A reverse split of shares
A reverse split does not affect the total value of a shareholder's investment.
Following the reverse split, each shareholder received one new share for every ten shares they previously held.
The company announced a reverse split to boost its share price above the minimum exchange requirement.

Related Terms

  • antonym: stock split - an increase in the number of a company's outstanding shares by dividing each share
