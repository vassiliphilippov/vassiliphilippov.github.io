plop

verb
to drop or fall with a splash-like sound
6.9
Pronunciation: / plɑp / (AmE), / plɒp / (BrE)
to drop or fall into liquid with a soft, splash-like sound

Examples

To plop ice cubes
He plopped the ice cubes into his drink, enjoying the noise they made.
She let the stone plop into the pond and watched the ripples spread.
The frog plopped from the lily pad into the water below.
A frog plopped into the still pond, leaving ripples behind.
He plopped his bag onto the floor after a long day at work.
She plopped the ice cubes into her glass and listened to them crackle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: splash - to hit or fall into liquid so that drops of it go in all directions
