horsebean

noun
thorny shrub or tree with fragrant yellow-orange flowers
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˈhɔrsˌbin / (AmE), / ˈhɔːsˌbiːn / (BrE)
a large shrub or shrubby tree (Parkinsonia aculeata) with sharp spines, pinnate leaves, and fragrant yellow-orange flowers, native to tropical America and naturalized in the southern United States, often grown as an ornamental, hedge, or emergency fodder for livestock

Examples

Horsebean shrub
During drought years, farmers sometimes use horsebean as emergency fodder for their cattle.
The rancher planted horsebean trees along the property border to create a natural hedge.
The sweet scent of the horsebean blossoms attracted bees to the garden.
