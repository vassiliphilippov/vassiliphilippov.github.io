watt-hour

noun
unit of energy (one watt for one hour)
Pronunciation: / ˈwɑt haʊɚ / (AmE), / ˈwɒt ˌhaʊə / (BrE)
A unit of energy equal to the amount of work done by a power of one watt operating for one hour.

Examples

One watt-hour unit
The battery can store up to 500 watt-hours of energy.
The device used less than one watt-hour during testing.
To compare appliances, check how many watt-hours they consume per day.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: kilowatt-hour - unit of energy equal to one thousand watt-hours
