rosette

noun
potato disease caused by Rhizoctonia
9.9 7.8
Pronunciation: / roʊˈzɛt / (AmE), / rəʊˈzɛt / (BrE)
A disease of potatoes caused by the fungus Rhizoctonia, leading to stunted growth and yellowing of leaves.

Examples

Potato rosette disease
Farmers lost a significant part of their crop when rosette spread through the fields.
Proper crop rotation can help minimize the risk of rosette in potatoes.
The agronomist identified the yellow patches on the plants as symptoms of rosette.
