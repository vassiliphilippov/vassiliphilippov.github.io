polybasic

adjective
multiple replaceable hydrogens
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑliˈbeɪsɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɒlɪˈbeɪsɪk / (BrE)
having more than one replaceable hydrogen atom or basic group per molecule, especially in acids or bases

Examples

Polybasic acid
Sulfuric acid is a polybasic acid because it can donate two protons.
Phosphoric acid is considered polybasic since it has three replaceable hydrogen atoms.
In chemistry class, we learned how to identify polybasic acids by their molecular structure.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: dibasic - having two replaceable hydrogen atoms per molecule
  • antonym: monobasic - having only one replaceable hydrogen atom per molecule
