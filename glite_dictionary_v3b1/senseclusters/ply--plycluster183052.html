ply

verb
travel a route regularly, especially by ship or vehicle
7.2 0.59/m
Pronunciation: / plaÉª / (AmE), / plaÉª / (BrE)
(of a vehicle, ship, or boat) to travel or sail regular routes, especially for commercial purposes or between fixed points.

Examples

Boats ply the river
Dozens of fishing boats ply the coastal waters during the season.
Ferries ply across the river every half hour.
The company used to ply routes between the mainland and the islands.

Synonyms

  • run
    2.7 27/m
    run is used for vehicles or services that follow a set route and schedule, while ply means sail regularly for commerce.
  • sail
    1.0 15/m
    sail describes moving by boat on water in general, while ply means to travel regular commercial routes between fixed points.

Related Terms

  • synonym: run - to travel along a fixed route regularly (as of buses, trains, etc.)
