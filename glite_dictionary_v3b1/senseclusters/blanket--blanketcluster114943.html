blanket

noun
protective layer in a nuclear reactor
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈblæŋkət / (AmE), / ˈblaŋkɪt / (BrE)
A layer of material, such as lead, surrounding and protecting the core of a nuclear reactor.

Examples

A reactor blanket
A depleted uranium blanket can be used to breed plutonium-239 in certain reactor designs.
Technicians inspected the lead blanket around the active core for any signs of damage.
The reactor's blanket absorbs neutrons to increase safety and efficiency.
