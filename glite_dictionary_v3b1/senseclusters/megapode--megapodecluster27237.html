megapode

noun
Australasian mound-building bird
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛɡəˌpoʊd / (AmE), / ˈmɛɡəpəʊd / (BrE)
A ground-dwelling bird of Australasia with large feet and short wings, known for building mounds of decaying vegetation to incubate its eggs.

Examples

A megapode bird
Several species of megapode are found in forests across Australia and Indonesia.
The megapode builds its nest by piling up vegetation, which generates heat as it decomposes.
Unlike most birds, the megapode does not incubate its eggs using body heat.
