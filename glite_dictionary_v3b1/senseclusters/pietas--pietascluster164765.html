pietas

noun
Roman sense of duty
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpiː.əˌtɑs / (AmE), / ˈpaɪ.əˌtæs / (BrE)
a Roman virtue signifying duty, loyalty, and devotion to family, country, and the gods

Examples

Roman pietas virtue
The ancient Romans considered pietas one of the highest virtues a citizen could possess.
Aeneas is often praised for his pietas in fulfilling his obligations to his family and the gods.
In Roman culture, pietas guided people to respect their parents and honor their country.

Related Terms

  • synonym: devotion - strong loyalty or dedication to someone or something
  • antonym: disloyalty - lack of loyalty or faithfulness
  • synonym: duty - a moral or legal obligation; responsibility
