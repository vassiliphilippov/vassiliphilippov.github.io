syntonic

adjective
in resonance
Pronunciation: / sɪnˈtɑnɪk / (AmE), / sɪnˈtɒnɪk / (BrE)
having the same resonant frequency or being tuned to resonance, especially in electrical circuits or musical tones

Examples

Syntonic circuits
The two circuits became syntonic after careful adjustment by the engineer.
Musicians strive to make their instruments syntonic for a harmonious performance.
When the radio was syntonic with the transmitter, the signal was clear and strong.

Related Terms

  • antonym: dissonant - not in harmony or resonance
  • synonym: resonant - having or exhibiting resonance
