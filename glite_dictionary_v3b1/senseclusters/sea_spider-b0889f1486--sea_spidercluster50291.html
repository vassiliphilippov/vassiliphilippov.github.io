sea spider

noun
marine arthropod resembling a spider
2.8 6.1
Pronunciation: /siː ˈspaɪ.dər/ (AmE), /siː ˈspaɪ.də/ (BrE)
a small marine arthropod with a slender body and long, thin legs, resembling a spider, found in oceans worldwide

Examples

A sea spider animal
A sea spider can often be mistaken for a true spider because of its long, slender legs.
Scientists study the unique anatomy of the sea spider to learn more about marine arthropods.
The diver was fascinated by the delicate movements of the sea spider on the ocean floor.
