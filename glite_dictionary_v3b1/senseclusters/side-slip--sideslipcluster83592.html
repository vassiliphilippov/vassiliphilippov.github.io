side-slip

verb
move an aircraft sideways downward in the air
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪdˌslɪp / (AmE), / ˈsaɪdˌslɪp / (BrE)
(of an aircraft) to move sideways downward through the air along an inclined lateral axis, typically as part of a maneuver to lose altitude without increasing speed.

Examples

To side-slip an aircraft
In training, students learn how to side-slip safely for better control during descent.
The pilot decided to side-slip to rapidly lose altitude before landing.
To avoid overshooting the runway, she began to side-slip the plane carefully.

Related Terms

  • synonym: slip - to move smoothly and quietly, or in aviation, to descend without losing speed
