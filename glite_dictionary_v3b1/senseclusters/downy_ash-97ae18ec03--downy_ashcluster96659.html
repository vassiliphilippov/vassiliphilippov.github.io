downy ash

noun
a North American ash tree with velvety twigs
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊni æʃ / (AmE), / ˈdaʊni æʃ / (BrE)
a small American ash tree (Fraxinus pubescens) with velvety branchlets and soft, downy undersides to its leaves

Examples

The downy ash tree
Conservation groups are working to protect native downy ash trees from invasive pests.
In the spring, the downy ash produces small, inconspicuous flowers along its velvety branches.
The downy ash is easily recognized by the soft, fuzzy texture on the underside of its leaves.
