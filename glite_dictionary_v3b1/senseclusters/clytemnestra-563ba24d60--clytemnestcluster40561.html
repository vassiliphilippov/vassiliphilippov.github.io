Clytemnestra

proper noun
wife of Agamemnon in Greek mythology
Pronunciation: / klaɪtəmˈnɛstrə / (AmE), / klaɪtəmˈnɛstrə / (BrE)
In Greek mythology, the wife of Agamemnon who arranged his murder upon his return from the Trojan War.

Examples

Clytemnestra in mythology
In classical literature, Clytemnestra is portrayed as both a wronged wife and a vengeful murderer.
Many Greek plays focus on the motivations behind Clytemnestra's decision to kill Agamemnon.
The tragedy of Clytemnestra has inspired countless authors and artists throughout history.
