bisporous

adjective
having two spores
9.8
Pronunciation: / baɪˈspɔːrəs / (AmE), / baɪˈspɔːrəs / (BrE)
producing or having two spores, especially referring to certain fungi or mushrooms

Examples

Bisporous mushroom species
The bisporous mushroom species can be identified by its two-spored basidia.
Researchers discovered a bisporous fungus while examining samples from the forest floor.
Unlike most mushrooms, this variety is bisporous and produces only two spores per basidium.

  • antonym: tetrasporous - producing or having four spores
