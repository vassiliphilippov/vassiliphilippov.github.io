headfast

noun
mooring line at a vessel's bow
7.4 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛdˌfæst / (AmE), / ˈhɛdˌfɑːst / (BrE)
A mooring line attached to the bow of a boat or ship, securing it to a wharf or dock.

Examples

The headfast line
Before leaving the ship, he checked that both the headfast and sternfast were secure.
During the storm, the headfast held firm and prevented the vessel from drifting.
The sailor tightened the headfast to keep the bow of the boat steady against the dock.
