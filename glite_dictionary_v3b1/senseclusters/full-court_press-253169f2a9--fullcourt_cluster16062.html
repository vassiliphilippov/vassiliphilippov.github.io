full-court press

noun
intense, aggressive effort or tactic
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌfʊlˈkɔrt ˌprɛs / (AmE), / ˌfʊlˈkɔːt ˌprɛs / (BrE)
an intense and aggressive effort to achieve a goal, originally a basketball tactic but often used figuratively

Examples

A full-court press campaign
The campaign launched a full-court press to win over undecided voters before the election.
The senator's office initiated a full-court press to gather support for the new bill.
With the deadline approaching, the team mounted a full-court press to complete the project on time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: all-out - involving all available resources or energy
  • synonym: blitz - a sudden, energetic effort, especially in attack or campaign
