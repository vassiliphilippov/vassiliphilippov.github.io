Shortia galacifolia

noun
plant with pink-tinged white flowers and glossy green leaves
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɔrtiə ˌɡæləsɪˈfoʊliə / (AmE), / ˈʃɔːtɪə ˌɡæləsɪˈfəʊlɪə / (BrE)
a plant native to the southeastern United States, known for its solitary white, pink-tinged funnel-shaped flowers and large glossy green leaves that turn bronze-red in fall
