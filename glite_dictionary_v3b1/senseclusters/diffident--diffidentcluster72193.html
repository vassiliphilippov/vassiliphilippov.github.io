diffident

adjective
shy; modestly reserved
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪfɪdənt / (AmE), / ˈdɪfɪdənt / (BrE)
shy and lacking in self-confidence; not assertive or reserved in manner

Examples

A diffident person
Although he had valuable insights, his diffident manner prevented him from speaking up at the meeting.
Despite his diffident nature, he eventually took charge of the project when needed.
She answered the interviewer’s questions in a diffident voice, rarely making eye contact.
Although she had good ideas, her diffident nature kept her from speaking up in meetings.
He gave a diffident answer, barely meeting anyone’s gaze.
Jason appeared diffident at the interview, unsure of his qualifications.

Related Terms

  • antonym: confident - having strong belief or full assurance; self-assured
  • synonym: shy - nervous or timid in the company of others
  • synonym: timid - easily frightened; showing a lack of courage or confidence
