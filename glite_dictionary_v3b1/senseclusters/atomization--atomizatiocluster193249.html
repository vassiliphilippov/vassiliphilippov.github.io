atomization

noun
breaking into atoms; annihilation
Pronunciation: / ˌæɾəmɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌatəməˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
The process of annihilating or destroying something by breaking it down into atoms or tiny particles.

Examples

Atomization by explosion
Nuclear explosions produce atomization effects, dispersing matter into its smallest components.
Some industrial processes aim for total atomization to create fine powders or sprays.
The atomization of the material resulted in its complete destruction at the molecular level.

Related Terms

  • synonym: annihilation - complete destruction or obliteration
