dominionism

noun
theological doctrine of rule
9.9
Pronunciation: / dəˈmɪnjənɪzəm / (AmE), / dəˈmɪnjənɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief or doctrine that humans have a God-given right to rule over the earth and its creatures

Examples

Dominionism over nature
Some religious groups promote dominionism as a justification for human control over nature.
Critics argue that dominionism can lead to environmental harm if taken to extremes.
The debate over dominionism influences how people view humanity's relationship with the planet.

Related Terms

  • antonym: stewardship - the responsible management and care of something, especially the environment
