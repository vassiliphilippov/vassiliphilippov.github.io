nosewheel

noun
front wheel of an airplane's landing gear
3.9 5.7
Pronunciation: /ˈnoʊz.wiːl/ (AmE), /ˈnəʊz.wiːl/ (BrE)
a wheel positioned under the front (nose) of an aircraft, forming part of the landing gear and supporting the plane during ground operations

Examples

The nosewheel touches down
After landing, the nosewheel touched down gently on the runway.
The mechanic inspected the nosewheel for signs of wear and tear.
The pilot reported a problem with the nosewheel just before takeoff.
