predecease

verb
die before (someone else)
8.5
Pronunciation: / priːdəˈsiːs / (AmE), / ˌpriːdɪˈsiːs / (BrE)
to die before (another person, especially a relative)

Examples

To predecease a relative
He predeceased all his siblings, leaving his estate to distant relatives.
If a beneficiary predeceases the policyholder, the payout may change.
It was a shock to the family when their mother predeceased their father.

Related Terms

  • antonym: survive - to live longer than another
