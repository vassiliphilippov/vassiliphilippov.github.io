sudden desire or urge

Examples

An impulse to buy
Buying the expensive watch was an impulse he later regretted.
He resisted the impulse to check his phone during the meeting.
On an impulse, she took the day off and went to the beach.
His sudden impulse to call his old friend surprised even himself.
Many people feel the impulse to help others in times of crisis.
She acted on an impulse and bought the painting without thinking.

Synonyms

  • urge
    3.2 13/m
    urge is a strong, steady desire to act, while impulse is a sudden push that often leads to quick, unplanned action.
  • whim
    4.2 6.35/m
    whim is a light, sudden idea or wish, while impulse feels stronger and more likely to make someone act quickly.
  • caprice
    8.5 0.58/m
    caprice is an odd, sudden change in mood or behavior, while impulse is a quick urge that makes someone act.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inhibition - a feeling that restrains impulse and prevents action
  • synonym: urge - a strong desire or impulse
