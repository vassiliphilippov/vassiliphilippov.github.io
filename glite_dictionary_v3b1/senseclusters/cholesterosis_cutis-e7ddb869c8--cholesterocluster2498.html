cholesterosis cutis

noun
skin condition with widespread xanthomas
9.9
Pronunciation: / koʊˌlɛstəˈroʊsɪs ˈkjuːtɪs / (AmE), / kəˌlɛstəˈrəʊsɪs ˈkjuːtɪs / (BrE)
A medical condition marked by widespread xanthomas, especially on the elbows and knees, often associated with disorders of lipid metabolism.

Examples

Cholesterosis cutis skin bumps
People with uncontrolled cholesterol levels may develop cholesterosis cutis, often noticed as yellowish skin bumps.
The dermatologist identified cholesterosis cutis on the patient's elbows and recommended further tests for lipid disorders.
Treatment for cholesterosis cutis involves managing the underlying lipid metabolism disorder.

Related Terms

  • synonym: xanthoma - fatty skin growths linked to high cholesterol
