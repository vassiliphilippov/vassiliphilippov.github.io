trading post

noun
store for trade in remote areas
Pronunciation: / ˈtreɪdɪŋ poʊst / (AmE), / ˈtreɪdɪŋ pəʊst / (BrE)
a store or settlement where goods are exchanged, especially in a remote or sparsely populated area, often serving as a center for trade.

Examples

A trading post store
In the 1800s, the trading post was the heart of many frontier settlements.
Local farmers brought their goods to the trading post to exchange for supplies.
The explorers stopped at the trading post to resupply before continuing their journey.

Related Terms

  • synonym: market - an area or place where commercial dealings are conducted
  • synonym: store - a place where goods are sold
