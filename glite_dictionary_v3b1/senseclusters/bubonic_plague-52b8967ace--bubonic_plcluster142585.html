bubonic plague

noun
serious infectious disease marked by swellings (buboes)
6.7 0.31/m
Pronunciation: / bjuÀàb…ën…™k Ààple…™…° / (AmE), / bjuÀêÀàb…ín…™k Ààple…™…° / (BrE)
a serious infectious disease caused by the bacterium Yersinia pestis, characterized by fever, chills, prostration, delirium, and swollen lymph nodes (buboes); historically responsible for multiple epidemics, including the Black Death.

Examples

The bubonic plague outbreak
Doctors in protective clothing cared for victims of the bubonic plague in isolated hospitals.
The bubonic plague causes painful swellings known as buboes, often in the groin or armpit.
The outbreak of the bubonic plague in the 14th century killed millions across Europe.

Synonyms

  • pestis bubonica
    9.9 0.00/m
    pestis bubonica is the scientific Latin name used in medical texts, while bubonic plague is the common English name for the same illness.
  • glandular plague
    9.9 0.00/m
    glandular plague describes swollen lymph glands and is a descriptive English name, while bubonic plague is the widely used medical term.
