kainogenesis

noun
emergence of new features in embryonic development
9.2
Pronunciation: / ˌkaɪnoʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌkaɪnəʊˈdʒɛnɪsɪs / (BrE)
The development of features or structures during embryonic growth that did not exist in earlier evolutionary forms of the species.

Examples

Kainogenesis in embryos
Some embryologists debate the frequency of kainogenesis in vertebrate development.
The concept of kainogenesis challenges the idea that all embryonic features are purely ancestral.
The evolution of the placenta in mammals is a classic example of kainogenesis.
