call the shots

verb
be in control; make key decisions
Pronunciation: / kɔl ðə ʃɑts / (AmE), / kɔːl ðə ʃɒts / (BrE)
to be in control or make the important decisions in a situation

Examples

She calls the shots
As team captain, she always calls the shots during important matches.
He likes to call the shots when it comes to family vacations.
It's not my job to call the shots here, so I let my manager decide.

Related Terms

  • synonym: be in charge - to have control or responsibility over something
  • antonym: follow orders - to do what one is told by someone in a higher position
  • synonym: run the show - to be the person in control of an activity or organization
