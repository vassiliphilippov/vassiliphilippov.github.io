Ybit

noun
unit of digital information (10^24 bits)
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪ.bɪt / (AmE), / ˈwɪb.ɪt / (BrE)
A unit of digital information equal to 1,000 zettabits or 10^24 bits.

Examples

One Ybit unit
A single Ybit represents an unimaginably vast amount of digital information.
Modern data centers are planning for storage capacities in the Ybit range.
The new supercomputer can theoretically process one Ybit of data per year.
