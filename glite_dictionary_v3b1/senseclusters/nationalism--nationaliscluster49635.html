nationalism

noun
belief in national superiority
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈnæʃənəlɪzəm /; / ˈnæʃənəˌlɪzəm /; / ˈnæʃ.ə.nəˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈnæʃənəlɪzəm /; / ˈnaʃənəlɪzəm /; / ˈnæʃ(ə)nəlɪz(ə)m /; / ˈnæʃ.ən.əl.ɪz.əm / (BrE)
the belief in and strong loyalty to one's own nation, often including the desire for independence and putting national interests above those of other countries

Examples

Nationalism and pride
Nationalism often shapes a country's policies and attitudes toward other nations.
His speech was full of nationalism and calls for unity.
The rise of nationalism in the early 20th century contributed to global conflicts.
Many leaders promoted nationalism in order to unite their countries and assert independence from colonial powers.
Rising nationalism often resulted in countries prioritizing their interests over global cooperation.
The doctrine of nationalism opposes the idea of relying on international organizations for decision-making.
The rise of nationalism played a key role in the country's struggle for freedom.
Throughout history, nationalism has inspired movements for independence around the world.
Waves of nationalism swept through the population as they sought to end colonial rule.

Related Terms

  • antonym: colonialism - policy or practice of acquiring control over another country
  • synonym: independence - freedom from control by another country or authority
  • antonym: internationalism - advocacy of cooperation among nations
  • degree lower: patriotism - love of one's country
