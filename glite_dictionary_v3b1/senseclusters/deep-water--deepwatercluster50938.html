deep-water

adjective
involving waters of great depth
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈdiːp ˌwɑtər / (AmE), / ˈdiːp ˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
Located in or involving waters of great depth, especially referring to activities, features, or environments far below the surface of the sea.

Examples

Deep-water creatures
Scientists are studying the unique creatures found in deep-water environments.
The country relies on its deep-water oil fields for much of its energy supply.
The harbor was built to accommodate deep-water vessels.
