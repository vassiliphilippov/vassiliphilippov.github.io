essential tremor

noun
neurological disorder causing rhythmic shaking
6.3 5.1
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈsen.ʃəl ˈtre.mər/ /; / /ə.ˈsen.ʃəl ˈtre.mər/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈsen.ʃəl ˈtre.mə/ /; / /ə.ˈsen.ʃəl ˈtre.mə/ (BrE)
a neurological disorder causing rhythmic shaking, usually of the hands and head, without a known cause, especially in older adults

Examples

Essential tremor symptoms
Although essential tremor is not life-threatening, it can affect daily activities like writing or drinking.
Her doctor diagnosed her with essential tremor after she noticed persistent shaking in her hands.
Many people with essential tremor find that stress or fatigue makes their symptoms worse.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Parkinson's disease - a progressive neurological disorder marked by tremor, stiffness, and slow movement
