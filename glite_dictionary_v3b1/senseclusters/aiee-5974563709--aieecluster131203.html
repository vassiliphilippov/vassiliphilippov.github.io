AIEE

proper noun
US engineering society
Pronunciation: / ˌeɪ aɪ iː ˈiː / (AmE), / ˌeɪ aɪ iː ˈiː / (BrE)
the American Institute of Electrical Engineers, a former professional organization for electrical engineers in the United States, active from 1884 to 1963

Examples

The AIEE organization
The AIEE played a key role in the development of electrical engineering standards in the early 20th century.
Many prominent inventors and engineers were members of the AIEE before it merged with the IRE.
Historical documents from the AIEE provide insight into the evolution of electrical technology in America.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: IEEE - Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers, the organization formed from the merger of AIEE and IRE
