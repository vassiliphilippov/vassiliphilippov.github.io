vertical flute

noun
vertical wind instrument with finger holes
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈvɝtɪkəl fluːt / (AmE), / ˈvɜːtɪkəl fluːt / (BrE)
A tubular wind instrument, held vertically, with eight finger holes and a fipple mouthpiece.

Examples

A vertical flute instrument
Children often learn to play the vertical flute before transitioning to other woodwind instruments.
She prefers the sound of the vertical flute to that of the transverse flute.
The musician played a haunting melody on his vertical flute during the performance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: recorder - a woodwind instrument with a similar design, held vertically and played with finger holes
  • antonym: transverse flute - a flute held horizontally and played by blowing across a hole
