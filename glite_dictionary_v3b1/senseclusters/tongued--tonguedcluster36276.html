tongued

adjective
used in compounds – manner of speaking
8.3
Pronunciation: / tʌŋd / (AmE), / tʌŋd / (BrE)
(used in combination) having a specified manner of speaking, often as part of a compound adjective.

Examples

A silver-tongued speaker
He is often described as silver-tongued because of his persuasive speeches.
Her sharp-tongued remarks offended many people at the meeting.
The diplomat, known for his smooth-tongued approach, was able to negotiate a difficult peace deal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: golden-tongued - speaking in an eloquent or persuasive manner
  • synonym: sharp-tongued - speaking in a harsh or critical manner
