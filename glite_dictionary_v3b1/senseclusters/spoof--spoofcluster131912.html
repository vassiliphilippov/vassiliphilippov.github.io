spoof

verb
to parody or make fun of
6.3 0.30/m
Pronunciation: / spuf / (AmE), / spuÀêf / (BrE)
to imitate or represent someone or something in a humorous or satirical way, often as a parody

Examples

To spoof a show
For April Fool's Day, the newspaper spoofed a serious news article with a satirical twist.
Many websites enjoy spoofing current events to entertain their readers.
The comedy troupe spoofed popular movies during their live performance.

Synonyms

  • parody
    5.3 1.60/m
    parody imitates an artist's style for comic effect, while spoof often copies a work to make fun.
  • take off
    4.2 0.58/m
    take off imitates someone's voice or manner in a funny way, while spoof can imitate whole works satirically.
  • burlesque
    8.3 0.01/m
    burlesque imitates and exaggerates for comic, often theatrical effect, while spoof is wider parody of people or works.
  • mimic
    3.1 6.75/m
    mimic copies a person's manner or voice to entertain or mock, while spoof usually parodies a whole person or style.
  • caricature
    6.6 0.73/m
    caricature shows exaggerated features in drawing or speech for laughs, while spoof is broader imitation or parody of subjects.
  • impersonate
    7.2 1.89/m
    impersonate means act like someone, copying voice or look for show or trick, while spoof focuses on comic imitation.
  • satirize
    7.2 1.06/m
    satirize uses irony and criticism to attack faults, while spoof often aims mainly for light, playful imitation or parody.
  • mock
    4.3 2.34/m
    mock imitates something to appear real, often for practice or testing, while spoof is a humorous, satirical imitation to make fun.

Related Terms

  • synonym: imitate - to copy the manner or style of someone or something
  • synonym: parody - to imitate someone or something humorously
  • synonym: satirize - to use satire to criticize or mock
