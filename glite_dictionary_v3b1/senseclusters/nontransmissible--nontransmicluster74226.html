Examples

Nontransmissible disease
It is important to distinguish between nontransmissible and infectious diseases when developing health policies.
One of the main focuses of public health is the prevention of nontransmissible diseases such as diabetes.
Unlike the common cold, heart disease is a nontransmissible condition.
Doctors reassured the public that the condition is nontransmissible and poses no infectious risk.
Genetic disorders can be classified as nontransmissible if they cannot be passed down to offspring.
Some traits are nontransmissible, meaning they are acquired, not inherited.

Synonyms

  • noncontagious
    4.4 0.01/m
    noncontagious is used for diseases that do not spread between people, while nontransmissible is a broader term for any non-spreading trait.
  • noncommunicable
    6.3 0.05/m
    noncommunicable is a formal public health label for diseases not spread between people, while nontransmissible names any non-spreading condition.
  • noninfectious
    4.6 0.04/m
    noninfectious means not caused by infection and not passed by germs, while nontransmissible covers anything that cannot be passed on.
  • uninfective
    6.9 0.01/m
    uninfective is rare and means unable to cause infection in others, while nontransmissible refers broadly to anything that cannot be passed on.
