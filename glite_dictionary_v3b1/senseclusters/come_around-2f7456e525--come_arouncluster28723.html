come around

verb
to occur or happen again regularly
Pronunciation: / kʌm əˈraʊnd / (AmE), / kʌm əˈraʊnd / (BrE)
(of a regular event or time) to occur or happen again, especially as part of a regular cycle.

Examples

Holidays come around
My birthday always seems to come around faster each year.
Once tax season comes around, everyone becomes more concerned about their finances.
The town festival comes around every summer in July.

Related Terms

  • synonym: recur - to happen again, especially at intervals
