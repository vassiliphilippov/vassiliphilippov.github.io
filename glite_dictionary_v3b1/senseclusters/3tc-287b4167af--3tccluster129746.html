3TC

noun
antiviral drug for HIV/AIDS treatment
Pronunciation: / θri ti si / (AmE), / θriː tiː siː / (BrE)
a drug used as a nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor, often in combination with other medications to treat HIV and AIDS

Examples

3TC drug treatment
Clinical studies show that 3TC can significantly reduce the viral load in patients.
Resistance to 3TC may develop if taken as a single agent, so it is used with other medications.
The doctor prescribed 3TC as part of a combination therapy for the patient with HIV.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lamivudine - an antiviral medication used to treat HIV and hepatitis B, also known as 3TC
