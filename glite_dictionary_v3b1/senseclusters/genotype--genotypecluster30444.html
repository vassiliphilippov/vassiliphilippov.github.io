genotype

verb
to analyze genes
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒiːnəˌtaɪp / (AmE), / ˈdʒiːnəˌtaɪp / (BrE)
to analyze the genetic makeup of an organism or sample, usually to identify specific genes or traits

Examples

To genotype a sample
Scientists genotype blood samples to detect inherited diseases in newborns.
The laboratory genotyped hundreds of plants to find those with drought resistance.
Doctors may genotype a virus to determine the best treatment for a patient.

Related Terms

  • antonym: phenotype - the observable characteristics of an organism
  • synonym: sequence - to determine the order of nucleotides in DNA
