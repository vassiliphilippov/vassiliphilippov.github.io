chelicerous

adjective
having chelicerae (specialized mouthparts)
Pronunciation: / kəˈlɪsərəs / (AmE), / kɪˈlɪsərəs / (BrE)
(of an animal) having chelicerae, the specialized mouthparts found in arachnids such as spiders and scorpions.

Examples

Chelicerous arachnid mouthparts
A spider is classified as chelicerous because it possesses specialized feeding appendages.
Most arachnids are chelicerous creatures, using their chelicerae to grasp and manipulate food.
Researchers studied the chelicerous mouthparts of scorpions to understand their feeding habits.

Related Terms
