overbalance

verb
cause to lose balance or become unsteady
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈbæləns / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈbæləns / (BrE)
to cause to lose balance or to become unsteady; to upset the equilibrium of something, physically or metaphorically

Examples

To overbalance the budget
Careless spending can easily overbalance the household budget.
He leaned too far forward and began to overbalance on the stool.
The sudden weight shift threatened to overbalance the ladder.

Related Terms
