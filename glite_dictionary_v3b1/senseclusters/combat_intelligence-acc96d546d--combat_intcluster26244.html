combat intelligence

noun
military intelligence for tactical planning
2.7
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmˌbæt ɪnˈtɛlədʒəns / (AmE), / ˈkɒmbæt ɪnˈtelɪdʒəns / (BrE)
information about enemy forces or terrain that is necessary for planning and carrying out tactical military operations.

Examples

Combat intelligence gathering
Effective use of combat intelligence can determine the outcome of a military engagement.
The commander relied heavily on combat intelligence to plan the surprise attack.
They collected combat intelligence from scouts and aerial surveillance.
