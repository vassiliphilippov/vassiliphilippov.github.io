semilog

noun
log-linear graph
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛmiˌlɔg / (AmE), / ˈsɛmiˌlɒg / (BrE)
a graph or plot in which one axis is on a logarithmic scale and the other is on a linear scale

Examples

Semilog plot
The scientist plotted the data on a semilog to better visualize the exponential growth.
A semilog is often used in biology to display population changes over time.
By using a semilog, the engineer could easily compare values that spanned several orders of magnitude.

  • degree higher: log-log - a graph with both axes on logarithmic scales
  • synonym: semilogarithmic - having one axis on a logarithmic scale and the other linear
