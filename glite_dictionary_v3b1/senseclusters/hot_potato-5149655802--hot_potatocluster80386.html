hot potato

noun
controversial or difficult situation
5.9
Pronunciation: / hɑt pəˈteɪɾoʊ / (AmE), / hɒt pəˈteɪtəʊ / (BrE)
a difficult or controversial issue that people avoid dealing with or try to pass on to others

Examples

A hot potato issue
She handled the hot potato of staff layoffs with surprising tact.
The issue of tax reform has become a real hot potato in Congress.
Whenever funding for the arts comes up, it turns into a political hot potato.
Her controversial comment turned into a hot potato at the press conference.
Management quickly passed the hot potato of budget cuts to the finance department.
The new tax plan became a hot potato in Parliament, with no one eager to discuss it.

  • antonym: open-and-shut case - a case or situation that is easy to decide or solve
