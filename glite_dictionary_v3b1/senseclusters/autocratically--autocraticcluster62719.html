autocratically

adverb
in an autocratic, absolute manner
Pronunciation: / ˌɔt̬əˈkrætɪkli /; / ˌɔtəˈkrætɪkli / (AmE), / ˌɔːtəˈkrætɪkli / (BrE)
in a way that uses absolute power or authority, like an autocrat or dictator, without considering others' opinions

Examples

To autocratically rule
Decisions made autocratically often ignore the needs and voices of the people.
Some leaders have governed their countries autocratically throughout history.
The manager ran the department autocratically, rarely listening to staff feedback.
She autocratically dismissed any criticism from her team during the meeting.
The CEO managed the company autocratically, making all the key decisions without input from others.
The board members complained that the chairperson behaved autocratically when allocating resources.

Related Terms

  • antonym: democratically - in a democratic manner
  • synonym: dictatorially - in a manner characteristic of a dictator
