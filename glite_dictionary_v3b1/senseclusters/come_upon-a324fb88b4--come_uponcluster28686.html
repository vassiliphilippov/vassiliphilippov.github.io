come upon

verb
take possession, especially by inheritance

(formal) to take possession of (property, assets, or rights), especially as an inheritance or through legal means.

Examples

To come upon an inheritance
After his uncle passed away, he came upon the family estate and its responsibilities.
She was surprised to come upon a small fortune when the will was read.
The lawyer explained how the heir would officially come upon all the property.

Synonyms

  • come into
    3.9 4/m
    come into means to receive money or property, often by chance or inheritance, while come upon is a formal legal way to take possession.
  • inherit
    3.6 11/m
    inherit means to receive money or property from someone after death, while come upon suggests a formal legal transfer.
  • enter upon
    6.8 0.1/m
    enter upon is to take formal possession of land or property, while come upon focuses on legal possession or inheritance.
