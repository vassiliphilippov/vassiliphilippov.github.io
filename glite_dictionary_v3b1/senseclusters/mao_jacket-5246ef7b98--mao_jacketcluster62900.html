Mao jacket

noun
Chinese jacket with high collar
2.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmaʊ ˌʤækɪt / (AmE), / ˈmaʊ ˌʤækɪt / (BrE)
A lightweight, hip-length jacket with a high, stand-up collar, often associated with Mao Zedong and commonly worn in China during his regime.

Examples

The Mao jacket style
Many leaders in the 1960s were photographed wearing the iconic Mao jacket.
She wore a navy Mao jacket to the reception, giving her outfit a distinctive look.
The Mao jacket became a symbol of the era during Mao Zedong's leadership in China.
