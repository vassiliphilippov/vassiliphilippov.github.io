new evangelization

noun
renewed Catholic effort to revitalize faith
7.9
Pronunciation: / nu ˌiːvæn.dʒə.ləˈzeɪ.ʃən /; / nu iˌvæn.dʒə.ləˈzeɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / njuː ɪˌvan.dʒə.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən /; / njuː iˌvan.dʒə.laɪˈzeɪ.ʃən / (BrE)
the renewed effort by the Catholic Church, especially promoted by Pope John Paul II, to revitalize faith in regions where Christianity is traditionally established but declining

Examples

The new evangelization effort
A key part of the new evangelization is using modern media to spread the Christian message.
Many parishes in Europe have launched new evangelization programs to reconnect with lapsed members.
The new evangelization is a central theme in modern Catholic teaching and outreach.
Many church leaders have organised conferences to discuss strategies for the new evangelisation.
Pope John Paul II was a key proponent of the new evangelisation in the late twentieth century.
The new evangelisation aims to rekindle faith among Catholics in traditionally Christian countries.

  • synonym: evangelization - the act of preaching the Christian gospel to convert others
