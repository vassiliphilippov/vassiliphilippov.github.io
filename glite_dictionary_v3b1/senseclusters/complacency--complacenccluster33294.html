complacency

noun
self-satisfaction; smugness
Pronunciation: / kəmˈpleɪsənsi / (AmE), / kəmˈpleɪsənsi / (BrE)
A feeling of uncritical satisfaction with oneself or one's achievements, often leading to a lack of awareness of potential dangers or deficiencies.

Examples

Her complacency worried colleagues
Her complacency in the face of obvious problems worried her colleagues.
His complacency about his skills made him overlook the need for further training.
Years of economic growth led to complacency among investors, who ignored the warning signs.

Related Terms

  • antonym: concern - worry or anxiety, opposite of complacency
  • synonym: self-satisfaction - a feeling of satisfaction with oneself
  • synonym: smugness - excessive pride in oneself
