fossa

noun
anatomical depression or hollow
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑsə / (AmE), / ˈfɒsə / (BrE)
An anatomical depression or hollow in a surface, especially in bones or organs.

Examples

Cranial fossa
Abnormalities may be detected in the cranial fossa on a brain scan.
During surgery, the doctor carefully examined the glenoid fossa of the shoulder joint.
The temporal fossa is an important area of the skull above the cheekbone.
