Baptisia lactea

noun
a plant species with white racemes, US native
Pronunciation: / bæpˈtɪʃiə ˈlæk.ti.ə / (AmE), / bapˈtɪsɪə ˈlak.tɪə / (BrE)
A species of wild indigo, an erect or spreading herb with racemes of creamy white flowers, native to the eastern United States.
