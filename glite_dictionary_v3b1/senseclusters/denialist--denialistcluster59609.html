denialist

adjective
rejecting established facts
Pronunciation: / dɪˈnaɪəlɪst / (AmE), / dɪˈnaɪəlɪst / (BrE)
refusing to accept or acknowledge established facts or evidence, especially in public debate or discussion

Examples

Denialist views
The senator's denialist stance on climate change drew criticism from scientists.
Many experts warn that denialist arguments can slow progress on important health issues.
The documentary exposed the denialist tactics used to spread misinformation about vaccines.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accepting - willing to believe or agree with established facts
  • synonym: skeptical - doubting or questioning accepted ideas
