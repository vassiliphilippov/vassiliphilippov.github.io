shiplap

noun
overlapping wooden boards
Pronunciation: / ˈʃɪpˌlæp / (AmE), / ˈʃɪpˌlæp / (BrE)
a type of wooden board with overlapping edges, commonly used for walls or siding in buildings

Examples

Wooden shiplap boards
The old barn was built with sturdy shiplap to protect it from the weather.
They decided to cover the kitchen walls with white shiplap for a modern farmhouse look.
After removing the wallpaper, they discovered original shiplap underneath.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clapboard - a long, thin, flat piece of wood used for covering the outside of buildings
  • antonym: drywall - a panel made of gypsum plaster used for interior walls
