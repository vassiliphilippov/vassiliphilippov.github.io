MMF

noun
multimode optical fiber
Pronunciation: / ɛm ɛm ɛf / (AmE), / ɛm ɛm ɛf / (BrE)
an abbreviation for multimode fiber, an optical fiber that carries multiple light signals for short-distance data transmission

Examples

MMF cable
The network upgrade required replacing all the old cables with MMF to support higher speeds.
Technicians installed MMF in the building to connect the server rooms efficiently.
For short-distance connections, MMF is often preferred over single-mode fiber due to lower cost.

Related Terms

  • antonym: single-mode fiber - an optical fiber that carries a single light mode for long-distance transmission
