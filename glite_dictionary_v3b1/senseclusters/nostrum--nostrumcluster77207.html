nostrum

noun
ineffective or secret remedy; cure-all
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑstrəm /; / ˈnɑːstrəm / (AmE), / ˈnɒstrəm / (BrE)
a medicine or remedy, especially one that is not effective or is sold with false claims

Examples

A nostrum medicine
During the 19th century, traveling salesmen often sold a nostrum with dubious medical benefits.
Many consumers wasted money on the nostrum that claimed to cure all ailments.
Some politicians offer a simple nostrum for complex social issues, but such solutions rarely work.
He became wealthy by marketing his signature nostrum, despite lacking any medical training.
Many patent medicines in the 19th century turned out to be nothing more than a nostrum.
The company sold a nostrum promising to cure everything from headaches to heart disease.
