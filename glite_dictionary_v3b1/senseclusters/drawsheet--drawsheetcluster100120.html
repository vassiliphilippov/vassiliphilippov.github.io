drawsheet

noun
sheet for moving patients
5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈdrɑː.ʃiːt/ /; / /ˈdrɔː.ʃiːt/ (AmE), /ˈdrɔː.ʃiːt/ (BrE)
a small sheet placed on a bed under a patient, used to help move or lift the patient or to protect the mattress

Examples

A drawsheet on bed
The nurse placed a drawsheet under the patient to make repositioning easier.
Always check that the drawsheet is smooth to prevent discomfort or skin problems.

The nurse carefully placed a clean drawsheet under the patient to keep the bed dry.
Hospitals often use a disposable drawsheet to make changing linens easier and more hygienic.
After the spill, they replaced the soiled drawsheet with a fresh one.
