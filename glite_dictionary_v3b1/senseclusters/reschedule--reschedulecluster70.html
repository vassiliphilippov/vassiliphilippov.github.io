reschedule

verb
to change the time of an event
Pronunciation: / riˈskɛdʒul / (AmE), / riˈʃɛdjuːl / (BrE)
to change the time of a planned event to a later or different time

Examples

To reschedule a meeting
Can you reschedule my haircut appointment for tomorrow afternoon?
Due to a conflict, we had to reschedule our meeting for next week.
The airline rescheduled the flight because of severe weather.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cancel - to decide a planned event will not take place
  • synonym: postpone - to delay something to a later time
