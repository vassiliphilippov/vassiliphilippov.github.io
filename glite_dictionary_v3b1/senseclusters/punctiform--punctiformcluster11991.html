punctiform

adjective
made of points
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈpʌŋk.tɪ.fɔːrm/ /; / /ˈpʌŋk.tə.fɔːrm/ (AmE), /ˈpʌŋk.tɪ.fɔːm/ /; / /ˈpʌŋk.tə.fɔːm/ (BrE)
consisting of or composed of points, having no extension, area, or interior, especially in mathematics or geometry

Examples

Punctiform set in math
In advanced geometry, a punctiform set contains only isolated points and no lines or surfaces.
The scientist described the distribution of stars as nearly punctiform in the vastness of space.
A punctiform object has no measurable length, width, or height, existing only as a point.

Related Terms

  • antonym: continuous - forming an unbroken whole without gaps or points
  • synonym: zero-dimensional - having no length, area, or volume; existing as a point
