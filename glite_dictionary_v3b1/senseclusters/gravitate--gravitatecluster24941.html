gravitate

verb
be attracted or drawn toward something
Pronunciation: / ˈɡrævɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈɡravɪteɪt / (BrE)
to be attracted or drawn to a person, place, or thing, usually because of interest or a natural tendency (figurative meaning)

Examples

People gravitate to cities
Many people gravitate to big cities in search of new opportunities.
She gravitated naturally to leadership roles throughout her career.
Young musicians often gravitate towards jazz for its creative freedom.
Many students naturally gravitate toward courses that interest them the most.
Our discussions always gravitated back to memories of our childhood days.
Over time, artists often gravitate to cities known for their vibrant creative communities.

Related Terms

  • synonym: attract - to draw by appeal or interest
  • antonym: repel - to drive or push away
