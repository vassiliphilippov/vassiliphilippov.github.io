checkrow

verb
to plant seeds in a patterned grid of rows
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɛkˌroʊ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɛkˌrəʊ / (BrE)
(of farmland) to plant seeds in a grid of equidistant rows, typically to allow for easier cultivation and weeding.

Examples

To checkrow cornfields
Farmers used to checkrow the cornfields to make cultivation easier.
Newer machinery has largely replaced the need to checkrow fields, but the method persists in some areas.
They checkrowed the crops last spring according to the traditional grid pattern.
