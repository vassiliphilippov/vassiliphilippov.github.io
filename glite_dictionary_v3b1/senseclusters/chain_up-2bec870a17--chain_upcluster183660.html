chain up

verb
fasten or restrain with chains
1.0
Pronunciation: / tʃeɪn ʌp / (AmE), / tʃeɪn ʌp / (BrE)
to fasten or restrain someone or something with chains

Examples

To chain up gates
During storms, farmers would chain up their gates to keep them from blowing open.
He didn't want to chain up the dog all night, but he had no choice.
The guards had to chain up the dangerous prisoner to prevent his escape.
