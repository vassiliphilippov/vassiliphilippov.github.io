gear shift

noun
change in strategy or approach
Pronunciation: / ɡɪr ʃɪft / (AmE), / ɡɪə ʃɪft / (BrE)
a sudden or significant change in strategy, approach, or activity

Examples

A gear shift in strategy
His presentation took a gear shift midway as he addressed the emerging challenges.
The coach called for a gear shift in tactics after halftime to boost the team's performance.
There was a noticeable gear shift in the company's priorities after the new CEO arrived.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pivot - a significant change in strategy or approach
  • antonym: status quo - the existing state of affairs
