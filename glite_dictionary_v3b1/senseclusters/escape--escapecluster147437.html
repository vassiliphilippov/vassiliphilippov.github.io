escape

noun
avoidance of danger or harm
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɪˈskeɪp / (AmE), / ɪˈskeɪp / (BrE)
the act of avoiding something unpleasant, harm, or responsibility

Examples

An escape from trouble
Her quick thinking gave her an escape from a potentially dangerous situation.
It was a narrow escape from disaster after the brakes failed on the road.
The hikers had a lucky escape when the storm suddenly changed direction.
His clever escape from jury duty annoyed his colleagues.
Some people seek escape from their responsibilities through deception.
The manager was criticized for his repeated escape from difficult decisions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: capture - the act of catching or being caught, opposite of getting away
  • synonym: evasion - the act of avoiding something, especially by cleverness or trickery
