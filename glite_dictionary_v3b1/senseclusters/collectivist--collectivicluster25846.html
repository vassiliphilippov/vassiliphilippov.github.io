collectivist

adjective
supporting collective or communal ownership
Pronunciation: / kəˈlɛktɪvɪst / (AmE), / kəˈlɛktɪvɪst / (BrE)
relating to the principle or practice of prioritizing the group over the individual, including collective ownership and cooperation

Examples

Collectivist society values
Some critics argue that collectivist policies can stifle personal initiative.
The new law had a distinctly collectivist approach to land distribution.
Their society was governed by collectivist ideals, prioritizing the needs of the community.
The company adopted a collectivist culture that encouraged employees to work together for common goals.
In many collectivist societies, family and community needs are placed above personal desires.
Her collectivist values influenced her decision to support group projects over individual achievements.

Related Terms

  • synonym: communal - shared by all members of a community
  • antonym: individualist - favoring individual rather than collective action or ownership
