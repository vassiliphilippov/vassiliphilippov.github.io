chromatics

noun
use of semitones
8.9
Pronunciation: / kroʊˈmætɪks / (AmE), / krəʊˈmætɪks / (BrE)
the use of notes outside the standard scale, especially moving by semitones, in music composition or performance

Examples

Chromatics in music
The pianist's skillful use of chromatics added depth and complexity to the piece.
Modern composers often experiment with chromatics to create unique musical effects.
Students learned about chromatics as part of their advanced music theory class.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chromaticism - the use of many notes outside the main scale in music
  • antonym: diatonic - using only the notes of a standard major or minor scale
