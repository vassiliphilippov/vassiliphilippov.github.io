cultivation

noun
development of mind or manners through education
Pronunciation: / ˌkʌl.təˈveɪ.ʃən /; / ˌkʌltɪˈveɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkʌl.tɪˈveɪ.ʃən /; / ˌkʌltɪˈveɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or quality of developing and improving one's mind, manners, or social skills; refinement and education

Examples

Personal cultivation
Her cultivation was evident in the way she spoke and carried herself.
The cultivation of manners is essential for polite society.
Years of cultivation transformed him into a true gentleman.
Her speech was marked by an impressive cultivation and elegance that captivated the audience.
The cultivation of her manners made her welcome in any social circle.
Years of study and travel gave him a cultivation rarely found in his peers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: coarseness - lack of refinement or manners
  • synonym: education - the process of teaching or acquiring knowledge and skills
  • synonym: polish - a high degree of refinement or elegance
  • synonym: refinement - the process of making improvements, especially in manners or education
