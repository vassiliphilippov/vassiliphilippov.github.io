in the bosom of

preposition
in intimate closeness or deep shelter
Pronunciation: / ɪn ðə ˈboʊsəm əv / (AmE), / ɪn ðə ˈbəʊzəm ɒv / (BrE)
in a position of intimate closeness or deep shelter; at the center or heart of something, often signifying protection or belonging.

Examples

In the bosom of friends
He sought comfort in the bosom of his closest friends during difficult times.
She always felt safe in the bosom of her family.
The village was nestled in the bosom of the mountains.

Related Terms
