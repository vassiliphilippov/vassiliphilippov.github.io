reeded

adjective
with raised ridges
Pronunciation: / ˈriːdɪd / (AmE), / ˈriːdɪd / (BrE)
having raised lines or ridges running along the surface, often for decoration or grip

Examples

The reeded edge
The reeded edge of the coin made it harder to counterfeit.
She admired the reeded columns that decorated the entrance to the building.
The craftsman created a reeded pattern on the wooden frame for added texture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grooved - having long, narrow cuts or channels on the surface
  • antonym: smooth - having an even and flat surface without ridges or bumps
