underworked

adjective
not busy enough
3.9
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈwɜrkt / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈwɜːkt / (BrE)
having too little work to do or not being busy enough at work

Examples

Underworked staff
The underworked employees often found themselves looking for ways to fill their time.
She felt underworked in her new position and asked for more responsibilities.
During the slow season, the staff became underworked and sometimes bored.

Related Terms

  • synonym: idle - not working or not being used
  • antonym: overworked - having too much work to do
