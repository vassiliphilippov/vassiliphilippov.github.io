Polaroid

noun
instant photograph
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊləˌrɔɪd / (AmE), / ˈpəʊləˌrɔɪd / (BrE)
a photograph taken with an instant camera that develops and prints the image within minutes

Examples

A Polaroid photo
He took a Polaroid of the sunset and watched it develop in his hand.
My grandmother has a box full of old Polaroids from family vacations.
They used a Polaroid camera at the party so everyone could take home a photo.

Related Terms

  • antonym: digital photo - a photograph taken and stored electronically
  • synonym: instant photo - a photograph that develops immediately after being taken
