instrumentate

verb
compose an instrumental score
Pronunciation: / ˈɪn.strə.mənˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈɪn.strə.mɛn.teɪt / (BrE)
to write or arrange a musical work for instruments; to compose an instrumental score for a piece of music

Examples

To instrumentate a score
It can be challenging to instrumentate music originally written only for piano.
She skillfully instrumentated the piece to highlight the woodwind section.
The composer was asked to instrumentate the opera for a chamber ensemble.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compose - to create music or a musical score
  • synonym: orchestrate - to arrange a piece of music for an orchestra
