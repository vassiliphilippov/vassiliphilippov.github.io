scholasticism

noun
medieval philosophy and theology
9.9
Pronunciation: / skəˈlæstəˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / skəˈlæstɪsɪzəm / (BrE)
A method of critical thought and learning associated with medieval European universities, characterized by strict adherence to traditional principles and systematic reasoning, especially as applied in theology and philosophy.

Examples

Medieval scholasticism method
His arguments were firmly rooted in the traditions of scholasticism, emphasizing logic and authority.
Medieval universities were centers of scholasticism, fostering rigorous debate on philosophical and theological issues.
Modern critics often reject scholasticism in favor of more progressive approaches to philosophy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: orthodoxy - authorized or generally accepted theory, doctrine, or practice
