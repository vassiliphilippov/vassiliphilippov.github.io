Oligocene epoch

noun
geological epoch (34–23 million years ago)
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈlɪg.əˌsin ˈɛp.ək / (AmE), / ˈɒl.ɪ.gəˌsiːn ˈɛp.ək / (BrE)
a geological epoch from about 34 to 23 million years ago, marked by significant changes in global climate and the appearance of modern mammal families

Examples

The Oligocene epoch period
During the Oligocene epoch, global temperatures cooled significantly, leading to major changes in vegetation.
The Oligocene epoch saw the early evolution of many modern mammal groups.
The fossil record from the Oligocene epoch provides key evidence for the appearance of sabertoothed cats.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Miocene epoch - the geological epoch directly following the Oligocene
