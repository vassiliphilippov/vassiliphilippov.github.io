pension

noun
regular retirement payment
3.2 39/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆpÉ›nÊƒÉ™n / (AmE), / ËˆpÉ›nÊƒÉ™n / (BrE)
A regular payment made by the government or a private company to someone who has retired or is no longer working, intended as an income for their support.

Examples

A monthly pension
After forty years of service, she now receives a monthly pension from the company.
His father saved carefully, hoping for a comfortable pension once he stopped working.
Many elderly people rely on their state pension to cover living expenses after retirement.

Synonyms

  • retirement pension
    2.7 0.13/m
    retirement pension is the regular payment given after someone retires to support them, while pension is the general retirement income term.
  • old-age pension
    3.2 0.25/m
    old-age pension is the payment given to people who reach pension age, while pension describes any regular retirement income paid by employer or state.
  • retirement benefit
    3.4 1.24/m
    retirement benefit is a payment or support from a pension plan or government scheme, while pension usually refers to the regular cash income for retirees.
  • retirement check
    3.1 0.10/m
    retirement check is the monthly paper payment a retiree receives, while pension means the overall retirement income paid by employer or government.

Related Terms

  • synonym: annuity - a fixed sum of money paid to someone each year
  • antonym: salary - regular payment by an employer to an employee
