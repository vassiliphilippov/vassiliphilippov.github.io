claustrophobic

adjective
uncomfortably closed or confined
6.7 0.94/m
Pronunciation: / kl…îstr…ôÀàfo äb…™k / (AmE), / kl…îÀêstr…ôÀàf…ô äb…™k / (BrE)
(of a space) uncomfortably small, closed, or confining; making one feel trapped or uneasy.

Examples

Claustrophobic room
He hates claustrophobic spaces like crowded buses or small closets.
People avoid the claustrophobic basement because it has no windows and a low ceiling.
The elevator felt so claustrophobic that I started to panic.

Synonyms

  • confining
    6.2 0.03/m
    confining is used for spaces or situations that limit movement and feel restrictive, while claustrophobic adds a sense of panic or trapped unease.
  • cramped
    3.2 2.59/m
    cramped describes a room with little space to move and feels physically tight, while claustrophobic also brings a strong trapped feeling.
  • incommodious
    9.9 0.01/m
    incommodious sounds formal and means inconveniently small or cramped, while claustrophobic focuses on a closed space that makes someone feel trapped.

Related Terms

  • antonym: spacious - having lots of space; opposite of confined or cramped
