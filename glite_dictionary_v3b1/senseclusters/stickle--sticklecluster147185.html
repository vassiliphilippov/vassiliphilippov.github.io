stickle

verb
argue stubbornly over minor points
8.3 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈstɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈstɪkəl / (BrE)
to argue or dispute stubbornly, especially about trivial or minor details

Examples

To stickle about details
If you stickle about every minor error, progress will be very slow.
She tends to stickle over the smallest details during meetings.
They stickled so much about where to eat that they ended up staying home.
