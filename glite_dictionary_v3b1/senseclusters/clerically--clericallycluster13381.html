clerically

adverb
in a priestly manner
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈklɛrɪkli / (AmE), / ˈklɛrɪkli / (BrE)
in a manner relating to the clergy or to religious clerics; in an ecclesiastical or priestly way

Examples

Clerically led service
The ceremony was conducted clerically according to ancient religious traditions.
He dressed clerically for the event, wearing the traditional robes of his office.
The documents were reviewed clerically to ensure they met all church requirements.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ecclesiastically - in a manner relating to the church or clergy
  • antonym: secularly - in a manner not connected with religion or the clergy
