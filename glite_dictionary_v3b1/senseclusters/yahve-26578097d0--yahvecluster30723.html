Yahve

proper noun
name for God in Old Testament
9.9
Pronunciation: / jɑːˈveɪ / (AmE), / ˈjɑːveɪ / (BrE)
A transliterated name for the God of the Old Testament, derived from the Hebrew consonants YHVH.

Examples

Yahve God name
In theological discussions, the distinction between Yahve and other names for God is significant.
Many scholars use Yahve when referring to the God described in the Hebrew scriptures.
The name Yahve is considered sacred in Judaism and is often not pronounced aloud.
