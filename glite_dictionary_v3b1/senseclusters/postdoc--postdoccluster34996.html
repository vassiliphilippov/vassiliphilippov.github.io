postdoc

noun
scholar doing research after a doctorate
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊstˌdɑk /; / ˈpoʊs(t)ˌdɑk / (AmE), / ˈpəʊstˌdɒk / (BrE)
a scholar or researcher working in academia after earning a doctoral degree (usually temporarily)

Examples

University postdoc
After defending her thesis, she started working as a postdoc at a major university.
Many postdocs hope to secure permanent faculty positions after completing their fellowship.
The chemistry department is looking to hire a postdoc for their latest research project.
After earning her PhD in physics, she accepted a postdoc position at a leading university.
He spent three years as a postdoc before landing a faculty job.
The lab relies heavily on the skills of its postdocs to conduct advanced experiments.
