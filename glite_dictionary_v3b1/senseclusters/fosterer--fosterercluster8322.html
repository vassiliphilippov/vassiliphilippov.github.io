fosterer

noun
temporary caregiver
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈfɑstərər / (AmE), / ˈfɒstərə / (BrE)
a person who temporarily cares for a child or animal that is not their own, usually until a permanent home is found

Examples

A fosterer of children
After training, she became a fosterer for dogs waiting to be adopted.
The organization is always looking for a new fosterer to help care for rescued animals.
As a fosterer, he provided a safe home for children in need.

Related Terms

  • antonym: adopter - a person who legally takes another's child or animal as their own
  • synonym: foster parent - a person who cares for a child temporarily as part of the foster care system
