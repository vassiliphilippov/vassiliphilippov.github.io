queenless

adjective
without a queen (chess)
7.5
Pronunciation: / ˈkwiːnləs / (AmE), / ˈkwiːnləs / (BrE)
in chess, having no queen remaining on the board after a capture or exchange

Examples

Queenless endgame
After the exchange of queens, both players entered a queenless endgame.
A queenless position often requires different strategies than one with queens on the board.
He felt more comfortable in queenless middlegames where tactics were less sharp.

Related Terms

  • antonym: queened - having a queen on the board in chess
