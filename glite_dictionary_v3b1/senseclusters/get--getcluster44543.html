get

verb
understand; grasp meaning
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt / (AmE), / ɡɛt / (BrE)
to understand, grasp, or perceive something (including catching what is said or being puzzled/confused)

Examples

She gets the point
Did you get the instructions, or should I explain them again?
I didn't get what you meant in your message last night.
Some people don't get complex jokes the first time they hear them.
He really got what made the novel special in his adaptation.
It's hard to get the exact mood of a city in a photograph.
The artist managed to get the feeling of nostalgia in her painting.
Can you get the total price by adding the taxes and fees?
If you add three and five, you get eight.
She wanted to know what answer you get if you divide 100 by 4.

Related Terms

  • synonym: calculate - to determine (something) mathematically, especially by numerical methods
  • synonym: capture - to accurately represent or convey something, especially in art or writing
  • synonym: catch - to hear and understand something that is said, especially when it is not clear or loud
  • synonym: compute - to determine by calculation; to reckon; to calculate
  • synonym: confuse - to make someone unable to think clearly
  • antonym: enlighten - to give someone greater knowledge or understanding
  • antonym: miss - to fail to perceive or represent something accurately
  • antonym: misunderstand - to fail to understand or interpret correctly
  • synonym: puzzle - to confuse or bewilder someone
  • synonym: understand - to grasp the meaning or significance of something
