civilize

verb
refine tastes or judgment
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪv.əˌlaɪz /; / ˈsɪvəˌlaɪz /; / ˈsɪv.ə.laɪz / (AmE), / ˈsɪv.ɪ.laɪz /; / ˈsɪvəlaɪz / (BrE)
to improve the behavior, manners, or culture of a person or group, making them more polite or developed

Examples

To civilize manners
She hoped to civilize his manners by introducing him to classical music.
The program aims to civilize young people's appreciation for literature.
Travel can civilize a person's taste in food and art.
Efforts were made to civilize the remote tribes through education and exposure to new customs.
Many reformers of the past believed that art and literature could civilize the masses.
The teacher hoped the new rules would help civilize the students' behavior in class.
She worked hard to civilise his uncouth manners.
The art class aims to civilise the students' sense of aesthetics.
Travel and literature can civilise the mind and broaden one's horizons.

Related Terms

  • antonym: barbarise - to make barbarous or to become barbaric
  • antonym: barbarize - to make or become less civilized; to become more barbaric
  • synonym: cultivate - to improve or develop by careful training or education
  • synonym: enlighten - to give someone greater knowledge or understanding; to educate
