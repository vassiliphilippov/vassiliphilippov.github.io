tendentially

adverb
with bias
Pronunciation: / tɛnˈdɛnʃəli / (AmE), / tɛnˈdɛnʃəli / (BrE)
in a way that shows or follows a tendency, bias, or general direction (not neutral)

Examples

Written tendentially biased
The article was written tendentially, favoring one side of the debate over the other.
Some news outlets report tendentially rather than presenting all the facts equally.
He argued tendentially, making his bias clear to the audience.
The conversation tendentially shifted toward politics as the evening went on.
His arguments were tendentially supportive of the new policy, though not directly stated.
The data tendentially points to an increase in demand over the next year.
