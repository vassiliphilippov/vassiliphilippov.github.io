The number of deaths in a given population, scaled to the size of that population, typically expressed per thousand individuals per year.

Examples

Mortality rate
Public health initiatives aim to lower mortality through better access to healthcare.
Researchers are studying the causes of high mortality in certain regions.
The infant mortality rate has decreased significantly over the past decade.

Synonyms

  • mortality rate
    3.8 4.37/m
    mortality rate is the technical statistic showing deaths per population per year, while mortality names the general number of deaths.
  • death rate
    2.7 3.02/m
    death rate counts deaths per thousand people in a period and is common in reports, while mortality is the formal population measure.
  • fatality rate
    4.9 0.42/m
    fatality rate often refers to deaths among a specific group such as disease cases, while mortality covers total deaths in a population.
