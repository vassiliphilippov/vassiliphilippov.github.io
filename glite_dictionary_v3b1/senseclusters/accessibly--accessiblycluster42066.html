accessibly

adverb
in an accessible way
3.6
Pronunciation: / əkˈsɛsəbli / (AmE), / əkˈsɛsəbli / (BrE)
in a way that is easy for people to use, reach, or understand (including for people with disabilities)

Examples

Designed accessibly
The museum is designed accessibly so that everyone can enjoy the exhibits.
Information on the website is presented accessibly for users with visual impairments.
The park was renovated accessibly, adding ramps and clear signs for all visitors.
The teacher explained the complex topic accessibly so that everyone could follow.
All public buildings should be designed accessibly for people with disabilities.
The author writes accessibly, making difficult ideas easy for general readers to grasp.
The museum is accessibly located near public transportation for all visitors.
Information was presented accessibly so that everyone could understand the main points.
The medication is priced accessibly to ensure it is available to those in need.

Related Terms

  • synonym: affordably - in a way that does not cost too much
  • synonym: clearly - in a way that is easy to see or understand
  • antonym: inaccessibly - in a way that is difficult or impossible to reach or use
  • synonym: inclusively - in a way that includes all people
  • antonym: obscurely - in a way that is difficult to understand
  • synonym: simply - in a straightforward or uncomplicated way
