felsic

adjective
light, silica-rich
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛlsɪk / (AmE), / ˈfɛlsɪk / (BrE)
describing igneous rocks or minerals that are light in color and rich in silica and feldspar

Examples

Felsic granite rock
Granite is a typical felsic rock commonly found in the Earth's continental crust.
The geologist explained that felsic minerals give the rock its light color.
Compared to basalt, felsic rocks contain more silica and are less dense.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mafic - describing rocks that are dark in color and rich in magnesium and iron
