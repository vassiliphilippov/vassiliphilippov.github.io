debenture

noun
certificate acknowledging a debt
9.9 7.7
Pronunciation: /də.ˈben.tʃər/ /; / /dɪ.ˈben.tʃər/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈben.tʃə/ /; / /dɪ.ˈben.tʃʊə/ (BrE)
a certificate or bond issued by a company or government as a promise to repay borrowed money with interest, usually not secured by specific assets

Examples

A debenture certificate

The company raised capital by issuing debentures to investors.
Unlike shares, a debenture does not confer any ownership in the issuing firm.
The company issued debentures to raise capital for its new project.
Investors often compare the risks of debentures with those of secured bonds.
Without collateral, a debenture relies on the issuer's creditworthiness.

Investors purchased debentures because they trusted the company's financial stability.
Unlike a mortgage, a debenture is not secured by any specific property.
