hemimetamorphous

adjective
undergoing incomplete metamorphosis (insects)
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛmɪˌmɛtəˈmɔrfəs / (AmE), / ˌhɛmɪˌmɛtəˈmɔːfəs / (BrE)
(of an insect with aquatic young) undergoing incomplete metamorphosis, in which the young does not resemble the adult.

Examples

Hemimetamorphous insect young
Dragonflies are classic examples of hemimetamorphous insects, as their young live underwater before becoming aerial adults.
In a hemimetamorphous life cycle, the transformation from nymph to adult is gradual and lacks a pupal stage.
Many aquatic bugs are hemimetamorphous, passing through nymph stages that differ greatly from the adult form.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hemimetabolous - (of insects) undergoing incomplete metamorphosis, especially with nymphs resembling adults
  • antonym: holometabolous - (of insects) undergoing complete metamorphosis with larval, pupal, and adult stages
