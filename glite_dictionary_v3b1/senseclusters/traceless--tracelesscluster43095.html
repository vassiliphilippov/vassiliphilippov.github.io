traceless

adjective
having zero trace
Pronunciation: / ˈtreɪsləs / (AmE), / ˈtreɪsləs / (BrE)
having no trace; especially, describing a matrix whose trace (the sum of its diagonal elements) is zero

Examples

Traceless matrix
A traceless matrix is important in certain areas of quantum mechanics.
The transformation is traceless, meaning the sum of its diagonal elements is zero.
Physicists often work with traceless operators when studying symmetry properties.

Related Terms

  • antonym: nontrivial - not having only the simplest or most obvious solution
  • synonym: singular - having a determinant of zero (in mathematics)
