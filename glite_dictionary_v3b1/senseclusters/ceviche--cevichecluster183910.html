ceviche

noun
marinated seafood dish
5.8
Pronunciation: / seˈviːtʃeɪ / (AmE), / seˈviːtʃeɪ / (BrE)
a Latin American dish of raw fish or seafood marinated in citrus juice, often mixed with onions, chili, and herbs

Examples

A plate of ceviche
We shared a plate of ceviche with lime and cilantro at the beachside café.
The chef prepared a spicy shrimp ceviche as the restaurant's signature starter.
Many people enjoy ceviche on hot days because it is light and refreshing.
We ordered ceviche as an appetizer at the seafood restaurant.
The chef prepared a spicy ceviche with shrimp, lime juice, and fresh cilantro.
On hot days, many people enjoy a cold plate of ceviche by the beach.
