bomb

noun
sealed vessel for measuring heat of combustion
Pronunciation: / bɑm / (AmE), / bɒm / (BrE)
a strong, sealed vessel used in laboratories to measure the heat of combustion of substances

Examples

A laboratory bomb
A bomb is essential equipment in the operation of a calorimetry experiment.
Oxygen is pumped into the bomb before ignition to ensure the complete combustion of the substance.
The technician placed the sample in the bomb to determine its calorific value.
