coeditor

noun
joint publication editor
Pronunciation: / ˌkoʊˈɛdɪtər / (AmE), / ˌkəʊˈɛdɪtə / (BrE)
a person who shares the responsibility of editing a publication with one or more others

Examples

The coeditor role
She was appointed as a coeditor for the new science magazine.
Both coeditors worked closely to finalize the content before publication.
As a coeditor, he shares decision-making duties with his colleague.
The two coeditors worked closely to finalize the magazine's special issue.
As a coeditor, he was responsible for reviewing all submitted articles.
The book credits three coeditors for their collaborative efforts.
