Wen-Ti

proper noun
Chinese god of literature
Pronunciation: / wɛnˈdi / (AmE), / wɛnˈtiː / (BrE)
In Chinese mythology, the god of literature who is revered by scholars and writers.

Examples

Wen-Ti god
Ancient scholars would pray to Wen-Ti for inspiration before important examinations.
Many legends describe how Wen-Ti rewarded diligent students with wisdom and eloquence.
The temple dedicated to Wen-Ti attracts writers and poets from all over China.
