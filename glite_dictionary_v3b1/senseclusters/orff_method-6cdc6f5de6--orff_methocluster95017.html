Orff method

noun
music teaching approach
6.7
Pronunciation: / ɔrf ˈmɛθəd / (AmE), / ɔːf ˈmɛθəd / (BrE)
a teaching approach for children that combines music, movement, and play to encourage learning and creativity

Examples

The Orff method teaching
The elementary school introduced the Orff method to make music classes more interactive and fun.
Teachers trained in the Orff method often use instruments and movement in their lessons.
Many educators believe the Orff method helps children develop creativity and a love for music.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Kodály method - a music education method emphasizing singing and hand signs
  • synonym: Suzuki method - a music teaching method focusing on listening and repetition
