perceive

verb
become aware or conscious of
3.0 52/m
Pronunciation: / p…ôrÀàsiv / (AmE), / p…ôÀàsiÀêv / (BrE)
to become aware of or conscious of something; to realize or understand

Examples

To perceive the truth
He finally perceived the reason for their disappointment.
It took her a while to perceive the true meaning behind his words.
They began perceiving a change in the atmosphere as the meeting progressed.

Synonyms

  • realize
    1.1 240/m
    realize often occurs as a sudden clear understanding, while perceive covers both immediate sensory notice and slower awareness.
  • see
    1.0 654/m
    see often means understand in everyday talk, while perceive sounds more formal and includes both senses and thought.
  • sense
    4.2 35/m
    sense usually means noticing by physical feeling or intuition, while perceive is broader and includes general awareness or understanding.
  • discern
    5.8 9.48/m
    discern means pick out or recognize something with difficulty, while perceive covers general noticing without that careful focus.
  • observe
    2.4 76/m
    observe means watch carefully to learn or record details, while perceive often means simply to become aware or notice.
  • detect
    3.2 43/m
    detect means discover a hidden or faint thing often by testing, while perceive is a more general becoming aware by senses or thought.

Related Terms

  • synonym: detect - discover or identify the presence of something
  • antonym: ignore - fail to notice or recognize
  • synonym: notice - become aware of something; observe
