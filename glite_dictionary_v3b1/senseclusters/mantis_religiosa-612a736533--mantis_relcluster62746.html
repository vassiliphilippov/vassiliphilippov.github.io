Mantis religiosa

noun
species of praying mantis
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˌmæntɪs rəˌlɪdʒiˈoʊsə / (AmE), / ˌmæntɪs rɛˌlɪdʒɪˈəʊsə / (BrE)
A species of praying mantis commonly found in Europe, characterized by its slender green body and folded forelegs held in a prayer-like position.

Examples

The Mantis religiosa insect
Many gardeners welcome Mantis religiosa because it feeds on insect pests.
The Mantis religiosa is known for its characteristic stance, which resembles someone at prayer.
The natural habitat of Mantis religiosa extends across large parts of Europe and Asia.

Related Terms

  • synonym: praying mantis - common name for various insects of the family Mantidae, noted for their posture
