frumpy

adjective
dowdy or old-fashioned, lacking style
7.3 0.51/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆfrÊŒm.pi / (AmE), / ËˆfrÊŒm.pi / (BrE)
(of a person or their clothes) old-fashioned, unattractive, and lacking in style or neatness; dowdy.

Examples

Frumpy old clothes
Despite her intelligence, some colleagues judged her by her frumpy appearance.
She felt frumpy in her old sweater and skirt at the glamorous party.
The frumpy dress she wore was terribly out of fashion.

Synonyms

  • dowdy
    7.1 0.47/m
    dowdy describes plain, unfashionable clothing that looks dull, while frumpy suggests messy, old-fashioned clothes lacking neatness and style.
  • frumpish
    9.9 0.01/m
    frumpish is prim and old-fashioned in style, while frumpy is more untidy, plain, and lacking neatness and fashion.
