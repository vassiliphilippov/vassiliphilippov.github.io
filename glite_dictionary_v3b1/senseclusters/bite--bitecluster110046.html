bite

verb
grip, cut, or tear with the teeth
Pronunciation: / baɪt / (AmE), / baɪt / (BrE)
to grip, cut, or tear something with the teeth or jaws, or to wound by biting (including by animals or insects)

Examples

To bite an apple
Be careful when you feed the dog, as it may bite.
Children are sometimes tempted to bite their pencils when they're nervous.
The mosquito tried to bite her arm, but she swatted it away.
A mosquito bite can make your skin itch for several days.
Be careful when walking barefoot outside, as ants might bite you.
Some insects bite to protect themselves from threats.
The dog started to bite the old shoe in the yard.
Be careful not to bite your tongue while eating quickly.
She felt the mosquito bite her arm during the night.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chew - to crush food with the teeth
  • antonym: lick - to pass the tongue over something
  • synonym: sting - to wound with a sharp-pointed body part, as bees or wasps do
