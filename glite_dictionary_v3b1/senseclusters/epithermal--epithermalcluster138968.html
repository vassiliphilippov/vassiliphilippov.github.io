epithermal

adjective
shallow mineral formation
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˌɛpɪˈθɜrməl / (AmE), / ˌɛpɪˈθɜːməl / (BrE)
formed at shallow depths and moderate temperatures, especially referring to mineral deposits created by hydrothermal processes near the Earth's surface

Examples

Epithermal gold deposit
The geologist explained that the epithermal gold deposit was created by hot fluids near the surface.
Many silver mines are classified as epithermal because of the conditions under which the minerals formed.
Exploration teams are searching for new epithermal veins in the volcanic region.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hypothermal - formed at greater depths and higher temperatures
  • degree lower: mesothermal - formed at intermediate depths and temperatures
