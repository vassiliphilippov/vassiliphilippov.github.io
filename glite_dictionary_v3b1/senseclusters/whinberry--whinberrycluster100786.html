whinberry

noun
a European shrub bearing blue-black berries
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪnbɛri / (AmE), / ˈwɪnbəri / (BrE)
A low-growing European shrub (Vaccinium myrtillus) of the heath family, with solitary flowers and edible blue-black berries, also called bilberry.

Examples

The whinberry bush
Local jam makers prize the tart flavor of whinberry preserves.
The whinberry bush was full of ripe, dark fruits by late summer.
We picked wild whinberries in the moorland for a delicious pie.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bilberry - another name for the whinberry or closely related species
