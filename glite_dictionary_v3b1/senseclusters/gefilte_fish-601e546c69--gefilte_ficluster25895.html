gefilte fish

noun
Jewish fish dish of ground fish balls or cakes
Pronunciation: / ɡəˈfɪltə fɪʃ / (AmE), / ɡəˈfɪltə fɪʃ / (BrE)
A traditional Jewish dish consisting of balls or cakes of ground fish mixed with eggs and breadcrumbs or matzo meal, seasoned and simmered in fish stock.

Examples

Traditional gefilte fish dish
I tried making gefilte fish for the first time, and it turned out better than I expected.
Many people eat gefilte fish with a bit of horseradish on the side.
My grandmother always serves gefilte fish during Passover dinner.
