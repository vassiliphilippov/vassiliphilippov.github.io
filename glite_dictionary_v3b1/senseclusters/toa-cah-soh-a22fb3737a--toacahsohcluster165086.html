TOA-CAH-SOH

noun
mnemonic for trigonometric ratios
Pronunciation: / ˌtiː oʊ eɪ ˌkɑː soʊ / (AmE), / ˌtiː əʊ eɪ ˌkɑː səʊ / (BrE)
a mnemonic phrase used to remember the definitions of the sine, cosine, and tangent functions in trigonometry: Tangent = Opposite/Adjacent, Cosine = Adjacent/Hypotenuse, Sine = Opposite/Hypotenuse

Examples

TOA-CAH-SOH mnemonic phrase
My teacher wrote TOA-CAH-SOH on the board to help us remember the formulas for trigonometry.
Some students find TOA-CAH-SOH a helpful way to recall which sides correspond to each function.
Whenever I do triangle problems, I always repeat TOA-CAH-SOH to myself before starting.
