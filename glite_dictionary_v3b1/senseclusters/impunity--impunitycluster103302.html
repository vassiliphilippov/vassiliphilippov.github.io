impunity

noun
exemption from punishment or consequences
9.7 8.3
Pronunciation: / ɪmˈpjunəti / (AmE), / ɪmˈpjuːnɪti / (BrE)
Exemption from punishment or freedom from the injurious consequences of an action.

Examples

Impunity from punishment

Protesters demanded justice, arguing that those responsible must not have impunity for their crimes.
The officials acted with complete impunity, knowing they would not be held accountable for their actions.
