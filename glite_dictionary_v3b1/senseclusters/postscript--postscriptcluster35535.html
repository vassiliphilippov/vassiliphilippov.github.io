postscript

noun
note added at end of a letter
1.6
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊstˌskrɪpt / (AmE), / ˈpəʊstˌskrɪpt / (BrE)
an extra note or message added at the end of a letter or other text, usually after the signature

Examples

A postscript at end
In the postscript, he apologized for forgetting to invite her to the meeting.
She added a postscript at the end of her letter to mention the new address.
The postscript contained a cheerful note that made me smile.
She added a postscript to her letter, mentioning the arrival of spring.
The author's postscript clarified several points from earlier in the book.
The report ended with a brief postscript acknowledging the research team's efforts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: P.S. - abbreviation for 'postscript' used in letters
  • synonym: addendum - something added, especially to the end of a document
  • antonym: preface - an introduction at the beginning of a book or publication
