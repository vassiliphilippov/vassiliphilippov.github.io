hurry up

verb
move or act more quickly
1.0 9.97/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆhÉœri ËŒÊŒp / (AmE), / ËˆhÊŒri ËŒÊŒp / (BrE)
to do something more quickly or to move or act with increased speed

Examples

To hurry up quickly
Please hurry up and finish your homework before dinner.
She told everyone to hurry up because the movie was about to start.
You need to hurry up or you'll miss your appointment.

Synonyms

  • hurry
    1.0 40/m
    hurry means moving or acting quickly in general, while hurry up is a direct command to speed up right now.
  • get a move on
    3.4 0.70/m
    get a move on is an informal phrase people say to push someone to start moving, while hurry up is more neutral.
  • rush
    1.0 37/m
    rush means acting quickly with strong urgency or speed, sometimes too fast, while hurry up is a plain request to be quicker.
  • hasten
    3.9 1.08/m
    hasten is a more formal verb often used in writing to speed an event, while hurry up is casual speech to urge someone.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rush - to move or do something quickly
