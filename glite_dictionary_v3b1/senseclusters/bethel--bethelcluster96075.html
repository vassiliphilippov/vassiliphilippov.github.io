bethel

noun
house of worship, esp. for sailors
8.2
Pronunciation: / ˈbɛθəl / (AmE), / ˈbɛθəl / (BrE)
a place of worship, especially one used by seafarers or for nonconformist religious meetings.

Examples

A bethel chapel
He volunteered at the bethel, offering comfort to traveling mariners.
Many communities built a bethel to serve the spiritual needs of the local fishermen.
The old seaport still has a bethel where sailors gather for evening prayers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chapel - a small place of worship, often attached to an institution
  • synonym: church - a Christian place of worship
