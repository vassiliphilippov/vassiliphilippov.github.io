stagecoach

noun
large horse-drawn passenger and mail coach
Pronunciation: / ˈsteɪdʒˌkoʊtʃ / (AmE), / ˈsteɪdʒˌkəʊtʃ / (BrE)
a large horse-drawn coach formerly used for carrying passengers and mail on regular routes between towns, especially before the advent of railways

Examples

A stagecoach ride
In the museum, you can see a restored stagecoach from the 19th century.
The old stagecoach rumbled along the dusty road towards the next town.
Travelers waited at the inn for the arrival of the stagecoach every morning.
