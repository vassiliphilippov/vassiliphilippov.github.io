willing

adjective
ready or prepared to do something
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈwɪlɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈwɪlɪŋ / (BrE)
ready or prepared to do something, or showing willingness to help or participate.

Examples

A willing helper
Having a willing attitude makes teamwork much more effective.
She is always a willing helper, ready to assist whenever needed.
The students were willing to participate in the project despite their busy schedules.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: eager - very enthusiastic and keen to do something
  • degree lower: reluctant - hesitant or not willing to do something
  • antonym: unwilling - not ready or prepared to do something; reluctant
