watertube

noun
boiler tube carrying water
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtərˌtuːb / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtəˌtjuːb / (BrE)
a tube in a boiler through which water circulates, used to generate steam

Examples

A watertube boiler
A leak in one watertube can significantly reduce a boiler's performance.
Modern power plants often use watertube boilers for greater efficiency and safety.
The engineer inspected each watertube to ensure there were no signs of corrosion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: firetube - a boiler tube through which hot gases pass
