small-townish value

noun
values typical of a small community
Pronunciation: / smɔl ˈtaʊnɪʃ ˈvæl.ju / (AmE), / smɔːl ˈtaʊnɪʃ ˈvæl.juː / (BrE)
values or attitudes considered characteristic of a small community, often implying traditionalism, conservatism, or close-knit social norms

Examples

Small-townish values and traditions
His small-townish values shaped his view on life.
She admired the small-townish values of friendliness and community spirit she found there.
Some criticize small-townish values for discouraging change and diversity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cosmopolitanism - attitudes or values characteristic of large or diverse cities
  • synonym: provincialism - narrow-mindedness typical of rural or small communities
