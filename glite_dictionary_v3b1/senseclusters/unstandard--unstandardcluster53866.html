unstandard

verb
to remove standardization
9.6
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈstændərd / (AmE), / ʌnˈstændəd / (BrE)
to make something not conform to an accepted or official standard; to remove or change standardization

Examples

To unstandard a process
The company chose to unstandard its reporting process to better fit each department's needs.
After years of strict guidelines, they decided to unstandard the curriculum for more flexibility.
We may need to unstandard the templates so teams can adapt them as required.

Related Terms

  • synonym: customize - to modify something to suit individual needs
  • antonym: standardize - to make things conform to a standard
