digraph

noun
two letters representing one sound
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪˌɡræf / (AmE), / ˈdʌɪɡrɑːf / (BrE)
A combination of two successive letters representing a single sound, as in 'sh' in 'shoe'.

Examples

A digraph sound
In the word 'phone', the digraph 'ph' represents the sound /f/.
Learning to read involves recognizing letter patterns like the digraph in 'chair'.
The English language contains many digraphs, such as 'ch', 'th', and 'sh'.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: trigraph - three letters representing a single sound, as in 'sch' in 'schmooze'
