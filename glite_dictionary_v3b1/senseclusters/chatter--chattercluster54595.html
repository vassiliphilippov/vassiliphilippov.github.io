chatter

verb
make rapid, repeated noises
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃæt̬ɚ / (AmE), / ˈtʃætə / (BrE)
to make a rapid series of short, high-pitched noises, such as teeth clicking together or objects rattling

Examples

Teeth chatter in cold
Her teeth chattered from the cold wind blowing across the lake.
Squirrels chattered angrily when the dog approached their tree.
The birds chattered excitedly in the early morning light.
During the winter storm, everyone’s teeth chattered uncontrollably.
The frightened child could not stop his teeth from chattering.
When he stepped outside, his teeth began to chatter from the icy wind.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clatter - to make a loud, rattling sound
  • synonym: jabber - to talk rapidly and excitedly but with little sense
  • synonym: rattle - to make a series of rapid, short, sharp sounds
