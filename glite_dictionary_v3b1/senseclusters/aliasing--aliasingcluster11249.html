aliasing

noun
sampling distortion
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪliˌæsɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈeɪliːəsɪŋ / (BrE)
a visual or audio distortion that occurs when a signal is sampled at too low a resolution, causing misleading or jagged effects

Examples

Image aliasing effects
The low-resolution image suffered from noticeable aliasing along the edges of objects.
Audio aliasing can make a recording sound harsh or unnatural if not properly filtered.
Graphic designers use anti-aliasing techniques to reduce aliasing in digital artwork.

Related Terms

  • synonym: artifact - an unwanted effect in digital images or sounds
  • synonym: distortion - a change that makes something appear different from reality
