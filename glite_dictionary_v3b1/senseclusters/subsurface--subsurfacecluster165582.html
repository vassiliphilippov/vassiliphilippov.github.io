located or occurring beneath the surface of something, especially the earth or another body

Examples

Subsurface conditions
Engineers study subsurface conditions before constructing a building foundation.
Scientists drilled into subsurface layers to analyze the composition of the rock.
The rover detected subsurface ice on Mars, suggesting the presence of water.

Synonyms

  • subterranean
    7.4 1.51/m
    subterranean is deep under the earth and often used for caves or tunnels, while subsurface is beneath any surface.
  • subterraneous
    8.8 0.00/m
    subterraneous is a formal word for things below the earth's surface, while subsurface means beneath any object's surface.
  • subjacent
    9.9 0.01/m
    subjacent means directly under something in a strict spatial sense, while subsurface is a general term for beneath a surface.
  • underground
    3.0 15/m
    underground describes things under the ground or secret activity, while subsurface only means physically beneath a surface.
  • belowground
    4.3 0.53/m
    belowground means situated under the ground in a plain physical way, while subsurface can mean under any surface.
  • underlying
    4.0 2.15/m
    underlying refers to what lies beneath or the real cause often used for ideas, while subsurface focuses on physical location.
