power plant

noun
an electricity-generating facility

A building or group of buildings where electricity is generated for distribution to consumers.

Examples

A power plant building
Engineers inspected the power plant after the thunderstorm caused a brief outage.
Plans to build a solar power plant were discussed at the town meeting.
The city relies on a coal-fired power plant for most of its electricity.

Synonyms

  • power station
    3.2 1.01/m
    a power station is a large complex that makes electricity for a wide area, while a power plant supplies consumers directly.
  • powerhouse
    4.7 0.28/m
    a powerhouse often refers to a facility that generates electricity, while a power plant is the neutral technical name used by utilities.
  • electrical plant
    3.8 0.05/m
    an electrical plant is a formal technical term for a facility supplying electricity as a utility, while a power plant is everyday usage.
