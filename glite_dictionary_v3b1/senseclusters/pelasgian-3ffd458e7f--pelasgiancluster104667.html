Pelasgian

noun
ancient Greek inhabitant
Pronunciation: / pəˈlæsdʒiən / (AmE), / pəˈlæsɡiən / (BrE)
a member of an ancient people believed to have lived in Greece before the arrival of the Greeks

Examples

A Pelasgian person
The ancient Pelasgian ruins were discovered on a remote Greek island.
Legends say the Pelasgian people built many of the region's earliest stone walls.
Historians debate the origins of the mysterious Pelasgian civilization.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Greek - a member of the people of Greece, especially after the arrival of the Greeks
  • synonym: Minoan - a member of the ancient civilization of Crete before classical Greece
