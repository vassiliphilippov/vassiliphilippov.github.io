blackdamp

noun
mine gas mix high in carbon dioxide
Pronunciation: / ˈblæk.dæmp / (AmE), / ˈblæk.damp / (BrE)
An atmosphere in a mine, especially after an explosion, that is high in carbon dioxide and incapable of supporting life.

Examples

After the blast, sensors detected deadly levels of blackdamp throughout the lower tunnels.
Miners are trained to recognize the signs of blackdamp to avoid suffocation.
The presence of blackdamp in the mine made rescue operations extremely dangerous.
