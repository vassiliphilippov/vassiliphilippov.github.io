Lir

proper noun
Irish mythological sea god
Pronunciation: / lɪr / (AmE), / lɪə / (BrE)
A figure in Irish mythology, personifying the sea and believed to be the father of Manannán; corresponds to the Welsh deity Llyr.

Examples

Lir in Irish myth
In Irish legend, Lir is revered as the god of the sea and father of Manannán.
Scholars often compare Lir to the Welsh sea deity Llyr due to their similar roles.
The story of the Children of Lir remains one of Ireland's most famous myths.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Llyr - Welsh counterpart of Lir, a deity of the sea
