Macbeth

proper noun
Shakespearean tragedy
Pronunciation: / məkˈbɛθ / (AmE), / məkˈbɛθ / (BrE)
the title character of Shakespeare's tragedy, a Scottish nobleman whose ambition leads to his downfall, and the play named after him

Examples

Shakespeare's Macbeth
We studied Macbeth in our English literature class.
The actor gave a powerful performance as Macbeth in the school play.
Many critics consider Macbeth one of Shakespeare's greatest tragedies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Hamlet - a tragic character and play by Shakespeare about indecision and revenge
  • synonym: Othello - a tragic character and play by Shakespeare about jealousy and betrayal
