take the offensive

verb
to begin attacking; take aggressive action
1.7
Pronunciation: / teɪk ði əˈfɛnsɪv / (AmE), / teɪk ði əˈfɛnsɪv / (BrE)
to begin attacking or adopt an aggressive approach in a conflict or competition

Examples

To take the offensive quickly
After a cautious start, the general decided to take the offensive and launch a surprise attack.
If you want to gain control in a debate, sometimes you have to take the offensive.
The company chose to take the offensive by releasing a series of bold new products.
