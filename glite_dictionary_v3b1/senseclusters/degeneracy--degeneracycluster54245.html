degeneracy

noun
decline in moral or mental qualities
7.9
Pronunciation: / dɪˈdʒɛnərəsi / (AmE), / dɪˈdʒɛnərəsi / (BrE)
the state of decline in moral, ethical, or intellectual qualities; corruption or deterioration

Examples

Moral degeneracy
Critics accused the artist of promoting degeneracy through his controversial works.
He was concerned about the apparent degeneracy of public morals in modern society.
Many historians point to the degeneracy of the elites as a cause of the empire's collapse.
Many critics blamed the city's downfall on the increasing degeneracy of its ruling elite.
Some fear that popular culture contributes to the moral degeneracy of society.
The novel explores themes of degeneracy in a once-proud civilization.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corruption - dishonest or immoral behavior; decay
  • synonym: decadence - moral or cultural decline as a result of excessive indulgence
  • synonym: depravity - moral corruption; wickedness
  • antonym: virtue - behavior showing high moral standards
