character-at-a-time printer

noun
printer that prints one character at a time
6.9
Pronunciation: /ˈkær.ək.tər ət ə taɪm ˈprɪn.tər/ (AmE), /ˈkær.ək.tə ət ə taɪm ˈprɪn.tə/ (BrE)
A type of printer that prints one character at a time, rather than an entire line or page.

Examples

A character-at-a-time printer
Many early computers used a character-at-a-time printer for report generation.
The old character-at-a-time printer was noisy but reliable in its output.
Unlike modern printers, a character-at-a-time printer is much slower because it prints individual characters.
