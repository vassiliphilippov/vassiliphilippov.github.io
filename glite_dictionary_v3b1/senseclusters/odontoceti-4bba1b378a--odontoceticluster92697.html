Odontoceti

noun
suborder of toothed whales
9.9
Pronunciation: / oʊˌdɒntəˈsiːtaɪ /; / oʊˌdɑːntəˈsiːtaɪ / (AmE), / əʊˌdɒntəˈsiːtaɪ / (BrE)
a scientific suborder of whales that have teeth, including dolphins, porpoises, sperm whales, and beaked whales

Examples

Odontoceti whales

All dolphins and porpoises belong to the suborder Odontoceti.
Members of the Odontoceti group are distinguished by having teeth instead of baleen plates.
Scientists study the Odontoceti to better understand the evolution of echolocation in marine mammals.
