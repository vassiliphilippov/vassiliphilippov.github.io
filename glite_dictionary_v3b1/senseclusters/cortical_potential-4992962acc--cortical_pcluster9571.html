cortical potential

noun
rapid voltage fluctuation in the brain cortex
Pronunciation: / ˈkɔrtɪkəl poʊˈtɛnʃəl / (AmE), / ˈkɔːtɪkəl pəˈtɛnʃəl / (BrE)
a rapid fluctuation of electrical voltage between areas of the cerebral cortex, measurable by an electroencephalograph

Examples

Abnormal cortical potentials
Abnormal cortical potentials may be signs of neurological disorders.
Changes in cortical potential can indicate different stages of sleep or alertness.
Scientists measured the cortical potential to study patterns of brain activity.
