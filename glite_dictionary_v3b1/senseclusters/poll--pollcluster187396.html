poll

verb
cut upper branches of trees
7.0
Pronunciation: / poʊl / (AmE), / pəʊl / (BrE)
to cut off or trim the top or a projecting part of something, such as the upper branches of a tree or the horns of an animal

Examples

To poll a tree
Every spring, the gardener polls the willow trees along the riverbank.
The farmer decided to poll the old oaks to encourage new growth.
They polled the trees in the orchard last year to improve their shape and health.
The farmer spent the morning polling the old oak to encourage new growth.
Traditionally, shepherds would poll the horns of young rams to prevent injury.
After the storm, workers polled the damaged branches from the trees along the road.

Related Terms

  • synonym: lop - to cut off branches or parts from a tree
  • synonym: pollard - a tree with the upper branches cut back to promote growth
  • synonym: prune - to trim a tree or bush by cutting away dead or overgrown branches
