eighth note

noun
a note worth one eighth of a whole note
Pronunciation: / ˈeɪtθ ˌnoʊt / (AmE), / ˈeɪtθ ˌnəʊt / (BrE)
a musical note with the time value of one eighth of a whole note, typically indicated by a solid note head with a stem and one flag

Examples

A eighth note flag
Each eighth note must be played evenly to maintain the rhythm.
She struggled to keep time during the fast eighth note runs.
The melody begins with an eighth note followed by two quarter notes.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: quarter note - a musical note twice the duration of an eighth note
  • synonym: quaver - British term for an eighth note (music)
  • degree lower: sixteenth note - a musical note with half the duration of an eighth note
