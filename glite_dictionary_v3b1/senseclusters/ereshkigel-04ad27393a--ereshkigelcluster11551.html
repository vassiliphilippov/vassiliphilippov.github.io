Ereshkigel

proper noun
Mesopotamian goddess of the underworld
Pronunciation: / ɪˈrɛʃkɪˌɡɛl / (AmE), / ɪˈrɛʃkɪˌɡɛl / (BrE)
In Mesopotamian mythology, the goddess of the underworld and death, and consort of Nergal.

Examples

Ereshkigel goddess underworld
In mythology, Ereshkigel was both feared and revered as the queen of the underworld.
Priests would perform rites to appease Ereshkigel and ensure a safe passage for the deceased.
The ancient Sumerians believed that Ereshkigel ruled over the land of the dead.
