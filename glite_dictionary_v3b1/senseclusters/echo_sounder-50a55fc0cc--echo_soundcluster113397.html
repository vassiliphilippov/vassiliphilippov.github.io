echo sounder

noun
depth-measuring device using sound pulses
Pronunciation: / ˈɛkoʊ ˌsaʊndər / (AmE), / ˈɛkəʊ ˌsaʊndə / (BrE)
a device that measures the depth of water by sending out sound pulses and timing their echoes from the seabed.

Examples

The echo sounder device
After installing the new echo sounder, the crew was able to map the seafloor more accurately.
Modern fishing vessels rely on an echo sounder to locate underwater schools of fish.
The ship used an echo sounder to determine the depth of the ocean during its voyage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sonar - a system for detecting objects in water using sound waves
