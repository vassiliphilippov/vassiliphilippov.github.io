restrictionism

noun
policy of limiting
Pronunciation: / rɪˈstrɪkʃənɪzəm / (AmE), / rɪˈstrɪkʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
the policy or practice of limiting or restricting something, especially immigration or trade

Examples

Immigration restrictionism
The government adopted restrictionism to control the number of immigrants entering the country.
Debates about restrictionism often arise during discussions on international trade agreements.
Some politicians argue that restrictionism protects local jobs and industries.

Related Terms

  • antonym: liberalism - the policy of allowing more freedom, especially in trade or immigration
  • synonym: protectionism - the policy of protecting domestic industries by restricting imports
