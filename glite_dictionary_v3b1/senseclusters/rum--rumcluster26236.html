rum

noun
a distilled alcoholic drink from sugarcane or molasses
Pronunciation: / rʌm / (AmE), / rʌm / (BrE)
a strong alcoholic drink made by distilling fermented sugarcane juice or molasses, typically produced in the Caribbean and other sugar-producing regions

Examples

A glass of rum
He brought back a bottle of aged rum from his trip to Jamaica.
Many tropical cocktails use rum as the main ingredient.
The bartender poured a generous measure of rum into the glass.

Related Terms

  • synonym: vodka - a clear distilled alcoholic beverage of Russian origin
  • synonym: whisky - a distilled alcoholic drink made from fermented grain mash
