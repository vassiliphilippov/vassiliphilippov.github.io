absolute scale

noun
temperature scale with absolute zero as zero
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈæbsəluːt skeɪl / (AmE), / ˈæbsəluːt skeɪl / (BrE)
A temperature scale in which absolute zero is defined as zero degrees, such as the Kelvin scale, where water freezes at 273.16 degrees and boils at 373.16 degrees.

Examples

The absolute scale of temperature
Scientists prefer to use the absolute scale when discussing thermodynamic properties.
Temperature on an absolute scale starts at absolute zero, the lowest possible temperature.
The absolute scale allows for precise measurement of temperature changes in experiments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Kelvin scale - temperature scale with absolute zero as zero and increments equal to Celsius degrees
