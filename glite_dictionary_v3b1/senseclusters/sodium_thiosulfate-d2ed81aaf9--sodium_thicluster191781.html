sodium thiosulfate

noun
chemical compound; photographic fixer
9.9 5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈsoʊ.diː.əm ˈθaɪ.oʊ.sʌl.feɪt/ /; / /ˈsoʊ.dəm ˈθaɪ.oʊ.sʌl.feɪt/ (AmE), /ˈsəʊ.dɪ.əm ˈθaɪ.əʊ.sʌl.feɪt/ /; / /ˈsəʊ.dəm ˈθaɪ.əʊ.sʌl.feɪt/ (BrE)
a white, crystalline, water-soluble compound (Na2S2O3) used chiefly as a photographic fixer (used in photographic processing), in chemical analysis, and as an antidote to cyanide poisoning.

Examples

Sodium thiosulfate antidote use
Sodium thiosulfate can also be used as an antidote in cases of cyanide poisoning.
The photographer added sodium thiosulfate to the developing solution to fix the image.
They bought sodium thiosulfate in bulk for use in the darkroom.
Sodium thiosulphate is commonly used in photography to fix developed film and make the image permanent.
In chemistry labs, sodium thiosulphate can neutralize chlorine solutions during water treatment experiments.
The stain on the fabric was removed after soaking it in a sodium thiosulphate solution.
