ridgepole

noun
beam at the top of a roof
7.7 8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈrɪdʒ.poʊl/ (AmE), /ˈrɪdʒ.pəʊl/ (BrE)
a long horizontal beam at the highest point of a roof, where the two sides meet, to which the upper ends of the rafters are attached

Examples

The ridgepole beam
Carpenters installed the ridgepole before attaching the rafters on either side of the roof.
Replacing the ridgepole was essential to restore the stability of the farmhouse roof.
The old barn's ridgepole had sagged under the weight of heavy snow.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rafter - one of the sloping beams supporting a roof
  • synonym: ridge - the long, narrow, raised top of a roof
