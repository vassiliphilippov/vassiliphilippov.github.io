celestial longitude

noun
angular coordinate in celestial astronomy
9.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / s…ôÀàl…õs.t É…ôl Ààl…ën.d í…ô.tuÀêd / (AmE), / s…™Ààl…õs.t É…ôl Ààl…ín.d í…™.tjuÀêd / (BrE)
(Astronomy) The angular distance of a point eastward along the celestial equator from the vernal equinox, used with declination to specify positions on the celestial sphere.

Examples

Celestial longitude angle
The observatory published the celestial longitude and declination of the newly discovered comet.
The star's celestial longitude helps astronomers pinpoint its exact location in the night sky.
To track the planet's movement, we record changes to its celestial longitude over time.

Synonyms

  • right ascension
    9.4 0.54/m
    right ascension is measured along the celestial equator and often given in hours, while celestial longitude is expressed in degrees.
  • RA
    9.8 0.92/m
    RA appears as the short form used in charts and notes, while celestial longitude is the full term used in textbooks and maps.

Related Terms

  • antonym: declination - the angular distance of a point north or south of the celestial equator, used with celestial longitude
  • synonym: right ascension - the equatorial coordinate equivalent to celestial longitude; measured eastward from the vernal equinox
