come out

verb
become detached and fall (hair, teeth, etc.)
2.0 1.50/m
Pronunciation: / kÊŒm aÊŠt / (AmE), / kÊŒm aÊŠt / (BrE)
(of hair, teeth, or spots) to become detached from the body or to emerge from under the surface

Examples

A tooth comes out
At that age, it's normal for children's baby teeth to come out.
Every time I brushed my hair, more strands would come out.
Many of his teeth came out after the accident.
Red spots began to come out on her arms after she touched the plant.
Sometimes, a wisdom tooth may come out in an awkward position, causing discomfort.
The baby's first tooth will come out soon, so don't worry about the swelling.

Synonyms

  • fall out
    3.5 1.76/m
    fall out means hair or teeth detach and drop away, while come out can mean they emerge from under the surface.
  • come off
    3.5 4.69/m
    come off means something becomes detached from where it is attached, while come out often implies emerging from under a surface.
  • push through
    3.8 0.33/m
    push through means new growth breaks up through a surface, while come out can also describe teeth or shoots appearing.
  • emerge
    3.5 31/m
    emerge means to come out from a hidden place and become visible, while come out often refers to detaching or surfacing.
  • break through
    3.7 2.33/m
    break through means to force a way through a barrier suddenly, while come out is usually less forceful and more general.
  • erupt
    4.2 9.06/m
    erupt means to start or break out suddenly and forcefully, while come out usually means to detach or emerge slowly.
