come out

verb
become detached and fall (hair, teeth, etc.)
1.0 1.8
Pronunciation: / kʌm aʊt / (AmE), / kʌm aʊt / (BrE)
(of hair, teeth, or spots) to become detached from the body or to emerge from under the surface

Examples

A tooth comes out
At that age, it's normal for children's baby teeth to come out.
Every time I brushed my hair, more strands would come out.
Many of his teeth came out after the accident.
Red spots began to come out on her arms after she touched the plant.
Sometimes, a wisdom tooth may come out in an awkward position, causing discomfort.
The baby's first tooth will come out soon, so don't worry about the swelling.
