flunky

noun
servile or obsequious subordinate
Pronunciation: / ˈflʌŋki / (AmE), / ˈflʌŋki / (BrE)
a person who does menial or subordinate work, often behaving in a servile or overly obedient way to someone in power

Examples

A flunky at work
He refused to become just a flunky for the senior partners in the firm.
The dictator's flunky followed every order without question.
The powerful CEO was always surrounded by a flunky who ran her errands.
As a flunky in a grand household, John was expected to attend to every request.
He was tired of being treated like a flunky and wanted more respect.
The rich man entered, followed by his loyal flunky carrying a silver tray.
She refused to be treated like a mere flunkey, following orders without question.
The diplomat's flunkeys stood at attention throughout the entire banquet.
The millionaire arrived surrounded by his loyal flunkeys carrying his bags.

Related Terms

  • antonym: boss - a person in authority or control
  • synonym: footman - a male servant, originally one who ran beside the carriage
  • synonym: lackey - a servant or someone who does menial tasks for others
  • synonym: servant - a person who performs duties for others, especially a household employee
  • synonym: sycophant - a person who acts obsequiously towards someone important
  • synonym: underling - a person lower in status or rank
