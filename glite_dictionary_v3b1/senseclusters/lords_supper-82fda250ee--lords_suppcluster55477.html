Lord's Supper

noun
Christian sacrament of bread and wine
4.4 7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈlɔrdz ˌsʌpər /; / lɔrdz ˈsʌpər / (AmE), / ˈlɔːdz ˌsʌpə /; / lɔːdz ˈsʌpə / (BrE)
the Christian ceremony or sacrament in which bread and wine are shared in remembrance of the meal Jesus had with his disciples before his crucifixion

Examples

The Lord's Supper ceremony
During the Lord's Supper, the congregation partakes of bread and wine in remembrance of Jesus.
Many churches celebrate the Lord's Supper as a central part of their worship service.
The pastor explained the significance of the Lord's Supper to the new members of the church.
During Holy Week, Christians reflect on the significance of the Lord's Supper.
Many Christian denominations commemorate the Lord's Supper in their worship services.
The painting by Leonardo da Vinci famously depicts the Lord's Supper.
