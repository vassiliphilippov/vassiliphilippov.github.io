rebel

noun
person fighting against authority or government
3.8 3.0
Pronunciation: /ˈre.bəl/ (AmE), /ˈre.bəl/ (BrE)
a person who opposes or resists authority, control, or tradition

Examples

A rebel leader
After many years, the rebel leader agreed to peace talks with the government.
He was labeled a rebel after joining the uprising against the dictatorship.
The rebel forces captured several key government buildings during the night.
Even as a child, Sarah was known as a rebel who questioned every rule.
He admired her for being a rebel, never afraid to follow her own path.
The great innovators throughout history are often described as rebels in their fields.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conformist - a person who adheres to conventional standards or customs
  • synonym: insurgent - a person who rises in forcible opposition to lawful authority
  • antonym: loyalist - a person who remains loyal to the established ruler or government
  • synonym: nonconformist - a person who does not conform to established customs, attitudes, or norms
