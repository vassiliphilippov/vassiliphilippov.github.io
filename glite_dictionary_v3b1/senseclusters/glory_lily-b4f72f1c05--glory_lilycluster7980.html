glory lily

noun
showy, poisonous climbing plant (Gloriosa genus)
7.3 8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlɔːri ˌlɪli / (AmE), / ˈɡlɒri ˌlɪli / (BrE)
A tropical climbing perennial plant of the genus Gloriosa, with showy yellow, red, or purple flowers, found in Africa and Asia; all parts are poisonous.

Examples

A glory lily flower
A single glory lily brightened the corner of the conservatory with its striking red petals.
Some cultures use extracts from the glory lily in traditional medicine, despite its toxic properties.
The gardener warned that all parts of the glory lily are highly poisonous if ingested.
