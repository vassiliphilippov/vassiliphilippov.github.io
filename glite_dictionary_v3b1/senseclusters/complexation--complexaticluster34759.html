complexation

noun
process of complicating
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑmplɛkˈseɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒmplɛkˈseɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something more complex or the state of becoming more complex

Examples

Complexation of a system
The complexation of the software design required additional planning and resources.
Unexpected changes led to the complexation of the entire negotiation process.
The complexation of the instructions confused many of the new employees.
