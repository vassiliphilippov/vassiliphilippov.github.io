red-baiting

noun
accusing of communism
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛdˌbeɪtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɛdˌbeɪtɪŋ / (BrE)
the act of accusing someone of being a communist or sympathizer, often to discredit or intimidate them

Examples

Political red-baiting
The senator was a victim of red-baiting during the heated political campaign.
Accusations of red-baiting were common in the United States during the Cold War.
Many careers were ruined by red-baiting in the 1950s.

Related Terms

  • synonym: smear campaign - an effort to damage someone's reputation with false or exaggerated accusations
  • synonym: witch-hunt - an attempt to find and punish people with unpopular views, often without proper evidence
