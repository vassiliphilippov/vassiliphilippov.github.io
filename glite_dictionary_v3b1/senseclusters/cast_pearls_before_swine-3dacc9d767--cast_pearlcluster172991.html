cast pearls before swine

verb
waste value on the unappreciative
7.9
Pronunciation: / kæst pɝlz bɪˈfɔr swaɪn / (AmE), / kɑːst pɜːlz bɪˈfɔː swaɪn / (BrE)
to offer something valuable to people who cannot or do not appreciate it

Examples

To cast pearls before swine
Giving him that rare book was like casting pearls before swine, as he didn't even open it.
She knew it was pointless to cast pearls before swine when her advice was constantly ignored.
They warned me not to cast pearls before swine because my efforts would go unappreciated.

Related Terms

  • synonym: waste one's breath - to say something that is ignored or not valued
