push out

verb
release or distribute (e.g., updates)
3.5
Pronunciation: /pʊʃ aʊt/ /; / /pʌʃ aʊt/ (AmE), /pʊʃ aʊt/ /; / /pʌʃ aʊt/ (BrE)
to release or distribute a product, update, or content to users or the market, often electronically or over a network.

Examples

To push out an update
The company pushed out a security patch to all users after discovering the vulnerability.
During the beta, they were pushing out several minor updates to fix interface bugs reported by testers.
The news site unexpectedly pushed out new content overnight, causing a spike in page views.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deploy - to make software or an update available for use, typically on servers or user devices
  • synonym: roll out - to introduce or make a product or service available to users or customers
