makuta

noun
African currency unit
Pronunciation: / maˈkuːtə / (AmE), / maˈkuːtə / (BrE)
a unit of currency used in some African countries, such as Malawi and the Democratic Republic of the Congo

Examples

Exchange makuta bills
He exchanged dollars for makuta at the border.
The price of bread was listed as 500 makuta in the local market.
Tourists often need to convert their money into makuta when visiting certain African countries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: franc - a unit of currency used in several countries
  • synonym: kwacha - the main currency of Malawi and Zambia
