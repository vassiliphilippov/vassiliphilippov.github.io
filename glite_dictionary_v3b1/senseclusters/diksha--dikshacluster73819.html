diksha

noun
spiritual initiation ceremony
Pronunciation: / ˈdiːkʃə / (AmE), / ˈdiːkʃə / (BrE)
a Hindu or Buddhist ceremony or blessing in which a spiritual teacher (guru) formally accepts a disciple and gives spiritual instruction

Examples

Receive diksha ceremony
The disciple received diksha from his guru in a traditional ceremony.
Many families gathered at the temple to witness the diksha of the new students.
After her diksha, she began her spiritual studies with great dedication.
The disciple received diksha from his guru before starting his spiritual journey.
Many followers travel long distances to obtain diksha from a respected teacher.
During the ceremony, the guru gave diksha by whispering a sacred mantra to the student.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blessing - a prayer or act of asking for divine favor
  • synonym: initiation - a ceremony or process that marks the beginning of something, especially membership or instruction
  • synonym: ordination - the act of conferring holy orders or spiritual authority
