woefulness

noun
state of intense sorrow or mournfulness
4.5
Pronunciation: / ˈwoʊfəlnəs / (AmE), / ˈwəʊfəlnəs / (BrE)
intense sorrow or mournfulness; the quality or state of being woeful.

Examples

Deep woefulness
She expressed her woefulness through poetry and melancholic paintings.
The woefulness of the music reflected the tragedy that had unfolded.
There was a distinct woefulness in his voice that touched everyone in the room.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grief - intense sorrow, especially caused by someone's death
  • antonym: joyfulness - state of being joyful, opposite of woefulness
  • synonym: woe - great sorrow or distress
