bottom

noun
low-lying land next to a river
5.8 3.27/m
Pronunciation: / Ààb…ëÀêt…ôm / (AmE), / Ààb…ít…ôm / (BrE)
Low-lying alluvial land adjacent to a river, often fertile and prone to flooding.

Examples

River bottom
Deer are often seen grazing in the bottom by the creek at dawn.
Many farmers prefer to plant crops in the bottom because the soil is especially rich there.
The river overflowed and flooded the entire bottom last spring.

Synonyms

  • bottomland
    7.2 0.48/m
    bottomland is low, fertile ground beside rivers that often floods, while bottom is the general term for nearby river lowland.
  • floodplain
    4.6 1.71/m
    floodplain is flat ground next to a river that regularly floods, while bottom is the smaller, local low-lying land beside the river.
  • alluvial plain
    5.8 0.08/m
    alluvial plain is a broad, flat area built by river sediments, while bottom is a smaller, nearby lowland often prone to flooding.
