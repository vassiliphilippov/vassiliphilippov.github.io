bottom

noun
low-lying land next to a river
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈbɑːtəm / (AmE), / ˈbɒtəm / (BrE)
Low-lying alluvial land adjacent to a river, often fertile and prone to flooding.

Examples

River bottom
Deer are often seen grazing in the bottom by the creek at dawn.
Many farmers prefer to plant crops in the bottom because the soil is especially rich there.
The river overflowed and flooded the entire bottom last spring.
