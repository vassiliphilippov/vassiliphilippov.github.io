post-obit bond

noun
bond secured by future inheritance
Pronunciation: / ˌpoʊst ˈoʊbɪt bɑnd / (AmE), / ˌpəʊst ˈəʊbɪt bɒnd / (BrE)
a bond, typically issued by a person entitled to a future inheritance, in which the borrower agrees to pay the lender out of that inheritance when it becomes payable, usually after the death of another person.

Examples

A post-obit bond
A post-obit bond obligates repayment only after the death of a specified individual.
He took out a post-obit bond to secure a loan against his expected inheritance.
Solicitors cautioned him about the risks involved in signing a post-obit bond.
