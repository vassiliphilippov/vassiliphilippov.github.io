crude oil

noun
unrefined petroleum as extracted from the ground
3.4 4.01/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàkruÀêd …î…™l / (AmE), / ÀàkruÀêd …î…™l / (BrE)
A naturally occurring, unrefined petroleum product composed mainly of hydrocarbons, extracted from the ground and used as a raw material in the production of fuels and chemicals.

Examples

Barrel of crude oil
Before it can be used as fuel, crude oil must be refined to remove impurities.
Large reserves of crude oil have been discovered off the coast of Brazil.
The price of crude oil rose sharply after the hurricane disrupted supply lines.

Synonyms

  • crude
    5.5 5.42/m
    crude is the short everyday name for dark unrefined oil used to make fuels, while crude oil is the formal full name.
  • petroleum
    4.4 9.75/m
    petroleum is the natural hydrocarbon liquid that industries refine into fuels and chemicals, while crude oil names the unrefined raw product.
  • fossil oil
    4.2 0.00/m
    fossil oil is oil formed from ancient plants and animals, stressing its origin, while crude oil is the traded unrefined petroleum product.

Related Terms
