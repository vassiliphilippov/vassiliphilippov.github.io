spoof

verb
to fake for deception
6.0 0.15/m
Pronunciation: / spuÀêf / (AmE), / spuÀêf / (BrE)
to trick someone by imitating or faking something, especially to deceive or mislead

Examples

To spoof an email
Hackers attempted to spoof the bank's email address to trick customers.
The caller ID was spoofed to make the number look familiar.
It is illegal to spoof someone's identity for fraudulent purposes.

Synonyms

  • impersonate
    7.2 1.89/m
    impersonate means pretending to be someone by copying their voice or look, while spoof often copies things to trick or joke.
  • masquerade
    6.9 1.99/m
    masquerade involves wearing a false identity or disguise to deceive others, while spoof is often a light or mocking imitation.
  • pass off
    6.9 0.96/m
    pass off means present something as real to fool people, while spoof usually focuses on imitating to trick or mock.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deceive - to make someone believe something that is not true
  • antonym: expose - to reveal the true nature of something false or hidden
  • synonym: imitate - to copy the actions or appearance of someone or something
