imitate

verb
to copy or mimic
3.0 2.3
Pronunciation: /ˈɪ.mə.teɪt/ /; / /ˈɪ.mɪ.teɪt/ (AmE), /ˈɪ.mɪ.teɪt/ /; / /ˈɪ.mə.teɪt/ (BrE)
to copy the actions, appearance, or behavior of someone or something

Examples

To imitate a person
Many artists imitate the styles of famous painters to learn new techniques.
The comedian can imitate dozens of different celebrity voices perfectly.
Young children often imitate their parents' behavior and speech.
Children often imitate their parents’ actions when they are learning new things.
It is dangerous to imitate the behavior seen in risky movies or TV shows.
The new product attempts to imitate the design of its more expensive competitor.
Many comedians imitate celebrities to entertain their audiences.
Parrots can often imitate human voices with surprising accuracy.
She tried to imitate her older sister's way of speaking.

Related Terms

  • synonym: copy - to make a similar or identical version of; to reproduce
  • synonym: emulate - to try to match or surpass someone by imitation
  • synonym: mimic - to imitate, especially in order to entertain or ridicule
  • antonym: originate - to create or initiate rather than copy
  • synonym: replicate - to make an exact copy of
