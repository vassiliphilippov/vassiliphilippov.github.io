exfiltration

noun
secret removal
Pronunciation: / ˌɛksfɪlˈtreɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɛksfɪlˈtreɪʃən / (BrE)
the secret removal or withdrawal of people from a dangerous or enemy area, often by military or rescue forces

Examples

Nighttime exfiltration
The team coordinated a nighttime exfiltration to safely remove the diplomats from the conflict zone.
After the mission, the soldiers awaited exfiltration by helicopter at the designated point.
Successful exfiltration required careful planning and precise timing to avoid detection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: evacuation - the removal of people from a dangerous place
  • antonym: infiltration - the secret entry of people into a place, especially for military purposes
