liberated

adjective
free from social restraints
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪbəˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈlɪbəˌreɪtɪd / (BrE)
Free from traditional social restraints or conventions, especially in behavior or attitudes.

Examples

Liberated attitude
He admired her liberated outlook, which inspired others to challenge conventions.
She led a liberated life, choosing her own path regardless of social expectations.
The 1960s saw a rise in liberated attitudes toward relationships and gender roles.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conventional - adhering to accepted customs and standards
