stall

verb
delay or postpone action
6.6 3.5
Pronunciation: /stɔːl/ /; / /stɑːl/ (AmE), /stɔːl/ (BrE)
to deliberately delay or postpone something, often to gain more time

Examples

To stall for time
He kept stalling and never actually started working on the project.
She tried to stall by asking the interviewer more questions.
The committee stalled their decision until more data was available.
He kept stalling when asked about his plans for the future.
She stalled for time by pretending to look for the documents.
The negotiators tried to stall the meeting until their lawyer arrived.
