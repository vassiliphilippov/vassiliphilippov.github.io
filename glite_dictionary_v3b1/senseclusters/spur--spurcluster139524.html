spur

verb
urge a horse with spurs
7.7 4.5
Pronunciation: /spɜːr/ (AmE), /spɜː/ (BrE)
to urge a horse forward or increase its speed by using spurs, or to equip a horse or rider with spurs

Examples

To spur a horse
As soon as he spurred his stallion, it broke into a gallop.
She spurred the mare gently, urging it up the hillside.
The jockey spurred his horse to take the lead in the final stretch.
Before the cavalry charge, the captain ordered the men to spur their horses.
It was customary for tournament knights to spur their mounts before entering the arena.
Trainers often spur the horses to enhance the authenticity of historical reenactments.
He spurs his mount whenever it starts to slow down.
She spurred the stallion over the final jump with a quick movement.
The rider spurred his horse to go faster during the race.

Related Terms

  • synonym: goad - to prod or urge on, especially with something pointed
  • synonym: prod - to poke or urge onward using something sharp
  • antonym: unspur - to remove spurs or the effect of spurs
