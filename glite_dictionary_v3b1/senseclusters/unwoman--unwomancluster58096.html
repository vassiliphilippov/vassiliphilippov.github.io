unwoman

verb
to remove feminine qualities
9.9
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈwʌmən / (AmE), / ʌnˈwʌmən / (BrE)
to deprive someone of qualities or characteristics considered typical of a woman

Examples

To unwoman a person
The strict rules of the institution seemed to unwoman her, leaving her feeling empty.
Some critics argued that the role would unwoman the character, stripping away her warmth.
Years of hardship had gradually unwomaned her, making her less expressive and gentle.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emasculate - to deprive a man of male qualities
  • antonym: feminize - to give feminine qualities to someone
