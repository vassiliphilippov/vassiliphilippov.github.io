humiliated

adjective
made to feel ashamed or embarrassed
1.0 3.1
Pronunciation: /hjuː.ˈmɪl.ɪ.eɪ.tɪd/ /; / /huː.ˈmɪl.ɪ.eɪ.tɪd/ (AmE), /hjuː.ˈmɪl.ɪ.eɪ.tɪd/ /; / /huː.ˈmɪl.ɪ.eɪ.tɪd/ (BrE)
made to feel ashamed, embarrassed, or having lost dignity or pride, often because of public disgrace or defeat

Examples

Feeling humiliated publicly
After losing the match by such a wide margin, the team members looked visibly humiliated.
He was humiliated by the unfair accusations made against him.
She felt humiliated when her mistake was pointed out in front of everyone.
After the defeat, the team was completely humiliated and avoided the press.
He was humiliated by the public criticism of his work.
She felt humiliated when she realized her mistake in front of everyone.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ashamed - feeling guilt or embarrassment
  • synonym: embarrassed - feeling awkward or self-conscious
  • antonym: proud - feeling deep pleasure or satisfaction from one's own achievements; opposite of humiliated in this emotional sense
