alkalize

verb
make more alkaline; lessen acidity
Pronunciation: / ˈæl.kəˌlaɪz /; / ˈæl.kə.laɪz / (AmE), / ˈæl.kə.laɪz / (BrE)
to make (a substance or solution) more alkaline or less acidic

Examples

To alkalize a solution
Adding baking soda will alkalize the solution, reducing its acidity.
Doctors sometimes recommend supplements to alkalize the body and correct blood pH imbalances.
Some diets aim to alkalize your system by encouraging the consumption of certain foods.
Adding baking soda will alkalise the water, increasing its pH.
Some diets claim to alkalise your body, though the scientific evidence is limited.
The liquid must be alkalised before use in this experiment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acidify - to make or become more acidic
