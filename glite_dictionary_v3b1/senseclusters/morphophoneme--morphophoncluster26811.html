morphophoneme

noun
linguistics: abstract unit underlying allomorphs
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˌmɔrfəˈfoʊnim / (AmE), / ˌmɔːfəˈfəʊniːm / (BrE)
(Linguistics) A theoretical unit in a language's structure that represents a morpheme as it is manifested by different phonemes in various contexts.

Examples

A morphophoneme in linguistics
Analyzing the data, she identified a unique morphophoneme underlying the different prefixes.
In English, the plural morphophoneme can be realized as /s/, /z/, or /ɪz/ depending on the word.
The concept of a morphophoneme helps linguists explain variations in word forms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allomorph - a variant form of a morpheme
  • degree higher: morpheme - the smallest meaningful unit in language
  • degree lower: phoneme - the smallest unit of sound distinguishing words
