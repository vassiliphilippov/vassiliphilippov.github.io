system of weights

noun
standardized units for measuring weight
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪstəm əv weɪts / (AmE), / ˈsɪstəm əv weɪts / (BrE)
A standardized set of units used to measure weight, such as the metric or avoirdupois systems.

Examples

A system of weights standard
Pharmaceuticals often require precise measurement using a specific system of weights.
The metric system of weights is widely used around the world for scientific measurements.
The old British system of weights included units like stones and pounds.
