incalculability

noun
immeasurability
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌkælkjələˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ɪnˌkælkjʊləˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being impossible to calculate, measure, estimate, or predict

Examples

The incalculability of size
The incalculability of the universe's size fascinates astronomers and philosophers alike.
He was overwhelmed by the incalculability of the risks involved in the expedition.
The incalculability of her influence on the community became clear after she retired.
The incalculability of the stock market makes investing risky for many people.
Scientists are frustrated by the incalculability of certain natural disasters.
The incalculability of his reaction left everyone uncertain about what to expect.

Related Terms

  • synonym: immeasurability - the state of being impossible to measure
  • antonym: predictability - the quality of being able to be predicted or calculated
  • synonym: uncertainty - the state of being uncertain or not known
  • synonym: uncountability - the quality of being impossible to count
  • synonym: unpredictability - the state of being impossible to predict
