highball

verb
to overestimate price
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪˌbɔl / (AmE), / ˈhaɪˌbɔːl / (BrE)
to estimate, state, offer, or demand a price or amount that is higher than what is reasonable or expected

Examples

To highball the price
The mechanic highballed the repair cost, hoping the customer would not notice.
If you highball your salary expectations, you might price yourself out of the job.
She highballed the estimate to leave room for negotiation.
The car dealer tried to highball me on the price, but I refused to pay that much.
If you highball your estimate, clients may look for someone more reasonable.
She accused the contractor of highballing the renovation costs to increase his profit.

Related Terms

  • antonym: lowball - to offer or demand an unreasonably low price or estimate
  • synonym: overcharge - to ask for more money than is fair or expected
  • antonym: undercharge - to ask for less money than is fair or usual
  • antonym: underestimate - to estimate something as being less than the actual amount
