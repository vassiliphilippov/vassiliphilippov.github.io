induction

noun
reasoning from specifics to general principles
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdʌkʃən / (AmE), / ɪnˈdʌkʃən / (BrE)

Examples

Scientific induction
His argument relied on induction, moving from several case studies to a broader conclusion.
Scientists often use induction to develop theories based on observed data.
The student confused induction with deduction during the logic exam.
