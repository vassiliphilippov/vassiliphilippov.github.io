scornfully

adverb
in a contemptuous or disdainful manner
6.0 0.74/m
Pronunciation: / Ààsk…îrn.f…ô.li / (AmE), / Ààsk…îÀên.f…ôli / (BrE)
In a way that shows strong dislike or a lack of respect; with contempt or disdain.

Examples

To scornfully reject
He scornfully rejected their offer, making it clear he thought it beneath him.
She looked scornfully at the proposal, dismissing it without a second thought.
The critic wrote scornfully about the movie, calling it a complete waste of time.
