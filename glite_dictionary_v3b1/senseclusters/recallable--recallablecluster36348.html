recallable

adjective
able to be withdrawn
5.1
Pronunciation: / rɪˈkɔləbəl / (AmE), / rɪˈkɔːləbəl / (BrE)
able to be called back, withdrawn, or revoked, such as a product, statement, or person

Examples

Recallable contract
The contract is recallable if either party fails to meet the agreed conditions.
Some government officials hold recallable positions, meaning they can be removed by a vote.
The company announced that the defective products were recallable and would be taken off the shelves.

Related Terms

  • antonym: irrevocable - impossible to change or take back
  • synonym: revocable - able to be cancelled or withdrawn
