common thorn apple

noun
poisonous annual plant with spiky fruit
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑmən θɔrn ˈæpəl / (AmE), / ˈkɒmən θɔːn ˈæpəl / (BrE)
A tall, coarse, and intensely poisonous annual plant with foul-smelling leaves, large white or violet trumpet-shaped flowers, and spiky fruits; also known as Datura stramonium.

Examples

The common thorn apple plant
Gardeners are warned to remove common thorn apple plants due to their poisonous nature.
In the wild, the common thorn apple can be recognized by its spiny fruit and large blossoms.
The seeds and leaves of the common thorn apple are dangerously toxic if ingested.

Related Terms

  • synonym: angel's trumpet - a related plant with similar characteristics, but usually referring to Brugmansia species
  • synonym: jimson weed - an alternative common name for the same plant, Datura stramonium
