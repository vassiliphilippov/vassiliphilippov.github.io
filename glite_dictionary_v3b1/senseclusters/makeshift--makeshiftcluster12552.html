makeshift

noun
temporary substitute; improvised solution
Pronunciation: / ˈmeɪkˌʃɪft / (AmE), / ˈmeɪkʃɪft / (BrE)
a temporary substitute or device used to meet an urgent need or emergency

Examples

A makeshift solution
During the blackout, candles served as a makeshift source of light.
We built a makeshift shelter out of branches when the tent was destroyed.
With the bridge out, a fallen tree became a makeshift crossing for hikers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: permanent - lasting or intended to last indefinitely
  • synonym: stopgap - a temporary way of dealing with a problem
