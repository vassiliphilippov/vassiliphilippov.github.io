mean

adjective
deliberately unkind or malicious
Pronunciation: / miːn / (AmE), / miːn / (BrE)
(of a person or behavior) unkind, cruel, or not generous; intending to hurt or upset others

Examples

A mean person
Don't be mean to your classmates just because you're upset.
He can be quite mean when he's in a bad mood.
It was mean of you to say such things about her.
He was known for his mean behavior toward colleagues who made mistakes.
It's mean to take advantage of someone's trust just for personal gain.
The novel portrays a mean and unscrupulous politician hungry for power.
It's mean to leave such a small tip after receiving excellent service.
She can be quite mean with her money, rarely buying anything for anyone.
The boss was so mean that he refused to provide even the simplest refreshments for the staff.

Related Terms

  • synonym: base - lacking moral principles; ignoble
  • synonym: cruel - causing pain or suffering to others deliberately
  • antonym: generous - (willing to give or share more than is strictly necessary)
  • antonym: kind - caring, considerate, and helpful
  • synonym: miserly - (showing lack of generosity; extremely unwilling to spend money)
  • antonym: noble - having or showing high moral qualities
  • synonym: stingy - (unwilling to spend or give; close-fisted)
