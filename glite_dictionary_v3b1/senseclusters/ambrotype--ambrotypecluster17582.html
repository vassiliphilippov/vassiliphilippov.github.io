ambrotype

noun
early glass photograph
Pronunciation: / ˈæmbroʊˌtaɪp / (AmE), / ˈæmbrəʊˌtaɪp / (BrE)
an early type of photograph made by creating a positive image on a glass plate, popular in the 1800s

Examples

A ambrotype photograph
The museum displayed a rare ambrotype of a Civil War soldier.
She inherited an ambrotype portrait of her great-grandparents from the 19th century.
Collectors value each ambrotype for its unique appearance and historical significance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: daguerreotype - an early photograph made on a silvered copper plate
  • synonym: tintype - a photograph made on a thin sheet of metal
