Lao-zi

proper noun
founder of Taoism; Chinese philosopher
Pronunciation: / laʊˈtsiː / (AmE), / laʊˈtsiː / (BrE)
A Chinese philosopher of the 6th century BC, regarded as the founder of Taoism and attributed author of the Dao De Jing.

Examples

Lao-zi teachings
Lao-zi's teachings form the foundation of Taoist philosophy.
Many scholars credit Lao-zi with writing the influential Dao De Jing.
The influence of Lao-zi continues to shape Chinese thought today.
