explant

noun
removed medical device
Pronunciation: / ˈɛksplænt / (AmE), / ˈɛksplɑːnt / (BrE)
a device, organ, or piece of tissue that has been removed from a body or organism for study or research

Examples

A tissue explant
The surgeon carefully removed the explant to analyze it for possible complications.
Researchers studied the explant to understand why the prosthesis had failed.
After the operation, the explant was sent to the laboratory for further testing.
The researcher placed the explant in a nutrient solution to observe its growth.
After removing the explant, the technician carefully transferred it to a sterile dish.
Scientists use a explant to study how cells develop outside the organism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: biopsy - a sample of tissue taken from a living body for examination
  • antonym: implant - a medical device or tissue placed inside the body
