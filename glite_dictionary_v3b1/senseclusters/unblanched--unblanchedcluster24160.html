unblanched

adjective
not boiled or whitened
8.6
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈblæntʃt / (AmE), / ʌnˈblɑːntʃt / (BrE)
not having been blanched, especially not briefly boiled or whitened as in food preparation

Examples

Unblanched almonds
The chef prefers to use unblanched almonds for a richer taste in the dessert.
You can buy unblanched spinach if you want to cook it yourself at home.
Some recipes specifically require unblanched nuts to preserve their natural flavor and texture.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blanched - briefly boiled or whitened, especially in cooking
