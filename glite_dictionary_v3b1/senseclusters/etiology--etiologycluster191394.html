etiology

noun
study of causation (philosophy)
Pronunciation: / ˌitiˈɑlədʒi /; / ˌiːtiˈɑːlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌiːtiˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
The philosophical study of the origins or causes of things, especially as a branch of philosophy concerned with causation.

Examples

Etiology of the universe
Discussions about the etiology of the universe are central to many philosophical traditions.
He devoted his doctoral research to the etiology of natural phenomena.
The course covers both historical and contemporary approaches to etiology in philosophy.
He wrote a treatise on the aetiology of natural phenomena.
Questions of aetiology are central to debates about the origins of the universe.
The discussion turned toward the aetiology of existence in ancient Greek philosophy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: causality - the relationship between cause and effect
