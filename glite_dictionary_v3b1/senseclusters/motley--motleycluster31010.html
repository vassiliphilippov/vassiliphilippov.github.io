motley

adjective
multicolored; variegated
7.4 0.02/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆmÉ‘tli / (AmE), / ËˆmÉ’tli / (BrE)
having sections or patches of different, usually bright, colors; multicolored or variegated in appearance

Examples

Motley colors
She wore a motley scarf that brightened up her winter coat.
The artist's studio was filled with a motley assortment of colorful fabrics and paints.
The parade featured a motley collection of flags and costumes from around the world.

Synonyms

  • multicolored
    2.9 1.17/m
    multicolored has many bright different colors, while motley suggests a patchwork mix often seen on costumes.
  • variegated
    7.2 1.13/m
    variegated shows patches, streaks, or spots of different colors, while motley means a bold patchy mix of bright colors.
  • pied
    7.9 0.07/m
    pied has two or more contrasting color patches, while motley refers to a more mixed, often brighter, patchwork of colors.
  • mottled
    6.3 1.26/m
    mottled is marked by irregular spots or blotches, often dull or subtle, while motley implies bright, deliberate patches.
