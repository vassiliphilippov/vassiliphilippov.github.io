kiwi

noun
climbing vine grown for its fruit
Pronunciation: / ˈkiːwi / (AmE), / ˈkiːwi / (BrE)
A climbing vine native to China and cultivated in New Zealand and elsewhere for its edible, fuzzy-skinned fruit with green flesh.

Examples

Kiwi vine
Botanists studied the rapid growth rate of the kiwi compared to other climbing vines.
Farmers in New Zealand grow kiwi vines for their tangy and nutritious fruit.
The kiwi plant thrives in temperate climates with plenty of sunlight and well-drained soil.

Related Terms
