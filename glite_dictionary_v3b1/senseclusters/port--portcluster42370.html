port

noun
surgically implanted venous access device
Pronunciation: / pɔrt / (AmE), / pɔːt / (BrE)
a small surgically implanted reservoir connected to a catheter that provides long-term access to a vein for repeated intravenous treatments, such as chemotherapy or frequent blood draws.

Examples

A medical port
The patient had a small port implanted under their skin before starting chemotherapy treatment.
Nurses accessed the port with a special needle to deliver the antibiotic quickly.
After months of use the doctor flushed the port to prevent clots and infection.

Related Terms

  • synonym: implantable port - an alternate term for a surgically implanted reservoir that provides long-term intravenous access.
  • synonym: port-a-cath - a common name for an implantable venous port used to deliver medication or take blood.
