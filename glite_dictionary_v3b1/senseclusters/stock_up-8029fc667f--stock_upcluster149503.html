stock up

verb
buy or gather a large supply
2.9 2.06/m
Pronunciation: / stÉ‘k ÊŒp / (AmE), / stÉ’k ÊŒp / (BrE)
to buy or collect a large supply of something to use in the future

Examples

To stock up supplies
Many people stocked up during the holiday sales.
She likes to stock up on supplies when there are discounts.
We should stock up on canned food before the storm arrives.

Synonyms

  • stockpile
    4.6 1.78/m
    stockpile is a formal act of keeping supplies for emergencies, while stock up is more casual buying for future use.
  • load up on
    4.5 0.69/m
    load up on suggests getting or eating large amounts now, while stock up means buying a supply to use later.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hoard - to collect and keep large amounts of something secretly
