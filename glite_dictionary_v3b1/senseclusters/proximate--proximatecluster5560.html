proximate

adjective
nearest in space, time, or order
7.3 1.16/m
Pronunciation: / Ààpr…ëÀêks…ôm…ôt /; / Ààpr…ëÀêk.s…™.m…ôt / (AmE), / Ààpr…íks…™m…ôt /; / Ààpr…ík.s…™.m…ôt / (BrE)
nearest or closest in space, time, order, or relationship

Examples

The proximate cause
Her proximate goal was to finish her degree before moving abroad.
Scientists focused on the proximate factors influencing animal behavior.
The proximate cause of the accident was a faulty brake.
The proximate cause of the confusion was a poorly worded email.
The store is proximate to the library, so we can visit both easily.
The two buildings have proximate entrances, making it easy to move between them.

Synonyms

  • near
    1.0 27/m
    near describes being close in distance or time in everyday speech, while proximate is more formal and technical.
  • close
    1.0 274/m
    close means very near in space, time, or feeling in common use, while proximate sounds more formal and exact.
  • next
    1.0 54/m
    next refers to something immediately beside or coming right after with no gap, while proximate means nearest but not always adjacent.
