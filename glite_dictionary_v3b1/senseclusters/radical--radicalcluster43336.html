radical

adjective
relating to a linguistic root
Pronunciation: / ˈrædɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈrædɪkəl / (BrE)
(Linguistics) relating to or constituting a root, especially a linguistic root in a word or word form.

Examples

Radical form
In Hebrew, every word can be traced back to its radical form.
Some dictionaries list radical meanings separately from affixed forms.
The radical structure of the verb determines its conjugation.
