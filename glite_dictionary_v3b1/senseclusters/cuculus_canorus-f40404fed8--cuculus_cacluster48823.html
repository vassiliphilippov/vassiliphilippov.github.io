Cuculus canorus

noun
scientific name for the common cuckoo
Pronunciation: / kuːˈkuːləs kəˈnoʊrəs / (AmE), / kjuːˈkjuːləs kəˈnɔːrəs / (BrE)
The scientific name for the common cuckoo, a European bird known for its distinctive two-note call and habit of laying eggs in the nests of other birds.

Examples

Cuculus canorus bird
Researchers studied the breeding behavior of Cuculus canorus in various habitats across Europe.
The call of the Cuculus canorus is a familiar sound in the European countryside during spring.
Unlike other birds, Cuculus canorus lays its eggs in the nests of different species.
