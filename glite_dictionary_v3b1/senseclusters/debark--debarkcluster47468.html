debark

verb
to remove tree bark
Pronunciation: / diˈbɑrk / (AmE), / diˈbɑːk / (BrE)
to remove the bark from a tree or log

Examples

To debark a log
The workers debark the logs before sending them to the sawmill.
It is important to debark the tree trunks to prevent insect infestation.
After they debarked the wood, it was ready for construction.
The workers debark the logs before they are processed at the sawmill.
It is important to debark the tree carefully to avoid damaging the wood underneath.
Modern machines can debark hundreds of logs in a single day.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bark - the outer covering of a tree
  • synonym: strip - to remove a layer or covering from something
