birth trauma

noun
mother's psychological distress after childbirth
5.8
Pronunciation: / bɝθ ˈtrɔmə / (AmE), / bɜːθ ˈtrɔːmə / (BrE)
psychological distress experienced by the birthing parent during childbirth or the postpartum period, often involving anxiety, intrusive memories, avoidance, or symptoms like post‑traumatic stress.

Examples

Mother's birth trauma
After the emergency cesarean, she was diagnosed with birth trauma and began therapy.
Many mothers report birth trauma due to intense pain and lack of communication during labor.
The hospital introduced a support group for women recovering from birth trauma after difficult deliveries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: postpartum PTSD - post‑traumatic stress disorder that can develop after a traumatic childbirth experience
  • synonym: traumatic birth - a childbirth event experienced as traumatic by the mother or birthing parent
