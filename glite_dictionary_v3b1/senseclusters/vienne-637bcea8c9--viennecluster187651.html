Vienne

noun
Church council (1311–1313) addressing Knights Templar and reform
Pronunciation: / viˈɛn / (AmE), / viˈɛn / (BrE)
an ecumenical council held from 1311–1313 that addressed alleged crimes of the Knights Templar, planned a new crusade, and sought reformation of the clergy

Examples

Vienne Council
Historians often debate the outcomes of Vienne regarding its intended crusade.
Many reforms were proposed during the Vienne council to address clergy misconduct.
The Council of Vienne marked a turning point in the history of the Knights Templar.
