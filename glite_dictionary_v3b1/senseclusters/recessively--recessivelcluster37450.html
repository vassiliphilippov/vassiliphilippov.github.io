recessively

adverb
in a recessive way
8.5
Pronunciation: /rɪ.ˈses.ɪv.li/ /; / /rə.ˈses.ɪv.li/ (AmE), /rɪ.ˈses.ɪv.li/ /; / /rə.ˈses.ɪv.li/ (BrE)
in a manner relating to or characteristic of a recessive gene or trait, especially in genetics.

Examples

Inherited recessively
The disorder is passed on recessively, so both parents must carry the gene for a child to be affected.
Some genetic conditions are inherited recessively and may not appear in every generation.
If a trait is expressed recessively, it will only show when two copies of the gene are present.
