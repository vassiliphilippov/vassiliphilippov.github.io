regent

noun
one who governs in a monarch's absence
8.4 0.73/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàriÀêd í…ônt / (AmE), / ÀàriÀêd í…ônt / (BrE)
a person who governs a country in place of a monarch who is absent, incapacitated, or too young to rule

Examples

Regent of the kingdom
Queen Sophia served as regent during her son's minority.
The council appointed a regent to rule until the prince came of age.
When the king fell seriously ill, a trusted noble acted as regent for the realm.
