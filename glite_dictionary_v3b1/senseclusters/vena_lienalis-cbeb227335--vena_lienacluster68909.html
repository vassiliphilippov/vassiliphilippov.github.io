vena lienalis

noun
vein draining the spleen
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈviː.nə liː.ə.ˈnæl.ɪs/ /; / /ˈviː.nə liː.ə.ˈneɪ.lɪs/ /; / /ˈviː.nə lɪ.ə.ˈnæl.ɪs/ (AmE), /ˈviː.nə liː.ə.ˈnæl.ɪs/ /; / /ˈviː.nə liː.ə.ˈneɪ.lɪs/ /; / /ˈviː.nə lɪ.ə.ˈnæl.ɪs/ (BrE)
a vein formed by several small veins on the surface of the spleen that joins the superior mesenteric vein to form the portal vein

Examples

The vena lienalis vein
Blockage of the vena lienalis can result in complications due to impaired blood flow.
During abdominal surgery, care was taken not to damage the vena lienalis.
The vena lienalis plays an important role in directing blood from the spleen to the liver.
