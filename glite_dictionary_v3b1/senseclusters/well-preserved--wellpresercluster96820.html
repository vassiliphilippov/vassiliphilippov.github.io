well-preserved

adjective
healthy or youthful despite age
Pronunciation: / ˌwɛl pɚˈzɝvd / (AmE), / ˌwɛl pəˈzɜːvd / (BrE)
(of a person) still in good health or appearance, especially considering their age.

Examples

A well-preserved person
Despite being in his seventies, John is remarkably well-preserved and enjoys hiking every weekend.
Many actors and actresses manage to stay well-preserved thanks to a healthy lifestyle and good genetics.
She was complimented on how well-preserved she looked for her age at the family reunion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: frail - weak or not strong, especially because of age
  • synonym: youthful - having the qualities of youth; appearing young
