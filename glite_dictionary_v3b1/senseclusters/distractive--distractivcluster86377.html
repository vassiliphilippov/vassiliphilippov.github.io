distractive

adjective
causing separation
Pronunciation: / dɪˈstræktɪv / (AmE), / dɪˈstræktɪv / (BrE)
causing separation or pulling apart by producing a force that moves parts away from each other, especially in technical or medical contexts

Examples

Distractive force applied
A distractive force was applied to gradually widen the gap between the bone fragments.
The device uses a distractive mechanism to separate the vertebrae during surgery.
Engineers designed a distractive tool to pull the components apart safely.

Related Terms

  • antonym: compressive - causing parts to be pressed or pushed together
