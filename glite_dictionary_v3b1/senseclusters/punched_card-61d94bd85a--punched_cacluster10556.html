punched card

noun
card for data storage via punched holes
8.4 0.08/m
Pronunciation: / p ånt Ét k…ërd / (AmE), / p ånt Ét k…ëÀêd / (BrE)
A stiff card on which data is stored in the form of patterns of holes, used especially in early computing and data processing systems.

Examples

Early punched cards
Early computers used punched cards to input both data and programs.
He sorted through a stack of punched cards, searching for the one he needed.
The museum displayed an antique punched card machine among its exhibits.

Synonyms

  • punch card
    7.4 0.48/m
    punch card is the common everyday name for a stiff card with data holes used in computing, while punched card is more formal and historical.
  • Hollerith card
    9.9 0.00/m
    Hollerith card refers to the inventor's early brand of punched hole cards used for tabulating, while punched card is the general term for such data cards.
