plantar reflex

noun
reflex causing toe flexion when sole is stroked
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈplæn.tɑr rɪˈflɛks / (AmE), / ˈplæn.tɑː rɪˈflɛks / (BrE)
a reflex movement in which the toes flex when the sole of the foot is stroked firmly, seen normally in adults

Examples

The plantar reflex test
An abnormal plantar reflex in adults may indicate a neurological problem.
During the examination, the physician noted a normal plantar reflex response in both feet.
The doctor checked the plantar reflex by running a blunt instrument along the bottom of my foot.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Babinski reflex - extension of the toes in response to stroking the sole, abnormal in adults
