ineffectually

In a way that does not produce the intended result; without success or effectiveness.

Examples

To wave ineffectually
He waved his arms ineffectually, unable to get anyone's attention.
She argued ineffectually against the company's new policy.
The team worked ineffectually to solve the urgent problem.

Synonyms

  • inefficaciously
    9.9 0.00/m
    inefficaciously is a formal, rare adverb for failing to produce results, while ineffectually is more common and neutral.
  • futilely
    7.4 0.50/m
    futilely means trying in a way that has no useful result, while ineffectually focuses on lack of success.
  • uselessly
    3.5 0.63/m
    uselessly suggests doing something with no use or benefit, while ineffectually highlights failure to achieve a goal.
  • abortively
    7.9 0.01/m
    abortively describes attempts that stop or fail before success, while ineffectually describes actions that simply do not work.
  • lamely
    5.6 0.73/m
    lamely means doing something weakly or unconvincingly, while ineffectually covers general failure without the idea of weakness.
  • unavailingly
