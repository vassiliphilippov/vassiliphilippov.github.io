whole-wheat

adjective
made from the whole wheat grain
1.0 2.4
Pronunciation: /ˈhoʊl.wiːt/ /; / /ˈhoʊl.hwiːt/ (AmE), /ˈhəʊl.wiːt/ /; / /ˈhəʊl.hwiːt/ (BrE)
(of food) made from wheat that contains all the parts of the grain, especially the bran and germ.

Examples

Whole-wheat bread
For a healthier option, try using whole-wheat pasta in your recipes.
She prefers whole-wheat bread because it is higher in fiber.
The bakery offered a selection of whole-wheat muffins and pastries.

Related Terms

  • antonym: refined - (of food) processed to remove bran and germ
  • synonym: wholemeal - made from flour containing all the natural parts of the grain
