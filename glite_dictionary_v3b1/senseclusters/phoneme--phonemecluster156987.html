phoneme

noun
distinct unit of sound in language
6.4 5.7
Pronunciation: /ˈfoʊ.niːm/ (AmE), /ˈfəʊ.niːm/ (BrE)
(Linguistics) One of the distinct units of sound in a specified language that distinguishes one word from another, such as the sound /p/ in 'pat' and /b/ in 'bat'.

Examples

A phoneme sound
Children often struggle to distinguish every phoneme when learning to read.
In English, the words 'bat' and 'pat' are distinguished by a single phoneme.
Linguists study how each phoneme functions to differentiate words in a language.
