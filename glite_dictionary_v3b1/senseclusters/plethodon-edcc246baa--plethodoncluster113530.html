Plethodon

noun
genus of North American lungless salamanders
Pronunciation: / ˈplɛθəˌdɑn /; / ˈplɛθ.əˌdɑn / (AmE), / ˈplɛθədɒn /; / ˈplɛθ.əˌdɒn / (BrE)
a genus of lungless salamanders native to North America, which is the type genus of the family Plethodontidae

Examples

Plethodon salamanders
Scientists discovered a new species within the Plethodon genus during the survey.
The Plethodon genus includes many species found in forests across eastern North America.
Unlike many salamanders, members of Plethodon do not have lungs and rely on cutaneous respiration.
Members of the Plethodon genus breathe through their skin, as they lack lungs.
Scientists discovered a new Plethodon salamander in the Appalachian Mountains.
The Plethodon species are often found hiding under logs in moist forests.
