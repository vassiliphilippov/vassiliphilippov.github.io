deposit

noun
initial part-payment of total price
Pronunciation: / dɪˈpɑzɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈpɒzɪt / (BrE)
an amount of money paid in advance, as security, or placed in a bank for safekeeping or to earn interest

Examples

A bank deposit
A $200 deposit is required before we process your order.
She left a deposit at the hotel and paid the rest at checkout.
You need to pay a deposit to reserve the apartment.
After each paycheck, he adds a small deposit to his bank balance.
She made a deposit of $500 into her savings account.
Your initial deposit is required to open the account.
A $200 deposit is required to reserve the venue for your event.
Her deposit will be returned as long as the equipment is undamaged.
You must pay a deposit when you rent the apartment.

  • synonym: savings - money set aside and typically held in a bank account
  • antonym: withdrawal - the act of taking money out of a bank account
