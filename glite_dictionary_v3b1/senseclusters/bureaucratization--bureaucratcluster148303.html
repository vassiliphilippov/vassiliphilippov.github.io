bureaucratization

noun
process of adding bureaucracy
Pronunciation: / bjʊˌrɑːkrətaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / bjʊəˌrɒkrətaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making an organization or system more controlled by bureaucracy, with more rules, procedures, and hierarchy

Examples

The bureaucratization process
The bureaucratization of the school system led to more paperwork for teachers.
Rapid bureaucratization can make it harder for companies to adapt to change.
Some employees complained that bureaucratization slowed down decision-making in the organization.
The bureaucratization of the healthcare system made it harder for patients to get quick treatment.
Many employees were frustrated by the increasing bureaucratization of company procedures.
Some reforms aimed to reduce the bureaucratization that slowed down government services.

Related Terms

  • synonym: centralization - the process of concentrating control under a single authority
  • synonym: red tape - excessive bureaucracy or rigid rules
  • antonym: streamlining - the process of making an organization simpler and more efficient
