daemon

noun
part-mortal, part-god being
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪ.mən / (AmE), / ˈdiː.mən / (BrE)
a being who is part mortal and part god, often found in ancient mythology and literature

Examples

Ancient daemon guardian
In Greek mythology, a daemon could act as a guardian spirit for individuals or places.
Some ancient cultures believed a daemon could influence a person's fate or character.
The hero of the story was guided by a wise daemon who was part god, part human.

Related Terms

  • synonym: demigod - a being with one divine and one mortal parent
