moly

noun
lubricant or alloy additive
9.4 0.24/m
Pronunciation: / Ààm…ëÀêli / (AmE), / Ààm…íli / (BrE)
an informal term for molybdenum or molybdenum disulfide, especially when used as a lubricant or alloy additive

Examples

Moly grease
He applied a small amount of moly to the gears to ensure smooth operation.
Some high-performance engines use moly additives to reduce wear and friction.
The mechanic recommended using moly grease for the moving parts of the machine.

Synonyms

  • molybdenum
    9.2 0.52/m
    molybdenum is a hard silver metal used to strengthen steel and alloys, while moly is an informal name for the element or its disulfide.
  • molybdenite
    9.9 0.00/m
    molybdenite is a soft lead-gray mineral and the main ore of molybdenum, while moly is an informal name for that mineral or the metal.
