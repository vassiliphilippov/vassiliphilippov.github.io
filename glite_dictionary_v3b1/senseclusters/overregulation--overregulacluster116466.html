overregulation

noun
excessive rules
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˌrɛɡjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˌrɛɡjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the situation in which there are too many rules or restrictions, making activities unnecessarily difficult or complicated

Examples

Too much overregulation
Many small businesses fail due to overregulation that increases their operating costs.
Critics argue that overregulation stifles innovation and discourages new companies from entering the market.
The government promised to review policies to reduce overregulation in the financial sector.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deregulation - the removal or reduction of rules and restrictions
  • degree lower: regulation - the act of controlling or governing by rules
