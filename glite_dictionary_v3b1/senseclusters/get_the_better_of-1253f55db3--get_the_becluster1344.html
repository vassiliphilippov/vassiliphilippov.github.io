get the better of

verb
defeat; prevail over
Pronunciation: / ɡɛt ðə ˈbɛtər əv / (AmE), / ɡɛt ðə ˈbɛtə rɒv / (BrE)
to defeat or gain control over someone or something, especially when it is hard to resist

Examples

To get the better of anger
Despite his efforts to stay calm, anger got the better of him during the meeting.
Her curiosity got the better of her, so she opened the mysterious box.
The team's nerves got the better of them in the final minutes of the match.
Don’t let your impatience get the better of you in difficult situations.
She tried to stay calm, but her nerves got the better of her before the interview.
Sometimes curiosity gets the better of me and I have to ask questions.
