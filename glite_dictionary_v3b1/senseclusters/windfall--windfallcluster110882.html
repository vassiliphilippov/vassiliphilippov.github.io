windfall

noun
fruit fallen from a tree
5.3 4.2
Pronunciation: /ˈwɪnd.fɔːl/ (AmE), /ˈwɪnd.fɔːl/ (BrE)
A piece of fruit that has fallen from a tree, especially due to the wind rather than being picked; sometimes gathered and used as food.

Examples

Windfall apples
She used windfall pears to make jam.
The ground was covered in windfall plums after last night's storm.
We collected several windfalls from beneath the old apple tree.
