comorbid

adjective
simultaneous conditions
6.6
Pronunciation: / koʊˈmɔrbɪd / (AmE), / kəʊˈmɔːbɪd / (BrE)
describing two or more medical conditions that exist at the same time in a person

Examples

Comorbid medical conditions
Patients with diabetes are often comorbid with high blood pressure.
The study found that many children with ADHD were also comorbid for learning disabilities.
Doctors must consider comorbid conditions when planning a treatment strategy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: concurrent - existing or happening at the same time
