alkene

noun
unsaturated hydrocarbon with a double bond
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˈælˌkin / (AmE), / ˈalkiːn / (BrE)
a compound consisting of an unsaturated aliphatic hydrocarbon with at least one carbon–carbon double bond

Examples

A alkene compound
Ethylene is the simplest alkene, containing just two carbon atoms and a double bond.
Many industrial chemicals are produced from alkenes through different reactions.
The presence of a carbon–carbon double bond distinguishes an alkene from an alkane.

Related Terms

  • antonym: alkane - saturated hydrocarbon with single bonds
  • degree higher: alkyne - unsaturated hydrocarbon with a triple bond
