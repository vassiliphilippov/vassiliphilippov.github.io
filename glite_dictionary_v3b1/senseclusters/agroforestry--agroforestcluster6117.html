agroforestry

noun
farming with trees
5.4
Pronunciation: /ˈæ.ɡroʊ.fɑːr.ɪ.striː/ /; / /ˈæ.ɡroʊ.fɔːr.ɪ.striː/ (AmE), /ˈæ.ɡrəʊ.fɒr.ɪ.striː/ (BrE)
a way of managing land that combines growing trees with crops (and sometimes animals) on the same area to improve the environment and productivity

Examples

Agroforestry land use
Many farmers are turning to agroforestry to increase biodiversity and improve soil health.
The government supports agroforestry as a sustainable way to use agricultural land.
By practicing agroforestry, communities can produce both food and timber on the same land.
Many farmers are adopting agroforestry to increase biodiversity and improve soil health.
The university offers courses in agroforestry as part of its environmental science program.
By practicing agroforestry, communities can produce food and timber while protecting the environment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: silviculture - the practice of managing and cultivating forests
