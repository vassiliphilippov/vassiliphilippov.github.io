scrub

noun
dense growth of stunted bushes or trees
6.2 3.48/m
Pronunciation: / skrÊŒb / (AmE), / skrÊŒb / (BrE)
Vegetation consisting of stunted trees or bushes, often forming dense, low thickets, especially in arid or semi-arid areas.

Examples

Scrub bushes
Many animals find shelter in the scrub along the riverbanks.
Much of the land was covered in thick scrub that made walking difficult.
The fire quickly spread through the dry scrub near the village.

Synonyms

  • scrubland
    6.6 0.20/m
    scrubland is land covered in low, stunted shrubs and bushes, while scrub refers to that dense, low vegetation.
  • shrubland
    4.6 0.13/m
    shrubland is mainly made of shrubs over large areas, while scrub often describes smaller, denser thickets of low plants.
  • dry brush
    4.7 0.04/m
    dry brush is low, dry shrubby undergrowth in arid places, while scrub is a general term for such stunted vegetation.
  • brush
    2.2 8.09/m
    brush is a dense growth of bushes or small trees in many climates, while scrub implies drier, more stunted thickets.
  • bush
    3.7 10/m
    bush is a single shrub or a wild area of small plants, while scrub names wider, low dense thickets often in dry regions.
