review

verb
examine or look at again
1.0
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvju /; / ɹɪˈvju / (AmE), / rɪˈvjuː /; / ɹɪˈvjuː / (BrE)
to examine or assess something again, especially to check details, make changes, or form an opinion (including official, medical, legal, or military contexts)

Examples

To review a report
He will review the security footage to check for any suspicious activity.
Let's review the agenda before starting the meeting.
The teacher asked the students to review their essays for any errors.
Each year, the president reviews the armed forces during the national ceremony.
The colonel reviewed the soldiers, observing their uniforms and equipment in detail.
The general will review the troops tomorrow morning during the military parade.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ignore - to pay no attention to something
  • synonym: inspect - to look at something carefully in order to learn more about it or find problems
  • synonym: reexamine - to examine something again
