loose change

noun
small coins, typically of low value
3.1 0.34/m
Pronunciation: / lus t Ée…™nd í / (AmE), / luÀês t Ée…™nd í / (BrE)
coins of small value that are not arranged or stored neatly, often kept in a pocket or scattered in various places.

Examples

A handful of loose change
After cleaning her bag, Maria found a handful of loose change.
He emptied his pockets and put all his loose change into a jar.
I always keep some loose change in my car for parking meters.

Synonyms

    2.5 0.29/m
    pocket change is coins someone carries for small expenses in a pocket, while loose change is scattered or not neatly stored.
  • spare change
    1.0 0.33/m
    spare change is small money left over and carried casually, while loose change refers to coins kept scattered and not neatly stored.
  • small change
    2.3 1.00/m
    small change means a tiny or unimportant amount of money, while loose change means actual coins often scattered in pockets.
