headstrong

adjective
stubborn; willful person

(of a person) determined to do as one wants, often in spite of advice or warnings; willful or stubborn.

Examples

A headstrong person
Despite everyone's warnings, the headstrong boy went ahead with his plan.
His headstrong behavior often got him into trouble at work.
She was so headstrong that she refused to listen to her teacher's advice.

Synonyms

  • self-willed
    7.3 0.04/m
    self-willed is determined to follow one's own wishes even against advice, while headstrong often acts without listening to warnings.
  • willful
    5.5 1.87/m
    willful describes deliberate refusal to obey rules or advice, while headstrong is more about strong personal stubbornness.
  • stubborn
    2.9 8.86/m
    stubborn means refusing to change ideas or actions, while headstrong suggests doing what one wants despite warnings.
  • obstinate
    7.5 1.15/m
    obstinate refuses to change opinion even with reasoned argument, while headstrong often acts without taking advice.
  • hardheaded
    6.7 0.20/m
    hardheaded is firmly practical and not easily influenced, while headstrong is driven by will and may ignore advice.
  • bullheaded
    7.4 0.09/m
    bullheaded is stubborn in a foolish, aggressive way, while headstrong is determined and may ignore advice.
