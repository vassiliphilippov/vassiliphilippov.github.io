illocutionary

adjective
speech act related
9.9 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / Àå…™lo äÀàkjuÀê É…ôÀån…õri / (AmE), / Àå…™l…ôÀàkjuÀê É…ôn…ôri / (BrE)
relating to an act performed by speaking, where saying the words accomplishes the action itself, such as promising or declaring.

Examples

Illocutionary act
The phrase 'I apologize' is an illocutionary act because saying it performs the apology.
In linguistics, researchers study the illocutionary force of different statements.
A judge's declaration in court can have an illocutionary effect, such as pronouncing a verdict.

Related Terms

  • synonym: performative - relating to an utterance that performs an action
  • antonym: perlocutionary - relating to the effect an utterance has on a listener
