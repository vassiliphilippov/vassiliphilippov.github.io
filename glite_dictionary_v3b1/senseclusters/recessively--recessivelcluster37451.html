recessively

adverb
in a less prominent way
7.9
Pronunciation: / rɪˈsɛsɪvli / (AmE), / rɪˈsɛsɪvli / (BrE)
in a way that is not prominent, dominant, or easily noticed; in the background or less conspicuously.

Examples

Colors blend recessively
The colors in the background blend recessively so that the main subject stands out.
He always preferred to act recessively in group projects, letting others take the spotlight.
The supporting characters appear recessively in the film, drawing little attention from the audience.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conspicuously - in a way that is easily seen or attracts attention
  • synonym: subtly - in a delicate or not obvious way
