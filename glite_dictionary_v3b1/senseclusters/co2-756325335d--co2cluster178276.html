CO2

noun
carbon dioxide gas
Pronunciation: / si oʊ tu / (AmE), / siː əʊ tuː / (BrE)
carbon dioxide; a heavy, colorless, odorless gas produced by respiration and decomposition, and absorbed by plants in photosynthesis.

Examples

CO2 gas
Burning fossil fuels releases large amounts of CO2 into the atmosphere.
Plants absorb CO2 during the process of photosynthesis.
Reducing CO2 emissions is essential for combating climate change.
