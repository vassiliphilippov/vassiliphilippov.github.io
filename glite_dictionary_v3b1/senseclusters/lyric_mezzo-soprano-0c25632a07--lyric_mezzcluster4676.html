lyric mezzo-soprano

noun
mezzo-soprano with light, expressive voice
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪrɪk ˌmɛtsoʊ səˈprɑːnoʊ / (AmE), / ˈlɪrɪk ˌmɛtsə səˈprɑːnəʊ / (BrE)
A type of mezzo-soprano singer characterized by a light, warm vocal quality and a range typically from G3 to A5, suited for expressive, melodic roles.

Examples

A lyric mezzo-soprano singer
Many roles in French opera call for a lyric mezzo-soprano rather than a dramatic voice.
She was cast as Carmen because her lyric mezzo-soprano suited the role's expressive demands.
The audition panel praised his daughter's lyric mezzo-soprano for its warmth and clarity.
