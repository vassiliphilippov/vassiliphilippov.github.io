set up

verb
to frame or ensnare someone
2.2
Pronunciation: / sɛt ʌp / (AmE), / sɛt ʌp / (BrE)
to trick someone or make them appear guilty of something they did not do, especially by deliberate deception

Examples

To set up someone
He claimed that he was set up by his former business partner.
She realized too late that the entire incident had been set up to discredit her.
The journalist was set up with false evidence and arrested.
She realized too late that they had set up the whole situation to make her look guilty.
The documentary revealed how several politicians were set up in a bribery scandal.
The police arrested him, but he claims he was set up by his business rivals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exonerate - to officially clear someone from blame or fault
  • synonym: frame - to make someone appear guilty of a crime they did not commit
