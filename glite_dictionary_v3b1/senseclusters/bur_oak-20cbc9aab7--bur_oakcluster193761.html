bur oak

noun
a North American oak tree with fringed acorns
Pronunciation: / bɜr oʊk / (AmE), / bɜː əʊk /; / bɜː əʊk / (BrE)
A large deciduous oak tree native to central and eastern North America, known for its deeply fringed acorn cups and strong, close-grained wood.

Examples

A bur oak tree
Many city parks have planted bur oak trees for their shade and resilience.
The bur oak is easy to identify by its thick bark and distinctive acorns.
The wood of the bur oak is valued for its durability and strength.
The old farm was shaded by a massive burr oak with wide spreading branches.
The tough wood of the burr oak is highly valued for making furniture.
We collected several burr oak acorns to plant in the garden.
