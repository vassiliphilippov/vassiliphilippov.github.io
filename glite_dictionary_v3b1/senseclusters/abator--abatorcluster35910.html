abator

noun
person who removes a nuisance
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈbeɪtər / (AmE), / əˈbeɪtə / (BrE)
a person who removes or puts an end to a nuisance, especially in legal contexts

Examples

The abator ends nuisance
An abator may enter the property to put an end to the public nuisance.
As the designated abator, she was responsible for eliminating the noise complaint.
The court determined that the abator acted within his rights to remove the hazardous waste.
