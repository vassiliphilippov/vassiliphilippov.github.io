smoking gun

noun
conclusive evidence of wrongdoing
5.6 4.3
Pronunciation: /ˈsmoʊ.kɪŋ gʌn/ (AmE), /ˈsməʊ.kɪŋ gʌn/ (BrE)
conclusive or clear evidence that proves someone has committed a crime or done something wrong

Examples

The smoking gun evidence
Her confession was the smoking gun the jury needed to reach a verdict.
Investigators searched for a smoking gun to tie the suspect directly to the crime.
The leaked emails became the smoking gun that implicated the officials in the scandal.
Journalists searched for a smoking gun in the leaked emails but found nothing conclusive.
The investigation turned up a smoking gun that linked the company to illegal activity.
They had suspicions for years, but the video finally provided the smoking gun.
