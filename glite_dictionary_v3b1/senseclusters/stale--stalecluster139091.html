stale

verb
to lose freshness
Pronunciation: / steɪl / (AmE), / steɪl / (BrE)
to become old, no longer fresh, or less interesting or appealing

Examples

Bread stales quickly
The bread staled overnight and was not good for sandwiches.
If you leave cookies uncovered, they will quickly stale and lose their crunch.
His jokes have staled over time and no longer make people laugh.

Related Terms

  • antonym: freshen - to make or become fresh again
  • synonym: spoil - to become unfit for use or consumption
