prophecy

noun
knowledge of the future from a divine source
6.8 5.06/m
Pronunciation: / Ààpr…ëÀê.f…ô.si / (AmE), / Ààpr…íf.…ô.si / (BrE)
knowledge of the future, especially as revealed by a divine source

Examples

Divine prophecy
He claimed that his dreams were a form of prophecy from the gods.
Many ancient cultures believed that the prophecy foretold the rise of a great leader.
The villagers lived in fear that the old prophecy about disaster would come true.

Synonyms

  • vaticination
    9.9 0.01/m
    vaticination is the act of foretelling events as a divine gift, while prophecy describes knowledge of the future revealed by a god.
  • oracle
    9.1 0.79/m
    an oracle is a message given by a priest and often sounds obscure, while prophecy is broader divine knowledge of the future.
  • prevision
    9.9 0.00/m
    prevision is a prophetic vision or dream that shows future images, while prophecy is knowledge revealed by a divine source.
  • prognostication
    9.3 0.59/m
    prognostication is a prediction based on signs or analysis and can be worldly, while prophecy implies divine revelation about the future.
  • foreknowledge
    8.9 0.64/m
    foreknowledge is knowing about an event beforehand in general, while prophecy points to divinely revealed future knowledge.
