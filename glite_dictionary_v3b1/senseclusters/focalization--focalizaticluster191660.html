focalization

noun
narrative perspective
8.8
Pronunciation: /foʊ.kəl.ə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /fəʊ.kəl.ə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the perspective or point of view from which a story or narrative is presented

Examples

Focalization in narration
The focalization in this novel changes from one character to another in each chapter.
Literary critics often analyze the focalization to understand whose perspective shapes the narrative.
By shifting focalization, the author reveals different sides of the story to the reader.
