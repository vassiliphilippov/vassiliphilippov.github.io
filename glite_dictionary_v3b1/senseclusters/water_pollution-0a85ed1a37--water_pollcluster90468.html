water pollution

noun
contamination of water
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtɚ pəˈluʃən / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə pəˈluːʃən / (BrE)
the contamination of water in rivers, lakes, or other bodies, typically by human activities, making it unsafe or harmful for use

Examples

Water pollution in rivers
Industrial waste is a major contributor to water pollution in many countries.
Oil spills often result in severe water pollution affecting both wildlife and humans.
Strict regulations have helped reduce water pollution in urban rivers.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: air pollution - pollution of the air by harmful substances
  • synonym: pollution - the presence or introduction of harmful substances into the environment
