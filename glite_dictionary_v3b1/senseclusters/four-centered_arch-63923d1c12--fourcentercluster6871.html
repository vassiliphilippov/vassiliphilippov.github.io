four-centered arch

noun
type of elliptical or pointed arch
5.1
Pronunciation: / fɔr ˈsɛntɚd ɑrtʃ / (AmE), / fɔː ˈsɛntəd ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
An arch with a low, elliptical or pointed form, produced by drawing arcs from four different centers; commonly used in Gothic architecture.

Examples

A four-centered arch design
Architects admired the decorative use of the four-centered arch in historic buildings.
The entrance to the chapel features a four-centered arch typical of the Tudor style.
The use of a four-centered arch allows for a wider span and a unique aesthetic.
