baas

noun
South African term for 'boss' or person in authority
Pronunciation: / bɑs / (AmE), / bɑːs / (BrE)
(in South African English) a boss or person in authority, especially a white employer over Black workers during the apartheid era.

Examples

The baas in apartheid
During apartheid, workers had to obey the baas without question.
He called his baas to report the completion of the task.
She addressed her manager as baas in accordance with local custom.
