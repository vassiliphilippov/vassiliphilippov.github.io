conversive

adjective
reciprocal relationship
9.9
Pronunciation: / kənˈvɜrsɪv / (AmE), / kənˈvɜːsɪv / (BrE)
describing a relationship between two terms that refer to the same situation from different perspectives

Examples

Conversive verbs
In linguistics, 'buy' and 'sell' are conversive verbs because they describe the same event from different viewpoints.
The terms 'teacher' and 'student' form a conversive pair in educational contexts.
Understanding conversive relationships helps clarify how words relate to each other in meaning.
