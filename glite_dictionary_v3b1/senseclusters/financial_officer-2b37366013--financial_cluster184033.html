financial officer

noun
person who manages financial transactions
Pronunciation: / fəˈnænʃəl ˈɑfəsər / (AmE), / faɪˈnænʃəl ˈɒfɪsə / (BrE)
an officer responsible for managing the receipt and disbursement of funds in an organization

Examples

Financial officer manages money
As a financial officer, she ensures that all transactions are recorded accurately and transparently.
Every major corporation employs a financial officer to oversee monetary operations.
The financial officer presented the annual budget report to the board of directors.
