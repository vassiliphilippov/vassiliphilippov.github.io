preadapted

adjective
suited for future change
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːəˈdæptɪd / (AmE), / ˌpriːəˈdæptɪd / (BrE)
having characteristics that make something suitable for a future change or environment before that change occurs

Examples

Preadapted plants
Certain plants are preadapted to thrive in dry climates due to their waxy leaves.
The company was preadapted for remote work because it already used digital tools.
Some animals are preadapted to survive in colder environments thanks to their thick fur.

Related Terms

  • synonym: prepared - ready for something in advance
  • antonym: unsuited - not appropriate or suitable for a particular purpose
