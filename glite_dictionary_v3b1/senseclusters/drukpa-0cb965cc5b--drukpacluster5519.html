Drukpa

proper noun
Tibetan Buddhist lineage
Pronunciation: / ˈdrʊk.pə / (AmE), / ˈdrʊk.pə / (BrE)
the Drukpa Kagyu school or lineage of Tibetan Buddhism, known for its monasteries and spiritual tradition

Examples

Drukpa lineage
The Drukpa lineage is especially influential in Bhutan, where it is the state religion.
Many monks in the Drukpa tradition dedicate their lives to meditation and teaching.
Pilgrims visit Drukpa monasteries to learn about Buddhist philosophy and practice.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Gelug - another main school of Tibetan Buddhism
  • synonym: Kagyu - a major school of Tibetan Buddhism
