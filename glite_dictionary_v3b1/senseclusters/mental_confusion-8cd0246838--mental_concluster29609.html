mental confusion

noun
disorder of thought, disorientation
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛn.tl̩ kənˈfju.ʒən / (AmE), / ˈmɛn.tl kənˈfjuː.ʒən / (BrE)
A mental state characterized by a lack of clear and orderly thought and behavior, often resulting in disorientation or difficulty thinking clearly.

Examples

Mental confusion during illness
Elderly people are sometimes more prone to mental confusion during illness.
Periods of mental confusion can make it difficult to complete daily tasks.
The patient showed signs of mental confusion after receiving anesthesia.

Related Terms

  • antonym: clarity - quality of being clear or easy to understand
  • synonym: disorientation - loss of sense of direction or identity
