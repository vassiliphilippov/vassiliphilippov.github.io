synthetic

adjective
involving synthesis or combination
4.7 4.1
Pronunciation: /sɪn.ˈθe.tɪk/ (AmE), /sɪn.ˈθe.tɪk/ (BrE)
Involving synthesis; combining separate elements to form a coherent whole, especially in scientific or philosophical contexts, as opposed to analysis.

Examples

Synthetic approach

Modern linguistics often uses both synthetic and analytic approaches when studying language evolution.
The report presented a synthetic summary of data from multiple disciplines.
