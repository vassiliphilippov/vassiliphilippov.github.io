tonality

noun
musical quality based on a key or scale
7.4 6.1
Pronunciation: / toʊˈnæləti / (AmE), / təˈnalɪti / (BrE)
the character of a piece of music as determined by the key or scale it is based on, especially in relation to the use of major or minor diatonic scales

Examples

Major tonality
Many composers experiment with tonality to create unique harmonic effects.
Shifting tonality throughout the piece keeps listeners on their toes.
The symphony’s bright tonality is established from the very first chord.
