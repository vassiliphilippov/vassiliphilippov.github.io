anthropologically

adverb
in a human-studies way
Pronunciation: / ˌænθrəpəˈlɑdʒɪkli / (AmE), / ˌænθrəpəˈlɒdʒɪkli / (BrE)
in a manner that relates to the study of humans, their societies, and cultures

Examples

Studied anthropologically
The festival was examined anthropologically to reveal its significance in the local community.
Researchers approached the ancient ruins anthropologically to better understand early human behavior.
The documentary analyzes family structures anthropologically across different continents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: culturally - in a way that relates to culture
  • synonym: sociologically - in a way that relates to the study of society
