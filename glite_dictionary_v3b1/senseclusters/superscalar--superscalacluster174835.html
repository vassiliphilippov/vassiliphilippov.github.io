superscalar

adjective
multi-instruction processor
9.9
Pronunciation: /suː.pər.ˈskeɪ.lər/ /; / /ˈsuː.pər.skeɪ.lər/ (AmE), /sjuː.pə.ˈskeɪ.lə/ /; / /suː.pə.ˈskeɪ.lə/ (BrE)
describing a computer processor that can execute more than one instruction simultaneously during a single clock cycle

Examples

Superscalar processor design
A superscalar processor can handle several instructions in a single clock cycle, increasing overall speed.
Engineers designed the new chip with a superscalar architecture to boost performance.
Many modern computers rely on superscalar technology to efficiently process complex tasks.

Related Terms

  • synonym: parallel - able to perform multiple operations at the same time
  • antonym: scalar - processing one instruction at a time
