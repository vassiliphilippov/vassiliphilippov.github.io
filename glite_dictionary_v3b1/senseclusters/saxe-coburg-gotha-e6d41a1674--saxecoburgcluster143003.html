Saxe-Coburg-Gotha

proper noun
former name of the British royal family (1901–1917)
9.9
Pronunciation: /sæks ˈkoʊ.bərg ˈɡoʊ.θə/ /; / /sæks ˈkoʊ.bɜːrɡ ˈɡoʊ.θə/ (AmE), /sæks ˈkəʊ.bəg ˈɡəʊ.θə/ /; / /sæks ˈkəʊ.bɜːg ˈɡəʊ.θə/ (BrE)
the dynastic name of the British royal family from 1901 until 1917, when it was changed to Windsor due to anti-German sentiment during World War I

Examples

The Saxe-Coburg-Gotha family
During World War I, the British royal family's Saxe-Coburg-Gotha name was criticized for its German origin.
Historians often refer to the royal family as Saxe-Coburg-Gotha in the context of early 20th-century Britain.
King George V belonged to the Saxe-Coburg-Gotha dynasty before it was renamed Windsor.
