Synonyms

  • scorn
    5.5 2/m
    scorn is open, often harsh contempt used to reject someone as unworthy, while disdain is a more cold, settled disrespect.
  • hold in contempt
    7.8 1/m
    hold in contempt is a phrase meaning to regard someone with strong disrespect, while disdain is to reject with cold contempt.
  • despise
    4.9 9/m
    despise means feeling strong dislike and hatred for someone, while disdain means thinking they are unworthy and rejecting them.
  • spurn
    7.7 1/m
    spurn is to reject someone or something openly and sharply, while disdain is to regard them as unworthy often without action.
