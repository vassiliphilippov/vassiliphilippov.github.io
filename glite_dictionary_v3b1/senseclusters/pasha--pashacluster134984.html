pasha

noun
Ottoman official or governor
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑːʃə / (AmE), / ˈpæʃə / (BrE)
a high-ranking civil or military official in the Ottoman Empire, especially in Turkey or Egypt

Examples

The pasha ruled
After the revolt, the pasha lost much of his influence in the province.
Dressed in elaborate robes, the pasha attended official ceremonies in the capital.
The pasha presided over the region and collected taxes for the sultan.
