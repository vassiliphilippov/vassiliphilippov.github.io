sleeving

noun
protective machine lining
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈsliːvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈsliːvɪŋ / (BrE)
a protective or replaceable lining, often metal, inserted into a machine part such as an engine cylinder to prevent wear or damage

Examples

Engine sleeving
The mechanic installed new sleeving in the engine to extend its lifespan.
Damaged sleeving can lead to serious problems in high-performance machinery.
Regular inspection of the sleeving helps prevent costly repairs in industrial equipment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bushing - a removable lining for a hole, used to reduce wear
  • synonym: lining - a layer of material covering the inside surface of something
