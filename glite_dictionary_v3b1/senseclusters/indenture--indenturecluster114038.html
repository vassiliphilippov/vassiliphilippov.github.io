indenture

noun
formal agreement for bonds or debts
6.0 7.5
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈden.tʃɜːr/ /; / /ɪn.ˈden.tʃər/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈden.tʃə/ (BrE)
a formal legal contract or agreement, especially one that binds a person to work for another for a period of time or specifies the terms of a bond

Examples

Legal indenture contract
Investors should review the indenture carefully before purchasing any new securities.
The corporation's indenture outlines all the repayment conditions for its bonds.
The trustee is responsible for ensuring that the terms of the indenture are observed.
In colonial America, many immigrants signed an indenture to pay for their passage by working for several years.
The blacksmith took on a new apprentice under a seven-year indenture.
When the indenture expired, she was finally free to start her own business.

Related Terms

  • synonym: apprenticeship - the position of being an apprentice, often under an indenture
  • synonym: contract - a legally binding agreement between two or more parties
