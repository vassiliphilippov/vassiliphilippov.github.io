biocidal

adjective
kills living organisms
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪoʊˈsaɪdəl / (AmE), / ˌbaɪəʊˈsaɪdəl / (BrE)
able or designed to kill, control, or prevent the growth of harmful living organisms such as bacteria, pests, or fungi

Examples

Biocidal agents
The laboratory tested several biocidal agents to find the most effective one against bacteria.
Farmers often use biocidal sprays to protect crops from insect infestations.
Concerns have been raised about the environmental impact of biocidal products in water systems.
Hospitals use biocidal sprays to keep surfaces free from dangerous bacteria.
The new paint contains biocidal agents to prevent mold growth in damp areas.
Researchers tested the biocidal properties of the chemical on several types of fungi.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antimicrobial - able to kill or inhibit the growth of microorganisms
  • antonym: biostatic - inhibiting the growth of living organisms without killing them
  • synonym: disinfectant - a substance used to destroy harmful microorganisms
