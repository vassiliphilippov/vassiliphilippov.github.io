llanero

noun
South American cowboy
Pronunciation: / jɑˈnɛɾoʊ /; / jaˈneɾoʊ / (AmE), / jɑˈnɛːrəʊ /; / jaˈneərəʊ / (BrE)
a person from the Llanos region of Venezuela or Colombia, especially a cowboy or cattle herder

Examples

Llanero cowboy
The llanero rode across the vast plains, guiding his herd through the tall grass.
Traditional music and stories often celebrate the bravery of the llanero in Venezuelan culture.
A skilled llanero can spend days on horseback without losing track of a single cow.
The llanero is known for his skills in cattle herding and horsemanship.
During the festival, many llaneros wear traditional clothing and play folk music.
A proud llanero spoke about life on the open plains of Venezuela.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cowboy - a person who herds cattle, especially in North America
  • synonym: gaucho - a cowboy of the South American pampas, especially in Argentina and Uruguay
