offer

verb
say you will do something; volunteer
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɑfər /; / ˈɑː.fɚ /; / ˈɔfər /; / ˈɑːfər / (AmE), / ˈɒfə /; / ˈɒf.ə /; / ˈɒf.ər / (BrE)
to say you are willing to do something, propose, or suggest (including help, marriage, negotiation, or ideas)

Examples

To offer help
I offered to watch their children so they could go out for dinner.
She offered to pick up some groceries on her way home from work.
When no one else would help, he offered to stay late and finish the project.
He finally offered to her after years of courtship.
In a touching ceremony, he offered his hand in marriage.
When James offered, it took her completely by surprise.
During the meeting, he offered his suggestions on how to improve the project.
She offered her congratulations to the newlyweds at the reception.
They offered their thanks to everyone who contributed to the charity event.

Related Terms

  • synonym: extend - to make (an offer, greeting, or feeling) known or available; especially to express formally
  • synonym: propose - to ask someone to marry you
  • synonym: suggest - to mention or recommend as an option
  • degree higher: threaten - to state one's intention to cause harm or take action as a warning
  • synonym: volunteer - to freely offer to do something
