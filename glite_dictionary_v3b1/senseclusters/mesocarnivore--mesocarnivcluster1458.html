mesocarnivore

noun
medium-level meat eater
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛsoʊˈkɑrnɪˌvɔr / (AmE), / ˌmiːsəʊˈkɑːnɪvɔː / (BrE)
an animal whose diet is mostly meat (about 50–70%) but also includes significant amounts of non-meat foods such as fruits or plants

Examples

A mesocarnivore animal
The red fox is a well-known mesocarnivore that adapts to many environments.
Raccoons are considered mesocarnivores because they eat both small animals and fruits.
Many wild canines are classified as mesocarnivores due to their varied diets.
The red fox is a typical mesocarnivore, eating rodents, insects, and fruit.
Many small wild cats are considered mesocarnivores because they eat both meat and plant material.
In some regions, the raccoon acts as a mesocarnivore, feeding on fish, eggs, and berries.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: carnivore - an animal that eats mostly or only meat
  • degree higher: hypercarnivore - an animal whose diet is more than 70% meat
  • degree lower: omnivore - an animal that eats both animal and plant foods in roughly equal amounts
