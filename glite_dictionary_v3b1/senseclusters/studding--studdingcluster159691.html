studding

noun
extra sail on ship
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈstʌdɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈstʌdɪŋ / (BrE)
an additional sail set outside a square sail on a square-rigged ship to increase speed in fair weather, or the act of setting such sails

Examples

Set the studding sail
The crew quickly set the studding to take advantage of the gentle wind.
In calm weather, adding studding helped the ship move faster across the open sea.
Sailors were trained in the proper use of studding during long ocean voyages.

Related Terms

  • antonym: mainsail - the principal sail on a ship's mainmast
  • synonym: studding sail - a light sail set outside the square sails on a ship
