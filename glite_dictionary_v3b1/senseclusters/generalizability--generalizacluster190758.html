generalizability

noun
applicability to wider cases or populations
Pronunciation: / ˌdʒɛn(ə)rələˌzaɪləˈbɪləti /; / dʒɛn.ə.rə.lə.zaɪ.ləˈbɪl.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌdʒɛn(ə)rəlaɪzəˈbɪlɪti /; / dʒɛn.ə.rə.laɪ.zəˈbɪl.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
The quality or extent to which results, findings, or other things can be applied or are relevant to a wider range of situations, groups, cases, or populations.

Examples

Study generalizability limits
Because the sample was limited, the study's generalizability is questionable.
Researchers must consider generalizability when designing experiments that seek broad conclusions.
The generalizability of these results remains a subject of debate among scientists.
Improving participant diversity can enhance the generalisability of scientific findings.
Researchers questioned the generalisability of the study's outcomes to other populations.
The generalisability of this research is limited due to a small sample size.

Related Terms

  • synonym: generalizability - the US spelling of generalisability
  • antonym: specificity - the quality of being specific rather than general
  • synonym: universality - the quality of being true or applicable everywhere or in all cases
