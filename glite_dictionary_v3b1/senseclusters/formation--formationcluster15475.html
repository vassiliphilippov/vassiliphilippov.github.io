formation

noun
distinct body of rock (geology)
Pronunciation: / fɔːrˈmeɪʃən / (AmE), / fɔːˈmeɪʃən / (BrE)
(Geology) A body of rock with identifiable characteristics and origin, forming a distinct unit in the earth's crust.

Examples

Rock formation
Fossils were found embedded in the shale formation along the riverbank.
Geologists are studying the new rock formation discovered near the valley.
The canyon walls revealed a spectacular formation of sandstone and limestone.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stratum - a layer of rock or soil with internally consistent characteristics
