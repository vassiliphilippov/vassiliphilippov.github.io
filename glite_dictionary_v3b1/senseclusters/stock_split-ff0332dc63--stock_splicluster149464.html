stock split

noun
increase in shares without change in value
Pronunciation: / stɑk splɪt / (AmE), / stɒk splɪt / (BrE)
an increase in the number of a company's outstanding shares by issuing more shares to current shareholders, without changing the total value of their holdings

Examples

A stock split event
After the stock split, shareholders owned twice as many shares at half the original price.
Many investors saw increased liquidity in the market after the stock split.
The company announced a stock split to make its shares more affordable.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reverse stock split - a decrease in the number of a company's outstanding shares by combining shares, increasing share price
