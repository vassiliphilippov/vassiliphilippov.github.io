low-bush blueberry

noun
a low-growing blueberry shrub
Pronunciation: / ˌloʊ ˌbʊʃ ˈbluˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˌləʊ ˌbʊʃ ˈbluːˌbɛri / (BrE)
A low-growing deciduous shrub native to northeastern North America, known for its sweet, dark blue berries.

Examples

The low-bush blueberry plant
Low-bush blueberry fields take on a brilliant red color in autumn.
The jam was made with handpicked low-bush blueberries from a local farm.
Wild low-bush blueberry plants thrive in the cool climates of Maine and eastern Canada.
