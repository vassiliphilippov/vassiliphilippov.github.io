Galium aparine

noun
a prickly-stemmed annual plant species
Pronunciation: / ˈɡeɪliəm əˈpɛrɪn / (AmE), / ˈɡalɪəm əˈparɪni / (BrE)
A species of annual plant in the bedstraw family, with stems covered in curved prickles, found in North America, Europe, and Asia; also known as cleavers or goosegrass.

Examples

Galium aparine plant
Galium aparine is often found growing in hedgerows and along woodland edges.
Farmers consider Galium aparine a weed because it can smother young crops.
The tiny hooked hairs on the stems of Galium aparine help it cling to clothing and animal fur.
