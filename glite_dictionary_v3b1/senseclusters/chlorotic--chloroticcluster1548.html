chlorotic

adjective
relating to or affected by chlorosis
9.9 0.04/m
Pronunciation: / kl…ëÀêÀàr…ëÀêt…™k / (AmE), / kl…îÀêÀàr…ít…™k / (BrE)
relating to or suffering from chlorosis, especially marked by pale or yellowish color due to lack of chlorophyll or hemoglobin

Examples

A chlorotic plant
The chlorotic appearance of the crops worried the farmers after several weeks of heavy rain.
The doctor noted her chlorotic complexion and ordered a blood test.
The leaves of the plant turned chlorotic due to iron deficiency in the soil.

Synonyms

  • pale-colored
    2.3 0.02/m
    pale-colored simply means light in color, while chlorotic describes a sickly yellow paleness caused by lack of green or blood pigment.
  • pale-hued
    5.1 0.00/m
    pale-hued describes a faint color tone, while chlorotic refers to a pale, often yellow, unhealthy appearance from lack of plant or blood pigment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chlorosis - a condition marked by abnormal yellowing, especially in plants or the human condition known as 'green sickness'
