homomorphic

adjective
same or similar form
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhoʊməˈmɔːrfɪk / (AmE), / ˌhəʊməˈmɔːfɪk / (BrE)
having the same or similar form or structure, especially in mathematics or biology

Examples

A homomorphic function
In mathematics, a homomorphic function preserves the structure between two algebraic systems.
The two crystals are homomorphic, sharing a similar external shape despite different compositions.
Researchers studied homomorphic features in the development of plant leaves.

Related Terms

  • antonym: heteromorphic - having different forms or structures
  • synonym: isomorphic - having a one-to-one correspondence in form or structure
