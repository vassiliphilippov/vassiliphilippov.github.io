riot

verb
celebrate noisily, often while drunk
5.9 5.7
Pronunciation: /ˈraɪ.ət/ (AmE), /ˈraɪ.ət/ (BrE)
(of people) to enjoy oneself or celebrate noisily, often in a boisterous or drunken manner.

Examples

To riot all night
During the festival, the villagers would riot late into the night, singing and dancing in the streets.
Every year, the students riot in celebration after their final exams, much to their professors’ dismay.
The young men rioted after the harvest, their laughter echoing across the fields.

Related Terms

  • synonym: carouse - to drink alcohol and enjoy oneself with others in a noisy, lively way
  • synonym: revel - to take great pleasure or delight in a boisterous activity, especially drinking and dancing
