level

adjective
having equal scores; tied
3.2
Pronunciation: / ˈlɛvəl / (AmE), / ˈlɛvəl /; / ˈlɛv(ə)l / (BrE)
having equal scores, being tied, or being steady and not changing suddenly

Examples

Level scores
At halftime, the teams remained level on points.

Despite the bad news, she spoke in a level voice, hiding all emotion.
He kept a level head during the crisis and didn't panic.
Her level gaze made it clear that she expected an honest answer.
