paraplegic

adjective
person with lower-body paralysis
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəˈpliːdʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌpærəˈpliːdʒɪk / (BrE)
Suffering from complete paralysis of the lower half of the body, typically due to damage to the spinal cord.

Examples

Paraplegic patient
After the accident, she became a paraplegic and had to use a wheelchair to get around.
As a paraplegic athlete, he competed in the Paralympic Games.
The hospital has special facilities to support paraplegic patients during rehabilitation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: able-bodied - not affected by any physical disability
  • degree higher: quadriplegic - person paralyzed in all four limbs
