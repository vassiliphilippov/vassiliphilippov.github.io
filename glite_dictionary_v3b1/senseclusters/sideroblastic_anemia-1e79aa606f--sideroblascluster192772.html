sideroblastic anemia

noun
anemia with ringed sideroblasts in the bone marrow
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪdɚˈblæstɪk əˈniːmiə /; / ˌsaɪ.dɚ.əˈblæs.tɪk əˈniː.mi.ə / (AmE), / ˌsʌɪdəˈblastɪk əˈniːmɪə /; / ˌsɪd.ər.əˈblæs.tɪk əˈniː.mi.ə / (BrE)
A type of anemia in which the bone marrow produces ringed sideroblasts—abnormal red blood cell precursors containing excess iron—instead of healthy red blood cells, leading to reduced oxygen transport; this condition can be rare or refractory.

Examples

Sideroblastic anemia diagnosis
A diagnosis of sideroblastic anemia was made after reviewing the bone marrow biopsy.
Patients with sideroblastic anemia often require regular blood transfusions to manage their condition.
The presence of ringed sideroblasts is a hallmark of sideroblastic anemia.
Doctors often test for vitamin B6 deficiency when investigating sideroblastic anaemia.
The patient was diagnosed with sideroblastic anaemia after bone marrow tests revealed abnormal cells.
Treatment options for sideroblastic anaemia may depend on whether it is hereditary or acquired.
