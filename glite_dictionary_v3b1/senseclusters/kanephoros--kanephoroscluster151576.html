kanephoros

noun
Greek basket-bearer
Pronunciation: / kəˈnɛfərɒs / (AmE), / kəˈniːfərɒs / (BrE)
a young woman in ancient Greece who carried sacred baskets in religious processions

Examples

The kanephoros carries baskets
During the Panathenaic festival, the kanephoros walked at the front of the procession.
The honor of being chosen as kanephoros was reserved for unmarried girls from noble families.
Ancient vases often depict a kanephoros carrying a basket on her head during ceremonies.
