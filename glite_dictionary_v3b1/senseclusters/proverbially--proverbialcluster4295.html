proverbially

adverb
as known from a proverb or reputation
Pronunciation: / prəˈvɜrb.i.ə.li / (AmE), / prəˈvɜː.bɪ.ə.li / (BrE)
In a way that refers to, or is characteristic of, a proverb or common saying; as is commonly known.

Examples

Be proverbially known
He became proverbially famous for his generosity in the town.
The dessert was proverbially sweet, just as everyone had promised.
This plant is proverbially bitter, much like wormwood.
