onto

preposition
movement to surface
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɑntu / (AmE), / ˈɒntu / (BrE)
to a position on or in contact with a surface, or moving to a new subject, activity, or stage

Examples

Jump onto the table
The children climbed onto the playground equipment to play.
She carefully placed the vase onto the table in the living room.
Rainwater spilled onto the floor after the window was left open.
After finishing the introduction, the speaker moved onto the main points of the presentation.
Let's move onto the next chapter after we finish this exercise.
The meeting quickly shifted onto more urgent matters as new information arrived.

Related Terms

  • antonym: off - away from a surface or position
  • synonym: upon - on the surface of, especially after movement
