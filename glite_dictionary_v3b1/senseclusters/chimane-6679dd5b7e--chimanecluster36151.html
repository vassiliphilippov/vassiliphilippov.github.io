Chimane

proper noun
Bolivian indigenous group
Pronunciation: / tʃiˈmɑːneɪ / (AmE), / tʃɪˈmɑːneɪ / (BrE)
an indigenous people of Bolivia, also known as the Tsimané, with distinct traditions and culture

Examples

The Chimane people
The Chimane live in the lowland forests of Bolivia and maintain traditional ways of life.
Researchers have studied the health and diet of the Chimane people for many years.
The Chimane language is spoken by thousands in the Beni region of Bolivia.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Aymara - an indigenous people of the Andes in Bolivia and Peru
  • synonym: Tsimané - another name for the Chimane people of Bolivia
