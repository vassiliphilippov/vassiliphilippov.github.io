headstream

noun
stream that is the source of a river
7.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààh…õdÀåstriÀêm / (AmE), / Ààh…õd.striÀêm / (BrE)
A stream that forms the source or origin of a river.

Examples

A headstream source
Glacial meltwater can give rise to a headstream that eventually feeds into a larger river system.
The Nile's primary headstream rises in the highlands of East Africa.
The expedition traced the Amazon to its most distant headstream in the mountains.
