slack

verb
to shirk responsibilities; be idle
7.2
Pronunciation: / slæk / (AmE), / slæk / (BrE)
to avoid responsibilities or work; to be idle or lazy at a task

Examples

To slack off work
He tends to slack off when the boss is not around.
If you slack at your job, you may get poor reviews.
She was caught slacking during her afternoon shift.

Related Terms

  • antonym: diligent - showing care and conscientiousness in work
  • synonym: shirk - to avoid or neglect a responsibility
