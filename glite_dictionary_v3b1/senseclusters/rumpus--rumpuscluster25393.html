rumpus

noun
noisy disturbance; commotion
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈrʌmpəs / (AmE), / ˈrʌmpəs / (BrE)
a noisy disturbance or commotion, especially one involving shouting or loud activity

Examples

A loud rumpus
A loud rumpus erupted outside the theater after the concert ended.
Please keep quiet and don’t start another rumpus during the meeting.
The children caused a rumpus when they found the cake in the kitchen.

Related Terms

  • synonym: commotion - noisy and confused disturbance
  • synonym: uproar - a state of noisy confusion or excitement
