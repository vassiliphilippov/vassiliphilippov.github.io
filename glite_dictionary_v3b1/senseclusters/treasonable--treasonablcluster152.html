treasonable

adjective
relating to or involving treason
9.1 0.11/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàtriÀêz…ôn…ôb…ôl / (AmE), / ÀàtriÀêz…ôn…ôbl / (BrE)
Showing or involving the betrayal of one's country; constituting or characteristic of treason.

Examples

Treasonable acts
His treasonable activities were discovered by the intelligence agency.
She was accused of spreading treasonable material during a time of war.
The judge declared the defendant's actions to be treasonable and a threat to national security.

Synonyms

    8.5 0.85/m

  • traitorous
    8.3 0.65/m
    traitorous describes a person or act that betrays trust generally, while treasonable refers specifically to betrayal of one's country.
