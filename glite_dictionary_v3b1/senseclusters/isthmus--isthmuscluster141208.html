isthmus

noun
narrow tissue connecting two larger anatomical parts
Pronunciation: / ˈɪs.məs / (AmE), / ˈɪs.məs / (BrE)
A narrow strip or band of tissue connecting two larger parts of an organ or structure, especially in anatomy.

Examples

Thyroid isthmus
During surgery, the surgeon had to carefully avoid damaging the isthmus of the brain's corpus callosum.
Some congenital conditions involve abnormal development of the uterine isthmus.
The thyroid isthmus connects the two lobes of the gland across the front of the trachea.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bridge - a structure or tissue that connects two parts
