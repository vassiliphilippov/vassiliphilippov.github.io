Hamiltonianism

noun
Hamiltonian formalism use
9.9
Pronunciation: /hæm.ɪl.tə.ˈniː.ə.nɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /hæm.ɪl.tə.ˈnɪə.nɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the use of the Hamiltonian formalism in physics or mathematics, especially for describing systems using a Hamiltonian function representing total energy and evolution

Examples

Hamiltonianism in physics
Many advanced physics courses introduce Hamiltonianism as a fundamental approach to analyzing dynamic systems.
The principles of Hamiltonianism are essential for understanding quantum mechanics and classical mechanics alike.
Researchers often rely on Hamiltonianism to model the total energy and evolution of complex systems.
