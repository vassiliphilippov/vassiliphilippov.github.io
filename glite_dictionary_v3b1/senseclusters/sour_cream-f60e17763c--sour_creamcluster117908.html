sour cream

noun
fermented cream with tangy flavor
Pronunciation: / ˌsaʊr ˈkrim / (AmE), / ˌsaʊə ˈkriːm / (BrE)
A thick cream that has been fermented by lactic acid bacteria, giving it a tangy flavor, commonly used in cooking and as a condiment.

Examples

A bowl of sour cream
At the party, we served chips with salsa and sour cream for dipping.
He topped his baked potato with a generous spoonful of sour cream.
The recipe calls for a cup of sour cream to give the cake extra moisture.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crème fraîche - a French fermented cream similar to but less sour than sour cream
