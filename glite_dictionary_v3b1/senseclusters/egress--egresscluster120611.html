egress

noun
reappearance after an eclipse (astronomy)
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈiːˌɡrɛs / (AmE), / ˈiːɡrɛs / (BrE)
(astronomy) The reappearance of a celestial body after being obscured during an eclipse or occultation.

Examples

Eclipse egress
Astronomers carefully recorded the time of egress during the solar eclipse.
The egress of the moon was visible just after midnight.
The crowd waited with anticipation for the egress of the planet from behind the sun.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ingress - the first appearance of a celestial body at the start of an eclipse
