going out

verb
leaving to socialize or for leisure
Pronunciation: / ˈɡoʊɪŋ aʊt / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊɪŋ aʊt / (BrE)
leaving one's home or usual place, especially to take part in social activities such as visiting friends, restaurants, bars, or events

Examples

To go out with friends
I don't feel like going out this weekend—I'd rather stay home and relax.
She spent hours getting ready before finally going out to the party.
We're going out for dinner with friends tonight if you want to join us.

Related Terms

  • antonym: staying in - remaining at home, not going out for social activities
