break off

verb
interrupt or stop early
1.0 2.7
Pronunciation: /breɪk ɔːf/ /; / /breɪk ɑːf/ (AmE), /breɪk ɒf/ /; / /breɪk ɔːf/ (BrE)
to stop or interrupt something suddenly before it is finished, such as a conversation, activity, or relationship

Examples

Negotiators had to break off talks when an agreement could not be reached.
She decided to break off the engagement after much thought.
