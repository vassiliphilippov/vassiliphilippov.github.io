transit

noun
surveying instrument for measuring angles
Pronunciation: / ˈtræn.sɪt / (AmE), / ˈtræn.zɪt / (BrE)
a surveying instrument for measuring horizontal and vertical angles, consisting of a small telescope mounted on a tripod

Examples

Surveying transit
After adjusting the tripod, she looked through the transit to take the necessary angular measurements.
Modern surveyors often use a transit equipped with digital readouts for greater precision.
The engineer set up the transit to accurately measure the property's boundaries.
