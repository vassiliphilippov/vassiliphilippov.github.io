spit

verb
eject saliva from the mouth
Pronunciation: / spɪt / (AmE), / spɪt / (BrE)
to force saliva, phlegm, or other substance out of the mouth, often in a quick or aggressive way

Examples

To spit on the ground
He stepped outside and spit on the ground.
It's rude to spit in public places.
She was so angry that she spit out the water in her mouth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: expectorate - to cough or spit out phlegm from the throat or lungs
