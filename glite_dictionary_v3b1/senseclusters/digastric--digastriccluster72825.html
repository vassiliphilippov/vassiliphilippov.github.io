digastric

noun
neck muscle with two parts
Pronunciation: / daɪˈɡæstrɪk / (AmE), / daɪˈɡæstrɪk / (BrE)
a muscle in the neck with two separate sections connected by a tendon, important for swallowing and jaw movement

Examples

The digastric muscle
The doctor explained that the digastric helps open the jaw during eating.
An injury to the digastric can make swallowing difficult.
During the anatomy lesson, students identified the digastric on the neck model.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mylohyoid - a thin muscle forming the floor of the mouth
  • synonym: sternocleidomastoid - a major neck muscle involved in head movement
