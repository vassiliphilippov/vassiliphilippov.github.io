case

verb
examine for criminal purposes
Pronunciation: / keɪs / (AmE), / keɪs / (BrE)
to examine a place, especially a building, in order to plan a crime such as robbery

Examples

To case a store
He realized the suspicious man was casing the jewelry store.
Police think the thieves had cased the building ahead of the robbery.
The burglars cased the street for days before breaking in.

Related Terms
