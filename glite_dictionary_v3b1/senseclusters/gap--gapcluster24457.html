gap

verb
make an opening or break in
8.1
Pronunciation: / ɡæp / (AmE), / ɡap / (BrE)
to make an opening or break in something; to create a gap

Examples

To gap a fence
Lightning struck the old fence, gapping it in several places.
Violent winds had gapped the roof, leaving the attic exposed to rain.
Workers used heavy machinery to gap the stone wall for a new entrance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: open - to make something not closed, especially by removing or creating an opening
