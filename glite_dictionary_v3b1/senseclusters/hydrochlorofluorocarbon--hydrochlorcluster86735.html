hydrochlorofluorocarbon

noun
type of refrigerant and propellant chemical
1.6
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪdroʊˌklɔːroʊˌflʊərəˈkɑːrbɑn / (AmE), / ˌhaɪdrəʊˌklɔːrəˌflʊərəˈkɑːbən / (BrE)
A chemical compound containing hydrogen, chlorine, fluorine, and carbon, used as a refrigerant and propellant, and considered less harmful to the ozone layer than chlorofluorocarbons.

Examples

A hydrochlorofluorocarbon chemical
Many refrigerators now use hydrochlorofluorocarbon instead of older, more harmful chemicals.
Scientists are studying the effects of hydrochlorofluorocarbon emissions on global warming.
The phaseout of hydrochlorofluorocarbon refrigerants aims to reduce ozone depletion.
