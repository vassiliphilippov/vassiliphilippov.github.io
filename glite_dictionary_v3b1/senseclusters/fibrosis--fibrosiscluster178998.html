fibrosis

noun
formation of excess fibrous tissue in the body
Pronunciation: / faɪˈbroʊsɪs / (AmE), / fɪˈbrəʊsɪs / (BrE)
the development of excess fibrous connective tissue in an organ or tissue, often as a result of injury or long-term inflammation

Examples

Lung fibrosis
Early diagnosis of fibrosis is crucial for effective treatment.
Liver fibrosis can eventually lead to cirrhosis if left untreated.
The patient's lungs showed signs of fibrosis after years of exposure to asbestos.
