Zarathustra

proper noun
founder of Zoroastrianism; Persian prophet
9.9
Pronunciation: / zæɹ.əˈθuː.strə / (AmE), / zɑː.rəˈθuː.strə / (BrE)
A Persian prophet, believed to have lived around 628–551 BC, who founded the ancient religion of Zoroastrianism.

Examples

Prophet Zarathustra
Many scholars consider Zarathustra to be one of the earliest religious reformers.
Some works of literature, such as Nietzsche's 'Thus Spoke Zarathustra,' draw inspiration from his legacy.
The teachings of Zarathustra had a profound influence on subsequent religious traditions.
