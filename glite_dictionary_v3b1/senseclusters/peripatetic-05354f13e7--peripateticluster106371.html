Peripatetic

proper noun
Aristotle's follower
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛrəpəˈtɛtɪk / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪpəˈtɛtɪk / (BrE)
a member of the school of philosophy founded by Aristotle in ancient Greece

Examples

The Peripatetic school
The Peripatetic explained Aristotle's theories to his students in the gardens of the Lyceum.
Many Peripatetics contributed to the development of logic and ethics in ancient philosophy.
As a Peripatetic, he valued observation and discussion as key methods of learning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Aristotelian - a follower of Aristotle's philosophy
  • antonym: Stoic - a member of the ancient Greek school of philosophy founded by Zeno
