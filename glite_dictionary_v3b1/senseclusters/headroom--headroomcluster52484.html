headroom

noun
capacity for extra volume without distortion
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛdˌruːm / (AmE), / ˈhɛdruːm / (BrE)
the amount by which a system can accommodate increases in volume or signal level before distortion occurs, especially in audio equipment.

Examples

Audio headroom
If you push the volume too high, you'll exceed the headroom and introduce unwanted noise.
Professional audio engineers always ensure there is enough headroom during recording sessions.
The new mixer provides plenty of headroom for live performances, reducing the risk of distortion.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dynamic range - the range between the softest and loudest sounds a system can reproduce
