tetrachoric

noun
statistical correlation measure
Pronunciation: / ˌtɛtrəˈkɔrɪk / (AmE), / ˌtɛtrəˈkɒrɪk / (BrE)
a statistical coefficient estimating the correlation between two variables that each have only two possible values, assuming an underlying continuous distribution

Examples

A tetrachoric coefficient
The researchers calculated the tetrachoric to assess the relationship between the two diagnostic tests.
A high tetrachoric suggests a strong association between the presence or absence of the two symptoms.
Statisticians often use the tetrachoric when analyzing binary survey responses that reflect underlying traits.

Related Terms

  • synonym: phi coefficient - a measure of association for two binary variables
