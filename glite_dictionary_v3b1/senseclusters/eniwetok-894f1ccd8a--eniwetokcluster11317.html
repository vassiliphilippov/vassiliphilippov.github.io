Eniwetok

proper noun
atoll; WWII battle site in Marshall Islands
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈe.nɪ.we.tɑːk/ /; / /ˈe.nɪ.weɪ.tɑːk/ (AmE), /ˈe.nɪ.we.tɒk/ /; / /ˈe.nɪ.weɪ.tɒk/ (BrE)
an atoll in the Marshall Islands, known for a major World War II battle and as a site for U.S. atomic bomb testing

Examples

Eniwetok atoll
During World War II, American forces fought fiercely to capture Eniwetok from the Japanese.
Historians often cite the battle of Eniwetok as a turning point in the Pacific campaign.
The island of Eniwetok later became a site for nuclear testing after the war.
Historians have studied the fierce fighting that took place on Eniwetok during World War II.
The United States conducted several atomic tests at Eniwetok in the 1940s and 1950s.
Today, Eniwetok remains a remote atoll, visited mainly by researchers and former residents.
