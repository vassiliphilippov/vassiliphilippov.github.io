nux vomica

noun
seed of Asian tree used medicinally
6.2 9.7
Pronunciation: / nʌks ˈvɑmɪkə / (AmE), / nʌks ˈvɒmɪkə / (BrE)
A medicinal preparation made from the seeds of an Asian tree (Strychnos nux-vomica), containing strychnine and brucine; formerly used as a stimulant and tonic.

Examples

nux vomica seeds
Herbalists once valued nux vomica for its alleged stimulating effects on the nervous system.
Nineteenth-century doctors sometimes prescribed nux vomica as a remedy for paralysis and weakness.
The seeds of nux vomica are highly toxic if misused.

Related Terms

  • synonym: brucine - an alkaloid found with strychnine in nux vomica
  • synonym: strychnine - a poisonous compound found in nux vomica
