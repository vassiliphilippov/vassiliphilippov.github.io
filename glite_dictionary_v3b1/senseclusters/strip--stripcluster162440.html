strip

verb
steal goods from
1.3 2.3
Pronunciation: /strɪp/ (AmE), /strɪp/ (BrE)
to remove a covering, layer, or all contents from something, leaving it bare

Examples

To strip the paint
After the factory closed down, vandals stripped the building of all metal fixtures.
Looters stripped the houses of anything useful during the blackout.
The abandoned cars were quickly stripped of their valuable parts by thieves.
Illegal logging has stripped the hillside of nearly all its trees.
The company decided to strip the old paint from the building before renovation.
Years of drought can strip the land of its fertile soil.
She carefully stripped the bed of its sheets and pillowcases.
The workers stripped the old paint from the door.
You will need to strip the wallpaper before painting the wall.
