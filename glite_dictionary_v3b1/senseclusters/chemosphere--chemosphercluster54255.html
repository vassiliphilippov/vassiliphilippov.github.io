chemosphere

noun
chemical environment
Pronunciation: / ˈkiːmoʊˌsfɪr / (AmE), / ˈkiːməʊˌsfɪə / (BrE)
the chemical environment surrounding cells, tissues, or organisms that affects their biological activity

Examples

Cell chemosphere
Researchers studied how the chemosphere around the tumor influenced its response to treatment.
Changes in the chemosphere can impact the way bacteria interact with their environment.
The introduction of new substances altered the chemosphere of the experimental cells.

Related Terms

  • antonym: biosphere - the regions of the earth where living organisms exist
  • synonym: microenvironment - the immediate environment surrounding a cell or tissue
