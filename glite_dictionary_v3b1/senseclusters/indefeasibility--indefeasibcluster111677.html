indefeasibility

noun
cannot be voided
9.9
Pronunciation: /ɪn.dɪ.fiː.zə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.ti/ (AmE), /ɪn.dɪ.fiː.zə.ˈbɪl.ɪ.ti/ (BrE)
the quality of being unable to be made void, undone, or overturned, especially in legal contexts

Examples

The indefeasibility of title
The indefeasibility of the land title protects the owner's rights from future legal challenges.
Lawyers discussed the indefeasibility of the contract in court.
The principle of indefeasibility is central to many property law systems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: irrevocability - the quality of being impossible to revoke or take back
  • antonym: voidability - the quality of being able to be made void or invalid
