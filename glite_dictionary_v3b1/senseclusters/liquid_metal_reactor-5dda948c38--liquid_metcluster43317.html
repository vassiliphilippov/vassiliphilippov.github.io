liquid metal reactor

noun
nuclear reactor using liquid metal as coolant
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪkwɪd ˈmɛt̬l riˈæktər / (AmE), / ˈlɪkwɪd ˈmɛtl riˈæktə / (BrE)
A type of nuclear reactor that uses liquid metal, such as sodium or lead, as a coolant to transfer heat.

Examples

A liquid metal reactor design
Japan has invested heavily in creating advanced liquid metal reactor facilities for nuclear power research.
Scientists are developing a new liquid metal reactor that could offer safer and more efficient energy production.
The liquid metal reactor uses sodium to transfer heat from the core to a steam generator.
