cell

noun
device generating electricity by chemical reaction
6.8 3.2
Pronunciation: /sel/ (AmE), /sel/ (BrE)
a device that produces an electric current through a chemical reaction, typically as a basic unit in batteries or fuel cells

Examples

Battery cell
A fuel cell generates electricity by combining hydrogen and oxygen.
Each AA battery contains a single cell that powers small electronic devices.
The flashlight stopped working because one cell in the battery had failed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: battery - a device consisting of one or more cells that generate electricity
