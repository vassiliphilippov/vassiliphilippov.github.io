messuage

noun
dwelling house with outbuildings and land (law)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛs.wɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈmɛs.juːɪdʒ / (BrE)
(Law) A dwelling house together with its outbuildings and the land assigned to its use.

Examples

A messuage and land
The contract specified the boundaries of the messuage included in the sale.
The deed transferred ownership of the messuage and its surrounding gardens to the new buyer.
Under medieval English law, a messuage often included the main residence and several farm buildings.
