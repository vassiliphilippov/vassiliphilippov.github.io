backhaul

noun
data transmission link
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌhɔl / (AmE), / ˈbækˌhɔːl / (BrE)
a high-capacity communications link or the process of transmitting data from a remote site or local network to a central network or the internet backbone

Examples

Fiber backhaul connection
The new fiber-optic backhaul improved internet speeds for the entire rural community.
Mobile networks rely on a strong backhaul to connect cell towers to the core network.
Without a reliable backhaul, remote offices may experience frequent data transmission delays.
The company upgraded its backhaul to handle more internet traffic from rural areas.
Reliable backhaul is essential for mobile networks to provide fast data services.
Engineers are working to reduce delays in the backhaul between local offices and headquarters.

Related Terms

  • antonym: downlink - a connection that receives data from a central system to a local system
  • synonym: uplink - a connection that sends data from a local system to a central system
