fugitivity

noun
fleeting quality
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåfjuÀêd í…ôÀàt…™v…ôti / (AmE), / ÀåfjuÀêd í…™Ààt…™v…™ti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being fleeting, short-lived, or difficult to hold onto

Examples

The fugitivity of moments
The fugitivity of childhood makes every moment with family especially meaningful.
Artists often try to capture the fugitivity of beauty before it fades away.
He reflected on the fugitivity of happiness during difficult times.

Synonyms

    8.2 0.02/m
    fleetingness describes something that lasts only a short time and passes quickly, while fugitivity adds a sense of slipping away.
  • ephemerality
    8.9 0.10/m
    ephemerality refers to things that last very briefly, often only hours or days, while fugitivity stresses their hard-to-hold nature.
  • transience
    8.1 0.62/m
    transience means the state of lasting only a short time, while fugitivity focuses on briefness and things slipping away.
  • impermanence
    8.2 0.41/m
    impermanence means not lasting forever and subject to change, while fugitivity emphasizes short life and things that quickly slip away.
