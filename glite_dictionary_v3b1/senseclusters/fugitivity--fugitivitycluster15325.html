fugitivity

noun
fleeting quality
Pronunciation: / ˌfjuːdʒəˈtɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌfjuːdʒɪˈtɪvɪti / (BrE)
the quality or state of being fleeting, short-lived, or difficult to hold onto

Examples

The fugitivity of moments
The fugitivity of childhood makes every moment with family especially meaningful.
Artists often try to capture the fugitivity of beauty before it fades away.
He reflected on the fugitivity of happiness during difficult times.

Related Terms

  • antonym: permanence - the state of lasting or remaining unchanged
  • synonym: transience - the state of lasting only a short time
