trope

noun
recurring theme or motif
Pronunciation: / troʊp / (AmE), / trəʊp / (BrE)
a commonly recurring theme, idea, or device in literature, art, or media

Examples

A common trope
The 'reluctant hero' is a classic trope found in many adventure stories.
Writers often use the 'fish out of water' trope to create humor in their narratives.
Some critics argue that the film relies too heavily on familiar tropes instead of original ideas.
The wise old mentor is a classic trope in many stories.
Superheroes with secret identities are a popular trope in comic books.
The film avoided the usual romantic tropes to create a fresh story.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cliché - an overused idea or expression
  • synonym: motif - a recurring element or idea in a work of art or literature
  • antonym: originality - the quality of being new or unique
