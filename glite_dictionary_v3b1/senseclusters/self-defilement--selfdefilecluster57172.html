self-defilement

noun
harming one's own self-worth or integrity
Pronunciation: / ˌsɛlf dɪˈfaɪlmənt / (AmE), / ˌsɛlf dɪˈfaɪlmənt / (BrE)
behavior or thoughts that damage one's own self-esteem, integrity, or sense of worth

Examples

Self-defilement behavior
Some people turn to self-defilement as a response to unresolved guilt.
Therapists help patients identify the roots of their self-defilement.
Years of self-defilement left him feeling empty and ashamed.

Related Terms

  • synonym: self-destruction - behavior leading to one's own ruin or downfall
  • synonym: self-harm - deliberately injuring oneself, often as a coping mechanism
  • antonym: self-respect - regard for one's own worth and dignity
