pedanticism

noun
overly detailed concern
Pronunciation: / pəˈdæntɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / pɪˈdantɪˌsɪzəm /; / pɪˈdantɪsɪzəm / (BrE)
the quality of being overly concerned with minor details, rules, or academic learning, often in a way that is showy or annoying

Examples

Her pedanticism in details
Her pedanticism often slowed down meetings as she insisted on discussing every small detail.
The professor's pedanticism made his lectures thorough but sometimes difficult to follow.
Some people appreciate pedanticism when accuracy is important, but others find it frustrating.
His pedanticism made meetings longer than necessary, as he insisted on correcting every small mistake.
The professor's pedanticism sometimes overshadowed the main points of his lectures.
She was criticized for her pedanticism when she focused on grammar instead of the content of the essay.

Related Terms

  • antonym: flexibility - willingness to change or adapt rules or details
  • antonym: modesty - the quality of not showing off one's knowledge or abilities
  • synonym: pedantry - excessive concern with minor details or rules
  • degree lower: precision - the quality of being exact and accurate
