backgrounding

noun
execution of programs in the background (computing)
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌɡraʊndɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈbækˌɡraʊndɪŋ / (BrE)
the running of lower priority computer programs in the background while the system is available, allowing higher priority programs to use the processor when needed

Examples

Backgrounding computer programs
Modern smartphones handle backgrounding more efficiently to conserve battery life.
The operating system supports backgrounding so you can run multiple tasks at once.
With backgrounding, music can keep playing while you check your messages.

Related Terms

  • antonym: foregrounding - the execution of high priority programs that have immediate use of the processor
