Skanda

proper noun
Hindu god of war and bravery
Pronunciation: / ˈskɑːndə / (AmE), / ˈskændə / (BrE)
In Hinduism, the god of war and bravery, son of Shiva and Parvati, and commander-in-chief of the gods' army.

Examples

Skanda god of war
According to Hindu mythology, Skanda led the gods in their battles against demons.
Devotees gather at the temple every year to celebrate the festival of Skanda.
The stories of Skanda emphasize courage, righteousness, and filial devotion.
