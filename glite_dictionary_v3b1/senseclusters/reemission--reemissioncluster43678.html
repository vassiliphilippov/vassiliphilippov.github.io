reemission

noun
emitting again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːɪˈmɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːɪˈmɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of emitting something again, especially energy, light, or particles after absorption

Examples

Reemission of light
The reemission of infrared radiation plays a key role in the greenhouse effect.
After absorbing ultraviolet light, the material undergoes reemission at a longer wavelength.
Scientists measured the reemission of particles from the surface after exposure to sunlight.

Related Terms

  • antonym: absorption - the process of taking in energy, light, or particles
  • synonym: emission - the act of sending out energy, light, or particles
