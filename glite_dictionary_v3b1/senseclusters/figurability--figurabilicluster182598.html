figurability

noun
ability to be visualized
Pronunciation: / ˌfɪɡjərəˈbɪləti / (AmE), / ˌfɪɡjʊərəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being able to be represented or expressed in figures, images, or visual form

Examples

Figurability of ideas
The figurability of mathematical ideas helps students understand complex concepts more easily.
Artists often explore the figurability of emotions through abstract paintings.
Some scientific theories challenge the figurability of reality by describing things we cannot see.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abstraction - the quality of being abstract or not easily represented visually
  • synonym: visualizability - the quality of being able to be visualized or imagined visually
