echo

noun
reflected signal (radio, TV, radar)
Pronunciation: / ˈɛkoʊ / (AmE), / ˈɛkəʊ / (BrE)
A reflected television, radio, or radar signal received after the original transmission, especially used in technical contexts.

Examples

Radar echo detected
Meteorologists can track storms by observing the echo returned from weather radar.
Sometimes, a television signal produces a ghost image due to an echo of the original broadcast.
The radar picked up an echo from an object several miles away.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reflection - the return of light, sound, or energy from a surface
