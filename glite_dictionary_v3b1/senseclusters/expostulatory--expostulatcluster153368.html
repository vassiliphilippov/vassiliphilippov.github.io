expostulatory

adjective
expressing protest
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈspɑstjəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ɪkˈspɒstjʊlətəri / (BrE)
expressing strong disagreement, protest, or objection, especially in speech or writing

Examples

An expostulatory letter
She sent an expostulatory email to the manager about the unfair policy.
His expostulatory remarks during the meeting made everyone reconsider the proposal.
The article took an expostulatory tone, criticizing the government's recent decision.

Related Terms

  • antonym: approving - showing agreement or acceptance
  • synonym: protesting - expressing objection or disapproval
