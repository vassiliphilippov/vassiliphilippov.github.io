regelation

noun
ice melting and refreezing
Pronunciation: / ˌriːdʒəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːɡɪˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
the process by which ice melts under pressure and refreezes when the pressure is removed

Examples

Regelation of ice
Scientists demonstrated regelation by pressing a wire through a block of ice, which refroze behind the wire.
The phenomenon of regelation helps explain how glaciers move over rocks.
You can observe regelation when ice cubes stick together after being squeezed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: freezing - the process of changing from liquid to solid
  • synonym: melting - the process of changing from solid to liquid
