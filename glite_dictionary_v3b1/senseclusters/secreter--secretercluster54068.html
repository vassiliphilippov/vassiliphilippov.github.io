secreter

noun
substance-producing cell
7.4
Pronunciation: / sɪˈkriːtər / (AmE), / sɪˈkriːtə / (BrE)
an organism or cell that produces and releases a particular substance, such as a hormone or enzyme

Examples

A secreter of hormones
The thyroid gland is a major secreter of important hormones in the body.
Scientists studied the secreter cells to understand how insulin is released.
Each secreter in the tissue plays a role in maintaining chemical balance.

Related Terms

  • antonym: absorber - an organism or cell that takes in a substance
  • synonym: secretor - an organism or cell that secretes a substance
