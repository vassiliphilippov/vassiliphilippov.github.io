Celastrus articulatus

noun
Asian ornamental vine with orange-yellow fruit
9.9
Pronunciation: / səˈlæstrəs ˌɑr.tɪˈkju.leɪ.təs / (AmE), / sɪˈlæstrəs ˌɑː.tɪˈkjuː.leɪ.təs / (BrE)
An ornamental vine native to Asia, notable for its attractive orange-yellow fruit with a scarlet aril, and commonly cultivated or found naturalized in North America.

Examples

Celastrus articulatus vine
After it became naturalized, Celastrus articulatus spread along forest edges and fence lines in North America.
Many horticulturists recommend Celastrus articulatus for its decorative berries and vigorous growth.
The garden featured a climbing Celastrus articulatus with striking orange-yellow fruit visible in the autumn.
