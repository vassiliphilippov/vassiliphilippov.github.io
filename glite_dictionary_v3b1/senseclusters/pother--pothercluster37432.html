pother

noun
a fuss or commotion
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑðər / (AmE), / ˈpɒðə / (BrE)
a state of fuss, commotion, or excited agitation about something, typically more noise or concern than the situation warrants

Examples

A big pother
She left quietly, not wishing to create a pother among her friends.
The small mistake caused quite a pother in the office.
Why make such a pother over a simple misunderstanding?

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - the state of being relaxed and free from agitation
  • synonym: commotion - a state of confused and noisy disturbance
