crake

noun
a small marsh-dwelling bird (rail family)
Pronunciation: / kreɪk / (AmE), / kreɪk / (BrE)
a small, short-billed bird of the rail family, found in the Old World and typically inhabiting marshes or reed beds.

Examples

A crake bird
During migration season, birdwatchers keep an eye out for the secretive spotted crake.
The presence of crakes indicates a healthy wetland ecosystem.
The water rails and crakes are most active at dusk and dawn in the reedy marsh.
