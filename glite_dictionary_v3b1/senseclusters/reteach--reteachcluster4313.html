reteach

verb
to teach again
3.7 0.08/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåriÀêÀàtit É / (AmE), / ÀåriÀêÀàtiÀêt É / (BrE)
to teach something again, usually to help someone understand better, correct mistakes, or reinforce learning

Examples

To reteach a lesson
After the students struggled with the concept, the teacher decided to reteach the lesson.
Sometimes it is necessary to reteach important safety procedures to ensure everyone remembers them.
He retaught the dance steps until everyone could perform them confidently.
After noticing confusion, the instructor decided to reteach the main concepts to the class.
Sometimes it is necessary to reteach important skills to ensure everyone is confident.
She retaught the math lesson using a different method to help the students understand.
After noticing confusion, the instructor decided to reteach the main concepts to the class.
Sometimes it is necessary to reteach difficult topics to ensure everyone understands.
She retaught the lesson using different examples to help the students grasp the material.

Synonyms

  • re-explain
    3.4 0.00/m
    re-explain gives a clearer verbal account of a point to fix confusion, while reteach repeats teaching and practice to build understanding.
  • reinstruct
    5.5 0.01/m
    reinstruct gives instructions again to correct previous guidance, while reteach focuses on teaching concepts and skills until learners understand.

Related Terms
