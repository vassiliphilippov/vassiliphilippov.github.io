canker

noun
harmful, persistent influence
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈkæŋkər / (AmE), / ˈkaŋkə / (BrE)
A harmful and persistent influence or source of corruption that is difficult to remove.

Examples

A moral canker
He warned that complacency would act as a canker eating away at the team's morale.
The novel explores how jealousy can become a canker within a close-knit community.
Unchecked greed was the canker that eventually destroyed the organization from within.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blessing - a beneficial thing; something that brings good
  • synonym: blight - a thing that spoils or damages something
  • synonym: corruption - dishonest or fraudulent conduct; moral perversion
