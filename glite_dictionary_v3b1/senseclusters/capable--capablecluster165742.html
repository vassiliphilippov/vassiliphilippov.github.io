capable

adjective
having the needed ability or qualities
3.5 1.3
Pronunciation: /ˈkeɪ.pə.bəl/ (AmE), /ˈkeɪ.pə.bəl/ (BrE)
having the ability, qualities, or skills needed to do something

Examples

Capable of hard work
Despite his age, he remains capable of hiking the most challenging trails.
She is a capable manager who handles every challenge with skill and confidence.
The new software is capable of processing large amounts of data in seconds.
Despite his age, the director was capable of running the operation efficiently.
Few thought she was capable of such a compassionate act.
He is capable of handling difficult situations with calm and confidence.
He proved himself to be highly capable during the crisis.
She is a capable leader who inspires her team to achieve great results.
The company hired a capable engineer to oversee the new project.
