falter

verb
speak hesitantly or unsteadily
5.4 0.59/m
Pronunciation: / Ààf…îlt…ôr / (AmE), / Ààf…îÀêlt…ô / (BrE)
to speak hesitantly or unsteadily, often due to uncertainty or emotion

Examples

His voice falters
His voice would falter whenever he spoke of his late parents.
She began to falter when asked about her decision to quit the job.
The witness seemed nervous and started to falter during the cross-examination.

Synonyms

  • hesitate
    2.5 26/m
    hesitate means to pause before saying or doing something when unsure, while falter shows doubt in the voice or words.
  • stumble over one's words
    5.6 0.05/m
    stumble over one's words means to make mistakes or pauses while speaking, while falter often shows a weak or unsteady voice.
  • hem and haw
    8.4 0.26/m
    hem and haw means to speak with many small pauses and unclear sounds, while falter is a more general shaky or hesitant speech.
  • choke on one's words
    7.8 0.01/m
    choke on one's words means to be unable to speak clearly from strong feeling, while falter is a softer, hesitant way of speaking.
  • choke out
    7.4 0.12/m
    choke out means to force words out with great difficulty because of emotion, while falter is more about shaky, hesitant speech.
  • stammer
    4.8 2.97/m
    stammer means to repeat or block sounds involuntarily when speaking, while falter is a general hesitant or unsteady speech often from doubt.

Related Terms

  • antonym: speak clearly - to speak in a way that is easy to understand without hesitation
  • synonym: stammer - to speak with sudden involuntary pauses and a tendency to repeat the initial letters of words
