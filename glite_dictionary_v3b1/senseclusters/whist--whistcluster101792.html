whist

noun
trick-taking card game for four players
Pronunciation: / wɪst / (AmE), / wɪst / (BrE)
A trick-taking card game played by four players in two partnerships, using a standard 52-card deck, where each side scores points for tricks taken above six.

Examples

Whist card game
He learned the rules of whist from his grandfather.
The club meets every week to enjoy several rounds of whist.
We spent the evening playing a game of whist with friends.
