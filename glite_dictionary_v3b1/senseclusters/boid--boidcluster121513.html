boid

noun
simulated flocking agent
9.9
Pronunciation: / bɔɪd / (AmE), / bɔɪd / (BrE)
a simulated autonomous agent in computer graphics or artificial life that follows simple rules to create flocking or collective behavior

Examples

Simulated boid movement
Each boid in the simulation follows simple rules to mimic the movement of a flock of birds.
The animation software allows you to adjust how each boid responds to its neighbors.
By increasing the number of boids, the group behavior becomes more realistic and complex.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agent - an entity that acts or makes decisions in a simulation
  • synonym: particle - a small unit in a simulation, often used in graphics
