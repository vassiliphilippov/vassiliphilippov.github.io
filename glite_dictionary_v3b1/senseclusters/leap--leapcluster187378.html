leap

noun
distance jumped in a leap
Pronunciation: / lip / (AmE), / liːp / (BrE)
a jump or spring through the air, or a sudden movement forward or upward (can also refer to a quick or strong emotional reaction)

Examples

A long leap
His record-breaking leap astonished the crowd.
She measured the leap and marked the spot with a flag.
The athlete made a leap of over seven meters in the long jump competition.
His heart gave a leap when he saw her coming.
The gymnast finished her routine with an impressive leap into the air.
With a graceful leap, the deer cleared the fence.
The athlete made a powerful leap over the high bar during the competition.
With a single leap, the frog crossed the small pond.
Her heart skipped a beat as she watched the dancer's graceful leap across the stage.
