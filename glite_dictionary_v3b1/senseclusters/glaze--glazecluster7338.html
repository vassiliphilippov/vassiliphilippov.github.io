glaze

noun
shiny coating applied to food
5.5 3.1
Pronunciation: /gleɪz/ (AmE), /gleɪz/ (BrE)
a smooth, shiny coating on a surface, such as on food, pottery, or fabric

Examples

A chocolate glaze
A thin chocolate glaze covered the doughnut, making it look irresistible.
Before serving, she added a savory balsamic glaze to the grilled chicken.
The chef brushed a honey glaze over the roasted carrots to give them a sweet shine.
A thick glaze covered the metal sculpture, giving it a luminous finish.
After painting it, she used a clear glaze to seal and protect the surface of the plate.
The potter applied a transparent glaze to the vase before firing it in the kiln.
Repeated washing can dull the glaze on delicate fabrics.
The silk had a beautiful glaze that caught the light perfectly.
This cotton is treated with a glaze to make it look more luxurious.

Related Terms

  • synonym: frosting - a creamy, sweet coating for cakes and pastries
  • synonym: icing - a sweet, sugary coating used especially on cakes
  • synonym: sheen - a soft luster on a surface, especially fabric
  • synonym: varnish - a liquid applied to surfaces that dries to form a hard, shiny coating
