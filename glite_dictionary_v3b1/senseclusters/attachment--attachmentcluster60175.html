attachment

noun
legal order for seizure of property
3.7
Pronunciation: / əˈtæt͡ʃmənt / (AmE), / əˈtatʃm(ə)nt / (BrE)
a legal order authorizing the seizure of property to secure payment of a judgment in a court case

Examples

Court attachment of property
A judge may grant an attachment if there is a risk of assets being concealed.
She applied for an attachment to ensure that the debtor's property was available for collection.
The court issued an attachment on his assets pending the outcome of the trial.

Related Terms

  • synonym: garnishment - a legal process of diverting a portion of a debtor's wages to the creditor
