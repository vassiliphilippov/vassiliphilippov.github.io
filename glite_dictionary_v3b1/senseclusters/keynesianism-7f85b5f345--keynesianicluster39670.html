Keynesianism

noun
theories of Keynes on government economic intervention
8.3 7.3
Pronunciation: /keɪn.ˈziː.ə.nɪ.zəm/ /; / /keɪn.ˈzɪ.ə.nɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /keɪn.ˈziː.ə.nɪ.zəm/ /; / /keɪn.ˈzɪ.ə.nɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the economic theories and principles of John Maynard Keynes, especially the advocacy of government intervention to manage economic cycles and maintain employment and stability

Examples

Keynesianism economic theory
Critics of Keynesianism argue that too much government intervention can stifle economic growth.
Many governments adopted Keynesianism to combat unemployment during the Great Depression.
She studied Keynesianism as part of her economics degree.

  • antonym: monetarism - an economic theory emphasizing the role of governments in controlling the amount of money in circulation
