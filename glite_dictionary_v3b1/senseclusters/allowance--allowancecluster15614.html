allowance

noun
reserve fund in company accounts
1.0 3.3
Pronunciation: / əˈlaʊəns / (AmE), / əˈlaʊəns / (BrE)
an amount set aside or adjusted in business or finance for possible changes, expenses, or special conditions

Examples

A business allowance
A large allowance was established to cover potential decreases in property values.
Each year, auditors review the allowance for obsolete inventory to ensure it is adequate.
The company made an allowance in its financial statements for doubtful debts.
An allowance for wastage was factored into the final cost estimate.
Make sure to include an allowance for unexpected expenses in your budget.
The accountant recommended a five percent allowance for depreciation on equipment.

Related Terms

  • antonym: addition - an amount added to something, especially for calculation purposes
  • synonym: deduction - an amount subtracted from a total, often for tax or calculation purposes
  • synonym: provision - an amount set aside in accounting for future liabilities or losses
