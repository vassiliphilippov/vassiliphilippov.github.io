plateau

verb
to stop progressing
5.1
Pronunciation: /plæ.ˈtoʊ/ (AmE), /plæ.ˈtəʊ/ (BrE)
to stop increasing or improving and remain at the same level for a period of time

Examples

Sales plateau after growth
After several weeks of rapid growth, sales began to plateau and stayed steady for months.
Her language skills plateaued after the initial improvement, despite continued practice.
If your progress seems to plateau, try changing your study routine for better results.
After several weeks of improvement, her test scores plateaued and did not change much.
The company's profits plateaued after a period of rapid growth.
Once your fitness level plateaus, you may need to change your workout routine.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decline - to decrease or become less
  • antonym: improve - to become better or make progress
  • synonym: level off - to stop rising or falling and stay the same
  • antonym: rise - to increase or go up
  • synonym: stagnate - to stop developing or making progress
