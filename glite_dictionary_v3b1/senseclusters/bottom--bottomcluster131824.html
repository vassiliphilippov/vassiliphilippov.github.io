bottom

verb
(of a ship) strike the ground
6.2 6.4
Pronunciation: /ˈbɑː.təm/ (AmE), /ˈbɒt.əm/ (BrE)
(of a ship) to strike the ground with the bottom of the hull, typically unexpectedly or due to shallow waters.

Examples

Ship bottoms on sandbar
During the storm, the old ship bottomed on a hidden sandbar.
The captain radioed for help as the vessel bottomed near the harbor entrance.
They feared the boat would bottom if they continued in the shallow channel.
