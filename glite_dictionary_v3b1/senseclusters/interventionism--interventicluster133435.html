interventionism

noun
foreign policy involvement
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈvɛnʃəˌnɪzəm / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈvɛnʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
the policy or practice of a country becoming involved in the affairs of other countries, especially through political or military means

Examples

Foreign interventionism
The government's policy of interventionism led to increased involvement in international conflicts.
Critics argue that interventionism can create more problems than it solves in foreign countries.
Debates about interventionism often arise during times of global crisis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interference - the act of getting involved in something without invitation
  • antonym: isolationism - a policy of avoiding involvement in international affairs
  • antonym: noninterventionism - a policy of not becoming involved in the affairs of other countries
