persona grata

noun
acceptable diplomat to a foreign government
9.9 7.2
Pronunciation: /pər.ˈsoʊ.nə ˈgrɑː.tə/ /; / /pɜːr.ˈsoʊ.nə ˈgrɑː.tə/ (AmE), /pə.ˈsəʊ.nə ˈgrɑː.tə/ /; / /pɜː.ˈsəʊ.nə ˈgrɑː.tə/ (BrE)
A person, especially a diplomat, who is acceptable to the government or authorities of the country to which they are sent.

Examples

A persona grata diplomat
After the negotiations, the ambassador was declared a persona grata by the host country.
Being named a persona grata is essential for a diplomat to fulfill their role abroad.
The government officially recognized her as a persona grata following years of careful diplomacy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: persona non grata - a person, especially a diplomat, who is not acceptable to the government or authorities of the host country
