sightread

verb
play music at first sight
Pronunciation: / ˈsaɪtˌriːd / (AmE), / ˈsaɪtˌriːd / (BrE)
to perform a piece of music by reading it for the first time, without prior practice

Examples

To sightread music
She needs to sightread this new song for the audition tomorrow.
The orchestra was asked to sightread an unfamiliar composition during rehearsal.
Very few students can sightread challenging pieces perfectly on their first attempt.

Related Terms

  • antonym: play by ear - to play music without using written notation, relying on memory or improvisation
