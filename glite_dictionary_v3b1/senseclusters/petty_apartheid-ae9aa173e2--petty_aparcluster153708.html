petty apartheid

noun
minor forms of racial segregation (especially in South Africa)
7.5 8.6
Pronunciation: /ˈpet.ɪ ə.ˈpɑːr.taɪt/ /; / /ˈpet.ɪ ə.ˈpɑːr.taɪd/ (AmE), /ˈpet.ɪ ə.ˈpɑː.θaɪt/ /; / /ˈpet.ɪ ə.ˈpɑː.taɪd/ (BrE)
Racial segregation applied to everyday public places such as hotels, restaurants, and transportation, especially as enforced under South African apartheid.

Examples

Petty apartheid in public places
Petty apartheid was visible in the segregated entrances for different racial groups at public buildings.
During the 1950s, petty apartheid laws prohibited black people from eating in certain restaurants.
Many South Africans vividly remember the humiliation caused by petty apartheid in train stations and movie theaters.
