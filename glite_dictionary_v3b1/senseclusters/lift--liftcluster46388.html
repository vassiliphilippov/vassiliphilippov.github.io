lift

verb
rise or move upwards
5.3
Pronunciation: / lɪft / (AmE), / lɪft / (BrE)
to rise or move upwards by itself, or to be raised to a higher position

Examples

Smoke lifts upward
A cloud of smoke lifted above the burning building, visible for miles around.
As the sun warmed the ground, the thick morning fog slowly lifted from the valley.
When the restrictions lift, we can finally return to normal life.
As the curtain lifted, the audience grew silent in anticipation.
The balloon lifted gently off the ground and floated away.
The heavy fog began to lift as the sun rose higher in the sky.
After the rain, the ground began to lift in certain areas of the field.
As the bread bakes, the dough lifts and becomes airy.
Cracks appeared as the tiles started lifting from the wet floor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: descend - to move from a higher position to a lower one
  • antonym: lower - to move something to a lower position
  • synonym: raise - to move something to a higher position; to increase
  • synonym: rise - to move from a lower position to a higher one
