power takeoff

noun
device transferring engine power to equipment
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈpaʊ.ɚ ˌteɪkˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈpaʊ.ə ˌteɪkɒf / (BrE)
a device that transfers mechanical power from an engine to another piece of equipment, especially in vehicles like tractors

Examples

The power takeoff device
Make sure the power takeoff is disengaged before servicing the machinery.
Many modern trucks are equipped with a power takeoff to run specialized equipment.
The tractor’s power takeoff was used to operate the rotary tiller attachment.
