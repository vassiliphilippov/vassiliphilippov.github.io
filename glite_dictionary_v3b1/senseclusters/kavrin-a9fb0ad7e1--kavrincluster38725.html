Kavrin

noun
alkaloid muscle relaxant medicine
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkæv.rɪn / (AmE), / ˈkæv.rɪn / (BrE)
A nonaddictive alkaloid medicine derived from opium, used to relax smooth muscles.

Examples

Kavrin medicine
My doctor prescribed Kavrin to help relieve my stomach cramps.
Pharmacists explained that Kavrin works by relaxing the smooth muscles in the body.
Unlike some other opium derivatives, Kavrin is considered nonaddictive.
