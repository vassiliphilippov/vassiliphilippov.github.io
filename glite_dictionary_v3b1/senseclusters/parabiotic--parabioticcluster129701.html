parabiotic

adjective
side-by-side living
Pronunciation: / ˌpærəbaɪˈɑtɪk / (AmE), / ˌpærəbaɪˈɒtɪk / (BrE)
living or occurring side by side, especially in the same habitat or ecological community.

Examples

Parabiotic ant colonies
The researchers observed parabiotic relationships between certain species of ants in the rainforest.
Some plants display parabiotic behavior by growing closely together without directly interacting.
The study focused on parabiotic animals that share the same habitat but do not compete for resources.

Related Terms

  • antonym: solitary - living alone or separately from others
  • synonym: symbiotic - living together in a close, often beneficial relationship
