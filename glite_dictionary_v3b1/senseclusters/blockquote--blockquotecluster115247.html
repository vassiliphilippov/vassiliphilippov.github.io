blockquote

noun
indented quotation
4.5
Pronunciation: /ˈblɑː.kwoʊt/ (AmE), /ˈblɒ.kwəʊt/ (BrE)
a section of text quoted from another source and set apart from the main text, often by indentation or special formatting (also, an HTML element for this purpose)

Examples

A blockquote section
The article included a blockquote from the author's original speech to emphasize her main point.
When writing essays, students often use a blockquote to present important evidence from sources.
The website's design made each blockquote stand out with a gray background and larger font.
The article included a blockquote to highlight the author's original words.
When designing the website, she used a blockquote to set off the testimonial.
A blockquote is often styled with indentation to show it is a quotation.

Related Terms

  • synonym: citation - a reference to a source of information
  • synonym: excerpt - a short extract from a film, broadcast, or piece of music or writing
  • synonym: quotation - a group of words taken from a text or speech and repeated by someone other than the original author
  • synonym: quote - to repeat or copy words from another source
