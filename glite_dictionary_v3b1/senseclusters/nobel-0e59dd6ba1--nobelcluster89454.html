Nobel

proper noun
Swedish inventor
Pronunciation: / noʊˈbɛl / (AmE), / nəʊˈbɛl / (BrE)
Alfred Nobel (1833–1896), a Swedish chemist, engineer, and inventor known for inventing dynamite and establishing the Nobel Prizes.

Examples

Alfred Nobel
Alfred Nobel is remembered for both his invention of dynamite and his legacy of the Nobel Prizes.
The life of Nobel inspired many to pursue scientific and humanitarian achievements.
Many people associate the name Nobel with excellence in science, literature, and peace.
