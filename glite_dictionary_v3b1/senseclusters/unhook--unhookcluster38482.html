unhook

verb
remove from a hook
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈhʊk / (AmE), / ʌnˈhʊk / (BrE)
to remove (something) from a hook or to detach something that is fastened or held by a hook

Examples

To unhook a fish
Can you unhook the fish from the line for me?
He unhooked the curtain from the rod and took it down.
She carefully unhooked the necklace before going to bed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: hook - to fasten or attach with a hook
