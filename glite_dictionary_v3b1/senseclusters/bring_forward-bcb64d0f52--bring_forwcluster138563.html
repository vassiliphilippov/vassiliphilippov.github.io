bring forward

verb
present for consideration
3.7
Pronunciation: / brɪŋ ˈfɔrwərd / (AmE), / brɪŋ ˈfɔːwəd / (BrE)
to introduce or present something for consideration or discussion, especially in a formal setting

Examples

To bring forward an idea
She decided to bring forward the proposal at the next meeting.
The committee will bring forward several new suggestions for discussion.
The finance report was brought forward by the chairman for immediate review.

Related Terms

  • synonym: propose - to suggest an idea or plan for consideration
  • synonym: submit - to formally present something for consideration or judgment
  • antonym: withdraw - to remove or take back something from consideration
