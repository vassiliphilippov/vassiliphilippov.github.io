idolatry

noun
worship of idols; idol worship
1.7
Pronunciation: / aɪˈdɑːlətɹi /; / aɪˈdɑːlətri / (AmE), / aɪˈdɒlətri / (BrE)
the worship of idols or physical objects as gods

Examples

Religious idolatry
Some religions strictly prohibit idolatry, viewing the worship of images as blasphemous.
The ancient temple was built for idolatry and rituals honoring many gods.
Throughout history, accusations of idolatry have led to conflicts and religious reforms.
The ancient temple was a center of idolatry for the local population.
Some critics argue that celebrity culture has become a form of modern idolatry.
He warned against the dangers of idolatry and losing sight of true values.

Related Terms

  • synonym: adoration - deep love and respect
  • antonym: atheism - the absence of belief in the existence of deities
  • antonym: iconoclasm - the rejection or destruction of religious images or idols
  • synonym: idolize - to admire, revere, or worship as an idol
  • synonym: worship - the act of showing reverence and adoration for a deity
