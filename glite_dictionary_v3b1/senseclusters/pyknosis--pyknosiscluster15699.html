pyknosis

noun
degeneration and shrinkage of a cell nucleus
Pronunciation: / pɪkˈnoʊsɪs / (AmE), / pɪkˈnəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A degenerative condition in which a cell nucleus shrinks and becomes dense, typically occurring during cell death.

Examples

Pyknosis of nucleus
Pyknosis is often observed in cells undergoing apoptosis as the nucleus condenses.
During histological examination, the pathologist noted areas of pyknosis in the tissue sample.
The presence of pyknosis indicates that the cell is in the late stages of degeneration.

Related Terms

  • synonym: karyolysis - destruction of a cell nucleus during cell death
