Danelaw

proper noun
historic Danish-ruled area
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪnˌlɔ / (AmE), / ˈdeɪnlɔː / (BrE)
the region of England under Danish control and law during the early Middle Ages

Examples

Danelaw in England
During the ninth century, much of northern England was known as the Danelaw.
The influence of the Danelaw can still be seen in place names across Yorkshire.
Historians study the Danelaw to understand the impact of Viking rule in England.
