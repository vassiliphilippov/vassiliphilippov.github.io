arbitrage

verb
profit by trading on price differences
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːrbɪˌtrɑːʒ / (AmE), / ˈɑːbɪtrɪdʒ / (BrE)
to buy and sell assets in different markets or forms to profit from price differences between them, especially in finance

Examples

To arbitrage price gaps
Many hedge funds arbitrage currency differences to maximize their profits.
The firm arbitraged the price gap between gold futures and spot prices.
Traders often arbitrage between stock exchanges in different countries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: speculate - to engage in buying and selling with the hope of profiting from market fluctuations
