electric doublet

noun
pair of equal and opposite electric charges
5.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɛktrɪk ˈdʌblət / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktrɪk ˈdʌblɪt / (BrE)
A pair of equal and opposite electric charges separated by a small distance, forming a dipole.

Examples

An electric doublet pair
In the experiment, an electric doublet was used to demonstrate the electric field created by dipoles.
The behavior of an electric doublet is fundamental to understanding certain chemical reactions.
The water molecule acts as an electric doublet due to its polar structure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dipole - a pair of equal and opposite charges or magnetic poles separated by a distance
