rafting

noun
assembling floating rafts
Pronunciation: / ˈræftɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈrɑːftɪŋ / (BrE)
the process of assembling logs or other materials into rafts for transport by water

Examples

Timber rafting
In the 1800s, rafting was a common way to transport timber down large rivers.
The painting depicted workers engaged in rafting logs together for a long journey.
Before modern transportation, rafting played a crucial role in moving goods by water.

Related Terms

  • synonym: log driving - the practice of moving logs down a river to a sawmill
  • antonym: rafting (recreation) - the sport of traveling on rivers in inflatable rafts
