penuriously

adverb
in a miserly or impoverished manner
3.9 8.0
Pronunciation: /pə.ˈnjʊr.ɪ.əs/ /; / /pə.ˈnjuːr.ɪ.əs/ (AmE), /pə.ˈnjʊə.rɪ.əs/ /; / /pə.ˈnjuə.rɪ.əs/ (BrE)
In a way that shows extreme poverty or unwillingness to spend money; in a miserly or impoverished manner.

Examples

Live penuriously
After losing his fortune, he was forced to live penuriously for many years.
The artist survived penuriously but remained dedicated to his craft.
They traveled penuriously, staying in the cheapest lodgings they could find.
