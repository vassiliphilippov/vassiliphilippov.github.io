unholy

adjective
morally wrong; impious

(of a person or behavior) morally wrong; having committed unrighteous or impious acts.

Examples

Unholy behavior
She warned them not to engage in unholy practices forbidden by their faith.
The unholy actions of the gang shocked the entire community.
The villain in the play is truly unholy, showing no remorse for his crimes.

Synonyms

  • unrighteous
    7.6 0.38/m
    unrighteous describes someone acting without moral justice or fairness, while unholy highlights impious or religiously wrong behavior.
  • sinful
    3.6 4.02/m
    sinful describes actions seen as sins in a religious view, while unholy stresses being impious or against sacred rules.
  • ungodly
    7.2 1.17/m
    ungodly means openly irreligious or shocking to believers, while unholy focuses on impious or morally wrong acts.
  • wicked
    4.6 6.51/m
    wicked describes very evil or bad behavior, while unholy often adds a religious idea of being impious or profane.
  • iniquitous
    9.8 0.17/m
    iniquitous is a strong, formal word for deeply unfair or evil deeds, while unholy is more common and religiously focused.
