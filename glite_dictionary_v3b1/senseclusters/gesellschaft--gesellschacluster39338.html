gesellschaft

noun
impersonal social association
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɡəˈzɛlʃɑːft /; / ɡəˈzɛlʃɑft / (AmE), / ɡəˈzɛlʃɑːft /; / ɡəˈzɛlʃaft / (BrE)
a type of social relationship or association based on impersonal, formal, and indirect ties, typical of modern urban societies (sociology)

Examples

Gesellschaft in cities
In sociology, gesellschaft describes relationships in large, modern cities.
The club operated more as a gesellschaft than a close-knit community.
Many people experience gesellschaft at work, where interactions are formal and goal-oriented.
Sociologists often use gesellschaft to describe the nature of relationships in large cities.
In a gesellschaft, people interact mainly through formal roles rather than personal connections.
The shift from gemeinschaft to gesellschaft marked a major change in social organization.
