wide

noun
cricket penalty ball
Pronunciation: / waɪd / (AmE), / waɪd / (BrE)
in cricket, a ball bowled so far from the batsman that it is not possible to hit, resulting in an extra run for the batting team

Examples

Cricket wide ball
The umpire signaled a wide after the ball passed far outside the batsman's reach.
The team scored several extra runs due to the bowler's repeated wides in the match.
A wide can change the momentum of a cricket game by giving the batting side an advantage.
