emigrant

noun
a person who leaves their country to settle elsewhere
Pronunciation: / ˈɛmɪɡrənt / (AmE), / ˈɛmɪɡrənt / (BrE)
a person who leaves their native country to live permanently in another

Examples

An emigrant from Europe
After years of struggle, he became a successful emigrant in his new country.
Many Irish emigrants settled in North America during the 19th century.
The emigrant family hoped for a better life in Australia.

Related Terms

  • antonym: immigrant - a person who comes to live permanently in a foreign country
  • synonym: migrant - a person who moves from one place to another, especially for work or better living conditions
