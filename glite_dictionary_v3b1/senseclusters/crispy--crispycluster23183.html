crispy

adjective
having a firm, brittle texture (esp. food)
1.0 2.74/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆkrÉªspi / (AmE), / ËˆkrÉªspi / (BrE)
(of food) pleasantly firm and brittle, often making a sharp sound when bitten or broken.

Examples

A crispy crust
After baking, the bread had a golden, crispy crust.
He loves starting his day with crispy bacon and eggs.
The onion rings were perfectly crispy on the outside and tender inside.

Synonyms

  • crisp
    1.0 6.60/m
    crisp means firm and fresh, often used for vegetables, while crispy describes brittle, fried or baked food that breaks with a sharp sound.
  • crunchy
    1.3 2.28/m
    crunchy is firm and makes a loud, chewing sound when bitten, while crispy is brittle and breaks with a quick, sharp sound.
