wash

noun
no net gain or loss
9.9 6.2
Pronunciation: /wɑːʃ/ /; / /wɔːʃ/ (AmE), /wɒʃ/ /; / /wɔːʃ/ (BrE)
a situation or enterprise in which the losses and gains cancel each other out, resulting in no net change

Examples

A financial wash
After accounting for expenses and revenue, the project turned out to be a wash.
All their efforts to get ahead in the market ended up as a wash.
Despite months of work, their partnership was essentially a wash financially.
