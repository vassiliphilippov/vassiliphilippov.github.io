Dark Ages

noun
historical period after the fall of Rome and before the Renaissance
1.0
Pronunciation: / dɑrk ˈeɪdʒɪz / (AmE), / dɑːk ˈeɪdʒɪz / (BrE)
the period in European history after the fall of the Roman Empire, often seen as a time of cultural and economic decline

Examples

The Dark Ages period
During the Dark Ages, much classical knowledge was preserved by monasteries.
Many historians now reconsider the idea that the Dark Ages lacked cultural progress.
The invention of the printing press marked the transition out of the Dark Ages in Europe.
During the Dark Ages, scientific and literary progress slowed significantly.
Many historians believe the printing press ended the Dark Ages in Europe.
Some scholars argue that the term Dark Ages is misleading and outdated.
