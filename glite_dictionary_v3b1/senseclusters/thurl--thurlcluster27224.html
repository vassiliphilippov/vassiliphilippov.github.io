thurl

noun
opening or hole
Pronunciation: / θɜːrl / (AmE), / θɜːl / (BrE)
an opening or hole, especially one made naturally or by piercing, now archaic or dialectal

Examples

A small thurl in stone
The mason discovered a small thurl in the old stone wall near the courtyard.
Light streamed through the thurl in the barn's wooden door.
He peered through the thurl to see what was happening on the other side.
