fly agaric

noun
poisonous red-capped woodland mushroom
Pronunciation: / flaɪ əˈɡɛrɪk / (AmE), / flaɪ əˈɡarɪk / (BrE)
a poisonous woodland mushroom with a bright red cap covered in white warts and white gills, commonly found in the Northern Hemisphere

Examples

The fly agaric mushroom
Children are taught not to touch the fly agaric because of its poisonous properties.
Photos of fly agaric often appear in books about fairy tales and folklore.
The forest floor was dotted with the distinctive fly agaric among the fallen leaves.
