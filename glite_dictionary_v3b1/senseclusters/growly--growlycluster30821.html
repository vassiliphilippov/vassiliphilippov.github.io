growly

adjective
irritable or sulky
Pronunciation: / ˈɡraʊli / (AmE), / ˈɡraʊli / (BrE)
showing or expressing irritation, sulkiness, or a surly mood, not necessarily by making a sound

Examples

Growly reply
She gave a growly reply when asked about her unfinished work.
After a long, stressful day, he was feeling especially growly and wanted to be left alone.
The teacher noticed her growly mood and decided to postpone the discussion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic
  • synonym: grumpy - easily annoyed or in a bad mood
