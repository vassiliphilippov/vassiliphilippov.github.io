an archaic verb ending used to form the second-person singular with 'thou', as in 'knowest' or 'goest'

Examples

Thou knowest
In old English texts, you might see verbs like 'thou knowest' instead of 'you know'.
Shakespeare often used forms such as 'thou goest' to address a single person.
The verb ending -est is rarely used in modern English except in poetry or historical writing.
