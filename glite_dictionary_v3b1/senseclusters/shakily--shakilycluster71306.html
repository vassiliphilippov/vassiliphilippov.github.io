shakily

adverb
in a trembling or unsteady way
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈʃeɪkəli / (AmE), / ˈʃeɪkɪli / (BrE)
in a way that is trembling, shaking, or unsteady, often because of fear, weakness, or uncertainty

Examples

He shakily signed
He signed his name shakily at the bottom of the letter.
Her voice wavered and she spoke shakily during the announcement.
The old man stood up shakily and reached for his walking stick.
He answered the tough question shakily, betraying his uncertainty.
She stood up shakily after the long illness, holding onto the chair for support.
The bridge swayed shakily in the strong wind, making everyone nervous.

Related Terms

  • antonym: firmly - in a secure, stable manner; antonym of 'shakily'
  • antonym: steadily - in a firm, steady, or stable manner
  • synonym: unsteadily - in an unsteady manner, synonym of 'shakily'
