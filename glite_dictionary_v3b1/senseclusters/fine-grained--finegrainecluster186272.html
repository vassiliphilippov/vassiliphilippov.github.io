fine-grained

adjective
highly detailed; granular
4.8
Pronunciation: /ˈfaɪn.greɪnd/ (AmE), /ˈfaɪn.greɪnd/ (BrE)
(in computing, data analysis, and related fields) having a high level of detail or granularity; describing categories, controls, or data divided into small, specific parts.

Examples

Fine-grained control
The dataset offers fine-grained categories enabling highly precise customer segmentation.
The security team implemented fine-grained permissions to control access across different user roles.
Machine learning models benefit from fine-grained labels that capture subtle differences between classes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: coarse-grained - having larger, less detailed divisions; the opposite of fine-grained
  • synonym: granular - divided into small, distinct parts or categories; detailed
