garble

verb
distort or make unclear (a message)
8.0 0.38/m
Pronunciation: / Àà…°…ërb…ôl / (AmE), / Àà…°…ëÀêb…ôl / (BrE)
to distort (a message, statement, or story) so that its meaning becomes unclear or misleading, especially by alteration or omission

Examples

To garble a message
If you garble what I say, people will get the wrong idea.
Rumors often garble the facts until the truth is barely recognizable.
The radio static garbled the pilot's message, making it impossible to understand.

Synonyms

  • distort
    4.6 8.13/m
    distort changes facts or details to give a false or misleading message, while garble implies messy alteration or omission.
  • warp
    7.4 1.82/m
    warp twists facts or stories so their truth seems bent or false, while garble implies messy alteration or omission that hides meaning.
  • obfuscate
    9.1 1.23/m
    obfuscate makes words or ideas hard to understand on purpose, while garble means altering or cutting parts so meaning becomes unclear.
