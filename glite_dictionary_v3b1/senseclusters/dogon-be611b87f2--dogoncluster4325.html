Dogon

noun
West African language group
Pronunciation: / ˈdoʊɡɒn / (AmE), / ˈdəʊɡɒn / (BrE)
any of the closely related languages spoken by the Dogon people of Mali in West Africa

Examples

Dogon language family
The Dogon languages are spoken by communities living along the Bandiagara Escarpment in Mali.
Researchers have studied the grammar and vocabulary of several Dogon dialects.
Many Dogon speakers also use French as a second language.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Bambara - a major language spoken in Mali, West Africa
  • synonym: Fulani - a West African language spoken by the Fulani people
