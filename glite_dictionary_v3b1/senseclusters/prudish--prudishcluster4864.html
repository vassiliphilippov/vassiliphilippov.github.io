prudish

adjective
excessively proper or modest
7.1 0.52/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàpruÀêd…™ É / (AmE), / ÀàpruÀêd…™ É / (BrE)
Easily shocked by matters relating to sex or nudity; excessively concerned with propriety or modesty.

Examples

A prudish person
He was considered prudish because he disapproved of swimwear at the beach.
Her prudish attitude made her blush at even the slightest hint of impropriety.
Some critics called the film too prudish for its refusal to show any romance scenes.

Synonyms

    7.1 0.12/m

  • puritanical
    8.2 1.02/m

  • prissy
    7.9 0.69/m

  • prim
    7.0 0.23/m
    prim acts very formal and proper in manners and dress, while prudish is mainly uneasy or shocked about sex or nudity.
