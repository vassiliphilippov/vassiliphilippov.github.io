variorum

noun
edition with notes from various scholars
Pronunciation: / ˌver.iˈɔr.əm / (AmE), / ˌvær.iˈɔː.rəm / (BrE)
An edition of a written work containing notes, comments, or different versions provided by various scholars or editors.

Examples

A variorum edition
A variorum is particularly valuable to students who wish to compare critical interpretations.
For her research, she relied on a variorum version of the poems, which included notes from several scholars.
The new variorum edition of Shakespeare's plays offers commentary from multiple renowned editors.
