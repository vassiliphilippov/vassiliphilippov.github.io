expressionism

noun
art movement emphasizing subjective expression
7.1 6.9
Pronunciation: /ɪk.ˈspreʃ.ən.ɪz.əm/ (AmE), /ɪk.ˈspreʃ.ən.ɪz.əm/ (BrE)
a style or movement in art, music, or literature in which the artist seeks to express emotional experience rather than impressions of the external world, often through the distortion of reality

Examples

The expressionism style
Many paintings from the early twentieth century display the intense emotional style of expressionism.
She wrote her thesis on expressionism in German cinema.
The exhibition focuses on the ways expressionism distorts reality to convey inner feelings.

Related Terms

  • antonym: impressionism - an art movement focusing on accurate depiction of light and color as perceived by the senses
  • degree higher: surrealism - an art movement emphasizing the unconscious and dream-like imagery
