fall apart

verb

2.8 6.48/m

to break into pieces or stop working properly (of objects or things)

Examples

Old house falls apart
After years of neglect, the roof began to fall apart during the storm.
If you don't handle it carefully, the old book will fall apart in your hands.
The delicate pastry would fall apart when touched.
After years in storage, the old book started to fall apart.

My shoes are starting to fall apart at the seams.

Synonyms

  • break apart
    3.0 1.62/m
    break apart often describes something forced into pieces, while fall apart suggests it breaks on its own or stops working.
  • come apart
    3.1 1.90/m
    come apart usually means parts separate or fall away slowly, while fall apart can mean stop working or break into pieces.
  • collapse
    3.9 31/m
    collapse is a sudden fall or failure of a building or system, while fall apart can be slower breaking or stopping.
  • crumble
    3.4 15/m
    crumble means breaking into small pieces or slowly losing strength, while fall apart covers general breaking or failing of objects.
  • break
    1.0 131/m
    break means to separate or damage often by force, while fall apart suggests gradual disintegration or failure without direct force.
  • fragment
    3.7 6.05/m
    fragment usually means breaking into small separate pieces in more formal use, while fall apart is common everyday speech for breaking.
  • break down
    3.2 8.68/m
    break down often describes machines or systems stopping work, while fall apart can mean physical pieces falling away or failing.
  • fail
    2.2 19/m
    fail often means a machine or system stops working completely, while fall apart can also mean breaking into pieces or losing shape.
