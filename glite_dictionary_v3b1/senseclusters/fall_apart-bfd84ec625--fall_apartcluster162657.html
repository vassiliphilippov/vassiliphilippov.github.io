fall apart

verb
break into pieces; disintegrate
3.5 2.4
Pronunciation: /fɑːl ə.ˈpɑːrt/ /; / /fɔːl ə.ˈpɑːrt/ (AmE), /fɔːl ə.ˈpɑːt/ (BrE)
to break into pieces or stop working properly (of objects or things)

Examples

Old house falls apart
After years of neglect, the roof began to fall apart during the storm.
If you don't handle it carefully, the old book will fall apart in your hands.
The delicate pastry would fall apart when touched.
After years in storage, the old book started to fall apart.
Be careful with that model; it could easily fall apart if you touch it.
The cake was so soft that it began to fall apart when I sliced it.
After years of use, the old sofa finally fell apart and had to be thrown away.
If you don't take care of your tools, they're going to fall apart quickly.
My shoes are starting to fall apart at the seams.

  • synonym: collapse - to fall down suddenly, often due to structural failure or defeat
  • synonym: come apart - to break or be separated into pieces
  • synonym: crumble - to break or fall apart into small fragments
  • synonym: disintegrate - to break into small parts or pieces, or lose cohesion
  • synonym: fall to pieces - to break into smaller parts
  • antonym: hold together - remain in one piece or functional
