flooded gum

noun
Australian eucalyptus tree of moist areas
Pronunciation: / ˈflʌdɪd ɡʌm / (AmE), / ˈflʌdɪd ɡʌm / (BrE)
Any of several Australian eucalyptus trees that grow on moist or alluvial soil, especially Eucalyptus grandis or related species.

Examples

Flooded gum tree
Conservationists are working to protect areas where flooded gum forests provide critical habitat for wildlife.
The flooded gum is a common sight along riverbanks in eastern Australia.
The timber from flooded gum is often used in construction due to its durability.

Related Terms

  • synonym: eucalyptus - genus of trees and shrubs native to Australia
