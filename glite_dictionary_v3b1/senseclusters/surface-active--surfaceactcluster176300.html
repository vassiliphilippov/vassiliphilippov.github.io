surface-active

adjective
reducing surface tension (e.g. detergents)
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˌsɝː.fɪs ˈæk.tɪv / (AmE), / ˌsɜː.fɪs ˈæk.tɪv / (BrE)
(of a substance) capable of lowering the surface tension of a liquid, especially used to describe detergents.

Examples

Surface-active detergent
Laundry detergents often contain surface-active ingredients to enhance their cleaning power.
The surface-active properties of the chemical make it effective in breaking down oil.
This surface-active agent enables the soap to mix easily with water.
