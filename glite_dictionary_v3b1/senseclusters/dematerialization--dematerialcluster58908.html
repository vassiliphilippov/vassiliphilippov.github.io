dematerialization

noun
becoming less physical
Pronunciation: / diˌmətɪriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌməˌtɪəriəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of becoming less physical or reducing the amount of physical substance involved in something

Examples

Digital dematerialization
The dematerialization of documents has made it easier to store and share information online.
With the rise of streaming services, the dematerialization of movies and music has changed how people consume media.
Many companies are encouraging the dematerialization of their processes to reduce costs and environmental impact.

Related Terms

  • synonym: digitization - the process of converting information into a digital format
  • antonym: materialization - the process of becoming physical or taking physical form
