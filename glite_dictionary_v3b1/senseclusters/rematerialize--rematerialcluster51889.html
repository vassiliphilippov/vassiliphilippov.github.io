rematerialize

verb
to appear again
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌriːməˈtɪriəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˌriːməˈtɪəriəlaɪz / (BrE)
to appear or become visible or physical again after having disappeared or become invisible

Examples

To rematerialize suddenly
The ghost would rematerialize in the old mansion every full moon.
After the storm, the mountain peak seemed to rematerialize from behind the clouds.
The lost file managed to rematerialize on my computer after a system update.
After a few seconds, the image began to rematerialize on the computer screen.
The lost file seemed to rematerialize in the folder after the system rebooted.
The ghost would rematerialize at midnight, startling everyone in the old house.
The magician made the coin rematerialize in his other hand, amazing the audience.
After the storm, the outline of the mountain seemed to rematerialize through the mist.
Scientists watched as the particles began to rematerialize after the experiment concluded.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reappear - to appear again after being gone or hidden
  • antonym: vanish - to disappear suddenly and completely
