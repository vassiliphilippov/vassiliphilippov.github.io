anticholinergic

noun
substance blocking acetylcholine
9.5 7.5
Pronunciation: / ˌæntiˌkoʊləˈnɜrdʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌantiˌkəʊlɪˈnɜːdʒɪk / (BrE)
a substance that opposes or blocks the action of acetylcholine in the nervous system

Examples

Anticholinergic drug
Elderly patients are more susceptible to side effects from anticholinergic drugs.
Some medications used to treat allergies have anticholinergic properties that can cause drowsiness.
The doctor prescribed an anticholinergic to help control the patient's overactive bladder.
