inefficient

adjective
not achieving effective results; not productive
4.8 3.0
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnəˈfɪʃənt / (AmE), / ˌɪnɪˈfɪʃənt / (BrE)
not effective or productive; wasting time, effort, or resources

Examples

An inefficient system

New management replaced the inefficient workers to improve productivity.
The company's outdated machines made the entire process inefficient.
Because of its inefficient design, the machine requires frequent repairs.
Government bureaucracy can sometimes be slow and inefficient.
The old heating system is inefficient and wastes a lot of energy.
