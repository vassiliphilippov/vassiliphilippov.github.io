co-production

noun
joint production by multiple companies
1.1
Pronunciation: / ˌkoʊ.prəˈdʌk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌkəʊ.prəˈdʌk.ʃən / (BrE)
a production, such as a film or television program, that is jointly created by two or more companies or organizations, often from different countries

Examples

International co-production
Our latest movie is actually a co-production with an independent European company.
The documentary was a co-production involving studios from Japan and France.
This TV series is a co-production between an American and a Canadian network.

Related Terms

  • synonym: collaboration - the action of working with someone to produce something
