offhanded

adjective, adverb
casually thoughtless or inconsiderate
7.2 5.8
Pronunciation: /ˈɑːf.hæn.dɪd/ /; / /ˈɔːf.hæn.dɪd/ (AmE), /ˈɒf.hæn.dɪd/ (BrE)
done or said in a casual, unprepared, or inconsiderate way; without previous thought or attention

Examples

Offhanded remarks
His offhanded remarks during the meeting upset several colleagues.
She gave an offhanded apology that seemed insincere.
The manager's offhanded manner left employees feeling unappreciated.
He gave an offhanded reply when asked about the project details.
Her offhanded comments sometimes come across as insensitive.
Without planning, they made an offhanded decision to travel for the weekend.
His offhanded answer revealed he hadn't given the problem much thought.
She made an offhanded remark that offended several people at the meeting.
The comedian's offhanded jokes kept the audience laughing throughout the evening.

  • synonym: impromptu - done without being planned or rehearsed
  • synonym: improvised - done without preparation; made up on the spot
  • antonym: rehearsed - prepared or practiced in advance
  • antonym: thoughtful - showing careful consideration for others
