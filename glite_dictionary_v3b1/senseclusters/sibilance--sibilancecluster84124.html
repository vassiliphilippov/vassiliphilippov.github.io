sibilance

noun
hissing speech sound
7.6
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪb.ə.ləns/ /; / /ˈsɪb.ɪ.ləns/ (AmE), /ˈsɪb.ə.ləns/ /; / /ˈsɪb.ɪ.ləns/ (BrE)
a hissing or whistling sound in speech, especially the 's' or 'sh' sound, sometimes unwanted in audio recordings

Examples

Audio sibilance
The sibilance in the singer's voice was clearly heard through the microphone.
Audio engineers often reduce sibilance to make speech recordings sound smoother.
Too much sibilance can make a song unpleasant to listen to on headphones.
The audio engineer used a special filter to reduce sibilance in the singer's voice.
Excessive sibilance can make a podcast unpleasant to listen to.
Some microphones are designed to minimize sibilance during recording sessions.

  • synonym: hiss - a sharp s-like sound
  • antonym: resonance - a deep, full, or reverberating sound
