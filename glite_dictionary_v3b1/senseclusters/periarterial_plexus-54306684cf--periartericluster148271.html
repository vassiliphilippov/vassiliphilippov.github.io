periarterial plexus

noun
nerve network around an artery
9.9
Pronunciation: /pə.riː.ɑːr.ˈtɪr.iː.əl ˈplek.səs/ (AmE), /pə.riː.ɑː.ˈtɪə.riː.əl ˈplek.səs/ (BrE)
an autonomic network of nerves that surrounds and accompanies an artery in the body

Examples

The periarterial plexus network
Inflammation can sometimes affect the periarterial plexus, leading to changes in blood flow.
Surgeons take care to avoid damaging the periarterial plexus during vascular procedures.
The periarterial plexus surrounding the coronary artery is crucial for regulating heart function.
