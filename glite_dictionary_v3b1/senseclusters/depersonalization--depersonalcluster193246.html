depersonalization

noun
treating a person as an object, removing individuality
8.8
Pronunciation: / diˌpɝːsənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən /; / diˌpɜrsənələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diːˌpɜːsənəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of regarding or treating a person as a thing, especially by removing their individuality or personal qualities

Examples

Workplace depersonalization
Critics argued that the company's policies led to the depersonalization of its employees.
Excessive bureaucracy often results in the depersonalization of patients in healthcare settings.
The film explores themes of depersonalization in modern urban life.
Some critics argue that technology causes the depersonalisation of our interactions, making relationships feel less genuine.
The depersonalisation of patients in large hospitals can negatively affect their mental well-being.
The company's strict rules led to the depersonalisation of its staff, making everyone feel like a mere number.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dehumanization - The process of depriving a person or group of positive human qualities.
  • synonym: objectification - the act of treating a person as an object or thing, especially sexually
  • antonym: personalization - The act of designing or producing something to meet someone's individual requirements.
