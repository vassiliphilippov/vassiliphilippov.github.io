incunable

noun
early printed book
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkjuːnəbəl / (AmE), / ɪnˈkjuːnəbjuːl / (BrE)
a book printed before the year 1501, during the earliest period of printing with movable type in Europe

Examples

A rare incunable
The museum displayed a rare incunable printed in 1495.
Scholars study incunables to learn about the history of early printing.
Owning an incunable is a dream for many collectors of antique books.

Related Terms

  • antonym: manuscript - a handwritten document or book
  • degree higher: post-incunable - a book printed after 1500 but still in the early period of printing
