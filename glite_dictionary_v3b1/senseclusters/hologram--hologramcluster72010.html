hologram

noun
photographic record for 3D image (holography)
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈhoʊləˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈhɒləɡræm / (BrE)
A photographic record produced by holography, containing information used to reproduce a three-dimensional image.

Examples

A hologram photo
A hologram can store far more information than a traditional photograph.
Engineers use a hologram to analyze stresses within mechanical structures.
The artist displayed a hologram that appeared to float in mid-air.

Related Terms

  • synonym: holography - the technique of producing holograms
