unman

verb
cause to lose courage or self-control
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈmæn / (AmE), / ʌnˈmæn / (BrE)
to make someone lose courage, confidence, or strength; to make someone feel weak or powerless

Examples

To unman someone
She found the ordeal so stressful that it nearly unmanned her.
The haunting noise in the old house unmans even the bravest visitors.
The unexpected criticism completely unmanned him during the meeting.
The terrifying experience seemed to unman him, leaving him unable to speak.
Even the most experienced soldiers can be unmanned by sudden fear.
The news was so shocking that it nearly unmanned the entire team.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cow - to frighten someone into submission or silence
  • synonym: demoralize - to make someone lose confidence or hope
  • antonym: encourage - to give someone confidence or support
