orinasal

noun
speech sound using both oral and nasal passages
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɔriˈneɪsəl / (AmE), / ˌɔːriˈneɪsəl / (BrE)
(in phonetics) a speech sound produced with both the oral and nasal passages open, such as certain French nasal vowels.

Examples

An orinasal sound
In French, the vowel in 'bon' is an orinasal sound.
Linguists study orinasal sounds to understand how air flows during speech.
Some regional accents make greater use of orinasal vowels than others.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nasal - a speech sound produced through the nose
