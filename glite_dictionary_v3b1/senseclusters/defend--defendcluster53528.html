defend

verb
protect against attack or harm
1.5 1.2
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈfend/ /; / /ə.ˈfend/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈfend/ /; / /ə.ˈfend/ (BrE)
to protect someone or something from attack, harm, or danger; to fight against threats

Examples

To defend yourself
She learned karate to better defend herself if necessary.
The soldiers tried to defend the city from enemy forces.
They were forced to defend their decision during the meeting.
Many citizens gathered to defend their rights against the new regulations.
She was determined to defend her decision despite the criticism.
The company plans to defend its market position from competitors.
It is important to defend your rights when they are threatened.
She was eager to defend her title as champion in the upcoming match.
The lawyer will defend her client against the accusations in court.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oppose - to resist or act against something
