androgenesis

noun
embryo development with only paternal chromosomes
Pronunciation: / ˌændroʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌandrəʊˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (BrE)
A form of reproduction in which an embryo develops containing only paternal chromosomes, usually due to the failure of the egg nucleus to participate in fertilization.

Examples

Androgenesis in plants
In plants, androgenesis can be used to produce haploid individuals for breeding purposes.
Scientists observed androgenesis in certain fish species under laboratory conditions.
The study focused on the mechanisms behind androgenesis and its applications in biotechnology.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gynogenesis - reproduction where the embryo contains only maternal chromosomes
