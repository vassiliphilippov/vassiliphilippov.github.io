deterrent

noun
something that discourages action
6.7
Pronunciation: / dɪˈtɜr.ənt / (AmE), / dɪˈtɛr.ənt / (BrE)
a factor or influence that discourages or prevents someone from taking a particular action

Examples

A deterrent to crime
High fines serve as a deterrent to reckless driving.
Lack of funding was a major deterrent to completing the project.
The fear of punishment can be an effective deterrent against crime.

Related Terms

  • antonym: incentive - a thing that motivates or encourages someone to do something
