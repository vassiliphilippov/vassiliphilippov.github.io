lace

noun
delicate decorative fabric of open pattern
Pronunciation: / leɪs / (AmE), / leɪs / (BrE)
a delicate decorative fabric made by weaving threads in an open, symmetrical pattern, often used for trimming or making garments

Examples

A lace dress
Antique shops often have delicate pieces of vintage lace for sale.
She bought a tablecloth trimmed with handmade lace at the market.
The wedding dress was decorated with beautiful lace along the sleeves and collar.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: netting - fabric made by weaving threads with open spaces but usually less decorative than lace
