Isoetales

noun
order of aquatic fern allies
Pronunciation: / ˌaɪsoʊˈɛtəliːz /; / ˌaɪsoʊˈitəliːz / (AmE), / ˌaɪsəʊˈɛtəliːz /; / ˌaɪsəʊˈiːteɪliːz / (BrE)
an order of aquatic or marsh-growing fern-like plants (fern allies) that have existed since the Cenozoic era

Examples

Some taxonomists classify Isoetales separately from the order Lycopodiales.

Scientists have studied fossils that show Isoetales have existed since the Cenozoic era.

Unlike many other plant groups, the Isoetales are true fern allies with unique adaptations to aquatic habitats.
