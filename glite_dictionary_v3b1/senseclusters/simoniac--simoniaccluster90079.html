simoniac

adjective
relating to simony
9.9
Pronunciation: / sɪˈmoʊniˌæk / (AmE), / sɪˈməʊniˌak / (BrE)
relating to or guilty of simony, especially involving the buying or selling of church offices or sacred things

Examples

Simoniac practices
The council condemned the simoniac practices that had corrupted the local church.
He was accused of being a simoniac bishop after evidence of bribery emerged.
Medieval texts often criticize simoniac behavior among high-ranking clergy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: corrupt - dishonest or immoral, especially involving bribery
  • antonym: honest - free of deceit or fraud
