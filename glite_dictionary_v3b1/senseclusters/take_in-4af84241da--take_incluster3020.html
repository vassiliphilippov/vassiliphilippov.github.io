take in

verb
earn or receive money, esp. as profit
Pronunciation: / teɪk ɪn / (AmE), / teɪk ɪn / (BrE)
(informal) To earn or receive (money), especially as profit or wages.

Examples

To take in money
He hopes to take in enough this year to afford a vacation.
The charity took in hundreds of donations after the appeal.
The restaurant managed to take in over $10,000 during its opening weekend.
