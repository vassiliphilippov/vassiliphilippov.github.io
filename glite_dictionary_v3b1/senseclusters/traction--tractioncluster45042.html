traction

noun
friction between a moving object and its surface
Pronunciation: / ˈtræk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈtræk.ʃən / (BrE)
the friction between a moving body and the surface it moves on, allowing it to move or stay in place, especially in reference to vehicles

Examples

Traction on ice
Good tires can improve traction in wet or slippery conditions.
Mountain bikes are designed to provide better traction on rough terrain.
The car lost traction on the icy road and began to slide.

Related Terms

  • synonym: friction - the resistance that one surface or object encounters when moving over another
