misguided

adjective
based on mistaken judgment or ideas
Pronunciation: / mɪsˈɡaɪdɪd / (AmE), / mɪsˈɡaɪdɪd / (BrE)
having or showing faulty judgment or reasoning; based on mistaken beliefs or ideas

Examples

A misguided effort
Despite his misguided efforts, the project failed to meet its goals.
She offered misguided advice, believing it would help her friend.
The new policy was widely criticized as misguided by experts.
He acted out of misguided loyalty to his friend.
Her misguided attempt to help only made the situation worse.
The policy was well-intentioned but ultimately misguided.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ill-conceived - poorly planned or thought out
  • synonym: mistaken - wrong or incorrect in opinion, judgment, or action
  • antonym: well-advised - based on careful or sensible judgment
  • antonym: well-informed - having accurate knowledge or understanding
