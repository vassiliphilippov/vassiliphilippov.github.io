trifid beggar-ticks

noun
a Eurasian bur marigold plant
5.9
Pronunciation: / ˈtraɪfɪd ˈbɛɡər tɪks / (AmE), / ˈtrɪfɪd ˈbɛɡə tɪks / (BrE)
A Eurasian species of bur marigold (Bidens tripartita), a plant with trifid leaves and small yellow flower heads, often found in wet habitats.

Examples

Trifid beggar-ticks seeds
After walking through the marsh, his socks were covered with seeds from trifid beggar-ticks.
Her field guide helped her identify the trifid beggar-ticks by their distinctive trifid leaves.
The wetland was full of trifid beggar-ticks growing among the other wildflowers.
