swamp

noun
entrenched political establishment
8.2 0.9/m
Pronunciation: / swÉ‘mp / (AmE), / swÉ’mp / (BrE)
informal (politics) the entrenched political establishment or bureaucracy, especially when regarded as corrupt, inefficient, or resistant to reform.

Examples

The political swamp

The candidate promised to drain the swamp and replace career politicians with fresh voices.
Reporters wrote that corruption among lobbyists and officials had turned the capital into a swamp.
