smooth alder

noun
North American shrub with smooth bark
Pronunciation: / smuð ˈɔldɚ / (AmE), / smuːð ˈɔːldə / (BrE)
a shrub native to the eastern United States, recognized for its smooth bark and commonly found in wetland areas

Examples

The smooth alder shrub
In early spring, the smooth alder produces small clusters of reddish catkins.
The smooth alder thrives in moist woodlands and along the banks of streams.
Wildlife often seek shelter among the dense branches of the smooth alder.
