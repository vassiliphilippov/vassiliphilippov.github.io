differential psychology

noun
branch of psychology studying individual differences
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃəl saɪˈkɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌdɪfəˈrɛnʃəl saɪˈkɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of psychology that studies the measurable differences between individuals, such as abilities, intelligence, or personality traits.

Examples

Differential psychology studies differences
Differential psychology focuses on how and why individuals differ in their mental processes.
Advances in differential psychology have improved methods for measuring personality traits.
She chose to specialize in differential psychology to better understand variations in intelligence.
