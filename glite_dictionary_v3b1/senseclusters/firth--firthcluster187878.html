firth

noun
long narrow estuary, esp. in Scotland
9.0 9.9
Pronunciation: / fɝːθ / (AmE), / fɜːθ / (BrE)
a long, narrow inlet of the sea; an estuary, especially on the coast of Scotland

Examples

A Scottish firth

The River Clyde widens into a broad firth before it meets the sea.
They enjoyed kayaking along the scenic firth separating the mainland from the islands.
