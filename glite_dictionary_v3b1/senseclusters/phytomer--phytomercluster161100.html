phytomer

noun
plant structural unit
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfaɪtoʊmər / (AmE), / ˈfaɪtəmə / (BrE)
a repeating structural unit in a plant, typically consisting of a stem segment, leaves, and buds

Examples

A phytomer unit
Each phytomer in the grass stem consists of a node, internode, and leaf.
Botanists study how phytomers develop to understand plant growth patterns.
The arrangement of phytomers affects the overall shape and function of the plant.

Related Terms

  • synonym: internode - the part of a plant stem between two nodes
  • synonym: node - the point on a plant stem where leaves or branches originate
