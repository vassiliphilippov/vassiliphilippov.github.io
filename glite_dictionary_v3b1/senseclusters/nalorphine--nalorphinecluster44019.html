nalorphine

noun
drug counteracting narcotic overdose
9.9
Pronunciation: / nəˈlɔrˌfin / (AmE), / nəˈlɔːfiːn / (BrE)
a drug used to counteract the effects of narcotic overdose, especially morphine, by acting as a narcotic antagonist

Examples

nalorphine drug
As an early narcotic antagonist, nalorphine was widely studied before more modern alternatives were developed.
Researchers evaluated the safety profile of nalorphine in treating opioid poisonings.
The doctor administered nalorphine to reverse the effects of the morphine overdose.

Related Terms

  • synonym: naloxone - a drug that reverses opioid overdoses
