noncausality

noun
lack of causation
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnkɔːˈzælɪti / (AmE), / ˌnɒnkɔːˈzælɪti / (BrE)
the state or condition in which there is no cause-and-effect relationship between two or more things

Examples

Noncausality of a link
The scientist emphasized the noncausality of the link between the two variables in her report.
Understanding noncausality is important when interpreting statistical correlations in research.
The professor explained that correlation does not imply noncausality or causality without further evidence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: causality - the relationship between cause and effect
  • synonym: independence - the state of being unrelated or not influenced by something else
