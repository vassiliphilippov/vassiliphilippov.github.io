Antonie van Leeuwenhoek

proper noun
Dutch pioneer in microscopy and microbiology
Pronunciation: / ˈæn.toʊ.ni væn ˈleɪ.vənˌhʊk / (AmE), / ˈæn.tə.ni væn ˈleɪ.vənˌhʊk / (BrE)
(1632–1723) A Dutch pioneer of microscopy who was one of the first to observe and describe single-celled organisms, laying the foundations for microbiology.

Examples

Antonie van Leeuwenhoek microscope pioneer
Antonie van Leeuwenhoek is often called the 'Father of Microbiology' due to his groundbreaking discoveries.
Students learned about Antonie van Leeuwenhoek and his observations of blood cells in their biology class.
The microscope designs improved by Antonie van Leeuwenhoek enabled scientists to see bacteria for the first time.
