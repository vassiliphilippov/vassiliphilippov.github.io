neostriatum

noun
brain region for movement
Pronunciation: / ˌniːoʊˈstraɪətəm / (AmE), / ˌniːəʊˈstraɪətəm / (BrE)
the part of the mammalian brain (basal ganglia) that includes the caudate nucleus and putamen, involved in movement, coordination, and habit learning

Examples

Mammalian neostriatum
Damage to the neostriatum can result in difficulties with movement and coordination.
Researchers study the neostriatum to better understand how habits are formed in the brain.
The neostriatum receives signals from many other brain regions to help control voluntary actions.
The neostriatum plays a key role in controlling voluntary movements.
Researchers studied how the neostriatum is affected in patients with Parkinson's disease.
Damage to the neostriatum can result in problems with motor coordination.

Related Terms

  • antonym: paleostriatum - an older part of the striatum, also called the globus pallidus
  • synonym: striatum - a subcortical part of the forebrain involved in movement and reward
