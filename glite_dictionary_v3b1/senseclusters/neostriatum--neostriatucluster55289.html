neostriatum

noun
brain region for movement
9.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåniÀêo äÀàstra…™…ôt…ôm / (AmE), / ÀåniÀê…ô äÀàstra…™…ôt…ôm / (BrE)
the part of the mammalian brain (basal ganglia) that includes the caudate nucleus and putamen, involved in movement, coordination, and habit learning

Examples

Mammalian neostriatum

Researchers study the neostriatum to better understand how habits are formed in the brain.
The neostriatum receives signals from many other brain regions to help control voluntary actions.

Synonyms

  • striatum
    9.9 0.26/m
    striatum is the brain region made of caudate and lentiform nuclei, while neostriatum refers specifically to the caudate and putamen.
  • corpus striatum
    9.1 0.06/m
    corpus striatum is the older name for the mass of caudate and lenticular nuclei, while neostriatum names just the caudate and putamen.
  • striate body
    9.9 0.00/m
    striate body is a traditional term for the caudate plus lenticular mass, while neostriatum refers specifically to the caudate and putamen.
