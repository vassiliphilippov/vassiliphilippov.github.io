transform

verb
change in form, appearance, or nature
1.0
Pronunciation: / trænˈsfɔrm /; / trænsˈfɔrm / (AmE), / trænsˈfɔːm / (BrE)
to change something significantly in form, appearance, or character; to make something completely different

Examples

To transform a city
Exposure to different cultures can transform your worldview.
The new policies will transform the way we approach education.
They hope to transform the abandoned warehouse into a community center.
He used his skills to transform a neglected garden into a vibrant park.
Modern technology can transform the way we communicate and share information.
The artist managed to transform scraps of metal into a beautiful sculpture.
The caterpillar will transform into a beautiful butterfly.
The city has transformed dramatically over the past decade.
With a wave of her wand, she transformed the pumpkin into a carriage.

  • synonym: translate - to change from one form, medium, or language into another
