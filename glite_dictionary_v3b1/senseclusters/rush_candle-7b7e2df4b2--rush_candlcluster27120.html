rush candle

noun
historical candle with a rush wick
9.7
Pronunciation: / rʌʃ ˈkændl̩ / (AmE), / rʌʃ ˈkændl / (BrE)
a candle made with a stem of rush as the wick, often coated in tallow

Examples

A rush candle wick
Before modern lighting, the humble rush candle was commonly used in rural homes.
In the dim light of a rush candle, she quietly read her letter by the fire.
They used a rush candle to illuminate the small cottage at night.
