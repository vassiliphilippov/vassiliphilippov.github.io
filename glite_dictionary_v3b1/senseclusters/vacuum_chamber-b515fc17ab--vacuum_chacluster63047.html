vacuum chamber

noun
sealed container with near vacuum
3.1 3.8
Pronunciation: /ˈvæk.juːm ˈtʃeɪm.bər/ /; / /ˈvæk.ju.əm ˈtʃeɪm.bər/ (AmE), /ˈvæk.juːm ˈtʃeɪm.bə/ /; / /ˈvæk.ju.əm ˈtʃeɪm.bə/ (BrE)
A sealed enclosure from which almost all air or other gases have been removed, creating a near vacuum, often used in scientific experiments or manufacturing processes.

Examples

A vacuum chamber test
Spacecraft components are often tested in a vacuum chamber to simulate outer space conditions.
Technicians monitored the pressure levels inside the vacuum chamber during the test.
The experiment was conducted inside a vacuum chamber to eliminate interference from air.
