accession

noun
formal agreement or consent, often unwillingly
Pronunciation: / əkˈsɛʃ.ən / (AmE), / əkˈsɛʃ.ən / (BrE)
the act of agreeing to a request, demand, or treaty, often reluctantly

Examples

Accession to a treaty
His accession to the treaty was motivated by political necessity rather than enthusiasm.
She gave her reluctant accession to the committee's proposal.
The government's accession to public demands came after weeks of intense protest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assent - the expression of approval or agreement
  • synonym: consent - permission for something to happen or agreement to do something
  • antonym: refusal - the act of declining or not agreeing
