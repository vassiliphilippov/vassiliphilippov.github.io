obscenely

adverb
in an obscene or lewd manner
7.6 0.11/m
Pronunciation: / …ôbÀàsinli / (AmE), / …ôbÀàsiÀênli / (BrE)
in a manner that is offensive to accepted standards of decency, especially in a sexual way; lewdly or indecently

Examples

Acting obscenely in public
He was fined for behaving obscenely in public.
The comedian's jokes were obscenely explicit, shocking some members of the audience.
The painting was removed from the gallery for being considered obscenely graphic.

Synonyms

  • lewdly
    8.5 0.05/m
    lewdly shows clear sexual indecency or rude sexual action, while obscenely covers things offensive to general standards of decency.
  • smuttily
    9.9 0.00/m
    smuttily describes something dirty or pornographic in a cheeky, informal way, while obscenely is more formal and seriously offensive.
  • lasciviously
    9.9 0.11/m
    lasciviously shows strong, open sexual desire or look, while obscenely refers to behavior that offends common standards of decency.
  • indecently
    6.7 0.09/m
    indecently means acting against moral or social rules, while obscenely usually points to especially sexual or shocking conduct.
  • vulgarly
    8.3 0.07/m
    vulgarly is crude, coarse, or in bad taste and may not be sexual, while obscenely usually has a stronger sexual meaning.
