jarl

noun
Scandinavian noble
Pronunciation: / jɑrl / (AmE), / jɑːl / (BrE)
a noble or chief in medieval Scandinavia, ranking just below a king and similar to an earl

Examples

The jarl ruled
The powerful jarl controlled vast lands and commanded many warriors.
During the feast, the jarl listened to the concerns of his people.
Legends tell of a jarl who united several clans under his rule.
The powerful jarl commanded respect from everyone in his territory.
In Viking society, a jarl held significant authority and wealth.
Legends tell of a wise jarl who united several clans under his rule.

Related Terms

  • synonym: earl - a British nobleman ranking below a marquess and above a viscount
  • degree higher: king - the male ruler of an independent state, especially one who inherits the position by right of birth
