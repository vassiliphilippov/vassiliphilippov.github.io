mean

verb
have as a consequence; indicate result
Pronunciation: / miːn / (AmE), / miːn / (BrE)
to have as a consequence or result; to indicate or bring about a particular outcome

Examples

Mean a result
For many parents, remote work means more time spent with their children.
Missing your flight means you’ll have to find a hotel for the night.
The new tax law means that some people will pay more next year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: entail - to involve as a necessary or inevitable consequence
  • synonym: imply - to entail or suggest as a logical consequence
