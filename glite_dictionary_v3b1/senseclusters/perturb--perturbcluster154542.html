perturb

verb
alter a celestial body's orbit
8.8 6.1
Pronunciation: /pər.ˈtɜːrb/ (AmE), /pə.ˈtɜːb/ (BrE)
to disturb, alter, or disrupt the normal path, state, or behavior of something, especially in a scientific or technical context

Examples

To perturb an orbit
Astronomers observed that Neptune’s presence would perturb the orbits of other planets.
Small asteroids can perturb a planet’s orbit if they pass close enough.
The gravitational pull of Jupiter can significantly perturb the paths of nearby comets.
External electromagnetic fields can perturb the orbits of electrons in an atom.
The experiment showed how a nearby molecule could perturb the energy levels of the atom.
When the system is perturbed by an external force, its natural motion is disrupted.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disturb - to interfere with the normal arrangement or position of something
  • antonym: stabilize - to make or become stable
