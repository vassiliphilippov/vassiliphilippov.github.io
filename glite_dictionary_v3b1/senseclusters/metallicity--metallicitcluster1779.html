metallicity

noun
proportion of heavy elements
9.9
Pronunciation: /me.tə.ˈlɪs.ɪ.tɪ/ (AmE), /me.tə.ˈlɪs.ɪ.tɪ/ (BrE)
the proportion of elements heavier than hydrogen and helium present in a star or other astronomical object

Examples

Star metallicity
Astronomers measured the metallicity of the distant star to learn about its formation.
A high metallicity in a planet's atmosphere can indicate the presence of complex molecules.
The metallicity of our sun is higher than that of many older stars.

  • synonym: abundance - the quantity of a particular element or substance present
