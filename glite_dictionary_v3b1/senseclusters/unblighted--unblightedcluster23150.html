unblighted

adjective
not damaged
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈblaɪtɪd / (AmE), / ʌnˈblaɪtɪd / (BrE)
not damaged, spoiled, or affected by blight or harm

Examples

An unblighted building
The old mansion stood unblighted among the abandoned buildings in the neighborhood.
Despite the drought, the crops remained unblighted and healthy throughout the season.
Her optimism was unblighted by the setbacks she faced during the project.
