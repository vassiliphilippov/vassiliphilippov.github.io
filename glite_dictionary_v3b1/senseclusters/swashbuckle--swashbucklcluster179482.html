swashbuckle

verb
to act daringly
Pronunciation: / ˈswɑʃˌbʌkəl / (AmE), / ˈswɒʃˌbʌkəl / (BrE)
to act in a daring, adventurous, or flamboyant way, especially like a heroic swordsman in stories or plays

Examples

To swashbuckle boldly
The actor loved to swashbuckle in his role as a pirate captain.
Children often swashbuckle around the playground, pretending to be heroes.
He would swashbuckle through the party, charming everyone with his bold stories.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cower - to crouch down in fear or behave timidly
  • synonym: daredevil - a person who enjoys taking dangerous risks
  • synonym: show off - to behave in a way that attracts attention
