seem

verb
appear to be; give an impression
5.1
Pronunciation: / siːm / (AmE), / siːm / (BrE)
to give the impression of being or doing something; to appear to be (especially when not certain)

Examples

It seemed like
From their conversation, it seemed like they already knew the outcome.
It seems that Margaret will arrive ahead of schedule despite the traffic.
Sometimes the truth seems stranger than fiction.
After the rain, the air seemed fresher and cooler.
It may seem strange at first, but you'll get used to it.
She doesn't seem interested in joining the team, though she hasn't said no directly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: appear - to become visible or to give the impression of being something
  • synonym: look - to have the appearance of being something
