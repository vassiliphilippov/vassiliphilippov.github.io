deadlight

noun
shutter for a ship's porthole
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛdˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɛd.laɪt / (BrE)
A strong metal or wooden shutter fitted over a ship's porthole to protect against heavy seas or storms.

Examples

A deadlight shutter
He checked that every deadlight was fastened tightly during the storm.
It is crucial to close the deadlight whenever rough weather is approaching at sea.
The crew secured the deadlight before the gale hit to prevent water from entering the cabin.
