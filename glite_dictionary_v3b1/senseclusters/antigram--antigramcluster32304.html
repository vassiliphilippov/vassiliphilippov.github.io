antigram

noun
anagram with opposite meaning
Pronunciation: / ˈæn.tiˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈæn.tɪˌɡræm / (BrE)
An anagram whose resulting word or phrase has a meaning opposite to that of the original.

Examples

An antigram example
In linguistics, an antigram is considered a special form of anagram with contrasting meanings.
She enjoys challenging her friends to discover an antigram from a difficult word.
The word 'united' and 'untied' form an antigram, as their meanings oppose each other.
