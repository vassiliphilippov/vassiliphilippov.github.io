telehealth

noun
remote health care
4.2
Pronunciation: / ˈtɛliˌhɛlθ / (AmE), / ˈtɛliˌhɛlθ / (BrE)
the use of technology to provide healthcare services or support remotely, including medical consultations, education, and administration

Examples

Telehealth appointment
Many patients now prefer telehealth appointments for convenience.
The hospital expanded its telehealth services to reach people in rural areas.
During the pandemic, telehealth became an essential way to access medical advice safely.
Many rural communities benefit from telehealth programs that offer health education and support remotely.
The hospital expanded its telehealth services to include online workshops for patients and staff.
Through telehealth, healthcare providers can deliver training and administrative support without being on site.

Related Terms

  • antonym: in-person care - health care provided face-to-face at a medical facility
  • synonym: telemedicine - the remote diagnosis and treatment of patients using telecommunications technology
