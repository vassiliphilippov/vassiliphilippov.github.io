parlous

adjective
fraught with danger or risk
9.9 0.10/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…ërl…ôs / (AmE), / Ààp…ëÀêl…ôs / (BrE)
full of danger or uncertainty; precarious or risky.

Examples

A parlous situation
Due to the parlous state of the economy, many families struggled to make ends meet.
The hikers faced a parlous climb up the icy mountain trail.
They made a parlous decision to continue sailing despite the approaching storm.

Synonyms

  • precarious
    7.6 4.17/m
    precarious describes an unstable, insecure situation that may collapse, while parlous adds a sense of danger and uncertainty.
  • perilous
    7.5 3.13/m
    perilous means very dangerous or hazardous in a clear, often physical way, while parlous includes risk and uncertainty.

Related Terms
