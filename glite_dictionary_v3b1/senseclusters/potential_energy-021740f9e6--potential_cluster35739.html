potential energy

noun
energy due to object's position or condition
1.0 3.2
Pronunciation: /pə.ˈten.ʃəl ˈen.ər.dʒiː/ (AmE), /pə.ˈten.ʃəl ˈen.ə.dʒi/ (BrE)
the energy that an object possesses because of its position or condition, rather than its motion

Examples

Potential energy of position
The boulder perched on the hill has a large amount of potential energy.
Water stored behind a dam has potential energy that can be converted into electricity.
When you stretch a rubber band, you increase its potential energy.

Related Terms
