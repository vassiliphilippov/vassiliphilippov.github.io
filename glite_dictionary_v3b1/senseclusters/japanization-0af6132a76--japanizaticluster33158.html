Japanization

noun
economic stagnation trend
9.9
Pronunciation: /dʒə.pæn.ə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /dʒə.pə.nə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /dʒæ.pæn.ə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /dʒə.pæn.ə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process by which a country's economy develops characteristics similar to Japan's post-1990 period, such as prolonged low growth, low inflation or deflation, and an aging population

Examples

Economic Japanization
Economists fear that prolonged low interest rates could lead to Japanization in other developed countries.
The government introduced new policies to avoid the Japanization of the national economy.
Some analysts argue that Japanization is a risk for countries facing an aging population and weak demand.

Related Terms

  • antonym: recovery - a return to economic growth after a downturn
  • synonym: stagnation - a period of little or no economic growth
