buskin

noun
knee-high boot, often historical or theatrical
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌskɪn / (AmE), / ˈbʌskɪn / (BrE)
a boot that covers the foot and lower leg, typically reaching halfway up to the knee, often associated with ancient Greek and Roman actors or traditional costume.

Examples

A pair of buskins
A pair of leather buskins was displayed in the museum's ancient footwear exhibit.
Medieval knights were sometimes depicted in paintings wearing ornate buskins.
The actor wore a buskin as part of his costume in the Greek tragedy.
