gone

adjective
dead; no longer alive (euphemistic)
Pronunciation: / ɡɑn / (AmE), / ɡɒn / (BrE)
dead, destroyed, or doomed; having no chance of survival or escape (including as a euphemism for death)

Examples

He is gone forever
After a long illness, he is finally gone but never forgotten.
Many of the old friends from school are now gone, but their memories remain.
She found comfort in believing that her mother, though gone, was always with her in spirit.
He realized their chances of winning were gone after the injury.
If the fire spreads to the barn, it's gone for sure.
Once the storm hit, we knew we were gone.

Related Terms

  • antonym: alive - living; not dead
  • synonym: dead - no longer alive
  • synonym: deceased - having died; dead (formal)
  • synonym: doomed - certain to fail or be destroyed
  • antonym: safe - not in danger or destroyed
