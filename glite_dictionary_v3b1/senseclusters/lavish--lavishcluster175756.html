lavish

adjective
extravagant; richly abundant
1.5 4.4
Pronunciation: /ˈlæ.vɪʃ/ (AmE), /ˈlæ.vɪʃ/ (BrE)
rich, luxurious, or very generous; given or done in great amounts

Examples

A lavish party
Her birthday party was a lavish affair, complete with live music and fireworks.
The wedding reception featured a lavish display of flowers and gourmet food.
They spent a lavish amount of money renovating their home.
He received lavish praise from his teachers for his hard work.
The guests were impressed by the lavish decorations at the wedding.
They donated a lavish amount of money to the charity last year.

Related Terms

  • antonym: frugal - sparing or economical with regard to money or food
  • synonym: generous - showing a readiness to give more of something than is strictly necessary or expected
  • synonym: luxurious - extremely comfortable or elegant, especially in a way that involves great expense
  • antonym: stingy - unwilling to give or spend; ungenerous
  • synonym: sumptuous - splendid and expensive-looking; involving great expense
