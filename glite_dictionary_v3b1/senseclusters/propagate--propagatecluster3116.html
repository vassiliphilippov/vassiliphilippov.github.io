propagate

verb
(of waves) travel through a medium or space
Pronunciation: / ˈprɑːpəˌɡeɪt / (AmE), / ˈprɒpəɡeɪt / (BrE)
to transmit or move through a medium, especially for waves like sound or light

Examples

Waves propagate through air
In a vacuum, light propagates faster than in any other medium.
Radio waves propagate through the atmosphere in different ways depending on their frequency.
The scientist explained how seismic waves propagate during an earthquake.
Light can propagate across millions of kilometers in the vacuum of space.
Sound waves propagate through air at different speeds depending on temperature and pressure.
Wireless signals propagate more efficiently in open spaces than through dense walls.

Related Terms

  • synonym: transmit - to send or transfer (especially waves or signals) from one place to another
