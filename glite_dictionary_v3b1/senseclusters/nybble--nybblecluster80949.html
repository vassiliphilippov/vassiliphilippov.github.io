nybble

noun
group of four bits (half a byte)
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈnɪb.əl / (AmE), / ˈnɪb.əl / (BrE)
a group of four bits, or half a byte, used in computing and digital systems

Examples

A nybble in computing
Each hexadecimal digit represents a nybble in binary code.
Programming with nybbles requires careful bit manipulation.
The processor can operate on both a nybble and a full byte.
