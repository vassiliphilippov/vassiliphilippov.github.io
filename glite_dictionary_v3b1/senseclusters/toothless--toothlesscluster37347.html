toothless

adjective
lacking effectiveness or power
9.1 4.5
Pronunciation: /ˈtuːθ.ləs/ /; / /ˈtuːθ.lɪs/ (AmE), /ˈtuːθ.ləs/ /; / /ˈtuːθ.lɪs/ (BrE)
(of a rule, law, organization, etc.) lacking power or effectiveness; unable to act or enforce

Examples

A toothless law
Critics say the treaty is largely toothless without a mechanism for imposing penalties.
Many consider the committee toothless because it has no authority to enforce its recommendations.
The new environmental regulation proved toothless after industry lobbyists weakened its main provisions.
Some believe the new policy is toothless because it lacks any real enforcement measures.
The proposed law was criticized as being toothless and unlikely to change anything.
Without proper funding, the organization remained toothless in the face of the crisis.
