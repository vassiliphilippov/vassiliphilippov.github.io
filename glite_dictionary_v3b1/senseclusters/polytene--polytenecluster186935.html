polytene

noun
giant chromosome
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑlɪˌtin / (AmE), / ˈpɒlɪˌtiːn / (BrE)
a giant chromosome consisting of many parallel copies of chromatids, commonly found in certain insect cells

Examples

A polytene under microscope
Scientists observed a polytene under the microscope to study gene expression in fruit flies.
The large size of a polytene makes it easier to analyze chromosomal structure.
Insect salivary glands often contain a visible polytene that helps researchers map genes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chromosome - a structure of DNA and protein carrying genetic information
