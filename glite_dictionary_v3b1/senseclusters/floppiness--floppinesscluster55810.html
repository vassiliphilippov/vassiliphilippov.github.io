floppiness

noun
muscle limpness
7.7
Pronunciation: / ˈflɑpinəs / (AmE), / ˈflɒpinəs / (BrE)
a medical condition in which muscles are unusually loose or limp, resulting in decreased resistance to movement

Examples

Muscle floppiness
The doctor noticed floppiness in the baby's arms and recommended a neurological evaluation.
Physical therapy can help improve muscle tone in children with floppiness.
Parents should consult a specialist if they observe persistent floppiness in their infant's movements.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hypotonia - reduced muscle tone or tension
  • antonym: stiffness - the quality of being rigid or difficult to move
