meld

verb
blend or merge gradually
7.9 4.3
Pronunciation: /meld/ (AmE), /meld/ (BrE)
to blend, mix, or combine things so that they become one

Examples

To meld flavors
Over the years, the town's culture has melded with the city's influence.
The flavors of the different ingredients slowly meld together as the stew simmers.
Their voices melded in perfect harmony during the choir performance.
As their voices melded in harmony, the song took on new life.
The artist managed to meld classical and modern styles in her painting.
The chef skillfully melds spices from around the world to create unique dishes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blend - to mix together so as to become inseparable
  • synonym: integrate - to make part of a whole; to combine
  • synonym: merge - to combine or unite to form a single entity
  • antonym: separate - to set or keep apart; not blend
