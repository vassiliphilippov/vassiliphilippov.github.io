Jean-Baptiste Lamarck

proper noun
French biologist; proposed inheritance of acquired traits
Pronunciation: / ʒɑ̃ bɑːpˈtist ləˈmɑrk / (AmE), / ʒɑ̃ bɑːpˈtiːst laˈmɑːk / (BrE)
(1744–1829) A French naturalist renowned for his theory that evolution occurs through the inheritance of acquired characteristics.

Examples

Jean-Baptiste Lamarck theory
Jean-Baptiste Lamarck was one of the first scientists to propose a comprehensive theory of evolution.
Many textbooks discuss the differences between Darwin's and Jean-Baptiste Lamarck's ideas.
The concept of the inheritance of acquired traits is closely associated with Jean-Baptiste Lamarck.

  • synonym: Charles Darwin - English naturalist who developed the theory of evolution by natural selection
