broadside

adjective, adverb
toward a full side; side-on
4.1
Pronunciation: / ˈbrɔdˌsaɪd / (AmE), / ˈbrɔːd.saɪd /; / ˈbrɔːdsaɪd / (BrE)
with the side facing or being hit; directed toward or hitting the entire side of something

Examples

Broadside impact
The building collapsed after receiving a broadside hit from the demolished wall.
The car was struck broadside by a speeding truck at the intersection.
The ship suffered a broadside attack in the middle of the night.
A strong wind struck the boat broadside, nearly overturning it.
The car was hit broadside by another vehicle in the intersection.
The truck was caught broadside by the oncoming train.
