corrugation

noun
ridge or groove on a corrugated surface
Pronunciation: / ˌkɔrəˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌkɒrəˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
a ridge or groove on a surface, or the process of forming material into ridges and grooves

Examples

Cardboard corrugations
Dust collects easily between the corrugations of the cardboard.
The metal sheet has a series of corrugations for added strength.
The roof was designed with deep corrugations to channel rainwater away quickly.
Cardboard with deep corrugation is commonly used for packaging fragile items.
Engineers examined the corrugation patterns to optimize the material's durability.
The corrugation of the sheet metal increased its strength.

Related Terms

  • synonym: groove - a long, narrow cut or depression in a surface
  • synonym: ridge - a raised or projecting part of a surface
