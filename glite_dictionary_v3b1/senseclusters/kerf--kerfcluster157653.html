kerf

noun
cut for bending
Pronunciation: / kɜrf / (AmE), / kɜːf / (BrE)
a cut, slit, groove, or series of notches made in a material by a saw or cutting tool

Examples

A kerf in wood
The carpenter made a kerf in the plywood to help it bend around the curve.

A shallow kerf allows the material to flex without breaking.
The width of the kerf determines how much wood is removed by the saw.
