rail

noun
conducting bar in an electromagnetic launcher
8.6
Pronunciation: /reɪl/ (AmE), /reɪl/ (BrE)
(engineering, electronics) a conducting bar or rail in an electromagnetic propulsion device or launcher that carries current between electrodes and helps generate the magnetic forces that accelerate a projectile or armature.

Examples

A rail in a railgun
Researchers replaced the damaged rail in the lab's railgun before the scheduled acceleration test.
High currents through the parallel rails created a strong magnetic field that propelled the projectile downrange.
Engineers designed a copper rail with integrated cooling channels to endure repeated electromagnetic launch cycles.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bar - a long, rigid piece of metal or other material; general physical counterpart
  • synonym: conductor - a material or component that allows electric current to flow; similar technical function
