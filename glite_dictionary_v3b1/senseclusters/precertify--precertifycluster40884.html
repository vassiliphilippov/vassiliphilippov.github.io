precertify

verb
to certify in advance
7.4
Pronunciation: /priː.ˈsɜːr.tɪ.faɪ/ /; / /prɪ.ˈsɜːr.tɪ.faɪ/ (AmE), /priː.ˈsɜː.tɪ.faɪ/ /; / /prɪ.ˈsɜː.tɪ.faɪ/ (BrE)
to officially certify or approve something in advance before it is used, sold, or distributed

Examples

To precertify a procedure
The insurance company must precertify the procedure before the hospital can schedule it.
Manufacturers often precertify their products to meet international safety standards.
You should precertify your travel documents to avoid delays at the border.
