chase

verb
cut a groove or furrow (esp. in masonry)
6.7
Pronunciation: / tʃeɪs / (AmE), / tʃeɪs / (BrE)
to cut a groove or pattern into a surface, especially for decoration or to embed something

Examples

To chase a groove
Before installing the pipe, workers chased a narrow furrow along the base of the pillar.

During the restoration, he chased new details onto the antique frame.
Skilled workers chase grooves into metal to create both functional and decorative lines.
The artisan carefully chased a delicate pattern into the silver goblet.
