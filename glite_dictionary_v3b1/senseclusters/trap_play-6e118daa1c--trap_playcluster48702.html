trap play

noun
American football play using a deceptive block
4.3 5.8
Pronunciation: /træp pleɪ/ (AmE), /træp pleɪ/ (BrE)
(American football) a play in which a defensive player is intentionally allowed to cross the line of scrimmage and is then blocked from the side or behind, allowing the ball carrier to advance through the vacated area.

Examples

A trap play in football
Executing a perfect trap play, the running back found a wide-open lane through the middle.
The coach called a trap play to catch the defense off guard near the goal line.
The team's ability to disguise the trap play helped them gain several crucial first downs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: draw play - a football play designed to appear as a pass but turns into a run
