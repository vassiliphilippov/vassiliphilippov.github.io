allotype

noun
protein genetic variant
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈæləˌtaɪp / (AmE), / ˈalətʌɪp / (BrE)
a genetically determined variant of a protein, especially an antibody, found within a species

Examples

Antibody allotype
Scientists identified a new allotype that may influence how patients respond to certain vaccines.
The presence of a specific allotype can affect the effectiveness of antibody treatments.
Researchers compared the allotype profiles of different populations to study genetic diversity.

Related Terms

  • synonym: idiotype - the unique set of antigen-binding sites of an antibody
  • antonym: isotype - a class or subclass of a protein, especially an antibody, shared by all members of a species
