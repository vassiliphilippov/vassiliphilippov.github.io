gumbo

noun
sticky, fine-grained silty soil
Pronunciation: / ˈɡʌmˌboʊ / (AmE), / ˈɡʌmbəʊ / (BrE)
A fine-grained, silty soil that becomes waxy and very sticky when saturated with water.

Examples

Sticky gumbo soil
During the spring, the gumbo in the riverbed became almost impassable for hikers.
Farmers often avoid areas with gumbo soil because machinery can get stuck.
The fields were difficult to plow after heavy rainfall because the gumbo turned extremely sticky.
