gorgonzola

noun
Italian blue cheese
Pronunciation: / ˌɡɔr.ɡənˈzoʊ.lə / (AmE), / ˌɡɔː.ɡənˈzəʊ.lə / (BrE)
A type of Italian blue cheese with a strong flavor, made from cow's milk and streaked with blue veins of mold.

Examples

A slice of gorgonzola
A sprinkle of gorgonzola adds a tangy flavor to any salad.
She served crackers with slices of gorgonzola at the party.
We ordered a pizza topped with gorgonzola and mushrooms.
