crime

noun
morally wrong or evil act
1.0
Pronunciation: / kraɪm / (AmE), / kraɪm / (BrE)
An action or behavior considered morally wrong or evil, even if not punishable by law.

Examples

A crime against morals
Many people believe that lying is a crime against one's own conscience.
Some see wasting food as a crime when so many are hungry.
The novel deals with emotional crimes committed within families.

Related Terms

  • synonym: offense - an act that violates a law, rule, or code of conduct
  • synonym: sin - an immoral act considered to be a transgression against divine law
  • antonym: virtue - behavior showing high moral standards
