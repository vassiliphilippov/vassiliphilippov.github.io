(in information security) a defensive strategy that uses decoy systems, fake data, traps, or other lures to mislead attackers, detect intrusions, and protect real resources.

Examples

Cybersecurity deception
Many organizations now deploy deception technologies to lure attackers into honeypots and gather forensic evidence.
The security team used network-level deception to detect lateral movement before sensitive data was compromised.
Deploying server honeypots and fake credentials is common practice in modern deception for cyber defense.
