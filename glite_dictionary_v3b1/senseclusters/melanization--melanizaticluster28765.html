melanization

noun
becoming darker with melanin
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛlənəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌmɛlənʌɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process or state of developing or accumulating melanin pigment in an organism, tissue, or cell, causing darkening

Examples

The melanization process
The melanization of the insect's shell provides protection against certain pathogens.
Researchers observed increased melanization in the skin cells after exposure to sunlight.
In some animals, melanization helps them blend into their environment for camouflage.
The melanization of insect cuticles helps protect them from harmful ultraviolet radiation.
Scientists observed increased melanization in the skin cells after exposure to sunlight.
Abnormal melanization can be a sign of certain genetic conditions in animals.

Related Terms

  • antonym: depigmentation - the loss or reduction of normal pigment
  • synonym: pigmentation - the coloring of tissues by pigment
