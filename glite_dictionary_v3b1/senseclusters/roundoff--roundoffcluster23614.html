roundoff

noun
rounding error
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˈraʊndˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈraʊndˌɒf / (BrE)
the difference between an exact number and its rounded value, often resulting from rounding in calculations

Examples

Roundoff error
The engineer checked the roundoff to ensure the calculations were still reliable.
A small roundoff can sometimes lead to noticeable differences in large financial reports.
When using a calculator, you may encounter a roundoff in the final result.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accuracy - the degree to which a value is exact
  • synonym: approximation - a value or quantity that is nearly but not exactly correct
  • synonym: rounding error - the error introduced when a number is rounded
