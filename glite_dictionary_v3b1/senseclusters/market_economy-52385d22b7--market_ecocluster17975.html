market economy

noun
economy driven by market forces
3.0 6.79/m
Pronunciation: / Ààm…ërk…ôt …™Ààk…ën…ômi / (AmE), / Ààm…ëÀêk…™t …™Ààk…ín…ômi / (BrE)
an economic system in which goods, services, and resources are allocated mainly through supply and demand in free markets, with prices determined by market forces

Examples

A market economy
In a market economy, consumer demand often influences what products are available.
Many countries transitioned to a market economy after the collapse of the Soviet Union.
The principles of a market economy prioritize private enterprise and competitive markets.

Synonyms

  • free enterprise
    3.8 2.80/m
    free enterprise describes private businesses competing largely free from state control, while market economy focuses on allocation by supply and demand.
  • capitalist economy
    4.2 0.60/m
    capitalist economy emphasizes private ownership and profit motive, whereas market economy highlights allocation and prices set by market forces.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: mixed economy - an economy combining private and public enterprise
  • antonym: planned economy - an economy where allocation is determined centrally by government
