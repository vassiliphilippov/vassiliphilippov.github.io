Freudian slip

noun
unconscious revealing error (speech/action)
Pronunciation: / ˈfrɔɪdiən slɪp / (AmE), / ˈfrɔɪdiən slɪp / (BrE)
an unintentional error in speech or action that is thought to reveal subconscious thoughts or feelings, according to Freudian theory

Examples

A Freudian slip in speech
During the interview, he made a Freudian slip and called his boss 'mom.'
Her Freudian slip revealed more than she intended about her feelings.
Reporters laughed when the politician made a Freudian slip about tax increases.

Related Terms

  • synonym: slip of the tongue - an unintentional mistake in speaking
