Synonyms

  • ill
    1.0 30/m
    ill is used for medical conditions and formal writing, while sick is more common in everyday speech and also means nauseous.
  • unwell
    1.0 0.7/m
    unwell means not feeling well or slightly ill, while sick can mean both serious illness and feeling sickly or nauseous.
  • poorly
    2.8 0.00/m
    poorly is common British English for feeling ill, while sick is more general and used in many varieties of English.
  • ailing
    5.6 0.3/m
    ailing describes someone in poor or long-term health, while sick often refers to a current or short-term illness.
