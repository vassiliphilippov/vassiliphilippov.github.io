Ormuzd

proper noun
chief deity of Zoroastrianism
9.9 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / …îrÀàmuÀêzd / (AmE), / …îÀêÀàmuÀêzd / (BrE)
the chief deity of Zoroastrianism, regarded as the source of light and embodiment of good

Examples

Ormuzd the god
In ancient Persia, Ormuzd was worshipped as the supreme god of light and goodness.
The teachings of Zoroaster centered around devotion to Ormuzd and the pursuit of truth.
Zoroastrian texts describe the cosmic battle between Ormuzd and his evil counterpart, Ahriman.

Synonyms

  • Ormazd
    9.9 0.00/m
    Ormazd is an alternative spelling used in some texts, while Ormuzd is the same chief Zoroastrian deity of light and goodness.
  • Ahura Mazda
    9.9 0.00/m
    Ahura Mazda is a formal two-word name that highlights truth and goodness, while Ormuzd is the chief Zoroastrian source of light.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Ahura Mazda - chief god of Zoroastrianism; another name for Ormuzd
