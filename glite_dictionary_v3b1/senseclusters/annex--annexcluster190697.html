annex

verb
seize territory and make it part of a country
5.7 6.3
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈneks/ /; / /ˈæ.neks/ (AmE), /ə.ˈneks/ /; / /ˈæ.neks/ (BrE)
to take control of a territory, especially by force or without permission, and declare it as part of one's own country

Examples

To annex a country
Critics argue that it is illegal to annex territory by military force.
Germany attempted to annex Austria in 1938.
The country decided to annex the disputed regions after years of conflict.
