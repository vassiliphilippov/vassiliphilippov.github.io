moiety

noun
one of two equal (or nearly equal) parts
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔɪ.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˈmɔɪ.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
each of two approximately equal parts into which something can be divided.

Examples

A moiety of the land
He donated a moiety of his estate to charity.
In medieval times, a kingdom might be divided into two moieties for administrative purposes.
Under the agreement, each partner received a moiety of the profits.
