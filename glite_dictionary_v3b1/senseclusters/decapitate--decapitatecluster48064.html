decapitate

verb
cut off the head
Pronunciation: / dɪˈkæpɪteɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈkapɪteɪt /; / dɪˈkæpɪteɪt / (BrE)
to cut off the head of a person, animal, or object

Examples

To decapitate a prisoner
During the Revolution, many prisoners were decapitated by guillotine.
If a snake is decapitated, its body may still move for a short time.
The myth says that Perseus decapitated Medusa with a single stroke.
The executioner was ordered to decapitate the criminal at dawn.
Protesters threatened to decapitate the statue as a sign of their anger.
In some legends, heroes must decapitate monsters to defeat them.
