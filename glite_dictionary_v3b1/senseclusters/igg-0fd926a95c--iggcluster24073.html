IgG

noun
main class of antibody in blood
5.7
Pronunciation: /aɪ dʒiː dʒiː/ (AmE), /aɪ dʒiː dʒiː/ (BrE)
a type of immunoglobulin that is the main antibody defense against bacteria and viruses in the blood; one of five major classes of immunoglobulins

Examples

Low IgG levels
Low IgG concentrations can indicate problems with the immune system.
Scientists studied IgG antibodies to understand how the body fights off bacteria.
The doctor ordered a blood test to check his IgG levels for signs of infection.
