aggressiveness

noun
quality of being hostile or forceful
Pronunciation: / əˈɡrɛsɪvnəs / (AmE), / əˈɡrɛsɪvnɪs /; / əˈɡresɪvnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being hostile, forceful, or ready to confront or attack others

Examples

Player's aggressiveness on defense
Coaches often value aggressiveness in players who need to win possession of the ball.
Her aggressiveness sometimes intimidates her colleagues during meetings.
The dog's aggressiveness made it difficult for the family to keep him as a pet.
Her aggressiveness during negotiations helped secure a better deal.
Some animals show more aggressiveness when they feel threatened.
The player's aggressiveness on the field intimidated his opponents.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hostility - hostile or unfriendly behavior
  • antonym: meekness - gentle, submissive, or non-aggressive quality
  • antonym: passiveness - lack of active response or resistance
