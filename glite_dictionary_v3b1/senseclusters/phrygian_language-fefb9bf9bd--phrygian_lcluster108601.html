Phrygian language

noun
ancient, now-extinct language of Phrygia
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfrɪdʒiən ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈfrɪdʒiən ˈlaŋɡwɪdʒ / (BrE)
an extinct Indo-European language once spoken by the ancient inhabitants of Phrygia, in central Anatolia, known mainly from a few surviving inscriptions

Examples

The Phrygian language inscriptions
Few people today have studied the Phrygian language, as so little material survives.
Researchers have recently deciphered a new Phrygian language inscription found in central Turkey.
The Phrygian language is known for its limited but intriguing collection of stone inscriptions.
