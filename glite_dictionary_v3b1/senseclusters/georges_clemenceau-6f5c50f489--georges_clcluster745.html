Georges Clemenceau

proper noun
French statesman; key figure at Versailles
Pronunciation: / ʒɔːʒ klɛˌmɑːnˈsoʊ / (AmE), / ʒɔːʒ klɛmɒ̃ˈso / (BrE)
(1841–1929) French statesman who served as Prime Minister and was a leading figure in the negotiation of the Treaty of Versailles after World War I.

Examples

Georges Clemenceau Prime Minister
Georges Clemenceau was instrumental in shaping the post-war peace settlement at Versailles.
Historians often debate the motivations behind Georges Clemenceau's stern approach to Germany.
The political leadership of Georges Clemenceau was crucial during France's final year of World War I.
