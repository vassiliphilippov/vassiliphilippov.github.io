femoral nerve

noun
nerve serving front thigh muscles and skin
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛmərəl nɜrv / (AmE), / ˈfɛmərəl nɜːv / (BrE)
a major nerve in the thigh that arises from the lumbar region and supplies the muscles and skin of the front part of the thigh

Examples

The femoral nerve injury
During the surgery, the doctor was careful not to damage the femoral nerve.
Loss of sensation in the thigh can indicate a problem with the femoral nerve.
The femoral nerve is essential for extending the knee and moving the hip.
