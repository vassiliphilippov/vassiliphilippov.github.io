Marco Polo sheep

noun
Central Asian wild sheep with long spiral horns
Pronunciation: / ˌmɑːrkoʊ ˈpoʊloʊ ʃiːp / (AmE), / ˌmɑːkəʊ ˈpəʊləʊ ʃiːp / (BrE)
a large wild sheep native to Central Asia, known for its exceptionally long spiral horns; sometimes considered a subspecies of the argali (Ovis ammon)

Examples

The Marco Polo sheep horns
Hunters consider the Marco Polo sheep one of the most coveted big game trophies due to its distinctive horns.
Researchers are studying the migration patterns of the Marco Polo sheep across the Pamir Mountains.
The Marco Polo sheep is renowned for its impressive, spiraled horns, which can reach up to 1.9 meters in length.
