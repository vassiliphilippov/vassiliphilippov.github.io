sublight

noun
speed below light
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌbˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈsʌbˌlaɪt / (BrE)
a speed or mode of travel that is slower than the speed of light, especially in science fiction contexts

Examples

Sublight speed
The spaceship traveled at sublight speeds to conserve energy during the long journey.
Most interplanetary missions in the story use sublight engines rather than faster-than-light technology.
Because their ship was limited to sublight travel, the crew spent decades in space.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: hyperspace - a fictional faster-than-light method of travel
  • degree higher: lightspeed - the speed at which light travels in a vacuum
