laicization

noun
removal from clergy
9.9
Pronunciation: /leɪ.ɪ.sɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /leɪ.ə.sɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /leɪ.ɪ.sɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /leɪ.ə.sɪ.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of officially removing a member of the clergy from clerical status, returning them to lay status

Examples

Clergy laicization
After years of controversy, the bishop's laicization was confirmed by the Vatican.
The process of laicization can be lengthy and involves several church authorities.
He requested laicization so he could pursue a different career outside the church.

Related Terms

  • synonym: defrocking - the formal removal of a priest's rights and duties
  • antonym: ordination - the process of officially making someone a member of the clergy
