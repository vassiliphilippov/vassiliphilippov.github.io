brake band

noun
device: band used to stop a shaft
Pronunciation: / ˈbreɪk ˌbænd / (AmE), / ˈbreɪk ˌbænd / (BrE)
A strong flexible band that can be tightened around a shaft or drum to slow down or stop its rotation, especially as part of a braking system.

Examples

A brake band tightens
If the brake band is too loose, the machine won't stop properly.
Proper adjustment of the brake band ensures safe operation of the elevator.
The technician replaced the worn brake band in the transmission system.
