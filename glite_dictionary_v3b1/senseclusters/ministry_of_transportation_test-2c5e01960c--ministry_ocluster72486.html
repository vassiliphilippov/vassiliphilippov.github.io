Ministry of Transportation test

noun
annual vehicle safety and emissions test
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪnɪstri əv træns.pɔrˈteɪ.ʃən tɛst / (AmE), / ˈmɪnɪstri əv trænspɔːˈteɪʃ(ə)n tɛst / (BrE)
a compulsory annual inspection of older motor vehicles to ensure they meet safety and exhaust emission standards

Examples

Annual Ministry of Transportation test
Failure to book your annual Ministry of Transportation test could result in a costly fine.
The mechanic said my brakes were too worn to pass the Ministry of Transportation test this time.
Your car will need to pass the Ministry of Transportation test before it can be registered for another year.
