osteosclerosis congenita

noun
congenital bone-hardening disorder
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌɑstioʊskləˈroʊsɪs kənˈdʒɛnɪtə / (AmE), / ˌɒstiəʊskləˈrəʊsɪs kənˈdʒɛnɪtə / (BrE)
An inherited disorder present from birth in which abnormal bone hardening leads to the replacement of cartilage by bone, often resulting in dwarfism.

Examples

Inherited osteosclerosis congenita
Children born with osteosclerosis congenita require careful monitoring of their skeletal development.
Doctors diagnosed his condition as osteosclerosis congenita after observing abnormal bone growth in infancy.
Research into the genetic causes of osteosclerosis congenita has advanced treatment options for affected families.
