Eastern Catholicism

noun
faith and practices of Eastern Catholic Churches
6.7
Pronunciation: / ˈiːstərn kəˈθɑlɪˌsɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈiːstən kəˈθælɪˌsɪzm / (BrE)
The faith, practices, and traditions of the Eastern Catholic Churches, which are in communion with the Roman Catholic Church but follow Eastern Christian rites.

Examples

Eastern Catholicism faith
His family has practiced Eastern Catholicism for generations, following the Byzantine rite.
Many believers appreciate the rich liturgical traditions of Eastern Catholicism.
Unlike in Western Catholicism, icons play a central role in the worship of Eastern Catholicism.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Eastern Orthodoxy - the beliefs and practices of the Eastern Orthodox Church
  • antonym: Roman Catholicism - the faith and doctrine of the Roman Catholic Church
