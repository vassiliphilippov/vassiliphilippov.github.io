cane blight

noun
disease of bush fruit canes
9.6
Pronunciation: / keɪn blaɪt / (AmE), / keɪn blaɪt / (BrE)
a plant disease that affects the stems (canes) of bush fruits such as raspberries or currants, causing dieback and reduced yields

Examples

Cane blight disease
Farmers took extra precautions to prevent cane blight from spreading in their raspberry fields.
Proper pruning and disposal of infected stems can help control cane blight.
The symptoms of cane blight include wilting and dieback of the plant's canes.
