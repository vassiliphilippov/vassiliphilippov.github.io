pressor

noun
agent raising blood pressure
8.3 7.8
Pronunciation: /ˈpres.ər/ (AmE), /ˈpres.ə/ (BrE)
a substance or agent that raises blood pressure by causing the constriction of blood vessels

Examples

Pressor drug
Certain medications act as pressors during episodes of hypotension.
Nicotine is classified as a pressor because it constricts blood vessels.
The doctor administered a pressor to stabilize the patient’s blood pressure.

Related Terms
