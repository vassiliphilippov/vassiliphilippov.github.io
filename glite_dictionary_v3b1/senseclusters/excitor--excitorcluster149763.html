excitor

noun
stimulating device or agent
Pronunciation: / ɪkˈsaɪtər / (AmE), / ɪkˈsaɪtə / (BrE)
a device or agent that causes excitation or stimulates activity, especially in electrical or biological systems

Examples

Electrical excitor device
The excitor in the machine triggers the initial electrical current.
Scientists used a chemical excitor to stimulate the nerve cells in the experiment.
A malfunctioning excitor can cause the entire system to fail.

Related Terms

  • antonym: inhibitor - a device or agent that slows down or prevents activity
  • synonym: stimulator - a device or agent that increases activity or response
