hemal arch

noun
vertebral structure enclosing tail blood vessels
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈhiːməl ɑːrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈhiːməl ɑːtʃ / (BrE)
a bony or cartilaginous structure projecting from the underside of a vertebral centrum, enclosing the caudal blood vessels in the tail region, especially in the tails of vertebrates.

Examples

The hemal arch bone
Damage to the hemal arch can result in impaired blood flow in the caudal region.
Researchers examined the shape of each hemal arch to study its evolutionary significance.
The hemal arch protects the major blood vessels running beneath the tail vertebrae of many fish and reptiles.
Damage to the haemal arch can compromise blood flow in the lower body of some animals.
In fish, the haemal arch protects the major blood vessels running through the tail.
Researchers carefully examined the haemal arch in several reptile skeletons to study their evolution.
