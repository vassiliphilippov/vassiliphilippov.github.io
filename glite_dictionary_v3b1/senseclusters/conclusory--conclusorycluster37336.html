conclusory

adjective
unsupported conclusion
8.7
Pronunciation: / kənˈkluːsəri / (AmE), / kənˈkluːsəri / (BrE)
stating a conclusion without providing supporting evidence or reasoning

Examples

A conclusory statement
The lawyer's conclusory statements failed to convince the court.
A conclusory claim without facts is not enough to win a case.
Her argument was dismissed as conclusory because it lacked supporting evidence.

Related Terms

  • synonym: assertive - stating something confidently, often without proof
  • antonym: substantiated - supported with evidence or proof
