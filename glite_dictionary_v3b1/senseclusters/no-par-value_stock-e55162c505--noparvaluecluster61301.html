no-par-value stock

noun
stock with no specified par value
9.8 6.3
Pronunciation: /noʊ pɑːr ˈvæl.juː stɑːk/ (AmE), /nəʊ pɑː ˈvæl.juː stɒk/ (BrE)
A share issued without a par value stated in the corporate charter or on the stock certificate.

Examples

Issue no-par-value stock
Many modern companies issue no-par-value stock to provide greater flexibility in their capital structure.
Purchasing no-par-value stock does not guarantee investors any minimum stated value per share.
The corporation's charter allowed it to offer no-par-value stock rather than specifying a nominal amount.
