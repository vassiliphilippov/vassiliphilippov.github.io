narwhal

noun
Arctic whale with a long spiral tusk
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈnɑrˌwɑl / (AmE), / ˈnɑː.wəl / (BrE)
A small Arctic whale, the male of which is distinguished by a long, spiral ivory tusk extending from its head.

Examples

A narwhal tusk
A male narwhal can have a tusk that grows over two meters long.
Scientists are studying the migration patterns of the narwhal in the Arctic Ocean.
The narwhal is often called the 'unicorn of the sea' because of its distinctive tusk.

Related Terms

  • synonym: beluga - a white Arctic whale without a tusk
