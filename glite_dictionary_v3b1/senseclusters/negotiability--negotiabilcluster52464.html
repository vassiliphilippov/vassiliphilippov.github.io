negotiability

noun
openness to compromise
6.3
Pronunciation: / nɪˌgoʊʃiəˈbɪlɪti / (AmE), / nɪˌgəʊʃɪəˈbɪlɪti / (BrE)
the quality of being open to discussion, compromise, or change during negotiation

Examples

Negotiability of terms
The negotiability of the contract terms allowed both parties to reach an agreement quickly.
Her high negotiability made her a valuable member of the bargaining team.
Some issues have little negotiability and must be accepted as they are.
The negotiability of the agreement allowed both sides to reach a compromise.
Some contracts have little negotiability, making it hard to change the terms.
The negotiability of the price was a key factor in the buyer's decision.

  • antonym: rigidity - unwillingness to change or compromise
