overthrust fault

noun
geological fault formed by horizontal compression
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˌθrʌst ˈfɔlt / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˌθrʌst ˈfɔːlt / (BrE)
a geological fault caused by horizontal compression, where the upper side appears to have been pushed upward and over the lower side

Examples

An overthrust fault layer
Evidence of an overthrust fault can be seen in the exposed rock layers.
Formation of an overthrust fault often results from intense tectonic forces.
The geologists identified an overthrust fault running through the mountain range.
