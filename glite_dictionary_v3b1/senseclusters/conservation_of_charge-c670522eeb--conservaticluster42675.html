conservation of charge

noun
the principle that electric charge is constant in an isolated system
4.0 4.2
Pronunciation: /kɑːn.sər.ˈveɪ.ʃən əv tʃɑːrdʒ/ /; / /kən.sər.ˈveɪ.ʃən əv tʃɑːrdʒ/ (AmE), /kɒn.sə.ˈveɪ.ʃən əv tʃɑːdʒ/ /; / /kən.sə.ˈveɪ.ʃən əv tʃɑːdʒ/ (BrE)
the principle that the total electric charge in an isolated system remains constant, regardless of any internal changes within the system

Examples

Law of conservation of charge
Even during complex chemical reactions, the law of conservation of charge holds true and charge is neither created nor destroyed.
The conservation of charge ensures that all electrical processes maintain a constant net charge.
Understanding conservation of charge is fundamental in explaining how batteries and circuits work.
