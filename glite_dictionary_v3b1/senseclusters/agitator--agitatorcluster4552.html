agitator

noun
person who incites public unrest
4.5 6.5
Pronunciation: / ˈædʒɪˌteɪtər / (AmE), / ˈædʒɪˌteɪtə / (BrE)
a person who stirs up public feeling or causes trouble, especially for political or social change

Examples

A agitator for change
Her speech inspired both admiration and suspicion, as some viewed her as a social agitator.
Several agitators were arrested during the political demonstration.
The union accused him of being an agitator trying to stir up dissent among the workers.
