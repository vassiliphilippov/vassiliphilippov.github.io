Dipodidae

noun
a family of jumping rodents (Old World jerboas)
Pronunciation: / daɪˈpɑdəˌdi / (AmE), / dɪˈpɒdɪdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, jumping rodents commonly known as Old World jerboas, native to parts of Eurasia and Africa.

Examples

The family Dipodidae
Researchers studying desert habitats often encounter various species belonging to the family Dipodidae.
The Dipodidae are known for their long hind legs, which help them leap great distances.
Unlike other rodents, members of Dipodidae have highly specialized adaptations for jumping in arid environments.
