fever blister

noun
blister caused by HSV-1 infection
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈfiːvər ˌblɪstər / (AmE), / ˈfiːvə ˌblɪstə / (BrE)
A small, painful blister, usually on the lips or face, caused by an infection with the herpes simplex virus type 1 (HSV-1).

Examples

A fever blister on lips
If you frequently get fever blisters, you should avoid sharing drinks with others.
She noticed a painful fever blister on her lip just before the big presentation.
The doctor explained that a fever blister is caused by the herpes simplex virus.

Related Terms
