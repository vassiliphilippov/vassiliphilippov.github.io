periodic sentence

noun
sentence with main clause at the end
Pronunciation: / ˌpɪriˈɑdɪk ˈsɛntəns / (AmE), / ˌpɪəriˈɒdɪk ˈsɛntəns / (BrE)
a complex sentence in which the main clause is placed at the end, following one or more subordinate clauses or phrases

Examples

A periodic sentence structure
Although he was tired and the hour was late, the speech continued.
Only after the storm had passed, and the sun had risen, did she feel safe, creating a classic periodic sentence.
To win the championship, despite the injuries and setbacks, was his greatest achievement.
