decommit

verb
to withdraw promise
Pronunciation: / ˌdiːkəˈmɪt / (AmE), / ˌdiːkəˈmɪt / (BrE)
to withdraw a previous commitment or promise, especially in a formal or official context

Examples

To decommit from a team
After reconsidering his options, the student chose to decommit from the college.
The company decided to decommit from the project due to budget constraints.
She announced that she would decommit from the event and apologized for any inconvenience.

Related Terms

  • antonym: commit - to make a firm promise or decision
  • synonym: withdraw - to take back or remove a promise or offer
