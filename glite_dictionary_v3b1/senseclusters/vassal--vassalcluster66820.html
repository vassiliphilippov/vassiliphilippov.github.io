A person in the Middle Ages who held land from a feudal lord and owed them allegiance and service in return.

Examples

A vassal lord

Synonyms

  • feudatory
    9.9 0.00/m
    feudatory is a formal, legal word for a person holding a fief, while vassal is more common in general histories.
  • liege
    9.9 0.01/m
    liege is used for someone under feudal service in some texts, while vassal is the standard medieval word for a landholder.
  • liege subject
    9.9 0.00/m
    liege subject is a landholder who swears loyalty to a feudal lord, while vassal is the common medieval term for that role.
  • liegeman
    9.9 0.00/m
    liegeman is an older, often gendered word for a loyal follower in medieval times, while vassal is the neutral common term.
