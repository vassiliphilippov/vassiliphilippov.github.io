coercion

noun
the use of force or threats to compel
6.9 5.94/m
Pronunciation: / ko äÀà…úr í…ôn / (AmE), / k…ô äÀà…úÀê É…ôn / (BrE)
the act of making someone do something by using force or threats

Examples

Use of coercion
Employees reported incidents of coercion by management to join the new union.
The confession was obtained through coercion, so it was thrown out in court.
The contract was signed under coercion, making it legally invalid.
He claimed his agreement was given out of fear and coercion, not free will.
The suspect confessed to the crime under coercion from the authorities.
There are strict laws against the coercion of witnesses in court.

Synonyms

  • duress
    7.9 1.44/m
    duress is threats or violence used to force someone, while coercion is the broader act of making someone obey by force.
  • compulsion
    6.5 1.17/m
    compulsion is a state of being forced or an inner urge, while coercion is specifically making someone act through force or threats.
  • pressure
    4.2 7.73/m
    pressure is trying to persuade or push someone to act, while coercion is using force or threats to make someone comply.

Related Terms

  • synonym: compulsion - the action of forcing or being forced to do something
  • synonym: duress - threats or pressure used to force someone to act against their will
  • antonym: persuasion - the act of convincing someone to do or believe something
