bitter orange tree

noun
tree with sour or bitter orange fruit
6.6
Pronunciation: / ˈbɪtɚ ˈɔːrɪndʒ ˌtri / (AmE), / ˈbɪtə ˈɒrɪndʒ ˌtriː / (BrE)
a type of orange tree that produces sour or bitter fruit, often used as a grafting stock

Examples

A bitter orange tree plant
Gardeners sometimes plant a bitter orange tree for its aromatic blossoms and resilient nature.
Marmalade is traditionally made from the fruit of the bitter orange tree.
The bitter orange tree is commonly used as a rootstock for grafting sweet oranges.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Seville orange - orange variety with sour, bitter fruit, commonly used for marmalade
  • antonym: sweet orange - orange tree yielding sweet fruit, commonly eaten fresh
