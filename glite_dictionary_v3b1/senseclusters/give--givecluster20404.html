give

verb
perform or present for an audience
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɡɪv / (AmE), / ɡɪv / (BrE)
to present or perform (a speech, event, performance, gesture, or signal) for others

Examples

To give a speech
She was thrilled to give her first public reading of the poem.
The community theater plans to give a charity concert next month.
The orchestra will give three performances of the new symphony this week.
He gave her a stern look when she interrupted the meeting.
She gave me a reassuring smile before the interview began.
The teacher gave a quick nod to signal that it was time to start.
She decided to give a farewell party for her colleagues.
The university will give a lecture on climate change next week.
They're planning to give a workshop on digital marketing this fall.

Related Terms

  • synonym: deliver - to formally present (a speech, lecture, etc.) to an audience
  • synonym: hold - to organize or conduct an event
  • synonym: host - to act as the organizer of an event
  • synonym: offer - to present something for acceptance; sometimes used for looks or gestures
  • synonym: present - to formally show or perform something to an audience
  • synonym: propose - to suggest or put forward formally, especially a toast or plan
  • synonym: send - to cause to go or be taken to a particular destination (figurative sense: send a glance)
  • synonym: show - to display something so that it can be seen by others
