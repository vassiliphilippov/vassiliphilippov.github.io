Western mountain ash

noun
North American ash tree species
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛstərn ˈmaʊntn ˌæʃ / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ˈmaʊntɪn ˌaʃ / (BrE)
a species of ash tree native to the western coast of North America, known for its clusters of red berries and pinnate leaves

Examples

The Western mountain ash tree
Gardeners appreciate the Western mountain ash for its ornamental value and resilience.
Hikers in the Pacific Northwest can often spot the Western mountain ash along forest trails.
The Western mountain ash produces vibrant clusters of orange berries in the autumn.
