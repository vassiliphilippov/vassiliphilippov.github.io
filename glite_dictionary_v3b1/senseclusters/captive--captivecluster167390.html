captive

noun
person dominated by emotion or passion
Pronunciation: / ˈkæp.tɪv / (AmE), / ˈkæp.tɪv / (BrE)
A person who is strongly influenced or controlled by a powerful emotion or passion.

Examples

Captive of love
He became a captive of his own ambition, unable to let go of his dreams.
She was a willing captive to the thrill of adventure.
Too often, people are captives of their fears and cannot move forward.

Related Terms

  • antonym: free - not controlled or constrained by emotion or passion
  • synonym: slave - a person who is excessively influenced or controlled by something
