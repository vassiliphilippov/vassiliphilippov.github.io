vernacularization

noun
adoption of local language
9.4
Pronunciation: /vər.næk.jə.lə.raɪˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /və.næk.jʊ.lə.raɪˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process of making language, ideas, or practices more local and accessible by using the everyday language of the people instead of a formal or foreign one

Examples

The vernacularization of language
The vernacularization of religious texts made them accessible to a wider audience.
Linguists have documented the vernacularization of classical languages in many regions.
The vernacularization of education helped more people understand and participate in learning.
The vernacularization of literature helps readers connect with stories in their own language and culture.
Through vernacularization, international films become more appealing to local audiences.
The vernacularization of religious texts made them accessible to a wider population.
The vernacularization of official documents made them more accessible to the general public.
Many countries have promoted the vernacularization of education to improve literacy rates.
The vernacularization of religious texts allowed more people to understand their faith.

Related Terms

  • synonym: localization - the process of making something suitable for a particular place or people
  • antonym: standardization - the process of making something conform to a standard
  • synonym: vernacular - the language or dialect spoken by ordinary people in a particular region
