going under

noun
the sinking of a ship
Pronunciation: / ˈɡoʊɪŋ ˈʌndɚ / (AmE), / ˈɡəʊɪŋ ˈʌndə / (BrE)
the act of a ship or boat sinking below the surface of water

Examples

The going under of a ship
A sudden storm led to the vessel's going under within minutes.
After the collision, the crew did everything they could to prevent going under.
The captain gave the order to abandon ship as they realized going under was inevitable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sinking - the process of a ship or object becoming submerged in water and descending below the surface
