degeneracy

noun
multiple equivalent states
8.7
Pronunciation: / dɪˈdʒɛnərəsi / (AmE), / dɪˈdʒɛnərəsi / (BrE)
a situation in which two or more different states or outcomes share the same value or condition, especially in science or mathematics

In quantum physics, degeneracy occurs when different energy states have the same energy level.
