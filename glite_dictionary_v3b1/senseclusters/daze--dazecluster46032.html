daze

verb
make unable to see or think clearly
2.8
Pronunciation: / deɪz / (AmE), / deɪz / (BrE)
to make someone confused or unable to think clearly, usually because of shock, surprise, or a strong effect

Examples

To daze someone
She was dazed by the midday sun after stepping outside from the dark theater.
The powerful spotlight dazed the performer for a moment before she recovered.
The sudden camera flash dazed the driver, making it hard for him to see the road.
His surprise confession seemed to daze his friends into silence.
She was completely dazed by the unexpected turn of events.
The sudden announcement dazed everyone in the room.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amaze - to fill someone with great surprise or wonder
  • synonym: bewilder - to confuse or puzzle someone completely
  • synonym: dazzle - to blind temporarily with a bright light
  • synonym: stun - to shock or overwhelm someone so that they cannot react immediately
