power through

verb
persevere through difficulties
Pronunciation: / ˈpaʊɚ ˌθru /; / ˈpaʊ.ɚ θru / (AmE), / ˈpaʊə ˌθruː /; / ˈpaʊ.ə θruː / (BrE)
to continue or finish something with determination, even when it is difficult or you are tired

Examples

To power through work
Despite feeling exhausted, she managed to power through the final hours of her shift.
He had to power through his last semester of college even though he was unwell.
We will need to power through these challenges if we want to succeed.
Despite the exhaustion, she powered through the last hour of her shift.
He powered through his cold and still made it to the meeting.
We powered through the dense paperwork to finish the project on time.

Related Terms

  • antonym: give up - to stop trying or admit defeat
  • synonym: persevere - to persist in doing something despite difficulty or delay
