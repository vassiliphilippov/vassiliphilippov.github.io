diathermy

noun
medical treatment using electric heat
Pronunciation: / ˈdaɪ.əˌθɜr.mi / (AmE), / ˈdaɪ.əˌθɜː.mi / (BrE)
a medical treatment that uses high-frequency electric currents to generate heat within body tissues, often for physical therapy or surgical purposes

Examples

Diathermy treatment
Advances in diathermy have made certain types of surgery less invasive.
Physiotherapists sometimes recommend diathermy as part of a rehabilitation program.
The patient received diathermy for pain relief in her shoulder joint.
