Indica

proper noun
ancient accounts of India
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈdiːkə / (AmE), / ɪnˈdiːkə / (BrE)
the title of various ancient or medieval works describing the geography, peoples, or history of India, especially by Greek or Roman authors

Examples

Ancient Indica book
The ancient Greek writer Megasthenes authored an Indica that detailed his observations of India.
Historians often reference the Indica to understand how foreigners viewed India in antiquity.
Several medieval texts titled Indica provide valuable insights into early Indian society.
