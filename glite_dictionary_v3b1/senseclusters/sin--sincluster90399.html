sin

verb
commit a moral fault or serious mistake
3.7
Pronunciation: / sɪn / (AmE), / sɪn / (BrE)
to do something that is considered wrong or against divine or moral law

Examples

To sin against rules
He knew he would sin if he lied on his tax return.
Many religions teach that everyone sins at some point in their lives.
She felt she had sinned by betraying her friend's trust.
After sinning, he felt a deep sense of guilt and sought redemption.
He confessed that he had sinned and asked for forgiveness.
She believes that everyone sins at some point in their life.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blunder - to make a careless or serious mistake
  • synonym: err - to make a mistake
  • antonym: repent - to feel or express sincere regret
  • synonym: transgress - to go beyond the limits of a moral principle or other established standard of behavior
