supervision

noun
managing by overseeing people or work
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpɚˈvɪʒən / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˈvɪʒən / (BrE)
the act of managing or directing people, activities, or work by closely watching and guiding them

Examples

Constant supervision
Constant supervision is necessary when children are playing near water.
He was promoted to a position that requires the supervision of a large team.
The new employees will be working under close supervision during their training period.

Related Terms

  • antonym: neglect - the failure to give proper care or attention
  • synonym: oversight - the act of overseeing something
