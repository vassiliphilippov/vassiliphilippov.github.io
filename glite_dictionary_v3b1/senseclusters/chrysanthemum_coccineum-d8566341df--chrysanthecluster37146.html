Chrysanthemum coccineum

A perennial garden plant native to Asia with finely divided aromatic leaves and white to pink-purple flowers, sometimes used as a source of natural insecticide.

Examples

Chrysanthemum coccineum plant
Pyrethrin is a well-known insecticide that is extracted from Chrysanthemum coccineum.
Some gardeners plant Chrysanthemum coccineum to naturally deter insect pests in their vegetable patches.
The Chrysanthemum coccineum adds vibrant color to spring gardens with its pink and purple blooms.
