Medicaid

noun
US health care program for low-income people
Pronunciation: / ˈmɛdɪˌkeɪd / (AmE), / ˈmɛdɪˌkeɪd / (BrE)
A government program in the United States that provides health care for people with low incomes and limited resources, jointly funded by federal and state governments.

Examples

Medicaid program
After losing his job, Tom applied for Medicaid to help cover his family's medical expenses.
Many low-income children and adults rely on Medicaid for essential health services.
The hospital accepts both Medicaid and private insurance from its patients.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Medicare - US government health program for people over 65 or with certain disabilities
