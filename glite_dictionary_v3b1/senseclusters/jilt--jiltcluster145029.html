jilt

verb
suddenly reject a lover
7.4
Pronunciation: / dʒɪlt / (AmE), / dʒɪlt / (BrE)
to suddenly reject or abandon (a lover or partner), especially in a careless or unfeeling way

Examples

To jilt a lover
After three years together, she jilted him without warning.
He feared being jilted again, so he hesitated to enter a new relationship.
The actor felt humiliated when his girlfriend jilted him just before their wedding.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dump - to suddenly end a romantic relationship with someone
  • synonym: spurn - to reject with disdain or contempt
