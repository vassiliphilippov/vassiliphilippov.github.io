prison farm

noun
correctional facility where prisoners work, often on farms
5.9 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈprɪz.ən fɑːrm/ (AmE), /ˈprɪz.ən fɑːm/ (BrE)
a correctional facility where trusted prisoners are employed to work, often in agricultural or government projects, usually located in a rural area

Examples

The prison farm
Many inmates at the prison farm learned valuable skills while tending crops and livestock.
She visited a prison farm to study alternative correctional methods in rural communities.
The old prison farm was converted into a rehabilitation center for former inmates.
