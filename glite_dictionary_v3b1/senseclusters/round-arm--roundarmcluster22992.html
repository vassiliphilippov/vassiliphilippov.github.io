round-arm

adjective, adverb
with a horizontal or outward arm swing
Pronunciation: / ˈraʊnd ɑrm / (AmE), / ˈraʊnd ɑːm / (BrE)
using or describing a throwing or hitting action where the arm swings outward at shoulder height, not over the top or underarm (especially in sports like cricket or baseball)

Examples

Round-arm bowling style
He prefers to hit the baseball round-arm rather than using the classic overarm style.
She threw the stone round-arm across the river, impressing her friends.
The bowler delivered the ball round-arm to surprise the batsman.
He prefers round-arm bowling techniques over the more conventional styles.
In early cricket, round-arm deliveries changed the way the game was played.
The bowler used a round-arm action to surprise the batsman.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overarm - with the arm swung vertically above the shoulder
  • antonym: underarm - with the arm swung below shoulder height
