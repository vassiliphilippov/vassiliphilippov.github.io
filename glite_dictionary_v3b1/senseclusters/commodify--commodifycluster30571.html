commodify

verb
to make into a commodity
Pronunciation: / kəˈmɑːdəˌfaɪ / (AmE), / kəˈmɒdɪfaɪ / (BrE)
to turn something into a product or service that can be bought or sold, often removing its special qualities or meaning

Examples

To commodify personal data
Some argue that social media platforms commodify personal data for advertising purposes.
There is concern that art galleries may commodify cultural artifacts by focusing only on their market value.
When traditions are commodified, they can lose their original meaning and significance.
Many critics argue that social media platforms commodify personal data for advertising purposes.
There is a debate about whether education should be commodified or remain a public good.
Some artists resist efforts to commodify their work, preferring to keep it outside the marketplace.
Some worry that modern society will commodify every aspect of life.
The company tried to commodify personal data by selling it to advertisers.
Art critics argue that mass production can commodify creativity and reduce its value.

Related Terms

  • synonym: commercialize - to make something available for sale or profit
  • antonym: decommodify - to remove from the status of being a commodity
  • synonym: devalue - to reduce or remove the value or importance of something
  • synonym: monetize - to earn money from something
