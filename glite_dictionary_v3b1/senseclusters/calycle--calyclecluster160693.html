calycle

noun
small cup-shaped biological structure
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˈkælɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈkælɪkəl / (BrE)
a small cup-shaped structure found in plants and animals, such as the cavity in a coral containing a polyp or a formation resembling a cup in tissues like taste buds or the optic cup

Examples

Coral calycle
Each polyp lives in a separate calycle within the colony, providing it with stability.
Researchers examined the calycles located at the base of each taste bud under the microscope.
The coral's calycle protects the soft polyp inside its hard structure.
