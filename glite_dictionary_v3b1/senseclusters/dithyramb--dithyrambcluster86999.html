dithyramb

noun
wildly enthusiastic speech or writing
9.9 9.6
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈθɪr.æm/ /; / /dɪ.ˈθɪr.əm/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈθɪr.æm/ /; / /dɪ.ˈθɪr.əm/ (BrE)
a wildly enthusiastic or impassioned speech or piece of writing, often in praise of something or someone

Examples

A dithyramb of praise
His acceptance speech was less a statement of gratitude and more a dithyramb of self-praise.
She wrote a dithyramb about her favorite musician, describing them in exuberant terms.
The critic's review turned into a lengthy dithyramb celebrating the author's genius.

Related Terms

  • synonym: panegyric - a formal public speech or written verse, delivered in high praise of a person or thing
  • antonym: tirade - a long, angry speech of criticism or accusation
