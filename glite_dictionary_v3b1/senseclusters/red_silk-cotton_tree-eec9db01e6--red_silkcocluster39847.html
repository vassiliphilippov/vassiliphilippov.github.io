red silk-cotton tree

noun
East Indian tree yielding inferior silk-like fibers
6.0 8.0
Pronunciation: /red ˈsɪlk.kɑː.tən triː/ /; / /red ˈsɪlk.kɔː.tən triː/ (AmE), /red ˈsɪlk.kɒt.ən triː/ /; / /red ˈsɪlk.kɒt.ən tʃriː/ (BrE)
An East Indian tree (Bombax ceiba) that yields silk-like fibers which are inferior to kapok.

Examples

The red silk-cotton tree fibers
Farmers in the region harvest the fibers of the red silk-cotton tree for use in stuffing pillows.
The red silk-cotton tree is known for its large, showy flowers and its cottony seed fibers.
Unlike kapok, the fibers from the red silk-cotton tree are considered of lesser quality for insulation.
