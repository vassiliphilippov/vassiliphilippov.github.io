megalithic structure

noun
prehistoric monument of large stones
9.3
Pronunciation: / ˌmɛɡəˈlɪθɪk ˈstrʌktʃɚ / (AmE), / ˌmɛɡəˈlɪθɪk ˈstrʌktʃə / (BrE)
a prehistoric monument consisting of very large stones, especially as seen in ancient sites in western Europe

Examples

A megalithic structure site
A single upright stone, or menhir, is a type of megalithic structure found in Brittany, France.
Archaeologists have studied many megalithic structures across Europe to learn about prehistoric cultures.
Stonehenge is perhaps the most famous megalithic structure in the world.

Related Terms

  • synonym: megalith - a large stone used to construct a structure or monument
