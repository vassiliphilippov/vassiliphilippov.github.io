unenroll

verb
to withdraw enrollment
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnɪnˈroʊl / (AmE), / ˌʌnɪnˈrəʊl / (BrE)
to remove someone from official enrollment, or to withdraw oneself from enrollment in a program or institution

Examples

To unenroll from school
After missing too many classes, the administration unenrolled him from the program last month.
She decided to unenroll from the course before the deadline to avoid a penalty.
If you unenroll now, you may not be able to rejoin until next year.

Related Terms

  • antonym: enroll - to officially join or register in a program or institution
  • synonym: withdraw - to remove oneself from participation or membership
