soever

adverb
emphatic intensifier
Pronunciation: / soʊˈɛvər / (AmE), / səʊˈɛvə / (BrE)
used after wh-words to emphasize any or all possibilities, meaning 'at all' or 'of any kind', now archaic

Examples

No reason soever
He had no reason soever to doubt her honesty.
You may choose whichever path soever you prefer, for all lead to the same place.
No evidence soever was found to support the claim.

Related Terms

  • synonym: at all - in any way or to any extent
  • synonym: whatever - anything or everything that; no matter what
