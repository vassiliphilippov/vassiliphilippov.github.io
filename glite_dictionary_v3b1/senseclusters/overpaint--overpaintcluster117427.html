overpaint

verb
to paint over artwork
6.2
Pronunciation: /ˈoʊ.vər.peɪnt/ (AmE), /ˈəʊ.və.peɪnt/ (BrE)
to apply paint over an existing layer, such as on a finished artwork or surface, often to cover, change, or enhance it

Examples

To overpaint a mural
The conservator decided not to overpaint the damaged section to preserve the original artist's intent.
Experts discovered that someone had overpainted the landscape, hiding important historical details.
Before restoring the mural, they checked if it had been overpainted in previous centuries.
The artist chose to overpaint the old mural with brighter colors for a fresh look.
Sometimes restorers overpaint damaged areas to preserve the original artwork.
He decided to overpaint the canvas to hide his earlier mistakes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: repaint - to paint something again
  • synonym: restore - to repair or renovate a work of art
  • synonym: retouch - to improve or repair a painting by making small changes
