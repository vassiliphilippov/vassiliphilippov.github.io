standard gauge

noun
railroad track width of 56.5 inches
5.9 3.5
Pronunciation: / ˈstændɚd ɡeɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˈstændəd ɡeɪdʒ / (BrE)
the distance between the inner sides of the two rails of a railroad track, measuring 56.5 inches (1,435 mm) and used as the standard in most of the world

Examples

The standard gauge track
A standard gauge allows trains to move between different regions without changing wheels.
Many European countries adopted the standard gauge for their main railway systems.
The new railway line was built using a standard gauge to ensure compatibility with existing networks.
