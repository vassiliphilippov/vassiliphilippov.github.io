nondifferentiation

noun
lack of differentiability
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑndɪˌfɛrənʃiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnɒndɪˌfɛrənʃiˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the property of a mathematical function not having a derivative at a point or over an interval

Examples

Point of nondifferentiation
The sharp corner in the graph caused nondifferentiation at that specific point.
Mathematicians study cases of nondifferentiation to understand unusual function behavior.
Because of nondifferentiation, the function could not be analyzed using standard calculus techniques.

Related Terms

  • synonym: continuity - the property of a function being continuous at a point or interval
  • antonym: differentiability - the property of a function having a derivative
