strait-laced

adjective
excessively proper or morally strict
9.9 6.2
Pronunciation: /ˈstreɪt.leɪst/ (AmE), /ˈstreɪt.leɪst/ (BrE)
having very strict or severe moral attitudes and opinions; excessively proper or prudish.

Examples

A strait-laced person
Her strait-laced parents criticized every aspect of her modern wardrobe.
The school upheld strait-laced rules about dress and behavior.
Though he appears strait-laced at work, he can be quite relaxed at parties.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conservative - holding to traditional attitudes and values; opposing change or innovation
  • antonym: permissive - allowing or characterized by great or excessive freedom of behavior
