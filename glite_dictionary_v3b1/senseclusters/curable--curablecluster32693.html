curable

adjective
able to be hardened or set (of substances)
5.1
Pronunciation: / ˈkjʊrəb(ə)l / (AmE), / ˈkjʊərəb(ə)l / (BrE)
(of a substance) capable of being hardened or set by some additive or agent, especially in reference to resins or polymers.

Examples

Curable plastic
Certain plastics are curable by exposure to ultraviolet light.
The adhesive is curable at room temperature with the addition of a catalyst.
The manufacturer provides both curable and non-curable versions of the sealant.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cure - to cause (a substance) to harden or set by chemical reaction
  • antonym: uncurable - not capable of being hardened or set
