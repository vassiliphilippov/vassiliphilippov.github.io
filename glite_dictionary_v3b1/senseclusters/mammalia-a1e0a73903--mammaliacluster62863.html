Mammalia

noun
class of warm-blooded vertebrate animals (mammals)
4.6
Pronunciation: / məˈmeɪliə / (AmE), / məˈmeɪlɪə / (BrE)
the class of warm-blooded animals (including humans, dogs, whales, etc.) that have hair or fur and whose females produce milk for their young

Scientists classify humans, whales, and dogs as Mammalia due to shared characteristics.
