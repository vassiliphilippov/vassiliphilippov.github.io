stone-gray

adjective
of a color like stone or slate
Pronunciation: / stoʊn ɡreɪ /; / stoʊn ˈɡreɪ / (AmE), / stəʊn ɡreɪ /; / stəʊn ˈɡreɪ / (BrE)
Of a color resembling that of slate, granite, or natural stone; a dull gray tinged with blue or brown.

Examples

Stone-gray color
He wore a stone-gray suit that gave him a calm and understated appearance.
Her eyes were a striking shade of stone-gray that seemed almost metallic in the sunlight.
The old castle's walls were painted a somber stone-gray, blending with the rocky landscape.
He wore a stone-grey suit that gave him a sophisticated appearance.
Her coat was a lovely shade of stone-grey that matched the wintry sky.
The artist chose stone-grey paint for the walls to create a calm atmosphere.
