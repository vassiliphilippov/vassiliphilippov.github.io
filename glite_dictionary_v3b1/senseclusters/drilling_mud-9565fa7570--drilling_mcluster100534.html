drilling mud

noun
mixture used in drilling to lubricate and support boreholes
Pronunciation: / ˈdrɪlɪŋ mʌd / (AmE), / ˈdrɪlɪŋ mʌd / (BrE)
a liquid mixture of water, clays, and chemicals used in drilling to lubricate and cool the drill bit, remove cuttings, and support the sides of the borehole

Examples

Barrels of drilling mud
Before starting the new well, the engineers prepared several barrels of drilling mud to ensure the process ran smoothly.
The properties of drilling mud must be carefully controlled to prevent blowouts and stabilize the wellbore.
Without the proper drilling mud, the drill bit could overheat and the hole might collapse.

Related Terms

  • synonym: drilling fluid - general term for fluids used in the process of drilling boreholes
