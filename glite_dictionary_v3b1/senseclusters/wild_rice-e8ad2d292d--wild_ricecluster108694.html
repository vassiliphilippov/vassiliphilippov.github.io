wild rice

noun
grain from North American aquatic grasses
5.5
Pronunciation: / waɪld raɪs / (AmE), / waɪld raɪs / (BrE)
a type of long, dark grain from certain North American aquatic grasses, used as food, or the plant that produces it

Examples

Many people enjoy wild rice as a healthy alternative to white rice.
The chef prepared a delicious salad with roasted vegetables and wild rice.
Wild rice grows naturally in shallow waters across parts of the northern United States and Canada.
Native Americans have harvested wild rice from lakes and rivers for centuries.
The chef prepared a delicious salad with organic wild rice and seasonal vegetables.
