potato fern

noun
Australasian fern with edible rhizome
7.0
Pronunciation: / pəˈteɪɾoʊ fɝn / (AmE), / pəˈteɪtəʊ fɜːn / (BrE)
a large Australasian evergreen fern with an edible rhizome, sometimes used as a vegetable by indigenous people

Examples

Edible potato fern
Botanists are studying how the potato fern adapts to different forest environments.
In some regions, the rhizomes of the potato fern are cooked as a traditional food.
The potato fern is valued for its edible roots by Aboriginal Australians.
