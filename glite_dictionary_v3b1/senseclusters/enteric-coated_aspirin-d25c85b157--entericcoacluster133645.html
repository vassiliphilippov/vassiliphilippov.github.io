enteric-coated aspirin

noun
aspirin coated to dissolve in the intestine
9.6
Pronunciation: / ɛnˈtɛrɪk ˈkoʊtɪd ˈæsprɪn / (AmE), / ɛnˈtɛrɪk ˈkəʊtɪd ˈæsprɪn / (BrE)
Aspirin that is coated to resist dissolving in the stomach and instead dissolves in the intestine to reduce stomach irritation.

Examples

Enteric-coated aspirin tablet
Many patients take enteric-coated aspirin to help prevent stomach upset.
Some doctors prescribe enteric-coated aspirin to people at risk of developing ulcers.
The pharmacist recommended switching to enteric-coated aspirin for better gastrointestinal tolerance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aspirin - a medicine used to reduce pain, fever, or inflammation
