spur-of-the-moment

adjective
done quickly without planning
8.8
Pronunciation: / spɝː əv ðə moʊmənt / (AmE), / spɜː əv ðə məʊmənt / (BrE)
done suddenly and without planning or preparation, often in response to an unexpected situation

Examples

A spur-of-the-moment decision
It was just a spur-of-the-moment purchase that I didn't plan.
Their spur-of-the-moment decision led to a fantastic evening.
We took a spur-of-the-moment trip to the beach last weekend.

Related Terms

  • synonym: impulsive - tending to act on sudden urges rather than careful thought
  • antonym: premeditated - planned or thought out in advance
