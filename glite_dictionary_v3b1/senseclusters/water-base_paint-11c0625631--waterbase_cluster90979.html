water-base paint

noun
paint with water as solvent
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔt̬ɚ beɪs peɪnt / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə beɪs peɪnt / (BrE)
paint in which water is used as the main solvent instead of oil or other chemicals

Examples

Water-base paint for furniture
Please check if the water-base paint is safe for children's furniture before you start painting.
Schools often use water-base paint in classrooms to reduce fumes and environmental impact.
We chose a water-base paint for the living room because it dries quickly and has low odor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: oil-based paint - paint using oil as the main solvent
