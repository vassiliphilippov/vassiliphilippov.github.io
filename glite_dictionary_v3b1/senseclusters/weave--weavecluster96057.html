weave

noun
pattern or structure of a fabric
5.2
Pronunciation: / wiːv / (AmE), / wiːv / (BrE)
the pattern or structure formed by interlacing threads in the production of a fabric

Examples

A tight weave
She examined the intricate weave of the traditional rug.
The scarf had a tight weave that made it very warm.
This fabric’s loose weave makes it lightweight and breathable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: texture - the feel or appearance of a fabric's surface
