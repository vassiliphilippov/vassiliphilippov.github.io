vest

verb
give authority or rights to someone
4.5
Pronunciation: / vɛst / (AmE), / vɛst / (BrE)
to give or grant someone a legal right, property, or authority, so that it becomes theirs

Examples

Rights vest in heirs
Ownership of the shares will vest in the children once they reach adulthood.
The constitution vests the power to declare war in the legislature.
The executive committee vested the authority to sign contracts in the managing director.
Ownership of the shares vests after five years of employment.
The estate will vest in the children when they turn eighteen.
Upon her father's death, the title vested in her name.
The company’s bylaws vest decision-making power in the board of directors.
The new constitution vests significant authority in the regional assemblies.
Under the agreement, all control will be vested in the independent board.

Related Terms

  • synonym: confer - to grant (a title, benefit, or right)
  • antonym: divest - to deprive someone of power, rights, or possessions
  • synonym: empower - to give someone the authority or power to do something
  • synonym: grant - to give or allow someone something, usually officially
  • antonym: revoke - to take back or withdraw a right or privilege
