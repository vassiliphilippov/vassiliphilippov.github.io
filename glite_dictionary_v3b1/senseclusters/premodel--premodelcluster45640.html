premodel

noun
preliminary model
Pronunciation: / ˈpriːˌmɑːdəl / (AmE), / ˈpriːˌmɒdəl / (BrE)
a preliminary model created before the final version or prototype to test ideas or designs

Examples

A premodel prototype
The engineers built a premodel to identify potential issues before starting full production.
By examining the premodel, the designers were able to make important improvements.
A detailed premodel helps teams visualize the final product early in the process.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mockup - a scale or full-size model used for demonstration or design evaluation
  • degree higher: prototype - an original model on which later versions are based
