loom

verb
appear indistinctly and often threateningly
Pronunciation: / lum / (AmE), / luːm / (BrE)
to appear as a large, unclear, or threatening form, either literally or figuratively

Examples

A shadow looms
A dark shape loomed out of the mist as we approached the old house.
As the deadline loomed, everyone felt increasing pressure to finish the project.
Storm clouds loomed on the horizon, threatening to ruin the picnic.
As the storm approached, dark clouds began to loom over the horizon.
In the dim light, unfamiliar shapes loomed in the distance, causing her to hesitate.
The castle loomed above the village, casting a long shadow at sunset.
A sense of danger seemed to loom in the abandoned building.
Dark clouds began to loom on the horizon as the storm approached.
Worries about the upcoming exam loom over him day and night.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fade - gradually disappear or become less distinct
  • synonym: threaten - to be a source of possible harm or danger
  • synonym: tower - to stand high above something or someone
