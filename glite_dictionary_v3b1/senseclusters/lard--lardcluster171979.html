lard

verb
to embellish with details
7.7 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / l…ërd / (AmE), / l…ëÀêd / (BrE)
to embellish speech, writing, or conversation with additional details, especially in a way that adds flavor or interest

Examples

To lard a story
His speech was larded with technical jargon that only experts could understand.
She likes to lard her stories with dramatic details for effect.
The professor larded his lecture with historical anecdotes to engage the students.
