thunder lizard

noun
large Jurassic herbivorous dinosaur
Pronunciation: / ˈθʌndɚ ˌlɪzɚd / (AmE), / ˈθʌndə ˌlɪzəd / (BrE)
a large quadrupedal herbivorous dinosaur, especially the Brontosaurus, which lived in North America during the late Jurassic period

Examples

A thunder lizard dinosaur
Children were amazed to see a life-sized model of a thunder lizard at the natural history museum.
Paleontologists uncovered new fossils belonging to a huge thunder lizard from the Jurassic period.
The most famous thunder lizard is the Brontosaurus, with its long neck and whip-like tail.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Brontosaurus - a genus of large sauropod dinosaurs known as 'thunder lizards'
