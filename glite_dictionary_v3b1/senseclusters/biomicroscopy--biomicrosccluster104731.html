biomicroscopy

noun
eye examination method
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˌbaɪ.oʊmaɪˈkrɑːskəpi /; / ˌbaɪoʊmaɪˈkrɑːskəpi / (AmE), / ˌbaɪ.əʊmaɪˈkrɒskəpi /; / ˌbaɪəʊmɪˈkrɒskəpi / (BrE)
a medical examination of living tissues, especially the eye, using a microscope (such as a slit-lamp)

Examples

Biomicroscopy of eye
The doctor performed biomicroscopy to check for any damage to the patient's cornea.
Routine biomicroscopy can help detect early signs of eye disease.
After the accident, biomicroscopy revealed a small scratch on the lens.
The ophthalmologist used biomicroscopy to check for abnormalities in the patient's cornea.
Advances in biomicroscopy have improved the diagnosis of many eye diseases.
Researchers rely on biomicroscopy to observe living cells in real time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: histology - the study of the microscopic structure of tissues
  • synonym: microscopy - the use of a microscope to view small objects
  • synonym: ophthalmoscopy - examination of the inside of the eye using an ophthalmoscope
  • synonym: slit-lamp examination - an eye test using a special microscope and light
