resetter

noun
person or machine resetting type
Pronunciation: / riˈsɛtər / (AmE), / riˈsɛtə / (BrE)
a person or machine that resets type or text layout, arranging pages and spacing for printing or publication

Examples

Text resetter
The publishing house employed a resetter to update the book's layout after major revisions.
A skilled resetter can quickly adjust the spacing and alignment of text for a clean look.
With new software, the role of a resetter has become more automated in modern printing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: editor - a person who prepares text for publication
  • synonym: typesetter - a person who arranges type for printing
