major mineral

noun
mineral required in large amounts by the body
1.5 2.9
Pronunciation: /ˈmeɪ.dʒər ˈmɪn.ər.əl/ (AmE), /ˈmeɪ.dʒə ˈmɪn.ər.əl/ (BrE)
a mineral that the body requires in relatively large amounts for normal physiological functioning, such as calcium, potassium, or magnesium.

Examples

Essential major minerals
A balanced diet should include all the essential major minerals for optimal health.
Calcium is considered a major mineral because the body needs it in relatively large quantities.
Nutritionists often distinguish between trace elements and major minerals when discussing dietary requirements.

Related Terms

  • antonym: trace element - a chemical element required by the body in very small amounts
