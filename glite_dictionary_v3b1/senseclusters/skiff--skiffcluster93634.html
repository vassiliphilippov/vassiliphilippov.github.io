skiff

noun
small light boat for one person
Pronunciation: / skɪf / (AmE), / skɪf / (BrE)
a small, light boat usually for one person, propelled by oars, sail, or motor

Examples

A skiff boat
A fisherman tied his skiff to the wooden dock after a long day at sea.
He rowed the skiff across the calm lake at dawn.
The storm quickly overturned the small skiff as it neared the shore.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dinghy - small inflatable or rowing boat
  • degree higher: launch - large motorboat, especially one used to carry passengers
