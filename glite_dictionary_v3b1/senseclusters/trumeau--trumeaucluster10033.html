trumeau

noun
decorated central pillar
Pronunciation: / truːˈmoʊ / (AmE), / truːˈməʊ / (BrE)
the central pillar or post between the doors of a large entrance, especially in churches, often decorated with carvings or paintings

Examples

Cathedral trumeau
The cathedral's trumeau was intricately carved with scenes from the Bible.
Restorers carefully cleaned the painted trumeau to reveal its original colors.
Visitors admired the detailed figures on the trumeau at the entrance to the ancient church.
The sculpted trumeau in the cathedral's portal depicted biblical scenes.
Restorers carefully cleaned the ancient trumeau to reveal its original carvings.
A massive stone trumeau supports the entrance to the medieval church.
