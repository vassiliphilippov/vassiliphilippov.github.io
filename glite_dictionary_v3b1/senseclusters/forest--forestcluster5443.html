forest

noun
large area covered with trees
1.2
Pronunciation: / ˈfɔːrɪst /; / ˈfɔr.ɪst / (AmE), / ˈfɒrɪst /; / ˈfɒr.ɪst / (BrE)
a large area of land covered mainly with trees and undergrowth

Examples

Dense forest
Several species of birds nest only in old-growth forests where the canopy is thick and undisturbed.
The Amazon rainforest is the largest forest in the world and is home to diverse wildlife.
We took a hike through the dense forest to enjoy the peaceful surroundings.
A variety of animals, from deer to foxes, make their home in the forest.
Much of the country's forest has been cleared for farmland.
We hiked through the ancient forest, listening to the sounds of birds and rustling leaves.

Related Terms

  • antonym: desert - area with little vegetation, especially few or no trees
  • synonym: jungle - land densely covered with tropical vegetation
  • synonym: woodland - land covered with trees and shrubs, usually smaller than a forest
