strophe

noun
section of a lyric poem or choral ode
8.8
Pronunciation: / ˈstroʊfi / (AmE), / ˈstrəʊfi / (BrE)
A group of lines forming a section of a lyric poem or choral ode, especially in classical Greek drama.

Examples

A strophe in poetry
During the strophe, the chorus moved from right to left across the stage.
Each strophe in the poem introduces a new idea or theme.
The poet divided his ode into a strophe, an antistrophe, and an epode.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antistrophe - the section of an ode answering the strophe, performed in response
