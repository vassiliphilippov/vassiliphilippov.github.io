mucus

noun
thick bodily secretion from mucous membranes
7.4 2.8
Pronunciation: /ˈmjuː.kəs/ (AmE), /ˈmjuː.kəs/ (BrE)
A thick, slippery secretion produced by mucous membranes, which lubricates and protects body passages such as the nose, throat, and gut.

Examples

Thick mucus secretion
Doctors examined the color of the mucus to check for signs of infection.
Excess mucus in the lungs can make breathing more difficult for asthma patients.
The cold left him with a constant flow of mucus from his nose.
