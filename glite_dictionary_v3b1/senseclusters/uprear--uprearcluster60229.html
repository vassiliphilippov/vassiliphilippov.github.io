uprear

verb
to rise upright
8.8
Pronunciation: / ʌpˈrɪr / (AmE), / ʌpˈrɪə / (BrE)
to rise up or lift the front part of the body into an upright position, especially of an animal rearing suddenly

Examples

The horse uprears
The frightened dog began to uprear when it saw the stranger approaching.
When startled by the noise, the horse would uprear and paw at the air.
The statue seemed to uprear above the crowd, towering over everyone in the square.
