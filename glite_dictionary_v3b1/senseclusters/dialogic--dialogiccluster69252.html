dialogic

adjective
involving dialogue
Pronunciation: / ˌdaɪəˈlɑdʒɪk /; / ˌdaɪəˈlɑːdʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌdaɪəˈlɒdʒɪk / (BrE)
involving or characterized by dialogue, conversation, or the interaction of multiple voices or perspectives

Examples

Dialogic discussion
The professor encouraged a dialogic approach to classroom discussions, allowing all students to share their perspectives.
Her writing style is highly dialogic, presenting different viewpoints without favoring one over the others.
A dialogic exchange between the two artists led to a collaborative project that blended their ideas.
The professor used a dialogic method to encourage student participation in the seminar.
A dialogic exchange between the two leaders helped resolve the conflict.
Modern education often values a dialogic approach over traditional lectures.

Related Terms

  • synonym: conversational - relating to or like a conversation
  • antonym: monologic - characterized by a single voice or viewpoint
