pinocytosis

noun
cellular uptake of fluid by engulfment
5.2
Pronunciation: / ˌpaɪnəsaɪˈtoʊsɪs / (AmE), / ˌpɪnəʊsaɪˈtəʊsɪs / (BrE)
A process by which certain cells engulf and internalize small droplets of fluid from their surroundings.

Examples

Pinocytosis in cells
Researchers observed increased pinocytosis in the immune cells under the microscope.
The process of pinocytosis enables a cell to absorb nutrients dissolved in fluid.
Unlike phagocytosis, pinocytosis involves the uptake of liquids rather than large particles.
