radiobiology

noun
study of effects of radiation on life
Pronunciation: / ˌreɪdioʊbaɪˈɑlədʒi / (AmE), / ˌreɪdɪəʊbaɪˈɒlədʒi / (BrE)
the branch of biology that studies the effects of radiation on living organisms

Examples

Radiobiology research
Many cancer treatments are informed by discoveries in radiobiology.
Recent advances in radiobiology have improved our understanding of how radiation affects cells at the molecular level.
She decided to specialize in radiobiology during her graduate studies in medical research.
