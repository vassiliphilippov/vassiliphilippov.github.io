heme

noun
iron-containing blood pigment
9.9 6.5
Pronunciation: /hiːm/ (AmE), /hiːm/ (BrE)
an iron-containing red pigment found in hemoglobin and other biological molecules, responsible for oxygen transport in the blood

Examples

The heme group
A deficiency of heme synthesis can result in certain types of anemia.
Scientists studied the structure of heme to understand how oxygen binds to blood.
The red color of blood comes from the heme groups in hemoglobin.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hematin - an oxidized derivative of heme found in some biological processes
  • synonym: hemoglobin - the protein in red blood cells containing heme, responsible for carrying oxygen
