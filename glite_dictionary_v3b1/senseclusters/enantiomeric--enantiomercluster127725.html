enantiomeric

adjective
mirror-image molecules
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˌnæntiəˈmɛrɪk / (AmE), / ɪˌnæntiəˈmɛrɪk / (BrE)
relating to molecules that are mirror images of each other but cannot be superimposed

Examples

Enantiomeric molecules
The scientist analyzed the enantiomeric composition of the compound to determine its purity.
Some drugs have different effects depending on their enantiomeric form.
The enantiomeric pairs of molecules can interact differently with biological systems.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chiral - having a structure that is not superimposable on its mirror image
