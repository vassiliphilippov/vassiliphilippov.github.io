polyphase

adjective
having two or more alternating voltages of different phases
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑliˌfeɪz / (AmE), / ˈpɒliˌfeɪz / (BrE)
(of an electrical system) using or generating two or more alternating voltages of the same frequency but differing in phase angle.

Examples

A polyphase motor
A polyphase motor is commonly used in industrial applications due to its smooth operation.
The electrician explained the benefits of a polyphase supply compared to a single-phase one.
The factory upgraded its machinery to a polyphase electrical system for greater efficiency.

Related Terms

  • antonym: single-phase - using a single alternating voltage
  • degree higher: three-phase - consisting of three alternating voltages with differing phases
