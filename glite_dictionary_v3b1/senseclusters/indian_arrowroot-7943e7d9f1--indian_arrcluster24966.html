Indian arrowroot

noun
tropical plant with edible starchy root
9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈɪndiən ˈæroʊˌruːt / (AmE), / ˈɪndiən ˈærəʊˌruːt / (BrE)
a tropical perennial herb native to the East Indies, Polynesia, and Australia, cultivated for its large, starchy edible root

Examples

The Indian arrowroot plant
Farmers in Australia cultivate Indian arrowroot for both local use and export.
The starchy root of Indian arrowroot is often used to make flour for baking.
Traditional dishes in Polynesia frequently include Indian arrowroot as a key ingredient.
