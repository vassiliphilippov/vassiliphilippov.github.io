momentously

adverb
in a significant or important manner
Pronunciation: / moʊˈmɛn.təs.li / (AmE), / məʊˈmɛn.təs.li / (BrE)
In a way that is very important or significant and likely to have a lasting effect.

Examples

To speak momentously
He spoke momentously, aware that his words would shape the nation's future.
The decision to launch the satellite was momentously announced to the press.
The treaty was momentously signed, marking a new era in international relations.
