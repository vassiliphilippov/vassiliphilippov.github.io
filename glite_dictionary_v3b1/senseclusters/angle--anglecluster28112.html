angle

verb
move or proceed at an angle
3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈæŋɡəl / (AmE), / ˈæŋɡl̩ / (BrE)
to move, turn, or position something so that it is not straight but at an angle

Examples

To angle a mirror
He angled his way through the crowded hallway, weaving between people.
She angled herself toward the podium to get a better view of the speaker.
The truck angled across the narrow bridge to avoid hitting the railing.
He angled the chair so he could see the television.
She carefully angled the mirror to catch the sunlight.
The artist angled his head to get a better view of the painting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: straighten - to make or become straight
  • synonym: tilt - to move or cause to move into a sloping position
