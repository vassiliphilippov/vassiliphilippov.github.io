extermination

noun
complete destruction or annihilation
Pronunciation: / ɪkˌstɜrməˈneɪʃən /; / ɛkˌstɝːməˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪkˌstɜːmɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the act or result of completely destroying or eliminating a group of living things (such as animals, pests, or people)

Examples

The extermination of pests
The extermination of native species can disrupt entire ecosystems.
The pesticide led to the extermination of the entire termite colony.
The plan called for the extermination of all invasive plants in the area.
Many historians study the extermination of populations during war.
The extermination of invasive species has helped restore the natural ecosystem.
The city launched an extermination campaign to control the rodent population.

Related Terms

  • synonym: annihilation - complete destruction; utter obliteration
  • synonym: eradication - the complete destruction of something harmful
  • antonym: preservation - the act of keeping something intact
