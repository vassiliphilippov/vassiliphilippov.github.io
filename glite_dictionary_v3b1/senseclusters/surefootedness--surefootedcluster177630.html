surefootedness

noun
steadiness in movement
6.5
Pronunciation: / ˌʃʊrˈfʊtɪdnəs / (AmE), / ˌʃɔːˈfʊtɪdnəs / (BrE)
the quality of being steady and confident in movement, especially on difficult or uneven surfaces

Examples

Goat's surefootedness
The mountain goat's surefootedness allows it to climb steep cliffs with ease.
Her surefootedness impressed everyone as she danced across the narrow beam.
Good surefootedness is essential for hikers on rocky or slippery trails.

Related Terms

  • synonym: balance - the ability to stay upright and not fall
  • antonym: clumsiness - the quality of moving awkwardly or without control
  • synonym: stability - the state of being steady and not likely to fall
