the quality of a material that allows it to be shaped or formed by hammering or pressure without breaking or cracking

Examples

The malleability of gold

Scientists tested the malleability of the new metal alloy under high pressure.
The malleability of copper allows it to be formed into thin sheets and wires.

Synonyms

  • formability
    6.0 0.01/m
    formability describes how easily a material is shaped in many ways without cracking, while malleability focuses on shaping by hammering or pressure.
  • plasticity
    6.1 0.29/m
    plasticity means a material can be permanently deformed and hold a new shape, while malleability implies shaping by pressure without cracking.
  • moldability
    5.8 0.01/m
    moldability refers to shaping a material in a mold or form easily, while malleability usually means shaping by hammering or pressure.
  • pliability
    6.0 0.03/m
    pliability means something bends or flexes easily without breaking, while malleability involves shaping under pressure without cracking.
