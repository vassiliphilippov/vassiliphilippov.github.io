dissociation

noun

a psychological condition in which part of a person's mind becomes separated from the rest, often functioning independently, as seen in disorders such as dissociative identity disorder

Examples

Trauma dissociation
In cases of severe trauma, dissociation may help a person cope by separating painful memories from conscious awareness.
She experienced episodes of dissociation when under extreme stress, feeling detached from her body and surroundings.
The therapist explained that dissociation can interrupt a person's sense of identity and memory.
