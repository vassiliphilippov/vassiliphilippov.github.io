button

noun
on-screen control (computing)
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈbʌtən / (AmE), / ˈbʌt.ən /; / ˈbʌtn̩ / (BrE)
a small device or icon that you press or click to control a machine, electronic equipment, or computer program

Examples

Click the button at the bottom of the screen to submit your application.
If you hold down the power button, the computer will shut down.
She accidentally pressed the delete button and lost her work.
Press the button to start the coffee machine.
She couldn't find the emergency button in the elevator.
The power button on the remote control was stuck.
He accidentally hit the red button and stopped the machine.
Please press the button to call the elevator.
The alarm will sound if you push this button under the desk.

Related Terms

  • synonym: icon - a small graphic symbol on a screen representing a program, file, or function
  • synonym: switch - a device for making and breaking the connection in an electric circuit
