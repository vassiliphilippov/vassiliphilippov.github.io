mid-channel

adjective
in the channel center
Pronunciation: / ˈmɪdˌtʃænəl / (AmE), / ˈmɪdˌtʃænəl / (BrE)
located in or relating to the central part of a communication, broadcast, or audio channel.

Examples

Mid-channel interference
The engineer adjusted the equipment to reduce mid-channel interference during the broadcast.
A mid-channel signal drop caused the audio to lose clarity in the middle frequencies.
Technicians often monitor for mid-channel issues to ensure balanced sound quality.

Related Terms

  • synonym: center - the middle point or part of something
  • antonym: edge - the outer or farthest part from the center
