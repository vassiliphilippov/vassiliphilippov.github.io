radical

noun
person with extreme or reformist views
Pronunciation: / ˈrædɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈrædɪkəl / (BrE)
a person who advocates or supports thorough or complete political or social reform; someone with extreme views or opinions

Examples

A political radical
Over time, the group attracted more radicals who wanted major changes in policy.
Some saw her as a radical, while others admired her courage to speak out.
The student was known as a radical for challenging traditional beliefs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: extremist - a person who holds extreme political or religious views
  • antonym: moderate - a person whose opinions are not extreme
