arbovirus

noun
virus transmitted by arthropods
Pronunciation: / ˈɑr.boʊˌvaɪ.rəs / (AmE), / ˈɑː.bəʊˌvaɪ.rəs / (BrE)
any of a group of viruses transmitted by arthropods such as mosquitoes and ticks, causing diseases in humans and animals.

Examples

Arboviruses spread by mosquitoes
Many arboviruses are responsible for diseases such as dengue and yellow fever.
Researchers are studying how climate change affects the spread of arboviruses globally.
The outbreak of Zika was traced to an arbovirus transmitted by local mosquitoes.
