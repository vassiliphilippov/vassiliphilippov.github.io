ecclesiarchy

noun
church leadership
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪˈkliːziˌɑːrki / (AmE), / ɪˈkliːziˌɑːki / (BrE)
the governing body or leadership of a church, especially those who exercise ecclesiastical authority

Examples

The ecclesiarchy meets
The ecclesiarchy convened to address important matters affecting the congregation.
Throughout history, the ecclesiarchy has played a central role in shaping church doctrine.
Disputes within the ecclesiarchy sometimes led to significant changes in church policy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clergy - the group of people ordained for religious duties in a church
  • antonym: laity - members of a religious community who are not clergy
