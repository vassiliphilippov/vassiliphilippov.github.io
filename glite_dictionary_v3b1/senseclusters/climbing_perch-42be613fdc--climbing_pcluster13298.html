climbing perch

noun
air-breathing fish that can travel on land
Pronunciation: / ˈklaɪmɪŋ pɜrtʃ / (AmE), / ˈklaɪmɪŋ pɜːtʃ / (BrE)
a small freshwater fish of South and Southeast Asia, able to breathe air and travel short distances on land using its spiny fins

Examples

A climbing perch fish
A fisherman spotted a climbing perch moving across the muddy bank to reach another pond.
Biologists study how the climbing perch travels short distances on land using its spiny fins.
The climbing perch can survive out of water for several hours by breathing air through its gills.
