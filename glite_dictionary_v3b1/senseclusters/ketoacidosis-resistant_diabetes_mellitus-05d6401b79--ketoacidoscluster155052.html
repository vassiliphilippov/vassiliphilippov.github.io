ketoacidosis-resistant diabetes mellitus

noun
form of adult-onset diabetes without ketoacidosis
7.6
Pronunciation: /kiː.toʊ.ə.ˈsaɪ.dɪs rɪ.ˈzɪs.tənt daɪ.ə.ˈbiː.tiz mə.ˈlaɪ.təs/ /; / /kiː.toʊ.ə.ˈsaɪ.dɪs riː.ˈzɪs.tənt daɪ.ə.ˈbiː.tiz mə.ˈlaɪ.təs/ (AmE), /kiː.təʊ.ə.ˈsaɪ.dɪs rɪ.ˈzɪs.tənt daɪ.ə.ˈbiː.tɪs mə.ˈlaɪ.təs/ /; / /kiː.təʊ.ə.ˈsaɪ.dɪs rə.ˈzɪs.tənt daɪ.ə.ˈbiː.tɪs mə.ˈlaɪ.təs/ (BrE)
A mild form of diabetes mellitus developing gradually in adults, often triggered by obesity, severe stress, menopause, or similar factors, and usually controlled by diet or hypoglycemic agents rather than insulin injections.

Examples

Managing ketoacidosis-resistant diabetes mellitus
Managing ketoacidosis-resistant diabetes mellitus often involves lifestyle changes and oral medications instead of insulin injections.
Obesity can increase the risk of developing ketoacidosis-resistant diabetes mellitus in adults.
The doctor diagnosed her with ketoacidosis-resistant diabetes mellitus after she showed high blood sugar but no signs of acidosis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: type 1 diabetes - form of diabetes mellitus resulting from the body's failure to produce insulin
  • synonym: type 2 diabetes - form of diabetes mellitus characterized by insulin resistance rather than lack of insulin production
