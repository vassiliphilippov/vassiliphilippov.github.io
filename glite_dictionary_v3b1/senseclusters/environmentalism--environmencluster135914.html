environmentalism

noun
belief that environment shapes intellect
9.9 4.2
Pronunciation: / ɪnˌvaɪrənˈmɛntəlɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪnˌvʌɪrənˈmɛnt(ə)lɪz(ə)m / (BrE)
The philosophical belief that environmental factors are more significant than heredity in determining intellectual development.

Examples

Environmentalism in psychology
During the debate, she argued for environmentalism by emphasizing the impact of upbringing and social factors on intelligence.

Modern psychology often considers both heredity and environmentalism when explaining human behavior.
