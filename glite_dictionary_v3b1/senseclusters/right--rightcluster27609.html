right

verb
set upright again; restore to proper position
9.9 3.6
Pronunciation: / raɪt / (AmE), / raɪt / (BrE)
to put something back into a correct, upright, or proper position; to correct or restore

Examples

To right a boat
After the boat capsized, they managed to right it and continue their journey.
She tried to right the fallen lamp before anyone noticed.
The crew worked quickly to right the overturned truck on the highway.
After the storm, villagers helped to right the trees blown over by the wind.
The rescue workers worked quickly to right the car after the accident.
With all their effort, the crew managed to right the overturned sailboat.
After realizing his mistake, he made an effort to right the situation.

The government tried to right the injustices of the past.
