subbituminous

noun
lower-rank coal
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˌsʌbˌbɪtəˈmɪnəs / (AmE), / ˌsʌbˌbɪtjʊˈmɪnəs / (BrE)
a type of coal that is lower in rank than bituminous coal and commonly used as fuel in power stations

Examples

Burning subbituminous in plants
The power plant relies on subbituminous to generate electricity for the entire region.
Compared to bituminous coal, subbituminous produces less energy but is still widely used.
Large reserves of subbituminous are found in the western United States.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: bituminous - a higher-rank type of coal with more carbon content
  • degree lower: lignite - a lower-rank, brownish coal with less carbon content
