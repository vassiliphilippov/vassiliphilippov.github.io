huckster

verb
to peddle goods from place to place
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌkstər / (AmE), / ˈhʌkstə / (BrE)
to sell or promote goods in an aggressive, pushy, or sometimes dishonest way

Examples

To huckster cheap goods
He huckstered homemade jewelry at fairs across the region last summer.
Street performers often huckster small items to tourists along the boardwalk.
The vendors would huckster their wares in every village they passed through.
He spent the afternoon huckstering cheap souvenirs to tourists.
The company was accused of huckstering miracle cures that did not work.
Street vendors huckster their goods loudly to attract passing customers.

  • synonym: market - to promote or sell products or services
  • synonym: peddle - to travel about selling goods
