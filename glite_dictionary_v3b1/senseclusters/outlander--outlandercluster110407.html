outlander

noun
a foreigner; outsider
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊtˌlændɚ / (AmE), / ˈaʊtˌlændə / (BrE)
a person from a foreign country; a foreigner or outsider, especially one who does not belong to the local community or nation

Examples

An outlander visitor
As an outlander, she found it difficult to adapt to the local customs.
The ancient laws distinguished between citizens and outlanders in terms of property rights.
The villagers viewed the outlander with suspicion, unsure of his intentions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: foreigner - a person from another country
  • antonym: native - a person born in or associated with a place by birth
