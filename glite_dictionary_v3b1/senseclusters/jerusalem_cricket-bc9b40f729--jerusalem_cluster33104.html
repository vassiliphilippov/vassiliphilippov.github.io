Jerusalem cricket

noun
large, wingless, burrowing insect of western US
Pronunciation: / dʒəˈruːsələm ˈkrɪkɪt / (AmE), / dʒəˈruːsələm ˈkrɪkɪt / (BrE)
a large, wingless, nocturnal insect found in loose soil along the Pacific coast of the United States, known for its burrowing behavior and distinctive appearance

Examples

The Jerusalem cricket insect
Despite its name, the Jerusalem cricket does not chirp like true crickets.
Many people mistake the Jerusalem cricket for a dangerous insect, but it is generally harmless.
The gardener found a Jerusalem cricket burrowing under the flower bed.
