tut-tut

interjection
expression of mild disapproval
8.5 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / t\u028ct\u02c8t\u028ct / (AmE), / t\u028ct\u02c8t\u028ct / (BrE)
An exclamation representing a short sound of disapproval or mild reproach, used to show annoyance or disapproval.

Examples

A tut-tut of annoyance
When she saw the messy room, she murmured tut-tut and shook her head.
The critic's review ended with a single tut-tut that made the author blush.
At the meeting, an older member clicked his tongue and said tut-tut under his breath.

Synonyms

  • tut
    8.8 0.32/m
    tut is a quick single exclamation showing mild annoyance, while tut-tut is a doubled sound that often shows firmer disapproval.
  • tsk
    9.9 0.12/m
    tsk is a sharp tongue click used to scold quickly, while tut-tut feels more like a soft, spoken reproach between people.
  • tch
    9.9 0.16/m
    tch is a short, often impatient click used in speech, while tut-tut is a longer, more clearly disapproving sound.
