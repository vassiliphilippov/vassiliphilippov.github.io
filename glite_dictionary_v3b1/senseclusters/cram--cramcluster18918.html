cram

verb
to pack tightly or completely
4.7 2.7
Pronunciation: /kræm/ (AmE), /kræm/ (BrE)
to fill something completely by forcing things or people into it so there is no space left

Examples

To cram a suitcase
She managed to cram all her clothes into a single suitcase.
They crammed the tiny room with balloons for the party.
Tourists tried to cram onto the last bus leaving the station.
Children crammed sweets into their pockets before leaving the party.
He tried to cram all his clothes into the small backpack.
The suitcase was crammed with shoes and souvenirs from her trip.

  • synonym: jam - to squeeze or pack tightly into a space
  • synonym: stuff - to fill tightly with something
