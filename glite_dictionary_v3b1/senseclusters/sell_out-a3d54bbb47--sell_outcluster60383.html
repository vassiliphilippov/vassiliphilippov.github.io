sell out

verb
to sell all available merchandise
1.0
Pronunciation: / sɛl aʊt / (AmE), / sɛl aʊt / (BrE)
to sell all of the available goods or tickets so that nothing remains; to dispose of all merchandise

Examples

Tickets sell out fast
By noon, the bakery had sold out of its famous chocolate croissants.
The concert tickets sold out within two hours of going on sale.
We expect the new model to sell out before the end of the week.
