electroweak interaction

noun
unified force of electromagnetism and weak nuclear force
9.2
Pronunciation: /ɪ.lek.trə.ˈwiːk ɪn.tə.ˈræk.ʃən/ /; / /ɪ.lek.troʊ.ˈwiːk ɪn.tə.ˈræk.ʃən/ /; / /ɪ.lek.trə.ˈwiːk ɪn.ər.ˈæk.ʃən/ (AmE), /ɪ.lek.trə.ˈwiːk ɪn.tə.ˈræk.ʃən/ /; / /ɪ.lek.trəʊ.ˈwiːk ɪn.tə.ˈræk.ʃən/ /; / /ɪ.lek.trə.ˈwiːk ɪn.ə.ˈræk.ʃən/ (BrE)
In particle physics, the unified fundamental force that combines electromagnetism and the weak nuclear force, described together by the electroweak theory.

Examples

The electroweak interaction theory
Research on the electroweak interaction has deepened our understanding of fundamental particles and forces.
The discovery of the W and Z bosons provided evidence for the electroweak interaction.
The unification of the electroweak interaction was a major breakthrough in theoretical physics.

Related Terms

  • synonym: electromagnetic interaction - The fundamental force governing interactions between charged particles; electromagnetism.
  • synonym: weak interaction - The force responsible for processes such as beta decay in nuclear physics; the weak nuclear force.
