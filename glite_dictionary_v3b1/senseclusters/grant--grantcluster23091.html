grant

noun
money given for a specific purpose
Pronunciation: / ɡrænt / (AmE), / ɡrɑːnt / (BrE)
a sum of money given by a government or organization for a specific purpose that does not have to be paid back

Examples

A grant application
Applicants must submit their proposals by June to be considered for the grant.
She received a grant from the government to fund her research project.
The museum renovation was made possible by a generous grant from a local foundation.
Nonprofit organizations often rely on government grants to support their programs.
She applied for a research grant to study environmental changes in the region.
The university received a large grant to fund its new science building.

Related Terms

  • antonym: loan - money that must be repaid, especially with interest
  • synonym: scholarship - money awarded to a student to help pay for education
  • synonym: subsidy - money given by an authority to help support an activity or reduce costs
