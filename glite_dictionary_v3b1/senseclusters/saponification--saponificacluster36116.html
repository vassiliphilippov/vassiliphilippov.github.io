saponification

noun
chemical process making soap from fat and alkali
Pronunciation: / səˌpoʊnəfɪˈkeɪʃən / (AmE), / səˌpɒnɪfɪˈkeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process in which a fat or oil reacts with an alkali, especially during the making of soap.

Examples

Saponification process
During the chemistry experiment, the students observed saponification by mixing vegetable oil and sodium hydroxide.
The industrial production of soap relies on saponification to convert fats into useful cleaning agents.
Understanding saponification is essential for anyone interested in traditional soap making.
