Silurian

noun
geological period, 443–419 million years ago
9.9
Pronunciation: /sɪ.ˈlʊr.iː.ən/ /; / /saɪ.ˈlʊr.iː.ən/ (AmE), /sɪ.ˈlʊə.riː.ən/ /; / /saɪ.ˈlʊə.riː.ən/ (BrE)
the geological period between the Ordovician and Devonian periods, around 443 to 419 million years ago, marked by the appearance of the first air-breathing animals and extensive marine life.

Examples

The Silurian period
During the Silurian, the first vascular plants appeared on land.
Fossils from the Silurian period reveal the early evolution of jawed fishes.
Geologists have identified many important geological formations dating back to the Silurian.
