myeloid leukemia

noun
chronic leukemia affecting white blood cells
5.6 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / Ààma…™.…ô.l…î…™d l…ôÀàkiÀê.mi.…ô / (AmE), / Ààma…™.…ô.l…î…™d l…™ÀàkiÀê.mi.…ô / (BrE)
A type of chronic leukemia marked by an abnormal increase in granular white blood cells, more commonly affecting older adults.

Examples

Myeloid leukemia in adults
Older adults are at a higher risk of developing myeloid leukemia.
Recent advances in treatment have improved survival rates for people with myeloid leukemia.

Synonyms

  • chronic myelocytic leukemia
    5.3 0.00/m
    chronic myelocytic leukemia is a long-lasting blood cancer with many granular white cells, while myeloid leukemia is the broader medical term.
  • myelocytic leukemia
    6.8 0.02/m
    myelocytic leukemia mainly shows a rise in immature myelocyte cells in the blood, while myeloid leukemia covers similar marrow cancers.
  • granulocytic leukemia
    6.8 0.00/m
    granulocytic leukemia means cancer with too many granule-rich white cells in the blood, while myeloid leukemia is the general clinical category.
