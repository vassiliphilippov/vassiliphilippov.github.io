molar

adjective
relating to concentration in moles per liter
Pronunciation: / ˈmoʊlər / (AmE), / ˈməʊlə / (BrE)
(of a solution or measurement) containing one mole of a substance per liter (used in chemistry)

Examples

A molar solution
A one molar solution contains exactly one mole of solute per liter of solvent.
Always label the concentration of acids in molar terms to avoid confusion.
The chemist prepared a molar solution of sodium chloride for the experiment.
Scientists determine molar mass to find out how much of a substance is present.
She added enough solute to reach a molar value of 1 mol/L.
The molar concentration of the solution was calculated for the experiment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mole - the SI base unit for amount of substance
