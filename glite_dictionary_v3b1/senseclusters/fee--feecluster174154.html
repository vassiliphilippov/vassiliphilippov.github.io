fee

noun
an inheritable interest in land (legal)
9.3 2.23/m
Pronunciation: / fi / (AmE), / fiË / (BrE)
a legal interest in land, especially one that can be inherited

Examples

Fee simple land
Many land titles in England originated as a fee held from a feudal lord.
The family held the land in fee for generations, passing it down from parent to child.
Under medieval law, a fee simple estate provided the owner full inheritable rights.
