Hamiltonian

noun
energy function in physics
Pronunciation: / ˌhæməlˈtoʊniən / (AmE), / ˌhæmɪlˈtəʊniən / (BrE)
a function in physics that represents the total energy of a system, used especially in mechanics and quantum mechanics

Examples

Hamiltonian function
In quantum mechanics, the Hamiltonian operator determines the evolution of a system over time.
The physicist wrote down the Hamiltonian to analyze the total energy of the particles.
Understanding the Hamiltonian is essential for solving many problems in classical and quantum physics.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Lagrangian - a function in physics representing the difference between kinetic and potential energy
  • synonym: energy - the capacity to do work or cause change
