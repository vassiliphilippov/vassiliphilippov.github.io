to leave a place and move away from it, especially in a formal or literary context

Examples

To go forth bravely
He commanded his soldiers to go forth and spread the message.
Let us go forth into the world with hope and determination.
The explorers will go forth at dawn to begin their journey across the mountains.

Synonyms

  • depart
    1.3 13/m
    depart is a formal verb for beginning a journey or leaving a place, while go forth is literary and old-fashioned in tone.
  • set forth
    3.9 0.38/m
    set forth means to begin a journey or travel to a destination, while go forth is more literary and less common.
  • set out
    2.4 6.66/m
    set out means to start a journey or trip with a clear plan, while go forth sounds more formal or poetic.
  • leave
    1.0 507/m
    leave means to go away from a person or place in everyday speech, while go forth is formal or poetic and rarely used.
  • exit
    1.0 8.88/m
    exit means to go out of a building or situation quickly, while go forth is broader and more literary in everyday use.
