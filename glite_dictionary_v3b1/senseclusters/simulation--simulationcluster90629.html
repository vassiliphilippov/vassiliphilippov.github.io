simulation

noun
computerized imitation of real-world processes
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪmjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsɪmjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
an imitation or model of a real-world process, system, or situation, often using computers, for study, training, or testing

Examples

Flight simulation training
Flight schools use advanced computer simulation to train pilots in handling emergencies.
Scientists ran a weather simulation to predict the impact of climate change over the next decade.
The new traffic system was tested through a simulation before being implemented in the city.
In science class, the teacher used a computer simulation to show how earthquakes happen.
The company created a virtual simulation of the new product before manufacturing it.
The flight simulation helped pilots practice for real emergencies.
Medical students used a virtual simulation to learn how to respond to emergencies.
The company developed a computer simulation to test the factory's workflow before construction.
The flight simulation allowed new pilots to practice landing in challenging conditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emulation - the process of imitating another system, especially in computing
  • synonym: model - a simplified representation used to explain or predict real-world processes
  • antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist
  • synonym: replica - an exact copy of something, often on a different scale
