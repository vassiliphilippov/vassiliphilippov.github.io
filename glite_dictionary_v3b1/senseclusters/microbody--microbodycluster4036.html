microbody

noun
cellular organelle
8.0
Pronunciation: / ˈmaɪkroʊˌbɑːdi / (AmE), / ˈmaɪkrəʊˌbɒdi / (BrE)
a small, membrane-bound structure within a cell, such as a peroxisome, that performs a specific function

Examples

Each microbody in the liver cell plays a role in breaking down harmful substances.
Researchers study the function of the microbody to better understand cellular metabolism.
