show-off

noun
person seeking attention or admiration
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈʃoʊ ˌɔf / (AmE), / ˈʃəʊ ˌɒf / (BrE)
a person who deliberately acts in a way intended to attract attention or admiration, often in a boastful or ostentatious manner

Examples

A show-off at party
He acted like a show-off at the party, constantly drawing everyone's attention to himself.
Julia can be a real show-off when she talks about her expensive shoes.
Nobody likes a show-off who tries to impress everyone all the time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: braggart - a person who boasts about achievements or possessions
  • antonym: modest person - someone who does not seek attention or boast
