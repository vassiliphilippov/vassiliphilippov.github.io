sego lily

noun
North American perennial with white bell-shaped flowers
8.9
Pronunciation: / ˈseɪɡoʊ ˈlɪli / (AmE), / ˈsiːɡəʊ ˈlɪli / (BrE)
A perennial plant native to western North America, having clusters of one to four showy white, bell-shaped flowers and edible bulbs.

Examples

The sego lily plant
Native Americans used the bulbs of the sego lily as a food source during hard times.
The sego lily thrives in arid regions and is the state flower of Utah.
We spotted several blooming sego lilies during our hike through the prairie.
