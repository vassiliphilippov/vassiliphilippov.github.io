skipper

noun
officer commanding a ship, especially in the navy
6.0 3.4
Pronunciation: /ˈskɪp.ər/ (AmE), /ˈskɪp.ə/ (BrE)
the captain or officer in command of a ship or boat

Examples

Ship skipper
After years at sea, she finally became the skipper of her own ship.
All the crew respected the skipper for his calm leadership during the storm.
The skipper gave the order to change course immediately.
As skipper, she was responsible for the safety of everyone on board.
The skipper of the cargo ship gave orders to the crew before departure.
When the weather turned rough, the skipper remained calm and navigated safely to port.

Related Terms

  • synonym: captain - the person in command of a ship
  • antonym: crew member - a member of a ship's crew, subordinate to the skipper
  • antonym: crewman - a member of the crew, not the commanding officer
