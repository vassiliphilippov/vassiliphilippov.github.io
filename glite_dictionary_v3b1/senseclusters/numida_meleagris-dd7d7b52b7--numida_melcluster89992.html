Numida meleagris

noun
African guineafowl species kept for food
9.9
Pronunciation: / nuˈmaɪdə məˈliːəɡrɪs / (AmE), / njuːˈmiːdə mɛˈliːəɡrɪs / (BrE)
A species of guineafowl native to Africa, characterized by dark plumage speckled with white, which is kept domestically for its meat and eggs.

Examples

The Numida meleagris bird
Many restaurants feature dishes made from Numida meleagris as a delicacy.
The farmer raises Numida meleagris for both their eggs and their flavorful meat.
Wild Numida meleagris are often spotted roaming the savannas of West Africa.
