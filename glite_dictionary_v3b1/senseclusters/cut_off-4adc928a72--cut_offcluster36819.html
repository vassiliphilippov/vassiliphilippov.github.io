cut off

verb
suddenly stop or discontinue
1.0 3.2
Pronunciation: /kʌt ɔːf/ /; / /kʌt ɑːf/ (AmE), /kʌt ɒf/ /; / /kʌt ɔːf/ (BrE)
to stop, interrupt, or block something suddenly or before it is finished

Examples

To cut off a call
He was cut off in the middle of his speech when the microphone stopped working.
Our phone call was suddenly cut off due to poor reception.
The principal cut off the music in the gym because it was too loud.
Her phone conversation was suddenly cut off when the call dropped.
Please don't cut off the movie; I'm watching.
The announcer had to cut off the speech for a breaking news update.
A fallen tree cut off access to the village during the storm.
She was talking when he rudely cut her off mid-sentence.
The driver in the red car cut off three vehicles as he changed lanes.
