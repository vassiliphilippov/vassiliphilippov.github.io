moody

adjective
frequently changing moods
Pronunciation: / ˈmuːdi / (AmE), / ˈmuːdi / (BrE)
often changing mood quickly, especially becoming unhappy, angry, or gloomy without warning

Examples

A moody person
He can be quite moody in the mornings, so it's best to give him space until he wakes up fully.
She became especially moody during her teenage years, sometimes happy and other times withdrawn for no apparent reason.
The weather was as moody as his artistic temperament, changing from sunshine to storms within an hour.
After receiving the bad news, he became quite moody and withdrew from the group.
She can be moody in the mornings, so it's best to let her wake up gradually.
The film's moody atmosphere added to its dramatic tension.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cheerful - noticeably happy and optimistic
  • antonym: even-tempered - not easily annoyed or angered; calm and steady
  • synonym: sullen - silent and bad-tempered; showing irritation or ill humor by a gloomy silence or reserve
  • synonym: temperamental - liable to unpredictable changes of mood or temper
