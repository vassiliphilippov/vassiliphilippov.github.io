tread

noun
surface of wheel, tire, or shoe in contact with ground
Pronunciation: / trɛd / (AmE), / trɛd / (BrE)
the part of a tire, shoe, wheel, or stair that touches the ground or is stepped on, often with a pattern for grip

Examples

Shoe tread pattern
After miles of hiking, the tread on his boots was almost completely worn away.
Inspect your shoe tread regularly to avoid slipping on smooth surfaces.
The tread on these tires is designed to improve traction in wet conditions.
Be careful; the tread on the third step is loose.
Each tread is made of oak to match the flooring.
The architect specified wider tread dimensions for safety.
Make sure the tread on your tires is deep enough to drive safely in the rain.
The mechanic checked the tread for signs of uneven wear before rotating the tires.
Worn tread can greatly increase the risk of skidding on wet roads.

Related Terms

  • antonym: riser - the vertical part between treads in a staircase
  • antonym: smooth tire - tire without grooves, lacking a tread
  • synonym: sole - the bottom part of a shoe or foot
  • synonym: track - continuous band of treads on vehicles for movement
