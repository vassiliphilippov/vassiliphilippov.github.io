superposition

noun
principle that lower strata are oldest
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərpəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəzɪˈʃən /; / ˌsuːpəpəˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
the principle or process in geology where rock or sediment layers are arranged with the oldest at the bottom and newer layers on top

Examples

Law of superposition
Geologists use superposition to determine the relative ages of different rock layers.
The law of superposition is fundamental for dating layers of sedimentary rock.
Without the principle of superposition, it would be difficult to interpret the geological history of an area.
Careful analysis of superposition allows researchers to reconstruct the geological history of an area.
In this region, superposition has resulted in a complex sequence of limestone and sandstone beds.
The law of superposition helps geologists determine the relative ages of sedimentary rock layers.
