monstrance

noun
religious vessel for displaying the Host
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmɑːn.strəns / (AmE), / ˈmɒn.strəns / (BrE)
a vessel, often made of gold or silver, used in the Roman Catholic Church to display the consecrated Host for adoration

Examples

A monstrance vessel
During the ceremony, the priest placed the consecrated Host in the monstrance for all to see.
Gold and silver are commonly used to craft a monstrance for use in Catholic liturgy.
The ornate monstrance gleamed under the church lights during the procession.
