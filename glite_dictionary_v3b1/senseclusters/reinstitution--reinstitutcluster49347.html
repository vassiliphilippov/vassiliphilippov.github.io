reinstitution

noun
restoring something
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌɪnstɪˈtuːʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌɪnstɪˈtjuːʃən / (BrE)
the act of bringing something back into use or effect after it has been stopped or discontinued

Examples

Reinstitution of a law
The reinstitution of the old rules surprised many employees at the company.
After several years, the reinstitution of the festival brought joy to the community.
Debate continues over the possible reinstitution of mandatory uniforms in schools.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abolition - the act of formally ending or stopping something
  • synonym: restoration - the act of returning something to a former condition
