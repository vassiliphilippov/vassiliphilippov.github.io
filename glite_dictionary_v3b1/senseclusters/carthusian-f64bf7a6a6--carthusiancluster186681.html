Carthusian

adjective
relating to the Carthusian religious order
7.2 9.7
Pronunciation: /kɑːr.ˈθjuː.ʒən/ /; / /kɑːr.ˈθjuː.zɪ.ən/ (AmE), /kɑː.ˈθjuː.ʒən/ /; / /kɑː.ˈθjuː.zɪ.ən/ (BrE)
of or relating to the Carthusian order, a Roman Catholic religious order of monks and nuns founded by St. Bruno in 1084.

Examples

Carthusian monastery life
She visited a remote Carthusian monastery in the French Alps.
The artwork was inspired by Carthusian spirituality and simplicity.
The monks followed the strict Carthusian rule, dedicating their lives to contemplation and silence.
