partial-birth abortion

noun
a controversial late-term abortion procedure
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑrʃəl ˈbɜrθ əˈbɔrʃən / (AmE), / ˌpɑːʃl ˈbɜːθ əˈbɔːʃən / (BrE)
a late-term abortion procedure in which the fetus is partially delivered before being terminated, particularly one known medically as intact dilation and extraction; a term used chiefly in political or legal contexts.

Examples

Partial-birth abortion procedure
Her article explores the ethical issues surrounding partial-birth abortion and its regulation.
Lawmakers debated whether to ban partial-birth abortion except in cases of medical necessity.
The court ruled on legislation restricting partial-birth abortion procedures nationwide.
