extensor muscle

noun
muscle that straightens a body part
2.0 4.2
Pronunciation: /ɪk.ˈsten.sər ˈmʌs.əl/ /; / /ek.ˈsten.sər ˈmʌs.əl/ (AmE), /ɪk.ˈsten.sə ˈmʌs.əl/ /; / /ek.ˈsten.sə ˈmʌs.əl/ (BrE)
A skeletal muscle whose contraction causes a limb or other body part to straighten or extend.

Examples

The extensor muscle contracts
Injuries to the extensor muscle can make it difficult to extend your fingers properly.
Physical therapy helped strengthen the extensor muscle after her wrist surgery.
The extensor muscle in your arm allows you to straighten your elbow after it has been bent.
