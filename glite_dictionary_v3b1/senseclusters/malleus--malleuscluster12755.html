malleus

noun
bone in the middle ear attached to the eardrum
5.8 8.0
Pronunciation: /ˈmæ.lɪ.əs/ (AmE), /ˈmæ.lɪ.əs/ (BrE)
the small hammer-shaped bone in the middle ear that is attached to the eardrum and transmits sound vibrations to the incus

Examples

The malleus bone
Damage to the malleus can result in hearing loss.
During the operation, the surgeon carefully examined the patient's malleus.
The malleus plays a crucial role in transmitting sound from the eardrum to the inner ear.

Related Terms

  • synonym: incus - the anvil-shaped bone in the middle ear receiving vibrations from the malleus
  • synonym: stapes - the stirrup-shaped bone in the middle ear, next to the incus
