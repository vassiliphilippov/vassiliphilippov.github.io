Frigid Zone

noun
either polar region with frigid climate
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈfrɪdʒɪd ˌzoʊn / (AmE), / ˈfrɪdʒɪd zəʊn / (BrE)
either of the two regions of the Earth surrounding the North or South Pole, characterized by very cold climates

Examples

The Frigid Zone
Few plants can survive the extreme cold of the Frigid Zone.
Polar bears are native to the Frigid Zone in the northern hemisphere.
Scientists conduct research in the Frigid Zone to study the effects of climate change.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: temperate zone - the part of the Earth's surface lying between the tropics and the polar circles, featuring moderate climates
  • antonym: torrid zone - the region of the Earth between the Tropic of Cancer and the Tropic of Capricorn, characterized by a hot climate
