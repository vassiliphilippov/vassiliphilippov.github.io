permeant

adjective
spreading throughout; able to penetrate
9.3 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…úÀêr.mi.…ônt / (AmE), / Ààp…úÀê.mi.…ônt / (BrE)
spreading or tending to spread throughout every part of a substance or area; able to penetrate or diffuse through.

Examples

Permeant smell
Fear became a permeant aspect of daily life during the crisis.
Some chemicals act as permeant agents, diffusing easily through membranes.
The permeant aroma of spices lingered in every corner of the kitchen.

Synonyms

  • permeative
    8.3 0.00/m
    permeative describes something that spreads through a whole material or group like dye through cloth, while permeant stresses being able to penetrate.
  • diffusible
    7.2 0.02/m
    diffusible means able to pass through by spreading like scent moving slowly through a room, while permeant emphasizes full penetration.

Related Terms
