blood cyst

noun
a cyst filled with blood
Pronunciation: / blʌd sɪst / (AmE), / blʌd sɪst / (BrE)
a cyst filled with blood, usually resulting from internal bleeding or vascular injury

Examples

A large blood cyst
A blood cyst may require treatment if it causes pain or grows in size.
On the ultrasound image, the radiologist identified a blood cyst in the patient’s kidney.
The surgeon discovered a blood cyst during the procedure, which had formed after a minor injury.
