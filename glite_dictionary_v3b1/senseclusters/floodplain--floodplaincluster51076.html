floodplain

noun
low-lying land by a river prone to floods
4.6 1.71/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆflÊŒdËŒpleÉªn / (AmE), / ËˆflÊŒdËŒpleÉªn / (BrE)
an area of low-lying ground next to a river, formed mainly of river sediments and subject to flooding.

Examples

A floodplain area
Developers must consider the risk of flooding before planning construction on a floodplain.
Much of the village was built on a floodplain, making it vulnerable during heavy rains.
The Mississippi River floodplain supports a wide range of plant and animal species.

Synonyms

  • bottomland
    7.2 0.48/m
    bottomland is low, fertile land by a river that often floods, while floodplain is any low area formed by river sediments.
  • bottom
    5.8 3.27/m
    bottom is low lying alluvial land near a river that often floods, while floodplain is an area formed by river sediments and prone to flooding.
  • alluvial plain
    5.8 0.08/m
    alluvial plain is a broad flat area of sediment left by rivers, while floodplain is the low river edge often flooded.

Related Terms

  • synonym: delta - landform at the mouth of a river formed by deposition of sediment
