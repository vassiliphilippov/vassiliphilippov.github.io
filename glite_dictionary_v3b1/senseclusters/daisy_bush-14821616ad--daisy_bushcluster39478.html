daisy bush

noun
Australian shrub with daisy-like flowers
Pronunciation: / ˈdeɪzi bʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈdeɪzi bʊʃ / (BrE)
An evergreen shrub of the genus Olearia, native mainly to Australia, cultivated for its attractive, daisy-like flowers and sometimes fragrant foliage.

Examples

A daisy bush plant
Some species of daisy bush have a pleasant fragrance that makes them popular ornamental plants.
The blue and purple flowers on the daisy bush attract bees and butterflies in spring.
The vibrant blooms of the daisy bush added color to her garden throughout the year.

Related Terms

  • synonym: olearia - genus of shrubs native to Australia and New Zealand, including the daisy bush
