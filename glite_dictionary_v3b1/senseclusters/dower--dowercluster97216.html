dower

verb
to provide with a dowry or endowment
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊ.ər / (AmE), / ˈdaʊ.ə / (BrE)
to provide a woman with a dowry or endowment, especially upon marriage

Examples

To dower a bride
In some traditions, the bride would be dowered as part of the wedding ceremony.
She was dowered with a vast estate by her wealthy uncle.
The princess was dowered handsomely upon her marriage to the prince.
