Rhamnaceae

noun
a family of thorny trees and shrubs (botany)
9.9
Pronunciation: / ræmˈneɪsiː / (AmE), / ramˈneɪsiː / (BrE)
A family of mostly thorny trees and shrubs, often with drupe-like fruits, several of which have medicinal uses.

Examples

Rhamnaceae plant family
Botanists have studied the medicinal properties of various plants in the Rhamnaceae.
Many Rhamnaceae species are characterized by their thorny branches and small fruits.
The Rhamnaceae family includes well-known species like the jujube and buckthorn.
