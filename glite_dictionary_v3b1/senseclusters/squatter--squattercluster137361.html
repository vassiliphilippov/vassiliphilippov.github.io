squatter

noun
person occupying property illegally
Pronunciation: / ˈskwɑtər / (AmE), / ˈskwɒtə / (BrE)
a person who settles on land or occupies property without legal right or title

Examples

Urban squatter
Authorities removed the squatters from the vacant lot after several complaints.
She lived as a squatter in the city for two years before finding a legal home.
The abandoned building was taken over by a group of squatters seeking shelter.

Related Terms

  • antonym: tenant - a person who pays rent to use land or property
  • synonym: trespasser - someone who enters property without permission
