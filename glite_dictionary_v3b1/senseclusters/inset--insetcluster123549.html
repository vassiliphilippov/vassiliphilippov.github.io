inset

noun
small picture within a larger one
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɪnˌsɛt / (AmE), / ˈɪnˌsɛt / (BrE)
a small picture, map, or illustration placed within the borders of a larger one, often to provide additional detail or information

Examples

A map inset
A small inset in the corner displayed the route in greater detail.
The atlas included an inset showing a detailed map of the city center.
The magazine featured an inset photograph to highlight the architectural details.
