sextuplet

noun
group of six notes
Pronunciation: / sɛkˈstʌplət / (AmE), / sɛkˈstjuːplət / (BrE)
in music, a group of six notes played in the time normally given to four or another number of notes

Examples

A sextuplet in music
The composer used a sextuplet to add rhythmic complexity to the melody.
It can be challenging for beginners to play a sextuplet smoothly at a fast tempo.
The violinist performed a rapid sextuplet during the solo section of the piece.

Related Terms

  • synonym: quintuplet - a group of five notes played in the time of four
  • antonym: triplet - a group of three notes played in the time of two
