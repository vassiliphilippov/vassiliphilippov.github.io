line width

noun
spectral line spread
7.3
Pronunciation: / laɪn wɪdθ / (AmE), / laɪn wɪdθ / (BrE)
the range of frequencies or wavelengths over which an emission or absorption line is spread in a spectrum, often measured as full width at half maximum

Examples

Spectral line width
The line width of the spectral feature indicated the temperature of the gas.
Scientists compared the line widths of different elements to study the star's composition.
A narrow line width suggests less turbulence in the observed region of space.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bandwidth - the range of frequencies within a given band, especially used in telecommunications
  • antonym: resolution - the ability to distinguish between two close spectral lines
