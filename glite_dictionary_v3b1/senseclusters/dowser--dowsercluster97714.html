dowser

noun
person who uses a divining rod
8.3
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊzər / (AmE), / ˈdaʊzə / (BrE)
a person who uses a divining rod to search for underground water or minerals

Examples

A dowser finds water
Many farmers hired a dowser to locate underground springs during the drought.
Skeptics question whether a dowser can truly detect hidden sources of water.
The dowser walked slowly across the field, holding the rod steady in his hands.
