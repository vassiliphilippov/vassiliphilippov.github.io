spooky

adjective
easily startled and unpredictable (esp. horses)
4.6 0.07/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàspuÀêki / (AmE), / ÀàspuÀêki / (BrE)
(of an animal, especially a horse) easily startled and unpredictable in behavior; likely to react suddenly, often with fear.

Examples

Spooky horse
That horse is spooky and jumps at the slightest movement in the field.
The trainer prefers calm horses to those that are overly spooky or unpredictable.
Young horses tend to be more spooky around loud noises or sudden motions.

Synonyms

  • skittish
    7.0 2/m
    skittish is easily frightened and jumps at sudden sounds, while spooky describes an animal likely to react suddenly from fear.
  • nervous
    1.3 50/m
    nervous feels anxious and tense in new situations, while spooky means an animal is easily startled and may react with fear.
  • flighty
    7.4 0.5/m
    flighty is changeable and distracted and often unreliable, while spooky describes sudden, fear-driven reactions in an animal, especially a horse.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nervous - easily agitated or alarmed; tending to be anxious or jumpy
  • antonym: steady - calm and not easily excited or startled
