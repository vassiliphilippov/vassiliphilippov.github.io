oil-rich seed

noun
seed high in oil content
Pronunciation: / ɔɪl rɪtʃ sid / (AmE), / ɔɪl rɪtʃ siːd / (BrE)
a seed containing a high amount of oil, often used for producing edible or industrial oils

Examples

Oil-rich seed crops
Farmers often rotate crops to include oil-rich seeds like canola and flax.
Sunflower is an example of an oil-rich seed used to produce cooking oil.
The demand for oil-rich seeds has increased due to the popularity of plant-based diets.
