weakish

adjective
somewhat weak
Pronunciation: / ˈwiːkɪʃ / (AmE), / ˈwiːkɪʃ / (BrE)
slightly weak or not very strong

Examples

A weakish coffee
The coffee was a little weakish this morning, so I added more grounds.
After his illness, he felt weakish but managed to go for a short walk.
The argument she made was weakish and failed to convince the audience.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mild - not strong or intense
  • antonym: strong - having great strength or power
  • degree higher: weak - lacking strength or power
