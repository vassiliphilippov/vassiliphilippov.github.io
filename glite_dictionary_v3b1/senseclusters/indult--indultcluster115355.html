indult

noun
official pardon or exemption
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈɪn.dʌlt/ /; / /ˈɪn.dəlt/ (AmE), /ˈɪn.dʌlt/ /; / /ˈɪn.dəlt/ (BrE)
an official permission, exemption, or special privilege granted by an authority, especially in legal or church contexts

Examples

A royal indult
The king granted an indult to the prisoners, allowing them to return home early.
In some cases, an indult may be issued to permit exceptions to strict regulations.
The church received an indult from the pope to celebrate a special feast day.
The priest applied for an indult to perform the ceremony in a different language.
An indult was granted, allowing the community to observe a unique tradition.
Without the bishop's indult, the exception to the rule could not be made.

  • antonym: penalty - a punishment imposed for breaking a law or rule
  • antonym: prohibition - the act of forbidding something by law or authority
  • synonym: remission - the cancellation of a penalty or debt
