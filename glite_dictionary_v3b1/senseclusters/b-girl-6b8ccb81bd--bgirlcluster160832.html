B-girl

noun
woman paid to socialize with bar patrons
Pronunciation: / ˈbiː ˌɡɜrl / (AmE), / ˈbiː ˌɡɜːl / (BrE)
a woman employed by a bar to socialize with male customers, often to encourage them to buy drinks

Examples

Bar B-girl
During the 1940s, it was common to find a B-girl mingling with patrons in city nightclubs.
He was wary of spending money after a B-girl kept persuading him to buy expensive drinks.
The detective suspected that the B-girl was involved in the scheme to overcharge customers.
