principle

noun
fundamental rule or law
1.0 3.2
Pronunciation: /ˈprɪn.sə.pəl/ /; / /ˈprɪn.sɪ.pəl/ (AmE), /ˈprɪn.sɪ.pəl/ /; / /ˈprɪn.sə.pəl/ (BrE)
a basic law or truth that explains how something works or forms the foundation of a system (especially in science, technology, or reasoning)

Examples

Scientific principle
She explained the basic principles behind engine operation to the students.
The principle of conservation of energy is fundamental in physics.
Understanding the principle of supply and demand is important in economics.
He refused to compromise on his principle of honesty, even when it was difficult.
Scientific experiments are designed to test the basic principle behind a theory.
The company's core principle is to treat all customers with respect.
One important principle in chemistry is the conservation of energy.
The principle of gravity explains why objects fall to the ground.
This engine operates based on the principle of air compression and expansion.
