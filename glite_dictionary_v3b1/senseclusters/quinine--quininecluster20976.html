quinine

noun
antimalarial compound from cinchona bark
Pronunciation: / ˈkwaɪniːn / (AmE), / kwɪˈniːn / (BrE)
a bitter compound obtained from the bark of cinchona trees, used especially to treat malaria

Examples

Bitter quinine medicine
Doctors once prescribed quinine to treat cases of malaria.
The discovery of quinine was a breakthrough in the fight against tropical diseases.
Tonic water contains a small amount of quinine, giving it its distinctive bitter taste.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chloroquine - a synthetic drug related to quinine, used to treat malaria
