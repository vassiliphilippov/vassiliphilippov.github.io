soar up

verb
rise or increase rapidly
Pronunciation: / sɔr ʌp / (AmE), / sɔː ʌp / (BrE)
to rise or increase rapidly and often to a great height or value.

Examples

Prices soared up
As news of the discovery spread, the stock prices soared up dramatically.
Profits for the company soared up after the launch of their new product line.
The temperature began to soar up as the afternoon sun grew stronger.

Related Terms

  • antonym: plummet - fall or drop straight down at high speed
  • synonym: rocket - rise suddenly and rapidly
