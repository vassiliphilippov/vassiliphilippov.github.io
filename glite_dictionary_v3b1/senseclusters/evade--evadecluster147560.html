evade

verb
avoid fulfilling a duty or answering a question
9.1
Pronunciation: / ɪˈveɪd / (AmE), / ɪˈveɪd / (BrE)
to avoid or try to avoid fulfilling, answering, or performing something, especially a duty, question, or issue

Examples

To evade a question
He tried to evade the question by changing the subject.
She skillfully evaded her responsibilities during the meeting.
Some companies use loopholes to evade paying taxes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: avoid - to keep away from or prevent oneself from doing something
  • antonym: confront - to face a problem, duty, or question directly
