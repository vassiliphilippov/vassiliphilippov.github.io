inclusion

noun
a small body within a cell
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkluʒən / (AmE), / ɪnˈkluːʒən / (BrE)
a small body or particle present inside a cell, often observed in certain diseases or under abnormal conditions

Examples

Cell inclusion
Certain viral infections cause the formation of unusual inclusions within cell nuclei.
One hallmark of the disease is the presence of cytoplasmic inclusions seen under the microscope.
The pathologist observed a large inclusion in the infected liver cell.
