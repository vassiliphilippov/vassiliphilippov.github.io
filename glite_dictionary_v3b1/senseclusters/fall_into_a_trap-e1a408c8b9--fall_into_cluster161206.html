fall into a trap

verb
be deceived or ensnared
Pronunciation: / fɔl ˈɪntu ə træp / (AmE), / fɔːl ˈɪntuː ə træp / (BrE)
to be deceived or caught in a difficult or dangerous situation, often through trickery or a mistake

Examples

To fall into a trap
During the debate, he skilfully avoided falling into a trap set by his opponent.
Many investors fall into a trap when they pursue unrealistic returns.
She fell into a trap by trusting someone she barely knew.

Related Terms

  • synonym: be caught - to be captured or trapped, often by surprise
  • antonym: escape - to get free from a trap or difficult situation
