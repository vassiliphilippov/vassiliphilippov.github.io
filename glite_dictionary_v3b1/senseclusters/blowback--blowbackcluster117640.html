blowback

noun
unexpected negative reaction
Pronunciation: / ˈbloʊˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈbləʊˌbæk / (BrE)
an unexpected negative reaction or result caused by a previous action or decision

Examples

Public blowback
The government received major blowback from the public after introducing the controversial law.
She did not expect the amount of blowback her comments would generate on social media.
The company faced significant blowback after announcing the new policy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: backlash - a strong negative reaction to an event or decision
  • synonym: consequence - a result or effect of an action or condition
