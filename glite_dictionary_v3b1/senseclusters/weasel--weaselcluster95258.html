weasel

noun
small elongated carnivorous mammal
Pronunciation: / ˈwiːzəl / (AmE), / ˈwiːzəl / (BrE)
a small carnivorous mammal with a slender, elongated body, short legs, and typically brown or white fur, belonging to the genus Mustela

Examples

A wild weasel
A weasel darted across the path and disappeared into the undergrowth.
Farmers sometimes have trouble keeping weasels away from their chicken coops.
The weasel is known for its agility and sharp hunting skills.

Related Terms

  • synonym: stoat - a related carnivorous mammal, also called the ermine
