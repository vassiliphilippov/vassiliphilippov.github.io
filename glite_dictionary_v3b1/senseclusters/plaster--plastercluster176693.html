plaster

noun
building material for walls and ceilings
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈplæstɚ /; / ˈplæstər / (AmE), / ˈplɑːstə / (BrE)
a mixture that hardens to form a smooth surface, used for covering walls, ceilings, or making casts

Examples

Wall plaster
After the plaster dried, the ceiling was ready to be painted.
Old houses often have beautifully decorated plaster moldings on the walls and ceilings.
The workers applied plaster to the damaged wall to make it smooth again.
After she broke her arm, the doctor applied a plaster cast to keep it stable.
Artists often use plaster to make detailed molds and sculptures.
The statue was created out of plaster and then painted to resemble bronze.
A fresh layer of plaster gave the ceiling a smooth, finished look.
She accidentally made a hole in the plaster while hanging a picture.
The old house had peeling plaster on many of the walls.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cement - a binding material used in construction
  • synonym: plaster of Paris - a common type of plaster made from calcined gypsum
  • synonym: stucco - a fine plaster used for coating wall surfaces or molding into architectural decorations
