swamp

verb
to drench or submerge; to flood
2.4 3.3
Pronunciation: /swɑːmp/ /; / /swɔːmp/ (AmE), /swɒmp/ (BrE)
to flood or overwhelm completely, either with water or with a large amount of something

Examples

To swamp a house
During the flood, water swamped homes near the river banks.
The rescue team was nearly swamped by the rising tide.
The surprise downpour swamped the football field within minutes.
Heavy rain can easily swamp the drains, causing local flooding.
Her schedule was so busy that she felt completely swamped by her responsibilities.
The customer service team was swamped with requests after the product launch.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flood - to cover with water, especially suddenly; to inundate
  • synonym: inundate - to flood or overwhelm someone with things or people to be dealt with
  • synonym: overwhelm - to overpower or strongly affect emotionally or physically
