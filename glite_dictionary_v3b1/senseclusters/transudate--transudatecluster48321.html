transudate

noun
fluid that passes through a membrane
9.5 8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈtræn.sjəˌdeɪt / (AmE), / ˈtranz.jʊ.deɪt / (BrE)
A fluid that passes through a membrane or tissue, often due to imbalances in pressure rather than inflammation, typically clear and with low protein content.

Examples

A transudate fluid
Pleural effusions can be categorized as either transudate or exudate based on their protein content.
The accumulation of transudate in the abdominal cavity can indicate liver disease.
Unlike exudate, transudate tends to be clear and low in cells.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exudate - fluid that leaks out due to inflammation
