defensor

noun
church legal official
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfɛnsɔr / (AmE), / dɪˈfɛnsɔː / (BrE)
an official in church law appointed to defend the validity of a marriage or protect legal rights in church proceedings

Examples

Church defensor
The defensor presented evidence to support the couple's marriage during the church tribunal.
A defensor is required in certain church legal proceedings to ensure fairness.
During the annulment process, the defensor argued that the marriage should be considered valid.

Related Terms

  • synonym: advocate - a person who publicly supports or defends a cause or person
  • antonym: prosecutor - a legal official who brings charges against someone
