Atlas

proper noun
Titan in Greek mythology who bore the sky
Pronunciation: / ˈætləs / (AmE), / ˈatləs / (BrE)
(In Greek mythology) a Titan condemned by Zeus to carry the sky on his shoulders.

Examples

Atlas holds sky
In ancient myths, Atlas was punished by Zeus to hold up the heavens forever.
Many stories cite Atlas as a symbol of strength and endurance.
The sculpture in the museum depicts Atlas carrying the celestial sphere.
