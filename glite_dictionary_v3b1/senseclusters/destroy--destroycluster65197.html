destroy

verb
to completely ruin or put an end to
Pronunciation: / dɪˈstrɔɪ / (AmE), / dɪˈstrɔɪ / (BrE)
to damage something so badly that it no longer exists or cannot be used or repaired

Examples

To destroy a building
If you drop your phone in water, it might destroy the device completely.
The old documents were destroyed to protect people's privacy.
The storm destroyed several buildings in the town last night.

Related Terms

  • antonym: create - to bring something into existence
  • synonym: demolish - to completely destroy a building
  • synonym: ruin - to severely damage so that no longer works or is valuable
