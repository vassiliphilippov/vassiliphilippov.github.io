ordainment

noun
formal decree
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɔrˈdeɪnmənt / (AmE), / ɔːˈdeɪnmənt / (BrE)
an official order, decree, or formal rule, especially in historical or archaic contexts

Examples

Royal ordainment
The king's ordainment established new laws for the entire kingdom.
According to the ancient ordainment, only nobles could own land in that region.
The council issued an ordainment to regulate trade within the city walls.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decree - an official order issued by a legal authority
  • synonym: ordinance - an official rule or law issued by an authority
