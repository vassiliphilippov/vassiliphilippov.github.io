Rolando's fissure

noun
groove separating frontal and parietal brain lobes
9.4
Pronunciation: / roʊˈlændoʊz ˈfɪʃər / (AmE), / rəʊˈlændəʊz ˈfɪʃə / (BrE)
a deep groove on the lateral surface of the brain separating the frontal and parietal lobes; also known as the central sulcus

Examples

The Rolando's fissure groove
A tumor near the Rolando's fissure can affect both movement and sensation in the body.
Damage to the Rolando's fissure can disrupt communication between the frontal and parietal lobes.
The neurosurgeon carefully identified the Rolando's fissure before proceeding with the operation.
