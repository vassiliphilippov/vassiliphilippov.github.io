ryot

noun
Indian peasant farmer
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈraɪ.ət/ /; / /ˈraɪ.ɪt/ (AmE), /ˈraɪ.ət/ /; / /ˈraɪ.ɪt/ (BrE)
a peasant or tenant farmer in India, especially during the period of British rule

Examples

A ryot farmer
The ryot worked long hours in the fields to support his family.
Under the ryotwari system, each ryot paid taxes directly to the colonial authorities.
Many ryots struggled to pay the high land revenue demanded by the government.
The ryot paid a portion of his harvest to the landowner each year.
During colonial times, many ryots struggled under heavy taxes and poor harvests.
A ryot often lived in a small village and worked the surrounding fields.
