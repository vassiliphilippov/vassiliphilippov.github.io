galleon

noun
large historic Spanish sailing ship
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈɡæliən / (AmE), / ˈɡalɪən / (BrE)
A large, square-rigged sailing ship with three or more masts, used mainly by the Spanish for commerce and war between the 15th and 18th centuries.

Examples

A galleon ship
A restored galleon is now on display in the maritime museum.
Pirates often targeted the slow-moving galleons carrying valuable cargo across the Atlantic.
The galleon set sail from Spain, loaded with gold and spices bound for the New World.
