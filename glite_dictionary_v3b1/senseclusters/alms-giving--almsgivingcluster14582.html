alms-giving

noun
giving money or help to the poor
Pronunciation: / ˈɑːlmzˌɡɪvɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɑːmzˌɡɪvɪŋ / (BrE)
The practice of voluntarily giving money, food, or other help to those in need, especially as a charitable act.

Examples

Alms-giving to the poor
During the festival, the community organizes alms-giving events for the poor.
His regular alms-giving brought comfort to many families in his neighborhood.
Throughout history, alms-giving has played an important role in many religious traditions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: charity - the act of giving help, usually money, to those in need
