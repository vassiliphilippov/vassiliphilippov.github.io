mass

noun
property of matter, causes weight
2.8 3.4
Pronunciation: /mæs/ (AmE), /mɑːs/ /; / /mæs/ (BrE)
the property of a physical body that gives it weight in a gravitational field and resistance to acceleration.

Examples

The mass of an object
Despite being the same size, lead has a greater mass than wood.
Physicists carefully measured the mass of each sample in the laboratory.
The mass of an object determines how much force is needed to move it.
