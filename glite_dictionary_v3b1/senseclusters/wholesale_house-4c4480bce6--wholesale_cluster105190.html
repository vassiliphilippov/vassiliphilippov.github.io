wholesale house

noun
a business selling goods in bulk at discount prices
3.8 3.6
Pronunciation: / ˈhoʊlˌseɪl haʊs / (AmE), / ˈhəʊlseɪl haʊs / (BrE)
a business that sells goods in large quantities at lower prices, typically to retailers or other businesses rather than to the general public

Examples

A wholesale house business
Many small retailers rely on a wholesale house for their inventory supply.
The company purchased electronics from a wholesale house to reduce costs.
The new wholesale house offers discounted prices to registered business customers only.

Related Terms

  • antonym: retail store - a business that sells goods directly to consumers
  • synonym: wholesaler - a person or company that sells goods in large quantities to retailers
