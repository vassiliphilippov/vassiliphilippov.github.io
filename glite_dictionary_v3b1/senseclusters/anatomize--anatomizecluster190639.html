anatomize

verb
analyze in minute detail
6.4
Pronunciation: / əˈnætəˌmaɪz /; / əˈnæt̬.əˌmaɪz /; / əˈnæt̬.ə.maɪz / (AmE), / əˈnætəmaɪz /; / əˈnæt.ə.maɪz / (BrE)
to examine or analyze something in great detail, especially by breaking it down into its parts

Examples

To anatomize a problem
Journalists anatomize political scandals to uncover the truth behind the headlines.
The book anatomizes the complexities of modern relationships with remarkable precision.
The professor anatomized every argument presented in the essay.
Critics often anatomize a novel to uncover underlying themes and symbols.
The scientist decided to anatomize the specimen to better understand its structure.
To truly understand the issue, we must anatomize its various components.
In his review, the critic carefully anatomised the novel’s plot and themes.
Medical students often anatomise cadavers to understand human anatomy in detail.
The scientist decided to anatomise the frog to learn more about its internal organs.

Related Terms

  • synonym: analyse - to examine in detail to understand something better
  • synonym: analyze - to examine something methodically by separating into parts
  • synonym: dissect - to cut apart body parts for examination
