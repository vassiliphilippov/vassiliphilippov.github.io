drizzle

noun
very light rain; fine drops
6.4
Pronunciation: / ˈdrɪzəl / (AmE), / ˈdrɪzəl / (BrE)
Rain falling lightly in very fine drops, stronger than mist but less intense than a shower.

Examples

A light drizzle
A light drizzle began to fall, making the streets shiny and damp.
By the time we reached the park, the drizzle had turned into a steady rain.
Despite the persistent drizzle, the football match went ahead as planned.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: downpour - a heavy fall of rain
  • degree lower: mist - a phenomenon of tiny water droplets suspended in the air
  • degree higher: shower - a brief and usually light fall of rain
