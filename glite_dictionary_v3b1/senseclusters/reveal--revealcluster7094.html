Synonyms

  • disclose
    4.1 13/m
    disclose is a formal verb used to make secret information known to others, while reveal is more general and common.
  • disclose
    4.0 0.87/m
    disclose is used to remove a cover or show something hidden physically, while reveal covers visible and secret facts.
  • divulge
    6.2 2.33/m
    divulge is used to tell secret or private information often against advice, while reveal is more neutral.
  • uncover
    3.5 20/m
    uncover is to find and make hidden facts or objects known, often in investigations, while reveal is broader.
  • bring to light
    3.7 1.73/m
    bring to light is a phrase for making hidden facts public and known, while reveal simply shows what was hidden.
  • unveil
    4.1 7.03/m
    unveil often describes a formal first showing of something new in public, while reveal can be private or casual.
  • expose
    4.1 21/m
    expose often reveals wrongdoing or harm by making secrets public, while reveal is more neutral about consequences.
  • bring out
    3.7 0.39/m
    bring out is used to make information or products public for people to see, while reveal is more general and simple.
  • give away
    3.4 3.57/m
    give away often means accidentally or carelessly telling a secret, while reveal may be planned or intentional.
  • unmask
    4.2 1.23/m
    unmask means show someone's true identity or nature, often proving a hidden lie, while reveal is more general.
