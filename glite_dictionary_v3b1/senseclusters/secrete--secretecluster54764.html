secrete

verb
produce and release a bodily substance
1.6 6.9
Pronunciation: / sɪˈkrit / (AmE), / sɪˈkriːt / (BrE)
(of cells or glands) to produce and release a substance, such as a hormone or enzyme, into the body or a bodily fluid.

Examples

Glands secrete hormones
Certain glands in the body secrete hormones that regulate growth.
Some frogs secrete toxins through their skin as a defense mechanism.
The pancreas secretes insulin to help control blood sugar levels.

Related Terms

  • antonym: excrete - to eliminate waste matter from the body
