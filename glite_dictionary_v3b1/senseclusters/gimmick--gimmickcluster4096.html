gimmick

noun
trick or device to attract attention
2.6 4.6
Pronunciation: /ˈgɪm.ɪk/ (AmE), /ˈgɪm.ɪk/ (BrE)
A clever or unusual trick or device, especially one intended to attract attention, publicity, or business.

Examples

Marketing gimmick
Some people saw the free giveaway as a marketing gimmick rather than a real offer.
The magician's act relied on a clever gimmick that amazed the audience.
The new packaging was just a gimmick to catch the eyes of shoppers.
