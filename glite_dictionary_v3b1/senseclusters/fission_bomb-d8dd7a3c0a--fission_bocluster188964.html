fission bomb

noun

A nuclear weapon that releases enormous energy by splitting the nuclei of heavy elements such as uranium-235 or plutonium-239.

Examples

A fission bomb explosion
A fission bomb relies on the splitting of atomic nuclei to unleash its destructive force.
Modern nuclear arsenals include both fission bombs and more advanced weapons.
The first fission bomb was detonated in 1945 during the Trinity test.
