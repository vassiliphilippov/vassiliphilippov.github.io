wrinkle

verb
form creases or folds
Pronunciation: / ˈrɪŋkəl / (AmE), / ˈrɪŋkl̩ /; / ˈrɪŋkəl / (BrE)
to make or become creased or folded, especially in skin, fabric, or a surface

Examples

To wrinkle the paper
Be careful not to wrinkle the paper when wrapping the gift.
If you leave your shirt on the floor, it will wrinkle quickly.
She tried not to wrinkle the invitation as she carried it.
He wrinkled his nose at the unpleasant smell.
She wrinkled the fabric by accident while ironing.
The old letter had wrinkled edges from being folded for years.
She frowned, causing her forehead to wrinkle in concern.
The old photograph had begun to wrinkle around the edges.
Try not to wrinkle your dress while packing for the trip.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crease - to make lines or folds in something by pressing or folding
  • synonym: pucker - to gather or contract into folds or wrinkles
  • antonym: smooth - to make something flat or even by removing wrinkles or folds
