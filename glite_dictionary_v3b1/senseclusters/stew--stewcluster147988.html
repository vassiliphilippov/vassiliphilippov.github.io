stew

verb
cook food slowly in liquid
Pronunciation: / stuː / (AmE), / stjuː / (BrE)
to cook food slowly in liquid over low heat for a long time

Examples

To stew vegetables
She let the apples stew gently until they became soft and sweet.
The chef prefers to stew the vegetables slowly rather than boiling them.
You can stew the beef with onions and carrots for extra flavor.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: boil - to cook food in water or liquid at 100°C
  • degree lower: simmer - to cook food gently just below boiling point
