feudalist

noun
supporter of feudalism
9.0
Pronunciation: /ˈfjuː.də.lɪst/ (AmE), /ˈfjuː.də.lɪst/ (BrE)
a person who supports, believes in, or is associated with the feudal system

Examples

A strict feudalist
The feudalist demanded loyalty and service from everyone living on his land.
Historians often debate the influence of the feudalist class in shaping medieval Europe.
A feudalist typically held both economic and political power in the feudal system.
The medieval scholar was considered a prominent feudalist by his peers.
As a feudalist, she often wrote articles defending the structure of feudal society.
The debate featured a feudalist and a critic of hereditary privilege.

Related Terms

  • synonym: aristocrat - a member of the highest social class, often with inherited titles or land
  • synonym: feudalism - the social system in medieval Europe based on land ownership and duties
  • antonym: serf - a person in a feudal system who works the land for a lord
