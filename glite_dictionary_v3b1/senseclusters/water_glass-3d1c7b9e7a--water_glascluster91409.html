water glass

noun
solution of sodium silicate (used in industry, preservation)
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔtər ˌɡlæs / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ˌɡlɑːs / (BrE)
a clear, viscous solution of sodium silicate, used as a cement, protective coating, or for preserving eggs

Examples

Water glass cement
Water glass is often used as a cement in the production of acid-resistant materials.
The eggs were coated with water glass to preserve them for several months.
The scientist used water glass to create a protective barrier on the metal surface.
