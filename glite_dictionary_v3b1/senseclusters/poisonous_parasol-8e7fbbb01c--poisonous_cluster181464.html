poisonous parasol

noun
poisonous mushroom with a parasol-shaped cap
Pronunciation: / ˈpɔɪzənəs ˈpærəˌsɑl / (AmE), / ˈpɔɪzənəs ˈpærəsɒl / (BrE)
a species of poisonous agaric mushroom with a parasol-shaped cap, commonly found in Europe and North America

Examples

A poisonous parasol mushroom
After the rain, several poisonous parasols appeared on the forest floor among the fallen leaves.
Experts warn that eating the poisonous parasol can cause severe illness or even death.
The poisonous parasol can easily be mistaken for edible mushrooms by amateur foragers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: agaric - a type of mushroom with gills under the cap
