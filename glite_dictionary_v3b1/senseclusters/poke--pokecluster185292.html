poke

verb
search or inquire in a meddlesome way
4.2
Pronunciation: / poʊk / (AmE), / pəʊk / (BrE)
to search or inquire in a meddlesome or intrusive way, often where one's presence or questions are not wanted

Examples

To poke into business
I don't appreciate it when people poke into my private affairs.
Reporters often poke into celebrities' personal lives.
She was poking around the files, looking for something she shouldn't see.

Related Terms

  • synonym: meddle - to interfere in something that is not one's concern
  • synonym: nose around - to search or look around inquisitively
