overachieve

verb
achieve more than expected
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərəˈtʃiv / (AmE), / ˌəʊvərəˈtʃiːv / (BrE)
to perform better or achieve a greater degree of success than was expected

Examples

To overachieve at work
If you overachieve in this project, you might receive a special award from your manager.
She tends to overachieve, constantly exceeding the goals she sets for herself.
Some students feel pressured to overachieve in both academics and extracurricular activities.

Related Terms

  • antonym: underachieve - to perform less well than expected
