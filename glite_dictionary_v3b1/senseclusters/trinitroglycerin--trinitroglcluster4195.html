trinitroglycerin

noun
explosive and medicinal chemical compound
Pronunciation: / ˌtraɪˌnaɪtroʊˈɡlɪsərɪn / (AmE), / ˌtraɪˌnʌɪtrəʊˈɡlɪsərɪn / (BrE)
A heavy yellow, poisonous, oily, and explosive liquid obtained by nitrating glycerol, used in explosives and as a vasodilator in medicine.
