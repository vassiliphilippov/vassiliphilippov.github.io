nonjury

noun
trial without jury
6.8
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˈdʒʊri / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˈdʒʊəri / (BrE)
a trial or hearing in which the decision is made by a judge rather than a jury

Examples

A nonjury hearing
The case was heard as a nonjury to ensure a quicker resolution.
In some civil matters, a nonjury is preferred for its efficiency.
The defendant requested a nonjury because of concerns about impartiality.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bench trial - a trial decided by a judge without a jury
  • antonym: jury trial - a trial in which a jury decides the outcome
