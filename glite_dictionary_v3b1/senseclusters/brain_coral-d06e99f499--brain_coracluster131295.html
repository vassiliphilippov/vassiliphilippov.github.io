brain coral

noun
type of convoluted reef-building coral
Pronunciation: / ˈbreɪn ˌkɔɹ.əl / (AmE), / ˈbreɪn ˌkɔː.rəl / (BrE)
A massive reef-building coral with a convoluted and furrowed surface that resembles a human brain.

Examples

A brain coral colony
A large colony of brain coral can provide shelter for many types of marine life.
Marine biologists are studying how climate change affects brain coral reproduction.
The divers marveled at the intricate patterns of the brain coral on the ocean floor.
