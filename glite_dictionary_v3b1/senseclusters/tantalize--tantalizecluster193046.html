tantalize

verb
to tease with desire
Pronunciation: / ˈtæntəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈtæntəlaɪz / (BrE)
to tease or tempt someone by showing or offering something desirable but keeping it out of reach

Examples

To tantalize with food
The aroma of freshly baked cookies would tantalize anyone walking past the kitchen.
She tantalized her little brother by holding the toy just out of his reach.
The advertisement tantalized viewers with glimpses of the new product but revealed no details.
The aroma of freshly baked cookies would tantalize anyone walking past the kitchen.
She used the promise of a reward to tantalize the children into finishing their homework.
The display of luxury cars in the showroom seemed to tantalize those who could not afford them.

Related Terms

  • antonym: satisfy - to fulfill a desire or need
  • synonym: tease - to make fun of or provoke in a playful way
  • synonym: tempt - to try to attract or persuade someone to do something
  • synonym: torment - to cause someone to experience severe mental or physical suffering
