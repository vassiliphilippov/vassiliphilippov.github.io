circulator

noun
signal-directing device
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈsɜːrkjəˌleɪtər / (AmE), / ˈsɜːkjʊleɪtə / (BrE)
an electronic device that directs high-frequency signals to flow in a specific direction between its ports

Examples

Microwave circulator
The technician used a circulator to control the direction of the microwave signals in the circuit.
A circulator is essential in many radio frequency systems to prevent signal interference.
Engineers often install a circulator between the transmitter and receiver to manage signal flow.

Related Terms

  • synonym: isolator - a device that allows signals to pass in one direction but blocks them in the opposite direction
