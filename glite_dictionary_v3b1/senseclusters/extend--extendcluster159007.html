extend

verb
increase quantity by adding substance
4.9 4.2
Pronunciation: /ɪk.ˈstend/ /; / /ek.ˈstend/ (AmE), /ek.ˈstend/ /; / /ɪk.ˈstend/ (BrE)
to increase the quantity or bulk of something by adding a cheaper or supplementary substance, often to make it go further

Examples

To extend a meal
In hard times, families may extend their meals by adding inexpensive ingredients.
She tried to extend the stew with extra potatoes to feed everyone.
The chef will often extend sauces with cream or stock to serve larger crowds.
