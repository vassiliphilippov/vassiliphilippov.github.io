hypocellularity

noun
state of having abnormally few cells
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪ.poʊˌsɛl.juˈlɛr.ə.ti / (AmE), / ˌhaɪ.pəʊˌsɛl.jʊˈlær.ɪ.ti / (BrE)
the condition of having an abnormally low number of cells in a tissue or organ

Examples

Low hypocellularity level
Doctors are concerned about hypocellularity when assessing patients with unexplained anemia.
Radiation exposure can sometimes lead to hypocellularity in affected tissues.
The bone marrow biopsy revealed hypocellularity, suggesting a possible bone marrow failure.
