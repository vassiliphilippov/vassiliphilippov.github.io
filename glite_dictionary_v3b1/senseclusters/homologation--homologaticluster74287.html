homologation

noun
chemical series extension
9.9
Pronunciation: /hɑː.mə.lə.ˈgeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /hoʊ.mə.lə.ˈgeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /hɒ.mə.lə.ˈgeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /hə.mə.lə.ˈgeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
a chemical process that converts a compound into the next member of a homologous series, usually by adding a repeating unit such as a methylene group

Examples

Chemical homologation process
The chemist used homologation to increase the length of the carbon chain in the compound.
A successful homologation reaction allowed the researchers to synthesize a new series of related molecules.
In organic synthesis, homologation is often employed to modify the properties of a substance.
