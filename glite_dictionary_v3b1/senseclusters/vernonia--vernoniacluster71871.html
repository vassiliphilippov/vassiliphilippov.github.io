vernonia

noun
genus of flowering plants with purple or rose heads
Pronunciation: / vərˈnoʊniə / (AmE), / vɜːˈnəʊniə / (BrE)
Any of various plants of the genus Vernonia, found in tropical and warm regions, especially in North America, with loose heads of purple to rose flowers that turn rusty as they age.

Examples

The vernonia plant
As autumn approached, the heads of the vernonia plants turned a rusty shade.
Many gardeners choose vernonia for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
The vernonia in the meadow bloomed with striking purple flowers each spring.
