scleroderma

noun
autoimmune disease causing skin hardening
9.9 0.12/m
Pronunciation: / skl…õr.…ôÀàd…ùÀê.m…ô / (AmE), / skl…õr.…ôÀàd…úÀê.m…ô / (BrE)
A chronic autoimmune disease in which hard, fibrous connective tissue is deposited in the skin and sometimes other organs, leading to thickening and hardening of affected areas.

Examples

Chronic scleroderma disease
Living with scleroderma can be challenging due to its impact on everyday activities.
Researchers are studying treatments to alleviate symptoms of scleroderma.
The patient was diagnosed with scleroderma after noticing thickening of the skin on her hands.

Synonyms

  • dermatosclerosis
    9.9 0.00/m
    dermatosclerosis affects mainly the skin, causing local thickening and hardening, while scleroderma often also involves internal organs.
