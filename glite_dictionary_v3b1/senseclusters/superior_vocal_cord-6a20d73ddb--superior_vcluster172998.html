superior vocal cord

noun
upper, non-sound-producing vocal fold

Pronunciation: / s…ôÀàp…™ri…ö Ààvo äkl k…îrd / (AmE), / suÀêÀàp…™…ôri…ô Ààv…ô äk…ôl k…îÀêd / (BrE)

Examples

The superior vocal cord
During surgery, the superior vocal cord is identified to avoid damaging important structures.
Inflammation of the superior vocal cord can sometimes be mistaken for other throat conditions.
The superior vocal cord is also called the false vocal cord.

Synonyms

  • false vocal cord
    7.8 0.00/m
    false vocal cord is the common everyday name used in medicine and speech, while superior vocal cord is the anatomical term.
  • vestibular fold
    8.5 0.01/m
    vestibular fold is the anatomical name used in textbooks and lectures, while superior vocal cord is a more general label.
  • ventricular fold
    9.5 0.02/m
    ventricular fold names the upper tissue above the true cords and is not usually active in voice, while superior vocal cord refers to the same structure.
