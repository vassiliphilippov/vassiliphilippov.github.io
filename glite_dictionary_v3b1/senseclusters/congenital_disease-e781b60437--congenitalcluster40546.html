congenital disease

noun
disease present from birth
1.0 3.5
Pronunciation: /kən.ˈdʒen.ɪ.təl dɪz.ˈiːz/ /; / /kən.ˈdʒe.nə.təl dɪz.ɪz/ (AmE), /kən.ˈdʒen.ɪ.təl dɪz.ˈiːz/ /; / /kən.ˈdʒe.nə.təl dɪz.ˈiːz/ (BrE)
a disease or disorder that is present from birth, often inherited genetically or caused by environmental factors during gestation

Examples

A congenital disease case
Genetic counseling can help families understand the risks of passing on a congenital disease.
Research into congenital diseases aims to uncover the genetic mutations responsible for these conditions.
The child was diagnosed with a congenital disease that required early intervention.

Related Terms

  • antonym: acquired disease - a disease developed after birth, not present from birth
  • synonym: hereditary disease - a disease passed from parents to offspring genetically
