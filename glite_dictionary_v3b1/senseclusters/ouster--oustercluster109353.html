ouster

noun
wrongful dispossession
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˈaʊstər / (AmE), / ˈaʊstə / (BrE)
the act of removing someone from a position, place, property, or rights, especially by force or legal action

Examples

Court ouster
Legal proceedings for ouster may result in the rightful owner regaining possession.
She claimed ouster after being excluded from property she jointly owned.
The landlord's attempt at ouster was challenged in court by the tenant.
After months of political tension, the parliament voted in favor of her ouster.
The board's sudden ouster of the CEO shocked the entire industry.
The lawsuit ended with the ouster of the tenant from the property.
