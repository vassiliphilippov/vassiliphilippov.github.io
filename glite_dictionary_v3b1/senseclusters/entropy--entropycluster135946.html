entropy

noun
measure of unavailable energy; disorder in thermodynamics
6.4 7.5
Pronunciation: /ˈen.trə.piː/ /; / /ˈen.trə.pɪ/ (AmE), /ˈen.trə.piː/ /; / /ˈen.trə.pɪ/ (BrE)
a measure of the unavailable energy in a thermodynamic system, often interpreted as the degree of disorder or randomness

Examples

Thermodynamic entropy
According to the second law of thermodynamics, entropy in a closed system will always increase over time.
As ice melts, the entropy of the water molecules increases due to greater movement.
Physicists study how energy becomes less available for work as entropy rises in a process.

Related Terms

  • synonym: disorder - state of confusion or lack of order, related to increasing entropy
  • antonym: order - organized or structured arrangement, in contrast to randomness or disorder
