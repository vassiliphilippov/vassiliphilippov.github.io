gallant

adjective
lively and spirited; dashing
Pronunciation: / ɡəˈlænt /; / ˈɡæl.ənt / (AmE), / ˈɡæl.ənt / (BrE)
brave, lively, or spirited, especially in a bold or courageous way

Examples

Gallant rescue
She admired his gallant attitude at the party, always energetic and full of charm.
The gallant young officer led his troops bravely into battle.
With a gallant leap, the performer stole the spotlight and delighted the crowd.
She made a gallant effort to finish the race despite her injury.
The gallant firefighters risked their lives to save people from the burning building.
The gallant soldiers held their position against overwhelming odds.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cowardly - lacking courage or spirit; opposite of gallant
  • synonym: dashing - lively, spirited, and stylish
  • synonym: valiant - showing courage or determination
