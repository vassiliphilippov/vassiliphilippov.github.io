Herschelian telescope

noun
reflecting telescope with a tilted mirror
3.9 8.5
Pronunciation: /hɜːr.ˈʃe.liː.ən ˈtel.ə.skoʊp/ /; / /hɜːr.ˈʃe.liː.ən ˈtel.ɪ.skoʊp/ (AmE), /hɜː.ˈʃe.liː.ən ˈtel.ɪ.skəʊp/ /; / /hɜː.ˈʃe.liː.ən ˈtel.ə.skəʊp/ (BrE)
A type of reflecting telescope with the mirror slightly tilted to direct the image to the side for viewing, eliminating the need for a secondary mirror.

Examples

The Herschelian telescope design
The Herschelian telescope was an innovative design that allowed astronomers to view celestial objects without a secondary mirror.
Unlike modern reflectors, the Herschelian telescope uses a tilted mirror to send the image to the side.
William Herschel built a massive Herschelian telescope to observe new stars and nebulae in the 18th century.
