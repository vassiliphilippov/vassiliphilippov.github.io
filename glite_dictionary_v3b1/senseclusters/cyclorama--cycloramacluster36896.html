cyclorama

noun
continuous panoramic picture
Pronunciation: / ˌsaɪ.kləˈrɑː.mə / (AmE), / ˌsɪ.kləˈrɑː.mə / (BrE)
a large picture or series of pictures arranged on a cylindrical surface to represent a continuous scene, often used as a backdrop in theaters or exhibitions.

Examples

A large cyclorama painting
The artist spent months completing the cyclorama for the historical exhibition.
The museum's cyclorama depicts a famous battlefield in stunning detail.
They used a painted cyclorama to create the illusion of an endless sky on stage.
