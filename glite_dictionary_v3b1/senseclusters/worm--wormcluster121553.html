worm

noun
small elongated limbless animal
2.1 1.0
Pronunciation: /wɜːrm/ (AmE), /wɜːm/ (BrE)
a small, elongated, soft-bodied invertebrate animal, typically lacking limbs and often living in soil or water; also used for some insect larvae

Examples

Earth worm
Birds often eat worms after a rainstorm when they come to the surface.
Some types of worms, like earthworms, help improve the quality of the soil.
While digging in the garden, she found a worm wriggling through the soil.
