word-worship

noun
excessive reverence for words
6.2
Pronunciation: / wɝːd ˈwɜrʃɪp / (AmE), / wɜːd ˈwɜːʃɪp / (BrE)
Excessive reverence for, or reliance on, words, especially at the expense of meaning or substance.

Examples

Excessive word-worship
Her essay suffered from word-worship, focusing more on impressive language than genuine ideas.
Many critics argue that modern academia is plagued by word-worship rather than meaningful engagement.
The philosopher warned against word-worship and urged his students to seek substance over style.

Related Terms

  • synonym: verbalism - excessive emphasis on words or verbal expression
