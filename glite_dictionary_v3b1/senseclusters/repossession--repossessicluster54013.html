repossession

noun
taking back property by a lender
5.2 5.4
Pronunciation: / ˌɹiːpəˈzɛʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːpɒˈzɛʃən / (BrE)
the action of taking back property, especially by a lender, when a borrower fails to keep up with payments on a loan

Examples

Car repossession
Facing repossession, the couple tried to negotiate a new payment plan.
If you fall behind on your mortgage, your home may be at risk of repossession.
The bank began repossession of the car after several missed payments.

Related Terms

  • synonym: foreclosure - legal process in which a lender takes possession of a mortgaged property due to nonpayment
  • synonym: seizure - the act of taking something by force or legal right
