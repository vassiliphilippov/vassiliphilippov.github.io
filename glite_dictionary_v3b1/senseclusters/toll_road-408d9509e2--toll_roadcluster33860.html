toll road

noun
road requiring a fee for use
4.0
Pronunciation: / ˈtoʊl ˌroʊd / (AmE), / ˈtəʊl ˌrəʊd / (BrE)
A road, especially a main highway, on which a fee is charged for use.

Examples

A busy toll road
After the storm, the toll road was closed for emergency repairs.
The new toll road significantly reduced travel time between the two towns.
We had to pay a fee to drive on the toll road to the city.

Related Terms

  • antonym: freeway - a major road without tolls, free for public use
  • synonym: turnpike - a major road where a toll is charged for passage
