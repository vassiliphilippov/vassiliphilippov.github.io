synapsid

noun
extinct reptile-like animal with skull openings
7.9
Pronunciation: / sɪˈnæp.sɪd / (AmE), / sɪˈnap.sɪd / (BrE)
an extinct reptile-like animal characterized by a single pair of lateral openings in the skull, including ancestors of mammals

Examples

Early synapsid ancestors
Scientists believe that mammals evolved from early synapsid ancestors millions of years ago.
The characteristic skull opening distinguishes a synapsid from other prehistoric reptiles.
The fossilized remains of a synapsid were discovered in the South African desert.
