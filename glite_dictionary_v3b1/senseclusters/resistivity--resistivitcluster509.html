resistivity

noun
electrical resistance of a material
Pronunciation: / ˌrɪzɪˈstɪvəti / (AmE), / ˌrɛzɪˈstɪvɪti / (BrE)
A measure of how strongly a material opposes the flow of electric current, expressed in ohm-meters.

Examples

Material resistivity value
Copper has a very low resistivity, making it ideal for electrical wiring.
Scientists measured the resistivity of the rock samples in the laboratory.
The resistivity of the material changes with temperature.

Related Terms

  • antonym: conductivity - the ability of a material to allow the flow of electric current
