scream

verb
utter a loud, piercing cry
1.0 1.0
Pronunciation: /skriːm/ /; / /skrim/ (AmE), /skriːm/ (BrE)
to make a loud, sharp, high-pitched cry or sound, especially because of strong emotion

Examples

To scream for help
He screamed for help when he got trapped in the elevator.
She couldn't help but scream when she saw the spider on her pillow.
The crowd began to scream in excitement as the band took the stage.
He screamed his name at the top of his lungs, hoping someone would recognize him.
Please don’t scream your answer; we can all hear you just fine.
She had to scream over the noise of the crowd to be heard.
Children screamed with excitement when the roller coaster started moving.
The engines screamed as the race cars shot forward at the start of the race.
The siren began to scream as the fire truck raced down the street.
