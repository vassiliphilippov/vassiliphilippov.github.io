sclerotization

noun
formation of sclerotia
9.9
Pronunciation: /sklɪ.rə.tə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /sklɪə.rə.tə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /sklɪə.rə.tə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /sklɪ.rə.tə.ˈzeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
the process by which certain fungi form sclerotia, which are hard, resistant resting structures for survival in adverse conditions

Examples

Sclerotization in fungi
During periods of drought, the fungus undergoes sclerotization to protect itself until conditions improve.
Researchers observed the sclerotization process in several species of soil fungi under laboratory conditions.
The ability for rapid sclerotization helps some fungi survive in harsh environments.
