weightlessness

noun
absence of gravity
4.9
Pronunciation: /ˈweɪt.ləs.nəs/ (AmE), /ˈweɪt.ləs.nəs/ (BrE)
the state in which a person or object appears to have no weight because the effects of gravity are not felt

Examples

Space weightlessness
Astronauts train for weightlessness before traveling to space.
The feeling of weightlessness can be disorienting for people who are not used to it.
During the flight, the passengers experienced brief moments of weightlessness as the plane descended rapidly.

Related Terms

  • antonym: gravity - the force that attracts objects toward the center of the earth
  • synonym: zero gravity - a condition in which there is no apparent force of gravity
