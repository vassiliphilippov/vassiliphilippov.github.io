grub

noun
larva of beetles or other insects
6.6 5.0
Pronunciation: /grʌb/ (AmE), /grʌb/ (BrE)
the soft, thick, wormlike larva of certain beetles and some other insects, especially as found living underground or inside decaying wood

Examples

A fat grub
Birds often feed on grub larvae hidden in the soil.
The wood was infested with grubs eating away at the inside.
We found a large grub under the log in the garden.

Related Terms

  • antonym: adult - the mature form of an insect after metamorphosis
  • synonym: larva - an immature form of an insect that does not look like the adult form
