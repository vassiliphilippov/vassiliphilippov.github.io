secondary censorship

noun
military censorship of personal communications
6.0 7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈsek.ən.dər.iː ˈsen.sər.ɪp/ /; / /ˈsek.ən.dər.ɪ ˈsen.sər.ɪp/ (AmE), /ˈsek.ən.dər.iː ˈsen.sə.ʃɪp/ /; / /ˈsek.ən.dər.ɪ ˈsen.sə.ʃɪp/ (BrE)
the examination and control of personal communications of officers, civilian employees, or enlisted personnel by armed forces, where these individuals are not subject to primary censorship

Examples

Secondary censorship of letters
All letters sent by the soldiers underwent secondary censorship to ensure sensitive information was not disclosed.
During wartime, secondary censorship was applied to personal phone calls made by enlisted personnel.
The military imposed secondary censorship to monitor communications of civilian employees stationed overseas.
