keen-sighted

adjective
having very sharp vision
Pronunciation: / ˈkin ˌsaɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˈkiːn ˌsaɪtɪd / (BrE)
having very sharp or excellent eyesight; able to see extremely well or to a great distance

Examples

A keen-sighted person
He is so keen-sighted that he can read road signs from half a mile away.
Thanks to her keen-sighted nature, she quickly noticed the subtle details others missed.
The keen-sighted hawk spotted its prey from high in the sky.
Even from a distance, her keen-sighted grandfather could spot tiny details that others missed.
Guides in the desert are often keen-sighted, able to notice wildlife far away.
The keen-sighted hawk circled high above the fields, searching for prey.

Related Terms

  • synonym: sharp-sighted - having very acute vision
  • antonym: short-sighted - unable to see distant objects clearly
