self-styled

adjective
claiming a title or status for oneself
5.3
Pronunciation: / sɛlf ˈstaɪld / (AmE), / sɛlf ˈstaɪld / (BrE)
Describing oneself as having a particular title, role, or status, especially without official or accepted authority.

Examples

A self-styled expert
He introduced himself as a self-styled entrepreneur, but nobody had heard of his company.
Several self-styled experts weighed in on the topic during the panel discussion.
The self-styled guru attracted a loyal following despite lacking formal training.

Related Terms

  • synonym: so-called - commonly called or referred to, often with skepticism to legitimacy
