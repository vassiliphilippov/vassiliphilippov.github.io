antinomasia

noun
substitution of a title for a name
8.0 9.9
Pronunciation: /æn.tɪ.nə.ˈmeɪ.ʒə/ /; / /æn.tɪ.nə.ˈmeɪ.sɪ.ə/ (AmE), /æn.tɪ.nə.ˈmeɪ.ʒə/ /; / /æn.tɪ.nə.ˈmeɪ.sɪ.ə/ (BrE)
The substitution of a descriptive phrase, title, or epithet in place of a proper name, especially as a rhetorical device.

Examples

Antinomasia in speech
Calling a famous leader 'the Iron Lady' is an example of antinomasia.
In classical literature, antinomasia is frequently used to stress a person's qualities.
Using antinomasia, people often refer to Shakespeare as 'the Bard'.
