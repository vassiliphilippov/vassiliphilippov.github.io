inflationist

noun
proponent of inflation
7.6
Pronunciation: /ɪn.ˈfleɪ.ʃən.ɪst/ (AmE), /ɪn.ˈfleɪ.ʃən.ɪst/ (BrE)
a person who supports or advocates policies that encourage inflation in an economy

Examples

An inflationist economist
The new government appointed an inflationist as the head of the central bank.
Critics argued that the inflationist approach could lead to higher prices for consumers.
During the debate, she defended her inflationist views on monetary policy.
The finance minister was called an inflationist after proposing large increases in government spending.
Critics argue that an inflationist approach can harm the economy in the long run.
Some economists identify as inflationists because they believe higher prices can stimulate growth.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deflationist - a person who supports policies that reduce the amount of money in circulation
  • synonym: expansionist - a person who favors increasing the supply of money or credit
