blow tube

noun
tube for directing air/gas into a flame
Pronunciation: / bloʊ tuːb / (AmE), / bləʊ tjuːb / (BrE)
a tube through which air or gas is blown into a flame to increase its heat or intensity, especially in scientific or glassworking applications

Examples

Glassworker's blow tube
A blow tube is essential in some chemistry experiments to obtain a hotter flame.
She used a blow tube to direct oxygen into the flame and shape the delicate ornament.
The glassblower carefully inserted the blow tube into the furnace to heat the molten glass.
