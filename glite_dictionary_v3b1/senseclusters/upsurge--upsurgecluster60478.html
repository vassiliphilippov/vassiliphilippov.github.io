upsurge

noun
sudden strong increase
Pronunciation: / ˈʌpˌsɝdʒ / (AmE), / ˈʌpˌsɜːdʒ / (BrE)
a sudden and strong increase or rise in something

Examples

An upsurge of excitement
After the announcement, an upsurge of excitement swept through the crowd.
The country experienced an upsurge of interest in renewable energy.
There has been an upsurge in online shopping during the holiday season.
An unexpected upsurge of anger swept through the crowd.
Scientists reported an upsurge in COVID-19 cases during the winter.
There was an upsurge in unemployment after the factory closed.

Related Terms

  • antonym: decline - a gradual and continuous loss or decrease
  • synonym: surge - a sudden powerful forward or upward movement
