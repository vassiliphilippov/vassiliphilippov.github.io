to give pleasure or satisfaction to someone; to please or satisfy

Examples

To gratify a person
He was gratified by the public's enthusiastic response to his work.
It would gratify me if you accepted my invitation.
The gift seemed to gratify her more than I expected.
Her success clearly gratified her parents, who had supported her from the beginning.
It would gratify me to know that my efforts made a difference in your life.
The company tried to gratify customer demands by improving its services.

Synonyms

  • please
    1.0 11/m
    please is a common, simple word for making someone happy or satisfied, while gratify is more formal and often means fulfilling a wish.
  • satisfy
    2.0 12/m
    satisfy means meeting someone's needs or expectations, while gratify often implies giving personal pleasure by meeting a wish.
  • gladden
    3.5 0.13/m
    gladden means causing a quick feeling of joy or gladness, while gratify means giving pleasure by meeting desires or needs.
  • indulge
    4.3 5.85/m
    indulge means allowing oneself or someone to enjoy something often to excess, while gratify means pleasing or satisfying someone.

  • antonym: dissatisfy - to fail to satisfy or please
