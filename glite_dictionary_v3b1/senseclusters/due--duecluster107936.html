due

adjective
caused by; attributable to
3.1 120/m
Pronunciation: / du / (AmE), / djuÀê / (BrE)
(of an event or result) caused by or attributable to something or someone.

Examples

Due to illness
His illness is due to overwork and stress.
The cancellation was due to unforeseen circumstances.
The flight was delayed due to heavy fog.

Video Examples

Synonyms

  • attributable
    5.4 5/m
    attributable is used to say a result comes from a clear cause in reports and studies, while due is more general.
  • ascribable
    6.9 0.03/m
    ascribable often means you can assign a cause in formal writing, while due is more common and less formal.
  • imputable
    8.6 0.01/m
    imputable often shows blame or legal responsibility for an action, while due is neutral and links a result to its cause.
  • referable
    7.1 0.02/m
    referable says something can be traced to a source, while due specifically means an event is caused by something.
