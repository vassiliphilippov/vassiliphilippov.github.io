second wind

noun
renewed energy after fatigue
7.2 3.5
Pronunciation: /ˈsek.ənd wɪnd/ /; / /ˈsek.ənd waɪnd/ (AmE), /ˈsek.ənd wɪnd/ /; / /ˈsek.ənd waɪnd/ (BrE)
a feeling of renewed energy or strength that allows you to continue after being tired, especially during exercise or activity

Examples

Get a second wind
After a short break, she got her second wind and finished the marathon strongly.
He felt exhausted at first, but a second wind came over him after the halfway mark.
Just when I thought I was too tired to study, I caught a second wind around midnight.
After struggling through the first few miles, she suddenly got her second wind and finished the race strongly.
I thought I was too tired to continue, but after a short break, I found my second wind.
Many marathon runners rely on a second wind to keep going during the last leg of the race.

  • synonym: resurgence - a rising again into life, activity, or prominence
