to withdraw or break away from an organization, association, or group, especially as a result of disagreement

Examples

To splinter from a group
Disagreements over policy led some activists to splinter off and form a rival group.
Several members decided to splinter from the main party and form a new alliance.
The church splintered into several factions after the controversial decision.

Synonyms

  • break away
    4.1 2.03/m
    break away often means a group leaves to form its own smaller faction after strong disagreement, while splinter refers to this split.
  • disaffiliate
    7.5 0.10/m
    disaffiliate means officially ending membership with an organization, often by formal vote or notice, while splinter implies leaving to form a new group.
  • dissociate
    6.7 0.47/m
    dissociate means to cut ties or distance oneself from people or ideas, often quietly, while splinter means leaving a group to form another.
  • withdraw
    2.8 26/m
    withdraw means to pull back or stop taking part, often quietly or temporarily, while splinter implies leaving to create a new faction.
  • pull out of
    3.2 2.99/m
    pull out of is used when someone stops taking part in an activity or agreement, often without forming a new group, while splinter means breaking away from an organization.
  • separate
    2.9 25/m
    separate means to move apart or end a connection in many contexts, often physical or personal, while splinter refers to leaving an organization after disagreement.
