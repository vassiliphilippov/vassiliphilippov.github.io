overspin

noun
excessive spin
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊvərˌspɪn / (AmE), / ˈəʊvəˌspɪn / (BrE)
a condition in which an object, especially a ball, spins more than is usual or intended, often affecting its movement

Examples

Tennis ball overspin
The tennis ball had too much overspin and bounced unpredictably.
Golfers try to control overspin to keep the ball from rolling too far on the green.
In table tennis, excessive overspin can make the ball difficult for opponents to return.

Related Terms

  • synonym: spin - the rapid turning or rotation of an object
  • antonym: underspin - a spin applied to a ball in the opposite direction, usually less than normal
