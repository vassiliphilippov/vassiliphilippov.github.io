lineup

noun
police identification procedure
Pronunciation: / ˈlaɪ.nʌp /; / ˈlaɪnˌʌp / (AmE), / ˈlaɪ.nʌp /; / ˈlaɪnʌp / (BrE)
a group of people arranged in a line, especially by police for identification by a witness

Examples

Police lineup
After the robbery, the police conducted a lineup to help find the perpetrator.
Five men stood in the lineup, waiting to be identified by the victim.
The witness picked the suspect out of the lineup at the police station.
The police asked the witness to identify the suspect from the lineup.
This year's concert lineup includes several internationally known bands.
The coach announced the starting lineup before the game began.
