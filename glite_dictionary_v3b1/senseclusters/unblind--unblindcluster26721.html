unblind

verb
to remove a blindfold
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈblaɪnd / (AmE), / ʌnˈblaɪnd / (BrE)
to remove a blindfold or covering from someone's eyes so they can see again

Examples

To unblind a person
After the experiment, the scientist unblinded the participant so she could see the results.
The children cheered when the teacher unblinded their classmate during the game.
He promised to unblind her as soon as the surprise was ready.
After the game, the teacher unblinded each student so they could see the surprise decorations.
The nurse gently unblinded the patient after the eye test was finished.
He unblinded his friend, revealing the beautiful view outside the window.

Related Terms

  • antonym: blindfold - to cover someone's eyes so they cannot see
  • synonym: uncover - to remove a cover from something
