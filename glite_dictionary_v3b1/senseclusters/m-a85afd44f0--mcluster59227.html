M

noun
unit for molarity (concentration)
6.0 2.73/m
Pronunciation: / É›m / (AmE), / É›m / (BrE)
a unit of concentration, equal to one mole of solute per liter of solution; used to express molarity in chemistry

Examples

1 M solution
A 1 M NaCl solution contains one mole of sodium chloride per liter.
Chemists often use M to indicate molarity in their calculations.
The solution was prepared to a final concentration of 0.5 M.
