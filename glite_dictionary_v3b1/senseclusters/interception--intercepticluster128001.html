interception

noun
catching an opponent's pass in football
4.2 4.2
Pronunciation: / ˌɪntərˈsɛpʃən / (AmE), / ˌɪntəˈsɛpʃən / (BrE)
(in American football) the act of catching a forward pass by a player on the opposing team, resulting in a change of possession.

Examples

A football interception
He celebrated wildly after his first career interception in the NFL.
The cornerback made a crucial interception late in the fourth quarter.
Throwing an interception can drastically change the momentum of the game.
