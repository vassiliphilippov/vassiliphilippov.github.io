anagram

verb
rearrange letters to discover a hidden meaning
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈænəˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈænəɡræm / (BrE)
to rearrange the letters of a word, phrase, or text to form a new word or phrase, often to discover a hidden meaning

Examples

To anagram a name
He anagrammed the author’s name to create a clever pseudonym.
She tried to anagram the word ‘listen’ and found ‘silent’.
The puzzle required us to anagram each clue before finding the answer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: unscramble - to rearrange something so that it is no longer confused or jumbled
