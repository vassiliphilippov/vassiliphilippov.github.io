italicize

verb
print or write in italics
Pronunciation: / ɪˈtæləˌsaɪz /; / ɪˈtælɪˌsaɪz /; / ɪˈtælɪsaɪz / (AmE), / ɪˈtælɪsaɪz / (BrE)
to print or write text in italics (slanted letters), often to emphasize words or for formatting

Examples

To italicize text
Please italicize the book titles in your essay.
She italicized the important points for emphasis.
The publisher decided to italicize all foreign words in the manuscript.
Please italicize any technical terms the first time you use them.
Writers often italicize foreign words to distinguish them from the main text.
You should italicize book titles in your bibliography.
Please italicise the book titles in your essay.
She italicised the key points to make them stand out.
The author chose to italicise the foreign words for emphasis.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emphasize - to give special importance to something
  • synonym: italicize - to print in italics (American spelling)
  • antonym: romanize - to print in ordinary roman type, as opposed to italics
