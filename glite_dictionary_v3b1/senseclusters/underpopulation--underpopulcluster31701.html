underpopulation

noun
very low population
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˌpɑpjəˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˌpɒpjʊˈleɪʃən / (BrE)
a situation where the number of individuals in a population is so low that it threatens survival or genetic diversity

Examples

Animal underpopulation
The underpopulation of certain animal species can lead to a loss of genetic diversity.
Rural areas sometimes face underpopulation as young people move to cities for work.
Conservationists are concerned about the underpopulation of endangered birds in the region.

Related Terms

  • synonym: depopulation - a reduction in the number of people or animals in an area
  • antonym: overpopulation - a situation where the number of individuals exceeds the environment's capacity
