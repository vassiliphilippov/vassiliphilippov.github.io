farinha

noun
cassava flour
Pronunciation: / fəˈriːnjə / (AmE), / fəˈriːnjə / (BrE)
a coarse flour made from cassava, commonly used in Brazilian and Portuguese cooking

Examples

Brazilian farinha side dish
In Brazil, people often serve farinha as a side dish with grilled meats.
The recipe calls for a cup of farinha to give the stew a unique texture.
She bought fresh farinha at the market to prepare a traditional meal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: cassava flour - flour made from the root of the cassava plant
  • synonym: manioc - another name for cassava, the plant used to make farinha
