one-time

adjective
former; in the past
4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈwʌn.taɪm / (AmE), / ˈwʌn.taɪm / (BrE)
(of a person or thing) having been something in the past; former.

Examples

One-time leader
He is a one-time champion who now mentors young athletes.
The property was the one-time residence of a famous writer.
Their one-time friendship ended years ago.

Related Terms

  • synonym: former - having previously filled a particular role or position
