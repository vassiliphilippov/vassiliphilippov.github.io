dibasic salt

noun
salt with two acid hydrogens replaced
Pronunciation: / daɪˈbeɪsɪk sɔlt / (AmE), / daɪˈbeɪsɪk sɒlt / (BrE)
a salt formed by replacing two hydrogen atoms in an acid molecule with metal ions or other radicals

Examples

A dibasic salt compound
Chemists often classify salts as monobasic, dibasic salt, or tribasic based on the number of replaced hydrogens.
Sodium carbonate is an example of a dibasic salt used in water softening.
When two hydrogens in sulfuric acid are replaced by metals, a dibasic salt is formed.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: monobasic salt - a salt formed by replacing one hydrogen atom in an acid molecule
  • degree higher: tribasic salt - a salt formed by replacing three hydrogen atoms in an acid molecule
