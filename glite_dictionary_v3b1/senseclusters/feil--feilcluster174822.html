feil

noun
turf or sod (Scotland)
Pronunciation: / fiːl / (BrE)
an old or dialect word for a piece of turf or sod, especially used in Scotland

Examples

A feil of turf
The villagers cut feil from the moor to use as fuel during the winter.
He carefully laid each feil to build a sturdy wall for the garden.
In old Scottish traditions, a stack of feil was a common sight near cottages.

Related Terms

  • synonym: peat - partially decayed plant matter used as fuel, often cut from bogs
  • synonym: sod - a piece of grass-covered soil held together by roots
