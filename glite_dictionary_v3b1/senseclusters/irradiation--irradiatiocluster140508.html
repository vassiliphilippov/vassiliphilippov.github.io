irradiation

noun
apparent size increase of a bright object on a dark background
9.9 8.6
Pronunciation: / ɪˌreɪdiˈeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌrædɪˈeɪʃən / (BrE)
the apparent enlargement of a bright object when viewed against a dark background, due to a visual perception effect

Examples

Irradiation of a light spot
Due to irradiation, a bright light can seem to spread into the surrounding darkness.
Scientists have studied irradiation to understand how brightness and contrast affect visual perception.
The irradiation of the white disk made it appear larger than it actually was.
