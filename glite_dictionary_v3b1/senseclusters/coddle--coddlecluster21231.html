coddle

verb
treat with excessive indulgence
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑd.əl / (AmE), / ˈkɒd.əl / (BrE)
to treat someone with excessive care or indulgence, often to the point of spoiling them

Examples

To coddle a child
He was coddled by his parents all through his childhood.
Some managers believe that too much praise coddles employees and reduces their motivation.
You shouldn't coddle your children if you want them to become independent.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mollycoddle - to treat someone in an overprotective way
  • synonym: pamper - to treat with excessive care or attention
