Anthropocene

noun
human-influenced epoch
9.7
Pronunciation: /ˈæn.θrə.pə.siːn/ /; / /ˈæn.θrɑː.pə.siːn/ (AmE), /ˈæn.θrə.pə.siːn/ (BrE)
the proposed geological time period when human activity has become the main influence on Earth's climate and environment

Examples

The Anthropocene epoch
Many scientists argue that the Anthropocene began with the Industrial Revolution.
The concept of the Anthropocene highlights the scale of human influence on the planet.
Debates continue about whether the Anthropocene should be officially recognized as a new epoch.
Many scientists debate when the Anthropocene began and how it should be defined.
The museum's new exhibit explores the impact of the Anthropocene on biodiversity.
Writers and artists are increasingly inspired by the challenges of the Anthropocene.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Holocene - the current geological epoch before the Anthropocene
  • synonym: epoch - a period of time in Earth's history marked by notable events
