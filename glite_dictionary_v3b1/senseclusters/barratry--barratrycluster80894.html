barratry

noun
fraud by a ship's captain or crew
Pronunciation: / ˈbær.əˌtri / (AmE), / ˈbær.ə.tri / (BrE)
(in maritime law) fraudulent breach of duty by the master or crew of a ship resulting in injury to the owner or cargo, such as theft, intentional sinking, abandonment, or embezzlement.

Examples

Ship barratry fraud
Acts of barratry can include intentionally damaging the ship or stealing its cargo.
Maritime insurance policies often exclude losses resulting from barratry.
The insurer refused to pay due to evidence of barratry by the ship's captain.
