fringe

noun
marginal or extreme social group
Pronunciation: / frɪndʒ / (AmE), / frɪndʒ / (BrE)
a group of people whose opinions or beliefs are considered extreme or not accepted by most others; the marginal, radical, or non-mainstream sector of a society or movement

Examples

Fringe group
Some politicians cater to the fringe in order to gain attention from the media.
The party's fringe members protested loudly against the new policy.
While the majority supported the plan, a vocal fringe opposed it vehemently.
