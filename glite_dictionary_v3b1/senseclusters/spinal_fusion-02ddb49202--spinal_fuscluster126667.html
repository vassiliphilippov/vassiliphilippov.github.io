spinal fusion

noun
surgical joining of spinal vertebrae
8.6 3.5
Pronunciation: /ˈspaɪ.nəl ˈfjuː.ʒən/ /; / /ˈspaɪ.nəl ˈfuː.ʒən/ (AmE), /ˈspaɪ.nəl ˈfjuː.ʒən/ (BrE)
A surgical procedure in which two or more vertebrae in the spine are joined together to stabilize the spine.

Examples

Spinal fusion surgery
After his accident, he underwent spinal fusion and extensive physical therapy.
Some patients recover mobility quickly following spinal fusion surgery.
The surgeon recommended a spinal fusion to stabilize her lower back.

Related Terms

  • synonym: laminectomy - surgical removal of a vertebral lamina
  • synonym: spinal decompression - procedure to relieve pressure on the spinal cord or nerves
