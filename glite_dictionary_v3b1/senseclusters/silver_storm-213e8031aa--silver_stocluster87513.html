silver storm

noun
storm with freezing rain coating ice
7.6 7.0
Pronunciation: /ˈsɪl.vər stɔːrm/ (AmE), /ˈsɪl.və stɔːm/ (BrE)
a storm during which freezing rain coats everything in a layer of ice, creating a silvery appearance

Examples

A silver storm
Driving was impossible during the silver storm because the roads became dangerously slippery.
Last night’s silver storm caused severe power outages as entire neighborhoods were left under sheets of ice.
The trees sparkled after the silver storm had passed, their branches encased in glistening ice.
