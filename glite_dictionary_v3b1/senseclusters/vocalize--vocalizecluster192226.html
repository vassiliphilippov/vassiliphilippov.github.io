vocalize

verb
pronounce as a vowel
Pronunciation: / ˈvoʊkəˌlaɪz / (AmE), / ˈvəʊkəlaɪz / (BrE)
(of a consonant, usually a liquid) to pronounce or articulate as a vowel rather than as a consonant.

Examples

To vocalize a consonant
In certain dialects, the consonant 'l' is often vocalized between vowels.
Some linguists study when liquids are vocalized in various languages.
The 'r' in this position may become vocalized rather than pronounced as a consonant.
During speech development, some sounds naturally vocalise over time.
In some dialects, the letter 'l' may be vocalised at the end of a word.
Linguists have studied how certain consonants are vocalised between vowels.
