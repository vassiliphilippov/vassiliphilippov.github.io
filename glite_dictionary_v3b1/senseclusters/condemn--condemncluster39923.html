condemn

verb
force into an unpleasant state or activity
Pronunciation: / kənˈdɛm / (AmE), / kənˈdɛm / (BrE)
to force someone to experience an unpleasant state or activity, especially something undesirable or inescapable

Examples

Condemn children to poverty
He felt that poverty condemned many children to lives of hardship.
Her injury condemned her to months of painful rehabilitation.
The war condemned thousands to long years of exile.

Related Terms

  • synonym: doom - to make certain that something unpleasant will happen
