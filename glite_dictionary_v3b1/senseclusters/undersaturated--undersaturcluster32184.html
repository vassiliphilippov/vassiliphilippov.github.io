undersaturated

adjective
not fully saturated
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndərˈsætʃəˌreɪtɪd / (AmE), / ˌʌndəˈsætʃəreɪtɪd / (BrE)
containing less than the maximum possible amount of a substance; not fully saturated

Examples

Undersaturated solution
The chemist noted that the solution was still undersaturated and could absorb more solute.
Rainfall was so light that the soil remained undersaturated throughout the summer.
If the air is undersaturated, more water can evaporate into it.

Related Terms

  • antonym: saturated - containing the maximum possible amount of a substance
  • degree higher: supersaturated - containing more than the normal maximum amount of a substance
