traverse

noun
zigzag path across a slope on skis
Pronunciation: / trəˈvɝs /; / trəˈvɜrs / (AmE), / trəˈvɜːs / (BrE)
a journey, crossing, or movement across an area or obstacle

Examples

A mountain traverse
During the lesson, we practiced controlling our speed on each traverse across the slope.
One long traverse brought us closer to the trail's end.
The skier made a careful traverse to avoid the steepest section of the mountain.
Completing the mountain's traverse required skill and endurance.
The climbers attempted a difficult traverse of the north face.
Their journey included a five-mile traverse across the glacier.
