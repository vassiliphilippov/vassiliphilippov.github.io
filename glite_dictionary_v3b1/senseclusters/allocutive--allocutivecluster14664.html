allocutive

noun
direct address form
9.9
Pronunciation: / əˈlɑːkjətɪv / (AmE), / əˈlɒkjʊtɪv / (BrE)
a grammatical form or word used to address someone directly, often indicating the addressee's person, gender, or social status

Examples

An allocutive word
In some languages, an allocutive form is required when speaking to elders to show respect.
The linguist explained how the allocutive changes depending on the gender of the person addressed.
Basque uses a special allocutive to distinguish between formal and informal speech.
