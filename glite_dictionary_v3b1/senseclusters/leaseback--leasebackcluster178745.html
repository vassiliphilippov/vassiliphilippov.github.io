leaseback

verb
to sell and rent back
Pronunciation: / ˈlisˌbæk / (AmE), / ˈliːsbæk / (BrE)
to sell property or an asset and then immediately rent it back from the new owner

Examples

To leaseback a building
The company decided to leaseback its office building to free up capital for expansion.
After selling the property, they chose to leaseback the space to continue their operations.
Many businesses leaseback assets as a way to improve cash flow without relocating.

Related Terms

  • synonym: rent - to pay money to use property or land owned by someone else
  • synonym: sell - to give something to someone in exchange for money
