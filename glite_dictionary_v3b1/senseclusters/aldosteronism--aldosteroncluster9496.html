aldosteronism

noun
excess of aldosterone hormone
Pronunciation: / ˌæl.doʊˈstɛr.əˌnɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˌæld.ɒˈstɛr.ə.nɪz.əm / (BrE)
A condition caused by excessive production of aldosterone, typically resulting in high blood pressure and imbalance of electrolytes.

Examples

Excessive aldosteronism effects
If left untreated, aldosteronism can increase the risk of cardiovascular complications.
The doctor diagnosed her with aldosteronism after noticing persistently high blood pressure and low potassium levels.
Treatment options for aldosteronism depend on its underlying cause and severity.
