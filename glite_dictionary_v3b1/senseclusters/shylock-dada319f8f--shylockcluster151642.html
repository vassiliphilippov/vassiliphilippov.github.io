Shylock

proper noun
offensive moneylender term
Pronunciation: / ˈʃaɪlɑk / (AmE), / ˈʃaɪlɒk / (BrE)
an offensive term for a ruthless or greedy moneylender, based on the character from Shakespeare's play 'The Merchant of Venice'; do not use

Examples

The name Shylock
The term Shylock is considered highly offensive and should not be used.
Some old novels refer to a ruthless lender as a Shylock, but this is now unacceptable.
Using Shylock as an insult perpetuates harmful stereotypes and is inappropriate.

Related Terms

  • synonym: usurer - a person who lends money at unreasonably high interest rates
