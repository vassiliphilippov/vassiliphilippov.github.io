fuzz

verb
to blur or obscure
5.6
Pronunciation: /fʌz/ (AmE), /fʌz/ (BrE)
to make something blurry, unclear, or indistinct in appearance or sound

Examples

Rain fuzzes the view
The rain on the window kept fuzzing the view of the street outside.
Old recordings sometimes fuzz the sound, making voices hard to recognize.
If you move the camera too quickly, it will fuzz the image.
The photographer used a filter to fuzz the background of the portrait.
You can fuzz the audio to create a vintage effect in your recording.
The rain on the window seemed to fuzz the city lights outside.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blur - to make something unclear or less distinct
  • antonym: sharpen - to make something clearer or more distinct
