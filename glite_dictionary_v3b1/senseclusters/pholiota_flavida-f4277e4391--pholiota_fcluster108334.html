Pholiota flavida

noun
a species of mushroom with brownish-orange cap
9.9
Pronunciation: / foʊˈliːoʊtə fləˈvaɪdə / (AmE), / fəʊˈlɪətə fləˈvʌɪdə / (BrE)
A species of mushroom that grows in clusters on the ground, with a brownish-orange, smooth, slightly sticky cap, whitish gills, and a cylindrical brown stalk.

Examples

Clusters of Pholiota flavida
Clusters of Pholiota flavida were found growing on the forest floor after heavy rains.
Researchers collected samples of Pholiota flavida for fungal biodiversity studies.
The Pholiota flavida is easily identified by its smooth, sticky, brownish-orange cap.
