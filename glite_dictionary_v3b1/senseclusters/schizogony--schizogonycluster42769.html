schizogony

noun
asexual reproduction by multiple fission
7.5 9.9
Pronunciation: /skɪ.ˈzɑː.gə.niː/ /; / /skɪ.ˈzɔː.gə.niː/ (AmE), /skɪ.ˈzɒ.gə.niː/ /; / /skɪ.ˈzɒ.gə.nɪ/ (BrE)
a type of asexual reproduction in which a single cell divides into multiple daughter cells, characteristic of certain protozoans, especially sporozoans.

Examples

Schizogony in protozoans
Malaria parasites reproduce by schizogony inside the red blood cells of their host.
Researchers studied the stages of schizogony to understand the life cycle of Plasmodium species.
The process of schizogony leads to the production of many new protozoan cells from one parent cell.
