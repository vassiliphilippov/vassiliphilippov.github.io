effects unit

noun
film/TV team creating effects
4.9
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈfekts ˈjuː.nɪt/ /; / /ə.ˈfekts ˈjuː.nɪt/ /; / /ɪ.ˈfekts ˈjuː.nət/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈfekts ˈjuː.nɪt/ /; / /ə.ˈfekts ˈjuː.nɪt/ /; / /ɪ.ˈfekts ˈjuː.nət/ (BrE)
a team or department in a film, television, or video production responsible for designing, creating, and executing practical on-set effects and/or supervising digital visual effects (VFX).

Examples

Film effects unit
The film's effects unit built a realistic explosion for the finale.
During rehearsals, the director consulted the effects unit about safety and camera placement.
Credit the effects unit in the program; they combined practical models with CGI to great effect.

Related Terms

  • synonym: special effects unit - a team that creates physical, mechanical, or practical effects on set
  • synonym: visual effects (VFX) team - a group responsible for creating or compositing digital computer-generated imagery for film or TV
