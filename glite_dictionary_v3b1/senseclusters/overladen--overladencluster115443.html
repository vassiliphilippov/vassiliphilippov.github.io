overladen

adjective
loaded past capacity
6.2
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈleɪdən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈleɪdən / (BrE)
loaded with too much of something; carrying a burden greater than it can easily bear

Examples

Overladen with work
Her schedule was overladen with meetings, leaving her little time for rest.
The table groaned under the weight of overladen platters at the banquet.
The truck was overladen and struggled to make it up the steep hill.

Related Terms

  • synonym: overloaded - carrying too heavy or too much a load
  • antonym: unburdened - not carrying a heavy load; relieved of a burden
