supersolid

noun
solid with superfluidity
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌsuːpərˈsɑːlɪd / (AmE), / ˌsuːpəˈsɒlɪd / (BrE)
a phase of matter that has both the rigid structure of a solid and the frictionless flow of a superfluid

Examples

A supersolid state
Researchers have created a supersolid in the laboratory using ultracold atoms.
The discovery of the supersolid phase challenges our understanding of quantum physics.
A supersolid can flow without friction while maintaining a solid structure.

Related Terms

  • synonym: solid - a state of matter with a fixed shape and volume
  • synonym: superfluid - a phase of matter with zero viscosity allowing frictionless flow
