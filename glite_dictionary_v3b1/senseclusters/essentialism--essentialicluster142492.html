essentialism

noun
belief in fixed nature
8.8
Pronunciation: / ɪˈsɛnʃəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ɪˈsɛnʃəlɪzəm / (BrE)
the belief that things, especially groups or categories, have an inherent and unchanging nature or set of characteristics

Examples

Philosophical essentialism
Some philosophers criticize essentialism for ignoring the influence of culture and environment.
The teacher's approach was based on essentialism, focusing on core knowledge and skills.
Debates about gender often involve arguments for or against essentialism.

Related Terms

  • antonym: constructivism - the belief that knowledge and meaning are constructed rather than inherent
  • synonym: determinism - the belief that events or outcomes are determined by existing causes
