Yalu River

proper noun
river on China–North Korea border
Pronunciation: / ˈjɑːlu ˈrɪvər / (AmE), / ˈjɑːlu ˈrɪvə / (BrE)
a river in East Asia that forms part of the border between North Korea and China, known for its historical significance

Examples

The Yalu River border

Chinese forces crossed the Yalu River to enter North Korea in 1950.
The Yalu River played a strategic role during the Korean War.
During the Korean War, United Nations forces advanced to the banks of the Yalu River.
Pollution levels in the Yalu River have been a concern for both countries.
The Yalu River serves as a natural border between China and North Korea.
