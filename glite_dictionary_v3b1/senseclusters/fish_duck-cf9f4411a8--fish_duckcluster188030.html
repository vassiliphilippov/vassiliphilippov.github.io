fish duck

noun
fish-eating diving duck (e.g. merganser)
8.9
Pronunciation: / fɪʃ dʌk / (AmE), / fɪʃ dʌk / (BrE)
a large crested fish-eating diving duck with a slender hooked bill and serrated edges, especially a merganser

Examples

A fish duck bird
Many birdwatchers observed a rare fish duck on the lake this morning.
The fish duck dived swiftly beneath the surface to catch a trout.
The serrated bill of the fish duck is perfectly adapted for gripping slippery prey.

Related Terms

  • synonym: merganser - a diving duck with a long, thin bill and crest, known for eating fish
