scumbling

noun
painting technique
Pronunciation: / ˈskʌmblɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈskʌmblɪŋ / (BrE)
a painting technique in which a thin, opaque layer of color is lightly brushed over another to soften or lighten it

Examples

Scumbling painting technique
The artist achieved a soft, atmospheric look by using scumbling over the background colors.
You can create a hazy effect in your painting with careful scumbling techniques.
Many landscape painters rely on scumbling to add depth and light to their work.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blending - a painting technique where colors are mixed smoothly together
  • antonym: glazing - a painting technique using thin, transparent layers of color
