ototoxic

adjective
toxic to hearing or balance organs
6.7 8.5
Pronunciation: /oʊ.tə.ˈtɑːk.sɪk/ /; / /oʊ.toʊ.ˈtɑːk.sɪk/ (AmE), /əʊ.tə.ˈtɒk.sɪk/ /; / /əʊ.təʊ.ˈtɒk.sɪk/ (BrE)
(of a substance) toxic to the organs of hearing, balance, or to the auditory nerve, causing possible damage or impairment.

Examples

Ototoxic drugs
Chemotherapy drugs sometimes have ototoxic properties, so patients' hearing is carefully monitored.
Some antibiotics are considered ototoxic and may cause hearing loss as a side effect.
The doctor explained that the medication could have ototoxic effects, especially with prolonged use.

Related Terms

  • synonym: nephrotoxic - toxic or destructive to kidney cells
