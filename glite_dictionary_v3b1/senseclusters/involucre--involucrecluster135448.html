involucre

noun
ring or whorl of bracts at flower base
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈvɑːljəkər / (AmE), / ɪnˈvɒljʊkə / (BrE)
a conspicuous whorl, pair, or ring of bracts located at the base of an inflorescence, especially in certain flowering plants

Examples

The involucre of flowers
The botanist explained that the involucre consists of several overlapping bracts.
The sunflower's prominent involucre protects its developing seeds.
You can easily spot the involucre encircling the base of the artichoke flower.
