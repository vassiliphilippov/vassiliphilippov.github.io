put up one's dukes

verb
prepare to fight (raise fists)
Pronunciation: / pʊt ʌp wʌnz duks / (AmE), / pʊt ʌp wʌnz djuːks / (BrE)
to raise one's fists in preparation for a fight or to defend oneself

Examples

To put up one's dukes
He shouted, 'Come on, put up your dukes!' to challenge his opponent.
If you keep taunting me, I might have to put up my dukes.
Whenever someone insulted her friend, she was ready to put up her dukes.

Related Terms

  • synonym: fight - to engage in a physical altercation
  • antonym: stand down - to cease fighting or withdraw from confrontation
