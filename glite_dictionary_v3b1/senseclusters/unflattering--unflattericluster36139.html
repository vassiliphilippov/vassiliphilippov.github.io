unflattering

adjective
not showing someone or something in a favorable way
5.7 4.8
Pronunciation: /ən.ˈflæt.ə.rɪŋ/ /; / /ʌn.ˈflæt.ə.rɪŋ/ (AmE), /ʌn.ˈflæt.ə.rɪŋ/ /; / /ən.ˈflæt.ə.rɪŋ/ (BrE)
(of a picture, comment, or appearance) showing or representing someone or something in a way that makes them look worse or less attractive than they really are.

Examples

An unflattering picture
His unflattering review of the play caused quite a stir among the actors.
She found the lighting in the room terribly unflattering to her complexion.
The magazine published an unflattering photo of the celebrity, which quickly went viral.

  • antonym: flattering - making someone or something look attractive or good
