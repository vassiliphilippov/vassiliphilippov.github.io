scudo

noun
heraldic shield
9.9 0.00/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàskuÀêdo ä / (AmE), / ÀàskuÀêd…ô ä / (BrE)
a shield or shield-shaped emblem, especially as used in heraldry or on coats of arms

Examples

A knight's scudo
The knight's scudo was decorated with a golden lion and red stripes.
Historians studied the scudo to learn more about the noble family's history.
A large scudo hung above the entrance, displaying the city's ancient emblem.

Synonyms

  • scutcheon
    9.9 0.01/m
    scutcheon is the heraldic shield that bears a coat of arms, while scudo means any shield-shaped emblem used on coats of arms.
  • escutcheon
    9.9 0.06/m
    escutcheon is a shield or emblem that shows a family's coat of arms, whereas scudo refers to a shield-shaped emblem.
