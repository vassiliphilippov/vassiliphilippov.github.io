elected

adjective
chosen by vote; having won an election
2.1
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɛktɪd / (AmE), / ɪˈlɛktɪd / (BrE)
(of a person) chosen by vote to hold public office.

Examples

An elected leader
Every elected leader must be accountable to the public.
He became the youngest elected official in the city’s history.
The president is an elected representative of the people.

Related Terms

  • antonym: appointed - placed in a position by designation rather than election
  • synonym: elective - open to being filled by election
