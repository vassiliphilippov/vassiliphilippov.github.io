sting

noun
a sudden, sharp pain (physical or emotional)
Pronunciation: / stɪŋ / (AmE), / stɪŋ / (BrE)
a sharp, sudden pain or injury, especially from something like an insect, plant, or irritant; or to cause such pain

Examples

A bee sting
He winced at the sting of the needle during the injection.
The sting of her criticism stayed with him all day.
There was a sting in his foot after he stepped on the thorn.
Apply ice to reduce the swelling from the wasp sting.
She felt a sharp sting on her arm and saw a bee flying away.
The jellyfish left a painful sting on his ankle after swimming.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bite - a wound made by the teeth of an animal or insect
  • synonym: prick - a sharp, sudden pain from a small puncture
