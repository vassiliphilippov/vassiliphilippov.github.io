eliminate

verb
kill or remove in large numbers
2.0 3.4
Pronunciation: / ɪˈlɪməˌneɪt /; / ɪˈlɪmɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / ɪˈlɪmɪneɪt / (BrE)
to completely remove, get rid of, or kill something or someone

Examples

To eliminate a problem
New policies were introduced to eliminate vermin that had infested the farmlands.
The dictator ordered the army to eliminate all opposition in the region.
The disease threatened to eliminate entire communities if left unchecked.
Doctors hope to completely eliminate the disease through vaccination.
The new technology could eventually eliminate the need for paper records.
They are working to eliminate unnecessary expenses from the budget.
New advances in medicine could eliminate the disease entirely.
She managed to eliminate unnecessary expenses from her monthly budget.
The company plans to eliminate several unprofitable departments next year.
