play

behave carelessly or unconcernedly
2.5 47/m
Pronunciation: / pleÉª / (AmE), / pleÉª / (BrE)
to behave, act, or pretend in a particular way, often to deceive, manipulate, or for amusement

Examples

To play with feelings
Don't play with my trust; this isn't a game to me.
He often plays about with serious matters, not realizing the consequences.
You shouldn't play with her emotions if you don't mean to commit.
He tried to play innocent, but everyone knew he was involved.
She likes to play dumb when asked difficult questions.
They would sometimes play sick to get out of gym class.
He played with the notion of moving abroad, but never made concrete plans.
She absentmindedly played with the buttons on her jacket during the meeting.
The child played with her food, pushing it around her plate rather than eating it.
