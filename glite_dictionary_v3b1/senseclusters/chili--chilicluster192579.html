chili

noun
a hot, spicy pepper
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈtʃɪli /; / ˈtʃiːleɪ / (AmE), / ˈtʃɪli / (BrE)
A small, very hot pepper with a pointed shape, used especially to add spiciness to food.

Examples

Red chili pepper
Farmers in Mexico grow dozens of varieties of chili every year.

The recipe calls for one red chili to add heat to the dish.
Be careful when handling raw chile peppers, as their oils can irritate your skin.
He added sliced chile to the salsa for extra heat.
This stew wouldn't be the same without a pinch of fresh chile.
Dried chillies are often used to spice up traditional Asian dishes.
She accidentally touched her eyes after slicing a chilli and felt a burning sensation.
The chef added a chilli to the curry to give it extra heat.
