Wooden Horse

noun
mythical horse used in the Trojan War
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˈwʊdən hɔrs / (AmE), / ˈwʊd(ə)n hɔːs / (BrE)
a large hollow wooden figure of a horse, said in myth to have been built by the Greeks to conceal soldiers, allowing them to enter Troy during the Trojan War.

Examples

The Wooden Horse of Troy
According to legend, the Wooden Horse was wheeled inside the city walls.
Children learn the story of the Wooden Horse in many history classes.
The Greeks hid inside the Wooden Horse to gain entry into Troy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Trojan Horse - a deceptive trick; the mythological wooden horse used to sack Troy
