play

noun
dramatic work for stage performance
Pronunciation: / pleɪ / (AmE), / pleɪ / (BrE)
a dramatic work written to be performed by actors on a stage, or the performance itself

Examples

A Shakespeare play
After reading the play, the director decided to cast her as the lead.
She acted in her first school play when she was only ten years old.
The theater will stage a new play by a famous American playwright next month.
Her first play was a huge success on Broadway.
The school is putting on a play for the annual festival.
We attended a play at the downtown theater last night.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: opera - a dramatic work set to music, sung, and staged
  • degree lower: skit - a short, typically humorous dramatic performance
