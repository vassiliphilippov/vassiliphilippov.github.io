malolactic

noun
wine fermentation process
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˌmæloʊˈlæktɪk / (AmE), / ˌmæləʊˈlæktɪk / (BrE)
the process of malolactic fermentation, in which malic acid is converted to lactic acid, especially in winemaking

Examples

A malolactic step
The winemaker closely monitored the malolactic to ensure a smooth, balanced flavor in the final product.
Some white wines do not undergo malolactic to preserve their crisp acidity.
After primary fermentation, the wine entered malolactic to soften its taste and improve stability.

Related Terms

  • antonym: alcoholic fermentation - the process in which sugars are converted to alcohol and carbon dioxide
  • synonym: fermentation - a chemical process that converts sugars into alcohol or acids
