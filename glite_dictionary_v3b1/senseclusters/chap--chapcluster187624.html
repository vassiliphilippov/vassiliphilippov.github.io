chap

noun
leather leg covering worn by cowboys
Pronunciation: / tʃæp / (AmE), / tʃap / (BrE)
A protective leather leg covering, usually worn in pairs (chaps), without a seat, secured by a belt, and typically worn by cowboys over trousers to protect the legs.

Examples

Leather chap for riding
He examined each chap for signs of wear before the rodeo.
One chap was torn during his ride through the thorny brush.
The cowboy strapped on a chap before heading out to tend the cattle.
