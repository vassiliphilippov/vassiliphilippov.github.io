take in charge

verb
accept responsibility or control
Pronunciation: / teɪk ɪn tʃɑrdʒ / (AmE), / teɪk ɪn tʃɑːdʒ / (BrE)
to accept responsibility for or control over something or someone

Examples

To take in charge a patient
After the accident, the nurse took in charge of the injured children immediately.
He takes in charge whenever the manager is away.
The committee will take in charge of the investigation next week.
