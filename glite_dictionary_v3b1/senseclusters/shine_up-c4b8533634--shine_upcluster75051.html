shine up

verb
ingratiate oneself (often insincerely)
4.4 8.0
Pronunciation: / ʃaɪn ʌp / (AmE), / ʃaɪn ʌp / (BrE)
to try to make oneself liked by someone, often with insincere praise or behavior; to ingratiate oneself

Examples

To shine up to manager
He's just trying to shine up to the manager to get a promotion.
She spent the whole evening shining up to the new boss, hoping to impress him.
You won't get anywhere by constantly shining up to your teachers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flatter - to praise excessively, often insincerely, to gain favor
  • antonym: offend - to cause displeasure or hurt feelings; the opposite of ingratiating oneself
  • synonym: suck up - to try to win favor by insincere praise or flattery
