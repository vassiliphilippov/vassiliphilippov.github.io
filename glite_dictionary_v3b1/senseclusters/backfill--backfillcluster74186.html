backfill

noun
material for filling
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌfɪl / (AmE), / ˈbækˌfɪl / (BrE)
material used to fill a hole, trench, or excavation after work has been completed, or the act of filling it

Examples

Trench backfill
The workers poured backfill into the trench after laying the new pipes.
Gravel is often used as backfill to support the foundation of a building.
After the repairs were finished, the crew added backfill to restore the ground level.

Related Terms

  • antonym: excavation - the process of digging out earth, rock, or other material
  • synonym: fill - material used to fill a space or hole
