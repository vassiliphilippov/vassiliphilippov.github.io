overcommercialization

noun
excessive profit focus
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvɚkəˌmɝːʃələˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəkəˌmɜːʃəlaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of making something excessively focused on profit or commercial interests, often at the expense of its original value or meaning

Examples

The overcommercialization of holidays
Many people worry about the overcommercialization of holidays like Christmas.
Critics argue that the overcommercialization of sports has changed the spirit of the games.
Some believe the overcommercialization of art reduces its cultural and emotional value.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: commercialization - the process of making something available for sale or profit
  • synonym: exploitation - the act of using something unfairly for personal gain
