annalism

noun
recording of events in annal form
7.4
Pronunciation: / ˈæn.əˌlɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈan.ə.lɪz.əm / (BrE)
The practice or style of recording historical events in the form of annals or yearly chronological reports.

Examples

Annalism style
By avoiding the dryness of annalism, the author created a more engaging historical narrative.
Many early medieval histories are characterized by a strict adherence to annalism.
The historian's work is a model of annalism, listing events year by year with careful detail.

Related Terms

  • synonym: chronicle - A factual written account of important or historical events in the order of their occurrence
