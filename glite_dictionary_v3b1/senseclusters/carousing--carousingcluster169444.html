carousing

adjective
noisy, drunken revelry
7.1
Pronunciation: / kəˈraʊzɪŋ / (AmE), / kəˈraʊzɪŋ / (BrE)
(of people or gatherings) engaging in noisy, lively, and drunken revelry.

Examples

A carousing party
After hours of carousing celebration, the party finally ended at dawn.
She avoided the carousing groups gathering around the tavern.
The carousing crowd spilled into the streets after the big win.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reveling - taking part in lively and noisy festivities
  • antonym: sober - not affected by alcohol; serious and sensible
