retreat

verb
withdraw from a commitment or activity
2.3 3.3
Pronunciation: / rəˈtrit /; / rɪˈtrit / (AmE), / rɪˈtriːt / (BrE)
to move back or withdraw from a place, situation, or commitment, especially to avoid danger, difficulty, or for privacy

Examples

To retreat from danger
Facing strong opposition, she chose to retreat from her initial proposal.
He had to retreat from the deal after discovering new information.
The company decided to retreat from its earlier promise to expand overseas.
After a busy day, she enjoys retreating to her garden for quiet reflection.
Every summer, the professor retreats to his lakeside cabin to write in peace.
He occasionally retreated from social events to gather his thoughts.
As the tide comes in, we must retreat from the shoreline to stay dry.
The army was forced to retreat after losing the battle.
When threatened, the animal will retreat into its burrow for safety.

Related Terms

  • antonym: advance - to move forward
  • synonym: back out - to withdraw from a commitment or promise
  • antonym: commit - to pledge or bind to a certain course or policy
  • synonym: recede - to move gradually away from a previous position
  • synonym: withdraw - to remove oneself from participation or presence
