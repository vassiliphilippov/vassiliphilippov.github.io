retreat

verb
withdraw from a commitment or activity
4.0 17/m
Pronunciation: / r…ôÀàtrit /; / r…™Ààtrit / (AmE), / r…™ÀàtriÀêt / (BrE)
to move back or withdraw from a place, situation, or commitment, especially to avoid danger, difficulty, or for privacy

Examples

To retreat from danger
Facing strong opposition, she chose to retreat from her initial proposal.
He had to retreat from the deal after discovering new information.
The company decided to retreat from its earlier promise to expand overseas.
After a busy day, she enjoys retreating to her garden for quiet reflection.
Every summer, the professor retreats to his lakeside cabin to write in peace.
He occasionally retreated from social events to gather his thoughts.
As the tide comes in, we must retreat from the shoreline to stay dry.
The army was forced to retreat after losing the battle.
When threatened, the animal will retreat into its burrow for safety.

Synonyms

  • retreat from
    4.4 1.14/m
    retreat from describes stopping taking part in a specific position or fight and leaving, while retreat means moving back more generally.
  • withdraw
    2.8 26/m
    withdraw means formally leaving or stopping participation from a place or activity, while retreat often focuses on moving back to avoid danger.
  • pull out
    3.4 7.21/m
    pull out means to leave or stop taking part in an activity, while retreat often implies physically moving back or withdrawing.
  • retire
    3.8 3.18/m
    retire means to move away to somewhere private or quiet, often to rest, while retreat covers many kinds of withdrawal.
  • beat a retreat
    6.0 0.03/m
    beat a retreat is an idiom for leaving quickly to avoid danger, while retreat is a neutral act of moving back.
  • pull back
    3.4 9.20/m
    pull back means to move away from a position or stop an action, while retreat is a broader withdrawal for safety or privacy.
  • back off
    2.8 9.03/m
    back off means to move away or stop interfering, often in speech, while retreat usually describes a physical or formal withdrawal.
  • back away
    3.9 4.16/m
    back away means to step or pull back because of fear or doubt, while retreat can be planned and not only fearful.
  • draw back
    4.1 0.77/m
    draw back means to move away suddenly from surprise or fear, while retreat may be slower or part of a planned exit.
  • move back
    1.7 9.38/m
    move back means to go to an earlier or more distant spot, while retreat covers leaving a situation or commitment as well.
  • fall back
    3.7 1.46/m
    fall back means to retreat under pressure, often in battle or difficulty, while retreat also includes quiet, planned withdrawal.
  • recede
    4.8 3.05/m
    recede means to move back or become less strong over time, while retreat is an active withdrawal by people or groups.
  • back out
    3.4 1.78/m
    back out means to break a promise or decide not to take part, while retreat can be a strategic or physical withdrawal.
  • pull away
    3.5 6.95/m
    pull away means to begin to make distance from people or things, often slowly, while retreat can be sudden or planned.
