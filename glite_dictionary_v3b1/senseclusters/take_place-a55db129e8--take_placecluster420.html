take place

verb
to happen or occur
1.0
Pronunciation: / teɪk pleɪs / (AmE), / teɪk pleɪs / (BrE)
to happen or occur, especially as the result of being planned or scheduled

Examples

Events take place
No changes can take place without your approval.
The accident took place late at night during a heavy storm.
The annual festival will take place next weekend in the city square.
