unbosom

verb
reveal one's innermost thoughts or feelings
Pronunciation: / ʌnˈbʊzəm / (AmE), / ʌnˈbʊzəm / (BrE)
to reveal or confess one's thoughts or feelings, especially those that are troubling or kept secret

Examples

To unbosom feelings
After months of keeping it to herself, she finally unbosomed her fears to her closest friend.
He felt relieved after he unbosomed himself to his therapist.
It took courage for her to unbosom her worries about the future.

Related Terms

  • synonym: confide - to tell someone a secret or private matter
