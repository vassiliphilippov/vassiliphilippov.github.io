herem

noun
divine ban of destruction
Pronunciation: / ˈhɛrɛm / (AmE), / ˈhɛrɛm / (BrE)
a thing, place, or group set apart for destruction or exclusion by a religious or divine command, especially in ancient contexts

Examples

Herem on a city
The ancient city was placed under herem after it defied the invading army.
According to tradition, anything under herem was to be completely destroyed.
The religious leaders declared herem on the group for violating sacred laws.

Related Terms

  • synonym: excommunication - the act of officially excluding someone from participation in the sacraments and services of a church
  • antonym: sanctuary - a sacred or holy place; a place of refuge
