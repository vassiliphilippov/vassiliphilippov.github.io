Hypatia

proper noun
Greek philosopher and astronomer (c. 370–415 AD)
9.9
Pronunciation: / haɪˈpeɪʃə / (AmE), / hɪˈpeɪtiə / (BrE)
(c. 370–415 AD) A Greek philosopher, mathematician, and astronomer, notable for her scholarly works in Alexandria and her inventions such as the astrolabe.

Examples

Hypatia of Alexandria
Hypatia made substantial contributions to mathematics and astronomy during her time in Alexandria.
Many scholars regard Hypatia as one of the earliest and most influential female philosophers.
The legacy of Hypatia has inspired countless works of literature and art.
