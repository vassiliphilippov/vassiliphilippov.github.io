disregard

verb
intentionally ignore or refuse to acknowledge
Pronunciation: / ˌdɪs.rɪˈɡɑrd /; / ˌdɪsrɪˈɡɑrd / (AmE), / ˌdɪs.rɪˈɡɑːd /; / ˌdɪsrɪˈɡɑːd / (BrE)
to ignore something or someone; to pay no attention to or treat as unimportant

Examples

To disregard advice
He chose to disregard her comments and continued with his presentation.
Many drivers disregard the no-parking signs on this street.
She disregarded his wave and walked straight past him.
She decided to disregard the rumors and focus on her work.
The manager chose to disregard the complaints from the staff and continued with his plan.
You should never disregard the safety instructions before operating the equipment.
Many drivers disregard the speed limit in this area.
She deliberately disregarded his warning about the icy roads.
The committee chose to disregard his suggested changes to the policy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ignore - to pay no attention to; to deliberately disregard
  • antonym: notice - to pay attention to or become aware of something or someone
