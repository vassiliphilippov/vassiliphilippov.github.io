headfish

noun
a large pelagic bony fish (ocean sunfish)
7.5 7.7
Pronunciation: /ˈhed.fɪʃ/ (AmE), /ˈhed.fɪʃ/ (BrE)
A very large, pelagic bony fish with an oval compressed body, high dorsal and anal fins, and a rudder-like reduced caudal fin, found in warm seas worldwide; also known as the ocean sunfish or mola.

Examples

A large headfish
Divers spotted a massive headfish swimming near the coral reef.
Many tourists come to the bay in hopes of seeing a headfish basking at the surface.
The headfish is famous for its unusual, flattened body shape.
