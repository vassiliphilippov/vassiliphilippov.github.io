booter

noun
DDoS attack tool
Pronunciation: / ˈbuːtər / (AmE), / ˈbuːtə / (BrE)
a program or online service used to launch denial-of-service attacks that disrupt network services

Examples

A booter attack
The hacker purchased a booter to take down several gaming servers.
Using a booter is illegal and can result in severe penalties.
Many websites have increased their security to defend against booter attacks.

Related Terms

  • antonym: firewall - a security system that controls incoming and outgoing network traffic
  • synonym: stresser - a service used to test network resilience by simulating denial-of-service attacks
