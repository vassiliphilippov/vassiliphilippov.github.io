craw

noun
food storage pouch in birds
9.9
Pronunciation: / krɔ / (AmE), / krɔː / (BrE)
a pouch in the throat of many birds and some lower animals, where food is stored and softened before digestion

Examples

A bird's craw
Farmers often check a chicken’s craw to assess its eating habits.
Food temporarily accumulates in the craw before it moves further into the digestive tract.
The bird had a full craw after feeding in the fields all morning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crop - the part of a bird's digestive system for storing food, similar to craw
