relaunch

verb
to start again
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˈlɔntʃ / (AmE), / ˌriːˈlɔːntʃ / (BrE)
to start or introduce something again, especially after it has stopped, failed, or been changed

Examples

To relaunch a website
After a year of redesign, the company will relaunch its main website to attract more users.
The author decided to relaunch her book with a new cover and marketing campaign.
They hope to relaunch the project next spring after resolving the technical issues.
The company decided to relaunch its website with a new design and better features.
After several years off the market, the team will relaunch the popular video game.
They hope to relaunch the campaign next month with a fresh approach.
The company will relaunch its popular app with new features next month.
After a year-long break, the artist decided to relaunch her music career.
They are planning to relaunch the advertising campaign to attract more customers.

Related Terms

  • antonym: abandon - to stop doing something completely
  • antonym: cancel - to stop something from continuing or happening
  • synonym: launch - to introduce something new to the public
  • synonym: restart - to start something again after stopping
  • synonym: resume - to begin again after a pause
