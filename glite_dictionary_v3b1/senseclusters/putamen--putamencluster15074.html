putamen

noun
part of the brain's lenticular nucleus
9.9
Pronunciation: / pjuˈteɪmən / (AmE), / pjuˈteɪmən / (BrE)
the outer reddish portion of the lenticular nucleus in the brain, involved in motor control

Examples

The putamen in brain
Damage to the putamen can lead to movement disorders such as Parkinson's disease.
On the MRI scan, the radiologist observed lesions in the patient's putamen.
The putamen is a critical structure in the brain's regulation of voluntary movements.
