European swift

noun
common European bird; Apus apus
6.7
Pronunciation: / jʊˈroʊpiən swɪft / (AmE), / jʊəˈrəʊpiən swɪft / (BrE)
a common European bird (Apus apus) with a shrieking call, noted for nesting mainly near eaves of buildings or on cliffs

Examples

The European swift bird
A European swift can be identified by its long, scythe-shaped wings and piercing call.
From early summer, European swift flocks fill the air above the city.
We found a European swift nesting beneath the eaves of our old house.
