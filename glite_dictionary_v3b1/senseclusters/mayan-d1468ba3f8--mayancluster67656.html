Mayan

noun
member of an indigenous Mesoamerican people
6.0
Pronunciation: /ˈmaɪ.ən/ (AmE), /ˈmaɪ.ən/ (BrE)
A member of an indigenous people of Yucatán, Belize, and Guatemala, known for their advanced civilization, especially in architecture, pottery, and astronomy, which reached its peak between AD 300 and 900.

Examples

Ancient Mayan civilization
A Mayan astronomer once calculated celestial events with remarkable precision.
Some descendants of the ancient Mayan people still speak their ancestral languages today.
The Mayan civilization left behind impressive stone cities and pyramids in Central America.
