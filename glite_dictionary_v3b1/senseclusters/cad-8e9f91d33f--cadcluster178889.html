CAD

noun
computer-aided detection/diagnosis (medical imaging)
4.6
Pronunciation: /kæd/ (AmE), /kæd/ (BrE)
(in medicine) a computer-aided detection or diagnostic system that assists clinicians by automatically identifying or highlighting possible abnormalities on medical images.

Examples

Medical CAD system
Radiologists used the hospital's new CAD to highlight possible tumors on routine chest X‑rays.
After reviewing the scan, the doctor noted that the CAD had missed a small lesion near the liver.
Clinical trials show combining radiologist expertise with CAD improves sensitivity for detecting early-stage breast cancer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: computer-aided detection - the process of using software to detect suspicious features in medical images
  • synonym: computer-aided diagnosis - the use of computer systems to assist in diagnosing disease from medical data or images
