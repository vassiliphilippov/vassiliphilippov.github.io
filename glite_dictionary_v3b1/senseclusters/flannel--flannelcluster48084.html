flannel

verb
to evade with words
Pronunciation: / ˈflænəl / (AmE), / ˈflænəl / (BrE)
to speak or act evasively or insincerely, using vague or flattering language to avoid giving a direct answer

Examples

To flannel a question
The politician flannelled when asked about the controversial policy, never giving a clear response.
She flannelled her way through the meeting, avoiding any firm commitments.
When pressed for details, he simply flannelled and changed the subject.

Related Terms

  • antonym: confront - to face or deal with something directly
  • synonym: evade - to avoid giving a direct answer or dealing with something honestly
  • synonym: waffle - to speak or write at length without saying anything important or definite
