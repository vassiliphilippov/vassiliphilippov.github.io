desert soil

noun
soil common in arid regions
2.5
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛzɚt sɔɪl / (AmE), / ˈdɛzət sɔɪl / (BrE)
soil that is characteristic of arid regions, typically low in organic matter and high in mineral content due to limited rainfall.

Examples

Desert soil farming
Farming in desert soil often requires special irrigation methods to produce crops.
Scientists conducted tests to analyze the composition of desert soil in the region.
The plants adapted to the harsh desert soil by developing deep roots.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arid soil - soil found in dry climates with minimal moisture
