rogatory

noun
day of prayer
Pronunciation: / ˈrɑːɡəˌtɔri / (AmE), / ˈrɒɡətəri / (BrE)
an archaic term for a day of public prayer or supplication, especially in some Christian traditions

Examples

A rogatory day
The villagers observed the rogatory with processions and special prayers for rain.
In medieval times, a rogatory was held each spring to bless the fields.
Few people today remember the meaning of a rogatory in the church calendar.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: festival - a day or period of celebration, often religious
  • synonym: supplication - the act of asking or praying for something earnestly
