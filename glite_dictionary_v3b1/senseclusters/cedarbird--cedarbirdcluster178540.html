cedarbird

noun
North American waxwing bird species
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsiːdərˌbɝːd / (AmE), / ˈsiːdəˌbɜːd / (BrE)
A North American bird of the waxwing family, recognized for its sleek plumage, crest, and yellow-tipped tail, commonly found in cedar and other woodlands.

Examples

A cedarbird flock
A flock of cedarbirds swooped down to feed on the red berries in the garden.
Each spring, cedarbirds return to our neighborhood, feasting on juniper and cedar fruits.
The cedarbird is easily recognized by its distinctive crest and yellow-tipped tail.

Related Terms

  • synonym: waxwing - songbird with silky plumage and bright markings, same family as cedarbird
