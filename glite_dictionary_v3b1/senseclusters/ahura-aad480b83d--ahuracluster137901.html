Ahura

noun
benevolent Zoroastrian deity
9.9
Pronunciation: /ə.ˈhʊr.ə/ /; / /ɑː.ˈhuː.rə/ (AmE), /ə.ˈhʊ.rə/ /; / /ɑː.ˈhuː.rə/ (BrE)
(in Zoroastrianism) a title for benevolent deities, especially as opposed to evil spirits, in ancient Persian religion.

Examples

The Ahura deities
In ancient Persia, the concept of Ahura played a central role in distinguishing good from evil spirits.
Some prayers in the Avesta are dedicated specifically to an Ahura rather than to a particular god.
The Ahura are seen as protectors of truth and justice in Zoroastrian scriptures.

Related Terms

  • antonym: daeva - (in Zoroastrianism) a class of malevolent supernatural beings or spirits
