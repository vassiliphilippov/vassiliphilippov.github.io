disorientate

verb
cause to lose sense of direction or become confused
7.7 4.6
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈɔriənˌteɪt / (AmE), / dɪsˈɔːrɪənteɪt / (BrE)
to make someone lose their sense of direction or feel confused about where they are or what is happening

Examples

To disorientate someone
Bright lights and loud noises can disorientate small children.
It is easy to disorientate yourself when hiking in dense forest.
The unfamiliar setting tended to disorientate some of the visitors at first.
