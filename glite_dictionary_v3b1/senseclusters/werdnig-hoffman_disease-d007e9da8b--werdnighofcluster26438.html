Werdnig-Hoffman disease

noun
rare genetic disease causing muscle weakness in infants
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈvɛrdnɪɡ ˈhɑfmən dɪˌziz / (AmE), / ˈvɜːdnɪɡ ˈhɒfmən dɪˌziːz / (BrE)
A rare, inherited disorder characterized by the degeneration of spinal and brain nerve cells, leading to muscle weakness, flaccid paralysis, and often early childhood death; also known as spinal muscular atrophy type 1.

Examples

Werdnig-Hoffman disease symptoms
A family history of Werdnig-Hoffman disease can increase the risk of recurrence.
Medical advances have improved the quality of life for patients with Werdnig-Hoffman disease.
The child was diagnosed with Werdnig-Hoffman disease shortly after birth.
