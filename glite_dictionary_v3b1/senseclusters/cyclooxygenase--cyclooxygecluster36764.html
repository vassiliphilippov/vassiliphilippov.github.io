cyclooxygenase

noun
enzyme involved in prostaglandin production
7.8
Pronunciation: / saɪ.kloʊˈɑks.ɪ.dʒəˌneɪs / (AmE), / saɪ.kləʊˈɒk.sɪ.dʒəˌneɪz / (BrE)
an enzyme, existing in two forms (COX-1 and COX-2), that regulates the production of prostaglandins and is inhibited by drugs such as aspirin

Examples

cyclooxygenase enzyme
Aspirin works by inhibiting the enzyme cyclooxygenase in the body.
Both forms of cyclooxygenase are important for regulating pain and fever.
Researchers are developing drugs that selectively target cyclooxygenase-2 to reduce inflammation.
