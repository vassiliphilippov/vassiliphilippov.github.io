controlling

adjective
having power to direct or determine policy
Pronunciation: / kənˈtroʊlɪŋ / (AmE), / kənˈtrəʊlɪŋ / (BrE)
having the power to direct, influence, or determine policy or actions, especially in a business or organizational context

Examples

A controlling stake
A controlling stake gave him the ability to shape the firm's future.
She holds a controlling position on the board and makes most of the decisions.
The company acquired a controlling interest in its chief competitor.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dominant - exercising control or authority over others
