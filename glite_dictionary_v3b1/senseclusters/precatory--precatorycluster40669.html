precatory

adjective
expressing a wish or request
9.9 9.2
Pronunciation: /ˈprek.ə.tɔː.r.iː/ /; / /ˈprek.ə.tɑː.r.iː/ (AmE), /ˈprek.ə.triː/ /; / /ˈprek.ə.tə.riː/ (BrE)
expressing a wish, entreaty, or request rather than a command or order; having the nature of entreaty or petition.

Examples

A precatory clause
In legal documents, a precatory clause expresses a wish but does not impose a binding obligation.
Many wills contain precatory language, leaving requests rather than enforceable instructions.
The committee issued a precatory statement urging cooperation among all parties.
