nondelegation

noun
prohibition on transferring powers
Pronunciation: / ˌnɑnˌdɛlɪˈɡeɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌnɒnˌdɛlɪˈɡeɪʃən / (BrE)
the principle that certain powers or responsibilities cannot be transferred from one authority to another

Examples

Nondelegation doctrine
The Supreme Court considered whether the law violated the nondelegation principle.
Legal scholars often debate the limits of nondelegation in constitutional law.
The doctrine of nondelegation restricts the transfer of legislative powers to other bodies.

Related Terms

  • antonym: delegation - the act of transferring authority or responsibility to another
