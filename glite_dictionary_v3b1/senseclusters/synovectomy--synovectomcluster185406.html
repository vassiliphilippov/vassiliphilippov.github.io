synovectomy

noun
removal of joint membrane
Pronunciation: / ˌsɪnoʊˈvɛktəmi / (AmE), / ˌsɪnəʊˈvɛktəmi / (BrE)
a surgical procedure to remove the synovial membrane from a joint

Examples

A synovectomy surgery
After years of pain, the doctor recommended a synovectomy to improve her knee function.
A synovectomy can help reduce swelling and discomfort in patients with severe arthritis.
The surgeon explained that a synovectomy involves removing the inflamed tissue from the joint.

Related Terms

  • synonym: arthroscopy - a minimally invasive surgical procedure on a joint
  • antonym: synovitis - inflammation of the synovial membrane
