Hutterite

noun
communal religious group
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈhʌtəˌraɪt / (AmE), / ˈhʌtəˌraɪt / (BrE)
a member of a communal Anabaptist religious group, known for living together and sharing property

Examples

A Hutterite family
The Hutterite community welcomed visitors to learn about their way of life.
Many Hutterites work together on large farms and share the harvest equally.
A Hutterite colony often includes its own school and communal dining hall.
The Hutterite colony produces most of its own food and goods through communal work.
Many Hutterites speak a unique dialect of German in their daily lives.
A Hutterite community welcomed visitors to learn about their traditions and beliefs.
