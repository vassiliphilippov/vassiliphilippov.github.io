in-person

adjective
requiring physical presence

(of an activity or event) involving the physical presence of someone, rather than taking place remotely or online.

Examples

In-person meeting
All employees are required to attend the meeting in-person next Monday.
Despite the convenience of online classes, some students prefer in-person learning.
He appreciated being able to resolve the issue in-person rather than over the phone.

Synonyms
