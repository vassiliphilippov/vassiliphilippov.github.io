economization

noun
focus on economics
Pronunciation: / ɪˌkɑːnəmaɪˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪˌkɒnəmaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of organizing or evaluating activities, institutions, or values mainly by economic principles such as profit or efficiency

Examples

Economization of education
The economization of education has led to increased tuition fees and budget cuts.
Critics argue that the economization of public services can undermine their social value.
Recent reforms reflect a trend toward the economization of cultural institutions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: commercialization - the process of managing or running something mainly for financial gain
  • synonym: monetization - the process of converting something into a source of income
