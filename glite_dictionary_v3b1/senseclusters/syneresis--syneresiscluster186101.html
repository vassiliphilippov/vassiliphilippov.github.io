syneresis

noun
contraction of vowels into a diphthong
7.3 9.9
Pronunciation: / sɪˈnɛrəsɪs / (AmE), / sɪˈnɛrɪsɪs / (BrE)
the contraction of two adjacent vowels into a single syllable, producing a diphthong

Examples

Syneresis in poetry
In poetry, syneresis often occurs when two vowels are pronounced together as one syllable.
Linguists study syneresis to understand changes in language pronunciation over time.
The word 'poet' can undergo syneresis in rapid speech, sounding like a single syllable.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crasis - the merging of two vowels into one, especially in Greek or Latin
