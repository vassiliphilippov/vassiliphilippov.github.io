in addition

adverb
used to add extra information
1.0
Pronunciation: / ɪn əˈdɪʃən / (AmE), / ɪn əˈdɪʃən /; / ɪn əˈdɪʃ.ən / (BrE)
used to introduce extra information or another point; also; furthermore

Examples

In addition, she speaks
In addition, she speaks three languages fluently.
More than fifty people attended the meeting; in addition, several others joined online.
The book is well researched and, in addition, very entertaining.
In addition to her responsibilities as a manager, she volunteers at a local shelter.
In addition, the company will offer free training sessions to its employees.
He was awarded a scholarship and, in addition, received a special honor for his research.
