gag

verb
to retch or choke involuntarily
6.5 4.1
Pronunciation: /gæɡ/ (AmE), /gæɡ/ (BrE)
to choke, retch, or have a strong reaction in the throat as if about to vomit

Examples

To gag on food
He started to gag as soon as he put the medicine in his mouth.
She couldn't brush her teeth without gagging on the toothbrush.

Some people gag at the sight of blood.
The smell of rotten eggs made her gag instantly.

  • degree higher: vomit - to eject stomach contents through the mouth
