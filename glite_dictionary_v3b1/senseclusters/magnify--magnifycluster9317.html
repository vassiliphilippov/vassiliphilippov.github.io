magnify

verb
exaggerate; make something seem greater
2.1 3.8
Pronunciation: /ˈmæɡ.nə.faɪ/ /; / /ˈmæɡ.nɪ.faɪ/ (AmE), /ˈmæɡ.nɪ.faɪ/ /; / /ˈmæɡ.nə.faɪ/ (BrE)
to make something seem more important, serious, or significant than it really is; to exaggerate

Examples

To magnify a problem
Anxiety can magnify your perception of potential problems.
He tends to magnify his achievements when telling stories.
It is easy to magnify minor issues until they seem overwhelming.
Reports in the media often magnify the significance of minor events.
She tends to magnify her problems instead of putting them in perspective.
The microscope can magnify tiny cells so that they are visible to the naked eye.

Related Terms

  • synonym: amplify - to increase the strength or intensity of something
  • synonym: enlarge - to make something bigger in size or amount
  • synonym: exaggerate - to make something seem larger or more important than it really is
  • antonym: minimize - to make something appear smaller or less important
  • antonym: understate - to make something seem less important or smaller than it really is
