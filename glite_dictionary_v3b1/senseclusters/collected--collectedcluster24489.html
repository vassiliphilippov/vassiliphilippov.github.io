collected

adjective
calm; self-possessed
7.2
Pronunciation: / kəˈlɛktɪd / (AmE), / kəˈlɛktɪd / (BrE)
(of a person) calm and in control of oneself, especially in a difficult situation.

Examples

Collected and calm
Despite the chaos around her, she remained collected and handled the emergency with ease.
He gave a collected response to the unexpected question, showing no signs of nervousness.
Her collected demeanor reassured the team during the stressful meeting.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agitated - feeling or appearing troubled or nervous
  • synonym: composed - calm and in control of one's emotions
  • antonym: distraught - deeply upset and agitated
  • synonym: unflappable - not easily upset or disconcerted
