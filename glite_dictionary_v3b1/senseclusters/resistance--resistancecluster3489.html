resistance

noun
ability to resist disease (medicine)
Pronunciation: / rɪˈzɪstəns / (AmE), / rɪˈzɪstəns / (BrE)
the ability of an organism or microorganism to withstand or defend itself against disease, infection, drugs, or harmful agents

Examples

Resistance to disease
Genetic factors can influence an individual's resistance to certain diseases.
Some bacteria have developed a strong resistance to antibiotics.
The vaccine improves the body's resistance to infection.
Scientists are studying the resistance of bacteria to common antibiotics.
The wheat variety was developed for its strong resistance to drought and pests.
These shrubs are valued for their resistance to air pollution in urban areas.
Drug resistance can make the treatment of infections much more difficult and expensive.
Scientists are working to understand the mechanisms behind bacterial resistance to common medications.
The spread of antibiotic resistance has become a major concern in hospitals worldwide.

Related Terms

  • synonym: immunity - the state of being resistant to a particular infection or toxin
  • antonym: susceptibility - the likelihood or capacity to be harmed or affected by something
