anagogic

adjective
mystical or spiritual interpretation
7.1
Pronunciation: / ˌæn.əˈɡɑː.dʒɪk / (AmE), / ˌæn.əˈɡɒ.dʒɪk / (BrE)
Relating to or based on anagoge; involving mystical or spiritual interpretation, especially of texts or events.

Examples

Anagogic interpretation
His readings of scripture are often anagogic, reaching for a higher, mystical significance.
Scholars debated the value of anagogic approaches compared to more literal or historical ones.
The poem invites anagogic interpretation, revealing deeper spiritual meanings beneath the literal text.

Related Terms

  • synonym: allegorical - relating to allegory or symbolic representation
