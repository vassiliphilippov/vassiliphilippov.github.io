pocket battleship

noun
small battleship built under treaty restrictions
7.4 8.6
Pronunciation: / ˈpɑkət ˈbætəlˌʃɪp / (AmE), / ˈpɒkɪt ˈbætl̩ˌʃɪp / (BrE)
a relatively small battleship built to comply with treaty restrictions on size and armament, used especially in the period from 1925 to 1930

Examples

A pocket battleship class
A pocket battleship typically had heavier guns than a cruiser but was smaller than a traditional battleship.
During World War II, the pocket battleship Admiral Graf Spee became famous for its actions in the Atlantic.
The German navy developed the pocket battleship to circumvent the Treaty of Versailles restrictions.
