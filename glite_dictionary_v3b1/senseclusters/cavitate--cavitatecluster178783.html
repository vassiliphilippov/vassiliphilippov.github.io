cavitate

verb
to form bubbles
Pronunciation: / ˈkævɪˌteɪt / (AmE), / ˈkævɪteɪt / (BrE)
to form cavities or bubbles in a liquid or substance, especially due to rapid changes in pressure

Examples

To cavitate in liquid
If the pump runs too fast, it may cavitate and lose efficiency.
The engineer explained how the submarine's propeller could cavitate under certain conditions.
When the pressure drops suddenly, water can cavitate and damage machinery.
If the pump is not properly maintained, it may cavitate and lose efficiency.
Ultrasound waves can cause water to cavitate, producing tiny bubbles that collapse rapidly.
The dentist warned that untreated decay could cause the tooth to cavitate over time.

Related Terms

  • synonym: bubble - to form bubbles in a liquid
  • synonym: erode - to wear away or form holes gradually
