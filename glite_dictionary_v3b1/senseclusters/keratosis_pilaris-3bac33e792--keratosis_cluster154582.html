keratosis pilaris

noun
skin condition with rough bumps
5.7 7.4
Pronunciation: /kə.rə.ˈtoʊ.sɪs pɪ.ˈlær.ɪs/ /; / /e.rə.ˈtoʊ.sɪs pɪ.ˈlær.ɪs/ (AmE), /kə.rə.ˈtəʊ.sɪs pɪ.ˈlær.ɪs/ /; / /e.rə.ˈtəʊ.sɪs pɪ.ˈlær.ɪs/ (BrE)
A common skin condition characterized by small, hard, rough bumps near hair follicles, typically on the arms and thighs.

Examples

Keratosis pilaris skin bumps
Many teenagers experience keratosis pilaris on their upper arms, especially during dry weather.
Moisturizing regularly can help reduce the appearance of keratosis pilaris.
The dermatologist diagnosed her with keratosis pilaris after examining the bumps on her arms.
