mercy

noun
leniency or compassion in justice
1.3 20/m
Pronunciation: / Ààm…ùÀêsi / (AmE), / Ààm…úÀêsi / (BrE)
kindness, compassion, or forgiveness shown to someone, especially when you have power over them or they are suffering

Examples

Show mercy to others
He appealed to the governor for mercy following his conviction.
The judge showed mercy by giving her a lighter sentence than expected.
The victim's family asked the court to grant mercy to the offender.
Her mercy was evident when she forgave him for his mistakes.
In times of war, mercy is often in short supply.
The judge showed mercy to the young defendant, giving him a second chance.
After the disaster, neighbors acted out of mercy to help those who had lost everything.
She showed great mercy toward the injured animal, nursing it back to health.
The organization extended mercy to the refugees by providing shelter and food.

Synonyms

  • clemency
    8.4 1.95/m
    clemency is an official act of leniency by an authority, while mercy is any kindness or forgiveness shown to someone suffering.
  • grace
    3.9 21/m
    grace is kindness or favor given freely, often in religious contexts, while mercy is kindness shown to those who suffer.
  • charity
    1.4 3.91/m
    charity is a forgiving, generous attitude toward others, but mercy often involves a concrete act of pardon or relief by someone in power.
  • compassion
    2.7 27/m
    compassion is a strong wish to help people who suffer, while mercy usually means forgiving or showing kindness from someone with power.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clemency - leniency granted by a person in authority, especially in sentencing
  • synonym: compassion - sympathetic pity and concern for others
  • antonym: cruelty - callous indifference to or pleasure in causing pain or suffering
