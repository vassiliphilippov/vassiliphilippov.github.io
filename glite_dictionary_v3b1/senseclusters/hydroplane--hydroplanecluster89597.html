hydroplane

verb
skim over water in a hydroplane
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˈhaɪdrəˌpleɪn / (AmE), / ˈhaɪdrəʊˌpleɪn / (BrE)
(of a boat or similar vehicle) to skim or glide over the surface of water at high speed.

Examples

To hydroplane over water
As soon as the hull lifted, we could feel the boat hydroplaning over the surface of the water.
The racing boat began to hydroplane as it reached full speed across the lake.
With a sudden burst of power, the vessel hydroplaned smoothly toward the finish line.

Related Terms

  • synonym: skid - to slide sideways, especially on a slippery surface
