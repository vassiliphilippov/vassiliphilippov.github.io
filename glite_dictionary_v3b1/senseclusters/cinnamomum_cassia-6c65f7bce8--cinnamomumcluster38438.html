Cinnamomum cassia

noun
species of Chinese cinnamon tree
Pronunciation: / sɪˌnæməˌnʊm ˈkæsiə / (AmE), / sɪˌnæmənəm ˈkæsiə / (BrE)
a species of Chinese tree with aromatic bark, widely used as a type of cinnamon, considered less desirable than Ceylon cinnamon

Examples

Cinnamomum cassia bark
Many traditional Chinese herbal medicines include extracts from Cinnamomum cassia.
The dried bark of Cinnamomum cassia is commonly sold as cinnamon in many grocery stores.
Unlike Ceylon cinnamon, Cinnamomum cassia has a stronger, more pungent flavor.

Related Terms

  • antonym: Ceylon cinnamon - cinnamon obtained from Cinnamomum verum, considered more desirable
