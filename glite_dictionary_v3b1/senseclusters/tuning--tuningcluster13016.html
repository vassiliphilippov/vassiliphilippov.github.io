tuning

noun
adjusting pitch or frequency to a standard
Pronunciation: / ˈtuːnɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈtjuːnɪŋ / (BrE)
The process of adjusting a musical instrument or electronic circuit so that it produces the correct pitch or operates at the desired frequency.

Examples

Tuning a guitar
He adjusted the tuning on his radio to get a clear signal.
Proper tuning is essential for the guitar to sound harmonious with other instruments.
The tuning of the piano took over an hour to complete.

Related Terms

  • antonym: detuning - the process of making an instrument or circuit less properly tuned
  • synonym: intonation - the accuracy of pitch in playing or singing
