tally-ho

interjection
fox-hunting call
9.9
Pronunciation: /ˈtæl.ɪ hoʊ/ (AmE), /ˈtæl.ɪ həʊ/ (BrE)
an exclamation used to announce the sighting of a target, especially in hunting, military, or aviation contexts

Examples

Tally-ho! Target spotted
The huntsman cried tally-ho as soon as the fox appeared in the clearing.
With a loud tally-ho, the riders urged their horses forward.
He shouted tally-ho in excitement, even though they were only playing a game.
The pilot shouted tally-ho as soon as he spotted the enemy aircraft on the horizon.
During the fox hunt, the leader cried tally-ho when the animal was seen running across the field.
Hearing the call of tally-ho, the team prepared to engage the approaching target.

Related Terms

  • synonym: look out - an exclamation warning of danger or drawing attention
  • synonym: yo-ho - a traditional shout used by sailors or hunters
