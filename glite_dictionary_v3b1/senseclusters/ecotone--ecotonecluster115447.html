ecotone

noun
zone between habitats
9.4
Pronunciation: / ˈiːkoʊˌtoʊn / (AmE), / ˈiːkəʊˌtəʊn / (BrE)
a transition area between two different ecological communities where species from both overlap and interact

Examples

Forest ecotone
The forest edge is an ecotone where woodland and grassland species can both be found.
Wetlands often act as an ecotone between rivers and dry land, supporting diverse wildlife.
Scientists studied the ecotone to understand how species adapt to changing environments.
The ecotone between the forest and the grassland supports a wide variety of plant and animal species.
Scientists studied the ecotone to understand how climate change affects both neighboring ecosystems.
Many rare species are found only in the ecotone where wetlands meet woodlands.
The ecotone between the desert and the forest is home to many unique species.
Scientists studied the ecotone to understand how animals adapt to changing environments.
A rich variety of plants can be found in the ecotone where the wetland meets the grassland.
