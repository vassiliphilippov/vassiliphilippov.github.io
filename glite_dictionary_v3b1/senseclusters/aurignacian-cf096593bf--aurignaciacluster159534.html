Aurignacian

noun
Upper Paleolithic culture
Pronunciation: / ˌɔːrɪɡˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌɔːrɪɡˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
the Upper Paleolithic culture in Europe and western Asia, known for early modern human tools, art, and ornaments

Examples

The Aurignacian period
Archaeologists discovered Aurignacian tools in a cave in southern France.
The Aurignacian period is famous for its early examples of cave art and figurines.
Researchers study Aurignacian sites to learn about the spread of modern humans in Europe.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: Gravettian - a later Upper Paleolithic culture in Europe known for distinctive stone tools
  • degree lower: Mousterian - a Middle Paleolithic culture associated with Neanderthals
