cohort

noun
group sharing a characteristic, often age
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊ.hɔrt / (AmE), / ˈkəʊ.hɔːt / (BrE)
A group of people who share a particular characteristic, often age, and are treated as a group for study purposes.

Examples

Study cohort
Each cohort in the survey represented a different decade of birth.
Researchers compared the health outcomes of the 1980s cohort to those born in the 1990s.
The study tracked a cohort of children born in 2010 over ten years.

Related Terms

  • synonym: generation - all people born and living at about the same time, regarded collectively
