contumacy

noun
stubborn resistance to authority
Pronunciation: / ˈkɑːn.tuˌmeɪ.si /; / ˈkɑːn.tʊˌmeɪ.si / (AmE), / ˈkɒn.tjʊ.mə.si / (BrE)
stubborn or willful refusal to obey authority, especially a court of law or legal order

Examples

Contumacy in court
His contumacy in defying the judge's orders resulted in additional charges against him.
The board was frustrated by the employee's persistent contumacy in refusing to follow company policies.
Throughout history, acts of contumacy have often led to significant social change.
A pattern of contumacy can severely damage one's standing in legal proceedings.
Her contumacy in ignoring the court summons resulted in additional legal penalties.
The defendant's persistent contumacy led the judge to issue a bench warrant for his arrest.

Related Terms

  • synonym: contempt of court - disobedience or disrespect towards a court's authority
  • synonym: insubordination - refusal to obey orders or authority
  • antonym: obedience - compliance with an order, request, or law
