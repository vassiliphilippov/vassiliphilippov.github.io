LWOP

noun
life sentence, no parole
9.9
Pronunciation: /el ˈdʌb.əl.juː oʊ piː/ (AmE), /el ˈdʌb.əl.juː əʊ piː/ (BrE)
a prison sentence of life without the possibility of parole; abbreviation of 'life without parole'

Examples

Sentenced to LWOP
The judge sentenced the defendant to LWOP for the series of violent crimes.
Many states reserve LWOP for the most serious offenses, such as murder.
Her lawyer argued against LWOP, hoping for a chance of parole in the future.

Related Terms

  • antonym: parole - the release of a prisoner before the end of their sentence under certain conditions
