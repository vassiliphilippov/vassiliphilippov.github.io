rose-tint perspective

noun
overly optimistic view of the past
4.9
Pronunciation: / ˈroʊz tɪnt pərˈspɛktɪv / (AmE), / ˈrəʊz tɪnt pəˈspɛktɪv / (BrE)
an excessively optimistic or idealized view of the past, overlooking any negatives

Examples

A rose-tint perspective on childhood
Her memoir takes a rose-tint perspective on childhood, ignoring the hardships her family faced.
It's tempting to adopt a rose-tint perspective when reminiscing about university days.
The film presents history with a rose-tint perspective, glossing over inconvenient truths.
