disperse

verb
cause to scatter or go apart
Pronunciation: / dɪˈspɝs /; / dɪˈspɜrs / (AmE), / dɪˈspɜːs / (BrE)
to scatter or spread things, people, or information over a wide area; to cause to break up and move apart

Examples

To disperse a crowd
A gust of wind caused the fog to disperse quickly across the field.
Police used loudspeakers to tell the crowd to disperse after the parade ended.
The professor asked the students to disperse and return to their dormitories.
He watched as she dispersed flower petals around the garden path.
Police used tear gas to disperse the crowd after the festival ended.
The wind began to disperse the seeds across the field.
It is important to disperse knowledge about public health measures during an outbreak.
Social media can rapidly disperse both facts and rumors to millions of people.
The press conference was held to disperse accurate information about the crisis.

Related Terms

  • antonym: assemble - to come together in a group
  • synonym: circulate - to pass or cause to pass from one person or place to another; to spread (information, news, etc.)
  • antonym: collect - to gather things together in one place
  • antonym: conceal - to keep from being known; to hide
  • antonym: gather - to bring together into a group or crowd
  • synonym: scatter - to throw or distribute things so that they cover an area
  • synonym: spread - to make widely known; to disseminate
