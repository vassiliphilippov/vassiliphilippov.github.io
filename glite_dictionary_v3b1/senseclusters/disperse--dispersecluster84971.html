Examples

To disperse a crowd
A gust of wind caused the fog to disperse quickly across the field.
Police used loudspeakers to tell the crowd to disperse after the parade ended.
The professor asked the students to disperse and return to their dormitories.
He watched as she dispersed flower petals around the garden path.

Social media can rapidly disperse both facts and rumors to millions of people.

Synonyms

  • scatter
    2.7 9/m
    scatter often means making people or animals run off quickly in different directions, while disperse means to break up and spread out.
  • spread out
    2.8 5/m
    spread out describes people or things moving apart over a wider area, while disperse focuses on scattering or breaking up.
  • scatter
    2.8 19/m
    scatter often means throwing or dropping items in an irregular pattern, while disperse stresses scattering widely or causing things to spread.
  • diffuse
    5.5 4/m
    diffuse means spreading something thinly so it covers a large area, while disperse means causing parts to move apart or scatter.
  • dissipate
    6.8 4/m
    dissipate implies fading or dispersing until something disappears, while disperse means to scatter or break up across an area.
  • break up
    3.4 5/m
    break up can mean causing a meeting or crowd to end, while disperse emphasizes scattering people or things across space.
  • break up
    2.7 4/m
    break up can mean splitting something into smaller pieces, while disperse means scattering those pieces or people across a wide area.
  • spread
    1.6 50/m
    spread often means becoming known or common among people, while disperse focuses on physically scattering or breaking a group apart.
  • distribute
    2.0 9/m
    distribute means giving out or sharing items to people, while disperse means scattering things widely without necessarily organizing who receives them.
  • disseminate
    6.8 7/m
    disseminate means actively spreading information to many people, while disperse can mean scattering people or things physically rather than sharing ideas.
  • circulate
    3.9 9/m
    circulate means passing information or documents among people repeatedly, while disperse usually means causing things or people to scatter and move apart.
  • broadcast
    3.0 4/m
    broadcast means making information publicly known to a large audience, while disperse usually means scattering items or people across an area physically.
  • propagate
    5.9 2/m
    propagate means actively promoting ideas or beliefs to many people, while disperse implies scattering objects or people without planned persuasion.
