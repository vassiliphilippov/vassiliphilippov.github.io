Byzantium

proper noun
ancient city on the Bosporus, later Constantinople
Pronunciation: / bɪˈzæn.ti.əm / (AmE), / bɪˈzæn.tɪ.əm / (BrE)
an ancient Greek city on the Bosporus, later known as Constantinople and now Istanbul

Examples

Ancient city Byzantium
Historians study the art and architecture of Byzantium to learn about early Eastern Roman civilization.
In 330 AD, Emperor Constantine made Byzantium the capital of the Roman Empire, renaming it Constantinople.
The ancient city of Byzantium was strategically located on the Bosporus strait.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Constantinople - the city formerly known as Byzantium, later Istanbul
