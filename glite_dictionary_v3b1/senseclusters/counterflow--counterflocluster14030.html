counterflow

verb
to flow in opposition
7.3
Pronunciation: /ˈkaʊn.tər.floʊ/ (AmE), /ˈkaʊn.tə.fləʊ/ (BrE)
to move or cause to move in the opposite direction to another flow, especially in a system or process.

Examples

Hot air counterflows
In the heat exchanger, hot air counterflows against the incoming cold air to maximize efficiency.
The engineers designed the system so that water would counterflow to the direction of the gas.
During the experiment, two different liquids counterflowed through the narrow tubes for better mixing.

Related Terms

  • synonym: oppose - to move or act in the opposite direction
  • antonym: parallel - to move in the same direction alongside something
