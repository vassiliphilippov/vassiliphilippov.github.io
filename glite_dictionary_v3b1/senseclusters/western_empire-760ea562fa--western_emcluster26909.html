Western Empire

noun
western part of the Roman Empire (395–476 CE)
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛstərn ˈɛmˌpaɪər / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ˈɛmpaɪə / (BrE)
the western part of the Roman Empire after its division in 395 CE, which lasted until its fall in 476 CE

Examples

The Western Empire fall
After the split of the Roman Empire, the Western Empire struggled to maintain its borders against invading tribes.
Many art and religious traditions from the Western Empire influenced the development of Western Europe.
The fall of the Western Empire in 476 marked the end of ancient Rome and the start of the Middle Ages.
