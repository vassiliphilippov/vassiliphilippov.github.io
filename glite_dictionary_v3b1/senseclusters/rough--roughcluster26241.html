rough

adjective
harsh or grating in sound
Pronunciation: / rʌf / (AmE), / rʌf / (BrE)
not smooth, even, or regular; having an uneven surface or quality (can refer to surfaces, sounds, journeys, finishes, or edges)

Examples

Rough surface
He spoke with a rough tone that made everyone uneasy.
Her rough laughter echoed through the quiet hall, sounding almost coarse.
The teacher's rough voice startled the students into silence.
Some towels are soft, but others feel quite rough on your skin.
The cat’s tongue felt rough against my hand.
We walked carefully over the rough ground near the construction site.
Many oak species are known for their rough leaf margins with deep lobes and scallops.
The rough outline of the leaf helps some plants deter herbivores.
The holly leaf is easily recognized by its rough edges that are sharply pointed and jagged.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crude - not carefully or expertly made
  • antonym: finished - worked with care, having a smooth or complete surface
  • synonym: harsh - (of a sound) unpleasantly rough or jarring to the senses
  • antonym: polished - made smooth or shiny by polishing; finished
  • antonym: smooth - having an even and regular surface; free from roughness
  • synonym: uncut - not shaped or trimmed; remaining in its original form
