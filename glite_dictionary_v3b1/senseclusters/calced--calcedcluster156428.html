calced

adjective
wearing shoes (religious orders)
9.1 9.3
Pronunciation: /kælst/ (AmE), /kælst/ (BrE)
(of members of certain religious orders) wearing shoes, as opposed to going barefoot or wearing sandals.

Examples

Calced religious members
Historically, debates arose between calced and discalced branches within some monastic communities.
Members of the calced Carmelite order wore shoes during their religious observances.
Unlike their discalced counterparts, the calced friars were permitted to wear footwear.
