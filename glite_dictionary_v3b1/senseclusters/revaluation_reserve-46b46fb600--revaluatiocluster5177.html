revaluation reserve

noun
account showing surplus from asset revaluation
6.9 6.4
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌvæl.juˈeɪ.ʃən rɪˈzɝːv / (AmE), / ˌriːˌvæl.juˈeɪ.ʃən rɪˈzɜːv / (BrE)
an account in a company's financial records that shows any surplus resulting from the revaluation of its assets above their original cost.

Examples

The revaluation reserve account
Auditors recommended an annual review of the revaluation reserve to ensure accurate reporting.
Changes in asset values are reflected in the revaluation reserve rather than directly in the profit and loss account.
The company transferred the surplus from the new property valuation into the revaluation reserve.

Related Terms

  • synonym: capital reserve - an account showing funds set aside for specific purposes, often unrelated to operational profits
