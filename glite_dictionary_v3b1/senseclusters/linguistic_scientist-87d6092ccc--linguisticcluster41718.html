linguistic scientist

noun
specialist in the science of language
Pronunciation: / lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪk ˈsaɪəntɪst / (AmE), / lɪŋˈɡwɪstɪk ˈsaɪəntɪst / (BrE)
A person who specializes in the scientific study of language; a linguist.

Examples

A linguistic scientist studies language
As a linguistic scientist, he spends much of his time analyzing ancient texts.
Many linguistic scientists collaborate with computer programmers to develop new language processing tools.
The linguistic scientist presented her findings at the international conference on language acquisition.
