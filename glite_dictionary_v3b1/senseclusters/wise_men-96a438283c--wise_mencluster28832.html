Wise Men

noun
the Magi who visited the infant Jesus
Pronunciation: / ˈwaɪz men / (AmE), / ˈwaɪz men / (BrE)
(in the New Testament) the sages, also known as the Magi, who visited the infant Jesus bearing gifts of gold, frankincense, and myrrh, after being guided by a star.

Examples

The Wise Men followed star
According to tradition, the Wise Men followed a star to Bethlehem.
Every Christmas, churches reenact the arrival of the Wise Men at the nativity scene.
The gifts brought by the Wise Men are symbolic in Christian teachings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Magi - another name for the Wise Men in the Nativity story
