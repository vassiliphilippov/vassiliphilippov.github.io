heed

verb
pay close attention to
5.0 6/m
Pronunciation: / hid / (AmE), / hiÀêd / (BrE)
to pay careful attention to something, especially advice or a warning

Examples

To heed a warning
If you heed the warning signs, you can avoid many problems.
She always heeds her parents' guidance before making important decisions.
You should heed your doctor's advice when it comes to your health.

Synonyms

  • take heed
    6.2 0.7/m
    take heed is a short phrase people use in warnings or advice, while heed is the single-word verb used more generally.
  • give heed
    6.4 0.08/m
    give heed is a formal way to tell someone to notice advice, while heed is the common verb for careful attention.
  • pay attention
    1.6 40/m
    pay attention means to focus your mind or watch someone closely, while heed especially means follow or act on advice or warnings.
  • listen
    1.0 130/m
    listen means paying attention by ear to words or warnings, while heed means noticing and acting on advice.
  • mind
    2.1 8/m
    mind often means be careful or avoid something, while heed means pay close attention to advice or a warning.
  • take note
    3.4 5/m
    take note means notice something to remember or record it, while heed means listen and act on important advice or warnings.
