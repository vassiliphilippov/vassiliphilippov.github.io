diphtheria

noun
acute contagious bacterial disease
6.7
Pronunciation: / dɪfˈθɪriə / (AmE), / dɪfˈθɪəriə / (BrE)
an acute, highly contagious bacterial disease causing inflammation of the mucous membranes, especially the throat, often leading to difficulty in breathing

Examples

Diphtheria infection
Because diphtheria is highly contagious, early diagnosis is essential to prevent its spread.
The outbreak of diphtheria in the village prompted immediate vaccination campaigns.
Untreated diphtheria can cause severe complications such as heart and nerve damage.
