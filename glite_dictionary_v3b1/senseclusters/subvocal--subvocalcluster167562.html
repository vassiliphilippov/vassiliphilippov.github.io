subvocal

adjective
silent or internal speech
Pronunciation: / sʌbˈvoʊkəl / (AmE), / sʌbˈvəʊkəl / (BrE)
relating to silent or barely audible speech, especially when spoken internally or under one's breath

Examples

Subvocal repetition
During the exam, he used subvocal repetition to help memorize the formulas.
Some people move their lips in a subvocal way when reading silently.
Researchers studied the effects of subvocal speech on memory retention.

Related Terms

  • antonym: audible - able to be heard
  • synonym: silent - not making any sound
