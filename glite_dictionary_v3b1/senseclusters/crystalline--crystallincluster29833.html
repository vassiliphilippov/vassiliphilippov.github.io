crystalline

adjective
extremely clear or transparent
Pronunciation: / ˈkrɪstəˌlaɪn / (AmE), / ˈkrɪstəlaɪn / (BrE)
extremely clear or transparent, like crystal; very clear or sharply defined (literal or figurative)

Examples

Crystalline water
She admired the crystalline structure of the snowflakes glittering in the sunlight.
The mountain lake was so crystalline that the fish were visible beneath the surface.
Through the crystalline glass, they could see the garden in perfect detail.
Her writing is praised for its crystalline clarity and vivid imagery.
The photograph captured the crystalline edges of the snowflake with stunning precision.
Under the microscope, the mineral's crystalline structure appeared perfectly outlined.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clear - easy to perceive, understand, or interpret
  • antonym: cloudy - not transparent; difficult to see through
  • synonym: limpid - (of liquid) completely clear and transparent
  • antonym: opaque - not able to be seen through; not transparent
  • synonym: pellucid - transparently clear; easily understandable
  • antonym: vague - not clearly or sharply defined
