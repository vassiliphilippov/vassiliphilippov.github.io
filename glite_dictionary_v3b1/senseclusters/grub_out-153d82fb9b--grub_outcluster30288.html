grub out

verb
remove by digging up, especially roots
7.3
Pronunciation: / ɡrʌb aʊt / (AmE), / ɡrʌb aʊt / (BrE)
to dig up or remove something from the ground, especially roots or stumps, by digging

Examples

To grub out roots
After hours of effort, we finally grubbed out the stubborn shrub from the garden.
The workers were hired to grub out the stumps left in the field.
They had to grub out all the old tree roots before planting new ones.
