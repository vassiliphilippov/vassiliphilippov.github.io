Anselm of Canterbury

(c. 1033–1109) An Italian Benedictine monk and philosopher who became Archbishop of Canterbury, known as a founder of scholasticism and for formulating an influential argument for the existence of God.

Examples

The writings of Anselm of Canterbury remain influential in Christian theology today.
