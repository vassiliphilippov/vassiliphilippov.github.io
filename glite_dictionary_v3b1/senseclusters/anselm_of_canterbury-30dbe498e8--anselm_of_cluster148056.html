Anselm of Canterbury

proper noun
archbishop and philosopher (1033–1109)
Pronunciation: / ˈæn.sɛlm əv ˈkæntərˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈæn.sɛlm əv ˈkæntəbri / (BrE)
(c. 1033–1109) An Italian Benedictine monk and philosopher who became Archbishop of Canterbury, known as a founder of scholasticism and for formulating an influential argument for the existence of God.

Examples

Anselm of Canterbury philosopher
Anselm of Canterbury is remembered for developing the ontological argument for the existence of God.
Many scholars consider Anselm of Canterbury one of the key figures in medieval philosophy.
The writings of Anselm of Canterbury remain influential in Christian theology today.
