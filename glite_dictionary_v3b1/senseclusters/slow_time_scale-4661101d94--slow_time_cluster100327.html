slow time scale

noun
simulation/data processing time stretching
3.8 6.3
Pronunciation: /sloʊ taɪm skeɪl/ (AmE), /sləʊ taɪm skeɪl/ (BrE)
In simulation or data processing, a time scale where the elapsed or modeled time is stretched, typically with a time-scale factor greater than one, so processes appear to occur more slowly than real-time.

Examples

A slow time scale in simulation
In climate modeling, experiments often employ a slow time scale to study effects unfolding over decades.
The simulation was run on a slow time scale to analyze long-term system behavior.
Using a slow time scale, the researchers were able to observe gradual changes more easily.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fast time scale - time scale in simulation with a time-scale factor less than one, making events appear faster than real time
