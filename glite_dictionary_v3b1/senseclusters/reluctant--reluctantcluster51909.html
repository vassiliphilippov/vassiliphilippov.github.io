Synonyms

  • unwilling
    3.4 6/m
    unwilling is firm refusal or lack of desire to act, while reluctant shows doubt and hesitates before doing something.
  • loath
    6.9 0.6/m
    loath is a formal, often old-fashioned word meaning unwilling, while reluctant is more common and shows hesitation.
  • disinclined
    6.4 0.2/m
    disinclined means not eager, often from mild dislike, while reluctant covers general hesitation or unwillingness.
  • hesitant
    3.2 3/m
    hesitant acts slowly or pauses from doubt, while reluctant feels unwilling and resists doing something.
  • indisposed
    6.8 0.00/m
    indisposed usually means slightly ill or unwilling, while reluctant focuses on hesitation and lack of eagerness.
