time out

noun
short pause for rest or other activity
4.8
Pronunciation: / taɪm aʊt / (AmE), / taɪm aʊt / (BrE)
a short break or pause from an activity, usually to rest or recover

Examples

Take a time out
After working for three hours, she took a time out to stretch and relax.
I need a quick time out before we continue this meeting.
The coach called a time out to discuss tactics with the players.
After a long meeting, the team took a time out to recharge.
Sometimes it's important to take a time out from your busy schedule.
The teacher gave the children a time out to calm down after the argument.
