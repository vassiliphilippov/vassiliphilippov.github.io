vasoactive

adjective
affecting blood vessels
8.6
Pronunciation: / ˌveɪzoʊˈæktɪv / (AmE), / ˌveɪzəʊˈæktɪv / (BrE)
affecting the diameter or tone of blood vessels, especially by causing them to constrict or dilate

Examples

Vasoactive drugs
The doctor prescribed a vasoactive medication to help regulate the patient's blood pressure.
Some vasoactive substances can quickly change how blood flows through the body.
Researchers are studying new vasoactive drugs to treat heart conditions.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: vasoconstrictor - a substance that causes blood vessels to narrow
  • degree higher: vasodilator - a substance that causes blood vessels to widen
