fool's errand

noun
pointless or hopeless task
Pronunciation: / fulz ˈɛrənd /; / ˈfulz ˌɛrənd / (AmE), / fuːlz ˈɛrənd /; / ˈfuːlz ˌɛrənd / (BrE)
a task or journey that is pointless, futile, or has no chance of success

Examples

A fool's errand
Many believed the expedition was a fool's errand, but they were determined to try.
They sent him on a fool's errand to find a tool that didn't exist.
Trying to convince her to change her mind was a fool's errand.
Being asked to fix the old computer was a fool's errand—it was beyond repair.
He quickly realized that arguing with the stubborn manager was a fool's errand.
Looking for her lost earring in the sand felt like a fool's errand.
