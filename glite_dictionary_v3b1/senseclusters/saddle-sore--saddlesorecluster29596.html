saddle-sore

adjective
sore after riding a horse or bicycle
Pronunciation: / ˈsædəlˌsɔr / (AmE), / ˈsæd(ə)lˌsɔː / (BrE)
(of a person) experiencing soreness or discomfort after riding a horse or bicycle, especially due to chafing or pressure from the saddle.

Examples

Saddle-sore cyclist
After a long day on the trail, I was completely saddle-sore and needed a break.
Cyclists sometimes become saddle-sore on long-distance rides.
New riders often find themselves saddle-sore after their first lesson.

  • synonym: sore - feeling physical pain or discomfort
