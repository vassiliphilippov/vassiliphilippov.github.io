western poison oak

noun
poisonous shrub of Pacific North America
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛstərn ˈpɔɪzən oʊk / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ˈpɔɪzən əʊk / (BrE)
a poisonous shrub native to the Pacific coast of North America, known for causing skin rashes upon contact

Examples

The western poison oak plant
After brushing against western poison oak, she developed an itchy, red rash on her arm.
Hikers are advised to avoid contact with western poison oak along the coastal trails.
The leaves of western poison oak resemble those of true oak but cause severe allergic reactions.

Related Terms

  • synonym: poison ivy - another North American plant that causes skin irritation
