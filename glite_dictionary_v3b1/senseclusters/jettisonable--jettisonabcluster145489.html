jettisonable

adjective
able to be discarded
7.9
Pronunciation: / ˈdʒɛtɪsənəbl / (AmE), / ˈdʒɛtɪsənəbl / (BrE)
able to be abandoned, omitted, or given up, especially when no longer needed or useful.

Examples

Jettisonable rules
The committee decided that several outdated policies were jettisonable during the reorganization process.
In times of crisis, only nonessential and jettisonable expenses are cut from the budget.
He reviewed the plan to identify which parts were truly jettisonable and which were vital.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dispensable - able to be done without or not necessary
  • antonym: essential - absolutely necessary or extremely important
