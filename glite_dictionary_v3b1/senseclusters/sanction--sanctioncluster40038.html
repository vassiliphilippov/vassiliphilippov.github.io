sanction

noun
official permission or approval
6.9
Pronunciation: / ˈsæŋkʃən /; / ˈsæŋk.ʃən / (AmE), / ˈsaŋkʃən /; / ˈsaŋk.ʃən / (BrE)
official permission or approval for an action, especially from an authority

Examples

Government sanction
The company needed government sanction to begin construction on the new highway.
The project received official sanction from the city council last month.
Without parental sanction, students are not allowed to leave the school premises.
The new policy cannot be implemented without the sanction of the board.
The project moved forward only after receiving governmental sanction.
They sought official sanction before releasing the documents to the public.
He completed the project only after receiving official sanction from the board.
The committee gave its sanction to the new policy after lengthy debate.
Without the government’s sanction, the building cannot proceed.

  • synonym: authorization - official permission or power to do something
  • antonym: disapproval - possession or expression of an unfavorable opinion
  • synonym: endorsement - an act of giving one's public approval or support
  • antonym: prohibition - an official order that forbids something
