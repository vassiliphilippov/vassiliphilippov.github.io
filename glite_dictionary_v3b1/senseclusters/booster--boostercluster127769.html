booster

noun
extra vaccine or medication dose
3.7 1.25/m
Pronunciation: / ÀàbuÀêst…ôr / (AmE), / ÀàbuÀêst…ô / (BrE)
an extra dose of a vaccine or other medication given after the initial dose to increase or renew effectiveness

Examples

Vaccine booster
After receiving her initial vaccine, Emily scheduled her booster for six months later.
Children often receive a booster when starting school to maintain immunity.
Health officials recommended a booster to ensure continued protection against the virus.

Synonyms

  • booster dose
    3.9 0.07/m
    booster dose is a formal term for an additional vaccine or medication amount after the initial series, while booster is a more general noun.
  • booster shot
    2.9 0.12/m
    booster shot is the common informal phrase for an extra vaccine dose after the initial shots, while booster is the general noun.
