defibrillator

noun
device for restoring heart rhythm
5.4 3.9
Pronunciation: /diː.ˈfɪb.rɪ.leɪ.tər/ /; / /dɪ.ˈfɪb.rɪ.leɪ.tər/ (AmE), /diː.ˈfɪb.rɪ.leɪ.tə/ /; / /dɪ.ˈfɪb.rɪ.leɪ.tə/ (BrE)
a device that delivers a controlled electric shock to the heart to restore normal rhythm, especially during cardiac arrest

Examples

A defibrillator device
Many public buildings now have a defibrillator available in case of sudden cardiac arrest.
The hospital installed a new defibrillator in the emergency room for quick access during cardiac emergencies.
When the patient collapsed, a nurse quickly used the defibrillator to try to restart his heart.
