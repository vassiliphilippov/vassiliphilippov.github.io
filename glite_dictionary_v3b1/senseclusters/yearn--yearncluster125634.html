yearn

verb
desire strongly or persistently
Pronunciation: / jɝːn /; / jɝn / (AmE), / jɜːn / (BrE)
to have a strong, persistent desire or longing for something, especially when it is difficult to have or is absent

Examples

To yearn for freedom
As the years passed, he began to yearn for adventure beyond his small town.
Many people yearn for a sense of purpose in their lives.
She would often yearn for her childhood home during difficult times.
After moving abroad, she would often yearn for the comforts of home.
Despite her busy life, she sometimes yearned for quiet moments of solitude.
He could not help but yearn for the days of his youth.

Related Terms

  • synonym: crave - to feel a powerful desire for something
  • synonym: desire - to want something strongly
  • antonym: loathe - to feel intense dislike or aversion to
  • synonym: long - to have a strong desire for something
