swell

verb
become filled with strong emotion
3.9
Pronunciation: / swɛl / (AmE), / swɛl / (BrE)
to become filled with or rise up with a strong emotion such as pride, anger, or excitement

Examples

To swell with pride
He began to swell with anger as the accusations continued.
She could not help but swell with pride when she saw her daughter's performance.
The team swelled with confidence after their big win.
A sense of pride began to swell within her as she listened to the announcement.
Dark clouds gathered and smoke swelled from the burning building.
His emotions swell whenever he hears that song, bringing back old memories.
As the music played, emotions began to swell inside her.
Tears swelled in his eyes as he listened to the story.
The river threatens to swell after the heavy rainfall.

Related Terms

  • antonym: deflate - to become less proud or confident
  • synonym: inflate - to become filled with pride or self-importance
  • synonym: well up - (of feelings) to rise to the surface
