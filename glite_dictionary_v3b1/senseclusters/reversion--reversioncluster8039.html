reversion

noun
property interest returning after an estate ends
7.6
Pronunciation: / rɪˈvɜːrʒən / (AmE), / rɪˈvɜːʃən / (BrE)
(Law) An interest in property that reverts to the original owner or their heirs after the expiration of a particular estate, such as upon the grantee's death.

Examples

Property reversion
Law students studied reversion as part of their course on property rights.
The original owner's reversion gives them a future interest in the estate.
Under the terms of the trust, the property will fall into reversion after the tenant's death.

Related Terms

  • synonym: remainder - (Law) an interest in property that passes to a third party on the expiration of a particular estate
