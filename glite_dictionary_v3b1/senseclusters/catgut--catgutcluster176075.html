catgut

noun
strong cord from animal intestines
6.7 8.3
Pronunciation: /ˈkæt.gʌt/ /; / /ˈkæt.gət/ (AmE), /ˈkæt.gʌt/ /; / /ˈkæt.gət/ (BrE)
a strong cord made from the intestines of sheep or other animals, formerly used in surgery and for stringing musical instruments

Examples

Strong catgut cord
Modern medicine has largely replaced catgut with synthetic materials for surgical sutures.
Surgeons once used catgut to stitch wounds because it is absorbed by the body.
The violin's strings were traditionally made of catgut, not actual cat’s gut.
