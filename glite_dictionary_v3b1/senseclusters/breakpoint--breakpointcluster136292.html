breakpoint

noun
DNA break location
8.4
Pronunciation: / ˈbreɪkˌpɔɪnt / (AmE), / ˈbreɪkˌpɔɪnt / (BrE)
a specific location on a chromosome or DNA where a break or structural change occurs, often related to genetic disorders

Examples

DNA breakpoint
Scientists discovered a breakpoint in the patient's DNA that explained the genetic disorder.
The analysis revealed a breakpoint where the chromosome had split and rearranged.
Identifying the exact breakpoint is crucial for understanding certain inherited diseases.

Related Terms

  • synonym: mutation - a change in the DNA sequence of a gene
  • synonym: recombination - the process by which genetic material is rearranged
