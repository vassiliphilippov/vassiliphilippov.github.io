cutwater

noun
bridge pier projection
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌtˌwɔtər / (AmE), / ˈkʌtˌwɔːtə / (BrE)
a wedge-shaped structure on the upstream side of a bridge pier that splits and diverts the flow of water

Examples

Bridge cutwater
The cutwater helped reduce the pressure of the river against the bridge during heavy rains.
Engineers inspected the stone cutwater for signs of erosion after the flood.
A well-designed cutwater can extend the lifespan of a bridge by minimizing water damage.

Related Terms

  • synonym: buttress - a projecting support built against a wall
