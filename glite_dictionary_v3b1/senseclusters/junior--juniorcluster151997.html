junior

adjective
younger or lower in rank/status
2.4 5.30/m
Pronunciation: / Ààd íuÀêni…ö / (AmE), / Ààd íuÀêni…ô / (BrE)
(of a person) younger or having a lower rank, status, or length of service than another; subordinate.

Examples

Junior officer
Several junior officers reported to the captain for instructions.
She is the most talented junior member of the design team.
Though he was junior to me in the company, his ideas were always valued.

Synonyms

  • junior-grade
    4.1 0.02/m
    junior-grade describes a formal lower rank within an organization, while junior also means younger or less senior in status.
  • lower-ranking
    3.8 0.13/m
    lower-ranking refers to a position with less status in a hierarchy, while junior often adds the idea of being younger or less senior.
  • subordinate
    4.8 4.46/m
    subordinate is used for someone under another's authority, while junior focuses on lower rank or being younger.

Related Terms

  • antonym: senior - older or higher in rank or status
