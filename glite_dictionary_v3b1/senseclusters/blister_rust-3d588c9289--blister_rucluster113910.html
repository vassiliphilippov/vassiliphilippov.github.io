blister rust

noun
a fungal disease affecting pines
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˈblɪstɚ ˌrʌst /; / ˈblɪstər rʌst / (AmE), / ˈblɪstə ˌrʌst /; / ˈblɪstə rʌst / (BrE)
a disease of pine trees caused by a fungus (genus Cronartium), marked by blisters and cankers on the bark

Examples

Blister rust disease
Foresters monitor trees closely for signs of blister rust, which include characteristic swellings and cankers.
The spread of blister rust is often facilitated by cool, moist weather conditions.
White pine forests have suffered significant losses due to blister rust infestations over the last century.
Efforts to control blister rust often involve removing nearby currant bushes.
The presence of blister rust can be identified by orange blisters on the pine's bark.
White pine forests have been severely affected by blister rust in recent decades.
