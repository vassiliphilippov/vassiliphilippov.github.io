caul

noun
membrane covering newborn's head at birth
Pronunciation: / kɔl / (AmE), / kɔːl / (BrE)
a thin, inner membrane covering the head of a newborn at birth, especially when still partly enclosed in the amniotic sac

Examples

Baby born with caul
Some cultures consider a caul at birth to be a sign of good luck.
The baby was born with a caul covering his head, which the doctor carefully removed.
The midwife explained that a caul is a rare but harmless birth occurrence.
