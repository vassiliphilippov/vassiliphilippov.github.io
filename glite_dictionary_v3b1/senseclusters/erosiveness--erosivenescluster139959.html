erosiveness

noun
quality of causing erosion
6.9 7.0
Pronunciation: /ɪ.ˈroʊ.sɪv.nəs/ /; / /ə.ˈroʊ.sɪv.nəs/ (AmE), /ɪ.ˈrəʊ.sɪv.nəs/ /; / /ə.ˈrəʊ.sɪv.nəs/ (BrE)
The quality or state of causing or tending to wear away or erode by gradual action.

Examples

The erosiveness of water
Reducing the erosiveness of water runoff is essential for protecting agricultural land from degradation.
Scientists measure the erosiveness of different soil types to understand their vulnerability to rainfall.
The erosiveness of ocean waves has dramatically altered the coastline over the past century.
