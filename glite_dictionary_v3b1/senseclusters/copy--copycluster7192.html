copy

verb
write or reproduce exactly
1.0 17/m
Pronunciation: / Ààk…ëÀêpi / (AmE), / Ààk…ípi / (BrE)
to make an exact or close reproduction of something, such as a document, object, or text

Examples

To copy the text
Please copy all the answers exactly as they appear on the board.
She copied his notes word for word during the lecture.
The teacher asked us to copy the poem into our notebooks.
It is illegal to copy software without permission.
Scientists can copy DNA in the laboratory for research purposes.
She copied the file to a different folder on her computer.
Could you copy this document for me before the meeting?
Many artists copy famous paintings as a way to learn technique.
She copied the recipe from her grandmother's old cookbook.

Synonyms

  • duplicate
    3.5 6.29/m
    duplicate means making an exact same item or doing something again in the same way, while copy refers to making reproductions of documents, objects, or text.
  • replicate
    4.9 6.03/m
    replicate means to make an exact duplicate, often in a lab or technical test, while copy is the general everyday word for making reproductions.
  • reproduce
    4.0 6.08/m
    reproduce means creating something that closely resembles the original, often with care, while copy is the common act of making duplicates.
  • photocopy
    2.3 0.94/m
    photocopy means making a paper image with a machine that uses light, while copy is the general act of making reproductions by any method.
  • xerox
    4.8 0.19/m
    xerox means making paper images with a well known photocopier brand, often used as a verb, while copy is the neutral, general word for making reproductions.
