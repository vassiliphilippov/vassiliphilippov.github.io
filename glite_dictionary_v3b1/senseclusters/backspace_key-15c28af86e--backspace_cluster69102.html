backspace key

noun
keyboard/typewriter key for moving cursor backward
Pronunciation: / ˈbækˌspeɪs ki / (AmE), / ˈbækˌspeɪs kiː / (BrE)
the key on a keyboard or typewriter that moves the cursor or carriage one position backward, usually to erase the last character typed

Examples

The backspace key on keyboard
He accidentally hit the backspace key and deleted the last word he had typed.
Most keyboards have a backspace key in the upper right corner above the Enter key.
To correct a mistake, simply press the backspace key to erase the unwanted letter.

Related Terms

  • synonym: delete key - key used to remove text after the cursor
