mutualist

noun
participant in mutual benefit
6.7
Pronunciation: /ˈmjuː.tʃu.əl.ɪst/ /; / /ˈmjuː.tʃə.lɪst/ (AmE), /ˈmjuː.tʃu.əl.ɪst/ /; / /ˈmjuː.tʃə.lɪst/ (BrE)
an organism or person that participates in a relationship where both parties benefit

Examples

A bee mutualist
A bee is a classic mutualist because it pollinates flowers while collecting nectar.
In the forest, the fungus acts as a mutualist by helping trees absorb nutrients.
Some people see themselves as mutualists in community projects, ensuring everyone benefits.

Related Terms

  • antonym: parasite - an organism that lives on or in another and benefits at the host's expense
  • synonym: symbiont - an organism living in close association with another, often benefiting both
