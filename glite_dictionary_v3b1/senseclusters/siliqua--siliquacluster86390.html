siliqua

noun
narrow, elongated seed capsule (botany)
5.5 9.8
Pronunciation: / ˈsɪlɪkwə / (AmE), / ˈsɪlᵻkwə / (BrE)
A narrow, elongated seed capsule characteristic of plants in the mustard family (Cruciferae), which splits open when ripe to release seeds.

Examples

A siliqua seed pod
A siliqua differs from a silicle by being much longer than it is wide.
Brassicaceae family members often produce seeds in a long, slender siliqua.
The siliqua of the mustard plant splits open to scatter seeds when it matures.
