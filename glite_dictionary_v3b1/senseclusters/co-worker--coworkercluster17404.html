co-worker

noun
a person one works with
1.8 0.01/m
Pronunciation: / Ààko äÀåw…úrk…ôr / (AmE), / Ààk…ô äÀåw…úÀêk…ô / (BrE)
A person with whom one works, typically someone at the same level or in a similar position within an organization.

Examples

A co-worker at office
Her co-worker helped her resolve a tricky problem at the office.
I discussed the project with my co-worker before presenting it to the team.
Most co-workers try to support each other during busy times.

Synonyms

  • colleague
    2.3 78/m
    colleague is often a professional, formal term for people in the same job, while co-worker is a general work companion.
  • workmate
    2.2 0.11/m
    workmate is a casual British word for someone at the same workplace, while co-worker is neutral and used in many varieties.
  • fellow worker
    2.0 0.44/m
    fellow worker is an older or formal phrase for someone who works with you, while co-worker is the common modern term.
  • officemate
    3.5 0.11/m
    officemate means someone who shares your office or desk, while co-worker can be any person you work with in the organization.
