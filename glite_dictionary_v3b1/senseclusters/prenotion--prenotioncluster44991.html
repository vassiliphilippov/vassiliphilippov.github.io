prenotion

noun
preconceived idea
9.3
Pronunciation: / priːˈnoʊʃən / (AmE), / priːˈnəʊʃən / (BrE)
an idea or impression formed before having actual knowledge or experience of something

Examples

A prenotion about something
She entered the meeting with a prenotion about the project's difficulty.
It is important to challenge any prenotion you have before judging a situation.
His prenotion of the city changed completely after his visit.

Related Terms

  • synonym: impression - an idea or feeling about something, often formed without full knowledge
  • synonym: preconception - an opinion formed before having evidence or experience
  • synonym: prejudice - a preconceived opinion not based on reason or experience
