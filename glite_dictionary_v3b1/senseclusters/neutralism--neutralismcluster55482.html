neutralism

noun
policy of neutrality in international affairs
Pronunciation: / ˈnuːtrəˌlɪzəm / (AmE), / ˈnjuːtrəˌlɪzəm / (BrE)
a policy or attitude of remaining neutral, especially in international affairs by not aligning with any major power blocs

Examples

Neutralism policy
Critics argued that neutralism limited the nation's influence on the global stage.
Some leaders advocated for neutralism to avoid entanglement in international conflicts.
The country's foreign policy was based on neutralism during the Cold War era.

  • synonym: neutrality - the state of not supporting either side in a conflict
