sunset

verb
retire or discontinue (a product, service, or policy)
7.2
Pronunciation: /ˈsʌn.set/ /; / /ˈsʌn.sət/ (AmE), /ˈsʌn.set/ /; / /ˈsʌn.sət/ (BrE)
to discontinue or phase out a product, service, or policy, usually over a set period

Examples

To sunset a product
The company decided to sunset its legacy API next quarter.
After customer complaints, the team began sunsetting outdated features to simplify the interface.
They sunset the old subscription plan and offered users a migration path.
The company is sunsetting its older software next year to focus on new technology.
After careful review, the board decided to sunset the outdated policy by the end of the year.
Many customers were notified that the service would be sunsetted in the coming months.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decommission - to take something, such as equipment or a facility, out of active service
  • antonym: launch - to introduce a new product or service
  • synonym: phase out - to end or discontinue something gradually over time
  • synonym: retire - to withdraw something from use, service, or circulation
