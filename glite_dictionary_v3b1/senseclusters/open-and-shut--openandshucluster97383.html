open-and-shut

adjective
easy to decide or solve; obvious
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊpən ən ˈʃʌt / (AmE), / ˌəʊpən ən ˈʃʌt / (BrE)
(of a problem, case, or situation) so clear or obvious that it can be easily decided or solved.

Examples

An open-and-shut case
It seemed like an open-and-shut problem, but complications soon arose.
The evidence made it an open-and-shut case for the prosecution.
The manager described the decision as open-and-shut and moved on.

Related Terms

  • antonym: ambiguous - having more than one possible interpretation; not clear
  • synonym: clear-cut - obvious or sharply defined, leaving no room for doubt
