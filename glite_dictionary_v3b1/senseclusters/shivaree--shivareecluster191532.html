shivaree

noun
mock serenade for newlyweds with noisy celebration
8.7
Pronunciation: / ˌʃɪvəˈri /; / ʃɪvəˈriː / (AmE), / ˌʃɪvəˈri /; / ʃɪvəˈriː / (BrE)
A noisy, boisterous celebration or serenade, especially one using pots, pans, and other implements, performed as a mock serenade for a newly married couple.

Examples

A noisy shivaree
After the wedding, friends and family planned a shivaree to celebrate the newlyweds.
The clatter and laughter during the shivaree could be heard all over town.
The entire village gathered outside the couple’s house to give them a traditional shivaree.
Our grandparents often told stories about participating in a chivaree with pots and pans.
The newlyweds were surprised by a traditional chivaree late that night.
The village gathered in front of the house to give the couple a loud chivaree after their wedding.

Related Terms
