hallucination

noun
mistaken belief or idea
1.0
Pronunciation: / həˌluːsɪˈneɪʃən / (AmE), / həˌluːsɪˈneɪʃən / (BrE)
A belief or idea that is mistaken or not based on reality.

Examples

A hopeful hallucination
His hope of immediate success was merely a hallucination with no basis in fact.
Some people mistake conspiracy theories for truth, but often they are just a hallucination.
To believe the company could double its profits overnight is a hallucination at best.

Related Terms

  • synonym: delusion - a false belief or impression
  • antonym: reality - the state of things as they actually exist
