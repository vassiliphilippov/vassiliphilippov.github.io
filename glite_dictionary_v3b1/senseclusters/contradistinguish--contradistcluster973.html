contradistinguish

verb
distinguish by contrast
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːn.trə.dɪˈstɪŋ.ɡwɪʃ / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.trə.dɪˈstɪŋ.ɡwɪʃ / (BrE)
to distinguish (something) by contrasting its qualities with those of another thing

Examples

To contradistinguish two ideas
In her thesis, she contradistinguishes modernism from postmodernism by emphasizing their differing philosophies.
It is important to contradistinguish legal rights from moral obligations in this discussion.
The lush, green hills were contradistinguished from the barren, rocky valleys below.
