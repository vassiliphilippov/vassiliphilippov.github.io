hermaphrodism

noun
condition with both male and female sex characteristics
Pronunciation: / hɚˈmæf.rəˌdɪz.əm / (AmE), / həˈmæf.rəˌdɪz.əm / (BrE)
A congenital condition in which an individual has both male and female reproductive organs or characteristics.

Examples

Human hermaphrodism condition
Medical professionals now use different terminology, but hermaphrodism was once commonly referenced in textbooks.
Researchers studied the genetic causes underlying hermaphrodism in plants to better understand sex determination.
The rare condition of hermaphrodism has been documented in both humans and certain animal species.
