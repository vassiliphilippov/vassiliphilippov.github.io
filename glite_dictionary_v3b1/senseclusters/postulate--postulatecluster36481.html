postulate

verb
require as necessary
7.8 0.64/m
Pronunciation: / Ààp…ëÀêst É…ôÀåle…™t / (AmE), / Ààp…ístj äle…™t / (BrE)
to require or assume something as a necessary condition or prerequisite

Examples

Postulate a condition
Implementing this theory postulates a high level of technical expertise.
International law sometimes postulates the cooperation of all member states.
The proposed policy changes postulate a significant increase in government funding.

Synonyms

  • presuppose
    8.3 3.13/m
    presuppose assumes something is needed beforehand for a discussion, while postulate states it as a necessary starting condition.
  • suppose
    5.3 124/m
    suppose suggests you accept an idea as true for now, while postulate treats it as a formal necessary condition.
  • assume
    3.2 138/m
    assume means accept something without proof in daily speech, while postulate presents it as a required premise.
  • require
    1.1 364/m
    require means make something necessary by rule or need, while postulate means assume it as a prerequisite for an argument.
  • call for
    3.2 20/m
    call for asks that a situation needs something to happen, while postulate assumes it as a required condition.
  • need
    1.0 1430/m
    need expresses that something is necessary or important in reality, while postulate assumes it as a prerequisite for theory.
