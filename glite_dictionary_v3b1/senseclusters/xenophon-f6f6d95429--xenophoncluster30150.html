Xenophon

proper noun
Greek general and historian
9.5
Pronunciation: / ˈzɛnəˌfɑn / (AmE), / ˈzɛnəfən / (BrE)
(c. 430–354 BC) A Greek general, historian, and philosopher, known for his writings on the history of his time and his association with Socrates.

Examples

Xenophon the historian
Xenophon wrote extensively about the Persian Empire in his historical works.
As a student of Socrates, Xenophon provides valuable insight into classical Greek philosophy.
The leadership of Xenophon during the March of the Ten Thousand is still studied by military historians.
