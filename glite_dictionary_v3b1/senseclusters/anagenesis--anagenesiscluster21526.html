anagenesis

noun
evolution in one lineage
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌænəˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (AmE), / ˌænəˈdʒɛnəsɪs / (BrE)
evolutionary change within a single species or lineage without branching into new species

Examples

Gradual anagenesis change
The fossil record provides evidence of anagenesis in the gradual transformation of certain species.
Scientists debate whether the changes observed in this lineage are due to anagenesis or branching evolution.
Unlike cladogenesis, anagenesis involves continuous change without the formation of new species.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cladogenesis - evolutionary change that results in the branching of a lineage into two or more species
  • synonym: evolution - the process by which different kinds of living organisms develop and change over time
