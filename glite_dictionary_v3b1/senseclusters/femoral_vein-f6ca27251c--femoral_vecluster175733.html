femoral vein

noun
major vein of the thigh
Pronunciation: / ˈfɛmərəl veɪn / (AmE), / ˈfɛmərəl veɪn / (BrE)
the large vein in the thigh that runs alongside the femoral artery, carrying blood from the lower limb back toward the heart; it is a continuation of the popliteal vein and becomes the external iliac vein

Examples

The femoral vein in thigh
An ultrasound revealed a blood clot in the patient's femoral vein.
The femoral vein is often accessed for medical procedures requiring central venous access.
The surgeon carefully identified the femoral vein during the procedure to avoid complications.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: external iliac vein - the vein that the femoral vein continues into
  • antonym: femoral artery - the main artery of the thigh
  • degree lower: popliteal vein - the vein that the femoral vein continues from
