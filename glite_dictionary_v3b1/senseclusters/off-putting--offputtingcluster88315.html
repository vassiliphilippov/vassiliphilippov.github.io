off-putting

adjective
causing dislike or annoyance
7.0
Pronunciation: / ˈɔfˌpʊtɪŋ /; / ˈɔf ˌpʊ.tɪŋ /; / ˈɔf ˌpʊtɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈɒfˌpʊtɪŋ /; / ˈɒf ˌpʊ.tɪŋ /; / ˈɒf ˌpʊtɪŋ / (BrE)
causing dislike, discomfort, or making someone feel unwilling to continue; unpleasant or discouraging

Examples

The restaurant's strange smell was a bit off-putting, but the food was excellent.
His rude behavior can be off-putting to new acquaintances.
Some people find the formal atmosphere of the restaurant off-putting.
The strong smell from the kitchen was rather off-putting.
Her off-putting attitude made it hard for her to make friends.
I find his arrogance really off-putting in social situations.
The strong smell in the room was quite off-putting to the guests.
