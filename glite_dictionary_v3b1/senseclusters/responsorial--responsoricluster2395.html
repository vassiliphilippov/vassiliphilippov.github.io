responsorial

adjective
call-and-response style
Pronunciation: / rɪˌspɑnˈsɔriəl /; / rɪˌspɑːnˈsɔːriəl / (AmE), / rɪˌspɒnˈsɔːriəl / (BrE)
describing or relating to a style where a leader's phrase is answered by a group, especially in music or religious services

Examples

A responsorial hymn
The choir performed a responsorial hymn, with the congregation singing the responses.
In some traditional ceremonies, prayers are recited in a responsorial manner.
The teacher used a responsorial approach, asking questions and having the class answer together.
The choir performed a responsorial chant during the Sunday service.
A responsorial reading involves the congregation replying to the leader's verses.
Many traditional ceremonies include responsorial singing between the priest and the people.

Related Terms

  • synonym: antiphonal - involving alternating responses between two groups or performers
