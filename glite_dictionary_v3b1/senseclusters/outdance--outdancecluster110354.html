outdance

verb
to dance better
Pronunciation: / ˌaʊtˈdæns / (AmE), / ˌaʊtˈdɑːns / (BrE)
to dance better than someone else, especially in a competition or comparison

Examples

To outdance competitors

The young performer hoped to outdance the reigning champion at the festival.
Despite his nerves, he managed to outdance everyone at the audition.
No one expected the youngest contestant to outdance the professionals on stage.
In the final round, she outdanced her main rival with an energetic routine.
