friction prop

noun
steel support regulated by friction, used in mining/engineering
7.0 8.0
Pronunciation: /ˈfrɪk.ʃən prɑːp/ (AmE), /ˈfrɪk.ʃən prɒp/ (BrE)
a steel support in which yield is controlled by friction between two sliding surfaces or telescopic tubes, commonly used in mining or engineering.

Examples

Adjustable friction prop
Modern excavation sites often use adjustable friction props for added safety.
The miners installed a friction prop to provide temporary roof support in the tunnel.
This design relies on the ability of the friction prop to yield under excessive load.
