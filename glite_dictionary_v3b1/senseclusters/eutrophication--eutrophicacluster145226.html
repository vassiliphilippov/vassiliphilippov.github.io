eutrophication

noun

The process by which a body of water becomes overly enriched with nutrients, leading to excessive growth of plants and algae, depletion of oxygen, and death of animal life.

Examples

Eutrophication of lakes
Many fish species disappeared as eutrophication depleted the oxygen levels in the water.
Scientists are studying ways to prevent eutrophication in freshwater reservoirs.
The lake suffered from eutrophication due to agricultural runoff and improper waste disposal.
