volunteerism

noun
unpaid community service
3.5
Pronunciation: /vɔː.lən.ˈtɪr.ɪ.zəm/ (AmE), /vɒ.lən.ˈtɪə.rɪ.zəm/ (BrE)
the practice or belief of giving your time and services freely, without pay, to help others or support a cause, often through organized activities

Examples

Volunteerism is encouraged in many schools as a way to build community spirit.
Her passion for volunteerism led her to organize several charity events each year.
The city recognized the importance of volunteerism during the disaster recovery efforts.
The organization relies heavily on volunteerism to run its community programs.
Schools often encourage volunteerism among students to build a sense of social responsibility.
Many people see volunteerism as a way to give back to their communities.
The spirit of volunteerism brought the community together after the hurricane.
Schools encourage volunteerism by organizing service projects for students.
Corporate volunteerism has increased as companies support employees in community service.

Related Terms

  • synonym: altruism - selfless concern for the well-being of others
  • antonym: compulsory service - work or service required by law or authority
  • antonym: employment - paid work or occupation
  • synonym: philanthropy - the desire to promote the welfare of others, often through donations
  • synonym: volunteering - the act of working without pay to help others
