wink

(of a light) to gleam or glow intermittently; to flash on and off repeatedly.

Examples

Lights wink at night
A red warning light began to wink on the dashboard.
Distant lanterns winked in the midnight fog.
The little bulbs on the tree quietly wink throughout the evening.

Synonyms

  • blink
    2.0 5.10/m
    blink is a very quick, often single on-off of light, while wink means a repeated intermittent glow of a light.
  • flick
    3.7 1.18/m
    flick is a sudden quick flash that often lasts a moment, while wink describes an on-and-off glow that repeats.
  • flicker
    3.6 7.32/m
    flicker is a small unsteady change in light that makes it seem to tremble, while wink is a clear on-off flash.
  • strobe
    5.4 0.15/m
    strobe is a rapid, mechanical flashing often used in shows, while wink is a softer intermittent light that repeats slowly.
  • twinkle
    1.5 2.28/m
    twinkle is many small sparkling points like stars, while wink is a single light that flashes on and off repeatedly.
  • flash
    1.0 8.36/m
    flash is a sudden bright burst of light that may be one instant, while wink is a repeated on-and-off gleam.
