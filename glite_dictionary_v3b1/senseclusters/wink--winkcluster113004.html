wink

verb
gleam or flash intermittently (of a light)
5.3 2.5
Pronunciation: / wɪŋk / (AmE), / wɪŋk / (BrE)
(of a light) to gleam or glow intermittently; to flash on and off repeatedly.

Examples

Lights wink at night
A red warning light began to wink on the dashboard.
Distant lanterns winked in the midnight fog.
The little bulbs on the tree quietly wink throughout the evening.

Related Terms

  • synonym: flash - to shine or burst out with a sudden light
  • synonym: flicker - to shine unsteadily; to vary rapidly in brightness
