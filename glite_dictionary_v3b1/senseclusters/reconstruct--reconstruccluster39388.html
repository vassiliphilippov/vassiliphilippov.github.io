reconstruct

verb
help someone adapt or reform socially or politically
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkənˈstrʌkt / (AmE), / ˌriːkɒnˈstrʌkt / (BrE)
to help someone adapt or be reformed socially or politically, especially after significant upheaval or change

Examples

To reconstruct a community
After the war, various programs were launched to reconstruct the displaced citizens, giving them new roles in society.
Efforts to reconstruct former rebels included education and vocational training to support their integration.
The government aimed to reconstruct communities that had been torn apart by decades of conflict.

Related Terms

  • synonym: reform - to make changes in order to improve something, especially a person's behavior or a system
