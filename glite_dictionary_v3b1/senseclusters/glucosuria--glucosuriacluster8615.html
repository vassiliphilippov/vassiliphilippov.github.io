glucosuria

noun
glucose in urine (medical)
6.3
Pronunciation: / ˌɡluːkoʊˈsʊriə / (AmE), / ˌɡluːkəˈsjʊəriə / (BrE)
the presence of abnormally high levels of glucose in the urine, typically indicating an underlying health condition such as diabetes

Examples

High glucosuria levels
Routine testing revealed glucosuria, prompting further evaluation for diabetes.
Severe glucosuria is uncommon in individuals with healthy kidney function.
The presence of glucosuria can be an early sign of metabolic disorders.
