radiation

noun
energy in rays, waves, or particles
3.1 3.7
Pronunciation: /reɪ.diː.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /reɪ.dɪ.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (AmE), /reɪ.dɪ.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ /; / /reɪ.diː.ˈeɪ.ʃən/ (BrE)
energy that is transmitted or emitted in the form of rays, waves, or particles, especially as a result of nuclear or atomic processes

Examples

Harmful radiation
Doctors sometimes use controlled doses of radiation to treat certain types of cancer.
Sunlight is a form of radiation that provides energy to the Earth.
The nuclear plant uses shields to block harmful radiation from escaping into the environment.
Exposure to excessive radiation can damage living tissue and increase cancer risk.
Nuclear power plants monitor levels of radiation to ensure safety for workers and the environment.
Scientists use special instruments to detect and measure radiation from radioactive materials.

Related Terms

  • antonym: absorption - the process by which something, especially radiation, is absorbed
  • synonym: emission - the production or discharge of something, especially gas or radiation
  • synonym: radioactivity - the emission of ionizing radiation or particles due to nuclear decay
