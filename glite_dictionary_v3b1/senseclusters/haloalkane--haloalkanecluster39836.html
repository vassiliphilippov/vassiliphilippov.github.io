haloalkane

noun
organic compound with halogen-substituted alkane
3.7 8.6
Pronunciation: /heɪ.loʊ.ˈæl.keɪn/ (AmE), /heɪ.ləʊ.ˈæl.keɪn/ (BrE)
an organic compound in which one or more hydrogen atoms of an alkane have been replaced by halogen atoms

Examples

A haloalkane compound
Many refrigerants belong to the haloalkane class of compounds.
Researchers analyzed how the haloalkane reacted under exposure to UV light.
The toxicity of a haloalkane depends on the type of halogen atom attached.
