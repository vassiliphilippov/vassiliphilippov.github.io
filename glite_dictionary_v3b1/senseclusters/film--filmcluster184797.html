film

verb
record with a camera
1.0 17/m
Pronunciation: / fÉªlm / (AmE), / fÉªlm / (BrE)
to record moving images using a camera; to make a movie or video

Examples

To film a scene
She asked permission before filming the performance at the theater.
The documentary crew arrived early to film the sunrise over the mountain.
They filmed several scenes on location in Italy for the new movie.
She filmed the parade from her balcony and shared it online.
The crew will film the final scene at sunset tomorrow.
Tourists are not allowed to film inside the ancient temple.

Synonyms

  • shoot
    3.2 21/m
    shoot often means to record photos or movies quickly, while film focuses on recording moving images to make a movie.
  • video
    3.2 0.27/m
    video means recording with a video camera for playback, while film often means making a movie or recording moving images.
  • take
    1.7 76/m
    take means to make a photograph or film of someone or something, while film emphasizes recording moving images for movies.

Related Terms

  • synonym: photograph - to take a photograph of; to capture a still image
  • synonym: record - to capture sounds or images for preservation or later reproduction
  • synonym: shoot - to record or photograph using a camera, especially for movies or television
