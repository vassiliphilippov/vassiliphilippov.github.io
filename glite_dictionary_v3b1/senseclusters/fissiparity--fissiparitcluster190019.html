fissiparity

noun
tendency to split into parts
Pronunciation: / ˌfɪsəˈpærəti / (AmE), / ˌfɪsɪˈparɪti / (BrE)
The tendency or process of splitting or breaking into parts, or of dividing into separate entities.

Examples

Fissiparity of a group
Biologists observe fissiparity in certain simple organisms that reproduce by splitting.
Some historians argue that political fissiparity led to the collapse of the empire.
The fissiparity of religious movements can result in many distinct sects over time.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cohesion - the act or state of sticking together; unity
  • synonym: fragmentation - the process or state of breaking or being broken into small or separate parts
