infante

noun
royal title in Iberia
9.9
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈfɑːnteɪ / (AmE), / ɪnˈfanteɪ / (BrE)
a title given to a son of the king of Spain or Portugal who is not the heir to the throne

Examples

Spanish infante
The infante attended the royal wedding alongside his siblings.
As an infante, he participated in many official ceremonies but was not the heir.
The king's younger son was granted the title of infante at birth.
The young infante was educated at the royal court alongside his siblings.
During the ceremony, the infante stood beside the king and queen.
Unlike the heir, the infante did not have a direct claim to the throne.

Related Terms

  • antonym: heir apparent - the person who is first in line to inherit a throne
  • synonym: prince - a male member of a royal family, especially a son of a king or queen
