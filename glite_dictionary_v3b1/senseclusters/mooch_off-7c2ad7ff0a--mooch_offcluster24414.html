mooch off

verb
get things from others without paying or repaying
8.6 0.11/m
Pronunciation: / mut É …îf / (AmE), / muÀêt É …íf / (BrE)
to obtain something, especially food or money, from someone without paying or intending to pay, by relying on their generosity

Examples

To mooch off friends
He always tries to mooch off his roommates when they order takeout.
She was tired of him mooching off her every time he ran out of money.
You can't just mooch off your friends; you need to contribute something too.

Synonyms

  • mouch
    9.9 0.00/m
    mouch is a less common variant used in some dialects for begging or taking, while mooch off is the usual informal phrasal verb.
  • freeload
    7.5 0.22/m
    freeload means getting food or a place to stay without giving anything and often suggests long-term dependence, while mooch off suggests casual borrowing.
  • leech
    7.5 0.33/m
    leech is taking advantage of someone repeatedly and sounds harsher, while mooch off suggests casual, temporary reliance on someone's help.

Related Terms

  • synonym: freeload - to take advantage of someone else's hospitality without giving anything in return
