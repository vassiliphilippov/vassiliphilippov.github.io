mooch off

verb
get things from others without paying or repaying
Pronunciation: / mutʃ ɔf / (AmE), / muːtʃ ɒf / (BrE)
to obtain something, especially food or money, from someone without paying or intending to pay, by relying on their generosity

Examples

To mooch off friends
He always tries to mooch off his roommates when they order takeout.
She was tired of him mooching off her every time he ran out of money.
You can't just mooch off your friends; you need to contribute something too.

Related Terms

  • synonym: freeload - to take advantage of someone else's hospitality without giving anything in return
