tikkun

noun
Torah practice book
9.9
Pronunciation: / tɪˈkuːn / (AmE), / tɪˈkuːn / (BrE)
a book used by Torah readers to practice public chanting, showing both the text with vowels and cantillation marks and the traditional scroll text without them

Examples

A tikkun book
Before the service, she studied the tikkun to prepare for her Torah reading.
The rabbi recommended using a tikkun to practice the correct pronunciation and melody.
He carried his tikkun to synagogue every week for rehearsal.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Torah - the central reference of the religious Judaic tradition
