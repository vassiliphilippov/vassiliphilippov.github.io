texture

noun
the feel of a surface or fabric
1.0 2.8
Pronunciation: /ˈteks.tʃər/ /; / /ˈtek.ʃər/ (AmE), /ˈteks.tʃə/ /; / /ˈtek.ʃə/ (BrE)
the way a surface or substance feels or looks, especially how smooth, soft, or rough it is

Examples

Soft texture
She loved the silky texture of the new fabric.
The bread has a light, airy texture that melts in your mouth.
The rough texture of the bark made it easy to climb the tree.
She admired the rough texture of the stone walls in the old house.
The texture of the fabric was smooth and silky to the touch.
The chef explained how the texture of the bread affected its taste.
I love the soft texture of cashmere sweaters against my skin.
The texture of the bread was too dense, making it difficult to eat.
The chef explained that the texture of the soup was creamy because it was blended thoroughly.

Related Terms

  • synonym: grain - the texture seen in wood or stone
  • antonym: roughness - the quality of having an uneven or coarse texture
  • antonym: smoothness - the quality of having an even or soft texture
  • synonym: surface - the outside layer or uppermost part of something
