preapprove

verb
to authorize in advance
Pronunciation: / ˌpriːəˈpruv / (AmE), / ˌpriːəˈpruːv / (BrE)
to officially approve or authorize something in advance, before it happens or is requested

Examples

To preapprove a loan
The bank may preapprove you for a credit card based on your financial history.
Before you start shopping for a house, try to get preapproved for a mortgage.
Some employers preapprove travel expenses to avoid reimbursement issues later.
The bank can preapprove your loan before you find a house.
You should get preapproved for a credit card to speed up the application process.
Employers may preapprove travel expenses before employees book their flights.

Related Terms

  • synonym: approve - to officially agree to or accept something
  • synonym: authorize - to give official permission for something
  • antonym: reject - to refuse to accept or approve something
