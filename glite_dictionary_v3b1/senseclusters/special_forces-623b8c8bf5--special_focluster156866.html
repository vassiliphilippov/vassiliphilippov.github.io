Special Forces

noun
elite military unit for special operations
Pronunciation: / ˈspɛʃəl ˈfɔrsɪz / (AmE), / ˈspɛʃəl ˈfɔːsɪz / (BrE)
A highly trained military unit specializing in unconventional warfare, counter-terrorism, and special operations, especially as part of the United States Army.

Examples

Special Forces soldiers
Graduates of the rigorous selection process join the Special Forces as elite soldiers.
He served in the Special Forces for nearly a decade, taking part in numerous covert missions.
The government deployed Special Forces to handle the hostage crisis in the remote village.
