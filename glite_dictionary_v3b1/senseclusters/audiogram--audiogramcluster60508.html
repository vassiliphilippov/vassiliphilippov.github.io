audiogram

noun
graph of hearing sensitivity
Pronunciation: / ˈɔːdioʊˌɡræm / (AmE), / ˈɔːdiəʊˌɡræm / (BrE)
a graph that shows a person's sensitivity to different sound frequencies, used to assess hearing ability

Examples

Hearing test audiogram
A yearly audiogram helps to monitor any changes in auditory function over time.
After her hearing test, the audiologist explained the results shown on the audiogram.
The patient's audiogram revealed significant hearing loss in the high-frequency range.
