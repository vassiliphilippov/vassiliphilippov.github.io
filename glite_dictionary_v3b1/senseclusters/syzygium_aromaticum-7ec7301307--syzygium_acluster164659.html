Syzygium aromaticum

noun
tree cultivated for cloves
Pronunciation: / saɪˈzɪdʒiəm əˌroʊməˈtɪkəm / (AmE), / sɪˈzɪdʒiəm əˌrəʊməˈtɪkəm / (BrE)
A species of evergreen tree native to the tropics, cultivated for its aromatic flower buds which are the source of cloves.

Examples

Syzygium aromaticum tree
Syzygium aromaticum is widely grown in Indonesia for its valuable clove buds.
Researchers are studying the medicinal properties of Syzygium aromaticum.
The dried flower buds of Syzygium aromaticum are a key spice in many cuisines.

Related Terms

  • synonym: clove - the aromatic flower bud obtained from Syzygium aromaticum, used as a spice
