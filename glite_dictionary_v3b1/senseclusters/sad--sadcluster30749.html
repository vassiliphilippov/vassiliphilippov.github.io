  • distressing
    4.6 3/m
    distressing causes sharp pain, worry, or sorrow in people who witness it, while sad is a softer general feeling of unhappiness.
  • dismal
    4.5 6/m
    dismal gives a dull, gloomy view of a place or result, while sad simply names something that causes unhappiness.
  • grim
    4.2 5/m
    grim feels severe and bleak, like a hopeless result or future, while sad covers ordinary unhappiness or sorrow.
  • somber
    4.6 4/m
    somber looks serious, dark, and quiet in mood or color, while sad is a plain word for causing unhappiness.
  • lamentable
    6.7 0.6/m
    lamentable describes a regrettable, unlucky thing that deserves pity or shame, while sad means causing feeling of sorrow.
  • sorry
    3.5 17/m
    sorry often means poor, weak, or deserving pity in quality, while sad usually names an emotional feeling of unhappiness.
