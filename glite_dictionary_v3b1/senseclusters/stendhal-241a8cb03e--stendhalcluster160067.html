Stendhal

proper noun
French novelist known for psychological analysis
Pronunciation: / stɛnˈdɑl / (AmE), / stɒ̃ˈdal / (BrE)
(1783–1842) French writer noted for pioneering psychological analysis in his novels, including 'The Red and the Black' and 'The Charterhouse of Parma'.

Examples

Stendhal the writer
Stendhal had a keen insight into the complexities of human motivation.
I recently finished reading Stendhal's most famous novel, 'The Red and the Black'.
Many critics consider Stendhal a master of psychological realism in literature.
