depressant

adjective
reducing physiological or psychological activity
4.8 6.6
Pronunciation: / dɪˈprɛsənt / (AmE), / dɪˈprɛsənt / (BrE)
(of a substance) reducing or suppressing physiological or psychological activity or response.

Examples

Depressant effect
Alcohol is considered a depressant because it slows down brain function and neural activity.
Caffeine is a stimulant, but antihistamines often act as a depressant on alertness.
Some medications have a depressant effect on the central nervous system.

Related Terms

  • antonym: stimulant - a substance that raises levels of physiological or nervous activity
