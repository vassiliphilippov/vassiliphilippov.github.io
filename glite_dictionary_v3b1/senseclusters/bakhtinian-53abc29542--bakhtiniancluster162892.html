Bakhtinian

adjective
of Bakhtin's theories
9.9
Pronunciation: / bɑːkˈtiːniən / (AmE), / bækˈtɪniən / (BrE)
relating to the theories or ideas of the Russian philosopher Mikhail Bakhtin, especially in literary criticism

Examples

Bakhtinian theory
Her analysis takes a distinctly Bakhtinian approach to literary dialogue.
The novel's structure reflects a Bakhtinian emphasis on multiple voices and perspectives.
Many scholars apply Bakhtinian concepts to understand the complexity of modern texts.

Related Terms

  • synonym: dialogic - relating to dialogue or Bakhtin's concept of dialogism
