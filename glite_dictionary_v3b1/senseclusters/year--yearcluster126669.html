year

noun
period for a planet's orbit around the sun
Pronunciation: / jɪr / (AmE), / jɪə / (BrE)
a period of twelve months, or the time it takes a planet (especially Earth) to go around the sun once

Examples

A year on Earth
A Martian year is almost twice as long as an Earth year.
Saturn takes nearly 30 Earth years to complete one orbit around the Sun.
The scientists measured the length of a year on each planet.
His daughter is six years old and starting school.
She plans to travel around the world next year.
The project will take at least one year to complete.
