Saint James

proper noun
one of the twelve apostles of Jesus
Pronunciation: / seɪnt ˈdʒeɪmz / (AmE), / seɪnt ˈdʒeɪmz / (BrE)
(in the New Testament) one of the twelve apostles of Jesus, brother of John, and traditionally regarded as the author of the Epistle of James.

Examples

Saint James the apostle
Saint James is believed to have written the Epistle of James in the New Testament.
Many pilgrims travel the Camino de Santiago to honor Saint James each year.
The church was dedicated to Saint James, the brother of John.

Related Terms

  • synonym: James - the biblical character; apostle and New Testament author
