matured

adjective
fully developed or perfected
Pronunciation: / məˈtʃʊrd / (AmE), / məˈtʃʊəd / (BrE)
(of a plan, opinion, or work) fully considered and perfected; brought to a state of completion after thoughtful development.

Examples

A matured idea
A matured plan should anticipate potential risks and outline clear steps for action.
Only after several drafts did the author consider his novel matured and ready for publication.
The committee presented a matured proposal after several months of research and discussion.

Related Terms

  • antonym: immature - not fully developed or lacking maturity
  • synonym: mature - fully developed, grown-up, or having reached an advanced stage
