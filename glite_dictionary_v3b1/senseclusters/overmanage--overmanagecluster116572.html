overmanage

verb
to control too much
7.3
Pronunciation: / ˌoʊvərˈmænɪdʒ / (AmE), / ˌəʊvəˈmænɪdʒ / (BrE)
to control or supervise something or someone too much, especially in a way that is unnecessary or unhelpful

Examples

To overmanage a project
The project failed because the director tried to overmanage every aspect of the process.
Employees often feel frustrated when their supervisor begins to overmanage their daily tasks.
If you overmanage your team, you may limit their creativity and independence.
If you overmanage your employees, they may feel untrusted and lose motivation.
Some parents tend to overmanage their children's schedules, leaving little room for free time.
The new supervisor was careful not to overmanage the team, allowing them to work independently.

Related Terms

  • antonym: delegate - to give responsibility or tasks to others
  • synonym: micromanage - to control every small detail of someone's work
