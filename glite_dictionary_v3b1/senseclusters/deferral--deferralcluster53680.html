deferral

noun
the act of delaying or postponing
5.5
Pronunciation: / dɪˈfɜrəl /; / dɪˈfɜr.əl / (AmE), / dɪˈfɜːrəl /; / dɪˈfɜː.rəl / (BrE)
the act or state of delaying or postponing something to a later time

Examples

A deferral of payment
Due to unforeseen circumstances, the project was put into deferral status until next quarter.
The committee agreed to a deferral of the final decision until more data could be gathered.
University students may request a one-year deferral before starting their courses.
His request for a deferral of the payment was granted due to financial hardship.
The company announced the deferral of its new product launch until next year.
University students may apply for a deferral if they are unable to attend exams due to illness.
