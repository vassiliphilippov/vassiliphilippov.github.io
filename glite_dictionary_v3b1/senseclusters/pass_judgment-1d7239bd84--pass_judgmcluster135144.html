pass judgment

verb
form or express a critical opinion
1.0 4.2
Pronunciation: / pæs ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (AmE), / pɑːs ˈdʒʌdʒmənt / (BrE)
to form or express an opinion, especially a critical one, about someone or something

Examples

To pass judgment on others
Critics often pass judgment based on first impressions.
She was quick to pass judgment on his decision without knowing all the facts.
We should not pass judgment before hearing both sides of the story.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: condemn - to express complete disapproval or to sentence someone officially
  • synonym: judge - to form an opinion or evaluation about someone or something
