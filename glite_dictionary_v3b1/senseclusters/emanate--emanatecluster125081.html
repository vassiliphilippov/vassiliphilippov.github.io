to come out from or be sent out from a source, especially something like a smell, sound, or feeling

Examples

To emanate a smell
A strange scent seemed to emanate from the old chest in the attic.
Brilliant ideas often emanate from creative discussions and brainstorming sessions.
Laughter and warmth emanate from their home during family gatherings.
A faint aroma seemed to emanate from the freshly baked bread.
An eerie light emanated from behind the old door, making us curious.
Warmth emanates from the fireplace during winter evenings.

Synonyms

  • emit
    4.4 8.62/m
    emit is used for sending out physical things like light, gas, or sound, while emanate suggests coming from a source.
  • give off
    2.6 4.19/m
    give off is a common phrasal verb for producing smells, heat, or light, while emanate sounds more formal and literary.
  • give forth
    5.5 0.09/m
    give forth often appears in older or formal writing to report sounds or words, while emanate is more neutral and modern.
  • radiate
    4.3 2.04/m
    radiate sends out energy, heat, or light strongly from a center, while emanate can mean anything or feeling from a source.
  • exude
    6.5 0.61/m
    exude releases a liquid, smell, or steady quality slowly over time, while emanate describes something coming from a source.

Related Terms

  • synonym: emit - to send out gas, heat, light, sound, etc.
  • synonym: exude - to give off a liquid or smell slowly and steadily
  • synonym: originate - to have a specified beginning, to start from a source
  • synonym: radiate - to send out rays or waves, especially from a central point
