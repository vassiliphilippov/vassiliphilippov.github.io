peridinian

noun
flagellate protozoan with plate-like covering
Pronunciation: / ˌpɛrɪˈdɪniən / (AmE), / ˌpɛrɪˈdɪniən / (BrE)
a type of flagellate protozoan characterized by having a thick, plate-like outer covering.

Examples

A peridinian protozoan
A thick outer test distinguishes the peridinian from other similar flagellates.
Marine ecosystems can be rich in peridinians, which play a significant role in plankton communities.
The scientist observed a peridinian under the microscope, noting its plate-like structure.
