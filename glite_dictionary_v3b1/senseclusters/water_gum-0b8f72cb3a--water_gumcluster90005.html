water gum

noun
North American swamp tree with soft wood
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈwɔt̬ɚ ɡʌm / (AmE), / ˈwɔːtə ɡʌm / (BrE)
A columnar swamp tree of southeastern to midwestern North America that produces pale, soft, easily worked wood.

Examples

Water gum tree
In the wetlands, water gum trees thrive in the moist, swampy environment.
Lumber mills in the region often process water gum for various wood products.
The furniture was crafted from water gum because of its soft, workable wood.
