red flag

noun
warning sign or provocation
5.0
Pronunciation: / ˌrɛd ˈflæɡ /; / rɛd flæɡ / (AmE), / ˌrɛd ˈflæɡ /; / rɛd flaɡ / (BrE)
something that serves as a warning sign or indication of a problem or danger

Examples

A red flag in dating
For some people, not returning messages promptly is a major red flag in a relationship.
Her constant lateness was a red flag to her manager.
Ignoring these symptoms can be a red flag for serious health issues.
He noticed several red flags in the apartment contract before deciding not to sign.
Her unexplained absences at work were a serious red flag to her supervisor.
In a job interview, vague answers can be a red flag for potential employers.

  • antonym: green light - signal or indication to proceed; opposite of warning
  • synonym: warning sign - an indication that something bad might happen
