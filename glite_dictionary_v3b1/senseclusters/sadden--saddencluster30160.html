sadden

verb
to make or become sad
Pronunciation: / ˈsædən /; / ˈsæd.ən / (AmE), / ˈsæd(ə)n /; / ˈsæd.ən / (BrE)
to make someone (or yourself) feel sad or unhappy

Examples

To sadden someone
He tried not to let the gloomy weather sadden his spirits.
It always saddens me to see animals being mistreated.
The news of his departure truly saddened everyone at the office.
His harsh words saddened his friends more than he realized.
It greatly saddened her to hear about the accident.
The gloomy weather always seems to sadden me.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cheer - to make or become happy or more cheerful
  • antonym: cheer up - make someone feel happier
  • synonym: depress - to make someone feel extremely unhappy or discouraged
