hypercholesterolemic

adjective
high cholesterol-causing
8.5
Pronunciation: / ˌhaɪpərkəˌlɛstərəˈlimɪk / (AmE), / ˌhaɪpəkɒˌlɛstərəˈliːmɪk / (BrE)
causing or characterized by an abnormally high level of cholesterol in the blood, especially due to diet or treatment.

Examples

Hypercholesterolemic diet
A hypercholesterolemic diet can increase the risk of heart disease if consumed over a long period.
Doctors warned that certain foods are highly hypercholesterolemic and should be eaten in moderation.
The study compared the effects of a normal diet and a hypercholesterolemic diet on blood cholesterol levels.

Related Terms

  • synonym: hyperlipidemic - having or causing high levels of lipids in the blood
  • antonym: hypocholesterolemic - causing or characterized by low levels of cholesterol in the blood
