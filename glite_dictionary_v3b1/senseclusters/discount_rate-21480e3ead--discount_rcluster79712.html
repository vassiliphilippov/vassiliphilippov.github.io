discount rate

noun
central bank interest rate for banks
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪsˌkaʊnt reɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɪskaʊnt reɪt / (BrE)
the interest rate set by a central bank that is charged to commercial banks for borrowing funds from the central bank

Examples

Central bank discount rate
A change in the discount rate often signals the central bank's monetary policy intentions.
Banks can borrow money from the central bank at the discount rate when they face short-term liquidity needs.
The Federal Reserve decided to lower the discount rate to stimulate the economy.

Related Terms

  • synonym: interest rate - the proportion of a loan that is charged as interest to the borrower
