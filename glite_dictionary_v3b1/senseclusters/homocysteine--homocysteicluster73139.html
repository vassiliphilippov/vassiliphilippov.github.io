homocysteine

noun
amino acid in blood
8.5
Pronunciation: /hoʊ.moʊ.ˈsɪs.tiːn/ /; / /hoʊ.moʊ.ˈsaɪ.stiːn/ (AmE), /həʊ.məʊ.ˈsɪs.tiːn/ /; / /həʊ.məʊ.ˈsaɪ.stiːn/ (BrE)
an amino acid found in the blood, high levels of which are linked to an increased risk of heart disease

Examples

High homocysteine levels
Doctors often check homocysteine levels to evaluate a patient's risk of heart disease.
A diet rich in folic acid can help lower homocysteine in the blood.
Researchers are studying how homocysteine may contribute to cardiovascular problems.
