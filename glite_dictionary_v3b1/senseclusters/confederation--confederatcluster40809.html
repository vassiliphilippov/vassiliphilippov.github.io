confederation

noun
state of being allied; alliance
4.2
Pronunciation: / kənˌfɛdəˈreɪʃən / (AmE), / kənˌfɛdəˈreɪʃən / (BrE)
a group of states, organizations, or people that join together for a common purpose, while keeping their independence

Examples

A confederation of states
The countries created a confederation to strengthen their collective security against external threats.
The spirit of confederation among the tribes allowed them to resist their enemies more effectively.
Their confederation was fragile, breaking apart as soon as their common interests diverged.
Historical documents outline the structure of the confederation established in 1867.
Switzerland is often described as a confederation of cantons.
The states chose to form a loose confederation rather than a strong central government.
After lengthy negotiations, the countries agreed on the formal confederation of their states.
The confederation of labor unions strengthened their bargaining position.
The successful confederation of several tribes led to increased security for the region.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disunion - the state of being separated or not united
  • synonym: federation - a group of states with a central government but independence in internal affairs
