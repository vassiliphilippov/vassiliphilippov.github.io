Tzolk'in

noun
Maya sacred calendar
Pronunciation: / soʊlˈkiːn / (AmE), / tsɒlˈkiːn / (BrE)
the 260-day sacred calendar used by the ancient Maya civilization for religious and ceremonial purposes

Examples

The Tzolk'in calendar
The Tzolk'in played a vital role in scheduling religious ceremonies among the ancient Maya.
Archaeologists study the Tzolk'in to better understand Maya beliefs and rituals.
Each day in the Tzolk'in calendar had its own unique significance and deity.
