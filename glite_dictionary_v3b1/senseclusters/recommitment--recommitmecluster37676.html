recommitment

noun
return to institution
Pronunciation: / ˌriːkəˈmɪtmənt / (AmE), / ˌriːkəˈmɪtmənt / (BrE)
the act of committing someone again to an institution, such as a prison or psychiatric hospital

Examples

Court recommitment
The court ordered the recommitment of the patient after reviewing new medical evidence.
Following his parole violation, his recommitment to prison was inevitable.
The psychiatrist recommended recommitment due to the patient's recurring symptoms.

Related Terms

  • synonym: commitment - the act of sending someone to an institution
  • antonym: release - the act of setting someone free from an institution
