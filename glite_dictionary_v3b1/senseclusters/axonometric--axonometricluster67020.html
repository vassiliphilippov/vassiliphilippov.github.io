axonometric

noun
3D technical drawing
Pronunciation: / ˌæksənoʊˈmɛtrɪk / (AmE), / ˌæksənəʊˈmɛtrɪk / (BrE)
a drawing or projection that represents a three-dimensional object using parallel lines, without perspective distortion

Examples

An axonometric sketch
The architect presented an axonometric to help the client visualize the building's structure.
In technical manuals, an axonometric often illustrates how machine parts fit together.
She learned to create an axonometric as part of her engineering coursework.

Related Terms

  • synonym: isometric - a type of axonometric drawing with equal angles between axes
  • antonym: perspective - a drawing technique that shows objects as they appear to the eye with depth and vanishing points
