future

adjective
expressing a later time (grammar)
Pronunciation: / ˈfjuːtʃər / (AmE), / ˈfjuː.tʃə / (BrE)
(in grammar) relating to a verb tense expressing an action or state that will happen after the present moment.

Examples

Future form
In English, the future form is often constructed with 'will' or 'shall.'
Teachers often ask students to write predictions using the future tense.
This sentence uses the future tense to describe what will happen next year.

Related Terms

  • antonym: past - relating to the verb tense expressing action that has occurred
  • antonym: present - relating to the verb tense expressing action occurring now
