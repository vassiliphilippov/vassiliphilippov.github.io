antiphonally

adverb
alternating voices
Pronunciation: / ænˈtɪfənəli / (AmE), / ænˈtɪfənəli / (BrE)
in a way where two groups or voices alternate in singing or speaking

Examples

Sing antiphonally in choirs
The two choirs sang antiphonally, creating a beautiful echo in the cathedral.
During the ceremony, the congregation responded antiphonally to the priest's chants.
The verses were read antiphonally by students on opposite sides of the hall.

Related Terms

  • synonym: alternately - in turns, one after the other
