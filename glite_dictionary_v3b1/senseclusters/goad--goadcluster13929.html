goad

verb
provoke or urge to action
Pronunciation: / ɡoʊd / (AmE), / ɡəʊd / (BrE)
to provoke, urge, or encourage someone to take action, often by annoying, teasing, or challenging them

Examples

To goad someone to act
She tried to goad her brother into apologizing with sarcastic comments.
The manager's constant criticism goaded him to improve his performance.
They kept goading the bull until it finally charged.
Her friends tried to goad her into speaking up at the meeting.
Sometimes criticism can goad an artist to create better work.
The coach used tough words to goad the players into giving their best.
He goaded his colleague with sarcastic comments until she finally responded.
She tried to goad him into an argument with her constant complaints.
The coach would often goad the players by pointing out their mistakes.

Related Terms

  • antonym: calm - to make someone tranquil or less agitated
  • antonym: deter - discourage someone from taking an action
  • antonym: discourage - to persuade someone not to take action; to make less likely to act
  • antonym: dissuade - to persuade someone not to do something
  • degree lower: encourage - to give support, confidence, or hope to someone
  • degree higher: incite - to stir up or urge on to action, especially unlawful or violent action
  • synonym: needle - to provoke or annoy, especially by persistent criticism
  • synonym: provoke - to stimulate or incite someone to do something
