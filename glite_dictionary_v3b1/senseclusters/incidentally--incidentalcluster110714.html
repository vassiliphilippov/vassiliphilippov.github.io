Synonyms

  • in passing
    3.8 1/m
    in passing is said quickly while doing something else, while incidentally points to a minor or secondary detail in speech.
  • secondarily
    5.2 0.3/m
    secondarily is used to show something is less important or follows from something else, while incidentally marks a side or minor point.
  • tangentially
    7.7 0.3/m
    tangentially connects only a little to the main topic, while incidentally describes something minor and not central to the main idea.
  • peripherally
    6.8 0.2/m
    peripherally refers to something at the edge or outer part, while incidentally means it is of lesser importance or subordinate.
  • collaterally
    7.1 0.00/m
    collaterally often means an indirect or side effect alongside something else, while incidentally means a minor, subordinate point.
