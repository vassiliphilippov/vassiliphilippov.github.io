Pheidole

proper noun
ant genus
Pronunciation: / faɪˈdoʊli / (AmE), / faɪˈdəʊli / (BrE)
a genus of ants characterized by distinct worker castes, including large-headed soldiers, and found worldwide

Examples

Pheidole ant genus
The Pheidole colony displayed a clear division between minor and major workers.
Scientists study Pheidole ants to understand the evolution of social behavior in insects.
Large-headed soldiers are a distinctive feature of the Pheidole genus.

Related Terms

  • synonym: Formica - a common genus of ants found in many regions
