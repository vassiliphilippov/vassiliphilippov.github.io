dispose

verb
make willing or inclined
2.0
Pronunciation: / dɪˈspoʊz /; / dɪsˈpoʊz / (AmE), / dɪˈspəʊz /; / dɪsˈpəʊz / (BrE)
to make someone ready, willing, or inclined to do something or have a particular attitude

Examples

Dispose someone to help
Her calm demeanor disposed the team to trust her judgment.
His generous actions disposed others towards friendship and cooperation.
The pleasant atmosphere disposed guests to stay longer after dinner.
A good night's sleep will dispose you to learn more effectively.
Her calm demeanor disposed the team to trust her leadership.
The training sessions disposed the staff to handle emergencies more confidently.

Related Terms

  • antonym: disqualify - to make unfit or not prepared for something
  • synonym: incline - make someone feel willing or disposed to do something
  • synonym: prepare - to make ready for use or consideration
