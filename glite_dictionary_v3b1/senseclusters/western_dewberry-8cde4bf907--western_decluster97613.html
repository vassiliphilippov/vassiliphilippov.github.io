western dewberry

noun
a type of American blackberry plant
Pronunciation: / ˈwɛstɚn ˈduːˌbɛri / (AmE), / ˈwɛstən ˈdjuːˌbəri / (BrE)
An American species of blackberry with oblong black fruit, found in western regions of North America.

Examples

The western dewberry fruit
Foraging hikers often spot the western dewberry growing along rural fence lines in the spring.
The western dewberry thrives in sandy soils throughout Texas and Oklahoma.
The fruit of the western dewberry is valued both for eating fresh and in homemade jams.

Related Terms

  • synonym: blackberry - a small dark purple-black fruit; also the plant that bears it
