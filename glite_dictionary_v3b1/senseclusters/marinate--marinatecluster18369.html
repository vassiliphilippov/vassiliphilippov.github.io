marinate

verb
soak food in a flavored liquid before cooking
Pronunciation: / ˈmærɪˌneɪt /; / ˈmæɹɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / ˈmærɪneɪt / (BrE)
to soak food in a seasoned liquid before cooking to add flavor or make it more tender

Examples

To marinate vegetables
Before grilling, she likes to marinate the vegetables in olive oil and herbs.
The chef marinated the fish to make it more tender and tasty.
You should marinate the chicken overnight for the best flavor.
Before grilling, they always marinate the meat to make it more flavorful.
She likes to marinate tofu before adding it to stir-fry dishes.
You should marinate the chicken in lemon juice for a few hours.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pickle - to preserve food in vinegar or brine
  • synonym: soak - to leave something in liquid so that it becomes wet or soft
