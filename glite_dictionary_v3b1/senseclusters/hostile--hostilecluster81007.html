hostile

adjective
business: opposed takeover or offer
4.4 5.0
Pronunciation: /ˈhɑː.staɪl/ /; / /ˈhɑː.stəl/ (AmE), /ˈhɒ.staɪl/ /; / /ˈhɒ.stəl/ (BrE)
belonging to or coming from an enemy or opposing side, especially in war or business (such as a hostile takeover or hostile troops)

Examples

Hostile takeover
After months of rumors, shareholders braced for a hostile takeover attempt.
Companies often change their bylaws to defend against hostile acquisition efforts.
The board rejected the hostile bid from the rival corporation.
Intelligence reports warned of hostile troops advancing toward the capital.
The navy prepared to intercept hostile ships attempting to breach the blockade.
Two hostile aircraft were detected near the border by the radar system.
