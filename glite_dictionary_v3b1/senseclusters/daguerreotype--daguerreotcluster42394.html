daguerreotype

noun
early photograph on a silver-coated plate
7.2 9.8
Pronunciation: /də.ˈge.rɪ.oʊ.taɪp/ /; / /də.ˈge.rɪ.ə.taɪp/ (AmE), /də.ˈge.rɪ.əʊ.taɪp/ (BrE)
An early type of photograph made by exposing a silver-coated copper plate to iodine vapor to create a light-sensitive surface, then developing the image with mercury vapor.

Examples

A vintage daguerreotype photo
Collectors value each daguerreotype for its unique clarity and historical significance.
She inherited a daguerreotype portrait of her great-great-grandmother.
The museum displayed a rare daguerreotype of Abraham Lincoln from the 1840s.
