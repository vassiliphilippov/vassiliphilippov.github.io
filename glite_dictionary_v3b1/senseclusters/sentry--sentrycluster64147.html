sentry

noun
guard or lookout
6.0 1.64/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆsÉ›ntri / (AmE), / ËˆsÉ›ntri / (BrE)
a soldier or guard stationed to keep watch and prevent unauthorized passage or danger

Examples

A sentry stands guard
A sentry spotted the approaching convoy and sounded the alarm.
During their hike, they passed an old guardhouse where a single sentry once kept watch.
The sentry stood motionless at the entrance to the castle all night.

Synonyms

  • sentinel
    7.4 1.83/m
    a sentinel stands watch as a formal guard on duty, while a sentry is specifically a soldier posted to stop unauthorized passage.
  • picket
    6.1 0.38/m
    a picket stands at a forward post or patrols a perimeter, while a sentry is a soldier on fixed duty guarding access.
  • watchman
    4.1 1.47/m
    a watchman guards buildings, often at night as an employee, while a sentry is a soldier posted to stop trespass or danger.
  • lookout
    3.7 3.17/m
    a lookout watches for danger or signals from a hidden place, while a sentry is a visible soldier guarding an entry.
