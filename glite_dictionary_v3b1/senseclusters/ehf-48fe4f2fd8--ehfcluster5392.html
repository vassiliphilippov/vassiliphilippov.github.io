EHF

noun
extremely high frequency band (30–300 GHz)
9.6
Pronunciation: /iː eɪtʃ ef/ (AmE), /iː eɪtʃ ef/ /; / /iː heɪtʃ ef/ (BrE)
the range of radio frequencies between 30 and 300 gigahertz, known as extremely high frequency

Examples

EHF radio frequencies
Military radars using EHF frequencies can provide high-resolution imaging of targets.
Satellite communication systems often operate in the EHF range to achieve high data rates.
The EHF band is less susceptible to interference, but can be absorbed by rain and humidity.
