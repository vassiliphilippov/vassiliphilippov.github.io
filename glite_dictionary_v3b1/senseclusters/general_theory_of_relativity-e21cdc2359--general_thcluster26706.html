general theory of relativity

noun
Einstein's theory of gravity and space-time
4.2 5.5
Pronunciation: /ˈdʒen.ə.rəl ˈθiː.ər.iː əv rel.əˈtɪv.ɪ.tiː/ (AmE), /ˈdʒen.ə.rəl ˈθɪə.riː əv rel.əˈtɪv.ɪ.tiː/ (BrE)
Einstein's theory describing gravity as the curvature of space-time, generalizing special relativity to include gravitational effects.

Examples

Einstein's general theory of relativity
Einstein developed the general theory of relativity to explain gravity in terms of space-time curvature.
Modern cosmology relies heavily on the predictions of the general theory of relativity.
The general theory of relativity predicted the bending of light around massive objects, later confirmed by observation.
