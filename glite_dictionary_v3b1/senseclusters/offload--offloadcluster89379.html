offload

verb
to transfer responsibility
Pronunciation: / ˈɔfˌloʊd /; / ˌɔfˈloʊd / (AmE), / ˈɒfˌləʊd /; / ˌɒfˈləʊd / (BrE)
to transfer a task, responsibility, or burden to someone else, often to reduce your own workload

Examples

To offload work
She tried to offload her extra assignments onto her team members.
The company plans to offload some of its less profitable divisions next year.
He felt relieved after he could finally offload his worries by talking to a friend.
He tried to offload some of his duties onto his assistant before the deadline.
The company decided to offload its less profitable divisions to focus on core business.
She managed to offload her old textbooks by selling them online.

Related Terms

  • synonym: delegate - to give a task or responsibility to someone else
  • antonym: retain - to keep something rather than give it to someone else
