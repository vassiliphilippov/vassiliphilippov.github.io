reacquisition

noun
buying back assets
Pronunciation: / ˌriːˌækwəˈzɪʃən / (AmE), / ˌriːˌækwɪˈzɪʃən / (BrE)
the act of getting something back that was previously lost, sold, or given up

Examples

Reacquisition of property
The company's reacquisition of its former subsidiary allowed it to reenter the European market.
After several years, the museum celebrated the reacquisition of a famous painting it had once owned.
Shareholders approved the reacquisition of outstanding shares to increase the firm's value.
The reacquisition of the painting brought it back to the museum after many years.
After lengthy negotiations, the company celebrated the reacquisition of its original headquarters.
Language reacquisition can occur when someone returns to a country where their native language is spoken.

  • antonym: divestment - the act of selling off assets or investments
  • antonym: loss - the state of no longer having something
  • synonym: recovery - the process of regaining possession or control of something
  • synonym: repurchase - the act of buying something again
