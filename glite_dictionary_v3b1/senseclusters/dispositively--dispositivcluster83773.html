dispositively

adverb
in a decisive manner
9.9
Pronunciation: / dɪsˈpɑːzɪtɪvli /; / dɪsˈpoʊzɪtɪvli / (AmE), / dɪsˈpɒzɪtɪvli / (BrE)
in a way that settles a legal matter or issue decisively and finally

Examples

To act dispositively in court
The court acted dispositively by granting the motion to dismiss the case.
Her argument addressed the issue dispositively, leaving no room for further debate.
The contract was interpreted dispositively by the arbitrator, resolving the dispute.
The judge ruled dispositively on the matter, leaving no room for further debate.
The contract dispositively assigned ownership of the property to the new company.
Her lawyer argued dispositively to resolve the dispute over the inheritance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: decisively - in a way that produces a definite result
  • antonym: tentatively - in a way that is not certain or fixed
