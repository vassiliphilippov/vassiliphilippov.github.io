stalking-horse

noun
initial bid used to set a floor in auctions
Pronunciation: / c8st54k6a4b h54rs / (AmE), / c8st54d0k6a4b h54d0s / (BrE)
(in finance and bankruptcy auctions) a bidder or bid accepted as an initial, provisional offer intended to set a minimum price (floor) and encourage higher competing bids.

Examples

Stalking-horse bid
The debtor's estate adopted a stalking-horse bid to maximize recovery at the auction.
As trustee, she recommended accepting the stalking-horse offer to encourage competitive bidding.
Lawyers negotiated protections for the stalking-horse bidder before advertising the sale to creditors.

Related Terms

  • synonym: opening bid - the first or initial offer made in an auction or sale
  • synonym: preliminary bid - an early or provisional offer intended to begin the competitive bidding process
