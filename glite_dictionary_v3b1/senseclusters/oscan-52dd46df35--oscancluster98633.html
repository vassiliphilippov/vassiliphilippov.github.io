Oscan

noun
extinct Italic language of ancient Italy
Pronunciation: / ˈɑskən / (AmE), / ˈɒskən / (BrE)
an extinct Italic language once spoken in ancient southern Italy by tribes such as the Samnites, Aurunci, and Sidicini

Examples

Oscan language inscription
Few people today can read or understand Oscan scripts carved on those ancient stones.
Many ancient inscriptions were found written in Oscan by archaeologists.
The Oscan language was once widely spoken throughout southern Italy.
