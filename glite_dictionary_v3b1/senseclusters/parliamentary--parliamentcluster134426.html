parliamentary

adjective
relating to government accountable to parliament
Pronunciation: / ˌpɑrləˈmɛntəri / (AmE), / ˌpɑːləˈmentəri / (BrE)
(of a system of government) having the executive power resting with a cabinet selected from and accountable to the parliament or legislature.

Examples

Parliamentary system
A parliamentary government requires the support of the majority in the legislature to remain in power.
Australia is known for its stable parliamentary democracy.
Many democracies, such as the United Kingdom, have a parliamentary system.

Related Terms

  • antonym: presidential - (of a system of government) where the president has significant separation from the legislature
