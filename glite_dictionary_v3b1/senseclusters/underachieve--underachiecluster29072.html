underachieve

verb
do worse than expected
Pronunciation: / ˌʌndɚəˈtʃiv / (AmE), / ˌʌndərəˈtʃiːv / (BrE)
to perform less well or with less success than expected or potential

Examples

To underachieve at school
Despite his talent, Tom continues to underachieve at school year after year.
If you underachieve in this role, it could affect your chances of promotion.
Many companies underachieved in the last quarter due to economic challenges.

Related Terms

  • antonym: overachieve - to perform better or with more success than expected
  • synonym: underperform - to do less well than expected, especially financially or in comparison with others
