fly

verb
travel over (an area) by aircraft
Pronunciation: / flaɪ / (AmE), / flaɪ / (BrE)
to operate or travel in an aircraft, or to transport people or goods by aircraft

Examples

To fly a plane
She hopes to fly the Pacific Ocean next year.
The pilot flew over the city to get a better view for the passengers.
They flew across the Atlantic to visit their relatives in Europe.
He dreams of one day being able to fly his own helicopter.
Only experienced pilots are allowed to fly commercial jets.
She learned to fly a plane before she turned twenty.
During the holidays, many companies fly gifts overseas for their clients.
The aid agency plans to fly medical supplies into the disaster zone this afternoon.
They fly fresh seafood to restaurants across the country every morning.

Related Terms

  • synonym: pilot - to act as the pilot of an aircraft
  • synonym: ship - to send goods or people by another form of transportation, especially by ship or freight
  • synonym: travel - to go from one place to another, typically over a distance
