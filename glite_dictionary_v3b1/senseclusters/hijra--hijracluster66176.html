hijra

noun
Muhammad's migration
9.7
Pronunciation: / ˈhɪdʒrə / (AmE), / ˈhɪdʒrə / (BrE)
the migration of the Prophet Muhammad and his followers from Mecca to Medina in 622 CE, marking the beginning of the Islamic calendar

Examples

The Hijra of Muhammad
The hijra is considered a pivotal event in Islamic history.
Muslims use the year of the hijra as the starting point for their calendar.
Many historians study the causes and effects of the hijra from Mecca to Medina.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exodus - a mass departure of people, especially emigrants
