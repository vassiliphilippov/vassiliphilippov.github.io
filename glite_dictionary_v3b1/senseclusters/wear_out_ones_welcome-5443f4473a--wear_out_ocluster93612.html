wear out one's welcome

verb
outstay one's hospitality or approval
Pronunciation: / wɛr aʊt wʌnz ˈwɛlkəm / (AmE), / wɛə aʊt wʌnz ˈwɛlkəm / (BrE)
to stay or remain in a place or situation longer than one is wanted or expected, causing irritation or loss of goodwill

Examples

To wear out one's welcome quickly
After he asked for another favor, he realized he'd worn out his welcome at the office.
I didn't want to wear out my welcome, so I left after dinner.
She had a habit of wearing out her welcome by staying until midnight.

Related Terms
