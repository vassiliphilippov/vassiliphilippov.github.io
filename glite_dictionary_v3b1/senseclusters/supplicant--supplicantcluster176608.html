supplicant

noun
one who humbly asks or prays for something
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈsʌpləkənt /; / ˈsʌplɪkənt / (AmE), / ˈsʌplɪkənt / (BrE)
a person who humbly and earnestly asks or prays for something, especially in a religious or formal context

Examples

A humble supplicant
At the temple, each supplicant brought offerings and hopes for a better future.
Outside the king's palace, a supplicant awaited an audience to plead for mercy.
The desperate supplicant knelt before the altar in silent prayer.
As a humble supplicant, she asked for forgiveness and a chance to make things right.
In times of crisis, many people become supplicants, seeking divine guidance or intervention.
The supplicant knelt at the altar with hands clasped in fervent prayer.

Related Terms

  • antonym: benefactor - a person who gives help, opposite of someone seeking help
  • synonym: beseecher - someone who earnestly requests or pleads for something
  • synonym: petitioner - a person who formally requests something from an authority
  • synonym: suppliant - a person making a humble plea to someone in power or authority
