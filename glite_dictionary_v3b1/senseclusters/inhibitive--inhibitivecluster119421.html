inhibitive

adjective
preventing or restraining
7.5
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈhɪbətɪv / (AmE), / ɪnˈhɪbɪtɪv / (BrE)
tending to prevent, restrain, or slow down an action or process

Examples

An inhibitive effect
The new policy has an inhibitive effect on small business growth.
Some chemicals are added to paint for their inhibitive properties against rust.
High costs can be inhibitive for many people considering higher education.

Related Terms

  • antonym: encouraging - giving support or confidence to do something
  • synonym: inhibitory - serving to inhibit or restrain
  • synonym: restrictive - limiting or controlling something
