hermeneutics

noun
theory of text interpretation in theology
6.0
Pronunciation: / ˌhɜrməˈnuːtɪks / (AmE), / ˌhɜːməˈnjuːtɪks / (BrE)
The study or theory of the principles and methods of interpreting texts, especially in theology.

Examples

Hermeneutics theory
He devoted his doctoral research to hermeneutics and its impact on biblical interpretation.
Many scholars debate the best approach to hermeneutics when analyzing sacred writings.
Modern hermeneutics has expanded beyond religious texts to include philosophy and literature.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exegesis - critical explanation or interpretation of a text, especially scripture
