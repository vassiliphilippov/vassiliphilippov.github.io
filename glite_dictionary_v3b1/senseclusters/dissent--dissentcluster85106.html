dissent

verb
to disagree or withhold assent
9.1 5.5
Pronunciation: /dɪ.ˈsent/ /; / /də.ˈsent/ (AmE), /dɪ.ˈsent/ (BrE)
to disagree with or refuse to accept an official or majority opinion, especially in a formal or public way

Examples

To dissent publicly
He was not afraid to dissent when he believed the policy was unjust.
Several members of the committee dissented from the final decision.
The judge dissented from the majority opinion in the landmark case.
Although most of the committee supported the decision, a few members chose to dissent.
In a democracy, citizens have the right to dissent peacefully from government policies.
Several board members dissent from the proposed changes to the company's mission statement.
During the meeting, she dissented openly, voicing her concerns about the new policy.
Not everyone is willing to dissent when they disagree with authority.
Several members of the committee chose to dissent from the final decision.

Related Terms

  • antonym: agree - to have the same opinion or to consent
  • synonym: disagree - to have or express a different opinion; to not agree
