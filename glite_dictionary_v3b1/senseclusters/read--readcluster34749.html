read

verb
say written words aloud
Pronunciation: / rid /; / ɹiːd /; / riːd / (AmE), / riːd / (BrE)
to look at and understand written or printed words or symbols (silently or aloud)

Examples

To read a book
At the ceremony, she will read the official document to the audience.
Please read the instructions before beginning the exam.
The teacher asked me to read the first paragraph of the story aloud.
He reads the newspaper every morning with his coffee.
I read the instructions carefully before assembling the furniture.
She likes to read novels before going to sleep.
It took him years to read Japanese newspapers with ease.
Many children learn to read English at an early age.
She can read both Russian and French fluently.

Related Terms

  • antonym: misread - to read incorrectly or fail to understand the meaning
  • synonym: peruse - to read carefully or thoroughly
  • synonym: recite - to repeat aloud from memory or read aloud a piece of writing
  • synonym: scan - to look through written material quickly
  • synonym: understand - to grasp the meaning of something
