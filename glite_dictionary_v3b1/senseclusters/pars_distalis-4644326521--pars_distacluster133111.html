pars distalis

noun
front part of the anterior pituitary gland
Pronunciation: / pɑrz dɪˈsteɪlɪs / (AmE), / pɑːz dɪˈstɑːlɪs / (BrE)
the anterior part of the anterior pituitary gland, responsible for producing and secreting several essential hormones

Examples

The pars distalis gland
Damage to the pars distalis can disrupt endocrine function.
Researchers studied the cell types found in the pars distalis of laboratory mice.
The pars distalis produces hormones such as growth hormone and prolactin.
