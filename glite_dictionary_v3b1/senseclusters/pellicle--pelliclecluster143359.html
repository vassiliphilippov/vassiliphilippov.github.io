pellicle

noun
thin protective membrane in protozoa
Pronunciation: / ˈpɛlɪkəl / (AmE), / ˈpɛlɪk(ə)l / (BrE)
a thin, flexible, protective membrane covering the body of certain protozoa and some other microorganisms

Examples

A pellicle membrane
A damaged pellicle can make protozoa vulnerable to changes in their environment.
The pellicle gives the organism structural support without restricting movement.
Under the microscope, the pellicle of the protozoan appeared as a clear, flexible lining.
