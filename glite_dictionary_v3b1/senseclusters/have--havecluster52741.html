have

verb
receive; come into possession
3.7
Pronunciation: / hæv / (AmE), / hæv / (BrE)
to receive, come into possession of, or accept something

Examples

Have a present
Children usually have many gifts on their birthday.
Did you have any news from the office this morning?
She was thrilled to have a letter from her friend waiting for her.
I can't have that kind of behavior in my classroom.
She wouldn't have any more excuses for being late.
We were delighted to have him stay with us for the weekend.

Related Terms

  • synonym: accept - to consent to receive or take something offered
  • synonym: get - to obtain or come into possession of
  • synonym: receive - to be given or presented with something
  • antonym: refuse - to indicate or show that one is not willing to do or accept something
