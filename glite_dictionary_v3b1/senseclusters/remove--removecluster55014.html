remove

verb
dismiss from a position
1.0
Pronunciation: / rɪˈmuv /; / rɪˈmuːv /; / ɹɪˈmuv / (AmE), / rɪˈmuːv /; / ɹɪˈmuːv / (BrE)
to take something or someone away from a place, position, or situation; to get rid of or eliminate something (physical or abstract); to move or dismiss someone from a position or place; to leave a place (formal/old-fashioned)

Examples

To remove a stain
The board decided to remove the CEO after several poor financial quarters.
The coach was removed from his position following the scandal.
They are considering removing her as chairperson at the next meeting.
After the decision, the company removed its headquarters to another city.
The army removed its forces from the front line to a safer location.
The court removed the case to a higher jurisdiction for a new hearing.
It's important to remove expired items from the refrigerator regularly.
She decided to remove all the clutter from her office before the new year.
The editor suggested we remove unnecessary paragraphs to make the article clearer.

Related Terms

  • synonym: absent - to stay away deliberately or leave a place
  • antonym: add - to put something in; opposite of remove
  • antonym: appoint - to assign someone to a position or office
  • synonym: depart - to leave, especially in order to start a journey
  • synonym: discard - to throw something away or get rid of it
  • synonym: dismiss - to remove someone from their job or position
  • synonym: eliminate - to remove something completely
  • synonym: evict - to force someone to leave a place or property
  • synonym: transfer - to move someone or something from one place to another, especially officially
