requisitory

noun
prosecutor's address
Pronunciation: / rɪˈkwɪzɪˌtɔri / (AmE), / rɪˈkwɪzɪtəri / (BrE)
a formal speech or address by a prosecutor urging conviction or punishment in a legal case

Examples

Requisitory speech
The prosecutor delivered a passionate requisitory demanding a guilty verdict from the jury.
In his requisitory, the lawyer emphasized the seriousness of the crime and the need for justice.
The judge listened carefully as the requisitory outlined the reasons for a severe sentence.

Related Terms

  • antonym: defense - the speech or argument made by the defense lawyer in a trial
  • synonym: summation - a final speech summarizing the main arguments in a trial
