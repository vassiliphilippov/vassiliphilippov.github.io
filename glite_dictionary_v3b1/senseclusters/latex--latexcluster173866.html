latex

noun
milky plant fluid; source of natural rubber
3.3 4.4
Pronunciation: / ˈleɪ.tɛks / (AmE), / ˈleɪ.tɛks / (BrE)
a milky fluid produced by certain plants, which coagulates when exposed to air and is the source of natural rubber and other products

Examples

Natural latex from trees
Natural latex is harvested to produce products like gloves and balloons.
Some plants release latex as a defense mechanism against herbivores.
When exposed to air, the latex quickly thickens and forms a solid.

Related Terms

  • synonym: resin - solid or semisolid plant substance exuded by some trees
  • degree higher: rubber - elastic material made from latex
