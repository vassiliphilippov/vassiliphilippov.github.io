glycerin

noun
sweet, syrupy alcohol used in food and medicine
2.9
Pronunciation: / ˈɡlɪsərɪn /; / ˈɡlɪsəˌrin / (AmE), / ˈɡlɪsərɪn /; / ˈɡlɪsəriːn / (BrE)
A sweet, syrupy, thick, colorless liquid alcohol used in food, medicine, cosmetics, and industry, obtained by saponifying fats and oils.

Examples

Glycerin syrup
Pharmaceutical syrups sometimes use glycerin as a sweetener and thickening agent.
The soap was made using glycerin to keep it gentle on the skin.
You often find glycerin listed as an ingredient in moisturizers and lotions.
Glycerine is often used in skincare products to keep the skin moisturized.
Cakes sometimes contain glycerine to maintain their softness.
The laboratory purchased a bottle of glycerine for use in experiments.
