lymphoid tissue

noun
tissue of the lymphatic system
5.5
Pronunciation: / ˈlɪm.fɔɪd ˈtɪʃ.uː / (AmE), / ˈlɪm.fɔɪd ˈtɪs.juː / (BrE)
tissue consisting primarily of lymphocytes and other cells that form part of the lymphatic system, such as in lymph nodes and the spleen.

Examples

Lymphoid tissue in spleen
Chronic inflammation can cause the lymphoid tissue in the spleen to enlarge.
Swollen glands often indicate increased activity in the lymphoid tissue during an infection.
The tonsils are composed mainly of lymphoid tissue and help protect the body from pathogens.
