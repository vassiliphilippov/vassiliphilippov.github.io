Ascaridia galli

noun
parasitic roundworm of domestic fowl
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌæskəˈrɪdiə ˈɡælaɪ / (AmE), / ˌaskəˈrɪdɪə ˈɡali / (BrE)
A species of parasitic roundworm that infests the intestines of domestic fowl, causing disease.

Examples

Ascaridia galli parasite
Control measures for Ascaridia galli are crucial in poultry farming to maintain flock health.
Heavy infections of Ascaridia galli can lead to weight loss and poor egg production in hens.
The veterinarian diagnosed the chickens with an infestation of Ascaridia galli.
