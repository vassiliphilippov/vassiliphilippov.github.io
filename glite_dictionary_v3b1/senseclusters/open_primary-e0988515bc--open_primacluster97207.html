open primary

noun
primary election open to all voters
5.5 5.2
Pronunciation: / ˈoʊpən ˈpraɪˌmɛri / (AmE), / ˈəʊpən ˈpraɪməri / (BrE)
a preliminary election in which any registered voter may participate, regardless of party affiliation, but may vote for candidates of only one party.

Examples

An open primary election
In an open primary, voters do not have to be members of a political party to participate.
Many believe that an open primary encourages more voter participation and competition.
The state changed its election laws to allow for an open primary system.

Related Terms

  • antonym: closed primary - a primary election in which only registered party members can vote
