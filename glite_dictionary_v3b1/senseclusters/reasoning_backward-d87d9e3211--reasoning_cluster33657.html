reasoning backward

noun
solving by working from conclusion to evidence
7.3 5.9
Pronunciation: /ˈriː.zən.ɪŋ ˈbæk.wərd/ (AmE), /ˈriː.zən.ɪŋ ˈbæk.wəd/ (BrE)
A method of solving problems by starting with the desired conclusion and working backward to deduce the steps or evidence needed to reach it.

Examples

Reasoning backward method
Chess players employ reasoning backward to plan their moves several steps ahead.
Detectives often use reasoning backward to figure out how a crime was committed.
Solving certain mathematical problems requires reasoning backward from the desired answer.

Related Terms

  • antonym: reasoning forward - solving problems by starting from the evidence and moving toward the conclusion
