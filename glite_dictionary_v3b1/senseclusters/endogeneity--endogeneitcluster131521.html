endogeneity

noun
correlation with error term
Pronunciation: / ˌɛndoʊdʒəˈnɪəti / (AmE), / ˌɛndəʊdʒɪˈnɪəti / (BrE)
in statistics or econometrics, the condition where an explanatory variable is correlated with the error term, leading to biased or inconsistent estimates

Examples

Endogeneity in data analysis
Researchers must address endogeneity to ensure their statistical models produce reliable results.
The presence of endogeneity can make it difficult to interpret the effect of one variable on another.
Economists often use instrumental variables to solve problems caused by endogeneity in their analyses.

Related Terms

  • antonym: exogeneity - the condition where explanatory variables are not correlated with the error term
