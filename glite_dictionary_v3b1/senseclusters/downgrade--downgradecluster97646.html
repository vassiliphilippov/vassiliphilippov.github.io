downgrade

verb
to reduce in status or value
5.3
Pronunciation: / ˈdaʊnˌɡreɪd / (AmE), / ˈdaʊnɡreɪd / (BrE)
to reduce to a lower grade, rank, value, or level of importance; to treat as less important or worthy

Examples

To downgrade a job
After the incident, he was downgraded to a less prestigious position within the firm.
Many users chose to downgrade their software when the new version caused compatibility issues.
The credit agency decided to downgrade the company's rating after its profits fell sharply.

Related Terms

  • synonym: demote - to move someone to a lower rank or position
  • antonym: promote - to raise to a higher rank or position
