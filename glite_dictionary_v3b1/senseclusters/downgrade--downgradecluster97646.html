downgrade

verb
to reduce in status or value
4.9 2.55/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆdaÊŠnËŒÉ¡reÉªd / (AmE), / ËˆdaÊŠnÉ¡reÉªd / (BrE)
to reduce to a lower grade, rank, value, or level of importance; to treat as less important or worthy

Examples

To downgrade a job
After the incident, he was downgraded to a less prestigious position within the firm.
Many users chose to downgrade their software when the new version caused compatibility issues.
The credit agency decided to downgrade the company's rating after its profits fell sharply.

Synonyms

  • demote
    5.6 1.90/m
    demote is to move someone to a lower job rank, while downgrade means reduce rank, value, or importance more broadly.
  • relegate
    6.8 4.92/m
    relegate often sends a person or team to a less important place as punishment, while downgrade covers many kinds of lowering.
  • depreciate
    6.5 0.23/m
    depreciate means to make something seem less valuable or important, while downgrade also implies lowering rank or status.
  • devalue
    5.0 2.13/m
    devalue reduces a thing's market or social worth, while downgrade can mean reducing rank, importance, or level.
  • debase
    6.9 0.57/m
    debase lowers quality or dignity, often by adding something inferior, while downgrade is broader and can mean lower rank.
  • lower
    1.0 58/m
    lower means to move or make something less in amount or height, while downgrade focuses on rank, value, or importance.

Related Terms

  • synonym: demote - to move someone to a lower rank or position
  • antonym: promote - to raise to a higher rank or position
