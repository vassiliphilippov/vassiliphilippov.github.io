obstruct

verb
block from sight or passage
2.6 4.2
Pronunciation: /əb.ˈstrʌkt/ (AmE), /əb.ˈstrʌkt/ (BrE)
to block or get in the way of something, preventing movement, progress, or being seen

Examples

To obstruct the view
A large billboard obstructs the view from my window.
During the parade, crowds obstructed the entrance to the building.
Make sure nothing obstructs the emergency exit at any time.
Construction work can sometimes obstruct pedestrian access to public buildings.
He was accused of trying to obstruct the investigation by destroying evidence.
The fallen tree obstructed the road, forcing drivers to find another route.
A fallen tree obstructed the main road, causing a long traffic jam.
Please do not leave bags in the aisle, as they may obstruct the exit.
The protesters tried to obstruct the entrance to the building.

Related Terms

  • synonym: block - to obstruct, prevent movement or access
  • antonym: clear - to remove obstruction or blockage
  • antonym: facilitate - to make an action or process easy or easier
  • synonym: hinder - to make it difficult for someone or something to do something or for something to happen
  • antonym: unblock - to remove an obstruction or block from somewhere
