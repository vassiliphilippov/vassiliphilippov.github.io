bicam system

noun
legislature with two chambers
7.1 6.6
Pronunciation: /ˈbaɪ.kæm ˈsɪs.təm/ (AmE), /ˈbaɪ.kæm ˈsɪs.təm/ (BrE)
a legislative system with two separate chambers, typically a lower and an upper house

Examples

A bicam system government
Countries like the United States and the United Kingdom operate under a bicam system.
Debates in a bicam system can lead to more balanced and thoroughly considered legislation.
The bicam system allows for laws to be reviewed by two separate legislative bodies before they are enacted.
