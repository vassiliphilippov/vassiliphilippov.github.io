Helicobacter

proper noun
bacteria genus
8.1
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛlɪkoʊˈbæktər / (AmE), / ˌhɛlɪkəʊˈbæktə / (BrE)
a genus of bacteria, including Helicobacter pylori, that infects the stomach and can cause gastritis and ulcers

Examples

The discovery of Helicobacter revolutionized the understanding of stomach ulcers.
Doctors often test for Helicobacter in patients with persistent stomach pain.
Certain antibiotics are used to treat infections caused by Helicobacter.
