moratory

noun
temporary suspension
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈmɔːrəˌtɔːri / (AmE), / ˈmɒrətəri / (BrE)
a temporary delay or suspension of an activity, payment, or legal obligation

Examples

A moratory on loans
The government announced a moratory on new construction projects in the city center.
After the natural disaster, the bank offered a moratory on loan repayments for affected customers.
The court issued a moratory to temporarily halt the enforcement of the new regulation.

Related Terms

  • antonym: continuation - the act of carrying on without interruption
  • synonym: moratorium - a legal authorization to postpone payment or activity
  • synonym: postponement - the action of delaying something
