acmeism

noun
Russian 20th-century poetic movement emphasizing clarity
9.9 9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈæk.miˌɪz.əm / (AmE), / ˈak.miˌɪz.əm / (BrE)
A Russian school of poetry from the early 20th century that emphasized clarity, concrete imagery, and precise language, in contrast to Symbolism.

Examples

Acmeism poetry movement
Anna Akhmatova was a leading figure in the acmeism movement of the early twentieth century.
Many modern critics compare acmeism with the Imagist movement in English poetry.
The rise of acmeism transformed Russian poetry by focusing on concrete images and direct expression.
