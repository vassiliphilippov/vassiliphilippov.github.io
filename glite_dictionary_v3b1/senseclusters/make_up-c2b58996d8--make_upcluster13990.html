make up

verb
do missed work later
Pronunciation: / meɪk ʌp / (AmE), / meɪk ʌp / (BrE)
to do something to compensate for something missed, lost, or lacking; to make amends

Examples

To make up missed work
After missing class last week, she needs to make up the homework assignments.
He stayed after school to make up the lessons he had missed.
If you miss a test, you can make up the exam during office hours.
She tried to make up the difference with her own savings.
The company offered discounts to make up for the delayed deliveries.
We can make up the lost time by working extra hours this week.
He missed last week's meeting but plans to make up for it by working late tonight.
She tried to make up what she owed him with a small gift.
They gave me an extra day off to make up for the confusion.
