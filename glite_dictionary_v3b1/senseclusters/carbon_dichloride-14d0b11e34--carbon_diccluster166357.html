carbon dichloride

noun
chemical; antiparasitic agent
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑrbən daɪˈklɔrˌaɪd / (AmE), / ˌkɑːbən daɪˈklɔːraɪd / (BrE)
A chemical compound (CCl₂), used as an anthelmintic agent to treat infections caused by hookworms and other nematodes.

Examples

Carbon dichloride chemical compound
Exposure to carbon dichloride should be carefully monitored in laboratory settings.
Researchers studied the effectiveness of carbon dichloride against various nematode parasites.
The veterinarian prescribed carbon dichloride to treat the dog's hookworm infection.

Related Terms
