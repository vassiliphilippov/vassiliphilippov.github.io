pasture brake

noun
a tall, coarse fern found in pastures
7.2
Pronunciation: / ˈpæs.tʃɚ breɪk / (AmE), / ˈpɑːs.tjə breɪk / (BrE)
a large, coarse fern, often growing several feet high, commonly found in pastures; considered a weed in many regions

Examples

Thick pasture brake plants
During summer, thick clusters of pasture brake can be seen along old fence lines.
Farmers often struggle to control pasture brake as it competes with grass for space and nutrients.
The field was overgrown with pasture brakes that towered above the wildflowers.
