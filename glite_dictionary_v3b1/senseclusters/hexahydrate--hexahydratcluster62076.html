hexahydrate

adjective
containing six water molecules
Pronunciation: / ˌhɛksəˈhaɪdreɪt / (AmE), / ˌhɛksəˈhaɪdreɪt / (BrE)
describing a chemical compound that contains six molecules of water of crystallization per formula unit

Examples

Hexahydrate salt crystals
The copper(II) sulfate hexahydrate crystals are bright blue due to the water molecules.
When heated, the hexahydrate loses its water and turns into an anhydrous compound.
The label indicated that the chemical was in its hexahydrate form, not the anhydrous form.

Related Terms

  • degree lower: pentahydrate - a compound containing five water molecules
