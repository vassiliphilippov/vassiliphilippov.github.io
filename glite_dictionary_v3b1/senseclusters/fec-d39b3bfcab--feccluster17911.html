FEC

noun
chemotherapy regimen
Pronunciation: / ɛf i si / (AmE), / ɛf iː siː / (BrE)
a chemotherapy regimen that combines fluorouracil, epirubicin, and cyclophosphamide to treat certain cancers, especially breast cancer

Examples

FEC chemotherapy regimen
After her diagnosis, the doctor recommended the FEC regimen as part of her cancer treatment plan.
Many patients receive FEC to help shrink tumors before surgery.
The side effects of FEC can include fatigue and nausea, but it is often effective.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: FEC-T - a chemotherapy regimen that adds docetaxel to FEC
  • synonym: chemotherapy - the treatment of disease, especially cancer, using chemical substances
