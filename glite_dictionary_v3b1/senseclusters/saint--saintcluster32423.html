saint

noun
person recognized for exceptional holiness
2.6
Pronunciation: / seɪnt / (AmE), / seɪnt / (BrE)
a person recognized for being exceptionally holy or virtuous, often officially declared a saint by the Christian Church

Examples

A saint of the Church
After his death, he was declared a saint by the Church for his compassion and faith.
Many people look up to her as a modern-day saint because of her selflessness.
Mother Teresa was often called a saint for her charitable work with the poor.
After years of devotion and miracles, she was officially recognized as a saint by the Church.
Many believers pray to a saint for guidance and protection.
The city was named after a famous saint who lived there centuries ago.

Related Terms

  • synonym: martyr - someone who is killed because of their religious or other beliefs
  • antonym: sinner - a person who transgresses divine law by committing sin
