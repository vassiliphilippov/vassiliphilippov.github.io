polearm

noun
long-shafted weapon with blade or point
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈpoʊlˌɑrm / (AmE), / ˈpəʊlˌɑːm / (BrE)
A weapon consisting of a long shaft with a bladed or pointed fighting part attached to the end, used in close combat to extend the user's reach.

Examples

A polearm weapon
A polearm allows the user to keep enemies at a distance while attacking.
During the battle reenactment, the actors wielded polearms with remarkable skill.
The museum displays several medieval polearms used by foot soldiers.

Related Terms

  • synonym: halberd - a type of polearm with an axe blade topped with a spike
  • synonym: spear - a pole weapon with a pointed head for thrusting or throwing
