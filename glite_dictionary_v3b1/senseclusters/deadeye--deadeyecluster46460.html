deadeye

noun
round wooden fitting used on sailing ships
9.9 8.7
Pronunciation: / ˈdɛdˌaɪ / (AmE), / ˈdɛdˌaɪ / (BrE)
(nautical) A round, flat hardwood disk with holes and a grooved edge, used on sailing ships to tighten and hold the shrouds (ropes or wires supporting the mast).

Examples

Sailing ship deadeye
Each deadeye was carefully set in place to help stabilize the mast.
Restoring the antique ship involved carving new deadeyes from hardwood.
The sailors replaced the old deadeye to ensure the shroud was properly tensioned.
