capital levy

noun
one-time tax on property or capital assets
4.8
Pronunciation: / ˈkæpɪtəl ˈlɛvi / (AmE), / ˈkapɪtəl ˈlɛvi / (BrE)
a tax imposed on capital assets or property, usually as a one-time measure rather than a recurring tax

Examples

A capital levy tax
After the war, the government imposed a capital levy to help rebuild the country's infrastructure.
Economists debated whether a capital levy would discourage investment or promote fairness.
Historically, a capital levy has been used as an extraordinary means to raise public funds.

Related Terms

  • synonym: property tax - a regular tax levied on property owners
