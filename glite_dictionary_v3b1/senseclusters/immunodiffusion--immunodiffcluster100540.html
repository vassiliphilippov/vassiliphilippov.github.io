immunodiffusion

noun
antibody detection method
9.3
Pronunciation: / ɪˌmjunəʊdɪˈfjuːʒən / (AmE), / ɪˌmjuːnəʊdɪˈfjuːʒən / (BrE)
a laboratory technique where antibodies and antigens move through a gel and form visible lines when they react, used to detect or measure them

Examples

Immunodiffusion test
The laboratory performed immunodiffusion to confirm the presence of specific antibodies in the sample.
Researchers use immunodiffusion to compare antigen concentrations in different blood samples.
A clear line formed in the gel during the immunodiffusion test, indicating a positive reaction.
The laboratory used immunodiffusion to identify the presence of specific antibodies in the patient's serum.
Results from the immunodiffusion test showed clear lines indicating a positive reaction.
Researchers compared samples using immunodiffusion to confirm the identity of the proteins.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ELISA - a laboratory test to detect and measure antibodies or antigens using enzymes
  • synonym: immunoelectrophoresis - a technique combining electrophoresis and immunodiffusion to separate and identify proteins
  • synonym: precipitin test - a test detecting antigen-antibody reactions by visible precipitate
