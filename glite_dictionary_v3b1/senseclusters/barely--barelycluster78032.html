barely

adverb
almost not; only just
1.0 65/m
Pronunciation: / Ààb…õrli / (AmE), / Ààb…õÀêli /; / Ààb…õ…ôli / (BrE)
almost not; only just; by a very small margin (in amount, degree, or time)

Examples

He barely passed
He barely passed the exam, but he was relieved nonetheless.
She had barely enough money to pay her rent this month.
We could barely see the road through the heavy fog.
I had barely finished my sentence when the phone rang.
She barely arrived at the station before the train left.
We barely knew him before he moved away.
He barely touched his dinner because he felt unwell.
She barely had enough time to finish her assignment before the deadline.
The patient was barely conscious after the operation.

Synonyms

  • hardly
    1.0 79/m
    hardly expresses difficulty or near impossibility in everyday speech, while barely means only just or by a very small margin.
  • scarcely
    4.2 11/m
    scarcely sounds slightly formal and suggests almost none or only a little, while barely is common in everyday speech.
  • just
    1.0 247/m
    just often means very recently or exactly and can mean barely too, while barely always highlights only a small margin.
  • minimally
    3.7 3.08/m
    minimally is formal and used for the smallest degree in reports or plans, while barely is common in everyday speech.
