Synonyms

    hardly expresses difficulty or near impossibility in everyday speech, while barely means only just or by a very small margin.
  • scarcely
    4.2 3/m
    scarcely sounds slightly formal and suggests almost none or only a little, while barely is common in everyday speech.
  • just
    1.0 400/m
    just often means very recently or exactly and can mean barely too, while barely always highlights only a small margin.
  • minimally
    3.7 1/m
    minimally is formal and used for the smallest degree in reports or plans, while barely is common in everyday speech.
