normal distribution

noun
bell-shaped probability distribution
4.9 5.8
Pronunciation: / ˈnɔrməl dɪstrəˌbjuʃən / (AmE), / ˈnɔːməl dɪstrɪˌbjuːʃən / (BrE)
a probability distribution that is symmetrical around its mean, depicting that data near the mean are more frequent in occurrence than data far from the mean

Examples

A normal distribution curve
If test scores are assumed to have a normal distribution, most students will score near the average.
The concept of the normal distribution is fundamental to many statistical analyses.
The heights of adults in a large population often follow a normal distribution.
