Madagascar pepper

noun
spice plant with dark red peppercorns
3.5 7.1
Pronunciation: /mæ.də.ˈgæs.kər ˈpep.ər/ (AmE), /mæ.də.ˈgæs.kə ˈpep.ə/ (BrE)
A tropical climbing plant grown for its dark red peppercorn-like berries, originally from southern India and Sri Lanka, but also found in Madagascar and parts of Southeast Asia.

Examples

Madagascar pepper plant
Research has shown that Madagascar pepper thrives in humid, tropical climates.
She bought a packet of Madagascar pepper at the spice market during her travels in Asia.
The chef seasoned the dish with freshly ground Madagascar pepper for a unique flavor.
