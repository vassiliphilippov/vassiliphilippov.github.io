settle

verb
become quiet, fixed, or established
Pronunciation: / ˈsɛt̬l̩ /; / ˈsɛt̬.əl /; / ˈsɛtl̩ /; / ˈsɛt̬əl / (AmE), / ˈsɛtl̩ /; / ˈsɛt.əl /; / ˈsɛtəl / (BrE)
to become calm, quiet, stable, or come to rest after movement or uncertainty

Examples

To settle down quietly
After an hour, the excited crowd finally began to settle and listen to the speaker.
Once the argument was over, a calm mood settled over the house.
The dust from the renovation work will settle in a few hours.
A peaceful silence settled in the classroom after the teacher began to speak.
A thick fog settled on the lake, obscuring the view.
As darkness settled over the city, the streets grew quiet.
After a long journey, he finally settled himself by the fireplace.
She settled into her favorite armchair with a sigh of relief.
The bird settled on the branch and tucked its wings.

Related Terms

  • synonym: descend - to move or fall downward
  • synonym: ensconce - to settle securely or comfortably
  • synonym: perch - to sit or rest in a raised or secure position
  • synonym: rest - to cease movement in order to relax or remain still
  • synonym: stabilize - to make or become steady and unlikely to change suddenly
  • antonym: unsettle - to make uneasy or disturb
