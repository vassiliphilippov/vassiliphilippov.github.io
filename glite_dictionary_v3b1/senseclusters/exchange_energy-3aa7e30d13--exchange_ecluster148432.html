exchange energy

noun
energy from exchange interaction in particles
6.6
Pronunciation: / ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒ ˈɛnərdʒi / (AmE), / ɪksˈtʃeɪndʒ ˈɛnədʒi / (BrE)
the energy resulting from the exchange interaction between particles, especially electrons, in quantum mechanics and molecular systems.

Examples

Exchange energy in molecules
At large internuclear distances, the exchange energy between molecules becomes very small.
Quantum chemists study how exchange energy affects the stability of electron configurations.
The calculation of exchange energy is essential in understanding magnetic interactions in materials.

Related Terms

  • synonym: exchange interaction - the quantum mechanical effect due to the indistinguishability of identical particles
