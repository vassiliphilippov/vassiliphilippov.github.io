non-cash

adjective
not involving cash
Pronunciation: / nɑn kæʃ / (AmE), / nɒn kæʃ / (BrE)
recorded in accounts but not involving any actual cash payment or receipt

Examples

Non-cash expenses
The company reported several non-cash expenses, such as depreciation, in its financial statements.
Stock-based compensation is a common non-cash adjustment in accounting.
Analysts often separate non-cash items from cash flows to better understand a business's liquidity.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cash - involving actual money payments or receipts
