refuse

verb
not allow someone to have something
1.7 41/m
Pronunciation: / r…ôÀàfjuz /; / r…™Ààfjuz / (AmE), / r…™ÀàfjuÀêz / (BrE)
to say no to a request, offer, permission, or to not allow or give something

Examples

To refuse an offer
His parents refuse him sweets before dinner.
The club refused her entry because she was underage.
The teacher often refuses students extensions on their homework assignments.
Although invited to the meeting, he politely refused.
She refused to answer any of their questions about the incident.
They refuse to participate in activities that go against their beliefs.
Due to safety concerns, the organizers had to refuse entry to latecomers.
He felt hurt when they decided to refuse his application for membership.
The club may refuse anyone who does not follow the dress code.

Synonyms

  • decline
    3.4 16/m
    decline is a polite, often formal way to say no to offers or requests, while refuse is more general and neutral.
  • turn down
    2.9 11/m
    turn down is a common, informal way to say no to offers or invitations, while refuse covers many kinds of refusal.
  • reject
    1.9 52/m
    reject is a forceful refusal that often means not accepting or approving something, while refuse is broader and less judgmental.
  • deny
    1.6 23/m
    deny is to refuse to give, allow, or admit something, often blocking access or facts, while refuse is a general saying no.
  • withhold
    5.1 12/m
    withhold means to keep something back and not give it, often over time, while refuse is a simple act of saying no.
  • turn away
    2.8 4.22/m
    turn away means refuse someone entry or membership at a door, while refuse is a broader way to say no to requests.

Related Terms

  • antonym: accept - to consent to receive or undertake something offered
  • antonym: admit - to allow entry or membership
  • synonym: decline - to politely refuse an invitation or offer
  • synonym: deny - to refuse to give or grant something to someone
  • antonym: grant - to give or allow someone what they ask for
  • synonym: reject - to refuse to accept, believe in, or agree with something or someone
