chelation

noun
formation of ring structures by bonding to a metal ion
Pronunciation: / kiˈleɪʃən / (AmE), / kiˈleɪʃ(ə)n / (BrE)
the process in which a molecule forms several bonds to a single metal ion, resulting in a ring-shaped structure

Examples

Metal chelation
In coordination chemistry, chelation can significantly increase the stability of a complex.
Some therapies use chelation to remove heavy metals from the bloodstream.
The chelation of the metal ion occurs when the ligand wraps around and binds at several points.

Related Terms

  • synonym: ligand - an atom, ion, or molecule that binds to a central metal atom to form a coordination complex
