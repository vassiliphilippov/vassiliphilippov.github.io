muffled

adjective
softened or unclear sound
4.1 3.1
Pronunciation: / ˈmʌfəld / (AmE), / ˈmʌfəld / (BrE)
(of a sound) made quieter or less distinct by being obstructed or covered.

Examples

Muffled voices
Heavy curtains made the street noises seem muffled inside the apartment.
The sound of the alarm was muffled by the thick walls.
We heard the muffled voices of people talking in the next room.

Related Terms

  • antonym: distinct - (of a sound) clear and easily heard; opposite of muffled
  • synonym: muted - (of a sound) made quieter or less distinct; similar in meaning
