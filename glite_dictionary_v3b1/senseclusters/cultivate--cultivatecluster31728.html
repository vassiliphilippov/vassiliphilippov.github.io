cultivate

verb
develop or refine taste, skill, or quality
Pronunciation: / ˈkʌltɪˌveɪt / (AmE), / ˈkʌltɪveɪt / (BrE)
to develop, improve, or encourage the growth of a quality, skill, or relationship

Examples

To cultivate a skill
He has cultivated an appreciation for fine art over the years.
Reading widely can help you cultivate a critical mind.
She tried to cultivate a taste for classical music in her children.
As a teacher, she worked hard to cultivate her students' intellectual curiosity.
The organization aims to cultivate leadership skills among young people.
You should cultivate good relationships with your colleagues to create a supportive environment.

Related Terms

  • synonym: develop - to cause something to grow or become more advanced
  • synonym: foster - to encourage the development of something
  • synonym: improve - to make or become better
  • antonym: neglect - to fail to care for or pay attention to
  • synonym: promote - to help something develop or increase
