three-ring circus

noun
chaotic or hectic situation
Pronunciation: / ˌθriː rɪŋ ˈsɝːkəs / (AmE), / ˌθriː rɪŋ ˈsɜːkəs / (BrE)
A situation characterized by confusion, disorder, or hectic activity, often with multiple things happening at once.

Examples

The meeting was a three-ring circus
The press conference quickly became a three-ring circus when everyone started asking questions at once.
Trying to juggle work, school, and family can sometimes feel like living in a three-ring circus.
With so many last-minute changes, the meeting turned into a three-ring circus.
