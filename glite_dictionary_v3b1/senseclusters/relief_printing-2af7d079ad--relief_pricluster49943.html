relief printing

noun
printing using raised surfaces
5.9
Pronunciation: / rɪˈlif ˈprɪntɪŋ / (AmE), / rɪˈliːf ˈprɪntɪŋ / (BrE)
a printing method in which ink is applied to the raised surfaces of a plate and then transferred to paper or another material

Examples

Relief printing technique
Many early books were produced using relief printing techniques like woodcut and letterpress.
Modern artists sometimes revisit relief printing to achieve distinctive textured effects in their work.
The artist used relief printing to create bold, high-contrast images on the poster.

Related Terms

  • antonym: intaglio printing - printing from an incised or engraved surface
  • synonym: letterpress - printing process using raised, movable type
