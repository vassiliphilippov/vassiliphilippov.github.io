sleazy

adjective
of poor quality; flimsy
9.6
Pronunciation: / ˈsliːzi / (AmE), / ˈsliːzi / (BrE)
of poor quality, cheaply made, or shabbily constructed (including thin or loosely woven fabric)

Examples

A sleazy hotel
He bought a sleazy jacket that fell apart after one wash.
The hotel room was sleazy, with peeling wallpaper and a stained carpet.
The market is filled with sleazy knockoffs that break easily.
Some old books have a sleazy fabric covering the spine, which wears out quickly.
The sleazy curtains barely blocked any light from the room.
The jacket was made of sleazy material that tore easily.
