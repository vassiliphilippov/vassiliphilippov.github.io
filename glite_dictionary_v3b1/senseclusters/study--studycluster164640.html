study

noun
an academic subject or branch of knowledge
2.2 62/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆstÊŒdi / (AmE), / ËˆstÊŒdi / (BrE)
a branch of knowledge acquired by learning or investigation, especially as a subject in an educational curriculum

Examples

The study of history
Anthropology is the study of human societies and cultures.
Linguistics is the study of language and its structure.
She specialized in the study of marine biology at university.

Synonyms

  • subject
    1.6 45/m
    a subject is an area of knowledge usually taught in school, while study refers to the branch learned through investigation or coursework.
  • discipline
    4.9 23/m
    a discipline is a formal academic branch studied at university, while study refers to any branch learned within a curriculum.
  • field
    2.9 120/m
    a field is a broad area of work or activity, while study is specifically a branch of knowledge learned in education.

Related Terms
