XX

noun
normal female sex chromosomes (genetics)
Pronunciation: / ɛks ɛks / (AmE), / ɛks ɛks / (BrE)
the normal combination of two X chromosomes present in the cells of typical female mammals, determining female sex; used chiefly in genetics and biology.

Examples

XX chromosomes
Genetic testing revealed that she had an XX karyotype, confirming the typical female chromosome pattern.
In mammals, individuals with an XX chromosome pair usually develop as females.
The presence of two XX chromosomes is characteristic of most female humans.

Related Terms

  • antonym: XY - normal male complement of sex chromosomes (one X and one Y)
