destigmatization

noun
removal of stigma
Pronunciation: / diˌstɪɡmətəˈzeɪʃən / (AmE), / diˌstɪɡmətaɪˈzeɪʃən / (BrE)
the process of removing shame, negative associations, or social disapproval from a person, group, or issue

Examples

The destigmatization of illness
The destigmatization of mental illness has encouraged more people to seek help.
Efforts toward destigmatization have changed public attitudes about addiction.
Media coverage played a key role in the destigmatization of HIV and AIDS.
The destigmatization of mental health issues has encouraged more people to seek help.
Efforts toward destigmatization can change public attitudes about addiction.
Media campaigns have played a key role in the destigmatization of certain diseases.

Related Terms

  • synonym: normalization - the process of making something accepted as normal
  • antonym: stigma - a mark of shame or social disapproval
  • antonym: stigmatization - the act of treating someone or something as disgraceful or shameful
