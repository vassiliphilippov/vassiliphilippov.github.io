caretaking

adjective
temporary official role
Pronunciation: / ˈkɛrˌteɪkɪŋ / (AmE), / ˈkeəˌteɪkɪŋ / (BrE)
serving in a temporary role, especially as a substitute or acting official until a permanent replacement is found

Examples

Caretaking manager
The caretaking committee managed the organization until new leaders were elected.
During the transition, a caretaking manager was appointed to oversee daily operations.
She served in a caretaking capacity while the principal was on leave.

Related Terms

  • synonym: acting - temporarily performing the duties of another
  • antonym: permanent - lasting or intended to last indefinitely
