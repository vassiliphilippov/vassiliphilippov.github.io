Panglossian view

noun
naively optimistic perspective
9.9
Pronunciation: / pænˈɡlɑːsiən vjuː / (AmE), / pænˈɡlɒsiən vjuː / (BrE)
an excessively or naively optimistic perspective, especially in the face of adversity or evidence to the contrary

Examples

An overly Panglossian view
Adopting a Panglossian view can lead to ignoring real risks and challenges.
Critics accused the politician of having a Panglossian view of the economic situation.
Even when the company was struggling, he maintained a Panglossian view about its future.

Related Terms

  • antonym: cynicism - an inclination to believe that people are motivated purely by self-interest
  • synonym: optimism - the tendency to focus on the positive side of things
  • antonym: realism - the attitude of accepting the facts of life as they are
