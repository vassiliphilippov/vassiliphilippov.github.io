spellbound

adjective
fascinated or captivated as if by magic
Pronunciation: / ˈspɛlˌbaʊnd / (AmE), / ˈspɛlbaʊnd /; / ˈspɛlˌbaʊnd / (BrE)
so fascinated or interested by something that you give it all your attention, as if by magic

Examples

Spellbound audience
His spellbound audience hung on every word of his dramatic speech.
She watched the fireworks spellbound, unable to look away from the dazzling colors.
The children listened spellbound as the storyteller spun his tale.
She was spellbound by the beauty of the ballet performance.
The audience listened spellbound to the storyteller's mesmerizing tale.
The children sat spellbound as the magician performed his tricks.

Related Terms

  • antonym: bored - feeling weary because one is unoccupied or uninterested
  • synonym: entranced - filled with wonder and delight; held spellbound
  • synonym: mesmerized - having your attention fixated as if by a spell
