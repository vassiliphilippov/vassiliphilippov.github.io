catchlight

noun
eye reflection in photos
7.6
Pronunciation: / ˈkætʃˌlaɪt / (AmE), / ˈkætʃˌlaɪt / (BrE)
a small reflection of light visible in a person's eyes in a photograph or painting, making the eyes appear more lively

Examples

A catchlight in eyes
The photographer adjusted the lighting to create a perfect catchlight in the model's eyes.
A subtle catchlight can make a portrait look much more engaging and natural.
Painters often add a small catchlight to the eyes to give their subjects a sense of life.
