unapprehensive

adjective
not alert to danger
Pronunciation: / ˌʌn.æp.rɪˈhɛn.sɪv / (AmE), / ˌʌn.æp.rɪˈhɛn.sɪv / (BrE)
Not aware of or slow to recognize danger or risk; lacking apprehension.

Examples

An unapprehensive person
Despite the warnings, she remained unapprehensive about hiking alone in the mountains.
His unapprehensive attitude led him to ignore the obvious risks involved.
The children were unapprehensive as they wandered through the unfamiliar streets.

Related Terms

  • antonym: apprehensive - anxious or fearful that something bad will happen
