cold

adjective
not close to finding something sought
6.8
Pronunciation: / koʊld / (AmE), / kəʊld / (BrE)
not close to finding the answer, object, or solution; not successful or no longer fresh (as in a trail or lead)

Examples

A cold guess
His guess was cold; he was nowhere near the correct answer.
In the treasure hunt, Emily's first clue led her cold away from the prize.
You're getting cold—try looking on the other side of the room.
After several days, the fox's cold scent was almost impossible for the hounds to trace.
By the time the detective arrived, all leads had gone cold.
The police abandoned the search because the cold trail led nowhere.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fresh - newly found or recently discovered; opposite sense in this context
  • antonym: hot - close to finding the object or answer, especially in a guessing game
  • degree higher: warm - somewhat close to finding the object or answer
