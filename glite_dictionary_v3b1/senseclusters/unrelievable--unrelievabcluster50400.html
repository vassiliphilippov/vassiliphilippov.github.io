unrelievable

adjective
unable to be relieved
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌʌnrɪˈliːvəbl̩ / (AmE), / ˌʌnrɪˈliːvəbl̩ / (BrE)
not able to be relieved, forgiven, excused, or legally discharged, especially in reference to debts or penalties

Examples

Unrelievable debt
The court ruled that the damages were unrelievable and must be paid in full.
He was burdened by an unrelievable sense of guilt after the incident.
Some debts are considered unrelievable and cannot be discharged through bankruptcy.

Related Terms

  • antonym: forgivable - able to be forgiven or excused
  • synonym: irredeemable - impossible to correct, recover, or make better
