capsaicin

noun
compound causing chili pepper spiciness
Pronunciation: / kæpˈseɪsɪn / (AmE), / kæpˈseɪsɪn / (BrE)
a colorless, pungent, crystalline compound found in chili peppers, responsible for their spicy heat

Examples

Capsaicin in peppers
Capsaicin cream is sometimes used as a treatment for nerve pain.
Researchers study capsaicin to understand its effects on human metabolism.
The fiery taste of jalapeños comes from their high capsaicin content.

Related Terms

  • synonym: capsicum - the plant genus containing chili and bell peppers
