restrictionism

noun
narrow legal interpretation
9.9
Pronunciation: / rɪˈstrɪkʃənɪzəm / (AmE), / rɪˈstrɪkʃənɪzəm / (BrE)
a legal or judicial approach that interprets laws or rights narrowly to limit their scope and application

Examples

Legal restrictionism
The judge's restrictionism led to a very limited reading of the new privacy law.
Critics argue that restrictionism can prevent necessary legal reforms from being implemented.
Some legal scholars support restrictionism to avoid unintended consequences in court decisions.

Related Terms

  • antonym: expansionism - a policy or approach favoring broader interpretation or application
  • synonym: literalism - strict adherence to the exact letter or meaning of the law
