microfluidic

adjective
tiny fluid control
7.9
Pronunciation: /maɪ.krə.ˈfluː.ɪ.dɪk/ /; / /maɪ.kroʊ.ˈfluː.ɪ.dɪk/ (AmE), /maɪ.krəʊ.ˈfluː.ɪ.dɪk/ /; / /maɪ.krə.ˈfluː.ɪ.dɪk/ (BrE)
relating to the control, manipulation, or study of extremely small amounts of fluids, often in channels or devices at the microscale

Examples

Microfluidic technology
Researchers use microfluidic chips to perform rapid blood tests with only a drop of blood.
The microfluidic system allows scientists to mix chemicals in channels thinner than a human hair.
Advances in microfluidic technology have made portable diagnostic devices more accessible.

Related Terms

  • antonym: macroscopic - large enough to be seen without a microscope
  • synonym: microfluidics - the science and technology of systems that process small amounts of fluids
