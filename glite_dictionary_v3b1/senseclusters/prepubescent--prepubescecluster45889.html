prepubescent

adjective
before the onset of puberty
7.4 0.35/m
Pronunciation: / ÀåpriÀêpjuÀêÀàb…õs…ônt / (AmE), / ÀåpriÀêpjuÀêÀàb…õs…ônt / (BrE)
(especially of a person) at or relating to the stage immediately before puberty, often marked by accelerated growth and physical development.

Examples

Prepubescent stage
Her voice was still high and clear, typical of a prepubescent girl.
Many prepubescent boys experience a sudden growth spurt before their voices change.
The study focused on prepubescent children and their patterns of physical activity.

Synonyms

  • prepubertal
    7.9 0.03/m
    prepubertal is the clinical term for the body stage of rapid growth before puberty, while prepubescent is the general word for that stage.
  • preadolescent
    5.5 0.24/m
    preadolescent refers to children roughly nine to twelve years old before puberty, while prepubescent names the biological pre-puberty stage itself.

Related Terms

  • degree higher: pubescent - having reached the stage of puberty or showing signs of sexual maturation
