transfiguration

noun
the act of changing to a more exalted form
5.6
Pronunciation: / ˌtræns.fɪˈɡjʊr.eɪ.ʃən / (AmE), / ˌtræns.fɪˈɡjʊə.reɪ.ʃən /; / ˌtræns.fɪˈɡjʊə.rə.ʃən / (BrE)
a complete or significant change in form, appearance, or nature, often making something more beautiful, improved, or spiritual

Examples

A transfiguration of appearance
Her kindness brought about a remarkable transfiguration in the lives of everyone around her.
The painting depicted the transfiguration of the ordinary man into a legendary hero.
The sunrise cast the mountain in golden light, a natural transfiguration that left the hikers speechless.
Her face underwent a remarkable transfiguration when she heard the good news.
The artist's mural brought about a transfiguration of the once dull city wall.
The charity hopes for a transfiguration of the neighborhood through community projects.
