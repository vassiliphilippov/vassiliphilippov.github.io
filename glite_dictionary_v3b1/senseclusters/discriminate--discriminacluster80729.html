discriminate

verb
to recognize a difference
Pronunciation: / dɪˈskrɪməˌneɪt /; / dɪˈskrɪmɪˌneɪt / (AmE), / dɪˈskrɪmɪneɪt / (BrE)
to recognize or notice a difference between things; to distinguish

Examples

To discriminate sounds
Advanced equipment helps scientists discriminate one species from another based on genetic material.
Children can easily discriminate between the voices of their parents and other adults.
He struggled to discriminate the subtle differences between the two wines.
Most people can discriminate between subtle shades of color.
She struggled to discriminate the individual instruments in the orchestra.
The machine is able to discriminate among various types of metals.

Related Terms

  • synonym: distinguish - to recognize or treat something as different
