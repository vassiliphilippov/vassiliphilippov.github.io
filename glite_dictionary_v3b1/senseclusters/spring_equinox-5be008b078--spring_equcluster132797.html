spring equinox

noun
the time in spring when day and night are equal
Pronunciation: / sprɪŋ ˈiːkwəˌnɑks, ˈekwəˌnɑks / (AmE), / sprɪŋ ˈiːkwɪnɒks, ˈekwɪnɒks / (BrE)
The time or date in spring, usually about March 21 in the northern hemisphere, when day and night are of approximately equal length because the sun crosses the celestial equator.

Examples

The spring equinox date
Many cultures celebrate the spring equinox with festivals and rituals welcoming the return of light.
On the spring equinox, the sun rises exactly in the east and sets exactly in the west.
The spring equinox marks the beginning of longer days and shorter nights in the northern hemisphere.
