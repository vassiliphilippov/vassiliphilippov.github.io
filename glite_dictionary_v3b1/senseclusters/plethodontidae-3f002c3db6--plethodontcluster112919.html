Plethodontidae

noun
family of lungless salamanders
Pronunciation: / plɛθəˈdɑntɪˌdi / (AmE), / plɛθəˈdɒntɪˌdiː / (BrE)
A family of small, mostly terrestrial New World salamanders that as adults lack both lungs and gills.

Examples

Plethodontidae salamander family
Scientists have discovered several new species within the Plethodontidae family in Central America.
The Plethodontidae are widely studied for their unique adaptations to terrestrial life.
Unlike most amphibians, adult Plethodontidae absorb oxygen through their skin instead of lungs or gills.

Related Terms

  • synonym: salamander - an amphibian typically with a slender body and tail
