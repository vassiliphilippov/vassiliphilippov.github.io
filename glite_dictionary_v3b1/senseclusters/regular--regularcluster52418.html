regular

adjective
used informally to intensify a description
1.0
Pronunciation: / ˈrɛɡjələr / (AmE), / ˈrɛɡjʊlə / (BrE)
(informal, chiefly attributive) Used as an intensifier to emphasize a characteristic or type, often in a negative or emphatic sense.

Examples

Regular mess
He made a regular mess of things during the meeting.
That movie was a regular disaster from start to finish.
You’re a regular genius, aren’t you? (said sarcastically)

Related Terms

  • synonym: absolute - used as an intensifier, especially before negative or pejorative nouns
  • synonym: veritable - used for emphasis, intensifier
