magnetic storage

noun
data storage method using magnetization
5.9 3.2
Pronunciation: /mæg.ˈnet.ɪk ˈstɔːr.ɪdʒ/ /; / /mæg.ˈnet.ɪk ˈstɔːr.ədʒ/ (AmE), /mæg.ˈnet.ɪk ˈstɔː.rɪdʒ/ /; / /mæg.ˈnet.ɪk ˈstɔː.rədʒ/ (BrE)
a method of storing data using patterns of magnetization on a medium, such as hard disks or magnetic tapes

Examples

Magnetic storage devices
Although solid-state drives are common today, magnetic storage is still used for backups and archiving.
Floppy disks were once a popular form of magnetic storage in personal computers.
The computer's hard drive uses magnetic storage to store all your files and programs.
