tree onion

noun
a perennial onion forming bulbils at stem tops
Pronunciation: / tri ˈʌnjən / (AmE), / tri ˈʌnjən / (BrE)
a perennial variety of onion (Allium × proliferum) grown mainly for its small bulbils that form at the top of the stem, often used for early salad onions or as a curiosity.

Examples

Fresh tree onion bulbils
She added finely chopped tree onion bulbils to her salad for a unique flavor.
Some gardeners enjoy growing tree onions for their unusual clusters of bulbils perched atop tall stalks.
Unlike regular onions, the tree onion produces small bulbs where flowers would normally form.
