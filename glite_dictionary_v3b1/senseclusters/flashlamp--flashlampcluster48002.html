flashlamp

noun
brief intense lamp
Pronunciation: / ˈflæʃˌlæmp / (AmE), / ˈflæʃˌlæmp / (BrE)
a lamp that emits a brief, intense burst of light, often used in photography or scientific experiments

Examples

Photography flashlamp
The photographer used a flashlamp to illuminate the subject for the perfect shot.
A powerful flashlamp was needed to start the chemical reaction in the laboratory.
Engineers tested the durability of materials by exposing them to repeated bursts from a flashlamp.

  • synonym: strobe light - a device that produces regular flashes of light
