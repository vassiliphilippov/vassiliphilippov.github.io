infrared light

noun
type of electromagnetic radiation
Pronunciation: / ˌɪnfrəˌrɛd ˈlaɪt / (AmE), / ˌɪnfrəˌrɛd ˈlaɪt / (BrE)
Electromagnetic radiation with wavelengths longer than those of visible light but shorter than those of radio waves.

Examples

Infrared light waves
Many security cameras use infrared light to see in complete darkness.
Remote controls operate by sending pulses of infrared light to electronic devices.
The astronomers studied distant galaxies using telescopes sensitive to infrared light.
