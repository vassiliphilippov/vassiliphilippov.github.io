hollow

adjective
lacking substance or sincerity
5.5 4.48/m
Pronunciation: / ËˆhÉ‘loÊŠ / (AmE), / ËˆhÉ’lÉ™ÊŠ / (BrE)
(of a person, feeling, thing, or words) lacking real value, sincerity, or meaningful substance; empty or insincere

Examples

Hollow words
After his friend left, he felt strangely hollow inside.
His victory speech sounded hollow because no one believed in his promises.
Their praise seemed hollow after the truth was revealed.
His apology felt hollow because his actions never changed.
The politician's promises sounded hollow to voters who had heard them all before.
They celebrated a hollow victory that brought them no real satisfaction.

Synonyms

  • empty
    3.7 16/m
    empty feels common and plain when describing lack of feeling or meaning, while hollow often suggests insincerity or fake value.
  • vacuous
    8.1 0.43/m
    vacuous sounds formal and criticizes empty thought or speech, while hollow says something lacks real feeling or honesty.
  • meaningless
    5.1 13/m
    meaningless points to no real purpose or value, while hollow often adds a sense of insincerity or emotional emptiness.
  • vapid
    7.9 0.69/m
    vapid feels dull and lacking energy or interest, while hollow emphasizes lack of true value, depth, or sincerity.
