to cause someone worry, distress, inconvenience, or discomfort; to bother or disturb

Examples

To trouble someone
I didn't mean to trouble you with all these details, but I need your advice.
It has always troubled her that he never explained his reasons for leaving.
The rising cost of living is troubling many families across the country.
It troubles me to think that we may never find the answer.
That question has troubled philosophers for centuries.
The news of the accident deeply troubled her, and she couldn't sleep all night.
I hate to trouble you at this late hour, but it's urgent.
Please don't trouble yourself on my account.
She didn't want to trouble her parents with her problems.

Synonyms

  • bother
    1.0 40/m
    bother means to annoy or slightly worry someone, while trouble often covers worry, distress, or practical difficulty.
  • disturb
    2.5 9.66/m
    disturb often means interrupt or deeply unsettle someone's mind, while trouble can mean cause worry or inconvenience.
  • upset
    1.0 11/m
    upset makes someone feel unhappy, worried, or shaken, while trouble also includes causing practical problems or annoyance.
  • inconvenience
    4.9 1.27/m
    inconvenience means make things harder or less easy for someone, while trouble includes both worry and practical difficulty.
  • put out
    5.8 1.34/m
    put out causes small annoyances or delays to someone, while trouble covers worry, distress, or bigger personal problems.
  • disquiet
    7.3 0.11/m
    disquiet makes someone feel uneasy and anxious in mind, while trouble can also mean causing real practical or emotional harm.
  • perturb
    7.0 0.50/m
    perturb strongly unsettles or worries someone in formal contexts, while trouble is the common everyday word for worry or inconvenience.
