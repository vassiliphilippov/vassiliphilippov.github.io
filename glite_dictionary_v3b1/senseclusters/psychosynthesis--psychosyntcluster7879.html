psychosynthesis

noun
integrative therapy method
9.9
Pronunciation: /saɪ.koʊ.ˈsɪn.θə.sɪs/ (AmE), /saɪ.kəʊ.ˈsɪn.θə.sɪs/ (BrE)
a therapeutic approach that aims to integrate different or conflicting parts of a person's mind or personality into a unified and harmonious self

Examples

Psychosynthesis therapy
The therapist introduced psychosynthesis to help the client understand different parts of their personality.
Many people find psychosynthesis useful for achieving greater self-awareness and emotional balance.
She studied psychosynthesis as part of her training in holistic counseling.
Through psychosynthesis, clients work to bring together different aspects of their personality.
The therapist introduced psychosynthesis techniques to help him resolve inner conflicts.
Many people find psychosynthesis helpful for achieving greater self-understanding and harmony.

Related Terms

  • antonym: fragmentation - the process of breaking into separate or conflicting parts
  • synonym: integration - the process of combining parts into a whole
  • synonym: psychoanalysis - a method of therapy focusing on unconscious motives and conflicts
  • synonym: psychotherapy - the treatment of mental and emotional problems by talking with a therapist
