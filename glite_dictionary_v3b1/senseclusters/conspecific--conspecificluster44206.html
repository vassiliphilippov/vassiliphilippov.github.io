conspecific

adjective
belonging to the same species
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˌkɑːn.spəˈsɪf.ɪk / (AmE), / ˌkɒn.spɪˈsɪf.ɪk / (BrE)
belonging to or coming from the same species; of the same species.

Examples

Conspecific animals
Cabbage and its wild relatives are conspecific, sharing the same species classification.
Researchers studied the interactions between conspecific individuals in the primate group.
The two populations of birds are considered conspecific despite their different feather colors.

Related Terms

  • antonym: heterospecific - belonging to a different species
