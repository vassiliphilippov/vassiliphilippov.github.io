incompetence

noun
lack of ability or qualifications
4.4 5.05/m
Pronunciation: / …™nÀàk…ëÀêmp…ôt…ôns / (AmE), / …™nÀàk…ímp…™t…ôns / (BrE)
The inability to do something successfully or as it should be done, due to lack of ability or qualifications.

Examples

Manager's incompetence
His incompetence as a manager led to several missed opportunities for the company.
She blamed the delays on the incompetence of the technical support staff.
The pilot was dismissed due to gross incompetence during an emergency landing.

Synonyms

  • ineptitude
    7.4 1.34/m
    ineptitude shows lack of skill often from little training or experience, while incompetence means failing because one lacks ability or qualifications.
  • ineptness
    6.4 0.32/m
    ineptness shows clumsy, awkward performance that reveals poor skill, while incompetence describes inability to do a task properly due to lack of ability.
  • inability
    2.8 17/m
    inability is the simple state of not being able to do something, while incompetence points to failing because of lacking skill or qualifications.
  • incapability
    6.0 0.13/m
    incapability is a general lack of power or capacity to do something, while incompetence implies failing due to insufficient skill or qualifications.
  • inaptitude
    8.2 0.00/m
    inaptitude is lack of natural ability or fitness for a task, while incompetence means being unable to perform successfully because of missing skill or qualifications.
  • nonproficiency
    7.9 0.01/m
    nonproficiency means not meeting required skill levels for a task, while incompetence describes failing to do a job well because of lack of ability.
  • unskillfulness
    6.6 0.01/m
    unskillfulness is the plain state of lacking skill in doing something, while incompetence suggests inability to do a role correctly due to inadequate ability.
