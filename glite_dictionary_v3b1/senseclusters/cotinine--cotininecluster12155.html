cotinine

noun
nicotine metabolite
9.9
Pronunciation: / ˈkoʊtɪniːn / (AmE), / ˈkɒtɪniːn / (BrE)
a chemical made in the body after nicotine is broken down, used to show if someone has used tobacco

Examples

cotinine blood test
Doctors often measure cotinine levels in blood to determine if someone has been exposed to tobacco smoke.
Researchers found high cotinine concentrations in participants who reported regular cigarette use.
The presence of cotinine in urine is a reliable indicator of recent nicotine intake.
Doctors often test for cotinine to determine if a patient has recently used tobacco products.
The presence of cotinine in the blood is a reliable indicator of smoking.
Researchers measured cotinine levels in participants to study the effects of secondhand smoke.
