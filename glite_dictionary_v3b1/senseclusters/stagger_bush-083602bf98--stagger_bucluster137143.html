stagger bush

noun
deciduous poisonous shrub of eastern US
7.8
Pronunciation: / ˈstæɡər bʊʃ / (AmE), / ˈstæɡə bʊʃ / (BrE)
A deciduous shrub native to the coastal plain of the eastern United States, bearing nodding pinkish-white flowers and known to be poisonous to livestock.

Examples

The stagger bush plant
In spring, the stagger bush produces attractive pinkish-white flowers.
Several cows became ill after grazing near the stagger bush in the pasture.
The farmer warned his children to stay away from the stagger bush growing near the fence line.
