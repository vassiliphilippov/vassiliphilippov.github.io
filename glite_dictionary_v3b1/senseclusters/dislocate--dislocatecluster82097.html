dislocate

verb
move out of normal position
5.7
Pronunciation: / ˈdɪs.loʊ.keɪt / (AmE), / ˈdɪs.lə.keɪt / (BrE)
to move something from its normal or proper place or position, especially so that it is no longer correctly arranged or functioning

Examples

To dislocate roof tiles
Heavy winds can dislocate roof tiles and cause significant damage.
The accident caused the bone to dislocate from its socket.
Violence in the region could dislocate thousands of families from their homes.

Related Terms
