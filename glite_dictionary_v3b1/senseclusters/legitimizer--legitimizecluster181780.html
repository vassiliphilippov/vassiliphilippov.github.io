legitimizer

noun
one who makes valid
Pronunciation: / lɪˈdʒɪtəˌmaɪzər / (AmE), / lɪˈdʒɪtɪˌmaɪzə / (BrE)
a person or thing that makes something accepted as valid, legal, or official

Examples

The legitimizer of rules
The court ruling became a powerful legitimizer for the new policy.
He acted as a legitimizer for the controversial decision by providing expert testimony.
Some see the award as a legitimizer of her unconventional artistic style.
The court acted as the official legitimizer of the new political party.
Without a recognized legitimizer, the document had no legal standing.
International organizations often serve as legitimizers for peace agreements between countries.

Related Terms

  • synonym: authorizer - a person or body that gives official permission
  • antonym: challenger - a person who questions or disputes the validity of something
  • synonym: justifier - a person or thing that provides reasons for something
  • synonym: validator - a person or thing that confirms something as valid
