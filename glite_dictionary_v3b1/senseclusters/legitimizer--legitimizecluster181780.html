legitimizer

noun
one who makes valid
6.7
Pronunciation: /lə.ˈdʒɪt.ə.maɪ.zər/ /; / /lə.ˈdʒɪt.ɪ.maɪ.zər/ (AmE), /lə.ˈdʒɪt.ə.maɪ.zə/ /; / /lə.ˈdʒɪt.ɪ.maɪ.zə/ (BrE)
a person or thing that makes something accepted as valid, legal, or official

Examples

The legitimizer of rules
The court ruling became a powerful legitimizer for the new policy.

Some see the award as a legitimizer of her unconventional artistic style.

Without a recognized legitimizer, the document had no legal standing.
International organizations often serve as legitimizers for peace agreements between countries.
