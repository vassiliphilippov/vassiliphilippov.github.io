nonthrombocytopenic purpura

noun
purpura not caused by low platelet count
Pronunciation: / nɑnˌθrɑmbəˌsaɪtəˈpiːnɪk pɜrˈpjʊrə / (AmE), / nɒnˌθrɒmbəˌsaɪtəˈpiːnɪk pɜːˈpjʊərə / (BrE)
A form of purpura caused by defects in the capillaries, often due to bacterial infection or drug reaction, and not by a low platelet count.

Examples

Nonthrombocytopenic purpura caused by infection
Cases of nonthrombocytopenic purpura often resolve when the underlying infection or drug exposure is treated.
The patient was diagnosed with nonthrombocytopenic purpura after antibiotics caused capillary damage.
Unlike thrombocytopenic purpura, nonthrombocytopenic purpura usually occurs despite a normal platelet count.

Related Terms

  • antonym: thrombocytopenic purpura - purpura caused by low platelet count
