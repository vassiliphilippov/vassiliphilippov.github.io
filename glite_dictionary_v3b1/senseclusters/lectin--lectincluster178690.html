lectin

noun
a sugar-binding protein in plants
6.9 6.6
Pronunciation: /ˈlek.tɪn/ (AmE), /ˈlek.tɪn/ (BrE)
a type of protein found in plants and some other organisms that binds specifically to certain sugars and can affect biological processes

Examples

Lectins in plants
Researchers are studying the role of lectins in human health and disease.
Some beans must be cooked thoroughly to destroy the lectin that can cause digestive problems.
The lectin in certain seeds can bind to cell surfaces and alter their function.
