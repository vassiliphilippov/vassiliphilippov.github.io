encrust

verb
cover or decorate lavishly, especially with gems
Pronunciation: / ɪnˈkrʌst / (AmE), / ɪnˈkrʌst / (BrE)
to decorate or cover a surface thickly or lavishly, especially with small, shiny objects such as gems or shells

Examples

To encrust with jewels
Over time, the ship's hull became encrusted with barnacles and seashells, creating a rugged surface.
She wore a dress encrusted with shimmering beads that sparkled under the lights.
The ancient crown was encrusted with precious jewels and gold filigree.

Related Terms

  • synonym: embellish - to make something more attractive by adding decorative details
  • antonym: strip - to remove decorations or coverings
